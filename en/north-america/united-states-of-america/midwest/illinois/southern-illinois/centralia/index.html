Introduction to Centralia

Centralia is a charming city located in Southern Illinois, United States of America. It is known for its historic landmarks, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. This travel guide will take you through all the must-do things and places in Centralia, along with insider tips and recommendations.

List of Places to Visit in Centralia

Centralia is home to several iconic landmarks and attractions. Here is a list of must-visit places in Centralia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Centralia Carillon - One of the largest carillons in the world, the Centralia Carillon features 65 bells ranging in size from 22 to 6,550 pounds.
  • Centralia Area Historical Society Museum - Exhibits the history of Centralia, including its founding and development, as well as local folklore and cultures.
  • Kinmundy Historical Society Museum - Features exhibits that showcase the history of Kinmundy, a small town located just a few miles from Centralia.
  • Centralia Balloon Fest - A popular annual event that features hot air balloons, food, and live music.

Natural Attractions of Centralia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Raccoon Lake Recreation Area - A beautiful park that offers fishing, hiking, camping, and boating activities.
  • Kaskaskia River State Fish & Wildlife Area - Provides opportunities for hunting, fishing, hiking, and wildlife observation.
  • Carlyle Lake - The largest man-made lake in Illinois, Carlyle Lake is a popular spot for swimming, boating, fishing, and camping.
  • Shoal Creek Nature Conservation Area - With over 1,200 acres of natural habitat, Shoal Creek offers hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife observation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Centralia

Centralia is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Kaskaskia River State Fish & Wildlife Area Trail - A 12-mile trail that runs alongside the Kaskaskia River and offers scenic views and wildlife spotting opportunities.
  • Madonna of the Trail - A short, half-mile trail that leads to a statue of a pioneer woman along the National Road.
  • Carlyle Lake Bike Trail - A 13-mile paved trail that circles Carlyle Lake, offering beautiful waterfront views.

Best Things to Do in Centralia: Activities and Tours

Centralia offers a variety of things to do for travelers of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Centralia:

  • Centralia Cultural & Heritage Museum Tour - Take a guided tour of the Centralia Cultural & Heritage Museum to explore the city’s history and culture.
  • Centralia Carillon Concert - Listen to the Centralia Carillon play beautiful melodies during its daily concerts.
  • Centralia Balloon Fest - Attend the annual Centralia Balloon Fest to watch hot air balloons take off and enjoy live music and food.

Food and Drink Scene of Centralia

Centralia boasts a diverse food and drink scene that features locally sourced ingredients, regional specialties, and international flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Centralia:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich - A classic Midwestern sandwich made with a large breaded and fried pork tenderloin served on a bun.
  • Fried Catfish - A Southern staple, fried catfish is a crispy and flavorful dish that’s typically served with sides like coleslaw and hush puppies.
  • Chicken and Dumplings - A hearty and comforting dish made with tender chicken, fluffy dumplings, and flavorful broth.
  • Grandma’s Pie - A regional specialty, Grandma’s Pie is a deep-dish apple pie topped with a crumbly oatmeal-based streusel.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - Centralia is located near several wineries, including Willow Ridge Winery and Roundhouse Wine Company.
  • Beer - Centralia’s historic downtown area is home to several craft breweries, including Nelson Brewing Company and Manti’s Microbrews.

Where to Stay in Centralia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Centralia has a range of accommodation options that cater to various budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Harlan Hall Bed & Breakfast - A historic B&B that offers charming rooms and personalized hospitality.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham - A budget-friendly hotel that’s great for families and solo travelers.
  • Best Western Plus Centralia Hotel & Suites - A modern and well-appointed hotel that’s great for business travelers and couples.

What to Buy in Centralia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Centralia is a great destination for souvenir shopping and local product hunting. Here are some of the top picks for souvenirs and local products:

  • Illinois-made products - Visit the Illinois Made Shop in downtown Centralia to find locally sourced and artisan-made products.
  • Centralia T-shirts and merch - Stop by Centralia Outdoors for a wide variety of outdoor-inspired t-shirts and merchandise.
  • Antique and vintage items - Browse the many antique shops and thrift stores in downtown Centralia to find unique and one-of-a-kind treasures.

Local Culture and Customs of Centralia

Centralia has a rich cultural heritage that’s deeply rooted in the history of the Midwest. Some of the local customs and traditions that you might encounter in Centralia include:

  • Friendly and welcoming locals - Centralians are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors.
  • Love of outdoor activities - With so many natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Centralians have a deep appreciation for the outdoors.
  • Emphasis on community and family - Centralia is a tight-knit community that places a strong emphasis on family and community values.

Best Time to Visit Centralia

The best time to visit Centralia is from April to October, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re looking for a budget-friendly trip, consider visiting in the off-season (November to March) when hotel rates are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Centralia

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Centralia:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and natural attractions in and around Centralia.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks, including Illinois-made wine and beer.
  • Explore the city’s rich history and cultural heritage by visiting the many museums and historical landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the importance of safety when engaging in outdoor activities, particularly when hiking or boating.
  • Forget to pack for the weather - Centralia can experience extreme temperatures and changing weather patterns, so be prepared to layer and pack accordingly.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs - Centralians are proud of their heritage and traditions, so be mindful of local customs and show respect for the community.

Safety and Health

Centralia is a relatively safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take necessary precautions when exploring a new city. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Centralia:

Safety in Centralia

  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly when walking alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when engaging in outdoor activities, particularly during the summer months.
  • Observe safety signs and guidelines when visiting natural attractions, and avoid engaging in risky or dangerous behavior.

Health in Centralia

  • Take necessary precautions to prevent insect bites and tick-borne illnesses when hiking or spending time outdoors.
  • Be aware of changes in weather and temperature, and pack accordingly to prevent illness or injury.
  • In case of a medical emergency, contact the local emergency services or seek immediate medical attention at the nearest hospital or clinic.

Getting to Centralia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Centralia is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Airports Near Centralia - The nearest airports to Centralia are St. Louis Lambert International Airport (STL) and MidAmerica St. Louis Airport (BLV).
  • Bus Stations Near Centralia - Greyhound and Megabus serve the Centralia area.
  • Other Options - You can also travel to Centralia by car or train.

How to Get Around in Centralia

Centralia is a walkable city that’s easy to navigate on foot, but you can also take advantage of public transportation or rental cars to explore further afield. Here are some options:

  • Centralia Car Rental - Rent a car from a local rental company to explore the area at your leisure.
  • Public Transportation - The Rides Mass Transit District provides public transportation services to Centralia and surrounding areas.
  • Walking - Centralia’s downtown area is easily walkable and offers plenty of sights and attractions within a short distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Centralia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Centralia is a great base for exploring nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some ideas for places to visit after your trip to Centralia:

  • Shawnee National Forest - Located less than two hours from Centralia, Shawnee National Forest is a stunning natural area that offers hiking, camping, and stunning views of the Illinois countryside.
  • St. Louis - Just an hour’s drive from Centralia, St. Louis is a vibrant city that’s home to the famous Gateway Arch, the St. Louis Zoo, and the Missouri Botanical Garden.
  • Springfield - A two-hour drive from Centralia, Springfield is the capital of Illinois and features several sites of historic and cultural significance, including the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum and the Old State Capitol.

Conclusion

Centralia is a hidden gem in the heart of the Midwest, with a rich history, beautiful natural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a relaxing getaway, Centralia has something for everyone. Use this travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Centralia and experience all that this beautiful city has to offer.
