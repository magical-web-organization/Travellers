Introduction to Shawnee National Forest

Shawnee National Forest is a 280,000-acre national forest located in Southern Illinois, in the Midwest region of the United States. The forest boasts an incredible diversity of flora and fauna, breathtaking rock formations, and unique geological features. Shawnee National Forest is a popular destination for both outdoor adventurers and those seeking a peaceful retreat in nature. This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Shawnee National Forest

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site: This UNESCO World Heritage site was home to a pre-Columbian Native American city that thrived between 900 and 1500 AD. Visitors can learn about the archaeological history of the site and explore the impressive earthen mounds.
  • Lincoln Heritage House: Abraham Lincoln’s parents lived in this 19th-century house, which has since been restored and turned into a museum.
  • Giant City Lodge: This rustic lodge was built by the Civilian Conservation Corps in the 1930s and is now a popular spot for hiking and lodging.

Natural Attractions of Shawnee National Forest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Garden of the Gods: This stunning rock formation offers panoramic views of the surrounding forest. Visitors can hike the easy-to-moderate trails and explore the unique rock formations.
  • Little Grand Canyon: This canyon was formed by the erosive power of a small stream and offers breathtaking views of the forest. Visitors can hike the trails and enjoy the scenery.
  • LaRue Pine Hills: This hilly region is covered in unique pine forests and offers incredible views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike the trails and enjoy the serene surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shawnee National Forest

Shawnee National Forest has over 300 miles of trails for hiking and biking. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • River to River Trail: This 165-mile trail goes from the Mississippi River to the Ohio River and passes through Shawnee National Forest. It offers spectacular views of the forest.
  • Rim Rock Trail: This 1.7-mile trail takes visitors on a loop around the stunning Rim Rock formation.
  • Garden of the Gods Observation Trail: This 1/4-mile trail offers stunning views of the unique rock formations in Garden of the Gods.

Best Things to Do in Shawnee National Forest: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: Several stables in the area offer horseback riding tours of the forest.
  • Water Sports: Visitors can enjoy kayaking, canoeing, and fishing in the many lakes and rivers in the forest.
  • Wine Tasting: Shawnee Hills Wine Trail is home to over a dozen wineries and is a popular destination for wine lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shawnee National Forest

Day 1: Arrive in the forest and check into your accommodation.

Day 2: Visit Garden of the Gods and nearby Rim Rock Trail.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive through the forest and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 4: Explore the Little Grand Canyon and nearby trails.

Day 5: Visit the Lincoln Heritage House and nearby Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site.

Day 6: Go horseback riding or enjoy water sports in the forest.

Day 7: Take a wine tasting tour of the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Shawnee National Forest

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: This classic Southern dish is a staple in the area.
  • Pulled Pork: Another Southern favorite, pulled pork is often served atop a bun with barbecue sauce.
  • Cornbread: A classic side dish that pairs well with Southern cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Shawnee Hills Wine Trail is a popular destination for wine lovers.
  • Beer: Several breweries in the area serve up delicious craft brews.

Where to Stay in Shawnee National Forest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Giant City Lodge: This rustic lodge offers comfortable rooms and cabins, as well as a restaurant and gift shop.
  • Harpole’s Heartland Lodge: This luxurious lodge offers plenty of amenities, including a pool, hot tub, and game room.
  • Shawnee Forest Cabins: These cozy cabins are surrounded by nature and offer a peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Shawnee National Forest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: If you’re a wine lover, bring home a bottle or two from one of the wineries in the area.
  • Local Crafts: Several shops in the region sell handcrafted goods made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Shawnee National Forest

The people in Shawnee National Forest are friendly and welcoming. Southern hospitality is a way of life in this region. Visitors should respect the natural environment and leave no trace.

Best Time to Visit Shawnee National Forest

The best time to visit Shawnee National Forest is in the fall when the leaves change color and the weather is mild. Spring is also a great time to visit when the forest is in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shawnee National Forest

Do:

  • Respect the environment and leave no trace.
  • Observe wildlife from a distance.
  • Bring comfortable clothing and footwear for hiking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Feed wildlife.
  • Take anything from the forest.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shawnee National Forest

  • Visitors should be aware of the potential for ticks and other pests.
  • Be cautious when hiking near cliffs or steep drop-offs.
  • Always carry plenty of water and snacks on hikes.

Health in Shawnee National Forest

  • Visitors should protect themselves from the sun and wear appropriate clothing and sunscreen.
  • Be aware of poison ivy and other plants that may cause skin irritation.
  • Bring any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Shawnee National Forest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shawnee National Forest

  • Williamson County Regional Airport
  • Cape Girardeau Regional Airport
  • Lambert-St. Louis International Airport

Bus Stations Near Shawnee National Forest

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Carbondale, IL

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car and drive to the forest.
  • Private Shuttle Services: Several private shuttle companies offer transportation to Shawnee National Forest.

How to Get Around in Shawnee National Forest

The best way to get around Shawnee National Forest is by car, as many of the attractions are spread out throughout the region. Visitors can also hike or bike the many trails in the forest.

Where to Go Next from Shawnee National Forest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Carbondale, IL: This college town offers a lively arts and culture scene.
  • St. Louis, MO: This major city is home to many museums, sports teams, and attractions.
  • Nashville, TN: Music City USA is just a few hours’ drive from Shawnee National Forest.

Conclusion

Shawnee National Forest is a true gem in the Midwest region of the United States. With its stunning natural beauty, unique rock formations, and many outdoor activities, it’s a popular destination for both adventurers and nature lovers. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this incredible national forest.
