Introduction to Cobden

Nestled in Southern Illinois, Cobden is a quaint and charming town that has something for everyone. With a rich history and stunning natural scenery, this small town is the perfect destination for those seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and a taste of small-town life. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Cobden and the surrounding areas.

List of Places to Visit in Cobden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cobden is steeped in history, and visitors can explore the town’s roots at a variety of museums and historical sites. Some notable highlights include:

  • The Yellow Moon Cafe: This local hotspot is housed in an old hardware store and is a great place to stop for live music and Southern-inspired cuisine.
  • The Union County Museum: Located in downtown Cobden, this museum highlights the area’s history, from local Native American tribes to the present day.
  • The Old Feed Store Antique Mall: Visitors can browse antiques and collectibles at this popular Cobden attraction.

Natural Attractions of Cobden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Cobden and its surrounding areas offer a variety of outdoor adventures and scenic vistas to explore. A few must-see attractions include:

  • Bell Smith Springs Recreation Area: Hikers and nature enthusiasts will love exploring the trails and scenic views at this popular recreation area.
  • Pomona Natural Bridge: This stunning natural bridge is a great place to stop for a scenic hike and picnic.
  • Cache River State Natural Area: Bird watchers will love spotting local species and enjoying the area’s beautiful wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cobden

Cobden is home to a variety of hiking and biking trails, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some great routes include:

  • The River to River Trail: This trail spans across Southern Illinois and is a great option for hikers and backpackers.
  • The Tunnel Hill State Trail: This popular biking trail spans over 45 miles and showcases some of the area’s most breathtaking scenery.
  • The Shawnee National Forest: This expansive national forest is home to a variety of hiking trails, wildlife, and scenic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Cobden: Activities and Tours

Cobden offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. A few must-see experiences include:

  • Tasting Tours of the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail: Visitors can sample local wines and enjoy scenic views on this popular tour.
  • Visiting Local Festivals and Events: Cobden is home to a variety of events throughout the year, including the famous Peach Festival, showcasing the area’s local produce.
  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can explore the area on horseback, with a variety of tour options and trails available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cobden

For visitors planning a longer stay in the area, here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Cobden:

  • Day 1: Hike Bell Smith Springs Recreation Area and enjoy a meal at Yellow Moon Cafe.
  • Day 2: Visit the Union County Museum and take a scenic drive through the Shawnee National Forest.
  • Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Tunnel Hill State Trail and visit the Old Feed Store Antique Mall.
  • Day 4: Explore the Pomona Natural Bridge and enjoy a wine tasting tour of the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail.
  • Day 5: Visit the Cache River State Natural Area and enjoy a horseback riding tour.
  • Day 6: Attend a local festival or event.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Cobden

Cobden and its surrounding areas are home to a variety of delicious local delicacies and beverages. Some must-try dishes and drinks include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Deep Dish Pizza: This famous dish is a staple throughout Southern Illinois.
  • BBQ: With a variety of locally-owned BBQ joints, visitors can enjoy world-class BBQ in Cobden.
  • Local Produce: Cobden is home to a variety of local produce, including peaches, apples, and blackberries.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Visitors can sample local wines at the many wineries located throughout the area.
  • Craft Beer: Cobden is home to a variety of microbreweries, showcasing the area’s unique beer scene.
  • Moonshine: Visitors can try locally-made moonshine and other spirits, with options available at many local restaurants and distilleries.

Where to Stay in Cobden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cobden and its surrounding areas offer a variety of lodging options, from charming guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some great options include:

  • The Shawnee Hills Cabin: These charming cabins offer visitors a rustic and cozy lodging experience.
  • The Davie School Inn: This converted schoolhouse is now a popular bed and breakfast, offering guests a unique and historic stay.
  • The Timber Ridge Outpost and Cabins: These cabins offer visitors a glamping experience with luxurious amenities.

What to Buy in Cobden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Cobden won’t want to leave without taking home some local products and souvenirs. A few must-buy items include:

  • Local Produce: Visitors can take home some of the area’s delicious local produce, including peaches, apples, and berries.
  • Craft Beer and Wine: Visitors can purchase bottles of local microbrews and wines to bring home.
  • Moonshine and Spirits: Local distilleries offer visitors the chance to take home some locally-made moonshine and spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Cobden

Cobden is a friendly and welcoming small town, and visitors should expect to experience Southern hospitality throughout their stay. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including the value placed on small-town community and family.

Best Time to Visit Cobden

Cobden can be enjoyed year-round, with each season offering its own unique charm. Fall is a particularly beautiful time to visit, with colorful foliage and local festivals celebrating the harvest season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cobden

Do:

  • Try local produce and cuisine: Cobden is home to some of the area’s best BBQ and locally-grown produce.
  • Explore the great outdoors: With plenty of hiking and biking trails, visitors won’t want to miss out on exploring the area’s natural beauty.
  • Attend local festivals and events: Cobden and its surrounding areas offer a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, showcasing the area’s unique culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful: Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Overlook local products and culture: Cobden and its surrounding areas offer visitors a unique and authentic Southern experience.
  • Miss out on the area’s natural beauty: With so many opportunities for outdoor adventure, visitors should make sure to take advantage of the area’s stunning scenery.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cobden

Cobden is a safe and welcoming town, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. Visitors should exercise common sense when exploring the area’s outdoor attractions and adhere to posted safety guidelines.

Health in Cobden

Visitors to Cobden should make sure they are prepared for outdoor adventures with proper gear and clothing. It’s also important to stay hydrated, particularly during the warmer months, and to take precautions against ticks and other pests.

Getting to Cobden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Cobden have a few transportation options available to them, including:

Airports Near Cobden

The nearest airport to Cobden is the Southern Illinois Airport, located approximately 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Cobden

Greyhound operates a bus stop in nearby Carbondale, about 20 minutes from Cobden.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore the area, or take advantage of local tour companies and shuttle services.

How to Get Around in Cobden

Cobden and its surrounding areas are best explored by car, as many of the area’s natural attractions are located off the beaten path. Visitors can also take advantage of local shuttle services and tour companies for guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Cobden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors looking to explore the areas around Cobden, some popular destinations and excursions include:

  • Garden of the Gods: This stunning natural area is located about an hour away from Cobden, and features breathtaking rock formations.
  • The Shawnee National Forest: Visitors can continue exploring the area’s natural beauty at the Shawnee National Forest, located just south of Cobden.
  • Carbondale: This college town is located about 20 minutes from Cobden and offers visitors a variety of cultural experiences and dining options.

Conclusion

Cobden and its surrounding areas offer visitors a unique and authentic Southern experience, with a variety of cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer stay, Cobden has something for everyone.
