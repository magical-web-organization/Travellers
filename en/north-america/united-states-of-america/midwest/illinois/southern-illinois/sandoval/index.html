As a small town nestled in the heart of Southern Illinois, Sandoval may not be on everyone’s radar when it comes to must-visit destinations in the Midwest. However, Sandoval’s rich history, natural beauty, and unique culture make it a hidden gem that is well worth a visit. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover all the best things to do, places to stay, where to eat, and how to get around in Sandoval.

Introduction to Sandoval

Sandoval is a small community located in Marion County, Southern Illinois, with a population of just over 1,000 residents. Founded in 1854, Sandoval has a rich history that is still evident today in its well-preserved historic buildings, museums, and landmarks. Sandoval is also home to a variety of outdoor attractions, including hiking and biking trails, natural parks, and scenic lakes and rivers.

List of Places to Visit in Sandoval

When planning a trip to Sandoval, be sure to visit the following must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sandoval Coal Miner’s Memorial Park: Pay tribute to the coal mining heritage of the region with a visit to this park, which features a memorial statue and walking trail.
  • Sandoval Historical Society Museum: Explore the town’s history with a visit to this museum, which houses artifacts and exhibits showcasing Sandoval’s farming, mining, and industrial past.
  • Sandoval Heritage Days: Held annually in August, this festival celebrates Sandoval’s history and features a parade, live music, food vendors, and other family-friendly activities.

Natural Attractions of Sandoval: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Raccoon Lake State Recreation Area: Ideal for hiking, camping, and fishing, this scenic park features a 2,700-acre lake and miles of trails through wooded areas.
  • Kaskaskia River State Fish and Wildlife Area: A popular destination for hunters and anglers, this area boasts sprawling wetlands, forested areas, and miles of riverfront access.
  • Shawnee National Forest: Just a short drive from Sandoval, this expansive national forest offers numerous hiking and biking trails, rock formations, and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandoval

  • Coal Springs Trail: This 2.5-mile hike takes visitors on a scenic trek through the heart of Sandoval’s coal mining history, with informational signs detailing the area’s geology and ecology.
  • Salem Lake Trail: Located just a short drive from Sandoval, this 7-mile hiking and biking trail loops around picturesque Salem Lake, offering beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Sandoval: Activities and Tours

  • Sandoval Magic Show: Experience the wonder and excitement of a classic magic show with this family-friendly attraction, hosted at the Sandoval Community Center.
  • Sandoval Corn Festival: Held annually in September, this festive event features live entertainment, carnival rides, food vendors, and plenty of sweet corn.
  • Sandoval Skate Park: Perfect for skateboarders and rollerbladers, this small park offers a variety of obstacles and ramps for all skill levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sandoval

Day 1: Visit the Sandoval Historical Society Museum and the Coal Miner’s Memorial Park. Day 2: Explore Raccoon Lake State Recreation Area and take a hike on the Coal Springs Trail. Day 3: Spend the day fishing or hunting at the Kaskaskia River State Fish and Wildlife Area. Day 4: Take a scenic drive to Shawnee National Forest and hike to Garden of the Gods. Day 5: Visit Sandoval Corn Festival and enjoy live entertainment and carnival rides. Day 6: Take a bike ride on Salem Lake Trail. Day 7: Relax at Sandoval Skate Park and attend a Sandoval Magic Show in the evening.

Food and Drink Scene of Sandoval

Sandoval may be a small town, but it offers a variety of dining options to suit any taste. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef Brisket from G&D’s BBQ: Located just outside of Sandoval, this popular BBQ joint serves up mouth-watering brisket, ribs, and pulled pork.
  • Fried Chicken at The Anchor: A local favorite, this cozy diner is known for its crispy and juicy fried chicken, served with classic sides like mashed potatoes and green beans.

Local Beverages

  • Sandoval Winery: This family-owned winery produces a range of sweet and dry wines using locally sourced grapes.
  • Sam’s Package Liquor Store: A popular spot for locals to pick up beer and spirits, Sam’s also offers a selection of craft beers from Illinois and beyond.

Where to Stay in Sandoval: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Sandoval doesn’t have many lodging options, there are a few comfortable and affordable places to stay:

  • Sandoval Lake House: This charming lakeside cabin sleeps up to eight guests and features all the amenities of home, including a full kitchen and outdoor grill.
  • Sandoval Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and comfortable rooms with free Wi-Fi and flat-screen TVs.
  • Oxbow Bed and Breakfast: Just a short drive from Sandoval, this cozy B&B offers beautiful countryside views and homemade breakfasts.

What to Buy in Sandoval: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift from Sandoval? Consider these local products:

  • Sandoval Coal Miner’s Memorial t-shirt or keychain: Available for purchase at the memorial park, these simple but meaningful items commemorate the town’s coal mining heritage.
  • Wine from Sandoval Winery: Bring home a bottle of locally made wine, available for purchase at the winery’s tasting room.
  • Olive oil from Oxbow Bed and Breakfast: Made with olives grown on the property, this flavorful oil is sold on-site at the B&B.

Local Culture and Customs of Sandoval

Sandoval is a tight-knit community with strong ties to its coal mining heritage. Visitors can expect a warm welcome and a friendly atmosphere, with locals eager to share their town’s unique history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sandoval

Sandoval is a year-round destination, with outdoor activities and cultural events taking place throughout the year. However, the best time to visit depends on your personal preferences - spring and fall offer mild temperatures and colorful foliage, while summer is ideal for boating and swimming at Raccoon Lake, and winter offers a cozy atmosphere perfect for indoor activities like the Sandoval Magic Show.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandoval

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs, including the area’s mining heritage.
  • Take advantage of the numerous outdoor activities and scenic views in the area.
  • Attend local events and festivals to fully immerse yourself in Sandoval’s unique culture.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or disturb natural habitats.
  • Disrespect local wildlife or nature.
  • Ignore local rules and regulations for hunting, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sandoval

Sandoval is a generally safe place to visit, with low rates of crime and a friendly and welcoming community. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions such as locking car doors and avoiding dangerous areas at night.

Health in Sandoval

While there are no major health concerns in Sandoval, visitors should be aware of potential tick-borne illnesses when spending time outdoors in wooded areas. Be sure to check for ticks regularly and seek medical attention if you experience symptoms such as fever or rash.

Getting to Sandoval: Transportation Options and Logistics

Located just off Interstate 57, Sandoval is easily accessible by car. Additionally, visitors can reach Sandoval via public transportation:

Airports Near Sandoval

  • St. Louis Lambert International Airport (STL): Located approximately 90 miles southwest of Sandoval, this major airport serves numerous national and international destinations.
  • Central Illinois Regional Airport (BMI): Located approximately 80 miles northeast of Sandoval, this smaller airport offers a range of domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Sandoval

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Morton, IL): Located approximately 90 miles north of Sandoval, this bus station provides service to several major cities throughout the Midwest.
  • Amtrak Station (Centralia, IL): Located approximately 15 miles southeast of Sandoval, this train station offers service to cities throughout the country.

How to Get Around in Sandoval

While public transportation is limited in Sandoval, visitors can easily get around town by car or bike. Bike rentals are available at Raccoon Lake State Recreation Area and in nearby Salem.

Where to Go Next from Sandoval: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Salem: Just a short drive from Sandoval, this charming small town is home to historic landmarks, antique shops, and beautiful parks.
  • Carlyle Lake: Located approximately 25 miles east of Sandoval, this massive lake is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • St. Louis: Located approximately 70 miles southwest of Sandoval, this major city offers world-class attractions such as the Gateway Arch, the Missouri Botanical Garden, and the St. Louis Zoo.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a unique destination off the beaten path, Sandoval is the perfect choice. With its blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, this small town in Southern Illinois has something for everyone. From hiking and fishing to local festivals and tasty cuisine, Sandoval is a hidden gem waiting to be explored.
