Introduction to Northern Illinois

Located in the Midwest region of the United States, Northern Illinois is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This picturesque region is home to stunning natural attractions, culturally rich museums and historical sites, delicious food and drink, and friendly locals. Northern Illinois is the perfect destination for outdoorsy types, history buffs, and foodies alike.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Illinois

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs or those with an interest in culture, Northern Illinois has plenty of options. Here are some of the best museums and historical places to visit:

  1. The Chicago History Museum - Located in Chicago, this museum guides visitors through the city’s rich history from its early days to the present.
  2. The National Museum of the Great Lakes - Located in Toledo, this museum offers a comprehensive look into the history of the Great Lakes.
  3. Illinois Railway Museum - Located in Union, this museum is the largest railway museum in the United States. It boasts an extensive collection of trains and offers train rides throughout the year.
  4. The Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum - Located in Springfield, this museum offers a glimpse into the life and legacy of President Abraham Lincoln.

Natural Attractions of Northern Illinois: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Those who love nature will find plenty to explore in Northern Illinois. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions worth visiting:

  1. Starved Rock State Park - Located in Utica, Starved Rock offers stunning views of the Illinois River, hiking trails, and waterfalls.
  2. The Chicago Botanic Garden - Located in Glencoe, this garden offers 27 display gardens and four natural areas spanning over 385 acres.
  3. The Illinois and Michigan Canal State Trail - This hiking and biking trail spans 61.5 miles from Joliet to LaSalle, following the historic Illinois and Michigan Canal.
  4. The Anderson Japanese Gardens - Located in Rockford, this garden features 12 acres of traditional Japanese gardens and tea houses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Illinois

Northern Illinois offers plenty of hiking and biking trails that will take nature lovers through picturesque countryside and breathtaking settings. Here are some of the best options:

  1. The Fox River Trail - This trail follows the Fox River through picturesque towns and villages and offers spectacular scenery along the way. It spans 43 miles from Oswego to McHenry.
  2. The Illinois Prairie Path - This trail covers 62 miles from Elgin to Aurora, featuring a beautiful mix of scenic spots, forests, and fields.
  3. Mississippi Palisades State Park Trail - This beautiful trail follows the Mississippi River and offers scenic views of the river and its steep bluffs.

Best Things to Do in Northern Illinois: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural beauty, Northern Illinois has plenty of things to see and do. Here are some of the must-try activities and tours:

  1. Chicago Helicopter Experience - Take a bird’s-eye view of Chicago and the surrounding areas. The helicopter tour offers panoramic views of the city’s skyscrapers and the Lake Michigan shoreline.
  2. Go Kayaking on the Chicago River - There are many vendors that offer guided tours down the Chicago River from the north branch of the river. It’s a unique way to see the city’s famous architecture.
  3. Head to a Casino - The Hollywood Casino Aurora and the Grand Victoria Casino in Elgin are both worth visiting for those looking for an entertainment-filled night.
  4. Tour a Winery - Take an afternoon in Galena and visit one of the many wineries in the area. Tour the wine cellars, participate in tasting classes and learn about the production process of your favorite wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Illinois

Northern Illinois offers a vast array of options for a week-long trip. Here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Head to Chicago to get familiarized with the city. Visit the Willis Tower, Navy Pier, and the Chicago Riverwalk.
2 Take a day trip to Starved Rock State Park. Hike the trails and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
3 Visit the Illinois Railway Museum and explore the collection. Finish your day in Geneva, a delightful town located in the Fox River Valley.
4 Enjoy the RiverEdge Park in Aurora and relax with a concert by the river. With a delicious dinner at one of the restaurants in the city center.
5 Take a hot air balloon ride over Galena, followed by a visit to one of its many wineries and local shops.
6 Explore Anderson Japanese gardens in Rockford and take a leisurely hike through the gardens. Head to the nearby Nicholas Conservatory for more garden views.
7 Finish the week with a boat tour along the Chicago River and Lake Michigan. End your trip at the Art Institute of Chicago, one of the most renowned art museums in the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Illinois

Foodies will love the culinary scene in Northern Illinois. From local delicacies to international cuisine, there’s something to please every palate.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Deep-dish pizza - A staple of Chicago, this deep-dish pizza is made with a thick crust, heaps of cheese, and savory tomato sauce.
  2. Pork Tenderloin sandwiches - This classic sandwich is a popular midwestern favorite made with fried pork tenderloin, lettuce, tomatoes, and pickles.
  3. Chicago-style hot dog - This iconic hot dog comes with yellow mustard, relish, onions, pickles, tomato slices, and a sprinkle of celery salt on a steamed poppy seed bun.
  4. Italian Beef sandwich - This classic sandwich is made with thin slices of beef, served on a French roll, and dripped in flavorful gravy.

Local Beverages

  1. Goose Island Beer Company - This craft brewery is located in Chicago and is famous for its craft beers including Honkers Ale and 312 Urban Wheat Ale.
  2. Wine Country - Northern Illinois is home to some of the best wineries in the state. Visit them for their award-winning wines and a unique tasting experience.
  3. Coffee Shops - Northern Illinois has endless options for coffee lovers with hip local coffee shops like Aroma Cafe, Two Brother’s Coffeehouse, and Coffee Mania.

Where to Stay in Northern Illinois: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Illinois has a variety of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay in Northern Illinois:

  1. The Hotel Baker, St Charles - A historical hotel located on the Fox River in St. Charles with a beautiful garden and outstanding rooms.
  2. Galena Log Cabin Getaway & Adventure Property, Galena - A classic Northern Illinois experience, enjoy a log cabin in the woods for a serene escape from the city.
  3. Hilton Garden Inn, Rockford - A comfortable and affordable option located near tourist attractions like the Anderson Japanese Garden and the Nicholas Conservatory.

What to Buy in Northern Illinois: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Illinois has plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts for family and friends including:

  1. Artwork - From local museums to local art galleries, you will find inspiring pieces of art made by local Northern Illinois artists that are sure to impress.
  2. Wine - Northern Illinois is home to some of the best wineries in the state. Bring back a bottle of your favorite wine to share with family and friends.
  3. Craft Brews - If wine is not your thing, Northern Illinois has an abundance of local craft breweries that offer unique flavors of beer that are sure to impress even the avid beer connoisseur.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Illinois

Northern Illinois is a welcoming and friendly place, where people are proud of their surroundings and eager to share their local customs with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Northern Illinois

The best time to visit Northern Illinois depends on your preferences. Summers are warm and perfect for enjoying the region’s outdoor activities. Fall is perfect for enjoying the foliage, with a burst of colors that stretch across the countryside. Winter months offer the perfect time to take winter sports like skiing, snowboarding, and ice-skating. Finally, Spring is an ideal time to enjoy the stunning botanical gardens and the local festivals that take place during that time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Illinois

Do:

  • Do take advantage of Northern Illinois’ natural beauty by hiking, biking, or kayaking.
  • Do explore the local cuisine and unique flavors of the region.
  • Do pack your camera to capture the breathtaking landscapes.
  • Do plan ahead and book your accommodation and tours in advance.
  • Do check out the local farmers markets and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack a variety of weather-appropriate clothes for the season you are visiting.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural scenery or environment.
  • Don’t forget to tip your waitstaff and service providers.
  • Don’t forget to plan extra travel time for the potential traffic or road closures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Illinois

Northern Illinois is a safe and friendly destination for visitors. Visitors are encouraged to take necessary safety precautions like locking their doors when driving and keeping their personal belongings safe.

Health in Northern Illinois

Northern Illinois has a top-class healthcare system, with world-class hospitals and healthcare professionals. However, it’s always advisable to carry a travel insurance policy to ensure that you’re well protected against any health emergencies while traveling.

Getting to Northern Illinois: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Illinois

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport - The closest major airport to Northern Illinois and is located about 17 miles from the city center of Chicago.
  • General Mitchell International Airport - Located approximately 77 miles south of Chicago in Milwaukee, WI. It serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Northern Illinois

  • Megabus and Greyhound - The two primary bus providers that offer daily services to Chicago and other major Northern Illinois cities.

Other Options

  • Amtrak - Amtrak offers long-distance train services via the famous California Zephyr route, which connects Chicago to the west coast.
  • Driving: Northern Illinois is easily accessible by car via the various highways that connect it to the rest of Illinois and surrounding states.

How to Get Around in Northern Illinois

The best way to get around Northern Illinois is by renting a car, as the region is spread out, and public transportation can be limited. Visitors can also travel by taxis and ride-hailing services offered by companies such as Lyft and Uber.

Where to Go Next from Northern Illinois: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

With its strategic location in the Midwest, there are many fantastic destinations that visitors can easily reach. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Milwaukee, WI - Located just 93 miles north of Chicago, Milwaukee is famous for its breweries, trendy neighborhoods, and lakefront activities.
  • Wisconsin Dells, WI - Located approximately 163 miles northwest of Chicago, this water park capital of the Midwest offers families an adventurous experience.
  • Lake Geneva, WI - Just 68 miles from Chicago, this lake town boasts stunning views of Lake Geneva, an outdoor adventure playground, and a cozy downtown area with delightful shops and spas.
  • Starved Rock Lodge and Conference Center, Utica - Ideal for a weekend getaway, the Starved Rock Lodge covers 2,630 acres of beautiful forests and canyons with plenty of outdoor activities to choose from like rock climbing, horseback riding, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Northern Illinois is an unforgettable destination with plenty to offer. From its stunning natural attractions to its rich culture and culinary scene, this region is a must-visit for anyone traveling through the Midwest. Use this guide to help plan your next adventure and enjoy everything this beautiful and friendly region has to offer.
