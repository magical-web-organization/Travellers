Introduction to McHenry County

Located in Northern Illinois, McHenry County is a picturesque region that offers visitors a blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and thrilling outdoor activities. From hiking through sprawling parks to touring historic sites, there is no shortage of things to do in McHenry County. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in McHenry County

Here are some of the top attractions to visit in McHenry County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • McHenry County Historical Society Museum: Explore the history of McHenry County through exhibits and artifacts.

  • Colonel Palmer House: Built in 1858, this historic home offers tours and educational programs.

  • Old Courthouse Arts Center: This former courthouse has been transformed into a vibrant arts center, showcasing the work of local artists.

  • World’s Largest Corn Maze: Located in Harvard, this massive corn maze attracts visitors from around the world.

Natural Attractions of McHenry County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chain O’Lakes State Park: With miles of hiking trails, boating, fishing, and camping, this park is an outdoor enthusiast’s dream.

  • Moraine Hills State Park: This park offers scenic hiking trails, fishing, and boating on its picturesque lakes.

  • Volo Bog State Natural Area: This unique natural area features a floating boardwalk through a quaking bog, offering visitors a rare glimpse at a unique ecosystem.

  • Marengo Ridge Conservation Area: This conservation area boasts over 800 acres of oak-hickory forest, scenic prairies, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McHenry County

McHenry County is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with an abundance of trails and routes to explore. Here are some popular options:

  • Prairie Trail: This 26-mile trail runs through McHenry County and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • McHenry County Conservation District Trails: With 20 sites and over 30 miles of trails to explore, the McHenry County Conservation District is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Fox River Trail: This 32-mile scenic trail follows the Fox River and is perfect for biking or hiking.

Best Things to Do in McHenry County: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of fun things to do in McHenry County. Here are some must-try activities:

  • Zip Line Adventure: Fly through the treetops on this thrilling zip line adventure in Lake Geneva, just a short drive from McHenry County.

  • Brewery Tours: Visit local breweries like Crystal Lake Brewing and Scorched Earth Brewing for a taste of McHenry County’s thriving craft beer scene.

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the rolling hills of McHenry County on horseback with a guided trail ride.

  • Rock Climbing: Test your skills on the indoor climbing walls at The Forge: Lemont Quarries.

Week-Long Itinerary for McHenry County

Day 1: Visit McHenry County Historical Society Museum and walk around Crystal Lake.

Day 2: Explore Chain O’Lakes State Park by hiking and boating.

Day 3: Take a Zip Line Adventure in Lake Geneva and enjoy a tour of Crystal Lake Brewing.

Day 4: Tour the Colonel Palmer House and go horseback riding at Horse-Drawn Ventures.

Day 5: Hike at Moraine Hills State Park and visit Old Courthouse Arts Center.

Day 6: Go rock climbing at The Forge: Lemont Quarries and have dinner at Scorched Earth Brewing.

Day 7: Visit Volo Bog State Natural Area and finish with a tour of the World’s Largest Corn Maze in Harvard.

Food and Drink Scene of McHenry County

McHenry County’s food scene is as diverse as it is delicious. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef Hot Dogs: A Chicago classic, McHenry County offers plenty of great spots to grab a delicious beef hot dog.

  • Deep Dish Pizza: Take a short drive to Chicago and indulge in the famous deep-dish pizza.

  • Pierogis: Visit Ethereal Confections in Woodstock for some mouth-watering pierogis.

  • Bratwurst: Enjoy a delicious bratwurst at one of the many Oktoberfest events held throughout McHenry County.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: McHenry County boasts a thriving craft beer scene, with breweries like Crystal Lake Brewing and Scorched Earth Brewing.

  • Wine: Visit the award-winning Prairie State Winery for a taste of locally made wines.

Where to Stay in McHenry County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly options, McHenry County offers accommodation options for every traveler. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hampton Inn McHenry: A modern hotel located in the heart of McHenry County, with amenities like an indoor pool and fitness center.

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: Located in Crystal Lake, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and access to nearby attractions.

  • Pheasant Run Resort: A luxury resort in St. Charles, just a short drive from McHenry County, with amenities like golf courses, a spa, and multiple dining options.

What to Buy in McHenry County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or local product to bring home? Here are some options:

  • Woodstock Farmers Market: Visit this year-round farmers market for fresh produce and handmade goods from local artisans.

  • Ethereal Confections: Take home some delicious chocolate truffles and other sweets from this artisanal chocolate shop in Woodstock.

  • Duke’s Alehouse and Kitchen: Pick up a bottle of Duke’s BBQ sauce to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of McHenry County

McHenry County is a rich mix of cultures, with roots deeply embedded in its history. Visitors should keep in mind that it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit McHenry County

The best time to visit McHenry County is during the fall, with the leaves turning vibrant shades of red and orange. Spring and summer are also great times to visit, with an abundance of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of McHenry County

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities.

  • Visit local farmers markets and support local businesses.

  • Explore the local craft beer scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter in parks and natural areas.

  • Disrespect wildlife and natural habitats.

  • Ignore safety warnings when hiking or boating.

Safety and Health

Safety in McHenry County

McHenry County is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. Visitors should still take standard precautions like locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in McHenry County

McHenry County is home to several medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care centers. Visitors should take standard health precautions and seek medical attention if necessary.

How to Get Around in McHenry County

Visitors can explore McHenry County by car or bike, or take public transportation options like taxis or buses.

Where to Go Next from McHenry County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

McHenry County is situated near several popular destinations, including:

  • Chicago: Just a short drive or train ride away, visitors can explore the Windy City.

  • Lake Geneva: Take a scenic drive to Wisconsin and explore this charming lakeside town.

  • Starved Rock State Park: Located a few hours southwest of McHenry County, this stunning park boasts waterfalls, canyons, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

McHenry County is a hidden gem that offers visitors a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re exploring Chain O’Lakes State Park, touring historic sites like Colonel Palmer House, or indulging in the local cuisine, there is something for everyone in McHenry County.
