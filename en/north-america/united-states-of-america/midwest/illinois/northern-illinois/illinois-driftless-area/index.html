Introduction to Illinois Driftless Area

Illinois Driftless Area is a hidden gem located in Northern Illinois, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. This area is unique because it was untouched by glaciers during the Ice Age, which created a diverse landscape of rolling hills and valleys. Illinois Driftless Area is perfect for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers, offering a wide range of attractions and activities for all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Illinois Driftless Area

Here are some of the top places to visit in the Illinois Driftless Area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Galena Historic District: This historic district is a step back in time, with over 85 percent of the buildings listed in the National Register of Historic Places. You’ll find museums, art galleries, antique shops, and more.
  • Ulysses S. Grant Home State Historic Site: This historic site is the home of the 18th president of the United States, Ulysses S. Grant, and offers a glimpse into his life and the Civil War era.
  • Apple River Fort State Historic Site: This site is a replica of the fort that saw one of the battles of the Black Hawk War. You can explore the fort, learn about the history of the area, and witness reenactments.

Natural Attractions of Illinois Driftless Area: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mississippi Palisades State Park: This park is known for its scenic views of the Mississippi River, towering bluffs, and unique rock formations. You can hike, bike, camp, fish, and explore the trails in the area.
  • Nora Springs Nature Preserve: This preserve has over a hundred acres of pristine natural habitat, including forests, wetlands, and prairies. You can hike, bike, observe the wildlife, and relax in nature.
  • Upper Mississippi River National Wildlife and Fish Refuge: This refuge is the perfect place for birdwatchers, with over 275 species of birds. You can also fish, hunt, boat, hike, and bike in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Illinois Driftless Area

The Illinois Driftless Area boasts several hiking and biking trails that offer beautiful views of the rolling hills and valleys. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Great River Trail: This trail offers 60 miles of uninterrupted scenic views of the Mississippi River. You can hike, bike, or jog.
  • Horseshoe Mound Preserve: This preserve offers three miles of trails that wind through prairies and forests. You can also enjoy the scenic views of the city of Galena.
  • Apple River Canyon State Park: This park has over six miles of trails that lead you through towering bluffs, scenic canyons, and streams.

Best Things to Do in Illinois Driftless Area: Activities and Tours

  • Hot air balloon rides: You can enjoy the breathtaking views of the Illinois Driftless Area from a hot air balloon ride.
