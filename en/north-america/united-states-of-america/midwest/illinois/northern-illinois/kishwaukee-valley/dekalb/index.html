Introduction to DeKalb

DeKalb is a picturesque city located in the Kishwaukee Valley, Northern Illinois. As the proud home of Northern Illinois University, DeKalb has a vibrant cultural scene, rich history, and stunning natural attractions to explore. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to DeKalb.

List of Places to Visit in DeKalb

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ellwood House Museum: Step back in time and explore one of DeKalb’s most prominent landmarks. This elegant museum showcases the history of the Ellwood family, as well as the DeKalb region.
  • NIU Art Museum: One of the most exciting attractions in DeKalb, the NIU Art Museum features permanent and rotating exhibits of contemporary art.
  • Egyptian Theatre: This restored movie palace and performing arts venue, which opened in 1929, is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Natural Attractions of DeKalb: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prairie Park Nature Center: Escape the hustle and bustle of the city and explore this tranquil nature reserve. The park features a variety of trails, including several that are wheelchair accessible.
  • DeKalb Farmers’ Market: A bustling marketplace filled with local vendors and craftspeople, the DeKalb Farmers’ Market is the perfect place to enjoy lively music, fresh produce, and delicious snacks.
  • DeKalb County Forest Preserve: This large forest preserve features miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as picnic areas, fishing spots, and educational exhibits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of DeKalb

  • Great Western Trail: This beautiful trail stretches for over 17 miles and passes through several different ecosystems, including woodlands, grasslands, and wetlands.
  • DeKalb Nature Trail: This trail, located in the Prairie Park Nature Center, is a popular destination for hikers and birdwatchers.

Best Things to Do in DeKalb: Activities and Tours

  • NIU Convocation Center: Catch a game or concert at this impressive venue, which can seat up to 10,000 spectators.
  • DeKalb Corn Fest: This beloved annual festival celebrates the region’s agricultural heritage with live music, games, and plenty of corn-related treats.
  • DeKalb County Barn Tour: Explore DeKalb’s charming agricultural landscape by visiting some of the area’s most historic and beautifully preserved barns.

Food and Drink Scene of DeKalb

Must-Try Dishes

  • Corn on the Cob: This delicious and iconic regional dish is a must-try for anyone visiting DeKalb.
  • Chicago-style Pizza: Whether you’re a deep-dish purist or a lover of thin-crust pizza, DeKalb has plenty of fantastic pizza restaurants to try.
  • Craft Beer: DeKalb is home to a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries and taprooms in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Lemonade: Sip on a refreshing glass of homemade lemonade at the DeKalb Farmers’ Market.
  • Coffee: DeKalb is home to several cozy coffee shops, perfect for sipping espresso and people-watching.
  • Wine: Take a tour of one of the area’s vineyards or wineries and sample some delicious local wines.

Where to Stay in DeKalb: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Red Roof Inn: This centrally-located hotel offers affordable rates and comfortable rooms.
  • Travelodge by Wyndham DeKalb: A budget-friendly option with a prime location near many of DeKalb’s top attractions.
  • Jane Fargo Hotel: This historic hotel, which dates back to the late 1800s, offers unparalleled charm and luxury.

What to Buy in DeKalb: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • DeKalb Corn Festival Memorabilia: Take home a souvenir from DeKalb’s famous Corn Festival.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local jewelry makers offer unique handmade pieces that make great gifts.
  • Regional Foods: Stock up on local treats like homemade jams, jellies, and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of DeKalb

DeKalb is known for its friendly and welcoming residents, as well as its charming Midwestern ambiance. Visitors should expect a slower pace of life and lots of countryside charm.

Best Time to Visit DeKalb

The best time to visit DeKalb is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor events and activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of DeKalb

Do:

  • Do explore DeKalb’s beautiful natural attractions.
  • Do try the local cuisine and sample some of the region’s famous corn dishes.
  • Do take part in local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the area’s outdoor attractions.
  • Don’t miss the opportunity to visit one of DeKalb’s many charming small towns.
  • Don’t underestimate the area’s rich cultural scene.

Safety and Health

Safety in DeKalb

DeKalb is a safe and welcoming community with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still exercise common sense and take basic safety precautions.

Health in DeKalb

DeKalb is home to several excellent hospitals and healthcare providers, making it easy to get quality medical care if needed.

Getting to DeKalb: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near DeKalb

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport: Located approximately 67 miles southeast of DeKalb.
  • Chicago Midway International Airport: Located approximately 57 miles east of DeKalb.

Bus Stations Near DeKalb

  • DeKalb Transit Center: Located in the heart of downtown DeKalb, this bus station offers connections to major cities throughout the region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the area’s natural attractions.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in DeKalb.

How to Get Around in DeKalb

DeKalb is a compact and walkable city, making it easy to explore on foot. Visitors can also take advantage of the local bus system or rent a bike to explore the region’s many bike trails.

Where to Go Next from DeKalb: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Starved Rock State Park: Located approximately 90 miles southwest of DeKalb, this stunning state park features incredible hiking trails, towering waterfalls, and breathtaking views.
  • Galena: This charming small town, located approximately 107 miles northwest of DeKalb, is known for its beautiful historic district, charming shops, and excellent restaurants.
  • Lake Geneva: Located approximately 70 miles north of DeKalb, this picturesque resort town is the perfect destination for a relaxing weekend getaway.

Conclusion

With its rich history, beautiful natural attractions, and friendly locals, DeKalb is the perfect destination for anyone seeking a taste of charming Midwestern hospitality.
