Introduction to LaSalle-Peru

Located in Northern Illinois, LaSalle-Peru is a city in LaSalle County known for its scenic beauty and rich history. This city attracts visitors from all over the world with its abundance of natural wonders, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, LaSalle-Peru has something for everyone. This comprehensive tour guide will take you on a journey through the city’s many attractions, highlighting the best places to visit, eat, sleep, and play.

List of Places to Visit in LaSalle-Peru

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

LaSalle-Peru is filled with museums and historical sites that allow visitors to explore the rich history and culture of the city. One of the most popular is the Hegeler Carus Mansion, a National Historic Landmark known for its unique architecture and beautiful gardens. The LaSalle County Historical Society has a number of notable exhibits and artifacts on display, including local archaeological finds and Civil War memorabilia. The Illinois Valley Area Chamber of Commerce and Tourism has a Visitor’s Center that is a must-see for those visiting the area for the first time.

Natural Attractions of LaSalle-Peru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

LaSalle-Peru is home to a number of natural wonders and outdoor attractions that make it a popular destination for nature lovers. Starved Rock State Park is the crown jewel of the area, featuring over 13 miles of hiking trails, canyons, waterfalls, and scenic views of the Illinois River. For those looking for a more challenging hike, Matthiessen State Park offers over 5 miles of trails through scenic sandstone outcroppings and a unique talus formation. Buffalo Rock State Park and Waterfall Glen offer opportunities for birdwatching, fishing, and picnicking. The I&M Canal Trail is a popular destination for biking enthusiasts, offering over 61 miles of scenic trail through Northern Illinois.

Hiking/Biking Routes of LaSalle-Peru

As mentioned earlier, LaSalle-Peru and the surrounding areas are home to many great hiking and biking routes. In addition to the previously mentioned Starved Rock and Matthiessen State Parks, the Hennepin Canal Parkway State Park is a popular destination for cyclists looking to enjoy over 100 miles of scenic trails. The I&M Canal Trail is also a great opportunity for biking enthusiasts, with over 61 miles of trail that embrace the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in LaSalle-Peru: Activities and Tours

Beyond the hiking and biking trails, there’s a plethora of other activities and tours to choose from in LaSalle-Peru. The city’s outdoor adventures include kayaking and fishing, as well as birdwatching and nature photography. There are a number of local wineries and microbreweries that are also worth a visit, such as the August Hill Winery and Tangled Roots Brewing Company. Visitors can embark on a culinary tour of the city, sampling local specialties such as fried chicken, sausage, and a variety of ethnic cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for LaSalle-Peru

For visitors wishing to explore the city more fully, spending a week in LaSalle-Peru is highly recommended. There is much to see and do in this city, and one week is sufficient time to take it all in. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Hegeler Carus Mansion and explore the beautiful gardens; take a leisurely hike through Buffalo Rock State Park.

Day 2: Take a tour of the LaSalle County Historical Society and experience the rich history of the area; explore the stunning Matthiessen State Park.

Day 3: Visit the Illinois Valley Area Chamber of Commerce and Tourism Visitor’s Center and learn about the area’s many attractions; take a scenic drive along the Illinois River.

Day 4: Enjoy a winery tour at the August Hill Winery and while in downtown LaSalle stop and enjoy a pastry from Lock 16 Cafe; go kayaking on the Illinois River.

Day 5: Explore Starved Rock State Park and take in the natural beauty of the area; in the evening, enjoy a delicious dinner at one of the area’s top-rated restaurants.

Day 6: Spend the day kayaking and fishing on the Illinois River; take a relaxing nature walk through Waterfall Glen.

Day 7: Take a scenic bike ride along the Hennepin Canal Parkway State Park; enjoy a farewell lunch at one of LaSalle-Peru’s top-rated restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of LaSalle-Peru

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to great food, LaSalle-Peru does not disappoint. A visit to this city is not complete without trying some of the area’s signature dishes. One of the must-try dishes in LaSalle-Peru is fried chicken. Another local specialty is the Italian Beef sandwich, a tasty combination of beef, giardiniera, and melted provolone. Visitors looking for a unique culinary experience should also try the area’s famous sausage, which is known for its flavor and quality.

Local Beverages

In addition to its great food, LaSalle-Peru also has a vibrant beverage scene, with several microbreweries and wineries in the area. Tangled Roots Brewing Company is a must-visit for beer lovers, serving up a variety of hand-crafted beers made with locally sourced ingredients. The August Hill Winery is another popular destination, offering wine tastings and tours of the winery and vineyard.

Where to Stay in LaSalle-Peru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

LaSalle-Peru has a variety of lodging options to choose from, whether you are looking for a bed and breakfast, hotel, or campground. Some popular hotels in the area include the Hampton Inn & Suites Peru, the Holiday Inn Express & Suites Peru, and the Quality Inn. For those on a budget, there are several budget-friendly options such as Super 8 by Wyndham Peru and Motel 6.

What to Buy in LaSalle-Peru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, LaSalle-Peru has plenty to offer. Some popular items include local artisanal cheeses, locally made maple syrup, and handcrafted pottery. Other popular souvenirs include t-shirts, magnets, and other memorabilia from the area’s historical sites.

Local Culture and Customs of LaSalle-Peru

LaSalle-Peru is a welcoming city with a rich history and vibrant culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and try to immerse themselves in the local culture. The friendly locals are always willing to share their knowledge and experience when it comes to the area’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit LaSalle-Peru

The best time to visit LaSalle-Peru is during the spring and summer months when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and other outdoor adventures in the area’s many parks and trails. The fall is also a great time to visit, with the changing colors of the trees making for a beautiful backdrop. The winter months are colder but can still be enjoyable for those who love winter sports such as skiing and snowmobiling.

Safety and Health

Safety in LaSalle-Peru

LaSalle-Peru is a generally safe city, with low levels of crime and few safety concerns for visitors. Visitors should still exercise caution while hiking and exploring outdoor areas, as accidents can happen.

Health in LaSalle-Peru

LaSalle-Peru has a variety of healthcare facilities in the area, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should always have travel insurance and carry any necessary medications or medical supplies with them.

Getting to LaSalle-Peru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near LaSalle-Peru

The Illinois Valley Regional Airport is a convenient option for those flying into LaSalle-Peru from other parts of the country. The airport is located just a few miles from the city center and offers a variety of flights to major cities across the United States.

Bus Stations Near LaSalle-Peru

Greyhound and other bus companies offer routes that go through LaSalle-Peru, making it a convenient option for visitors traveling by bus.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to LaSalle-Peru from Chicago in about two hours, making it a popular destination for weekend getaways or day trips. Car rentals are available at both the Illinois Valley Regional Airport and in the city center.

How to Get Around in LaSalle-Peru

Like many cities in Illinois, LaSalle-Peru is a car-friendly city with plenty of parking options. Visitors can also choose to rent a car, bike, or scooter from local rental companies.

Where to Go Next from LaSalle-Peru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

LaSalle-Peru is located near several other popular destinations, including Springfield, IL, and Bloomington, IL. Visitors can also take a day trip to nearby Starved Rock State Park or Matthiessen State Park. Those looking for a longer journey can plan a road trip to other nearby cities such as Peoria or Champaign.

Conclusion

LaSalle-Peru is a wonderful destination for those seeking a mix of natural beauty and rich history. With its many museums and cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and great food and drink scene, this city truly has something for everyone. Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, a trip to LaSalle-Peru is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
