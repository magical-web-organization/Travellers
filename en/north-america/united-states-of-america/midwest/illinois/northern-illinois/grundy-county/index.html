Introduction to Grundy County

Located in the northern part of Illinois, Grundy County is known for its rich history and natural beauty. While the county is home to fewer than 50,000 residents, it’s a hub of activity and tourism. Visitors can come here to explore stunning natural attractions, learn about American history and culture, and enjoy diverse activities and experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Grundy County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Grundy County has several museums and historical places that tell stories of the region’s past and culture. These include:

  1. The Grundy County Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the county’s history and culture. Visitors can explore exhibits and interactive displays that highlight the county’s agricultural, industrial and social fabric.

  2. The Chaney Mansion: Built in 1904, this beautiful mansion has stories steeped in the county’s history. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the mansion’s architecture, historical significance and interesting facts about its residents and guests.

  3. The Gaylord Building: This 1830s building used to be a warehouse and is now a national landmark. It has served several purposes over the years, including as a bank, post office, and retail space. Visitors can explore exhibits and events that showcase the building’s history and significance.

Natural Attractions of Grundy County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grundy County is also home to several natural attractions that offer a variety of outdoor activities for visitors. Here are a few of them:

  1. Gebhard Woods State Park: This park, with its stunning woods, prairies and streams, is a must-visit for nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, camping, and picnicking, among other activities.

  2. Heidecke Lake State Fish and Wildlife Area: This area is popular for fishing, especially for walleye, largemouth bass, and channel catfish. Visitors can also go hiking, horseback riding, and bird watching here.

  3. William G. Stratton State Park: This park offers visitors a glimpse into the county’s industrial and mining past. It has several preserved mine structures that tell the story of coal mining in Grundy County, as well as providing opportunities for hiking, picnicking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grundy County

Grundy County has excellent hiking trails, bike paths and scenic drives that offer a chance to explore its natural beauty. Here are some of the notable options:

  1. The I&M Canal State Trail: This historic trail runs for about 60 miles and follows the route of the Illinois & Michigan Canal. Along the way, visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, wildlife spotting, and enjoying several historic sites.

  2. The Mazonia-Braidwood Recreation Area Trail: This trail is popular for hiking and biking and offers a chance to explore scenic views and diverse habitats. Visitors can see birds, mammals, and amphibians, among other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Grundy County: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the county’s natural and cultural attractions, Grundy County offers several exciting activities and tours. Here are a few recommended options:

  1. Bicentennial Park Splash Pad: This park is perfect for families with children. It has a splash pad, playground, and other amenities that provide hours of entertainment for kids.

  2. Kayak Morris: This outdoor adventure company offers guided kayak tours and rentals. Visitors can explore the Illinois River, Mazon River, and other waterways while spotting birds, fish, and other wildlife.

  3. Tangled Roots Brewing Company: This brewery is known for its quality beer, food, and live music events. Visitors can take tours of the brewery and sample their craft beer selections.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grundy County

Grundy County’s natural landscapes, cultural attractions, and diverse activities make it a perfect destination for a week-long trip. Here’s an itinerary that can help visitors plan their trip:

Day 1-2: Explore the area’s industrial past with visits to the Gaylord Building, the Grundy County Historical Society Museum and the William G. Stratton State Park.

Day 3-4: Enjoy outdoor activities at the Gebhard Woods State Park, Heidecke Lake State Fish and Wildlife Area, and the I&M Canal State Trail.

Day 5-6: Take tours of the Chaney Mansion and other historical sites, followed by a visit to the Bicentennial Park Splash Pad or Kayak Morris.

Day 7: Visit Tangled Roots Brewing Company and other local eateries to indulge in local cuisine and beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Grundy County

Must-Try Dishes

Grundy County offers a range of delicious and unique dishes that both locals and visitors can enjoy. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  1. German food - Grundy County has a significant German heritage, which can be seen in its food. Some popular German dishes in the area include bratwurst, spätzle, and sauerbraten.

  2. Sweet corn - The county is known for its sweet corn production. Visitors can find several restaurants and food stands selling corn on the cob, sweet corn soup, and other corn dishes.

  3. Barbecue - Several local eateries serve barbecue ribs, pulled pork, and other meats, using traditional smoking methods and homemade sauces.

Local Beverages

Grundy County has a vibrant beverage scene, with several locally brewed beers and wines. Here are a few recommended beverages to try:

  1. Tangled Roots Brewing Company’s beer selection: This brewery offers a range of craft beers using locally sourced ingredients.

  2. Illinois Sparkling Company’s champagne and wine selection: Known for its world-class sparkling wines, this winery also offers still wine and beer.

Where to Stay in Grundy County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grundy County has an array of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Visitors can find several hotels, motels, bed and breakfasts, guesthouses, and campgrounds in the area. Here are some recommended options:

  1. The Morris Motel: This family-owned motel offers clean rooms and friendly service at reasonable prices.

  2. The River House Bed & Breakfast: This historic guesthouse offers scenic views of the Illinois River and provides guests with a luxury experience, complete with gourmet breakfasts.

What to Buy in Grundy County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Grundy County offers visitors several locally made products and souvenirs that make perfect gifts or keepsakes. Here are some recommended items to check out:

  1. Sweet corn - Fresh sweet corn from the area’s farms makes for a tasty souvenir.

  2. Grundy County-themed apparel - Visitors can show their love for the county by purchasing locally made shirts and other gear.

  3. Local crafts - Visit the Grundy County Arts Directory to find locally made pottery, jewelry, and other crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Grundy County

Grundy County has a friendly and hospitable culture, with a strong focus on community and preserving its heritage. Visitors should respect the area’s customs and etiquette, which may vary based on the location or occasion.

Best Time to Visit Grundy County

The best time to visit Grundy County is from May to September when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy the wildflowers, fresh produce, and harvest festivals during this time.

Safety and Health

Grundy County is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions when traveling to unfamiliar or remote areas.

Grundy County has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, where visitors can receive medical attention if needed. Visitors should bring any necessary prescriptions with them and take standard health precautions, like staying hydrated and using sunscreen.

Getting to Grundy County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grundy County

The Midway International Airport and the O’Hare International Airport in Chicago are the closest major airports to Grundy County, with about a 1-1.5 hour’s drive depending on traffic.

Bus Stations Near Grundy County

Greyhound bus station and Megabus bus station are two of the closest bus stations in nearby towns, with bus services connecting to other major cities within Illinois and neighboring states.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxi services are readily available in the area as a convenient option for transportation.

How to Get Around in Grundy County

Visitors can get around Grundy County via car, bike, or by foot. Rental cars, bike rentals, and taxi services are available options for getting around.
