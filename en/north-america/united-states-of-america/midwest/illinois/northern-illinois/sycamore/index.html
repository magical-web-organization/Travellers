Introduction to Sycamore

Sycamore, situated in Northern Illinois, is a charming city that offers a unique combination of scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural richness. This vibrant city is known as the County Seat of DeKalb County and has a rich history and culture. The city is surrounded by multiple parks, golf courses, and scenic trails that offer visitors the opportunity to explore the nature and marvel at the amazing views. There are several cultural attractions, shopping centers, and restaurants that provide visitors with a genuine feel of the local culture.

This guide will assist you in exploring the best of Sycamore, including its must-visit attractions, places to eat and shop, where to stay and what to do, along with safety tips and mode of transportation.

List of Places to Visit in Sycamore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sycamore History Museum: This museum is an excellent place to explore the history of Sycamore. It showcases the city’s historical artifacts, paintings, and photographs, and engages visitors with interactive tours.

  • Ellwood House Museum: This is a 19th-century mansion with a rich history. The mansion has original furnishings and provides insight into Victorian-era life. The tours provide a glimpse into the lives of the family that once lived there.

  • NIU Art Museum: Located on the Northern Illinois University campus, this museum showcases contemporary and modern art. It features exhibitions of paintings, sculptures, photographs, and multimedia installations.

Natural Attractions of Sycamore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prairie Pathways: This trail runs through multiple parks in Sycamore and is perfect for hiking, jogging or cycling. The scenic trail is a great place to enjoy the autumn leaves or winter snowfall.

  • Sycamore Park District: The Sycamore Park District offers visitors access to multiple parks and recreation centers. Visitors can enjoy activities such as swimming, hiking, playing sports, and relaxing in the park.

  • Diamond Lake: Located near the town of Rochelle, this lake offers visitors a variety of outdoor activities such as fishing, boating, and swimming. The lake is surrounded by beautiful landscape, making it a perfect spot for a family picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sycamore

  • Great Western Trail: Stretching over 17 miles, this trail runs from Sycamore to St. Charles. Visitors can enjoy the breathtaking views and wildlife while hiking or cycling the path.

  • Sinnissippi Loop Trail: A 2-mile trail that runs along the Kishwaukee River, the trail offers visitors the opportunity to explore lush green forests and enjoy the river’s refreshing breeze.

Best Things to Do in Sycamore: Activities and Tours

  • Escape Room: If you are up for an adventure, the Escape Room at Sycamore is perfect for you. Visitors get to solve puzzles and clues to unlock the door and escape within the allotted time.

  • Thunder Ridge Vineyards: Visitors can enjoy a glass of wine while relaxing in the serene setting of the vineyard. The vineyard also offers tours and wine-tasting sessions for visitors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sycamore

Day Activities
1 Visit the Ellwood House Museum, take a walk in the Prairie Pathways and have lunch at Taxco Restaurant.
2 Hike along the Great Western Trail, enjoy lunch at the Sweet Dream Desserts, and finish the day with a wine tasting at The Forge Vineyards.
3 Explore the Sycamore History Museum, have lunch at the Blumen Garden Bistro and try out the escape room.
4 Go shopping at Stomp Shoes and Boutique, have lunch at Nat’s on Maple and end the day with a walk in the Sycamore Park District.
5 Spend the day at Lake Shabbona State Park doing water activities such as swimming, fishing, and boating.
6 Explore the NIU Art Museum in DeKalb, have lunch at the O’Leary’s Irish Pub, and hike the Sinnissippi Loop Trail.
7 Have breakfast at The Sweet Dream Desserts and depart to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Sycamore

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled Portabella and Spinach Quesadilla: This delicious dish is served at Nat’s on Maple and is filled with mushrooms, spinach and melted cheese.

  • Chicken Enchiladas: Taxco Restaurant offers delicious enchiladas filled with seasoned chicken and homemade salsa.

  • Burgers with Special Sauce: PJ’s Courthouse Tavern and grille offers a variety of burgers with their special sauce that’s to die for.

Local Beverages

  • Thunder Ridge Vineyard Wines: The vineyard produces a range of red, white, and sweet wines made for every visitor’s taste buds.

  • Craft Beers: Sycamore has several breweries, such as Taxco and Forge Brewhouse that offer craft beers.

Where to Stay in Sycamore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Country Inn and Suites by Radisson: This hotel offers visitors all the comforts of home, including luxurious rooms with free Wi-Fi and Netflix. It’s located conveniently, close to shopping centers and parks and is perfect for a relaxing stay.

  • Prairie View Bed and Breakfast: It’s a cozy and serene bed and breakfast featuring a beautiful garden, comfortable rooms with fireplaces and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Sycamore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sycamore History Museum’s Store: It offers visitors a wide range of products, including books, clothing, paintings, and other artifacts featuring Sycamore’s history.

  • Ollie’s Frozen Custard: This is a local family-owned business that offers visitors a delicious frozen custard available in various flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Sycamore

The locals of Sycamore are very welcoming and pride themselves on their history and culture. The city is known for its Victorian architecture, and it’s always a good idea to dress appropriately.

Best Time to Visit Sycamore

The best time to visit Sycamore is from June through September when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sycamore

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Explore the historic sites, museums, and parks

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter the parks and other public places.

  • Ignore traffic laws.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing to historical sites and events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sycamore

The city is relatively safe, but it’s always a good idea to take appropriate precautions, such as locking your car and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Sycamore

The city’s healthcare facilities are much larger in the surrounding regions. There are three nearby hospitals: Northwestern Medicine Kishwaukee Hospital, Mercyhealth, and AMITA Health.

Getting to Sycamore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sycamore

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport: The nearest airport to Sycamore is Chicago O’Hare International Airport, which is located about 60 miles east of Sycamore.

  • Chicago Midway International Airport: This airport is situated about 65 miles southeast of Sycamore.

Bus Stations Near Sycamore

  • Greyhound Bus Station: It’s located in downtown DeKalb, only 6 miles west of Sycamore.

Other Options

The city is small and easy to navigate, and public transportation is limited to taxi services.

How to Get Around in Sycamore

Sycamore is a small city, and most areas can be explored on foot. However, renting a car or taking a taxi is an option for traveling further distances.

Where to Go Next from Sycamore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rockford: A city with a vibrant downtown filled with restaurants, breweries, and cultural attractions, located 30 miles northwest of Sycamore.

  • Chicago: The city offers visitors world-class museums, restaurants, and is located approximately 60 miles from Sycamore.

  • Starved Rock State Park: Known for its hiking and skiing trails and water activities like fishing, swimming, and boating, the park is located about 100 miles from Sycamore.

Conclusion

Sycamore is a wonderful city to visit for its diverse culture, rich history, and outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy soothing countryside landscapes, unique dining experiences, and explore historical places.
