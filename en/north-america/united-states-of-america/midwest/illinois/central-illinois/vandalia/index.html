Introduction to Vandalia

Vandalia is a charming city located in Central Illinois, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. Established in 1819, Vandalia served as the state’s capital from 1819 to 1839. Today, the city is famous for its rich history, scenic natural surroundings, and active outdoor way of life.

Whether you’re interested in historical landmarks, outdoor activities, or indulging in some delicious local cuisine, there is plenty to keep you occupied in Vandalia.

This comprehensive Vandalia travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to this fascinating city. From the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and eat, and more, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Vandalia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Vandalia State House State Historic Site

The Vandalia State House State Historic Site is a must-visit attraction in Vandalia. Built-in 1836, this iconic building was the State Capitol for ten years. Today, visitors can explore its museum exhibits and enjoy a guided tour to learn about Illinois’s early history.

Fayette County Museum

The Fayette County Museum is another cultural gem in Vandalia. It features a vast collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the area’s history. The museum also hosts special events, such as art exhibits, concerts, and more.

Natural Attractions of Vandalia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kaskaskia River

The Kaskaskia River is an excellent spot for kayaking, canoeing, and fishing. The river stretches over 325 miles and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding natural landscapes. Visitors can also enjoy hiking trails and picnicking areas at Riverfront Park.

Carlyle Lake

Carlyle Lake is another popular outdoor destination in Vandalia. It’s the largest man-made lake in Illinois, stretching over 25,000 acres. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and water sports, or hike and bike trails around the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vandalia

National Road Interpretive Center Trail

The National Road Interpretive Center Trail is a 12-mile-long paved trail that follows the path of the historic National Road. The trail offers scenic views of rural landscapes, small towns, and historic landmarks.

Vandalia Railroad Trail

The Vandalia Railroad Trail is another popular route for hikers and bikers. It’s a 7.5-mile-long trail that follows the former Mooseheart Railroad. Visitors can enjoy beautiful views of the Kaskaskia River and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Vandalia: Activities and Tours

Vandalia Ghost Tours

Vandalia Ghost Tours is a unique activity that takes visitors on a spooky adventure through the city’s most haunted locations. A knowledgeable guide will share fascinating stories and legends while you explore some of Vandalia’s oldest and creepiest sites.

Vandalia Lake

Vandalia Lake is a beautiful spot to relax and enjoy a day out by the water. Visitors can swim, fish, and picnic in the park. The lake also offers hiking and biking trails, playgrounds, and camping areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Vandalia

Must-Try Dishes

Pork Tenderloin Sandwich

The pork tenderloin sandwich is a local favorite in Vandalia. It’s a breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet served on a bun with mayo, lettuce, and tomato.

Sweet Corn

Sweet corn is a staple in Midwest cuisine, and Vandalia is no exception. The area’s rich soil and climate conditions produce some of the best sweet corn in the country.

Local Beverages

Wine

The Midwest region is famous for its wineries, and Vandalia has a few excellent options. Visit the Walker’s Bluff Vineyard and Winery or Hidden Lake Winery & Banquet Center to sample some award-winning wines.

Craft Beer

If you’re a beer lover, you won’t want to miss the craft beer scene in Vandalia. Check out St. Nicholas Brewing Co. or Big Daddy’s Brew Works for a unique selection of local brews.

What to Buy in Vandalia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Crafts and Artwork

Many local artists in Vandalia sell unique and handmade crafts, such as pottery, textiles, and jewelry. Visit the Artworks Gallery & Gifts or the Fayette County Farmers Market to find one-of-a-kind souvenirs.

Sweet Corn

Don’t leave Vandalia without taking home some sweet corn. Visitors can buy fresh sweet corn at local farmers markets or at restaurants and grocery stores around town.

Local Culture and Customs of Vandalia

Vandalia has a rich history and culture that visitors can learn about and appreciate. The area is known for its agriculture, with corn and soybeans being major crops. The people of Vandalia are welcoming and friendly, and visitors are encouraged to embrace their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Vandalia

The best time to visit Vandalia is from May to September. The weather is mild, and many outdoor activities are available during this time. The fall months are also a popular time to visit, as the area’s trees change color, providing a stunning backdrop.

  • Take time to appreciate the local history and culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Vandalia

Vandalia is a safe city with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking valuables in cars and being aware of surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Vandalia

Visitors to Vandalia should ensure they have travel insurance and that they are up-to-date on their routine vaccinations. Medical facilities are available in the city, and emergency services can be reached by calling 911.

Getting to Vandalia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vandalia

The closest major airport to Vandalia is Lambert-St. Louis International Airport. It’s about an hour and a half drive from Vandalia and offers flights to major cities across the United States.

Bus Stations Near Vandalia

Greyhound Buses offer services to St. Louis, Missouri, which is a one-hour drive from Vandalia. The Illinois Business Route 51 passes through the city, providing visitors with convenient access to the area.

Other Options

Visitors to Vandalia can also travel by car, as the city is easily accessible from major highways, including Interstate 70, US Route 40, and Illinois Route 185.

How to Get Around in Vandalia

Vandalia is a relatively small city, and visitors can easily get around by car, bike, or on foot. Consider renting a car if you’re flying into nearby St. Louis or taking a bus to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Vandalia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gateway Arch, St. Louis

Visitors can take a day trip to St. Louis to see the iconic Gateway Arch, explore the city’s museums and attractions, or enjoy its vibrant nightlife and dining scene.

Shawnee National Forest

Shawnee National Forest is a nature lover’s paradise and a great destination for those looking to get away from the city. It offers rugged hiking trails, scenic drives, and breathtaking views of rock formations and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Vandalia is a hidden gem in the Midwest, offering a unique blend of history, outdoor adventures, and hospitality. From exploring fascinating museums and hiking picturesque trails, to indulging in some of the area’s delicious local cuisine and wine, there’s something for everyone in Vandalia. Plan your trip today and discover why this charming city is one of Illinois’s best-kept secrets.
