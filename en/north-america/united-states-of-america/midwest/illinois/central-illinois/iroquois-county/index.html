Introduction to Iroquois County

Located in Central Illinois, Iroquois County is a beautiful and serene destination that attracts tourists from various parts of the world. The county covers an area of 1,118 square miles and has a population of approximately 30,000 people. The county is named after the Native American tribe that once lived in the area. Iroquois County boasts an array of cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures that cater to all types of travelers, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a unique and fulfilling vacation experience.

List of Places to Visit in Iroquois County

Iroquois County is home to numerous cultural attractions and natural wonders that provides an unforgettable experience for visitors. Below are some of the top places to visit in the county.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iroquois County Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the history and culture of Iroquois County. Exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and other historical memorabilia.
  • Vermilion County Museum: Located in Danville, this museum is home to a vast collection of art, artifacts, and history of the region.
  • Kankakee County Historical Society Museum: This museum features an incredible collection of artifacts from the area’s native peoples and pioneer settlers, as well as a wide range of exhibits on the area’s industrial heritage.

Natural Attractions of Iroquois County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Birding Trail: Iroquois County is home to many birds, and the county’s birding trail is a popular attraction for birdwatchers. The Birding Trail runs along the Iroquois River and includes several different habitats.
  • Forest Glen Preserve: This 1,800-acre nature preserve includes more than 25 miles of hiking trails, a nature center, a bird sanctuary, and picnic shelters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iroquois County

Iroquois County’s scenic landscapes offer various hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Below are some of the top routes to consider.

  • Iroquois County River Trail: This trail follows the Iroquois River and is ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Kankakee River State Park: This park offers various hiking trails, including the River Trail, which follows the banks of the Kankakee River.
  • Forest Glen Nature Trail: This trail is a popular hiking route that takes visitors through the forest and meadows of the Forest Glen Preserve.

Best Things to Do in Iroquois County: Activities and Tours

Iroquois County offers numerous activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. Some of the best things to do in the county include:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: The Iroquois River and Kankakee River offer ideal locations for kayaking and canoeing.
  • Hunting and Fishing: Iroquois County is known for its hunting and fishing opportunities, which include pheasant, quail, deer, and various fish species.
  • Golfing: The county has several golf courses, including Shewami Country Club and Oak Springs Golf Course.

Week-Long Itinerary for Iroquois County

A week-long trip to Iroquois County provides an excellent opportunity for tourists to explore the destination fully. Below is an ideal itinerary for a week-long trip to the county:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Iroquois County Historical Society Museum, and get acquainted with the history of the region.
2 Explore the Forest Glen Preserve and take in the breathtaking natural beauty of the area.
3 Go kayaking or canoeing on the Iroquois River or Kankakee River.
4 Head out to the Kankakee River State Park and use the day to hike the River Trail.
5 Take a guided tour of the local wineries, including the Castle Finn Winery and the Mackinaw Winery.
6 Explore the Birding Trail and get an up-close look at the area’s diverse bird species.
7 Spend your final day exploring the local food scene and trying out some of the region’s renowned delicacies, including Muddy Waters BBQ and Dixie Kitchen & Bait Shop.

Food and Drink Scene of Iroquois County

Iroquois County’s diverse culinary scene features numerous restaurants, cafes, and bars that offer delicious local delicacies. Below are some of the must-try dishes and beverages when visiting the county:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iroquois Chili: This recipe has been passed down through the generations and features slow-cooked chili with ground beef, beans, and a range of spices.
  • Kankakee Fried Chicken: This fried chicken recipe has been a favorite of Iroquois County residents for decades, owing to its unique blend of herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Illinois Wine: The local wineries in Iroquois County offer visitors a wide range of wine options to choose from, including the Castle Finn Winery and the Mackinaw Winery.
  • Bourbon Old Fashioned: A classic cocktail made from bourbon, bitters, sugar, and a splash of soda, it is a must-try beverage in Iroquois County.

Where to Stay in Iroquois County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Iroquois County offers many accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Below are some of the best places to consider when looking for a place to stay in the county:

  • Harvest Guest House: Nestled in the heart of the countryside, this guesthouse provides a peaceful retreat with a range of amenities for guests.
  • The Cissna Park Bed & Breakfast: This bed and breakfast is situated in a beautifully restored 1800s mansion and features luxury amenities and gourmet breakfast options.
  • Best Western Monticello Gateway Inn: This hotel provides a convenient location near major attractions in the area, spacious rooms, and great service.

What to Buy in Iroquois County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Iroquois County has numerous shops and markets where visitors can buy great souvenirs and local products to take home. Below are some of the best products to look out for:

  • Iroquois Pottery: Handcrafted pottery items make excellent souvenirs and gifts for loved ones back home.
  • Meat Products: The county is well-known for its delicious meat products, including sausages, jerky, and bacon.

Local Culture and Customs of Iroquois County

Iroquois County takes pride in its culture, which is highly influenced by the county’s rich history and traditions. Some of the customs that visitors should take note of include:

  • Respect for History: With numerous museums and historical landmarks, Iroquois County residents place great importance on their history, and visitors are expected to show respect for this.
  • Friendliness: Visitors can expect warm and friendly locals in Iroquois County.

Best Time to Visit Iroquois County

The best time to visit Iroquois County is during the summer months (between June and August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and most outdoor activities are available. Fall (September to November) is also a great time to explore the county’s natural beauty when the fall foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iroquois County

When visiting Iroquois County, it’s essential to observe some of the do’s and don’ts. Below are some of the things to consider:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture: Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions when visiting Iroquois County.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities: With numerous natural attractions, visitors should take advantage of the various outdoor activities available.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Visitors should avoid littering and should dispose of their trash responsibly.
  • Ignore the history: Iroquois County is rich in history, and visitors should make an effort to appreciate and experience this.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iroquois County

Iroquois County is generally safe for visitors. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their valuables in their cars when not in use, being cautious when out at night, and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Iroquois County

Iroquois County has several hospitals and clinics that offer quality medical care. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate medical insurance and carry all necessary medication when visiting the county.

Where to Go Next from Iroquois County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
