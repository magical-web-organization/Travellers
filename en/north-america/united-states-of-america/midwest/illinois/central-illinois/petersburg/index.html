Petersburg is a charming town located in Central Illinois, known for its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and friendly locals. Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or culture vulture, Petersburg has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through all the must-visit places in Petersburg, the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

Introduction to Petersburg

Founded in 1833, Petersburg is a small town with a population of approximately 2,200. The town is located in Menard County and is only 20 miles northwest of the state capital, Springfield. With its well-preserved historic district, Petersburg showcases Illinois’s rich history and heritage. The town is also home to many natural attractions, including State Parks and Wildlife Refuges, providing visitors with endless opportunities for outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Petersburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Petersburg’s rich history is proudly displayed in its many museums and historical sites. From Civil War artifacts to historic homes, there are plenty of opportunities for history buffs to explore. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Petersburg include:

  • Lincoln’s New Salem State Historic Site: This living history museum features a reconstruction of New Salem, where Abraham Lincoln spent his early adulthood.
  • Museum of Funeral Customs: This unique museum showcases the history and evolution of funeral practices in America.
  • The Elijah Iles House: This restored historic home belonged to one of Springfield’s founding fathers and is now open to the public for guided tours.
  • The Old State Capitol: This Statehouse was the site of Lincoln’s “House Divided” speech and later the place where his casket lay in state.

Natural Attractions of Petersburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Petersburg boasts numerous natural attractions, offering visitors plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors. Here are some natural wonders of Petersburg that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Lincoln’s Trail State Park: This park features miles of trails for hiking, biking, or horseback riding, including the 10-mile Lincoln’s Trail, named after Abraham Lincoln.
  • Petersburg Wildlife Refuge: Covering over 900 acres, this refuge is home to several species of wildlife, including deer, raccoons, and songbirds.
  • New Salem State Park Lake: A beautiful lake surrounded by trees and wildlife, the New Salem State Park Lake is perfect for fishing, boating, or picnicking.
  • Jim Edgar Panther Creek State Fish and Wildlife Area: This area offers ample opportunities for hunting, fishing, hiking, and wildlife observation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petersburg

Petersburg is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many beautiful trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Petersburg:

  • Lincoln’s Trail: This 10-mile trail runs through Lincoln’s New Salem State Historic Site and offers a glimpse into Abraham Lincoln’s early life.
  • Sangamon Valley Trail: This 14-mile trail stretches across Menard County and provides breathtaking views of the Sangamon River.
  • Wolf Creek State Park Trail: Located within the Wolf Creek State Park, this trail offers scenic views of the lake, creek, and wooded areas.
  • Jim Edgar Panther Creek State Fish and Wildlife Area Trails: With over 10 miles of designated hiking trails, this area offers plenty of opportunities for hikers to explore the wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Petersburg: Activities and Tours

Petersburg has something to offer for everyone, from history buffs to nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best things to do in Petersburg:

  • Take a guided tour of Lincoln’s New Salem State Historic Site: Learn about Abraham Lincoln’s early life and the community he lived in.
  • Visit the Museum of Funeral Customs: Explore the fascinating history and evolution of funeral customs in America.
  • Go fishing at New Salem State Park Lake: A beautiful lake surrounded by trees, it’s a perfect spot for anglers to catch bass, bluegill, and catfish.
  • Go hiking on the nature trails at the Jim Edgar Panther Creek State Fish and Wildlife Area: Enjoy a peaceful hike through the woods and observe the wildlife.
  • Go birdwatching at the Petersburg Wildlife Refuge: It is home to various species of birds, including the American bald eagle!

Food and Drink Scene of Petersburg

Petersburg’s food and drink scene is a testament to the town’s rich agricultural heritage. From traditional American cuisine to locally sourced produce, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Corn on the cob: A staple of Midwestern cooking, the corn on the cob in Petersburg is sweet and juicy.
  • Pulled Pork Sandwiches: Slow-cooked and smoky, the pulled pork sandwiches in Petersburg are a treat for meat lovers.
  • Country Fried Steak: A classic Midwestern dish, the country-fried steaks in Petersburg are seasoned to perfection and served with creamy gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Illinois wine: Illinois has a growing winemaking industry, and many local wineries offer tastings and tours.
  • Craft beer: The town has several breweries, and their craft beers are sure to impress any beer lover.

Local Culture and Customs of Petersburg

Petersburg has a rich cultural history, and visitors are encouraged to experience it firsthand. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect the local history: Many of the attractions in Petersburg are sacred to the town and state’s history, and visitors should treat them with respect.
  • Friendliness is key: The locals are known for their hospitality and friendliness, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation.
  • Embrace the small-town charm: Petersburg is a small town with a relaxed pace of life, so embrace the slower pace and enjoy the moment.

Best Time to Visit Petersburg

The best time to visit Petersburg is during the summer months, between June and August. The weather is warm and pleasant, and the parks and wildlife refuges are in full bloom. However, if you’re looking for a quieter and more peaceful experience, consider visiting during the fall or spring.

Getting to Petersburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Petersburg

The nearest major airport is Abraham Lincoln Capital Airport in Springfield, about 25 miles from Petersburg.

Bus Stations Near Petersburg

Greyhound Bus Lines operate a bus station within walking distance in Springfield.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Petersburg by driving on I-55 and then following signs.

How to Get Around in Petersburg

Petersburg is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer, you can take a taxi, Uber, or rent a car.

Where To Go Next from Petersburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Petersburg is close to several other popular destinations in Illinois. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Springfield: Just 20 miles away, Springfield is the state capital and home to many attractions such as the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum.
  • Jacksonville: Located 25 miles north of Petersburg, Jacksonville has several historic sites, antique shops, and art galleries.
  • Lake Shelbyville: About an hour’s drive away, Lake Shelbyville is perfect for water sports and fishing.
