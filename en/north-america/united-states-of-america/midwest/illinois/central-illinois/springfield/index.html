Introduction to Springfield

Springfield, the capital of Illinois, is a vibrant city located in the heart of the Midwest. It is known for its rich history, famous landmarks, and amazing cultural attractions. Visitors can explore the hometown of Abraham Lincoln and learn more about the 16th President of the United States. Springfield offers a diverse range of opportunities to explore, with its museums, parks, restaurants, and recreational activities.

List of Places to Visit in Springfield

Whether you are traveling alone, with family or friends, Springfield has something for everyone. Here are the top places to visit in Springfield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Abraham Lincoln Presidential Museum - This museum offers visitors an informative journey through President Lincoln’s presidency, complete with exhibits highlighting his life, impact, and legacy.
  • Illinois State Capitol - This is an architectural masterpiece that presents a brilliant combination of classical and modern design. Visitors can take a tour of the Capitol building and learn about its significance in Illinois politics.
  • Lincoln Tomb and War Memorials - This historic site is the final resting place of President Lincoln. Visitors can explore the beautifully kept gravesite, and the memorials commemorating the Civil War soldiers.

Natural Attractions of Springfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lincoln Memorial Garden and Nature Center - This garden is an enchanting site that features winding trails, stunning vistas, and gorgeous natural habitats.
  • Washington Park - This park is a favorite of both locals and tourists, a perfect place for picnics, outdoor games, and sporting events.
  • Henson Robinson Zoo - This zoo is an ideal outing for animal lovers. It houses a wide selection of exotic and native animals, from tigers and kangaroos to bald eagles and river otters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Springfield

For those who love outdoor activities, Springfield offers beautiful hiking and biking trails that are perfect for exploring the natural surroundings.

  • Lost Bridge Trail - This trail features a 2.2-mile paved path through rolling hills, breathtaking natural landscapes, and peaceful wildlife.
  • Southwind Park Trail - This trail winds around the lovely Southwind Park, offering visitors a chance to enjoy stunning views of the park, playgrounds, and wooded areas.

Best Things to Do in Springfield: Activities and Tours

Springfield is full of fun and unique things to do for visitors. Below are some of the top things to do in Springfield:

  • Hop-On Hop-Off Trolley Tour - This tour is a fantastic way to explore the city’s famous landmarks, including the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Museum, the Illinois Capitol, and the Lincoln Tomb.
  • Sangamon River Trail - Visitors can join guided kayak and canoe tours down the Sangamon River, exploring Illinois’s natural beauty up close.
  • Dana-Thomas House - One of Frank Lloyd Wright’s masterpieces, this architectural marvel is a must-visit attraction to see the iconic designer’s work up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Springfield

Springfield’s food and drink scene is quickly gaining popularity among locals and tourists, offering a range of cuisines that are sure to satisfy any taste.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Horseshoe Sandwich - A Springfield staple, this dish is an open-faced sandwich topped with cheese sauce and served with a side of fries.
  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich - Another local favorite, this sandwich boasts a crispy breaded pork loin topped with pickles and mustard.
  • Cozy Dog - A hot dog on a stick, coated in cornmeal batter, and deep-fried until golden brown.

Local Beverages

  • Sangria and Mojitos - Springfield’s Latin American scene is vibrant and offers delicious sangria and refreshing mojitos.
  • Microbreweries - Springfield has become known for its microbreweries, which offer a range of local, craft beers.

Where to Stay in Springfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Springfield has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference.

  • President Abraham Lincoln - A DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel - This charming hotel located in the city center offers guests comfortable, luxurious rooms and state-of-the-art amenities.
  • Carpenter Street Hotel - This boutique hotel offers spacious and cozy rooms, with a central location that makes it a great choice for exploring the city.
  • Springfield International Hostel - Backpackers and budget-conscious travelers can stay at this hostel with shared dormitories and private rooms.

What to Buy in Springfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Abraham Lincoln Memorabilia - Visitors can buy souvenirs and trinkets of the 16th President to remember their trip to Springfield.
  • Handmade Soaps and Skincare Products - Springfield has a range of locally made soaps and skincare products, made of natural ingredients that are perfect for gift-giving.

Local Culture and Customs of Springfield

Springfield is a welcoming and friendly city, with residents that are proud of their state’s history and heritage. Visitors can expect to find locals happy to answer any questions about the city’s sights and attractions.

Best Time to Visit Springfield

The best time to visit Springfield is during the spring and summer months. The warm weather makes it perfect for exploring outdoor attractions, from hiking trails and nature parks to outdoor festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Springfield

Do:

  • Do visit the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum.
  • Do try the local food.
  • Do explore the trails in Lincoln Memorial Garden and Nature Center.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore the history.
  • Don’t miss the opportunity to visit the Illinois State Capitol.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable hiking shoes for outdoor excursions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Springfield

Springfield is generally a safe city to visit, with a low crime rate. Visitors should always take standard safety precautions like keeping an eye on their belongings, not walking alone at night, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Springfield

Illinois is a healthy state, with good water and air quality. However, visitors should bring any necessary prescription medicines, first aid kits, and bug spray to avoid any health concerns.

Getting to Springfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Springfield

  • Abraham Lincoln Capital Airport - This is Springfield’s only airport, located about six miles northwest of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Springfield

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - This station is on North 9th Street, just a short drive from the city center.

Other Options

  • Amtrak - Springfield has an Amtrak station that connects to major cities like Chicago and St. Louis.

How to Get Around in Springfield

Springfield is a walkable city, with most attractions located within walking distance of each other. However, visitors can also rent a bike or a car to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Springfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Springfield is located in the heart of Illinois, making it easy to visit other major cities and attractions.

  • St. Louis, Missouri - Approximately 100 miles southwest of Springfield, St. Louis offers visitors amazing ZOO and the Gateway Arch.
  • Chicago, Illinois - Just under 200 miles north of Springfield, Chicago is a world-renowned city that offers visitors incredible attractions, such as the Cloud Gate, Navy Pier and the Wrigley Field to name a few.

Conclusion

Springfield, Illinois, is a city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and adventure. From world-class museums and historic sites to scenic parks and outdoor activities, Springfield is the perfect destination for travelers. With amazing food and drink options, plenty of accommodation choices, and friendly locals, Springfield is a must-visit attraction in the Midwest.
