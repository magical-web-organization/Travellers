Introduction to Pontiac

Located in Central Illinois, Pontiac is a quaint city known for its rich history and natural beauty. The city was named after Chief Pontiac, a leader of the Ottawa tribe, who played a significant role in the history of the area during the 18th century.

Visitors to Pontiac can explore the city’s unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in museums, outdoor adventures, or delicious food, Pontiac has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Pontiac

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Pontiac:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Pontiac-Oakland Automobile Museum - This museum showcases the history of the Pontiac and Oakland automobile brands, both of which were created in Pontiac. Visitors can view a collection of vintage cars, including many rare models.

  2. Livingston County War Museum - This museum honors the brave men and women who served their country during wartime. The museum features exhibits on various periods of American history, including World War I, World War II, and the Vietnam War.

  3. Illinois Route 66 Hall of Fame and Museum - Route 66 played an important role in American history, and this museum celebrates the iconic highway’s history and impact. Visitors can learn about the people, landmarks, and businesses that shaped Route 66’s legacy.

  4. The Jones House - A historic house museum that was built in 1858. This house was home to two prominent Pontiac residents, Dr. William and Ella Jones. Visitors can learn about the lives of the Jones family, as well as the history of the house and the community.

Natural Attractions of Pontiac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Humiston-Riverside Park - This beautiful park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including fishing, hiking, and picnicking. Visitors can also enjoy scenic views of the Vermilion River.

  2. Prairie Creek Public Fishing Area - Fishing enthusiasts will enjoy this popular fishing spot, which is home to a diverse array of fish species. The area is also frequented by local wildlife, including deer and waterfowl.

  3. Dwight-Pontiac Trail - This 17-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the Illinois countryside. Visitors can hike, bike, or even ride horseback along the trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pontiac

Pontiac has a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. Dwight-Pontiac Trail - This scenic trail offers stunning views of the Illinois landscape. The trail is well-maintained and is suitable for both hiking and biking.

  2. Humiston-Riverside Park Trail - This trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride. The trail circles the park and offers views of the Vermilion River.

  3. The Hennepin Canal Trail - Located just a short drive from Pontiac, this trail is known for its stunning scenery and historic canal locks.

Best Things to Do in Pontiac: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities and tours to experience in Pontiac. Here are just a few:

  1. Pontiac Ghost Tour - This spooky tour takes visitors on a journey through Pontiac’s haunted history. Visitors can learn about the city’s most famous ghost stories and paranormal sightings.

  2. Wright’s Furniture Tours - This tour takes visitors through the world-renowned furniture factory and showroom. Visitors can observe skilled craftsmen at work, and even purchase Wright’s furniture at a discount.

  3. Heritage Days Festival - This annual festival celebrates Pontiac’s culture and history. Visitors can enjoy live music, craft vendors, and traditional Illinois cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Pontiac

Pontiac is home to many restaurants and brewpubs, serving up delicious local cuisine and refreshing drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pork chops - A Midwest classic, pork chops are a must-try in Pontiac. Many local restaurants offer their own unique take on this classic dish.

  2. Corn on the cob - With the abundance of corn farms in the area, it’s no surprise that corn on the cob is a popular menu item in Pontiac.

  3. Horseshoe sandwich - A specialty of the region, the horseshoe sandwich consists of bread, meat, french fries, and a cheese sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Hand-crafted beer - Pontiac is home to several brewpubs that serve up delicious hand-crafted beers. Be sure to try a local brew during your visit.

  2. Sweet corn soda - A unique local beverage, sweet corn soda is made from fresh corn and has a distinct, refreshing flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Pontiac

Like many areas in the Midwest, Pontiac has a rich cultural heritage. The city is proud of its history and celebrates it through various festivals and cultural events throughout the year.

Visitors to Pontiac will find friendly locals who are proud of their town and eager to share its history and attractions with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Pontiac

The best time to visit Pontiac is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. The city comes alive with various festivals and outdoor events during this time.

Getting to Pontiac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pontiac is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Bus Stations Near Pontiac

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in nearby Bloomington, this bus station offers daily bus service to many major cities throughout the Midwest.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Pontiac
