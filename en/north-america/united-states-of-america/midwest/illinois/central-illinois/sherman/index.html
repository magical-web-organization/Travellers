Introduction to Sherman

Sherman is a quaint village located in Central Illinois, known for its rich historical heritage and beautiful natural landscapes. The town has a population of approximately 4,000 people and provides visitors with plenty of activities to explore, from hiking through its scenic trails to visiting its renowned museums and cultural centers.

List of Places to Visit in Sherman

Here’s a list of the top attractions Sherman has to offer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Route 66 Garage Museum & Cafe: This museum celebrates the history of the iconic Route 66, offering visitors a unique experience to learn about the highway’s past.
  • Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum: See the legacy of Abraham Lincoln, one of Illinois’ most well-known and influential residents, at this museum and library.
  • Prairie Village Museum: Get a glimpse into the past at Prairie Village Museum with its collection of historical buildings, including the Old Stone House, built in 1836.

Natural Attractions of Sherman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lincoln Memorial Garden: Spread out over 100 acres, this garden is a great place to go hiking and explore the natural beauty of Illinois.
  • Lake Springfield: Enjoy the lake’s many fishing opportunities, ski on its smooth waters, or simply soak up the sun on its many beaches.
  • Southwind Park: Walk, hike, picnic, or play sports at this park that offers something for everyone.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sherman

Sherman provides visitors with numerous hiking and biking routes that will take your breath away. Here are some of our favorite routes:

  • Sangamon Valley Trail: This 15-mile-long trail meanders through open fields, forests, and wetlands, offering both hiking and biking trails.
  • Adams Wildlife Sanctuary Trail: A nature lover’s paradise, the trail offers an ideal opportunity to explore the sanctuary’s wildlife and vegetation.
  • Mallard Point Trail: This is a great trail for visitors who want to get a feel for central Illinois’ beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Sherman: Activities and Tours

Sherman has a range of activities to suit a variety of interests. Here’s what we recommend:

  • Knight’s Action Park & Caribbean Water Adventure: For a fun-filled day with the family, head to Knight’s Action Park and Caribbean Water Adventure to enjoy water slides and the Caribbean-themed waterpark.
  • Illinois State Capitol Tour: Take a guided tour of the Illinois State Capitol to learn about its unique architecture and see its legislative chamber.
  • Fulgenzi’s Trattoria: Go out for dinner and enjoy the local cuisine at this popular Italian eatery known for its excellent food and cozy atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of Sherman

Sherman offers visitors a wide range of dining options, from locally sourced farm-to-table restaurants to barbecue joints and Italian eateries.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Horseshoe: This dish is a classic Illinois dish consisting of toast, a meat such as hamburger patties or ham, and french fries, all smothered in a cheese sauce.
  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: A regional specialty and Illinois’ Fall Food, the pork tenderloin sandwich is a must-try. It features a battered, deep-fried pork tenderloin on a bun, and is typically served with lettuce, tomato, pickles, and mayonnaise.

Local Beverages

  • Illinois Wine: Visitors can head to Danenberger Family Vineyards to try some of the state’s best wine.
  • Sangamon Tail Ale: This local beer is brewed by Hand of Fate Brewing Company and a great choice for beer aficionados.

Where to Stay in Sherman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Sherman will find a range of accommodations to choose from, whether it be a budget-friendly hostel or a luxury hotel. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Crowne Plaza: Elegant, modern hotel, equipped with an indoor pool and located conveniently near all the major attractions.
  • State House Inn: Featuring luxurious accommodations, this hotel is located within walking distance to downtown Springfield and all its attractions.
  • Comfort Suites: For an affordable stay, the Comfort Suites provides comfortable amenities and a convenient location near all Sherman has to offer.

What to Buy in Sherman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique and local items to consider buying in Sherman:

  • Glassware from the Prairie Glass Studio: Take home a piece of handmade glassware or pottery from this local craft studio.
  • Fresh produce from Farmers Markets: Take home fresh fruits and vegetables grown locally from vendors located throughout town.

Local Culture and Customs of Sherman

Sherman is a small mid-western town that prides itself on a close-knit community. Residents are friendly, welcoming, and known for their hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Sherman

The best time to visit Sherman is during the spring and summer, between May to August. This is when the town’s outdoor activities are at their peak, and the weather is typically warm and balmy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sherman

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Sherman:

Do:

  • Do take advantage of all the natural attractions, hiking trails, and scenic views that Sherman has to offer.
  • Do try the local food, especially Illinois’ famous horseshoe dish.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip 15-20% at local restaurants and bars.
  • Don’t forget to bring your sunscreen if you plan to spend a lot of time outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sherman

Sherman is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise caution and take the usual safety measures such as keeping belongings out of sight and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Sherman

Visitors to Sherman can be assured of receiving top-quality medical care at the nearby Memorial Medical Center. It’s best to take out travel insurance, as medical care can be expensive.

Getting to Sherman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Sherman can choose from several transportation options. Here are some of the most popular:

Airports Near Sherman

  • Abraham Lincoln Capital Airport is the nearest airport, offering both domestic and international flights from nearby cities.

Bus Stations Near Sherman

  • Springfield Station: This Amtrak train station is located near downtown Springfield and offers a variety of transport options to Sherman

Other Options

  • Driving: Visitors can always rent a car and drive to Sherman using major roads I-55 and I-72.

How to Get Around in Sherman

Visitors can easily explore Sherman using a rental car, public transportation, or by walking. Rental cars are readily available at the airport or nearby stations.

Where to Go Next from Sherman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sherman provides visitors with an array of excursion options. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Chicago: This world-class city is only a few hours away by car and can be a perfect addition to your itinerary.
  • St. Louis: Home to the iconic Gateway Arch, St. Louis is only a few hours of driving away and an enjoyable getaway.

Conclusion

Sherman offers an abundance of activities for tourists to enjoy, including hiking trails, beautiful natural landscapes, and a variety of museums and cultural centers. Whether it’s for the weekend or a week-long stay, visitors will find ample opportunity to explore everything the town has to offer.
