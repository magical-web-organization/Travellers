Introduction to Decatur

Located in Central Illinois, Decatur is a city with a rich history and a vibrant culture. Known for its beautiful parks, impressive museums, and delicious food scene, Decatur is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling through the Midwest. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Decatur has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Decatur

If you’re planning a trip to Decatur, here’s a list of top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Macon County History Museum: Showcases the rich history of Decatur through exhibits on industry, transportation, and local heritage.
  • Governor Oglesby Mansion: Once home to Governor Richard J. Oglesby, this historic mansion now serves as a museum and is open for tours.
  • Hieronymus Mueller Museum: A museum dedicated to the history of Mueller Co., which has its headquarters in Decatur.
  • Scovill Zoo: A 40-acre zoo featuring over 400 animals from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Decatur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rock Springs Nature Center: A popular spot for hiking, bird watching, and camping, featuring 1,300 acres of woodland, prairie, and wetlands.
  • Millikin Homestead: The former estate of James Millikin, founder of Millikin University, featuring a beautiful garden and a greenhouse.
  • Griswold Conservation Area: A natural area featuring a 186-acre lake and over 700 acres of woods and grasslands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Decatur

For the outdoorsy traveler, Decatur offers several hiking and biking trails:

  • Lake Decatur Trail: A 7.5-mile trail that circles Lake Decatur, offering scenic views of the lake and the surrounding countryside.
  • Sand Creek Conservation Area Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that winds through the woods and along Sand Creek.
  • Unquakable Trail: A 2.5-mile trail that winds through the Griswold Conservation Area, featuring scenic views of the lake and the woods.

Best Things to Do in Decatur: Activities and Tours

Apart from the places to visit above, here’re some other fun activities to do in Decatur:

  • Take a guided tour of the James Millikin Homestead.
  • Enjoy a picnic at Rock Springs Nature Center.
  • Catch a movie at the Avon Theatre.
  • Visit Decatur Brew Works for a local beer tasting.
  • Attend a performance at the Decatur Civic Center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Decatur

If you have a week to spend in Decatur, here’s a comprehensive itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Macon County History Museum, the Scovill Zoo, and enjoy some local cuisine at Krekel’s Custard.
Day 2 Hike the Lake Decatur Trail and have a picnic at Rock Springs.
Day 3 Spend the day golfing at the Hickory Point Golf Course.
Day 4 Check out the Hieronymus Mueller Museum and take a guided tour of the James Millikin Homestead.
Day 5 Visit the Griswold Conservation Area and enjoy an outdoor barbecue.
Day 6 Take a day trip to nearby Springfield to visit the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum.
Day 7 Spend the day relaxing at the Decatur Park District swimming pool or visit Scovill Golf Course.

Food and Drink Scene of Decatur

Decatur’s food and drink scene has something for every palate:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza: Try the square-cut pizza at Brienzo’s.
  • Burger: Head to the Burger Barge for their famous “Barge Burger”.
  • Ice Cream: Visit Dacey’s Taphouse for a scoop of their homemade ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Decatur Brew Works is known for their local brews and relaxed atmosphere.
  • Wine: Visit Blue Sky Vineyard for endless views and award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Decatur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Decatur has plenty of accommodation options to choose from:

  • Hotel Accommodations: The Decatur Conference Center and Hotel and Soy City Motel are two popular options.
  • Hostel Accommodations: The Hosteling International Chicago is a great option for budget-conscious travelers.
  • Guesthouses: The Decatur Guest House is a top-rated option for those looking for a more personalized stay.

Local Culture and Customs of Decatur

Decatur is a welcoming and friendly city with a rich cultural heritage. People are helpful, and you will find locals who are happy to help make your stay in the city as comfortable and enjoyable as possible. The city also boasts a great sense of community, with many events and festivals held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Decatur

The best time to visit Decatur is during the summers when the weather is pleasant, and the city is buzzing with activity and events. However, if you’re a fan of winter sports or winter scenery, you may want to visit during the winter season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Decatur

Do:

  • Do tip your servers at restaurants.
  • Do take advantage of the many parks and outdoor spaces.
  • Do attend local cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and natural areas.
  • Don’t drink and drive.
  • Don’t make loud noises in the residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Decatur

Decatur is a safe city, but like any other city, it is essential to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone late at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight when you’re out and about.
  • Be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Decatur

Decatur has a good healthcare system, with many hospitals and healthcare facilities located throughout the city. The city is also served by multiple pharmacies and clinics.

  • Public Transportation: The Decatur Public Transit System operates several bus routes throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Decatur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Decatur is surrounded by several small towns and cities, making it an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations:

  • Springfield, IL: Visit the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum.
  • Peoria, IL: See the giant Caterpillar equipment in the Caterpillar Visitors Center.
  • Bloomington, IL: Discover the Miller Park Zoo and the McLean County Museum of History.

Decatur is a charming city with a lot to offer to travelers of all kinds. From the outdoor adventures to the historical landmarks and the food and drink scene, there is no shortage of things to do and places to visit in Decatur.
