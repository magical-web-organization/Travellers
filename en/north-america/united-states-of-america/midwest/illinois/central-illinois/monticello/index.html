Introduction to Monticello

Monticello is a charming town located in Central Illinois and boasts a rich history and natural beauty. This small town is perfect for a refreshing getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. With a variety of attractions and activities, Monticello is a must-see destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Illinois.

List of Places to Visit in Monticello

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Monticello Railway Museum: This museum showcases the history of Illinois railroads and offers outdoor train rides.
  • Piatt County Museum: Visitors can learn about Monticello’s past by browsing the exhibits, including antique cars, farming equipment, and historical artifacts.
  • Allerton Park and Retreat Center: This former private estate features impressive gardens, sculptures, and a Georgian mansion that visitors can tour.

Natural Attractions of Monticello: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clinton Lake: This reservoir offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and camping. With multiple lakeside trails, visitors can enjoy the scenic views while hiking or biking.
  • Robert Allerton Park: In addition to the mansion and gardens, this park has over 14 miles of hiking trails through forests, meadows, and prairies.
  • Lake of the Woods Forest Preserve: This 900-acre park features a small lake with a sandy beach, a golf course, and over 10 miles of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monticello

Monticello is a hiker’s paradise, with miles of trails through the rolling hills and scenic landscapes. Some popular hiking and biking trails include:

  • The River Trail of Illinois: This 42-mile stretch of beautiful scenery runs from Kickapoo State Park to Salt Fork River Park, two nearby state parks perfect for camping, fishing, and paddling.
  • The Sangamon River Trail: A 7.2-mile, scenic nature trail follows the banks of the Sangamon River, a beautiful area for hiking, bird-watching, or wildlife spotting.
  • The Railsplitter Trail: A 13-mile, crushed limestone surface trail that follows the historic 1850’s Chicago and Mississippi Railroad line from Urbana to East of Monticello, IL.

Best Things to Do in Monticello: Activities and Tours

  • Framer’s Market: A weekly market during the summer and early fall where visitors can buy locally grown produce, baked goods, and crafts.
  • Monticellobration: An annual festival held in September featuring live music, vendors, and a parade.
  • Wine Down Wednesdays: Every Wednesday during the summer months Piedmont Wine Trail in downtown Monticello offers wine tasting and socializing.

Food and Drink Scene of Monticello

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: A traditional Midwestern dish, the tenderloin sandwich is a lightly breaded and fried pork cutlet served on a bun with toppings like lettuce, tomato, onions, and pickles.
  • Chicago-Style Pizza: A deep-dish pizza with a thick crust and layers of cheese and toppings that originated in Chicago. Monical’s Pizza is one of the most popular pizza spots in Monticello.
  • Corn: With acres of farmland surrounding Monticello, sweet corn is a must-try. Local farms source the popular Rossville Sweet Corn.

Local Beverages

  • Bluewater Grill: Offers local beers and assorted wines from Illinois wineries, and signature cocktails like the Berry Sangria.
  • White Heath Vineyards: A vineyard outside Monticello, offers tastings and tours on their estate.

Where to Stay in Monticello: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Monticello Gateway Inn: A comfortable hotel located near popular attractions like Allerton Park and Retreat Center and Lake of the Woods.
  • Allerton Farms: Offers a unique experience for visitors who want to experience overnight accommodations at one of the Manor House or other properties located on the estate.
  • Nature’s Resort: A family-friendly campground that offers options like cabins, tentsites, RV Rentals and Buffalo Trace tours.

What to Buy in Monticello: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sweet Corn: Take home a dozen fresh ears of sweet corn to enjoy the taste of Illinois farm country.
  • Handmade Crafts: The Monticello Farmers Market features a variety of local handmade crafts, jewelry, and accessories.
  • Wine: Buy a bottle of locally-made wine at Piedmont Wine Trail.

Local Culture and Customs of Monticello

Monticello is a small town with a friendly and welcoming atmosphere. The town honors patriotic holidays with festivals, parades, and fireworks. Agriculture plays a significant role in Monticello’s economy and culture.

Best Time to Visit Monticello

The best time to visit Monticello is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town’s outdoor attractions are open. Spring and Fall are also great times to visit when nature in the area is in full bloom or fall foliage peaks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monticello

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Monticello with outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Visit the local museums, historical sites, and parks.
  • Sip some locally made wine at any of the wineries.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the natural attractions or hiking trails.
  • Exceed posted speed limits or observe traffic rules while driving.
  • Trespass or leave designated trails in any natural parks or discovered off-road areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monticello

Monticello is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors are encouraged to take standard travel precautions, like locking their car, and preventing valuables from driving through town.

Health in Monticello

Be sure to have health insurance before visiting Monticello. The medical facilities in the area are limited, so visitors should take any necessary prescription medications or bring basic first-aid items.

Getting to Monticello: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monticello

  • University of Illinois – Willard Airport (CMI) - 25 miles from Monticello
  • Central Illinois Regional Airport (BMI) - 70 miles from Monticello

Bus Stations Near Monticello

  • Greyhound bus station is in Champaign, IL (20 miles from Monticello)

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient option to get to Monticello, and State Route 105 and Champaign County Highway 9 are two major routes leading to the town.

How to Get Around in Monticello

The best way to get around Monticello is by car or bike. Visitors can rent bicycles at Allerton Park and Retreat Center or Nature’s Resort to explore the local trails.

Where to Go Next from Monticello: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Champaign, IL, located 20 miles east of Monticello, is home to the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign, a vibrant downtown area, and numerous cultural events.
  • Springfield, IL, located 70 miles southwest of Monticello, is the state capital and boasts historical sites like the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum and the Illinois State Capitol.

Conclusion

Monticello, Illinois, is a perfect getaway for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and foodies. With a variety of outdoor activities, museums, natural attractions, and unique local experiences, Monticello has something to offer everyone. So, what are you waiting for?
