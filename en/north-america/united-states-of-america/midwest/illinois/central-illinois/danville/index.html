Introduction to Danville

Danville is a small city located in Central Illinois, nestled between the banks of the Vermilion River and the rolling hills of the Prairie State. It is known for its rich history and vibrant community, offering visitors a unique blend of small-town charm and big-city entertainment. Danville is located on the historic Route 66, making it a popular stop for road trippers and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Danville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vermilion County Museum: Located in a former post office, this museum showcases local history through interactive exhibits.
  • Fischer Theatre: A beautifully restored theatre from 1884 that offers concerts, plays, and art shows.
  • Harmon Art Museum: This museum houses a collection of American art, European art, and fine crafts.

Natural Attractions of Danville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kickapoo State Park: This park offers hiking trails, fishing, canoeing, and camping.
  • Kennekuk County Park: A 3000-acre park with 14 different hiking trails, horseback riding, and fishing.
  • Forest Glen Preserve: A 1800-acre nature preserve with over 6 miles of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Danville

  • Danville River Walk: A 2.4 mile paved path along the Vermilion River that offers scenic views and easy access to downtown.
  • The Kickapoo Rail Trail: A 24.5 mile multi-use trail that winds through some of the most beautiful scenery in the state.
  • Salt Kettle Rest Area: A scenic spot where visitors can take a break and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Danville: Activities and Tours

  • Danville Symphony Orchestra: An award-winning symphony orchestra that performs throughout the year.
  • Danville Dans Baseball: A collegiate summer baseball team that plays at the Danville Stadium.
  • Danville Art League: A non-profit organization dedicated to promoting art and artists in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Danville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork tenderloin sandwich: A beloved Midwestern classic that can be found at any local diner or café.
  • Chicago style pizza: A deep-dish pizza that is world-famous and can be found at several pizzerias in Danville.
  • Corn on the cob: Freshly picked sweet corn is a favorite summertime treat in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Gene’s Root Beer: A local bottling company that has been producing root beer for over 70 years.
  • Norton’s Brewing Company: A local brewery that serves craft beer, cider, and wine.
  • Sleepy Creek Vineyards: A winery and vineyard that offers tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Danville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Danville: This hotel offers a modern and comfortable stay in a convenient location.
  • Red Roof Inn & Suites Danville: A budget-friendly option that is perfect for travelers on a tight budget.
  • The Rose Hotel: A charming and historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Danville.

What to Buy in Danville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Red Oak II: A local shop that specializes in handcrafted pottery, woodworking, and metalwork.
  • Main Street Artisans: A collective of local artisans who sell handmade jewelry, ceramics, and artwork.
  • Big R: A local farm supply store that offers unique local products, including food items and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Danville

Danville is known for its warm and welcoming community. Visitors are often struck by the friendly and hospitable people they encounter. It is important to remember that the residents of Danville take pride in their city and appreciate visitors who show respect for their home.

Best Time to Visit Danville

The best time to visit Danville is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities and festivals. It is important to note that temperatures can often reach into the 90s, so visitors should come prepared with sunscreen and water.

Do’s and Don’ts of Danville

Do:

  • Do explore the historic downtown area and the Vermilion River.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including pork tenderloin sandwiches and Chicago-style pizza.
  • Do be respectful and friendly to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any natural or historic sites.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and bring water during the summer months.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to locals or their beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Danville

Danville is generally a safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas. It is important to follow common sense safety precautions.

Health in Danville

Visitors to Danville should bring any prescription medications they may need and have adequate health insurance. There are several medical facilities in Danville, including the OSF Healthcare Sacred Heart Medical Center.

Getting to Danville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Danville

  • Willard Airport (CMI) is located approximately 44 miles from Danville.
  • Indianapolis International Airport (IND) is located approximately 84 miles from Danville.

Bus Stations Near Danville

  • Greyhound Bus Station is located in Champaign-Urbana, approximately 35 miles from Danville.
  • Jefferson Lines Bus Station is located in Indianapolis, approximately 85 miles from Danville.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car or taking a taxi to Danville.

How to Get Around in Danville

Visitors can easily get around Danville by car or public transportation. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available. Bicycles can be rented from several local shops, making it a great way to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Danville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Champaign-Urbana: A bustling urban hub with great dining, shopping, and entertainment options.
  • Bloomington-Normal: A vibrant college town with plenty of cultural and recreational activities.
  • Indianapolis: A larger city with world-class museums, theaters, and sports venues.

Conclusion

Danville is a hidden gem in Central Illinois, offering plenty of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and unique attractions for visitors to enjoy. With its historic downtown area, scenic riverfront, and friendly community, visiting Danville is sure to be a memorable experience.
