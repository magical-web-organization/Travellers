Introduction to Cherry

Nestled in Western Illinois, Cherry is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This quaint town boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a welcoming community. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Cherry has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cherry

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cherry is steeped in history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore. The Cherry Mine Disaster Memorial marks the site of the 1909 mining tragedy, while the Wyett House Museum offers a glimpse into 19th-century life. Other must-visit cultural attractions include the Bureau County Historical Society Museum, the Red Covered Bridge, and the Sheffler Monument.

Natural Attractions of Cherry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Cherry offers an abundance of outdoor activities and scenic views. Matthiessen State Park is a popular destination for hiking, rock climbing, and picnicking, while Hall Township Conservation Area boasts beautiful wetlands and a 10-acre lake perfect for fishing and kayaking. For a more leisurely pace, enjoy a scenic drive through the rolling hills and farms that define Cherry’s countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cherry

Cherry is home to several hiking and biking trails that showcase the town’s natural beauty. The Hennepin Canal Parkway State Park Trail is a 104-mile long trail that winds through Cherry, offering beautiful views of the canal and its surrounding landscapes. For a shorter hike, explore the LaSalle Canyon Trail in Matthiessen State Park, which leads to a stunning waterfall and canyon.

Best Things to Do in Cherry: Activities and Tours

In addition to its outdoor adventures and cultural attractions, Cherry offers many activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Take a guided tour of the Red Covered Bridge, one of only four remaining Howe truss bridges in Illinois, or enjoy the award-winning wines and beautiful scenery at August Hill Winery. Other must-try activities include visiting the local farmers market, attending a live performance at The Barn III Dinner Theatre, and touring one of the town’s many historic homes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cherry

For those looking to make the most of their trip to Cherry, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Cherry Mine Disaster Memorial and the Bureau County Historical Society Museum.
  • Day 2: Explore Matthiessen State Park’s hiking trails and waterfalls.
  • Day 3: Sample local wines at August Hill Winery and enjoy a scenic drive through Cherry’s countryside.
  • Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Red Covered Bridge, followed by a visit to the charming Wyett House Museum.
  • Day 5: Relax at Hall Township Conservation Area, fishing and kayaking on the lake.
  • Day 6: Visit the charming town of Princeton for shopping and dining.
  • Day 7: Attend a live performance at The Barn III Dinner Theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of Cherry

Must-Try Dishes

Cherry’s food scene is a reflection of the town’s rural charm and agricultural heritage. Be sure to try a slice of the town’s famous cherry pie at the Cherry Strudel Company, or stop by the City Limits Bakery & Café for fresh pastries and coffee. Other local favorites include the Homestead Restaurant’s hearty comfort food and Wise Guys Pizza’s delicious pies.

Local Beverages

Illinois is known for its wine, and Cherry is no exception. August Hill Winery offers a selection of award-winning wines in a picturesque setting. For the non-wine drinkers, Cherry also boasts several local breweries, including Bishop Hill Brewing Co. and Tangled Roots Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Cherry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cherry offers several lodging options to suit every budget. The Brightwood Inn and the Kishauwau Country Cabins offer cozy and rustic accommodations, while the Hampton Inn and the Days Inn offer more traditional hotel amenities. For a unique experience, consider staying at one of Cherry’s many bed and breakfasts, such as the Pinehill Inn or the chestnut Street Inn.

What to Buy in Cherry: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Cherry is complete without bringing home a little piece of the town’s charm. The Cherry Strudel Company’s cherry pies make a delicious souvenir, as do the locally made jams and honey found at the farmers market. For a more lasting memento, consider purchasing locally crafted pottery or a piece of handblown glass from the Emporium in nearby Princeton.

Local Culture and Customs of Cherry

Cherry’s community is rooted in its rich agricultural heritage, with many residents working in farming and agribusiness. Visitors should also be mindful of the town’s historic ties to mining and the Cherry Mine Disaster. Despite its small size, Cherry is a warm and welcoming town, and visitors should make an effort to engage with locals and learn about the town’s unique culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Cherry

Cherry’s natural beauty and charming atmosphere make it a great place to visit year-round. However, the best time to visit depends on your interests. Spring is beautiful with wildflowers blooming in Matthiessen state parks, summer is great for outdoor activities and festivals, fall is perfect for leaf-peeping, and winter offers peaceful beauty of snow covered landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cherry

Do:

  • Be respectful of Cherry’s history and cultural heritage.
  • Take advantage of the town’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s safety rules, especially near railroad tracks, mines and quarries.
  • Leave nature trails and parks with litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cherry

Like any rural community, Cherry has its own unique safety concerns. Visitors should be cautious near railroad tracks, mining sites, and quarries, and be mindful of town safety measures. In addition, visitors should always take caution when exploring natural areas and hiking trails.

Health in Cherry

Cherry has not only lower air pollution than the national average, but it offers plenty of opportunities to get fresh air and exercise. Still, visitors should take normal precautions, especially in the age of COVID-19.

Getting to Cherry: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cherry

The nearest airport to Cherry is Chicago’s O’Hare International Airport, which is roughly 100 miles away. Visitors can rent a car or take a bus or private shuttle service to Cherry from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Cherry

Greyhound Bus Line and Megabus do not operate in Cherry. Tri-County Rural Transportation offers local bus services to visitors in and around Bureau County.

Other Options

Visitors who want to drive can take advantage of the town’s proximity to major highways, as Cherry is located just off Interstate 180 and Highway 34.

How to Get Around in Cherry

The best way to get around Cherry is by car. Visitors can rent a car at O’Hare International Airport or take public transit to Bureau County and then rent a car locally. Bike rentals are also available near Matthiessen State Park.

Where to Go Next from Cherry: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cherry is surrounded by beautiful natural and historic attractions. Visitors can head to nearby Starved Rock State Park, the charming town of Princeton, or the historic Bishop Hill community for more exploring.

Conclusion

Cherry, Illinois may be small, but it certainly packs a punch in terms of natural beauty, cultural charm, and welcoming community. Plan your trip to experience everything this hidden gem has to offer.
