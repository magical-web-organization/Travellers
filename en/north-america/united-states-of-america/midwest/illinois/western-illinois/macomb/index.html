Introduction to Macomb

Macomb is a charming city located in Western Illinois, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. It is the county seat of McDonough County and is home to Western Illinois University, a public university with over 10,000 students. Macomb is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful outdoor attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see and do in Macomb, as well as provide you with helpful information on where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Macomb

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Western Illinois Museum: This museum showcases the heritage and history of Western Illinois. It features a variety of exhibits, including information on local agriculture, transportation, and architecture.
  • McDonough County Courthouse: A beautiful building with stunning architectural features. It is located in the town square and is a great place to take photos.
  • Macomb Carnegie Library: A historic library that has been around since the early 1900s. It features a beautiful architecture and hosts a variety of events.

Natural Attractions of Macomb: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spring Lake Park: A 700-acre park with a picturesque lake, walking trails and picnic areas.
  • Horn Field Campus: A nature reserve with hiking trails and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Argyle Lake State Park: A scenic park with hiking trails, fishing, and camping facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Macomb

One of the best ways to explore Macomb is by hiking or biking through its scenic trails. Here are some of the top spots to check out:

  • WIU Macomb Nature Preserve: A 4-mile trail that takes you through a wooded area and near a river.
  • Emory Creek Bike Trail: A 4-mile paved trail perfect for biking or walking.
  • Blandinsville Park: A small park with a 0.5-mile paved trail and a stunning lake view.

Best Things to Do in Macomb: Activities and Tours

  • Macomb Balloon Rally and Heritage Days: A must-see annual event featuring hot air balloons, live music, and local food vendors.
  • Art in the Park: A weekend-long art festival celebrating the work of local artists. Takes place in Chandler Park.
  • Macomb Farmers Market: Held every Thursday from 3-6 pm at Chandler Park. Features locally grown produce, artisanal crafts, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Macomb

Day 1: Visit the Western Illinois Museum and grab lunch at The Old Dairy.

Day 2: Go for a hike at Spring Lake Park and stop by The Pink Elephant Antique Mall for some shopping.

Day 3: Visit Emory Creek Bike Trail and grab lunch at The Red Ox Restaurant.

Day 4: Check out the McDonough County Courthouse and the Macomb Carnegie Library.

Day 5: Go on a nature walk at Horn Field Campus and have dinner at Magnolia’s.

Day 6: Explore Argyle Lake State Park and have lunch at Allison’s on the Square.

Day 7: Visit Chandler Park and catch a show at the Macomb Community Theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of Macomb

Must-Try Dishes

  • ButterBurgers: Juicy burgers topped with butter and cheese, served at Culver’s.
  • Deep dish pizza: Thick crust pizza with plenty of toppings, served at Papa Del’s.
  • Pork tenderloin sandwich: A Midwestern classic, served at The Red Ox Restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Fire Coffee Roasters: Locally roasted coffee beans and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Prairie Bomb!: A popular imperial stout beer from Prairie Artisan Ales in Tulsa, Oklahoma, served at The Forum.
  • Wine on Broadway: A wine bar with a selection of local and international wines.

Where to Stay in Macomb: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Macomb: A comfortable hotel with a pool and fitness center.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham Macomb: An affordable option with a free breakfast buffet.
  • Harmony House Bed and Breakfast: A charming guesthouse with Victorian-style rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Macomb: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Macombopoly: A fun board game featuring local businesses and landmarks.
  • Western Illinois University gear: Show your school spirit with t-shirts, hats, and more.
  • Handmade crafts: Check out the local farmers market or artisanal shops for unique, handcrafted items.

Local Culture and Customs of Macomb

Macomb is a friendly and welcoming community with a strong sense of pride in its history and culture. Visitors should be prepared to engage with locals and learn about the rich regional history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Macomb

The best time to visit Macomb is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The summers can be hot, but they also offer a variety of festivals and events, such as the Balloon Rally and Heritage Days.

Do’s and Don’ts of Macomb

Do:

  • Explore the natural attractions and hiking trails.
  • Visit the Western Illinois Museum to learn about local history.
  • Try the local cuisine, such as butterburgers and pork tenderloin sandwiches.

Don’t:

  • Neglect to bring comfortable shoes for hiking and biking.
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray during the summer months.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Macomb

Macomb is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take normal precautions, such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Macomb

Macomb has a hospital and several medical clinics for routine health care. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them when traveling and make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Macomb: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Macomb

The closest airport to Macomb is the Quad City International Airport (MLI), which is approximately a 90-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Macomb

The closest bus station to Macomb is the Greyhound Bus Lines station in Galesburg, which is approximately a 30-minute drive away.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Macomb, as it is located near several major highways, including Interstate 74 and U.S. Route 136.

How to Get Around in Macomb

The best way to get around Macomb is by car. However, there are also several taxi services and ride-sharing options available.

Where to Go Next from Macomb: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nauvoo: A historic town located on the banks of the Mississippi River, known for its Mormon history and architecture.
  • Springfield: The state capital of Illinois, home to the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum and other historic sites.
  • St. Louis: A major metropolitan area located just a few hours to the south, known for its iconic Gateway Arch and extensive dining and shopping options.

Conclusion

Macomb is a hidden gem with a rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in learning about local history, hiking through scenic trails, or trying out the local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Macomb. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be all set to plan your dream Macomb vacation.
