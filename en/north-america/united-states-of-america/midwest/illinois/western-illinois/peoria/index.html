Introduction to Peoria

Peoria is a city located in Western Illinois, United States. It is the largest city on the Illinois River and the county seat of Peoria County. Peoria is a vibrant and booming city that offers visitors a wealth of attractions, from cultural landmarks to natural beauty. It has a rich history, a thriving arts scene, and a growing food and drink culture.

List of Places to Visit in Peoria

Here is a list of must-visit places when you’re in Peoria:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Peoria Riverfront Museum: This museum features exhibits and interactive galleries on art, science, history, and culture.
  • Caterpillar Visitors Center: This museum showcases the history of the famous Caterpillar brand.
  • Pettengill-Morron House Museum: This historical house museum is an excellent example of early 20th-century architecture and interior design.
  • Illinois Holocaust Museum & Education Center: This museum honors the memory of the Holocaust victims and educates visitors on tolerance and diversity.

Natural Attractions of Peoria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Forest Park Nature Center: This nature center offers hiking trails, bird watching, and a chance to explore the local flora and fauna.
  • Peoria Zoo: This award-winning zoo is home to over 100 species of animals and has an extensive conservation program.
  • Peoria Heights Tower Park: This park offers a panoramic view of the city and the Illinois River. It’s a perfect spot for a picnic or a hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peoria

Peoria has more than 100 miles of hiking and biking trails that cater to different levels of difficulty and terrain. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Rock Island Trail: This 26-mile trail runs through Peoria, heading west to Toulon. It’s perfect for cycling.
  • Detweiller Park Loop Trail: This 5.5-mile trail is ideal for hiking and offers scenic views of the nearby lake and forest.
  • Illinois River Trail: This 9-mile trail runs alongside the Illinois River, offering stunning views of the waterway.

Best Things to Do in Peoria: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for fun things to do in Peoria, here are some suggestions:

  • Take a Riverfront tour: Experience Peoria’s beautiful waterfront sights via the Spirit of Peoria paddlewheel boat or a kayak tour.
  • Attend a sporting event: Peoria is home to numerous minor league sports teams, including the Peoria Chiefs baseball team.
  • Visit the Peoria Civic Center: This entertainment complex hosts concerts, comedy shows, and other events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peoria

If you’re planning to spend a week in Peoria, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the riverfront and visit the Peoria Riverfront Museum.
Day 2 Take a hike at the Forest Park Nature Center and spend the afternoon at the Peoria Zoo.
Day 3 Visit the Caterpillar Visitors Center and take a brewery or distillery tour.
Day 4 Take a day trip to the nearby Wildlife Prairie Park and spend the night in nearby Washington, Illinois.
Day 5 Visit the Pettengill-Morron House Museum and the Illinois Holocaust Museum & Education Center.
Day 6 Take a bike ride on the Illinois River Trail and enjoy an outdoor concert at the Peoria Civic Center.
Day 7 Spend the last day in Peoria Heights Tower Park, taking in the scenic views of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Peoria

Peoria has a thriving food and drink scene that caters to different tastes and budgets.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Deep dish pizza: Peoria has its variation of Chicago-style pizza. Don’t miss it!
  • Italian beef sandwich: A Chicago classic which has crossed borders to Peoria.
  • Beef-a-roo: A local burger joint that’s been serving up burgers, shakes, and fries since 1967.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: The beer scene in Peoria is booming, with multiple local breweries offering unique and tasty brews.
  • Whiskey: Peoria is a whiskey lover’s delight, with several distilleries producing both classic and innovative blends.

Where to Stay in Peoria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peoria has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly motels to luxurious hotels.

  • Mark Twain Hotel: A historic downtown hotel that has been recently renovated and modernized.
  • Stoney Creek Hotel and Conference Center: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, an indoor/outdoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Peoria Marriott Pere Marquette: A luxury hotel with an on-site restaurant, fitness center, and an indoor pool.

What to Buy in Peoria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some recommendations:

  • Peoria Chiefs jerseys and merchandise: Take home something to remember the Peoria Chiefs game.
  • Artisanal goods: Peoria has a growing artisanal scene, ranging from handmade jewelry to pottery and textiles.
  • Gift baskets from local wineries or distilleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Peoria

Peoria has a friendly community with welcoming people. The city values sustainability and conservation efforts. So, you can find a lot of green initiatives in place.

Best Time to Visit Peoria

The best time to visit Peoria is from late spring to early fall, roughly from May to October. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peoria

Do:

  • Do Explore the riverfront and nature trails.
  • Do Try the local cuisine.
  • Do checkout the multi-cultural arts and theater scene.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothes and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t litter or waste resources. Peoria is an eco-friendly city, please respect it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peoria

As with most cities, visitors should take precautionary measures to avoid potential risks. Pickpocketing or petty theft can occur in busy areas and attractions. It’s best not to roam alone at night in unknown areas. If a medical emergency occurs, dial 911.

Health in Peoria

Peoria has numerous hospitals and primary care clinics. Visit Peoria Travel Medicine for updated health information about the area and suggested immunizations.

Getting to Peoria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peoria

Peoria International Airport is the closest airport to the city center. From here, you can hire a taxi, Uber/Lyft rides, or a rental car.

Bus Stations Near Peoria

Greyhound bus line has a station downtown. MegaBus also has a stop in Peoria, providing additional transportation options for travellers.

Other Options

Travelers can use Amtrak or airport shuttle services to reach Peoria.

How to Get Around in Peoria

Rent a car, a bike, or hail a taxi to travel within the city. Most popular destinations within the city are walkable.

Where to Go Next from Peoria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peoria’s location offers excellent opportunities for excursions, including:

  • Starved Rock State Park: Two hours away from Peoria and boasts 13 miles of trails, seasonal waterfalls, and rock formations created by glaciers.
  • Springfield, Il: The capital of Illinois, about 2 hours northeast of Peoria, and home to many Abraham Lincoln historical landmarks.
  • Mississippi River Valley Wineries: Take a 30-minute drive across the river from Peoria, and you’ll find multiple wineries to choose from.

Conclusion

Peoria is a city with a vibrant culture and numerous attractions that can delight travelers of all types. Don’t forget to explore the natural wonders, the cultural landmarks, and the eclectic food and drink scene. Plan your next visit to Peoria and enjoy the city’s many wonders.
