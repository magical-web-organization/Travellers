Introduction to Jacksonville

Jacksonville, located in the heart of the Midwest region in Illinois, is a small city brimming with history, culture and natural beauty. Known for its museums, parks, trails and historic sites, the city is a perfect getaway for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs and families. With a rich cultural heritage, Jacksonville has something for visitors of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Jacksonville

Whether you want to explore the cultural landmarks, immerse in nature or indulge in local cuisine, Jacksonville has loads to offer. Here is a list of some must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jacksonville Area Museum: The museum showcases the history and culture of the region through art, artifacts and exhibits. It also houses a research library and a gift shop.
  • Elijah Iles House: The historic house, built in 1837, is a classic example of American Federal architecture. The house was occupied by several prominent residents and is now a museum.
  • Lincoln Square: The square features a statue of American legend Abraham Lincoln, who spoke at the site on several occasions during his tenure.

Natural Attractions of Jacksonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Community Park: The park is home to many sports fields and is a great destination for hiking and picnicking.
  • Lake Jacksonville: The scenic lake covers 860 acres and offers fishing, boating and swimming facilities.
  • Mauvaiseterre Lake: A jewel of a park, Mauvaiseterre Lake is home to a lush forest and a top spot for birdwatching and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jacksonville

The city has many trails and multi-use paths for visitors to explore its natural beauty. The most popular routes include:

  • The Morgan County Conservation Area: The park features several trails for hiking and biking that traverse through woodlands, lakes and prairies.
  • The Woodhaven Lakes: With five miles of hiking and biking trails, the area traverses through forests, alongside lakes and reservoirs, making it a must-visit spot for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Jacksonville: Activities and Tours

Besides the cultural and natural attractions, the city has a plethora of activities and tours for visitors to indulge in. Some popular options include:

  • Illinois College TheatreWorks: The college theatre group performs world-class productions throughout the year.
  • Lincoln Ghost Walks: Explore the haunted history of Jacksonville’s local historic sites with the spooky Lincoln Ghost Walks.
  • Jacksonville Farmers Market: Support local farmers and artisans by visiting the city’s farmers market, selling fresh produce, flowers and goodies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jacksonville

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Jacksonville:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Jacksonville Museum
2 Hike or bike the Morgan County Conservation Area trails
3 Explore the city’s historic downtown, featuring many antique shops
4 Visit Mauvaiseterre Lake for birdwatching and fishing
5 Relax at the Lake Jacksonville beach
6 Watch a production by the Illinois College TheatreWorks
7 Take the Lincoln Ghost Walks tour

Food and Drink Scene of Jacksonville

The city’s food and beverage scene is a mix of international and local flavors, catering to all palates. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Horseshoe Sandwich: An iconic dish of the city, the horseshoe sandwich comprises a thick juicy meat patty topped with crinkle-cut fries and a cheese sauce.
  • Pork tenderloin sandwich: Often referred to as the unofficial state sandwich, the pork tenderloin sandwich is a breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet served on a bun.

Local Beverages

  • Illinois Wines: The state of Illinois is renowned for its wine production, and the local wineries offer some of the best-tasting wines in the US.
  • Sangria Senorial: A refreshing Mexican soft drink made from real grapes, Sangria Senorial is a popular non-alcoholic beverage in the city.

Where to Stay in Jacksonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The city has several accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Hampton Inn Jacksonville: Situated near the town square, the Hampton Inn offers modern amenities, clean rooms, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Country Inn & Suites by Carlson: Featuring an indoor pool and whirlpool, Country Inn & Suites is a comfortable and convenient place to stay.
  • Timber Creek Bed and Breakfast: The cozy and quaint bed and breakfast offers personalized service and charming rooms.

What to Buy in Jacksonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The city has a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make excellent gifts for friends and family. Some of the popular items include:

  • Antiques from the Downtown: Jacksonville’s historic downtown has several antique shops selling unique and vintage items.
  • Jacksonville Chocolate Factory: The chocolate factory sells handcrafted chocolate truffles, bars, and other confectionery delights.

Local Culture and Customs of Jacksonville

The people of Jacksonville are friendly, welcoming and proud of their unique heritage. Visitors are expected to be respectful of local customs and traditions while exploring the city.

Best Time to Visit Jacksonville

The best time to visit Jacksonville is from May to October, the city’s peak tourism season. The mild and sunny weather during this time makes it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jacksonville

When visiting the city, here’s what you should and shouldn’t do:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try the local delicacies and beverages.
  • Explore the city’s historic downtown.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural resources.
  • Disrespect local traditions or beliefs.
  • Cross busy roads or highways carelessly.

Safety and Health

While Jacksonville is a safe city, visitors are advised to take precautionary measures to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in Jacksonville

  • Beware of fake items or counterfeit currency while shopping.
  • Always keep your belongings with you while on the go.
  • Avoid isolated areas, especially at night.

Health in Jacksonville

  • Get vaccinated for seasonal and region-specific diseases before travelling.
  • Avoid consuming tap water and always drink bottled water.
  • Carry sunscreen and insect repellent while exploring outdoors.

Getting to Jacksonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jacksonville is well-connected to major cities in the Midwest region through various modes of transportation. Here are some ways to get there:

Airports Near Jacksonville

  • Abraham Lincoln Capital Airport in Springfield: Located 30 miles away, this airport is nearest to Jacksonville and connected to several major cities in the US.

Bus Stations Near Jacksonville

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Springfield: Located about 30 miles away, this station connects to several cities in the Midwest region and beyond.

Other Options

  • Jacksonville is easily accessible via car or taxi from nearby cities such as Chicago and St. Louis.

How to Get Around in Jacksonville

The city has several options for transportation, including car rentals, taxis, and buses. However, the most convenient way to explore the city is on foot or bike.

Where to Go Next from Jacksonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jacksonville is located in a prime location for exploring other cities and attractions nearby. Some popular destinations include:

  • Springfield: The capital city of Illinois, located 30 miles away from Jacksonville, is known for its historic sites and museums.
  • St. Louis: Located around 90 miles away, the city offers top attractions such as the Gateway Arch and the Missouri Botanical Garden.

Conclusion

Jacksonville, Illinois, may be a small city, but it is a treasure trove of culture and natural beauty. From hiking and exploring the region’s historic sites to trying the local cuisine, Jacksonville offers something for every kind of traveler. So pack your bags and head to Jacksonville for an enriching and memorable experience.
