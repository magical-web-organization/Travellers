Introduction to Plymouth

Plymouth, a small unincorporated community in Western Illinois, offers visitors a chance to experience the charm of rural America. Located in McDonough County, Plymouth boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and a thriving cultural scene. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or a place to unwind, Plymouth has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Plymouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The following are some of the culturally important attractions that Plymouth has to offer:

  • The Plymouth Historical Society Museum: This museum is home to fascinating artifacts and memorabilia from Plymouth’s past. Visitors can learn about the town’s early settlers, the railroads that helped shape its growth, and its role in the region’s agricultural industry.
  • The Old School House Museum: This historic schoolhouse has been lovingly restored and now houses a collection of vintage children’s books, toys, and games. Visitors can step back in time and experience a glimpse of education from a bygone era.
  • Plymouth Rock Memorial: This landmark is a tribute to the Pilgrims who founded the Plymouth Colony in Massachusetts. The monument was erected in the late 1800s and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Plymouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures and scenic views, Plymouth has plenty to offer. Check out these natural attractions:

  • The La Moine River: The river is a great spot for fishing, boating, and birdwatching. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful picnic while taking in the stunning scenery of the La Moine River Valley.
  • Argyle Lake State Park: This 1,700-acre park offers hiking, fishing, camping, and boating opportunities. Argyle Lake is a popular spot for anglers looking to catch crappie, bass, and catfish.
  • The Mississippi River: The historic Mississippi River is just a short drive from Plymouth. Visitors can take a scenic drive along its banks or hop on a riverboat for a unique perspective of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plymouth

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast looking to explore Plymouth on foot or by bike, check out these popular routes:

  • The Macomb/McDonough County Bicycle Trail: This scenic trail follows a former railway through picturesque woodlands and farmland, offering a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.
  • The Argyle Lake State Park Trail System: Visitors can choose from several hiking trails that wind through the park’s different habitats, including grasslands, wetlands, and forests. The trails are well-marked and offer varying degrees of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Plymouth: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Plymouth:

  • Take a Tour of the La Moine River Valley: Visitors can sign up for guided tours of the La Moine River Valley, which offer a chance to see some of the area’s most beautiful scenery while learning about its history and ecology.
  • Visit the Amish Community: The Amish community in nearby Macomb offers a unique glimpse into a way of life that has remained largely unchanged for centuries. Visitors can tour Amish farms and purchase traditional handicrafts.
  • Explore the Macomb Farmers Market: The Macomb Farmers Market is held every Saturday during the summer and fall. Visitors can sample local produce, purchase artisanal goods, and enjoy live music and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plymouth

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to Plymouth:

Day 1 Visit the Plymouth Rock Memorial and the Plymouth Historical Society Museum
Day 2 Explore Argyle Lake State Park and take a hike on one of the park’s trails
Day 3 Rent a boat and spend the day fishing on the La Moine River
Day 4 Take a guided tour of the Amish community in Macomb
Day 5 Visit the Old School House Museum and the Macomb Farmers Market
Day 6 Drive along the Mississippi River and stop at scenic overlooks
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Plymouth at a leisurely pace

Food and Drink Scene of Plymouth

Must-Try Dishes

Plymouth offers visitors a taste of traditional Midwest cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Corn on the Cob: Sweet corn is a staple of Midwestern cuisine, and Plymouth is no exception. Be sure to try some freshly picked corn on the cob, which is a popular summer treat.
  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: Pork tenderloin sandwiches are a favorite among locals, and Plymouth boasts several worthy contenders for the title of “best sandwich in town”.
  • Deep Dish Pizza: Chicago may be famous for its deep-dish pizza, but that doesn’t mean you can’t find a great slice in Plymouth. Check out some of the local pizzerias for a hearty meal that will satisfy any appetite.

Local Beverages

Plymouth has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries offering unique brews. Visitors can sample locally brewed beers at the following establishments:

  • Forgotten Hollow Brewing Co.: This family-owned brewery is located in nearby Macomb and serves up a rotating selection of artisanal brews.
  • Rushville Brewing Company: This brewery, located just a short drive from Plymouth, offers a cozy atmosphere and a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Plymouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While there are no hotels or hostels located in Plymouth itself, visitors can find plenty of comfortable accommodations in nearby towns. Here are some options:

  • The Hampton Inn Macomb: This hotel is perfect for visitors looking for a comfortable, convenient base from which to explore the area. Amenities include an indoor pool, fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Wesley Village Guest Suites: These charming guest suites are located in nearby Macomb and offer visitors a home away from home, with comfortable furnishings and all the amenities of a hotel.
  • The Wilderness Retreat Bed & Breakfast: Located just a short drive from Plymouth, this cozy bed and breakfast offers visitors a chance to unwind and enjoy the natural beauty of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Plymouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Plymouth, consider the following souvenirs:

  • Amish Handicrafts: The Amish community in nearby Macomb produces a variety of handmade crafts, including quilts, baskets, and wooden toys.
  • Sweet Corn: Plymouth is known for its fresh, sweet corn that is available during the summer months. Consider taking home some ears for a taste of the local cuisine.
  • Local Beer: If you’re a fan of craft beer, consider picking up a few bottles of locally brewed beer to enjoy back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Plymouth

Plymouth, like many rural communities, places a high value on family, community, and hard work. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly and welcoming locals who take pride in their town and its history. The Amish community in nearby Macomb maintains a traditional way of life that is unique to this part of the country, and visitors should be respectful of their customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Plymouth

The best time to visit Plymouth depends on your interests. If you’re looking for outdoor adventures and scenic views, the warmer months of spring, summer, and fall are ideal. Visitors who want to experience the area’s natural beauty in the winter can enjoy cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plymouth

Do:

  • Do explore the La Moine River Valley
  • Do try some of the local craft beers
  • Do attend the Macomb Farmers Market

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the customs of the Amish community
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural habitats of the area’s wildlife
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray

Safety and Health

Safety in Plymouth

Plymouth is generally a safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise caution when exploring natural areas, watch for potential hazards like uneven terrain or slippery rocks, and be mindful of weather conditions.

Health in Plymouth

Visitors to Plymouth should take precautions to stay healthy, including staying hydrated, using insect repellent to prevent tick and mosquito bites, and practicing safe food handling techniques to prevent illness.

Getting to Plymouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plymouth

The nearest airport to Plymouth is the Greater Peoria Regional Airport, located approximately 70 miles to the southeast.

Bus Stations Near Plymouth

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus station in nearby Macomb, approximately 12 miles from Plymouth.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Plymouth via car, with several major highways passing through the area. The nearest major city is Peoria, approximately 70 miles to the southeast.

How to Get Around in Plymouth

Visitors will need a car to travel around Plymouth and the surrounding area, as there is no public transportation available.

Where to Go Next from Plymouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Illinois, consider the following nearby destinations:

  • Springfield: The Illinois state capital is home to numerous historic sites, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • Chicago: The Windy City is just a few hours’ drive from Plymouth and offers endless opportunities for sightseeing, dining, and entertainment.
  • Galena: This charming town in northwestern Illinois offers visitors a chance to step back in time and experience the historic architecture and quaint charm of a bygone era.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking to relax and unwind, Plymouth, Illinois has something for everyone. With its natural beauty, rich cultural scene, and friendly locals, Plymouth is the perfect destination for anyone looking for an authentic taste of rural America.
