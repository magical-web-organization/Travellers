Introduction to Moline

Located in Western Illinois, Moline is a city in Rock Island County. With a population of over 42,000 residents, Moline sits on the Mississippi River and is home to numerous museums, outdoor activities, and cultural institutions. Founded in the mid-19th century, the city has a rich history, diverse population, and bustling economy. In this comprehensive guide, we’ve put together everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Moline.

List of Places to Visit in Moline

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

John Deere Pavilion

The John Deere Pavilion is an interactive museum where visitors can learn about the history of the iconic John Deere company. Featuring over 100 pieces of equipment, exhibits showcase everything from the history of John Deere’s first plow to innovative agricultural technology in use today. Admission is free, and the museum is open year-round.

Deere-Wiman House

Located in Moline’s historic Highland Park neighborhood, the Deere-Wiman House is a historic mansion built in the 1870s. Featuring ornate architecture and perfectly maintained grounds, the museum provides visitors with a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of the early 20th century.

Quad City Botanical Center

The Quad City Botanical Center is a 3.5-acre garden oasis in the heart of Moline. Designed to showcase the beauty and diversity of plants, visitors can explore a wide variety of themed gardens and exhibits. Highlights include a butterfly habitat, dwarf conifer garden, and herb garden.

Natural Attractions of Moline: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Millennium Park

Located on the Mississippi River, Millennium Park is a 80-acre park that features a wide range of outdoor activities such as fishing, skating, bike trails and amazing scenic views. During the summer months, visitors can enjoy live music and other outdoor events.

Great River Trail

The Great River Trail is a 60-mile biking and hiking trail that runs along the Mississippi River. Visitors can enjoy a scenic ride or walk and explore numerous towns along the way. A local bike rental shop is available and cars can be left at any of the nearby parking lots.

Rock Island Arsenal

The Rock Island Arsenal is the largest government-owned weapons manufacturer in the United States. Visitors can tour the facility, learn about the history of the arsenal’s role in defending the nation, and explore the museum. The Arsenal is open year-round.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moline

Moline has many beautiful hiking and biking trails. From the Great River Trail to the community’s parks, there is something for everyone. Additionally, the city has bike rental shops that offer rentals and guided tours.

Best Things to Do in Moline: Activities and Tours

Mississippi River Cruises

A Mississippi River Cruise is the perfect way to enjoy the river and see Moline from a unique perspective. These tours range from simple hour-long excursions to full-day adventures with food and drinks provided. Visitors can experience life on the river and see stunning scenery.

Putnam Museum and Science Center

The Putnam Museum and Science Center is the largest museum in the Quad Cities. The Putnam has numerous permanent exhibits featuring scientific and cultural artifacts, as well as rotating exhibits. With interactive exhibits for adults and kids, this is an amazing family experience.

## Week-Long Itinerary for Moline

Day 1: Visit the John Deere Pavilion and learn about the history of John Deere Day 2: Explore the Rock Island Arsenal and enjoy the museum surrounded by scenic views. Day 3: Take a day cruise on the Mississippi River and enjoy the breathtaking views. Day 4: Visit the Quad City Botanical Center and stroll through the beautiful gardens. Day 5: Explore the Deere-Wiman House and learn about the history of the mansion. Day 6: Spend the day bike riding or hiking on the Great River Trail. Food and Drink Scene of Moline

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a staple dish in Moline, and one that a trip to the city wouldn’t be complete without tasting. Local favorites such as “Whitey’s” and “Huck’s” serve up crispy, juicy chicken and comfort food all day long.

Taco Pizza

A unique Midwestern dish, taco pizza consists of a classic pizza base, topped with seasoned ground beef, refried beans, lettuce, and tomatoes. Get it to-go or sit-in at one of the many pizzerias and try this local favorite.

Local Beverages

Craft beer

Moline has a great craft beer scene, with several breweries producing amazing ales in various flavors. Blue Cat Brewery and Bent River Brewing Company are two popular options. Augustana Brewery is another great option, as it’s student-run, and has great craft beers.

Where to Stay in Moline: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous options for accommodations in Moline, including hotels, motels, and guesthouses. The Deere-Wiman House offers bed-and-breakfast options for those seeking a unique and historical experience. Additionally, travelers can find all the major hotel chains in the downtown Moline area

What to Buy in Moline: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moline has a diverse shopping scene, with options ranging from major retail stores to local boutiques and markets. For souvenirs, visit Deere & Company World Headquarters, which has a large gift shop filled with John Deere memorabilia and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Moline

Moline residents are proud of their heritage and welcoming to visitors. The friendly and welcoming nature of the community is a testament to their commitment to hospitality and Midwest values.

Best Time to Visit Moline

Moline is a great year-round destination. The summer, particularly June through August, offers nice weather and many outdoor activities. During the winter, indoor activities, like museum tours, and specialty festivals throughout all seasons also make for great experiences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moline

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Moline.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and outdoor spaces.
  • Don’t be intrusive or inconsiderate of private property.
  • Don’t skip out on outdoor experiences throughout all seasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moline

Moline is a generally safe city. However, it is always recommended that visitors take the necessary precautions regarding personal safety and property. Take care of your belongings and lock your car when it’s unoccupied. Respect private property and avoid explaining too much personal information to strangers.

Health in Moline

Medical facilities in Moline provide quality healthcare. In case of an emergency, visitors can find several hospitals and clinics within close proximity.

Getting to Moline: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Moline

The Quad City International Airport services Moline and the surrounding areas. The airport serves several major airlines, and travelers can easily find scheduled flights in and out of Moline.

Bus Stations Near Moline

Moline is accessible by Greyhound buses that operate in the area. Multiple bus lines make a stop in downtown Moline daily.

Other Options

Driving is highly encouraged as it allows for more flexibility during a trip to Moline. While rideshare and taxi options are available, like Uber and Lyft, car rentals are a great choice to have more freedom in exploring the city.

How to Get Around in Moline

Moline is relatively easy to navigate thanks to its extensive public transportation system. The MetroLINK is Moline’s public transportation provider. Travelers can also rent bicycles or cars for added convenience.

Where to Go Next from Moline: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Moline is located in a beautiful part of the country and is surrounded by many compelling destinations. Try visiting the Black Hawk State Historic Site in nearby Rock Island. The Niabi Zoo, Hauberg Civic Center, and the Quad City Symphony Orchestra are also popular options for trips.

Conclusion

Moline is a charming city that offers a diverse range of experiences for visitors. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and fantastic cuisine, Moline has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Moline safely and effectively, and make the most out of your time in this charming Midwestern city.
