Introduction to Driftless Area

Tucked away in the Midwest region of the United States, the Driftless Area is a hidden gem for travelers looking for a unique adventure. This region covers four states- spanning throughout southwestern Wisconsin, northeastern Iowa, southeastern Minnesota, and extreme northwestern Illinois. It is a stunning and unique geological wonder that is unlike anywhere else in the country.

The Driftless Area is named so because it was missed by the last glaciers that carved much of the landscape of the Midwest. It has steep cliffs, rolling hills, and rocky outcroppings with many rivers snaking through the valleys. It’s perfect for exploring the outdoors, relaxing in the tranquillity of the countryside or immersing yourself in local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Driftless Area

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There is a rich cultural heritage to explore in Driftless Area. A few places you must visit are:

  • Vesterheim Norwegian-American Museum (Decorah, IA): A world-renowned museum that celebrates the Norwegian-American heritage.
  • Hamburger Museum of America (Seymour, WI): A museum exclusively dedicated to America’s favourite food, the hamburger.
  • Historical Fort Snelling (St. Paul, MN): A place steeped in history, visit this historic site to discover Minnesotan and American history.

Natural Attractions of Driftless Area: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Famous natural attractions in the Driftless Area include:

  • Effigy Mounds National Monument (Harpers Ferry, IA): Home to impressive and unique Native American earthworks.
  • Pikes Peak State Park (McGregor,Iowa): A beautiful state park with unforgettable hiking trails and great views of the Mississippi River.
  • Driftless Area Scenic Byway (WI-35 along the Mississippi River): Drive through the beauty of the Driftless Area with stunning views of the Mississippi River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Driftless Area

Driftless Area is famous for its scenic hiking and biking trails. A few of the best ones include:

  • Great River State Trail (WI-MN): A 24-mile bike trail filled with scenic beauty, it offers opportunities to stop and explore quintessential small river towns.
  • Kickapoo Valley Reserve (near La Crosse, WI): With more than 25 miles of scenic trails, this reserve is a nature lover’s paradise.
  • Maiden Rock Bluff State Natural Area (near Stockholm, WI): This trail offers stunning views of the Mississippi river passing through Wisconsin and MInnesota.

Best Things to Do in Driftless Area: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, the Driftless Area has a diverse range of activities worth exploring:

  • Wine and Cheese Tour (Vines and Rushes Winery, Ripon, WI): A delicious tour that takes you through some of the Driftless Area’s famous wineries and creameries.
  • Trout fishing (several spots along the Driftless Area): The region’s steep, winding streams offer some of the best trout fishing in the country, making it a popular destination for anglers.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride (Galena, IL): Get a bird’s eye view of this miraculous landscape with a Hot Air Balloon ride.

  • Pasties (Emma Krumbee’s Bakery, Shakopee, MN): These turnovers are a traditional meal of seasoned meat and veggies wrapped in pastry.
  • Cheese Curds (Culvers, and other diners throughout the Driftless Area): Deep-fried and delicious cheese snacks.

Local Beverages

There are many great places to have a drink in Driftless area, but make sure you try these local beverages:
