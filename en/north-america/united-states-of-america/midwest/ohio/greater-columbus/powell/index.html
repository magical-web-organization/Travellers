Powell is nestled in Greater Columbus, located in the Midwest region of the United States. This charming city boasts of various natural and cultural attractions, making it a hidden gem in Ohio. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a place to unwind, Powell has something in store for you.

Introduction to Powell

Powell was founded in 1801, but it wasn’t until 1947 that it was officially recognized as a city. Today, it has grown into a thriving community, known for its parks, museums, and lively events. It’s also home to several restaurants and cafes that cater to locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Powell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Olentangy Indian Caverns - A historic natural landmark that takes visitors on a tour of the caverns, which served as a shelter for indigenous people thousands of years ago.
  2. Worthington Historical Society - Discover the rich history of the city in the Worthington Historical Society’s Museum.
  3. Powell Liberty Historical Society - Learn about Powell’s history, from its founding to modern times through the artifacts displayed in Powell Liberty Historical Society.

Natural Attractions of Powell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Highbanks Metro Park - Hike, bike or take a leisurely stroll on one of the many trails this park offers.
  2. Slate Run Metro Park - Experience farm life with a visit to Slate Run Farm, a living history museum.
  3. The Columbus Zoo and Aquarium - Enjoy an animal safari in the heart of Powell. Columbus Zoo and Aquarium is home to more than 10,000 animals across 600 species!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Powell

  1. Olentangy Trail - Covers 22 miles from downtown Columbus to Worthington. Ideal for walking, jogging, biking, or rollerblading.
  2. Alum Creek State Park - Offers bike trails and a beach. Also great for kayaking and fishing.

Best Things to Do in Powell: Activities and Tours

  1. Balloon Rides - Take a hot air balloon tour and get a bird’s eye view of Powell’s lush green scenery.
  2. Wine Tasting Tour - Savor locally made wines while visiting Powell’s vineyards and picturesque wineries.
  3. Golfing - Powell has several world-class golf courses like The Club of Tartan Fields, and Kinsale Golf and Fitness Club.

Food and Drink Scene of Powell

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ice Cream at Graeter’s - The Graeter’s grand sundae, black raspberry chip ice cream topped with whipped cream and cherry on top, is a classic favorite in Powell.
  2. Pizza at Sawmill Pizza - Made from scratch, this pizzeria’s pies come in many flavor varieties to indulge in.
  3. Patio Tacos at Local Cantina - Powell Local Cantina serves delicious tacos in their outdoor patio area.

Local Beverages

  1. Lineage Brewing - An award-winning local brewery that offers seasonal and limited-batch craft beers.
  2. Powell Village Winery - A family-owned boutique winery that features world-class wines made from Ohio-grown grapes and honey.

Where to Stay in Powell - Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Holiday Inn Express and Suites Columbus-Polaris Parkway - A stylish, affordable option complete with an indoor pool located near Alum Creek State Park.
  2. Cambria Hotel Columbus - Polaris - A luxurious option complete with indoor heated pool, shuttle to activities, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Powell - Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Powell Farmers Market - Shop locally made food, bread, fresh produce, and crafts while getting to know Powell’s locals.
  2. Powell Apparel and Accessories - A blend of trendy and classic local fashion made in Powell.

Local Culture and Customs of Powell

Powell festivities are a way of life in the city. They hold various fairs, festivals and parades throughout the year, which celebrate customs and culture. Powell traditions like the Fall festival, parades, and markets, provide an immersive experience to get to know the culture.

Best Time to Visit Powell

The best time to visit Powell is from September to November or from April to June when the weather is pleasant, and parks are open. Summers are busy, and winters often come with snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Powell

Do:

  1. Hike or bike through a park or trail.
  2. Attend a festival or fair to experience the local culture.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or harm local wildlife.
  2. Ignore traffic rules and signals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Powell

Powell is safe as crime rates here are low. As a tourist, you need to be careful and take precautions while out and about your travels.

Health in Powell

Powell has several health clinics and centers to assist people with health issues. Emergency care is speedy and available at medical facilities like Powell Emergency Medical Service.

Getting to Powell - Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Powell

  1. John Glenn Columbus International Airport (CMH) - 25 miles from Powell.
  2. Dayton International Airport (DAY) - 70 miles from Powell.

Bus Stations Near Powell

  1. Columbus Greyhound Bus Station - 16 miles from Powell.

Other Options

  1. Uber or Lyft ridesharing services are available in the city.
  2. Car rental services are available, or tourists can use their own car.

How to Get Around in Powell

Powell can be navigated easily by car, bike, or foot. Car rentals as available, or ride-sharing services available within the city.

Where to Go Next from Powell - Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  2. Delaware - Visit the historic downtown and enjoy outdoor activities at Alum Creek State Park.

Conclusion

Whether it’s history, natural wonders, or cultural attractions you seek, Powell provides an immersive experience of Ohio’s heritage, culture, and way of life. With its dynamic offerings of food, adventure, and entertainment, Powell is the perfect destination for a unique and unforgettable American experience.
