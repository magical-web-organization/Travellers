Dublin, a city in the Greater Columbus area of Ohio, is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With numerous museums, historical sites, parks, and biking trails, Dublin offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Dublin, as well as practical information on accommodations, food, transportation, safety, and local culture.

Introduction to Dublin

Dublin is a vibrant city surrounded by natural beauty. It’s known for its Irish heritage, picturesque parks, and scenic biking trails. As a part of Greater Columbus in the Midwest region of the United States, Dublin offers numerous cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Dublin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dublin’s rich history can be seen in its many museums and historical sites. The following are some must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Dublin Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of Dublin from its early settlement in the 1800s to present day. Visitors can tour the exhibits and participate in guided tours.

  • Indian Run Falls: This natural wonder offers an interesting look into the Native American history of the area.

  • The Ohio State University Stadium: This football stadium is one of the largest and most iconic in the country.

Natural Attractions of Dublin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dublin has several parks and scenic trails where visitors can get close to nature. Below are some of the best outdoor adventures in Dublin:

  • Indian Run Falls Park: This park features a waterfall, creeks, and hiking trails.

  • Dublin Arts Council Gallery: This outdoor art gallery has various exhibitions throughout the year, which includes performances and events.

  • The Antique Tractor Shed: This spot is perfect for families with children and exhibits antique tractors, fire trucks, and other vintage machinery from the past.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dublin

Dublin has numerous trails and pathways for hiking and biking, where visitors can explore nature and get some exercise. The following are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Dublin:

  • Dublin Community Recreational Center Trail: this 19-mile trail takes bikers through the beautiful landscapes of Dublin.

  • Emerald Parkway Path: This 3-mile trail is perfect for novice bikers and provides magnificent views of the Scioto River.

  • Sycamore Trail: This 4.5-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking, and offers views of the Indian Run Falls.

Best Things to Do in Dublin: Activities and Tours

Dublin offers a plethora of options for visitors to spend their time. Below are the best activities and tours visitors can engage in:

  • Dublin Irish Festival: This event is the largest 3-day Irish Festival in the world, held annually in August. Visitors can enjoy Irish food, drinks and live music.

  • Dublin Village Tavern Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the historic village of Dublin and taste some delicious food and drinks made by the local chefs.

  • Ballroom Dancing: This activity is perfect for people wanting to learn dancing and enjoy an evening out.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dublin

Dublin has so much to offer that visitors may want to spend a week exploring the area. Below is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Dublin Historical Society Museum and Indian Run Falls
Day 2 Explore Indian Run Falls Park
Day 3 Tour The Ohio State University Stadium

Food and Drink Scene of Dublin

Dublin offers several culinary delights for its visitors. Below are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dublin Chicken: This dish is a local specialty that features a juicy chicken with a blend of sweet spices.

  • Irish Stew: This dish is a hearty and filling stew, full of vegetables and meat.

  • Potato Soup: This soup is a must-try when visiting Dublin, made with creamy potatoes and other flavorful ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Dublin Beer: This beer is a local specialty that is served in many local pubs and bars.

  • Irish Coffee: This coffee features a blend of two popular tastes, coffee, and whiskey.

  • Dublin Red: This wine is a must-try and features a blend of red grapes from the local vineyards.

Where to Stay in Dublin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dublin has numerous accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget to luxurious stays. Below are some of the best options:

  • The Paddock Pub: This budget-friendly accommodation is located within walking distance of the local attractions.

  • AC Hotel Dublin: This hotel offers luxurious services and has an on-site restaurant and bar.

  • Home2 Suites by Hilton Dublin: This hotel offers guests numerous amenities such as a 24-hour fitness center, complimentary shuttle service, and a heated pool.

What to Buy in Dublin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dublin has a plethora of shops and boutiques selling local products such as hand-crafted jewelry and unique paper products. Below are some of the best places to shop for souvenirs:

  • Dublin Arts Council Gallery: This outdoor art gallery sells unique hand-crafted jewellery, paper products, and pottery.

  • Snotra House Studio: This art studio features paintings, sculptures, and other works of art made by local artists.

  • Green Door Studio: This studio sells handmade items such as candles, soap, and skincare products.

Local Culture and Customs of Dublin

Dublin has a rich and vibrant culture that visitors can experience during their stay. Below are some cultural norms visitors should be aware of:

  • People in Dublin are friendly and welcoming, so visitors should be prepared to meet new people.

  • Dublineers are fiercely proud of their Irish heritage and are deeply connected to their local community.

Best Time to Visit Dublin

The best time to visit Dublin is from June to September when the weather is pleasant, and the parks and outdoor areas are in full bloom. The summers in Dublin are comfortable, with temperatures ranging from 70s° to 80s°F range.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dublin

Do:

  • Do visit the historic sites and monuments present in Dublin.

  • Do try the local drinks and dishes such as Dublin red, Dublin beer, Irish Coffee, and Dublin chicken.

  • Do explore Dublin’s beautiful biking and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and always clean up after yourself.

  • Don’t leave anything visible in your car, as petty theft may occur.

  • Don’t be disrespectful of the native people and their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dublin

Dublin is a relatively safe city, with low levels of crime, so visitors should not worry about their safety. It is, however, recommended that visitors take certain precautions:

  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.

  • Know where you are going before you leave and always be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Dublin

Visitors may encounter seasonal allergies, but otherwise, there are no significant health issues to be concerned about in Dublin.

Getting to Dublin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dublin is located near Columbus, Ohio, and can be accessed via multiple transportation options:

Airports Near Dublin

  • John Glenn Columbus International Airport: This airport is located approximately 20 miles from Dublin.

  • Rickenbacker International Airport: This airport is located approximately 35 miles from Dublin.

Bus Stations Near Dublin

  • Greyhound: This bus service is available in Columbus, Ohio and can provide transportation to Dublin.

  • Megabus: This bus service is available in Columbus, Ohio as well and can also provide transportation to Dublin.

Other Options

  • Train: There is no direct train service to Dublin, but visitors can take a train to the nearby town of Columbus and arrive in Dublin by bus.

  • Car: Visitors can choose to rent a car and drive to Dublin; roads are well-maintained and easy to navigate.

How to Get Around in Dublin

Dublin offers various transportation options for visitors, including buses, taxis, and car rentals. The city’s bike-friendly layout also makes it easy for visitors to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Dublin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dublin’s location in the Greater Columbus area provides visitors with numerous opportunities to explore nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Columbus, Ohio: This city is just a 20-minute drive from Dublin and is known for its art, music, and culinary delights.

  • The Columbus Zoo and Aquarium: Just a 15-minute drive from Dublin, this zoo is one of the largest and best in the country.

  • The Hocking Hills State Park: Just a one-hour drive from Dublin, this state park is a lush and scenic wonderland with hiking trails, waterfalls, and spectacular views.

Conclusion

Dublin, Ohio, is an exciting destination with a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and year-round outdoor adventures. Visitors can experience the best of both worlds in Dublin, with a bustling downtown area and tranquil parklands. By using this tour guide, visitors will be able to make the most out of their stay in Dublin, experiencing all the fascinating things that this city has to offer.
