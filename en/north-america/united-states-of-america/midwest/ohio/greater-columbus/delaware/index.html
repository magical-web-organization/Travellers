Introduction to Delaware

Located in Greater Columbus, in the Midwest region of the United States of America, Delaware is a small but charming city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning landscapes, well-preserved historical landmarks, museums, galleries, and delicious cuisine, Delaware is a perfect destination for adventure-seekers and history buffs alike. In this travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Delaware

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Delaware County Historical Society

One of the top cultural attractions in Delaware is the Delaware County Historical Society. Established in 1947, the society is dedicated to preserving the history of Delaware County and provides visitors with a chance to explore the county’s rich history. The society operates several museums and historical sites, including the Nash House Museum, the Meeker Homestead Museum, and the Cryder Historical Center.

The Arts Castle

If you are looking for a cultural experience in Delaware, the Arts Castle is a must-visit destination. The Arts Castle is a local art center and gallery that showcases the works of local artists. The center offers a range of art classes and workshops for visitors of all ages and is a great place to explore and appreciate local art.

Natural Attractions of Delaware: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alum Creek State Park

For outdoor enthusiasts, Alum Creek State Park is a popular attraction in Delaware. The park is home to a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and boating. The park’s centerpiece is the Alum Creek Reservoir, which is perfect for swimming and water sports.

Delaware State Park

Another popular natural attraction in Delaware is the Delaware State Park. The park offers visitors a chance to explore the beautiful landscapes of Delaware, including pristine forests, grassy meadows, and sparkling lakes. The park is home to a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delaware

Delaware is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts with its scenic roads and trails. Some popular routes include:

Delaware Dam Loop Trail

The Delaware Dam Loop Trail is a beautiful and scenic trail that takes visitors through the Delaware State Park and offers stunning views of the Alum Creek Reservoir.

Olentangy Trail

Another popular route is the Olentangy Trail, which is a 13.7-mile paved trail that runs through Columbus, Worthington, and Delaware. The trail offers scenic views of the Olentangy River and passes through several parks and natural areas.

Best Things to Do in Delaware: Activities and Tours

Stratford Ecological Center

The Stratford Ecological Center is a popular attraction in Delaware that offers visitors a chance to learn about sustainable farming practices and ecological conservation. The center provides guided tours of the farm and offers workshops and classes on a range of topics related to sustainability.

Delaware County Fair

Another popular event in Delaware is the Delaware County Fair, which takes place every September. The fair features a range of activities, including livestock shows, horse races, live music, and carnival rides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delaware

Day 1: Explore the Delaware County Historical Society Day 2: Enjoy outdoor activities at Alum Creek State Park Day 3: Visit the Arts Castle for a cultural experience Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Stratford Ecological Center Day 5: Attend the Delaware County Fair Day 6: Take a bike ride along the Olentangy Trail Day 7: Relax at Delaware State Park

Local Culture and Customs of Delaware

Delaware has a rich cultural history, with an emphasis on local agriculture and sustainability. Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Delaware

The best time to visit Delaware is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delaware

Do:

  • Explore the historical landmarks and museums
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb natural habitats
  • Play music or make noise disturbance in residential areas
  • Smoke indoors or in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Delaware

Delaware is a safe destination, with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise caution when exploring the city at night and should lock their doors when staying in hotels.

Health in Delaware

Visitors should drink bottled water to prevent stomach illnesses, and should be up to date with their routine vaccinations before traveling to Delaware.

Getting to Delaware: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Delaware

The closest airport to Delaware is Port Columbus International Airport, which is located approximately 25 miles from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Delaware

The Greyhound bus station in Columbus is the closest bus station to Delaware, which is located approximately 20 miles from the city center.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Delaware from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Delaware

The best way to get around Delaware is by car. Visitors can also explore the city on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Delaware: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Delaware is located within driving distance of several other popular destinations, including:

  • Columbus, Ohio
  • Cleveland, Ohio
  • Indianapolis, Indiana
  • Cincinnati, Ohio

Conclusion

Delaware offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that is perfect for visitors looking for adventure and exploration. With its range of outdoor activities, historical landmarks, museums, and delicious cuisine, Delaware is a perfect destination for the entire family.
