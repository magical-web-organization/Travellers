Introduction to Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is a small city located in Knox County, Ohio, in the Midwest region of the United States. Founded in 1805, the city has a rich history that is reflected in its numerous museums, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions. Mount Vernon is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are plenty of things to see and do in Mount Vernon, whether you are traveling with family, friends or solo.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Vernon

Here’s a list of the best places to visit in Mount Vernon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Knox County Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases artifacts and documents related to the history of Knox County, including its Native American and military history.

  • Woodward Opera House: The oldest surviving opera house in the United States. Now showcases performing arts and live music shows.

  • The Knox County Veterans Memorial Building and Museum: Provides a comprehensive showcase of the military history of Knox County from 1775 to present day.

Natural Attractions of Mount Vernon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mohican State Park: Explore the winding trails through the gorges and greenery of one of Ohio’s gems, Mohican State Park.

  • Kokosing River: Go paddling along the tree-lined river or enjoy a riverside picnic with friends and family.

  • The Mount Vernon Bike Path: An ideal location for bikers, walkers or runners to relax and enjoy stunning scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Vernon

Apart from bike path, Mount Vernon is a haven for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few of the best trails:

  • The Kokosing Gap Trail: A 14-mile paved trail between Mount Vernon and Danville, popular with cyclists and horseback riders.

  • The Honey Run Highlands Park Trail: A family-friendly loop trail through the park that is popular for hiking and wildlife watching.

  • Heart of Ohio Trail: A 15-mile paved trail that stretches between Mount Vernon and Centerburg.

Best Things to Do in Mount Vernon: Activities and Tours

There are a few must-try activities in Mount Vernon, including:

  • Horseback Riding: Saddle up and explore Mount Vernon’s beautiful countryside on horseback, with a guided tour.

  • Antique Shopping: Mount Vernon’s downtown is home to several antique shops that sell furniture, art, and other unique home decor items.

  • Wine and Cheese Tastings: Visit one of the wineries near Mount Vernon and taste some of the locally produced wines, paired with artisanal cheese.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Vernon

If you plan to spend a week in Mount Vernon, here’s an itinerary that can help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Knox County Historical Society Museum and Woodward Opera House
2 Hike the Kokosing Gap Trail, followed by Wine and Cheese tasting at a local winery
3 Spend the day at Mohican State Park
4 Horseback riding and antique shopping in downtown Mount Vernon
5 Relax and explore the town on the Mount Vernon Bike Path
6 Visit the Knox County Veterans Memorial Building and Museum
7 Take a leisurely paddle down the Kokosing River

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon has an exciting food and drink scene that emphasizes the use of fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • J-Bos Pizza: A local favorite for Pizza, make sure to try their famous “Taco” pizza.

  • Oldtimers Barbecue: Enjoy delicious barbecue dishes that are prepared using locally sourced meat and vegetables.

  • Flappers Bar and Grille: An ideal place for families, the restaurant serves a range of dishes, from hamburgers to pizza.

Local Beverages

  • Kokosing River Winery: This local winery produces award-winning wines using grapes grown in the nearby vineyards.

  • The Alcove: Enjoy a wide range of locally and internationally crafted beers here with a “great outdoor” atmosphere.

  • Java Hut: Try a cup of locally roasted coffee, blended to perfection at this cozy coffee shop.

Where to Stay in Mount Vernon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mount Vernon has a range of options when it comes to accommodation, from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are a few places that you might want to consider:

  • The Mount Vernon Grand Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Mount Vernon that offers a range of luxurious amenities.

  • The Mount Vernon Inn: A cozy and welcoming inn that offers visitors a chance to explore the nearby attractions with ease.

  • Birkwood Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast that has been restored to its original splendor, with a personalized experience for each of its visitors.

What to Buy in Mount Vernon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cooper’s Mill: This local producer has a range of jams, jellies, and apple butter available for purchase.

  • The Kokosing River Honey Company: They craft beeswax candles and a variety of honey for sale.

  • Ariel Foundation Park Gift Shop: Purchase souvenirs, local products, and artwork created by local artists at the gift shop in this park.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon’s culture is steeped in its rich history and agricultural activities. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Mount Vernon

The best time to visit Mount Vernon is from June to September, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities. During this time, the town also hosts several festivals and events that are worth attending.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Vernon

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture.

  • Take care of the environment and avoid littering in natural parks and trails.

  • Visit the local wineries and breweries.

Don’t:

  • Don’t damage historic landmarks and other cultural attractions.

  • Don’t disturb the wildlife and plants in natural parks.

  • Don’t trespass on private properties.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Vernon

While Mount Vernon is generally safe, visitors should take basic precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables unattended, especially in parks or natural areas.

Health in Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is home to a few medical facilities, including the Knox Community Hospital, which provides emergency care and basic medical services.

Getting to Mount Vernon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Vernon

The nearest airport to Mount Vernon is the John Glenn Columbus International Airport, located approximately 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Mount Vernon

Greyhound Bus Line, Jefferson Lines, and Go Bus all have local stations within the county, making it easy to travel to Mount Vernon.

Other Options

If you are driving, Mount Vernon is easily accessible by car, with easy connections to interstates and state highways.

How to Get Around in Mount Vernon

To explore the town and its surrounding areas, visitors can use regular taxi services, Uber, or rent a car. Biking is also a popular option to get around Mount Vernon.

Where to Go Next from Mount Vernon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting:

  • Kenyon College: A liberal arts college located in Gambier, Ohio.

  • The Amish Country: A picturesque rural region that is home to the largest Amish community in the United States.

  • Chestnut Ridge Metro Park: Located in Ohio. It is ideal for hiking, bird watching, and horse riding.

Conclusion

Mount Vernon is an ideal destination for people looking to have a relaxed vacation that also offers outdoor activities, history, and culture. Now that you have read this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you are ready to head out and explore everything that Mount Vernon has to offer.
