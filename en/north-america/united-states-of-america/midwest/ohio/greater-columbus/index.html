Introduction to Greater Columbus

Welcome to the bustling capital city of Ohio, Greater Columbus! This vibrant metropolis is located in the Midwest region of the United States and is a great destination for history buffs, outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, and culture seekers. Columbus is a city that truly has something for everyone, from picturesque parks and hiking trails to world-renowned museums and art galleries.

List of Places to Visit in Greater Columbus

If you’re wondering where to start your exploration of Greater Columbus, here’s a list of must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ohio Statehouse: Explore Ohio’s rich political history at this stunning historical landmark, which houses the offices of the governor and the state legislature.
  • National Veterans Memorial and Museum: This remarkable museum pays tribute to the men and women who have served in the US Armed Forces and showcases their experiences through immersive exhibitions and personal stories.
  • Columbus Museum of Art: Discover an impressive collection of American and European art, as well as contemporary works by local artists.
  • Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens: This breathtaking conservatory features exotic plant species from around the world, as well as seasonal displays and special events.

Natural Attractions of Greater Columbus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scioto Mile: Take a walk along the Scioto River and enjoy stunning views of the Columbus skyline, as well as fountains, gardens, and sculptures.
  • Highbanks Metro Park: Explore the rugged landscape of this 1,200-acre park, which features towering cliffs, deep ravines, and serene forests.
  • Columbus Zoo and Aquarium: Meet over 10,000 animals from all corners of the world at this world-class zoo, which also features a water park, golf course, and zip line adventure.
  • Battelle Darby Creek Metro Park: Hike, bike, or horseback ride through this enormous park that spans over 7,000 acres, and spot wildlife like bison, coyotes, and eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greater Columbus

Are you a nature lover looking for some outdoor fun? Greater Columbus offers plenty of fantastic trails and scenic routes for hiking, biking, and running. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Olentangy Trail: This 18-mile long trail follows the Olentangy River and connects downtown Columbus to several parks and suburbs. It’s a popular route for walkers, joggers, and bikers, and offers beautiful views of the river and surrounding greenery.
  • Scioto Greenway Trail: This 9-mile long trail spans from Scioto Audubon Metro Park to the Grandview Heights neighborhood, and is ideal for runners and hikers. It features scenic overlooks, riverfront benches, and native plants and trees.
  • Alum Creek Greenway Trail: This 22-mile long trail runs through several suburbs of Columbus and features lakes, wetlands, and forests. It’s a popular route for birdwatchers and wildlife enthusiasts, and offers plenty of opportunities for fishing and kayaking.

Best Things to Do in Greater Columbus: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re into sports, arts, or history, Greater Columbus has plenty to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Ohio State University Campus Tour: Discover the stunning architecture and rich history of one of the largest universities in the US on a guided tour of the Ohio State University campus.
  • Columbus Food Tour: Sample some of the best local delicacies and artisanal products in Columbus on a guided food tour that takes you through the city’s vibrant neighborhoods.
  • Crew SC Soccer Game: Cheer on the Columbus Crew SC, the city’s professional soccer team, at a thrilling home game at the Mapfre Stadium.
  • Columbus Symphony Orchestra: Catch a world-class performance by the Columbus Symphony Orchestra, which showcases classical and contemporary music in a stunning venue.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greater Columbus

Are you planning to spend a week in Greater Columbus? Here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the best sights and experiences:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Ohio Statehouse and take a tour of the Statehouse Museum and Rotunda.
Day 2 Explore the exhibits at the Columbus Museum of Art, and then head to Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens.
Day 3 Hike or bike along the Scioto Mile and then visit the National Veterans Memorial and Museum.
Day 4 Spend the day at the Columbus Zoo and Aquarium, which features over 10,000 animals.
Day 5 Take a guided food tour of Columbus and then catch a Crew SC soccer game in the evening.
Day 6 Visit Highbanks Metro Park and then explore the quaint shops and galleries in the Short North Arts District.
Day 7 Take a day trip to Ohio’s famed Amish country, which is just an hour’s drive from Columbus.

Food and Drink Scene of Greater Columbus

Columbus is a food lover’s paradise, with a vibrant culinary scene that includes local staples, international cuisines, and artisanal products. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Candy: These sweet treats are a Columbus specialty, featuring peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate.
  • Columbus-style Pizza: This thin-crust pizza is known for its crispy edges and unique toppings, such as spicy banana peppers and white sauce.
  • Jeni’s Splendid Ice Creams: This artisanal ice cream shop offers a variety of creative flavors, such as Salted Peanut Butter with Chocolate Flecks and Wildberry Lavender.

Local Beverages

  • North High Brewing: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Watershed Distillery: This artisanal distillery produces small-batch gins, vodkas, and bourbons, and offers tours and tastings.
  • Columbus Coffee Trail: Columbus has a thriving coffee culture, with several excellent roasters and cafes. Try the espresso at Crimson Cup or the pour-over at One Line Coffee.

Where to Stay in Greater Columbus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Greater Columbus, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Westin Columbus: This historic hotel is located in the heart of downtown Columbus, and features spacious rooms and suites, as well as an award-winning restaurant.
  • The Wayfaring Buckeye Hostel: This cozy hostel is located near Ohio State University and provides affordable dorm rooms and private suites, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge.
  • German Village Guesthouse: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the picturesque German Village neighborhood, and features elegant rooms and suites, as well as a lovely garden and patio.

What to Buy in Greater Columbus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Columbus? Here are some of the best options:

  • Johnson’s Real Ice Cream: This family-owned ice cream parlor has been serving homemade ice cream since 1950, and offers over 60 flavors, as well as ice cream cakes and pies.
  • The Book Loft: This beloved independent bookstore in German Village features 32 rooms filled with new and used books, as well as music, movies, and gifts.
  • Winan’s Chocolates and Coffees: This local chocolatier and coffee roaster offers handcrafted truffles, chocolate bars, and coffee blends.

Local Culture and Customs of Greater Columbus

Columbus is a friendly and welcoming city, with a strong sense of community and a rich cultural heritage. Some things to keep in mind when visiting the city:

  • Be polite and respectful: Columbusians are known for their hospitality and friendliness, so be sure to greet people with a smile and use “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals.
  • Be open to new experiences: Greater Columbus offers a variety of cultural events and festivals throughout the year, so be sure to check the local calendar and participate in the celebrations.
  • Embrace diversity: Columbus is a diverse city, with a thriving LGBTQ+ community and a large population of international students. Be respectful of different cultures and lifestyles, and try new foods and experiences.

Best Time to Visit Greater Columbus

The best time to visit Greater Columbus depends on your interests and preferences. The city experiences four distinct seasons, with hot summers and cold winters, and has plenty of outdoor activities and indoor attractions to enjoy year-round. Here are some tips on when to visit:

  • Spring/Fall: The mild weather and colorful foliage make spring and fall ideal for outdoor activities like hiking and biking, as well as indoor attractions like museums and art galleries.
  • Summer: The warm weather and long days make summer ideal for outdoor festivals and events, such as the Columbus Arts Festival and the Ohio State Fair.
  • Winter: The cold weather and holiday festivities make winter ideal for indoor activities like shopping and ice-skating, as well as outdoor activities like sledding and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greater Columbus

To make the most of your visit to Greater Columbus, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine: Columbus is known for its eclectic culinary scene, with plenty of unique dishes and local specialties to try.
  • Do visit the museums and art galleries: Greater Columbus has an impressive cultural scene, with a variety of museums and art galleries that showcase local and international talent.
  • Do explore the great outdoors: Columbus offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and sightseeing in its many parks and wildlife areas.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip: Tipping is a customary practice in Greater Columbus, so be sure to tip your servers and tour guides accordingly.
  • Don’t be disrespectful: Columbus is a diverse and inclusive city, so be sure to be respectful of different cultures and lifestyles.
  • Don’t forget to check the weather: The weather in Greater Columbus can be unpredictable, so be sure to check the forecast and pack accordingly.

Safety and Health

Greater Columbus is a generally safe and healthy destination for travelers. However, it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of any potential risks or health concerns. Here are some tips on safety and health in Greater Columbus:

Safety in Greater Columbus

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Columbus is a safe city, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and exercise caution, especially in crowded areas and at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe: Pickpocketing and theft can occur in any city, so be sure to keep your valuables safe and secure, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Stay informed: Keep up-to-date on any local news or alerts, and follow the advice of local authorities in case of emergencies or severe weather.

Health in Greater Columbus

  • Stay hydrated: Columbus can have hot summers, so be sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially when doing outdoor activities.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: Wear sunscreen, sunglasses, and hats when doing outdoor activities, especially during peak sunlight hours.
  • Seek medical assistance when needed: If you experience any health concerns during your trip to Greater Columbus, seek medical assistance promptly, and keep your travel insurance policy information handy.

Getting to Greater Columbus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Traveling to Greater Columbus is easy and convenient, with several transportation options available, including air travel, bus, train, and car. Here’s a quick guide to getting to Greater Columbus:

Airports Near Greater Columbus

  • John Glenn Columbus International Airport (CMH): Located about 6 miles east of downtown Columbus, this airport serves over 35 airlines and offers flights to over 140 destinations.

Bus Stations Near Greater Columbus

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Columbus, this bus station offers intercity and interstate bus services to over 3,800 destinations in North America.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Columbus does not have an Amtrak station, but the closest one is in Cincinnati, which is about two hours southwest of Columbus.
  • Driving: Greater Columbus is easily accessible by car, with several major highways and interstates passing through the city, including I-70, I-71, and I-270.

How to Get Around in Greater Columbus

Getting around Greater Columbus is easy and affordable, with several transportation options available, including public transit, bike rentals, and rideshares. Here are some of the best ways to get around:

  • COTA Bus: The Central Ohio Transit Authority (COTA) operates a comprehensive bus network that serves the entire city, with fares starting at $2 per trip.
  • CoGo Bike Share: This bike rental program offers over 600 bikes at 80 stations throughout the city, with affordable hourly and daily rates.
  • Rideshares: Popular ridesharing services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in Greater Columbus, with competitive fares and reliable service.

Where to Go Next from Greater Columbus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Greater Columbus is surrounded by several exciting destinations and excursions that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Hocking Hills State Park: Located about an hour southeast of Columbus, this scenic park features waterfalls, caves, and hiking trails, as well as cabins and campsites for overnight stays.
  • Cincinnati: This vibrant city is located about two hours southwest of Columbus and offers a range of attractions, including a world-class zoo, a renowned art museum, and a thriving food and drink scene.
  • Cleveland: Located about two hours northeast of Columbus, Cleveland is a bustling city that offers a variety of cultural attractions, including the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, as well as a lively music and nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Greater Columbus is a fantastic destination, offering a wide variety of attractions, activities, and experiences for travelers of all ages and interests. From scenic hiking trails and world-class museums to artisanal food and drink, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant Midwestern city.
