Introduction to Granville

Located in the heart of Midwest in Ohio, Granville is a charming town known for its beautiful historic district, scenic hiking trails, and vibrant cultural scene. With a population of just over 5,000 people, Granville welcomes tourists year-round from nearby Columbus, as well as from all over the United States.

In this comprehensive tour guide to Granville, we will cover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat. We hope this guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Granville.

List of Places to Visit in Granville

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or just looking for a relaxing weekend getaway, Granville has something to offer for everyone. Here are some top places to visit in Granville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Granville Historical Society
  • Bryn Du Mansion
  • Robbins Hunter Museum

Natural Attractions of Granville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Denison Biological Reserve
  • Wildwood Park
  • Infirmary Mound Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Granville

Granville is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering numerous trails and routes perfect for hikers and bikers. Here are some popular hiking and biking trails in Granville:

  • The Raccoon Valley Trail
  • The Cherry Valley Trail
  • The T. J. Evans Trail

Best Things to Do in Granville: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Granville:

  • Take a guided tour of Bryn Du Mansion
  • Visit the Granville Farmers Market
  • Attend an exhibit or event at the Denison Museum of Art

Week-Long Itinerary for Granville

Day 1: Explore downtown Granville, visit the Granville Historical Society, and enjoy a meal at one of the local restaurants.

Day 2: Go for a hike or bike ride on the Raccoon Valley Trail, followed by a picnic at Wildwood Park.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of Bryn Du Mansion and visit the Denison Museum of Art.

Day 4: Spend the day at Infirmary Mound Park, enjoying the beautiful natural scenery and outdoor activities.

Day 5: Explore the town of Newark, just a short drive from Granville, and visit the Newark Earthworks.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Hocking Hills State Park, known for its stunning waterfalls and hiking trails.

Day 7: Spend the day at the Granville Farmers Market, sampling local produce and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Granville

Granville is a close-knit community that values its rich history and strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including supporting local businesses and attending community events.

Best Time to Visit Granville

The best time to visit Granville is during the fall, when the leaves change color and the weather is cool and crisp. Visitors can also enjoy the Granville Farmers Market, which runs from May through October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Granville

Do:

  • Support local businesses and attend community events.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Granville.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect historic landmarks and buildings.
  • Disrupt the peaceful and friendly atmosphere of the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Granville

Granville is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Granville

Granville has several medical facilities, including Licking Memorial Hospital and Granville Family Practice. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage during their stay in Granville.

Getting to Granville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Granville

The nearest airport to Granville is John Glenn Columbus International Airport, located about a 25-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Granville

The closest bus station to Granville is located in Newark, just a few miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Granville using major highways such as I-70 and I-71.

How to Get Around in Granville

Visitors can navigate Granville by walking or biking, or by driving. Some attractions in Granville may require a car, so visitors are encouraged to rent a car if necessary.

Where to Go Next from Granville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Granville:

  • Hocking Hills State Park
  • The Dawes Arboretum
  • Ohio State University

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive tour guide to Granville has inspired you to visit this charming Midwest town. With its beautiful natural scenery, vibrant cultural scene, and delicious local cuisine, there is no shortage of things to see and do in Granville. Safe travels!
