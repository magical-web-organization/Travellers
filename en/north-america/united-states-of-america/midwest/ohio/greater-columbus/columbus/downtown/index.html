Introduction to Downtown Columbus

Downtown Columbus, located in the heart of Ohio’s capital city, is a bustling urban hub where history blends seamlessly with modernity. With its impressive collection of museums, restaurants, shops, and outdoor spaces, there’s never a dull moment in Downtown Columbus.

Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Downtown Columbus has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit, dine, stay, and play in Downtown Columbus.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown Columbus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Downtown Columbus is home to a plethora of culturally significant attractions, including:

  • Ohio Statehouse: This historic landmark, built in the 19th century, is home to the Ohio General Assembly and the offices of the Governor and Lieutenant Governor.
  • COSI: The Center of Science and Industry is an interactive science museum that provides hands-on learning experiences for the whole family.
  • National Veterans Memorial and Museum: The NVMM is the first national museum dedicated to the experiences of American veterans of all eras.
  • Ohio History Center: A museum and research facility that explores the history and culture of Ohio through exhibits and educational programming.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re in the mood for some outdoor adventure, Downtown Columbus won’t disappoint. Some of the best natural attractions in the area include:

  • Scioto Mile: A 175-acre park that stretches along the Scioto River, featuring fountains, sculptures, bike trails, and more.
  • Bicentennial Park: Located within the Scioto Mile, this park features an interactive fountain, a performance pavilion, and a beautiful riverfront promenade.
  • Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens: Explore indoor and outdoor gardens and exhibitions spanning 88 acres, including a butterfly conservatory and a bonsai collection.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown Columbus

Biking and hiking are popular activities in Downtown Columbus, thanks to its extensive trail network. Some of the best routes include:

  • Ohio to Erie Trail: A 326-mile trail that connects Cincinnati to Cleveland, passing through Downtown Columbus along the way.
  • Scioto Trail: A 9-mile trail that meanders through the heart of Downtown Columbus, winding along the Scioto River and featuring stunning city views.

Best Things to Do in Downtown Columbus: Activities and Tours

There are countless entertaining activities and tours available in Downtown Columbus, including:

  • SegAway Tours of Columbus: Explore the city on a Segway with an expert guide.
  • Columbus Food Adventures: Sample delicious cuisine at some of Columbus’ best local restaurants.
  • The Candle Lab: Create your own scented candle in this fun, interactive workshop.

Week-Long Itinerary for Downtown Columbus

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Downtown Columbus:

Day Activities
1 Tour the Ohio Statehouse and COSI.
2 Visit the National Veterans Memorial and Museum and take a Segway tour of the city.
3 Explore the Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens, then take a Columbus Food Adventure tour.
4 Hike or bike the Scioto Trail and visit nearby attractions like the Ohio History Center.
5 Take a wine and cheese tour of local markets, then see a show at the famous Palace Theatre.
6 Go shopping in the Short North Arts District and experience the local art scene.
7 Visit the Columbus Museum of Art, then relax in Bicentennial Park and enjoy the beautiful riverfront views.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown Columbus

Must-Try Dishes

Columbus’ food scene is diverse and delicious. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Pierogies: Polish-style dumplings filled with potato, cheese, and other savory ingredients.
  • Buckeye Candy: Peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate, resembling the Ohio state tree’s nuts.
  • Jeni’s Splendid Ice Cream: Artisanal ice cream made from the freshest ingredients, with unique flavors like Salty Caramel and Sweet Cream Biscuits & Peach Jam.

Local Beverages

Columbus is also known for its craft beer, with such breweries as Seventh Son and Land-Grant creating unique concoctions. Don’t miss out on trying:

  • Columbus Brewing Company: Try their popular IPA, Bodhi, or one of their other seasonal brews.
  • Watershed Distillery: Taste distinctive gin and other spirits prepared with local ingredients as they have their own distillery.

Where to Stay in Downtown Columbus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown Columbus has a variety of accommodation options suitable for different budgets, including:

  • The Westin Columbus: A luxury hotel located in a beautifully restored historic building.
  • Drury Inn & Suites Columbus Convention Center: A mid-range hotel with modern amenities and a great central location.
  • Wayfaring Buckeye Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel offering affordable private rooms as well as dorm-style accommodations.

What to Buy in Downtown Columbus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect gift or souvenir to remember your time in Downtown Columbus? Some great options include:

  • Anthony-Thomas Chocolates: Take home a box or two of delicious, locally-made chocolates from this Columbus-based company.
  • Short North Arts District: Browse through boutiques, galleries, and shops selling unique, handmade items made by local artists and artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown Columbus

Columbus is known for its friendly and welcoming culture, its residents known as “Buckeyes” and Ohio State University’s cultural hotspots. While there are no quaint Ohioan customs to be aware of, it is always polite to say “Hi” when bumping into someone on the trails or at convenience stores.

Best Time to Visit Downtown Columbus

Summer (June-August) is the most popular time to visit Downtown Columbus, but spring and fall can offer smaller crowds and milder weather. Winter (December-February) offers more affordable pricing; the city’s decorated streets excel in spreading holiday cheer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown Columbus

Do:

  • Explore the Scioto Mile and enjoy the beautiful scenic views.
  • Visit a brewery or distillery and try some of the unique, locally brewed beverages.
  • Attend a Columbus Blue Jackets hockey game or a BalletMet Columbus performance.

Don’t:

  • Jaywalk; there are plenty of crosswalks provided to legally cross the street.
  • Stand in the bike lanes while enjoying outdoor spaces such as the Scioto Mile.
  • Overlook the incredible museums located in downtown.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown Columbus

Downtown Columbus is generally safe, but it is always a good idea to take precautions such as being aware of your surroundings at all times, avoiding deserted areas at night, and keeping your valuables secure.

Health in Downtown Columbus

There are several hospitals located within or around Downtown Columbus, including the Ohio State University Wexner Medical Center.

Getting to Downtown Columbus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown Columbus

  • John Glenn Columbus International Airport: The airport resides eight miles east of Downtown Columbus honoring John Glenn.

Bus Stations Near Downtown Columbus

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: With lines headed to Chicago and Pittsburgh among others.
  • Megabus: With lines headed to other major cities such as Cincinnati and Detroit among others.

Other Options

Allianz Transportation Services provides shuttle service to all nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Downtown Columbus

Columbus has an easy and affordable public transportation system known as the Central Ohio Transit Authority (COTA) that charges $2 per ride, including transfers. With their mobile application, buying tickets is easier than ever.

Where to Go Next from Downtown Columbus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some great options for day trips or excursions from Downtown Columbus include:

  • Hocking Hills State Park: An hour’s drive south of the city, where the visitors can spend time hiking or viewing waterfalls.
  • Ohio Amish Country: An hour, 15-minute drive southeast of the city with horse-drawn wagon tours and artisanal shops.
  • Cedar Point: Two and a half hours drive to the north, with award-winning roller coasters and beach-side resorts.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Downtown Columbus is an energetic and engaging city, offering a plethora of things to see and do for visitors of all ages and interests. With its rich cultural, historical, and artistic offerings, natural outdoor features, and delicious cuisine, Downtown Columbus will surprise and delight first-time visitors, while also keeping locals entertained time and time again.
