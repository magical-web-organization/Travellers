Introduction to Northwest Ohio

Located in the Midwest region in the United States of America, Northwest Ohio is a hidden gem that offers a rich blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. Composed of thirteen counties, this region boasts of friendly people, bustling cities, remarkable museums, diverse wildlife, and scenic landscapes.

Whether you’re a fan of outdoor thrills, art, history, or culture, Northwest Ohio has something for everyone. In this guide, we introduce you to some of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest Ohio

From museums to natural parks, Northwest Ohio has a lot to offer visitors. Below are some of the must-visit attractions in the region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Toledo Museum of Art: This museum is one of the best in the country, featuring over 30,000 works of art from around the world. It has an extensive collection of glass art, paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts. Visitors can also enjoy art classes, films, music performances, and lectures.

  • National Museum of the Great Lakes: Located in Toledo, this museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the history of the Great Lakes region. It features exhibits on maritime history, shipwrecks, and Great Lakes ecology.

  • Fort Meigs State Memorial: This historical landmark is a must-visit for history buffs. It’s the largest reconstructed wooden-walled fort in the United States, built during the War of 1812. Visitors can explore the barracks, blockhouses, and storehouses, and watch reenactments and demonstrations.

Natural Attractions of Northwest Ohio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maumee Bay State Park: This park offers breathtaking views of Lake Erie and a wide range of outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, wildlife watching, and camping. The park also has a lodge, cabins, and a golf course.

  • Oak Openings Preserve: This 7,000-acre nature preserve boasts of diverse ecosystems, including wetlands, forests, and sand dunes. Visitors can hike, bike, horseback ride, and birdwatch. The preserve also features the “Skyway,” a 100-foot tower that offers panoramic views of the park.

  • Wildwood Preserve Metropark: This urban park offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. It has over 500 acres of forests, meadows, and streams, and offers hiking and biking trails, picnicking areas, and nature programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest Ohio

Northwest Ohio is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails that offer stunning views of nature. Below are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region.

  • Wabash Cannonball Trail: This 63-mile trail is the longest continuous rail-trail in Ohio. It runs through several counties in Northwest Ohio and offers scenic views of nature and small towns.

  • Miami and Erie Canal Towpath Trail: This 47-mile trail follows the Miami and Erie Canal and offers a glimpse into the region’s industrial past. Visitors can see canal locks, aqueducts, and historic landmarks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Northwest Ohio: Activities and Tours

From sightseeing to adventure, Northwest Ohio offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Below are some of the best things to do in the region.

  • Cedar Point Amusement Park: Located in Sandusky, Cedar Point is known as the “roller coaster capital of the world.” It boasts of over 70 rides, including 18 roller coasters.

  • Put-in-Bay: This small island in Lake Erie is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and water sports. Visitors can also explore the historic sites, restaurants, and bars on the island.

  • Sauder Village: This living history village in Archbold transports visitors back in time to the 19th century. It features historic buildings, craft demonstrations, and hands-on activities for visitors of all ages.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest Ohio

Northwest Ohio’s food and drink scene is as diverse as its attractions. From hearty American fare to international cuisine, the region has something for every palate. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Northwest Ohio.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Candy: This Ohioan dessert is made with peanut butter and chocolate and is named after the state tree, the buckeye.

  • Pierogies: This Polish dumpling is popular in the region, and can be found in many local restaurants and festivals.

  • Walleye: This freshwater fish is a delicacy in Northwest Ohio and is commonly served fried or grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Great Lakes Brewing Company: This Cleveland-based brewery is one of the best in the country, featuring a wide range of craft beers, including seasonal and limited edition varieties.

  • Toledo Spirits Company: This distillery in Toledo offers a unique range of spirits, including bourbon, vodka, and gin, sourced from local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Northwest Ohio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses, Northwest Ohio has a wide range of accommodations to choose from. Below are some of the best places to stay in the region.

  • The Grand Plaza Hotel: This historic hotel in Toledo offers luxury accommodations, top-notch service, and breathtaking views of the city and the river.

  • Maumee Bay Lodge and Conference Center: This state park lodge offers comfortable rooms, a golf course, and easy access to Maumee Bay State Park.

  • Holiday Inn Toledo-Maumee: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and easy access to the area’s attractions.

What to Buy in Northwest Ohio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for unique souvenirs or local products to bring home, Northwest Ohio has plenty of options. Below are some of the best places to shop in the region.

  • Toledo Farmers’ Market: This indoor market offers a wide range of fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts from local vendors.

  • Ohio Artisan Collective: This shop in Perrysburg features a wide range of locally-made artisan products, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest Ohio

Northwest Ohio is known for its friendly and welcoming people, who take pride in their rich culture and heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, and to be mindful of others.

Best Time to Visit Northwest Ohio

The best time to visit Northwest Ohio is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest Ohio

To make the most of your trip to Northwest Ohio, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind.

Do:

  • Do visit the local museums and historical landmarks to learn about the region’s rich history and culture.

  • Do try the local food and drink, including the fresh produce and craft beers.

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions and be mindful of others.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural landscapes and wildlife.

  • Don’t be disrespectful or insensitive to the local community.

  • Don’t forget to pack for the weather and plan accordingly for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwest Ohio

Northwest Ohio is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors are advised to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Be aware of your surroundings, keep your valuables secured, and avoid leaving your car or belongings unattended.

Health in Northwest Ohio

Visitors to Northwest Ohio should be aware of the risks of tick-borne illnesses, including Lyme disease and Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever. Be sure to wear protective clothing, use insect repellent, and check for ticks after spending time outdoors.
