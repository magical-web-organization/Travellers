Introduction to Reynoldsburg

Reynoldsburg is a charming city in Greater Columbus, Ohio, with a rich history of cultural landmarks and natural attractions. Located in the Midwest region of the United States, Reynoldsburg is a peaceful retreat for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and families alike.

In this comprehensive tour guide for Reynoldsburg, you will discover the top places to visit, things to do, and the local culture and customs. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or relaxing vacation, Reynoldsburg will not disappoint.

List of Places to Visit in Reynoldsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Reynoldsburg Truro Historical Society - Take a trip back in time and learn about the history of Reynoldsburg and Truro Township through engaging exhibits, artifacts, and guided tours.
  • Elliott-Hudson Homestead Museum - Discover the life of a 19th-century farm family at this museum, which features a restored farmhouse and outbuildings.
  • Ohio History Connection - Immerse yourself in Ohio’s history and culture at this museum, which features a vast collection of artifacts, exhibitions, and programs.

Natural Attractions of Reynoldsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blacklick Woods Metro Park - Go hiking, biking, or fishing at this scenic park, which features pristine forests, wetlands, and open fields.
  • Pine Quarry Park - Enjoy a day out with your family at this multi-use park, with playgrounds, walking trails, picnic areas, and ballfields.
  • Raider Park - Take a stroll or play a game at this beautiful park, which features trails, a playground, and basketball courts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reynoldsburg

  • Blacklick Creek Trail - Enjoy a walk or bike ride on this scenic trail, which meanders along the banks of Blacklick Creek and offers stunning views of the woods and meadows.
  • Pond Trail - Take a hike through this easy-to-moderate trail, which features a pond and a wooded area.
  • Pickerington Ponds Wetland Preserve - Go on a bird-watching adventure at this beautiful preserve, which includes several hiking trails of varying difficulty levels.

Best Things to Do in Reynoldsburg: Activities and Tours

  • Reynoldsburg Farmers Market - Shop for fresh produce and artisanal products at this vibrant farmers market, which runs from May to October.
  • R Adventure Park - Have an adrenaline-rushing experience at this adventure park, which features zipline and ATV tours, kayaking, and paintball.
  • Marcus Theatres - Watch the latest blockbuster movies in style at this modern theater, which has plush seats, a gourmet snack bar, and IMAX screens.

Food and Drink Scene of Reynoldsburg

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ohio Farmhouse Breakfast - Indulge in a hearty breakfast of eggs, bacon, sausage, toast, and homemade jam at Cap City Fine Diner.
  • Reuben Sandwich - Savor a classic sandwich made with corned beef, Swiss cheese, sauerkraut, and thousand island dressing at The Thurman Cafe.
  • Seafood Platter - Enjoy a delicious platter of fried shrimp, clams, and scallops at the Olde Pickerington Village Tavern.

Local Beverages

  • BrewDog Beer - Sip on award-winning craft beer made locally at the BrewDog DogTap Columbus.
  • Wine - Sample a wide selection of wines made by Ohio wineries at the Valley Vineyards Wine and Beer Festival.
  • Coffee - Start your day with a cup of freshly brewed coffee at the Reynoldsburg Coffee Depot.

Where to Stay in Reynoldsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites Columbus East - Relax in spacious rooms with plush bedding, free Wi-Fi, and breakfast at this mid-range hotel.
  • Quality Inn & Suites Reynoldsburg-Columbus East - Benefit from convenient amenities like a 24-hour fitness center, outdoor pool, and free breakfast at this top-rated hotel.

What to Buy in Reynoldsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antiques - Explore a variety of antique shops like Imogene’s Antique Boutique and Snooty Fox Antiques.
  • Fresh Produce - Buy locally grown fruits and vegetables from the Reynoldsburg Farmers Market.
  • Artisanal Products - Purchase handmade soaps, candles, and jewelry from the 12 Monkeys Creative Arts Studio.

Local Culture and Customs of Reynoldsburg

  • Respect For Elders - Reynoldsburg values its elders and visitors are expected to show respect and courtesy towards them.
  • Family-Oriented - Reynoldsburg is a great place for families, and the city caters to children of all ages.
  • Traditional Values - Reynoldsburg is known for its traditional Ohio values, which include hard work, respect for law and order, and a sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Reynoldsburg

The best time to visit Reynoldsburg is from May to September, when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall months from October to November are also a great time to visit, as the trees display beautiful fall foliage colors. Winter in Reynoldsburg is cold and snowy, making indoor activities like visiting museums and shopping centers ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reynoldsburg

Do:

  • Do try local foods and drinks, such as Ohio Farmhouse Breakfast and BrewDog Beer.
  • Do explore Reynoldsburg’s historical landmarks and natural attractions.
  • Do show respect for the city’s traditional values and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural or cultural landmarks.
  • Don’t disrespect elders or other members of the community.
  • Don’t expect nightlife and entertainment similar to big cities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Reynoldsburg

Reynoldsburg is a safe city with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take usual precautions such as securing valuables and taking care in crowded areas. In case of emergency, dial 911.

Health in Reynoldsburg

Reynoldsburg has several medical facilities, including the OhioHealth Pickerington Medical Campus and the Nationwide Children’s Hospital. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance, and those visiting from outside the US may require medical visas.

Getting to Reynoldsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Reynoldsburg

  • John Glenn Columbus International Airport - Located just 10 miles from Reynoldsburg, the airport offers domestic and international flights.
  • Rickenbacker International Airport - Located 12 miles south of Reynoldsburg, the airport offers cargo and military flights.

Bus Stations Near Reynoldsburg

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located just 15 miles from Reynoldsburg, the station offers bus services to various cities across the US.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Visitors can rent cars from various rental companies in Reynoldsburg or Columbus.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services - Reynoldsburg has several taxi companies and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft.

How to Get Around in Reynoldsburg

Reynoldsburg has a public transit system, COTA, which connects the city to other parts of Greater Columbus. Visitors can also rent cars or use ride-sharing services to get around. Biking and walking are also popular options in the city, with several bike paths and pedestrian walkways.

Where to Go Next from Reynoldsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Columbus - Visit Ohio’s capital city, Columbus, renowned for the Ohio State University, COSI Science Center, and Columbus Museum of Art.
  • Hocking Hills - Explore the natural beauty of Hocking Hills, a scenic region known for its waterfalls, rock formations, and hiking trails.
  • Cincinnati - Discover the Queen City of Cincinnati, famous for its chili, craft beer scene, and Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden.

Conclusion

Whether you are looking for the quaint charm of a small town or the excitement of a big city, Reynoldsburg is the perfect destination for you. With its rich history, natural beauty, and traditional values, Reynoldsburg offers something for every visitor. Plan your visit today and experience the best of the Midwest.
