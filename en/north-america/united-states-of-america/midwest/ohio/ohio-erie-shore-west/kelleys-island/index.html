Introduction to Kelleys Island

Located on Ohio’s Erie shore, Kelleys Island is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The island is a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and offers a mix of natural beauty and cultural attractions. With stunning beaches, breathtaking views, and plenty of outdoor activities, Kelleys Island should be on your list of must-visit destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Kelleys Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kelleys Island Historical Association Museum

The Kelleys Island Historical Association Museum is a must-visit for history enthusiasts. It features exhibits and artifacts that highlight the island’s rich history. The museum is located in an old church and has a small admission fee.

Inscription Rock Petroglyphs

The Inscription Rock Petroglyphs are a testament to the Native Americans who once lived on Kelleys Island over 400 years ago. The petroglyphs are etched onto a large limestone rock and are believed to be a message left by the Erie tribe.

Natural Attractions of Kelleys Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Glacial Grooves State Memorial

Glacial Grooves State Memorial is a geological wonder that features deep grooves etched into limestone rocks. The grooves date back to the Ice Age and offer a glimpse into the island’s past.

Kelleys Island State Park

Kelleys Island State Park is a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts. It features a sandy beach, hiking trails, picnic areas, and campgrounds. The park is perfect for a day trip or for a longer stay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kelleys Island

Kelleys Island is a paradise for nature lovers, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the popular routes include:

North Pond Trail

This easy trail runs through a quiet area and is a perfect spot for birdwatching.

South Loop Trail

This trail is a bit more challenging and offers stunning views of Lake Erie. It is a great option for experienced hikers looking for a moderate challenge.

Best Things to Do in Kelleys Island: Activities and Tours

Wine Tasting

Kelleys Island is home to several wineries that offer tasting tours. Some popular wineries include Island Wine Company and Kelleys Island Wine Company.

Kayaking

Kayaking is a popular activity on Kelleys Island. Several local tour companies offer guided kayaking tours that take visitors to scenic spots around the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kelleys Island

Day 1 - Visit Kelleys Island Historical Association Museum and explore Inscription Rock Petroglyphs.

Day 2 - Take a hiking or biking tour of the North Pond Trail.

Day 3 - Spend the day at Kelleys Island State Park lounging at the beach and enjoying the hiking trails.

Day 4 - Take a guided kayaking tour around the island.

Day 5 - Visit Glacial Grooves State Memorial and learn about the island’s unique geological history.

Day 6 - Take a tour of one of Kelleys Island’s wineries and enjoy a wine-tasting session.

Day 7 - Explore the island’s food scene before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Kelleys Island

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Bisque

The Lobster Bisque served at the Kelleys Island Brewery is a local favorite. The creamy soup is made with fresh cream and chunks of Lobster.

Perch Sandwich

The Perch Sandwich served at The Dockside Cafe is a must-try for seafood lovers. The sandwich is made with fresh Lake Erie perch and served on a grilled bun.

Local Beverages

Kelleys Island Brewery

Kelleys Island Brewery is a popular spot for beer lovers. They offer a variety of locally brewed craft beers that pair perfectly with the island’s seafood.

Kelleys Island Wine Company

Kelleys Island Wine Company offers a range of award-winning wines. Visitors can enjoy a wine tasting and take home a bottle of their favorite vintage.

Where to Stay in Kelleys Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kelleys Island Venture Resort

Kelleys Island Venture Resort is perfect for couples looking for a romantic getaway. The resort features luxurious suites with stunning views of Lake Erie.

The Island House Hotel

The Island House Hotel is a family-friendly hotel with spacious rooms and an outdoor pool. The hotel is located within walking distance of several local attractions.

What to Buy in Kelleys Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wine and Beer

Kelleys Island is known for its wineries and breweries, making wine and beer a popular souvenir for visitors to bring home.

Lake Erie Stone Jewellery

Lake Erie Stone jewellery is a unique and popular souvenir that is sold in several local shops. The jewellery is made using stones found on the island’s beaches.

Local Culture and Customs of Kelleys Island

The locals on Kelleys Island are friendly and welcoming. Visitors can expect a laid-back island vibe and a warm reception from the locals.

Best Time to Visit Kelleys Island

The best time to visit Kelleys Island is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. The island is also busy during the peak season, so visitors should plan ahead and book accommodations early.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kelleys Island

Do:

  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and biking.
  • Do visit the local wineries and breweries.
  • Do explore the island’s beaches and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the island’s beaches.
  • Don’t swim in areas that are marked unsafe.
  • Don’t disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kelleys Island

Kelleys Island is a safe destination, but visitors should still take the usual precautions. This includes avoiding secluded areas at night, locking doors and windows at night, and keeping valuables out of plain sight.

Health in Kelleys Island

There are no major health concerns on Kelleys Island, and visitors can use the local hospital in case of an emergency.

Getting to Kelleys Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kelleys Island

The closest airport to Kelleys Island is the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, located about an hour and a half away.

Bus Stations Near Kelleys Island

Greyhound and Megabus have stations in Cleveland, Ohio, from where visitors can take a bus to Sandusky, Ohio.

Other Options

Visitors to Kelleys Island can also take a ferry from Marblehead, Ohio or Sandusky, Ohio.

How to Get Around in Kelleys Island

Visitors can rent bicycles, golf carts, or scooters to get around the island. There is also a local taxi service that is available for visitors who prefer to use a car.

Where to Go Next from Kelleys Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cedar Point Amusement Park

Cedar Point Amusement Park is located in Sandusky, Ohio, and is a popular destination for families. It features several thrilling roller coasters and other rides.

Put-In-Bay

Put-In-Bay is another island located nearby and is a popular destination for boating enthusiasts. It also features several historical attractions and a lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Kelleys Island is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a mix of natural beauty and cultural attractions. From hiking and biking to wine tasting and kayaking, there is something for everyone on this stunning island. Book your trip today and discover all that Kelleys Island has to offer.
