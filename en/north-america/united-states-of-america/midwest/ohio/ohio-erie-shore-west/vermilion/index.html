Introduction to Vermilion

Vermilion is a charming small town on Lake Erie’s south shore in northern Ohio. Known for its historic buildings, scenic views, and outdoor adventures, Vermilion attracts thousands of visitors each year. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s history, taking a scenic hike or bike ride, or enjoying the local cuisine, Vermilion has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Vermilion

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Vermilion offers visitors an extensive list of museums and historical landmarks to explore. These must-see attractions include:

  • Vermilion River Reservation: This reservation features miles of well-marked hiking trails that follow along the Vermilion River. Visitors can also take a canoe or kayak ride through the river’s calm waters.
  • Inland Seas Maritime Museum: This museum is located in the oldest remaining US customs house on Lake Erie. The museum houses exhibits that narrate the history of shipping and boatbuilding on the Great Lakes.
  • Vermilion Lighthouse: This lighthouse is one of the oldest lighthouses in Ohio, and its construction dates back to the 19th century.
  • Old Vermilion Jailhouse: This jailhouse has been converted into a museum, showcasing the history of Vermilion’s criminal justice system from the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Vermilion: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vermilion’s natural landscapes and outdoor adventures provide visitors with an opportunity to escape from their daily routine. Some of the famous outdoor activities that visitors can enjoy during their visit include:

  • Sherod Park: This park is a popular destination for hiking and bird watching. Visitors can enjoy views of Lake Erie from the park’s observation deck.
  • Vermilion River Reservation: In addition to hiking and paddling, this reservation offers visitors guided nature walks and interactive exhibits about local flora and fauna.
  • Mill Hollow at Vermilion River Reservation: This park features trails and a boardwalk that lead visitors through the park’s forest habitats, wetlands, and meadows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vermilion

Vermilion offers visitors an extensive network of hiking and biking trails to explore. The Vermilion River Reservation has miles of well-marked trails that run parallel to the river. Visitors can bike or walk the trails and enjoy scenic views of the river and surrounding forests. Sherod Park also has a network of trails, including the scenic Nature Trail and the Quarry Trail, which offers visitors views of Lake Erie.

Best Things to Do in Vermilion: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, Vermilion offers tourists numerous activities and tours options. These activities and tours include:

  • Vermilion Valley Vineyards: Take a tour and wine tasting at Vermilion Valley Vineyards, offers visitors wine tastings and vineyard tours.
  • Lake Erie Canopy Tours: Visitors can zip line over stunning sawgrass, wetlands, woodlands, and do challenging ropes courses.

Visitors can spend days exploring the town of Vermilion and surrounding areas. Here’s one itinerary suggestion for a week in Vermilion:

Food and Drink Scene of Vermilion

Vermilion’s food and drink scene focuses on locally sourced, fresh ingredients. Visitors must try the following dishes and local beverages during their visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lake Erie Yellow Perch: A regional delicacy, this dish is a must-try for all seafood lovers.
  • Ohio City Farm Bibb salad: The Ohio City Farm Bibb salad is a perfect blend of fresh, locally sourced greens and garnishes.
  • Cedar Point’s famous elephant ears: The iconic elephant ears are the perfect snack for exploring Vermilion’s scenic spots.

Local Beverages

  • Lake Erie Wines: Vermilion Valley Vineyards serves wines made locally from grapes grown on-site.
  • Great Lakes Brewing Company: This brewery serves locally crafted beer and has a variety of options on tap.

Where to Stay in Vermilion: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors can choose to stay in one of Vermilion’s many lodging options. These range from B&Bs and guesthouses to hotels and motels, and some recommendations include:

  • The Captain Gilchrist Guesthouse: This 100-year-old house is a historic building in downtown Vermilion and provides visitors with a cozy, home-like feel.
  • Red Roof Inn Vermilion: This budget-friendly accommodation option is ideal for travelers who want comfortable lodging options at an affordable rate.

Local Culture and Customs of Vermilion

Best Time to Visit Vermilion

Visitors should consider the following factors while planning their trip to Vermilion:

  • The peak tourist season is from May through September.
  • The fall months have crisp cool weather, stunning autumn foliage, and fewer crowds.
  • Winter in Vermilion is cold with occasional snow, but Christmas season offers unique events and festivities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vermilion

Visitors should ensure they follow safety guidelines in Vermilion, such as:

  • Carry a mobile phone with emergency contact details programmed into it.
  • Check the weather forecast before venturing out on an adventure.
  • Wear appropriate footwear and clothing while outdoors.

Health in Vermilion

Visitors should be aware of any health risks while staying in Vermilion:

  • Lyme disease is present in the area- visitors should take precautions such as covering up when hiking.
  • COVID-19 has affected Vermilion and visitors should follow CDC guidelines during their visit.

Getting to Vermilion: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vermilion

The Cleveland Hopkins International Airport is the nearest airport to Vermilion, around 30 miles away, having domestic and international coverage.

Bus Stations Near Vermilion

Visitors can catch a bus that will take them into Vermilion from Cleveland, which has a Greyhound bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can get into Vermilion by driving. Vermilion is easily reachable from Cleveland, Toledo, Detroit, and Columbus.

How to Get Around in Vermilion

Visitors can explore Vermilion’s attractions on foot or by car. Visitors should consider renting a bike or a kayak for outdoor exploration.

Where to Go Next from Vermilion: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can plan day trips to nearby cities from Vermilion. These include:

  • Sandusky, Ohio (20 miles away): Famous for Cedar Point amusement park, water parks, and beautiful beaches.
  • Port Clinton, Ohio (30 miles away): Attractions include African Safari Wildlife Park, Sandusky Bay, and Put-in-Bay island.

Conclusion

Vermilion is an ideal destination for history, nature, and adventure enthusiasts. With its rich history, scenic landscapes, and charming downtown area, Vermilion offers visitors a vacation that is both relaxed and exciting. Tourists can indulge in exploring nature, engage in water activities, and have some local foods while enjoying Vermilion’s creative and comfortable accommodation options.
