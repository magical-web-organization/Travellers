Introduction to Fremont

Located in the Ohio Erie shore west, Fremont is a charming city in Ohio that boasts a unique blend of natural beauty and rich history. Visitors can explore several museums, historic sites, and natural attractions that offer a glimpse into the city’s past and present. Apart from these attractions, there are several outdoor activities, hiking trails, and adventure sports to engage in. This Fremont travel guide will help you plan an exciting vacation and discover everything that Fremont has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Fremont

Fremont has a lot to offer for visitors looking to explore the best of Ohio, from cultural attractions to natural wonders, there’s something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Fremont:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rutherford B. Hayes Presidential Library & Museums: This museum offers a glimpse into the life and times of the 19th President of the United States. Visitors can explore several exhibits, including original artifacts, manuscripts, and photographs.
  • Sandusky County Historical Society Museum: The museum harbors a vast collection of historical documents, artifacts, and photographs that showcase the history of Sandusky County and Fremont City.
  • Spiegel Grove State Park: This historic Victorian mansion was the home of Rutherford B. Hayes and his family. Visitors can tour the house and grounds and learn about the history of the Hayes family.

Natural Attractions of Fremont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Erie Sandusky Bay Water Trails: This trail stretches 27 miles and offers excellent opportunities for kayaking, canoeing, and paddleboarding.
  • White Star Park: This park is perfect for those looking for an outdoor adventure. Visitors can explore the hiking trails, fishing spots, and swimming areas that offer stunning views of the White Star Quarry.
  • Creek Bend Farm: This is a perfect spot to get out and enjoy nature. The farm has a series of hikes and trails that take visitors through beautiful fields and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fremont

Fremont offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the city. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • North Coast Inland Trail: This 128-mile trail runs from Buffalo to Toledo and passes through Fremont. The trail offers excellent opportunities for hiking and biking through the scenic countryside.
  • Bayshore Road: This 14-mile scenic route runs along the Lake Erie shoreline and offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding landscape.
  • River Bend Nature Walk: This trail stretches for 1.5 miles and takes visitors through beautiful forests, open fields, and along the banks of the Sandusky River.

Best Things to Do in Fremont: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours to engage in while in Fremont. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Ghostly Manor Thrill Center: This attraction offers a range of fun activities and games, including a haunted house, go-karts, mini-golf, and laser tag.
  • Liberty Aviation Museum: The museum is home to several restored vintage war planes, including a B-25 bomber and other rare aircraft.
  • The Depot: This venue offers several forms of entertainment, including live music, comedy shows, and theater performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fremont

Here’s an itinerary to help you plan your week-long trip to Fremont:

Day 1: Arrive in Fremont and explore the Downtown area.

Day 2: Visit the Rutherford B. Hayes Presidential Library & Museums and the Spiegel Grove State Park.

Day 3: Hike the North Coast Inland Trail or explore White Star Park.

Day 4: Take a tour of the Liberty Aviation Museum and explore the Sandusky County Historical Society Museum.

Day 5: Kayak down the Lake Erie Sandusky Bay Water Trails and take a scenic drive along Bayshore Road.

Day 6: Visit Creek Bend Farm and explore the River Bend Nature Walk.

Day 7: Relax and indulge in the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Fremont

Fremont has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers visitors a range of dining options and local specialties to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye: This freshwater fish is a popular local dish that is served in several restaurants in Fremont.
  • Lake Erie Perch: This fish is another local delicacy that can be found in most restaurants in the city.
  • Buckeyes: These chocolate-covered peanut butter balls are a classic Ohio dessert and are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Great Lakes Brewing Company: This brewery produces a range of craft beers that can be sampled in several bars and restaurants around town.
  • Firelands Winery: This winery offers a range of locally produced wines that can be tasted in their tasting room.

Where to Stay in Fremont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fremont offers several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Fremont: This hotel is a popular choice for visitors and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Fremont: The hotel offers a range of amenities, including complimentary breakfast, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Little Ted’s Cottages: This quaint guesthouse offers several cozy cottages that are perfect for those looking for a unique experience.

What to Buy in Fremont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products that visitors can buy in Fremont to take back as a reminder of their vacation. Here are some of the best:

  • Fremont memorabilia: Visitors can find several souvenir shops in Downtown Fremont that sell t-shirts, hats, and other items that feature the city’s landmarks and slogans.
  • Sandusky County Maple Syrup: This maple syrup is produced locally and can be found in several specialty food stores in Fremont.
  • Erie Islands Hot Sauce: This hot sauce is produced by a local company and is a popular souvenir for spice lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Fremont

Fremont has a rich history and culture that is reflected in the city’s architecture, festivals, and events. Visitors should be aware of the local customs and etiquette while exploring Fremont. The locals are friendly and welcoming, so visitors should respect their traditions and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Fremont

The best time to visit Fremont is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and dry, and most of the outdoor activities and events take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fremont

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Fremont:

Do:

  • Explore the local food and drink scene.
  • Visit the Rutherford B. Hayes Presidential Library & Museums.
  • Take a scenic drive along Bayshore Road.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and etiquette.
  • Leave valuables unattended while exploring the city.
  • Miss out on the opportunity to kayak down the Lake Erie Sandusky Bay Water Trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fremont

Fremont is a safe city to visit, and violent crime rates are relatively low. Visitors should take general safety precautions, such as keeping valuables hidden and locking their car doors when parked in public areas.

Health in Fremont

Fremont has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality healthcare to visitors and residents.

Getting to Fremont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Fremont via several transportation options, including airports and bus stations.

Airports Near Fremont

  • Toledo Express Airport (TOL): The airport is approx. 45 miles from Fremont.
  • Detroit Metropolitan Airport (DTW): The airport is approx. 90 miles from Fremont.

Bus Stations Near Fremont

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The station is located in Toledo, approx. 30 miles from Fremont.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Fremont, as it is easily accessible via several highways and roads.

How to Get Around in Fremont

Visitors can get around Fremont via several transportation options, including rental cars, taxis, and rideshares.

Where to Go Next from Fremont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore while in Fremont, including:

  • Sandusky: This city is known for its amusement parks, beaches, and water sports and is located approx. 20 miles from Fremont.
  • Cedar Point: This amusement park is located in Sandusky and is a popular attraction among locals and visitors.
  • South Bass Island: This island is located in Lake Erie and can be accessed via a ferry ride from Sandusky.

Conclusion

Fremont is a charming city in Ohio that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical sites or engaging in outdoor activities or indulging in the local food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone in Fremont. This travel guide will help you plan an exciting vacation and discover everything that Fremont has to offer.
