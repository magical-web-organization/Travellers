Introduction to Huron

Huron, Ohio, is a small town located on the Erie shore, west of Cleveland. Known for its stunning views of Lake Erie and outdoor adventures, Huron is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. From its culturally important museums to its picturesque parks, Huron has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Huron

If you’re planning a trip to Huron, Ohio, here are some places you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Huron Historical Society - This museum showcases the history of Huron, including exhibits on Native American artifacts and early settlers.
  • Osborn Learning Center - The center has interactive exhibits on natural history and the Huron River watershed, with a focus on the Great Lakes ecosystem.
  • Old Woman Creek National Estuarine Research Reserve - This reserve offers guided tours, hikes, and hands-on exhibits highlighting the importance of estuaries.

Natural Attractions of Huron: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nickel Plate Beach - Known for its sandy shores and freshwater swimming, Nickel Plate also has kayaking and paddleboarding rentals.
  • Milton C. Thompson Park - This park spans over 140 acres and has numerous scenic hiking trails.
  • Sheldon Marsh State Nature Preserve - Colorful marshes, sand dunes, and stunning Lake Erie views make this a must-see destination.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huron

Huron has a wealth of outdoor recreation opportunities. Here are a few hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Huron River Greenway - This 1.5-mile trail offers a beautiful view of the Huron River and its surrounding wildlife.
  • Huron River Pathway - This 2.5-mile trail connects to the Huron River Greenway and is perfect for both biking and hiking.
  • East Harbor State Park Trail - This 3.5-mile trail offers a scenic hike through the woods and vistas of Lake Erie.

Best Things to Do in Huron: Activities and Tours

  • Huron Playhouse - This local theater has been entertaining guests for over 70 years with a range of productions, from musicals to dramas.
  • Jet Express Ferry - Take a ride across the Lake Erie to Put-in-Bay or Kelly’s Island to enjoy a day of exploration and adventure.
  • Huron Yacht Club - For a unique experience, take a tour of the Huron Yacht Club and admire the impressive boat collection.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huron

Day 1: Nickel Plate Beach and Huron River Greenway Day 2: Sheldon Marsh State Nature Preserve and Milton C. Thompson Park Day 3: Huron Playhouse and Old Woman Creek National Estuarine Research Reserve Day 4: Jet Express Ferry to Put-in-Bay and Perry’s Victory and International Peace Memorial Day 5: Osborn Learning Center and Huron Yacht Club Day 6: East Harbor State Park Trail and Lake Front Park Day 7: Relax day or other activities as desired.

Food and Drink Scene of Huron

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lake Erie Perch - This freshwater fish is lightly breaded and fried and served with tartar sauce, a local specialty.
  • Walleye Sandwich - Similar to perch, walleye is another freshwater fish served in a sandwich or dinner plate.
  • Huron Market Cafe’s Lobster Bisque - This local cafe offers a special lobster bisque that is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Vacationland Winery - Offering unique fruit wines and expanded vineyard for tasting wines, Vacationland is one of the best wineries in Ohio.
  • Funky Truckeria - This brewery creates a range of high-quality beers, from floral wheat brews to spicy porters.
  • Sawmill Creek Vineyards - This winery is family-owned and operated and offers a variety of tastings, tours, and events throughout the year.

Where to Stay in Huron: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sawmill Creek Resort - A beautiful resort located alongside Lake Erie’s shores, with on-site restaurants, golf, and water sports.
  • Econo Lodge - Affordable and family-friendly with excellent reviews includes free breakfast, wifi and fitness center.
  • Captain Montague’s Bed & Breakfast - Located in the heart of Huron, this charming B&B offers personalized service and good amenities that will make for a cozy stay.

What to Buy in Huron: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vacationland Winery’s Blackberry Wine - This blackberry fruit wine is made from a proprietary blend of blackberries and has won numerous awards.
  • Betsy’s Gifts - This boutique sells locally made jewelry, home decor, and other unique items, perfect for souvenir shopping.
  • A gift from Sassy Sal’s Sweet Treats & Gifts - Get some homemade fudge or caramel apples or make your own gift basket.

Local Culture and Customs of Huron

  • Respect the natural environment: Huron is known for its stunning natural beauty, so it’s essential to leave it as you found it.
  • Employ good etiquette: Huron locals are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Showing respect towards them is important while exploring the town.
  • League Park: A classic baseball field where locals love to spend summer nights, sit back, and enjoy the game.

Best Time to Visit Huron

The best time to visit Huron, Ohio, is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny and facilitates leisure activities with natural surroundings. In the summer, the town hosts the Huron River Fest, with live music, boats parades, and fireworks, an extra attraction worth considering.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huron

Do:

  • Do try local food and drink: Huron has an excellent reputation for its seafood, wine, and beers.
  • Do visit natural attractions: Huron has many scenic parks and nature preserves that are an incredible sight to see.
  • Do bring comfortable walking shoes: Many of Huron’s best attractions are free to explore by foot, so bring your most comfortable pair.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: As previously mentioned, Huron is known for its natural beauty, and littering can harm wildlife and the environment.
  • Don’t forget sunscreen: The summer sun in Huron can be strong, so don’t forget to bring sunscreen and protect yourself from UV rays.
  • Don’t forget to incorporate rest into your itinerary: With so much to see, it can be tempting to pack your schedule with activities. Make sure you take breaks for rest and relaxation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huron

Huron is an adequately safe town, but it’s important to take precautions like locking car doors and keeping valuables safe while exploring the area. It’s recommended to stay aware of the surroundings and keep an eye on the weather, hurons is vulnerable to thunderstorms, so please note the weather conditions before going to any outdoor sites.

Health in Huron

In case of health emergencies, Huron has its own Fire Department, providing emergency services as and when needed. Also, you should carry your necessary medications with you wherever you go.

Getting to Huron: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huron

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport (CLE) - 51 miles away, an average drive of 58 minutes.
  • Toledo Express Airport (TOL) – 68 miles away, an average drive of 1 hour and 10 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Huron

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal - 305 W Prospect Rd, Ashtabula, Ohio 44004, located approximately 32 miles from Huron.
  • Greyhound Bus Terminal - 245 E Tallmadge Ave, Akron, Ohio 44310, located approximately 68 miles from Huron.

Other Options

  • Car rentals and taxis are readily available at both airports and other freeway exits.

How to Get Around in Huron

Walking and cycling are the best ways to get around Huron. There’s an excellent network of cycling routes, and many attractions are within easy walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Huron: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cedar Point Amusement Park: A well-known theme park located in Sandusky, Ohio, just a 15-minute drive away.
  • Kelleys Island: Located a short ferry ride away, this small island is known for its wineries, outdoor recreation, and stunning beaches.
  • Cleveland: Just an hour’s drive away, Cleveland is a vibrant and cultural city, with excellent museums, dining, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Huron, Ohio, is a wonderful destination to explore. From its natural beauty to its rich culture, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Whether you spend a week or a weekend, you’ll be sure to have an unforgettable time in Huron, Ohio.
