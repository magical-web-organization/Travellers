Introduction to Dayton

Dayton, located in the Miami-Scioto basins of Ohio, is a vibrant city filled with rich history, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. Known as the birthplace of aviation, Dayton has much to offer visitors with a diverse range of interests.

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, museums and historical sites, or best places to eat and drink, there’s something for everyone in Dayton. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore all that Dayton has to offer with a list of must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, where to stay, what to eat and drink, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Dayton

Here’s a comprehensive list of must-visit places in Dayton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Museum of the US Air Force: This museum offers a fascinating journey through the history of aviation and space exploration, with a huge collection of aircraft, spacecraft, and exhibits.

  • Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Site: This site celebrates the lives of the Wright Brothers, who invented the first successful powered flight in 1903, and their impact on aviation.

  • Dayton Art Institute: This museum houses a world-class collection of art, including American, European, and Asian art, as well as special exhibitions and events.

  • Paul Laurence Dunbar House: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of Paul Laurence Dunbar, a famous African American poet, novelist, and playwright born in Dayton.

Natural Attractions of Dayton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dayton Five Rivers MetroParks: This park system offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, paddling, fishing, and more.

  • Cox Arboretum and Gardens: This beautiful garden comprises 189 acres of flowers, trees, ponds, and walking paths.

  • John Bryan State Park: This park is a popular spot for hiking, camping, and fishing, with stunning views of the Little Miami River.

  • Glen Helen Nature Preserve: This preserve features 1,000 acres of forests, streams, and waterfalls, with over 25 miles of trails for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dayton

Dayton is blessed with a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best:

  • Great Miami Riverway: This 99-mile trail follows the Great Miami River through beautiful landscapes and historic towns, offering plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and paddling.

  • Little Miami Scenic Trail: This 50-mile trail follows the Little Miami River, offering opportunities for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as great views of the river and wildlife.

  • Mad River Trail: This 3.2-mile trail offers a scenic view of the Mad River and its wildlife, with opportunities for hiking, biking, and bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Dayton: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for family-friendly attractions or adventurous activities, Dayton has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Dayton:

  • Dayton Dragons Baseball Game: Catch a minor league game of America’s favorite pastime at Fifth Third Field.

  • Oregon Historic District: Take a walking tour of this charming historic district, with restored homes and buildings from the 1800s.

  • RiverScape MetroPark: Enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including ice skating, fishing, and concerts, at this park located in downtown Dayton.

  • Segway Tours of Dayton: Take a guided tour of Dayton on a Segway, discovering the city’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dayton

Here’s a one-week itinerary for exploring Dayton:

  • Day 1: Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Site and Wright Brothers National Museum

  • Day 2: National Museum of the US Air Force and Dayton Art Institute

  • Day 3: Cox Arboretum and Gardens and RiverScape MetroPark

  • Day 4: John Bryan State Park and Mad River Trail

  • Day 5: Oregon Historic District and Segway Tours of Dayton

  • Day 6: Dayton Dragons Baseball Game and Dayton Five Rivers MetroParks

  • Day 7: Glen Helen Nature Preserve and Little Miami Scenic Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Dayton

No visit to Dayton is complete without experiencing its vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dayton-style pizza: A thin crust pizza with a unique sweet-and-spicy sauce and toppings of sausage and red pepper flakes.

  • Buckeye candy: A delicious confectionery made with peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate.

  • Cincinnati-style chili: A chili with a unique blend of spices, served over spaghetti and topped with cheese and onions.

  • Fried bologna sandwich: A local delicacy made with fried bologna, cheese, and condiments on a soft bun.

Local Beverages

  • Dayton Beer Company: A local brewery with a variety of handcrafted beers to choose from.

  • Warped Wing Brewing Company: Another popular brewery in Dayton, offering unique blends and flavors of beer.

  • Boston Stoker Coffee Company: A local coffee roaster, offering special blends of coffee and seasonal drinks.

Where to Stay in Dayton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dayton offers a variety of accommodations, from budget-friendly hotels to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay in Dayton:

  • Dayton Marriott: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of downtown Dayton.

  • Inn Port Lodging: A cozy bed and breakfast with charming rooms and personalized service.

  • SpringHill Suites Dayton South/Miamisburg: A budget-friendly option with spacious rooms and great amenities.

  • AirBnB: A variety of affordable homes and apartments available to rent, perfect for families or groups.

What to Buy in Dayton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home from Dayton:

  • Aviation-themed souvenirs: Pay homage to Dayton’s aviation heritage with souvenirs like airplane models, t-shirts, or coffee mugs

  • Handcrafted Dayton products: Visit Second Street Market and pick up locally-made products like candles, soaps, leather goods and knick-knacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Dayton

Dayton is a welcoming, diverse city that is proud of its history, culture, and local customs. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Dayton:

  • Be respectful of local customs: Always be respectful to locals, and adhere to their customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately: Dayton has a variety of outdoor activities, so it’s essential to bring weather-appropriate attire along with comfortable footwear.

  • Use local lingo: Don’t be surprised if someone gives you directions using local lingo such as “down the pike” (down the highway).

Best Time to Visit Dayton

The best time to visit Dayton is between May and September when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing. If you’re a fan of aviation, visit in July for Aviation Heritage Month or come during peak Fall season from September to November to enjoy the beautiful foliage and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dayton

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Dayton:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine: Dayton boasts a remarkable variety of local cuisine. Don’t leave without trying them.

  • Do visit the parks: Dayton’s parks offer stunning views and outdoor adventures.

  • Do take a tour: Segway tours give visitors the chance to see downtown Dayton in a unique and fun way.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Always use designated trash receptacles in the parks or anywhere else.

  • Don’t leave valuables in the car: Take essential personal items with you when leaving valuables behind in the car.

  • Don’t be late for events: If you’re attending an event, arrive early to get the best seat.

Safety and Health

Here’s what you need to know about safety and health in Dayton:

Safety in Dayton

Dayton is a relatively safe city, but it’s best to take some precautions to ensure your safety:

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings: Especially when visiting crowded areas.

  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly when walking alone at night.

  • Stick to well-lit areas, and avoid taking shortcuts through unknown neighborhoods.

Health in Dayton

Dayton has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, providing excellent medical care for residents and visitors alike.

Getting to Dayton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options for getting to Dayton:

Airports Near Dayton

  • Dayton International Airport: This airport serves Dayton and the surrounding areas, and is located about a 20-minute drive from downtown.

  • Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport: This airport serves the region and is located about an hour’s drive south of Dayton.

Bus Stations Near Dayton

  • Greyhound: This bus company has a terminal located in Dayton, which provides service to various destinations.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: There are several car rental companies in Dayton for visitors who prefer to rent a car.

How to Get Around in Dayton

Here are some transportation options for getting around Dayton:

  • Bus: The Greater Dayton Regional Transit Authority provides local bus services throughout Dayton.

  • Car rentals: Car rental companies are located both at the Dayton International Airport and in downtown Dayton.

  • Bike: Dayton has several bike rental services that allow visitors to explore the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Dayton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dayton is located in the Midwest, providing plenty of options for nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the best:

  • Columbus: Ohio’s state capital city, located about an hour’s drive east of Dayton and home to Ohio State University, museums, and art galleries.

  • Indianapolis: The capital city of Indiana, located about two hours drive west of Dayton, with various attractions, including the Indianapolis Motor Speedway and art museums.

  • Cincinnati: A vibrant city with plenty of attractions for visitors, located about an hour’s drive south of Dayton, known for its bridges, museums, and parks.

Conclusion

Dayton is a beautiful and vibrant city, filled with outdoor adventures, history, culture, and the best local food and drinks. This comprehensive tour guide will help you explore and discover the best places to visit, stay, and eat, and give you a taste of Dayton’s unique local flavor. Start planning your trip to Dayton today, we are confident that you’ll never forget this fantastic experience.
