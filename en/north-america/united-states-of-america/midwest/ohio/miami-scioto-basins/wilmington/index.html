Introduction to Wilmington

Tucked away in the Miami - Scioto basins of Ohio, Wilmington is a charming small town that offers a delightful blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural significance. If you’re looking for a unique and off-the-beaten-path destination in the Midwest, Wilmington should definitely be on your list!

List of Places to Visit in Wilmington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wilmington is steeped in history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s heritage.

  • Clinton County Historical Society: This museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts and memorabilia that tell the story of Clinton County’s past.
  • Caesar Creek State Park: This park features an impressive dam that was built in the 1970s to provide flood control and recreational opportunities to the local community.
  • Roberts Convention Centre: This convention centre is a popular venue for a wide range of events, including trade shows, weddings, and conferences.
  • Fort Ancient Earthworks and Nature Preserve: This state park is home to a variety of hiking trails and some of the most well-preserved Native American earthworks in the country.

Natural Attractions of Wilmington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventures and breathtaking scenery, Wilmington has plenty to offer.

  • Cowen Lake State Park: This park offers a variety of recreational opportunities, including hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Little Miami Scenic Trail: This 70-mile trail winds its way through the stunning countryside of southwestern Ohio, providing breathtaking views of the Great Miami River.
  • Hocking Hills State Park: Just a short drive away from Wilmington, this state park is a natural wonderland that boasts a variety of hiking trails and stunning waterfalls.
  • Caesar Creek Gorge State Nature Preserve: This nature preserve features rugged hiking trails and stunning views of Caesar Creek Gorge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilmington

Wilmington is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Little Miami Scenic Trail: This 70-mile trail winds its way through the stunning countryside of southwestern Ohio, providing breathtaking views of the Great Miami River.
  • Caesar Creek State Park: This park features over 43 miles of trails that wind through forests, meadows, and lakeshores.
  • Kings Mills Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is perfect for an easy, scenic walk or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Wilmington: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a fun family activity or a unique cultural experience, Wilmington has plenty to offer.

  • Murphy Theatre: This historic theatre hosts a wide range of live performances, including concerts, plays, and musicals.
  • Ohio Renaissance Festival: Held annually in nearby Waynesville, this festival brings the magic of the Renaissance to life with jousting tournaments, interactive theatre performances, and more.
  • Wilmington Farmers Market: Held on Saturdays from May to October, this market features local produce, handcrafted goods, and live music.
  • Wilmington Air Park: This former military base is now a thriving cargo airport, and you can take a tour of the facilities to see how it operates.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wilmington

Day 1 - Explore the historic downtown area of Wilmington, including the Clinton County Courthouse and the Wilmington Commercial Historic District.

Day 2 - Visit the Clinton County Historical Society and enjoy a picnic lunch at Cowen Lake State Park.

Day 3 - Take a hike on one of the many trails at Caesar Creek State Park.

Day 4 - Tour the Fort Ancient Earthworks and Nature Preserve, and then enjoy a delicious dinner at one of Wilmington’s many restaurants.

Day 5 - Visit the Ohio Renaissance Festival in nearby Waynesville.

Day 6 - Shop for souvenirs at the Wilmington Farmers Market and take a tour of the Wilmington Air Park.

Day 7 - Relax and unwind at a local spa, or take a scenic drive to nearby Hocking Hills State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Wilmington

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Candy: This iconic Ohio treat features a peanut butter filling covered in chocolate.
  • Hot Slaw: This tangy salad is a classic regional dish that features cabbage, vinegar, and spices.
  • Buckeye Burger: This juicy burger is topped with bacon and cheddar cheese, and served on a pretzel bun.
  • Cincinnati Chili: This unique twist on chili features a savory meat sauce that’s served over spaghetti.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Wilmington is home to several craft breweries, including Warped Wing Brewery and The Guardian Brewing Company, that offer a wide range of unique and flavorful beers.
  • Buckeye Bourbon: This smooth and flavorful bourbon is distilled in Ohio and is a favorite of locals and visitors alike.
  • Elderberry Wine: This sweet and fruity wine is made from locally grown elderberries and is a must-try for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Wilmington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express Wilmington: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a complimentary breakfast and fitness center.
  • The General Denver Hotel: This historic hotel features charming rooms and suites, as well as an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • Wilmington Inn: This quaint bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Wilmington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Pottery: The Ohio countryside is home to many talented potters, and you can find a wide range of unique and beautiful pottery at local shops and galleries.
  • Local Honey: Ohio is known for its delicious honey, and you can find a variety of flavors at farmers markets and specialty shops throughout Wilmington.
  • Handmade Soaps: These all-natural soaps are made by local artisans using high-quality ingredients, and make the perfect souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilmington

Wilmington is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, as well as its love for traditional American values and cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Wilmington

The best time to visit Wilmington is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the scenery is breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilmington

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including hot slaw and Cincinnati chili.
  • Do explore the many hiking and biking trails in the area.
  • Do take a tour of the Wilmington Air Park.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the power of Ohio weather - be sure to check the forecast before you go!
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for all the hiking and exploring you’ll be doing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wilmington

Wilmington is a very safe town, but as with any travel destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions.

Health in Wilmington

Wilmington is home to several hospitals and medical facilities, and visitors can expect easy access to quality healthcare if needed.

Getting to Wilmington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wilmington

  • Dayton International Airport (DAY)
  • Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport (CVG)

Bus Stations Near Wilmington

  • Greyhound Station - Wilmington

Other Options

  • Rental cars are available at both Dayton International Airport and Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport.
  • Amtrak trains run through Cincinnati and Dayton, both of which are a short drive from Wilmington.

How to Get Around in Wilmington

The best way to get around Wilmington is by car, but there are also several taxi and ridesharing services available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Wilmington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cincinnati: Just an hour’s drive south of Wilmington, Cincinnati is a bustling city with plenty to see and do, including the Cincinnati Zoo, the Cincinnati Museum Center, and the Great American Ball Park.
  • Columbus: Ohio’s capital city is just a short drive north of Wilmington and offers a wide range of cultural attractions, including the Columbus Museum of Art, the Ohio Theatre, and the Columbus Zoo and Aquarium.
  • Hocking Hills State Park: Just a short drive away from Wilmington, this state park is a natural wonderland that boasts a variety of hiking trails and stunning waterfalls.

Conclusion

Wilmington, Ohio may be a small town, but it’s packed with hidden gems just waiting to be discovered. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, there’s something for everyone in this charming Midwest destination.
