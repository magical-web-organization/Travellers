Introduction to Portsmouth

Located in the Midwest region of the United States, Portsmouth is a charming city situated in the Miami-Scioto basin in Ohio. The city is known for its rich history, scenic beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff or simply looking for a peaceful getaway, Portsmouth has something to offer to everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Portsmouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Southern Ohio Museum and Cultural Center: This museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts, artwork, and historical exhibits. The building itself is a work of art, designed by famous architect Frank Lloyd Wright.

  • Portsmouth Floodwall Murals: These murals are a series of paintings that depict the history and heritage of Portsmouth and Scioto County. The murals cover over 2,000 feet of the city’s flood walls.

  • The Roy Rogers-Dale Evans Museum: Fans of the classic Western films will enjoy visiting this museum featuring memorabilia and exhibits from the iconic Roy Rogers and Dale Evans.

  • Shawnee State Park Museum: This museum is located in the Shawnee State Park, and showcases the history of the region as well as the flora and fauna of the area.

Natural Attractions of Portsmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shawnee State Park: Located in the Appalachian foothills, this state park covers over 4,000 acres of forests, lakes, and streams. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing and many other outdoor activities.

  • Scioto Trail State Park: Another state park with a variety of outdoor recreational opportunities including hiking, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Ohio River Valley: The Ohio River runs through Portsmouth and offers a picturesque view of the surroundings. Visitors can take a relaxing boat trip along the river or enjoy a scenic walk along its banks.

  • Shawnee State Forest: This is Ohio’s largest state forest and offers hiking trails, camping, and fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portsmouth

  • Little Scioto Trail: This 14-mile trail connects Portsmouth to the Little Scioto Wildlife Area and is ideal for hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing.

  • Shawnee State Park Backpack Trail: This trail is about 20 miles long and features some of the most scenic views in the area. The trail is open to hiking, backpacking, and camping.

  • Eagle Creek State Nature Preserve Loop: This is a 1.5-mile loop trail located in the Eagle Creek State Nature Preserve, ideal for those who prefer a shorter hike.

Best Things to Do in Portsmouth: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Walks: For those who are interested in the supernatural, Portsmouth offers a variety of guided ghost walks that explore the city’s haunted past.

  • Brewery Tours: The Portsmouth area is home to several breweries that offer tours and tastings. Visitors can learn about the brewing process and sample some of the best craft beer in the region.

  • Riverboat Tours: Visitors can enjoy a relaxing cruise along the Ohio River aboard a riverboat equipped with dining and entertainment options.

Food and Drink Scene of Portsmouth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeyes - This is a traditional Ohio dessert made from peanut butter and chocolate, and shaped like the buckeye tree nut.

  • Glier’s Goetta - This is a breakfast food made from a mixture of meat, spices, and oats. Locals often enjoy it with eggs and toast.

  • Burger Burger - A classic burger joint that serves delicious burgers with homemade buns.

Local Beverages

  • Jackie O’s Brewery - A brewery that features a variety of handcrafted beers, including IPAs, stouts, and seasonal brews.

  • Pivot Brewing Company - A brewery that specializes in sour beers and offers live music and food trucks on weekends.

Where to Stay in Portsmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shawnee Lodge & Conference Center: Located in the Shawnee State Park, this lodge features comfortable rooms, dining, and a range of outdoor recreational opportunities.

  • Holiday Inn Portsmouth: This hotel features spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant on-site.

  • Murphin Ridge Inn: This guesthouse is located in the rolling hills of the Appalachian Mountains and offers comfortable rooms with stunning views.

What to Buy in Portsmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artwork from Southern Ohio Museum and Cultural Center: Visitors can purchase prints, posters, and other artwork from the museum’s gift shop.

  • Buckeye candies - A sweet and tasty souvenir that can be found in many local stores.

  • Handcrafted pottery from local artisans: Portsmouth is home to several pottery studios, and visitors can purchase unique and handmade pieces as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Portsmouth

Portsmouth is a charming city with friendly people and a welcoming culture. The city is proud of its heritage and history, and visitors can learn more about it through its museums, historical sites, and local festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Portsmouth

The best time to visit Portsmouth is during the summer months when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. The city also hosts several festivals and events during this time, such as the River Days and Riverfront Jazz Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portsmouth

Do:

  • Explore the local hiking and biking trails
  • Visit the Southern Ohio Museum and Cultural Center
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beers

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural beauty of Portsmouth
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals
  • Forget to buy souvenirs and local products

Safety and Health

Safety in Portsmouth

Like any other city, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety while visiting Portsmouth. They should avoid walking alone in deserted areas late at night and always keep an eye on their belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Portsmouth

Visitors should make sure they are up to date on their vaccines before visiting Portsmouth. They should also take precautions to protect themselves against ticks and other insects that may carry diseases.

Getting to Portsmouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Portsmouth

  • Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport: Located about 100 miles from Portsmouth, this is the closest major airport.

  • John Glenn Columbus International Airport: Located about 125 miles from Portsmouth, this is another major airport that offers a range of flight options.

Bus Stations Near Portsmouth

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Portsmouth, this bus station offers a range of services to various destinations across the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Portsmouth by car or train. The city is well-connected by highways, and the nearest Amtrak station is in Huntington, WV, about 50 miles from Portsmouth.

How to Get Around in Portsmouth

Visitors can easily get around Portsmouth by car, bike or on foot. Several rental car companies are located in the city, and there are also bike rental shops. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Portsmouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cincinnati, OH: Located about 100 miles from Portsmouth, this city is known for its arts and cultural scene, as well as its famous chili.

  • Lexington, KY: Home to the famous Kentucky Derby, this charming city is located about 150 miles from Portsmouth and features beautiful horse farms and bourbon distilleries.

Conclusion

Portsmouth is a beautiful city in Ohio that offers a range of attractions, activities, and scenic beauty. Visitors can enjoy museums and historical sites, outdoor recreational activities, and try some of the local cuisine and craft beers. Whether you are looking for a peaceful retreat or an adventure, Portsmouth has something for everyone. So plan your next vacation to Portsmouth and experience the best of Ohio!
