Introduction to Miami - Scioto Basins

Miami - Scioto Basins stretches across Ohio in Midwest, offering visitors an array of natural wonders, cultural landmarks, and adventurous activities. Whether you’re in the mood for world-class museums, picturesque hiking trails, or flavorful dishes, Miami - Scioto Basins has something for everyone to enjoy.

Here’s our complete guide to everything you need to know before planning your trip to Miami - Scioto Basins.

List of Places to Visit in Miami - Scioto Basins

Miami - Scioto Basins has an abundance of cultural landmarks and natural attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Museum of the United States Air Force: One of the largest aviation museums in the world, offering visitors an opportunity to explore the impressive aircraft collection.
  • The Boonshoft Museum of Discovery: A fascinating interactive museum that offers hands-on learning opportunities for the entire family.
  • The Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Park: The site commemorates the Wright Brothers’ life and achievements, including their first flight in 1903.
  • The Paul Laurence Dunbar House: A historical site that celebrates the legacy of Paul Laurence Dunbar, who was one of the first African American poets to be nationally recognized.

Natural Attractions of Miami - Scioto Basins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hocking Hills State Park: Hiking and nature lovers should prioritize visiting this park, a breathtaking natural wonder that offers several hiking trails that lead to rocky gorges, waterfalls, and caves.
  • John Bryan State Park: Located in Greene County, this park features a scenic corridor of the Little Miami River that is perfect for kayaking, hiking, and fishing.
  • Clifton Gorge State Nature Preserve: Another destination for hiking in Miami - Scioto Basins, Clifton Gorge offers a mix of geological formations and breathtaking waterfalls.
  • The Ohio Caverns: A natural wonder that is home to America’s most extensive cave system, with over two miles of survey passages and rooms to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miami - Scioto Basins

  • Ohio to Erie Trail: This trail spans across Ohio, taking visitors through woodland, small towns, and a handful of scenic lakes and rivers.
  • Buckeye Trail: A 1,400-mile trail system that passes through Ohio’s rural countryside and significant cities, providing a challenging and rewarding hike for both novice and experienced hikers.
  • Little Miami Scenic Trail: Consistently named one of the best trails in America, Little Miami Scenic Trail offers a paved path for biking and a river basin for paddleboarding.

Best Things to Do in Miami - Scioto Basins: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the National Museum of the United States Air Force
  • Explore Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Park
  • Discover Hocking Hills State Park
  • Take a zip-line tour at the John Bryan State Park
  • Attend a live performance at the La Comedia Dinner Theatre

Week-Long Itinerary for Miami - Scioto Basins

Here’s a sample 7-day itinerary for your trip to Miami - Scioto Basins:

Day 1: Arrive in Columbus, Ohio, and check into your hotel.

Day 2: Drive to Dayton to explore the aviation history of the National Museum of the United States Air Force.

Day 3: Take a stroll through the Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Park, where the Wright Brothers made their first flight.

Day 4: Drive to Hocking Hills State Park for a day of hiking and exploring the beautiful natural setting.

Day 5: Explore nearby Paul Laurence Dunbar House and the Boonshoft Museum of Discovery.

Day 6: Take a scenic bike ride or hike through Little Miami Scenic Trail, followed by a live performance at the La Comedia Dinner Theatre.

Day 7: Return to Columbus, Ohio, or extend your trip with an extra day in Miami - Scioto Basins.

Food and Drink Scene of Miami - Scioto Basins

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye candies: A popular type of candy that is made with peanut butter and chocolate.
  • Cincinnati-style chili: This type of chili contains unusual ingredients like cinnamon, cocoa, and allspice. Served on top of spaghetti noodles with a heap of cheese on top, it’s a local favorite.
  • Fresh Grilled Corn: A typical summer food in Ohio, corn on the cob is often served as a side dish with grilled meats.

Local Beverages

  • Buckeye Vodka: A high-quality vodka distilled locally in Ohio, Buckeye Vodka is excellent for a refreshing summer cocktail.
  • Red Rock Brewery: This brewery is located in Cincinnati, serving a variety of craft brews including seasonal alternatives such as pumpkin beers in the fall.
