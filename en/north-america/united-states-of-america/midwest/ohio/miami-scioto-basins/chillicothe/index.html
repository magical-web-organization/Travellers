Introduction to Chillicothe

Nestled in the picturesque Miami - Scioto basins of Ohio, Chillicothe is a hidden gem that offers a mix of rich history, outdoor adventures, and culinary delights. Known as the first capital of Ohio, Chillicothe boasts a colorful past that visitors can uncover by exploring its many museums and historical sites. Besides the cultural attractions, Chillicothe has something for everyone, including hiking and biking trails, jaw-dropping scenic views, and a burgeoning food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Chillicothe

When it comes to things to see and do in Chillicothe, visitors won’t run out of options. From world-class museums to scenic hikes, here is a list of places you can’t afford to miss on your trip to Chillicothe.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chillicothe is home to some of the most culturally significant and fascinating historical sites and museums in the Midwest.

Adena Mansion and Gardens

Built-in 1805, Adena Mansion and Gardens are known for being the home of Thomas Worthington, Ohio’s sixth governor. Visitors can take guided tours of the mansion and learn about the Worthington family and their impact on Chillicothe’s history. Don’t forget to take a stroll through the beautiful gardens, which boast a variety of species of plants and trees, including cherry trees that are a sight to behold during cherry blossom season.

Hopewell Culture National Historic Park

The Hopewell Culture National Historic Park is a must-see for history buffs. The park preserves the earthen mounds and artifacts of the Hopewell Native American culture that thrived in the area over 2,000 years ago. Visitors can explore the six-mile trail through the park and take guided tours to learn about the life and culture of the Hopewell people.

Ross County Heritage Center

Located in the heart of downtown Chillicothe, Ross County Heritage Center offers an immersive experience into the area’s history. The museum houses a variety of exhibits about Ross County’s rich and diverse past, including a section about the Underground Railroad and the role it played in the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Chillicothe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chillicothe is blessed with natural beauty and stunning landscapes, making it perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Great Seal State Park

Great Seal State Park is the perfect spot for hiking and picnicking. The park boasts a network of trails that wind through the forest and lead to breathtaking scenic views, including a cliff that offers a panoramic view of the forest and mountains in the distance.

Scioto Trail State Park

Located just 20 minutes outside of Chillicothe, Scioto Trail State Park is another gem that offers a wide range of outdoor activities. Hiking, fishing, boating, and camping are all on the agenda at this state park. The park also offers guided horseback riding tours that take visitors through the park’s many trails.

Zaleski State Forest

Zaleski State Forest is a nature lover’s paradise. The vast forest spans over 26,000 acres and is home to some of the most stunning and rugged terrain in the state. The forest offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, horseback riding, and hunting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chillicothe

Chillicothe’s many hiking and biking trails offer a unique way to explore and experience the region’s natural beauty. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or just starting, there is an option for everyone.

Paint Creek State Park

Paint Creek State Park is a hidden gem that offers some of the best hiking and mountain biking trails in the area. The park boasts over 5,000 acres of rolling hills and stunning scenery. The loop around the lake is an easy hike that offers a variety of scenic views, including one of the lake’s more tranquil areas.

Buzzards Roost

Buzzards Roost is a challenging hiking trail that takes visitors up to a rocky outcropping that offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and hills. The trail is marked as difficult and features steep, rocky sections that will challenge even the most experienced hiker.

Best Things to Do in Chillicothe: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for a unique way to explore and experience Chillicothe, try one of these activities or tours.

Chillicothe Ghost Tour

Chillicothe’s rich history and haunting past make it a perfect location for ghost hunting. Join a guided ghost tour and explore the town’s most haunted locations. Expect to see spooky cemeteries and abandoned buildings that are said to be visited by ghosts.

Explore Chillicothe by bike

Chillicothe offers some of the most scenic bike routes in Ohio. Visitors can rent bikes and explore the town at their own pace. The bike rental company also offers guided tours that take visitors through some of Chillicothe’s most remarkable sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Chillicothe

Besides being a cultural and outdoor destination, Chillicothe is also a burgeoning food and drink scene that visitors cannot miss.

Must-Try Dishes

Chillicothe is known for its hearty and satisfying cuisine that reflects its rural roots.

Tomato Sandwich

The tomato sandwich is a regional specialty and a must-try when visiting Chillicothe. It’s a simple but delicious sandwich consisting of sliced tomatoes, bread, mayonnaise, and salt and pepper.

Buckeyes

Buckeyes are a beloved Ohio staple and one of the state’s signature desserts. The dessert is a ball-shaped candy made from peanut butter and chocolate that resembles the state tree’s buckeye nut.

Local Beverages

Ohio is home to a variety of delicious craft beers, spirits, and wines, and Chillicothe has many options that visitors can try.

Tecumseh and Tar Hollow by Single Tree Winery

Chillicothe is home to the Single Tree Winery, which produces delicious wines unique to the area. The Tecumseh and Tar Hollow are popular choices and have a distinct regional flavor.

Beers by the Schlegel Family Brewery

The Schlegel Family Brewery, located in nearby Circleville, produces some of the best craft beers in Ohio. Visitors can find a variety of flavorful beers on tap, including the Hop-fermented and Monkless Belgian.

Where to Stay in Chillicothe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

No matter what your budget is, there is an option for every traveler when it comes to accommodation in Chillicothe.

Best Western Adena Inn

The Best Western Adena Inn is a family-friendly hotel located conveniently near many of the town’s major attractions. The hotel offers modern amenities, including WiFi, a fitness center, and a pool.

Christopher Inn and Suites

Christopher Inn and Suites is a reasonably priced hotel that boasts clean rooms, friendly service, and a great location. Visitors staying at this hotel will appreciate the continental breakfast and easy access to the town’s restaurants and attractions.

What to Buy in Chillicothe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chillicothe has a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring back home.

Paper City Coffee

Paper City Coffee is a popular local coffee shop that offers delicious coffee and other drinks. Visitors can take back some of their favorite blend of coffee beans and enjoy a taste of Chillicothe at home.

Chillicothe Arts Council

The Chillicothe Arts Council houses a variety of handmade objects and artwork produced by local artists. Visitors can find unique souvenirs made of ceramics, glass, and wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Chillicothe

Chillicothe is known for its friendly people, hearty food, and appreciation of Ohio’s rural culture. Visitors should embrace and respect the area’s traditions while enjoying the town’s diverse offerings.

Best Time to Visit Chillicothe

The best time to visit Chillicothe is from late spring to early fall. During this time, visitors can enjoy the town’s many outdoor attractions and enjoy the mild weather and scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chillicothe

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the famous tomato sandwich and Buckeyes.
  • Visit the town in late summer to see the colorful wildflowers.
  • Explore the area’s wineries and craft breweries.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the area’s rural culture or traditions.
  • Ignore the town’s fascinating historical sites and museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chillicothe

Chillicothe is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal safety precautions and avoid walking alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Chillicothe

There are several healthcare facilities available in Chillicothe, including Adena Regional Medical Center and Genesis Healthcare System. Visitors should carry adequate health insurance and be prepared for unexpected medical emergencies.

Getting to Chillicothe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chillicothe

Chillicothe does not have its airport. The nearest airports are:

  • Port Columbus International Airport (CMH) - 65 miles
  • Rickenbacker International Airport (LCK) - 52 miles

Visitors can hire a cab or rent a car from any of the airports.

Bus Stations Near Chillicothe

The nearest bus stations are:

  • Chillicothe Bus Station
  • Columbus Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Chillicothe by train. The nearest Amtrak station is in Cincinnati, which is about 90 miles from Chillicothe.

How to Get Around in Chillicothe

Chillicothe is well connected with various transport options for getting around, including car rental, taxi, and public transportation.

Car Rental

Several national car rental companies operate in Chillicothe. Renting a car is a great option for exploring the town and its scenic surroundings.

Taxi

Several local cab companies offer taxi services in Chillicothe. Visitors should make sure the driver is licensed and the car has proper insurance before getting in.

Public Transportation

There is public bus service in Chillicothe that visitors can use to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Chillicothe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chillicothe is a great base for exploring the neighboring towns and attractions.

Columbus

Columbus is just an hour’s drive away from Chillicothe and boasts a vibrant art and cultural scene, including the Columbus Museum of Art and the Franklin Park Conservatory.

Hocking Hills State Park

Hocking Hills State Park offers a scenic escape in nature and has an array of hiking trails, caves, and waterfalls to explore. It’s just an hour’s drive from Chillicothe.

Conclusion

Chillicothe is a hidden gem that offers visitors a range of experiences, from exploring the town’s rich history to discovering the stunning natural beauty of the Miami - Scioto basins. With its vast range of outdoor activities, mouth-watering cuisine, and comfortable accommodation options, Chillicothe is the perfect getaway for both solo travelers and families. Explore Ohio’s hidden gem today!
