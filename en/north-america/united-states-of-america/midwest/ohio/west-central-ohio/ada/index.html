Ada may not be the most popular destination in Ohio, but it definitely deserves a spot on your travel bucket list. Located in West Central Ohio, Ada offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and a welcoming community. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best attractions, activities, accommodations, and local culture in Ada, Ohio.

Introduction to Ada

Ada is a small village in Hardin County, Ohio, with a population of around 5,000 people. The village was founded in 1853 and named after Ada Reed, the daughter of the town’s founder. The village is home to Ohio Northern University, which adds to the town’s charm and vibrant energy. The village’s rich history, beautiful natural attractions, and friendly community make it an excellent place to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ada

As a small town, Ada may not have numerous attractions, but it offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions that you won’t want to miss. Here are our top picks for the best places to visit in Ada:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ada Historical Museum: Learn about the town’s history and see artifacts from its founding at the Ada Historical Museum. The museum is located in the former town hall and is a must-visit for anyone interested in local history.
  • Hardin County Courthouse: The Hardin County Courthouse is an architectural masterpiece, with a unique design that combines Romanesque and Victorian elements. It is a must-see for anyone interested in architecture.
  • MacDonell House: The MacDonell House is a beautiful Victorian-style mansion built in the late 1800s. It is now a museum that showcases the life of upper-class families during that time.

Natural Attractions of Ada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ada Park: Ada Park is a beautiful green space that offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and a playground. It’s an excellent place to enjoy a relaxing afternoon in nature.
  • Hog Creek Falls: Located just outside of Ada, Hog Creek Falls is a stunning natural attraction that is worth the short drive. The falls are surrounded by beautiful scenery, making it a great place for a hike or picnic.
  • Black Swamp Preserve: Black Swamp Preserve is a 150-acre nature preserve that features wetlands, woods, and prairies. It’s an excellent place for birdwatching, hiking, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ada

If you enjoy hiking or biking, Ada offers plenty of options for you to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Ada:

  • Heritage Trail: The Heritage Trail is a 2-mile paved trail that runs from Ada to Ohio Northern University. It’s a great option for a leisurely walk or bike ride.
  • Black Swamp Preserve Trail: Black Swamp Preserve offers three different trails that range from 0.5 to 2 miles long. Each trail offers a unique look at the preserve’s diverse landscape.
  • Bicycle Route 25: Bicycle Route 25 runs through Ada and is part of the bike trail system that spans the state of Ohio. It’s a great option for experienced cyclists looking for a long-distance ride.

Best Things to Do in Ada: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Ada offers plenty of other activities and tours that you won’t want to miss. Here are some of the best things to do in Ada:

  • Wine Tasting at Vino Bellissimo: Vino Bellissimo is a winery located just outside of Ada, offering tastings of their award-winning wines. It’s an excellent way to spend an afternoon with friends or family.
  • Ada Theatre: The Ada Theatre is a historic movie theater that still shows the latest releases. It’s a great option for a night out in the town.
  • Ada Farmers Market: The Ada Farmers Market is held every Saturday morning from May to October. It’s an excellent place to stock up on fresh produce and handmade goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ada

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ada, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1-2: Explore the cultural attractions of Ada, including the museum, courthouse, and MacDonell House.

Day 3-4: Spend time in nature, hiking the trails at Ada Park and visiting Hog Creek Falls and Black Swamp Preserve.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby cities like Lima or Findlay.

Day 6: Enjoy a wine tasting at Vino Bellissimo and catch a movie at the Ada Theatre.

Day 7: Visit the Ada Farmers Market and spend the afternoon relaxing in the park or biking along the Heritage Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Ada

The food and drink scene in Ada may be small, but it’s mighty. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Ada:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burger at Wilson’s Sandwich Shop: Wilson’s Sandwich Shop is a local institution famous for its burgers and milkshakes. It’s a must-visit if you’re in Ada.
  • Pizza at Padrones: Padrones is a family-owned pizzeria that serves up delicious pies and subs.
  • Soft Serve Ice Cream at the Twist: The Twist is an ice cream stand that serves up some of the creamiest soft serve you’ll ever try.

Local Beverages

  • Wine at Vino Bellissimo: Vino Bellissimo produces award-winning wines that are a must-try for any wine lover.
  • Beer at Abbott’s Tavern: Abbott’s Tavern is a local bar that serves up a selection of craft beer from Ohio breweries.

Where to Stay in Ada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite being a small town, Ada offers plenty of options for accommodations. Here are some of the best places to stay in Ada:

  • Inn at ONU: The Inn at Ohio Northern University is a luxurious hotel located on the university’s campus. It offers comfortable rooms, excellent amenities, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: The Holiday Inn Express & Suites offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Airbnb: There are several Airbnb options in Ada, ranging from private rooms to entire apartments.

What to Buy in Ada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some ideas:

  • Ada Bulldogs Gear: If you’re a sports fan, show your support for the Ada Bulldogs with some gear from the ONU bookstore.
  • Ohio-made Products: Several stores in Ada sell Ohio-made products, including handmade soaps, candles, and jewelry.
  • Art at the Ada Artisan Market: The Ada Artisan Market is held every Saturday during the Farmers Market and features handmade art and crafts from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Ada

Ada is a friendly and welcoming community where visitors are treated like family. Some local customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting include:

  • Saying Hello: It’s customary to greet others with a friendly “Hello” or “Howdy” in Ada.
  • Showing Respect: Ada is a small town where everyone knows everyone else. It’s essential to show respect to locals as you explore the town.
  • Supporting Local Businesses: Ada is a tight-knit community where supporting local businesses is a top priority. Be sure to shop and dine at locally owned establishments during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Ada

The best time to visit Ada is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Spring and fall are also excellent times to visit for outdoor activities, as the temperatures are mild and pleasant. Winter can be harsh in Ohio, so if you plan to visit during the winter months, be sure to dress warmly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ada

Do:

  • Do greet locals with a friendly hello or howdy.
  • Do support locally owned businesses.
  • Do take time to explore the beautiful natural attractions in Ada.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or vandalize public spaces.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to locals or their customs.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ada is generally not a concern, but visitors should take reasonable precautions as they would in any small town. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Ada:

Safety in Ada

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking in secluded areas.
  • Always lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Ada

  • Be sure to use sunscreen and stay hydrated during outdoor activities.
  • If you need medical attention, Ada’s Hardin Memorial Hospital is located just a few miles outside of town.

Getting to Ada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ada

The nearest airport to Ada is the Toledo Express Airport, which is located 75 miles north of Ada. Alternatively, the John Glenn Columbus International Airport is approximately 90 miles south of Ada.

Bus Stations Near Ada

Greyhound and Trailways both have bus stations in Lima, which is approximately 15 miles from Ada. There is also a local bus service provided by Area Agency on Aging.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Ada and the surrounding areas. If you’re flying into the region, renting a car is the easiest way to get to Ada.

How to Get Around in Ada

Walking, biking, or driving are the most convenient ways to get around Ada. The Heritage Trail is an excellent option for walking or biking, and most of the town’s attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Ada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for nearby destinations to visit while in Ada, here are some options:

  • Lima: Lima is a larger city located just 15 miles from Ada. It offers shopping, dining, and cultural attractions, including the Allen County Museum and the Lima Symphony Orchestra.
  • Findlay: Findlay is a small town located 30 miles from Ada. It offers outdoor recreational activities, including hiking and fishing, as well as cultural attractions, including the Mazza Museum of International Art from Picture Books.
  • Marblehead Lighthouse State Park: Marblehead Lighthouse State Park is located approximately 70 miles from Ada. It offers beautiful Lake Erie views, hiking trails, and a historic lighthouse to tour.

Conclusion

Ada, Ohio, may be a small town, but it offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and a welcoming community that make it worth a visit. From hiking and biking to exploring the cultural attractions and enjoying the local food scene, there’s something for everyone in Ada. Plan your trip today and discover why this town in the heart of Ohio is more than just a small village.
