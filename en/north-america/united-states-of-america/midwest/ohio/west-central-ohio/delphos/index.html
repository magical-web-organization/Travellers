Introduction to Delphos

Located in West Central Ohio, Delphos is a charming small town that is rich in history and natural beauty. Known for its welcoming community, local eateries, vibrant arts scene, and outdoor recreation, Delphos has something to offer everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Postal History

Learn about the history of the postal service at the Museum of Postal History. Exhibits showcase the evolution of the postal system from its early days to the present, including stamp artwork, post office equipment, and historical delivery vehicles.

Delphos Canal Commission

The Delphos Canal Commission is a non-profit organization that preserves the history of the canal systems that played a significant role in the area’s economic development. The commission’s headquarters is located in the historic Canal Warehouse, which is now a museum, and offers tours of the canal boat replica.

Veterans Memorial Park

Located in downtown Delphos, the Veterans Memorial Park honors all veterans who have served the country. The park includes a statue honoring the town’s Vietnam veterans and monuments dedicated to all other branches of the military.

Stadium Park

Stadium Park hosts a variety of community events and festivals throughout the year. Visitors can also enjoy a 9-hole disc golf course, playgrounds, picnic areas, and nature trails.

A&W Drive-In

A&W Drive-In has been serving up classic American fast food and root beer since 1919. The drive-in is the perfect spot for a nostalgic dining experience with its car-hop service, vintage décor, and friendly staff.

Delphos Canal Boat Ride

Take a ride on the Delphos Canal Boat to experience the area’s rich canal history. The boat ride includes a guided tour and showcases the canal’s restored lock and waterway.

Lock Sixteen Historical District

Lock Sixteen Historical District offers visitors a glimpse into the area’s past, with restored buildings that date back to the 19th century. The district includes Canal Park, which features a replica canal boat, a restored lock, and a walking path.

Natural Attractions of Delphos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ottoville Reservoir

The Ottoville Reservoir provides opportunities for fishing, boating, kayaking, and bird watching. It is also a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Jennings Environmental Education Center

Jennings Center is a nature reserve that features walking trails, bird watching, and wildlife spotting. The center also offers educational programs about the region’s native plants and animals.

Middle Point Wetlands Preserve

The Middle Point Wetlands Preserve is home to diverse and unique wetland habitats that are home to a variety of species, including migratory birds and rare plants. The preserve offers hiking trails and guided nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delphos

Delphos Canal Towpath Trail

Following the banks of the Miami and Erie Canal, the Delphos Canal Towpath Trail features hiking and biking paths that provide scenic views of the Delphos and Ottoville communities. It offers opportunities to observe wildlife and birds.

Lincoln Highway Buyway

Stretching over 200 miles throughout Ohio, the Lincoln Highway Buyway is a popular scenic drive through Delphos and other historic towns. The route takes in historic attractions, neighborhoods and farmlands.

Best Things to Do in Delphos: Activities and Tours

Vermillion Winery & Vineyards

Indulge in wine tasting and tours of the vineyards at Vermillion Winery, just south of Delphos. Visitors can experience the country’s best wines and a sweeping view of the countryside.

Art Center for the Arts

Art Center for the Arts is a community art studio that offers classes, workshops, and exhibitions. The center focuses on a number of art forms, including ceramics, painting, and drawing.

Drive-In Movie Theater

Experience a blast from the past at the Van-Del Drive-In Theater. Visitors can enjoy a double feature while sitting in their car or on lawn chairs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delphos

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Delphos, here’s an itinerary that you may find helpful:

Day 1: Explore downtown Delphos and visit the Museum of Postal History and Veterans Memorial Park.

Day 2: Take a ride on the Delphos Canal Boat and tour the Delphos Canal Commission’s canal warehouse on foot.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Cleveland, visiting the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the various local restaurants and shops.

Day 4: Hike the Delphos Canal Towpath Trail and the walking trails at the Middle Point Wetlands Preserve.

Day 5: Visit the Ottoville Reservoir for a day of fishing and kayaking.

Day 6: Visit the Van-Del Drive-In theater and enjoy a night under the stars.

Day 7: End the trip by shopping for local crafts and souvenirs at Lock Sixteen Historical District.

Food and Drink Scene of Delphos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Delphos Pizza has been serving up authentic, thin crust pizza and Italian dishes since 1954.
  • The Old Mill Stream Inn, established in 1929, is known for their broasted chicken and homemade pies.
  • Rustic Cafe serves delicious breakfast and lunch items with a local flair.

Local Beverages

  • The University of Northwestern Ohio Beer Company, located on campus, offers local brews with seasonal favorites.
  • The Kewpee, a regional fast food chain originating in Lima, Ohio, is famous for its creamy milkshakes.

Where to Stay in Delphos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: Located in the center of town, the hotel has modern amenities like an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Delphos Inn: This Intimate inn offers lodgings in a restored mansion with an exclusive on site restaurant serving American comfort food.
  • Guesthouse Delphos, Live Music Hub: This alternative guesthouse provides a unique experience with a rustic theme, with live music events and craft beers available.

What to Buy in Delphos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lock Sixteen Historical District has a great selection of antiques, home decor and local souvenirs. The farmers market is open during summer months, offering local fresh produce.
  • Downtown Delphos has a variety of boutiques and gift shops offering clothing, accessories, home goods and various gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Delphos

Delphos is a town that values its history and its community. Residents take pride in preserving the town’s heritage and many events revolve around bringing people together to celebrate the place they call home. Guests are welcomed by friendly locals, and engaged in activities, events and attractions that exemplify the small town atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Delphos

The best time to visit Delphos is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and sunny, and community events, festivals and outdoor activities are plenty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delphos

Do:

  • Explore the historic Lock Sixteen District and the Delphos Canal Commission.
  • Visit downtown Delphos for an authentic small-town experience.
  • Savor locally sourced food and fresh brews.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the area’s rich history and natural wonders.
  • Forget to respect the comfort and privacy of local residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Delphos

Delphos is generally a safe area, but visitors should still take precautions when exploring the town. Always be aware of your surroundings and lock up valuables in your car or hotel room when not in use.

Health in Delphos

Visitors to Delphos should practice basic hygiene when traveling, including washing your hands frequently and keeping well-hydrated. There are also several nearby hospitals and medical centers should you need medical attention during your stay.

Getting to Delphos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Delphos

The nearest major airport to Delphos is Fort Wayne International Airport, located an hour’s drive away in Fort Wayne, Indiana.

Bus Stations Near Delphos

Delphos is served by Greyhound Bus Lines, which stops at the Lima Bus Station, just 25 minutes away.

Other Options

Visitors may explore the historic town by their own cars, but renting a car or using a ride service may be a more flexible option.

How to Get Around in Delphos

Walking and biking are the ideal ways to get around Delphos, as the town is mostly flat and pedestrian-friendly. Taxis, rental cars, and ride-sharing services are other available options.

Where to Go Next from Delphos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Lake St. Marys State Park, just 30 minutes away, offers swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Lima, Ohio, a larger city located 20 minutes away provides for bigger entertainment, shopping, and dining options.
  • The university town of Bowling Green, Ohio, is just a 1-hour 30-minute drive from Delphos.

Conclusion

Delphos, Ohio, is a small town with a big heart and plenty of things to do and see. Visitors can explore the area’s rich canal history, enjoy outdoor recreation in the natural beauty, and savor local cuisine and drinks. Whether it’s a day trip or a week-long vacation, Delphos is a destination that should not be missed.
