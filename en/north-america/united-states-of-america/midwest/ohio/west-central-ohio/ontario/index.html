Introduction to Ontario, Ohio

Ontario is a city in West Central Ohio, situated in the Midwest region of the United States of America. Its strategic location near major highways provides easy access and convenience for travelers. Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Ontario has something for everyone. The city boasts of numerous attractions, hiking trails, hotels, eateries, and cultural events all year round.

List of Places to Visit in Ontario

If you are wondering what to see in Ontario, we recommend visiting the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kingwood Center Gardens - a beautiful 47-acre public garden and a historic estate with elegant gardens, trails, and greenhouses.
  • Ohio State Reformatory - explore the famous prison featured in the movie “The Shawshank Redemption” and learn about its history.
  • Ohio Genealogical Society - discover your family history and find out more about Ohio’s history.
  • Brownella Cottage and Malabar Farm State Park - tour the historic Brownella Cottage and explore the storytelling farm of famous author Louis Bromfield, which is the location for movie “The Shawshank Redemption”

Natural Attractions of Ontario: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mohican State Park - with over 1,100 acres of preserved forests and trails, Mohican State Park is a nature lover’s paradise, which features kayaking, swimming, and picnicking.
  • Clearfork Reservoir - a popular spot for fishing, boating, hiking, and picnicking.
  • B&O Bike Trail - a 16.5 miles long trail that cuts across Richland County and has scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ontario

Ontario has a variety of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of these include:

  • Richland B&O Trail - a 10.5 mile trail through the beautiful countryside of Richland County.
  • Gorman Nature Center Trail - a 2.2 miles loop trail in Mansfield with easy terrain and suitable for families with kids.
  • Malabar Farm State Park Trail - a 3.7 miles loop trail near Lucas with a beautiful waterfall as its highlight.

Best Things to Do in Ontario: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Ontario:

  • Take a tour of the famous Ohio State Reformatory and see where “The Shawshank Redemption” was filmed.
  • Spend a day exploring the beautiful Kingwood Center Gardens and admire the colorful florals and architectural details.
  • Go on a kayaking adventure on the Mohican River in Mohican State Park.
  • Check out some thrilling roller coasters and family rides at Cedar Point Amusement Park in nearby Sandusky, Ohio.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ontario

Here is an ideal one week itinerary if you’re planning a trip to Ontario:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrival in Ontario and check-in at your hotel. Explore Downtown Mansfield and visit the Book Loft and Little Buckeye Children’s Museum.
Day 2 Visit Bellefontaine, OH, and hike the beautiful Zane Shawnee Caverns and explore Logan County History Center.
Day 3 Hop on the Richland B&O Trail for some scenic biking and hiking. Visit the Mansfield Art Center and the Ohio Bird Sanctuary.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Cedar Point Amusement Park in Sandusky, Ohio.
Day 5 Spend the day exploring the Mohican State Park and Clear Fork Reservoir.
Day 6 Take the day to learn about Ohio’s rich history and tour the Ohio State Reformatory, and Malabar Farm State Park.
Day 7 Departure from Ontario.

Food and Drink Scene of Ontario

Ontario is a great city for foodies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sloppy Joe’s - a classic American sandwich with a ground beef filling, onions, and tomato sauce served on a bun.
  • Buckeye Candy - peanut butter balls partially dipped in chocolate to resemble the nut of the Ohio State Buckeye tree.

Local Beverages

  • Mid-Ohio Brewing Company - This brewery produces a variety of beer, including stouts, ales, and IPAs.
  • Phoenix Brewing Company - This Ohio brewery offers year-round and seasonal beers, and unique food like pizzas and nachos.
  • Mansfield Distributing - This local beverage distributor has a wide selection of draft beers, wines, and spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Ontario

Ohio is a diverse state with a mix of urban and rural cultures. The people of Ontario are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Always ask for permission before taking photos of people or their property. Smoking is prohibited in public places and parks.

Best Time to Visit Ontario

The best time to visit Ontario is during spring and fall when the temperatures are moderate, and the nature turns into brilliant colors. Summers can be hot and humid, with average daytime temperatures in the 80s, and winters can be cold with average daytime temperatures in the 30-50s.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ontario

Here are some do’s and don’ts of visiting Ontario:

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated during summer months.
  • Respect local traditions and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Smoke in public places such as restaurants, bars, or parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ontario

Ontario is generally considered a safe place to visit. Still, you should take necessary precautions like locking your car, hotel room, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. While hiking trails, always carry a map and be aware of the wildlife, like snakes and poisonous spiders.

Health in Ontario

Ontario has a few medical facilities and hospitals that can provide medical care for minor and major illnesses.

Getting to Ontario: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ontario is well-connected via highways and has a small municipal airport in close proximity.

Airports Near Ontario

  • Akron-Canton Regional Airport - 68 miles away from Ontario
  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport - 81 miles away from Ontario

Bus Stations Near Ontario

  • Greyhound Bus - 3 miles away from Ontario

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available at nearby airports.

How to Get Around in Ontario

The most convenient way to get around Ontario is by renting a car. Local taxi and shuttle services are available for transportation to and from the airport.

Where to Go Next from Ontario: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and day trips to explore from Ontario:

  • Cleveland, Ohio - 80 miles away
  • Columbus, Ohio - 70 miles away
  • Sandusky, Ohio - 67 miles away

Conclusion

Ontario, Ohio, is a hidden gem in the Midwest region of the United States. Its history, nature, and attractive landmarks make it an ideal destination for solo travelers, couples, and families. Plan your trip to Ontario and experience everything this city has to offer, from nature parks, museums, local foods and beverages, cultural events to outdoor adventures.
