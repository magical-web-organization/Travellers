Introduction to Lexington

Located in the heart of Ohio, Lexington is a charming small town that’s perfect for a leisurely weekend getaway or a week-long vacation filled with outdoor adventures and cultural attractions. With its picturesque countryside scenery and friendly locals, Lexington is an ideal destination for families, couples, and solo travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Lexington

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Lexington. Here are some of the top places to visit in Lexington:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ohio State Reformatory: A must-visit for history lovers, the Ohio State Reformatory is a former prison that’s now a museum. It’s famous for its role in the movie The Shawshank Redemption.

  • Mansfield Memorial Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Mansfield area and its people.

  • Malabar Farm State Park: The birthplace and former home of author Louis Bromfield, this park offers a glimpse into Ohio farm life during the first half of the 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Lexington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mohican State Park: Known for its hiking trails, horseback riding, and canoeing, Mohican State Park is a great place to spend a day outdoors.

  • Clear Fork Reservoir: With plenty of fishing, boating, and hiking opportunities, Clear Fork Reservoir is a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike.

  • Kingwood Center Gardens: This 47-acre estate features beautifully landscaped gardens, a greenhouse, and a historical mansion that’s open for tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lexington

For outdoor enthusiasts, hiking and biking are two of the best ways to explore the beautiful countryside of Lexington. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Lexington:

  • B&O Trail: This 18-mile pathway runs between Lexington and Butler and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or hike.

  • Richland B&O Trail: This 18-mile trail runs through the heart of Richland County and is popular with hikers and mountain bikers.

  • Mohican State Park: With over 15 miles of hiking trails, Mohican State Park has something for everyone, from easy walks to challenging climbs.

Best Things to Do in Lexington: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Lexington. Here are some of the best activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Ghost Walks of Lexington: Take a spooky tour of the Ohio State Reformatory with the Ghost Walks of Lexington and learn about the prison’s haunted history.

  • Mohican Canoe Livery: For a fun day on the water, rent a canoe or kayak from the Mohican Canoe Livery and paddle down the Mohican River.

  • Richland Carrousel Park: This colorful carousel is a popular attraction for kids and adults alike, with 52 hand-carved animals and a restored 1899 G.A. Dentzel mechanism.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lexington

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Lexington, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Lexington

No trip to Lexington is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeyes: These peanut butter and chocolate candies are a favorite treat throughout Ohio.

  • Chicken Paprikash: This Hungarian-inspired dish features tender chicken in a creamy paprika sauce served over homemade spaetzle.

  • Apple Dumplings: A delicious dessert made with apples, cinnamon, sugar, and a flaky pastry crust.

Local Beverages

  • Ohio craft beer: With dozens of craft breweries throughout the state, Ohio is known for its delicious and unique beers.

  • Wine: The Lexington area is home to several wineries, including Wishmaker House Winery and The Winery at Wolf Creek.

Where to Stay in Lexington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Lexington has plenty of places to stay. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Lexington:

  • Spruce Hill Inn & Cottages: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy guest rooms and private cottages in a quiet countryside setting.

  • Holiday Inn & Suites Mansfield-Conference Center: With its modern amenities and convenient location, this hotel is a great choice for business and leisure travelers alike.

  • Mohican Lodge and Conference Center: This spacious lodge is located inside Mohican State Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest.

What to Buy in Lexington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Lexington by shopping for souvenirs and local products during your visit. Here are some top picks:

  • Ohio-made maple syrup: Sweeten up your pancakes with maple syrup made in Ohio.

  • Amish-made furniture: The Amish community in Ohio is renowned for its handcrafted furniture, which you can purchase at local shops and markets.

  • Spices and seasonings: Bring home a taste of Ohio with locally made spices and seasonings, such as Lake Erie Walleye Rub and Buckeye Bourbon Rub.

Local Culture and Customs of Lexington

One of the things that sets Lexington apart is its friendly and welcoming community. Here are some cultural customs to be aware of during your visit:

  • Say “please” and “thank you”: Ohioans value politeness and courtesy, so be sure to use these phrases when interacting with locals.

  • Dress appropriately: While Lexington is a relatively casual town, it’s always a good idea to dress respectfully when visiting cultural attractions or dining at upscale restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Lexington

The best time to visit Lexington is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Spring and fall are also good times to visit, with mild temperatures and colorful foliage. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it’s a great time to enjoy cozy indoor activities like wine tasting and museum hopping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lexington

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Lexington:

Do:

  • Do sample the local cuisine, especially traditional dishes like chicken paprikash and Ohio buckeyes.

  • Do explore the great outdoors by hiking, biking, or canoeing along the Mohican River.

  • Do take a tour of the Ohio State Reformatory and learn about the prison’s fascinating history and Hollywood connections.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather and activities. Layers are always a good idea in Ohio.

  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals. Ohioans are friendly and welcoming.

  • Don’t miss out on the local wine and craft beer scene. Ohio is home to some of the best wineries and breweries in the country.

Safety and Health

Lexington is a safe and healthy destination for travelers. Here are some tips to keep in mind during your visit:

Safety in Lexington

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially when walking in unfamiliar areas.

  • Keep valuables like cameras and wallets out of sight when not in use.

  • Use caution when swimming or boating in open bodies of water and always wear a life jacket.

Health in Lexington

  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot summer months.

  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin from harmful UV rays.

  • Pack insect repellent to protect against mosquitoes and ticks.

Getting to Lexington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you’re traveling by plane, car, or bus, getting to Lexington is easy and convenient.

Airports Near Lexington

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport: Located 90 miles north of Lexington, this airport is a major hub for domestic and international flights.

  • John Glenn Columbus International Airport: Located 70 miles south of Lexington, this airport serves several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Lexington

  • Greyhound: The closest Greyhound bus station to Lexington is located in Mansfield, just a 15-minute drive from the town center.

Other Options

  • Car rental: If you’re flying into a nearby airport, renting a car is a convenient way to get around.

  • Private transportation: Local taxi and rideshare services are available in Lexington.

How to Get Around in Lexington

While having your own car is the easiest way to get around Lexington and its surroundings, there are other public transportation options available, such as:

Public Transportation

  • Richland County Transit: This public transportation system serves Lexington and the surrounding areas with several bus routes.

  • Uber and Lyft: Rideshare services are available in Lexington.

Bike Rentals

  • Black Fork Bicycles: Located in Mohican, this bike rental shop offers a variety of bikes for rent, including mountain bikes and road bikes.

Where to Go Next from Lexington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Ohio, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Cleveland: Located two hours north of Lexington, Cleveland is a vibrant city with a rich cultural scene and plenty of dining, shopping, and entertainment options.

  • Columbus: Located two hours south of Lexington, Columbus is Ohio’s capital city and home to several museums, parks, and attractions.

  • Amish Country: Just a short drive from Lexington, Amish Country is a peaceful and picturesque region known for its traditional culture and handcrafted goods.

Conclusion

With its beautiful scenery, friendly locals, and wealth of cultural and outdoor attractions, Lexington is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or explore new sights and sounds, Lexington has something for everyone.
