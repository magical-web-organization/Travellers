Introduction to Kenton

Kenton is a charming town located in West Central Ohio that offers a range of unique attractions for travelers of all interests. The town is known for its historical landmarks, outdoor spaces, and vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Kenton has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kenton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kenton is rich in history, and there are many museums and historical sites to explore. The Hardin County Historical Museum houses exhibits on local history and culture, including artifacts from the county’s Native American and early settler populations. The Simon Kenton Memorial Bridge honors the life of the legendary frontiersman, Simon Kenton, and provides stunning views of the Scioto River. The Kenton Armory is a unique Art Deco-style building that was built in the 1930s.

Natural Attractions of Kenton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy exploring the many parks and outdoor spaces in and around Kenton. The Simon Kenton Trail is a scenic biking and hiking trail that runs through many of the town’s parks and recreational areas. The Kenton City Parks offer a range of outdoor activities, including playgrounds, picnic areas, and sports fields. For a more challenging hike, head to the nearby Huffman Prairie State Natural Landmark, which boasts over 100 acres of protected woods and prairie and a range of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kenton

Kenton is a great place to bike or hike, with many scenic routes to choose from. The Simon Kenton Trail bike path is a 35-mile trail that runs through Kenton and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is open year-round and is a popular destination for both locals and visitors.

Best Things to Do in Kenton: Activities and Tours

Kenton offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. The Hardin County Fair is held each September and features livestock exhibits, carnival rides, and live music. For a unique experience, take a horse-drawn carriage ride through Kenton’s historic downtown.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kenton

Here is a week-long itinerary for Kenton:

Day 1: Visit the Hardin County Historical Museum in the morning, and then head to the Simon Kenton Memorial Bridge for lunch and stunning river views. Spend the afternoon hiking the Simon Kenton Trail and exploring the Kenton City Parks.

Day 2: Take a horse-drawn carriage ride through downtown Kenton in the morning. After lunch, head to the Huffman Prairie State Natural Landmark for some challenging hiking.

Day 3: Visit the Hardin County Fairgrounds and enjoy the fair’s many exhibits, rides, and concerts.

Day 4: Take a scenic drive through the countryside and stop at local farms and farmers’ markets to sample fresh produce and local cuisine.

Day 5: Visit the Kenton Armory in the morning, and then head to the local breweries and wineries in the afternoon for tastings and tours.

Day 6: Spend the day exploring the nearby towns of Ada and Bellefontaine.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Kenton’s many parks or recreational areas, such as the Saulisberry Park or Wharton Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Kenton

Must-Try Dishes

Kenton is known for its classic American fare and regional specialties. Be sure to try the Buckeye Burger, which features Ohio beef topped with cheddar cheese and a peanut butter sauce. The Frita is another local favorite, which is a Cuban-inspired burger made with pork and chorizo.

Local Beverages

Kenton has a growing beer and wine scene, with several local breweries and wineries that offer tastings and tours. Be sure to try the Kenton-made beers and wines at the Bellefontaine Brewing Company and the V-Twin Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Kenton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kenton offers a range of lodging options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The Plaza Inn is a popular choice for its comfortable accommodations and convenient location. The Kenton Station Bed and Breakfast is also a great option for those seeking a more intimate experience.

What to Buy in Kenton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a unique souvenir, head to the Tinker’s Dam Gift Shop, which offers handmade crafts and goods from local artisans. The Kenton Farmers’ Market is also a great place to pick up fresh produce and locally made goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Kenton

Kenton is a classic American town with a rich agricultural history. The community values hard work, family, and good food and drink. Visitors will find the locals friendly and welcoming.

Best Time to Visit Kenton

The best time to visit Kenton is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and there are many outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kenton

Kenton is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should be mindful of their belongings and take basic safety precautions.

Health in Kenton

Kenton has several medical facilities and hospitals that offer quality care. Visitors should be prepared for the weather, which can be hot and humid in the summer months.

Getting to Kenton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kenton is easily accessible by car, with many major highways running through the town. The closest airports are the Dayton International Airport and the Port Columbus International Airport.

How to Get Around in Kenton

Kenton is a small town that is easily walkable. Biking and hiking are also popular modes of transportation. Visitors can also rent cars or take taxis if needed.

Where to Go Next from Kenton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kenton is located in the heart of West Central Ohio, with many nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Some nearby towns and attractions include Ada, Bellefontaine, and the Ohio Caverns.

Conclusion

Kenton is a hidden gem in West Central Ohio that offers a range of unique attractions for visitors. With its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene, there is something for everyone to enjoy in this charming Midwestern town.
