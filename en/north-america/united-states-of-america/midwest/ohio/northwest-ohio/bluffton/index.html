Introduction to Bluffton

Nestled in the heart of Northwest Ohio, Bluffton is a charming town that you won’t find on many maps. Despite its size, however, Bluffton is packed with attractions and activities for travelers of all interests. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoors enthusiast, or a foodie looking for your next culinary adventure, Bluffton is sure to have something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bluffton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bluffton’s history can be traced back to the mid-1800s, and many of the town’s most iconic landmarks date back to this time period. Some of the must-see cultural attractions in Bluffton include:

  • Bluffton University – This liberal arts institution was founded in 1899 and is one of the best-known landmarks in the area. Visitors can take a campus tour or attend one of the many events hosted by the university throughout the year.
  • Bluffton Presbyterian Church – This historic church dates back to 1857 and is an architectural marvel. Be sure to take some time to admire the stained glass windows and intricately carved woodwork inside!
  • Bluffton Public Library – This library is much more than just a place to check out books. It’s also home to a wealth of historical information about Bluffton and the surrounding area, including photographs, maps, and documents.

Natural Attractions of Bluffton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to get some fresh air and take in some beautiful scenery, Bluffton has plenty of outdoor activities to choose from. Some of the most popular natural attractions in the area include:

  • Riley Creek Trail – This 5-mile trail winds through the heart of Bluffton and is perfect for hiking, biking, or just taking a leisurely stroll. Along the way, you’ll pass through parks, residential neighborhoods, and even the downtown business district.
  • Oakwoods Nature Preserve – This 200-acre nature preserve is home to more than 2 miles of hiking trails, as well as fishing ponds, picnic areas, and a variety of wildlife.
  • Blanchard River Nature Trail – This 1.6-mile trail follows the meandering course of the Blanchard River and offers plenty of opportunities for bird-watching, fishing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bluffton

If you’re looking to explore Bluffton on foot or by bike, you’re in luck. The town is home to several well-maintained trails and routes, including:

  • Bluffton Bikeway – This 3-mile paved trail winds through the town’s parks and residential areas, offering a great opportunity to get some exercise and take in some scenery.
  • Easterday Nature Preserve Trail – This 2-mile trail is located just outside of Bluffton and features a mix of wooded areas and open fields. It’s a great spot for bird-watching and wildlife viewing.
  • Mt. Cory to Bluffton Trail – This 6-mile unpaved trail offers challenging terrain and breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. If you’re up for a workout, this is the trail for you!

Best Things to Do in Bluffton: Activities and Tours

For those looking for some organized activities or tours, Bluffton has plenty of options. Some of the most popular include:

  • Bluffton Farmer’s Market – This vibrant market takes place every Saturday morning from May through October and features a wide variety of local produce, baked goods, and crafts.
  • Bluffton Historic Walking Tour – This self-guided tour takes you through the most important historical landmarks in the town, including Bluffton University, the Presbyterian Church, and the Village Pump.
  • Buckeye Trail Hike and Beer Tasting Tour – This guided tour takes you on a scenic hike along the Buckeye Trail, followed by a visit to a local microbrewery for some well-deserved beers!

Week-Long Itinerary for Bluffton

If you’re planning to spend a week in Bluffton, there’s plenty to keep you busy. Here’s one possible itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Bluffton University and take a walking tour of the town’s historic landmarks.
  • Day 2: Explore the Oakwoods Nature Preserve and go fishing at one of the park’s ponds.
  • Day 3: Take a guided biking tour of the area’s best trails.
  • Day 4: Visit the Bluffton Farmer’s Market and enjoy some local produce for lunch.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Lima, OH, to visit the Allen County Museum and enjoy some shopping and dining.
  • Day 6: Spend the day hiking the Blanchard River Nature Trail and taking in the local wildlife.
  • Day 7: Visit a local microbrewery and enjoy a beer tasting tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Bluffton

Must-Try Dishes

If you’re looking to sample some of the local cuisine during your stay in Bluffton, be sure to try the following dishes:

  • Buckeyes – These peanut butter and chocolate candies are a staple of Ohio cuisine, and you’ll find them on many menus in Bluffton.
  • Bologna Sandwiches – Yes, you read that right. Bluffton is home to the annual Bologna Festival, and bologna sandwiches are a popular local delicacy.
  • Amish-style Fried Chicken – Many of the restaurants in Bluffton offer their take on this classic dish, which features crispy, breaded chicken served with sides like mashed potatoes and green beans.

Local Beverages

Beer lovers will find plenty to enjoy in Bluffton, thanks to the town’s active microbrewery scene. Be sure to try a pint of the local brew during your visit!

Where to Stay in Bluffton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bluffton has a variety of lodging options to choose from, including:

  • Bluffton Comfort Suites – This modern hotel features comfortable rooms and an indoor heated pool.
  • The Cask Room Guesthouse – This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of downtown Bluffton and offers easy access to the town’s many attractions.
  • The Claymore Bed & Breakfast – This beautiful Victorian-style mansion offers elegant rooms and a peaceful, serene setting.

What to Buy in Bluffton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to remember your Bluffton trip? Consider purchasing one of the following local products:

  • Handcrafted Pottery – Bluffton is home to several skilled potters, and you’ll find their unique pieces for sale at local shops and markets.
  • Bath and Body Products – Many local artisans specialize in creating organic, all-natural bath and body products, including soaps, lotions, and scrubs.
  • Local Honey – Beekeeping is a popular hobby in the area, and you’ll find plenty of fresh, local honey for sale at farmers’ markets and roadside stands.

Local Culture and Customs of Bluffton

Bluffton is a tight-knit community with a strong emphasis on family, faith, and tradition. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including the town’s strong Mennonite and Amish heritage.

Best Time to Visit Bluffton

The best time to visit Bluffton is in the late spring or early fall, when the weather is mild and the town’s many outdoor attractions are at their most beautiful. However, visitors can enjoy Bluffton’s many offerings year-round!

Do’s and Don’ts of Bluffton

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Bluffton:

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the town’s many parks and outdoor attractions.
  • Support local businesses by shopping at farmers’ markets, artisan shops, and other small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Make assumptions about local lifestyles or beliefs.
  • Leave litter or damage local landmarks or natural areas.
  • Ignore local traffic laws or safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bluffton

Bluffton is a safe and welcoming town with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors and securing valuables, but can generally feel comfortable exploring all parts of the town.

Health in Bluffton

Bluffton is home to several small clinics and pharmacies, but visitors with serious health concerns should seek care at a larger medical center in nearby Lima, OH.

Getting to Bluffton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bluffton

The nearest airport to Bluffton is the Toledo Express Airport, located about 1 hour and 15 minutes to the north.

Bus Stations Near Bluffton

The nearest bus station to Bluffton is the Greyhound station in Lima, OH, about 20 minutes to the west.

Other Options

For visitors with their own vehicles, Bluffton is easily accessible via several major highways, including I-75 and U.S. Route 30.

How to Get Around in Bluffton

Bluffton’s compact size makes it easy to get around on foot or by bike. However, visitors may find it helpful to have a car for longer trips or to visit attractions outside of town.

Where to Go Next from Bluffton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to spend in the area, consider visiting one of these nearby destinations:

  • Findlay, OH – This charming town is located just 20 minutes to the south of Bluffton and is home to several parks, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • Put-in-Bay, OH – Located about 2 hours to the north, Put-in-Bay is a beautiful island town known for its sandy beaches, water sports activities, and lively nightlife.
  • Toledo, OH – About an hour to the north of Bluffton, Toledo is a thriving urban center with a world-class zoo, several museums, and a vibrant arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Bluffton may be a small town, but it’s big on charm, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or gastronomy, there’s plenty to explore and discover in this beautiful corner of Northwest Ohio. Be sure to add Bluffton to your travel bucket list!
