Introduction to Montpelier

Montpelier is a charming town located in Northwest Ohio, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. Montpelier is known for its rich history, beautiful scenery, and friendly locals. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Montpelier has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, local culture and customs, safety tips, and transportation options in Montpelier.

List of Places to Visit in Montpelier

Montpelier is home to several culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here are some places you should definitely check out during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Williams County Historical Society Museum: The museum houses an impressive collection of local artifacts and treasures that showcase Montpelier’s rich history.
  • The Spangler Candy Company: Take a tour of the factory and learn about the history of this iconic candy company that has been producing delicious treats for over a century.
  • St. Joseph Catholic Church: This beautiful church is a pillar of the local community and features stunning stained-glass windows and intricate architecture.

Natural Attractions of Montpelier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harrison Lake State Park: This park is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and features several hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and camping sites.
  • Tiffin River Wildlife Area: Immerse yourself in nature at this wildlife area that features a wide range of flora and fauna, including several species of birds and mammals.
  • Covered Bridge Park: Take a stroll through this peaceful park that features a historic covered bridge and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montpelier

Montpelier is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several trails suitable for all skill levels. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • The Harrison Lake State Park Trail: This 4.3-mile trail takes you through the heart of Harrison Lake State Park and features stunning views of the lake and surrounding forest.
  • The Buckeye Trail: This 1,444-mile trail crosses through Ohio and features several sections that are accessible from Montpelier.
  • The Kiwanis Walking Trail: This 2-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll and features several benches and picnic areas along the way.

Best Things to Do in Montpelier: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling solo, with friends, or with family, Montpelier offers plenty of fun activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Go fishing or boating at Harrison Lake State Park
  • Take a candy-making class at the Spangler Candy Company
  • Book a hot air balloon ride for a unique perspective of Montpelier and the surrounding area
  • Attend a local festival such as the Williams County Fair or the Montpelier Bean Days

Week-Long Itinerary for Montpelier

Day 1: Check-in at a local hotel or guesthouse and explore downtown Montpelier.

Day 2: Visit the Williams County Historical Society Museum and learn about the town’s history. In the afternoon, take a hike at the Harrison Lake State Park Trail.

Day 3: Spend the day at the Spangler Candy Company and take a candy-making class. In the evening, attend a local event or festival.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Toledo and visit the Toledo Museum of Art and the Toledo Zoo.

Day 5: Explore the Tiffin River Wildlife Area and go birdwatching or wildlife spotting. In the afternoon, visit the St. Joseph Catholic Church and admire its beautiful architecture.

Day 6: Spend the day at Covered Bridge Park and have a picnic. In the evening, take a hot air balloon ride for a memorable experience.

Day 7: Depart from Montpelier and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Montpelier

Montpelier offers plenty of delicious food and drink options that are sure to please every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Burger: A local specialty that features a juicy burger topped with peanut butter and bacon.
  • Pour House Pizza: Mouth-watering pizza served with a variety of toppings.
  • Bean Soup: A hearty soup made from local beans and served with cornbread.

Local Beverages

  • Spangler Candy Canes: A classic candy cane that comes in a variety of flavors such as peppermint, cinnamon, and grape.
  • Cranberry Wine: A sweet and tart wine made from local cranberries.
  • Hominy Corn Beer: A traditional Native American beer that is still brewed locally.

Where to Stay in Montpelier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montpelier offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Montpelier:

  • The Montpelier Inn: A charming hotel located in the heart of downtown Montpelier.
  • The Buckeye Inn: A budget-friendly motel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • The Country Hearth Inn and Suites: A luxurious hotel with spacious rooms and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Montpelier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Montpelier with you by picking up some local souvenirs or products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Montpelier:

  • Spangler Candy: Stock up on your favorite candy canes and other treats from the Spangler Candy Company.
  • Handmade Crafts: Montpelier is home to several talented artisans who create beautiful handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Local Maple Syrup: Ohio is known for its delicious maple syrup, and Montpelier is no exception. Pick up a bottle of local syrup to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Montpelier

Montpelier is a friendly and welcoming town that values its traditions and history. When visiting Montpelier, it is important to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • When dining out, it is customary to leave a small tip for your server.
  • Greet locals with a smile and a friendly “hello.”

Best Time to Visit Montpelier

The best time to visit Montpelier is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, consider visiting in the fall or spring.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montpelier

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s history and culture.
  • Do try the local food and drinks.
  • Do greet locals with a smile and a friendly “hello.”

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public areas.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to locals or their property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montpelier

Montpelier is a generally safe town, but it is always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep your valuables in a secure location, such as a hotel safe or locked car trunk.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Follow traffic laws and wear a helmet when biking.

Health in Montpelier

Montpelier has several hospitals and medical clinics in case of emergencies. Be sure to pack any necessary medications and consult with your doctor before traveling.

  • Fort Wayne International Airport (FWA)

How to Get Around in Montpelier

Montpelier is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. However, if you prefer to drive or take public transportation, several options are available:

  • Rental Car: Several rental car agencies operate in the area, making it easy to explore nearby attractions.
  • Bicycle: Several bike rental shops operate in the area.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available for hire throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Montpelier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montpelier is surrounded by several beautiful towns and cities that are worth exploring. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you should consider:

  • Toledo: A vibrant city located about 50 miles from Montpelier. Toledo offers several cultural attractions, including museums and theaters.
  • Fort Wayne: Located about an hour’s drive from Montpelier, Fort Wayne is home to several parks and museums.
  • Cedar Point: If you are looking for an adrenaline rush, consider visiting Cedar Point, located about two hours’ drive from Montpelier.

Conclusion

Montpelier is a small town with a big heart. Its rich history, stunning scenery, and friendly locals make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling in the Midwest. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a chance to relax and unwind, Montpelier has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
