Perrysburg, a city in Northwest Ohio, boasts a culturally rich heritage and scenic beauty that makes it a great tourist destination all year round. With its vast number of museums, historical landmarks, natural attractions, and outdoor adventure activities, Perrysburg is a perfect vacation spot for solo travelers, couples, and families alike. In this guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive list of places to visit, exciting things to do, local customs, historical facts, and much more that you wouldn’t want to miss on your trip to Perrysburg.

Introduction to Perrysburg

Perrysburg is located in Wood County, Ohio, United States, and is known for its great transportation network, exceptional schools, and stunning natural beauty. The city is at the confluence of the Maumee River and the Miami and Erie Canal, which gives it a unique waterfront culture and adventurous outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Perrysburg

There are numerous places to visit in Perrysburg, ranging from museums and art galleries to historical landmarks and natural attractions. Let’s take a look:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perrysburg houses several museums and historical landmarks that showcase the city’s rich heritage and culture. Here are a few you don’t want to miss:

  • Fort Meigs State Memorial: Built in 1813, this is one of the largest reconstructed wooden-walled forts in the nation. The fort played a crucial role in defending the country during the War of 1812.

  • Harrison Rally Day: Harrison Rally Day is an annual event held on the first Saturday of September that commemorates William Henry Harrison’s victory at the Battle of Lake Erie.

  • Perrysburg Area Historical Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the city’s history by showcasing local artifacts, collectibles, and historical tales.

  • The 577 Foundation: This is a unique cultural center that provides opportunities for artists to exhibit their talent, encourages environmental improvements, and fosters community spirit.

Natural Attractions of Perrysburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an adventure seeker and love nature, give these attractions a visit:

  • Bicentennial Park: This park offers breathtaking views of the Maumee River and is perfect for picnics, strolling, biking, and bird-watching.

  • Buttonwood Park: Situated on the banks of the Maumee River, this park has over 15 acres of green space with picnic shelters, playgrounds and a beach perfect for swimming and kayaking.

  • Side Cut Metropark: This park offers hiking, biking, fishing, and bird-watching opportunities. It also features the historic canal locks and the beautiful Maumee River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perrysburg

Perrysburg offers some of the best trails and routes that run through its parks, rivers, and historic sites, which includes:

  • Wabash Cannonball Trail: An 105-mile trail that stretches across several counties in Ohio.

  • Oak Openings Loop Trail: This trail is an 8.1-mile hike that takes you through the largest Metropark in Toledo.

  • The Towpath Trail: The Towpath Trail is located at the Miami and Erie Canal and Maumee River, and is perfect for a scenic bike ride or a leisurely walk.

Best Things to Do in Perrysburg: Activities and Tours

Perrysburg has thrilling activities throughout the year; some of the best things to do here include:

  • Perrysburg Farmer’s Market: Held every Thursday during the season, this market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and live entertainment.

  • The Summer Fair in Perrysburg: This fun and family-friendly fair typically takes place in the first week of August and offers endless entertainment, carnival rides and games and tasty food.

  • Canoeing and kayaking on the Maumee River: Sign up for a guided tour or rent equipment to paddle along the Maumee River and witness beautiful wildlife and scenery.

  • Wine Tasting at Mon Ami: Mon Ami is Ohio’s oldest winery; book a tour to learn about the winemaking process and taste some amazing wine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perrysburg

If you’re planning a week-long trip, here’s an itinerary that can help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Visit Fort Meigs and learn about the history of Perrysburg
2 Go bird-watching at Side Cut Metropark

4 Take a guided tour and go canoeing on the Maumee River
5 Go for a scenic bike ride at Oak Openings Loop Trail

7 Spend the day exploring and shopping at downtown Perrysburg

Food and Drink Scene of Perrysburg

No trip is complete without exploring the local food and drink scene. The city of Perrysburg offers excellent dining options, local wines, and craft beers that you should definitely try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garlic Butter Steak: One of the famous dishes in Perrysburg, served with mixed veggies and garlic mashed potatoes.

  • Basa Grouper: The lightly breaded Basa Grouper is served with a side of heaped smashed sweet potatoes and a side salad.

Local Beverages

  • Mon Ami Wine: Mon Ami is the most sought-after winery in Perrysburg, and their wines are top-rated in Ohio.

  • Left Foot Charley: This brewery in Perrysburg offers excellent beers and ciders, perfect for a relaxing evening out.

Where to Stay in Perrysburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perrysburg has numerous accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are a few you can consider:

  • The Belamere Suites Hotel: This is a luxurious all-suite hotel offering indoor pools and hot tubs, perfect for couples looking for a romantic getaway.

  • Hilton Garden Inn Toledo/Perrysburg: The modern hotel is located near local attractions, and offers a fitness center, indoor pool and free Wi-Fi.

  • The Heritage Inn: The traditional Inn offers comfortable rooms and continental breakfast at affordable rates.

What to Buy in Perrysburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pick up some of these locally made products and souvenirs to bring home from your visit to Perrysburg:

  • Glass Artwork: Toledo is known for its stunning glass artwork, which you can purchase at The Toledo Museum of Art.

  • Handmade Jewelry: You can find some unique handmade jewelry created by locals at the Packer Creek Pottery and Bead/Craft Store.

Best Time to Visit Perrysburg

The best time to visit Perrysburg is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is perfect for most outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perrysburg

Do:

  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes when you’re out exploring the city.

  • Respect local customs, traditions, and public places.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or use tobacco products inside buildings or on public properties.

  • Litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perrysburg

Perrysburg is generally a safe city to visit, but it’s always wise to take general precautions, such as:

  • Avoid dark and isolated areas during the night.

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Perrysburg

Perrysburg has several hospitals and medical centers spread across the city, with trained medical professionals available 24/7.

Getting to Perrysburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are multiple ways to get to Perrysburg, including:

Airports Near Perrysburg

  • Detroit Metro Airport

Bus Stations Near Perrysburg

Other Options

  • You can drive to Perrysburg via the I-75, I-280, or Ohio Turnpike.

How to Get Around in Perrysburg

The most popular ways to get around Perrysburg include renting a car, using public transport (TARTA), or hiring a taxi or ride-sharing service.

Where to Go Next from Perrysburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The historic town of Defiance offers cultural and historical landmarks only 40 minutes away from Perrysburg.

Conclusion

Perrysburg is a beautiful, cultural, and adventurous city where you can explore nature, learn history, and make unforgettable memories. Whether you are planning a week-long trip with family and friends or a solo adventure, this guide provides you with the best things to see and do to make your visit to Perrysburg a memorable one.
