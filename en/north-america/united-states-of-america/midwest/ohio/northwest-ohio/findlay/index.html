Introduction to Findlay

Located in Northwest Ohio, Findlay is a charming small town that offers a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. Despite its relatively small size, there are plenty of activities and sites to keep visitors entertained throughout their stay.

List of Places to Visit in Findlay

Looking for suggestions on what to see and do in Findlay? Check out our list of must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hancock Historical Museum: Learn about the history of Hancock County through exhibits on everything from the Civil War to the Corliss Steam Engine.
  • Jones Mansion: This beautiful Victorian mansion-turned-museum offers a glimpse into the lives of wealthy families in the late 19th century.
  • Marathon Center for the Performing Arts: Catch a show or concert at this beautifully restored theater, which hosts a variety of performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Findlay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Riverbend Recreation Area: This park offers hiking trails, a disc golf course, and plenty of opportunities for fishing and boating.
  • Oakwoods Nature Preserve: Explore the forest, prairies, and wetlands of this scenic preserve that is home to a variety of wildlife.
  • Litzenberg Memorial Woods: Escape the hustle and bustle of city life with a picnic, hike, or horseback ride through this tranquil park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Findlay

Are you an avid hiker or biker looking for some great trails in Findlay? These routes are sure to impress:

  • Findlay Reservoir No. 2 Loop Trail: This 5.7-mile loop offers scenic views of the reservoir and surrounding woodlands.
  • Blanchard River Greenway Trail: This 13-mile trail runs along the Blanchard River and is perfect for both hiking and biking.
  • Riverbend Trail System: With over 10 miles of trails, this system offers a variety of hiking and biking options for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Findlay: Activities and Tours

Want to make the most of your time in Findlay? Consider these top activities and tours:

  • Beer Barrel Pizza & Grill Tour: Get a behind-the-scenes look at how the famous Beer Barrel Pizza is made with this tour that includes lunch and beer samples.
  • Ghost Tours of Findlay: Explore the spooky side of town with this evening walking tour that highlights some of the city’s haunted locations.
  • Findlay Farmers Market: Sample local produce and artisanal goods at this popular open-air market, held every Saturday during the summer months.

Week-Long Itinerary for Findlay

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Findlay, consider this sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Hancock Historical Museum and Jones Mansion, followed by dinner at Rossilli’s.
2 Hike the Findlay Reservoir No. 2 Loop Trail, then grab lunch at The Greek Garden. In the evening, catch a show at the Marathon Center for the Performing Arts.
3 Spend the day exploring the Oakwoods Nature Preserve, then enjoy dinner at the Bistro on Main.
4 Bike the Blanchard River Greenway Trail, followed by lunch at Alexandria’s. In the evening, take a Ghost Tour of Findlay.
5 Head to Riverbend Recreation Area for a day of fishing and boating, then feast on steak at Logan’s Roadhouse.
6 Take a tour of the Beer Barrel Pizza & Grill, followed by shopping at the Findlay Farmers Market. For dinner, try the sushi at En Lai.
7 Enjoy a day of horseback riding at Litzenberg Memorial Woods, then finish your trip with a meal at Jac & Do’s Pizza.

Food and Drink Scene of Findlay

One of the best parts of visiting Findlay is trying out the local cuisine. Here are some dishes and beverages you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beer Barrel Pizza: No visit to Findlay is complete without trying the famous Beer Barrel Pizza. You won’t be disappointed!
  • Greek Salad at The Greek Garden: This refreshing salad is the perfect choice on a warm summer day.
  • The Big Dave at Alexandria’s: This BBQ sandwich is piled high with pulled pork, coleslaw, and BBQ sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer at Findlay Brewing Company: This popular brewery offers a rotating selection of beers on tap, and even offers beer delivery within the city limits.
  • Wine at the Findlay Wine Merchant: This cozy wine bar and shop offers a variety of wines from around the world, as well as local Ohio vintages.
  • Coffee at Stix Restaurant: Sip on a cup of locally roasted coffee at this popular breakfast spot.

Where to Stay in Findlay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Looking for a place to stay in Findlay? Consider one of these options:

  • Hancock Hotel: This upscale hotel offers elegant rooms and a prime location in the heart of downtown.
  • Findlay Inn and Conference Center: This mid-range hotel offers comfortable rooms and ample meeting space.
  • The Gaslight Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B offers cozy rooms and a warm welcome from the friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Findlay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Findlay empty-handed! Consider picking up one of these souvenirs or local products:

  • Blanchard River Nature Preserve t-shirt: Show your love for the outdoors with a t-shirt from this scenic preserve.
  • Findlay flag: This unique flag features the city’s emblematic elk statue.
  • Amish-made jams and jellies: Stock up on these delicious spreads at the Findlay Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Findlay

While Findlay is a friendly and welcoming town, it’s always good to be aware of some local customs and culture. Some tips to keep in mind:

  • Saying “please” and “thank you” is always appreciated
  • Ohioans are proud of their Buckeye football team, so if you’re a sports fan, expect some lively debate about the team’s chances
  • Slow down and enjoy the relaxed pace of Findlay life

Best Time to Visit Findlay

Visit Findlay in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the trees are changing color. However, if you prefer warmer temperatures, summer is also a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Findlay

Make the most of your trip by following these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Visit the local farmers market
  • Sample some local beer or wine
  • Take a hike on one of the scenic trails

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying Beer Barrel Pizza
  • Miss the chance to explore the Hancock Historical Museum

Safety and Health

Safety in Findlay

Findlay is a safe and welcoming town. However, as with any new place, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and take normal safety precautions.

Health in Findlay

Findlay is home to several hospitals and urgent care centers, so you’ll be able to access medical care if necessary. Additionally, be sure to pack sunscreen and bug spray if you plan on spending time outdoors.

Getting to Findlay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Findlay

The closest airport to Findlay is Toledo Express Airport, located about an hour away. Additionally, Detroit Metropolitan Airport is about 2.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Findlay

Findlay is served by Greyhound and offers bus service to destinations across the Midwest.

Other Options

For those who prefer to drive, Findlay is conveniently located just off Interstate-75.

How to Get Around in Findlay

The best way to get around town is by car. However, if you don’t have access to a car, there is a local taxi service available.

Where to Go Next from Findlay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to extend your trip? Consider these nearby destinations:

  • Maumee Bay State Park: This scenic state park features a golf course, hiking trails, and a beach.
  • Put-in-Bay: Located on South Bass Island in Lake Erie, this charming town is worth the ferry ride.
  • Columbus: Ohio’s capital city is located about two hours south of Findlay and offers a variety of cultural attractions, including the Columbus Museum of Art.

Conclusion

With its mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine, Findlay is a hidden gem worth exploring. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, there is something for everyone in this charming small town.
