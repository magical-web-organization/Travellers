Introduction to Bowling Green

Bowling Green, Ohio, is a quaint and charming city located in the northwest region of the state. Home to Bowling Green State University, this college town boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a thriving cultural scene. Whether you’re a student, tourist, or resident, there are plenty of things to do and see in Bowling Green.

List of Places to Visit in Bowling Green

No matter what your interests are, there is something for everyone in Bowling Green. Here are just a few of the must-see attractions in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wood County Historical Center and Museum: Learn about the history of the area and step back in time with this museum’s exhibits and reconstructed buildings.
  • National Tractor Pulling Championships: Held annually at the Wood County Fairgrounds, this event showcases the world’s most powerful farm vehicles.
  • Educational Agricultural Exhibit and Resource Center: Located on the BGSU campus, this exhibit offers an interactive experience highlighting the importance of agriculture in Ohio.

Natural Attractions of Bowling Green: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Simpson Garden Park: Take a leisurely walk through this serene park with paved paths, gardens, and a pond.
  • Black Swamp Preserve: This nature reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna and offers hiking trails and bird watching.
  • Bowling Green City Park: With over 300 acres of green space, this park has sports fields, playgrounds, and trails for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bowling Green

  • Wetlands Preserve: This 20-acre park offers a 0.8-mile loop trail and a boardwalk through the wetlands.
  • Slippery Elm Trail: This 13-mile paved rail-trail is perfect for walking, running, or biking.

Best Things to Do in Bowling Green: Activities and Tours

  • Carter Historic Farm: This living history farm offers tours and interactive exhibits, showing visitors what life on a farm was like in the 1930s.
  • Downtown Bowling Green: Stroll the streets of the historic downtown area and visit local shops, restaurants, and bars.
  • Bowling Green Farmers Market: Held every Wednesday and Saturday, this market offers locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bowling Green

Day 1: Visit the Wood County Historical Center and Museum and explore the displays on life in the early 1900s. Day 2: Hike the Wetlands Preserve and enjoy the peaceful scenery. Day 3: Get a taste of the local food scene with a visit to the Bowling Green Farmers Market. Day 4: Take a tour of Bowling Green State University’s campus, including the Bowen-Thompson Student Union and the Wolfe Center for the Arts. Day 5: Spend the day at the National Tractor Pulling Championships at the Wood County Fairgrounds. Day 6: Rent a bike and ride the Slippery Elm Trail. Day 7: Relax at Simpson Garden Park and enjoy the serene atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of Bowling Green

No trip to Bowling Green is complete without trying some of the delicious and unique local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taco Bell (Bowling Green-style): A local twist on the classic fast food restaurant’s tacos, made with Dorito shell and cheese.
  • Pulaski Polish Sausage: A traditional Polish sausage made locally in Bowling Green.
  • Tony’s Quarter Deck Pizza: A family-owned pizzeria with a classic thin crust and a variety of toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Bowling Green Beer Works: Offering a variety of unique and flavorful craft beers made in house.
  • Flying Rhino Coffee & Chocolate: A cozy café with a focus on premium coffee and handmade chocolates.

Where to Stay in Bowling Green: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to chain hotels, there are plenty of options for every budget in Bowling Green. Here are a few top picks:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: A newer hotel with modern rooms and amenities, located near BGSU.
  • The Seasons Bed and Breakfast: A charming and cozy bed and breakfast offering comfortable rooms and delicious homemade breakfast.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.

Local Culture and Customs of Bowling Green

Bowling Green is a friendly and welcoming community, with a deep appreciation for its history and agricultural roots. Locals take pride in the beautiful natural surroundings and strive to preserve the local environment.

Best Time to Visit Bowling Green

The best time to visit Bowling Green is in the summer months when temperatures are mild and the natural surroundings are in full bloom. Visitors can also enjoy local events and festivals, including the National Tractor Pulling Championships in August and the Black Swamp Arts Festival in September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bowling Green

Do:

  • Take a walk through the beautiful Simpson Garden Park.
  • Go for a scenic bike ride on the Slippery Elm Trail.
  • Visit the Wood County Historical Center and Museum to learn about the history of the area.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the beautiful natural surroundings. Take advantage of the area’s outdoor opportunities!
  • Skip the local food scene. Bowling Green has some unique and delicious options to try.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bowling Green

Bowling Green is a safe city to visit with a low crime rate. As with any city, visitors should take basic precautions like locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Bowling Green

Medical facilities and emergency services are readily available in Bowling Green. The city also boasts a number of fitness centers and outdoor activities to promote overall health and wellness.

Getting to Bowling Green: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bowling Green

The closest airport to Bowling Green is Toledo Express Airport, located approximately 20 minutes away. Detroit Metropolitan Airport and Cleveland Hopkins International Airport are also within driving distance.

Bus Stations Near Bowling Green

The closest bus station to Bowling Green is the Greyhound station located in Toledo, approximately 25 minutes away.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Bowling Green by car, train, or ride-sharing services such as Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Bowling Green

Walking, biking, and driving are the primary modes of transportation in Bowling Green. Car rentals and bike rentals are available in the city, and many attractions are located within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Bowling Green: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of fantastic destinations within driving distance of Bowling Green, including:

  • Toledo: A larger city with museums, arts and culture, and outdoor opportunities.
  • Sandusky: Known for its amusement parks and summer attractions on Lake Erie.
  • Cleveland: A thriving metropolitan city with diverse cultural attractions and world-class dining.

Conclusion

Bowling Green is a charming and welcoming city with plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, and relaxation. Whether you’re visiting for a weekend or a week, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your time in this hidden gem of northwest Ohio.
