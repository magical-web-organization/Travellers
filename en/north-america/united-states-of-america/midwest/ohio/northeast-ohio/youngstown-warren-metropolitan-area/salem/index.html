Introduction to Salem

Nestled in the Youngstown-Warren Metropolitan Area, Salem is one of Ohio’s best-kept secrets. Steeped in history, Salem has plenty of attractions and activities to keep visitors entertained.

List of Places to Visit in Salem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Salem Historical Society Museum
  • The Burchfield Homestead Museum
  • The Dale Shaffer Research Library and Museum

Natural Attractions of Salem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Guilford Lake State Park
  • The Beaver Creek State Park
  • The Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salem

Salem offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the state of Ohio. Here are our top picks:

  • The Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail
  • The Mill Creek MetroParks Hike and Bike Trail
  • The Great Miami Riverway Trail

Best Things to Do in Salem: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Salem Second Saturday Art Walk
  • Tour the vineyards at The Vineyards at Pine Lake
  • Grab a pint at the Numbers Brewing Company

Local Culture and Customs of Salem

Salem has a rich history and a strong sense of community. Visitors may notice the friendly atmosphere and welcoming spirit of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Salem

The best time to visit Salem is during the autumn months when the leaves are changing color, and Salem offers a plethora of fall activities and fairs.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salem

Do:

  • Dress in layers, as the weather can change quickly.
  • Try local craft beer.
  • Take a hike through the state parks

Don’t:

  • Litter - Salem takes pride in its natural beauty.
  • Be disrespectful towards local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salem

Salem has a low crime rate and is generally considered a safe place to visit. Visitors should still exercise caution and be mindful of their surroundings.

Health in Salem

Medical facilities and hospitals are available in nearby cities and towns. Visitors should pack any necessary medication and always carry their insurance card.

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport (62 miles)
  • Akron-Canton Airport (35 miles)

Visitors can also drive to Salem from nearby major cities such as Akron, Cleveland, and Youngstown.

How to Get Around in Salem

Visitors can easily navigate Salem on foot or by car. Car rentals are available from nearby airports and cities.

Where to Go Next from Salem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pittsburgh (71 miles)

Conclusion

Salem has something for everyone - from scenic hiking trails and natural attractions to museums and cultural experiences. With its welcoming atmosphere and charming small town feel, Salem is the perfect destination for a peaceful getaway.
