Are you planning a trip to Northeast Ohio and looking for hidden gems to visit? Look no further than Columbiana, a charming city in the Youngstown-Warren Metropolitan Area. This travel guide will provide all the information you need to plan your trip to Columbiana, including where to stay, what to eat, and the must-see attractions.

Introduction to Columbiana

Columbiana is a small city with a population of just over 6,000 people. It is known for its historic downtown area, which features preserved 19th-century architecture, boutique shops, and locally owned restaurants. The city is also surrounded by scenic countryside, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Columbiana is conveniently located near major Ohio cities like Cleveland and Pittsburgh, making it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or day trip.

List of Places to Visit in Columbiana

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the biggest draws of Columbiana is its rich history, which is preserved in its many museums and historical sites.

  • The Joy of Christmas: This popular holiday light display takes place every December at the city’s Firestone Park. With over a million lights and festive decorations, it’s a must-see during the holiday season.
  • The Log House Museum: This 1840s log cabin houses numerous pioneer artifacts and is an excellent place to learn about the early settlement of the area.
  • The Harvey S. Firestone Park: This 52-acre park features a beautiful rose garden, playgrounds, tennis courts, and a swimming pool, making it a great spot for a picnic or family outing.

Natural Attractions of Columbiana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those looking to connect with nature, Columbiana offers a variety of outdoor activities and scenic landscapes.

  • Beaver Creek State Park: Located just a few miles from Columbiana, this sprawling park has over 2,000 acres of forests, creeks, and hiking trails.
  • The Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail: This 12-mile bike trail follows the Little Beaver Creek and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Oasis Bird Sanctuary: This 400-acre wildlife sanctuary is home to over 100 different species of birds and is a great spot for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbiana

The surrounding countryside of Columbiana offers plenty of opportunities for hikers and bikers to explore the outdoors.

  • The Moderate Trail Loop at Beaver Creek State Park: This 4.4-mile trail loop is perfect for those looking for a moderate hike through the woods.
  • The Hellbender Bluff Trail at Hellbender Preserve: This 5-mile hike offers stunning views of forested ravines and streams, making it a great option for nature enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Columbiana: Activities and Tours

While there is plenty of outdoor recreation to enjoy in Columbiana, there are also many other activities and tours worth exploring.

  • Columbiana Wine Cellar: This local winery offers tastings and tours of their vineyards and production facilities.
  • Columbiana Fun Center: This family-friendly entertainment center features a miniature golf course, batting cages, and an arcade.
  • The Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania: This museum is dedicated to the history of the Pennsylvania railroad and is located just a few miles from Columbiana.

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbiana

If you’re planning on spending a full week in Columbiana, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your trip.

Day Activity
1 Explore downtown Columbiana and visit the Log House Museum
2 Hike the Moderate Trail Loop at Beaver Creek State Park
3 Take a bike ride along the Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail
4 Visit the Columbiana Wine Cellar and sample some local wines
5 Spend the day at the Harvey S. Firestone Park
6 Take a tour of the Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania
7 Relax and enjoy the scenic countryside of Columbiana

Food and Drink Scene of Columbiana

No trip to Columbiana is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Burger at Detwiler’s Farm Market: This burger is topped with peanut butter and bacon and is a unique flavor experience.
  • Chicken Paprikash at the Taste of Country: This Hungarian-style chicken dish is a classic in the area.

Local Beverages

  • The Malvern Cider Mill: This family-owned cider mill produces delicious fresh apple cider and other fruit juices.
  • The Birdfish Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers and has a cozy taproom for visitors to gather and enjoy a cold brew.

Where to Stay in Columbiana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options for visitors to Columbiana, from cozy bed and breakfasts to modern hotel chains.

  • Das Dutch Village Inn: This charming inn is located in nearby Canfield, Ohio, and offers a unique experience with its Dutch-style architecture and decor.
  • The Chateau Resort: This modern hotel features an indoor pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Columbiana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home, Columbiana has plenty of options.

  • The Farmer’s Market in Downtown Columbiana: This weekly market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other locally made items.
  • The Antique Emporium: This shop features a wide variety of antique and vintage items, including furniture, jewelry, and home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbiana

Like many small towns in America, Columbiana values community and hard work. Expect to see friendly faces and locals who are proud of their town and its heritage.

Best Time to Visit Columbiana

Columbiana is a year-round destination, with something to offer every season. Spring and fall offer ideal temperatures for outdoor activities, while winter is a popular time to visit for the Joy of Christmas light display. Summer is also a great time to visit, with plenty of outdoor recreation and events like the Columbiana Wine Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbiana

Do:

  • Explore downtown Columbiana and enjoy the historic architecture and local shops.
  • Sample some of the local food and drink, like the Buckeye Burger and fresh apple cider.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreation opportunities, like hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying some of the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Skip the museums and historical sites, like the Log House Museum and Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania.
  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes for exploring the outdoor trails and parks.

Safety and Health

Columbiana is a safe destination, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should still take basic precautions like locking their vehicles and securing valuables.

Safety in Columbiana

  • Use caution when hiking or biking on outdoor trails and always let someone know your itinerary.
  • Be aware of signage and obey rules and regulations while visiting outdoor recreation areas.

Health in Columbiana

  • Be sure to pack plenty of sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated and be aware of your physical limitations while hiking or biking.

Getting to Columbiana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Columbiana is conveniently located near several major Ohio cities and is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Columbiana

  • Pittsburgh International Airport: This airport is approximately 50 miles south of Columbiana and offers numerous domestic and international flights.
  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport: This airport is approximately 65 miles north of Columbiana and offers flights to many major U.S. cities.

Bus Stations Near Columbiana

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound bus station is in nearby Youngstown, Ohio, approximately 13 miles from Columbiana.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: There are several major car rental companies located near the airports in Pittsburgh and Cleveland.
  • Rideshare Services: Popular rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Columbiana

While having a car is convenient for exploring the surrounding countryside, it’s also possible to get around Columbiana and see the city’s main attractions on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Columbiana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Columbiana is surrounded by numerous other destinations worth exploring in Northeast Ohio, including:

  • Youngstown, Ohio: This nearby city is known for its industrial history and has many museums and cultural attractions worth exploring.
  • Lake Erie: This vast lake is only a few hours’ drive from Columbiana and is home to numerous beaches and vacation resorts.

Conclusion

Columbiana may be a small town, but it’s packed with charm, history, and outdoor recreation opportunities. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or foodie, Columbiana has something to offer every type of traveler. Start planning your trip today and discover this hidden gem in Northeast Ohio.
