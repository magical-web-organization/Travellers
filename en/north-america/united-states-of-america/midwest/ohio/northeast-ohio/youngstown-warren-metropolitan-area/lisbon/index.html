Introduction to Lisbon

Lisbon, nestled in the Youngstown-Warren Metropolitan Area in Northeast Ohio, offers travelers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and contemporary amenities. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and cultural attractions or hiking through parks and reserves, Lisbon has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lisbon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Beaver Creek State Park: Located in East Liverpool, about 15 minutes from Lisbon, Beaver Creek State Park spans over 2,722 acres with stunning wildlife and nature. It offers visitors a glimpse into the lifestyle of the pioneers, with a reconstructed 19th-century village, log cabins, and a working grist mill.

The Museum of Ceramics: Located in East Liverpool, the Museum of Ceramics chronicles the city’s rich ceramics industry, which dates back to the 19th century. Visitors can see the works of local artists and historical items from Italy and China.

Kennekuk County Park: Located near Lake Scroggs, Kennekuk County Park provides an excellent opportunity for hiking or fishing amidst stunning natural beauty. The park features four lakes and several hiking trails, but the 1.5-mile trail up to the waterfall is especially popular among tourists.

The Columbiana County Fair: Every year in the first week of August, the Columbiana County Fair comes to town, making it an ideal time for a visit. This county fair is one of the largest in the state of Ohio and offers a variety of events such as horse shows, tractor pulls, and demolition derbies.

Natural Attractions of Lisbon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Guilford Lake State Park: Located east of Lisbon, Guilford Lake State Park offers visitors 11 miles of shoreline for fishing, swimming, and boating. There are multiple picnic areas with grills, a launch ramp, and a sports complex.

Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail: Running from Lisbon to Washingtonville, the Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail covers 12 scenic miles and makes for an excellent spot for a bike ride or a leisurely walk amidst nature.

Lake Milton State Park: Located around 20 minutes outside Lisbon, Lake Milton State Park features a sandy beach, a boat launch, and hiking trails that offer stunning waterside views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lisbon

In addition to the Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail mentioned above, which is open to hiking and biking, there are several other trails around Lisbon that are worth exploring:

  • Mill Creek MetroParks Bikeway
  • Sandy Beaver Trail
  • Steel Valley Trail
  • Morningstar Trail

Best Things to Do in Lisbon: Activities and Tours

Enjoy a Wine Tasting: Visit a local vineyard and indulge in some of their delicious wines, like those from The Vineyards at Pine Lake or Mastropietro Winery.

Visit the Local Farmers Market: Located in downtown Lisbon, the farmers market features fresh produce and handmade crafts.

Take a Ghost Walk Tour: Visit the historic district of Lisbon and explore the mysteries of local hauntings and ghost stories. Ghost Walk Tours for tourists are available from spring to fall every year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lisbon

  • Day 1: Explore the Beaver Creek State Park, Grist Mill, and Historical Village.
  • Day 2: Visit the Guilford Lake State Park and Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail.
  • Day 3: Take a winery tour and sample some local wines at Mastropietro Winery.
  • Day 4: Visit the Museum of Ceramics or the East Liverpool Museum of Local History.
  • Day 5: Go biking on the Sandy Beaver Trail.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Cleveland to visit the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Museum.
  • Day 7: Relax at Lake Milton State Park and enjoy some water sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Lisbon

Must-Try Dishes

Potato Candy: Lisbon is home to ‘Potato Candy,’ a unique dish made from mashed potatoes, confectioners’ sugar and peanut butter.

Pork Chops: Ohio is known for its pork production, and visitors to Lisbon should try the pork chops at Das Dutch Haus restaurant! It’s delicious.

Pumpkin Pie Squares: A pumpkin pie filled with a melt-in-your-mouth cinnamon-flavored topping is a signature dish in Lisbon.

Local Beverages

Apple Cider: Lisbon has several family-owned orchards that make excellent apple cider.

Craft Beers: Visit Steel Valley Brew Works for a taste of their popular craft beer, the Apple Pie Ale.

Where to Stay in Lisbon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Lisbon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pottery and Ceramics: Lisbon is known for its rich ceramic history and offers visitors unique pottery items to take back home.

Waggoners Chocolates: Head to East Liverpool to buy some of the chocolates famous in the region. They come in different flavors and shapes that will surely satisfy your sweet tooth.

Local Culture and Customs of Lisbon

Respect local customs and avoid disrespecting the beliefs and cultures of the people in Lisbon. Locals are friendly and hospitable, but it’s essential to be respectful and curious at the same time.

Best Time to Visit Lisbon

The best time to visit Lisbon is the fall season – September through November. The temperature is comfortable for strolling, and gardens and parks show off their colors in the autumn season. Summer months can be too hot and humid for activities like hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lisbon

Do:

  • Take a bike ride along the Little Beaver Creek Greenway Trail.
  • Visit local farmers’ markets and buy fresh fruits and vegetables.
  • Try local dishes and beverages, like potato candy and waggoners chocolates.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter around or pollute natural trails.
  • Don’t smoke or litter in nature parks or preserves.
  • Don’t use profanity or be disrespectful towards locals or tourists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lisbon

Lisbon is a safe place to visit in Ohio, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid isolated areas at night.

Health in Lisbon

Local medical assistance is readily available in Lisbon, and tourists should carry their regular medications. Mosquito repellent and sunscreen are essential in summer months.

Getting to Lisbon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lisbon

  • Pittsburgh International Airport
  • Cleveland-Hopkins International Airport
  • Akron - Canton Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Lisbon

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Canton, Ohio.
  • Ohio Bus Lines in Youngstown, Ohio.

Other Options

  • Lisbon is easily accessible by car from Pittsburgh, Cleveland, and Akron.

How to Get Around in Lisbon

Rental cars are available in Lisbon, but Uber and local taxi services are preferred means of transportation for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Lisbon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania: This city is just a two-hour drive from Lisbon and offers visitors a chance to explore a city that is rich in history and flavor.

  • Columbus, Ohio: The capital city of Ohio is located about three hours from Lisbon. Columbus offers tourists an array of options, including the Columbus Zoo and Aquarium, the Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens, and the Ohio Statehouse.

  • West Virginia: Lisbon is about an hour’s drive to the West Virginia border. Tourists can explore cities like Charleston, Morgantown, and Bridgeport.

Conclusion

Lisbon, Ohio, is an excellent destination for tourists who want to explore natural beauty, try local cuisine, and witness the rich culture and history of the area. Be sure to use this comprehensive travel guide to plan an unforgettable trip to Lisbon.
