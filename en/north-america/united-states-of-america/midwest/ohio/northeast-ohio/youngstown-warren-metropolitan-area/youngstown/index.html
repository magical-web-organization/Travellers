Introduction to Youngstown

Located in the Youngstown-Warren Metropolitan Area in Northeast Ohio, Youngstown is a mid-sized city that offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. The city was once a hub for steel manufacturing, but has since transformed into a thriving destination that boasts a vibrant arts and culture scene, delicious cuisine, and world-class museums.

List of Places to Visit in Youngstown

When planning a trip to Youngstown, make sure to include the following attractions on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Butler Institute of American Art: The first museum of American art in the country, houses over 20,000 works of art from American artists, including works from Winslow Homer, Andrew Wyeth, and Edward Hopper.
  • The Youngstown Historical Center of Industry and Labor: Explore the region’s industrial heritage at this interactive museum, which showcases the history of steelmaking, manufacturing, and labor unions in the area.
  • The Arms Family Museum: A beautifully restored mansion that dates back to the early 20th century. Visitors can explore the period furnishings and learn about life during the Industrial Revolution.
  • The McDonough Museum of Art: Part of Youngstown State University, the McDonough Museum of Art features contemporary art exhibitions and installations by emerging artists.

Natural Attractions of Youngstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mill Creek Park: A must-visit for nature lovers, this 2,600-acre park features a variety of hiking and biking trails, scenic waterfalls, and picturesque picnic areas.
  • Fellows Riverside Gardens: Located within Mill Creek Park, these beautiful gardens offer visitors a colorful oasis of flowers, foliage, and water features.
  • Bear’s Den Studio & Gallery: Offers visitors the opportunity to view fine art while also exploring the surrounding natural beauty.
  • The Mill Creek MetroParks Farm: A working farm that offers visitors the chance to see agricultural animals up close and partake in farm-related activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Youngstown

One of the best ways to explore Youngstown’s natural beauty is on foot or on a bicycle. Some popular trails to explore include:

  • The MetroParks Bikeway: A 11.2-mile trail that winds through rural landscapes, city streets, and suburban neighborhoods. The trail is paved and accessible to cyclists of all skill levels.
  • East Cohasset Hike: This 3.6-mile trail is a challenging hike that rewards visitors with scenic views of the Mahoning River Valley.
  • Mill Creek Park Trail: A challenging trail that offers hikers a diverse landscape of waterfalls, lakes, creeks, and stunning rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Youngstown: Activities and Tours

Youngstown offers a variety of activities and tours that will keep visitors entertained throughout their stay. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Lanterman’s Mill: A working 19th-century mill that provides visitors with an insight into Youngstown’s industrial past.
  • The Ohio Wine Tour: Tour the beautiful wineries and vineyards of the Ohio region, including several in nearby Youngstown.
  • The Youngstown Farmers Market: Held every Saturday morning, the market offers visitors the chance to sample local cuisine and browse fine art, crafts, and other local products.
  • Wick Park: A beautiful public park that features a range of recreational activities, from horseshoes and bocce ball to basketball courts and playgrounds.

Week-Long Itinerary for Youngstown

If you plan on staying in Youngstown for a week, consider the following itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the museums dedicated to the region’s industrial heritage, including The Youngstown Historical Center of Industry and Labor and The Arms Family Museum. Day 2: Spend the day exploring Mill Creek Park, including Fellows Riverside Gardens, the hiking and biking trails, and the scenic waterfalls. Day 3: Visit The Butler Institute of American Art, which houses the largest collection of American art in the country, followed by a tour of the McDonough Museum of Art. Day 4: Take a wine tour of the Ohio region, visiting local vineyards like Mastropiero Winery and Myrddin Winery. Day 5: Spend the day at the Mill Creek MetroParks Farm, interacting with the animals and partaking in farm-related activities. Day 6: Take a stroll through the Wick Park neighborhood, admiring the historic architecture and charming gardens. Day 7: Pack a picnic and spend the day exploring Bear’s Den Studio & Gallery and the nearby natural surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Youngstown

Youngstown is renowned for its diverse culinary scene, which includes traditional American cuisine as well as a range of international offerings. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Hot Italian: A classic sandwich that includes Italian sausage, peppers, onions, and sauce, all served on a fresh hoagie bun.
  • Lake Erie Perch: A local favorite, this dish consists of lightly fried perch served with tartar sauce.
  • Polish Boys: A sausage sandwich that is typically served with French fries, coleslaw, and barbecue sauce.

Local Beverages

  • The Buckeye Beer: A beer brewed by the Buckeye Brewing Company, which has been serving the Youngstown area since 1999.
  • The Long Island Iced Tea: A refreshing cocktail that features a blend of gin, rum, tequila, triple sec, and cola.
  • The Ohio Wine: Ohio is known for its wine, and visitors to Youngstown can enjoy a variety of locally produced wines at area vineyards.

Where to Stay in Youngstown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Youngstown offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget. Popular hotels and inns include:

  • The DoubleTree by Hilton Youngstown Downtown: Located in the heart of Youngstown, this hotel is within walking distance of many of the city’s top attractions.
  • The Hampton Inn Youngstown-West: Situated just outside of the city, this hotel provides visitors with a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle.
  • The Avalon Inn & Resort: Located in nearby Warren, Ohio, this resort features luxurious accommodations and a range of recreational activities, from golfing to spa treatments.

What to Buy in Youngstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Youngstown is home to a range of unique souvenir shops, gift shops, and markets that offer visitors the opportunity to take home a piece of the local culture. Some popular items to shop for include:

  • Youngstown State University Merchandise: Show your support for the local university by picking up a t-shirt, hat, or other branded merchandise.
  • Handmade Crafts: Browse a range of handcrafted pottery, jewelry, and other items at local markets and boutiques.
  • Local Food Products: Take home a taste of Youngstown by purchasing locally produced cheese, jams, sauces, and other food products.

Local Culture and Customs of Youngstown

When visiting Youngstown, it’s important to be aware of the local customs and cultural practices. Some important things to know include:

  • The City’s History: Youngstown has a rich industrial history, and visitors are encouraged to explore the city’s museums and historical sites to learn more about the region’s past.
  • Respect for Others: Youngstown residents are known for their friendly and welcoming attitude, and visitors are expected to show respect for the local community and their customs.
  • Religious Traditions: A large number of Youngstown residents are Roman Catholic, and visitors should be mindful of the city’s religious customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Youngstown

The best time to visit Youngstown is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. Summer is another popular time to visit, as the city hosts a variety of outdoor festivals and events during this season. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it’s also the perfect time to experience the excitement of the holiday season in Youngstown.

Do’s and Don’ts of Youngstown

When visiting Youngstown, make sure to keep the following do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Visit the area’s museums and historical sites to learn more about the region’s past.
  • Take advantage of the city’s beautiful parks and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Sample the local cuisine, including traditional American dishes and international offerings.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local community or their customs.
  • Engage in illegal or dangerous activities.
  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring the city’s natural beauty and outdoor attractions.

Safety and Health

Youngstown is generally a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety. Some important safety and health tips to keep in mind include:

Safety in Youngstown

  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in urban areas.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and in a secure location, such as a hotel safe.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and report any suspicious activity to local authorities.

Health in Youngstown

  • Stay hydrated, especially during the summer months when temperatures can be high.
  • Wear sunscreen and appropriate clothing when spending time outdoors.
  • Speak to a healthcare professional before traveling if you have any underlying health conditions.

Getting to Youngstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Youngstown is easily accessible by a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Youngstown

  • Youngstown-Warren Regional Airport (YNG) - Located approximately 10 miles north of downtown Youngstown, this airport offers non-stop flights to several major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Youngstown

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in downtown Youngstown, this station offers bus service to several major cities in the United States.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Visitors can also rent a car to explore the Youngstown area at their own pace.
  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft - Ride-sharing services and taxis are readily available in Youngstown.

How to Get Around in Youngstown

Youngstown is a relatively small city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. However, visitors who prefer to use public transportation can take advantage of the local bus system, which operates throughout the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Youngstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Youngstown is conveniently located near several other popular destinations and excursion options, including:

  • The Allegheny National Forest: A scenic national forest that features hiking trails, waterfalls, and other natural attractions.
  • Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania: This vibrant city is located just a short drive from Youngstown and boasts world-class museums, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine.
  • Cleveland, Ohio: Another nearby city that is worth exploring, Cleveland is home to world-renowned museums, beautiful parks, and a thriving culinary scene.

Conclusion

Youngstown, Ohio is a destination that offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in exploring the area’s industrial heritage, sampling local cuisine, or taking in the stunning natural scenery, Youngstown has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience all that this vibrant city has to offer.
