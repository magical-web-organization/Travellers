Introduction to Massillon

Located in the Canton-Massillon Metropolitan Area in Northeast Ohio, Massillon is a hidden gem for those looking for a peaceful getaway. Rich in history, nature, and cultural attractions, this city has something for everyone. Visitors can explore museums, parks, and hiking trails, sample local cuisine, or simply relax in comfortable accommodations. In this travel guide, we will take you through everything you need to know to plan a delightful trip to Massillon.

List of Places to Visit in Massillon

Massillon offers a variety of attractions, ranging from historical sites to outdoor adventures, making it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway. Below are some of the must-visit places when in Massillon.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Massillon Museum

The Massillon Museum is a place to explore the history and culture of Massillon. The museum hosts exhibitions on local art, history, and cultural diversity. Entry is free, and visitors can check the schedule for upcoming events and programs.

Breymann Brewing Company

This historic brewery is a landmark that dates back to 1856. It is open to visitors to learn the history of the brewery and its contributions to the brewing industry in Ohio. Visitors can tour the facilities and sample a brew.

Spring Hill Historic Home

Spring Hill Historic Home is a Greek Revival-style mansion listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The interior is decorated with fine art and furniture, and the site offers a guided tour of the mansion, grounds, and gardens.

Natural Attractions of Massillon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sippo Lake Park

With over 100 acres of natural beauty to explore, Sippo Lake Park is an excellent place to enjoy the outdoors. Visitors can take a walk along the trails or go fishing on the lake. The park also has a nature center that hosts educational programs about the local flora and fauna.

The Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath Trail

The Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath Trail is a scenic 110-mile trail, perfect for hiking, biking, or a leisurely stroll. The trail winds through the Ohio countryside, offering visitors a glimpse of the state’s natural beauty.

Clay’s Park Resort

Clay’s Park Resort is a vacation destination with a water park, zip line, camping, and recreational activities. This 500-acre park is located at the southern tip of Lake Erie and has something to offer visitors of all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Massillon

Massillon is a paradise for hikers and bikers owing to its scenic terrain. Visitors can take a tour of the countryside on foot or by bike with these routes.

Towpath Trail

The Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath is a breathtaking trail that is ideal for beginners and experienced hikers alike. Visitors can spend a day or two exploring the picturesque landscapes and wilderness.

Duncan Plaza Paths

Duncan Plaza has several paved and unpaved paths for visitors to explore. The paths that lead around the lake and the park offer beautiful views and are well-maintained.

Best Things to Do in Massillon: Activities and Tours

Massillon has plenty of activities for visitors, which include:

Massillon Ghost Walk

Visitors can explore the city’s haunted history with a guided ghost walk through the streets of Massillon. The guides will share spooky tales and legends of the city’s history.

Amish Country Tour

Visitors can take a leisurely one-hour drive through Ohio’s Amish country. The tour offers the chance to see the lifestyle and culture of the Amish people, their homes, and farms.

Week-Long Itinerary for Massillon

For those planning to spend a week in Massillon, here is a recommended itinerary to make the most of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Massillon

Massillon has a wide array of food options, from casual to fine dining. Visitors can taste the local cuisine at the following places.

Must-Try Dishes

The Erie Street Kitchen

The Erie Street Kitchen is a family-owned restaurant specializing in homemade comfort food. Visitors can enjoy dishes such as roast beef, mac and cheese, and biscuits and gravy.

Bender’s Tavern

Bender’s Tavern offers fine dining in a cozy atmosphere. Visitors can try their famous prime rib or lobster bisque.

Local Beverages

Shale Brewing Company

Shale Brewing Company is a local craft brewery that offers a rotating selection of craft beers. Visitors can enjoy a brew on their outdoor patio or take a six-pack to go.

Grapes in a Glass

Grapes in a Glass is a wine bar and bistro that offers a wide selection of wines and small plates. Visitors can indulge in some delicious cheese and meats while enjoying a glass of wine.

Where to Stay in Massillon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Massillon has several options for accommodation, which cater to different budgets.

Best Western

Best Western is located in Massillon city centre, making it an ideal place for those who want to be at the heart of all the actions. It boasts a fitness centre and indoor pool.

Hampton Inn by Hilton

Hampton Inn by Hilton is perfect for those seeking quality rest, with comfortable beds, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Massillon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Massillon has many options for those who want to take a piece of the town back home. Some of the popular souvenirs include:

Massillon Tigers Apparel

Massillon is famous for its high school football team, the Massillon Tigers, and visitors can take home a piece of the team’s history by purchasing a Massillon Tigers t-shirt or cap.

Handcrafted Pottery and Ceramics

Massillon has many local shops where visitors can find unique pottery and ceramics, such as the Mud Pie Pottery and Massillon Museum Store.

Local Culture and Customs of Massillon

Massillon is a small town with friendly locals who take pride in their community’s heritage. Visitors are expected to be respectful of the town’s traditions and welcome.

Best Time to Visit Massillon

The best time to visit Massillon is from April to June or from September to October when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Massillon

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area
  • Try the local cuisine and beer
  • Visit Massillon Museum and Spring Hill Historic Home

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Smoke in public areas
  • Disrespect locals
  • Ignore the traffic rules

Safety and Health

Safety in Massillon

Massillon is a safe town, but visitors should take normal precautions to safeguard themselves and their belongings.

Health in Massillon

The town’s hospitals, dentist offices, and pharmacies are available for any medical needs visitors may have.

Getting to Massillon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Massillon

The Akron-Canton Regional Airport is located a few miles from Massillon and offers commercial flight services.

Bus Stations Near Massillon

The Greyhound Bus Station in downtown Canton is the nearest bus station to Massillon, and visitors can take a taxi to their destination from there.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Massillon from nearby cities or rent a car from the Akron-Canton Airport.

How to Get Around in Massillon

Massillon is a walkable town, but those who prefer can rent a bike, car, or taxi to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Massillon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Canton

Canton is Massillon’s neighboring town and is home to the Pro Football Hall of Fame and the William McKinley Presidential Library and Museum.

Cleveland

Located less than an hour’s drive from Massillon, Cleveland has many attractions, including the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the Cleveland Museum of Art.

Conclusion

Massillon is an exciting, hidden gem in Ohio that has something to offer visitors of all ages and interests. From hiking trails to cultural attractions, delicious food to comfortable accommodation, Massillon should be on every traveler’s list. We hope this travel guide has been useful for planning your trip and has added Massillon to your bucket list.
