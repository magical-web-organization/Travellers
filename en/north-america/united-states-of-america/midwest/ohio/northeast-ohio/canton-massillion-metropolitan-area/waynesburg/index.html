Introduction to Waynesburg

Waynesburg is a small town located in the Canton-Massillon Metropolitan Area, in Northeast Ohio, and is known for its scenic views and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed trip, Waynesburg has something for everyone. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Waynesburg, Ohio, by highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Waynesburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Kenyon College Brown Family Environmental Center

The Kenyon College Brown Family Environmental Center is a must-visit attraction for nature lovers and hikers. Located just five miles south of Waynesburg, the center offers more than 430 acres of hiking trails, conservation areas, and educational exhibits. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and animal tracking.

The Massillon Museum

The Massillon Museum is known for its extensive collection of art and artifacts related to the history of Stark County. The museum has exhibits that showcase the art and culture of the region, including Native American history, Civil War artifacts, and local art. The museum also offers educational programs, events, and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Waynesburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Exploration Gateway

Exploration Gateway is a nature center located at Quail Hollow Park in Hartville, just a short drive from Waynesburg. The center offers several exhibits, including live animals and interactive displays, as well as numerous outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, birdwatching, and more.

Sandy Beach Park

Sandy Beach Park is a scenic 40-acre park located in nearby Hartville. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, fishing spots, picnicking areas, and playgrounds. The park is also home to a popular swim beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waynesburg

Waynesburg offers several hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular trails include:

The Towpath Trail

The Towpath Trail is a 101-mile trail that goes from Cleveland to Tuscarawas County. The trail was built along the route of the Ohio & Erie Canal and offers scenic views of the Cuyahoga River. Visitors can hike, bike, or even ride horses along the trail.

The Stark Parks Trail

The Stark Parks Trail is an 18-mile trail that runs through Stark County. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the countryside along the way.

Best Things to Do in Waynesburg: Activities and Tours

The Canton Museum of Art

The Canton Museum of Art is a world-class museum that showcases contemporary art and regional history. The museum features a variety of exhibits, including paintings, sculptures, and photography.

The Pro Football Hall of Fame

The Pro Football Hall of Fame is located in nearby Canton and is a must-visit attraction for sports fans. The museum showcases the history of football and features exhibits on the sport’s greatest players and coaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Waynesburg

Must-Try Dishes

Baked Steak

Baked steak is a local favorite in Waynesburg. The dish consists of tender pieces of beef that are smothered in gravy and served over mashed potatoes.

Buckeye Candy

Buckeye candy is a sweet treat made with peanut butter and chocolate. The candy is shaped like a buckeye, the state tree of Ohio.

Local Beverages

Lake Erie Wine

Ohio is home to several wineries, including the nearby Lake Erie Wine. Visitors can enjoy tastings of the region’s best wine while enjoying scenic views of the vineyards.

Akron Coffee Roasters

Akron Coffee Roasters is a local coffee shop that offers some of the best coffee in the region. Visitors can enjoy a cup of coffee while relaxing in the cozy atmosphere of the shop.

Where to Stay in Waynesburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Waynesburg offers several lodging options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the most popular options include:

Best Western Plus in North Canton

Best Western Plus in North Canton is a comfortable and affordable hotel located just a short drive from Waynesburg. The hotel offers modern amenities and great customer service.

The McKinley Grand Hotel

The McKinley Grand Hotel is a historic hotel located in nearby Canton. The hotel offers luxurious accommodations and is a great option for travelers looking for a more upscale experience.

What to Buy in Waynesburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hartville Marketplace

Hartville Marketplace is one of the largest indoor markets in Ohio, featuring over 100 unique vendors. Visitors can find everything from handmade crafts and artisanal foods to vintage clothing and antiques. This is the perfect place to find a unique souvenir from your trip to Waynesburg.

Local Culture and Customs of Waynesburg

Waynesburg is known for its Midwestern hospitality and friendly people. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and should be prepared to embrace the slower pace of life in the town.

Best Time to Visit Waynesburg

The best time to visit Waynesburg is during the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the scenery is at its most picturesque. Visitors should also consider attending one of the town’s annual festivals, such as the Hartville Chocolate Walk or the Waynesburg Strawberry Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waynesburg

Do:

  • Take time to explore the town’s historic landmarks and scenic views.
  • Try the local cuisine, including baked steak and buckeye candy.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t rush through your trip. Take time to enjoy the town’s slower pace of life.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waynesburg

Waynesburg is a safe town, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables in their car and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Waynesburg

There are several medical facilities in the area, including Mercy Medical Center, located in nearby Canton. Visitors should also be prepared with any necessary medications and travel insurance.

Getting to Waynesburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Waynesburg

The Akron–Canton Airport is the closest airport to Waynesburg, located just 13 miles away. Other nearby airports include the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport and the Pittsburgh International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Waynesburg

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in Canton, which is located about 15 miles from Waynesburg.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Waynesburg. The town is located near Interstate 77 and is easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Waynesburg

Visitors can explore the town on foot, by bike, or by car. Rental cars are available at the Akron–Canton Airport and several nearby rental agencies.

Where to Go Next from Waynesburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Canton, Ohio

Canton is located just a short drive from Waynesburg and is known for its cultural attractions, including the Canton Museum of Art, the Pro Football Hall of Fame, and the McKinley Presidential Library and Museum.

Cleveland, Ohio

Cleveland is located about an hour’s drive from Waynesburg and is known for its art and music scene, as well as its historic landmarks, including the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

Conclusion

Waynesburg is a hidden gem in Northeast Ohio, offering visitors a scenic escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Whether you’re looking to hike, explore historic landmarks, or simply relax in the town’s quaint charm, Waynesburg has something for everyone. Use this travel guide to plan your trip to Waynesburg, Ohio, and get ready to experience the best of the Midwest.
