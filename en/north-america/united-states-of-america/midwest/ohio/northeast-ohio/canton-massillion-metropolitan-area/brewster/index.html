Introduction to Brewster

Brewster is a small town in the Canton-Massillon Metropolitan Area in Northeast Ohio. It sits in Stark County, just 60 miles south of Cleveland and 20 miles southwest of Akron, with a population of around 2000 residents.

While Brewster may be a small town, it has historic and cultural charms that make it worth a visit. The town’s rich history dates back to 1815, and it is known for its vibrant community and outdoor recreational activities. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and so much more. So, let’s start exploring Brewster.

List of Places to Visit in Brewster

Visitors can enjoy a wide range of attractions, whether it’s a museum, park or downtown boutique. Below is a brief overview of some of the must-visit sites in Brewster.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brewster is a town steeped in history, showcasing an appreciation for local culture and heritage. The following are some cultural highlights to add to your itinerary.

  1. Brewster Village Cemetery - Established in 1831, this cemetery serves as the final resting place of many famous American Patriots including Dr. Eli Eichelberger and John C. Spink.

  2. The Massillon Museum - This museum boasts the collection of past artifacts, paintings, glass, and many other items, including a collection of vintage firefighting equipment.

  3. Hoover Historical Center - Visitors can experience life during the 1800s at this unique historical house museum, which also serves as the Birthplace of the 31st President of the United States, Herbert Hoover.

Natural Attractions of Brewster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brewster is surrounded by natural beauty, from parks to hiking trails and many others.

  1. Silver Creek - This creek is popular for fly fishing, canoeing, and kayaking as you explore the winding waterways.

  2. Brewster Park - Located in the heart of the town, this community park offers activities such as hiking, an indoor pool, bird watching, picnic areas, and sports areas.

  3. Ohio-Erie Canal Towpath Trail - This trail is great for cycling and hiking, where visitors can explore the historic Ohio & Erie Canal that runs from Cleveland to New Philadelphia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brewster

Brewster is known for its beautiful landscapes, and there are plenty of activities for nature enthusiasts. The area has great trails and adventures to explore, from hiking to biking and birding.

  1. Wheeling & Lake Erie Trail - This rail-trail winds around for miles and is perfect for both casual walking and serious biking.

  2. The Stark Parks Hiking Trails - There are plenty of trails to explore in the area, all very easy to access.

  3. Dad Elliott’s Trail - This is a picturesque and easy hiking trail, perfect for beginners.

Best Things to Do in Brewster: Activities and Tours

There’s always something fun in Brewster, and the town offers plenty of outdoor and indoor activities for visitors. Some of the top things to do in Brewster include:

  1. Enjoy a trip to the Herrick Fen Nature Preserve – Hike trails and learn about the area’s unique ecosystem with naturalist-led tours.

  2. Visit the Lions Lincoln Theatre – This beautifully renovated theatre is the perfect setting for concerts and inspiring shows.

  3. Take a trip to The Wilderness Center – Discover nature with hiking trails, nature walks, and other outdoor exploring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brewster

To see all the best of Brewster in a week, check out the itinerary below:

  • Day 1: Visit the Hoover Historical Center and the massillon museum
  • Day 2: Go fishing at Silver Creek.
  • Day 3: Bike along the Ohio-Erie Canal Towpath.
  • Day 4: Explore the activities at Brewster Park.
  • Day 5: Take a naturalist-led tour at Herrick Fen Nature Preserve.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a show at the Lions Lincoln Theatre.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at The Wilderness Center

Food and Drink Scene of Brewster

Sampling local cuisine is a great way to experience a town’s culture, and Brewster has much to offer.

Must-Try Dishes

  2. The burger at The Laziza Cafe – Famous for its juicy, American-style burgers and sides.

  3. The steak at The Pub 1918 – Regional cuts of meat with a rustic downtown atmosphere.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking for a refreshing beverage in the area, try out these local favorites:

  1. The craft beer at Paradigm Shift Brewing.
  2. The classic cocktails, beer, and wine at The Barrel Room.

Local Culture and Customs of Brewster

Brewster is a small, friendly town, and visitors can immerse themselves in its local culture by attending community events and festivals such as Brewster Fall Festival and the Brewster Fireman’s Festival. Respect the local community, and be courteous when visiting all the picturesque towns.

Best Time to Visit Brewster

The best time to visit Brewster is during summer, late May to early September. In this season, visitors can enjoy hiking, cycling, and outdoor festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brewster

  • Ask locals for suggestions on must-visit sites in the area.
  • Support local businesses
  • Admire the area’s natural beauty.

  • Trespass on private property.

Visitors can explore Brewster on foot or by bike, both of which are popular ways to get around town. For longer distances, rental cars or ride-sharing services are excellent alternative options.

Where to Go Next from Brewster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brewster is a great starting point for exploring Northeast Ohio. Below are some of the nearby destinations to consider:

  2. Akron – home to the famous Stan Hywet Hall and Gardens.
  3. Canton – a city packed with museums and parks.

Conclusion

Brewster, Ohio, may be a small town, but it has something to offer everyone. From the natural beauty of the parks to the historical significance of the Ohio & Erie Canal, the town has an old-world charm that visitors will love. Plan your travels in advance and don’t forget to try out local cuisines, shop for souvenirs, and tour the area’s unique attractions. However, always remember to respect the community and follow basic safety precautions for an unforgettable experience.
