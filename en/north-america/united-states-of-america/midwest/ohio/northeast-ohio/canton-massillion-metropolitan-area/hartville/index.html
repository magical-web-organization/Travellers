Introduction to Hartville

Nestled in the rolling hills of Northeast Ohio, Hartville is a charming town with a rich history and natural beauty. This quaint community is located in the Canton-Massillon Metropolitan area and is popular for its unique shops, delicious food, and exciting outdoor activities. Whether you’re traveling for a weekend getaway or an extended stay, Hartville has plenty to offer.

Local Culture and Customs of Hartville

  • As a Midwestern town, Hartville is known for its hospitality and friendly demeanor.
  • Locals are very proud of their heritage and the historic nature of the town.
  • The town has a strong farming history and is known for its agricultural sector.

Best Time to Visit Hartville

The best time to visit Hartville is from late spring to early fall when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are at their peak. The popular Hartville Marketplace & Flea Market is open year-round, but visiting during the warmer months means enjoying the fresh air and sunshine while shopping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hartville

Do:

  • Explore the local culture by attending one of the town’s festivals or events.
  • Go horseback riding at Bridlewood Stables.
  • Visit the Hartville Chocolate Factory for an indulgent treat.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray, especially when enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Leave your trash behind when enjoying the town’s beautiful natural scenery.
  • Ignore the long history of the town when visiting museums and historical places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hartville

Hartville is a safe town, but it’s still recommended to practice basic precautions like locking your car and not leaving valuables in plain sight. When enjoying outdoor activities, make sure you’re aware of your surroundings and be cautious when hiking or biking.

Health in Hartville

For emergency medical attention, visitors can head to Aultman Hospital’s North Canton Medical Center or Mercy Medical Center.
