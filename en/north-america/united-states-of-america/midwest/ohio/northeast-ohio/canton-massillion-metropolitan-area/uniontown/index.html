Ultimate Travel Guide to Uniontown, Ohio

Uniontown, located in the Canton-Massillon Metropolitan Area of Northeast Ohio, is a charming town that offers visitors a blend of cultural landmarks, natural scenery, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your perfect getaway to Uniontown, Ohio.

List of Places to Visit in Uniontown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Uniontown is home to several museums and landmarks that are culturally significant, providing insight into the town’s history and heritage:

  • The Ohio Military Museum - showcases the military history of Ohio, with an impressive collection of military artifacts and memorabilia.

  • The MAPS Air Museum - an aviation museum that features restored planes and helicopters that played a significant role in air defense history.

  • The Hartville Marketplace and Flea Market - visitors can shop locally produced crafts, antiques, clothing, and fresh farm produce at this open-air market.

Natural Attractions of Uniontown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Uniontown is surrounded by rolling hills, woodland trails, and tranquil lakes, offering several opportunities for outdoor adventures:

  • The Uniontown Lion’s Park - a spacious park with well-maintained trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

  • The Portage Lakes State Park - located nearby, it has several recreational activities within: boating, swimming, fishing, camping, and trail walking.

  • The Tuscarawas River - offers visitors the chance to enjoy kayaking, tubing, and fishing, providing scenic views throughout the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uniontown

Uniontown offers several hiking trails with scenic views of the picturesque town. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes to explore:

  • The Quarry Trail - a 2.8-mile loop trail that takes visitors through the abandoned quarries and offers scenic views of the ravines.

  • The Ohio & Erie Canal Towpath Trail - a 20-mile trail that extends from Canal Fulton to Akron, providing views of Uniontown’s unique landscapes.

  • The Dogwood Trail - A 1.7 km loop located in the Lion’s Park with an excellent scenic overlook on the top of a hill.

Best Things to Do in Uniontown: Activities and Tours

Uniontown offers several unique experiences and tours that are worth trying:

  • The Hartville Kitchen Cooking Classes - Participate in cooking classes taught by local chefs to learn to cook your favorite dishes.

  • The Glamorgan Castle Tour - an hour journey back to time taking you through the historic castle of the town.

  • The Maize Valley Winery Tasting Tour - features a guided tour through the winery, complete with tastings and expert knowledge.

A Week-Long Itinerary for Uniontown

Planning to spend a whole week in Uniontown? Below is an ideal itinerary to make the most of your stay:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Quarry trail in the morning and attend the Hartville Kitchen Cooking class in the evening
Day 2 Visit the MAPS Air Museum and end the day with a Maize Valley Winery tasting tour
Day 3 Spend the day at Portage Lake State Park, enjoying the beautiful scenery, and participating in any desired activities
Day 4 Visit the Ohio Military Museum in the morning and a Glamorgan Castle Tour in the afternoon
Day 5 Attend a local event or try local cuisine at the Hartville Marketplace in the evening
Day 6 Visit the nearby Canton McKinley’s Monument, William McKinley Presidential Library and Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Uniontown

Uniontown is known for its delicious and diverse cuisine. Here are some notable dishes and local beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hartville Kitchen Fried Chicken - a local favorite that is deliciously seasoned and fried to perfection.

  • Pizzelles - thin and crispy Italian waffle cookies that come in different varieties.

  • Local potato chips - made with Ohio potatoes and hand-fried, they are crunchy and satisfying.

Local Beverages

  • Maize Valley Winery - a great winery that makes wine from their vineyards locally.

  • Hoppin’ Frog Brewery - serves different types of beer locally brewed with high-quality ingredients.

Where to Stay in Uniontown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Uniontown has several excellent accommodations for visitors to choose from, depending on their budget and specific requirements:

  • The Hilton Garden Inn Akron Canton Airport - A comfortable hotel with 24-hour facilities.

  • The M Star Hotel - A budget-friendly and pet-friendly hotel that offers free parking and Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Uniontown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Uniontown offers a variety of locally made products and souvenirs that represent the town. Here are some of the best options:

  • Potato chips from Shearer’s Foods - a local brand with long-lasting and delicious potato chips that make great souvenirs.

  • The Mercer Flower and Gift Shoppe - see lovely flowers arranged in vases and bouquets made locally.

  • The Heartland Cyclery Shoppe - An opportunity to buy locally made tools, gears, and bicycles.

Local Culture and Customs of Uniontown

At the heart of Uniontown’s culture is the community-focused nature that promotes engagement and interaction between the town’s residents. Visitors are welcomed with open arms and encouraged to participate in local festivities.

Best Time to Visit Uniontown

The best time to visit Uniontown is between April and October when the weather is agreeable to participate in outdoor activities. Winters in Uniontown can be harsh, so it’s not an ideal time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uniontown

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and indulge in various outdoor activities the town has to offer.

  • Engage with the community and participate in local events.

Don’t:

  • Litter around; the town takes pride in its clean streets and surroundings.

  • Ignore safety precautions for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Uniontown

Uniontown is a relatively safe town, it’s wise to take necessary precautions while exploring.

Health in Uniontown

Uniontown has several health facilities that provide reliable medical assistance when required.

Getting to Uniontown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Uniontown

  • Akron-Canton Regional Airport: Located within 10 minutes from Uniontown, it is the closest.

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport - situated within an hour drive from Uniontown

Bus Stations Near Uniontown

  • Greyhound Station Canton - located within 15 minutes of Uniontown

  • Stark Area Regional Transit Authority (SARTA) offers transit coming into and around the Uniontown region, providing an array of transportation options.

Other Options

  • Driving to Uniontown is a great option, and the town provides plenty of parking spaces.

How to Get Around in Uniontown

Uniontown is relatively easy to navigate on foot, especially when exploring the downtown area. Alternatively, renting a car allows for greater mobility when traveling to outer areas.

Where to Go Next from Uniontown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Uniontown’s location in Northeast Ohio opens the gate to explore other towns and cities in the region:

  • Akron, Ohio - known for its diverse music and art scene, is only a 20-minute drive from Uniontown.

  • Cleveland, Ohio - a 50-minute drive from Uniontown, is known for its vibrant nightlife and Rock & Roll Hall of Fame.

  • Cuyahoga Valley National Park - is located 30-minutes from Uniontown and provides outdoor activities, hiking, and biking trails, horseback riding, and several other attractions.

Conclusion

Uniontown, Ohio, is the perfect travel destination for cultural enlightenment, outdoor adventure, and locally inspired cuisine. With a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant community spirit, Uniontown has much to offer everyone. This guide will direct you to the best sights, activities, and things to do, helping you plan the ultimate Uniontown getaway.
