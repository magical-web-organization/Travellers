Introduction to Chardon

Chardon is a small town in Northeast Ohio, located approximately 30 miles east of Cleveland. The town is known for its charming downtown area with boutiques, cafes, and restaurants, as well as its historic architecture and outdoor attractions. Visitors to Chardon can enjoy a variety of activities, from hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical landmarks. With its proximity to Cleveland and other nearby attractions, Chardon is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer trip to explore the Midwest.

List of Places to Visit in Chardon

Chardon has a variety of attractions to offer visitors, from museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top places to visit in Chardon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chardon Square: Chardon Square is the heart of the town and offers visitors a glimpse of the town’s history. It features a 19th-century courthouse, unique shops, restaurants, and a weekly farmers market.

  • Geauga County Historical Society: The historical society features several exhibits that showcase the rich history of Chardon and the surrounding area. Visitors can explore the society’s museum, which includes collections of artifacts related to early settlers and Native Americans in the area.

Natural Attractions of Chardon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Punderson State Park: Punderson State Park is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers hiking and biking trails, fishing, boating, and a championship golf course.

  • The Holden Arboretum: The Holden Arboretum features 3,500 acres of gardens, forests, and meadows. Visitors can explore the arboretum’s hiking trails, which offer stunning views of Lake Erie and the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chardon

Chardon has several hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the town’s natural beauty. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Chardon include:

  • Maple Highlands Trail: The Maple Highlands Trail is a 20-mile trail that stretches through Geauga County and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Chagrin River Park Trail: The Chagrin River Park Trail is a 4-mile trail that runs along the Chagrin River and features beautiful views of the river and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Chardon: Activities and Tours

Chardon offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do in Chardon include:

  • Wine Tasting in the Grand River Valley: Visitors can take a wine tour of the Grand River Valley, which is home to more than 20 wineries. Guests can sample delicious wines and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding vineyards.

  • Horseback Riding at Valley View Riding Stable: Valley View Riding Stable offers guided horseback rides through the scenic countryside of Chardon. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful views and experience the natural beauty of the area.

Here is a week-long itinerary for visitors to Chardon looking to experience the best of what this charming town has to offer:

Day 1: Explore downtown Chardon, visiting the unique boutiques, cafes, and restaurants in the area.

Day 2: Visit the Geauga County Historical Society and explore the museum and exhibits showcasing the rich history of Chardon and the surrounding area.

Day 3: Take a wine tour of the Grand River Valley and sample delicious wines from more than 20 wineries in the area.

Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Chardon at Punderson State Park, hiking or biking along the park’s trails and enjoying the scenic views.

Day 6: Go horseback riding at Valley View Riding Stable, experiencing the natural beauty of Chardon from a different perspective.
