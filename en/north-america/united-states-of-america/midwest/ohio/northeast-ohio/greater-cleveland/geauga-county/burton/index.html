Introduction to Burton

Burton, Ohio, is a small village located in Geauga County, in the northeastern part of Ohio, United States. This charming town is known for its historic architecture, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant cultural scene. The village is home to about 1,500 residents and attracts visitors from all over the world, especially during the summer months when the weather is pleasant.

List of Places to Visit in Burton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Century Village Museum

The Century Village Museum is a living history museum that features well-preserved homes, buildings, and artifacts that date back to the early 19th century. Visitors can see how the early settlers lived, worked, and played in this part of Ohio. The museum also hosts events and demonstrations throughout the year.

The Burton Antiques Market

If you are a collector or a fan of antiques, be sure to visit the Burton Antiques Market. This indoor market features over 40 dealers who sell a wide variety of antiques, including furniture, jewelry, pottery, books, and more.

The Welshfield Inn

The Welshfield Inn is a historic restaurant and inn located in Burton. The building dates back to 1842 and has been carefully restored to preserve its original charm and character. The restaurant serves traditional American cuisine, and the inn has several guest rooms available for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Burton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Burton Wetlands Nature Preserve

The Burton Wetlands Nature Preserve is a 95-acre natural area that features wetlands, ponds, and woods. Visitors can explore the trails, spot wildlife, and enjoy the serene beauty of nature. The preserve also hosts guided walks and educational programs throughout the year.

Headwaters Trail

The Headwaters Trail is a scenic trail that runs through Geauga County. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding, and it offers beautiful views of the countryside. Visitors can access the trail in Burton at the South Street trailhead.

Punderson State Park

Punderson State Park is a popular destination for outdoor activities such as camping, hiking, fishing, and boating. The park features a 150-acre lake, a golf course, and several trails. Visitors can also stay overnight in one of the cabins or campsites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burton

Headwaters Trail

As mentioned earlier, the Headwaters Trail is a popular route for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail runs for 20 miles through Geauga County and passes through Burton.

Burton Wetlands Nature Preserve

The Burton Wetlands Nature Preserve has several trails that visitors can explore. The trails run through wetlands, ponds, and woods, and offer great opportunities for bird watching and nature photography.

Best Things to Do in Burton: Activities and Tours

Tour of the Century Village Museum

One of the best things to do in Burton is to take a tour of the Century Village Museum. Guides dressed in period clothing will take you through the various buildings and give you a glimpse into life in the 19th century.

Visit the Burton Antiques Market

Another must-do activity in Burton is to visit the Burton Antiques Market. This indoor market features over 40 dealers who sell a wide variety of antiques. You can spend hours browsing through the collections and finding unique pieces to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Burton

Must-Try Dishes

Amish Country Donuts

Amish Country Donuts are a popular treat in Geauga County, and Burton has a few places where you can get them fresh and hot. These donuts are soft, fluffy, and coated in cinnamon sugar.

Lake Erie Perch Sandwich

The Lake Erie Perch Sandwich is a classic dish in Ohio. The sandwich features breaded and fried perch fillet, lettuce, tomato, and tartar sauce, and it is served on a bun.

Local Beverages

Wine

Geauga County is a burgeoning wine region, and Burton has several wineries that offer tastings and tours. Some popular wineries in the area include Maple Ridge Vineyard, Chardon BrewWorks and Eatery, and Laurentia Vineyard and Winery.

Craft Beer

Burton also has a growing craft beer scene. Market Garden Brewery at The Bass Lake Taverne is known for its farm-to-table cuisine and locally brewed beers.

Where to Stay in Burton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Red Maple Inn

The Red Maple Inn is a beautiful bed and breakfast that features comfortable rooms, breathtaking views, and top-notch amenities. The inn also has an on-site restaurant that serves delicious cuisine made with locally sourced ingredients.

Punderson State Park

Punderson State Park has several cabins and campsites available for overnight stays. The cabins are cozy and comfortable, and the campsites offer a chance to sleep under the stars.

What to Buy in Burton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maple Syrup

Geauga County is known for its maple syrup, and visitors can find several local producers in the area. Some popular syrup brands include Burton Maplewood Farm, Al-Bi Winery, and Middlefield Original Cheese Co-op.

Amish Quilts

Amish Quilts are a popular souvenir in Geauga County. Visitors can find handmade quilts at several local shops, including Miller’s Dry Goods, Sol’s in Berlin, and Helping Hands Quilt Shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Burton

Burton has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its Amish and Mennonite communities. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly and avoiding photography of the Amish people without permission.

Best Time to Visit Burton

The best time to visit Burton is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, the fall season, from September to November, is also a great time to visit, as the foliage is breathtaking and several festivals and events take place during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burton

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the Amish and Mennonite communities.
  • Try the local food and drink, especially the maple syrup and Amish Country Donuts.
  • Visit the Century Village Museum and the Burton Antiques Market.
  • Take a hike in the Burton Wetlands Nature Preserve.
  • Attend the annual Burton Apple Butter Festival, held in October.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the Amish people without permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore the local traffic laws and speed limits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burton

Burton is generally a safe and peaceful town. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid theft and other crimes.

Health in Burton

There are several hospitals and medical facilities near Burton, including University Hospitals Geauga Medical Center and Cleveland Clinic Chardon Family Practice.

Getting to Burton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burton

The closest major airport to Burton is Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, located about 46 miles away. Akron-Canton Regional Airport is also an option, located about 43 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Burton

Greyhound Bus Lines and Megabus both have stops in Cleveland, which is about 35 miles from Burton.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Burton, as the town is located near several major highways, including I-480 and I-90.

How to Get Around in Burton

Burton is a small town, and many of the attractions are within walking or biking distance of each other. However, visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car or hire a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Burton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cleveland

Cleveland is located about 35 miles from Burton and is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, world-class museums, and sports teams.

Cuyahoga Valley National Park

Cuyahoga Valley National Park is located about 25 miles from Burton and offers a chance to explore nature, hike, bike, and watch wildlife. The park also has several historic sites and beautiful views.

Conclusion

Burton, Ohio, is a hidden gem in the heart of Northeast Ohio. With its historic landmarks, natural beauty, and vibrant cultural scene, it offers something for everyone. Whether you are an antique lover, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie, you are sure to find something to enjoy in Burton.
