Introduction to Middlefield

Nestled in the heart of Geauga County, Middlefield boasts a rich cultural heritage, bountiful natural beauty, and one-of-kind attractions. A country oasis in the heart of Northeast Ohio, Middlefield is known for its Amish community, rustic charm and world-class outdoor recreation.

With a population of just over 2,700 residents, Middlefield is the perfect getaway destination if you’re looking for a quiet and relaxing retreat. Let’s explore all the wonderful things that this charming town has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Middlefield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

An essential part of Middlefield’s allure lies in its cultural assets. It is home to the Middlefield Historical Society and Museum, which houses historical artifacts, memorabilia and exhibits that tell the story of this charming town. You can also visit the Century Village Museum, which offers visitors a glimpse into the life and history of the Amish community.

Natural Attractions of Middlefield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Middlefield is a nature lover’s paradise. One of the most popular attractions in the area is Punderson State Park, which is home to a stunning lake, hiking trails, picnic areas, and camping facilities. The beautiful Swine Creek Reservation, with its lush forests, is another must-visit spot that’s perfect for a quiet stroll, bird watching, and enjoying natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Middlefield

If you love the outdoors, you’re in luck. There are plenty of trails to explore in Middlefield. Visit Headwaters Trail, a 6.7-mile trail that winds through wooded areas, wetlands, and open fields. It is perfect for hiking, biking and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Middlefield: Activities and Tours

Middlefield has plenty of fun-filled activities, including tours of Amish Country, an antique shopping expedition in historic downtown, and a buggy ride with an authentic Amish guide. Take a relaxing stroll through Mary Yoder’s Amish Kitchen, and indulge in traditional Amish fare, or pop into Middlefield Cheese House for mouth-watering samples of homemade cheeses, fudge, and sausage.

Week-Long Itinerary for Middlefield

Day 1: Explore Punderson State Park Day 2: Take part in an Amish Country tour Day 3: Visit the Middlefield Historical Society and Museum Day 4: Go antiquing in historic downtown Middlefield Day 5: Take a buggy ride through the countryside Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing stroll through the Swine Creek Reservation Day 7: Indulge in traditional Amish fare at Mary Yoder’s Amish Kitchen

Food and Drink Scene of Middlefield

Must-Try Dishes

Middlefield has a rich culinary tradition. The town is famous for its Amish-made cheeses, meats, and baked goods. When you visit, be sure to try some of the delectable dishes like the fresh-baked bread, smoked sausages, apple butter, homemade jellies, and traditional pies.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking to imbibe, make sure you try some of Middlefield’s local beverages like craft beer, wine, and cider. Pop into Red Maple Vineyard and Winery for an exquisite wine tasting or try cider tasting at the pioneer in apple wine, Rick’s Hard Cider.

Where to Stay in Middlefield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Middlefield has an array of housing options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Stay at the award-winning Red Maple Inn, a Victorian-style oasis that offers the ultimate in comfort, elegance and style. Or, if you’d prefer something a little more rustic, try out the Hideaway Cabins, a collection of cozy log cabins nestled in the woods near Punderson State Park.

What to Buy in Middlefield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Middlefield back home with you by shopping for local products like handmade Amish quilts, artisanal cheeses, maple syrup, and jams. You can also find authentic Amish furniture, gifts, crafts, and other keepsakes at shops like Yoder’s Amish Home and Country Store and the Middlefield Cheese House.

Local Culture and Customs of Middlefield

Middlefield has a rich cultural heritage that’s intertwined with the local Amish population. Respect for their traditions, horse-drawn buggies and simple lifestyle are essential to the town’s charm. As a tourist, it’s important to respect their privacy and way of life and treat them with the kindness and respect that all people deserve.

Best Time to Visit Middlefield

The best time to visit Middlefield is during the fall season, when the leaves change to vibrant hues. Spring is also a great time to explore the outdoors, while summer brings a variety of outdoor festivals, including the popular Geauga County Maple Festival in April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middlefield

Do:

  • Visit the local farmer’s markets to buy fresh produce.
  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Amish community.
  • Take a guided tour of the Century Village Museum and the Middlefield Historical Society.

Don’t:

  • Drive recklessly around horse-drawn buggies.
  • Disturb wild animals when hiking or walking through nature reserves.
  • Litter or disrespect the natural beauty of Middlefield.

Safety and Health

Safety in Middlefield

Middlefield is generally a safe and peaceful town with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to be vigilant and take necessary precautions when traveling.

Health in Middlefield

Middlefield has a variety of medical facilities in the area, including University Hospitals Geauga Medical Center, which is a well-equipped hospital that offers world-class medical care.

Getting to Middlefield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Middlefield

The closest commercial airport to Middlefield is the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, which is about 60 miles away. From there, you can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Middlefield.

Bus Stations Near Middlefield

There are several bus services available that will take you to neighboring towns and cities in Ohio. Connect to Cleveland or Akron via RTA on Laketran’s Park-n-Ride service via State Route 44.

Other Options

You can also reach Middlefield by renting a car, or driving in by taking one of the main highways running through Middlefield.

How to Get Around in Middlefield

The best way to explore Middlefield and its many attractions is by car. Alternatively, you can bike or walk around town, especially when visiting Punderson State Park or the Swine Creek Reservation.

Where to Go Next from Middlefield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bedford, a delightful town located a little over 25 miles to the west of Middlefield, is known for its historic buildings, antique shops and charming ambiance. The city of Cleveland, where you can view the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame or catch a Cleveland Indians baseball game, is another destination worth visiting.

Conclusion

Middlefield is an enchanting, quaint town that’s filled with rich history, natural beauty, and world-class attractions. If you’re looking for a peaceful, relaxing escape from the hectic pace of city life, look no further than Middlefield, Ohio. From hiking and biking trails to cultural landmarks, there’s always something for everyone in this charming town.
