Introduction to Medina County

Nestled in the Greater Cleveland region of Northeast Ohio, Medina County is a tranquil destination for tourists seeking a diverse experience. With a rich history dating back to the 19th century, this thriving county boasts well-maintained parks and hiking trails, cultural attractions, and a thriving culinary scene that appeals to every traveler’s palette.

List of Places to Visit in Medina County

From culturally important museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic vistas, Medina County promises to be a paradise for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Here are some must-visit places in Medina County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Medina Toy and Train Museum: Recognized as one of the most exclusive train museums in Ohio, this impressive museum features an extensive collection of model trains, antique toys, and rare railroad artifacts dating back to the 1800s.

  • Medina County Historical Society and Museum: Housed in an 1840s Greek Revival-style home, this museum offers visitors a glimpse into the county’s rich history through interactive exhibits, including Civil War memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Medina County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buckeye Woods Park: This 423-acre park boasts miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing lakes, picnic areas, and a spectacular wildlife habitat that promotes conservation of local fauna and flora.

  • Letha House Park: Spread over 366 acres of forested land, this park offers a serene environment for picnicking, bird-watching, and nature walks. The observation tower provides panoramic views of the Rocky River Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medina County

Medina County is a hub for outdoor activities as it has a wealth of hiking and biking trails that run through its scenic countryside. Here are some of the best routes to experience the natural beauty of Medina County:

  • Chippewa Rail-Trail: This 7.2-mile trail that stretches from Chippewa Lake to Creston is an excellent route for cycling enthusiasts. The trail passes through mesmerizing farmlands, lush greenery and quiet back roads.

  • Beulah Park Trail: This 1.5-mile paved trail is perfect for families that enjoy strolling, jogging, and cycling. The trail boasts a beautiful view of the park’s duck pond and relaxing picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Medina County: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in shopping for rare antiques, taking a hot balloon tour, or attending a concert, Medina County has plenty of activities and events that cater to every interest. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Medina Antique Mall: This antique mall is located in a 1906 landmark building and boasts 52,000-square-feet of merchandise from over 500 dealers. Shop for vintage items ranging from furniture, jewelry, pottery, and much more.

  • Medina County Fair: A yearly event that attracts thousands of visitors to the county, The Medina County Fair is a five-day event that features rides, farm animals, baking contests, rodeo showings, and a marketplace of local goods and crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Medina County

The Medina County food scene has something for everyone, from hearty home-style fine dining experience to exquisitely crafted brews. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: Pierogies are a traditional Polish dish that is one of the most popular comfort foods in Medina County. These delicious dumplings are usually stuffed with cheeses, potatoes, and other savory fillings and are sautéed in butter.

  • Buckeye Burger: A true local delight, the Buckeye Burger is a burger patty that’s smothered with peanut butter and topped with bacon and cheddar cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Serpentini Wines: This vineyard provides visitors with an elegant wine-tasting experience only half an hour’s drive from downtown Cleveland.

  • Breweries: There are several craft breweries in Medina County such as Wadsworth Brewing Company and Fat Head’s Brewery that offer a variety of world-class beers.

Where to Stay in Medina County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many affordable and comfortable accommodation options for visitors who want to stay in Medina County, from cozy inns to luxurious spa hotels. Here are some of the recommendations:

  • Hampton Inn Medina: Conveniently located near Interstate 71, this hotel provides visitors with easy access to local attractions such as the Medina County Fairgrounds, Chippewa Lake, and more.

  • The 1840s House Bed and Breakfast: Located just outside of downtown Medina, this cozy bed and breakfast offers visitors traditional Victorian-style rooms that enhance the experience of Medina County’s rich history.

What to Buy in Medina County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Medina County is bursting with small businesses and makers who sell locally-made artisanal goods. Here are some of the top souvenirs and products to bring home as a memory of your trip to Medina County:

  • Local Honey: Medina County is known for its locally produced honey. The Food Co-op on West Liberty Street is an excellent place to find high-quality local honey.

  • Antiques: Medina County is home to a world-famous antique mall, where antique enthusiasts can find unique home décor items, vintage pottery, and antique furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Medina County

Medina County prides itself in its tight-knit community and hospitality. Friendly conversation and politeness are a part of the local culture, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms at local establishments. The people here are proud of their community, heritage, and traditions, and they enjoy sharing them with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Medina County

The best time to visit Medina County is between May and October when the weather is moderate, and outdoor activities and events are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medina County

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty: Medina County has an abundance of parks, hiking trails, and scenic drives that showcase the beauty of Northeast Ohio.

  • Experience the local food and traditions: Don’t miss out on some of the best pierogies, local honey, and craft beer in the region. Attend the Medina County Fair for an authentic local experience.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the county’s history: Medina County has a deep-rooted history, as evidenced by its extensive historical society and museums. Make sure to visit some of the historic landmarks throughout the county.

  • Be rude or difficult: Medina County is known for its friendly and welcoming culture. Being impolite or dismissive is not appreciated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Medina County

Medina County is a relatively safe place for tourists. However, as with any other place, there are some precautions visitors should take, such as being cautious at night and not leaving valuables in your car.

Health in Medina County

There are several hospitals and clinics in Medina County, making it easy for visitors to access medical care if needed.

Getting to Medina County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Medina County

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport: Located 30 miles north of Medina County, this airport is the nearest international airport to the region.

Bus Stations Near Medina County

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station is located in Cleveland and offers bus services to and from various cities within Ohio and the neighboring states.

Other Options

Renting a car or taking a taxi is another viable option for visitors to get around.

How to Get Around in Medina County

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Medina County. Visitors who prefer not to drive can consider taking a taxi or hiring a ride-share service like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Medina County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

With its central location in Northeast Ohio, Medina county provides easy access to several popular destinations:

  • Cleveland: Cleveland is home to the Cleveland Museum of Art, the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, and one of the largest urban parks in the country, the Cleveland Metroparks.

  • Akron: Located just 20 miles south of Medina County, Akron is home to several tourist attractions like the Akron Zoo, Akron Civic Theatre, and more.

Conclusion

Medina County is a treasure trove of experiences to be had, from scenic drives, hiking trails, and local cuisine to historic landmarks and cultural experiences. Medina County is an ideal destination for those looking for an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and a place where natural beauty and local charm come together to provide a unique travel experience.
