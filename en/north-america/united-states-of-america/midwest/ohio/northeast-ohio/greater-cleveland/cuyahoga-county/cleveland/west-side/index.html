Introduction to West Side

Welcome to West Side, a vibrant neighborhood in Cleveland. Located in Cuyahoga County, in Northeast Ohio, West Side is nestled along the shore of Lake Erie and boasts some of the most beautiful landscapes in the Midwest. The area is home to many cultural and natural attractions, as well as a thriving food and drink scene. This comprehensive guide will provide all the information you need to plan your trip to West Side, Cleveland.

List of Places to Visit in West Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

West Side is rich in history and culture, which is reflected in the many museums and historical sites found here. One such site is the West Side Market, a century-old indoor food market that showcases the best of local cuisine. The Cleveland Museum of Art, located in nearby University Circle, is another must-see attraction. This massive museum features over 50,000 works of art from around the world, making it one of the largest art museums in the United States.

If you’re more interested in history, the USS Cod Submarine Memorial is a fascinating attraction that lets you explore the inner workings of a World War II-era submarine. Other historical sites in West Side include the Cleveland History Center, the A Christmas Story House, and the James A. Garfield National Historic Site.

Natural Attractions of West Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

West Side’s natural beauty is unparalleled. The area is home to numerous parks and nature reserves, including Edgewater Park, Rocky River Reservation, and the Cleveland Metroparks Zoo. These parks offer a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, kayaking, and picnicking.

The Cleveland MetroParks bike trails system is a hit for cyclists and is known for their scenic highlighted nature trails. These trails are adjacent most parks and offer easy and moderate-level rides. The nearby Cuyahoga Valley National Park is also a must-visit for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Side

Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or just looking to get out for a stroll, West Side has plenty of hiking trails to offer. The Rocky River Reservation is one of the most popular hiking areas in the region, featuring over 11 miles of trails. The trails are well-maintained and wind through a stunning natural landscape of forests, rivers, and waterfalls.

If you prefer biking, The City of Cleveland provides free loaner bikes for individuals with identification at several bike stations located all throughout the city. You can also pick up your ride at one of the Relay Bike Share rental stations located throughout the city.

Best Things to Do in West Side: Activities and Tours

In West Side, there is never a shortage of things to do and see. You can get a glimpse of local events and entertainment by checking the Cleveland Calendar of Events. The nightlife is just as lively, with numerous bars, clubs and live music venues located throughout the neighborhood.

Be sure to take the C-Town Tour, a guided tour that showcases the best of Cleveland and all its hidden gems. If you are more drawn to indoor activities, the Capitol Theatre is a must-visit. This historic theater dates back to the 1920s and features classic movie screenings and live performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Side

If you’re planning a week-long trip to West Side, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Start by exploring the West Side Market, followed by a visit to the USS Cod Submarine Memorial.
  • Day 2: Spend the day at Rocky River Reservation hiking along the river and through forest trails.
  • Day 3: Visit the Cleveland Museum of Art in the morning, followed by a picnic at the Cleveland Metroparks Zoo in the afternoon.
  • Day 4: Take the C-Town Tour and see all the local sites that Cleveland has to offer.
  • Day 5: Spend the day kayaking in Edgewater Park before enjoying a sunset over the lake.
  • Day 6: Head over to the Capitol Theatre to enjoy a movie or live performance.
  • Day 7: End your trip with a day trip to nearby Cuyahoga Valley National Park, filled with plenty of trails, waterfalls, and nature.

Food and Drink Scene of West Side

Must-Try Dishes

Cleveland is known for its food and drink scene, and West Side is home to some of the best restaurants and breweries in town. The city’s signature dish is the Polish Boy, a kielbasa sausage sandwich topped with coleslaw, fries, and a signature barbecue sauce. You can find this and other local specialties at Sokolowski’s University Inn, one of the oldest restaurants in the city.

Other must-try dishes in West Side include pierogi, bibimbap, and falafel.

Local Beverages

No visit to West Side would be complete without trying some of the local brews. There are numerous craft breweries located throughout the neighborhood, including Great Lakes Brewing Company. This brewery has been serving up some of the best beers in the city since the 1980s. Other must-visit breweries include Platform Beer Co, Market Garden Brewery, and Brew Kettle.

For your morning pick me up, try Gypsy Beans and Baking Company for their signature coffee blends.

Where to Stay in West Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Side offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. The Hyatt Regency Cleveland at the Arcade is a historic hotel that offers luxurious accommodations in the heart of downtown Cleveland. The Lakefront Hostel is a more affordable option that offers shared dorms and private rooms for travelers on a budget.

Other great options include DoubleTree by Hilton Cleveland Westlake and The Kimpton Schofield Hotel, perfect for Midtown nightlife and access to nearby downtown destinations.

What to Buy in West Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for gifts or souvenirs to bring home, West Side has plenty of options. The West Side Market is a great place to start, offering everything from local produce to handmade crafts. You can also find unique items at Banyan Tree, a local boutique that specializes in handmade jewelry, home goods, and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of West Side

West Side is known for its friendly locals, and visitors should always feel welcome. Be sure to embrace the neighborhood’s cultural diversity and try some of the local cuisine and beverages.

Best Time to Visit West Side

The best time to visit West Side is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny. This is the ideal time for hiking and other outdoor activities. The winter months (December-February) are bitterly cold, and many outdoor activities are closed.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Side

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Visit the museums and historical sites.
  • Be respectful of the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car when parked.
  • Engage with individuals who appear to be under the influence of substances.
  • Leave the trails and pathways when exploring the parks and reservations.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Side

West Side is a relatively safe neighborhood, but visitors should always take precautions. Be sure to keep an eye on your belongings, especially when in crowded areas. Always lock your car and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in West Side

The health facilities in West Side offer good healthcare facilities with hospitals and medical centers within the vicinity. You can locate the nearest urgent care facilities near you or visit the Cleveland Clinic branch for any medical assistance.

Getting to West Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

West Side can be accessed by car, public transportation, bike-share or Ride Share. The nearby Cleveland Hopkins International Airport is the most convenient way to reach West Side If you plan to have a rental car, various Car Rental Services are available at the airport.

How to Get Around in West Side

Getting around in West Side is easy thanks to the city’s public transportation system. The Greater Cleveland RTA Rapid Transit train routes make it easy to travel throughout the city.

The city’s Relay Bike Share service is another convenient option for getting around, with over 100 bike docking stations located throughout West Side.

Where to Go Next from West Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West Side is well-positioned for exploring Northeast Ohio, and there are plenty of nearby destinations worth visiting. The nearby Cuyahoga Valley National Park is just 20 miles away and is renowned for its stunning natural beauty.

Other nearby destinations include Akron, which is just 35 miles away and is home to the famed Akron Zoo, and the scenic Port Clinton, which is just an hour’s drive away on the Lake Erie Shore.

Conclusion

West Side is a vibrant neighborhood that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, food, or nature, there’s always something to see and do. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-equipped to make the most of your trip and create lasting memories in West Side, Cleveland.
