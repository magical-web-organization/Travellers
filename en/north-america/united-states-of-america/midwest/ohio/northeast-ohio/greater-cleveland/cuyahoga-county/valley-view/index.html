Introduction to Valley View

Valley View is a charming village in Cuyahoga County in Northeast Ohio. Located in Greater Cleveland, it is a beautiful getaway destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike. The town is surrounded by the Cuyahoga River and the Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath Trail. With a population of around 2400, Valley View has a small-town feel that makes it a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Valley View

If you’re planning a trip to Valley View, make sure to include the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Canal Visitor Center

The Canal Visitor Center is a must-visit for anyone interested in the town’s history and culture. It tells the story of the Ohio & Erie Canal and how it transformed the region. It also showcases the local flora and fauna and gives visitors a chance to hike or bike on the towpath trail.

Boston Mills/Brandywine Ski Resort

The ski resort is situated in the scenic Cuyahoga Valley National Park. It offers visitors skiing, snowboarding, and tubing experiences. Boston Mills/Brandywine Ski Resort is open year-round as it transforms into an adventure park in the summer, offering visitors zip-lining experiences.

Natural Attractions of Valley View: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cuyahoga River

The Cuyahoga River is one of the town’s defining features, drawing visitors for water-based outdoor activities like kayaking and paddleboarding.

Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath Trail

Running through Valley View for 2.5 miles is the Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath Trail. The trail provides an opportunity for visitors to explore the valley’s scenic beauty while walking, biking, or horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valley View

Valley View is a fantastic place for outdoors enthusiasts, providing a range of hiking and biking trails, such as:

  • Buckeye Trail
  • Laurel Loop Trail
  • Station Road Trailhead
  • Towpath Trail - Ohio & Erie Canal Reservation

Best Things to Do in Valley View: Activities and Tours

Valley Riding Stables

Valley Riding Stables offers visitors a unique horseback riding experience. Riders get to explore the beautiful valley while on horseback.

Tinkers Creek Gorge Scenic Overlook Hike

The Tinkers Creek Gorge Scenic Overlook Hike is a great option for visitors who enjoy scenic hikes. The hike offers a peek at the gorge and some areas where bird-watching is possible.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valley View

Day 1 - Explore the Canal Visitor Center, hike the Towpath Trail.

Day 2 - Visit Boston Mills/Brandywine Ski Resort, try skiing and snowboarding, or enjoy zip-lining activities.

Day 3 - Visit the Tinkers Creek Gorge Scenic Overlook Hike, explore the natural beauty.

Day 4 - Go horse riding at Valley Riding Stables, experience the local flora and fauna.

Day 5 - Hire a kayak or paddleboard and explore the Cuyahoga River.

Day 6 - Relax and unwind, visit Gepetto’s Wine Cellar, sample some of the region’s best wines.

Day 7 - Explore the historic Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Valley View

Must-Try Dishes

Swensons Drive-In

Swensons Drive-In is known for its delicious milkshakes and Galley Boy Burgers.

Slim and Chubby’s Bar and Grill

Slim and Chubby’s Bar and Grill is known for serving the best wings in town. If you’re a wing lover, this is the place to be.

Local Beverages

Gepetto’s Wine Cellar

Gepetto’s Wine Cellar is not only known for its wine but also for the stunning views of the valley it provides. Visitors can sample some of the best wines the region has to offer while enjoying the ambiance and stunning views.

Where to Stay in Valley View: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valley View offers an array of accommodation options for visitors, including:

Hampton Inn & Suites

A popular option with travelers, the Hampton Inn & Suites is located near the Cuyahoga Valley National Park. The hotel provides a shuttle service to the airport.

Holidaу Inn

The Holiday Inn is situated near the Cuyahoga River. The hotel boasts an on-site restaurant, an indoor pool, and fitness center.

Guesthouse Studios

If you’re on a budget, the Guesthouse Studios are a good option. The hotel is located near the Cuyahoga Valley National Park.

What to Buy in Valley View: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The town has various local businesses that offer unique souvenirs like:

Cleveland Metroparks

The Cleveland Metroparks offer souvenirs and local products that visitors can purchase.

5 LAKES Crafts

5 LAKES Crafts are known for their handcrafted wood products made using local materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Valley View

The residents of Valley View are welcoming and hospitable. The town celebrates its heritage and culture, and visitors are always welcome to participate.

Best Time to Visit Valley View

The best time to visit Valley View is during the spring or fall when the weather is moderate. Summers can be hot and humid, so be sure to carry sunscreen and water.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valley View

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors, especially the Cuyahoga River area.
  • Visit the Canal Visitor Center and Boston Mills/Brandywine Ski Resort.
  • Try the local foods, especially the Galley Boy Burger.

Don’t:

  • Litter in parks or water bodies.
  • Visit during peak summer months if you dislike hot weather.
  • Forget to try some local wines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valley View

Valley View is a relatively safe town, but visitors should exercise normal safety precautions, like keeping an eye on their belongings and using caution when exploring riverside areas.

Health in Valley View

There are various hospitals, health centers and pharmacies located near Valley View. Visitors should carry adequate medical insurance and carry any required medications when travelling.

Getting to Valley View: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Valley View

The Cleveland Hopkins International Airport is 15 minutes away from Valley View by car.

Bus Stations Near Valley View

Greyhound Bus Station Cleveland Ohio is located 8 miles away from Valley View.

Other Options

Visitors may opt for rental cars or hire taxis from the airport to reach Valley View.

How to Get Around in Valley View

Visitors can easily explore Valley view on foot or rent bicycles. Taxis are also available to take you around.

Where to Go Next from Valley View: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations and excursions include:

Cleveland

Cleveland is just a few miles away and offers an opportunity to explore its downtown, Playhouse Square, the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, and other museums and art attractions.

Cuyahoga Valley National Park

Valley View is on the fringes of the Cuyahoga Valley National Park, which offers a range of outdoor activities as well as scenic drives and hikes.

Conclusion

Valley View is a charming village with plenty of natural beauty and outdoor activities for visitors to explore. Along with its vibrant culture and history, the town is perfect for a mini-getaway or a family vacation. With everything from horse-riding to skiing, visitors will never run short of things to do in Valley View. So pack your bags and head over to this hidden gem in the Midwest!
