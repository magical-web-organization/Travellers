Introduction to East Side

Located in Cleveland, Ohio, East Side is a region that encompasses a number of cities in Cuyahoga County. This region has a rich cultural heritage, with many museums, historical landmarks, and natural attractions that draw visitors from all over the world. In this comprehensive guide to East Side, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip, including the best places to visit, the top things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in East Side

When you’re in East Side, there are many places that you must visit. Here are some of the top attractions that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cleveland Museum of Art: This museum is one of the finest art museums in the country, with more than 45,000 works of art from around the world.

  2. Cleveland Museum of Natural History: This museum features exhibits on dinosaurs, Ohio’s natural history, and space travel.

  3. A Christmas Story House: This museum is a must-visit for fans of the classic holiday movie “A Christmas Story.”

  4. Karamu House: This theater was founded in 1915 and has been a hub for African American arts and culture ever since.

  5. Cleveland Botanical Garden: This garden features 10 acres of indoor and outdoor gardens, including a rainforest and a butterfly exhibit.

Natural Attractions of East Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cleveland Metroparks: This park system covers more than 23,000 acres and includes hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic overlooks.

  2. Cuyahoga Valley National Park: This park is a short drive from East Side and features hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic vistas.

  3. Lake View Cemetery: This cemetery features beautiful gardens, walking paths, and the final resting places of many famous Clevelanders.

  4. Euclid Creek Reservation: This reservation features hiking trails, a fishing pond, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Side

Many visitors to East Side love to explore the region on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking in East Side:

  1. The Lakefront Bike Trail: This trail runs for 17 miles along the shore of Lake Erie and offers stunning views of the lake and the Cleveland skyline.

  2. Bedford Reservation: This reservation offers miles of wooded hiking trails, as well as a mountain biking trail.

  3. North Chagrin Reservation: This reservation features hiking trails, fishing ponds, and a nature center.

  4. South Chagrin Reservation: This reservation offers miles of hiking trails, as well as a polo field and a nature center.

Best Things to Do in East Side: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions listed above, there are many other things to see and do in East Side. Here are some activities and tours that you won’t want to miss:

  1. Lolly the Trolley Tour: This tour takes you on a guided tour of Cleveland’s most famous landmarks, including the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the West Side Market.

  2. Cuyahoga River Cruise: Take a relaxing cruise down the Cuyahoga River and enjoy the beautiful scenery of Northeast Ohio.

  3. SmARTspace at 78th Street Studios: This art gallery and event space features the work of local artists and hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including art shows, concerts, and workshops.

  4. The Cleveland Orchestra: This renowned orchestra performs at Severance Hall and Blossom Music Center throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Side

If you have a week to explore East Side, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Cleveland Museum of Art and the Cleveland Museum of Natural History.

Day 2: Take a Lolly the Trolley tour and visit the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

Day 3: Hike in Cuyahoga Valley National Park and have lunch at the historic Stanford House.

Day 4: Explore the cultural diversity of East Side with visits to Karamu House and the Ukrainian Museum-Archives.

Day 5: Get outside and enjoy nature at Cleveland Metroparks and Lake View Cemetery.

Day 6: Take a Cuyahoga River Cruise and visit the Euclid Beach Park and Horseshoe Casino.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery of East Side with a day on the shores of Lake Erie.

Food and Drink Scene of East Side

East Side has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the region’s rich cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pierogies: These Polish dumplings are a staple of Cleveland’s cuisine and can be found at many restaurants throughout East Side.

  2. Polish Boy Sandwich: This sandwich is a Cleveland classic and features a kielbasa sausage topped with coleslaw, french fries, and barbecue sauce.

  3. Corned Beef Sandwich: East Side is known for its corned beef sandwiches, which are usually served on rye bread with a side of pickles.

  4. Paczki: These Polish donuts are a must-try when you’re in Cleveland. They’re traditionally eaten on Fat Tuesday, but you can find them at bakeries and cafes throughout the year.

Local Beverages

  1. Great Lakes Brewing Company: This brewery is located in Cleveland’s Ohio City neighborhood and offers tours and tastings.

  2. Mitchell’s Homemade Ice Cream: This local ice cream shop features classic flavors as well as unique, seasonal offerings.

  3. Platform Beer Co.: This brewery is located in Cleveland’s Ohio City neighborhood and features a large selection of craft beers.

  4. Pour Cleveland: This coffee shop is a must-visit for coffee lovers, with a wide selection of specialty coffee drinks made from locally roasted beans.

Where to Stay in East Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to lodging in East Side, there are many options to choose from. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the region:

  1. The Hilton Cleveland Downtown: This upscale hotel is located in the heart of downtown Cleveland and offers stunning views of the city and Lake Erie.

  2. The Glidden House: This historic hotel is located near the Cleveland Museum of Art and features charming rooms and suites.

  3. The Cleveland Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located in Ohio City and offers dorm-style and private rooms.

  4. The Courtyard by Marriott Cleveland University Circle: This hotel is located near many of East Side’s top cultural attractions, including the Cleveland Museum of Art and the Cleveland Museum of Natural History.

What to Buy in East Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for a souvenir to remind you of your visit or a gift for a friend back home, there are many local products to choose from in East Side. Here are some ideas:

  1. Local Honey: You can find honey from local beekeepers at farmers’ markets and specialty food stores throughout the region.

  2. Cleveland T-Shirt: Show off your love for the city with a Cleveland-themed t-shirt from a local boutique.

  3. Handmade Jewelry: Many local artists sell their handmade jewelry at galleries and gift shops throughout East Side.

  4. Polish Pottery: You can find a wide selection of vibrant, hand-painted pottery at Silesia City Hall.

Local Culture and Customs of East Side

As with any destination, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs when you’re visiting East Side. Here are some tips:

  1. Clevelanders are known for their friendliness and hospitality, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a local.

  2. East Side has a rich cultural heritage, so take some time to explore the museums and cultural attractions to learn more about the region’s history.

  3. Many residents of East Side are proud of their Polish heritage, so be sure to try some pierogies and other Polish dishes while you’re in town.

Best Time to Visit East Side

East Side is a great destination to visit year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re interested in outdoor activities like hiking and biking, the spring and fall are great times to visit. If you’re interested in cultural festivals and events, the summer months are a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Side

When you’re visiting East Side, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do explore the museums and cultural attractions to learn more about the region’s history.

  • Do try some of the local cuisine, like pierogies and corned beef sandwiches.

  • Do take a hike in one of the region’s beautiful parks or reservations.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect the local culture and customs.

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural beauty of the parks and reserves.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask a local for recommendations or directions.

Safety and Health

When you’re traveling to East Side, it’s important to be aware of any safety or health concerns. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in East Side

East Side is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in East Side

Ohio has a humid continental climate, with hot summers and cold winters. Be sure to bring appropriate clothing for the season and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.

Getting to East Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

When you’re planning your trip to East Side, there are a few different transportation options to consider:

Airports Near East Side

Cleveland Hopkins International Airport is the main airport serving the Cleveland area, including East Side.

Bus Stations Near East Side

Greyhound and Megabus both operate bus routes that serve the Cleveland area, including East Side.

Other Options

If you’re driving, East Side is easily accessible via several major highways, including I-90 and I-271.

How to Get Around in East Side

Getting around East Side is fairly easy using public transportation, rental cars, or taxis. The Greater Cleveland Regional Transit Authority (RTA) operates bus and train routes throughout the region.

Where to Go Next from East Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore, there are many nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are a few ideas:

  1. Cedar Point: This amusement park is located about an hour west of Cleveland and features roller coasters and other thrill rides.

  2. Put-in-Bay: This island on Lake Erie is a popular summer destination for boating, swimming, and sunbathing.

  3. Amish Country: Located about 90 minutes southeast of Cleveland, this region features Amish farms, shops, and restaurants.

Conclusion

East Side is a beautiful and vibrant region that has something to offer for every type of traveler. From cultural attractions and natural beauty to delicious food and drink, this part of Ohio is sure to leave a lasting impression. Use this guide to plan your trip and explore all that East Side has to offer!
