Introduction to Downtown

Downtown Cleveland is the commercial, cultural, and entertainment epicenter of Cleveland, Ohio. Located in Cuyahoga County, Greater Cleveland, Northeast Ohio, and Midwest, this bustling district offers a unique blend of historic landmarks, contemporary architecture, iconic sports venues, top-notch museums, outdoor recreation, and vibrant nightlife. Set against the scenic backdrop of Lake Erie and the Cuyahoga River, Downtown Cleveland is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a quintessential urban experience in the heartland of America.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame: The ultimate shrine to the greatest musicians and influencers of the genre.

  • Cleveland Museum of Art: Features an astonishing collection of over 45,000 artworks from different periods and cultures.

  • Cleveland History Center: Take a deep dive into the city’s intriguing past through exhibits, interactive displays, and memorabilia.

  • Terminal Tower Observation Deck: Enjoy panoramic views of Downtown Cleveland from the 42nd floor of this iconic skyscraper.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Edgewater Park: A scenic lakeside park with pristine beaches, picnic areas, playgrounds, and nature trails.

  • Cuyahoga Valley National Park: Only a short drive away, this national park boasts stunning waterfalls, forested trails, and diverse wildlife.

  • The Flats: An industrial-chic entertainment district that spans both sides of the Cuyahoga River, featuring restaurants, bars, and riverfront activities.

  • Wade Oval: An open-air plaza that hosts seasonal events, concerts, and festivals that celebrate Cleveland’s cultural diversity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

  • Towpath Trail: Take a scenic, 85-mile ride along the historic Ohio Canal.

  • Euclid Avenue: A historic street in Cleveland with a dedicated bike lane and beautiful architecture.

  • Lake Link Trail: A 4.6-mile trail that runs along the shore of Lake Erie, from Edgewater Park to Gordon Park.

  • Ohio & Erie Canalway: Another scenic trail that stretches over 100 miles, connecting several historic towns along the way.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

  • Cleveland Cavaliers Game: Watch the NBA’s perennial powerhouse play at the Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse.

  • Progressive Field: Catch a Major League Baseball game at home of the Cleveland Indians.

  • A Christmas Story House: Relive the classic holiday movie by visiting the real-life house where it takes place.

  • Playhouse Square: The largest performing arts center in the country outside of New York City offers world-class shows, cultural events, and public art installations.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cleveland-style pizza: A square, crispy pizza with cheese on the bottom and toppings on the top.

  • Polish Boy: A sausage sandwich with coleslaw, french fries, and barbecue sauce.

  • Pierogi: A dumpling filled with potato, cheese, or sauerkraut, often served with sour cream.

  • Corned beef sandwich: A classic deli sandwich with slow-cooked, tender corned beef, Swiss cheese, and thousand island dressing.

Local Beverages

  • Burning River Pale Ale: Brewed by Great Lakes Brewing Company, this award-winning pale ale honors the Cuyahoga River’s restoration.

  • Christmas Ale: A seasonal favorite, this spiced ale is perfect for warming up during the holidays.

  • Lemonade: A refreshing drink made with fresh-squeezed lemon juice and local honey.

  • Aperol Spritz: A popular Italian cocktail made with Aperol, Prosecco, and club soda.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Kimpton Schofield Hotel: A stylish boutique hotel that combines historic charm with modern amenities.

  • The Westin Cleveland Downtown: A luxury hotel with breathtaking views of Lake Erie and the city skyline.

  • The Hyatt Regency Cleveland at The Arcade: A landmark property that features dome ceilings, elegant architecture, and upscale amenities.

  • The Cleveland Hostel: An affordable and comfortable option for budget travelers, located in the trendy Ohio City neighborhood.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rock and Roll Hall of Fame memorabilia: T-shirts, posters, pins, and other merchandise that pay homage to the greats.

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics from the Cleveland Museum of Art gift shop.

  • Sports memorabilia and apparel from the Cavs, Indians, and Browns official stores.

  • Cleveland-made hot sauces, chocolates, and other delicious local treats from the West Side Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

  • Clevelanders are proud of their city’s blue-collar roots and strong work ethic.

  • The city’s diverse and multiethnic population contributes to its vibrant cultural scene.

  • Cleveland’s sports teams are a major source of pride and passion for residents.

  • The city has a friendly and welcoming vibe that makes visitors feel at home.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown Cleveland is from June to September, when the weather is warm, and outdoor festivals and events abound. However, visitors can also enjoy the district’s attractions year-round, including winter sports, holiday celebrations, and indoor cultural activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Do try the local food and craft beer scene.

  • Do visit the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Museum.

  • Do take a bike ride along the Towpath Trail.

  • Do catch a game at one of the city’s iconic sports venues.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the importance of safety, especially when traveling alone at night.

  • Don’t overlook the city’s historic and cultural landmarks, such as the Cleveland Museum of Art and the Cleveland History Center.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for suggestions, as they are often happy to share their favorite spots and hidden gems.

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the city on foot.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Cleveland is a relatively safe area, but visitors should exercise common-sense precautions, such as staying in well-lit areas, avoiding empty streets and alleys, and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and report suspicious activity or incidents to law enforcement.

Health in Downtown

Cleveland boasts excellent medical facilities, including the world-renowned Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals. Visitors with medical needs or emergencies should seek medical assistance promptly, either by visiting an urgent care center or calling 911.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport: Serves as the primary airport and is located approximately 12 miles southwest of Downtown Cleveland.

  • Akron-Canton Airport: Located approximately 52 miles southeast of Downtown Cleveland and offers a more budget-friendly option.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located at 1465 Chester Avenue, is a major transportation hub servicing the city and surrounding areas.

  • Megabus Station: Located at 1465 Chester Avenue, it connects Cleveland to various cities in the Midwest and beyond.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Cleveland’s historic Union Terminal serves Amtrak’s daily trains, including the Lakeshore Limited and Capitol Limited lines.

  • Ride-sharing apps: Uber, Lyft, and other ride-share services provide reliable and convenient transportation from and to Downtown Cleveland.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Downtown Cleveland is a relatively compact area that can be easily explored on foot. Visitors may also opt for bike-sharing services, such as Cleveland’s UHBikes, or take advantage of the city’s public transit system, including buses and trains operated by the Greater Cleveland Regional Transit Authority (RTA). Taxis and ride-share services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Erie Islands: Take a ferry ride to Put-in-Bay, Kelley’s Island, or Middle Bass Island for a laid-back island adventure.

  • Akron: A vibrant city that offers historic landmarks, museums, parks, and botanical gardens.

  • Canton: Home to the Pro Football Hall of Fame and other sports attractions, as well as the picturesque McKinley Presidential Library and Museum.

  • Cleveland Heights: A trendy urban neighborhood with a lively arts scene, hip cafes, and quirky boutiques.

Conclusion

Downtown Cleveland is a dynamic and exciting district that offers a unique blend of culture, history, recreation, and entertainment. Whether you’re a die-hard sports fan, a foodie, a music lover, or simply looking to discover a city with a rich past and a thriving present, Downtown Cleveland is the perfect destination for you. Start planning your trip today and get ready to experience the best that the heartland of America has to offer.
