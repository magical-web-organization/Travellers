Ashtabula is a city nestled in Ashtabula County in Northeast Ohio. Located in Greater Cleveland, Ashtabula offers a lot to explore, including scenic views, historic sites, outdoor adventures, and a vibrant food and drink scene.

If you are planning a trip to Ashtabula and wondering where to start, this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect vacation. From a list of must-visit places to top-rated hotels, here is everything you need to know about Ashtabula.

Introduction to Ashtabula

Ashtabula is a small city that boasts of breathtaking natural beauty, soothing landscapes, and rich history. With attractions and activities that cater to everyone, Ashtabula has emerged as one of the most popular tourist destinations in Northeast Ohio.

List of Places to Visit in Ashtabula

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hubbard House Underground Railroad Museum: Dedicated to preserving the history of the Underground Railroad, the Hubbard House offers a peek into the lives of people who helped slaves escape to freedom.

  2. Ashtabula Maritime Museum: Discover the fascinating history of shipping and shipbuilding in Ashtabula through exhibits, interactive displays, and guided tours at the Ashtabula Maritime Museum.

  3. Ashtabula County Historical Society: Step back in time as you explore the Ashtabula County Historical Society’s collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the story of Ashtabula’s rich heritage.

Natural Attractions of Ashtabula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Geneva State Park: Known for its clear blue waters and sandy beaches, Geneva State Park is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and hiking.

  2. Pymatuning State Park: Boasting of the largest lake in Pennsylvania, Pymatuning State Park offers outdoor enthusiasts plenty of activities to enjoy, including fishing, hiking, camping, and more.

  3. Ashtabula County Metroparks: Covering more than 1,200 acres, the Ashtabula County Metroparks offer visitors an opportunity to explore nature through its parks, hiking trails, fishing lakes, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashtabula

  1. Western Reserve Greenway: Spanning over 43 miles, Western Reserve Greenway is popular among hikers and bikers alike for its panoramic views of wildlife, woodlands, and waterways.

  2. Lake Erie Birding Trail: Ashtabula’s location on the shores of Lake Erie makes it an excellent destination for bird watching. The Lake Erie Birding Trail offers 84 miles of scenic routes for people interested in feathered friends.

  3. Walnut Beach Trail: Located in scenic Conneaut, the Walnut Beach Trail offers visitors the perfect opportunity to explore the scenic beauty along Lake Erie’s shore.

Best Things to Do in Ashtabula: Activities and Tours

  1. Bike and Wine Tour: Experience the beautiful countryside of Ashtabula while sipping on delicious wine with a bike and wine tour. You’ll visit different wineries, taste wines, and get the chance to explore the picturesque countryside on two wheels.

  2. Covered Bridge Tour: Ashtabula County is home to an impressive collection of covered bridges that offer a glimpse of the area’s past. Take a tour of these historical covered bridges for an unforgettable experience.

  3. Kayaking or Canoeing on the Ashtabula River: Get a new perspective of Ashtabula’s natural beauty by kayaking or canoeing along the Ashtabula River. You can rent canoes and kayaks and explore the waterways at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashtabula

Day 1: Visit Hubbard House Underground Railroad Museum and then head to Geneva State Park to soak in the sun.

Day 2: Start your day with a bike and wine tour and spend the evening exploring Covered Bridges in the region.

Day 3: Go hiking along Western Reserve Greenway and enjoy scenic views along the trail.

Day 4: Visit Ashtabula Maritime Museum and learn about the shipping history; Spend the evening watching the beautiful sunset from Lake Erie Bluffs.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of Ashtabula County Historical Society and then enjoy kayaking or canoeing on the Ashtabula River.

Day 6: Visit Pymatuning State Park, the largest lake in Pennsylvania, and spend the day fishing, hiking, and camping.

Day 7: Head to Walnut Beach Trail and enjoy a walk along the shore.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashtabula

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ashtabula County Apple Pie: Try the iconic Ashtabula County Apple Pie, a delicious dessert loved by locals and visitors alike.

  2. Lake Erie Perch: Savor the taste of fresh Lake Erie Perch, a regional specialty that is fried to perfection.

  3. Wineries: From Kosicek Vineyards to Ferrante Winery & Ristorante, Ashtabula is known for its wine and offers visitors plenty of opportunities to taste excellent wines.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: Sample the refreshing local craft beer scene of Ashtabula, with spots like Rennick Meat Market and Bascule Brewery & Public House.

  2. Apple Cider: Sip on delicious apple cider, a refreshing fall drink that is made using local apples.

Where to Stay in Ashtabula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Lakehouse Inn: A beautiful inn that features luxury accommodations, excellent wine, and delicious food, all while offering unparalleled views of the lake.

  2. Economy Inn: A budget-friendly option, located conveniently near popular attractions such as Ashtabula County Metroparks and Hubbard House Underground Railroad Museum.

  3. Holiday Inn Express & Suites: A comfortable and modern option for business and leisure travelers, equipped with complimentary breakfast and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Ashtabula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Wine: Ashtabula’s wine selection is undoubtedly one of the best in Ohio. Bring home a bottle or two of your favorite wine as a souvenir.

  2. Covered Bridge Items: Ashtabula County is famous for its collection of beautiful covered bridges, and you’ll find plenty of souvenirs to remind you of this distinctive feature, such as mugs, fridge magnets, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashtabula

Ashtabula has a rich cultural heritage. The region is known for its wineries, apple orchards, and picturesque countryside. The locals are friendly and welcoming and proud of their unique history, which is an essential part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Ashtabula

Spring and fall are the best times to visit Ashtabula. The weather is pleasant, and there are many events and festivities to enjoy. The peak tourist season falls between June and August when the weather is warm, and the days are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashtabula

Do:

  1. Do explore the covered bridges that are unique to Ashtabula.

  2. Do taste the local wine and delicious apple pie.

  3. Do enjoy the scenic beauty of Ashtabula’s countryside.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to carry insect repellent when hiking or participating in any outdoor activity.

  2. Don’t litter, Ashtabula is proud of its many natural wonders and strives to keep its environment clean and healthy.

  3. Don’t forget to bring along a warm coat during the chilly winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashtabula

Ashtabula is a safe city to visit and has a low crime rate. However, it’s always essential to take necessary precautions when traveling to a new location, like keeping an eye on your belongings, staying in well-lit areas, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Ashtabula

Ashtabula is well-equipped with medical facilities. There is a hospital within easy reach of most tourist sites, and visitors can always contact the nearest emergency services in case of any health emergency.

Getting to Ashtabula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ashtabula

The closest airport to Ashtabula is the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, about 73 miles away. Another nearby airport is the Akron-Canton Regional Airport, located about 80 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Ashtabula

Greyhound Bus Lines operates from the Ashtabula Bus Terminal, located in the heart of downtown Ashtabula.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are readily available for travel within the city.

How to Get Around in Ashtabula

The best way to get around Ashtabula is by car. Car rentals are widely available at the airport and in the city. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Ashtabula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cleveland, OH: Just an hour’s drive away from Ashtabula, visit Cleveland and explore its world-renowned art museum, beautiful parks, and a vibrant food and drink scene.

  2. Buffalo, NY: Buffalo is a 2.5-hour drive away from Ashtabula and offers visitors beautiful waterfront landscapes, a bustling food scene, and the impressive Frank Lloyd Wright-designed Darwin D. Martin House.

  3. Pittsburgh, PA: A 2.5-hour drive from Ashtabula, Pittsburgh is a vibrant city that offers world-class museums, botanical gardens, and picturesque riverfronts.

Conclusion

Ashtabula, Ohio, is undoubtedly a hidden gem that deserves a spot on every traveler’s bucket list. With its scenic beauty, rich history, and exciting attractions, Ashtabula promises an unforgettable travel experience. Plan your trip to Ashtabula today!
