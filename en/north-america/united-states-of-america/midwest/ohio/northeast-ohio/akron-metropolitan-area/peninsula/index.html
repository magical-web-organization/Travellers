Introduction to Peninsula

Peninsula is a charming village located in the heart of Cuyahoga Valley National Park, which is a part of Akron Metropolitan Area in Northeast Ohio. Surrounded by stunning natural scenery of the Cuyahoga River and the Ohio and Erie Canal towpath trails, Peninsula offers visitors a wonderful blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delightful cuisine.

The village is well-known for its historic sites, such as the Peninsula Depot and the Lock 29 Trailhead, as well as for its popular events, such as the annual Boston Mills Artfest and the Peninsula Flea Market. This travel guide provides you with an extensive list of things to do, places to visit, and sights to see while in Peninsula, Ohio.

List of Places to Visit in Peninsula

There are a variety of culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures to explore in Peninsula.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Several museums and historical places are located in Peninsula, Ohio, including:

  • Peninsula Depot: A historic train station that now serves as a visitor center for the Cuyahoga Valley Scenic Railroad.
  • Boston Store Visitor Center: A reconstructed general store that provides an interesting glimpse into the past and is now a visitor center for the Cuyahoga Valley National Park.

Natural Attractions of Peninsula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Peninsula is located amid the incredible natural scenery of Cuyahoga Valley National Park, where visitors can enjoy outdoor adventures and scenic views, such as:

  • Ohio & Erie Canal Towpath Trail: A 101-mile trail, the longest of its kind in Ohio, that offers hiking, biking, and horseback riding opportunities along the historic canal.
  • Brandywine Falls: A spectacular 65-foot waterfall surrounded by a beautiful natural landscape.
  • Ledges Trail: A scenic hiking trail that leads through unique rock formations and offers breathtaking views of the Cuyahoga River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peninsula

Peninsula offers many hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Towpath Trail: A 20-mile trail that leads through Cuyahoga Valley National Park.
  • Stanford Trail: A 2.2-mile trail that traverses through the forest and offers great views of Medina County.
  • Monarch Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that runs through the pine forests of the National Park and offers a great opportunity to view Monarch Butterflies.

Best Things to Do in Peninsula: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Peninsula, Ohio, such as:

  • Cuyahoga Valley Scenic Railroad: Take a ride on the vintage train and enjoy the breathtaking views of the National Park.
  • Boston Mills Ski Resort: Enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and tubing during the winter season.
  • Cuyahoga Valley Art Center: Take an art class or visit the gallery for unique artwork by local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peninsula

To make the most of your visit to Peninsula, here is a week-long itinerary:

Day 1 Visit the Peninsula Depot, tour Boston Store Visitor Center

Food and Drink Scene of Peninsula

Peninsula, Ohio offers a variety of tasty dishes and local beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: These are popular in Ohio, stuffed with mashed potatoes, cheese, sauerkraut, and meat.
  • Buckeye candies: These are delicious peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Ohio has several craft breweries, and the surrounding Akron area has innovative brewers, many of which use local ingredients.
  • Wine: The state of Ohio has unique wines made from local grapes. Several vineyards near Peninsula offer tastings of their locally produced wines.

Where to Stay in Peninsula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Peninsula, Ohio, such as:

  • The Inn at Brandywine Falls: A historic bed and breakfast located next to Brandywine Falls.
  • The Spicy Lamb Farm: This upscale rustic farm stay offers modern amenities in the scenic countryside surrounding Peninsula.
  • Hiram Inn & Conference Center: A clean, modern hotel located in nearby Hiram for visitors that prefer more traditional lodging.

What to Buy in Peninsula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peninsula has a variety of local products and souvenirs to take home as a memento of your visit, such as:

  • Artwork: Local artists sell their artwork in Peninsula, and you can find unique paintings, sculptures, and photography.
  • Pottery and Ceramics: Pottery and ceramics are popular in Peninsula and can be found in local shops.
  • Jewelry: Bracelets, necklaces, and earrings made by local artisans are available in several gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Peninsula

The people of Peninsula are welcoming and friendly, and visitors are expected to be respectful of local customs. Visitors should be aware that smoking is prohibited in all public buildings, parks, and any enclosed areas in Ohio, including bars and restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Peninsula

The best time to visit Peninsula, Ohio is in the Spring (March to May) and Fall (September to November). The weather is mild, and the trees change colors, making for a scenic view of the park.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peninsula

Do:

  • Respect Ohio laws and local customs.
  • Visit the Brandywine Falls and Ledges Trail for scenic hikes.
  • Taste the local craft beer and wine scene in Akron and the surrounding areas.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public buildings, bars, and restaurants.
  • Neglect to check the weather before planning your visit to the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peninsula

Peninsula, Ohio is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take general precautions, such as not leaving valuables in visible areas of their vehicle, especially in unattended parking lots.

Health in Peninsula

Peninsula, Ohio has several clinics, hospitals, and pharmacies. Visitors should stay hydrated, especially if they are participating in outdoor activities during the summer season.

Getting to Peninsula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peninsula

The nearest airport to Peninsula, Ohio is Hopkins International Airport (CLE), located in Cleveland, 35 miles away. Akron-Canton Airport (CAK) is also an option for travelers and is located 30 miles from Peninsula.

Bus Stations Near Peninsula

Greyhound bus maintain station in Cleveland, 35 miles away from Peninsula.

Other Options

Renting a car is an option for visitors that wish to explore the Cuyahoga Valley National Park at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Peninsula

Visitors can easily get around Peninsula, Ohio via car rental, transit, or taxi services. Many hotels in the area also offer shuttle services to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Peninsula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peninsula is well situated amid several nearby destinations and excursions, such as:

  • Cleveland: Located 35 minutes north of Peninsula, this lively city is home to a variety of attractions, such as the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the Cleveland Museum of Art.
  • Akron: Located 20 miles south of Peninsula, Akron is home to the Well’s Kane House Museum and the Stan Hywet Hall & Gardens, as well as several cultural attractions and local breweries.
  • Columbus: Ohio’s capital city is a two-hour car ride from Peninsula. Columbus is home to excellent food and craft beer, as well as cultural attractions such as the Center of Science and Industry.

Conclusion

Peninsula, Ohio is the ideal destination for anyone looking for an authentic taste of Midwest living.
