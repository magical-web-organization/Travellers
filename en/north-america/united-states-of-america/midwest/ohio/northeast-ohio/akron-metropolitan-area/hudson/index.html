Introduction to Hudson

Located in the Akron Metropolitan Area in Northeast Ohio, Hudson is a charming town with a population of around 22,000 people. Often referred to as a “storybook village,” Hudson is known for its vibrant downtown area, beautiful parks, and historic landmarks. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just some relaxation, Hudson has something to offer everyone. Keep reading for a comprehensive travel guide to Hudson!

List of Places to Visit in Hudson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Hudson Community First & Merchants Block Historic District: This historic district is home to over 50 buildings, most of which were built in the 1800s. Take a stroll and admire the beautiful architecture and learn about the town’s history.
  • The Hudson Museum: Located on the Western Reserve Academy campus, this museum features exhibits about Hudson’s history, including its early settlers and the Underground Railroad.
  • The Hudson Library and Historical Society: This library is not just a place to check out books— it also has exhibits and events about local history, including the famous Hudson Annual House Tour.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hudson Springs Park: This park is perfect for a picnic, leisurely walk, or fishing. A paved trail wraps around the lake, offering beautiful views of the water and surrounding greenery.
  • Brandywine Falls: Just a short drive from Hudson, Brandywine Falls in Cuyahoga National Park is a breathtaking sight. These 65-foot falls are surrounded by lush forest and rock formations and are definitely worth the visit.
  • Cascade Valley Metro Park: This park has tons of hiking and biking trails, including a section of the popular Ohio and Erie Canal Towpath Trail. With beautiful views of the Cuyahoga River, this park is perfect for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hudson

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Hudson is a great place to explore! Some popular routes include:

  • Hudson Springs Park Loop: This 1.4-mile trail that circles the lake at Hudson Springs Park is paved and great for all skill levels.
  • Bike & Hike Trail: This trail stretches over 34 miles, connecting Hudson with other towns in the area. It is mostly flat and perfect for biking, walking, or running.
  • Haskell Run Trail: This 1.7-mile trail is for hikers only, offering scenic views of the Cuyahoga River and Haskell Pond.

Best Things to Do in Hudson: Activities and Tours

  • The Hudson Farmers Market: Held every Saturday, this market features local vendors selling fresh produce, baked goods, and homemade crafts.
  • Hudson Wine Festival: This annual festival in late July showcases dozens of Ohio wineries, live music, and food vendors.
  • Ghost Tour of Hudson: Learn about the town’s haunted history on this spooky walking tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hudson

Day Activity
Monday Explore downtown Hudson and grab a bite to eat at one of the many local restaurants.
Tuesday Visit the Hudson Museum on the Western Reserve Academy campus, then head to Hudson Springs Park for a picnic.
Wednesday Take a day trip to Cuyahoga Valley National Park and hike to Brandywine Falls.
Thursday Spend the day biking on the Bike & Hike Trail, stopping for lunch at a local cafe.
Friday Sign up for a wine tasting tour at one of the many wineries in the area.
Saturday Relax at Cascade Valley Metro Park or attend the Hudson Farmers Market.
Sunday Take a spooky tour of Hudson’s haunted locations with Ghost Tour of Hudson.

Food and Drink Scene of Hudson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Bisque from The Learned Owl Book Shop Cafe: This cozy cafe is a favorite among locals and features a delicious lobster bisque.
  • Fish Tacos from Three Palms Grille: This restaurant features a seafood-focused menu, and their fish tacos are a must-try.
  • Beef Tongue Tacos from Aladdin’s Eatery: Looking for something a little more unique? Try the beef tongue tacos from Aladdin’s Eatery, a Mediterranean restaurant in town.

Local Beverages

  • Lager from Mucky Duck Brewery: This local brewery serves up their flagship lager, along with other seasonal beers, in a cozy taproom.
  • Wine from Laurello Vineyards: Take a winery tour and tasting at Laurello Vineyards, known for their bold, flavorful wines.

Where to Stay in Hudson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at Brandywine Falls: Just a short drive from Hudson, this bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and beautiful views of the surrounding forest.
  • Comfort Suites Hudson: This hotel is located just minutes from downtown and offers amenities like an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Hostel 81 Hudson: Looking for an affordable option? Check out Hostel 81 Hudson, which offers dorm-style rooms for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Hudson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Toy Trains at The Learned Owl Book Shop: This beloved bookstore offers a large selection of toy trains, perfect for any train enthusiast.
  • Handmade Soaps and Candles at The Elements Gallery: This shop features handmade soaps and candles, plus jewelry and other locally made goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Hudson

Hudson is known for its strong sense of community and commitment to preserving its historic landmarks. Locals take pride in their town and are happy to share its history with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Hudson

While Hudson is beautiful year-round, the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hudson

Do:

  • Do visit the many historic landmarks in town, like The Hudson Community First & Merchants Block Historic District.
  • Do explore the beautiful parks and natural areas around Hudson.
  • Do try some of the local cuisine, like the fish tacos from Three Palms Grille.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in Hudson’s parks or natural areas— locals take great care in keeping these areas clean and beautiful.
  • Don’t miss out on one of the many local events, like the Hudson Wine Festival or Ghost Tour of Hudson.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hudson

Hudson is a safe town with a low crime rate. As with any location, be aware of your surroundings and practice common sense safety precautions.

Health in Hudson

There are several medical facilities in Hudson, including Cleveland Clinic Akron General Express Care and Summa Health Center for Health Equity. In case of emergency, call 911.

Getting to Hudson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hudson

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport (CLE)
  • Akron-Canton Airport (CAK)
  • Youngstown-Warren Regional Airport (YNG)

Bus Stations Near Hudson

  • Akron METRO Transit Center
  • Greyhound Bus Station in downtown Akron

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a great option for getting around Hudson and surrounding areas.
  • Uber and Lyft are also available for transportation.

How to Get Around in Hudson

The best way to get around Hudson is by car, but bicycles and walking are also great options in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Hudson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cleveland: Just a 30-minute drive from Hudson, Cleveland offers a lively art scene, professional sports teams, and the famous Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • Akron: The city of Akron is just a 20-minute drive from Hudson, and offers attractions like the Akron Art Museum and Akron Zoo.

Conclusion

Hudson, Ohio is a charming town with a lot to offer. From beautiful parks and hiking trails to local events and delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Hudson. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your next trip to this lovely town!
