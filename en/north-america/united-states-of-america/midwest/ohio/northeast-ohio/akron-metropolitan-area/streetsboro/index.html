Introduction to Streetsboro

Located in the heart of Northeast Ohio, amidst rolling hills and lush green forests, Streetsboro is a quaint town with a rich history and vibrant culture. Despite being a small town, Streetsboro has a lot to offer to its visitors - from scenic hiking trails and natural attractions to culturally significant museums and historical landmarks.

Whether you’re a city slicker looking to escape the hustle and bustle of urban life or a nature lover seeking adventure, Streetsboro has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Streetsboro, where to stay, where to eat, and what to see.

List of Places to Visit in Streetsboro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Streetsboro is home to several unique and captivating museums and historical sites. Here are a few must-visit places:

  • The Streetsboro Heritage Park & Museum - Discover the rich history of Streetsboro at this fascinating museum that showcases artifacts and exhibits from the early 1800s to the present day.
  • The Penniman House - Step back in time and explore the beautifully restored and furnished mansion that belonged to one of the original settlers of Streetsboro.
  • The Hudson Library & Historical Society - While not technically in Streetsboro, this library located in nearby Hudson is worth a visit. The library houses a vast collection of historical documents, photographs, and local artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Streetsboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Streetsboro is blessed with breathtaking natural beauty, including lush forests, rolling hills, and picturesque lakes. Here are some top natural attractions to explore:

  • Tinker’s Creek State Park - This gorgeous park offers something for everyone, including hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and even hunting during open seasons.
  • Headwater Park - A hidden gem, this 111-acre park features several hiking trails, a picturesque creek, and stunning waterfall views.
  • Wingfoot Lake State Park - Located just a short drive from Streetsboro, this stunning park has plenty to offer, from boating and fishing to hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Streetsboro

For outdoor enthusiasts, Streetsboro is a paradise with numerous hiking and biking trails offering stunning views of the town’s natural beauty. Some popular trails include:

  • The Buckeye Trail - This 1,444-mile trail passes through 26 counties in Ohio, including Streetsboro. The trail features breathtaking views of the Cuyahoga River and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and mountain biking.
  • The Headwater Trail - This 6.7-mile trail starts at Headwater Park and meanders through the stunning natural scenery. The trail is perfect for hiking, running, and biking.

Best Things to Do in Streetsboro: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the town’s natural beauty, there are plenty of other things to do in Streetsboro. Here are a few top activities and tours:

  • Zip-line Tour at Zipcity - Zip through the lush forests of Northeast Ohio on a thrilling zip-line adventure at Zipcity. With over 8 zip lines and 171 obstacles, this tour is perfect for adrenaline junkies.
  • Tour the Glacial Grooves - These natural wonders were created over 20,000 years ago during the last ice age and are a must-see. The glacial grooves are located on Kelleys Island in Lake Erie, which can be reached via a ferry ride from Marblehead.
  • Attend the Streetsboro Family Days Festival - Held annually in July, this festival is a beloved tradition in town and features live music, fireworks, carnival rides, and entertainment for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Streetsboro

If you’re planning on spending a week in Streetsboro, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1-2: Explore the natural beauty of Streetsboro’s parks and hiking trails, such as Headwater Park and the Buckeye Trail.

Day 3: Discover the town’s rich history and culture at the Streetsboro Heritage Park & Museum and the Penniman House.

Day 4: Take a tour of the Hidden Valley Orchards and sample delicious local produce.

Day 5: Go fishing or boating at Wingfoot Lake State Park.

Day 6: Indulge in some retail therapy at Aurora Farms Premium Outlets.

Day 7: Attend a local festival or event.

Food and Drink Scene of Streetsboro

Streetsboro may be a small town, but it boasts an impressive food scene, with plenty of options to satisfy every craving. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies at The Pierogi Lady - This local eatery serves up delicious pierogies stuffed with a variety of fillings, from classic potato and cheese to more adventurous flavors like Buffalo chicken.
  • BBQ at Treno Ristorante - Treno Ristorante offers some of the best BBQ in town, with perfectly smoked meats and flavorful sides.
  • Pizza at Guido’s Pizza - You can’t go wrong with a classic pizza at this beloved local pizzeria.
  • Fried Chicken at New York Style Deli & Pizza - This restaurant serves up some of the tastiest fried chicken in town, as well as delicious deli sandwiches and pizza.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer at Thirsty Dog Brewing Company - This local brewery offers an array of craft beers, from hoppy IPAs to sweet stouts.
  • Wine at My Friends Vineyard - Located just a short drive from Streetsboro, this vineyard offers delicious wines and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Streetsboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Streetsboro, be sure to pick up some local souvenirs and products to bring home with you. Here are some top choices:

  • Cheese from Lucky Penny Farm - This family-owned farm produces some of the best goat cheese in the area, with plenty of unique flavors to choose from.
  • Handmade Soap from Indigo Perfumery - This unique store sells handcrafted soaps, perfumes, and skincare products made with all-natural ingredients.
  • Local honey from Trapp Family Farm - This farm produces delicious, sweet honey from its own bees and sells it at local farmers’ markets and stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Streetsboro

Streetsboro is a friendly and welcoming town with a strong sense of community. Visitors will find that locals are happy to share their knowledge of the area, point out the best places to eat or visit, and make recommendations for activities or events.

Best Time to Visit Streetsboro

The best time to visit Streetsboro is from May to October when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities. During this time, there are also several festivals and events taking place in the town and surrounding areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Streetsboro

Do:

  • Explore the town’s natural beauty on foot or bike.
  • Learn about the town’s rich history at the Streetsboro Heritage Park & Museum.
  • Indulge in local cuisine like pierogies and BBQ.
  • Visit nearby wineries and breweries for a taste of Northeast Ohio’s beverages.
  • Attend the Streetsboro Family Days festival for live music, entertainment, and fireworks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines while hiking or biking.
  • Litter in the town’s parks and natural areas.
  • Ignore local customs and be respectful of the town’s culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Streetsboro

Streetsboro is a safe and welcoming town. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take basic safety precautions like locking their vehicles and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Streetsboro

Streetsboro has several hospitals and clinics that provide medical care to residents and visitors of the town. Visitors should be aware of any allergies or medical conditions and carry their necessary medication with them.

Getting to Streetsboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Streetsboro

The closest airport to Streetsboro is Akron-Canton Airport, which is located approximately 25 miles from the town center. Cleveland Hopkins International Airport is also nearby, located approximately 35 miles from Streetsboro.

Bus Stations Near Streetsboro

Greyhound bus service is available in the nearby town of Akron, approximately 20 miles from Streetsboro.

Other Options

Streetsboro is easily accessible by car, with several major highways and Interstates within close proximity. Visitors can also take a train to nearby cities like Cleveland or Akron and rent a car to travel to Streetsboro.

How to Get Around in Streetsboro

The best way to get around Streetsboro is by car, as there is limited public transportation available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Streetsboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Streetsboro is conveniently located near several exciting destinations and attractions. Here are a few nearby places worth a visit:

  • Cleveland - Just a short drive from Streetsboro, Cleveland offers plenty of attractions, including the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the Cleveland Museum of Art.
  • Akron - Akron is located approximately 20 miles from Streetsboro and is home to several museums, including the Akron Art Museum and the Akron Zoo.
  • Cuyahoga Valley National Park - This stunning National Park is located approximately 35 miles from Streetsboro and offers breathtaking views of Ohio’s natural beauty.

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and welcoming community, Streetsboro is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Northeast Ohio. Explore the town’s parks and hiking trails, indulge in local cuisine, and immerse yourself in the area’s unique culture.
