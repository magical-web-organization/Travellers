Introduction to Northfield

Northfield is a charming village located in the Akron Metropolitan Area in Northeast Ohio. With its rich history, excellent outdoor activities, and cultural attractions, it is a popular tourist destination. Whether you are planning a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Northfield has something to offer for everyone. This travel guide will help you plan your trip and explore the best of Northfield.

List of Places to Visit in Northfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Northfield Center Town Hall & Schoolhouse Museum: This historic building was once a one-room schoolhouse and now serves as a museum dedicated to the history of Northfield.

  2. Maltz Museum of Jewish Heritage: This museum showcases the stories and artifacts of Jewish individuals and their contributions to American culture and history. The museum also includes interactive displays and temporary exhibits.

  3. Cuyahoga Valley Historical Museum: This museum offers insights into the history of the region, including the Ohio & Erie Canal, railroads, and early settlements.

Natural Attractions of Northfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Buckeye Trail: This trail is 1,444 miles long, stretching across Ohio. It passes through Northfield and offers beautiful views of the Cuyahoga Valley National Park.

  2. Cascade Valley Metro Park: This park covers 157 acres and includes hiking trails, playgrounds, picnic areas, and a nature center.

  3. Cuyahoga Valley National Park: This park has over 125 miles of trails, including the Towpath Trail, a 101-mile trail that passes through Northfield. The park also boasts waterfalls, historic structures, and a scenic railway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northfield

Northfield offers several popular hiking and biking routes that showcase the region’s natural beauty. Some of the most recommended routes are:

  1. Buckeye Trail: As mentioned before, this trail offers beautiful views of the Cuyahoga Valley National Park and is perfect for hiking and biking.

  2. Escarpment Trail: This trail is a 5.8-mile loop that offers an excellent workout, including some steep inclines and declines. The trail is also perfect for bird-watching.

  3. Trails in the Cuyahoga Valley National Park: There are over 125 miles of trails in the park, ranging from easy to difficult. Visitors can choose from a variety of routes, including the Towpath Trail.

Best Things to Do in Northfield: Activities and Tours

  1. Ride the Cuyahoga Valley Scenic Railroad: This scenic railway runs through the Cuyahoga Valley National Park and offers passengers stunning views of the park.

  2. Experience the entertainment at the Hard Rock Rocksino: This casino and entertainment venue features over 2,200 slot machines, table games, and a variety of shows and events.

  3. Visit the Northfield Park Racetrack: This racetrack is home to harness racing and is a popular destination for racing fans.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northfield

Day 1: Visit the Northfield Center Town Hall & Schoolhouse Museum and enjoy a picnic at Liberty Park.

Day 2: Explore the Maltz Museum of Jewish Heritage and take a walk on the Buckeye Trail.

Day 3: Visit the Cuyahoga Valley Historical Museum and ride the Cuyahoga Valley Scenic Railroad.

Day 4: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Towpath Trail and see the sights of the Cuyahoga Valley National Park.

Day 5: Visit the Hard Rock Rocksino and try your luck at the slot machines or enjoy one of the many shows.

Day 6: Spend the day at the Northfield Park Racetrack and watch the harness racing.

Day 7: Go on a hiking adventure at the Cascade Valley Metro Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Northfield

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pierogies: This classic dish of Polish origin consists of a savory filling (usually potato and cheese) wrapped in dough and boiled or fried. You can find delicious pierogies at shops and restaurants throughout Northfield.

  2. Corned Beef Sandwich: This sandwich is a staple of the Cleveland area and is made with corned beef, Swiss cheese, and Thousand Island dressing on rye bread. You can find it at many restaurants and delis in Northfield.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Northeast Ohio is home to many craft breweries, some of which have taprooms in Northfield. Check out Missing Mountain Brewing Co. or McArthur’s Brew House for some excellent craft beer.

  2. Ohio Wine: Ohio is a wine-producing state, and several wineries offer tastings and tours in the region. Visit Filia Cellars for some delicious Ohio wine.

Where to Stay in Northfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Spitzer House Bed & Breakfast: This elegant bed and breakfast is located in a historic home and offers luxurious accommodations.

  2. Motel 6: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

  3. Quality Inn: This hotel offers spacious rooms with free Wi-Fi and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Northfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Maple Syrup: Ohio is a top producer of maple syrup, and you can find delicious syrup at several shops and markets in Northfield.

  2. Polish Pottery: Northfield is home to some excellent stores that sell beautiful and unique Polish pottery.

  3. Vintage Records: If you are a music lover, check out the vinyl records at Time Traveler Records.

Local Culture and Customs of Northfield

Northfield has a rich history and is proud of its Polish heritage. The village celebrates this heritage with an annual Polish Festival in August. Northfield is also known for its harness racing, and many residents are passionate about this sport.

Best Time to Visit Northfield

The best time to visit Northfield is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the fall (September to November) is also a great time to visit, as the leaves change color and the scenery is breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northfield

Do:

  • Do explore the Cuyahoga Valley National Park and take advantage of the excellent hiking and biking trails.

  • Do attend the Northfield Polish Festival and experience the village’s Polish heritage.

  • Do try some delicious Polish food and Ohio wine during your stay.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking and biking.

  • Don’t miss the harness racing at the Northfield Park Racetrack.

  • Don’t forget to take some maple syrup and Polish pottery home as souvenirs!

Safety and Health

Safety in Northfield

Northfield is a safe and welcoming community. However, it is always important to take common-sense precautions when traveling, such as being aware of your surroundings and using caution when walking or hiking alone.

Health in Northfield

Northfield has several medical facilities, including a hospital and urgent care centers, to ensure your health and safety while in the village.

Getting to Northfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northfield

The closest airport to Northfield is Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, which is located approximately 25 miles north of the village.

Bus Stations Near Northfield

Greyhound operates a station in nearby Akron, approximately 10 miles from Northfield.

Other Options

Several car rental companies operate in Cleveland, and it is easy to rent a car at the airport and drive to Northfield.

How to Get Around in Northfield

The best way to get around Northfield is by car. However, if you prefer to bike or walk, many of the attractions and hiking trails are easily accessible from the village.

Where to Go Next from Northfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cleveland: The city of Cleveland is located approximately 20 miles north of Northfield and is home to several museums, attractions, and excellent dining and entertainment options.

  2. Akron: The city of Akron is located approximately 10 miles south of Northfield and offers a variety of attractions, including the Akron Zoo and the Akron Art Museum.

Conclusion

Northfield is a hidden gem in Northeast Ohio, with excellent outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and a rich history. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Northfield has something to offer for everyone. Use this travel guide to explore the best of Northfield and make the most of your trip.
