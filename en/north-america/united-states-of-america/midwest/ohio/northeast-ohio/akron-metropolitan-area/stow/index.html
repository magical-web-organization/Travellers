Stow is a city located in the Akron Metropolitan Area of Northeast Ohio, United States. A charming town with a population of approximately 34,837 people, Stow is known for its beautiful parks, fascinating museums and historical attractions, and a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or a foodie, there are plenty of things to see and do in Stow. So, let’s explore the city in this comprehensive tour guide.

Introduction to Stow

Stow has a rich history and culture that dates back centuries. The city was founded in 1805 and has since grown into a bustling hub of activity. Stow sits at the crossroads of several major highways, making it an easy destination to get to from anywhere in the United States. The city boasts a variety of attractions, including stunning parks, vibrant cultural events, and fascinating museums.

List of Places to Visit in Stow

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Stow Historical Society - This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Stow. Visitors can see a variety of exhibits and artifacts that show what life was like in Stow during different periods.
  2. Cuyahoga Valley Scenic Railroad - Take a ride on a vintage train and explore the beauty of Cuyahoga National Park.
  3. Hale Farm and Village - This living museum showcases life in the 19th century. Visitors can see a variety of historical buildings and participate in interactive exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Stow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Silver Springs Park - This park features a variety of hiking trails, scenic views, and picnic areas.
  2. Cuyahoga Valley National Park - A beautiful national park that has a variety of hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife.
  3. O’Neil Woods Metropark - A great place for hiking, bird-watching, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stow

  1. Bike and Hike Trail - This trail is a favorite of locals and visitors alike. It’s a 34-mile trail that goes from the Cuyahoga Valley National Park to the Cleveland Metroparks.
  2. Munroe Falls Trail - This trail is a 1.8-mile loop that offers beautiful views of the forest and the river.

Best Things to Do in Stow: Activities and Tours

  1. Escape Room Cleveland - Solve puzzles with your friends and escape a themed room in under an hour.
  2. Lovers Lane Murders Tour - Take a tour of the infamous Lovers Lane Murders, a case that dates back to the 1940s.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stow

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Stow Historical Society Museum and take a walk around Silver Springs Park.
Day 2 Take a scenic ride on the Cuyahoga Valley Scenic Railroad and visit Hale Farm and Village.
Day 3 Go for a hike on the Bike and Hike Trail and have a picnic at O’Neil Woods Metropark.
Day 4 Visit the Lovers Lane Murders Tour and have lunch at one of Stow’s many restaurants.
Day 5 Take a day trip to Cleveland to see the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame or the Cleveland Museum of Art.
Day 6 Bike the Munroe Falls Trail and have a picnic by the river.
Day 7 Visit the Blossom Music Center, an outdoor venue that hosts live music performances throughout the year.

Food and Drink Scene of Stow

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stow Pizza - A local favorite, Stow Pizza makes some of the best pizza in the city.
  2. The Courtyard - This restaurant offers a variety of American classics, including burgers, sandwiches, and salads.
  3. Stow Cafe - This diner serves up classic diner fare, including breakfast, pancakes, and milkshakes.

Local Beverages

  1. The Brew Kettle - This brewery serves up a variety of craft beers in a relaxed, laid-back atmosphere.
  2. Mingo Kitchen and Lounge - This restaurant and bar offers a variety of cocktails and an extensive wine list.

Local Culture and Customs of Stow

Stow is known for its welcoming and friendly locals. Visitors should expect to be greeted with a smile and a kind word. The city also has a strong sense of community, and local events and festivals are often organized to bring people together.

Best Time to Visit Stow

The best time to visit Stow is in the fall when the leaves change color and the temperatures are mild. The city also has a variety of festivals and events during this time, including the Stow-Hudson Pumpkin Festival and the Grape Escape Wine Festival.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stow

Stow is a safe city, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their car doors and watching their belongings.

Health in Stow

Stow has several hospitals and medical centers, including Akron General Hospital and Summa Health System.

Getting to Stow: Transportation Options and Logistics

  2. Akron-Canton Regional Airport - A smaller airport located approximately 20 miles from Stow.

  2. Megabus Stop - Located in Cleveland, approximately 35 miles from Stow.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Stow

The best way to get around in Stow is by car. The city also has several bike trails and paths for those who prefer to travel by bike.

Where to Go Next from Stow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  2. Cuyahoga Valley National Park - A beautiful national park located just a few miles from Stow. The park has a variety of hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or simply relax and take in the beautiful sights, Stow is the perfect destination for your next vacation.
