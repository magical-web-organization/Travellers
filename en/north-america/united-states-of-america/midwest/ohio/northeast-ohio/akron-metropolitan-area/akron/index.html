Introduction to Akron

Akron is a beautiful city in Northeast Ohio, known as the “Rubber Capital of the World.” With a population of around 200,000, it is the fifth-largest city in Ohio. It is a bustling city with a rich history and a diverse culture, offering plenty of attractions for tourists. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will show you the best places to visit, dine, and stay in Akron, along with activities and tours that will make your trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Akron

Akron is home to many culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and scenic views. Here are some of the best places to visit in Akron.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Akron Art Museum: This museum in downtown Akron features modern and contemporary art, including paintings, sculpture, and photography.
  2. Stan Hywet Hall and Gardens: This 70-acre estate features a Tudor Revival mansion, as well as beautiful gardens and a conservatory.
  3. National Museum of Psychology: This museum is dedicated to the history of psychology, including exhibits on the founding of the American Psychological Association.
  4. Akron Civic Theatre: The Akron Civic Theatre is a historic theater that was built in 1929. It hosts concerts, plays, and other live events.

Natural Attractions of Akron: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cuyahoga Valley National Park: The Cuyahoga Valley National Park features stunning waterfalls, hiking and biking trails, and scenic train rides.
  2. Portage Lakes State Park: This state park features beautiful lakes perfect for boating and fishing.
  3. Akron Zoo: The Akron Zoo features a diverse array of animals, including lions, tigers, bears, and more.
  4. F.A. Seiberling Nature Realm: This nature reserve features beautiful walking trails that wind through prairies, forests, and wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akron

Akron is a great destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Akron:

  1. Ohio & Erie Canal Towpath Trail: This trail follows the historic route of the Ohio and Erie canal, running through the Cuyahoga Valley National Park.
  2. Summit Metro Parks: Summit Metro Parks offers over 150 miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through beautiful forests and wetlands.
  3. Bike & Hike Trail: The Bike & Hike Trail is a 34-mile trail that runs from Sagamore Hills to Munroe Falls, crossing several parks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Akron: Activities and Tours

  1. Goodyear Blimp Ride: Take a ride on the iconic Goodyear blimp and get a unique perspective of Akron from high above.
  2. Canton Food Tours: Explore the culinary scene of Akron and Canton on a guided food tour.
  3. Akron Symphony Orchestra: Enjoy a night out with the Akron Symphony Orchestra, featuring world-class performances.
  4. Akron Ghost Tours: Explore the spooky side of Akron on a ghost tour, learning about its haunted history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Akron

Day 1: Visit the Akron Art Museum and Stan Hywet Hall and Gardens. Day 2: Spend the day in the Cuyahoga Valley National Park, taking a scenic train ride and hiking some of the park’s trails. Day 3: Explore the Akron Zoo and F.A. Seiberling Nature Realm. Day 4: Take a Goodyear Blimp Ride and visit the National Museum of Psychology. Day 5: Take a Canton food tour and enjoy a night out at the Akron Symphony Orchestra. Day 6: Visit the Akron Civic Theatre, and do some shopping at the local markets and boutiques downtown. Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the nearby spas or golf courses.

Food and Drink Scene of Akron

Akron has a diverse food scene, with options for every taste and budget. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Akron-style chili: This unique style of chili features spaghetti topped with a meaty chili sauce and often served with cheese and onions.
  2. Swenson’s Galley Boy: Swenson’s is a local fast-food chain that serves up burgers topped with two special sauces and a pickle.
  3. Ohio City Pasta: This local pasta shop offers a variety of fresh, handmade pastas.

Local Beverages

  1. Hoppin’ Frog Brewery: Hoppin’ Frog Brewery is a local microbrewery with a wide array of craft beers.
  2. Portage Lakes Brewing Company: Portage Lakes Brewing Company is another local brewery with a great selection of craft beers.
  3. Norka Soda: Norka Soda is a local soda company that offers unique flavors like Root Beer and Orange Creamsicle.

Where to Stay in Akron: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Akron offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s needs. Here are some of the most popular:

  1. The Hilton Akron/Fairlawn: This hotel features a fitness center, indoor pool, and on-site restaurant.
  2. Courtyard Akron Downtown: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Akron, with easy access to many of the city’s attractions.
  3. The Akron B&B: This cozy bed and breakfast features comfortable rooms and a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Akron: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Harry London Gourmet Chocolates: Harry London is a local chocolate shop that offers handmade chocolates in many flavors.
  2. Akron Honey Company: Akron Honey Company offers locally sourced honey in a variety of flavors.
  3. The Gardener of Bath: The Gardener of Bath offers a variety of handcrafted natural skincare products made with local ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Akron

Akron has a diverse and welcoming culture, with strong ties to its industrial past. Residents are generally friendly and helpful to visitors, and there is a strong sense of community throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit Akron

The best time to visit Akron is in the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer cooler temperatures, the fall months (September to November) offer pleasant weather and beautiful foliage. Winters can be cold and snowy, but if you don’t mind the cold, winter also offers a unique experience in Akron.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akron

Do:

  1. Enjoy the local cuisine, especially Akron-style chili and Swenson’s burgers.
  2. Visit the Cuyahoga Valley National Park for a day out in nature.
  3. Explore the local museums and historical sites.
  4. Meet the friendly locals and engage with the community.

Don’t:

  1. Leave valuables in your car or unattended in public areas.
  2. Litter or damage the local parks and natural attractions.
  3. Disrespect local customs or cultural sensitivities.
  4. Leave without trying the local craft beers and soda.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akron

Akron is generally a safe city, but it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep your valuables close by. If you are driving, be cautious of busy intersections and always lock your car doors.

Health in Akron

Akron has excellent healthcare facilities, including several hospitals and clinics. If you need medical attention, ask for recommendations from your hotel or the visitor center.

Getting to Akron: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Akron

  1. Akron-Canton Regional Airport
  2. Cleveland Hopkins International Airport

Bus Stations Near Akron

  1. Akron Metro Regional Transit Center

Other Options

  1. Rental cars: There are several rental car options available at both airports.
  2. Taxis and rideshare: Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are available in Akron.

How to Get Around in Akron

Akron is a walkable city, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. There is also a reliable public transportation system operated by the Akron Metro Regional Transit Center, as well as several bike and scooter rental services.

Where to Go Next from Akron: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cleveland: Visit the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame or explore the Cleveland Museum of Art.
  2. Canton: Take a trip to the Pro Football Hall of Fame or go on a Canton food tour.
  3. Columbus: Visit the Ohio Statehouse or take a stroll through the Short North Arts District.

Conclusion

Akron, Ohio, is a hidden gem in the Midwest, offering a diverse range of attractions, a vibrant culture, and a welcoming community. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Akron, from the best places to visit and dine, to where to stay and what to bring home. It’s time to pack your bags and discover all that Akron has to offer.
