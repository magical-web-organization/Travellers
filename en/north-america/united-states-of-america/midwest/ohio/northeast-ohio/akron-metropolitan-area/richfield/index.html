Introduction to Richfield

Located in the Akron Metropolitan area, in Northeast Ohio, Richfield is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural attractions. The town is known for its abundance of outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, biking and fishing. Visitors to Richfield can also enjoy a rich history which includes the Marsh House, a restored 19th-century historic house open for tours.

List of Places to Visit in Richfield

If you’re planning a trip to Richfield, Ohio, don’t miss these must-see destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marsh House: Explore the 19th-century historic house, with guided tours given by knowledgeable docents.

  • Richfield Heritage Preserve: A 412-acre nature preserve that offers a glimpse into the town’s past as an important stagecoach stop on the Cleveland to Akron road.

Natural Attractions of Richfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cuyahoga Valley National Park: Hike or bike through the park’s stunning trails, some of which pass through steep-walled gorges, scenic overlooks, and waterfalls.

  • Furnace Run Metro Park: A popular spot for picnicking, hiking, and fishing, with several trails and streams in a scenic woodland setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Richfield

  • The Ohio & Erie Canal Towpath Trail: A 110-mile trail that stretches from Cleveland to New Philadelphia, with several trailheads in Richfield

  • Buckeye Trail: Explore the iconic yellow-blazed trail, which circles the entire state of Ohio, offering stunning views of the countryside and Richfield’s rolling hills.

Best Things to Do in Richfield: Activities and Tours

  • Driving tours: Take a scenic drive through Richfield’s rolling hills and countryside and stop along the way to indulge in local cuisine, attractions, and quaint shops.

  • Horseback riding: Explore the town’s natural beauty on horseback. The Hinckley Equestrian Center offers riding lessons, horse rentals and guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Richfield

Day 1: Explore Marsh House and Richfield Heritage Preserve

Day 2: Hike the Trails in Cuyahoga Valley National Park

Day 3: Horseback Riding at Hinckley Equestrian Center

Day 4: Visit the Hinckley Reservation and enjoy a picnic in Forest Meadows

Day 5: Drive along Route 303 and Route 21 through beautiful countryside

Day 6: Attend an event at the Richfield Coliseum

Day 7: Go fishing at Furnace Run Metro Park

Food and Drink Scene of Richfield

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Potatoes: A dish of potatoes, bacon, and onions smothered in Ohio’s signature cheese sauce.

  • Lake Erie Perch Sandwich: Freshly caught from nearby Lake Erie, this sandwich is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • Ohio Apple Cider: A delicious treat made with locally grown apples.

  • Ohio Beer: Enjoy a locally brewed beer from some of the town’s microbreweries, including Great Lakes Brewing Company and Hoppin’ Frog Brewery.

Where to Stay in Richfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Richfield: A popular hotel which offers spacious rooms, fitness center, and free Wi-Fi.

  • Super 8 Richfield Area: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms and amenities such as a pool and fitness center.

What to Buy in Richfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Ohio is known for its sweet maple syrup, and Richfield offers many local shops that sell the syrup on-site.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Check out local crafts markets and shops around town for intricate, handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Richfield

  • Richfield is known for its agricultural and rural roots, with many farms and historic barns dotting the town’s countryside.

  • The town hosts several annual events, including the Cleveland National Air Show, the Medina County Fair, and the Ohio State Fair.

Best Time to Visit Richfield

The best time to visit Richfield is in the fall as the foliage changes colors and landscapes become stunningly panoramic. Ohio summers bring high humidity and heavy traffic, but late September and October provide cooler temperatures and captivating views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Richfield

Do:

  • Follow the trail safety guidelines when hiking and biking through Cuyahoga Valley National Park and other outdoor recreation areas.

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the signature Buckeye Potatoes and Ohio apple cider.

Don’t:

  • Litter in any of the town’s natural areas, especially in Cuyahoga Valley National Park and the Furnace Run Metro Park.

  • Ignore the cultural and historical significance of Marsh House and Richfield Heritage Preserve.

Safety and Health

Safety in Richfield

Richfield is generally a safe town, but visitors should always take precautions while hiking and biking, especially in Cuyahoga Valley National Park.

Health in Richfield

Richfield offers several health clinics, including the Cleveland Clinic, the Summa Health Richfield Medical Center, and University Hospitals.

Getting to Richfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Richfield

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport (CLE): 23 miles

  • Akron-Canton Regional Airport (CAK): 25 miles

Bus Stations Near Richfield

  • Akron-Canton Airport Bus Stop: 24 miles

  • Cleveland Greyhound Bus Station: 23 miles

Other Options

  • Car rental agencies: including Hertz, Enterprise, and Alamo.

  • Taxi and rideshare services: including Uber and Lyft.

How to Get Around in Richfield

Getting around Richfield is easiest by car. Visitors can rent cars or use rideshare services such as Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Richfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cleveland: Located only 20 miles from Richfield, Cleveland offers a range of activities including shopping, museums, and one-of-a-kind dining.

  • Akron: A quick 13-mile drive from Richfield, Akron offers visitors cultural attractions and activities like the Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company and the Akron Art Museum.

Conclusion

Richfield, Ohio, offers a breathtaking blend of outdoor recreation, history, and culture. Visitors to the town can explore its stunning natural areas, including Cuyahoga Valley National Park, indulge in its delicious cuisine, and enjoy cultural attractions like the Marsh House. Safe and accessible, Richfield is a must-visit destination in Ohio.
