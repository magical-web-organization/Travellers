Introduction to Kidron

Located in the charming Wooster area of northeast Ohio, Kidron is a small town known for its rich cultural history and beautiful natural attractions. With plenty of things to see and do for visitors of all ages, Kidron is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Kidron

Kidron offers a variety of things to see and do, from exploring its rich cultural heritage to taking in the stunning scenery. Here are some of the top attractions and activities in Kidron:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Kidron Heritage Center: Learn about Kidron’s fascinating history at this museum, which showcases artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that highlight the town’s unique heritage.
  • The Lehman’s Hardware Store: Visit this historic hardware store, which has been in operation since 1955 and still uses old-fashioned methods to create high-quality products.
  • The Amish and Mennonite Heritage Center: Explore the rich cultural heritage of the area’s Amish and Mennonite communities through exhibits, guided tours, and multimedia presentations.

Natural Attractions of Kidron: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Wilderness Center: Hike or bike through the beautiful Nature Preserve, which includes over 200 acres of forests, wetlands, and grasslands.
  • The Quail Hollow State Park: Explore the park’s scenic trails, which wind through forests, meadows, and wetlands.
  • The Smithville Western Railway: Hop aboard this scenic railway and take in the stunning views of the countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kidron

Kidron is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of scenic trails to explore. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Kidron:

  • The Towpath Trail: Follow this historic trail along the scenic Ohio and Erie Canal, passing through forests, wetlands, and historic landmarks.
  • The Buckeye Trail: Hike or bike through Kidron’s beautiful countryside on this scenic trail, which winds through forests, meadows, and farmland.

Best Things to Do in Kidron: Activities and Tours

In addition to its cultural and natural attractions, Kidron offers a variety of fun things to do for visitors of all ages. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Take a horse-drawn carriage ride through Kidron’s picturesque countryside.
  • Sample delicious local cuisine at one of Kidron’s many farm-to-table restaurants.
  • Visit the Kidron Auction House and bid on unique, handmade items at one of the area’s famous auctions.

Food and Drink Scene of Kidron

No trip to Kidron would be complete without sampling the area’s delicious local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Kidron:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Swiss Steak: A local favorite, this hearty dish features tenderized beef cooked in a savory tomato sauce.
  • Fried Chicken: Crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, Kidron’s fried chicken is a crowd-pleaser.
  • Shoofly Pie: A classic Amish dessert made with molasses and sweet crumbs, this pie is the perfect way to end a meal.

Local Beverages

  • Birch Beer: This sweet, carbonated beverage is a Kidron favorite, and can be found at most local restaurants and diners.
  • Apple Cider: Made from freshly-picked Ohio apples, this refreshing drink is perfect for fall.

Where to Stay in Kidron: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kidron offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Kidron:

  • The Wallhouse Hotel: This luxury hotel offers spacious suites with high-end amenities like spa tubs, fireplaces, and flat-screen TVs.
  • The Lamp Post Lodge: This charming bed and breakfast offers comfortable guest rooms and a delicious homemade breakfast each morning.
  • The Kidron House: This restored 19th-century home offers cozy guest rooms and easy access to Kidron’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Kidron: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade crafts to delicious local foods, Kidron offers plenty of unique souvenirs and products to bring home. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Handmade quilts and crafts from local artisans.
  • Homemade jams and jellies made with fresh Ohio fruits.
  • Wooden toys and puzzles made at the Lehman’s Hardware Store.

Local Culture and Customs of Kidron

Kidron is home to a thriving Amish and Mennonite community, which contributes to the town’s unique culture and customs. Visitors should be aware of the customs and traditions of these communities, including a focus on sustainability and simple living.

Best Time to Visit Kidron

The best time to visit Kidron is during the fall, when the area’s beautiful foliage is in full display. Visitors can also enjoy the town’s many fall festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kidron

When visiting Kidron, it’s important to respect the customs and traditions of the local communities. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Take a horse-drawn carriage ride through the countryside.
  • Try some of the delicious local cuisine, including Swiss steak and shoofly pie.
  • Be respectful of the area’s Amish and Mennonite communities.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of Amish or Mennonite individuals without their permission.
  • Wear immodest clothing or use loud language in public places.
  • Interrupt a horse-drawn carriage or buggy while in motion.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kidron

Kidron is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming community. Visitors should exercise common sense and be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or biking in the area’s natural attractions.

Health in Kidron

Travelers to Kidron should be aware of the potential for tick-borne diseases, especially during the summer months. It’s important to take precautions, such as wearing protective clothing and using insect repellent, when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Kidron: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kidron can be easily accessed by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Kidron

  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport - 60 miles away
  • Akron-Canton Regional Airport - 25 miles away

Bus Stations Near Kidron

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Wooster) - 10 miles away

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a convenient way to get around the area and explore the surrounding countryside.
  • Taxi or Ride-sharing Service: Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in the area, although they may be less accessible in rural areas.

How to Get Around in Kidron

Kidron is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. However, renting a car can be a convenient option for exploring the area’s natural attractions and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Kidron: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Ohio’s beautiful countryside, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Cuyahoga Valley National Park: Just a short drive from Kidron, this stunning national park offers hiking, biking, and scenic train rides.
  • The Mohican State Park: Explore the park’s scenic trails and campgrounds, or take a canoe trip down the Mohican River.
  • The Amish Country Byway: This scenic drive winds through some of the most beautiful countryside in Ohio, passing through charming towns and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Kidron offers something for everyone. With its scenic countryside, friendly community, and rich heritage, Kidron is the perfect destination for a relaxing vacation or a fun-filled weekend getaway.
