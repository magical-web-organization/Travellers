Introduction to Marshallville

Marshallville is a quaint village located in Wayne County, Ohio near the city of Wooster. This picturesque town is rich in history, filled with cultural and outdoor activities, and is a perfect escape for solo tourists and families alike. From exploring the historic downtown area to canoeing down the scenic Ohio & Erie Canalway, Marshallville has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive list of things to do, sights to see, and where to stay and eat in Marshallville.

List of Places to Visit in Marshallville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baughman Township Historical Society Museum: Experience the history and culture of Marshallville and surrounding areas through exhibits showcasing local industries, schools, and historic sites.
  • The Canal Lands Park Canalway Center: Learn about the engineering marvels of the Ohio & Erie Canalway and the ecosystems that thrive along its banks.
  • Marshallville Packing Company: Tour the facility where meat is still hand processed in the same way it was over a century ago.
  • Lerch’s Donuts: Established in 1936, this classic donut shop serves up delicious treats and a glimpse into the past.

Natural Attractions of Marshallville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ohio & Erie Canalway Towpath Trail: The 85-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and running. Start at Lock 4 and take in the beautiful scenery along the way.
  • Sterling Farm Bridle Trail: The 4.6-mile trail is open to hikers, bikers, and horseback riding. The trail is marked with breathtaking wildflowers and beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Orrville Railroad Heritage Society Museum: Displays the history of the railroad industry and features full-size locomotives, cars, and artifacts.
  • Sippo Valley Trail: Whether you prefer hiking, biking, or horseback riding, this 10.5-mile rail trail offers stunning scenery and is perfect for an outdoor adventure.
  • Wayne County Rails to Trails: Take in the beautiful views and fresh air on this paved biking and hiking trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marshallville

  • Ohio & Erie Canalway Towpath Trail
  • Sterling Farm Bridle Trail
  • Sippo Valley Trail
  • Wayne County Rails to Trails

Best Things to Do in Marshallville: Activities and Tours

  • Canal Fulton Canoe Livery: Experience the beauty of the Ohio & Erie Canalway from a different perspective by renting a canoe, kayak, or paddleboat.
  • The Pine Tree Barn: Shop for unique gifts, home decor, and garden accessories while enjoying the peaceful surroundings of the Pine Tree Barn.
  • The Great Wayne County Bike Tour: This annual event allows participants to explore the beautiful countryside of Wayne County while taking in the local sights and sounds.
  • Wayne County Fair: The county fair, held in September, is a fun-filled event featuring livestock shows, amusement rides, and local food vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Marshallville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bologna: Made by Amish families in the area, this unique bologna can be found in most local restaurants and markets.
  • Apple Butter: This sweet and tangy spread is available at most farmers markets and specialty shops in the area.
  • Shish-Ka-Bobs: These skewers of seasoned vegetables and meat can be found at most local summer festivals.
  • Peach Pie: Made with locally grown juicy peaches, this pie is a delicious sweet treat.

Local Beverages

  • JAFB Wooster Brewery: This local brewery offers a variety of handcrafted beer and live music on weekends.
  • The Winery at Wolf Creek: Sample a variety of locally made wine while taking in the beautiful vineyards and winery grounds.
  • Main Street Fudge and Popcorn Co.: Indulge in gourmet popcorn and delicious fudge while enjoying a movie at The Ohio Light Opera.

Where to Stay in Marshallville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Barn Inn Bed and Breakfast: Stay in a beautifully restored 19th-century barn while enjoying a home-cooked breakfast and countryside views.
  • The Marshallville Inn: This historic inn offers comfortable rooms and a charming restaurant serving local favorites.
  • The Pine Tree Barn: Stay in the cozy guesthouse while enjoying the beautiful scenery and shopping at The Pine Tree Barn.

What to Buy in Marshallville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amish-Made Quilts: The Wayne County area is known for its high-quality, hand-stitched Amish quilts.
  • Hand-Crafted Wooden Furniture: Many local craftsmen sell their furniture at markets and shops in the area.
  • Local Artwork and Crafts: The local farmers market and specialty shops feature works by talented local artists and craftsmen.

Local Culture and Customs of Marshallville

Marshallville is a small village rich in history and rural Amish culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs, which include slowing down and enjoying the simple things in life.

Best Time to Visit Marshallville

The best time to visit Marshallville is during the summer months when many outdoor events and festivals take place. The fall season also offers beautiful foliage, and the Wayne County Fair is always a fun-filled event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marshallville

Do:

  • Respect Amish traditions and customs.
  • Take time to explore the scenic countryside.
  • Try local foods and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of local Amish people without permission.
  • Disrupt local horse-drawn buggies on the road.
  • Ignore signs and regulations on hiking and biking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marshallville

Marshallville is a safe place to visit, but as with any destination, visitors should exercise caution and common sense. Keep valuables secured, follow traffic laws, and respect local customs and traditions.

Health in Marshallville

Visitors can bring home fresh produce and other goods from local farmers markets and shops. It’s always a good idea to wash produce and other food items before consuming. Water in the area is safe to drink, but travelers may prefer bottled water if they have any concerns.

Getting to Marshallville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marshallville

  • Akron-Canton Regional Airport - 35 miles from Marshallville
  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport - 58 miles from Marshallville

Bus Stations Near Marshallville

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Wooster, OH (6 miles from Marshallville)

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Available at numerous locations in Akron, Canton, and Cleveland.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing: Available in Wooster and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Marshallville

The best way to get around Marshallville is by car, as public transportation is limited. Bike rentals are also available in some areas for those interested in exploring the trails and countryside paths.

Where to Go Next from Marshallville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cuyahoga Valley National Park: This stunning national park is just a short drive away and offers opportunities for hiking, biking, and sightseeing.
  • Berlin, OH: This charming Amish town is only an hour’s drive from Marshallville and is filled with local culture, shopping, and dining.
  • Cleveland, OH: Just an hour’s drive from Marshallville, Cleveland offers a variety of entertainment options, including world-class museums, theaters, and sports stadiums.

Conclusion

Marshallville is a charming village with a rich history and local traditions. From exploring the beauty of the Ohio & Erie Canalway to sampling local foods and beverages, there’s something for everyone in this quaint town. Plan your next trip to Marshallville and see for yourself what this hidden gem has to offer.
