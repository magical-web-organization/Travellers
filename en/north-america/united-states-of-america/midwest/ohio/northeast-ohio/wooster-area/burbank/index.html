Introduction to Burbank

Burbank is a quaint village located in Wooster area of Northeast Ohio. Nestled in the heart of Amish country, Burbank offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern amenities. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Burbank has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Burbank

Here are some of the top places to visit in Burbank:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center (OARDC): Founded in 1882, OARDC is one of the largest and most diverse agricultural research centers in the world. The center’s Wooster campus offers a range of exhibits, including a butterfly house, botanical gardens, and an interactive science museum.

  • Lehman’s Hardware Store: Founded in 1955, Lehman’s Hardware Store is a living museum of sorts, featuring thousands of ancient and modern gadgets, tools, and implements used in the home and on the farm.

  • Smucker’s Store and Cafe: The famous Smucker’s Company got its start in Ohio back in 1897. Today, visitors can tour the Smucker’s Store and Café in nearby Orrville to learn about the history of the company and sample some of their delicious jam and jelly products.

Natural Attractions of Burbank: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ohio Erie Canalway Towpath Trail: The Ohio Erie Canalway Towpath Trail is a 110-mile trail that runs through the heart of Ohio. The trail passes through Burbank and offers visitors a chance to hike, bike, or horseback ride through scenic forests and past beautiful waterways.

  • Mohican State Park: Located nearby, Mohican State Park features over 1,100 acres of hiking trails, equestrian trails, fishing lakes, and picnic areas. Visitors can also rent cabins or campgrounds in the park.

  • Killbuck Marsh Wildlife Area: The Killbuck Marsh Wildlife Area is a 5,600-acre wetland preserve that is home to over 200 species of birds. Visitors can go birdwatching, kayaking, or fishing on the marsh.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burbank

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, these are some of the best routes to explore in Burbank:

  • Ohio and Erie Canalway National Heritage Route: This scenic route stretches over 110 miles and features a towpath trail that is perfect for both biking and hiking.

  • Buckeye Trail: The Buckeye Trail is a 1,444-mile trail that circles the entire state of Ohio. The route passes through Burbank and offers hikers a chance to explore the local woodlands and wetlands.

  • Mohican Valley Trail: This 4.5-mile trail follows an old rail bed and offers a scenic hiking and biking route that passes through forests, wetlands, and farmlands.

Best Things to Do in Burbank: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to do in Burbank:

  • Ohio Amish Country Tour: Join a guided tour to explore Ohio’s Amish country and learn about the history, culture, and traditions of the Amish community.

  • Horse Drawn Wagon Rides: Take a horse-drawn wagon ride through the scenic countryside and enjoy the natural beauty of the landscape.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: The nearby vineyards offer a variety of wine tasting tours where visitors can sample some of the finest vintages and learn about the winemaking process.

Food and Drink Scene of Burbank

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Burbank:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amish-style fried chicken: A classic dish served in many Amish restaurants in the area.

  • Peanut butter spread: A delicious spread made from peanut butter and honey that is a staple of Amish cuisine.

  • Homemade pie: Made with fresh seasonal fruit, these pies are a signature dish of Amish country.

Local Beverages

  • Apple cider: A refreshing and delicious drink made with fresh apples and spices that is popular in autumn.

  • Wine: The nearby wineries offer a variety of wines from dry whites to sweet reds.

  • Craft beer: Several breweries in the area offer a variety of craft beers with unique flavor profiles.

Where to Stay in Burbank: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Burbank, including:

  • The Wooster Inn: A charming hotel located in nearby Wooster that offers beautiful rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Amish Country Lodging: A collection of cozy cabins and cottages nestled in the heart of Amish country.

  • The Barn Inn Bed and Breakfast: A converted 19th-century barn that now offers guests comfortable and stylish rooms, a hot tub, and a full gourmet breakfast.

What to Buy in Burbank: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Burbank:

  • Handmade crafts: The local Amish community is known for their beautiful handcrafted furniture, quilts, baskets, and more.

  • Maple syrup: The nearby sugar maple trees produce some of the best maple syrup in the world, and many local shops sell this delicious product.

  • Local cheeses and meats: The area is known for its high-quality cheese and meats, which are produced using traditional methods.

Local Culture and Customs of Burbank

Burbank’s proximity to the Amish community means that visitors can experience a unique and traditional way of life. Some customs and traditions to bear in mind include:

  • Respectful dress: The Amish community dress modestly, so visitors should dress conservatively as well.

  • No photography: The Amish community believes that photographs violate the Second Commandment, so visitors should refrain from taking photos of them.

  • No alcohol: The Amish community does not consume alcohol, and neither should visitors when in their presence.

Best Time to Visit Burbank

The best time to visit Burbank is in the summer months, especially between June and August when the weather is warm and dry, and the outdoor activities are plentiful. Autumn is also a great time to visit when the leaves are changing, and the local harvest festivals are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burbank

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Burbank:

Do:

  • Do try the local food: Burbank’s food scene is rich and varied, so be sure to sample some of the local delicacies.

  • Do be respectful of the Amish community: Visitors should be respectful of the Amish community’s way of life and customs when in their presence.

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities: Hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of the area are some of the best things to do in Burbank.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of the Amish community: Visitors should respect the Amish community’s wishes and refrain from taking photos of them.

  • Don’t drink alcohol in the presence of the Amish community: The Amish community does not consume alcohol, so visitors should not drink in their presence.

  • Don’t dress inappropriately: Visitors should dress conservatively and respectfully when visiting Amish country.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burbank

Burbank is a relatively safe area, but visitors should always take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Practice situational awareness, and be mindful of your surroundings when out and about.

  • Lock your car and your valuables: Always lock your car and keep your valuables out of sight.

  • Follow traffic rules: Always wear a helmet and follow traffic rules when riding a bike on the road.

Health in Burbank

Burbank is a relatively healthy area, but visitors should always take precautions to ensure their health. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially if you are spending time outdoors.

  • Use sunscreen: Protect your skin from the sun by using sunscreen and wearing a hat and sunglasses.

  • Pack insect repellent: If you are hiking or spending time outdoors, pack insect repellent to protect yourself from ticks and other biting insects.

Getting to Burbank: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burbank

The nearest major airport to Burbank is the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Burbank

The Greyhound bus station in Wooster is only a few miles away from Burbank.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Burbank, as there is ample parking available in the village. Car rental services are also available at the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport.

How to Get Around in Burbank

Visitors can explore the village by car, bike, or foot. Some rental car companies operate in the area, and there are several bike rental shops nearby as well.

Where to Go Next from Burbank: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have explored Burbank and still have time, you may consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Cleveland: Visit the famous Rock and Roll Hall of Fame or take a stroll through the beautiful Cleveland Metroparks.

  • Akron: Known for its art museums, botanical gardens, and craft breweries, Akron is a great destination for a day trip.

  • Columbus: Ohio’s capital city, Columbus, is a vibrant and exciting destination with plenty of museums, parks, and restaurants to explore.

Conclusion

Burbank, Ohio, is a hidden gem in the heart of Amish country, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern amenities. From the scenic hiking trails to the delicious Amish cuisine, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Burbank. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your next trip to this charming village.
