Introduction to Montgomery

Montgomery, a city in Hamilton County, is a must-visit destination for history buffs and nature lovers alike. Located in Greater Cincinnati, in Ohio, in the Midwest, in the United States of America, in North America, Montgomery is a city rich in history and natural splendor. The city is home to some of the best museums, historical landmarks, and scenic vistas in the area. This comprehensive tour guide for Montgomery will help you plan a memorable trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Montgomery

Montgomery offers a wealth of attractions and activities that cater to a variety of interests. We have divided our list of places to visit into two categories: culturally important attractions and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Heritage Village Museum: Take a step back in time and explore the rich history of Montgomery and the surrounding region. This museum features 13 historic buildings and exhibits that showcase life in the 19th century.
  • The Montgomery Community Players: Catch a live play or musical at the Montgomery Community Players. This theater group features talented local actors and produces a range of shows throughout the year.
  • The Pioneer Cemetery: Located just outside of Montgomery, the Pioneer Cemetery is a fascinating historical site that dates back to the early 1800s. This cemetery is home to some of the earliest settlers in the area and offers a unique glimpse into the region’s past.

Natural Attractions of Montgomery: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Dulle Park: This quaint park is home to a butterfly garden, playground, and plenty of space for a picnic with your loved ones. Take a stroll around the lake and admire the beautiful scenery.
  • The Indian Hill Winter Club: This club offers ice skating, curling, and hockey for the sports enthusiasts. Rent equipment, take lessons, or simply enjoy watching the games!
  • Swaim Park: A vast park with sports fields, a playground, walking paths, and picnic areas. The park also lends a perfect setting for a family day out or a romantic walk with scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montgomery

Montgomery boasts of a rich network of hiking and biking trails running throughout the city. These paths offer a great way to explore the city’s natural beauty on foot or two wheels. Here are some of the city’s best routes:

  • The Little Miami Scenic Trail: A 50-mile trail that runs through the city and offers stunning views of the Little Miami River. The trail is perfect for walking, biking, and jogging.
  • Sharon Woods Park Trail: This trail runs through the Sharon Woods Park and offers a peaceful, scenic route for joggers and walkers.

Best Things to Do in Montgomery: Activities and Tours

Montgomery offers a range of activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, there’s something for everyone in Montgomery. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Ride the Cincinnati Dinner Train: Take a scenic train ride through the city and enjoy a delicious dinner along the way.
  • Go on a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in views of Montgomery and the surrounding areas from high up in the sky on a hot air balloon ride.
  • The Cincinnati Zoo & Botanical Garden: This delightful spot is home to more than 500 animal species and can be a great way to spend time with family.

Food and Drink Scene of Montgomery

Montgomery’s food and drink scene are a reflection of its diverse cultural heritage. Here’s a rundown of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goetta: A pork-and-oatmeal sausage that is a regional specialty in Cincinnati.
  • Montgomery Inn’s Ribs: The signature dish of Montgomery Inn is a dish consisting of tender, fall-off-the-bone pork ribs smothered in a secret sauce.
  • Graeter’s Ice Cream: A local favorite, Graeter’s Ice Cream is handcrafted in small batches using fresh ingredients and features unique flavor combinations.

Local Beverages

  • MadTree Brewing Company: This local brewery produces some of the city’s best craft beers and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Rhinegeist Brewery: Located in Cincinnati, Rhinegeist Brewery offers a range of refreshing beers and an excellent atmosphere to enjoy them in.
  • Sonder Brewing: A relatively new brewery that makes excellent beer, Sonder Brewing is a must-visit for anyone interested in trying the latest offerings.

Where to Stay in Montgomery: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montgomery offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Inn at Montgomery: A charming bed and breakfast with comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • The Cincinnati Marriott North: This modern hotel offers all the amenities expected from a Marriott hotel.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Cincinnati North: This suite-style hotel is a great option for families or group travelers as it offers spacious rooms and kitchenette facilities.

What to Buy in Montgomery: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montgomery has a range of shops and stores that sell souvenirs and local products. Here are some suggestions:

  • The Artisans’ Exchange: A shop that features a variety of handcrafted items, such as pottery, jewelry, and home decor.
  • The Cincinnati Museum Center Gift Shop: The gift shop at the Cincinnati Museum Center features a range of souvenirs and educational items that make great gifts.
  • The Montgomery Farmers Market: The Montgomery Farmers Market features locally produced goods such as fresh produce, honey, and baked goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Montgomery

Montgomery has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the year with various events and festivals. The city also has several cultural venues that promote art, music, and theater.

Best Time to Visit Montgomery

The best time to visit Montgomery is during the spring and fall when temperatures are comfortable, and the city’s parks and outdoor spaces are in full bloom. Summer is a popular season for tourists, but temperatures can get very hot. Winter can be chilly, but the city still offers plenty of indoor attractions and activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montgomery

Do:

  • Do visit the Heritage Village Museum to learn about the region’s rich history.
  • Do try the local cuisine and craft beers.
  • Do explore the city’s natural attractions and outdoor spaces.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave garbage in public spaces.
  • Don’t smoke in public spaces or near children.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and social norms.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montgomery

Montgomery is a relatively safe city, but it’s always best to take necessary precautions while traveling. Avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas and stick to well-lit and crowded areas. Be aware of your surroundings, and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Montgomery

Montgomery has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality healthcare services. It’s always best to have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

How to Get Around in Montgomery

Montgomery is a small city and is best explored on foot or by bike. Several bike rental companies operate in the city, and there are also several walking tours that offer a great way to explore the city’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Montgomery: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montgomery is well-connected to several nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some suggestions:

  • Cincinnati: Located just a few miles away, Cincinnati is a vibrant city with an excellent food and drink scene, cultural attractions, and great shopping.
  • Loveland: This quaint town is located just a short drive from Montgomery and is home to several historic landmarks and picturesque parks.
  • Dayton: Dayton is an hour’s drive from Montgomery and is a great destination for history enthusiasts, boasting the Wright Brothers National Museum and the National Museum of the United States Air Force.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality, Montgomery is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a memorable trip. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip, and immerse yourself in all that Montgomery has to offer.
