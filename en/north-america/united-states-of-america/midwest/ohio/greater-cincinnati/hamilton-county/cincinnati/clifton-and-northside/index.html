Introduction to Clifton and Northside

Clifton and Northside are two charming neighborhoods located in the city of Cincinnati, Ohio. Clifton, with its sleek Victorian architecture and beautiful parks, is a peaceful residential area with a small-town vibe. Northside, on the other hand, is a vibrant and hip neighborhood known for its thriving art and music scene.

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or art lover, there is something for everyone in Clifton and Northside. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a close look at the best things to do, where to stay, and what to know before you go.

List of Places to Visit in Clifton and Northside

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cincinnati Art Museum

Located in Clifton’s Eden Park, the Cincinnati Art Museum is home to an impressive collection of artworks from around the world. The museum has over 67,000 pieces, including paintings, sculptures, and artifacts spanning over 6,000 years of human history. Admission is free, but you can make a donation to support the museum’s programs and exhibits.

National Underground Railroad Freedom Center

The National Underground Railroad Freedom Center is a must-see destination if you’re interested in African American history and the abolitionist movement. The museum tells the story of the Underground Railroad, a network of people and safe houses that helped slaves escape to freedom in the 19th century. Exhibits explore the history of slavery, the Civil War, and contemporary human rights issues.

Natural Attractions of Clifton and Northside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Burnet Woods Park

Located in the heart of Clifton, Burnet Woods Park is a wooded oasis in the middle of the city. The park has over 90 acres of green space, walking trails, and a picturesque lake perfect for picnics and relaxation. You can also rent a paddleboat or go fishing in the lake.

Spring Grove Cemetery

Spring Grove Cemetery is a beautifully landscaped cemetery located in Northside that offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of city life. The cemetery spans over 700 acres and features stunning mausoleums, monuments, and statues. You can take a self-guided walking tour or opt for a guided tour to learn about the famous people buried there.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clifton and Northside

Cincinnati Nature Center

Located just a short drive from Clifton and Northside, the Cincinnati Nature Center boasts over 1,000 acres of natural beauty. The center has over 16 miles of hiking trails that wind through forests, meadows, and wetlands. If you’re a bird-watcher, be sure to bring your binoculars – the center is home to over 220 species of birds.

Best Things to Do in Clifton and Northside: Activities and Tours

Brewery Tours

Northside is home to several craft breweries, including Urban Artifact, which specializes in sour beers, and Higher Gravity, which has an impressive selection of local, national, and international craft brews. Many of the breweries offer guided tours, tastings, and events throughout the year.

Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden

The Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden is one of the oldest and most renowned zoos in the country. It is home to over 500 animal species, including lions, tigers, giraffes, and penguins. The zoo also has a wide variety of botanical gardens, including a butterfly garden and a rainforest exhibit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clifton and Northside

Day 1: Visit the Cincinnati Art Museum and take a stroll through Eden Park

Day 2: Explore the shops, galleries, and restaurants in Northside

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the National Underground Railroad Freedom Center

Day 4: Spend a day hiking and bird-watching at the Cincinnati Nature Center

Day 5: Go on a brewery tour in Northside

Day 6: Visit the Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden

Day 7: Take a self-guided walking tour of Spring Grove Cemetery

Food and Drink Scene of Clifton and Northside

Must-Try Dishes

Goetta

Goetta is a traditional Cincinnati dish made from ground meat (usually pork and beef), oats, and spices. It’s often served for breakfast with eggs and toast or as part of a sandwich for lunch.

Cincinnati-style Chili

Cincinnati-style chili is a unique spin on the classic American dish. It’s typically made with ground beef, tomatoes, and a blend of spices, including cinnamon and cocoa powder. The chili is served over spaghetti and topped with cheese and onions.

Local Beverages

Cincinnati Beer

Cincinnati has a thriving craft beer scene, with breweries like Rhinegeist, MadTree, and Taft’s Ale House offering a wide variety of local brews.

Graeter’s Ice Cream

Graeter’s is a Cincinnati institution known for its rich, creamy ice cream. The company has been making ice cream since 1870 and uses a traditional French pot method to create its signature texture.

Where to Stay in Clifton and Northside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gaslight Bed and Breakfast

The Gaslight Bed and Breakfast is a charming Victorian-style home located in Clifton. The inn offers three guest rooms, each with its own private bathroom and unique decor. Amenities include a full breakfast, free Wi-Fi, and a garden patio.

Airbnb

There are several Airbnb options available in Clifton and Northside, ranging from cozy apartments to spacious houses. Staying in an Airbnb is a great way to experience the local culture and get tips from your host on the best things to see and do in the area.

What to Buy in Clifton and Northside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rookwood Pottery

Rookwood Pottery is a Cincinnati-based company that has been making handcrafted ceramics since 1880. Their unique designs and high-quality craftsmanship make them a popular choice for collectibles and souvenirs.

Craft Beer

Cincinnati’s craft beer scene is thriving, and there are several local breweries that offer bottled and canned beer for purchase. Take home a few bottles of your favorites to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Clifton and Northside

Clifton and Northside are known for their laid-back, friendly vibe and tight-knit communities. Residents are proud of their neighborhoods and often participate in local events and festivals. Be sure to say hello to your neighbors and take part in the local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Clifton and Northside

The best time to visit Clifton and Northside is from April to October when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events. The fall months are particularly beautiful, with colorful foliage and cool temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clifton and Northside

Do:

  • Do spend time exploring the local shops, galleries, and restaurants

  • Do try the local cuisine, including goetta and Cincinnati-style chili

  • Do visit the Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural areas in parks and trails

  • Don’t be disrespectful to local residents or property

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for walking and hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clifton and Northside

Clifton and Northside are generally considered safe neighborhoods, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions, such as locking your car and being aware of your surroundings at night. If you’re hiking or exploring natural areas, be sure to check the weather forecast and bring appropriate gear and supplies.

Health in Clifton and Northside

Clifton and Northside have several hospitals and medical clinics, including UC Health and The Christ Hospital. Pharmacies, grocery stores, and convenience stores are also readily available.

Getting to Clifton and Northside: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clifton and Northside

The closest airport to Clifton and Northside is Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport, located about 30 minutes away by car. The airport services several domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Clifton and Northside

The Cincinnati Greyhound Station is located in downtown Cincinnati and offers bus services to several cities in Ohio and neighboring states. The station is about a 15-minute drive from Clifton and Northside.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Clifton and Northside are easily accessible from several major highways, including I-71 and I-75. There are also several public transportation options, including buses and the Cincinnati Bell Connector streetcar.

How to Get Around in Clifton and Northside

Clifton and Northside are both very walkable neighborhoods, with lots of shops, restaurants, and attractions within easy walking distance. If you’re planning to explore further afield, renting a bike or taking public transportation can be a good option.

Where to Go Next from Clifton and Northside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dayton, Ohio

Dayton, Ohio, is located about an hour’s drive from Cincinnati and offers several interesting attractions, including the National Museum of the United States Air Force and the Dayton Art Institute.

Louisville, Kentucky

Louisville, Kentucky, is located about two hours’ drive from Cincinnati and is home to several historic landmarks, including Churchill Downs (home of the Kentucky Derby) and the Louisville Slugger Museum and Factory.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Clifton and Northside have something to offer. From museums and historical sites to parks and outdoor activities, there’s never a dull moment in these charming Cincinnati neighborhoods.
