Introduction to Over-the-Rhine

Over-the-Rhine is a neighborhood in Cincinnati, located in Hamilton County, Greater Cincinnati, Ohio. This area was originally settled by German immigrants in the mid-19th century, and it is known for its historic architecture and cultural heritage. The neighborhood has undergone significant revitalization efforts in recent years, and it is now a vibrant, trendy destination with a thriving arts scene, unique eateries, and a bustling nightlife. This guide will take you through some of the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and where to stay in Over-the-Rhine.

List of Places to Visit in Over-the-Rhine

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cincinnati Museum Center at Union Terminal

Cincinnati Museum Center at Union Terminal is a National Historic Landmark that opened in 1933 as a train station. Today, it houses three museums, an OMNIMAX Theater, and a library. You can explore the Cincinnati History Museum, the Duke Energy Children’s Museum, and the Museum of Natural History and Science.

Harriet Beecher Stowe House

Harriet Beecher Stowe, author of the acclaimed novel “Uncle Tom’s Cabin,” lived in this house while her husband was a professor at Lane Theological Seminary. The house has been restored and is now open to the public as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Over-the-Rhine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Washington Park

Washington Park is a 10-acre urban park in the heart of Over-the-Rhine. The park features a playground, a dog park, a sprayground, and a large outdoor stage for concerts and events.

Ziegler Park

Ziegler Park is another popular park in the neighborhood, known for its swimming pool, basketball courts, and fitness center. The park also hosts movies in the park during the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Over-the-Rhine

If you want to explore the outdoors in Over-the-Rhine, you can take the Ohio River Trail, which is a 23-mile trail that runs along the Ohio River. You can also take the Little Miami Scenic Trail, which is a 50-mile paved trail that runs from Newtown to Springfield.

Best Things to Do in Over-the-Rhine: Activities and Tours

Findlay Market

Findlay Market is a public market that has been in operation since 1855. You can find local produce, meats, cheeses, and baked goods while enjoying live music and street performers.

Music Hall

Music Hall is a historic theater that has been renovated to include a state-of-the-art performance facility. You can enjoy a wide range of musical performances, theater productions, and more.

Taft Museum of Art

Taft Museum of Art is a small art museum that features works of art from around the world, including a collection of European and American paintings and decorative art objects.

Day 1: Visit Cincinnati Museum Center at Union Terminal and Harriet Beecher Stowe House Day 2: Explore Washington Park and Ziegler Park Day 3: Take the Ohio River Trail Day 4: Visit Findlay Market and Music Hall Day 5: Take a guided tour of Taft Museum of Art Day 6: Explore the Little Miami Scenic Trail Day 7: Relax and enjoy Over-the-Rhine’s food and drink scene

Food and Drink Scene of Over-the-Rhine

Must-Try Dishes

Goetta

Goetta is a regional dish made with ground meat, oats, and spices. It is usually served as a breakfast dish.

Cincinnati Chili

Cincinnati Chili is a sweeter, spicier version of chili that is typically served over spaghetti or hot dogs.

Local Beverages

Rhinegeist Brewery

Rhinegeist Brewery is a local favorite, known for its craft beer and indoor and outdoor spaces perfect for socializing.

16-Bit Bar + Arcade

16-Bit Bar + Arcade is a retro-style arcade bar featuring classic video games, craft cocktails, and an extensive beer list.

Where to Stay in Over-the-Rhine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

21c Museum Hotel Cincinnati

21c Museum Hotel Cincinnati is a boutique hotel that features contemporary art and a rooftop bar.

Symphony Hotel

Symphony Hotel is a historic bed and breakfast that is known for its fine dining restaurant and live music events.

What to Buy in Over-the-Rhine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Findlay Market

Findlay Market is a great place to find local souvenirs and products, including artisanal foods, handmade crafts, and vintage finds.

Local Culture and Customs of Over-the-Rhine

Over-the-Rhine has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through various festivals and events throughout the year, including Bockfest, Oktoberfest, and the Cincinnati Winter Blues Fest.

Best Time to Visit Over-the-Rhine

The best time to visit Over-the-Rhine is during the summer, when there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy, such as the Cincinnati Reds baseball games and the Cincinnati Music Festival. The fall is also a beautiful time to visit, as the leaves change color and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Over-the-Rhine

Do:

  • Explore the neighborhood by foot to truly appreciate its architecture and history.
  • Take a guided tour of one of the museums or historical sites in the area.
  • Try the local food and drink specialties.
  • Attend one of the neighborhood’s many festivals or events.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car or unattended in public places.
  • Ignore safety warnings in outdoor areas.
  • Venture into unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Over-the-Rhine

Over-the-Rhine has improved in terms of safety in recent years, but visitors should still exercise caution when exploring the area. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid carrying large sums of cash or expensive belongings.

Health in Over-the-Rhine

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in Cincinnati that visitors can access if needed, including the University of Cincinnati Medical Center and Christ Hospital.

Getting to Over-the-Rhine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Over-the-Rhine

The nearest major airport to Over-the-Rhine is Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport (CVG), which is approximately 13 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Over-the-Rhine

The nearest bus station is the Cincinnati Greyhound station, which is located downtown.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the Metro bus system, which operates throughout Cincinnati and provides easy access to Over-the-Rhine. Taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Over-the-Rhine

The best way to get around Over-the-Rhine is on foot or by bike. The neighborhood is easily walkable and there are several bike rental options available. Visitors can also use the Metro bus system to access other parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Over-the-Rhine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newport, Kentucky

Newport, Kentucky is a charming riverfront town that is home to several historic attractions, including the Newport Aquarium and the Southgate House Revival music venue.

Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden

The Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden is one of the oldest zoos in the United States and is known for its conservation efforts and unique animal exhibits.

Conclusion

Over-the-Rhine is a vibrant and dynamic neighborhood in Cincinnati that offers something for everyone. From its rich cultural heritage to its trendy eateries and bustling nightlife, this area is a must-visit for any traveler exploring the Midwest.
