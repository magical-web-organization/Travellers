Introduction to Downtown

Downtown Cincinnati is a vibrant and historic neighborhood located in the heart of the city. With a rich history dating back to 1788, this area is filled with culture, arts, entertainment, and natural attractions that make it a popular destination for visitors and locals alike.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Underground Railroad Freedom Center: Learn about the history of the Underground Railroad and the fight for freedom and civil rights.
  • Cincinnati Art Museum: Discover a vast collection of paintings, sculptures, and decorative art from around the world.
  • Taft Museum of Art: Explore one of the oldest and most beautiful houses in Cincinnati and its exquisite art collection.
  • Cincinnati History Museum: Trace the city’s history from its early days to modern times.
  • Great American Ball Park: Catch a Cincinnati Reds game in this iconic baseball stadium.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Smale Riverfront Park: Enjoy a stunning view of the Ohio River and the nearby cities, walk the winding pathways, or take a ride on the carousel.
  • John A. Roebling Suspension Bridge: Cross the Ohio River on this historic and iconic bridge, a predecessor to the Brooklyn Bridge.
  • Eden Park: Take a hike or a bike ride on the many trails and paths of this beautiful park, situated on a hill with a panoramic view of downtown Cincinnati.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

  • Ohio River Trail: A 23-mile paved trail running alongside the Ohio River, connecting several cities and neighborhoods.
  • Little Miami Scenic Trail: A 50-mile paved trail running through several parks, forests, and charming towns.
  • Purple People Bridge: A pedestrian-only bridge connecting Newport, Kentucky, to Cincinnati, giving a great view of the riverfront and the Cincinnati skyline.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

  • Queen City Underground Tour: Take a guided tour through the city’s underground tunnels and learn about the city’s history and legends.
  • Spirit of Cincinnati Distillery Tour: Go on a guided tour of a local bourbon distillery and taste some of their award-winning products.
  • ArtWorks Public Art Tour: Take a self-guided tour of the city’s vibrant murals and public art installations.
  • Cincinnati Wine and Dine Tour: Join a foodie tour of the city’s best restaurants and bars, and discover the diversity and quality of the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goetta: A local breakfast sausage made of pork and steel-cut oats.
  • Cincinnati-style chili: A chili made with cinnamon, chocolate, and served on spaghetti or hot dogs.
  • Graeter’s ice cream: A local ice cream institution with unique flavors and high-quality ingredients.
  • Bratwurst: A grilled sausage made with pork and spices, served with sauerkraut and mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Local craft beers: Try a range of beers from breweries such as Rhinegeist, MadTree Brewing, and Taft’s Ale House.
  • Bourbon whiskey: Take a bourbon tour and taste spirits from distilleries such as New Riff, Boone County, and Old Pogue.
  • Root beer: Try the unique flavor of homemade root beer from the Root Beer Stand or the Old Milford Parlor.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • 21c Museum Hotel Cincinnati: A trendy hotel with a contemporary art museum and a rooftop bar.
  • Renaissance Cincinnati Downtown Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in a beautifully restored 1901 building.
  • Hostelling International Cincinnati: A budget-friendly hostel in the heart of Over-the-Rhine district.
  • Symphony Hotel and Restaurant: A charming boutique hotel with an award-winning restaurant and live music events.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics from Rookwood Pottery.
  • Local artisanal candles and soaps from the Candle Lab.
  • Local honey and beeswax products from Bee Haven Honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

  • Respect the cultural heritage and contributions of the African-American community to the city’s history and arts scene.
  • Support local businesses and restaurants that use and promote local ingredients and products.
  • Observe the city’s etiquette and dress codes when visiting high-end restaurants, theaters, or museums.
  • Be aware of the city’s history of segregation and racial tension and support efforts to promote diversity and inclusion.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown Cincinnati is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild, and there are many outdoor events, festivals, and markets. However, the winter months can also be fun, with holiday decorations, ice-skating rinks, and cozy indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Visit the open-air markets, such as Findlay Market and Fountain Square, for local produce, street food, and artisanal products.
  • Take a walk or bike ride on the riverfront and the Ohio River Trail for stunning views and exercise.
  • Go on a guided tour to learn about the city’s history, art, and culture.
  • Check the local events calendar for festivals, concerts, and other activities.

Don’t:

  • J-walk or cross the street without proper signals, as this is prohibited and can be dangerous.
  • Leave valuables or belongings unattended in public areas, especially at night.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions, or engage in discriminatory or offensive behavior.
  • Overlook the city’s diverse neighborhoods and communities, from Over-the-Rhine and West End to Walnut Hills and Avondale.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Cincinnati is generally safe, but visitors should take the usual precautions against pickpockets, theft, and other crime incidents. It’s recommended to stay in well-lit areas at night, avoid walking alone, and be aware of sudden changes in the surroundings.

Health in Downtown

Downtown Cincinnati has several medical facilities and pharmacies, including the Christ Hospital, Good Samaritan Hospital, and the University of Cincinnati Medical Center. Visitors should bring enough medication and a copy of their medical records and insurance, in case of emergency.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

  • Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport: Located 22 miles southwest of Downtown Cincinnati, this airport serves as a hub for several major airlines and offers transport options such as taxis, buses, and shuttle services.
  • Dayton International Airport: Located 64 miles northeast of Downtown Cincinnati, this airport serves several regional airlines and offers rental cars and shared-ride shuttles.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

  • Greyhound Station: Located at 1005 Gilbert Avenue, this station offers intercity bus services to several destinations in the region and beyond.
  • Cincinnati Metro: The city’s public transportation system operates several bus routes and a streetcar line, connecting Downtown to other neighborhoods and attractions.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station to Cincinnati is located in Connersville, Indiana, about an hour’s drive from Downtown Cincinnati.
  • Rental Cars: Several car rental companies operate in Cincinnati, including Avis, Hertz, and Enterprise.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Downtown Cincinnati is relatively small and easy to navigate on foot or by bike. The city’s public transportation system, Cincinnati Metro, also operates several bus lines and a streetcar line, connecting Downtown to other neighborhoods and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newport, Kentucky: Cross the Ohio River and discover the charm and history of this riverfront town, with its historic mansions, Newport Aquarium, and Newport on the Levee entertainment complex.
  • Over-the-Rhine: Explore Cincinnati’s trendiest and most vibrant neighborhood, known for its historic architecture, boutique shops, and diverse dining scene.
  • Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden: Visit one of the oldest zoos in the country and explore its vast collection of animals and plants.

Conclusion

Downtown Cincinnati is a wonderful destination for those seeking a mix of history, culture, outdoor adventures, and culinary delights. With its unique museums, natural landmarks, vibrant neighborhoods, and friendly locals, it’s a city that offers something for everyone. Plan your trip wisely, and enjoy your stay!
