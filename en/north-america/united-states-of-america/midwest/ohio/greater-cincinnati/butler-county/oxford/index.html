Introduction to Oxford

Located in Butler County, in the Greater Cincinnati area of Ohio, Oxford is a charming college town that is filled with history, culture, and natural beauty. Home to Miami University, Oxford is a popular destination for tourists, students, and locals alike. With its tree-lined streets, beautiful parks, and welcoming community, it’s no wonder why Oxford consistently ranks as one of the top places to live in Ohio. In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Oxford, highlighting some of the top places to visit, things to do, and local customs to experience.

List of Places to Visit in Oxford

If you’re planning a trip to Oxford, there are plenty of places to visit and things to do. Here is a list of some of the top attractions in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Miami University Art Museum: Located on the Miami University campus, this museum features a collection of over 17,000 art pieces from around the world.
  • William Holmes McGuffey Museum: Dedicated to the life and work of William Holmes McGuffey, author of the McGuffey Readers, one of the most widely-used educational books in American history.
  • Hueston Woods State Park: This 3,000-acre state park features hiking trails, fishing, boating, and camping amidst a scenic natural backdrop.

Natural Attractions of Oxford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Black Covered Bridge: This historic covered bridge is the only one of its kind in Butler County and is a popular spot for photography.
  • Miami University Natural Areas: With over 1,000 acres of protected land, including forests, wetlands, and meadows, the Miami University Natural Areas are a perfect place to go hiking and relax in nature.
  • Peffer Park: This beautiful park features walking paths, picnic areas, and a playground for children.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oxford

If you’re interested in hiking or biking, there are several trails in and around Oxford that are worth exploring. Some popular trails include:

  • Hueston Woods State Park: Featuring over 12 miles of trails for hiking and mountain biking with scenic views of the lake and waterfront.
  • Miami University Natural Areas: With over 4 miles of trails, hikers and bikers can explore the natural beauty of the area while enjoying a leisurely stroll, jog, or bike ride.
  • Talawanda Oxford Bikeway: A great option for bikers, this 4.25-mile trail starts at Oxford Community Park and heads towards the campus of Miami University.

Best Things to Do in Oxford: Activities and Tours

For those looking for more than just hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do and see in Oxford. Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience:

  • OUA Ghost Walks: Take a spooky tour of the Miami University campus and learn about the hauntings and legends that surround this historic institution.
  • Horseback Riding at Whitewater Woods: Sign up for a guided horseback ride through the trails of Whitewater Woods and experience the beauty of the area from a unique perspective.
  • Butler County Donut Trail Tour: A fun and unique way to sample some of the best donuts in the area, the Butler County Donut Trail Tour takes you on a delicious journey through various local bakeries.

Food and Drink Scene of Oxford

Oxford’s food and drink scene is a mix of international flavors and classic American fare. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jalapeno Popper Grilled Cheese: A spicy twist on a classic grilled cheese sandwich, this dish is available at Bagel & Deli, one of Oxford’s iconic local restaurants.
  • BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwich: Served on a fresh-baked bun and topped with crispy onion rings, this sandwich is a savory delight that is available at local favorite, Quarter Barrel Brewery.

Local Beverages

  • Black Raspberry Chocolate Chip Ice Cream: A Miami University specialty, this creamy ice cream is made with chunks of chocolate and locally-sourced black raspberries.
  • Sorority Hill Apple Cider: Made from freshly-picked apples, this seasonal cider is available in the fall and is a must-try for visitors to Oxford.

Where to Stay in Oxford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast or a modern hotel, there are plenty of options for accommodation in Oxford. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Elms Bed and Breakfast: Located in a historic 19th-century home, The Elms offers a warm and welcoming atmosphere that will make you feel right at home.
  • Marcum Hotel and Conference Center: Located on the Miami University campus, this modern hotel features comfortable accommodations and easy access to all of the area’s attractions.
  • Best Western Sycamore Inn: Situated just a few minutes from downtown, this hotel offers convenient access to the city’s top attractions and a comfortable, affordable stay.

What to Buy in Oxford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a unique souvenir or a taste of local flavor, Oxford has plenty of options. Here are some of the best local products to shop for:

  • Miami University Merchandise: Whether you’re a fan of the local sports teams or just want to show your school spirit, the Miami University Bookstore has a wide selection of merchandise and clothing for purchase.
  • Marcum Conference Center Honey: Produced by the bees that call the Marcum Conference Center home, this delicious honey is available for purchase in the hotel lobby.
  • Brink Brewing Co. Beers: A popular local brewery, Brink Brewing Co. offers a wide range of beers that are available for purchase in their taproom.

Local Culture and Customs of Oxford

Oxford is a laid-back, welcoming community that prides itself on its vibrant cultural scene and its commitment to diversity and inclusion. Visitors to the area can expect to find plenty of opportunities to engage with the local culture through music, art, and events. Additionally, Miami University is deeply ingrained in the local community, playing a significant role in shaping the area’s traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Oxford

The best time to visit Oxford is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the area is beautiful year-round, with each season offering its own unique charm and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oxford

To make the most of your visit to Oxford, here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do take a leisurely stroll through the Miami University campus and take in the beauty of the historic architecture.
  • Do visit the Black Covered Bridge for a unique photo opportunity.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including the bagels at Bagel & Deli and the beer at Brink Brewing Co.
  • Do plan to spend plenty of time outdoors, exploring the parks and natural areas that surround Oxford.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking or biking.
  • Don’t underestimate the amount of time you’ll need to explore the Miami University campus.
  • Don’t eat too many donuts on the Butler County Donut Trail Tour!

Safety and Health

Safety in Oxford

Oxford is a safe and welcoming community that is known for its low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should still exercise caution when traveling alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Oxford

Oxford has several hospitals and medical centers that are easily accessible for visitors in case of a medical emergency. Local pharmacies and health food stores are also available for any basic needs.

Getting to Oxford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oxford

The Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport is the closest major airport to Oxford, located about 40 miles away. There are several other regional airports within a few hours’ drive of the city.

Bus Stations Near Oxford

The Oxford Bus Station provides transportation in and out of the city, making it easy to access Oxford from other nearby cities.

Other Options

For visitors who prefer to drive, there are several major highways that run through the area, including Interstate 74 and US Route 27. Additionally, several taxi and ride-share services are available to provide transportation around the city.

How to Get Around in Oxford

The best way to get around Oxford is by walking or biking. The city is relatively small and easy to navigate, with many of the top attractions located within a short walking distance of one another. Additionally, there are several bike rental services available for visitors who prefer to explore on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Oxford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Oxford, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are some of the top options:

  • Cincinnati: Just a short drive away, Cincinnati is a vibrant city with a diverse range of attractions, including the Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden, the Cincinnati Art Museum, and the Findlay Market.
  • Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Park: This national park is dedicated to the legacy of the Wright Brothers and offers visitors a chance to explore the birthplace of aviation.
  • The Kentucky Bourbon Trail: A must-do for bourbon lovers, the Kentucky Bourbon Trail takes visitors on a journey through the state’s distilleries and offers a glimpse into the history and culture of bourbon production.

Conclusion

Oxford, Ohio is a charming and picturesque destination that is perfect for anyone looking for a laid-back, relaxing vacation surrounded by natural beauty and rich cultural history. With plenty of things to do and see, comfortable accommodations, and a welcoming community, Oxford offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature lover, a foodie, or a history buff, this destination is definitely worth adding to your travel bucket list.
