Introduction to Sharonville

Located in Butler County, in Greater Cincinnati, in Ohio, in the Midwest region of the United States, Sharonville is a small city with a big heart. With a population of just over 13,000, this family-friendly destination is known for its warm welcomes, fun attractions, and scenic beauty. Whether you’re passing through on a road trip or looking for a weekend getaway, Sharonville has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Sharonville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sharonville has a rich history that is well-preserved and on display for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the standout cultural attractions in town:

  • Heritage Village Museum: This living history museum takes you back to the 1800s with its collection of restored buildings and period exhibits.
  • Cincinnati Circus Company: This unique attraction offers circus classes, performances, and even birthday parties.
  • Sharon Woods Park: Home to the Gorman Heritage Farm, Sharon Woods Park is a peaceful retreat with hiking trails, a lake, and a nature center.

Natural Attractions of Sharonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Sharonville won’t disappoint. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Trammel Fossil Park: This paleontological preserve is a must-see for anyone interested in geology or fossils.
  • Miami Whitewater Forest: Just a short drive from Sharonville, this park boasts over 4,000 acres of woods, hiking trails, and fishing opportunities.
  • Kings Island: Home to some of the best roller coasters in the country, Kings Island is a popular theme park that’s great for families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sharonville

Sharonville has plenty of opportunities for recreational activity. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Sharon Woods Lake Trail: This 2.6-mile loop around the park’s lake is an easy hike with stunning views.
  • Winton Woods Trail: This 2.6-mile paved trail is a popular spot for joggers, cyclists, and walkers.
  • Great Miami Riverway: This 23-mile trail follows the river from Sidney to Hamilton, with plenty of parks and attractions along the way.

Best Things to Do in Sharonville: Activities and Tours

Aside from the cultural and natural attractions, Sharonville also has plenty of other activities and tours to keep you busy. Here are some of the best:

  • Escape the Room Challenge: Challenge your problem-solving skills by trying to escape from one of the themed rooms.
  • Cincinnati Food Tours: Take a guided tour of the city’s best restaurants and learn about the history of Cincinnati-style chili.
  • Sky Zone Trampoline Park: This indoor trampoline park is a fun way to burn off some energy.

Food and Drink Scene of Sharonville

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Sharonville has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-try dishes in town:

  • Cincinnati-style chili: This unique chili is typically served over spaghetti with a mound of shredded cheddar cheese.
  • Goetta: A regional specialty, goetta is a sausage made with pork, beef, oats, and spices.
  • German-inspired dishes: Sharonville has a strong German heritage, so be sure to try a pretzel or bratwurst.

Local Beverages

Of course, no meal is complete without a beverage! Here are some local drinks to try:

  • Rhinegeist Beer: This popular brewery in Cincinnati offers a variety of craft beers.
  • Strauss Brands Grassfed Beef: This local beef brand has been in business for over 150 years and is known for its high-quality meat.
  • Coffee Emporium: This coffee shop and roaster has been around since 1973 and has won numerous awards for its delicious coffee beans.

Where to Stay in Sharonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly option or a luxurious stay, Sharonville has something for every traveler. Here are some of the best places to stay in town:

  • Drury Inn & Suites: This mid-range hotel offers free breakfast, an indoor pool, and a hot tub.
  • Sonesta ES Suites: A top-rated hotel on TripAdvisor, Sonesta ES Suites offers spacious studios and one-bedroom suites with a full kitchen.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel has free breakfast, Wi-Fi, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Sharonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take a piece of Sharonville home with you, here are some local products to consider:

  • Artisanal soaps and candles: Check out Paragon Soaps for handcrafted soaps and candles made with all-natural ingredients.
  • Vintage finds: The Flea Market Warehouse is a great spot for unique knick-knacks and vintage items.
  • Local honey: Head to the Cincinnati Honey Company to taste some of the city’s best honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Sharonville

Sharonville has a proud German heritage that is still celebrated today. Visitors should be prepared to encounter friendly locals, delicious food, and a strong sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Sharonville

The best time to visit Sharonville is from mid-May to mid-October when the weather is warm and sunny. Be sure to check local events calendars for festivals and other special events happening during your visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sharonville

Do:

  • Try Cincinnati-style chili
  • Explore the outdoors at one of the local parks
  • Check out the local farmers market on Saturdays

Don’t:

  • Forget your sunscreen in the summer months
  • Expect everything to be open on Sundays

Safety and Health

Safety in Sharonville

Overall, Sharonville is a very safe city, but as always, visitors should exercise common sense and take necessary precautions.

Health in Sharonville

There are several medical facilities in Sharonville, including TriHealth Bethesda North Hospital and Mercy Health - Fairfield Hospital.

Getting to Sharonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sharonville

  • Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport (CVG)
  • Dayton International Airport (DAY)

Bus Stations Near Sharonville

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Cincinnati, just a short drive from Sharonville

Other Options

  • Rental cars are widely available at both airports and in the Cincinnati area.

How to Get Around in Sharonville

The best way to get around Sharonville is by car. However, there are also bike and walking trails for those who prefer to explore on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Sharonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your travels beyond Sharonville, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Cincinnati: Just a short drive away, Cincinnati is a vibrant city with plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • Dayton: Home to the National Museum of the United States Air Force, Dayton is a must-see destination for history buffs.
  • Louisville, KY: About an hour and a half away, Louisville has plenty to see and do, including the famous Kentucky Derby.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, indulge in delicious food, or simply relax and unwind, Sharonville is a destination that truly has it all. From museums and historical sites to theme parks and outdoor adventures, this charming Midwestern city will keep you entertained and engaged throughout your visit.
