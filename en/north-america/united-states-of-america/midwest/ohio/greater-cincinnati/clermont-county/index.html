Clermont County is a hidden gem nestled in Greater Cincinnati, Ohio, offering a mix of history, natural beauty, and adventure. Whether you’re looking for cultural attractions, outdoor activities, or a relaxing getaway, Clermont County has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore all that Clermont County has to offer and help you plan your next trip.

Introduction to Clermont County

Clermont County is located in the Midwest region of the United States, in the state of Ohio. It’s known for its scenic rolling hills, picturesque countryside, and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and camping. The county is home to a diverse mix of towns, including Batavia, Milford, Amelia, and Williamsburg, each with its own unique charm and character.

List of Places to Visit in Clermont County

If you’re planning a trip to Clermont County, here are the top places you should consider adding to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Harriet Beecher Stowe House in Cincinnati, Ohio
  • The Grant Memorial in Georgetown, Ohio
  • The Elisha Morgan Farmhouse in Milford, Ohio
  • The Chilo Lock 34 Visitor Center in Chilo, Ohio
  • The Rankin House in Ripley, Ohio

Natural Attractions of Clermont County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • East Fork State Park
  • Stonelick State Park
  • Lake Isabella
  • The Little Miami Scenic Trail
  • The Cincinnati Nature Center in Milford, Ohio

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clermont County

Clermont County has a wealth of hiking and biking routes for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Loveland Bike Trail
  • The Little Miami Scenic Trail
  • The Quail Hollow State Park Trail
  • The East Fork State Park Trail
  • The Caesar Creek State Park Trail

Best Things to Do in Clermont County: Activities and Tours

  • Treetop Adventures at the Cincinnati Zoo & Botanical Garden
  • Water sports at Lake Isabella
  • Zip-lining at Ozone Zipline Adventures
  • Wine tasting at Harmony Hill Vineyards & Estate Winery
  • Horseback riding at Venture Trails Nature Preserve

Week-Long Itinerary for Clermont County

If you’re looking to spend a week in Clermont County, you can follow this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Cincinnati Nature Center in Milford, Ohio
  • Day 2: Go hiking or biking on the Little Miami Scenic Trail
  • Day 3: Explore the cultural attractions of Clermont County, including the Grant Memorial and the Chilo Lock 34 Visitor Center
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Cincinnati and check out attractions such as the Cincinnati Zoo & Botanical Garden and the National Underground Railroad Freedom Center
  • Day 5: Spend a day at East Fork State Park and enjoy water sports and other outdoor activities
  • Day 6: Go on a tour of Harmony Hill Vineyards & Estate Winery and enjoy a tasting of their wines
  • Day 7: Wrap up your week by exploring downtown Milford and shopping for souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Clermont County

Clermont County has a rich food and drink scene, with a range of options for every taste and budget. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cincinnati-style chili at Skyline or Gold Star Chili
  • Buckeye candy, a chocolate peanut butter treat
  • Goetta, a pork and oatmeal sausage
  • Fried chicken and biscuits at Kapok’s
  • Ice cream at Graeter’s

Local Beverages

  • Beer from the Old Firehouse Brewery in Williamsburg, Ohio
  • Wine from Meranda-Nixon Winery in Ripley, Ohio
  • Cider from Harmony Hill Vineyards & Estate Winery in Bethel, Ohio
  • Bourbon from the New Riff Distillery in Newport, Kentucky

Local Culture and Customs of Clermont County

Clermont County is known for its friendly, welcoming residents who take pride in their local heritage and culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including saying hello and being polite.

Best Time to Visit Clermont County

The best time to visit Clermont County is during the fall when the leaves change color. The weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy, including hiking and biking.

Safety and Health

Clermont County is generally safe for visitors. However, it’s always important to keep safety and health in mind. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Clermont County

  • Be aware of your surroundings
  • Keep valuables out of sight
  • Follow state park and outdoor activity regulations

Health in Clermont County

  • Use sunscreen and wear appropriate clothing to protect from the sun
  • Check for ticks and other insects after participating in outdoor activities

Getting to Clermont County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clermont County is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some options:

Airports Near Clermont County

  • Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport
  • Dayton International Airport

Bus Stations Near Clermont County

  • Greyhound Bus Lines in Cincinnati, Ohio
  • Megabus in Cincinnati, Ohio

Other Options

  • Driving: Clermont County is located near major highways, including I-71 and I-275.
  • Car Rental: Rental cars are available at Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport and other locations.

How to Get Around in Clermont County

Clermont County is best explored by car, but there are other transportation options. Here are some options:

  • Rental cars: Rental cars are available at Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport and other locations.
  • Taxis: Taxi services are available in some areas, including Batavia and Milford.
  • Bus: The Clermont Transportation Connection (CTC) offers bus service throughout the county.

Where to Go Next from Clermont County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to expand your trip outside of Clermont County, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Cincinnati, Ohio: Visit attractions such as the Cincinnati Zoo & Botanical Garden, Cincinnati Art Museum, and Findlay Market.
  • Newport, Kentucky: Check out Newport Aquarium or take a riverboat tour.
  • Dayton, Ohio: Visit the National Museum of the United States Air Force or the Dayton Art Institute.

Conclusion

Clermont County is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the natural beauty and cultural heritage of the Midwest. With its range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine, Clermont County has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your next trip and make the most of your time in this hidden gem of Ohio.
