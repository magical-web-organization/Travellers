Amelia is a small town located in Clermont County, Ohio, in the heart of Greater Cincinnati. With its rich history, scenic natural landscapes, and diverse cultural offerings, Amelia is a hidden gem that is definitely worth exploring. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ve compiled a list of the best places to visit, things to do, and local recommendations to help you plan your perfect trip to Amelia.

Introduction to Amelia

Amelia was founded in the early 1800s and has a rich history that’s worth exploring. The town is known for its laid-back atmosphere, friendly locals, and scenic parks. Visitors to the area will find a treasure trove of cultural and natural attractions, including museums and historical landmarks, hiking and biking trails, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Amelia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amelia has a rich history that’s evident in its many museums and historical sites. One of the most popular attractions is the Stonelick Covered Bridge, which was built in 1878 and is one of the last remaining covered bridges in the area. Visitors can also explore the Harsha Lake Wildlife Area, which boasts 2,200 acres of scenic nature trails, fishing ponds, and hunting areas.

Natural Attractions of Amelia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty to love in Amelia. The East Fork State Park is a popular destination for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers. The park features more than 4,900 acres of rolling hills, dense forest, and scenic lake shores. The park offers over 300 campsites ranging from primitive to full hook-up sites, as well as 14 cottages. The park also boasts horseback riding, fishing, boating and hunting opportunities. For those looking for a unique outdoor experience, look no further than the Rock Pinnacles State Nature Preserve. The preserve is home to a unique rock formation that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amelia

Amelia is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the region. The Little Miami Scenic Trail is a popular destination for bikers and hikers. The trail runs along the Little Miami River and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. Visitors can also explore the Stonelick State Park, which features over 100 acres of scenic hiking trails that lead to stunning views of the nearby lakes and forested areas.

Best Things to Do in Amelia: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor adventures, visitors to Amelia can enjoy a variety of activities and tours. The historic Clermont County Courthouse is a must-see attraction for history buffs, while the nearby Windy Way Winery offers wine tastings and tours for those who enjoy good wine Visit the ASWA State Wildlife Area, one of Ohio’s premier public hunting and fishing areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amelia

Day 1: Explore the Stonelick Covered Bridge and Harsha Lake Wildlife Area. Day 2: Hike the trails at Little Miami Scenic Trail and enjoy biking tours of the town. Day 3: Visit East Fork State Park and participate in outdoor sports like boating, fishing, and hunting. Day 4: Take a tour of Clermont County Courthouse and Windy Way Winery. Day 5: Witness the majestic Rock Pinnacles State Nature Preserve for its breathtaking views. Day 6: Explore the ASWA State Wildlife Area in the heart of Amelia. Day 7: Relax along with the fresh breeze of the town and enjoy the city’s leisurely pace.

Food and Drink Scene of Amelia

Must-Try Dishes

One of the best ways to experience Amelia’s cultural scene is through its food. Visitors can enjoy local favorites such as the famous Cincinnati-style chili at Skyline Chili. Amelia is also known for its fried chicken and barbecue at Eli’s BBQ.

Local Beverages

Amelia has a growing craft beer scene, with a number of local breweries such as Narrow Path Brewing and Old Firehouse Brewery offering a wide variety of beers. Visitors can also sample local wines at the nearby Harmony Hill Vineyards and Estate Winery.

Where to Stay in Amelia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amelia has a number of accommodation options for visitors. The Holiday Inn Eastgate and the Hilton Garden Inn are both located in convenient locations, while the Old Coach House Bed & Breakfast offers a charming, home-away-from-home experience for those looking for a more intimate stay.

What to Buy in Amelia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring a piece of Amelia home with them by shopping for local products like handmade crafts, pottery, and jewelry at the Amelia Village Museum. Local farmers’ markets such as the Amelia Farmers Market offer fresh produce, artisan cheeses, and homemade baked goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Amelia

Amelia’s culture is diverse and welcoming, with a laid-back pace that is perfect for relaxing and taking in the local sights. Visitors are encouraged to explore local history, traditions, and customs by visiting museums and historical sites, attending local festivals, and trying local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Amelia

The best time to visit Amelia is during the spring and fall when temperatures are mild and the landscapes are vibrant with color. Summer is also an excellent time to visit for those who enjoy outdoor activities and festivals, while the winter months are ideal for those who want to enjoy a cozy, laid-back getaway.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amelia

Do:

  • Do visit the Stonelick State Park and East Fork State Park for outdoor adventures.
  • Do visit the local breweries and wineries for tastings and tours.
  • Do try the famous Cincinnati-style chili at Skyline Chili.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and natural areas.
  • Don’t underestimate the value of comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t skip the opportunity to learn about local history and culture by visiting museums and historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amelia

Amelia is a safe destination, but visitors are encouraged to exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid leaving valuables in their vehicles.

Health in Amelia

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Amelia. It is always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Amelia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Amelia is easy, with access to several major highways and airports. The closest airport is the Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport. Visitors can also take the bus, and car rental options are also available.

Airports Near Amelia

  • Dayton International Airport
  • Port Columbus International Airport

How to Get Around in Amelia

Amelia is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by car. Visitors can also rent bikes or take advantage of local taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Amelia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Amelia is located in a prime location for exploring nearby destinations such as Cincinnati, Dayton, and Columbus. Cincinnati is known for its rich culture and history, while Dayton is home to the National Museum of the U.S. Air Force. Columbus is home to the Ohio State Capitol and the popular Columbus Zoo and Aquarium.

Conclusion

Amelia, Ohio, is a hidden gem that’s just waiting to be discovered. With its rich history, scenic natural landscapes, and diverse cultural offerings, Amelia is the perfect destination for visitors seeking a laid-back, charming getaway in Greater Cincinnati.
