Introduction to Pickerington

Nestled in Southeast Ohio, Pickerington is a charming town that offers visitors a perfect balance of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. The town, which is part of Fairfield County, is known for its rich heritage, numerous parks, and world-class restaurants. With its bustling downtown area, plenty of museums and historical sites to explore, and a variety of things to do, Pickerington definitely deserves a spot on your travel bucket list.

Best Things to Do in Pickerington: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adrenaline-pumping adventure or a relaxing afternoon with your family, Pickerington has something for everyone to enjoy. Some of the top things to do in the area include:

  • Wine Tasting at Slate Run Vineyard
  • Horseback Riding at Marmon Valley Farm

Week-Long Itinerary for Pickerington

If you’re planning to spend a week in Pickerington, here’s a suggested itinerary you might want to follow:

Day 1 - Visit the Motorcycle Hall of Fame Museum and Sycamore Creek Park. Day 2 - Go on a wine-tasting tour at Slate Run Vineyard and enjoy the scenic views. Day 3 - Tour the Pickerington-Violet Township Historical Society Museum and Wagnalls Memorial Library. Day 4 - Explore Pickerington Ponds Metro Park and its beautiful hiking trails. Day 5 - Check out the Heron Pond Nature Preserve and try some trout fishing. Day 6 - Go golfing at Turnberry Golf Course and have a relaxing afternoon. Day 7 - Take a horseback riding tour at Marmon Valley Farm and enjoy the picturesque landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Pickerington

When visiting Pickerington, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. The locals are generally quite friendly and welcoming, but it’s always a good idea to observe basic etiquette and be mindful of your surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Pickerington

The best time to visit Pickerington is during the spring or fall months, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful. The summers can be quite hot and humid, so if you’re not used to that kind of weather, you may want to avoid visiting during that time of year.
