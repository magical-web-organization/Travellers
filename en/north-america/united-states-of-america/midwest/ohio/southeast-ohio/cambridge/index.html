Introduction to Cambridge

Cambridge, Ohio is a small city located in the southeastern part of the state. It has a rich history and many historic sites and buildings, as well as a beautiful natural landscape. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, there’s something for everyone in Cambridge.

List of Places to Visit in Cambridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Museum of Cambridge Glass - Displays and exhibits glassware produced by Cambridge Glass Company from 1902 to 1958.
  • The Southeastern Ohio Symphony Orchestra - Features professional musicians and offers a variety of performances throughout the year.
  • The Historic National Road - A stretch of the original highway, complete with restored inns and restaurants that were once stops along the road.

Natural Attractions of Cambridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salt Fork State Park - Offers boating and fishing on Ohio’s largest state park lake, as well as hiking and camping opportunities.
  • Seneca Lake - A 3,500-acre lake with plenty of boating and fishing.
  • The Wilds - A wildlife conservation center that features rare and endangered species from around the world on over 10,000 acres of reclaimed mine land.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cambridge

  • The Salt Fork Multi-Use Trail - A 13-mile paved trail that is perfect for hiking, biking, and rollerblading.
  • The Great Guernsey Trail - A 12-mile trail that features beautiful scenery and several access points.

Best Things to Do in Cambridge: Activities and Tours

  • The Guernsey County Courthouse - A beautiful historical building that is available for tours.
  • The Cambridge City Park - Features a swimming pool, skate park, and playgrounds.
  • The Living Word Outdoor Drama - An outdoor performance that tells the story of Christ through 16 scenes with live animals, special effects, and a powerful musical score.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cambridge

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit The National Museum of Cambridge Glass and explore the historic downtown area.
Day 2 Spend the day at Salt Fork State Park, go hiking, and enjoy a picnic.
Day 3 Visit Seneca Lake and enjoy the lake view
Day 4 Take a tour of The Wilds and visit the animals
Day 5 Explore Great Guernsey Trail on foot or by bike
Day 6 Take a stroll along the Historic National Road and visit the restored inns and restaurants
Day 7 Relax at Cambridge City Park, enjoy swimming, and the skate park

Food and Drink Scene of Cambridge

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Candy - Peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate
  • Apple Butter Spread - A sweet and spicy spread made with apples and cinnamon
  • Pierogies - A Polish dumpling filled with mashed potatoes and cheese, served with sour cream and onions

Local Beverages

  • John Glenn Brewery - Offers a variety of locally brewed craft beers
  • Rainbow Hills Winery - Produce the best wines in the area.

Where to Stay in Cambridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cambridge Inn - A budget-friendly hotel that is well-equipped with all necessary amenities.
  • Hampton Inn - A comfortable, mid-range hotel that offers a delicious breakfast buffet.
  • Salt Fork Lodge - A luxurious property located within Salt Fork State Park.

What to Buy in Cambridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cambridge Glassware - Beautiful and unique pieces of glassware available at the National Museum of Cambridge Glass.
  • John Glenn memorabilia - Commemorative items related to the famous astronaut and senator.
  • Apple Butter Spread - A tasty souvenir that your friends and family will enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Cambridge

The people of Cambridge are friendly and welcoming to visitors. They take pride in their history and cultural heritage. While there are no specific customs to note, it is important to respect the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Cambridge

The best time to visit Cambridge is the fall. The weather is mild and the fall foliage is stunning. Also, the Salt Fork Festival is held annually in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cambridge

Do:

  • Visit the National Museum of Cambridge Glass and learn about the history of the town.
  • Take a hike or bike around Salt Fork State Park.
  • Try the local dishes like pierogies and buckeye candy.
  • Be friendly and respectful to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter outdoors or disrespect nature in any way.
  • Get too carried away with taking photos and distracting other visitors.
  • Enter abandoned buildings or off-limit areas without proper permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cambridge

Cambridge is a generally safe and peaceful town. However, it is always wise to take precautions, like locking the doors and windows of your accommodation and keeping important goods secure. Avoid walking alone at night, and if possible, take the help of tour operators if you have plans to enjoy outdoor activities.

Health in Cambridge

There are several medical facilities available in Cambridge, Ohio. Hence, tourists need not worry in the event of emergencies.

Getting to Cambridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cambridge

The closest airport is Port Columbus International Airport in Columbus, Ohio, which is less than a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Cambridge

Greyhound serves Cambridge, Ohio.

Other Options

Renting a car is recommended for visitors who want to explore the natural beauty of Cambridge, Ohio.

How to Get Around in Cambridge

The best way to get around Cambridge is by car. Other modes of transportation are limited and do not provide much flexibility. Alternatively, rent a bike and explore the downtown area on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Cambridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Columbus, Ohio - The capital city of Ohio, located less than two hours away from Cambridge.
  • Wheeling, West Virginia - A charming city located a short drive across the border from Cambridge.
  • Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania - A big city with plenty of attractions and located just over an hour’s drive from Cambridge.

Conclusion

Cambridge is a great destination for those who love history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure. Plan to spend a week or more in the area to fully experience everything it has to offer. Whether you’re exploring the downtown area, hiking around Salt Fork State Park, or enjoying the local food scene, Cambridge, Ohio, will leave you with unforgettable memories.
