Introduction to Wayne National Forest

Located in Southeast Ohio, Wayne National Forest is a 241,000-acre public land that offers numerous outdoor recreation opportunities, cultural attractions, and scenic views. Managed by the USDA Forest Service, it is divided into three units: Athens, Marietta, and Ironton. The forest is a popular destination for hiking, camping, hunting, fishing, and horseback riding. It also has historic sites, museums, and cultural events that showcase the region’s rich heritage. Its diverse ecosystem includes hills, valleys, rivers, streams, and caves that provide habitats for various plants and animals. Come and discover the wonders of Wayne National Forest!

List of Places to Visit in Wayne National Forest

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ohio River Museum: Located in Marietta unit, this museum displays exhibits related to the history and culture of the Ohio River and its communities.

  • The Campus Martius Museum: Also in Marietta unit, this museum is dedicated to the early settlements and pioneers of Ohio. It features artifacts and exhibits from the 18th and 19th century.

  • The Southeast Ohio History Center: This center in Athens unit showcases the cultural and social history of the region through exhibits, educational programs, and lectures.

  • The Moonville Tunnel: Located in Ironton District, this tunnel is considered one of the most haunted places in Ohio. It was once part of a railroad that transported coal from Moonville to Athens.

Natural Attractions of Wayne National Forest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Hocking Hills: This area in Athens unit is famous for its natural beauty, including waterfalls, cliffs, caves, and hiking trails. The most popular sites include the Old Man’s Cave, the Cedar Falls, and the Ash Cave.

  • The Wildcat Hollow Trail: This trail in Marietta unit is a 16-mile loop that offers a challenging hiking experience and scenic views of the forest, creeks, and rock formations.

  • The Lake Vesuvius Recreation Area: Located in Ironton District, this area is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and camping. It features a 143-acre lake, a beach, and several hiking and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wayne National Forest

Wayne National Forest has numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to different skill levels and interests. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • The Buckeye Trail: This trail covers 1,444 miles across Ohio and includes several segments in Wayne National Forest. It passes through scenic areas, historic sites, and cultural landmarks.

  • The North Country National Scenic Trail: This trail spans 4,600 miles across seven states, including Ohio. The trail passes through Wayne National Forest and offers a unique opportunity to explore the forest’s natural and cultural heritage.

  • The Baileys Trail System: This trail network in Athens unit is currently under development but is expected to have over 26 miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails. It will provide access to scenic vistas, waterfalls, and rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Wayne National Forest: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to do in Wayne National Forest:

  • Camping: The forest has numerous campgrounds, including primitive campsites, RV sites, and group campsites. Some sites offer amenities such as showers, water, and firewood.

  • Fishing: The forest has several lakes, rivers, and streams that provide excellent fishing opportunities for species such as bass, trout, and catfish.

  • Horseback riding: The forest has over 300 miles of designated horseback riding trails that cater to different levels of experience. It also has several equestrian campsites.

  • Canoeing/Kayaking: The forest has several waterways such as the Hocking River, the Ohio River, and the Little Muskingum River, which provide scenic spots for paddling.

  • Guided tours: Several companies offer guided tours of the forest, including hiking, biking, and kayak tours. These tours are led by experienced guides who provide insights into the forest’s ecology, geology, and history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wayne National Forest

For a week-long trip to Wayne National Forest, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive at the forest, set up camp at one of the campgrounds and explore the nearby trails.

  • Day 2: Visit the Ohio River Museum and the Campus Martius Museum in Marietta unit.

  • Day 3: Hike the Wildcat Hollow Trail in Marietta Unit.

  • Day 4: Explore the Hocking Hills in Athens unit.

  • Day 5: Go fishing or boating at the Lake Vesuvius Recreation Area in Ironton District.

  • Day 6: Take a guided kayak tour of the Ohio River.

  • Day 7: Visit the Southeast Ohio History Center and the Moonville Tunnel in Athens Unit.

Food and Drink Scene of Wayne National Forest

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buckeye Candy: This sweet treat is made with peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate, resembling the shape of Ohio’s state tree, the buckeye.

  • Fried green tomatoes: This Southern favorite is made by coating sliced green tomatoes with cornmeal and frying them until crispy.

  • Pierogies: This dish is a popular in Eastern Ohio since many residents have Eastern European roots. Pierogies are filled dumplings that can be stuffed with potatoes, cheese, onions, or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Ohio wine and craft beer: Ohio has several wineries and breweries that produce high-quality wines and beers. Some popular ones include the Shade Winery and Jackie O’s Brewery.

  • Sassafras tea: This tea is made from boiling the root of the sassafras tree, which is abundant in the forest. It has a unique flavor and is believed to have medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Wayne National Forest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wayne National Forest has several lodging options, including:

  • Campgrounds: The forest has several campgrounds, including those with amenities such as shower and toilet facilities.

  • Cabins: There are several cabin rentals in the forest that offer a more comfortable camping experience.

  • Hotels and motels: There are several hotels and motels in nearby towns such as Athens, Marietta, and Ironton.

What to Buy in Wayne National Forest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Wayne National Forest:

  • Maple syrup: Ohio is one of the top maple syrup producers in the United States, and Wayne National Forest has several maple farms that produce high-quality syrup.

  • Handmade crafts: Several local artisans sell handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and wood carvings.

  • Local produce: The forest has several farmers’ markets that sell fresh fruits, vegetables, and dairy products.

Local Culture and Customs of Wayne National Forest

The region around Wayne National Forest has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its people, food, and events. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and often celebrate their Appalachian roots through festivals and music.

Best Time to Visit Wayne National Forest

The best time to visit Wayne National Forest is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its peak. However, each season has its charm, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wayne National Forest

Do:

  • Follow the forest’s regulations and rules to help preserve its natural beauty.

  • Pack appropriately for the activities you plan to do, including water, snacks, and insect repellent.

  • Respect the wildlife and their habitats by not disturbing them or feeding them.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind. Use designated trash receptacles and pack out your trash.

  • Don’t damage the forests by cutting trees or removing rocks or plants.

  • Don’t start fires outside designated fire rings or grills.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wayne National Forest

  • Be aware of your surroundings, and avoid hiking alone.

  • Let others know your plans and when you expect to return.

  • Be prepared for weather changes and stay informed about forecasted weather conditions.

Health in Wayne National Forest

  • Bring proper medication and a first aid kit.

  • Avoid drinking untreated water from streams or lakes

  • Protect yourself from insects by wearing long sleeves, pants, and insect repellent.

How to Get Around in Wayne National Forest

The best way to get around Wayne National Forest is by car, bike, or foot. The forest has several scenic drives, and many trails are designated for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Where to Go Next from Wayne National Forest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore after visiting Wayne National Forest:

  • Hocking Hills State Park: This scenic park is located a short distance from the forest and offers several hiking trails, waterfalls, and forested areas.

  • Amish Country, Ohio: Located in northeastern Ohio, Amish Country is known for its picturesque landscape, handmade crafts, and unique culture.

  • Cincinnati, Ohio: Located in southwestern Ohio, Cincinnati is a vibrant city with several museums, restaurants, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Wayne National Forest is a treasure trove of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor recreation. With its diverse ecosystems, scenic drives, and exciting activities, it offers visitors an unparalleled travel experience. By following the do’s and don’ts and taking necessary safety precautions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Wayne National Forest.
