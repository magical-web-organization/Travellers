Introduction to Hocking Hills

Nestled in Southeast Ohio, Hocking Hills is a natural wonderland that boasts scenic views, breathtaking hiking trails, and cultural attractions that appeal to visitors of all ages. This small town is known for its deep gorges, towering hemlocks, and waterfalls that cascade into turquoise pools. The rolling hills, lush valleys, and spectacular rock formations create a picturesque setting that draws millions of tourists each year.

Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in Hocking Hills. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the must-visit attractions, outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture, safety tips and more.

List of Places to Visit in Hocking Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hocking Valley Scenic Railway: Take a trip aboard a vintage railway to explore the historic town of Nelsonville, where you can visit the Hocking County Historical Society Museum.
  • Decorative Arts Center of Ohio: This museum presents a wide range of exhibits exploring the fine and decorative arts across the Ohio Valley region. Discover artifacts that trace the rich history of Southeast Ohio.
  • Paul A. Johnson Pencil Sharpener Museum: The world’s only pencil sharpener museum features a collection of over 3,400 sharpeners, highlighting the evolution of pencil sharpening technology for over a century.

Natural Attractions of Hocking Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ash Cave: One of the largest recess caves east of the Mississippi, Ash Cave is a popular destination that draws visitors from all over the region. The cave is surrounded by towering cliffs, and its iconic waterfall plunges 90 feet into a shimmering pool.
  • Old Man’s Cave: This craggy canyon features towering cliffs, tranquil streams, and stunning waterfalls that are a marvel to behold. With several hiking trails that wind through the area, Old Man’s Cave is the perfect spot for an outdoor adventure.
  • Rock House: This stunning rock formation features a narrow chimney that provides a stunning view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hocking Hills

  • Hocking Hills State Park Trails: This park features several hiking trails with varying levels of difficulty: Cedar Falls Trail, Cantwell Cliffs Trail, Ash Cave Trail, Old Man’s Cave Trail, Conkle’s Hollow Trail, and Rock House Trail.
  • Bike the Greenway Trail in Logan: This paved trail stretches over 6 miles from Logan to Nelsonville, offering a scenic ride through the heart of Hocking Hills.

Best Things to Do in Hocking Hills: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The Hocking River offers several opportunities for water sports such as canoeing, kayaking, tubing, and fishing.
  • Zip Lining: Experience the adrenaline rush of soaring above the tree-line on a zip line that offers panoramic views of Hocking Hills.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the nature trails on horseback to soak in the beauty of the natural surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Hocking Hills

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgers and Fries: Enjoy a juicy burger and crispy fries at the Olde Dutch Restaurant and Banquet Haus in Logan.
  • Hocking Hills Moonshine: Sample the local moonshine at Hocking Hills Moonshine, a spirits distillery that offers daily tastings.
  • Pizza at Jimbo’s: This family-owned pizzeria offers a wide variety of toppings, crispy crusts, and delicious sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Hocking Hills Winery: Nestled in the heart of Hocking Hills, this winery offers several varietals that pair perfectly well with hearty American cuisine.
  • Muddy Boots Brewery: This award-winning microbrewery offers a variety of craft beers that can be paired with meals at local restaurants or enjoyed in the brewery taproom.

Where to Stay in Hocking Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hocking Hills Cabins: From cozy one-room cabins to luxury log homes, Hocking Hills Cabins offers a range of options to suit all tastes and budgets.
  • Ain’t Misbehavin Lodge: This stunning lodge boasts breathtaking views, a private fishing pond, and a hot tub for guests to unwind in.
  • Inn & Spa at Cedar Falls: This charming inn offers rustic elegance, delicious dining, and a full-service spa that is the perfect retreat after a long day of hiking.

What to Buy in Hocking Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pencil Sharpeners: Visit the Paul A. Johnson Pencil Sharpener Museum to get your own pencil sharpener from their gift shop.
  • Moonshine: Take home some of the famous Hocking Hills Moonshine, available for purchase at the distillery.
  • Local Artisan Crafts: Hocking Hills is home to several artists specializing in pottery, glassblowing, and mixed media. Check out the local farmers’ markets and galleries for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hocking Hills

Hocking Hills is a tight-knit community that values hard work, hospitality, and a deep appreciation for the natural beauty of the area. Visitors are welcomed with open arms, and a friendly, down-to-earth spirit pervades the town.

Best Time to Visit Hocking Hills

The best time to visit Hocking Hills is from March to May or September to November. The temperatures are mild, and the fall foliage colors blanket the hills and valleys, making for a stunning natural backdrop.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hocking Hills

Do:

  • Do respect the environment. Hocking Hills is home to several delicate ecosystems and rare plant and animal species.
  • Do take advantage of the natural surroundings. Hocking Hills is a nature lover’s paradise, with several hiking, biking, and water sports options.
  • Do show respect to the local customs and traditions. The people of Hocking Hills are proud of their heritage and history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Help us keep Hocking Hills clean and beautiful for generations to come.
  • Don’t go off marked trails. The area is known for rugged terrain and dangerous drop-offs.
  • Don’t take plants or animals from the wilderness. The local flora and fauna are vital to the region’s ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hocking Hills

  • Stick to the marked trails when hiking and biking.
  • Be careful when exploring waterfalls, as the rocks can be slippery.
  • Keep a safe distance from wild animals, and never attempt to feed or approach them.

Health in Hocking Hills

  • Wear sunscreen and bug spray when exploring the great outdoors.
  • Bring plenty of water and snacks to stay hydrated.
  • Check your skin for ticks after spending time in heavily wooded areas.

Getting to Hocking Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hocking Hills

  • John Glenn Columbus International Airport (CMH): Located 50 miles away from Hocking Hills.
  • Rickenbacker International Airport (LCK): Located 45 miles away from Hocking Hills.

Bus Stations Near Hocking Hills

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Columbus, approximately 40 miles away from Hocking Hills.

Other Options

  • Just over 1-hour drive west from Columbus on US Route 33 in Southeast Ohio, Hocking Hills is easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Hocking Hills

The best way to get around Hocking Hills is by car. However, visitors can also choose to explore the area on foot, bike, or horseback.

Where to Go Next from Hocking Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Athens, Ohio: This vibrant college town is home to Ohio University, eclectic shopping, and dining scenes, and several cultural landmarks.
  • Cincinnati, Ohio: Located approximately three hours away from Hocking Hills, the “Queen City” is known for its riverfront views, top-rated museums, and the famous Cincinnati Zoo.

Conclusion

Hocking Hills is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, foodies, and culture enthusiasts. With its stunning natural landscape, charming local community, and an abundance of sights and attractions to explore, Hocking Hills provides the perfect getaway for anyone looking for an unforgettable vacation experience. Plan your trip today and immerse yourself in the beauty and culture of Hocking Hills!
