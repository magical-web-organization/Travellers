Introduction to New Philadelphia

Nestled in the heart of Tuscarawas County, New Philadelphia is a picturesque town that provides the perfect blend of rich history, outdoor adventure, and scenic beauty. Situated in Southeast Ohio, New Philadelphia is within easy reach of major cities like Columbus and Pittsburgh. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a culture lover, or a foodie, New Philadelphia has something to offer everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tuscarawas County Courthouse

Built in 1881, the Tuscarawas County Courthouse is one of the most significant examples of Second Empire architecture in Ohio. Visitors can take a free self-guided tour of the courthouse and see its exquisite interior features, including stained glass windows and a hand-painted ceiling.

The Warther Museum

Founded by the world-renowned woodcarver Ernest “Mooney” Warther, the Warther Museum is a unique tribute to American crafts and engineering. The museum houses a vast collection of Warther’s intricate carvings along with a fully functional steam locomotive and his wife’s button collection.

Schoenbrunn Village and Museum

Schoenbrunn Village and Museum is a reconstructed 18th-century Moravian town that once served as the capital of the Ohio Country. Visitors can explore over a dozen historic buildings and learn about the daily life and culture of the Native Americans and European settlers who inhabited the area.

Natural Attractions of New Philadelphia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Uhrichsville Water Park

Nestled in Bimeler Park, Uhrichsville Water Park offers a range of recreational activities for the entire family, including swimming, water slides, and a lazy river. Visitors can also enjoy other amenities such as fishing, golfing, and tennis.

The Buckeye Trail

The Buckeye Trail is a 1444-mile hiking trail that winds through Ohio’s forests and countryside. The section of the trail that runs through Tuscarawas County is particularly beautiful, offering great opportunities for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

The Norma Johnson Center

Located on the former farm of Norma Johnson, the Norma Johnson Center is a nature preserve in New Philadelphia that encompasses 303 acres of forests, wetlands, and fields. Visitors can explore hiking trails, birdwatching, fishing, and cross-country skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Philadelphia

The Buckeye Trail

The Buckeye Trail is the perfect place to hike, cycle or horseback ride, offering various forested sections and magnificent views of the surrounding countryside.

The Ohio & Erie Canal Trail

A 101-mile trek that runs from Tuscarawas County to Cleveland, the Canalway provides unique ways of discovering Ohio history while enjoying the natural beauty of the Ohio and Erie Canal.

Best Things to Do in New Philadelphia: Activities and Tours

The Tuscarawas Valley Farmers Market

Located on East High Avenue, the Tuscarawas Valley Farmers Market provides organic produce, fresh meat, bread, and other farm-grown products. Besides a great day out, visitors can also participate in the many workshops and other exciting events.

The Tuscarawas County Fairgrounds

For the perfect family day out, head to the Tuscarawas County Fairgrounds, and attend numerous fairs, carnivals, and shows for all ages to enjoy.

Food and Drink Scene of New Philadelphia

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes include Amish and Swiss-inspired cuisine, such as shoofly pie, Swiss cheese, and apple butter. The area is known for its hearty meals and generous portions, with menus featuring a host of dishes made with local produce and meats.

Local Beverages

When in New Philadelphia, don’t forget to sample the locally brewed craft beers, including the Railroad Brewing Company, the Lockport Brewery, and the Hoodletown Brewing Company. Visitors can also savor a wide range of locally produced wines from the Three Oaks Vineyard.

What to Buy in New Philadelphia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring home locally produced items such as handmade pottery, candles, Amish quilts, and maple syrup. Souvenir seekers will also find a wide range of products to choose from, including postcards, magnets, and keychains.

Local Culture and Customs of New Philadelphia

New Philadelphia has a significant Amish population, and visitors are expected to be respectful of their lifestyle and customs. Purchases and businesses closed on Sundays are common as Amish typically do not conduct business on that day.

Best Time to Visit New Philadelphia

The best time to visit New Philadelphia is during the spring and fall, when the temperatures are mild, and the foliage is in its full glory.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Philadelphia

Do:

  • Respect the area’s Amish community and observe their customs.
  • Bring sturdy footwear and clothing suitable for outdoor activities.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the area’s Amish community by taking photos or asking too many personal questions.
  • Litter or damage scenic spots.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Philadelphia

New Philadelphia is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. Nevertheless, visitors are advised to take precautions and be aware of their surroundings when out and about in the town.

Health in New Philadelphia

Visitors are advised to consult their physician regarding any necessary vaccinations before traveling to New Philadelphia. The town has excellent healthcare services ranging from urgent care centers to hospitals.

Getting to New Philadelphia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Philadelphia

The Akron-Canton Regional Airport is the closest international airport, located just 40 minutes from New Philadelphia. The Pittsburgh International Airport is also nearby, just an hour and fifteen minutes drive to the south.

Bus Stations Near New Philadelphia

The Greyhound Station in Canton, Ohio, is the nearest bus station, approximately half an hour away from New Philadelphia.

Other Options

A car is the most convenient way to explore New Philadelphia and its surroundings. Visitors can rent a car at the Akron-Canton Regional Airport or other closeby car rental companies.

How to Get Around in New Philadelphia

The best way to get around New Philadelphia is by car. Visitors can also use the local public transport, including taxis or ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft.

Conclusion

New Philadelphia, Ohio, has something for everyone, from history buffs and culture lovers to outdoor adventure enthusiasts and foodies.
