Introduction to Coshocton

Nestled in the heart of Ohio’s Amish Country, Coshocton is a small city in Southeast Ohio that offers a perfect getaway destination for those who are looking to explore the countryside. Coshocton is a historic city that boasts a rich heritage, which can be seen in its numerous museums, historical sites, and landmarks. From exploring the beautiful outdoors to discovering the local culture, there is no shortage of exciting things to do in Coshocton.

List of Places to Visit in Coshocton

Coshocton offers a wide range of attractions for visitors to explore. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Coshocton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coshocton County Historical Museum: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into the history of Coshocton County through various exhibits and artifacts.
  • Johnson-Humrickhouse Museum: This museum showcases the local culture, art, and history of Coshocton and its neighboring communities.
  • Roscoe Village: This restored canal-era town is a living history museum that takes visitors back in time to the early 1800s.
  • Historic Roscoe Village: This restored canal-era town features shops, restaurants, and museums that showcase the local arts and crafts.

Natural Attractions of Coshocton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Park: This 150-acre park is home to a beautiful lake, a golf course, and various hiking trails.
  • Clary Gardens: This botanical garden features a wide variety of flowers and plants and offers an idyllic setting for picnics, weddings, and other events.
  • Mohican State Park: This state park is just a short drive from Coshocton and offers visitors a chance to explore the serene wilderness of Ohio.
  • Amish Country: This region is known for its beautiful farmland, horse-drawn buggies, and simple way of life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coshocton

Coshocton is an excellent destination for hiking, biking, and other outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Three Rivers Bikeway: This 28-mile trail follows the path of the Muskingum, Tuscarawas, and Walhonding Rivers and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Ohio-Erie Canalway: This historic pathway follows the historic Ohio and Erie Canal and is an excellent route for hiking, biking, and exploring.
  • Conkle’s Hollow State Nature Preserve: This nature preserve features beautiful waterfalls, sandstone cliffs, and hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Coshocton: Activities and Tours

When in Coshocton, there are plenty of fun things to do. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Canal Boat Ride: Take a boat ride down the restored Ohio and Erie Canal and learn about the history of Coshocton and its canal system.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit some of the local wineries and sample the delicious wines that make Coshocton famous.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take to the skies and enjoy the beautiful views of Coshocton and its surrounding countryside.
  • Horseback Riding: Enjoy a horseback ride through the scenic Amish countryside and experience a unique way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coshocton

For those who are looking to spend a week in Coshocton, here is an itinerary that covers everything:

Day 1: Arrival and Coshocton City Tour

After checking in to your hotel, take a guided tour of Coshocton city and visit some of the local landmarks.

Day 2: Johnson-Humrickhouse Museum and Roscoe Village

Spend the day exploring the Johnson-Humrickhouse Museum and the restored canal-era town of Roscoe Village.

Day 3: Wine Tasting and Canalboat Ride

Visit some of the local wineries and enjoy a canal boat ride down the restored Ohio and Erie Canal.

Day 4: Conkle’s Hollow State Nature Preserve and Clary Gardens

Go for a hike at Conkle’s Hollow State Nature Preserve and visit the beautiful Clary Gardens.

Day 5: Amish Country Experience

Experience the unique culture and lifestyle of the Amish community by visiting the local shops and farms.

Day 6: Mohican State Park and Hot Air Balloon Ride

Spend the day exploring Mohican State Park and enjoy a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful Ohio countryside.

Day 7: Departure

Say goodbye to Coshocton and depart for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Coshocton

Coshocton is home to a vibrant food and drink scene that offers everything from comfort food to fine dining. Here are some must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amish-style fried chicken
  • Homemade apple butter
  • Baked beans cooked in molasses
  • Buckeye candy (peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate)

Local Beverages

  • Wines from Raven’s Glenn Winery
  • Mead from Rainbow Hills Winery
  • Craft beer from Wooly Pig Farm Brewery
  • Spiced apple cider from Keene Creek Farm Market

Where to Stay in Coshocton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Coshocton offers a variety of accommodations for visitors to choose from. Here are some of the best options:

  • Coshocton KOA: This campground features cabins, RV sites, and tent sites and is located just minutes away from Mohican State Park.

  • Handmade Amish quilts and crafts
  • Soy candles and soaps from Keene Creek Farm Market

Local Culture and Customs of Coshocton

Coshocton boasts a rich culture and heritage that is reflected in its local events and attractions. The Amish community in Coshocton is an integral part of the region’s culture, and visitors are welcomed to explore their way of life and learn about their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Coshocton

The best time to visit Coshocton is from May to October, when the weather is mild, and the region’s natural beauty is at its peak. The fall foliage is particularly beautiful in October when the colors are at their most vibrant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coshocton

Do:

  • Explore the local culture and heritage of Coshocton
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Take part in the outdoor activities and adventures
  • Respect the Amish community’s way of life

Don’t:

  • Disrupt the peaceful lifestyle of the Amish
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions

Getting to Coshocton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coshocton

The nearest commercial airport to Coshocton is the Akron-Canton Regional Airport, which is about 75 miles away. Other major airports in the region include the Columbus International Airport (approximately 80 miles away) and the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport (approximately 110 miles away).

Bus Stations Near Coshocton

Greyhound offers bus service to nearby cities, including Newark, Zanesville, and Canton.

Other Options

Taxis, car rentals, and shuttle services are also available in the region.

How to Get Around in Coshocton

The best way to get around Coshocton is by car or bike. There are several car rental agencies and bike rental shops in the region that offer rental services.

Where to Go Next from Coshocton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Coshocton is a small city with a big heart and plenty of exciting things for visitors to see and do.
