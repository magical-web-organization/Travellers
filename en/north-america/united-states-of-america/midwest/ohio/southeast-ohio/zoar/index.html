Introduction to Zoar

Zoar, a small historic village located in southeast Ohio, boasts an impressive collection of historic buildings, museums, and scenic outdoor activities. Named after the biblical story of Lot’s escape from the city of Sodom, Zoar was founded in 1817 by German immigrants who sought religious freedom. Today, the village offers visitors a glimpse into 19th-century life and a variety of outdoor and cultural activities.

List of Places to Visit in Zoar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zoar Village State Memorial: Get transported back in time as you explore the restored homes, gardens, and craft demonstrations of Zoar’s pioneers.
  • Bimeler Museum: Learn about the town’s early settlers and see their daily lives through historic artifacts and photographs.
  • Fort Laurens State Memorial: Discover Ohio’s only Revolutionary War fort and museum set on the banks of the Tuscarawas River.

Natural Attractions of Zoar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tuscarawas River: Go fishing, canoeing, or kayaking on the serene waters of the Tuscarawas River. With over 50 miles of river to explore, it’s the perfect place to enjoy the outdoors.
  • Zoar Valley Trail: Rent a bike or take a leisurely stroll along the former Ohio and Erie Canal towpath and enjoy the scenic views of the Tuscarawas River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zoar

  • Zoar Valley Trail: This 20-mile trail, perfect for hiking or biking, takes visitors through scenic views of the river and historic canal-era structures.
  • Canal Lands Trail: A more challenging trail, this 120-mile path through zoar includes hiking and biking and showcases the unique landscape of the area.

Best Things to Do in Zoar: Activities and Tours

  • Historic Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of Zoar and learn about the town’s fascinating history and culture.
  • Zoar Craftsmen’s Fair: Held annually in August, experience the region’s talented artisans and craftsmen.
  • Holiday Tours: Visit Zoar during the holiday season to take tours of the beautifully decorated homes and churches.

Food and Drink Scene of Zoar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bimeler’s Bakery: Satisfy your sweet tooth with a slice of shoofly pie, a Pennsylvania Dutch specialty with molasses and crumbly topping.
  • Canal Tavern of Zoar: Try the local favorite, the Zoar Sampler, a combination of potato soup, chicken salad, and homemade bread.
  • Zoar Harvest Kitchen: Sample the farm-to-table menu at this cozy restaurant, featuring dishes like apple and sausage stuffed pork tenderloin and cherry glazed chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Broad Run Cheesehouse: Sample local wines and cheeses, including Ohio’s signature grape, the Catawba.
  • Ohio Brewing Company: Take a tour of the brewery and enjoy a pint of their flagship beer, the Cardinal Ale.

Where to Stay in Zoar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Zoar School Inn Bed and Breakfast: Stay at this historic school-turned-bed-and-breakfast and enjoy the cozy guestrooms, homemade breakfast, and scenic views of the countryside.
  • The Cabins at Sugarcreek: For a more rustic experience, stay at one of the eight handcrafted log cabins on the banks of the tranquil Big Creek.

What to Buy in Zoar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Zoar Store and Visitors’ Center: Pick up gifts and souvenirs at this charming store, including handmade items, local honey, and antique tinware.
  • Broad Run Cheesehouse: Bring home a taste of Ohio with locally-produced cheeses and other specialty food items.

Local Culture and Customs of Zoar

German culture and heritage still play a significant role in Zoar, from its architecture and food scene, to its festivals and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Zoar

The best time to visit Zoar is between May and October, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The Zoar Harvest Festival is held in September and the Zoar Christmas Festival is held in December, both offering unique experiences in the village.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zoar

Do:

  • Do take guided tours and learn about the rich history of Zoar.
  • Do enjoy the outdoor activities, including biking, hiking, and kayaking.
  • Do sample the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for walking and hiking.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t miss out on the Zoar Harvest Festival or Christmas Festival.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zoar

Zoar is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should still take precautions while exploring the town and enjoying outdoor activities.

Health in Zoar

Visitors should take standard precautions to stay healthy, such as using sunscreen and bug spray while hiking and exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Zoar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zoar

  • Akron-Canton Regional Airport
  • Cleveland Hopkins International Airport

Bus Stations Near Zoar

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Ohio Coach Lines

Other Options

  • Car rental companies are available in both Akron and Cleveland.
  • Taxi and ride-sharing services are available.

How to Get Around in Zoar

Zoar is a small town that is easily walkable, but visitors can also rent bicycles or bring their own to explore the Zoar Valley Trail and Canal Lands Trail.

Where to Go Next from Zoar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Amish Country: About 20 minutes away from Zoar, explore the historic roots of Ohio’s Amish community.
  • Pro Football Hall of Fame: Located in Canton, Ohio, just a 30-minute drive from Zoar, visit the shrine of football history.
  • Cuyahoga Valley National Park: About an hour away from Zoar, enjoy the natural beauty of this park, including hikes, bike rides, and scenic rail trips.

Conclusion

Zoar, Ohio, offers visitors a unique mix of history and outdoor adventure. From touring the village’s historic buildings to biking along scenic trails, visitors are sure to create unforgettable memories. Sample the local cuisine, pick up some unique souvenirs, and explore the nearby attractions to fully experience all that Zoar has to offer.
