Columbus, Indiana, is a charming city located in the Midwest region of the United States. With its rich history and beautiful attractions, it’s become an ideal travel destination for tourists. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at Columbus and everything it has to offer.

Introduction to Columbus

Columbus has a population of approximately 44,000 people and covers 27 square miles. Known as the “Athens of the Prairie,” it has a rich history in architecture and art. You’ll find a variety of styles in Columbus, including modernism, postmodernism, and traditional architecture. The city offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Columbus

When planning your trip to Columbus, you’ll want to make sure you visit some of the city’s top attractions. Here are our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Columbus Museum of Art and Design - This museum features an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art.
  2. Bartholomew County Historical Society - A great opportunity to explore the history of Columbus and its surrounding county.
  3. The Miller House and Garden - This historic home is a prime example of mid-century modern architecture.

Natural Attractions of Columbus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mill Race Park - This park offers miles of trails for hiking and biking as well as beautiful views of White River.
  2. Parkside Playground - Perfect for families with young children, this park features various play areas and picnic spots.
  3. Brown County State Park - A short drive from Columbus, this park offers ample opportunities for camping, hiking, and horseback riding among the rolling hills and dense forest of one of Indiana’s most beloved natural areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbus

Columbus is a great place to lace up your hiking boots or hop on your bike and explore. Here are our top recommendations:

  1. People Trail - This 27-mile trail network will take you on a beautiful tour around Columbus, taking you through parks, neighborhoods and downtown areas.
  2. Mill Race Trail - A flat, paved trail that is perfect for biking or walking and goes alongside the beautiful White River.
  3. Sycamore Loop Trail - This hiking trail is a one-mile loop that leads you through a beautiful mixed hardwood forest.

Best Things to Do in Columbus: Activities and Tours

  1. Architecture tours - Take a guided tour and learn about the many architectural styles of Columbus.
  2. Columbus Food Tours - A fun and delicious way to explore the food scene in Columbus.
  3. Visit local wineries - Enjoy a day trip to the local wineries to sample some delicious Indiana wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbus

If you have a week to spend in Columbus, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Tour the city’s famous architecture
  • Day 2: Visit the Columbus Museum of Art and Design
  • Day 3: Explore Brown County State Park
  • Day 4: Take a food tour of Columbus
  • Day 5: Visit the Miller House and Garden
  • Day 6: Go hiking on the Mill Race Trail
  • Day 7: Spend the day at People Trail, enjoying the views of downtown Columbus.

Food and Drink Scene of Columbus

Columbus has a lively food and drink scene that features local ingredients and innovative cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried chicken and biscuits - A Southern comfort food staple. Visit Mississippi Belle to try their mouth-watering version.
  2. Shu Sho - A local sushi joint with a creative flair, Shu Sho offers unique rolls that feature local ingredients.
  3. Zaharakos - Visit this classic soda fountain to indulge in some old-style fountain treats.

Local Beverages

  1. Quarryland Beer - This craft brewery in Bloomington Indiana offers a range of unique draft beers, all brewed with local ingredients.
  2. Simmons Winery - A great spot for wine lovers, Simmons Winery offers tours and tastings of their fine Indiana wines.
  3. Upland Brewing Company - This Bloomington-based brewery offers a variety of beers from their brewpub.

Where to Stay in Columbus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Columbus has a wide variety of accommodation options suited for all types of travelers. Here are our top recommendations:

  1. The Hotel Indigo - A boutique hotel that offers a unique take on modern Indiana decor.
  2. The Yellowwood State Forest - This state park located near Columbus offers various camping options for those looking for a getaway in nature.
  3. Holiday Inn Express and Suites - Nestled in the heart of Columbus, this hotel offers easy access to the city’s attractions.

What to Buy in Columbus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Columbus, you’ll want to take home some unique souvenirs that capture the spirit of the city and the region. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  1. Kirchhoff’s Bakery - A Columbus staple, Kirchhoff’s offers some delicious baked goods that make for an excellent souvenir.
  2. Brown County Winery - Visit this winery to grab some of their fantastic Indiana wines.
  3. Pepsi Cola Bottling Company - Get your “retro fix” with the Pepsi memorabilia sold at their on-site store.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbus

When visiting Columbus, it’s important to be aware of the local customs and culture. Columbus is a friendly, hospitable community that values hard work and family. The city’s rich history of craftsmanship and artistic design is reflected in the local architecture.

Best Time to Visit Columbus

The best time to visit Columbus is during the fall, in late September or October. This is when the leaves change color and offer spectacular scenery. The weather is cooler than the summer but still pleasant enough to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbus

Do:

  1. Take a guided architecture tour of the city to learn about the unique styles featured in Columbus.
  2. Visit the Brown County State Park and enjoy the great outdoors.
  3. Attend one of the many annual events and festivals such as the Columbus Daffodil Show or the Mill Race Marathon.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to tip at restaurants.
  2. Don’t litter or leave trash in public places.
  3. Don’t make derogatory comments about local architecture or history.

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbus

Columbus is generally a safe city to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips to keep you safe:

  1. Be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.
  2. Keep your belongings secure and out of sight when walking in public places.
  3. Follow traffic rules when driving in the city.

Health in Columbus

There are several medical facilities in Columbus where you can access medical care. Here are some of the best healthcare facilities in the city:

  1. Columbus Regional Hospital - A full-service acute-care hospital that offers a range of medical services.
  2. Columbus Medical Center - A primary care facility that offers preventive care and treatment for common illnesses.
  3. Columbus Family Health Center - A family-oriented primary care facility that offers affordable care and family planning services.

Getting to Columbus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Columbus is easy to reach by car, bus, or air. Here are some of the transportation options available from nearby areas:

Airports Near Columbus

  1. Indianapolis International Airport - Approximately 50 miles from Columbus, this airport offers flights to major US and international destinations.
  2. Cincinnati International Airport - Approximately 100 miles from Columbus, this airport offers flights to major US and international destinations.
  3. Louisville International Airport - Approximately 120 miles from Columbus, this airport offers flights to major US and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Columbus

  1. Greyhound Bus Lines - Greyhound offers bus service to and from Columbus.
  2. Coach USA - This company operates various bus routes to and from Columbus.

Other Options

  1. You can reach Columbus by car via Interstate 65 and State Road 46.
  2. Taxis and ride share services, such as Uber and Lyft, are available in Columbus.

How to Get Around in Columbus

Columbus is a relatively small city, and it’s easy to navigate on foot or by bike. However, if you need to travel further, or the weather doesn’t permit outdoor activities, there are several transportation options available:

  1. Columbus Transit - This service operates fixed routes with schedules and maps that can be found on their website.
  2. Taxis - Local taxi services are available in Columbus.
  3. Renting a car - There are several car rental companies in Columbus, including National, Hertz, and Enterprise.

Where to Go Next from Columbus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Columbus is located in a beautiful area of Indiana, and there are plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Indianapolis - Visit the capital of Indiana, located less than an hour’s drive from Columbus, to see top attractions such as the Indianapolis Motor Speedway or the Indianapolis Museum of Art.
  2. Bloomington - Located about an hour’s drive from Columbus, this university town is known for its beautiful parks and university campus.
  3. Louisville - This romantic river city, located just over two hours drive from Columbus, offers great attractions like The Louisville Slugger Museum & Factory, historic Churchill Downs racetrack, and unique distilleries along the Bourbon Trail.

Conclusion

Columbus is a charming city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. With delicious food, fine wine, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure, it’s no wonder why Columbus has become a popular travel destination. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s architecture or just getting away in nature, Columbus has something for everyone.
