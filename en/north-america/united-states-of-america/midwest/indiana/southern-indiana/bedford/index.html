Introduction to Bedford

Located in Southern Indiana, Bedford is a charming town that offers an abundance of things to see and do. The town is known for its stunning limestone architecture, rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and friendly locals. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Bedford has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bedford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lawrence County Museum of History

The Lawrence County Museum of History is a must-visit destination for history buffs. The museum is housed in a beautiful limestone building that was once the local Carnegie Library. The exhibits feature artifacts and photographs that showcase the history of Lawrence County, Indiana, and the surrounding area.

John Hay Center

The John Hay Center is another highly recommended museum in Bedford. The center is composed of four historic buildings and features exhibits on Indiana history, local architecture, and the life of John Milton Hay, a famous American statesman.

Bluespring Caverns Park

Bluespring Caverns Park is a natural wonder that you can’t miss while in Bedford. The park features underground boat tours through the magnificent cavern system, where visitors can see amazing formations and underground waterfalls.

Natural Attractions of Bedford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spring Mill State Park

Spring Mill State Park is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park boasts beautiful hiking trails, a scenic lake that’s perfect for fishing and swimming, and a fully restored pioneer village where visitors can experience what life was like in the 1800s.

Hoosier National Forest

The Hoosier National Forest is a peaceful and scenic escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Visitors can enjoy hiking and camping in the forest’s lush greenery, or go fishing in the nearby streams and rivers.

McCormick’s Creek State Park

McCormick’s Creek State Park is Indiana’s first state park and is known for its magnificent waterfalls, hiking trails, and colorful foliage in the fall. The park also features a beautiful swimming pool, a nature center, and a fully furnished pioneer log cabin.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bedford

Milwaukee Trail

The Milwaukee Trail is a beautiful paved trail that is perfect for hiking, running, or biking. The trail is ideal for all levels of fitness, and it’s a great way to experience the beauty of Bedford’s natural scenery.

Knobstone Trail

The Knobstone Trail is Indiana’s longest backpacking trail, spanning over 58 miles. The trail is challenging and rewarding, offering hikers a chance to explore the rugged and remote backcountry of the Hoosier National Forest.

Best Things to Do in Bedford: Activities and Tours

Bluespring Caverns Park Boat Tour

The Bluespring Caverns Park Boat Tour is a unique and thrilling adventure that takes visitors through an underground river and cavern system. The tour is led by an experienced guide and is suitable for all ages.

Virgil I. Grissom Memorial

The Virgil I. Grissom Memorial is an important historical site that honors the memory of Virgil “Gus” Grissom, an astronaut who lost his life in the Apollo 1 tragedy. The memorial features exhibits and artifacts that showcase Grissom’s life and achievements.

Food and Drink Scene of Bedford

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Biscuits

Fried biscuits are a Southern classic and can be found in many restaurants around Bedford. These fluffy and buttery delights are perfect for breakfast or as a snack.

Persimmon Pudding

Persimmon pudding is a traditional Hoosier dessert made with persimmons, sugar, and spices. It’s a must-try for anyone visiting Bedford.

Local Beverages

Wine

Indiana is becoming famous for its wine production, and Bedford is no exception. There are several local vineyards that offer wine tasting tours and a chance to sample some of the best wines in the state.

Craft Beer

Bedford has a vibrant craft beer scene, with several local breweries that offer unique and delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Bedford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Legend of French Lick

The Legend of French Lick is a historic hotel that offers luxurious accommodations and top-notch amenities. The hotel is located on a sprawling estate and features beautiful gardens, a spa, and a restaurant.

Holiday Inn Express

The Holiday Inn Express in Bedford offers comfortable and affordable accommodations for travelers. The hotel features an indoor pool, a fitness center, and a free breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Bedford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Limestone Products

Bedford is known for its limestone architecture, and you can bring a piece of that history home with you. There are several local shops that offer limestone souvenirs, including bookends, coasters, and even miniature buildings.

Wine

Indiana produces some of the best wines in the country, and you can bring a taste of that home with you. Several local vineyards offer wine tasting tours and sell bottles of their best wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Bedford

Bedford is a friendly and welcoming town, with a strong sense of community. The locals are proud of their rich history and are always happy to share their knowledge with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Bedford

The best time to visit Bedford is in the fall, when the leaves are changing colors and the weather is crisp and cool. However, the town is beautiful year-round, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and cultural attractions throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bedford

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the natural beauty of the town and its surroundings

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas
  • Ignore the history and culture of the town
  • Be disrespectful to locals or other visitors

Safety and Health

Safety in Bedford

Bedford is a safe town, but visitors should still take precautions while exploring the area. Be aware of your surroundings, lock your car doors, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Bedford

The town has several healthcare facilities to take care of any health-related issues.

Getting to Bedford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bedford

The closest airport to Bedford is the Indianapolis International Airport, which is located about 80 miles away. Alternatively, the Louisville International Airport is located about 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Bedford

The Greyhound bus station in Bloomington, Indiana, is located about 25 miles away from Bedford.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bedford by car, with several major highways connecting the town to nearby cities and states.

How to Get Around in Bedford

The easiest way to get around Bedford is by car. However, visitors can also explore the town on foot or by bike, thanks to the many hiking and biking trails in the area.

Where to Go Next from Bedford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bloomington, Indiana

Bloomington is a vibrant college town that’s located about 25 miles away from Bedford. The town is known for its beautiful parks, cultural attractions, and delicious restaurants.

Louisville, Kentucky

Louisville is a bustling city that’s located about 100 miles away from Bedford. The city is known for its rich history, delicious bourbon, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Bedford is a charming town that offers something for everyone. From stunning natural scenery and exciting outdoor activities to fascinating historical sites and delicious cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this beautiful town. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty and hospitality of Bedford, Indiana.
