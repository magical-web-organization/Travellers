Introduction to Southern Indiana

Southern Indiana, situated in the Midwest region of the United States of America, is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, local cuisine, and small-town charms. The region is located south of Indianapolis, and is home to some of the state’s most scenic views, world-class museums, and historic landmarks. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Southern Indiana has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Indiana

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southern Indiana has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its museums and historical landmarks. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Indiana State Museum and Historic Sites: One of the best places to learn about the history and culture of Indiana, this museum features exhibits on everything from natural history to contemporary art.

  • The West Baden Springs Hotel: A historic hotel in French Lick that has been beautifully restored to its former glory. It is considered one of the most luxurious hotels in the world.

  • Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial: Located near Lincoln City, this memorial site offers a glimpse into the early life of Abraham Lincoln and his family.

  • Culbertson Mansion State Historic Site: A grand Victorian mansion in New Albany that provides a glimpse into the life of a wealthy family during the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Southern Indiana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Indiana offers plenty of outdoor adventures and natural beauty to be explored. Here are some of the best natural attractions:

  • Marengo Cave National Landmark: A series of underground caves that are home to a diverse array of geological formations, including stalagmites and stalactites.

  • Blue River: A popular destination for kayaking and canoeing, the Blue River is crystal clear and surrounded by beautiful forests.

  • Indiana Dunes National Park: Located on the shores of Lake Michigan, this park offers miles of sandy beaches, sand dunes, and hiking trails.

  • Brown County State Park: Indiana’s largest state park is known for its beautiful cabins, hiking trails, mountain biking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Indiana

Southern Indiana has a diverse range of hiking and biking trails that appeal to all skill levels and interests, all year round. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Knobstone Trail: The longest hiking trail in Indiana, Knobstone offers a challenging yet rewarding experience with stunning views of Southern Indiana’s hills and valleys.

  • B-Line Trail: A popular urban trail in Bloomington that winds through downtown and connects to several parks and other trails.

  • Buck Creek Trail: A great family-friendly trail in Indianapolis that features playgrounds, picnic areas, and beautiful scenic views.

  • Ohio River Greenway Trail: A trail that follows the Ohio River, offering breathtaking views of the water and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Southern Indiana: Activities and Tours

Southern Indiana offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours, from exploring museums and historic landmarks, to outdoor adventures and local festivals. Here are some of the best things to do in Southern Indiana:

  • Visit Holiday World and Splashing Safari: A popular amusement park in Santa Claus, Holiday World features thrilling rides, a water park, and live entertainment.

  • Explore Evansville Museum of Arts, History & Science: This museum showcases a variety of exhibits, including artwork, natural history, and space science.

  • Go on a Bourbon Trail Tour: Southern Indiana is home to several distilleries that produce some of the best bourbons around. Take a tour and learn about the history and process of bourbon-making.

  • Attend the Bloomington Farmers Market: Featuring locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and live entertainment, this market is a must-visit for any food or culture lover.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Indiana

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Southern Indiana:

Day 1: Visit the Indiana State Museum and Historic Sites, then head over to White River State Park for a picnic and a ride on the canal boats.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring Brown County State Park, including hiking trails and horseback riding.

Day 3: Take a tour of the West Baden Springs Hotel in French Lick, and visit the French Lick Winery for some wine tasting.

Day 4: Head to the Blue River for kayaking and canoeing.

Day 5: Explore the Marengo Cave National Landmark, then visit Patoka Lake Marina and rent a boat for the afternoon.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Louisville, Kentucky, and visit the Muhammad Ali Center and Kentucky Derby Museum.

Day 7: Attend a local festival or market, such as the Bloomington Farmers Market or the Jasper Strassenfest.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Indiana

No trip to Southern Indiana would be complete without experiencing the region’s delicious cuisine and local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Biscuits and Apple Butter: A classic Southern Indiana comfort food.

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: A must-try sandwich that features a breaded tenderloin served on a bun with lettuce, tomato, and pickle.

  • Sugar Cream Pie: A rich and creamy pie made from a custard-like filling and a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Indiana Wine: Southern Indiana is home to several wineries, offering a variety of reds, whites, and fruit wines.

  • Schnapps: A distilled spirit that is commonly served in Southern Indiana, with flavors ranging from peach to apple.

Where to Stay in Southern Indiana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Indiana offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • French Lick Springs Hotel: A historic luxury hotel that features a world-class spa, casino, and golf course.

  • Bartle House Bed and Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast located in Batesville that offers comfortable rooms and delicious breakfast.

  • Spring Mill Inn: A rustic lodge located in Mitchell that overlooks the beautiful Spring Mill State Park.

What to Buy in Southern Indiana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southern Indiana is home to plenty of unique and locally made products that make perfect souvenirs. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Handmade Pottery: Southern Indiana is known for its handmade pottery, which can be purchased at local artisan shops.

  • Jewelry: Local jewelry designers offer unique and beautiful pieces that are perfect for souvenirs or gifts.

  • Home Decor: The region is home to several shops that sell furniture and home decor pieces made from local materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Indiana

Southern Indiana’s culture is very influenced by its history and natural scenery. The locals are proud of their heritage and are known for their friendliness and hospitality. Visitors should embrace Southern Indiana’s laid-back lifestyle and appreciate its unique charm.

Best Time to Visit Southern Indiana

Southern Indiana can be enjoyed all year round, with each season offering its own unique experience. Summer is the busiest time and offers perfect weather for outdoor activities. Fall is the most beautiful time, with vibrant colors and cool temperatures. Winter is perfect for cozy indoor activities and snow sports, while spring offers the start of the flower and tree blooming season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Indiana

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Southern Indiana:

Do:

  • Embrace the laid-back lifestyle and take time to enjoy your surroundings.

  • Explore Southern Indiana’s natural scenery and outdoor activities.

  • Sample the region’s local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

  • Be rude or impolite to locals.

Safety and Health

Southern Indiana is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities and be aware of any potential risks. As with any travel destination, it is important to keep safety in mind and take necessary precautions. In terms of health, visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations and protect themselves against tick bites, as tick-borne illnesses are common in the region.

Getting to Southern Indiana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southern Indiana is accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Southern Indiana

The nearest airports to Southern Indiana are:

  • Louisville International Airport: Located approximately an hour away from Southern Indiana.

  • Indianapolis International Airport: Located about two hours away from Southern Indiana.

Bus Stations Near Southern Indiana

  • Greyhound Lines: Several bus routes connect major cities in Southern Indiana to other parts of the country.

  • Amtrak: A train station is located in Indianapolis, connecting to other major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at both Indianapolis and Louisville airports, as well as in several cities in Southern Indiana.

How to Get Around in Southern Indiana

The best way to get around Southern Indiana is by car. However, visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and shuttles. Several major cities in Southern Indiana, like Bloomington, offer bike-share programs and bike rentals for easy exploration of the local areas.

Where to Go Next from Southern Indiana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southern Indiana is surrounded by a wealth of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some suggestions:

  • Louisville, Kentucky: Visit the Kentucky Derby Museum, Muhammad Ali Center, and Churchill Downs, home of the Kentucky Derby.

  • Nashville, Indiana: Known for its art galleries, antiques shops, and scenic beauty, this charming town is located only an hour away from Southern Indiana.

  • Cincinnati, Ohio: Home to some of Ohio’s best museums, a world-class zoo, and beautiful parks.

Conclusion

Southern Indiana offers an array of exciting destinations, breathtaking attractions, and natural beauty that offer a unique experience to visitors. With a rich cultural heritage, friendly locals, and charming small towns, Southern Indiana is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a one-of-a-kind adventure.
