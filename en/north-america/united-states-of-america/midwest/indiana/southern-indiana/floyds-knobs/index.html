Introduction to Floyds Knobs

Floyds Knobs is a small, affluent town located in the scenic hills of Southern Indiana. Its picturesque landscape, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage make it an idyllic travel destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture seekers alike. Whether you’re looking for a serene escape from the city’s bustle or an adventurous trip in nature, Floyds Knobs has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Floyds Knobs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Floyds Knobs Historical Society and Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Floyds Knobs and surrounding areas through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.

  • Preston Arts Center: Experience the local arts scene by visiting the Preston Arts Center, located on the campus of Floyd Central High School. The center hosts concerts, theatrical productions, and art exhibits throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Floyds Knobs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Knobs State Forest: Home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, Knobs State Forest is a great place for hiking, camping, birdwatching, and fishing.

  • Squire Boone Caverns: Explore the underground world of Squire Boone Caverns, with its stunning limestone formations, underground streams, and underground waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Floyds Knobs

Floyds Knobs is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular hiking and biking trails are:

  • Floyds Knobs Loop Trail: This 6.5-mile trail is a great option for seasoned hikers, as it is rated as a moderate-difficult trail.

  • Harmony Trail: A relatively easy trail, this one-mile loop trail is great for beginners and families with kids.

Best Things to Do in Floyds Knobs: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic trails of Floyds Knobs on horseback with Horse Camp or Elk Creek Riding Stables.

  • Wine Tasting: Sip and savor the local wines at the Turtle Run Winery, which offers wine tastings and tours of its vineyards and production facilities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Floyds Knobs

Day 1: Visit the Floyds Knobs Historical Society and Museum, followed by a scenic hike in Knobs State Forest.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of Squire Boone Caverns, followed by a visit to Turtle Run Winery for some wine tasting.

Day 3: Explore the local arts scene by attending a concert or theatrical performance at the Preston Arts Center.

Day 4: Horseback riding at Horse Camp or Elk Creek Riding Stables.

Day 5: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Harmony Trail, followed by a picnic at the park.

Day 6: Visit the downtown area for some shopping and souvenir hunting.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local scenery, or take a day trip to nearby attractions and destinations.
