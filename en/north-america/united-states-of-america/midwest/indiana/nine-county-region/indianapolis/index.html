Introduction to Indianapolis

Located in the Nine-County Region of Indiana, Indianapolis is a vibrant and exciting city that offers something for everyone. Known for its world-famous speedway, the city is the perfect destination for motorsports enthusiasts. But there is much more to Indianapolis than just racing. The city has a rich cultural heritage, amazing food and drink scene, and plenty of outdoor adventures to enjoy.

Whether you’re stopping in Indianapolis for a weekend or planning to stay for an entire week, this travel guide has all the information you need to plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Indianapolis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Indianapolis has a rich cultural heritage, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore. Here are some of the must-visit sites:

  • Indianapolis Zoo and White River Gardens: A popular attraction for families, the Indianapolis Zoo is home to over 1,400 animals from around the world. The White River Gardens, located on the zoo grounds, are a beautiful botanical garden that is perfect for a relaxing stroll.
  • Indiana State Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Indiana. It features exhibits on the state’s natural history, as well as its arts and culture.
  • Eiteljorg Museum of American Indians and Western Art: This museum is home to an extensive collection of Native American art, as well as art and artifacts from the American West.
  • Indiana War Memorial Museum: This museum pays tribute to the men and women from Indiana who have served in the military.

Natural Attractions of Indianapolis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, there are plenty of natural attractions to explore in and around Indianapolis. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Eagle Creek Park: This massive park covers over 1,400 acres and is home to a large reservoir, as well as hiking trails, picnic areas, and a high ropes course.
  • Indianapolis Canal Walk: Located in downtown Indianapolis, the Canal Walk is a peaceful and scenic path along the canal that is perfect for a relaxing stroll.
  • White River State Park: This park is home to several museums and attractions, including the Indiana State Museum and the Eiteljorg Museum, as well as the Indianapolis Zoo and the White River Gardens.
  • The Indianapolis Cultural Trail: This eight-mile paved trail runs through downtown Indianapolis and connects several neighborhoods. It’s perfect for cycling or a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Indianapolis

For those who enjoy hiking or cycling, there are numerous trails and paths to explore in and around Indianapolis. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Monon Trail: This 26-mile trail runs from downtown Indianapolis all the way to Carmel, and is a popular route for both runners and cyclists.
  • The Indianapolis Cultural Trail: As mentioned earlier, this trail meanders through downtown Indianapolis and connects several neighborhoods.
  • Eagle Creek Park: In addition to the activities mentioned earlier, Eagle Creek Park is also home to several hiking trails of varying lengths and difficulties.
  • Central Canal Towpath: This six-mile trail runs alongside the canal and offers scenic views of the waterway and several downtown landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Indianapolis: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions mentioned earlier, there are plenty of other things to see and do in Indianapolis. Here are some popular activities and tours:

  • Indianapolis Motor Speedway: Of course, no trip to Indianapolis would be complete without a visit to the famous speedway. Take a tour of the track and check out the Hall of Fame Museum.
  • Lucas Oil Stadium Tour: Sports fans will also want to check out this tour of the home of the NFL’s Indianapolis Colts.
  • Indianapolis Symphony Orchestra: The ISO is world-renowned for their performances, and offers a diverse range of concerts throughout the year.
  • Segway Tours: Take a guided Segway tour of downtown Indianapolis and see all the sights while learning about the history of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Indianapolis

If you’re planning to spend a week in Indianapolis, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore downtown Indianapolis, taking in the sights of the Canal Walk and the Indiana State Museum.
  • Day 2: Visit the Indianapolis Zoo and White River Gardens.
  • Day 3: Take a guided Segway tour of downtown Indianapolis.
  • Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Eagle Creek Park.
  • Day 5: Visit the Eiteljorg Museum of American Indians and Western Art.
  • Day 6: Catch a performance by the Indianapolis Symphony Orchestra.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Bloomington and explore the Indiana University campus.

Food and Drink Scene of Indianapolis

Must-Try Dishes

Indianapolis is known for its delicious comfort food, including:

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: This Indiana staple features a thick, breaded pork cutlet served on a bun with lettuce, tomato, and mayo.
  • Sugar Cream Pie: Also known as “Hoosier Pie,” this dessert features a creamy, custard-like filling and a flaky crust.
  • Fried Chicken: There are plenty of great places in Indianapolis to get delicious fried chicken, but some of the best are Kountry Kitchen Soul Food Place, Hollyhock Hill, and Maxine’s Chicken & Waffles.

Local Beverages

Indiana has a thriving craft beer scene, and Indianapolis is no exception. Some of the best breweries to check out include:

  • Sun King Brewing: This brewery is one of the largest in the state and offers a wide variety of beers, including seasonal and limited edition releases.
  • TwoDEEP Brewing Co.: Located in downtown Indianapolis, TwoDEEP offers a small but impressive lineup of beers.
  • Metazoa Brewing Co.: This brewery donates a portion of their profits to animal welfare organizations and has a spacious taproom that is perfect for hanging out with friends.

Where to Stay in Indianapolis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of great places to stay in Indianapolis, no matter your budget. Here are some options:

  • The Alexander: This sleek, modern hotel is located in the heart of downtown Indianapolis and offers a wide range of amenities, including a fitness center and rooftop bar.
  • JW Marriott Indianapolis: This luxury hotel features spacious rooms with stunning views, as well as a spa and a wide range of dining options.
  • HI Indianapolis Hostel: If you’re on a budget, this hostel offers affordable dormitory-style rooms and private rooms in a great location.
  • The Nestle Inn: This charming guesthouse is located in the historic Chatham Arch neighborhood and offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast in the morning.

What to Buy in Indianapolis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, here are some local products to check out:

  • Indianapolis Motor Speedway Memorabilia: There are plenty of shops around the speedway that offer t-shirts, hats, and other gear featuring logos and designs from the famous track.
  • Local Artisan Products: Indianapolis has a thriving artisan scene, and you can find a wide range of handmade goods, including pottery, jewelry, and clothing.
  • Indiana Wine: There are several wineries located in and around Indianapolis that offer delicious locally-made wine, including Oliver Winery and Mallow Run Winery.

Local Culture and Customs of Indianapolis

Hoosiers, as people from Indiana are known, are friendly and welcoming. Indianapolis is a diverse city, and residents are proud of the city’s cultural heritage and traditions. It’s common for people from Indianapolis to hold doors open for others, say “please” and “thank you” often, and strike up friendly conversations with strangers.

Best Time to Visit Indianapolis

The best time to visit Indianapolis is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor events and festivals taking place. However, summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy, so be sure to pack accordingly if you’re visiting during those seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Indianapolis

Do:

  • Do explore downtown Indianapolis and the beautiful Canal Walk.
  • Do try some of the local comfort foods, like the pork tenderloin sandwich or sugar cream pie.
  • Do check out the Indianapolis Motor Speedway and take a tour of the track.
  • Do take advantage of the city’s many outdoor attractions and natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to leave a tip- tipping is customary in Indianapolis.
  • Don’t litter- Indianapolis is a clean, well-maintained city, and residents take pride in keeping it that way.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions- Hoosiers are proud of their heritage and culture, and by respecting it, visitors can have a more authentic experience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Indianapolis

Indianapolis is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid being targeted by crime. It’s best to avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas, and to keep valuables out of sight. Areas to avoid are divided by a diagonal line that many locals call the “Red Line”. Tourists are advised to avoid neighborhoods to the North and East of the diagonal line.

Health in Indianapolis

For those in need of health care, there are several hospitals in the city, including the Indiana University Health Hospital and the St. Vincent Indianapolis Hospital. Visitors are advised to have health insurance in case of an emergency.

Getting to Indianapolis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Indianapolis

The Indianapolis International Airport is located just 11 miles southwest of downtown Indianapolis and offers non-stop flights to several major U.S. cities, as well as several international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Indianapolis

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown Indianapolis, making it easy to travel to and from the city by bus.

Other Options

For those driving to Indianapolis, several major highways, including I-65 and I-70, run through the city. Amtrak also offers train service to Indianapolis.

How to Get Around in Indianapolis

There are several transportation options in Indianapolis, including:

  • IndyGo Bus System: The IndyGo bus system operates throughout the city and offers an affordable way to get around.
  • Ride-Sharing Services: Uber and Lyft are both available in Indianapolis.
  • Bicycle Sharing: Pacers Bikeshare offers bike rental stations throughout the city.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate out of the Indianapolis International Airport and throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Indianapolis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Indiana, there are several nearby destinations to consider:

  • Bloomington: This charming college town is located just an hour’s drive south of Indianapolis and is home to Indiana University.
  • Southern Indiana: The rolling hills and river valleys of southern Indiana offer breathtaking natural beauty, as well as charming small towns to explore.
  • Chicago: If you’re looking for a larger city, Chicago is located just a few hours north of Indianapolis and is easily accessible by car or train.

Conclusion

Indianapolis is a unique and exciting city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or just great food and drink, you’re sure to find plenty of options here. Use this travel guide to plan your trip to Indianapolis and experience all that this vibrant city has to offer.
