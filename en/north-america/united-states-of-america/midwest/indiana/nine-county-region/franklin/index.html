Introduction to Franklin

Nestled in the heart of the Nine-County Region in Indiana, Franklin is a charming town brimming with historic landmarks, natural beauty, and a vibrant community. Known for its rich history and cultural significance, Franklin offers a plethora of activities, attractions, and events for visitors to enjoy.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll showcase the best things to do in Franklin, including a list of places to visit, must-try dishes, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, how to get around, and more. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Franklin.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Franklin boasts a wealth of significant historical and cultural sites that visitors should not miss. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Johnson County Museum of History: Learn about the rich history of Johnson County through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • The Historic Artcraft Theatre: Step back in time with a visit to this restored historic movie theater, featuring classic films and live events.
  • Utzman-Chambers House Museum: Explore this 1868 Victorian-style house that showcases the life of a prosperous farmer in the 19th century.
  • The Barn at Crystal Spring Farm: Attend an event or wedding at this restored 1930s dairy barn.

Natural Attractions of Franklin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure and breathtaking views, Franklin has got you covered. Here are some of the best natural attractions you won’t want to miss:

  • Franklin Greenway Trail: Enjoy a leisurely walk or bike ride along this scenic 2.5-mile trail that winds through forests and parks.
  • Morgan-Monroe State Forest: Hike or bike through the rolling hills and picturesque landscapes of this beautiful state forest.
  • Blue River Memorial Park: Go fishing in the Blue River, have a picnic, or take a stroll through the park’s gardens and trails.
  • The Historic Whiteland Cemetery: Visit this scenic cemetery and stroll through the beautiful gardens and grave markers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin

Franklin is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Franklin Greenway Trail: This 2.5-mile paved path is perfect for a relaxing walk or bike ride.
  • The Pennsy Trail: Stretching 11 miles, this converted railroad track offers a scenic route for both hiking and biking.
  • Morgan-Monroe State Forest Trail System: This extensive network of trails offers challenging hikes and scenic routes for mountain bikers and hikers.

Best Things to Do in Franklin: Activities and Tours

Franklin offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Antiquing: With over a dozen antique shops, Franklin is a paradise for vintage lovers.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Visit local Indiana wineries such as Mallow Run and Buck Creek for wine tastings, tours, and events.
  • Johnson County Fairgrounds: Attend the annual Johnson County Fair for carnival rides, livestock shows, concerts, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin

Must-Try Dishes

If you’re a foodie, Franklin has plenty of delicious bites to offer. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Fried Chicken: Try this southern classic at several local restaurants in Franklin, including Kopper Kettle Inn and Brozinni Pizzeria.
  • Burgers and Fries: Grindstone Public House serves up mouth-watering burgers and fries in a cozy, rustic atmosphere.
  • Pie: Enjoy fresh, homemade pies at The Sugary.

Local Beverages

In addition to its food scene, Franklin also has a thriving craft brewery scene. Here are some of the best local breweries and drink options.

  • Hoosier Brewing Company: Enjoy cold brews and food trucks at this local craft brewery.
  • Shale Creek Brewing: Tucked away in an historic barn, this microbrewery offers an abundance of hand-crafted beer.
  • Mallow Run Winery: Take in the beautiful landscapes while savoring a glass of locally-made wine.

Where to Stay in Franklin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Franklin has an abundance of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hotels to high-end resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Holiday Inn Express: This affordable hotel offers modern amenities and a convenient location near downtown Franklin.
  • The Flying Frog Bed and Breakfast: Stay in historic charm at this B&B housed in a 1800s gristmill.
  • The Alexander Hotel: This upscale boutique hotel offers luxurious amenities and a prime downtown location.

What to Buy in Franklin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, Franklin has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Hand-Crafted Pottery and Glass Art: Visit the Hoosier Hot Glass Studio and Artcraft Theatre Center to find exquisite handmade pottery and glass art.
  • Vintage Clothing and Home Décor: Antiquing is a popular pastime in Franklin, with several shops offering vintage and antique clothing, furniture, and home décor.
  • Local Wines and Beers: Bring home a sample of the local brewing scene with beer and wine from Hoosier Brewing Company and Mallow Run Winery.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin

Franklin is a friendly and welcoming community that values its rich history and culture. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and customs when visiting. Here are some tips:

  • Always greet people with a smile and a friendly hello.
  • Respect local landmarks and historical sites by obeying rules and regulations.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Franklin

The best time to visit Franklin is during the summer months when the weather is warm and pleasant. This is also the peak tourist season, so expect larger crowds and longer wait times for popular attractions. The fall months are also a great time to visit, with cooler weather and colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin

Do:

  • Visit the local historical landmarks and museums.
  • Go hiking or biking on one of the scenic trails.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or deface historical sites and landmarks.
  • Leave valuables unattended in your car or in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin

Franklin is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should still exercise caution when exploring the town. Here are some tips to stay safe:

  • Lock your car and always keep valuable items out of sight.
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night.
  • Always wear a helmet when biking or hiking in Franklin’s outdoor areas.

Health in Franklin

Franklin has several hospitals and medical centers for visitors in need of health services. There are also pharmacies and drug stores for basic supplies and medications.

Getting to Franklin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Franklin is easily accessible by car, and is located near several major highways. Here are some transport options to help you get here:

Airports Near Franklin

  • Indianapolis International Airport (IND) – 30 miles away
  • Greenwood Municipal Airport (HFY) – 2.5 miles away

Bus Stations Near Franklin

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Mini Bus Charter

Other Options

  • Car rentals

How to Get Around in Franklin

While most attractions in Franklin are accessible by car, the town also offers a public bus system for visitors on a budget. Private taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Franklin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Southern Indiana, here are some nearby destinations that should be on your list:

  • The Children’s Museum of Indianapolis
  • The Indianapolis Zoo
  • Brown County State Park

Conclusion

From its rich history to its stunning natural vistas, Franklin is a must-visit destination in Southern Indiana. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, there’s plenty to explore in this charming town.
