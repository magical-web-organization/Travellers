Introduction to Anderson

Anderson, a city in the Nine-County Region of Indiana, is a great travel destination for those looking to experience both outdoor adventures and historical attractions. With a mix of museums, scenic views, hiking/biking routes, and plenty of local shops and restaurants, Anderson offers something for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Anderson

If you’re planning a trip to Anderson, here are some of the top places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Madison County Historical Society: Explore the artifacts, photographs, and exhibits related to Madison County and its history at the Madison County Historical Society Museum.
  • Hoosier Park Racing & Casino: Place a few bets, enjoy some food and drinks, and have some fun! At Hoosier Park Racing & Casino, you can relax in a friendly atmosphere and try your luck on the slots, tables, or even bet on horse racing.
  • Anderson Center for the Arts: View the diverse collections of artwork by local artists at the Anderson Center for the Arts, which features 11 galleries.

Natural Attractions of Anderson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mounds State Park: Hiking trails through this beautiful park lead visitors to the mounds — ancient earthworks created by the Adena-Hopewell people more than 2,000 years ago.
  • White River Greenway Trail: Located on the banks of White River, this scenic trail offers around 6 miles of pedestrian and bike paths, picnicking, playgrounds, and even fishing opportunities.
  • Shadyside Memorial Park: A combination of both modern amusements and historical remnants, this park is home to a nationally recognized circus heritage museum, a community pool, and several athletic fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anderson

For outdoor enthusiasts, Anderson offers many hiking and biking trails to enjoy. Here are some top routes to choose from:

  • Cardinal Greenway: The longest rail-trail in Indiana, Cardinal Greenway provides 62 miles of paved trail to explore by foot or bike.
  • Mounds State Park Trail: A hiking trail that leads up to three different mounds: the Great Mound, the Fiddleback Mound, and the Henge.
  • White River Greenway Trail: An easy walk or bike ride through a scenic route, this trail is perfect for families and introductory riders, alike.

Best Things to Do in Anderson: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums and outdoor activities, Anderson also offers some unique experiences to include in your itinerary:

  • The Paramount Theatre Centre and Ballroom: This restored 1929 theater, with its stunning marble staircase and intricate plasterwork, hosts live performances and movies.
  • Society of Salvage: Located inside the historic Hoosier Drill Company, this shop is full of unique antiques, architectural salvage, and vintage goods.
  • Blue’s Canoe Livery: Rent a canoe or kayak and enjoy a peaceful float down the White River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anderson

If you’re spending a week in Anderson, here is a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Madison County Historical Society Museum and the Anderson Center for the Arts.
Day 2 Explore Mounds State Park and dine at Gaither Family Resource Center.
Day 3 Spend the day at Hoosier Park Racing & Casino followed by cocktails at The Edge.
Day 4 Hike or bike the Cardinal Greenway and eat at The Lemon Drop.
Day 5 Enjoy scenic views and picnicking at Shadyside Memorial Park.
Day 6 Visit Society of Salvage and shop at Anderson Farmers Market.
Day 7 Rent a canoe or kayak at Blue’s Canoe Livery and explore the White River.

Food and Drink Scene of Anderson

Anderson’s food and drink scene is a mix of chains and unique local eateries. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Euchre’s Cafe’s Breaded Pork Tenderloin Sandwich
  • Bela’s Chorizo Tacos
  • Lemon Drop’s Danish Meatballs

Local Beverages

  • Austin Bros. Beer Co.: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beer styles, from hazy IPAs to rich stouts.
  • Ruhe152 Brewery: A fun and casual brewery featuring a comfortable taproom and outdoor area, with an assortment of beers brewed on-site.

Where to Stay in Anderson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anderson has several lodging options to choose from, from affordable hotels to luxury resorts. Here are some suggestions:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: This comfortable hotel offers an indoor pool, free Wi-Fi and a fitness center.
  • Hampton Inn Anderson: Breakfast is included at this modern hotel that features a fitness center and a pool.
  • Quality Inn & Suites Anderson: With amenities like free hot breakfast, an indoor pool, and free parking, this hotel is both affordable and comfortable.

What to Buy in Anderson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For souvenir shopping, here are some great suggestions:

  • Anderson Farmers Market: This farmers market features more than just produce, with a variety of homemade goods from local vendors.
  • Boutique Unique: With a fun and eclectic mix of vintage and modern items, this shop is the perfect place to pick up unique gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Anderson

Anderson residents take pride in their Midwestern hospitality and kind, friendly demeanor. They are known for their love of sports, particularly basketball. Hoosier Hysteria is alive and well in Anderson.

Best Time to Visit Anderson

The best time to visit Anderson is during the summer months, from June to August, when you can enjoy beautiful weather and outdoor activities. However, if you prefer fewer crowds and lower prices, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of spring (March through May) or fall (September through October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Anderson

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and hiking trails.
  • Visit museums to learn about the rich history of Anderson.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beers.

Don’t:

  • Litter in parks, trails, or other public spaces.
  • Disrespect locals by stereotyping or making fun of their Midwestern culture.
  • Eat at chain restaurants only.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anderson

Anderson is a relatively safe destination, but as with any city, it is important to take basic safety precautions. Do not leave your belongings unattended in public spaces and be aware of your surroundings at night, particularly in less-populated areas.

Health in Anderson

Anderson offers a variety of health care clinics and providers, including large medical centers and smaller family clinics. It is always a good idea to have health insurance when traveling.

Getting to Anderson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anderson

  • Indianapolis International Airport (IND) is 47 miles southwest of Anderson.
  • Dayton International Airport (DAY) is 65 miles east of Anderson.

Bus Stations Near Anderson

  • Greyhound Lines located in Indianapolis offers daily trips to Anderson.
  • Hoosier Ride offers a daily trip from Indianapolis to Anderson.

Other Options

  • Taxi and limousine services are available from both airports and bus stations to Anderson.
  • Uber and Lyft operate in Anderson.

How to Get Around in Anderson

Anderson is a walkable city with plenty of bike trails. Buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services are available for those who need transportation outside of walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Anderson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anderson’s location in the Midwest makes it a great hub to explore nearby cities and destinations. Here are some suggestions:

  • Indianapolis, Indiana (45 minutes away)
  • Dayton, Ohio (1 hour away)
  • Fort Wayne, Indiana (1.5 hours away)
  • Cincinnati, Ohio (2 hours away)

Conclusion

Anderson offers a wonderful mix of outdoor adventures and culturally significant attractions, making it a great destination for all kinds of travelers. Be sure to visit its scenic parks, enjoy its unique food and drink, and learn about the area’s rich history. We hope this tour guide to Anderson has been helpful in planning your trip!
