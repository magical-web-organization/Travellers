Introduction to Brownsburg

Brownsburg is one of the best kept secrets of Indiana’s heritage. Located in Nine-County Region, in the Midwest of the United States of America, Brownsburg is famous for its exceptional museums, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and exciting food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Brownsburg

No matter what your interests are, Brownsburg has something to suit everyone. Here are some of the top attractions that this charming city has to offer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Brownsburg Town Hall Museum - A 150-year-old heritage building turned museum displaying artifacts and items from Brownsburg history.

  2. Thomas A. Hendricks Monument and Gravesite - A historical monument and gravesite of the US’s 21st Vice President.

  3. Old Friends Cemetery - A historical cemetery that dates back to the early 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Brownsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Williams Park - A family-friendly park that has a beautiful pond, nature trails, and a play area.

  2. Brownsburg Parks and Recreation - Enjoy some of the city’s outdoor adventures via a picnic or hike.

  3. Arbuckle Acres Park - A park that offers scenic views, playgrounds, hiking trails, and a serene atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brownsburg

  1. B&O Trail - A beautiful route that stretches beyond Hendricks County and connects to existing trails across the state.

  2. Eagle Creek Trail - A 6.4 mile-long paved trail that’s suitable for hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts, surrounded by scenic waterways and picturesque bridges.

  3. Vandalia Trail - A 6-mile long trail whose railway history has been restored and now exists as a peaceful and calm biking and hiking trail.

Best Things to Do in Brownsburg: Activities and Tours

  1. Hendricks Country Flyer Train Excursions - A vintage train attraction that offers a coach class, senior class, and dinner trains.

  2. Rolls-Royce Heritage Trust Allison Branch - A museum that offers a tour of the history and time of Rolls-Royce aircraft engines.

  3. Brownsburg Bowl - A friendly bowling alley that offers open lanes, birthday parties, and good food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brownsburg

  • Day 1: Check-in to your Hotel and head to Brownsburg Town Hall Museum, and end your night with dinner at one of the city’s popular eateries.

  • Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking to explore the scenic views of Brownsburg like Eagle Creek Trail or B&O Trail.

  • Day 3: Explore the cultural heritage of Brownsburg visiting Thomas A. Hendricks Monument and Gravesite or Old Friends Cemetery.

  • Day 4: Visit Arbuckle Acres Park and enjoy a picnic with your family and head to Hendricks County Flyer Train Excursions for a vintage experience.

  • Day 5: Take the day out to explore the Stephens Park Skating facility and participate in some fun activities.

  • Day 6: Visit Rolls-Royce Heritage Trust Allison Branch, and witness the history and exhibits of the Rolls-Royce aircraft engines.

  • Day 7: End your week’s trip with some shopping and dining at one of Brownsburg’s famous stores and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Brownsburg

  2. Fried chicken sandwich - A juicy and crispy sandwich that’s worth trying at least once!

  3. Indiana style pizza - A specialty pizza made in Brownsburg that is served with a cracker-thin crust and toppings.

Local Beverages

  2. Blackhawk Winery and Vineyard - A vineyard that’s perfect for wine lovers and wine tours.
