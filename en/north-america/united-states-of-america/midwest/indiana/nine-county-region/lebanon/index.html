Introduction to Lebanon

Located in the Nine-County Region of Indiana, Lebanon is a charming city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Lebanon has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Lebanon.

List of Places to Visit in Lebanon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lebanon has a rich history that is reflected in its numerous museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • Boone County Historical Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the history of Boone County through interactive exhibits and artifacts.

  • Ulen Country Club and Mansion: A historic mansion built in 1924 that now serves as a private country club. The mansion is open for tours by appointment only.

  • Thorntown Heritage Museum: A small museum in the nearby town of Thorntown that displays artifacts and exhibits related to the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Lebanon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lebanon is home to several beautiful parks, natural preserves, and hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Some of the best natural attractions in Lebanon include:

  • Lebanon Memorial Park: A beautiful park with a playground, picnic areas, and a splash pad during the summer months.

  • Cedar Creek Winery: A scenic winery that offers tours and wine tastings in a beautiful setting.

  • Abner Longley Park: A peaceful park with a pond, hiking trails, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lebanon

If you enjoy hiking or biking, Lebanon has a variety of scenic trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Some of the best trails in Lebanon include:

  • Big 4 Rail Trail: A 6.5-mile trail that takes you through Lebanon and connects to nearby towns.

  • Prairie Grass Trail: A 5.7-mile trail that takes you through beautiful prairies and wetlands.

  • Sugar Creek Trail: A 12-mile trail that follows the scenic Sugar Creek through Boone County.

Best Things to Do in Lebanon: Activities and Tours

Lebanon offers a variety of activities and tours that are sure to keep you entertained during your visit. Some of the best things to do in Lebanon include:

  • Lebanon Grand 7 Theater: Catch a movie at this historic theater that has been in operation since 1929.

  • Lebanon Farmers Market: Shop for fresh produce, artisanal goods, and handmade crafts at the Lebanon Farmers Market during the summer months.

  • Melody Drive-In Theater: Catch a movie under the stars at this drive-in theater that has been in operation since 1949.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lebanon

For those planning a week-long trip to Lebanon, we recommend the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Boone County Historical Museum and explore the downtown area.

  • Day 2: Hike the Sugar Creek Trail and enjoy a picnic at Abner Longley Park.

  • Day 3: Take a scenic drive through the countryside and stop by Cedar Creek Winery for a wine tasting.

  • Day 4: Visit the nearby town of Thorntown and explore the Thorntown Heritage Museum.

  • Day 5: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Big 4 Rail Trail and grab lunch at one of the trailside cafes.

  • Day 6: Visit the Ulen Country Club and Mansion for a tour and play a round of golf.

  • Day 7: Catch a movie at the historic Lebanon Grand 7 Theater and enjoy dinner at one of the downtown restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Lebanon

Lebanon has a variety of restaurants and bars that offer delicious food and refreshing drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork tenderloin sandwich: A classic Indiana dish that features a crispy and juicy pork tenderloin served on a bun with lettuce, tomato, and mayonnaise.

  • Sugar cream pie: A sweet and creamy pie made with sugar, cream, and vanilla that is a beloved Indiana dessert.

  • Fried chicken: Indiana is known for its delicious fried chicken, and Lebanon has several restaurants that serve up this classic dish.

Local Beverages

  • Indiana wine: Cedar Creek Winery and nearby Hopwood Cellars Winery offer a variety of delicious wines made with locally grown grapes.

  • Sun King Brewery beers: Sun King Brewery, located in nearby Indianapolis, offers a variety of craft beers that are available in many of Lebanon’s bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Lebanon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lebanon offers a variety of accommodations to fit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Lebanon:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Lebanon: A comfortable hotel located in the heart of downtown Lebanon.

  • Harmeyer Retreat: A cozy bed and breakfast located in a historic building in downtown Lebanon.

  • Camping at Abner Longley Park: A peaceful and affordable option for those who enjoy camping.

What to Buy in Lebanon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some of the best places to shop in Lebanon:

  • Lebanon Farmers Market: Shop for fresh produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal goods at this popular farmers market in the summer months.

  • Brianne’s Boutique: A cute boutique that offers women’s clothing, jewelry, and gifts.

  • Boone County Treasures: A charming gift shop that offers a variety of unique, locally made items.

Local Culture and Customs of Lebanon

Lebanon is a friendly and welcoming city that values community and tradition. Here are some local customs and cultural practices to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Be polite and friendly: Lebanon residents are known for their hospitality and friendliness, so be sure to greet locals with a smile and a hello.

  • Respect local traditions: Indiana has a rich history and culture, so be respectful of the local customs and values.

Best Time to Visit Lebanon

Lebanon can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. Summer is also a great time to visit for those who want to enjoy outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lebanon

Do:

  • Be friendly and respectful

  • Explore the downtown area

  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Ignore local traditions and customs

  • Litter or disrespect the environment

  • Skip the Boone County Historical Museum

Safety and Health

Safety in Lebanon

Lebanon is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to ensure your safety.

Health in Lebanon

Lebanon has several medical facilities and pharmacies to take care of any medical needs. It is always recommended to have travel insurance and be up-to-date on routine vaccinations.

Getting to Lebanon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lebanon

The closest airport to Lebanon is the Indianapolis International Airport, which is located approximately 35 miles southeast of Lebanon.

Bus Stations Near Lebanon

There are several bus stations located in nearby cities that offer transportation to Lebanon. Some of the closest stations include the Lafayette Greyhound Station and the Indianapolis Greyhound Station.

Other Options

Lebanon is easily accessible by car, as it is located near several major highways including I-65 and I-74.

How to Get Around in Lebanon

Lebanon is a walkable city, and many attractions and activities are within walking distance of each other. However, for those who prefer to drive, there are several rental car companies located in Lebanon as well as Uber and Lyft services.

Where to Go Next from Lebanon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lebanon is located in the heart of Indiana and is just a short drive away from several other popular destinations, including:

  • Indianapolis: The capital city of Indiana is located just 30 miles southeast of Lebanon and offers a variety of attractions and activities.

  • Lafayette: Located 40 miles northwest of Lebanon, Lafayette is home to Purdue University as well as several museums and historical sites.

Conclusion

Lebanon is a hidden gem in the heart of Indiana, offering a charming and friendly atmosphere, rich history, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Lebanon has something to offer for everyone. Use this guide to plan your visit to Lebanon and discover all that this wonderful city has to offer.
