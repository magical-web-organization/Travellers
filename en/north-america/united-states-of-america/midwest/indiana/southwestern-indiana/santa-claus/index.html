A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Santa Claus, Indiana

Santa Claus, a charming town in southwestern Indiana, offers visitors a unique Christmas experience year-round. If you’re planning a trip to this festive destination, our comprehensive guide has everything you need to know, from the best things to do and places to eat, to where to stay and how to get around.

Introduction to Santa Claus

Santa Claus, an unincorporated town in Spencer County, is known as the “Christmas capital of the world.” The town’s name dates back to the late 1800s when residents wanted to attract more tourists by capitalizing on the Christmas spirit. Santa Claus has since become famous for its Christmas-themed attractions, such as Holiday World & Splashin’ Safari, Santa Claus Christmas Store, and the Santa Claus Museum & Village.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Claus

Here are some of the top attractions Santa Claus has to offer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Santa Claus Museum & Village: A museum dedicated to the town’s history, including Christmas-themed exhibits, letters to Santa, and photos with Santa.
  • Santa Claus Post Office: Visitors can send postcards and letters with the official Santa Claus postmark.
  • Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial: A historical park that honors Abraham Lincoln’s 14 years of life in Indiana.

Natural Attractions of Santa Claus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Holiday World & Splashin’ Safari: An amusement park with roller coasters, water rides, and a water park.
  • Lake Rudolph Campground & RV Resort: A family-friendly camping and lodging destination with a water park and mini-golf course.
  • Lincoln State Park: A scenic park with hiking trails, fishing, boating, and camping opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Claus

Santa Claus has several hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Lincoln Boyhood Trail: A 2-mile trail that goes through the woods and passes several historical landmarks.
  • Hoosier National Forest: A scenic forest with trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Monon Trail: A 10-mile trail that connects Santa Claus to other nearby towns and attractions.

Best Things to Do in Santa Claus: Activities and Tours

  • Santa’s Candy Castle: A candy store and museum that offers chocolate-making and taffy-pulling demonstrations.
  • Santa’s Stables: A horseback riding adventure through the scenic countryside.
  • Santa Claus Land of Lights: A drive-through light show that features more than 300 light displays.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Claus

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Santa Claus:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Santa Claus Museum & Village, Santa’s Candy Castle, and Santa’s Stables
Day 2 Spend the day at Holiday World & Splashin’ Safari
Day 3 Explore Lincoln State Park and Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial
Day 4 Take a scenic drive through the countryside and visit nearby towns like Jasper and French Lick
Day 5 Go kayaking or canoeing on the Ohio River
Day 6 Enjoy a round of mini-golf and water park fun at Lake Rudolph Campground & RV Resort
Day 7 Visit Santa Claus Land of Lights and take a Christmas-themed tour

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Claus

Santa Claus has several unique dining options, serving traditional Hoosier and Christmas-themed food.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: A Hoosier staple served at many local restaurants, including Brick Oven Pizza and Santa’s Lakeside Café.
  • Sugar Cream Pie: A rich custard pie that originated in Indiana and can be found at many local bakeries.
  • Christmas Cookies: A must-have treat during the holiday season, available at Santa’s Candy Castle and other bakeries in town.

Local Beverages

  • Schnitz Brewery & Pub: A local brewery that serves a variety of craft beers.
  • Monkey Hollow Winery: A winery that uses locally-grown grapes to produce a variety of wines.

Where to Stay in Santa Claus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Claus has several lodging options, catering to all budgets and preferences.

  • Lake Rudolph Campground & RV Resort: A family-friendly lodging option with cabins, RV sites, and tent camping.
  • Santa’s Lakeside Cottages: Fully-furnished cottages with lake views and kitchen facilities.
  • Holiday Inn Express: A hotel located near Holiday World & Splashin’ Safari.

What to Buy in Santa Claus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Santa Claus-themed ornaments and decorations: Available at Santa Claus Christmas Store and Santa’s Village in the Santa Claus Museum.
  • Local wines and craft beers: Purchasable at Monkey Hollow Winery and Schnitz Brewery & Pub.
  • Christmas-themed apparel: A fun souvenir to remember your trip, available at many local shops and stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Claus

The Santa Claus community is proud of its Christmas traditions, and locals follow them throughout the year. The town celebrates Christmas in July, with Santa Claus making an appearance to celebrate the halfway point to Christmas. Every Christmas Eve, the town holds a Christmas tree lighting ceremony, followed by a parade.

Best Time to Visit Santa Claus

The best time to visit Santa Claus is during the holiday season, from November to January. However, the town’s attractions are open all year, making it accessible for visitors throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Claus

Do:

  • Visit the Santa Claus Museum & Village for a unique Christmas-themed experience.
  • Check out the Christmas lights display at Lake Rudolph Campground & RV Resort during the holiday season.
  • Try the fried chicken, sugar cream pie, and locally brewed beer and wine.

Don’t:

  • Miss the Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial, honoring President Lincoln’s Indiana roots.
  • Forget to bring your Christmas spirit.
  • Visit during the busy holiday season without planning in advance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Claus

Santa Claus is a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, always exercise caution when exploring natural attractions, hiking trails, and outdoor activities.

Health in Santa Claus

Santa Claus has several medical clinics and pharmacies, providing visitors with easy access to healthcare. However, visitors should carry travel insurance and consult with their doctor before traveling.

Getting to Santa Claus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Claus

  • Louisville International Airport: Approximately 75 miles from Santa Claus.
  • Evansville Regional Airport: Approximately 45 miles from Santa Claus.

Bus Stations Near Santa Claus

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Station located in Evansville, approximately 45 miles from Santa Claus.

Other Options

  • Personal vehicle: Santa Claus is located conveniently off of Interstate 64.
  • Private shuttle: Several shuttle services run between Evansville and Santa Claus.

How to Get Around in Santa Claus

The best way to get around Santa Claus is by car. Several car rental companies operate in the area, providing visitors with easy access to transportation. Alternatively, visitors can bike or hike on the many trails in the area.

Where to Go Next from Santa Claus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • French Lick, Indiana: A resort town with several historical landmarks, scenic views, and a casino.
  • Louisville, Kentucky: A bustling city with impressive architecture, art galleries, museums, and parks.
  • Owensboro, Kentucky: A riverside town known for its delicious BBQ and rich music heritage.

Conclusion

Santa Claus, Indiana, is a unique destination that celebrates Christmas year-round. From museums and outdoor activities to food and drink options, Santa Claus has something for everyone. Plan your trip using our comprehensive guide and experience the magic of Christmas in Santa Claus.
