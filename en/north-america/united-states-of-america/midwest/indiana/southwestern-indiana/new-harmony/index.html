Introduction to New Harmony

Nestled in Southwestern Indiana, New Harmony is a charming town located in the Midwest region of the United States of America. Also known as a utopian community during the 19th century, today New Harmony has become a popular tourist destination due to its rich history, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and how to get around New Harmony.

List of Places to Visit in New Harmony

Before diving into the details, let’s take a look at the must-visit places in New Harmony:

  • Harmonie State Park – A scenic state park with hiking trails, a lake for fishing and boating, and campsites for overnight stays.
  • New Harmony Historic District – A beautifully preserved area with historic buildings, gardens, and sculptures.
  • Atheneum Visitors Center – A modern architectural wonder designed by Richard Meier, featuring exhibitions about the history of New Harmony.
  • Working Men’s Institute Museum and Library – A historic library and museum showcasing the town’s history and artifacts.
  • Cathedral Labyrinth and Garden – A tranquil garden with a circular labyrinth made of limestone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

New Harmony has a rich history in communal living and the arts. Here are some culturally important attractions in the town:

  • New Harmony Museum and Historic Site – A collection of historic buildings featuring informative signs, making it a great option for self-guided tours.
  • Philip Johnson’s Roofless Church – A beautiful and unique outdoor church with a stunning view of the sunset.
  • Red Geranium Restaurant and Historic New Harmony Employee Log Cabins – A historic restaurant serving delicious American cuisine with a charming outdoor seating area next to restored log cabins.
  • Thomas Say’s Historic Home – The home of the notable naturalist and scientist, Thomas Say, which visitors can explore on guided tours.
  • Murphy Auditorium and Thrall’s Opera House – Two historic venues for the arts, where visitors can catch plays, musical performances, and other artsy events.

Natural Attractions of New Harmony: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The town’s natural beauty is a testament to its designation as a utopia. Here are some natural attractions of New Harmony that all outdoor enthusiasts will enjoy:

  • Wabash River – A scenic river with multiple entry points for kayaking and canoeing.
  • Hoosier National Forest – A large nearby national forest with a variety of hiking trails.
  • New Harmony State Fish and Wildlife Area – A fishing and hunting area that is open to public use all year long.
  • Double Rock Park – A serene park with massive limestone formations and a scenic view of the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Harmony

New Harmony offers some fantastic trails for hikers and bikers. Here are a few routes to try out:

  • New Harmony Riverfront Trail – A biking and walking trail that follows the Wabash River.
  • Yellow Banks and Tupelo Trails – Two hiking trails that lead to an overlook with a fantastic view of the Wabash River.
  • Harmonie State Park Trails – Multiple trails in Harmonie State Park catering to intermediate to advanced level hikers with diverse terrains and elevation changes.
  • New Harmony Wabash and Erie Canal Trails – A 24-mile trail that passes through scenic landscapes along the canal.

Best Things to Do in New Harmony: Activities and Tours

New Harmony offers a range of activities and tours for all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in New Harmony:

  • New Harmony Ghost Walk – A guided tour where visitors learn about the town’s history of hauntings and legends.
  • Cruise on the Wabash River – Enjoy a scenic river cruise that showcases the town’s natural beauty and historical significance.
  • Black Lodge Coffee Roasters Tour – A tour of the town’s coffee roasting facility that also includes a tasting session of their award-winning coffee.
  • New Harmony Gallery of Contemporary Art – A contemporary art gallery in the historic district, showcasing regional and national artists.
  • New Harmony Clay Project – A fascinating pottery studio offering pottery classes and workshops or watch local pottery artists at work.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Harmony

For a week-long stay, here’s an itinerary to help you explore all of New Harmony’s must-visit places:

Day Activities
Day 1 Check-in, visit the Atheneum Visitors Center, dine-in at Red Geranium Restaurant
Day 2 Explore New Harmony Museum and Historic Site, and tour the Phillips Johnson Roofless Church
Day 3 Hike the Yellow Banks and Tupelo Trails followed by a leisurely walk through the working men’s Institute Museum and Library
Day 4 Canoe on the Wabash River and participate in a pottery workshop at New Harmony Clay Project
Day 5 Visit the Harmonie State Park Trails and hike the 10-mile trail, followed by an afternoon picnic in the park
Day 6 Take the New Harmony Ghost Walk and conclude the evening with a cruise on the Wabash River
Day 7 Depart after spending the day fishing at New Harmony State Fish and Wildlife Area

Food and Drink Scene of New Harmony

New Harmony has an excellent food and drink scene, with a range of unique options to satisfy all (food) types and budgets. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages in New Harmony.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Red Geranium Restaurant – Indiana’s famous bacon-wrapped meatloaf, a dish that is not to be missed.
  • Sara’s Harmony Way – A reliable food truck serving tasty and refreshing tacos, pulled pork sandwiches, and homemade sweet tea.
  • Harvest Pizzeria – An authentic Neapolitan-style pizza spot serving fresh ingredients and local brews.
  • MacLeod’s Coffee House – Famous for their delicious baked goods, hearty breakfast sandwiches, and perfectly-crafted coffee drinks.
  • Main Café and Creamery – The perfect lunch location offering American classics, soups, sandwiches, and ice cream deserts.

Local Beverages

  • New Harmony Coffee Company – Get a hot cup of joe, grab some beans to bring back home or go for Hoosier Hooch, a local favorite.
  • Black Lodge Coffee Roasters – A local roasting company, offering specialty coffee drinks paired with fresh pastries.
  • River View Tavern and Grille – A popular spot for a drink and presenting live entertainment with a selection of local brews.

Where to Stay in New Harmony: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Harmony has several lodging options that cater to your preferences and budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in New Harmony:

  • New Harmony Inn Resort - A waterfront resort located on the banks of the Wabash River, with comfortable rooms, dining options, and other amenities.
  • Harmonie State Park Camping - A peaceful campsite with water & electric hookups for RV and tent camping close to nature and outdoor activities.
  • Ella’s Bed and Breakfast - A charming B&B located in the historic district with cozy rooms and delicious breakfast.
  • Old Rooming House on Church Street - A peaceful guest house with a shared kitchen and living area, perfect for budget travelers.
  • New Harmony Inn Lodge - A picturesque lodge overlooking the Wabash River, featuring comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in New Harmony: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

New Harmony offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts. Here are some of the best places to shop in New Harmony:

  • New Harmony Soap Company - Natural handmade soap, skincare, and aromatherapy products made with locally sourced materials.
  • Murphy Auditorium Gift Shop – A shop inside a historic venue with a range of local and unique gift items.
  • Antiques on Main – A treasure trove of antiques, collectibles and vintage objects, much-revered by collectors of antiques.
  • Kathryn of New Harmony - Handmade pottery and paintings by local artist, Kathryn Waters.
  • New Harmony Arts Co-op - A cooperative of artists and artisans selling handmade, one-of-a-kind items, including woodwork and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of New Harmony

New Harmony has a vibrant cultural scene, and visitors should take note of the following customs:

  • Respect local traditions – New Harmony has a rich history and a friendly community. Be respectful of the customs and traditions of local residents and companies.
  • Enjoy the arts scene – New Harmony has many galleries, museums, and theatre companies showcasing local and national talent. Be sure to partake in some of the artistic offerings and enjoy the local color.
  • Say hi and make friends – New Harmony is a welcoming town with friendly locals. Be sure to say hello to people and make friends – that’s how you’ll experience New Harmony most authentically!

Best Time to Visit New Harmony

New Harmony is enjoyable all year round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and crisp. Summer is also a popular time to visit due to the abundance of outdoor activities. However, to avoid huge crowds and high prices, opt for a trip to New Harmony between September and April when there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Harmony

Here are some things that you should and shouldn’t do when visiting New Harmony:

Do:

  • Do remember to bring comfortable shoes as much of the town is walkable.
  • Do try the local cuisine and craft brews, as they are an excellent representation of the local culture.
  • Do book accommodations and popular tours in advance to avoid the last-minute rush.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and respect the environment during your stay.
  • Don’t forget your sunscreen, sunglasses or a hat, especially during the warmer months.
  • Don’t be rude to the locals or ignore their customs.

Safety and Health

Here’s everything you need to know about safety and health during your New Harmony visit:

Safety in New Harmony

New Harmony is a safe town overall, but it’s always essential to be aware of your surroundings and belongings. Here are a few tips to stay safe during your visit:

  • Secure your valuable items such as cash, credit cards, and passports before leaving the hotel room.
  • Use crosswalks while walking or cycling around town to stay safe.
  • Don’t walk alone at night.

Health in New Harmony

New Harmony is a safe town regarding health concerns, but visitors should take note of the following:

  • Use mosquito repellent during outdoor adventures as Indiana is a rural state with mosquitoes that carry viruses.
  • Wear properly-fitted masks when visiting crowded areas or attractions.

Getting to New Harmony: Transportation Options and Logistics

New Harmony is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some options:

Airports Near New Harmony

  • Evansville Regional Airport (EVV) – The nearest airport, located about 25 miles from New Harmony, Indiana.

Bus Stations Near New Harmony

  • Greyhound Bus Station – Located in Evansville, Indiana. Inter-city buses run through this station.

Other Options

  • A rental car is the most convenient option to explore New Harmony and Southern Indiana.

How to Get Around in New Harmony

Walking, biking, or driving are the best ways to get around in New Harmony. As majority of the attractions are concentrated in the historic district, parking is readily available. Alternatively, biking enthusiasts can rent bikes from some of the local companies within town and explore the Wabash River trails.

Where to Go Next from New Harmony: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Harmony offers an array of nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Evansville, Indiana – A nearby city with plenty of outdoor activities, museums, and entertainment options.
  • Hoosier National Forest – A national forest with excellent hiking and camping opportunities in the Southern Indiana region.
  • Illinois Shawnee National Forest – A national forest in Southern Illinois with activities such as hiking, berry picking, and horseback riding.

Conclusion

With its rich history, artistic offerings, outdoor activities, and fantastic food and drink scene, New Harmony is a great place to visit in the Midwest region of the United States. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan and enjoy your New Harmony trip. Safe travels!
