Introduction to Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial

Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial is a significant historic site located in southwestern Indiana, the birthplace of the United States’ 16th President, Abraham Lincoln. The site commemorates the 14 formative years of his childhood spent in Indiana. It covers an area of 202 acres near Gentryville, Indiana. The park and its surroundings provide a unique insight into Abraham Lincoln’s early years, the life of Indiana pioneers, and the natural beauty of the land.

List of Places to Visit in Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial

Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial offers various cultural and natural attractions for visitors to discover, explore, and learn. Below is a list of the essential places to visit in the park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lincoln Living Historical Farm: An interactive museum showcasing the daily life of Lincoln’s family. Visitors can participate in activities like splitting rails, making crafts, or cooking food.
  • Memorial Visitor Center: A museum with exhibits on Lincoln’s life, Indiana pioneer life, and the Civil War’s impact on the nation and Indiana.
  • Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial Cemetery: A small cemetery where Thomas and Nancy Hanks Lincoln, Lincoln’s parents, are buried.

Natural Attractions of Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Trail of Twelve Stones: A trail dedicated to Lincoln’s life, where each stone represents a significant milestone in his journey.
  • Lincoln Boyhood Trail: A trail that replicates the path that Lincoln walked from his childhood home to the Anderson Creek, which he crossed while travelling to school.
  • Lake Lincoln: A small lake in the park that provides recreational activities like fishing.
  • Picnic Area: A designated area where visitors can enjoy a picnic with friends and family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial

Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial offers several hiking and biking routes that visitors can explore to witness the natural beauty, unique flora and fauna, and experience a sense of adventure. The trails are suitable for all age groups, and the park provides necessary amenities like restrooms and drinking water.

  • Trail of Twelve Stones (0.5 miles): The trail connects various historic sites through paved surfaces and provides a brief history of Lincoln’s life to visitors.
  • Lincoln Boyhood Trail (15 miles): The trail follows the path that young Lincoln took daily from home to school. The trail is narrow, and cyclists must be cautious of pedestrians.
  • Hiking and biking in the park: Visitors can also stroll through the park, enjoying nature’s beauty and experience the paved trails.

Best Things to Do in Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial: Activities and Tours

There is much to explore at Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial that will cater to a wide range of visitor interests and preferences:

  • Participate in ranger-led programs: The park offers various activities for visitors like Junior Ranger Programs, interpretive talks, historical demonstrations, and walking tours.
  • Fishing: Bring your fishing gear and enjoy fishing at Lake Lincoln.
  • Picnicking: The park has a designated picnic area.
  • Hiking and Biking in the Park: Explore nature and enjoy the fresh air.
  • Exploring Historic Sites: Visitors can visit the various historic sites like the Lincoln Living Historical Farm, Memorial Visitor Center, and Lincoln Boyhood Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial

Visitors can enjoy several local dishes and beverages and experience Indiana’s rural culture while exploring Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: Commonly found in rural Indiana, fried chicken served with favourite sides is a must-try dish.
  • Pork Tenderloin: Another Indiana specialty, it is a breaded and deep-fried slice of pork that’s served on a bun.
  • Corn-on-the-cob: A classic Midwestern dish, where corn is boiled and served with butter.

Local Beverages

Indiana’s local beverages are an excellent way to quench your thirst while enjoying the sights and sounds of the park.

  • Indiana Wine Trail: Indiana’s wine trail in the southern parts of the state has several award-winning wineries.
  • Sun King Brewery: Serves a range of locally brewed beers, frequently ranked as the best brewery in Indiana.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial

Indiana is famous for its rural culture and values. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, greetings, and hospitality. Best Time to Visit Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial
