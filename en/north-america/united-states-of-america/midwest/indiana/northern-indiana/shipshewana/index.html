Introduction to Shipshewana

Located in Northern Indiana, Shipshewana is a charming town known for its Amish community and home to the third largest Amish settlement in the United States. It offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures making it a perfect destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Shipshewana

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Menno-Hof - A museum dedicated to the 500-year history of the Mennonites and Amish people. It features audiovisual presentations, interactive exhibits, and historical artifacts.
  • Davis Mercantile - A shopping and dining destination housed in a restored 19th-century building. It features craft shops, antique stores, a bakery, and a food court.
  • Amish Acres - A historic farm and house tour where visitors can experience the lifestyle of Amish families and see demonstrations of traditional crafts.
  • Shipshewana Auction & Flea Market - A weekly Amish auction and flea market held on Tuesdays and Wednesdays from May to September. It is the largest flea market in the Midwest.

Natural Attractions of Shipshewana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pigeon River State Fish and Wildlife Area - A 12,000-acre preserve along the Pigeon River known for its fishing, hiking, and hunting opportunities.
  • Shade’s State Park - A beautiful park with sandstone cliffs, deep ravines, and clear streams. It offers hiking, canoeing, picnicking, and camping.
  • Dutch Creek Farm Animal Park - An interactive farm where visitors can feed and pet various animals such as alpacas, camels, and kangaroos.
  • Blue Gate Carriage Company - An authentic Amish carriage ride that takes visitors on a scenic tour of the countryside around Shipshewana.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shipshewana

  • MapleHeart Trail - A 3.2-mile paved trail that connects Shipshewana to Middlebury. It is suitable for walking, jogging, and biking.
  • Pigeon River Trail - A 12-mile trail that follows the Pigeon River and is suitable for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Oswald Family Farm Trail - A beautiful 1.5-mile loop trail that leads through fields and woods of an Amish farm, with an observation deck over a lake.

Best Things to Do in Shipshewana: Activities and Tours

  • Shipshewana Trading Place Auctions & Flea Market - A popular shopping venue where visitors can browse antiques, unique collectibles, and crafts.
  • Murder Mystery Dinner Theatre - An interactive dinner experience that provides entertainment with a murder mystery game while enjoying a delicious dinner buffet.
  • Tuttle’s House of Terror - A haunted house attraction located near Shipshewana featuring scary and spooky moments guaranteed to thrill every visitor.
  • Shipshewana Country Tours - A guided tour that takes visitors to all the best attractions that Shipshewana has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shipshewana

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore downtown Shipshewana, dine at the Essenhaus or Blue Gate Restaurant, and see a show at the Blue Gate Theatre.
Day 2 Visit Menno-Hof, Shipshewana Auction & Flea Market, and Shipshewana Trading Place Auction.
Day 3 Discover the Amish way of life at Amish Acres, take an authentic Amish carriage ride, and enjoy a homecooked meal at an Amish home.
Day 4 Take a hike at Pigeon River State Fish and Wildlife Area, then have lunch at the Dutch Creek Farm Animal Park.
Day 5 Visit the Quilt Gardens along the Heritage Trail and learn about the history of quilt-making in the area.
Day 6 Take a farm tour at a local Amish farm, dine at Aunt Karen’s Cafe & Bakery and see a show at the Blue Gate Theatre.
Day 7 End your trip with a relaxing day at Shade’s State Park - go hiking, fishing or paddling, and have a picnic along the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Shipshewana

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amish-style fried chicken - A classic dish made with seasoned breading, deep-fried to crispy perfection.
  • Homemade noodles - A filling and tasty side dish often served with chicken and gravy.
  • Sugar cream pie - A local specialty pie made with a filling of heavy cream, butter, sugar, and flour.
  • Fried apple pie - A sweet and crispy dessert filled with apples and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Root Beer or Cream Soda - A classic and refreshing choice for a drink with sweet vanilla or rich cream flavors.
  • Fresh Milk - A staple in many Amish households, visitors can buy a fresh from the farm glass of milk.
  • Yoder’s Homestyle Fresh Apple Cider - A local cider produced from locally harvested apples and served fresh.

Where to Stay in Shipshewana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Van Buren Hotel - A comfortable and affordable option located in downtown Shipshewana.
  • Blue Gate Garden Inn - A luxurious and modern hotel within walking distance from the town’s attractions.
  • Farmstead Inn & Conference Center - A charming inn located near Amish Acres, offering a peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Shipshewana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amish Quilts - A beautiful handmade quilt made from traditional fabrics and patterns.
  • Baked Goods - Freshly baked pies, cookies, bread, and other baked goods from local Amish bakeries.
  • Handcrafted furniture and home decor - Amish-made furniture is known for its quality, durability, and beautiful designs.
  • Unique antiques and collectibles - From vintage clothing to rare books and rare coins, the antique shops offer a variety of treasures to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Shipshewana

Shipshewana’s Amish community has a rich culture and tradition, often called “Plain” because of their lack of traditional Western adornment. Visitors should respect their customs by dressing modestly, speaking softly, taking photos of them only upon permission, and asking questions to learn more about their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Shipshewana

The best time to visit Shipshewana is from May to September when many attractions are open, and the weather is pleasant. The fall months, from late September to November, are also great, with beautiful autumn colors and harvest festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shipshewana

Do:

  • Learn about the local culture and customs of the Amish and respect them.
  • Try local foods and drinks such as fried chicken, apple cider, and sugar cream pie.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the area’s history, culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures of the Amish community without their permission.
  • Don’t wear revealing or flashy clothes when visiting the Amish community.
  • Don’t drive recklessly on country roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shipshewana

Shipshewana is generally a safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. It is advisable to watch out for traffic, especially when traveling around the Amish farms, and to hike with a companion.

Health in Shipshewana

Medical services are available in town for emergency and non-emergency services. Visitors should take suitable precautionary measures for bugs while hiking in wooded areas.

Getting to Shipshewana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shipshewana is accessible by car and bus. The nearest airport is South Bend International Airport, approximately 50 miles away.

Airports Near Shipshewana

  • South Bend International Airport - Approximately 50 miles from Shipshewana.

Bus Stations Near Shipshewana

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Approximately 10 miles from Shipshewana, located in Howe, Indiana.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals - Car rentals are available at South Bend International Airport and in nearby Goshen, Indiana.

How to Get Around in Shipshewana

The best way to get around Shipshewana is by car or bike. However, if that is not possible, visitors can also rent a horse-drawn carriage, rent bikes, or book a tour.

Where to Go Next from Shipshewana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Amish Acres - A historic farmstead in nearby Nappanee that offers guided tours and hosts various festivals throughout the year.
  • Elkhart Riverwalk - A picturesque park located along the St. Joseph and Elkhart rivers in downtown Elkhart, offering walking and biking trails, as well as various events and activities throughout the year.
  • Heritage Trail - A scenic driving route featuring the Quilt Gardens through several towns in Elkhart County.

Conclusion

Shipshewana is an excellent destination for those seeking a peaceful, unique, and cultural vacation experience. From exploring the Amish way of life to taking a hike through scenic trails, this town has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Shipshewana with ease and make the most of their vacation experience.
