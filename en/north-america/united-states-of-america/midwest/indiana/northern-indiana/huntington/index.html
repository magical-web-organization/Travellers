Introduction to Huntington

Located in northern Indiana, Huntington is a charming city with a rich history and natural beauty that is sure to captivate visitors. With easy access to some of the top attractions in the Midwest, Huntington is a perfect destination for a fun-filled vacation. From the birthplace of legendary actor Dan Quayle and the vibrant downtown with its unique shops and restaurants, to the rolling hills and lush forests that surround the city, there is something for everyone in Huntington.

List of Places to Visit in Huntington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dan Quayle Vice Presidential Museum - Learn about the life and career of the 44th Vice President of the United States, Dan Quayle, and explore his interactive museum in Huntington.

  2. LaFontaine Arts Council - Discover the local arts scene at LaFontaine Arts Council, which hosts concerts, art exhibits, and other cultural events.

  3. Forks of the Wabash Historic Park - Explore the history of Huntington and the Wabash River at the Forks of the Wabash, where you can visit the Chief Richardville House, the oldest Native American dwelling still standing in Indiana.

Natural Attractions of Huntington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Salamonie Lake State Recreation Area - Enjoy a day of fishing, boating, hiking or camping at Salamonie Lake, a 12,000-acre state recreation area.

  2. Memorial Park - Take a stroll through Memorial Park, a beautiful green space in the heart of Huntington, and enjoy its walking trails, picnic areas, and serene pond.

  3. Roush Lake Fish & Wildlife Area - If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, Roush Lake Fish & Wildlife Area offers over 8,000 acres of hiking, fishing, and hunting opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntington

  1. Heritage Trail - Take a scenic walk or bike ride along the 2.5-mile Heritage Trail, which follows the route of an old railroad track and offers glimpses of Huntington’s history.

  2. Salamonie River State Forest - Hike or bike through the 8500-acre Salamonie River State Forest, which includes four trails with varying difficulty levels.

Best Things to Do in Huntington: Activities and Tours

  1. Wabash County Historical Museum Walking Tour - Take a guided walking tour of Huntington’s historic downtown and explore the city’s architecture and history.

  2. Dollar Saver Cinema - Catch a movie at the Dollar Saver Cinema, one of the few remaining dollar theaters in the Midwest.

  3. Kissinger Family Wine Tasting Room - Sample local wines at the Kissinger Family Wine Tasting Room, which offers a variety of award-winning wines from Indiana.

Food and Drink Scene of Huntington

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tenderloin sandwich - Try the famous pork tenderloin sandwich, a Hoosier State specialty, at Nick’s Kitchen in downtown Huntington.

  2. Bison burger - Sample a hearty bison burger at Joseph Decuis, a fine dining establishment specializing in farm-to-table cuisine.

  3. Fudge - Treat your sweet tooth to some delicious homemade fudge at Crain’s Creamery, a local ice cream shop.

Local Beverages

  1. Mad Anthony Brewing Company - Savor a pint of craft beer at Mad Anthony Brewing Company, which has a rotating selection of beers brewed onsite.

  2. Two-EE’s Winery - Taste local wines and listen to live music at Two-EE’s Winery, which offers a beautiful setting on a hilltop overlooking Huntington.

Where to Stay in Huntington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Comfort Inn - This budget-friendly hotel is located close to downtown Huntington and offers a complimentary breakfast.

  2. Huntington Inn - Budget-conscious travelers will appreciate the Huntington Inn, which provides simple rooms at an affordable rate.

  3. Pine Cottage Bed & Breakfast - Stay in a charming cottage at Pine Cottage Bed & Breakfast, a cozy retreat located in the heart of Huntington.

What to Buy in Huntington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Peanut Brittle - Pick up a bag of homemade peanut brittle from the Historic Forks of the Wabash gift shop.

  2. Candles - Take home a hand-poured soy candle from Harriet & Annie’s Candle Company, a local shop specializing in artisanal candles.

Local Culture and Customs of Huntington

Huntington’s local culture centers around a love of the outdoors, history, and local cuisine. Visitors should be prepared to engage in friendly conversations with locals and explore the many natural and cultural attractions Huntington has to offer.

Best Time to Visit Huntington

The best time to visit Huntington is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing color. Summer is also a popular time to visit, with plenty of opportunities to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huntington

Do:

  1. Do visit the Dan Quayle Vice Presidential Museum and learn about Indiana’s famous son.

  2. Do explore the city’s charming downtown area and its unique shops and restaurants.

  3. Do spend some time outdoors, hiking, fishing, or boating at one of Huntington’s many natural attractions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t miss the chance to try a pork tenderloin sandwich, a Hoosier State specialty.

  2. Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent if exploring the outdoors.

  3. Don’t miss the historic Chief Richardville House when visiting the Forks of the Wabash Historic Park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huntington

Huntington is a safe city with low crime rates. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables locked away and not leaving personal items unattended.

Health in Huntington

Huntington has a number of healthcare facilities, including Parkview Huntington Hospital and St. Vincent’s Medical Center, that provide quality medical care to residents and visitors.

Getting to Huntington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huntington

  1. Fort Wayne International Airport is the closest major airport to Huntington, located approximately 25 miles away.

  2. Indianapolis International Airport is another option, located approximately two hours south of Huntington.

Bus Stations Near Huntington

  1. Greyhound offers bus service to Fort Wayne, located approximately 25 miles away.

  2. Indiana University-Purdue University Fort Wayne provides shuttle service to and from Fort Wayne International Airport.

Other Options

  1. Rented Car - Travelers can rent a car from Fort Wayne International Airport or other nearby locations.

  2. Train - Amtrak’s Cardinal line serves nearby Wabash and Waterloo.

How to Get Around in Huntington

A rental car is recommended to explore Huntington and the surrounding area. Taxis are available, but public transportation options are limited.

Where to Go Next from Huntington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Fort Wayne - Only 25 miles away, Fort Wayne offers a variety of family-friendly attractions, including the Fort Wayne Children’s Zoo and the Foellinger-Freimann Botanical Conservatory.

  2. Lafayette/West Lafayette - Drive two hours south of Huntington and explore the charming towns of Lafayette and West Lafayette. Visit Purdue University and its museums, parks, and art galleries.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural offerings, Huntington is a hidden gem in the Midwest. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures or cultural pursuits, Huntington has plenty to offer for all kinds of travelers. Follow our comprehensive guide to get the most out of your visit to Huntington.
