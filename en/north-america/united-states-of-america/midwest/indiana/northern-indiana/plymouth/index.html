Introduction to Plymouth

Plymouth, Indiana, is a small town located in Northern Indiana, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. It is known for its rich history, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Plymouth has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Plymouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Marshall County Historical Society Museum - This museum offers a glimpse into the history of Marshall County and its residents. It features exhibits on Native American culture, local industry, and famous residents.

  2. Plymouth Railroad Museum - This museum is dedicated to the history of railroading in Plymouth. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia related to the railroad industry.

  3. Hoosier Old Wheels Antique Auto Museum - This museum is home to a large collection of antique and classic cars. Visitors can take a stroll down memory lane and view some of the most iconic cars from the 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Plymouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Yellow River State Forest - This forest spans over 23,000 acres and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

  2. Pretty Lake - This lake is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and fishing. It has several public access points and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Potato Creek State Park - This park is home to several hiking and biking trails, a swimming beach, and a nature center. It’s the perfect place to spend a day outdoors with family and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plymouth

  1. Plymouth Greenway Trail - This trail is a 2.5-mile paved path that runs through the heart of Plymouth. It’s perfect for walking, jogging, or biking.

  2. Yellow River Trail - This trail runs along the Yellow River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It’s ideal for hiking or biking and provides plenty of opportunities for wildlife viewing.

Best Things to Do in Plymouth: Activities and Tours

  1. Plymouth Farmers Market - This farmers market is held every Saturday from May through October. Visitors can purchase locally grown produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

  2. Blueberry Festival - This festival is held every Labor Day weekend and celebrates the harvest of blueberries. It features food vendors, live entertainment, and a parade.

  3. Hoosier Racing Tire SCCA Super Tour - This annual event attracts thousands of auto racing enthusiasts from around the world. It features a full weekend of racing and other activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Plymouth

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Indiana Sweet Corn - This locally grown sweet corn is a staple of Indiana cuisine. It’s at its best during the summer months and can be found at local farmers markets and roadside stands.

  2. Pork Tenderloin Sandwich - This classic Midwestern sandwich features a breaded and fried pork tenderloin on a bun. It’s often served with lettuce, tomato, and other toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. 18th Street Brewery - This local brewery offers a variety of beers and ales, including several seasonal offerings.

  2. Oliver Winery - This family-owned winery produces a wide variety of wines, including several award-winning varieties.

Where to Stay in Plymouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Swan Lake Resort - This resort features a golf course, spa, and several dining options. It’s the perfect place to relax and unwind after a day of exploring Plymouth.

  2. Days Inn by Wyndham Plymouth - This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location near downtown Plymouth.

What to Buy in Plymouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Blueberries - Plymouth is known for its blueberry harvest, and visitors can purchase fresh blueberries from local farms and markets.

  2. Handmade Crafts - There are several local artists and crafters in Plymouth who sell their wares at farmers markets and other events.

Local Culture and Customs of Plymouth

Plymouth residents are known for their friendliness and hospitality. Visitors should expect to be greeted with a warm smile and a friendly conversation wherever they go. The town is also deeply rooted in its agricultural heritage, and visitors can expect to see fields of corn and soybeans stretching as far as the eye can see.

Best Time to Visit Plymouth

The best time to visit Plymouth is between May and October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are plentiful. The Blueberry Festival, which is held over Labor Day weekend, is a particularly popular time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plymouth

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the town’s rich history and culture.

  • Do try the local cuisine, including sweet corn and pork tenderloin sandwiches.

  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in and around Plymouth.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any natural or cultural sites.

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plymouth

Plymouth is a generally safe town, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight. The town is also prone to severe weather, including thunderstorms and tornadoes, so visitors should be aware of emergency procedures.

Health in Plymouth

Plymouth has several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as washing hands frequently and staying hydrated, to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Plymouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plymouth

The closest airport to Plymouth is South Bend International Airport, which is approximately 30 miles away. Chicago O’Hare International Airport is also a viable option and is approximately 120 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Plymouth

Greyhound Lines operates a bus station in South Bend, which is approximately 30 miles away from Plymouth.

Other Options

Car rentals are available at both South Bend International Airport and Chicago O’Hare International Airport. Visitors can also book private transportation or hire a taxi or ride-sharing service.

How to Get Around in Plymouth

The best way to get around Plymouth is by car. There are several car rental companies available, and the town is easily accessible via major highways, including U.S. Route 30 and State Road 17.

Where to Go Next from Plymouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
