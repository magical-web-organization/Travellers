Introduction to Logansport

Logansport, Indiana, is a hidden gem of the Midwest, offering visitors a mix of natural beauty, local culture, and history. Located just two hours north of Indianapolis, Logansport boasts a charming downtown district and a wealth of outdoor activities in nearby parks and lakes. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there is something for you here in Logansport.

List of Places to Visit in Logansport

When planning your trip to Logansport, there are plenty of attractions and activities to choose from. Here are some of the must-see places to visit in Logansport:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cass County Historical Society Museum: Learn about the history of Cass County with rotating exhibits and collections of photographs, artifacts and documents.

  • The McHale Performing Arts Center: Located in the heart of downtown, the McHale Performing Arts Center is a venue that hosts a variety of shows, including plays, concerts, and comedy performances.

  • The Dykeman House: This historic home/museum provides an authentic glimpse into life in a small Midwestern town in the late 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Logansport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • France Park: This park boasts wooded trails, a swimming beach, and a beautifully paved bike path. Visitors can also go camping, fishing, and kayaking.

  • Eel River Run: Enjoy hiking or biking on this well-maintained trail which winds along the beautiful Eel river.

  • Tippecanoe State Park: This popular park offers more than 300 campsites, along with hiking trails, boating, fishing, swimming, and various interpretive programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Logansport

In Logansport, there are plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking, whether you’re a beginner or an experienced adventurer. Some popular routes include:

  • Erie Canal Towpath Trail: This rail trail is a crushed limestone path that follows the old Erie Canal along the Wabash River from Peru, Indiana, to Delphi, Indiana. The portion that runs through Logansport passes by the Little Turtle Waterway Plaza and the historic long-span Wabash River Bridge.

  • The Panhandle Pathway: This trail follows the former Penn Central Railroad right-of-way from Winamac, Indiana, through Royal Center and Logansport, to Kenneth. It is a multipurpose trail suitable for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  • River Bluff Trail: This 1.8-mile trail loops around Columbia Park and offers scenic views of the Wabash River.

Best Things to Do in Logansport: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Logansport. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Logansport Farmers Market: Open Saturdays, June through October, the Logansport Farmers Market features a variety of locally grown produce, flowers, baked goods, and crafts.

  • Haunted Walking Tour: Learn about Logansport’s haunted history with a guided walking tour of the city’s downtown area.

  • The Cass County Arts Alliance: This organization offers art classes, workshops, and gallery exhibits throughout the year.

A week is a perfect amount of time to explore everything that Logansport has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary for your Logansport adventure:

Food and Drink Scene of Logansport

When it comes to food and drink in Logansport, there is plenty to tempt your taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Breaded Tenderloin: This Midwestern classic is a pork tenderloin that is thinly pounded, breaded, and fried. It’s served on a bun with typical toppings such as pickles, onions, and mustard.

  • Pot roast sandwich: Another local favorite, this sandwich features tender slices of beef pot roast, caramelized onions, and horseradish sauce.

  • Persimmon pudding: A Logansport staple, this dessert is made with persimmons, sugar, flour, and eggs and is topped with whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Zooka Soda: This old-fashioned soda is made in Logansport using pure cane sugar and comes in flavors like root beer, orange cream, and grape.

  • McClure’s Orchard Hard Cider: Made with apples from the nearby McClure’s Orchard, this hard cider is a refreshing choice.

Where to Stay in Logansport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Logansport, ranging from hotels to bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • Holiday Inn Express: Located in downtown Logansport, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

  • Breezy Hill Bed and Breakfast: This peaceful bed and breakfast is located just outside of Logansport and features rustic rooms with modern amenities and private bathrooms.

  • Shoup House: This historic home has been converted into a cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a shared living area.

What to Buy in Logansport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products in Logansport, there are plenty of options. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Logansport T-Shirts and Hats: Show your Logansport pride with a souvenir t-shirt or hat.

  • Gooseberry Wine: Made from local berries, this fruity wine is a unique souvenir to bring home.

  • Handmade Pottery and Crafts: Check out the Cass County Arts Alliance Gallery for locally made pottery and crafts to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Logansport

Logansport is a welcoming and friendly community, but it’s always good to know the local customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be respectful: As in any small town, it’s important to be respectful of locals and their customs.

  • Be friendly: Locals are friendly and always happy to welcome visitors to Logansport.

  • Slow down: Life in Logansport moves at a slower pace than in larger cities, so take your time and enjoy the charm of this small town.

Best Time to Visit Logansport

The best time to visit Logansport is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a great time to visit and enjoy the changing colors of the leaves. Winter can be cold and snowy, but there are still plenty of indoor activities to enjoy.

Safety and Health

When it comes to safety and health in Logansport, visitors should be aware of the following:

Safety in Logansport

Logansport is a safe and friendly community, but visitors should still be alert and aware of their surroundings, particularly when exploring outdoor areas.

Health in Logansport

Logansport has a few medical clinics and a hospital for routine medical care and emergencies. Visitors should also be aware of the local tick and mosquito populations and take appropriate precautions to avoid tick and mosquito-borne illnesses.

How to Get Around in Logansport

In Logansport, the best way to get around is by car. There is also a local bus service, but it has limited hours and routes. Walking and biking are also good options, particularly in downtown Logansport.

Where to Go Next from Logansport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Hoosier Heartland Wine Trail: Visit the wineries along this trail for a taste of Indiana’s local wine.

  • Prophetstown State Park: This nearby state park offers hiking, biking, and birdwatching opportunities, along with replicas of Native American and pioneer homes.

Conclusion

Logansport, Indiana, may be a small town, but it offers visitors a wealth of experiences, from outdoor adventures and cultural attractions to local cuisine and history. Whether you’re spending a day or a week in Logansport, you’re sure to fall in love with its charming Midwestern charm and friendly locals.
