Introduction to Peru

Peru is a South American country known for its diverse culture, picturesque landscapes, and ancient ruins. From the breathtaking Machu Picchu to the vibrant city of Lima, Peru has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit in Peru, the top things to do, the best time to visit, and everything else you need to know before traveling to Peru.

List of Places to Visit in Peru

Peru has a lot to offer tourists, and here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Machu Picchu: This ancient Incan city was built in the 15th century and is now considered one of the new seven wonders of the world.

  • Huaca Pucllana: This archaeological site is located in the heart of Lima and offers visitors a glimpse into the pre-Columbian history of Peru.

  • Plaza de Armas: Located in the historic center of Cusco, the Plaza de Armas is a popular spot that offers a view of the city’s colonial architecture.

  • Chan Chan: This ancient adobe city was once the capital of the Chimu Kingdom and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Natural Attractions of Peru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rainbow Mountain: Located in the Andes, this mountain boasts beautiful colors and scenic views.

  • Nazca Lines: These ancient geoglyphs located in the Nazca Desert are a mystery to many and make for an interesting visit.

  • Lake Titicaca: This beautiful lake is located on the border of Peru and Bolivia and is known for its stunning scenery and traditional floating villages.

  • Colca Canyon: This canyon is located in southern Peru and is one of the world’s deepest canyons. It offers breathtaking views and unique wildlife, like the Andean Condor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peru

Peru is a hiker’s paradise, and here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Inca Trail: This iconic trail takes visitors on a four-day trek to Machu Picchu.

  • Ausangate Trek: This challenging five-day trek offers hikers beautiful views of the Andes and the Ausangate mountain peak.

  • North Coast Biking Route: This route takes cyclists from the bustling city of Lima to the laid-back beaches of the north coast of Peru.

Best Things to Do in Peru: Activities and Tours

There are so many things to do in Peru, and here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Peruvian Cooking Class: Learn to cook traditional Peruvian dishes like ceviche and lomo saltado in a cooking class.

  • Sandboarding in Huacachina: Visit the desert oasis of Huacachina and try your hand at sandboarding.

  • Paragliding in Lima: Take in the stunning views of Lima from the air on a paragliding adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peru

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Peru:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Lima and explore the historic center.
Day 2 Visit Huaca Pucllana and walk along the Miraflores boardwalk.
Day 3 Fly to Cusco and explore the city.
Day 4 Hike the Inca Trail to Machu Picchu.
Day 5 Explore the ruins of Machu Picchu and take the train back to Cusco.
Day 6 Take a day trip to the Sacred Valley.
Day 7 Depart Peru.

Food and Drink Scene of Peru

Peruvian cuisine is delicious and unique, and here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: This dish consists of raw fish marinated in lime juice, chili, and onions.

  • Lomo Saltado: This dish is made from stir-fried beef, tomatoes, onions, and french fries.

  • Aji de Gallina: This creamy chicken dish is made with aji amarillo peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco Sour: This cocktail is made with pisco (a grape brandy), lime juice, egg whites, and simple syrup.

  • Chicha Morada: This non-alcoholic beverage is made from purple corn and spices and is often served with meals.

Where to Stay in Peru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peru offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Belmond Sanctuary Lodge: This hotel is located near Machu Picchu and offers stunning views of the ruins.

  • Selina: This hostel chain has several locations in Peru and offers affordable accommodation for budget travelers.

  • Airbnb: Renting an Airbnb is a great way to save money and get a more local experience.

What to Buy in Peru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peru is known for its unique handicrafts and textiles, and some of the best souvenirs to buy include:

  • Alpaca wool products: Sweaters, scarves, and blankets made from alpaca wool make great gifts.

  • Peruvian textiles: Handwoven textiles, like tapestries and rugs, are colorful and unique.

  • Pottery: Peru is known for its pottery, like Cusco’s famous ceramic bulls.

Local Culture and Customs of Peru

Peru has a rich culture and history, and here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Language: The official language of Peru is Spanish, but many people also speak Quechua.

  • Currency: The currency of Peru is the Peruvian sol.

  • Greeting: It is customary to shake hands or give a light kiss on the cheek when greeting someone.

Best Time to Visit Peru

The best time to visit Peru is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is mild, and there is less rainfall during this time, making it ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peru

When traveling to Peru, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the food: Peruvian cuisine is delicious, so be sure to try as much as you can.

  • Learn some Spanish: Knowing some basic Spanish phrases can make your trip easier and more enjoyable.

  • Be respectful: Peru has a rich cultural heritage, so be respectful of the customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: Stick to bottled water to avoid getting sick.

  • Leave valuables unattended: Keep your valuables safe and with you at all times.

  • Forget sunscreen: The sun can be strong in Peru, so wear sunscreen to avoid getting burned.

Safety and Health

Peru is a relatively safe country, but it’s always important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Peru

  • Take taxis at night: Walking alone at night can be dangerous, so take a taxi instead.

  • Watch your belongings: Be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings close at all times.

  • Avoid political demonstrations: If there are political demonstrations, it’s best to avoid them.

Health in Peru

  • Get vaccinated: Make sure you’re up to date on all your vaccinations, including yellow fever, before traveling to Peru.

  • Be mindful of altitude sickness: Many areas in Peru are at high altitudes, so be aware of the symptoms of altitude sickness and take steps to prevent it.

Getting to Peru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Peru is easier than you might think, and here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Peru

  • Jorge Chavez International Airport: Located in Lima, this is the largest airport in Peru.

  • Alejandro Velasco Astete International Airport: This airport is located in Cusco and is a popular gateway to Machu Picchu.

Bus Stations Near Peru

  • Cruz del Sur: This bus company offers comfortable coaches with reclining seats, onboard meals, and Wi-Fi.

  • Oltursa: This bus company offers overnight buses with reclining seats and air conditioning.

Other Options

  • Train: The Cusco to Machu Picchu train offers a scenic journey through the Andes.

How to Get Around in Peru

Getting around Peru is easy, and here are some transportation methods:

  • Bus: Buses are a budget-friendly option for getting around Peru.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Peru and are a great way to get around the cities.

  • Train: Trains are a great option for getting to Machu Picchu.

Where to Go Next from Peru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peru is surrounded by other beautiful countries, and here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Bolivia: Visit Lake Titicaca and explore the Uyuni Salt Flats.

  • Chile: Visit the Atacama Desert and the beautiful landscapes of Patagonia.

  • Ecuador: Explore the Galapagos Islands and the Amazon Rainforest.

Conclusion

Peru is a beautiful country with so much to offer tourists. From the ancient ruins of Machu Picchu to the bustling city of Lima, there is something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to plan your trip to Peru with ease.
