Introduction to Syracuse

Syracuse is a charming town located in Northern Indiana, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. It’s conveniently situated between the cosmopolitan city of Chicago and the bustling metropolis of Indianapolis, making it an ideal destination for travelers who want to explore the natural beauty of Indiana. The town is known for its diverse culture, historical landmarks, and adventurous outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Syracuse

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Syracuse is rich in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical landmarks worth visiting. Some of the must-see attractions include:

  • Turtle Days - A festival in June that celebrates the town’s unique connection to turtles.
  • Wawasee Area Conservancy Foundation - A nature preserve that offers beautiful scenic views of the lake and hosts several events throughout the year.
  • Eli Lilly Cultural Center - A museum dedicated to the life and accomplishments of Eli Lilly, the founder of the Eli Lilly pharmaceutical company.
  • Syracuse-Wawasee Historical Museum - A museum that showcases the history of the town and its people.

Natural Attractions of Syracuse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Syracuse is surrounded by natural beauty and offers plenty of outdoor activities for adventurous travelers. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit in Syracuse:

  • Lake Wawasee - The largest lake in Indiana, Lake Wawasee is a popular spot for boating, fishing, swimming, and other water activities.
  • Syracuse Lake - A smaller lake that offers scenic views and is a great spot for fishing and paddleboarding.
  • Syracuse-Wawasee Trail - A 11-mile trail that offers scenic views of Syracuse Lake and is perfect for hiking, biking, and walking.
  • Oakwood Resort - A historic resort that offers access to the lake, beach, and outdoor activities like mini-golf and tennis.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Syracuse

Syracuse is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several excellent hiking and biking routes in the area. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Syracuse-Wawasee Trail - A 11-mile trail that runs from Syracuse to Wawasee and offers scenic views of the lake.
  • Ride+Walk Warsaw+Winona Lake - A 14-mile loop that connects the towns of Warsaw and Winona Lake and offers a mix of urban and rural scenery.
  • Pike Lake Trail - A 3.5-mile trail that meanders through Pike Lake and offers access to several scenic viewpoints.

Best Things to Do in Syracuse: Activities and Tours

Whether you want to explore the natural beauty of the area or dive into the town’s rich culture and history, there are plenty of things to do in Syracuse. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Rent a Boat - There’s no better way to explore the lakes of Syracuse than by boat. Rent a pontoon, jet ski, or kayak and spend the day on the water.
  • Take a Walking Tour - Join a local guide and explore the town’s historical landmarks on foot.
  • Visit a Winery - Indiana’s wine country is just a short drive from Syracuse, and there are several excellent wineries to visit in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Syracuse

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Syracuse, here’s a sample itinerary that will allow you to experience the best of what the town has to offer:

Day 1: Arrive in Syracuse and check into your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the town’s historic district.

Day 2: Rent a boat and spend the day on Lake Wawasee. Be sure to stop at one of the lakeside restaurants for lunch or dinner.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Syracuse-Wawasee Trail and explore the surrounding natural beauty.

Day 4: Visit a local winery and enjoy a tasting of Indiana wines.

Day 5: Spend the day at Oakwood Resort. Take a dip in the lake, enjoy a game of mini-golf or tennis, and relax on the beach.

Day 6: Visit the Eli Lilly Cultural Center and learn about the life and accomplishments of Eli Lilly.

Day 7: Take one last stroll through the town’s historic district before departing for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Syracuse

Must-Try Dishes

Syracuse has a vibrant food scene that reflects the town’s diverse culture and history. Here are some dishes that you must try when visiting Syracuse:

  • Turtle Soup - A local delicacy that’s available at many of the town’s restaurants.
  • Indiana Cornbread - A twist on traditional cornbread that’s made with local Indiana corn.
  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich - A Hoosier classic that’s a must-try when visiting Indiana.

Local Beverages

Indiana is known for its brewing scene, and Syracuse is no exception. Here are some local beverages to check out:

  • Mad Anthony Brewing Company - A brewery that offers a wide variety of brews and delicious food.
  • Two-EE’s Winery - A local winery that specializes in small-batch wines made from local grapes.
  • Zorn Brew Works - A brewery located in nearby Warsaw that offers a rotating selection of craft brews.

Where to Stay in Syracuse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Syracuse offers several accommodation options for travelers, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a cozy guesthouse. Here are some of the best places to stay in Syracuse:

  • Oakwood Resort - A historic resort located on the shores of Lake Wawasee.
  • Brook Pointe Inn - A modern hotel with lakefront views and excellent amenities.
  • The Frog and Peach - A cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of the town’s historic district.

What to Buy in Syracuse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back home, Syracuse has plenty of options. Here are some of the best places to shop for local products:

  • The Pier and Back Porch - A boutique that sells unique gifts, accessories, and home decor.
  • JoJo’s Pretzels - A local bakery that sells delicious hand-twisted pretzels.
  • Pickwick Place - A farm market that sells fresh produce, handmade crafts, and locally-made products.

Local Culture and Customs of Syracuse

Syracuse is a friendly town that welcomes visitors with open arms. Here are some local customs and etiquette to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect the Environment - Syracuse is known for its natural beauty, and it’s important to be respectful of the environment by leaving no trace and picking up after yourself.
  • Be Polite - Hoosiers are known for their friendliness, and it’s important to be polite and respectful to everyone you meet.
  • Turtle Days - If you’re visiting in June, be sure to participate in the town’s annual Turtle Days festival.

Best Time to Visit Syracuse

The best time to visit Syracuse is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also beautiful during the fall months, when the leaves change colors and the air is crisp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Syracuse

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Syracuse.
  • Do visit local wineries and breweries.
  • Do participate in the town’s cultural events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the natural areas.
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to locals or other travelers.
  • Don’t miss the town’s historical landmarks and museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in Syracuse

Syracuse is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, it’s important to take general safety precautions, such as locking your car and home, and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Syracuse

Syracuse has several medical facilities for emergency care and urgent care. However, it’s always important to travel with health insurance and to take prophylactic measures, such as using bug spray to prevent tick and mosquito bites.

Getting to Syracuse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Syracuse

The closest airport to Syracuse is Fort Wayne International Airport, located about an hour’s drive away. The airport offers regular flights to major U.S. cities.

Bus Stations Near Syracuse

Greyhound bus stations are located in Fort Wayne and South Bend, both of which are about an hour’s drive away from Syracuse.

Other Options

Syracuse is located along U.S. Route 6 and Indiana State Road 13, making it easily accessible by car. However, there are no public transportation options within the town.

How to Get Around in Syracuse

The best way to get around Syracuse is by car, as there are no public transportation options within the town. Rental cars are available at major airports and in nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Syracuse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Syracuse is located in a beautiful area of Indiana, with several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring. Here are some of the best options:

  • Fort Wayne - A larger city located about an hour’s drive away from Syracuse that offers several cultural attractions and outdoor activities.
  • Amish Country - Located about 45 minutes away from Syracuse, Amish Country is known for its beautiful countryside and unique culture.
  • Shipshe - A charming town located about an hour’s drive away from Syracuse that offers shopping, dining, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Syracuse is a hidden gem in the Midwest, offering a unique mix of culture, history, and outdoor adventures. Plan your trip today and experience the natural beauty and warm hospitality of this charming town.
