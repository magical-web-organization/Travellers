Northwestern Indiana is a region located in the Midwest of the United States of America. Known for its rich history and natural beauty, this area attracts visitors from around the world. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and other important information to help plan your trip to Northwestern Indiana.

Introduction to Northwestern Indiana

Northwestern Indiana is a region located in the northwestern part of the state of Indiana. The region spans across six counties, including Lake, Porter, LaPorte, Newton, Jasper, and Pulaski counties. The region is bordered by Lake Michigan to the north and is situated near the borders of Illinois and Michigan. Northwestern Indiana is a popular destination for travelers looking to explore the Midwest and its natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Northwestern Indiana

Northwestern Indiana is brimming with historical sites, museums, and outdoor recreational activities. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Indiana Dunes National Park: Home to over 15,000 acres of sand dunes, forests, wetlands, and prairies, this park is perfect for hiking, biking, and beach activities.
  • John Dillinger Museum: This museum houses artifacts and exhibits chronicling the life and times of notorious bank robber John Dillinger.
  • The Barker Mansion: Built in the 1800s, this stunning mansion features Romanesque architecture and is open to the public for tours.
  • Lubeznik Center for the Arts: This contemporary art museum features rotating exhibitions and is a hub for art and culture in the region.

Natural Attractions of Northwestern Indiana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Indiana Dunes State Park: A beautiful natural area with breathtaking views of Lake Michigan, hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and camping.
  • Jasper-Pulaski Fish and Wildlife Area: A popular spot for bird-watching, particularly Sandhill Cranes during their migration season.
  • Taltree Arboretum & Gardens: A serene park with a variety of gardens showcasing native plants from the region.
  • West Beach: A beautiful beach on the shores of Lake Michigan, featuring miles of sandy coastline and clear blue waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwestern Indiana

Northwestern Indiana offers many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts to take in the natural beauty of the region. Here are some popular routes:

  • Cowles Bog Trail: A moderate hiking trail that takes you through dunes, forests, and wetlands of Indiana Dunes National Park.
  • Marquette Park Trail: A paved trail in Gary that offers stunning views of Lake Michigan and is great for biking and walking.
  • Prairie Duneland Trail: A 9.5-mile path that connects Chesterton and Porter, passing through stunning wetlands and forests.
  • Erie Lackawanna Trail: A 17-mile multipurpose trail that passes through Hobart, Lake Station, and connects to Crown Point.

Best Things to Do in Northwestern Indiana: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Northwestern Indiana can enjoy a variety of activities and tours:

  • Brewery and Winery Tours: Northwestern Indiana is home to many craft breweries and wineries, offering tours and tastings for guests.
  • Skydiving: Experienced skydivers and beginners alike can take the plunge and experience the thrill of jumping out of a plane.
  • Fishing: Northwestern Indiana is home to many fishing spots, including rivers, lakes, and streams.
  • Golf: Golf enthusiasts can enjoy a game on one of Northwestern Indiana’s many golf courses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northwestern Indiana

Day 1-2: Explore Indiana Dunes National Park and surrounding areas, including hiking trails and beaches.

Day 3-4: Visit the John Dillinger Museum and Barker Mansion, and take a stroll through downtown Michigan City and Chesterton.

Day 5-6: Enjoy outdoor activities such as fishing, biking, and golfing in the region.

Day 7: End your trip with a relaxing visit to a local spa or winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwestern Indiana

Northwestern Indiana boasts a diverse food and drink scene, including ethnic cuisine and craft breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogi: A Polish dumpling filled with savory ingredients such as potato and cheese.
  • Italian Beef Sandwiches: Thinly sliced beef piled high on a hoagie roll, topped with peppers and other condiments.
  • Fruit Pies: Indiana is known for its many fruit orchards, producing delicious pies filled with apples, berries, and other seasonal fruits.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Northwestern Indiana is home to many award-winning craft breweries, producing a variety of styles.
  • Wine: Wine enthusiasts can enjoy a visit to one of the many wineries in the region, offering tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Northwestern Indiana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northwestern Indiana offers a variety of lodging options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Blue Chip Casino Hotel and Spa: A luxury hotel and spa featuring a casino, restaurants, and bars.
  • Comfort Inn and Suites: A budget-friendly option located in Michigan City, offering amenities such as free breakfast and Wi-Fi.
  • Serenity Springs: A unique resort offering private cabins and glamping tents, perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Northwestern Indiana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Northwestern Indiana, be sure to check out these local products:

  • Perogies and Sausages: Visit a local Polish deli to bring home some authentic perogies and sausages.
  • Indiana Dunes Merchandise: Souvenirs such as t-shirts, hats, and postcards are available at many local shops and attractions.
  • Handcrafted Items: Northwestern Indiana is home to many artisans and crafters, offering handmade goods such as pottery and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwestern Indiana

Northwestern Indiana has a rich history and strong sense of community. Visitors can expect to experience friendly Midwestern hospitality and a love of sports, particularly basketball and football.

Best Time to Visit Northwestern Indiana

The best time to visit Northwestern Indiana is in the spring and fall when temperatures are mild and the landscape is full of color. Summer is also a popular time to visit for outdoor activities and beach days. Winter can be cold and snowy, but visitors can enjoy winter sports such as sledding and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwestern Indiana

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the region, including Indiana Dunes National Park.
  • Do attend a local festival or event, such as the Pierogi Fest in Whiting or Porter County Fair.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather, be prepared for sudden changes in temperature and weather conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwestern Indiana

Northwestern Indiana is a safe destination, but visitors should take standard safety precautions such as locking car doors and being aware of your surroundings in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Northwestern Indiana

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Northwestern Indiana. The region has a variety of hospitals and medical facilities should medical attention be needed.

How to Get Around in Northwestern Indiana

Visitors can get around Northwestern Indiana by car, bike, or public transportation. The South Shore Line provides train service to several cities in the region, including Michigan City and Gary.

Where to Go Next from Northwestern Indiana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Northwestern Indiana, visitors can venture out to nearby destinations:

  • Chicago, Illinois: The bustling city of Chicago is just a short drive or train ride from Northwestern Indiana.
  • Indianapolis, Indiana: The state capital of Indiana offers museums, shopping, and dining experiences.
  • St. Joseph, Michigan: A charming beach town located on Lake Michigan, offering beautiful sandy beaches, attractions, and dining options.

Conclusion

Northwestern Indiana offers a rich history and natural beauty that is sure to delight visitors. From hiking and biking to wineries and museums, this region has something for everyone. With its Midwestern charm and friendly hospitality, Northwestern Indiana is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
