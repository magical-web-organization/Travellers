Introduction to Kentland

Nestled in the heart of Northwestern Indiana, Kentland, Indiana, is a small town that packs a big punch. Founded in 1860, Kentland’s history and charm beckon visitors to explore its cultural landmarks, natural attractions, food and drink scenes, and more. Whether you’re looking for a weekend escape or a longer adventure, Kentland offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kentland

To make the most of your trip to Kentland, here’s a list of the top places to see and things to do:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kentland is steeped in history, and these museums and historical places showcase its rich past:

  • Kentland Depot – A beautifully preserved train station that is now home to the Newton County Historical Society Museum, showcasing the history of the county from its Native American origins to modern times.
  • St. Joseph’s Catholic Church – A charming church built in 1899 and added to the National Register of Historic Places in 2003. Visitors can attend mass or appreciate the stunning architecture and stained-glass windows.
  • Benton County Wind Farm and Windmill Museum – Learn about the history of windmills and their importance to rural life in this fascinating museum.

Natural Attractions of Kentland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, you’ll want to visit these natural attractions in Kentland:

  • Stewart-Greenebaum Nature Preserve – A beautiful conservation area with over 300 acres of hiking trails, wetlands, and woodlands. Visitors can also bird-watch, fish, and observe natural wildlife.
  • Newton County Amish Country – Witness picturesque Amish farms, artisan shops, and horse-drawn carriages in this serene and rustic setting.
  • Kent’s Caves – Explore Indiana’s natural history by visiting the only public cave in the state, featuring fossils and rocks from over 400 million years ago.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kentland

Kentland offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region. If you’re ready to hit the trails, check out these spots:

  • Wabash River Trail – This scenic route runs along the Wabash River, offering 18 miles of biking and hiking trails through stunning terrain and wildlife.
  • Kentland Trail System – Explore the Kentland area on foot or bike through the 14 miles of trails within the Kentland Park and Nature Preserve.

Best Things to Do in Kentland: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for unique and exciting things to do, Kentland offers these top activities and tours:

  • Wine Tasting Tour – Visit Blackhawk Winery to taste their award-winning wines, participate in vineyard tours, and enjoy the scenic view of the countryside.
  • Country Living Trail – Meet local farmers, artisans, and beekeepers on this exciting tour and learn about their daily lives and experiences.
  • Camping Under the Stars – Pitch a tent and escape city life in the peaceful and serene Parks and Nature Preserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kentland

For those seeking a week-long adventure, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Kentland, visit the Kentland Depot and take a walking tour of the quaint downtown area
2 Spend the day exploring the natural beauty of Stewart-Greenebaum Nature Preserve and the Wabash River Trail
3 Immerse yourself in Amish life during the Country Living Trail
4 Visit the Benton County Wind Farm and Windmill Museum, and then take a relaxing walk through Kent’s Caves
5 Taste award-winning wines and enjoy the views at Blackhawk Winery
6 Camp under the stars in the Parks and Nature Preserve
7 Depart Kentland with memories to last a lifetime

Food and Drink Scene of Kentland

Indulge your taste buds in the culinary delights of Kentland and try some of these must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gus’s Family Restaurant – Famous for its famous fried chicken and home-style cooking.
  • KFC Express – The best spot for Kentucky Fried Chicken.
  • Arni’s Restaurant – Offering world-famous pizza that is baked to perfection every time.

Local Beverages

  • Blackhawk Winery – Sample award-winning wine, made from locally grown grapes.
  • Homestead Winery – Sip a glass of unique wine and tour the vineyard.

Where to Stay in Kentland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Super 8 by Wyndham Kentland – A budget-friendly hotel with convenient amenities, including breakfast, free Wi-Fi, and cable TV.
  • Shamrock Motel – A family-owned motel featuring clean and comfortable rooms.
  • The Kentland Inn – A quaint bed and breakfast with cozy rooms and home-cooked breakfast.

What to Buy in Kentland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of Kentland with these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Homestead Winery Wine – A bottle of wine made from locally grown grapes.
  • Kent’s Caves Memorabilia – Take home a piece of the only public cave in Indiana.
  • Amish Quilts and Crafts – Pick up some handmade crafts or quilts from the Amish communities in Newton County.

Local Culture and Customs of Kentland

Kentland’s vibrant culture and friendly people are reflected in its unique customs and traditions. Visitors should be aware of these cultural norms:

  • Respectful Behavior – Kentland residents prioritize a respectful and humble approach to visitors.
  • Religious Observance – A significant part of the community is made up of religious followers and churchgoers.

Best Time to Visit Kentland

The best time to visit Kentland is from late spring to early fall, where you can enjoy the beautiful scenery and experience outdoor activities without worrying about the weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kentland

When visiting Kentland, keep these guidelines in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the Local Culture – Be respectful and mindful of the local customs and culture.
  • Enjoy the Outdoors – Take advantage of the town’s hiking and biking trails, nature parks, and scenic outdoor views.

Don’t:

  • Litter – Visitors must dispose of their trash in designated areas.
  • Disrespect Public Property – Do not vandalize or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Kentland offers a safe and healthy environment for tourists.

Safety in Kentland

In case of any emergencies, visitors may reach out to local emergency services.

  • Kentland Police Department – 911
  • Newton County Sheriff’s Office – 219-474-5661

Health in Kentland

Visitors must carry necessary medication or vaccines. In case of any emergencies, these health services are available:

  • Kentland Clinic – A local medical center with all facilities.
  • Kentland Community Pharmacy – A full-service pharmacy operating for the people of Kentland.

Visitors can get around Kentland by renting cars, using bike paths, or walking.

Where to Go Next from Kentland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kentland is situated in the heart of Northwestern Indiana and is close to many popular attractions. These nearby destinations offer exciting excursions and add extra fun to your trip:

  • Chicago, Illinois – Explore the famous Chicago skyline, architecture, and cuisine, a short drive from Kentland.
  • Lafayette – Visit the home of Purdue University and explore its attractions, including art and history museums, parks, and gardens.
  • Indianapolis – Indiana’s capital city offers a range of activities, including cultural landmarks, sports venues, museums, and zoos.

Conclusion

Kentland, Indiana, offers visitors a refreshing escape from the demands of everyday life. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, Kentland has something to offer everyone. We hope this guide serves as a helpful starting point for your exciting trip to Kentland.
