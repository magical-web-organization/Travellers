Introduction to Chesterton

Located in the heart of Northwestern Indiana, Chesterton is a charming town that offers visitors a delightful mix of history, natural beauty, and small-town charm. Surrounded by forests, rolling hills, and nestled along the shores of Lake Michigan, Chesterton is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers, as well as for those who enjoy cultural attractions and unique local experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Chesterton

Chesterton offers a variety of things to see and do, from historic sites and museums to natural wonders and outdoor activities. Here are some of the must-visit places in Chesterton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Westchester Township History Museum: dedicated to preserving the history of Chesterton and the surrounding area, this museum features exhibits on local history, industry, and culture.
  • Bailly Homestead and Chellberg Farm: this living history museum showcases life in the 1800s along the Little Calumet River, with tours of the Bailly Homestead and Chellberg farmhouses.
  • Dunes National Park Visitor Center: located at the entrance of Indiana Dunes National Park, this center features exhibits and information about the park’s history, ecosystems, and recreational opportunities.

Natural Attractions of Chesterton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Indiana Dunes National Park: a breathtaking national park on the southern shore of Lake Michigan, renowned for its sandy beaches, towering sand dunes, and diverse ecosystems.
  • Coffee Creek Watershed Preserve: a stunning natural park with trails, ponds, and wetlands, perfect for hiking, birding, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Cowles Bog Trail: a scenic hiking trail through a diverse ecosystem of marshes, dunes, and forests, with stunning views of Lake Michigan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chesterton

Chesterton and the surrounding area offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the top routes and trails to explore include:

  • Calumet Trail: a scenic trail that follows the historic route of the South Shore Railroad and offers excellent views of the dunes and Lake Michigan.
  • Indiana Dunes State Park Trails: a network of trails that offers a variety of hiking experiences, from rugged backcountry hikes to leisurely walks through the forest.
  • The Prairie Duneland Trail: a paved trail that connects Chesterton with nearby towns and offers a great way to explore the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Chesterton: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the natural wonders of Chesterton, there are also plenty of other fun activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Chesterton:

  • Sunset Cruise on Lake Michigan: a relaxing cruise along the shores of Lake Michigan, with stunning views of the sunset and the surrounding dunes.
  • Vintage Train Ride: a nostalgic ride on a vintage train through the scenic countryside, with stops at historic towns and landmarks.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: a delightful tour of the local wineries and vineyards, featuring tastings of the best local wines and gourmet foods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chesterton

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long vacation in Chesterton, showcasing the best that the town and the surrounding area have to offer:

Food and Drink Scene of Chesterton

With a variety of locally-owned restaurants, cafes, and bars, Chesterton has a vibrant food and drink scene, with something for every palate. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roasted Corn: a seasonal snack that is a staple at local markets and festivals, roasted until it’s golden and packed with flavor.
  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: a classic Midwest dish that features a breaded and fried pork tenderloin cutlet, served on a bun with toppings of your choice.
  • Chicago-style Deep Dish Pizza: just a short drive away from Chesterton, Chicago is home to the famous deep-dish pizza, with a thick crust, plenty of cheese, and lots of toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Indiana Wine: there are several wineries in the Chesterton area that produce a variety of award-winning wines, including dry reds, crisp whites, and fruity rosés.
  • Three Floyds Brewery: this internationally renowned microbrewery is located just a few miles from Chesterton and offers a variety of famous craft beers, such as Zombie Dust, Gumballhead, and Alpha King.

Where to Stay in Chesterton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Chesterton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a unique souvenir or a gift for a loved one, consider these local products and specialties:

  • Indiana Dunes-themed souvenirs: from t-shirts to mugs, there are plenty of Indiana Dunes-themed souvenirs that you can bring home to remember your trip.
  • Artisanal Cheese: Indiana is home to several artisanal cheese makers, who produce a variety of delicious cheeses, including cheddar, gouda, and blue cheese.
  • Handcrafted Pottery and Ceramics: several local artists and potters create beautiful and unique pottery and ceramics inspired by the natural beauty of the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Chesterton

Chesterton is a town that takes pride in its heritage and traditions, and visitors are welcome to participate and observe. Some of the local customs and events to keep in mind include:

  • Roasting of Chestnuts: every year in November, Chesterton celebrates the Roasting of Chestnuts, a historic tradition that dates back to the 19th century.
  • Fourth of July Parade: a popular event in Chesterton that features a colorful parade, live music, and fireworks.
  • Christmas Craft Show: this annual craft show, held in early December, showcases the work of local artisans and craftsmen and offers unique gifts and decorations for the holiday season.

Best Time to Visit Chesterton

The best time to visit Chesterton depends on your preferences and interests. Here are some things to consider:

  • Summer (June-August): this is the peak tourist season, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and outdoor attractions are bustling with activity.
  • Fall (September-November): this is a great time to visit if you want to enjoy the fall foliage, visit the local orchards for apple picking, and attend the local festivals and events.
  • Winter (December-February): while the weather can be cold and snowy, this is a great time to enjoy winter sports, such as skiing and snowshoeing, and to attend the local holiday events and markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chesterton

To make the most of your trip to Chesterton, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the Outdoors: Chesterton and the surrounding area offer plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, boating, and fishing, so make sure you take advantage of the natural beauty of the region.
  • Enjoy the Local Cuisine: Chesterton has a vibrant food scene, so make sure you try the local specialties, such as roasted corn and pork tenderloin sandwiches.
  • Attend Local Events and Festivals: Chesterton and the surrounding area are home to several festivals and events throughout the year that celebrate the local heritage and culture.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the Local Culture: Chesterton has a rich history and cultural heritage, so make sure you take the time to learn about the local customs and traditions and participate in the local events.
  • Neglect Safety Precautions: as with any destination, make sure you take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car, staying aware of your surroundings, and following local traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Chesterton is a safe and welcoming destination for travelers. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Chesterton

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: while Chesterton is a safe town, like any destination, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and to avoid any unsafe situations.
  • Lock your car: make sure you lock your car and don’t leave valuables or important documents inside.
  • Follow traffic laws: make sure you follow all traffic laws and be especially cautious when driving in winter conditions.

Health in Chesterton

  • Stay hydrated: make sure you drink plenty of water, especially if you’re exploring the outdoors or engaging in physical activities.
  • Protect against mosquitoes and ticks: if you’re hiking or spending time outdoors in the summer, make sure you apply bug repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites or tick-borne illnesses.
  • Be prepared for seasonal allergies: pollen and other allergens can be a problem in the Midwest, so make sure you bring appropriate medications or remedies if you suffer from allergies.

Getting to Chesterton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chesterton is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some of the transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Chesterton

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport (ORD): located about 60 miles northwest of Chesterton, O’Hare is a major hub for domestic and international flights.
  • Chicago Midway International Airport (MDW): located about 50 miles northwest of Chesterton, Midway offers flights with several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Chesterton

  • South Shore Line Train: a commuter train that runs between Chesterton and downtown Chicago, with several stops along the way.

Other Options

  • Driving: Chesterton is easily accessible by car, with several major highways and interstates passing through the region.
  • Ridesharing services: several ridesharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, are available in the Chesterton area.

How to Get Around in Chesterton

While Chesterton is a small town, there are still several transportation options available to visitors:

  • Walking or Biking: many of the attractions in downtown Chesterton are easily accessible on foot, and several bike rental services are available for visitors who want to explore the town and the surrounding area on two wheels.
  • Driving: car rental services are available in the Chesterton area, and visitors can explore the surrounding area at their own pace.
  • Public Transportation: the South Shore Line Train offers a commuter service between Chesterton and downtown Chicago, with several stops along the way.

Where to Go Next from Chesterton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Midwest and the surrounding area, Chesterton is the perfect starting point. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Chicago: just a short train or car ride away, Chicago offers a wealth of attractions, from world-class museums and restaurants to theaters and nightlife.
  • Amish Country: located in nearby Elkhart and LaGrange counties, Amish Country offers a glimpse into the unique culture and lifestyle of the Amish community, with traditional crafts, food, and music on display.

Conclusion
