Introduction to Hobart

Located in Lake County, Northwestern Indiana, Hobart is a city with a population of 29,059. Hobart’s rich past, thriving present, and natural beauty make it an ideal destination for tourists. With several iconic museums, delicious food, scenic vistas, and exciting outdoor activities, Hobart is an excellent getaway for families, solo travelers, and adventure seekers. Let’s dive into the places to visit and things to do in Hobart.

List of Places to Visit in Hobart

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hobart boasts several historically significant museums and places that are a must-visit for every traveler.

1. Hobart Historical Society Museum

Hobart Historical Society Museum located at 605 E 4th St, is an excellent starting point to explore the city’s past. The museum has a rotating exhibit that highlights different aspects of Hobart’s history, including the brick-making industry, schools, and businesses.

2. Collier Lodge

Another must-visit historical attraction in Hobart is the Collier Lodge. Located at 1074 South Main Street, the Collier Lodge was historically known as the Last Confederate Headquarters. The lodge has now been converted into a museum with a small collection of artifacts from this era.

3. Deep River County Park

Deep River County Park, located at 9410 Old Lincoln Hwy, is a historic park that was established in 1970, has several exciting features. The park has preserved several historic landmarks, including an 1874 Porter County Jail and an 1885 Angelin Cottage that offer a glimpse into life in the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Hobart: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hobart’s natural beauty is unparalleled and has several outdoor activities for all nature lovers.

1. Hobart Marsh

Hobart Marsh, located at Liverpool Road and Lake Park Avenue, is one of the largest freshwater marshes in Northwest Indiana. The area is home to a diverse range of waterfowl, migratory birds, and reptiles.

2. Lake George

Surrounded by a park, Lake George is an excellent spot for a picnic, hiking, or fishing. The lake has several boat ramps and fishing piers that are open during the summer months.

3. Hidden Lake Park

Hidden Lake Park is a 1.3-mile long trail that is ideal for hikers and joggers. The park features a picturesque waterfall, a small beach, and several beautiful gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hobart

There are several hiking and biking routes in Hobart that are perfect for those who are looking for an adventure.

1. Hobart Prairie Grove Trail

Situated on the former Detroit, Toledo, and Ironton railway line, Hobart Prairie Grove Trail is a 2-mile long nature trail that is ideal for hiking and biking. The trail offers several scenic views of the wetlands and natural grasses.

2. Captain Robert E. Hartman

Captain Robert E. Hartman, located at 528 W. Old Ridge Road, is a 2.5-mile trail that offers scenic views of Hobart’s wetlands and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Hobart: Activities and Tours

Hobart offers several exciting things to do, from exploring its rich history to experiencing its natural beauty.

1. Touring Hobart’s Historic Sites

Hobart features several historical landmarks that are must-visits for history buffs. Take a walking tour of the city’s historic downtown that features several home-like structures, including the historic County Courthouse, the Hobart Opera House, and the first city hall.

2. Enjoying Sports and Adventure Activities

Hobart offers several sports and adventure activities that range from skating to playing a round of golf. Deep River County Park offers picnic and playground areas, fishing, biking and nature trails, basketball/skateboarding areas, and even an ice-skating rink in the winter. Additionally, the Oak Hill Park offers an 18-hole golf course.

Food and Drink Scene of Hobart

Hobart has something to offer for every palate, from classic American cuisine to authentic Mexican and Italian cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Teddy’s Red Hots

Located at 628 E 3rd St, Teddy’s Red Hots serves a delicious hot dog that features a natural-casing with a secret spice mix that makes it stand out.

2. El Taco Real

El Taco Real is one of Hobart’s most popular Mexican restaurants. The restaurant serves authentic Mexican food that includes tacos, burritos, and enchiladas.

Local Beverages

1. Winerak Market and Tapas Bar

Winerak Market and Tapas Bar, located at 620 E. 3rd Street, offers a wide selection of wines from various regions in America and around the world.

2. 3 Floyd’s Brewing

3 Floyd’s Brewing, located at 9750 Indiana Pkwy, Munster, is the perfect spot for beer lovers. The brewery offers award-winning beers that are sure to tantalize your taste buds.

Where to Stay in Hobart: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hobart offers several accommodation options, from budget-friendly hotels to luxurious suites. Here are a few recommendations:

1. Best Western Hobart

Best Western Hobart, located at 343 W. U.S. Route 30, offers comfortable, modern rooms with free Wi-Fi, an indoor pool, and a complimentary breakfast buffet.

2. Comfort Inn Hobart

Comfort Inn Hobart, located at 1915 Mississippi St, offers family-friendly accommodation, including a kids’ game room and pet-friendly rooms.

What to Buy in Hobart: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several local products in Hobart that are worth taking home as souvenirs. One of the popular souvenirs is handmade bricks that are produced by the city’s brick-making industry. Deep River County Park also features a gift shop where you can find several unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hobart

Hobart is known for its rich history and traditional American culture. It is not unusual to see people sporting apparel supporting the town’s high school athletic teams. Additionally, Hobart is home to several festivals and events that showcase the town’s cultural heritage, including the Lake County Fair and the Brickie Bowl Football Game.

Best Time to Visit Hobart

The best time to visit Hobart is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hobart

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their property.
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the parks and nature reserves.
  • Try the hotdogs from Teddy’s Red Hots.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the city parks and on the hiking/biking trails.
  • Avoid visiting areas that feel unsafe or are advised by the locals.

Safety and Health

Hobart is generally a safe city, but it is essential to take standard safety precautions, especially when hiking or walking alone.

Safety in Hobart

  • Avoid walking alone in areas that are isolated.
  • Lock the doors of the car when you park in the city’s residential areas.
  • Be cautious about pickpocketing tourist traps.

Health in Hobart

Hobart’s healthcare facilities provide comprehensive health care. However, don’t forget to bring bug spray or sunscreen, depending on the season.
