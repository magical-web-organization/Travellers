Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Lake Station, Indiana

Nestled in the northwestern corner of Indiana, Lake Station is one of the great Midwest’s hidden gems. With a rich cultural heritage and a beautiful natural landscape, there is something for everyone to enjoy. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top places to visit, the best things to do, and local culture and customs to help you plan your perfect trip to Lake Station.

Introduction to Lake Station

Lake Station is a city in Lake County, Indiana, situated in the northern part of the state. The city is known for its manufacturing and its proximity to some of the region’s most beautiful natural attractions. Many visitors are drawn to the city’s rich cultural heritage, as there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Station

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lake Station has a rich history that visitors can learn about by exploring the city’s museums and historical places. Here are a few must-visit sites:

  • Lake Station Historical Society Museum - A museum that offers visitors a chance to learn about the city’s history through artifacts and displays.

  • Hobart Art Theater - A historic theater that still screens movies and hosts live performances on occasion.

  • Old City Hall Museum - A museum that explores the history of Lake Station through displays with collections of artifacts and photos.

Natural Attractions of Lake Station: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Station’s natural landscape is as diverse as it is beautiful, and there are plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic vistas. Here are a few places worth visiting:

  • Lake Etta County Park - A beautiful park with playgrounds, hiking trails and fishing ponds.

  • Deep River Park - A park with an abundance of outdoor activities like hiking, mountain biking, picnicking, fishing, and kayaking.

  • Buffington Harbor - A beautiful harbor with access to the Lake Michigan shoreline where visitors can admire the boats or take a refreshing swim.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Station

For those who love the great outdoors, Lake Station has plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are a few trails to check out:

  • The Marquette Trail - A 10-mile trail accessible for hiking and biking with a mix of surfaces including forested trails and paved bike routes.

  • Lake View Loop Trail - A beautiful 2.7-mile trail with varying terrain and a fantastic view of Lake Michigan.

  • Little Calumet River Trail - A 6-mile trail through a forested area winding its way down to the Little Calumet River.

Best Things to Do in Lake Station: Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural and cultural attractions, there are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Lake Station. Here are a few activities to put on your must-do list:

  • Indiana Dunes National Park - A popular spot for hiking, camping, fishing, and bird watching.

  • Vertical Drop Ski & Snowboard School - A place to ski or snowboard for the adventurous and the experts.

  • Indiana Dunes Birding Festival - A birdwatching festival in a grand scale with experts who lead groups that entertain nature lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Station

A week in Lake Station gives you enough time to explore the best the city has to offer thoroughly. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1:

Visit Lake Station Historical Society Museum and Hobart Art Theater.

Day 2:

Spend a day enjoying the outdoors at Lake Etta County Park and Buffington Harbor.

Day 3:

Explore the beautiful Lake View Loop Trail.

Day 4:

Discover the natural beauty of Indiana Dunes National Park.

Day 5:

Visit the Old City Hall Museum and Deep River Park, or book a session at Vertical Drop Ski & Snowboard School if visiting in winter months.

Day 6:

Go birdwatching at Indiana Dunes Birding Festival.

Day 7:

Shop for souvenirs or local products to take home and relax after a week of exploring.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Station

No trip is complete without indulging in the local food and drinks. Here are a few dishes and local beverages to try while in Lake Station:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Chicago Style Pizza - A deep-dish pizza with layers of cheese and toppings.

  • Pierogi - A Polish dish with boiled dumplings stuffed with meats, cheese, or vegetables.

  • Hand-dipped ice cream - A local favorite ice cream parlor that offers hand-dipped ice cream in different flavors.

Local Beverages:

  • Three Floyds Brewery - An award-winning brewery that offers a unique array of beers with a distinct Indiana flavor.

  • Bartlett’s Gourmet Grill - A restaurant serving bourbon, craft beer, and wine.

Where to Stay in Lake Station: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury resorts or budget-friendly options, Lake Station has accommodation choices to cater to every budget and preference. Here are some places to consider:

  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel and Suites - A modern hotel located near Lake Michigan shoreline.

  • Rodeway Inn - A budget-friendly option located next to the I-65 expressway.

  • Waterbird Lakeside Inn - A cozy, locally-owned inn located near the beautiful Lake Etta County Park.

What to Buy in Lake Station: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To bring home a piece of Lake Station, check out these souvenir shops and find the perfect memento for your trip:

  • Gifts and More of Lake Station - A place that sells a variety of handmade gifts, souvenirs, and trinkets.

  • Lake Station Flea Market - A place to hunt for antique treasures to bring home.

  • Lake Michigan Books & Gifts - A store with souvenirs with a beachy theme, unique jewelry, and diverse selections of books.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Station

To make the most of your trip to Lake Station, it’s helpful to know a bit about local customs and culture. The people of Lake Station are welcoming and friendly, and you’ll find that the city has a relaxed pace of life. The area is rich in historical significance and cultural traditions, making it an excellent destination for those interested in learning about Midwest heritage.

Best Time to Visit Lake Station

The best time to visit Lake Station is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is warm and perfect for exploring the city’s natural landscapes. Spring is lovely too, with beautiful blooms in the region. The winters in this region can be harsh and may hinder outdoor activities. For ski and snowboard enthusiasts, they can visit in winter months, once the snow has arrived, or attend any of the indoor winter activities that the city has to offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Station

Do:

  • Respect the city’s cultural sites and events.

  • Explore the natural treasures of the area.

  • Try the Chicago-style pizza and pierogi.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural areas, keep them clean.

  • Be rude to locals or other tourists.

  • Ignore the traffic rules.

Safety and Health

Lake Station is generally safe to travel or reside in, and you can explore most parts of the city without fear. Visitors should take precautions common to most travel destinations, such as keeping an eye on personal belongings and avoiding unlit, unfamiliar areas at night.

Safety in Lake Station:

  • Watch your personal belongings and avoid walking in unlit areas at night.

  • Keep the police department’s phone number handy in case of emergencies.

Health in Lake Station:

  • If you need medical care, Lake Station has a range of hospital and clinic choices to choose from.

Getting to Lake Station: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lake Station is easily accessible via different modes of transportation. Here are some of your options:

Airports Near Lake Station:

  • Chicago Midway International Airport (MDW) - 30 miles away from Lake Station

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport (ORD) - 80 miles away from Lake Station

Bus Stations Near Lake Station:

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: A terminal located in nearby Gary, Indiana.

Other Options:

  • Amtrak Train Station - Located in nearby Hammond, Indiana, this train station connects travelers to cities like Chicago, Indianapolis, and beyond.

  • Car rental service - Lake Station has car rental services to allow visitors to rent a car and move around the city with ease.

How to Get Around in Lake Station

Getting around Lake Station is simple thanks to several affordable public transportation options and car rentals. While public transport is mostly limited to the city center, it’s a convenient way to get around the area.

Visitors can also choose to rent a bike, car, or hire a cab service to get to their desired location. Hiking to the nearby sites is also an excellent choice for experiencing Lake Station’s beauty at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Lake Station: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lake Station is conveniently located near other Midwestern destinations. Take a day trip to any of these nearby cities for some additional adventure:

  • Chicago, Illinois - Famous for its architecture, food, and culture, this bustling city has something for everyone.

  • Indianapolis, Indiana - Known for its art, theater, and museums.

  • Milwaukee, Wisconsin - A beautiful city with various museums, parks, and a stunning lakefront.

Conclusion

From exploring Lake Etta County Park to trying Chicago-style pizza and pierogi, Lake Station has something for everyone. The city’s history, nature, and hospitable locals are just a few of the things that make it a must-visit destination in the Midwest. We hope that this travel guide provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip and that you have an enjoyable experience exploring the heart of Lake County, Indiana, in Lake Station.
