Introduction to Hammond

Located in Lake County, in Northwestern Indiana, Hammond is a vibrant city that offers a mix of urban and outdoor attractions. Hammond is in close proximity to Chicago, which makes it a popular destination for travelers who want to explore the region. The city is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for families, solo travelers, and couples.

List of Places to Visit in Hammond

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hammond is home to a number of cultural and historical attractions, including:

  • Hammond Historical Society and Museum
  • John Dillinger Museum
  • Indiana Welcome Center

The Hammond Historical Society and Museum features exhibits that highlight the city’s rich history, while the John Dillinger Museum pays tribute to the notorious bank robber who was born and raised in the city. The Indiana Welcome Center is a great starting point for visitors to learn more about the region and plan their trip.

Natural Attractions of Hammond: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hammond offers a variety of outdoor recreation options and scenic views, including:

  • Wolf Lake Memorial Park
  • Gibson Woods Nature Preserve
  • Calumet Park

Wolf Lake Memorial Park is a popular recreational area for fishing, boating, and hiking. Gibson Woods Nature Preserve is home to a variety of wildlife, and offers hiking trails, bird-watching, and seasonal events. Calumet Park is a great place to relax and enjoy the views of Lake Michigan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hammond

If you’re looking for a great way to explore Hammond’s natural beauty, there are a number of hiking and biking routes available, such as:

  • George Lake Trail
  • Erie Lackawanna Trail
  • Whiting Lakefront Park Trail

The George Lake Trail is a 2-mile loop around the lake, while the Erie Lackawanna Trail is a 11-mile trail that connects various towns and parks in the area. The Whiting Lakefront Park Trail offers scenic views of the lake and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Hammond: Activities and Tours

Hammond offers a range of activities and tours to keep you entertained, such as:

  • Horseshoe Casino
  • Indiana Dunes National Park
  • Harrah’s Joliet Casino

Horseshoe Casino is a popular destination for gambling, dining, and entertainment. Indiana Dunes National Park is a short drive away and offers opportunities for hiking, swimming, and bird-watching. Harrah’s Joliet Casino is located just across the state border in Illinois, and offers a variety of gaming options and live entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hammond

If you have a week to explore Hammond and the surrounding areas, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Hammond Historical Society and Museum
2 Hike the George Lake Trail at Wolf Lake Memorial Park
3 Take a tour of the John Dillinger Museum
4 Visit the Indiana Dunes National Park for a day of outdoor recreation
5 Try your luck at Horseshoe Casino
6 Explore the Calumet Park and the Lake Michigan shoreline
7 Take a day trip to Chicago to see the sights

Food and Drink Scene of Hammond

Must-Try Dishes

Hammond is home to a diverse range of restaurants that offer a variety of cuisines, including:

  • Pulled Pork Sandwich at Schoop’s Hamburgers
  • Italian Beef Sandwich at Outriggers
  • Pizza at Luigi’s Pizza

Schoop’s Hamburgers is a local institution that’s been serving up burgers and fries since 1948. Outriggers is famous for its Chicago-style Italian beef sandwiches, while Luigi’s Pizza is know for its thin-crust pizza.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking for a drink, check out these popular spots:

  • Three Floyds Brewing Company
  • Crown Brewing

Three Floyds Brewing Company is a popular craft brewery that’s known for its bold and flavorful beers. Crown Brewing offers a variety of beer styles, as well as a food menu.

Where to Stay in Hammond: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hammond offers a variety of accommodation options, including:

  • Best Western Northwest Indiana Inn
  • Comfort Inn & Suites
  • Super 8 Motel

Best Western Northwest Indiana Inn is a budget-friendly hotel that offers a complimentary breakfast and on-site fitness center. Comfort Inn & Suites offers a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center. Super 8 Motel offers simple rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Hammond: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or want to take home some local products, check out these spots:

  • Cabela’s
  • Lighthouse Place Premium Outlets
  • Horseshoe Casino Gift Shop

Cabela’s is a sporting goods store that specializes in hunting, fishing, and outdoor recreation gear. Lighthouse Place Premium Outlets is a shopping mall that features a variety of stores, including popular brands. Horseshoe Casino Gift Shop offers a selection of gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hammond

Hammond is a diverse city with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, which may vary depending on the community. In general, residents of Hammond are friendly and welcoming, and enjoy sharing their culture with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Hammond

The best time to visit Hammond is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, visitors may want to avoid the peak tourist season, which can be crowded and expensive. Spring and fall are also good times to visit, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hammond

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreation opportunities

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural areas
  • Be overly loud or disruptive in public places
  • Take photographs of people without their consent

Safety and Health

Safety in Hammond

Hammond is generally a safe city to visit, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Locking car doors and securing valuables
  • Keeping an eye on belongings in public areas
  • Sticking to well-lit and populated areas at night

Health in Hammond

Visitors to Hammond should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you.

Getting to Hammond: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hammond

The closest airport to Hammond is O’Hare International Airport, which is located in Chicago. Other nearby airports include:

  • Midway International Airport
  • Gary/Chicago International Airport

Bus Stations Near Hammond

Greyhound and Megabus both offer bus service to Hammond. The closest Greyhound bus station is in Gary, Indiana.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Hammond by car or train. The city is located near major highways, including I-80/94 and I-65, and is served by a nearby Amtrak station in Dyer, Indiana.

How to Get Around in Hammond

Hammond is a walkable city, but visitors may want to use a car or public transportation to reach attractions located outside the downtown area. Uber and Lyft are also available in Hammond.

Where to Go Next from Hammond: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region, check out these nearby destinations:

  • Chicago, Illinois
  • Indiana Dunes National Park
  • Michigan City, Indiana

Chicago is just a short drive or train ride away from Hammond and offers a variety of attractions, including museums, shopping, and dining. Indiana Dunes National Park offers outdoor recreation opportunities, while Michigan City is home to a variety of shops and restaurants.

Conclusion

Hammond offers something for everyone, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures and gaming. Visitors can explore the city’s history, enjoy the natural beauty of the region, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine and culture. Whether you’re traveling solo or with family and friends, Hammond is a great destination to add to your travel itinerary.
