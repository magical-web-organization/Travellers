Kokomo, Indiana, is a charming city located in the heart of the Midwest. With a booming downtown, great natural scenery, and a rich history, there is no shortage of things to do and see in Kokomo. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, this travel guide will help you discover the hidden gems and must-visit attractions of Kokomo.

Introduction to Kokomo

Kokomo is a small city situated in Central Indiana, in the United States of America. It has a population of around 57,000 people. Kokomo is known as the City of Firsts, as it was home to the first automobile to be manufactured in America and has a long history of innovation. Today, it boasts a thriving downtown area with restaurants, shops, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Kokomo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kokomo has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places worth visiting. The Kokomo Automotive Museum showcases the city’s proud history in automotive manufacturing and is a must-visit for car enthusiasts. The Seiberling Mansion is an impressive historic home that now serves as a museum, offering tours and exhibitions showcasing the history of Kokomo.

Another landmark worth visiting is the Kokomo Opalescent Glass factory, which has been producing beautiful stained glass for over 125 years. Visitors can tour the factory and purchase unique glass creations from the gift shop.

Natural Attractions of Kokomo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kokomo is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The Kokomo Reservoir Park offers stunning views of the lake and has several walking trails and picnic areas. The Wildcat Creek Trail is a 12-mile hike and bike trail that winds through the city’s natural areas, offering great views of the creek and surrounding landscape.

The Highland Park offers more than just playgrounds and open spaces. Guests can also enjoy the playground, fishing, picnic areas, and sports fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kokomo

For avid hikers and bikers, Kokomo has several great options. The Industrial Heritage Trail is a 7.3-mile scenic trail that winds through the city and offers views of the historic factory buildings and downtown area. The Nickle Plate Trail is a 36-mile trail that passes through Kokomo and offers a great opportunity for a long bike ride or hike.

Best Things to Do in Kokomo: Activities and Tours

Kokomo offers a wide range of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. The Kokomo Escape Room is a great option for a fun and unique group activity. Tourists can go bowling, to cinema, skating in the Spin Zone Family Fun Center. DeKalb-Perry Railroad provides the Midnight Limited ride that will bring guests back in time and give an experience in the 30’s.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kokomo

Day 1: Visit the Seiberling Mansion and the Kokomo Automotive Museum. Day 2: Hike the Wildcat Creek Trail and visit the Kokomo Opalescent Glass factory. Day 3: Visit the Kokomo Reservoir Park and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 4: Take a bike ride on the Nickle Plate Trail and explore the downtown area. Day 5: Try out the Kokomo Escape Room and visit the Highland Park for more outdoors activities. Day 6: Go bowling, to cinema, and skating at the Spin Zone Family Fun Center. Day 7: Go back in time with the DeKalb-Perry Railroad and enjoy a romantic night with a dinner at sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Kokomo

Kokomo has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of options to satisfy every taste bud.

Must-Try Dishes

The Pork Tenderloin sandwich is a classic dish that originated in Indiana and is a must-try when visiting Kokomo. Another local favorite is the Hoosier (or sugar) Cream Pie, a sweet and creamy pie that is a perfect dessert after a meal.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Kokomo is known for its craft beers. The Half Moon Brewery is a great place to sample some local brews. Wine lovers can visit the Near Eastside Vineyard and Winery for a taste of locally produced wine.

Where to Stay in Kokomo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kokomo has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. For those on a budget, the Super 8 by Wyndham Kokomo is a great option, while the Courtyard by Marriott Kokomo offers more luxurious amenities. The Kokomo Inn is another great option for those looking for a cozy and quaint place to stay.

What to Buy in Kokomo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring back home, Kokomo has several options. The Kokomo Opalescent Glass factory gift shop sells beautifully crafted glassware, while the White House Gifts offers a wide range of Kokomo-themed souvenirs and locally made products.

Local Culture and Customs of Kokomo

Kokomo is a friendly and welcoming community that values hospitality and kindness. It is important to respect the local culture and customs and to be polite and courteous to everyone you meet.

Best Time to Visit Kokomo

The best time to visit Kokomo is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and pleasant, and many outdoor activities are available. Fall and spring are also good times to visit, with mild temperatures and colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kokomo

Do:

  • Explore the downtown area and try out some local restaurants and breweries.
  • Visit the Kokomo Opalescent Glass factory and other cultural landmarks.
  • Enjoy the beautiful natural scenery and go hiking or biking.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in plain sight in your car.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Be rude to locals or other tourists.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kokomo

Kokomo is a safe city, but visitors should take necessary precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and not walking alone at night in deserted areas. The city boasts a well-trained safety department that serves both residents and tourists.

Health in Kokomo

Kokomo has several good hospitals and medical centers, including St. Vincent Kokomo Hospital and Community Howard Regional Health. Visitors to Kokomo should always be mindful of their health and take necessary precautions, such as wearing sunscreen during outdoor activities in the summer months.

Getting to Kokomo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kokomo

The nearest airport to Kokomo is Indianapolis International Airport, located around 70 miles to the south.

Bus Stations Near Kokomo

Kokomo has a Greyhound Bus station, located in the downtown area.

Other Options

Visitors can also use Amtrak services to get to Kokomo, with a stop located in Beech Grove, around 50 miles south of Kokomo.

How to Get Around in Kokomo

Kokomo is a walkable city with plenty of sidewalks and pedestrian-friendly areas. Visitors can also use city buses to get around, with several routes available throughout the city. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer not to drive themselves.

Where to Go Next from Kokomo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kokomo is surrounded by several great destinations worth visiting. Indianapolis, the state capital, is located around 60 miles south of Kokomo and offers a plethora of cultural attractions, including the Indianapolis Zoo and the Indiana State Museum. The Indiana Dunes National Park, located on the shores of Lake Michigan, is around 120 miles north of Kokomo and offers stunning views and hiking opportunities.

Conclusion

Kokomo is a charming city with plenty to offer visitors. From cultural landmarks and museums to scenic hiking trails and a thriving food and drink scene, there is something for everyone in Kokomo. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are all set to plan your perfect trip to Kokomo, Indiana.
