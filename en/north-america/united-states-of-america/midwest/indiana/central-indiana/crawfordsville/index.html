Introduction to Crawfordsville

Located in central Indiana’s Montgomery County, Crawfordsville is a small city with a charming downtown and a rich history. Founded in 1823, the city blends small town hospitality with big city amenities and makes for a perfect getaway destination. Crawfordsville has a lot to offer visitors, from its diverse food and drink scene, scenic outdoors, historic attractions to engaging activities.

List of Places to Visit in Crawfordsville

Here’s a list of places to explore in Crawfordsville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Crawfordsville is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the city’s past. Some of the prominent ones include:

  • General Lew Wallace Study & Museum: This museum showcases the life and works of the famous author, diplomat, and Civil War general, General Lew Wallace. Visitors can view exhibits on his literary works, war writings, and see the personal artifacts and galleries.
  • Linden Depot Museum: A restored train depot that showcases Crawfordsville’s rich railroad history. This museum features a collection of vintage railroad artifacts, including a working telegraph.
  • Rotary Jail Museum: Crawfordsville’s rotary jail is an architectural marvel that showcases eccentric designs of the late 19th century. This museum features the museum displays that showcase the unique design of the jail, and visitors witness the original rotary cage in operation.

Natural Attractions of Crawfordsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature enthusiasts will enjoy exploring the natural beauty of Crawfordsville’s outdoors. Here are some options you should not miss:

  • Shades State Park: A scenic park that offers captivating views, ranging from waterfalls to challenging hiking trails, unique geological compositions, and camping options for visitors.
  • Sugar Creek: A serene waterway that offers canoeing and kayaking to visitors and unparalleled wildlife sightings from the water.
  • Lieber State Recreation Area: A vast recreation area with a beach, boating, fishing, trails, and campsites, in the vicinity of Cagles Mill Lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crawfordsville

Crawfordsville has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the Midwest. Here are some options you can check out:

  • Sugar Creek Trail: A scenic trail that spans Crawfordsville and is ideal for visitors looking for a leisurely cycling or hiking experience. Visitors get to enjoy a breathtaking view of the natural setting, passing through forests, waterfalls, and picturesque neighborhoods.
  • Cades Hollow Trail: A 1.5-mile loop trail located in Shades State Park, which is ideal for visitors looking for a challenging hike with superb views as they trek along cliffs and rugged terrain.
  • Lieber State Recreation Area: The largest park in Montgomery County, it features a 17.5-mile mountain biking trail named the Red Trail, popular with mountain bikers for its rough-hewn terrain providing stunning views of the lake. Visitors may also choose to combine hiking and biking trails around Mill Creek or Cagle’s Mill Lake to turn a day trip into an overnight adventure.

Best Things to Do in Crawfordsville: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to historic tours, Crawfordsville offers endless activities to visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Crawfordsville:

  • Taste & Tour: A guided tour with an immersion in Crawfordsville’s rich history. Visitors will get to experience unique culinary offerings and hear stories and insights about the city’s past. These tours cater to the varied interests of different kinds of travelers.
  • Art Walk: A refreshing outdoor experience that provides a glimpse of the vibrant art scene of the city. Visitors can explore the various art exhibitions in the downtown area, enjoy live music, and meet local artists.
  • Kayak Tours: Paddle down Sugar Creek with expert guides who offer a unique perspective on the wildlife and history of the Crawfordsville outdoors.

Food and Drink Scene of Crawfordsville

Crawfordsville boasts of a diverse food and drink scene, with an array of delicious dining options that cater to different palates. Here are some must-try dishes to sample while in the city:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taco Salad: A crunchy and delicious bowl containing beans, beef, cheese, lettuce, and salsa.
  • Sugar Cream Pie: A classic dessert that originated in Indiana - a rich cream pie made from milk, flour, vanilla, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Brick Street Brews: A local craft brewery located in the historic downtown area that offers a wide range of flavorful and well-crafted beers.
  • Bivouac Ciderworks: A brewery that specializes in producing handcrafted ciders made from locally sourced fruits.

Where to Stay in Crawfordsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crawfordsville has a variety of lodging options, whether you’re looking for luxurious accommodation or an affordable stay. Some of the best options include:

  • Creekside Lodge: An excellent option for families and budget travelers, featuring an indoor pool and complimentary breakfast.
  • 1893 House Bed & Breakfast: A charming bed & breakfast located within walking distance to downtown Crawfordsville, featuring cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Hampton Inn: A hotel that offers luxurious amenities such as an indoor pool, fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Crawfordsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Crawfordsville has several stores and shops that offer unique souvenirs and locally made products to bring back home. Here are some items you should consider:

  • Fudge: Fudge shops such as the Peppermint Pig located in the downtown area, offers unique and delicious fudges made with real cream and butter that are a must-try.
  • Antiques: Crawfordsville boasts of numerous antique shops that offer unique finds and treasures.
  • Local Wine: Along with food, Crawfordsville has local vineyards like Sugar Creek Winery that offer authentic local wine for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Crawfordsville

Crawfordsville has a strong sense of community, and visitors are expected to respect the local customs. Here is some pertinent information on the local culture:

  • Tipping: Tipping is common in Crawfordsville, with the standard tip being around 15-20% in restaurants and bars.
  • Greeting: People in Crawfordsville usually greet each other with a smile and a friendly hello.
  • Dress Code: Crawfordsville is considered a casual city, with most restaurants accepting casual and comfortable attire.

Best Time to Visit Crawfordsville

The best time to visit Crawfordsville is in the fall season (September - November), as the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities. Additionally, during fall, the city celebrates several festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crawfordsville

Do:

  • Engage with locals and explore the downtown area: The locals are friendly and always willing to offer help and recommendations.
  • Respect nature: Crawfordsville has a rich natural environment, and visitors should always take care to not litter or harm the natural surroundings.
  • Try local cuisine: Crawfordsville has a diverse culinary scene, and visitors should experience and enjoy the different flavors on offer.

Don’t:

  • Ignore social etiquettes: Crawfordsville may be a small city, but visitors should keep in mind basic social etiquettes and courtesy.
  • Forget sunscreen and bug spray: Crawfordsville’s outdoor activities could expose visitors to the sunshine and mosquitoes. Visitors must plan accordingly to protect themselves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Crawfordsville

Crawfordsville is a safe and secure city for leisure and tourism with its low crime rates, but visitors must always follow standard precautions such as:

  • Stay alert: Visitors should practice situational awareness in crowded places and not leave their belongings unattended.
  • Obey traffic rules: Visitors should be careful on the road, respect traffic signals and avoid reckless driving.

Health in Crawfordsville

Crawfordsville has excellent medical facilities, including a hospital and a health center. Visitors must carry their medications and adequate medical coverage.

How to Get Around in Crawfordsville

Getting around Crawfordsville is easy and convenient, with several options available. Visitors can choose from the following:

  • Car rentals or ride-share services: The most convenient option for those who prefer flexibility and convenience.
  • Public transportation: Crawfordsville’s public transportation service is limited to the R2R bus service, which is available on select hours, and a few taxi services.
  • Walking and biking: Crawfordsville is a pedestrian and bike-friendly town, with several routes and trails.

Where to Go Next from Crawfordsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montgomery County and the areas around Crawfordsville have several exciting destinations and attractions worth exploring. Here are some of our top choices:
