Introduction to Knightstown

Located in Central Indiana, Knightstown is a charming small town with a population of approximately 2,200. The town is rich in history dating back to the mid-19th century with many well-preserved buildings, a picturesque town square, and friendly residents. Knightstown is an ideal destination for history aficionados, outdoor enthusiasts, and travelers looking for a quiet and laid-back experience off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Knightstown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Hoosier Gym: Built in 1922, the Hoosier Gym is famous for being the filming location of the basketball scenes in the movie “Hoosiers.” Visitors can take a guided tour and even play a game of basketball on the same court where Gene Hackman coached his team in the movie.
  • Knightstown Academy: Established in 1848, Knightstown Academy is a former private school that is now a museum and historical site. The academy offers a glimpse into the town’s educational history with exhibits on the early school years in the Midwest.
  • The Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation Museum: Housed in the former Knightstown High School building, the Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation Museum preserves and celebrates the educational and athletic history of the town.

Natural Attractions of Knightstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wilbur Wright Fish and Wildlife Area: With over 1,000 acres of sprawling forests and lakes, the Wilbur Wright Fish and Wildlife Area is a dream destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy fishing, hiking, hunting, and bird watching.
  • Westwood Park: Westwood Park is a 63-acre public park that offers a variety of recreational activities, including a playground, a disc golf course, a skate park, and a walking trail. The park is perfect for families with kids and for those who want to spend a leisurely day in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Knightstown

Knightstown is surrounded by some of the most beautiful and natural landscapes in Indiana. Here are some hiking/biking routes to explore:

  • Carthage Trail: A 4-mile trail that winds through farmland and forests connecting Knightstown to the town of Carthage.
  • Wilbur Wright Trail: A 10-mile trail that starts at the Wilbur Wright Fish and Wildlife Area and continues south to the town of Morristown.

Best Things to Do in Knightstown: Activities and Tours

  • Knightstown Carriage Company: Take a horse-driven carriage tour around Knightstown’s historic district and learn about the town’s rich history and architecture.
  • Knightstown Farmers Market: Buy fresh produce, artisanal goods, and crafts from local vendors every Saturday morning at the Knightstown Farmers Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Knightstown

Must-Try Dishes

  • John’s Famous Stew: A hearty stew made with beef, potatoes, carrots, corn, and green beans.
  • Pork tenderloin sandwich: A breaded and fried pork tenderloin sandwich served with pickles and mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Country Charm Winery and Vineyard: A family-owned winery that produces a variety of wines from locally grown grapes.
  • Knightstown Coffee Company: A cozy coffee shop that serves locally roasted coffee, tea, and pastries.

Where to Stay in Knightstown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Carriage House: A cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of Knightstown’s historic district.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham: A budget-friendly hotel located on the outskirts of Knightstown.

What to Buy in Knightstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hoosier Gym Merchandise: Pick up a t-shirt, hat, or basketball from the Hoosier Gym gift shop.
  • Local Honey: Buy a jar of locally produced honey at the Knightstown Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Knightstown

Knightstown residents are known for their friendly and welcoming demeanor. It is customary to wave and say hello to strangers on the street.

Best Time to Visit Knightstown

The best time to visit Knightstown is during the fall when the leaves change color, and the weather is mild.

Safety and Health

Safety in Knightstown

Knightstown is a safe and quiet town, but it is always wise to take precautions such as locking your car and home and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Knightstown

Knightstown has a hospital and several medical clinics for routine and emergency care.

Getting to Knightstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bus Stations Near Knightstown

Other Options

How to Get Around in Knightstown

The best way to get around Knightstown is by car. It is also possible to travel by bicycle, but the town’s roads can be busy, so it is important to be cautious.

Where to Go Next from Knightstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Connersville: A charming town known for its beautiful Victorian architecture and antique shops is a 30-minute drive from Knightstown.
