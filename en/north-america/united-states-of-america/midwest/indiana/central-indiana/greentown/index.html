Welcome to Greentown! A charming town in Central Indiana, nestled in the heart of the Midwest, Greentown is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored. From scenic outdoor adventures to historical museums and cultural events, there is something for everyone in this town. Our comprehensive tour guide will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Greentown

Greentown is a small town in the Howard County of Indiana and is an hour’s drive from downtown Indianapolis. With a population of around 2,500, it is a tight-knit community that is proud of its heritage, customs, and culture. It was incorporated in 1873 and saw rapid industrial development in the early 20th century with the discovery of natural gas. Today, Greentown has managed to retain its small-town charm while embracing new development and growth.

List of Places to Visit in Greentown

There are many exciting places to visit and explore in Greentown. Here is a list of some of the best ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Greentown Glass Museum – A tribute to the town’s rich history in glassmaking and home to an extensive collection of glassware.
  • Museum of the Soldier – A museum that honors American military personnel and their contributions to the country.
  • The Howard County Historical Society Museum – A museum dedicated to preserving, exhibiting, and interpreting the history of Howard County.

Natural Attractions of Greentown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Greentown Trails and Nature Park – Offers an extensive network of trails for hiking and biking, fishing ponds, and a playground.
  • The Howard County Vietnam Veterans Memorial – A beautiful park that pays tribute to the sacrifices made by Vietnamese veterans and their families.
  • Wildcat Creek Reservoir – A stunning artificial lake with a beautiful walking trail, picnic spots and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greentown

Greentown Trails and Nature Park has a network of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for exploration by both seasoned and amateur hikers. The park features the Marsh Track trail, which is 1.1 miles long, and the paved Heritage Trail, which is 0.6 miles long.

Best Things to Do in Greentown: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting things to do in Greentown, such as:

  • Take a tour of the Greentown Glassworks to learn about the history and art of glassmaking.
  • Visit the farmer’s market that takes place on Saturday mornings.
  • Attend one of the many cultural events and festivals, such as the Howard County Fair, the Annual Glass Festival, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greentown

Greentown has something to offer for everyone, whether you’re looking for history, culture, or outdoor adventures. Here’s a sample weekly itinerary that you can follow:

Food and Drink Scene of Greentown

Greentown has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork tenderloin sandwich – A delicious sandwich featuring breaded and fried pork tenderloin, served with pickles, lettuce, tomato, and mayo.
  • Hoosier-style fried chicken – A crispy and juicy fried chicken dish that is a staple in Indiana.
  • Sugar cream pie – A sweet and creamy dessert made with sugar, cream, and pie crust.

Local Beverages

  • Indiana craft beer – Sample some of the best local brews from breweries like Thr3e Wise Men, Taxman, and Sun King.
  • Colts Neck Coffee – Experience the taste of local coffee from one of the oldest coffee shops in Greentown.

What to Buy in Greentown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and gifts to take home, Greentown has plenty of options. Here are some of the best local products to bring home:

Local Culture and Customs of Greentown

Greentown has a rich heritage that is reflected in its culture and customs. Locals take great pride in their town’s history and traditions. Greentown is known for its friendly and welcoming community and its strong connection to its agricultural roots.

Best Time to Visit Greentown

The best time to visit Greentown is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. The town is vibrant with cultural events, festivals, and outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing. Fall is also an excellent time to visit Greentown when the leaves start changing colors, and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greentown

Here are some tips to help you navigate the local customs and traditions in Greentown:

Do:

  • Show respect for the town’s history and traditions.
  • Be friendly and courteous to locals.
  • Try the local food, especially the pork tenderloin sandwich.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the natural or cultural attractions in Greentown.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Touch any exhibits or displays at museums and cultural attractions.

Safety and Health

Greentown is a safe and healthy town with access to quality medical services. Here are some key points to keep in mind:

Safety in Greentown

  • Greentown is generally a safe place, but it’s always wise to take common-sense precautions like locking your doors and not walking alone at night.
  • Be aware of wildlife when hiking or biking in Greentown Trails and Nature Park.

Health in Greentown

  • There are several quality healthcare facilities in Greentown, including Community Howard Regional Health and Saint Vincent Kokomo Hospital.
  • Make sure to stay hydrated during outdoor activities, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Greentown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Greentown is easily accessible by car and has several options for transportation. Here are some key points to keep in mind:

Airports Near Greentown

  • Indianapolis International Airport (IND) is about an hour’s drive from Greentown.
  • Fort Wayne International Airport (FWA) is about two hours’ drive from Greentown.

Bus Stations Near Greentown

  • Greyhound has a station in Kokomo, which is around 20 minutes’ drive from Greentown.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is the most convenient option for getting around Greentown.

How to Get Around in Greentown

Driving is the most common way to get around in Greentown. However, the town’s compact size makes it a great place to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Greentown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Greentown is near many attractions in central Indiana. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Kokomo – A nearby city that is home to several museums and parks, including the Kokomo Beach Family Aquatic Center.
  • Indianapolis – The state capital and home to many attractions, such as the Indianapolis 500, the Indianapolis Zoo, and the Children’s Museum of Indianapolis.
  • Turkey Run State Park – A beautiful state park with scenic trails, waterfalls, and limestone ravines.

Conclusion

Greentown is a hidden gem that is rich in history and culture, and offers plenty of outdoor activities and scenic beauty. Whether you’re looking for a romantic geataway or a family vacation, there’s something for everyone in Greentown. So come and explore the heart of the Midwest and create memories that will last a lifetime!
