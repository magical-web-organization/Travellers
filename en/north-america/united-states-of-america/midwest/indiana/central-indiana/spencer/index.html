A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Spencer, Indiana

Spencer, Indiana is a charming town located in the heart of Central Indiana, surrounded by luscious green hills and beautiful landscapes. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Spencer has something for everyone to enjoy. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your next adventure to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Spencer

Spencer offers a wide variety of places to visit, from culturally important attractions to natural outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of must-see destinations in Spencer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Owen County Courthouse: Built in 1859, the courthouse is a historic landmark that offers a beautiful blend of Italianate and Second Empire architecture. Take a guided tour to learn more about the history of the courthouse and the town of Spencer.
  • Owen County Heritage and Culture Center: This museum preserves the history of Owen county and its surrounding communities. Learn about the area’s geology, wildlife, and notable residents through interactive exhibits.
  • McCormick’s Creek State Park: Established in 1916, McCormick’s Creek State Park is Indiana’s first state park. Explore over 10 miles of hiking trails, take a dip in the park’s swimming pool, and visit the state park’s nature center to learn about local flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Spencer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Canyon Inn: Located within McCormick’s Creek State Park, the Canyon Inn offers breathtaking views of the outdoors. Take a scenic drive or hike to see the beautiful rock formations and wildlife of the park.
  • Spencer Pride Community Center: A community center dedicated to promoting inclusivity and diversity in Spencer and throughout Owen County. Attend one of their educational events or check out their extensive library.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spencer

Spencer is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of hiking and biking routes for all skill levels. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • McCormick’s Creek State Park Trails: With over 10 miles of hiking trails, McCormick’s Creek State Park offers a variety of terrains and difficulty levels. From easy, paved trails to rugged backcountry paths, there’s something for every hiker.
  • Owen-Putnam State Forest Trails: Featuring over 5,000 acres of beautiful forest, Owen-Putnam State Forest is home to a variety of hiking trails. Be sure to check out the trailhead at Wolf Cave for a unique geological formation.
  • Spencer-Own-Yankee Road Trail: This 17-mile trail is perfect for bikers and hikers of all levels. Take in the scenic views of the nearby forests and have a picnic lunch at the scenic stop.

Best Things to Do in Spencer: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a hands-on experience or a guided tour, there are plenty of things to do in Spencer. Here are our top picks:

  • Owen County Art Guild: A non-profit organization dedicated to promoting and preserving the visual arts in Owen County. Check out one of their rotating art exhibitions or take a painting or ceramics class.
  • McCloud Nature Park: A beautiful park that offers a variety of programs and activities for all ages. Take a guided nature walk or try your hand at fishing in the stocked ponds.
  • Local Food and Drink Tours: Take a guided tour of the town’s best restaurants and breweries. Sample local brews and try some of the town’s famous southern comfort food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spencer

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Spencer, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Get settled in town, and take a walk through the Owen County Courthouse
Day 2 Visit the Owen County Heritage and Culture Center for an interactive history lesson

Food and Drink Scene of Spencer

Spencer boasts a rich culinary scene with an emphasis on southern comfort food with a modern twist. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken and Waffles: This southern staple is a must-try in Spencer. Try it at Jeff’s Pizza & Grill or the One-Eyed Jacks Bar & Grill.
  • Peach Bread Pudding: A fruity twist on a classic dish, this dessert is a seasonal favorite at Chambers Smorgasbord.

Local Beverages

  • Upland Brewing Company: Located in nearby Bloomington, Indiana, Upland Brewing Company offers a wide variety of craft beers that can be found on tap in many Spencer bars and restaurants.
  • Salt Creek Winery & Tasting Room: A local winery that offers a range of handcrafted wines and tasting options.

Where to Stay in Spencer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spencer offers a variety of accommodation options to fit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top choices:

  • Canyon Inn: Located within McCormick’s Creek State Park, the Canyon Inn offers a variety of rooms and cabins with scenic views of the park.
  • Spencer Inn Bed and Breakfast: A cozy bed and breakfast with charming Victorian-era decor, located just a short walk from the town square.
  • Spencer Hostel: A rustic hostel located in the heart of Spencer. This hostel offers affordable accommodations for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Spencer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Spencer is complete without bringing home some local souvenirs and goods. Here are some top local products to look for:

  • Owen County Cornhole Sets: These locally made cornhole sets are a fun way to remember your trip to Spencer.
  • McCormick’s Creek State Park Memorabilia: Take home a shirt or hat with the park’s logo as a memento.

Local Culture and Customs of Spencer

Spencer prides itself on its friendly small-town Southern charm. Its slow-paced lifestyle is refreshing to those used to the hustle and bustle of the big city. Southern hospitality and manners are greatly appreciated while visiting Spencer.

Best Time to Visit Spencer

Spencer has a humid continental climate with year-round appeal, but the best time to visit is in the spring and summer months. This is when the weather is most enjoyable, and you can enjoy outdoor activities like fishing, hiking, and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spencer

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the great outdoors, Spencer’s natural beauty is a unique feature.
  • Do be friendly! Southern hospitality and friendliness is appreciated.
  • Do take time to talk to locals to learn more about the town and its history

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind in the parks and natural areas.
  • Don’t start campfires in undesignated areas to protect Spencer’s natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spencer

Spencer is a small town with low crime rates. However, always practice safety precautions like locking your belongings and not leaving valuables out in the open.

Health in Spencer

Spencer has a hospital with an emergency room, as well as a variety of local clinics for minor ailments.

Getting to Spencer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spencer is located in central Indiana, making it easily accessible from many different directions. Here are the transportation options available to visitors:

Airports Near Spencer

  • Indianapolis International Airport: Located approximately 65 miles northeast of Spencer.
  • Monroe County Airport: Located approximately 30 miles south of Spencer.

Bus Stations Near Spencer

  • Bloomington Transit Center: Located approximately 20 miles south of Spencer. This bus station offers transportation options throughout the area.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Many rental car companies are available in nearby Bloomington.
  • Taxis and Ride Shares: Various taxi and ride-share companies are available in the area.

How to Get Around Spencer

Many of Spencer’s attractions are within walking distance of each other, and there are sidewalks throughout town. Car rentals, taxis, and ride-shares are also available. Biking is also a popular mode of transportation, with rental bikes available in nearby Bloomington.

Where to Go Next from Spencer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Indiana, here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Bloomington, Indiana: A lively college town with a thriving arts and culture scene, located approximately 20 miles south of Spencer.
  • French Lick, Indiana: Just a short drive from Spencer, this town features beautiful historical homes, a casino, and its own waterpark.
  • Nashville, Indiana: A quaint artist colony known for its shops and galleries, located approximately 30 miles south of Spencer.

Conclusion

Spencer, Indiana may be a small town, but it packs a punch when it comes to outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and delicious food and drink options. Whether you’re visiting for a day or a week, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Plan your trip today and experience the warmth and charm of Spencer for yourself!
