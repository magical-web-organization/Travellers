Introduction to Covington

Located in the heart of Indiana’s central region, Covington is a charming town with a rich history. This hidden gem offers the perfect blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, unique shops, and delicious cuisine. With great options for accommodation and transportation, Covington can be an ideal destination for a weekend trip or a weeklong vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Covington

When planning a trip to Covington, make sure to include these must-visit attractions on your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Covington Heritage Museum: Learn about the town’s history and see exhibits showcasing the area’s connection with the Underground Railroad.
  • Rochester Gear Works Museum: Explore the history of manufacturing in Covington through exhibits and interactive displays.
  • The Covington Circle: Discover the significance of the unusual roundabout design and the historical markers placed around it.
  • Mud Creek Civil War Battlefield: Take a self-guided tour of the battlefield and learn about the important role Covington played in the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Covington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Freeman: Rent a boat, go fishing, or take a swim at this breathtaking lake with stunning views.
  • Shawe Lake and Nature Reserve: Explore this picturesque nature reserve with trails for hiking and bird-watching.
  • Wabash Heritage Trail: Go biking or walking on this trail that winds through the scenic countryside of Covington.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Covington

If you’re looking to explore the natural beauty around Covington, try these popular biking and hiking routes.

  • Covington Circle Trail: This 3.5-mile trail passes through the town’s parks and downtown area.
  • Griffith Woods Nature Reserve: This 2-mile hiking trail has breathtaking views of the forest and creek.
  • Sugar Creek Trail: Go biking along this 9-mile trail over bridges and through the beautiful countryside.

Best Things to Do in Covington: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in Covington:

  • Shuttle Service and Winery Tours: Explore the best wineries in the area with a guided tour and tasting.
  • Indoor Sports Center: Enjoy indoor sports including basketball, volleyball, soccer, and more.
  • Fishing and Hunting: Covington offers great options for fishing and hunting enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Covington

Day 1: Explore the town’s history - visit Covington Heritage Museum and Rochester Gear Works Museum.

Day 2: Outdoor adventure - go boating or swimming at Lake Freeman.

Day 3: Biking/hiking - ride or hike through Sugar Creek Trail.

Day 4: Try local food - dine at one of the local restaurants that serve delicious handmade dishes.

Day 5: Tour local wineries - Visit one of the wineries for a wine tour and tasting.

Day 6: Visit historical site - Take a self-guided tour of Mud Creek Civil War Battlefield.

Day 7: Explore nearby attractions - Go on a day trip to Indiana’s capital, Indianapolis.

Food and Drink Scene of Covington

Covington has a diverse culinary scene with options ranging from traditional Midwest fare to international cuisine. Don’t miss these must-try dishes and local beverages during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sugar Cream Pie: A classic Midwestern dish made with cream, sugar, and butter, complete with a flaky crust.
  • Pork tenderloin sandwiches: A local favorite, this sandwich is made with breaded pork and served with various toppings, including mustard, pickles, and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Indiana craft beer: Covington is home to great local breweries such as Blackrock Brewery and People’s Brewing Company.
  • Wine from local wineries: Experience the flavors of Indiana’s countryside with a glass of wine from one of the wineries in the area.

Where to Stay in Covington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Covington offers ample lodging options for travelers of all budgets. Here are some of the top places to stay.

  • Best Western Plus: A comfortable and affordable hotel conveniently located near downtown Covington.
  • Prairie Guest House: A bed and breakfast known for its warm hospitality and beautiful surroundings.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham: A budget-friendly accommodation option with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Covington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique gifts and souvenirs to take back home? Covington has plenty of options to choose from.

  • Quilts and handmade crafts: The Covington Quilt Barn is a great place to find beautiful handmade crafts and quilts.
  • Local wine and beer: Bring back a bottle of local wine or beer from one of the wineries or breweries in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Covington

Covington has a rich cultural history with influences from different ethnic groups. The town is known for its traditions, including its annual fall festival, held every October.

Best Time to Visit Covington

The best time to visit Covington is in the late spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the fall foliage is breathtaking. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Covington

Do:

  • Respect the town’s historical sites and landmarks.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural attractions.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Covington

Covington is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always advisable to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when walking alone at night.

Health in Covington

Covington has a good healthcare system with several hospitals and clinics in the area. Make sure to bring any necessary medication with you on your trip.

Getting to Covington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Covington is easily accessible by various transportation options.

Airports Near Covington

The nearest major airport is Indianapolis International Airport, located about 70 miles from Covington.

Bus Stations Near Covington

Greyhound Bus Line has a station in West Lafayette, which is located about 25 miles from Covington.

Other Options

Renting a car or using a ride-sharing service is also an option for getting around town.

How to Get Around in Covington

It’s easy to get around Covington on foot, by bike, or by car. Local taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Covington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Covington is centrally located and is within easy reach of many great destinations. Here are some of the nearby excursions worth considering:

  • Lafayette: A larger city with plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, located just 25 miles north of Covington.
  • Turkey Run State Park: A stunning natural area with hiking trails, scenic gorges, and waterfalls, located about 30 miles east of Covington.
  • Indianapolis: The thriving capital city of Indiana with an abundance of cultural activities, shopping, dining, and entertainment options, located about 70 miles southeast of Covington.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural history, stunning natural scenery, delicious cuisine, and ample lodging options, Covington has everything you need for an unforgettable vacation. Plan your trip today and experience the best that this charming town has to offer.
