Introduction to Odon

Nestled in the heart of Central Indiana, Odon is a small town with a lot to offer. Known for its rich history, charming Main Street, and friendly locals, Odon will leave an impression on every traveler who passes through. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, you’ll find plenty to see and do in this hidden gem of a town.

List of Places to Visit in Odon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Daviess County Historical Museum: Explore the history of Odon and Daviess County through exhibits showcasing artifacts and memorabilia from local families and businesses.

  • Odon Memorial Park: This park is home to several monuments and memorials dedicated to Odon’s veterans and notable citizens, including a tribute to coal miners who worked in the town’s coal mines.

Natural Attractions of Odon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glendale Fish and Wildlife Area: Get back to nature with fishing, camping, hiking, and hunting opportunities, all amidst stunning views of forests, marshes, and prairies.

  • West Boggs Creek Reservoir: This 1,200-acre lake is perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming, and its scenic trails provide ample opportunities for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Odon

The West Boggs Trail System offers over 13 miles of well-marked hiking and biking trails woven throughout the town’s natural beauty. From easy to challenging trails, adventure seekers of all levels can enjoy exploring Odon’s scenic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Odon: Activities and Tours

  • Daviss County Amish Country: Experience the unique Amish culture with a visit to Daviess County Indiana’s Amish Country, where you can take a tour of Amish homes and farms, shop for handcrafted goods, and savor authentic homemade Amish cooking.

  • Wineries: Odon is home to several wineries, such as Windy Knoll Winery, where you can enjoy tastings, live music, and stunning vineyard views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Odon

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Daviess County Historical Museum and learn about Odon’s history. Enjoy dinner at Windy Knoll Winery.
Day 2 Explore the West Boggs Trail System followed by lunch at Odon Locker.
Day 3 Get back to nature with a day at Glendale Fish and Wildlife Area, followed by dinner at Monte’s Pizza
Day 4 Take a tour of Amish homes and farms in Daviess County’s Amish Country and enjoy lunch at Country Porch Winery.
Day 5 Spend the day on the water at West Boggs Creek Reservoir followed by dinner at Up in Smoke BBQ.
Day 6 Enjoy a day trip to nearby attractions such as Indiana Caverns and French Lick Resort.
Day 7 Relax and unwind before heading home. Visit Odon Memorial Park and enjoy lunch at Buffalo Wings & Rings.

Food and Drink Scene of Odon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amish Wedding Chicken: A classic dish made with whole chicken cooked in a Dutch oven over an open fire and seasoned with Amish spices.

  • Persimmon Pudding: A sweet dessert made with persimmon pulp, flour, sugar, eggs, and spices, that is best served warm with ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Windy Knoll Winery: Known for producing award-winning wines made with locally grown grapes, this winery is a must-visit for any wine lover.

  • Odon Locker: A local favorite, this butcher shop offers a wide selection of meats and homemade sausage, perfect for grilling and BBQs.

Where to Stay in Odon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express: Located just minutes from Odon’s attractions, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, a gym, and complimentary breakfast.

  • Airbnb: Stay in a cozy guesthouse or cottage, and immerse yourself in Odon’s local culture and vibe.

What to Buy in Odon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artisan Crafts: Stop by the Country Shops of Graber Supply to browse handcrafted Amish goods such as pottery, quilts, and woodworking pieces.

  • Maple Syrup: Purchase locally-produced maple syrup, a staple of Indiana agriculture, as a delicious and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Odon

Odon is a melting pot of cultures that have come together to form a unique way of life. Visitors will be charmed by the rich Amish culture and the warm hospitality of the town’s residents.

Best Time to Visit Odon

Spring and fall are ideal times to visit Odon, when temperatures are mild and nature is at its most vibrant. However, each season in Odon has its unique charm, making it a great year-round destination.

Do’s and Don’ts of Odon

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting Amish communities.

  • Take advantage of Odon’s natural beauty by enjoying outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of Amish individuals or their property without their permission.

  • Litter or damage Odon’s natural beauty and wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Odon

Odon is a safe and friendly town. However, visitors are advised to take normal precautions when exploring natural surroundings and participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Odon

There is limited medical care available in Odon, with emergency care accessed in nearby towns such as Washington. Visitors should take normal precautions and be prepared with any necessary medications or medical supplies.

Getting to Odon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Odon

  • Indianapolis International Airport: 2-hour drive from Odon.

  • Louisville International Airport: 2-hour drive from Odon.

Bus Stations Near Odon

  • Greyhound: The closest station is located in Washington, approximately 25 minutes from Odon.

Other Options

The best way to explore Odon and its surroundings is by car. Visitors can opt to rent a car at nearby airports or use ride-share services.

How to Get Around in Odon

Most of Odon’s main attractions are located within walking distance of one another. Alternatively, visitors can rent bicycles or use ride-share services for added convenience.

Where to Go Next from Odon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Indiana Caverns: Explore the underground world of Indiana’s caves and caverns, located just 30 minutes from Odon.

  • French Lick Resort: Enjoy luxurious accommodations, fine dining, and spa treatments at this historic resort, located just 45 minutes from Odon.

Conclusion

Odon may be a small town, but it has a lot to offer visitors looking for a unique, authentic experience. From its historic sites and natural beauty to its friendly locals and rich cultural traditions, Odon is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure.
