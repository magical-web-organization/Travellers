Introduction to Pago Pago

Pago Pago is a small but vibrant town located on the island of Tutuila, American Samoa. It’s situated in the South Pacific Ocean and is known for its lush greenery, serene bays, and dramatic landscapes. The town’s name means “Pā’ā in Samoan, which refers to “the throwing of rocks.” Pago Pago serves as a gateway to an array of natural wonders and rich cultural experiences that draw visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Pago Pago

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Jean P. Haydon Museum

Located at the American Samoa Community College, this museum is a treasure trove of Samoan history and culture. It houses a vast collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that bring to life the island’s fascinating past.

2. Fagatele Bay National Marine Sanctuary

This protected bay is a haven for marine life and offers visitors a chance to explore its pristine waters while protecting the delicate ecosystem. Visitors can go snorkeling, diving, or take a glass-bottom boat tour to see the colorful corals and fascinating marine life.

Natural Attractions of Pago Pago: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Mount Alava

A 9-mile hiking trail to the summit of Mount Alava offers stunning panoramic views of Pago Pago Harbor and the surrounding lush forest. On the way, you’ll pass by ancient fortified villages, burial sites, and discover the flora and fauna of the island ecosystem.

2. Nu’uuli Falls

Nu’uuli Falls is a hidden gem that offers a picturesque waterfall amid a pristine tropical forest. Visitors can hike to the falls, swim, or picnic on the banks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pago Pago

The scenic hiking trails of Pago Pago offers visitors the chance to get up close and personal with the island’s natural beauty. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Masefau Falls Trail
  • Pola Island Trail
  • Blunts Point Trail

Best Things to Do in Pago Pago: Activities and Tours

1. Scuba Diving

Pago Pago is surrounded by crystal clear waters that offer scuba divers an unforgettable experience. The rich marine life, colorful corals, and underwater caves are a diver’s paradise.

2. Fishing

Fishing is a way of life on the island of Tutuila, and visitors can charter a boat to go fishing on the Pacific Ocean. It’s a great opportunity to catch a variety of fish, including Yellowfin tuna, Mahi-Mahi, and Sailfish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pago Pago

Food and Drink Scene of Pago Pago

Must-Try Dishes

1. Samoan Chop Suey

A delicious blend of noodles, vegetables, and your choice of chicken, pork, or beef, Samoan Chop Suey is a staple dish in Pago Pago. It’s hearty and flavorful, and perfect for a quick lunch or dinner.

2. Koko Samoa

Koko Samoa is a traditional Samoan hot chocolate traditionally made with cacao beans, water, and coconut cream. It’s mild and not sweetened, perfect for a morning beverage.

Local Beverages

1. Vailima Beer

Vailima beer is a locally brewed beer that’s made from the pure natural spring water of the island.

2. Samoan Kava

Samoan Kava is an herbal drink made from the roots of the kava plant. It’s a social drink, and it’s drunk with friends and family during ceremonies.

Where to Stay in Pago Pago: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Tradewinds Hotel

Located near the airport, the Tradewinds Hotel is a stylish hotel that offers guests spacious rooms with stunning views of Pago Pago Harbor.

2. Sadie’s by the Sea

A comfortable and affordable beach resort, Sadie’s offers guests basic amenities and an excellent location near the coast.

What to Buy in Pago Pago: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Samoan Handicrafts

Pago Pago is known for its vibrant handicrafts, and visitors can get their hands on some of the best Samoan arts and crafts at the local markets.

2. Tapa Cloth

Tapa cloth is a traditional Samoan cloth that’s handmade from the inner bark of the paper mulberry tree. It’s a symbol of Samoan culture and heritage, and you can find it at local markets in Pago Pago.

Local Culture and Customs of Pago Pago

Samoan culture is steeped in tradition and history. Respect for elders and family values is central to the Samoan way of life. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and show respect for the culture.

Best Time to Visit Pago Pago

The weather in Pago Pago is consistent throughout the year, and the best time to visit is from April to September. The temperature averages around 85 degrees, and there’s a lot to do during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pago Pago

Do:

  • Respect the culture and traditions of the Samoan people.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Take part in the local customs and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Touch the head of a Samoan person, as it’s considered disrespectful.
  • Disrespect the environment by littering or damaging natural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pago Pago

Pago Pago is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take common-sense precautions to avoid theft and other petty crimes.

Health in Pago Pago

Visitors should take precautions to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Use mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved shirts and pants.

Getting to Pago Pago: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pago Pago

Pago Pago International Airport is the primary gateway to the island and is served by several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Pago Pago

There are several local bus services that run in and around Pago Pago, making it easy to get around the town.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available for visitors to use, but visitors should be aware that driving is on the left side of the road in American Samoa.

How to Get Around in Pago Pago

The best way to get around Pago Pago is by car, taxi, or local bus. Rental cars are readily available, and local buses are cheap and frequent.

Where to Go Next from Pago Pago: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Ofu Beach

Ofu Beach is a secluded beach located on the island of Ofu, just a short flight or ferry ride from Pago Pago.

2. Ta‘ū Island

Ta’ū Island is the easternmost island in American Samoa and home to some of the most stunning natural landscapes in the region.

Conclusion

Pago Pago is a destination like no other, with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a beach bum, there’s something for everyone on this island. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip and make unforgettable memories.
