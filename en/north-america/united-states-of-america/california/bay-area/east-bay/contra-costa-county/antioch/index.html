Antioch is a hidden gem in Contra Costa County, located in the East Bay area of the San Francisco Bay Area, California. With its rich history, beautiful scenery, and vibrant culture, Antioch is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. In this travel guide, we will explore the best attractions, dining options, lodging, and activities that Antioch has to offer.

Introduction to Antioch

Antioch is a small city in Northern California with a population of over 110,000 people. The city is known for its natural beauty, scenic views, cultural heritage, and historic landmarks. Antioch has a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and hot dry summers. The best time to visit Antioch is from March to May or from September to November when the weather is pleasant.

List of Places to Visit in Antioch

Antioch offers a diverse range of attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers. From exploring historical landmarks to hiking scenic routes, here are the best places to visit in Antioch:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Antioch Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the local history of Antioch with exhibits, artifacts, and photographs. It is a must-visit for history buffs.
  2. Black Diamond Mines Regional Preserve: This historic park features preserved coal mines, hiking trails, and guided tours.
  3. Dow Wetlands Preserve: This wetland park is an ecological oasis with hiking trails, birdwatching spots, and picnic areas.
  4. The East County Veteran’s Memorial: This war memorial pays tribute to the soldiers who served from East County.
  5. El Campanil Theatre: This atmospheric theatre hosts a variety of live performances including plays, music shows, and comedy acts.

Natural Attractions of Antioch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Contra Loma Regional Park: This park boasts a protected swimming lagoon, fishing lakes, hiking trails, and picnic areas.
  2. Lone Tree Golf Course: This golf course has stunning views of Mount Diablo and features a pro shop and a clubhouse.
  3. Antioch Dunes National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge is a protected area for endangered species of flora and fauna and has sand dunes that are over thirty feet tall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Antioch

Antioch has many trails and routes designed for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few popular routes:

  1. The Black Diamond Trail: This 6.5-mile trail features stunning views of the surrounding hills and passes through old coal mines.
  2. The Delta de Anza Trail: This trail starts from Antioch and follows the beautiful Sacramento River before ending in Martinez.
  3. The Marsh Creek Trail: This 7-mile trail crosses into Clayton and passes through rolling hills, picturesque valleys, and marshes.

Best Things to Do in Antioch: Activities and Tours

Antioch offers a diverse array of activities and tours that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here are the top things to do in Antioch:

  1. Take a guided tour of Black Diamond Mines Regional Preserve and learn about the city’s coal mining history.
  2. Go fishing at Contra Loma Regional Park or Hidden Lakes Park.
  3. Attend a live performance at El Campanil Theatre.
  4. Take a scenic drive through Antioch’s countryside and explore the Delta Ranches and vineyards.
  5. Visit the Antioch Paintball Park with friends.
