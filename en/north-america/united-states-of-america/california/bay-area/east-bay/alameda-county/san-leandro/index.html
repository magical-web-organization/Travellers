Introduction to San Leandro

San Leandro is a mid-sized city in California’s San Francisco Bay Area. It offers a quieter and more relaxed vibe than its bigger neighbors Oakland and San Francisco, with a quaint downtown area, plenty of parks, and a rich history.

San Leandro is home to many cultures, including African American, Latino, and Vietnamese communities, which is evident in its vibrant arts scene and diverse culinary offerings.

This travel guide will help you plan your trip to San Leandro, with information on places to visit, activities to try, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in San Leandro

San Leandro offers a variety of cultural and natural attractions. Here are some of the must-see places during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Leandro History Museum: Learn more about the history of San Leandro and view artifacts and exhibits that showcase the city’s past.

  • Pardee Home Museum: This Victorian-era mansion was once home to the Pardee family, who were instrumental in shaping California’s history. Take a guided tour of the house and gardens.

  • Cherry Festival: Held annually in June, this festival is a celebration of San Leandro’s agricultural history and features live entertainment, food, and crafts.

Natural Attractions of San Leandro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Chabot Regional Park: This park offers hiking trails, fishing, boating, and picnic areas, along with stunning views of Lake Chabot.

  • Cameron Park: This park is a popular spot for jogging, picnicking, and playing sports, with its many athletic fields and playgrounds.

  • Marina Park: Located on the San Leandro shoreline, this park has a picnic area, trails, and a fishing pier, with views of the San Francisco Bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Leandro

San Leandro is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails:

  • Bay Trail: This 500-mile trail is accessible from Marina Park and offers lovely views of the Bay.

  • Anthony Chabot Regional Park: This park has over 70 miles of trails, including the famous Skyline Trail, which is great for hiking and biking.

  • San Leandro Creek Trail: This trail runs through the city and offers a peaceful escape from urban life.

Best Things to Do in San Leandro: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, here are some other activities to try during your visit:

  • Chocolate Factory Tour: Take a tour of the Ghirardelli chocolate factory, located in San Leandro, and learn about the history of chocolate-making.

  • Wine Tasting: Visit one of the many wineries in the nearby Livermore Valley and try some great local wines.

  • Art Galleries: Check out some of the local art galleries in downtown San Leandro, which showcases the work of local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Leandro

If you have a week to explore San Leandro, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the San Leandro History Museum and take a picnic lunch at Marina Park.
  • Day 2: Take a hike in Anthony Chabot Regional Park and go wine tasting in Livermore Valley.
  • Day 3: Explore the Pardee Home Museum and stop by a local art gallery.
  • Day 4: Enjoy a day at Lake Chabot Regional Park.
  • Day 5: Visit Cameron Park and take a stroll through downtown San Leandro.
  • Day 6: Take a chocolate factory tour and grab a bite at a local Vietnamese restaurant.
  • Day 7: Attend the Cherry Festival and enjoy the live entertainment.

Food and Drink Scene of San Leandro

San Leandro has a great mix of cuisines, with a heavy focus on Vietnamese food. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pho: This traditional Vietnamese soup is a must-try. Check out Pho Lucky for some of the best pho in town.

  • Oakland-style Barbecue: San Leandro has its own take on barbecue, with several popular spots, including Everett & Jones and Tony’s.

  • Ghirardelli Chocolate: Take a tour of the Ghirardelli chocolate factory and indulge in some delicious chocolate.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: San Leandro has several breweries, including 21st Amendment and Cleophus Quealy.

  • Wine: The nearby Livermore Valley is home to many wineries, including Wente and Concannon.

Where to Stay in San Leandro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Leandro has several options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Oakland/San Leandro: This hotel is located near the Oakland International Airport and has easy access to all the major attractions in San Leandro.

  • San Leandro Marina Inn: This hotel is located across from Marina Park and offers great views of the Bay.

  • Z Hotel Jack London Square: This hotel is located a few miles from San Leandro in Oakland’s Jack London Square and offers modern amenities.

What to Buy in San Leandro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cherries: San Leandro was once a major producer of cherries, and you can still find many cherry products, including jams and dried cherries, at the Cherry Festival.

  • Art and Crafts: Check out some of the local art galleries and craft shops for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of San Leandro

San Leandro is a diverse community, with several cultures and customs. Be respectful and courteous to everyone you meet.

Best Time to Visit San Leandro

San Leandro has a typical California Mediterranean climate, with mild winters and warm summers. The best time to visit is from May through October when the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Leandro

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ privacy and personal space.

  • Try the local food and beverages.

  • Explore the outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car.

  • Carry large amounts of cash.

  • Ignore the local traffic rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Leandro

San Leandro is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, take precautions to ensure your personal safety, such as avoiding walking alone at night in unlit areas.

Health in San Leandro

San Leandro has several hospitals and medical facilities, including Kaiser Permanente and San Leandro Hospital.

Getting to San Leandro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Leandro

The Oakland International Airport is the nearest airport to San Leandro and is only a 15-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near San Leandro

The San Leandro BART station is the closest bus station, connecting San Leandro to the rest of the Bay Area through the Bay Area Rapid Transit (BART).

Other Options

You can also drive to San Leandro or take a taxi or ride-share service.

How to Get Around in San Leandro

San Leandro has good transportation options, including buses and BART. However, renting a car is the best option for exploring the area.

Where to Go Next from San Leandro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Leandro is located in the heart of the Bay Area and is in close proximity to many popular destinations, including:

  • San Francisco: Explore the city’s iconic landmarks, including the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and Fisherman’s Wharf.

  • Oakland: Check out the Oakland Museum of California and the Oakland Zoo, along with great restaurants and bars.

  • Berkeley: Explore the University of California, Berkeley campus and enjoy the city’s vibrant arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

San Leandro offers a peaceful and relaxed retreat from the busy Bay Area cities. Plan your trip to San Leandro, and enjoy the city’s rich history, diverse culture, and outdoor attractions.
