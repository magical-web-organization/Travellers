Introduction to Newark

Located in Alameda County in the East Bay region of the Bay Area, Newark is a small yet charming city in California that serves as the perfect destination for travelers seeking a blend of natural beauty and urban amenities. Despite being a lesser-known travel destination, Newark boasts several cultural and historical attractions, scenic hiking and biking trails, and delicious food and drink scenes.

This comprehensive travel guide highlights all the must-visit destinations, top attractions, and things to do in Newark, including tips on where to stay, what to eat, and how to make the most of your visit. So, let’s dive in and explore the hidden gems of Alameda County.

List of Places to Visit in Newark

Here are some of the best places to visit in Newark, California, that will take you on a journey through the city’s rich culture, history, and scenic landscapes.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Masjid Al-Momineen: It is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Newark, known for its grand architecture and unique Islamic Art. The mosque is a popular place for Muslims and non-Muslims alike.
  2. The Silliman Activity and Family Aquatic Center: A beautiful recreational place featuring a large swimming pool with a slide, a shallow wading pool for children, and picnic areas.
  3. Ardenwood Historic Farm: A 205-acre regional park dedicated to preserving the agricultural history of the area. This historical farm has several 19th-century buildings, gardens, and orchards that you can explore.
  4. Murray Rockwell Mansion: A Victorian-style house that is constructed back in 1876 to become a private home. This historical place is notable for its beautiful architecture and home to several artifacts and historical archives.

Natural Attractions of Newark: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Coyote Hills Regional Park: A vast open space park in the San Francisco Bay Area that offers over 978 acres of marshlands, grasslands, and oak trees. Perfect for hiking, nature walks, and birdwatching.
  2. Don Edwards San Francisco Bay National Wildlife Refuge: One of the largest urban nature preserves in the United States. Featuring over 30,000 acres of salt ponds, marshes, and mudflats, it is home to a variety of birds and other wildlife.
  3. Newark Community Park: A breathtaking park with a myriad of recreational activities for visitors, including playgrounds, picnic areas, basketball and tennis courts, and a skate park.
  4. Lake Elizabeth: Located in the suburbs of Newark, Lake Elizabeth is a scenic man-made lake that is surrounded by beautiful walking trails, parks, and a wildlife sanctuary.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newark

Hiking and biking are some of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Newark. Here are the top hiking and biking routes that you can explore:

  1. Coyote Hills Trail: This trail is a 3.6-mile hike that takes you through the scenic marshes and hills of Coyote Hills State Park.
  2. Alameda Creek Regional Trail: A trail that stretches from the San Francisco Bay to Niles Canyon and offers a fantastic biking route with stunning views of the natural landscapes.
  3. Quarry Lakes Regional Recreation Area: A beautiful park that is home to an extensive network of hiking and biking trails, including the Alameda Creek Trail and the Bay Area Ridge Trail.
  4. Dumbarton Bridge Trail: This trail is a popular biking route that spans across the San Francisco Bay and offers incredible views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Newark: Activities and Tours

  1. Wine Tours: Explore some of the best vineyards and wineries near Newark, and enjoy tastings and vineyard tours.
  2. Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the breathtaking views of the Newark skyline and the San Francisco Bay by taking a hot air balloon ride.
  3. Brewery Tours: Discover the local brew scene through a brewery tour in the city.
  4. Farmer’s Market at Newpark Mall: A Saturday morning farmers’ market that features fresh produce, food vendors, and an array of artisanal goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newark

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring the best of Newark:

Day 1-2

Explore the historical and cultural attractions of Newark, including Masjid Al-Momineen, the Murray Rockwell Mansion, and the Ardenwood Historic Farm.

Day 3-4

Discover the natural beauty of Newark by hiking or biking through the Coyote Hills Trail, Alameda Creek Regional Trail, or Quarry Lakes Regional Recreation Area.

Day 5

Take a wine tour or brewery tour and explore the local wine and brew culture.

Day 6

Take a hot air balloon ride and experience the breathtaking views of the city skyline and the San Francisco Bay.

Day 7

Visit the Newpark Mall Farmer’s Market and indulge in some locally sourced produce and artisanal goods.

Food and Drink Scene of Newark

Newark has a diverse and eclectic food scene that caters to all kinds of palates. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tacos: Super-tasty tacos are must-try dishes for visitors. For Authentic Mexican cuisine, visit Taqueria La Penita or La Costa Mariscos.
  2. Asian Cuisine: There are several top-rated Asian restaurants for visitors to taste east-Asian culinary delights, such as the famous Pho Cafe Vietnam, Teo Chow Fried Rice, and Saigon Kitchen.
  3. Seafood: Try the seafood specialties like sushi or fresh seafood delicacies sourced from the Bay Area. There are several seafood restaurants to visit, including the popular Joe’s Corner, King Crab, and The Crawfish Boys.

Local Beverages

  1. Napa Valley Wines: Explore the local wine scene and taste some fantastic California wines on a wine tour. Check some well-known vineyards near Newark.
  2. Microbreweries: Newark is known for its local microbreweries. Get a taste of some fantastic beer from some of the city’s must-visit breweries, including the popular Altamont Beer Works and Faction Brewing.

Where to Stay in Newark: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Newark has several accommodation options that cater to all budgets, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some top choices:

  1. DoubleTree by Hilton Newark-Fremont: A top-rated hotel that offers luxury amenities and stylish accommodations.
  2. Homewood Suites by Hilton Newark-Fremont: A pet-friendly hotel with spacious, modernly furnished rooms and fantastic recreational facilities.
  3. EZ 8 Motel Newark: A budget-friendly option known for its clean and comfortable rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Newark: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For souvenirs and local products, there are several options in Newark. Some of the best options include:

  1. Newark Farmers Market: A popular market that offers fresh produce, artisanal products, and locally sourced goods.
  2. NewPark Mall: It’s a great place to find souvenirs, accessories, apparel, gaming electronics, and other signature items.
  3. The San Francisco Chocolate Factory: A perfect place to buy chocolates and other confectionery treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Newark

Newark is a multicultural city that celebrates various cultural festivals and events throughout the year, such as Arabic and Islamic celebrations. Visitors are requested to dress modestly and respectfully when visiting mosques. The city also promotes and supports eco-friendly activities, such as recycling, bicycle riding, and green cleanups.

Best Time to Visit Newark

The best time to visit Newark is during the spring and autumn months (from March to May and September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and the city is less crowded. The summer months (from June to August) are the peak season and have high tourist traffic.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newark

Do:

  1. Dress in layers so that you can adjust to the changing weather conditions.
  2. Respect cultural and religious customs while visiting religious places.
  3. Explore the local wineries, breweries, and farmer’s markets.
  4. Use public transportation, bike riding, or distinct walking as your preferred mode of transportation wherever possible.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or harm wildlife while exploring the city’s natural surroundings.
  2. Leave your valuables unattended or unsecured while enjoying the sites and attractions.
  3. Jaywalk or ignore traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newark

Newark is a relatively safe city for travelers. However, it is always advisable to take basic precautions to ensure personal safety. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid walking alone at night, and keep your valuables secure.

Health in Newark

There are several medical facilities in Newark that cater to the healthcare needs of travelers. Visitors are requested to bring enough medication or prescriptions while traveling in Newark and seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Newark: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Newark

San Francisco International Airport is the closest major airport with a distance of approximately 28 miles from Newark. Oakland International Airport is the second nearest, located 22 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Newark

The Alameda-Contra Costa Transit District (AC Transit) offers several bus services to and from Newark. The Fremont BART station is the closest option for visitors traveling via Bay Area Rapid Transit (BART) and has regular train services to San Francisco and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also use rental cars, taxi services, or ride-share car services such as Uber and Lyft to travel around Newark and the Bay Area.

How to Get Around in Newark

Exploring Newark and the Bay Area is easy with several transportation options available, including buses, trains, and ferries. Visitors can also rent a car, use ride-sharing services or enjoy biking options that are widely available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Newark: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newark serves as an excellent base for exploring nearby cities and destinations. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  1. San Francisco: Experience the ultimate Bay Area destination by exploring one of the most iconic cities in the world, San Francisco.
  2. Oakland: Visit Oakland, the rapidly evolving urban destination in California, known for its diverse people and culture.
  3. San Jose: Explore the Silicon Valley and enjoy the Southern California vibe while visiting the city of San Jose.
  4. Napa Valley: Take a day trip to the Napa Valley and explore some of the best vineyards and wineries in the world.

Conclusion

Newark is a hidden gem in the Bay Area that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and urban amenities. From scenic hiking and biking trails to historical and cultural landmarks, Newark has something for everyone.
