Introduction to Belmont

Belmont is a charming city located in San Mateo County in the heart of the San Francisco Bay Area. Known for its beautiful parks, natural preserves, and preserved historic landmarks, Belmont is one of the top destinations for travelers seeking serenity, adventure, and culture. Whether you’re looking for outdoor activities, cultural experiences, or simply looking to relax, Belmont has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Belmont

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Belmont Historical Society Museum

The Belmont Historical Society Museum is an excellent place to explore the rich history of Belmont. Discover how the city has evolved over the years and learn about its past through artifacts, photographs, and exhibits. The museum also features docent-led tours and events throughout the year.

Twin Pines Art Center

The Twin Pines Art Center is a community art center that showcases the works of local artists. The center features rotating exhibits, classes, and workshops for visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Belmont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Waterdog Lake Park

Waterdog Lake Park is a 236-acre preserve that features hiking trails, scenic views of the Bay Area, and a beautiful lake. The park is a perfect place to enjoy a picnic, kayaking, or birdwatching.

Cipriani Park

Cipriani Park is a public park that features beautiful walking trails, a playground, and plenty of green space. This park is perfect for families looking to spend a peaceful afternoon outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belmont

Belmont is home to some of the most beautiful hiking/biking trails in the Bay Area. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Sawyer Camp Trail
  • Waterdog Lake Trail
  • Crystal Springs Regional Trail

Best Things to Do in Belmont: Activities and Tours

Belmont Farmers’ Market

If you’re looking for locally-produced fruits, vegetables, and other goods, make sure to visit the Belmont Farmers’ Market. The market takes place every Sunday and offers a variety of fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, bread, and more.

Belmont Go Karts

For a fun-filled day with family and friends, head over to Belmont Go Karts. The park offers a variety of go-kart tracks, mini-golf, and arcade games.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belmont

Day 1: Explore the Belmont Historical Society Museum and Twin Pines Art Center.

Day 2: Take a hike on Sawyer Camp Trail and enjoy a picnic at the shores of Crystal Springs Reservoir.

Day 3: Visit the Waterdog Lake Park, rent a kayak or bring your own boat and spend a few hours on the lake.

Day 4: Grab some locally-sourced produce at the Belmont Farmers’ Market and spend the afternoon at Cipriani Park.

Day 5: Head to Belmont Go Karts and enjoy a day of racing and mini-golf.

Day 6: Visit the nearby city of San Mateo and explore the San Mateo County History Museum.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Belmont’s many parks or take a scenic drive along the coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Belmont

Must-Try Dishes

Mexican Cuisine at La Hacienda

La Hacienda is a beloved restaurant serving some of the best Mexican cuisine in Belmont. Try the taquitos, burritos, or huevos rancheros for a delicious taste of traditional Mexican food.

Mediterranean Food at Falafelle

Falafelle is a Mediterranean restaurant located in the heart of Belmont. Choose from a variety of hummus, falafel, and shawarma dishes that are sure to delight your taste buds.

Local Beverages

Devil’s Canyon Brewing Company

Devil’s Canyon Brewing Company is a local brewery in Belmont that serves some of the best craft beers in the Bay Area. Make sure to try the Deadicated Amber Ale and the Full Boar Scotch Ale.

Notre Dame des Souvenirs

Notre Dame des Souvenirs is a cozy cafe known for its delicious coffee, pastries, and sandwiches. This is the perfect spot for a quick bite or a leisurely brunch.

Where to Stay in Belmont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Country Inn & Suites by Radisson

The Country Inn & Suites by Radisson is a comfortable hotel located in the heart of Belmont. The hotel offers complimentary breakfast, free Wi-Fi, and an outdoor pool.

Belmont Inn & Suites

The Belmont Inn & Suites is a budget-friendly hotel that features clean rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Belmont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Twin Pines Park Souvenir Shop

The Twin Pines Park Souvenir Shop is a great place to shop for unique Belmont-themed gifts and souvenirs. Choose from Belmont-themed t-shirts, hats, postcards, and more.

Cipriani Farmers Market

The Cipriani Farmers Market is a great place to buy locally-sourced produce, artisanal cheeses, bread, and chocolates. It’s perfect for those looking to bring home a taste of Belmont.

Local Culture and Customs of Belmont

Belmont has a unique culture that is shaped by its rich history and diverse community. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and engage with residents to learn more about the city’s traditions.

Best Time to Visit Belmont

The best time to visit Belmont is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and crowded, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belmont

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city’s historic landmarks and natural attractions.
  • Try the delicious local cuisine and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Disrespect local residents or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belmont

Belmont is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid becoming a victim of crime. Always lock your car doors and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight. If you’re traveling alone, make sure to stay in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Belmont

Belmont has several medical facilities that can meet the needs of visitors. Please note that medical services can be expensive in the United States, so it’s important to have health insurance when traveling.

Getting to Belmont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belmont

The closest airport to Belmont is San Francisco International Airport (SFO), which is about 20 miles away. Other airports in the area include Oakland International Airport (OAK) and San Jose International Airport (SJC).

Bus Stations Near Belmont

The closest bus station to Belmont is the Belmont Caltrain Station, which is served by the SamTrans bus system.

Other Options

Belmont is easily accessible by car and is located near several major highways, including Highway 101 and Interstate 280.

How to Get Around in Belmont

Belmont is a small city that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use the SamTrans bus system to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Belmont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Francisco

San Francisco is located just 25 miles north of Belmont and is one of the most iconic cities in the world. Explore the Golden Gate Bridge, Fisherman’s Wharf, or Alcatraz Island.

Half Moon Bay

Half Moon Bay is located just 15 miles west of Belmont and is a popular destination for beach lovers. Spend the day surfing, swimming, or sunbathing.

Conclusion

Belmont is a captivating city that offers something for everyone. From hiking and biking to cultural experiences and delicious cuisine, Belmont has it all. Plan your next visit to Belmont and discover the best of the Bay Area.
