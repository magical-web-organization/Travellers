San Mateo is a vibrant city located in the Peninsula region of the San Francisco Bay Area, California. The city is known for its rich heritage, cultural attractions, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in San Mateo.

Introduction to San Mateo

San Mateo is home to numerous parks, hiking trails, museums, art galleries, and theaters. The city’s downtown area offers shopping, dining, and entertainment opportunities. San Mateo also hosts a variety of events and festivals throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in San Mateo

San Mateo has a lot of attractions for visitors to explore. Here are a few must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Mateo County History Museum: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into the history of San Mateo County. Exhibits showcase the region’s cultural heritage, natural resources, and significant people and events.
  • CuriOdyssey: This science center and zoo is designed to inspire children’s curiosity about science and nature. It features interactive exhibits, live animal habitats, and daily animal presentations.
  • Filoli: Filoli is a historic site and garden, which is now open to the public. It features a stunning Georgian mansion, formal gardens, and hiking trails.

Natural Attractions of San Mateo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coyote Point Recreation Area: This park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, picnicking, fishing, and sailing. Coyote Point also features a playground, a beach, and a wildlife refuge.
  • Crystal Springs Regional Trail: This trail runs along the west side of the Crystal Springs Reservoir, offering stunning views of the water and the mountains beyond. The trail is ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • San Mateo Japanese Garden: This garden is a tranquil oasis in the city located in Central Park. The garden features a winding path around a koi pond, a teahouse, and a bamboo grove.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Mateo

San Mateo is home to several trails that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are three of the most popular routes:

  • Sawyer Camp Trail: This trail runs for six miles along the Crystal Springs Reservoir and offers stunning views of wildflowers, wildlife, and the reservoir.
  • Hillsdale Loop Trail: This 1.5-mile loop trail winds through the hills above Hillsdale Shopping Center and offers panoramic views of the San Francisco Bay.
  • Bay Trail: This trail is part of a larger 500-mile network of trails around the San Francisco Bay. It runs along the San Mateo County shoreline, offering sweeping views of the bay and opportunities to see migratory birds and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in San Mateo: Activities and Tours

San Mateo has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors busy. Here are some of the best:

  • Visit the Hillsdale Shopping Center: This shopping center has over 130 stores and restaurants, perfect for a day of shopping and dining.
  • Hop on a Winery Tour: San Mateo is part of the larger San Francisco Bay Area wine region and features many wineries that are open for tours and tastings.
  • Take a Boat Tour: Rent a boat or book a tour and explore the San Francisco Bay and surrounding waterways.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Mateo

Visitors who have a week to explore San Mateo should consider the following itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the San Mateo County History Museum and Coyote Point Recreation Area.

Day 2: Hike the Sawyer Camp Trail in the morning and then visit CuriOdyssey in the afternoon.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of the San Francisco Bay.

Day 4: Explore the Hillsdale Shopping Center.

Day 5: Visit Filoli and the San Mateo Japanese Garden.

Day 6: Take a winery tour of the San Francisco Bay Area.

Day 7: Walk along the Bay Trail and enjoy the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of San Mateo

San Mateo offers a diverse culinary scene, featuring options from around the world. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: San Mateo is located close to the coast, giving access to fresh seafood choices. Try the lobster bisque from Viognier.
  • Dim Sum: San Mateo has numerous restaurants which serve authentic Dim Sum. Yank Sing and Mayflower Restaurant are just some of the popular choices.
  • Italian cuisine: If you like Italian cuisine, you won’t be disappointed in San Mateo’s Vivace Ristorante.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Check out David’s Tea and Coffee Shop, where the friendly staff will serve you a delicious coffee.
  • Wine: San Mateo is part of the larger San Francisco Bay Area wine region and has many wineries that offer tastings, so it’s worth trying local wines.

Where to Stay in San Mateo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Mateo has a variety of accommodation options to suit the needs and budgets of its visitors. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Hotel Triton: An eco-friendly hotel that features quirky decor and modern amenities.
  • San Mateo Marriott: A comfortable hotel located near the Hillsdale Shopping Center.
  • The Peninsula Regent: A luxurious retirement apartment building, available to rent as guesthouses.

What to Buy in San Mateo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Mateo offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts to bring home to family and friends. Here’s a list to help you out:

  • Artistic Souvenirs: San Mateo has a thriving art scene, and you’ll find beautiful and unique pieces at various art galleries.
  • Local Wines: The San Francisco Bay Area is known for its wine. You can bring home bottles of wine from wineries in San Mateo.
  • Chocolate: San Francisco Bay Area is home to several locally-owned chocolate makers, and San Mateo is no exception. Try Ho’s Chocolate for delicious chocolate souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of San Mateo

San Mateo is home to a diverse community of people. It’s important to be respectful of cultural customs. English is the dominant language, but you may hear other languages depending on the area. The city hosts various events throughout the year - such as the Japanese Obon Festival - which celebrate different cultures with food and performance.

Best Time to Visit San Mateo

San Mateo experiences a Mediterranean climate, with mild temperatures throughout the year. The best time to visit San Mateo is during its dry season between May and October. It’s best to avoid visiting during the winter months, as it can get cold and wet.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Mateo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs.
  • Explore the outdoor attractions.
  • Experience the local food scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Ignore traffic and pedestrian rules.
  • Leave valuables visible in a car.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Mateo

San Mateo is a safe city, but visitors should always take necessary precautions to stay safe. The downtown areas are known to be safe, but other neighbourhoods may be less so. Avoid walking alone at night in secluded streets. This can mitigate some of the risks of theft and mugging. Additionally, take care when driving in the area and be mindful of traffic laws and regulations.

Health in San Mateo

San Mateo has access to quality health care, with several hospitals and urgent care centres located within the city. Ticks carrying Lyme disease can be found in some areas of San Mateo. Hikers, campers, and outdoor enthusiasts should dress appropriately and take necessary precautions when spending time outdoors.

Getting to San Mateo: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Mateo is well-connected and can be accessed by road, rail, and air. Here are some ways to get to the city:

Airports Near San Mateo

  • San Francisco International Airport: San Mateo is approximately five miles from the San Francisco International Airport (SFO).
  • Oakland International Airport: The Oakland International Airport (OAK) is approximately 28 miles from San Mateo.
  • Norman Y. Mineta San Jose International Airport: The Norman Y. Mineta San Jose International Airport (SJC) is approximately 25 miles from San Mateo.

Bus Stations Near San Mateo

  • SamTrans: SamTrans provides local bus services throughout San Mateo County.

Other Options

  • Caltrain: Caltrain runs along the San Francisco Peninsula, from San Francisco to San Jose and makes stops in San Mateo.
  • Car rental: Car rentals can be accessed from various car rental agencies located throughout San Mateo.

How to Get Around in San Mateo

San Mateo is a walkable city, but it’s advisable to use other transportation options to get around if you are unfamiliar with the city. Taxis, ride-sharing services, and public transit options are available to visitors.

Where to Go Next from San Mateo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Mateo is located in the San Francisco Bay Area, making it a convenient base for exploring some of the nearby attractions. Consider visiting the following destinations:

  • San Francisco: This iconic city is just a short drive away and is known for its vibrant culture, delicious food, and unique landmarks.
  • Menlo Park: Located less than 10 miles from San Mateo, Menlo Park is a quaint town, known for its art galleries, historic architecture, and fine dining.
  • Half Moon Bay: A popular seaside destination located just a short drive away from San Mateo. The town is famous for its beaches, coastal hiking trails, and tide pool exploration opportunities.

Conclusion

Now that you have a comprehensive guide to San Mateo, it’s time to pack your bags and begin exploring! Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, San Mateo has something for everyone.
