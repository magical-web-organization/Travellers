Introduction to Burlingame

Nestled in Peninsula, in the Bay Area of California, Burlingame is a charming and picturesque city that’s worth exploring. It boasts a unique blend of natural and man-made attractions, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure-seekers, history buffs, and foodies. Here is a comprehensive tour guide to the top things to do and see in Burlingame.

List of Places to Visit in Burlingame

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Burlingame Museum of Pez Memorabilia

The Burlingame Museum of Pez Memorabilia is a quirky museum dedicated to showcasing the largest collection of Pez dispensers from around the world. Here you’ll find over 900 Pez dispensers, from vintage to modern, and various limited edition dispensers that are exclusive to the museum.

Burlingame Historical Society Museum

The Burlingame Historical Society Museum is a must-visit for history lovers who want to know more about the city’s rich past. The museum displays various historical artifacts and documents that tell the stories of Burlingame’s early settlers, from its inception to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Burlingame: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Crystal Springs Regional Trail

The Crystal Springs Regional Trail is a 17-mile trail that starts in San Bruno and ends in Woodside, passing through the heart of Burlingame. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is perfect for hiking, biking, and even horseback riding.

Coyote Point Recreation Area

Coyote Point Recreation Area is a 670-acre park located on a peninsula jutting out into the San Francisco Bay. It is a perfect spot for outdoor enthusiasts, with facilities for windsurfing, kayaking, fishing, and picnicking. The park also has a child-friendly museum, the CuriOdyssey, featuring interactive science exhibits and live animal displays.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burlingame

Sawyer Camp Trail

The Sawyer Camp Trail is a 6-mile trail located in San Mateo, just a stone’s throw away from Burlingame. The trail is popular among hikers, runners, and cyclists because of its picturesque views of the Crystal Springs Reservoir and beautiful hills.

Burlingame Avenue Bicycle Boulevard

The Burlingame Avenue Bicycle Boulevard is a 2-mile bicycle path that follows Burlingame Avenue. It is a designated bike lane, giving cyclists priority on the road and creating a safer and more comfortable experience.

Best Things to Do in Burlingame: Activities and Tours

Burlingame Farmer’s Market

The Burlingame Farmer’s Market takes place every Sunday, and it’s a perfect spot for foodies looking for local and seasonal produce. You’ll find a variety of fruits, vegetables, bread, meat, cheese, and other culinary delights.

Burlingame Wine Trail

The Burlingame Wine Trail is a self-guided tour featuring some of the best wineries and tasting rooms in the city. Here you’ll be able to taste some of the finest local wines and learn about the wine-making processes.

Food and Drink Scene of Burlingame

Must-Try Dishes

Boudin SF

Boudin SF is a local bakery chain known for its sourdough bread. Their sourdough clam chowder bowl is a must-try when visiting Burlingame.

Broadway Grill

Broadway Grill is a popular steakhouse featuring an extensive menu of meat, seafood, and pasta dishes. Their signature California classic burger is a fan favorite.

Local Beverages

Barebottle Brewing Company

Barebottle Brewing Company offers a wide selection of hand-crafted beers brewed on-site. They specialize in unique and experimental flavors that reflect the local area’s tastes.

Mr. Tipple’s Recording Studio

Mr. Tipple’s Recording Studio is a jazz bar and cocktail lounge known for their lively atmosphere and classic cocktails.

Where to Stay in Burlingame: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Francisco Airport Marriott Waterfront

San Francisco Airport Marriott Waterfront is a luxurious hotel located by the San Francisco Bay. Their rooms feature stunning views of the water, and they have various amenities, including a restaurant, pool, and fitness center.

Hilton Garden Inn San Francisco Airport/Burlingame

Hilton Garden Inn San Francisco Airport/Burlingame is a modern and comfortable hotel located near the airport. Their rooms feature refrigerators and microwaves, and they have an on-site restaurant and fitness center.

What to Buy in Burlingame: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pez Dispensers

Bring home a unique Pez dispenser from the Burlingame Museum of Pez Memorabilia as a quirky and fun souvenir.

Burlingame Sourdough Bread

Boudin SF sells their sourdough bread, a popular local treat, making for a perfect edible souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Burlingame

Burlingame has a rich and diverse culture with a strong community spirit. Residents often participate in local events such as farmers’ markets, art shows, and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Burlingame

The best time to visit Burlingame is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the city is a year-round destination, as it has mild weather throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burlingame

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture
  • Attempt to try local dishes and beverages
  • Explore the city by foot or bike
  • Visit local museums and historical sites

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended
  • Litter and pollute the natural environment
  • Disrespect the local community and its traditions
  • Enter private property without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Burlingame

Burlingame is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions such as keeping valuables out of sight, locking doors, and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Burlingame

Burlingame has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors are encouraged to have travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Getting to Burlingame: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burlingame

The San Francisco International Airport is the closest airport to Burlingame, located just 3 miles away. The airport has several transportation options, including shuttles, taxis, and rental cars.

Bus Stations Near Burlingame

The Burlingame Caltrain Station is the closest train station to the city, providing access to the surrounding areas, including San Francisco and Silicon Valley. Bus and shuttle services are also available.

Other Options

Burlingame is easily accessible by car, with several major highways, including US Route 101 and Interstate 280, passing through the city.

How to Get Around in Burlingame

Burlingame is a walkable and bike-friendly city, with several bike trails and designated bike lanes. Rental cars, taxis, and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Burlingame: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Francisco

San Francisco, located just 17 miles away, is a must-visit city for any traveler. It’s known for its iconic landmarks, diverse neighborhoods, and renowned food and drink scene.

Silicon Valley

Silicon Valley is a short drive away from Burlingame and is home to several major technology companies, including Google, Apple, and Facebook. Visitors can tour the company campuses and learn more about the tech industry.

Conclusion

Burlingame is a charming and picturesque city that’s worth visiting. Its unique blend of natural and man-made attractions, outdoor adventures, food and drink scene, and cultural events make it a perfect destination for any type of traveler. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your next visit to Burlingame and make the most of your trip.
