Introduction to Colma

Tucked away in the Peninsula region of the San Francisco Bay Area lies the small town of Colma, California. With a rich history dating back to the mid-1800s, Colma has become known as the “City of the Dead” due to being the final resting place for over 1.5 million people. Despite its eerie nickname, Colma boasts beautiful parks, a thriving cultural scene, and delicious food options. This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to create an unforgettable trip to Colma.

List of Places to Visit in Colma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Holy Cross Catholic Cemetery: Founded in 1887, this cemetery features stunning architecture and several notable burials including Joe DiMaggio and William Randolph Hearst.
  • Cypress Lawn Memorial Park: Another prominent cemetery, Cypress Lawn offers picturesque gardens, intricate mausoleums, and a museum housing fascinating artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Colma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Diamond Heights: Part of the San Francisco Bay Trail, this scenic viewpoint offers breathtaking views of the bay and downtown San Francisco.
  • Colma Creek: Take a relaxing stroll along the Colma Creek Trail and enjoy the lush greenery and peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colma

  • San Bruno Mountain State Park: Just a short drive from Colma, San Bruno Mountain State Park offers several trails for hiking and mountain biking, as well as stunning views of the San Francisco Bay.

Best Things to Do in Colma: Activities and Tours

  • Green Hills Memorial Park: This serene cemetery offers memorial services and educational tours.
  • Colma Historical Association Museum: Learn about the rich history of Colma, including the town’s evolution from a dairy farming community to its current status as a hub for the cemetery industry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colma

Day Activity
1 Visit Holy Cross Catholic Cemetery and Cypress Lawn Memorial Park

Food and Drink Scene of Colma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garlic Crab: A San Francisco Bay Area classic, this dish is typically served in a spicy garlic sauce.
  • Dim sum: Colma has several authentic Chinese dim sum restaurants, so don’t miss out on this delicious brunch option.

Local Beverages

  • Anchor Steam Beer: A San Francisco staple, this beer is brewed by Anchor Brewing Company and is a must-try for any beer lover.
  • California wines: With plenty of wineries nearby, Colma offers a great opportunity to sample some of California’s finest wines.

Where to Stay in Colma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly option located just a short drive from San Francisco.
  • Inn at Oyster Point: This waterfront hotel offers stunning views and luxurious accommodations.

What to Buy in Colma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cemetery Memorabilia: Many of the cemeteries in Colma offer unique souvenirs, including books, postcards, and clothing.
  • Local Wines: Bring home a bottle of wine from one of the nearby wineries as a delicious reminder of your trip to Colma.

Local Culture and Customs of Colma

Colma is a town with a strong appreciation for its history and a unique industry centered around cemeteries and memorial parks. Visitors are encouraged to respect the solemnity of the cemeteries and be mindful of the town’s unique culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Colma

Colma’s mild climate makes it a great destination year-round. However, the summer months (June-September) tend to be the busiest and most expensive due to peak tourist season. Spring (March-May) and fall (October-November) offer mild temperatures, smaller crowds, and cheaper accommodations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colma

Do:

  • Take a scenic hike at San Bruno Mountain State Park.
  • Visit one of Colma’s many cemeteries and memorial parks.
  • Sample the local cuisine, including garlic crab and dim sum.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the solemnity of the cemeteries.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Leave without trying a local wine or beer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colma

Colma is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always take the usual safety precautions when traveling, including locking car doors and keeping wallets and valuables safe.

Health in Colma

Colma has several medical facilities nearby, including Seton Medical Center and San Francisco General Hospital.

Getting to Colma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colma

  • San Francisco International Airport: Located just 7 miles north of Colma, this is the closest airport.
  • Oakland International Airport: Located 22 miles southeast of Colma, this airport is a viable option for those seeking a cheaper flight.

Bus Stations Near Colma

  • Colma BART Station: A major regional transit hub, with connections to the San Francisco Bay Area and beyond.
  • SamTrans: The local bus system that connects Colma to the surrounding region.

Other Options

  • Ridesharing apps such as Uber and Lyft are widely available.
  • Biking is also a popular mode of transportation in Colma, with several bike lanes and paths throughout town.

How to Get Around in Colma

Walking or biking are great options for getting around Colma. Public transportation is also available through the Colma BART Station and local bus system.

Where to Go Next from Colma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Francisco: Just a short drive or train ride away, San Francisco offers world-class entertainment, dining, and shopping.
  • Half Moon Bay: A charming coastal town, Half Moon Bay is known for its stunning beaches, beautiful natural parks, and great seafood.

Conclusion

From history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts, Colma has something to offer everyone. With its beautiful landscapes, vibrant culture, and unique industry, Colma is a must-see destination for anyone visiting the San Francisco Bay Area.
