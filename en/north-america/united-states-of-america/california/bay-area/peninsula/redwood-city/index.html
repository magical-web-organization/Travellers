Introduction to Redwood City

Located in the heart of Silicon Valley, Redwood City is a bustling city that serves as the county seat of San Mateo County. This charming city is filled with exciting attractions, outdoor adventures, and tasty dining options. With its prime location near San Francisco, Napa Valley, and Santa Cruz, Redwood City is the perfect gateway to the Bay Area.

List of Places to Visit in Redwood City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Mateo County History Museum

Located inside a historic courthouse, the San Mateo County History Museum explores the fascinating history of the region. Exhibits include an overview of the Native American tribes who lived in the area, the Gold Rush-era, and the Silicon Valley boom.

Fox Theatre

The Fox Theatre is a gorgeous Art Deco venue that first opened in 1929. Today, it hosts concerts, plays, and other performances.

Natural Attractions of Redwood City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Edgewood Park and Natural Preserve

Edgewood Park and Natural Preserve is an idyllic spot for hiking and nature walks. Trails are well-maintained and wind through lush forests, fields, and hills.

Pulgas Water Temple

Also known as the Pulgas Temple of Love, this unique attraction was built in 1934. The temple’s grand architecture and scenic surroundings make it a popular spot for weddings and photography sessions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redwood City

Pulgas Ridge Reserve

This park has over 300 acres of hiking trails with stunning views of the Peninsula.

Sawyer Camp Trail

This trail is the perfect spot to bike or hike with a beautiful view of the Crystal Springs Reservoir.

Best Things to Do in Redwood City: Activities and Tours

San Francisco Bay Sailing Tour

Take a scenic two-hour sailing tour of the San Francisco Bay. Enjoy views of the Golden Gate Bridge and San Francisco skyline.

Revolution Escape Room

For a thrilling adventure, try the Revolution Escape Room. You and your team will solve puzzles and work together to escape a themed room in under an hour.

Food and Drink Scene of Redwood City

Must-Try Dishes

Donato Enoteca

This Italian restaurant serves mouth-watering homemade pasta, wood-fired pizza, and a selection of wine.

Vesta

Vesta specializes in wood-fired cuisine, including their oak-fired meats and roasted vegetable dishes.

Local Beverages

Half Moon Bay Brewing Company

The Half Moon Bay Brewing Company serves up craft beer made on-site. Enjoy a pint of beer while taking in a view of the harbor.

Domenico Winery

Domenico Winery produces handcrafted, small-batch wines featuring Italian varietals, along with a few Californian classics.

Where to Stay in Redwood City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Western Plus Executive Suites

This hotel is located within walking distance of the Caltrain station and offers guests a variety of amenities, including an indoor pool and hot tub.

Pacific Euro Hotel

This European-style budget hotel offers cozy, clean rooms in a central location.

What to Buy in Redwood City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Studio Shop

This shop showcases the work of local artists and craftspeople. Browse one-of-a-kind jewelry, pottery, and art prints.

Woodside Store

Built in 1854, this historic general store now sells a variety of souvenirs and local treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Redwood City

Redwood City is a diverse and welcoming community. It is important to respect the city’s traditions and customs, including its commitment to the environment and sustainability.

Best Time to Visit Redwood City

The best time to visit Redwood City is from September to November, when temperatures are mild and the crowds have thinned out. Spring is also a good time to visit, with blooming wildflowers throughout the parks and hiking trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redwood City

Do:

  • Bring a reusable water bottle and shopping bag to reduce your environmental impact.
  • Visit the San Mateo County History Museum to learn about the city’s rich history.
  • Try the local cuisine, including wood-fired pizza and oak-cooked meats.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables visible in your car.
  • Litter or leave trash in the parks and hiking trails.
  • Visit outside of peak hours if you want to avoid crowds.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redwood City

While Redwood City is generally a safe city, visitors should take precautions when walking alone at night or in unpopulated areas. Keep valuables out of sight in your car to avoid break-ins.

Health in Redwood City

No specific health risks are associated with Redwood City. However, visitors should be aware of the risks of sunburn and dehydration when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Redwood City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Redwood City

The closest airport to Redwood City is San Francisco International Airport (SFO), approximately 20 miles north of the city.

Bus Stations Near Redwood City

The nearest bus station is the Redwood City Caltrain Station, which is located in the heart of downtown.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-hailing services such as Uber and Lyft are readily available in Redwood City.

How to Get Around in Redwood City

The best way to get around Redwood City is by car. However, there are also bike routes throughout the city, and Caltrain can take you up or down the Peninsula.

Where to Go Next from Redwood City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Francisco

Head north to the iconic city of San Francisco, known for its hilly streets, Golden Gate Bridge, and the historic Alcatraz prison.

Napa Valley

Go east to the famous wine country of Napa Valley, where you can explore vineyards and sample delicious wine.

Santa Cruz

Head south to Santa Cruz, a popular beach destination known for its boardwalk and classic amusement park rides.

Conclusion

Redwood City offers visitors a wide range of attractions and activities, from hiking and biking to exploring museums and indulging in delicious food and drink. With its proximity to other Bay Area destinations, it’s the perfect place for a weekend getaway or a longer stay.
