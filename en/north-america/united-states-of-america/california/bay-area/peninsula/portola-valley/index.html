Introduction to Portola Valley

Nestled in the scenic Santa Cruz Mountains in the San Francisco Bay Area, Portola Valley is a small town with big charm. Its idyllic surroundings and laid-back atmosphere make it a perfect weekend getaway destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. If you’re planning a trip to Portola Valley, this travel guide will help you narrow down the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Portola Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Portola Valley has a rich history, and visiting these cultural attractions will give you insight into the town’s past.

  • The Portola Valley Town Center is a great starting point for your exploration. The center has a beautiful community garden, a playground, and an environmental education center with exhibits on the local ecology.
  • The Alpine Inn & Beer Garden, also known as Rossotti’s, is a historic saloon that has been serving patrons since 1852. The building has been well-preserved and is a local favorite for burgers and beer.
  • The Portola Valley Library is a small but cozy library with a collection of books, magazines, and movies perfect for a rainy day.
  • The Pigeon Point Lighthouse Station is a historical landmark located about an hour from Portola Valley. The lighthouse built-in 1872 is a popular spot for whale watching during the migration season.

Natural Attractions of Portola Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Portola Valley is surrounded by picturesque scenery - from lush forests to rolling hills and scenic lakes. Here are a few must-visit natural attractions:

  • The Windy Hill Open Space Preserve is a perfect spot for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The preserve has over 12 miles of trails with scenic views of the San Francisco Bay and surrounding hills.
  • The Russian Ridge Open Space Preserve is a great destination for wildflower fans. The preserve is filled with blooming flowers in the spring and summer, and its hilltops provide panoramic views of the Bay Area.
  • The Foothills Park is a 1,400-acre preserve with hiking trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds. The park is a great spot to relax and unwind while enjoying the natural beauty of Portola Valley.
  • The Arastradero Preserve is a popular destination for bird watchers and nature photographers. The preserve has over 10 miles of trails and is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and bobcats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portola Valley

Portola Valley has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails, catering to hikers and bikers of all skill levels. Here are a few popular routes:

  • The Portola Valley Loop Trail is a 3.8 miles trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and countryside. The trail is easy to navigate and suitable for beginners.
  • The Woodside to Portola Valley Trail is a 3.5 miles trail that connects the two towns. The trail is easy and is popular amongst hikers and mountain bikers.
  • For more challenging hikes, check out the Windy Hill Open Space Preserve, which has over 12 miles of trails and plenty of hills to climb.

Best Things to Do in Portola Valley: Activities and Tours

Portola Valley offers a range of activities and tours to satisfy every traveler’s taste. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Take a horseback riding tour of the Windy Hill Open Space Preserve for a unique perspective of the countryside.
  • Get your adrenaline pumping with a skydiving tour of the Bay Area. Skydiving companies operate in the area and offer breath-taking views as you freefall.
  • Visit the local farmers’ market to taste fresh produce, sample local honey, and chat with the farmers about their sustainable farming practices.
  • Head to the Alpine Inn & Beer Garden for a relaxing beer and burger session with the locals.
  • Sign up for a photography tour to learn about the local flora and fauna and to capture stunning photos of the Bay Area.

Food and Drink Scene of Portola Valley

Must-Try Dishes

Portola Valley has a range of dining options to choose from. Be sure to try these local dishes:

  • The Alpine Burger at the Alpine Inn & Beer Garden is a must-try. It’s a classic cheeseburger served with fries and beer.
  • The wild mushroom risotto at the local Italian restaurant is a perfect vegetarian dish and capitalizes on the local harvest.
  • The seafood selection at the local restaurants features fresh Dungeness crab, salmon and other seafood from the Pacific Coast.

Local Beverages

Portola Valley is home to a number of craft breweries and wine bars. Here are a few local beverages you must try:

  • The local beer is a favorite amongst locals and visitors with dozens of local breweries in the surrounding regions
  • The region is particularly known for their full-bodied fruit forward Cabernet Sauvignons, and Pinot Noirs.

Where to Stay in Portola Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Portola Valley has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are a few suggestions:

  • The Portola Inn Bed and Breakfast offers cozy and comfortable rooms, with views of the surrounding hills and gardens.
  • If you’re looking for a budget accommodation option, the HI-Pigeon Point Lighthouse Hostel provides a unique experience in a historic setting.
  • For a luxurious stay, check out the Rosewood Sand Hill Hotel and Resort, located just outside the town on the east slope of the Santa Cruz Mountains.

What to Buy in Portola Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Portola Valley without picking up a few souvenirs to take home. Here are some recommendations:

  • Raw Honey from the local farms
  • Handmade birdhouses and environmentally friendly toys for little ones
  • Wooden cutting boards crafted by local artisans using local hardwoods.

Local Culture and Customs of Portola Valley

Portola Valley is a small town, and the locals are warm and friendly. The farmer’s market and community events are the perfect way to connect with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Portola Valley

The best time to visit Portola Valley is from March to October when the weather is mild. The bustling Farmers Market in Downtown Portola Valley is open every Thursday from the first Thursday in May to the last Thursday in October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portola Valley

Do:

  • Do respect the natural beauty of the surroundings by leaving no trace and being mindful of your interactions with the environment.
  • Do try the local craft beer and wine scene.
  • Do learn about the local history and attend events hosted in the town center.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t drive off-road, stay on the designated trails.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate hiking and outdoor gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Portola Valley

Portola Valley has a low crime rate, but visitors should still exercise caution and use common sense like in any city, especially when hiking, biking and exploring outdoor spaces

Health in Portola Valley

If you’re visiting Portola Valley from abroad, pack insect repellent and long sleeves as a precaution against mosquito-borne diseases. Beware that exposure to the sun can be intense, so bring sunscreen and sunglasses.

Getting to Portola Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Portola Valley

The closest airports to Portola Valley are the San Jose International Airport (SJC) and the San Francisco International Airport (SFO), both located within an hour’s drive.

Bus Stations Near Portola Valley

The closest bus stations are located in Menlo Park and Redwood City, both of which are accessible via CalTrain.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Portola Valley. However, for those who prefer to limit their carbon footprint, electric bikes are available for rent within the town.

How to Get Around in Portola Valley

The town of Portola Valley is small enough to explore on foot or by bike. A rental car company is located in town for those wishing to explore the surrounding regions.

Where to Go Next from Portola Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many great places to visit within an hour’s drive from Portola Valley including Stanford University and downtown Palo Alto in the neighboring town of Los Altos Hills or Half Moon Bay located on the Pacific coast, with its breathtaking beaches and picture-perfect cliffs.

Conclusion

Portola Valley offers an escape to natural beauty and a small-town feel in the bustling Bay Area. With plenty of activities, sightseeing and dining options to choose from, Portola Valley is the perfect weekend getaway destination for travelers.
