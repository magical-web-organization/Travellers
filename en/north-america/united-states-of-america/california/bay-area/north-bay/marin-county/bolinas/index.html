Introduction to Bolinas

Bolinas is a hidden gem tucked away in Marin County’s unspoiled beauty. Just a 30-minute drive from San Francisco, it’s an ideal destination to enjoy breathtaking natural sceneries, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.

This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive tour of Bolinas, including recommendations on where to stay, what to eat, and what to do while there. Whether you’re a solo adventurer or traveling with family and friends, Bolinas has everything to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bolinas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bolinas Museum - showcases the town’s rich history, culture, and art. It’s open Friday to Monday, and admission is free.

Natural Attractions of Bolinas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bolinas Beach - a tranquil beach famous for its wildlife, tide pools, and surf breaks.

  • Point Reyes National Seashore - a protected coastal area that boasts stunning natural beauty, diverse flora and fauna, and recreational activities like hiking, camping, and bird watching.

  • Mount Tamalpais State Park - a 6,300-acre park with breathtaking views, scenic trails, and outdoor activities like mountain biking, horseback riding, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bolinas

Bolinas offers some of the best hikes and biking trails in California, where you can explore the town’s stunning natural beauty.

  • Coastal Trail - a 10-mile trail with stunning views of the ocean, beaches, and cliffs.

  • Palomarin Trailhead - a 13.5-mile trail that takes you through dense forests, streams, and stunning views of the Pacific.

Best Things to Do in Bolinas: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking - explore Bolinas Bay by kayak and enjoy breathtaking views of the coast and the wildlife.

  • Fishing - Bolinas is a famous spot for fishing enthusiasts, where you can catch salmon, rockfish, halibut, and other species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bolinas

If you have a week to spend in Bolinas, here’s our suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Bolinas town and visit Bolinas Museum
Day 2 Kayaking in Bolinas Bay and beach picnic at Bolinas Beach
Day 3 Hike Palomarin Trailhead and enjoy the scenic views
Day 4 Visit Point Reyes National Seashore and explore the lighthouse
Day 5 Relax at Mount Tamalpais State Park and enjoy outdoor activities
Day 6 Fishing trip in Bolinas Bay and nature walks around the town
Day 7 Visit nearby towns like Stinson Beach and Sausalito

Food and Drink Scene of Bolinas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bolinas Smoked Fish - made from locally caught fish, you can enjoy it at local eateries, or you can buy it from the Bolinas Fish Co-Op.

  • Bovine Bakery - known for its freshly baked pastries and bread, this bakery is a must-visit spot for breakfast or lunch.

Local Beverages

  • Bolinas Beer - a locally brewed beer made from locally sourced ingredients, you can enjoy it at the Coast Cafe.

Where to Stay in Bolinas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • HI Point Reyes Hostel - located in the Point Reyes National Seashore, this hostel offers cozy rooms, scenic views, and a friendly atmosphere.

  • Olema House - a charming and peaceful hotel located in Olema town, just a short drive from Bolinas.

What to Buy in Bolinas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bolinas Smoked Fish - a famous local product that makes a great souvenir or gift.

  • Local Art - Bolinas is home to many artists, and you can find quirky and unique artwork in the local galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Bolinas

Bolinas is a laid-back and unconventional town, known for its free spirit and alternative lifestyle. It’s essential to respect the town’s culture and customs, which include respecting nature, supporting local businesses, and conserving resources.

Best Time to Visit Bolinas

The best time to visit Bolinas is from May to October, when the weather is warm and dry. However, you can visit Bolinas at any time of the year, and you’ll find something unique to enjoy during each season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bolinas

Do:

  • Respect nature and always leave no trace.

  • Support local businesses and farmers.

Don’t:

  • Visit the town without respecting its culture and customs.

  • Take photos of locals or their property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bolinas

Bolinas is a safe town, but it’s essential to take precautions like locking your car and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Bolinas

Bolinas has limited medical facilities, and it’s advisable to carry any necessary medications with you. It’s also essential to take precautions like using insect repellent as there are mosquitoes in the area.

Getting to Bolinas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bolinas

  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO) - 30 miles away

  • Oakland International Airport (OAK) - 40 miles away

Bus Stations Near Bolinas

  • Golden Gate Transit - operates buses that connect Bolinas to San Francisco and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Car rentals - you can rent a car from San Francisco or Oakland International Airports, or from nearby towns like Mill Valley or Sausalito.

How to Get Around in Bolinas

Bolinas is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot or bicycle. You can also rent a car or take a taxi if you want to explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Bolinas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stinson Beach - a scenic beach town famous for its surfing and art galleries.

  • Muir Woods National Monument - a protected area that features old-growth redwood trees and scenic hiking trails.

Conclusion

Bolinas is an ideal destination for those seeking tranquility and natural beauty.
