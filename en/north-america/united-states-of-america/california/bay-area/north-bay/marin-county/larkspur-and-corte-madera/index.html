Introduction to Larkspur and Corte Madera

Nestled in Marin County in the North Bay region of the Bay Area, Larkspur and Corte Madera offer a serene escape from the bustling city life of San Francisco. With a population of just over 10,000 residents, Larkspur is known for its charming, small-town vibe, while Corte Madera boasts stunning views of Mount Tamalpais and the San Francisco Bay. The two towns are connected by the beautiful Larkspur-Corte Madera paved bike path.

List of Places to Visit in Larkspur and Corte Madera

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Marin Museum of Contemporary Art, located in Novato, just a short drive from Larkspur and Corte Madera, is a must-see for art lovers. The museum exhibits works from contemporary artists across a range of mediums.

The San Francisco Bay Model, located in Sausalito, is a fascinating museum that showcases a working hydraulic model of the San Francisco Bay and Delta. Visitors can see how tides, winds, and other environmental factors affect the Bay’s waters.

Natural Attractions of Larkspur and Corte Madera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Tamalpais State Park is a natural wonder that offers countless outdoor activities. Visitors can hike the trails that range in difficulty and length, bike along scenic paths, or take in the breathtaking views of the Bay Area.

The Paradise Beach County Park is perfect for a family day trip. The park has a beach with calm waters for swimming and kayaking, as well as BBQ grills for cooking. The park also offers stunning views of the Bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Larkspur and Corte Madera

The Larkspur-Corte Madera Bike Path is a popular route that connects the two towns. The paved path offers stunning views of the Bay, and riders can enjoy the fresh sea breeze. The trail is flat and perfect for all skill levels.

The Baltimore Canyon Open Space Preserve, located in Larkspur, offers a wide range of hiking trails. The park has easy to strenuous hikes that showcase the natural landscape of the area.

Best Things to Do in Larkspur and Corte Madera: Activities and Tours

The Lark Theater, located in downtown Larkspur, is a well-known landmark. The theater shows independent films and has live performances from time to time.

For a unique experience, visitors can take a ferry from Larkspur to San Francisco. The ferry departs from the Larkspur Landing Ferry Terminal, and the ride offers stunning views of the Bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Larkspur and Corte Madera

Day 1: Start with a morning hike in the Baltimore Canyon Open Space Preserve. In the afternoon, visit the Marin Museum of Contemporary Art.

Day 2: Take a scenic bike ride along the Larkspur-Corte Madera Bike Path in the morning and visit the San Francisco Bay Model museum in the afternoon.

Day 3: Spend the day at Mount Tamalpais State Park and enjoy the hiking trails and scenic views.

Day 4: Visit the Paradise Beach County Park in the morning, then head to downtown Larkspur for lunch. In the afternoon, catch a movie at the Lark Theater.

Day 5: Take a ferry to San Francisco and explore the city’s many attractions.

Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day at the beach at Stinson Beach, just a short drive from Larkspur and Corte Madera.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the Point Reyes National Seashore, where you can hike the breathtaking trails or visit the lighthouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Larkspur and Corte Madera

Must-Try Dishes

The Lighthouse Bar & Grill, located in Corte Madera, offers classic American cuisine with an emphasis on fresh seafood. Visitors can’t go wrong with the restaurant’s clam chowder and fish and chips.

The Left Bank Brasserie, located in Larkspur, is a French restaurant that serves classic dishes like escargot and steak frites. The restaurant also has an extensive wine list.

Local Beverages

The Marin Brewing Company, located in Larkspur, is a must-visit for beer lovers. The brewery produces a variety of delicious beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

For a unique beverage experience, visitors can stop by the M.H. Bread and Butter, located in San Anselmo, just a short drive from Larkspur and Corte Madera. The bakery offers a wide range of artisanal bread and coffee.

Where to Stay in Larkspur and Corte Madera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Marin Suites Hotel, located in Corte Madera, offers spacious suites with fully equipped kitchens. The hotel also has an outdoor pool and hot tub.

The Courtyard by Marriott, located in Larkspur, offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities like free Wi-Fi and a fitness center. The hotel is located in a convenient location near downtown Larkspur.

What to Buy in Larkspur and Corte Madera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Marin County Mart, located in Larkspur, is a must-visit for shopping enthusiasts. The market has a variety of shops that sell local products and souvenirs.

The Mill Valley Market, located in Mill Valley, just a short drive from Larkspur and Corte Madera, offers a wide range of locally sourced products, including artisanal cheeses and meats.

Local Culture and Customs of Larkspur and Corte Madera

Larkspur and Corte Madera have a relaxed and welcoming culture. Visitors should be courteous and respectful of locals while exploring the town.

Best Time to Visit Larkspur and Corte Madera

The best time to visit Larkspur and Corte Madera is from May to October when the weather is mild and there is less rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Larkspur and Corte Madera

Do:

  • Do take a bike ride along the Larkspur-Corte Madera Bike Path.
  • Do visit the Marin Museum of Contemporary Art
  • Do take a ferry to San Francisco

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and water while exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Larkspur and Corte Madera

Larkspur and Corte Madera are generally safe towns. Visitors should stay aware of their surroundings while exploring the area.

Health in Larkspur and Corte Madera

Visitors should bring any necessary medication with them and have proper protection from the sun while enjoying outdoor activities.

Getting to Larkspur and Corte Madera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Larkspur and Corte Madera

The closest airport to Larkspur and Corte Madera is San Francisco International Airport, located approximately 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Larkspur and Corte Madera

The Golden Gate Transit has a bus station in Larkspur and Corte Madera.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Larkspur and Corte Madera by car.

How to Get Around in Larkspur and Corte Madera

The best way to get around Larkspur and Corte Madera is by car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Larkspur and Corte Madera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sausalito

Sausalito is just a short drive from Larkspur and Corte Madera and offers stunning views of the Bay. The town also has a lively art scene and excellent restaurants.

Napa Valley

Napa Valley, known for its world-famous wineries and vineyards, is just an hour’s drive from Larkspur and Corte Madera.

Conclusion

Larkspur and Corte Madera offer a serene escape from the bustling city life of San Francisco. Visitors can explore the town’s natural wonders, enjoy delicious local cuisine, and take in the laid-back culture of the area. From hiking and biking to art and culture, Larkspur and Corte Madera have something for everyone.
