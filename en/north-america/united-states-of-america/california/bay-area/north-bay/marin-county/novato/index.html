Introduction to Novato

Novato, a charming city in Marin County, California, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Surrounded by rolling hills, oak trees, and vineyards, Novato offers visitors a tranquil retreat from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. The city is known for its rich history, beautiful parks, and vibrant downtown area. Novato also serves as a gateway to some of Northern California’s iconic attractions, such as the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and Muir Woods National Monument. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Novato has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Novato

  • Historic Downtown Novato - Explore the charming downtown area lined with boutiques, antique shops, and restaurants. Don’t miss the Novato History Museum, which showcases the city’s past.

  • Hamilton Field History Museum - Learn about the rich history of Hamilton Air Force Base, which played a critical role in World War II and the Korean War.

  • Olompali State Historic Park - This park features a historic adobe house, gardens, and miles of hiking trails.

  • Mount Burdell Open Space Preserve - One of the best places in Novato for hiking and mountain biking, Mount Burdell offers stunning views of the Bay Area.

  • Indian Valley Golf Course - Golfers will love this scenic, 18-hole course with challenging greens and beautiful views.

  • Pacheco Ranch Winery - Visit this family-owned winery to taste their award-winning wines and learn about the winemaking process.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Novato is a city with a rich history, and there are many cultural attractions to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Novato History Museum - Located in downtown Novato, this museum showcases the city’s history through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.

  • Hamilton Field History Museum - Learn about the military history of Hamilton Air Force Base, which was an important training center during World War II and the Korean War.

  • Marin Museum of Contemporary Art - Art lovers will appreciate this museum’s ever-changing exhibits featuring works by local and international artists.

  • Olompali State Historic Park - Discover the history of Native American tribes who lived in this area for thousands of years, as well as the history of the area’s early Spanish settlers.

Natural Attractions of Novato: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Novato is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, offering visitors plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top places to explore:

  • Mount Burdell Open Space Preserve - This park features miles of hiking and mountain biking trails, offering stunning views of the Bay Area.

  • Stafford Lake County Park - A popular spot for picnicking and fishing, Stafford Lake offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills.

  • Olompali State Historic Park - In addition to its cultural attractions, Olompali has miles of trails for hiking and horseback riding, as well as a campground for overnight stays.

  • Indian Valley Open Space Preserve - This preserve features miles of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding, through sweeping valley vistas and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novato

Novato is an excellent destination for hikers and bikers of all levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Mount Burdell Open Space Preserve - This park features over 10 miles of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging.

  • Stafford Lake County Park - This park features a 4.5-mile loop trail around the lake, perfect for hiking and biking.

  • Indian Valley Open Space Preserve - This preserve offers over 15 miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails, with beautiful mountain vistas.

  • Burdell Mountain - This is a favorite of hikers, who appreciate the sweeping views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Novato: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy in Novato. Here are some of the best:

  • Wine Tasting Tours - Novato is surrounded by vineyards, and there are plenty of wineries to explore. Take a wine tasting tour to sample some of the region’s best wines.

  • Wildcare Animal Rescue Center - This center offers tours and educational programs to teach visitors about local wildlife and conservation efforts.

  • Marin French Cheese Company - A must-visit for cheese lovers, Marin French Cheese Company offers tours and tastings of their award-winning cheeses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Novato

Day 1 - Explore downtown Novato and visit the Novato History Museum Day 2 - Visit Olompali State Historic Park and hike the trails Day 3 - Take a wine tasting tour of local vineyards Day 4 - Hike Mount Burdell Open Space Preserve Day 5 - Visit the Hamilton Field History Museum and learn about the military history of the area Day 6 - Take a tour of the Marin French Cheese Company and taste some of their cheeses Day 7 - Enjoy outdoor activities at Stafford Lake County Park

Food and Drink Scene of Novato

Novato offers plenty of delicious dining options and local specialties to try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Marin French Brie - This creamy, award-winning cheese is a local favorite.

  • Grilled Oysters - The nearby Tomales Bay produces some of the finest oysters in the world, which are grilled to perfection in many local restaurants.

  • Hog Island Clams - Another local favorite, these clams are steamed to perfection and served with butter and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - Novato is surrounded by vineyards, and there are plenty of wineries to explore. Try some of the region’s best wines on a wine tasting tour.

  • IPA Beer - Local breweries offer some of the best IPAs around.

What to Buy in Novato: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Novato offers a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home as a reminder of your visit. Here are some of the best:

  • Marin French Cheese - Bring home some of the region’s award-winning cheeses to share with friends and family.

  • Local Wines - Novato is surrounded by vineyards, and local wineries offer some of the best wines in the region.

  • Handmade Crafts - Check out local boutiques and shops for handmade crafts and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Novato

Novato’s culture is heavily influenced by its history and natural surroundings. Respect for the environment and a love for outdoor activities are central values in the community. Be sure to take in the local art scene, try some of the local specialties, and respect the natural surroundings while exploring the area.

Best Time to Visit Novato

Novato has a Mediterranean climate, with mild temperatures year-round. However, the best time to visit is during the spring and fall, when temperatures are comfortable and crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novato

Do:

  • Explore the beautiful outdoor spaces and parks in the area

  • Take a wine tasting tour of local vineyards

  • Shop at local boutiques and markets for unique souvenirs

Don’t:

  • Ignore the environment - be sure to respect the natural surroundings on your hikes and exploration of the area

  • Speeding - the community values safety on the local roads and drivers should accordingly behave to avoid accidents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Novato

Novato is generally a safe place to visit. Visitors should take standard precautions when exploring the area, such as watching for traffic, being aware of surroundings, and storing valuables safely.

Health in Novato

Visitors should drink plenty of water and use sunscreen to protect against the sun’s rays.

Getting to Novato: Transportation Options and Logistics

Novato is easily accessible by car, plane or train. Here are some of the best transportation options:

Airports Near Novato

  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO) - 33 miles south of Novato

  • Oakland International Airport (OAK) - 40 miles south of Novato

Bus Stations Near Novato

  • Novato Hamilton/San Marin Bus Stop

  • Novato Vintage Oaks Shopping Center Bus Stop

Other Options

  • Local buses, trolleys, and shuttles

  • Car rental options, with on-site airport location

How to Get Around in Novato

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Novato, and there are plenty of rental options in the city. Novato also has a public bus system, which is affordable and convenient.

Where to Go Next from Novato: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Novato is a great starting point for exploring the Bay Area. Here are some nearby destinations worth a visit:

  • San Francisco - Just a short drive south, San Francisco offers a wealth of attractions, including the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz Island.

  • Muir Woods National Monument - Just a short drive west, this beautiful park features towering, ancient redwood trees.

  • Sonoma and Napa Valleys - A short drive north, these valleys offer some of the best wine tasting and vineyard tours in the region.

Conclusion

Novato offers visitors a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in exploring its cultural attractions, hiking and biking trails, trying local specialties, or simply relaxing in a charming small-town atmosphere, Novato has something for everyone. Plan your visit now and discover the wonders of this hidden gem in the Bay Area.
