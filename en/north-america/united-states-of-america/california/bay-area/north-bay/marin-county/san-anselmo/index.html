Introduction to San Anselmo

San Anselmo is a charming town nestled in Marin County, North Bay, in the Bay Area of California, United States of America. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture, San Anselmo is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the heart of Northern California.

List of Places to Visit in San Anselmo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Anselmo is steeped in history, and there are many museums and historical sites to explore in the town. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • The San Anselmo Historical Museum: housed in a beautifully restored train depot, this museum tells the story of San Anselmo’s past through fascinating exhibits and artifacts.
  • The Ross Valley Players: the oldest continuously operating community theatre on the West Coast, the Ross Valley Players have been entertaining audiences for over 90 years.
  • The San Francisco Theological Seminary: established in 1871, this historic seminary is a beautiful example of Victorian architecture and offers tours to visitors.

Natural Attractions of San Anselmo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, San Anselmo has plenty to offer. Some of the best natural attractions in the town include:

  • The Phoenix Lake Trail: this 2.7-mile trail offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is a popular destination for hiking and biking.
  • The San Anselmo Creek: this picturesque creek runs through the heart of the town and is a favorite spot for picnics and leisurely walks.
  • The Mount Tamalpais State Park: located just a short drive from San Anselmo, this park is home to some of the most breathtaking scenery in California and offers hiking, camping, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Anselmo

San Anselmo is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are many beautiful hiking and biking routes to explore in the town. Some of the best trails include:

  • The Crown Road Trail: this 2.5-mile trail takes hikers and bikers on a journey through San Anselmo’s scenic hills.
  • The Sleepy Hollow Trail: this 2.4-mile trail offers stunning views of Mount Tamalpais and is a popular destination for hikers and joggers.
  • The Old Railroad Grade Trail: this 3.2-mile trail follows the path of a historic railroad and offers a glimpse into San Anselmo’s past.

Best Things to Do in San Anselmo: Activities and Tours

San Anselmo has something for everyone, whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie. Some of the best things to do in San Anselmo include:

  • Take a self-guided historical walking tour of the town.
  • Visit the San Anselmo Farmers Market and sample some of the best local produce.
  • Explore the local art scene by attending a gallery opening or art walk.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Anselmo

San Anselmo is a great destination for a week-long trip. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your stay:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of San Anselmo by taking a self-guided walking tour and visiting the San Anselmo Historical Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a hike on one of San Anselmo’s many trails, such as the Phoenix Lake Trail or the Crown Road Trail.
  • Day 3: Visit the nearby Mount Tamalpais State Park and spend the day hiking, camping, or picnicking.
  • Day 4: Explore the local food scene by visiting the San Anselmo Farmers Market and dining in one of the town’s many restaurants.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby attractions such as Muir Woods National Monument or the Golden Gate National Recreation Area.
  • Day 6: Learn about San Anselmo’s vibrant arts scene by attending a gallery opening or art walk.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind by visiting one of San Anselmo’s many spas or enjoying a leisurely day in the town’s parks and gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of San Anselmo

Must-Try Dishes

San Anselmo is home to a diverse range of restaurants and cafes, offering everything from farm-to-table cuisine to international fare. Some of the must-try dishes in San Anselmo include:

  • The “Butter Burger” at the Comforts Cafe: this juicy burger is made with grass-fed beef and topped with a generous slab of butter - need we say more?
  • The local oysters at L’Appart Resto: these fresh oysters are served on the half-shell and are a true taste of the Bay Area’s seafood scene.
  • The homemade pasta at Insalata’s: this cozy Italian restaurant serves up some of the best pasta in San Anselmo, made with locally-sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

San Anselmo has a thriving coffee scene, with many local cafes serving up artisanal brews. Some of the best coffee shops in San Anselmo include:

  • Marinita’s Cafe: this cozy cafe serves up delicious coffee, pastries, and sandwiches in a relaxed setting.
  • Red Whale Coffee: offering a variety of specialty brews, Red Whale is a must-visit destination for coffee connoisseurs.
  • M.H. Bread and Butter: known for its artisanal bread and pastries, M.H. Bread and Butter also serves up excellent coffee.

Where to Stay in San Anselmo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Anselmo has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Some of the best places to stay in San Anselmo include:

  • The San Anselmo Inn: located in the heart of town, the San Anselmo Inn is a charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • The Mill Valley Inn: while not located in San Anselmo proper, this cozy inn is just a short drive away and offers easy access to some of the town’s best attractions.
  • The HI Marin Headlands Hostel: if you’re looking for budget-friendly accommodation, the HI Marin Headlands Hostel offers comfortable dorm rooms and private suites in a stunning setting.

What to Buy in San Anselmo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Anselmo is a great place to pick up some unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the best things to buy in San Anselmo include:

  • Locally-made products from the San Anselmo Farmers Market, such as honey, jams, and artisanal cheeses.
  • Handcrafted jewelry and clothing from the town’s many boutiques and shops.
  • Artwork by local artists, available at the town’s many galleries and art shows.

Local Culture and Customs of San Anselmo

San Anselmo is known for its laid-back culture and friendly locals. Visitors to the town should be aware of some local customs, such as:

  • Respect for nature: San Anselmo is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, and visitors should take care to preserve these spaces by following the “Leave No Trace” principles.
  • Community involvement: San Anselmo is a close-knit community, and visitors will often see locals involved in town events and activities. Visitors are encouraged to join in the fun and make new connections with the community.
  • Wellness and mindfulness: San Anselmo is home to many spas, yoga studios, and wellness centers, reflecting the town’s emphasis on mindful living and self-care.

Best Time to Visit San Anselmo

San Anselmo is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities. Spring and fall offer cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, making them ideal for outdoor enthusiasts. Winter is a great time to explore San Anselmo’s cozy cafes and galleries, as well as nearby ski resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Anselmo

Do:

  • Take time to explore the town on foot or by bike.
  • Sample the local cuisine, including farm-fresh produce and artisanal cheeses.
  • Take advantage of San Anselmo’s world-class hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the town’s natural landscapes and historical sites.
  • Be afraid to strike up a conversation with friendly locals.
  • Forget to take some time to relax and unwind in one of San Anselmo’s many spas and wellness centers.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Anselmo

San Anselmo is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings while walking at night.

Health in San Anselmo

San Anselmo has several medical clinics and pharmacies, as well as a hospital located just a short drive away in nearby Fairfax. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and bringing enough water.

Getting to San Anselmo: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Anselmo is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near San Anselmo

The closest major airport to San Anselmo is San Francisco International Airport, located approximately 30 miles away. Oakland International Airport and San Jose International Airport are also within easy driving distance.

Bus Stations Near San Anselmo

San Anselmo is served by several bus lines, including the Golden Gate Transit and Marin Transit.

Other Options

For those traveling without a car, taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft are widely available in the area.

How to Get Around in San Anselmo

San Anselmo is a pedestrian-friendly town, and many of the town’s best attractions can be reached on foot or by bike. For those looking to explore further afield, rental cars and taxis are widely available.

Where to Go Next from San Anselmo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Anselmo is located in the heart of Northern California and is a great base for exploring nearby attractions such as:

  • Muir Woods National Monument: just a short drive from San Anselmo, Muir Woods is home to some of the most majestic redwood trees in the world.
  • The Golden Gate National Recreation Area: this stunning park is home to some of the most iconic landmarks in California, including the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz Island.
  • The wine country of Napa and Sonoma: located just a short drive north of San Anselmo, Napa and Sonoma are home to some of the best wineries and vineyards in the world.

Conclusion

San Anselmo is a hidden gem in the heart of Northern California, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s charming streets, hike through its stunning hills, or simply relax and unwind, San Anselmo has something for everyone.
