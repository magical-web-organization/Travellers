Introduction to Stinson Beach

Stinson Beach is a small, picturesque community located in Marin County, North Bay, California. It is known for its pristine beaches, scenic hiking trails, and relaxed atmosphere. The beach town’s proximity to San Francisco makes it a popular destination for weekend getaways and day trips.

Whether you’re looking for a peaceful escape or an adventure-filled vacation, Stinson Beach has something to offer. In this travel guide, we have compiled a list of the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Stinson Beach.

List of Places to Visit in Stinson Beach

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stinson Beach has several museums and historical places that reflect the town’s rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Bolinas Museum: A local museum that showcases the history and culture of the Bolinas-Stinson area.

  • Stinson Beach Historical Society: A non-profit organization that preserves and promotes the history of Stinson Beach.

  • Mt. Tamalpais State Park: A state park that offers breathtaking views of the Bay Area and is home to several historical landmarks.

Natural Attractions of Stinson Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stinson Beach is famous for its outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Stinson Beach: A mile-long beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing.

  • Muir Woods National Monument: A redwood forest that is home to some of the tallest trees in the world.

  • Mount Tamalpais State Park: A state park that offers hiking, biking, and camping opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stinson Beach

Stinson Beach has several hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking views of the California coast. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Dipsea Trail: A challenging 7.4-mile trail that runs from Mill Valley to Stinson Beach.

  • Steep Ravine Trail: A scenic 3.5-mile trail that runs through a redwood forest and ends at Stinson Beach.

  • Matt Davis Trail: A moderate 4.8-mile trail that runs through a grassland and ends at Stinson Beach.

Best Things to Do in Stinson Beach: Activities and Tours

Stinson Beach offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests and age groups. Here are some of the best things to do in Stinson Beach:

  • Surfing and boogie boarding: Stinson Beach is known for its excellent waves, which are ideal for beginner and intermediate surfers.

  • Kayaking and canoeing: The calm waters of Bolinas Lagoon are perfect for kayaking and canoeing.

  • Whale watching: Stinson Beach is a great place to spot gray whales during their annual migration.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stinson Beach

If you’re planning to spend a week in Stinson Beach, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Stinson Beach and spend the day relaxing on the beach.

Day 2: Visit Muir Woods National Monument and hike one of the trails.

Day 3: Take a surfing lesson or rent a kayak at Stinson Beach.

Day 4: Explore the town of Bolinas and visit the Bolinas Museum.

Day 5: Go whale watching or take a sunset sailboat tour.

Day 6: Hike the Dipsea Trail or bike the Coastal Trail.

Day 7: Relax on the beach, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy a local meal.

Food and Drink Scene of Stinson Beach

Stinson Beach has several restaurants, cafes, and bars that offer a variety of cuisine and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish tacos: Stinson Beach is known for its fresh seafood, and fish tacos are a popular dish among locals and visitors.

  • Crab cakes: Made with local crab meat, crab cakes are a savory treat that is perfect for a light lunch or dinner.

  • Oysters: The oysters from nearby Tomales Bay are some of the best in the world and are a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Napa Valley and Sonoma Valley are a short drive away from Stinson Beach and are known for their world-class wine.

  • Beer: Stinson Beach has several breweries that offer a variety of craft beer.

Where to Stay in Stinson Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stinson Beach has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Sandpiper Lodging at the Beach: A pet-friendly hotel located across the street from Stinson Beach.

  • Stinson Beach Motel: A boutique hotel that offers ocean views and is located next to Stinson Beach.

  • HI Point Reyes Hostel: A hostel located in nearby Point Reyes that offers affordable accommodation and is ideal for backpackers.

What to Buy in Stinson Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Stinson Beach has several shops that sell souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Stinson Beach:

  • Local art: Stinson Beach has a vibrant art community, and there are several galleries that sell local art.

  • Sea glass jewelry: Stinson Beach is known for its sea glass, and several shops sell jewelry made from recycled sea glass.

  • Local honey: Marin County is home to several honey producers, and local honey is a popular souvenir among visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Stinson Beach

Stinson Beach has a laid-back culture that is reflected in its relaxed atmosphere and friendly locals. Here are some of the customs and traditions of Stinson Beach:

  • Respect the environment: Stinson Beach is located in a sensitive ecosystem, and visitors are encouraged to respect the environment by not littering or disturbing wildlife.

  • Embrace the outdoors: Stinson Beach is known for its outdoor adventures, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the outdoors by hiking, biking, and surfing.

Best Time to Visit Stinson Beach

The best time to visit Stinson Beach is from April to October when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Stinson Beach is a popular destination year-round, and each season offers something unique.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stinson Beach

Do:

  • Do visit Muir Woods National Monument and hike one of the trails.

  • Do try the local seafood and wine.

  • Do embrace the outdoors by hiking, biking, or surfing.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb wildlife.

  • Don’t bring glass bottles or containers to the beach.

  • Don’t swim alone or without a lifeguard on duty.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stinson Beach

Stinson Beach is a safe community, but visitors should still take precautions to stay safe. Here are some safety tips:

  • Follow beach rules and swim only in designated areas.

  • Hike and bike with a partner and tell someone your route.

  • Lock your car and avoid leaving valuables in it.

Health in Stinson Beach

Stinson Beach has a small medical clinic that offers primary care services. For emergencies, visitors should seek medical attention at a hospital in nearby Marin County.

Getting to Stinson Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Stinson Beach is located approximately 20 miles from San Francisco, and there are several transportation options to get there.

Airports Near Stinson Beach

  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO)

  • Oakland International Airport (OAK)

  • Sonoma County Airport (STS)

Bus Stations Near Stinson Beach

  • Golden Gate Transit

  • Marin Transit

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in San Francisco, and visitors can rent a car to drive to Stinson Beach.

  • Private transportation: There are several private transportation companies that offer shuttle and taxi services to Stinson Beach.

How to Get Around in Stinson Beach

The best way to get around Stinson Beach is by foot, bike, or car. There is limited public transportation in Stinson Beach, and most visitors prefer to explore the town on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Stinson Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stinson Beach is located near several other popular destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Bolinas: A small, charming town that is known for its natural beauty and local attractions.

  • Point Reyes National Seashore: A pristine natural area that offers hiking, biking, and wildlife watching opportunities.

  • San Francisco: A vibrant city that is known for its diverse culture, world-class cuisine, and iconic landmarks.

Conclusion

Stinson Beach is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful escape or an adventure-filled vacation, Stinson Beach is the perfect destination. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Stinson Beach.
