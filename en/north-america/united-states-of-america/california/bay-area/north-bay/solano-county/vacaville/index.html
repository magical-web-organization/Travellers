Introduction to Vacaville

Vacaville is a city in Solano County, California, located in the North Bay area of the state. The city is known for its scenic landscapes, outdoor recreation opportunities, historic downtown, and vibrant food scene. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Vacaville, with information on where to go, what to see, and what to do during your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Vacaville

Located halfway between San Francisco and Sacramento, Vacaville offers a variety of things to see and do. Here are some of the best places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vacaville Museum: This local history museum is housed in a Victorian mansion and showcases the cultural heritage of Vacaville and Solano County.

  • Budweiser Brewery Experience: Take a tour of the Budweiser brewing facility and taste some of their famous beers.

  • Lagoon Valley Park: This park is home to a historic covered bridge and has a variety of walking and biking trails.

Natural Attractions of Vacaville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pena Adobe and Lagoon Valley Park: This park is home to the historic Pena Adobe, a two-story adobe structure built in 1842, and has hiking trails, picnic areas, and fishing opportunities.

  • Napa Valley Wine Country: Just a short drive from Vacaville, Wine Country is home to some of the best vineyards and wineries in the country.

  • Vacaville Premium Outlets: Shop at over 120 brand name stores, including Nike and Coach, at this popular outlet mall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vacaville

Vacaville has a variety of hiking and biking trails, including the Lagoon Valley/ Pena Adobe Regional Park Trail, a 2700-acre park with five multi-use trails that offer a variety of scenic views and a number of activities like bird watching, horseback riding, and fishing. For those who prefer biking, the Putah Creek bike trail is a popular route.

Best Things to Do in Vacaville: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to try during your visit:

  • Safari West: Take a guided safari tour of an African wildlife preserve located in Sonoma County, just a short drive from Vacaville.

  • Six Flags Discovery Kingdom: This amusement park has a variety of thrill rides, including roller coasters and water rides, and also has a wildlife exhibit.

  • Putah Creek Fishing: With over 20 miles of fishable water and a variety of fish, including trout, bass, and catfish, Putah Creek is a popular spot for anglers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vacaville

Day 1: Explore downtown Vacaville and visit the local shops, followed by dinner at one of the city’s many restaurants.

Day 2: Visit the Vacaville Museum and take a tour of the historic Pena Adobe. End the day with a walk through Pena Adobe Park.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Napa Valley and explore the vineyards and wineries.

Day 4: Visit the Budweiser Brewery Experience and learn about the beer-making process.

Day 5: Spend the day at Lagoon Valley Park, hiking on one of the park’s many trails, boating, or fishing.

Day 6: Take a safari tour at Safari West or visit the Six Flags Discovery Kingdom amusement park.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the Vacaville Premium Outlets and enjoy a final meal at one of Vacaville’s best restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Vacaville

Vacaville’s food scene is diverse and has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgers at Dandy’s Drive-In: Dandy’s has been serving up burgers and shakes since 1938.

  • Gourmet Pizza at Pieology: This popular pizza chain has a location in Vacaville and allows customers to create their own pizzas.

  • Mexican at Taqueria El Favorito: This taqueria serves up authentic Mexican cuisine, including street tacos and burritos.

Local Beverages

  • Mankas Beer Works: This brewery offers a variety of hand-crafted beers, including IPAs, stouts, and seasonal brews.

  • Green River Brewing and Taproom: This local brewery serves up a variety of craft beers and local wines.

Where to Stay in Vacaville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vacaville has a variety of lodging options, including hotels, motels, and bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Vacaville: Located near the Premium Outlets, this hotel offers spacious rooms and suites and complimentary breakfast.

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites Vacaville Napa Valley: This hotel is located in the heart of Vacaville and offers free Wi-Fi and breakfast.

  • The White House: This Victorian-style bed and breakfast is located in downtown Vacaville and offers rooms with antique furnishings.

What to Buy in Vacaville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some souvenirs and local products to consider buying in Vacaville include:

  • Olive Oil: Vacaville is located in the heart of California’s olive growing region and is home to a number of olive oil producers.

  • Chocolates at Posh Chocolat: This boutique chocolate shop specializes in high-quality, handmade chocolates.

  • Farm-Fresh Produce: Visit the local farmers’ market and take home some of the freshest produce and fruits grown right in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Vacaville

Vacaville is known for its agricultural roots, and the city holds numerous festivals and events throughout the year to celebrate the harvest. Spain, Mexico, and other Latin American cultures have also had an influence on the region’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Vacaville

The best time to visit Vacaville is during the fall, from September to November, when the weather is mild and the autumn colors are at their peak. Spring and summer are also good times to visit, but temperatures can be hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vacaville

Do:

  • Visit the local shops and restaurants
  • Explore the natural attractions like Lagoon Valley Park and Putah Creek
  • Take a tour of the Budweiser Brewery and learn about beer-making

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or natural areas
  • Ignore traffic signs and signals
  • Be loud and disruptive in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Vacaville

Vacaville is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions like keeping valuables locked up and out of sight and being aware of their surroundings in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Vacaville

Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with sun and heat exposure during the summer months. It is also recommended to wear appropriate clothing and footwear when hiking, biking or engaging in other outdoor activities.

Getting to Vacaville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Vacaville is conveniently located near a number of airports, bus stations, and major highways, making it easy to get to by car or plane.

Airports Near Vacaville

  • San Francisco International Airport: Approximately a 90-minute drive from Vacaville.

  • Oakland International Airport: Approximately a 60-minute drive from Vacaville.

  • Sacramento International Airport: Approximately a 50-minute drive from Vacaville.

Bus Stations Near Vacaville

Greyhound Lines operates a bus station in nearby Davis, about a 15-minute drive from Vacaville. The station offers connections to destinations throughout California and the United States.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Vacaville by Amtrak train, which stops in nearby Davis. Alternatively, driving a rental car is another popular option.

How to Get Around in Vacaville

Visitors can get around Vacaville by car, bike, or foot. Rental cars and bikes are available from a number of local companies, or visitors can rely on taxis or ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Vacaville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vacaville is located in the heart of Northern California, offering visitors a variety of nearby destinations to explore. Here are some of the best excursions to take from Vacaville:

  • Napa Valley: Located just a short drive from Vacaville, Napa Valley is home to some of the best wineries and vineyards in the world.

  • San Francisco: Head south to San Francisco and explore the city’s famous landmarks, including the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz Island.

  • Lake Berryessa: This nearby lake offers a variety of water sports, including boating, fishing, and swimming.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural attractions, outdoor recreation opportunities, or food scene, Vacaville has something to offer everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful California city.
