Introduction to Solano County

Nestled in the heart of the Bay Area in California, Solano County boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and an array of unique attractions that make it a must-visit destination. From outdoor adventures to museums, there is no shortage of things to do and see in this region.

List of Places to Visit in Solano County

Here is a comprehensive list of the top attractions and places to visit in Solano County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Travis Air Force Base Heritage Center: This museum showcases the history of Travis Air Force Base and features a vast collection of aircraft, vehicles, and military artifacts.
  • The Jelly Belly Factory: Take a fascinating tour of the Jelly Belly candy factory, learn about the candy-making process, and sample some of the factory’s delicious treats.
  • Benicia Capitol State Historic Park: Built in 1853, this historic park showcases the state’s third capitol building and boasts a fascinating collection of artifacts and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Solano County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Berryessa: This picturesque lake is a popular destination for boating, fishing, camping, and hiking. It boasts stunning views of the nearby hills and mountains.
  • Mount Diablo State Park: This park features an impressive network of hiking and biking trails that offer panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. During clear days, visitors can see the Golden Gate Bridge from the summit.
  • Rush Ranch Open Space: This 2,070-acre nature reserve boasts a diversity of flora and fauna, making it ideal for bird watching, hiking, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Solano County

Solano County is home to a vast network of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience. Some of the best routes include:

  • Skyline Wilderness Park: This park boasts more than 25 miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through rolling hills, valleys, and oak woodlands.
  • Lynch Canyon Open Space: This nature preserve offers a variety of hiking trails that showcase the area’s diverse plant and animal life, giving visitors a chance to observe wildlife such as eagles, hawks, and coyotes.
  • Rockville Hills Regional Park: This park features more than 10 miles of hiking and mountain biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Solano County: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Solano County:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Experience the stunning beauty of Solano County from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Visit local wineries: Solano County is home to a thriving wine industry, and there are dozens of wineries throughout the region that offer tastings and tours.
  • Go shopping: Discover unique shops and boutiques that showcase locally made products, such as fine art, jewelry, and pottery.

Food and Drink Scene of Solano County

Solano County is home to many excellent restaurants, coffee shops, and bars that offer a diverse range of cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Solano County is known for its fresh seafood, so be sure to try local favorites such as clam chowder and fish tacos.
  • The region is also known for its locally sourced produce, so be sure to try dishes made with fresh fruits and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Solano County is home to a thriving wine industry, with vineyards and wineries throughout the region that offer tastings and tours.
  • The area is also known for its craft breweries and distilleries, with local beer, spirits, and cider produced using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Solano County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Solano County, including hotels, motels, bed and breakfasts, and vacation rentals.

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Fairfield and offers comfortable rooms and great service.
  • The Inn at Benicia Bay: This charming bed and breakfast is located in historic downtown Benicia and offers stunning views of the Carquinez Strait.
  • Budget Inn Motel: This affordable motel is centrally located and offers basic amenities for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Solano County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Solano County offers a variety of locally made products that make for great souvenirs or gifts. Some popular options include:

  • Fine art and pottery from local artisans.
  • Locally made wine, beer, and spirits.
  • Farm-fresh produce and foods, such as olive oil and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Solano County

Solano County has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Native American, Mexican, and American cultures. Visitors to the area should respect local customs and traditions, which may include:

  • Participating in local festivals and events, such as the Solano County Fair and the Benicia Waterfront Festival.
  • Supporting local businesses and artisans who contribute to the area’s unique character and charm.

Best Time to Visit Solano County

The best time to visit Solano County is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. However, summer and winter also offer fantastic opportunities for outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Solano County

When visiting Solano County, here are some things to do and avoid:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and events.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.
  • Ignore safety warnings or regulations.
  • Disrespect wildlife or habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Solano County

Solano County is a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding high-crime areas after dark.

Health in Solano County

Visitors to Solano County should drink bottled water or boiled tap water to avoid getting sick. It is also essential to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated during outdoor activities.

Getting to Solano County: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for getting to Solano County, including:

Airports Near Solano County

  • Sacramento International Airport (SMF): Located approximately 60 miles from Solano County.
  • Oakland International Airport (OAK): Located approximately 50 miles from Solano County.
  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO): Located approximately 70 miles from Solano County.

Bus Stations Near Solano County

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Fairfield, Solano County’s largest city.

Other Options

  • Car: Solano County is easily accessible by car, with major highways running through the region.
  • Train: Amtrak offers train service to several cities in Solano County.

How to Get Around in Solano County

The best way to get around Solano County is by car, but there are also bus and train options available.

Where to Go Next from Solano County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Solano County is located in the heart of the Bay Area, with several nearby destinations worth visiting, including:

  • San Francisco: Located approximately 40 miles from Solano County, San Francisco offers world-class attractions, dining, and entertainment.
  • Napa Valley: Located approximately 20 miles from Solano County, Napa Valley is known for its excellent wineries, restaurants, and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Solano County is a must-visit destination for travelers who are seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and unique attractions. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and diverse range of activities and events, this region is sure to impress visitors of all ages and interests.
