St. Helena, California: Your Ultimate Travel Guide

If you’re looking for a relaxing vacation in the heart of California’s wine country, St. Helena is the perfect destination. Located in the scenic Napa Valley, this charming town offers a wealth of attractions, activities, and dining options. Whether you’re a wine enthusiast, history buff, or outdoor adventurer, you’ll find plenty to love in St. Helena.

List of Places to Visit in St. Helena

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Helena is home to a number of fascinating museums and historic sites:

  • Bale Grist Mill State Historic Park: This park features a restored 19th-century water-powered grist mill, as well as hiking trails and picnic areas.
  • Charles Krug Winery: Founded in 1861, this winery is one of the oldest in Napa Valley and offers tours and tastings.
  • Robert Louis Stevenson Museum: Learn about the life and works of the famous author at this small museum in downtown St. Helena.
  • St. Helena Catholic Church: This historic church dates back to 1872 and features beautiful stained glass windows and wooden pews.

Natural Attractions of St. Helena: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of St. Helena and the surrounding Napa Valley make outdoor activities a must-do:

  • Bothe-Napa Valley State Park: Offering over 10 miles of hiking trails, camping sites, and swimming holes, Bothe-Napa is a great place to visit year-round.
  • Culinary Institute of America at Greystone: With beautiful gardens and stunning views of the valley, this campus of the renowned culinary school is worth a visit even if you’re not attending a class.
  • Robert Louis Stevenson State Park: This park includes a small museum dedicated to the famous author, as well as hiking trails that offer views of the valley and nearby Mount St. Helena.
  • St. Helena Farmers Market: Held every Friday, this farmers market offers locally grown produce, fresh baked goods, and artisanal products from the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Helena

If you’re an avid hiker or mountain biker, the trails around St. Helena won’t disappoint. Some of the top routes include:

  • Rainbow Ridge Trail: This moderate 4.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the valley and Lake Hennessey.
  • Oat Hill Mine Trail: A more challenging 8-mile hike, the Oat Hill Mine Trail offers breathtaking views of Calistoga and the Palisades.
  • Linda Falls Trail: This easy 2.6-mile hike takes you to beautiful Linda Falls, which is particularly picturesque in the spring when the wildflowers are in bloom.
  • Horseback Riding: Several local stables offer guided horseback rides through Napa Valley vineyards and hills, for an unforgettable experience.

Best Things to Do in St. Helena: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other things to do in St. Helena:

  • Wine Tasting: With over 400 wineries in the Napa Valley, there is no shortage of tastings and tours to choose from. Some of the most popular wineries in St. Helena include Hall Wines, Prager Winery & Port Works, and Beringer Vineyards.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take in breathtaking views of the Napa Valley from above with a hot air balloon ride. Companies like Napa Valley Balloons and Balloons Above the Valley offer daily flights.
  • Spa Day: Relax and unwind with a trip to one of St. Helena’s many spas, like the Spa at Harvest Inn or the Health Spa Napa Valley.
  • Shopping: From charming boutiques to high-end designer stores, downtown St. Helena has something for every shopper. Check out stores like Molly’s, Slate Interiors, and Napa Valley Vintage Home.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Helena

If you have a week to spend in St. Helena, here is an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive and Relax

Get settled into your accommodations and spend the day exploring downtown St. Helena. Grab lunch at a local restaurant like Cook St. Helena, and check out some of the town’s boutique shops like SHED or Napa Valley Vintage Home.

Day 2: Wine Tasting

Visit a few of St. Helena’s wineries, like Beringer Vineyards or Salvestrin Winery. Alternatively, sign up for a wine tour that includes visits to several vineyards in the area.

Day 3: Outdoor Adventures

Take a hike in Bothe-Napa Valley State Park or try horseback riding with a local stable like Triple Creek Horse Outfit. Cap off the evening with a soak in the hot tub at your hotel or a spa treatment.

Day 4: History and Culture

Visit the Charles Krug Winery and then head to the Robert Louis Stevenson Museum for an afternoon of learning about the famous author. End the day with dinner at one of St. Helena’s top restaurants, like Press or Cindy’s Backstreet Kitchen.

Day 5: Relax and Explore

Spend the day exploring more of the Napa Valley, perhaps taking a scenic drive up the Silverado Trail. Stop at a farm stand or two to pick up fresh produce for a picnic lunch, then head back to St. Helena for some downtime.

Day 6: Balloon Ride

Take a sunrise hot air balloon ride over the vineyards and hills of the Napa Valley with Napa Valley Balloons or Balloons Above the Valley.

Day 7: Departure

Spend your last day in St. Helena with a leisurely breakfast at a local cafe, then hit the road for your next adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Helena

St. Helena is a foodie’s paradise, with a variety of dining options to suit every taste and budget.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine: St. Helena’s restaurants are known for using locally sourced ingredients to create delicious dishes that are seasonal and fresh. Try Farmstead at Long Meadow Ranch, Archetype, or The Charter Oak for great farm-to-table cuisine.
  • Burgers and BBQ: For something a little more casual, check out places like Gott’s Roadside, which serves up gourmet burgers and milkshakes, or The Q Restaurant and Bar, which has a variety of BBQ options.
  • Italian Classics: Don’t miss out on some of the great Italian restaurants in St. Helena, like Tra Vigne, where you can enjoy traditional dishes like pasta carbonara or a wood-fired pizza.

Local Beverages

Of course, no visit to St. Helena would be complete without a glass of local wine or beer. Some of the best places to drink in St. Helena include:

  • Napa Valley Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of beers on tap, as well as wood-fired pizzas and other pub fare.
  • Charbay Distillery & Winery: Check out this family-owned distillery and winery to experience small-batch spirits and unique flavored vodkas.
  • The Restaurant at Meadowood: The wine list at this Michelin-starred restaurant is impressive, with a focus on Napa Valley varietals like Cabernet Sauvignon and Chardonnay.

Where to Stay in St. Helena: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodations in St. Helena to suit every budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: If you’re looking for a luxurious stay, try the Harvest Inn or the Meadowood Napa Valley, which has a Michelin-starred restaurant and a championship golf course.
  • Boutique Hotels: For a more intimate experience, check out the Ink House or the Wydown Hotel.
  • Budget Hotels and Hostels: The Wine Country Inn & Cottages and St. Helena Motel offer more affordable lodging options without sacrificing comfort.

What to Buy in St. Helena: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from St. Helena include:

  • Local Wine: With so many wineries in the area, you’re sure to find a bottle or two (or more!) to take home.
  • Olive Oil: Napa Valley is also known for its olive oil. Check out shops like Round Pond Estate or Olivier Napa Valley for some great options.
  • Handmade Crafts: Downtown St. Helena is home to several shops featuring handmade crafts and artisanal products. Check out Woodhouse Chocolate, for example, for some delicious sweet treats.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Helena

St. Helena is a welcoming and friendly community, where visitors are always treated with respect and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit St. Helena

The best time to visit St. Helena depends on what you’re looking for. The summer months are the busiest and most expensive, but offer the best weather for outdoor activities. Spring and fall are great for wine tasting, as the vineyards are in full bloom during this time. Winter is the quietest time of year in St. Helena, but can be a great time to snag deals on lodging and explore the area without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Helena

Do:

  • Respect the environment: Napa Valley is a beautiful and fragile ecosystem. Be sure to pack out any trash you bring in and always stay on designated trails.
  • Taste local wines and foods: Napa Valley is known for its vineyards and farm-to-table cuisine. Be sure to try some of the local specialties while you’re here.
  • Be friendly: St. Helena is a welcoming and friendly community. Smile, say hello, and strike up a conversation with locals to get a true taste of St. Helena’s charm.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive: Never drink and drive, especially when touring the local wineries.
  • Litter: Do not throw cigarette butts, bottles, or other trash on the streets or in the countryside.
  • Be disruptive: Napa Valley is a peaceful community, and visitors are expected to be respectful of others’ peace and tranquility.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Helena

St. Helena and the surrounding Napa Valley are generally very safe areas. However, visitors should exercise the same caution that they would in any other unfamiliar place.

Health in St. Helena

St. Helena has several medical clinics and hospitals, including the St. Helena Hospital and the Queen of the Valley Medical Center.

Getting to St. Helena: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Helena

The closest airport to St. Helena is Sacramento International Airport (SMF), which is about 90 miles away. San Francisco International Airport (SFO) and Oakland International Airport (OAK) are both about 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near St. Helena

The nearest bus station is in Napa, about 12 miles south of St. Helena. Greyhound and Amtrak both stop there regularly.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can rent a car at any of the nearby airports or in downtown San Francisco and drive to St. Helena.

How to Get Around in St. Helena

The easiest way to get around in St. Helena is by car, although biking and walking are also popular options. Several local companies (like Uber and Lyft) and taxi services operate in the area as well.

Where to Go Next from St. Helena: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of California after your time in St. Helena, some nearby destinations worth considering include:

  • San Francisco: Just a little over an hour’s drive from St. Helena, San Francisco offers a wealth of culture, dining, and shopping options.
  • Lake Tahoe: About two hours east of St. Helena, Lake Tahoe is a popular destination for outdoor activities like skiing, snowboarding, and hiking.
  • Monterey/Carmel: About a three-hour drive south of St. Helena, Monterey and Carmel offer stunning coastal views and a wealth of attractions, including the Monterey Bay Aquarium and Cannery Row.

Conclusion

St. Helena is a charming and picturesque town that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in wine tasting, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a beautiful setting, St. Helena has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to make the most of your time in this delightful California destination.
