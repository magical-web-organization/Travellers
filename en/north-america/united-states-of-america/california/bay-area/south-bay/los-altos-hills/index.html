Introduction to Los Altos Hills

Los Altos Hills is a small, rural town located in the Santa Clara County of the San Francisco Bay Area, California, United States. With a population of just over 8,200, this exclusive community is home to rolling hills, abundant parks and open spaces, meandering roads, and stunning views of the Bay and the Silicon Valley. The town boasts of extensive hiking, biking, and equestrian trails, and a strong commitment to preserving its rural character and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Los Altos Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history enthusiast, then Los Altos Hills has quite a few places for you to explore.

  • Los Altos History Museum: This museum exhibits the history of Los Altos and Los Altos Hills from the time of the Ohlone Indians to the present.
  • Filoli: A magnificent 654-acre property boasting of 16 acres of formal gardens, a Georgian-style Country Estate, and approximately 240 acres of natural open space. The estate was built in 1915 by William Bowers Bourn II, a prominent San Franciscan Statesman. There are guided tours available, and visitors are encouraged to explore the house and the grounds.
  • NASA Ames Research Center: Founded in 1939 on the site of the former Moffett Field Naval Air Station, it is a research center for various fields of science and engineering conducted for aeronautics and space exploration, and houses various facilities such as the NASA Advanced Supercomputing Division (NAS), the Applied Sciences Directorate (ASD), and the Space Biosciences Division (SBD). Visits are not allowed inside the center, but visitors can see the historic Hangar One from the outside.

Natural Attractions of Los Altos Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, be sure to visit some of Los Altos Hills’ beautiful outdoor spaces.

  • Foothills Park: The 1,400-acre park is closed to non-residents on weekends, but a visit on weekdays will allow you to enjoy the lake, trails, and stunning views of the bay and the valley.
  • Purisima Creek Redwoods Preserve: Located on Skyline Boulevard, the preserve offers miles of hiking and biking trails and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna native to the redwood forest.
  • Hidden Villa: This 1600-acre organic farm offers year-round public programs, including hikes, nature exploration, and farm tours.
  • Montebello Open Space Preserve: With over 3,800 acres of pristine open space, the preserve offers miles of hiking and equestrian trails, serenity, and stunning views of both the hills and the Pacific Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Los Altos Hills

Los Altos Hills offers a variety of trails for all levels of outdoor enthusiasts, as well as some challenging routes for seasoned bikers and hikers.

  • Arastradero Preserve: With approximately 10 miles of hiking and biking trails, this preserve offers views of Palo Alto Foothills and the South Bay Area, as well as lake- and pond-side picnicking spots.
  • Rancho San Antonio Preserve: The landscape varies from oak woodland to riparian, to chaparral with a rich array of animal and plant life surrounding 23 miles of hiking and biking trails.
  • The Dish: The 3.7-mile trail explores the Stanford foothills and offers stunning views of the Bay Area and the Stanford campus. It is one of the most popular and challenging hiking and running trails in the Bay Area.

Best Things to Do in Los Altos Hills: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions and hiking trails, Los Altos Hills offers something for everyone.

  • Horseback Riding at Webb Ranch: The ranch provides horseback riding opportunities for all ages and levels of riders in addition to summer camps and lessons.
  • Golf at Los Altos Golf and Country Club: This exclusive country club offers an 18-hole par-71 course designed by renowned course architect William P. Bell and known for its beautiful views of the Bay Area.
  • San Francisco Bay Sailing School: Take a sailing lesson or chart a course for a sailboat rental, and explore the calm, sheltered waters of the South Bay Area.
  • Tesla Factory Tour: About a 25-minute drive from Los Altos Hills, Tesla offers free tours of its famous factory on select days, where you can see how electric cars are manufactured.

Week-Long Itinerary for Los Altos Hills

If you want a week-long trip to Los Altos Hills, here is a suggested itinerary of things to do.

Food and Drink Scene of Los Altos Hills

Must-Try Dishes

As a town known for its exclusive community, cuisine in Los Altos Hills is eclectic and high-end.

  • Quiche at Bumble Cafe: Try the mouth-watering quiche at the quaint Bumble Cafe located in The Homestead. They feature a wide variety of quiches, sandwiches, and breakfast options.
  • Steamed Manila Clams at The Basin: This upscale seafood restaurant offers fresh and sustainable seafood sourced exclusively from Northern California, along with cocktails inspired by the South Pacific.
  • Barbecue Ribs at Los Altos Grill: Located on Main Street, this grill-styled restaurant offers a menu featuring American fare with Californian twists and specializes in BBQ ribs.

Local Beverages

California is globally known for its wine, and Los Altos Hills is no exception.

  • Ridge Vineyards: The estate offers wine tastings and vineyard tours, and it was voted as one of the top 10 tasting rooms in America.
  • Picchetti Winery: This family-owned winery offers a range of estate wines with picturesque views of the vineyards and the surrounding hills.
  • The Wine Room: This wine bar located in downtown Los Altos Hills features only California wines and offers tasting flights and wine-friendly food.

Where to Stay in Los Altos Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Los Altos Hills is a small town with a handful of accommodations nearby for visitors.

  • Garden Court Hotel: Located in the heart of downtown Palo Alto, just minutes from Los Altos Hills, Garden Court Hotel features a choice of elegant rooms with luxury amenities.
  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel and Suites: This comfortable hotel in Mountain View offers an outdoor pool, gym and free breakfast with affordable room rates.
  • Stanford Guest House: This historic and charming hotel was once the residence of the university president, and it offers comfortable and affordable guest rooms just minutes from Los Altos Hills.

What to Buy in Los Altos Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Filoli Mementos: Take home a souvenir from one of the most prestigious and historic country estates on the West Coast.
  • California Wines: Bring home a bottle of California wine, and if possible, some from the local vineyards, as a reminder of your trip.
  • Organic Farm Produce: Hidden Villa, the 1600-acre organic farm, offers produce boxes that you can order online and have delivered to you or carry back home for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Los Altos Hills

The residents of Los Altos Hills are an exclusive and well-to-do community. Visitors are expected to dress appropriately and show respect towards the local customs, particularly regarding the natural serenity of the area. Littering, loud music, and public intoxication are not allowed.

Best Time to Visit Los Altos Hills

The best time to visit Los Altos Hills is during the spring and fall months, particularly from March through May and September through November. The weather is pleasant, and the hills are lush and green with wildflowers and blooming trees.

Do’s and Don’ts of Los Altos Hills

Do:

  • Admire the natural beauty and serenity of the area.
  • Wear appropriate clothing while hiking or biking.
  • Visit the vineyards and the golf course.

Don’t:

  • Litter, disturb the peace, or break any eco-friendly or noise pollution laws.
  • Play loud music in public areas or while hiking or biking.
  • Wear revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Los Altos Hills

Los Altos Hills is an extremely safe town, with a negligible rate of crime. However, visitors should exercise caution while hiking or biking on the trails and stay aware of the mountain lions and other wildlife.

Health in Los Altos Hills

Los Altos Hills offers a variety of hospitals and health clinics, with both general and specialized healthcare services. Visitors are advised to bring their medications, especially if they have any preexisting health conditions.

Getting to Los Altos Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Los Altos Hills

  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO): Approximately 25 miles northwest of Los Altos Hills.
  • San Jose International Airport (SJC): Approximately 13 miles east of Los Altos Hills.

Bus Stations Near Los Altos Hills

  • Los Altos Transit Center
  • Mountain View Transit Center

Other Options

  • Caltrain: Provides access to San Francisco and San Jose, with stops in Los Altos, Mountain View, and Palo Alto.
  • Car rentals: Available at both San Francisco and San Jose airports.

How to Get Around in Los Altos Hills

The best way to get around Los Altos Hills is by car. Visitors can also ride a bike, use a ride-sharing app, or take public transportation to get around.

Where to Go Next from Los Altos Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Los Altos Hills is situated in the heart of the Silicon Valley, and there is plenty to discover in the surrounding towns and cities within driving distance.

  • Palo Alto: Known for Stanford University, high-end shopping, dining, and vibrant nightlife.
  • San Francisco: Known for the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, Fisherman’s Wharf, and various cultural landmarks and neighborhoods.
  • Santa Cruz: Located just over the mountains from Los Altos Hills, this beach town is known for its famous boardwalk and superb surfing opportunities.

Conclusion

Los Altos Hills is an exclusive and charming town with a variety of things to offer its visitors. From exploring the natural beauty on the hiking trails to savoring the exclusive food and drink scene, Los Altos Hills has something for everyone. Plan your trip and enjoy exploring!
