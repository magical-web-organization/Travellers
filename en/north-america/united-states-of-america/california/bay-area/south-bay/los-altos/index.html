Introduction to Los Altos

Los Altos is a charming city located in the heart of Silicon Valley, with a small-town feel and a vibrant community. This picturesque city is known for its tree-lined streets, beautiful parks, and excellent weather all year round. From hiking and biking trails to cultural attractions, Los Altos has something for every traveler to explore and enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Los Altos

Los Altos has a rich history and culture, as well as natural beauty and outdoor activities. Here are some must-see places in Los Altos:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Los Altos History Museum: This museum showcases the city’s local history and culture through exhibits, programs, and events.

  • J. Gilbert Smith House: A historical house museum that offers a glimpse into the early 20th-century lifestyle in the city.

  • Foothill College Observatory: A science and education center where visitors can explore the stars and planets through telescopes and educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Los Altos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rancho San Antonio Open Space Preserve: One of the most popular open space preserves in the bay area with over 24 miles of hiking trails, picnic areas, and wildlife viewing.

  • Hidden Villa: An organic farm and nature reserve that offers scenic trails, farm tours, and educational programs.

  • Redwood Grove Nature Preserve: A forested area with a creek and scenic trails that provide breathtaking views of redwood trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Los Altos

Los Altos has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the Bay Area. Here are some top picks:

  • Los Altos Hills Pathways: A 44-mile network of trails and bike paths that connects various neighborhoods and parks.

  • Permanente Creek Trail: An 8.7 mile trail that starts at Shoreline Park and follows Permanente Creek.

  • Los Altos - Foothills - Arastradero Loop: A 17.2 mile scenic loop with moderate difficulty and beautiful views.

Best Things to Do in Los Altos: Activities and Tours

Los Altos offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Los Altos:

  • Hire a bike and explore the city: Rent a bike and discover the picturesque streets and hidden gems of Los Altos.

  • Take a walking tour: Join a guided walking tour and learn about the city’s history and culture from a local guide.

  • Visit the farmers market: The Los Altos Farmers Market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other local products every Thursday morning.

Week-Long Itinerary for Los Altos

If you’re planning to spend a week in Los Altos, here’s a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Los Altos

Los Altos has a diverse food and drink scene that offers something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Los Altos Omelet: A delicious omelet made with avocado, bacon, and cheese.

  • Los Altos Burger: A classic cheeseburger with a twist, topped with guacamole and salsa.

  • Baklava: A traditional Greek dessert made with phyllo pastry, honey, and nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Try the local craft beer at Freewheel Brewing Company, which offers a rotating selection of beer on tap.

  • Wine: Los Altos is home to several wineries and vineyards, such as Thomas Fogarty Winery and Byington Vineyard & Winery.

  • Coffee: Los Altos has several coffee shops that offer high-quality coffee and pastries, such as Red Berry Coffee Bar and Peet’s Coffee & Tea.

Where to Stay in Los Altos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Los Altos offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are a few options:

  • Garden Court Hotel: A 4-star hotel in the heart of downtown Los Altos that offers luxurious accommodations and amenities.

  • Enchante Boutique Hotel: A French-inspired hotel that offers charming guest rooms and a serene garden.

  • Global Luxury Suites in Los Altos: A budget-friendly option that offers fully furnished apartments with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Los Altos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Los Altos with these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Artwork: Find beautiful artwork and prints at the Los Altos Art & Wine Festival or at the Los Altos Art Museum.

  • Local wine: Bring home a bottle of wine from one of the local wineries, such as Byington Vineyard & Winery or Thomas Fogarty Winery.

  • Handmade crafts: Visit the Art in the Park event in Los Altos to find handmade crafts and gifts from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Los Altos

Los Altos has a friendly and welcoming community that values nature, culture, and education. Respect for the environment and a relaxed lifestyle are also key aspects of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Los Altos

Los Altos has a Mediterranean climate with mild winters and warm summers. The best time to visit is from April to October when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Los Altos

Do:

  • Do visit the local wineries and vineyards

  • Do explore the hiking and biking trails

  • Do attend the Los Altos Arts & Wine Festival

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces

  • Don’t park in restricted areas

  • Don’t disturb the local wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Los Altos

Los Altos is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take standard precautions such as locking doors and windows, and avoiding walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Los Altos

Los Altos has several medical facilities and hospitals, such as El Camino Hospital. Visitors should also take standard precautions and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Los Altos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Los Altos is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Los Altos:

Airports Near Los Altos

The closest airport to Los Altos is San Jose International Airport, which is approximately 14 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Los Altos

The Santa Clara Valley Transportation Authority operates buses throughout the Bay Area, with several stops in Los Altos.

Other Options

For those traveling by car, Los Altos is located near major highways such as Highway 280 and Highway 85. There are also several car rental companies in the area.

How to Get Around in Los Altos

Los Altos is a walkable city, with many attractions and places to visit within easy walking distance. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a taxi or ride-sharing service such as Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Los Altos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Los Altos is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations and attractions, such as:

  • Stanford University: Located just a few miles away, Stanford University is a renowned institution with a beautiful campus and great museums.

  • Palo Alto: A vibrant city with a thriving arts scene, excellent shopping, and dining options.

  • San Francisco: Just a short drive away, the iconic city of San Francisco is a must-see destination with stunning views, iconic landmarks, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Los Altos is a beautiful city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and culture. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Los Altos. Plan your trip today and experience the charm and vibrancy of this lovely city.
