Introduction to Mountain View

Mountain View is a city located in the South Bay region of the San Francisco Bay Area, California, USA. It is home to many high-tech companies and has been dubbed the “Capital of Silicon Valley”. However, apart from being a hub of innovation, Mountain View also boasts of attractions that are deeply rooted in its cultural and natural heritage. In this tour guide, we have compiled a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, and insider tips to help you make the most of your Mountain View experience.

List of Places to Visit in Mountain View

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, put the following on your list of places to visit:

  • Computer History Museum: This museum houses the world’s largest collection of computing artifacts. From ancient computing devices to the latest innovations in technology, the Computer History Museum has it all.

  • Moffett Field Historical Society Museum: Located within the Moffett Federal Airfield, this museum features exhibits and artifacts from the history of the Airfield, Silicon Valley, and NASA Ames Research Center.

  • Rengstorff House: Built-in 1867, the Rengstorff House is a restored Victorian-era home that tells the story of Mountain View’s early history and development.

Natural Attractions of Mountain View: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to reconnect with nature and explore the great outdoors, check out these natural attractions:

  • Shoreline Lake Park: This park is a favorite among locals and visitors. You can rent a kayak, paddleboat or sailboat to enjoy the lake. You can also take a leisurely stroll around the lake, go fishing or have a picnic.

  • Stevens Creek Trail: This trail runs for 5.6 miles and is a popular spot for hiking, jogging, cycling, and bird watching. The trail follows Stevens Creek and provides beautiful views of the surrounding scenery.

  • Rancho San Antonio Open Space Preserve: This park features 24 miles of hiking trails, picnic areas, equestrian facilities, and a nature center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mountain View

Mountain View’s picturesque and natural setting also makes it a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Bay Trail: This trail spans over 500 miles and goes through Mountain View, offering scenic views of the San Francisco Bay.

  • Stevens Creek Trail: As mentioned earlier, this trail is perfect for hiking, jogging, and biking.

  • San Andreas Fault Trail: This trail stretches for 3.4 miles and offers views of the historic San Andreas Fault Zone.

Best Things to Do in Mountain View: Activities and Tours

From interactive museums to outdoor adventures, here are some of the best things to do in Mountain View:

  • Googleplex: You can’t visit Mountain View without stopping by Googleplex, the sprawling headquarters of Google. Take a tour to learn about the company’s history and get a glimpse of how they operate.

  • Shoreline Amphitheatre: This outdoor concert venue hosts performances by big-name artists year-round. Check their schedule for upcoming shows.

  • Moffett Field Historic Tours: Experience a guided tour of the airfield and the NASA Ames Research Center. You’ll learn about the history of the airfield and get an up-close look at some of the planes and spacecraft.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mountain View

Here’s a detailed itinerary for a week in Mountain View:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Computer History Museum, and the Moffett Field Historical Society Museum
2 Hike the Stevens Creek Trail, and visit the Rengstorff House
3 Explore the Shoreline Lake Park, and take a tour of Googleplex
4 Bike the Bay Trail, and visit Rancho San Antonio Open Space Preserve
5 Attend a concert at the Shoreline Amphitheatre, and check out the San Andreas Fault Trail
6 Take a guided tour of the Moffett Field Historic, and visit the NASA Ames Research Center
7 Visit a local farmers’ market, and enjoy a scenic picnicking experience at Shoreline Lake Park

Food and Drink Scene of Mountain View

Mountain View has a diverse and vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • California Burrito: This burrito is made with fries, sour cream, guacamole, and rolled-up with your choice of carne asada, grilled chicken, or shrimp.

  • Garlic Fries: Crispy fries tossed with fresh garlic, parsley, and parmesan cheese.

  • Korean Fried Chicken: Chicken wings or drumsticks covered in a crispy and spicy batter.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Try local brews at the Red Rock Coffee Company, and the Steins Beer Garden and Restaurant.

  • Wine: Head over to the Rok Wine Bar, and the Wine Room to explore some delicious wine.

Where to Stay in Mountain View: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mountain View offers a wide range of accommodations. From luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses, there’s something to fit every budget. Here are some recommendations for places to stay:

  • Hotel Strata: Modern hotel located in downtown Mountain View with a rooftop lounge and spa.

  • Quality Inn & Suites: Affordable hotel located near Shoreline Amphitheatre.

  • Acacia Tree Lodge: Cozy and affordable guest house located within walking distance of downtown Mountain View.

What to Buy in Mountain View: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs to bring home? Here are some things to buy in Mountain View:

  • Google Merchandise: Visit the Google Store at the Googleplex to purchase some Google-themed merchandise.

  • Artisanal Cheese: Head over to The Milk Pail Market to get a variety of unique artisanal cheese.

  • Local Wines: Wine-lovers can pick up a bottle from the Santa Cruz Mountains Winegrowers Association, or the Los Altos Tasting Room.

Local Culture and Customs of Mountain View

Mountain View has a diverse and inclusive community. The city hosts many cultural events, such as the A La Carte and Art Festival, and the Mountain View Art and Wine Festival. Be sure to respect the cultural diversity of the area.

Best Time to Visit Mountain View

The best time to visit Mountain View is from April to November as the weather is mild and enjoyable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mountain View

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages.

  • Explore the natural landscape with a hiking or biking trip.

  • Attend a concert or festival.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.

  • Jaywalk.

  • Be dismissive of cultural diversity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mountain View

Mountain View is relatively safe, but like any city, it’s important to take some safety precautions. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.

  • Always wear a helmet when cycling.

Health in Mountain View

Mountain View has several hospitals and medical centers that are easily accessible. They include :

  • El Camino Hospital

  • Palo Alto Medical Foundation

Getting to Mountain View: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mountain View is easily accessible by road, air, and rail. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Mountain View

  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO): 21 miles from Mountain View.

  • Norman Y. Mineta San Jose International Airport (SJC): 11 miles from Mountain View.

Bus Stations Near Mountain View

  • Greyhound Bus Station, 630 N Rengstorff Ave, Mountain View, CA 94043

  • Caltrain Station, 600 W Evelyn Ave, Mountain View, CA 94041

Other Options

  • Ride-hailing apps such as Lyft or Uber.

  • Biking and walking are also popular options in Mountain View.

How to Get Around in Mountain View

Mountain View is a small city, and it’s easy to get around. You can rent bikes or scooters, use ride-hailing apps or public transportation like buses and light rail.

Where to Go Next from Mountain View: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of California, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • San Francisco: Just an hour’s drive or a 40-minute Caltrain ride, San Francisco is a must-visit destination for its iconic landmarks like the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz Island.

  • Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk: A family-friendly amusement park that is just a one-hour drive from Mountain View.

  • Napa Valley: Famous for its vineyards and wine tastings, Napa Valley is just a two-hour drive from Mountain View.

Conclusion

Mountain View offers a perfect blend of culture, natural beauty, and technological advancements. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can make the most of your Mountain View experience. From museums and natural attractions to the food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone in this bustling Californian city.
