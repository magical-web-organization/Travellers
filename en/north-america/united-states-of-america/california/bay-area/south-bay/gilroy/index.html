Introduction to Gilroy

Nestled in the heart of the Santa Clara Valley, Gilroy is a charming city located in South Bay, in the Bay Area of Northern California, United States. Known as the “Garlic Capital of the World,” Gilroy is famous for its annual Garlic Festival, which attracts visitors from all around the world. Besides its garlic production, Gilroy boast of its fascinating history, natural attractions, picturesque beauty, and welcoming community.

List of Places to Visit in Gilroy

There are plenty of things to see and explore in Gilroy. Here is a list of must-visit places in Gilroy:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Garlic World: A museum dedicated to the history and significance of garlic.
  • Gilroy Museum: A local museum exhibiting the history of Gilroy and the surrounding area.
  • Historic Gilroy Downtown: A charming downtown with a variety of local shops, restaurants, and galleries.
  • Casa de Fruta: A popular tourist attraction featuring a fruit stand, gourmet deli, wine tasting, RV park, and more.
  • Fortino Winery: A family-owned and operated winery with vineyards, tasting room, gift shop, and picnic area.

Natural Attractions of Gilroy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gilroy Gardens: A theme park with unique gardens, water attractions, rides, and shows.
  • Coyote Lake-Harvey Bear Ranch County Park: A park offering various activities such as hiking, camping, fishing, boating, and picnicking.
  • Henry W. Coe State Park: California’s second-largest state park covering more than 87,000 acres and offering hiking, camping, horseback riding, and fishing.
  • Mount Madonna County Park: A park featuring hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the Santa Clara Valley.
  • Uvas Canyon County Park: A park with waterfalls, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gilroy

Gilroy is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts with plenty of hiking and biking trails. Here are some top hiking and biking destinations:

  • Coyote Ridge Trail: a moderate 7.5-mile hike with beautiful views of Coyote Valley.
  • Eagle Ridge Trail: a challenging 5.5-mile hike with strenuous uphill sections and breathtaking views.
  • Madrone Trail: a serene 4-mile hike with seasonal wildflowers and stunning views of the valley.
  • Gilroy Yamato Hot Springs & Coyote Lake Loop Trail: a moderate 12-mile hike with a mix of chaparral, oak woodlands, and stunning lake views.
  • Santa Teresa County Park: a park with multiple options for hiking and biking, including the popular Stile Ranch Trail, which is a 4.2-mile loop.

Best Things to Do in Gilroy: Activities and Tours

Gilroy offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy, including the following:

  • Garlic Festival: An iconic festival held annually in July, celebrating garlic with food, drink, music, and games.
  • Gilroy Hot Springs: A historic hot springs resort that has now become a day-use park offering hiking and picnicking options
  • Gilroy Premium Outlets: Featuring over 145 shops offering discounted designer and brand-name goods.
  • Guglielmo Winery: A family-owned winery that produces award-winning wines with guided tours, tastings, and events.
  • Morgan Hill Mushroom Mardi Gras: A family-friendly festival celebrating mushrooms with food, music, parade, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gilroy

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Gilroy:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Downtown Gilroy, including the Gilroy Museum and Garlic World.
Day 2 Enjoy the scenic views and outdoor activities at Coyote Lake-Harvey Park.
Day 3 Take a hike at Henry W. Coe State Park.
Day 4 Visit the famous Gilroy Premium Outlets and shop till you drop.
Day 5 Take a guided tour and tasting at the Guglielmo Winery.
Day 6 Enjoy the Garlic Festival or visit Coyote Ridge Trail for a beautiful hike.
Day 7 Take a leisurely stroll at Morgan Hill Mushroom Mardi Gras.

Food and Drink Scene of Gilroy

Gilroy offers a mix of international and regional cuisine with a focus on garlic in many dishes. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garlic Ice Cream: The classic and unusual ice cream with a garlic tang.
  • Garlic Fries: French fries tossed with garlic and olive oil.
  • Garlic Bread: Toasted bread with garlic and butter.
  • Pasta with Garlic and Olive Oil: A classic vegetarian dish with a flavorful garlic twist.

Local Beverages

  • Gilroy Garlic Wine: A unique wine with a distinct garlic flavor available at Fortino Winery.
  • Sprecher Garlic Root Beer: A non-alcoholic drink with a sweet garlic taste available at Garlic World.

Where to Stay in Gilroy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gilroy offers a range of accommodations, from budget-friendly to luxury options. Here are some of the popular places to stay:

  • Hilton Garden Inn: A modern hotel located near the Gilroy Premium Outlets and Gilroy Gardens.
  • Best Western Plus Forest Park Inn: A family-friendly hotel close to Gilroy’s attractions and wineries.
  • Economy Inn Gilroy: A budget-friendly motel near downtown Gilroy.
  • Camping Sites: Coyote Lake-Harvey Bear Ranch County Park and Henry W. Coe State Park offer campsites for RV and tent camping.

What to Buy in Gilroy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gilroy is well known for its garlic-related products. Some of the popular items to take back home include:

  • Garlic: Fresh garlic bulbs or garlic-infused oils, spreads, and sauces.
  • Garlic-themed souvenirs: T-shirts, hats, mugs, and fridge magnets.
  • Wine: Gilroy has several wineries that offer unique flavorful wines.
  • Fruit and Nuts: Dried fruits, candied nuts, and fresh-made pies are popular Gilroy specialties.

Local Culture and Customs of Gilroy

Gilroy has a diverse population with a friendly, welcoming community. The town has a rich history and proud agricultural heritage, which is represented in the architecture and local cuisine. Visitors should follow simple etiquette while visiting the area. It is customary for local people to greet each other with a smile and eye contact. Visitors should dress appropriately while visiting churches, temples, and other religious sites. In addition, tipping is customary in restaurants and bars.

Best Time to Visit Gilroy

Gilroy is a year-round destination, although summer months attract the maximum number of visitors. The best time to visit Gilroy is in the summer (June to August) when the weather is warm and pleasant. October is also a great time to visit Gilroy when the Garlic Festival takes place. If visitors prefer to avoid crowded events, the shoulder months of April to May and September to October offer milder weather with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gilroy

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts for visiting Gilroy:

Do:

  • Do try the garlic-themed dishes and beverages.
  • Do take a guided tour of local wineries.
  • Do visit the Garlic Festival or Mushroom Mardi Gras.
  • Do follow trail rules while hiking and leave no trace.
  • Do respect the culture and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in natural areas and campgrounds.
  • Don’t waste water while camping or hiking.
  • Don’t be loud and disturbing in residential areas and campgrounds.
  • Don’t enter private property without permission.
  • Don’t underestimate the heat and weather conditions while hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gilroy

Gilroy is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should follow standard safety precautions, such as keeping valuables secured, locking car doors, and staying aware of surroundings, especially at night. Coyote Lake-Harvey Bear Ranch County Park has a history of mountain lion sightings, and visitors should take necessary precautions while hiking in the park.

Health in Gilroy

The tap water in Gilroy is safe to drink, and the town has quality medical facilities and services. Visitors must take necessary sun protection measures while hiking and exploring outdoor areas.

Getting to Gilroy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gilroy

The closest airport to Gilroy is San Jose International Airport, which is approximately 34 miles away. Visitors can rent a car or hail a taxi or ride-sharing services from the airport to Gilroy.

Bus Stations Near Gilroy

Gilroy has a bus station located on Monterey Street that connects the town to San Jose and other nearby cities. Visitors can use Greyhound or other local bus services to travel to Gilroy.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Gilroy by car via Highway 101 or by train via the Caltrain. The town has a free downtown shuttle service called the Garlic City Shuttle that connects various tourist destinations in the town.

How to Get Around in Gilroy

Gilroy is a small town that is best explored by car or bicycle. Visitors can rent a car from local rental services or bring their cars. Bicycles can be rented from local bike rental services or bike shops. The town also has Uber, Lyft, and taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Gilroy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gilroy is centrally located, offering visitors to explore nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some popular options:

  • San Francisco: Only a two-hour drive or train ride from Gilroy.
  • Monterey: A coastal town with rich history and scenic beauty, approximately an hour away from Gilroy.
  • Santa Cruz: A beach town known for its surf culture, about 45 minutes away from Gilroy.
  • Big Basin Redwoods State Park: A state park about two hours away from Gilroy, featuring ancient redwood trees and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Gilroy is a unique destination with plenty to offer visitors, whether exploring natural attractions or immersing in cultural experiences. The friendly community, delicious cuisine, and beautiful scenic views make Gilroy a perfect place for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Gilroy and make unforgettable memories in the Garlic Capital of the World.
