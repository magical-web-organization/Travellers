Introduction to Merced County

Located in San Joaquin Valley, California, Merced County boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and cultural diversity. It is a perfect destination for outdoor adventures, museum hopping, and culinary experiences. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the top attractions, activities, and practical information to plan your perfect trip to Merced County.

List of Places to Visit in Merced County

Whether you are looking for natural wonders, cultural landmarks, or family-friendly activities, Merced County has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-see places to visit in Merced County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Merced County has a rich history, dating back to the indigenous tribes, early settlers, and the Gold Rush era. Here are some of the most significant cultural attractions to visit in Merced County:

  • Merced County Courthouse Museum: Housed in a beautiful 1875 Victorian building, the museum showcases the history of Merced County and offers guided tours and exhibits.
  • Castle Air Museum: Located in Atwater, the museum features over 70 restored military aircraft, including the B-52 and SR-71 Blackbird.
  • Merced Multicultural Arts Center: The Center hosts cultural performances, exhibits, and workshops that celebrate the diversity of Merced County’s ethnic communities.
  • San Joaquin Valley National Cemetery: This tranquil cemetery honors the veterans who served in the U.S. Armed Forces with a central monument, memorial walkway, and burial plots.

Natural Attractions of Merced County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Merced County is blessed with natural beauty, from the undulating hills to the meandering rivers and lakes. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore in Merced County:

  • Yosemite National Park: Although not technically in Merced County, Yosemite is a must-visit destination for its stunning waterfalls, granite cliffs, and hiking trails.
  • Lake Yosemite: This serene lake is perfect for boating, fishing, camping, and enjoying the sunset.
  • Merced River: The river offers various recreational activities, such as kayaking, rafting, and fishing. You can also take a scenic drive along the river’s canyon to see the geological formations and wildlife.
  • Merced National Wildlife Refuge: Covering over 10,000 acres, the refuge is home to various bird species, including the threatened sandhill cranes and snow geese.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Merced County

If you love hiking or biking, Merced County has numerous trails and paths to suit your level of experience. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Yosemite Valley Trail: A moderate hike that leads you to the iconic Yosemite Valley floor, where you can see landmarks such as Half Dome, Yosemite Falls, and El Capitan.
  • Merced River Trail: A 13-mile trail that follows the Merced River and offers beautiful views of the canyon and wildlife.
  • Lake Yosemite Loop Trail: A 2.6-mile loop that encircles Lake Yosemite, taking you through oak groves and a beach area.
  • Merced Irrigation District Bike Path: A flat trail that spans 14 miles along the historic irrigation canals, offering scenic views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Merced County: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural and cultural attractions, Merced County offers plenty of fun activities and tours for all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Merced County:

  • UC Merced Arts and Lecture Series: The University of California, Merced hosts various arts and lecture series throughout the year, featuring renowned scholars, musicians, and artists.
  • Merced County Fair: Held annually in June, the fair showcases local agriculture, crafts, carnival rides, and concerts.
  • Castle Air Oktoberfest: Taking place in October, this celebration features live music, German food and beverages, and military displays.
  • Merced Antique Fly-In and Air Show: Held in September, the event showcases vintage aircraft, aerial demonstrations, and aviation feats.

Food and Drink Scene of Merced County

One of the best ways to experience the local culture of Merced County is through its food and drink scene. From farm-to-table cuisine to ethnic eateries, you’ll find plenty of delicious options here.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tri-tip: This tender and juicy steak is a Merced County specialty, often served with garlic bread and salad.
  • Menudo: A spicy stew made with tripe, hominy, and chili peppers, a popular traditional Mexican dish.
  • Ambrosia Burger: A juicy burger made with local beef, topped with avocado, bacon, and blue cheese.
  • Garlic Fries: A popular appetizer made with crispy fries drizzled with garlic and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Merced Ale: A craft beer made with locally sourced hops and barley, available at local breweries such as Casa De Tamales and The Partisan.
  • Wine from Vista Ranch and Cellars: A family-owned winery that produces high-quality wines from its vineyard in Merced County.
  • Coffee from Red Rock Coffee: A local coffee shop that serves organic, fair-trade coffee roasted on-site.

Where to Stay in Merced County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Merced County has various accommodation options to suit your budget and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Merced County:

  • The Tioga Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in Downtown Merced, with an on-site restaurant, rooftop bar, and fitness center.
  • Budget Inn: A budget-friendly motel in Merced, with free Wi-Fi, flat-screen TVs, and a 24-hour front desk.
  • Sweet Dreams Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast in Atwater, with themed rooms, a pool, and hot tub.
  • Yosemite RV Resort: An RV and camping site located an hour’s drive from Yosemite, with full hook-ups, cabins, and amenities such as a pool and playground.

What to Buy in Merced County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some of the best shops to visit in Merced County:

  • Gustine Market: A farmer’s market that offers fresh produce, artisanal bread, and local crafts.
  • Quady Winery Tasting Room: A wine tasting room that offers award-winning dessert wines and vermouth.
  • Runnin’ Rooster: A boutique that sells handmade jewelry, clothing, and accessories made by local artisans.
  • The Tin Peddler: An antique store in Merced that offers vintage collectibles, tools, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Merced County

Merced County celebrates its cultural diversity through various festivals, events, and landmarks. Some of the notable cultural traditions include:

  • Cinco de Mayo: A celebration of Mexican heritage and pride, held annually in May, with parades, food, and music.
  • Merced County Arts Council: A nonprofit organization that promotes local artists and performers through exhibits, workshops, and performances.
  • Merced County Courthouse Museum: A historic landmark that showcases the architecture and history of Merced County.

Best Time to Visit Merced County

The best time to visit Merced County depends on your preferences and activities. The summers are hot and dry, perfect for outdoor adventures, while the winters are mild and wet, ideal for indoor activities and sightseeing. Some of the popular events in Merced County include the Merced County Fair in June, the Castle Air Oktoberfest in October, and the Merced Antique Fly-In and Air Show in September.
