Introduction to Exeter

Located in the heart of Tulare County in the San Joaquin Valley, Exeter is a charming small town in California, known for its rich history and natural beauty. The town was founded in 1888 and is home to many historical landmarks, museums, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Exeter

Exeter has a lot to offer visitors who are looking for an authentic California experience. Here are the top places to visit in Exeter:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Exeter Historical Museum: This museum showcases artifacts and exhibits that highlight the town’s rich history, from the Native American era to the early 20th century.
  • Exeter Courthouse Gallery: Located in a restored courthouse, this gallery features local artwork and hosts events throughout the year.
  • Gardens of the World: This 6-acre garden features themed sections that represent different regions of the world, including Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
  • Foothills Visitor Center: This is a great place to start exploring the nearby Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks. Visitors can learn about the park’s history and get advice on hiking, camping, and other activities.

Natural Attractions of Exeter: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Kaweah: This beautiful lake is perfect for hiking, fishing, swimming, and boating. Visitors can rent kayaks, canoes, and paddleboards at the marina.
  • Exeter Walking Trail: This trail is a scenic walking path that runs through the town and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and countryside.
  • Exeter City Park: This park is a popular spot for picnics, sports, and outdoor events. There are playgrounds, a skate park, and plenty of open space for activities.
  • Lemon Hill Trailhead: This trailhead offers access to the Sierra Foothills and is a great place to go hiking or mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Exeter

Exeter has some of the best hiking and biking trails in California. Here are our top picks:

  • Kaweah Oaks Preserve: This preserve has several easy, family-friendly trails that wind through an oak forest and offer great opportunities to spot wildlife.
  • Sequoia National Forest: This forest features some of the most scenic trails in California, with options for all skill levels.
  • Yokohl Valley Trail: This trail is a moderate hike that offers stunning views of the Sierra Nevada Mountains and the surrounding countryside.
  • Mooney Grove Park: This park has several trails that wind through a beautiful grove of ancient oak trees.

Best Things to Do in Exeter: Activities and Tours

Exeter offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Exeter:

  • Visit the Exeter Farmers Market: This market is open every Thursday and features local produce, crafts, and live music.
  • Take a horseback ride: Several local outfitters offer guided horseback rides through the foothills and countryside.
  • Go wine tasting: Exeter is located in the heart of California’s wine country, and there are several wineries located within a short drive of the town.
  • Attend a local festival: Exeter hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Fall Festival, Christmas Parade, and Lemon Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Exeter

Exeter offers a unique culinary experience that combines the local produce with a creative approach to cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lemon ricotta pancakes: This is a popular breakfast dish in Exeter, made with locally-sourced lemons and ricotta cheese.
  • Tri-tip steak: This cut of beef is a specialty of California’s Central Valley and can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Orange Blossom honey: This honey is made from the nectar of the orange blossoms that bloom in the spring and is a popular gift item.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: There are several local breweries in Exeter, including Rocky Hill Brewery and Brewbakers Brewery.
  • Wine: There are several wineries in the area, including Condor’s Hope Vineyard and Ficklin Vineyards.
  • Orange juice: The Central Valley is known for its citrus production, and the local orange juice is a must-try.
