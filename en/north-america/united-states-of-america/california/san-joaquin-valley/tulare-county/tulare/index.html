Introduction to Tulare

Tulare is a charming city located in Tulare County, California, USA. It is known for its beautiful surroundings and warm, welcoming locals. The city is located in the heart of San Joaquin Valley, which boasts plenty of outdoor activities for nature lovers, including hiking, bird watching, and water sports. Tulare is a popular tourist destination and attracts visitors from all over the world, making it a must-see stop on any itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Tulare

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tulare Historical Museum: The Tulare Historical Museum is a must-visit attraction for history enthusiasts and art lovers. It showcases the rich history, arts, and culture of Tulare County and its surrounding areas. The exhibits are interactive and engaging, making it an exciting experience for visitors of all ages.

  • World Ag Expo: The World Ag Expo is the largest annual agricultural trade show in the world, held every February in Tulare. It brings together over 1,400 exhibitors showcasing the latest in agriculture technology and trends. It’s a great opportunity to learn about the state of the art in modern agriculture.

Natural Attractions of Tulare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sequoia National Park: Sequoia National Park is a must-see attraction in Tulare County. The park is home to the largest trees in the world, including the General Sherman Tree. It offers hiking trails, camping sites, and plenty of opportunities to explore nature’s beauty.

  • Lake Kaweah: Lake Kaweah is a man-made lake located near the city of Tulare. It is a popular destination for water sports, including fishing, boating, and swimming. Visitors can also enjoy the scenic views and natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tulare

  • Tule River Parkway Trail: The Tule River Parkway Trail is a scenic, 6-mile trail that runs along the Tule River in Tulare. It’s a great place for hiking, jogging, or biking, and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding areas.

  • Lindsay-Strathmore Irrigation District Trail: The Lindsay-Strathmore Irrigation District Trail is a 10-mile trail that runs through the heart of the San Joaquin Valley. It’s an ideal destination for cyclists and hikers of all skill levels, offering beautiful views of the valley’s gardens, orchards, and vineyards.

Best Things to Do in Tulare: Activities and Tours

  • TTP Outdoors: TTP Outdoors is a local company that offers fishing and hunting tours, as well as a range of other outdoor activities. Visitors can choose from lake or river fishing, hunting parties or take a sunset cruise on Lake Kaweah.

  • Tulare County Fair: The Tulare County Fair is an annual event that takes place in September and is one of the most anticipated events of the year. It features live music, carnival rides, petting zoo, agriculture and craft exhibits, and delicious food and drink.

Food and Drink Scene of Tulare

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos al Pastor: Tacos al Pastor is a famous dish in Tulare, inspired by the cuisine of Mexico. It’s a delicious dish made of grilled meat, pineapple, and vegetables, served on a tortilla.

  • Tri-tip: Tri-tip is a classic barbeque dish in the San Joaquin Valley, with a unique smoky flavor and is cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • California wines: The San Joaquin Valley is home to some of the best vineyards in California. Visitors can take a tasting tour and enjoy some of the region’s best wines.

  • Apple cider: Apple cider is a popular, locally produced non-alcoholic beverage that can be found throughout Tulare County.

Local Culture and Customs of Tulare

Tulare was originally home to Native American tribes, who maintained their culture and traditions for thousands of years prior to European contact. Today, Tulare celebrates its diverse culture through various festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Tulare

The best time to visit Tulare is during the spring and fall when the temperatures are cooler, and the weather is mild.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tulare

Tulare is generally a safe city and has a low crime rate. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions and practice basic safety measures such as locking their cars and staying alert in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Tulare

Visitors should ensure they are up to date with their vaccinations and take precautions to protect themselves against diseases like Lyme disease or West Nile virus, which can be prevalent in outdoor areas.

Getting to Tulare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tulare

The nearest airport to Tulare is Fresno Yosemite International Airport. From Fresno airport, travelers can take a rental car or take a shuttle into Tulare.

Bus Stations Near Tulare

Greyhound Lines and Orange Belt Stages are the most convenient bus services for those traveling to Tulare. Both provide frequent services to Tulare County throughout the day.

Other Options

If you’re looking for a more independent mode of transport, hiring a car is a great option. Car rentals in Tulare are affordable and convenient. Travelers can also take advantage of the Amtrak train service that runs through Tulare, providing a simple and comfortable way to explore Tulare County.

How to Get Around in Tulare

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Tulare and its surrounding areas. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Tulare: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Visalia: Visalia is a vibrant town located near Tulare, full of boutiques, restaurants, and shops to explore.

  • Bakersfield: Bakersfield is a short drive from Tulare and offers a variety of attractions, including music venues, museums, and tasty restaurants.

  • Fresno: Fresno is a larger city that is popular for its vibrant nightlife, urban parks, and museums.

Conclusion

Tulare is a fantastic destination for those looking to explore the great outdoors while still immersing themselves in a vibrant culture. With a thriving food and drink scene, plenty of historical landmarks, and breathtaking national parks nearby, visitors are sure to have a memorable experience in Tulare.
