Introduction to Clovis

Clovis is a charming city in Fresno County, California, located in the heart of San Joaquin Valley. As the fifth-largest city in the valley, Clovis is home to many picturesque sights, rich history, and cultural landmarks that make it an ideal destination for adventure and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Clovis

When it comes to exploring Clovis, there’s no shortage of things to do and see. Here is a list of top tourist attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clovis-Big Dry Creek Museum: This museum showcases the history of Clovis from its early days as a farming community to present-day.
  • San Joaquin Depot Museum: This museum takes visitors on a journey through the history of railroads and their role in shaping the development of the San Joaquin Valley.
  • Old Town Clovis: This historic district is home to many unique shops, cafes, restaurants, and bars.

Natural Attractions of Clovis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sierra National Forest: This forest offers a great escape for hiking, camping, and enjoying stunning panoramic views.
  • Woodward Park: This park features miles of hiking trails, picnic areas, playgrounds, and a Japanese garden.
  • Clovis Botanical Garden: This garden is a great place to explore California’s native plants, succulents, and cacti.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clovis

Clovis has many hiking and biking trails, making it an ideal destination for outdoor lovers. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Dry Creek Trail: This 5.3-mile trail is ideal for hiking and biking, featuring stunning views of the San Joaquin Valley.
  • Enterprise Trail: This 2.8-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking, surrounded by beautiful meadows and wildflowers.
  • Clovis Old Town Trail: This 1.1-mile trail is a great way to explore the historic district of Old Town Clovis on foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Clovis: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for fun things to do in Clovis, here are some activities and tours to consider:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Sample some of California’s best wines by taking a wine tasting tour of nearby wineries.
  • Blossom Trail Tour: Take a self-guided or guided tour of the beautiful Blossom Trail, which features breathtaking views of almond and fruit trees in bloom.
  • Hot Air Balloon Tour: Enjoy a bird’s eye view of the San Joaquin Valley by taking a hot air balloon tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clovis

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Clovis:

Day 1 Explore Old Town Clovis and visit the Clovis-Big Dry Creek Museum
Day 2 Hike the Dry Creek Trail in the morning, have lunch at Dusty Buns
  Sandwiches and head to Woodward Park in the afternoon
Day 3 Take a day trip to Yosemite National Park (1.5-hour drive)
Day 4 Relax by the pool at your hotel or go shopping at Sierra Vista Mall
Day 5 Take a hot air balloon tour in the morning, visit the San Joaquin Depot Museum in the afternoon

Food, Drink Scene of Clovis

Clovis has a thriving food and drink scene, offering a diverse range of dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tri-Tip: This tender and flavorful cut of meat is a local specialty and often served with BBQ sauce.
  • Fresno Chili: This mild chili is a staple in many local dishes.
  • Basque Cuisine: This cuisine is influenced by Basque immigrants and features hearty, family-style meals.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Clovis has several breweries that serve locally crafted beers.
  • Wine: Clovis is home to many wineries that produce award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Clovis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clovis has many accommodations to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hotels to high-end resorts. Here are some options:

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott Clovis: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and is located near many tourist attractions.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Clovis: This hotel features an outdoor pool and a fitness center.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton: This hotel has spacious suites and a full kitchen, making it great for families or extended stays.

What to Buy in Clovis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Clovis, here are some ideas:

  • Almond products: Clovis is located in the middle of the almond belt, so it’s no surprise that almonds are a popular product here. You can find almond butter, almond milk, and almond desserts at local stores.
  • Clovis Rodeo Merchandise: Every year, Clovis hosts a popular rodeo event, and you can find rodeo-themed merchandise at local shops.
  • Local Artwork: Clovis has a thriving art scene, and you can find unique artwork and crafts at local galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Clovis

Clovis is known for its laidback and friendly atmosphere. However, it’s important to be aware of some local customs and etiquette:

  • Respect the farmers: Agriculture is a significant part of Clovis’ economy, and many locals work in the farming industry. Be respectful of farmers and their work.
  • Be friendly and hospitable: Clovis locals are known for being friendly and hospitable, so it’s always a good idea to reciprocate the same attitude.
  • Embrace the cowboy culture: Clovis has a rich cowboy culture, so don’t be afraid to try on a cowboy hat or boots and embrace your inner cowboy.

Best Time to Visit Clovis

The best time to visit Clovis is from April to June and October to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds than in the peak summer months. However, if you’re looking for sunny weather and a chance to attend the annual Clovis Rodeo, then the month of April is an excellent time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clovis

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Clovis:

Do:

  • Do try the tri-tip and the Fresno Chili
  • Do visit Old Town Clovis and the Clovis-Big Dry Creek Museum
  • Do be respectful to farmers and locals
  • Do embrace cowboy culture
  • Do take a hot air balloon ride for scenic views

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage ecosystems while on hikes
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for recommendations
  • Don’t forget to tip appropriately at restaurants

Safety and Health

Clovis is generally a safe city, but it’s essential to take precautions like any other place. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Clovis

  • Clovis has a low crime rate, but it’s always a good idea to remain vigilant when visiting any unfamiliar area.
  • Carry identification and valuables with you at all times.
  • Avoid traveling alone, especially at night.

Health in Clovis

  • Be sure to check the weather before embarking on any outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen while hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Be mindful of any allergies to certain food products.

Getting to Clovis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clovis is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some ways to get to Clovis:

Airports Near Clovis

  • Fresno Yosemite International Airport: This airport is the closest airport to Clovis, located only 10 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Clovis

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This station is located in downtown Fresno, only 7 miles away from Clovis.

Other Options

  • Renting a car for easy access
  • Taking a private shuttle to your destination

How to Get Around in Clovis

Clovis is a walkable city, but visitors may prefer rental cars to explore more significant distances. Alternatively, Uber and Lyft are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Clovis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clovis is surrounded by many exciting destinations and excursions. Here are some ideas:

  • Yosemite National Park: This iconic national park is located only 1.5 hours away from Clovis, and it’s a perfect day trip.
  • Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks: These two national parks are located only 2.5 hours from Clovis and are home to some of the world’s largest trees.
  • San Francisco: This city is located about a four-hour drive away, making it a great option for a weekend getaway.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Clovis has something for everyone. Plan your visit today to experience the best of California’s San Joaquin Valley.
