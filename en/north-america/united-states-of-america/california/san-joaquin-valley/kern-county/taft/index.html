Introduction to Taft

Located in Kern County, in the San Joaquin Valley of California, Taft is a small city with a population of just over 9,000. It was named after William Howard Taft, the 27th President of the United States, and was once a bustling oil town with over a hundred active oil wells. Today, it is known for its small-town charm, oil industry heritage, and stunning natural surroundings. In this comprehensive travel guide to Taft, we’ll explore the city’s top cultural and natural attractions, recommend the best things to do, and provide tips to help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Taft

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Kern Oil Museum: This museum displays the rich history of oil discovery and production in the region. You can see exhibits showcasing vintage oil drilling equipment, photos of early oil fields, and interactive displays of drilling techniques.

  • Taft Historic Fort: This fort was once used to protect the oil fields during the early days of Taft’s oil boom. Today, it is a popular attraction and is home to a museum that displays the history of the fort and the surrounding area.

  • Kern County Raceway Park: This raceway park hosts a variety of exciting events throughout the year, including car races, monster truck shows, and concerts. The park is also home to a museum showcasing the history of racing in the area.

Natural Attractions of Taft: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buena Vista Lake: This lake is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and other water sports. You can rent boats here or explore the area on one of the hiking trails that surround the lake.

  • Carrizo Plain National Monument: This natural wonder is known for its stunning displays of wildflowers in the spring, as well as its sprawling grasslands and unique geological formations. Visitors can go hiking, stargazing, or explore the area’s many archaeological sites.

  • Taft Heights Park: This park features hiking trails that lead to stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and is a popular destination for birdwatching and wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taft

  • Carrizo Plain National Monument: With numerous hiking trails that take you through grasslands, canyons, and unique geological formations, the Carrizo Plain National Monument is a hiker’s paradise. The monument is home to several established trails, ranging from easy to difficult, making it perfect for hikers of all skill levels.

  • Taft Heights Park: This park features several hiking trails that take you through the great outdoors, past stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Buena Vista Lake: Take a leisurely hike or bike ride around the lake to soak in the beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in Taft: Activities and Tours

  • Oil Well Tour: Learn about the history of the oil industry in Taft with the Oil Well Tour. Visitors can visit an oil rig, see the oil well pumping, and even visit an oil museum.

  • Whitewater Rafting: For the more adventurous travelers, whitewater rafting on the nearby Kern River is a popular activity. There are plenty of outfitters in the area that offer guided tours for both beginners and experienced rafters.

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of Taft’s natural landscapes on horseback. Several outfitters offer guided horseback riding tours, providing the perfect opportunity to explore the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Taft

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak and Eggs: A hearty breakfast of two eggs cooked your way with a juicy steak on the side.

  • Taft Burger: A juicy burger made from locally sourced beef and topped with crispy bacon, cheese, and all the fixings.

  • Taco Salad: A refreshing salad topped with seasoned beef, lettuce, cheese, and tomatoes – served with crispy tortilla chips.

Local Beverages

  • Locally Brewed Beer: Several microbreweries in the area offer locally brewed beers, including traditional IPAs, porters, and stouts.

  • Local Wine: The nearby vineyards offer a variety of delicious wines, including Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Chardonnay.

Local Culture and Customs of Taft

Taft has a rich cultural history, with a strong focus on the oil industry and the natural environment. The area is home to several Native American tribes, and visitors can learn about their traditions and customs at the nearby museums and cultural centers.

Best Time to Visit Taft

The best time to visit Taft is during the spring and fall seasons when the days are sunny and warm, and the evenings are cool and comfortable. The summer months tend to be hot and dry, while the winter months can be cold and rainy.

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Disrespect the wildlife and natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taft

Taft is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Taft

Visitors should make sure to bring any necessary medication, as the nearest hospital is located in Bakersfield, which is about 20 miles away.

Getting to Taft: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taft

  • Meadows Field Airport: Meadows Field Airport in Bakersfield is the nearest airport to Taft, located about 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Taft

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The Greyhound Bus Station in Bakersfield is the nearest bus station to Taft, located about 20 miles away.

Other Options

  • Driving: Taft is easily accessible by car and is located off Highway 33.

How to Get Around in Taft

The best way to get around Taft is by car, as the city is spread out and many of the attractions are located outside the city limits. Visitors can also rent bicycles or go on guided tours to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Taft: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bakersfield (20 miles away): The largest city in the region, Bakersfield offers a variety of attractions, including museums, botanical gardens, and shopping centers.

  • Sequoia National Park (80 miles away): This national park is home to some of the largest trees on earth, and visitors can explore the stunning natural landscape on hiking trails, scenic drives, and camping trips.

Conclusion

Taft is a hidden gem in California’s San Joaquin Valley, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural history, and small-town charm. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s rich oil heritage, hiking through stunning landscapes, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, Taft has something for everyone.
