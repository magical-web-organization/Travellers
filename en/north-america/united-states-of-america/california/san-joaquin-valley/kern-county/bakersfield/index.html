Introduction to Bakersfield

Located in Kern County, in the heart of San Joaquin Valley, Bakersfield is a charming city known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The city has a diverse population, reflecting its mining and agricultural roots. From museums and historical landmarks to hiking and biking trails, Bakersfield offers a wealth of experiences for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Bakersfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kern County Museum (1000 words)

Kern County Museum is a sprawling 16-acre facility that features 56 historic buildings, including a re-created 19th-century town, a one-room schoolhouse, a blacksmith shop, and a courthouse. The museum offers docent-led tours, educational programs and workshops, and special events throughout the year.

Bakersfield Museum of Art (1000 words)

Located in the heart of the city, Bakersfield Museum of Art displays a diverse collection of contemporary and historical art from California and the world. The museum offers a range of programs and events, including exhibitions, artist talks, and educational workshops.

Fox Theater (1000 words)

Built in 1930, Fox Theater is a historic landmark that has been meticulously restored to its former glory. The theater hosts concerts, plays, and other events throughout the year, and visitors can take guided tours to learn about the history of the venue.

Natural Attractions of Bakersfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kern River Parkway Trail (1000 words)

Kern River Parkway Trail is a scenic hiking and biking trail that runs alongside the Kern River for approximately 20 miles. The trail offers stunning views of the river, as well as opportunities for birdwatching, picnicking, and fishing.

The Park at River Walk (1000 words)

Located in the heart of Bakersfield, The Park at River Walk is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers a range of activities, including walking trails, fishing ponds, and picnic areas.

Wind Wolves Preserve (1000 words)

Wind Wolves Preserve is the largest nonprofit nature preserve in California, encompassing over 93,000 acres of land. The preserve offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, camping, and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bakersfield

Bakersfield offers a wealth of hiking and biking trails for visitors. Some of the most popular include Kern River Parkway Trail, The Park at River Walk, and Wind Wolves Preserve. Visitors can also explore the city’s scenic neighborhoods and downtown area on foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Bakersfield: Activities and Tours

Buck Owens’ Crystal Palace (1000 words)

Buck Owens’ Crystal Palace is a popular music venue and museum that pays tribute to the late country music legend. The venue hosts live music performances, as well as special events and private tours.

Murray Family Farms (1000 words)

Murray Family Farms is a working farm that offers a range of activities and experiences for visitors, including farm tours, u-pick produce, and a corn maze. The farm also hosts seasonal events throughout the year.

Street Murals (1000 words)

Bakersfield is home to several colorful street murals that reflect the city’s cultural diversity and history. Visitors can take a self-guided walking tour to explore the murals, or book a guided tour to learn more about the art and the artists behind it.

Food and Drink Scene of Bakersfield

Must-Try Dishes

Bakersfield is known for its hearty, traditional cuisine, including Basque-style dishes and American classics. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Basque-style lamb stew
  • Tri-tip steak sandwiches
  • Mexican-style street tacos
  • Homemade ice cream

Local Beverages

Bakersfield is home to several craft breweries, wineries, and distilleries, offering visitors a range of locally made beverages to sample.

Where to Stay in Bakersfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bakersfield offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to upscale hotels and resorts. Some popular options include:

  • Padre Hotel
  • Hotel Rosedale
  • Four Points by Sheraton Bakersfield

What to Buy in Bakersfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bakersfield is known for its agricultural products, including nuts, fruits, and wines, as well as its traditional Basque-inspired handicrafts, such as pottery and woodworking. Some popular souvenirs and products to bring home include:

  • Almonds and other nuts
  • Citrus fruits
  • Local wines and craft beers
  • Handcrafted pottery and woodworks

Local Culture and Customs of Bakersfield

Bakersfield’s culture and traditions are deeply rooted in its mining and agricultural history, as well as its vibrant multicultural population. Visitors can explore the city’s history and culture through its museums, art galleries, and festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Bakersfield

The best time to visit Bakersfield is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the city hosts many outdoor events and festivals. Summers can be hot, while winters can be cool and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bakersfield

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and cultural sites.
  • Try the traditional Basque-style cuisine.
  • Explore the city’s scenic neighborhoods and outdoor areas.
  • Attend a live music or arts performance.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car.
  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring outdoors.
  • Disrespect local religion and traditions.
  • Litter or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bakersfield

Bakersfield is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take common-sense precautions when exploring the city, especially at night. Avoid leaving valuables in your car, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Bakersfield

Bakersfield’s air quality can be poor due to agricultural and industrial activity, so visitors with respiratory problems should take appropriate precautions. Visitors should also stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun when exploring the outdoors.
