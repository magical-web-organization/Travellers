Introduction to Kern County

Kern County is a sprawling county located in the southern part of San Joaquin Valley in California. It is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, diverse culture, and abundance of outdoor recreational opportunities. The county has a rich history that is evidenced by the numerous cultural attractions and historical landmarks found here. Kern County is also home to exciting cities and towns that are packed with entertainment and dining options.

List of Places to Visit in Kern County

Here are some of the top destinations that you simply cannot miss when visiting Kern County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kern County Museum: This fascinating museum chronicles Kern County’s rich history through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Bakersfield Museum of Art: Art lovers will appreciate the collection of contemporary and traditional works of art from local and international artists.
  • The Fox Theater: This historic theater in Bakersfield is a must-visit for its stunning Art Deco architecture and its impressive calendar of live performances.

Natural Attractions of Kern County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sequoia National Forest: This magnificent national forest boasts pristine lakes, towering trees, and stunning mountain vistas that make it a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Kern River Canyon: Home to some of the best white-water rafting opportunities in the West, the Kern River Canyon is also a great spot for hiking, fishing, and camping.
  • Red Rock Canyon State Park: This stunning park is famous for its striking red rock formations that are best explored on foot or by bike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kern County

Kern County has no shortage of trails that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best:

  • The Pacific Crest Trail: This iconic trail stretches 2,650 miles from Mexico to Canada and passes right through Kern County.
  • Tejon Ranch: This vast nature reserve offers a network of well-maintained trails that are perfect for hikers and bikers of all levels.
  • Wind Wolves Preserve: This large nature preserve offers beautiful hiking and biking trails that wind through rolling hills and stunning grasslands.

Best Things to Do in Kern County: Activities and Tours

No matter what your interests, there is always something fun to do in Kern County. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Wine Tasting: Kern County is home to many excellent wineries that offer tours and tastings of their award-winning wines.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take to the skies over Kern County for a bird’s-eye view of the stunning scenery.
  • ATV Tours: For those looking for an adrenaline rush, consider taking an ATV tour through the sprawling countryside of Kern County.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kern County

Looking to spend a week exploring Kern County? Here is a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time here:

Day 1: Visit the Kern County Museum and explore downtown Bakersfield. Day 2: Hike the trails at Wind Wolves Preserve and spend the afternoon wine tasting in the nearby vineyards. Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the stunning scenery of Kern County. Day 4: Whitewater rafting down the Kern River Canyon. Day 5: Take an ATV tour of the expansive Tejon Ranch. Day 6: Visit Sequoia National Forest and take a scenic drive through the Giant Sequoia groves. Day 7: Relax at one of Kern County’s picturesque lakes

Food and Drink Scene of Kern County

No visit to Kern County is complete without sampling some of the delicious local cuisine and libations. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Basque-style lamb: A favorite in Kern County, this hearty dish is slow-roasted with paprika and garlic.
  • Bakersfield-style tacos: These flavorful tacos are stuffed with spicy meats, fresh cilantro and onions, and a squeeze of lime juice.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Kern County has an excellent craft beer scene, with many breweries and taprooms offering tastings of their unique brews.
  • Wine: Wine lovers are sure to appreciate the quality of the local wines, which are produced in the vineyards that adorn the hills and valleys of Kern County.

Where to Stay in Kern County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kern County offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Padre Hotel: Located in downtown Bakersfield, this boutique hotel is known for its elegant Art Deco design and modern amenities.
  • The Historic Dorrington Hotel: This charming hotel is housed in a historic building that offers a unique glimpse of Kern County’s past.
  • KOA Camping: For those who prefer to sleep under the stars, Kern County’s KOA campgrounds offer a range of camping experiences that cater to every type of camper.

What to Buy in Kern County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Kern County, be sure to bring back some souvenirs and local products that capture the essence of this beautiful region. Here are some ideas:

  • Native American pottery and jewelry
  • Local olive oil
  • High-quality almonds

Local Culture and Customs of Kern County

Kern County is known for its friendly and welcoming culture, which celebrates diversity and individuality. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, including the Basque culture that is prevalent in some parts of the county.

Best Time to Visit Kern County

The best time to visit Kern County is in the spring or fall, when the weather is pleasant and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and crowded, while winters can be chilly and wet.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kern County

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment
  • Get off the beaten path and explore
  • Try the local cuisine and libations

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the natural areas
  • Trespass on private property
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Kern County

While Kern County is generally considered safe for visitors, it is always important to take basic safety precautions when traveling. These include avoiding walking alone after dark, keeping valuable items out of sight, and staying alert to your surroundings.

Health in Kern County

Kern County has excellent healthcare facilities, with several hospitals and medical centers located throughout the county. Visitors are also advised to invest in good travel insurance to ensure that they are covered in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Kern County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kern County is easily accessible by several transportation options. Here are some of the best ways to get here:

Airports Near Kern County

  • Meadows Field Airport
  • Los Angeles International Airport
  • Bob Hope Airport

Bus Stations Near Kern County

  • Kern County Bus Station
  • Greyhound Station

Other Options

  • Driving: Kern County is easily accessible by car, with several major highways and roads leading to the county.
  • Train: Amtrak serves the city of Bakersfield with several train routes running daily.

How to Get Around Kern County

While driving is the easiest and most convenient way to explore the county, there are other transportation options available. These include:

  • Public transportation: The Kern County Transit offers bus routes throughout the county.
  • Rental cars: Renting a car is a great option for those who want to explore Kern County at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Kern County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kern County is within easy reach of several nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some suggestions:

  • Los Angeles: This vibrant city is just a short drive away and offers endless entertainment and cultural attractions.
  • Death Valley National Park: This stunning park is a must-visit for its spectacular scenery and unique desert landscapes.
  • Santa Barbara: This charming coastal town is known for its stunning beaches, Spanish Colonial architecture, and excellent dining.

Conclusion

Kern County is a destination that will captivate and delight visitors of all types. With stunning natural beauty, exciting cities, and a wealth of cultural attractions, it is easy to see why this region is one of California’s best-kept secrets. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to plan your perfect trip to Kern County and make memories that will last a lifetime.
