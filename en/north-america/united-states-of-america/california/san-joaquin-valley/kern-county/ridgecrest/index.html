Introduction to Ridgecrest

Located in the heart of San Joaquin Valley, Ridgecrest is a quaint city in Kern County, California. With its unique combination of natural beauty and cultural attractions, it has become a popular travel destination for both domestic and international tourists. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Ridgecrest.

List of Places to Visit in Ridgecrest

Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Ridgecrest has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Ridgecrest:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maturango Museum: A must-visit for anyone interested in Native American history and the natural history of the Mojave Desert.
  • Naval Museum of Armament and Technology: Explore the history of the U.S. Navy and its involvement in the development of weapons and technology.
  • Petroglyph Tours: Explore Native American rock art with a guided tour from one of Ridgecrest’s many local experts.

Natural Attractions of Ridgecrest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Red Rock Canyon State Park: The park’s desert cliffs and canyons make it a popular spot for hiking, camping, and geology enthusiasts.
  • Death Valley National Park: Visitors can explore the hottest, driest, and lowest national park in the United States.
  • Trona Pinnacles: These unique geological formations are an other-worldly sight.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ridgecrest

With miles of stunning natural beauty around, Ridgecrest is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are our top recommended hiking and biking routes:

  • Pacific Crest Trail: This iconic trail passes through Ridgecrest and is a must-try for avid hikers.
  • Indian Wells Canyon Trail: With stunning views of the Indian Wells Valley, this trail is a moderate hike that leads to the top of Indian Wells Canyon.
  • Backbone Trail: This 67-mile trail is popular with mountain bikers and hikers alike, and offers breathtaking views of the Sierra Nevadas.

Best Things to Do in Ridgecrest: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to keep visitors engaged. Here are some of the top things to do in Ridgecrest:

  • ATV tours: Explore the desert terrain on a guided ATV tour.
  • Stargazing: Ridgecrest has some of the clearest skies in the world, making it perfect for stargazing.
  • Art Galleries: Ridgecrest has a vibrant arts community, and the local galleries are a must-visit for any art lover.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ridgecrest

To make the most out of your visit to Ridgecrest, we recommend this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the natural beauty of Red Rock Canyon State Park and nearby trails.
  • Day 3: Learn about the history of the area by visiting the Maturango Museum and Petroglyph Tours.
  • Day 4-5: Visit Death Valley National Park for two days of hiking and exploring.
  • Day 6: Try one of the many adventure activities from ATV tours to stargazing.
  • Day 7: Visit local art galleries and shops to buy souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Ridgecrest

While Ridgecrest may be a smaller city, it still offers a unique culinary scene for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Basque cuisine: Ridgecrest has a strong Basque heritage, and visitors can enjoy dishes like lamb stews and sausages at local restaurants.
  • Sourdough bread: For a taste of Ridgecrest’s history in mining, try sourdough bread.
  • Mexican Food: California is known for its Mexican cuisine, and Ridgecrest has some amazing Mexican eateries.

Local Beverages

  • Mojave Red Beer: This spicy beer is inspired by the flavors of the Mojave Desert.
  • Local Wines: Ridgecrest has a burgeoning wine scene with local wineries producing some delicious varietals.

Where to Stay in Ridgecrest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for budget-friendly options or luxurious resorts, Ridgecrest has something to offer to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in Ridgecrest:

  • SpringHill Suites by Marriott: A modern and comfortable hotel with plenty of amenities.
  • Heritage Inn and Suites: A budget-friendly option close to all major attractions.
  • Budget Inn and Suites: A family-run guesthouse offering personal service at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Ridgecrest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ridgecrest offers a range of shopping options for visitors looking to bring home a unique souvenir. Here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Native American Artifacts: From pottery to jewelry, Ridgecrest offers a wide range of Native American art.
  • Mining Memorabilia: Ridgecrest has a rich history of mining, and visitors can buy one-of-a-kind mining artifacts and memorabilia from local shops.
  • Local Wines: The nearby wineries offer amazing varietals that make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ridgecrest

Ridgecrest has a diverse cultural heritage, with strong influences from Native American, Basque, and Mexican cultures. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, such as removing your shoes before entering someone’s home and asking for permission before taking photographs of Native American artifacts.

Best Time to Visit Ridgecrest

Ridgecrest is an ideal travel destination throughout the year with the summer months (June to August) providing more comfortable temperatures. Fall provides an amazing relief in temperature and fewer crowds. If you are interested in stargazing, visit Ridgecrest during the winter for the clearest skies.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ridgecrest

Do:

  • Take the time to learn about the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the local natural beauty and hiking trails.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of Native American artifacts without permission.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore road and safety precautions when exploring the desert terrain.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ridgecrest

Ridgecrest is a safe city with a low crime rate. Visitors should be aware of the extreme weather conditions in the surrounding areas and follow safety recommendations in state parks. Those who choose to adventure in the high deserts terrain should be aware of flash flooding and high temperatures.

Health in Ridgecrest

Ridgecrest has medical facilities, a regional hospital, and emergency medical services available in case of emergency.

Getting to Ridgecrest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ridgecrest is located about 120 miles northeast of Los Angeles and can be reached by car, plane, or bus.

Airports Near Ridgecrest

  • Inyokern Airport: About a 15-minute drive away from Ridgecrest, the airport provides limited flights, usually to LAX.
  • Meadows Field Airport: The second closest airport, about a 1-hour and 30-minute drive to Ridgecrest.

Bus Stations Near Ridgecrest

Greyhound and Eastern Sierra Transit both operate in Ridgecrest.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors. Many travelers take scenic routes from Los Angeles or Sacramento and enjoy the views.

How to Get Around in Ridgecrest

The best option is to rent a car in Ridgecrest or bring your own vehicle. Due to the natural beauty and distance of attractions, a vehicle would provide the most freedom of exploration.

Where to Go Next from Ridgecrest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ridgecrest is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding San Joaquin Valley and nearby destinations. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Sequoia National Forest: About 2 hours eastward, perfect for a weekend camping trip.
  • Mammoth Lakes: Located in the Eastern Sierras, about 1 hour north).
  • Bishop: A small town located approximately 1 hour and 10 minutes north of Ridgecrest, known for its stunning scenery.

Conclusion

Ridgecrest is a hidden gem in California and offers unique experiences. From natural beauty to cultural attractions, there’s something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit, and enjoy all that Ridgecrest has to offer.
