Introduction to Delano

Located in the heart of the San Joaquin Valley in Kern County, California, Delano is a diverse and vibrant city known for its rich history, breathtaking natural scenery, and welcoming locals. This charming city is home to a variety of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all interests.

Whether you’re planning a quick weekend getaway or a more extended trip, this comprehensive tour guide will help you discover the best of Delano. From top attractions and activities to accommodations and travel tips, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Delano

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the rich history and culture of Delano, there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore:

  • Cesar E. Chavez National Monument: This national monument commemorates the life and legacy of civil rights leader Cesar E. Chavez, who fought for the rights of farm workers in California’s Central Valley. The site includes a visitor center, memorial garden, and Chavez’s gravesite.

  • Delano Historic District: This area is home to several historic landmarks and buildings, including the 1913 Delano Post Office and the 1921 Southern Pacific Railroad Depot.

  • Delano Heritage Park: This park features a historical museum dedicated to the history of Delano and Kern County, as well as a restored 1920s train depot and a 1930s oil derrick.

Natural Attractions of Delano: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to immerse yourself in the breathtaking natural beauty of Delano, there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic locations to explore:

  • Sequoia National Forest: Located just a short drive from Delano, this sprawling forest is home to some of the tallest trees in the world, as well as scenic hiking trails, fishing streams, and camping sites.

  • Lake Woollomes Recreation Area: This picturesque lake is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and picnicking. The surrounding area also features hiking trails and scenic views of the nearby mountains.

  • Tule Elk State Reserve: This wildlife reserve is home to a large population of tule elk, a species of elk found only in California. Visitors can observe the elk from a distance and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delano

With its stunning natural scenery and mild climate, Delano is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Kern River Parkway Trail: This paved trail runs along the banks of the Kern River and offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and wildlife. The trail is open to hikers, bikers, and equestrians.

  • Lake Woollomes Trail: This scenic trail follows the perimeter of Lake Woollomes and offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

  • Sequoia National Forest Trails: The Sequoia National Forest features a variety of hiking trails for all skill levels, ranging from short walks to challenging multi-day backpacking trips. Popular hikes include the Trail of 100 Giants and the Pacific Crest Trail.

Best Things to Do in Delano: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, there is no shortage of activities to enjoy in Delano. Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

  • Take a tour of local wineries: Delano is located in the heart of California’s wine country, and there are plenty of wineries in the surrounding area to explore. Some popular options include Bakersfield Country Wine Tours and South Coast Winery.

  • Visit the Delano Farmers Market: Held on Saturday mornings, this lively farmers market features fresh produce, homemade crafts, live music, and food trucks.

  • Go on a wildlife safari: The nearby California Living Museum offers a unique opportunity to see some of California’s native wildlife up close, including mountain lions, coyotes, and bald eagles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delano

If you’re planning a longer trip to Delano, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Delano and check into your accommodations. Take a stroll through the Delano Historic District and visit the Delano Heritage Park.

  • Day 2: Explore the Sequoia National Forest with a hiking or biking excursion.

  • Day 3: Take a tour of local wineries and enjoy a vineyard picnic lunch.

  • Day 4: Visit the Cesar E. Chavez National Monument and learn about the life and legacy of this civil rights leader.

  • Day 5: Learn about the local agriculture industry with a tour of Delano’s farms and orchards.

  • Day 6: Go on a wildlife safari at the California Living Museum.

  • Day 7: Finish up your trip with a relaxing day at Lake Woollomes Recreation Area, complete with kayaking and picnicking.

Food and Drink Scene of Delano

No visit to Delano would be complete without sampling the delicious local cuisine and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: These traditional Mexican-style corn husk-wrapped tamales are a local favorite, often filled with pork, beef, or cheese and served with spicy salsa.

  • Basque cuisine: Delano has a rich Basque heritage, and many local restaurants serve up traditional Basque dishes like lamb stew, paella, and pintxos.

  • Fresh fruit: Delano is located in California’s Central Valley, one of the most fertile agricultural regions in the world. Sample the locally-grown fruit, like strawberries, oranges, and grapes, in a fruit salad or smoothie.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The nearby Central California Wine Country is home to some of the best wineries in the world, producing varietals like cabernet sauvignon, chardonnay, and zinfandel.

  • Craft beer: The local craft beer scene is booming in Delano, with several breweries in the area offering unique brews and live entertainment.

  • Agua fresca: This refreshing Mexican-style fruit drink is popular in Delano and comes in a variety of flavors, like watermelon, cucumber, and lime.

Where to Stay in Delano: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, Delano has a variety of accommodations to choose from. Here are some top options:

  • Best Western Liberty Inn: This comfortable hotel is located in the heart of downtown Delano and features free Wi-Fi, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.

  • Holiday Inn Express Delano: This modern hotel offers free breakfast, an outdoor pool, and a business center.

  • Delano Motel: This budget-friendly motel is centrally located and includes free Wi-Fi, cable TV, and a mini-fridge in each room.

What to Buy in Delano: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home from your trip? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Hand-picked fruit: Take home some of the fresh, locally-grown fruit that Delano is known for, like oranges, grapes, and strawberries.

  • Artisanal crafts: Delano has a thriving arts and crafts scene, with many talented artisans creating unique jewelry, ceramics, and textiles.

  • Wine: Bring home a bottle (or two) of California wine to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Delano

Delano is a diverse and welcoming community that celebrates its rich cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Language: Spanish is widely spoken in Delano, so it’s helpful to know a few common phrases if you’re unfamiliar with the language.

  • Religion: Delano is home to many different faiths and religions, including Catholicism, Christianity, Islam, and Judaism. Visitors should be respectful of religious customs and traditions.

  • Etiquette: It’s considered polite to greet people with a smile and a handshake in Delano.

Best Time to Visit Delano

The best time to visit Delano is during the spring or fall, when the temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant. Summer can be hot and dry, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delano

  • Do respect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Delano

Delano is generally a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in public places
  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Be aware of your surroundings
  • Follow local laws and regulations

Health in Delano

Delano has several medical facilities and pharmacies to handle any health needs you may have during your trip. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you and to have travel health insurance in case of an emergency.

Getting to Delano: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Delano

The nearest airport to Delano is Meadows Field Airport in Bakersfield, which is about 21 miles away. Other nearby airports include Fresno Yosemite International Airport and Los Angeles International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Delano

The Greyhound bus station in Delano provides transportation to destinations throughout California and the United States.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to rent a car to explore the surrounding area, as there is limited public transportation in Delano. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

How to Get Around in Delano

While much of Delano is walkable, visitors may find it helpful to have a car to explore the surrounding area. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, as is the local bus system.

Where to Go Next from Delano: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your California adventure after your visit to Delano, consider these nearby destinations:

  • Bakersfield: Located just 30 minutes south of Delano, Bakersfield is a vibrant city with a thriving music and arts scene.

  • Visalia: This charming city is located about an hour northwest of Delano and offers easy access to the majestic Sequoia National Park.

  • Los Angeles: The bustling metropolis of Los Angeles is about two hours south of Delano and offers endless opportunities for entertainment and adventure.

Conclusion

No matter what your interests, Delano has something to offer every traveler. With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Delano is an ideal destination for a California getaway. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this beautiful city.
