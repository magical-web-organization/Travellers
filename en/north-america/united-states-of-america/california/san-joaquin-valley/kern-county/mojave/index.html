Introduction to Mojave

Mojave is a picturesque desert town located in Kern County, in San Joaquin Valley, California, in United States of America. Known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and clear blue skies, Mojave is a popular destination for travelers who are looking for a peaceful escape from the city’s hustle and bustle.

This guide will take you through everything you need to know about Mojave, including the best places to visit, must-try dishes, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Mojave

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mojave Air and Space Port: This world-famous airport is home to many aviation-related companies and the famous Mojave Spaceport. You can learn about the history of aviation and space exploration, and even witness a rocket launch.

  • The Mojave Desert Heritage and Cultural Association Museum: The museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the Mojave Desert. You can learn about the Native American tribes, early explorers, and the wildlife of the region.

  • Tropico Gold Mine: Take a guided tour of the Tropico Gold Mine to experience the history and significance of the mining industry in California.

Natural Attractions of Mojave: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Red Rock Canyon State Park: Red Rock Canyon is a must-visit natural attraction in Mojave. The colorful rock formations, desert wildlife, and stunning vistas make for an unforgettable experience.

  • Jawbone Canyon OHV Area: The Jawbone Canyon OHV Area is a popular destination for off-road vehicles. You can explore the vast network of trails, rock formations, and sand dunes.

  • Wind Wolves Preserve: The Wind Wolves Preserve is the largest nonprofit nature preserve on the West Coast. It boasts over 93,000 acres of land, home to a variety of rare and native species such as Tule elk, California condor, and long-eared owl.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mojave

Mojave is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The following are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Mojave:

  • Horse Canyon Trail: This trail is a moderate 5.6-mile hike through the beautiful Horse Canyon.

  • Pacific Crest Trail: The Pacific Crest Trail offers a challenging 400-mile hike through the high desert and mountain ranges.

  • Tehachapi Mountain Trail: The trail winds through the woods and offers panoramic views of the valley.

Best Things to Do in Mojave: Activities and Tours

  • Skydive Mojave: Skydive Mojave offers an experience of a lifetime. You can jump out of an airplane and experience freefall with an instructor.

  • Mountain Valley Express: Mountain Valley Express offers tours of Mojave’s most scenic routes on a specially designed motorcycle.

  • Petroglyph Tours: Take a tour of the ancient Petroglyphs situated in the Southern Sierra Nevada mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mojave

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Mojave and check-in at your hotel. Spend the day exploring the city’s downtown area and try some of the local cuisine.
2 Take a tour of the Mojave Air and Space Port and witness a rocket launch.
3 Spend the day at Wind Wolves Preserve and explore the beautiful landscape.
4 Go on a guided tour of the Tropico Gold Mine and learn about the gold mining industry in California.
5 Hike up the Tehachapi Mountain Trail and enjoy the stunning views from the top.
6 Spend the day at Red Rock Canyon State Park and explore the beautiful rock formations and wildlife.
7 Depart from Mojave.

Food and Drink Scene of Mojave

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tri-tip sandwich: This classic California-style sandwich features perfectly cooked tri-tip beef with a variety of toppings.

  • Mexican-style tacos: Try the authentic tacos from local food trucks, such as el pastor and carne asada.

  • Burgers: You will find some of the best burgers at local diners such as The Goose.

Local Beverages

  • Local brewery: Check out the Indian Wells Brewing Company, a local brewery that serves a variety of handcrafted beer.

  • Wine: There are several wineries a short drive from Mojave. Head to the Antelope Valley Winery for a tasting.

Where to Stay in Mojave: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn & Suites: This hotel is located just off the highway and offers a comfortable and convenient stay.

  • Motel 6: A budget-friendly motel located in the heart of the city.

  • Best Western Desert Winds: Located in the middle of the city, this hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Mojave: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Petroglyph art: Buy some beautiful prints of the ancient rock carvings found in the area.

  • Antiques: You will find several antique shops in the downtown area, selling vintage and unique items.

  • Local handmade crafts: Check out local artisan shops for handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Mojave

Mojave has a rich history and culture. The town was originally inhabited by the Kawaiisu Native American tribe. You will find many cultural events and festivals throughout the year, celebrating the traditions and customs of the region.

Best Time to Visit Mojave

The best time to visit Mojave is between March to May and September to November. The temperature during these months is mild, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mojave

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Be mindful of the weather and carry enough water and sun protection.
  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the culture.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or light fires in the open desert.
  • Litter or disturb the environment.
  • Pick up or remove any natural resources such as rocks or fossils.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mojave

Mojave is a relatively safe place, but it’s always better to be cautious and take the necessary precautions. For outdoor activities, be mindful of the wildlife and carry enough water and provisions. For personal safety, avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Mojave

The high temperatures in Mojave can cause heat exhaustion, and dehydration. It’s essential to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Mojave: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mojave

The nearest airport to Mojave is Meadows Field Airport (BFL), located about 65 miles away. You can also fly into Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) and drive to Mojave in about two hours.

Bus Stations Near Mojave

The Greyhound Bus Station in Lancaster is the closest bus station, about 25 miles from Mojave.

Other Options

You can drive to Mojave from major cities in California such as Los Angeles, San Francisco, and San Diego.

How to Get Around in Mojave

The best way to get around Mojave is by car. You can rent a car at the airport or in the city center. There are also several bike rental shops in the city, which is a great way to explore the surrounding natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Mojave: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Death Valley National Park: Head to one of the most famous national parks in the country, and explore the beauty of the desert.

  • Las Vegas, Nevada: Las Vegas is just a few hours’ drive from Mojave. You can explore the vibrant city, take in a show, or try your luck at the casinos.

Conclusion

Mojave is a beautiful and unique destination in California, perfect for those who love outdoor activities and cultural experiences. Whether you’re interested in aviation, history, or nature, there’s something for you in Mojave. Use this guide to plan your trip and explore all that Mojave has to offer.
