Introduction to Keene

Keene, located in Kern County in the San Joaquin Valley of California, is a small town with a lot to offer visitors. Surrounded by natural beauty and rich cultural history, Keene is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a peaceful and adventurous vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Keene

If you’re planning a trip to Keene, here are some places you don’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cesar E. Chavez National Monument: This monument is dedicated to the life and work of civil rights activist and labor leader Cesar E. Chavez. You can take a guided tour of his home and the monument to learn about his legacy.
  • Tehachapi Depot Museum: This museum houses exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of the Tehachapi Valley Railroad and the town’s history.
  • Kawaiisu Native American Museum: This museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the history and culture of the Kawaiisu people who have lived in the area for thousands of years.

Natural Attractions of Keene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wind Wolves Preserve: This 93,000-acre preserve offers hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and expansive views. You can also take a guided tour or attend a special event.
  • Tomo-Kahni State Historic Park: This park features guided tours of a Kawaiisu Native American village site, which has evidence of human habitation dating back over 8,000 years.
  • Tehachapi Mountain Park: This park offers hiking trails with panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keene

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love the hiking and biking options in Keene. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Tehachapi Mountain Trail: This 5.5-mile trail offers views of the surrounding mountains and the valley below.
  • Wind Wolves Preserve Trails: The preserve offers several hiking trails ranging in length from 0.6 to 8.2 miles. The paths take you through grasslands, forests, and past waterfalls.
  • Loop the Lake Trail: This scenic 4.4-mile hike takes you around the beautiful Buena Vista Lake.

Best Things to Do in Keene: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural beauty and cultural attractions, Keene also offers several fun activities and tours:

  • Horseback Riding: Several companies offer guided horseback riding tours through the mountains or the Wind Wolves Preserve.
  • Wine Tasting: The area around Keene is home to several vineyards and wineries that offer tastings and tours. Some notable ones include Triassic Vineyards and Mojave Desert Spring.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take to the skies and get a bird’s eye view of the beautiful countryside with a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keene

If you’re planning to spend a week in Keene, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1: Visit Wind Wolves Preserve and take a guided hike

Day 2: Explore the Cesar E. Chavez National Monument

Day 3: Take a horseback riding tour of the mountains

Day 4: Visit the Tehachapi Depot Museum and Kawaiisu Native American Museum

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride

Day 6: Sample local wines with a vineyard tour

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views at Tehachapi Mountain Park

Food and Drink Scene of Keene

Keene may be a small town, but it offers some great dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tri-tip: This tender cut of beef is a local specialty. You can find it on many restaurant menus in Keene.
  • Basque cuisine: Keene has a rich Basque heritage, and you can try traditional dishes like chorizo, oxtail stew, and lamb.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Kern River Brewing Company is a local favorite with a variety of brews, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Wine: Several vineyards and wineries in the area produce excellent wines. Don’t miss the opportunity to try a local varietal.

Where to Stay in Keene: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Keene may be small, but it has a few great options for lodging. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a cozy bed and breakfast, here are some suggestions:

  • Tehachapi Mountain Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in the mountains and offers beautiful views and comfortable accommodations.
  • Bakersfield Marriott at the Convention Center: This centrally located hotel has modern amenities and easy access to Keene and surrounding areas.
  • Hamilton Ranch Retreat: If you’re looking for a more rustic experience, this guest ranch offers cabins, horseback riding, and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Keene: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to pick up some souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Keene. Here are some ideas:

  • Tri-tip seasoning: Pick up a jar of seasoning blend that’s perfect for grilling tri-tip.
  • Basque ciders: Try a bottle of local Basque cider for a taste of the area’s culinary heritage.
  • Native American crafts: The Kawaiisu Native American Museum has a gift shop with handmade crafts and artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Keene

Keene has a rich cultural history, which is evident in the museums and historic sites around town. It’s also a close-knit community with a strong appreciation for nature and the outdoors. When visiting Keene, be sure to respect the local customs and take in the natural beauty of the area.

Best Time to Visit Keene

The best time to visit Keene is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot, with temperatures exceeding 100 degrees Fahrenheit, and winter can bring snow and icy conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keene

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace when hiking or camping.
  • Visit the local museums and historic sites to learn about the area’s rich cultural history.
  • Try the local cuisine, including tri-tip and Basque dishes.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the natural areas around Keene.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Speed on the windy mountain roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keene

Keene is a safe town with low crime rates. When hiking or exploring the outdoors, be sure to bring plenty of water and sunscreen, and watch out for wildlife.

Health in Keene

Keene has several medical clinics and hospitals, including a Kaiser Permanente facility. However, it’s important to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you when traveling.

Getting to Keene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keene

The nearest major airport to Keene is Los Angeles International Airport (LAX), which is approximately 120 miles away. There is also a small airport in nearby Bakersfield.

Bus Stations Near Keene

Greyhound and several other bus companies serve nearby Bakersfield.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around the area, and there are several rental car companies available in Bakersfield.

How to Get Around in Keene

Walking or biking around town is easy and enjoyable. If you’re planning to explore outside of town, a car or organized tour is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Keene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of California, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Los Angeles: This sprawling city is approximately 120 miles away and has endless entertainment options.
  • Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Park: These beautiful parks are just a few hours’ drive from Keene and offer incredible scenery and outdoor adventures.
  • Death Valley National Park: This unique national park is approximately 4 hours’ drive from Keene and is home to some of the most extreme environments in the United States.

Conclusion

Keene, California, offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or local cuisine, you’ll find something to love in this small town. Plan your trip today and experience all that Keene has to offer!
