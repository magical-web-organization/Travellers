Introduction to Wasco

Nestled in San Joaquin Valley, Wasco is a city in Kern County, California, known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, and vibrant cultural scene. The city boasts a variety of historical and cultural attractions, as well as scenic vistas and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, exploring museums and historical sites, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Wasco.

List of Places to Visit in Wasco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wasco Historical Society: Discover the rich history of the area through exhibits, artifacts, and memorabilia at this museum, located downtown.
  • Buena Vista Museum of Natural History: Explore the natural history of Central California and beyond through interactive exhibits and a planetarium at this museum in nearby Bakersfield.
  • Kern County Museum: Learn about Kern County’s unique history and culture through exhibits, demonstrations, and a collection of historic buildings at this museum in Bakersfield.

Natural Attractions of Wasco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bowles Canyon: Hike and explore the rugged terrain or take in breathtaking views of the surrounding valley from this picturesque natural area.
  • Lake Woollomes: Enjoy fishing, boating, and picnicking at this scenic lake.
  • The Wildlands Conservancy - Wind Wolves Preserve: This 93,000-acre plateau boasts an array of outdoor adventure opportunities. Hiking, biking, and wildlife watching are all popular activities here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wasco

  • The Ridge Route Trail: This 16.8-mile trail winds through the foothills of the Diablo Range offering stunning views at the 1,735 summit of Sawmill Mountain.
  • Wind Wolves Preserve: With over 25 miles of trails, the preserve offers an array of hiking options, from the easy two-mile Springhill Trail, to the 13-mile-long Tule Elk Trail.
  • Mossy Falls Trail: Located in the nearby Sequoia National Forest, this picturesque trail takes you through old-growth redwood forests to a stunning 30-foot waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Wasco: Activities and Tours

  • Wasco Farmers Market: Enjoy fresh produce, artisanal crafts, live music, and more at this vibrant seasonal market from late May to early November.
  • Grapevine Canyon Ranch: Experience the Old West as you explore this historic ranch on horseback, go on a cattle drive or take a nature walk through the hills.

Food and Drink Scene of Wasco

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carne Asada Fries: A beloved local delicacy, these fries are topped with juicy carne asada, guacamole, sour cream, and cheese.
  • Pistachios: The region is famous for its pistachio farms, and you can find a variety of tasty treats made from these locally grown nuts, such as pistachio ice cream and roasted pistachios.
  • Tri-Tip Sandwich: This popular sandwich features tender slices of tri-tip steak, often paired with onion rings and barbecue sauce on a fresh roll.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Wasco is home to TDE Brewing, a brewery that offers a range of hand-crafted beers, from IPAs to sours.
  • Wine: The region’s wineries produce a variety of award-winning wines, including Cabernet Sauvignon, Chardonnay, and Zinfandel.
  • Juice: Kern County is the largest almond and pistachio producing region in the world, so it’s no surprise that you can find delicious freshly squeezed juices made from these nuts.

Where to Stay in Wasco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Wasco Inn: A well-regarded budget hotel offering a swimming pool, free continental breakfast and shuttle service to and from the airport.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham: A pet-friendly, upscale hotel with spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.
  • 49er Motel: A quirky, retro-themed motel with clean and comfortable rooms, a picnic area, and a BBQ grill.

What to Buy in Wasco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pistachios: Take home some of the region’s most famous food products, such as locally grown pistachios in a variety of flavors, from salted and roasted to garlic and chili.
  • Memorabilia from Wasco Historic Society: Locally-made unique souvenirs, featuring historic photos and artifacts to commemorate your trip.
  • Wine from TDE Brewing: Unique and locally-produced wines from the Wasco wine region, available for purchase at the on-site tasting room.

Local Culture and Customs of Wasco

  • Wasco is a predominantly agricultural area, so it is always advisable to learn some local terms, customs, and etiquettes before visiting.
  • Rodeos and livestock auctions are common events, especially later in the season.
  • Be sure to treat the area’s natural resources with respect and care by packing out trash and staying on designated trails.

Best Time to Visit Wasco

The best time to visit Wasco is from March through May or September through November when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, there is plenty to do all year round, whether it is attending the annual rodeo, exploring the natural beauty in the surrounding area, or enjoying the cooler weather of winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wasco

Do:

  • Visit the Farmers Market
  • Respect the local culture and etiquettes
  • Explore the natural trails and preserves

Don’t:

  • Throw trash or litter on the trails and other natural resources
  • Ignore the cultural practices and etiquettes
  • Miss exploring the Sequoia National Forest nearby.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wasco

Generally, Wasco is a safe place to visit, but it is advisable to take common precautions such as knowing your surroundings, having a map or GPS available and not leaving your belongings unattended.

Health in Wasco

COVID-19 is still a major health concern, and visitors should take appropriate precautions and follow CDC guidelines by wearing masks, washing hands frequently, and maintaining social distancing as much as possible.

Getting to Wasco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wasco

The nearest airports to Wasco are Meadows Field Airport (BFL), which is approximately 23 miles away, and Los Angeles International Airport (LAX), which is approximately 125 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Wasco

Greyhound Bus Lines is the only available bus line that runs through Wasco, and the nearest station to the city is located approximately 20 miles away in Bakersfield.

Other Options

  • For those preferring to drive, Wasco is located right off of the I-5 freeway, making it an easy destination to access by car.
  • Taxis and ride share services are also available in Wasco.

How to Get Around in Wasco

The easiest way to get around Wasco is by car since the city is relatively small and many of its attractions are located outside of its downtown core. However, visitors can also choose to bike or walk around the city if they prefer.

Where to Go Next from Wasco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bakersfield: Just a short drive from Wasco, like many towns of San Joaquin Valley. The city offers various attractions such as art museums, theaters and shopping centers, popular food and drink places, and historical sites.
  • Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks: These picturesque parks located in Sierra Nevada mountain range could make for an enjoyable weekend outing or overnight stay.
  • Los Angeles: A few hours away, it is the perfect weekend getaway from Wasco offering an array of attractions, cultural experiences, food and drink options, and sightseeing opportunities.

Conclusion

Wasco is an exciting and unique place to visit. With its historic sites, outdoor adventures, and unique dining scene, the city offers something for everyone. Hopefully, this comprehensive travel guide serves as a useful resource to anyone planning their next trip to Wasco.
