Chowchilla, a small city located in the heart of San Joaquin Valley in California, is definitely not the most popular tourist destination, but it has a unique charm that makes it worth a visit. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, and great food and drinks, Chowchilla has everything you need for an enjoyable trip. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the different attractions, activities, food, and insider tips on enjoying your trip to Chowchilla.

Introduction to Chowchilla

Chowchilla is a charming city in California with a rich history and a nice blend of tradition and modernity. The city is known for its friendly people, welcoming culture, and diverse attractions. The city has a population of approximately 20,000 people and sits in Madera County, about 40 miles north of Fresno and 15 miles west of Merced. Chowchilla is also only a few hours from other popular destinations such as Yosemite National Park and San Francisco.

List of Places to Visit in Chowchilla

Chowchilla may be a small city, but it has plenty of attractions for tourists to explore. From museums and historical landmarks to natural wonders, here are some of the must-visit places in Chowchilla:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chowchilla Historical Museum: The Chowchilla Historical Museum is a great place to learn about the city’s history and culture. The museum has a collection of artifacts, photographs, and other memorabilia that tell the story of Chowchilla from its early beginnings to the present day.

  • Fossil Discovery Center of Madera County: This museum is a great place to learn about prehistoric creatures that once lived in the area including the largest land animal ever discovered: the Indricotherium. You can also see fossils and interactive exhibits that teach about the geological history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Chowchilla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chowchilla River Wetlands: The Chowchilla River Wetlands is a beautiful natural attraction in the city. The wetlands offers opportunities for bird watching, hiking, fishing, and picnicking. The wetlands are home to many species of birds and other animals, making it a great place to take nature walks.

  • Fossil Discovery Center Trail and Wetlands: Take a stroll or bike ride on the well-maintained trail and stop by the wetlands and museum mentioned above. The Fossil Discovery Center Trail and Wetlands provides stunning vistas of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chowchilla

Chowchilla’s natural beauty is best seen through its hiking and biking trails. Whether you are a beginner or a seasoned hiker, these trails offer great views of the stunning landscape near Chowchilla. Here are some of the popular hiking/biking routes:

  • Chowchilla Parkway Bike Trail: It is a flat 7.5-mile trail perfect for biking and walking alike. The bike trail is lined with trees and connects Chowchilla to the nearby cities of Fairmead and Madera.

  • Fossil Discovery Center Trail: This trail is a flat and easy 1.5-mile loop, suitable for all ages and abilities. You can take a leisurely stroll and take in the scenic views of the surrounding hills.

  • Gabilan Trail: This trail offers a diverse landscape complete with oak tree canopies, rocky outcroppings, and a seasonal creek lined with cottonwood trees. It is a moderate hike, gaining 700 feet in elevation over 5 miles.

Best Things to Do in Chowchilla: Activities and Tours

Chowchilla offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do when in Chowchilla:

  • Chowchilla Speedway: The Chowchilla Speedway is a popular venue for racing enthusiasts. It hosts races during the weekends in the summer, attracting both locals and tourists.

  • Gilliard Farms: Gilliard Farms offers a variety of experiences for agricultural enthusiasts ranging from harvesting different crops, flower picking, and seasonal events. They offer tours throughout the day, and reservations are required.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chowchilla

Chowchilla is a relatively small city, and visitors can explore its main attractions within a few days. However, if you want to get the full experience, here’s a week-long itinerary you can follow:

Day Activity
1 Explore Chowchilla River Wetlands

Food and Drink Scene of Chowchilla

Chowchilla has a diverse food scene, with various options that cater to different tastes. Here’s a rundown of the most popular food and drinks in Chowchilla:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burger and Fries @ Mike’s Grill: One of the most popular spots in Chowchilla is Mike’s Grill, a family-owned restaurant known for their juicy burgers, fries, and sandwiches.

  • Indian Curry @ Green Olive Restaurant: If you’re looking for more international cuisine, Green Olive Restaurant serves delicious Indian curries and a full assortment of curry variations, all prepared fresh and served hot.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee @ The Morning Star Coffee House & Roastery: The Morning Star Coffee House and Roastery is a popular cafe known for roasting their beans in-house. They offer a range of beverages, such as coffee, cappuccino, and iced coffees.

  • Craft Beer @ Fasi Estate Winery: Fasi Estate Winery is a local winery that produces artisanal wines and craft beers. The winery offers free tastings of its products and has a beautiful setting perfect for a relaxing afternoon.

Where to Stay in Chowchilla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chowchilla has a decent selection of accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hotels to high-end resorts. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay in Chowchilla:

  • Days Inn by Wyndham Chowchilla Gateway to Yosemite: Centrally located and offers clean and spacious rooms with all basic amenities, including a free breakfast.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Chowchilla - Yosemite Park Area: A modern hotel offers amenities such as an outdoor pool, hot tub, fitness center, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Chowchilla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chowchilla has a few places where you can buy souvenirs or local products, such as the Chowchilla Historical Museum gift shop, and the Gilliard Farms store. Here are some of the popular souvenirs and local products you can bring home:

  • Wine: Fasi Estate Winery offers wine under its label along with local wines of the Sierra foothills and the Central Valley region.

  • Petite Jeanne Popcorn: This quirky small business sells different flavors of popcorn.

Local Culture and Customs of Chowchilla

While Chowchilla is a small city, it has a rich culture and tradition that visitors should appreciate. The city has a distinct agricultural identity, with many people working in the farming industry. The community is relatively close-knit, and visitors may notice people generally take a friendly and welcoming attitude towards one another.

Best Time to Visit Chowchilla

Chowchilla has a Mediterranean climate with hot summers and mild winters. The best time to visit Chowchilla is in the spring or fall, when the temperatures are mild, and you can enjoy the city without the sweltering heat of the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chowchilla

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Hike or bike on the trails
  • Visit the local museums and historical landmarks

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Trespass on private land or farms
  • Disturb the natural habitats or wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Chowchilla

Chowchilla is a generally safe city, but it is always a good idea to exercise caution, particularly at night. Visitors should stick to well-lit areas and avoid leaving valuables in their cars.

Health in Chowchilla

The health and safety of visitors are typically not a cause for concern. However, it is always a good idea to carry sunscreen and wear appropriate clothing for the weather. In case of medical emergencies, visitors can access healthcare from Mercy Medical Center of Merced and Madera Community Hospital.

Getting to Chowchilla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chowchilla can be accessed through different means of transport. Here are some of the ways you can get to Chowchilla:

Airports Near Chowchilla

The closest airport is Merced Regional Airport (MCE). However, in terms of flights and routes available, Fresno Yosemite International Airport (FAT) is a better option.

Bus Stations Near Chowchilla

Greyhound and other bus lines have services serving from Fresno to Merced and vice versa. The closest stop to Chowchilla is located in Merced.

Other Options

Travelers who want more privacy can hire a car and drive to Chowchilla using one of the many state highways.

How to Get Around in Chowchilla

The best way to get around Chowchilla is by car. There are a few taxi services available, but you may need to call them in advance. Rental cars are also available at Fresno Yosemite International Airport.

Where to Go Next from Chowchilla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have an extra day or two, there are a few nearby destinations you can explore from Chowchilla:

  • Yosemite National Park
  • Fresno, CA
  • Merced, CA
  • Sonoma County

Conclusion

Chowchilla may not be among the top tourist destinations in California, but it has its charms. From the natural wonders and museums to the friendly people, visitors can enjoy an unforgettable trip to this small city. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you can make the most out of your trip to Chowchilla. Happy travels!
