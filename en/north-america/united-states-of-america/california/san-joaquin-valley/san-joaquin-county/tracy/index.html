Introduction to Tracy

Located in the lush San Joaquin Valley of California, Tracy is a hidden gem that lures travelers for its beautiful landscape, vibrant culture, and friendly locals. Witness the mighty delta, wander through historical landmarks, or take a relaxing stroll in green parks - Tracy has something to offer to every visitor.

List of Places to Visit in Tracy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tracy Historical Museum: Here you can delve into the history of Tracy, from its ranching and railroad days to the present. A must-visit place for history buffs!
  • Lolly Hansen Senior Center Museum: The museum displays a wide range of historical artifacts that depict Tracy’s culture, arts, and people from the previous decades.
  • AgVenture Tours: Discover Tracy’s agriculture heritage and enjoy a guided farm tour with your family and friends.
  • Tracy Transit Center: The station is a historical landmark that was once a mission-style train station built in 1913; the station currently serves as an intermodal transit center for San Joaquin Regional Transit District.

Natural Attractions of Tracy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tracy Hills Open Space: Enjoy hiking, biking, or horseback riding across the beautiful hills while taking in stunning views of the valley below.
  • Tracy Fishery: A perfect place for fishing, Tracy Fishery is a man-made lake that offers fishing for catfish, trout, and bass.
  • Tracy Oasis Marina Resort: A paradise for watersports enthusiasts, the resort offers boating, sailing, jet skiing, and kayaking adventures.
  • Galaxy Theatre: This outdoor theater hosts open-air performances for the audience to enjoy the dramatic sunset views while watching a live show.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tracy

Tracy offers a plethora of exciting routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

  • Tracy Hills Trail System: The trail system boasts many routes that offer picturesque views of the valley and oak groves; these trails offer an excellent opportunity to hike and mountain bike.
  • Delta De Anza Regional Trail: The trail stretches 17 miles from Stockton to Pittsburg, featuring abundant scenic beauty, including stunning wetlands, farmlands, and riverbanks.

Best Things to Do in Tracy: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Tuolumne River: Kayak, raft, or paddleboard your way through the scenic Tuolumne River.
  • Wente Vineyards: Experience the taste of fine wine and dine at the Winery Restaurant.
  • OwlBox Bakery: Stop by the bakery to grab some delicious bread, pastries, cakes, and more!

Food and Drink Scene of Tracy

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asparagus: Asparagus is part of Tracy’s culture and history; indulge in this fresh and tasty vegetable dish in many local restaurants.
  • Cherry Pie: Grab a slice of cherry pie, and indulge in the freshest local cherries in town.
  • BBQ: There are many BBQ restaurants in Tracy that serve delicious BBQ dishes with unique sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Wente Winery is known for its fine quality of wine; enjoy tastings and dine in the Winery Restaurant.
  • Beer: Head to Tracy BrewFest to experience the region’s finest craft breweries.

Where to Stay in Tracy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Tracy: A few minutes away from the freeway, the hotel offers catered breakfast and other amenities.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: The hotel is close to the freeway and offers an outdoor pool and complimentary breakfast service.
  • Motel 6: The motel offers a friendly atmosphere, free Wi-Fi, and laundry services.

What to Buy in Tracy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cherries: Take home bags of cherries freshly picked from local farms.
  • Asparagus: Take home some of the green goodness and enjoy a healthy meal back home.
  • Lolly Hansen Senior Center Logo Apparel: Here you can buy apparel that bears the logo of Lolly Hansen Senior Center Museum — designed by Tracy residents.

Local Culture and Customs of Tracy

Tracy celebrates its culture in many ways; the city has an all year round calendar of events. Buckaroo Breakfast, Fourth of July Parade, Taste of the Valley, Men’s Grand Masters Softball Tournaments, and Tracy Dry Bean Festival are some of the major cultural events that the locals celebrate.

Best Time to Visit Tracy

Tracy’s weather is favorable throughout the year. During spring and summer, the valley blooms, which makes it the best time for outdoor activities. However, if you’re after discounted hotels and a quieter atmosphere, winter can be the best time for you to visit Tracy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tracy

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Engage in outdoor activities and explore the beauty of Tracy.
  • Visit the local farmer’s market and support the local vendors.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Do not park your car anywhere except designated areas.
  • Do not over speed while driving.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tracy

Tracy is a safe city; however, it’s always better to be careful and aware of one’s surroundings. Be sure to lock your car and bike, and keep valuables out of sight. Be cautious while traversing the natural surroundings and avoid venturing into unsafe areas.

Health in Tracy

The city provides quality medical care services and has a well-maintained network of hospitals and medical centers.

Getting to Tracy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tracy

Two of the nearest major airports near Tracy are:

  • Stockton Metropolitan Airport
  • Oakland International Airport

Bus Stations Near Tracy

  • Tracy Transit Station
  • Victory Park & Ride
  • West Valley Mall
  • Lathrop/Manteca Station

Other Options

The most popular transportation option to reach Tracy is driving via I-5 and I-205. If you’re traveling from San Francisco, the estimated travel time is around an hour and 30 minutes.

How to Get Around in Tracy

The best way to get around Tracy is by driving or renting a car. Tracy is a car-oriented city, but you can also use public transportation for getting around the city.

Where to Go Next from Tracy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Francisco: Absorb the compelling attractions, scenic beauty, and cultural activities of San Francisco.
  • Yosemite National Park: Explore the majestic beauty of nearby Yosemite National Park.

Conclusion

Tracy is an all-year-round destination that offers something for everyone, from scenic outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and delightful food scenes. Plan a trip and experience the beautiful valley of Tracy!
