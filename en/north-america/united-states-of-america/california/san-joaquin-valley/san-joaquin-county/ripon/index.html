Introduction to Ripon

Nestled in San Joaquin County, in San Joaquin Valley, California, is the small and charming city of Ripon. With a population of just over 16,000, this city offers visitors a peaceful and relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of larger cities. Ripon embraces its agricultural roots, evident in its scenic countryside, numerous orchards, and vineyards dispersed throughout the city. In addition, it offers plenty of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and local cuisine that make it a top choice for tourists. This travel guide provides comprehensive information to help you plan your next trip to Ripon, California.

List of Places to Visit in Ripon

Below are some of the top attractions to visit and things to do in Ripon.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ripon has a rich history dating back to the 1850s, and its cultural heritage is evident through the numerous museums and historical places in the city.

  • Ripon Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of early Ripon and surrounding areas. The exhibits showcase the agricultural legacy of the city, with displays about the farming equipment, local produce, and pioneer life in Ripon.
  • Birthplace of John Muir: Located in Fruit Colony Park, this museum commemorates the birthplace of John Muir, the famous naturalist, and preservationist. The museum showcases John Muir’s life and legacy through interactive exhibits and guided tours.
  • Stouffer Field: This historical location played an important role in WWII as a training facility for Army Air Corps. It is now home to an aviation museum that displays military aircraft from various eras.

Natural Attractions of Ripon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ripon offers plenty of opportunities to enjoy its scenic beauty and indulge in outdoor adventures.

  • Casper’s Wilderness Park: The park features close to 8,000 acres of wilderness, home to deer, mountain lions, coyotes, and hiking and biking trails that take you through the scenic hills.
  • Casper’s Regional Park: This park features picnic areas, fishing areas, and a natural walking trail.
  • Ripon Skate Park: This park is designed for skateboarders and features bowls, ramps and rails for skateboarders to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ripon

The rolling hills of Ripon provide some of the most scenic bike and hiking trails in the valley. Enjoy the outdoors with these top hiking and biking routes in Ripon:

  • Casper’s Wilderness Park Trail: This 5-mile trail offers scenic views of the park and the countryside.
  • River Walk: This path is a 1.5-mile trail that circles Mistlin Lake and follows the Stanislaus River and leads to a picturesque covered bridge.
  • Stanislaus County Bike Trail: This 15-mile trail runs from Modesto to Riverbank and passes through Ripon.

Best Things to Do in Ripon: Activities and Tours

There is something for everyone in Ripon. Check out these top things to do in Ripon:

  • Wine Tasting: Take a scenic drive through one of the many vineyards and wineries in Ripon, meet the locals, and, of course, sample some of the famous wines of the valley.
  • Golfing: The Spring Creek Country Club is a private 18-hole golf course that features a challenging course for golf lovers.
  • Shopping: Main Street in Downtown Ripon has a number of unique shops to explore and find treasures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ripon

Day 1: Start with a visit to the Ripon Historical Society Museum, followed by lunch at one of the local family-owned restaurants. Afterward, take a leisurely stroll through the Casper’s Wilderness Park Trail.

Day 2: Take a day tour of the John Muir National Historic Site, or head to nearby cities of Lodi and Stockton to explore their parks and museums.

Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Mistlin Lake on the River Walk Trail, and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 4: Head to one of the local vineyards for a wine-tasting tour, or try the nearby Spring Creek Country Club for golfing, followed by a drink at the club, overlooking the green fields.

Day 5: Take a leisurely walk through Downtown Ripon and stop at local shops for window shopping. End the day with dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 6: Take a visit to the Stouffer Field Aviation Museum and learn about the city’s role in World War II.

Day 7: Check out the Stanislaus County Bike Trail and enjoy the scenic views and fresh air before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Ripon

The Central Valley is widely known for its fresh produce, and Ripon celebrates this agricultural heritage in every meal. Here are some local treats that you must try on your visit to Ripon:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Almond Brittle: Made from locally-sourced almonds and served in most local shops and restaurants.
  • Asparagus and Artichokes: Ripon is located in the heart of the asparagus and artichoke-producing region, so make sure to enjoy these vegetables in their freshest form.
  • Fruit and Berry Desserts: Fresh seasonal fruits and berries are incorporated into most desserts and sweet dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Numerous vineyards and wineries dot the area, producing exquisite wines that you must try.
  • Beer: Ripon has a vibrant beer culture, with several microbreweries and craft beer options available.

Where to Stay in Ripon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ripon has several accommodations options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: This hotel features spacious rooms with modern amenities, and a bonus breakfast bar for guests.

  • Lavender Inn: A quaint bed and breakfast, located in the countryside of Ripon, this inn provides beautifully decorated rooms and tranquil surroundings.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites: This hotel is suited for both tourists and business travelers, featuring excellent amenities, fitness center, and a pool and sauna for guests.

What to Buy in Ripon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ripon offers a range of unique souvenirs to bring home with you, including local food and drink products, handmade crafts, and local wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Ripon

Ripon is a tight-knit community that values its agricultural heritage and promotes friendliness, warmth, and inclusiveness. It is best to respect the locals and follow local customs when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Ripon

The best time to visit Ripon is during the spring and fall seasons. Temperatures are mild, and the vast orchards and vineyards are in full bloom or in harvest season. Summer months can be hot and dry, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ripon

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Enjoy the fresh produce and local wines.
  • Visit the historical and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in public places.
  • Do not carry firearms or weapons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ripon

Ripon is a generally safe and peaceful city, with low crime rates. However, it is always recommended to take basic precautions such as carrying minimal cash and valuables while wandering in public places.

Health in Ripon

California enjoys a mild climate throughout the year, but it is advisable to wear sunscreen and carry water while exploring the outdoors in Ripon.

Getting to Ripon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ripon

The largest airport near Ripon is Sacramento International Airport, located approximately 67 miles away. Alternatively, Oakland International Airport and San Francisco International Airport are at a couple of hours drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Ripon

Greyhound bus service has a stop in the nearby city of Modesto. It’s just 10 minutes drive to Ripon.

Other Options

Ripon is easily accessible by personal car with Highway 99 running through the city.

How to Get Around in Ripon

The best way to explore Ripon is by car. Car rental is available at the nearby airports and in the city. Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Ripon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ripon is situated in a prime location for exploring Northern California. Here are some of the places you can explore nearby:

  • Yosemite National Park: California’s most iconic National Park, closest to Ripon by about 100 miles.
  • Big Sur: The famous coastline drive, it’s a magnificent drive with ocean views
  • Lake Tahoe: A beautiful lake that is a part of the Sierra mountain range.
