Introduction to Campo

Campo is a small town situated in San Diego County, Southern California. It boasts of a rich history, scenic views, and outdoor recreational activities that make it a desirable destination for tourists. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, sightseeing, or simply getting away from the bustle of the city, Campo can be an ideal retreat. In this guide, we will provide you with all the travel information you need to make the best of your trip to Campo.

List of Places to Visit in Campo

Campo offers many places of interest for tourists. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Campo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pacific Southwest Railway Museum: This is an interactive museum that showcases the history of railways in California. It has an extensive collection of vintage locomotives, boxcars, and cabooses. You can also take a ride in a restored railway car.
  • Campo Stone Store Museum: This museum was once a local store during the gold rush era. Today, it showcases the history of Campo, including exhibits on the Kumeyaay people who lived in the region.

Natural Attractions of Campo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Morena County Park: This park is located in the mountains near Campo and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. You can enjoy hiking, fishing, boating, and camping here.
  • Hauser Wilderness Area: This is a pristine wilderness area that is perfect for hiking, bird-watching, and photography. You can see a variety of flora and fauna, including deer, bobcats, and mountain lions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campo

Campo has many hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular trails to explore:

  • Pacific Crest Trail: This trail passes through Campo and extends all the way to Canada. It is over 2,600 miles long and offers stunning views of the Sierra Nevada and Cascade mountain ranges.
  • Hauser Creek Trail: This is a moderate hike that takes you through Hauser Wilderness Area. You’ll see beautiful wildflowers, a scenic creek, and possibly even some wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Campo: Activities and Tours

Here are some exciting things to do in Campo:

  • Ghost Town Tours: You can explore the ghost towns of La Posta and Jacumba on a guided tour. These towns were once thriving gold rush settlements but are now abandoned.
  • Horseback Riding: You can take a guided horseback ride through the scenic backcountry of Campo. It’s an unforgettable experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Campo

If you’re planning to visit Campo for a week, here’s an itinerary we recommend:

  • Day 1: Visit the Pacific Southwest Railway Museum and take a ride on a restored railway car.
  • Day 2: Go on a hike at Hauser Wilderness Area and take in the breathtaking views.
  • Day 3: Explore the Campo Stone Store Museum and learn about the history of the region.
  • Day 4: Take a guided horseback ride through the backcountry of Campo.
  • Day 5: Visit Lake Morena County Park and enjoy fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Day 6: Explore the ghost towns of La Posta and Jacumba on a guided tour.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Campo.

Food and Drink Scene of Campo

Campo offers a variety of dining options that cater to different tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carne Asada Fries: This is a popular local dish made with French fries, grilled carne asada, guacamole, and cheese.
  • Fish Tacos: You can try some delicious fish tacos made with locally caught fish.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Campo is home to several breweries that offer excellent craft beer.
  • Wine: You can sample some local wines at Campo’s wineries.

Where to Stay in Campo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Campo offers a variety of accommodations that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some options to consider:

  • Lazy H Ranch Motel: This is a popular budget-friendly motel that offers comfortable rooms and a pool. It’s located near Lake Morena County Park.
  • Iron Door Hotel: This historic hotel was originally built in the 1800s and has since been restored. It offers cozy rooms and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Campo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some options to consider:

  • Arts and Crafts: You can find handmade crafts and artworks at local markets and shops in Campo.
  • Olive Oil: Campo is home to several olive groves that produce high-quality olive oil.

Local Culture and Customs of Campo

Campo has a rich history and cultural heritage that is reflected in its museums, festivals, and traditions. The Kumeyaay people, who lived in the region for centuries, have influenced the local culture. Respect for the environment and nature is also an important part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Campo

The best time to visit Campo is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be cold and wet. Consider visiting Campo in May when the wildflowers are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Campo:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and nature.
  • Explore the backcountry on guided tours.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines while hiking or biking.
  • Disturb the wildlife in Hauser Wilderness Area.
  • litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy while visiting Campo:

Safety in Campo

  • Always carry plenty of water while hiking or biking.
  • Follow all safety guidelines while exploring Hauser Wilderness Area.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure while travelling.

Health in Campo

  • Be prepared for the hot and dry climate by wearing appropriate clothing and sunscreen.
  • Keep any required medication with you, as there are limited medical facilities in the area.

Getting to Campo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Campo is accessible by several transportation options. Here are some ways to reach Campo:

  • By Car: You can reach Campo by car via Interstate 8.
  • By Bus: Greyhound and other bus companies provide services to Campo.
  • By Air: The nearest airport is San Diego International Airport, approximately 50 miles away.

How to Get Around in Campo

The best way to get around Campo is by car as public transport options are limited. You can also rent a car or bike to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Campo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • San Diego: This vibrant city is located just an hour drive from Campo and offers a variety of attractions, including the San Diego Zoo and the USS Midway Museum.
  • Anza-Borrego Desert State Park: This park is located about 60 miles from Campo and offers stunning views of the California desert.

Conclusion

Campo is a hidden gem in San Diego County that offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, outdoor recreation, and local culture. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope that you can plan your visit to Campo and make the most of your trip.
