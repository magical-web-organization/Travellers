Introduction to Inland

Inland is a beautiful region located in San Diego County, Southern California. This region is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, such as large lakes, hills, and state parks. Inland also has fascinating historical landmarks, making it the perfect destination for both nature lovers and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Inland

Inland has many attractions that make it difficult to decide where to start your adventure. Here are the top places to visit in Inland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Diego Museum of Art: This museum features an extensive collection of art from around the world and has a focus on Spanish art.
  • The California Center for the Arts: This is a cultural hub that hosts various art events throughout the year, such as plays, concerts, and art exhibits.
  • Mission San Diego de Alcala: Founded in 1769, this is the first of 21 missions constructed across California. It’s a great place to learn about the state’s history and Catholicism.

Natural Attractions of Inland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Poway: This stunning lake offers a variety of activities like hiking, fishing, and boating to visitors.
  • Iron Mountain Trail: This is a fantastic hiking trail that offers breathtaking scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Lake Hodges: Known for its fishing, this lake supports various species of fish and is a perfect place for a peaceful picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Inland

Inland offers a range of hikes and biking trails through the hills and valleys. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Mount Woodson Trail: This five-mile hike is ideal for adventurous hikers; the top of the mountain offers a stunning panoramic view of the entire Inland region.
  • Iron Mountain Trail: With a moderate level of difficulty, this hike is a favorite among locals and is known for its beautiful wildflowers.
  • Lake Hodges Trail: This eight-mile trail around the lake is perfect for a peaceful biking trip with gorgeous views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Inland: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the region’s natural and cultural attractions, Inland offers plenty of activities and tours for all kinds of travelers. Some of the best things to do in Inland include:

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take in the beauty of Inland from the sky with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Winery Tours: Explore the vineyards and sample some of the delicious wines produced in the region.
  • Camping: Inland has a range of campgrounds that allow visitors to enjoy the beauty of nature while exploring the region at their own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inland

If you’re planning to spend a week in Inland, here’s an itinerary that you can follow:

  • Day 1: Visit Mission San Diego de Alcala and take a cultural tour of the most significant landmarks in the state.
  • Day 2: Enjoy hiking Iron Mountain Trail, have a picnic at Lake Hodges, and unwind with a spa day at the wellness center.
  • Day 3: Take a winery tour and get to know the wine-making history and process.
  • Day 4: Explore Balboa Park, visit the San Diego Museum of Art, and take in the cultural heritage of the region.
  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride and admire the picturesque views of the landscape from the sky.
  • Day 6: Visit Lake Poway and engage in various activities like fishing, boating, and picnic.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the parks, or explore the region at leisure.

Food and Drink Scene of Inland

Inland has plenty of dining options that cater to a variety of tastes and budgets. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • California Burrito: A burrito with carne asada, guacamole, cheese, and French fries.
  • Fish Tacos: A staple of the region; it features battered and fried fish with cabbage, pico de gallo, and crema.
  • Carne Asada Fries: French fries covered with carne asada, cheese, guacamole, and sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Inland is famous for its craft breweries, which produce a variety of ales, lagers, and stouts.
  • Wine: Inland’s location provides ideal conditions for wine-making; try some of the local wines to taste the region’s distinct flavors.

Where to Stay in Inland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Inland has various options for accommodation, depending on your budget and style: from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top places to stay in Inland:

  • The Inn at Rancho Santa Fe: A luxurious resort that offers golfing, relaxation, and spa services.
  • La Jolla Cove Suites: A stunning hotel overlooking La Jolla Cove, and with a rooftop pool, fitness center, and restaurant.
  • Oakwood Apartments: A budget-friendly option that provides fully furnished apartments for rent.

What to Buy in Inland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Inland has various souvenir shops and local markets that sell unique products and local produce. Here are some souvenirs to buy and bring back home:

  • Olive oil: Inland is famous for its Olive Groves; you can purchase excellent quality olive oil as a souvenir.
  • Goat cheese: Local goat cheese is famous in the region and sold at the local markets.
  • Lemon Cakes: Inland is known for its delicious lemon cakes, which you can buy at various bakeries and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Inland

Inland has a history of rich cultural heritage, mainly due to its proximity to Mexico. It’s a wonderful blend of American and Mexican culture. The locals are generally friendly and welcoming to tourists.

Best Time to Visit Inland

The best time to visit Inland is during the spring and fall seasons. The weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds, making it ideal for outdoor activities like hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inland

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Inland:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Take care of the natural surroundings and avoid littering.
  • Always carry enough water and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables like cash, mobile phones, and jewelry in the car while exploring.
  • Ignore the weather forecast; sudden changes in weather can be hazardous.

Safety and Health

Safety in Inland is generally not a concern; however, it is advisable to take precautions and heed any warning signs when hiking or exploring the outdoors. Regarding health, drinking the tap water is generally safe, but visitors should check with their accommodation provider to be sure.

Getting to Inland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Inland is easily accessible from San Diego and Los Angeles. Here are the transportation options to get to Inland:

Airports Near Inland

  • San Diego International Airport
  • John Wayne Airport, Orange County

Bus Stations Near Inland

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Amtrak

Other Options

  • Car rental services are a popular option for visitors.
  • Taxis and ride-hailing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Inland

Getting around Inland is easy with a car. Car rental services are available at the airport and throughout the region, making it easy to explore Inland at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Inland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Inland is located in Southern California, so there are many nearby destinations to explore after your visit, such as:

  • San Diego: An ideal spot to continue your exploration of stunning beaches and amazing cultural destinations.
  • Joshua Tree National Park: A popular spot for camping and hiking, and only a few hour’s drive from Inland.
  • Los Angeles: Visit famous landmarks like the Hollywood sign and Universal Studios only a couple of hours away.

Conclusion

Inland is a fantastic destination for visitors who want to explore scenic natural beauty and learn about California’s cultural heritage. With a wide range of activities and sights to enjoy, Inland is a must-visit destination for all tourists traveling to Southern California.
