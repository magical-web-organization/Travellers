Introduction to San Ysidro

Welcome to San Ysidro, a vibrant community in Southern California, located in San Diego County. San Ysidro is a border town that separates the USA from Mexico. It is a multicultural hub which offers an array of activities for visitors. The city boasts of museums, historical places, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and a wide selection of tasty cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in San Ysidro

When exploring San Ysidro, there are many places to visit and things to do. Below are the top destinations that visitors should not miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Ysidro Historical Museum: Learn about the long history of San Ysidro through a series of exhibits that will take you on a journey from the 1700s to present-day.

  • Tijuana River National Estuarine Research Reserve: This wildlife refuge provides visitors with an opportunity to enjoy stunning birdwatching or hiking trails while enjoying the Salt Marsh, River Mouth, and Beach habitats.

  • Las Americas Premium Outlets: A shopper’s paradise, Las Americas is home to over 125 stores, where you’ll find designer clothing, shoes, and accessories.

Natural Attractions of San Ysidro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tijuana Estuary Visitors Center: Discover an abundance of wildlife while hiking and birdwatching in this Ecological Reserve. The Visitor Center provides information on what you could do to preserve the natural habitats found in the Reserve.

  • Border Field State Park: Enjoy a picnic while admiring the picturesque views of the beach, lagoons, and estuary, or go on a guided tour of the park for a more hands-on experience.

  • Imperial Beach: With six miles of beachfront, Imperial Beach is a surfer’s paradise. Visitors can also enjoy a casual stroll, bike ride, or sunbathe on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Ysidro

San Ysidro is home to lots of hiking and biking trails. Trails of varying difficulty offer breathtaking views of coastal and inland territories. A few of these trails include the Tijuana River Valley Regional Park, San Ysidro Mountains Trails, and San Diego Bayshore Bikeway.

Best Things to Do in San Ysidro: Activities and Tours

San Ysidro offers visitors a plethora of activities and tours, including:

  • Taste of Baja Food Tour: Explore the local cuisine and learn more about Baja California’s culture through an exciting gastronomic journey.

  • San Ysidro Farmer’s Market: For fans of organic and locally-produced products, the Farmer’s Market held every Saturday along West San Ysidro Boulevard is a must-see.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Ysidro

If you have a week in town, here’s an itinerary to follow:

Day 1: Start by visiting the San Ysidro Historical Museum in downtown San Ysidro to learn more about the city’s history.

Day 2-4: Explore the great outdoors! Visit the Tijuana River Estuary, Border Field State Park and Imperial Beach.

Day 5: Head over to Las Americas Premium Outlets, home to over 125 designer stores, and shop till you drop!

Day 6: Take a Baja Food Tour and enjoy the local foods and drinks.

Day 7: Relax with a visit to a local spa or check out the San Ysidro Farmer’s Market for some local products

Food and Drink Scene of San Ysidro

The food scene in San Ysidro offers many delicious options for visitors. Here are some homegrown delicacies that visitors should taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Tacos: Enjoy the taste of Baja California with some fish tacos. You can find some of the best seafood restaurants in Plaza Viva Tijuana.

  • Birria: A popular Mexican stew, made with goat, beef, or mutton. This authentic dish is a must-try for visitors.

Local Beverages

  • Margaritas: Margaritas are a staple in San Ysidro and are best enjoyed at Tio Choy’s in central San Ysidro.

  • Tequila: If you’re looking for some liquors that capture the true essence of the region, Tequila is a spirit you simply can’t miss.

Where to Stay in San Ysidro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Ysidro has a variety of options for all types of travelers:

  • Best Western Americana Inn: A budget-friendly option, Best Western is located close to the Las Americas Premium Outlets.

  • Hotel Lucerna: This 4-star hotel is fabulous, with air-conditioned rooms that come with a minibar, cable TV, and free Wi-Fi.

  • Quality Inn & Suites: This hotel offers a pleasant stay and optimal location for easy access to nearby attractions and activities.

What to Buy in San Ysidro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for authentic souvenirs and products to take home, look no further:

  • Mexican Handicrafts: The Plaza Viva Tijuana is a great option to find authentic handicrafts like pottery, textiles, and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of San Ysidro

San Ysidro boasts a diverse population that includes Mexican and American culture.

Best Time to Visit San Ysidro

San Ysidro has a Mediterranean climate, making it an all-year-round destination. Travelers, however, can avoid the crowds by visiting between September and November.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Ysidro

Do:

  • Try different types of cuisine, especially if you haven’t tried a region’s delicacies before.

  • Visit all the cultural centers, museums, and parks to learn more about San Ysidro’s interesting history and culture.

Don’t:

-  Don’t make unnecessary noise during your stay, as this may upset community members.

  • Don’t litter in the parks or on the beach. Keep San Ysidro clean!

Safety and Health

Safety in San Ysidro

San Ysidro is generally safe for travelers, but visitors should always be cautious while exploring the city’s neighborhoods at night.

Health in San Ysidro

There are no mandatory vaccinations to enter San Ysidro or the USA, and medical facilities are accessible throughout the city.

Getting to San Ysidro: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Diego International Airport is the closest airport, located 23 miles away from San Ysidro. It is also a popular destination for driving tours. Some popular options for getting around are renting a car, taking public transportation, or going with a ride-sharing service.

Airports Near San Ysidro

  • San Diego International Airport: 23 miles away, a 20-minute drive from San Ysidro.

Bus Stations Near San Ysidro

  • Greyhound: Seven minutes away from San Ysidro, located at 753 W San Ysidro Blvd.

Other Transportation Options

  • Bike Sharing Program: The city’s Bike Sharing Program is a great option for travelers who want to explore San Ysidro on a limited budget.

  • Ride Sharing: Services like Uber or Lyft offer travelers affordable transportation options in and around San Ysidro.

How to Get Around in San Ysidro

San Ysidro has a good transportation network with many options for getting around the city. These include public buses, taxis, ride-sharing services, and walking. Biking is also a fantastic way to explore the city conveniently.

Where to Go Next from San Ysidro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that visitors can explore from San Ysidro:

  • Rosarito: A bustling oceanfront city located south of San Ysidro.

  • Tijuana: A neighboring city that boasts an abundance of restaurants, museums, and cultural centers visitors can explore.

Conclusion

We’ve highlighted the best things to do, places to stay, cultural attractions, and the food and beverage scene in this beautiful border town. All that’s left is to pack your bags and enjoy your San Ysidro adventure!
