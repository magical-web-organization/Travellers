Introduction to Claremont

Nestled in the picturesque San Gabriel Valley of Southern California, Claremont is a hidden gem that promises an unforgettable experience for travelers. Dubbed as the “City of Trees and Ph.D.s,” Claremont boasts the perfect blend of natural beauty and intellectual stimulation, making it an ideal destination for those who yearn for relaxation and inspiration. Its tree-lined streets, beautiful parks, and welcoming atmosphere offer something for everyone, from nature lovers and history buffs to foodies and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Claremont

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rancho Santa Ana Botanic Garden - A 86-acre garden that is home to over 22,000 native California flora species. Visitors can enjoy guided tours, plant sales, and educational programs.
  • Claremont Museum of Art - Showcases the works of local artists and hosts various exhibitions throughout the year. It also offers art classes and workshops for all ages.
  • Claremont Heritage - A non-profit organization dedicated to preserving the city’s history and architecture. Visitors can take a historic walking tour to learn more about the city’s rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Claremont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thompson Creek Trail - A picturesque 3.7-mile hiking trail that winds through the wilderness of the foothills. The trail offers stunning views of the San Gabriel Mountains and has many birdwatching spots.
  • Claremont Hills Wilderness Park - A 1,620-acre park with over 7 miles of hiking trails. Visitors can enjoy picnicking, wildlife watching, and nature photography.
  • Sunset Hills Memorial Park - A peaceful and serene park with rolling hills, water features, and beautiful gardens. It offers a perfect spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Claremont

  • San Antonio Heights Loop Trail - A 5-mile easy hiking trail that begins at the Claremont Hills Wilderness Park. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and canyons.
  • Claremont Hills Wilderness Park Loop Trail - A 5.6-mile moderate hiking trail that features wildflowers in the spring and summer.
  • Marshall Canyon Trail - A 6.1-mile moderate hiking and biking trail that starts from Claremont and leads to the foothills of the San Gabriel Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Claremont: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Claremont Village - A charming pedestrian-friendly shopping district that offers a mix of boutiques, galleries, cafes, and restaurants.
  • Attend a Concert at Bridges Auditorium - An iconic and historic venue that hosts various musical and theatrical performances throughout the year.
  • Take a Craft Brewery Tour - Claremont is home to several craft breweries, such as Claremont Craft Ales and Last Name Brewing. Visitors can take a tour and enjoy tastings of some of the best beers in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Claremont

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgers at The Back Abbey - A popular gastropub that offers a variety of burgers made with grass-fed beef and unique toppings.
  • Thai Food at Spice & Rice Thai Kitchen - A family-owned restaurant that serves authentic Thai cuisine made with fresh ingredients.
  • Tacos at Soho Tacos - A food truck that offers mouth-watering tacos made with gourmet ingredients and unique flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Claremont Craft Ales - A craft brewery that offers a variety of beers made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Vom Fass - A specialty store that offers a range of artisanal oils, vinegars, and spirits from around the world.
  • The Press - A tea and coffee bar that offers a selection of organic and fair trade drinks.

Where to Stay in Claremont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Claremont - A modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a pool.
  • Claremont Lodge - A budget-friendly motel that offers clean and simple rooms with basic amenities.
  • Casa 425 - A stylish boutique hotel in Claremont Village that offers unique rooms and suites, a courtyard, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Claremont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally-made artisanal cheese from Cheese Cave - A gourmet cheese shop that offers a range of artisanal cheeses from California and beyond.
  • California-grown produce from Claremont Farmer’s Market - A year-round local market that offers fresh and organic produce, artisanal food, and handmade crafts.
  • Handmade jewelry from Studio Claremont - A boutique that showcases the works of local artists and offers unique handmade jewelry, pottery, and art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Claremont

Claremont is a vibrant and diverse community that celebrates its rich cultural heritage and welcomes visitors with open arms. Its historic architecture, tree-lined streets, and friendly locals create a warm and inviting atmosphere that’s hard to resist. Visitors should respect local customs, such as keeping noise levels low in residential neighborhoods and following trail etiquette on hiking trails.

Best Time to Visit Claremont

The best time to visit Claremont is from March to May or September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and outdoor activities are abundant. Summers can be hot, with temperatures ranging from the mid-70s to the mid-90s Fahrenheit, and winters are mild, with temperatures ranging from the mid-40s to the mid-60s Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Claremont

Do:

  • Do bring sunscreen, a hat, and a water bottle when hiking or spending time outdoors.
  • Do visit Claremont Village for unique shopping and dining experiences.
  • Do take a tour of the Rancho Santa Ana Botanic Garden to learn about native California flora species.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb wildlife when hiking in the Claremont Hills Wilderness Park.
  • Don’t park in residential areas without a permit.
  • Don’t ignore trail signs and follow trail etiquette when hiking in the foothills.

Safety and Health

Safety in Claremont

Claremont is a safe and peaceful community with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise common sense and take normal precautions, such as avoiding dark and deserted areas at night and locking their cars when parked.

Health in Claremont

Claremont has several hospitals and medical centers that offer high-quality medical care. Visitors should also take precautions against heat stroke and dehydration when spending time outdoors during the hot summer months.

Getting to Claremont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Claremont

  • Ontario International Airport - Located about 10 miles east of Claremont, Ontario International Airport offers domestic and international flights.
  • Los Angeles International Airport - Located about 50 miles west of Claremont, Los Angeles International Airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Claremont

  • Claremont Transit Center - Located in Claremont Village, the transit center offers local and regional bus services.
  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in nearby Pomona, the Greyhound Bus Station offers national bus services.

Other Options

  • Metrolink Train - The Metrolink train is a convenient and affordable way to travel to and from Claremont. The Claremont Metrolink Station offers daily train services to Los Angeles, San Bernardino, and other neighboring cities.
  • Car Rental - Visitors can rent a car from several car rental companies located in Claremont and nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Claremont

Claremont is a walkable and bike-friendly community with several bike share programs and rental options. Visitors can also take local buses, rideshare services, or rent a car to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Claremont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mount San Antonio - A popular hiking destination located in the San Gabriel Mountains, Mount San Antonio offers stunning views and challenging hiking trails.
  • Joshua Tree National Park - Located about 90 miles east of Claremont, Joshua Tree National Park offers stunning desert landscapes and unique rock formations.
  • Santa Monica Pier - Located about 50 miles west of Claremont, Santa Monica Pier is a popular coastal destination that offers amusement park rides, shopping, and dining experiences.

Conclusion

Claremont is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and intellectual stimulation. Its welcoming atmosphere, charming architecture, and vibrant community promise an unforgettable experience for travelers who seek relaxation and inspiration. From hiking trails and outdoor adventures to eclectic cuisine and artisanal products, Claremont has something for everyone. So pack your bags and embark on a journey that’s sure to make lasting memories.
