Introduction to Monterey Park

Nestled in the San Gabriel Valley of Southern California, Monterey Park is an inviting city with a diverse cultural heritage. Known as the “Chinese Beverly Hills”, Monterey Park offers a unique blend of Chinese and American cultures, from its cuisine to its festivals.

Whether you’re a foodie looking to indulge in delicious Chinese dishes or an outdoor enthusiast seeking picturesque views, this comprehensive tour guide for Monterey Park has got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Monterey Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Vincent Price Art Museum - This museum houses a collection of contemporary and modern art, along with extensive works from Mexican-American artists. Admission is free.

  • The Monterey Park Historical Museum - This museum offers a glimpse into the city’s past with historical artifacts and photographs. Admission is also free.

  • The Lummis Home - Built by writer and activist Charles Lummis in 1897, this impressive structure offers visitors a chance to admire the eclectic design and learn about Lummis’ efforts to preserve Native American culture.

Natural Attractions of Monterey Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sequoia Park - Spanning 7.2 acres, this park offers visitors a chance to hike, bike, and enjoy breathtaking views of Mt. Baldy and the San Gabriel Mountains.

  • Echo Mountain via the Sam Merrill Trail - Lace up your hiking shoes and head to this picturesque trail which leads to the ruins of the Mount Lowe Railway.

  • The Monterey Park Farmers Market - Not quite a park, but this weekly farmers market offers visitors a chance to revel in the freshest produce and locally-made foods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monterey Park

  • The Rio Hondo Trail - This 17-mile trail runs through Pico Rivera, El Monte, and Whittier, leading hikers and bikers through scenic hills, parks, and bridges.

  • The San Gabriel River Trail - Stretching over 38 miles, this trail is perfect for the avid rider. The trail passes through cities like El Monte, Baldwin Park, and Temple City.

Best Things to Do in Monterey Park: Activities and Tours

  • Dim Sum Tour - Take a culinary tour of the city and sample some of the best dim sum restaurants around.

  • Monterey Park Cherry Blossom Festival - Held annually in April, this festival celebrates the city’s Japanese heritage with food, entertainment, and traditional ceremonies.

  • Urban Adventure Quest Scavenger Hunt - Perfect for families and groups, this scavenger hunt allows them to explore city landmarks and earn points for each answer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monterey Park

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Monterey Park Historical Museum and the Vincent Price Art Museum
Day 2 Visit Sequoia Park and hike or bike the Rio Hondo Trail
Day 3 Take turns sitting at the monkey statues of Barnes Park, and be sure to grab lunch from one of the nearby dim sum restaurants.
Day 4 Go for a walk in Echo Mountain via the Sam Merrill Trail, then stop by the Monterey Park Farmers Market
Day 5 Catch a movie at the Atlantic Times Square IMAX Theater and grab dinner at one of the nearby restaurants.
Day 6 Visit the Lummis Home and learn about Charles Lummis’ efforts to preserve Native American culture.
Day 7 Have breakfast in a local restaurant, and shop for souvenirs at the Monterey Park Plaza Shopping Center

Food and Drink Scene of Monterey Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dim Sum - Monterey Park offers some of the best dim sum restaurants around, so be sure to sample some siu mai, har gow, and cheong fun.

  • Beef Noodle Soup - A popular Taiwanese dish, this soup is made with fresh noodles, beef broth, and tender beef slices.

  • Hong Kong-Style Cafe Food - Don’t miss out on the unique blend of Chinese and Western cultures by trying dishes like baked pork chop rice or egg waffles.

Local Beverages

  • Boba/ Bubble Tea - This sweet and refreshing drink comes in a variety of flavors and toppings, perfect for a hot day.

  • Craft Beers - Local breweries like Hop Secret Brewing Co. and Progress Brewing offer a variety of craft beers on tap for beer lovers to enjoy.

Where to Stay in Monterey Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Los Angeles/San Gabriel - Located minutes away from Monterey Park, this hotel offers luxurious accommodations and amenities like a fitness center, pool, and restaurant.

  • Lincoln Hotel - Budget travelers can enjoy a comfortable stay at this hotel, which offers clean rooms and affordable rates.

  • GreenTree Inn & Suites - This hotel offers a variety of rooms and suites to meet the needs of every traveler. The hotel also offers free breakfast and a pool.

What to Buy in Monterey Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea - Take home some traditional Chinese tea from Ten Ren’s Tea Time, the largest Chinese tea retailer in North America.

  • Chinese Calligraphy Art - Get a glimpse into the local art scene and purchase a piece of Chinese calligraphy art as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Monterey Park

In addition to the city’s rich cultural heritage, Monterey Park also has a unique set of customs and traditions, many of which are tied to its Chinese heritage. Visitors should:

  • Be polite and courteous to locals

  • Respect traditional Chinese customs and traditions

  • Try to learn a few Chinese phrases and greetings

Best Time to Visit Monterey Park

The best time to visit Monterey Park is during the spring and fall months when temperatures are mild and the air is crisp. However, visitors should be mindful of smog during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monterey Park

Do:

  • Try the dim sum

  • Explore historic landmarks

  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter or vandalize public property

  • Disrespect local culture

  • Forget to tip your servers at restaurants

Safety and Health

Safety in Monterey Park

Like any city, visitors should exercise caution when walking around at night and in areas with low visibility. Visitors should also be mindful of traffic when crossing streets.

Health in Monterey Park

Visitors should also be mindful of air quality, especially during the summer months when smog levels can be high. Those with respiratory issues should take preventative measures.

Getting to Monterey Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monterey Park

  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) - Located 30 minutes away from Monterey Park, this airport offers domestic and international flights.

  • Bob Hope Airport (BUR) - Located 25 miles away from Monterey Park, this airport serves the greater Los Angeles area.

Bus Stations Near Monterey Park

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located 10 minutes away from Monterey Park, this station offers bus routes throughout California.

Other Options

  • Ride-Sharing Services - Services like Uber and Lyft are readily available in Monterey Park.

  • Car Rentals - Visitors can rent a car at one of the many rental car agencies located throughout Los Angeles.

How to Get Around in Monterey Park

Visitors can get around Monterey Park on foot or via ride-sharing services. Public transportation options include the Monterey Park Spirit Bus, which offers convenient routes throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Monterey Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Los Angeles - Enjoy the city’s entertainment and cultural offerings, such as Hollywood, Downtown LA, or the beach cities.

  • Santa Monica - Take a short drive to this famous beach town and enjoy the boardwalk and beach.

  • Pasadena - Explore this charming city filled with museums, art galleries, and botanical gardens.

Conclusion

Monterey Park offers a unique blend of Chinese and American cultures, making it an inviting destination for travelers. From its delicious dim sum to its scenic trails and parks, there’s something for everyone in Monterey Park.
