Introduction to Pomona

Nestled in the heart of San Gabriel Valley, Pomona is a vibrant city that offers an alluring blend of SoCal charm and contemporary amenities. Known for its rich history, cultural landmarks, and natural attractions, Pomona offers something for everyone.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a food lover, or just looking for a weekend getaway, Pomona has a lot to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best of Pomona and help you plan an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Pomona

From cultural attractions to natural wonders, Pomona is packed with interesting places to explore. Here are some of the must-visit destinations for a memorable experience:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pomona is rich in history and cultural landmarks, which is why you must visit some of the most popular attractions, including:

  • Los Angeles County Fairgrounds: Since 1922, the venue hosts an annual fair that is known for its carnival games, livestock exhibitions, and food stands.
  • Wally Parks NHRA Motorsports Museum: Visitors can take a stroll among some of the most iconic and historic cars on display.
  • American Museum of Ceramic Art: Art lovers will appreciate the collection of ceramics from all over the world, making the museum one of the most important in the country.
  • Phillips Mansion: This beautiful house is a hidden gem that is part of the National Register of Historic Places, decorated with antiques, furniture, art, and architecture design elements, and hosts a range of events.

Natural Attractions of Pomona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love nature, Pomona has plenty of exciting outdoor adventures. From scenic hikes to picturesque views, these are the outdoor attractions you can’t miss:

  • Pomona Valley Mining Company: A restaurant that features a rustic yet refined atmosphere, situated atop a hill with breathtaking panoramic views.
  • Pomona Valley Park: The park is a peaceful and family-friendly space, perfect for trekking or just enjoying a picnic with friends and family.
  • Bonelli Regional Park: With acres of gorgeous nature, this park offers a range of outdoor activities like camping, fishing, boating, and hiking.
  • Sunset Ridge Trail: Ideal for nature hikes with views of the San Gabriel Valley and the Orange County coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pomona

If you love to explore nature while biking or hiking, then Pomona has a lot to offer. These are some of the most scenic routes to follow:

  • Claremont Hills Wilderness Trail: A 2.7-mile hiking and biking path that provides panoramic views of the valley.
  • San Gabriel River Trail: More than 38 miles long, it extends through several cities and boasts beautiful natural scenery.
  • Mount San Antonio (Mt. Baldy): A quant 10,069-foot peak above sea level is a hiker’s paradise and an adventure itself.

Best Things to Do in Pomona: Activities and Tours

There’s always something exciting happening in Pomona. Here are some fun activities and tours to add to your Pomona itinerary:

  • Wine Tasting: Pomona Valley is home to many excellent wineries, offering wine tasting experiences and guided tours.
  • Trap Zone: The trendy and immersive facility offers exciting games that are perfect for groups of friends or family.
  • Art Walk East: Attend the monthly Art Walk event and stroll through the city, which is the best way to explore the thriving art scene in Pomona.
  • Pamona Swap Meet & Classic Cars Show: The swap meet is one of the largest of its kind in the world, showcasing pre-1985 cars, auto parts and memorabilia, attracting thousands of car enthusiasts every year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pomona

To make the most of your trip to Pomona, plan a weeklong itinerary that covers all the must-visit attractions. Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Wally Parks NHRA Motorsports Museum, Los Angeles County Fairgrounds, and the American Museum of Ceramic Art.
  • Day 2: Check out the Phillips Mansion, and spend the afternoon picnicking at Pomona Valley Park.
  • Day 3: Go for a hike at Sunset Ridge Trail, and catch the sunset from Pomona Valley Mining Company.
  • Day 4: Explore the Bonelli Regional Park and have a BBQ.
  • Day 5: Enjoy the Claremont Hills Wilderness Trail in the morning, then head downtown to participate in the Pomona Art Walk.
  • Day 6: Take a scenic drive along the San Gabriel River Trail to Mount San Antonio (Mt. Baldy).
  • Day 7: Visit the Pomona Swap Meet & Classic Cars Show, and taste wines at one of the local wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Pomona

With a mix of culinary influences and fresh produce from the surrounding farms, Pomona’s food and drink scene is dynamic and exciting.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Double Cheeseburger: Mariscos El Jato’s classic double cheeseburger is stuffed with cheddar cheese, crispy bacon, and a side of fries.
  • Birria Tacos: This taco joint is known for its tender and juicy birria tacos, paired with a side of consomé broth and lime wedges.
  • Seafood Platter: Mariscos Jalisco’s signature platter is not to be missed, featuring deep-fried shrimp and fish tacos topped with creamy avocado sauce, cabbage, and pico de gallo.
  • Pho Soup: New Golden Horse’s pho is a restaurant staple, made with rice noodles, tender beef, and a savory broth.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Check out Sanctum Brewing Company, where locals and visitors enjoy flights of IPAs, stouts, and porters.
  • Wine Tasting: Go wine tasting at Galleano Winery, which has been family-owned and operated for over a century, making it one of the most celebrated wineries in the region.

Where to Stay in Pomona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget stay or a luxurious experience, Pomona has all options covered. Check out these popular accommodation options:

  • Sheraton Fairplex Hotel & Conference Center: A 2-minute walk from the Pomona Fairplex, the hotel is perfect for business travelers and families.
  • Motel 6 Pomona: Budget accommodations that are comfortable, clean, and trendy.
  • Kellogg West Conference Center & Lodge: Offering beautiful views of the San Gabriel Mountains, experience rustic charm and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Pomona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To take a piece of Pomona with you, consider these popular souvenirs and local products:

  • Farm Fresh Produce: Head to the Pomona Farmers Market and pick up some fresh citrus, avocados, strawberries, and other local fruits.
  • Steel Magnolias: A trendy gift shop that offers funky pottery, colorful bags, and unique locally-sourced collectibles.
  • Madre Oaxacan Artisan Products: Find unique handmade products such as wooden spoons, pottery, and embroidered textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Pomona

Pomona is a diverse and multicultural city, where locals take pride in their heritage, traditions, and celebrations. One of the unique customs is serving champurrado, a hot Mexican sweet drink of corn and chocolate, during the cold winter season.

Best Time to Visit Pomona

Pomona enjoys sunny and mild year-round weather, so visitors can plan a trip anytime. However, the best time to go is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and there are many outdoor activities in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pomona

Do:

  • Visit the Fairplex: Make sure to check out the LA County Fairgrounds that feature an exciting lineup of entertainment, rodeos, and cultural events throughout the year.
  • Hike the San Gabriel Mountains: The breathtaking views make the hikes worthwhile, so make sure to bring a good pair of shoes and plenty of water.
  • Shop at the Farmers Market: A great way to experience the local culture and taste fresh produce.

Don’t:

  • Ignore Safety Precautions: As with any city, be mindful of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unknown areas.
  • Rush through Attractions: Take your time to explore the attractions, museums, restaurants, and natural beauty to fully enjoy your experience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pomona

Pomona is generally a safe city, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in downtown and near the Fairplex. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: When traveling at night, make sure to walk in a group or use ride-sharing services.
  • Avoid Flash Displays of Wealth: Avoid flashing cash, jewelry, and other valuables, which could make you a target for theft.

Health in Pomona

Visitors are advised to carry some essentials like a hat, sunscreen, and sunglasses to protect from sudden weather changes in Southern California.

Getting to Pomona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pomona

The two major airports near Pomona are LA/Ontario International Airport (ONT), which is about 10 miles away, and John Wayne Airport (SNA) in Orange County, which is about 25 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Pomona

Greyhound Bus and Flixbus both have stations in nearby cities, with direct routes to Pomona.

Other Options

Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available throughout the city. Renting a car is also an option if you prefer driving.

How to Get Around in Pomona

The best way to get around Pomona is by car, as public transportation is limited. You can also rent a bike or scooter to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Pomona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for nearby destinations to explore, check out these popular options:

  • San Gabriel Valley: This region is full of charming cities and towns, like Pasadena, San Gabriel, and Arcadia, each with their unique landmarks and attractions.
  • Los Angeles: A 30-minute drive from Pomona, LA never disappoints visitors with its endless options for entertainment, dining, and shopping.
  • Joshua Tree National Park: A must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts, Joshua Tree is within a two-hour drive from Pomona.

Conclusion

Pomona, Southern California, is a hidden gem that is full of surprises and exciting things to do, with a rich history and cultural heritage, and home to some of the most stunning views and nature hikes. Use our comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Pomona, and be sure to add these must-visit attractions to your itinerary. Happy traveling!
