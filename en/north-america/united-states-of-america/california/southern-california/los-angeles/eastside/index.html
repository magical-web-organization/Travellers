Introduction to Eastside

Eastside is one of the most dynamic, diverse, and historic neighborhoods of Los Angeles, located in Southern California. Spanning across several neighborhoods, including Echo Park, Los Feliz, Silver Lake, Boyle Heights, and Highland Park, Eastside offers a vibrant mix of culture, nature, food, and creativity that attracts many tourists and locals alike.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the artsy bohemian side of the city, diving into its rich cultural heritage, enjoying its scenic natural beauty, or indulging in its eclectic food scene, Eastside has something for everyone. So, whether you’re planning a weekend getaway, a family vacation, or a solo adventure, our comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Eastside

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Broad – One of the most popular modern art museums in Los Angeles, showcasing works by contemporary artists, such as Andy Warhol, Jeff Koons, and Yayoi Kusama.
  2. Natural History Museum of Los Angeles County - A huge natural history museum with exhibits such as Dinosaur Hall, the Age of Mammals, and the Nature Lab.
  3. The Huntington Library, Art Museum, and Botanical Gardens – A stunning estate housing a library, art museum, and botanical gardens with one of the largest collections of rare books in the world, as well as rare art and exotic plants.
  4. The Getty Center – Situated on a hilltop with panoramic views of the city, The Getty Center features a vast art collection, impressive architecture, and gardens.
  5. Japanese American National Museum - A museum dedicated to the Japanese American experience from immigration to internment in the US.

Natural Attractions of Eastside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Griffith Park – One of the largest urban parks in the US, with numerous hiking and biking trails, picnic areas and scenic views of the city, including the famous Hollywood sign.
  2. Echo Park Lake – A serene park with a stunning lake, ideal for picnics, boating, fishing and leisure walks.
  3. Angeles National Forest – A vast wilderness area that covers more than 600,000 acres, featuring crystal clear streams, towering trees, deep canyons and high peaks that offer excellent hiking, camping, fishing, and skiing opportunities.
  4. Elysian Park – A tranquil green space with a beautiful combination of nature, trails, and incredible views of Downtown Los Angeles.
  5. Silver Lake Reservoir - An urban oasis perfect for walking, picnic, and admiring the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastside

  1. Griffith Observatory Hike – A moderate 2.5 miles hike that leads up to the iconic Griffith Observatory.
  2. El Cid Trail to Griffith Park Summit – A 3.8 miles hike that leads you to breathtaking views of the city.
  3. Eastside Mural Ride - A leisurely bike tour that takes you through the vibrant neighborhood of Eastside to admire its colorful murals, street art, and landmarks.
  4. Beachwood Canyon – A scenic biking route that takes you through winding roads, lush vegetation, and historic homes with views of the Hollywood sign along the way.

Best Things to Do in Eastside: Activities and Tours

  1. Sunset Strip Segway Tour – A fun and unique way to explore the Sunset Strip and its iconic landmarks like the Chateau Marmont and the Whisky A Go-Go.
  2. Brewery Art Walk – An annual event that showcases the works of local artists across different media in one of the largest artist colonies in the world.
  3. Eastside Food Tour – A delicious gastronomic tour that takes you through some of Eastside’s most authentic and eclectic eateries, cafes, and markets that reflect its rich cultural diversity.
  4. Echo Park Pedal Boats - Rent a pedal boat in Echo Park to admire the scenic backdrop of Downtown Los Angeles and feed the ducks and swans.
  5. Hollywood Forever Cemetery - Attending a screening here in summer is a must. Pack a picnic, sit on the grass, and watch a movie.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastside

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Griffith Park, hike up to the Observatory, and enjoy the sunset.
Day 2 Visit The Broad and the nearby Walt Disney Concert Hall, followed by lunch at Grand Central Market.
Day 3 Take a bike ride through Silver Lake and continue to Echo Park, stopping for lunch at a local cafe.
Day 4 Discover the Japanese American National Museum and the nearby Little Tokyo, then stop for dinner at one of the many ramen or sushi restaurants.
Day 5 Explore Highland Park, visiting galleries, independent shops, and street art. Stop by for lunch at Highland Park Bowl, a vintage bowling alley.
Day 6 Spend a day exploring the Huntington Library and Botanical Gardens, and grab dinner in the nearby town of San Marino.
Day 7 Relax in Elysian Park, take a walk, or just soak in the views of the city skyline.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastside

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tacos from Guisados - These authentic Mexican tacos with hand made tortillas and flavorful fillings like chorizo, steak, and cactus, are a local favorite.
  2. Burgers from The Oinkster - This retro burger joint, famous for its pastrami and pulled pork burgers, homemade fries, and craft beers, offers a delicious and hearty meal.
  3. French Dip Sandwich from Philippe’s French Dip - This iconic sandwich with tender beef, crusty bread, and tangy dipping sauce, has been a Los Angeles staple since 1918.
  4. Pizza from Pizzanista! - These creative pies, with toppings like mac and cheese, honey, and kale, are a true treat for pizza lovers.
  5. Ice cream from Donut Friend - This vegan-friendly donut shop is an Eastside institution, with unique donut flavors, ice cream sandwiches, and coffee.

Local Beverages

  1. Coffee from Intelligentsia Coffee - This high-end coffee roaster and retailer takes coffee seriously and sources beans from around the world for a delicious and smooth cup of coffee.
  2. Craft Beer from Eagle Rock Brewery - This award-winning brewery offers a variety of handcrafted beers, including seasonal brews and limited editions, in a welcoming and unpretentious atmosphere.
  3. Cocktails from Bar Angeles – This popular trendy bar serves artisan cocktails made with fresh ingredients and top-shelf spirits, with creative names like Buzzo Espresso, Echo Sunset, and Johnny wants a Gin & Tonic.

Where to Stay in Eastside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Ace Hotel Downtown Los Angeles – This historic hotel offers minimalist yet stylish rooms with exposed brick and concrete, a rooftop bar, a swimming pool, and a 24-hour fitness center.
  2. Hotel Covell – This boutique hotel features spacious and stylish suites, each with its own kitchen, living area, and balcony, in the trendy Los Feliz neighborhood.
  3. The LINE Hotel – This retro-inspired hotel features modern rooms with industrial decor, two restaurants, a swimming pool, and a creative atmosphere that appeals to artsy types.
  4. Hi Los Angeles Santa Monica – This budget-friendly hostel offers a fun and social environment, with shared rooms, private rooms, and dormitories, a communal kitchen, a game room, and a rooftop lounge with views of the city.

What to Buy in Eastside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Eastside Market Italian Deli - This family-owned and operated deli has been selling fresh Italian sandwiches, cold cuts, cheeses, and wines for over 60 years.
  2. In Sheep’s Clothing - This independent music store specializes in vinyl and sells jazz, funk, soul, and world music records to music enthusiasts and collectors.
  3. Soap Plant/Wacko – This unique store offers quirky and kitschy products, including books, toys, gifts, art, and souvenirs, with a focus on pop culture and local artists.
  4. Glassell Park Pool Supply Co – This store sells a wide range of vintage and contemporary pool toys, inflatables, and accessories for some fun in the sun.
  5. Kaldi Coffee & Tea – This local coffee roaster and retailer sells single-origin coffee beans and more than 50 different teas from around the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastside

Eastside is a melting pot of cultures, lifestyles, and values that reflect the diversity of Los Angeles. Locals pride themselves on being creative, sustainable, and community-oriented, with a strong sense of history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Eastside

The best time to visit Eastside is from March to May and September to November, when temperatures are moderate, and crowds are thinner. During these times, you can enjoy outdoor activities, festivals, and cultural events without feeling overwhelmed by the heat, humidity, or traffic.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastside

Do:

  • Do explore the local neighborhoods, parks, and attractions on foot, bike, or public transportation to experience the city’s diversity and authenticity.
  • Do try the local food, drinks, and snacks, from taco stands to craft breweries, and discover new flavors and cuisines.
  • Do attend a live show or performance in one of the many theaters, concert halls, or clubs, to get a taste of Eastside’s vibrant arts and entertainment scene.
  • Do respect the local customs, traditions, and beliefs, and be open-minded and tolerant of different cultures and backgrounds.
  • Do dress comfortably, wear sunscreen, and bring a reusable water bottle and snack to stay hydrated and energized.

Don’t:

  • Don’t rely on driving or parking in Eastside, as traffic can be heavy, and public parking is often limited and expensive.
  • Don’t be too loud or rowdy in public places, and avoid disturbing residents or businesses with excessive noise or behavior.
  • Don’t litter, smoke, or use drugs in public spaces, as it’s prohibited by law and disrespectful to the environment and the community.
  • Don’t forget to tip for good service, as it’s customary and expected in most restaurants, cafes, and bars in Eastside.
  • Don’t wander into areas or neighborhoods that are unfamiliar or dangerous, and always stay aware of your surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastside

Eastside is a generally safe neighborhood, but like any other urban area, there are some areas that can be more risky than others. To stay safe, you should avoid walking alone at night, especially in deserted areas, and keep your belongings close to you, especially in crowded spots or public transportation. It’s also advisable to use common sense, be aware of your surroundings, and follow traffic and safety rules.

Health in Eastside

Eastside has several hospitals, clinics, and health centers that offer a wide range of medical services, from routine check-ups to emergency care. To stay healthy, you should drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and protect yourself from mosquito bites, as well as other insects and animals. In case of an emergency, you can dial 911 or visit the nearest hospital or health center for assistance.

Getting to Eastside: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastside is easily accessible by public transportation, bike, or car. The nearest airports are Los Angeles International Airport (LAX), Burbank Airport (BUR), and Long Beach Airport (LGB), which are all within a 30-45 minutes drive from Eastside.

Airports Near Eastside

  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) - The largest airport in Southern California, with international and domestic flights to a variety of destinations.
  • Burbank Airport (BUR) – A smaller airport located in Burbank, with domestic flights to several major cities in the US.
  • Long Beach Airport (LGB) - A small airport located in Long Beach, with domestic flights to several major cities in the US.

Bus Stations Near Eastside

Eastside is well-connected to the city’s public transportation network, including buses and trains, operated by the Los Angeles Metro. It’s also easy to hail a taxi, Uber, or Lyft or rent a bike or scooter to get around.

Other Options

For those who prefer driving, there are several highways and main roads that connect Eastside to other parts of Los Angeles and Southern California, including the 5, 2, 110, and 101 freeways.

How to Get Around in Eastside

Eastside is a walkable neighborhood, with many shops, restaurants, and attractions within walking distance of each other. For longer distances, you can use public transportation, such as buses and trains, or ride a bike or scooter, which are available for rent throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Eastside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Los Angeles has many destinations and excursions that are within a short drive or train ride from Eastside. Some popular options include:

  • Hollywood - Just a short drive from Eastside towards the west, explore the glitz and glamour of Hollywood by visiting its iconic landmarks, such as Hollywood Boulevard, the Walk of Fame, and the Chinese Theatre.
  • Santa Monica - This laid-back beach town located to the west of Eastside, offers great shopping, dining, and entertainment options with stunning ocean views.
  • Downtown LA - Located to the south of Eastside, Downtown LA is a hub of culture, history, and entertainment, with many museums, theaters, and sports events, including the Staples Center and the LA Live complex.
  • Universal Studios Hollywood – This theme park offers entertainment for all ages, with thrilling rides, live shows, and behind-the-scenes tours of your favourite movies.

Conclusion

Eastside is a neighbourhood that captures the essence of Los Angeles, with its vibrant mix of culture, nature, food, and creativity. Whether you’re a local or a tourist, there’s always something new to discover in Eastside, from its historic landmarks to its hidden gems. So, pack your bags, grab your camera and explore the charm and spirit of Eastside with our comprehensive guide.
