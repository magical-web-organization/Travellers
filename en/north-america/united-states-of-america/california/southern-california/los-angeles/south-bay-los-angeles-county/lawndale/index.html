Introduction to Lawndale

Located in the South Bay region of Los Angeles County, Lawndale is a hidden gem often overlooked by travelers. Known for its proximity to the beach, Lawndale is home to a diverse community and boasts a range of activities for visitors.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, the top hiking and biking routes, the food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Lawndale

Whether you’re visiting with family or solo, you’ll find plenty of attractions in Lawndale. From cultural landmarks to natural wonders, here are some of the must-visit places in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Automobile Driving Museum: Automobile enthusiasts will enjoy exploring classic and vintage cars at this museum.
  • South Bay Customs: A hub for motorcycle lovers, South Bay Customs showcases custom bikes and hosts events.
  • The Wayfarers Chapel: A stunning glass chapel designed by Lloyd Wright.

Natural Attractions of Lawndale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Redondo Beach: Located just a few miles away, Redondo Beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, or simply taking a stroll on the pier.
  • Hermosa Beach: A favorite among surfers, Hermosa Beach offers some of the best waves in Southern California.
  • El Segundo Blue Butterfly Preserve: A nature reserve that provides a habitat to the endangered El Segundo blue butterfly.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lawndale

For the adventurous traveler, Lawndale offers several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding South Bay area.

Some of the best routes include:

  • South Bay Bicycle Trail: Starting from Will Rogers State Beach, this 22-mile-long trail takes you through the coastal towns of Manhattan Beach, Hermosa Beach, Redondo Beach, and Torrance.
  • Greenbelt Trail: A 2.8-mile-long trail that begins near the intersection of Marine Avenue and Inglewood Avenue, the Greenbelt Trail is a popular route for hikers and runners.

Best Things to Do in Lawndale: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities to enjoy in Lawndale. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Shopping at the South Bay Galleria: A popular shopping center located just a few miles from Lawndale, the South Bay Galleria boasts over 140 stores, restaurants, and entertainment options.
  • Kayaking at Marina Del Rey: Located just 7 miles from Lawndale, Marina Del Rey is a great place to go kayaking and enjoy views of the coast.
  • A day trip to Disneyland: Located less than an hour away, Disneyland is a must-visit attraction for families.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lawndale

To make the most out of your trip to Lawndale, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that covers the best attractions and activities in the area:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Automobile Driving Museum and South Bay Customs.
Day 2 Take a morning hike on the Greenbelt Trail, then head to the beach for swimming and sunbathing.
Day 3 Take a day trip to Disneyland.
Day 4 Go shopping at the South Bay Galleria and enjoy dinner at one of the many restaurants in the mall.
Day 5 Rent a kayak and explore Marina Del Rey.
Day 6 Visit the Wayfarers Chapel and take a walk through the El Segundo Butterfly Preserve.
Day 7 Spend the day exploring the nearby towns of Hermosa Beach and Redondo Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Lawndale

Lawndale is known for its diverse dining options, ranging from fast food to fine dining. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • In-N-Out Burger: A California classic, In-N-Out is known for their burgers and fries.
  • Tito’s Tacos: Enjoy authentic Mexican food, including tacos, burritos, and more.
  • Pho Consomme: A local favorite, Pho Consomme serves up delicious Vietnamese cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Juice Crafters: Enjoy fresh juices, smoothies, and acai bowls at this health-focused juice bar.
  • Los Angeles Ale Works: A local brewery that offers craft beer and a rotating food menu.
  • The Coffee Bean & Tea Leaf: A chain but this location has its charm. You can find your common drink items and pick up some souvenir items to boot.

Where to Stay in Lawndale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly options to luxury accommodations, Lawndale has a range of lodging options to fit every traveler’s needs.

Some of the top hotels in the area include:

  • Baymont by Wyndham Lawndale: Offers a pool, continental breakfast, and free WiFi.
  • Best Western Plus Gardena Inn & Suites: Features an outdoor pool, complimentary breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Hyatt Place LAX El Segundo: Offers a free airport shuttle, complimentary breakfast, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Lawndale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home, Lawndale has several options. Some of the best places to shop include:

  • Torrance Farmer’s Market: Located nearby, the Torrance Farmer’s Market offers fresh produce, flowers, and local souvenirs.
  • Del Amo Fashion Center: One of the largest malls in the area, Del Amo has over 200 stores, including popular retailers and local boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Lawndale

Lawndale has a diverse community, with a mix of cultures and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including dress codes at religious sites and proper etiquette when dining at restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Lawndale

The best time to visit Lawndale is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re looking to avoid crowds and save money on accommodations, consider visiting during the shoulder season (March to May or September to November).

Do’s and Don’ts of Lawndale

As with any destination, it’s important to be mindful of local customs and etiquette. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Lawndale:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and keep your belongings safe.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or other travelers.
  • litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Lawndale should take precautions when it comes to safety and health.

Safety in Lawndale

While Lawndale is generally a safe destination, travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Be sure to lock your car and hotel room, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Lawndale

It’s important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors in Lawndale. If you’re visiting during the summer months, be aware of the signs of heatstroke, and seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms.

Getting to Lawndale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lawndale is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Lawndale

The nearest airport to Lawndale is Los Angeles International Airport (LAX), which is located about 6 miles northwest of the city.

Bus Stations Near Lawndale

Lawndale is served by several bus lines, including the Torrance Transit, Gardena Transit, and Metro Bus.

Other Options

Travelers can also get to Lawndale via ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft, or by renting a car.

How to Get Around in Lawndale

Lawndale is easy to navigate by car or public transportation.

Some of the top transportation options include:

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in and around Lawndale, including Avis, Hertz, and Enterprise.
  • Public transportation: Lawndale is served by several bus lines, including the Torrance Transit, Gardena Transit, and Metro Bus.

Where to Go Next from Lawndale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore beyond Lawndale, there are plenty of nearby destinations worth checking out:

  • Santa Monica: Just 20 miles northwest of Lawndale, Santa Monica boasts a beautiful pier, shopping options and great beaches.
  • Long Beach: Located just 13 miles south of Lawndale, Long Beach offers a bustling downtown area and the charming waterfront area.

Conclusion

With so many attractions and activities, Lawndale makes for the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore Southern California. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, delicious cuisine, or simply taking in the local culture, Lawndale has something for everyone.
