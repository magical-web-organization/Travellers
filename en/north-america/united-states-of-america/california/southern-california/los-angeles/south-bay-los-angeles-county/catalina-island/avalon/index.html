Introduction to Avalon

Avalon is a picturesque city located in Catalina Island, South Bay (Los Angeles County), Southern California, United States of America. It is known for its crystal clear waters, pristine beaches, and diverse wildlife. Whether you are looking for a quiet weekend getaway or an adventurous trip with your family and friends, Avalon has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Avalon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Catalina Island Museum: This museum features exhibits on the island’s history, including its Hollywood connections, the Catalina Island tile and pottery industry, and the artwork of local artist William Wrigley Jr.

  • Catalina Island Pottery and Tile Factory: See the displays and learn about the history of Catalina Pottery and Tile. You can also purchase souvenirs, collectibles, and gift items.

  • Wrigley Memorial and Botanic Garden: This stunning garden features a unique plant collection from the island and California, as well as a memorial dedicated to the late William Wrigley Jr, the famous businessman who brought the Chicago Cubs to the island for spring training.

Natural Attractions of Avalon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Catalina Island Conservancy: The Conservancy offers a range of adventures to preserve and protect Catalina’s natural resources. It provides a unique opportunity to experience the natural beauty of the island, including hiking, backpacking, and camping.

  • Descanso Beach Club: This is a great place to soak up the sun and enjoy the stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. You can also enjoy activities such as kayaking, paddleboarding, and snorkeling.

  • Two Harbors: This is a remote destination located on the west end of the island, and accessible only by boat. It is a great place for hiking and relaxing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avalon

Avalon has a few exciting biking and hiking trails to explore. Here are a few of them:

  • Trans-Catalina Trail: This 38-mile trail is perfect for advanced hikers and mountain bikers. It takes you from the Avalon trailhead to Two Harbors, and along the way, you can enjoy breathtaking views of the island’s natural flora and fauna.

  • Hermit Gulch Trail: Accessible from Avalon, this is a relatively easy hiking trail that takes you through the heart of the island. Enjoy some of the island’s most beautiful views while exploring the unique flora and fauna of the island.

  • Wrigley Botanic Garden Trail: Another great option for a leisurely hike, this trail leads you through the beautiful gardens of William Wrigley Jr Memorial and Botanic Garden.

Best Things to Do in Avalon: Activities and Tours

  • Zip Line Eco Tour: Take an exciting zip line ride through the canyons and enjoy the breathtaking views of the island.

  • Glass Bottom Boat Tour: See the wonders of the ocean without ever getting wet! You can watch the fish, coral reefs, and even sunken ships from the comfort of a glass-bottom boat.

  • Segway Tour: Take a Segway tour around the city and enjoy the scenic views while learning about Avalon’s rich history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avalon

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in. Explore the town of Avalon and get your bearings.

  • Day 2: Take a guided Segway tour around Avalon and get insights about the history and culture of the city.

  • Day 3: Go on an exciting zip line eco-tour and experience the thrill of flying through the canyons.

  • Day 4: Take a glass-bottom boat tour and explore the beautiful ocean flora and fauna of Catalina Island.

  • Day 5: Enjoy a relaxing day on the Descanso Beach and indulge in water activities like paddleboarding and snorkeling.

  • Day 6: Explore the island’s natural beauty on a Catalina Island Conservancy hike or a bike ride along the Trans-Catalina Trail.

  • Day 7: Departure day. Spend the morning shopping for souvenirs and take home memories of your trip to Avalon.

Food and Drink Scene of Avalon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish & Chips: Enjoy the delicious fish and chips at the Bluewater Avalon Seafood Restaurant, overlooking the beautiful ocean view.

  • Island Pizza: Stop by Coney Island West and try their famous Island Pizza, which uses ingredients from the island’s local farmers and producers.

  • Catalina Island Bison Burger: Visit the Avalon Grille and try their signature dish, the Catalina Island Bison Burger, which is made with locally raised bison and local cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Catalina Island Brew House: The Brew House offers refreshments brewed on the island. You can treat yourself to some refreshing beer and enjoy live music at the same time.

  • Descanso Paradise Cocktails: Enjoy a refreshing Descanso Paradise cocktail while relaxing on the beach at the Descanso Beach Club.

Where to Stay in Avalon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avalon has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses.

  • Hotel Metropole: This four-star hotel is located right on the beach and offers stunning ocean views.

  • Catalina Island Seacrest Inn: This boutique B&B-style hotel features charming rooms and suites with unique decor and Jacuzzi tubs.

  • Catalina Island Dormitory: This hostel is a great option for budget travelers. It is located in the heart of Avalon and offers dormitory-style accommodations.

What to Buy in Avalon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Catalina Island Pottery: Take home some souvenirs from the Catalina Pottery and Tile Factory and choose from a variety of handcrafted pottery and tiles.

  • Avalon Taffy: Try the famous Avalon Taffy, which is made from a secret recipe and comes in a variety of flavors. You can buy this local delicacy from several shops in the city.

  • Catalina Island Wine Mixer: Treat yourself to a bottle of wine from Catalina Island Wine Mixer, where you can choose from varietals like Cabernet Sauvignon and Chardonnay.

Local Culture and Customs of Avalon

Avalon has a unique blend of cultures, and its customs reflect its island-life style. For instance, visitors are encouraged to “take their time,” slow down, and enjoy the natural beauty of the island.

Best Time to Visit Avalon

The best time to visit Avalon is from May to October, when the weather is mild and sunny. The summer months are perfect for water sports and beach activities, while spring and autumn are ideal for hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avalon

Do:

  • Respect local wildlife and flora.

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities that the island has to offer.

  • Learn about the island’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or in any public place.

  • Interrupt the peace and tranquility of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avalon

Avalon is a safe city to visit, but visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions like locking their car and valuables.

Health in Avalon

The main health risks in Avalon are dehydration and sunburn. Visitors should drink plenty of water and wear sun protection when spending time in the sun.

Getting to Avalon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Avalon by ferry or helicopter from Long Beach, San Pedro, and Dana Point. Once on the island, visitors can get around by renting a golf cart, bike, or using the local shuttle service.

Airports Near Avalon

The nearest airport to Avalon is Long Beach Airport, which is about 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Avalon

Visitors can take a bus to Catalina Island from the Long Beach or San Pedro harbors.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a helicopter from Long Beach, San Pedro, or Dana Point.

How to Get Around in Avalon

Visitors can rent golf carts or bikes to get around Avalon. The local shuttle service is also available.

Where to Go Next from Avalon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other parts of Catalina Island, such as Two Harbors, which is only accessible by boat. Or explore nearby areas around Los Angeles, such as Santa Catalina Island and South Bay.

Conclusion

Avalon is a beautiful island destination, offering a variety of outdoor activities, natural beauty, and distinct culture and customs. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their trip to Avalon and experience all that this island paradise has to offer.
