Introduction to Van Nuys

Van Nuys is a neighborhood in San Fernando Valley in Los Angeles, California, known for its diverse population, urban parks, and cultural institutions. The area is named after state Senator Isaac Newton Van Nuys, who founded the town in the early 20th century. Van Nuys is home to several museums, historical landmarks, hiking trails, and local businesses that attract tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Van Nuys

Van Nuys offers a range of cultural, natural, and recreational attractions for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the must-see places in Van Nuys.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Valley Relics Museum: A museum dedicated to preserving the history of San Fernando Valley, featuring classic neon signs, rare documents, photographs, and artifacts from the region’s past.
  • The Japanese Garden: Located on the grounds of the Tillman Water Reclamation Plant, this garden is a tranquil oasis of Japanese architecture, waterfalls, koi ponds, and lush vegetation.
  • The Andres Pico Adobe: A restored adobe house that was once owned by a prominent Californio, featuring period furnishings, exhibits, and guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Van Nuys: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Van Nuys Sherman Oaks Park: A large urban park that offers playgrounds, picnic areas, baseball fields, and basketball courts, as well as a bike path and a lake.
  • Sepulveda Basin Wildlife Reserve: A 200-acre wildlife preserve that provides habitat for more than 200 bird species, including migratory birds and endangered species such as the least Bell’s vireo and the southwestern willow flycatcher. Visitors can hike, bike, or birdwatch on the trails and observation platforms.
  • The Van Nuys Airport Observation Deck: A popular spot for plane spotting, this deck is located above the passenger terminal and offers views of the airport’s runway and control tower.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Van Nuys

Van Nuys offers several hiking and biking trails that provide access to nature and scenic views. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Sepulveda Basin Bike Path: A 9-mile bike path that runs along the Los Angeles River, from the Vanalden Avenue trailhead to the Golden State Freeway trailhead.
  • The Woodley Park Fitness Trail: A 1.3-mile trail that circles the lake at Woodley Park, offering exercise stations and benches, as well as views of the waterfowl and turtles that inhabit the lake.
  • The Haskell Canyon Open Space Trail: A 4.5-mile trail that winds through the oak woodlands and grasslands of the Santa Susana Mountains, offering views of the San Fernando Valley and the Angeles National Forest.

Best Things to Do in Van Nuys: Activities and Tours

Besides visiting the attractions and exploring the natural surroundings, there are many activities and tours to enjoy in Van Nuys, such as:

  • The Hot Rod Tour: A guided tour that takes visitors to the famous hot rod shops and museums of Van Nuys, where they can see vintage cars and motorcycles, meet the owners and builders, and learn about the history of the hot rod culture.
  • The Street Art Tour: A self-guided tour that takes visitors to the murals and graffiti art of Van Nuys, showcasing the work of local and international artists and the diversity of the community.
  • The Food Tour: A culinary tour that takes visitors to the best restaurants, cafes, and food trucks of Van Nuys, where they can sample the local cuisine, such as Mexican, Thai, Middle Eastern, and Italian.

Food and Drink Scene of Van Nuys

Van Nuys offers a diverse range of cuisines, from street food to fine dining, and a lively bar scene, where locals and tourists can enjoy craft beers, cocktails, and live music. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages of Van Nuys.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos al Pastor: One of the most popular Mexican dishes, made with marinated pork, pineapple, onions, and cilantro, served on warm tortillas.
  • Pad Thai: A Thai classic dish, made with stir-fried rice noodles, shrimp or chicken, eggs, tofu, bean sprouts, and peanuts, flavored with tamarind sauce and lime juice.
  • Falafel: A Middle Eastern vegetarian dish, made with ground chickpeas, herbs, and spices, formed into balls or patties, and fried or baked.

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: A refreshing Mexican drink, made with rice, milk, cinnamon, and sugar, served over ice.
  • Boba Tea: A Taiwanese drink, also known as bubble tea or pearl milk tea, made with tea, milk, and tapioca balls, served with a wide straw that allows the pearls to be sucked up and chewed.
  • Craft Beer: A local specialty, made by small breweries that offer unique and flavorful brews, such as MacLeod Ale Brewing Co. and 8one8 Brewing.

Where to Stay in Van Nuys: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Van Nuys offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Van Nuys are:

  • The Hampton Inn & Suites Los Angeles/Sherman Oaks: A comfortable hotel that offers free hot breakfast, Wi-Fi, and parking, as well as a fitness center, a pool, and a business center.
  • The Airtel Plaza Hotel: A retro-inspired hotel that offers aviation-themed rooms, a fitness center, a pool, and free shuttle service to the airport and major attractions.
  • The Sherman Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and cozy rooms, free Wi-Fi, and parking, as well as a 24-hour reception and vending machine.

What to Buy in Van Nuys: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Van Nuys offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that reflect the culture and heritage of the area, including:

  • Neon Signs: A unique and colorful reminder of the Valley Relics Museum, where visitors can buy vintage neon signs and related merchandise, such as T-shirts, hats, and stickers.
  • Hot Rod T-Shirts: A classic and stylish souvenir of the hot rod tour, where visitors can buy custom-made T-shirts and other apparel that feature the logos and designs of the local shops and museums.
  • Coffee and Tea: A fragrant and flavorful souvenir of the local coffee shops and tea houses, where visitors can buy locally roasted coffee beans and loose leaf tea, as well as brewing equipment and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Van Nuys

Van Nuys is a diverse and multicultural area that celebrates various customs and traditions, such as:

  • Cinco de Mayo: A Mexican holiday that commemorates the victory of the Mexican army over the French troops in 1862, celebrated with parades, music, and food.
  • Hanukkah: A Jewish holiday that lasts for eight days and nights, celebrated with the lighting of the menorah, the playing of dreidel, and the eating of latkes and other traditional foods.
  • Ramadan: A Muslim holiday that lasts for one month and involves fasting, prayer, and acts of charity, culminating in the feast of Eid al-Fitr.

Best Time to Visit Van Nuys

The best time to visit Van Nuys is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and crowded, and winter can be rainy and chilly. However, Van Nuys is a year-round destination that offers indoor and outdoor activities for all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Van Nuys

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Van Nuys:

Do:

  • Respect the diversity and richness of the local culture and customs, and learn about them.
  • Enjoy the natural attractions and scenic views, but be aware of the risks and safety rules, such as hiking with a partner or group, staying hydrated, and avoiding suspicious activity or wildlife.
  • Drive or bike defensively and follow the traffic rules and signs, as well as the parking restrictions and fees.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuable items in your car or in public places, such as wallets, phones, or cameras, and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Engage in illegal or risky behavior, such as drug use, excessive alcohol consumption, or illicit activities.
  • Disrespect the locals or the environment, and leave no trace of your visit, such as litter or graffiti.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are important aspects of travel, and Van Nuys is no exception. Here are some tips and information to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Van Nuys

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone or in deserted areas, especially at night.
  • Use common sense and caution when using public transportation, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • In case of emergency, call 911 or the local police or fire department.

Health in Van Nuys

  • Drink plenty of water and avoid dehydration and heat stroke, especially during the summer months.
  • Use sunscreen and protective clothing to prevent sunburn and skin damage.
  • In case of illness or injury, seek medical attention at a local hospital or urgent care center.

Getting to Van Nuys: Transportation Options and Logistics

Van Nuys is easily accessible by various means of transportation, such as:

Airports Near Van Nuys

  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): The largest airport in the region, located about 25 miles south of Van Nuys, offering domestic and international flights and ground transportation options.
  • Bob Hope Airport (BUR): A smaller airport located about 8 miles southeast of Van Nuys, offering domestic flights and ground transportation options.

Bus Stations Near Van Nuys

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: A national bus company that operates from Van Nuys Bus Station, offering connections to major cities in the United States and Canada.
  • Metro Bus Lines: A local bus company that operates from Van Nuys Transit Center, offering connections to many destinations in Los Angeles County and beyond.
