Introduction to North Hollywood

North Hollywood is a district located in San Fernando Valley, in the city of Los Angeles, Southern California. It is home to over 86,000 residents and is known for its vibrant arts scene, trendy restaurants, and diverse culture. North Hollywood is a great place to explore for visitors who want to experience the authentic culture of Southern California.

List of Places to Visit in North Hollywood

North Hollywood offers a wide range of attractions for visitors of all ages. From museums to natural attractions, North Hollywood has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Academy of Television Arts & Sciences: This museum exhibits a collection of television sets, costumes, and other memorabilia from popular TV shows.
  • The Museum of the San Fernando Valley: This museum features exhibits on the history and culture of the San Fernando Valley.
  • The Mural Mile: A 1.3-mile stretch of the North Hollywood Arts District features 7 stunning murals.

Natural Attractions of North Hollywood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tujunga Wash Greenway: This 6.5-mile trail is perfect for a scenic bike ride or hike along the Los Angeles River.
  • Woodbridge Park: This park is a popular landmark for visitors and locals and features a playground, picnic area, and hiking trails.
  • Fryman Canyon Park: This park offers stunning views of the San Fernando Valley and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Hollywood

North Hollywood is a hub for outdoor enthusiasts. With over 100 bike lanes and miles of hiking trails, visitors can explore the natural beauty of the area.

  • NoHo Arts District Bike Tour: A 3-mile self-guided tour that takes visitors through the North Hollywood Arts District and other trendy neighborhoods.
  • Chandler Bikeway: This 2.9-mile bike path runs from North Hollywood through Burbank and features scenic views of the Verdugo Mountains.
  • Hollywood Bowl Overlook Trail: This 3-mile hiking trail starts in Fryman Canyon Park and ends at the iconic Hollywood Bowl Amphitheater.

Best Things to Do in North Hollywood: Activities and Tours

There are many fun things to do in North Hollywood, from exploring the arts scene to attending outdoor events.

  • NoHo Arts District: This trendy neighborhood features art galleries, theaters, and trendy restaurants.
  • Third Thursdays on Lankershim: On the third Thursday of each month, the North Hollywood Arts District hosts a free event featuring live music, food trucks, and open galleries.
  • North Hollywood Farmer’s Market: This market offers fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and handmade crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of North Hollywood

North Hollywood offers a diverse food and drink scene with options ranging from trendy cafes to authentic Mexican restaurants.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken or beef Shawarma from Skewers Cafe.
  • Tacos al pastor from Tacos Los Guichos.
  • Margherita pizza from Pitfire Pizza.

Local Beverages

  • Cold-pressed juice from The Juice Parlor.
  • Handcrafted beers from Boomtown Brewery.
  • Craft cocktails from The Federal NoHo.

Where to Stay in North Hollywood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Hollywood offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to affordable hostels.

  • Best Western Burbank Airport Inn: This hotel is conveniently located near the Bob Hope Airport and offers free shuttle service.
  • Tilt Hotel Universal Hollywood, Ascend Hotel Collection: This trendy hotel offers a rooftop pool and is within walking distance to Universal Studios Hollywood.
  • PodShare North Hollywood: This budget-friendly hostel offers shared communal spaces and secure personal pods.

What to Buy in North Hollywood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to North Hollywood can take home unique souvenirs and locally-made products as mementos of their trip.

  • Handmade pottery from The Ceramic Studio.
  • Souvenirs from the Universal Studios Hollywood gift shop.
  • Fresh produce from the North Hollywood Farmer’s Market.

Local Culture and Customs of North Hollywood

North Hollywood is a diverse and inclusive community that embraces its history while looking to the future. Visitors should take time to appreciate the rich cultural traditions and values of the area.

Best Time to Visit North Hollywood

North Hollywood enjoys a Mediterranean climate with mild winters and hot summers. The best time to visit North Hollywood is from March to May, when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Hollywood

Do:

  • Dress comfortably for outdoor activities.
  • Explore the arts and culture scene.
  • Try local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Travel alone at night.
  • Leave valuables in your car.
  • Ignore traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Hollywood

North Hollywood is generally safe for visitors, but it is recommended to take standard safety precautions such as being aware of your surroundings and not walking alone at night.

Health in North Hollywood

Visitors should be aware of the high air pollution levels in the Los Angeles area and consider wearing a mask during times of poor air quality.

Getting to North Hollywood: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Hollywood is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near North Hollywood

  • Bob Hope Airport (BUR): 3 miles away.
  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): 27 miles away.

Bus Stations Near North Hollywood

  • North Hollywood Station: Served by the Metro Red and Orange Lines.
  • Bob Hope Airport Station: Served by the Metro Red and Orange Lines.

Other Options

  • Car rental.
  • Ride-sharing services.

How to Get Around in North Hollywood

North Hollywood is best explored on foot, bike, or public transportation. The area is bike-friendly, with over 100 confirmed bike lanes.

Where to Go Next from North Hollywood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Universal Studios Hollywood: A popular amusement park located just a short distance from North Hollywood.
  • Griffith Park: An urban park featuring hiking trails, the Griffith Observatory, and the Los Angeles Zoo.
  • Downtown Los Angeles: The vibrant center of the city and home to a variety of cultural attractions and businesses.

Conclusion

North Hollywood is a diverse and exciting destination that offers visitors a variety of activities and attractions. Whether you’re interested in exploring the arts scene or enjoying the natural beauty of Southern California, North Hollywood is a must-visit destination.
