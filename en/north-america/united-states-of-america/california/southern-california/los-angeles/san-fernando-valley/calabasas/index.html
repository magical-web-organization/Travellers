Introduction to Calabasas

Calabasas is a picturesque city located in the San Fernando Valley of Los Angeles, Southern California. With its serene landscapes, stunning views, and rich history, Calabasas is an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and cultural enthusiasts alike. The city is also famous for being home to many celebrities, including the Kardashians and Justin Bieber. Whether you are planning a weekend getaway or a week-long trip, Calabasas has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Calabasas

Here are some of the must-visit places in Calabasas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Leonis Adobe Museum: This adobe house, built-in 1844, is a state historical monument that features artifacts and antiques from early California life. It is an excellent place to learn about the history of Calabasas.

  2. The Calabasas Historical Society: This society aims to preserve and promote the history of Calabasas. Visit their website to learn more about their events and projects

  3. The Mosaic Tile House: This quirky and colorful house is a hidden gem of Calabasas. The entire house is covered in intricate mosaic tiles, and it is open to the public for tours.

Natural Attractions of Calabasas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Malibu Creek State Park: This park is a paradise for nature lovers. With hiking trails, creeks, and stunning rock formations, it is no surprise that this park was used as a filming location for many movies and TV shows.

  2. Topanga State Park: This park is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. With a variety of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, this park has something for everyone.

  3. Las Virgenes Canyon: This scenic canyon offers breathtaking views of the Santa Monica Mountains. There are many hiking trails to explore, and it is also an excellent spot for picnicking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calabasas

Calabasas is an excellent place for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. The Santa Monica Mountains Backbone Trail: This 67-mile trail is part of the National Trail System and offers spectacular views of the mountains and the Pacific Ocean.

  2. Calabasas Peak Motorway Trail: This 3.5-mile trail offers panoramic views of the Santa Monica Mountains and the San Fernando Valley.

  3. Stunt High Trail: This 8.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the mountains and the city. It is a challenging hike, but the views are worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Calabasas: Activities and Tours

  1. Wine Tasting Tours: Calabasas is home to many vineyards and wineries that offer tours and tastings. Enjoy a day of wine tasting and learn about the history of winemaking in the region.

  2. Horseback Riding: Explore the beautiful scenery of Calabasas on horseback. There are many ranches and stables in the area that offer guided horseback tours.

  3. Shopping: Calabasas is famous for its luxury boutique stores and high-end shopping centers. Visit The Commons at Calabasas and The Promenade at Westlake for a day of shopping.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calabasas

Day 1: Hike the Santa Monica Mountains Backbone Trail Day 2: Explore the Leonis Adobe Museum and Las Virgenes Canyon Day 3: Take a horseback riding tour Day 4: Enjoy a day of wine tasting Day 5: Visit Malibu Creek State Park Day 6: Shopping at The Commons at Calabasas and The Promenade at Westlake Day 7: Relax at one of the local spas

Where to Stay in Calabasas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calabasas offers a wide range of accommodation options. Here are some of the best:

  1. The Four Seasons Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of Calabasas and offers stunning views of the mountains.

  2. The Anza Hotel: This boutique hotel is located near The Commons at Calabasas and offers modern amenities and a chic atmosphere.

  3. Oakwood Apartments: If you are looking for a more private and home-like experience, Oakwood Apartments offers fully furnished apartments with kitchens and laundry.

Local Culture and Customs of Calabasas

Calabasas is a diverse and inclusive community that values its history and natural beauty. Residents of Calabasas take pride in their city and are welcoming to visitors. It is also common for locals to participate in outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Time to Visit Calabasas

The best time to visit Calabasas is during the spring and fall seasons when temperatures are mild, and the scenery is the most beautiful. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be rainy. However, even during the winter and summer months, Calabasas still has many indoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calabasas

Do:

  1. Respect the natural environment and wildlife
  2. Take advantage of outdoor activities and scenic views
  3. Visit local museums and landmarks

Don’t:

  1. litter or damage the public property
  2. disrespect local customs and culture
  3. engage in any illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Calabasas

Calabasas is a safe community, and visitors should take normal precautions against theft and petty crime. When outdoors, it is essential to be prepared for potential wildlife encounters like snakes or mountain lions.

Health in Calabasas

Calabasas has many healthcare facilities and hospitals that offer quality care in case of emergency. Visitors should have their health insurance and necessary medication with them.

Getting to Calabasas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calabasas can be reached by car or public transportation. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Calabasas

  1. Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) - 30 miles away
  2. Burbank Bob Hope Airport (BUR) - 23 miles away

Bus Stations Near Calabasas

  1. Calabasas Park and Ride
  2. Warner Center Park and Ride

Other Options

  1. Car Rental
  2. Taxi
  3. Uber/Lyft

How to Get Around in Calabasas

Calabasas is a car-centric city, and most visitors prefer to rent a car. However, there are also ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft available. The city also has a good public transportation system with bus routes that connect Calabasas to other cities in Los Angeles County.

Where to Go Next from Calabasas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Malibu: This beach town is only a 20-minute drive from Calabasas and offers stunning beaches and a relaxed atmosphere.
  2. Los Angeles: The city of Los Angeles is only 30 minutes away and has many tourist attractions like Hollywood, Beverly Hills, and Universal Studios.
  3. Santa Barbara: This charming coastal town is two hours north of Calabasas and is worth a day trip to explore the beautiful beaches and architecture.

Conclusion

Calabasas is a unique and beautiful city that offers a perfect mix of outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, and luxury experiences. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip to Calabasas confidently and enjoy all the best that this city has to offer.
