Introduction to Long Beach

Located in the Gateway Cities region of Los Angeles County, Southern California, Long Beach is a vibrant coastal city known for its beautiful beaches, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and diverse food and drink scene. With a population of over 460,000, Long Beach is the seventh most populous city in California.

List of Places to Visit in Long Beach

There are endless things to see and do in Long Beach. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Queen Mary: One of the most famous landmarks of Long Beach, this historic ocean liner is now a museum and hotel.
  • Long Beach Museum of Art: Housed in a historic mansion overlooking the ocean, this museum features contemporary art exhibitions.
  • Museum of Latin American Art: This museum showcases contemporary and modern art of Latin American artists.
  • Rancho Los Alamitos: Explore the history of Long Beach through this beautifully restored ranch house, gardens, and barns.

Natural Attractions of Long Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Long Beach Waterfront: Take a stroll along the beach and enjoy the sunset or rent a bike and explore the bike path along the shoreline.
  • El Dorado Nature Center: This serene nature reserve offers hiking and bird-watching opportunities with over 100 bird species and scenic views of the San Gabriel Mountains.
  • Naples Island: This charming island is a must-visit for its canals, walkways, Italian gondola rides, and scenic views of the Long Beach skyline.

Best Things to Do in Long Beach: Activities and Tours

  • Aquarium of the Pacific: Explore the diverse marine life of the Pacific Ocean including sharks, rays, sea lions, and penguins.
  • Harbor Breeze Cruises: Go whale-watching, island hiking, or participate in a sunset dinner cruise.
  • Long Beach Symphony: Enjoy world-class performances by the Long Beach Symphony Orchestra.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Beach

Day 1: Visit the Aquarium of the Pacific and explore the Long Beach Waterfront.

Day 2: Rent a bike and explore the bike path along the shoreline and enjoy the sunset at the beach.

Day 3: Hike at the El Dorado Nature Center and bird-watch.

Day 4: Take a tour of the Queen Mary and enjoy the views of Long Beach from the ship.

Day 5: Explore the art scene of Long Beach including the Long Beach Museum of Art and the Museum of Latin American Art.

Day 6: Take a sunset dinner cruise with Harbor Breeze Cruises.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the Long Beach Antique Market and say goodbye to Long Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Beach

Long Beach offers a diverse food and drink scene ranging from seafood and Mexican cuisine to craft beers and cocktails. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Tacos: Try the classic Baja-style fish tacos at Hole Mole.
  • Cambodian Noodles: Satisfy your cravings for exotic cuisine with noodles at Sophy’s.
  • Doughnuts: Taste the famous doughnuts with eclectic flavors at The Doughnut Hole.

Local Beverages

  • Long Beach Beer Lab: Sample craft beers with unique flavors like hibiscus and horchata.
  • Portuguese Bend Distilling: Savor handmade gin, vodka, and whiskey in this distillery with a cozy atmosphere.
  • Restauration: Enjoy the farm-to-table cocktails with organic ingredients at this trendy restaurant.

Where to Stay in Long Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Beach offers various options for accommodations including luxury hotels, budget hostels, and cozy guesthouses. Some of the popular choices are:

  • The Queen Mary: Stay in the historic ocean liner and experience the unique ambiance of the ship.
  • Hyatt Regency Long Beach: Enjoy the stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and Long Beach skyline from this luxury hotel.
  • Beachrunner’s Inn: This cozy guesthouse offers a home-like atmosphere and is within walking distance to the beach.

What to Buy in Long Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Long Beach Antique Market: Shop for vintage items, antiques, collectibles, and unique souvenirs at this open-air market.
  • 4th Street Retro Row: Explore the vintage shops, boutiques, cafes, and bookstores on 4th Street.
  • Belmont Shore: Stroll down the Second Street and shop for boutique clothing stores, home decor, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Beach

Long Beach embraces a diverse culture with a mix of ethnicities and communities. It’s known for its LGBTQ+ community, annual Pride parade, and cultural festivals. The city also has a vibrant street art scene with murals and graffiti around the city.

Best Time to Visit Long Beach

The best time to visit Long Beach is between late June and early September. The weather is warm and dry, and there are various outdoor events and activities. However, it’s also the busiest season, and the hotel rates are higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Beach

Do:

  • Do explore the local art galleries, food markets, and beaches.
  • Do take a bike along the beach bike path.
  • Do attend the cultural festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave valuables in your car.
  • Don’t litter or damage the public property.
  • Don’t swim at the beach when there are red flags indicating hazardous water conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Beach

Long Beach is generally a safe city, but it’s always good to take some safety precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, especially in deserted areas. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables with you.

Health in Long Beach

Long Beach has several hospitals and medical centers, and most pharmacies are open 24/7. If you need emergency medical assistance, dial 911.

Getting to Long Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Long Beach

  • Long Beach Airport: Located only three miles northeast of downtown Long Beach, this airport offers domestic flights from several airlines including JetBlue, Southwest, and Delta.
  • Los Angeles International Airport: This airport is about 20 miles from Long Beach and offers domestic and international flights from various airlines.

Bus Stations Near Long Beach

  • Greyhound: Located at 1463 E. Anaheim Street, this bus station offers daily services to and from Long Beach.
  • Amtrak: Located at 601 N. Alameda Street, this station offers train services to and from Long Beach.

Other Options

Long Beach is easily accessible by car and train, and several car rental companies operate in the city.

How to Get Around in Long Beach

Long Beach offers several transportation options including buses, bikes, taxis, and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Long Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Catalina Island: Take a ferry ride from Long Beach to the nearby island for a day trip of hiking, snorkeling, and relaxing on the beach.
  • Disneyland: Located in Anaheim, about 20 miles from Long Beach, Disneyland is a must-visit attraction for families and kids.
  • Los Angeles: Explore the cultural and entertainment hub of California with several attractions like Hollywood, Beverly Hills, and museums.

Conclusion

Long Beach is a diverse and exciting city with endless opportunities for adventure and fun. From the stunning beaches and outdoor activities to the rich cultural attractions and food scene, Long Beach has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and explore the best of Long Beach, California.
