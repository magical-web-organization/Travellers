Introduction to Lakewood

Located in Gateway Cities, Los Angeles County, Southern California, Lakewood is a city with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. The city is known for its numerous historical sites, scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. With plenty to see and do, Lakewood is a great destination for visitors of all ages, whether you’re traveling with family, friends, or solo.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, local culture and customs, and safety and health tips. Additionally, we’ll provide you with information on how to get there, getting around, and nearby destinations that you can explore after your visit to Lakewood.

List of Places to Visit in Lakewood

When it comes to places to visit in Lakewood, visitors have a wide range of options. Our top picks include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lakewood boasts several museums and historical places that represent various aspects of the city’s heritage. These include:

  • Lakewood Heritage Center: A living history museum that showcases life in Lakewood during the 20th century.
  • McCoy’s Museum: A private collection of antique cars, trucks, tractors, and other forms of transportation.
  • Paramount Iceland: A historic ice skating rink that has been in use since 1940.

Natural Attractions of Lakewood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lakewood offers a range of natural attractions, including parks, hiking/biking trails, and scenic views. Some of our top picks include:

  • Mayfair Park: A public park with picnic facilities, playgrounds, sports fields, and a swimming pool.
  • San Gabriel River Trail: A 38-mile paved trail that connects several cities and offers stunning views of the San Gabriel Mountains.
  • El Dorado Nature Center: A 105-acre park with nature trails, lakes, bird-watching opportunities, and a visitor center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakewood

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love exploring the hiking/biking trails in and around Lakewood. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • San Gabriel River Trail: A 38-mile paved trail that runs through several cities and offers scenic views of the San Gabriel Mountains.
  • El Dorado Park: A park with 4 miles of hiking trails that take you through oak woodlands, riparian zones, and other natural habitats.
  • Whittier Greenway Trail: A 4.5-mile trail that runs along an abandoned railway line and offers views of local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Lakewood: Activities and Tours

Lakewood is full of fun and exciting activities that cater to every age group and interest. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Long Beach Aquarium of the Pacific: A world-class aquarium that showcases the marine life of the Pacific Ocean.
  • The Queen Mary: A historic ocean liner that now serves as a hotel and museum.
  • Rancho Los Alamitos: A restored adobe ranch house that showcases the history of California’s early ranching period.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakewood

Lakewood’s food and drink scene is diverse and flavorful, with lots of options for all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • California Burrito: A burrito filled with carne asada, french fries, cheese, guacamole, and sour cream.
  • Fish Tacos: Tacos filled with grilled or fried fish, cabbage, pico de gallo, and crema.
  • Asian Fusion: Lakewood has a range of Asian-inspired fusion restaurants that offer unique and delicious dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Lakewood has several craft breweries that offer a wide range of beer styles.
  • Coffee Shops: Lakewood has several coffee shops that offer specialty coffee drinks and pastries.
  • Cocktails: Lakewood has a range of restaurants and bars that offer creative and delicious cocktails.

Where to Stay in Lakewood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lakewood offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, whether you’re looking for a hotel, hostel, or guesthouse. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Lakewood Country Club: A hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a golf course, and a range of amenities.
  • Rodeway Inn and Suites: A budget-friendly hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Airbnb: Lakewood has several Airbnb options that offer affordable and unique accommodations.

What to Buy in Lakewood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Lakewood has a range of shopping options. Some of our top picks include:

  • Lakewood Center: A shopping mall that offers a range of clothing, accessories, and electronics stores.
  • Long Beach Antique Market: A flea market that offers vintage and antique items.
  • Local Farmer’s Market: Several local farmer’s markets sell fresh produce, handmade goods, and other local products.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakewood

Lakewood is a city that values community, diversity, and history. Visitors can experience the city’s culture and customs through its museums, historical sites, and events. Some notable events include the Lakewood Fourth of July Celebration, the Lakewood Ice Show, and the Long Beach Crawfish Festival.

Best Time to Visit Lakewood

The best time to visit Lakewood is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and crowds are comparatively lower. During summers, visitors can experience the city’s outdoor events and activities, but be sure to pack light and wear sunscreen.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakewood

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Explore the city’s hiking/biking routes and natural attractions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural habitats.
  • Engage in unsafe or disruptive behavior.
  • Disrespect local communities or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakewood

Lakewood is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take basic precautions, such as not leaving valuables in their cars or walking alone at night. The city has a well-trained police department and emergency services.

Health in Lakewood

Lakewood has several medical facilities and hospitals that offer emergency and non-emergency medical treatment. Visitors should drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and take necessary precautions while hiking or biking in natural habitats.

Getting to Lakewood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Lakewood by air, bus, or car. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Lakewood

The nearest airports to Lakewood are:

  • Long Beach Airport (LGB): Located 6 miles from Lakewood, LGB is the most convenient airport.
  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): Located 22 miles from Lakewood, LAX is a major airport that offers a wide range of flights.

Bus Stations Near Lakewood

The nearest bus stations to Lakewood are:

  • Los Angeles Union Station: Located 20 miles from Lakewood, Union Station is the main transportation hub for the city.
  • Long Beach Transit Mall: Located 7 miles from Lakewood, the Transit Mall offers several bus routes that connect to nearby cities and destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lakewood via rental car services, ride-sharing services such as Uber/Lyft, or private transportation services such as taxis or limousines.

How to Get Around in Lakewood

The best way to get around Lakewood is by car, either rental or private. The city has several car rental options and offers free public parking. Visitors can also use ride-sharing services such as Uber/Lyft, or public transportation services such as buses or shuttles.

Where to Go Next from Lakewood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Lakewood, visitors can plan nearby excursions to:

  • Long Beach: Known for its beaches, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • Los Angeles: Known for its museums, nightlife, and entertainment industry.
  • Catalina Island: A stunning island paradise with beaches, water sports, and hiking trails.
