Introduction to Cerritos

Cerritos is a charming city located in the Gateway Cities area of Los Angeles County, Southern California. Known as the “City of Innovation,” Cerritos has a rich history and an exciting contemporary culture. The city is home to various art pieces, museums, parks, and attractions, making it a suitable destination for families, solo travelers, and couples.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, eat, and stay in Cerritos, along with other helpful tips and information.

List of Places to Visit in Cerritos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cerritos Sculpture Garden: A serene park that features a vast collection of public art, including sculptures and murals, created by well-known artists from around the world.
  • Heritage Park: A beautiful park that showcases the history of Cerritos and Southern California. It features several historic buildings, including the Cerritos Museum and a blacksmith shop.
  • Cerritos Center for the Performing Arts: A premier performing arts venue that hosts world-renowned performers and shows throughout the year.
  • Richard Nixon Library and Birthplace: A presidential museum and library dedicated to the life and achievements of the 37th President of the United States, Richard Nixon.

Natural Attractions of Cerritos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Liberty Park: A beautifully landscaped park with a lake, walking trails, and picnic areas. It hosts several community events and festivals throughout the year.
  • Cerritos Regional Park: A large park with lakes, sports facilities, and walking trails. It is a popular spot for jogging, biking, and family picnics.
  • Rio San Gabriel Park: A scenic park with hiking trails, a fishing lake, and picnic areas. It offers a peaceful escape from the busy city life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cerritos

Cerritos and its surrounding areas offer several scenic hiking and biking trails that are suitable for all levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • San Gabriel River Trail: An 8-mile-long trail that follows the San Gabriel River and offers stunning views of the surrounding nature.
  • Whittier Greenway Trail: A peaceful and scenic trail that runs parallel to Greenleaf Avenue and features beautiful trees, plants, and flowers.
  • La Mirada Creek Trail: A picturesque trail that follows the La Mirada Creek and offers a serene environment for walking and jogging.

Best Things to Do in Cerritos: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural and cultural attractions, Cerritos offers several fun activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  • Go Kart World: A thrilling indoor go-karting venue that offers high-speed rides for children and adults.
  • Knott’s Berry Farm: A popular amusement park with roller coasters, water rides, and live entertainment shows. It is located just a few miles away from Cerritos.
  • Cerritos Farmers Market: A weekly event that features fresh produce, local crafts, and food trucks. It takes place every Saturday from 8 am to 12 pm.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cerritos

If you’re planning to spend a week in Cerritos, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Cerritos

Cerritos offers a diverse and delicious food and drink scene that reflects the city’s cultural diversity. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Santouka Ramen: A popular Japanese chain that offers delicious and comforting ramen bowls.
  • El Rocoto: A Peruvian restaurant that serves authentic dishes, including ceviche and lomo saltado.
  • Kang Ho-dong Baekjeong: A Korean BBQ restaurant that offers high-quality meat cuts and side dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Tea: A sweet and creamy iced tea made with black tea, condensed milk, and spices.
  • Michelada: A popular Mexican drink that is made with beer, lime juice, and a variety of spices and sauces.
  • California Wine: The state of California is known for producing some of the best wines in the world. You can find several wineries and wine bars in and around Cerritos.

Getting to Cerritos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cerritos is conveniently located in Southern California, and there are several transportation options available to reach the city.

Airports Near Cerritos

  • Long Beach Airport: A small airport located about 11 miles away from Cerritos. It offers domestic flights by several airlines, including Delta and Southwest.
  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): One of the busiest airports in the world, located about 25 miles away from Cerritos. It offers domestic and international flights by several airlines.
  • John Wayne Airport: An airport located about 22 miles away from Cerritos, offering domestic flights by several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Cerritos

  • Norwalk/Santa Fe Springs Transit Center: A bus station located about 4 miles away from Cerritos. It connects to several local and regional bus routes.
  • Long Beach Transit: A bus station located about 10 miles away from Cerritos. It connects to several local and regional bus routes.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in and around Cerritos, including Avis, Hertz, and Budget.
  • Taxi/Cab: Several taxi and cab services are available in Cerritos, including Uber and Lyft.
