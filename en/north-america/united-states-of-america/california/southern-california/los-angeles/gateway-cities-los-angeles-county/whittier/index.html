Introduction to Whittier

Whittier is a beautiful city located in the Gateway Cities region of Los Angeles County, Southern California. It is home to a vibrant community, stunning natural landscapes, and many attractions that will captivate both locals and visitors. If you’re planning a trip to Southern California and considering Whittier as a destination, this travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know to make your visit unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Whittier

Whittier is a city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or an adventure seeker, you’ll find plenty of places to visit in Whittier.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Whittier has a rich cultural and historical heritage, which is reflected in the numerous museums and historical places that you can visit. Some of the must-see attractions include:

  • Whittier Museum: The Whittier Museum is located in the historic uptown district and showcases the history of the city. It has many exhibits that depict the life of the people who lived in Whittier through the centuries.
  • Pio Pico State Historic Park: This park contains the adobe home of Pio Pico, the last governor of California under Mexican rule. It’s a great place to learn about the history of Whittier and California.
  • Hellman Park: Hellman Park is great for hiking and exploring the natural beauty of Whittier. The park has stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Natural Attractions of Whittier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whittier is nestled in a beautiful valley, surrounded by hills and mountains. The city has many natural attractions that you can explore. These include:

  • Whittier Greenway Trail: This trail is perfect for biking, jogging, or walking. This 4.5-mile-long path runs through the heart of the city and provides stunning views of Whittier’s surroundings.
  • Turnbull Canyon: Turnbull Canyon is a great place to hike and explore. The canyon is teeming with wildlife and offers breathtaking views of the valley.
  • Murphy Ranch Park: Murphy Ranch Park is tucked away in the hills of Whittier. It features many hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whittier

For those who love hiking and biking, Whittier has many trails to offer. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • Hellman Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking and provides stunning views of the valley and the surrounding mountains.
  • Skyline Trail: Skyline Trail is a great place to go hiking or biking. The trail winds through the hills and provides beautiful views of the city.
  • Greenway Trail: As mentioned before, the Greenway Trail is perfect for biking or walking. It offers fantastic views of the city as you ride or walk along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Whittier: Activities and Tours

Whittier has many activities to offer visitors. Here are a few of the best things to do in Whittier:

  • Whittier Farmer’s Market: The Whittier Farmer’s Market is held every Friday from 8 am to 1 pm in the Historic Uptown District. The market offers a variety of fresh produce, food vendors, and live music.
  • Whittier Antique Faire: On the first Saturday of every month, the Whittier Antique Faire is held. Here you can browse through antiques, vintage items, and memorabilia.
  • Whittier Village Cinemas: Catch the latest movie at Whittier Village Cinemas, situated in the Whittwood Town Center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Whittier

If you’re planning to spend a week in Whittier, here’s a recommended itinerary:

  • Monday: Explore the Whittier Museum and learn about the city’s history.
  • Tuesday: Head to Turnbull Canyon for a morning hike followed by lunch at one of the local restaurants in Historic Uptown Whittier.
  • Wednesday: Rent a bike and ride the Greenway Trail.
  • Thursday: Visit the Pio Pico State Historic Park and learn about California’s Mexican heritage.
  • Friday: Check out the Whittier Farmer’s Market and browse through the stalls.
  • Saturday: Visit the Whittier Antique Faire and shop for unique finds.
  • Sunday: Take a drive to the nearby Santa Ana Mountains and explore the trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Whittier

Whittier’s food scene is diverse and offers plenty of options for all tastes. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tortas Ahogadas: This is a traditional Mexican sandwich with beef or pork, served in spicy tomato sauce.
  • Pupusas: Pupusas are stuffed corn tortillas filled with cheese, beans, or meat. They’re served with a side of coleslaw and tomato sauce.
  • Bacon-Wrapped Hot Dogs: This is a delicious Mexican street food that features hot dogs wrapped in bacon and topped with grilled onions and peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: Horchata is a rice-based drink mixed with cinnamon and sugar.
  • Micheladas: Micheladas are Mexican beers mixed with lime juice, tomato juice, hot sauce, and spices.

Where to Stay in Whittier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whittier has many accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Whittier: This hotel offers luxurious rooms and is centrally located in the city.
  • Friendly Hills Inn: This hotel is perfect for those who want to stay close to the hiking trails and natural attractions.
  • Travelogue Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of Whittier.

What to Buy in Whittier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, these are some popular items you can purchase in Whittier:

  • Whittier Pottery: Whittier has been renowned for its pottery for more than a century, so this is a great souvenir to bring back home.
  • Vintage Items: The Whittier Antique Faire offers many vintage finds like old postcards, jewelry, and memorabilia.
  • Local Craft Beer: Whittier has several breweries that offer local craft beer. Try some of the best varieties and bring them back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Whittier

Whittier is a multicultural city with a rich history. The city celebrates this diversity in its annual cultural events and festivals. One of the most significant cultural events is the Dia de Los Muertos in Historic Uptown Whittier.

Best Time to Visit Whittier

The best time to visit Whittier is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is milder, and the tourist crowds are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whittier

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Whittier’s hiking trails.
  • Attend the events and festivals that showcase the city’s cultural diversity.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural areas or disrespectful of them in any way.
  • Bring outside animals to Whittier’s parks and hiking trails.
  • Overspeed in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Whittier

Whittier is a safe city, but like any other place, visitors should practice common sense and take reasonable precautions, especially when traveling late at night to the outdoor areas.

Health in Whittier

Whittier has many medical facilities that provide general healthcare services. Some of the hospitals and clinics are PIH Health Hospital - Whittier, Oaktree Healthcare, and Whittier Urgent Care.

Getting to Whittier: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whittier is located about 25 miles southeast of Downtown Los Angeles. Here are the ways you can get to Whittier:

Airports Near Whittier

  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): This airport is located about 30 miles from Whittier.
  • John Wayne Airport (SNA): This airport is located about 20 miles from Whittier.

Bus Stations Near Whittier

  • Greyhound Station: This station is located in downtown Los Angeles, about 20 miles from Whittier.
  • Metro Bus Station: This station is located in Norwalk, about 6 miles from Whittier.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: You can rent a car from LAX or John Wayne airport and drive to Whittier.
  • Ridesharing: Services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in the area.

How to Get Around in Whittier

Whittier is a small city, and most places are accessible by foot or bike. However, if you need to take public transit, Whittier Transit is the local bus service that runs around the city.

Where to Go Next from Whittier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whittier is just a short drive away from many other exciting destinations. Here are some places that you might want to add to your itinerary:

Santa Ana Mountains

The Santa Ana Mountains offer miles of beautiful trails for hiking and exploring, with stunning vistas and waterfalls.

Catalina Island

Catalina Island offers visitors with beautiful beaches, snorkeling, and hiking.

Conclusion

Whittier is a hidden gem in Southern California, with a rich cultural history, stunning natural landscapes, and a diverse food scene. Whether you’re visiting for the hiking, food, or just a relaxing vacation, you won’t be disappointed in Whittier.
