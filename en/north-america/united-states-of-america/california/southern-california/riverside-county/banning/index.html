Introduction to Banning

Banning is a hidden gem located in Riverside County, Southern California. Nestled against the foothills of San Bernardino Mountains, Banning is a charming destination that boasts of rich history, stunning landscapes, and outdoor adventures that will surely take your breath away. The city is home to about 30,000 residents and is known for its pleasant weather, excellent cuisine, and friendly locals.

Whether you’re a history buff, adrenaline junkie, or nature lover, there’s something for everyone in Banning. From exploring the city’s historic landmarks and museums to hiking on beautiful trails and immersing yourself in the captivating scenery, there’s no dull moment in Banning.

Read on to discover all the places to visit, activities to do, and things to know before visiting Banning, California.

List of Places to Visit in Banning

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gilman Historic Ranch and Wagon Museum: The Gilman Ranch is a former cattle ranch and apple orchard that is now a museum dedicated to showcasing the history of the area. Visitors can tour the ranch house, see the old wagons, and learn about early California ranch life.
  • San Manuel Gathering Hall: This Native American museum and cultural center provides a deep insight into the tribe’s culture and history. It showcases a wide range of cultural artifacts such as baskets, jewelry, as well as interactive exhibits, and performances.
  • Malki Museum: The Malki Museum is an education and research center that focuses on the history and cultures of the Cahuilla, Luiseño, and Serrano Indian tribes. Visitors can participate in lectures and workshops about Native American culture and history.

Natural Attractions of Banning: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • San Bernardino National Forest: San Bernardino National Forest, located just minutes from Banning, is a stunning natural area that offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, fishing, and more. The forest is known for its unique rock formations, dense forests, and breathtaking vistas.
  • Mt. San Jacinto State Park: Mt. San Jacinto State Park is a popular hiking destination that offers stunning views of the desert, mountains, and valleys. Visitors can hike through pine forests, lush meadows, and rocky terrain.
  • Morongo Valley Preserve: The Morongo Valley Preserve is a natural oasis that features a variety of wildlife, including bighorn sheep, coyotes, and more. Hiking trails wind through the preserve, taking in stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banning

Banning is an ideal destination for both hiking and biking enthusiasts. The city is home to several trails and paths that offer scenic views and challenging terrain.

Hiking Trails

  • Skyline Trail: Skyline Trail is a popular hiking trail that follows the ridgeline of Mount San Jacinto. The trail is challenging but offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Banning Bench Trail: Banning Bench Trail is a 1.5-mile trail that offers stunning views of the valley and the San Bernardino Mountains.
  • 3 Sisters Waterfalls Trail: 3 Sisters Waterfalls Trail is a 4.3-mile hike that takes you through beautiful streams, waterfalls, and pools before leading to the three cascading waterfalls.

Biking Paths

  • Bogart Park Mountain Bike Trail: Bogart Park Mountain Bike Trail is a challenging 14-mile trail that winds through the San Bernardino National Forest.
  • Whitewater Preserve: Whitewater Preserve is a scenic mountain bike trail that spans 25 miles, taking in a variety of terrains, from rocky singletrack to fire roads.

Best Things to Do in Banning: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Banning. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone in this charming destination.

  • Palm Springs Aerial Tramway: Located just a few miles from Banning, Palm Springs Aerial Tramway is a must-do experience. The tram takes visitors up to the top of Mount San Jacinto, where they can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Oak Glen Apple Orchards: The Oak Glen Apple Orchards offer a fun family-friendly experience, where visitors can pick their apples, enjoy homemade apple pies, and learn about the area’s agriculture.
  • Banning Stagecoach Days: Banning Stagecoach Days is an annual festival that celebrates the area’s western heritage. The festival features live music, food vendors, and a rodeo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Banning

Day 1: Explore the Gilman Historic Ranch and Wagon Museum and hike the Banning Bench Trail. Day 2: Visit the San Manuel Gathering Hall and take a mountain bike ride on the Bogart Park Mountain Bike Trail. Day 3: Hike the Skyline Trail and visit the Malki Museum. Day 4: Take the Palm Springs Aerial Tramway and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding area. Day 5: Pick apples and enjoy homemade apple pies at the Oak Glen Apple Orchards. Day 6: Hike the 3 Sisters Waterfalls Trail and take a tour of the San Bernardino National Forest. Day 7: Attend the Banning Stagecoach Days festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Banning

Must-Try Dishes

Banning’s food scene is a delightful blend of Mexican, American, and Asian influences. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Fish Tacos: Fish tacos are a staple of Southern California’s cuisine, and Banning is no different. Try the fish tacos at El Sarape or El Rancho.
  • Tri-Tip Sandwich: Tri-tip sandwiches are a Banning classic. Lee’s Famous Recipe Chicken is known for its delicious tri-tip sandwiches.
  • Pho: Banning is home to several excellent Vietnamese restaurants, including PHO TYC and Pho Star.

Local Beverages

Banning is known for its craft beer scene. Visit the Banning Brewery and sample some of their unique brews. If wine is more your style, visit the Morongo Valley Winery and enjoy some of their delicious wines.

Where to Stay in Banning: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Banning has several accommodation options for visitors.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Banning-Beaumont: This hotel is located just minutes from several attractions, including the Oak Glen Apple Orchards and the Gilman Historic Ranch and Wagon Museum.
  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites Banning: This is another excellent choice for visitors to Banning. The hotel is conveniently located and offers excellent amenities.
  • Stagecoach Inn: Stagecoach Inn is a charming bed and breakfast that offers a unique and authentic experience of the area’s western heritage.

What to Buy in Banning: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Banning offers several unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home with them:

  • Banning Branded Merchandise: Visit the Banning Chamber of Commerce and pick up some Banning-branded merchandise, including t-shirts, hats, and keychains.
  • Oak Glen Apples: Take some of the area’s most famous produce home with you. Stop by one of the many apple orchards in the area and pick up some apples to take home.
  • Native American Crafts: Visit the Malki Museum and pick up some beautiful, handcrafted Native American crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Banning

Banning is a friendly and welcoming community that celebrates its rich history and culture. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s Native American heritage and traditions. It’s also important to remember that Banning is a small town, and locals may move at a slower pace than visitors are used to.

Best Time to Visit Banning

The best time to visit Banning is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be quite hot, with temperatures often reaching into the triple digits. Winter can be chilly, with occasional snow in the surrounding mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banning

Do:

  • Do take a scenic drive up Mount San Jacinto.
  • Do try some of the local craft beers.
  • Do explore the area’s Native American history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any of the area’s natural landmarks.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the area’s Native American traditions.
  • Don’t ignore the traffic laws and posted speed limits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Banning

Banning is a relatively safe destination with low crime rates. Visitors should always practice common sense and take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Banning

Banning, like the rest of California, is prone to wildfire season. Visitors should check with local authorities to verify if conditions are safe for outdoor activities.

Getting to Banning: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Banning

The closest major airport to Banning is Palm Springs International Airport, which is about 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Banning

Banning is serviced by several bus companies, including Greyhound and Flixbus.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Banning from Los Angeles or other nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Banning

Banning is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bike. Having access to a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Banning: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Banning is located in a beautiful part of Southern California, and there are several nearby destinations that visitors should consider:

  • Palm Springs: Palm Springs is a popular destination known for its spas, art galleries, and stunning natural surroundings.
  • Joshua Tree National Park: Joshua Tree National Park is a unique desert landscape that is home to several unique species of plants and wildlife.
  • San Diego: San Diego is a vibrant city with a rich history, excellent cuisine, and stunning beaches.

Conclusion

Banning is a charming destination that has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, this small town in Southern California is an ideal getaway. With its rich history, stunning landscapes, and friendly locals, there’s no better time to visit Banning.
