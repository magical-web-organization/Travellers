Introduction to Palm Desert

Palm Desert, also known as the heart of the Palm Springs Valley, is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. This vibrant city is located in Riverside County, Southern California, and is part of the famous Agua Caliente Band of Cahuilla Indians tribal lands. The city is surrounded by beautiful mountains, palm trees, and breathtaking desert landscapes.

Palm Desert has become a popular vacation destination for those seeking relaxation, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and family fun. With year-round sunshine and warm weather, visitors can explore the city’s natural wonders, unique museums, art galleries, and upscale shopping and dining options.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best things to do and see in Palm Desert, where to stay, what to eat, how to get around, and much more. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Palm Desert

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Palm Springs Air Museum: This museum showcases military aircraft and related artifacts, as well as honoring combat veterans. Tour the vast collections of World War II combat planes, Vietnam-era battleships, and much more.

  2. Sunnylands: This iconic mid-century modern estate once belonged to billionaires Walter and Leonore Annenberg. The estate boasts stunning architecture, scenic gardens, and impressive art collections. You can visit its historic home, walk around the gardens and lakes or even catch a glimpse of the migratory birds in the area.

  3. Cabot’s Pueblo Museum: This unique museum welcomes visitors to discover the life and legacy of Cabot Yerxa, a homesteader, artist, and visionary. It is an architectural masterpiece that tells a lot about the early life of palm desert settlers.

Natural Attractions of Palm Desert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Joshua Tree National Park: This park is a short drive from Palm Desert and boasts some of the most awe-inspiring landscapes in America. Wander through the trails, hike the mountains, or explore the rock formations, just to tick off any adventure from your bucket list.

  2. Living Desert Zoo and Gardens: This unique attraction combines animal encounters with unique exhibits displaying exotic animals and an eye-catching array of flora and fauna unique to this landscape. Be sure to catch an exceptional view of the animals and exhibit at sunset.

  3. Indian Canyons: Visitors can hike or bike through these stunning canyons, including stunning natural wonders such as Palm Canyon, Andreas Canyon, and Murray Canyon. The trails are well-marked, with breathtaking views at every turn.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palm Desert

  1. Bump and Grind Trail: This scenic trail offers incredible views of the desert landscape from the top. It is a challenging 3.5-mile hike that will elevate your adrenaline in no time.

  2. Art Smith Trail: This moderate 6.7-mile trail crosses the scenic Santa Rosa and San Jacinto Mountains. A few steep uphill portions lead to panoramic views of Palm Desert and challenges your fitness level.

  3. Hahn Buena Vista Loop: This 3.7-mile moderate hike offers a beautiful desert view in less than two hours. The trail is the perfect beginner-level hike for anyone who wants to see that stunning beauty of Palm Desert.

Best Things to Do in Palm Desert: Activities and Tours

  1. Palm Desert Aquatic Center: This water park features two Olympic-size swimming pools, a water playground for kids, and a massive water slide that gets the adrenaline pumping for children of all ages.

  2. El Paseo Shopping District: Called the “Rodeo Drive of the Desert,” El Paseo is the perfect destination for shopping and dining. You can find over 300 boutique stores, as well as cafes, restaurants, and galleries showcasing world-class art.

  3. Palm Springs Aerial Tramway: Visitors can ride the tram from the desert floor to the top of the San Jacinto Mountains while enjoying the stunning views of the city below. There are many hiking routes and tours available at the top.

Food and Drink Scene of Palm Desert

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Date shake: The Palm Desert is famous for its date farms that provide visitors with the freshest dates and other date-based delicacies while in the city. Try a date shake while exploring the city to get the perfect taste of Palm Desert.

  2. Fish tacos: This dish is a Californian favorite. You can find them in different food stands or restaurants around the city. Experience the delicious Melvyn’s Restaurant, located at Ingleside Inn and capable of producing mouth-watering fish tacos.

  3. Roast Chicken: Roast chicken is a must-have plate at the Cork Tree Restaurant, which is located in the area. You will find perfectly cooked roast chicken served with seasonal vegetarian sides.

Local Beverages

  1. Margarita: A classic Palm Desert cocktail. Made with tequila, fresh lime juice, and a touch of orange liqueur, this beverage is best enjoyed while enjoying the city’s breathtaking views of the desert.

  2. Local Craft Beer: Beer lovers, this city has a lot to offer. Check out La Quinta Brewing Co. tasting room for the seasonal beers and ales that display Palm Desert’s beer-making skills.

  3. Wine: Local wines are produced and sold in the city. Try the Ed Petersen Cellars in the city to try one of the city’s best vintages.

Where to Stay in Palm Desert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. JW Marriott Desert Springs Resort & Spa: This iconic hotel is situated within a natural habitat encircling two golf courses overlooking mountain ranges. Offering deluxe rooms with balcony views, a full-service spa, and six restaurants, visitors are guaranteed to have a luxurious, comfortable stay.

  2. Embassy Suites by Hilton Palm Desert: This hotel features lovely suites with modern amenities, two pools, and a free breakfast. Moreover, you get access to a fitness center, and outside sport activities center like golf courses.

  3. Shadow Mountain Resort & Club: A picturesque resort for families and individuals alike. Shadow Mountain is nestled amidst 4.6 acres of lush botanical gardens, offering one- to two-bedrooms secluded villas that feature complete equipped kitchens.

What to Buy in Palm Desert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Local Art: Palm Desert is home to many talented artists. You can purchase their art pieces at local galleries or the CODA Gallery.

  2. Local Food: Farmers’ Markets sell fresh produce and other local delicacies such as honey, jams, chutneys, and date-based snacks. Try Shields Date Garden for the widest variety of date-based products.

  3. Designer Clothes: El Paseo, Palm Desert’s shopping district, is home to some of the top designers and luxury stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Palm Desert

Palm Desert has a rich cultural heritage with many galleries, art shows, cultural events, a vibrant music scene, and the Indian Canyons. Also, the city’s residents are friendly and hospitable.

Best Time to Visit Palm Desert

The best time to visit Palm Desert is between late fall and early spring, where temperatures are mild, and the winter sun still shines bright. Avoid visiting during summertime because it can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palm Desert

Do:

  1. Wear suitable clothes.
  2. Drink plenty of water.
  3. Respect local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash in natural areas.
  2. Hike on restricted trails.
  3. Litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palm Desert

Palm Desert is a safe city for tourists; however, it is always essential to keep an eye on your belongings and not venture into unsafe neighborhoods.

Health in Palm Desert

Palm Desert enjoys a warm climate year-round; staying hydrated and using sunscreen is advisable. The city has comprehensive medical facilities that cater to both locals and tourists.

Conclusion

In summary, Palm Desert is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, luxury, and relaxation. The city offers numerous attractions, and those with a passion for hiking, biking, and mountain climbing will find it especially appealing. From world-class museums and shopping districts to delightful restaurants and hotels, Palm Desert is a world-class vacation destination that satisfies travelers from all over the world.
