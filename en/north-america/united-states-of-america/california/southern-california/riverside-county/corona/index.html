Introduction to Corona

Corona is a charming city located in Riverside County, Southern California. Known for its breathtaking natural scenery, fascinating museums and historical landmarks, and vibrant cultural offerings, Corona is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a unique and unforgettable experience. With its numerous hiking and biking trails, world-class cuisine, and exciting cultural events, there’s something to see and do for everyone in this lovely city.

List of Places to Visit in Corona

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll be pleased to know that Corona has an abundance of museums and historical sites waiting to be explored. Some of the best ones to visit include:

  • Corona Heritage Park & Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Corona. With its impressive collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents, the Corona Heritage Park & Museum gives you a glimpse into the city’s past.

  • Corona Historic Preservation Society: Located in the heart of Corona, the Corona Historic Preservation Society is an excellent place to learn about the city’s unique architecture and landmarks. The society offers guided tours of the area, and you’ll get to see some of the city’s oldest and most iconic buildings.

  • Fender Museum of Music and the Arts: If you’re a music lover, you simply can’t miss the Fender Museum of Music and the Arts. This museum pays tribute to one of the world’s most iconic guitar brands and features an impressive collection of guitars and other musical instruments.

Natural Attractions of Corona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Corona’s natural beauty is truly breathtaking, and there are plenty of ways to take it all in. Whether you prefer hiking and biking or simply enjoying a leisurely stroll, these outdoor attractions are not to be missed:

  • Skyline Drive Trailhead: The Skyline Drive Trailhead is a popular hiking spot that offers some of the most spectacular views of the Corona area. With its well-maintained trails and stunning vistas, this is a great place to soak up the city’s natural beauty.

  • Santa Ana River Trail: If you’re a keen cyclist, the Santa Ana River Trail is a must-visit destination. Stretching over 30 miles from Corona to Huntington Beach, this trail is an excellent way to get some exercise while enjoying the area’s stunning scenery.

  • Lake Mathews: Located just a short drive from Corona, Lake Mathews is a popular spot for fishing, hiking, and picnicking. With its tranquil waters and breathtaking views, it’s a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corona

Corona is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Skyline Drive Trailhead: As mentioned above, the Skyline Drive Trailhead is a popular hiking spot that offers some of the most spectacular views of the Corona area. With its well-maintained trails and stunning vistas, this is a great place to soak up the city’s natural beauty.

  • Santa Ana River Trail: The Santa Ana River Trail is a fantastic biking route that stretches over 30 miles from Corona to Huntington Beach. The trail is well-maintained and relatively flat, making it an excellent choice for cyclists of all skill levels.

  • Butterfield Overland Trail: The Butterfield Overland Trail is a historic route that was used by 19th-century pioneers. Today, it’s a popular hiking and biking trail that offers a glimpse into the city’s past.

Best Things to Do in Corona: Activities and Tours

From thrilling adventures to cultural experiences, there’s no shortage of things to do in Corona. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • A Day at Glen Ivy Hot Springs: Glen Ivy Hot Springs is a luxurious spa that offers a range of treatments and services. Spend a day here and enjoy the mineral baths, saunas, and relaxation areas.

  • Tom’s Farms: Tom’s Farms is a popular destination for families and features a range of activities for all ages. From pony rides to petting zoos, there’s something for everyone.

  • Carlton Winery & Vineyard: Take a tour of the Carlton Winery & Vineyard and sample some of the region’s best wines. With its stunning views and tranquil setting, this winery is a must-visit destination for wine lovers.

Food and Drink Scene of Corona

Corona has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers a range of flavors and cuisines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • California Burrito: This hearty burrito is filled with carne asada, French fries, and guacamole.

  • Corona Burger: A classic burger with a twist, the Corona Burger features a juicy patty topped with avocado, bacon, and crispy onions.

  • Mexican Street Corn: This delicious snack is made from grilled corn on the cob and covered in mayo, cotija cheese, chili powder, and chopped cilantro.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Corona has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries offering a range of flavorful brews.

  • Margaritas: No trip to Corona is complete without trying one of the city’s signature margaritas.

Local Culture and Customs of Corona

Corona is a diverse city that celebrates its cultural heritage through a range of events and festivals throughout the year. Some of the most popular include:

  • Cinco de Mayo: A celebration of Mexican heritage and culture, Cinco de Mayo is a lively festival that features food, music, and dancing.

  • Corona Annual Art Festival: This annual festival showcases the work of local artists and includes live music, food vendors, and art demonstrations.

Best Time to Visit Corona

Corona has a pleasant year-round climate, making it an ideal destination for any season. However, the best time to visit is between March and May when the temperatures are mild and the wildflowers are in bloom.

Safety and Health

Getting to Corona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Corona is located just a short drive from several major cities, including Los Angeles and San Diego. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Corona

  • Ontario International Airport: Located 25 miles from Corona, Ontario International Airport is the closest major airport.

  • John Wayne Airport: Located 30 miles from Corona, John Wayne Airport offers flights to several major domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Corona

  • Corona Transit Center: Located in the heart of Corona, the Corona Transit Center offers local and regional bus services.

  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers national bus services and has a station located just a short distance from Corona.

How to Get Around in Corona

The best way to get around Corona is by car. However, there are also several public transportation options available, including local and regional buses.
