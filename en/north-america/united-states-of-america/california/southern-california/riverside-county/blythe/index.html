Introduction to Blythe

Blythe, a small city in the southeastern corner of California, is a hidden gem along the Colorado River. With over 350 days of sunshine, this city is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts who flock to its vast natural wonders and diverse recreation opportunities. From hiking in the nearby mountains to boating on the river, Blythe has something for everyone. Not only does this city offer scenic landscapes and outdoor adventures, but it also boasts a unique cultural heritage, evident in its museums, historical landmarks, and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Blythe

Here is a list of places to visit in Blythe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palo Verde Historical Museum and Society: A museum dedicated to preserving the city’s local history and culture.
  • Blythe Intaglios or Blythe Geoglyphs: Ancient, large-scale drawings on the ground, visible only from the air, depict human forms, animal forms, and geometric shapes.
  • Hauser Geode Beds: An open field where you can find many geodes.

Natural Attractions of Blythe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colorado River: A must-visit destination for water sports like jet skiing, boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Palo Verde Mountains: A scenic trail with stunning views of the valley.
  • California Poppy Reserve: A state park that is home to the colorful and peaceful poppy fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blythe

Hiking and biking enthusiasts would have a wonderful time exploring the many trails that Blythe has to offer. Here are just a few options:

  • Blythe Bluff Trail: A seven-mile trail that follows the riverbank and has great views of the nearby mountains.
  • Big Maria Mountains: A rugged trail with stunning desert views, leading to the highest peak in the area.
  • Walter’s Camp Trail: A scenic, easy-going trail by the Colorado River with beautiful views of the nearby mountains.

Best Things to Do in Blythe: Activities and Tours

  • Colorado River Tours: A river tour that provides scenic views of the Blythe area and learning about its rich history.
  • Skydiving: A thrilling experience to admire the great scenery from above.
  • ATV Rentals: Rent ATVs to explore the desert and mountains around the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blythe

  • Day 1: Visit the Palo Verde Historical Museum and Society, Blythe Intaglios, and Hauser Geode Beds.
  • Day 2: Visit the Colorado River and try water sports such as jet-skiing, boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Day 3: Hike the Palo Verde Mountains Trail.
  • Day 4: Visit the California Poppy Reserve.
  • Day 5: Go on a Colorado River Tour.
  • Day 6: Rent ATVs and explore the rugged desert around Big Maria Mountains.
  • Day 7: Go skydiving for an unforgettable experience above the breathtaking scenery of Blythe.

Food and Drink Scene of Blythe

Get ready for some delicious cuisine in Blythe! Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Pozole: A savory and spicy soup made with pork, hominy, and fragrant spices.
  • Machaca: A savory meat dish made with marinated, shredded beef.
  • Salsa and Guacamole: Blythe is known for its spicy and fresh guacamole and salsa.

Local Beverages

  • Margarita: A classic Mexican cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and orange-flavored liqueur.
  • Craft Beer: The Blythe area boasts several craft breweries that produce unique, locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Blythe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blythe has a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference. Here are some options:

  • Quality Inn Blythe: A cozy and comfortable hotel situated in a convenient location close to the Colorado River and the city’s main attractions.
  • Motel 6 Blythe: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • Hidden Beaches Resort: A picturesque beachfront resort that offers luxurious facilities and services.

What to Buy in Blythe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Blythe, don’t forget to purchase the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Geodes: The Hauser Geode Beds offer visitors the chance to find their own geodes, which make for the perfect souvenir.
  • Local Beer: Blythe’s craft breweries offer unique and delicious beer that you can share with friends back home.
  • T-shirts and Caps: Various shops offer them, often featuring Blythe’s iconic symbols such as the intaglios and the Colorado River.

Local Culture and Customs of Blythe

Blythe is a culturally rich city, and visitors can learn about its heritage by visiting its museums and historical landmarks. The locals are known for their hospitality and friendliness. It’s a good idea to respect local customs and dress modestly, especially when visiting religious or cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Blythe

The best time to visit Blythe is from mid-September to mid-May when temperatures are mild and tolerable. Avoid visiting during the summer months when temperatures can soar up to 120°F.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blythe

Do:

  • Bring sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses when exploring the outdoors.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Stay properly hydrated when exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and always pack out what you pack in.
  • Don’t underestimate the desert heat. Always carry enough water and have sun protection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blythe

Blythe is a generally safe city, but visitors should always be aware of their surroundings, especially when exploring the outdoors or going off the beaten path.

Health in Blythe

Due to its hot and arid weather, visitors should take the necessary precautions to avoid heat-related illnesses. Drink plenty of water, wear protective clothing, and avoid going outdoors during the hottest hours of the day.

Getting to Blythe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blythe

  • Blythe Airport (BLH)
  • Yuma International Airport (YUM)
  • Palm Springs International Airport (PSP)

Bus Stations Near Blythe

  • Greyhound Bus Station
  • Tufesa Bus Station

Other Options

  • Driving: Blythe is accessible via I-10 and US-95.
  • Amtrak: The nearest stations are located in Palm Springs, Yuma, and Indio.

How to Get Around in Blythe

The best way to get around Blythe is by car, as public transportation is limited. Car rentals are available at the airport or in town.

Where to Go Next from Blythe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Joshua Tree National Park

Famous for its remarkable desert landscape, Joshua Tree National Park boasts hiking trails, rock formation, and panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Lake Havasu City

A small city known for London Bridge, water sports, and breathtaking sunsets over Colorado River.

Phoenix, Arizona

About 150 miles from Blythe, Phoenix offers a vibrant culture and history, rich architecture, and urban amenities.

Conclusion

Blythe, a small city nestled in the southeastern corner of California, provides an array of outdoor recreation opportunities, natural beauty, and cultural attractions that are sure to make visitors of all ages happy. Whether you’re hiking in the nearby mountains or exploring the natural wonders of the city, visitors can create unforgettable memories in Blythe, California.
