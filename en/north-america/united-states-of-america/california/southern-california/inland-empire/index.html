Comprehensive Tour Guide for Inland Empire

Located in Southern California, Inland Empire is a region that boasts of a quirky mix of rugged natural landscapes and bustling city life. From historical museums to outdoor adventures, this place has everything that you can ask for while planning a vacation. This comprehensive tour guide outlines the list of places to explore in Inland Empire, culture and customs, must-try dishes, and more to make your trip memorable.

  2. Water Resources Institute at California State University, San Bernardino
  3. San Bernardino County Museum
  4. Riverside Metropolitan Museum
  5. Rialto Historical Society

Best Things to Do in Inland Empire: Activities and Tours

  1. Tour the famous Route 66
  2. Visit the California Citrus State Historic Park
  3. Enjoy the view at the Big Bear Lake
  4. Explore the historic Mission Inn Hotel and Spa
  5. Go on a hot air balloon ride

Week-Long Itinerary for Inland Empire

Day 1 - Visit the Riverside Metropolitan Museum, March Field Air Museum, and enjoy the view of the city from the top of Mount Rubidoux. Day 2 - Take a hot air balloon ride or go on a horseback tour in Temecula Valley. Day 3 - Explore the natural beauty of Lake Arrowhead, hike the famous Trail of the Arrowheads, and visit the Skypark at Santa’s Village. Day 4 - Take a day trip to Palm Springs and visit the famous Palm Springs Aerial Tramway. Day 5 - Take a scenic drive along the Route 66 and visit the California Citrus State Historic Park. Day 6 - Visit the San Bernardino County Museum, Rialto Historical Society, and take a bike ride along the Santa Ana River Trail. Day 7 - Explore the historic Mission Inn Hotel and Spa, and shop for souvenirs at the Riverside Plaza.

Local Culture and Customs of Inland Empire

The region is known for its iconic Route 66, citrus agriculture, and historically rich landmarks. The local people are friendly and welcoming towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Inland Empire

The best time to visit Inland Empire is from March to May and from September to November, when the weather is pleasant, and tourism is relatively low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inland Empire

Do:

  1. Drive along Route 66.
  2. Visit the California Citrus State Historic Park.
  3. Explore the natural beauty of Lake Arrowhead.

Don’t:

  1. Litter at any of the natural attractions.
  2. Park vehicles in the ‘No Parking’ Zones.
  3. Try to climb rocks which have the sign of ‘No Climbing.’

Safety and Health

Safety in Inland Empire

The region is generally safe for travelers. However, it is always advisable to stay aware of your surroundings and be cautious late at night in certain areas.

Health in Inland Empire

The Inland Empire has access to quality medical care, including urgent care clinics and hospitals.
