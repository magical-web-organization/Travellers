Introduction to Anaheim Hills

Located in the Inland Cities region of Orange County, Southern California, Anaheim Hills is a picturesque community that offers an array of natural attractions, cultural activities, and entertainment options. This guide provides an overview of the must-visit places, things to do, where to stay, food and drink scene, safety and health, and nearby destinations to help you plan your trip to Anaheim Hills.

List of Places to Visit in Anaheim Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Richard Nixon Presidential Library and Museum: This museum showcases the life and legacy of the 37th President of the United States. Visitors can explore exhibits, artifacts, a replica of the Oval Office, and the President’s final resting place.
  • Anaheim Heritage Center: A beautifully preserved historical building that includes a gallery showcasing the city’s history and heritage.
  • Muzeo Museum and Cultural Center: This center features rotating exhibits showcasing art, culture, and history.

Natural Attractions of Anaheim Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oak Canyon Nature Center: This nature center is a natural oasis that offers hiking trails, oak groves, and creeks.
  • Yorba Regional Park: Spanning 140 acres, this park features a beautiful lake, hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds.
  • Santa Ana River Trail: This scenic trail is ideal for biking, hiking, and running. The trail spans 30 miles, providing stunning views of the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anaheim Hills

  • Weir Canyon Loop Trail: This 5.3-mile hiking trail offers breathtaking scenery and a challenging terrain.
  • Anaheim Hills Trail: This 2.3-mile trail offers scenic views of the hills and a moderately challenging terrain.
  • Santiago Oaks Regional Park Trails: The park offers an extensive network of trails for hiking, biking, and equestrian use. The trails range in difficulty, making it perfect for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Anaheim Hills: Activities and Tours

  • Disneyland: Located just 10 minutes from Anaheim Hills, Disneyland is a must-visit attraction for families and fans of all ages.
  • Anaheim Packing District: This restored historic district offers a variety of eateries, bars, and artisanal shops.
  • Santiago Creek Wildlife and Watershed Center: This nature center offers interactive exhibits and programs that educate visitors about local wildlife and watersheds.

Food and Drink Scene of Anaheim Hills

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chimichurri Fries: A delicious dish of garlic fries topped with chimichurri sauce at Urth Caffe.
  • Pork Belly Tacos: These mouth-watering tacos are served with pickled onions and avocado at orangehillrestaurant.com.
  • Gourmet Donuts: Satisfy your sweet tooth with the gourmet donuts at the Cupcake Store. They offer a variety of unique flavors such as maple bacon, creme brulee, and nutella.

Local Beverages

  • Anaheim Brewery: This brewery offers a variety of hand-crafted beers, seasonal releases, and rotating guest taps.
  • Pali Wine Co.: An intimate tasting room that offers a selection of award-winning wines made from California grapes.

Where to Stay in Anaheim Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Marriott Suites Anaheim: A family-friendly hotel that offers spacious suites, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Hyatt Regency Orange County: A contemporary hotel with modern amenities, including a pool, fitness center, and shuttle service to Disneyland.
  • Americas Best Value Inn: A budget-friendly option that offers clean, comfortable rooms and easy access to all of Anaheim Hills’ major attractions.

What to Buy in Anaheim Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Anaheim Ducks Merchandise: If you’re a hockey fan, head to the Ducks team store located at the Honda Center.
  • Historical Books: Visit the Anaheim Heritage Center for an extensive collection of books on the city’s history and heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Anaheim Hills

Anaheim Hills is a multicultural community that celebrates diversity. The city hosts a variety of cultural events and festivals throughout the year, including the Anaheim Fall Festival and the Anaheim International Film Festival.

Best Time to Visit Anaheim Hills

The best time to visit Anaheim Hills is from March to May or September to November when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds visiting Disneyland and other attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anaheim Hills

Do:

  • Do carry a map or GPS when exploring the hiking trails.
  • Do carry plenty of water and snacks when hiking.
  • Do plan your visit to Disneyland ahead of time and book your tickets online to avoid long lines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks or nature reserves.
  • Don’t feed the wildlife.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and a hat when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anaheim Hills

Anaheim Hills is a safe community, but visitors should take precautionary measures, such as locking their cars and securing valuables. When hiking, be aware of your surroundings, and stick to marked trails.

Health in Anaheim Hills

The tap water in Anaheim Hills is safe to drink. Visitors should take care of themselves by carrying a first-aid kit, sunscreen, and insect repellent, especially when exploring the great outdoors.

Getting to Anaheim Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anaheim Hills

  • John Wayne Airport: Located 20 minutes from Anaheim Hills, this airport offers both domestic and international flights.
  • Los Angeles International Airport: Located approximately 45 miles from Anaheim Hills, this airport offers a more extensive range of flights and airlines.

Bus Stations Near Anaheim Hills

  • Anaheim Bus Center: This bus station is located 10 minutes from Anaheim Hills and provides connections to major cities in California.
  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located 18 miles away, this bus station offers connections to destinations across the United States.

Other Options

  • Train: The Amtrak train station is located 10 minutes from Anaheim Hills and offers connections to major cities across the United States.
  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available at both John Wayne and Los Angeles International airports.

How to Get Around in Anaheim Hills

The best way to explore Anaheim Hills is by car. Car rentals are easily available from any of the major airports. Alternatively, taxicabs and ridesharing services such as Uber or Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Anaheim Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Los Angeles: Located just over an hour’s drive away, Los Angeles offers a plethora of attractions, including Hollywood, Universal Studios, and the Getty Center.
  • San Diego: A two-hour drive from Anaheim Hills, San Diego is home to famous attractions, including the San Diego Zoo, La Jolla Cove, and the USS Midway Museum.
  • Joshua Tree National Park: A three-hour drive from Anaheim Hills, Joshua Tree National Park is a stunning display of desert beauty, with breathtaking landscapes and a wide variety of plant and animal species.

Conclusion
