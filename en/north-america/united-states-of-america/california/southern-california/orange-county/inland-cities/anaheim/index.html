Introduction to Anaheim

Anaheim is a magical city located in Orange County, Southern California. Like most Californian cities, it boasts of year-round sunshine with warm temperatures and blue skies. Perhaps the most significant attraction of Anaheim is its theme parks, first and foremost Disneyland Resort - a dreamland for kids, and adults alike. The city, however, has much more to offer besides the theme parks. From historical landmarks, museums, outdoor adventures, shopping, dining, and culture, there’s always something for everyone in Anaheim.

List of Places to Visit in Anaheim

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Anaheim has a rich cultural history. Learn about it by visiting the following museums and landmarks:

  • Muzeo Museum and Cultural Center: A history museum that showcases changing exhibitions. Check out its collections of both local and international art pieces.
  • The Anaheim Packing District: A restored citrus packing house, now a foodie heaven filled with over 20 artisan eateries. Enjoy tasty local cuisine while learning about the history of Orange County’s orange groves.
  • The Mother Colony House: A historic city landmark built-in 1857, one of the oldest in Anaheim. It tells the story of Anaheim’s founding by German immigrants.
  • The Santa Ana River Trail: A bike trail that stretches 30 miles along the Santa Ana Riverbed. It’s a great place to walk, run, bike, or picnic while enjoying scenic views of Southern California.

Natural Attractions of Anaheim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will have a blast in Anaheim. Here are a few places to consider:

  • Oak Canyon Nature Center: A nature reserve located in the Anaheim Hills. Expect hiking trails, streams, bouldering, and other nature-related activities.
  • Yorba Regional Park: A beautiful park with stunning views of Santa Ana River. Perfect for fishing, picnicking, or walking among the nature trails.
  • Carbon Canyon Regional Park: Go on an adventure hike in the park’s canyon trailhead. See the century-old redwood trees, rolling hills, and creeks.
  • Downtown Disney District: Enjoy an evening walk at the open-air promenade between Disneyland Resort theme parks. It offers a ton of dining, shopping, and entertainment options.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anaheim

Anaheim’s proximity to the mountains and the coast means hikers and bikers can find world-class trails within easy reach, such as:

  • Weir Canyon Trail: A popular trail for hiking and biking with six out-and-back sections built for everyone from beginners to advanced riders.
  • Santa Ana River Trail: The longest multipurpose trail in Orange County. It is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding.
  • Santiago Oaks Regional Park Trail: This hidden gem offers hikers and bikers a chance to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city. Expect scenic views, perfect for family-friendly biking.

Best Things to Do in Anaheim: Activities and Tours

Anaheim’s attractions are diverse and endless. Here are a few popular activities and tours to add to your list:

  • Disneyland Resort: A trip to Anaheim is incomplete without visiting Disneyland Resort. Meet your favorite Disney characters, ride thrilling rides, watch spectacular parades, and enjoy fantastic live entertainment.
  • Anaheim Brewery: Take a guided tour of the brewery and discover how Anaheim’s local beer is made. You’ll get to taste some fantastic beer too.
  • Angels Stadium: Enjoy a great family day out at the largest baseball stadium in Orange County. Take a tour or watch a game.
  • Anaheim Garden Walk: A shopping and dining entertainment center. It offers a unique outdoor shopping experience with an array of dining options and nightlife activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Anaheim

Anaheim’s food scene is as bright and vibrant as its culture. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clams and Sausage at Oktoberfest: A hearty German dish of clams and sausage cooked in beer and served with bread.
  • Chicken and Waffles at Roscoe’s House of Chicken and Waffles: Tender chicken and sweet waffles, a Southern classic dish.
  • Shoyu Ramen at Fukada: Enjoy a bowl of thick broth made with pork and chicken bones, soy sauce, and other spices. A popular Japanese dish.

Local Beverages

Anaheim is known for its craft beer scene. Don’t leave before trying:

  • Anaheim Hefeweizen at Anaheim Brewery: A popular wheat beer with a light body and a refreshing finish.
  • Apricot Wheat at Karl Strauss Brewery: A light-bodied beer with hints of apricots and honey. Perfect on a hot day.
  • Cali Creamin at Mother Earth Brew Co: A rich and delicious vanilla cream ale with a smooth texture.

Where to Stay in Anaheim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anaheim offers plenty of accommodation options to suit different budgets. Here are a few top picks:

  • Disney’s Grand Californian Hotel & Spa: A luxurious hotel located inside Disneyland Resort with direct access to theme parks.
  • Anaheim Majestic Garden Hotel: A hotel located near Disneyland Resort with excellent amenities, including a complimentary shuttle service to the theme parks.
  • The Anaheim Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with family-friendly amenities. It’s just a short walk from Disneyland Resort.

What to Buy in Anaheim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are a few souvenirs and local products you should grab before leaving Anaheim:

  • Disney Merchandise: Disneyland Resort is known for its fantastic merchandise. Take home a shirt, a souvenir keychain, or even some Mickey Mouse ears!
  • Citrus Products: Get locally made marmalades or jams made from oranges or different Citrus fruits in the Anaheim Packing District.
  • Craft Beer: Don’t forget to take home some unique and delicious beer made in Anaheim and Orange County.

Local Culture and Customs of Anaheim

Anaheim’s culture is a potpourri of different ethnicities, traditions, and lifestyles. To experience the local culture:

  • Get to know the German roots: Visit the Mother Colony House and learn about the German immigrant population that settled in Anaheim
  • Attend Oktoberfest: Celebrate the German culture with other locals every September/October at the Phoenix Club.
  • Respect the Disney culture: Anaheim is the home of the world’s most famous Disney theme park. Embrace the Disney spirit by watching a parade and joining Mickey and Minnie in their adventure.

Best Time to Visit Anaheim

Anaheim has a mild climate and enjoys warm temperatures throughout the year. However, the peak seasons when the crowd is high is usually in the summer months, from June to August. Planning a visit during spring or fall may be ideal when the crowd is low and the weather is cooler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anaheim

Do:

  • Carry sunscreen: Anaheim has a sunny and warm climate; hence it would be best for visitors to always wear sunscreen during outdoor activities.
  • Take advantage of public transport: Anaheim offers reliable public transport options such as buses or Anaheim Resort Transit to navigate through the city.
  • Book tickets to Disneyland Theme parks early: Disneyland Resort is very popular, and tickets can sell out fast, so it is best to book tickets early to avoid disappointment.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas: Smoking is prohibited in public areas within the city, including the parks and beaches.
  • Be careless with your belongings: Keep an eye on your belongings as crime rates can get high in some parts of the city.
  • Plan your entire itinerary around Disneyland Resort: As much as the Disneyland Resort is a fantastic attraction, reserve some time to explore other interesting places around Anaheim.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anaheim

Anaheim is generally a safe city to visit. However, visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially at night, to avoid becoming a victim of crime. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night: Stick to well-lit and safe areas.
  • Keep valuables out of sight: Avoid walking around with jewelry, watches, or electronics in plain sight.
  • Stay in the tourist areas: The main tourist areas such as Disneyland Resort and the Anaheim Garden Walk are safer than the other areas.

Health in Anaheim

Anaheim has excellent medical facilities, and visitors can seek both public and private hospitals for medical attention. Emergency numbers are 911 and 311 for non-emergency situations.

Getting to Anaheim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Anaheim is accessible by train, car, or air. Here are some transportation options:

  • Airports Near Anaheim: John Wayne Airport, Long Beach Airport, and LAX International Airport are within driving distance to Anaheim.
  • Bus Stations Near Anaheim: The Anaheim Regional Transportation Intermodal Center (ARTIC) is the main transportation hub for bus routes in and out of Anaheim.
  • Other Options: Lyft, Uber, or taxi are other options to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Anaheim

The best way to get around Anaheim is by car. However, visitors can also use public transportation such as the Anaheim Resort Tranist (ART) and buses to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Anaheim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anaheim’s proximity to the mountains, beaches, and other cities makes it a good base for nearby excursions. Here are a few options:

  • Knott’s Berry Farm: Located in Buena Park, less than a 15-minute drive from Anaheim, this is a theme park featuring thrilling rides, shows, and attractions.
  • Huntington Beach: Located about 30 minutes from Anaheim, it is known for its excellent surf conditions, and hosts the US open surfing tournament every year.
  • La Habra Children’s Museum: Just 15 minutes from Anaheim, this museum offers unique interactive exhibits designed for kids.
  • Laguna Beach: Located south of Anaheim about 35 minutes drive, it is a sceneric beach and home to several art galleries and art exhibits.

Conclusion

Anaheim is a sun-soaked city with fantastic attractions for visitors to explore and enjoy. Its cultural history, theme parks, natural wonders, dining, and nightlife are just a few of the gems that make it a top tourist destination. Start planning your trip, and we hope our comprehensive travel guide has been helpful.
