Introduction to Costa Mesa

Welcome to Costa Mesa, a vibrant city nestled in Inland Cities, Orange County, Southern California. This diverse and colorful city boasts an impressive array of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities, making it a must-visit destination for both locals and tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Costa Mesa

Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s rich history, immersing yourself in nature, or indulging in the local cuisine, Costa Mesa has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-see places to visit in Costa Mesa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Triangle - A bustling entertainment district with a variety of restaurants, bars, and shops.
  • The Segerstrom Center for the Arts - A world-class performing arts venue showcasing Broadway shows, concerts, and more.
  • The Costa Mesa Historical Society Museum - A fascinating museum dedicated to preserving Costa Mesa’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Costa Mesa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fairview Park - A sprawling park with beautiful hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the city.
  • Upper Newport Bay Nature Preserve - A vast wetland with hiking and biking trails, birdwatching, and kayaking opportunities.
  • Banning Ranch Conservancy - A pristine nature reserve with hiking trails, birdwatching, and stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Costa Mesa

Costa Mesa is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, with an abundance of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes to check out during your visit:

  • Talbert Nature Preserve Trail - A scenic trail through the Talbert Nature Preserve, featuring stunning views of the coast and wetland habitats.
  • Back Bay Loop Trail - A 10.5-mile loop through Upper Newport Bay, offering stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and Newport Beach.
  • Fairview Park Trail - A scenic hike through Fairview Park, showcasing picturesque views of the city and the Santa Ana River.

Best Things to Do in Costa Mesa: Activities and Tours

Costa Mesa has a wide variety of activities and tours to suit every interest. Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

  • OC Mix Farmers’ Market - A weekly farmers’ market at SOCO and The OC Mix, offering a variety of organic produce, artisanal foods, and local goods.
  • The LAB Anti-Mall - A unique and eclectic shopping experience featuring locally owned boutiques, vintage shops, and art galleries.
  • Costa Mesa Food Tours - A guided food tour through Costa Mesa’s best culinary destinations, featuring tastings of local specialties.

Week-Long Itinerary for Costa Mesa

Day 1: Explore the cultural attractions in Costa Mesa, such as The Triangle and the Segerstrom Center for the Arts.
Day 2: Hike and explore the beautiful natural wonders of Fairview Park, Upper Newport Bay Nature Preserve and Banning Ranch Conservancy.
Day 3: Visit the Costa Mesa Historical Society Museum and learn about the city’s rich history.
Day 4: Experience the local cuisine with a visit to the OC Mix Farmers’ Market and a Costa Mesa food tour.
Day 5: Enjoy some retail therapy at The LAB Anti-Mall.
Day 6: Take a scenic drive to Newport Beach or Laguna Beach for a day of beachcombing.
Day 7: Wrap up your trip with some light hiking and relaxing with the views on Fairview Park Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Costa Mesa

The food and drink scene in Costa Mesa is diverse and delicious, featuring a variety of locally sourced and artisanal ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish tacos - Fresh and flavorful, Costa Mesa’s fish tacos are a local favorite. Try them at Bear Flag Fish Co. or Wahoo’s Fish Taco.
  • Banh mi - A Vietnamese-inspired sandwich served on a French baguette. Try it at Lee’s Sandwiches or The Straw.
  • Poke bowls - A Hawaiian-inspired bowl featuring fresh fish and veggies. Try it at PokiNometry or North Shore Poke Co.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer - Costa Mesa is home to several award-winning microbreweries, such as Barley Forge Brewing and Gunwhale Ales.
  • Cold-pressed juice - For a refreshing and healthy beverage, try a cold-pressed juice at places like Nekter Juice Bar or Juice Served Here.
  • Kombucha - A fermented tea that’s refreshing and healthy. Try it at Boochcraft or Fermentation Farm.

Where to Stay in Costa Mesa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Costa Mesa has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay during your visit:

  • The Westin South Coast Plaza, Costa Mesa - A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Costa Mesa.
  • Ayres Hotel & Suites Costa Mesa/Newport Beach - An elegant boutique hotel with spacious suites and a beautiful outdoor pool.
  • Hostelling International Orange County - A budget-friendly hostel located in the nearby city of Fullerton.

What to Buy in Costa Mesa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Costa Mesa home with you by purchasing some of the city’s unique and locally made products. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home from your trip:

  • Artwork - Costa Mesa is home to many talented artists, and you can find their work at galleries such as The ARTery and The CAMP Art Gallery.
  • Candles - Find a variety of handmade candles at Ritual Zen or The Lab Fragrances.
  • Surf-inspired clothing and gear - Grab some surfer-inspired clothing and gear at local shops like The Raconteurs Collective or Jack’s Surfboards.

Local Culture and Customs of Costa Mesa

Costa Mesa is a diverse and welcoming community, with a strong focus on arts, culture, and environmental sustainability. The locals here are friendly and helpful, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the laid-back and easygoing vibe of the city.

Best Time to Visit Costa Mesa

The best time to visit Costa Mesa is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) months when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are thinner. However, the summer months (June to August) are also a popular time to visit due to the abundance of outdoor events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Costa Mesa

Here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Costa Mesa:

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the city’s many hiking and biking trails.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including fish tacos and poke bowls.
  • Do visit the local galleries and museums to learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks or natural areas.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of the local customs and culture.
  • Don’t miss out on the city’s many outdoor events and festivals.

Safety and Health

Costa Mesa is a safe and healthy city to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions and stay vigilant. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Costa Mesa

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid isolated areas at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and locked in your hotel room.
  • Follow all safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities and adventures.

Health in Costa Mesa

  • Drink plenty of water, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.
  • Practice safe sex and take necessary precautions to avoid sexually transmitted diseases.

Getting to Costa Mesa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Costa Mesa is easy, with several transportation options available. Here are some of the best ways to get to and around the city:

Airports Near Costa Mesa

  • John Wayne Airport (SNA) - Located just five miles from Costa Mesa, this airport is served by several major airlines, including Southwest and American Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Costa Mesa

  • Costa Mesa Transit Center - The main bus station in Costa Mesa, featuring several local and regional routes.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Several car rental companies operate out of John Wayne Airport and Costa Mesa for those who prefer to drive themselves.

How to Get Around in Costa Mesa

Costa Mesa is a walkable and bike-friendly city, featuring several bike lanes and pedestrian trails throughout the city. However, for those who prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies in the city, and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Costa Mesa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Costa Mesa is ideally located near several other amazing destinations, and it’s the perfect base for exploring everything Southern California has to offer. Some nearby places to consider include:

  • Newport Beach - Just a short drive from Costa Mesa, Newport Beach is a picturesque coastal community with gorgeous beaches and a vibrant boardwalk.
  • Laguna Beach - Known for its stunning beaches, art galleries, and outdoor adventures, Laguna Beach is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts.
  • Disneyland - Just a 20-minute drive from Costa Mesa, Disneyland is one of the most popular theme parks in the world and a perfect day trip destination.

Conclusion

Costa Mesa is a vibrant and exciting destination that offers something for everyone. Visitors here can immerse themselves in the city’s rich history and culture, explore the natural wonders of the area, indulg in the delectable local cuisine, and relax in the laid-back and friendly vibe of the city. Start planning your next trip to Costa Mesa today!
