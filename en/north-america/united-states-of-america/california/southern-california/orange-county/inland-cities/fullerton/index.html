Fullerton, located in Southern California’s Orange County, offers an extensive list of attractions and activities suitable for a vacation or weekend getaway. From scenic outdoor destinations to cultural and historical landmarks, visitors can find it all in Fullerton. This comprehensive travel guide is designed to help travelers plan their trip, including essential logistical information to maximize their time spent exploring this vibrant city.

Introduction to Fullerton

Fullerton is a city of approximately 139,000 residents located in the heart of Orange County, California. Fullerton has a rich cultural history, as evidenced by the many museums and art galleries found throughout the city.

List of Places to Visit in Fullerton

There are many places to visit in Fullerton, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fullerton Museum Center: One of the most popular cultural attractions in Fullerton, the Fullerton Museum Center features diverse exhibitions about art, science, and local history.
  • Leo Fender Museum: A specialty museum dedicated to the life and work of music innovator Leo Fender, inventor of the first electric guitar.
  • Muckenthaler Cultural Center: A historic mansion that has been transformed into a center for art and music.
  • Fullerton Arboretum: A botanical garden and nature preserve that showcases different plant varieties from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Fullerton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hillcrest Park: A scenic park that stretches across 36 acres, featuring playgrounds, picnic areas, and hiking trails with stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Laguna Lake Park: A serene lake situated in an expansive park with a playground, walking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Craig Regional Park: This park features lush greenery, nature trails, and a fishing pond.
  • Bastanchury Park: A beautiful park with a large lake, perfect for fishing, as well as basketball courts and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fullerton

Fullerton offers many hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. These include:

  • The Fullerton Loop: A popular 12-mile mountain biking trail that traverses through hills and valleys for a thrilling adventure.
  • Hiking Trails at Laguna Lake Park: There are several easy hiking trails around the tranquil Laguna Lake that offer scenic views of the surrounding hills, wildlife, and plants.
  • The Juanita Cooke Greenbelt & Trail: This wonderful trail offers biking, jogging, or serene walks with ample nature views. It’s a perfect place to visit for a break from the fast-paced city life.

Best Things to Do in Fullerton: Activities and Tours

Aside from visiting the various cultural landmarks and destinations, there are several other activities in Fullerton that visitors can engage in. These include:

  • Cal State Fullerton’s Titan Athletics: Visitors can watch the athletics program at the renowned division-one sports programs of California State University, Fullerton.
  • Summit House Restaurant: To make an unforgettable memory, visiting the Summit House Restaurant is a must. Here, you can enjoy the best view of the area and feast on the delicious meals that are sure to tantalize your taste buds.
  • Shopping at Downtown Fullerton: The vibrant downtown area is an excellent place for visitors to explore boutique shops, cafes, galleries, and entertainment.
  • The Maverick Theater: A small theater in the downtown area that offers visitors an intimate theater experience. From comedy shows to dramatic productions, visitors are sure to find something to enjoy.
  • The Fullerton Pub Crawl Tour: An epic experience for those who wish to taste the local craft beer and eat at some of Fullerton’s best restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fullerton

For a week-long itinerary in Fullerton, visitors might follow this sample schedule:

Day 1: Arrive in Fullerton and relax at one of the local hotels, hostels, or guesthouses.

Day 2: Visit the Fullerton Museum Center and explore the lively downtown area.

Day 3: Go hiking at the Juanita Cooke Greenbelt Trail for the breathtaking trails.

Day 4: Have a fun-filled day with friends exploring Fullerton Loop.

Day 5: Visit Hillcrest Park for a picnic and breathtaking views.

Day 6: Visit the historical Plummer Auditorium and watch a show.

Day 7: Wrap your week-long trip indulging in sumptuous meals from famous restaurants and shopping at the retail areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Fullerton

Fullerton offers a wide range of high-quality dining establishments, as well as local dishes and beverages to enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mexican Food: You can find fantastic traditional Mexican dishes in almost every corner of the city. Some of the most recommended include tacos, burritos, and ceviche.
  • Fullerton Burger: Found in restaurants like Heroes Bar and Grill or The Hungry Bear, this burger is a must-try local delicacy.
  • Desserts: Several bakeries in Fullerton serves delightful pastries like Italian Cannoli, Cheesecake, Almond Croissants, and many more.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Fullerton’s beer scene is remarkable. The “Bootlegger Brewery” is known for producing a variety of popular local beers.
  • Wine: The city’s wine bars offer excellent wine tastings and pairings for visitors to explore.

Where to Stay in Fullerton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fullerton boasts several accommodation options suitable for different travelers’ budgets and preferences. These include:

  • Fullerton Marriott at California State University: A luxury hotel perfect for couples, business travelers, or those seeking a refined experience.
  • Aloha Motel: A budget-friendly motel established in the 1960s and offers visitors a vintage motel experience
  • Days Inn & Suites Fullerton: A family-friendly option equipped with a children’s pool and allows visitors to bring pets.

What to Buy in Fullerton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fullerton offers unique souvenirs and local products perfect as gifts or keepsakes. Here are a few local products visitors can consider taking back home:

  • The Fullerton Pod Art Book: A book about the public artwork found around Fullerton.
  • Support Downtown Fullerton T-shirt: Buy a “Support Downtown Fullerton” t-shirt to show your support.
  • Fullerton Candle Company Candles: These candles handmade in small batches offer a wonderful fragrant smell.

Local Culture and Customs of Fullerton

Fullerton is a melting pot of cultures and has developed a culture of its own. The people of Fullerton value tradition, the arts, and outdoor activities. Visitors can expect a friendly and welcoming attitude from locals.

Best Time to Visit Fullerton

Visitors can visit Fullerton all year round, thanks to its warm climate. However, the best times to visit are from March to June or September to November, when the weather is comfortable, and most of the tourists are absent.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fullerton

Do:

  • Do drink a lot of water to stay hydrated, particularly on hot days.
  • Do wear sunscreen and bring a hat to protect your face from the sun.
  • Do be friendly and polite to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the streets or leave garbage in public areas.
  • Don’t forget your ID if you are planning to enjoy the local beer scene.
  • Don’t forget to tip waitstaff at restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fullerton

Fullerton is generally safe; however, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions such as always locking doors of vehicles, luggage, and being cautious about their surroundings especially in the evening hours.

Health in Fullerton

Visitors are advised to drink tap water, which is safe. However, be mindful of water hygiene if camping or hiking or on an adventure like kayaking. Additionally, They should ensure all the necessary vaccinations have been taken, and they should seek medical attention immediately if they develop any sudden illness.

Getting to Fullerton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Fullerton using several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Fullerton

  • John Wayne Airport (SNA): Located approximately 14 miles south of Fullerton, this airport is the most convenient option and receives flights from various airlines.
  • Long Beach Airport (LGB): This airport is approximately 20 miles away from Fullerton and is also convenient.

Bus Stations Near Fullerton

Greyhound operates a station in Fullerton, and visitors can also use the Orange County Transit Authority to travel around the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, hail a taxi, or use a ride-hail service like Lyft or Uber.

How to Get Around in Fullerton

Visitors can rent a car, use ride-hail services, or take advantage of the Orange County Transportation Authority’s bus service. There is also a reliable rail system, the Metrolink, that links Fullerton to other destinations within Orange County.

Where to Go Next from Fullerton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby attractions that visitors to Fullerton could explore, including:

  • Disneyland: Located approximately 7 miles away from Fullerton.
  • Newport Beach: Located approximately 20 miles away from Fullerton, Newport Beach has excellent beaches and plenty of hiking opportunities.
  • Knott’s Berry Farm: Located approximately 8 miles away from Fullerton, this theme park is a perfect place to visit with family or friends.

Conclusion

Fullerton is a charming city that’s packed with cultural and natural wonders, making it a fantastic destination for various types of travelers. From lodging, dining, and transportation options, visitors can now plan their trip confidently with the information provided in this guide.
