Introduction to Orange

Orange, a city in Orange County, is situated in Southern California. The town was named after the citrus fruit that was the backbone of its modern economic origin. The city has a warm and pleasant climate, attracting tourists throughout the year. The city has a rich history, being home to many cultural attractions, museums, and historical places. If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Orange is an excellent place to enjoy hiking, biking, and scenic views. The city is also renowned for its excellent restaurants and food scene, where you can find some of the best local delicacies.

List of Places to Visit in Orange

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Orange County Regional History Center - A museum that showcases the history of Orange County from its Native American past to the present day.
  • Bowers Museum - A museum with exhibitions on world cultures and art.
  • Old Towne Orange Historic District - A historic district with antique shops, restaurants, and vintage architecture.
  • Hilbert Museum of California Art - A museum dedicated to California art, including works by famous artists such as Millard Sheets and Sam Hyde Harris.

Natural Attractions of Orange: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Santiago Oaks Regional Park - A popular park with hiking, biking, and equestrian trails. The park also has picnic areas and scenic views.
  • Peters Canyon Regional Park - A park with hiking trails, a lake, and bird-watching opportunities.
  • Irvine Regional Park - A park with a lake, pony rides, train rides, and hiking trails.
  • Saddleback Butte State Park - A desert park with hiking trails, wildflowers, and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orange

  • Peters Canyon Trail - A 5.3-mile loop trail with scenic views of the canyon and lake.
  • Irvine Ranch Natural Landmarks - Several trails with various difficulty levels, including the Hicks Haul Loop, Loma Ridge, and Quail Hill.
  • Santiago Oaks Regional Park - Several trails ranging from easy to challenging, including the Santiago Creek Trail, Ridge Top Trail, and Waterfall Trail.
  • Santa Ana River Trail - A 30-mile trail that runs from San Bernardino to the Pacific Ocean, passing through Orange.

Best Things to Do in Orange: Activities and Tours

  • Old Towne Orange Walking Food Tour - A guided food tour that takes you through the historic district, with stops at several restaurants for tastings.
  • Orange International Street Fair - A yearly festival with food and drink vendors, live music, and cultural entertainment.
  • Balloon Rides Over Orange - A hot air balloon ride over the city, offering stunning views of the landscape.
  • Disneyland Park and Disney California Adventure Park - Two theme parks that offer a range of attractions, rides, and entertainment for all ages.

Food and Drink Scene of Orange

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish tacos - A popular dish made with grilled fish, cabbage, and salsa.
  • California burrito - A burrito with carne asada, french fries, cheese, and guacamole.
  • Orange County’s famous breakfast burrito - A burrito with eggs, potatoes, bacon, and cheese.
  • Gourmet hot dogs - Delicious hot dogs with unique toppings such as avocado and Korean BBQ.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer - Orange has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries such as Green Cheek Beer Co. and Valiant Brewing Company.
  • Wine - Orange is home to several vineyards and wineries, including the Orange Coast Winery and Rock Hill Winery.
  • Fresh Orange juice - A refreshing and healthy drink made from the city’s namesake fruit.

Where to Stay in Orange: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ayres Hotel Orange - A luxurious hotel near Disneyland, with excellent amenities and service.
  • DoubleTree by Hilton Anaheim - Orange County - A modern hotel with a prime location near Disneyland and Anaheim Convention Center.
  • Candlewood Suites Orange County - A comfortable and affordable hotel with a convenient location near shopping centers and restaurants.
  • Big A Motel - A budget-friendly option with basic amenities and a great location.

What to Buy in Orange: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Orange-themed souvenirs - Keychains, magnets, and t-shirts adorned with the city’s name and citrus fruit.
  • Unique antique items - Old Towne Orange Historic District is a great spot to find special and unusual antique items.
  • California olive oil - Many local stores offer California olive oil, a delicious and healthy addition to any kitchen.
  • California wine - As mentioned before, Orange is home to several vineyards and wineries offering unique and exquisite wine flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Orange

  • Respect for cultural diversity - Orange is home to a diverse population, and locals are welcoming and respectful of differences.
  • Casual dress code - Southern California is known for its casual and laid-back lifestyle, and Orange is no exception.
  • Love for the outdoors - With its pleasant weather and endless scenic views, locals enjoy spending time outdoors and engaging in outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Orange

The best time to visit Orange is during the spring (March-May) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Summer (June-August) is hot and crowded, while winter (December-February) can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orange

Do:

  • Do explore Old Towne Orange Historic District and its unique antique shops and vintage architecture.
  • Do try the local delicacies and visit the famous food scene.
  • Do take advantage of outdoor activities and explore the natural attractions of Orange.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Don’t disrespect cultural diversity and customs.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orange

Overall, Orange is a safe city. However, it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions when traveling. Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Orange

Orange has excellent medical facilities, including St. Joseph Hospital and Chapman Global Medical Center. In case of any medical emergency, call 911 or head to the nearest hospital.

Getting to Orange: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Orange

  • John Wayne Airport, Orange County (SNA) - A convenient airport located in Santa Ana, just a 20-minute drive from Orange.
  • Long Beach Airport (LGB) - A smaller airport located in Long Beach, about a 30-40 minute drive from Orange.
  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) - A larger airport located in Los Angeles, about a 60-90 minute drive from Orange.

Bus Stations Near Orange

  • Orange Metrolink Station - A train station that also offers bus services.
  • Orange Transportation Center - A transportation hub with bus connections to various areas.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Orange is easily accessible by car, and several car rental companies can be found at nearby airports.
  • Uber/Lyft - Ride-sharing services are readily available in Orange.

How to Get Around in Orange

The most convenient way to get around Orange is by car. Several rental companies can be found in the city. Alternatively, ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft are also available. Public transportation in the form of buses and trains also operates throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Orange: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Anaheim - Home to Disneyland, Anaheim is just a few miles from Orange.
  • Laguna Beach - A seaside town with stunning beaches, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • San Diego - A beautiful city with beaches, parks, museums, and other attractions, located about two hours south of Orange.
  • Palm Springs - A desert oasis with hot springs, golf courses, and resorts, located about two hours east of Orange.

Conclusion

Orange, California, offers a blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and a great food scene. With its location in Southern California, the city is a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to Orange and discover all the amazing things the city has to offer.
