Introduction to Amboy

Amboy is a small unincorporated community located in the Mojave Desert in California, USA. It is situated along Route 66, the iconic highway that crosses the country from Chicago to Santa Monica. Despite its small size, Amboy has a rich history and offers visitors a glimpse into the past. With its vintage roadside attractions, abandoned buildings, and stunning desert landscapes, Amboy is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered.

List of Places to Visit in Amboy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roy’s Motel and Cafe: A popular stop along Route 66 in the 1950s and 60s, Roy’s Motel and Cafe is now a historic landmark that offers visitors a step back in time. The motel rooms have been restored to their original condition, and the cafe serves classic American fare.
  • Amboy Schoolhouse: The Amboy Schoolhouse is a well-preserved example of early 20th-century school architecture. It served as a school until 1999 and now houses a small museum showcasing the history of the area.
  • The Salt Ponds: The Salt Ponds are a series of man-made pools used to extract salt from the desert. They were constructed in the 1930s and are still in operation today. Visitors can take a self-guided tour and learn about the salt-making process.

Natural Attractions of Amboy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amboy Crater: Amboy Crater is a cinder cone volcano that rises 250 feet above the surrounding terrain. Visitors can hike up to the rim for breathtaking views of the desert landscape. The hike is moderately challenging and takes about an hour to complete.
  • Bristol Lake: Bristol Lake is a dry lakebed that is home to a variety of birds and other wildlife. Visitors can take a scenic drive around the lakebed and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Giant Rock: Giant Rock is a massive boulder that measures seven stories tall and covers an area of 5,800 square feet. It is believed to be one of the largest freestanding boulders in the world. Visitors can explore the area around the rock and learn about its significance to the Native American tribes that once inhabited the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amboy

  • Amboy Crater Trail: This 3-mile roundtrip hike takes visitors to the rim of Amboy Crater. The trail is moderate to challenging and takes about an hour to complete.
  • Bristol Lake Loop: This 10-mile drive around Bristol Lake offers stunning views of the desert landscape. Visitors can also stop at various turnoffs to explore the area on foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Amboy: Activities and Tours

  • Route 66 Drive: Take a drive along historic Route 66 and experience the nostalgia of a bygone era.
  • Stargazing: With its remote location and clear desert skies, Amboy is the perfect place for stargazing.
  • Salt Flat Tours: Take a tour of the salt flats and learn about the salt-making process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amboy

Day 1: Arrive in Amboy, check into a hotel or motel, and take a drive along Route 66. Day 2: Visit Roy’s Motel and Cafe and explore the surrounding area. Day 3: Hike to the top of Amboy Crater and enjoy the stunning views. Day 4: Take a tour of the salt flats and learn about the salt-making process. Day 5: Explore the area around Bristol Lake and go birdwatching or hiking. Day 6: Visit the Amboy Schoolhouse and learn about the history of the area. Day 7: Depart from Amboy and head to your next destination.

Local Culture and Customs of Amboy

  • Respect the desert environment and leave no trace.
  • Be aware of the extreme temperatures and bring plenty of water.

Best Time to Visit Amboy

The best time to visit Amboy is in the spring or fall when temperatures are mild. Summer temperatures can be extreme, with temperatures often exceeding 100 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amboy

Do:

  • Respect the history and culture of the area.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Amboy

  • Watch out for snakes and other wildlife while hiking or exploring the area.
  • Be aware of the extreme temperatures and bring plenty of water and sunscreen.

Health in Amboy

  • The nearest medical facilities are located in nearby towns, so it is important to take any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Amboy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amboy

  • Palm Springs International Airport (PSP): Located 80 miles southwest of Amboy.

Bus Stations Near Amboy

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Needles, 60 miles northeast of Amboy.

Other Options

  • Driving: Amboy is located along Route 66, making it easily accessible by car.
  • Private Plane: There is a small airstrip located in Amboy for private planes.

How to Get Around in Amboy

The best way to get around Amboy is by car. Many of the attractions are spread out and require driving to reach. Biking or hiking is also an option for some areas, but be aware of the extreme temperatures and be prepared with plenty of water.

Where to Go Next from Amboy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Joshua Tree National Park: Located 70 miles southwest of Amboy, Joshua Tree National Park offers stunning desert landscapes and unique rock formations.
  • Route 66 Museum: Located in Barstow, 70 miles west of Amboy, the Route 66 Museum showcases the history and culture of the iconic highway.

Conclusion

Amboy, California, offers visitors a unique glimpse into the past with its historic relics and stunning desert landscapes. From exploring Route 66 to hiking to the top of Amboy Crater, there is something for everyone in this hidden gem in the Mojave Desert.
