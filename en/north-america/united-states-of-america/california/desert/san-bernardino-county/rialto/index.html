Introduction to Rialto

Located in San Bernardino County in the Desert region of California, Rialto is a charming city that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Nestled at the base of the San Bernardino Mountains, Rialto is home to a diverse range of attractions that cater to the interests of every type of traveler. From hiking trails and scenic views to museums and historical sites, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Rialto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Rialto Historical Society: This local museum offers a glimpse into the city’s past with exhibits showcasing Rialto’s development from a rural town to a thriving city.

  • Knights of Columbus Museum: The exhibit here includes historical artifacts and photographs from the organization’s founding in 1882 to the present day.

  • The Center for History and Culture: The Center for History and Culture showcases the history of the city of Rialto. In addition to exhibits, the center hosts a variety of events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Rialto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lytle Creek: This scenic area is perfect for visitors who want to experience the outdoors. The area is home to hiking trails, campsites, and a popular off-road vehicle area.

  • San Bernardino National Forest: Visitors can take a hike in the San Bernardino National Forest, which includes over 800,000 acres of forest land, including Big Bear Lake and the San Bernardino Mountains.

  • Glen Helen Regional Park: Ideal for families, Glen Helen Regional Park offers a variety of outdoor activities such as swimming, fishing, horseshoes, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rialto

  • Rialto Heights Trail: This 2.2-mile hiking trail is perfect for beginners and offers a beautiful view of the city.

  • Frisbee Golf Course: Located in the San Bernardino Valley, this 18-hole course is great for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy a round of frisbee golf.

Best Things to Do in Rialto: Activities and Tours

  • Rialto Theatre: This restored Art Deco theater is a landmark in the city and is home to live theater performances, musical events, and classic films.

  • Catch a Game: Visitors can catch minor league and major league sports games at a number of stadiums in the area, including San Manuel Stadium and the Victoria Gardens Cultural Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Rialto

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carne Asada Fries: A local favorite, this dish is a delicious combination of crispy fries, juicy carne asada, guacamole, cheese, and sour cream.

  • Menudo: A hearty and flavorful soup made with tripe, hominy, chiles, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Hangar 24 Brewery: This local craft brewery is known for creating award-winning beers and ciders.

  • Arrowhead Mountain Spring Water: Visitors have the opportunity to tour the factory where Arrowhead Mountain Spring Water is bottled and learn about the production process.

Where to Stay in Rialto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Plus Heritage Inn: This hotel offers comfortable amenities, including free Wi-Fi and a complimentary breakfast.

  • Knights Inn San Bernardino: Located near popular attractions, this hotel offers affordable accommodations for budget-minded travelers.

  • Bear Creek Bed and Breakfast: This beautifully landscaped guesthouse is perfect for those seeking a peaceful and serene retreat.

What to Buy in Rialto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • California Citrus State Historic Park: Visitors can purchase locally grown citrus fruit and other regional products at the park’s gift shop.

  • Farmers Markets: These local markets offer a variety of local produce, artisanal products, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Rialto

  • Rialto is known for its diverse population and strong sense of community. Visitors can attend local events and festivals to experience this sense of camaraderie firsthand.

Best Time to Visit Rialto

The best time to visit Rialto is during the fall and winter months, when the temperatures are cooler and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rialto

Do:

  • Be respectful to the local community and their cultural customs.

  • Tip appropriately for good service.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural environments.

  • Engage in unsafe or illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rialto

Rialto is generally a safe city, but visitors should use common sense precautions such as locking their cars and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Rialto

Visitors should stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors. They should also bring any necessary medications with them as there are limited medical facilities in the area.

Getting to Rialto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rialto

The nearest airports to Rialto are LA/Ontario International Airport and John Wayne Airport.

Bus Stations Near Rialto

Greyhound and Flixbus offer service to Rialto.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Rialto or take a train to San Bernardino and then a taxi to Rialto.

How to Get Around in Rialto

Visitors can rent a car or hop on a bus to get around Rialto.

Where to Go Next from Rialto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Palm Springs: Just an hour’s drive from Rialto, Palm Springs is known for its hot springs, spas, and outdoor activities.

  • Joshua Tree National Park: Visitors can explore the unique rock formations and desert landscapes of this park, located about an hour and a half from Rialto.

Conclusion

With its diverse range of attractions and natural beauty, Rialto is a great destination for travelers who want to experience the best that California has to offer. From hiking and biking to historic landmarks and lively festivals, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Come explore Rialto and create memories that will last a lifetime.
