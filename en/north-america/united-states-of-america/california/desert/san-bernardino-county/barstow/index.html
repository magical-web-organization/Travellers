Introduction to Barstow

Barstow is a small yet charming city located in San Bernardino County, in the heart of the Mojave Desert, California. Founded in 1886, Barstow has a rich history and is home to several fascinating museums, historic landmarks, and natural wonders. The city is often referred to as a gateway to Death Valley, Las Vegas, and other nearby destinations, making it an ideal stopover for road trippers. On top of that, Barstow is also known for its delicious food scene, with many local eateries serving up mouth-watering dishes that you won’t find anywhere else. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a longer stay, Barstow has plenty to offer for travelers of all kinds!

List of Places to Visit in Barstow

Here are some of the must-visit places in and around Barstow:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Route 66 Mother Road Museum: Learn about the history of Route 66 and its impact on American culture. The museum is located in the old Casa Del Desierto, which served as a Harvey House hotel and restaurant in the early 1900s.
  2. Western America Railroad Museum: Explore the largest rail museum in the Southwestern United States, with a vast collection of locomotives, equipment, and artifacts related to railroading.
  3. Mojave River Valley Museum: Discover the rich history and geology of the Mojave Desert, with exhibits on Native American culture, pioneer life, mining, and paleontology.
  4. Calico Ghost Town: Step back in time and experience what life was like in a real Old West mining town. The town features restored buildings, a train ride, gold panning, and gunfight shows.
  5. Harvey House Railroad Depot and Casa Del Desierto: Visit the restored train station and hotel that served as a stop for travelers on the Santa Fe Railway and Route 66 in the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Barstow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Rainbow Basin Natural Area: Enjoy the spectacular scenery of eroded badlands, canyons, and rock formations. The area is popular for hiking, wildlife watching, and geology tours.
  2. Afton Canyon Nature Preserve: Experience the beauty of the Mojave River and its surroundings, with opportunities for camping, hiking, fishing, and kayaking.
  3. Mojave National Preserve: Discover the vast wilderness of the Mojave Desert, with stunning landscapes, unique wildlife, and recreational activities such as hiking, biking, and camping.
  4. Joshua Tree National Park: Explore the iconic rock formations, cactus gardens, and desert landscapes of one of California’s most popular national parks. The park is located about an hour’s drive from Barstow.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barstow

Barstow and its surroundings offer plenty of hiking and biking opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails:

  1. Mojave Riverwalk Trail: A 7.5-mile paved trail that follows the banks of the Mojave River, offering scenic views of the river and its wildlife.
  2. Owl Canyon Trail: A short yet challenging hike through a narrow canyon with stunning rock formations and petroglyphs.
  3. Barstow Petroglyphs Trail: A moderate hike to a rock art site with hundreds of petroglyphs dating back to ancient times.
  4. Rainbow Basin Loop Trail: A 1.5-mile hike through colorful badlands and rock formations, with opportunities for wildlife viewing and geology interpretation.
  5. Kelso Dunes Trail: A strenuous hike to the top of the highest sand dunes in the Mojave Desert, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and desert.

Best Things to Do in Barstow: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re into history, culture, or outdoor adventures, Barstow has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Take a ghost town tour: Explore the abandoned mining town of Calico and learn about its history and legends.
  2. Visit the Route 66 Museum: Experience the nostalgia of the famous “Mother Road” and its impact on American culture.
  3. Go stargazing: Barstow’s clear skies offer ideal conditions for a night of stargazing and sky watching.
  4. Attend a festival: Barstow hosts several annual festivals and events, including the Barstow Rodeo, Barstow Music Festival, and Route 66 Mother Road Festival.
  5. Take a hot air balloon ride: See the city and its surroundings from a bird’s eye view and experience the thrill of a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Barstow

Barstow has a vibrant food scene that reflects its rich cultural and historical heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Barstow-style chili dogs: These hot dogs are topped with a special chili sauce and are a local favorite.
  2. California burritos: These burritos are filled with carne asada, French fries, cheese, and guacamole, and are a signature dish of Southern California.
  3. Chicken fried steak: This classic American dish is a staple at many local diners and is served with gravy and mashed potatoes.
  4. Date shakes: Made with sweet Medjool dates, these shakes are a refreshing and unique treat.

Local Beverages

  1. Mojave Red Beer: A local craft beer that is known for its delicious blend of spices and herbs.
  2. Desert Bloom Wine: A winery that uses grapes grown in the Mojave Desert to produce award-winning wines.
  3. Margaritas: A popular cocktail in California, these drinks can be enjoyed at many local Mexican restaurants.

Where to Stay in Barstow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barstow has plenty of accommodation options for travelers of all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Ayres Hotel Barstow: A luxury hotel with spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  2. Comfort Suites Barstow: A mid-range hotel with modern amenities, including a fitness center and a hot tub.
  3. California Inn Barstow: An affordable motel with comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Barstow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, Barstow has plenty of options. Here are some ideas:

  1. Route 66 memorabilia: T-shirts, hats, postcards, and other items related to the famous “Mother Road.”
  2. Mojave Desert rocks and minerals: These make great souvenirs for geology enthusiasts.
  3. Calico Ghost Town souvenirs: Visit the gift shop and take home a piece of Old West history.

Local Culture and Customs of Barstow

Barstow is a welcoming and friendly city, but there are a few things to keep in mind when visiting:

  1. Respect the desert environment: The Mojave Desert is a fragile ecosystem that needs to be protected, so make sure to pack out all of your trash and be mindful of your impact on the environment.
  2. Be prepared for extreme temperatures: The desert can get very hot during the day and very cold at night, so make sure to bring appropriate clothing and gear.
  3. Follow local customs and traditions: Barstow has a rich cultural heritage, so take the time to learn about it and respect it.

Best Time to Visit Barstow

The best time to visit Barstow is during the spring and fall, when the temperatures are mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer can be very hot, with temperatures averaging in the 90s and 100s, while winter can be chilly, with temperatures dropping below freezing at night.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barstow

Do:

  1. Do explore the city’s rich history and culture by visiting its museums, landmarks, and festivals.
  2. Do take advantage of the scenic beauty of the desert by going on a hiking or biking trip.
  3. Do try the local cuisine, including the famous Barstow-style chili dogs and California burritos.
  4. Do respect the environment and follow local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  2. Don’t underestimate the extreme temperatures of the desert, always come prepared.
  3. Don’t be disrespectful of local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barstow

Barstow is a safe city, but there are a few things to keep in mind to stay safe:

  1. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night and in high-traffic areas.
  2. Lock your car and keep valuable items out of sight.
  3. Do not leave belongings unattended.

Health in Barstow

Barstow has medical facilities that provide quality healthcare services. However, it’s always a good idea to bring a first aid kit and any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Barstow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barstow

The nearest airports to Barstow are:

  1. Ontario International Airport (ONT): About 85 miles away, this airport serves several major airlines and has daily flights to many destinations.
  2. Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): About 120 miles away, this airport is one of the busiest in the United States and serves many international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Barstow

Barstow has a Greyhound bus station that serves several routes throughout California and the Southwest.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get to Barstow, as the city is located at the intersection of several major highways, including Interstate 15 and Route 66.

How to Get Around in Barstow

The best way to get around in Barstow is by car, but there are also several taxi and ride-sharing services available. If you’re looking for an eco-friendly option, you can explore the city by bike or on foot.

Where to Go Next from Barstow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Barstow is a gateway to several nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  1. Las Vegas: Just a few hours’ drive away, this world-famous city offers endless entertainment options, from casinos and shows to fine dining and shopping.
  2. Death Valley National Park: Located about 2 hours’ drive from Barstow, this park features stunning desert landscapes, canyons, and sand dunes.
  3. Palm Springs: About 2 hours’ drive away, this desert oasis is known for its hot springs, spas, and golf courses.
  4. Los Angeles: About 2 hours’ drive away, this iconic city offers endless sightseeing options, from Hollywood and Beverly Hills to the beaches of Santa Monica and Venice.

Conclusion
