Introduction to Big Bear Lake

Nestled in the San Bernardino Mountains of California, Big Bear Lake is a popular year-round destination known for its picturesque beauty and outdoor activities. The lake is surrounded by stunning peaks and is home to an array of activities, from skiing to hiking, boating to fishing.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll give you everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Big Bear Lake. From where to stay to what to eat, we’ve got you covered to make your experience unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Big Bear Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Big Bear Valley Historical Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Big Bear Lake, dating back to the native Serrano Indians and pioneers of the 1800s.
  • The Village: The heart of Big Bear Lake, The Village is a charming shopping and entertainment district with a diverse range of stores, restaurants, and venues for live music and events.

Natural Attractions of Big Bear Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Bear Discovery Center: The Discovery Center offers a wide range of activities for all ages, including nature walks, stargazing events, and ranger-led nature tours.
  • Big Bear Alpine Zoo: The alpine zoo is home to many animals that are indigenous to the mountains, including black bears, wolves, and mountain lions.
  • Pine Knot Trail: This scenic trail winds through the San Bernardino Mountains and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the lake and surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Bear Lake

  • Castle Rock Trail: This moderate hike leads to a scenic viewpoint and overlooks the lake from above.
  • Alpine Pedal Path: This easy, family-friendly trail is perfect for biking and walking. The trail follows the shoreline around the lake and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding mountains.
  • Sugarloaf Mountain Trail: This intermediate-level hike takes you to the top of Sugarloaf Mountain, which offers some of the best views of Big Bear Lake.

Best Things to Do in Big Bear Lake: Activities and Tours

  • Snow Summit Ski Resort: Skiing and snowboarding are popular winter activities at Big Bear Lake, and Snow Summit Ski Resort offers some of the area’s best slopes and trails.
  • Helicopter Tour: Take to the skies with a helicopter tour of Big Bear Lake and enjoy breathtaking aerial views of the surrounding mountains and the lake.
  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular year-round activity at the lake, with plenty of opportunities to catch freshwater fish such as trout and bass.

Food and Drink Scene of Big Bear Lake

Must-Try Dishes

  • Big Bear Burger: A classic burger topped with BBQ sauce and onion rings, served alongside sweet potato fries.
  • Trout: Trout is a local delicacy, and many restaurants in town offer fresh, locally-caught trout on their menu.
  • Huckleberry Pie: This pie is made using the tart, tart-sweet berry native to the area.

Local Beverages

  • Big Bear Brewing Company: This local brewery serves up craft beers that are inspired by the natural beauty of Big Bear Lake.
  • Teddy Bear Restaurant: Their signature drink, Bear Bait, is made up of a mix of different spirits and served in a souvenir glass shaped like a bear.

Where to Stay in Big Bear Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Lodge at Big Bear Lake: This cozy hotel offers lakefront views and access to various outdoor activities.
  • Big Bear Hostel: The perfect budget-friendly option for solo travelers or small groups.
  • Knickerbocker Mansion: For a unique, upscale experience, stay at Knickerbocker Mansion, a Victorian-era mansion that’s been renovated into a boutique hotel.

What to Buy in Big Bear Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Big Bear Lake T-Shirts and Hoodies: Show off your love for Big Bear Lake with some branded apparel.
  • Huckleberry Products: From jams and syrups to tea and candy, bring home some locally-made huckleberry products.
  • Handmade Crafts: The town of Big Bear Lake boasts creativity in the form of handmade crafts including jewelry and pottery items for tourists to take back as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Big Bear Lake

The culture of Big Bear Lake is best-known for its outdoor adventure-scene. The town is also known for its local artists and creatives. Visitors can enjoy activities such as hiking, skiing, fishing, boating, etc. The locals are friendly and welcoming to tourists, and visitors should make sure to respect their beautiful environment by leaving trails, lakes, and parks cleaner and more beautiful than they found them.

Best Time to Visit Big Bear Lake

Big Bear Lake is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. For winter sports enthusiasts, the best time to visit is between December and February for skiing and snow activities. For those interested in enjoying the summer activities, the best time to visit is from June to August when temperatures are warm with cool breezes and the lake activities are in full swing. Fall can also be ideal for outdoor activities, particularly hiking, as the leaves change color and crowds thin out.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Bear Lake

Do:

  • Drive cautiously on the winding mountain roads.
  • Leave the outdoors and natural areas better than you found them.
  • Respect the privacy and nature of the wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in natural areas.
  • Do not feed wild animals.
  • Do not light a fire outside of designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Big Bear Lake

Big Bear Lake is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always good to practice common sense and take basic safety precautions, such as locking doors, keeping valuables out of sight, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Big Bear Lake

Rev up your energy before hitting the adventurous trails of Big Bear Lake. Carry a water bottle at all times to keep yourself well-hydrated. Stay away from potentially dangerous wildlife and make sure to apply sunscreen while outdoors to protect yourself from the sun’s rays.

Getting to Big Bear Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Big Bear Lake

The nearest airport to Big Bear Lake is the LA/Ontario International Airport, which is located about 60 miles away from the city of Big Bear.

Bus Stations Near Big Bear Lake

Greyhound provides bus service to a bus terminal in Downtown San Bernardino, which is about 30 miles away from Big Bear Lake.

Other Options

You can rent a car at LA/Ontario International Airport to get around Big Bear Lake comfortably. Alternatively, Uber or Lyft services are also available in and around Big Bear Lake.

How to Get Around in Big Bear Lake

For visitors who don’t have a car, the Mountain Transit bus system is available to travel locally. Alternatively, bicycles are available to rent from different stores in town. Visitors can also use ride sharing apps like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Big Bear Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Arrowhead: Located about 30 minutes from Big Bear, Lake Arrowhead is renowned for its exclusive resort and tourist area with a vast selection of outdoor activities
  • Joshua Tree National Park: This famous national park is located less than two hours from Big Bear and is known for its unique desert landscapes and photo opportunities
  • Palm Springs: Located about 75 miles southeast of Big Bear, Palm Springs is a desert resort city known for its hot springs, golf courses, and spas.

Conclusion

Big Bear Lake is truly a year-round destination with an endless array of outdoor activities, entertainment, restaurants, and shopping. Whether you’re planning to hit the ski slopes or go on a scenic hike, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this hidden gem of California.
