Introduction to Parker Dam

Parker Dam is a small town located in San Bernardino County, California, in the United States. While the town is not very populous, it has a lot to offer to its visitors, from outdoor adventures and scenic views to cultural experiences and historical landmarks. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an adrenaline-filled adventure, Parker Dam is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Parker Dam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Desert Bar Museum: Experience the old western lifestyle while exploring this museum dedicated to the history of Parker Dam.
  • Parker Pioneer Homestead: Take a tour of the oldest standing homestead in Parker Dam and learn about the pioneering history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Parker Dam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colorado River: Go water skiing, wakeboarding, or simply enjoy a leisurely boat ride on the Colorado River.
  • Bill Williams River National Wildlife Refuge: Hike through this wildlife sanctuary to experience a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parker Dam

  • Parker Dam Rd.: This road offers picturesque views of the surrounding mountains and the Colorado River. It’s perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a scenic drive.
  • Castle Rock Trail: This 3.5 mile hike is suitable for beginners and offers mesmerizing views of the Colorado River and the local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Parker Dam: Activities and Tours

  • Jet Ski Tours: Take a guided jet ski tour of the Colorado River and be enchanted by the beauty of Parker Dam and its surroundings.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Go on an exhilarating hot air balloon ride to experience stunning views of the surrounding mountains and the Colorado River.

Food and Drink Scene of Parker Dam

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sonoran Hot Dogs: A local favorite, this hot dog is wrapped in bacon and served with a variety of toppings, including grilled onions and peppers, beans, guacamole, and salsa.
  • Carne Asada Fries: A hearty dish that includes fries topped with grilled steak, cheese, guacamole, and sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado River Bourbon: A locally distilled bourbon that has a smooth and distinct flavor.
  • Parker Dam Pale Ale: A refreshing pale ale brewed locally.

Where to Stay in Parker Dam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • BlueWater Resort & Casino: This hotel features a casino, a spa, and an outdoor pool with a bar.
  • Havasu Springs Resort: A waterfront resort that offers a variety of activities, including a private beach, kayaking, and hiking.

What to Buy in Parker Dam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Colorado River Rocks: Unique and colorful river rocks that make great souvenirs and decorations.
  • Local Artisanal Crafts: Hand-made crafts that showcase the local art scene.

Local Culture and Customs of Parker Dam

  • Western Styles: Parker Dam is known for its cowboy culture, so dressing in western-style clothing will help you blend in with the locals.
  • Respect for Nature: The town values its natural environment, so it’s important to be respectful of nature and avoid littering.

Best Time to Visit Parker Dam

The best time to visit Parker Dam is during the fall, winter, and spring seasons (October to April) as temperatures tend to stay in the comfortable range of 60 to 80 degrees Fahrenheit. However, if you’re into water sports, the summer months (May to September) are perfect for you, with the temperature highs usually in the 90s.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parker Dam

Do:

  • Dress in comfortable, layered clothing to adjust to changing temperatures.
  • Respect the wildlife and natural habitat.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Leave valuables in plain sight in your car.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parker Dam

  • Avoid consuming untreated water and make sure you have enough water to stay hydrated.
  • Be cautious when driving, especially when navigating the winding mountain roads.
  • Stay aware of flash floods when visiting the Bill Williams River National Wildlife Refuge.

Health in Parker Dam

  • The town is not known for any health hazards, but it’s important to be prepared for sudden weather changes and unpredictable wildlife.

Getting to Parker Dam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Parker Dam

  • Laughlin/Bullhead International Airport (IFP) - 35 miles from Parker Dam
  • Lake Havasu City Airport (HII) - 40 miles from Parker Dam

Bus Stations Near Parker Dam

  • Parker Greyhound Station - 2 miles from Parker Dam

Other Options

  • Driving: Parker Dam is easily accessible by car, with Highway 95 running through the town.
  • Boat: If you’re arriving by water, marinas and boat rentals are available.

How to Get Around in Parker Dam

The best way to get around Parker Dam is by car. While the town is small, it can be difficult to access the natural attractions without private transportation. Biking is also a great option for exploring the town, its surroundings and the Colorado River.

Where to Go Next from Parker Dam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Havasu: Known for its water sports and famous London Bridge, Lake Havasu is a short drive from Parker Dam.
  • Joshua Tree National Park: About a 3-hour drive away, the national park provides an otherworldly experience filled with unique geological formations and stunning vistas.

Conclusion

Parker Dam is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, adventurous travelers and history buffs alike. The town’s unique culture and natural beauty will leave you with an unforgettable experience. Whether you’re traveling solo, with a significant other or with family, Parker Dam is the perfect place to explore.
