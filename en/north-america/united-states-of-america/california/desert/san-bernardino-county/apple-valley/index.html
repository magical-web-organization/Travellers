Introduction to Apple Valley

Nestled in the Mojave Desert of Southern California, Apple Valley is a charming and surprisingly diverse small town with a population of just over 73,000 people. It is surrounded by picturesque scenery, including the San Bernardino National Forest, the Mojave River, and the towering San Gabriel Mountains.

If you’re looking for a break from the bustling cities of California, Apple Valley is the perfect destination. It offers visitors a variety of exciting outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. In this tour guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Apple Valley and help you plan your ultimate trip to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Apple Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Apple Valley has a rich history and is home to several culturally important attractions. Here are some must-visit places:

Victor Valley Museum

The Victor Valley Museum offers visitors an in-depth look at the history, culture, and natural resources of the High Desert region. The museum features exhibits on mining, railroads, Native American culture, and more.

Apple Valley Legacy Museum

The Apple Valley Legacy Museum tells the story of Apple Valley’s past, from its early settlers to its role in the aerospace industry. It features exhibits on local history, including photos and artifacts from the town’s early days.

Natural Attractions of Apple Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Apple Valley is the perfect destination. Here are some natural attractions to explore:

Mojave Riverwalk

The Mojave Riverwalk is a relaxing trail that takes you through a peaceful green oasis in the middle of the desert. The trail is perfect for walking, jogging, or biking and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Deep Creek Hot Springs

Deep Creek Hot Springs is a hidden gem located just a short drive from Apple Valley. It is an idyllic spot to soak in hot springs while surrounded by breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apple Valley

Apple Valley has a variety of hiking and biking routes that will take visitors through stunning scenery. Here are some must-visit routes:

Apple Valley Trails

The Apple Valley Trails system is a network of trails that are perfect for hiking and biking. The trails offer stunning views of the San Gabriel Mountains and take you through beautiful natural scenery.

Horsemen’s Center Park Trail

The Horsemen’s Center Park Trail is a scenic route that is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. It takes you through the park’s picturesque landscape, featuring rolling hills and natural vegetation.

Best Things to Do in Apple Valley: Activities and Tours

Apple Valley has a lot to offer visitors, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions. Below are some of the best things to do in Apple Valley:

Visit the High Desert Farmers Market

The High Desert Farmers Market is a must-visit destination for foodies. The market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other treats from local farmers and vendors.

Take a Tour of the Western Science Center

The Western Science Center is a fascinating museum that offers visitors an in-depth look at the natural history of the region. The museum features exhibits on dinosaurs, Ice Age mammals, and local Native American culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Apple Valley

If you’re planning to spend a week in Apple Valley, you have plenty of exciting things to do. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Explore the Mojave Riverwalk and visit the Victor Valley Museum

Day 2: Take a tour of the Western Science Center and enjoy a relaxing evening at Deep Creek Hot Springs

Day 3: Go on a hiking or biking adventure along the Apple Valley Trails

Day 4: Visit the High Desert Farmers Market and try some delicious local cuisine

Day 5: Relax and enjoy the scenery at Horsemen’s Center Park

Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Apple Valley Legacy Museum

Day 7: Explore nearby destinations, such as Joshua Tree National Park or Big Bear Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Apple Valley

Apple Valley’s food and drink scene is diverse and delicious. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Apple Valley Burger

This local specialty features a juicy burger patty, crispy bacon, tangy pickles, and fresh lettuce and tomato. It is served on a toasted bun and is a must-try for burger lovers.

Vegan Tacos

Vegan tacos are a delicious and healthy option for those looking for plant-based meals. They feature fresh vegetables, avocado, and flavorful seasonings.

Local Beverages

Apple Valley Coffee

Apple Valley Coffee is a locally roasted coffee that is known for its rich flavor and smooth finish. It is a must-try for coffee aficionados.

Stone Brewing Company Beer

Stone Brewing Company is a local craft brewery that offers a variety of delicious beers. The brewery is known for its bold and flavorful IPAs.

Where to Stay in Apple Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a comfortable hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Apple Valley has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best options:

Hilton Garden Inn

This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool. It is conveniently located in the heart of Apple Valley.

Ayres Hotel

The Ayres Hotel offers elegant rooms, a fitness center, and a relaxing spa. It is a great option for those looking for luxury accommodation.

What to Buy in Apple Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir or local product to bring home, Apple Valley has plenty of options. Here are some of the top products to buy:

Local Honey

Apple Valley is known for its delicious, locally produced honey. It is a sweet and natural souvenir that is perfect for foodies.

Western Wear

Apple Valley is located in cowboy country, so it is no surprise that there are plenty of stores that sell western wear. You can find everything from cowboy boots to hats to belts.

Local Culture and Customs of Apple Valley

Apple Valley has a strong sense of community and pride. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly locals who are happy to share their town’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Apple Valley

The best time to visit Apple Valley is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. The temperatures can be extreme in the summer and winter, so it is best to plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apple Valley

Do:

  • Explore the natural scenery and outdoor activities
  • Visit the local museums to learn about the town’s history
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions when hiking or biking
  • Disregard local traditions and customs
  • Litter or damage the natural environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Apple Valley

Apple Valley is a safe destination for visitors. However, it is important to take normal precautions when hiking or biking on trails, such as bringing plenty of water and sunscreen.

Health in Apple Valley

Visitors should ensure they are properly hydrated and use sunscreen when exploring the outdoors. It is always advisable to carry a first aid kit in case of unexpected injuries.

Getting to Apple Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apple Valley

The nearest airports to Apple Valley are LA/Ontario International Airport and San Bernardino International Airport, both about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Apple Valley

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in Victorville, just 15 miles from Apple Valley.

Other Options

Car rental and taxi services are the most convenient options for getting around Apple Valley and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Apple Valley

Visitors can get around Apple Valley by car, bike, or foot. There are several car rental options available, and many trails are suitable for biking. Walking is a great way to explore the local scenery and culture.

Where to Go Next from Apple Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Apple Valley is located near several exciting destinations. Here are some nearby places to visit:

Joshua Tree National Park

Joshua Tree National Park is known for its stunning rock formations and unique ecosystem. It is just a short drive from Apple Valley and is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Big Bear Lake

Big Bear Lake is an idyllic alpine destination that offers visitors a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and skiing. It is a great option for a day trip or weekend getaway from Apple Valley.

Conclusion

Apple Valley is a charming and unique destination that offers visitors a mix of outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. We hope this tour guide has helped you plan your ultimate trip to this hidden gem of Southern California.
