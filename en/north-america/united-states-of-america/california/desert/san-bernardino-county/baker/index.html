Introduction to Baker

Located in the Mojave Desert, Baker is a small town in the San Bernardino County of California, United States. This town is known for its iconic roadside attractions, including the world’s tallest thermometer and the famous Alien Fresh Jerky store. This remote town is a popular stop for travelers along the I-15 as they head to or from Las Vegas, and it’s known as the gateway to Death Valley. In this guide, we’ll explore the various attractions and activities to help you plan your visit to Baker.

List of Places to Visit in Baker

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Baker Community Museum – This museum is dedicated to the early settlers of Baker, including Native American tribes and the Spanish missionaries. Exhibits include photographs, documents, and artifacts from the past.

  2. The Mad Greek Cafe – This Greek restaurant has been a staple in Baker for over 30 years. The colorful exterior, complete with a giant Greek statue, makes it hard to miss.

Natural Attractions of Baker: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Death Valley National Park – Just an hour away from Baker, this national park has stunning landscapes and unique geological formations. The park offers various activities like hiking, stargazing, and exploring ghost towns.

  2. The Mojave National Preserve – This national preserve boasts of diverse wildlife, over 350 miles of hiking trails, and scenic drives.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baker

Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Baker:

  1. The Kelso Dunes Trail – This hiking trail stretches for 3 miles and takes visitors to the top of the Kelso Dunes. The view of the Mojave Desert from the top is breathtaking.

  2. The Mojave Lava Tube Trail – This trail takes visitors through fascinating lava tubes that were formed during a volcanic eruption over 15,000 years ago.

Best Things to Do in Baker: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Baker:

  1. Visit the World’s Tallest Thermometer – Standing at 134 feet tall, this thermometer has been measuring the temperature in Baker since 1991.

  2. Go on a UFO Sightseeing Tour – Learn about the infamous Area 51 and other paranormal sightings in the Mojave Desert.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baker

Food and Drink Scene of Baker

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Mad Greek Salad – A blend of tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, and feta cheese, dressed with a homemade Greek vinaigrette.

  2. Bun Boy Burger – A classic burger with all the fixings from the famous Bun Boy restaurant in Baker.

Local Beverages

  1. Alien Amber Beer – A locally brewed beer from the Alien Fresh Jerky store.

  2. Pioneer Saloon Spirits – A variety of spirits from the historic Pioneer Saloon in Goodsprings, Nevada, just 30 minutes away from Baker.

Where to Stay in Baker: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Baker:

  1. The Baker Hotel – This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and free Wi-Fi.

  2. The Bun Boy Motel – This motel features clean and comfortable rooms, and it’s right next to the famous Bun Boy restaurant.

What to Buy in Baker: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Alien Fresh Jerky – This store sells a variety of local and unique jerky flavors, including alien-themed packaging.

  2. Stargazers Inn and Observatory Souvenirs – Visitors can take home t-shirts, mugs, and other souvenirs from this unique hotel and observatory.

Local Culture and Customs of Baker

Baker is a small town with a laid-back vibe. Locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. The town is home to various artists and creatives, and visitors should take the time to engage with the local community.

Best Time to Visit Baker

The best time to visit Baker is during the fall and winter seasons (October to February). The temperature is mild, making it comfortable for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Summer can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching up to 120 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baker

Do:

  • Do try the Mad Greek Cafe’s authentic Greek dishes.
  • Do visit the Baker Community Museum and learn about the town’s history.
  • Do take advantage of the outdoor adventures, including hiking and sightseeing.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, as the desert landscape is sensitive to garbage.
  • Don’t underestimate the heat during the summer months.
  • Don’t miss the opportunity to take a UFO Sightseeing Tour.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baker

Baker is relatively safe, but visitors should take precautions when traveling through the desert. Always carry plenty of water, and let someone know your travel plans before venturing out.

Health in Baker

The dry desert air can cause dehydration and dry skin. Make sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, and use sunscreen and moisturizers to protect your skin from the sun and wind.

Getting to Baker: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baker

The closest airport to Baker is the Las Vegas McCarran International Airport, which is approximately 80 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Baker

Greyhound offers bus service to Baker, with a stop at the Alien Fresh Jerky store.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Baker by car, bike, or motorcycle by taking the I-15.

How to Get Around in Baker

The best way to get around in Baker is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car at the Las Vegas McCarran International Airport or rent a bike from the Mad Greek Cafe.

Where to Go Next from Baker: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Las Vegas – A vibrant city just an hour and a half drive from Baker, filled with entertainment, nightlife, and casinos.

  2. Salem’s Lot – An abandoned ghost town located in the Mojave Desert, a perfect spot for urban explorers.

Conclusion

Baker may be a small town, but it offers a lot of unique and exciting experiences for travelers. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or exploring the local culture, Baker has something to offer. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this hidden gem of California.
