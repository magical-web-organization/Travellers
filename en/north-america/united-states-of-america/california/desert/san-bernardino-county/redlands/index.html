Introduction to Redlands

Nestled within the heart of San Bernardino County’s Desert region, Redlands is a charming city in California, United States of America. Redlands is known for its beautiful Victorian architecture, historical landmarks, and scenic outdoor spaces. It’s a perfect blend of natural beauty and urban culture that makes it a must-visit destination in California.

List of Places to Visit in Redlands

Redlands has many cultural and natural attractions that you must visit for a complete Redlands experience. We’ve compiled a list of must-see Redlands attractions for you:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Lincoln Memorial Shrine: Located beside the A.K. Smiley Public Library, the shrine honors Abraham Lincoln, who is known for his contributions to American history.

  2. Redlands Bowl: Dating back to 1924, the Redlands Bowl presents free summer concerts that feature music, dance, and theater performances.

  3. Kimberly Crest House & Gardens: The 1897 French chateau-style mansion turned museum is a must-visit attraction in Redlands.

Natural Attractions of Redlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. San Timoteo Canyon: The canyon is famous for its natural beauty and hiking trails that offer scenic views of Redlands and the nearby cities.

  2. Mount San Gorgonio: Rising 11,500 feet above sea level, the peak is the highest point in Southern California and a popular hiking spot.

  3. Oakmont Park: The beautiful park features a playground, picnic area, and walking trails which make it perfect for a day out.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redlands

Redlands is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular trails that you can trek or bike:

  1. Santa Ana Trail: The trail stretches over 30 miles and runs from San Bernardino to the Pacific Ocean. Hikers and bikers can enjoy scenic views of the city and the mountains along the way.

  2. San Timoteo Nature Sanctuary: The 200-acre nature sanctuary is one of the best places to explore the scenic beauty of Redlands. Hikers can spot a variety of birds and wildlife while enjoying the peaceful atmosphere.

  3. Live Oak Canyon Trail: An easy and scenic hiking trail, the Live Oak Canyon Trail is perfect for beginners. It is a 2.5-mile hike through the beautiful redwood trees.

Best Things to Do in Redlands: Activities and Tours

Apart from the museums and hiking trails, there are other exciting things that you can do in Redlands:

  1. Smiley Park: The park has a skate park, basketball court, and playground, making it perfect for families with young children.

  2. Ayres Hotel: The hotel is built on the site of a former orange grove and is one of the best places to stay in Redlands. It is known for its stunning architecture and luxurious amenities.

  3. Redlands Market Night: Held every Thursday evening, the Market Night is an outdoor event where local farmers and artisans gather to sell their products. It’s a great place to buy local souvenirs and gifts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Redlands

For a more comprehensive Redlands experience, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary. Follow this itinerary to cover all the attractions mentioned above and more:

Day 1 Visit the Lincoln Memorial Shrine and explore the A.K. Smiley Public Library
Day 2 Head to the San Timoteo Canyon for a scenic hike
Day 3 Explore the Redlands Bowl and attend a concert
Day 4 Go on a biking tour of Santa Ana Trail
Day 5 Visit the Kimberly Crest House & Gardens
Day 6 Take a day trip to the summit of Mount San Gorgonio
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and local products at the Redlands Market Night, and dine at local restaurants

Food and Drink Scene of Redlands

Redlands has an exciting food and drink scene, with a variety of options to suit everyone’s taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Orange Blossom Burger: The juicy patty is topped with onion rings, cheese, and honey mustard sauce, making it a must-try dish

  2. Carnitas Tacos: The local carnitas tacos are a must-try. They’re made with roast pork, cilantro, and lime, making them the perfect snack.

  3. Creme Brule French Toast: This dish is a unique take on a classic breakfast. The French Toast is topped with caramelized sugar, making it a delicious treat.

Local Beverages

Redlands has its own local breweries, where you can experience the local beer scene. Sudwerk Brewing Company and Brewcakes are two of the most popular local breweries.

Where to Stay in Redlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Redlands has a variety of accommodation options for travelers. Here are three of the best options:

  1. Ayres Hotel Redlands: The hotel is in the heart of the city, making it easily accessible to all Redlands attractions. It has luxurious amenities for a comfortable stay.

  2. Comfort Suites Redlands: The guesthouse offers spacious rooms and a complimentary breakfast buffet.

  3. The Big Bear Hostel: Located a short distance from the city, the hostel offers affordable accommodation for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Redlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Redlands has an array of unique local products that make for great souvenirs. Here are a few must-buy items:

  1. Citrus-Flavored Honey: Since Redlands is well-known for its citrus fruits, it’s no surprise that the city also has an abundance of honey that is flavored with grapefruit, orange, and lemon.

  2. Redlands Wine: The city has several wineries that produce exquisite wines. Stop by one of the local wineries and bring back a bottle home.

Local Culture and Customs of Redlands

Redlands has a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. The city’s Victorian architecture and vintage Heritage Homes are a testament to this. The locals are friendly and welcoming, making it easy for visitors to immerse themselves in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Redlands

The best time to visit Redlands is between September and November when the weather is mild and the city is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redlands

Here are a few things to keep in mind while exploring Redlands:

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Visit the local museums and landmarks to learn about the city’s history and culture.

  3. Go hiking or biking to explore the scenic beauty of Redlands.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or cause harm to natural attractions.

  2. Disrespect the locals or their traditions.

  3. Leave valuables unattended in public spaces.

Safety and Health

While Redlands is generally safe for tourists, it’s essential to take precautions to ensure a pleasant and safe experience.

Safety in Redlands:

  1. Keep your valuables with you at all times.

  2. Travel in a group, if possible.

  3. Stay in well-lit areas and avoid deserted places.

Health in Redlands:

  1. Stay hydrated during outdoor activities.

  2. Wear sunscreen and protective clothing during outdoor activities.

  3. Consult a medical professional before hiking or biking if you have any health problems.

Getting to Redlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Redlands is easily accessible by multiple transportation options:

Bus Stations Near Redlands:

  1. Greyhound Station: Located in downtown San Bernardino, only a few miles from Redlands.

Where to Go Next from Redlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
