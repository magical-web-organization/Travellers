Introduction to Lake Arrowhead

Lake Arrowhead is a small but picturesque town nestled in the San Bernardino Mountains of Southern California. It’s a great year-round destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy the great outdoors.

This town is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, complete with majestic pine trees, crystal-clear lake waters, and an abundance of wildlife. Apart from that, Lake Arrowhead offers a wide variety of attractions to its visitors, including cultural sights, outdoor adventures, culinary delights, and much more.

Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Lake Arrowhead promises to offer an unforgettable experience that you’ll cherish forever. Keep scrolling down to discover what this wonderful destination has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Arrowhead

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lake Arrowhead Village

The Lake Arrowhead Village is a charming shopping destination that offers a variety of shops, dining options, and entertainment venues. Visit this iconic destination to find the perfect souvenirs, clothing, jewellery, and art pieces to take back home.

Lake Arrowhead Fire Station Museum

The Lake Arrowhead Fire Station Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the history of this town. Come and see the memorabilia, antique fire trucks, and equipment that have been used by the local fire department since the 1920s.

Rim of the World Historical Society Museum

The Rim of the World Historical Society Museum, located in nearby Cedar Glen, is a treasure trove of information about the history of the San Bernardino Mountains and the surrounding areas. Come and explore the exhibits that showcase the fascinating stories of the local pioneers, miners, and loggers.

Natural Attractions of Lake Arrowhead: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Arrowhead Trail

The Lake Arrowhead Trail is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and horseback riding enthusiasts. The trail winds through beautiful forests, streams and offers stunning views of the San Bernardino Mountains and the picturesque Lake Arrowhead.

Heaps Peak Arboretum

The Heaps Peak Arboretum is a 28-acre botanical garden that showcases an extensive collection of native and exotic plants, trees, and shrubs. Come and visit this peaceful sanctuary to learn more about the flora of Southern California.

Wildhaven Ranch

The Wildhaven Ranch is a wildlife sanctuary that houses various wild animals, including black bears, mountain lions, bobcats, and coyotes. Take a guided tour to get up close and personal with these majestic creatures and learn more about their natural habitats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Arrowhead

Lake Arrowhead boasts an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer a thrilling outdoor experience to visitors. Here are some of the most popular routes to explore:

Lake Gregory Trail Network

The Lake Gregory Trail Network is a 12.5-mile-long trail that offers a variety of terrains, ranging from smooth and easy paths to challenging ascents and descents. It provides some of the best views of the San Bernardino Mountains and the surrounding areas.

Pacific Crest Trail

The Pacific Crest Trail is a 2,650-mile-long trail that runs from Mexico to Canada. Lake Arrowhead is one of the many access points to this trail. Hikers and bikers can choose from varying sections of the trail to explore, depending on their level of experience and fitness.

North Shore National Recreation Trail

The North Shore National Recreation Trail is a 5-mile-long trail that meanders through the serene forests and streams along the northern shore of Lake Arrowhead. It offers some of the most scenic views of the lake and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Lake Arrowhead: Activities and Tours

Lake Arrowhead offers a variety of fun-filled activities and tours to its visitors, ranging from water sports to scenic drives. Here are some of the best things to do in Lake Arrowhead:

Go on a Lake Cruise

Take a leisurely cruise on the Lake Arrowhead Queen, a charming paddleboat that offers tours of the lake’s spectacular scenery.

Go Skiing or Snowboarding

During the winter months, head to Ski Resort Lake Arrowhead to experience world-class skiing and snowboarding adventures among magnificent mountains.

Indulge in Water Sports

The Lake Arrowhead Marina offers a variety of water sports, including boating, fishing, swimming, kayaking, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Arrowhead

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a fun-filled week in Lake Arrowhead:

Day 1

Visit the Lake Arrowhead Village and enjoy its many attractions, including shopping, dining, and entertainment.

Day 2

Go on a lake cruise and have a relaxing day on the water, soaking up the scenery.

Day 3

Discover the history of the San Bernardino Mountains and the surrounding areas at the Rim of the World Historical Society Museum.

Day 4

Take a guided tour of Wildhaven Ranch and get up close and personal with the local wildlife.

Day 5

Explore the Lake Gregory Trail Network and enjoy a day of hiking or biking in the great outdoors.

Day 6

Take a scenic drive along Rim of the World Drive and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Day 7

Spend your last day exploring the nearby towns of Big Bear and Crestline and discover their many unique attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Arrowhead

The food and drink scene of Lake Arrowhead is abundant with fresh local cuisine and unique beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks of the region:

Must-Try Dishes

Lake Arrowhead Trout

Try a fresh batch of Lake Arrowhead trout, which is cooked to perfection and served with mouth-watering sides.

Mountain Berry Pie

Grab a slice of a mouth-watering mountain berry pie, a regionally-famous dish that will leave your taste buds craving more.

Local Beverages

Arrowhead Purified Drinking Water

The crystal-clear water of Lake Arrowhead is known for its pristine taste and refreshing quality. Get a taste of the region’s best drinking water in a bottle at the local grocery stores.

Where to Stay in Lake Arrowhead: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lake Arrowhead offers a variety of accommodation options, catering to all types of travelers. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a budget-friendly hostel, you’ll find something that suits your needs. Here are some of the top picks:

Lake Arrowhead Resort and Spa

The Lake Arrowhead Resort and Spa is a luxurious hotel that offers a wide range of amenities, including a full-service spa, a private beach, and an on-site restaurant.

Saddleback Inn

The Saddleback Inn is a charming guesthouse that offers cozy rooms, gourmet breakfast, and a communal hot tub.

Arrowhead Pine Rose Cabins

The Arrowhead Pine Rose Cabins offer a rustic and nature-themed stay that is perfect for couples or families. Enjoy a cozy night in front of the fireplace in one of their beautiful cabins.

What to Buy in Lake Arrowhead: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring back some souvenirs or local products to commemorate your trip to Lake Arrowhead. Here are some of the best options:

Pinecone Crafts

Lake Arrowhead is famous for its handmade crafts made of pinecones. Get a pinecone birdhouse or picture frame as a reminder of your trip.

Locally-grown Apples

The crisp and delicious apples grown in the nearby orchards of Oak Glen are a must-try when visiting Lake Arrowhead. Pack a few to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Arrowhead

The local culture and customs of Lake Arrowhead are heavily influenced by its natural beauty and proximity to the San Bernardino Mountains. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Nature Conservation

Lake Arrowhead is committed to protecting its natural resources, and visitors are expected to do the same. Follow the “leave no trace” policy and pack out what you pack in.

Respect Wildlife

Wildlife is abundant in Lake Arrowhead, and visitors are expected to respect their space and keep a safe distance.

Best Time to Visit Lake Arrowhead

Lake Arrowhead is an all-year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. The town has something to offer in every season, whether it is for water activities, winter sports, or hiking and biking. However, the busiest time of the year is during the summer months of June to August. If you are looking for a more peaceful trip, choose the spring or fall seasons when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Arrowhead

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Lake Arrowhead:

Do:

  • Explore the local hiking and biking trails
  • Respect the local wildlife and natural surroundings
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Approach the local wildlife too closely
  • Ignore the traffic laws and rules

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Arrowhead

Lake Arrowhead is a relatively safe destination, but it is still essential to take some precautions when travelling, especially when hiking or biking in the mountains:

  • Follow the marked trails and warning signs
  • Carry plenty of water and snacks for hiking and bike trails
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent

Health in Lake Arrowhead

Lake Arrowhead is relatively remote, so it is essential to be prepared before visiting. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Carry basic medications and a first-aid kit
  • Stay hydrated and avoid alcohol on hot days
  • Watch out for altitude sickness if you are hiking or biking in the mountains

Getting to Lake Arrowhead: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to consider when travelling to Lake Arrowhead:

Airports Near Lake Arrowhead

The nearest airports to Lake Arrowhead are the Ontario International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Lake Arrowhead

The San Bernardino Greyhound Station or the San Bernardino Transit Center are the closest bus stations to Lake Arrowhead. From there, you’ll need to take a taxi or rideshare service to get to your destination.

Driving to Lake Arrowhead

Lake Arrowhead is accessible by car, and driving to the town will provide some stunning views along the way.

How to Get Around in Lake Arrowhead

The best way to get around Lake Arrowhead is by car or bike, especially if you plan to explore the nearby hiking and biking trails. You can also take a taxi or rideshare service to get around. Public transportation options may be limited, and it’s best to check with your hotel for any shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Lake Arrowhead: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lake Arrowhead is close to several other popular destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some of the nearby towns you can visit:

Big Bear Lake

Big Bear Lake is a charming mountain town that offers a variety of outdoor activities, including skiing, snowboarding, and water sports.

Crestline

Crestline is a peaceful mountain town that boasts stunning views of the nearby mountains and forests. It’s a great place to visit when looking for a tranquil escape.

Conclusion

Lake Arrowhead is a beautiful destination that has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to explore the nearby hiking and biking trails, relax on the lake, or discover the history and culture of the area, Lake Arrowhead promises to provide an unforgettable experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now equipped to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this stunning destination.
