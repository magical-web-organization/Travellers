Introduction to Upland

Nestled in the heart of San Bernardino County, Upland is a charming city that boasts a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. From historical landmarks to scenic views, hiking trails, and delicious cuisine, Upland has something for everyone.

Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, foodie, or adventure seeker, there’s no shortage of things to do and explore in this hidden gem of California. Our comprehensive guide will help you plan your perfect Upland itinerary, from must-see attractions to hidden gems and local hotspots.

List of Places to Visit in Upland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cooper Regional History Museum

Located in the preserved and restored historic home of the Cooper family, the Cooper Regional History Museum is a must-visit for history lovers. The museum houses artifacts, photographs, and exhibits from the past 200 years of Upland’s history.

Madonna of the Trail

One of a dozen statues created in the 1920s by the National Society Daughters of the American Revolution, the Madonna of the Trail monument commemorates the pioneer spirit and bravery of early American settlers.

Cable Airport

Built in 1945, Cable Airport is the oldest operating airport in San Bernardino County. Visitors can take a tour of the airport and learn about its history at the Cable Airport Foundation Museum.

Natural Attractions of Upland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Baldy

Standing tall at 10,064 feet, Mount Baldy is the highest peak in the San Gabriel Mountains and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike, bike or take the ski lift to the top and witness the breathtaking beauty that surrounds them.

San Antonio Heights Trailhead

This scenic trailhead offers visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Upland’s foothills and experience its unique flora and fauna. Hikers can take a 3.5-mile trail to the summit and enjoy panoramic views of the city below.

Euclid Avenue

A picturesque street lined with sycamore trees, Euclid Avenue is a favorite spot among locals for jogging, running, and biking. The street offers breathtaking views of the San Bernardino Mountains and is a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of Upland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upland

The Loop Trail

The Loop Trail is a scenic 2.5-mile hike that encircles the Upland Hills Country Club. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is a great place for hiking, trail running, and mountain biking.

The Sycamore Canyon Trail

The Sycamore Canyon Trail is a 3.4-mile trail that parallels the 210 Freeway. The trail is a great place for hiking, biking, running, and offers a glimpse into Upland’s unique flora and fauna.

The San Antonio Heights Loop

A 10-mile loop that takes hikers through Upland’s foothills, the San Antonio Heights Loop offers breathtaking views of the San Bernardino Mountains. The trail is challenging and takes about four hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Upland: Activities and Tours

Explore Historic Downtown Upland

A walk through historic downtown Upland offers visitors an opportunity to experience the city’s unique architecture, quaint shops, and historic landmarks. The area is a great place for photo ops, shopping and dining.

Visit the Upland Lemon Festival

The Upland Lemon Festival is an annual event that celebrates the city’s citrus heritage with food, music, and carnival rides. The festival is held every April and is a great place for families to enjoy a day out.

Wine Tasting at San Antonio Winery

Founded in 1917, the San Antonio Winery is the oldest winery in Los Angeles and offers visitors an opportunity to sample local wines and learn about the wine-making process.

Food and Drink Scene of Upland

Must-Try Dishes

Garlic Noodles

A local favorite, garlic noodles are a must-try in Upland. This fragrant dish is made with thin rice noodles, garlic, and vegetables, served with chopped peanuts and a wedge of lime.

Pork Belly Tacos

A popular taco shop staple, pork belly tacos are a delicious blend of juicy pork belly, fresh vegetables, and tangy salsa, served on a warm tortilla.

Local Beverages

Citrus Cocktails

Upland’s citrus heritage is reflected in its local signature cocktails such as the Lemonade Martini and Grapefruit Margarita.

Craft Beer

Upland boasts a thriving craft beer scene, with local brewers such as Last Name Brewing and Hamilton Family Brewery offering a variety of unique brews.

Where to Stay in Upland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guesthouse at Historic Euclid

Located in the heart of historic downtown Upland, the Guesthouse at Historic Euclid is a charming bed and breakfast that offers a cozy and quiet retreat for visitors.

DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Claremont

Located in nearby Claremont, the DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Claremont offers a comfortable and convenient base for visitors to Upland.

What to Buy in Upland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Citrus Products

Upland’s citrus heritage is reflected in its local products, such as citrus-scented candles, jams, and syrups.

Local Arts and Crafts

Upland’s thriving arts community produces a variety of unique and locally made items such as pottery, paintings, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Upland

Upland’s culture is heavily influenced by its citrus heritage and pioneer spirit. Visitors should expect to encounter friendly locals who take pride in their city’s unique history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Upland

The best time to visit Upland is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers in Upland can be hot and dry, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upland

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore Upland’s natural parks and scenic trails.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Disrespect local landmarks and historical sites.
  • Ignore safety guidelines while exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Upland

Upland is a safe city, but visitors should exercise caution when exploring its natural parks and trails. It’s vital to stay on designated paths and bring plenty of water and sunscreen.

Health in Upland

Upland has several hospitals and urgent care centers that cater to the needs of visitors and locals alike. Visitors should ensure they have the appropriate medical insurance and carry a list of emergency contacts with them at all times.

Getting to Upland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Upland

Ontario International Airport is the closest airport to Upland, located just 6 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Upland

The closest bus station to Upland is the Foothill Blvd. & 7th Ave Station, located just a few blocks away from downtown Upland.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi or rideshare service to get around Upland and its neighboring cities.

How to Get Around in Upland

Upland is easily navigable on foot or by bicycle, and there are several bike rental stations located throughout the city. Visitors can also use rideshare services or rent a car to get around.

Where to Go Next from Upland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Claremont

Located just five miles away from Upland, Claremont is a charming city that offers a variety of cultural and outdoor activities for visitors.

Big Bear Lake

Located an hour away from Upland, Big Bear Lake is a popular destination for skiing, snowboarding, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Upland is a hidden gem in the heart of San Bernardino County, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Visitors can explore the city’s history and landmarks, enjoy the great outdoors, and indulge in the delicious cuisine and local products. Plan your next adventure to Upland today and discover the charm and beauty of this California secret.
