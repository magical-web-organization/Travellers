Introduction to Bombay Beach

Bombay Beach is a small town situated in Imperial County in the middle of California’s Colorado Desert. Despite being just three miles long and a half-mile wide, the town is full of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures that attract thousands of visitors. Bombay Beach is also home to one of the largest and oldest man-made saltwater lakes in the world, the Salton Sea.

Local Culture and Customs of Bombay Beach

Even though Bombay Beach is a small town; it is a melting pot of different cultures and traditions. Visitors are welcomed in the town and are expected to respect the locals and their customs.

Best Time to Visit Bombay Beach

The best time to visit Bombay Beach is between October and May when the weather is cooler and the humidity is lower. In summers, temperatures can rise up to 120°F, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bombay Beach

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs
  • Bring sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses for protection from the sun
  • Stay hydrated
  • Carry cash as most places do not accept cards

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Go off-trail while hiking or biking
  • Enter private property without permission
