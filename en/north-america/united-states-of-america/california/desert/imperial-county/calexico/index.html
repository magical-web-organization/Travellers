Calexico is a small desert town located in Imperial County, California, in the United States of America. With a population of around 40,000, it is a lesser-known tourist destination that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, Calexico has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you explore the town’s hidden gems and plan an unforgettable trip to Calexico.

Introduction to Calexico

Calexico is located on the US-Mexico border, separated from its sister city (Mexicali) by a wall and a river. The town’s cultural heritage is a fascinating blend of American and Mexican cultures, which is evident from its architecture, cuisine, and festivals. The majority of the population speaks both Spanish and English, offering visitors an opportunity to immerse themselves in bilingual culture.

The town’s climate is hot and dry, with average temperatures ranging from 40°C in summer to 20°C in winter. The best time to visit Calexico is between November and February when the temperatures are milder, and the desert landscape is at its most beautiful.

List of Places to Visit in Calexico

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calexico has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit attractions for history buffs:

  • Calexico Carnegie Library: Built-in 1905, this library was designed by William H. Weeks and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Carmen Durazo Cultural Arts Center: Named after a local educator and activist, this historic building hosts cultural events and workshops.
  • Centinela State Prison: This prison, built in the late 1940s, was used to house Japanese Americans during World War II and is now a state prison.
  • Imperial Valley Pioneers Museum: Located in nearby Imperial, this museum features exhibits on the region’s history, including farming, transportation, and mining.

Natural Attractions of Calexico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calexico’s desert landscape has plenty to offer the outdoor enthusiast. Here are some of the natural attractions that you should add to your itinerary:

  • Cibola National Wildlife Refuge: Located just north of the town, this refuge is home to over 275 bird species and features hiking trails, fishing, and boating.
  • Picacho State Recreation Area: This state park is located north of the town and offers opportunities for hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Salvation Mountain: Created by artist Leonard Knight, this brightly painted hillside is located near the town of Niland and is a popular attraction for tourists.
  • Imperial Sand Dunes: These sand dunes, located west of the town, offer a unique desert experience and are a popular destination for off-road vehicle enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calexico

Calexico has a variety of hiking trails and biking routes that offer visitors an opportunity to explore the town’s natural beauty. Here are some top picks:

  • Mount Signal Trail: This hike offers stunning views of the Imperial Valley and the Sierra Mountains and is a moderate 4-mile round trip.
  • Carrera del Gato Trail: This scenic, 7-mile hike starts at the base of Mount Signal and offers stunning views of the US-Mexico border.
  • Fish Creek Wash Trail: This easy 2.5-mile hike is suitable for all skill levels and offers views of the towering sand dunes.

Best Things to Do in Calexico: Activities and Tours

Here are some top things to do in Calexico:

  • Explore the Downtown Area: The downtown area of Calexico offers a unique blend of American and Mexican cultures. You can explore the area on foot and admire the colorful murals and historic buildings.
  • Attend the Mariachi Festival: This annual festival, held every March, features live mariachi music, food, and dance.
  • Visit the Border Fence: The US-Mexico border fence, located along the Colorado River, offers a unique opportunity to witness the geopolitical situation between the two countries.

Food and Drink Scene of Calexico

Calexico’s food scene is a blend of American and Mexican cuisines, offering visitors a unique culinary experience. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Carne Asada Fries: This dish features crispy French fries topped with grilled beef, avocado, cheese, pico de gallo, and sour cream.
  • Tacos al Pastor: These tacos are made with marinated pork that’s been cooked on a spit and served with pineapple, onions, and cilantro.
  • Menudo: A hearty soup made with beef tripe, hominy, and a spicy chili broth.

As for local beverages, try the cerveza, tequila, and margaritas, which are popular in the area.

Where to Stay in Calexico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calexico has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Calexico: This hotel offers a complimentary breakfast, a fitness center, and a heated outdoor pool.
  • Border Motel: This budget-friendly motel is located in downtown Calexico and offers free Wi-Fi and parking.
  • Motel 6 El Centro: Located in nearby El Centro, this motel offers free Wi-Fi and parking and is pet-friendly.

What to Buy in Calexico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring back home, consider buying:

  • Mexican Blanket: These colorful blankets are a popular souvenir and can be used as a throw or a picnic blanket.
  • Handmade Pottery: Local artisans in Calexico create beautiful, handcrafted pottery that makes a perfect souvenir.
  • Mexican Candy: Calexico has a variety of Mexican candy stores that offer a unique blend of sweet and spicy flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Calexico

Calexico’s culture is a unique blend of American and Mexican influences, which is reflected in its architecture, festivals, and cuisine. The town’s residents are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the bilingual culture.

Best Time to Visit Calexico

The best time to visit Calexico is between November and February when the temperatures are milder, and the desert landscape is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calexico

Do:

  • Do embrace the bilingual culture and try to speak some Spanish.
  • Do carry plenty of sunscreen and water when exploring the desert.
  • Do be respectful of the town’s cultural heritage and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t cross the US-Mexico border illegally or attempt to smuggle illegal goods.
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car when parked in public areas.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environments when exploring the town’s outdoor attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calexico

Calexico is a safe community, but visitors to the town should exercise the usual safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows and not leaving valuables in public places.

Health in Calexico

Calexico has a good network of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical insurance before arriving in the town.

Getting to Calexico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calexico

The nearest airports to Calexico are:

  • Imperial County Airport (IPL)
  • Yuma International Airport (YUM)
  • San Diego International Airport (SAN)

Bus Stations Near Calexico

Greyhound and Tufesa both have bus stations in Calexico.

Other Options

Calexico is easily accessible by car via the Interstate 8.

How to Get Around in Calexico

The best way to get around Calexico is by car. Visitors can rent cars from various providers at the airports or in town.

Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Calexico, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Anza-Borrego Desert State Park: This state park, located about an hour and a half drive from Calexico, offers hiking, stargazing, and wildflower viewing.
  • Joshua Tree National Park: This national park, located about two hours’ drive from Calexico, offers hiking, rock climbing, and stargazing.
  • San Diego: Located about two hours’ drive from Calexico, San Diego offers a variety of attractions, including beaches, museums, and theme parks.

Conclusion
