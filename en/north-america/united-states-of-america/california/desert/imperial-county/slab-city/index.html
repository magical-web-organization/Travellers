Introduction to Slab City

Slab City is a unique desert community located in Imperial County, California, USA. Also known as “The Slabs,” it has become a famous tourist destination as it is one of the last free-living places in the United States. Slab City is an abandoned military base that turned into a home for squatters, artists, and those who want to live off the grid. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture of Slab City in California, USA.

List of Places to Visit in Slab City

If you are planning to visit Slab City, here are the top places you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Salvation Mountain: A colorful and vibrant mountain made of adobe, straw, and thousands of gallons of paint. It was built by Leonard Knight, a renowned artist who wanted to spread the message of love and faith.
  • East Jesus: An experimental art museum where artists showcase their work using recycled materials. You can see unique sculptures, installations, and interactive pieces throughout the museum.
  • Range Market: A flea market where locals sell their handmade crafts and goods. You can find everything from vintage clothing to handmade jewelry.

Natural Attractions of Slab City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Slab City Hot Springs: Relax in the natural hot springs surrounded by desert landscapes.
  • The Tank: A popular hangout spot for locals, The Tank is a large concrete structure where visitors can climb to the top and enjoy panoramic views of the desert.
  • Salton Sea: A large lake formed by accident in the early 1900s, the Salton Sea offers a unique natural landscape with a diverse range of bird species, lakeside beaches, and campsites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Slab City

If you are an outdoor enthusiast, you will enjoy the hiking and biking routes in Slab City. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Slab City Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that takes you around the outskirts of the city, offering scenic views of the nearby mountain ranges.
  • Badlands Trail: A 3-mile trail that takes you through the stunning badlands at the edge of the city.
  • Painted Canyon Trail: A 4.3-mile trail that takes you to a unique geological landscape with multicolored rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Slab City: Activities and Tours

  • ATV Tour: Explore the desert landscape on an ATV tour with experienced guides.
  • Star Gazing Tour: Witness the beautiful night sky in the desert, with the naked eye or with the help of telescopes and knowledgeable guides.
  • Off-Roading: If you have your own off-road vehicle, you can take it out and explore the untouched desert lands surrounding Slab City.

Week-Long Itinerary for Slab City

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Slab City:

Day 1 Arrive in Slab City, check-in to your hotel or campsite
Day 2 Visit Salvation Mountain and East Jesus
Day 3 Enjoy hiking, ATV tour or off-roading
Day 4 Relax at Slab City Hot Springs and the Salton Sea
Day 5 Visit the Range Market, explore the local crafts and goods
Day 6 Experience star gazing tour
Day 7 Depart from Slab City

Food and Drink Scene of Slab City

If you are a foodie, you will find many local dishes and beverages to try in Slab City.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Date shakes: Made with locally grown dates, this sweet and refreshing drink is a must-try in Slab City.
  • BBQ Ribs: Experience the authentic taste of BBQ ribs made by locals using their secret recipes.
  • Huevos Rancheros: A traditional Mexican breakfast dish, Huevos Rancheros is a delicious combination of eggs, beans, salsa, and tortillas.

Local Beverages

  • Local beers: Imperial County has several microbreweries that produce top-rated craft beers. Don’t miss out each one has its unique flavor and character.
  • Agua Fresca: A refreshing fruit-based drink that is perfect for the hot desert weather.

Where to Stay in Slab City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Slab City offers several accommodation options for travelers. From camping to hotels, there is something for every budget and preference. Some of the popular options include:

  • Slab City Hostel: A unique hostel that offers affordable stays in repurposed structures and trailers.
  • Desert View RV Park: A popular camping site near the Salton Sea that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Red Earth Casino: If you prefer a hotel stay, Red Earth Casino is a nearby option that offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Slab City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home some local products or souvenirs, here are some of the items you can consider:

  • Artwork: Several local artists display and sell their work in Slab City. You can find unique paintings, sculptures, and installations in the East Jesus Museum and The Range Market.
  • Date products: Imperial County is known for its dates. You can bring home locally made date jams, syrups, or date shakes.
  • Handmade crafts: The Range Market has several stalls where locals sell handmade jewelry, clothing, and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Slab City

Slab City is a unique place with a distinct culture and customs. Here are some things you should know:

  • Slab City is a free-living community, and there are minimal rules and regulations.
  • Slab City residents value their privacy and often prefer to be left alone. Respect their boundaries and do not take pictures of people or their living spaces without permission.
  • Most of the locals rely on solar power for their energy needs, so it is important to conserve energy and resources during your stay.

Best Time to Visit Slab City

The best time to visit Slab City is between November and February when the temperatures are mild and enjoyable. In the summer months, temperatures can reach over 100 degrees Fahrenheit, making it uncomfortable to spend time outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Slab City

Do:

  • Respect the privacy and living spaces of Slab City residents
  • Learn about the local culture and customs
  • Conserve energy and resources

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people or their living spaces without permission
  • Bring your pet without permission
  • Leave trash or waste behind

Safety and Health

Safety in Slab City

Slab City is a safe place to visit, but it is essential to follow basic safety precautions.

  • Do not wander off alone in the desert; it is easy to get lost.
  • Keep valuables and cash securely and out of sight.
  • Have a basic understanding of desert survival if you are going on a hike or outdoor trip.

Health in Slab City

Slab City has few basic amenities like running water and electricity. It would be helpful to bring your own water and food provisions, as well as other necessities like sunscreen, insect repellent, and first aid kits.

Getting to Slab City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Slab City

The closest airport to Slab City is Yuma International Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away. Other airports include Palm Springs International Airport and San Diego International Airport, which are approximately 130 miles and 150 miles away, respectively.

Bus Stations Near Slab City

There are several bus stations near Slab City, including Greyhound and Flixbus. The closest bus station is in El Centro, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Other Options

If you prefer driving, you can rent a car at any of the major airports or cities nearby.

How to Get Around in Slab City

The best way to get around Slab City is by car. You can rent a car or bring your vehicle. Be sure to bring a GPS or a map as the city does not have formal roads or street signs.

Where to Go Next from Slab City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore nearby destinations from Slab City, here are some options you may consider:

  • Joshua Tree National Park: Known for its unique geology and stunning landscapes, Joshua Tree National Park is just a few hours away from Slab City.
  • Anza-Borrego Desert State Park: Approximately 90 miles east of Slab City is the Anza-Borrego Desert State Park, known for its unique wildflower blooms and hiking trails.
  • Palm Springs: A popular destination in California, Palm Springs is a few hours away from Slab City and offers stunning desert landscapes, luxury spas, and world-class golf courses.

Conclusion

Slab City is an extraordinary destination that offers a unique experience to visitors. From its colorful landmarks to stunning natural landscapes, there is something for everyone in Slab City. Be respectful of the local culture and customs, conserve resources, and enjoy your stay in this free-living desert community.
