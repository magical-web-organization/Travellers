Introduction to Desert

Nestled in the southeastern part of California, Desert is a stunning city that truly lives up to its name with its dry and arid climate. This desert oasis is a popular vacation destination that boasts a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural attractions, and thrilling outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a nature lover or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in Desert.

List of Places to Visit in Desert

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palm Springs Art Museum - If you’re a fan of art, then a visit to the Palm Springs Art Museum should be on your itinerary. This museum has an impressive collection of contemporary and modern art, as well as exhibitions and special events throughout the year.
  • Sunnylands Center & Gardens - Once the winter home of the late billionaire Walter Annenberg and his wife, Sunnylands Center & Gardens features a stunning mid-century modern mansion and 9-acres of beautifully landscaped gardens. Tour the estate, learn about the Annenberg family, and take a walk through the gardens to see the beauty of the desert oasis.
  • Cabot’s Pueblo Museum - This unique museum showcases the life and accomplishments of Cabot Yerxa, a homesteader who built his pueblo-style house and museum from reclaimed materials. Tour the museum and experience the history of the Coachella Valley.

Natural Attractions of Desert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Joshua Tree National Park - Known for its iconic rock formations and unique trees, Joshua Tree National Park is a true natural wonder. Take a hike, go rock climbing, or simply enjoy the stunning views.
  • San Jacinto Mountains - Ride the Palm Springs Aerial Tramway and enjoy a panoramic view of the Coachella Valley and San Jacinto Mountains. When you reach the top, you’ll find a variety of hiking trails that offer scenic views and stunning landscapes.
  • Indian Canyons - For a more leisurely outdoor adventure, head to Indian Canyons for a hike through stunning palm oases, towering rock formations, and beautiful desert landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Desert

  • Andreas Canyon Trail - A 1.5-mile hike through Andreas Canyon, this trail is perfect for hikers of all skill levels.
  • Murray Canyon Trail - A 5.6-mile hike through the Santa Rosa Mountains, this trail offers stunning views and a unique mix of natural landscapes.
  • La Quinta Cove Hiking Trail - An easy, flat 2.7-mile trail that offers stunning views of the mountains and a glimpse into the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Desert: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Desert by Jeep - Take a guided tour of the desert in a 4x4 jeep and experience the rugged terrain and stunning vistas.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - Get a bird’s eye view of the desert with a hot air balloon ride. See the sunrise from above and enjoy a champagne toast after your flight.
  • Visit a Date Farm - Desert is home to many date farms. Take a tour, taste some dates, and learn about this unique desert fruit.

Food and Drink Scene of Desert

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mexican cuisine - Thanks to its close proximity to the Mexican border, the Desert area is a great place to sample authentic Mexican cuisine. Don’t leave without trying the tacos, enchiladas, and chile rellenos.
  • Date Shake - Made with local dates, this sweet and refreshing shake is a must-try when in Desert.
  • Farm-to-Table Fare - Many restaurants in Desert pride themselves on using fresh, locally-sourced ingredients. Be sure to check out some of the farm-to-table options in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Springs Craft Beer Scene - With several breweries in the area, beer lovers won’t be disappointed.
  • California Wine Scene - Take a tour of some of the local wineries and vineyards in the area and taste the flavors of California.

Where to Stay in Desert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Parker Palm Springs - A luxurious hotel that boasts beautiful gardens, two swimming pools, and an on-site spa.
  • Ace Hotel Palm Springs - A mid-century modern hotel with a laid-back vibe and plenty of amenities.
  • Desert Riviera Hotel - A charming boutique hotel that offers a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Desert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Date Products - Shop for local dates, date shakes, and other date products to bring home as a tasty souvenir.
  • Mid-Century Modern Decor - Palm Springs is known for its mid-century modern architecture and design. Browse local shops and markets for unique décor items.

Local Culture and Customs of Desert

Desert is a laid-back city that’s known for its relaxed and casual atmosphere. Respect the local culture and customs by dressing appropriately and being mindful of noise levels in residential areas. Remember to tip your service providers, as this is a common practice in the hospitality industry in the United States.

Best Time to Visit Desert

The best time to visit Desert is from October to April, when temperatures are cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers in the desert can be scorching, with temperatures reaching over 100°F. However, if you don’t mind the heat, visiting in the summer can mean fewer crowds and lower accommodation prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Desert

Do:

  • Do stay hydrated! The dry desert climate can be dehydrating, so be sure to drink plenty of water.
  • Do enjoy the outdoors! The natural beauty of Desert is one of its biggest draws, so take advantage of the many hiking trails and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter! Help keep the desert clean by packing out what you bring in.
  • Don’t underestimate the desert sun. Be sure to wear sunscreen and a hat, and seek shade during the hottest parts of the day.

Safety and Health

Safety in Desert

Desert is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Always be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night. When exploring the outdoors, take extra care in the heat and bring plenty of water and snacks.

Health in Desert

The dry desert climate can be tough on your skin and respiratory system. Be sure to pack moisturizer, lip balm, and any necessary medication. Wear comfortable, breathable clothing and avoid overexerting yourself during outdoor activities.

Getting to Desert: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Desert

  • Palm Springs International Airport - The closest airport to Desert, Palm Springs International Airport offers flights from major cities across the United States.

Bus Stations Near Desert

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in downtown Palm Springs, this bus station offers routes to Los Angeles, Phoenix, and beyond.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Rent a car and explore the desert at your own pace.
  • Rideshare Services - Services like Uber and Lyft are available in the Palm Springs area.

How to Get Around in Desert

The best way to get around Desert is by car. Many of the area’s top attractions and activities require a vehicle to reach. Rent a car at the airport or in town, or consider using rideshare services.

Where to Go Next from Desert: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Los Angeles, California - Just a few hours west of Desert, Los Angeles is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring California.
  • San Diego, California - Head south to San Diego for beautiful beaches, great restaurants, and a laid-back vibe.
  • Las Vegas, Nevada - If you’re feeling lucky, head to Las Vegas to try your luck at the casinos and stay in one of the city’s many world-class hotels.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural history, and unique attractions, Desert, California is a destination that you won’t want to miss. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Desert has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to the desert!
