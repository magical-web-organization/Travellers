Introduction to Angels Camp

Nestled in the heart of Gold Country in Calaveras County, California, Angels Camp is a quaint city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and unparalleled outdoor recreation opportunities. The city is named after Henry Angel, a shopkeeper who established the first trading post in the area.

Whether you’re looking for a fun-filled weekend getaway or a week-long adventure with your family, Angels Camp has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best places to visit, eat, stay, and things to do in Angels Camp.

List of Places to Visit in Angels Camp

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Angels Camp Museum and Carriage House: Explore the rich history and culture of Angels Camp’s gold rush era as you wander through exhibits featuring vintage carriages, mining equipment and artifacts.
  • Calaveras County Museum: This is a 3-acre complex consisting of two gold rush-era structures and exhibits that depict the life of miners in the 1800s.
  • Mark Twain Tribute: The celebrated author once called Angels Camp the “Jumping Frog Capital of the World”, and this tribute exhibit chronicles Twain’s life and his association with Angels Camp.

Natural Attractions of Angels Camp: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • New Melones Lake: Go kayaking, boating, and enjoy the stunning views at the New Melones Lake.
  • Mercer Caverns: Explore the spectacular limestone caves that date back to the gold rush era at Mercer Caverns.
  • Natural Bridges: Located in nearby Calaveras Big Trees State Park, this natural bridge is a stunning marvel of nature that shouldn’t be missed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Angels Camp

  • Angels Creek Trail: this trail is a 6.5-mile hike that offers scenic views of Angels Creek.
  • Calaveras Big Trees State Park: this park offers visitors an opportunity to hike amidst giant sequoias and other flora and fauna.
  • Rail Trail: This 6.3-mile trail is perfect for cyclers, hikers and horseback riders alike. It offers various stops with picnic areas and restrooms along the way.

Best Things to Do in Angels Camp: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Historic Downtown: Check out the quaint architecture of the era and boutique shops presenting local goods and food.
  • Play a Round of Golf: Greenhorn Creek Golf Course offers an 18-hole course for golf enthusiasts.
  • Taste the Wines: With varietals that rival California’s finest, The Angels Camp area houses numerous wineries and vineyards open for wine tastings and tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Angels Camp

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frog Jumping Jubilee Cake: This cake is a tribute to Twain’s celebrated short story and is available at Lila and Sage Bakery.
  • Angus Rib-Eye Steak: Enjoy perfectly cooked steaks at CAMPS Restaurant.
  • Calaveras Angels Eggs: A must-try dish, available at the Hard Rock Cafe.

Local Beverages

  • Angels Camp Brewing Company: Sample the finest locally brewed craft beer at this microbrewery.
  • Twisted Oak Winery: Situated amidst picturesque vineyards, this winery is known for its award-winning wines

Where to Stay in Angels Camp: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Travelodge by Wyndham Angels Camp: This hotel is conveniently located in the heart of the city, with comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • Best Western Cedar Inn & Suites: features a swimming pool, as well as a hot tub and well-equipped rooms.
  • Jumping Frog Motel: Offering excellent value for money, this recently refurbished motel is located close to all local attractions.

What to Buy in Angels Camp: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Murphys Fine Gold Jewelry: Visit this store to buy locally made fine gold jewelry.
  • Middleton’s of Angels Camp: This store offers a vast collection of locally made artwork, gifts, and decor.
  • Calaveras Olive Oil Company: Grab a bottle of artisanal olive oil’s at this store and impress your loved ones with a fantastic culinary gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Angels Camp

Angels Camp is a city that cherishes its rich history, and respects the iconography and lore inspired by the gold rush era. The town considers the frog-jumping contest as a cult event and hosts the annual Calaveras County Fair and Jumping Frog Jubilee in the third week of May.

Best Time to Visit Angels Camp

The best time to visit Angels Camp is during the late spring, summer, and fall. The weather is mild, and most outdoor attractions, including water activities, are available during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Angels Camp

Do:

  • Respect the town’s cultural and historical significance.
  • Explore the local museums to learn more about the city’s rich history.
  • Engage in outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and water recreation.

Don’t:

  • Litter in heavily visited tourist spots.
  • Build campfires on unauthorized campsites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Angels Camp

Angels Camp is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution and avoid venturing into deserted areas at night. Always practice proper safety precautions such as locking your doors and securing your valuables.

Health in Angels Camp

Visitors to Angels Camp are advised to bring mosquito repellent and wear protective clothing to guard against insect bites. Sunscreen is also recommended, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Angels Camp: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Angels Camp

The closest airport to Angels Camp is the Stockton Metropolitan Airport, which is approximately 63 miles away. Other nearby airports include Sacramento International Airport and San Francisco International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Angels Camp

The Greyhound bus station in Sonora is the closest bus station to Angels Camp and is approximately 4 miles away.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent option for those who prefer controlling their own pace and mobility. Taxis and ride-share services are also available but may not be as readily accessible in surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Angels Camp

Angels Camp is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. Few car rentals and taxi services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Angels Camp: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yosemite National Park: Located approximately 96 miles from Angels Camp, it is one of America’s greatest natural wonders.
  • Sacramento: Located approximately 77 miles away, it is the capital city of California, famous for its historic landmarks and museums.
  • Sonora: Located approximately 4 miles south of Angels Camp, it is another Sierra Foothills town known for its rich history.

Conclusion

Angels Camp is a charming and historical town that’s perfect for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous vacation. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip, explore new experiences, and creates unforgettable memories.
