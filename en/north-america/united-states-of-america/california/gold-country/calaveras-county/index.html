Introduction to Calaveras County

Nestled in the heart of California’s Gold Country, Calaveras County is a charming region renowned for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. This hidden gem is home to picturesque towns, gold rush-era landmarks, and breathtaking outdoor spaces that attract visitors from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Calaveras County

  • Angels Camp: A historic town famous for its connection to Mark Twain’s “The Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County”
  • Ironstone Vineyards: A must-visit for wine lovers, with beautiful grounds and award-winning wines
  • Calaveras Big Trees State Park: Home to ancient giant sequoias and scenic hiking trails
  • Columbia State Historic Park: Step back in time and experience a Gold Rush-era town
  • Mokelumne River: Ideal for kayaking, fishing, and camping
  • Murphys: A charming and lively town known for its wine tasting rooms and outdoor recreation opportunities
  • Moaning Cavern Adventure Park: Explore the depths of this massive cavern that’s said to “moan” with the sound of underground water

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calaveras County is steeped in history and culture, and visitors can explore a range of landmarks and museums that showcase the region’s fascinating heritage. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Calaveras County include:

  • Calaveras County Museum Complex: Located in San Andreas, this museum offers a glimpse into the area’s fascinating history and cultural heritage, featuring exhibits on the Gold Rush, logging, and more.
  • Tuolumne County Museum and History Center: This museum in nearby Sonora offers a comprehensive look into the culture and history of the Sierra Nevada region, with interactive exhibits and art displays.

Natural Attractions of Calaveras County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calaveras County is home to diverse landscapes, from rolling hills to towering mountains, lush forests to sparkling lakes and rivers. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in Calaveras County:

  • Calaveras Big Trees State Park: This beautiful park is home to two groves of towering giant sequoias, as well as diverse flora and fauna, and several hiking trails.
  • Stanislaus National Forest: Stretching across several counties, this vast forest offers breathtaking views, diverse wildlife, and a wide range of outdoor activities.
  • New Melones Lake: A popular destination for fishing, swimming, and boating, this lake also offers scenic picnic areas and hiking trails.
  • Natural Bridges: A fascinating geological formation, this natural bridge over Coyote Creek is a must-see for nature lovers.
  • Yosemite National Park: While not located in Calaveras County, the neighboring Yosemite National Park is a world-famous destination, and visitors to Calaveras County should consider taking a day trip to explore this stunning natural wonder.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calaveras County

Calaveras County is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and paths that wind through its natural landscapes. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Calaveras County:

  • Annie’s Creek Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike through the stunning Stanislaus National Forest
  • Arnold Rim Trail: A scenic 10-mile trail that boasts stunning views of the surrounding Sierra Nevada Mountains.
  • Bear Valley to Lake Alpine Trail: This 8-mile trail offers spectacular views of the region’s alpine lakes and towering mountains.
  • Calaveras Big Trees State Park: With over 6,000 acres of natural beauty, this park offers numerous hiking trails for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Calaveras County: Activities and Tours

Calaveras County is full of exciting activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests, from wine tasting to historical tours and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best things to do in Calaveras County:

  • Tour of the area’s wineries: Calaveras County is home to numerous renowned wineries, and visitors can enjoy guided tours and tasting sessions at Ironstone Vineyards and other top wineries.
  • Gold Rush history tours: History buffs will love exploring the area’s rich Gold Rush heritage, with tours of historic sites like Columbia State Historic Park and Angels Camp Museum.
  • Fly fishing: Calaveras County has numerous rivers and lakes that are ideal for fly fishing, with experienced guides available for hire.
  • Zip-lining tours: Thrill-seekers can soar above the treetops on a zip-lining tour of Moaning Cavern Adventure Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calaveras County

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Calaveras County, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1: Explore the historic town of Angels Camp, with a visit to the Angels Camp Museum and a stroll along Main Street

Day 2: Discover the beauty of Calaveras Big Trees State Park, with a hike through the giant sequoias and picnic lunch in the park

Day 3: Take a wine-tasting tour of the area’s top wineries, including Ironstone Vineyards and Murphys Wine Tasting rooms

Day 4: Go on a guided fly-fishing tour on Mokelumne River

Day 5: Explore the charming town of Murphys, with a walking tour of historic downtown and a visit to local artisan shops and galleries

Day 6: Go on a thrilling zip-lining tour of Moaning Cavern Adventure Park

Day 7: Take a day trip to Yosemite National Park to witness the majestic beauty of this natural wonder.

Food and Drink Scene of Calaveras County

Calaveras County is renowned for its delicious local cuisine and world-class wines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calaveras Frog Jumping Jubilee Fudge: This locally-made fudge is a true Calaveras County classic, named after the annual Frog Jumping Jubilee held in Angels Camp.
  • Greenhorn Creek’s Jackass Soup: This hearty soup is made with pork and beans and other savory ingredients, and is a popular dish at the Greenhorn Creek Resort
  • Gold Country Roasted Garlic Goat Cheese: This artisanal cheese made in the region’s creameries is a true gourmet treat.

Local Beverages

  • Ironstone Vineyards Wine: This award-winning winery offers a range of varietals, including Cabernet Sauvignon and Chardonnay.
  • Calaveras Brewing Company Beer: This popular microbrewery produces a range of beers, including their signature “Cali Gold” ale.
  • What Cheer Saloon Whiskey: As the oldest continuously operating saloon in California, What Cheer Saloon offers a range of premium whiskeys.

Where to Stay in Calaveras County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calaveras County offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels and B&Bs to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the top places to stay in Calaveras County include:

  • Murphys Hotel: This historic hotel in Murphys offers a charming atmosphere and comfortable rooms, with easy access to the town’s attractions.
  • Cedar Creek Lodge: Located in the scenic town of Arnold, this lodge offers cozy cabins and beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Gold Lodge: This budget-friendly motel in Angels Camp is ideal for travelers on a tight budget, with rooms that are clean, comfortable, and well-equipped.

What to Buy in Calaveras County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Calaveras County can bring home a range of unique and locally-made souvenirs and products that capture the region’s charm and character. Here are some of the must-buy items in Calaveras County:

  • Gold Rush-era souvenirs and relics: Visitors can purchase historic items like old mining equipment and Gold Rush-era souvenirs at shops in Columbia State Historic Park.
  • Artisanal cheeses and wines: Calaveras County is famous for its artisanal cheeses and world-class wines, available at local creameries and wineries.
  • Handmade arts and crafts: Local artisans and crafters offer a range of handmade products, from pottery and jewelry to paintings and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Calaveras County

Calaveras County has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its events, festivals, and traditions. Visitors can experience the region’s local culture and customs through events like the Calaveras County Fair and Jumping Frog Jubilee, held annually in May.

Best Time to Visit Calaveras County

The best time to visit Calaveras County is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the foliage is at its most beautiful. Summer can be crowded and hot, while winter can be cold and snowy, making some outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calaveras County

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Calaveras County:

Do:

  • Do plan to spend plenty of time outdoors, exploring the area’s scenic hiking trails, rivers, and lakes.
  • Do try the local wines and cheeses, which are renowned for their quality and flavor.
  • Do visit the area’s historic towns and sites, which offer a fascinating glimpse into the region’s Gold Rush past.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore safety precautions when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, as weather conditions and terrain can be challenging.
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing for varied weather conditions, such as layers for cool evenings and sun protection for daytime outings.
  • Don’t miss the chance to experience the area’s unique culture and heritage, from local festivals to artisanal products.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when visiting Calaveras County:

Safety in Calaveras County

  • Be aware of weather conditions, especially during summer months, and avoid outdoor activities during extreme heat.
  • Be mindful of wildlife such as bears and mountain lions when hiking or camping, and be prepared to safely store food and trash to avoid attracting wild animals.
  • Use caution when driving on back roads, which can be steep and winding.

Health in Calaveras County

  • Stay hydrated during outdoor activities, especially during hot weather.
  • Use sun protection to avoid sunburn and skin damage.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of altitude sickness when at higher elevations.

Getting to Calaveras County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calaveras County is easily accessible by car, with several major highways connecting the region to nearby cities and airports. Visitors can also take advantage of shuttle services and public transportation options in the area.

  • Sacramento International Airport: Located approximately 1.5 hours from Calaveras County, this airport offers flights from destinations around the world.
  • Oakland International Airport: Located approximately 2 hours from Calaveras County, this airport offers flights from destinations in the US and abroad.
  • San Francisco International Airport: Located approximately 2.5 hours from Calaveras County, this airport offers flights from destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near Calaveras County

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This national bus service offers stops in nearby towns like Sonora and Jamestown.
  • Amtrak: This popular train service offers easy access to nearby cities like Sacramento and San Francisco.

Other Options

  • Rental car services: Several car rental companies operate in nearby cities and airports, making it easy for visitors to explore the region on their own.
  • Shuttle services: Several shuttle companies offer transportation from airports and other major cities to Calaveras County.

How to Get Around in Calaveras County

Calaveras County is easily navigable by car or bicycle, with numerous scenic roads and bike trails that wind through its beautiful landscapes. Visitors can also take advantage of public transportation options in the area, including shuttle services and local buses.

Where to Go Next from Calaveras County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Calaveras County, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • San Francisco: Just a few hours’ drive away, the vibrant city of San Francisco offers world-class attractions, dining, and cultural experiences.
