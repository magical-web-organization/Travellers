Introduction to El Dorado Hills

El Dorado Hills is a beautiful and thriving community located in El Dorado County in Gold Country, California. The town offers a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor recreation activities, and historical landmarks, making it an ideal destination for visitors looking to experience the best of California.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Dorado Hills

El Dorado Hills is perfect for those who love the great outdoors. The area is home to several beautiful trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding natural beauty. Some popular hiking/biking routes in the area include:

  • Salmon Falls Trail
  • New York Creek Trail
  • Darrington Trail
  • South Fork American River Trail
  • Granite Bay to Beal’s Point Trail

Best Things to Do in El Dorado Hills: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting at local wineries such as Boeger Winery and Crystal Basin Cellars
  • Visiting the Apple Hill area in the fall for pumpkin patches and apple picking
  • Taking a hot air balloon ride over the Sierra Nevada Mountains
  • Horseback riding at the Green Valley Ranch
  • Golfing at the Serrano Country Club
  • Kayaking at Folsom Lake
  • River rafting on the American River

Week-Long Itinerary for El Dorado Hills

Day 1: Hike the Darrington Trail and enjoy lunch at the top with a scenic view of El Dorado Hills.

Day 2: Visit the El Dorado Hills Town Center and browse the local shops. Have lunch and watch a movie at the local theater.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the Sierra Nevada Mountains in the morning and then visit the Boeger Winery for wine tasting.

Day 4: Explore the historical landmarks at Marshall Gold Discovery State Historic Park and have lunch at Coloma Club.

Day 5: Golf at the Serrano Country Club in the morning and then spend the afternoon kayaking at Folsom Lake.

Day 6: Horseback riding at Green Valley Ranch in the morning and then visit Apple Hill to pick apples and buy local produce.

Day 7: Relax at Lake Natoma and enjoy a picnic by the water with a view of the Sierra Nevada Mountains.

Local Culture and Customs of El Dorado Hills

El Dorado Hills is a laid-back community with friendly locals who cherish nature and outdoor activities. Residents in the area are typically well-educated and enjoy spending time with family and friends. Visitors to El Dorado Hills should respect the local customs by being friendly and courteous towards others and protecting the beautiful natural environment.

Best Time to Visit El Dorado Hills

The best time to visit El Dorado Hills is during the spring and fall. In the spring, the wildflowers are in full bloom, and the weather is perfect for hiking and outdoor activities. In the fall, the Apple Hill area is at its peak for apple picking and pumpkin patches.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Dorado Hills

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment
  • Explore the many hiking and biking trails
  • Try the local produce and wines
  • Respect the local customs
  • Be friendly towards others

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Park in areas marked “No Parking”
  • Disrupt the peace and quiet of local residents
  • Be rude or aggressive towards others

Safety and Health

Safety in El Dorado Hills

El Dorado Hills is a relatively safe community with low crime rates. Visitors to the area should exercise basic safety precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in El Dorado Hills

El Dorado Hills has several medical facilities, including the Mercy Hospital of Folsom and the El Dorado Hills Urgent Care Center.
