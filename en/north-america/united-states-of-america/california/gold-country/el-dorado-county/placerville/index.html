Placerville, located in El Dorado County in the state of California, is a charming town steeped in history and surrounded by natural beauty. Nestled in the foothills of the Sierra Nevada Mountains, Placerville is known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and lush forests. Whether you want to explore the town’s rich cultural heritage, take part in outdoor activities, or simply relax and enjoy the scenery, Placerville has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, places to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Placerville

Placerville, formerly known as “Hangtown,” is the county seat of El Dorado County. The town has a long and colorful history dating back to the gold rush era. Gold was discovered in the area in 1848, leading to a rush of gold seekers and settlers. The town grew rapidly, and by the 1850s, it had become a bustling commercial center.

Today, Placerville is a vibrant town with a unique character. The town has preserved its historic buildings, and visitors can take a step back in time and experience life in the gold rush era. In addition to its fascinating history, Placerville is known for its natural beauty, with hiking and biking trails, waterfalls, and scenic drives.

List of Places to Visit in Placerville

Placerville has a lot to offer visitors, from museums and historic landmarks to natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of some of the best places to visit in Placerville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • El Dorado County Historical Museum: This museum is located in a historic house and features exhibits on the history of El Dorado County, including mining artifacts, Native American artifacts, and more.

  • Gold Bug Park and Mine: This park features a mine that visitors can tour, as well as a museum with exhibits on mining history.

  • Hangman’s Tree: This historic landmark is where criminals were once hanged in the town.

Natural Attractions of Placerville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Apple Hill: This scenic drive takes visitors through apple orchards, pumpkin patches, and Christmas tree farms.

  • South Fork American River: This river is popular for whitewater rafting, kayaking, and fishing.

  • Sly Park Lake: This lake is surrounded by lush forests and has hiking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Placerville

Placerville is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in the town include:

  • Cronan Ranch Regional Trails Park: This park has a network of trails that range from easy to difficult and offer stunning views of the American River.

  • El Dorado Trail: This trail is a multi-use path that stretches from El Dorado to Placerville.

  • Sly Park Lake Trail: This trail is a 3.5-mile loop around the lake and is perfect for hiking and jogging.

Best Things to Do in Placerville: Activities and Tours

Placerville has a variety of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit a Winery: Placerville is home to several wineries, including Lava Cap Winery and Boeger Winery. Visitors can take tours, taste wines, and learn about the winemaking process.

  • Go on a Ghost Tour: Placerville is said to be haunted, and there are several ghost tours available that take visitors to haunted buildings and tell ghost stories.

  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the Sierra Nevada Mountains and enjoy stunning views of the scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Placerville

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Placerville, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in the town:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the El Dorado County Historical Museum and Hangman’s Tree.
Day 2 Hike the Cronan Ranch Regional Trails Park and visit a local winery.
Day 3 Tour Gold Bug Park and Mine and go on a ghost tour.
Day 4 Explore the Apple Hill scenic drive and have a picnic at Sly Park Lake.
Day 5 Go whitewater rafting on the South Fork American River.
Day 6 Take a hot air balloon ride over the Sierra Nevada Mountains.
Day 7 Visit the Placerville Farmers Market and explore the local shops and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Placerville

Placerville is known for its farm-fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and local wines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Placerville:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Pie: Placerville is famous for its apple pies, and there are several bakeries in the town that make delicious pies.

  • Tri-Tip Steak: This type of steak is a local specialty and can be found in many restaurants in Placerville.

  • Farm-Fresh Salad: Placerville is surrounded by farmland, and many restaurants in the town offer farm-fresh salads made with local produce.

Local Beverages

  • Apple Cider: Placerville is known for its apple orchards, and visitors can sample fresh apple cider at several local orchards.

  • Wine: Placerville is home to several wineries, including Lava Cap Winery and Boeger Winery.

Where to Stay in Placerville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Placerville has several options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Placerville:

  • Eden Vale Inn: This bed and breakfast offers upscale accommodations and is located near several wineries.

  • Historic Cary House Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Placerville and offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.

  • Mother Lode Motel: This budget-friendly motel is located on Main Street in Placerville and offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Placerville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Placerville is known for its locally made products and crafts. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Gold Panning Equipment: Placerville is in Gold Country, and visitors can buy gold panning equipment and try their luck panning for gold in the rivers and streams in the area.

  • Handmade Crafts: Placerville has several galleries and shops that sell handcrafted items made by local artisans.

  • Wine: Placerville is home to several wineries, and visitors can bring home a bottle of locally made wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Placerville

Placerville is a small town with a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local culture and customs by being courteous, friendly, and mindful of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Placerville

The best time to visit Placerville is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. Visitors can also enjoy the stunning fall colors during October and November.

Safety and Health

Safety in Placerville

Placerville is a safe town, but visitors are advised to take normal safety precautions and secure their belongings.

Health in Placerville

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude and take precautions to prevent altitude sickness, such as drinking plenty of water and taking it slow when exercising. It’s also important to protect against ticks and other insects when hiking in the woods.

Getting to Placerville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Placerville is located about 44 miles east of Sacramento. Visitors can reach the town by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Placerville

The nearest international airport to Placerville is Sacramento International Airport (SMF), which is about 52 miles from the town.

Bus Stations Near Placerville

The nearest bus station to Placerville is in Sacramento, which has connections to cities all over California.

Other Options

Visitors can rent cars at the Sacramento International Airport and drive to Placerville. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

How to Get Around in Placerville

Placerville is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bikes or cars to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Placerville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Placerville is located in Gold Country, and visitors can take advantage of this to explore nearby destinations and excursions. Some nearby places to consider include:

  • Lake Tahoe: This scenic lake is about an hour’s drive from Placerville and offers hiking, swimming, and skiing.

  • Amador County: This nearby county is known for its wineries and gold rush history.

  • Sacramento: California’s capital has museums, cultural attractions, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Placerville is the perfect destination for travelers who want to explore California’s gold rush history and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing and enjoying the scenery, Placerville has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan an unforgettable trip to the heart of Gold Country.
