Introduction to El Dorado County

El Dorado County is located in Gold Country, California, in the United States of America. The county is known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adrenaline junkie, El Dorado County has something for everyone. In this tour guide, we will take you through all the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and what to do in El Dorado County.

List of Places to Visit in El Dorado County

  • Marshall Gold Discovery State Historic Park
  • El Dorado National Forest
  • Apple Hill
  • Placerville Downtown Historic District
  • South Fork American River

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

El Dorado County is steeped in history, and there are several museums and historical places that are well worth a visit:

  • Marshall Gold Discovery State Historic Park: This park is where gold was first discovered in California in 1848. Visitors can take a guided tour, pan for gold, and learn about the history of the gold rush.
  • El Dorado County Historical Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of El Dorado County and is located in Placerville.
  • Hangtown’s Gold Bug Park and Mine: Visitors can explore the park, mine, and museum and learn about the life of a gold miner in the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of El Dorado County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

El Dorado County is home to several natural attractions that offer outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • El Dorado National Forest: The forest covers over 600,000 acres and offers several hiking trails, fishing spots, and campgrounds.
  • South Fork American River: The river is known for its whitewater rafting, kayaking, and tubing.
  • Lake Tahoe: The lake is located just a short drive from El Dorado County and offers year-round activities such as skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Dorado County

El Dorado County has several hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views:

  • Cronan Ranch Regional Trails Park: The park has over 12 miles of trails that offer scenic views of the American River.
  • Mount Tallac Trail: This trail is located in the Eldorado National Forest and offers panoramic views of Lake Tahoe.
  • South Fork American River Trail: This trail is located along the South Fork American River and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in El Dorado County: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: El Dorado County is home to several award-winning wineries where visitors can sample local wines.
  • Whitewater Rafting/Kayaking: The South Fork American River offers several rapids for whitewater rafting or kayaking.
  • Apple Hill: Visitors can go apple picking, visit pumpkin patches, and enjoy cider at the many orchards in Apple Hill.

Food and Drink Scene of El Dorado County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Pie: El Dorado County is known for its delicious apple pie, and you can find it at various bakeries and restaurants in the area.
  • BBQ: Several restaurants in El Dorado County offer BBQ, including pulled pork, brisket, and ribs.
  • Mexican Food: There are several Mexican restaurants in the area that offer authentic cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: El Dorado County is known for its award-winning wines, and there are several wineries in the area that offer tastings.
  • Cider: You can find delicious cider at the many orchards in Apple Hill.
  • Craft Beer: There are several breweries in the area, including El Dorado Brewing Company and Outpost Brewery & Taproom.

Where to Stay in El Dorado County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

El Dorado County has several accommodation options to fit any budget:

  • Hotel: There are several hotels in the area, including Best Western Plus Placerville Inn and Holiday Inn Express.
  • Hostel: The American River Hostel is a popular hostel located in Coloma.
  • Guesthouses: There are several guesthouses in the area, including The Cary House Hotel and Glenmorey Country House Bed & Breakfast.

What to Buy in El Dorado County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: El Dorado County is known for its wine, and you can bring home a bottle or two as a souvenir.
  • Apple Pie: Several bakeries in the area offer delicious apple pie that you can take home with you.
  • Handmade Jewelry: You can find beautiful handmade jewelry at various shops in El Dorado County.

Local Culture and Customs of El Dorado County

El Dorado County has a rich history, and visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs. The county is known for its gold rush history, and there are several historic sites and museums that visitors should take time to explore.

Best Time to Visit El Dorado County

The best time to visit El Dorado County is in the fall when the leaves change colors and the weather is mild. Spring and summer are also popular times to visit, with warmer weather and plenty of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Dorado County

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Visit historic sites and museums.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind.
  • Ignore safety warnings.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Dorado County

El Dorado County is a relatively safe area, but visitors should take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities, such as wearing appropriate clothing and footwear and bringing plenty of water.

Health in El Dorado County

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude in El Dorado County and take precautions to avoid altitude sickness, such as drinking plenty of water and taking it easy for the first few days.

Bus Stations Near El Dorado County

How to Get Around in El Dorado County

The best way to get around El Dorado County is by car. There are several rental car agencies in the area, and the roads are well-maintained.

Where to Go Next from El Dorado County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yosemite National Park: Located a few hours from El Dorado County, Yosemite National Park is known for its stunning scenery and hiking trails.
  • San Francisco: A few hours from El Dorado County, San Francisco is known for its lively culture, food scene, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

El Dorado County is a rich destination for travelers looking for adventure and history. From outdoor activities to cultural landmarks, it offers something for everyone. Plan your trip according to your interests, and enjoy all that El Dorado County has to offer.
