Introduction to Roseville

Roseville, California, is a vibrant city located in Northern California’s Placer County in Gold Country. It is about 16 miles northeast of Sacramento and has a population of over 140,000 people. Roseville is known for its rich history, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and fantastic shopping. In this travel guide, we will explore the best attractions, activities, places to stay, and more in Roseville.

List of Places to Visit in Roseville

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Roseville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Historical Places

  • Maidu Museum & Historic Site: Located in the heart of Roseville, this museum and historic site offers a glimpse of the life and culture of the Maidu people, who were the first inhabitants of the area.
  • Roseville Telephone Museum: Discover the history of communication technology at the Roseville Telephone Museum. The museum has exhibits that span over a century of telephone history.
  • Carnegie Museum: The Carnegie Museum is a historic landmark building that was once the city’s first library. Today, it houses exhibits about the history of Roseville and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Roseville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Miner’s Ravine Trail: This scenic trail is perfect for hiking, biking, or walking. The trail runs for 8.2 miles and offers beautiful views of the surrounding nature.
  • Folsom Lake State Recreation Area: Located about 20 miles from Roseville, the Folsom Lake State Recreation Area is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and hiking. It is also home to a diverse range of wildlife.
  • Roseville Utility Exploration Center: Explore environmental sustainability, water conservation, and energy efficiency with interactive exhibits, themed gardens, and live animals at Roseville Utility Exploration Center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roseville

Roseville is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • Miner’s Ravine Trail: This multi-use trail is suitable for hiking, biking, and walking. The trail runs for 8.2 miles and offers beautiful views of the surrounding nature.
  • Saugstad Park Loop: This is a scenic 1.7-mile hiking trail that follows Dry Creek. It’s a dog-friendly trail, so you can bring your furry friend along.
  • Woodcreek Oaks Trail: This 6.2-mile trail is perfect for biking or walking. It’s a mostly flat trail that runs through residential neighborhoods and parks.

Best Things to Do in Roseville: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Roseville:

  • Go shopping: Roseville is known for its fantastic shopping, and there are several malls to choose from, including the Westfield Galleria at Roseville and the Fountains at Roseville.
  • Attend a concert at the Toyota Amphitheatre: The Toyota Amphitheatre is an open-air venue that hosts concerts and events throughout the year. It’s a great place to catch your favorite band or artist.
  • Visit Golfland Sunsplash: This family-friendly water park and amusement park is the perfect place to cool off on a hot day. It features waterslides, pools, mini-golf, bumper boats, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Roseville

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Roseville:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sourdough pizza: Try the delicious sourdough pizzas at Fat’s Asia Bistro.
  • Crab Cakes: Enjoy fresh crab cakes at Sienna Restaurant.
  • Waffle sandwiches: Try the creative waffle sandwiches at The Waffle Experience.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Visit the Wise Villa Winery to taste local wines.
  • Beer: Check out the Out of Bounds Brewing Company for some fantastic beer.

Where to Stay in Roseville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Roseville has a plethora of accommodations to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly options to high-end hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hyatt Place Sacramento/Roseville: This upscale hotel is located in the heart of Roseville and features an outdoor pool, fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Courtyard by Marriott Roseville: This hotel is located near the Westfield Galleria at Roseville and features an outdoor pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.
  • Heritage Inn Express Roseville: This budget-friendly hotel is located near the historic district of Roseville and features free Wi-Fi and complimentary breakfast.

Local Culture and Customs of Roseville

Roseville has a rich history, and residents take pride in their heritage. When visiting Roseville, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Roseville

The best time to visit Roseville is either in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roseville

Roseville is a safe city to visit, but it’s still essential to remain vigilant and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Roseville

Roseville has excellent medical facilities, including Sutter Roseville Medical Center and Kaiser Permanente. You can also find several urgent care clinics in the area.

Getting to Roseville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roseville

The two closest airports to Roseville are the Sacramento International Airport, located approximately 27 miles away, and the San Francisco International Airport, which is about 2.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Roseville

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in Sacramento, about 16 miles away from Roseville.

Other Options

There are several car rental and ride-sharing options available in Roseville, including Uber, Lyft, and rental car companies like Enterprise and Hertz.

How to Get Around in Roseville

Roseville is a dispersed city, and having a car is the easiest way to get around. Alternatively, ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Roseville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting from Roseville:

  • Sacramento: Visit the state capital and experience its rich history, art, and culture.
  • Napa Valley: Explore the world-renowned wineries and vineyards in Napa Valley.
  • Lake Tahoe: Go on a scenic drive and enjoy the breathtaking views of Lake Tahoe.

Conclusion

Roseville offers visitors the best of California’s natural beauty, rich history, cultural attractions, and fantastic food and drink scene. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan the perfect trip to Roseville, California.
