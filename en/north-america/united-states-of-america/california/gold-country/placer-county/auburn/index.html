Introduction to Auburn

Nestled in Placer County, Auburn is a picturesque town in Northern California’s Gold Country. It has a rich history dating back to the Gold Rush era, and visitors can still experience that history through the beautifully preserved artifacts and buildings listed in the National Register of Historic Places.

From its natural attractions, outdoor activities, and art museums to the well-rounded food culture, Auburn is a place worth visiting. Here’s a comprehensive tour guide of the town to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Auburn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Auburn State Theatre

One of the crown jewels of Auburn, the Auburn State Theatre was originally opened in 1930. Today, it has been restored to its original grandeur and shows a variety of independent, classic, and foreign films, live performances, and concerts.

Bernhard Museum Complex

The Bernhard Museum Complex is a fascinating historic site that provides an insight into the history of Placer County. The property includes the Victorian-style Bernhard House, the Stanford Gallery, and the Gold Rush-era doctor’s office.

Placer County Museum

The Placer County Museum is a fascinating place to visit to learn about the rich history of the Gold Rush in California, and the influence it had on the development of the area.

Natural Attractions of Auburn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Auburn State Recreation Area

Located along the American River, the Auburn State Recreation Area offers 30 miles of hiking trails, biking, horseback riding, swimming, and fishing opportunities. Visitors can also enjoy the stunning views of the American River Canyon.

Hidden Falls Regional Park

Hidden Falls Regional Park is a 1,200-acre park that offers scenic hiking and biking trails, various waterfalls, and a great opportunity to see local wildlife. It is a perfect place for a family day out.

Lake Clementine Trail

Lake Clementine is a man-made lake that provides stunning views of the American River Canyon. The Lake Clementine Trail is a 4-mile hike that will take you through oak woodlands, manzanita shrubs, and volcanic rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auburn

Auburn is a haven for nature lovers and adventurous souls. From steep canyon hikes to leisure biking routes, here are some of the best options for hiking and biking in and around Auburn:

  • Western States Trail
  • Hidden Falls Trail
  • Foresthill Divide Loop Trail
  • Quarry Road Trail
  • Culvert Trail

Best Things to Do in Auburn: Activities and Tours

White Water Rafting

Auburn is a rafting and kayaking paradise, with the American river nearby. Many local tour companies offer white-water rafting trips that range from easy, family-friendly floats to challenging class III rapids for adrenaline junkies.

Wine Tasting

Auburn is also known for its vineyards and wine production culture. You can visit local wineries and tasting rooms to try some of the best local wines the area has to offer.

Horseback Riding

Auburn is a great place to go horseback riding, with its vast expanse of scenic trails. Several local stables offer horseback riding tours that are perfect for anyone interested in exploring the nearby areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auburn

Day 1: Check into your hotel and settle in. Visit the Auburn Historic Theater and enjoy an evening show.

Day 2: Hike Hidden Falls Trail in the morning, then go wine tasting in the afternoon.

Day 3: Go horseback riding through the Western States Trail in the morning, then relax at the Lake Clementine beach.

Day 4: Take a scenic drive on the historic Highway 49 and explore the many small towns along the way.

Day 5: Raft the American River and enjoy a picnic lunch along the banks.

Day 6: Visit the Placer County Museum and learn about the Gold Rush era.

Day 7: Enjoy scenic views while hiking the Foresthill Divide Loop Trail in the morning, then have dinner at a local restaurant before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Auburn

When it comes to food and drink, Auburn has plenty to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try while in town.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Classic American breakfast at Awful Annie’s
  • Unique sandwiches at Carpe Vino
  • BBQ and fried chicken at Joe Caribe’s
  • Tacos and margaritas at La Parilla

Local Beverages

  • Local wines from Lone Buffalo Vineyards and Mt Vernon Winery
  • Handcrafted beers at Moonraker Brewing Company and Knee Deep Brewing Company
  • Creative cocktails at Bootlegger’s Old Town Tavern and Wine Down Wednesday

Where to Stay in Auburn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auburn Marriott Courtyard

A modern hotel located on Bell Road. Excellent amenities include an indoor pool, a fitness center, and contemporary accommodation.

The Latest Guest House and Hostel

The Latest Guest House and Hostel is an affordable option for budget travelers. It offers comfortable beds and a communal kitchen.

Power’s Mansion Inn

Power’s Mansion Inn is a well-restored, 19th-century mansion that offers luxury accommodation with a four-star restaurant on-site.

What to Buy in Auburn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique souvenirs and locally made products to bring home from Auburn:

  • Handmade jewelry from California Organized to Alleviate Homelessness (COAH)
  • Local wine from Lone Buffalo Vineyards and Mt Vernon Winery
  • Handmade pottery from Blue Oaks Arts

Local Culture and Customs of Auburn

Auburn is culturally significant and is home to a thriving art scene. The town hosts several art fairs, festivals, and music events each year. It is known for its small-town charm, where everybody knows their neighbors.

Best Time to Visit Auburn

The best time to visit Auburn is between late spring and early fall when the weather is warm and dry. The autumn season is especially beautiful with the change in scenery. Winter can be cold, but it is a great time for winter sports activities, such as skiing and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auburn

Do:

  • Explore the historic downtown area of Auburn
  • Visit the local art galleries and museums
  • Spend a day hiking the beautiful Auburn State Recreation Area

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind
  • Ignore safety signs at the parks or natural attractions
  • Be disrespectful to local culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Auburn

Overall, Auburn is a safe place to visit, but as always, it’s better to stay cautious. Avoid leaving valuable items in your car, and always lock your accommodation. When hiking, stay on the marked trails and follow safety rules while accessing natural attractions.

Health in Auburn

There are no particular concerns to worry about when it comes to health in Auburn, but it is always recommended to check updates on endemic diseases in the area.

Getting to Auburn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Auburn

Sacramento International Airport is the closest airport to Auburn. It is approximately a 45-minute drive from Auburn.

Bus Stations Near Auburn

Greyhound and Amtrak buses stop regularly in Auburn from San Francisco and Sacramento.

Other Options

The best and convenient way to get around Auburn is by car. You can rent one at the Sacramento airport or in the nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Auburn

The best way to get around Auburn is also by car. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or explore the town on foot.

Where to Go Next from Auburn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sacramento, the capital of California, just a 30-minute drive from Auburn.
  • Lake Tahoe, a two-hour drive from Auburn.
  • San Francisco, the most populous city in Northern California, is just a two-hour drive from Auburn.

Conclusion

Auburn, California, has much to offer, from its vast history and cultural significance to its outdoors adventures, local art scene, and epicurean delights. This comprehensive tour guide is all you need to plan your trip and immerse yourself in the beauty of California’s Gold Country.
