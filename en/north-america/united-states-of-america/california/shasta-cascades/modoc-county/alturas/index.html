Introduction to Alturas

Nestled in the heart of Shasta Cascades, Alturas is a small town surrounded by breathtaking scenery and rugged wilderness. Its fascinating history, diverse culture, and warm hospitality make Alturas an idyllic destination for anyone seeking adventure and relaxation. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Alturas has something for everyone. This travel guide will help you discover the hidden gems of Alturas and plan your perfect vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Alturas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Modoc County Historical Museum: Housed in a former railroad depot, this museum showcases exhibits that chronicle the fascinating history of Modoc County, including Native American artifacts, pioneer memorabilia, and natural history specimens.

  • Alturas Native Daughters of the Golden West Parlor No. 64: This historic building dates back to 1902 and now serves as a museum dedicated to the preservation of local history and culture.

  • Veterans Memorial Hall: Built in 1948, this is the oldest continually active Veterans Memorial Hall in California. The museum exhibits cover military history from the Civil War until present time, and the facility is open for rentals.

Natural Attractions of Alturas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Modoc National Wildlife Refuge: Spread over 7,000 acres, this wildlife refuge is home to a wide range of bird species, including bald eagles, white-faced ibis, and sandhill cranes. Visitors can take a refuge tour, hike the trails, or enjoy birdwatching from the observation deck.

  • Hart Mountain National Antelope Refuge: If you’re looking for a unique and unforgettable wildlife experience, the Hart Mountain National Antelope Refuge is your ideal destination. Open year-round and free of charge, the refuge offers opportunities for hiking, wildlife observation and photography, stargazing, and camping.

  • Fandango Pass: This scenic mountain pass near Alturas offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding areas. The pass is located along the historic Applegate Trail, which was used by pioneers during the Gold Rush era.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alturas

Alturas offers a range of hiking and biking routes for all levels of outdoor enthusiasts. From leisurely strolls to challenging mountain treks, there’s a trail for everyone. Here are our top picks:

  • Warner Mountains: The Warner Mountains are a range of mountains located east of Alturas, offering some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area. The trails offer scenic views of the surrounding valleys, mountains, and streams.

  • Modoc Line Rail Trail: This 25-mile trail offers a mix of paved and unpaved paths, perfect for both biking and hiking. The trail runs alongside the historic Southern Pacific railroad tracks and offers stunning views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Alturas: Activities and Tours

  • Modoc County Fair: The Modoc County Fair is held every August and is a must-see event for anyone visiting Alturas during that time. The fair offers a range of activities, including live music, rodeo events, carnival rides, and traditional fair food.

  • Modoc Volcanic Scenic Byway: A scenic drive through Modoc County, the Modoc Volcanic Scenic Byway is a 100-mile route that showcases the area’s volcanic landscapes and historic sites.

  • Alturas Balloon Fest: Held every October, Alturas Balloon Fest is an exciting event that features hot air balloons and a range of fun activities and entertainment for the whole family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alturas

Day 1: Visit the Modoc County Historical Museum and explore downtown Alturas Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking along the Warner Mountains Day 3: Take a guided tour of Modoc National Wildlife Refuge and enjoy birdwatching Day 4: Drive along the Modoc Volcanic Scenic Byway and explore the volcanic landscapes Day 5: Visit the Alturas Native Daughters of the Golden West Parlor No. 64 museum and learn about the local culture and history Day 6: Take a day trip to Hart Mountain National Antelope Refuge and experience the wildlife and natural beauty Day 7: Attend the Modoc County Fair or the Alturas Balloon Fest (depending on the season)

Food and Drink Scene of Alturas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Basque cuisine: Alturas has a rich Basque heritage, and you can’t leave without trying traditional Basque dishes like lamb stews, chorizo, and paella.

  • Burgers: Alturas has some of the best burgers in California. Satisfy your craving at the Burger Barn or the Cowboy Cafe.

Local Beverages

  • Lava Beds Brewing Company: Located in Tulelake, a short drive from Alturas, Lava Beds Brewing Company is a locally-owned microbrewery that offers a range of craft beers.

  • Organic wines: Modoc County is known for its natural and organic wineries. Visit Cedarville Vineyard or Madonna Estate to taste the local wines.

Where to Stay in Alturas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Trailside Inn: This pet-friendly hotel in Alturas offers guests comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and an outdoor pool.

  • Vagabond Inn Executive - Lakeview: Located in Lakeview, this hotel is a great option for travelers visiting Alturas for business or leisure. The hotel offers a range of amenities, including free Wi-Fi, on-site gym, and continental breakfast.

  • Red Coach Inn: This cozy guesthouse is the perfect option for travelers seeking a more intimate and personalized experience. The Red Coach Inn has rooms featuring classic Western decor and a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Alturas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Modoc County honey: Sample the local honey from Modoc County at local farmers’ markets or purchase it at local shops.

  • Crafts and artwork: Discover unique artworks and handmade crafts made by local artisans at the Modoc County Art Center.

Local Culture and Customs of Alturas

Alturas has a rich cultural heritage, including a vibrant Basque community and Native American tribes. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions and seek out opportunities to learn about the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Alturas

The best time to visit Alturas is from May to October, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alturas

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Explore the hidden gems and hiking trails
  • Try the local Basque cuisine and craft beer
  • Check the schedule for the Modoc County Fair and Alturas Balloon Fest

Don’t:

  • Ignore the safety rules and regulations on hiking trails
  • Disrupt the natural habitats of the wildlife in the national parks
  • Litter or disturb the environment
  • Underestimate the unpredictable weather

Safety and Health

Safety in Alturas

Overall, Alturas is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take necessary precautions while hiking, camping, and exploring the wilderness. Be familiar with the local wildlife, carry water, and wear proper footwear.

Health in Alturas

Alturas has a health care clinic and pharmacy for basic health needs. Additionally, visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications and be prepared for the change in altitude.

Getting to Alturas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alturas

  • Jack McNamara Field Airport: This is the closest airport to Alturas, located about 5 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Alturas

  • Greyhound Bus: Greyhound provides a bus service to Alturas from various cities across California.

Other Options

  • Driving: Alturas is easily accessible by car, with easy access to Highway 395.

How to Get Around in Alturas

Alturas is a small town, and a car is the most convenient option for exploring the area. However, visitors can also rent a bike or take a guided tour to experience the natural beauty of the town.

Where to Go Next from Alturas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Crater Lake National Park: A stunning volcanic lake that is only a few hours’ drive from Alturas.

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: Visit the geothermal wonders and explore the trails in the volcanic park, located just a few hours’ drive away.

  • Sunstone Mines: Visit the local mines to collect stunning sunstones, located a short drive from Alturas.

Conclusion

Alturas is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by travelers. This travel guide has provided a comprehensive itinerary of the top-rated attractions, outdoor adventures, must-try dishes, and local culture of Alturas. Come and experience the beauty of California’s Shasta Cascades in all its glory!
