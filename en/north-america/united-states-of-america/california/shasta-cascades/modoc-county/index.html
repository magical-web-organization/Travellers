Introduction to Modoc County

Located in California’s Shasta Cascades region, Modoc County is a destination that often goes overlooked. However, those who do venture to this sparsely populated county are rewarded with scenic vistas, outdoor adventures, and a slice of authentic Wild West culture. Modoc County is also home to the historic Modoc War, which made it a significant place in Native American history.

List of Places to Visit in Modoc County

Modoc County may be vast, but it’s not short on things to see and do. Take a look at some of the top tourist attractions of Modoc County.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Modoc County Historical Museum: This museum features exhibits that detail the area’s rich history, including displays on the Modoc War, pioneer life, and Native American cultures.

  • Canby Pioneer Museum: Housed in a restored 1880s schoolhouse, the Canby Pioneer Museum showcases artifacts from early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Modoc County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lava Beds National Monument: This national monument boasts nearly 800 caves, historic battlefields, and unique geologic formations.

  • Warner Mountains: Part of the northernmost extension of the Sierra Nevada range, the Warner Mountains offer incredible scenery and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Modoc County

Thanks to its rugged landscapes and natural beauty, hiking and biking are popular activities in Modoc County. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Plum Valley Trail: An easy 3.5-mile trail through a dense pine forest, with opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

  • Big Sage Trail: A 9-mile trail that follows an old logging road, offering stunning views of the Warner Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Modoc County: Activities and Tours

  • Wilderness Adventures: Take a guided horseback ride or pack trip into the Warner Mountains, exploring the region’s rugged wilderness.

  • Cycling Tours: Sign up for a cycling tour of Modoc County, where you can take in the stunning scenery and learn about the area’s rich history from a local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Modoc County

Day 1: Arrive in Alturas, the county seat, explore local museums. Day 2: Visit the Lava Beds National Monument, hike the Schonchin Butte Trail. Day 3: Take a tour of the Warner Mountains by horseback or pack trip. Day 4: Hike the Big Sage Trail, admire the Warner Mountain views. Day 5: Experience Modoc’s Wild West culture in Canby, stop by the Canby Pioneer Museum. Day 6: Go on a guided cycling tour, enjoy the beautiful scenery. Day 7: Visit the nearby Modoc National Wildlife Refuge or go fishing on the Pit River.

Food and Drink Scene of Modoc County

When it comes to food and drink in Modoc County, you’ll find hearty American fare and a few local specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef Jerky: Modoc County is known for its beef jerky, which is made locally.

  • Basque Cuisine: Some local restaurants offer traditional Basque dishes, such as lamb chops, oxtail stew, and paella.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The Brass Rail in Alturas offers a rotating selection of local and regional craft beers.

Where to Stay in Modoc County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Modoc County isn’t known for its luxury hotels, but it does offer several budget-friendly options for travelers.

  • Niles Hotel: This historic hotel in Alturas dates back to 1912 and offers simple rooms with shared bathrooms and free Wi-Fi.

  • Warner Mountain Lodge: Located in the heart of the Warner Mountains, this lodge offers cabins with kitchens and outdoor grills.

What to Buy in Modoc County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and locally made products, be sure to check out these places:

  • Modoc Country Store: This store in Alturas offers a wide range of locally made products, including honey, jams, and artisan crafts.

  • Modoc County Beef Jerky: Stop by the Modoc Meat & Processing plant in Alturas to pick up some of the county’s famous beef jerky.

Local Culture and Customs of Modoc County

Modoc County has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Native American and Wild West history. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Modoc County

The best time to visit Modoc County is during the summer months, when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall can also be pleasant times to visit, with fewer crowds and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Modoc County

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the county’s outdoor activities and scenic vistas.
  • Try locally made products and cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.
  • Approach wildlife or stray from designated trails in nature reserves.
  • Disrespect Native American traditions or sacred sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Modoc County

Modoc County is generally a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Wildlife encounters are possible, so exercise caution when hiking or camping.
  • Carry plenty of water and sunscreen when going outdoors.

Health in Modoc County

Modoc County has limited medical facilities, so visitors should come prepared with any necessary medication or medical supplies.

Getting to Modoc County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Modoc County

The nearest airports to Modoc County are:

  • Klamath Falls Airport (KLAM), located approximately 145 miles to the north in Oregon.
  • Reno-Tahoe International Airport (RNO), located approximately 200 miles to the south in Nevada.

Bus Stations Near Modoc County

Greyhound bus service operates in Alturas, with connections to major cities in California and Oregon.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to explore Modoc County, as public transportation is limited.

How to Get Around in Modoc County

Driving is the most common way to get around Modoc County, as public transportation is limited. Bicycle rentals are also available in some areas.

Where to Go Next from Modoc County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Crater Lake National Park: Located just over the border in Oregon, Crater Lake National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: This national park in neighboring Shasta County offers stunning landscapes and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Modoc County may not be on everyone’s radar, but it’s a gem of a destination for those who seek authentic Wild West culture, scenic vistas, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re ready to explore all that Modoc County has to offer.
