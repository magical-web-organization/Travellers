Introduction to Millville

Welcome to Millville, a charming little town nestled in the heart of Shasta Cascades, California. This peaceful place is famous for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals. Whether you’re an adventure enthusiast, a foodie, or a history buff, Millville has something for you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll tell you everything you need to know about Millville, including the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Millville

Millville is a small town, but it offers plenty of exciting attractions and things to do. Here are some of the top places to visit in Millville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Millville Plains Baptist Church - Built in 1877, this historic church is a fascinating example of the traditional American Gothic style. Visitors can learn about the history of the church and its role in the community in the early days.

  • Shasta State Historic Park - Located in nearby Redding, this state park is home to a collection of historic buildings and artifacts from the California Gold Rush era. Visitors can explore a reconstructed gold-rush town, learn about the Native American history of the area, and walk through the beautiful Shasta Valley.

  • Turtle Bay Exploration Park - This fun and educational park offers a variety of exhibits and activities related to ecology, natural history, and Native American culture. Visitors can interact with live animals, enjoy beautiful botanical gardens, and explore the history of the area through interactive exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Millville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Whiskeytown National Recreation Area - This national park is a beautiful place to visit throughout the year. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, swimming, hiking, and camping in the wilderness of the park.

  • Shasta Lake - With over 370 miles of shoreline, Shasta Lake is a boater’s paradise. Visitors can rent a boat or take a guided tour to explore the lake’s crystal-clear waters and breathtaking views.

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park - This stunning natural wonder offers visitors the chance to explore a unique volcanic landscape. Visitors can hike through the park’s dramatic geologic features, such as volcanic peaks, hot springs, and fumaroles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Millville

For those who love to hike and bike, Millville is a dream destination. The town offers several beautiful trails and routes for you to explore, ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Buckhorn Summit Trail - This scenic trail travels through the Shasta-Trinity National Forest and offers plenty of beautiful views along the way.

  • Sacramento River Trail - This easy-to-navigate trail along the Sacramento River offers plenty of highlights, including beautiful views of Mount Shasta, the Sundial Bridge, and the Turtle Bay Exploration Park.

  • Iron Mountain Road Trail - This moderate, 7-mile trail offers some of the most stunning natural beauty in the area. At the top, you can take in panoramic views of Mt. Shasta and Lassen Peak.

Best Things to Do in Millville: Activities and Tours

In Millville, there is no shortage of fun things to do and see. Whether you want to explore the great outdoors or dive into local culture, Millville has it all. Here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • Fly Fishing Trips - The Sacramento River and its surrounding tributaries are known for world-class fly fishing. Take a guided trip with a local outfitter to get the most out of your experience.

  • Whiskeytown Waterfall Tour - This guided tour will take you on a scenic drive through the park to some of the area’s most beautiful waterfalls, including Brandy Creek Falls and Whiskeytown Falls.

  • Lassen Peak Full Moon Snowshoe Hike - Take an unforgettable guided hike through the snow-covered landscape of Lassen Volcanic National Park, led by a local naturalist. This hike is only offered during the winter months.

Food and Drink Scene of Millville

Millville’s food and drink scene is characterized by local flavors, fresh ingredients, and a strong sense of community. Here are some must-tries:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salmon - Millville is close to the ocean, which means that you can find delicious fresh salmon dishes in many local restaurants.

  • Burger - This American classic is served up with all the trimmings at several local restaurants. Try one with local cheese and bacon for a real treat.

  • Apple Pie - Made with crisp local apples and a flaky crust, this dessert is the perfect way to end your meal.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - California is known for its world-class wines, and Millville is no exception. Try a glass of local Zinfandel or Chardonnay.

  • Craft Beer - Several local breweries offer unique and flavorful beers, ranging from rich stouts to refreshing IPAs.

Where to Stay in Millville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Millville offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Shasta Pines Motel - This budget-friendly motel offers simple and clean rooms, and is conveniently located near downtown Millville.

  • Highland Dell Lodge - This charming mid-range lodge offers comfortable rooms and beautiful views of the Lassen Volcanic National Park.

  • Shasta Dam Motel - This affordable motel offers spacious rooms and a convenient location near many of the area’s best attractions.

What to Buy in Millville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring a piece of Millville home with you, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Local Wine - Take a bottle of California’s finest wine home with you.

  • Farm-Fresh Produce - The Millville area is known for its fresh produce, including fruit, vegetables, and nuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Millville

Millville is a friendly and welcoming community that takes pride in its local traditions and history. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and try to learn as much about the area’s unique culture as possible. Some local customs to be aware of include:

  • Religion - Millville has a strong Christian community, and visitors may notice many references to Christian beliefs and values throughout the town.

  • Fishing - Fishing is a popular pastime in Millville, and visitors should be respectful of local fishing regulations and customs.

  • Farmers Markets - The Millville area hosts several farmers markets throughout the year, which are great places to try local food and mingle with locals.

Best Time to Visit Millville

The best time to visit Millville is in the summer months, from June to September. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and most of the area’s outdoor attractions are open. However, be prepared for crowds and higher prices during peak season. If you prefer a quieter and more affordable experience, consider visiting in the fall or spring.

Do’s and Don’ts of Millville

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Millville:

Do:

  • Try Local Food and Drink - Millville has a rich culinary tradition, and visitors should make an effort to sample some of the area’s best dishes and drinks.

  • Explore the Outdoors - With so many beautiful natural attractions, it would be a shame not to spend some time outside in Millville.

  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions - Be respectful of local culture and customs, and try to learn as much as you can about the area’s history and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Millville is a beautiful place, and visitors should do their part to keep it that way. Be sure to dispose of your trash responsibly.

  • Trespass - Some of Millville’s natural attractions are on private property, so be sure to respect posted signs and stay on designated trails.

  • Disrespect Locals - Millville is a welcoming and friendly community, so be sure to treat locals with respect and kindness.

Safety and Health

Millville is a safe and healthy place to visit, but there are a few things to keep in mind:

Safety in Millville

  • Wildlife - Millville is home to a variety of wildlife, including bears, mountain lions, and rattlesnakes. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions when hiking or camping in the wilderness.

  • Summer Fires - The hot and dry summers can be a fire hazard in Millville. Visitors should be aware of fire restrictions and take extra care when camping or using grills.

  • Traffic - Millville is a small town with limited traffic, but visitors should still observe local traffic laws and be cautious when driving on rural roads.

Health in Millville

  • Altitude - Some of Millville’s natural attractions are at high altitude, which can cause altitude sickness in some visitors. Be sure to drink plenty of water and take it easy if you experience symptoms.

  • Ticks - Millville is home to ticks, which can carry diseases like Lyme disease. Visitors should wear protective clothing and use insect repellent when hiking or spending time outside.

Getting to Millville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Millville is located in Northern California and can be easily accessed by car or public transportation. Here are some of the best ways to get to Millville:

Airports Near Millville

  • Redding Municipal Airport - Located about 18 miles from Millville, this small airport offers several daily flights to and from major cities on the West Coast.

  • Sacramento International Airport - About a 2-hour drive from Millville, this larger airport offers flights from major domestic and international carriers.

Bus Stations Near Millville

  • Redding Greyhound Station - This bus station in Redding offers several daily connections to major cities in California and Oregon.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Millville is easily accessible by car, and several major car rental companies have locations in Redding.

How to Get Around in Millville

Millville is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, if you need to travel outside of town, a car is recommended. Several car rental companies have locations in Redding, which is located about 18 miles away from Millville.

Where to Go Next from Millville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Millville is located in one of the most beautiful areas of California, with plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some of the top places to visit:

  • Mt. Shasta - This majestic peak is a must-see for any visitor to the area. Hike to the top for stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Redding - This bustling city is located just a short drive from Millville and offers a variety of attractions, including a stunning bridge, a variety of restaurants and bars, and a variety of outdoor recreational opportunities.
