Introduction to Montgomery Creek

Montgomery Creek in Shasta County, California, is a small, scenic town known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. It is located in the Shasta Cascades and is surrounded by mountains, valleys, and forests, making it an ideal destination for those who want to explore nature, hike, bike, or indulge in outdoor activities.

  • McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park

Natural Attractions of Montgomery Creek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Montgomery Creek offers some of the best hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular hiking routes are:

Week-Long Itinerary for Montgomery Creek

Day 1: Explore Shasta State Historic Park, Turtle Bay Exploration Park, and Sundial Bridge at Turtle Bay. Day 2: Head to Lassen Volcanic National Park and hike to Lassen Peak. Day 3: Visit McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park and enjoy the Burney Falls. Day 4: Explore Mount Shasta and take a scenic drive around the mountain. Day 5: Go Whitewater rafting on the Sacramento River. Day 6: Relax at Lake Shasta and enjoy some fishing or boating. Day 7: Take a wildlife watching tour in the surrounding forests.

Local Culture and Customs of Montgomery Creek

  • The town is known for its western heritage and cowboy culture.

Best Time to Visit Montgomery Creek

The best time to visit Montgomery Creek is between May and October when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Montgomery Creek

Montgomery Creek is a relatively safe place with low crime rates. However, visitors should take care of their belongings and avoid unsafe areas.

Health in Montgomery Creek

Montgomery Creek has medical and emergency services available, but visitors should carry their basic medications and first aid kits.
