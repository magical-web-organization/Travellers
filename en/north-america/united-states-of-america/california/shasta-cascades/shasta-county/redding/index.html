Introduction to Redding

Redding is a city located in Shasta County, in Shasta Cascades, California, United States of America, North America. The city is named after the family who settled in the area in the 1800s, Benjamin Redding and his wife, Mary. Redding is known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor adventures, fascinating museums, and rich local culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s history, indulging in outdoor activities, or simply immersing yourself in the local culture, Redding has something fun and exciting for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Redding

If you’re planning a trip to Redding, here are some of the top places you should consider visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Turtle Bay Exploration Park - This 300-acre park features a museum, botanical gardens, and a wildlife sanctuary. You can explore local history, art, and culture while surrounded by stunning natural beauty.

  • Shasta State Historic Park - This park contains ruins of an old gold rush-era mining town, and visitors can explore the remains of the old courthouse, a jail, and several shops.

  • Redding Railroad Museum - This museum highlights the history of the railway that used to run through Redding, including historical exhibits and restored train cars.

Natural Attractions of Redding: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park - This park boasts a unique volcanic landscape, hot springs, and amazing hiking trails that offer breathtaking views.

  • Whiskeytown National Recreation Area - This park has clear streams, sparkling waterfalls, and a serene lake perfect for swimming and boating.

  • Sundial Bridge at Turtle Bay - This pedestrian bridge over the Sacramento River has a unique design and is inspired by the city’s famous sundial.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redding

  • Buckhorn Biking and Hiking Trail - This trail is perfect for avid bikers and hikers. It spans 9 miles and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Sacramento River Trail - This 17-mile trail runs alongside the Sacramento River, offering breathtaking views and access to some of the city’s most popular attractions.

  • Iron Mountain Hiking Trail - This trail is a moderate to difficult hike that takes you to the summit of Iron Mountain, where you’ll be rewarded with incredible views of the city and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Redding: Activities and Tours

  • Shasta Lake Dinner Cruise - Enjoy a delicious meal and stunning views of Shasta Lake on this relaxing dinner cruise.

  • River Rafting - Redding has some of the best white water rafting in California, and visitors can choose from a variety of guided tours and excursions.

  • Wildlife Safari - Experience the local wildlife up-close with a guided tour of the Lassen Volcanic National Park or the Whiskeytown National Recreation Area.

Food and Drink Scene of Redding

When it comes to food and drink, Redding has a lot to offer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tri-tip Sandwich - This delicious sandwich is made with tri-tip steak, grilled onions, and cheese, and it’s a local favorite.

  • Sourdough Bread - The sourdough bread in Redding is unlike anything you’ll find anywhere else in the world. It’s made with a special local starter that gives it a unique flavor.

  • Blackberry Pie - Redding is known for its blackberries, and the local blackberry pie is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Redding Beer Works - This local microbrewery offers a variety of craft beers and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

  • California Wine - The nearby wine country produces some of the best wines in California, and visitors can enjoy a glass or a bottle at any of the local wineries.

Where to Stay in Redding: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Redding has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Gaia Hotel and Spa - This boutique hotel has a spa, restaurant, and stunning views of the local landscape.

  • Bridge Bay Resort - This resort offers cabins, RV rental sites, and a marina, and it’s located right on the shores of Shasta Lake.

  • The Inn at Shasta Lake - This charming bed and breakfast offers stunning views of Shasta Lake and a peaceful setting.

What to Buy in Redding: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Redding has several local products and souvenirs you might want to bring back home, including:

  • Blackberry Products - Redding is known for its blackberries, and you can find blackberry jam, syrup, and wine at local shops and markets.

  • Handmade Crafts - The local artists and craftsmen make unique pottery, jewelry, and art that you won’t find anywhere else.

Local Culture and Customs of Redding

Redding has a rich local culture and customs, including:

  • Western Heritage - Redding has a strong connection to its western heritage, and you’ll find plenty of rodeos, cattle drives, and other western-themed events.

  • Outdoor Lifestyle - The locals of Redding love their outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and boating.

Best Time to Visit Redding

The best time to visit Redding is during the spring or fall, when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner. Summers can get very hot, and winters can be rainy and cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redding

Do:

  • Do explore the local museums and historical sites to learn about the city’s history.

  • Do indulge in the local cuisine and try the tri-tip sandwich and blackberry pie.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the natural parks and preserves, and always respect the wildlife.

  • Don’t underestimate the strength of the sun, even during the cooler months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redding

Redding is generally a safe city, but visitors should take basic safety precautions like locking their cars and keeping their valuables out of sight. Avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas and always stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Redding

Redding has several healthcare clinics and hospitals for emergency and non-emergency medical care. It’s recommended to have travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Redding: Transportation Options and Logistics

Redding is easily accessible by car and plane. Here are the options:

Airports Near Redding

  • Redding Municipal Airport - This airport has direct flights to several major cities, including San Francisco, Seattle, and Portland.

Bus Stations Near Redding

  • Greyhound Bus Station - This station is located downtown and offers bus service to several nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Redding has several car rental agencies for those who want to rent a car and explore the city and surrounding area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Redding

Redding has several transportation options to help visitors get around. The most popular options include:

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a popular option for visitors who want to explore the city and surrounding area on their own.

  • Bus Service - The local bus service, called RABA, covers most of the city and offers several different routes and schedules.

Where to Go Next from Redding: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring some of the nearby areas, here are some of the top nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Mt. Shasta - This nearby mountain offers spectacular views and hiking trails.

  • Trinity River - This river is known for its white water rafting and fishing opportunities.

  • Sacramento - This city is located just 2 hours south of Redding and is the capital of California.

Conclusion

Redding is a truly unique and beautiful city with a lot to offer visitors. From stunning natural beauty to rich local culture, there’s something for everyone in this wonderful city. Whether you want to explore the local history and museums, indulge in the delicious cuisine, or simply bask in the outdoor activities, Redding is a perfect destination for your next vacation.
