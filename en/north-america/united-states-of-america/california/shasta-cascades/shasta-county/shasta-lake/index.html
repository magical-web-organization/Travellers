Introduction to Shasta Lake

The majestic waterbody known as Shasta Lake is located in the beautiful Shasta Cascades of California, USA. Shasta Lake is considered one of the largest reservoirs in the state of California, with a sprawling 365 miles of shoreline. The beauty of Shasta Lake lies in its crystal clear waters and lush green forests surrounding it, making it a prime location for outdoor enthusiasts who love to explore nature. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to Shasta Lake.

List of Places to Visit in Shasta Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shasta Dam: Nicknamed the “keystone of the Central Valley project,” Shasta Dam has played a massive role in California’s agriculture and cities. Visit this attraction to learn about its rich history and astonishing construction.
  • Turtle Bay Exploration Park: This all-in-one attraction features a botanical park, museum, and aquarium. It’s perfect for families with children who want to learn more about nature and local wildlife.
  • Cascade Theatre: This 1935 art deco theatre still hosts concerts, plays, and other events to this day. Grab some popcorn and enjoy a night out on the town in Shasta county.

Natural Attractions of Shasta Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shasta Lake Waterfalls: The lake is a treasure trove of beautiful waterfalls that flow into the lake. Explore the various falls around the lake for a unique adventure.
  • Shasta-Trinity National Forest: This vast expanse of wilderness offers ample opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Lake Shasta Caverns: Nestled deep within a mountain, this natural wonder offers tours that explore the geology and history of the caves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shasta Lake

  • Bailey Cove Trail: A 2.5 mile easy trail that takes you through beautiful forests and down by the lake’s pristine waters.
  • Sacramento River Rail Trail: A 13-mile paved trail that runs along the Sacramento River. Perfect for cyclists and hikers alike.
  • Clear Creek Greenway Trail: A 7-mile trail that offers panoramic views of the lake and connects to the Whiskeytown National Recreation Area.

Best Things to Do in Shasta Lake: Activities and Tours

  • Shasta Lake Dinner Cruise: Enjoy a fine dining experience with a beautiful view of the lake as you soak in the sunset.
  • Houseboat Rental: Rent a houseboat or a speedboat and enjoy the unspoiled beauty of the lake.
  • Fishing: Shasta Lake is renowned for its fantastic fishing spots, including spotted bass, catfish, and rainbow trout.

Food and Drink Scene of Shasta Lake

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tri-tip Sandwich: A delicious sandwich filled with juicy meat and tangy BBQ sauce.
  • Crispy Fried Chicken: A favorite of locals, served with rich, flavorful gravy over a bed of fluffy mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Californian Wine: Try the local Zinfandel, Pinot, and Cabernet for a taste of California’s unique terroir.
  • Microbreweries: Taste some original local brews, including stouts, IPAs, and ales.

Where to Stay in Shasta Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Garden Inn Redding: This hotel is perfect for both business and leisure travelers and offers numerous amenities like free Wi-Fi, an outdoor pool, and fitness center.
  • Bridge Bay Resort: With a private beach and marina, this resort is perfect for houseboat and speedboat enthusiasts who want a convenient and picturesque place to stay.
  • Salt Creek Inn: This classic, cozy inn is nestled in Shasta Lake’s downtown. With free Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, and a hot tub, this inn caters to all your basic needs.

What to Buy in Shasta Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Wines: Choose from different varieties and bring home a taste of Shasta Lake.
  • Shasta Lake Shirt: Take home a t-shirt or hoodie showcasing the lake’s beauty as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Shasta Lake

  • Shasta Lake is home to numerous Native American tribes, and visitors are urged to respect their traditions and culture.
  • The region is also famous for its agriculture, and visitors can participate in numerous farm tours and taste fresh, locally grown produce.

Best Time to Visit Shasta Lake

The best time to visit Shasta Lake is during the summer months, from June to September, when temperatures are warm and the weather is ideal for water sports and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shasta Lake

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the area by keeping it clean and leaving no trash behind.
  • Take advantage of the numerous outdoor activities available and explore the surrounding wilderness.
  • Respect the culture and traditions of the local Native American communities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave any trace of your visit.
  • Don’t engage in activities that harm the environment or disturb local wildlife.
  • Don’t drink and drive or operate a boat under the influence.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shasta Lake

  • Be prepared for the hot weather during summertime and wear sunscreen and protective clothing.
  • Be cautious when swimming in the lake, especially with children, and heed all signs and warnings regarding water safety.

Health in Shasta Lake

  • Be careful when hiking or exploring the wilderness as some wildlife in the area can be dangerous, especially rattlesnakes.

Getting to Shasta Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shasta Lake

  • Redding Municipal Airport (RDD) is the nearest airport to Shasta Lake and is about 34 miles away.
  • Sacramento International Airport (SMF) is about 162 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Shasta Lake

  • Greyhound has a station in Redding, which is around 20 minutes away from Shasta Lake.

Other Options

  • Shasta Regional Transit System provides bus service between Redding and Shasta Lake.

How to Get Around in Shasta Lake

  • The best way to get around Shasta Lake is by car as renting a car gives you the flexibility to explore.
  • Bike rentals and tours are also available for those who prefer more physical activity.
  • Boat rentals and tours are also available to explore the stunning lake’s beauty.

Where to Go Next from Shasta Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: This national park, located about 70 miles from Shasta Lake, is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and exploring geothermal areas.
  • Whiskeytown National Recreation Area: This beautiful area is worth a visit for its fantastic fishing, swimming, and hiking trails.
  • Mount Shasta: A must-visit destination for all nature lovers, for its stunning natural beauty and adventure sports.

Conclusion

Shasta Lake is an unspoiled natural wonder that promises visitors an unforgettable experience. With its lush green forests, crystal clear waters, and endless outdoor recreational opportunities, it’s no wonder Shasta Lake is a popular destination for outdoor lovers.
