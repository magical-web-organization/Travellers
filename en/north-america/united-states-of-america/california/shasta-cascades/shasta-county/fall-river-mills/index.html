Introduction to Fall River Mills

Fall River Mills is a small town nestled in the heart of Shasta County, in the Shasta Cascades of Northern California, United States. It sits at the confluence of three major rivers, the Pit, Fall, and Tule, offering visitors some of the most breathtaking scenery in the state. Fall River Mills is known for its natural beauty and recreational opportunities, including fishing, hiking, and hunting. Additionally, it houses fascinating historical landmarks, making it a perfect destination both for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Fall River Mills

Fall River Mills has something for every traveler, and here are some top attractions you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Crook Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Northern California, with emphasis on the significant military history of Fort Crook.
  • Pit River Tribal Museum: A small museum that exhibits the rich history and culture of the Pit River Tribe.

Natural Attractions of Fall River Mills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lassen National Forest: Covers over 2,500 square miles, renowned for its mountain peaks, dense forests, hiking trails, and waterfalls.
  • Ahjumawi Lava Springs State Park: Boasts one of the most spectacular natural wonders, with more than 13,000 acres of lava fields, waterways, and bird habitats.
  • McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park: A must-see spot in Northern California, features a 129-foot waterfall, which cascades into a crystalline pool, and surrounded by a lush forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fall River Mills

Fall River Mills is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, particularly hiking and biking enthusiasts, and here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Fall River Valley Trail: A 13-mile hike that takes you through gorgeous vistas, and wildlife, in the heart of Fall River Mills.
  • Ahjumawi Lava Springs State Park Trails: Multiple trails of varying lengths, offering some of the most stunning views of Northern California’s natural wonders.

Best Things to Do in Fall River Mills: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Fall River Mills is a popular fishing destination, and visitors can catch a wide variety of fish, including trout, bass, and catfish.
  • Whitewater Rafting: Journey down the Pit River, which provides Class III and IV rapids that will take you through some breathtaking scenery.
  • Hot Air Ballooning: Enjoy a bird’s eye view of Fall River Mills and its surroundings through hot air ballooning.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fall River Mills

Day 1: Visit Fort Crook Museum and explore the historic military fort.

Day 2: Hike the Fall River Valley Trail and take in the stunning views of the town’s surroundings.

Day 3: Go fishing on the Pit River and catch some of the most sought-after fish in Northern California.

Day 4: Check out Ahjumawi Lava Springs State Park, and explore the incredible scenic vistas of the park.

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon tour and witness the town from a unique perspective.

Day 6: Tour the Pit River Tribal Museum and learn about the history and culture of the native Pit River tribe.

Day 7: Hike the McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park and witness the beauty of California’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Fall River Mills

Fall River Mills offers visitors a variety of dining options, from casual to fine dining, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled Trout: Fresh fish caught locally and grilled to perfection served with vegetables
  • Biscuits and Gravy: Homemade flaky biscuits with gravy made from scratch
  • Farm-to-table cuisine: Many restaurants in Fall River Mills offer farm-to-table cuisine that is sourced locally

Local Beverages

  • California Wine: California has some of the best wineries worldwide, and many of them are easily accessible from Fall River Mills.
  • Craft Beer: Northern California is home to some of the best craft beer breweries in America, including Fall River Brewery.

Where to Stay in Fall River Mills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fall River Mills offers a wide range of accommodations that cater to every budget, some of which include:

  • Fall River Hotel: A historic hotel that has been serving guests since 1935.
  • Green Gables Motel & Suites: Affordable Suites with all the necessary amenities.
  • River Rock Guesthouse: A cozy and private guesthouse with spectacular views.

What to Buy in Fall River Mills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey
  • Organic lavender products: From essential oils to handcrafted soaps.
  • Artisanal ceramics: Handmade pottery, mugs, and vases made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Fall River Mills

Fall River Mills has a rich history, with its cultural heritage deeply rooted in its native tribes. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local tribes’ traditions and learn about their culture through various tribal events.

Best Time to Visit Fall River Mills

The best time to visit Fall River Mills is during the late spring to early autumn season when the weather is warm, and the recreational opportunities are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fall River Mills

Do:

  • Respect the local tribes and learn about their traditions.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreational opportunities the town offers.
  • Try some farm-to-table cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or waterways.
  • Ignore posted signs and warnings on outdoor recreational activities.
  • Disrespect the local tribes’ traditions, beliefs, or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fall River Mills

Fall River Mills is a relatively safe town; however, visitors should take the necessary precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or fishing, to avoid accidents or injuries.

Health in Fall River Mills

Visitors should take necessary measures to protect themselves from the sun and bring plenty of water to stay hydrated when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Fall River Mills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fall River Mills

  • Redding Municipal Airport: Located about an hour’s drive from Fall River Mills.

Bus Stations Near Fall River Mills

  • Burney Bus Service: Provides service between Burney, California, and Klamath Falls, Oregon.

Other Options

Visitors may also rent a car or hire a private shuttle to reach Fall River Mills.

How to Get Around in Fall River Mills

The best way to get around Fall River Mills is by car. Many of the town’s outdoor recreational activities, including hiking and fishing, are not easily accessible on foot.

Where to Go Next from Fall River Mills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: America’s premier volcanic park, located less than an hour’s drive from Fall River Mills.
  • Redding, California: A vibrant city with many outdoor recreational opportunities, located about an hour’s drive from Fall River Mills.

Conclusion

Fall River Mills has much to offer both outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs. From scenic hikes to rich cultural history, this small town has something to offer for everyone. Come enjoy Fall River Mills and discover a gem hidden in the heart of Northern California.
