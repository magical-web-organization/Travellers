Introduction to Big Bend

Nestled in Shasta County, Shasta Cascades, Big Bend is a hidden gem in California, offering a much-needed break from the concrete jungle. The area has a rich history dating back to the 1800s, a fascinating culture, and breathtaking natural beauty. This comprehensive tour guide will help you discover the top things to do, places to visit, and where to stay, eat, and shop in Big Bend.

List of Places to Visit in Big Bend

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Crook Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the history of Fort Crook, one of California’s earliest military posts established in 1857.
  • Pioneer Cemetery: A historical cemetery with fascinating stories of pioneers and early settlers.
  • Little Country Church: An old-fashioned country church built-in 1896, still in use to this day.

Natural Attractions of Big Bend: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sacramento River Trail: A picturesque multi-use trail ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Lake Shasta Caverns: A fascinating cave system at the base of a 200-foot cliff that offers boat tours and visits to the caverns.
  • McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park: A state park with one of the most spectacular waterfalls in California, located in the Shasta Cascade region of the state.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Bend

  • Pacific Crest Trail: A 2,650-mile trail that runs from the US-Mexico border to Canada and passes through Big Bend.
  • Iron Mountain: A challenging 6.5-mile hike with a stunning view of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Big Bend: Activities and Tours

  • Whiskeytown Lake: A favorite spot for swimming, boating, and fishing among locals and tourists alike.
  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: A unique park where you can witness the volcanic activity of Northern California and explore the park’s rich flora and fauna.
  • Shasta Lake: A popular recreational spot that offers activities like fishing, boating, camping, and hiking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Big Bend

Day Activity
1 Explore Fort Crook Museum and Pioneer Cemetery.
2 Drive to McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park for a day hike and sunset-watching.
3 Visit Lassen Volcanic National Park to witness volcanic activity and explore the park’s rich flora and fauna.
4 Take a guided kayak tour of Whiskeytown Lake.
5 Relax and spend the day at Shasta Lake.
6 Visit the Lake Shasta Caverns followed by a scenic drive on the Sacramento River Trail.
7 Attend church at the Little Country Church and end your trip with a relaxing picnic.

Food and Drink Scene of Big Bend

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blackberry festival pie: This highly anticipated homemade pie combines fresh blackberries with a crumbly crust.
  • Tri-tip: A grilled beef dish that is a favorite among locals.

Local Beverages

  • Shasta Daisy: A refreshing cocktail made with vodka, triple sec, lemon juice, and soda.

Where to Stay in Big Bend: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Big Bend Lodge: A cozy lodge with rustic charm and an excellent restaurant and bar.
  • Baymont Inn & Suites Anderson: A great option for families traveling with children, offering a pool and free breakfast.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Red Bluff - South Redding Area: A modern hotel with a fitness center and a heated pool.

What to Buy in Big Bend: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted pottery: Big Bend has a rich tradition of creating beautiful pottery, and you’ll find plenty of local shops selling handmade pieces.
  • Jams and Jellies: Made from the freshest local fruit, the jams and jellies of Big Bend are a must-try.

Local Culture and Customs of Big Bend

The culture of Big Bend is influenced by its history, natural beauty, and diverse community. Attend church on Sunday at the Little Country Church, and explore the history of Fort Crook Museum, Pioneer Cemetery, and other historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Big Bend

The best time to visit Big Bend is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is sunny and warm, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Bend

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be respectful of nature and the environment.
  • Visit local museums and historical landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural habitats.
  • Smoke in non-smoking areas.
  • Ignore safety warnings and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Big Bend

Big Bend is a safe place for tourists, but it’s always essential to take precautions. Keep your valuables in a secure place, and don’t leave them unattended. Be cautious about hiking in unfamiliar terrain and always carry plenty of water and sunscreen.

Health in Big Bend

In case of a medical emergency, call 911. The nearest hospital is Shasta Regional Medical Center, which is around a 30-minute drive from Big Bend.

Getting to Big Bend: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Big Bend

  • Redding Municipal Airport: The closest airport to Big Bend, located around 16 miles away.
  • Chico Municipal Airport: Located around 70 miles away from Big Bend.

Bus Stations Near Big Bend

  • Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Redding, approximately 16 miles from Big Bend.
  • Siskiyou Transit and General Express: A regional bus service that operates in Shasta County.

Other Options

  • Car rental: The most convenient way to get around Big Bend is by car. Several car rental companies operate in the area.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Redding and other nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Big Bend

The best way to get around in Big Bend is by car. However, there are several bike rental companies in the area, and you can explore the place on bike as well.

Where to Go Next from Big Bend: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mount Shasta: A scenic 1-hour drive from Big Bend.
  • Redding: The largest city in Shasta County, located about 16 miles from Big Bend.
  • Lava Beds National Monument: A great place to visit if you are fascinated by volcanic activity, located around 70 miles from Big Bend.

Conclusion

Big Bend is a hidden gem in California, perfect for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and explore the natural beauty and history of California. Use this guide to plan your trip to Big Bend, and you are sure to have a memorable vacation.
