Introduction to McArthur

McArthur is a small town in Shasta County, located in the northeastern region of California. Known for its picturesque views and stunning landscapes, McArthur is a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

The town is situated in the heart of the Shasta Cascades, surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests, and pristine lakes. Visitors to McArthur can expect to find plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and boating, as well as a vibrant culture and fascinating history.

List of Places to Visit in McArthur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park: A must-see for anyone visiting McArthur, this state park boasts a 129-foot waterfall surrounded by lush greenery and vibrant wildlife. The park is also home to a rich history, with artifacts and exhibits dedicated to the Native American tribes that once inhabited the area.
  • Fort Crook Museum: Located in nearby Fall River Mills, this museum showcases the history of the Fort Crook Military Reservation and the local communities that sprang up around it. Visitors can explore exhibits on local wildlife, Native American culture, and the region’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of McArthur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ahjumawi Lava Springs State Park: This remote park is accessible only by boat, but it’s well worth the journey. Ahjumawi is home to a series of crystal-clear lakes and wetlands, as well as an abundance of wildlife, including black bears, bald eagles, and otters.
  • Baum Lake: A popular spot for fishing and boating, Baum Lake is surrounded by beautiful meadows and forests. Visitors may also catch a glimpse of the lake’s resident bald eagle pair.
  • Mount Shasta: Standing at over 14,000 feet, Mount Shasta is a defining feature of the Shasta Cascades. Visitors can hike or climb the mountain, or simply take in the stunning views from the base.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McArthur

  • Pacific Crest Trail: The Pacific Crest Trail runs through the heart of the Shasta Cascades, passing through McArthur and offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Pit River Falls Trail: This moderate hike takes visitors to the base of the Pit River Falls, a beautiful cascade surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Great Shasta Rail Trail: This trail follows the path of an old railway, winding through the forests and meadows of the Shasta Cascades and offering stunning views of Mount Shasta.

Best Things to Do in McArthur: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: McArthur is known for its abundance of trout, bass, and other fish species. Visitors can fish in any of the area’s lakes, rivers, or streams, or sign up for a guided fishing trip with one of the many local outfitters.
  • Boating: Many of McArthur’s lakes and rivers are perfect for boating and kayaking. Visitors can rent a boat or bring their own and explore the beautiful waters of the Shasta Cascades.
  • Wildlife Watching: Shasta Cascades is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including black bears, bald eagles, and rare plant and animal species. Visitors can explore the area’s state parks and wildlife reserves on guided tours or by themselves.

Week-Long Itinerary for McArthur

Day Activities
1 Visit McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park and take a hike to the waterfall.
2 Go fishing or boating on Baum Lake.
3 Take a guided wildlife watching tour in Ahjumawi Lava Springs State Park.

7 Take a trip to Lassen Volcanic National Park and explore the area’s volcanic landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of McArthur

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trout: Freshly caught trout can be found on many of the area’s menus, cooked in a variety of styles from grilled to smoked to pan-fried.
  • Wild Game: McArthur is also known for its wild game offerings, including venison, elk, and wild boar.
  • Huckleberries: These small, tart berries are native to the area and can be found in a variety of dishes, from desserts to sauces to savory entrees.

Local Beverages

  • Lassen Ale Works: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers brewed on-site, as well as a cozy taproom perfect for relaxing after a long day of hiking or fishing.
  • Shasta Starr Ranch Winery: This family-owned winery is located in nearby Dunsmuir and offers tastings of their award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in McArthur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • McArthur Burney Falls State Park Campground: This rustic campground is situated within the state park and offers opportunities for tent and RV camping.
  • Green Gables Motel & Suites: This cozy motel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a communal BBQ area and free Wi-Fi.
  • Rising Sun Fit for Life Retreat: This wellness retreat offers cabins, studios and a guest suite with yoga classes, workshops, guided hikes, an organic garden, a meditation garden and access to the nearby nature preserve.

What to Buy in McArthur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Products: Visitors can find a variety of huckleberry jams, syrups, and candies in local gift shops and markets.
  • Native American Crafts: Many local shops and markets offer handmade jewelry, pottery, and other crafts made by local Native American artists.

Local Culture and Customs of McArthur

McArthur is home to a vibrant rural culture, with strong ties to the area’s Native American heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting Native American sites or participating in guided tours.

Best Time to Visit McArthur

The best time to visit McArthur is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors should be prepared for crowds during peak season and should book accommodations and activities well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of McArthur

Do:

  • Do bring appropriate clothing and gear for hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Do book accommodations and activities well in advance, particularly during peak season.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disturb wildlife or damage natural habitats.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind.
  • Don’t trespass on private property or Native American sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in McArthur

McArthur is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. This includes keeping valuables locked up and out of sight, particularly in vehicles or campsites.

Health in McArthur

Visitors to McArthur should take precautions to avoid dehydration and heat exhaustion, particularly during the summer months. It’s also important to take precautions against ticks and other pests, as some may carry diseases such as Lyme disease.

Getting Around in McArthur

Visitors will need to have a car to fully explore the area as public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from McArthur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: Located just two hours from McArthur, this national park is home to an active volcano, hot springs, and unique geological landscapes.
  • Redding, CA: Just an hour’s drive from McArthur, Redding is a vibrant city with plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Oregon Caves National Monument: A three-hour drive from McArthur, this national monument offers guided tours of a unique underground cave system.

Conclusion

McArthur is a small town with a big personality, boasting stunning natural beauty, a fascinating history, and plenty of outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in hiking, fishing, or simply taking in the scenery, McArthur is a destination you won’t want to miss.
