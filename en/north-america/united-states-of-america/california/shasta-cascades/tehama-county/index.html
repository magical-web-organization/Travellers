Tehama County is a hidden gem tucked away in the northern part of California’s scenic Shasta Cascades region. The county is blessed with a diverse range of attractions, from museums and historical sites to natural wonders, hiking trails, and more. If you’re planning to visit Tehama County, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Tehama County

Tehama County is located in the northern part of California, in the United States of America. The county is bordered by Shasta County to the north, Glenn County to the south, and Butte and Plumas Counties to the east. The county seat is Red Bluff, a small city with a rich history. Tehama County is a primarily rural area, known for its natural beauty, friendly people, and relaxed way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Tehama County

Here is a list of the top places to visit and things to do in Tehama County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tehama County Museum: The museum is located in a historic home that was built in the 1800s. It features exhibits on the county’s history, including displays on Native American culture, the gold rush era, and more.
  • William B. Ide Adobe State Historic Park: This park features a historic adobe home that belonged to William Ide, a California pioneer. Visitors can tour the home and learn about life in the mid-1800s.
  • Westwood Museum: The museum is dedicated to the history of Westwood, a small town in Tehama County. It features exhibits on the town’s logging and mining industries, as well as displays on the local Native American tribes.

Natural Attractions of Tehama County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: This park is located just north of Tehama County and is a must-see destination for outdoor enthusiasts. It features hiking trails, scenic drives, and stunning views of the park’s active volcano.
  • Sacramento River Bend Area: This area is located near Red Bluff and features miles of hiking and equestrian trails, as well as fishing opportunities and scenic views of the Sacramento River.
  • Tehama Trail: This scenic driving route takes visitors through the heart of Tehama County, highlighting the region’s natural beauty and agricultural heritage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tehama County

Tehama County is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • South Yolla Bolly Mountains: This mountain range is located in the Mendocino National Forest and offers challenging hiking trails and scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Sacramento River Trail: This trail follows the Sacramento River for 11 miles, providing stunning views of the river and its surrounding wilderness.
  • Red Bluff Riverwalk Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is located in the heart of Red Bluff and offers a pleasant stroll along the Sacramento River.

Best Things to Do in Tehama County: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Tehama County is part of the Northern California wine region and features several wineries and tasting rooms. Some of the top wineries in the area include New Clairvaux Vineyard and Tuscan Ridge Estate Winery.
  • Fishing: Tehama County is home to several excellent fishing spots, including the Sacramento River, Deer Creek, and Battle Creek. Visitors can fish for trout, salmon, steelhead, and more.
  • White Water Rafting: The Sacramento River and nearby rivers are popular destinations for white water rafting. Visitors can book guided tours with local outfitters.

Food and Drink Scene of Tehama County

Tehama County is a food lover’s paradise, with a variety of cuisines and restaurants to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef Tri-Tip: This is a local specialty, consisting of a juicy, tender cut of beef that’s grilled to perfection.
  • Tehama Trail Mix: This snack mix is made with nuts, dried fruits, and chocolate chips, and is perfect for long hikes and road trips.
  • Sourdough Bread: Tehama County is known for its delicious sourdough bread, which is made using a local sourdough culture.

Local Beverages

  • Red Bluff Ale: This beer is brewed by the California Brewing Company in nearby Chico and is a popular choice among locals.
  • Wine: Tehama County is home to several wineries and tasting rooms, offering a variety of reds, whites, and rosés.
  • Coffee: Tehama County has several excellent coffee shops, offering everything from traditional drip coffee to artisanal lattes and cappuccinos.

Where to Stay in Tehama County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Tehama County, ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious. Here are some suggestions:

  • Best Western Antelope Inn: This hotel is located in Red Bluff and offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Paskenta Nomlaki Foundation: This Native American-owned resort offers yurts and camping sites, as well as cultural programming and outdoor activities.
  • Lassen Mineral Lodge: This lodge is located near Lassen Volcanic National Park and offers cozy cabins and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Tehama County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some options:

  • Olive Oil: Tehama County is home to several olive growers and producers, offering a variety of high-quality oils.
  • Almonds: Tehama County is a major almond producer, and visitors can find delicious roasted and flavored almonds at local markets.
  • Handmade Crafts: Tehama County has a thriving artisan community, offering handmade crafts and products such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Tehama County

Tehama County is a welcoming and friendly place, where people value community, nature, and tradition. The area has a rich Native American heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Tehama County

The best time to visit Tehama County is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and dry, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tehama County

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment and practice Leave No Trace principles.
  • Explore the natural attractions and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or heritage.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Ignore safety precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tehama County

Tehama County is a safe and welcoming place, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking valuables in their cars and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Tehama County

Visitors should take care of their health when visiting Tehama County, including staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen, and avoiding overheating. Visitors should also be aware of any local health concerns, such as tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Tehama County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tehama County can be reached by car or plane. The nearest airports are Redding Municipal Airport and Sacramento International Airport. Once in the county, visitors can get around by car, bike, or on foot.

Airports Near Tehama County

  • Redding Municipal Airport (RDD)
  • Sacramento International Airport (SMF)

Bus Stations Near Tehama County

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Redding
  • Amtrak Station in Chico

Other Options

  • Rental Cars
  • Taxi Services

How to Get Around in Tehama County

The best way to get around Tehama County is by car, as the area is largely rural and public transportation options are limited.

Where to Go Next from Tehama County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for other destinations to visit near Tehama County, here are some options:

  • Redding: This city is located just north of Tehama County and offers attractions such as the Sundial Bridge and Turtle Bay Exploration Park.
  • Mount Shasta: This towering mountain is located to the east of Tehama County and is a popular destination for hiking and skiing.
  • Bidwell Park: This park is located in nearby Chico and offers hiking trails, swimming holes, and picnic areas.

Conclusion

Tehama County is a hidden gem in the Shasta Cascades region, offering a blend of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, food lover, or history buff, Tehama County has something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to this wonderful destination!
