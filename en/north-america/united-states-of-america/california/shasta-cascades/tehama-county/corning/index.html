Introduction to Corning

Located in Tehama County, in Shasta Cascades, in California, in the United States of America, in North America, Corning is a small town that is home to many interesting attractions, breathtaking natural beauty, and a rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by rugged mountain ranges, scenic lakes, and rolling hills dotted with vineyards, Corning is a perfect destination for adventure seekers, nature enthusiasts, and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Corning

Here are some of the top attractions to visit when in Corning:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Corning Museum of Glass: A must-visit for all art lovers, the Corning Museum of Glass, is home to over 50,000 glass objects from all over the world. The museum showcases a variety of glass artifacts, artwork, and demonstrations.

  • Bell-Carter Olive Factory: Discover the goodness of olives and olive oil with an exciting tour of the largest olive factory in the world. Learn about the history of olive cultivation in California and see how the olives are picked, processed and sent out to the world.

  • Lucero Olive Oil: Another olive factory that you must check out is the Lucero Olive Oil. Enjoy free samples of olive oil and learn about the olive varieties cultivated in the region.

  • The Vina Museum: Take a step back in time to the days of Spanish missions and explorers at the Vina Museum. Learn about the history of the town, the Catholic missions, and the life of the pioneers who settled here.

Natural Attractions of Corning: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Woodson Bridge State Recreation Area: A 350-acre park, it offers a variety of outdoor activities like boating, fishing, hiking and camping. The park is a haven for bird watchers with sightings of Bald Eagles, ospreys, and nearly 200 other bird species.

  • Sacramento River Discovery Center: The center’s main attraction is a visitor center showcasing the importance of the Sacramento River to the region’s ecology and economics.

  • Black Butte Lake: Get the perfect view of the Siskiyou Mountains from Black Butte Lake. The park offers camping, swimming, boating, and hiking opportunities that are sure to satisfy any adventurer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corning

Corning is known for its beautiful and endless landscapes. Here are some hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Sacramento River Bend Trail: A 9-mile hiking and biking trail that wends it way around the Sacramento River Bend resulting in some stunning views

  • Tuscan Ridge Trail: A 3.5-mile hike through the foothills lined with grapevines. You will enjoy the scenic beauty of the area while exploring the vineyards.

  • Black Butte Trail: A 6-mile hike to Black Butte Summit leads you through open meadows and shady forest groves that culminate in breathtaking views of the mountains and the valley.

Best Things to Do in Corning: Activities and Tours

There’s so much to do in Corning! Here are some of the best activities:

  • Wine Tasting in Tehama Trail: Corning has many vineyards and wineries that offer tours and wine tastings. Try the local varietals and learn about the regional wine making process from experts.

  • Olive Oil Tasting and Appreciation: Visit olive oil factories and enjoy free samples of olive oil. Try different oils and flavors and learn about the history of olive cultivation in California.

  • Explore Mount Shasta and Lassen Volcanic National Park: Take a day trip to see two of the most magnificent natural attractions in the region and experience nature at its best.

Week-Long Itinerary for Corning

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long visit to Corning:

  • Day 1: Visit the Corning Museum of Glass and take a guided tour of the glass factory.

  • Day 2: Explore the Sacramento River Bend Trail and appreciate the beauty of the Sacramento River.

  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Mount Shasta and Lassen Volcanic National Park.

  • Day 4: Discover the art and craft of olive cultivation and oil production at the Bell-Carter Olive Factory and the Lucero Olive Oil.

  • Day 5: Take a bike tour to Tuscan Ridge Trail, through vineyards that are as old, as the gold rush.

  • Day 6: Visit the Vina Museum, and learn about the history and culture of Corning’s Spanish mission era.

  • Day 7: Spend time at the Woodson Bridge State Recreation Area camping, fishing, or boating.

Food and Drink Scene of Corning

Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Corning:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Olive Tapenade: Made from Corning’s locally grown olives, the olive Tapenade is a perfect appetizer or topping that is full of flavor.

  • BBQ Duck: Corning’s BBQ Duck is a local favorite. The duck is cooked to perfection and served with a side of savory beans.

  • Pistachio Pesto: The pistachio pesto is one of Corning’s favorite dishes. It is prepared with freshly peeled pistachios blended with olive oil and other local spices to create a flavorful sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sangria made from local wines: Made with a variety of local wines and fresh fruit, the sangria is a perfect drink to enjoy on a sunny afternoon.

  • Olive oil and Balsamic vinegar tasting: taste the different olive and balsamic flavors at the Lucero Olive Oil and enjoy the aromatic intensity of the product.

Where to Stay in Corning: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Corning has a range of accommodation options for all budgets, here are some of the top picks:

  • The Lodge at Rolling Hills Casino & Resort: one of the best resorts in the region, the lodge offers a wide range of amenities including swimming pools, fitness centers, and a spa.

  • Sunset Inn: This motel is budget-friendly and is located close to the Corning Museum of Glass with clean, comfortable and well-maintained rooms.

  • Olive City Bed and Breakfast: A charming bed & breakfast with a warm atmosphere and a historic ambiance. It is situated in the center of Corning and features 4 stylish and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Corning: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Corning without taking some of the local products home with you. Here are some of the best souvenirs to pick up:

  • Olives and Olive Oil: Make sure to stock up on the local olive oil, which has a rich, fruity flavor that is unique to the region.

  • Glass Art: Local glass artworks are often inspired by the natural beauty, and culture of Corning – perfect souvenirs to decorate your home with.

  • Wine: The region’s wine is renowned for its rich and fruity flavors, so make sure to pick up a bottle or two during your visit to Corning.

Local Culture and Customs of Corning

Corning’s local culture and customs have been profoundly influenced by its Spanish and Native American heritage and its diverse agricultural industry. The town is known for the warmth and hospitality of its people, the richness of its food, and its commitment to preserving its natural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Corning

The best time to visit Corning is between April to June and September to November when the weather is mild, making it perfect for outdoor activities. The winter season in Corning can be quite harsh, while summers can get exceedingly hot.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when traveling to Corning:

Safety in Corning

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure
  • When hiking or biking, carry a map and plenty of water.

Health in Corning

  • When exploring natural attractions, protect yourself from the sun with sunscreen, a hat, and protective clothing.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking lots of water, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Corning: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to get to Corning:

  • Airports Near Corning: The closest airport is the Redding Municipal Airport, which is about 45 minutes away. The Sacramento International Airport and the San Francisco International Airport are both about a 3-hour drive away.

  • Bus Stations Near Corning: Greyhound bus station in Red Bluff is the nearest bus station located just 20 minutes to Corning.

  • Other Options: You can also rent a car or take a taxi to get to Corning.

How to Get Around in Corning

The best way to get around Corning is to rent a car, as public transportation is limited. You can also opt for bike rental, or explore the town on foot.

Where to Go Next from Corning: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that you can add to your travel itinerary:

  • Chico, California: Known for its vibrant culture, Chico is a charming town that offers a plethora of activities for travelers.

  • Mount Shasta, California: A breathtaking natural attraction, offering adventure and outdoor activities like hiking, skiing, and fishing.

  • Redding, California: Located just 20-minutes away, Redding is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Explore the nearby mountains, kayaking, fishing, and take a stroll over the Sundial Bridge.

Conclusion

Corning promises to deliver a unique experience that combines the best of outdoor adventures, cultural enrichment, and natural beauty. Plan your visit to this charming town, and discover the historical landmarks, trails, and cuisine that make Corning so special.
