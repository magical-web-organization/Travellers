Introduction to Klamath River

Located in Siskiyou County, in the Shasta Cascades region of California, the Klamath River flows for almost 257 miles, beginning in Oregon and flowing down to the Pacific Ocean. The Klamath River is a paradise for adventurous travelers who are looking for a perfect outdoor getaway along with scenic beauty, wildlife, and rich cultural history.

List of Places to Visit in Klamath River

Take a look at our list of top attractions in Klamath River:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yurok Indian Reservation: Explore the Yurok Tribe and its traditions and customs at the reservation. It’s the largest reservation in California and also houses the Native American Indian or Klamath River Museum.
  • Fort Jones Museum: Discover the history of early 19th-century California at the Fort Jones Museum, which includes many artifacts related to the first settlers and gold rush era.
  • Siskiyou County Museum: Located in Yreka, this museum showcases the history and culture of the region, including the Native American tribes, gold miners, and pioneers.

Natural Attractions of Klamath River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Klamath National Forest: This enormous forest is spread over 1.7 million acres and offers numerous outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, camping, and nature watching.
  • Redwood National and State Parks: Explore the scenic park on the coast of Northern California, which features the world’s tallest trees, rocky beaches, and wildlife such as elk and sea lions.
  • Salmon River: This river is perfect for rafting, kayaking, and fishing. The stunning canyon views make it a popular spot for camping too.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Klamath River

Klamath River offers countless hiking trails and biking routes, some of which include:

  • Sawyers Bar Road: This 8.8-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the Marble Mountains, Trinity Alps Wilderness, and the Klamath Mountains.
  • Grider Creek Trail: This moderately challenging trail is 16.5 miles long and features beautiful views of Grider Creek and its surrounding forests.
  • Pistol River Trail: This 6.2-mile trail is located within a beautiful old-growth forest, and hikers can spot many different bird species along the route.

Best Things to Do in Klamath River: Activities and Tours

Some of the most exciting things to do in Klamath River include:

  • Rafting and Kayaking Tour: Take a guided tour down the scenic Klamath River in a kayak, raft or canoe.
  • Fishing Tour: Klamath River is an excellent destination for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Horseback Riding: Enjoy a guided horseback riding tour through the beautiful forests and hills of Klamath River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Klamath River

Day 1-2: Explore the Yurok Tribe and Klamath River Museum, and embark on a kayaking tour of the Klamath River.

Day 3-4: Journey to the Klamath National Forest and spend time hiking and camping.

Day 5-6: Visit the Redwood National and State Parks and take in the stunning views.

Day 7: Take a fishing tour on the Salmon River and end the day at a local brewery or winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Klamath River

Klamath River has some amazing local food and drinks to try on your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salmon: Klamath River is famous for its fresh salmon dishes. Must try dishes include smoked salmon and salmon burgers.
  • Berries: Klamath River is known for its berries like blackberries, blueberries, and raspberries. Try them in the form of jams, pies, and pastries.

Local Beverages

  • California Wines: Savor local wines in vineyards around the area. Some of the most famous ones include the Ashland Vineyard and Paschal Winery and Vineyard.
  • Craft Beer: Hang out at local breweries like Mt. Shasta Brewing Company and Etna Brewing Company for a taste of craft beer.

Where to Stay in Klamath River: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Klamath River offers plenty of accommodations for every traveler’s taste, whether you prefer luxury hotels or budget guesthouses:

  • Klamath River RV Park: This park offers full-service RV camping, cabins, and tent sites.
  • Jefferson Inn: A historic inn located in Yreka with all modern amenities for a comfortable stay.
  • Rainbow Lodge: A cozy and affordable lodge located near the Klamath River.

What to Buy in Klamath River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Klamath River back home with you with these local products:

  • Native American Artifacts: Klamath River is home to many Native American tribes; take a piece of their history and culture back with you in the form of handmade jewelry, pottery, and weavings.
  • Local Wines: Pick up a bottle of the famous Ashland Vineyard or Paschal Winery and Vineyard’s wine.
  • Wild Berries: Buy some berry jams, jellies, or syrups to take a piece of Klamath River back home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Klamath River

Klamath River is home to several Native American tribes, including the Yurok, Karuk, and Hoopa Valley tribes. Respect their culture and traditions, and educate yourself before visiting about their customs and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Klamath River

The best time to visit Klamath River is from May to September when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Klamath River

Do:

  • Respect the nature and wildlife of the area
  • Hire a guide or local company for outdoor activities like rafting and kayaking
  • Be respectful of Native Americans’ traditions and customs

Don’t:

  • Leave a trail of garbage behind
  • Leave fires unattended
  • Destroy natural habitats or remove natural elements like stones or plants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Klamath River

Klamath River is generally safe. However, always exercise caution while hiking or camping, and follow necessary safety guidelines for outdoor activities.

Health in Klamath River

Be prepared with good quality hiking gear since the terrain can be rugged and uneven. Carry ample drinking water and sunscreen at all times to protect yourself from the harsh sun.

Getting to Klamath River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Klamath River

  • Rogue Valley International-Medford Airport in Oregon (90 miles)
  • Eureka-Arcata (ACV) (118 miles)
  • Redding Municipal Airport (90 miles)

Bus Stations Near Klamath River

  • Greyhound Bus Lines in Medford (90 miles)
  • Local Transfer Service in Happy Camp

Other Options

  • Rent-a-Car: Car rental services are available in nearby cities like Redding, Ashland, and Yreka.

How to Get Around in Klamath River

Car rental is the best way to get around Klamath River. Taxis are available in the nearby cities, although prices can be steep.

Where to Go Next from Klamath River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations for an extension of your trip from Klamath River include:

  • Crater Lake National Park: Located in Oregon, this stunning park has a lake formed within the remains of an ancient volcano.
  • Mount Shasta: This majestic mountain located in Northern California is a heavenly spot for trekkers and hikers.
  • Lava Beds National Monument: This remote park is filled with jagged lava formations and caves to explore.

Conclusion

Klamath River is a nature lover’s paradise, with its vast lush forests, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking scenic beauty. With so many outdoor activities, museums, restaurants, and unforgettable attractions, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a unique experience in the great outdoors of California.
