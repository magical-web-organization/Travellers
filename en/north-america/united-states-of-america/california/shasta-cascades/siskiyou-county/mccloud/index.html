Introduction to McCloud

Located in the picturesque Shasta Cascades region of Northern California, McCloud is a charming mountain town that offers visitors a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. This historic community is steeped in natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, making it an ideal destination for anyone looking to unwind and explore the great outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in McCloud

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • McCloud Heritage Junction Museum - Offering a fascinating glimpse into the town’s history, this museum is housed in a restored train depot and features exhibits showcasing the region’s railroad, logging, and mining industries.
  • McCloud Hotel - Built in 1915 and impeccably restored, this historic hotel is a must-visit for architecture and history buffs alike.
  • The McCloud River Mercantile Building - Explore the town’s rich commercial history and unique architecture in this iconic building which is now used as a shopping center.

Natural Attractions of McCloud: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • McCloud Falls - Comprised of three magnificent waterfalls nestled in the heart of the Shasta-Trinity National Forest, the McCloud Falls are a breathtaking sight to behold.
  • Mount Shasta - Standing at a majestic 14,162 feet, Mount Shasta is a must-visit destination for hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Squaw Valley Creek - Winding through lush forests, Squaw Valley Creek is home to rainbow and brown trout, making it a popular spot for fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McCloud

Hiking Trails

  • McCloud River Trail - This 3.5-mile round-trip trail weaves through beautiful forests and past the stunning McCloud Falls.
  • Castle Crags State Park - This popular hiking spot boasts a diverse range of trails, including the challenging Castle Dome Trail.

Biking Routes

  • Gateway Trail - Located just 5 minutes from downtown McCloud, this trail offers stunning views of Mount Shasta and the surrounding forest.
  • Great Shasta Rail Trail - Stretching from McCloud to Burney, this 80-mile trail winds through some of the most beautiful scenery in Northern California.

Best Things to Do in McCloud: Activities and Tours

  • McCloud Wine & Spirits Tasting Room - Take a tour or try samples of local craft beer and wine at this popular tasting room.
  • Fly Fishing - With an abundance of trout-filled streams and rivers, McCloud is the perfect destination for anglers.
  • McCloud Golf Course - This well-maintained and challenging 9-hole course offers stunning views of Mount Shasta.

Week-Long Itinerary for McCloud

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore McCloud’s historic downtown, visit the McCloud Heritage Junction Museum, stay at the McCloud Hotel.
Day 2 Hike the McCloud River Trail and explore the McCloud Falls.
Day 3 Take a scenic drive to Mount Shasta and hike the Castle Dome Trail.
Day 4 Go trout fishing in Squaw Valley Creek.
Day 5 Take a biking tour of the Great Shasta Rail Trail.
Day 6 Enjoy a round of golf at the McCloud Golf Course.
Day 7 Relax and unwind at the McCloud Wine & Spirits Tasting Room.

Food and Drink Scene of McCloud

Must-Try Dishes

  • McCloud River Breakfast Burrito - A hearty breakfast wrap packed with spicy sausage, scrambled eggs, cheese, and vegetables.
  • McCloud Burger - A local favorite, this juicy burger is made with locally-sourced beef and served on a fresh bun.

Local Beverages

  • McCloud River IIPA - Produced by the McCloud River Brewery, this crisp IPA is the perfect thirst-quencher after a day of exploring the great outdoors.
  • Shasta Daisy Wine - Produced locally, this crisp and refreshing white wine is made with grapes grown in the surrounding region.

Where to Stay in McCloud: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • McCloud Hotel - An impeccably restored historic hotel boasting elegant rooms and unparalleled mountain views.
  • McCloud RV Resort - Offering stunning natural scenery and plenty of outdoor activities, this RV resort is perfect for nature lovers.
  • McCloud River Mercantile Hotel - Stay at this unique boutique hotel housed within the iconic McCloud River Mercantile Building.

What to Buy in McCloud: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • McCloud Fly Fishing Gear - Whether you’re an avid angler or just looking for a unique souvenir, McCloud’s local fly fishing gear is a must-buy.
  • Handmade Crafts and Jewelry - From intricate beadwork to hand-carved wooden sculptures, McCloud’s local artists create some of the most stunning crafts and jewelry in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of McCloud

McCloud prides itself on its rich history and unique cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s historic downtown, which has been impeccably restored to its original 19th-century charm.

Best Time to Visit McCloud

The best time to visit McCloud is in the spring and summer, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors should be aware that temperatures can drop quickly in the evening, so warm clothing is a must-pack item.

Do’s and Don’ts of McCloud

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and local wildlife.
  • Take advantage of all the outdoor activities available in the area.
  • Support local businesses and artisans by buying their handmade crafts and products.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.
  • Disturb or harm local wildlife.
  • Ignore posted signs and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in McCloud

McCloud is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should exercise caution and common sense, particularly when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in McCloud

Visitors should be aware that the high altitude of McCloud can lead to altitude sickness, which can cause headaches, nausea, and shortness of breath. Taking time to acclimate to the altitude and drinking plenty of water can help prevent these symptoms.

Getting to McCloud: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near McCloud

The nearest airport to McCloud is the Redding Municipal Airport, which is located approximately 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near McCloud

Visitors can take the Greyhound Bus to the nearby town of Mount Shasta, which is located just 10 miles from McCloud.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to McCloud via the scenic Interstate 5 corridor, which offers stunning views of Mount Shasta and the surrounding landscapes.

How to Get Around in McCloud

McCloud is easily walkable, but visitors should be prepared for steep hills. For those looking to venture further afield, bike rentals are available in town.

Where to Go Next from McCloud: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mount Shasta - Just a short drive from McCloud, Mount Shasta is a must-visit destination for hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Burney Falls - Located approximately 50 miles east of McCloud, these stunning waterfalls are a must-visit destination for nature lovers.
  • Lassen Volcanic National Park - Located approximately 75 miles from McCloud, this beautiful national park is home to scenic drives, breathtaking vistas, and unique geological formations.

Conclusion

McCloud is a truly unique destination that offers visitors the opportunity to experience the natural beauty and rich cultural heritage of Northern California. With its stunning natural landscapes, unique historical sites, and vibrant local culture, it’s no wonder that McCloud is quickly becoming a must-visit destination for both domestic and international travelers alike!
