Introduction to Dunsmuir

Nestled in the heart of Shasta Cascades region of California, Dunsmuir is a charming town that offers a plethora of activities and attractions for all kinds of travelers. Rich in history and culture, the town is surrounded by scenic natural beauty, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. This travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Dunsmuir.

List of Places to Visit in Dunsmuir

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Siskiyou Arts Museum: A small yet fascinating museum that showcases the works of local artists and hosts various cultural events throughout the year.
  • Dunsmuir Railroad Depot Historical Society: A museum dedicated to the town’s rich railroad history, featuring exhibits, artifacts, and restored train cars.
  • Mossbrae Falls: A stunning waterfall located on the Sacramento River, which is considered a sacred site to the local Native American tribes.

Natural Attractions of Dunsmuir: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Castle Crags State Park: A popular destination for hiking, camping, rock climbing, and fishing with stunning mountain views.
  • Mount Shasta: A majestic mountain that rises over 14,000 feet above sea level, perfect for hiking and skiing.
  • McCloud River Falls: A series of three waterfalls located just outside of Dunsmuir, surrounded by forests and offering fantastic hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dunsmuir

  • Box Canyon Dam: A trail that takes you to a historic dam, offering breathtaking views of the river and the surrounding mountains, perfect for biking or hiking.
  • Dunsmuir Botanical Gardens: A beautiful garden with a variety of plants and flowers, with several trails for walking or jogging.
  • Squaw Valley Creek Trail: A scenic trail that follows the Squaw Valley Creek, surrounded by lush forests, perfect for hiking or mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Dunsmuir: Activities and Tours

  • Fly-Fishing: Dunsmuir is famous for its trout fishing, with several tour operators offering guided fly-fishing tours.
  • Rafting: The Sacramento River offers exciting rafting opportunities for all levels of adventurers.
  • Climbing: Castle Crags State Park is a popular spot for rock climbing, with various trails and climbing routes available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dunsmuir

Day 1 Explore Castle Crags State Park
Day 2 Visit Mossbrae Falls and Dunsmuir Railroad Depot Historical Society
Day 3 Hike to Squaw Valley Creek Trail
Day 4 Go fly-fishing on the Sacramento River
Day 5 Visit Mount Shasta and McCloud River Falls
Day 6 Take a rafting adventure on the Sacramento River
Day 7 Relax at the Dunsmuir Botanical Gardens

Food and Drink Scene of Dunsmuir

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trout: Freshly caught trout is a local specialty, and several restaurants serve it in various delicious ways.
  • Burgers: Several local restaurants serve mouth-watering burgers made from locally sourced beef.
  • Ice Cream: Dunsmuir is also famous for its homemade ice cream, made with locally produced fruits and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Dunsmuir has several local breweries that serve some of the best craft beer in California.
  • Wine: Several wineries and vineyards in the nearby areas offer wine tasting and tours.

Where to Stay in Dunsmuir: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Railroad Park Resort: A unique lodging option consisting of renovated vintage train cars and cabins.
  • Mount Shasta Resort: A luxury resort offering scenic views of Mount Shasta and various amenities, including a golf course and spa.
  • Motel Dunsmuir: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of the town, offering comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Dunsmuir: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: There are several stores in Dunsmuir that offer handmade crafts made by local artisans.
  • Local Wine: Several wineries in the area offer bottles of locally produced wine that make perfect gifts for friends and family.
  • Trout Art: Local artists sell beautiful trout-themed artwork, including paintings and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Dunsmuir

Dunsmuir has a small-town culture that values community and nature. The town is known for its involvement in preserving its rich railroad history and its support for the arts and local businesses. Outdoor activities, like hiking and fishing, are favorite pastimes of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Dunsmuir

The best time to visit Dunsmuir is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are at their prime. Winter is also an excellent time to visit, with plenty of opportunities for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dunsmuir

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and nature.
  • Enjoy the local art and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local flora and fauna.
  • Ignore safety warnings while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dunsmuir

Dunsmuir is generally a safe place to visit. However, it is advised to take safety precautions while hiking or participating in outdoor activities like rafting, fishing, and climbing.

Health in Dunsmuir

Medical facilities are available in nearby cities like Mount Shasta and Redding. It is advised to bring necessary medications and first aid kits while participating in outdoor activities.
