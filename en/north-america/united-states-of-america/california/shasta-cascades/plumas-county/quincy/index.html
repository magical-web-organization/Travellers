Introduction to Quincy

Nestled in the heart of the Shasta Cascades region of California, the charming town of Quincy is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and small-town hospitality. Surrounded by pristine forests, sparkling lakes, and majestic mountains, Quincy is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking, biking, fishing, and camping. The town itself is steeped in history, with a vibrant arts scene and a variety of cultural attractions that showcase the region’s rich heritage. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing weekend getaway or an action-packed adventure, Quincy has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Quincy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plumas County Museum: Discover the rich history of Plumas County and Quincy through fascinating exhibits showcasing Native American artifacts, mining equipment, and other historical objects.
  • Main Street: Stroll along Quincy’s historic Main Street which features a variety of quaint shops, restaurants, and galleries housed in beautifully preserved Victorian buildings.
  • Quincy Library and Courthouse: Visit the historic Quincy Library and Courthouse, which was built in 1918 and now serves as a museum and cultural center.

Natural Attractions of Quincy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plumas National Forest: Explore the stunning natural beauty of the Plumas National Forest, which boasts over 1.2 million acres of forested land, lakes, and rivers perfect for camping, hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Bucks Lake: Enjoy swimming, boating, fishing, and kayaking on the crystal clear waters of Bucks Lake, one of the most picturesque lakes in the region.
  • Feather River Canyon: Take in the breathtaking views of the Feather River Canyon, a scenic drive that winds through the rugged canyon walls and offers spectacular views of the river and surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quincy

  • Pacific Crest Trail: Hike a portion of the legendary Pacific Crest Trail, which stretches 2,650 miles from Mexico to Canada and passes through the Plumas National Forest.
  • Spanish Creek Trail: Take a leisurely hike through the Spanish Creek Trail, a beautiful 5-mile trail that leads to Spanish Falls.
  • Mount Hough Trail: Bike or hike to the top of Mount Hough, which offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Quincy: Activities and Tours

  • Quincy Farmers Market: Visit the Quincy Farmers Market, held every Thursday from June to September, to savor fresh produce, locally baked goods, and handmade arts and crafts.
  • Plumas County Fair: Attend the Plumas County Fair, held every August, to enjoy live music, carnival rides, and agricultural exhibits.
  • Butterfly Valley Botanical Area: Take a guided tour of the Butterfly Valley Botanical Area, a rare high-altitude butterfly habitat that is home to dozens of rare and endangered plant and animal species.

Food and Drink Scene of Quincy

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tri-Tip Sandwich: A local favorite, the tri-tip sandwich is made with juicy beef sirloin, grilled to perfection and served on a soft roll with a side of barbecue sauce.
  • Lumberjack Burger: Sink your teeth into the satisfying Lumberjack Burger, a hearty burger with bacon, avocado, onion rings, and a delicious sauce.
  • Tahoe Blue Martini: Sip on a refreshing Tahoe Blue Martini, made with locally distilled Tahoe Blue Vodka, blue curacao, and a splash of lemon juice.

Local Beverages

  • Knotty Pine Brewery: Savor a pint of ale or lager at Knotty Pine Brewery, a local brewery that brews its beer on-site using only the freshest ingredients.
  • Quincy Natural Foods: Quench your thirst with a cold-pressed juice or smoothie from Quincy Natural Foods, a health food store that offers a variety of organic and locally sourced drinks.

Where to Stay in Quincy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pioneer RV Park: Nestled in a peaceful forest setting, Pioneer RV Park offers RV sites with full hookups, tent sites, and cozy cabins.
  • Quincy Courtyard Suites: Stay in the heart of Quincy at the Quincy Courtyard Suites, a charming inn that offers spacious guest rooms with modern amenities.
  • Greenhorn Creek Guest Ranch: Get away from it all at the Greenhorn Creek Guest Ranch, a rustic ranch that offers cozy cabins, horseback riding, and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Quincy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Plumas County Honey: Bring home a jar of sweet and delicious Plumas County honey, made from locally sourced bees.
  • Handmade Crafts: Browse the unique handmade crafts at the Quincy Farmers Market, where you’ll find everything from jewelry to pottery to woven baskets.
  • Tahoe Blue Vodka: Pick up a bottle of locally distilled Tahoe Blue Vodka, made with pure alpine water and handcrafted in small batches.

Local Culture and Customs of Quincy

Quincy is a small town with a big heart, known for its friendly locals, vibrant arts scene, and rich heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the natural environment and to engage with the local community by attending events, visiting local businesses, and learning about the region’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Quincy

The best time to visit Quincy is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and dry and outdoor activities are plentiful. However, fall is also a great time to visit, as the leaves change colors and the local farms offer apple picking and other seasonal activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quincy

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of the Plumas National Forest.
  • Do respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Do engage with the local community by attending events and supporting local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t disturb wildlife or their natural habitats.
  • Don’t ignore the region’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Quincy

Quincy is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. Visitors should take standard precautions such as locking their cars and securing their valuables, but are generally free to explore and enjoy the town without worry.

Health in Quincy

Quincy has a small hospital and several doctors and clinics, but visitors with complex medical needs may need to go to a larger city such as Reno or Sacramento for treatment. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of altitude sickness and dehydration when hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities.

Getting to Quincy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Quincy

  • Reno-Tahoe International Airport (RNO): 90 miles
  • Sacramento International Airport (SMF): 145 miles

Bus Stations Near Quincy

  • Quincy Junction: Serves Greyhound and Amtrak Thruway Bus Services.
  • Oroville Bus Depot: Serves Greyhound and other bus lines.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several major car rental companies have offices at Reno-Tahoe International Airport and in nearby Chico.
  • Shuttle Services: There are a few shuttle services, including Plumas Transit, that serve Quincy and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Quincy

Quincy is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Several local taxi services and rental car agencies are also available for visitors who need more flexibility or want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Quincy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Tahoe: Explore the sparkling waters and scenic views of Lake Tahoe, located just a few hours’ drive from Quincy.
  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: Discover the awe-inspiring natural wonders and geothermal activity of Lassen Volcanic National Park, located about an hour and a half from Quincy.
  • Reno: Get your fill of entertainment, nightlife, and casinos in Reno, a vibrant city located about two hours’ drive from Quincy.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie at heart, Quincy is the perfect destination for your next getaway. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and welcoming community, this hidden gem in the Shasta Cascades offers something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of Quincy for yourself!
