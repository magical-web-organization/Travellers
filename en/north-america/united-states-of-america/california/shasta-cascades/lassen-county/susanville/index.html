Introduction to Susanville

Nestled in the heart of Shasta Cascades, Susanville is a picturesque city in Lassen County, California. Breathtaking natural landscapes surround this city, making it a perfect stop for outdoor enthusiasts. The city boasts a fascinating history with multiple museums and cultural sites. Moreover, if you’re looking for a laidback vacation spot to free your mind from the daily hustle and bustle, Susanville is an ideal choice.

List of Places to Visit in Susanville

Here is a list of the must-visit places in Susanville.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lassen Historical Museum: The museum showcases the history of Lassen County and the surrounding regions, including the history of the local indigenous tribes and the impact of mining and timber industries.
  • Roop’s Fort: Built-in 1854, Roop’s Fort is one of the oldest buildings in Susanville that served as a trading post, jail, courthouse and home to the Roop family.
  • Lassen County Courthouse: The courthouse was built in 1921 and is known for its neoclassical architecture and remarkable stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Susanville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: The park is one of the most popular attractions in Susanville and boasts a network of trails, wildflowers, and stunning volcanic landscapes.
  • Bizz Johnson Trail: The trail follows the route of an old railway line and offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscapes while on a bike ride or hike.
  • Eagle Lake: Located a few miles north of Susanville, this lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Susanville

  • Cinder Cone Trail: This 4-mile trail leads to the summit of the Cinder Cone, a dormant volcano that offers a magnificent view of Lassen National Forest and Mount Lassen.
  • Eagle Lake Trail: This 11.6-mile trail is perfect for hiking and scenic views of Eagle Lake.

Best Things to Do in Susanville: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Local Art Scene: Susanville has a thriving art scene, with galleries and studios that showcase the works of local artists.
  • Visit Honey Lake: Honey Lake is a popular spot for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Get a bird’s eye view of Susanville and its surroundings on a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Susanville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Basque-style Cuisine: Try traditional Basque dishes such as lamb stew or ox tail soup at a local Basque restaurant.
  • BBQ: Enjoy delicious BBQ at one of the many local BBQ joints.

Local Beverages

  • Lassen Ale Works: The local brewery features a range of beers crafted with locally sourced ingredients.
  • California Wine: You can also taste wine from the nearby wineries.

Where to Stay in Susanville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Diamond Mountain Casino and Hotel: The hotel rooms are spacious and comfortable, and the casino offers various games for entertainment.
  • High Country Inn: A cozy hotel that offers comfortable rooms for guests.
  • Carson Chalets: Cozy cottages with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Susanville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lassen Hiking/Mountaineering Books: Lassen County is a hiker’s paradise, and books on mountain trails in the area make a perfect souvenir for hikers.
  • Local Crafts and Art: Visit one of the local art galleries or studios to purchase unique handmade crafts and art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Susanville

Susanville is home to multiple indigenous tribes who have a significant impact on the local culture and customs. Visitors are requested to be considerate of their culture and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Susanville

The best time to visit Susanville is from late spring to early fall, when temperature ranges from 70°F to 90°F.

Do’s and Don’ts of Susanville

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the indigenous tribes.
  • Explore the natural attractions and outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Susanville

Susanville is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors are advised to take adequate precautions and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Susanville

Visitors should carry appropriate medication and consult a doctor before visiting to get the required vaccines.

Getting to Susanville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Susanville

  • Reno-Tahoe International Airport: Located 2 hours from Susanville, this is the nearest international airport.
  • Susanville Municipal Airport: Small airport that serves private planes.

Bus Stations Near Susanville

Greyhound bus service provides transport to the nearby cities of Westwood and Chester.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxi services are available for visitors.

How to Get Around in Susanville

Visitors can use car rentals, taxis, or bike rentals to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Susanville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Tahoe: Located 2 hours from Susanville, Lake Tahoe offers water activities, scenic views, and mountain activities.
  • Reno: A 2-hour drive from Susanville, Reno is known for its casinos, entertainment, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Susanville is an ideal escape for outdoor enthusiasts, cultural enthusiasts, and anyone looking for a relaxing getaway. Explore the historical and cultural sites, hike or bike along scenic routes, taste the local cuisine and wine, and take in the natural beauty of this picturesque city.
