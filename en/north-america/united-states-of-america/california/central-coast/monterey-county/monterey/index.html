Introduction to Monterey

Monterey is a popular destination located in Monterey County, in Central Coast, California, United States of America, North America. It is a beautiful seaside town that is perched on the southern edge of Monterey Bay and is surrounded by picturesque hills and stunning scenery. Monterey is known for being the birthplace of California’s statehood and is a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around in Monterey.

List of Places to Visit in Monterey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Monterey State Historic Park - Explore the Monterey State Historic Park that consists of 55 historic buildings, including the Custom House, California’s first government building.

  • Cannery Row - A historic street that used to be home to sardine canning factories and is now a popular tourist destination with shops, restaurants, and hotels.

  • Monterey Bay Aquarium - A world-class aquarium that is home to over 35,000 animals and plants from the marine life.

  • Lighthouse Avenue - Enjoy the flavorful history of Pacific Grove together with the well-preserved Victorian architecture of the Pacific Grove Historic Monuments.

Natural Attractions of Monterey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pebble Beach - A world-renowned golf course and a stunning scenic drive with picturesque views of the ocean and coastline.

  • 17-Mile Drive - A scenic drive that takes you through Pebble Beach and Pacific Grove with stops at stunning natural attractions like the Lone Cypress and Bird Rock.

  • Monterey Bay Coastal Trail - A 29-mile-long pedestrian and bike path that runs along the coastline of the Monterey Peninsula.

  • Point Lobos State Natural Reserve - A protected reserve with diverse wildlife, tide pools, and hiking trails along the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monterey

  • Garland Ranch Regional Park - A picturesque park located on the Santa Lucia Mountain range and features some of the best hiking trails.

  • Fort Ord National Monument - A scenic and adventurous park that consists of 14,000 acres of protected land with plenty of hiking and biking trails.

  • Monterey Bay Coastal Trail - A 29-mile-long pedestrian and bike path that runs along the coastline of the Monterey Peninsula.

Best Things to Do in Monterey: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours - Take a guided tour of Monterey County’s best wineries and vineyards.

  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding - Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the stunning shores of Monterey Bay.

  • Whale Watching - Take a boat tour and watch the magnificent humpback whales and blue whales swimming in the Monterey Bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monterey

Food and Drink Scene of Monterey

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder - A creamy soup made with clams, bacon, potatoes, and cream. It’s a popular dish and can be found in many restaurants in Monterey.

  • Fish Tacos - Fresh seafood wrapped in soft tortillas and topped with spicy salsa, sour cream, and guacamole. A perfect meal to have after a day at the beach.

  • Artichokes - A local dish that is usually grilled or deep-fried as an appetizer. Artichokes are native to California.

Local Beverages

  • Monterey Wines - Monterey County is known for its wine production, and there are many wineries that you can visit to have a taste of their produces.

  • Craft Beers - Monterey is home to many craft breweries that offer a wide range of beers.

Where to Stay in Monterey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Intercontinental The Clement Monterey - A luxurious hotel located right on the Cannery Row with spectacular views of the Monterey Bay.

  • Spindrift Inn - A romantic seaside hotel located on Cannery Row with stunning ocean views and beautiful rooms.

  • HI Monterey Hostel - A budget-friendly accommodation option located in a historic building in the heart of Monterey.

What to Buy in Monterey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Monterey Bay Aquarium Merchandise - The Monterey Bay Aquarium has a gift shop that offers souvenirs, jewelry, and artworks.

  • Artichokes - Artichokes are native to California and can be purchased in many farmer’s markets and specialty food stores.

  • Wine - Monterey produces some of the best wines in California, which can be purchased from local wineries and wine shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Monterey

Monterey has a rich history and culture, and it’s essential to respect the customs and traditions of the locals. Make sure to visit the museums and historical places to understand their culture better.

Best Time to Visit Monterey

The best time to visit Monterey is between September and November and March and May when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are less. The summer months of June through August can be crowded and expensive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monterey

Do:

  • Visit the Monterey State Historic Park and learn about the history of Monterey.

  • Take a scenic drive along the 17-Mile Drive and explore the beautiful coastline.

  • Try the local seafood and wines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and beaches.

  • Don’t disturb the marine life while swimming or kayaking.

  • Don’t forget to dress in layers as the weather can change quickly in Monterey.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monterey

Monterey is generally a safe place to visit. However, it’s always important to take precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings, not leaving valuables in your car, and following the rules while on the beach.

Health in Monterey

Monterey has several hospitals and medical facilities that can handle minor to major medical emergencies.

Getting to Monterey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monterey

  • Monterey Regional Airport - Located just a few minutes away from downtown Monterey.

  • San Francisco International Airport - 1.5 hours away from Monterey and offers international flights.

Bus Stations Near Monterey

  • Monterey Transit Plaza - A bus station located in downtown Monterey that offers local and regional bus services.

  • Salinas Transit Center - A bus station located in Salinas, which is 20 minutes away from Monterey.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a great option to explore Monterey and the surrounding areas.

  • Taxi - Monterey has several taxi companies that can take you around the town.

How to Get Around in Monterey

The best way to get around Monterey is by car or by bike. There are several car rental options available, and the city has several bike lanes and bike rental companies.

Where to Go Next from Monterey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Big Sur - A stunning coastal region located just south of Monterey with breathtaking views and scenic drives.

  • Carmel-by-the-Sea - A charming town located just south of Monterey with quaint streets, beautiful beaches, and world-class dining.

Conclusion

Monterey is a beautiful destination that offers a blend of history, culture, and scenic natural beauty. Plan your trip to Monterey and discover the best places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, and how to get around in this comprehensive travel guide.
