Introduction to Seaside

Located in Monterey County, in Central Coast, California, Seaside is a serene coastal city brimming with cultural and natural attractions. Situated near the popular tourist destinations of Monterey and Carmel-by-the-Sea, Seaside boasts beautiful beaches, hiking and biking trails, historical landmarks, art galleries, and more. Whether you are looking for a relaxing weekend getaway or an adventurous outdoor excursion, Seaside has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Seaside

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Ord Dunes State Park: Witness the natural beauty of Seaside with over four miles of scenic coastline and over 50 miles of hiking trails.
  • Monterey Bay Aquarium: Explore over 35,000 sea creatures with interactive exhibits that showcase the incredible diversity of Pacific Ocean marine life.
  • Lovers Point Park: Take in panoramic views of Monterey Bay, picnic, or stroll around the beautiful coastal park.

Natural Attractions of Seaside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seaside Beach: One of the city’s most popular beaches with ample parking, restrooms, and lifeguards on duty in peak season.
  • Del Monte Beach: Located near Monterey Bay, this beach is ideal for hiking, jogging, cycling, kayaking, and surfing.
  • Asilomar State Beach: This secluded beach is a great place for a relaxing walk or a romantic picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Seaside

  • Fleming Arboretum Trail: A three-mile hiking and biking trail with beautiful views of Seaside and Monterey Bay.
  • Fort Ord National Monument: This former military base has various trails suitable for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.
  • Monterey Bay Coastal Recreation Trail: This popular 18-mile trail is great for walking, jogging, biking, and skating alongside the scenic Monterey Bay shoreline.

Best Things to Do in Seaside: Activities and Tours

  • Paddle boarding: Explore the calm waves of Monterey Bay with a paddleboard rental or take a guided tour.
  • Whale Watching: Observe majestic whales up close during a boat tour on Monterey Bay.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit the nearby Carmel Valley and savor some of the best wines California has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Seaside

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Monterey Bay Aquarium and explore Cannery Row.
Day 2 Hike the trails at Fort Ord Dunes State Park.
Day 3 Paddle board or kayak in Monterey Bay.
Day 4 Explore the historic Cannery Row with a self-guided tour.
Day 5 Take a scenic drive down Pacific Coast Highway to Big Sur.
Day 6 Wine tasting in Carmel Valley.
Day 7 Relax and soak up the sun at Seaside Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Seaside

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalbi Beef Tacos: Pacific Bowls & Rolls serves delicious tacos with a Korean twist.
  • Seafood Pasta: Sample some of the best seafood pasta in the area at Gusto Handcrafted Pasta & Pizza.
  • Crab Benedict: Enjoy brunch at the Beach House at Lovers Point and savor their mouth-watering crab benedict.

Local Beverages

  • English Ale: Try a pint or two of English Ale at Post No Bills Craft Beer House.
  • Carpe Diem Coffee: Enjoy a freshly brewed cup of coffee at Carpe Diem Fine Books & Cafe.
  • Monterey Wines: Sip on some of the best wines California has to offer at A Taste of Monterey.

Where to Stay in Seaside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Embassy Suites by Hilton: A picturesque hotel near Monterey Bay with comfortable rooms, a hot tub, and free breakfast.
  • Sand Castle Inn: A cozy, budget-friendly motel that offers great amenities, including free Wi-Fi and continental breakfast.
  • Monterey Tides: A beachfront hotel with stunning views of Monterey Bay, luxurious rooms, and great dining options.

What to Buy in Seaside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Monterey Bay Aquarium Merchandise: Bring home a memento from your visit to the aquarium, like a fun sea creature stuffed animal or apparel.
  • Carmel Valley Wine: Take home a bottle (or more!) of Carmel Valley wine and enjoy a taste of California at home.
  • Monterey Bay Memorabilia: Shop for Seaside and Monterey Bay postcards, hats, and t-shirts at the “Monterey Bay Marketplace” located in the Cannery Row Shopping Center.

Local Culture and Customs of Seaside

Seaside is a peaceful and inviting city with a laid-back California vibe. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. It is customary to tip in most restaurants, cafes, and bars in the area. Outdoor activities such as hiking and biking are popular, and many people enjoy spending time at the beach.

Best Time to Visit Seaside

The best time to visit Seaside is from June to August when the weather is sunny and clear, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, it can be crowded during this time, so visitors looking for a quieter experience should consider visiting in the fall or spring months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seaside

Do:

  • Respect the environment and avoid littering in Seaside’s parks and beaches.
  • TIP: It is customary to tip in most restaurants, cafes, and bars in the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and protective gear when participating in outdoor activities to avoid sunburn or injury.
  • Don’t swim in the ocean alone or after dark to avoid unnecessary risks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Seaside

Seaside is generally a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas.

Health in Seaside

Visitors should take necessary precautions to protect themselves from the sun, especially during the hot summer months. Visitors should also stay hydrated and carry water at all times when hiking or participating in any outdoor activities.

Getting to Seaside: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Seaside

  • Monterey Regional Airport (MRY) is the closest airport to Seaside.

Bus Stations Near Seaside

  • Greyhound is the primary bus service that runs in and to Seaside.

Other Options

  • Uber and Lyft rideshare services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Seaside

Seaside is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bikes or take a taxi to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Seaside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Monterey: Visit the famous Cannery Row, the Monterey Bay Aquarium, and Point Lobos State Natural Reserve.
  • Carmel-by-the-Sea: Explore charming shops, cafes, and galleries, or take a scenic drive through the picturesque 17-Mile Drive.
  • Big Sur: Take a day trip to Big Sur and enjoy hiking and outdoor activities amidst some of California’s most beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Seaside is an ideal destination for visitors looking for a relaxing, scenic coastal getaway and outdoor activities. From hiking and biking to exploring cultural landmarks and enjoying local food and wine, Seaside has it all. With its friendly locals and laid-back vibe, Seaside is a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring the Central Coast of California.
