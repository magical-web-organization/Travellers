Moss Landing is a serene seaside town located in Monterey County, Central Coast, California. Nestled between the Pacific Ocean and the fertile Salinas Valley, it is famous for its estuary, wildlife, and maritime history. With meandering creeks, verdant fields, and pristine beaches, Moss Landing is an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and history buffs. This comprehensive tour guide provides a list of places to visit, hiking/biking routes, activities/tours, food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, nearby destinations, and safety and health tips for a hassle-free trip.

List of Places to Visit in Moss Landing

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler to Moss Landing, you shouldn’t miss these cultural and natural attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moss Landing Marine Laboratories: A leading marine research center, it has a public outreach program where you can learn about marine science, conservation, and sustainability.
  • Captain Yohn Gideon House Museum: This historic house museum, built in 1890, showcases the lifestyle of a wealthy captain and his family in the early 20th century.
  • Power of Blue: This mural on the side of a building by artist John Cerney, depicts the life cycle of a humpback whale and is a prominent landmark in Moss Landing.

Natural Attractions of Moss Landing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elkhorn Slough National Estuarine Research Reserve: A pristine wetland that harbors more than 700 species of plants and animals, including sea otters, harbor seals, and migratory birds. You can explore the estuary via kayaking, walking, or wildlife watching.
  • Moss Landing State Beach: A long sandy beach ideal for beachcombing, sunbathing, and watching sunset. You can spot whales, dolphins, and seabirds from the shore.
  • Salinas River State Beach: A windswept beach that is popular for fishing, horseback riding, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moss Landing

Moss Landing offers several scenic trails and bike paths that let you explore the countryside, coastline, and wetlands. Some popular routes are:

  • Moss Landing Bike Trail: A 7-mile-long paved trail that starts from the Moss Landing Harbor and follows the Elkhorn Slough and Salinas River to Castroville.
  • Moss Landing Wildlife Area Trail: A 2-mile-long dirt trail that meanders through the salt marshes and upland grasslands of the Moss Landing Wildlife Area. You can spot shorebirds, raptors, and coyotes on the trail.
  • Moss Landing Beach Walk Trail: A 1.5-mile-long trail that runs parallel to the beach and offers stunning views of the ocean and dunes. You can see sand dollars, jellyfish, and crabs on the beach.

Best Things to Do in Moss Landing: Activities and Tours

Moss Landing offers several fun-filled activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. Some of the must-do experiences are:

  • Whale Watching: A boat trip that takes you close to the spectacular humpback and blue whales that frequent the Moss Landing coast. You can see breaching, tail-slapping, and feeding behaviors of these gentle giants.
  • Kayaking: A paddle trip that allows you to explore the hidden coves, channels, and tidal pools of the Elkhorn Slough and encounter sea otters, harbor seals, and sea lions. You can rent a kayak or join a guided tour.
  • Fishing: A sport that lets you catch a variety of fish, including salmon, halibut, lingcod, and rockfish, from the Moss Landing Harbor or the nearby ocean. You can rent a boat or join a charter.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moss Landing

To make the most of your trip to Moss Landing, you can follow this week-long itinerary:

Day 1 Arrive in Moss Landing, check-in at your hotel, and take a leisurely walk on the Moss Landing Beach Trail.
Day 2 Explore the Elkhorn Slough by kayaking or taking a wildlife watching tour.
Day 3 Visit the Moss Landing Marine Laboratories and the Captain Yohn Gideon House Museum.
Day 4 Go fishing in the Moss Landing Harbor or take a fishing charter in the Pacific Ocean.
Day 5 Bike on the Moss Landing Bike Trail to Castroville and have lunch at the Giant Artichoke Restaurant.
Day 6 Take a whale watching tour and watch humpback and blue whales in their natural habitat.
Day 7 Visit the Power of Blue mural and enjoy the scenic views of the Salinas River State Beach. Depart from Moss Landing.

Food and Drink Scene of Moss Landing

Moss Landing has several restaurants, cafes, and wineries that offer a diverse range of cuisines and beverages. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Abalone: A mollusk that is delicately flavored and tender. You can try it at the Haute Enchilada Cafe and Gallery.
  • Artichoke Soup: A creamy soup made from fresh artichokes. You can taste it at the Whole Enchilada Marketplace.
  • Cioppino: A seafood stew that contains fish, crab, prawns, and clams in a tomato-based broth. You can savor it at the Phil’s Fish Market.

Local Beverages

  • Moss Landing White Wine: A crisp and aromatic white wine made from Chardonnay and Pinot Grigio grapes. You can sip it at the Moss Landing Tasting Room.
  • Moss Landing Amber Ale: A smooth and malty amber ale brewed by the Elkhorn Slough Brewing Company. You can drink it at the taproom.

Where to Stay in Moss Landing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moss Landing has several accommodation options that suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Captain’s Inn at Moss Landing: A romantic bed and breakfast inn that offers panoramic ocean views, gourmet breakfast, and cozy rooms with fireplaces.
  • Moss Landing Marine Motel: A family-friendly motel that has a swimming pool, barbecue area, and spacious rooms with kitchenettes.
  • HI Monterey Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that provides free breakfast, WiFi, and bicycle rentals.

What to Buy in Moss Landing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moss Landing has several shops and stores that sell unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the best things to buy are:

  • Elkhorn Slough Honey: A pure and natural honey made by the bees that forage on the Elkhorn Slough wildflowers. You can buy it at the Elkhorn Slough Foundation.
  • Captain Yohn Gideon House Museum Memorabilia: A collection of postcards, magnets, and mugs that depict the historic house museum. You can buy them at the gift shop.
  • Phil’s Fish Market Special Sauce: A secret recipe sauce that complements the seafood dishes of Phil’s Fish Market. You can buy it at the market.

Local Culture and Customs of Moss Landing

Moss Landing has a rich maritime culture and customs that reflect its history and geography. Some of the notable traditions are:

  • Fishermen’s Festival: A annual event that celebrates the local fishing industry with music, food, and competitions. It is held in April.
  • Whale Watching Etiquette: A set of guidelines that promote responsible and respectful whale watching practices, such as keeping a safe distance from the whales and avoiding sudden movements.
  • Elkhorn Slough Bird Festival: A bi-annual event that showcases the diverse bird species that inhabit the Elkhorn Slough Reserve. It is held in March and September.

Best Time to Visit Moss Landing

Moss Landing has a Mediterranean climate that is mild and comfortable throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is from May to October, when the weather is warm and dry, and you can indulge in outdoor activities and tours. The winters are cool and wet, but also ideal for whale watching and bird watching.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moss Landing

To have a pleasant and safe trip to Moss Landing, you should follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and their habitats.
  • Dress in layers and wear comfortable shoes.
  • Carry sunscreen, hats, and water bottles.
  • Observe the traffic rules and parking signs.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the animals or plants.
  • Litter the beaches or trails.
  • Feed or touch the wildlife.
  • Park your car in restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Moss Landing is a safe town that has low crime rates and a friendly community. However, you should take precautions to avoid accidents and illnesses.

Safety in Moss Landing

  • Wear life jackets while kayaking or boating.
  • Avoid swimming in the ocean during high tide or strong currents.
  • Stay alert while driving on the scenic routes.
  • Follow the instructions of the tour guides and the signage.

Health in Moss Landing

  • Protect yourself from mosquito bites by using repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.
  • Avoid eating raw or undercooked seafood.
  • Drink bottled water or boiled water if the tap water is not potable.
  • Carry a first-aid kit and necessary medications.
