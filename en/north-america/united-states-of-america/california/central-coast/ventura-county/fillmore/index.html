Introduction to Fillmore

Nestled in Ventura County’s rolling hills, Fillmore is a charming city that offers a blend of natural and cultural attractions to its visitors. The city’s warm and friendly atmosphere, combined with its historic downtown, beautiful parks, and scenic landscapes, makes it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway from Los Angeles or Santa Barbara.

List of Places to Visit in Fillmore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fillmore is home to some of the most significant cultural attractions in California. The city’s rich history is evident in its many museums and historical buildings, including:

  • Fillmore Historical Museum: This museum is home to a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Fillmore’s early settlers and the region’s history. The museum is housed in the former Southern Pacific Railroad Depot, which was constructed in 1887.
  • Elkins Ranch Golf Course and Ranch: This historic ranch is known for its beautiful vistas and 18-hole golf course that opened in 1936. The ranch has been a favorite of celebrities and politicians for decades, including Ronald Reagan and Winston Churchill.
  • Sespe Creek Distillery: This family-owned distillery produces craft spirits using locally grown ingredients, including whiskey, gin, and brandy. Visitors can take a guided tour, enjoy a tasting, and purchase bottles to take home.
  • Rancho Camulos National Historic Landmark: Located just outside of Fillmore, this historic landmark is a former Mexican land grant and was the inspiration for the novel “Ramona” by Helen Hunt Jackson. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the area’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Fillmore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fillmore is also home to some of the most spectacular natural attractions in California. From hiking and biking to scenic drives and wildlife viewing, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Los Padres National Forest: This beautiful forest covers more than 1.7 million acres and offers hiking and biking trails, camping, and fishing opportunities.
  • Sespe Wilderness: This vast wilderness area is home to a wide range of wildlife, including black bears and mountain lions. Visitors can take guided hikes or go backpacking for a more immersive experience.
  • **Santa Clara River: ** This scenic river runs through Fillmore, offering kayaking, fishing, and hiking opportunities.
  • Heritage Valley: This agricultural region is known for its scenic vistas and offers a wide range of activities, including wine tasting, farm tours, and pick-your-own produce experiences.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fillmore

Fillmore is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The city offers a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Sespe Creek Trail: This 20-mile trail follows the Sespe Creek and offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Two Trees Trail: This 5.2-mile trail is located on South Mountain and offers panoramic views of the city of Fillmore and the Santa Clara River Valley.
  • Los Padres National Forest Trails: The national forest offers a range of hiking and biking trails, including the 2.7-mile Reyes Peak Trail and the 4.3-mile Potrero John Trail.

Best Things to Do in Fillmore: Activities and Tours

Fillmore offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. Some of the best things to do in Fillmore include:

  • Fillmore & Western Railway: This vintage train takes visitors on a scenic journey through the Heritage Valley.
  • Pick-Your-Own Orchards: Fillmore is home to a variety of orchards that offer pick-your-own experiences, including strawberries, oranges, and persimmons.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the stunning landscapes of Fillmore.
  • Santa Paula Aviation Museum: This museum features a collection of antique planes, artifacts, and exhibits that tell the story of aviation in Ventura County.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fillmore

If you’re planning to spend a week in Fillmore, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Fillmore

Must-Try Dishes

Fillmore is known for its fresh, farm-to-table cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Farm-to-Table Pizza at Pizzeria Venti: This downtown Fillmore pizzeria is known for its gourmet pizza that uses fresh, locally sourced ingredients.
  • Tacos and Margaritas at La Cabana: This family-owned restaurant serves up some of the best Mexican cuisine in Fillmore, including their famous tacos and margaritas.
  • Brunch at El Pescador Seafood: This seafood restaurant is known for its weekend brunch that includes fresh oysters, shrimp cocktails, and seafood chilaquiles.

Local Beverages

Fillmore has a thriving craft beer and wine scene. Some of the best local beverages to try include:

  • Fillmore Brews from Institution Ale Company: This family-owned craft brewery produces a range of beers, including their famous Fillmore Daze, a Belgian-style golden ale named after the city itself.
  • Wines from Heritage Valley: The Heritage Valley is home to several wineries that produce a wide range of wines, including chardonnay, pinot noir, and syrah.

Where to Stay in Fillmore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fillmore has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Fillmore Inn: This historic bed and breakfast is located in downtown Fillmore and offers comfortable and spacious rooms.
  • Good Nite Inn: This affordable hotel is located near the Fillmore & Western Railway and offers clean and basic accommodations.
  • Filmore KOA: For a more rustic experience, you can stay at the KOA campground just outside of Fillmore.

What to Buy in Fillmore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Fillmore include:

  • Fresh Fruits and Vegetables: Fillmore is known for its fresh produce, including oranges, avocados, and strawberries.
  • Craft Beer and Wine: Fillmore has a booming craft beer and wine scene that produces some of California’s best beverages.
  • Artisanal Products: Fillmore is home to several artisanal food producers, including Sespe Creek Distillery and Ojai Olive Oil Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Fillmore

Fillmore is known for its friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as:

  • Respecting Farming Communities: Fillmore is located in an agricultural region, and visitors should be mindful of farmers and their property when exploring the area.
  • Taking Care of the Environment: Fillmore is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, and visitors should be mindful of their impact on the environment.

Best Time to Visit Fillmore

The best time to visit Fillmore is during the spring or fall when the weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds. Summer can be hot and crowded, and winter can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fillmore

Do:

  • Explore Downtown Fillmore: Fillmore’s historic downtown is full of charming shops, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Take a Scenic Drive: Fillmore’s Heritage Valley is known for its orchards and vineyards and offers a beautiful scenic drive.
  • Hike or Bike in the Wilderness: Fillmore’s wilderness areas offer some of California’s most beautiful hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on Farmers’ Property: Fillmore is an agricultural community, and visitors should be respectful of local farmers and their property.
  • Leave Trash Behind: Visitors should always pack out their trash and leave natural areas as they found them.
  • Ignore Wildlife Rules: Fillmore’s wilderness areas are home to a variety of wildlife, and visitors should always respect the rules and guidelines for interacting with wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fillmore

Fillmore is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions when exploring the area:

  • Be Aware of Fire Danger: Fillmore is located in a region prone to wildfires, and visitors should be mindful of the fire danger and follow local guidelines for preventing fires.
  • Be Careful on Hiking Trails: Fillmore’s hiking trails can be steep and challenging, and visitors should always take appropriate precautions and be prepared for emergencies.

Health in Fillmore

Visitors to Fillmore should take the usual health precautions, including staying hydrated, protecting their skin from the sun, and being aware of the risks of tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Fillmore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fillmore is located about an hour’s drive from Los Angeles and about 40 minutes’ drive from Santa Barbara. Some of the transportation options for getting to Fillmore include:

Airports Near Fillmore

The closest airports to Fillmore are:

  • Burbank Bob Hope Airport: Located about 50 miles southeast of Fillmore.
  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): Located about 70 miles southeast of Fillmore.
  • Santa Barbara Municipal Airport: Located about 45 miles northwest of Fillmore.

Bus Stations Near Fillmore

The closest bus stations to Fillmore are:

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Santa Paula: Located about 10 miles south of Fillmore.
  • Thousand Oaks Transit Center: Located about 20 miles east of Fillmore.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing service to get to Fillmore.

How to Get Around in Fillmore

While Fillmore is a relatively small city, visitors will need a car or bike to get around and explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Fillmore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fillmore is located in the heart of Ventura County, offering visitors easy access to a wide range of nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the best places to visit include:

  • Ojai: This nearby city is known for its arts and culture scene, outdoor activities, and hot springs.
  • Ventura Beach: This nearby beach town offers family-friendly attractions, excellent surfing, and a range of restaurants and shops.
  • Santa Paula: This historic city is located just south of Fillmore and offers a wide range of cultural attractions, including museums and historic murals.

Conclusion

Fillmore is a hidden gem in Ventura County, offering a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in hiking and outdoor adventures, farm-to-table dining, or exploring California’s rich history and culture, Fillmore has something for everyone. So pack your bags, hit the road, and come discover the charms of Fillmore.
