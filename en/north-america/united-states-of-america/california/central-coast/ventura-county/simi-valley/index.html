Introduction to Simi Valley

Located in Ventura County, in Central Coast, California, Simi Valley is a peaceful and historic city known for its vast landscapes, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With a population of over 125,000 people, Simi Valley is a wonderful destination for those looking for a tranquil and scenic escape from the hustle and bustle of the big city. In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit, food and drink recommendations, where to stay, and tips on how to stay safe and healthy while exploring Simi Valley.

List of Places to Visit in Simi Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Simi Valley has a rich history, and there are many culturally significant attractions throughout the city. Here are some museums and historical places worth visiting:

  • Ronald Reagan Presidential Library and Museum: The Ronald Reagan Presidential Library houses memorabilia from the former President’s time in office, including a replica of the Oval Office, Air Force One, and many interesting artifacts and exhibits.

  • Strathearn Historical Park and Museum: This park features historic buildings dating back to the 1800s, including a blacksmith shop, a Victorian house, and a carriage house. Visitors can tour the buildings and see what life was like in Simi Valley over a century ago.

  • Rancho Simi: Visitors can tour the historic house, built in 1892, and learn about the history of the Rancho Simi land grant, which the city was founded on.

Natural Attractions of Simi Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Simi Valley is blessed with many beautiful landscapes and outdoor spaces. Here are some of the natural attractions worth visiting:

  • Corriganville Movie Ranch: This former movie set has been turned into a public park and offers visitors many hiking trails with stunning views of the mountains and valleys, and also includes a picnic area, ponds, streams and a playground.

  • Hummingbird Ranch: This ranch offers visitors a chance to get up close and personal with a variety of animals, including miniature donkeys, alpacas, and goats. Visitors can also enjoy hiking trails through the canyon and birdwatching.

  • Chumash Park: This park has a playground, picnic tables, and hiking trails, but what makes it unique is the stunning views of the city and the valley. It’s a great spot for a picnic or a sunrise/sunset hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simi Valley

Simi Valley has many hiking and biking trails to explore, for those who enjoy nature and outdoor activities. Here are some of the best routes in the city:

  • Chumash Trail: This 4-mile trail offers scenic views of the valley and is perfect for a morning or evening hike.

  • Hummingbird Trail: This 5-mile trail winds through the canyon and includes a small waterfall along the way.

  • Simi Peak Trail: This 6-mile trail takes hikers to the highest point in Simi Valley, offering panoramic views of the city and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Simi Valley: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Simi Valley. Here are some of the best:

  • Simi Valley Town Center: This outdoor shopping mall features a variety of stores and restaurants, as well as a movie theater and a free summer concert series.

  • Roundhouse Aquarium: A small aquarium with emphasis on local sea life from the Southern California coast, this is a great place to take kids for an interactive learning experience.

  • Skydive Coastal California: For the adventurous traveler, Skydive Coastal California offers tandem skydiving jumps over the beautiful landscapes of Simi Valley.

Week-Long Itinerary for Simi Valley

For visitors who want to spend a week exploring Simi Valley, we have put together a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Ronald Reagan Presidential Library and Museum.
  • Day 2: Explore the history of Rancho Simi.
  • Day 3: Take a hike on the Chumash Trail and enjoy a picnic in Chumash Park.
  • Day 4: Head to Corriganville Movie Ranch and go on a film location tour.
  • Day 5: Drive up to Simi Peak Trail for a scenic hike.
  • Day 6: Spend the day at Hummingbird Ranch and enjoy the animals and hiking trails.
  • Day 7: Visit Roundhouse Aquarium with kids or go on a tandem skydiving jump with Skydive Coastal California for the adventurous traveler.

Food and Drink Scene of Simi Valley

Must-Try Dishes

Simi Valley has a great food scene with many delicious dining options. Here are some must-try dishes for visitors:

  • The Junkyard Café: This restaurant has a unique atmosphere with its industrial-chic vibe and serves delicious breakfasts with all the classics including omelettes, pancakes, and french toast.

  • Wood Ranch BBQ and Grill: This popular restaurant serves up mouth-watering barbeque ribs, chicken, and brisket in a friendly atmosphere.

  • Leeward Winery & Vineyards: This Winery is nestled in the Five Knolls of Simi Valley and offers tastings and tours throughout the week.

Local Beverages

Simi Valley has a growing local craft beer scene and we recommend checking out these breweries:

  • Tapia Brothers: Tapia Brother Brewing is a family-owned and operated brewery known for its unique and flavorful brews.

  • 14 Cannons Brewing Company: 14 Cannons is a popular brewery famous for its IPA’s, Porters and Seasonals.

Where to Stay in Simi Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Simi Valley, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Grand Vista Hotel: Elegant and comfortable rooms, surrounded by beautiful mountain views. With a swimming pool, hot tub, and gym on the premises.

  • Motel 6: Affordable and clean rooms, perfect for budget-conscious travelers.

  • The Hummingbird Inn: This charming bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms and a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Simi Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Simi Valley has many options. Here are some recommendations:

  • Simi Valley Farmers’ Market: This market features locally-grown produce, artisanal crafts and food stalls, and a great place to purchase gifts.

  • Simi Valley Antique Mall: This antique mall has many vintage collectibles, including jewelry, art, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Simi Valley

Simi Valley is a friendly and welcoming community, and visitors should keep in mind that the city has many cultural and historical landmarks of significance. If visiting these places, visitors should follow proper etiquette and display respect for the local history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Simi Valley

Simi Valley enjoys a Mediterranean climate with mild winters and hot summers. However, visitors should expect higher temperatures during summer months and fewer crowds during the off-season which is usually in late fall and early spring. We recommend visiting Simi Valley in late spring before the summer heat and the crowds arrive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simi Valley

Do:

  • Visit the culturally important attractions such as the Ronald Reagan Presidential Library and Museum and explore the Ranch Simi history.
  • Take advantage of the numerous outdoor adventure opportunities like hiking on the scenic trails or skydiving.
  • Try local food and craft beer at renowned establishments like The Junkyard Café and Tapia Brothers.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the city’s rich cultural and historical landmarks such as the Rancho Simi and other culturally important sites.
  • Ignore hiking and safety tips when exploring the nearby mountainous terrain.

Safety and Health

Safety in Simi Valley

Simi Valley is a safe city, with low crime rates, and a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should always follow general safety practices like being aware of their surroundings, keeping valuable items secured, and locking doors, windows when staying in hotels and motels. Visitors should also remain aware of the safety risks of hiking and engage in necessary precautions like staying on marked trails and carrying essential supplies like water bottles, hiking shoes, and sun protection.

Health in Simi Valley

Simi Valley has good healthcare services with several hospitals and private clinics. Visitors should take necessary health precautions like staying hydrated when exploring the outdoors and wearing sunscreen when the sun is strong.

Getting to Simi Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Simi Valley is accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some options for travelers:

Airports Near Simi Valley

The nearest airports to Simi Valley are:

  • Bob Hope Airport (BUR)
  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX)
  • John Wayne Airport (SNA)

Bus Stations Near Simi Valley

There is a bus station in downtown Simi Valley, providing travelers with several local and regional transportation options.

Other Options

Visitors should also consider renting a car or taking a taxi or rideshare service to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Simi Valley

Simi Valley is a car-friendly city, and travelers will find it easy to explore the city by car rental. There are also several taxi or rideshare services available, as well as public bus options.

Where to Go Next from Simi Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Simi Valley is near several other popular California destinations, giving travelers plenty of options for day trips or excursions. Here are some recommendations:

  • Los Angeles: Famous for its film industry, beaches, shopping, and attractions like the Santa Monica Pier and Hollywood Boulevard, LA is just an hour’s drive from Simi Valley.

  • Santa Barbara: With its history and cultural landmarks, renowned vineyards, and beautiful beaches, Santa Barbara is a popular day trip for those visiting Simi Valley.

  • Channel Islands National Park: Visitors can explore this unique protected sanctuary by taking a boat tour from the Ventura or Oxnard harbors, with access to five islands that function like time-capsules, preserving California as it once was.

Conclusion

Simi Valley is a beautiful and welcoming city, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, outdoor recreation, and gastronomy. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your Simi Valley trip, and that you are now ready to explore everything this city has to offer.
