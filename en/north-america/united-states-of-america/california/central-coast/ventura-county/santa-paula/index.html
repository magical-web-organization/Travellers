Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Santa Paula

Santa Paula is a hidden gem in Ventura County, California, and is well worth a visit by both domestic and international travelers. With its rich culture, natural beauty, and friendly locals, Santa Paula offers something for everyone. Here is everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Santa Paula!

List of Places to Visit in Santa Paula

Santa Paula has a wide range of attractions, from culturally significant museums to breathtaking natural wonders. Here are just a few of the must-see places to visit in Santa Paula:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Agricultural Museum: This museum highlights the rich history of farming and agriculture in Ventura County and Santa Paula. From equipment and tools to photos and artifacts, this museum offers an immersive experience.
  • Santa Paula Art Museum: Showcasing works from both national and local artists, this museum is a nestled-between-two-mountain-elbow-room-gallery that features works from Ojai, Ventura, & Malibu.
  • Union Oil Company Building: A historic landmark in Santa Paula, the Union Oil Company Building was commissioned in 1887. Now, the building is home to the California Oil Museum which features interactive exhibits and artifacts related to California’s oil history.

Natural Attractions of Santa Paula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Los Padres National Forest: An outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, Los Padres offers over 1,200 miles of trails, campgrounds, and unique vistas that run north to south, and outline the beautifully shaped coast near Santa Paula.
  • Santa Paula Canyon Falls: One of the most scenic hikes in the area, the canyon hike takes you through lush foliage and alongside a refreshing stream that leads up to a beautiful waterfall.
  • Steam Tunnels: If you’re looking for a more adventurous activity, the steam tunnels are sure to please. With over two miles of tunnels to explore, this is a unique experience not to be missed!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Paula

With its abundant natural beauty, Santa Paula offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few of our favorite routes:

  • Olivas Canyon Trail: The perfect pet-friendly-fitness-friendly trial! The Olivas Canyon Trail is a moderately difficult path that runs through the Olivas Canyon Wilderness Area. Visitors can see stunning scenery and the Olivas Adobe which was built in 1847.
  • Santa Paula Ridge Trail: Hikers, horseback riders, and mountain bikers will appreciate this trail. The Santa Paula Ridge Trail is the perfect way to get a bird’s eye view of the surrounding Santa Paula mountains and valleys.
  • Sisar Canyon Trail: The Sisar Canyon Trailhead is a great spot for seasoned hikers and trail runners. This trail is commonly used as a starting point for a popular thru-hike, the Ventura River Preserve.

Best Things to Do in Santa Paula: Activities and Tours

Santa Paula has no shortage of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained during their stay. Here are a few of the best things to do in Santa Paula:

  • Horseback Riding: Take a leisurely horseback ride through beautiful trails that take you through miles of steep farmhouses, rolling hills and winding roads through the Los Padres National Forest.
  • Airplane Rides: Experience the thrill of flying by taking a scenic airplane tour over the Santa Paula countryside. It’s an unforgettable way to see the town and surrounding area.
  • Cheese and Wine Tasting: Wind your way through the back roads of the Santa Paula countryside and stop at one of the many hidden wineries, sample cheese, wine and pick as many grapefruits and oranges as you like.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Paula

Santa Paula has a thriving food and drink scene with plenty of options to satisfy any palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orange Fries: Made from fresh oranges and breaded with special seasonings, these fries are a local favorite.
  • Ceviche: Fresh, locally-caught seafood is transformed into a citrusy masterpiece with Ceviche, made with tomato, onion, cilantro and a tostado on the side.
  • Barbecue Tri-Tip Sandwich: A BBQ staple, the tri-tip sandwich is served on a soft bun with barbecue sauce and a side of coleslaw.

Local Beverages

  • Brewed Beer: Santa Paula has many local breweries where you can taste delicious brewed beer. Topa Topa Brewery is a local favorite.
  • Pomegranate-Lime Margarita: This unique twist on a classic cocktail features fresh juice and a delicious pomegranate liqueur.
  • Orchard Granita: Made from tart cherry juice and shaved ice with sour cloves, the Orchard Granita is a refreshing way to end a meal.

Where to Stay in Santa Paula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Paula offers a variety of accommodation options. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, here are a few options in Santa Paula:

  • Glen Tavern Inn: Built-in 1911, this vintage hotel is a designated SoCal landmark and is on the National Register of Historic places. It features five-star quality dining and other amenities.
  • Chantico Inn & Suites: This is one of the most affordable prices, highest-rated hotels in Santa Paula and consistently offers good value. The charming Southwest-inspired rooms will give your stay an authentic feel.
  • The Oaks at Ojai Health Spa: If you’re looking for a tranquil place to escape the hustle and bustle of life and have a relaxing spa, this guesthouse is perfect for you.

What to Buy in Santa Paula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Santa Paula has a rich history of agriculture, so it’s no surprise that many of the best souvenirs revolve around locally-grown produce. Here are a few ideas:

  • Oranges: It’s hard to visit Santa Paula without sampling some of the delicious oranges harvested in the area. They make for a great souvenir too!
  • Lavender Products: Santa Paula has several lavender farms that offer products ranging from oils to sachets to soap.
  • Chili Oil: The area offers unique chili oil that can be bought in stores or may have small scale chili oil producers. It’s a great gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Paula

Santa Paula has a rich and storied history, which visitors can learn about through its museums and landmarks. Locals take pride in their community, tradition and the food.

Best Time to Visit Santa Paula

The best time to visit Santa Paula is in the early or late spring when the weather is mild but not too hot. In the summer, heat can climb up well over 90 degrees, so the shoulder seasons in late spring or early fall usually provide the best weather for outdoor activities. Winter months can be chilly, but some of the attractions offer tours during this seasons as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Paula

Do:

  • Respect the land: Santa Paula’s natural wonder and cultural attractions are a valuable asset, so please respect them.
  • Take the time to talk to locals to get an authentic local experience while visiting Santa Paula.
  • Look up local events ahead of time: Santa Paula has a variety of festivals, concerts, and events throughout the year so make sure to check out the schedule to make the most of it!

Don’t:

  • Litter: Santa Paula just banned plastic bags, and locals take pride in keeping the area clean.
  • Be disrespectful: Santa Paula is a welcoming community which values respect for all people and cultures.
  • Ignore the agricultural history: Santa Paula’s agriculture industry is a significant part of the area’s culture, so make sure to recognize its importance and impact.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Paula

Santa Paula is a relatively safe destination with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take standard precautions to protect themselves. Downtown Santa Paula at night might be not be suitable for everyone so make arrangements to travel back to your accommodation if you’re not staying within walking distance.

Health in Santa Paula

Santa Paula houses health centers where medical help is available, so you don’t have to worry about getting sick during your stay.

Getting to Santa Paula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Santa Paula is relatively easy, thanks to its proximity to major roads and airports. Here are some transportation options for getting to Santa Paula:

Airports Near Santa Paula

  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX)
  • Burbank Bob Hope Airport (BUR)

Bus Stations Near Santa Paula

  • Greyhound at Garey Avenue, Pomona

Other Options

  • Hiring a Car: If you’re traveling from nearby, the best way to get to Santa Paula is by hiring or driving your car.
  • Taxi or Rideshare Service: Another option is to arrange a taxi or rideshare service such as Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Santa Paula

Getting around Santa Paula is quite easy, with plenty of options for transportation. You can hire a car, use local taxi services or use bus services which run within the area as well as transferring to the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Santa Paula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Santa Paula is ideally situated for exploring the rest of Ventura County and Los Angeles County. Here are some other destinations worth considering:

  • Ojai: A charming mountain town with a thriving cultural scene, Ojai is the perfect destination for a day trip or a weekend getaway.
  • Santa Barbara: Located just 45 minutes from Santa Paula, Santa Barbara is home to stunning beaches, delicious restaurants, and a vibrant downtown scene.
  • Los Padres National Forest: Encompassing more than 1,200 square miles, Los Padres National Forest is perfect for those who love the outdoors and offers a variety of exciting opportunities for adventure from Santa Paula.

Conclusion

With its rich culture, natural beauty, and friendly locals, Santa Paula is a must-visit destination in California. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Follow this travel guide, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable experience in Santa Paula.
