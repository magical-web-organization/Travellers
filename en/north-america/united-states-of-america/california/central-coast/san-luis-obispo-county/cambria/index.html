Introduction to Cambria

Located in San Luis Obispo County, in Central Coast, in California, in the United States of America, Cambria is a picturesque small town surrounded by natural beauty. Known for its beautiful coastline, Cambria also boasts a rich history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Cambria

From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in Cambria.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

In Cambria, you’ll find several must-visit museums and historical sites, including:

  • Cambria Historical Museum: Housed in an old schoolhouse, this museum showcases the town’s history through exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • Nitt Witt Ridge: This quirky historical landmark is a testament to the unique personality of Cambria. It’s a must-visit for anyone interested in unusual architecture and design.
  • Hearst Castle: Just a short drive north of Cambria, this grand estate was built by newspaper magnate William Randolph Hearst and is considered one of the most spectacular homes in America.

Natural Attractions of Cambria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the best things about Cambria is the stunning natural beauty that surrounds it. Here are some of the top natural attractions to check out in the area:

  • Moonstone Beach: This beach is named for the smooth, round stones that wash up on its shores. It’s a great spot for a relaxing stroll, picnic, or even a dip in the ocean.
  • Fiscalini Ranch Preserve: This 430-acre nature sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife, including sea otters, whales, and more than 200 species of birds. The preserve features several hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding hills.
  • Piedras Blancas Elephant Seal Rookery: This protected area is home to hundreds of elephant seals, which can be seen lounging on the beach and sparring with each other. It’s a unique and unforgettable sight to see!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cambria

If you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of Cambria on foot or by bike, you’re in luck. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • Coastal Trail: This 1.5-mile trail runs along the coastline and offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding hills.
  • Bluff Trail: This 3-mile trail winds through Fiscalini Ranch Preserve and offers panoramic views of the ocean and Cambria’s coastline.
  • Santa Rosa Creek Trail: This 2-mile trail travels through a peaceful oak forest and along scenic Santa Rosa Creek. It’s a great spot for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Cambria: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural attractions and cultural sites, there are plenty of fun activities and tours to enjoy in Cambria:

  • Wine Tasting: The Central Coast of California is famous for its excellent wine, and Cambria is no exception. Take a tour of one of the local wineries and savor some of the region’s best varietals.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore Cambria’s scenic countryside on horseback. Several local ranches offer guided rides through the hills and along the coastline.
  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour from nearby Morro Bay and get up close and personal with the majestic whales that migrate through the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cambria

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Cambria, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Cambria Historical Museum and take a stroll along Moonstone Beach
2 Take a hike through Fiscalini Ranch Preserve and enjoy a wine tasting at one of the local wineries
3 Explore the unique architecture of Nitt Witt Ridge and take a tour of Hearst Castle
4 Spend the day whale watching in Morro Bay
5 Enjoy a horseback ride through the countryside and then relax with an afternoon at one of the local spas
6 Explore the Piedras Blancas Elephant Seal Rookery and then take a sunset sail along the coastline

Food and Drink Scene of Cambria

Cambria may be small, but it boasts a vibrant food and drink scene that’s sure to delight any palate.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Abalone: This local seafood delicacy can be prepared in several ways, including grilled, breaded, and fried.
  • Artichokes: The Central Coast is famous for its artichokes, and you can find them on many restaurant menus in Cambria.
  • Olallieberry Pie: This sweet pie is made with local olallieberries and is a must-try for anyone visiting Cambria.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: As mentioned, the Central Coast is home to some of the best wineries in the country. Be sure to try some of the local varietals, including pinot noir, chardonnay, and syrah.
  • Beer: Several local breweries offer refreshing craft beers that are perfect for sipping on a sunny day in Cambria.

Where to Stay in Cambria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cambria offers a variety of accommodations for every budget and taste, including cozy inns, boutique hotels, and beachfront resorts.

  • Cambria Pines Lodge: This rustic lodge is set on 25 acres of beautiful gardens and offers a variety of rooms, suites, and cottages to choose from.
  • Sea Otter Inn: This charming inn is just a short walk from Moonstone Beach and features cozy rooms with fireplaces and private balconies.
  • FogCatcher Inn: This beachfront hotel offers stunning ocean views, a complimentary breakfast, and beautifully appointed rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Cambria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home from Cambria, here are some suggestions:

  • Pottery: Many local artisans create beautiful pottery using traditional techniques. Look for pieces that feature the colors and patterns of the Central Coast.
  • Olive Oil: The climate and soil of the Central Coast are perfect for growing olives, and there are several local producers that offer high-quality olive oil.
  • Artwork: The stunning natural beauty of Cambria has inspired many artists, and you can find a variety of paintings, prints, and sculptures for sale in local galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Cambria

Cambria is a tight-knit community that values tradition, creativity, and sustainability. Visitors are encouraged to respect the natural environment and to support local businesses by buying and eating locally.

Best Time to Visit Cambria

The best time to visit Cambria is in the spring and fall, when temperatures are mild and crowds are smaller. Summer can be busy and hot, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cambria

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty: Take advantage of the many hiking trails, beaches, and nature preserves that surround Cambria.
  • Support local businesses: Cambria’s small businesses are a vital part of the community, and your support can make a big difference.
  • Take a tour: Whether it’s a wine tasting tour or a house tour of Hearst Castle, tours are a great way to learn more about Cambria’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind: Always pack out what you bring in and leave the natural areas of Cambria as pristine as you found them.
  • Disturb the wildlife: Whether it’s elephant seals, birds, or other creatures, it’s important to observe wildlife from a distance and not disturb their natural habitat.
  • Forget your sunscreen: With plenty of sunny days throughout the year, it’s important to protect your skin from harmful UV rays.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cambria

While Cambria is generally a very safe town, visitors should take standard precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. This includes locking doors and windows of your accommodations and not leaving valuables visible in your car.

Health in Cambria

As with any travel destination, it’s important to stay healthy while visiting Cambria. Be sure to drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and protect yourself from ticks if you’re hiking or spending time outdoors. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you, as medical services may be limited in the area.

Getting to Cambria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cambria is accessible by car or bus, and the nearest airports are in San Luis Obispo and Santa Barbara.

Airports Near Cambria

  • San Luis Obispo County Regional Airport: Located about 40 minutes south of Cambria, this airport offers flights to several major U.S. cities.
  • Santa Barbara Municipal Airport: Located about 2 hours south of Cambria, this airport offers flights to several major U.S. and international cities.

Bus Stations Near Cambria

  • Greyhound Bus: There is a Greyhound bus station in nearby San Luis Obispo, which is about 40 minutes south of Cambria.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore the Central Coast at your own pace.
  • Private Transportation: Several local companies offer private transportation services to and from Cambria and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Cambria

While having a car is ideal for exploring the surrounding area, it’s also possible to get around Cambria on foot, by bike, or with local transportation services.

  • Walk: Cambria’s small town center is easy to navigate on foot, and many of the top attractions are within walking distance of each other.
  • Bike: There are several bike rental companies in town, and the quiet streets and scenic paths make for a great biking experience.
  • Shuttle Services: Several local companies offer shuttle services that can take you to nearby attractions, wineries, and beaches.

Where to Go Next from Cambria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip or explore more of the Central Coast, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Big Sur: Just an hour north of Cambria lies the breathtaking Big Sur coastline. With its rugged cliffs, towering redwoods, and stunning ocean views, it’s a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Paso Robles: About 45 minutes northeast of Cambria, Paso Robles is a charming wine country town with plenty of tasting rooms, restaurants, and shops to explore.
  • San Luis Obispo: Just 40 minutes south of Cambria, San Luis Obispo is a vibrant college town with a lively downtown area, outdoor markets, and a historic mission.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking natural beauty, cultural attractions, or simply a relaxing getaway, Cambria is the perfect destination. With its stunning coastline, rich history, and vibrant culture, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this beautiful Central Coast town.
