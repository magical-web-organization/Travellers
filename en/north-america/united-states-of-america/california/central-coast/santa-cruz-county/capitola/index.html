Introduction to Capitola

Located in Santa Cruz County, in Central Coast, in California, in the United States of America, Capitola is a unique beach town with picturesque scenery and a friendly atmosphere. The town is nestled on the coast of Monterey Bay and is known for its vibrant culture, stunning beaches, and historic landmarks.

With an abundance of outdoor activities, excellent dining options, and an array of cultural attractions, Capitola is a perfect destination for weekend getaways, family vacations, and romantic retreats. In this guide, we will share everything you need to know about Capitola, including the top things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Capitola

Whether you love the outdoors or are more of a history buff, Capitola has something for everyone. Here is a list of the top places to visit during your trip to Capitola:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Capitola Historical Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the town’s rich history and diverse culture, including exhibits on the early days of surfing in California and the town’s founding families.
  • Capitola Venetian Hotel: Built-in 1924, this historic landmark is a must-visit for architecture lovers. The hotel’s unique design, complete with spires and arches, was inspired by the city of Venice, Italy.
  • The Pacific Gardens Inn: This beautiful inn is located just steps away from the beach, and it’s a perfect getaway for couples seeking a romantic retreat.

Natural Attractions of Capitola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Capitola Beach: This beautiful sandy beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, surfing, and other water activities. With its stunning views and crystal blue waters, it is one of the most popular beaches in California.
  • Capitola Wharf: This historic landmark is located at the end of Capitola Beach and is a great place to go fishing and enjoy the beautiful views of Monterey Bay.
  • New Brighton State Beach: Located a short drive from Capitola, New Brighton State Beach offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of the town. It’s a perfect destination for camping, hiking, and nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Capitola

Whether you prefer hiking or biking, Capitola has some of the most scenic trails in California. Here are the top hiking and biking routes in Capitola:

  • Capitola to New Brighton Beach Trail: This six-mile trail starts at Capitola Beach and follows the coastline to New Brighton Beach, offering stunning views of the ocean along the way.
  • Soquel Demonstration Forest: This beautiful forest offers over 50 miles of hiking and biking trails through redwood trees, creeks, and amazing views of the surrounding area.
  • Nisene Marks State Park: Located a short drive from Capitola, this park offers over 30 miles of hiking and biking trails through the beautiful redwood forest, streams, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Capitola: Activities and Tours

Capitola offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the top things to do during your trip to Capitola:

  • Surfing Lessons: With its prime location near some of the best surf breaks in California, Capitola is the perfect place to learn how to surf. There are several surf schools in town that offer lessons for beginners and experienced surfers.
  • Wine Tasting: There are several wineries located near Capitola that offer wine-tasting tours, including Storrs Winery, Sones Cellars, and Burrell School Vineyards.
  • Kayaking and Stand-Up Paddleboarding: Experience the beauty of Capitola Bay from the water by renting a kayak or stand-up paddleboard. Several rental companies offer hourly and daily rentals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Capitola

If you are planning to spend a week in Capitola, here is a sample itinerary that covers all the must-see places and activities:

Food and Drink Scene of Capitola

Capitola is home to several excellent dining options that cater to all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Capitola:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Tacos: Fresh fish tacos are a staple dish in Capitola, and there are several restaurants that offer delicious versions of this classic beach dish. Check out Margaritaville or Paradise Beach Grille for some of the best fish tacos in town.
  • Chowder Soup: Capitola offers several restaurants that specialize in chowder soup. If you’re a fan of seafood and creamy goodness, then you’ll love chowder soup from places like The Crow’s Nest or Zeldas.
  • Beach Burgers: Capitola’s burgers are legendary, with several mouthwatering options available. Head to Mr. Toots for a classic burger, or drive a little further to Aptos and try Aptos BBQ for some of the best burgers in California.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Capitola offers several local breweries that offer tasty craft beers, including Sante Adairius Rustic Ales and Corralitos Brewing Company.
  • Wine: The Santa Cruz Mountains offer excellent wines, and there are several wineries located near Capitola that offer wine-tasting tours.

Where to Stay in Capitola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Capitola offers several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Capitola:

  • Capitola Venetian Hotel: This historic hotel offers beautifully appointed rooms with ocean views. It’s the perfect option for those seeking a touch of class and luxury.
  • Capitola Inn: Located just a few blocks from the beach, this hotel offers convenient access to all of Capitola’s top attractions.
  • Quality Inn & Suites Capitola by the Sea: This family-friendly hotel offers spacious rooms, a heated outdoor pool, and a hot tub.

What to Buy in Capitola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Capitola offers several unique shopping experiences. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Surf Gear: Capitola is known for its excellent surf breaks, and there are several surf shops where you can purchase top-quality surf gear.
  • Pottery: Capitola has a thriving arts community, and there are several pottery studios where you can purchase handmade ceramics.
  • Local Artwork: Capitola offers several art galleries and boutiques that sell unique local artwork and handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Capitola

Capitola is a friendly, laid-back town where the locals embrace a relaxed lifestyle. Here are a few tips to help you fit in with the locals:

  • Say Hello: Capitola locals are friendly and welcoming, so don’t be afraid to say hello and strike up a conversation.
  • Dress Casual: Capitola is a beach town, so dress comfortably and casually. Shorts and flip-flops are the norm.
  • Respect the Environment: Capitola locals take pride in their town’s natural beauty, so be sure to dispose of trash properly and respect the environment.

Best Time to Visit Capitola

Capitola’s weather is mild year-round, making it an excellent destination to visit anytime. However, if you’re looking for the best weather and fewer crowds, then the best time to visit Capitola is during the shoulder seasons – in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November).

Do’s and Don’ts of Capitola

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Capitola:

Do:

  • Explore the Beach: Capitola Beach is one of the town’s top attractions, and it’s a must-visit during your trip.
  • Try Surfing: Capitola is known for its excellent surfing, so why not give it a try?
  • Visit Local Wineries: The Santa Cruz Mountains offer excellent wines, so be sure to visit one of the local wineries in the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or Damage the Environment: Capitola locals take pride in their natural environment, so be sure to dispose of trash properly and respect the surroundings.
  • Leave Valuables Unattended: As with any tourist destination, it’s important to keep an eye on your belongings at all times.
  • Ignore Road Closures or Warnings: Capitola is prone to flooding during heavy rains, so make sure to heed any road closures or warnings during your visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Capitola

Capitola is a safe town, and crime rates are low. However, it’s always essential to take precautions while traveling, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Capitola

Capitola has several medical facilities, including an urgent care center and a hospital, so visitors can feel confident about receiving medical care if needed.

Getting to Capitola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Capitola

The nearest major airport to Capitola is San Jose International Airport, which is approximately 45 miles northwest of town. Other options include San Francisco International Airport, which is approximately 72 miles north of Capitola, and Monterey Regional Airport, which is approximately 40 miles south of Capitola.

Bus Stations Near Capitola

Capitola is easily accessible by bus, with several Greyhound and Amtrak bus stations located nearby. The closest stations are in Santa Cruz and Watsonville, which are both just a short drive from Capitola.

Other Options

Capitola is located just off the Pacific Coast Highway (Highway 1), making it easily accessible by car or bike. There are several car rental companies located at nearby airports, and several bike rental shops located within the town.

How to Get Around in Capitola

Capitola is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, if you prefer to use public transport, there are several bus lines that service the town. Taxis and ride-share services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Capitola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Capitola is located near several popular destinations, making it an excellent base for exploring Northern California. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Santa Cruz: Located just a few miles north of Capitola, Santa Cruz is a bustling beach town known for its vibrant culture, excellent dining, and outdoor activities.
  • Monterey: Located approximately 40 miles south of Capitola, Monterey is a beautiful coastal town known for its aquarium, historic landmarks, and art galleries.
  • San Francisco: Located approximately 70 miles north of Capitola, San Francisco is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Northern California. With iconic landmarks like the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and Cable Cars, there is no shortage of sightseeing opportunities in San Francisco.

Conclusion

Capitola is a charming, laid-back town that offers something for everyone, from rural hiking trails and outdoor excursions to cultural landmarks and beachside attractions. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Capitola.
