Introduction to Incline Village

Located in Lake Tahoe, in Sierra Nevada, in California, in the United States of America, Incline Village is a must-visit destination for those seeking scenic natural attractions and exciting outdoor adventures. This small town boasts a plethora of activities and attractions that cater to every type of traveler, from families to couples and solo explorers. Whether you want to hike through stunning backcountry or relax on stunning beaches, Incline Village has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Incline Village

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Gatekeeper’s Museum: Discover the rich history of the Lake Tahoe area and its Native American inhabitants through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • Thunderbird Lodge Historic Site: This 1930s mansion boasts a blend of luxurious living and native traditions, and visitors can explore beautifully landscaped gardens and scenic views.
  • Mark Twain Cultural Center & Toccata Guttman Music Hall: A unique gem in Incline area, this live music venue and art gallery celebrates the works of Mark Twain, the world-renowned author and humorist.

Natural Attractions of Incline Village: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Tahoe Nevada State Park: Home to the sandy beaches of Sand Harbor, this park also offers camping, boating, and picnic areas.
  • Tahoe Rim Trail: A 165-mile trail that circles Lake Tahoe, the Tahoe Rim Trail offers hikers stunning views through forests, meadows, and mountain ridges.
  • Diamond Peak Ski Resort: This ski resort attracts visitors in the wintertime, but also offers summer activities like hiking, mountain biking, and zip-lining.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Incline Village

  • Flume Trail: A 13-mile epic ride (or hike) that offers scenic views of Lake Tahoe, as well as the dense forest and mountains surrounding Incline Village.
  • Tunnel Creek Trail: A moderate-level hike or bike ride that offers views of the glittering water before embarking on a hike through the Carson Range’s lovely forested slopes.
  • Eagle Rock Hiking Trail: A popular, family-friendly hike that offers stunning panoramic views of Lake Tahoe.

Best Things to Do in Incline Village: Activities and Tours

  • Parasailing: If you’re looking for a one-of-a-kind adventure, consider parasailing high above the blue waters of Lake Tahoe.
  • Boating: Lake Tahoe offers boating for every type of traveler, from catamaran rides to kayak and paddleboard rentals.
  • Guided Tours: Join a knowledgeable guide for one of many types of tours, including nature walks, historical tours, and brewery tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Incline Village

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pistol Pete’s BBQ & Cafe: Try their mouth-watering pulled pork, brisket, and ribs.
  • Bite: This upscale restaurant offers innovative dishes, including Hokkaido scallops, grilled Spanish octopus, and pan-seared duck breast.
  • Crosby’s: Located at the Championship Golf Course, this restaurant boasts panoramic views and offers tasty dishes like coconut breaded shrimp, wagyu beef sliders, and lamb lollipops.

Local Beverages

  • Alibi Ale Works: This trendy local brewery offers a variety of seasonal beers, complemented by a relaxing outdoor seating area and great music.
  • Lone Eagle Grille: Relax with the beautiful lake views while enjoying a wide variety of excellent cocktails, wines and craft beer to enjoy at the restaurant, on the sunny deck or outside by the bonfire.

Where to Stay in Incline Village: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hyatt Regency Lake Tahoe Resort, Spa, and Casino: This luxurious resort offers direct access to Lake Tahoe. Enjoy the many amenities, from private beach to casino complex, two-story spa, and several dining options.
  • Club Tahoe Resort: Featuring a pool, spa, and various outdoor opportunities, this comfortable resort offers a peaceful and relaxing escape.
  • Parkside Inn at Incline: This quaint, budget-friendly inn is perfect for solo or group travelers. Enjoy a range of adventures, from kayaking to snowshoeing, and excellent dining and nightlife options nearby.

What to Buy in Incline Village: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tahoe Blue Vodka: This locally distilled vodka is made from pure alpine water and the finest grains; it’s perfect for a refreshing post-hike cocktail.
  • Tahoe Queen: This medium-roasted coffee blend is a must-try for coffee aficionados, with its subtle nutty flavor and smooth finish.
  • Lake Tahoe Apparel: From t-shirts to knit caps, you’ll find everything you need to celebrate your trip to Incline Village.

Local Culture and Customs of Incline Village

  • Respect the environment: Incline Village is a beautiful natural paradise that must be respected. Ensure you follow Leave No Trace principles and respect the “pack-out” policy for waste and trash.
  • Tip expectation for your servers and bartenders is around 20%. Bars naturally have a lower tip bar of $1 to $2 per drink.
  • During summer months, the community asks their visitors to consider minimizing visits on weekends, as the area is heavily populated then.

Best Time to Visit Incline Village

Incline Village is a year-round attraction, with its gorgeous summer activities and exciting wintertime attractions. The best time to visit Incline Village is from May through October, with pleasant temperatures and sunny days ideal for outdoor activities. For skiing enthusiasts, the best time is during the winter months, starting from December until March, offering excellent snowfall and cold breezes while the spring offers its own magical scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Incline Village

Do:

  • Do carry insect repellent for hikes as many trails in the area have mosquitos and other bugs.
  • Do take cash in case you are planning to visit a small shop or local market.
  • Do check ahead for reservations and availability, especially in peak season.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget your sunscreen and sunglasses to protect yourself from strong sun rays, and ensure to wear proper footwear while hiking.
  • Don’t take plant and animal souvenirs with you; it is not only illegal but disrupts the natural ecosystem.
  • Don’t litter – please dispose of rubbish properly.

Safety and Health in Incline Village

Safety in Incline Village

Incline Village is generally considered a safe destination, but visitors should take standard precautions against theft, including keeping valuable items locked up and out of sight. Visitors need to avoid hiking alone, especially on the more remote trails, and ensure to carry a charged cellphone with the area’s quickly reachable contacts.

Health in Incline Village

Altitude sickness can affect some, and it is recommended visitors drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol to reduce dehydration. Additionally, visitors need to pack essentials such as sunscreen, insect repellent, and first-aid kits, as smaller stores in the area may not stock these items.

Getting to Incline Village: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Incline Village

  • Reno-Tahoe International Airport: Located approximately 34 miles south of Incline Village, this airport offers direct flights from major domestic cities and is the nearest destination.

Bus Stations Near Incline Village

  • North Lake Tahoe Express: This service offers scheduled shuttles from the Reno-Tahoe International Airport to Incline Village and other Tahoe area destinations.

Other Options

  • Car rental: A car rental is recommended in the Incline Village area to have easy access to activities and excursions.
  • Taxi and ride-sharing services: These can be convenient for shorter trips or to get to nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Incline Village

Incline Village is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or rented bicycles. Public transportation is uneven in the area, so visitors should consider renting a car to explore the natural attractions outside the city.

Where to Go Next from Incline Village: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Reno-Tahoe Territory: A bustling city well-known for its many casinos and outdoor adventures, from hiking to ski resorts.
  • Virginia City: This charming, historical mining town is known for its delightful ambience, museums, and cultural sites, as well as the attraction, the Virginia and Truckee Railroad.
  • Carson City: Discover this bustling capital city that has many museums, eateries, and a good shopping scene, which is at a convenient distance from Incline Village.

Conclusion

Incline Village is a beautiful, year-round destination that offers something for everyone. With a wealth of natural attractions, along with several cultural and historical sites, visitors will find plenty of exciting adventures and activities to enjoy. From skiing to hiking and enjoying good food and drink with lake view, Incline Village never disappoints. With this comprehensive tourist guide, visitors to Incline Village will be well-prepared for a memorable trip.
