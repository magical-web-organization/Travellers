Introduction to Owens Valley

Nestled in the eastern Sierra Nevada region of California, Owens Valley is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and explore the great outdoors. The valley is surrounded by towering peaks, crystalline waters, and vast expanses of open land that make it perfect for hiking, biking, fishing, and other outdoor activities. If you’re planning a trip to Owens Valley, this comprehensive tour guide has everything you need to know to make your visit unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Owens Valley

There are plenty of attractions and things to do in Owens Valley for visitors of all interests. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Laws Museum - Learn about the history of the valley and the life of the miners and settlers who once called it home at this museum.
  • Manzanar National Historic Site - This historic site provides a look into the lives of Japanese Americans who were interned during WWII.

Natural Attractions of Owens Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alabama Hills Recreation Area - This area boasts a unique landscape of tumbled rocks and boulder formations that are perfect for hiking, rock climbing, horseback riding, and mountain biking.
  • Bishop Creek Canyon - This natural wonder is a great place for hiking, fishing, and taking in the amazing views of the crystal clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Owens Valley

Owens Valley is a hiker’s paradise with numerous trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • Mount Whitney - This route is perfect for experienced hikers who want to climb the highest peak in the contiguous United States.
  • Long Lake Trail - This trail offers stunning views of the Sierras and is a great moderate level hike.

Best Things to Do in Owens Valley: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Owens Valley, such as:

  • Bishop Mule Days - This annual festival features mules and donkeys in competitions, parades, and other events.
  • Sierra Nevada Experience - Take a scenic flight over the Sierras to enjoy the stunning views from the air.

Week-Long Itinerary for Owens Valley

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Owens Valley, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore Laws Museum and Bishop City Park.
2 Hike the Long Lake Trail.
3 Take a scenic flight over the Sierras with Sierra Nevada Experience.
4 Spend the day at Alabama Hills Recreation Area.
5 Visit the Manzanar National Historic Site.
6 Drive scenic Highway 168 and fish at Bishop Creek Canyon.
7 Enjoy the Bishop Mule Days festival (if timing allows) or relax and take in the views.

Food and Drink Scene of Owens Valley

No trip to Owens Valley would be complete without indulging in the local food and drink scene. Here’s what you need to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bishop Burger - A juicy burger served with local cheese and toppings.
  • Basque Family-Style Dining at the 180 Room - Enjoy a hearty family-style meal that features traditional Basque cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Snowy Range Brewery - This local brewery features a wide variety of craft beers and delicious food selections.
  • Lone Pine Brewing Company - Taste locally brewed beers while enjoying the beautiful mountain views.

Where to Stay in Owens Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodations options in Owens Valley that cater to different travelers’ needs and preferences. Here are some of the top options:

  • Creekside Inn - A charming bed and breakfast that offers a relaxing and comfortable stay.
  • Hostel California - A budget-friendly option that offers shared and private rooms.

What to Buy in Owens Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Owens Valley home with you by purchasing unique souvenirs and local products such as:

  • Bishop Arts and Crafts Show - This year-round show features local artisans who create unique and beautiful crafts.
  • Mammoth Brewing Company - Purchase locally brewed beer and merchandise to take home as a gift or a reminder of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Owens Valley

Owens Valley has a rich history and culture that has been shaped by the various miners, ranchers, and other settlers who once called the region home. Respect the local culture and customs by:

  • Visiting the Laws Museum - Learn about the local history and heritage.
  • Attending a Native American Powwow - Immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Owens Valley

The best time to visit Owens Valley is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Summer can be quite hot, while winters can be too harsh with occasional snowstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Owens Valley

Here are some things to do and avoid doing while in Owens Valley:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment - This includes following the Leave No Trace principles and being mindful of wildlife.
  • Support local businesses - Support the local community by shopping and dining at local establishments.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Help keep the valley beautiful by disposing of your waste properly.
  • Trespass - Respect private property and stay on designated trails and recreation areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Owens Valley

Owens Valley is generally a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of potential hazards such as:

  • Wildlife - Keep a safe distance from wildlife, and always carry bear spray or other wildlife deterrents when hiking.
  • Weather - Check the local weather forecast and be prepared for sudden changes in weather, especially in higher elevation areas.

Health in Owens Valley

The valley has a few medical facilities in the case of an emergency, but visitors should also take precautions for their health, such as:

  • Sunscreen and hydration - Protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays by wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated.
  • Altitude sickness - If you plan on hiking or visiting higher altitude areas, acclimate yourself gradually to avoid altitude sickness.

Getting to Owens Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Owens Valley is accessible by several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Owens Valley

  • Bishop Airport - The closest airport to Owens Valley, located just south of Bishop.

Bus Stations Near Owens Valley

  • Eastern Sierra Transit Authority - Provides public transit to various destinations within the valley.

Other Options

  • Rental Car - Renting a car is the most convenient way to explore the valley and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Owens Valley

The best way to get around Owens Valley is by renting a car, especially if you plan on exploring the more remote areas of the valley. However, public transit and bike rentals are also available for those who prefer alternative transportation options.

Where to Go Next from Owens Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Owens Valley is situated in a beautiful part of California that offers visitors a chance to explore other nearby destinations. Some popular excursions include:

  • Death Valley National Park - A unique desert landscape that’s only a few hours away from Owens Valley.
  • Yosemite National Park - A world-famous park known for its stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Owens Valley is a spectacular destination that offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in nature and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities and cultural attractions. Use this comprehensive tour guide to help plan your trip and make lasting memories in this beautiful part of California.
