Introduction to Glenn County

Located in the picturesque Sacramento Valley in California, Glenn County is a land of contrasts. Home to two cities, Willows and Orland, and numerous small towns and farming communities, Glenn County is a blend of urban and rural landscapes that offers a unique experience for visitors.

Glenn County boasts a wealth of attractions, from culturally and historically significant sites to natural wonders. It is also known for its recreational opportunities, including hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and camping sites.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to see and do in Glenn County, as well as where to stay, eat, and shop. We’ll also provide practical information on safety, health, transportation, and more, so you can plan your trip with confidence.

List of Places to Visit in Glenn County

Here’s a list of must-see attractions in Glenn County.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Glenn County Museum in Willows is a treasure trove of artifacts and memorabilia that tell the story of the county’s colorful past. Exhibits include early Native American life, the arrival of Spanish explorers, the Gold Rush, and more.

  • The Sacramento National Wildlife Refuge Complex in Willows is a 10,819-acre (43.8 km2) protected area comprising several different refuges. It is home to dozens of species of birds and other wildlife, making it a popular spot for birdwatching and nature enthusiasts.

  • The Orland Buttes Recreation Area in Orland is a stunning wilderness area covering 28,000 acres (110 km2). Visitors can hike, bike, and explore the rugged landscape, which features scenic views, volcanic rock formations, and abundant wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Glenn County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stony Gorge Reservoir near Elk Creek is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and camping. The reservoir covers 1,500 acres (6.1 km2) and is stocked with various species of fish, including bass, trout, and catfish.

  • Black Butte Lake Recreation Area near Orland is a 4,460-acre (18.0 km2) man-made lake surrounded by rolling hills and oak woodlands. Visitors can fish, swim, boat, hike, and camp in the area.

  • Sacramento River National Wildlife Refuge in Willows is a 10,819-acre (43.8 km2) protected area along the Sacramento River. Visitors can hike, birdwatch, and observe wildlife in its natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glenn County

Glenn County has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the landscape. Some of the best routes include:

  • Sacramento River Trail, a 9.4-mile (15.1 km) trail that runs along the Sacramento River and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Orland Unit Trails, a network of trails that wind through the Orland Buttes Recreation Area, offering a challenging hike and stunning views.
  • Black Butte Trail, a 7.1-mile (11.4 km) trail that climbs to the summit of Black Butte, a volcanic peak that offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Glenn County: Activities and Tours

Glenn County offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the county’s rolling hills and scenic countryside.
  • Go fishing at one of the many lakes and rivers in the area, including Stony Gorge Reservoir, Black Butte Lake, and the Sacramento River.
  • Visit the Annual Glenn County Fair, a week-long celebration of the county’s agricultural heritage, featuring carnival rides, live music, and a variety of food vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Glenn County

Glenn County is renowned for its fresh produce, locally sourced ingredients, and farm-to-table restaurants. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled Tri-Tip, a classic California dish that originated in the Central Valley and is a staple in many local restaurants.
  • Orland Artichokes, a local specialty that’s famous for its sweet, tender flavor and delicate texture.
  • Glenn County Almonds, a popular snack that’s grown locally and comes in a variety of flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Sierra Nevada Pale Ale, a classic West Coast beer that’s brewed in nearby Chico.
  • New Clairvaux Vineyard, a winery in nearby Vina that’s famous for its award-winning wines.
  • Oregon Street Brewery, a microbrewery in downtown Orland that serves a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Glenn County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Glenn County offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and traveler. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Baymont by Wyndham Willows, a budget-friendly hotel in downtown Willows that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • Newville Inn Bed and Breakfast, a charming B&B located in the heart of the Sacramento Valley that offers a peaceful and relaxing retreat.
  • Oak Creek RV Park, a quiet and scenic campground near Stony Gorge Reservoir that’s perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Glenn County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Glenn County is known for its local products and crafts, such as handmade pottery, jewelry, and paintings. Here are a few places to shop for souvenirs:

  • Glenn County Farmer’s Markets, a network of markets that feature locally grown produce, crafts, and handmade products.
  • Willows Gift and Thrift Shop, a thrift store in downtown Willows that offers a wide variety of unique and quirky items.
  • The Mendocino National Forest Discovery Center, a visitor center that offers a selection of books, maps, and souvenirs related to the nearby wilderness area.

Local Culture and Customs of Glenn County

Glenn County is largely a rural and farming community, characterized by its friendly and down-to-earth residents and strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Saying “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals
  • Dressing modestly and appropriately in rural areas
  • Being mindful of road safety and wildlife while driving

Best Time to Visit Glenn County

Glenn County can be visited throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall months when temperatures are mild, and the landscape is lush and green. Summer is also a popular time to visit, especially for outdoor activities, but temperatures can be hot and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glenn County

Here are a few suggested do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Glenn County:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the area
  • Visit local farmers’ markets and support local businesses

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in nature areas
  • Disrupt wildlife or destroy natural habitats
  • Use offensive language or behave disrespectfully toward locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Glenn County

Glenn County is a safe destination for visitors, but visitors should take appropriate precautions, such as:

  • Staying aware of their surroundings and avoiding dangerous areas, especially at night
  • Keeping valuables secure and out of sight
  • Following traffic laws and driving defensively

Health in Glenn County

Glenn County has several hospitals and medical clinics that provide quality medical care for visitors. It’s also recommended that visitors take precautions to avoid heatstroke and dehydration during the summer months.

Getting to Glenn County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Glenn County

The two closest airports to Glenn County are:

  • Chico Municipal Airport (CIC), located approximately 25 miles (40 km) north of Willows.
  • Sacramento International Airport (SMF), located approximately 80 miles (130 km) southeast of Willows.

Bus Stations Near Glenn County

Greyhound offers bus service to the following locations near Glenn County:

  • Orland, CA (ORL)
  • Willows, CA (WIL)
  • Corning, CA (CNG)

Other Options

Glenn County is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area, including Interstate 5 and California State Route 162.

How to Get Around in Glenn County

Glenn County is best explored by car, as many of the attractions and activities are located outside of the cities and towns. Visitors can also rent bicycles and explore the area’s numerous bike-friendly routes.

Where to Go Next from Glenn County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Glenn County is a convenient starting point for exploring other nearby areas, such as:

  • Lake Shasta, a vast wilderness area located approximately 2 hours northeast of Willows, offering unparalleled opportunities for fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Lassen Volcanic National Park, approximately 2 hours north of Willows, offering spectacular views of volcanic peaks, geothermal features, and stunning mountain lakes.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Glenn County has something for everyone. From its scenic landscapes and outdoor activities to its rich cultural heritage and warm hospitality, Glenn County is a destination that’s worth exploring. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful corner of California.
