Introduction to Sacramento County

Sacramento County is a picturesque destination located in Sacramento Valley, California. Known for its rich history, cultural significance, and natural beauty, the county attracts tourists from all over the world. With a unique blend of urban and rural experiences, Sacramento County offers something for everyone.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the county’s must-visit attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking trails, and food and drink scene. We will also provide you with important information on where to stay, what to buy, and safety and health tips to ensure you have a memorable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Sacramento County

Sacramento County is replete with places that offer historical, cultural, and natural significance. Here are the top places you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. California State Capitol Museum: The State Capitol is the heart of California’s government and also a museum that explores California’s history and culture.

  2. Crocker Art Museum: The museum has an extensive collection of Californian, European, and Asian art, as well as special temporary exhibitions.

  3. Sutter’s Fort State Historic Park: This former military and trading post offers insights into life in the 19th century in Sacramento and California.

  4. California State Railroad Museum: The museum contains the country’s largest collection of historic railway locomotives, cars, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Sacramento County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. American River Bike Trail: This trail offers stunning views of the city and its landscapes.

  2. Lake Tahoe: The lake is famous for its stunning scenery, skiing, swimming, camping, and hiking.

  3. Folsom Lake Recreation Area: This park offers a variety of outdoor activities, such as biking, hiking, fishing, camping, and boating.

  4. Cosumnes River Preserve: The preserve is home to a variety of flora and fauna and offers hiking trails, bird watching, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sacramento County

With its breathtaking natural scenery, Sacramento County has a plethora of hiking and biking routes to choose from. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  1. American River Bike Trail: This is a paved trail that follows the American River for 32 miles and offers scenic views of the countryside and the city.

  2. Jedediah Smith Memorial Trail: This trail is 32 miles long and offers breathtaking views of the Sacramento River and its surroundings.

  3. Cronan Ranch Trail: This trail offers a mix of single-track and fire roads, challenging climbs, and stunning views of the Sierra Nevada.

  4. Yolo Causeway Bike Path: This is a 3.5-mile trail that runs over a causeway and offers unobstructed views of the wetlands and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Sacramento County: Activities and Tours

Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a food and drink connoisseur, Sacramento County has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in the county:

  1. Take a food and drink tour of the city: Sacramento is renowned for its farm-to-fork cuisine and craft beers.

  2. Visit Sacramento Zoo: The zoo is home to a variety of animals, including endangered species.

  3. Take a walking tour of Old Sacramento: This historic district offers insights into California’s history and culture.

  4. Go rafting in the American River: The river offers a thrilling experience for those who love adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sacramento County

Here’s a week-long itinerary for your Sacramento County travel:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the California State Capitol Museum and Old Sacramento.
Day 2 Go on a food and drink tour of Sacramento.
Day 3 Explore the American River Bike Trail and the Jedediah Smith Memorial Trail.
Day 4 Visit the Crocker Art Museum and the California State Railroad Museum.
Day 5 Go camping and hiking at Folsom Lake Recreation Area or Cosumnes River Preserve.
Day 6 Take a day trip to Lake Tahoe.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the city’s beautiful parks and gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Sacramento County

The farm-to-fork cuisine and local craft beer scene make Sacramento County an ideal destination for food and drink enthusiasts. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Grilled Tri-Tip: A popular beef dish that’s marinated and grilled to perfection.

  2. Gumbo: A stew made with seafood or meat and served with rice.

  3. Crab Cakes: Delicious patties made with fresh crab meat.

  4. Sourdough Bread: A staple in California’s cuisine that’s perfect for dipping in soups or using for sandwiches.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Sacramento County has an abundance of breweries that offer a variety of craft beers.

  2. Wine: California produces some of the world’s best wines, and Sacramento County is no exception.

Where to Stay in Sacramento County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you are looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Sacramento County has something for everyone. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Hyatt Regency Sacramento: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city.

  2. The Citizen Hotel: A boutique hotel with spacious, elegant rooms and beautiful views of the city.

  3. The Greens Hotel: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

  4. HI Sacramento Hostel: A fantastic option for travelers on a budget, where guests can choose from dorms or private rooms.

What to Buy in Sacramento County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sacramento County is renowned for its unique products that make for perfect souvenirs. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  1. California Olive Oil: The county is home to some of the best olive oil producers in the state.

  2. Honey: The county’s apiaries produce some of the best honey in the country.

  3. Local Art: The county is home to several artists who create unique pieces.

  4. Wine: Sacramento County has several wineries, making it an ideal destination to purchase wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Sacramento County

Sacramento County reflects the state’s diverse culture and customs. People are friendly, easygoing, and welcoming. The county embraces its history and cultures through various community events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Sacramento County

The best time to visit Sacramento County is between April and October when the weather is pleasant, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sacramento County

Do:

  1. Respect local culture and customs.

  2. Explore the city’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

  3. Try the farm-to-fork cuisine and local craft beer.

  4. Take a walking tour of Old Sacramento.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the parks and natural surroundings.

  2. Engage in illegal activities or behavior.

  3. Disrespect monuments and historic sites.

  4. Leave valuables in your car.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sacramento County

Sacramento County is generally safe, with low crime rates. However, tourists should take precautions to ensure their safety and avoid any inconvenience.

Health in Sacramento County

Sacramento County has excellent healthcare facilities, with several hospitals and medical centers in the area. Visitors should ensure they have health insurance before their trip.

Getting to Sacramento County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sacramento County is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus. Here are some transportation options you can consider:

Airports Near Sacramento County

  1. Sacramento International Airport: Located 10 miles northwest of the city and offers flights to several domestic and international destinations.

  2. Oakland International Airport: Located 80 miles from Sacramento, it offers several flights to domestic locations.

Bus Stations Near Sacramento County

  1. Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in downtown Sacramento and offers several routes.

Other Options

  1. Amtrak: Offers train service to several locations within California and beyond.

  2. Rental Cars: Several rental car companies operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Sacramento County

Sacramento County has an extensive public transportation system, including buses, light rails, taxis, and ride-sharing services. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Sacramento County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sacramento County is strategically located within driving distance to several exciting destinations in California. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. San Francisco: A popular destination known for its iconic landmarks, such as the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz Island.

  2. Yosemite National Park: A must-visit attraction that’s famous for its stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and natural beauty.

  3. Lake Tahoe: An excellent destination for winter sports and summer activities, such as hiking and camping.

Conclusion

Sacramento County offers a plethora of experiences, from cultural and historical attractions to outdoor adventures and farm-to-fork cuisine. In this guide, we have provided you with a list of must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, food, and drink scene, and other important information to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Sacramento County. So, pack your bags, prepare your itinerary, and get ready to explore the beauty and cultural richness of Sacramento County.
