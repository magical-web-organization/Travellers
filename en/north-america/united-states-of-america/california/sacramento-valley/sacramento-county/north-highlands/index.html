Introduction to North Highlands

North Highlands is a small unincorporated community in Sacramento County, in the Sacramento Valley of California, USA. It is a diverse neighborhood that offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions and activities, making it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a family vacation. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best of North Highlands, including its top places to visit, things to do, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in North Highlands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aerospace Museum of California: Discover the history of aviation and space exploration through interactive exhibits, guided tours, and live demonstrations.
  • Effie Yeaw Nature Center: Learn about the local flora and fauna through educational displays, workshops, and hikes.
  • Folsom History Museum: Explore the rich history of Folsom through artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits.
  • Governor’s Mansion State Historic Park: Step back in time and see how California’s governors lived in the past.
  • Sutter’s Fort State Historic Park: Discover the story of John Sutter and the early pioneers who settled in California.

Natural Attractions of North Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • American River Parkway: Hike, bike, fish, and picnic along the scenic American River and its adjacent parkway.
  • Nimbus Fish Hatchery: Learn about salmon and trout hatcheries and witness a fish run in action.
  • Folsom Lake State Recreation Area: Enjoy swimming, water sports, camping, and hiking in this popular state park with scenic lake views.
  • Lake Natoma: Rent a boat or kayak and explore the calm waters of this beautiful lake situated within the American River Parkway.
  • Ancil Hoffman Park: Walk along the well-maintained trails and witness the wildlife, including deer, wild turkeys, and waterfowl.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Highlands

  • American River Bike Trail: A paved 32-mile bike trail that runs from Sacramento to Folsom, following the scenic American River.
  • Jedediah Smith Memorial Trail: A popular hiking and biking trail that follows the American River and has access to several parks, beaches, and riverfront restaurants.
  • Discovery Park Loop: A 5-mile flat trail that runs along the Sacramento River and offers a scenic view of the city skyline.
  • Effie Yeaw Nature Trail: A 2.5-mile loop trail that explores the oak woodlands and river views of the Ancil Hoffman Park.

Best Things to Do in North Highlands: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Visit local wineries in the Sierra Foothills region and taste award-winning wines.
  • Sacramento River Cruises: Enjoy a scenic cruise along the Sacramento River and see the city from a different perspective.
  • Gold Country Adventure Tours: Take an off-road adventure tour of the Sierra Foothills and mines.
  • California State Capitol: Take a tour of the State Capital and see the offices of the Governor and State Assembly Members.
  • Old Sacramento: Explore the historic district of Sacramento, with its preserved 19th-century buildings, museums, shops, and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of North Highlands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gold Rush Chili: A hearty chili made with beef, onions, tomatoes, and beans.
  • Tri-Tip Sandwich: A popular California sandwich made with thinly sliced beef, served on a crusty roll with salsa and mayonnaise.
  • Fish Tacos: Fresh grilled or fried fish served in a taco with coleslaw and a spicy sauce.
  • Sourdough Bread: A San Francisco classic bread with a tangy flavor and chewy texture.
  • Grilled Artichokes: A California favorite, artichokes are grilled with a garlic and lemon butter sauce.

Local Beverages

  • California Wines: California is known for producing award-winning wines, including Chardonnay, Cabernet, and Zinfandel varieties.
  • Craft Beer: Sacramento has a growing craft beer scene with many local breweries offering a variety of beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Coffee: Sacramento has many small coffee shops and roasters offering a range of carefully sourced beans and artisanal brews.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Sacramento-Airport-Natomas: A comfortable hotel with free breakfast, a fitness center, and a heated outdoor pool.
  • Quality Inn Sacramento North: A budget-friendly hotel with free breakfast, an outdoor pool, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in North Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Look for handmade jewelry, pottery, and artwork from local artists and crafters.
  • Gourmet Food: Bring home locally made jams, honey, chocolates, and olive oil from the Sacramento Farmers’ Market.
  • Wine and Beer: Stock up on local wines and craft beers from wineries and breweries in the area.
  • Sourdough Bread: Take home a loaf of San Francisco-style sourdough bread from one of Sacramento’s artisanal bakeries.

Local Culture and Customs of North Highlands

  • Respect the Outdoors: North Highlands has many parks and natural attractions, so it’s important to respect the environment and leave no trace.
  • Tip Generously: Service industry workers rely on tips, so be sure to tip generously at restaurants, cafes, and bars.
  • Be Polite: North Highlanders are generally polite and friendly, so it’s important to greet people with a smile and a hello.

Best Time to Visit North Highlands

The best time to visit North Highlands is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are smaller than during the summer. The summer can be hot and crowded, while the winter can be rainy and cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Highlands

  • DO explore the outdoor attractions and trails.
  • DO dress in layers, as the weather can change throughout the day.

Don’t:

  • DON’T leave your valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Highlands

North Highlands is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions, such as locking your car and keeping your belongings close to you. Use common sense when walking alone at night, and avoid poorly lit areas. If you see any suspicious activity, call the police.

Health in North Highlands

North Highlands has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, in case of any medical emergencies. Be sure to bring any necessary medication with you, and make sure your travel insurance covers medical emergencies and accidents.

Getting to North Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Sacramento International Airport: Located 10 miles from North Highlands and served by several major airlines, including Delta, American, and Southwest.

Bus Stations Near North Highlands

  • Greyhound: Located in Sacramento, 8 miles from North Highlands.

Other Options

How to Get Around in North Highlands

The best way to get around North Highlands is by car, as public transportation is limited. However, renting a bike or taking a taxi or ride-sharing service (such as Uber or Lyft) is also an option.

Where to Go Next from North Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Highlands is a hidden gem in the Sacramento Valley, offering a mix of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and scenic views.
