Introduction to Windsor

Located in Sonoma County, in the North Coast region of California, Windsor is a charming town known for its picturesque landscapes, bustling food and drink scene, and exciting outdoor adventures. Here’s everything you need to know about planning your trip to Windsor.

List of Places to Visit in Windsor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Windsor Historical Society - A museum showcasing the early history of Sonoma County.
  2. Windsor Community Art Gallery - A gallery dedicated to promoting local artists and their works.
  3. Hembree House Cultural Center - A historic home that has been converted into a cultural center featuring exhibits and educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Windsor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Foothill Regional Park - A scenic park featuring over 200 acres of rolling hills and hiking trails.
  2. Windsor Oaks Vineyards and Winery - A popular winery offering tastings and tours of its vineyards.
  3. Russian River Valley - An idyllic area with wineries, river activities, and hillside views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windsor

  1. Shiloh Ranch Regional Park - A sprawling park featuring over 850 acres of trails and equestrian facilities.
  2. Windsor Bluebird Preserve - A nature preserve with multiple trails and birdwatching opportunities.
  3. Windsor Town Green - A 4 acre park in the center of town with biking routes and open green space.

Best Things to Do in Windsor: Activities and Tours

  1. Chill Seekers Wine Tour - A tour company that offers wine and brewery tours around the surrounding area.
  2. Windsor Farmers Market - A local farmers market with local produce and vendors.
  3. Bowling at Windsor Bowl - A fun activity for families and friends.

Week-Long Itinerary for Windsor

Food and Drink Scene of Windsor

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Windsor Certified Farmers Market - Fresh produce and local food vendors.
  2. El Farolito - A delicious Mexican restaurant with authentic cuisine.
  3. KC’s American Kitchen - A laid-back diner with classic American dishes.

Local Beverages

  1. Russian River Brewing Company - A popular brewery that serves craft beers.
  2. Breathless Wines - A winery that is known for its sparkling wine.
  3. Windsor Vineyards - A winery that offers wine tastings and vineyard tours.

Where to Stay in Windsor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hampton Inn & Suites Windsor - A newly-built hotel in the heart of Windsor.
  2. Holiday Inn Windsor Wine Country - A scenic hotel with views of the surrounding vineyards.
  3. Vineyard Creek Hotel & Spa - A spa resort with luxurious amenities and outdoor gardens.

What to Buy in Windsor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Windsor Certified Farmers Market - Local produce and handmade goods.
  2. Windsor Tasting Lounge - A gift shop offering a range of Sonoma County products.
  3. Grapevine Organic Wine Tours - A tour company that lets you bring home your favorite wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Windsor

Windsor is a welcoming town with a laid back and friendly vibe. Visitors are encouraged to participate in local events and festivals, such as the Certified Farmers Market and the Windsor Sonoma Wine & Food Affair.

Best Time to Visit Windsor

The best time to visit Windsor is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny. The town is also known for its harvest season, which runs from late September to early November when the vineyards are buzzing with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Windsor

Do:

  • Explore the local vineyards and wineries.
  • Shop at the Windsor Certified Farmers Market.
  • Attend the town’s annual events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in prohibited areas.
  • Litter in the public parks or streets.
  • Ignore local traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Windsor

Windsor is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or biking in the wilderness areas.

Health in Windsor

Windsor has several medical facilities and pharmacies throughout the town for emergency care and routine check-ups.

Getting to Windsor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Windsor

  1. Charles M. Schulz - Sonoma County Airport - Located seven miles northwest of Windsor.
  2. San Francisco International Airport - Located 62 miles southeast of Windsor.

Bus Stations Near Windsor

  1. Santa Rosa Transit Mall - A major transit hub with multiple bus routes.
  2. San Francisco Greyhound Bus Station - A long-distance bus station located 60 miles south of Windsor.

Other Options

  1. Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from one of the rental agencies located at the airport.
  2. Taxi: Several taxi companies offer services in Windsor and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Windsor

Renting a car or taking a taxi is the easiest way to get around Windsor; however, biking and hiking is also popular in the town’s natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Windsor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Healdsburg - A charming town with excellent wineries and restaurants.
  2. San Francisco - A world-famous city with diverse cultures and attractions.
  3. Sonoma - A historic town known for its wine culture and mission architecture.

Conclusion

Whether you are looking for an outdoor adventure or a relaxing weekend getaway, Windsor has something for everyone. With its natural beauty, delicious food and drink scene, and friendly locals, Windsor is the perfect destination for a memorable vacation.
