Introduction to Trinidad

Trinidad, a charming coastal town located in Humboldt County, California, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. From stunning beaches to towering redwoods, the town boasts some of the most captivating natural wonders in the state. Trinidad is home to fewer than 500 residents, making it an idyllic destination for those seeking a peaceful escape. The town is rich in natural beauty, and offers a blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary experiences. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Trinidad has got something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, local culture and customs, and more in Trinidad.

List of Places to Visit in Trinidad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Trinidad has a rich cultural history that can be explored through its various museums and historical sites. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • Trinidad Museum: Discover the town’s history through its exhibitions showcasing the Yurok and Tolowa indigenous tribes, the logging industry, and more.
  • Trinidad Art Gallery: This community-run art gallery showcases the works of local artists, and also hosts various events and workshops throughout the year.
  • Trinidad Memorial Lighthouse: A tribute to lives lost at sea, the lighthouse offers sweeping views of the rugged coastline.

Natural Attractions of Trinidad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trinidad is known for its astonishing natural beauty, which can be experienced through various outdoor activities and scenic viewpoints, such as:

  • Patrick’s Point State Park: With miles of hiking trails, gorgeous beaches, and stunning redwood forests, this state park is a must-visit destination for nature-lovers.
  • Trinidad Head: A popular spot for hiking and picnicking, Trinidad Head offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean, and the town’s surrounding hills.
  • Big Lagoon County Park: Visitors can kayak, fish, or swim in the lagoon, which is home to over 200 species of birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trinidad

Trinidad boasts some of the most picturesque hiking and biking trails in California. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Trinidad Head Loop Trail: This 1.5 mile, moderately-rated trail takes hikers through lush forests and coastal grasslands, offering stunning ocean views.
  • The Elk Head Trail: A more challenging trail, with a steep climb up to 800 feet, the Elk Head Trail offers sweeping views of the coastline and the Trinidad Harbor.
  • The Hammond Coastal Trail: A relatively flat trail that winds its way along the cliffs overlooking the Pacific, offering stunning sunset views.

Best Things to Do in Trinidad: Activities and Tours

Trinidad offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors. Here’s a list of some of the most popular things to do in the town:

  • Whale Watching Tour: Head out to sea on a guided whale watching tour to spot gray whales, humpbacks, and orcas.
  • Trinidad Bay Kayak Tour: Paddle your way through the quiet waters of the Trinidad Bay, and explore the town’s coastline and wildlife.
  • Trinidad Art Nights: On the first Friday of every month, the town hosts a vibrant art festival, featuring live music, art exhibitions, and local food and drinks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trinidad

Day 1: Explore the Trinidad Museum, followed by a leisurely hike on the Elk Head Trail. Day 2: Spend the day discovering Patrick’s Point State Park, and end the evening with dinner at the Moonstone Grill. Day 3: Take a guided whale-watching tour, and enjoy a seafood lunch at Trinidad Harbor. Day 4: Enjoy a kayaking tour of the Trinidad Bay, followed by a visit to the Trinidad Art Gallery. Day 5: Visit the Big Lagoon County Park, and then head back to town for an evening of live music at Trinidad Art Nights. Day 6: Take a rest day, and enjoy the stunning ocean views at Trinidad Head. Day 7: Bid farewell to Trinidad, and go on an excursion to nearby destinations such as Eureka or the Redwood National and State Parks.

Food and Drink Scene of Trinidad

Must-Try Dishes

Trinidad’s food scene is heavily influenced by its location and history. Here are some must-try dishes to try in the town:

  • Pacific Oysters: Sourced directly from the Humboldt Bay, Trinidad’s oysters are plump, fresh, and delicious.
  • Fish Tacos: Perfectly paired with homemade salsa and guacamole, fish tacos are a must-try dish in Trinidad.
  • Fresh-made pasta: Enjoy delicious, locally-sourced pasta dishes at Trinidad’s cozy Italian restaurant, Larrupin Cafe.

Local Beverages

Trinidad also offers some refreshing local drinks that you need to try:

  • Humboldt Cider: Made with locally sourced apples, Humboldt County’s cider scene continues to grow.
  • Lost Coast Brewery: Sample a pint (or two) of Lost Coast Brewery’s craft beers, which are brewed with high-quality ingredients and made locally.

Where to Stay in Trinidad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trinidad has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay in the town:

  • The Sunset Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers comfortable rooms, friendly staff, and an unbeatable location near the beach.
  • The Old Home Bed and Breakfast: Stay in a cozy, historic home that boasts stunning views of the ocean.
  • Emerald Forest Cabins: These secluded cabins are nestled in the heart of the redwoods, offering a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

What to Buy in Trinidad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trinidad offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home as gifts:

  • Trinidad Bay Art Gallery: Purchase local art pieces, pottery, and jewelry made by local artists and artisans.
  • Miners By Moonlight: These beautiful, handcrafted candles are made with local beeswax, and come in a variety of scents.
  • Moonstone Crossing Winery: Sample some of the finest Humboldt County wines, and bring home a bottle of your favorite.

Local Culture and Customs of Trinidad

Trinidad’s culture is heavily influenced by its location and history. Respect for nature and the community is valued in the town. It is important for visitors to be mindful of their impact on the environment and do their part in preserving the town’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Trinidad

Trinidad’s climate is consistently mild, making it an ideal destination any time of year. However, the best time to visit is during the summer months (June through August), when the temperature is at its warmest. Fall and winter months offer fewer crowds, providing a more peaceful experience for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trinidad

Do:

  • Respect nature and the town’s surroundings.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Overpack - pack light and leave ample room for local souvenirs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trinidad

Trinidad is a safe town to visit for the most part, provided visitors take the usual precautions and keep their belongings safe.

Health in Trinidad

Visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and take precautions when in natural areas such as trails and parks. It’s important to pack enough water and food, as well as the necessary supplies such as sunscreen, insect repellent, and first-aid kits.

Getting to Trinidad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trinidad

The nearest airport to Trinidad is the Arcata-Eureka airport which is approximately 20 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Trinidad

Greyhound buses stop at the nearby location of Arcata, which is approximately 15 minutes away from Trinidad.

Other Options

It’s recommended to have a car, as it offers the most flexibility when exploring areas outside of Trinidad. However, there are local shuttle services available to transport visitors to and from nearby destinations. Biking and hiking is a popular option for those who want to explore the town itself.

How to Get Around in Trinidad

Walking and hiking can be enjoyed in Trinidad itself, and a car is the best option for travelling to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Trinidad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trinidad is ideally located to explore nearby destinations such as:

  • Eureka: Located just a 30 minute drive away, Eureka offers stunning Victorian architecture, restaurants, and beaches along with exceptional shopping opportunities.
  • Redwood National and State Parks: Visit the world-renowned redwood forests and embark on a hike through paradise.

Conclusion

Trinidad is a small town packed with big adventures. From the towering redwoods to the serene coastline, this town has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a quiet escape or an outdoor adventure, Trinidad is the place to be. Respect the town’s culture, take precautions when exploring the natural beauty, and enjoy the stunning scenery and local cuisine.
