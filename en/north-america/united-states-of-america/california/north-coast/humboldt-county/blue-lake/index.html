Introduction to Blue Lake

Located in the heart of Humboldt County in North Coast, California, Blue Lake is a small town known for its pristine natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene. The town derives its name from the nearby Blue Lake, a glacial lake that is home to a wide variety of aquatic life.

With its scenic hiking trails, historic landmarks, and cultural institutions, Blue Lake has something for everyone, whether you’re an adventure seeker, a history buff, or an art lover. In this travel guide, we’ll give you all the insider tips on the best things to do, see, and experience in Blue Lake.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Lake

Here are some of the top places to visit in Blue Lake:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mad River Hatchery: A state-of-the-art fish hatchery that produces a diverse range of fish for recreation and conservation purposes.
  • The Blue Lake Museum: A repository of local history that features exhibits on Native American culture, logging industry, and pioneering settlers.
  • The Dell’Arte International School of Physical Theatre: A renowned institution that trains actors, dancers, and performers in physical theatre techniques.
  • Chumayo Spa: A luxury spa that offers a range of rejuvenating treatments inspired by indigenous healing practices.
  • The Pierson Big Tree Trail: A stunning redwood trail known for its giant redwood trees, some of which are over 500 years old.

Natural Attractions of Blue Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Lake: This glacially formed lake is one of the most popular attractions in Blue Lake. Visitors can swim, boat, and fish in its crystal-clear waters or take a leisurely stroll around its scenic banks.
  • The Arcata Community Forest: This expansive forest is home to miles of hiking and mountain biking trails, lush groves of redwood trees, and breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Headwaters Forest Reserve: A pristine wilderness area with over 7,000 acres of old-growth redwood forest, rare flora and fauna, and remarkable geological formations.
  • Patrick’s Point State Park: With its rugged coastline, dramatic cliffs, and secluded beaches, Patrick’s Point State Park is a nature lover’s paradise. It’s also a great place to observe wildlife, including whales, seals, and sea lions.
  • The Azalea State Reserve: A 30-acre reserve that is home to over 200 species of native plants, including rhododendrons, madrones, and huckleberries. It’s a great place to enjoy a peaceful picnic or a relaxing walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Lake

Blue Lake is a great place to get outdoors and explore. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes to check out:

  • Arcata Community Forest Trail: This scenic trail is a favorite among hikers and mountain bikers alike. It meanders through the lush green forest, offering breathtaking views of the redwood groves and the Pacific Ocean.
  • Annie and Mary Trail: A picturesque trail that runs alongside the Mad River and through the historic town of Blue Lake, offering views of the town’s charming Victorian homes.
  • Water Tower Trail: This short but steep trail leads to a water tower that offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Hammond Trail: This 5.5-mile trail hugs the coastline, offering stunning views of Trinidad Bay, Houda Point, and Elk Head.

Best Things to Do in Blue Lake: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a thrill-seeker, a culture vulture, or a foodie, Blue Lake has something to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Blue Lake:

  • Take a tour of the Mad River Hatchery or go fishing on the Mad River.
  • Catch a show at the Dell’Arte International School of Physical Theatre or visit the Blue Lake Museum to learn more about the town’s rich history.
  • Go for a hike or mountain bike ride in the Arcata Community Forest or the Headwaters Forest Reserve.
  • Enjoy a scenic drive along the Avenue of the Giants, one of the most stunning roads in California.
  • Indulge in some retail therapy by shopping for local handicrafts and souvenirs at the Blue Lake Emporium.
  • Enjoy a relaxing spa treatment at Chumayo Spa or learn to make your own natural skincare products at the It’s All About You day spa.
  • Take part in the Blue Lake Solstice Music Festival, a yearly event that celebrates the summer solstice with a lineup of world-renowned musicians and performers.

If you’re planning to spend a week in Blue Lake, here’s a day-by-day itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Lake

Blue Lake is a foodie’s paradise, with its wide variety of locally-sourced produce, fresh seafood, and diverse culinary offerings. Here are some of the must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Humboldt Bay oysters: These locally-sourced oysters are famous for their briny flavor and delicate texture. Enjoy them raw, grilled, or fried.
  • Tri-tip steak: A popular cut of beef that’s cooked over an open flame and served with tangy barbecue sauce.
  • Redwood Coast smoked salmon: This rich and flavorful smoked salmon is cured with locally harvested wildflowers and herbs.
  • Vegan and vegetarian options: Blue Lake offers plenty of options for vegans and vegetarians, including kale salads, veggie burgers, and quinoa bowls.

Local Beverages

  • Redwood Curtain IPA: A hoppy and intense IPA that’s made with locally sourced hops and barley.
  • Humboldt Fog Blackberry Ale: A fruity and refreshing ale that’s brewed with blackberries and other local ingredients.
  • Mad River Brewing Company Steelhead Extra Pale Ale: A light and crisp pale ale that’s perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.
  • Regional wines: Blue Lake is surrounded by world-class wineries that specialize in varietals such as pinot noir, chardonnay, and zinfandel.

Where to Stay in Blue Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury resort or a budget-friendly hostel, Blue Lake has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Blue Lake Casino Hotel: A luxury resort that offers gaming, dining, live entertainment, and plenty of amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and pool.
  • The Red Roof Inn Arcata: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and easy access to the Arcata Community Forest and Humboldt Bay.
  • The Eagle House Victorian Inn: A historic guesthouse that offers period furnishings, elaborate woodwork, and modern amenities, including flat-screen TVs and free WiFi.
  • The Mad River Inn Bed and Breakfast: A charming B&B that offers cozy rooms, delicious breakfasts, and breathtaking views of the Mad River Valley.

What to Buy in Blue Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Blue Lake is home to a wide range of artisanal shops and boutiques, offering everything from handmade crafts to locally sourced produce. Here are some of the best places to shop in Blue Lake:

  • Blue Lake Emporium: This charming gift shop offers a wide range of souvenirs, including locally made crafts, clothing, and jewelry.
  • Arcata Farmer’s Market: This weekly farmer’s market offers a wide variety of fresh produce and handmade products, including artisanal cheeses, fruits, and vegetables.
  • The Garden Gate: This boutique nursery offers a wide range of rare and exotic plants, as well as local gifts and souvenirs.
  • The Works: This art gallery and shop features works by local artists, including paintings, sculptures, and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Lake

Blue Lake is a friendly and welcoming town that celebrates its diverse cultural heritage. Here are some tips to help you navigate local customs and culture:

  • Respect the environment: Blue Lake is known for its pristine natural beauty and diverse wildlife, so be sure to follow the Leave No Trace principles, avoid littering, and stay on designated trails.
  • Support local businesses: Blue Lake is home to a thriving local economy, so be sure to shop at independent stores and buy locally sourced products.
  • Embrace the free-spirited vibe: Humboldt County is known for its relaxed and free-spirited culture, so don’t be surprised if you see people embracing alternative lifestyles and artistic expressions.

Best Time to Visit Blue Lake

The best time to visit Blue Lake is between April and September when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, if you prefer cooler weather and fewer crowds, consider visiting between October and March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Lake

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Blue Lake:

Do:

  • Do explore the great outdoors and take advantage of the town’s nature trails and scenic views.
  • Do support local businesses and shop at independent stores and markets.
  • Do respect the town’s cultural heritage and celebrate its diverse population.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the trails or in public spaces.
  • Don’t partake in illegal activities, such as drug use or underage drinking.
  • Don’t use offensive language or engage in discriminatory behavior towards others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blue Lake

Blue Lake is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still exercise caution when exploring the area. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Always lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night.
  • Pay attention to road signs and drive defensively, especially on winding mountain roads.

Health in Blue Lake

Blue Lake has several medical clinics, hospitals, and emergency services available in case of illness or injury. However, it’s always a good idea to bring your own travel health insurance and medications, just in case.

Getting to Blue Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Blue Lake is easy, thanks to its convenient location and accessible transportation options. Here are some of the best ways to get to Blue Lake:

Airports Near Blue Lake

The closest airport to Blue Lake is the Arcata-Eureka Airport (ACV), which is located just 15 miles southwest of the town. Other nearby airports include San Francisco International Airport (SFO) and Oakland International Airport (OAK), both of which are located about four hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Blue Lake

Greyhound and Amtrak both offer bus and train service to the nearby town of Arcata, which is about five miles from Blue Lake. From there, visitors can take a local bus or taxi to reach their final destination.

Other Options

Blue Lake is also accessible by car, with several major highways and scenic routes passing through the town. Visitors can rent a car from one of the major car rental companies or take a shuttle from the airport to the town.

How to Get Around in Blue Lake

Blue Lake is a small town, so getting around is easy and convenient. Visitors can rent a bike or a car, take a taxi, or use the local bus service to get from place to place.

Where to Go Next from Blue Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blue Lake is conveniently located near several other popular destinations and attractions in Humboldt County. Here are some of the top places to check out:

  • Trinidad: A charming seaside village known for its picturesque beaches, historic lighthouse, and eclectic arts scene.
  • Eureka: A classic California town with a vintage charm, an industrial heritage, and a vibrant arts and culture scene.
  • Ferndale: A small town known for its well-preserved Victorian architecture, antiques shops, and locally sourced cuisine.
  • Redwood National and State Parks: One of the most iconic natural attractions in California, known for its towering redwood trees, scenic hiking trails, and abundant wildlife.

Conclusion

Blue Lake is a hidden gem of California, offering visitors a unique blend of nature, culture, and history. Whether you’re into hiking, fishing, shopping, or simply soaking up the sun, Blue Lake has something for everyone. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready for an unforgettable experience in one of California’s most beautiful and welcoming towns.
