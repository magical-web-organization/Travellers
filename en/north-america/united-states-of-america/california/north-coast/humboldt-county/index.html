Introduction to Humboldt County

Nestled among towering redwoods and dramatic coastline, Humboldt County is a nature lover’s paradise. Located in the North Coast region of California in the United States of America, visitors can explore miles of pristine beaches, hike through ancient forests, and learn about the county’s rich history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Humboldt County

Humboldt County is home to a diverse range of attractions. From cultural institutions to natural wonders, there is something for everyone. Some of the top places to visit in Humboldt County include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clarke Historical Museum: Located in Eureka, the Clarke Historical Museum explores the history, art, and culture of Humboldt County through its exhibits and programs.
  • Fort Humboldt State Historic Park: This park in Eureka houses the remains of a 19th-century military post. Visitors can tour the historic buildings and learn about the area’s history.
  • Humboldt State University Natural History Museum: The museum in Arcata features exhibits on the natural history of the region, including flora, fauna, and geology.

Natural Attractions of Humboldt County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Avenue of the Giants: This scenic road takes visitors through the heart of Humboldt Redwoods State Park, home to some of the tallest trees in the world.
  • Redwood National and State Parks: This collection of parks protects hundreds of thousands of acres of old-growth redwood forest, as well as scenic beaches and rugged coastline.
  • Lost Coast: This remote and rugged section of coastline in the southern part of the county is a popular spot for backpacking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Humboldt County

Humboldt County is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. With miles of hiking and biking trails, visitors can explore the natural beauty of the area up close. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Humboldt County include:

  • Fern Canyon Trail: This easy hike in Prairie Creek Redwoods State Park takes visitors through a picturesque canyon lined with ferns and redwoods.
  • Humboldt Bay Trail: This 14-mile trail circumnavigates Humboldt Bay and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding landscape.
  • Community Forest Trail: Located in Arcata, this 790-acre forest has over 13 miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as picnic areas and a playground.

Best Things to Do in Humboldt County: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Humboldt County can experience a wide range of activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Humboldt County include:

  • Kayaking on Humboldt Bay: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the bay and surrounding waterways.
  • Whale Watching: Between December and April, visitors can take a whale watching tour from Trinidad to see the migration of gray whales.
  • Brewery Tours: With over a dozen breweries in the area, a brewery tour is a great way to sample some of the county’s best beers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Humboldt County

For visitors looking to spend a week in Humboldt County, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the charming downtown of Eureka, including the Clarke Historical Museum and Old Town Eureka.
  • Day 2: Hike or bike through the towering redwoods of Redwood National and State Parks.
  • Day 3: Visit the coastal town of Trinidad, explore the beaches and take a whale watching tour.
  • Day 4: Drive the scenic Avenue of the Giants and visit Humboldt Redwoods State Park.
  • Day 5: Take a kayaking tour of Humboldt Bay and explore the surrounding wetlands.
  • Day 6: Visit the charming college town of Arcata and its community forest.
  • Day 7: Take a brewery tour and sample some of the county’s best beers.

Food and Drink Scene of Humboldt County

Humboldt County is home to a thriving food and drink scene, with a focus on locally sourced ingredients and craft beverages. Some of the must-try dishes and drinks in Humboldt County include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters: Humboldt County is known for its fresh oysters, which can be found on menus at many local restaurants.
  • Humboldt Fog Cheese: This goat cheese from Cypress Grove Chevre in Arcata is famous for its tangy flavor and distinctive ash coating.
  • Burgers: Humboldt County has a reputation for great burgers, with many local restaurants offering unique and delicious variations.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: With over a dozen breweries in the area, Humboldt County is a great place to sample a wide variety of craft beers.
  • Wine: Visitors can visit local wineries to sample wines made with grapes grown in the area.
  • Cider: Humboldt County is home to several cideries, producing small-batch, artisanal ciders.

Where to Stay in Humboldt County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Humboldt County has a variety of accommodation options to fit every budget and taste. Some of the top places to stay in Humboldt County include:

  • The Carter House Inns: Located in Eureka, this luxurious inn offers elegant rooms and a fine dining restaurant.
  • Red Lion Hotel Eureka: This hotel in Eureka offers comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant and lounge.
  • HI-Redwood Hostel: This hostel in Orick offers clean, comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Humboldt County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Humboldt County can take home a variety of souvenirs and local products to remind them of their trip. Some of the best things to buy in Humboldt County include:

  • Humboldt Fog Cheese: This locally made cheese is a must-try for foodies.
  • Craft Beer: Visitors can purchase beer from local breweries to take home and enjoy.
  • Redwood Crafts: Visitors can find a variety of crafts made from redwood, including bowls, cutting boards, and carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Humboldt County

Humboldt County is known for its laid-back, friendly culture. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s natural resources and wildlife, and follow Leave No Trace principles while enjoying the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Humboldt County

The best time to visit Humboldt County depends on visitors’ interests. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with warm temperatures and long days perfect for outdoor activities. Fall is a great time to visit for those interested in mushrooms and fall foliage. Winter is a good time to visit for storm watching and whale watching, and spring is a good time to visit for wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Humboldt County

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and minimize impact while enjoying the outdoors
  • Support local businesses by purchasing locally made products and supporting farmers markets and independent retailers
  • Be open to new experiences and learning about the history, culture, and natural resources of the area

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or damage natural resources while enjoying the outdoors
  • Disrespect local residents or culture
  • Take risks or attempt dangerous activities while exploring the area

Safety and Health

Safety in Humboldt County

Humboldt County is a safe destination for visitors. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking cars and securing valuables, and be aware of their surroundings while exploring the area.

Health in Humboldt County

Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical insurance while traveling in Humboldt County. Medical facilities are available throughout the area, but visitors should take standard precautions to avoid illness, such as hand-washing and drinking bottled water.

Getting to Humboldt County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Humboldt County is accessible by car, bus, and air. Some of the transportation options available to visitors include:

Airports Near Humboldt County

  • Arcata-Eureka Airport: The regional airport offers flights to and from San Francisco and Los Angeles.

Bus Stations Near Humboldt County

  • Greyhound Bus: This national bus service stops in Eureka and Arcata.
  • Redwood Transit System: This regional bus service offers transportation throughout Humboldt County.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from several rental agencies at the Arcata-Eureka Airport or in Eureka.
  • Bike Rental: Visitors can rent bikes from several shops in the area.

How to Get Around in Humboldt County

Visitors to Humboldt County can explore the area by car, bike, or bus. Some of the transportation options available in Humboldt County include:

  • Rental cars: Visitors can rent a car from several rental agencies in the area.
  • Bike rental: Visitors can rent bikes from several shops in the area.
  • Bus: The Redwood Transit System offers bus service throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Humboldt County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Humboldt County, visitors can continue their California adventure with a trip to nearby destinations, such as:

  • Sonoma County: Known for its world-class wineries and scenic countryside, Sonoma County is located south of Humboldt County.
  • Mendocino County: This region just south of Humboldt County is known for its rugged coastline and charming small towns.
  • Crater Lake National Park: This stunning national park in Oregon is known for its crystal-clear lake and dramatic landscapes.

Conclusion

Humboldt County offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re exploring the towering redwoods, sampling local cuisine and craft beverages, or learning about the area’s rich history and culture, Humboldt County has something for everyone.
