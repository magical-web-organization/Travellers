Introduction to McKinleyville

McKinleyville, located in Humboldt County, California, is a charming coastal town that attracts both avid outdoor enthusiasts and those seeking some peace and quiet. The town is situated in North Coast, known for its rugged coastlines, towering redwoods, and picturesque beaches. Whether you’re looking for some outdoor adventure or want to learn more about the local culture and history, McKinleyville is the perfect place to visit.

List of Places to Visit in McKinleyville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in history and culture, McKinleyville offers several notable attractions worth visiting, including:

  • Clarke Historical Museum: Located in nearby Eureka, this museum features exhibits on regional history and Native American culture.
  • Trinidad Museum: A small museum showcasing the town’s history.
  • Humboldt State University Natural History Museum: An excellent place to learn about the ecology and geology of the area.

Natural Attractions of McKinleyville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

McKinleyville is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with many wonderful parks, trails, and natural attractions to explore, such as:

  • Patrick’s Point State Park: A popular park featuring picturesque coastal views, hiking trails, and camping sites.
  • Arcata Community Forest: Offering over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails, this forest is a local favorite.
  • Mad River Beach County Park: A beautiful beach known for its scenic views and wildlife sightings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McKinleyville

McKinleyville offers several exciting hiking and biking routes that take you through some of the most beautiful areas in the region. Here are some top choices for trails and routes:

  • Hammond Coastal Trail: This 5-mile trail offers stunning ocean views and passes through dunes, forests, and beaches.
  • Humboldt Bay Trail: A 14-mile trail that follows the bay, offering excellent bird watching opportunities and scenic views.
  • Trillium Falls Trail: A 3-mile trail that leads you through the forest to the Trillium Falls, a picturesque waterfall.

Best Things to Do in McKinleyville: Activities and Tours

McKinleyville offers many exciting activities and tours, from whale watching to wine tasting. Here are some top choices:

  • Kayak Tours: Explore the nearby rivers and ocean by kayak with a guided tour.
  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour and spot gray whales during the migration season.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit local wineries to sample some of the region’s best wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for McKinleyville

Day 1: Arrival and Relaxation in the town.

Day 2: Humboldt State University Natural History Museum and Arcata Community Forest exploration.

Day 3: Spend the day at Patrick’s Point State Park, hiking and picnicking.

Day 4: Visit the Clarke Historical Museum in Eureka and Trinidad Museum.

Day 5: Go on a whale watching tour or wine tasting tour.

Day 6: Visit Mad River Beach County Park, have a picnic, and enjoy some beach time.

Day 7: Kayak tour on the nearby rivers or go on a hike at Trillium Falls Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of McKinleyville

Must-Try Dishes

McKinleyville has a vibrant food scene with many delicious local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Humboldt Fog Cheese: A locally produced goat cheese with a distinct flavor and crumbly texture.
  • Samoa Cookhouse Pancakes: The Samoa Cookhouse is a historic restaurant famous for its all-you-can-eat pancakes.
  • Crab Cakes: A coastal delicacy that is served in many restaurants in the area.

Local Beverages

The region is known for many locally produced beers, ciders, and wines. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Lost Coast Brewery’s Great White Beer: A refreshing Belgian-style wheat beer with a hint of coriander.
  • Dick Taylor Craft Chocolate Drinking Chocolate: A rich and smooth hot chocolate made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Briceland Vineyards Pinot Noir: A smooth and flavorful red wine produced by one of the region’s finest wineries.

Where to Stay in McKinleyville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

McKinleyville offers several lodging options that cater to different budgets and needs. Here are some top choices:

  • Holiday Inn Express: A comfortable and modern hotel with great amenities and excellent customer service.
  • Red Roof Inn Arcata: A budget-friendly option located near the town’s major attractions.
  • Fernbridge Historic Inn: A charming and historic inn with lovely ambiance and scenic views.

What to Buy in McKinleyville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

McKinleyville has many shops and stores selling locally sourced products, making it a great place to buy unique souvenirs and gifts. Here are some top choices:

  • Locally produced cheeses and wines
  • Artisanal soaps and candles
  • Handmade jewelry and pottery

Local Culture and Customs of McKinleyville

McKinleyville has a laid-back and friendly atmosphere, and locals are proud of their community’s history and culture. It is customary to greet people with a smile and take your time to enjoy the beautiful scenery and outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit McKinleyville

The best time to visit McKinleyville is between May and October when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers are sunny and warm, with average temperatures in the mid-70s. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit when the trees start changing colors, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of McKinleyville

Do:

  • Take your time to explore the beautiful parks and trails.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Admire the local art and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in McKinleyville

McKinleyville is a relatively safe place to visit, but it is always essential to take some safety precautions, such as:

  • Avoid leaving valuables in your car.
  • Stay on designated trails and paths.
  • Be aware of your surroundings.

Health in McKinleyville

McKinleyville has excellent medical facilities, and there are several clinics and hospitals in the area that provide quality medical care.

Getting to McKinleyville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near McKinleyville

The nearest airport to McKinleyville is the Arcata-Eureka Airport, located about 3 miles away. The airport serves several major airlines, and you can easily rent a car or take a taxi to the town center.

Bus Stations Near McKinleyville

There are several Greyhound bus stations located near McKinleyville. The Arcata Transit Center is the nearest bus terminal, located about 5 miles away from the town center.

Other Options

You can also drive to McKinleyville via Highway 101 or take the Amtrak from Eureka or Arcata.

How to Get Around in McKinleyville

The best way to get around McKinleyville is by car. You can rent a car at the airport or in town. Alternatively, you can take public transportation, such as the Mad River Transit or Humboldt Transit Authority.

Where to Go Next from McKinleyville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

McKinleyville offers many exciting nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring, such as:

  • Redwood National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located about an hour’s drive from McKinleyville.
  • Arcata: A charming college town located about 10 minutes’ drive from McKinleyville.
  • Trinidad: Another picturesque coastal town located about 15 minutes’ drive from McKinleyville.

Conclusion

McKinleyville is a unique and charming town that offers something for everyone. Whether you want to hike the trails, kayak, whale watch, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine, McKinleyville is the perfect place to visit. With its friendly locals, beautiful scenery, and excellent facilities, it’s no wonder why McKinleyville is a favorite destination for many visitors.
