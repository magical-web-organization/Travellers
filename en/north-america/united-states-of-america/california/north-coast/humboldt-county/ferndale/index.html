Introduction to Ferndale

Located in Humboldt County, California, Ferndale is a charming little town rich in history and culture. Known for its Victorian architecture and quaint downtown areas, Ferndale attracts visitors from far and wide with its old-world charm and picturesque landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Ferndale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Ferndale Museum

The Ferndale Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about the town’s history. Located in an original 19th-century Victorian building, the museum showcases artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that tell the story of Ferndale’s past.

The Victorian Inn

The Victorian Inn is another landmark building in Ferndale that is worth a visit. Built in 1890, the inn features stunning Victorian architecture, antique furnishings, and elegant guest rooms.

Ferndale Cemetery

If you’re interested in local history, the Ferndale Cemetery is a fascinating place to explore. Established in 1868, the cemetery is the final resting place of many prominent citizens and early settlers of the town.

Natural Attractions of Ferndale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Humboldt Redwoods State Park

Located just a short drive from Ferndale, the Humboldt Redwoods State Park is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. The park is home to the world’s tallest trees, the coastal redwoods, as well as a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Lost Coast Trail

For adventurous hikers, the Lost Coast Trail is a must-do activity in Ferndale. The trail takes you through a remote wilderness area along the coast, offering stunning views of the ocean and rugged coastline.

Russ Park

Russ Park is a popular destination for hikers and mountain bikers, offering beautiful views of the valley below. The park also features a disc golf course, picnic areas, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ferndale

Ferndale offers a range of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular trails include the Bear River Ridge Trail, the Salt River Trail, and the Lost Coast Trail.

Best Things to Do in Ferndale: Activities and Tours

Ferndale Repertory Theatre

The Ferndale Repertory Theatre is a great place to catch a live show or theatrical performance. The theatre is housed in a historic building and features a full calendar of performances throughout the year.

Humboldt Bay Oyster Tours

If you’re a foodie, don’t miss the chance to explore Humboldt Bay with a guided oyster tour. The tours take you out on the water to learn about oyster farming and taste fresh, locally grown oysters.

Ferndale Farmers’ Market

If you’re visiting Ferndale during the summer months, be sure to visit the Ferndale Farmers’ Market. The market offers a wide array of fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ferndale

Day Activity
1 Explore Ferndale’s Victorian architecture and historic downtown district
2 Visit the Ferndale Museum and cemetery, grab lunch at a local cafe
3 Hike the Bear River Ridge Trail or explore Humboldt Redwoods State Park

5 Visit the Lost Coast Trail for a scenic hike
6 Check out a live performance at the Ferndale Repertory Theatre
7 Shop for souvenirs at the Ferndale Farmers’ Market and pack up for departure

Food and Drink Scene of Ferndale

Must-Try Dishes

Creamery Building Restaurant

The Creamery Building Restaurant is a local favorite in Ferndale, offering a range of delicious dishes made with fresh, local ingredients. Some must-try dishes include the grilled chicken sandwich and the shrimp and grits.

The Ivanhoe

The Ivanhoe is a historic saloon in Ferndale that has been serving up tasty dishes and drinks since 1913. Be sure to try their famous Ivanhoe burger, topped with all the fixings.

Local Beverages

Lost Coast Brewery

Located just a short drive from Ferndale, Lost Coast Brewery is a must-visit for craft beer lovers. The brewery offers a range of delicious brews, including their flagship Great White and Downtown Brown.

Humboldt Distillery

If you prefer spirits to beer, head over to Humboldt Distillery to sample their locally made vodka, whiskey, and rum.

Where to Stay in Ferndale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Gingerbread Mansion Inn

The Gingerbread Mansion Inn is a stunning Victorian bed and breakfast in the heart of Ferndale. The inn features elegant guest rooms, a beautiful garden, and an on-site spa.

Ye Olde Danish Inn

For a more budget-friendly option, check out Ye Olde Danish Inn, a cozy bed and breakfast with charming rooms and welcoming hosts.

What to Buy in Ferndale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ferndale Meat Company

The Ferndale Meat Company offers a range of locally sourced meats and sausages, as well as delicious deli sandwiches to-go. Be sure to stock up on their famous pepperoni sticks to take home with you.

The Blacksmith Shop

The Blacksmith Shop is a unique store that sells handcrafted iron works and other metal goods. You’re sure to find a one-of-a-kind souvenir to bring home from here.

Local Culture and Customs of Ferndale

Ferndale’s local culture is deeply rooted in its Victorian past, and the town takes great pride in preserving its historic architecture and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to explore the downtown area and take part in the town’s many festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Ferndale

The best time to visit Ferndale is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the town is bustling with activity. However, Ferndale’s beauty can be appreciated year-round, so plan your visit according to your preferences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ferndale

Do:

  • Take a guided oyster tour on Humboldt Bay
  • Explore Ferndale’s Victorian architecture and historic downtown district
  • Visit the Lost Coast Trail for a scenic hike
  • Try the locally sourced meats and sausage from Ferndale Meat Company

Don’t:

  • Leave without exploring Humboldt Redwoods State Park
  • Miss the chance to catch a live show at the Ferndale Repertory Theatre
  • Skip the chance to try local craft beer from Lost Coast Brewery

Safety and Health

Safety in Ferndale

Ferndale is a safe town to visit, but visitors should still take normal safety precautions, such as locking their valuables in their hotel room and being aware of their surroundings at night.

Health in Ferndale

Visitors to Ferndale should be aware of the risks of tick-borne illnesses and take appropriate precautions when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Ferndale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ferndale

The nearest major airport to Ferndale is the San Francisco International Airport, located about 5 hours south of the town.

Bus Stations Near Ferndale

The Greyhound Bus Station in Eureka, located about 20 minutes from Ferndale, offers bus services to various destinations throughout California and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Ferndale by car or bicycle, as the town is located along Route 101 and is a popular stop for cycling tours along the Pacific Coast.

How to Get Around in Ferndale

Ferndale is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. However, visitors may also choose to rent a car to explore the surrounding area more easily.

Where to Go Next from Ferndale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eureka

Just a short drive from Ferndale, Eureka is a larger city with a thriving arts scene, quirky shops, and a historic Old Town.

Avenue of the Giants

The Avenue of the Giants is a scenic drive through Humboldt Redwoods State Park, offering breathtaking views of the towering redwood trees.

Trinidad

A charming seaside town, Trinidad is a popular destination for fishing, kayaking, and beachcombing.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, Ferndale has something for everyone. With its small-town charm and stunning natural beauty, it’s no wonder that visitors to Ferndale often leave feeling enchanted and captivated by this unique and magical place.
