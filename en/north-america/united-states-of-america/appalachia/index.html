Are you ready to explore the natural beauty and rich culture of Appalachia? From the Blue Ridge Mountains to the Great Smoky Mountains, this region of the United States is full of scenic vistas, outdoor adventures, and fascinating history.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a look at the top places to visit, things to do, and tips to make the most of your time in Appalachia.

Introduction to Appalachia

Appalachia is a region that stretches from northern New York to southern Mississippi in the eastern United States. It is known for its rugged landscape, rolling hills, and dense forests. The region is also home to a unique culture and history, including Native American tribes, European settlers, and African American communities.

List of Places to Visit in Appalachia

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or art lover, there’s something for everyone in Appalachia. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Appalachia in Tennessee showcases the history and culture of the region, including artifacts and exhibits from early settlers, Native Americans, and the Civil War.
  • The National Museum of African American Music in Nashville celebrates the contributions of African American musicians to the country and blues genres.
  • The John C. Campbell Folk School in Brasstown, North Carolina offers classes on traditional arts and crafts, including pottery, weaving, and blacksmithing.

Natural Attractions of Appalachia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Great Smoky Mountains National Park straddles the Tennessee-North Carolina border and is known for its hiking trails, fall foliage, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • The Appalachian Trail runs from Georgia to Maine and passes through a portion of Appalachia, offering hikers a chance to experience the natural beauty and scenic vistas of the region.
  • The New River Gorge Bridge in West Virginia is the longest steel-arch bridge in the Western Hemisphere and offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountain landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Appalachia

Appalachia is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with hundreds of miles of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes:

  • The Blue Ridge Parkway in Virginia and North Carolina offers over 400 miles of scenic drives and hiking trails through the Appalachian Mountains.
  • The Virginia Creeper Trail in Virginia is a 34-mile rail trail that winds through the scenic countryside and small towns of southwestern Virginia.
  • The C&O Canal Trail in Maryland and West Virginia follows the path of the Chesapeake and Ohio Canal and offers a flat, easy hike along the Potomac River.

Best Things to Do in Appalachia: Activities and Tours

  • Take a whitewater rafting trip down the New River or Gauley River in West Virginia.
  • Attend a bluegrass music festival, such as the annual Bristol Rhythm and Roots Reunion in Virginia and Tennessee.
  • Visit the Biltmore Estate in Asheville, North Carolina, a massive mansion built for the Vanderbilt family that offers tours, gardens, and wine tastings.

If you have a week to spend in Appalachia, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Appalachia

Appalachia is known for its traditional cuisine and unique local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried green tomatoes
  • Pimento cheese
  • Hot chicken
  • Country ham

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon whiskey, produced in Kentucky
  • Sweet tea, a popular drink in the South
  • Cheerwine, a cherry-flavored soda from North Carolina
  • Moonshine, a type of homemade whiskey popular in Appalachia

Where to Stay in Appalachia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Appalachia has something to offer. Here are some top choices:

  • The Omni Homestead Resort in Hot Springs, Virginia is a historic hotel that offers luxury accommodations, fine dining, and a wide range of recreational activities.
  • The Nantahala Outdoor Center in North Carolina offers a range of lodging options, including cabins, bunkhouses, and campsites, as well as outdoor activities like whitewater rafting and zipline tours.
  • The Hostel in the Forest in Brunswick, Georgia is a unique eco-hostel that offers a rustic, sustainable experience in a natural setting.

What to Buy in Appalachia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Appalachia home with you by picking up some of these local souvenirs and products:

  • Traditional Appalachian crafts, such as pottery, weaving, and woodworking
  • Moonshine, available in various flavors and strengths
  • Local honey and jam
  • Blue Ridge Parkway merchandise, such as t-shirts and magnets

Local Culture and Customs of Appalachia

Appalachia has a distinct culture and history, influenced by the early settlers and Native American tribes who lived in the region. Some customs and traditions you may encounter include:

  • Celebrating holidays like Christmas and Halloween with unique regional customs and folklore.
  • The importance of family and community, with extended families often living close to one another.
  • A respect for nature and the land, reflected in traditional crafts and art forms.

Best Time to Visit Appalachia

The best time to visit Appalachia depends on what you want to experience. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Spring (March to May) is a great time for wildflower viewing and outdoor activities.
  • Summer (June to August) is peak tourist season and offers warm weather for outdoor adventures and festivals.
  • Fall (September to November) is the most popular time to visit for foliage viewing and autumn festivals.
  • Winter (December to February) offers the chance to experience winter sports and holiday celebrations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Appalachia

Here are some general guidelines to follow when visiting Appalachia:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the region.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the rugged terrain and outdoor conditions.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.
  • Assume that all areas of Appalachia have the same cultural and historical significance.

Safety and Health

As with any travel destination, it’s important to be aware of safety and health concerns while in Appalachia.

Safety in Appalachia

  • Watch for wildlife while hiking or camping, such as black bears and rattlesnakes.
  • Observe warning signs and follow safety protocols for outdoor activities like whitewater rafting.
  • Watch your surroundings and keep valuables secure in urban areas.

Health in Appalachia

  • Stay hydrated in the summer months, when temperatures can be high.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent tick bites and Lyme disease.
  • Make sure you have any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Appalachia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Appalachia is spread out over a large region, so getting there and getting around can require some planning.

Airports Near Appalachia

  • Charlotte Douglas International Airport (CLT) in North Carolina
  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL) in Georgia
  • Nashville International Airport (BNA) in Tennessee

Bus Stations Near Appalachia

  • Greyhound Bus Lines offers service to multiple cities in Appalachia, including Roanoke, Virginia and Knoxville, Tennessee.
  • Amtrak offers train service through a few cities in Appalachia, including White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia and Cumberland, Maryland.

Other Options

  • Driving: A car is the most convenient way to explore Appalachia, as many of the top attractions are in rural areas. Renting a car is recommended for those traveling from outside the region.
  • Ride-sharing: Services like Uber and Lyft are available in some areas of Appalachia.

How to Get Around in Appalachia

Once in Appalachia, you’ll need to figure out how to get around. Here are some options:

  • Car rental: Renting a car is recommended for those planning to explore the region. Car rental companies are available at major airports and urban areas.
  • Public transportation: While limited, public transportation is available in some areas of Appalachia, such as bus service in larger cities and train service through Amtrak.
  • Walking and biking: Many towns and cities in Appalachia have pedestrian-friendly downtown areas, and biking is a popular way to explore parks and scenic areas.

Where to Go Next from Appalachia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Appalachia, consider these nearby destinations:

  • Charleston, South Carolina: Known for its historic charm and southern hospitality, Charleston is a popular tourist destination.
  • Savannah, Georgia: Savannah offers historic homes, picturesque squares, and a lively food and drink scene.
  • Washington, D.C.: The nation’s capital is just a few hours’ drive from Appalachia and offers a wealth of historic and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Appalachia is a region full of natural beauty, unique history, and fascinating culture. Whether you’re looking to hike through the mountains, explore local crafts and traditions, or sample the local cuisine, Appalachia has something to offer. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide and get ready for an unforgettable adventure.
