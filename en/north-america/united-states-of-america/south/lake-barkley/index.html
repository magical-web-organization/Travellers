Introduction

Located in the heart of South, Lake Barkley is a stunning freshwater lake and reservoir that attracts visitors from all over the world. With its calm waters surrounded by lush forests, it’s the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and anyone looking for a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life. In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive list of all the must-see attractions and activities in Lake Barkley, as well as tips on where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Barkley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Woodlands Nature Station: Learn about the flora and fauna of Lake Barkley and the surrounding area at this well-maintained nature center.
  • Don F. Pratt Museum: This military museum showcases the history of the 101st Airborne Division, which is based at nearby Fort Campbell.
  • Jefferson Davis State Historic Site: Visit the birthplace of Jefferson Davis, the president of the Confederacy during the American Civil War, and explore the historic museum on the grounds.

Natural Attractions of Lake Barkley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area: This massive park encompasses over 170,000 acres of forest, wetlands, and wildlife, and offers numerous hiking, camping, and boating opportunities.
  • Lake Barkley State Park: Situated on the lake’s western shore, this state park offers fishing, boating, hiking, and camping opportunities amid gorgeous natural surroundings.
  • The Elk and Bison Prairie: Drive through this 700-acre preserve to see majestic elk, bison, and other wildlife up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Barkley

  • Hematite Trail: This scenic 2.5-mile hiking trail winds through the forest along Lake Barkley’s shoreline.
  • Canal Loop Trail: This easy, 3-mile trail offers beautiful views of the lake and surrounding forests, and is a favorite among birdwatchers.
  • Honker Loop Trail: This 7.5-mile trail is perfect for a longer hike or bike ride, and offers lovely views of the lake and LBL’s scenic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Lake Barkley: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Lake Barkley is home to several species of fish, including bass, catfish, and crappie, and numerous fishing guides and outfitters offer trips throughout the year.
  • Water Sports: From kayaking and canoeing to paddleboarding and jet skiing, Lake Barkley has plenty of options for adventurous water lovers.
  • Cruises and Boat Tours: Take a scenic cruise along the lake’s calm waters and enjoy stunning views of the shoreline and surrounding forests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Barkley

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive at Lake Barkley State Park and settle into your cabin. Spend the afternoon exploring the park’s hiking trails or enjoying the lake’s waters.
Day 2 Take a guided fishing or sightseeing tour of the lake, and enjoy a picnic lunch on one of the lake’s scenic beaches.
Day 3 Visit the Woodlands Nature Station and learn about the local wildlife, then head to the Elk and Bison Prairie to see these majestic animals up close.
Day 4 Spend the day exploring Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area, and hike the Hematite Trail or Canal Loop Trail.
Day 5 Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the lake’s secluded coves and inlets, or take a sunset cruise and enjoy the beautiful colors of the evening sky.
Day 6 Explore the region’s history at the Don F. Pratt Museum and Jefferson Davis State Historic Site.
Day 7 Depart Lake Barkley State Park and head home, taking with you memories of a relaxing and rejuvenating week in one of the most beautiful areas of South.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Barkley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Brown Sandwich: Originally created at the Brown Hotel in Louisville, this iconic Kentucky dish consists of toast points topped with turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce.
  • Burgoo: A rich stew made with a variety of meats, vegetables, and spices, often served with cornbread or crackers.
  • Derby Pie: This is a creamy and rich chocolate and nut pie that is served almost exclusively in Kentucky and southern Indiana, and was debuted at the Melrose Inn in 1950.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: With over 95% of the world’s bourbon produced in the state of Kentucky, it’s no surprise that this is the beverage of choice in Lake Barkley. Take a tour of one of the area’s many distilleries, such as the nearby Heaven Hill Distillery or Maker’s Mark, and enjoy a sample of this quintessential southern spirit.
  • Ale-8-One: This is a locally produced ginger and citrus soda that is popular throughout Kentucky and the surrounding area. It is commonly served as a mixer in bourbon drinks or on its own.

Where to Stay in Lake Barkley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lake Barkley State Resort Park: This beautiful park offers a variety of accommodations, including cabins, cottages, and campsites, all amidst stunning natural scenery.
  • Comfort Inn & Suites: This hotel is conveniently located just off of I-24, and offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as a pool and fitness center.
  • Piney Campground: This quiet and serene campground is situated on a bluff overlooking the lake, and offers rustic tent and RV accommodations.

What to Buy in Lake Barkley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kentucky Bourbon: Bring a taste of Lake Barkley and the surrounding area home with you by picking up a bottle of locally produced Kentucky bourbon.
  • Handmade Crafts: Explore the area’s many craft fairs and markets, and find unique and colorful handmade items such as quilts, pottery, and wood carvings.
  • Artisanal Food Products: Stock up on local jams, jellies, and other food products made from regional fruits and vegetables, such as blackberries and tomatoes.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Barkley

Lake Barkley is located in the heart of the American South, and its culture reflects this region’s hospitality, charm, and love of good food and drink. Visitors should be aware of the area’s conservative and religious traditions, and dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites. Southerners are typically polite and friendly, and visitors should take the time to engage in small talk and chat with locals, as this is an important part of the region’s social fabric.

Best Time to Visit Lake Barkley

The best time to visit Lake Barkley depends on your interests and preferences. For warm weather water sports and outdoor activities, the spring and summer months are ideal. Fall is a lovely time to visit and enjoy the changing leaves and cooler temperatures, while winter can be a cozy and romantic time to snuggle up in a cabin and enjoy the area’s natural beauty covered with snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Barkley

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife of the area, and pack out any trash or waste.
  • Try the local food and drink offerings, such as Kentucky bourbon and southern-style cooking.
  • Engage in friendly conversation with locals and other visitors, and soak up the southern hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions and parks.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  • Be rude or dismissive to locals or other visitors. Remember that manners and politeness are highly valued in southern culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Barkley

As with any natural area, visitors to Lake Barkley should be aware of potential hazards such as slippery rocks, swift currents, and uneven terrain. Stay on designated trails and obey all signs and warnings. Additionally, wildlife such as ticks, snakes, and spiders can be present in the area, so be cautious when hiking or camping.

Health in Lake Barkley

Visitors to Lake Barkley should be careful when spending time in or around the lake’s waters, as waterborne illnesses can be a risk. Additionally, visitors should be aware of the area’s climate, which can be hot and humid during the summer months. Bring plenty of sunscreen, insect repellent, and water when exploring the region’s parks and hiking trails.

Getting to Lake Barkley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Barkley

The nearest major airport to Lake Barkley is Nashville International Airport, which is approximately 80 miles away. A number of regional airports, such as the Barkley Regional Airport in Paducah, KY, are also within driving distance of the lake.

Bus Stations Near Lake Barkley

Greyhound bus stations are located in nearby towns such as Hopkinsville, KY, and Clarksville, TN.

Other Options

Visitors to Lake Barkley can also drive from nearby cities such as Nashville or Louisville.

How to Get Around in Lake Barkley

To get around Lake Barkley, visitors can drive their own vehicles or rent a car. Additionally, many of the area’s attractions can be reached by boat, and numerous kayak, canoe, and paddleboard rental shops are available near the lake’s shores.

Where to Go Next from Lake Barkley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Located just an hour and a half north of Lake Barkley, this immense limestone cave system is one of the most popular destinations in the region.
  • Nashville, TN: Known as the “Music City,” Nashville is just an hour and a half away from Lake Barkley and offers numerous attractions such as the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum, the Ryman Auditorium, and the Grand Ole Opry.
  • Land Between the Lakes Scenic Byway: Take a drive along this picturesque route, which stretches for 85 miles through the heart of the Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat surrounded by pristine natural beauty, or an outdoor adventure that will leave you breathless, Lake Barkley is the perfect destination for any traveler. With its stunning parks and reserves, rich cultural heritage, and mouthwatering local cuisine, this region of South has something for everyone.
