Introduction to Laurel

Located in the heart of the Mississippi Gulf Coast, Laurel is a charming town best known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. Founded in 1882, Laurel has grown into a bustling modern city with plenty of attractions for visitors of all ages.

Famous for being the birthplace of legendary football coach Walter Payton, Laurel is a perfect destination for sports enthusiasts. Additionally, history buffs, nature lovers, and foodies will also find lots to love in this vibrant town. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top things to do, see, and experience in Laurel, Mississippi.

List of Places to Visit in Laurel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lauren Rogers Museum of Art: A must-visit spot for art lovers, the Lauren Rogers Museum of Art boasts an impressive collection of American and European artworks.
  • Landrum’s Homestead and Village: This historic village invites visitors to take a step back in time and experience life in 1800s Mississippi. Explore the restored homes and learn about the town’s pioneer roots.
  • Chase Street Neighborhood: A great example of early 20th century architecture, the Chase Street Neighborhood boasts beautiful homes and interesting landmarks.

Natural Attractions of Laurel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Okatoma River: For visitors interested in outdoor activities, the Okatoma River is a great spot for kayaking, canoeing, and fishing.
  • Daphne Park: A lovely community park with walking trails, picnic areas, and a splash pad, perfect for families with children.
  • Gaylord Opryland Resort Gardens: One of the most beautiful botanical gardens in the area, the Gaylord Opryland Resort Gardens is home to more than 50,000 tropical plants and flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laurel

For visitors who love to explore the outdoors on foot or by bike, Laurel boasts plenty of scenic routes. Some of the best trails include:

  • Longleaf Trace: A 41-mile paved trail perfect for walkers, runners, and cyclists.
  • Bonita Lakes Trail: A 13-mile trail that winds around scenic Bonita Lakes.
  • Little Rock Trail: A 2.5-mile trail through a beautiful forested area with stunning views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Laurel: Activities and Tours

  • Walter Payton Center at Jones College: A great spot for sports enthusiasts, the Walter Payton Center is a state-of-the-art sports facility that offers a range of activities from basketball to volleyball.
  • Downtown Laurel: Take a leisurely stroll through the historic Downtown area, visit local shops and restaurants, and immerse yourself in the town’s charming ambiance.
  • Laurel Mercantile Co.: This lovely shop is a great spot to find souvenirs and unique gifts, and offers a true taste of the town’s local culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Laurel

Laurel is known for its friendly locals, who take pride in their town’s rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s many museums, historic sites, and landmarks, and to try the local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Laurel

The best time to visit Laurel is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are plentiful. In the summer, temperatures can soar, so visitors are advised to stay hydrated and take precautions against the sun. In the winter, temperatures can dip below freezing, so visitors are advised to pack warm clothing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laurel

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and natural beauty of the area.
  • Try the local cuisine and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the local community or damage historic sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laurel

Laurel is generally a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise basic safety precautions such as locking their cars and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Laurel

Visitors to Laurel should take precautions against heat stroke and sunburn during the summer months, particularly if engaging in outdoor activities. Visitors are also advised to pack sunscreen and wear protective clothing.
