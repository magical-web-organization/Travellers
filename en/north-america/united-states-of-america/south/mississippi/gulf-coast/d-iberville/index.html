Introduction to D’Iberville

Located on the Gulf Coast of Mississippi, D’Iberville is a charming town that offers visitors a wide range of cultural and natural attractions. With a rich history that dates back to the 17th century, D’Iberville is a great place to explore and learn about the region’s past. Whether you’re looking to spend your days hiking, biking, and exploring the outdoors or visiting museums and historical sites, D’Iberville has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything the town has to offer and help you plan the perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in D’Iberville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

D’Iberville has a rich cultural heritage that’s on display at many of the town’s museums and historical sites. Here are a few of the top attractions:

  • Ohr-O’Keefe Museum of Art: This museum celebrates the life and work of George E. Ohr, who is known as the “Mad Potter of Biloxi.” The museum also features works by other local artists and hosts a variety of events and exhibitions throughout the year.
  • Biloxi Lighthouse: Visitors can climb to the top of this historic lighthouse for stunning views of the surrounding area. Built in 1848, the lighthouse is a National Historic Landmark.
  • Beauvoir: The home of Confederate President Jefferson Davis, Beauvoir is a must-visit for history buffs. The estate includes a museum, library, and historic cemetery.

Natural Attractions of D’Iberville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its cultural attractions, D’Iberville is also home to stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities. Here are a few of the top natural attractions:

  • Gulf Islands National Seashore: This beautiful park spans the coasts of Mississippi and Florida, and includes barrier islands, coastal marshes, and beautiful beaches. Visitors can swim, fish, hike, kayak, and more.
  • Pascagoula River Audubon Center: This nature center offers visitors the opportunity to see a variety of birds, mammals, and reptiles in their natural habitats. Guided tours, educational exhibits, and eco-cruises are available.
  • Big Ridge Bike and Pedestrian Trail: This scenic trail winds through the woods in D’Iberville and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. Bikes can be rented at the trailhead, and there are several picnic areas along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of D’Iberville

D’Iberville is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are a variety of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some top options include:

  • Big Ridge Bike and Pedestrian Trail: This 3.5-mile trail is accessible to walkers, runners, and cyclists, and offers beautiful views of the surrounding woods.
  • Tuxachanie Trail: Located about 45 minutes from D’Iberville, this 12.3-mile trail is one of the most popular hiking spots in the area. It traverses through beautiful forests and streams, and includes boardwalks and bridges.
  • Long Leaf Trace: This 44-mile trail starts in Hattiesburg and ends in Prentiss, and includes a variety of paved and unpaved sections. Bikes can be rented at several points along the way.

Best Things to Do in D’Iberville: Activities and Tours

In addition to its stunning natural landscapes and cultural attractions, D’Iberville offers a variety of activities and tours. Here are a few options:

  • Fishing Charters: D’Iberville is home to several fishing charter companies that offer half-day and full-day trips. Visitors can catch a variety of fish, including redfish, snapper, and speckled trout.
  • Brewery Tours and Tastings: Visitors can take a tour of Lazy Magnolia Brewery, the oldest brewery in Mississippi, and sample some of their award-winning beers.
  • Escape Room: Visitors can challenge themselves by attempting to escape from the escape room at Mississippi Escape Rooms.

Week-Long Itinerary for D’Iberville

If you’re planning a week-long trip to D’Iberville, here’s a sample itinerary that will allow you to see and do as much as possible:

Day 1: Visit the Ohr-O’Keefe Museum of Art and take a stroll along the Biloxi Beach Boardwalk. Day 2: Spend the day exploring the Pascagoula River Audubon Center and taking an eco-cruise along the river. Day 3: Go hiking on the Tuxachanie Trail and have a picnic lunch. Day 4: Take a fishing charter and try to catch some redfish or speckled trout. Day 5: Visit the Beauvoir estate and museum. Day 6: Rent bikes and explore the Big Ridge Bike and Pedestrian Trail. Day 7: Take a day trip to New Orleans, which is about 90 minutes away.

Food and Drink Scene of D’Iberville

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to dining in D’Iberville, there are a few dishes that you simply have to try:

  • Seafood: From shrimp to fish and oysters to crab, D’Iberville is known for its fresh seafood. Some top seafood spots include Half Shell Oyster House, Shaggy’s Gulfport Beach, and Mary Mahoney’s Old French House.
  • Po’ Boys: Another local specialty, po’ boys are sandwiches that can be filled with shrimp, oysters, crawfish, or roast beef. Some popular spots for po’ boys include Bozo’s Grocery, Darwell’s Cafe, and The Fillin’ Station.
  • Barbecue: If you’re in the mood for some barbecue, check out Murky Waters BBQ or The Shed BBQ and Blues Joint.

Local Beverages

In addition to its restaurants, D’Iberville is also home to a few local breweries and distilleries. Some options to try include:

  • Lazy Magnolia Brewery: This brewery is located in nearby Kiln, Mississippi, and offers tours and tastings of their award-winning beers.
  • Cathead Distillery: Located in Jackson, Mississippi, Cathead Distillery produces a variety of craft liquors, including gin, vodka, and bourbon.
  • Coast Roast Coffee & Tea: If you’re in need of a caffeine fix, head to this local coffee shop for a cup of freshly roasted coffee.

Where to Stay in D’Iberville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

D’Iberville has a variety of lodging options to suit every budget and preference. Some top choices include:

  • Suburban Extended Stay Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms with kitchenettes and is located just a few miles from the Gulf Coast.
  • Scarlet Pearl Casino Resort: This luxury resort features a casino, multiple restaurants and bars, and a luxurious spa.
  • Hilton Garden Inn Biloxi: This hotel is located just a few miles from the beach and offers a pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in D’Iberville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, D’Iberville has a few options to choose from:

  • Gulf Coast Seafood: If you love seafood, consider bringing home some fresh Gulf Coast shrimp or oysters to cook at home.
  • Artwork: The Ohr-O’Keefe Museum of Art has a gift shop that sells artwork and other souvenirs created by local artists.
  • Mississippi-made Products: Look for products made in Mississippi, such as Cathead vodka or Lazy Magnolia beer, to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of D’Iberville

D’Iberville is a friendly, welcoming community with a strong sense of southern hospitality. When visiting, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  • Dress Code: Casual attire is generally acceptable in most places, although some restaurants may have a dress code.
  • Greetings: Punctuality is important in D’Iberville, so be sure to arrive on time for appointments and meetings. When meeting someone for the first time, a handshake is appropriate.
  • Mardi Gras: D’Iberville is known for its Mardi Gras celebrations, which feature parades, parties, and plenty of beads. If you’re visiting during Mardi Gras season, be sure to join in on the festivities.

Best Time to Visit D’Iberville

The best time to visit D’Iberville is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. In the summer, temperatures can be hot and humid, and in the winter, temperatures can be cool but still comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do:

  • Explore the natural landscapes and take advantage of outdoor activities.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in D’Iberville

D’Iberville is a relatively safe town, but travelers should use common sense and take precautions to ensure their safety. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Use caution when swimming in the Gulf of Mexico, as riptides and other hazards can be present.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking, especially in remote areas.
  • Lock your car and secure valuables to prevent theft.

Health in D’Iberville

D’Iberville has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, in case of emergency. Additionally, there are several pharmacies and drug stores in town for any medication or health needs.

Getting to D’Iberville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near D’Iberville

The closest airport to D’Iberville is Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport, which is located about 20 minutes away. The airport offers several flights a day to major U.S. destinations, including Atlanta, Dallas, and Houston.

Bus Stations Near D’Iberville

Greyhound has a bus station in Gulfport, which is about 15 minutes from D’Iberville. The station offers several daily departures to major U.S. cities, including New Orleans, Houston, and Memphis.

Other Options

Renting a car is a good option for travelers who want to explore the surrounding area or who are arriving at a nearby airport. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in D’Iberville.

How to Get Around in D’Iberville

D’Iberville is a small town that’s easily navigable. Walking or biking is a great way to explore the downtown area and surrounding neighborhoods. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from D’Iberville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Gulf Coast region, here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Biloxi: This neighboring town is home to several casinos, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • New Orleans: Located about 90 minutes away, New Orleans is known for its food, music, and vibrant culture.
  • Mobile, Alabama: This historic port city is about 2 hours away and offers visitors the opportunity to learn about the region’s history and culture.

Conclusion

D’Iberville, Mississippi is a charming town with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural landscapes. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, hiking through the woods, or indulging in local cuisine, D’Iberville has something for everyone. By following this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip and make the most of your time in this unique and welcoming town.
