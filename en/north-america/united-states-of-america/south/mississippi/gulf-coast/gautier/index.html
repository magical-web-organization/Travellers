Introduction to Gautier

Located in Gulf Coast, Mississippi, Gautier is a charming city known for its historical landmarks, museums, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re a nature lover or a history enthusiast, Gautier has something to offer for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, how to get there, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Gautier

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gautier has a rich cultural heritage, and there are several museums and historical places you can visit to learn about the city’s history and traditions. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Walter Anderson Museum of Art: This museum showcases the works of Walter Anderson, a famous artist who spent his life in Gautier. The museum features paintings, drawings, and sculptures by Anderson, as well as other artists from the region.

  • La Pointe-Krebs House & Museum: Built in the 1750s, this historical landmark is the oldest structure in Mississippi. The house has been restored to its original condition, and visitors can take a tour to learn about the history of the house and the region.

  • Mississippi Sandhill Crane National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge is home to the endangered Mississippi sandhill crane, and visitors can take a tour to see these beautiful birds in their natural habitat.

Natural Attractions of Gautier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gautier is a paradise for nature enthusiasts, with several outdoor activities and scenic spots to explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Pascagoula River Audubon Center: This center is located in the heart of Gautier and offers several outdoor activities, such as birdwatching, kayaking, and hiking. The center also has a museum and educational programs for visitors.

  • G.I. Museum: This museum honors the military history of Gautier and the surrounding area. Visitors can see military uniforms, artifacts, and vehicles.

  • Gautier City Park: This park is a must-visit for families and outdoor enthusiasts. It has several playgrounds, picnic areas, and sports fields. Visitors can also take a stroll through the park’s beautiful trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gautier

Gautier has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the city’s natural beauty. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Red Creek Trail: This trail runs through the beautiful Mississippi Sandhill Crane National Wildlife Refuge and offers visitors the chance to see the sandhill crane and other wildlife up close.

  • Little Bluff Trail: This trail is located in the Pascagoula River Audubon Center and is perfect for hiking and birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Gautier: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and exploring the city’s cultural and natural attractions, there are several other activities and tours you can enjoy in Gautier, such as:

  • Gautier Mural Tour: Gautier has several eye-catching murals painted on its buildings, and visitors can take a tour to see these beautiful artworks.

  • Paddleboarding on the Pascagoula River: Visitors can rent paddleboards and explore the Pascagoula River at their own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gautier

If you’re planning to spend a week in Gautier, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Walter Anderson Museum of Art and explore the city’s downtown area.

Day 2: Spend the day at the Mississippi Sandhill Crane National Wildlife Refuge and take a tour to see the cranes.

Day 3: Go hiking on the Red Creek Trail in the morning and visit the La Pointe-Krebs House & Museum in the afternoon.

Day 4: Take a paddleboard tour on the Pascagoula River in the morning and visit the G.I. Museum in the afternoon.

Day 5: Spend the day at the Gautier City Park and explore its beautiful trails and picnic areas.

Day 6: Take a guided mural tour of the city and visit the Pascagoula River Audubon Center in the afternoon.

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at your hotel or guesthouse and enjoy the city’s food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Gautier

Gautier has a vibrant food and drink scene that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood gumbo: This dish is a Cajun classic and is made with shrimp, crab, and okra.

  • Po’boy sandwich: This sandwich is a New Orleans favorite and is made with crispy fried shrimp, oysters, or catfish.

Local Beverages

  • Mugshots Grill & Bar: This bar offers a wide selection of local craft beers, as well as tasty cocktails.

  • The Shed: This iconic barbecue joint has a lively outdoor bar and serves up delicious drinks such as their “Hillbilly Tea.”

Where to Stay in Gautier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gautier has several options for accommodation, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area:

  • Magnuson Hotel Gautier: This hotel has comfortable rooms and is located close to many of the city’s top attractions.

  • Bayou Log Cabins: These cozy cabins offer a unique experience and are perfect for those who want to be close to nature.

  • Autumn Inn: This bed and breakfast is located in a historic building and offers guests a charming and comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Gautier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Gautier

Gautier has a rich culture, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful of local customs and traditions. The city is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are always happy to share their culture with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Gautier

The best time to visit Gautier is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly.

Getting to Gautier: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gautier

The nearest airport to Gautier is Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport, which is located approximately 30 miles away. There are plenty of transportation options available from the airport, including taxis, rental cars, and shuttles.

Bus Stations Near Gautier

Greyhound Bus Lines has a terminal in Mobile, Alabama, which is approximately 25 miles from Gautier. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Gautier.

Other Options

For those who prefer to drive, Gautier can be easily accessed via Interstate 10, which runs through the city.

How to Get Around in Gautier

Gautier is a small city, and many of its attractions can be accessed on foot or by bicycle. However, visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car or hire a taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Gautier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Biloxi: This nearby city is known for its casinos, beaches, and seafood restaurants.

  • Pascagoula: This charming city is located on the banks of the Pascagoula River and has several historic sites and outdoor attractions.

Conclusion
