Introduction to Ocean Springs

Located in the Gulf Coast of Mississippi, Ocean Springs is a picturesque seaside town that offers visitors a chance to relax amidst natural beauty and explore a rich cultural heritage. Known for its lively arts scene, outstanding cuisine, and recreational opportunities, Ocean Springs is the perfect destination for travelers looking to experience a unique mix of Southern hospitality and beach vacation.

This travel guide is designed to help you plan your trip to Ocean Springs, providing a comprehensive overview of the town’s main attractions, helpful tips on accommodation and transportation, and insider information on local customs and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Ocean Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ocean Springs is home to several museums and historic landmarks that offer a glimpse into the town’s past and present. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

Walter Anderson Museum of Art

The Walter Anderson Museum of Art celebrates the life and work of Walter Anderson, one of Mississippi’s most renowned artists. The museum houses a vast collection of Anderson’s paintings, ceramics, and prints, as well as temporary exhibitions featuring contemporary artists.

Mary C. O’Keefe Cultural Center

The Mary C. O’Keefe Cultural Center is a multi-purpose venue that hosts art exhibitions, theater performances, music concerts, and workshops. The center also features a pottery studio, a ceramics museum, and a garden.

Historic Ocean Springs L&N Depot

The Historic Ocean Springs L&N Depot is a preserverd railroad station buildt by the Louisvilke and N;asville Railrod Company in 1907. The Depot Museum has interactive exhibits and historic railroad memorabilia. The L&N Depot offers a trolley and free walking tours and a year-round events.

Natural Attractions of Ocean Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy exploring Ocean Springs’ scenic coastline, marshlands, and parks. Here are some of the top natural attractions to add to your itinerary:

Gulf Islands National Seashore

The Gulf Island National Seashore is a protected area that spans across several islands and stretches of coastline in Mississippi and Florida. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, hiking, and exploring historic fortifications. You can also see ospreys, herons, and pelicans in their natural habitats.

Davis Bayou Park

Davis Bayou Park is part of the Gulf Islands National Seashore and offers visitors an opportunity to hike, bike, fish, and birdwatch. There is also an information center and kayak/canoe rentals available.

Shearwater Pottery

Shearwater Pottery is a family-owned pottery studio that has been creating unique and collectible pottery since 1928. Visitors can take a tour of the studio, watch artists at work, and purchase handcrafted pottery pieces.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocean Springs

For those who enjoy the outdoors, Ocean Springs has plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking.

Tuxachanie Trail

Located in Gulf Coast National Recreation Area, The Tuxachanie Trail is about 12 miles long and is suitable for experienced hikers. The trail is surrounded by marshes and pine forests and provides scenic views of the Gulf Coast.

Marsh Trail

The Marsh Trail is a short trail that takes visitors to the top of a mound, offering panoramic views of the Gulf Coast. It’s primarily a bird-watching route, and you can keep an eye out for great blue herons, snowy egrets, and red-winged blackbirds.

Best Things to Do in Ocean Springs: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Ocean Springs offers an array of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests.

Biloxi Boat Tours

Biloxi Boat Tours offers sightseeing and eco-tours of the Gulf Coast and Barrier Islands departing from Biloxi, which is just a short drive from Ocean Springs. Visitors can see dolphins, sea turtles, and coastal birds, and learn about the area’s history and ecology.

Walter Anderson Museum of Art

In addition to its exhibitions of Anderson’s work, the Walter Anderson Museum of Art hosts a variety of workshops, classes, and events for all ages. Visitors can participate in art-making activities, attend lectures and performances, and meet with artists in residence.

Food and Drink Scene of Ocean Springs

Ocean Springs has a reputation for a great food and drink scene. You’ll find traditional Southern comfort food, seafood delicacies, and fusion cuisine with a twist.

Must-Try Dishes

Shrimp and Grits

This classic Southern dish is made with Gulf Coast shrimp and stone-ground grits, served with bacon, green onions, and a savory sauce.

BBQ Oysters

BBQ oysters are a popular delicacy in Gulf Coast cuisine. The oysters are marinated in a spicy butter mixture, grilled, and served with lemon wedges and crackers.

Local Beverages

Margaritas

A popular cocktail in Ocean Springs, margaritas are made with fresh lime juice, tequila, and triple sec.

Craft Beers

Ocean Springs has several breweries that produce unique craft beers. Be sure to try local breweries like Crooked Letter Brewing Company and Chandeleur Island Brewing.

Where to Stay in Ocean Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ocean Springs has numerous lodging options, ranging from quaint bed and breakfasts to luxurious resorts.

The Roost Ocean Springs

This award-winning bed and breakfast is located in the heart of downtown Ocean Spring and offers luxurious accommodations with a Southern hospitality charm.

Gulf Hills Hotel

The historic Gulf Hills Hotel is located in a beautiful seaside community on the outskirts of Ocean Springs. The hotel offers a pool, spa, and fine dining.

What to Buy in Ocean Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure you bring a taste of Ocean Springs back with you with these local souvenirs.

Shearwater Pottery

Shearwater Pottery is known both nationally and internationally for its unique brand of creative pottery.

The French Potager

The French Potager is a local market and cafe specializing in French cuisine, offering sauces and condiments to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ocean Springs

Ocean Springs has a rich cultural heritage. Be sure to check out and respect local customs and traditions when traveling to the area.

Hospitality and Friendliness

The people of Ocean Springs are known for their Southern hospitality and friendliness. Expect to be greeted with a smile and politeness anywhere you go.

Arts

Ocean Springs is known for its thriving arts community, with many festivals and art events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Ocean Springs

The best time to visit Ocean Springs is during the spring and fall when the weather is pleasant and comfortable with less humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ocean Springs

Do:

  • Explore the town’s many attractions.

  • Indulge in the region’s exceptional cuisine.

  • Enjoy the laid-back beach atmosphere.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb wildlife.

  • Ignore the town’s local customs and mannerisms.

  • Leave your sunscreen at home.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ocean Springs

Ocean Springs is generally safe, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their cars, avoiding suspicious areas, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Ocean Springs

Visitors should take precautions when out in the sun and stay hydrated, especially during the hot and humid summer months.

Getting to Ocean Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocean Springs

The nearest major airport is Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport (GPT), located about 25 miles west of Ocean Springs. Southern Airways Express offers regular flights between GPT and Memphis, TN.

Bus Stations Near Ocean Springs

The nearest bus stations are Greyhound terminals in Biloxi and Pascagoula, both of which are about 10 miles away from Ocean Springs. From there, travelers can take a taxi or Uber to their final destination.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Ocean Springs by car, with major interstates and highways connecting the town to many other US destinations

How to Get Around in Ocean Springs

Visitors can get around Ocean Springs by car or bike. You can also hail a taxi or use Uber.

Where to Go Next from Ocean Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations from Ocean Springs include Biloxi, which is known for its casinos and beachfront attractions, and Pascagoula, a small town with a historic city center and beautiful beachfronts.

Conclusion

Ocean Springs is a hidden gem in the Gulf Coast that offers visitors the best of both worlds: a relaxing beach vacation and a culturally rich experience. With its natural beauty, arts scene, and culinary delights, Ocean Springs is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Southern experience.
