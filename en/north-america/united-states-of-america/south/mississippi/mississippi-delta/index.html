Introduction to Mississippi Delta

Mississippi Delta is a region located in the western part of Mississippi, USA, stretching from Memphis, Tennessee to Vicksburg, Mississippi. The region is known for its rich history, beautiful outdoors, and soulful music.

Mississippi Delta is a paradise for adventure seekers, history buffs, nature lovers, and food enthusiasts. Whether you are traveling solo or with your family, you will find plenty of things to do, see, and experience in Mississippi Delta.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, activities to do, insider tips, and essential information for a perfect Mississippi Delta travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Mississippi Delta

Mississippi Delta is home to several iconic attractions that showcase the region’s rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history aficionados, Mississippi Delta has a plethora of museums and historical sites, including:

  • Delta Blues Museum: Located in Clarksdale, this museum is dedicated to preserving and promoting the blues music heritage of Mississippi.
  • B.B. King Museum and Delta Interpretive Center: This museum in Indianola commemorates the life and legacy of the legendary blues musician B.B. King.
  • Medgar Evers House Museum: Visit this museum in Jackson, and learn about the life and activism of an iconic civil rights leader, Medgar Wiley Evers.
  • Vicksburg National Military Park: This park is a national historic landmark where the 47-day Siege of Vicksburg occurred during the American Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Mississippi Delta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mississippi Delta is a true wonderland for nature lovers. From peaceful wetlands to rugged trails, the natural attractions of the region are breathtakingly beautiful. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Tunica Hills Wildlife Management Area: This management area offers hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and a chance to see rare plant species.
  • Delta National Forest: Spread across 60,000 acres, Delta National Forest is a popular destination for camping, hiking, bird watching, and hunting.
  • Cat Island National Wildlife Refuge: Located on the Mississippi River, this refuge is home to a variety of migratory birds, river otters, and alligators.
  • Tishomingo State Park: This park is known for its scenic views, nature trails, rock formations, and is home to the famous swinging bridge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mississippi Delta

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Mississippi Delta, and the region boasts several trails that cater to all skill levels. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Mississippi Delta:

  • Natchez Trace Parkway: This 444-mile trail starts in Natchez, Mississippi, and ends in Nashville, Tennessee, and features scenic hiking and biking trails.
  • Tanglefoot Trail: Located in Houston, Mississippi, this 43-mile trail is perfect for families and offers beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Bear Creek Outcropping Trail: This 4.6-mile trail in Tishomingo State Park boasts stunning views of the Bear Creek Canyon and is popular among seasoned hikers.
  • Leflore County Rail Trail: Stretching for 10 miles, this trail is ideal for biking and offers beautiful views of the Mississippi River.

Best Things to Do in Mississippi Delta: Activities and Tours

Mississippi Delta offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained throughout their stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Mississippi Delta:

  • Visit the B.B. King Museum and Interpretive Center: Learn about the life and legacy of the blues legend B.B. King and immerse yourself in the musical heritage of Mississippi.
  • Take a Mississippi River Cruise: A Mississippi River cruise is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that offers breathtaking views of the river and its picturesque surroundings.
  • Participate in a Civil Rights Tour: Mississippi Delta played a pivotal role in the Civil Rights Movement, and taking a Civil Rights Tour is an excellent way to learn about the region’s history.
  • Enjoy Local Cuisine: Mississippi Delta is famous for its soul food, including fried chicken, barbecue ribs, and traditional southern dishes like po’boys and gumbo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mississippi Delta

Here’s a week-long itinerary for making the most of your Mississippi Delta visit:

  • Day 1: Visit the Vicksburg National Military Park and explore the historic significance of the region.
  • Day 2: Take a Mississippi River cruise and treat yourself to the spectacular beauty of the river and its surroundings.
  • Day 3: Visit the B.B. King Museum and Interpretive Center and experience the musical heritage of Mississippi.
  • Day 4: Enjoy outdoor adventures at the Tishomingo State Park.
  • Day 5: Explore the Civil Rights history of Jackson and visit the Medgar Evers House Museum.
  • Day 6: Discover the local food culture of Mississippi Delta and enjoy the soulful cuisine.
  • Day 7: Visit the Delta Blues Museum and revel in the musical brilliance of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Mississippi Delta

Mississippi Delta is renowned for its soulful cuisine that brings together the flavors of southern, African, and Creole cooking. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Mississippi Delta:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: Mississippi Delta has some of the best fried chicken joints in the country, and you must try this ultimate comfort food when in the region.
  • Barbecue Ribs: Mississippi Delta’s barbecue ribs are a local specialty that will leave you craving for more.
  • Cornbread: Made with a blend of cornmeal, flour, and spices, cornbread is a staple in Mississippi Delta’s cuisine.
  • Gumbo: This Creole stew, made with seafood, smoked sausage, and okra, is a must-try for any food enthusiast visiting Mississippi Delta.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A quintessential southern drink, sweet tea is a refreshing beverage that complements Mississippi Delta’s spicy cuisine perfectly.
  • Beer: Mississippi Delta is home to several local breweries that offer a range of craft beers that you should try.
  • Muscadine Wine: This sweet wine made from Muscadine grapes is a local specialty and worth trying.

Where to Stay in Mississippi Delta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mississippi Delta offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Mississippi Delta:

  • Hampton Inn and Suites, Vicksburg: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites and is located near the Vicksburg National Military Park.
  • Isle of Capri Casino Hotel, Lula: This casino hotel features a range of amenities, including a fitness center, outdoor pool, and several dining options.
  • The Alluvian, Greenwood: This boutique hotel features luxurious rooms, a spa, and a fine-dining restaurant.
  • Mayfield Guesthouse, Clarksdale: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Clarksdale and is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Mississippi Delta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mississippi Delta boasts several local products and souvenirs that make for excellent gifts and keepsakes. Here are some of the best things to buy in Mississippi Delta:

  • Blues Memorabilia: Mississippi Delta is the birthplace of the blues, and you can find several blues-themed souvenirs, including T-shirts, posters, and memorabilia at the Delta Blues Museum.
  • Locally Made Crafts: Mississippi Delta has a vibrant arts and crafts scene, and you can find several handcrafted items like pottery, baskets, and woodwork.
  • Food and Beverages: Bring home the taste of Mississippi Delta with locally made food products like jellies, jams, hot sauce, and wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Mississippi Delta

Mississippi Delta is known for its soulful music, rich history, and warm hospitality. When visiting Mississippi Delta, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some cultural do’s and don’ts:

  • Do Respect the Music and Culture: Mississippi Delta is the birthplace of the blues, and music is an integral part of the local culture. Respect the local music and artists and attend live performances to immerse yourself in the soulful sounds of the region.
  • Don’t Use Offensive Language: Mississippi Delta has a diverse population, and it’s important to respect cultural differences. Avoid using offensive language or making remarks that may offend locals.
  • Do Dress Appropriately: Dress modestly and appropriately when visiting museums, historical sites, and religious places.
  • Don’t Ignore Local Customs: When visiting someone’s home, it’s essential to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Mississippi Delta

The best time to visit Mississippi Delta is between March and May and September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are lesser. Summers in Mississippi Delta can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and wet, so plan your visit accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mississippi Delta

Do:

  • Do embrace the local culture and customs.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do attend live music performances.
  • Do visit the significant historical and natural sites of the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t use offensive language or make derogatory remarks.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t litter or harm the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mississippi Delta

Mississippi Delta is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take necessary safety precautions like securing your valuables and avoiding high-crime areas.

Health in Mississippi Delta

Public health facilities in Mississippi Delta are of high quality, and the region is free of any significant health risks. However, it’s always good to carry a first-aid kit and take necessary precautions against mild illnesses like common cold and flu.

Getting to Mississippi Delta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mississippi Delta

The nearest commercial airports to Mississippi Delta are:

  • Jackson-Medgar Wiley Evers International Airport (JAN)
  • Memphis International Airport (MEM)
  • Greenville Mid-Delta Regional Airport (GLH)

Bus Stations Near Mississippi Delta

Greyhound operates a bus service that serves several cities in Mississippi Delta, including Jackson and Greenville.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are also available in Mississippi Delta.

How to Get Around in Mississippi Delta

Driving is the most convenient and efficient way to get around Mississippi Delta as public transportation options are limited. Several car rental companies operate in the region, and taxis are also available in major cities.

Where to Go Next from Mississippi Delta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mississippi Delta is conveniently located near several popular destinations, including:

  • New Orleans, Louisiana (264 miles)
  • Nashville, Tennessee (209 miles)
  • Birmingham, Alabama (225 miles)
  • Gulfport, Mississippi (279 miles)

Conclusion

Mississippi Delta is a treasure trove of music, history, and nature, and a must-visit destination for any traveler seeking a unique experience. The region offers plenty of attractions, activities, local cuisine, and warm hospitality, making it a great spot for a memorable vacation.
