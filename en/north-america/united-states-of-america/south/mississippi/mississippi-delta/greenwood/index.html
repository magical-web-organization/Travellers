Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Greenwood is steeped in history and culture, with plenty of fascinating museums and historical sites to explore. The following are some of the must-visit attractions:

The Mississippi Delta Blues Museum

The Mississippi Delta is the birthplace of the blues, and the Mississippi Delta Blues Museum is dedicated to preserving and celebrating this important musical genre. Visitors can explore a range of exhibits and collections that showcase the history of blues music in the region.

The Museum of the Mississippi Delta

The Museum of the Mississippi Delta is a great place to learn about the rich cultural history of the region. This museum features a range of exhibits that cover everything from Native American history to early explorers, the Civil War, and the arts.

The Amzie Moore House

The Amzie Moore House is a historic home that belonged to a prominent Civil Rights activist. Visitors can take a tour of the house and learn about Moore’s life and work, as well as the broader Civil Rights movement of the 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Greenwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Mississippi Delta boasts a unique landscape, with winding rivers, dense forests, and sprawling wetlands. Visitors to Greenwood can explore this stunning natural setting through a range of outdoor activities and adventures. Here are some of the top options:

Leflore County Wildlife Management Area

The Leflore County Wildlife Management Area is a massive natural reserve that encompasses over 30,000 acres of pristine wilderness. Visitors can hike, bike, or birdwatch through the area, or even take a guided hunting trip.

Tallahatchie National Wildlife Refuge

The Tallahatchie National Wildlife Refuge is located just outside of Greenwood and is home to a range of wildlife species, from migratory birds to white-tailed deer. Visitors can explore the scenic trails or take a guided tour of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenwood

Greenwood is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes:

The Delta Heritage Trail

The Delta Heritage Trail is a scenic hiking and biking route that takes visitors through the heart of the Mississippi Delta. The trail stretches over 80 miles and offers stunning views of the natural landscape and local communities.

The Greenwood Gravel Grind

The Greenwood Gravel Grind is a challenging biking route that takes riders through some of the most scenic areas around Greenwood. This 50-mile route features plenty of hills, rough terrain, and stunning vistas.

Best Things to Do in Greenwood: Activities and Tours

Greenwood is a vibrant city with plenty of activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of the top options:

The Viking Cooking School

The Viking Cooking School offers a range of cooking classes and workshops, where visitors can learn how to create authentic Southern dishes. Classes cover everything from barbecue to seafood and are held in state-of-the-art kitchens.

The Riverboat Tour

The Riverboat Tour takes visitors on a leisurely cruise down the Mississippi River, showcasing the stunning natural beauty of the area. Visitors can enjoy a range of onboard amenities, from live music to delicious food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenwood

Greenwood has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of delicious local cuisine to sample. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Southern Style Barbecue

Southern Style Barbecue is a staple of the Mississippi Delta, and Greenwood has plenty of great joints. Be sure to try juicy pulled pork, tender ribs, or smoky brisket.

Fried Catfish

Fried Catfish is another Southern favorite and can be found all over Greenwood. Enjoy crispy fillets with hushpuppies and vinegar slaw on the side.

Where to Stay in Greenwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greenwood offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the top places to stay:

Alluvian Hotel

The Alluvian Hotel is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Greenwood. The hotel offers stylish rooms and suites, as well as a range of amenities including a spa, fitness center, and fine dining restaurant.

The Americas Best Value Inn

The Americas Best Value Inn is a budget-friendly option with clean, comfortable rooms and a range of amenities including a swimming pool and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Greenwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Greenwood is home to a range of unique shops and boutiques, offering a range of locally made products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best options:

Turnrow Books

Turnrow Books is a charming bookstore located in the heart of Greenwood. The store carries a wide selection of local and regional literature, as well as gifts and souvenirs.

Leflore County Farmer’s Market

The Leflore County Farmer’s Market is a great place to find locally grown produce, artisanal products, and handmade crafts. Visitors can stock up on everything from fresh fruits and vegetables to homemade jams and jellies.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenwood

Greenwood is a city with a rich cultural history, and visitors should take the time to learn about local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Southerners are known for their hospitality, so expect warm greetings and friendly conversation.
  • The Mississippi Delta is famous for its music, so be sure to check out a live performance while you’re in town.
  • Southern cuisine is a point of pride in Greenwood, so be adventurous and try something new.

Best Time to Visit Greenwood

The best time to visit Greenwood is in the spring or fall when the weather is pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and damp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenwood

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially Southern style barbecue and fried catfish.
  • Do visit the many museums and historical attractions in Greenwood.
  • Do take advantage of the city’s natural attractions, including the wildlife reserves and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen, bug spray, and a hat for outdoor excursions.
  • Don’t mention controversial political or social topics.
  • Don’t be late for scheduled activities or appointments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenwood

Greenwood is a generally safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions to stay safe. Here are some tips:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight to avoid theft.
  • Avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Greenwood

Greenwood has a few medical facilities to cater to your health needs, and emergency services are available.

Getting to Greenwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greenwood

The closest airport to Greenwood is the Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport, which is approximately 110 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Greenwood

Greyhound operates a bus service to Greenwood, connecting the city to other major locations in Mississippi and neighboring states.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a convenient option for getting around the city.

How to Get Around in Greenwood

Greenwood is a small city, and the best way to get around is by car. Taxis and rental cars are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Greenwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The historic town of Natchez, located on the Mississippi River, is just over a two-hour drive from Greenwood.
  • The vibrant city of Jackson, Mississippi’s state capital, is just over an hour’s drive from Greenwood.
  • The Vicksburg National Military Park, site of an important Civil War battle, is just under a two-hour drive from Greenwood.

Conclusion

From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural landscapes and world-class food scene, Greenwood is a destination that should be on every traveler’s radar. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your next Southern adventure in Greenwood.
