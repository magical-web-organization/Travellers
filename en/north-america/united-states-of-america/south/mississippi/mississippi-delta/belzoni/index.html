A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Belzoni, Mississippi

Belzoni, located in the heart of the Mississippi Delta, is a charming small town that serves as an excellent base for exploring this historic region. In this comprehensive travel guide to Belzoni, we’ll take a closer look at what to see and do in this picturesque town, as well as discover the best outdoor pursuits, cultural attractions, local customs, and where to stay. So, let’s dive right in!

List of Places to Visit in Belzoni

Belzoni may be small, but it is packed full of things to see and do. Here are some of the must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Catfish Museum and Welcome Center: This interesting museum offers a unique insight into the world of catfish farming, which is a major industry in the Mississippi Delta.

  • The George P. Cossar State Park: The park is located on one of the largest lakes in the state, Enid Lake, complete with a marina, boat ramp, swimming beach, picnic areas, playgrounds, cabins, and campsites.

  • The Mississippi Delta Chinese Heritage Museum and Store: Housed in a historic 1920s Chinese grocery store, this museum tells the fascinating story of the Chinese community that settled in the Delta in the late 19th century to work on plantations.

Natural Attractions of Belzoni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Humphreys County WMA: This wildlife management area is an outdoor lover’s paradise, with miles of hiking and biking trails, excellent birdwatching opportunities, and an abundance of wildlife, from deer to wild turkeys.

  • Sunflower River: This river is popular for kayaking and fishing. It is considered one of the clearest and most scenic rivers in the state.

  • Catfish Lake: Located near Belzoni, this lake is a popular destination for fishing for catfish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belzoni

Belzoni and the surrounding area are a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • Catfish Capital Trail: This trail takes visitors through the heart of downtown Belzoni and showcases its rich history and culture.

  • Enid Lake Multi-Use Trail: This 26-mile trail winds around picturesque Enid Lake and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Delta National Forest Trails: The Delta National Forest, located just south of Belzoni, offers several trails that wind through pine forests and along winding creeks.

Best Things to Do in Belzoni: Activities and Tours

Belzoni offers plenty of things to do for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best activities:

  • Catfish Festival: Held every April, this annual festival celebrates the town’s most famous product – catfish. The festival features live music, delicious food, arts and crafts, a catfish cooking contest, and much more.

  • The Blues Trail: The Mississippi Delta is considered the birthplace of the blues, and Belzoni is a great place to start exploring this rich musical heritage. The Blues Trail features markers and sites related to the blues, including the Riverside Hotel, where legends such as B.B. King and Ike Turner stayed.

  • Farm Tours: Visitors can tour working farms and learn about the agricultural processes that take place in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Belzoni

No trip to Belzoni is complete without sampling some of its delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Catfish: Undoubtedly the town’s most famous dish, catfish is served in a variety of ways – fried, grilled, blackened, and more.

  • Fried Okra: A classic southern side dish, fried okra is a must-try when in Belzoni.

  • Sweet Potato Pie: This delicious dessert, made with sweet potatoes, is a southern staple.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A southern classic, sweet tea is the perfect beverage to complement any meal.

  • Craft Beer: Many local establishments offer a variety of craft beers, brewed both locally and regionally.

Where to Stay in Belzoni: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belzoni may be a small town, but it offers plenty of options for visitors seeking accommodation. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Belzoni Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers basic accommodations and is located in the heart of downtown Belzoni.

  • Humphreys County WMA Campground: Located in the wildlife management area, this campground offers a rustic experience for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Guest House at the Church Home: This historic home has been converted into a charming bed and breakfast that offers a unique and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Belzoni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belzoni offers plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home. Here are some ideas:

  • Catfish Breading Mix: Take home a taste of Belzoni with some catfish breading mix.

  • Blues Music Memorabilia: There are plenty of blues-related souvenirs available, from CDs to t-shirts.

Local Culture and Customs of Belzoni

Belzoni is a small town with a rich, fascinating history. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, which include:

  • Southern Hospitality: Southerners are known for their hospitality, so visitors can expect friendly and welcoming service.

  • Religious Traditions: Many locals attend church on Sundays, and visitors should respect this by refraining from loud noise during this time.

Best Time to Visit Belzoni

Belzoni can be visited year-round, but the most pleasant time to visit is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belzoni

To make the most of your visit to Belzoni, here are some things to do and not to do:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Explore Outdoor Adventures: Belzoni and the surrounding area offer a wealth of outdoor activities, so be sure to take advantage of them.

Don’t:

  • Be Impolite: Visitors should always be polite and courteous to locals.

  • Be Rude to Service Industry Workers: Belzoni is a small town, so it’s likely that visitors will encounter some of the same service industry workers more than once during their stay.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Belzoni should be aware of safety and health concerns:

Safety in Belzoni

Belzoni is a generally safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as not leaving valuables visible in cars and keeping car doors locked.

Health in Belzoni

Visitors should take care to protect themselves against ticks and mosquitoes, both of which can carry diseases.

Getting to Belzoni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Belzoni is located in the heart of the Mississippi Delta, making it reasonably accessible from many nearby destinations. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Belzoni

  • Greenwood-Leflore Airport: Located about 30 miles from Belzoni, this airport offers daily flights to Memphis, Tennessee.

  • Jackson-Medgar Wiley Evers International Airport: Located about 100 miles from Belzoni, this airport offers daily flights to several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Belzoni

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Greenwood, about 25 miles from Belzoni, this bus station offers daily service to several destinations.

Other Options

  • Driving: Belzoni is easily accessible by car, and many major highways pass through the town.

  • Railway: There is no railway station in Belzoni, but nearby Greenwood is served by Amtrak.

How to Get Around in Belzoni

Belzoni is a small town, and many of the best attractions can be reached on foot. However, visitors who wish to explore the surrounding area may wish to rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Belzoni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belzoni is an excellent base for exploring the Mississippi Delta region. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Greenwood: This historic town is located about 25 miles from Belzoni and boasts several attractions, including the Greenwood Blues Heritage Museum and the historic Alluvian Hotel.

  • Vicksburg: Located about 60 miles from Belzoni, Vicksburg is a fascinating historic town that played a significant role during the Civil War.

  • Clarksville: Located about 120 miles from Belzoni, Clarksville is known for its historic downtown area and scenic views.

Conclusion

Belzoni may be a small town, but it is packed full of things to see and do. From the cultural attractions to the outdoor pursuits and local cuisine, visitors to Belzoni are sure to have a memorable experience. So, why not plan your trip today and come and discover this charming Mississippi town for yourself?
