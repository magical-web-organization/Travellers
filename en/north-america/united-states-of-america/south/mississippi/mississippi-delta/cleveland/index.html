Introduction to Cleveland

Cleveland is a quaint town located in the Mississippi Delta, in the southern region of the United States. This charming town is home to many cultural attractions, natural wonders, and a delicious food scene. With its historical landmarks and excellent outdoor recreational activities, Cleveland is the perfect destination for travelers seeking adventure and tranquility.

You can reach Cleveland via air, bus, or car. The closest airport is the Mid-Delta Regional Airport, which is served by a few major airlines. If you prefer bus travel, you can take Greyhound and other regional bus services. If you plan to drive, Cleveland is conveniently located close to three major highways.

List of Places to Visit in Cleveland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Martin and Sue King Railroad Heritage Museum

The Martin and Sue King Railroad Heritage Museum displays a vast collection of railroad artifacts, and visitors can learn the rich history of the town’s railroad and how it influenced the area’s development.

GRAMMY Museum® Mississippi

The GRAMMY Museum® Mississippi showcases the historical and cultural impact of music in Mississippi and the region. With interactive exhibits, music performances, and educational programs, it is a must-visit destination for music lovers.

Natural Attractions of Cleveland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Delta State University Trails

The Delta State University Trails offers hiking and biking enthusiasts miles of breathtaking scenic views. It has several trails of varying difficulty, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging hikes through the rugged terrain.

Delta Wildlife Refuge

The Delta Wildlife Refuge is a 33,000-acre wildlife sanctuary, with walking trails and boating activities. Wildlife enthusiasts will enjoy seeing the variety of flora and fauna native to the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cleveland

Cleveland is an outdoor enthusiast’s dream, with various hiking and biking trails set amidst natural beauty.

Bear Pen Park

Bear Pen Park is a tranquil park, with a great system of hiking and biking trails. The park is surrounded by woodlands groves and boasts of a spring-fed pond, a perfect spot for relaxation.

McCarty’s Pottery

McCarty’s Pottery is a unique spot that combines history and art. A visit to this facility will provide guests with the opportunity to hike through the beautiful hardwood groves and wetlands, enjoy the scenic views, and learn how the artisans create their beautiful pottery creations.

Best Things to Do in Cleveland: Activities and Tours

The Bologna Performing Arts Center

The Bologna Performing Arts Center offers visitors a broad selection of performances ranging from dance to plays to music. This center provides an excellent opportunity to watch established artists and discover upcoming acts.

The Bayou

The Bayou is a fantastic tour where visitors can go on a guided boat excursion along the bayou, offering a chance to observe the natural habitats and wildlife in the area up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cleveland

Cleveland is a diverse town with plenty of attractions and activities to fill a week-long itinerary.

Day What to do
1 Arrive and settle in. Explore The Martin and Sue King Railroad Heritage Museum
2 Hike through the Delta State University Trails, then visit the GRAMMY Museum® Mississippi
3 Go on a guided boat tour of the bayou, followed by a visit to McCarty’s Pottery
4 Explore the Delta Wildlife Refuge, then relax at Bear Pen Park

Food and Drink Scene of Cleveland

Must-Try Dishes

The Airport Grocery’s Fried Chicken

The Airport Grocery serves some of the best fried chicken you will ever taste. This famous spot in town also serves sausage, biscuits, and gravy for breakfast.

The Downtown BBQ

The Downtown BBQ serves succulent pulled pork, smoked ribs, and brisket seasoned with Mississippi pecan wood for a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

Mississippi Beer and Home Brew Supplies

Mississippi Beer and Home Brew Supplies is a fantastic place to sample local beers or purchase some home brew supplies to take home.

Cathead Distillery

Cathead Distillery is another must-visit destination in Cleveland. It offers tours and tastings of their distilled spirits, including vodka, gin, and bourbon.

Where to Stay in Cleveland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Comfort Inn Cleveland

Comfort Inn Cleveland is an affordable and comfortable hotel located near several local attractions. It includes a free breakfast buffet, fitness center, and outdoor pool.

Hampton Inn Cleveland

Hampton Inn Cleveland offers a comfortable stay for visitors with its array of amenities, including free breakfast, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.

Cotton House Cleveland

Cotton House Cleveland is a modern boutique hotel located in the heart of the town’s historic district. It features stylized rooms, upscale dining venues, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Cleveland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

McCarty’s Pottery

McCarty’s Pottery produces beautiful pottery pieces that make excellent souvenirs, which showcase the town’s unique culture and history.

Cleveland Farmers Market

The Cleveland Farmers Market is the perfect place for purchasing locally grown produce, including fresh fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Cleveland

The town’s local culture is synonymous with soul and southern hospitality. Visitors can expect to be greeted with a warm welcome and a desire to share the town’s rich traditions and history with them.

Best Time to Visit Cleveland

The best time to visit Cleveland is during the fall or spring seasons. During this time, visitors can enjoy comfortable temperatures for exploring the town’s outdoor attractions, plus the added perk of the stunning fall foliage or beautiful spring blooms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cleveland

Do:

  • Embrace the relaxed pace of life and savor the town’s southern charm
  • Show respect to residents’ traditions and cultural beliefs
  • Take bug spray with you when hiking or exploring the natural attractions

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage anything while exploring the natural surroundings, as it’s against Mississippi law
  • Be disruptive late at night, as the town tends to be calm and quiet

Safety and Health

Safety in Cleveland

Cleveland is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is advisable to take precautions, like safeguarding your valuables and avoiding unknown, low-lit, and deserted areas late at night.

Health in Cleveland

Visitors to Cleveland should take precautions to protect themselves from mosquito bites, as the town is situated in a region with a high incidence rate of arboviral diseases. It is recommended to use repellents and dressing in long clothes.

How to Get Around in Cleveland

Getting around Cleveland is relatively easy, and walking is possible for those that prefer the slow. Taxis, rental cars, and bikes are also available for commuting.

Where to Go Next from Cleveland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dockery Farms, MS

Dockery Farms is a historic landmark and is believed to be the birthplace of blues music. It is located just a few miles from Cleveland.

Delta National Forest, MS

Delta National Forest, located approximately 50 miles away, is an exciting destination for visitors seeking adventure and exploring the South’s natural flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Cleveland, Mississippi, is a town steeped in traditions, culture, and history. This charming town will readily capture your heart with its soulful charm, natural beauty, and engaging activities and attractions. From delving into blues music’s origins to immersing yourself in the town’s culinary scene, you’re bound to find something to enjoy in Cleveland.
