Introduction to Mississippi Hills

Located in the state of Mississippi, in the southern United States of America, Mississippi Hills is a beautiful region filled with rich history, breathtaking natural landscapes, and a vibrant cultural heritage. This area is well-known for its rolling hills, lush green forests, and picturesque small towns that are home to friendly locals who are eager to welcome visitors.

In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about planning the perfect trip to Mississippi Hills. From the best places to visit and the top things to do, to where to stay and what to eat, we’ve got you covered. So, whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or just looking to relax and unwind, you’re sure to have an amazing time in Mississippi Hills.

List of Places to Visit in Mississippi Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mississippi Hills is home to a diverse array of museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the area’s rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Elvis Presley Birthplace & Museum: Located in Tupelo, Mississippi, this museum pays homage to one of the most iconic musicians of all time. Visitors can tour the humble home where Elvis was born, as well as explore the museum’s extensive collection of memorabilia and exhibits.
  • Vicksburg National Military Park: This historic site commemorates one of the most significant battles of the American Civil War. Visitors can take a self-guided tour of the park, which includes monuments, exhibits, and a restored Union gunboat.
  • Natchez Trace Parkway: This 444-mile scenic drive passes through three states, including Mississippi, and offers stunning views of the area’s natural beauty and historic landmarks.

Natural Attractions of Mississippi Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mississippi Hills is home to some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in the country. Whether you enjoy hiking, fishing, or just taking in beautiful views, there’s something for everyone in this area. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Trace State Park: This state park is located in Pontotoc County and boasts over 35 miles of hiking trails, as well as a beautiful lake that’s perfect for fishing and boating.
  • Tishomingo State Park: This park features rugged rock formations, lush forests, and peaceful streams. Visitors can enjoy hiking, rock climbing, and swimming in the park’s natural pools.
  • Grenada Lake: This picturesque lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and water sports. With over 35,000 acres of water and 148 miles of shoreline, there’s plenty of space to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mississippi Hills

Mississippi Hills is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, there’s a trail that’s perfect for you. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Tanglefoot Trail: This 43-mile trail runs through the heart of Mississippi Hills and offers stunning views of the area’s natural beauty. Whether you’re hiking, biking, or even horseback riding, this trail is a must-visit.
  • Jeff Busby Park: Located along the Natchez Trace Parkway, this park features several hiking trails of varying lengths and difficulty levels. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the park’s rolling hills and forests.
  • Chickasaw Trail: This 6-mile trail is located in Tishomingo State Park and offers a challenging hike through rugged terrain. The trail features stunning views of Bear Creek Canyon and the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Mississippi Hills: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural beauty and cultural attractions, Mississippi Hills offers a wide range of activities and tours that are sure to appeal to visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Delta Blues Tour: This tour takes visitors on a journey through the area’s rich musical heritage, with stops at iconic venues like Ground Zero Blues Club and the B.B. King Museum.
  • Tupelo Buffalo Park and Zoo: This family-friendly attraction features over 260 acres of wildlife exhibits, including bison, zebras, and giraffes.
  • Antique Shopping: Mississippi Hills is home to several charming small towns that offer a wide selection of antique shops and boutiques. From unique vintage finds to one-of-a-kind handmade crafts, there’s something for everyone.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mississippi Hills

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Mississippi Hills, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore Tupelo, including the Elvis Presley Birthplace and Museum, Tupelo Buffalo Park and Zoo, and historic downtown Tupelo.
  • Day 2: Visit the Natchez Trace Parkway and stop at sites like Jeff Busby Park, Mount Locust Inn and Plantation, and the Mississippi Craft Center.
  • Day 3: Spend the day in Vicksburg, exploring the Vicksburg National Military Park and learning about the city’s fascinating history.
  • Day 4: Head to Tishomingo State Park for hiking, rock climbing, and swimming in the park’s natural pools.
  • Day 5: Visit Grenada Lake and enjoy fishing, boating, and water sports.
  • Day 6: Take a Delta Blues Tour and learn about the area’s rich musical heritage.
  • Day 7: Spend the day exploring one of the charming small towns in the area, like Oxford or Water Valley, and shop for unique souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Mississippi Hills

Must-Try Dishes

Mississippi Hills is known for its delicious Southern cuisine, including dishes like:

  • Fried Catfish: A local favorite, fried catfish is often served with hushpuppies and coleslaw.
  • Barbecue: Mississippi Hills is home to several top-notch barbecue joints, including Abe’s Bar-B-Q and The Rib Shack.
  • Pecan Pie: No trip to Mississippi Hills is complete without trying a slice of this classic Southern dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: This classic Southern beverage is a staple in Mississippi Hills, and can be found at most restaurants and cafes.
  • Craft Beer: Mississippi Hills is home to several local breweries, including Yalobusha Brewing Company and Lucky Town Brewing Company.
  • Mint Julep: This refreshing cocktail is a Southern classic and can be found at many bars and restaurants in the area.

Where to Stay in Mississippi Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a charming guesthouse, Mississippi Hills has a wide variety of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some recommendations:

  • Graduate Oxford: This stylish hotel is located in the heart of Oxford, Mississippi, and offers stylish rooms and excellent amenities.
  • The Alluvian Hotel: Located in Greenwood, Mississippi, this luxurious hotel features plush rooms, a full-service spa, and an award-winning restaurant.
  • The Nests B&B: This charming bed and breakfast is located in Water Valley, Mississippi, and offers cozy rooms and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Mississippi Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Mississippi Hills, here are some ideas:

  • Blues CDs and Vinyl: Mississippi Hills is home to some of the most talented blues musicians in the world, and you can find their music in local record stores and music shops.
  • Pottery: The area is known for its traditional pottery, with several local artists creating beautiful and unique pieces.
  • Hot Sauce: Mississippi Hills boasts several hot sauce makers, including Steen’s Syrup and Chef Ron’s Gumbo Stop.

Local Culture and Customs of Mississippi Hills

Mississippi Hills is a friendly and welcoming area, with a strong sense of community and tradition. Visitors can expect to experience Southern hospitality at its best, with locals eager to share their culture and history with others. Some cultural norms include:

  • Saying “ma’am” and “sir” as a sign of respect.
  • Embracing a laid-back, leisurely pace of life.
  • Enjoying delicious Southern cuisine and music.

Best Time to Visit Mississippi Hills

The best time to visit Mississippi Hills is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. However, the area can be visited year-round, with activities and attractions available in all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mississippi Hills

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including fried catfish, barbecue, and pecan pie.
  • Do explore the area’s natural beauty, including state parks, hiking trails, and scenic drives.
  • Do attend a live music performance, whether it’s at a small bar or a large music festival.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the importance of Southern hospitality and politeness.
  • Don’t forget to bring insect repellent when visiting outdoor attractions.
  • Don’t ignore the area’s rich history, including its Civil War and blues music heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mississippi Hills

Mississippi Hills is a safe and welcoming area, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or exploring outdoor areas.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and locked up when not in use.
  • Use caution when driving on rural roads, especially at night.

Health in Mississippi Hills

Visitors to Mississippi Hills should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling. They should also take precautions to avoid tick bites and protect themselves from the sun and heat. It’s important to stay hydrated when spending time outdoors, especially during the warmer months.

Getting to Mississippi Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mississippi Hills

  • Memphis International Airport

Bus Stations Near Mississippi Hills

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Tupelo, Mississippi

Other Options

  • Driving: Mississippi Hills is located just off of Interstate 55, making it easily accessible by car.
  • Amtrak: The city of New Albany, Mississippi is served by the Amtrak station.

How to Get Around in Mississippi Hills

The best way to get around Mississippi Hills is by car, as many attractions and points of interest are spread out and not easily accessible by public transportation. However, there are several car rental companies in the area, as well as taxi and shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Mississippi Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the South, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Memphis, Tennessee: The home of Elvis Presley and the birthplace of rock and roll, Memphis is a must-visit for music lovers.
  • New Orleans, Louisiana: Famous for its vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, and iconic music scene, New Orleans is a unique and exciting destination.
  • Birmingham, Alabama: This lively city is known for its rich history, thriving arts scene, and delicious Southern cuisine.

Conclusion

Mississippi Hills is a truly special destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking to explore the area’s rich history, relax in nature, or immerse yourself in Southern culture, you’re sure to have an amazing time in Mississippi Hills.
