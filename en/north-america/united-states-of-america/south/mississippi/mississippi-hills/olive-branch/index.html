Introduction to Olive Branch

Nestled in the Mississippi Hills, Olive Branch is a charming city in Mississippi, Southern United States. Olive Branch is a popular tourist destination, thanks to its excellent cultural and natural attractions, historical significance, and exciting outdoor adventures. The city is perfect for travelers seeking a relaxing escape from their busy routines. They can learn about the local culture, try the delicious food and beverages, and enjoy scenic views. Suppose you are planning to visit Olive Branch, Mississippi. In that case, this comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information and tips to make your journey comfortable and memorable.

List of Places to Visit in Olive Branch

Suppose you are planning to visit Olive Branch, Mississippi. In that case, here is a list of the top places you must see:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • DeSoto County Museum – This museum features exhibits about the history of DeSoto County, including artifacts, photographs, and interactive displays.
  • Hernando de Soto Historical Museum – Learn about the life and times of the Spanish explorer Hernando de Soto, who discovered the Mississippi River and founded many settlements in the area.
  • Chickasaw Trails Marker – This historical marker is dedicated to the Chickasaw Indians who were the first inhabitants of Olive Branch.

Natural Attractions of Olive Branch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Midsouth Ice House – Midsouth offers ice skating, curling, and hockey, as well as a roller-skating rink.
  • Olive Branch City Park – This large park offers picnic areas, a playground, fishing areas, and great views of the nearby lake.
  • White Hill Nature Park – White Hill features walking trails, a butterfly garden, and a beautiful overlook.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Olive Branch

Olive Branch is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Olive Branch City Park Trail – This five-mile loop is perfect for an easy hike or bike ride. The trail features beautiful views of the lake and passes through woods and meadows.
  • Thunderbird Trail at South Germantown Preserve – This seven-mile trail winds through forests and creeks, and has several scenic spots perfect for picnicking.
  • Tour de Olive Branch – This annual bike race features several routes through Olive Branch and is perfect for experienced bikers.

Best Things to Do in Olive Branch: Activities and Tours

Suppose you are looking for some exciting things to do in Olive Branch, Mississippi. In that case, here are some recommendations:

  • Take a tour of the Strawberry Plains Audubon Center – The center offers several tours, including bird watching, butterfly walks, and nature hikes.
  • Try the Memphis Barbecue Company – This restaurant has the best barbecue in town, according to locals and tourists alike.
  • Attend a concert at the BankPlus Amphitheater at Snowden Grove – The amphitheater hosts concerts and events throughout the year, with big-name performers like Tim McGraw, Lady Antebellum, and Luke Bryan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Olive Branch

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip to Olive Branch:

Day Itinerary
1 Visit the DeSoto County Museum and enjoy a picnic at Olive Branch City Park
2 Take a bike ride on the Olive Branch City Park Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Olive Branch

No trip to Olive Branch is complete without trying the local food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue – Olive Branch is famous for its Southern-style barbecue. Visit Memphis Barbecue Company and experience the authentic taste of Mississippi.
  • Hot Tamales – These spicy cornmeal pockets, stuffed with meat and cheese, are a staple of Mississippi cuisine. Try the Delta-style tamales at the convenient Tamale Shak.
  • Fried Chicken – No Southern meal is complete without fried chicken! Shrimp Basket, just a few minutes away in Memphis; has especially excellent fried chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - No visit to Mississippi is complete without a glass of sweet tea. The best place to try it is Uncle Lou’s Fried Chicken, a local favorite.
  • Southern Comfort – This popular whiskey originated in Mississippi. You can try it at The Piping Pig, which offers a variety of classic and creative cocktails.

Where to Stay in Olive Branch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Olive Branch has several excellent accommodation options, including:

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott – Located near Olive Branch City Park, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a beautiful pool.
  • Magnolia Inn and Suites – This hotel is great for budget-friendly stays and enjoys a central location near many Olive Branch attractions.
  • Best Western Plus Olive Branch Hotel & Suites – This hotel offers a wide range of amenities, including a fitness center, hot breakfast, and high-speed Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Olive Branch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Suppose you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Olive Branch, Mississippi. In that case, here are a few great options:

  • Mississippi-made Wine – Pick up a bottle or two of wines made right here in Mississippi. Visit the Whiskey Walk or Wits End to sample and purchase!
  • Pottery and Crafts – The DeSoto Art Council Gift Shop features an array of pottery, paintings, and other handmade crafts, all created by local artists.
  • Olive Branch Merchandise – Visit the Olive Branch Chamber of Commerce to check out some merchandise featuring the city’s logo and landmarks such as Station Square and Olive Branch City Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Olive Branch

The people of Olive Branch are known for their Southern hospitality and friendly manner. Respect for elders and traditions is essential.

Best Time to Visit Olive Branch

The best time to visit Olive Branch, Mississippi, is during the spring season. The weather is mild, and the city’s parks and nature preserves come alive with blooming flowers. Fall is also a great time to visit Olive Branch, as the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Olive Branch

Do:

  • Be respectful of locals and their traditions.
  • Take a stroll through the Downtown area and visit the local boutiques and shops.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty and take in the sights around town.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Touch or remove any artifacts or historical objects.
  • Use foul or abusive language in public.

Safety and Health

Olive Branch is a safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take necessary safety precautions while traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Olive Branch

  • Always lock your car doors and hotel room.
  • Be aware of your surroundings during the night hours.
  • Check for any weather alerts before planning any outdoor activities.

Health in Olive Branch

  • Stay hydrated during your visit to Olive Branch, especially on warmer days.
  • Use sunscreen and wear a hat while enjoying the outdoors.
  • Wash your hands frequently and carry your hand sanitizer during your visit to Olive Branch.

Getting to Olive Branch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Olive Branch

The closest airport to Olive Branch is the Memphis International Airport, which is about 20 minutes away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Olive Branch

Greyhound Bus Lines, Megabus, and Trailways Bus Lines offer service throughout the United States and have stops in nearby Memphis.

Other Options

Car rental services are available at the Memphis airport, which will take you directly to Olive Branch, Mississippi.

How to Get Around in Olive Branch

Olive Branch is small enough that walking, biking, or using a car rental are suitable options for getting around the city.

Where to Go Next from Olive Branch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Memphis – Home to Elvis Presley’s Graceland and the famous Beale Street, Memphis is just a short drive from Olive Branch. It is one of the most prominent cities in the southern United States.
  • Sardis Lake – Located just 30 minutes from Olive Branch, Mississippi. Sardis Lake offers boating, camping, fishing, and hiking opportunities.
  • Tunica – Tunica is a popular gambling destination located near the Mississippi River that boasts several casinos, including Gold Strike Casino and Horseshoe Casino.

Conclusion

Olive Branch, Mississippi, is a hidden treasure waiting to be discovered. With its breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique cuisine, Olive Branch has something for everyone. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or simply looking for some relaxation, Olive Branch is a great alternative to the tourist crowds of larger Southern cities. Book your visit today and immerse yourself in the charm of Olive Branch, Mississippi.
