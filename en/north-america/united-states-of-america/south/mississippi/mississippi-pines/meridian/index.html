Introduction to Meridian

Meridian, located in Mississippi Pines in Mississippi, is a charming city filled with southern hospitality, culture, and history. It’s the sixth largest city in Mississippi with a population of approximately 38,000. Meridian, once a thriving railroad town, has transformed into a hub of arts, commerce, and tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Meridian

Meridian is filled with cultural and natural attractions worth visiting. Here are some of the places you should include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Meridian Museum of Art

The Meridian Museum of Art is a visual arts museum with an extensive collection of 19th and 20th-century American art. The museum also features an outdoor sculpture garden.

2. Riley Center

The Riley Center is a historic performing arts venue located in a beautifully restored 1889 opera house. The center hosts a variety of performances throughout the year.

3. Mississippi Industrial Heritage Museum

The Mississippi Industrial Heritage Museum showcases the city’s industrial history, including exhibits on railroads, timber, and manufacturing.

4. Meridian’s historic district

A walk through downtown Meridian’s historic district is essential to understanding the city’s past. Highlights of the district include the Threefoot Building, Meridian City Hall, and the Meridian Railroad Museum.

Natural Attractions of Meridian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Bonita Lakes

The Bonita Lakes are a must-visit for those looking for outdoor activities such as fishing, hiking, and picnicking. The lakes also have a beach area for swimming.

2. Highland Park

Highland Park is a beautiful space to relax and enjoy scenic views of Meridian. The park features walking trails and a disc golf course.

3. Dunn’s Falls

Dunn’s Falls is a picturesque waterfall located just outside of Meridian. Visitors can swim in the waters below the falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meridian

Meridian has a variety of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

1. Bonita Lakes Loop

The Bonita Lakes Loop is a 7-mile trail that circles the Bonita Lakes and offers beautiful views of the lakes and surrounding forests.

2. Long Creek Nature Preserve

The Long Creek Nature Preserve has several trails with varying difficulty levels, making it perfect for all types of hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Meridian: Activities and Tours

1. Meridian Symphony Orchestra

The Meridian Symphony Orchestra is a must-see for music enthusiasts. The orchestra performs a variety of classical and contemporary pieces throughout the year.

2. Meridian Farmers’ Market

For a taste of the local flavor, visit the Meridian Farmers’ Market. The market is open on Saturdays and offers fresh produce, baked goods, and crafts.

3. Meridian Little Theatre

The Meridian Little Theatre features a variety of local productions throughout the year, including plays and musicals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Meridian

If you’re planning to stay in Meridian for a week, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Meridian Museum of Art and the Mississippi Industrial Heritage Museum
Day 2 Explore downtown Meridian’s historic district
Day 3 Hike the Bonita Lakes Loop
Day 4 Relax at Highland Park
Day 5 Visit Dunn’s Falls and swim in the waters below
Day 6 Attend a performance at the Riley Center
Day 7 Shop at the Meridian Farmers’ Market and see a local production at the theatre

Food and Drink Scene of Meridian

Meridian’s food and drink scene is a blend of southern comfort food and local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Fried Chicken and Cornbread

Fried chicken and cornbread are staples of southern cuisine, and you can find them at many restaurants in Meridian.

2. Mississippi Catfish

Mississippi catfish is a local delicacy and can be found at many restaurants around the city.

Local Beverages

1. Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a southern classic and a perfect complement to any meal.

2. Beer

Meridian has several breweries, including Meridian Underground Brewery and Mayhew Junction Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Meridian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Meridian has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some options:

1. Hilton Garden Inn Meridian

The Hilton Garden Inn Meridian is a modern hotel in downtown Meridian and is within walking distance of many attractions.

2. Super 8 by Wyndham Meridian

Super 8 by Wyndham Meridian is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location.

3. Meridian RV Resort

For those traveling with an RV, the Meridian RV Resort is a great option. The resort has all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Meridian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Meridian has several unique souvenir options. Here are some items to consider taking home:

1. Bling Dog Collars

Bling Dog Collars are a popular souvenir item in Meridian. They are handmade collars adorned with jewels and rhinestones.

2. Elvis Presley Merchandise

Meridian is the birthplace of Elvis Presley, and you can find Elvis merchandise at many shops and boutiques around the city.

3. Local Artwork

Meridian has a thriving arts community, and you can find locally made artwork at the Meridian Museum of Art and other galleries around the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Meridian

Meridian’s southern hospitality is evident in its culture and customs. It’s common to greet strangers with a smile and strike up a conversation. Additionally, manners are taken seriously in Meridian, and it’s essential to use “sir” and “ma’am” when addressing others.

Best Time to Visit Meridian

The best time to visit Meridian is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meridian

Do:

  • Use “sir” and “ma’am” when addressing others
  • Smile and engage in conversation with locals
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to others
  • Forget your manners
  • Leave without exploring the city’s arts and culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Meridian

Meridian is a safe city, but like any other city, it’s essential to take necessary precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas

Health in Meridian

Meridian has several hospitals and medical clinics. However, it’s essential to have adequate travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Conclusion

Meridian is a charming city filled with southern hospitality, culture, and history. It’s a must-visit for anyone wanting to experience the best of Mississippi. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, Meridian has something for everyone.
