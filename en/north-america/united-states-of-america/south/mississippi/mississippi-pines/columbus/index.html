Introduction to Columbus

Located in the Mississippi Pines of Mississippi, Columbus is a charming city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and Southern hospitality. With its wide range of attractions and activities, Columbus has something to offer everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, you won’t want to miss the many unique experiences this city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Columbus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tennessee Williams Welcome Center - Dedicated to the famous playwright Tennessee Williams, this museum offers an in-depth look into his life and accomplishments.
  • Columbus Air Force Base Museum - Highlights the history of the Columbus Air Force Base and features a collection of aircraft and memorabilia.
  • Starkville Civil Rights Walking Trail - A self-guided walking tour that explores the history of the civil rights movement in Columbus and its impact on the community.
  • Rosenzweig Arts Center - A hub for the arts in Columbus, this historic building hosts events, exhibitions, and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Columbus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tombigbee River - A popular spot for fishing, boating, and kayaking.
  • Columbus Riverwalk - A scenic trail that runs alongside the Tombigbee River, perfect for a relaxed walk or bike ride.
  • Waverly Mansion - A well-preserved antebellum mansion that offers tours of the grounds and gardens.
  • Plymouth Bluff Environmental Center - A nature preserve that features miles of hiking trails and stunning views of the Tombigbee River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbus

  • The Tornado Alley Trail - A popular cycling route that showcases the city’s historic downtown, scenic neighborhoods, and beautiful natural surroundings.
  • The Riverwalk - A flat, paved trail that is perfect for a scenic stroll or bike ride.
  • The Waverly Loop Trail - A 2.5-mile hiking trail that winds through the scenic Waverly Plantation and features beautiful views of the nearby woods and gardens.

Best Things to Do in Columbus: Activities and Tours

  • Columbus Ghost and Legend Tour - This guided tour takes visitors on a spooky journey through some of Columbus’ most haunted sites.
  • Columbus Pilgrimage Tour - This annual event takes place in the spring and showcases the city’s historic homes and landmarks.
  • Columbus Road Trip - A self-guided driving tour that explores the city’s architecture, culture, and history.
  • Columbus Second Saturdays - A monthly event that features live music, food, and vendors in downtown Columbus.

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbus

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Tennessee Williams Welcome Center in the morning, followed by a picnic lunch at one of the city’s parks. In the afternoon, explore the history of the Columbus Air Force Base Museum.
Day 2 Spend the day outdoors at the Waverly Mansion and Plymouth Bluff Environmental Center, taking in the stunning natural scenery.
Day 3 Take a ghost tour of Columbus in the evening and explore the city’s historic downtown during the day.
Day 4 Take a day trip to nearby Starkville to explore the civil rights history of the city.
Day 5 Take a road trip around Columbus and explore its architecture, culture, and history.
Day 6 Experience the local food and drink scene, trying out different dishes and beverages.
Day 7 Spend your last day in Columbus at the Riverwalk, taking a leisurely stroll or bike ride along the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Columbus

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mississippi Mud Pie - A rich, chocolatey dessert that originated in Mississippi and is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.
  • Catfish - A Southern staple, catfish is a must-try in Columbus.
  • Grits - A traditional Southern dish that is often served for breakfast or as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A Southern classic, sweet tea is a refreshing beverage that is perfect for a warm day.
  • Muscadine Wine - Made from a type of grape that grows in the South, muscadine wine is a local favorite that pairs well with many of the dishes found in Columbus.

Where to Stay in Columbus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Columbus Inn and Suites - A budget-friendly option located in the heart of downtown Columbus.
  • The Immaculate Conception Guest House - A charming bed and breakfast located in a historic building in downtown Columbus.
  • Courtyard by Marriott - A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, located near the Columbus Air Force Base.

What to Buy in Columbus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mississippi-made Crafts - From pottery to textiles, there are many locally-made crafts to choose from in Columbus.
  • Sweet Treats - Stock up on some Mississippi Mud Pie or other locally-made sweets to bring home as a treat.
  • Muscadine Wine - Another local favorite that makes for a great gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbus

The people of Columbus are known for their warmth and hospitality. Southern hospitality is important in this city, and visitors can expect to be treated like family. The city is also known for its rich history, and many residents feel a deep connection to their heritage and the customs of their ancestors.

Best Time to Visit Columbus

The best time to visit Columbus is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the city is full of activity. The annual Columbus Pilgrimage Tour takes place in the spring, and visitors can expect to see many historic homes decorated for the occasion. The fall is an ideal time to explore the city’s outdoor attractions, like the Riverwalk and Plymouth Bluff Environmental Center.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbus

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the city’s rich history and culture.
  • Do try some of the local food and drink, particularly Mississippi Mud Pie and muscadine wine.
  • Do take advantage of the city’s outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for exploring the city’s many outdoor attractions.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with the friendly people of Columbus.
  • Don’t miss out on the chance to attend the annual Columbus Pilgrimage Tour.

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbus

Columbus is generally a safe city to visit, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. It is recommended that visitors stay in well-lit, populated areas, especially when traveling alone at night. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Columbus

Visitors to Columbus should take the same precautions that they would when traveling anywhere else in the United States. Tap water is safe to drink, but visitors should still be cautious about where they eat and drink. It is recommended that visitors have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling to the area.

Getting to Columbus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Columbus

  • Golden Triangle Regional Airport - Located just 15 minutes from downtown Columbus, this airport offers several daily flights to major cities in the region.

Bus Stations Near Columbus

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in the heart of downtown Columbus, this station offers daily bus service to major cities throughout the South.

Other Options

  • Driving - Columbus is easily accessible from the major highways in the region and is approximately 2.5 hours from Jackson, MS and Birmingham, AL.

How to Get Around in Columbus

While public transportation is limited in Columbus, the city is easy to navigate by car, bike, or on foot. Visitors can also take advantage of the many walking tours and guided tours offered throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Columbus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tupelo, MS - Home of Elvis Presley, Tupelo is a must-visit destination for music lovers.
  • Birmingham, AL - Located just two hours from Columbus, Birmingham is a vibrant city with a rich history and cultural scene.
  • Memphis, TN - Located just three hours from Columbus, Memphis offers visitors a chance to experience the birthplace of blues and rock and roll.

Conclusion

Columbus, Mississippi is a city with a rich history, natural beauty, and Southern hospitality that is hard to resist. With its many attractions and activities, visitors to this city will have no shortage of things to do and see. Whether you’re exploring the city’s historic downtown, taking in the stunning natural scenery, or indulging in the local food and drink scene, there is something for everyone in Columbus, Mississippi.
