Introduction to Ridgeland

Located in the heart of Mississippi, Ridgeland is a charming city that is often referred to as the “best-kept secret” of the state. It is a vibrant and welcoming community with a rich history and culture, beautiful natural scenery, and a variety of outdoor activities.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Ridgeland has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Ridgeland, Mississippi.

List of Places to Visit in Ridgeland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ridgeland is a city that is deeply rooted in history, and visitors can explore several historically significant museums and buildings. The following are some of the top cultural attractions in Ridgeland:

  • Old Trace Park - This beautiful park is located on the historic Natchez Trace Parkway and is home to a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Ridgeland City Hall - This historic building is one of the oldest in the city and offers tours to visitors interested in local history.
  • Mississippi Crafts Center - This center showcases the work of Mississippi’s most talented artisans and designers.
  • Natchez Trace Parkway Visitor Center - This visitor center is the perfect place to start your journey on the historic Natchez Trace Parkway.

Natural Attractions of Ridgeland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ridgeland is known for its stunning natural beauty and is home to a number of parks, lakes, and nature trails. The following are some of the top natural attractions in Ridgeland:

  • Ross Barnett Reservoir - This beautiful lake offers a variety of water sports, including fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • Ridgeland Multi-Use Trail - This scenic trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and jogging and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • LeFleur’s Bluff State Park - This park is home to a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking trails, fishing, and boating.
  • Natchez Trace Parkway - This historic Parkway stretches 444 miles and offers breathtaking views of Mississippi’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ridgeland

Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or just starting, Ridgeland has a variety of trails for all skill levels. Here are some of the top hiking and biking trails in Ridgeland:

  • Ridgeland Multi-Use Trail - This 14-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and jogging and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Northpark Trail - This 2.1-mile paved trail is perfect for biking or hiking and offers plenty of shade and scenic views.
  • LeFleur’s Bluff State Park Trails - This state park has over 4 miles of hiking trails ranging in difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Ridgeland: Activities and Tours

Ridgeland has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained during their stay. Here are some of the top things to do in Ridgeland:

  • Visit Renaissance at Colony Park - This outdoor shopping center is home to a variety of upscale retailers and restaurants.
  • Attend a Local Festival - Ridgeland hosts a number of festivals throughout the year, including the Ridgeland Fine Arts Festival and the Mississippi Craft Show.
  • Take a Cooking Class - Ridgeland is known for its delicious cuisine, and visitors can learn to make traditional southern dishes by taking a cooking class.
  • Explore the Reservoir - The Ross Barnett Reservoir is a popular destination for water sports, including fishing, boating, and swimming.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ridgeland

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ridgeland, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your stay:

Day 1: Arrival and Relaxation

  • Check into your hotel or guesthouse and take a stroll through Old Trace Park.
  • Enjoy dinner at one of Ridgeland’s many restaurants.

Day 2: Historic Sites

  • Start the day by visiting Ridgeland City Hall and taking a tour of the historic building.
  • Visit the Mississippi Crafts Center and explore the work of Mississippi’s most talented artisans and designers.
  • End the day by visiting the Natchez Trace Parkway Visitor Center.

Day 3: Outdoor Activities

  • Spend the morning biking or hiking on the Ridgeland Multi-Use Trail.
  • Enjoy lunch at one of the many lakeside restaurants.
  • Spend the afternoon kayaking or fishing on the Ross Barnett Reservoir.

Day 4: LeFleur’s Bluff State Park

  • Spend the day at LeFleur’s Bluff State Park, exploring hiking trails and enjoying a picnic lunch.
  • End the day by attending a yoga class at the park.

Day 5: Renaissance at Colony Park

  • Spend the day shopping and dining at the upscale outdoor shopping center, Renaissance at Colony Park.
  • Enjoy dinner at one of the many restaurants, featuring a variety of cuisines.

Day 6: Cooking Class

  • Spend the day learning to make traditional southern dishes at a cooking class.
  • Join a local food tour and try Ridgeland’s best eats.

Day 7: Relaxation and Departure

  • Spend the morning enjoying the scenery at the Ross Barnett Reservoir.
  • Check out of your hotel or guesthouse and depart from Ridgeland.

Food and Drink Scene of Ridgeland

Ridgeland is known for its delicious southern cuisine and craft beers. Visitors can sample the best of Mississippi’s food and drink scene at the following establishments:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken - A southern classic, fried chicken is a must-try dish when visiting Ridgeland.
  • Biscuits and Gravy - A perfect breakfast meal, this southern favorite will leave you full and satisfied.
  • BBQ - Mississippi is known for its delicious BBQ, and Ridgeland has a number of places to try this southern specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A staple of the south, sweet tea is the perfect refreshing drink for a hot day in Ridgeland.
  • Craft Beer - Ridgeland boasts a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries offering a variety of unique and flavorful brews.
  • Soda Fountain Favorites - For a classic taste of Mississippi, visitors can try a variety of soda fountain favorites at local diners and soda fountains.

Where to Stay in Ridgeland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ridgeland offers a variety of lodging options to suit any budget or taste. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Ridgeland has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to stay in Ridgeland:

  • Hyatt Place Ridgeland - This modern hotel is located in the heart of Ridgeland and offers spacious rooms and a variety of amenities.

What to Buy in Ridgeland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Ridgeland can take home a piece of Mississippi’s culture and heritage with the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Locally Made Crafts - Mississippi Crafts Center features work from Mississippi’s most talented artisans and designers.
  • BBQ Sauces and Spices - Ridgeland boasts a thriving BBQ scene, and visitors can bring home a taste of Mississippi with locally made sauces and spices.
  • Handmade Soaps - Mississippi is home to several soapmakers, and visitors can take home a variety of unique and natural handcrafted soaps.

Local Culture and Customs of Ridgeland

Ridgeland, like much of the south, is known for its hospitality and friendly culture. Visitors can expect a warm welcome and friendly conversation wherever they go in Ridgeland.

Best Time to Visit Ridgeland

The best time to visit Ridgeland is during the fall and spring months when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are at their peak. Summertime can get quite hot and humid, while winter can bring cold temperatures and unpredictable weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ridgeland

Do:

  • Expect to be greeted with a smile and friendly southern hospitality.
  • Try the local cuisine, including fried chicken and BBQ.
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities like hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the need for sunscreen, even on overcast days.
  • Be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals.
  • Miss a chance to attend a local festival or fair.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ridgeland

Ridgeland is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still exercise caution while exploring the city. Here are some safety tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid poorly lit areas at night.
  • Keep your valuables secured and out of sight.
  • Use caution when walking or biking on busy roads.

Health in Ridgeland

Visitors to Ridgeland should take precautions to protect their health, including:

  • Staying hydrated and drinking plenty of water.
  • Wearing sunscreen and staying in the shade during the hottest parts of the day.
  • Using insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Ridgeland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ridgeland is easily accessible by car or public transportation. The following are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Ridgeland

  • Jackson-Medgar Wiley Evers International Airport - Located approximately 20 minutes from Ridgeland, this airport serves domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Ridgeland

Where to Go Next from Ridgeland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ridgeland is located in the heart of Mississippi and is a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some of the top excursions from Ridgeland:

  • Jackson - The capital city of Mississippi is filled with history and culture and is located approximately 20 minutes from Ridgeland.
  • Vicksburg - This historic city is located approximately 45 minutes from Ridgeland and is known for its civil war history and beautiful views of the Mississippi River.
