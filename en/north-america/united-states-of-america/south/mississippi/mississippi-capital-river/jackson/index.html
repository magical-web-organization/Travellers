Introduction to Jackson

Jackson, the capital city of Mississippi, is a destination worth exploring. The city is known for its rich history, natural beauty, museums, and a thriving food and drink scene. The city is located in the South region of the United States, offering visitors an excellent opportunity to immerse themselves in Southern culture and hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Jackson

If you are planning a trip to Jackson, don’t miss these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mississippi Civil Rights Museum: This museum chronicles the struggle for civil rights in Mississippi and the United States through various exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Eudora Welty House and Garden: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of Pulitzer Prize-winning author Eudora Welty and her contributions to American literature.
  • Old Capitol Museum: The museum is housed in a National Historic Landmark and was the former state capitol building, offering visitors a glimpse into the political history of Mississippi.

Natural Attractions of Jackson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Natchez Trace Parkway: A scenic drive through the forested hills of Mississippi with access to hiking and biking trails and historic landmarks.
  • LeFleur’s Bluff State Park: The park offers visitors various outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, picnicking, and camping in a beautiful natural setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jackson

Jackson boasts several hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Ridgeland Multi-Use Trail System: A 14-mile trail system with interconnected loops suitable for hiking and mountain biking.
  • LeFleur’s Bluff State Park Trail: A 10-mile trail system offers scenic views of the Pearl River and access to the Mississippi Museum of Natural Science.

Best Things to Do in Jackson: Activities and Tours

  • Capital City Kayak Adventures: Paddle along the Pearl River for a unique experience and a different perspective of Jackson.
  • Mississippi Food Tours: An opportunity to explore the city’s food culture and taste traditional Southern dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jackson

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your week-long trip to Jackson:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Mississippi Civil Rights Museum and Old Capitol Museum
2 Go on a nature hike or bike ride at LeFleur’s Bluff State Park
3 Explore the Eudora Welty House and Garden and Mississippi Museum of Natural Science
4 Visit the Smith Robertson Museum and Cultural Center and the Manship House Museum
5 Take a guided tour of downtown Jackson, including the Governor’s Mansion and Mississippi State Capitol
6 Indulge in the city’s food scene by going on a food tour or visiting a local restaurant
7 Take a relaxing paddle down the Pearl River with Capital City Kayak Adventures

Food and Drink Scene of Jackson

Jackson’s food and drink scene is a mix of traditional Southern cuisine and contemporary gastronomy. Don’t miss these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: A traditional Southern dish often served with sides such as hushpuppies, coleslaw, and potato salad.
  • BBQ: Mississippi-style BBQ features pulled pork or beef with a tangy tomato-based sauce.
  • Pecan Pie: A classic Southern dessert made with pecans, brown sugar, and corn syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern favorite, sweet tea is made with black tea and sweetened with sugar or simple syrup.
  • Mississippi Mule: A twist on the classic Moscow Mule, made with Southern-style ginger beer.

Where to Stay in Jackson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jackson has various accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are a few recommendations:

  • The King Edward Hotel: A historic hotel in downtown Jackson that has been restored and converted into a luxury boutique hotel.
  • The Westin Jackson: A modern hotel located in the heart of downtown Jackson with an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • HI Jackson: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Jackson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave the city without taking home some souvenirs or local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Mississippi Crafts Center: A must-visit for handmade traditional crafts such as pottery, quilts, and baskets.
  • The Mississippi Gift Company: Offering a selection of Mississippi-made products such as jellies, snacks, and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Jackson

Mississippi’s culture is deeply rooted in music, art, food, and history. The state is known for blues music, literary greats, and traditional Southern cuisine. Visitors should immerse themselves in the local culture while showing respect for customs, beliefs, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Jackson

The best time to visit Jackson is in the spring and fall when the weather is pleasant, and the city hosts various festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jackson

Do:

  • Explore the city’s museums and historical sites.
  • Embrace the local culture and hospitality.
  • Try traditional Southern dishes.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs.
  • Ignore safety precautions, especially when visiting natural attractions such as parks and trails.
  • Overlook the city’s rich literary history and contributions to American literature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jackson

Jackson has a high crime rate, so visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. Avoid walking alone in isolated areas and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Jackson

Jackson has several hospitals and medical facilities for emergencies and routine care. Visitors should take necessary health precautions such as wearing sunscreen, staying hydrated, and protecting themselves from mosquitoes.

Getting to Jackson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jackson

  • Jackson-Medgar Wiley Evers International Airport: Located just a few miles from downtown Jackson, this airport serves regional and national airlines.

Bus Stations Near Jackson

  • Greyhound Bus Station
  • JATRAN Transit System

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Several rental car companies operate in Jackson, offering flexibility and convenience.
  • Taxis and Ride-sharing: Visitors can use Uber and Lyft or local taxi services such as Jackson Taxi and AAA Yellow Cab.

How to Get Around in Jackson

Visitors can use various transportation modes to navigate the city, including:

  • JATRAN Transit System: A public bus system that operates throughout Jackson and surrounding areas.
  • Taxis and Ride-sharing: Taxis and ride-sharing services are available throughout the city.
  • Biking and Walking: Jackson offers several bicycle and pedestrian-friendly routes for outdoor enthusiasts.

Where to Go Next from Jackson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jackson is an excellent base for exploring nearby attractions and destinations such as:

  • Natchez, Mississippi: A historic town on the Mississippi River known for its antebellum homes and Southern charm.
  • Vicksburg National Military Park: A historic site that preserves the history and memory of the Battle of Vicksburg during the American Civil War.

Conclusion

Jackson, Mississippi, is a fascinating destination offering visitors a mix of history, natural beauty, and Southern culture. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan a trip that covers the city’s top attractions, delicious food and drink scene, accommodations, and nearby destinations.
