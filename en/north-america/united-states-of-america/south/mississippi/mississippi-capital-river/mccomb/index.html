Introduction to McComb

McComb is a charming city in Mississippi Capital-River, known for its historical significance, natural beauty, and southern hospitality. Founded in 1872, McComb is named after a civil engineer and cotton magnate, Henry Simpson McComb.

The city has something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs to foodies and culture hounds. In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive list of the top things to do in McComb, as well as where to eat, stay, and shop.

List of Places to Visit in McComb

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • McComb Railroad Museum – This museum documents the history of railroads in the United States, with a focus on how railroads shaped the growth of McComb. You’ll find exhibits, artifacts, and a working model train layout.

  • Pike County Chamber of Commerce – The chamber has a visitor center and art gallery that showcases local artists and their works. It also provides information on local events and festivals.

  • Cheneyville Historic District – This district features historic buildings dating back to the mid-1800s, including homes, churches, and schools.

  • Edgewood Plantation – This 19th-century plantation home, with a Greek Revival-style of architecture, is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Natural Attractions of McComb: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Percy Quin State Park – This park is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and camping. It has a lake, hiking trails, a golf course, and cabins for rent.

  • Bogue Chitto National Wildlife Refuge – This wildlife refuge is home to a variety of native animals, including alligators, deer, and migratory birds. Visitors can go hiking, birdwatching, or hunting.

  • Clear Springs Recreational Area – This area has a natural spring swimming hole, hiking trails, and picnic areas. It’s a great place for families to spend the day.

  • Homochitto National Forest – This forest is filled with pine and hardwood trees, waterfalls, streams, and wildlife. Visitors can hike, bike, hunt, and fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McComb

  • Longleaf Trace Trail – This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. It’s 44 miles long, and goes from Prentiss to Hattiesburg, Mississippi.

  • Percy Quin State Park Trails – This park has several hiking trails that range in difficulty. One of the most popular trails is the Quail Trail, which takes you through forests and along the lake.

  • Bogue Chitto Water Trail – This water trail is great for kayaking, canoeing, and paddleboarding. It’s 43 miles long, and goes from McComb to Bogalusa, Louisiana.

Best Things to Do in McComb: Activities and Tours

  • McComb City Railroad Tours – Take a train ride through McComb, and learn about the city’s railroad history.

  • Pike County Ole Homestead Tour – This tour takes you through several historic homes in Pike County, including the Edgewood Plantation.

  • Homochitto National Forest ATV Trails – If you’re an adrenaline junkie, try the ATV trails at Homochitto National Forest.

Food and Drink Scene of McComb

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken – You can’t visit Mississippi without trying their famous fried chicken.

  • Catfish – Another southern classic, catfish is a staple in many McComb restaurants.

  • Jambalaya – Thanks to McComb’s proximity to Louisiana, you can find delicious Creole dishes like jambalaya.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea – In the south, sweet tea is a way of life. You’ll find it in almost every restaurant in McComb.

  • Craft Beer – McComb has a growing craft beer scene, with several local breweries gaining popularity.

  • Bourbon – Mississippi may not have the same recognition as Kentucky for its bourbon, but it’s certainly worth trying.

Where to Stay in McComb: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express – This hotel is located in the heart of McComb, and offers comfortable rooms and a free hot breakfast.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites – This hotel has a fitness center, indoor pool, and free breakfast. It’s a great option for families.

  • Woodland Trails Bed & Breakfast – If you’re looking for a more intimate experience, this B&B has cozy rooms and a beautiful outdoor area.

What to Buy in McComb: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mississippi-made crafts – There are several shops that sell handmade crafts and products, such as pottery, candles, and woodwork.

  • Catfish-inspired souvenirs – Show your love for Mississippi’s famous catfish with t-shirts, hats, and other souvenirs.

  • Bourbon – If you tried and loved Mississippi bourbon, why not bring home a bottle?

Local Culture and Customs of McComb

  • Southern Hospitality – McComb residents are known for their friendliness and hospitality towards visitors.

  • Religious Values – Mississippi is often referred to as the “Bible Belt,” so it’s not uncommon to see people attending church on Sundays.

Best Time to Visit McComb

The best time to visit McComb is in the spring or fall. The weather is mild, with temperatures in the 70s and low 80s. Winter temperatures can drop below freezing, while summers can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of McComb

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine – You won’t regret trying some of the delicious southern dishes in McComb.

  • Be respectful of local customs – McComb has a conservative and religious population, so it’s important to be respectful of their values.

Don’t:

  • Litter – Keep McComb clean by disposing of trash in designated areas.

  • Be rude to locals – Southern hospitality is a way of life in McComb, so be kind and courteous to residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in McComb

McComb is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions like locking doors, securing valuables, and avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.

Health in McComb

There are several hospitals and clinics in McComb, including Southwest Mississippi Regional Medical Center. It’s always a good idea to have health insurance when traveling.

Getting to McComb: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near McComb

  • Jackson-Medgar Wiley Evers International Airport (JAN) – This airport is about 80 miles north of McComb.

  • Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport (BTR) – This airport is about 90 miles south of McComb.

Bus Stations Near McComb

  • Greyhound Bus Station – This station is located in the heart of McComb, on South Railroad Boulevard.

  • Amtrak Station – The closest Amtrak station is in New Orleans, approximately 120 miles southwest of McComb.

Other Options

  • Driving – McComb is located near interstate 55, making it easily accessible by car.

  • Ridesharing/Taxis – Uber and Lyft are available in McComb for those who prefer not to drive.

How to Get Around in McComb

The best way to get around McComb is by car. Rental cars are available at the nearby airports, or in McComb itself. Taxis and ridesharing apps like Uber are also available.

Where to Go Next from McComb: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Orleans, Louisiana – Head south for a taste of Creole and Cajun culture, as well as world-famous food and drink.

  • Natchez Trace Parkway – This scenic drive takes you through 444 miles of history, culture, and natural beauty.

  • Vicksburg National Military Park – This park commemorates the siege of Vicksburg during the Civil War, and has several historic landmarks to visit.

Conclusion

McComb is a hidden gem in Mississippi, with something for everyone. From outdoor adventures to historical sites, this city is full of surprises. We hope our tour guide has helped you plan your visit to McComb, and that you’ll be able to experience all the city has to offer.
