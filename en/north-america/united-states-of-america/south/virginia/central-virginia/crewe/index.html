Introduction to Crewe

Located in Central Virginia, Crewe is a charming town that offers visitors a delightful blend of natural beauty, historical architecture, and cultural heritage. The town was founded in 1888 and named after the grand master of the Knights of Pythias, General John Fletcher Crewe. Crewe is surrounded by picturesque countryside, and visitors to the town can expect to find a range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and welcoming locals.

List of Places to Visit in Crewe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Crewe is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage. Here are our top picks:

  • The Crewe Railroad Museum: This museum is dedicated to sharing the history of the railroad industry in the town. It features exhibits of railroad equipment, photographs, and artifacts from the golden age of railroads.

  • The Crewe Historic District: This district is home to 49 historical buildings that are recognized by the National Register of Historic Places. Visitors can take a walking tour of the area and admire the Victorian, Queen Anne and Colonial Revival architecture.

  • The Robert Russa Moton Museum: This museum is named after Robert Russa Moton, the African American educator who fought for access to education for black students. The museum exhibits tell the story of the civil rights struggle of Prince Edward County.

Natural Attractions of Crewe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Crewe has plenty of natural attractions to explore:

  • High Bridge Trail State Park: This park offers 31 miles of multi-use trails that are perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The highlight of the park is the towering High Bridge, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Nottoway Falls: The falls are located a short drive from Crewe, towards the west of Burkeville. They are a popular destination for swimming, hiking, and picnicking. The trails around the falls provide scenic views of the falls and the river in the gorge.

  • Blackstone River Park: Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll along the boardwalk that surrounds the river as it runs through the park. The area is also popular for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crewe

Crewe has several trails that offer a variety of experiences for hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts:

  • High Bridge Trail State Park: This trail is one of the most popular in Virginia. The trail is 31 miles long and offers a flat surface that is perfect for beginner-level hikers and cyclists. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the rolling hills and woodlands along the path.

  • Brunswick Mineral Springs Wildlife Management Area: This beautiful area features a network of trails that explore the natural beauty of the area. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities, including hiking, fishing, and hunting.

  • The Tobacco Heritage Trail: This trail covers a distance of 20 miles and passes through several historic towns, including Crewe. The trail follows the old railroads and traverses the beautiful countryside of Southern Virginia.

Best Things to Do in Crewe: Activities and Tours

From visiting museums and historical attractions to outdoor adventures, the town of Crewe provides a variety of activities and tours for visitors to experience:

  • Explore the Historic District: A walking tour of the historic district is a great way to explore the Victorian, Queen Anne and Colonial Revival architecture.

  • Visit Crewe Railroad Museum: A great place to learn about the history of the railroad industry in the town.

  • Hike High Bridge Trail: A scenic trail that is perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.

  • Visit Local Wineries: The area surrounding Crewe has several notable wineries that are worth a visit.

Food and Drink Scene of Crewe

Crewe has a range of restaurants, cafes, and diners that offer delicious food and a variety of drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Country Fried Steak: A Southern classic, this dish features a breaded and fried steak served with gravy and mashed potatoes.

  • Barbecue: The town has several barbecue restaurants that serve mouth-watering pulled pork, beef brisket, and smoked chicken.

  • Mac and Cheese: A Southern staple, mac and cheese is popular in Crewe and can be found in many restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine: The area surrounding Crewe has several notable wineries that produce world-class Virginia wines.

  • Virginia Beer: The state has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors can sample some of the best local brews in the town.

Safety and Health

Getting to Crewe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Crewe

  • Richmond International Airport: This airport is the closest major airport to Crewe, located about an hour’s drive from the town.

  • Piedmont Triad International Airport: This airport is located in Greensboro, North Carolina, and is about two and a half hours’ drive from Crewe.

Bus Stations Near Crewe

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Richmond, Virginia, this bus station is about an hour’s drive from Crewe.

  • Amtrak Station: This station is located in nearby Burkeville and offers regular train service to Richmond and other major cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car or hiring a private taxi to get to Crewe from neighboring towns.

How to Get Around in Crewe

Visitors can get around town by renting a car or using a taxi service. Biking is also a popular option for getting around the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Crewe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Charlottesville: Known for its scenic countryside, charming downtown area, and a range of cultural attractions, Charlottesville is about a two-hour drive from Crewe.

  • Richmond: The capital of Virginia is less than two hours away from Crewe and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions, including colonial-era architecture, museums, parks, and more.

Conclusion

Crewe is a charming town that offers visitors a delightful blend of natural beauty, historical architecture, and cultural heritage. With its range of attractions and activities, Crewe is a must-visit destination in Central Virginia.
