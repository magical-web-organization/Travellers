Introduction to Northside

Northside is a charming district in Richmond, Virginia, known for its unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, adventure seeker, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Northside.

List of Places to Visit in Northside

Here are some of the must-see attractions in Northside:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Virginia Museum of Fine Arts: One of the nation’s top comprehensive art museums with more than 23,000 works of art.
  • The Valentine Museum: A museum dedicated to preserving Richmond’s unique history.
  • The Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden: The Garden is a 50-acre living campus offering spectacular landscapes and gardens year-round.
  • The Maggie L. Walker National Historic Site: Celebrates the life of one of the country’s most significant and influential African American businesswomen and civic leaders.
  • The Third Street Bethel African Methodist Episcopal Church: A historic church that was the first independent black congregation in Richmond, Virginia, established in 1841.

Natural Attractions of Northside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Bryan Park: A 262-acre park featuring a lake, multiple trails, and picnic sites.
  • Pony Pasture: A great spot for swimming, fishing, and kayaking in the James River.
  • Chimborazo Park: A unique park with an elevated location providing panoramic views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northside

Northside has many scenic trails, including:

  • The Northbank Trail: A 3-mile hiking trail that runs along the James River and the Richmond downtown waterfront.
  • The Buttermilk Trail: This 1.5-mile hike features stunning views of the James River and is perfect for both beginners and experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Northside: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Northside offers many other activities, including:

  • Trolley Tours: Take a guided tour through Richmond’s colorful history on a vintage trolley.
  • Brewery Tours: Richmond is home to several microbreweries such as Hardywood and The Veil Brewing Co.
  • Richmond Flying Squirrels: Watch a minor league baseball game in the beautiful Diamond stadium.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northside

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Northside:

Day Activity
1 Visit The Virginia Museum of Fine Arts and The Valentine Museum
2 Explore the Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden and Bryan Park
3 Visit the Maggie L. Walker National Historic Site and Third Street Bethel African Methodist Episcopal Church
4 Hike the Northbank Trail, enjoy a Trolley Tour, and visit The Veil Brewing Co.
5 Swim at Pony Pasture and visit Chimborazo Park
6 Watch a game at The Diamond and enjoy a brewery tour at Hardywood
7 Relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery at Belle Isle and the James River Park System

Food and Drink Scene of Northside

Northside has a thriving food and drink culture. Don’t miss out on these local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rappahannock Oysters: Sourced from the Chesapeake Bay, these oysters sell out quickly so early orders are essential!
  • Peanut Soup: Virginia’s version of this traditional soup is a flavorful and creamy soup that is a must-try.
  • Country Ham: A Virginia staple, this cured ham is delicious in sandwiches, biscuits, or on its own.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine: Virginia is known for its excellent wine, so be sure to try a bottle.
  • Richmond Beer Trail: Richmond is home to several microbreweries, so be sure to explore the Richmond Beer Trail while in town.

Where to Stay in Northside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northside has several accommodation options, including:

  • The Jefferson Hotel: One of the most luxurious and historic hotels in Richmond.
  • HI Richmond Hostel: A charming, budget-friendly hostel in the heart of downtown Richmond.
  • The Linden Row Inn: A boutique hotel in an elegant and historic rowhouse.

What to Buy in Northside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here’s a list of items worth bringing home as souvenirs:

  • Virginia Peanuts: Virginia is known for its peanuts, which make great gifts for family and friends.
  • Handmade Jewelry: From handcrafted silver to beadwork, you can find unique pieces made by local artisans in Northside.
  • Local Artwork: Gallery hopping is a fun way to explore Northside’s art scene and find unique pieces to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Northside

Here are some important things to know about Northside culture:

  • Greeting: People in Northside are generally friendly and warm. It’s common to greet others with a smile and a casual “hello”.
  • Etiquette: Northside is a polite and welcoming place that values civility and good manners.
  • Sports: Virginians are sports enthusiasts, and the enthusiasm is infectious, especially when it comes to high school and college football games.

Best Time to Visit Northside

The best time to visit Northside is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild, and foliage is at its peak. The peak tourist season is from April through November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northside

Do:

  • Explore the James River: There’s no better way to see Richmond then by exploring the James River. Rent a bike, kayak, or paddleboard and get out on the water.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Virginia’s food scene is alive and thriving, and you don’t want to miss out on it.
  • Be Respectful: Northside is a friendly and welcoming place, so be sure to be courteous to everyone you meet.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep Northside and Richmond beautiful by disposing of your litter properly.
  • Be Impatient: The pace of life in Northside is relaxed, so be sure to take your time and enjoy your trip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northside

Northside is generally a safe place to visit, but as with any city, it’s important to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Stay Alert: Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Stay on Main Roads: Stick to well-lit, busy areas, especially at night.
  • Keep Your Valuables Safe: Don’t carry large sums of money, jewelry, or other valuables with you.

Health in Northside

Northside has excellent healthcare facilities, including:

  • VRS Richmond Rehabilitation Hospital: This rehabilitation hospital provides specialized care for patients recovering from injuries, strokes, or other medical conditions.
  • Richmond Community Hospital: A general acute care hospital that provides a wide range of medical and surgical services.

Getting to Northside: Transportation Options and Logistics

Richmond is well-connected by both air and land. Here’s how to get to Northside:

Airports Near Northside

  • Richmond International Airport: The city’s primary commercial airport, located just 15 minutes from Northside.

Bus Stations Near Northside

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound has a bus station located in downtown Richmond, just a 10-minute drive from Northside.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Car rental companies at Richmond International Airport include Avis, Budget, Enterprise, and Hertz.
  • Rideshare Services: Uber and Lyft are both available in Richmond.

How to Get Around in Northside

The best way to get around Northside is by car or bike. There are several car rental options available at the airport, while bikes can be rented from places such as the nearby Virginia Capital Trail.

Where to Go Next from Northside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northside is well-positioned for day trips to other popular destinations in Virginia, including:

  • Colonial Williamsburg: A 45-minute drive to a living-history museum and outdoor activity center.
  • Shenandoah National Park: A two-hour drive to this iconic Virginia park.
  • Virginia Beach: A two-hour drive to Virginia’s most popular vacation spot.

Conclusion

Northside, Richmond is a vibrant and unique district that offers something for every traveler. From history and culture to outdoor adventures and excellent food and drink, Northside is a must-visit destination in Virginia. So start planning your trip today!
