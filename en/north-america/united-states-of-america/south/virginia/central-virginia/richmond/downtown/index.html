Introduction to Downtown

Downtown Richmond, VA is a bustling and historic district that features a mix of old-world charm and modern amenities. It’s known for its cultural significance, outdoor activities, and delicious food and drink scene. The area is easily walkable and offers a unique blend of architecture, from cobblestone streets to towering skyscrapers. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Downtown Richmond has something for you.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Downtown Richmond is steeped in history, making it home to a plethora of museums and historical hotspots. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • The American Civil War Museum: This museum offers a unique perspective on one of the most crucial periods in US History. The museum’s exhibits feature copies of actual letters from soldiers and civilians and a life-size diorama of a Civil War battlefield.

  • The Virginia Holocaust Museum: The Virginia Holocaust Museum is an emotionally charged, yet educational museum that documents the events of the Holocaust through poignant exhibits.

  • The Edgar Allan Poe Museum: The Edgar Allan Poe Museum is a must-visit for literature enthusiasts. Located in Richmond’s historic Shockoe Bottom neighborhood, the museum offers an insight into the famous writer’s life, work, and legacy.

  • St. John’s Church: This iconic 18th-century church is known as the birthplace of American independence. It was here that Patrick Henry gave his famous “Give me liberty or give me death” speech, spurring on the American Revolution.

  • The Virginia Museum of Fine Arts: The Virginia Museum of Fine Arts is one of the leading art museums in the country. The museum’s exhibits showcase over 50,000 works of art from ancient times to the present day, including works by famous artists such as Monet, Degas, and Picasso.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Despite being an urban area, Downtown Richmond offers plenty of opportunities to explore nature. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The James River Park System: The James River Park System is a group of parks located on the banks of the James River. The park system offers over 550 acres of hiking trails, fishing spots, and scenic overlooks.

  • Belle Isle: Belle Isle is a small island located in the James River. The island features hiking trails, a suspension bridge, and several ruins of historic structures.

  • Canal Walk: The Canal Walk is a 1.25-mile trail that runs parallel to the James River. The trail features several historic landmarks, murals, and educational exhibits.

  • Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden: Just north of Downtown, Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden is known for its extensive collection of flowers, plants, and trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

Downtown Richmond has a wide selection of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Buttermilk Trail: This 3.5-mile trail runs alongside the banks of the James River and offers scenic views of the water.

  • North Bank Trail: The North Bank Trail is a 3-mile-long trail that features scenic views of the James River and the Richmond skyline.

  • Capital Trail: The Capital Trail is a 52-mile multi-use trail that runs from Richmond to Jamestown. The trail is perfect for biking or walking and is open year-round.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

Downtown Richmond offers plenty of fun activities and tours. Here are some ideas for your itinerary:

  • Segway Tour: A Segway tour is a great way to experience the sights and sounds of Downtown Richmond. The tour takes you through historic neighborhoods and landmarks.

  • Ghost Tour: Get spooked on a ghost tour of some of Richmond’s most haunted landmarks.

  • Riverfront Canal Cruise: A leisurely ride on the Canal Cruise offers a unique perspective of the city.

  • Richmond Brewery Tour: Richmond is home to a thriving craft beer scene, and a brewery tour is an excellent way to sample some of the city’s best brews.

Week-Long Itinerary for Downtown

If you’re planning to spend a week in Downtown Richmond, here’s a sample itinerary to help plan your trip:

Day 1: Start your trip with a Segway tour of Downtown Richmond.

Day 2: Explore some of the city’s museums and historical landmarks, such as the Edgar Allan Poe Museum and St. John’s Church.

Day 3: Spend the day outdoors, hiking or cycling in the James River Park System.

Day 4: Take a Riverfront Canal Cruise and visit some of the city’s top entertainment venues like the Altria Theater or Dominion Energy Center for the Performing Arts.

Day 5: Visit one of Richmond’s many breweries, such as The Answer or Legend Brewing Company.

Day 6: Indulge in some of Richmond’s best cuisine, such as Southern-style barbecue or fresh seafood.

Day 7: End your trip with a stroll through Richmond’s beautiful Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

No trip to Downtown Richmond is complete without indulging in the city’s famous food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pimento Cheese: This Southern classic can be found on the menus of many downtown restaurants. A blend of cheese, mayonnaise, and pimentos, it’s often served as a spread on crackers or toasted bread.

  • Virginia Ham: A staple of Southern cuisine, Virginia ham is a must-try when visiting Richmond. It’s usually served thinly sliced and cured with salt.

  • Chesapeake Bay Oysters: Looking for some fresh seafood? Richmond’s proximity to the Chesapeake Bay means that you can find some of the freshest oysters around.

  • Lamb Shank: Braised lamb shanks are a popular entrée here, cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine: Virginia is known for its excellent vineyards, and Downtown Richmond has several wine bars where you can sample some of the state’s top wines.

  • Richmond Craft Beer: Richmond’s craft beer scene is thriving, with over 30 breweries in the area. Some must-visit spots include The Veil Brewing Co. and Hardywood Park Craft Brewery.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown Richmond has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Jefferson Hotel: A historic, luxury hotel in the heart of downtown.

  • Quirk Hotel: A trendy, modern hotel with spacious rooms and a rooftop bar.

  • HI Richmond Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for solo travelers or groups.

  • Linden Row Inn: A historic boutique hotel located on a charming cobblestone street.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home? Here are some of the best local products and souvenirs to pick up in Downtown Richmond:

  • Virginia Peanuts: Grown locally and sold in many stores throughout the city, Virginia peanuts’ roasted flavor makes them a must-try.

  • Craft Beer: Richmond has a thriving craft beer scene, so why not bring home a six-pack of your favorite local brew?

  • Artisan Crafts: From jewelry to pottery, take home a hand-crafted piece of Richmond artwork from one of the many local boutiques downtown.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Richmond’s culture is deeply rooted in Southern hospitality, and locals are known for their friendly and welcoming attitudes. When dining in the city, it’s common to receive a glass of water and small plate of snacks upon being seated at a restaurant.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown Richmond is during the fall when temperatures are mild, and the leaves change color. Summer is also an excellent time to visit, with lots of outdoor activities and lively events going on.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Do take a tour: One of the best ways to learn about the city’s history and culture is to take a guided tour.

  • Do try the local cuisine: Richmond’s food scene is not to be missed, so be sure to try some local favorites.

  • Do visit the parks: Richmond’s parks are some of its most beautiful and accessible attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Keep the city clean by disposing of trash properly.

  • Don’t forget sunscreen: Whether hiking or touring, make sure to wear sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for directions: Richmond locals are friendly, and they’re always willing to help visitors navigate the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Richmond is generally a safe area to explore, but as with any city, it’s essential to take precautions such as keeping valuables out of sight and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Downtown

If you’re traveling to Downtown Richmond from abroad, it’s recommended that you check with your healthcare provider regarding any necessary vaccines.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

  • Richmond International Airport: Located 7 miles southeast of downtown, Richmond International Airport is the closest airport to Downtown Richmond.

  • Washington Dulles International Airport: Located roughly 100 miles north of Downtown Richmond, Dulles International Airport is a major hub for international flights.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

Greyhound Lines offers bus service from several terminals in the Richmond area.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Amtrak offers rail service from several stations in the Richmond area.

  • Driving: Downtown Richmond is well-connected to several major highways and interstates, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Downtown Richmond is a walking-friendly area, and many of the top attractions are within a short distance of each other. If you prefer to get around by bike, there are several bike rental companies located downtown.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to explore outside of Downtown Richmond? Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Williamsburg: Located an hour east of Downtown Richmond, Williamsburg is a historic town that’s home to Colonial Williamsburg, Busch Gardens, and Water Country USA.

  • Shenandoah National Park: Roughly a two-hour drive west of Richmond, Shenandoah National Park is known for its scenic Skyline Drive and hiking trails.

  • Virginia Beach: A two-hour drive southeast of Richmond, Virginia Beach offers miles of coastline, boardwalk attractions, and water sports.

Conclusion

Downtown Richmond is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a mix of culture, nature, and adventure. With plenty of museums, hiking trails, and delicious local cuisine, it’s no wonder the city has become a favorite for travelers. Plan your trip today to experience all that Downtown Richmond has to offer.
