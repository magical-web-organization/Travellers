Introduction to Southside

Located in Central Virginia, Southside is one of the leading tourist destinations in Richmond, Virginia. This vibrant region of the city offers a plethora of attractions, from scenic views and outdoor adventures to museums and historical places. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information you need to explore the best of Southside, Richmond.

List of Places to Visit in Southside

Southside is home to several attractions that cater to different interests and preferences. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Southside:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Virginia Museum of Fine Arts: This museum boasts a collection of over 35,000 works of art, ranging from ancient to contemporary.

  • Historic Tredegar: Once a bustling iron works factory, it now serves as a museum recounting the history of the Civil War.

  • The Edgar Allan Poe Museum: Located in the poet’s former residence, this museum showcases Poe’s life and work.

  • St. John’s Church: One of the oldest surviving churches in Virginia, it’s where Patrick Henry delivered his famous “Give me liberty or give me death” speech.

Natural Attractions of Southside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canal Walk: A scenic walking trail along the James River Canal that takes you to the downtown area.

  • Pony Pasture: A riverside park with picturesque views, great hiking trails, and perfect spots for picnics and sunbathing.

  • The T. Tyler Potterfield Memorial Bridge: A pedestrian-friendly bridge, providing scenic views of the river and the city.

  • Maymont Park: A lush 100-acre park featuring a Japanese garden, a children’s zoo, and an arboretum.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southside

Southside offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventurers, from hiking to biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Belle Isle Trail: A flat, easy trail that provides scenic views of the James River.

  • Buttermilk Trail: A challenging trail with steep inclines and rocky paths that offers a rewarding view of the city skyline.

  • Pocahontas State Park: Offers over 80 miles of hiking and biking trails, including the popular Bright Hope Trail.

Best Things to Do in Southside: Activities and Tours

Southside has plenty of activities and tours for everyone. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Richmond Riverfront Canal Cruise: A popular 40-minute narrated tour that covers Richmond’s history and offers stunning views of the city.

  • Richmond Trolley Company: A hop-on, hop-off trolley that covers major tourist attractions in the city.

  • Segway Tour: A fun and unique way to explore Richmond’s downtown, including the historic Jackson Ward area and the State Capitol.

Food and Drink Scene of Southside

Southside is not only famous for its attractions but also for its culinary delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Virginia Ham: A cured ham served as a main course or on sandwiches.

  • Fried Chicken: A southern classic, crispy and delicious.

  • Pimento Cheese: A cheesy spread made with cheddar, cream cheese, and pimentos.

  • Shrimp and Grits: A popular southern dish with spicy shrimp served over a bed of creamy grits.

Local Beverages

  • Lemonade: A refreshing drink made with fresh lemons and sugar.

  • Sweet Tea: A southern sweetened iced tea, perfect for hot summer days.

  • Craft Beer: Richmond is known for its craft beer scene. Visit some of the local breweries to try out some unique brews.

Where to Stay in Southside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southside has plenty of places to stay, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular options include:

  • The Jefferson Hotel: A historic landmark hotel that offers luxury accommodations and an array of amenities.

  • The Graduate Richmond: A trendy boutique hotel decorated with vintage finds.

  • HI Richmond Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers shared and private accommodations.

What to Buy in Southside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some great options:

  • Peanut Butter: Virginia is known for its peanut production. Grab a jar of locally made peanut butter.

  • Virginia Wine: Virginia has a thriving wine industry. Purchase some bottles of local wines to enjoy at home.

  • Virginia Ham: A cured ham that is a local delicacy.

Local Culture and Customs of Southside

Richmond has a unique culture and a rich history. The city has a long history of civil rights activism and is home to several African American cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Southside

The best time to visit Southside is during the fall (September-November). The weather is mild, and the fall foliage is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southside

Do:

  • Do visit the museums and historical sites. Southside is home to several museums and historical sites that offer insights into the city’s past and present.

  • Do indulge in the local cuisine. Richmond offers a variety of culinary delights that are not to be missed.

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities. Southside has plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and river cruises.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Keep the city clean by properly disposing of your trash.

  • Don’t be disrespectful to the local culture. Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help. The locals are friendly and always happy to help.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southside

Richmond is generally a safe city, but like any city, it’s important to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings. Always be aware of your surroundings, especially in unfamiliar areas.

  • Avoid walking alone at night. Stick to well-lit areas and be aware of your surroundings.

  • Lock your car doors. Keep your car doors locked when driving around the city.

Health in Southside

It’s always important to stay healthy while traveling. Here are some tips:

  • Stay hydrated. Drink plenty of water, especially during the hot summer months.

  • Protect yourself from the sun. Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
