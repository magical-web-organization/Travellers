Introduction to Locust Dale

Located in Central Virginia, Locust Dale is a charming small town that offers a mix of outdoor adventures and cultural attractions. With its rich history, scenic beauty, and friendly locals, Locust Dale is an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation with friends and family. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Locust Dale’s top attractions, including its must-see museums and historical sites, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and more. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Locust Dale

Locust Dale is home to a variety of attractions that offer something for every type of traveler. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Exchange Hotel Civil War Medical Museum: This museum was once a Confederate hospital during the Civil War and now features exhibits that showcase the role of medicine during the war.
  • Pompey Museum: Located in an antebellum mansion, the museum features artifacts and exhibits on the history of Locust Dale and its surrounding areas.
  • Montpelier: The former home of President James Madison, Montpelier is now a National Historic Landmark and offers tours of the estate and gardens.

Natural Attractions of Locust Dale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shenandoah National Park: Located just a short drive from Locust Dale, this park is home to over 500 miles of hiking trails, scenic overlooks, and camping sites.
  • Rapidan River Kayak Company: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the beautiful Rapidan River, which winds through the town.
  • Greene County Trails and Parks: With over 40 miles of trails, these parks offer a variety of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Locust Dale

Locust Dale is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, whether you prefer to hike, bike, or just take a leisurely stroll. Here are some of the town’s top hiking and biking routes:

  • Rapidan Wildlife Refuge Trail: This 4.4-mile trail winds through the Rapidan Wildlife Refuge and offers scenic views of the river and surrounding woods.
  • Montpelier Trails: With over eight miles of trails, Montpelier offers a variety of hiking and biking options.
  • Shenandoah National Park Trails: Located nearby, the park offers over 500 miles of hiking trails that range from easy to strenuous.

Best Things to Do in Locust Dale: Activities and Tours

Aside from its outdoor adventures and cultural attractions, Locust Dale offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do in the town:

  • Ride horses at Oakland Heights Farm: Take a guided horseback ride through the scenic countryside.
  • Visit a vineyard: Locust Grove Vineyards, just outside of town, offers tastings and tours of their vineyard and winery.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Take in the beauty of the town and surrounding areas from above with Blue Ridge Balloon Company.

Week-Long Itinerary for Locust Dale

If you’re planning to spend a week in Locust Dale, here’s an itinerary that will help you experience the best the town has to offer:

Day 1: Visit the Exchange Hotel Civil War Medical Museum and Pompey Museum.

Day 2: Explore the outdoor beauty of the town with a hike on the Rapidan Wildlife Refuge Trail and a kayak ride on the Rapidan River.

Day 3: Take a leisurely bike ride through Montpelier trails and visit the Montpelier estate and gardens.

Day 4: Visit the Shenandoah National Park for a day of hiking and scenic views.

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride with Blue Ridge Balloon Company and visit a local vineyard for a wine tasting.

Day 6: Horseback ride through the countryside at Oakland Heights Farm.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a picnic at one of the town’s parks, such as Yowell Meadow Park or Lydia Mountain Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Locust Dale

Locust Dale offers a variety of dining options and local beverages that are sure to please every palate. Here are some of the town’s must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork BBQ: Locust Dale is known for its Southern-style pork BBQ, which is served at many local restaurants.
  • Locally sourced meats and cheeses: Take a trip to the farmers’ market and pick up some locally sourced meats and cheeses for a picnic.
  • Fried Catfish: A Southern classic, fried catfish can be found on menus throughout the town.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine: Locust Grove Vineyards and other nearby wineries offer a variety of award-winning Virginia wines.
  • Craft Beer: Locust Dale has a growing craft beer scene, with breweries such as Bald Top Brewing Company and Blue Mountain Brewery offering a variety of brews.

Where to Stay in Locust Dale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Greene Book Inn: This quaint bed and breakfast is located just outside of town and offers a peaceful retreat.

Getting to Locust Dale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Locust Dale

The closest major airport to Locust Dale is Charlottesville Albemarle Airport, which is located approximately 32 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Locust Dale

The closest Greyhound bus station is located in Culpeper, approximately 16 miles away from Locust Dale.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Locust Dale is easily accessible by car via Interstate 64 and Route 522.
