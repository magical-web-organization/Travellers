Introduction to Lynchburg

Located in Central Virginia, Lynchburg is rich in natural beauty and history. The city is home to several important museums, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, making it a popular destination for tourists. This guide will provide a comprehensive overview of Lynchburg, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Lynchburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Appomattox Court House National Historical Park

Visit the site where General Robert E. Lee surrendered to General Ulysses S. Grant, effectively ending the American Civil War. Explore the courthouse, historic buildings, and exhibits that highlight the significance of the event.

Lynchburg Museum

Learn about Lynchburg’s history with exhibits that showcase the city’s economic, cultural, and social development. The museum also features rotating exhibits and special events.

Point of Honor

This historic house was constructed in 1815 and offers a glimpse into the lifestyle of affluent Virginians during the early 19th century. Guided tours are available.

Natural Attractions of Lynchburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

James River Heritage Trail

Hike, bike, or jog along this scenic trail with views of the James River and Lynchburg skyline. The trail is approximately 10 miles long.

Peaks of Otter

Located in the nearby Blue Ridge Mountains, the Peaks of Otter offer stunning views of the surrounding wilderness. Enjoy hiking and wildlife viewing.

Liberty Mountain Snowflex Centre

Experience skiing and snowboarding year-round on the country’s first synthetic snow slope, located at Liberty University.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lynchburg

Blackwater Creek Trail

This 4.5-mile trail follows the course of the Blackwater Creek and offers picturesque views of the James River. It’s perfect for biking, walking, or jogging.

Percival’s Island Natural Area

This popular area has a 2-mile trail that is great for a leisurely walk or jog. It’s also great for bird watching, fishing, and picnics.

Best Things to Do in Lynchburg: Activities and Tours

Legacy Museum of African-American History

This museum provides an in-depth look at the impact of slavery and racism in the United States. The museum features interactive exhibits and events that explore the history of African Americans.

Amazement Square

This children’s museum offers interactive exhibits that encourage learning through play. It’s a great place for families with young children.

Riverwalk Trail

Take a scenic walk along the James River on this 4.5-mile trail. You’ll see great views of the river and the surrounding mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lynchburg

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Appomattox Court House National Historical Park

Food and Drink Scene of Lynchburg

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Chicken

Lynchburg is known for their fried chicken, which is crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside. Be sure to try it at Mrs. Joy’s Absolutely Fabulous Treats or My Dog Duke’s Diner.

BBQ

Slow-smoked, tender meats are a popular dish in Lynchburg. Visit Maylynn’s Creamery and BBQ for some of the best BBQ in the city.

Local Beverages

Virginia Wines

Lynchburg is located near several wineries that produce award-winning Virginia wines. Check out the Wine Trail of Botetourt County for a list of top wineries in the area.

Virginia Craft Beer

Lynchburg has several craft breweries that produce unique, handcrafted beers. Be sure to check out Apocalypse Ale Works and Three Roads Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Lynchburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Virginian Lynchburg

Located in the heart of downtown Lynchburg, The Virginian Lynchburg is a historic hotel that has been recently renovated. It features spacious rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a rooftop bar.

Craddock Terry Hotel and Event Center

Housed in a former shoe factory, the Craddock Terry Hotel features boutique-style rooms and suites. It also has an on-site restaurant and brewery.

The Carriage House Inn Bed and Breakfast

This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a home-cooked breakfast. It’s located within walking distance of downtown Lynchburg.

What to Buy in Lynchburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lynchburg Lemonade

This refreshing drink is made with Lynchburg’s famous lemonade mix and Jack Daniel’s whiskey. Bring home a bottle of the lemonade mix to make your own Lynchburg Lemonade at home.

Virginia Ham

Virginia ham is a staple in Southern cuisine and makes for a great souvenir. Check out Doughtery’s Roadside Market for the best Virginia ham in Lynchburg.

Local Culture and Customs of Lynchburg

Lynchburg is a Southern city with a rich history and strong sense of community. Residents are well-known for their hospitality and love of food.

Best Time to Visit Lynchburg

The best time to visit Lynchburg is in the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when temperatures are mild and the foliage is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lynchburg

Do:

  • Try the fried chicken.
  • Take a walk along the Riverwalk Trail.
  • Visit the Appomattox Court House National Historical Park.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray during the summer months.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in sacred places, such as churches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lynchburg

Lynchburg is a relatively safe city, but visitors should be cautious when walking alone at night or in areas that are unfamiliar.

Health in Lynchburg

Lynchburg has several hospitals and medical centers that offer quality healthcare for visitors in case of emergency.

Getting to Lynchburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lynchburg

  • Lynchburg Regional Airport
  • Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Lynchburg

  • Greyhound Bus Station
  • Megabus Station

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station

How to Get Around in Lynchburg

The best way to get around Lynchburg is by car. Rental cars are available at the Lynchburg Regional Airport, and there are several car rental companies located in the city.

Where to Go Next from Lynchburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Natural Bridge

Visit the Natural Bridge, a geological formation that is over 400 million years old. It’s located approximately 1 hour south of Lynchburg.

Charlottesville

Located approximately 1 hour northeast of Lynchburg, Charlottesville is home to several historic sites, including Monticello, the home of Thomas Jefferson.

Conclusion

Lynchburg is a hidden gem in Central Virginia that offers something for everyone. From museums and historic sites to outdoor adventures and unique culinary experiences, Lynchburg is a city that should not be missed. Plan your trip today and discover all that Lynchburg has to offer!
