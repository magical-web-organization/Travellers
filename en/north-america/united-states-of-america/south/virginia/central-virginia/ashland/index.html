Introduction to Ashland

Nestled in the heart of Virginia, Ashland is a small town with a rich history and vibrant culture. This charming destination is known for its picturesque streets, quaint shops, and friendly locals. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Ashland has something to offer. From hiking and biking tours to cultural attractions and museums, there are countless activities that will make your visit to Ashland unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Ashland

No visit to Ashland is complete without exploring its top attractions. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ashland’s rich history is preserved in its many museums and historical sites. Here are the top cultural attractions in Ashland:

  • Ashland Museum - This museum showcases the town’s history through interactive exhibits and artifacts. Visitors can learn about the railroad industry, Ashland’s role in the Civil War, and the town’s people and culture.
  • Hanover Tavern - This historic landmark was once a stagecoach stop, serving travelers and locals alike. Today, it is a museum and restaurant that offers tours and live performances.
  • The Centre Hill Museum - This museum is dedicated to the history of Hanover County, with a focus on the Civil War and the people who lived in the area during that tumultuous time.

Natural Attractions of Ashland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Ashland is an outdoor paradise. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • James River State Park - This park offers hiking trails, camping, fishing, and even canoeing. Visitors can explore the scenic river views and diverse wildlife.
  • Patrick Henry’s Hanover County Home - The home of the famous orator and Revolutionary War patriot, this site offers a glimpse into the natural world that inspired Henry’s famous speeches. Visitors can explore the gardens, hike the nature trail, and visit the historic home.
  • Bear Creek Lake State Park - This park is the perfect place for a picnic or a day on the lake. Visitors can fish, boat, or swim, or hike one of the many trails that wind through the surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashland

Ashland is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some top routes:

  • Virginia Capital Trail - This scenic trail runs through Ashland and connects Richmond to Williamsburg. The trail is perfect for biking, jogging, or walking, and features stunning views of the James River.
  • Hanover County Parks and Recreation Trails - These trails wind through the scenic countryside, offering hikers and bikers stunning views of the landscape.
  • Ashland Trolley Line Trail - This trail follows an old trolley line and takes hikers through some of the town’s most historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Ashland: Activities and Tours

From cultural and historical tours to outdoor adventures, Ashland has something for everyone. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Ashland Walking Tours - These guided tours take visitors through the town’s historic district, showcasing the best sights and landmarks.
  • Ashland Berry Farm - This family-owned farm offers a wide range of activities, from pumpkin picking in the fall to hayrides and petting zoos.
  • Ashland Theatre - This historic theatre has been renovated and now offers live performances and film screenings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashland

For those who want to explore Ashland in depth, here is a week-long itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the town’s historic district, visit the Ashland Museum and Hanover Tavern
Day 2 Hike the Virginia Capital Trail and visit Patrick Henry’s Hanover County Home
Day 3 Visit Ashland Berry Farm and enjoy some of its seasonal activities
Day 4 Take a day trip to Richmond and visit the Virginia Museum of Fine Arts and the Virginia State Capitol
Day 5 Explore the James River State Park and enjoy a picnic lunch
Day 6 Take a guided tour of the town’s historic sites, including Centre Hill Museum
Day 7 Relax at Bear Creek Lake State Park and go for a swim or a boat ride

Food and Drink Scene of Ashland

Ashland has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for both locals and visitors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken and waffles - This Southern classic is a local favorite, featuring crispy fried chicken and fluffy waffles drizzled in syrup.
  • Brunswick stew - This hearty stew is made with chicken, pork, and vegetables, simmered in a rich tomato-based sauce.
  • Barbecue - Ashland is known for its delicious barbecue, with plenty of places to try pulled pork, brisket, and other smoky meats.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia wine - Ashland is located in the heart of Virginia wine country, and there are plenty of local wineries to explore.
  • Hard cider - This refreshing beverage is made with fresh apples and can be found at many local breweries and cideries.
  • Virginia craft beer - This area is home to a thriving craft beer scene, with plenty of local microbreweries to explore.

Where to Stay in Ashland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ashland has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  • The Henry Clay Inn - This historic hotel is located in the heart of Ashland’s downtown district and features comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving classic American cuisine.
  • The Ashland Motel - This budget-friendly option offers clean and comfortable rooms, with easy access to the town’s top attractions.
  • The Martha Hotel and Spa - This luxurious hotel is located just outside of town and features a spa, pool, and upscale dining options.

What to Buy in Ashland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade crafts to local wines and ciders, Ashland offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some top picks:

  • Ashland Farmers’ Market - This weekly market features fresh local produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal cheeses and meats.
  • James River Cellars Winery - This local winery offers tastings and tours, giving visitors a chance to try some of Virginia’s best wines.
  • Ashland Hanover Artisan Shop - This shop features handmade crafts and art created by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashland

Ashland is home to a diverse and friendly community, known for its welcoming spirit and laid-back vibe. Visitors to Ashland can expect to experience Southern hospitality at its finest, with locals always ready to share a smile and a friendly conversation.

Best Time to Visit Ashland

The best time to visit Ashland depends on your preferences. Spring and fall are the most popular seasons, with mild temperatures and beautiful foliage. Summer can be hot and humid, but there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. Winter is the quietest time of year in Ashland, but it can be a beautiful time to visit if you’re looking for a peaceful getaway.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashland

To make the most of your visit to Ashland, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s historic district and cultural attractions.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray.
  • Don’t miss out on the town’s outdoor adventures and natural attractions.
  • Don’t forget to say hello to friendly locals!

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashland

Overall, Ashland is a safe destination for visitors. However, it’s always important to take the usual precautions when traveling, such as being aware of your surroundings, locking your car and valuables, and following any safety guidelines at parks and other outdoor attractions.

Health in Ashland

Ashland has several medical facilities, including the Hanover Emergency Center and the Virginia Physicians for Women. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you, and to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Ashland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ashland is conveniently located off Interstate 95, making it easily accessible by car. The nearest airport is Richmond International Airport, which is located about 25 miles south of Ashland. Visitors can also take the train to Ashland’s own Amtrak station, which offers daily service to and from major cities along the East Coast.

Airports Near Ashland

  • Richmond International Airport (RIC) - 24 miles from Ashland

Bus Stations Near Ashland

  • Greyhound Bus Station - 21 miles from Ashland

Other Options

  • Amtrak Ashland Station - Located in the heart of downtown Ashland, this station offers daily service to and from major cities along the East Coast.

How to Get Around in Ashland

The easiest way to get around Ashland is by car, but there are also several tour companies that offer guided tours of the town and surrounding areas. Visitors can also explore Ashland on foot or by bike, taking advantage of the town’s many parks and outdoor trails.

Where to Go Next from Ashland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ashland is located in the heart of Virginia, making it a great jumping-off point for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some top picks:

  • Richmond - This historic city is located just 20 miles south of Ashland and offers a wide range of cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and theaters.
  • Williamsburg - This charming colonial town is located about an hour southeast of Ashland and is home to many historic sites, including Colonial Williamsburg and the Jamestown Settlement.
  • Charlottesville - Located about 80 miles west of Ashland, Charlottesville is home to the University of Virginia, as well as many historic sites and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Ashland is a hidden gem of Virginia, with a rich history and vibrant culture that make it a must-visit destination. From outdoor adventures and natural attractions to cultural landmarks and delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone in this charming town. Whether you’re looking to explore Virginia’s rich history or simply relax in a quaint Southern setting, Ashland is the perfect destination for your next trip.
