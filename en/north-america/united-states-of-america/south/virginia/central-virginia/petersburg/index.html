Introduction to Petersburg

Petersburg is a historic city located in the heart of Central Virginia, USA. It is known for its rich history, outdoor recreation, and vibrant culture. Visitors can explore museums, historic landmarks, and hiking trails while enjoying local cuisine and friendly hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Petersburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Petersburg is home to several museums and historical landmarks that reflect the city’s rich cultural heritage. The city’s Historic District is a designated National Historic Landmark and is filled with stunning architecture, museums, and galleries. The following are the must-visit culturally important attractions:

Petersburg National Battlefield

Petersburg National Battlefield marks the site of a crucial battle during the Civil War. Visitors can explore the battlefield and learn about the lives of soldiers and civilians affected by the war.

Siege Museum

The Siege Museum explores the history of the Civil War’s longest siege. Visitors can see artifacts, exhibits, and multimedia displays that document this historic event.

Centre Hill Museum

Centre Hill Museum is a restored mansion that dates back to 1823. Visitors can explore the home’s interior and learn about its history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Petersburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Petersburg has an abundance of outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing. The city boasts several beautiful state parks, lakes, and nature preserves. The following are the must-visit natural attractions of Petersburg:

Pocahontas State Park

Pocahontas State Park is a beautiful park that features over 7,500 acres of forests, wetlands, and waterways. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, boating, and camping, amongst other activities.

Appomattox River Trail

Appomattox River Trail is a beautiful trail that runs for 23 miles along the Appomattox River. Hikers, bikers, and nature lovers can enjoy scenic views, local wildlife, and recreational activities like fishing and boating.

Swift Creek Reservoir

Swift Creek Reservoir is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and kayaking. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the lake and surrounding forests while relaxing and soaking in the natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petersburg

Petersburg is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with miles of scenic trails and routes. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Petersburg:

Petersburg National Battlefield Trail

The Petersburg National Battlefield Trail winds through the historic battlefield and covers over 10 miles. Visitors can explore both Union and Confederate fortifications and learn more about the war era history.

Appomattox River Trail

The Appomattox River Trail is a 23-mile-long riverside trail that offers stunning views of the river and surrounding forests. The trail is a perfect spot of biking and hiking enthusiasts.

City Point Bike Trail

The City Point Bike Trail is a 5.8-mile rails-to-trails path that takes visitors through historic City Point. The trail offers scenic views of the river and the city and is perfect for cycling.

Best Things to Do in Petersburg: Activities and Tours

Petersburg is an exciting and vibrant city with numerous activities and tours. Whether you’re interested in history, entertainment, or outdoor recreation, there is something for everyone. Here are some of our best things to do in Petersburg:

Guided Tour of Civil War Sites

A guided tour of Petersburg’s Civil War sites is a must-do activity for history buffs. Visitors can explore battlefields, historic landmarks, and museums while learning about the Civil War.

Virginia State University Art Center

The Virginia State University Art Center showcases local artists, historical artifacts, and contemporary artwork. Visitors can attend lectures, workshops, and exhibitions throughout the year.

Friday for the Arts!

Friday for the Arts! is a monthly event that highlights local and regional artists, musicians, and performers. Visitors can explore the city’s art galleries, museums, and historic landmarks while enjoying live music, food, and drinks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Petersburg

Visitors can enjoy a weeklong stay in Petersburg, with something new to explore every day. The following is our suggested itinerary for a week in Petersburg:

Day 1: Explore the Petersburg National Battlefield Day 2: Visit the Siege Museum and learn about the Civil War’s longest siege Day 3: Hike or bike the Appomattox River Trail Day 4: Explore the Centre Hill Museum Day 5: Enjoy a picnic and kayaking trip at Swift Creek Reservoir Day 6: Attend Friday for the Arts! and explore local galleries and museums Day 7: Visit Pocahontas State Park for hiking and outdoor recreation activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Petersburg

Petersburg has a rich and diverse culinary scene that combines Southern comfort food with international flavors. Visitors can enjoy fresh seafood, barbeque, and farm-to-table cuisine, amongst other options. Here’s what you need to know about the food and drink scene in Petersburg:

Must-Try Dishes

Don’t miss out on the following classic Petersburg dishes:

  • Fried chicken with mac and cheese
  • Shrimp and grits
  • Garlic-rubbed rib-eye steak
  • She-crab soup

Local Beverages

Petersburg boasts a variety of craft breweries, wineries, and distilleries. Visitors can enjoy locally brewed beer, fine wines, and distilled spirits.

Where to Stay in Petersburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Petersburg has plenty of options for accommodations, from cozy inns to modern hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Petersburg:

Hilton Richmond-Petersburg

The Hilton Richmond-Petersburg is a luxurious hotel that offers modern amenities, stylish decor, and convenience to local attractions.

The Jury’s Inn

The Jury’s Inn is a charming and budget-friendly option for travelers. The inn is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms and friendly hospitality.

The Ragland Mansion

The Ragland Mansion is an elegant bed and breakfast that dates back to 1860. The manor has been restored to its original grandeur and features antiques, fine art, and period furnishings.

What to Buy in Petersburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Petersburg has several unique shopping destinations that offer locally made products and souvenirs. The following are some of the best spots to buy souvenirs and local products:

Petersburg Pickers

Petersburg Pickers is a vintage store that offers antiques, collectibles, and unique items from local vendors. Visitors can find everything from vintage clothing, furniture, and paintings.

Sycamore Rouge

Sycamore Rouge is a gift shop that offers locally made products, souvenirs, and gifts. Visitors can find items such as handmade jewelry, candles, and pottery, amongst other unique items.

Local Culture and Customs of Petersburg

Petersburg has a rich and diverse cultural heritage that reflects its history and traditions. Visitors can enjoy local festivals, music, and customs while exploring the city. Here are some of the local cultures and customs of Petersburg:

Juneteenth Celebration

Juneteenth is a popular celebration that honors the emancipation of African American slaves. The event features music, food, and cultural performances and is held every June.

Old Towne Arts and Crafts Festival

The Old Towne Arts and Crafts Festival is an annual event that showcases local and regional artists, musicians, and performers. The festival takes place every May and offers a great opportunity to experience local arts and culture.

Best Time to Visit Petersburg

The best time to visit Petersburg is during the fall season and spring. The weather is moderate and comfortable, and visitors can enjoy the beautiful fall foliage and spring blooms.

Safety and Health

Safety in Petersburg

Petersburg is a safe city but visitors need to be cautious as some areas are not tourist-friendly. It is recommended to avoid any secluded areas after dark.

Health in Petersburg

Visitors to Petersburg are advised to take normal precautions to prevent illness, especially during pandemics. Check health guidelines before traveling, and wear a mask and practice social distancing when required.
