Introduction to Chesapeake

Located in Eastern Virginia, Chesapeake is a versatile city that offers a perfect combination of modernity and history. Surrounded by natural beauty and packed with outdoor activities, Chesapeake is a perfect vacation destination for both adventure seekers and history enthusiasts. In this travel guide, we will be exploring all the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and more in Chesapeake.

List of Places to Visit in Chesapeake

Whether you’re interested in history or outdoor adventure, Chesapeake has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the top places you should consider visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Great Dismal Swamp National Wildlife Refuge: This natural paradise offers 111,200 acres of pristine wilderness that’s home to black bears, bobcats, and a wealth of bird species.
  • Chesapeake City Park: A beautiful park with playgrounds for kids, walking trails, athletic fields and picnic shelters, Chesapeake City Park is a great spot for a family day out.
  • Chesapeake Arboretum: Set across 48 acres, the Chesapeake Arboretum is a nature preserve and park offering walkways, gardens and educational tours.
  • Chesapeake Planetarium: For space enthusiasts, the Chesapeake Planetarium offers a immersive virtual tour of space and the solar system.
  • Riddick’s Folly House Museum: This beautiful antebellum mansion offers visitors a glimpse into the life of those who lived in Chesapeake during the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Chesapeake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Northwest River Park & Campground: Whether you’re an avid camper or just looking for a fun day trip, the Northwest River Park & Campground offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and even cabins for rent.
  • Cedar Hill Cemetery: This historic graveyard is a peaceful and eye-opening visit, showcasing Chesapeake’s past.
  • Elizabeth River Trail: A beautiful trail with rolling hills and gorgeous views of the river, the Elizabeth River Trail is perfect for bicyclists, runners, and walkers.
  • Dismal Swamp Canal Trail: This scenic trail offers over 8 miles of gorgeous views and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chesapeake

For outdoor enthusiasts, Chesapeake has an abundance of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Chesapeake Arboretum Trails
  • Northwest River Park Trails
  • Dismal Swamp Canal Trail
  • Elizabeth River Trail

Best Things to Do in Chesapeake: Activities and Tours

From kayaking to wildlife watching, Chesapeake has plenty of activities to keep you occupied during your visit. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Kayaking on the Elizabeth River: With several outfitters to choose from, kayaking on the Elizabeth River is a must-do activity.
  • Fishing: Whether on a local charter boat or your own, fishing in the Chesapeake Bay is top-notch.
  • Golfing: Chesapeake is home to several exceptional golf courses, providing you with a chance to work on your swing while surrounded by beautiful landscapes.
  • Tasting local craft breweries: The Chesapeake area is home to a handful of craft breweries, where you can enjoy local brews and tasty food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chesapeake

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Chesapeake:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Great Dismal Swamp National Wildlife Refuge and enjoy a nature walk.
Day 2 Visit the Chesapeake City Park for a family picnic and playground fun.
Day 3 Head to the Cedar Hill Cemetery and learn about Chesapeake’s past.
Day 4 Explore the Northwest River Park & Campground and take a hike through the trails.
Day 5 Rent a kayak and paddle down the Elizabeth River.
Day 6 Take a scenic bike ride on the Dismal Swamp Canal Trail.
Day 7 Enjoy a day at Chesapeake’s best golf courses.

Food and Drink Scene of Chesapeake

When it comes to food and drinks, Chesapeake is home to a handful of must-try dishes and brews.

Must-Try Dishes

  • She Crab Soup: A local specialty, Chesapeake’s she crab soup is similar to clam chowder but made with crab meat and cream.
  • Soft-Shell Crab Sandwich: Served on a bun or as part of a wrap or salad, Chesapeake’s soft-shell crab is a true delicacy.
  • Ham and Biscuits: Often served at brunch or as a snack, ham and biscuits are a southern staple that can’t be missed.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: With an abundance of local craft breweries, Chesapeake is home to a bustling craft beer scene.
  • Ginger Ale: A ginger ale from Blenheim Ginger Ale Company is a must-try when in Chesapeake.

Local Culture and Customs of Chesapeake

Chesapeake’s culture is shaped by both its history in farming and its proximity to the ocean. The city is also known for its appreciation of the arts.

Best Time to Visit Chesapeake

The best time to visit Chesapeake is during spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chesapeake

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Chesapeake:

Do:

  • Rent a kayak or bike and explore Chesapeake’s natural wonders
  • Try local beers and seafood dishes
  • Visit local museums and historical sites

Don’t:

  • Ignore the weather forecast
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Chesapeake

Chesapeake is a relatively safe city with little violent crime. However, like any city, it’s important to be cautious and aware of your surroundings while walking or driving at night.

Health in Chesapeake

Chesapeake has several hospitals and clinics that provide quality medical care.

How to Get Around in Chesapeake

While renting a car is the most convenient way of getting around in Chesapeake, there are also several other options available, including taxis, ride-share services, and public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Chesapeake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Virginia Beach: Famous for its boardwalk, amusement parks, and pristine beaches, Virginia Beach is just a short drive away from Chesapeake.
  • Norfolk: With several museums and landmarks to visit, Norfolk is a must-visit for history lovers.
  • Williamsburg: For a taste of colonial history, Williamsburg is just a short drive away, offering some of the best historical sites in Virginia.

Conclusion

Chesapeake is a wonderful vacation destination full of natural beauty, rich history, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in kayaking, hiking, or just dining on delicious seafood, Chesapeake has something for everyone. Use this guide to create your perfect itinerary and make the most out of your visit to Chesapeake.
