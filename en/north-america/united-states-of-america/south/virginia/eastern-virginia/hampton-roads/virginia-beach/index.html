Introduction to Virginia Beach

Virginia Beach is a coastal city situated in Hampton Roads in Eastern Virginia, United States of America. This popular tourist destination boasts 35 miles of sandy beaches and a wide array of attractions, including museums, hiking and biking trails, and aquatic adventures. Moreover, Virginia Beach is home to the Military Aviation Museum, First Landing State Park, and Town Center, which offer visitors a chance to learn more about the history, culture, and bustling life that Virginia Beach is famous for.

List of Places to Visit in Virginia Beach

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Military Aviation Museum: Explore the world’s largest private collection of World War I and World War II aircraft, including the restored Curtiss JN-4D Jenny biplane from 1917.
  • First Landing State Park: Take a stroll on the white sandy beaches of Chesapeake Bay, the site where colonists first landed in 1607.
  • Virginia Aquarium & Marine Science Center: Discover underwater life and creatures up close with hands-on exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Cape Henry Lighthouse: Climb 191 steps up the lighthouse to get an impressive view of the Bay and the Atlantic Ocean.

Natural Attractions of Virginia Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandbridge Beach: A secluded, quiet beach where visitors can enjoy swimming, surfing, and fishing.
  • Back Bay National Wildlife Refuge: An 8,000-acre refuge for local birds and wildlife, visitors can enjoy bike and walking trails, kayaking and paddleboarding.
  • False Cape State Park: Hike or bike 6 miles along scenic trails, fish or camp out near the ocean or bay under the starlit sky.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Virginia Beach

  • First Landing State Park: Offers 20 miles of hiking trails with sea creatures, sand dunes, wetlands, and bald eagles to observe.
  • Mount Trashmore Park: Enjoy an urban environment with cycling trails and paths around Lake Trashmore
  • Seashore State Park: Tackle the state’s longest coastlines hiking route which offers 2884ft paths, picturesque beach views and seagull spotting.

Best Things to Do in Virginia Beach: Activities and Tours

  • Virginia Beach Boardwalk: A three-mile boardwalk that connects hotels, restaurants, and shops along the beach. Enjoy great views and live music performances.
  • Dolphin Watching Tours: Sail along with your loved ones and witness the amazing creatures of the ocean. The boat tours run from Rudee Inlet to the Atlantic Ocean and back.
  • Surfing Lessons: Sign up for a surfing lesson at one of the many surf schools in Virginia Beach. Unleash your hidden surfer talent or sit back and watch the experts catch a wave in action.

Food and Drink Scene of Virginia Beach

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chesapeake Bay Blue Crab: A local favorite, the Chesapeake Bay blue crab is a sweet, succulent delicacy that is served with Old Bay seasoning.
  • Clam Chowder: Whether you prefer it creamy or tomato-based, clam chowder is a signature dish of Virginia Beach that is a must-try.
  • Oyster Roasts: Head to any local seafood shack and order a dozen oysters or a whole roast. It’s a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • An area renowned for craft beers, the “757” brew stands out amongst the locals. Try the local distinct flavor, The Oatmeal Porter from local brewer, Smartmouth Brewing.
  • If you’re also a coffee enthusiast, head over to Three Ships Coffee Roasters and try their famous beans roasted locally.

  • Virginia Peanuts: A Virginia Beach specialty, these peanuts bring new flavor to the palate.
  • Surf and beach: Head over to the local markets or shops to score kitschy beachwear or surf-inspired home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Virginia Beach

The locals are proud and extremely welcoming. Don’t hesitate to strike up a friendly conversation or ask for recommendations to take full advantage of the rich culture and local customs.

Best Time to Visit Virginia Beach

The best time to visit Virginia Beach is between late spring and early fall when the beaches are warm and the shore breeze is cool. Keep in mind the possibility of occasional rain showers during the hurricane season months of August and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Virginia Beach

Do:

  • Respect the ocean rules and guidelines while on the beach.
  • Explore historical places, parks, and natural trails for a scenic view of the city.
  • Tip your servers and guides appropriately, showing your appreciation.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or the streets.
  • Cause noise disturbance in public areas or residential areas while traveling in groups.
  • Ignore traffic and parking regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Virginia Beach

Virginia Beach maintains a significantly low crime rate. However, it’s advisable always to be alert and keep personal belongings safe, particularly when tourists visit busy areas or crowded restaurants.

Health in Virginia Beach

Virginia Beach’s warm weather and high humidity can cause dehydration, sunstroke, or mosquito-borne diseases. Ensure to carry suitable protective gear, including sunscreen and drink plenty of fluids when exploring tourist attractions.

Getting to Virginia Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Virginia Beach

  • Norfolk International Airport: Situated about 20 minutes from Virginia Beach, this is the nearest airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Virginia Beach

  • Hampton Roads Transit: This transport service offers local bus rides and regional connections to other parts of Hampton Roads.

Other Options

  • Taxis: A taxi ride is an excellent option if you prefer a more comfortable, private mode of transportation.
  • Rental Car: Renting a car is ideal for visitors who want more flexibility in transportation, particularly if you want to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Virginia Beach

Virginia Beach is well-connected with an efficient transport network. One can get around via public transport buses, bike rentals, taxis, or rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Virginia Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Norfolk: Home to world-famous attractions and museums like the Battleship Wisconsin and the Chrysler Museum, this city is just a 20-minute drive from Virginia Beach.
  • Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel: This 23 miles stretch connects Virginia Beach to the DelMarVa Peninsula, and it’s a pleasant drive through scenic views.
  • Colonial Williamsburg: Take a step back in time and witness the daily life of colonists in this restoration of a colonial American city, situated approximately 43 miles from Virginia Beach.

Conclusion

Virginia Beach is the ideal destination for travelers who appreciate outdoor excursions, scenic sites, culture, history, and fantastic dining experiences. From free attractions like state parks to foodie experiences, Virginia Beach caters to tourists of all ages, interests, and travel preferences. Plan your next vacation to Virginia Beach and take advantage of its natural beauty and local charm.
