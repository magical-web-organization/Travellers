Introduction to Portsmouth

Welcome to Portsmouth, a charming city located in Eastern Virginia, in the United States of America. Known for its rich history, cultural diversity, natural beauty, and delicious food scene, Portsmouth is truly a hidden gem just waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, food lover, or simply looking for a dose of southern hospitality, Portsmouth has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Portsmouth, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, local culture, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Portsmouth

There are plenty of amazing attractions to explore in Portsmouth, from museums and historic sites to scenic parks and outdoor adventures. Here are some must-visit places in Portsmouth.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Portsmouth Naval Shipyard Museum: This museum takes visitors through the city’s historic shipbuilding past. The museum is located in the original 19th-century office building of the largest and oldest naval shipyard in the country.

  • Portsmouth Art & Cultural Center: Housed within a restored 1846 courthouse, this center features rotating exhibits galleries, and public programs. Anchored as home base for the Virginia Arts Festival each spring.

  • City of Portsmouth Museums: This group includes the Hill House Museum, Lightship Portsmouth Museum, and the Portsmouth Colored Community Library Museum. The Hill House Museum serves as the main museum and includes the offices of the Portsmouth Museums.

  • The Virginia Sports Hall of Fame and Museum: This museum provides an opportunity to learn about Virginia’s athletic history and the many famous sports stars that have hailed from the state.

Natural Attractions of Portsmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Portsmouth City Park: This park offers visitors a mix of natural features including sports fields, nature trails, picnic areas and a lake perfect for fishing or paddle boats.

  • The Elizabeth River Ferry: The Elizabeth River Ferry is the best way to experience the Elizabeth River and explore the surrounding area. The ferry takes passengers from Portsmouth to Norfolk, with stops offering a unique vantage point of the historic Portsmouth Naval Shipyard and breathtaking views of the downtown Norfolk skyline.

  • Portsmouth Olde Towne Farmer’s Market: Local farmers, artisans, and vendors gather here to offer a unique variety of locally grown and made offerings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portsmouth

Portsmouth and the surrounding area offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation, from scenic hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Portsmouth:

  • Elizabeth River Trail: The trail is an 11.5 mile long pedestrian and bicycle path that traverses five cities along the Elizabeth River that can be accessed from the city park in Portsmouth. Offering stunning views of the river and skyline, this trail offers an ideal pathway for leisurely biking and strolling.

Best Things to Do in Portsmouth: Activities and Tours

In addition to the many attractions and outdoor adventures, Portsmouth also offers a variety of activities and tours that are worth checking out. Here are some of the best things to do in Portsmouth:

  • Ghost Tour of Olde Towne Portsmouth: Join in the guided tour around Old Towne Portsmouth for a spooky experience. Learn about the area’s haunted history as you walk through the city’s landmarks experienced staff.
  • Portsmouth Brewery Tours: Sample the local brews and learn about the brewing process behind them with Portsmouth Brewery tours. Enjoy educational tastings and even get to visit the facilities where the beer is made.
  • Discover Portsmouth Walking Tour: Take in the beauty of Portsmouth’s historic landmarks, beautiful homes, and outdoor sculptures on this walking tour. Knowledgeable guides will provide historical, architectural, and cultural insights along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Portsmouth

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Portsmouth, you’ll find plenty of things to see and do. Here are some itinerary ideas to help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1: Visit the Portsmouth Naval Shipyard Museum to learn about the history of shipbuilding. Enjoy the city park as you take a stroll, enjoy some fishing or take a paddle boat ride by the lake.

Day 2: Explore the City of Portsmouth Museums, including the Hill House Museum and the Library Museum. Take a scenic walking tour of the Olde Towne district later in the day.

Day 3: Take the Elizabeth River Ferry to Norfolk and explore the famous Norfolk Botanical Gardens, a local gem that features beautiful gardens and winding nature walks.

Day 4: Enjoy the Downtown Portsmouth Cultural Arts District and galleries, and catch a show or performance at the Virginia Symphony Orchestra.

Day 5-7: Spend the last few days of your trip enjoying outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and biking on the Elizabeth River Trail and in the city parks.

Food and Drink Scene of Portsmouth

One of the best ways to experience Portsmouth’s local culture and traditions is by trying the local food and drinks. From seafood classics to inventive brews, Portsmouth has a lot to offer for foodies. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Virginia Ham Biscuits: This dish features Virginia’s famous ham on a buttery biscuit with cheese and jelly. It’s the perfect breakfast or snack.

  • Crab Cakes: Virginia is known for its luscious crab cakes. Served fried or broiled, you can’t go wrong with this local favorite.

  • Old Bay Wings: Seasoned with a blend of spices from Maryland, Old Bay Wings is a regional favorite that packs a punch.

Local Beverages

  • Gosling’s Black Seal Rum: This rum has been brewed in Bermuda since 1857 and is used in many of the area’s popular rum cocktails like the Dark and Stormy.

  • Natty Boh: Short for National Bohemian, this beer has become a regional icon.

  • Wineries: Virginia produces a wide range of wines, and you can sample and take home the best varieties. Different vineyards include the Chateau Morrisette, Gray Ghost, and Horton.

Where to Stay in Portsmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels, Portsmouth offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Portsmouth:

  • Bowers Hill Inn: Budget-friendly, comfortable, and conveniently located within driving distance to Portsmouth and the surrounding area.

  • Holiday Inn Portsmouth: Located in the heart of Portsmouth, this hotel offers modern amenities and proximity to attractions such as museums, restaurants, and shopping.

  • Renaissance Portsmouth-Norfolk Hotel: If you’re looking for a luxurious and indulgent stay in Portsmouth, this hotel is the perfect choice. This facility features restaurants, and spa services.

What to Buy in Portsmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to remember your trip to Portsmouth by, there’s plenty to choose from. Here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Peanuts: Virginia is famous for its peanuts, and there are plenty of different varieties and flavors to choose from. Head to Mount Hope Farms for the best offerings.

  • Pottery: Portsmouth is home to a thriving community of artists and craftsmen that produce beautiful and unique pottery. You can find a wide selection of hand-made artisan pottery at the Olde Towne Arts District.

Local Culture and Customs of Portsmouth

Portsmouth is a blend of different cultures and traditions, with strong influences from the military, farming, and the music scene. One of the most notable aspects of Portsmouth’s local culture is the emphasis on hospitality and a welcoming attitude toward visitors.

Best Time to Visit Portsmouth

The best time to visit Portsmouth is in spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months can get hot and humid which is not conducive to a pleasant trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portsmouth

It’s essential to be respectful of the local culture and customs when visiting Portsmouth, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Say “yes’m” and “no’m” which is acceptable to the locals for “Yes, ma’am” or “No, ma’am”.

  • Be respectful of religious, cultural, and racial differences as Portsmouth has people of different stripes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t assume that the friendly southern hospitality is necessarily flirtatious or offensive.

  • Don’t leave without patronizing the local businesses, since these will enable them to thrive and add to the unique spirit of this community.

Safety and Health

As with anywhere, it’s essential to take care of your safety and health while visiting Portsmouth.

Safety in Portsmouth

For staying safe in Portsmouth, make sure to:

  • Avoid unauthorized entrances and back alleys.

  • Keep valuables and cash locked up in your hotel room safe.

  • Be aware and respectful of the local laws, such as smoking bans and proper alcohol consumption.

Health in Portsmouth

To stay healthy while enjoying Portsmouth, be sure to:

  • Stay hydrated with lots of water.

  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when needed

  • Watch out for ticks since certain parts of Portsmouth contain wooded areas.

Getting to Portsmouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Portsmouth is conveniently located in the Eastern Virginia area with easy accessibility through transportation.

Airports Near Portsmouth

The nearest airport to Portsmouth is the Norfolk International Airport, located approximately 15 minutes away from downtown Portsmouth. Numerous flights operate from the airport, making it easy to connect with other cities in the US.

Bus Stations Near Portsmouth

The Hampton Roads Transit Company runs buses throughout Portsmouth and surrounding cities. Check, the local bus stations for schedules and routes.

Other Options

The most reliable way to get around Portsmouth and nearby areas is by cab/car or bike rentals.

How to Get Around in Portsmouth

Whether you’re looking to explore the city by foot, bicycle, or public transportation, Portsmouth offers plenty of options for getting around.

  • Pedestrian walkways are provided along the roadways.

  • Hampton Roads Transit (HRT) operates buses. Specific timetables available for visitors to plan accordingly.

  • Taxi services are easy to use, readily available, and offer efficient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Portsmouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Portsmouth, there are plenty of exciting destinations to explore in the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Virginia Beach: A vibrant and lively seaside resort, Virginia Beach offers plenty of outdoor activities, water sports, nightlife, and entertainment.

  • Norfolk: Norfolk is Virginia’s second largest city and the home to the renowned Norfolk Botanical Gardens, museums, and many other attractions.

  • Chesapeake Bay: The estuary of the Chesapeake Bay is a beautiful maritime paradise that offers sailing, paddle boating, kayaking and more.

Conclusion

With its rich history, cultural diversity, natural beauty, and delicious food scene, Portsmouth is a destination that is not to be missed. From the historic shipyard museums and walking tours to scenic parks and outdoor adventures, you’ll find plenty of things to see and do here. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Portsmouth, and that you’ll soon be exploring all that this charming city has to offer.
