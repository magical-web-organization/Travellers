Introduction to Westmoreland County

Westmoreland County is located in Eastern Virginia, in the United States. The county has a rich history and is known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive tour guide will show you the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more in Westmoreland County.

List of Places to Visit in Westmoreland County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Westmoreland County has a rich history that is reflected in its museums and historical places. Some of the top attractions in this category are:

  • George Washington Birthplace National Monument: This monument is a tribute to the first President of the United States, George Washington. It includes the home site where he was born, a museum, and a replica of the Washington family’s 18th-century farm.
  • Stratford Hall: The birthplace of Robert E. Lee, Stratford Hall is a historic house museum that showcases the history of one of Virginia’s most prominent families.
  • Westmoreland State Park: This state park is home to the Westmoreland Berry Farm, which offers year-round activities including pick-your-own berries, a pumpkin patch, and a Christmas tree farm.

Natural Attractions of Westmoreland County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Westmoreland County is home to a diverse range of natural attractions that offer outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the top natural attractions in the county include:

  • A.W. Jones Heritage Center and Nature Trail: This nature trail is home to several historic sites, including the ancestral home of Captain John Smith. The trail offers a scenic view of the Potomac River and is a popular spot for hiking and bird watching.
  • Caledon State Park: This park is a wildlife refuge that is home to over 90 species of birds. It offers hiking trails, kayaking, and fishing opportunities.
  • James Monroe Birthplace: This historical site features a replica of James Monroe’s birthplace, as well as a nature trail that offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westmoreland County

Westmoreland County is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several trails in the county that offer beautiful views of the natural scenery. Some of the top hiking/biking routes in the county are:

  • Westmoreland Berry Farm Nature Trail: This trail is located within the Westmoreland Berry Farm and is a popular spot for hiking and bird watching.
  • Potomac Heritage National Scenic Trail: This trail stretches over 700 miles through Virginia and other nearby states. In Westmoreland County, it offers scenic views of the Potomac River and opportunities for hiking and biking.
  • Belle Isle State Park Trail: This trail is a 3.1-mile loop that offers scenic views of the Rappahannock River and opportunities for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Westmoreland County: Activities and Tours

Westmoreland County has a wide range of activities and tours to offer visitors. Some of the top things to do in the county are:

  • Westmoreland State Park: This park offers year-round activities, including fishing, hiking, and boating. Visitors can also enjoy a picnic, swim in the park’s swimming pool, or set up camp in one of the park’s campsites.
  • Lancaster Farmers Market: This farmers market features a wide range of fresh produce and locally made products.
  • Virginia Wine Tour: Westmoreland County is home to several wineries that offer wine tastings and tours. Some of the top wineries in the county include Ingleside Vineyards and Hague Winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Westmoreland County

Must-Try Dishes

Westmoreland County is known for its delicious Southern-style cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes in the county are:

  • Fried chicken: A Southern classic that is often served with mashed potatoes and gravy.
  • Crab cakes: Made with fresh blue crab meat, these cakes are a local specialty.
  • Brunswick stew: A hearty, slow-cooked soup that typically contains chicken, corn, and lima beans.

Local Beverages

Westmoreland County also has a rich brewing and distilling tradition. Some of the top local beverages in the county include:

  • Colonial Beach Brewing: This brewery offers a range of craft beers, including porters, stouts, and IPAs.
  • A. Smith Bowman Distillery: This distillery produces a range of award-winning bourbons and other spirits.

Where to Stay in Westmoreland County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Westmoreland County has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top places to stay in the county are:

  • The Tides Inn: Located on the banks of the Rappahannock River, The Tides Inn is a luxury hotel that offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding countryside. The hotel features a spa, golf course, and several dining options.
  • The Inn at Stratford Hall: This inn is located on the grounds of Stratford Hall and offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a pool and tennis courts.
  • Belle Isle State Park Cabins: These rustic cabins are located within Belle Isle State Park and offer a quiet, secluded place to stay in the heart of nature.

What to Buy in Westmoreland County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Westmoreland County is known for its local products, including handmade crafts, souvenirs, and food products. Some of the top things to buy in the county are:

  • Crab-related products, including seasonings, mallets, and crab-themed gift items.
  • Homemade jellies and jams made from locally grown fruit.
  • Handmade pottery and other crafts made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Westmoreland County

Westmoreland County has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its history, traditions, and way of life. The county is known for its Southern hospitality and friendly locals, who take pride in their heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Westmoreland County

The best time to visit Westmoreland County is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. Summer is also a popular time to visit, especially for outdoor activities, but it can be hot and humid.

Getting to Westmoreland County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westmoreland County

The closest major airport to Westmoreland County is Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport, which is located approximately 60 miles northeast of the county.

Bus Stations Near Westmoreland County

The closest bus station to Westmoreland County is the Greyhound bus station in Richmond, which is approximately 50 miles northwest of the county.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Westmoreland County by car, taking Interstate 95 and then Highway 3 to reach the county.

How to Get Around in Westmoreland County

The best way to get around Westmoreland County is by car. There are several car rental companies located in nearby cities, such as Fredericksburg and Richmond.

Where to Go Next from Westmoreland County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Westmoreland County is located within easy driving distance of several other popular destinations, including:

  • Colonial Beach: Located on the banks of the Potomac River, Colonial Beach is a popular summer destination that offers swimming, boating, and other outdoor activities.
  • Fredericksburg: This historic city is located approximately 30 miles north of Westmoreland County and features a range of museums, historic sites, and shopping destinations.
  • Virginia Beach: Located approximately 120 miles southeast of Westmoreland County, Virginia Beach is a popular destination for beachgoers and offers a wide range of activities and attractions.

Conclusion
