Introduction to Eastern Shore

Eastern Shore is a beautiful peninsula located in Eastern Virginia, South United States of America, and lies on the Atlantic Coast. It is surrounded by water on three sides and is known for its natural beauty, historic attractions, unique culture, and scenic vistas. The Eastern Shore of Virginia has been inhabited by Native Americans for thousands of years, and is now home to some of the most popular tourist destinations in Virginia.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Shore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cape Charles Museum and Welcome Center

The Cape Charles Museum and Welcome Center is a great place to learn about the history of the town and the Eastern Shore. It houses artifacts, photos, and documents depicting the area’s past, including the railway industry, the oyster industry, and the ferry service. It also has a scale model of the Chesapeake Bay Bridge Tunnel and teaches visitors about the ecology of the Chesapeake Bay.

Chincoteague Island

Chincoteague Island is a beautiful barrier island that is part of the Chincoteague Wildlife Refuge, where visitors can catch a glimpse of wild ponies, enjoy water sports, or spend the day at the beach. The island is also a great place to learn about the local fishing industry and enjoy delicious seafood.

Barrier Islands Center

The Barrier Islands Center is housed in a restored schoolhouse and contains exhibits on the life of Tangier’s watermen, as well as on the area’s ecology and history. Visitors can watch boat builders at work, see old photographs, and learn about the life of the barrier island residents.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Shore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Assateague Island National Seashore

Assateague Island National Seashore is a beautiful coastal area that stretches from Maryland to Virginia and is home to wild horses, sandy beaches, and marshlands. Visitors can hike, bike, kayak, or simply relax and enjoy the scenery.

Kiptopeke State Park

Kiptopeke State Park is located at the mouth of the Chesapeake Bay and is one of the best bird-watching locations in the Eastern Shore. It also has hiking trails, fishing spots, and scenic views of the bay.

Wallops Island

Wallops Island is a NASA launch site where visitors can view rocket launches and learn about space exploration. They can also hike, bird-watch, and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Shore

The Eastern Shore of Virginia has several hiking and biking trails that visitors can explore, including:

  • The Eastern Shore Bike Trail- a 70-mile bike trail from Cape Charles to Chincoteague Island
  • The Assateague Island Bike Trail- a 1.5-mile bike trail on the barrier island
  • The Savage Neck Dunes Natural Area Preserve- a hiking trail that leads to a beautiful beach

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Shore

Day 1: Arrive in Cape Charles and explore the town. Stay at Hotel Cape Charles.

Day 2: Take a ferry to Tangier Island and spend the day exploring the island. Stay at Tangier Island Bed and Breakfast.

Day 3: Visit the Chincoteague Wildlife Refuge and observe the wild ponies. Stay at Best Western Chincoteague.

Day 4: Visit Assateague Island National Seashore and go hiking or biking on the trails. Stay at Island Resort.

Day 5: Explore the Eastern Shore Railway Museum and sample local seafood. Stay at The Chesapeake Club.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive along Route 13 and visit the Wallops Island Visitors Center. Stay at Sunset Beach Resort.

Day 7: Visit Kiptopeke State Park, go bird-watching or hiking, and watch the sunset over the Chesapeake Bay.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Shore

The Eastern Shore is home to a unique culture that is influenced by its location, history, and architecture. The residents of the Eastern Shore are friendly and welcoming, and visitors can expect to be greeted with southern hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Shore

The best time to visit the Eastern Shore is from late spring to early fall when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, the fall season also offers beautiful foliage views and the opportunity to see migrating birds.

  • Visit the local farmers’ markets

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Disturb the wild horses
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in public places
  • Leave valuables in your car

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Shore

The Eastern Shore is generally a safe and peaceful place to visit. However, visitors should be aware of the usual safety precautions such as keeping an eye on personal belongings, lock your hotel room, avoid unlit areas at night and know the emergency contacts.

Health in Eastern Shore

There are many healthcare facilities in the Eastern Shore, including urgent care centers, pharmacies, and hospitals. Visitors are advised to have health insurance and carry a first aid kit. Water in the area is safe to drink.
