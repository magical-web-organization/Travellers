Introduction to Floyd

Floyd, nestled in the heart of the Blue Ridge Mountains of Southwest Virginia, is a charming and picturesque town that offers visitors a taste of mountain music, outdoor adventure, and local culture. This small town has a big personality and holds many exciting surprises for tourists visiting Virginia. In this comprehensive travel guide for Floyd, we have outlined everything you need to plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Floyd

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Floyd County Historical Society Museum is a great place to take a glimpse into the area’s past. The museum showcases various exhibits that detail the county’s history, from prehistoric times to present-day. The museum building itself is of historical significance as it served as Floyd’s first courthouse and county jail.

The Floyd Country Store is a must-visit if you are looking to experience the authentic local culture of Floyd. The store features a wide range of products, including musical instruments, clothing, and homemade goods. During your visit, be sure to catch a live show at the store’s famous Friday Night Jamboree.

Natural Attractions of Floyd: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Blue Ridge Parkway, known as “America’s Favorite Drive,” crosses through the Floyd area, providing endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Visitors can embark on scenic hikes, including the Rocky Knob Trail and Buffalo Mountain Trail. The panoramic views of the mountains and valleys are breathtaking and should not be missed.

The Chateau Morrisette Winery offers both wine tasting and guided winery tours. The winery, which sits atop of the Blue Ridge Mountains, features a beautiful vineyard and fantastic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Floyd

Floyd’s scenic trails offer tourists an exciting way to connect with the great outdoors. The Rocky Knob Trail and Buffalo Mountain Trail, mentioned earlier, are both excellent options for hiking, with varying degrees of difficulty.

One popular route for bikers is the Blue Ridge Parkway. This bicycle-friendly parkway offers breathtaking views and many picturesque stops along the way.

Best Things to Do in Floyd: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, other exciting activities include fishing, rafting, and kayaking on Little River. For art lovers, visiting the Jacksonville Center for the Arts is a must-do. The center features a revolving series of visual arts exhibitions and hosts exciting art events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Floyd

Food and Drink Scene of Floyd

Must-Try Dishes

Floyd’s food scene is rooted in traditional southern cuisine with a dash of international flavors. The Floyd Country Store offers classic American fare such as burgers and fries, while Oddf3llows Cantina offers a fresh fusion of American and Mexican cuisine.

One popular dish in Floyd is the farm-fresh barbecue, which is slow-cooked to perfection and combined with tangy, delicious sauces.

Local Beverages

The Chateau Morrisette Winery, located on the Blue Ridge Parkway, offers wine tastings and serves the finest local wines. The winery also offers fantastic views of the surrounding area, which makes for an unforgettable experience. For beer lovers, Buffalo Mountain Brewery is a must-visit, offering delicious, locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Floyd: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Pine Tavern Lodge Hotel offers cozy rooms and reasonable accommodations. The Hotel Floyd offers comfortable guest rooms, making it a perfect place to stay for the weekend. For those looking for a more rustic experience, consider staying at the Bent Mountain Lodge, which offers exceptional amenities amid the mountain range.

What to Buy in Floyd: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Floyd’s vibrant arts and crafts community is famous throughout Virginia. You’ll find plenty of unique and hand-crafted items such as pottery, jewelry, and artwork at the Floyd Country Store. If you are seeking local food products to bring home, visit the Floyd Farmers Market for locally-sourced items.

Local Culture and Customs of Floyd

Floyd prides itself on its southern hospitality and traditions. Visitors should expect friendly locals and a welcoming atmosphere that showcases Virginia’s unique culture.

Best Time to Visit Floyd

The fall season is the best time to visit Floyd, as the trees change colors, and the weather is moderately cool. The winter season is also ideal for those looking for a winter wonderland experience, with snow-covered mountains, great skiing opportunities, and warm, cozy night accommodations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Floyd

Do:

  • Be respectful of the area’s natural resources by following the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Engage in various outdoor activities to get the most out of your trip.
  • Visit the Floyd Country Store and Chateau Morrisette Winery for an authentic experience.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the wildlife in the area.
  • Miss out on the local food such as farm-fresh barbecue and locally brewed beer.
  • Forget to bring your camera and capture the stunning mountain views.

Safety and Health

Safety in Floyd

Floyd is relatively safe and welcoming to tourists. However, visitors should always avoid leaving valuable items in their cars or unsecured accommodation rooms and should take precautions while enjoying outdoor activities.

Health in Floyd

Floyd does not have significant health concerns or standard public health emergencies; however, visitors should maintain proper hygiene practices to prevent the spread of infectious diseases.

Getting to Floyd: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Floyd

The Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport is the closest airport to Floyd, approximately one hour and a half’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Floyd

There are several bus stations in and around Floyd that connect to major cities, including the Roanoke Bus Station and the Blacksburg Bus Station.

Other Options

Driving to Floyd is a beautiful adventure. The town is accessible via Route 8, Route 221, and the Blue Ridge Parkway.

How to Get Around in Floyd

The best way to explore the area is by car or bike. Visitors can also take advantage of the public transportation system by using taxis or calling private taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Floyd: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations include natural attractions such as the Fairy Stone State Park, Smith Mountain Lake, and Peaks of Otter. For those interested in exploring other Virginia cities, Roanoke and Blacksburg offer rich cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Floyd, Virginia is a hidden gem in the heart of the Blue Ridge Mountains. This mountain town boasts of natural beauty, rich culture, and exceptional experiences that cater to all types of travelers. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are guaranteed an unforgettable trip filled with exciting adventures, cultural experiences, and stunning mountain views. So, plan your trip, pack your bags, and embark on a journey full of incredible memories.
