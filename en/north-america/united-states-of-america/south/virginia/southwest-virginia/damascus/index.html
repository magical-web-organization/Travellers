Introduction to Damascus

Nestled in the lush mountains of Southwest Virginia, Damascus is a charming town that boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and plenty of outdoor activities. Known as the “Trail Town USA,” it is the official headquarters of the Appalachian Trail and is a gateway to the Virginia Creeper Trail. From scenic views to cultural attractions, Damascus has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Damascus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Barter Theatre - Known as the State Theatre of Virginia, this is one of the nation’s longest-running professional theaters.
  • William King Museum of Art - This museum has an extensive collection of art exhibits focused on the Appalachian region and American folk art.
  • Abingdon Historic District - A charming, small-town feel with local restaurants, bakeries, and shops.

Natural Attractions of Damascus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Appalachian Trail - The official headquarters of the trail, and a hub for hikers from all over the world.
  • Virginia Creeper Trail - A multi-use trail that winds through the mountains of Southwest Virginia, it’s perfect for biking, hiking, and horseback riding.
  • Mount Rogers National Recreation Area - A sprawling natural reserve with hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic views.
  • Grayson Highlands State Park - Home to wild ponies, forests, and sweeping mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Damascus

Damascus is a dream destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, and the town offers a range of trails and routes for visitors of all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Appalachian Trail - Trailheads throughout town, including one at the center of Main Street.
  • Virginia Creeper Trail - 34 miles of scenic trail that starts in the town of Abingdon. Bikes can be rented from various local shops around town.
  • Feathercamp Trail- A trail that starts at the Appalachian Trail parking lot and leads to the great scenic views of Backbone Rock.

Best Things to Do in Damascus: Activities and Tours

  • Biking Adventures - Rent a bike and explore the Virginia Creeper Trail, or book a shuttle service to take you to the top of the trail for a leisurely downhill ride.
  • Outdoor Adventures - Go hiking, camping, fishing, or horseback riding in one of the many natural parks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Damascus

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Damascus, visit town center for shopping and dining.
2 Rent a bike and bike the Virginia Creeper Trail.
3 Hike the Appalachian Trail or visit the Grayson Highlands State Park.
4 Explore the local museums and galleries, including the William King Museum of Art.
5 Spend the day at the lake swimming, fishing, or kayaking.
6 Take a guided horseback riding tour through the mountains of Southwest Virginia.
7 Depart Damascus.

Food and Drink Scene of Damascus

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ - Barbecue is a staple of Southern cooking, and there are several restaurants that serve up slow-smoked, melt-in-your-mouth meats.
  • Country Ham - A salty, cured ham that’s popular in the area and served in many restaurants.
  • Biscuits and Gravy - A hearty breakfast that’s a classic in the South.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wines - The rolling hills of Southwest Virginia produce some of the finest wines in the state.
  • Local Craft Beers - The town has numerous microbreweries with unique brews on tap.

Where to Stay in Damascus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Thistlewood Guesthouse - Offers several cozy rooms, a garden area, and a hot tub.
  • The Lion & The Lamb Inn - Located in the heart of Damascus, this inn offers several unique rooms, including a room that’s a replica of a train car.
  • The Hikers Inn - A hostel designed specifically for hikers, it offers affordable accommodations and a comfortable place to rest after a long day of hiking.

What to Buy in Damascus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Crafts - From pottery and textiles to jewelry and handmade furniture, Damascus is a hub for local artisans.
  • Outdoor Gear - With so many outdoor activities available in the area, there are several shops that sell hiking and biking gear.
  • Appalachian Trail Souvenirs - Pick up a t-shirt, patch, or sticker to commemorate your visit to the official headquarters of the Appalachian Trail.

Local Culture and Customs of Damascus

Damascus is a town that prides itself on its Southern hospitality, friendly locals, and rich history. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town, appreciate the natural beauty, and immerse themselves in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Damascus

The best time to visit Damascus is during the spring or fall months when temperatures are mild, and the foliage is in full bloom. Summer is also a great time to visit if you enjoy outdoor activities, but expect higher temperatures and more tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Damascus

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Do visit the local museums and galleries to learn more about the town’s history and culture.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including country ham and biscuits and gravy.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations on things to do and see in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Damascus

Damascus is a small town, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should take standard precautions like locking car doors and avoiding dark, isolated areas at night.

Health in Damascus

Visitors should be aware of potential tick bites in the area, especially during the spring and summer months. Lyme disease is prevalent in the region.

Getting to Damascus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Damascus

  • Tri-Cities Regional Airport (TRI) - Located 52 miles away.
  • Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport (ROA) - Located 88 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Damascus

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Located in Abingdon, 16 miles from Damascus.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station - Located in Bristol, 25 miles from Damascus.
  • Car Rentals - Easily accessible from the nearby airports.

How to Get Around in Damascus

The best way to get around Damascus is on foot, bike, or car. Many restaurants and shops are located in the downtown area, making them easily accessible on foot. Bikes can be rented from local shops, and rental cars are available at nearby airports.

Where to Go Next from Damascus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Abingdon, Virginia - A nearby town with a rich history, charming downtown, and several cultural attractions.
  • Bristol, Tennessee/Virginia - A border town that straddles Tennessee and Virginia, Bristol is famous for being the birthplace of country music.
  • Shenandoah National Park - A stunning natural reserve that is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Damascus has something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions. With its small-town charm and friendly locals, it’s the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city. So start planning your trip now and experience the beauty of Southwest Virginia for yourself.
