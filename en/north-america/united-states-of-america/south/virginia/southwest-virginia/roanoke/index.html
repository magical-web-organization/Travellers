Introduction to Roanoke

Located in the heart of the Blue Ridge Mountains of Southwest Virginia, Roanoke is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its stunning natural scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture, Roanoke is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, foodies, and anyone looking for an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Roanoke

Roanoke has something to offer for everyone. Here are some places to add to your list while in Roanoke.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roanoke is home to a rich history and culture. To fully experience it, visit these museums and historical places:

  • The Taubman Museum of Art
  • The Virginia Museum of Transportation
  • The O. Winston Link Museum
  • The Mill Mountain Star and Park
  • The Roanoke Valley History Museum

Natural Attractions of Roanoke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Roanoke is surrounded by stunning natural beauty that offers endless outdoor adventure opportunities. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Roanoke:

  • The Blue Ridge Parkway
  • The Roanoke River Greenway
  • The Carvins Cove Natural Reserve
  • The Mill Mountain Zoo
  • The Natural Bridge of Virginia

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roanoke

The Blue Ridge Mountains offer some of the best hiking and biking trails in the world. Here are some of the best routes in Roanoke:

  • The Appalachian Trail
  • The Roanoke Valley Greenways
  • The Virginia Creeper Trail
  • The Dragon’s Tooth Trail
  • The McAfee Knob Trail

Best Things to Do in Roanoke: Activities and Tours

Roanoke offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to a wide range of interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Roanoke:

  • Take a food tour of downtown Roanoke
  • Explore the local craft beer scene with a brewery tour
  • Embark on a hot air balloon ride over the Blue Ridge Mountains
  • Visit the local farmers’ market for a taste of fresh produce
  • Take a guided kayaking tour of the Roanoke River

Week-Long Itinerary for Roanoke

Here is a week-long itinerary that includes all of the above:

Day Activity
1 Explore downtown Roanoke and visit the Taubman Museum of Art
2 Hike the Dragon’s Tooth Trail
3 Take a brewery tour and learn about the local craft beer scene
4 Go kayaking on the Roanoke River
5 Visit the Virginia Museum of Transportation and the O. Winston Link Museum
6 Drive the Blue Ridge Parkway and make stops at scenic overlooks
7 End the week with a hot air balloon ride over the Blue Ridge Mountains

Food and Drink Scene of Roanoke

When it comes to food and drink, Roanoke is a foodie’s paradise. From Southern comfort food to craft beers, here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried chicken and waffles at Scratch Biscuit Company
  • Pulled pork BBQ at Blue Ridge BBQ & Brew Festival
  • Virginia ham at The Regency Room
  • Peanut soup at The River and Rail
  • Sweet potato fries at The Green Goat

Local Beverages

  • Roanoke Railhouse Brewery’s Track 1 IPA
  • Starr Hill Brewery’s Northern Lights IPA
  • Three Notch’d Brewing Company’s Ghost of the 43rd Stout
  • Cardinal Gin from Virginia Distillery Company
  • Chateau Morrisette Winery’s Black Dog Red

What to Buy in Roanoke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Roanoke offers a variety of locally-made products that are perfect for souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Roanoke:

Local Culture and Customs of Roanoke

Roanoke has a rich culture that is heavily influenced by its history and natural beauty. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect the natural surroundings and leave no trace
  • Say “hello” to strangers as it is considered to be friendly
  • Be polite and respectful to locals
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activity at hand
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the places you visit

Best Time to Visit Roanoke

While Roanoke is a year-round destination, the best time to visit is during the fall when the leaves change colors, and the weather is mild. Spring is also a great time to visit for outdoor activities, while summer can be hot and crowded. Winter offers the perfect opportunity for skiing and snowboarding in nearby resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roanoke

Do:

  • Explore the natural surroundings
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Take a brewery tour or visit a winery
  • Visit the local museums
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the places you visit

Don’t:

  • Litter and leave no trace
  • Forget to tip at restaurants
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their customs
  • Ignore safety warnings and signage
  • Hesitate to ask for help or directions

Safety and Health

Safety in Roanoke

Roanoke is a safe city, but like anywhere else, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions. Lock your car and hotel room, avoid leaving valuables in your car, and be cautious while exploring the natural surroundings.

Health in Roanoke

Roanoke offers high-quality health care facilities, including multiple hospitals and medical clinics. It’s important to have international health insurance before visiting Roanoke.

Getting to Roanoke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roanoke

The nearest airport to Roanoke is Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport (ROA), which is located just 7 miles from the city center. Other nearby airports include Lynchburg Regional Airport (LYH) and Charlottesville Albemarle Airport (CHO).

Bus Stations Near Roanoke

The main bus station serving Roanoke is the Roanoke Transportation Center, which is located downtown. Greyhound, Megabus, and other regional carriers operate from this station.

Other Options

If you prefer driving, Roanoke is easily accessible from Interstate 81 and US Route 220. You can also take the Amtrak train to the Roanoke Amtrak Station.

How to Get Around in Roanoke

Roanoke is a small city that is easy to navigate. Walking, biking, and driving are popular options. Roanoke Valley Greenways offers over 25 miles of paved trails that are perfect for biking and walking.

Where to Go Next from Roanoke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, including:

  • The Natural Bridge of Virginia
  • The Homestead Resort in Hot Springs
  • The Virginia Safari Park in Natural Bridge
  • The Blue Ridge Parkway
  • The Shenandoah Valley

Conclusion

Roanoke is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious food and drink scene, there is something for everyone in Roanoke. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Roanoke is the perfect destination.
