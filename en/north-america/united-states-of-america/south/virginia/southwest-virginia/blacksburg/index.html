Introduction to Blacksburg

Blacksburg is a charming town located in Southwest Virginia, United States of America. Known for being the home of Virginia Tech, Blacksburg has a vibrant college town atmosphere with easy access to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Despite its relatively small size, there is an abundance of things to do, see, and explore in Blacksburg. With this travel guide, let’s dive into the top attractions, food and drink scene, where to stay, and travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Blacksburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Virginia Museum of Natural History

The Virginia Museum of Natural History is a must-see attraction for anyone interested in science and history. With over 22 million artifacts, exhibits showcase the natural wonders of Virginia and interpret the science of the natural world. The museum also offers year-round programs and special events, including lectures, workshops, and field trips.

Smithfield Plantation

Located on the Virginia Tech campus, Smithfield Plantation is a beautifully restored 1774 colonial home that stands as a tribute to the affluent Preston family. Guided tours are available to explore the house and its history through exhibits, oral histories, and a collection of personal belongings of the Preston family.

Natural Attractions of Blacksburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cascades Falls

Cascades Falls is a stunning waterfall located in the Jefferson National Forest. A hike on the 4-mile round-trip Cascade Falls Trail will lead you to the impressive 69-foot waterfall. With a clear pool at its base and picnic tables nearby, it’s a perfect spot to relax and enjoy nature.

Pandapas Pond

Pandapas Pond is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the forest. It is perfect for fishing, freshwater swimming, and hiking trails with scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blacksburg

Blacksburg has numerous hiking and biking trails. The Huckleberry Trail is the most popular paved trail in the area and perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk. The Appalachian Trail, located just a few miles from Blacksburg, and offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Blacksburg: Activities and Tours

Blacksburg Farmers Market

The Blacksburg Farmers Market is a gathering spot for farmers, artists, and local food makers. Every Saturday morning, the market brings together the best in local produce, meats, cheeses, and crafts.

Winery Tours

The area around Blacksburg is filled with wineries, producing some of Virginia’s best wines. A winery tour can be both educational and delightful, with the opportunity to learn about the process of wine making while enjoying the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Blacksburg

Must-Try Dishes

Blackstone Restaurant

The Blackstone Restaurant is a local favorite and serves up classic American fare, such as burgers, sandwiches, and salads with a focus on local ingredients.

The Farmhouse

The Farmhouse offers farm-to-table dining and exceptional service. Their menu features seasonal and creative dishes and is a great place to enjoy a date night or special occasion.

Local Beverages

Beale’s Brewery

Beale’s Brewery is a microbrewery with a taproom located in Downtown Blacksburg. It offers beers that are both unique and delicious.

Beliveau Estate Winery

Beliveau Estate Winery is a beautiful vineyard and winery located just outside of Blacksburg. It offers tastings of their award-winning wines, with a focus on Virginia-grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Blacksburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Courtyard by Marriott Blacksburg

The Courtyard by Marriott Blacksburg is ideally located near the Virginia Tech campus and downtown Blacksburg. It offers comfortable rooms, high-speed internet, and an on-site restaurant.

The Inn at Virginia Tech

The Inn at Virginia Tech is a stylish and modern hotel located on the Virginia Tech campus. It has spacious rooms, a fitness center, and two on-site restaurants.

What to Buy in Blacksburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Blacksburg Farmers’ Market offers a wide selection of locally made foods, honey, beeswax candles, pottery, jewelry, wine, and craft beer. Jackson Street Booksellers is a locally-owned, independent bookstore with a large selection of new and used books.

Local Culture and Customs of Blacksburg

Blacksburg is a college town, with a friendly and welcoming atmosphere. The town is known for its active community involvement, supporting and promoting local businesses and organizations.

Best Time to Visit Blacksburg

The best time to visit Blacksburg is from mid-March to mid-May when the weather is mild and the flowers are in bloom. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the changing colors of the leaves. Summer can be hot and humid, and winters are cold with some snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blacksburg

Do:

  • Be friendly and respectful to local people and culture
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area
  • Visit the local food and drink establishments.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas
  • Be loud and disruptive
  • Ignore traffic rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blacksburg

Blacksburg is generally safe, but take the usual precautions like keeping valuables locked in your car or hotel room and not leaving your belongings unattended.

Health in Blacksburg

The local medical center, Carilion Clinic, is readily accessible if you are in need of any medical attention. It is always recommended to have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Blacksburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blacksburg

  • Roanoke–Blacksburg Regional Airport (ROA) (approx. 40 mins)

Bus Stations Near Blacksburg

  • Virginia Tech Squires Student Center (bus service provided by Virginia Breeze)
  • Blacksburg Transit Center (local Blacksburg transit)

Other Options

  • Self-drive: Blacksburg is accessible by car via Interstate 81.
  • Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Blacksburg

Blacksburg has a reliable and affordable bus service called Blacksburg Transit, which runs frequently throughout the town. Bikes are also a popular way to get around and explore the town or ride along the Huckleberry Trail.

Where to Go Next from Blacksburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roanoke

Roanoke is a vibrant and historic city that is known for its outdoor activities, music scene, and cultural attractions. It is approximately 40 minutes from Blacksburg.

Shenandoah National Park

Shenandoah National Park is a beautiful mountain national park located just three hours from Blacksburg. It offers breathtaking scenic drives, hiking trails, and abundant wildlife.

Conclusion

Blacksburg is a hidden gem filled with natural beauty, vibrant culture, and charming tourist attractions. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now equipped to plan a memorable trip to this Virginia jewel.
