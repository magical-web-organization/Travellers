Introduction to Virginia

Located in the southern region of the United States, Virginia is a historic and scenic state that boasts a unique blend of southern charm and East Coast sophistication. The state is home to a variety of culturally significant landmarks, natural wonders, and vibrant neighborhoods that offer travelers of all interests a memorable vacation experience.

List of Places to Visit in Virginia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Virginia played a significant role in American history, and travelers who want to learn more can visit several key landmarks throughout the state. Some of the highlights include:

  • Jamestown Settlement: This attraction is a living museum that transports visitors back to the early 17th century when Jamestown was first established as a British colony.
  • Mount Vernon: The former plantation home of George Washington is now a museum that showcases the life and legacy of one of America’s most revered founding fathers.
  • Monticello: The home of Thomas Jefferson offers a glimpse into the life of one of America’s most intellectual and influential statesmen.
  • Arlington National Cemetery: This historic site is the final resting place of more than 400,000 soldiers, including many who gave their lives in service to the United States of America.

Natural Attractions of Virginia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For travelers who love the great outdoors, Virginia offers a stunning array of natural attractions. Some of the must-see destinations include:

  • Shenandoah National Park: This beautiful park offers breathtaking views of the Blue Ridge Mountains and is a popular destination for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching.
  • Virginia Beach: With miles of pristine coastline and a vibrant oceanfront boardwalk, Virginia Beach is a top destination for beach lovers.
  • Luray Caverns: Visitors can explore more than 4,000 stunning underground caves in this natural wonder.
  • Blue Ridge Parkway: This scenic drive offers stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains and is one of the most popular scenic drives in the United States.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Virginia

Virginia offers numerous hiking and biking routes for visitors who want to explore the state’s stunning natural beauty. Some popular trails include:

  • The Virginia Creeper Trail: A scenic bike trail that winds through the Appalachian Mountains and offers stunning views of fall foliage.
  • The Mount Vernon Trail: This 18-mile trail offers panoramic views of the Potomac River and takes visitors from George Washington’s Mount Vernon estate to Theodore Roosevelt Island.
  • The AT (Appalachian Trail): This 544-mile trail is a bucket list destination for hikers and offers stunning views of Virginia’s wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Virginia: Activities and Tours

In addition to the stunning natural attractions and historic landmarks, Virginia also offers a variety of activities and tours for travelers. Some of the best options include:

  • Virginia Wine Tours: Virginia is home to more than 300 wineries, and visitors can enjoy tours and tastings at many of them.
  • Richmond Segway Tour: Explore the historic city of Richmond on a Segway tour and learn about the city’s past, present, and future.
  • Virginia Beach Whale Watching: Take a boat tour and see humpback whales as they migrate along the coast of Virginia.
  • Colonial Williamsburg: This living museum offers visitors a unique opportunity to step back in time and experience life in Colonial America.

Week-Long Itinerary for Virginia

  • Day 1 - Shenandoah National Park: Spend the day hiking and exploring this beautiful national park.
  • Day 2 - Monticello and University of Virginia: Visit the former home of Thomas Jefferson and explore the gorgeous grounds of UVA.
  • Day 3 - Yorktown and Colonial Williamsburg: Learn about the Revolutionary War and America’s colonial past at these historic landmarks.
  • Day 4 - Virginia Beach: Spend the day lounging on the beach or exploring the coastal city.
  • Day 5 - Richmond: Visit the Virginia Museum of Fine Arts, take a Segway tour, and enjoy some great food and beer in this vibrant city.
  • Day 6 - Arlington National Cemetery and The National Mall: Pay your respects to America’s fallen soldiers and explore DC’s famous landmarks.
  • Day 7 - Luray Caverns and Virginia Wine Tour: Take a guided tour of Luray Caverns and spend the afternoon sampling some of Virginia’s delicious wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Virginia

Must-Try Dishes

One of the best ways to experience Virginia’s rich culinary traditions is to try some of the state’s most iconic dishes. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Virginia Ham: This cured ham is a staple of Southern cuisine and is featured on many restaurant menus throughout the state.
  • Chesapeake Bay Crab Cakes: Made with fresh blue crab meat, these cakes are a delicious taste of the Chesapeake Bay region.
  • Brunswick Stew: A hearty stew made with chicken, pork, and vegetables, Brunswick Stew is a classic Virginia comfort food.
  • Peanut Pie: Made with locally sourced Virginia peanuts, this pie is a unique and delicious dessert.

Local Beverages

Virginia is home to a variety of local breweries, wineries, and distilleries that offer travelers the chance to sample some of the state’s best beverages. Some of the highlights include:

  • Virginia Wine: With more than 300 wineries, Virginia is the fifth-largest wine-producing state in the United States.
  • Virginia Craft Beer: From Richmond to Roanoke, Virginia is home to numerous craft breweries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Virginia Whiskey: Several distilleries throughout the state produce top-quality whiskey using locally sourced grains.

Where to Stay in Virginia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Virginia offers lodging options for travelers of all budgets and preferences. Some top choices include:

  • The Jefferson Hotel (Richmond): This historic hotel is a true luxury experience and offers guests gorgeous rooms and unparalleled service.
  • HI Richmond Hostel (Richmond): Backpackers and budget travelers will love this affordable and welcoming hostel in the heart of Richmond.
  • The Inn at Little Washington (Washington): This exclusive inn offers world-class dining and luxurious accommodations in a stunning rural setting.

What to Buy in Virginia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For travelers who want to bring home a piece of Virginia’s unique culture and history, there are plenty of great souvenirs to choose from. Some of the best options include:

  • Virginia Peanuts: These locally sourced peanuts are a delicious snack and make a great gift for friends and family back home.
  • Colonial Williamsburg Souvenirs: From historical clothing to handcrafted pottery, there are many souvenirs available that celebrate America’s colonial past.
  • Virginia Wine: Take home a bottle of your favorite Virginia wine and savor the memories of your trip long after you return home.

Local Culture and Customs of Virginia

Virginia is a state with deep historical roots, and its culture and customs reflect that heritage. Visitors to Virginia are likely to encounter friendly locals who take pride in their state’s history and natural beauty. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for Historical Sites: Many of Virginia’s landmarks and attractions are of national historical significance. Be sure to treat these sites with respect and follow any posted rules and regulations.
  • Southern Hospitality: Southerners are known for their friendly and welcoming demeanor, and Virginia is no exception. Be prepared for random acts of kindness and friendly conversation with locals.
  • Appreciation of Nature: Virginia’s natural beauty is a point of pride for many residents, so be sure to treat the environment with respect and follow any posted rules for parks and outdoor spaces.

Best Time to Visit Virginia

Virginia experiences four distinct seasons, so the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. For outdoor enthusiasts, spring and fall are the best times to visit, with mild temperatures and stunning foliage. Summer is a popular time for beachgoers, and winter offers the chance to explore Virginia’s ski resorts and cozy indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Virginia

Do:

  • Explore Virginia’s rich history and culture at the state’s many museums and attractions.
  • Take advantage of the hiking and biking trails throughout the state.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages, including wine, craft beer, and classic Southern dishes.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the traffic around major cities like Richmond and Virginia Beach.
  • Forget to tip your servers and service industry professionals.
  • Ignore posted rules and regulations at historical and natural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Virginia

Virginia is a safe state for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions when visiting any new place. Some tips for staying safe in Virginia include:

  • Lock your car doors and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas like beaches and major attractions.
  • Watch for traffic when crossing busy streets.

Health in Virginia

Travelers to Virginia should be aware of any specific health concerns, including ticks and mosquito-borne illnesses like Lyme disease and West Nile virus. It’s also important to stay hydrated during summer months and to use proper sun protection when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Virginia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Virginia

Virginia is served by three major airports: Richmond International Airport (RIC), Reagan National Airport (DCA), and Dulles International Airport (IAD). Many major airlines offer regular flights to these airports from destinations across the United States and beyond.

Bus Stations Near Virginia

Several bus companies operate routes to Virginia, including Greyhound and Megabus. Major bus stations in Virginia include the Richmond Greyhound Station and the Virginia Beach Greyhound Station.

Other Options

Train travel is also an option for travelers to Virginia, with Amtrak offering regular service to many major cities throughout the state. In addition, driving is a popular option for those coming from nearby states.

How to Get Around in Virginia

Virginia is a large state, so it’s important to have reliable transportation when exploring all that it has to offer. Some good options include renting a car or taking advantage of public transportation in major cities like Richmond and Virginia Beach. For those who prefer more active options, biking and hiking are great ways to get around and see the sights.

Where to Go Next from Virginia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Virginia is a great starting point for exploring the rest of the East Coast. Some nearby destinations and excursions to explore include:

  • Washington, D.C.: The nation’s capital is just a short drive from Virginia and offers countless museums, landmarks, and cultural attractions.
  • The Outer Banks: This beautiful stretch of coastline offers miles of beaches, historic lighthouses, and charming seaside towns.
  • The Great Smoky Mountains: Head west to explore one of America’s most stunning natural wonders in Tennessee and North Carolina.

Conclusion

Virginia is a diverse and fascinating state that offers something for every kind of traveler. From the stunning natural beauty of Shenandoah National Park and Virginia Beach to the rich history and culture of Colonial Williamsburg and Mount Vernon, there’s no shortage of things to see and do. Be sure to sample the local cuisine, immerse yourself in Virginia’s unique customs and culture, and above all, enjoy your journey through this unforgettable state.
