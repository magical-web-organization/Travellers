Introduction to Quantico

Located in the Northern Virginia region of the state of Virginia, Quantico is a small town that is home to a United States Marine Corps installation. The town itself has a rich history and offers tourists a variety of culturally important attractions, natural wonders, hiking/biking trails, and food experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Quantico

Here are some of the top attractions in Quantico that you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of the Marine Corps: A museum that showcases the history of the United States Marine Corps and features exhibits that explore the different battles that the Corps has fought in.
  • Quantico National Cemetery: A national cemetery that is the final resting place for veterans who served in the United States military.
  • Prince William Forest Park: A park that was once home to several Civilian Conservation Corps camps and now offers visitors the opportunity to hike, camp, and explore the great outdoors.

Natural Attractions of Quantico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Potomac Point Winery and Vineyard: A local winery that offers a selection of wine tastings, tours, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Chopawamsic Backcountry Area: A beautiful and remote wilderness area that is perfect for hiking, camping, and exploring.
  • Quantico Creek: A beautiful waterway that offers visitors the opportunity to kayak, canoe, or fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quantico

Quantico is home to several excellent biking and hiking trails, including the:

  • Quantico Creek Trail: A scenic trail that winds along the Quantico Creek and offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Chopawamsic Backcountry Area Trails: A collection of trails that offer hikers the chance to explore the beautiful backcountry of Quantico.
  • Battlefield Hike and Bike Trail: A trail that follows the historic route used by Union soldiers during the Civil War.

Best Things to Do in Quantico: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Quantico:

  • Take a guided tour of the National Museum of the Marine Corps
  • Go hiking or camping in Prince William Forest Park
  • Explore the beautiful Potomac Point Winery and Vineyard
  • Go kayaking or canoeing on Quantico Creek
  • Take a visit to the Quantico National Cemetery

Week-Long Itinerary for Quantico

Day 1: Explore the National Museum of the Marine Corps; Day 2: Go hiking and camping in Prince William Forest Park; Day 3: Take a guided tour of Quantico National Cemetery and visit the local wineries; Day 4: Go kayaking or canoeing on Quantico Creek; Day 5: Take a visit to the local farmers market; Day 6: Bike the Chopawamsic Backcountry Area Trails; Day 7: Visit nearby cities such as Richmond, VA.

Food and Drink Scene of Quantico

Quantico boasts a variety of local dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Virginia ham: A smoked ham that is cured in saltwater and honey and typically served during the holidays.
  • Crabcakes: A local specialty that is made with fresh crabmeat that is seasoned with Old Bay seasoning and breaded with bread crumbs.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia wine: A variety of wines can be found throughout the region, including the Potomac Point Winery, which offers tastings as well as tours, events, and more.
  • Virginia beer: There are several local breweries in the area that offer a wide variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Quantico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Quantico has a range of accommodation options, including:

  • Holiday Inn Quantico Center: A centrally located hotel that offers comfortable rooms and ample amenities like a fitness center and pool.
  • Hampton Inn Dumfries/Quantico: A hotel that offers a complimentary hot breakfast and an outdoor pool.
  • Quantico-Bethel Bed and Breakfast: A cozy bed and breakfast that offers a home-like atmosphere and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Quantico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the top souvenirs and local products in Quantico include:

  • National Museum of the Marine Corps merchandise: Many unique items related to the U.S. Marine Corps can be purchased at the museum’s gift shop.
  • Virginia wines: Take home a bottle of wine from the Potomac Point Winery and Vineyard.
  • Virginia peanuts: These peanuts are grown throughout the state and make a delicious and affordable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Quantico

Quantico has a unique military culture due to the presence of the United States Marine Corps base. Visitors should be mindful of this culture and respectful of the men and women serving in the Marines.

Best Time to Visit Quantico

The best time to visit Quantico is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters are cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quantico

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to Quantico:

Do:

  • Visit the National Museum of the Marine Corps
  • Hike and camp in Prince William Forest Park
  • Try local cuisine and wines

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Be disrespectful to the U.S. Marines
  • Trespass on government property or restricted areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Quantico

Quantico is a relatively safe town with low levels of crime. Visitors should still take standard precautions like locking their vehicles and securing valuables.

Health in Quantico

Quantico has several medical facilities, including clinics and hospitals, that can provide visitors with medical care if needed.

Getting to Quantico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Quantico

  • Reagan National Airport: Approximately 37 miles away, a popular option for domestic travelers.

Bus Stations Near Quantico

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Woodbridge: Approximately 15 miles away, external website provides a direct connection to several destinations throughout the country.

Other Options

  • Train service from the Quantico train station: Located near the U.S. Marine Corps base, provides service via Amtrak’s Northeast Corridor.

How to Get Around in Quantico

It is recommended to rent a car or use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft to get around Quantico.

Where to Go Next from Quantico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that you might want to explore:

  • Washington DC: The nation’s capital is just a short drive away and offers a variety of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Richmond, VA: A charming southern city that is home to several interesting museums and landmarks.
  • Shenandoah National Park: A beautiful park that is perfect for hiking, camping, and exploring the great outdoors.

Conclusion

Quantico is a small town with a lot to offer tourists. From its rich history and cultural attractions to its beautiful natural wonders and food scene, Quantico is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Northern Virginia.
