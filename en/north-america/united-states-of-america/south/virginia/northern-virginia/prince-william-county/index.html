Introduction to Prince William County

Prince William County is a scenic destination located in the state of Virginia, USA. It is situated in Northern Virginia, just a short distance from Washington, D.C. The county is home to a rich US history and culture, stunning natural landscapes, and a thriving food and drink scene.

Whether you are interested in outdoor pursuits, cultural attractions, or simply soaking up the local atmosphere, Prince William County has something for everyone. With a range of places to stay, a diverse culinary scene, and plenty of things to do, Prince William County is a great destination for a short break or a longer vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Prince William County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of the Marine Corps

Located in Triangle, VA, the National Museum of the Marine Corps is a must-visit attraction for history buffs and military enthusiasts. The museum features exhibits on the history of the Marine Corps, including its role in major conflicts throughout US history.

Manassas National Battlefield Park

For Civil War history, head to Manassas National Battlefield Park. This park is the site of two major Civil War battles, and features a visitor center, educational exhibits, and a range of walking and hiking trails.

The Weems-Botts Museum

The Weems-Botts Museum is a historic house museum in Dumfries, VA. The museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Dumfries and Prince William County. Visitors can take a guided tour of the museum, which features exhibits on local history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Prince William County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Prince William Forest Park

Prince William Forest Park is a serene natural destination that boasts over 37 miles of hiking trails, scenic drives, and picnic areas. Visitors can explore the park’s diverse wilderness areas, which are home to a range of wildlife and plant species.

Leesylvania State Park

Located on the shore of the Potomac River, Leesylvania State Park is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and picnicking. The park is also home to a range of hiking trails and historical sites.

Occoquan Bay National Wildlife Refuge

Nature lovers should not miss Occoquan Bay National Wildlife Refuge, which features 644 acres of wetlands, hardwood forests, and meadows. The refuge is home to a range of bird species, and offers visitors exceptional birdwatching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prince William County

Prince William County is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Bull Run-Occoquan Trail
  • Prince William Forest Park Loop Trail
  • Neabsco Creek Boardwalk

These trails are suitable for all levels of fitness and experience, and offer some of the best views of Prince William County’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Prince William County: Activities and Tours

From scenic train rides to adrenaline-pumping zipline adventures, there are plenty of things to do in Prince William County. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Potomac Eagle Scenic Railroad
  • The Adventure Park at Virginia Aquarium
  • Escape Room Woodbridge

Week-Long Itinerary for Prince William County

For those looking to explore everything Prince William County has to offer, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the National Museum of the Marine Corps and grab lunch in nearby Triangle.

Day 2: Explore the hiking trails in Prince William Forest Park, and enjoy a picnic lunch in one of the park’s scenic areas.

Day 3: Take a scenic train ride on the Potomac Eagle Scenic Railroad, and enjoy a leisurely dinner in Occoquan.

Day 4: Visit Leesylvania State Park, and rent kayaks or paddleboards to explore the Potomac River.

Day 5: Spend the day at The Adventure Park at Virginia Aquarium, and grab dinner at a local restaurant in Woodbridge.

Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Weems-Botts Museum, and check out the historic town of Dumfries.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Prince William County’s spas, or indulge in some retail therapy at Potomac Mills Mall.

Food and Drink Scene of Prince William County

Must-Try Dishes

Prince William County’s food scene is as diverse as it is delicious. Some of the must-try local dishes include:

  • Virginia ham and biscuits
  • Cowboy chili
  • Smith Island cake

Local Beverages

For those looking for a local drink, try a craft beer from BadWolf Brewing Company or a glass of wine from The Winery at La Grange.

  • A bottle of wine from a local winery

Local Culture and Customs of Prince William County

Prince William County is a welcoming and friendly destination, and visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture. It is customary to greet locals with a smile and a friendly “hello,” and to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Prince William County

The best time to visit Prince William County is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the natural landscapes of the county are at their most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prince William County

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the county’s natural beauty
  • Sample the local cuisine and drink
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in natural areas
  • Be overly loud or disruptive in public spaces
  • Disrespect historic sites or landmarks

Safety and Health

Safety in Prince William County

Prince William County is generally a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is important to take basic safety precautions such as locking your car and avoiding leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Prince William County

Prince William County has a range of healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance before their trip and to carry any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Prince William County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prince William County

The closest airports to Prince William County are Washington Dulles International Airport and Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport. From there, travelers can rent a car or take public transportation to reach Prince William County.

Bus Stations Near Prince William County

The Greyhound Bus Station in Manassas, VA, is the closest bus station to Prince William County.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to a number or train stations in Prince William County, including Manassas and Woodbridge.

How to Get Around in Prince William County

Prince William County is best explored by car, and visitors can rent a car from a number of rental agencies at airports and train stations in the area. Taxis and ride-share services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Prince William County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore beyond Prince William County, some of the best nearby destinations include:

  • Washington, D.C.
  • Shenandoah National Park
  • Virginia Beach

Conclusion

As you can see, Prince William County is a diverse and exciting destination that offers something for every type of traveler. From natural beauty and historic landmarks to delicious food and drink, visitors will find plenty to explore and enjoy in this Virginia gem.
