Introduction to Triangle

Located in Prince William County, in Northern Virginia, Triangle is a quaint town that offers visitors a chance to experience a unique blend of rich history and natural beauty. Its strategic location makes it a convenient base for exploring Northern Virginia’s surrounding areas and attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Triangle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of the Marine Corps: Home to more than 60,000 Marine Corps artifacts, this museum is a tribute to the US Marines and their rich history.
  • Prince William Forest Park: Once home to Native American tribes, this national park now serves as an outdoor museum showcasing the region’s rich natural and cultural history.
  • Freedom Museum: Dedicated to preserving the legacy of military aviation, this museum exhibits a wide variety of military artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Triangle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Quantico Creek: This scenic waterway is perfect for water sports and fishing enthusiasts.
  • Leesylvania State Park: This park offers beautiful water views, hiking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Prince William Sound: Take a boat tour to experience the natural beauty of Virginia’s coastline, or rent a kayak or paddleboard for a more personalized experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Triangle

  • Mattaponi and Laurel Trails at Prince William Forest Park: These trails offer beautiful scenery and are suitable for hikers and bikers of all levels.
  • Raven’s Nest Trail: This trail meanders through a diverse forest and offers beautiful views.
  • Chopawamsic Backcountry Area: This area boasts over 4,000 acres of wilderness, with over 10 miles of hiking trails to choose from.

Best Things to Do in Triangle: Activities and Tours

  • Prince William County Fair: This annual event showcases Virginia’s unique local culture through food, music, and games.
  • Chinn Park Aquatics and Fitness Center: Not just your typical gym, this center offers numerous fitness classes along with indoor and outdoor pools.
  • ATV Adventure Tours: Take an off-road adventure tour through the scenic backcountry of Northern Virginia.

Food and Drink Scene of Triangle

Must-Try Dishes

  • Virginia Ham: Indulge in Virginia’s famous ham, which has a unique flavor due to the curing process.
  • Crab Cakes: Made from Virginia’s own Chesapeake Bay blue crabs, these cakes are a true local delicacy.
  • Virginia Peanut Soup: This soup is a blend of peanuts, broth, and cream, and has been a traditional Southern dish for centuries.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wines: The North American climate favors the growth of many grape varieties, allowing Virginia to produce a diverse range of delicious wines.
  • Virginia Breweries: The craft beer scene has exploded in Virginia, and there are numerous breweries to choose from in and around Triangle.

Where to Stay in Triangle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Courtyard by Marriott Quantico: This hotel offers comfortable accommodations and is conveniently located near Quantico Marine Corps Base.
  • Hampton Inn Stafford/Quantico & Conference Center: This hotel offers comfortable accommodations and a central location for exploring the surrounding attractions.
  • Quality Inn Triangle - Quantico: This hotel is conveniently located near the National Museum of the Marine Corps and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Triangle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Virginia Peanuts: These peanuts make a great and delicious souvenir for friends and family.
  • Local Wine and Beer: Cherish the memories of your visit with a bottle of Virginia vintage wine or a craft beer brewed in Triangle.
  • Artisanal Crafts: Browse the local shops and markets and find a unique hand-crafted souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Triangle

Virginia has a rich cultural history that is evident in its numerous museums and historic sites. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including a strong sense of community and respect for the environment.

Best Time to Visit Triangle

The best time to visit Triangle is during the fall months, when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the foliage is at its most stunning. Winter is also a great time to visit, with numerous winter activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Triangle

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the outdoors and natural beauty of Northern Virginia.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local property.
  • Ignore posted signage or regulations in parks or historic sites.
  • Dress inappropriately for cultural or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Triangle

Triangle is generally safe for travelers. However, it is always important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Triangle

Triangle has access to adequate healthcare facilities. Visitors with specific medical needs should consult with their physician before traveling and make sure to pack adequate medical supplies.

Getting to Triangle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Triangle

  • Reagan National Airport: Located approximately 40 miles from Triangle, this airport is the closest to the area.
  • Dulles International Airport: Located approximately 50 miles from Triangle, this airport is a convenient alternative for travelers.
  • Richmond International Airport: Located approximately 70 miles from Triangle, this airport is a more distant option but can offer cheaper flights.

Other Transportation Options

  • Train: Amtrak trains run through the town of Quantico, just a few miles away from Triangle.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies offer rental services in the area.
  • Bus: Greyhound offers bus services to nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Triangle

While the town itself is relatively small, a car is recommended for visiting the various nearby attractions. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available as well.

Where to Go Next from Triangle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Middleburg: This charming town is located approximately 35 miles from Triangle and offers numerous wineries and equestrian centers.
  • Mount Vernon: Located approximately 20 miles from Triangle, this estate was the residence of George Washington and is now a national historic site.
  • Washington, DC: Located approximately 30 miles from Triangle, the nation’s capital offers numerous attractions, including museums, monuments, and historic sites.

Conclusion

Triangle is a unique destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the rich history and natural beauty of Northern Virginia. With numerous activities, attractions, and local cuisine, visitors are sure to have a memorable trip and might even want to extend their stay.
