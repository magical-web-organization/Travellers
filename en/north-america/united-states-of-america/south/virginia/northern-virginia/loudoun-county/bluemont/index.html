A Comprehensive Guide to Bluemont, Virginia

Bluemont is a charming town located in Loudoun County, Virginia, in the heart of the Blue Ridge Mountains. It boasts scenic views, outdoor adventures, and a rich history, making it a perfect destination for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Bluemont has something for everyone. Plan your trip with this comprehensive travel guide and experience the best of Bluemont.

List of Places to Visit in Bluemont

Bluemont is a small town, but it has a lot to offer in terms of attractions and activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Great Country Farms: A working farm that offers seasonal fruit-picking, farm animals, and a farm market.
  • Bear’s Den Overlook: A scenic hiking trail with breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Bluemont General Store: A historic country store that offers a selection of gifts, souvenirs, and local products.
  • The Jack Kent Cooke Foundation: A non-profit organization that supports high-achieving, low-income students.

Natural Attractions of Bluemont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Ridge Center for Environmental Stewardship: A nature reserve that features miles of hiking trails, birdwatching, and educational programs.
  • The Appalachian Trail: A world-renowned hiking trail that spans 2,200 miles, with a section that runs through Bluemont.
  • The Shenandoah River: A scenic river that offers kayaking, tubing, and fishing.
  • Snickers Gap: A scenic overlook that provides stunning panoramic views of the Virginia countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bluemont

Bluemont is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its plethora of scenic trails that offer breathtaking views of the Blue Ridge Mountains. Some of the best trails include:

  • Bear’s Den Trail: A moderate hike that takes you to a stunning overlook.
  • Raven Rocks Trail: A challenging hike that rewards you with panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Blue Ridge Center for Environmental Stewardship: This nature reserve features 10 miles of hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Bluemont: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Bluemont is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours, including Bluemont Vineyard and Dirt Farm Brewing.
  • Apple Picking: Great Country Farms offers seasonal fruit-picking, including apples.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take a hot air balloon ride with Blue Ridge Balloon.
  • Horse Riding: Experience the Virginia countryside on horseback with Blue Ridge Trail Rides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bluemont

Day 1: Visit Great Country Farms and pick seasonal fruits.

Day 2: Hike the Bear’s Den Trail and visit the Bluemont General Store.

Day 3: Go wine-tasting at Bluemont Vineyard and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride with Blue Ridge Balloon.

Day 5: Visit the Appalachian Trail and hike the Raven Rocks Trail.

Day 6: Go horseback riding with Blue Ridge Trail Rides.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Bluemont.

Food and Drink Scene of Bluemont

Bluemont is the perfect destination for foodies, with its variety of dining options that cater to all tastes and budgets.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Country-style breakfast at The Bluemont General Store.
  • Angus beef burger at Dirt Farm Brewing.
  • BBQ ribs at Monk’s BBQ.
  • Pizza at Bluemont Vineyard.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia wine at Bluemont Vineyard.
  • Craft beer at Dirt Farm Brewing.
  • Apple cider at the Bluemont General Store.

Where to Stay in Bluemont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bluemont is a small town, and there are limited options for accommodations. However, there are several charming guesthouses and B&Bs that provide a cozy and comfortable stay.

  • The Goodstone Inn & Restaurant: A luxurious guesthouse that offers fine dining and scenic views.
  • Stone Manor Bed and Breakfast: A historic B&B that offers a tranquil escape.
  • The Inn at Vint Hill: A charming B&B that offers a unique experience.

What to Buy in Bluemont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bluemont is known for its local products, including wine, cider, and crafts. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Virginia wine from Bluemont Vineyard.
  • Apple cider from the Bluemont General Store.
  • Hand-crafted pottery from Bluemont Pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Bluemont

Bluemont is a small town with a rich history and a strong sense of community. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs, including:

  • Using “ma’am” and “sir” when addressing locals.
  • Observing dress codes at religious sites.
  • Being respectful of nature and the environment.

Best Time to Visit Bluemont

The best time to visit Bluemont is during the fall when the foliage is at its peak and the weather is pleasant. Spring and summer are also good times to visit, but they can be crowded. Winter is the least popular time to visit due to the cold weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bluemont

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the scenic trails and natural attractions.
  • Sample the local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Refuse hospitality when offered.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bluemont

Bluemont is a safe town, and crime rates are low. Visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, including locking doors and protecting personal belongings.

Health in Bluemont

Bluemont has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer medical services to residents and visitors alike. Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving in Bluemont.

How to Get Around in Bluemont

Bluemont is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also explore the town by bike or car.

Where to Go Next from Bluemont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
