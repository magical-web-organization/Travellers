Introduction to Middleburg

Middleburg, located in Loudoun County in Northern Virginia, is a small town with a rich history and picturesque surroundings. Being situated at the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains, it is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The town is also known for its quaint shops and charming atmosphere, making it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Middleburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Sporting Library and Museum - This museum houses a vast collection of literature and art about equestrian sports. It also has exhibits on the history of fox hunting and steeplechase racing.

  • The Aldie Mill Historic Park - This park is a restored 19th-century grist mill and gives a glimpse into the area’s agricultural history.

  • Oatlands Historic House and Gardens - This mansion-turned-museum has a rich history dating back to the early 1800s, and its stunning gardens are worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Middleburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sky Meadows State Park - This park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and picnicking. The park also has beautiful views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Bears Den Trail Center - This center has two hiking trails, the Appalachian Trail and the Bears Den Trail, both of which offer stunning views of the countryside.

  • Middleburg Community Center Pool - This pool is a great spot to relax and cool down on a hot summer day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Middleburg

Middleburg offers various hiking and biking trails, which allow tourists to explore the beauty of the area at close proximity. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • The Washington and Old Dominion Trail - This 45-mile trail is a paradise for bikers, runners, and hikers, offering stunning views of the countryside.

  • The Virginia Creeper Trail - This 34-mile trail is perfect for those looking for a more relaxed hiking or biking trail, with its gradual inclines and stunning views of the Virginia mountains.

  • The Appalachian Trail - This famous trail passes through Middleburg and is a great spot for serious hikers.

Best Things to Do in Middleburg: Activities and Tours

  • Middleburg Tasting Room and Cider Garden - This tasting room features local wines and provides a perfect spot for wine lovers.

  • Greenhill Winery and Vineyards - This winery offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and has an excellent collection of wines.

  • Salamander Resort and Spa - This luxurious and stylish resort offers a range of activities, including horseback riding, tennis, and fitness classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Middleburg

Day 1: Visit the National Sporting Library and Museum

Day 2: Hike on the Washington and Old Dominion Trail

Day 3: Head to the Greenhill Winery and Vineyards

Day 4: Visit the Aldie Mill Historic Park

Day 5: Adventure day at Sky Meadows State Park, with camping and hiking

Day 6: Visit Oatlands Historic House and Gardens

Day 7: Relaxing day at the Salamander Resort and Spa

Food and Drink Scene of Middleburg

Middleburg has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of options for foodies and wine lovers alike.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes - This local seafood specialty is a must-try while in Middleburg.

  • Vineyard-fresh produce - The area has several vineyards that offer farm-to-table produce, which is a treat for food lovers.

  • Virginia Ham - This southern staple is a delicious option for meat lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wines - Middleburg has numerous vineyards that offer local wines, from Chardonnay to Cabernet Sauvignon.

  • Beer - Several breweries in the area offer unique craft beer options.

Where to Stay in Middleburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Salamander Resort and Spa - This luxurious resort has its spa, wellness center, and fitness center, and it is located in the heart of Middleburg.

  • Goodstone Inn & Restaurant - This inn is perfect for romantic getaways and offers plush accommodations and a gourmet restaurant.

  • Middleburg Country Inn - For a more affordable option, the Middleburg Country Inn provides budget-friendly rooms.

What to Buy in Middleburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Middleburg-made wine - Local wines are a perfect souvenir to take back home.

  • Horse-themed souvenirs - Being a town with a rich equestrian history, many shops offer horse-themed souvenirs.

  • Antiques - Middleburg has several antique shops that offer unique items.

Local Culture and Customs of Middleburg

*Being a small town, Middleburg has a close-knit community feel, and the locals take pride in preserving their rich history and traditions. The area has a strong equestrian culture and is famous for horse races and fox hunting.

Best Time to Visit Middleburg

The best time to visit Middleburg is during the fall season (September to November) when the foliage is at its peak, and the weather is mild. Spring (March to May) is also an excellent time to visit, with pleasant weather and blooming flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middleburg

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs

  • Try the local wines and food

  • Take hikes and explore the area’s natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Middleburg

Middleburg is a generally safe town, but tourists should exercise caution when hiking or exploring natural areas. It is recommended to carry a first-aid kit and ample water when hiking.

Health in Middleburg

Middleburg has several medical centers and hospitals to handle any medical emergency tourists may face during their visit.

Getting to Middleburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Middleburg

  • Washington Dulles International Airport - 23 miles away from Middleburg

  • Reagan National Airport - 50 miles away from Middleburg

Bus Stations Near Middleburg

  • Union Station Bus Terminal - 42 miles away from Middleburg

  • Dulles Town Center Transit Station - 25 miles away from Middleburg

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Several car rental options are available in the area.

  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available at the airports and other transportation hubs.

How to Get Around in Middleburg

The best and most convenient way to get around Middleburg is by car. The area is not well-connected by public transportation, and taxis are limited.

Where to Go Next from Middleburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Leesburg - This charming town is only a 20-minute drive from Middleburg and is famous for its shops and historical sites.

  • Harper’s Ferry - This tiny town on the border of Virginia and West Virginia is a history buff’s paradise and is only an hour’s drive from Middleburg.

Conclusion

Middleburg is a delightful small town that offers beautiful natural landscapes, a rich equestrian culture, and plenty of activities for visitors. Plan your visit to Middleburg today and enjoy a relaxing, yet exciting getaway in this charming town.
