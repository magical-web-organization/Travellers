Introduction to Aquia Harbour

Aquia Harbour is a waterfront community located in Stafford County, Virginia, on the Potomac River. It offers beautiful views, serene surroundings, and plenty of outdoor activities for visitors of all ages. The community is located just off Interstate 95, making it a convenient stop for anyone traveling along the East Coast.

List of Places to Visit in Aquia Harbour

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aquia Harbour has a rich history dating back to the colonial era. Some of the most significant cultural attractions in the area include:

  • Aquia Episcopal Church: Founded in 1757, it is one of the oldest churches in the region with unique interior furnishings and wooden statues from Germany. The church was added to the National Park Service’s National Register of Historic Places in 1972.

  • Potomac Point Winery and Vineyard: Located on the outskirts of Aquia Harbour, the winery features daily wine tastings, a bistro, and beautiful views of the vines. It is a must-visit destination for wine lovers.

  • Stafford County Museum: This museum showcases the history of the region, including exhibits on the Civil War and local industries. It is a great place to learn about the significant events that shaped the area.

Natural Attractions of Aquia Harbour: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aquia Harbour is blessed with picturesque natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy various outdoor activities, including:

  • Aquia Landing Beach Park: The park is a popular spot for fishing, swimming, and picnicking, with plenty of grills, picnic tables, and public restrooms.

  • Crow’s Nest Natural Area Preserve: The preserve consists of a wide range of habitats, including forests, fields, streams, and wetlands. It is an excellent place for hiking, bird-watching, and wildlife viewing.

  • Aquia Creek: Visitors can rent kayaks and canoes to explore the creek or fish for bass, catfish, or bluegill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aquia Harbour

Aquia Harbour is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts with picturesque trails and routes that offer stunning views of the Potomac River and the surrounding natural beauty. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Chesapeake & Ohio Canal National Historical Park: A scenic trail for runners, hikers, and bikers, which stretches for miles along the Potomac River with campgrounds, boat rentals and picnic areas.

  • Accokeek Creek Trail: An excellent trail for bikers, which winds through woods and offers fantastic views of aquamarine water and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Aquia Harbour: Activities and Tours

Aquia Harbour has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors engaged throughout the day. Some of the popular activities include:

  • Aquia Harbour Golf Course: A beautiful and challenging 18-hole golf course with breath-taking views of the Potomac River.

  • Dolphin Express Tour Boat Ride: A fun and educational boat tour, which offers plenty of opportunities to watch dolphins and learn about the history and ecology of the Potomac River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aquia Harbour

Day 1: Start your day with a visit to the Stafford County Museum and then head to Potomac Point Winery for a wine tasting. Spend the afternoon relaxing at Aquia Landing Beach Park.

Day 2: Take a morning kayaking tour around Aquia Creek. Afternoon visit to Crow’s Nest Natural Area Preserve for wildlife watching.

Day 3: Enjoy a round of golf at Aquia Harbor Golf Course. In the afternoon, relax in the beautiful surroundings with a picnic.

Day 4: Take the Dolphin Express Tour Boat Ride. Spend the afternoon at Accokeek Creek Trail for a bike ride.

Day 5: Spend the day exploring Washington, D.C., which is just a short drive from Aquia Harbour.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Shenandoah National Park for scenic drives, hiking, and outdoor activities.

Day 7: Visit the nearby town of Fredericksburg for shopping and dining.

Food and Drink Scene of Aquia Harbour

Must-Try Dishes

Aquia Harbour offers plenty of culinary options, ranging from local favorites to international cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Crab cakes: Made with locally sourced crab, it is a must-try dish for seafood lovers.

  • Barbeque: Virginia-style barbeque is slow-cooked in a pit and served with a variety of sauces to tantalize your taste buds.

Local Beverages

Aquia Harbour has several wineries and breweries, offering different tastes and styles. Some popular local beverages include:

  • Potomac Point Winery and Vineyard: Known for its award-winning wines.

  • Adventure Brewing Company: It is known for its craft beers, made with locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Aquia Harbour: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aquia Harbour has several accommodation options suitable for every budget. Some of the popular options include:

What to Buy in Aquia Harbour: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take a piece of Aquia Harbour back home with them by purchasing local souvenirs from gift shops and local markets. Some popular options include:

  • Farmer’s Markets: Offer a range of homemade produce and goods, including olive oils, jams, and baked goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Aquia Harbour

Aquia Harbour boasts a rich and diverse culture, with influences from Native Americans, Spanish, and English settlers. Visitors can experience the local culture by attending events such as:

  • Aquia Harbour Independence Day Celebration: Includes a parade, food vendors, and fireworks.

  • Aquia Harbour Fine Art & Crafts Show: Features a diverse range of artisans and their works.

Best Time to Visit Aquia Harbour

The best time to visit Aquia Harbour is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy, which can limit some outdoor activities.

Conclusion
