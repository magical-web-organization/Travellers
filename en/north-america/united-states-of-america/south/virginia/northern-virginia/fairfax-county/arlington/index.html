If you’re looking for a great travel destination in the United States, Arlington is worth considering. Located in Fairfax County, Northern Virginia, Arlington is a popular destination thanks to its rich history, excellent museums, outdoor activities, and vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re traveling with family, friends, or solo, there’s plenty to see and do in Arlington. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best attractions, food, and places to stay.

Introduction to Arlington

Arlington is a small but vibrant community located just across the Potomac River from Washington, D.C. While many people visit Arlington to see the iconic landmarks of Washington D.C, including the White House, Lincoln Memorial or Washington Monument, there’s also much to explore within Arlington itself. The city is steeped in history and offers a diverse range of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, shopping and dining options to keep visitors entertained.

Arlington is home to several museums that are worth a visit. From the National 9/11 Pentagon Memorial to the United States Marine Corps War Memorial, here are the must-visit historical places and museums in Arlington:

  • Arlington National Cemetery

  • The Air Force Memorial
  • The Women’s Memorial

Best Things to Do in Arlington: Activities and Tours

From exploring historic neighborhoods to playing golf, there are plenty of things to do in Arlington. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

Local Culture and Customs of Arlington

Arlington is a diverse community with a rich history and unique culture. While there are no specific customs or traditions to note, visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and cultural sensitivities.

Best Time to Visit Arlington

Arlington is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and events, consider visiting during the summer months when the weather is warm. On the other hand, if you’re interested in cultural attractions and museums, the winter months may be a better option.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arlington

Do:

  • Visit Arlington National Cemetery and pay your respects to fallen soldiers.
  • Take a walking tour of the city to explore hidden gems.
  • Try the local cuisine at one of Arlington’s many restaurants.

Don’t:

  • Park illegally or in reserved spots.
  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Be disrespectful to local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arlington

Overall, Arlington is a safe city for visitors. However, as with any destination, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Always lock your car and valuables, and be aware of your surroundings when walking alone or at night.

Health in Arlington

Arlington’s healthcare system is excellent, and visitors can easily access medical care if needed. However, visitors should take standard precautions to avoid illness, such as washing hands, staying hydrated, and getting enough rest and exercise.
