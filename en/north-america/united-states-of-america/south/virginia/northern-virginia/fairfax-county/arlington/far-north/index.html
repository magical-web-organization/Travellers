Introduction to Far North

Located in Arlington, in Fairfax County, in Northern Virginia, Far North is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and exciting activities, it offers a unique travel experience for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Far North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arlington Cemetery: Visit this national cemetery, which serves as the final resting place for thousands of American servicemen and women, and experience its iconic changing of the guard ceremony.
  • National Museum of African American History and Culture: Discover exhibits and collections that portray the African American story, heritage, and culture.
  • Mount Vernon: The home of George Washington showcases his life, legacy, and family at this historic 500-acre estate.

Natural Attractions of Far North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Falls Park: Enjoy scenic views of Potomac River, hike along the trails and have a picnic at this 800-acre park.
  • Shirlington Dog Park: Take your furry friend for a run and socialize with other dogs at this popular dog park.
  • Upton Hill Regional Park: Features mini-golf, batting cages, and a waterpark, and offers plenty of outdoor fun for families and visitors of all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far North

Far North is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts with various scenic hiking and biking routes.

  • Potomac Overlook Regional Park: Features 70 acres of woodland, trails and nature center.
  • Mount Vernon Trail: A trail running along the Potomac from Theodore Roosevelt Island to George Washington’s Mount Vernon.

Best Things to Do in Far North: Activities and Tours

  • Escape Room Arlington: Work as a team and try to escape from a themed room before the time runs out.
  • The Observation Deck at CEB Tower: Take the elevator to the 31st floor and enjoy panoramic views of the Washington D.C. area.
  • Arlington Cinema & Drafthouse: Enjoy a movie while having drinks and food at this retro cinema and bar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Far North

Day 1: National Museum of African American History and Culture > Upton Hill Regional Park > Escape Room Arlington.

Day 2: Arlington Cemetery > Mount Vernon Trail > The Observation Deck at CEB Tower.

Day 3: Mount Vernon > Great Falls Park > Arlington Cinema & Drafthouse.

Day 4: Walk around Crystal City Center > Fashion Centre at Pentagon City > Pentagon Row.

Day 5: National Air and Space Museum > National Mall > US Capitol.

Day 6: Smithsonian’s National Zoo > Tidal Basin > Hains Point.

Day 7: Sugar Shack Donuts & Coffee > Thornton River Grille > Little Washington Winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Far North

Far North represents a diverse food scene with restaurants that cater to a variety of tastes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Half-smoke: A spicy sausage served with mustard, onion, and chili.
  • Crab cakes: Crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, made with local Chesapeake Bay crab meat.
  • Shrimp and grits: A classic southern dish made with creamy grits and shrimp.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Try some locally brewed beer, such as Port City Brewing Company, Lost Rhino Brewing Co., and New District Brewing Company.
  • Wine: Virginia is a wine destination, and there are local wineries like Little Washington Winery, Paradise Springs Winery, and Barrel Oak Winery.

Where to Stay in Far North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Far North, ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious.

  • The Westin Arlington Gateway: Offers luxe rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.
  • HI Arlington Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to stay in a hostel.
  • Hyatt Centric Arlington: A modern hotel located near the metro station, and is conveniently located for commuters and tourists.

What to Buy in Far North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Far North has many local products worth taking home as a souvenir.

  • Virginia peanuts: A great snack to take home, you can purchase these at the Virginia Peanut Company.
  • Artisanal chocolates: Try some locally-made chocolates at Artisan Confections.
  • Local wine and beer: Bring home some local wine or beer from one of the region’s wineries or breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Far North

Far North has a rich cultural heritage with diverse traditions and customs. It is common to see people from different backgrounds coexisting in the area, and there is a significant emphasis on community building and support.

Best Time to Visit Far North

The best time to visit Far North is in the spring and fall when the temperatures are mild, and the region is abundant in natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far North

Do:

  • Do wear comfortable shoes when exploring the region.
  • Do be respectful of cultural differences.
  • Do take metro or bus to explore the region, as parking can be challenging.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the region.
  • Don’t engage in activities that are prohibited or illegal.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Far North

Far North is generally a safe area to visit, but as a visitor, it’s best to take precautions. Keep valuables in a safe place, and be aware of your surroundings. It’s always better to explore the area in groups.

Health in Far North

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in and around Far North, so visitors can rest easy knowing they have access to quality healthcare.

Getting to Far North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Far North

  • Reagan National Airport: The closest airport to Far North and the fastest way to get to the region.

Bus Stations Near Far North

  • Union Station: The main bus terminal in Washington, D.C. and a major transportation hub for the region.

Other Options

  • Metro: The region has a comprehensive metro system, which is the most convenient way to travel around.

How to Get Around in Far North

Getting around in Far North is relatively easy with metro, buses, or taxis. The areas around the major attractions are walkable, so exploring on foot is also possible.

Where to Go Next from Far North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Washington D.C.: The nation’s capital has a host of attractions and activities, including historic landmarks, museums, and monuments.
  • Virginia Beach: A popular coastal destination known for its wide beach and bustling boardwalk.
  • Shenandoah National Park: A national park that boasts the scenic Skyline Drive and numerous hiking trails.

Conclusion

Far North is a region worth visiting and exploring for its unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. From historic landmarks to outdoor adventures and a diverse food scene, it offers something for every type of traveler.
