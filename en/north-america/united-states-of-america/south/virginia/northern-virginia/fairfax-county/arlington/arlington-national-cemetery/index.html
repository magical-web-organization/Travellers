Introduction to Arlington National Cemetery

Arlington National Cemetery is one of the most important national cemeteries in the United States. Located in Arlington, Virginia, the cemetery is the final resting place for more than 400,000 military service members, veterans, and their families. Founded during the Civil War, the cemetery has a rich history and offers a solemn and respectful tribute to those who have served and sacrificed for their country.

Visiting the cemetery can be a deeply moving experience, and it’s important to plan ahead to ensure that you get the most out of your trip. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of everything you need to know before you go, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, must-try dishes, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Arlington National Cemetery

There are many places to visit in Arlington National Cemetery, each with its own unique history and significance. Here are some of the most important and culturally significant attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arlington House: This historic mansion was built in 1802 and was the home of Robert E. Lee before the Civil War. Today, the house is run by the National Park Service and serves as a museum that honors Lee’s legacy while also exploring the history of slavery and the Civil War.

  • Memorial Amphitheater: This impressive structure, located at the heart of the cemetery, is home to the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier and serves as the site of many important ceremonies and events.

  • Women in Military Service for America Memorial: This memorial honors the contributions of women who have served in the armed forces throughout American history.

Natural Attractions of Arlington National Cemetery: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • John F. Kennedy Gravesite: Located on a hillside with a beautiful view of Washington, D.C., this gravesite is the final resting place for President Kennedy and his family members.

  • Arlington National Cemetery Tour: This guided tour takes visitors around the cemetery to explore historic sites, learn about the cemetery’s history, and pay respect to the soldiers buried here.

  • Changing of the Guard at the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier: This moving ceremony takes place every hour on the hour and serves as a reminder of the sacrifices made by those who have given their lives in service to their country.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arlington National Cemetery

Arlington National Cemetery offers several hiking and biking routes for visitors who want to explore the grounds at their own pace. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Memorial Walk: This 1.4 mile walk takes visitors through important historical sites within the cemetery, including the grave sites of President Kennedy and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier.

  • Self-guided bicycle tour: Visitors can rent bicycles and explore the cemetery on their own, stopping at historic sites and taking in the beautiful scenery along the way.

Best Things to Do in Arlington National Cemetery: Activities and Tours

In addition to visiting important sites within the cemetery, there are several other activities and tours that visitors can experience. Here are a few examples:

  • Sunrise and Sunset Tours: These guided tours take visitors through the cemetery during the early morning or late evening, offering a unique perspective on the grounds and solemn moments of reflection.

  • Wreath Laying Ceremony: Visitors can participate in a solemn ceremony to honor the memory of fallen soldiers by laying a wreath at gravesites throughout the cemetery.

  • Tram Tours: For visitors who prefer a more relaxed experience, a tram tour offers a comprehensive overview of the cemetery’s history and most important sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arlington National Cemetery

For visitors who want to get the most out of their trip to Arlington National Cemetery, here is a sample itinerary for a week-long visit:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Arlington, check into accommodations, and explore the immediate area
Day 2 Take a self-guided bicycle tour of the cemetery and stop at important historical sites
Day 3 Take a guided tour of the cemetery and attend a wreath laying ceremony

Day 5 Spend the day exploring Washington, D.C.
Day 6 Attend the Changing of the Guard ceremony at the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier and take a tram tour of the cemetery
Day 7 Depart Arlington

Food and Drink Scene of Arlington National Cemetery

While there are no restaurants within the cemetery itself, there are several great dining options in the surrounding area. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Half Smokes: This signature dish of Washington, D.C. is a spicy sausage served with mustard, onions, and chili.

  • Virginia Ham: This salty, cured ham is a classic Southern dish and can be found at many restaurants in Arlington.

  • Crab Cakes: Made with fresh blue crab meat, crab cakes are a staple of the Maryland/Virginia seafood scene and can be found at many seafood restaurants in Arlington.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine: Virginia is home to many great wineries, and visitors can enjoy local wines at many restaurants and bars in Arlington.

  • Port City Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers and is a great place to stop for a beer after a long day of exploring the cemetery.

Where to Stay in Arlington National Cemetery: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in the Arlington area, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few options to consider:

  • The Americana Hotel: This affordable hotel is located just a few miles from the cemetery and offers clean, comfortable rooms.

  • Hi Washington, D.C. Hostel: For travelers on a budget, this hostel offers both shared and private rooms and is located within easy access to the cemetery and other attractions.

  • The Morrison-Clark Historic Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of downtown Washington, D.C. and offers elegant rooms and top-notch amenities.

What to Buy in Arlington National Cemetery: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many great souvenirs and local products to bring home from a visit to Arlington National Cemetery. Here are a few ideas:

  • Challenge Coins: These coins, often given out as military awards, come in a variety of designs and make great collectibles.

  • Historical Books: Many bookstores in the Arlington area offer a range of historical books on the Civil War and other military events.

  • Military Apparel: Visitors can purchase t-shirts, hats, and other apparel that honors the memory of fallen soldiers and veterans.

Local Culture and Customs of Arlington National Cemetery

Visitors to Arlington National Cemetery are reminded to maintain a solemn and respectful demeanor during their visit. Dressing appropriately, remaining quiet, and avoiding photography in certain areas are all signs of respect for the soldiers and families whose loved ones are buried here.

Best Time to Visit Arlington National Cemetery

Arlington National Cemetery is open year-round, but visitors should be aware that certain times of year may be more crowded than others. The spring and fall are popular times to visit when the weather is mild and the grounds are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arlington National Cemetery

When visiting Arlington National Cemetery, visitors should observe the following guidelines:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately, wearing comfortable shoes and clothing that is respectful of the solemnity of the surroundings.

  • Remain quiet and respectful during your visit, avoiding conversations and noise that may be disruptive to those around you.

  • Use photography responsibly, being mindful of the feelings of those visiting loved ones at the cemetery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t bring food or drink onto the grounds.

  • Don’t damage or deface any of the grave markers or monuments.

  • Don’t behave in a way that is disrespectful to the memory of the fallen soldiers and their families.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Arlington National Cemetery should be aware of certain safety and health concerns before they arrive.

Safety in Arlington National Cemetery

Visitors should remain aware of their surroundings and take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid wandering into restricted areas and always act respectfully toward other visitors.

Health in Arlington National Cemetery

Visitors should be prepared for the weather by wearing appropriate clothing and bringing sunscreen and water. Those with mobility issues should be aware that the cemetery is located on a hillside, and may require extra time and effort to navigate.

Getting to Arlington National Cemetery: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arlington National Cemetery is easily accessible via public transportation or car. Here are a few options to consider:

Airports Near Arlington National Cemetery

  • Ronald Reagan National Airport: Located just a few miles from the cemetery, this airport is a convenient option for visitors arriving by air.

  • Dulles International Airport: Located about 30 minutes outside of Arlington, this airport offers connections to many major cities throughout the world.

Bus Stations Near Arlington National Cemetery

  • Greyhound: Buses from this popular bus line arrive and depart from several stations in the Washington, D.C. area.

Other Options

  • Metro: Visitors can take the Metro to the Arlington Cemetery station, which is located just a short walk from the cemetery.

  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft: Taxis and ride-sharing services are widely available in the Arlington area and offer a convenient way to get around.

How to Get Around in Arlington National Cemetery

Arlington National Cemetery is best explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take a guided tram tour or rent a personal mobility assistive device (PMAD) for a more leisurely experience.

Where to Go Next from Arlington National Cemetery: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many great destinations to explore near Arlington National Cemetery, including:

  • Washington, D.C.: This historic city is just a few miles away from the cemetery and offers a wide variety of museums, monuments, and other cultural attractions.

  • George Washington’s Mount Vernon: This historic estate, located just a few miles south of the cemetery, was the home of George Washington and is now a popular tourist destination.

  • Old Town Alexandria: This charming historic district is home to many great restaurants, shops, and museums and is just a few miles from the cemetery.

Conclusion

Arlington National Cemetery is a place of solemn remembrance and tribute and is an important destination for anyone interested in the history of the United States or the sacrifices made by military service members and their families. By planning your visit carefully and observing the customs of the cemetery, you can ensure that your visit is respectful, meaningful, and informative.
