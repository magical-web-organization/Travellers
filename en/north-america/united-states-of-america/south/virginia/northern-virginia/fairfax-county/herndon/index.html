Introduction to Herndon

Herndon is a charming town in northern Virginia, located in Fairfax County. It is filled with historical landmarks, natural wonders, and a welcoming community that values its culture and traditions. Visitors can experience the town’s unique blend of modern amenities and rich history, making it an excellent destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.

In this comprehensive guide to Herndon, we will share all the best things to do, see, and eat in this beautiful town, as well as offer tips on local customs, safety, transportation, and nearby destinations to explore. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Herndon

Herndon is a town filled with a variety of attractions, both man-made and natural. Here are some of the must-see places to visit during your trip to Herndon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Herndon Depot Museum - a restored train station built in 1857 that houses exhibits on the town’s history and transportation industry
  • Elden Street Players Industrial Strength Theater - a local theater that showcases classic plays, musicals, and contemporary productions
  • Smithsonian National Air and Space Museum’s Udvar-Hazy Center - a museum with interactive exhibits and over 300 aircraft and space artifacts

Natural Attractions of Herndon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Frying Pan Farm Park - a 135-acre park that features a farm and working blacksmith shop, as well as farm animals and seasonal events
  • Sugarland Run Stream Valley Park - a park with hiking trails, picnic areas, and a creek for fishing and kayaking
  • Lake Fairfax Park - a picturesque park with a 20-acre lake, campsites, and plenty of outdoor activities like swimming, hiking, and fishing

Hiking/Biking Routes of Herndon

Herndon is a town that has plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Herndon:

  • Washington and Old Dominion Trail - a 45-mile paved trail that runs from Shirlington to Purcellville, passing through Herndon
  • Herndon Centennial Trail - a 4.7-mile trail that runs through the heart of Herndon
  • Difficult Run Trail - a 6.8-mile trail that offers a challenging hike through beautiful scenery and picturesque streams

Best Things to Do in Herndon: Activities and Tours

Herndon offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors of all ages entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Herndon:

  • Visit the Friday Night Live! Concert Series - a weekly summer concert series featuring live music, food trucks, and family-friendly activities
  • Take a Segway Tour of Herndon - a guided tour that takes visitors on a fun and unique way to see the town’s landmarks and attractions
  • Take a Wine Tour - Herndon is situated near several award-winning wineries that offer tastings and tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Herndon

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Herndon:

Local Culture and Customs of Herndon

Herndon is a town proud of its diversity, rich history, and local traditions. Visitors should be aware of some of the local cultures and customs in the town:

  • Herndon farmers market is held every Thursday and promotes local agriculture and agricultural tourism.
  • Herndon’s local festivals include the Herndon Festival, the Labor Day Festival, and the Friday Night Live! Concert Series.

Best Time to Visit Herndon

The best time to visit Herndon is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Visitors can also enjoy the town’s festive spirit during the summer months, with plenty of outdoor concerts, festivals, and events to attend.

Getting to Herndon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Herndon is located near several major airports and other transportation options. Here are some ways to get to Herndon:

Airports Near Herndon

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA) - located about 25 miles from Herndon

Bus Stations Near Herndon

  • Herndon-Monroe Park & Ride Lot - a transit hub with bus service to major destinations in Northern Virginia and Washington, D.C.
  • Fairfax Connector Bus - a local bus service with routes in and around Herndon

  • Public transportation - Herndon has a local bus service, Fairfax Connector, that offers routes to major destinations in Northern Virginia and Washington, D.C.

Where to Go Next from Herndon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
