Introduction to Falls Church

Welcome to Falls Church, Virginia – a charming city located in Fairfax County, Northern Virginia. It is conveniently located near major attractions like Washington D.C (15-minute drive), Arlington (10-minute drive), and Great Falls National Park (30-minute drive). Falls Church is home to a vibrant community, with a rich culture and history, delicious cuisine, and natural beauty. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, activities to do, and local culture to experience in Falls Church.

List of Places to Visit in Falls Church

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Falls Church Episcopal - A historic church that dates back to 1734, with stunning architecture and a rich history.
  • Tinner Hill Historic Site - A site dedicated to celebrating the first rural branch of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP).
  • Cherry Hill Farmhouse - A restored farmhouse that tells the story of life in Falls Church during the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Falls Church: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Browns Mill Park - A serene park with walking trails, play equipment, and a creek.
  • W&OD Trail - A 45-mile trail that runs through Falls Church, perfect for those who want to hike, bike, or jog.
  • Tuckahoe Park - This park has sports fields, picnic areas, and playgrounds, making it the perfect spot for family outings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Falls Church

  • W&OD Trail - The 45-mile trail is perfect for both hiking and biking, and provides a beautiful backdrop for any outdoor enthusiast.
  • Tuckahoe Park - With plenty of trails and paths around the park, visitors can enjoy scenic views while hiking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Falls Church: Activities and Tours

  • Falls Church Farmers Market - Held weekly on Saturdays, the farmers market features local vendors, fresh produce, and live music.
  • Escape Room Fairfax - Perfect for families and groups, this escape room offers challenging puzzles and games.
  • The State Theatre - A restored historic theatre that hosts concerts, shows, and events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Falls Church

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Tinner Hill Historic Site, followed by lunch at The Sandwich Shop (try their famous cheesesteak!). End the day with a relaxing stroll at Tuckahoe Park.
Day 2 Get your day started for a hike or bike ride on the W&OD Trail. Afterward, enjoy brunch at Famous Toastery before heading to The State Theatre for an evening show.
Day 3 Spend the day exploring the many museums and historical places in the city, like The Falls Church Episcopal and Cherry Hill Farmhouse. Cap off the day with dinner at the wildly popular taco spot, Taco Bamba.
Day 4 Take a trip to Great Falls National Park for some hiking and breathtaking views. Stop at Caboose Brewing Company on the way back to Falls Church for a delicious dinner and locally brewed beer.
Day 5 Spend the day shopping and exploring the arts and crafts scene at the Torpedo Factory Art Center. Dine at Clare and Don’s Beach Shack, known for its fresh seafood and beach vibes.
Day 6 Take a day-trip to the National Mall in Washington D.C, and visit the many monuments, museums, and landmarks.
Day 7 End your trip with a visit to Falls Church Farmers Market for fresh produce and local vendors. Enjoy the outdoors at Browns Mill Park, followed by dinner at La Côte d’Or Café, offering traditional French cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Falls Church

Falls Church is a diverse and inclusive community, known for its rich history and friendly locals. Residents of Falls Church value their culture and heritage, which can be seen in the many museums and historical places throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit Falls Church

The best time to visit Falls Church is in the fall season from September to November, with mild weather and beautiful foliage. Spring season from March to May is also a great time to visit, as the city comes alive with cherry blossoms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Falls Church

Do:

  • Learn about the rich history of Falls Church by visiting the many museums and historical sites.
  • Try the delicious cuisine of the city, including cheesesteaks, tacos, and seafood.
  • Go hiking or biking on the W&OD trail for scenic views of the city.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.
  • Don’t be rude to locals or other tourists.
  • Don’t ignore the amazing local culture and heritage of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Falls Church

Falls Church is generally a safe city, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take general safety precautions, like securing their belongings and being alert in crowded areas.

Health in Falls Church

Visitors to Falls Church should take standard preventative measures, like staying hydrated, washing hands regularly, and wearing sunscreen.

How to Get Around in Falls Church

Falls Church is a pedestrian-friendly city, with many attractions and shops within a short walking distance. Visitors can also take advantage of ridesharing services like Uber and Lyft, or rent a bike and explore the city on the W&OD trail.
