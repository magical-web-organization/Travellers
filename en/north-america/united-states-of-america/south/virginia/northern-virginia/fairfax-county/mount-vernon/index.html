Introduction to Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is a charming historic town located in Fairfax County, Northern Virginia, just a short drive from Washington, DC. Best known as the home of George Washington, the first president of the United States, Mount Vernon is a must-visit destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure seekers alike.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the rich history of the town, taking in the natural beauty of the area, or simply relaxing and enjoying some good food and drink, Mount Vernon has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Vernon

Looking for a list of the top places to visit in Mount Vernon? Check out our recommendations below.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • George Washington’s Mount Vernon: The most famous attraction in Mount Vernon, George Washington’s Mount Vernon is a beautifully preserved historic estate that was once the home of the first president of the United States. Visitors can tour the mansion, explore the beautiful gardens, and learn about the life and legacy of George Washington.
  • Mount Vernon’s African American Museum and Memorial: This newly opened museum is dedicated to honoring the contribution of African Americans to the history of Mount Vernon and America as a whole.
  • Gadsby’s Tavern Museum: Located in nearby Alexandria, VA, Gadsby’s Tavern Museum is a historic site that was once the center of social and political life in the area. Visitors can tour the museum and learn about the tavern’s rich history and the important role it played in American history.

Natural Attractions of Mount Vernon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Vernon Trail: This beautiful trail runs for 18 miles along the Potomac River, providing stunning views of the George Washington Memorial Parkway, Washington, DC, and the river.
  • Huntley Meadows Park: This 1,500-acre park is home to a wide variety of wildlife and is a popular spot for hiking, birdwatching, and nature photography.
  • Piscataway Park: Located just across the river in Maryland, this beautiful park features stunning views of the Potomac River, and is home to a variety of historic sites, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Vernon

If you’re looking for some great hiking and biking routes in Mount Vernon, be sure to check out:

  • Mount Vernon Trail: This 18-mile trail runs from Mount Vernon to Rosslyn in Arlington, VA, and is popular with both hikers and cyclists.
  • George Washington Memorial Parkway: This scenic drive runs from Mount Vernon to Great Falls Park, providing stunning views of the Potomac River and Washington, DC.
  • Fort Hunt Park: This beautiful park is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching, and offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Mount Vernon: Activities and Tours

Looking for some fun activities and tours to do while in Mount Vernon? Here are some great suggestions:

  • Potomac Riverboat Company: This company offers a variety of tours and cruises along the Potomac River, providing stunning views of Washington, DC and the surrounding area.
  • Chesapeake & Ohio Canal National Historical Park: This beautiful park is a great place to take a peaceful bike ride or hike along the canal towpath, and features stunning views of the Potomac River.
  • Mount Vernon Inn Restaurant: This historic inn offers delicious Southern cuisine and a charming atmosphere, making it a great place to relax and enjoy a meal after a day of sightseeing.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Vernon

Looking to try some delicious local cuisine while visiting Mount Vernon? Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: This Southern classic is a must-try at the Mount Vernon Inn Restaurant.
  • Peanut Soup: This unique dish is a Virginia specialty, and can be found at many local restaurants, including the Mount Vernon Inn Restaurant.
  • Virginia Ham: Another Virginia classic, this delicious ham can be found at many local restaurants and markets.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine: The Mount Vernon area is home to many wineries, making it a great place to sample some local Virginia wine.
  • Hard Cider: Cideries are becoming increasingly popular in the area, with many offering tasting rooms and tours.
  • Craft Beer: The area is home to a number of craft breweries, offering a wide variety of local beers to sample.

Where to Stay in Mount Vernon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Looking for a great place to stay while in Mount Vernon? Here are some top options:

  • The Inn at Mount Vernon Farm: This beautiful inn is located in nearby Sperryville, VA, and features stunning views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
  • The Holladay House Bed and Breakfast: Located in nearby Orange, VA, this charming bed and breakfast is a great option for those looking for a quieter, more peaceful retreat.
  • Hilton Alexandria Old Town: This beautiful hotel is located in nearby Alexandria, VA, and features luxurious accommodations and a great location near many of the area’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Mount Vernon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a special souvenir or local product to bring home from Mount Vernon? Here are some top options:

  • George Washington-related souvenirs: From Mount Vernon-themed clothing to replica cherry trees, there are plenty of souvenirs related to the town’s most famous resident.
  • Virginia wine: Many local wineries offer tasting rooms and gift shops, making it easy to bring home a bottle of Virginia wine.
  • Artisanal cheese: The area is home to many cheese makers, offering a wide variety of delicious, locally made cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Vernon

When visiting Mount Vernon, it’s important to be respectful of the area’s rich history and heritage. Be sure to:

  • Dress respectfully when visiting historic sites and museums.
  • Be mindful of local traditions and customs.
  • Respect the environment and natural beauty of the area.

Best Time to Visit Mount Vernon

The best time to visit Mount Vernon is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the area is at its most beautiful. Summer can be crowded and hot, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Vernon

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mount Vernon:

Do:

  • Visit George Washington’s Mount Vernon.
  • Take a hike or bike ride along the Mount Vernon Trail.
  • Try some Virginia wine or cider.

Don’t:

  • Be disruptive or disrespectful of historic sites or museums.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

When visiting Mount Vernon, it’s important to stay safe and healthy. Here are some tips:

Safety in Mount Vernon

  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert for any potential dangers.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Be cautious when driving, especially on narrow, winding roads.

Health in Mount Vernon

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Stay hydrated when spending time outdoors.
  • Seek medical attention if you develop any symptoms of illness.

Getting to Mount Vernon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Located just a short drive from Washington, DC, Mount Vernon is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Mount Vernon

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport
  • Dulles International Airport
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport

Bus Stations Near Mount Vernon

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in nearby Alexandria, VA.
  • Megabus: Offers service to Washington, DC.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Offers service to Washington, DC.
  • Uber/Lyft: Available in the area.

How to Get Around in Mount Vernon

While having a car can be helpful when exploring Mount Vernon, there are also plenty of other options for getting around, including:

  • Walking: Many of the area’s top attractions are located within walking distance of each other.
  • Biking: The Mount Vernon Trail is a great place to take a bike ride and explore the area.
  • Public transportation: Bus and train services are available, making it easy to get around without a car.

Where to Go Next from Mount Vernon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking for some nearby destinations and excursions to explore after your visit to Mount Vernon? Here are some top options:

  • Washington, DC: Just a short drive or train ride away, the nation’s capital offers endless opportunities for sightseeing and exploration.
  • Shenandoah National Park: Located just a few hours southwest of Mount Vernon, this beautiful park features stunning mountain landscapes and a variety of hiking trails.
  • Harpers Ferry National Historical Park: Located just over an hour northwest of Mount Vernon, this historic park is a great place to learn about the area’s rich history and enjoy some scenic hiking trails.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or simply relaxing and enjoying some good food and drink, Mount Vernon is a must-visit destination. With so much to see and do, it’s easy to see why this charming town has become such a popular destination for travelers from around the world.
