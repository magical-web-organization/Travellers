Introduction to Great Falls

Great Falls is a scenic town in Fairfax County, located in Northern Virginia, United States of America. The town is named after the stunning Great Falls of the Potomac River, which provides a backdrop for the town’s beautiful natural landscape. Great Falls has something for everyone, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors to Great Falls can explore culturally significant museums, indulge in outdoor adventures, taste local cuisine, and more. Read on to discover the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and more in Great Falls, Virginia.

List of Places to Visit in Great Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Great Falls is steeped in history, and visitors can explore the town’s rich past by visiting museums and historical places. Here are some of the top culturally significant attractions to explore in Great Falls:

  • Great Falls Grange: This historic building was built in 1929 and was previously used as a community center. Today, it serves as a museum and event venue.
  • Colvin Run Mill: This restored 19th-century water-powered mill provides visitors a glimpse into the life and times of early Great Falls residents.
  • Forestville Schoolhouse: This restored one-room schoolhouse is a testament to educational institutions of the early 1900s, providing an educational opportunity for visitors from all over the world.
  • Museum of the Shenandoah Valley: This museum houses an impressive collection of Virginia’s art, decorative arts, and architectural styles from the 18th, 19th, and early 20th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Great Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Great Falls is home to several natural attractions that provide opportunities for outdoor adventures and breathtaking scenic views. Nature lovers will appreciate these top outdoor attractions in Great Falls:

  • Great Falls Park: This National Park offers stunning views of the Potomac River and its cascading waterfalls. Activities include hiking, kayaking, fishing, and rock climbing.
  • Scott’s Run Nature Preserve: This nature preserve covers 336 acres of scenic wilderness, providing trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • River Bend Park: This park offers walking, hiking, and biking trails, picnic areas, and views of the Potomac River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Falls

Great Falls is a haven for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and routes for beginners to advanced-levels.

  • The Billy Goat Trail has three sections, A, B, and C. Section A is a must-do and offers dramatic views of the Potomac River, while section B is less difficult than section C.
  • Difficult Run Trail is a 4.5-mile hike that features steep terrain and beautiful views of the Potomac River and local flora and fauna.
  • Potomac Heritage Trail is a 10-mile out-and-back trail that offers visitors beautiful river views, wildflowers, and wildlife sightings.
  • Washington and Old Dominion Railroad Trail is a 44.8-mile trail that features scenic views of nature and historic sites from Arlington to Purcellville.

Best Things to Do in Great Falls: Activities and Tours

Great Falls offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top things to do in Great Falls:

  • Great Falls Park Boat Tours: Visitors can take a guided tour of the Potomac River and learn about the history and ecology of the area.
  • Great Falls Park Scenic Drive: Visitors can take a leisurely drive along the Potomac River and enjoy breathtaking views of the park.
  • Great Falls Farmers Market: Visitors can explore the local food scene by attending the Great Falls Farmers Market, which offers a variety of products, including fresh produce, meats, eggs, and artisanal foods.
  • Colvin Run Mill Tour: Visitors can learn about the history and workings of this restored 19th-century water-powered mill.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great Falls

Visitors can spend an entire week in Great Falls exploring the local culture, cuisine, and natural beauty. Here is a sample week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Great Falls Park and Billy Goat Trail
  • Day 2: Colvin Run Mill and Forestville Schoolhouse
  • Day 3: Visit the Museum of the Shenandoah Valley and Scott’s Run Nature Preserve
  • Day 4: Washington and Old Dominion Railroad Trail bike ride, followed by a farm-to-table dinner at the local restaurant.
  • Day 5: Great Falls Park Boat Tour and River Bend Park picnic
  • Day 6: Great Falls Farmers Market and National Mall Tour (just 30 minutes away)
  • Day 7: Historic Georgetown and Downtown DC Tour (just 15 minutes away)

Food and Drink Scene of Great Falls

Great Falls has an excellent food and drink scene, with local restaurants featuring fresh, locally-sourced foods and craft beers and wines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Virginia ham and biscuits
  • Crab cakes
  • Oysters
  • Grass-fed beef burgers

Local Beverages

  • Virginia wines from local vineyards, such as Paradise Springs Winery and 868 Estate Vineyards
  • Craft beer from Alehouse and Caboose Brewing Company
  • Hard apple ciders from Wild Hare Ciders

Where to Stay in Great Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Great Falls offers visitors a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top accommodation options in Great Falls:

  • The Inn at Great Falls: This charming and cozy inn is located near the Great Falls Park and features comfortable and spacious rooms and suites.
  • B&Bs and Guesthouses: Great Falls has several guesthouses and B&Bs, such as the River Bend Bed and Breakfast, which is perfect for couples and families.
  • Budget-Friendly Options: Great Falls has several budget-friendly options, such as the Extended Stay America, which is perfect for long-term stays.

What to Buy in Great Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Great Falls can take home some beautiful reminders of their trip, such as:

  • Handcrafted pottery and ceramics
  • Handmade jewelry
  • Local honey and jams
  • Virginia peanuts

Local Culture and Customs of Great Falls

Virginia has a rich and diverse culture, which visitors can experience while exploring Great Falls. Here are a few aspects of local culture and customs in Great Falls:

  • Southerners value hospitality and are always welcoming to visitors.
  • Virginia has a deep history and is proud of its monuments, museums, and historical sites.
  • Virginia is home to various ethnic groups, including African American, Native American, and European American.

Best Time to Visit Great Falls

The best time to visit Great Falls is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild and enjoyable. Additionally, autumn is a perfect time for nature lovers to experience the fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Falls

Do:

  • Respect the dress code in churches and other religious institutions.
  • Try the local cuisine, such as Virginia ham, crab cakes, and oysters.
  • Be respectful to other visitors and locals in tourist places.

Don’t:

  • Bring firearms into restaurants or other public places.
  • Use drugs or drink alcohol in public places.
  • Litter or damage natural sites and parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Falls

Great Falls is a safe and peaceful town, with friendly locals and low crime rates. However, tourists should take normal precautions against theft and use caution when hiking or participating in other outdoor recreation.

Health in Great Falls

Great Falls has several medical facilities, including pharmacies, clinics, and hospitals, that offer emergency and non-emergency medical care. Visitors are advised to carry adequate health insurance while traveling.

Getting to Great Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Great Falls is easily accessible by car, bus, and airplane.

Airports Near Great Falls

  • Reagan National Airport in Washington, D.C. is the closest airport to Great Falls, only 20 miles away.
  • Dulles International Airport in Virginia is a bit further from Great Falls, 24 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Great Falls

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal in Washington D.C. offers easy connectivity with Great Falls.
  • Metro bus service connects Great Falls with Virginia’s surrounding areas.

Other Options

  • If you are driving, Great Falls is easily accessible via I-495, I-95, and GW Memorial Parkway.
  • Taxis, ride-sharing services, and car rental services are the easiest ways to get around Great Falls.

How to Get Around in Great Falls

Visitors can get around the town by renting a car or riding the bus. Alternatively, travelers can also take advantage of ride-sharing services such as Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Great Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Great Falls is a perfect base for exploring the nearby attractions, such as:

  • Washington D.C.: The nation’s capital is only a 30-minute drive from Great Falls and offers a range of activities and tourist attractions.
  • Arlington Cemetery: Tours of the cemetery are available daily and are a must-see for history buffs.
  • Virginia Wine Country: Local vineyards provide daily tours and tastings.

Conclusion

Great Falls is a hidden gem in Northern Virginia, offering a blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural diversity. Visitors to Great Falls can explore museums, indulge in outdoor adventures, taste local cuisine, and more. With so much to see and do, Great Falls is ideal for anyone looking for a unique and peaceful vacation.
