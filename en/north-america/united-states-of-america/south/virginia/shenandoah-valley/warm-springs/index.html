Introduction to Warm Springs

Warm Springs is a captivating town in the Shenandoah Valley of Virginia, located in the southern region of the United States of America. Renowned for its natural hot springs that have been attracting visitors since the 1700s, this town offers much more than a relaxing soak. Its rich history, stunning mountain views, and outdoor adventure activities make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a mix of relaxation, culture, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Warm Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bath County Historical Society
  • Warm Springs Gallery
  • Garth Newel Music Center
  • The Warm Springs Presbyterian Church Historical Landmark

Natural Attractions of Warm Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Omni Homestead Resort - Virginia’s premier resort known for its four championship golf courses, skiing, and ice-skating facilities.
  • Waterfall Hike - A 2.7-mile trail that ends at a 30-feet waterfall.
  • Lake Moomaw - An idyllic spot for boating, swimming, and fishing.
  • Jefferson Pools - Relax in the natural hot springs where Thomas Jefferson is said to have soaked in.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Warm Springs

  • Hidden Valley Trail - A 3.2-mile loop trail ideal for hiking and trail running.
  • Bolar Mountain Recreation Area - A network of trails ideal for mountain biking, horseback riding, and hiking.
  • Jackson River Scenic Trail - A 14-mile scenic trail perfect for biking, hiking, and walking.

Best Things to Do in Warm Springs: Activities and Tours

  • Take a dip in the hot springs at the Jefferson Pools.
  • Go fishing at Lake Moomaw.
  • Experience fly-fishing on the Jackson River.
  • Take a guided tour of The Homestead Casino Parlor.

Week-Long Itinerary for Warm Springs

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Bath County Historical Society, Jefferson Pools, and warm springs.
Day 2 Experience mountain biking on the Bolar Mountain Recreation Area trails.
Day 3 Explore Lake Moomaw and go for a swim and take scenic hike.
Day 4 Visit Garth Newel Music Center for a concert.
Day 5 Go fly-fishing in the Jackson River.
Day 6 Take a guided tour of The Homestead Casino Parlor.
Day 7 Spend a day exploring Warm Springs Gallery & do some souvenir shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Warm Springs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Virginia Ham Biscuits - A Southern classic ideal for breakfast.
  • Fried Chicken - A traditional Southern favorite served up with sides such as mac and cheese, collard greens, and biscuits.
  • Peanut Butter Fudge - A popular locally made sweet treat.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine - Visit a local vineyard and sample some of the world-renowned Virginia wine.
  • Homestead Beer - Enjoy a refreshing pint of Homestead beer, brewed locally.

Where to Stay in Warm Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Omni Homestead Resort - A premier resort offering luxurious accommodation including traditional guest rooms, suites, and private cottages.
  • Country Inn at High View - A cozy and quaint inn in the heart of Warm Springs.
  • Fort Lewis Lodge - A historic farm-stay ideal for guest looking to immerse themselves in the area’s natural beauty.

What to Buy in Warm Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-crafted pottery from Warm Springs Gallery.
  • Artwork from the Garth Newel Music Center.
  • Virginia ham from local farmers markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Warm Springs

Warm Springs and the surrounding areas are home to many Appalachian traditions and customs. These include a love of arts and crafts, music, and food. Visitors should look out for the many local art galleries, music concerts, and craft fairs. Manners and politeness are also highly valued here, and visitors are expected to show respect to residents and their customs.

Best Time to Visit Warm Springs

The best time to visit Warm Springs depends on what kind of weather and activities you prefer. The peak seasons are summer (June-August) when the weather is warm and autumn (September-November) when the leaves change color. Winter (December-February) is ideal for winter sports fans, while spring (March-May) is perfect for hikers and those who want to experience the beauty of Virginia’s wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Warm Springs

Do:

  • Say “Please,” “Thank you,” and “Excuse me.”
  • Get out in nature and explore the countryside.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or dump trash in natural areas or on trails.
  • Speak loudly or be disruptive in quiet areas.
  • Disrespect local traditions and residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Warm Springs

Warm Springs is a safe place to visit, but visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and practice normal safety precautions like locking car doors and staying on well-lit and busy streets at night.

Health in Warm Springs

Visitors should be prepared for hot weather, particularly in the summer months. Insect repellent is a must-have in the warmer months. Sunscreen and a hat are also recommended when spending time outdoors in the sun.

Getting to Warm Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Warm Springs

  • Roanoke Regional Airport (ROA) - Approximately 1 hour and 20 minutes away.
  • Greenbrier Valley Airport (LWB) - Approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Warm Springs

  • Greyhound Station Staunton, VA - A 45-minute drive from Warm Springs.
  • Megabus Station Charlottesville, VA - A 1 hour and 45-minute drive from Warm Springs.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Available at both Roanoke and Greenbrier Valley airports.
  • Private Car Service - Taxi and car service options are available from both Roanoke and Greenbrier Valley airports.

How to Get Around in Warm Springs

The best way to get around in Warm Springs is by car. There are rental cars available at both Roanoke Regional Airport and Greenbrier Valley Airport. Taxis and private car services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Warm Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Staunton, VA - A 45-minute drive, explore the town’s historic museums and other attractions.
  • Lexington, VA - A 1 hour and 30-minute drive, visit the Virginia Military Institute and the Stonewall Jackson House Museum.
  • Snowshoe, WV - A 1 hour and 50-minute drive, perfect for skiing and other snow sports.

Conclusion

Warm Springs offers something for everyone, from relaxation to outdoor adventure and cultural attractions. Visitors can soak in natural hot springs, hike in picturesque mountain trails, and explore the historical landmarks. The food and drink scene is also exceptional, with locally-sourced dishes and wine. Whether you’re looking for a romantic getaway, a weekend of family fun, or simply a place to escape the hustle and bustle of city life, Warm Springs awaits you with open arms.
