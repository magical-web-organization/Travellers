Introduction to Middletown

Located in the breathtaking Shenandoah Valley of Virginia, Middletown is a small town with plenty of charm and history. It’s the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking for a relaxing getaway. The town is known for its beautiful parks, historic landmarks, and friendly locals. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Middletown, Virginia.

List of Places to Visit in Middletown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Belle Grove Plantation: This 18th-century mansion was built in the Federal style and served as a home for many of the most important figures in American history, including James Madison.
  • Cedar Creek Battlefield and Belle Grove National Historical Park: This park features a variety of exhibits and interactive displays that help visitors better understand the events that took place during the Civil War in the Valley.
  • Stonewall Jackson’s Headquarters Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of Stonewall Jackson, one of the most important Confederate generals of the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Middletown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Shenandoah Valley: This breathtaking valley is an outdoor enthusiast’s dream, with plenty of hiking trails, scenic drives, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • George Washington National Forest: This forest covers over a million acres and offers hiking, camping, fishing, and other outdoor sports.
  • Shenandoah River: This river is perfect for kayaking, canoeing, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Middletown

Middletown is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in the Shenandoah Valley. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Signal Knob: This hike offers stunning views of the valley below and is one of the most popular trails in the area.
  • Woodstock Tower: This trail is a challenging hike that leads to an observation tower with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Shenandoah River State Park: This park boasts over 1,500 acres of trails and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Middletown: Activities and Tours

  • Skyline Drive: This scenic drive offers breathtaking views of the Shenandoah Valley and is one of the most popular attractions in the area.
  • Luray Caverns: These spectacular underground caves are a must-see for anyone visiting the area.
  • Wine Tours: There are several wineries in the area that offer tours and tastings, including the Blue Ridge Whiskey Wine Loop and the Shenandoah Valley Wine Trail.

Week-Long Itinerary for Middletown

If you’re planning to spend a week in Middletown, here’s a recommended itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Belle Grove Plantation and Cedar Creek Battlefield.
  • Day 2: Take a scenic drive along Skyline Drive and hike one of the park’s trails.
  • Day 3: Visit Stonewall Jackson’s Headquarters Museum and take a walking tour of the town.
  • Day 4: Go on a wine tour and spend the night in a local bed and breakfast.
  • Day 5: Visit Luray Caverns and take a guided tour of the caves.
  • Day 6: Spend the day hiking in the Shenandoah River State Park.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Middletown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shenandoah Valley Trout: This dish features locally caught trout and is a staple of the region’s cuisine.
  • Apple Pie: Middletown is known for its delicious apple pie, thanks to the nearby orchards.
  • BBQ: Barbecue is a southern staple, and Middletown has several great spots to try it.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wine: The Shenandoah Valley is known for its wine, and there are several wineries in the area that offer tastings.
  • Craft Beer: Middletown has several great craft breweries, including Swover Creek Farm Brewery and Backroom Brewery.

Where to Stay in Middletown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Middletown offers a variety of lodging options, from quaint bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels.

  • Belle Grove Plantation Bed and Breakfast: This historic B&B offers comfortable rooms and delicious breakfasts.
  • Hampton Inn Winchester-North: This modern hotel is located just a few miles from Middletown and offers comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • Woodruff Inns Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in the heart of Middletown and offers cozy rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Middletown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Wine: The Shenandoah Valley is known for its wine, and bringing home a bottle is a great souvenir.
  • Apples and Apple Products: Middletown is surrounded by apple orchards, and there are plenty of great products to bring home, including apple butter and apple cider.
  • Handmade Crafts: Middletown has several great shops that sell handmade crafts, including pottery, woodworking, and quilting.

Local Culture and Customs of Middletown

Middletown is a small town with a strong sense of community. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and there are several annual events that celebrate the town’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Middletown

The best time to visit Middletown is in the spring and fall, when the weather is pleasant and the foliage is at its peak. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middletown

Do:

  • Take a scenic drive along Skyline Drive
  • Visit the local wineries and breweries
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local wildlife and habitats
  • Disrespect historic landmarks and buildings
  • Engage in dangerous outdoor activities without proper gear or training.

Safety and Health

Safety in Middletown

Middletown is a safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Be wary of busy roads and drive cautiously, and avoid leaving valuables unattended in public areas.

Health in Middletown

It’s always a good idea to check with your doctor before traveling to a new location. If you need medical assistance while in Middletown, there are several clinics and hospitals in the area that can provide care.

Getting to Middletown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Middletown

The closest airports to Middletown are the Shenandoah Valley Regional Airport (57 miles) and Washington Dulles International Airport (66 miles).

Bus Stations Near Middletown

The closest bus stations to Middletown are located in Winchester (13 miles) and Front Royal (15 miles).

Other Options

There are several car rental companies located in the area, and many visitors opt to drive to Middletown from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Middletown

The best way to get around Middletown is by car or bike. There are several bike rental shops in the area, and many of the town’s attractions are within biking distance.

Where to Go Next from Middletown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Winchester: This nearby city is home to several historic attractions, including the Museum of the Shenandoah Valley and the Old Court House Civil War Museum.
  • Front Royal: This charming town is located near Shenandoah National Park and offers plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Charlottesville: This city is just a few hours’ drive away and is home to several renowned vineyards, as well as the University of Virginia.

Conclusion

Middletown, Virginia is a small town with a lot to offer. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a beautiful setting, Middletown has something for you. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this charming town.
