Introduction to New Market

New Market is a charming town located in the Shenandoah Valley, in Virginia, and is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The town has a rich history and culture, and offers a range of attractions for visitors. From outdoor activities to museums and historical sites, there’s something for everyone in New Market.

List of Places to Visit in New Market

Here are some of the best places to visit in New Market:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Virginia Museum of the Civil War: This museum is a must-visit for history buffs, as it offers a glimpse into the state’s role in the Civil War.
  • New Market Battlefield State Historical Park: This park is home to the site of the Battle of New Market, which was fought during the Civil War. Visitors can learn about the battle and explore the battlefield with a self-guided tour.
  • Hall of Valor Civil War Museum: This museum showcases the role of Virginia’s military history in the Civil War and offers artifacts and exhibits to educate visitors.

Natural Attractions of New Market: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shenandoah Caverns: A popular attraction in the region, the Shenandoah Caverns are a beautiful underground cave system with stunning formations and passages.
  • Endless Caverns: Another cave system in the area, Endless Caverns offers both guided and self-guided tours, and is known for its stunning stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Shenandoah Valley Overlook: For breathtaking scenic views of the Shenandoah Valley, head to the Shenandoah Valley Overlook. The overlook provides a panoramic view of the valley and is perfect for photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Market

New Market offers a range of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are a few popular routes:

  • Shenandoah National Park: Located just a short drive from New Market, Shenandoah National Park offers a range of trails for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.
  • Massanutten Mountain Trails: This trail system is located in the George Washington National Forest and offers both easy and challenging hikes and bike rides through the mountains.
  • New Market Battlefield State Historical Park: In addition to its historical significance, the state park offers hiking trails that allow visitors to explore the battlefield.

Best Things to Do in New Market: Activities and Tours

There’s plenty to do in New Market, whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or food and drink. Here are some top things to do:

  • Take a Civil War Tour: For history buffs, taking a Civil War tour is a great way to learn about the region’s rich history. Several companies in the area offer guided tours.
  • Go Wine Tasting: The Shenandoah Valley is known for its wineries, and there are several located near New Market that offer tastings and tours.
  • Attend a Local Festival: Throughout the year, New Market hosts several festivals and events, including the Virginia Wine & Garlic Festival and the Shenandoah Valley Hot Air Balloon & Wine Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Market

If you’re planning a week-long visit to New Market, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of New Market

The food and drink scene in New Market offers something for everyone, from local favorites to international cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: Virginia is known for its fried chicken, and many local restaurants in New Market serve up their own versions of this classic dish.
  • Pork Barbecue: BBQ is a staple in Virginia, and several local restaurants in New Market offer their own take on this classic dish.
  • Apple Butter: Made from fresh apples, apple butter is a sweet and tangy spread that’s a favorite among locals.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia Wine: The Shenandoah Valley is known for its wineries, and many local restaurants and bars serve up a selection of Virginia wines.
  • Beer: Several local breweries are located near New Market, and offer a range of craft beers.

Where to Stay in New Market: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Market offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are a few top choices:

  • The Inn at Narrow Passage: This historic inn offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a restaurant and bar.
  • Budget Inn: If you’re on a tighter budget, this no-frills motel offers affordable rates and clean rooms.
  • Shenvalee Golf Resort: For those who enjoy golfing, the Shenvalee Golf Resort offers comfortable rooms and access to a golf course.

What to Buy in New Market: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to New Market is complete without picking up a few souvenirs and local products to take home. Here are a few top choices:

  • Apple Butter: Made from fresh apples, apple butter is a sweet and tangy spread that’s a favorite among locals.
  • Handmade Crafts: Several local artisans and crafters sell their handmade goods at markets and shops throughout the town.
  • Stone Ground Grits: Grits are a staple in southern cuisine, and stone ground grits made from locally-sourced corn are a popular product to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of New Market

New Market is a small town with a close-knit community, and residents take pride in their history and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and take the time to learn about the region’s rich history and culture.

Best Time to Visit New Market

The best time to visit New Market is during the fall when the leaves change color, and the temperatures are mild. Spring and summer are also good times to visit, but tend to be more crowded with tourists. The winter months can be cold and snowy, but offer a unique experience for visitors interested in winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Market

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the region’s history and culture.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take part in outdoor activities and enjoy the region’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions or historical sites.
  • Disrespect local residents or culture.
  • Ignore safety warnings or precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Market

New Market is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take the same precautions they would in any new place. Avoid walking alone at night, lock your doors, and be mindful of your surroundings.

Health in New Market

Healthcare facilities are located nearby in Harrisonburg, and there are several pharmacies and clinics located within the town.

Getting to New Market: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Market

The nearest airport to New Market is the Shenandoah Valley Regional Airport, which is located approximately 20 miles north of the town. Another option is the Charlottesville Albemarle Airport, located approximately 80 miles southeast of New Market.

Bus Stations Near New Market

Bus service to New Market is limited but can be found in Harrisonburg, which is located approximately 16 miles south of the town.

Other Options

Car rental companies are located at both airports and in Harrisonburg. Visitors can also use a rideshare service or taxi service to travel to New Market.

How to Get Around in New Market

New Market is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use a rideshare service or taxi service to get around if needed.

Where to Go Next from New Market: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore beyond New Market, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are a few top choices:

  • Harrisonburg: Located just a short drive south of New Market, Harrisonburg is a larger town with more dining and shopping options.
  • Shenandoah National Park: This beautiful national park is located just a short drive from New Market and offers a range of outdoor activities and natural attractions.
  • Luray Caverns: Known for its stunning underground formations, Luray Caverns are a popular attraction located approximately 25 miles east of New Market.

Conclusion

New Market is a charming town with a rich history and culture, and a range of attractions for visitors to enjoy. With its beautiful natural attractions, outdoor activities, and fascinating museums and historical sites, the town offers something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in New Market.
