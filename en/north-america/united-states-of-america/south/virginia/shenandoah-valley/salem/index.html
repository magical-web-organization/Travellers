Introduction to Salem

Salem, located in the Shenandoah Valley in Virginia, is a charming small town with a rich history and a vibrant culture. The town is known for its picturesque scenery, outdoor adventure opportunities, and a wide range of cultural and natural attractions that make it a great destination for anyone seeking a relaxing yet exciting vacation. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Salem.

List of Places to Visit in Salem

Salem has a variety of cultural and natural attractions that cater to any visitor’s interests. Here’s a list of the best places to visit in Salem that you wouldn’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Salem Museum and Historical Society: This museum features exhibits that focus on Salem’s history since its establishment in 1808.
  • Andrew Lewis Birthplace: Andrew Lewis was a famous American Patriot, and this historical landmark is his birthplace.
  • Roanoke Pinball Museum: This museum houses over 50 pinball machines from the 1940s to the present, making it a fun activity for both families and adults.

Natural Attractions of Salem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salem Bike Routes: Salem is home to various bike trails for all skill levels that provide stunning views of the Blue Ridge Mountains, vast farmlands, and scenic countryside.
  • Dixie Caverns and Pottery: Dixie Caverns is an underground series of caverns and rock formations that is a natural wonder.
  • Salem Lake: A beautiful lake that offers several recreational activities, including kayaking, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salem

Salem is a haven for adventure enthusiasts, with various hiking and biking trails that you can explore and enjoy. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes of Salem:

  • Appalachian Trail: A 2,200-mile-long hiking trail that stretches from Georgia to Maine, passing through Salem, Virginia.
  • Mill Mountain Star Trail: One of the most popular hiking trails in Salem that rewards you with a breathtaking view of the entire valley once you reach the summit.
  • Salem Upper Lake Trail: This bike path will take you around the beautiful Salem Lake and through the surrounding woods and hills.

Best Things to Do in Salem: Activities and Tours

There’s nothing more exciting than engaging in activities and tours that let you discover Salem’s essence truly. Here are some of the best things to do in Salem:

  • Salem Ghost Tours: This tour will take you on a spine-tingling journey to explore Salem’s supernatural and ghostly history.
  • Salem Farmers Market: A great way to experience Salem’s local culture is by visiting the Farmers Market. Head over there early to get the freshest produce and local foods available.
  • Dr Pepper Park: This park hosts various events throughout the year, from concerts and movies to food festivals and craft shows. Check their website to see what’s happening when you’re in town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salem

Salem is a destination that invites you to unwind and explore at your own pace. Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you plan your vacation in Salem:

Day Activity
1 Visit Salem Museum and Historical Society, followed by a bike ride on the Roanoke River Greenway
2 Tour the Andrew Lewis Birthplace and spend the afternoon hiking the Mill Mountain Star Trail
3 Explore the Dixie Caverns and Pottery, then spend an afternoon at Roanoke Pinball Museum
4 Enjoy a relaxing day kayaking or fishing at Salem Lake
5 Go on a Salem Ghost Tour, followed by a visit to Roanoke Farmers Market
6 Take a scenic drive on the Blue Ridge Parkway, with stops at Mabry Mill and Rocky Knob Visitors Center
7 End your vacation with an evening concert or movie at Dr Pepper Park

Food and Drink Scene of Salem

Salem’s food and drink scene is diverse, with a range of local dishes and beverages you wouldn’t want to miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Country Ham: Virginia’s famous country ham is a must-try dish when in Salem.
  • Peanut Soup: A traditional Virginia dish made with Virginia peanuts, potatoes, and cream that you can find at various restaurants in Salem.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: A classic Southern dish served for breakfast in several restaurants around Salem.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Virginia is home to several vineyards and wineries, including the West Wind Farm Vineyard and Winery in Salem.
  • Beer: Salem is surrounded by numerous breweries in the Roanoke Valley area, including Parkway Brewing Company and Twin Creeks Brewing Company.
  • Moonshine: Virginia is notorious for its moonshine, and Salem is no different, with various distilleries where visitors can taste this locally made drink.

Where to Stay in Salem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Salem has several accommodation options for visitors to choose from, catering to any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Salem:

  • Hampton Inn Salem Roanoke Area: A budget-friendly hotel located in Salem close to various attractions.
  • The Inn at Burwell Place: A charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Salem’s historic district.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Salem: Comfortable and affordable accommodation located in a peaceful and convenient location.

What to Buy in Salem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salem has several unique gift shops where visitors can purchase souvenirs and local products. These include:

  • Vintage in Gatsby’s: This gift shop sells vintage items, old records, and books, making it a great place to find unique souvenirs.
  • Chocolatepaper: This chocolate and gift shop has a wide selection of chocolates and other sweets from all around the world, making it perfect for those who love sweets.
  • Agriculture of Virginia: The state-operated gift shop sells Virginia made products, including locally made wines, beers, and spirits that make fantastic gifts for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Salem

Virginia is known for its traditional culture and customs, so it is essential to keep them in mind when visiting. Here are some of the customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect for elders: Virginians place a high value on respecting and honoring their elders, so be sure to show respect when interacting with older people in the community.
  • Hospitality: Virginia is known for its hospitality, and people in Salem are no exception. Expect to be greeted warmly and treated kindly during your stay.
  • Southern manners: Following Southern manners and etiquette can go a long way in Salem. Saying “Please” and “Thank you” often, and holding doors open for others are among the most important ones to remember.

Best Time to Visit Salem

The best time to visit Salem is during the Spring and Fall. During this time, the weather is perfect, and the foliage is stunning. Salem is also known for its many festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Salem Fair in July, Roanoke’s Festival in the Park in May, and the Blue Ridge Folklife Festival in October, which are all great reasons to plan your trip to Salem around these dates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salem

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Salem:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture: Virginia has a rich history and culture, so be sure to respect it when visiting the area.
  • Stay hydrated: Salem can get hot during the summer, so be sure to stay hydrated and wear appropriate clothing if you’re planning to be outside for an extended period.

Don’t:

  • Litter: This should go without saying, but please don’t litter. There are public trash cans in Salem, and it is essential to use them.
  • Smoke inside public places: Virginia has strict smoking laws, and smoking inside public places, including restaurants and bars, is not allowed.

Safety and Health

Salem is a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should take precautions to ensure they stay healthy and safe throughout their stay.

Safety in Salem

  • Watch out for ticks: Virginia is known for its ticks, so make sure to wear appropriate clothing and check your skin after being outside.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: As with any destination, it’s important to keep an eye on your surroundings and belongings to ensure your safety.

Health in Salem

  • Stay hydrated: Salem can get hot during the summer, so be sure to stay hydrated and wear appropriate clothing if you’re planning to be outside for an extended period.
  • Wear sunscreen: Since Salem is a destination that encourages outdoor activities, wearing sunscreen is essential to avoid getting sunburned.

Getting to Salem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Salem is well-connected and easy to reach, with several transportation options available for visitors.

Airports Near Salem

  • Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport (ROA): Located just 12 miles from Salem, Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport (ROA) provides daily flights to major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Salem

  • Greyhound Bus Station at Roanoke: Located just 7 miles from Salem, the Greyhound Bus station in Roanoke is the closest bus station to Salem.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Car rentals are available at Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport and in Roanoke, which will provide you with the most flexibility when exploring Salem.

How to Get Around in Salem

Salem is a small town, and most attractions are easily accessible on foot. Visitors can also use taxis, ride-sharing, and rental cars to explore Salem’s surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Salem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Salem is located in the heart of the Shenandoah Valley, making it a perfect base to explore surrounding areas and attractions. Here are some of the best nearby destinations to explore:

  • Roanoke: Located just 7 miles from Salem, Roanoke is perfect for those looking for a slightly larger city experience with plenty of restaurants, shopping, and outdoor activities.
  • Natural Bridge: Located an hour’s drive from Salem, Natural Bridge is a natural wonder and a popular tourist attraction that travelers shouldn’t miss.
  • Blue Ridge Parkway: A scenic drive on the Blue Ridge Parkway can take you through some of Virginia’s most stunning views and destinations, including several hiking trails, waterfalls, and overlooks.

Conclusion

Salem is a beautiful destination with a mix of cultural and natural attractions that caters to all interests and preferences. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, delicious food, or a relaxing break, Salem has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip to Salem, Virginia, now, and get lost in its beauty and charm.
