Introduction to Front Royal

Located in the picturesque Shenandoah Valley in eastern Virginia, Front Royal offers a charming mix of old-fashioned Southern hospitality and natural beauty. With its small-town appeal, historic landmarks, and scenic vistas, it’s no wonder that Front Royal has been dubbed the “Gateway to the Shenandoah National Park.”

If you’re planning a trip to Front Royal, there are plenty of exciting attractions and activities to explore. Discover the best hiking and biking trails in the region, visit cultural landmarks like local museums, and indulge in the area’s vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Front Royal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Warren Rifles Confederate Museum: Learn about the Civil War from a Southern perspective at this museum, which features exhibits on the area’s Confederate history.
  • Belle Boyd Cottage: Explore the former home of Belle Boyd, a famous Confederate spy.
  • Front Royal Historic District: Take a stroll through the town’s charming historic district and admire the well-preserved 19th-century architecture.

Natural Attractions of Front Royal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shenandoah National Park: A must-visit for nature lovers, Shenandoah National Park stretches for 105 miles along the Blue Ridge Mountains and offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and breathtaking views.
  • Skyline Drive: This winding mountain road is the best way to experience the national park’s stunning vistas, with 75 overlooks along the way.
  • Cedar Creek and Belle Grove National Historical Park: Step back in time with a visit to this park, which features a preserved 18th-century plantation and the site of the Battle of Cedar Creek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Front Royal

  • Appalachian Trail: The iconic hiking trail passes through Shenandoah National Park and offers plenty of opportunities to explore the region’s natural beauty.
  • Hawksbill Green Loop Trail: This popular, scenic trail takes you to the summit of Hawksbill Mountain, the highest peak in Shenandoah National Park.
  • Sherando Lake Loop Trail: A family-friendly hiking route, this 2.9-mile trail takes you past a scenic mountain lake and is perfect for swimming and picnics.

Best Things to Do in Front Royal: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing and Kayaking on the Shenandoah River: Experience the region’s natural beauty from a new perspective with a guided canoe or kayak tour on the Shenandoah River.
  • Front Royal Trolley Tours: Sit back and relax with a guided trolley tour of Front Royal, featuring stops at local landmarks and historic sites.
  • Front Royal Outdoors Zipline Canopy Tour: For an adrenaline rush, try this exciting zipline tour through the forest canopy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Front Royal

Looking to make the most of your trip to Front Royal? Follow this itinerary to experience a little bit of everything the town and its surroundings have to offer:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Front Royal’s historic district and visit the Warren Rifles Confederate Museum.
Day 2 Hike the Hawksbill Green Loop Trail to take in the stunning views of the Shenandoah Valley.
Day 3 Take a guided trolley tour of Front Royal and see Belle Boyd Cottage and other historic sites up close.
Day 4 Head to Shenandoah National Park and drive the scenic Skyline Drive, stopping at overlooks along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Front Royal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple butter: A local favorite, apple butter is a sweet, thick spread made from slow cooking apples with sugar and spices.
  • Virginia ham: A staple of Southern cuisine, Virginia ham is a cured, smoked, and aged ham that’s been produced in the region for centuries.
  • Peanut soup: A unique dish that’s a Virginia classic, peanut soup is a thick soup made with peanuts, chicken broth, and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Virginia wine: The state’s mild climate and varied soil types make it an ideal region for winemaking, with several wineries near Front Royal offering tastings and tours.
  • Craft beer: Virginia is home to many craft breweries, and Front Royal has several excellent options to choose from, including Escutcheon Brewing and Front Royal Brewing Company.
  • Hard cider: A refreshing alternative, hard cider is made locally using locally grown apples.

Where to Stay in Front Royal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at Little Washington: If you’re looking for luxury accommodations, this acclaimed inn is a must-stay.
  • Mimslyn Inn: This historic inn, built in 1931, is perfectly situated near local attractions and offers a variety of rooms and suites.
  • Front Royal RV Park: For those traveling by RV, this park offers spacious, full-hookup sites and easy access to nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Front Royal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shenandoah National Park souvenirs: Visitors can pick up shirts, hats, and other souvenirs featuring the park’s iconic skyline logo at the visitor center.
  • Dried apples: A delicious and convenient snack, dried apples are hand-picked and dried on local orchards.
  • Local crafts and art: From handmade pottery to original paintings, Front Royal is home to many artisanal shops selling unique local products.

Local Culture and Customs of Front Royal

Visitors to Front Royal should be aware of the town’s friendly, Southern hospitality and willingness to help others. Residents are proud of the town’s history and natural beauty and are happy to share their knowledge and experiences with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Front Royal

The best time to visit Front Royal depends on your interests and what you want to do. Spring and fall are ideal for taking in the region’s natural beauty, while summer is perfect for outdoor activities and taking advantage of the long days and warm weather. Winter is a quiet time in the town, but still offers opportunities for hiking and indoor activities like visiting museums and local shops.

Do’s and Don’ts of Front Royal

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the surrounding natural beauty, from Shenandoah National Park to the Shenandoah River.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including Virginia ham, apple butter, and peanut soup.
  • Do be respectful of the town’s historic landmarks and local customs, such as Southern hospitality and politeness.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking around town.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to check the weather before planning outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Front Royal

Front Royal is a safe town with a low crime rate, but visitors should still take normal precautions, such as keeping valuables locked up or out of sight and staying aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Front Royal

Visitors to Front Royal should take precautions against tick-borne illnesses such as Lyme disease, as the region has a high incidence of ticks in wooded areas. Be sure to use insect repellent containing DEET and wear long sleeves and pants when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Front Royal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Front Royal

  • Washington Dulles International Airport: Located approximately 50 miles away from Front Royal.
  • Charlottesville Albemarle Airport: Located approximately 75 miles away from Front Royal.

Bus Stations Near Front Royal

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in nearby Culpeper, about 30 miles away from Front Royal.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Many major car rental companies operate out of Dulles International Airport.
  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station is located in Culpeper, approximately 30 miles away from Front Royal.

How to Get Around in Front Royal

The best way to get around Front Royal is by car, as many of the town’s attractions and outdoor activities are spread out. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, but may be limited during busy seasons.

Where to Go Next from Front Royal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Luray: Home to the Luray Caverns, a natural wonder featuring underground caverns with stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Winchester: Another nearby historic town with a charming downtown area and several museums and landmarks.
  • Charlottesville: Home to the University of Virginia and the historic Monticello home of Thomas Jefferson.

Conclusion

Front Royal is a hidden gem in Virginia’s Shenandoah Valley, offering a blend of natural beauty and historic charm. Whether you’re interested in exploring the great outdoors, taking in local culture and cuisine, or just enjoying some rest and relaxation, Front Royal has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and start discovering all that this vibrant town has to offer!
