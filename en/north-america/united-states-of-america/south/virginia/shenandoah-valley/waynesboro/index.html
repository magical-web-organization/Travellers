Introduction to Waynesboro

Located in the Shenandoah Valley region of Virginia, Waynesboro is a charming town that offers excellent opportunities for outdoor adventures and rich cultural experiences. The town is home to several historic landmarks, scenic natural areas, and a thriving food and drink scene, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a diverse range of activities. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore some of Waynesboro’s top attractions, best accommodations, must-try dishes, and local customs.

List of Places to Visit in Waynesboro

Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Waynesboro has something to offer. Here are some of the top places to visit in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • P. Buckley Moss Museum: This museum features the works of renowned artist P. Buckley Moss and offers a glimpse into the region’s history and culture.
  • Waynesboro Heritage Foundation Museum: Explore Waynesboro’s rich history through exhibits and artifacts from the town’s past.
  • Augusta County Courthouse: Built in 1901, this historic courthouse offers an excellent example of neoclassical architecture.
  • Plumb House Museum: This restored 19th-century house is home to an extensive collection of artifacts from the Civil War era.

Natural Attractions of Waynesboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shenandoah National Park - South District: Take a scenic drive on the Blue Ridge Parkway, hike on one of the many trails, or enjoy breathtaking views from the Skyline Drive.
  • South River Falls: Take a leisurely hike to this magnificent waterfall in the Shenandoah National Park.
  • Rockfish Gap Hawk Watch: Birdwatchers will enjoy this popular spot to observe migratory birds in the fall.
  • Ridgeview Park: This picturesque park offers hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waynesboro

Waynesboro is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views and a chance to experience the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Waynesboro Water Trail: Enjoy a peaceful paddle down the South River, which runs through town.
  • Appalachian Trail: For a more challenging hike, take a day trip to the nearby Appalachian Trail, which passes through the Shenandoah Valley.
  • Blackrock Summit Trail: This 1.2-mile trail in Shenandoah National Park leads to stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Rockfish Gap Loop Trail: This 6.9-mile hike near the Blue Ridge Parkway offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Waynesboro: Activities and Tours

  • Waynesboro Farmers Market: A vibrant farmers market that operates from April to December, offering locally grown fresh produce and handmade crafts.
  • Fishburne Military School Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Fishburne Military School, a private military school in Waynesboro.
  • Humpback Rocks Farm: Visitors can experience life on a 19th-century farmstead and learn about historical farming practices.
  • Paddle the Shenandoah River: Rent a paddleboard, canoe, or kayak and explore the South River’s calm waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Waynesboro

Day 1: Explore the town’s cultural landmarks, including P. Buckley Moss Museum and the Waynesboro Heritage Foundation Museum.

Day 2: Hike the Blackrock Summit Trail or enjoy a scenic drive through Shenandoah National Park’s South District.

Day 3: Visit nearby Blue Ridge Parkway and take in the stunning mountain views.

Day 4: Discover the region’s agricultural heritage by visiting the Waynesboro Farmers Market and Humpback Rocks Farm.

Day 5: Take a kayak or paddleboard trip down the South River.

Day 6: Enjoy a birdwatching excursion at Rockfish Gap Hawk Watch or take a tour of Fishburne Military School Museum.

Day 7: Relax at Ridgeview Park and enjoy the park’s hiking trails, picnic areas, and playground.

Food and Drink Scene of Waynesboro

Waynesboro’s food and drink scene is a reflection of the region’s agricultural heritage, with a focus on farm-to-table cuisine and local craft beer and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbeque: The region is known for barbeque, and Waynesboro has several excellent barbeque restaurants, such as the South River Grill and Moe’s Original Barbeque.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: Apple cider is a popular beverage in the region, and donuts made with apple cider are a local specialty.
  • Fresh Seafood: Although Waynesboro is not a coastal town, many local restaurants source fresh seafood from the nearby coast, such as the Rockfish Bistro and The Green Leaf Grill.
  • Burgers: Several local eateries, such as the Barren Ridge Vineyards Tasting Room and the Waynesburger, offer delicious burgers.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The region’s craft beer scene is thriving, with several excellent breweries in the area, such as Seven Arrows Brewing, Stable Craft Brewing, and Basic City Beer Co.
  • Wine: Waynesboro is also home to several acclaimed wineries, such as Barren Ridge Vineyards, Afton Mountain Vineyards, and Veritas Vineyard & Winery.
  • Apple Cider: The region’s apple cider is a refreshing and delicious beverage that is perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Waynesboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Waynesboro offers a variety of accommodations to suit different tastes and budgets. Here are some options:

  • Iris Inn Bed & Breakfast: A cozy and romantic bed & breakfast that offers stunning views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
  • The Buckhorn Inn: A historic inn that features elegant rooms and delicious on-site dining.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Waynesboro-Route 340: A modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a fitness center.
  • The Waynesboro Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers looking for affordable, no-frills accommodation.

What to Buy in Waynesboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Virginia Wine and Craft Festival: Held in downtown Waynesboro every fall, this festival is a great place to sample local wines and crafts.
  • Fresh Produce: The Waynesboro Farmers Market is an excellent place to stock up on locally grown fresh produce.
  • P. Buckley Moss Prints: The P. Buckley Moss Museum Gift Shop sells prints of the artist’s works.

Local Culture and Customs of Waynesboro

The people of Waynesboro are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect for Nature: Waynesboro’s residents take great pride in the area’s natural beauty and prioritize conservation efforts.
  • Graciousness: Southern hospitality is prevalent in Waynesboro, and visitors should expect warm and welcoming interactions with locals.
  • Love of History: The region has a rich history, and locals are often enthusiastic about sharing stories and insights into the area’s past.

Best Time to Visit Waynesboro

The best time to visit Waynesboro is from April to October when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The town is particularly beautiful in the fall when the leaves change color and create a stunning landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waynesboro

Do:

  • Enjoy the Outdoors: Waynesboro has plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, so make sure to take advantage of the town’s many hiking, biking, and paddling trails.
  • Visit the Farmers Market: The Waynesboro Farmers Market is an excellent place to sample local produce and crafts.
  • Take a Winery Tour: The region’s wineries offer tours, tastings, and stunning views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

Don’t:

  • Litter: The residents of Waynesboro take great pride in their town’s natural beauty, so make sure to dispose of trash properly.
  • Ignore Cultural Landmarks: The town has several cultural landmarks, such as the P. Buckley Moss Museum and the Waynesboro Heritage Foundation Museum, that offer insights into the region’s history and culture.
  • Disrespect Local Customs: Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as Southern hospitality and love for nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waynesboro

Waynesboro is a safe town, and visitors should not encounter any major safety concerns. However, visitors should take precautions while hiking or exploring the natural areas and should follow park rules and guidelines.

Health in Waynesboro

Visitors should be aware of ticks, which can carry Lyme disease, and should avoid contact with them. Visitors should also take precautions to prevent mosquito bites, which can transmit diseases such as West Nile Virus.

Getting to Waynesboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Waynesboro is easily accessible by several transportation options. Here are a few:

Airports Near Waynesboro

  • Charlottesville-Albemarle Airport (CHO): 30 miles
  • Shenandoah Valley Regional Airport (SHD): 22 miles

Bus Stations Near Waynesboro

  • Greyhound Bus Station, Charlottesville: 27 miles
  • Greyhound Bus Station, Staunton: 10 miles

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from several rental companies at the airports.
  • Private Transportation: Uber and Lyft are available in Waynesboro.

How to Get Around in Waynesboro

Visitors can get around Waynesboro easily by car, bike, or on foot. Public transportation options are limited, so visitors without a car may need to rely on ride-hailing services or taxis.

Where to Go Next from Waynesboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Staunton: A picturesque town that offers charming historic districts and stunning architecture.
  • Charlottesville: Home to the University of Virginia, Charlottesville is a vibrant town with cultural attractions and an excellent food and drink scene.
  • Blue Ridge Parkway: A scenic drive that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Conclusion

Waynesboro is a hidden gem in Virginia that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to historic landmarks. Visitors to Waynesboro should make sure to explore the town’s cultural attractions, food and drink scene, and natural beauty.
