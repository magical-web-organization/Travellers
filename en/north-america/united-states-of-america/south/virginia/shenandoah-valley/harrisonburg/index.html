Introduction to Harrisonburg

Nestled in the Shenandoah Valley, Harrisonburg is a historic and charming city in Virginia, United States. This vibrant city boasts rich cultural and natural attractions, excellent food and drink scene, and an impressive range of outdoor recreational opportunities, making it an ideal tourist destination for people of all ages and interests. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure enthusiast, or a foodie, Harrisonburg has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat, stay, and shop in Harrisonburg.

List of Places to Visit in Harrisonburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Virginia Quilt Museum: This museum showcases an extensive collection of quilts and offers workshops and classes.
  • Explore More Discovery Museum: This interactive museum provides a fun learning experience for kids and adults alike.
  • Valley Turnpike Museum: This museum preserves the history of Valley Turnpike, a major transportation route during the Civil War era.
  • Harrisonburg Heritage Museum: This museum delves into the rich history and heritage of Harrisonburg.

Natural Attractions of Harrisonburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shenandoah National Park: This park boasts 200,000 acres of natural beauty, including stunning vistas, hiking trails, and waterfalls.
  • Massanutten Resort: This all-season resort offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including skiing, snowboarding, golfing, and hiking.
  • Edith J. Carrier Arboretum: This 125-acre botanical garden features a variety of plants and vegetation.
  • James Madison University Arboretum: This arboretum is a peaceful oasis within the city, with walking trails and beautiful gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harrisonburg

  • Purcell Park Trail: A 1-mile long loop in Purcell Park perfect for a quick walk or jog.
  • Hillandale Park Trail: A 1.5-mile long loop in Hillandale Park featuring a lake and scenic views.
  • Northend Greenway Trail: A 2-mile bike and pedestrian trail connecting downtown Harrisonburg to Purcell Park.
  • Massanutten Western Slope Trail: A challenging 3.8-mile mountain bike trail through the Massanutten Resort.

Best Things to Do in Harrisonburg: Activities and Tours

  • Clementine: A popular music venue and restaurant featuring live music, delicious food, and drinks.
  • Rocktown Boulders: A premier indoor rock climbing gym for all skill levels.
  • Southern Kitchen: An iconic southern-style restaurant serving up delicious comfort food and homemade pies.
  • Hardesty-Higgins House Visitor Center: This historic building houses the Harrisonburg Visitor Center and offers walking tours of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Harrisonburg

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgers at Jack Brown’s Beer and Burger Joint: This burger joint is known for its delicious burgers and extensive beer selection.
  • Pork BBQ at BBQ Ranch: This BBQ joint serves up mouthwatering pork BBQ and homemade sauces.
  • Pizzas at Bella Luna Wood-Fired Pizza: This popular pizzeria serves up authentic, wood-fired pizzas in a cozy atmosphere.
  • Pastries at Heritage Bakery and Café: This bakery and café offers a variety of pastries, cakes, and bread, all made in-house.

Local Beverages

  • Wine at CrossKeys Vineyards: This vineyard offers award-winning wines and scenic views of the valley.
  • Beer at Brothers Craft Brewing: This brewery offers a rotating selection of craft beers.
  • Coffee at Shenandoah Joe Coffee Roasters: This coffee shop roasts their own beans and offers an excellent selection of coffee and tea.

Where to Stay in Harrisonburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Madison: A luxurious hotel in the heart of downtown Harrisonburg with excellent amenities.
  • The Village Inn: A charming bed and breakfast with a cozy atmosphere and delicious breakfast options.
  • By the Side of the Road: A romantic and intimate bed and breakfast with beautiful gardens and a serene atmosphere.
  • Microtel Inn and Suites: A budget-friendly hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Harrisonburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery from the Larkin Arts: This art studio offers handmade pottery and other crafts by local artists.
  • Wine from CrossKeys Vineyards: This vineyard offers a selection of wines made with grapes grown on their estate.
  • Quilts from the Virginia Quilt Museum: The museum gift shop sells handmade quilts and other quilting supplies.
  • Harrisonburg T-shirts from the Visitor Centers: The Visitor Centers sell Harrisonburg-branded t-shirts and other souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Harrisonburg

Harrisonburg is a diverse and welcoming community that values local heritage and cultural traditions. Visitors can expect to see a mix of urban, suburban, and rural lifestyles in the city. The Harrisonburg Farmers Market and the First Fridays Downtown Art Walk are popular community events that celebrate local art, food, and music. Residents value environmental sustainability and have a strong commitment to recycling and reducing waste.

Best Time to Visit Harrisonburg

The best time to visit Harrisonburg is during the fall, between September and November, when the leaves turn into vibrant shades of yellow, orange, and red, and the temperatures are mild. Spring, between March and May, is also a great time to visit, with pleasant temperatures and blooming flowers. Summer, between June and August, can be hot and humid, but offers a great opportunity for outdoor activities, particularly in the nearby mountains. Winter, between December and February, can be cold and snowy, but offers a great opportunity for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harrisonburg

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors at Shenandoah National Park and Massanutten Resort.
  • Try some of the delicious southern-style food and local beer and wine.
  • Visit the Virginia Quilt Museum to learn about the art of quilting and the history of the Shenandoah Valley.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local cultural traditions and heritage.
  • Drive recklessly or under the influence.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harrisonburg

Harrisonburg is a safe city overall, but visitors should take usual precautions, such as securing valuables in parked cars and avoiding walking alone at night. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for natural hazards, such as flash floods in low-lying areas during heavy rains and steep drops in mountainous areas.

Health in Harrisonburg

Harrisonburg has excellent healthcare facilities, including Sentara RMH Medical Center and Augusta Health Fishersville. Visitors should come prepared with necessary medications and travel insurance. Tap water is safe to drink in Harrisonburg.

Getting to Harrisonburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harrisonburg

  • Shenandoah Valley Regional Airport: This small airport is located just outside of Harrisonburg and offers limited flights.

Bus Stations Near Harrisonburg

  • Greyhound Harrisonburg Station: The station is located in downtown Harrisonburg and offers bus services to various destinations in the United States.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from various rental agencies at the Shenandoah Valley Regional Airport and other nearby locations.
  • Taxis and Uber: Taxis and Uber are available in Harrisonburg for local transportation.

How to Get Around in Harrisonburg

  • Walking: Downtown Harrisonburg can be easily explored on foot.
  • Biking: Harrisonburg has an extensive biking trail network, including the Northend Greenway Trail.
  • Driving: Harrisonburg has a well-maintained road system, making it easy to get around by car.

Where to Go Next from Harrisonburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Charlottesville: A charming college town known for its history, culture, and food scene, located about an hour and a half away from Harrisonburg.
  • Luray: A quaint town known for its underground Luray Caverns, located about 30 minutes away from Harrisonburg.
  • Richmond: The capital of Virginia, known for its rich history, culture, and vibrant food and drink scene, located about two hours away from Harrisonburg.

Conclusion

Harrisonburg is a delightful destination that offers something for everyone, from historic landmarks and museums to outdoor adventures and delicious food. Whether you are looking to relax and unwind or to explore and discover, Harrisonburg is sure to captivate your heart and soul. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you will have everything you need to plan the perfect trip to Harrisonburg.
