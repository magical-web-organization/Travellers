Introduction to Northwest High Country

Northwest High Country is a region in Historic High Country, Georgia, located in the southern part of the United States of America. This area is known for its stunning natural beauty, pristine mountain vistas, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or an outdoor enthusiast, Northwest High Country has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest High Country

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chieftains Museum - This museum is dedicated to telling the story of the Cherokee Nation and the Trail of Tears.
  • Booth Western Art Museum - Featuring one of the largest collections of western art in the country, this museum is a must-visit for art lovers.
  • The Southern Museum of Civil War and Locomotive History - This museum features exhibits on the Civil War, the construction of the Western and Atlantic Railroad, and the Glover Machine Works.

Natural Attractions of Northwest High Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amicalola Falls State Park - Home to the highest waterfall in Georgia, this park is perfect for hiking, fishing, and camping.
  • Lake Blue Ridge - This 3,300-acre lake offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • Brasstown Bald - This is the highest point in Georgia, and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest High Country

  • Benton MacKaye Trail - This 300-mile trail runs from Georgia to Tennessee, offering scenic views of the Appalachian Mountains.
  • Pinhoti Trail - This 335-mile trail runs through Alabama and Georgia and offers opportunities for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Mountain Bike Trails at Blankets Creek - Located in Canton, this trail system offers over 15 miles of trails for mountain bikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Northwest High Country: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Ocoee River - The Ocoee River is one of the best places in the country for whitewater rafting.
  • Zip-lining at Historic Banning Mills - Historic Banning Mills offers the world’s longest and largest zip-line canopy tour.
  • Wine Tasting at Wolf Mountain Vineyards and Winery - This winery offers award-winning wines and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest High Country

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Green Tomatoes - A southern classic, this dish features lightly battered and fried slices of green tomatoes.
  • Boiled Peanuts - A salty and savory snack, boiled peanuts are a southern tradition and a staple at roadside stands.
  • Pecan Pie - Made with Georgia pecans, this sweet and nutty pie is a must-try dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A southern favorite, this sweet and refreshing drink is perfect for hot summer days.
  • Moonshine - A distilled spirit made from corn, moonshine is a staple of southern culture and is often flavored with fruit or spices.
  • Wine - The North Georgia Wine Trail is home to over a dozen wineries, offering a variety of reds, whites, and blushes.

Where to Stay in Northwest High Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Brasstown Valley Resort & Spa - Located in Young Harris, this resort offers luxurious accommodations and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Lodge at Copperhead - This rustic lodge in Blairsville offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to outdoor activities.
  • Mountain Aire Cottages & Inn - Located in the quaint town of Ellijay, this inn offers cozy cabins and a peaceful setting.

What to Buy in Northwest High Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery - The North Georgia Mountains are home to a thriving pottery community, with many artists offering unique and handmade pieces.
  • Local Honey - Georgia is known for its delicious honey, made by bees that feed on local flowers and plants.
  • Handcrafted Furniture - Many local craftsmen create beautiful and functional pieces of furniture using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest High Country

Northwest High Country is steeped in southern culture, with a rich history and a deep appreciation for nature. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly locals, southern hospitality, and a laid-back way of life.

Best Time to Visit Northwest High Country

The best time to visit Northwest High Country is in the fall, when the leaves turn vibrant shades of red, yellow, and orange. The weather is cool and crisp, and outdoor activities are at their peak. Spring is also a great time to visit, with mild temperatures and beautiful wildflowers in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest High Country

Do:

  • Do explore the local hiking trails and outdoor activities.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with friendly locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwest High Country

Northwest High Country is generally a safe area to visit, but visitors should be aware of the potential for outdoor hazards such as rattlesnakes and black bears. Always follow park regulations and guidelines while hiking or participating in other outdoor activities.

Health in Northwest High Country

Visitors to Northwest High Country should be aware of the potentially high altitude and the need to stay hydrated. Tick-borne illnesses such as Lyme disease are also a concern in the area, so bring insect repellent and be vigilant about checking for ticks.

Getting to Northwest High Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northwest High Country

  • Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport - Located 56 miles from Northwest High Country.
  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport - Located 93 miles from Northwest High Country.

Bus Stations Near Northwest High Country

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Blairsville Bus Station - Located in Blairsville, GA.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Northwest High Country

The best way to get around Northwest High Country is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also explore the area by bike or on foot, particularly in the many parks and scenic areas.

Where to Go Next from Northwest High Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Savannah, GA - Located 270 miles southeast of Northwest High Country, Savannah is known for its historic architecture, lively nightlife, and stunning coastal beauty.
  • Nashville, TN - Located 165 miles northwest of Northwest High Country, Nashville is a music lover’s paradise and a hub of entertainment and culture.
  • Asheville, NC - Located 60 miles northeast of Northwest High Country, Asheville is a quirky and vibrant city famous for its food, beer, and art scene.

Conclusion

Northwest High Country is a hidden gem in the southern United States, offering stunning scenery, outdoor adventures, and a rich history and culture. From hiking to wine tasting, there is something for everyone in this beautiful and unique region. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of Northwest High Country for yourself.
