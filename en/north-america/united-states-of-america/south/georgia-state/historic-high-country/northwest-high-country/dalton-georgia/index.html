Introduction to Dalton (Georgia)

Dalton, Georgia is a small city located in the Northwest High Country region of Georgia, known for its rich history in the carpet industry. But beyond its industrial roots, Dalton also boasts picturesque scenery and plenty of outdoor adventure opportunities. This guide will provide you with an overview of what to see and do in Dalton, along with tips for where to stay, eat, and explore.

List of Places to Visit in Dalton (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Whitfield-Murray Historical Society and Museum: Explore the rich history of Dalton and Whitfield County at the Whitfield-Murray Historical Society and Museum. The museum houses a variety of exhibits and artifacts, including the largest collection of dolls in the country.
  • Prater’s Mill: Take a step back in time at Prater’s Mill, a historic grist mill that dates back to the mid-19th century. Tours are available to learn about the milling process and the history of the mill.
  • Resaca Battlefield Historic Site: This Civil War battlefield was the site of the Battle of Resaca, fought on May 14-15, 1864. Today, visitors can explore the site and learn about the battle and its impact on the area.

Natural Attractions of Dalton (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fort Mountain State Park: For outdoor adventure enthusiasts, Fort Mountain State Park is a must-visit destination. Hike or bike the trails, set up camp in the campground, or take a dip in the lake.
  • Rocky Face Mountain Park: This historic park is home to a variety of hiking and biking trails, as well as stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Be sure to snap a photo at the iconic overlook platform.
  • Chief Vann House Historic Site: Immerse yourself in the history and culture of the Cherokee people at Chief Vann House Historic Site. The site features a beautifully restored mansion and other historic buildings, as well as hiking trails and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dalton (Georgia)

  • Pinhoti Trail: This 335-mile trail spans from Alabama to Georgia, and runs through Dalton. It offers breathtaking views and diverse terrains for hikers and bikers.
  • Bandy Creek Trail: Located in Fort Mountain State Park, this trail offers a challenging hike with rewarding scenic views of the mountains.
  • Kahuna Trail: For a moderate hike, explore the Kahuna Trail in Rocky Face Mountain Park. This trail offers unique rock formations and plenty of wildlife spotting opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Dalton (Georgia): Activities and Tours

  • Dalton Ghost Tours: Get spooked on a Dalton Ghost Tour, where trained guides will take you on a walking tour of the city’s most haunted sites.
  • Dalton Brewing Company: Sample local craft beers and enjoy live music at Dalton Brewing Company.
  • Dalton Distillery: Take a tour of Dalton Distillery and sample their award-winning moonshine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dalton (Georgia)

Day Activity
Day 1 Check in to your hotel, explore the downtown area, and enjoy dinner at one of the many restaurants in the city center.
Day 2 Visit Prater’s Mill and take a hike at Fort Mountain State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Dalton (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carpetbagger Steak: This local specialty features a filet mignon stuffed with fried oysters.
  • Brunswick Stew: A savory stew made with chicken, vegetables, and barbecue sauce.
  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple, sweet tea is the perfect refreshing beverage to accompany any meal.

Local Beverages

  • Dalton Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their popular “Carpetbagger” porter.
  • Dalton Distillery: Sample their award-winning moonshine or enjoy their signature cocktail, “Georgia Peach Shine.”

Where to Stay in Dalton (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Courtyard by Marriott Dalton: This convenient hotel is located in the downtown area and offers spacious rooms and amenities such as a fitness center and an on-site restaurant.
  • Fairfield Inn & Suites Dalton: A great option for families, this hotel offers an indoor pool and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Dalton Depot: For a unique experience, stay at this historic train station turned bed and breakfast.

What to Buy in Dalton (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dalton Distillery Moonshine: Bring home a bottle of award-winning moonshine from Dalton Distillery.
  • Handmade Pottery: Dalton is home to several local potters, offering unique pieces that make for great souvenirs.
  • Carpet and Floor Coverings: As the “Carpet Capital of the World,” it’s no surprise that Dalton is a great place to shop for floor coverings.

Local Culture and Customs of Dalton (Georgia)

The people of Dalton are proud of their industrial heritage and their love for Southern hospitality. Visitors can expect to be greeted with a friendly smile and warm welcome, and should be sure to take the time to learn about the city’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Dalton (Georgia)

The best times to visit Dalton are during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter may bring snow and colder temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dalton (Georgia)

Do:

  • Do take the time to learn about the city’s history and culture.
  • Do try local specialties such as carpetbagger steak and Brunswick stew.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring your camera to capture the picturesque scenery.
  • Don’t miss the chance to try local craft beers and moonshine.
  • Don’t leave without experiencing Southern hospitality first-hand.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dalton (Georgia)

Dalton is a safe city, but as with any travel destination, visitors should take the usual precautions such as locking doors, securing valuables, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Dalton (Georgia)

Visitors to Dalton should have no concerns about their health, but as always, it’s a good idea to bring any necessary medications and check with your healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Dalton (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dalton (Georgia)

The closest major airport to Dalton is Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport, located about 30 miles away. Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is also accessible, located approximately 90 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Dalton (Georgia)

Greyhound and Southeastern Stages both have bus stations in Dalton.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a great option, as it allows more flexibility in exploring the area.

How to Get Around in Dalton (Georgia)

The best way to get around Dalton is by car, but visitors can also explore the downtown area on foot.

Where to Go Next from Dalton (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chattanooga, Tennessee: Just a short drive away, Chattanooga offers a variety of attractions, including the Tennessee Aquarium and the Lookout Mountain Incline Railway.
  • Blue Ridge, Georgia: Explore the stunning scenery of the Blue Ridge Mountains and indulge in outdoor activities such as hiking and zip lining.
  • Asheville, North Carolina: Known for its vibrant arts scene and beautiful mountain views, Asheville makes for a great day trip or weekend getaway.

Conclusion

Dalton, Georgia may be a small city, but it offers plenty of opportunities for exploration and adventure. From hiking through the mountains to sampling local craft beers and moonshine, there’s something for everyone. Plan your trip today and come discover the charm of Dalton for yourself.
