Introduction to Commerce (Georgia)

Welcome to Commerce, Georgia, a charming town in the Historic High Country region of Georgia. Located in the eastern part of the state, Commerce offers a great blend of rich history, natural beauty, and modern amenities. With its friendly locals, excellent food, and plenty of outdoor activities to choose from, Commerce is an ideal destination for anyone looking for a relaxing and enjoyable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Commerce (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Commerce can explore the area’s rich history and culture by visiting its many museums and historical sites. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Tanger Outlets: perfect destination for shopping lovers.
  • Old Jefferson Historic District: take a step back in time by exploring the charming historic district of Commerce, which features many well-preserved buildings from the 19th century.
  • Jackson County Courthouse: enjoy the great architecture of the courthouse to capture your moments.
  • Skate-N-Fun Zone: the amazing roller rink and family center makes for a perfect destination to have a great time with family and friends.

Natural Attractions of Commerce (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Commerce is an ideal destination for those who love the great outdoors. The town is surrounded by beautiful forests, stunning natural landscapes, and a host of outdoor activities to choose from. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions in Commerce:

  • Shankle Park: is set aside for recreational activities and its the largest park in Commerce.
  • Hurricane Shoals Park: explore the great hiking trails and the beautiful riverside park.
  • Sandy Creek Nature Center: is a natural reserve and perfect for bird-watchers and nature lovers.
  • New Salem Campground & Deli: offers a variety of outdoor activities like camping, hiking, and fishing. There is also a deli that serves delicious meals and sandwiches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Commerce (Georgia)

Commerce features many great trails and routes for hikers and bikers of all levels. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Commerce:

  • Commerce Downtown Circuit: is a great biking route that explores the beautiful historic downtown area.
  • Sandy Creek Nature Center Trails: offers five beautiful hiking and biking trails of varying levels, all of which are surrounded by stunning natural landscapes.
  • Stone Creek Trail: is a three-mile hiking and biking trail that winds through the beautiful Stone Creek Park
  • The Furman Trail: is the longest trail in Commerce, with a length of 7.5 miles. This trail is ideal for seasoned hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Commerce (Georgia): Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to see and do in Commerce. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day at the park or an exciting adventure, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try out in Commerce:

  • Shankle Park Splash Pad: perfect for kids and families, this splash pad is a great way to cool off on a hot day.
  • Tasting tours: visit various wineries and distilleries to taste local Georgian wines and spirits.
  • Spa Day: Enjoy an incredible day at the spa, with different types of spa services in Commerce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Commerce (Georgia)

If you’re planning to spend a week in Commerce, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Explore the Historic Downtown District Day 2: Spend a day hiking or biking on one of the many beautiful trails in the area Day 3: Visit the wineries/distilleries and taste some of the best wines and spirits of Georgia. Day 4: Enjoy a relaxing day at one of the many beautiful parks in Commerce. Day 5: Visit some of the town’s museums and historical sites, including the Tanger Outlets. Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, including Braselton, Athens, and nearby State Parks. Day 7: Try out some of the best local restaurants and bars and indulge in some delicious local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Commerce (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

Commerce is well known for its delicious local cuisine, which draws on traditional Southern cooking and local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes during your visit:

  • Fried Chicken: enjoy the best of Southern fried chicken and homemade biscuits.
  • Pecan Pie: indulge in this sweet treat which is a local specialty
  • Burgers and BBQ: enjoy some of the best burgers and barbeque in Georgia.

Local Beverages

In addition to its delicious food, Commerce is also known for its local wines and spirits. The town is home to several wineries and distilleries that produce some of the best beverages in the state. Here are some of the best local beverages to try:

  • Julep Cocktail: a refreshing beverage made with mint and bourbon, a must-have during the summer season.
  • Georgia Beer: is a popular drink which is a must-try to relax in the sun.

Where to Stay in Commerce (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Commerce offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Commerce:

  • Hampton Inn: a modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a wide range of amenities, including an outdoor pool and fitness center.
  • Baymont Inn & Suites: this budget-friendly hotel offers great value for money and is located conveniently close to the major attractions.
  • The Cozy Cottage: a charming guesthouse that offers a cozy and home-like environment with comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Commerce (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a memento from your trip to Commerce, there are plenty of great options to choose from. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Commerce include:

  • Georgia Wines and Spirits: pick up a bottle or two of locally-made wines and spirits.
  • Handicrafts: explore the local markets in Commerce to buy handmade gifts for your loved ones.
  • Pecans: bring home some delicious locally grown pecans - a favorite local snack.

Local Culture and Customs of Commerce (Georgia)

Commerce is a town with a strong sense of community and hospitality, where locals are always happy to welcome visitors. Here are some local customs and cultural practices to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Southern Hospitality: the locals in Commerce are known for their incredible hospitality and welcoming nature. Expect to be greeted with a warm smile and friendly conversation wherever you go.
  • Respectful behavior: Commerce is a small town with strong ties to its history and culture. As a visitor, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Appropriate dress code: The town of Commerce is easy going but carries certain traditional values. It is important to dress modestly as you visit the flea markets and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Commerce (Georgia)

The best time to visit Commerce is during the fall when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing colors. This is also harvest season when local wineries and distilleries offer special events that are not to be missed

Safety and Health

Safety in Commerce (Georgia)

Commerce is a safe and friendly town, with low crime rates and safe streets. While walking around the downtown area, visitors should exercise caution at night and keep their valuables safe. Visitors should always pay attention to the surroundings to avoid any untoward incidents.

Health in Commerce (Georgia)

Commerce has several medical centers to address immediate health issues of its visitors.

Getting to Commerce (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Commerce (Georgia)

The closest airport to Commerce is the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Commerce (Georgia)

There are several bus stations located near Commerce, including the Greyhound Bus Station in Athens, which is about 30 minutes away from the town center.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Commerce by driving. The town is easily reachable by taking I-85 North of Atlanta.

How to Get Around in Commerce (Georgia)

Commerce is small enough to explore on foot, and most attractions are within walking distance from the town center. Alternatively, visitors can rent a car or bike to explore the area at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Commerce (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting while in Commerce. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Braselton: is a beautiful town located about 20 minutes away from Commerce, which is famous for its vineyards and natural beauty.
  • Athens: is a vibrant college town located about 30 minutes away from Commerce, and features a diverse range of cultural attractions and outdoor activities.
  • Nearby State Parks: are a great place to explore the natural beauty of Georgia, including the Oconee State Park with its stunning lake and the Sandy Creek Nature Center.

Conclusion

Commerce is a great destination for those looking to experience the best of Georgia’s natural beauty and hospitality. From its charming historic downtown to its beautiful parks and outdoor attractions, Commerce has something for everyone. By following this guide, you’re sure to enjoy a memorable and enjoyable vacation in one of Georgia’s most beautiful small towns.
