Introduction to Clayton (Georgia)

Clayton is a small town located in northeastern Georgia, USA, which is rising in popularity for its spectacular views of the Blue Ridge Mountains, historic landmarks, natural wonders, and warm, welcoming locals. Clayton is surrounded by and in close proximity to other popular destinations such as Highlands, North Carolina, and Helen in Georgia.

List of Places to Visit in Clayton (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Main Street Gallery - A vibrant, contemporary fine art gallery that showcases local and national artists’ works.
  • Foxfire Museum and Heritage Center - This Museum is dedicated to the preservation of Southern Appalachian culture and its history through exhibits and educational programs.
  • Rabun County Historical Society and Museum - This museum highlights the rich history of Rabun County.
  • Andrews House - One of the oldest buildings in Clayton is the oldest surviving example of vernacular architecture in the region.

Natural Attractions of Clayton (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bartram Trail - Bartram Trail is a 37-mile trail that runs through the Chattooga River District of the national forests of North Georgia and the Chattahoochee National Forests.
  • Tallulah Gorge State Park - Tallulah Gorge State Park offers one of the most spectacular gorges in the eastern United States. The gorge is 2 miles long and nearly 1,000 feet deep.
  • Black Rock Mountain State Park - Black Rock Mountain State Park is Georgia’s highest park, offering spectacular views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
  • Rabun County Waterfalls - Clayton is surrounded by several beautiful waterfalls that are hidden gems in the Chattahoochee National Forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clayton (Georgia)

  • Bartram Trail
  • Lakeside Trail
  • Tallulah Gorge Trail
  • James E. Edmonds Backcountry Trail
  • Chattooga River Trail
  • Three Forks to Springer Mountain

Best Things to Do in Clayton (Georgia): Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting - Go rafting at Wildwater Chattooga while enjoying the beauty of the forest surroundings.
  • Zip Lining - Go zip-lining with North Georgia Canopy Tours for a thrilling experience.
  • Spas - Enjoy a hot tub experience at the nearby Black Rock Mountain Lodge, which boasts stunning views.
  • Wine Tasting - Taste different types of wine at the nearby Tiger Mountain Vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Clayton (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grits - A staple dish of the South, served as a side for most breakfast dishes.
  • Fried Chicken - A classic Southern dish consisting of chicken that’s coated in seasoned flour and then fried.
  • Cornbread - An essential side dish for any Southern meal.
  • Pimento Cheese - A popular Southern spread made with cheddar cheese and pimentos.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - Sweetened iced tea that is a staple beverage in the South.
  • Moonshine - A type of strong Southern whiskey typically brewed by locals.

Where to Stay in Clayton (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rabun Manor - A historic bed and breakfast that offers cozy rooms and delicious breakfast prepared with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Black Rock Mountain Lodge - This lodge offers some stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is perfect for a relaxing getaway.
  • The White Birch Inn - A charming bed and breakfast with beautiful gardens and a patio with rocking chairs.

What to Buy in Clayton (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery - The Foxfire Gallery sells handmade pottery made by local artists.
  • Wine - Buying wines from the nearby Tiger Mountain Vineyards can make a great souvenir.
  • Honey and Jams - Rabun County is known for its natural honey and fresh fruit preserves that make for great gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Clayton (Georgia)

Clayton is heavily influenced by Southern culture. It is always suggested to greet the locals and be polite and cordial. Southerners value hospitality, and it’s not uncommon for strangers to be treated like friends. Overuse of the word “y’all” might make one a bit more endearing to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Clayton (Georgia)

Spring and fall are the ideal times to visit Clayton, GA. The beautiful surroundings, pleasant temperatures, and an array of outdoor activities make these seasons perfect for a visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clayton (Georgia)

Do:

  • Be polite and courteous to everyone.
  • Leave no trace while enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Appreciate Southern hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private land.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Leave trash in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clayton (Georgia)

Clayton is known as a safe town with low crime rates. It is always suggested to practice general caution when walking alone at night.

Health in Clayton (Georgia)

There is no major health concern in Clayton. However, it would be good to pack mosquito repellents as it is a foreside area and ticks can also be a concern during summer months.
