Introduction to Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Blue Ridge, nestled in the Northeast High Country and Historic High Country in Georgia, is a charming town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. It’s a popular tourist destination for those who love outdoor adventures, scenic views, and small-town vibes.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Here are the must-visit places in Blue Ridge (Georgia):

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Blue Ridge Scenic Railway: Take a scenic train ride through the mountains and countryside.
  • Heritage House: Visit this 19th century raised cottage and learn about the local history and culture.
  • The Art Center: Experience the artistic side of Blue Ridge with exhibitions, events, and classes.

Natural Attractions of Blue Ridge (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Ridge Mountains: Hike the scenic trails of the Blue Ridge Mountains, with beautiful views and wildlife.
  • Lake Blue Ridge: Enjoy boating, fishing, and swimming in this serene lake.
  • Falls Branch Falls: Take a hike to this beautiful waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Blue Ridge offers several hiking and biking trails for adventurers. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Aska Trails: 17 miles of interconnected trails for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Benton MacKaye Trail: A 6.3-mile trail with scenic views and an opportunity to see wildlife.
  • Toccoa River Swinging Bridge: A 270-foot bridge across the Toccoa River with a 4-mile hiking or biking trail.

Best Things to Do in Blue Ridge (Georgia): Activities and Tours

Here are some activities and tours to try in Blue Ridge:

  • Ziplining: Take a zipline tour through the trees and enjoy beautiful views of the mountains.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the region by horseback, with a variety of trails and guided tours available.
  • Fly Fishing: Enjoy the fishing experience, with guided tours available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Blue Ridge:

Day Activity
1 Explore the downtown area, including shopping and dining
2 Take a scenic train ride on the Blue Ridge Scenic Railway
3 Hike the trails of Blue Ridge Mountains
4 Relax and enjoy a day by Lake Blue Ridge
5 Go ziplining or horseback riding
6 Visit museums and historical places
7 End the trip with fly fishing and a picnic by the water

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Blue Ridge offers a diverse food and drink scene, with several local and international cuisines available. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken from Joe’s BBQ: Delicious southern-style fried chicken served with homemade hot sauce.
  • Trout and grits from Harvest on Main: Freshly caught trout served with creamy cheese grits.
  • Barbecue ribs from Blue Ridge Mountain BBQ: Juicy and flavorful BBQ ribs, smoked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Apple cider from Mercier Orchards: Locally produced fresh apple cider, served chilled or hot.
  • Craft beer from Grumpy Old Men Brewing: A variety of delicious craft beers, served in a cozy taproom.
  • Georgia wine from Serenberry Vineyards: A family-owned winery producing exquisite Georgia wines and offering tastings.

Where to Stay in Blue Ridge (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blue Ridge offers a variety of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some popular choices:

  • Blue Ridge Inn Bed and Breakfast: A cozy and charming B&B in the heart of downtown Blue Ridge.
  • Blue Ridge Treehouse Rentals: Unique and secluded treehouse rentals for a memorable experience in nature.
  • The Murphy House: A spacious and elegant guesthouse for families or groups.

What to Buy in Blue Ridge (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products to consider bringing home from Blue Ridge:

  • Handmade pottery from Appalachian Art Studio: Unique and beautiful pottery made by local artisans.
  • Gourmet chocolate from Blue Ridge Mountain Chocolate Factory: Delicious and handcrafted chocolates with many flavors.
  • Local honey from BumbleBee’s Café and Market: Pure honey harvested from the local bees and available in jars and squeeze bottles.

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Blue Ridge is proud of its southern and Appalachian heritage. Here are some local culture and customs:

  • The Blue Ridge Mountain Arts Association hosts various cultural events and exhibitions throughout the year, showcasing art, music, and dance performances.
  • The town’s annual Blue Ridge Mountains Wine and Jazz Festival attracts wine enthusiasts and jazz lovers from all over the region.
  • The annual Georgia Mountain Fair features regional food and crafts, concerts, and carnival rides.

Best Time to Visit Blue Ridge (Georgia)

The best time to visit Blue Ridge is spring, summer, and fall, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor adventures. The fall colors are especially breathtaking in the mountains during October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Here are some guidelines to keep in mind when visiting Blue Ridge:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and craft beer or wine.
  • Do take advantage of the scenic trails and outdoor activities.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips for visiting Blue Ridge:

Safety in Blue Ridge (Georgia)

  • Be aware of wildlife such as snakes and bears, and keep a safe distance.
  • Always follow the hiking and biking trail guidelines and signage.
  • Be cautious when driving on the mountain roads, especially in the winter.

Health in Blue Ridge (Georgia)

  • Always use sunscreen and stay hydrated when spending time outdoors.
  • Check for ticks during and after your time in the woods.
  • Wear proper footwear when hiking or doing outdoor activities.

How to Get Around in Blue Ridge (Georgia)

Blue Ridge is a small town, and many attractions are within walking distance of downtown. However, a car or bike may be necessary for exploring the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Blue Ridge (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blue Ridge is surrounded by several towns and attractions worth exploring. Here are some ideas for nearby destinations:

  • Blairsville, Georgia: A charming town with hiking trails, wineries, and sightseeing opportunities.
  • Chattanooga, Tennessee: A vibrant city with attractions such as the Tennessee Aquarium and the Hunter Museum of American Art.
  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: A spectacular national park with numerous hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife.
