Introduction to Morganton (Georgia)

Morganton is a charming town situated in the Northeast High Country region of Georgia. The town is surrounded by breathtaking mountain ranges and scenic views, making it a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

The town features a blend of old-world charm and modern amenities, offering visitors a unique experience. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Morganton has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will dive into the best places to visit, food and drink options, where to stay, and more in Morganton, Georgia.

List of Places to Visit in Morganton (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. The Old Federal Road

The Old Federal Road was once a major transportation route in the early 19th century. Today, visitors can walk the trail and learn about the road’s history, including its importance in transporting goods and people from Tennessee to Alabama.

2. Chattahoochee-Oconee National Forest

Chattahoochee-Oconee National Forest spans across multiple counties in Georgia and offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the region. The forest offers opportunities for hiking, fishing, camping and more.

Natural Attractions of Morganton (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Cohutta Wildlife Management Area

The Cohutta Wildlife Management Area is located on the northwest side of Morganton and features over 96,000 acres of protected wilderness. The area boasts a range of hiking trails, campsites, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

2. Lake Blue Ridge

Lake Blue Ridge is a stunning reservoir that offers visitors various recreational activities, including fishing, water sports, and boat rentals. The picturesque lake is surrounded by rolling hills and lush forests, making it a serene escape for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morganton (Georgia)

1. Jacks River Trail

The Jacks River Trail is a challenging hike that rewards visitors with scenic river views and beautiful waterfalls. The trail is 16.3 miles long and is best suited for experienced hikers.

2. Bear Creek Trail

The Bear Creek Trail is a moderate hiking trail that offers views of the Chattahoochee National Forest. The trail is 7.5 miles long and is best for those looking for a shorter hike.

Best Things to Do in Morganton (Georgia): Activities and Tours

1. Rafting

Rafting down the Ocoee River is an exhilarating experience for adventure lovers. The river is known for its class III and IV rapids, providing a thrilling ride.

2. Horseback Riding

Horseback riding is a great way to explore the beauty of the Chattahoochee National Forest. Morganton Horseback Riding offers guided tours through the forest and provides visitors with a unique perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Morganton (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

1. Fried Green Tomatoes

Fried green tomatoes are a popular Southern dish and can be found at most restaurants around town.

2. Biscuits and Gravy

Biscuits and gravy is a classic Southern breakfast dish and is a staple on most breakfast menus in Morganton.

Local Beverages

1. Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a staple beverage in the South and can be found at most restaurants around town.

2. Apple Cider

Morganton is known for its apple orchards, making apple cider a popular beverage choice for locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Morganton (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Aska Lodge Bed and Breakfast

Aska Lodge Bed and Breakfast is a charming bed and breakfast that offers a cozy, home-like atmosphere. The lodge is situated on the edge of the Chattahoochee National Forest, providing stunning views of the surrounding nature.

e ‘D’ River Cabins

Double ‘D’ River Cabins offer visitors a chance to stay in private log cabins surrounded by nature. The cabins are located on the shores of the Toccoa River and offer a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life.

What to Buy in Morganton (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Apple Butter

Apple butter is a popular food product in Morganton and is a great souvenir to bring home. The apple butter is made from locally grown apples and can be found at most local markets.

2. Handmade Crafts

Morganton is home to many talented artists and craftsmen, making handmade crafts a popular souvenir option. Visitors can find a wide variety of crafts at local markets and boutiques around town.

Local Culture and Customs of Morganton (Georgia)

Morganton is a small town with a tight-knit community. Southern hospitality is a way of life here, and visitors will experience warm and welcoming locals everywhere they go.

Best Time to Visit Morganton (Georgia)

The best time to visit Morganton is during the fall months when the leaves change colors, creating a picturesque backdrop for outdoor activities and scenic drives.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morganton (Georgia)

Morganton is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should still take necessary precautions such as locking their cars and securing valuable items.

Health in Morganton (Georgia)

Visitors should take necessary precautions when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and rafting. It’s important to bring sufficient water and wear appropriate clothing and footwear.

Getting to Morganton (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morganton (Georgia)

The nearest airport to Morganton is the Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport, located approximately 65 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Morganton (Georgia)

The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in Dalton, Georgia, approximately 30 miles from Morganton.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to reach Morganton from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Morganton (Georgia)

The best way to get around Morganton is by car. Visitors can rent a car or drive their own vehicle to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Morganton (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Blue Ridge

Blue Ridge is a nearby town that offers visitors fantastic mountain views and quaint shops and restaurants.

2. Ellijay

Ellijay is a small town located in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains. The town is known for its apple orchards and cider houses, making it a perfect weekend destination for food and beverage enthusiasts.
