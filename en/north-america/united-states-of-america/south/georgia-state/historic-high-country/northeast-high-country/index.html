Introduction to Northeast High Country

Welcome to Northeast High Country, located in the Historic High Country region of Georgia, in the southern part of the United States of America. Renowned for its incredible natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Northeast High Country is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. From its towering mountains and pristine rivers to its quaint towns and historic landmarks, there’s no shortage of amazing things to see and do here.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Northeast High Country’s best attractions, share insider tips on food and accommodations, and give you all the information you need to plan the perfect trip. So pack your bags and get ready to explore one of the most beautiful regions of the American South!

List of Places to Visit in Northeast High Country

Whether you’re interested in history or outdoor adventures, Northeast High Country has something for every type of traveler. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Georgia Mountain Fairgrounds: Home to the annual Georgia Mountain Fair, this venue features concerts, arts and crafts, a flower show, a petting zoo, a carnival, and a series of attractions, such as the Petting Zoo and a new exhibit on the impact of dams on the local fauna.
  2. Museum of Appalachia: This living history museum in Blairsville showcases what life was like for early Appalachian settlers through a collection of houses, cabins, and other structures outfitted with period furnishings.
  3. Vogel State Park: With its Civilian Conservation Corps-era grist mill, cabins, and historical lodge, Vogel State Park is an excellent place to learn about the history and culture of the region’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Northeast High Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Brasstown Bald: At an elevation of 4,784 feet, Brasstown Bald is Georgia’s highest peak. Visitors can hike to the top or take a shuttle, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  2. Nottely River: This picturesque river is perfect for fishing, tubing, and kayaking. With its clear waters and beautiful scenery, it’s a must-visit spot for nature lovers.
  3. Blood Mountain: One of the most popular hiking destinations in the region, Blood Mountain offers a challenging but incredibly rewarding climb to the summit, where hikers can take in breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeast High Country

Northeast High Country is a hiker’s paradise, with countless trails winding through the region’s rugged mountains and forests. Here are a few of the best hikes and bike trails in the area:

  1. Appalachian Trail: The most famous hiking trail in the region, the Appalachian Trail runs through Northeast High Country and offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  2. Benton MacKaye Trail: This 300-mile trail passes through some of the most beautiful areas of the Southern Appalachians, including Northeast High Country.
  3. Richard Russell Scenic Highway Bike Trail: This bike trail follows the scenic Richard B. Russell Scenic Highway and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Northeast High Country: Activities and Tours

  1. Zip-lining: Experience the thrill of flying through the treetops on a zip-line tour of the region’s mountains and forests.
  2. Fly-fishing: With its pristine rivers and abundant trout populations, Northeast High Country is a paradise for fly-fishing enthusiasts.
  3. Wine Tasting Tours: Sample the delicious wines produced by the local vineyards of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northeast High Country

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Northeast High Country, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

1 Hike to the summit of Brasstown Bald for breathtaking views

Food and Drink Scene of Northeast High Country

No trip to Northeast High Country would be complete without sampling some of the delicious local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried Green Tomatoes: A Southern classic, fried green tomatoes are a must-try dish in Northeast High Country.
  2. Pimento Cheese: Made with cheddar cheese and pimentos, this tangy spread is a staple in Southern cuisine.
  3. Biscuits and Gravy: This hearty breakfast dish is a favorite of locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  1. Moonshine: This high-proof distilled spirit is a staple of the region’s drinking culture.
  2. Sweet Tea: No Southern meal is complete without a glass of sweet tea.
  3. Craft Beer: With a growing number of microbreweries in the region, Northeast High Country is quickly becoming a hub for craft beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Northeast High Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast or a luxurious spa resort, Northeast High Country has plenty of options to choose from. Here are a few of the best accommodations in the area:

  1. Brasstown Valley Resort and Spa: This luxurious resort offers stunning mountain views, an 18-hole golf course, and a full-service spa.
  2. Blue Ridge Mountain Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers comfortable and clean accommodations for solo travelers and families.
  3. The Ridges Resort and Marina: This lakeside resort features cozy cabins, a marina, and a restaurant with stunning lake views.

What to Buy in Northeast High Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home from Northeast High Country, here are a few ideas:

  1. Handmade Pottery: The region is known for its beautiful handmade pottery, which makes a great addition to any home.
  2. Local Honey: With its abundance of wildflowers, Northeast High Country is a honey lover’s paradise.
  3. Moonshine: Bring home a bottle of locally distilled moonshine as a unique and memorable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Northeast High Country

Northeast High Country is steeped in rich cultural traditions that date back centuries. Here are a few customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting the area:

  1. Southern Hospitality: Northeast High Country is known for its friendly locals, so be prepared for plenty of warm welcomes and friendly conversations.
  2. Respect for Nature: The region’s natural beauty is a source of pride for locals, so treat the environment with respect and care.
  3. Music and Folklore: Music and storytelling are important parts of the region’s cultural heritage, so be sure to check out a local music festival or storytelling event.

Best Time to Visit Northeast High Country

The best time to visit Northeast High Country is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most colorful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy. Nevertheless, the region is still worth visiting any time of year for its beautiful sights and rich cultural heritage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northeast High Country

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Northeast High Country:

Do:

  1. Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  2. Do explore the region’s natural beauty through hiking and other outdoor activities.
  3. Do participate in local cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  2. Don’t underestimate the difficulty of hiking trails.
  3. Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations and advice.

Safety and Health

Northeast High Country is generally a safe destination, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are a few tips for staying safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Northeast High Country

  1. Always use caution when hiking and follow trail markers and safety guidelines.
  2. Keep valuables locked in a secure location and be mindful of your surroundings.
  3. Only visit reputable outfitters when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Northeast High Country

  1. Remember to drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen while enjoying outdoor activities.
  2. Don’t forget to pack a first aid kit and any necessary medications.
  3. Be aware of tick-borne illnesses, which can be prevalent in the region during the summer months.

How to Get Around in Northeast High Country

While renting a car is the easiest way to get around Northeast High Country, there are also several other options to consider:

  2. Public Transportation: MARTA and Greyhound Bus Lines offer convenient options for getting around the area.
  3. Shuttle Services: Shuttle services are available for transport from one attraction to another.

Where to Go Next from Northeast High Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Once you’ve explored Northeast High Country, there are many other destinations within easy driving distance to consider. Here are a few ideas:

  1. Savannah, GA: Located on the Atlantic Coast, Savannah is famous for its historic architecture and Southern charm.
  2. Great Smoky Mountains National Park: Located just north of Northeast High Country, this national park features some of the most stunning mountain views in the country.
  3. Chattanooga, TN: Located just across the border in Tennessee, Chattanooga is home to a thriving arts and culture scene, as well as a number of outdoor adventures.
