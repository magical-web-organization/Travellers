Introduction to Helen

If you’re searching for a unique destination in the Northeast High Country of Georgia, look no further than Helen. Originally a logging town, this quaint village has transformed into a Bavarian-style alpine town. Nestled in the mountains, Helen offers visitors a perfect mix of outdoor adventures, historic sites, and cultural experiences. Whether you’re looking for a romantic getaway, family vacation, or solo adventure, Helen has something for everyone. Read on to plan your perfect trip to this charming mountain town.

List of Places to Visit in Helen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Charlemagne’s Kingdom: Step into the world of miniature trains at this museum that showcases a collection of German model trains.
  • Nora Mill Granary: Learn about the history of milling in the area and sample freshly made grits, cornbread, and other baked goods.
  • Hardman Farm State Historic Site: Take a guided tour of this beautifully preserved antebellum mansion and gardens.
  • Sautee Nacoochee Center: Explore the history and culture of the area through art exhibits, theater shows, and festivals.

Natural Attractions of Helen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Unicoi State Park: Hike, fish, bike, or paddle in this stunning park that offers breathtaking views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
  • Anna Ruby Falls: Witness the stunning two waterfalls fed by Curtis and York Creeks at this popular recreation area.
  • Smithgall Woods: Go fly fishing in the pristine trout stream that winds through this scenic wilderness area.
  • Chattahoochee River: Tubing down this famous river is a must-do summer activity in Helen.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Helen

Helen is paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. With its location in the heart of Chattahoochee National Forest, the town provides access to miles of hiking and biking trails. Here are some routes to consider:

  • Anna Ruby Falls Trail: This 4.6-mile out-and-back trail takes hikers to the stunning twin waterfalls of Anna Ruby Falls.
  • Unicoi State Park Trails: The park offers over 8 miles of hiking and biking trails along with other outdoor activities like swimming and fishing.
  • Dukes Creek Falls Trail: Explore the 2.2-mile trail that leads to the beautiful Dukes Creek Falls and take in the stunning views of the forest.

Best Things to Do in Helen: Activities and Tours

  • Alpine Helen Carriage Rides: Take a romantic horse-drawn carriage tour through Helen’s charming, alpine-style architecture.
  • Habersham Vineyards & Winery: Enjoy wine tasting at the oldest and largest winery in Georgia, located only a few miles from Helen.
  • Zip Line Tours: Get an adrenaline rush while taking in the stunning views of the North Georgia Mountains at Unicoi Zip Line.
  • Gold Mine Tours: Experience gold mining as it was done in the 1800s with a tour of a real gold mine at Consolidated Gold Mine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Helen

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore downtown Helen and dine at one of the authentic German restaurants.
Day 2 Go tubing in Chattahoochee River and visit Habersham Vineyards & Winery.
Day 3 Take a scenic fall driving tour of the area and visit Hardman Farm State Historic Site.
Day 4 Hike Anna Ruby Falls trail and explore Unicoi State Park.
Day 5 Go fly fishing in Smithgall Woods and attend a show at Sautee Nacoochee Center.
Day 6 Take a zip line tour and explore Unicoi State Park Trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Helen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schnitzel: This classic German dish consists of breaded and fried cutlets of meat that is served with a variety of sides.
  • Bratwurst: A popular German sausage often served with sauerkraut, bread, and mustard.
  • Apfelstrudel: A sweet pastry filled with cinnamon, sugar, and apples, often served with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • German Beer: A variety of German beer is available in Helen’s restaurants and bars. Enjoy a pint of your favorite alongside your meal.
  • Georgia Wine: The area surrounding Helen is home to many vineyards, offering numerous opportunities for wine tasting.

Where to Stay in Helen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Alpine Hilltop Haus: This romantic bed and breakfast is located in the heart of downtown Helen, offering stunning mountain views and luxurious amenities.
  • Unicoi State Park & Lodge: Featuring rustic cabins, cozy hotel rooms, and a wide range of outdoor activities, this state park is a great option for families.
  • Riverbend Motel and Cabins: Situated along the Chattahoochee River, this motel and cabin resort offers stunning views, comfortable lodging, and easy access to the town’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Helen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Helen Old Town Gift & Christmas Shop: Find unique souvenirs and seasonal gifts at this family-owned shop that has been a landmark in Helen for over 30 years.
  • Helen’s Country Store: Browse this store for homemade jams, jellies, and other local products like honey, candles, and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Helen

Helen’s German heritage is reflected in the town’s festivals and events throughout the year, such as Oktoberfest and the Christkindlmarkt. Traditional attire like lederhosen and dirndls can be seen worn during these celebrations. Visitors are encouraged to participate in the festivities and embrace the town’s cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit Helen

Helen is a popular destination year-round, but peak season is in the fall when the leaves change color. However, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, wine tasting, and shopping in the area throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Helen

Do:

  • Try the German food and beer.
  • Visit the local vineyards.
  • Engage in outdoor activities like hiking and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Use the river as a restroom.
  • Be excessively loud or disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Helen

Helen is a relatively safe destination for travelers. Nonetheless, visitors should remain aware of their surroundings and stay alert, particularly in crowded areas.

Health in Helen

Helen’s warm and humid climate makes it essential for visitors to stay well hydrated. Visitors should use sunscreen, wear hats and protective clothing, and avoid excessive exposure to the sun, particularly during the summer.

Getting to Helen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Helen

  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport: Located approximately 100 miles south of Helen, this airport has domestic and international flights and is the busiest airport in Georgia.

Bus Stations Near Helen

  • Greyhound Bus Station: located in the nearby town of Cleveland, GA, approximately 6 miles east of Helen.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is an excellent way to explore the area surrounding Helen at your own pace.
  • Private Transportation: Visitors can also arrange private transportation services to and from the town.

How to Get Around in Helen

Visitors can easily walk through downtown Helen. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take a horse-drawn carriage through the town.

Where to Go Next from Helen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Savannah, Georgia: Visit the famous city’s historic district, savannah river, and Tybee Island beach.
  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: This U.S. National Park is home to over 187,000 acres of pristine forest, hiking trails, and wildlife.
  • Atlanta, Georgia: Known as the “Capital of the South,” Atlanta is famous for its culture, arts, restaurants, and shopping.

Conclusion

Helen is the perfect destination for those who enjoy outdoor recreation, scenic drives, and authentic German culture. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming mountain town has to offer.
