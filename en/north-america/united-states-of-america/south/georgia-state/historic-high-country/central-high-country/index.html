Introduction to Central High Country

Central High Country is a captivating region located in the heart of Historic High Country, Georgia. It is a scenic and historic place that offers visitors an excellent opportunity to experience both the natural beauty and rich culture of this area. With its charming towns and villages, stunning natural landscapes, and fascinating cultural attractions, Central High Country is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the Southeastern region of the United States.

List of Places to Visit in Central High Country

If you are planning a trip to Central High Country, you will find a plethora of attractions and destinations to explore in this area. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Central High Country is home to numerous museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the area’s rich culture and heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions in this category include:

  • Etowah Indian Mounds State Historic Site: This is an impressive archaeological site where visitors can learn about the history of the indigenous people who lived in the area for more than a thousand years.

  • Tellus Science Museum: This inspiring museum features interactive exhibits and displays that cover everything from geology to astronomy.

  • Booth Western Art Museum: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the best in Western art, with an impressive collection of paintings, sculptures, and other works of art.

Natural Attractions of Central High Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Central High Country is a nature lover’s paradise, with its rugged terrain, scenic vistas, and abundant wildlife. Some of the top natural attractions in this category include:

  • Coosa Bald: This is the highest peak in the Bald Mountain Range, with a summit that offers incredible views of the surrounding area.

  • Amicalola Falls State Park: This park is home to the highest waterfall in Georgia, as well as numerous hiking trails, campsites, and picnic areas.

  • Carters Lake: This stunning reservoir is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming, with its crystal-clear waters and scenic surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central High Country

Central High Country is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with its challenging hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding region. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in this area:

  • Pine Mountain Trail: This is a challenging 23-mile hiking trail that traverses the stunning Pine Mountain Wilderness Area.

  • Silver Comet Trail: This 61-mile paved trail is excellent for biking, with its smooth surface and scenic surroundings.

  • Springer Mountain Trail: This iconic hiking trail is the southern terminus of the Appalachian Trail, offering hikers a challenging trek through some of the area’s most beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Central High Country: Activities and Tours

Central High Country offers visitors an endless list of activities and tours to choose from, whether you are looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to do in Central High Country:

  • Whitewater rafting on the Ocoee River: This is a thrilling activity that will take you through some of the most stunning scenery in the region.

  • Wine tasting at local vineyards: Central High Country is home to several excellent wineries that offer tours and tastings of their award-winning wines.

  • Antique shopping in small towns: This area is known for its charming small towns that are rich in history and antiquities. You can spend hours browsing the local stores for unique treasures and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central High Country

For those who want to make the most of their time in Central High Country, here is a sample week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Etowah Indian Mounds State Historic Site.

4 Try whitewater rafting on the Ocoee River.

Food and Drink Scene of Central High Country

Central High Country has a diverse food and drink scene that will delight any foodie. Whether you are looking for traditional Southern cuisine or something more contemporary, you will find plenty of options in this area.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Central High Country include:

  • Fried Green Tomatoes: This Southern classic is a delicious appetizer or side dish.

  • Barbecue: Central High Country is home to several excellent barbecue joints that serve up some of the best smoked meats in the region.

  • Sweet Tea: This is a traditional Southern beverage that is brewed with copious amounts of sugar, making it a sweet and refreshing drink.

Local Beverages

Central High Country is also a great place to sample some of the local beverages, including:

  • Moonshine: This strong, clear liquid is a popular local spirit that is distilled from corn and sugar.

  • Craft Beer: There are several excellent microbreweries in the area that serve up some of the best craft beer in Georgia.

Where to Stay in Central High Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Central High Country offers visitors a wide variety of accommodations to choose from, whether you are looking for luxury hotels or budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Central High Country:

  • The Ridges Resort & Marina: This lovely resort offers stunning views of Lake Chatuge, with comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities.

  • Brasstown Valley Resort and Spa: This luxurious resort features a world-class spa, as well as a range of outdoor activities and dining options.

  • The Hiker Hostel: This charming hostel caters to hikers and outdoor enthusiasts, with comfortable bunk beds and a charming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Central High Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking to bring home a souvenir or gift from your trip to Central High Country, you will find plenty of options in this area. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy include:

  • Handcrafted pottery: Central High Country is home to several talented potters who create beautiful, functional pottery.

  • Local honey: There are several beekeepers in the area who produce delicious, high-quality honey that is perfect for gift-giving.

  • Antiques: With its rich history and culture, Central High Country is a great place to buy unique antiques and collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of Central High Country

Central High Country is known for its welcoming, friendly culture, with a deep respect for tradition and history. Visitors to this area will appreciate the warmth and hospitality of the locals, as well as the opportunity to experience a unique and vibrant culture.

Best Time to Visit Central High Country

The best time to visit Central High Country is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the natural landscapes are in full bloom. Summer is also a great time to visit, particularly if you are interested in outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central High Country

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Central High Country:

Do:

  • Explore the natural landscapes and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing.

  • Try the local cuisine and sample the regional beverages, including moonshine and craft beer.

  • Take the time to learn about the local culture and history, including the indigenous people and the early settlers of the area.

Don’t:

  • litter in any of the natural areas.

  • ignore safety guidelines when participating in extreme outdoor activities.

  • overdo it with alcohol consumption.

Safety and Health

Central High Country is a safe and healthy destination for visitors, with few health risks and a low crime rate. However, visitors should take precautions when participating in outdoor activities, such as wearing proper footwear and staying hydrated.

Safety in Central High Country

  • Follow safety guidelines when participating in extreme outdoor activities like whitewater rafting and hiking.

  • Be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight when exploring tourist areas.

Health in Central High Country

  • Bring plenty of water and sunscreen when participating in outdoor activities.

  • Always check for ticks after spending time in wooded areas.

  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness while visiting Central High Country.

Getting to Central High Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Central High Country is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip:

Airports Near Central High Country

  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport: This is the closest major airport to Central High Country, located approximately two hours away by car.

  • Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport: This airport is located approximately one hour away from Central High Country, offering direct flights to several major cities.

Bus Stations Near Central High Country

  • Greyhound Bus Station, Cleveland, GA: This is the closest major bus station to Central High Country, located approximately 30 minutes away by car.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: There are several rental car companies located at the airports near Central High Country, providing a convenient way to get around the area.

  • Taxis/Uber/Lyft: These services are available in Central High Country, providing a convenient way to get around without having to rent a car.

How to Get Around in Central High Country

Central High Country is best explored by car, as many of the attractions and destinations are spread out throughout the region. However, visitors can also use taxis, buses, or rental bikes to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from Central High Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time after exploring Central High Country, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Blue Ridge, GA: This charming town is located just a short drive away from Central High Country, offering stunning natural beauty and cultural attractions.

  • Chattanooga, TN: This vibrant city is located just over an hour away from Central High Country, offering excellent dining, shopping, and entertainment options.

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: This stunning national park is located just a few hours drive away from Central High Country, offering some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the country.

Conclusion

Central High Country is a stunning and culturally rich destination that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the Southeastern region of the United States. With its charming towns, impressive museums, and stunning natural landscapes, Central High Country is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Georgia and the surrounding area.
