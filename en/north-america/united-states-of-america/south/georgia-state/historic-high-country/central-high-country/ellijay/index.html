Introduction to Ellijay

Nestled in the heart of Central High Country, Ellijay, Georgia is a picturesque destination where natural beauty and rich history combine to offer visitors an unforgettable travel experience. Surrounded by the rolling Appalachian Mountains and the Chattahoochee National Forest, this charming town is known for its breathtaking scenery, vibrant cultural scene, and outdoor adventures.

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or seeking a peaceful retreat, Ellijay has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Ellijay. We’ll also provide you with tips on local culture and customs, safety, health, and logistics for a hassle-free trip. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Ellijay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Georgia Apple Festival

Every fall, the Georgia Apple Festival draws thousands of visitors from near and far to Ellijay. This two-weekend event celebrates the region’s apple harvest with live music, arts and crafts, and delicious apple treats. Be sure to try the famous apple cider donuts and take home some fresh apples and apple products from local vendors.

Gilmer County Historical Society

History buffs will enjoy a visit to the Gilmer County Historical Society. The museum features exhibits on local history, including the Cherokee Nation, early settlers, and the civil war. The museum also showcases traditional Appalachian crafts and artifacts, for an immersive cultural experience.

Natural Attractions of Ellijay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Amicalola Falls State Park

Located just outside of Ellijay, Amicalola Falls State Park is a must-visit attraction for nature lovers. The park boasts the tallest waterfall in Georgia, hiking trails, zip lines, and a lodge for overnight stays.

Carters Lake

Carters Lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. The lake comprises 3,200 acres of water and over 60 miles of shoreline. You can also hike, bike, or horseback ride the trails around the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellijay

Ellijay River Trail

The Ellijay River Trail is a 1.9-mile trail that runs alongside the Ellijay River. The trail is ideal for walking, biking, or jogging, and offers scenic views of the river and surrounding hills.

Bear Creek Trail

The Bear Creek Trail is a 2.4-mile trail that winds through the Chattahoochee National Forest. The trail offers a varied terrain, with some steep inclines and switchbacks, and is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Ellijay: Activities and Tours

Ellijay Wine Country

Wine lovers won’t want to miss a tour of the Ellijay Wine Country. The region features several wineries and vineyards, offering Georgia-grown wine tastings and tours.

Gilmer Arts

Gilmer Arts is a non-profit arts organization that hosts exhibitions, events, and performances year-round. Visitors can attend art classes, workshops, and concerts, for an immersive cultural experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellijay

Must-Try Dishes

Apple Pie

Ellijay is the apple capital of Georgia, so it’s only fitting that visitors try the famous local apple pie. Many local shops and restaurants offer their version of the iconic apple pie, so you can’t go wrong with whichever one you choose.

Fried Green Tomatoes

Another Southern staple, fried green tomatoes, can be found at many local restaurants in Ellijay. Be sure to try them alongside other traditional Southern sides on menus, like mac and cheese or collard greens.

Local Beverages

Apple Cider

The apple cider is a must-try beverage in Ellijay, especially during the Georgia Apple Festival season, when it’s freshly made. You can also find it year-round in stores and restaurants.

Wine

As mentioned earlier, Ellijay Wine Country features several wineries and vineyards that offer Georgia-grown wine tastings and tours. You can also find local wine selections at many restaurants and stores in the area.

Where to Stay in Ellijay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Martyn House

The Martyn House is a charming bed and breakfast featuring rustic guest cabins, luxurious amenities, and gracious hospitality. The property sits on 18 acres of private forested land, with hiking trails and scenic views.

Cartecay River House

The Cartecay River House is a family-owned vacation rental property situated on the banks of the Cartecay River, which offers tubing, kayaking, and fishing. The spacious house feels like a home away from home, with a fully equipped kitchen, cozy living room, and comfortable bedrooms.

What to Buy in Ellijay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Apple Products

As the apple capital of Georgia, Ellijay is known for its apple products such as apple butter, apple cider, and apple chips. You can find these products at local farmers’ markets, grocery stores, and festivals.

Artisanal Crafts

Located in Historic High Country, Ellijay is home to many talented artisans who create unique handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and woodwork. These items can be purchased at local boutiques and artisanal markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellijay

Ellijay is a small town with a strong sense of community and pride in its heritage. Southern hospitality is always on display, with friendly locals welcoming visitors with open arms. Visitors should also be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as proper dress codes for religious sites and modesty.

Best Time to Visit Ellijay

The best time to visit Ellijay is during the fall season, which runs from September to November. The weather is pleasant, and the autumn foliage colors are vibrant. The Georgia Apple Festival is a highlight of this season, offering a glimpse into Ellijay’s rich apple-growing culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellijay

Do:

  • Take in the stunning natural beauty of Ellijay by hiking, biking, or kayaking through the mountains, rivers, and forests.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially dishes that feature Ellijay’s world-famous apples.
  • Engage with the community and locals through festivals, events, and cultural experiences.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions and customs, especially in religious sites.
  • Litter or damage natural resources while exploring the town.
  • Ignore safety precautions while participating in outdoor activities or driving on the winding mountain roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ellijay

Like any other small town in the US, Ellijay is generally safe, but visitors should exercise caution when exploring the area. Be mindful of your surroundings, lock your car doors, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Ellijay

Visitors should ensure their personal health and safety by bringing insect repellent, sunscreen, and appropriate clothing for outdoor activities. It’s also important to have adequate medical insurance coverage in case of emergency.

Getting to Ellijay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ellijay is accessible by car, bus, or air. The closest airport is the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is approximately 90 miles south of Ellijay. Visitors can also take a bus from Atlanta to Ellijay, with several daily departures from Atlanta’s Greyhound Bus Station. Once in town, renting a car or bike is the best way to get around the area.

How to Get Around in Ellijay

The best way to get around Ellijay and the surrounding area is by rental car, bike, or taxi. Some attractions, such as Amicalola Falls State Park or Carters Lake, require a car for access. Downtown Ellijay is walkable, and many attractions and restaurants are accessible on foot.

Where to Go Next from Ellijay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blue Ridge

Located just 15 miles from Ellijay, Blue Ridge is an idyllic mountain town known for its charming main street, antique shops, and scenic train rides.

Dahlonega

Dahlonega is a charming town located approximately 48 miles from Ellijay. Dahlonega is home to the University of North Georgia and is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, historic buildings, and wineries.

Conclusion

Ellijay, Georgia, has something for everyone. From the picturesque natural beauty of Amicalola Falls State Park to the vibrant cultural scene of the Georgia Apple Festival, Ellijay offers a unique travel experience that showcases the best of Central High Country. Plan a trip to Ellijay today and experience the charm and wonder of this hidden gem in the heart of Georgia.
