Introduction to West High Country

Located in the Historic High Country of Georgia, West High Country is a picturesque region that boasts of natural beauty and rich history. From its majestic mountains to its lush forests and crystal-clear waters, West High Country is a destination that has something to offer for everyone. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast seeking adventure or a history buff looking to explore the local culture, West High Country is the perfect place to be.

List of Places to Visit in West High Country

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

West High Country is steeped in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that are a must-visit for anyone traveling to the region. Some of the top attractions include:

  • The Carter Center: This museum and historic site is dedicated to the life and legacy of President Jimmy Carter and his wife, Rosalynn. Visitors can learn about their contributions to human rights, health care, and peacebuilding.

  • The Booth Western Art Museum: This museum is home to one of the largest collections of Western American art in the world. Visitors can explore exhibits that showcase the culture and heritage of the American West.

  • The Southern Museum of Civil War and Locomotive History: This museum houses artifacts and exhibits that document the Civil War and the impact of locomotives during the war.

Natural Attractions of West High Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

West High Country is renowned for its natural beauty and offers several outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Brasstown Bald: This is the highest point in Georgia and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Ocoee Whitewater Center: This center is the venue for the 1996 Summer Olympics whitewater events and is a popular spot for kayaking, rafting, and hiking.

  • Amicalola Falls State Park: This park features a stunning 729-foot waterfall and offers several hiking trails and camping sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West High Country

West High Country has several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the region’s natural beauty. Some of the top trails include:

  • Appalachian Trail: This iconic trail runs through the region and offers several scenic spots and camping sites.

  • Pinhoti Trail: This trail runs through the Chattahoochee National Forest and offers stunning vistas and challenging hiking opportunities.

  • Silver Comet Trail: This trail is a paved trail that spans more than 60 miles and is perfect for bicyclists and walkers.

Best Things to Do in West High Country: Activities and Tours

West High Country offers several activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests. Some of the top activities include:

  • Wine Tours: The region is home to several wineries that offer tours and tastings.

  • Horseback Riding: There are several stables that offer horseback riding tours through the scenic countryside.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take an unforgettable journey through the skies with a hot air balloon ride that offers stunning views of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for West High Country

Here is a week-long itinerary that covers some of the top attractions and activities in West High Country:

  • Day 1: Explore the Brasstown Bald and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Day 2: Visit the Carter Center and learn about the life and legacy of President Jimmy Carter and his wife, Rosalynn.

  • Day 3: Take a horseback riding tour through the scenic countryside and visit one of the local wineries for a tasting.

  • Day 4: Visit the Booth Western Art Museum and explore the exhibits that showcase the culture and heritage of the American West.

  • Day 5: Explore the Ocoee Whitewater Center and enjoy some kayaking or rafting.

  • Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy stunning views of the region from the sky.

  • Day 7: Visit Amicalola Falls State Park and enjoy the stunning 729-foot waterfall and the several hiking trails that the park has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of West High Country

Must-Try Dishes

West High Country is renowned for its Southern-style cuisine that offers a mix of traditional and modern dishes. Some of the top must-try dishes include:

  • Fried Chicken: This classic Southern dish is a staple in the region and is often served with biscuits and gravy.

  • Brunswick Stew: This hearty stew is made with a mix of meats and vegetables and is a favorite among locals.

  • Pecan Pie: This iconic Southern dessert is a must-try when in the region.

Local Beverages

West High Country is home to several wineries and breweries that offer a mix of local and international drinks. Some of the top local beverages to try include:

  • Georgia Peach Wine: This local wine is made with fresh peaches and is a refreshing drink to enjoy during the summer.

  • Wild Heaven Brewery: This brewery offers a mix of popular and unique brews that are a must-try for any beer enthusiast.

Where to Stay in West High Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West High Country offers several accommodation options that cater to a variety of budgets and preferences. Some of the top options include:

  • Brasstown Valley Resort and Spa: This luxury resort is located in the heart of the region and offers stunning views and top-notch amenities.

  • Serenity Inn: This cozy bed and breakfast is ideal for travelers who are looking for a comfortable and affordable stay.

  • Blairsville Lodge and Restaurant: This affordable lodge offers clean and comfortable rooms and a restaurant that serves delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in West High Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

West High Country offers several local products and souvenirs that make for great gifts and mementos. Some of the top options include:

  • Local Honey: This honey is made from the region’s flowers and is a delicious souvenir to take back home.

  • Handmade Crafts: The region is home to several skilled artisans who craft beautiful ceramics, pottery, and other handmade items.

Local Culture and Customs of West High Country

West High Country has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its food, music, and art. The region is known for its hospitality and friendliness, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit West High Country

The best time to visit West High Country is during the fall when the leaves change colors and the weather is mild. Spring and summer are also great times to visit as the region’s natural beauty is in full bloom during these seasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in West High Country

West High Country is a safe place to visit, and visitors should take standard safety precautions such as staying alert and aware of their surroundings.

Health in West High Country

Visitors should carry insect repellent as the region is home to several insects such as mosquitoes. It is also recommended to carry plenty of water and sunscreen during outdoor activities.

Getting to West High Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West High Country

The nearest airport to West High Country is the Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport, which is located about 70 miles away. Other nearby airports include the Atlanta Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and the Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport.

Bus Stations Near West High Country

The nearest bus station to West High Country is the Greyhound Bus Station in Gainesville, which is located about 45 miles away.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are also available for visitors who prefer to explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in West High Country

The best way to get around West High Country is by car as the region is spread out and offers several scenic driving routes.

Where to Go Next from West High Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West High Country is located in close proximity to several other destinations that are worth exploring. Some of the top nearby destinations include:

  • Atlanta: This bustling city is located about 70 miles away from West High Country and offers several attractions and activities.

  • Blue Ridge: This charming mountain town is located about 30 min away from West High Country and offers several outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Conclusion

West High Country is a destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and Southern charm. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, West High Country is the perfect place to explore and discover. With several top attractions, accommodation options, and activities, West High Country should definitely be on your travel bucket list.
