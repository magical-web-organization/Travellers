Introduction to Cedartown

Cedartown is a city located in West High Country, in Polk County, Georgia. The city was founded in 1834 and was named after the many cedar trees that were in the area. It is known for its rich history, as it was an important railway hub during the Civil War and was a major producer of textiles in the early 1900s. Today, Cedartown has a population of approximately 9,500 residents and is a vibrant community that welcomes visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Cedartown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Big Spring Park - A beautiful park in the heart of downtown Cedartown, featuring a 200-foot-long fountain, a rose garden, walking trails, and a playground.
  • Cedartown Museum of Coca-Cola Memorabilia - A museum dedicated to the history of Coca-Cola, featuring over 1,000 Coca-Cola artifacts, including rare bottles, posters, and vending machines.
  • Polk County Historical Society & Village - A living history museum that showcases life in Polk County during the 1800s, featuring historical buildings, artifacts, and demonstrations.
  • Cedartown Dixie Youth Baseball - A historic baseball stadium that has been home to many famous baseball players, including Jackie Robinson, Hank Aaron, and Ty Cobb.

Natural Attractions of Cedartown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pocket - A scenic state park that features a 1,000-foot-deep gorge formed by Hurricane Creek, offering hiking trails, waterfalls, and rock formations.
  • Cedar Creek Park - A beautiful park that features a swimming beach, fishing lake, hiking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Silver Comet Trail - A 61.5 mile paved multi-use trail that stretches from Smyrna, Georgia to the Alabama state line, offering breathtaking views of the Georgia countryside.
  • Chieftains Museum/Major Ridge Home - A National Historic Landmark that was the home of Major Ridge, a Cherokee leader who played a key role in the Trail of Tears.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cedartown

  • The Pocket Trail - A 5.5-mile loop trail that offers breathtaking views of Hurricane Creek, Big Cedar Mountain, and the surrounding forest.
  • The Silver Comet Trail - A 61.5-mile paved trail that is perfect for hiking, jogging, cycling, and horseback riding.
  • Cedar Creek Park Trail - A 2.4-mile loop trail that winds through the woods and around the lake, offering beautiful views of the water.

Best Things to Do in Cedartown: Activities and Tours

  • Go on a Walking Tour of Historic Downtown Cedartown - Stroll through the charming streets of downtown Cedartown and learn about the city’s rich history and architecture.
  • Take a Guided Hike to The Pocket - Explore the breathtaking views and natural beauty of The Pocket with a guide who will provide fascinating insights into the area’s geology and ecology.
  • Go Fishing at Cedar Creek Park - Spend a peaceful day fishing in the lake at Cedar Creek Park.
  • Take a Bike Tour of the Silver Comet Trail - Rent a bike and explore the scenic beauty of the Silver Comet Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Cedartown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pimento Cheese Fritters - A delicious appetizer made from pimento cheese, breaded, and fried to crispy perfection.
  • Shrimp and Grits - A southern classic featuring succulent shrimp served over creamy grits.
  • Fried Chicken and Biscuits - A southern comfort food favorite, featuring crispy fried chicken served with fluffy biscuits.

Local Beverages

  • Coca-Cola - As the birthplace of Coca-Cola, Cedartown is the perfect place to enjoy an ice-cold Coke.
  • Southern Sweet Tea - A southern classic, sweet tea is a must-try when visiting Cedartown.
  • Georgia Craft Beer - Georgia is home to many craft breweries, and Cedartown has its own brewery, the Muckalee Creek Craft Brewery.

Where to Stay in Cedartown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Hampton Inn Cedartown - A modern hotel located in the heart of downtown Cedartown, offering comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Cedartown Inn & Suites - An affordable hotel located just a few minutes from downtown Cedartown, offering comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a swimming pool.
  • Airbnb Rentals - Cedartown has a great selection of Airbnb rentals, including cozy cottages, historic homes, and peaceful retreats.

What to Buy in Cedartown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coca-Cola Memorabilia - Visit the Cedartown Museum of Coca-Cola Memorabilia to buy unique Coke-themed souvenirs, including rare bottles, posters, and vending machines.
  • Handmade Crafts - Cedartown has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and you can find beautiful handmade items at local shops and markets.
  • Georgia Peaches - Nothing says “Georgia” like fresh, juicy peaches. Visit a local farmers’ market to buy some of the state’s best peaches.

Local Culture and Customs of Cedartown

Cedartown is known for its friendly and welcoming residents, who take great pride in their city’s rich history and culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including being polite, dressing appropriately, and being mindful of others.

Best Time to Visit Cedartown

The best time to visit Cedartown is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cedartown

Cedartown is a generally safe city, but visitors should take common sense precautions, such as locking their cars, being aware of their surroundings at night, and avoiding walking alone in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Cedartown

Visitors to Cedartown should be aware of the risk of tickborne illnesses, which are prevalent in the area. Wear long sleeves and pants, use insect repellent, and check for ticks after spending time outdoors.

How to Get Around in Cedartown

Cedartown is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car or take a taxi.
