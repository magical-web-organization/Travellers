Introduction to Carrollton (Georgia)

Located in West High Country, in Historic High Country, in Georgia, in the South of the United States of America, Carrollton is a charming city that offers a mix of Southern hospitality, history, and natural beauty. The city has a relatively small population of just over 26,000, which makes it an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful, laid-back vacation away from the hustle and bustle of big cities.

List of Places to Visit in Carrollton (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Carrollton is home to several museums and historical sites that offer insight into the city’s rich past. One of the must-visit attractions is the Carrollton Cultural Arts Center, which hosts various art exhibitions, theater performances, and music events throughout the year. For history buffs, the Southwire Company Museum is a great place to learn about the city’s industrial heritage, while the Oak Hill & The Martha Berry Museum is a beautifully preserved historic home that offers guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Carrollton (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Carrollton offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, camping, picnicking, and more. The city is home to several parks, such as John Tanner State Park, which features a lake with a beach, hiking trails, camping grounds, and mini-golf. McIntosh Reserve Park is another popular destination, known for its scenic views, equestrian trails, and a historic nature center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carrollton (Georgia)

Carrollton has several trails that are ideal for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. The Carrollton Greenbelt Trail is a popular route that covers over 16 miles and offers scenic views of the city’s parks, neighborhoods, and open spaces. The city’s other trails include the Carrollton Outer Loop Trail, which is a 4.2-mile loop trail that offers stunning views of John Tanner State Park, and the Little Tallapoosa River Water Trail, which is perfect for kayaking and canoeing.

Best Things to Do in Carrollton (Georgia): Activities and Tours

Apart from the museums, parks, and hiking trails, there are many other things to do in Carrollton. One of the best ways to explore the city is by taking a guided tour, such as the Carrollton Historic Walking Tour or the Carrollton Food Tour. For those looking for adventure, there are plenty of activities to choose from, including zip-lining, horseback riding, and fishing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carrollton (Georgia)

To make the most of your vacation in Carrollton, here’s a week-long itinerary that includes the best places to visit and things to do in the city:

Day 1: Visit the Southwire Company Museum and Oak Hill & The Martha Berry Museum.

Day 2: Explore John Tanner State Park and enjoy water sports activities.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of Carrollton’s historic sites, such as the Carrollton Cultural Arts Center.

Day 4: Go horseback riding at McIntosh Reserve Park.

Day 5: Enjoy a scenic drive along the Carrollton Outer Loop Trail.

Day 6: Take the Carrollton Food Tour and experience the city’s culinary scene.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at Little Tallapoosa River Water Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Carrollton (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

Carrollton has a vibrant food scene, with several restaurants that serve traditional Southern cuisine, as well as international flavors. Some must-try dishes include the fried chicken at Scott’s Walk-Up BBQ, the hot chicken sandwich at The Alley Cat, and the shrimp and grits at 302 South Street.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Carrollton has several breweries and wineries that offer a range of locally produced beers and wines. The Printer’s Ale Manufacturing Co. and Snake Creek Distillery are two popular destinations.

Where to Stay in Carrollton (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carrollton has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and style. Some of the best hotels in the city include Courtyard by Marriott Carrollton, Hampton Inn Carrollton, and Holiday Inn Express & Suites Carrollton West.

What to Buy in Carrollton (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Carrollton has several gift shops and boutiques that sell handmade crafts, artworks, and local food products. Some of the best places to shop include The Scarlet Poppy, Cambridge on the Square, and The Nesting Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Carrollton (Georgia)

Carrollton is known for its Southern hospitality, which means that visitors can expect warm and friendly interactions with the locals. Respect for tradition and family values is also an important aspect of the community, reflected in the city’s many cultural events and celebrations, such as the annual Taste of Carrollton festival and the Carrollton Candlelight Tour of Homes.

Best Time to Visit Carrollton (Georgia)

The best time to visit Carrollton is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the city is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carrollton (Georgia)

Do:

  • Respect the city’s cultural heritage.

  • Book accommodation and activities in advance.

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural attractions.

  • Disrespect the locals or their traditions.

  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carrollton (Georgia)

Carrollton is generally a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions to avoid theft and stay safe. It’s also important to follow traffic rules and be cautious while hiking or camping in parks.

Health in Carrollton (Georgia)

Carrollton has several healthcare facilities, including the Tanner Health System, which offers a range of medical services, from emergency care to rehabilitation. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Carrollton (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carrollton (Georgia)

The closest airport to Carrollton is the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is about 45 miles away. From there, visitors can take a shuttle, taxi, or rent a car to reach Carrollton.

Bus Stations Near Carrollton (Georgia)

Greyhound Bus Lines and Megabus have several stops in Atlanta, which is about 45 miles from Carrollton.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Carrollton by car, as the city is conveniently located near major highways, including Interstate 20 and U.S. Route 27.

How to Get Around in Carrollton (Georgia)

Carrollton is a relatively small city, and most of the attractions are within walking distance or a short drive away. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a taxi to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Carrollton (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Carrollton include:

  • Atlanta, which is about 45 miles away and offers a range of cultural, shopping, and entertainment options.

  • Chattanooga, which is about 100 miles away and offers stunning natural attractions, such as Lookout Mountain and Ruby Falls.

  • Birmingham, which is about 120 miles away and offers a rich history, beautiful parks, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Carrollton is a hidden gem in West High Country, offering a mix of history, culture, natural beauty, and Southern hospitality. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, savor traditional cuisine and beverages, and explore the city’s many museums and historic sites. With its friendly locals and relaxed atmosphere, Carrollton is a perfect destination for a peaceful and memorable vacation.
