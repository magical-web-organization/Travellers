Introduction to Alpharetta

Located in the Metro Atlanta area of Georgia, Alpharetta is a city known for its rich history, diverse culture, and natural beauty. Founded in the 1830s, the city has evolved into a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a wide range of attractions, activities, and events.

List of Places to Visit in Alpharetta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alpharetta and Old Milton County History Museum: This museum showcases the history of Alpharetta and surrounding areas, featuring exhibits on Native American settlements, Civil War battles, and early 20th-century life.
  • Barrington Hall: Built in the 1830s, this historic home offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of wealthy plantation owners during the antebellum period.
  • Autrey Mill Nature Preserve and Heritage Center: This 46-acre nature preserve boasts walking trails, animal exhibits, and a historic village with restored buildings dating back to the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Alpharetta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Creek Greenway: This 8.2-mile paved trail offers visitors a peaceful escape into nature, with scenic views of the surrounding wetlands and woodlands.
  • Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area: Located just a short drive from Alpharetta, this park offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including fishing, hiking, and kayaking.
  • Avalon: This mixed-use development is a popular shopping and dining destination, featuring a scenic park and an outdoor ice skating rink during the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alpharetta

  • Big Creek Greenway: As mentioned above, the Big Creek Greenway offers a picturesque and peaceful path for walkers, joggers, and bikers alike.
  • Rock Mill Park Trail: This moderate 1.4-mile out and back trail is perfect for hikers of all skill levels, featuring beautiful views of the surrounding woods and a creek.

Best Things to Do in Alpharetta: Activities and Tours

  • North Point Mall: This premier shopping destination boasts a wide range of stores, restaurants, and entertainment options, including a movie theater and indoor amusement park.
  • Topgolf: Golf enthusiasts and beginners alike can enjoy this high-tech driving range, featuring climate-controlled hitting bays and a full bar and restaurant.
  • Live Music at Ameris Bank Amphitheater: This outdoor music venue hosts a variety of musicians and bands, with concerts ranging from country and rock to pop and hip hop.

Food and Drink Scene of Alpharetta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken at South Main Kitchen: This restaurant is known for its mouthwatering fried chicken, brined for flavor and fried for crispiness.
  • Seafood at The El Felix: This Mexican restaurant offers a wide range of seafood dishes, including shrimp ceviche and lobster tacos.
  • Pizza at Coal-Fired Pizza: This pizza joint serves up delicious pies baked in a coal-fired oven, with toppings like prosciutto and arugula and spicy soppressata.

Local Beverages

  • Beer at Jekyll Brewing: This brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their flagship Hop Dang Diggity IPA and seasonal brews like Figgy Pudding Ale.
  • Wine at Chateau Elan Winery: Take a short drive outside of Alpharetta to Braselton, GA and enjoy wine tastings at this award-winning winery’s picturesque vineyard.
  • Coffee at Land of a Thousand Hills: This coffee shop is dedicated to ethical sourcing and provides coffee drinkers with the chance to learn about the farmers who provide their beans.

Where to Stay in Alpharetta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel at Avalon: Located in the heart of the Avalon development, this hotel offers guests luxury accommodations and easy access to shopping and dining.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Alpharetta: This hotel offers convenient access to Alpharetta’s many attractions and features comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Friendly Hostel: This budget-friendly option is great for travelers looking for a cozy and social atmosphere, with shared rooms and common areas for meeting other visitors.

What to Buy in Alpharetta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery at Alpha Fired Arts: This pottery studio offers visitors the chance to create unique pieces using the studio’s glazes and firing services.
  • Honey at Humble Hive Farms: This local farm sells natural, raw honey harvested from their own hives, as well as honey-based products like lip balm and soap.
  • Artwork at Anne Irwin Fine Art Gallery: This art gallery features a wide range of art styles and mediums, showcasing the work of both local and nationally recognized artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Alpharetta

Alpharetta prides itself on its welcoming and hospitable culture, with locals eager to share their heritage and traditions with visitors. Georgian culture is heavily influenced by its history, with elements of Native American, African American, and European traditions mixing together to create a unique blend.

Best Time to Visit Alpharetta

The best time to visit Alpharetta is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and comfortable, and the city’s many outdoor attractions are at their most beautiful. Summer can be quite hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy, limiting some of the outdoor activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alpharetta

Do:

  • Do plan to spend plenty of time outdoors, exploring the city’s many nature preserves and walking trails.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including some of the city’s unique fusion dishes that mix southern soul food with international flavors.
  • Do get out and experience Alpharetta’s vibrant arts and culture scene, with music venues and art galleries showcasing the work of local and nationally recognized artists.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent, especially during the summer months when the weather can be quite hot and humid.
  • Don’t miss the chance to learn about Alpharetta’s rich history and cultural heritage, which has played a large role in shaping the city into what it is today.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with the friendly locals, who are always happy to share their city with visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alpharetta

Alpharetta is generally a safe city to visit, with low levels of crime and a strong police presence in the area. Visitors should take standard precautions when traveling, such as keeping their belongings secure and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Alpharetta

Alpharetta has many excellent medical facilities, including Northside Hospital, which is ranked as one of the top hospitals in the state. Visitors should have no trouble finding quality medical care in the area.

Getting to Alpharetta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alpharetta

  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport: This is the closest major airport to Alpharetta, located about 40 minutes south of the city.

Bus Stations Near Alpharetta

  • MARTA North Springs Station: This station is located in Sandy Springs, about 15 minutes south of Alpharetta, and provides access to MARTA’s train and bus services.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Alpharetta and explore some of the surrounding areas, such as the Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area.
  • Taxi / Rideshare: Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are also available in the area, providing a convenient and affordable way to get around town.

How to Get Around in Alpharetta

  • Walk / Bike: Alpharetta is a pedestrian-friendly city, with many attractions and amenities located within walking or biking distance of each other.
  • Public Transit: MARTA provides bus and train services in the area, with several stops located throughout Alpharetta.
  • Car: Rental cars are widely available in the city, and parking is generally easy to find.

Where to Go Next from Alpharetta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Atlanta: Just a short drive south of Alpharetta, Atlanta is a world-class city known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and diverse cuisine.
  • Stone Mountain: Located about an hour east of Alpharetta, Stone Mountain offers visitors a chance to hike, bike, and explore one of Georgia’s most beautiful natural landmarks.
  • Macon: This charming southern town is located about 2 hours south of Alpharetta, boasting a rich history, beautiful architecture, and delicious southern cuisine.

Conclusion

With its rich culture, vibrant arts scene, and beautiful natural attractions, Alpharetta is a must-see destination for any traveler visiting the Metro Atlanta area. Whether you’re looking to explore historic museums and landmarks, hike through beautiful forests and nature reserves, or simply enjoy some delicious southern cuisine, Alpharetta has something for everyone to enjoy.
