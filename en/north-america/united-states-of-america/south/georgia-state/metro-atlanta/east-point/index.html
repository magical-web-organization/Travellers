Introduction to East Point

Welcome to East Point, a vibrant city located in the southern suburbs of Atlanta, Georgia. East Point boasts an impressive history with a well-preserved downtown area, offering a mix of modern amenities and historical landmarks. If you’re looking to experience Southern hospitality, delicious cuisine, and a wide range of activities, then come and explore East Point.

List of Places to Visit in East Point

East Point offers a range of attractions, from museums to natural parks, here are a few must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Delta Flight Museum - Learn about the history of Delta Airlines and its significant contribution to the aviation industry.
  • Civil War Trail Markers - Follow the trail markers to discover historical sites related to the Civil War era.
  • East Point Historical Society - Explore the rich history and culture of East Point through interactive exhibits and collections.

Natural Attractions of East Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sykes Park - A perfect place for a picnic or a stroll with a beautiful lake.
  • Connally Nature Park - A nature park with trails that have wonderful views of the city skyline.
  • Lions Park - A beautiful park with a playground for the kids and plenty of open space for picnics and games.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Point

For the adventure enthusiast, East Point has various hiking and biking trails with stunning views. Here are a few popular routes:

  • East Point PATH Trail - This biking and jogging trail offers scenic views and is suitable for all fitness levels.
  • Atlanta Beltline Trail - A 22-mile trail that stretches around Atlanta and goes through East Point offering excellent views of the city.

Best Things to Do in East Point: Activities and Tours

There’s always something to do in East Point, and whether you’re looking for a relaxing experience or an adventure, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in the city.

  • Take a Food Tour - East Point is known for its delicious Southern cuisine, so be sure to take a food tour.
  • Visit the Delta Flight Museum - This museum offers an exciting journey through the history of Delta Airlines.
  • Go on a Walking Tour - Join a walking tour to explore the rich history and culture of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Point

Here is an itinerary to make the most of your one-week trip to East Point:

Day 1 - Visit the Atlanta Botanical Garden, one of the most beautiful gardens in the city.

Day 2 - Take a tour of Delta Airlines’ headquarters, and then head to the College Football Hall of Fame.

Day 3 - Spend the day hiking in the Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area, an outdoor paradise set in the beautiful North Georgia mountains.

Day 4 - Explore the East Point Greenway Trail before stopping at the MET Complex to enjoy a performance.

Day 5 - Take a food tour of the city, exploring the various cuisines that the town has to offer.

Day 6 - Visit the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site in downtown Atlanta.

Day 7 - Walk around lovely Downtown East Point and visit local museums and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of East Point

East Point’s cuisine is a fusion of Southern, soul, and contemporary flavors, with an array of delicious dishes to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southern Fried Chicken - A popular dish loved by locals and visitors alike.
  • Collard Greens - These traditional greens are slow-cooked with smoked ham until tender and served with hot sauce.
  • Peach Cobbler - A traditional Southern dessert made with juicy, ripe peaches, and topped with flaky pastry.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A Southern classic made with black tea and plenty of sugar.
  • Georgia Peach Sangria - This refreshing drink is made with peaches, white wine, and a variety of citrus flavors.

Where to Stay in East Point: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Point has a range of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget. Here are our top picks:

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Atlanta Airport - This hotel offers comfortable, stylish rooms and is conveniently located close to the airport.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Atlanta - This all-suite hotel offers a range of amenities, including a fitness center and a pool.
  • The Westin Peachtree Plaza - This luxury downtown hotel boasts spectacular views and top-class facilities.

What to Buy in East Point: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East Point has a range of local shops that sell unique products and souvenirs to bring back with you.

  • Atlanta t-shirts - A perfect souvenir to remind you of your trip to East Point and Atlanta.
  • Peach Jam - This local jam is a perfect gift or souvenir made from the juiciest Georgia peaches.

Local Culture and Customs of East Point

East Point is known for its friendly locals who are always happy to welcome visitors. Southern hospitality is a way of life. Please respect the culture and customs of the area by being polite and friendly to residents and fellow visitors.

Best Time to Visit East Point

The best time to visit East Point is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild, and the city is not too crowded. The summer months can be very hot and humid, and winters can be unpredictable with occasional snow and sleet.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Point

Do:

  • Take a food tour to experience local cuisine.
  • Visit the Delta Flight Museum.
  • Be polite and respectful to locals and other visitors.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local property.
  • Be disrespectful or rude to locals or other visitors.
  • Leave valuables in your car.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Point

East Point is generally safe, but visitors should take necessary precautions, such as being aware of pickpocketing and keeping an eye on personal belongings.

Health in East Point

Medical facilities are available in East Point and the surrounding areas. Visitors are advised to have health insurance and necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to East Point: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Point is easily accessible by air, road, and rail.

Airports Near East Point

Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is the closest airport to East Point and is just a few minutes’ drive away.

Bus Stations Near East Point

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown Atlanta and is easily accessible by taxi or public transport.

Other Options

Visitors to East Point can also consider renting a car or taking a taxi to get around the city.

How to Get Around in East Point

East Point has a network of public transportation options, including buses and trains.

MARTA - Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority

MARTA offers a range of transportation options, including buses and trains, running seven days a week, and covering the East Point and Atlanta region.

Where to Go Next from East Point: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East Point is surrounded by other fantastic travel destinations, including:

  • Atlanta - The biggest city near East Point offers a range of attractions, tourist spots, and landmarks.
  • Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area - Visit the beautiful park for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Piedmont Park - A beautiful park located in downtown Atlanta, with lakes and walking trails.

Conclusion

East Point is a city full of history, culture, and adventure, that offers a memorable experience for travelers from all over the world. With its excellent transportation, top-class amenities, and welcoming locals, East Point is a perfect year-round destination for everyone. Plan your visit today, and get ready for an unforgettable experience in East Point, Atlanta.
