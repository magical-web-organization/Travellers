Introduction to Lawrenceville

Located in Metro Atlanta, Lawrenceville, Georgia is a charming town with a rich history and a thriving modern community.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lawrenceville is steeped in history, and its museums and historical places allow visitors to explore this rich past. The Gwinnett History Museum displays artifacts and exhibits that highlight the region’s history, while the Gwinnett Veterans War Memorial Museum honors the sacrifices of local veterans.

Natural Attractions of Lawrenceville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tribble Mill Park and Little Mulberry Park are two must-visit destinations for nature enthusiasts in Lawrenceville. Tribble Mill Park offers hiking, fishing, and boating along its scenic lakes, while Little Mulberry Park offers a variety of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lawrenceville

Lawrenceville’s scenic parks provide ample opportunity for hiking and biking. The 3.5-mile Ivy Creek Greenway is a paved trail that winds through the city and connects with other local trails.

Best Things to Do in Lawrenceville: Activities and Tours

  • Aurora Theatre
  • Gwinnett Braves Stadium

Aurora Theatre

The Aurora Theatre is a performing arts venue that offers a range of shows and events, including musicals, plays, and improv comedy.

Gwinnett Braves Stadium

Sports fans won’t want to miss the chance to catch a game at the Gwinnett Braves Stadium. The stadium hosts minor league baseball games in a friendly and family-oriented atmosphere.
