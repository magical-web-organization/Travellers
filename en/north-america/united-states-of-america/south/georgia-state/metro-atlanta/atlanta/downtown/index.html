Introduction to Downtown

Downtown Atlanta is the heart of the city where the old meets the new. It’s the perfect place to experience the vibrant culture and rich history of the city while also enjoying modern amenities. From world-class museums and galleries to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Downtown Atlanta has something for everyone. This travel guide will help you discover the best places to visit, eat, stay and things to do in Downtown Atlanta.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Center for Civil and Human Rights The Center for Civil and Human Rights is a museum dedicated to exploring the history of civil rights in America and the struggle for social justice. It features exhibits on the American Civil Rights Movement as well as other contemporary struggles for human rights around the world.

Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park The Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park is a tribute to the life and legacy of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. It includes the home where he was born, the church where he preached, and the memorial where he is buried.

Georgia State Capitol The Georgia State Capitol is a stunning building that houses the offices of the Governor and the state legislature. Visitors can take a tour of the building and learn about the history of the state and its government.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Piedmont Park Piedmont Park is a beautiful urban green space located just north of Downtown Atlanta. It’s the perfect place to enjoy outdoor activities such as jogging, biking, or picnicking.

Atlanta Beltline The Atlanta Beltline is a former railway corridor that has been transformed into a 22-mile long trail for biking, running, and walking. It provides stunning views of the city and connects to several parks and neighborhoods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

Atlanta Beltline Eastside Trail The Atlanta Beltline Eastside Trail is a 3-mile long trail that runs from Piedmont Park to the Reynoldstown neighborhood. It’s perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk and offers stunning views of the city.

Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area The Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area is located just a short drive from Downtown Atlanta. It covers over 15 miles of the Chattahoochee River and has several hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

Georgia Aquarium The Georgia Aquarium is one of the largest aquariums in the world and is home to over 120,000 animals. It’s the perfect place to go with your family and kids and learn about aquatic animals from around the world.

World of Coca-Cola The World of Coca-Cola is a museum dedicated to the history and culture of Coca-Cola. It features exhibits on the history of the company and the iconic brand and includes a tasting room where visitors can sample different Coca-Cola products from around the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Chicken Atlanta is known for its delicious southern-style fried chicken. A few places worth trying are Busy Bee Cafe, Gus’s World Famous Fried Chicken, and JCT Kitchen & Bar.

Peach Cobbler Peaches are the state fruit of Georgia, and peach cobbler is a must-try when in Downtown. Some great places to try it are Mary Mac’s Tea Room and The Real Milk and Honey.

Local Beverages

Sweet Tea Sweet tea is a refreshing beverage that’s popular in the south. You can find it at most restaurants and cafes in Downtown.

Coca-Cola Coca-Cola was created in Atlanta and is still headquartered there. You can try different Coca-Cola products from around the world at the World of Coca-Cola.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Ritz-Carlton, Atlanta The Ritz-Carlton, Atlanta is a five-star hotel located in the heart of the city. It has luxurious amenities such as a spa, fitness center, and fine dining restaurant.

The Ellis Hotel The Ellis Hotel is a chic and stylish hotel located in a historic building. It offers modern amenities such as a rooftop lounge, fitness center, and complimentary breakfast as well.

HI Atlanta Hostel HI Atlanta Hostel is a budget-friendly option located in the heart of Downtown. It offers both private and shared rooms along with free Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, and a communal kitchen and living room areas.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peach Products Peaches are Georgia’s state fruit, and you can find peach jams, preserves, and even scented candles at Sweet Auburn Curb Market and other local shops.

Georgia Peanuts Georgia peanuts are known for their sweet and buttery flavor. You can find them at the Peanut Depot located in Downtown.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Atlanta is a very diverse city, and that is reflected in its culture. The city is known for its rich history in civil rights and social justice. It’s also known for its music scene, particularly in Hip-Hop. It’s important to be respectful of different cultures and enjoy the unique experiences that the city has to offer.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown is in the fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the trees turn beautiful shades of red and orange. Spring (March to May) is also a great time to visit when the weather is pleasant, and flowers are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Visit historical landmarks such as the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site and the Center for Civil and Human Rights to learn about the city’s rich history.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially southern-style fried chicken and peach cobbler.
  • Wander around the Atlanta Beltline and appreciate the stunning views of the city.

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone in Atlanta has a southern accent.
  • Joke about the city’s traffic problems as it’s a sensitive issue to locals.
  • Visit unsafe areas of the city late at night by yourself.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Atlanta is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid going out alone at night in some areas. Also, it’s important to watch out for pickpockets and keep valuables safely secured.

Health in Downtown

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in Downtown Atlanta, including Grady Memorial Hospital and Emory University Hospital Midtown.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is the primary airport serving Atlanta, and it’s located only 10 miles south of Downtown. Visitors can take a taxi, Uber/Lyft, or MARTA (Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority) train to reach Downtown.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

The Greyhound bus station is located in Downtown, making it easy to arrive or depart from the city by bus.

Other Options

Visitors can also take Amtrak trains that arrive and depart from the Atlanta Peachtree Station.

How to Get Around in Downtown

MARTA

The Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority (MARTA) is the easiest and most affordable way to get around Downtown Atlanta. It has a rail and bus system, and visitors can purchase a Breeze card to travel around the city.

Uber/Lyft

Uber and Lyft are both available in Downtown Atlanta, and they offer a convenient way to get around the city in comfort.

Walking/Biking

Downtown Atlanta is a walkable and bike-friendly city with several dedicated trails and bike lanes.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stone Mountain Park Stone Mountain Park is located only 30 minutes from Downtown Atlanta and features a 1,686-foot-tall mountain with a carving of three Confederate heroes, a hiking trail, and bike trails.

Six Flags Over Georgia Six Flags Over Georgia is located just west of Downtown Atlanta and features over 40 rides and attractions.

Conclusion

Downtown Atlanta is a vibrant and exciting city that has something for everyone. From its rich history and culture to outdoor adventures and stunning views, this travel guide has covered everything you need to know to plan a trip to Downtown Atlanta.
