Introduction to Atlanta

Located in the heart of Georgia, Atlanta is the capital of the south that has become a hub for business, entertainment, and culture. It’s a diverse city with something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor adventurer, Atlanta has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Atlanta

Visitors to Atlanta will appreciate the wealth of cultural and natural attractions, and delicious food and drink scene. Here are some of the top places to visit in Atlanta:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • (1) Atlanta History Center: This complex includes a museum, historic homes, gardens, and trails that showcase Atlanta’s history and culture.
  • (2) Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park: A tribute to the civil rights movement, this park includes MLK’s childhood home and a historic church.
  • (3) High Museum of Art: Known for its contemporary and classic artwork, the High Museum of Art is a must-see place in Atlanta.

Natural Attractions of Atlanta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • (1) Piedmont Park: This large urban park offers a beautiful oasis of greenery, with walking trails beautiful views of the city skyline.
  • (2) Atlanta Botanical Garden: The Atlanta Botanical Garden is famous for its colorful flowers and is a popular spot for relaxing and taking in beautiful views.
  • (3) Stone Mountain Park: Named after the massive stone mountain carving, this park is a favorite for hiking, rock climbing, and watching the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Atlanta

Atlanta is also famous for its hiking/biking routes. Here are some of the best routes:

  • (1) BeltLine Trail: This 22-mile trail offers a picturesque urban trek that passes by eclectic neighborhoods, restaurants, and street art.
  • (2) Chattahoochee Nature Center: This trail offers a relaxing nature walk alongside the Chattahoochee River, where visitors can watch birds and explore animal habitats.
  • (3) Arabia Mountain Trail: This trail is more industrial in nature, with granite quarries and abandoned railroad tracks, which provide for a unique and interesting hike.

Best Things to Do in Atlanta: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking/biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Atlanta. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • (1) Georgia Aquarium: Known for its unique underwater experience, guests can see a variety of marine life from dolphins to sharks.
  • (2) World of Coca-Cola: The birthplace of Coke, this museum offers an interactive experience dedicated to the history and culture of this famous fast drink.
  • (3) Fox Theatre: Known for its beauty and grandeur, the Fox Theatre hosts a wide variety of musical acts, such as Broadway shows and orchestral performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Atlanta

No tour guide for Atlanta can be complete without mentioning the vibrant food and drink scene of Atlanta. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Fried Chicken: A southern staple, fried chicken is a must-try when in Atlanta. It’s soft, juicy, and flavored to perfection!
  • (2) Pulled Pork Sandwiches: Slow and low barbecue is a way of life in the south. Try the pulled pork sandwiches at Fox Bros. BBQ for a real treat!
  • (3) Peach Cobbler: Georgia is famous for its sweet peaches, so don’t forget to try the peach cobbler at Mary Mac’s Tea Room, which serves delicious southern comfort food.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Coke: The World of Coca-Cola is a testament to how much coke means to Atlanta, its history, and its people.
  • (2) Sweet Tea: A crisp, cold refreshing drink, sweet tea is everywhere in the south. Go to Arnold’s for a sweet tea that packs a punch!

Where to Stay in Atlanta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Atlanta has a wide range of accommodation options for every budget, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Indigo: A chic and centrally located hotel in downtown Atlanta.
  • Ponce City Market: A renovated historic building that is now a retail, dining, and residential space that offers a unique and cozy living experience.
  • The Biltmore: A historic grand hotel which has been renovated to include modern amenities and luxury facilities.

What to Buy in Atlanta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Atlanta is well-known for its souvenirs and local products, including:

  • (1) Sweet Georgia Peaches: As mentioned earlier, don’t forget to take home some sweet Georgia peaches and enjoy them long after your visit!
  • (2) Local Craft Beer: Atlanta has a booming craft beer scene, with breweries like Hop City and SweetWater Brewing Company, offering unique and distinct flavors.
  • (3) Hot Sauce: A southern staple, pick up some hot sauce from one of Atlanta’s famous barbecue joints like Daddy D’z BBQ Joynt.

Local Culture and Customs of Atlanta

Atlanta is known for its deep-rooted southern hospitality, which means friendliness, politeness, and good manners. It’s an inclusive city that is accepting of everyone, regardless of race, color, or religion.

Best Time to Visit Atlanta

The best time to visit Atlanta is during the spring and fall, between March to May and September to November. The weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds than during the peak summer and winter seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Atlanta

Do:

  • (1) Do try the fried chicken in Atlanta. It is heavenly!
  • (2) Do explore local neighborhoods like Little Five Points and Old Fourth Ward.
  • (3) Do try the coffee at Octane Coffee Bar and Lounge.

Don’t:

  • (1) Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking/biking.
  • (2) Don’t skip out on the local food scene, it’s some of the best in the country!
  • (3) Don’t miss out on the Martin Luther King Jr. National Park, it’s a cornerstone of Atlanta’s history.

Safety and Health

Safety in Atlanta

Like any big city, Atlanta has its share of crime; however, it’s generally safe for tourists. Visitors should exercise caution while traveling alone or at night in certain areas. Always keep your valuables locked away securely and watch out for pickpockets.

Health in Atlanta

Atlanta is home to many first-rate hospitals and medical facilities, with Emory University as one of the top-ranked medical centers in the country. Visitors should ensure they have health insurance coverage before traveling to Atlanta.

Getting to Atlanta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Atlanta has multiple transportation options, including air travel, buses, and local transportation.

Airports Near Atlanta

  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL)

Bus Stations Near Atlanta

  • Greyhound Station
  • MARTA Station

Other Options

  • Rental Cars
  • Taxis

How to Get Around in Atlanta

Atlanta is not known for its public transport system, but MARTA, Atlanta’s public transport system, offers affordable and convenient options to move about the city. Visitors can also use taxis, rental cars, or ride-share services.

Where to Go Next from Atlanta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Atlanta is located near many beautiful and exciting destinations that are perfect for a day trip or weekend getaway:

  • (1) Chattanooga, Tennessee
  • (2) Savannah, Georgia
  • (3) Asheville, North Carolina

Conclusion

In conclusion, Atlanta is a beautiful, vibrant city that offers something for everyone. Whether visitors want to explore the city’s rich culture, hike in one of its beautiful trails or taste its delicious food, Atlanta is a city that will capture the hearts of all who visit.
