A Comprehensive Guide to Exploring Buckhead in Atlanta, Georgia

Located in the northern part of the city, Buckhead is arguably Atlanta’s most upscale neighborhood. It boasts a rich cultural heritage and a thriving commercial district that make it a top tourist attraction in Georgia. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll feature everything you need to know to have the best experience when exploring Buckhead.

List of Places to Visit in Buckhead

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Atlanta History Center - Explore the exhibits and indoor exhibitions at the Atlanta History Center, which boasts an extensive collection of Civil War artifacts, photography and paintings, and more.

Swan House - Take a tour of this iconic mansion to learn about the early 20th-century culture and lifestyle, innovation, and legacy of the most prominent Atlantans. You can walk around the beautiful gardens surrounding this grand house.

Governor’s Mansion - Catch a glimpse of architecture and Southern charm in Georgia’s Governor’s Mansion. Built-in 1968, it is a grand palace on 18 acres of land that hosts various events.

Natural Attractions of Buckhead: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Atlanta Botanical Garden - Discover the stunning collections of plants and wildflowers here with different themes and seasonal displays. Attend the annual light show during Christmas.

Chattahoochee River National Recreation Center - Go kayaking, rafting, hiking, and fishing while exploring the beautiful Chattahoochee River with stunning natural views.

Stone Mountain Park - A must-visit attraction when in Buckhead, Stone Mountain Park has the world’s largest carving on its granite walls of President’s Davis, Lincoln, and Lee. Enjoy the scenic railway, cable car, and nature trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buckhead

Chattahoochee River Trail - With a 1.5-mile trail, admire the natural beauty of this river, go hiking or walking.

Whitewater Creek Trail - Explore the hidden trails at this stunning park while enjoying strolls or bike rides.

Best Things to Do in Buckhead: Activities and Tours

Loring Heights Park - This park offers a fantastic vista of the Atlanta skyline, where you can have a picnic, jog, or take selfies with friends and family.

Atlanta History Center Swan Coach House - Attend workshops and events to learn Georgian culture, arts, and culinary experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Buckhead

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Chicken - Try the traditional Southern-style fried chicken at Mary Mac’s Tea Room.

Pimento Cheese - Taste the creamy pimento with mayonnaise and cheddar at South City Kitchen.

Local Beverages

Sweet Tea - Georgia is famous for its sweet tea, which is sweetened using sugar along with lemon or peach.

Mint Julep - An all-time favorite with bourbon, sugar syrup, water, and muddled mint leaves.

Where to Stay in Buckhead: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Whitley, a Luxury Collection Hotel - A luxurious hotel with a five-star rating, located on Peachtree Road.

The Ritz Carlton Buckhead - A popular choice with safe and spacious rooms, spa, and beautiful views.

The Westin Buckhead Atlanta - This hotel has a central location, spacious rooms, and a rooftop pool.

What to Buy in Buckhead: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Georgia Peach Products - Get peach jams, hot sauce, preserves, and other savory items at the Peachtree Road Farmers Market.

Tin Can Trading Post - The perfect place to buy souvenirs, gifts, pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Buckhead

Buckhead has a rich southern culture and it is polite to greet with a smile and a gracious attitude. It is customary to say “Yes Ma’am/Sir” and “No Ma’am/Sir” when addressing elders and your superiors.

Best Time to Visit Buckhead

The best time to explore Buckhead is from March to May and from September to November. You’ll enjoy the mild, pleasant weather in spring and fall. Expect the temperatures to be warm and humid during the summer months, while winters are cool with occasional precipitation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buckhead

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and wear comfortable footwear, especially when visiting museums and historical places.
  • Practice good manners and be polite when interacting with locals.
  • Try Southern cuisine that is unique to Buckhead.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public spaces.
  • Don’t be loud or obnoxious in public and disturb others.
  • Don’t forget to tip the staff when dining or using hospitality services.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buckhead

Buckhead is generally a safe neighborhood, but it is always advisable to be vigilant and follow safety rules. Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas or unfamiliar places, and don’t leave valuable items unattended in your car.

Health in Buckhead

The most important thing is to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when exposed to the sun for long hours. Keep a stock of your prescription medicine and follow all COVID-19 regulations.

Getting to Buckhead: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buckhead

Hartsfield–Jackson Atlanta International Airport - The busiest airport with a distance of about 20 miles.

DeKalb Peachtree Airport - Located just a few miles north of Buckhead.

Bus Stations Near Buckhead

Megabus - Buses have stops at 14th St & Peachtree St, and 4th St & Peachtree St.

Other Options

MARTA - A train system that operates throughout Atlanta, including Buckhead.

Taxi and Ride Share - Popular ride-sharing services like Lyft and Uber are also available.

How to Get Around in Buckhead

It is easy to walk around Buckhead’s urban core, but car rentals, ride-sharing services, and cabs are also readily available. Marta offers train and bus services that are convenient to navigate around the city.

Where to Go Next from Buckhead: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Piedmont Park - Located in Midtown, Piedmont Park is a beautiful green space with picnic areas, walking trails, off-leash dog parks, and a farmers’ market.

World of Coca-Cola - This museum is dedicated to the history of Coca-Cola, and you can sample beverages from around the globe.

Krog Street Market - A popular food market that features a diverse range of eateries, boutiques, and a farmer’s market selling clothing, artwork, and food.

Conclusion

With its vibrant history, stunning landscapes, and rich culture, Buckhead is a top destination in Georgia that highlights Atlanta’s cultural and commercial side in full glory. Follow this guide to ensure a memorable and informative visit to Buckhead.
