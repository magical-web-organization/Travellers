Introduction to Decatur

Nestled in the heart of Metro Atlanta, Decatur, Georgia, offers visitors a charming small-town feel with all the amenities of a big city. Known for its vibrant culture, incredible food, and rich history, Decatur has something for every type of traveler.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit in Decatur, including its culturally significant museums and historical sites, outdoor adventures and scenic views, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, week-long itinerary, food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, local customs and culture, safety and health tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Decatur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Decatur is home to several museums and historical sites that highlight the city’s rich cultural heritage. These include:

  • DeKalb History Center: This museum showcases DeKalb County’s history, architecture, and culture through permanent and rotating exhibits.

  • Waffle House Museum: Located in a restored 1955 diner, this museum celebrates the history of the Waffle House, a beloved Southern restaurant chain.

  • Michael C. Carlos Museum: This museum, located in nearby Emory University, houses an impressive collection of art and artifacts from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Decatur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Decatur is surrounded by natural beauty, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions in and around Decatur:

  • Stone Mountain Park: This massive park is located just a short drive from Decatur and offers hiking, biking, and scenic views of the famous Stone Mountain.

  • Arabia Mountain: Located near Stone Mountain, this unique natural landmark offers hiking trails, scenic views, and wildlife viewing.

  • Fernbank Forest: This old-growth forest is located in the heart of the city and offers visitors an opportunity to experience Georgia’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Decatur

Decatur offers several hiking and biking trails that showcase the area’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best:

  • East Decatur Greenway: This paved trail winds through scenic neighborhoods and connects to downtown Decatur.

  • South Peachtree Creek Trail: This trail follows South Peachtree Creek and offers scenic views of the surrounding forest.

  • Lullwater Park Trail: Located in nearby Emory University, this hiking trail winds through lush forest and offers views of Candler Lake.

Best Things to Do in Decatur: Activities and Tours

Decatur offers a variety of activities and tours for travelers of all ages and interests:

  • Decatur Ghost Tour: This tour takes visitors through the city’s most haunted locations, complete with spooky stories and historical facts.

  • Fernbank Museum of Natural History: This museum showcases natural history exhibits, including fossils, dinosaur skeletons, and live animals.

  • Color Wheel Studio: Visitors can unleash their inner artist with a painting class at Color Wheel Studio, located in downtown Decatur.

Week-Long Itinerary for Decatur

Day 1: Visit the DeKalb History Center and explore the city’s historic downtown.

Day 2: Take a hike at Arabia Mountain and visit the Waffle House Museum.

Day 3: Explore Emory University’s Michael C. Carlos Museum and Lullwater Park Trail.

Day 4: Take a bike ride on the East Decatur Greenway and visit the Decatur Farmers Market.

Day 5: Visit Fernbank Museum of Natural History and hike through Fernbank Forest.

Day 6: Take a ghost tour of Decatur and try one of the city’s famous restaurants.

Day 7: Spend the day at Stone Mountain Park and enjoy hiking, picnicking, and scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Decatur

Decatur is known for its incredible food and drink scene, which combines Southern staples with international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and grits: This classic Southern dish can be found on nearly every Decatur menu and is a must-try for visitors.

  • Barbecue: Decatur is known for its barbecue, with several restaurants offering mouth-watering ribs, pulled pork, and brisket.

  • Farm-to-table cuisine: Decatur’s farmers market and locally sourced restaurants offer fresh, high-quality ingredients that can be enjoyed in dishes like house-made pasta and roasted vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: No Southern meal is complete without a glass of sweet tea, and Decatur serves up some of the best.

  • Local craft beer: Decatur is home to several craft breweries, including Three Taverns Brewery and Wild Heaven Beer.

  • Coffee: Decatur’s coffee scene is thriving, with Java Monkey and Kudzu Coffee among the top local spots.

Where to Stay in Decatur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Decatur offers a variety of accommodations to fit every budget and travel style. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, there’s something for everyone:

  • The Courtyard by Marriott Atlanta Decatur Downtown/Emory: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Decatur and offers modern rooms and amenities.

  • The University Inn at Emory: Located near Emory University, this guesthouse is cozy and quaint, with rooms that feel like home.

  • The Decatur Hostel: This affordable hostel offers cozy dorm-style rooms and a friendly, communal vibe.

What to Buy in Decatur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Decatur offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts for loved ones back home. Here are some top picks:

  • Local honey: Decatur’s farmers market offers a variety of local honeys from nearby beekeepers.

  • Artisanal soap: Visitors can find unique, handcrafted soaps at local shops like Blue Moon and Honey.

  • Decatur T-shirts and apparel: Several local shops, including Squash Blossom Boutique and Worthmore Jewelers, offer Decatur-themed apparel and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Decatur

Decatur residents are known for their Southern hospitality, and visitors will experience warm welcomes everywhere they go. Here are a few local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Smiling and greeting: Decatur residents are typically friendly and welcoming, so don’t be shy about greeting others with a smile and a hello.

  • Southern cuisine: Decatur’s food scene is a huge part of its culture, so don’t be surprised if locals ask about your favorite restaurants or offer suggestions for where to eat.

  • Community events: Decatur hosts several community events throughout the year, including the Decatur Arts Festival and the Decatur Book Festival, so visitors may want to consult the calendar when planning their trip.

Best Time to Visit Decatur

The best time to visit Decatur is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and wet. However, visitors can find enjoyable activities and events year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Decatur

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine: Decatur is known for its incredible food scene, so be sure to sample some of the city’s best restaurant offerings.

  • Do visit the local museums: Decatur’s museums provide a fascinating look at the city’s past and present, making them a must-visit for history and culture buffs.

  • Do explore the local parks and outdoor areas: Decatur’s natural beauty is not to be missed, with hiking trails, scenic views, and outdoor activities for all ages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip: It’s customary to tip 18-20% at restaurants, cafes, and bars in Decatur.

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind: Decatur prides itself on maintaining a clean and beautiful environment, so be sure to dispose of trash properly.

  • Don’t forget to be respectful of local customs: Visitors should always be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes and social norms.

Safety and Health

Safety in Decatur

Decatur is generally a safe and friendly city, with low levels of crime. However, visitors should still take basic safety precautions such as locking doors and windows, traveling in groups in unfamiliar areas, and not leaving valuables in plain sight in parked cars.

Health in Decatur

Decatur has several hospitals and medical centers that are easily accessible to visitors. However, visitors should still take basic health precautions such as washing hands regularly, staying hydrated, using sunscreen and bug spray, and seeking medical attention if needed.

Getting to Decatur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Decatur is easily accessible by car, bus, and train. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Decatur

  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport: This is the main airport in Atlanta and is located approximately 15 miles south of Decatur.

Bus Stations Near Decatur

  • MARTA: The Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority (MARTA) offers bus service to Decatur and other destinations throughout the city.

Other Options

  • Ride-share services: Visitors can use popular ride-share services like Uber and Lyft to get around Decatur and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Decatur

Decatur is a walkable and bike-friendly city, with several bike lanes and greenways throughout the city. Visitors can also use MARTA buses or ride-share services to get around.

Where to Go Next from Decatur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Decatur is located in the heart of Metro Atlanta, making it a perfect base for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some top picks:

  • Atlanta: Just a short drive or train ride away, Atlanta offers visitors a variety of attractions, including the Georgia Aquarium, World of Coca-Cola, and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site.

  • Chattanooga: This charming Tennessee city is located approximately two hours north of Decatur and offers visitors a variety of activities and attractions, including the Lookout Mountain Incline Railway, the Tennessee Aquarium, and Rock City Gardens.

  • Savannah: This historic Georgia city is located approximately three hours southeast of Decatur and features charming architecture, delicious cuisine, and several historic attractions, including the Savannah Historic District and the Bonaventure Cemetery.

Conclusion

Decatur is a vibrant and charming city that offers visitors a variety of attractions and experiences. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and incredible cuisine, there’s something for every type of traveler in Decatur, Georgia.
