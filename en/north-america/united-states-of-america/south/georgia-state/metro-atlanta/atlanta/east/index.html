Introduction to East

East is a vibrant neighborhood located in Metro Atlanta, Georgia. Known for its lovely parks and fascinating museums, East is an excellent destination for those interested in Southern culture and history.

If you are planning to visit East, it’s a good idea to plan your itinerary in advance. To help you make the most of your trip, we’ve compiled a comprehensive guide with all the information you need.

List of Places to Visit in East

To make the most of your trip to East, make sure to include the following must-visit destinations in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site: Located in the heart of East, this site is dedicated to honoring the life and legacy of Martin Luther King Jr. Learn about the Civil Rights movement and visit King’s birth home and tomb.
  • Atlanta History Center: A fantastic place for history buffs, the Atlanta History Center is home to five museums, including the Kenan Research Center and the Margaret Mitchell House.
  • Carter Center: Founded by former President Jimmy Carter and his wife Rosalynn, the Carter Center is an independent, non-governmental organization focused on advancing human rights and promoting peace. The center features a museum and library dedicated to the Carters’ life and work.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stone Mountain Park: Located just outside of East, Stone Mountain Park offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and golf. Admire the stunning views of the city from the top of Stone Mountain, which is the largest exposed granite dome in North America.
  • Piedmont Park: This 189-acre park is a popular spot for picnics, sports, and outdoor concerts. Take a stroll around the park’s beautiful lake or enjoy the Botanical Garden’s stunning flowers and plants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

East is known for its scenic trails and bike routes that offer some of the city’s best views. Here are some of the area’s best hiking and biking trails:

  • Atlanta Beltline: This 22-mile trail is an excellent place to bike, walk, or run while taking in the city’s skyline views.
  • Freedom Park Trail: This 2.4-mile trail along a former railroad corridor is a perfect place for biking and walking. The path is dotted with art installations and murals that reflect the city’s history and culture.
  • Eastside Trail: Part of the Atlanta Beltline, this trail is 2.25 miles long and runs through several popular neighborhoods. The path features several parks and public art displays.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums, parks, and trails, East also offers a range of activities and tours to help visitors explore the area. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Oakland Cemetery Tour: This historic cemetery is the final resting place of some of Atlanta’s most notable figures, including Margaret Mitchell, Bobby Jones, and Maynard Jackson. Guided tours of the cemetery are available year-round.
  • The High Museum of Art: This museum features a range of art exhibits and installations, from classic paintings to contemporary sculptures. The museum also offers guided tours and educational programs for visitors of all ages.
  • Atlanta Symphony Orchestra: If you’re a music lover, make sure to catch a performance by the Atlanta Symphony Orchestra. The orchestra performs a range of classical and contemporary pieces at the Atlanta Symphony Hall.

Week-Long Itinerary for East

If you’re planning a week-long trip to East, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your days:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site and the Carter Center

7 Take a day trip outside of East to explore other parts of Atlanta

Food and Drink Scene of East

East is home to a range of restaurants and bars that offer delicious Southern cuisine and local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: This Southern staple is a must-try when visiting East. Head to The Busy Bee Cafe for some of the city’s best fried chicken.
  • Peach Cobbler: Made with Georgia’s famed fruit, peach cobbler is a delicious dessert that can be found at many of East’s restaurants.
  • Shrimp and Grits: A classic Southern dish, shrimp and grits can be found at many of the area’s seafood restaurants. Try it at Highland Bakery for a delicious twist on the classic recipe.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern tradition, sweet tea is a refreshing drink that can be found at many of East’s restaurants and cafes.
  • Georgia Beer: There are several craft breweries in Atlanta that offer delicious Georgia-made beer. Check out SweetWater Brewery for a range of tasty options.
  • Coca-Cola: As the birthplace of Coca-Cola, Atlanta has a special connection to the famous drink. Visit the World of Coca-Cola to taste a variety of Coca-Cola drinks from around the world.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East has a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the area’s best places to stay:

  • The Highland Inn: This charming boutique hotel is located in the heart of East and offers comfortable rooms with a vintage vibe.
  • Ponce City Hostel: If you’re looking for a budget-friendly option, check out the Ponce City Hostel. The hostel offers both private rooms and dorms and is located near several popular attractions.
  • The Burgess Hotel: This luxury hotel is located near the Atlanta History Center and offers upscale amenities, including a rooftop bar and a pool.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, here are some options to consider:

  • Sweet Georgia Peaches: Georgia is known for its delicious peaches, so why not take some home with you? You can find them at many of East’s farmers’ markets and grocery stores.
  • Artisan Crafts: East is home to several artisan craft markets where you can purchase handmade items like pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Atlanta Falcons Merchandise: If you’re a football fan, consider picking up some Atlanta Falcons gear to commemorate your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of East

East is a diverse neighborhood with a rich history and culture. To respect the local customs, it’s important to remember these etiquette tips:

  • Dress conservatively while visiting churches and cemeteries.
  • Use “Ma’am” and “Sir” when speaking to elders as a mark of respect.
  • Never leave a tip on the table at a restaurant; always hand it directly to the server.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is warm and the parks and trails are in full bloom. However, if you’re looking for a quieter time to explore the area, consider visiting in the winter when the crowds have thinned out.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

To make the most of your trip to East, consider these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Do visit the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site and the Carter Center to learn about the area’s history and culture.
  • Do try the delicious Southern cuisine and beverages.
  • Do take advantage of the area’s parks, trails, and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip your servers at restaurants and bars.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated during the summer months.

Safety and Health

When visiting East, it’s essential to take proper safety precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Safety in East

  • Avoid walking alone at night and in unfamiliar neighborhoods.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables secure.
  • Follow traffic laws when biking or walking on the city’s streets.

Health in East

  • Be aware of the high temperatures and humidity during the summer months and take precautions to avoid heat stroke and dehydration.
  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

East is located in Metro Atlanta, Georgia, and is easily accessible by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near East

  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is located approximately 15 miles southwest of East and is the busiest airport in the world.

Bus Stations Near East

  • The Megabus offers transportation from several cities to Atlanta, including Houston, Dallas, and Chicago.
  • Greyhound Bus Lines also offer transportation to Atlanta from several cities.

Other Options

  • Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in Atlanta and can provide easy transportation around the city.

How to Get Around in East

The best way to get around East is by walking or biking. The area is bike-friendly and features several bike lanes and trails. Public transportation is also available and includes buses and trains operated by the Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority (MARTA).

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Georgia, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Savannah: This charming city is located approximately 250 miles southeast of Atlanta and is known for its beautiful historic architecture and delicious food.
  • Helen: This Bavarian-style village is located approximately 90 miles northeast of Atlanta and offers visitors a taste of Germany in the heart of Georgia.
  • Blue Ridge: This mountain town is located approximately 90 miles north of Atlanta and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can go hiking, fishing, and kayaking in the area’s stunning surroundings.

Conclusion

East is a vibrant neighborhood in Atlanta, Georgia, that offers visitors a chance to experience Southern culture and history. From its fascinating museums and landmarks to its beautiful parks and trails, East has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this charming neighborhood.
