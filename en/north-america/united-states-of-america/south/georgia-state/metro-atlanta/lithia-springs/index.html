Introduction to Lithia Springs

Lithia Springs is a picturesque city located in Metro Atlanta, in Georgia, known for its beautiful natural springs and rich history. It is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture aficionados alike.

Lithia Springs was once a booming industrial town, with mining and textiles being the primary industries. Today, it has transformed into a charming city that offers visitors a chance to step back in time and enjoy the beauty of nature.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Lithia Springs.

List of Places to Visit in Lithia Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lithia Springs is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the city’s rich history. Below are some of the most popular attractions to visit:

  • Sweetwater Creek State Park and Visitor Center: This state park is a 2,500-acre nature reserve that features hiking trails, fishing, kayaking, and more. Visitors can explore the ruins of the New Manchester Manufacturing Company, a mill that was destroyed during the Civil War.
  • Lithia Springs Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Lithia Springs and its surrounding areas. It features artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that showcase the city’s past.
  • Six Flags Over Georgia: This amusement park is one of the most popular attractions in the area. It features several roller coasters, water rides, and more.

Natural Attractions of Lithia Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lithia Springs is blessed with a beautiful natural environment that offers visitors a chance to connect with nature. Below are some of the most popular outdoor attractions to visit:

  • Lithia Springs Park: This 42-acre park features hiking trails, picnic areas, and a natural spring that is believed to have healing powers.
  • Sweetwater Creek State Park: This state park features over 15 miles of hiking trails, fishing, kayaking, and more. Visitors can also explore the ruins of the New Manchester Manufacturing Company, a mill that was destroyed during the Civil War.
  • Powder Springs Park: This 25-acre park features a playground, picnic areas, and a small lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lithia Springs

Lithia Springs offers several scenic hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Below are some of the most popular:

  • Sweetwater Creek State Park Trails: This park features 15 miles of hiking trails that range from easy to difficult. The trails offer scenic views of waterfalls, rapids, and more.
  • Silver Comet Trail: This 61.5-mile paved trail runs from Smyrna, Georgia, to the Alabama state line. It is perfect for biking, skating, or walking.
  • Heritage Park Trail: This trail is a 1-mile loop that goes around the pond at Heritage Park. It is perfect for a short walk or jog.

Best Things to Do in Lithia Springs: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Lithia Springs offers several activities and tours that are worth experiencing. Below are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing at Sweetwater Creek State Park: Visitors can rent kayaks and canoes to explore the park’s waterways.
  • Wine Tasting at Six Flags Over Georgia: Visitors can enjoy a variety of red and white wines at Six Flags Over Georgia.
  • Ziplining at Historic Banning Mills: This zipline is the longest and largest in the world. Visitors can experience the thrill of ziplining over rushing waterfalls and canyons.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lithia Springs

Lithia Springs is a city that can easily be explored in a week. Below is a suggested itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Lithia Springs and check into your hotel. Visit Sweetwater Creek State Park and explore the New Manchester Mill ruins.
  • Day 2: Go kayaking or canoeing at Sweetwater Creek State Park.
  • Day 3: Explore the Lithia Springs Historical Society Museum.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at Six Flags Over Georgia.
  • Day 5: Visit Powder Springs Park and go for a hike on the park’s trails.
  • Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour at Six Flags Over Georgia.
  • Day 7: Visit Historic Banning Mills and experience the thrill of ziplining.

Food and Drink Scene of Lithia Springs

Lithia Springs has a diverse food and drink scene that offers visitors a chance to indulge in delicious local cuisine. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southern Fried Chicken: This classic Southern dish is a must-try when in Lithia Springs. It is usually served with collard greens, mac and cheese, and cornbread.
  • Sweetwater Creek State Park Fried Pies: These fried pies are a sweet treat made with seasonal fruit and a flaky crust.
  • Shrimp and Grits: This iconic Southern dish is made with large shrimp, creamy grits, and a spicy tomato-based sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sweetwater Creek State Park Spring Water: This natural spring water is believed to have healing powers.
  • Georgia Peach Tea: This refreshing sweet tea is made with fresh peaches and is a favorite of locals.
  • Bourbon from ASW Distillery: This local distillery produces award-winning bourbon that is a must-try for bourbon lovers.

Where to Stay in Lithia Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lithia Springs offers several accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Below are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Hyatt Place Atlanta/Cobb Galleria: This hotel offers spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Hilton Atlanta/Marietta Hotel and Conference Center: This hotel features a conference center, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.
  • Holiday Inn Express Atlanta West - Theme Park Area: This hotel offers a complimentary breakfast, fitness center, and a business center.

What to Buy in Lithia Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lithia Springs offers several local products and souvenirs that are worth bringing home. Below are some of the most popular:

  • Sweetwater Creek State Park Merchandise: Visitors can purchase t-shirts, hats, and other souvenirs at the park’s gift shop.
  • Local Crafts and Artwork: Visitors can purchase local crafts and artwork at the Lithia Springs Historical Society Museum.
  • Peach Preserves and Syrup: Georgia is famously known for its peaches. Visitors can purchase peach preserves and syrup at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Lithia Springs

Lithia Springs is a city that values community and connection. Visitors are welcomed with warm hospitality and a sense of belonging. The city is known for its love of food, music, and the arts.

Best Time to Visit Lithia Springs

The best time to visit Lithia Springs is from March to May and from September to November. The weather is mild during these months, and the crowds are less compared to the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lithia Springs

Lithia Springs is a safe city for visitors. However, visitors should always exercise caution, especially when hiking or biking in natural areas.

Health in Lithia Springs

Lithia Springs has several medical facilities that offer high-quality care. Visitors are advised to pack any medication they may need and ensure they have proper travel insurance.

Conclusion
