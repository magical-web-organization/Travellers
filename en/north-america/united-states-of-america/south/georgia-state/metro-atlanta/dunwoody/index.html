Introduction to Dunwoody

Located in Metro Atlanta, Dunwoody is a charming small town in Georgia, United States of America. It is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a mix of cultural attractions, scenic views and outdoor adventures. Nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains, Dunwoody offers a unique blend of natural beauty and modern amenities. Whether you are a solo traveler, a couple or a family with children, Dunwoody has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Dunwoody

Here is a comprehensive list of top attractions in Dunwoody, categorized by their type:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Spruill Center for the Arts: Offers a variety of art classes, exhibits, and events.
  • The Donaldson-Bannister Farm: A restored 1870s farmstead showcasing the preservation of Dunwoody’s history.
  • The Dunwoody Nature Center: Offers tours, nature trails, and educational programs.
  • The Williams-Payne House Museum: A preserved, 19th-century home that hosts tours and events.

Natural Attractions of Dunwoody: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area: A great way to enjoy the natural beauty and explore the surrounding area. Offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and canoeing opportunities.
  • Brook Run Park: With over 100 acres, the park offers visitors a playground, dog parks, an indoor skate park, and bike trails.
  • Dunwoody Park: Great for picnics, fishing and a quiet leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dunwoody

Dunwoody has a variety of trails, parks, and greenways to explore on foot or by bike:

  • The Dunwoody Trailway: A beautiful 4.2-mile trail that runs along the perimeter of the town.
  • The Path Foundation Trails: A network of trails utilized for walking and biking that wind through Brookhaven, Dunwoody and Sandy Springs.
  • The Atlanta Beltline Northside Trail: The northside trail offers scenic views and is only a few miles away from Dunwoody.

Best Things to Do in Dunwoody: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tours of Atlanta: A guided tour through Atlanta’s culinary scene which includes delicious bites and interesting facts.
  • Dunwoody Wine Stroll: A yearly event where wine connoisseurs can walk around the town and sample different wines to support local businesses.
  • Escape The Room: An interactive puzzle game that is a great way to challenge your mind with some friends or family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dunwoody

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Dunwoody:

  • Day 1: Explore the Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area
  • Day 2: Tour the Spruill Center for the Arts and the Williams-Payne House Museum
  • Day 3: Relax at Brook Run Park and Dunwoody Park
  • Day 4: Try the Escape The Room activity
  • Day 5: Walk or bike the Dunwoody Trailway
  • Day 6: Enjoy a Food Tour of Atlanta
  • Day 7: Participate in the Dunwoody Wine Stroll

Food and Drink Scene of Dunwoody

Dunwoody’s food scene features a mixture of ethnic cuisines and classic Southern dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken with Gravy: A true Southern classic that can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Peach Cobbler: A popular dessert made with fresh Georgia peaches.
  • Shrimp and Grits: A classic Southern dish featuring shrimp on top of creamy grits.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple, available at nearly every restaurant and every grocery store.
  • Coca-Cola: One of Georgia’s most famous exports, Coke can be found everywhere.
  • Sweetwater Brewing: A local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Dunwoody: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dunwoody has plenty of accommodation options, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly mid-range hotels and Airbnb homes for rent:

  • Le Meridien Atlanta Perimeter: A luxury hotel in the town centre with easy access to all major attractions.
  • The Hyatt Place Perimeter: A standard hotel with clean rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Airbnb: A popular accommodation option in Dunwoody for travellers looking for a more flexible and economic stay.

What to Buy in Dunwoody: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dunwoody is home to several local shops, markets and stores that offer unique souvenirs and local products:

  • The Spruill Gallery and Gift Shop: Offers a variety of handcrafted goods from local artisans and crafters.
  • The Lemon Tree: A charming boutique shop with a selection of quirky and stylish gifts.
  • The Fresh Market: A grocery store that offers a wide selection of fresh produce and cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Dunwoody

Southern culture is known for its hospitality, friendliness, and pride in tradition. Dunwoody is no exception to these characteristics. Visitors should be prepared to experience both urban and rural lifestyles during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Dunwoody

The best time to visit Dunwoody is during the spring or the fall. These seasons have mild temperatures, perfect for outdoor activities and events. Winters can be cold and summers can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dunwoody

Do:

  • Try local Southern cuisine.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty and take a walk or bike ride.
  • Take in the local arts and culture scene.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or disturb wildlife during outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dunwoody

Dunwoody is generally a safe town for tourists, but visitors should always take precautions as they would in any other urban area. Crime rates are low but awareness is advised for personal belongings in public spaces.

Health in Dunwoody

For emergency medical assistance, dial 911. There are several hospitals and health care facilities located within Dunwoody.

Getting to Dunwoody: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dunwoody

The closest major airport to Dunwoody is the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, approximately 30 minutes away by car or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Dunwoody

The closest bus station to Dunwoody is the Marta train station, in the Dunwoody town centre.

Other Options

Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are popular transportation options in Dunwoody, and rent-a-car services are also available.

How to Get Around in Dunwoody

The easiest way to get around in Dunwoody is by car or taxi. The town also has a public transportation system, the Marta train, which is a cost-effective option but limited in its routes.

Where to Go Next from Dunwoody: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stone Mountain Park: Located about an hour from Dunwoody, this park offers a variety of outdoor activities and cultural events.
  • Helen, GA: A quaint Alpine town filled with outdoor activities and Bavarian-style architecture, located about two hours from Dunwoody.
  • Athens, GA: Known for its nightlife and college scene, Athens is located about an hour from Dunwoody.

Conclusion

Dunwoody is a charming town that has something to offer for travelers of all ages. With its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, it’s an ideal destination for anyone seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city living. Whether you’re looking for an active vacation or just a relaxing getaway, Dunwoody has something to offer.
