Dallas (Georgia) is a charming city situated in the Metro Atlanta region of Georgia, United States. It is known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and wide array of outdoor activities and cultural attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover or adventure seeker, Dallas is the perfect destination for you.

Introduction to Dallas (Georgia)

Founded in 1854, Dallas (Georgia) is a historic town rich in Civil War history. It is named after George M. Dallas, who was the Vice President of the United States during the 1840s. The city has a population of approximately 13,000 people and is situated just a short drive away from Atlanta, making it a popular weekend getaway for locals and tourists alike. It is home to a number of impressive historical sites, scenic parks, and delicious dining options.

List of Places to Visit in Dallas (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Paulding County Courthouse

The Paulding County Courthouse, located in downtown Dallas, is a National Register of Historic Places landmark that dates back to 1892. It is a popular attraction in the city and is known for its distinctive architecture and beautiful stained-glass dome.

Civil War Trailhead

The Civil War Trailhead is an important historic site in Dallas that honors the memory of the brave soldiers and citizens who fought in the Civil War. Visitors can explore the many artifacts, exhibits, and educational displays that offer insight into this tumultuous period in American history.

Natural Attractions of Dallas (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pickett’s Mill Battlefield State Historic Site

The Pickett’s Mill Battlefield State Historic Site is a 765-acre park with a rich Civil War history. Visitors can explore the museum and its exhibits, take a guided tour of the battlefield, and enjoy a picnic with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Silver Comet Trail

The Silver Comet Trail is a 61.5 mile-long paved trail that runs from Smyrna, Georgia, to the Georgia/Alabama border. It is a popular destination for biking, hiking, and recreational activities throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dallas (Georgia)

Dallas Landing Park

Dallas Landing Park is a popular park with a beautiful beach, hiking trails, boat ramps, and a playground. Visitors can enjoy fishing, kayaking, and picnicking in the scenic outdoor setting.

Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park

Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park is a 2,965-acre park that preserves a Civil War battlefield. It offers visitors over 20 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as scenic vistas and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Dallas (Georgia): Activities and Tours

Southern Tee Golf Course

Southern Tee Golf Course is a public golf course that offers scenic 18-hole rounds with plenty of challenges to keep players entertained. Visitors can enjoy the course’s stunning views, challenging water hazards, and friendly staff.

Dallas Theater

The Dallas Theater is a historic venue that showcases live performances of music, drama, and comedy throughout the year. Operated by the City of Dallas, the theater is a popular destination for locals and visitors alike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dallas (Georgia)

Day 1: Explore the Paulding County Courthouse and Civil War Trailhead in downtown Dallas

Day 2: Hike the trails at Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park

Day 3: Visit the Pickett’s Mill Battlefield State Historic Site

Day 4: Play a round of golf at Southern Tee Golf Course

Day 5: Take a bike ride along the Silver Comet Trail

Day 6: Enjoy a picnic and kayaking at Dallas Landing Park

Day 7: Attend a performance at the Dallas Theater

Food and Drink Scene of Dallas (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

BBQ from Harrison’s

Harrison’s is the go-to spot for BBQ in Dallas. Their tender and juicy ribs and pulled pork are sure to satisfy any barbecue lover’s hunger.

Southern Cuisine from Hubbard’s Off Main

Hubbard’s Off Main is a cozy Southern restaurant that serves up classics like fried chicken, hot biscuits, and macaroni and cheese.

Local Beverages

Four Fat Cows Ice Cream

Four Fat Cows Ice Cream is a local favorite that serves up delicious ice cream in a variety of flavors, including butter pecan, chocolate chip cookie dough, and classic vanilla.

The Grand Theatre

The Grand Theatre is a fantastic place to grab a drink, catch a movie, and enjoy some live entertainment. They offer a variety of beer and wine options.

Where to Stay in Dallas (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ramada by Wyndham Dallas Atlanta West

The Ramada by Wyndham Dallas Atlanta West is conveniently located near downtown Dallas and offers comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.

Hampton Inn Atlanta/Dallas

The Hampton Inn Atlanta/Dallas is a great choice for those looking for a more upscale hotel experience. They offer great amenities, including complimentary breakfast and high-speed internet.

What to Buy in Dallas (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Dallas Farmers Market

The Dallas Farmers Market is a great place to find locally made products, including fresh produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal goods.

Georgia Marketplace

The Georgia Marketplace offers a wide selection of Georgia-made products, including local wines and spirits, handmade soaps and candles, and locally sourced food items.

Local Culture and Customs of Dallas (Georgia)

Dallas is located in the Southern part of the United States and is known for its friendly locals, Southern hospitality, and relaxed, laid-back vibe. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs by being courteous, respectful, and engaging with the community.

Best Time to Visit Dallas (Georgia)

The best time to visit Dallas is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than during the peak summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dallas (Georgia)

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the many historical sites and cultural attractions in Dallas
  • Try the local BBQ and Southern cuisine
  • Pack comfortable shoes for hiking and outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray for outdoor activities
  • Be afraid to engage with the locals and strike up a conversation
  • Leave without trying the locally made ice cream and handmade products

Safety and Health

Safety in Dallas (Georgia)

Dallas is generally a safe city to visit, but it’s always important to take common-sense safety precautions, such as locking your car and being aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Dallas (Georgia)

Dallas has plenty of healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, in case of an emergency or illness during your visit.

Getting to Dallas (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dallas (Georgia)

The closest airport to Dallas is Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is located approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Dallas (Georgia)

The closest bus station to Dallas is the Greyhound station in Atlanta, which is approximately 40 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Dallas is easily accessible by car, and there are several major highways that run through the city.

How to Get Around in Dallas (Georgia)

The best way to get around Dallas is by car, as there is limited public transportation in the city. Visitors can also rent bicycles or use a ride-sharing app to get around.

Where to Go Next from Dallas (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Atlanta

Atlanta is just a short drive away from Dallas (Georgia), and it offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. Visitors can explore places such as the Georgia Aquarium, the World of Coca-Cola, and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site.

Marietta

Marietta is a charming town located just 20 minutes away from Dallas. Visitors can explore the Marietta Square, which is home to several historic buildings, boutique shops, and delicious dining options.

Conclusion

Dallas (Georgia) is a hidden gem in the Metro Atlanta region, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. With its scenic beauty, friendly locals, delicious cuisine, and rich cultural heritage, it is the perfect destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Georgia’s charming Southern hospitality.
