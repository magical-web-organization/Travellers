Introduction to Roswell (Georgia)

Roswell, located in the Metro Atlanta area of Georgia, boasts a rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and a thriving local culture. From museums and historic sites to outdoor adventures and delicious dining options, there’s something for everyone in this charming Southern city. Read on for a comprehensive travel guide to Roswell, Georgia.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to explore the history and culture of Roswell is by visiting its many museums and historical sites. Bulloch Hall, the childhood home of President Theodore Roosevelt’s mother, is a must-see for history buffs. The Roswell Mill, which played a significant role in the city’s manufacturing industry, has been transformed into a park with stunning views of the surrounding dam and river. The Archibald Smith Plantation Home offers a glimpse into the life of a wealthy Southern family in the mid-1800s. For those interested in the Antebellum South, Barrington Hall and Mimosa Hall and Gardens are both great options to explore the history of the region. The Southern Trilogy Historic House Museums consist of Bulloch Hall, Barrington Hall and Smith Plantation.

Natural Attractions of Roswell (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Roswell has plenty of natural beauty to explore, and Chattahoochee Nature Center is a fantastic place to start. With over 127 acres of ponds and woodland, it provides endless opportunities for hiking and wildlife spotting. Vickery Creek Trail affords a scenic hike along the creek and waterfall. You can also go kayaking, paddle boarding or fishing on the Chattahoochee River. Additionally, the Roswell Riverwalk offers beautiful scenic views of the Chattahoochee River with different public art sculptures and benches for enjoying the breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roswell (Georgia)

In addition to the aforementioned Vickery Creek Trail, Roswell boasts several fantastic hiking and biking trails. The Big Creek Greenway follows Big Creek for 8 miles, offering lovely scenery and a flat surface that’s perfect for beginner bikers and hikers. Big Creek Trail is a connector trail to Roswell that is easily accessible. The Roswell Riverwalk is a stretch of trails along the Chattahoochee river Suwanee Creek Greenway in Forsyth County offers a 4.2-mile trail with clear directions and scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Roswell (Georgia): Activities and Tours

The Roswell Cultural Arts Center offers live music performances, dance performances, and theater acts throughout the year. The Chattahoochee River is ideal for paddleboarding and kayaking, and various local companies offer rental services. Local festivals, including the Roswell Arts Festival and Alive in Roswell, are great ways to experience the city’s thriving arts scene. GHOST Tour in Roswell is a guided ghost tour through the historic city center. The Preservation Roswell Home Tour is also an interesting activity and allows you to see the inside of unique historic homes who are open in select weekends.

Food and Drink Scene of Roswell (Georgia)

Roswell has a diverse food and drink scene, with plenty of options to satisfy any craving. For classic Southern fare, head to J. Christopher’s or Chow King Grill & Buffet. For international cuisine, check out Rio Balsas Taqueria (Mexican), Thai Emerald (Thai), or Foundation Social Eatery (American and European). 1920 Tavern is also a local favorite for bar bites and cocktails. Some other local favorites include Table & Main and The Crabspot.

Must-Try Dishes

Roswell is known for its Regional Southern cooking, which features dishes like fried chicken, biscuits, grits, collard greens, and barbecue. Some specific dishes worth trying at local restaurants are chicken and waffles at J. Christopher’s, Fried chicken wings at the Crabspot, and BBQ pork sandwiches at Swallow at the Hallow.

Local Beverages

Roswell has a bustling craft beer scene, with breweries like Gate City Brewing and Variant Brewing Company both offering unique and tasty options. Ironshield Brewing and From the Earth Brewing Company are also popular options. Roswell Tasting Room is also a must-visit for wine lovers, where you can sample a vast array of wines from Georgia. For non-alcoholic options, visit Roswell Farmers and Artisan Market to taste local homemade juices, or try Sweet Apple Donut, where you can find fresh coffee and delicious pastries.

Where to Stay in Roswell (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Roswell has a range of accommodation options, from cozy B&Bs to modern hotels. The Doubletree by Hilton Atlanta/Roswell is a popular pick, thanks to its comfortable rooms and central location. The Roswell Suites is another great option, offering spacious rooms and a complimentary breakfast buffet. For something cozier, check out The Cottage on Canton Street. For a more bed and breakfast atmosphere you can checkout Awake Cottage. Otherwise, there are several Airbnb options in the area.

What to Buy in Roswell (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Roswell has a thriving arts scene, and there are plenty of local artists and craftspeople selling their wares throughout the city. Chattahoochee Nature Center’s gift shop has local environmentally friendly options such as bamboo socks and local honey. Georgia Artisan in the heart of historic Roswell offers an array of hand-crafted, unique items from artisans across the Peach State. Otherwise, you can find Georgia-made products of all kinds at Southern Sisters Boutique.

Local Culture and Customs of Roswell (Georgia)

Roswell, like many Southern cities, is known for its hospitality and friendly locals. The city is also actively working to preserve its rich history and cultural landmarks, which can be seen throughout the area. When visiting Roswell, it’s best to embrace the relaxed, easygoing pace of Southern life and savor the taste of Southern cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Roswell (Georgia)

The best time to plan your Roswell trip depends on the activities and weather conditions you prefer. For cooler weather, plan your trip between September and November. For outdoor activities and cultural festivals, visit in June or July. However, Georgia summers can be quite hot, so spring can be an optimal time to visit with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roswell (Georgia)

Do:

  • Do visit the many museums, historic sites, and natural attractions of Roswell.
  • Do try local Southern cuisine and sample craft beer.
  • Do stay in a bed and breakfast or cozy Airbnb to experience Roswell’s hospitality.
  • Do embrace the relaxed pace of Southern life.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the opportunity to explore Vickery Creek Trail and Chattahoochee Nature Center.
  • Don’t miss the local wines at Roswell Tasting Room.
  • Don’t forget insect repellent if traveling during the hot summer months.
  • Don’t assume everyone in Georgia is a southern food lover.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roswell (Georgia)

Roswell is considered a safe city to visit. Visitors should take normal safety precautions such as locking car doors and not leaving valuable items in plain sight as per routine.

Health in Roswell (Georgia)

Visitors should keep sufficient sunscreen, drinking enough water, and dress accordingly during hot days.

Getting to Roswell (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roswell (Georgia)

The nearest major airport to Roswell is Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is 30 miles south of Roswell. Travelers can reach Roswell by taking either a traditional taxi or an Uber or Lyft.

Bus Stations Near Roswell (Georgia)

MARTA rail and bus services offer public transportation throughout the city of Atlanta, and there are several options for reaching Roswell from there. Alternatively, Greyhound station is closest in Doraville, GA.

Other Options

Another way to traverse throughout Atlanta and Roswell is via taxies, rental cars, or shuttle services.

How to Get Around in Roswell (Georgia)

Travelers can rent a car or bring their own as the easiest way to get around Roswell. As previously mentioned, Uber and Lyft options are available as well. Biking in Roswell is also a popular mode of transport.

Where to Go Next from Roswell (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sandy Springs Franklin Hayward Fine Arts Center, Dunwoody Nature Center, Smith Plantation,McDaniel Farm, and Alpharetta Arboretum at Wills Park are all fabulous attractions within a few miles away from Roswell and worth visiting.

Conclusion

Roswell, Georgia, has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s rich history, enjoying the outdoors, or indulging in Southern cuisine and local drinks, there’s something to be said for the welcoming culture and charm of this charming Southern destination. Book your trip today and get ready to experience all that Roswell has to offer!
