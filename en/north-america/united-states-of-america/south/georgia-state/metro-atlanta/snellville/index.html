Introduction to Snellville

Snellville is a peaceful and scenic town located in the heart of Metro Atlanta, Georgia. The town is home to a vibrant community, rich history, and numerous attractions that make it a must-visit destination for travelers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Snellville has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Snellville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Snellville is home to several museums and historical places, making it a great destination for history buffs. Visit the Snellville Historical Museum to learn more about the town’s rich history or explore the McDaniel Farm Park, which is dedicated to preserving the history of the late 1800s and early 1900s. The Briscoe Park also features a replica of the town’s first schoolhouse and a log cabin from the mid-1800s.

Natural Attractions of Snellville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Snellville is a paradise for nature lovers, offering numerous natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor activities. Head to the Yellow River Park to enjoy hiking and biking trails, fishing opportunities, and kayaking/canoeing facilities. For scenic views, visit the Lenora Park or the Briscoe Park, which both offer walking trails and picnic areas with beautiful lake views.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Snellville

Snellville features several biking and hiking trails suitable for visitors of all levels. The Yellow River Trail is a popular choice for bikers and hikers, with a scenic route that runs along the Yellow River. The Briscoe Park Trail is another popular option, with a 1.4-mile loop trail that takes you through the park’s wooded areas.

Best Things to Do in Snellville: Activities and Tours

Snellville offers several activities and tours that visitors can enjoy during their stay. Take a tour of the Vines Botanical Gardens or visit the Norton Park Art Gallery to explore the local art scene. For family-friendly activities, head to the Summit Skyride in Stone Mountain or visit the Yellow River Game Ranch to see wildlife up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Snellville

Day 1: Visit the McDaniel Farm Park and the Yellow River Park for a day of outdoor activities such as fishing, kayaking, and hiking. Day 2: Explore the Snellville Historical Museum and the Briscoe Park. Day 3: Take a tour of the Vines Botanical Gardens and visit the Norton Park Art Gallery. Day 4: Visit the Summit Skyride in Stone Mountain or the Yellow River Game Ranch. Day 5: Enjoy a day of shopping at the local boutiques and gift shops in the town center. Day 6: Take a picnic lunch and enjoy the picturesque views at the Lenora Park. Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Snellville’s renowned spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Snellville

Must-Try Dishes

Snellville’s food scene is as diverse as it is delicious. Head to The Snellville Diner for classic breakfast dishes or visit the Bullpen Rib House for the best BBQ in town. For a unique twist on pizza, visit the Your Pie eatery, where you can customize your own pizza with fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

For a local brew, visit the Anderby Brewing Company and sample their craft beers made with the highest quality ingredients. The Wine Shop at home Cellars features a vast selection of wines, from local varieties to international classics, that you can enjoy in-store or take home for later.

Where to Stay in Snellville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Snellville offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget and preference. For a luxurious stay, book a room at the Marriott Stone Mountain Inn or the Hilton Garden Inn. If you’re looking for something more affordable, try the motels located on the outskirts of town.

What to Buy in Snellville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a piece of Snellville home with you by shopping for souvenirs and local products at the town center boutiques. Visit the Snellville Towne Center or the Village Square Center to browse for artisan-made jewelry, locally roasted coffee, and charming crafts made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Snellville

Snellville is a friendly and welcoming community where visitors can experience the best of southern hospitality. Be sure to try some sweet tea, a signature local beverage, and engage with locals in shops and restaurants to learn more about the town’s heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Snellville

The best time to visit Snellville is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and comfortable. The town can get quite crowded during the summer, with temperatures reaching high humidity levels. If you’re visiting in the winter, be prepared for colder temperatures and possible snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Snellville

Do:

  • Do sample the local fare, including BBQ, sweet tea, and craft beer.
  • Do engage with locals to learn more about the town’s heritage and traditions.
  • Do visit one of Snellville’s spas for a relaxing and rejuvenating experience.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen, water, and appropriate outdoor gear while hiking and biking.
  • Don’t disturb local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Snellville

Snellville is a safe and friendly town with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take general safety precautions, such as locking car doors and maintaining situational awareness, especially when exploring natural attractions.

Health in Snellville

Snellville has excellent medical facilities, with several hospitals and clinics located in the town center. Visitors are advised to carry a basic first aid kit and seek immediate medical attention in case of an emergency.

Getting to Snellville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Snellville

The closest airport to Snellville is the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is located approximately 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Snellville

The Gwinnett County Transit bus system offers several bus routes that connect Snellville to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Rental cars and taxis are easily available options for transportation around Snellville.

How to Get Around in Snellville

Driving is the most convenient and preferred way to get around Snellville. The town offers ample parking facilities, and traffic is generally light.

Where to Go Next from Snellville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Snellville is located in the heart of Georgia, making it an ideal base for exploring nearby destinations. Visit the scenic Lake Lanier or head to Atlanta to explore its famous attractions, such as the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site or the Georgia Aquarium.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Snellville has something for everyone. With a rich cultural heritage, scenic natural attractions, and a vibrant food scene, Snellville is a must-visit destination that promises a unique and unforgettable experience.
