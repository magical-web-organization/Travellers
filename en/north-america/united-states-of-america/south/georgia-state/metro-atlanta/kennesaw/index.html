Introduction to Kennesaw

Kennesaw is a quaint city situated in Metro Atlanta, Georgia, in the southern region of the United States. The city is best known for its Civil War history and southern charm. Visitors can explore the rich history of the city, enjoy the great outdoors, and indulge in southern hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Kennesaw

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park

Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park is a must-visit destination for history buffs. The park commemorates the Battle of Kennesaw Mountain, fought on June 27, 1864. Visitors can hike the trails that wind through the battlefield and visit the museum to learn more about the Civil War.

Southern Museum of Civil War and Locomotive History

The Southern Museum of Civil War and Locomotive History houses more than 10,000 artifacts from the Civil War era. Visitors can explore the exhibits that showcase the role of railroads during the war and learn about the life of soldiers and civilians during that time.

Natural Attractions of Kennesaw: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Noonday Creek Trail

The Noonday Creek Trail is a 7.8-mile multi-use trail that winds through Kennesaw and connects to Roswell. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and running and offers beautiful views of the creek and surrounding nature.

Swift-Cantrell Park

Swift-Cantrell Park is a 42-acre park that features playgrounds, trails, and sports fields. Visitors can enjoy a picnic, play a game of soccer, or take a walk on the trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kennesaw

Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park Trails

Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park has several hiking trails that vary in length and difficulty. The trails offer stunning views of the mountains and surrounding nature.

Silver Comet Trail

The Silver Comet Trail is a 61.5-mile trail that extends from Smyrna to the Georgia/Alabama state line. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and running and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kennesaw: Activities and Tours

Ghost Tours of Kennesaw

Ghost Tours of Kennesaw offer a spooky look at the city’s past. Visitors can take a walking tour led by a knowledgeable guide who shares stories of paranormal encounters and haunted locations.

Smith-Gilbert Gardens

Smith-Gilbert Gardens is a 16-acre botanical garden featuring more than 3,000 plant species. Visitors can explore the gardens and take a guided tour to learn about the various plants and trees.

Food and Drink Scene of Kennesaw

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a southern staple, and Kennesaw has some of the best. Several local restaurants serve up crispy fried chicken that visitors won’t want to miss.

Biscuits and Gravy

Biscuits and gravy is a traditional southern breakfast dish that is popular in Kennesaw. Visitors can try this savory dish at several local breakfast spots.

Local Beverages

Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a classic southern drink that is popular in Kennesaw. Visitors can enjoy this refreshing beverage at several local restaurants.

Craft Beer

Kennesaw has several local breweries that serve up delicious craft beer. Visitors can take a brewery tour and taste the various brews on offer.

Where to Stay in Kennesaw: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Embassy Suites by Hilton Atlanta Kennesaw Town Center

The Embassy Suites by Hilton Atlanta Kennesaw Town Center is a great option for visitors looking for a comfortable hotel with all the amenities. The hotel offers free breakfast, a fitness center, and a pool.

Chateau Elan Winery and Resort

Chateau Elan Winery and Resort is an upscale resort located in nearby Braselton. The resort features a winery, spa, golf course, and several restaurants.

What to Buy in Kennesaw: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Civil War Memorabilia

Kennesaw has a rich Civil War history, and visitors can purchase souvenirs and memorabilia related to the war at several local shops.

Local Honey

Local honey is a popular souvenir in Kennesaw. Visitors can purchase jars of honey from local beekeepers and take a taste of the south home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Kennesaw

Kennesaw is known for its southern hospitality and laid-back lifestyle. Visitors can expect friendly locals, small-town charm, and a relaxed pace of life.

Best Time to Visit Kennesaw

The best time to visit Kennesaw is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the trees are in full bloom. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kennesaw

Do:

  • Embrace southern hospitality and culture.
  • Explore the beautiful natural areas around Kennesaw.
  • Try the fried chicken and sweet tea.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray.
  • Expect big-city amenities and nightlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kennesaw

Kennesaw is generally a safe city, but visitors should be mindful of their surroundings, particularly when hiking or exploring the city at night.

Health in Kennesaw

Visitors should stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun and insects when enjoying outdoor activities in Kennesaw.

Getting to Kennesaw: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kennesaw

The nearest airport to Kennesaw is Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, located approximately 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Kennesaw

The Cobb County Transit bus system serves Kennesaw, with several bus stops located throughout the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing service to get around Kennesaw and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Kennesaw

Visitors can get around Kennesaw on foot, by bike, or by car. Kennesaw also has a public transit system that connects the city to Atlanta.

Where to Go Next from Kennesaw: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Atlanta

Atlanta is located just a short drive from Kennesaw and offers a wealth of attractions, including the Georgia Aquarium, World of Coca-Cola, and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site.

Lake Allatoona

Lake Allatoona is a popular destination for boating, swimming, and fishing. The lake is located just a short drive from Kennesaw and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Conclusion

Kennesaw is a charming city with a rich history and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the city’s museums and historical sites, hike the beautiful trails, and enjoy southern hospitality. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their time in Kennesaw and experience all that the city has to offer.
