Introduction to Moultrie

Nestled in the heart of Plantation Midlands in Georgia, Moultrie is a charming southern town that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventures. Known for its historic sites, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, this hidden gem of Georgia is the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy some relaxed southern hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Moultrie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ellen Payne Odom Genealogy Library: A great place to research your family history and learn more about the town’s history and culture.
  • Colquitt County Arts Center: This historic building is home to several art galleries with rotating exhibits and hosts frequent live performances, workshops, and art classes.
  • Moultrie-Colquitt County Library: A great resource for locals and visitors, this library offers a wide variety of books, audiobooks, and e-books, as well as free public events and programs.

Natural Attractions of Moultrie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Reed Bingham State Park: This picturesque park offers hiking, fishing, boating, and camping opportunities, and is home to several rare species of animals and plants.
  • C.A.R.E. Wildlife Rehabilitation Center: This nonprofit animal rescue center allows visitors to get up close and personal with native animals and learn more about conservation efforts in the region.
  • Sunset on the Lake: A popular spot for picnics and sunset watching, this beautiful lake offers great views of the surrounding landscape and a peaceful atmosphere perfect for relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moultrie

  • The Little River Trail: This 4.5-mile trail offers stunning views of Little River and a chance to see several rare species of birds and wildlife.
  • The Talmadge Greenway: A 15-mile-long paved trail that winds through scenic rural areas and historic neighborhoods, perfect for biking or jogging.
  • Birdsong Nature Center: This 565-acre nature preserve offers several hiking trails, bird watching opportunities, and a chance to see some of the region’s rare flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Moultrie: Activities and Tours

  • The Sunbelt Agricultural Exposition: Held every October, this annual event is one of the largest farm shows in the country and offers a chance to learn more about the region’s agriculture, attend live performances and demonstrations, and enjoy carnival rides and games.
  • The Moultrie Swap Meet: One of the largest automotive swap meets in the country, this event offers a chance to buy, sell, or trade car parts and accessories, as well as attend car shows and auctions.
  • Canoeing on the Little River: Rent a canoe and travel down the Little River, enjoying the scenic views and peaceful atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moultrie

Food and Drink Scene of Moultrie

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried catfish: A southern staple that is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, served with hushpuppies and coleslaw.
  • Barbecue pork: Smoked over hickory wood and served with a tangy vinegar-based sauce.
  • Pecan pie: A classic southern dessert, made with fresh Georgia pecans and a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: A southern classic, this refreshing drink is made with black tea and plenty of sugar.
  • Coca-Cola: Moultrie is home to a Coca-Cola production plant, and locals swear it tastes better than anywhere else.

Where to Stay in Moultrie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Barber-Tucker House: A historic bed and breakfast located near downtown Moultrie, offering elegant rooms and a full breakfast each morning.
  • The Hampton Inn Moultrie: Located near several of the town’s popular attractions, this modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Guest Lodge: A cozy guesthouse located in a historic building, offering affordable rooms and easy access to downtown Moultrie.

What to Buy in Moultrie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pecans and pecan products: Moultrie is located in the heart of Georgia’s pecan country, and the town is known for its delicious pecan pies, pralines, and other pecan-related products.
  • Nutcrackers: This wooden figure is a popular Christmas decoration in the region, and Moultrie has several shops that specialize in unique and handcrafted nutcrackers.
  • Artwork and crafts: The Colquitt County Arts Center and several local galleries offer a wide variety of locally made artwork, crafts, and souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Moultrie

Moultrie is known for its friendly southern hospitality, and visitors should expect to be greeted with a warm welcome everywhere they go. The town also has a strong agricultural tradition, and residents take great pride in their farms and other rural businesses. Dress codes are generally casual, but there are several formal events throughout the year that may require more formal attire.

Best Time to Visit Moultrie

The best time to visit Moultrie is in the fall, when the weather is mild and several popular events take place, such as the Sunbelt Agricultural Exposition and the Moultrie Swap Meet. Spring and summer offer great outdoor adventures and outdoor festivals, while winter is a quieter time with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moultrie

Do:

  • Say “yes ma’am” and “no sir”: Moultrie residents value respect and politeness, and these phrases are considered a sign of good manners.
  • Try the local cuisine: Moultrie is known for its delicious southern cuisine, so be sure to try some of the local specialties.
  • Get off the beaten path: Some of the best things to do in Moultrie are off the beaten path, so don’t be afraid to explore and try something new.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing: Moultrie may be a casual town, but residents still appreciate modesty and respect for local customs.
  • Refuse a dish or drink: Southern hospitality is a big deal in Moultrie, and refusing a dish or drink may be seen as rude.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moultrie

Moultrie is a generally safe town, although visitors should still take common-sense precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight. If hiking or participating in outdoor activities, be aware of local wildlife and hazards, such as snakes and uneven terrain.

Health in Moultrie

Moultrie has several healthcare facilities, including the Colquitt Regional Medical Center and several urgent care clinics, but visitors should still take care to avoid illness or injury by staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen, and being aware of local health hazards, such as mosquito-borne illnesses and tick bites.

Getting to Moultrie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moultrie is located about 200 miles south of Atlanta and can be easily accessed by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Moultrie

  • Southwest Georgia Regional Airport: Located about 30 miles from Moultrie, this airport is served by Delta and offers daily flights to Atlanta.

Bus Stations Near Moultrie

  • Greyhound: The nearest bus station is located in nearby Valdosta, about 40 miles from Moultrie.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Many visitors to Moultrie opt for car rental to easily navigate the town and surrounding areas.
  • Taxi or rideshare: Several taxi and rideshare companies operate in the area and can provide easy transportation around town.

How to Get Around in Moultrie

Moultrie is a relatively small town, and many attractions can be easily accessed on foot or by bike. Taxis and rideshare services are available for those who need them, and car rental is also a popular option for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Moultrie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Savannah: Located about four hours east of Moultrie, this historic city is known for its charming atmosphere, great dining and shopping, and beautiful architecture.
  • Tallahassee: Located about two hours south of Moultrie in Florida, Tallahassee is home to several museums, historic sites, and outdoor adventures perfect for a day trip.
  • St. Simon’s Island: Located about two hours east of Moultrie, this island offers some of the best beaches and outdoor activities in the region.

Conclusion

Moultrie may be a small town, but it’s big on southern hospitality and charm. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or simply relaxing, this hidden gem of Georgia has something for everyone. From delicious cuisine to outdoor adventures, there’s plenty to see and do in Moultrie, and visitors are sure to leave with memories that will last a lifetime.
