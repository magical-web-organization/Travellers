Douglas, Georgia, is a hidden gem located in the heart of Plantation Midlands in Georgia. This small town is big on charm and offers a unique blend of rich history, natural beauty, and cultural heritage that will capture your heart and imagination. In this ultimate travel guide to Douglas, we will introduce you to the must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, where to stay, local food and drink scene, and more.

Introduction to Douglas (Georgia)

Douglas is a quaint town located in Coffee County, Georgia. It is situated in the southern part of the state and is known for its sweet tea, friendly locals, and historic buildings. It is home to a diverse population of approximately 11,000 people from different backgrounds and cultures. The town has a rich history that dates back to the 1800s, and its Victorian architecture and historic landmarks are a testament to its past. Douglas is also a hub for outdoor enthusiasts, with several parks, hiking trails, and fishing spots.

List of Places to Visit in Douglas (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. WWII Flight Training Museum: This museum is dedicated to commemorating the history of the WWII Army Air Corps Flying Training Program that took place in Douglas. The museum showcases artifacts, memorabilia, and exhibits that offer a glimpse into the lives of the servicemen who trained here.

2. Heritage Station Museum: The museum is located in the former Atlantic Coast Line Railroad depot and features exhibits on the history of railroads in Georgia. It also has a model train exhibit and a gift shop.

3. General Coffee State Park: The park is named after General John Coffee, a veteran of the War of 1812, and the son of a planter who owned a nearby plantation. The park offers camping, hiking, fishing, and boating opportunities, and has a small museum that showcases the local flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Douglas (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Broxton Rocks: These towering sandstone cliffs and boulders are a natural wonder that provides a unique hiking experience. The area is part of the Nature Conservancy and is home to several rare plant species.

2. Satilla River Water Trail: This scenic water trail meanders through the beautiful Satilla River and is perfect for canoeing, kayaking, and fishing. It is known for its diverse wildlife and stunning scenery.

3. Beaverdam Wildlife Management Area: This area offers visitors the chance to see a variety of animals in their natural habitat. It has hiking trails, camping sites, and a 180-acre fishing lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Douglas (Georgia)

1. General Coffee State Park Trails: The park has several hiking trails that range from easy to moderate and offer scenic views of the park’s hardwood forests, swamps, and streams.

2. South Georgia Rails to Trail: This trail is a rails-to-trails path that runs through Douglas and the surrounding areas. It is perfect for cycling, walking, or jogging and provides scenic views of the town’s historic landmarks and natural scenery.

3. Broxton Rocks Trail: This trail is a moderate hike that takes visitors through the towering sandstone cliffs of Broxton Rocks and showcases the unique plant and animal species that call it home.

Best Things to Do in Douglas (Georgia): Activities and Tours

1. Coffee County Farm Tour: This tour takes visitors to several local farms and provides an opportunity to learn about agriculture in Georgia. Visitors can sample fresh produce, berries, honey, and other farm-fresh products.

2. Douglas Downtown Antique Market and Art Walk: This event takes place on the second Saturday of each month and offers visitors the chance to explore downtown Douglas and browse through antique shops and local art galleries.

3. Coffee County Courthouse Tour: This historic courthouse has been standing since 1915 and is a stunning example of the Beaux-Arts architectural style. Visitors can take guided tours of the building and learn about its history and significance.

Food and Drink Scene of Douglas (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

1. Fried Catfish: This Southern classic is served at many restaurants in Douglas. It is crispy on the outside and moist on the inside, and is served with a side of hushpuppies and coleslaw.

2. Sweet Tea: You cannot visit Douglas and not try their famous sweet tea. It is a refreshing beverage that is sweetened with sugar and served over ice.

3. Brunswick Stew: This hearty stew is made with chicken, pork, and vegetables and is a staple in Southern cuisine.

Local Beverages

1. Jekyll Island Red Bug Ale: This locally brewed beer is named after a unique mode of transportation used on Jekyll Island in the early 1900s. It is a deep red color and has a full-bodied flavor.

2. Muscadine Wine: This sweet wine is made from muscadine grapes that are grown locally in Georgia. It is perfect for sipping and enjoying on a warm summer day.

3. Georgia Peach Tea: This sweet tea is made with fresh peaches, sugar, and tea, and is a refreshing twist on the classic sweet tea.

Where to Stay in Douglas (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Western Inn Express: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and is located near several local restaurants and shops.

2. Hampton Inn Douglas: This hotel offers modern amenities and is located close to several attractions, including General Coffee State Park and the Heritage Station Museum.

3. Holiday Inn Express & Suites Douglas: This hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a pool. It is located in the heart of downtown Douglas and is close to several eateries and shops.

What to Buy in Douglas (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Pecans: Georgia is famous for its pecans, and Douglas is no exception. You can buy roasted, salted, or candied pecans from several local stores and markets.

2. Peanut Butter: Georgia is also known for its peanut butter, and Douglas has several local brands that produce high-quality peanut butter.

3. Sweet Tea: Bring home a taste of Douglas by purchasing some locally made sweet tea. It comes in several flavors, including peach, raspberry, and lemon.

Local Culture and Customs of Douglas (Georgia)

Douglas is known for its hospitality and friendliness towards visitors. The locals are proud of their heritage and are happy to share it with those who are interested. The town’s architecture, landmarks, and natural beauty are a testament to the region’s rich history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Douglas (Georgia)

The best time to visit Douglas is in the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and pleasant. The town comes alive during the Douglas Downtown Antique Market and Art Walk, which takes place on the second Saturday of each month.

Do’s and Don’ts of Douglas (Georgia)

Do:

  • Try the famous sweet tea, fried catfish, and other local delicacies.
  • Visit local museums and landmarks to learn about the town’s history and culture.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area by hiking or biking through the trails and parks.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or disturb the natural habitats of the local wildlife.
  • Litter or leave trash in the parks or on the trails.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Douglas (Georgia)

Douglas is a safe town, but visitors should take normal precautions when traveling and exploring the area. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables safe and secure.

Health in Douglas (Georgia)

There are several hospitals and medical clinics in Douglas that provide medical services to residents and visitors.

Getting to Douglas (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Douglas (Georgia)

  • Douglas Municipal Airport is the closest airport to Douglas and is located just a few miles from the town center. It offers limited commercial flights.

  • Jacksonville International Airport is located approximately 120 miles south of Douglas and offers several daily flights to major cities in the US and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Douglas (Georgia)

  • Greyhound operates a bus station in Waycross, which is located approximately 35 miles northwest of Douglas.

Other Options

  • Rental cars are available at both Jacksonville International Airport and Douglas Municipal Airport.

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Douglas (Georgia)

The best way to get around Douglas is by car. Rental cars are available at both airports and in town. There is also a local bus service provided by Coffee Regional Transit that operates Monday through Friday.

Where to Go Next from Douglas (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Okefenokee Swamp: This natural wonder is located approximately 40 miles south of Douglas and is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including alligators, black bears, and hundreds of bird species.

  • Jacksonville, Florida: This vibrant city is located approximately 2 hours south of Douglas and offers world-class shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

  • Savannah, Georgia: This historic city is known for its stunning architecture, cobblestone streets, and lively nightlife. It is located approximately 3 hours northwest of Douglas.

Conclusion

Douglas is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage. Whether you want to hike, bike, fish, or just relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene, Douglas has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to pack your bags and discover the charm of Plantation Midlands.
