Introduction to Piedmont (Georgia)

Piedmont is a charming city located in the southern part of Georgia in the United States. It is blessed with natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural importance. Whether you are visiting Piedmont for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, the place has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Piedmont (Georgia)

Piedmont has plenty of attractions, ranging from outdoor adventures and scenic views to culturally important museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit places in Piedmont:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hudgens Center for the Arts - Hudgens Center is a contemporary art museum that showcases both regional and national artists.

  • Atlanta History Center - The Atlanta History Center is a museum and research center that preserves and interprets Atlanta’s history and culture.

  • Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site - The Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site is a museum complex dedicated to the life and legacy of Martin Luther King Jr.

  • Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park - Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park is a historic site that features a Civil War museum and nature trails.

Natural Attractions of Piedmont (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stone Mountain Park - Stone Mountain Park is a state park that features the largest exposed granite dome in North America and offers hiking trails, an aerial tram, and a laser light show.

  • Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area - Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area is a nature park that features the Chattahoochee River, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

  • Sweetwater Creek State Park - Sweetwater Creek State Park is a nature park that features the ruins of a mill, hiking trails, and fishing areas.

  • Piedmont Park - Piedmont Park is a popular urban park that features a lake, playgrounds, and sports fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Piedmont (Georgia)

Piedmont is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some popular trails include:

  • Appalachian Trail - The Appalachian Trail is a world-famous hiking trail that crosses Piedmont.

  • Silver Comet Trail - Silver Comet Trail is a paved bike trail that runs over 61 miles from Smyrna, Georgia to Alabama.

  • Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area Trails - The Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area has over 50 miles of hiking trails that offer scenic views of the Chattahoochee River.

  • Sweetwater Creek State Park Trails - Sweetwater Creek State Park has over nine miles of hiking trails that run through the park’s beautiful forested areas and alongside the creek.

Best Things to Do in Piedmont (Georgia): Activities and Tours

Piedmont offers a range of activities and tours for visitors, from family-friendly fun and outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and culinary delights. Here are some must-do activities in Piedmont:

  • Visit the High Museum of Art - The High Museum of Art is one of the leading art museums in the southeastern United States and offers world-class collections and exhibitions.

  • Take a Walking Tour of the Historic Oakland Cemetery - The Historic Oakland Cemetery is a beautiful Victorian garden cemetery that is home to over 70,000 Atlanta citizens.

  • Experience the Children’s Museum of Atlanta - The Children’s Museum of Atlanta is a hands-on museum that offers interactive exhibits and fun activities for kids.

  • Explore the CNN Center - The CNN Center offers a behind-the-scenes guided tour of the world’s largest news organization.

Food and Drink Scene of Piedmont (Georgia)

Piedmont’s food and drink scene is as diverse as its attractions, offering a mix of Southern favorites, international cuisine, and craft breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken - Fried chicken is a quintessential Southern dish, and Piedmont has some of the best.

  • Pimento Cheese - Pimento cheese is a cheesy spread that is popular in the South.

  • Grits - Grits are a Southern breakfast staple made from ground corn.

  • Peach Cobbler - Peach cobbler is a delicious dessert made with fresh peaches and a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - Sweet tea is the quintessential Southern beverage, and you can find it everywhere in Piedmont.

  • Craft Breweries - Piedmont has a thriving craft beer scene, with many breweries offering tours and tastings.
