Introduction to Americus (Georgia)

Located in the heart of Georgia, Americus is a charming city that offers a wide range of attractions to explore. From historical museums to beautiful natural parks, Americus has something for everyone. This guide will provide you with an in-depth look at what Americus has to offer and help you plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Americus (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Windsor Hotel

Built-in 1892, the Windsor Hotel is a historic landmark in Americus. It’s been beautifully restored to its original grandeur and is a must-visit destination for those interested in history and architecture. Take a guided tour of the hotel to learn more about its rich history.

The Rylander Theatre

The Rylander Theatre is a beautifully restored 1920s-era theater that features plays, concerts, and other live performances. Take a tour of the theater or catch a show while you’re in town.

Habitat for Humanity’s Global Village & Discovery Center

Habitat for Humanity’s Global Village & Discovery Center is an educational exhibit that highlights the work of Habitat for Humanity in over 70 countries worldwide. It’s an inspiring and educational destination that’s definitely worth a visit.

Andersonville National Historic Site

Andersonville National Historic Site preserves the site of a former Confederate prison camp during the Civil War. Visitors can tour the camp, see the National Prisoner of War Museum, and learn about this important moment in American history.

Natural Attractions of Americus (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Blackshear Resort and Golf Club

Lake Blackshear Resort and Golf Club is a beautiful destination that offers a variety of outdoor activities, including boating, fishing, hiking, and golfing. Stay overnight in one of the resort’s comfortable accommodations and enjoy the beautiful scenery that surrounds the lake.

Georgia Veterans State Park

Georgia Veterans State Park is a 1,308-acre park that offers visitors a wide range of recreational options, including camping, fishing, hiking, and swimming. It’s also home to the Georgia Veterans Memorial Golf Course, which is one of the top-rated public courses in the state.

Plains Peanut Festival

If you’re visiting Americus in late September or early October, be sure to check out the Plains Peanut Festival. This annual event celebrates the peanut harvest with live music, food vendors, arts and crafts, and a parade.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Americus (Georgia)

The Americus-Sumter Trail

The Americus-Sumter Trail is a 9-mile paved trail that connects Americus to nearby Sumter County. It’s ideal for both hiking and biking and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

The Lake Blackshear Trail

The Lake Blackshear Trail is a 2.2-mile trail that runs between Lake Blackshear Resort and Georgia Veterans Memorial State Park. It offers stunning views of the lake and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Americus (Georgia): Activities and Tours

The Americus Downtown Walking Tour

Take a self-guided walking tour of downtown Americus to learn more about the city’s history and architecture. The tour includes stops at the historic Windsor Hotel, Rylander Theatre, and several other historic landmarks.

The Americus Trolley Tour

If you prefer a guided tour, hop on the Americus Trolley Tour. This 90-minute tour takes you through the city’s historic districts and includes stops at several important landmarks and attractions.

The Peanut Butter Trail

If you’re a fan of peanut butter, don’t miss the Peanut Butter Trail. This self-guided tour takes you to several important landmarks in the life of former President Jimmy Carter, including his boyhood home and the Plains Peanut Butter Company.

Week-Long Itinerary for Americus (Georgia)

Day 1: Arrive in Americus and check into your hotel.

Day 2: Visit the Andersonville National Historic Site and the National POW Museum.

Day 3: Take a walking tour of downtown Americus and visit the Windsor Hotel.

Day 4: Explore the Georgia Veterans State Park and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and boating.

Day 5: Take the Americus Trolley Tour to see the city’s historic landmarks.

Day 6: Visit the Habitat for Humanity Global Village & Discovery Center and learn more about this important organization.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the amenities at Lake Blackshear Resort and Golf Club.

Food and Drink Scene of Americus (Georgia)

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Americus is complete without trying some of the local specialties. Be sure to try items like boiled peanuts, fried chicken, and peach cobbler.

Local Beverages

If you’re a fan of craft beer, be sure to check out the offerings from the local brewery, The Museum. They offer a wide variety of styles, from a traditional Irish red ale to a hoppy double IPA.

Where to Stay in Americus (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Windsor Hotel

The Windsor Hotel is a historic landmark in Americus that has been beautifully restored to its original grandeur. It offers luxurious accommodations and top-notch amenities, including an on-site restaurant and fitness center.

Lake Blackshear Resort and Golf Club

Lake Blackshear Resort and Golf Club offers a range of accommodations, from cozy lodge rooms to spacious cottages. Its location on the banks of Lake Blackshear makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Americus Garden Inn Bed and Breakfast

If you’re looking for a more intimate lodging experience, check out the Americus Garden Inn Bed and Breakfast. This charming inn offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Americus (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Be sure to pick up some souvenirs to remember your trip to Americus. Popular items include boiled peanuts, local honey, and handmade crafts from the Americus-Sumter County Art Council.

Local Culture and Customs of Americus (Georgia)

The people of Americus are known for their friendliness and hospitality. Be sure to say hello to locals as you explore the city.

Best Time to Visit Americus (Georgia)

The best time to visit Americus is in the mild spring or fall, when temperatures are comfortable and crowd sizes are not at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Americus (Georgia)

Do:

  • Say hello to locals as you explore the city
  • Try the local specialties, including boiled peanuts and fried chicken
  • Take a walking tour of downtown Americus

Don’t:

  • Forget to check out the Peanut Butter Trail if you’re a fan of peanut butter
  • Miss the opportunity to explore the natural beauty that surrounds Americus
  • Ignore the city’s rich history and architecture

Safety and Health

Safety in Americus (Georgia)

Americus is a safe city, but like any urban area, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

Health in Americus (Georgia)

There are several hospitals and medical centers in Americus, making it easy to access quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Americus (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Americus (Georgia)

The closest airport to Americus is the Southwest Georgia Regional Airport, which is located in Albany, GA, about 40 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Americus (Georgia)

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Americus, making it easy to travel to and from the city by bus.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Americus can easily be accessed via major highways like I-75 and I-10.

How to Get Around in Americus (Georgia)

The best way to get around Americus is by car, although bicycles are also a popular option for some. Taxis and rideshare services are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Americus (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savannah

Savannah, Georgia is a beautiful coastal city that’s known for its historic architecture and rich southern culture. It’s about a 3-hour drive from Americus.

Atlanta

Atlanta, Georgia is a bustling metropolis that’s home to top-notch museums, shopping, and dining. It’s about a 2.5-hour drive from Americus.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or just relaxing in a beautiful setting, Americus has something to offer. Use this guide to plan your perfect trip and discover all that this charming city has to offer.
