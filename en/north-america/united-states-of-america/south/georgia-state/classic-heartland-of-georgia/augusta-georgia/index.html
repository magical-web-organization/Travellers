Introduction to Augusta (Georgia)

Augusta, located in the Classic Heartland of Georgia, is a city that combines a rich history with modern amenities and attractions. Known as the “Garden City”, Augusta boasts over 50 public parks and green spaces, making it a nature lover’s paradise. From museums to outdoor adventures, Augusta has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Augusta (Georgia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Augusta has a rich history, and visitors can explore it firsthand by visiting the numerous museums and historical places in the city. Some of the top cultural attractions in Augusta include:

  1. Augusta Museum of History - This museum is dedicated to preserving and celebrating the history of Augusta and the Central Savannah River Area.

  2. The Lucy Craft Laney Museum of Black History - This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and legacy of the African American experience in Augusta and the surrounding area.

  3. The Boyhood Home of President Woodrow Wilson - This house museum is where President Wilson spent his formative years and features exhibits and artifacts from his life and presidency.

Natural Attractions of Augusta (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Augusta is surrounded by natural beauty, and visitors can explore this pristine landscape through the city’s various parks and outdoor adventures. Some of the top natural attractions in Augusta include:

  1. Augusta Canal National Heritage Area - This historic canal offers opportunities for hiking, biking, and kayaking.

  2. Phinizy Swamp Nature Park - This nature preserve offers hiking trails and birdwatching opportunities.

  3. The Savannah River - Visitors can fish, kayak, or take a scenic boat ride along this historic waterway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Augusta (Georgia)

Augusta offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Some of the top routes include:

  1. The Augusta Canal Trail - This 7.5-mile trail runs along the historic Augusta Canal and offers scenic views and opportunities for hiking and biking.

  2. The North Augusta Greeneway - This 7.5-mile trail connects North Augusta to Augusta and features beautiful views of the Savannah River.

  3. The Riverwalk Augusta - This 1.5-mile trail runs along the Savannah River and offers views of downtown Augusta.

Best Things to Do in Augusta (Georgia): Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Augusta offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the top things to do in Augusta include:

  1. Visit the Augusta National Golf Club - This world-renowned golf course hosts the annual Masters Tournament and is a must-see for any golf enthusiast.

  2. Take a Segway Tour of Augusta - This unique tour allows visitors to explore Augusta’s historic downtown on a Segway.

  3. Visit the Augusta Riverwalk Marina - This marina offers boat tours, fishing charters, and other water-based activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Augusta (Georgia)

Visitors to Augusta can easily spend a week exploring all the city has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Augusta Museum of History and take a stroll through Augusta’s historic Summerville neighborhood.

  • Day 2: Explore Augusta’s parks and outdoor spaces, including the Phinizy Swamp Nature Park and the Augusta Canal National Heritage Area.

  • Day 3: Take a Segway tour of Augusta’s downtown and explore the city’s shops and restaurants.

  • Day 4: Visit the Boyhood Home of President Woodrow Wilson and learn about his life and presidency.

  • Day 5: Play a round of golf at the Augusta National Golf Club or watch a tournament practice round.

  • Day 6: Take a scenic boat ride along the Savannah River or explore the riverfront on foot.

  • Day 7: Visit the Lucy Craft Laney Museum of Black History and learn about the African American experience in Augusta.

Food and Drink Scene of Augusta (Georgia)

No trip to Augusta is complete without trying some of the city’s delicious food and drink. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried Chicken - Augusta is known for its delicious fried chicken, and visitors can find it at numerous local restaurants.

  2. Barbecue - Augusta has a thriving barbecue scene, and visitors can sample a variety of styles and flavors.

  3. Peach Cobbler - Georgia is known for its peaches, and Augusta’s peach cobbler does not disappoint.

Local Beverages

  1. Sweet Tea - This southern classic is a must-try in Augusta.

  2. Craft Beer - Augusta has a growing craft beer scene, and visitors can sample local brews at numerous breweries.

  3. Pecan Ridge Wine - This local winery offers tastings and tours of its vineyards and wine-making facilities.

Where to Stay in Augusta (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Augusta offers a variety of accommodations for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top options include:

  1. The Partridge Inn Augusta - This historic hotel offers beautiful views of the city and luxurious accommodations.

  2. Augusta Downtown Hostel - This budget-friendly hostel offers dorm-style accommodations and a communal kitchen.

  3. Rosenwald Cottage Guest House - This charming guesthouse is located in Augusta’s historic Summerville neighborhood.

What to Buy in Augusta (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Augusta can bring home a piece of the city’s history and culture by purchasing local souvenirs and products. Some top options include:

  1. Augusta-themed apparel and accessories - Visitors can find t-shirts, hats, and other items showcasing Augusta’s landmarks and attractions.

  2. Pecans and other local foods - Georgia is known for its delicious pecans, and visitors can bring home a bag of these sweet treats.

  3. Hand-crafted pottery and ceramics - Augusta has a thriving arts scene, and visitors can find unique, hand-crafted ceramics and pottery at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Augusta (Georgia)

Augusta has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by its history and southern traditions. Visitors should be respectful of these customs and may want to learn more about the city’s history and culture through visiting its museums and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Augusta (Georgia)

The best time to visit Augusta is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. However, visitors can enjoy Augusta’s attractions year-round, with summer being the busiest tourist season due to the Masters Tournament.

Do’s and Don’ts of Augusta (Georgia)

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including fried chicken and barbecue.

  • Do take a stroll through Augusta’s parks and green spaces.

  • Do explore the city’s museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage Augusta’s natural areas.

  • Don’t be disrespectful of Augusta’s cultural heritage.

  • Don’t forget to try a glass of sweet tea.

Safety and Health

Safety in Augusta (Georgia)

As with any city, visitors to Augusta should be aware of their surroundings and take common safety precautions. It is also recommended to avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Augusta (Georgia)

Visitors to Augusta should bring any necessary prescription medications and practice good hygiene. It is also recommended to stay hydrated and protect against the sun’s rays during outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Augusta (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Augusta can continue their southern adventure by exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Some top options include:

Conclusion

Augusta (Georgia) is a city that combines history, culture, and outdoor adventure. From museums and historical sites to hiking and biking routes, there is something to see and do for every visitor. Don’t forget to try the delicious southern cuisine and sweet tea, and take in the beautiful natural surroundings.
