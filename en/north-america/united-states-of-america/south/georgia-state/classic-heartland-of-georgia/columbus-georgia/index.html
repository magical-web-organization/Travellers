Introduction to Columbus (Georgia)

Columbus, located in Classic Heartland of Georgia, is a great destination for travelers looking for a combination of history, outdoor activities, and Southern charm. From museums and historical sites to scenic parks and hiking trails, Columbus has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do, see, and experience in Columbus.

List of Places to Visit in Columbus (Georgia)

Whether you’re interested in history or outdoor adventures, Columbus has a lot of places to explore. Here’s a list of must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Infantry Museum and Soldier Center: This museum is dedicated to telling the story of the U.S. Infantryman and features exhibits, tours, and events.
  • Columbus Museum: Explore the history and art of the Chattahoochee Valley region in this museum, which also houses a planetarium and a historic house.
  • The Springer Opera House: This historic opera house has been in operation since 1871 and is known for its beautiful architecture and productions.
  • Columbus, Georgia Historic District: Take a walk through this 19th-century district, which includes antebellum homes, monuments, and museums.

Natural Attractions of Columbus (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chattahoochee RiverWalk: Take in views of the river and enjoy outdoor activities along this 15-mile trail.
  • Lake Harding: Go fishing, boating, or swimming in this 5,850-acre lake.
  • Providence Canyon State Park: Known as the “Little Grand Canyon,” this park features unique rock formations and hiking trails.
  • Flat Rock Park: This park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds, making it a great spot for families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbus (Georgia)

If you love hiking or biking, Columbus has some beautiful trails that showcase the region’s natural beauty. Here are some routes to consider:

  • Columbus Fall Line Trace: This 11-mile trail can be used for walking, running, biking, and skating.
  • Dragonfly Trail: This three-mile trail offers scenic views of North Columbus and winds through forests and wetlands.
  • Standing Boy Creek State Park: This park has over 25 miles of trails for hiking and biking, as well as fishing and camping.

Best Things to Do in Columbus (Georgia): Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun and unique things to do in Columbus. Here are some activities and tours to consider:

  • WhiteWater Express: Go white-water rafting down the Chattahoochee River.
  • The Escape Game: Solve puzzles and clues to escape from one of several themed rooms.
  • Ride on the Historic Columbus Railway: Take a scenic ride along the Chattahoochee River on a vintage passenger train.
  • Visit the Market Days on Broadway: This weekly event brings together farmers, artisans, and food vendors for a lively street market.

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbus (Georgia)

If you’re planning on spending a week in Columbus, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: National Infantry Museum and Soldier Center
  • Day 2: Columbus Museum and planetarium
  • Day 3: Whitewater rafting on the Chattahoochee River
  • Day 4: Standing Boy Creek State Park for hiking and biking
  • Day 5: Market Days on Broadway
  • Day 6: Explore the historic district and The Springer Opera House
  • Day 7: Lake Harding for fishing and swimming

Food and Drink Scene of Columbus (Georgia)

Columbus has a rich culinary scene, offering everything from Southern classics to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Green Tomatoes: This Southern classic is a must-try in Columbus.
  • Brunswick Stew: A hearty stew with vegetables and meat, Brunswick stew is a perfect dish to enjoy on a cool evening.
  • Peach Cobbler: Georgia is known for its peaches, and peach cobbler is a delicious way to enjoy them.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple, sweet tea is a must-try in Columbus.
  • Chattabrewchee Southern Brewhouse: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers with Southern twists.
  • Muscogee Kryptonite: This cocktail, made with local ingredients, is a favorite among Columbus locals.

Where to Stay in Columbus (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Columbus has plenty of options when it comes to accommodations. Here are some popular choices:

  • Columbus Marriott: This upscale hotel is located in downtown Columbus and offers a fitness center, outdoor pool, and onsite restaurant.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Columbus: This hotel is located near the Columbus Airport and offers free shuttle service, breakfast, and wifi.
  • Rothschild-Pound House Inn: This historic bed and breakfast, located in the historic district, offers charm and comfort.

What to Buy in Columbus (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to bring home from Columbus? Here are some ideas:

  • Chattahoochee River Gear: Pick up a t-shirt, hat, or other gear featuring the Chattahoochee River.
  • Moon Lake Trading Company: This store offers a variety of handmade items, including pottery, jewelry, and clothing.
  • Iron Bank Coffee: Stock up on locally roasted coffee beans to bring home and enjoy later.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbus (Georgia)

Columbus is known for its Southern hospitality and charm. When visiting, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Southern manners: Saying “please” and “thank you” and being polite goes a long way in Columbus.
  • Dress code: Southern style tends to be more formal, so it’s a good idea to dress up a bit when dining out or attending events.
  • Southern food culture: Southern cuisine is a big part of Columbus culture, so be sure to try some local dishes during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Columbus (Georgia)

The best time to visit Columbus is in spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can get chilly. Be sure to check the weather forecast before your trip and pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbus (Georgia)

When visiting Columbus, here are some things to do and avoid:

Do:

  • Explore the historic district
  • Go white-water rafting on the Chattahoochee River
  • Try local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  • Miss the National Infantry Museum and Soldier Center
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions
  • Drive under the influence of alcohol

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbus (Georgia)

Columbus is generally a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night
  • Keep valuables out of sight and locked in your car
  • Use caution when walking or biking on busy streets

Health in Columbus (Georgia)

It’s important to stay healthy while traveling. Here are some health tips for your visit to Columbus:

  • Stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months
  • Wear sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun
  • Pack any prescription medications you may need

Getting to Columbus (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Columbus is easily accessible from other parts of Georgia and beyond. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Columbus (Georgia)

  • Columbus Metropolitan Airport (CSG)
  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL)

Bus Stations Near Columbus (Georgia)

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Southeastern Stages

Other Options

  • Rental cars
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services

How to Get Around in Columbus (Georgia)

The best way to get around Columbus is by car, but there are other options:

  • Walk: Many attractions in Columbus are within walking distance of one another.
  • Bike: The city has bike lanes and trails, making it easy to get around on two wheels.
  • Public Transportation: Chattahoochee Valley Transit Authority offers bus service in the area.

Where to Go Next from Columbus (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Columbus is a great starting point for exploring other parts of Georgia and the South. Here are some destinations to consider:

  • Callaway Gardens: This beautiful nature reserve is located about an hour from Columbus and features gardens, hiking trails, and a beach.
  • Atlanta: Georgia’s capital city is located about 100 miles north of Columbus and is known for its history, culture, and nightlife.
  • Auburn, Alabama: This college town is located about 30 miles west of Columbus and is home to Auburn University.

Conclusion

Columbus, Georgia is a great destination for travelers looking for a mix of history, outdoor adventures, and Southern charm. Whether you’re interested in museums and historical sites, scenic parks and hiking trails, or delicious Southern cuisine, Columbus has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip and to make the most of your visit to this charming city.
