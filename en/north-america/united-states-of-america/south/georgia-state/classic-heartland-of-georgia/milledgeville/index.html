Looking for a peaceful and cultural escape in Georgia? Milledgeville, a small city located in the Classic Heartland region of Georgia, could be just what you’re looking for. Known for its rich history, lovely architecture, pleasant climate, and natural beauty, Milledgeville has something to offer everyone, from history buffs to outdoor adventurers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the best things to do, see, eat, and stay in Milledgeville, and give you all the important information you need to plan your trip.

Introduction to Milledgeville

Founded in 1803 as the capital city of Georgia, Milledgeville served as the center of state government until 1868. The city played an important role in the Civil War and Reconstruction, and also in the development of higher education, as it was the home of several colleges and universities, including the first state-supported female college in the United States, Georgia Female College (now known as Georgia College & State University). Today, Milledgeville is a lively and charming city with a population of approximately 19,000, where history and culture blend seamlessly with nature and modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Milledgeville

To help you plan your Milledgeville trip, here’s a list of the must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Governor’s Mansion: Built in 1839, this National Historic Landmark served as the residence of Georgia’s governors until 1868. The mansion is a prime example of Greek Revival architecture and features elegant interiors, beautiful gardens, and many original furnishings.

  • Georgia’s Old Capital Museum: Located in the former State Capitol building of Georgia, this museum offers a glimpse into the city’s political and social history, with exhibits on the Civil War, Reconstruction, and the capital city’s move to Atlanta.

  • Lockerly Arboretum: A must-visit for nature lovers, this historic garden and natural preserve features a diverse collection of native and exotic plants, including azaleas, magnolias, camellias, and Japanese maples. The Arboretum is also home to a stunning 1852 Greek Revival mansion, which is open to visitors for tours.

Natural Attractions of Milledgeville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oconee River Greenway: Stretching over three miles along the Oconee River, this popular recreational area offers scenic views, picnic spots, playgrounds, and miles of riverside trails for walking, running, biking, or fishing.

  • Bartram Forest: A 2,500-acre natural reserve located just outside Milledgeville, this area offers opportunities for hiking, bird-watching, and camping.

  • Rock Hawk Effigy and Trails: A unique and fascinating archaeological site, Rock Hawk features six stone animal effigies (including a stunning eagle effigy) dating back to 2,000 years ago, as well as a network of scenic trails for hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milledgeville

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love the hiking and biking options in and around Milledgeville. Here are a few top picks:

  • Central State Hospital Trail: A 7.5-mile, multi-use trail that circles several scenic lakes and through a wooded area, offering opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Bartram Forest Trail: A 10-mile, single-track trail through the Bartram Forest area, featuring challenging climbs and scenic vistas.

  • Greenway Trail: A 1.7-mile paved trail along the Oconee River Greenway, offering easy biking and walking and great views.

Best Things to Do in Milledgeville: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Milledgeville, whether you want to soak up some culture or enjoy some outdoor fun. Here are a few top picks:

  • Haunted Trolley Tour: A unique and spooky way to explore the city’s history, this tour takes you on a journey through Milledgeville’s ghost stories and superstitions.

  • Lunch and Learn: Hosted by Georgia College & State University, this free monthly event features a lunchtime lecture by a guest speaker on a wide range of cultural and educational topics.

  • Andalusia Farm: The home of famous author Flannery O’Connor, Andalusia Farm is a picturesque and peaceful retreat in Milledgeville’s countryside, where you can take a guided tour and learn more about O’Connor’s life and work.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milledgeville

If you’re planning a longer stay in Milledgeville, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Old Governor’s Mansion and stroll around downtown Milledgeville.
  • Day 2: Explore the Georgia’s Old Capital Museum, and take a walking tour of the historic district.
  • Day 3: Spend the day outdoors at the Oconee River Greenway, and enjoy a picnic lunch.
  • Day 4: Take a haunted trolley tour of the city in the evening.
  • Day 5: Go on a hiking or biking trail at the Bartram Forest.
  • Day 6: Visit Andalusia Farm and take a guided tour.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at the Lockerly Arboretum and the adjacent Rose Hill.

Food and Drink Scene of Milledgeville

Milledgeville boasts a diverse culinary scene that’s sure to satisfy all palates. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pimento Cheese: A Southern staple, pimento cheese is a spread made from cheese, mayonnaise, and pimento peppers. Try it at The Brick, a popular local restaurant.

  • Fried Catfish: Another Southern favorite, fried catfish is crispy, flavorful, and served with a side of hushpuppies and coleslaw. Head to Aubri Lane’s, a casual seafood restaurant, for the best catfish in town.

  • Sweet Potato Fries: A healthier alternative to regular fries, sweet potato fries are crispy, sweet, and delicious. Order them at Blackbird Coffee, a cozy café in the heart of downtown.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A hallmark of Southern hospitality, sweet tea is a cold and refreshing drink made with black tea and sugar. You’ll find it everywhere in Milledgeville, but try it at Buffington’s, a charming tea house.

  • Craft Beer: Milledgeville has a burgeoning craft beer scene, with several local breweries offering unique and tasty brews. Try the Dirty South IPA at Oconee Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Milledgeville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Milledgeville has a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to historic bed and breakfasts. Here are a few top picks:

  • Antebellum Inn: A charming and elegant bed and breakfast located in a historic Victorian mansion, the Antebellum Inn offers old-world charm and modern amenities, including a pool and hot tub.

  • Days Inn by Wyndham: A chain hotel located just outside downtown, the Days Inn offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and easy access to nearby attractions.

  • Milledgeville Bed and Breakfast: Another historic and cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of downtown, the Milledgeville B&B offers charming rooms, a porch, and a library.

What to Buy in Milledgeville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and authentic souvenirs to bring home from Milledgeville, here are some suggestions:

  • Sweetgrass Baskets: Made from woven sweetgrass, these baskets are a Southern tradition and make for beautiful and functional home decor.

  • Moonshine Jelly: Made from locally-distilled moonshine, this spicy and sweet jelly is a favorite among visitors looking for a taste of the South.

  • Georgia Grown Produce: Milledgeville is surrounded by fertile farmland that produces a variety of fruits and vegetables, including peaches, pecans, and sweet potatoes. Look for these and other locally grown products at the Milledgeville Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Milledgeville

Milledgeville residents are known for their friendly and welcoming Southern hospitality and pride in their city’s rich history and culture. Visitors should respect the city’s historic landmarks, natural areas, and local customs, such as saying “y’all” and tipping generously.

Best Time to Visit Milledgeville

Milledgeville enjoys a mild and temperate climate year-round, with hot and humid summers and cool winters. Spring and fall are the best times to visit, as the weather is mild and pleasant, and there are many outdoor festivals and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milledgeville

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do take a walking tour of the city’s historic district.
  • Do visit the natural areas surrounding the city.

  • Don’t forget to tip generously.

Safety and Health

Safety in Milledgeville

Milledgeville is generally a safe city, but visitors should exercise the usual caution and common sense, such as avoiding unlit areas after dark, locking their car doors, and watching their belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Milledgeville

Milledgeville has several hospitals and medical clinics, including the Oconee Regional Medical Center and the Carl Vinson VA Medical Center.

Getting to Milledgeville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Milledgeville is located in the center of Georgia and is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Milledgeville

The nearest major airport to Milledgeville is Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is approximately 90 miles west of the city.

Bus Stations Near Milledgeville

Greyhound operates several bus routes to and from Milledgeville, with the nearest station located in Macon, approximately 30 miles west of the city.

Other Options

Amtrak trains stop in the nearby cities of Atlanta and Gainesville, while several rental car companies operate in Milledgeville.

How to Get Around in Milledgeville

Milledgeville is a small and walkable city, but visitors may also want to rent a car or use public transportation. The Oconee Regional Transit Authority operates several bus routes in and around the city, while several car rental companies, such as Enterprise and Thrifty, operate in Milledgeville.

Where to Go Next from Milledgeville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to extend your trip to Georgia, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Macon: Located just 30 miles west of Milledgeville, Macon is a vibrant and historic city known for its music, arts, and culture.

  • Oconee National Forest: Located just outside Milledgeville, this vast wilderness area offers hiking, camping, fishing, and hunting opportunities.

  • Atlanta: Georgia’s capital city is approximately 90 miles west of Milledgeville and offers endless entertainment, dining, and shopping options.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and Southern charm, Milledgeville is a wonderful destination for anyone looking for a relaxing and cultural getaway.
