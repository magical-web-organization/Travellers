Introduction to Jekyll Island

Located in Coastal Georgia, Jekyll Island is a paradise for nature lovers and history buffs. The island has a rich cultural heritage and offers a range of outdoor activities, making it a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. The island is also home to some luxurious golf courses, making it a go-to destination for golf enthusiasts. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip to Jekyll Island.

List of Places to Visit in Jekyll Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jekyll Island Museum

The Jekyll Island Museum offers visitors a glimpse into the island’s history. The museum is housed in a restored cottage and features exhibits on everything from the island’s Native American heritage to its role as a playground for the rich and famous.

Mosaic Jekyll Island Museum

The Mosaic Jekyll Island Museum is dedicated to telling the story of the African American experience on the island. Visitors can explore exhibits on everything from slavery to the Civil Rights Movement.

Horton House

The Horton House is one of the oldest surviving buildings on Jekyll Island. Built-in 1742, the house was once home to Major William Horton, who was stationed on the island as part of the British military. Today, visitors can explore the house and learn about the island’s early history.

Natural Attractions of Jekyll Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Driftwood Beach

Driftwood Beach is a must-visit attraction on Jekyll Island. The beach is famous for its beautiful driftwood trees, which have been sculpted into fascinating shapes by the ocean’s tides. Visitors can stroll along the beach, relax on the sandy shore, and soak in the spectacular views.

Jekyll Island State Park

Jekyll Island State Park covers the southern half of the island and offers visitors a range of outdoor activities. The park has miles of hiking and biking trails, kayaking, fishing, and wildlife tours.

Georgia Sea Turtle Center

The Georgia Sea Turtle Center is a great place to learn about sea turtles and their conservation. Visitors can explore exhibits, meet live turtles, and learn about the center’s rescue and rehabilitation efforts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jekyll Island

Jekyll Island has miles of hiking and biking trails, offering visitors the perfect opportunity to explore the island’s natural beauty on foot or by bike. Here are some popular routes:

Jekyll Island Trail System

The Jekyll Island Trail System is a network of paved and unpaved trails that covers more than 20 miles. The trails wind through marshes, forests, and along the water’s edge, offering hikers and bikers stunning views of nature.

Beach Loop Trail

The Beach Loop Trail is a 3.5-mile loop that takes visitors through some of the island’s most beautiful beaches. The trail is easy to navigate and is suitable for all fitness levels.

Best Things to Do in Jekyll Island: Activities and Tours

Dolphin Tours

One of the island’s most popular activities is a dolphin tour. Visitors can take a boat tour of the water around Jekyll Island and see dolphins playing in their natural habitat.

Golfing

Jekyll Island is famous for its golf courses, which are considered some of the best in the country. Visitors can choose from a range of courses, including the Great Dunes Course, the Indian Mound Course, and the Pine Lakes Course.

Island Horseback Riding

Visitors can explore Jekyll Island from a different perspective by going horseback riding along the beach. Experienced guides take visitors on horseback and ride along the water’s edge, offering views of the island’s beautiful coast.

Weekly Itinerary for Jekyll Island

Day 1: Start your day by exploring the Jekyll Island Museum and learning about the island’s history. Afterward, head to the Georgia Sea Turtle Center and learn about the center’s conservation efforts. In the evening, take a sunset dolphin tour.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring Jekyll Island State Park. Hike or bike the island’s scenic trails, take a kayak tour of the water, or go fishing in one of the many waterways.

Day 3: Start your day by visiting the Horton House, one of the oldest surviving buildings on Jekyll Island. Afterward, head to Driftwood Beach and spend the afternoon relaxing on the shore and soaking in the spectacular views.

Day 4: Spend the day golfing at one of the island’s many luxurious golf courses. Afterward, head to one of the island’s many restaurants and enjoy some of the local seafood cuisine.

Day 5: Take an island horseback riding tour and explore the island from a different perspective. In the evening, head to the Mosaic Jekyll Island Museum and learn about the African American community’s history on the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Jekyll Island

Must-Try Dishes

Low Country Boil

A Low Country Boil is a traditional seafood dish that includes crab, shrimp, sausage, and corn. It’s a must-try dish when visiting Jekyll Island.

Oysters Rockefeller

Oysters Rockefeller is a classic dish that originated in New Orleans but can be found on many Jekyll Island menus. It’s a dish made with oysters, spinach, and a delicious butter sauce.

Local Beverages

Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a staple beverage in the South. It’s a delicious and refreshing drink to enjoy while exploring the island.

Peach Cobbler Martini

The Peach Cobbler Martini is a popular drink served in many Jekyll Island restaurants. It’s a cocktail that tastes like a dessert and is perfect for those who enjoy sweet drinks.

Where to Stay in Jekyll Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jekyll Island Club Resort

The Jekyll Island Club Resort is a historic hotel that dates back to 1886. The hotel is located in the heart of Jekyll Island and offers visitors luxurious accommodations, fine dining, and a range of amenities and activities.

Westin Jekyll Island

The Westin Jekyll Island is a modern hotel that offers visitors beautiful ocean views and luxurious accommodations. The hotel is located on the beach and is perfect for those who want to spend their days soaking up the sun.

What to Buy in Jekyll Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jekyll Island T-Shirts

A Jekyll Island T-Shirt is a great souvenir to bring home. There are many different designs to choose from, so you’re sure to find one that suits your style.

Seafood Spices

One thing Jekyll Island is known for is its delicious seafood cuisine. Bring home some of the island’s seafood spices, and you can recreate some of those delicious seafood dishes in your own kitchen.

Local Culture and Customs of Jekyll Island

The people of Jekyll Island are warm and friendly, and visitors are always welcome. However, it’s important to respect the island’s natural beauty and to follow the leave no trace policy.

Best Time to Visit Jekyll Island

The best time to visit Jekyll Island is from March to May when the weather is mild and the island is in full bloom. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, consider visiting in the off-season when prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jekyll Island

Do:

  • Explore the island’s natural beauty by hiking or biking the trails.
  • Respect the island’s wildlife by following the leave no trace policy.
  • Try the local seafood cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or trails.
  • Ignore warning signs and venture into unsafe areas.
  • Disturb the island’s natural beauty by taking souvenirs or disturbing the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jekyll Island

Jekyll Island is a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and to take appropriate safety precautions, such as locking your car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Jekyll Island

Jekyll Island has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. However, it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medication with you when traveling.

Getting to Jekyll Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jekyll Island

The closest airport to Jekyll Island is Brunswick Golden Isles Airport, which is located just a few miles away.

Bus Stations Near Jekyll Island

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus station in Brunswick, which is just a few miles away from Jekyll Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Jekyll Island by car or by taking a taxi or rideshare service from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Jekyll Island

Visitors can explore Jekyll Island by rental car, bike, or on foot. There are also taxis and rideshare services available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Jekyll Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Jekyll Island can consider exploring, including:

  • Savannah, Georgia
  • St. Simons Island, Georgia
  • Cumberland Island, Georgia

Conclusion

Jekyll Island is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, delicious food, and a rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious golf getaway or an outdoor adventure, Jekyll Island has something to offer everyone. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to this beautiful island paradise.
