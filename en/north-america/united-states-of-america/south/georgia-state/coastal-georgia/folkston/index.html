Introduction to Folkston

Folkston is a small city located in Coastal Georgia in the southern region of the United States. It prides itself on its rich history, charming downtown, and the Okefenokee National Wildlife Refuge just a short drive away. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or looking for a relaxing getaway, Folkston is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Folkston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Folkston Railroad Transportation Museum: Discover the history and significance of the railroad industry in Folkston and its impact on the town’s growth and development.
  • Charlton County Courthouse and Square: Admire the beautiful architecture and learn about the county’s history at the courthouse, which has been on the National Register of Historic Places since 1980.
  • St. George Museum: Explore the unique history and culture of Folkston’s neighboring town, St. George, through exhibits that feature artifacts, photos, and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Folkston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Okefenokee National Wildlife Refuge: Discover the beautiful landscape of the Okefenokee Swamp, home to over 400 species of wildlife, including alligators and black bears. You can hike, kayak or even go on a guided boat tour to see it all.
  • Stephen C. Foster State Park: This park is named after a famous songwriter, offers a variety of outdoor activities, such as fishing, boating, camping and hiking trails. You can also rent boats and canoes to explore the waterways.
  • Railroad Trail Park: This park is located in the heart of Folkston and offers a walking trail that provides scenic views of the town and the Historic Tower.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Folkston

  • Okefenokee Swamp Park Trails: With over 120 miles of trails, explore the wetlands of Folkston on foot, or even take a bicycle tour on the trails.
  • Laura S. Walker State Park: Experience the sights and sounds of nature while taking any of the 4 hiking trails available varying in difficulty. Some trails also offer fishing opportunities while relaxing and observing the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Folkston: Activities and Tours

  • Canoe/Kayak Tours: Multiple tour companies offer guided tours through the winding rivers of the Okefenokee Wildlife Refuge, providing an up-close look at the unique flora, fauna and wildlife of the area.
  • Train watching: Nature and Railroad enthusiasts must have their cameras ready to capture an unforgettable experience of trains passing through the Railroad platform.
  • First Friday Events: It is an energetic monthly outdoor event that takes place in Folkston’s historic downtown where shops are open late and local artists perform music on stage.

Food and Drink Scene of Folkston

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brunswick Stew: A hearty Southern stew made with lima beans, corn, okra, and meat (usually chicken or pork)
  • Shrimp and Grits: A classic Southern dish consisting of shrimp and white hominy grits with a variety of seasonings and toppings
  • Peach Cobbler: A delicious Southern dessert made with fresh peaches and a sweet biscuit-like topping

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple made with black tea and lots of sugar, served over ice
  • Pecan Pie Coffee: A delicious coffee with the flavors of pecan pie
  • Georgia Peach Wine: A sweet and fruity wine made from Georgia peaches

Where to Stay in Folkston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Western Motel Inn and Suites: This hotel offers affordable rates and a convenient location near downtown and the Okefenokee Swamp.
  • Cabin on the St. Mary’s River: A secluded cabin rental for those looking for peace and tranquility.
  • Private room in Folkston: Airbnb accommodations are also available for those looking for a more personalized and homey stay.

What to Buy in Folkston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pecans: Georgia is known for its pecans, so bring some home to share with friends and family.
  • Local Honey: Folkston is surrounded by many flower fields and nature reserves, so take a local bottle of honey to add some sweetness to your trip.
  • Stained Glass: Folkston has a variety of art galleries and craft stores that sell stained glass art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Folkston

Folkston prides itself on its rich history and Southern charm. You can see this in the architecture, the friendly locals, and various events throughout the town, like the yearly Folkston Funnel Festival.

Best Time to Visit Folkston

The best time to visit Folkston is during the Fall season from September to November. You can enjoy the beautiful colors of the foliage. Moreover, it is also not too hot and humid like the summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Folkston

Do:

  • Do try the local Southern cuisine
  • Do bring bug spray and sunscreen, especially during the hotter months
  • Do plan to visit the Okefenokee Swamp for an adventure always

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural wildlife resources of the area.
  • Don’t talk too much about politics or religion, as they can be divisive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Folkston

Folkston is a relatively safe small town, but as with any place, you should still be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions. If you are hiking or boating, make sure you have enough water and check weather reports before venturing out.

Health in Folkston

Folkston has health services available at the Charlton Memorial Hospital located in nearby Folkston city, providing both primary and specialty care services.

Getting to Folkston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Folkston

  • Jacksonville International Airport (JAX): About 50 minutes away in Jacksonville, Florida.
  • Brunswick Golden Isles Airport (BQK): About 1 hour away in Brunswick, Georgia.

Bus Stations Near Folkston

  • Greyhound: Located within 30 minutes from Folkston in Waycross, Georgia.

Other Options

Renting a car or taking a taxi from a nearby airport may be the best option. Train service is also available with Amtrak running its Silver Service/Palmetto between New York City and Miami, with a stop in nearby Jesup, Georgia.

How to Get Around in Folkston

Walking and biking are the best options to get around Folkston because everything is relatively close. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available to travel outside of town.

Where to Go Next from Folkston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St. Simon’s Island: This beautiful island features golden sand beaches, moss-draped oak trees, and fishing and boating opportunities just an hour away.
  • Jacksonville, Florida: Jacksonville is the largest city in Florida and is home to a variety of attractions like beaches, museums and parks, and art galleries, bookstores, and cozy cafes.

Conclusion

Folkston is a charming Southern city where you can explore the wilderness of Okefenokee Swamp and enjoy its rich culture and history. From outdoor adventures to delicious food and drink, Folkston has something for everyone. With this ultimate travel guide to Folkston, Georgia, you are sure to have an unforgettable experience.
