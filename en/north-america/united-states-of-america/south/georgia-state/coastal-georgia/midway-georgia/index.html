Introduction to Midway (Georgia)

Midway is a charming town nestled in Coastal Georgia, located in the southern region of the United States of America. The city’s rich history, combined with its natural beauty, makes it a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a Southern experience. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Midway (Georgia)

When it comes to places to visit, Midway has a lot to offer. Here are some of the top ones you wouldn’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Seabrook Village: This living history museum depicts life in Midway during the 19th century, with historical buildings, gardens, and exhibits.
  • Dorchester Academy: This historic school played a significant role in the civil rights movement, and now serves as a museum and community center.
  • Midway Museum: This museum tells the story of the city’s history, with exhibits on the American Revolution, the Civil War, and more.

Natural Attractions of Midway (Georgia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colonel’s Island: A popular spot for fishing, boating, and birdwatching, this island offers stunning views of the marshes and wildlife.
  • St. Catherine’s Island: This barrier island is known for its pristine beaches, salt marshes, and wildlife, including sea turtles and alligators.
  • Fort Morris Historic Site: This state park features a historic fort, as well as trails for hiking and camping opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Midway (Georgia)

Midway has a variety of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the top ones:

  • Colonel’s Island Trail: This 1.5-mile trail offers a scenic stroll through the marshes and wildlife.
  • LeConte-Woodmanston Plantation and Botanical Gardens Trail: This 2-mile trail takes you through the former plantation, which now serves as a botanical garden and wildlife sanctuary.
  • Fort Morris Nature Trail: This 0.6-mile trail leads to the Fort Morris State Park, with beautiful views of the area’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Midway (Georgia): Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more activities and tours, here are some to consider:

  • Kayaking or canoeing: Take a guided tour through the rivers and waterways, where you can spot wildlife and enjoy the scenery.
  • Golfing: Midway has several golf courses, including the Cherokee Rose Country Club and the Club at Savannah Harbor.
  • Ghost Tour: Discover the city’s haunted history on a walking tour, with spooky tales of the past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Midway (Georgia)

If you have a full week to explore Midway, here is a sample itinerary to make the most out of your trip:

Day Activities
1 Visit Seabrook Village and Dorchester Academy

Food and Drink Scene of Midway (Georgia)

Midway boasts its Southern charm with a variety of local restaurants and bars to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and Grits: A Southern staple, this dish features shrimp and grits served in a creamy sauce.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes: Another Southern classic, these tomatoes are breaded and fried, served with a dipping sauce.
  • Boiled Peanuts: A snack food popular in the South, boiled peanuts are soft and salty, and can be found in convenience stores.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A tea that is brewed with a lot of sugar, making it sweet and refreshing.
  • Mint Julep: This cocktail is made with mint, sugar, and bourbon, and is a classic Southern drink.
  • Georgia Peach: This cocktail is made with peach schnapps, vodka, and orange juice, and is a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Midway (Georgia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Midway has various options for accommodations. Here are some to consider:

  • The Inn at Midway: This cozy inn features a historic feel, with beautiful gardens and amenities like a pool and fitness center.
  • Holiday Inn Express Midway: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities like a free breakfast buffet and a fitness center.
  • Airbnb and Vacation Rentals: You can also find unique and affordable accommodations through Airbnb and other vacation rental websites.

What to Buy in Midway (Georgia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and products to bring home, here are some ideas:

  • Peach and pecan products: Georgia is known for its peaches and pecans, and you can find a variety of products, including jams, jellies, pies, and candies.
  • Handmade crafts: Midway has a variety of handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles, made by local artisans.
  • Civil Rights memorabilia: Since the city played a significant role in the civil rights movement, you can find various memorabilia and souvenirs related to this topic.

Local Culture and Customs of Midway (Georgia)

Midway has a rich history and culture, with influences from Southern and African-American traditions. Some customs and cultural events to look out for include:

  • Juneteenth Celebration: Held in June, this event commemorates the end of slavery, with music, food, and cultural events.
  • Gullah-Geechee Festival: This festival celebrates the Gullah-Geechee culture of the Coastal South, with music, food, and crafts.
  • Southern hospitality: Midway and the South, in general, are known for their hospitality, with locals welcoming visitors warmly and with kindness.

Best Time to Visit Midway (Georgia)

The best time to visit Midway is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is comfortable and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and dreary. Spring and early fall are also peak seasons for outdoor activities, including exploring the trails and waterways, making it an ideal time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Midway (Georgia)

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Midway:

Do:

  • Do try out different Southern foods and drinks, such as fried green tomatoes, peach cocktails, and sweet tea.
  • Do respect the city’s history and culture, and be open to learning about its significance in the civil rights movement.
  • Do take advantage of outdoor activities, including kayaking, fishing, and hiking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray, as Midway can be hot and humid, with plenty of mosquitoes.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public places, and respect the city’s natural beauty.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and bars, as tipping is customary in the United States.

Safety and Health

Safety in Midway (Georgia)

Midway is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always wise to take precautions, such as locking your doors at night and not leaving valuables in plain sight. Also, keep an eye out for natural hazards, such as hurricanes and thunderstorms, which can occur during the summer months.

Health in Midway (Georgia)

Midway, like the rest of the United States, has high-quality medical care and hospitals. Additionally, it’s important to be aware of the weather conditions and prepare accordingly, such as bringing sunscreen and staying hydrated.

Getting to Midway (Georgia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Midway is accessible by several means of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Midway (Georgia)

  • Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport: This airport is the closest major airport to Midway, located about 35 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Midway (Georgia)

  • Liberty Transit Station: This station connects Midway to other cities and towns in the region, including Savannah and Hinesville.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: You can rent a car at the airport or in nearby cities, making it easy to explore Midway and the surrounding areas.
  • Taxis and Rideshare: Several taxi and rideshare companies operate in the area, providing reliable transportation.

How to Get Around in Midway (Georgia)

Midway is a small town, so walking or biking is a great way to get around and explore. If you prefer driving, car rentals are available, and there are also taxis and rideshare options. Liberty Transit also provides public transportation in the region.

Where to Go Next from Midway (Georgia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Savannah: Just a short drive away, Savannah is known for its historic charm and Southern hospitality, with stunning architecture, museums, and restaurants.
  • Tybee Island: This barrier island is known for its beaches and lighthouse, as well as recreational activities like fishing and kayaking.
  • Jekyll Island: This island is a popular resort destination, with beautiful beaches, golf courses, and historic mansions.

Conclusion

Midway is a hidden gem in Coastal Georgia, offering a mix of history, natural beauty, and Southern hospitality.
