Introduction to Savannah

Savannah, Georgia is a charming city located in Coastal Georgia, known for its historic beauty, antebellum architecture, and thriving arts and culture scene. The city has a rich history, dating back to the 18th century, and is home to several noteworthy museums, monuments, and parks.

Beyond its historic landmarks, Savannah is also a bustling hub for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of scenic hiking and biking trails, picturesque beaches, and varied ecosystems, including marshlands and subtropical forests. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an adventurous escape, Savannah has something to offer.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the best things to do, places to see, food to try, and where to stay in Savannah. From cultural landmarks to natural attractions, local customs and traditions, and even transportation options, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Savannah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Savannah is renowned for its historic landmarks, dramatic architecture, and monuments to the past. From museums to parks to walking tours, there’s plenty to see and experience.

Some of the city’s top museums include:

  • Savannah History Museum: Located in the historic Central of Georgia Railway Station, this museum showcases Savannah’s 275-year history, with exhibits on local industries, wars, and famous residents.
  • Telfair Museums: Comprised of three distinct sites, Telfair Museums are home to a rich collection of American and European art, as well as decorative arts, photography, and contemporary art.
  • The Mercer Williams House Museum: Known for its associations with the Savannah socialite Jim Williams and as the setting for the book and movie “Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil,” this 1860 house offers a glimpse into antebellum Savannah society.
  • The Savannah African Art Museum: Showcasing the art and artifacts of the African continent, this museum includes works from more than 20 different countries and cultures.

For those interested in Savannah’s historic architecture, some top landmarks and places to visit include:

  • Forsyth Park: The centerpiece of Savannah’s Historic District, this 30-acre park is home to fountains, monuments, and plenty of open green space for picnics and people-watching.
  • Bonneventure Cemetery: One of Savannah’s most famous and picturesque cemeteries, Bonneventure is home to over 100,000 graves, including several notable people.
  • The Cathedral of St. John the Baptist: A stunning Gothic Revival cathedral dating back to 1876, this church boasts intricate stained glass windows, soaring arches, and an awe-inspiring pipe organ.
  • River Street: Located on the Savannah River, this cobblestone street is home to shopping, dining, and plenty of historic buildings.

Natural Attractions of Savannah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Savannah’s natural beauty is one of its biggest draws for outdoor enthusiasts. With plenty of hiking trails, waterways, and beaches to explore, there’s no shortage of adventures to be had.

Some top natural attractions include:

  • Savannah-Ogeechee Canal Museum and Nature Center: This historic site combines a museum on the history of the man-made Savannah-Ogeechee Canal with a nature preserve and hiking trails.
  • Skidaway Island State Park: A 588-acre park located on Skidaway Island, this park includes salt marshes, a boardwalk, hiking trails, and dozens of wildlife species, including bald eagles.
  • Wormsloe Historic Site: This plantation site dates back to 1736 and is home to the iconic avenue of oaks, a stunning 1.5-mile drive lined with massive oak trees draped in Spanish moss.
  • Tybee Island: Located just 20 minutes east of downtown Savannah, this laid-back island is home to gorgeous beaches, fishing charters, and plenty of water sports like kayaking and paddleboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savannah

With its flat terrain and beautiful scenery, Savannah and the surrounding areas provide ideal conditions for both hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore on foot or by bike:

  • The Savannah-Skiddaway Island Trail: A 6-mile bike path that runs from near the entrance to Skidaway Island State Park to the Isle of Hope Marina, passing through picturesque oak-lined streets and neighborhoods.
  • Fort Pulaski National Monument: A 5-mile loop trail that circles around the fort and through the surrounding wetlands, offering stunning views of the river.
  • Bonaventure Cemetery Loop: A 5-mile bike route that passes through the iconic Bonaventure Cemetery and along the Wilmington River, showcasing Bonaventure’s impressive mausoleums and other monuments.
  • Historic District Walking Tour: A 2.5-mile walking tour of the Historic District of Savannah, including major landmarks and points of interest, such as the Juliette Gordon Low Birthplace and Colonial Park Cemetery.

Best Things to Do in Savannah: Activities and Tours

Savannah is renowned for its art and culture scene, and there’s always plenty to see and do. From concerts to art exhibitions, guided tours to outdoor adventures, here are some top things to do in Savannah:

  • Savannah City Market: This open-air market features local artisan vendors, shops, and restaurants, and is a hot spot for foodies looking for local delicacies and Southern cuisine.
  • Savannah Ghost Tours: An eerie walking tour of Savannah’s historic homes and graveyards, night-time ghost tours transport visitors back to Savannah’s spooky past.
  • Forsyth Farmers’ Market: This producers-only market on Forsyth Park showcases local food and artisan goods, with fresh produce, honey, cheese, and much more.
  • Savannah Art Walk: A monthly event that takes place on the second Saturday of each month, showcasing local artists and galleries, with everything from painting to sculpture to photography.

Week-Long Itinerary for Savannah

If you’re planning to spend a week in Savannah, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your time.

Day 1: Arrive in Savannah, check into your hotel, grab lunch at a local restaurant, and take an afternoon stroll in Forsyth Park.

Day 2: Spend the morning taking a walking tour of the Historic District, then head to the Telfair Museums for an afternoon of art and culture.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Tybee Island, with plenty of time for beachcombing, fishing, and other water sports.

Day 4: Explore the historic architecture of Savannah’s southern neighborhoods, including the Mercer Williams House Museum and the Cathedral of St. John the Baptist.

Day 5: Hike the trails of Skidaway Island State Park, taking in the breathtaking scenery of coastal Georgia.

Day 6: Go shopping and explore Savannah’s local food scene at the Savannah City Market and Forsyth Farmers’ Market.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a sunset ghost tour, exploring Savannah’s eerie past and concluding with a delicious dinner at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Savannah

Savannah’s culinary scene is a highlight of any visit, with a blend of Southern and regional specialties, fresh seafood, and international flavors. Here’s what you need to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Low Country Boil: A classic Savannah dish, this hearty seafood boil features shrimp, crab, sausage, and vegetables.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes: A Southern classic, this dish features fresh green tomatoes, breaded and fried until crispy and topped with a spicy remoulade or aioli sauce.
  • She-Crab Soup: A creamy soup made from crabmeat and roe, this dish is a favorite of seafood lovers in Savannah.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: Served almost everywhere in Savannah, this refreshing Southern staple is a must-try.
  • Chatham Artillery Punch: A boozy punch drink that dates back to the 1800s, made with rum, brandy, and plenty of fruit juices and spices.
  • Moon River Brewing Co. Beer: A local brewery in Savannah, offering a range of handcrafted beers and ales.

Where to Stay in Savannah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Savannah offers plenty of options for lodging, depending on your budget and preferences. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, here are some options to consider:

  • The Kimpton Brice Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Savannah’s Historic District, with modern amenities and a rooftop pool.
  • The Marshall House: A historic hotel that dates back to 1851, the Marshall House features elegant rooms, a complimentary wine hour for guests, and local artwork in every room.
  • The Thunderbird Inn: A budget-friendly motel located on Oglethorpe Avenue, the Thunderbird Inn features kitschy decor, clean rooms, and complimentary Krispy Kreme doughnuts in the morning.
  • The Savannah Hostel: A popular choice for backpackers and solo travelers, the Savannah Hostel is located near the Historic District and provides both private and shared dormitory-style rooms.

What to Buy in Savannah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Savannah offers a range of unique and locally made souvenirs and products, including:

  • Savannah Bee Company honey: This artisanal honey company offers honey from different regions and floral sources, as well as beeswax candles and beauty products.
  • Savannah-made hot sauce: With several local hot sauce makers in town, there’s no shortage of spicy souvenirs to bring home, including Ghost Coast Distillery and Spiceology.
  • Gullah baskets: Handmade baskets woven by the Gullah people of coastal Georgia and South Carolina, these baskets come in various sizes and shapes and make a beautiful and meaningful gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Savannah

Savannah’s history and culture are just as rich as its physical attractions. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Hospitality: Southern hospitality is an important value in Savannah, and many locals will go out of their way to make visitors feel welcome.
  • Manners: Good manners are also important in Savannah, with polite words and gestures expected both on the street and in businesses.
  • Respect: Savannah’s rich history and cultural traditions are worth exploring and respecting, with sensitivity and appreciation for the past.

Best Time to Visit Savannah

Savannah enjoys relatively mild weather throughout the year, but there are some seasonal considerations to keep in mind. The best times to visit Savannah are:

  • Spring (March-May): Great weather, fewer crowds, and plenty of festivals and events, including the Savannah Music Festival and the Savannah Tour of Homes and Gardens.
  • Fall (September-November): Cooler temperatures, lower humidity, and fewer crowds, with the Savannah Film Festival and the Annual Savannah Food and Wine Festival taking place in late fall.
  • Winter (December-February): Although cooler and sometimes rainy, winter is a great time to explore Savannah’s museums and indoor attractions, with plenty of holiday celebrations and events.
  • Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, summer in Savannah is characterized by plenty of outdoor activities and events, including the Fourth of July fireworks, the Savannah Sand Gnats minor league baseball team, and the summer beach season on Tybee Island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savannah

If you’re visiting Savannah for the first time, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Take a walking tour of the historic district: Savannah’s rich history and beautiful architecture are best appreciated on foot.
  • Try the local cuisine: From gourmet restaurants to food trucks to hole-in-the-wall BBQ joints, Savannah is a foodie’s paradise.
  • Explore the local culture: Whether it’s visiting a museum, attending a concert, or simply people-watching, there’s plenty of local culture to explore in Savannah.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Savannah is known for its cleanliness and charm, and littering is frowned upon.
  • Ignore the No Smoking signs: Several public places, including parks and restaurants, prohibit smoking, and failure to comply can result in fines.
  • Overlook the local customs: Savannahians are proud of their traditions, and visitors should show respect and appreciation for the local culture.

Safety and Health

Savannah is a relatively safe city, with low levels of violent crime and theft. However, as in any city, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as being aware of one’s surroundings, not flashing valuables, and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Safety in Savannah

  • Carry cash and credit cards separately: In case of theft or loss, it’s wise to have both forms of payment available.
  • Take a taxi or ride-share service at night: Walking alone at night can be risky, and taking a taxi or ride-share is a safer option.
  • Cross the street with care: Pedestrians should be aware of cars and bicycles, and should always use crosswalks and obey traffic signals.

Health in Savannah

  • Stay hydrated: Especially during the summer months, it’s important to drink plenty of water and protect against heatstroke and dehydration.
  • Beware of mosquitoes: Savannah has a mild climate that attracts mosquitoes, so repellent and long sleeves and pants are necessary, particularly in the evenings and mornings.
  • Watch out for wildlife: Savannah features plenty of natural beauty, and the wildlife that calls it home - from alligators to sand gnats - should be appreciated from afar.

Getting to Savannah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Savannah is easily accessible by air, bus, train, or car. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Savannah

  • Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport: This airport serves most major airlines and is located just a few miles from downtown Savannah.

Bus Stations Near Savannah

  • Greyhound: The Greyhound station is located just a few blocks from the Historic District of Savannah, with buses to and from most major destinations.
  • Megabus: Megabus has several routes to and from Savannah, with a stop at the Savannah Visitor Information Center.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The Amtrak station in Savannah offers daily service on the Palmetto and Silver Service trains.
  • Car Rental: Rentals are widely available at the airport, including from major rental car companies like Hertz, Enterprise, and Avis.

How to Get Around in Savannah

Savannah’s Historic District is best explored on foot, but for those who need to travel farther, there are several options:

  • Chatham Area Transit (CAT): Savannah’s public transit system offers both buses and streetcars, with fares ranging from $1.50 to $3.
  • Taxis and rideshare services: Taxis and apps like Uber and Lyft are readily available throughout Savannah.
  • Bike rentals: Several shops in Savannah offer bike rentals, with rates starting at around $10 per hour.

Where to Go Next from Savannah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider after exploring the beauty of Savannah. Some top options include:

  • Charleston, South Carolina: Just two hours north of Savannah, this charming Southern city is known for its antebellum homes, historic landmarks, and waterfront parks.
  • St. Simons Island: A gorgeous island located just an hour south of Savannah, St. Simons is home to beautiful beaches, historic lighthouses, and a thriving food and arts scene.
  • Jekyll Island: A protected island home to pristine beaches and a sea turtle conservation center, Jekyll Island is a popular day trip destination for Savannahians and visitors alike.

Conclusion

Savannah, Georgia is a city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore the vibrant arts and culture scene, hike along scenic trails, or simply relax and enjoy Southern hospitality, Savannah has something to offer for every type of traveler. This comprehensive guide to Savannah will help you plan your trip, with tips on top attractions, food and drink, lodging options, local customs, and much more. Don’t miss out on the charm and beauty of Savannah - plan your trip today!
