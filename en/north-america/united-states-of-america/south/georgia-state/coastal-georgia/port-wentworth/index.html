Introduction to Port Wentworth

Located in Coastal Georgia, Port Wentworth is a charming city that attracts visitors with its rich history, scenic views, and outdoor recreation opportunities. With a variety of interesting sights and activities, Port Wentworth is a great destination for those looking for an authentic southern experience.

List of Places to Visit in Port Wentworth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Georgia State Railroad Museum - This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the history of rail travel in Georgia. Visitors can see vintage locomotives and railcars, learn about the role of railroads in the state’s development, and take a ride on the daily train excursion.
  • The Mighty Eighth Air Force Museum - This museum honors the heroism of the men and women who served in the Eighth Air Force during World War II. Exhibits include aircraft, vehicles, equipment, and personal artifacts from the war.
  • The Savannah-Ogeechee Canal Museum & Nature Center - This museum focuses on the history of the Savannah-Ogeechee Canal, a 19th-century waterway that played an important role in the region’s economy. Visitors can also explore the surrounding trail system, which provides opportunities for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

Natural Attractions of Port Wentworth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stoney Creek Park - This 25-acre park features walking trails, picnic areas, and a fishing lake.
  • Mulberry Grove Wildlife Management Area - This 5,700-acre area offers opportunities for hunting, fishing, hiking, and wildlife viewing. Visitors can also explore the historic site of Mulberry Grove, where Eli Whitney invented the cotton gin.
  • Goshen Plantation Golf Club - This 18-hole golf course is surrounded by scenic forests and wetlands, making for a challenging and picturesque game.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Wentworth

  • Savannah-Ogeechee Canal Trail - This 16-mile trail runs along the historic canal and provides opportunities for hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • Tour de Port Wentworth - This 30-mile cycling route takes visitors through the city and the surrounding countryside, offering great views of the area’s natural beauty.
  • Lake Mayer Trail - This 1.5-mile paved trail circles Lake Mayer, a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking.

Best Things to Do in Port Wentworth: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Tours of Savannah - These tours take visitors through the haunted streets of Savannah, including stops at some of the city’s most famous haunted houses and cemeteries.
  • Segway of Savannah - Visitors can explore the city in a unique way on a Segway tour, which covers historic sites, parks, and neighborhoods.
  • Savannah Riverboat Cruises - These cruises offer a relaxing way to see the city’s historic waterfront, with dinner, brunch, and sightseeing options available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Wentworth

Food and Drink Scene of Port Wentworth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lowcountry Boil - This popular southern dish is a combination of shrimp, sausage, corn, and potatoes, served with plenty of Old Bay seasoning.
  • Seafood Gumbo - This hearty soup is made with shrimp, crab, andouille sausage, and a spicy roux. It’s usually served over rice.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes - This southern classic features tart green tomatoes that are battered and fried to crispy perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - This southern staple is a refreshing and sweetened iced tea that’s the perfect drink on a hot day.
  • Peach Bellini - This cocktail is made with peach puree and sparkling wine, and is a popular southern drink for brunch or special occasions.
  • Georgia Peach Martini - This cocktail features peach schnapps, vodka, and orange juice, and is a deliciously fruity and refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Port Wentworth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Savannah - North - This hotel features a free breakfast buffet, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Savannah Bed & Breakfast Inn - This charming inn offers cozy rooms, a daily breakfast, and a central location in historic Savannah.

What to Buy in Port Wentworth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pecans - Georgia is known for its delicious pecans, and visitors can bring home bags of roasted or candied nuts as a tasty souvenir.
  • Moonshine - For a unique local product, try some Georgia moonshine, a strong and flavorful distilled spirit.
  • Handmade Crafts - There are many local artisans in the Port Wentworth area who sell handmade pottery, jewelry, and other crafts that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Wentworth

  • Southern Hospitality - Visitors to Port Wentworth can expect a warm welcome and friendly service from locals, who take pride in their southern hospitality.
  • Religious Faith - Many residents of the area are devoutly religious, and religious services and traditions are an important part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Port Wentworth

The best time to visit Port Wentworth is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant. Summer can be very hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Wentworth

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the seafood dishes.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Do take advantage of the area’s outdoor recreation opportunities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t ignore local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Wentworth

Port Wentworth is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take usual precautions such as locking their car doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Port Wentworth

Visitors should drink plenty of water and take precautions against the heat and humidity, especially in the summer months. Mosquitoes can also be a problem, so it’s important to wear insect repellent.

Getting to Port Wentworth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port Wentworth

The closest airport to Port Wentworth is Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport, which is about a 15-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Port Wentworth

The Greyhound bus station is located in nearby Savannah, about a 20-minute drive from Port Wentworth.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Port Wentworth, as the city is conveniently located near major highways such as I-95 and I-16.

How to Get Around in Port Wentworth

The best way to get around in Port Wentworth is by car. There are no public transportation options within the city, so visitors will need to drive or use a rideshare service.

Where to Go Next from Port Wentworth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Savannah - This historic city is just a short drive from Port Wentworth and offers a variety of attractions, including museums, parks, and historic homes.
  • Tybee Island - This barrier island is known for its beautiful beaches, natural beauty, and laid-back vibe.
  • Hilton Head Island - This popular vacation destination in South Carolina is just a short drive from Port Wentworth and offers golf, beaches, and other recreation opportunities.
