Introduction

Troy, a city in Alabama’s River Heritage region, is a charming destination with plenty to offer travelers. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, there is something for everyone in this Southern town. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your visit to Troy, with tips on the best things to see, do, and eat during your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Troy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Troy has a rich history, and visitors can learn about it by visiting several museums and landmarks. The Johnson Center for the Arts is a must-see for art lovers, with rotating exhibitions featuring the works of local and regional artists. The Pioneer Museum of Alabama chronicles the state’s rural history with over 18,000 artifacts and exhibits.

Another must-see attraction is the historic downtown square, home to the Pike County Courthouse and other notable landmarks. Take a stroll down the tree-lined streets and admire the grand architecture of the historic homes and businesses.

Natural Attractions of Troy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Troy is situated near several natural wonders that offer an abundance of outdoor activities. The Conecuh National Forest is a vast wilderness area that provides opportunities for camping, hiking, and wildlife watching. The Blue Springs State Park is another popular destination, with crystal-clear springs that are perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

For a scenic drive, head to the Troy University Arboretum and stroll through the beautiful gardens, which feature over 150 species of plants and trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Troy

Troy is a great destination for hikers and bikers, with several parks and trails to explore. The Wiregrass Trail is a popular hiking and biking route that winds through the Conecuh National Forest.

Bibb Graves Trail is a paved path that circles Troy University’s campus and provides views of beautiful gardens and historic buildings. The trail is perfect for jogging, walking, and bike riding.

Best Things to Do in Troy: Activities and Tours

To get the most out of your visit to Troy, consider taking a guided tour. The Troy Historic District Tour is a walking tour that provides a fascinating glimpse into the city’s past. During the tour, you will visit several landmarks, including the Johnson Center for the Arts and the historic downtown square.

For a more hands-on experience, visit the Troy University Observatory and take a peek through the telescope. The facility is open to the public during special events, and visitors can view planets, stars, and other astronomical wonders.

Week-Long Itinerary for Troy

Day 1: Visit the Pioneer Museum of Alabama and explore downtown Troy. Day 2: Go hiking on the Wiregrass Trail. Day 3: Visit the Troy University Arboretum and take a guided tour. Day 4: Take a day trip to Blue Springs State Park. Day 5: Explore the Conecuh National Forest. Day 6: Visit the Johnson Center for the Arts and attend a performance. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Troy

Must-Try Dishes

Troy is known for its Southern-style cooking, with plenty of delicious dishes to try. Be sure to sample the fried catfish, a Southern classic, at The Old Barn Restaurant. For a hearty breakfast, head to the Butter & Egg Diner and try the biscuits and gravy.

Local Beverages

No Southern meal is complete without sweet tea, and Troy has its own unique take on the classic drink. At Momma Goldberg’s Deli, you can order a “Tea-Bag,” which is a gallon of sweet tea topped with a choice of fruit juice.

For something stronger, visit The Front Porch and enjoy a craft beer in a relaxed outdoor setting.

Where to Stay in Troy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Troy offers several options for accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. The Courtyard by Marriott is a popular choice for travelers, with modern amenities and a central location. The Hampton Inn Troy is another excellent option, with comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

For a more unique experience, try staying in a guesthouse. The Gibson Inn is a historic property that has been converted into guest rooms, offering a glimpse into Troy’s rich history.

What to Buy in Troy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Troy, look for locally made products that reflect the town’s Southern heritage. Visit the Troy Farmers Market for fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

For a unique souvenir, stop by the Jean Lake Gallery and pick up a piece of art by a local artist. The gallery features paintings, sculptures, and other works that are inspired by the beauty of the South.

Local Culture and Customs of Troy

Troy has a friendly and welcoming culture, with Southern hospitality at its core. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites, and always say “yes, sir” or “no, ma’am” when speaking with locals.

Best Time to Visit Troy

The best time to visit Troy is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Troy

Do:

  • Try the Southern-style cooking.
  • Take a walking tour of downtown Troy.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Disrespect local culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Troy

Troy is a safe destination for travelers, but as in any place, visitors should take precautions to avoid becoming a victim of crime. Keep valuables out of sight, especially in crowded areas, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Troy

Troy has several medical facilities, including the Troy Regional Medical Center and the Pike Internal Medicine Clinic. Stay hydrated during the hot summer months and use sunscreen to avoid sunburns.

Getting to Troy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Troy

The nearest airport to Troy is Montgomery Regional Airport, which is located about 50 miles away. From the airport, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to reach Troy.

Bus Stations Near Troy

Greyhound Bus Lines has a bus station in Troy that provides connections to other cities in the region.

Other Options

Troy is easily accessible by car, with several major highways passing through the town. Visitors can also take Amtrak to the nearby city of Montgomery and then rent a car to complete the journey.

How to Get Around in Troy

The best way to get around Troy is by car, as public transportation options are limited. Several car rental agencies serve the area, and taxis are available for short trips around town.

Where to Go Next from Troy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Troy is an excellent base for exploring other Southern destinations. Montgomery, the state capital, is about an hour’s drive away and offers historical landmarks and cultural attractions.

For adventure seekers, consider visiting the Gulf Shores, which is about a three-hour drive away and features beautiful beaches and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Troy, Alabama, is a destination that has something to offer everyone, from history buffs to nature lovers. This comprehensive guide provides everything you need to plan your visit, including tips on the best places to stay, eat, and explore. With its Southern charm and welcoming hospitality, Troy is sure to provide an unforgettable travel experience.
