Introduction to Auburn

Located in the River Heritage region of Alabama, Auburn is a charming city that boasts a mixture of natural beauty, rich culture, and thrilling adventures. The city is home to Auburn University, a prestigious institution with a student population of over 30,000, making it a vibrant and lively college town.

Auburn is also known for its art and music scene, outdoor recreation, and local cuisine. Whether you’re a local or a first-time visitor, this comprehensive tour guide will help you discover the best experiences that Auburn has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Auburn

There are numerous attractions to see in Auburn, ranging from historic landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places in Auburn:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jule Collins Smith Museum of Fine Art - This art museum features a diverse collection of American and European art from the 19th and 20th centuries, including the work of famous artists such as Pablo Picasso, Auguste Rodin, and Roy Lichtenstein.

  • Kiesel Park - This park is ideal for a leisurely walk, picnic, or bike ride. It features an extensive network of trails, playgrounds, and a fishing pond.

  • Auburn University Chapel - This historic chapel is located on the university campus and is open to the public. Its iconic Gothic Revival architecture and stained glass windows make it a beautiful spot for photos.

Natural Attractions of Auburn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chewacla State Park - This state park spans over 700 acres and offers visitors the chance to hike, camp, fish, swim, and paddle in the park’s lake.

  • Toomer’s Corner - This landmark is where Auburn fans gather after a victory to celebrate. It’s also a perfect spot to take a photo with the Toomer’s Oaks, one of Auburn’s most famous icons.

  • Jordan-Hare Stadium - Home to Auburn University’s football team, this stadium is where you can witness the spirit and passion of Tigers fans during game-day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auburn

Auburn is famed for its fantastic outdoor recreational activities and ample hiking and biking trails. If you love the great outdoors, here are some of the best hiking and biking trails to explore in Auburn:

  • Chewacla State Park Trails - This state park features a myriad of trails suitable for hiking, running, or biking. The trails vary in length and difficulty and offer fantastic views of the park’s natural surroundings.

  • The Preserve Trail - This trail is located in the southern part of the city and is ideal for hikers, runners, and mountain bikers. The trail offers stunning views of the Hill’s Heart of Auburn nature preserve.

Best Things to Do in Auburn: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking or biking, there are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Auburn. Here are some of the best:

  • Auburn Food Tour - This tour takes you on a culinary journey through Auburn’s most trendy food spots. You’ll try local delicacies and learn about the city’s culinary scene from knowledgeable tour guides.

  • Kayak Tour on Saugahatchee Creek - This tour is perfect for those looking for a bit of adventure. You’ll paddle on Saugahatchee Creek and admire the beautiful scenery along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auburn

If you have a week’s time to spend in Auburn, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you enjoy the best that the city has to offer:

Food and Drink Scene of Auburn

Auburn’s cuisine is rich and varied, featuring a mix of Southern and international flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Auburn:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken and Waffles - This dish is a Southern classic, and Auburn’s Chicken Salad Chick makes some of the best. Perfect crispy fried chicken is served with fluffy waffles for a mouth-watering combination.

  • Barbecue Ribs - The folks at the legendary Acre restaurant on Glenn Avenue can cook up amazing, smoky ribs that will surely satisfy your meat cravings.

Local Beverages

  • Toomer’s Lemonade - This fresh, homemade lemonade is an Auburn tradition, and it’s served at Toomer’s Drugs, which has been a fixture in Auburn for over 100 years.

  • John Emerald Distilling Co. Whiskey - This unique whiskey, which is hand-crafted and distilled locally in Auburn, is famous for its smoothness and flavor.

Where to Stay in Auburn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auburn offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Auburn:

  • The Collegiate Hotel at Auburn - This hotel is located near Auburn University, offers modern rooms, and has fantastic amenities such as a rooftop lounge and a fitness center.

  • The Wick Hotel - This quaint boutique hotel features luxurious rooms with vintage decor and is located in the historic downtown area.

  • The Quarters at Auburn - This budget-friendly accommodation offers spacious, furnished apartments with all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Auburn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from your trip to Auburn, here are some of the best options:

  • Auburn University Souvenirs - There are numerous Auburn-themed souvenirs available, including t-shirts, hats, and other apparel.

  • Toomer’s Coffee - You can take home a little piece of Auburn by getting a bag of locally roasted coffee from Toomer’s Drugs.

Local Culture and Customs of Auburn

Auburn is a friendly and welcoming community. Some Etiquette and social customs that may be helpful If you are visiting Auburn:

  • Greeting people - It is customary to greet people with a smile and a warm “Hello” in Auburn.

  • Respect local traditions - Auburn is a proud college town and rich in tradition. Respect is given to university events, and locals appreciate visitors’ participation and respect for traditions.

Best Time to Visit Auburn

The best time to visit Auburn is during the fall and spring months, as the mild temperatures are perfect for outdoor adventures and exploration. Visitors can also experience the excitement of Auburn football season in the fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auburn

To ensure a smooth and memorable trip to Auburn, here are some essential Do’s and Don’ts:

Do:

  • Do mingle with the friendly locals and enjoy their company.

  • Do respect the history and traditions of the city, especially when visiting the university.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm nature when exploring the city’s outdoors.

  • Don’t disrespect others and spread negativity, as Auburn is known for its friendly community spirit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Auburn

Auburn is a relatively safe city, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to stay safe. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid walking alone after dark and always stay on well-lit, busy streets.

  • Lock your car doors and never leave valuables visible inside your vehicle.

Getting to Auburn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Auburn is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Auburn

  • Columbus Metropolitan Airport - This airport is located in Columbus, Georgia, and serves the Auburn area, being around 30 mins away.

  • Montgomery Regional Airport - This airport is located in Montgomery, Alabama, and is around 1 hour and 15mins away from Auburn.

Bus Stations Near Auburn

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Opelika, this bus station offers daily services to and from Auburn.

  • Auburn University Bus Terminal- Located on campus, junction of West Magnolia Avenue and South Donahue Drive, this bus terminal provides daily services to nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Several car rental companies operate in Auburn, and renting a car is an excellent way to explore the city at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Auburn

Getting around Auburn is easy with several transportation options available. Here are some of the best ways to get around Auburn:

  • Auburn University Transit (AU Transit) - This shuttle system is free to riders and provides transportation around the university campus and nearby areas.

  • Taxi and Rideshare Services - Taxis and rideshare services are available in Auburn, making it easy to navigate the city’s streets.

Where to Go Next from Auburn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Auburn is located close to several other fantastic destinations, and it’s easy to plan day trips or excursions from the city. Some of the recommended nearby destinations include:

  • Tuskegee - Home to Tuskegee University and the Tuskegee Airmen National Historic Site, this historic town is just a short drive from Auburn.

  • Montgomery - This vibrant city is Alabama’s state capital and is located about an hour and a half drive from Auburn. It’s home to several museums, a historic district and offers great shopping and dining options.

Conclusion

Auburn is an incredible destination for those looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and great food. Plan your visit with this comprehensive tour guide, and you’ll discover all the best spots and activities that Auburn has to offer. Don’t forget to immerse yourself in the city’s traditions and culture to make the most of your time in Auburn!
