Introduction to Boaz

Located in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, Boaz is a charming city in Alabama that boasts of scenic beauty and rich cultural heritage. With its small-town charm and friendly locals, thousands of visitors flock to Boaz every year to enjoy its natural wonders and experience its unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Boaz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Boaz has a variety of museums and historical sites that can be explored by visitors. The following are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Boaz:

  • Boaz Area Museum and Archives: Located in the historic downtown area, the Boaz Area Museum and Archives houses exhibits and artifacts that showcase the city’s history, culture, and people. Visitors can also view the Boaz mural, a hand-painted wall mural that depicts the city’s rich history.
  • Sardis City Museum: The Sardis City Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Sardis City and the surrounding areas. It houses artifacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the area’s history from the Native American period to present day.
  • Alabama Historical Radio Museum: The Alabama Historical Radio Museum has a vast collection of antique radios and memorabilia that date back to the days of Marconi and Armstrong. Visitors can take a step back in time and learn about the history of radio broadcasting.

Natural Attractions of Boaz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Boaz has a wealth of natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. From scenic drives to hiking trails, here are some of the top natural attractions of Boaz:

  • Lake Guntersville State Park: Located just 25 miles from Boaz, Lake Guntersville State Park is a major attraction for nature lovers. The park has over 6,000 acres of natural beauty, including a 69,000-acre lake, hiking trails, camping sites, and fishing opportunities.
  • Little River Canyon National Preserve: Little River Canyon National Preserve is a natural playground for outdoor adventurers. The preserve boasts of waterfalls, rapids, and boulders that offer great opportunities for kayaking, rock climbing, and hiking. Visitors can also enjoy scenic drives and bird watching.
  • Cherokee Rock Village: Cherokee Rock Village is a popular rock climbing destination that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, picnicking, and camping in this natural wonder.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boaz

Boaz is a paradise for hikers and bikers with its scenic trails and routes. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Boaz:

  • Guntersville State Park Trail System: The Guntersville State Park Trail System offers several trails of varying lengths and difficulties. The trails run through diverse terrain, including hardwood forests, mountain ridges, and creeks.
  • Desoto State Park: Desoto State Park has over 25 miles of hiking trails that wind through waterfalls, overlooks, and historic sites. Visitors can also enjoy biking, fishing, and swimming in the park.
  • Little River Canyon National Preserve: The Little River Canyon National Preserve has several trails that offer breathtaking views of the canyon and the surrounding wilderness. The trails range from easy to strenuous, and visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Boaz: Activities and Tours

Boaz offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Boaz:

  • Go Fishing: Boaz is surrounded by several lakes and rivers that offer great opportunities for fishing. Visitors can try their hand at catching bass, crappie, catfish and more.
  • Visit the Farmer’s Market: The Boaz Farmer’s Market is a great place to find fresh produce, local crafts, and homemade goodies. Visitors can enjoy live music and entertainment while browsing the wares.
  • Take a Walking Tour: Visitors can take a walking tour of the historic downtown area of Boaz and learn about the city’s rich history and architecture.
  • ** Go Shopping at the Outlet Mall:** Boaz is home to the Boaz Outlet Mall, which has over 70 stores with a wide variety of merchandise at discounted prices.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boaz

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Boaz:

  • Day 1: Arrive and explore the historic downtown area of Boaz and visit the Boaz Area Museum and Archives.
  • Day 2: Visit Lake Guntersville State Park and enjoy hiking, fishing, and boating.
  • Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Little River Canyon National Preserve and take a hike or enjoy water sports.
  • Day 4: Take a walking tour of downtown Boaz and visit the local shops and restaurants.
  • Day 5: Visit Cherokee Rock Village and enjoy rock climbing or hiking.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a day of shopping at the Boaz Outlet Mall.
  • Day 7: Depart from Boaz.

Food and Drink Scene of Boaz

Must-Try Dishes

Boaz has a vibrant culinary scene, with a variety of restaurants serving up traditional Southern dishes and international cuisine. The following are some of the must-try dishes in Boaz:

  • Fried Catfish: Fried catfish is a favorite among locals and visitors alike. The fish is typically served with hushpuppies, coleslaw, and french fries.
  • Barbecue: Boaz has several barbecue joints that serve up juicy pulled pork, smoked ribs, and brisket.
  • Pepperoni Rolls: A regional specialty, pepperoni rolls are a savory pastry filled with pepperoni and cheese.

Local Beverages

Boaz has several local breweries and wineries that produce high-quality beverages. Here are some of the local beverages to try in Boaz:

  • Back Forty Beer Company: Back Forty Beer Company is a local craft brewery that produces a range of beers, including the flagship Truck Stop Honey Brown Ale.
  • Jules J. Berta Vineyards: Jules J. Berta Vineyards is a family-owned winery that produces award-winning wines from locally sourced grapes.

Where to Stay in Boaz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Boaz has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Luxury hotels, cozy guesthouses, and budget-friendly motels are all available in the city. Here are some of the best places to stay in Boaz:

  • Hampton Inn Guntersville: The Hampton Inn Guntersville is located near Lake Guntersville and offers modern amenities, including a fitness center, outdoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Lake Guntersville Bed and Breakfast: The Lake Guntersville Bed and Breakfast is a family-owned inn that offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the lake.
  • Key West Inn Boaz: The Key West Inn Boaz is a budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Boaz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Boaz has a variety of local products and souvenirs that visitors can bring back home. The following are some of the best things to buy in Boaz:

  • Quilts: Boaz has a long tradition of quilting, and visitors can find beautiful handmade quilts in local shops and markets.
  • Antiques and Collectibles: Boaz has several antique shops that are filled with rare and unique items from the past.
