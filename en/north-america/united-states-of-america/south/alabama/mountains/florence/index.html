Introduction to Florence

Florence is a delightful city situated in the Mountains of Alabama, in the southern region of the United States of America. It boasts an eclectic blend of history, culture, and nature, and visitors from around the world flock to its stunning landscapes, museums, and inviting streets.

Whether you’re an art lover, a history buff, or just looking for a fun and exciting getaway, Florence has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best attractions, activities, and experiences in Florence.

List of Places to Visit in Florence

Below is a list of the top attractions that you should definitely include in your Florence itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Indian Mound and Museum - A fascinating museum that explores the history of America’s First City, Florence, Alabama.

  • W.C. Handy Birthplace, Museum & Library - A national historic landmark located on the west side of Florence, which celebrates the life of W.C. Handy, the father of the Blues.

  • Pope’s Tavern Museum - A former inn turned into an old-fashioned museum that showcases the history of the Civil War era and sketches what life was like in Florence and the local area.

  • Kennedy-Douglass Center for the Arts - The cultural heart of Florence, which regularly hosts exhibitions, concerts, and events.

Natural Attractions of Florence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Deibert Park - A beautiful park with picnic areas, walking trails, and a playground for children.

  • Wildwood Park - Perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, Wildwood Park offers hiking trails, camping grounds, and excellent fishing opportunities.

  • The Shoals - A scenic river area that provides visitors with a chance to take a stroll along the riverbank or go fishing.

  • Cypress Creek - A popular spot for swimming, paddling, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Florence

Florence is a hiker’s paradise and offers several beautiful trails that showcase southern Alabama’s stunning natural beauty. Some of the popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Florence Rail Trail - A scenic 2-mile trail that winds through beautiful countryside.

  • The Lauderdale County Trail - A trail that runs 34 miles through Colbert and Lauderdale Counties, offering scenic views of Alabama’s magnificent landscape.

  • Key Cave National Wildlife Refuge - A unique wildlife refuge that offers hiking trails and bird watching opportunities for visitors.

  • DeSoto State Park - Located in the nearby town of Fort Payne, this state park offers breathtaking waterfalls, hiking trails, and biking routes through picturesque terrain.

Best Things to Do in Florence: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, here are some of the best activities and tours to do when in Florence:

  • Tennessee River Cruises - The perfect way to take a leisurely stroll along the river and experience Florence’s beauty and tranquility.

  • Florence Harbor Marina - You can rent boats, take sailing lessons or arrange for a sunset cruise along the Tennessee River.

  • The University of North Alabama – Home to one of the most historic and enchanting universities, from the beautiful fountain outside of the Bibb Graves building featuring its marble statue of Athena as she gazes over the campus, to the intriguing Rosenbaum House, its countless hidden treasures and intriguing features are a must-visit.

  • Shoals Theatre - A historic theatre in downtown Florence showcasing concerts, plays, musicals, and local acting groups.

  • Florence Downtown Historic Walking Tours - Get a taste of history by exploring Florence’s historic downtown areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Florence

As a traveler, you don’t want to miss out on Florence’s food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southern-style Barbecue - Florence is famous for its delicious BBQ, and you will find plenty of options around the city.

  • Fried Catfish – A classic Southern dish that you should definitely try while in Florence.

  • Pimento Cheese - A delicious comfort food which is a popular local staple.

  • Banana Pudding - A delightful, creamy dessert that’s a southern classic.

Local Beverages

  • To quench your thirst, try the local beer at Singin’ River Brewing Company, one of Florence’s finest breweries.

  • Also, while in Florence, make sure to visit The GunRunner Boutique Hotel, where you can enjoy craft cocktails with stunning rooftop views overlooking the Tennessee River.

Where to Stay in Florence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Florence offers a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Florence:

  • The GunRunner Boutique Hotel - A plush hotel with stylish rooftop terraces, perfect for evening drinks.

  • Marriott Shoals Hotel & Spa - A 4-star hotel with golf courses, waterfalls, and an on-site spa.

  • Florence/Huntsville KOA - A cozy camping area with RV sites, cabins, and modern amenities.

  • Budget Inn Florence – if you’re looking for a budget option, this could be an attractive option as well.

What to Buy in Florence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you’re in Florence, don’t miss the opportunity to bring back some unique souvenirs and local products.

  • Arts and Crafts - Visit local artisans and craft makers at the Florence Handmade Market for unique handmade crafts and artwork.

  • Sweet Home Alabama - Bring home a piece of Alabama and even Florence with the Sweet Home Alabama brand products that are a celebration of Southern culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Florence

Florence’s culture is deeply rooted in its Southern heritage, which is reflected in its arts, crafts, music, and authentic cuisine. The locals are welcoming and warm, which adds to the charming vibe of the city. When in Florence, dress comfortably, be courteous, and engage with locals to get a sense of the southern hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Florence

The best time to visit Florence is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds. Local festivals, such as the W. C. Handy Music Festival and the Renaissance Faire, make summer and winter also an excellent time to experience the vibrant atmosphere of the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Florence

Do:

  • Do take a leisurely stroll by the Tennessee River to catch stunning views of the countryside.

  • Do explore the city by bike or on foot to savor the beauty of Florence.

  • Do try the local BBQ and Southern-inspired cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen and bug spray when venturing outside.

  • Don’t litter and respect nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Florence

Florence is relatively safe, but like any other city, it’s essential to take precautions. Exercise caution when venturing out at night and keep an eye on your belongings while exploring the city.

Health in Florence

If you’re traveling from another country, it’s essential to check the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s website to ensure that you have all the necessary vaccinations before arriving in the US. It’s also important to carry health insurance since the healthcare costs in America can be expensive.

Getting to Florence: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Florence

If you’re traveling from overseas or long distances, you can fly into one of the below major airports, closest to Florence are:

  • Huntsville International Airport (HSV)

  • Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport (BHM)

  • Nashville International Airport (BNA)

Bus Stations Near Florence

Greyhound bus stops at the City of Florence Bus Station, which is conveniently located at 310 West Tennessee Street.

Other Options

If you’re traveling around the city, you can use the taxi service, which is readily available, or, as mentioned above, consider biking or walking to explore the area better.

How to Get Around Florence

The best way to travel around Florence depends on the distance and the purpose of your journey.

  • By Car - Renting a car is an excellent way to explore Florence’s countryside and nearby attractions.

  • By Bike- Florence is a cyclist-friendly city, and you’ll find plenty of biking options everywhere.

  • On Foot - One of the best ways to explore the historic downtown area is on foot.

Where to Go Next from Florence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for nearby destinations to explore, you can plan a trip to:

  • Huntsville, AL - Just 70 miles away, Huntsville is a great choice for adventure seekers looking to explore the famous Space Camp USA.

  • Nashville, TN - At a distance of 125 miles, Nashville is a popular onward destination with famous attractions like the Country Music Hall of Fame, Cheekwood Estate and Gardens, and the Jack Daniel’s Distillery.

  • Chattanooga, TN - This city is around 120 miles away and is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts with highlights like the Lookout Mountain, Tennessee Aquarium, and Southern Belle Riverboat.

Conclusion

Florence is undoubtedly one of the hidden gems in Southern Alabama. It combines the best elements of nature, culture, and history to create an exciting and unforgettable destination for travelers. Whether you’re an art aficionado, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Florence has something to offer everyone. So, when planning your next trip, keep Florence on your radar!
