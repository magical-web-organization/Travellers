Introduction to Fort Payne

Located in the heart of Northeastern Alabama, Fort Payne is a charming city nestled amidst the scenic beauty of Lookout Mountain. Known for its rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and friendly locals, Fort Payne is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the sassy side of Alabama.

From hiking and biking through stunning mountain trails to discovering some of the most interesting historical sites, museums, and cultural landmarks, Fort Payne has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Payne

Here’s a list of some of the most popular places to visit in Fort Payne:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Alabama Fan Club and Museum - Dedicated to the country music group ‘Alabama,’ this museum is a must-visit for fans of the legendary band. The museum displays various memorabilia, including awards, gold records, and personal belongings of the band members.
  • Fort Payne Depot Museum - Housed in a beautifully restored train depot, the Fort Payne Depot Museum showcases the rich history of the city, including artifacts, exhibits, and displays that depict the early days of the railroad in Alabama.
  • Little River Canyon National Preserve - Spanning over 14,000 acres, this national preserve is a natural wonder, offering visitors an opportunity to explore the scenic beauty of Alabama’s Lookout Mountain region. The preserve is home to waterfalls, forests, rivers, and diverse wildlife.
  • Cherokee Rock Village - Offering some of the best scenic views in the region, Cherokee Rock Village is a popular destination for hiking, rock climbing, and outdoor recreation. The 200-acre park is home to ancient boulder formations that are perfect for capturing Instagram-worthy moments.

Natural Attractions of Fort Payne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • DeSoto State Park - Covering over 3,000 acres, DeSoto State Park is a haven of natural beauty, offering visitors a chance to camp, hike, bike, fish, and swim in its many streams, lakes, and waterfalls.
  • Mentone Springs - Known as the “Wildflower Capital of Alabama,” Mentone Springs is the perfect destination for garden lovers. The park features over 400 species of wildflowers and several hiking trails filled with scenic views of the Alabama countryside.
  • Little River Canyon Center - Managed by the National Park Service, the Little River Canyon Center is an educational and interpretive center that features exhibits, displays, and programs on the natural and cultural history of the Little River Canyon National Preserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Payne

Fort Payne is a paradise for hikers and bikers, offering several scenic trails and routes for enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking options in Fort Payne include:

  • Sallie Howard Memorial Chapel Trail - A short, well-maintained trail that leads to the picturesque Sallie Howard Memorial Chapel. The trail offers stunning views of the valley below and is perfect for a peaceful walk.
  • Desoto State Park Mountain Bike Trail - A challenging mountain trail with several steep climbs and descents. The trail offers some of the most mesmerizing views of the DeSoto State Park region.
  • Little River Canyon Rim Road Trail - A winding trail that follows the rim of the Little River Canyon. The trail offers stunning views of the canyon’s many waterfalls and is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Fort Payne: Activities and Tours

From guided tours of cultural sites to adrenaline-pumping adventure sports, Fort Payne has something for everyone. Some of the best things to do in Fort Payne include:

  • Zip-lining at True Adventure Sports - Located just outside Fort Payne, True Adventure Sports offers visitors a chance to fly through the canopy on a zip-line.
  • Horseback Riding at DeSoto State Park - Explore the natural beauty of DeSoto State Park on horseback. Several rental stables in the park offer guided horseback tours.
  • Fort Payne Oktoberfest - Join the locals in celebrating Oktoberfest and enjoy craft beer, live music, and delicious local food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Payne

If you’re planning to spend a week in Fort Payne, here’s an ideal itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the cultural heritage of Fort Payne by visiting the town’s museums and historical landmarks.
  • Day 3-4: Spend a few days in DeSoto State Park and explore the many hiking and biking trails, waterfalls, and lakes.
  • Day 5: Take a scenic drive along the rim of the Little River Canyon and take in the stunning views.
  • Day 6: Head over to Cherokee Rock Village for an epic outdoor adventure.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the local spas or hot springs in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Payne

Fort Payne is a melting pot of Southern and Appalachian cuisine, offering visitors a wide array of delicious meals and mouth-watering local delicacies. Some of the must-try dishes in Fort Payne include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish - A staple in Southern cuisine, catfish is breaded and fried to perfection and served with a side of hushpuppies.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes - Another popular dish in the South, fried green tomatoes are a delicious appetizer, served with a side of remoulade sauce.
  • Chicken and Dumplings - A hearty meal of tender chicken and fluffy dumplings, served in a warm, savory broth.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A Southern classic, sweet tea is a refreshing beverage that pairs well with any meal.
  • Alabama Bubbly - A sweet and fruity wine that is made in several wineries in the region.

Where to Stay in Fort Payne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort Payne offers several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hampton Inn Fort Payne - A well-known chain hotel that offers modern amenities, comfortable rooms, and excellent service.
  • DeSoto State Park Lodge and Cabins - Located within the DeSoto State Park, these cabins offer a closer experience to Mother Nature and the beauty of the state park.
  • Lookout Mountain Parkway Hotel - Located just minutes away from Fort Payne’s downtown area, this hotel offers excellent value for money, comfortable rooms, and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Fort Payne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fort Payne is known for its antique shops, historical landmarks, and natural beauty, making it a perfect place to shop for souvenirs and local products to take home with you. Some of the best things to buy in Fort Payne include:

  • Antiques and Collectibles - Browse through the many antique shops and collectible stores in Fort Payne and find unique treasures.
  • Iron-On Transfers - These unique transfers can be found at several stores in the city, and are perfect for customizing t-shirts and bags.
  • Handmade Quilts - Made by local craftsmen, these beautiful quilts make for an excellent souvenir or a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Payne

Fort Payne is a city steeped in tradition and rich cultural heritage. Some of the local customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of include:

  • Respect for Elders - In Southern culture, elders are given the utmost respect, and visitors are encouraged to follow this tradition.
  • Appropriate Dressing - Although Fort Payne is a relatively casual city, visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and appropriately to the occasion.
  • Southern Hospitality - Known for their friendly nature, Southerners are welcoming and hospitable, and visitors should expect to be greeted warmly wherever they go in Fort Payne.

Best Time to Visit Fort Payne

Fort Payne experiences a pleasant climate throughout the year, making it an ideal destination for outdoor activities, sightseeing, and exploring. However, the best time to visit Fort Payne is in the spring and the fall. The mild temperatures during these seasons make it perfect weather for outdoor activities, and the fall foliage adds an extra element of beauty to the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Payne

When visiting Fort Payne, visitors should be aware of the local norms and customs. Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Tip Service Staff - It’s customary to tip servers, bartenders, and other service staff in Fort Payne.
  • Be Polite - Southerners are known for their friendly and polite nature, and visitors should strive to be courteous at all times.
  • Take Your Time - Fort Payne is a laid-back city, and visitors are encouraged to take their time and enjoy the sights and sounds at their own pace.

Don’t:

  • Be Rude - Visitors should avoid rude or aggressive behavior as it’s not tolerated in Fort Payne.
  • Make Assumptions - Just like any other Southern city, Fort Payne has its culture and customs, and visitors should avoid making assumptions about the people and the city.
  • Litter - Visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and avoid littering.

Safety and Health

Fort Payne is a safe and healthy city, with low crime rates and excellent healthcare facilities. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety and health while visiting the city.

Safety in Fort Payne

  • Stay Alert - Visitors should remain conscious of their surroundings and avoid deserted and unsafe areas.
  • Lock Your Doors and Windows - Visitors should secure their doors and windows when leaving their accommodation.
  • Use Sunscreen - The sun can be intense in Fort Payne, and visitors should use sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Health in Fort Payne

  • Stay Hydrated - Visitors should stay hydrated at all times and avoid dehydration by drinking plenty of water.
  • Prepare for Allergies - Visitors who are allergic to pollen should prepare in advance and carry antihistamines and inhalers.

Getting to Fort Payne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fort Payne is well-connected with several transportation options that cater to visitors’ needs. Here are some of the best ways to get to Fort Payne:

Airports Near Fort Payne

  • Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport - Located about 60 miles from Fort Payne, this airport offers several domestic flights.
  • Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport - Located about 90 miles from Fort Payne, this airport offers several international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Fort Payne

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Located in Downtown Fort Payne, this bus station offers several daily routes connecting the city with other major cities in the region.
  • Guntersville Bus Station - Located about 30 miles from Fort Payne, this bus station offers daily routes connecting the two cities.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars - Visitors can rent a car from several rental companies at the airports or in the city.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services - Visitors can use taxi or ride-sharing services to get around Fort Payne.

How to Get Around in Fort Payne

Fort Payne is a small city, and visitors can get around easily by walking, biking, or using car rental services. Several taxi and ride-sharing services are also available for visitors who don’t want to drive.

Where to Go Next from Fort Payne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fort Payne is an excellent base to explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Northeastern Alabama. Visitors can plan day trips or overnight excursions to several nearby destinations, including:

  • Birmingham, Alabama - About a two-hour drive from Fort Payne, Birmingham offers several cultural sites, museums, and outdoor adventures.
  • Chattanooga, Tennessee - About an hour’s drive from Fort Payne, Chattanooga is a vibrant city known for its musical heritage, outdoor recreation, and friendly locals.
  • Lookout Mountain, Georgia - Located about 30 miles from Fort Payne, Lookout Mountain offers several scenic trails, waterfalls, and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Fort Payne is a hidden gem in Northeastern Alabama, offering visitors a chance to explore the best of Southern culture and natural beauty. With a rich cultural heritage, a friendly and welcoming community, and several outdoor adventures, Fort Payne is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Alabama.
