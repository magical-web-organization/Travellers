Introduction to Scottsboro

Located in the mountains of Alabama, Scottsboro is a small city nestled in the scenic countryside of the southern United States. This charming town is renowned for its outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, fishing, and boating, as well as its many cultural and historical attractions. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Scottsboro has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Scottsboro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Scottsboro-Jackson Heritage Center

The Scottsboro-Jackson Heritage Center is a must-visit for anyone interested in local history. This fascinating museum showcases the heritage and culture of Jackson County, Alabama, from its earliest Native American settlements to the present day. Visitors can explore exhibits on early pioneer life, the Civil War, and the area’s rich industrial heritage, as well as enjoy guided tours, special events, and educational programs.

The Unclaimed Baggage Center

Located in Scottsboro, the Unclaimed Baggage Center is a unique shopping destination that is unlike anything else in the South. Here you’ll find an incredible selection of lost and unclaimed items that have been recovered from all over the world, including clothing, jewelry, electronics, and even musical instruments. In addition to browsing the large showroom, visitors can also take a behind-the-scenes tour of the facility and learn about how the Unclaimed Baggage Center operates.

Goose Pond Colony Resort

For those seeking a more relaxing and scenic experience, the Goose Pond Colony Resort is the perfect destination. Located on the banks of beautiful Lake Guntersville, this luxurious resort offers a range of accommodations, including cabins, villas, and campsites. In addition to its stunning views and tranquil atmosphere, the resort also offers a range of on-site amenities, including golf courses, swimming pools, restaurants, and more.

Natural Attractions of Scottsboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Guntersville State Park

With over 6,000 acres of pristine wilderness, Lake Guntersville State Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, including hiking, boating, fishing, and camping, as well as explore the park’s many scenic vistas and natural wonders. Highlights include the stunning waterfall at Town Creek, the scenic Lake Guntersville, and the miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through the park’s picturesque forests.

The Walls of Jericho

Named for its towering cliffs and deep ravines, the Walls of Jericho is a unique natural wonder located just outside of Scottsboro. This remote wilderness area is home to a number of rare plant and animal species, as well as some of the most dramatic scenery in the South. Hikers and nature lovers will love exploring the park’s many trails and experiencing the breathtaking sights and sounds of the Walls of Jericho.

The Alabama Trail of Tears

For history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts alike, the Alabama Trail of Tears is a must-visit. This historic trail follows the route that Native Americans were forced to take from their homes in Alabama to Indian Territory in Oklahoma in the 1830s. Today, visitors can explore over 200 miles of the trail, which winds through some of the most scenic and historically significant areas of the state.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scottsboro

Scottsboro is renowned for its many natural wonders and outdoor adventures, including some of the best hiking and biking trails in the South. Some of the most popular routes include the Walls of Jericho, the Alabama Trail of Tears, and the miles of trails within Lake Guntersville State Park. Additionally, the area is also home to many scenic back roads and off-road trails, perfect for exploring on foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Scottsboro: Activities and Tours

For those looking for more organized activities and tours, Scottsboro offers a range of options to suit every taste and interest. Some of the most popular activities include guided fishing trips, wine and brewery tours, historical walking tours, and hot air balloon rides over the scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scottsboro

Day 1: Explore the Scottsboro-Jackson Heritage Center and the Unclaimed Baggage Center. Day 2: Take a hike along the Walls of Jericho or explore the many trails at Lake Guntersville State Park. Day 3: Enjoy a day of fishing or boating on Lake Guntersville, or take a scenic drive along the local back roads. Day 4: Visit the Alabama Trail of Tears and learn about the history of Native Americans in the area. Day 5: Take a tour of the local wineries and breweries, or enjoy a hot air balloon ride over the stunning countryside. Day 6: Spend the day at the Goose Pond Colony Resort, enjoying the many on-site activities and amenities. Day 7: Relax and unwind with a leisurely stroll through the historic downtown area, or enjoy a day of shopping and dining.

Food and Drink Scene of Scottsboro

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Scottsboro is famous for its southern comfort cuisine, including barbecue, fried chicken, and all the fixings. Some of the must-try dishes in the area include the famous barbecue at Payne’s Barbecue, the fried chicken and biscuits at the Dotted T, and the fresh seafood at the Top O’ the River.

Local Beverages

In addition to its great food, Scottsboro also offers a range of local beers, wines, and spirits. Some of the best breweries in the area include the Back Forty Beer Company, the Blue Pants Brewery, and the Yellowhammer Brewery. Wine lovers will also enjoy visiting the local wineries, such as the Jules J. Berta Vineyards and the Wills Creek Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Scottsboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Scottsboro offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference, from luxurious resorts to cozy cabins and guesthouses. Some of the most popular options include the beautiful Goose Pond Colony Resort, the charming Renaissance Hotel, and the comfortable Comfort Inn and Suites.

What to Buy in Scottsboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, Scottsboro has plenty to offer. Some of the best options include handmade crafts and jewelry from local artisans, fresh produce and baked goods from the farmers’ market, and regional wines and spirits from the local vineyards and breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Scottsboro

Scottsboro is a friendly and welcoming city that values its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, including the importance of family and community, the love of outdoor activities and sports, and the southern hospitality and friendliness that is a hallmark of the region.

Best Time to Visit Scottsboro

The best time to visit Scottsboro is from late spring through early fall, when the weather is mild and the natural beauty of the area is at its peak. However, the area is a great destination year-round, with plenty of indoor and outdoor activities and attractions to enjoy no matter the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scottsboro

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, boating, and fishing.
  • Do explore the local history and culture, including the many museums and historic sites.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, including the fried chicken, barbecue, and craft beer.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions, including southern hospitality and friendliness.

  • Don’t ignore the importance of family and community in the area.

  • Don’t forget to bring your camera and capture the stunning natural beauty of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scottsboro

Scottsboro is a safe and friendly town that is welcoming to visitors. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables, to ensure their safety while traveling in the area.

Health in Scottsboro

The health care facilities in Scottsboro are excellent, with plenty of options for medical care and emergency services. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as washing their hands frequently and staying up to date on recommended vaccinations, to stay healthy while traveling.

Getting to Scottsboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scottsboro

The closest major airport to Scottsboro is the Huntsville International Airport, located approximately 50 miles away. Visitors can also fly into the Nashville International Airport, located approximately 120 miles away, or the Atlanta International Airport, located approximately 150 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Scottsboro

The closest Greyhound bus station to Scottsboro is located in Huntsville, approximately 40 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors traveling to Scottsboro can also drive in via car or bike, or take a train to the nearby city of Chattanooga, Tennessee, and then rent a car or take a bus to Scottsboro.

How to Get Around in Scottsboro

The best way to get around in Scottsboro is by car or bike, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can also take a taxi or use a ride sharing app to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from Scottsboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Scottsboro is just a short drive away from a range of nearby destinations and excursions, including the cities of Huntsville and Chattanooga, Tennessee, as well as the beautiful Natchez Trace Parkway and the historic civil rights sites in Montgomery and Birmingham.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, and ample opportunities for outdoor recreation and adventure, Scottsboro is a truly unique and unforgettable destination for travelers from around the world. Whether you’re seeking a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, this charming town has something to offer everyone. So why wait? Start planning your trip to Scottsboro today and experience the best that the South has to offer!
