Introduction to Monroeville

Monroeville is a charming town located in Gulf Coast, Alabama, United States of America. This town is famous for its cultural history and natural beauty. Monroeville is the birthplace of famous author Harper Lee, who wrote “To Kill a Mockingbird”. Visitors can explore museums that showcase her life’s work in the town. You can also enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

List of Places to Visit in Monroeville

There are several attractions in Monroeville that visitors can enjoy.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Monroe County Heritage Museum - The Monroe County Heritage Museum is located in the Old Monroe County Courthouse, which was the inspiration for the courthouse in “To Kill a Mockingbird”. The museum showcases the life and work of famous author Harper Lee, including original manuscripts, letters, and personal items.
  • Old Monroe County Courthouse - The Old Monroe County Courthouse was built in 1903 and used as the model for the courthouse in “To Kill a Mockingbird”. Visitors can tour the courthouse and learn about its history.
  • Alabama Writers’ Forum Literary Awards Dinner - Every year the Alabama Writers’ Forum hosts a literary award dinner. The event celebrates the best writers in Alabama and draws people from all over the state.

Natural Attractions of Monroeville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Claiborne Lake - Claiborne Lake is a beautiful spot for boating, fishing, and water sports. The lake is surrounded by scenic views of forests and hills.
  • Claiborne Lock and Dam - The Claiborne Lock and Dam is a hydroelectric power plant that was built in the 1960s. Visitors can tour the plant and learn about the generation of electricity from hydropower.
  • Monroeville Walking Trail - The Monroeville Walking Trail is a 1.5-mile trail that runs through the town’s historic district. The trail passes through several parks and offers beautiful views of historic buildings and monuments.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monroeville

Monroeville offers several hiking and biking routes that visitors can explore.

  • Claiborne Lake Trail - The Claiborne Lake Trail is a 3-mile hike that winds along the Claiborne Lake. The trail offers beautiful views of the lake and is a great spot for birdwatching.
  • Monroeville Biking Trail - The Monroeville Biking Trail is a 7-mile trail that runs through the town’s historic district. The trail is perfect for avid cyclists and offers beautiful views of historic buildings and monuments.

Best Things to Do in Monroeville: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and visiting attractions, Monroeville offers several activities and tours that visitors can enjoy.

  • Alabama Scenic River Trail - The Alabama Scenic River Trail is a 631-mile trail that winds along the Alabama River. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, canoeing, and other water sports.
  • Monroeville Ghost Tour - The Monroeville Ghost Tour is a guided walking tour that takes visitors through the town’s haunted history. Visitors can learn about famous ghost sightings and local legends.
  • Birdwatching Tour - Monroeville is home to several bird species, including bald eagles, ospreys, and blue herons. Visitors can take a guided birdwatching tour to see these magnificent birds up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Monroeville

Monroeville offers a range of dining options, from regional cuisine to international fare.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish - Fried catfish is a local classic and is served at most restaurants in Monroeville. The fish is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.
  • BBQ Ribs - BBQ Ribs are a staple in Southern cuisine, and Monroeville is no different. The ribs are slow-cooked and smothered in sweet, tangy BBQ sauce.
  • Pecan Pie - Southern pecan pie is a popular dessert in Monroeville. The pie is made with fresh pecans and has a rich, sweet filling.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - Sweet tea is a Southern staple and can be found at most restaurants in Monroeville. The tea is served over ice and is sweetened with sugar.
  • Alabama Craft Beer - Alabama is home to several craft breweries, and Monroeville is no exception. Visitors can sample local beers and learn about the brewing process at local breweries.
