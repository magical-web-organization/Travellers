Evergreen is a small city located on the Gulf Coast of Alabama in the United States. Known for its rich history and vibrant culture, Evergreen has much to offer visitors of all ages and interests. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ve compiled the must-visit attractions, best things to do, places to stay, and more to help you plan your perfect Evergreen vacation.

Introduction to Evergreen

Founded in 1819, Evergreen has a long and fascinating history. The city is home to many important landmarks and cultural institutions that reflect both its past and present. Visitors to Evergreen can expect to find a charming southern town with friendly locals, a relaxed pace of life, and plenty of natural beauty to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Evergreen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Evergreen Railroad Depot Museum: This historic train depot was built in 1907 and now serves as a museum showcasing the history of the railroad in Evergreen and the surrounding area.
  • The Conecuh County Courthouse: Built in 1902, this courthouse is an important local landmark and still serves as the county seat.
  • The Georgiana Coke Plant Museum: This museum is housed in the old Georgiana Coke Plant, which was a major industrial site in the early 20th century.
  • The Butler County Courthouse Museum: Located in nearby Greenville, this museum tells the story of the county’s history and features exhibits on everything from Civil War battles to the rise of the automobile.

Natural Attractions of Evergreen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Conecuh National Forest: Located just outside of town, the Conecuh National Forest offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the region with miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing, and more.
  • Frank Jackson State Park: This beautiful park is home to an 1,100-acre lake, perfect for swimming, fishing, boating, and picnicking.
  • Blue Springs State Park: This state park is known for its crystal-clear blue waters and is a popular spot for swimming, diving, and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Evergreen

  • The Conecuh Trail: This 20-mile trail winds through the Conecuh National Forest and offers stunning views of the forest and nearby streams.
  • The Gantt Lake Trail: This 11-mile trail loops around Gantt Lake and is a popular spot for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Evergreen: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the annual Evergreen Sausage Festival: Every November, Evergreen hosts a festival celebrating its famous Conecuh sausage, featuring plenty of food, music, and fun.
  • Take a ghost tour of downtown Evergreen: Learn about the city’s spooky past with a guided ghost tour of some of its most haunted locations.
  • Go on a birdwatching tour of the Conecuh National Forest: The forest is home to hundreds of bird species, making it a must-visit destination for birdwatchers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Evergreen

Day 1 Visit the Evergreen Railroad Depot Museum and downtown historic district
Day 2 Explore the Conecuh National Forest on foot or by bike
Day 3 Take a day trip to Greenville to visit the Butler County Courthouse Museum and other nearby attractions
Day 4 Spend the day swimming, fishing, or boating at Frank Jackson State Park
Day 5 Take a guided ghost tour of downtown Evergreen
Day 6 See the stunning views of the Blue Springs State Park
Day 7 Attend a local festival or event, such as the annual Evergreen Sausage Festival

Food and Drink Scene of Evergreen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Conecuh Sausage: No visit to Evergreen is complete without trying its famous Conecuh Sausage, a local delicacy known for its smoky flavor.
  • Fried Catfish: Freshly caught catfish is a staple of southern cuisine, and you can find some of the best in Evergreen.
  • Pecan Pie: Made with locally grown pecans and a flaky crust, this sweet treat is a must-try for dessert lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A southern classic, sweet tea is a beloved beverage in Evergreen and can be found at nearly every restaurant in town.
  • Conecuh Ridge Whiskey: A local distillery produces Conecuh Ridge Whiskey, a bourbon with a smooth finish and a hint of pecan.

Where to Stay in Evergreen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Legacy Inn & Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites with modern amenities such as free Wi-Fi and a complimentary breakfast.
  • The Camellia House Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in downtown Evergreen and offers guests the perfect combination of comfort and southern hospitality.
  • The Pineapple House Guest Cottage: This cozy guesthouse is perfect for solo travelers or couples looking for a quiet retreat.

What to Buy in Evergreen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Conecuh Sausage: Bring a taste of Evergreen home with you by purchasing some Conecuh sausage to take with you.
  • Pecans: Alabama is known for its delicious pecans, and you can find them in many shops and markets throughout Evergreen.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Many local artisans produce beautiful handcrafted pottery inspired by the natural beauty of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Evergreen

Evergreen is known for its friendly locals and southern hospitality. Visitors should be prepared for a slower pace of life and a more laid-back atmosphere than they may be used to in larger cities.

Best Time to Visit Evergreen

The best time to visit Evergreen is in the fall or spring when the weather is mild, and the local festivals and events are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Evergreen

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of the Conecuh National Forest.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including Conecuh sausage and freshly caught catfish.
  • Do take a ghost tour of downtown Evergreen to learn about the city’s spooky past.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be in a hurry. Evergreen is a relaxed and laid-back town, so take the time to slow down and soak up the southern charm.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent if you plan to spend time outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Evergreen

Evergreen is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should always take standard safety precautions and keep an eye on their surroundings.

Health in Evergreen

Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage and pack any necessary medications before traveling to Evergreen.

Getting to Evergreen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Evergreen

  • Pensacola International Airport: 84 miles from Evergreen
  • Montgomery Regional Airport: 93 miles from Evergreen

Bus Stations Near Evergreen

  • Greyhound Bus Station: 29 miles from Evergreen in Brewton, Alabama

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or rideshare service to Evergreen if they do not wish to fly or take the bus.

How to Get Around in Evergreen

The best way to get around Evergreen is by car or taxi. There are no public transportation options in the city.

Where to Go Next from Evergreen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mobile, Alabama: A 90-minute drive from Evergreen, Mobile is known for its maritime history and vibrant dining and nightlife scene.
  • Gulf Shores, Alabama: Just over an hour’s drive from Evergreen, Gulf Shores is a popular beach destination with pristine sand and clear blue waters.
  • Montgomery, Alabama: A two-hour drive from Evergreen, Montgomery is the capital of Alabama and is home to many important historic sites and museums.

Conclusion

Evergreen offers a unique mix of natural beauty, southern charm, and rich history that is sure to captivate visitors. With its abundance of attractions, delicious local cuisine, and welcoming locals, Evergreen is the perfect destination for a relaxing and unforgettable vacation.
