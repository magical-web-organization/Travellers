Introduction to Hoover

Welcome to the charming city of Hoover! Located in Metropolitan Alabama in the South, Hoover is a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. This city has something for everyone, whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, museums, local cuisine, or shopping. In this travel guide, get ready to explore the top attractions, activities, and hidden gems in Hoover.

List of Places to Visit in Hoover

Here’s a list of the must-visit attractions and activities in Hoover:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aldridge Gardens - a picturesque botanical garden featuring beautiful landscapes and sculptures
  • Hoover Historical Society Museum - a museum showcasing Hoover’s history and heritage
  • The Birmingham Civil Rights Institute - located nearby and highlights the history of the American Civil Rights Movement
  • Alabama Jazz Hall of Fame - a museum dedicated to Alabama’s jazz contributions

Natural Attractions of Hoover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oak Mountain State Park - Alabama’s largest state park with hiking, biking, fishing, golfing and more
  • Moss Rock Preserve - a 349-acre nature preserve with trails, waterfalls and boulders
  • Hoover Dog Park - a dog-friendly park with walking trails, playgrounds for pups, and water fountains

Hiking/Biking Routes in Hoover

  • Greenway Trail - a paved trail spanning over 2.5 miles for walking, biking, and running
  • Shades Creek Greenway Trail - a scenic trail along Shades Creek with benches, rest areas and picnic tables
  • Ross Bridge Loop Trail - a 2.1-mile hiking trail of moderate difficulty suitable for all levels of hikers

Best Things to Do in Hoover: Activities and Tours

  • Birmingham Ghost Walk - an after-dark walking tour featuring Birmingham’s haunted locations
  • Barber Vintage Motorsports Museum - an interesting car museum with over 1500 vintage and modern motorcycles
  • Sloss Furnaces National Historic Landmark - a museum with guided tours showcasing iron smelting history

Week-Long Itinerary for Hoover

Day 1: Visit Hoover Historical Museum, explore Aldridge Gardens and enjoy a delicious dinner at Front Porch.

Day 2: Hike at Oak Mountain State Park, visit the Alabama Jazz Hall of Fame, and have dinner at the Cheesecake Factory.

Day 3: Explore the McWane Science Center, enjoy the Birmingham Ghost Walk, and dine at the Fish Market.

Day 4: Biking at Greenway Trail, enjoy the Uptown Food Tour, and have dinner at Margarita Grill.

Day 5: Visit the Barber Vintage Motorsports Museum, hiking at Ross Bridge Loop Trail, and dine at Ruth’s Chris Steak House.

Day 6: Experience the Sloss Furnaces National Historic Landmark, enjoy visiting Hoover Dog Park, and dine at City Bowls.

Day 7: Explore Moss Rock Preserve, and enjoy a brunch at Another Broken Egg Cafe.

Food and Drink Scene of Hoover

When it comes to food, Hoover has a diverse range of options to offer. From classic barbeque to seafood, and local breweries to trendy coffee shops, Hoover has everything a food lover could ask for.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chopped Pork - Alabama’s classic barbeque dish
  • Fried Green Tomatoes - a regional Southern dish
  • Gumbo - a New Orleans-inspired seafood soup

Local Beverages

  • Local Breweries - Good People Brewing Company, Cahaba Brewing Company
  • Coffee Shops - Seeds Coffee Company

Where to Stay in Hoover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hoover has several accommodation options including hotels, motels, and guesthouses. Some of the top places to consider are:

  • Embassy Suites by Hilton Birmingham-Hoover - a classic hotel with great amenities
  • Hyatt Place Birmingham/Hoover - a contemporary hotel with clean and spacious rooms
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Hoover - a popular hotel with a friendly atmosphere
  • Renaissance Birmingham Ross Bridge Golf Resort & Spa - a luxurious resort with a golf course and spa services

What to Buy in Hoover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • T-shirts, caps, and mugs with Hoover’s emblem and name
  • Artifacts from the Hoover Historical Museum Gift Shop
  • Perfumes and hair products from The Body Shop

Local Culture and Customs of Hoover

Hoover is a predominantly Christian city where people like to dress conservatively. Public display of affection is not encouraged in Hoover. Always greet someone by saying “Hello” or “Good morning”/”Good afternoon”.

Best Time to Visit Hoover

The best time to visit Hoover is during Spring (March to May) and Fall (September to November). The weather is mild, and the leaves changing colors during Fall produce striking views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hoover

Do:

  • Always say “please” and “thank you” in Hoover.
  • Respect its Christian culture.
  • Pay respect to local customs.

Don’t:

  • Unless you’re in a bar or a night hangout, flirty behavior and public display of affection are not appreciated in Hoover.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hoover

Hoover is generally a safe and secure city, but it is always advisable to take precautions while traveling. Avoid walking alone or in dark alleys at night, and keep your belongings safe.

Health in Hoover

Hoover has several healthcare facilities and hospitals that provide quality medical care. In case of an emergency, call 911 or visit Hoover Urgent Care Center.

Getting to Hoover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hoover

  • Birmingham–Shuttlesworth International Airport (BHM) - 17 miles from Hoover
  • Montgomery Regional Airport (MGM) - 95 miles from Hoover

Bus Stations Near Hoover

  • Greyhound - 10 miles from Hoover

Other Options

Rental cars are available from Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport or Montgomery Regional Airport.

How to Get Around in Hoover

  • Car Rental - rental services like Alamo, Hertz, Budget, and Enterprise Rent-A-Car are popular in Hoover
  • Uber or Lyft ridesharing services

Where to Go Next from Hoover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Birmingham - Only a 20-minute drive from Hoover, Birmingham has several tourist attractions to offer, including the Birmingham Zoo, Birmingham Botanical Gardens, and the Sloss Furnaces National Historic Landmark.

Conclusion

Hoover is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty, making it a must-see destination in Alabama. From hiking and biking to museums, Hoover has something for everyone. This travel guide provides you with a comprehensive list of must-visit places, safety tips, and insider knowledge for making the most of your trip to the city.
