Introduction to Bessemer

Nestled in Metropolitan Alabama lies the city of Bessemer, a historic city that showcases the best of Southern charm. This city is named after Sir Henry Bessemer, the inventor of steel processing, and it boasts a rich history and a vibrant culture. It’s a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and history that defines the American South.

List of Places to Visit in Bessemer

If you’re planning a trip to Bessemer, there are plenty of attractions and activities to explore. Here’s a rundown of the top places to visit in Bessemer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bessemer Hall of History Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Bessemer, from its founding in 1887 to its rise as a major industrial hub in the region. The museum features a range of exhibits that document the city’s growth and development.

  2. Alabama Splash Adventure: A family-friendly attraction that features a range of water rides and activities, as well as amusement park attractions. It’s a fun way to beat the heat on a hot summer day.

  3. Jefferson County Veterans Memorial: This memorial pays tribute to the heroic men and women who have served in the armed forces throughout history. It’s a somber reminder of the sacrifices made by these brave individuals.

Natural Attractions of Bessemer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Oak Mountain State Park: Arguably the crown jewel of Bessemer, this park is a nature lover’s paradise. It boasts over 50 miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as a large lake for swimming, boating, and fishing.

  2. Tannehill Ironworks Historical State Park: This park features a range of historic attractions, including an 1850s blast furnace and a restored steam-powered cotton gin. It’s a great place to learn about the region’s industrial heritage.

  3. Alabama Wildlife Center: This center is dedicated to the conservation of native wildlife species in Alabama. Visitors can see a range of birds of prey, from hawks to owls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bessemer

Bessemer is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in the state of Alabama. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  1. Oak Mountain State Park Trail: This 17-mile loop trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests. It’s a challenging route, but the rewards are well worth the effort.

  2. Tannehill Ironworks Trail: This 8-mile trail follows the historic ironworks site, offering a glimpse into the region’s industrial past. It’s an easy and scenic route that’s great for families.

  3. Shades Creek Greenway Trail: This 4-mile trail is perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. It follows the beautiful Shades Creek and passes through some of Bessemer’s most picturesque neighborhoods.

Best Things to Do in Bessemer: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore the downtown area: Bessemer’s historic downtown area is filled with charming shops, restaurants, and galleries. It’s a great place to stroll and take in the local sights and sounds.

  2. Take a historical walking tour: Bessemer’s rich history is best experienced through a guided walking tour. You’ll learn about the city’s early days, its industrial past, and its modern-day revival.

  3. Visit the Farmers Market: The Bessemer Farmers Market is a vibrant community hub that sells fresh produce, handmade crafts, and local products. It’s a great place to mingle with locals and discover the best of Bessemer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bessemer

Day 1: Explore downtown Bessemer and take a historical walking tour

Day 2: Visit the Oak Mountain State Park and go hiking or biking

Day 3: Visit the Tannehill Ironworks Historical State Park and learn about the region’s industrial heritage

Day 4: Take a road trip to Birmingham and explore the city’s museums and landmarks

Day 5: Go fishing or swimming in the beautiful Lake Purdy

Day 6: Take a scenic drive through the region’s countryside and enjoy the stunning natural beauty

Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local spa or wellness center

Food and Drink Scene of Bessemer

Bessemer’s food scene is a delightful mix of Southern cuisine and international flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried catfish: This Southern staple is a must-try when visiting Bessemer. It’s crispy, flavorful, and oh-so-satisfying.

  2. BBQ ribs: Bessemer is known for its mouth-watering BBQ ribs, which are slow-cooked to perfection and slathered in tangy BBQ sauce.

  3. Pimento cheese: A Southern classic, pimento cheese is a spreadable cheese made from cheddar, pimentos, and spices. It’s delicious on crackers, sandwiches, or even as a dip.

Local Beverages

  1. Sweet tea: A Southern classic, sweet tea is a deliciously refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot summer days.

  2. Alabama craft beer: Bessemer is home to several acclaimed craft breweries, such as Good People Brewing Company and Back Forty Beer Company.

  3. Bourbon: Tennessee may get the credit for the best bourbon in the South, but Bessemer is home to some great distilleries as well. Check out the Iron City Distillery for a taste of the local whiskey.

Where to Stay in Bessemer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bessemer offers a range of lodging options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top picks:

  1. Hampton Inn Birmingham/Bessemer: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.

  2. Motel 6 Birmingham, Bessemer: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and simple rooms for a no-frills stay.

  3. Bright Star Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in a historic building and features beautifully decorated rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Bessemer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Alabama pottery: Bessemer is home to several talented craftsmen who create stunning pottery pieces. Look for unique vases, bowls, and kitchen ware to bring home.

  2. Local honey: Bessemer’s farmers market offers a range of delicious local honey products, which make for tasty souvenirs or gifts.

  3. Handmade crafts: The Bessemer downtown area is filled with charming shops selling handmade crafts, such as jewelry, candles, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Bessemer

Bessemer’s culture and customs are a mix of Southern hospitality and its industrial heritage. It’s a friendly and welcoming city, where locals take pride in their history and traditions. The city is known for its blue-collar work ethic, and its residents are proud of their contributions to the region’s industrial success.

Best Time to Visit Bessemer

The best time to visit Bessemer is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and rainy. However, each season brings unique opportunities to explore Bessemer’s natural beauty and cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bessemer

Do:

  1. Take a historical walking tour to learn about Bessemer’s rich history.

  2. Explore Oak Mountain State Park for hiking, biking, and outdoor adventures.

  3. Try the local cuisine, especially the BBQ ribs and sweet tea.

Don’t:

  1. Ignore the city’s industrial heritage. Bessemer’s history is what make it unique, and it’s worth learning about.

  2. Be afraid to venture outside of the downtown area. Bessemer has plenty of charming neighborhoods and natural beauty to explore.

  3. Underestimate the importance of Southern hospitality. Bessemer’s locals are known for their friendliness, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a stranger.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bessemer

Bessemer is a safe city to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions when traveling. Avoid walking alone at night, and be aware of your surroundings. It’s also a good idea to lock your car doors and refrain from leaving valuable items in plain sight.

Health in Bessemer

Bessemer has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, for any medical emergencies.

Getting to Bessemer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bessemer is easily accessible from several major transportation hubs. Here are some of the top options:

Airports Near Bessemer

  1. Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport (BHM): This airport is located approximately 19 miles from Bessemer and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Bessemer

  1. Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus network offers several routes to Bessemer and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  1. Car rental: Bessemer is best explored by car, and several car rental agencies are available at the airport and in the downtown area.

  2. Taxi and ride-sharing services: Several taxi and ride-sharing services operate in Bessemer and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Bessemer

Bessemer is best explored by car, but there are several other options available, including:

  1. Walk: Bessemer’s downtown area is pedestrian-friendly and easy to navigate on foot.

  2. Bike: Several bike rental companies operate in Bessemer, and there are numerous bike paths and trails to explore.

  3. Public transportation: Bus services are available for short trips within the city and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Bessemer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bessemer is centrally located within the state of Alabama, making it a great base for exploring nearby destinations. Here are some of the top picks:

  1. Birmingham: The largest city in Alabama, Birmingham is a thriving urban center that’s home to a range of museums, galleries, and cultural attractions.

  2. Tuscaloosa: This vibrant college town is known for its lively nightlife and beautiful campus.

  3. Montgomery: The state capital of Alabama, Montgomery is a historic city that played a pivotal role in the Civil Rights Movement.

Conclusion

Bessemer is a hidden gem in the heart of Alabama, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something to discover in this charming Southern city. So why not plan a trip to Bessemer today, and experience the best of Alabama’s hidden gems?
