Introduction to Clanton

Clanton is a charming town in Metropolitan Alabama that is known for its peach orchards, museums, and cozy small town feel. With its rich history and outdoor adventures, Clanton is a great place to visit for families and solo travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Clanton

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Clanton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clanton Railroad Depot & Museum: This museum showcases the history of the railroad industry in Clanton and features model trains, photographs, and artifacts.

  • Chilton County Historical Society Museum: Learn about the history of Chilton County at this museum, which has exhibits on Native Americans, the Civil War, and local industry.

  • Confederate Memorial Park: This park is home to the Confederate Soldiers Memorial Museum, which contains artifacts and exhibits related to the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Clanton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peach Park: This 150-acre park is home to a peach orchard, a restaurant, and a playground. In the summer, the orchard is open for peach picking.

  • Mineral Springs Park: Take a stroll in this serene park and enjoy the scenery. There are also picnic areas and a playground.

  • Lake Mitchell: This large lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. There are several public access points, including Higgins Ferry Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Clanton

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, Clanton has several hiking and biking trails to choose from. The most popular routes include:

  • Peachtree Trail: This 1.5-mile trail winds through a peach orchard and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Clanton City Park Trail: This easy, 1.1-mile trail loops around a lake in Clanton City Park. It’s a great spot for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride.

  • The Alabama Trail: This long-distance trail runs from Fort Payne to the Gulf of Mexico and passes through Clanton. There are several sections of the trail that are suitable for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Clanton: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Clanton:

  • Peach Festival: This annual festival celebrates Clanton’s peach harvest. There are food vendors, live music, and arts and crafts.

  • Chilton County Fair: This popular fair is held in September and features carnival rides, food stalls, and agricultural exhibits.

  • Clanton Golf and Country Club: Golf enthusiasts will enjoy playing a round at this scenic golf course.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clanton

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Clanton, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Chilton County Historical Society Museum and take a picnic lunch to Mineral Springs Park
2 Explore Confederate Memorial Park and take a sunset hike on Peachtree Trail
3 Spend the day at Lake Mitchell fishing, swimming, and picnicking
4 Visit the Clanton Railroad Depot & Museum and enjoy lunch at Peach Park
5 Take a day trip to Birmingham to visit the Birmingham Zoo and the Birmingham Civil Rights Institute
6 Play a round of golf at Clanton Golf and Country Club and take a leisurely bike ride on Clanton City Park Trail
7 Go peach picking at Peach Park and attend a concert at the Chilton County Performing Arts Center

Food and Drink Scene of Clanton

Must-Try Dishes

When in Clanton, be sure to try these local dishes:

  • Peach cobbler: This classic Southern dessert is made with fresh peaches and topped with a buttery crust.

  • Fried green tomatoes: These tangy tomatoes are dredged in cornmeal and fried until crispy. They’re often served with a spicy remoulade sauce.

  • Barbecue: Clanton is known for its delicious barbecue, which is slow-cooked over hickory wood.

Local Beverages

Quench your thirst with these local beverages:

  • Peach tea: This refreshing iced tea is made with fresh peaches and sweetened with a hint of honey.

  • Alabama apple cider: Made with apples grown in nearby orchards, this cider is a fall favorite.

  • Muscadine wine: This sweet wine is made from muscadine grapes, which are grown in the Southeast.

Where to Stay in Clanton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clanton has several options for accommodation. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Best Western Inn: This hotel is conveniently located near downtown Clanton and features amenities such as an outdoor pool and free breakfast.

  • Days Inn by Wyndham Clanton AL: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and is just a short drive from Peach Park.

  • Peach Queen Campground: If you’re looking for a more rustic option, Peach Queen Campground offers tent and RV sites in a peach orchard setting.

What to Buy in Clanton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Clanton home with you by purchasing these local products:

  • Peach preserves: Made with fresh peaches, these preserves are a sweet and tangy spread that pairs well with toast or biscuits.

  • Handmade pottery: Local artists create beautiful pottery that is perfect for decorating your home.

  • Souvenir t-shirt: Show your love for Clanton with a t-shirt that features a peach-themed design.

Local Culture and Customs of Clanton

Clanton is a friendly, hospitable town with a strong sense of community. Locals take pride in their traditions, including the Peach Festival and the Chilton County Fair. When visiting, be sure to respect the town’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Clanton

The best time to visit Clanton is in the summer, when the peach orchards are in full bloom and the weather is warm. However, spring and fall are also great times to visit, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. Winter can be chilly, but it’s a good time to take advantage of off-season rates on accommodation and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clanton

Do:

  • Do try the peach cobbler.

  • Do attend the Peach Festival if you’re in town in June.

  • Do visit the museums and historical sites to learn about Clanton’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Clanton takes pride in its clean streets and parks.

  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a local. Clanton is known for its friendly residents.

  • Don’t skip the hiking and biking trails. The fresh air and beautiful scenery are not to be missed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clanton

Clanton is a safe town, with low crime rates and friendly residents. However, it’s always a good idea to use caution when traveling in an unfamiliar place. Be sure to lock your car and don’t leave valuables in plain sight.

Health in Clanton

Clanton has a few medical facilities, including Chilton County Hospital and several urgent care clinics. If you have any medical concerns or emergencies during your visit, seek medical attention as soon as possible.

Getting to Clanton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clanton

The closest airport to Clanton is Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport (BHM), which is about 50 miles away. From the airport, you can rent a car or take a taxi to Clanton.

Bus Stations Near Clanton

Greyhound has a bus station in Birmingham, which is about an hour’s drive from Clanton. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi to Clanton.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Clanton is located along Interstate 65, which runs from Mobile to Chicago. If you’re coming from the east or west, you can take US Highway 280, which runs through Birmingham.

How to Get Around in Clanton

The best way to get around Clanton is by car. Rental cars are available at Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport, or you can bring your own vehicle. Biking and walking are also great options, depending on your itinerary and fitness level.

Where to Go Next from Clanton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in the area, consider a day trip to one of these nearby destinations:

  • Birmingham: The largest city in Alabama, Birmingham has attractions ranging from the Birmingham Zoo to the Birmingham Botanical Gardens.

  • Montgomery: The capital city of Alabama, Montgomery is steeped in history and culture, with attractions such as the Rosa Parks Museum and the Alabama State Capitol.

  • Tuscaloosa: Home of the University of Alabama, Tuscaloosa is a vibrant college town with plenty of restaurants, shops, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Clanton is a hidden gem in Alabama that is worth a visit for its unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this charming Southern town.
