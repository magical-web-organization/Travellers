Introduction to Anniston

Located in Metropolitan Alabama, in the southern region of the United States, Anniston is a small city packed with Southern charm and unique attractions. This hidden gem is the perfect destination for those seeking outdoor adventures, history, and culture. In this travel guide, we will take you through the top things to do, places to visit, where to stay and eat, and everything you need to know to plan your ultimate getaway to Anniston.

List of Places to Visit in Anniston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Anniston is rich in history and heritage, making it a must-visit destination for history buffs and museum lovers. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit:

  • Anniston Museum of Natural History: This museum houses over 2,000 natural history specimens from around the world, including many from Alabama. It also features interactive exhibits and a beautiful botanical garden.
  • Fort McClellan Military Museum: Explore the history of Fort McClellan, a former United States Army installation, through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Alabama Historic Ironworks Commission: This historic site features a reconstructed blast furnace, the only one of its kind in Alabama. Visitors can learn about the iron-making process and history of the site through interpretive exhibits and guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Anniston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Explore the natural beauty of Anniston through its stunning parks, trails, and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Cheaha State Park: Known as the “top of Alabama,” Cheaha State Park has over 2,800 acres of forested mountain landscape. Visitors can hike, bike, and camp in the park, and take in the stunning views from the observation tower.
  • Talladega National Forest: This national forest covers nearly 400,000 acres and offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventure, including hiking, fishing, swimming, and camping.
  • Coldwater Mountain Bike Trail: For thrill-seekers, the Coldwater Mountain bike trail offers over 35 miles of singletrack trails through the scenic mountain terrain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anniston

Anniston is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, and hiking and biking are two of the most popular activities. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • Pinhoti Trail: This 337-mile trail runs from Alabama to Georgia and features stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains. The Alabama portion of the trail can be accessed at the Cheaha State Park.
  • Coldwater Mountain Bike Trail: With over 35 miles of trails, Coldwater Mountain is a destination for mountain bikers looking for a challenge.
  • Bumblebee Trail: This 6.6-mile trail in the Talladega National Forest is a popular hiking and biking destination, with stunning views of the surrounding forest and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Anniston: Activities and Tours

Anniston is packed with activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Anniston:

  • Berman Museum of World History: This museum offers a unique collection of over 8,000 objects from around the world, including many from the Roman Empire and ancient Egypt.
  • Anniston Ghost Tours: Explore the haunted history of Anniston on a guided tour through the city’s historic downtown.
  • Anniston Walking Tour: Take a self-guided walking tour of Anniston’s historic downtown, featuring over 35 historic landmarks and attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anniston

If you’re looking to spend a week in Anniston, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Anniston, check into your hotel, and take a self-guided walking tour of the historic downtown.
  • Day 2: Explore the Anniston Museum of Natural History and the Berman Museum of World History.
  • Day 3: Hike the Pinhoti Trail at Cheaha State Park and take in the stunning views from the Cheaha Mountain Observation Tower.
  • Day 4: Take a ghost tour of Anniston’s historic downtown and visit the Fort McClellan Military Museum.
  • Day 5: Bike the Coldwater Mountain Bike Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch at the park.
  • Day 6: Visit the Alabama Historic Ironworks Commission and hike the Bumblebee Trail in the Talladega National Forest.
  • Day 7: Depart Anniston and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Anniston

Anniston’s food and drink scene is a true representation of Southern cuisine. From barbecue to sweet tea, here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: Anniston is home to some of the best barbecue in the South. Don’t miss out on a plate of pulled pork or brisket from one of the local barbecue joints.
  • Fried Chicken: No Southern meal would be complete without a plate of delicious fried chicken. Try it at one of Anniston’s many diners or soul food restaurants.
  • Grits: Grits are a staple of Southern cuisine and can be found on most breakfast menus in Anniston. Try them topped with cheese or bacon for a savory treat.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A true Southern classic, sweet tea can be found at nearly every restaurant and café in Anniston.
  • Muscadine Wine: Made from a variety of grapes native to the South, muscadine wine is a unique beverage found in many local wineries.
  • Moonshine: For something a little stronger, try some of the locally made moonshine at one of the area’s distilleries.

Where to Stay in Anniston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anniston has a variety of accommodations to suit any traveler’s needs. From luxury hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts, here are some top choices for where to stay in Anniston:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Anniston Oxford: This modern hotel features spacious rooms and an indoor swimming pool, and is located close to many of Anniston’s top attractions.
  • The Victoria Inn: This historic bed and breakfast features well-appointed rooms, a daily breakfast, and a charming garden.
  • Cheaha Resort State Park: This state park offers a variety of accommodations, from campsites to cozy cabins and lodge rooms, all with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Anniston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anniston is home to many unique gift shops and boutiques, offering local products and souvenirs to bring home. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Pottery: Anniston and the surrounding area is known for its pottery, with many local artisans selling their wares in nearby shops.
  • Muscadine Jelly: Made from the grapes used to make muscadine wine, this sweet jelly is a local favorite and makes a great souvenir.
  • Handmade Soaps: Many local shops sell handmade soaps using all-natural ingredients and fragrances.

Local Culture and Customs of Anniston

Southern hospitality is a way of life in Anniston, and locals are known for their friendly and welcoming demeanor. When visiting Anniston, it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions. Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites, and always use “yes ma’am” and “no sir” when addressing locals.

Best Time to Visit Anniston

The best time to visit Anniston is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is colorful. Summers in Anniston can be hot and humid, with temperatures often reaching the 90s. During the winter months, temperatures can dip below freezing, although snow is rare.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anniston

Do:

  • Do take a self-guided walking tour of the historic downtown.
  • Do visit Cheaha State Park for stunning views and hiking opportunities.
  • Do sample some of the local barbecue and sweet tea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural sites and attractions.
  • Don’t miss out on visiting the many museums and historical sites in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anniston

Anniston is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to exercise caution and common sense. Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Anniston

Visitors to Anniston should be aware of the potential for exposure to ticks, which can carry Lyme disease and other illnesses. It’s important to dress appropriately for outdoor activities, use insect repellent, and check yourself for ticks after spending time outdoors.

Getting to Anniston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anniston

The nearest airport to Anniston is the Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport, located approximately 65 miles away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle to Anniston.

Bus Stations Near Anniston

The Greyhound bus station in Anniston is located downtown and offers connections to many destinations throughout the South.

Other Transportation Options

Visitors to Anniston can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing service like Uber or Lyft to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Anniston

The best way to get around Anniston is by car, as many of the top attractions and natural sites are spread out throughout the area. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a taxi or ride-sharing service.

Where to Go Next from Anniston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anniston is a great starting point for exploring many of the nearby destinations in the southern United States. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Birmingham, Alabama: Located just an hour’s drive away, Birmingham is a vibrant city with a rich history and culture.
  • Atlanta, Georgia: Just two hours away, Atlanta is a major metropolitan city with world-class attractions and restaurants.
  • Chattanooga, Tennessee: This charming city is located just over two hours away and offers stunning natural beauty, culture, and history.

Conclusion

Anniston, Alabama, may be a small city, but it packs a big punch in terms of activities, attractions, and charm. From outdoor adventure to museums and cultural attractions, Anniston has something for every type of traveler. Plan your visit today and discover the best of Metropolitan Alabama!
