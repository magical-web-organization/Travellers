Introduction to Eutaw

Eutaw is a small town situated in Greene County, Alabama, in the southern region of the United States. Founded in 1838, the town has a rich history that can be seen in its preserved historic districts and museums. Eutaw also boasts of breathtaking natural attractions and outdoor adventures, making it an excellent destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Whether you are looking for a quiet getaway or an exciting outdoor adventure, Eutaw has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take a closer look at the top attractions, activities, and places to stay in Eutaw.

List of Places to Visit in Eutaw

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Greene County Courthouse: Built-in 1867, the courthouse is a classic example of southern architecture and is listed in the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Kirkwood Mansion: A beautiful, well-preserved antebellum mansion built-in 1860, Kirkwood Mansion is considered one of the most exceptional historic homes in Alabama.
  • Forks of Cypress: A Greek Revival mansion built-in 1830, the house was destroyed in 1966 by arsonists. The ruins are a popular tourist attraction.
  • Jemison-Van de Graaff Mansion: Built-in 1859, this mansion is a fine example of Italianate architecture, and the interiors have been preserved to their original state.
  • Old Tavern: Built-in 1827, the building was used as a tavern, hotel, and a stagecoach stop on the Old Federal Road. Today, it serves as a museum and a public library.

Natural Attractions of Eutaw: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake LU: A man-made lake located near Eutaw, Lake LU offers excellent fishing opportunities for crappie, catfish, and bass.
  • Tannehill State Park: Located a few miles outside of Eutaw, Tannehill State Park is renowned for its hiking trails, waterfalls, and historic iron works.
  • Fallen Oak Farms: Nestled among a forest of oaks and pines, Fallen Oak Farms offer horseback riding, hayrides, and a petting zoo.
  • Greenetrack: Greenetrack is a dog track which offers live greyhound racing and simulcasting of horse races.
  • Greene County Public Fishing Lake: This fishing lake offers fishing for largemouth bass, bluegill sunfish, and channel catfish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eutaw

  • Tannehill Ironworks Trail: A 1.5-mile-long trail that takes visitors to the historic Tannehill Ironworks and offers scenic views of the park.
  • Lost Falls Trail: A 2.5-mile-long trail that leads hikers past cascading waterfalls and excellent wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Pinhoti Trail: The Pinhoti Trail is a long-distance hiking trail that passes through Alabama, Georgia, and Tennessee. The trail is known for its diverse ecology and offers hikers excellent views.

Best Things to Do in Eutaw: Activities and Tours

  • Fur Harvesters of the Heartland: Learn about trapping and fur harvesting from experienced guides. The tour also offers insights into the history of the fur trade in the south.
  • Greenetrack: Take in some live greyhound racing or simulcast horse races at this dog track.
  • Lake LU: Rent a boat and spend the day fishing on Lake LU.
  • Fallen Oak Farms: Go horseback riding or take the family on a hayride at this fun-filled farm.
  • Eutaw Antiques: Shop for antiques and collectibles in historic downtown Eutaw.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eutaw

Food and Drink Scene of Eutaw

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: Eutaw is known for its mouth-watering barbecue. Popular barbecue joints include Ann’s BBQ, Barnett’s BBQ, and Hilltop BBQ.
  • Fried Chicken: Whether it’s hot wings or classic southern-style fried chicken, Eutaw’s restaurants offer delicious fried chicken options. Try Chicken Shack for some of the best fried chicken in town.
  • Sweet Tea: No southern meal is complete without a glass of sweet tea. Try the sweet tea at Felix’s Restaurant and Oyster Bar for a refreshing drink.

Local Beverages

  • Alabama Wines: Try the delicious wines produced by the vineyards of Alabama. Popular wineries include Ozan Vineyard and Cellars and Morgan Creek Vineyards.
  • Breweries: Eutaw has several breweries that produce craft beers. Try the beers at Druid City Brewing Company or Good People Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Eutaw: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Western Inn: A top-rated hotel in Eutaw, Western Inn offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities, including free Wi-Fi.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham: Another great option in Eutaw, Days Inn offers guests a pool, free Wi-Fi, and a hot breakfast.
  • The Columns of Eutaw: This charming guesthouse is located in a restored historic home and features elegant decor and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Eutaw: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Alabama Wines: Bring home some delicious wines from the vineyards of Alabama.
  • Pottery: Eutaw and the surrounding areas are known for their beautiful handmade pottery. Visit the Sarah Bates Studio to see some examples of their work.
  • Antiques: Historic downtown Eutaw has numerous antique stores. Browse them to find unique souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Eutaw

Eutaw has a rich history and culture, and it’s essential to respect local customs and traditions. The town is known for its southern hospitality, so be sure to be polite and respectful to everyone you meet.

Best Time to Visit Eutaw

The best time to visit Eutaw is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are small. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eutaw

Do:

  • Do explore the natural attractions and outdoor adventures that Eutaw has to offer.
  • Do try the mouth-watering barbecue and fried chicken that the town is famous for.
  • Do visit the historic landmarks and museums to learn more about Eutaw’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Don’t litter or damage any of the natural attractions.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eutaw

Eutaw is considered a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take standard precautions to avoid any potential issues.

Health in Eutaw

Visitors should ensure that they have all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Eutaw. Those who plan to engage in outdoor activities should be aware of the potential risks and should take necessary precautions such as using insect repellent.

Getting to Eutaw: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eutaw

The closest airports to Eutaw are the Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport and the Montgomery Regional Airport. Both are approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Eutaw

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Tuscaloosa, which is approximately 50 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Visitors may choose to drive to Eutaw as there are several major highways that run through the town, making it easy to access by car.

How to Get Around in Eutaw

The best way to get around Eutaw is by car. Visitors may choose to rent a car from one of the rental agencies located at the airports or in nearby cities.

Where to Go Next from Eutaw: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Birmingham: Visit Alabama’s largest city to experience its rich cultural attractions, including the Birmingham Museum of Art and the Birmingham Civil Rights Institute.
  • Montgomery: The capital of Alabama, Montgomery, is home to some of the state’s most important historical landmarks, including the Rosa Parks Museum and the Alabama State Capitol.
  • Tuscaloosa: This university town is located just 50 minutes from Eutaw and offers visitors numerous attractions, including the Paul W. Bryant Museum and the historic Druid City Brewing Company.

Conclusion

Eutaw may be a small town, but it offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in exploring the town’s rich history, enjoying the great outdoors, or feasting on some delicious southern cuisine, Eutaw has something for everyone.
