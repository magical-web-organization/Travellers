Introduction to Florida Parishes

Located in the southern region of Louisiana, Florida Parishes is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. It includes seven parishes, namely Livingston, St. Helena, St. Tammany, Tangipahoa, Washington, East Baton Rouge, and East Feliciana. These parishes are rich in history, culture, and natural wonders that will leave any traveler awestruck.

Florida Parishes is also called the “Florida Parishes of Louisiana” because after the purchase of Louisiana, it became a part of West Florida. Today, visitors can witness a unique blend of Creole and Cajun culture with a twist of Southern hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Florida Parishes

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or looking for outdoor adventures, Florida Parishes has something for everyone. Here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Abita Mystery House

A unique roadside attraction in Abita Springs, Louisiana, is the Abita Mystery House. It is also called UCM Museum, which stands for “Unusual Collections and Miniature.” Visitors can witness a collection of weird and wacky artifacts, including a hairball that weighs 50 pounds, a giant front porch, and vintage arcade machines.

2. Louisiana Children’s Discovery Center

The Louisiana Children’s Discovery Center is a fun-filled and interactive museum for children of all ages. Kids can learn about science, math, and technology through hands-on exhibits and activities.

3. Bogue Falaya Park

A historic park in Covington, Louisiana, Bogue Falaya Park, is a blend of nature and man-made attractions. The park is situated on the banks of the Bogue Falaya River and is a popular spot for picnics, fishing, and hiking. Visitors can also witness the historic Covington Trailhead, which is an old train depot converted into a museum.

Natural Attractions of Florida Parishes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Tickfaw State Park

Tickfaw State Park is a nature lover’s paradise, offering a variety of outdoor activities. Visitors can hike, bike, or walk along the trails, go canoeing, kayaking, or fishing in Tickfaw River. The park also has an RV campground and cabin rentals.

2. Global Wildlife Center

Global Wildlife Center is a 900-acre wildlife preserve located in Folsom, Louisiana. Visitors can take a safari tour to witness animals like giraffes, zebras, and antelopes closely. The center also offers educational programs for children.

3. Fontainebleau State Park

Fontainebleau State Park is situated on the north shore of Lake Pontchartrain in Mandeville, Louisiana. The park offers hiking trails, bike paths, beach access, and camping facilities. Visitors can also witness the ruins of the old sugar mill built-in 1829.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Florida Parishes

Florida Parishes is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the landscape. Here are the top hiking and biking routes:

Tammany Trace

TammanyTrace is a 31-mile dedicated hiking and biking trail that runs through Covington, Abita Springs, Mandeville, and Lacombe in St. Tammany Parish.

Fountainebleau State Park Trails

Fountainbleau State Park offers several miles of hiking and biking trails that take visitors through the woods, swamps, and beach area.

Northlake Nature Center Trails

Northlake Nature Center has several trails that offer a glimpse into the flora and fauna of Louisiana. The trails are marked and range from easy to moderate difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Florida Parishes: Activities and Tours

Tchefuncte River Tours

Tchefuncte River Tours offer boat tours through Lake Pontchartrain, Bayou Lacombe, and Tchefuncte River. Visitors can witness the wildlife and scenic views of Louisiana Bayou closely.

The Great Passion Play

The Great Passion Play is a live outdoor theatrical show that depicts the last days of Jesus Christ’s life and crucifixion. The show takes place annually at Easter in Hammond, Louisiana.

Louisiana Renaissance Festival

The Louisiana Renaissance Festival is a popular attraction that takes place in November and December each year. Visitors can witness a medieval village and enjoy performances, games, crafts, and gastronomy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Florida Parishes

Day 1: Visit Abita Mystery House and Louisiana Children’s Discovery Center

Day 2: Explore Tickfaw State Park and Global Wildlife Center.

Day 3: Visit Bogue Falaya Park and Northlake Nature Center.

Day 4: Take the Tchefuncte River Tours and explore Covington Trailhead.

Day 5: Visit Folsom, Louisiana and take day tours of nearby attractions.

Day 6: Visit Fontainebleau State Park and go hiking.

Day 7: Spend the day at the Louisiana Renaissance Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Florida Parishes

Must-Try Dishes

1. Jambalaya

Jambalaya is a signature dish of Louisiana, made with rice, meat, seafood, vegetables, and spices.

2. Crawfish Etouffee

Crawfish Etouffee is a rice dish topped with a thick, roux-based sauce made with crawfish and vegetables. It’s a staple of Cajun cuisine.

3. Beignets

Beignets are fluffy square donuts covered in powdered sugar and are best enjoyed with coffee.

Local Beverages

1. Abita Beer

Abita Beer is a popular local beer brewed in Abita Springs, Louisiana. It comes in various flavors like Purple Haze, Turbo Dog, and Amber.

2. Hurricane Cocktail

The Hurricane Cocktail is a fruity cocktail made with rum, fruit juice, and grenadine syrup. It is often served in a tall glass.

3. Sweet Tea

Sweet Tea is a refreshing cold beverage made with tea and sugar, and it’s a staple of Southern cuisine.

Where to Stay in Florida Parishes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Florida Parishes has different types of accommodations, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

Luxury Hotels

The Ritz-Carlton is a 5-star hotel located in New Orleans. The hotel offers luxurious rooms, restaurants, a spa, and a rooftop pool.

2. Hotel Indigo, New Orleans Garden District

Mid-Range Hotels

Best Western Plus is a 3-star hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a swimming pool. It’s located near Covington Trailhead.

2. The Southern Hotel, Covington

The Southern Hotel is a historic hotel located in downtown Covington. The hotel has a restaurant, a bar, and a pool.

Budget-Friendly Guesthouses

DeSoto Beach Bed and Breakfast is a charming guesthouse located near Fontainebleau State Park. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and free breakfast.

2. Country Inn & Suites by Radisson, Covington, LA

Country Inn & Suites is a budget-friendly hotel located in Covington, Louisiana. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a free breakfast.

1. Local Crafts

Florida Parishes has several local fairs and markets that offer handmade crafts, including pottery, home decor, and jewelry.

2. Hot Sauce

Louisiana is famous for its hot sauces, and Florida Parishes has several local brands that are worth trying.

3. Original Art

Florida Parishes has numerous art galleries and studios that showcase the original works of local artists, including paintings, sculptures, and photography.

Local Culture and Customs of Florida Parishes

Florida Parishes is a vibrant and diverse region that celebrates its unique culture and heritage. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

1. Hospitality

Southern hospitality is the norm in Florida Parishes, and visitors can expect a warm and friendly welcome from locals.

2. Mardi Gras

Mardi Gras is a popular festival celebrated throughout Louisiana, including Florida Parishes. Visitors can witness colorful parades, costumes, and traditional Cajun food during the festival.

3. Cajun and Creole Cuisine

Florida Parishes is a melting pot of cultures that have influenced its cuisine. Visitors can expect spicy and flavorful dishes like Jambalaya, Gumbo, and Crawfish Etouffee.

Best Time to Visit Florida Parishes

The best time to visit Florida Parishes is during the fall (September-November) and spring (March-May) seasons. The weather is mild and pleasant, and there are several festivals and events happening during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Florida Parishes

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ customs and traditions.
  • Try local dishes and beverages.
  • Behave in a friendly and courteous manner.

Don’t:

  • Refuse an invitation to a home-cooked meal.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Overpack your itinerary and rush through the attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Florida Parishes

Florida Parishes is a safe and friendly region with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take the usual precautions like not leaving valuables in parked vehicles and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Florida Parishes

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like West Nile virus and Zika virus, especially during the summer months. It’s advisable to wear protective clothing and use mosquito repellent.
