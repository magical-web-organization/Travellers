Introduction to St. Francisville

Nestled in the heart of the Florida Parishes, St. Francisville is a charming small town located just an hour’s drive from Baton Rouge. Known for its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and Southern charm, St. Francisville offers a unique experience for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in St. Francisville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rosedown Plantation State Historic Site
  • The Myrtles Plantation
  • West Feliciana Historical Society Museum
  • Butler Greenwood Plantation

Natural Attractions of St. Francisville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clark Creek Natural Area
  • Tunica Hills Wildlife Management Area
  • Bayou Sara
  • Cat Island National Wildlife Refuge

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Francisville

For those looking to experience St. Francisville’s scenic beauty on foot or bike, there are plenty of options available. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Tunica Hills Wildlife Management Area Trail
  • Clark Creek Natural Area Trails
  • Mary Ann Brown Preserve Trails

Best Things to Do in St. Francisville: Activities and Tours

  • Take a scenic drive along the River Road Historic Byway
  • Go on a ghost tour of The Myrtles Plantation
  • Visit the Angola Prison Rodeo and Craft Fair (held twice a year)
  • Attend the Bayou Afro Fest (held in September)

Day Activity

Local Culture and Customs of St. Francisville

St. Francisville prides itself on its rich history and Southern hospitality. Visitors should be prepared for slower-paced living, friendly locals, and traditional Southern customs.

Best Time to Visit St. Francisville

The best time to visit St. Francisville is in the spring (March-May) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild and the natural scenery is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Francisville

Do:

  • Tour the town’s historic houses and plantations
  • Take a drive along the River Road Historic Byway
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Visit during the hot and humid summer months (June-August)

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Francisville

St. Francisville is a safe and welcoming small town, but visitors should still take precautions such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in St. Francisville

Visitors should take precautions for insect bites, particularly during the summer months, and should stay hydrated while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to St. Francisville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Francisville

  • Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport (BTR) – 45 miles away
  • Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport (MSY) – 100 miles away

Bus Stations Near St. Francisville

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Baton Rouge – 35 miles away

Other Options

  • Car rental options are available in Baton Rouge and New Orleans

How to Get Around in St. Francisville

The best way to get around St. Francisville is by car. However, biking and walking are also popular options for exploring the town’s scenic beauty.

Where to Go Next from St. Francisville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Baton Rouge, Louisiana (45 miles away)
  • New Orleans, Louisiana (110 miles away)
  • Natchez, Mississippi (70 miles away)

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and Southern hospitality, St. Francisville is the perfect destination for anyone looking to experience the charm and tranquility of small-town Louisiana. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, historic landmarks, or just good food and drink, St. Francisville has something to offer everyone.
