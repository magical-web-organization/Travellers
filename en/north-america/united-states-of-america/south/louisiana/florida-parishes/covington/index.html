Introduction to Covington

Covington is a city located in the Florida Parishes region of Louisiana, United States. It is known for its rich history and natural charm, as well as the thriving arts and cultural scene. Visitors can experience a blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures while in the city. Whether you are a local or a first-time visitor, Covington has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Covington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Southern Hotel and St. Tammany Art Association

The Southern Hotel is a historic hotel that has been restored to its former glory. It features a luxurious spa and restaurant, as well as an art gallery run by the St. Tammany Art Association (STAA). The association promotes and supports local artists and hosts regular exhibitions.

2. Covington Trailhead Museum

The Covington Trailhead Museum is located in the heart of the city and offers a glimpse into the unique history of the area, from Native American times to the present day. Visitors can view exhibits featuring local artists, craftsmen, and writers as well as collections of historical artifacts.

3. Columbia Street Landing

Columbia Street Landing is a popular spot for locals to hang out and enjoy the views of the Bogue Falaya River. It is a great spot to take pictures of the historic Covington scenery.

Natural Attractions of Covington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Bogue Falaya Park

Bogue Falaya Park is a 33-acre park located in downtown Covington, which is famous for its hiking trails, biking paths, and playgrounds. Dogs are welcome to join their owners in this family-friendly park.

2. Insta-Gator Ranch and Hatchery

Insta-Gator Ranch is a unique destination that aims to educate visitors about alligators, from eggs to adults. Visitors can see and hold baby alligators, feed the adults, and learn about these fascinating creatures in a safe and fun environment.

3. St. Tammany Trace

The 31-mile St. Tammany Trace runs from Covington to Slidell and offers a beautiful trail for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. Rent a bike and enjoy scenic views of the Louisiana countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Covington

Covington offers many hiking and bike trails, and many of the places mentioned above have good trails. The Tammany Trace is one of the most popular and is suitable for all skill levels. Bogue Falaya Park and Abita Springs Trailhead are also popular choices.

Best Things to Do in Covington: Activities and Tours

1. Covington Breweries Tour

Covington is home to several breweries and brewpubs, and taking a brewery tour is a great way to experience the local beer scene. You can visit the Abita Brewing Company, Covington Brewhouse, or Gnarly Barley for unique beers and tours.

2. Covington Farmers Market

Located in the heart of the city, the Covington Farmers Market is a great place for foodies, featuring fresh produce and artisan products. The market is held on Saturdays and Wednesdays, offering a wide range of local delicacies.

3. Art Galleries Exploration

If you are an art lover, you can explore the local art scene by visiting the Covington Art Association, Michalopoulos Gallery, and other galleries. You can take a guided art tour of downtown Covington on Saturdays or explore the galleries on your own.

Week-Long Itinerary for Covington

Day 1: Explore the local art scene by visiting galleries in the area

Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride along the St. Tammany Trace.

Day 3: Visit the Covington Trailhead Museum and Southern Hotel, and check out the STAA’s art exhibitions.

Day 4: Take a brewery tour of the local breweries or grab some fresh produce at the Farmers Market

Day 5: Take a tour of the Insta-Gator Ranch and Hatchery

Day 6: Take a scenic drive to Abita Springs Trailhead.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the many parks and scenic views around the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Covington

Must-Try Dishes

1. Gumbo

Gumbo is a traditional Creole dish that features seafood and okra, served over rice. It is a staple of Louisiana cuisine, and visitors should not miss out on trying this signature dish.

2. Beignets

Beignets are a popular dessert in the Deep South and are similar to a donut, but with a lighter and flakier texture. You can try these sweet treats at many local restaurants and bakeries.

3. Po’Boys

A Po’Boy is a sandwich that is made with meat or seafood and served on a French bread roll with lettuce, tomato, and mayonnaise. Find a Po’Boy filled with shrimp, catfish, oysters, or roast beef for an authentic Louisiana experience.

Local Beverages

1. Abita Beer

Abita Beer is one of the most well-known local breweries, with signature beers like Purple Haze and Turbo Dog. Try their seasonal beers too, like Bourbon Street Imperial Stout and Mardi Gras Bock.

2. Cocktail

Covington has a great cocktail scene, and visitors should try some of the local favorites like Sazerac or Hurricane.

3. Iced Coffee

Iced coffee is the perfect summer beverage, and you’ll find the best ones in the Covington area.

Local Culture and Customs of Covington

Louisiana has a distinct culture, and visitors should embrace their traditions when visiting Covington. The state is known for its music, food, and art, so take advantage of these rich traditions when you visit.

Best Time to Visit Covington

The best time to visit Covington is during the fall, winter, and early spring, where the temperatures are mild, and there are fewer crowds. The peak tourism season in the area is in the summer months when the weather is hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Covington

Do:

  • Wander around the scenic trails
  • Savor the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Overlook safety precautions while exploring outdoor attractions
  • Litter in public areas
  • Disrespect the local cultural customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Covington

Visitors should take precautions when hiking trails and exploring outdoor areas, including wearing protective gear, staying hydrated, and always carrying a cell phone.

Health in Covington

Visitors should check with their health care provider before traveling and take preventative measures such as wearing sunscreen and insect repellent to prevent illness.

Getting to Covington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Covington

The closest airports to Covington are the Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport and Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport, located 42 miles and 84 miles, respectively.

Bus Stations Near Covington

There are several Greyhound bus stations located in New Orleans, which is approximately 40 miles away from Covington.

Other Options

Car rentals or taxis are the best option for travelers who want to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Covington

The easiest way to get around Covington is to drive or rent a car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in the area, but they can be costly

Where to Go Next from Covington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Orleans

New Orleans is a famous cultural hub located just an hour’s drive from Covington. Visitors can explore the French Quarter, take a riverboat tour, and enjoy some live jazz music.

Baton Rouge

Baton Rouge is another excellent destination, located just an hour and a half’s drive from Covington. The city is known for its rich history, museums, and beautiful plantations.

Conclusion

Covington is a beautiful and unique city with a variety of cultural and natural attractions. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, exploring museums and galleries, and indulging in the delicious local cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can create their own itinerary and have an enjoyable experience in this fascinating part of Louisiana.
