Introduction to Slidell

Located in Florida Parishes, Louisiana, Slidell is a small city nestled between the Mississippi River and Lake Pontchartrain. Known for its charming historic district, Slidell offers a perfect mix of outdoor adventures, natural beauty, and cultural experiences. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Slidell has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, top dishes to try, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Slidell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Slidell Museum

Located in the heart of the city’s historic district, the Slidell Museum showcases the history of the city. From the early days of the Native Americans to the present-day, the museum tells the story of Slidell’s growth and development over the years.

Camp Salmen Nature Park

Located on the edge of Slidell, Camp Salmen Nature Park is a unique natural attraction. With hiking trails, fishing, and picnic areas, you can spend a whole day exploring the park’s beauty.

Natural Attractions of Slidell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Honey Island Swamp

One of the most popular natural attractions in Slidell, Honey Island Swamp is a pristine wilderness that is home to a diverse range of wildlife. Take a swamp tour and explore the beauty of this unique ecosystem.

Bayou Bonfouca

A beautiful waterway that’s perfect for kayaking, fishing, and other water activities, Bayou Bonfouca is a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Slidell

Tammany Trace

The Tammany Trace is a 31-mile hiking and biking trail that runs from Covington to Slidell, passing through some of the most beautiful scenery in the region. With plenty of places to stop and explore along the way, the Tammany Trace is a must-do for anyone who loves the outdoors.

Best Things to Do in Slidell: Activities and Tours

Slidell Jazz and Blues Festival

If you’re in Slidell in the spring, don’t miss the Slidell Jazz and Blues Festival. This festival brings together some of the best musicians from Louisiana and beyond for two days of music and fun.

Scenic Boat Tour

Explore the natural beauty of Lake Pontchartrain with a scenic boat tour. You’ll see some of the most beautiful spots in Slidell, and learn about the history and culture of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Slidell

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the Slidell Museum and the historic district
Day 2 Take a swamp tour of Honey Island Swamp
Day 3 Hike the Tammany Trace
Day 4 Kayak or fish on Bayou Bonfouca
Day 5 Enjoy the Slidell Jazz and Blues Festival
Day 6 Take a scenic boat tour of Lake Pontchartrain
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Camp Salmen Nature Park

Food and Drink Scene of Slidell

Must-Try Dishes

Crawfish etouffee

Crawfish etouffee is a delicious Louisiana dish that’s a must-try when visiting Slidell. Made with crawfish, rice, and a rich sauce, this dish is the perfect comfort food.

Po’ boys

A New Orleans staple, po’ boys are sandwiches made with meat or seafood on a French bread roll. Slidell is home to some of the best po’ boys in Louisiana.

Local Beverages

Abita Beer

Abita Beer is a Louisiana-based brewery known for its delicious and unique brews. From classic beers like Amber and Turbodog to seasonal favorites like Strawberry Lager, Abita has something for everyone.

Hurricane Cocktail

The Hurricane Cocktail is a sweet and fruity drink that’s perfect for sipping on a hot Louisiana day. Made with rum, passion fruit juice, and grenadine, this cocktail is a Slidell classic.

Where to Stay in Slidell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hampton Inn Slidell

Located near the historic district, the Hampton Inn Slidell is a great choice for visitors who want to be close to all the action. With comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities, this hotel is perfect for families and couples alike.

Woodridge Bed and Breakfast of Louisiana

Looking for a cozy and charming place to stay? The Woodridge Bed and Breakfast of Louisiana is a beautiful guesthouse that offers a home-away-from-home experience.

What to Buy in Slidell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional Cajun spices

Slidell is known for its delicious food, and Cajun spices are a must-have for anyone who loves to cook. Pick up a bag of traditional spices to bring home and add some Louisiana flavor to your meals.

Mardi Gras Beads

Mardi Gras is a big deal in Louisiana, and Slidell is no exception. Pick up some colorful Mardi Gras beads to bring home and remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Slidell

Slidell has a rich cultural heritage, with strong ties to the Creole and Cajun cultures of Louisiana. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including the importance of Mardi Gras and the celebration of local food and music.

Best Time to Visit Slidell

The best time to visit Slidell is in the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Slidell

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially crawfish and po’ boys.
  • Do take a swamp tour and explore the natural beauty of Honey Island Swamp.
  • Do bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Do support local businesses by shopping and eating locally.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the environment.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.
  • Don’t forget to stay hydrated in the Louisiana heat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Slidell

Slidell is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions such as locking their car doors and not leaving valuables visible.

Health in Slidell

Visitors should be aware of the high humidity levels in Slidell and stay hydrated. Insect repellent is also recommended to avoid bites from mosquitos and other insects.

Getting to Slidell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Slidell

The closest airport to Slidell is Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport, which is located about 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Slidell

Greyhound offers bus service to Slidell from New Orleans and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to Slidell, with service from New Orleans.

How to Get Around in Slidell

The best way to get around Slidell is by car. Visitors can also rent bikes or take taxis.

Where to Go Next from Slidell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Orleans

Located just 30 miles away, New Orleans is a popular destination for visitors to Slidell. With its world-famous food, music, and culture, New Orleans is a must-see for anyone visiting Louisiana.

Fontainebleau State Park

Located just 15 miles from Slidell, Fontainebleau State Park is a beautiful natural area that’s perfect for hiking, fishing, and camping.

Conclusion

Slidell is a hidden gem in Louisiana, with plenty of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and delicious food. Whether you’re looking for an outdoor adventure or a cultural experience, Slidell has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to Slidell and make the most of your time in this charming city.
