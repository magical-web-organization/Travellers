Introduction to Lecompte

Located in central Louisiana, Lecompte is a small town with a population of about 1,200 people. It was founded in the 19th century as a railroad town and is known for its rich history and culture. Visitors can explore the town’s museums, natural attractions, hiking and biking trails, and delicious food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Lecompte

When in Lecompte, make sure to visit these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lecompte Historical Museum: This museum showcases the town’s history, including artifacts and exhibits related to the railroad industry and the Civil War.
  • Louisiana Maneuvers and Military Museum: Located in nearby Pineville, this museum highlights the military history of the region, including displays of weapons and uniforms used during World War II.
  • Kent Plantation House: This historic home offers tours of the property, including the main house, kitchen, and slave cabin.

Natural Attractions of Lecompte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Indian Creek Lake and Recreation Area: This serene lake offers fishing and boating opportunities, as well as hiking trails and picnic areas.
  • Alexandria Zoological Park: Located in nearby Alexandria, this zoo is home to over 500 animals from around the world, including tigers, giraffes, and zebras.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lecompte

Lecompte offers several scenic hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Kisatchie National Forest: This vast forest includes over 600,000 acres of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for fishing, camping, and hunting.
  • Indian Creek Greenway: This paved trail runs along Indian Creek and offers beautiful views of the water and surrounding forest.

Best Things to Do in Lecompte: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Tours of Lecompte: Take a guided tour of the town to learn about its history and see its historic landmarks and buildings.
  • Louisiana History and Heritage Trail: This driving tour takes you through several historic towns in central Louisiana, including Lecompte.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lecompte

Day 1: Arrive in Lecompte and explore the local food and drink scene. Day 2: Visit the Lecompte Historical Museum and learn about the town’s railroad history. Day 3: Spend the day hiking or biking in Kisatchie National Forest. Day 4: Take a guided tour of Lecompte’s historic landmarks. Day 5: Explore the Louisiana Maneuvers and Military Museum in Pineville. Day 6: Visit Indian Creek Lake and Recreation Area for a day of fishing and picnicking. Day 7: Depart from Lecompte.

Food and Drink Scene of Lecompte

Lecompte is known for its delicious Southern cuisine and local delicacies, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crawfish Etouffee: A Cajun dish made with crawfish, rice, and a rich roux-based sauce.
  • Boudin: A type of sausage made with pork, rice, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Abita Beer: This Louisiana-based craft brewery produces a variety of beers, including the popular Purple Haze.
  • Sweet Tea: A classic Southern beverage made with tea leaves and sweetened with sugar.

Where to Stay in Lecompte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Lecompte is a small town, there are several options for accommodations:

  • Hotel Bentley: This historic hotel was originally built in 1908 and features antique furnishings and modern amenities.
  • Cane River Cottage and Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse offers a peaceful retreat in the heart of Lecompte.

What to Buy in Lecompte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cajun spices and seasonings: Bring home some of Louisiana’s famous spices and seasonings to add a kick to your home-cooked meals.
  • Local pottery and glassware: Check out the local artisan shops and bring home a unique piece of pottery or glassware.

Local Culture and Customs of Lecompte

Lecompte is rooted in Southern hospitality and has a strong sense of community. Visitors should expect friendly locals, delicious food, and a slower pace of life.

Best Time to Visit Lecompte

The best time to visit Lecompte is in the spring or fall when the temperatures are mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lecompte

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink scene.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the town’s history.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lecompte

Lecompte is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still exercise basic safety precautions like locking their cars and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Lecompte

Visitors should drink bottled water to avoid any potential gastrointestinal issues.

Getting to Lecompte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lecompte

  • Alexandria International Airport (AEX): Located 10 miles from Lecompte, AEX offers flights to and from several major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Lecompte

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Alexandria, about 20 minutes from Lecompte.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car from the airport or nearby cities to get around Lecompte.

How to Get Around in Lecompte

The best way to get around Lecompte is by car or bike. Several rental car companies offer services at the Alexandria International Airport.

Where to Go Next from Lecompte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Natchitoches: This charming town is located about an hour from Lecompte and is known for its historic plantations and beautiful architecture.
  • Toledo Bend Reservoir: This massive lake is located about two hours from Lecompte and offers boating and fishing opportunities.

Conclusion

Lecompte may be a small town, but it offers plenty of opportunities for exploration and adventure. From its rich history and culture to its outdoor recreation and delicious food, Lecompte has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the Southern hospitality of this Louisiana gem.
