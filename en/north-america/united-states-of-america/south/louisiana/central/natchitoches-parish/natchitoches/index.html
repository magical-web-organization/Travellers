Introduction to Natchitoches

Natchitoches is a historic city in Louisiana, USA, known for its diverse culture, charming architecture, and natural beauty. It is the oldest settlement in the Louisiana Purchase territory and has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Native American, Spanish, French, African, and Creole traditions. The city is home to several museums, historical sites, natural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a unique and authentic experience. This travel guide provides an overview of the best places to visit, stay, eat, and explore in Natchitoches.

List of Places to Visit in Natchitoches

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame and Northwest Louisiana History Museum: This museum showcases the history of sports in Louisiana and the contributions of athletes from the region. The Northwest Louisiana History Museum exhibits artifacts and stories from the diverse cultures and communities that have shaped the Natchitoches region over the centuries.
  • Fort St. Jean Baptiste State Historic Site: This 18th-century fort offers visitors a glimpse into the colonial history of Louisiana. The site includes a reconstructed fort, museum, and gardens.
  • Cane River Creole National Historical Park: This park preserves the cultural heritage of the Cane River Creole community, including the Melrose Plantation, Oakland Plantation, and Magnolia Plantation.

Natural Attractions of Natchitoches: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cane River Lake: This scenic lake offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and picnicking. It is surrounded by historical homes and plantations, making it a picturesque spot for a leisurely afternoon.
  • Kisatchie National Forest: This massive national forest covers over 600,000 acres and offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, fishing, and hunting. The forest is home to diverse flora and fauna, including the Louisiana pine snake and Olympic bat.
  • Natchitoches National Fish Hatchery: This fish hatchery raises a variety of fish species, including alligator gar, to stock lakes and rivers in Louisiana and other states. Visitors can learn about the process of fish farming and see different stages of fish development.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Natchitoches

  • Cane River Heritage Trail: This scenic trail follows the Cane River and passes through historic plantations, churches, and cemeteries. It is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and picnicking.
  • Kisatchie Hills Wilderness Trail: This 8-mile trail offers stunning views of the Kisatchie Hills and leads hikers through pine forests, hardwood bottoms, and rocky bluffs.

Best Things to Do in Natchitoches: Activities and Tours

  • Natchitoches Christmas Festival: This annual festival celebrates the holiday season with over 300,000 lights, a fireworks show, live music, and food vendors. It is a beloved tradition for locals and tourists alike.
  • Walking Food Tour: This guided tour takes visitors on a culinary journey through Natchitoches, featuring local dishes like gumbo, boudin, and meat pies.
  • Cane River Paddle and Pedal: This outdoor adventure company offers kayak and bike rentals for exploring the Cane River and surrounding areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Natchitoches

Must-Try Dishes

  • Natchitoches Meat Pie: This savory pie is a local specialty, featuring seasoned ground beef and vegetables in a flaky crust.
  • Gumbo: This hearty stew is a Louisiana staple, featuring seafood, sausage, and okra in a rich broth.
  • Boudin: This Cajun sausage is made from pork, rice, and spices and is often served with crackers or on a po’ boy.

Local Beverages

  • Cane River Brewing Company: This craft brewery serves a variety of locally-made beers, including the popular La Louisiane and Kisatchie Coffee Stout.
  • Lasyone’s Meat Pie Kitchen: This iconic restaurant serves Louisiana-style cocktails like the Hurricane, Sazerac, and Bourbon Street Punch.
  • Cane River Wine: This local winery produces a range of red, white, and fruit wines, including the award-winning Southern Magnolia.

Where to Stay in Natchitoches: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Château Saint-Denis: This boutique hotel is located in the heart of downtown Natchitoches and offers luxurious rooms and suites, a spa, and on-site dining.
  • Church Street Inn: This historic inn is located in a renovated church building and offers spacious suites with modern amenities and antebellum decor.
  • Sweet Cane Inn Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in a Victorian-style home and serves a homemade breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Natchitoches: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Natchitoches Meat Pies: These savory pies are a popular souvenir and can be found at local bakeries and markets.
  • Front Street Shops: The shops along Front Street offer a variety of souvenirs, including handmade jewelry, pottery, and art.
  • Cane River Pecans: This local company produces a range of pecan products, including roasted pecans, pecan candy, and pecan oil.

Local Culture and Customs of Natchitoches

Natchitoches has a unique blend of cultures and traditions influenced by Native American, European, and African roots. The city celebrates its history and heritage through events like the Natchitoches Christmas Festival, which attracts visitors from all over the world. The locals are known for their hospitality and warm welcome to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Natchitoches

The best time to visit Natchitoches is during the fall and winter months when the weather is cooler and the city hosts several festivals and events, including the Natchitoches Christmas Festival and the Meat Pie Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Natchitoches

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Attend a live music or art event in the downtown area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage historical sites and natural areas.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Carry open containers of alcohol in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Natchitoches

Natchitoches is a relatively safe city. However, visitors should take common safety precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables unattended. It’s also important to be aware of the weather conditions and follow any recommendations or warnings from local authorities.

Health in Natchitoches

Natchitoches has a few medical facilities, including Natchitoches Regional Medical Center and Christus Coushatta Health Care Center. Visitors should have travel insurance and be prepared for any unforeseen medical issues.

Getting to Natchitoches: Transportation Options and Logistics

Natchitoches is located about two hours from Shreveport and three hours from New Orleans. The city is accessible by car, bus, and air.

Airports Near Natchitoches

  • Shreveport Regional Airport: This is the closest airport to Natchitoches, located about 2 hours northeast of the city.
  • Alexandria International Airport: This airport is located about an hour southeast of Natchitoches.

Bus Stations Near Natchitoches

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station is located in downtown Natchitoches and offers buses to several destinations in Louisiana and other states.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in the Natchitoches area, including Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis.

How to Get Around in Natchitoches

Natchitoches is a walkable city, and many attractions are located within the downtown area. However, visitors may want to rent a car or bike to explore the surrounding areas and natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Natchitoches: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Orleans: This vibrant city is located about three hours southeast of Natchitoches and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and nightlife.
  • Shreveport: Located about two hours north of Natchitoches, Shreveport is known for its casinos, museums, and riverfront attractions.
  • Toledo Bend Reservoir: This massive lake is located about an hour southwest of Natchitoches and offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and camping.

Conclusion

Natchitoches is a hidden gem in Louisiana, offering visitors a glimpse into the diverse cultures and communities that have shaped the region over the centuries. From historic sites and museums to outdoor adventures and delicious food, Natchitoches has something for everyone. This travel guide provides an overview of the best places to visit, stay, eat, and explore in Natchitoches, helping visitors plan their trip and make lasting memories.
