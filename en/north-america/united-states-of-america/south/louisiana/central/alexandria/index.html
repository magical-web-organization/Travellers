Introduction to Alexandria

Located in the heart of Louisiana, Alexandria boasts rich history, cultural attractions, and natural wonders. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or food lover, Alexandria has something for everyone. Here is a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your visit to Alexandria:

List of Places to Visit in Alexandria

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alexandria Museum of Art: Located in the downtown arts district, this museum features a diverse collection of contemporary art.
  • Kent Plantation House: Built in the early 1800s, this fully restored plantation offers a glimpse into the lives of early settlers in Louisiana.
  • Louisiana History Museum: Learn about Louisiana’s rich history at this museum, housed in a former post office building.
  • Southern Forest Heritage Museum: Explore the logging and sawmill industry that shaped Louisiana’s economy at this museum, located in nearby Long Leaf.

Natural Attractions of Alexandria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Indian Creek Recreation Area: This popular spot offers fishing, hiking, and camping opportunities, as well as stunning views of the nearby Kisatchie National Forest.
  • Kisatchie National Forest: This expansive forest is perfect for hiking, fishing, and exploring Louisiana’s natural beauty.
  • Spring Bayou Wildlife Management Area: With over 12,000 acres of wilderness, this area is a haven for wildlife viewing and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alexandria

  • Alexandria Levee Trail: This paved trail follows the Red River and offers stunning views of the waterway and surrounding wetlands.
  • Wild Azalea Trail: This 31-mile trail, located in Kisatchie National Forest, is perfect for experienced hikers and backpackers.

Best Things to Do in Alexandria: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Alexandria Zoo: This family-friendly attraction boasts over 500 animals from around the world.
  • Take a Cajun Food Tour: Sample Cajun cuisine and learn about the history of Louisiana’s culinary traditions on this guided tour.
  • Hunt for Treasures at the Louisiana Purchase Gardens and Zoo Antiques Marketplace: This unique shopping experience offers antiques, vintage, and collectibles alongside exotic animals.

Food and Drink Scene of Alexandria

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crawfish etouffee: A classic Cajun dish featuring crawfish smothered in a rich, roux-based sauce.
  • Red beans and rice: A hearty dish made with red beans, rice, and a variety of spices.
  • Boudin: A type of sausage that combines pork, spices, and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Abita Beer: Brewed in nearby Abita Springs, Louisiana, this beer has become a local favorite.
  • Sazerac cocktail: A New Orleans classic featuring whiskey, bitters, and absinthe.

Where to Stay in Alexandria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Bentley: A historic hotel located in downtown Alexandria, featuring stylish rooms and a rooftop bar.
  • Courtyard Alexandria: A modern hotel featuring an outdoor pool and convenient location near shopping and dining options.
  • The Grand Hotel: A luxurious option located in nearby Natchez, Mississippi.

What to Buy in Alexandria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Louisiana hot sauce: A spicy addition to any kitchen, Louisiana hot sauce is a must-buy for spice-lovers.
  • Mardi Gras beads: Although Mardi Gras is celebrated primarily in New Orleans, you can find Mardi Gras beads and decorations throughout Louisiana.
  • Local crafts: From pottery to jewelry, Louisiana artisans offer a variety of handmade crafts that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Alexandria

Louisiana is known for its unique culture and customs, heavily influenced by French, African, and Native American traditions. Visitors to Alexandria may encounter terms and phrases specific to Acadiana, the region of Louisiana that includes Alexandria.

Best Time to Visit Alexandria

The best time to visit Alexandria is in the fall or spring, when temperatures are mild and outdoor activities are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alexandria

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including seafood and Cajun dishes.
  • Do visit museums and historical sites to learn about Louisiana’s rich history.
  • Do visit local markets and shops to support small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in outdoor areas.
  • Don’t ignore the warning signs in natural areas, such as flash flood warnings.
  • Don’t underestimate the humidity in summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alexandria

Alexandria is generally a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take the usual precautions of staying aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Alexandria

Visitors to Louisiana should be aware of the state’s high rate of obesity and related health concerns. It’s recommended to maintain a healthy diet and exercise routine while traveling.

Getting to Alexandria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alexandria

  • Alexandria International Airport: Located about 6 miles from downtown Alexandria, this airport offers flights from major hubs throughout the country.

Bus Stations Near Alexandria

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in downtown Alexandria, this bus station provides service to major cities throughout the region.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: Visitors can rent a car from major providers at the airport or in downtown Alexandria.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services: Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are available throughout Alexandria.

How to Get Around in Alexandria

Visitors to Alexandria will find that a car is the most convenient mode of transportation, but taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Alexandria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Natchitoches: Located about an hour from Alexandria, this charming town features historic architecture, bed and breakfasts, and the annual Natchitoches Christmas Festival.
  • New Orleans: Located about 3 hours from Alexandria, New Orleans is known for its vibrant music scene, delicious cuisine, and colorful Mardi Gras celebrations.
  • Lake Charles: Located about 2 hours from Alexandria, Lake Charles features casinos, outdoor activities, and Cajun-style cuisine.

Conclusion

Alexandria offers something for everyone, from history and culture to outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan your perfect trip.
