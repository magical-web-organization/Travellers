Introduction to Saint Rose

Saint Rose, located in River Parishes, Louisiana, is a hidden gem that boasts of a rich history, diverse culture, and natural beauty. The small town is a must-visit destination for tourists who are searching for an authentic Louisiana experience. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, historic sites, or mouth-watering local cuisine, Saint Rose offers something for everyone.

To help you make the most out of your trip, we have listed the top places to visit in Saint Rose:

  • Destrehan Plantation
  • San Francisco Plantation
  • Whitney Plantation
  • Ormond Plantation
  • Bonnet Carre Spillway Historical Marker
  • Ama Cemetery

Best Things to Do in Saint Rose: Activities and Tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint Rose

  • Day 1: Tour the historic Destrehan Plantation and then take a swamp tour to see alligators and other native wildlife up close.
  • Day 2: Visit the San Francisco Plantation and then take a bike ride on the Wetland Watchers Park Trail.
  • Day 3: Explore New Orleans and try some of the famous Louisiana cuisine, such as gumbo, jambalaya, and beignets.
  • Day 4: Take a kayaking or paddleboarding tour at Bayou Adventure and then visit the Bonnet Carre Spillway Historical Marker.
  • Day 5: Take a tour of the Whitney Plantation and then go fishing in the Bayou Des Allemands.
  • Day 6: Go on an airboat ride and then relax at Alice S. Ecusta Park.
  • Day 7: Visit the Ormond Plantation and then end the day with a sunset picnic on the levee.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Rose

When visiting Saint Rose, it is important to understand and respect the local customs and culture. Some of the unique aspects of Saint Rose include the Mardi Gras celebrations, jazz music, and the Creole culture.

Best Time to Visit Saint Rose

The best time to visit Saint Rose is during the fall and spring periods when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities. The summer can be quite hot and humid, while the winter can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint Rose

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including gumbo, jambalaya, and beignets.
  • Take a swamp tour to see alligators and other native wildlife up close.
  • Visit one of the many historic plantations and learn about Louisiana’s rich history.
  • Relax and soak up the beauty of the natural scenery.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and culture.
  • Litter in the natural areas; help keep Saint Rose clean and beautiful.
  • Overdo it on the Cajun seasoning; it can be very spicy.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Saint Rose, it is important to take necessary precautions for safety and health.

Safety in Saint Rose

Saint Rose is considered to be a safe destination; however, it is always essential to be aware of your surroundings and take the necessary precautions to avoid pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Health in Saint Rose

Be aware of the high humidity and heat in the summer months, and protect yourself from mosquito bites, which can cause diseases like West Nile Virus and Zika Virus.

Getting to Saint Rose: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint Rose

There are two airports near Saint Rose, which include the Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport and the Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport.

Bus Stations Near Saint Rose

The Jefferson Transit Authority operates regular bus routes in and around Saint Rose.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars, take taxis or hire private drivers from nearby cities to get to Saint Rose.

How to Get Around in Saint Rose

Getting around in Saint Rose can be accomplished through a rental car, taxi service or public transportation.
