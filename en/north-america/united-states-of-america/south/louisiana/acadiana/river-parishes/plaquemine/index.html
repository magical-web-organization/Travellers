Introduction to Plaquemine

Located in the River Parishes of Acadiana, Louisiana, Plaquemine is a charming southern town with a rich history and a thriving cultural scene. The city is situated on the banks of the Mississippi River and surrounded by lush green forests, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and delicious food, Plaquemine has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Plaquemine

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Plaquemine has plenty of exciting things to do and see. Here are some must-visit attractions in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plaquemine Lock State Historic Site: This historic site dates back to the 1800s when steamboats would travel up and down the Mississippi River. Visitors can explore the lock house, see a replica of a steam-powered paddlewheel boat, and learn about the city’s history.
  • Iberville Museum: This museum showcases the history of Iberville Parish from Native American settlements to the present day. Visitors can also see exhibits about local industries, including sugar cane farming and seafood harvesting.

Natural Attractions of Plaquemine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bayou Plaquemine Waterfront Park: This park is situated on the banks of Bayou Plaquemine and features a walking path, fishing pier, and picnic areas. Visitors can also rent kayaks and explore the bayou at their own pace.
  • Bogue Falaya Park: This park is the perfect place to enjoy a picnic or take a leisurely stroll. It’s also a popular spot for birdwatching, with many species of waterfowl and songbirds calling the area home.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Plaquemine

Plaquemine is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Bogue Falaya Trail: This 1.5-mile trail meanders through Bogue Falaya Park and offers scenic views of the bayou and surrounding forests.
  • Comite Trail: This 7.5-mile trail winds through Comite River Park and features rolling hills and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Plaquemine: Activities and Tours

  • Sunset Riverboat Tours: Take a relaxing boat tour along the Mississippi River and watch the sun set over the water.
  • Louisiana Cajun Mansion Bed & Breakfast Tours: This historic bed and breakfast offers tours of its stunning grounds and gardens, as well as cooking classes and wine tastings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plaquemine

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your week-long trip to Plaquemine:

  • Day 1: Explore Plaquemine Lock State Historic Site
  • Day 2: Visit the Iberville Museum and Bayou Plaquemine Waterfront Park
  • Day 3: Take a hike or bike ride along the Bogue Falaya Trail
  • Day 4: Enjoy a sunset riverboat tour
  • Day 5: Take a cooking class at Louisiana Cajun Mansion Bed & Breakfast
  • Day 6: Explore the countryside with a visit to a local farm or plantation
  • Day 7: Day trip to nearby Baton Rouge

Food and Drink Scene of Plaquemine

No trip to Plaquemine would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jambalaya: A flavorful rice dish made with spices, vegetables, and meat or seafood.
  • Gumbo: A thick stew made with a roux, meat or seafood, and vegetables.
  • Fried Catfish: Fresh-caught catfish breaded and fried to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Hurricanes: A sweet and fruity cocktail made with rum, fruit juice, and grenadine.
  • Mint Julep: A refreshing cocktail made with bourbon, sugar, and fresh mint.

Where to Stay in Plaquemine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Plaquemine has a variety of accommodations to fit every budget and preference.

Budget-Friendly:

  • Best Western Plaquemine Inn: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool at an affordable price.

Mid-Range:

  • The Inn at Houmas House Plantation: This guesthouse offers luxury accommodations on the grounds of the historic Houmas House Plantation.

Luxury:

  • Louisiana Cajun Mansion Bed & Breakfast: This opulent bed and breakfast offers beautifully appointed rooms, gourmet breakfasts, and stunning gardens.

What to Buy in Plaquemine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hot Sauce: Louisiana is known for its spicy cuisine, and hot sauce makes a great souvenir to bring home.
  • Local Artwork: Plaquemine has a thriving arts scene, and visitors can find beautiful artwork and crafts at local galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Plaquemine

Southern hospitality is alive and well in Plaquemine, and visitors can expect to be greeted warmly wherever they go. The city is also home to a vibrant Cajun culture, with unique traditions and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Plaquemine

The best time to visit Plaquemine is in the fall, when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing colors. Spring is another great time to visit, as the flowers are in bloom and the weather is pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plaquemine

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Plaquemine is known for its delicious Creole and Cajun cuisine, so don’t be afraid to try something new.
  • Take a boat tour: Louisiana’s waterways are one of its greatest treasures, so don’t miss the chance to explore them.
  • Visit a plantation: Louisiana is known for its beautiful antebellum plantations, and Plaquemine is no exception.

Don’t:

  • Forget the bug spray: Mosquitoes can be a problem in Plaquemine, especially during the summer months.
  • Leave without trying a Hurricane: This local cocktail is a must-try for any visitor to Louisiana.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plaquemine

Plaquemine is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take the usual precautions when traveling. Keep valuables out of sight, lock your car doors, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Plaquemine

Plaquemine has several medical facilities, including the West Baton Rouge Parish Medical Center. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Zika and West Nile virus, by wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Plaquemine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Plaquemine is located about 15 miles from Baton Rouge, the nearest major city. Visitors can reach Plaquemine by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Plaquemine

  • Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport: Located 17 miles from Plaquemine, this airport offers flights to major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Plaquemine

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Baton Rouge, about 15 miles from Plaquemine.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent a car at the Baton Rouge airport or in the city.

How to Get Around in Plaquemine

The best way to get around Plaquemine is by car, as the city’s attractions are spread out. Visitors can also rent a bike or kayak to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Plaquemine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Baton Rouge: Louisiana’s capital city is just a short drive from Plaquemine and offers a variety of cultural attractions, including the Louisiana State Capitol and the Shaw Center for the Arts.
  • Nottoway Plantation: This historic plantation is just a short drive from Plaquemine and offers tours of its stunning antebellum mansion and grounds.

Conclusion

Plaquemine is a hidden gem in southern Louisiana, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical sites, taking a scenic hike, or simply relaxing with a cocktail on the banks of the Mississippi River, Plaquemine has something for everyone.
