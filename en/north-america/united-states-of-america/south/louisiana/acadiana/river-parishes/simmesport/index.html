Introduction to Simmesport

Simmesport is a charming town situated in the River Parishes of Louisiana, in the southern part of the United States. It is a small town with a population of just over 2,000 people, but there is no shortage of things to see and do in Simmesport. Whether you are interested in history, nature, or outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in this town.

List of Places to Visit in Simmesport

Simmesport may be a small town, but there is no shortage of attractions to visit here. We have listed some of the most significant places to visit in Simmesport below.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tunica-Biloxi Tribe Museum

The Tunica-Biloxi Tribe Museum is located just outside Simmesport and is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about the region’s Native American history. The museum showcases the rich culture and history of the Tunica-Biloxi tribe through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.

Fort DeRussy State Historic Site

Fort DeRussy State Historic Site is a Civil War fort built in 1862 that was used to defend the Red River Valley. Today the site has been restored, and visitors can explore the fort’s history, including relics, battlefields, and barracks.

Morganza Floodway

Another historical place near Simmesport is the Morganza Floodway, built-in 1954 to protect New Orleans from flooding. Visitors can learn about the planning, construction, and history of the floodway and how it has been used over the years to prevent flooding.

Natural Attractions of Simmesport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Atchafalaya National Heritage Area

The Atchafalaya National Heritage Area is a vast natural area home to wildlife, birdwatching, and outdoor recreational opportunities. Visitors can hike, bike, canoe, and kayak through the Bayou’s scenic waterways, parks, and wildlife refuges.

Cat Island National Wildlife Refuge

Another natural attraction is the Cat Island National Wildlife Refuge. The refuge was established in 2000 and is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including deer, alligators, and bald eagles. Visitors can hike, fish, and enjoy the scenic beauty of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simmesport

Atchafalaya Levee Trail

One of the best places to hike or bike in Simmesport is the Atchafalaya Levee Trail. This trail follows the levee along the Atchafalaya River and provides beautiful views of the river and surrounding wetlands. The trail is approximately six miles long and is flat and easy to navigate.

Bayou des Glaises

Bayou des Glaises is another popular area for hiking and biking. The bayou is surrounded by scenic forested areas and offers some of the best birdwatching in the area.

Best Things to Do in Simmesport: Activities and Tours

Tunica-Biloxi Powwow

A must-attend event if you’re in Simmesport during the last weekend of May is the Tunica-Biloxi Powwow. This powwow is an opportunity to experience traditional Native American dances, singing, and drumming.

Hunting and Fishing

Simmesport is an excellent destination for hunting and fishing enthusiasts, with opportunities to catch bass, catfish, and crappie in the nearby lakes and rivers. Hunting opportunities for deer, wild pigs, and other game are also available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Simmesport

Day 1: Fort DeRussy State Historic Site and Museum

On your first day of visiting Simmesport, it’s a good idea to explore the history of the area. Visit the Fort DeRussy State Historic Site and Museum.

Day 2: Atchafalaya National Heritage Area

Spend your second day exploring Atchafalaya National Heritage Area. You can go canoeing or kayaking, hiking, birdwatching, and more.

Day 3: Cat Island National Wildlife Refuge

Take a trip to Cat Island National Wildlife Refuge, where you can hike on designated trails, fish, or enjoy the beauty of the island.

Day 4: Tunica Biloxi Powwow Grounds

Visit the Tunica Biloxi Powwow Grounds where traditional Native American dances, singing, and drumming are held.

Day 5: Hunting or Fishing

Day 5 is the day for hunting or fishing. You can go hunting in the nearby woods or fishing in Atchafalaya River, Red River, or other nearby lakes.

Day 6: Biking and Hiking

On day 6, explore the scenic beauty of the Atchafalaya Levee Trail, which is perfect for biking and hiking.

Day 7: Morganza Floodway

Privilege to visit the Morganza Floodway on your last day in Simmesport, Louisiana.

Food and Drink Scene of Simmesport

Must-Try Dishes

Crawfish Etouffee

One of Louisiana’s most famous dishes is Crawfish Etouffee. Simmesport is known for its delicious Crawfish Etouffee, so you must try it when you’re here.

Gumbo

Another Louisiana classic is Gumbo, a stew made with seafood, sausage, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Sazerac

Sazerac is a New Orleans cocktail made with whiskey, Herbsaint, and bitters.

Abita Brewing Company Beer

Abita Brewing Company is a Louisiana-based brewery that produces a variety of beers, including Amber, Purple Haze, and Turbodog.

Where to Stay in Simmesport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget Option: Simmesport Motel

Simmesport Motel is an excellent budget option, offering clean rooms and reasonable prices.

Mid-Range Option: Paragon Casino Resort

Paragon Casino Resort is a great mid-range option, with a range of rooms and suites to choose from, a casino, and plenty of dining options.

Luxury Option: The Cook Hotel and Conference Center

The Cook Hotel and Conference Center is a luxurious option for those looking for an indulgent stay in Simmesport.

What to Buy in Simmesport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Louisiana Hot Sauce

If you’re a fan of spicy food, you must buy some Louisiana Hot Sauce. It’s available in various heat levels, so be sure to choose one that suits your taste buds.

Mardi Gras beads and masks

Mardi Gras is a famous festival in Louisiana, and you can bring home authentic Mardi Gras beads and masks from Simmesport to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Simmesport

Cajun Culture

One of the most significant parts of Simmesport’s culture is the rich Cajun culture. Cajun music, cuisine, and traditions have a significant influence on the area’s way of life.

Mardi Gras Festivities

Mardi Gras is an important festival celebrated in Simmesport. The town hosts various parades and events during the carnival season, so be sure to plan your trip accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Simmesport

The best time to visit Simmesport is during the fall and spring seasons when the weather is pleasant, and the outdoor activities can be easily enjoyed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simmesport

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty and wildlife in Simmesport.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially Crawfish Etouffee.
  • Attend the Tunica-Biloxi Powwow.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural areas or disturb the wildlife.
  • Be disrespectful to local traditions and customs.
  • Miss out on the opportunity to experience Cajun culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Simmesport

Simmesport is a small town and generally considered safe. As with any travel, it is best to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Simmesport

Visitors to Simmesport should take measures to protect themselves against mosquito bites, which can carry diseases like West Nile virus and Eastern Equine Encephalitis.

Getting to Simmesport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Simmesport

The nearest airport is the Alexandria International Airport, located about 30 miles from Simmesport.

Bus Stations Near Simmesport

Greyhound Bus Station is located in Alexandria and is the nearest bus station to Simmesport.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get to Simmesport, and many car rental companies operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Simmesport

The best way to get around Simmesport is by car. Be sure to rent a vehicle or bring your own if traveling to Simmesport via car.

Where to Go Next from Simmesport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Natchitoches

If you’re looking for a charming historic town to explore, head to Natchitoches, located about an hour and a half drive from Simmesport.

Lafayette

Lafayette is a cultural hub in Louisiana and is known for its excellent Cajun cuisine and music. It’s located about two hours’ drive from Simmesport.

Conclusion

Simmesport may be a small town, but it offers plenty of attractions, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences. From hiking and biking through the scenic trails of the Atchafalaya National Heritage Area to exploring the museums and historical sites, Simmesport has something for everyone. With our comprehensive travel guide, plan your next visit to Simmesport and experience the best of what this charming town has to offer.
