Introduction to Vacherie

Nestled in the heart of River Parishes in Acadiana, Louisiana, Vacherie is a charming town known for its rich history and natural beauty. With a population of just over 4,000, Vacherie offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of the big city. The town is named after a French term for cowshed and is home to several historic plantations. Visitors to Vacherie can explore these plantations, sample delicious Cajun and Creole cuisine, and marvel at the stunning scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Vacherie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Oak Alley Plantation - This antebellum plantation is one of the most popular attractions in Vacherie. Visitors can tour the mansion and surrounding grounds, which includes a stunning alley of oak trees that dates back to the early 18th century.
  2. Laura Plantation - This Creole plantation offers a unique glimpse into the lives of the enslaved people who lived and worked there. Visitors can take a guided tour of the property and learn about the plantation’s history and its inhabitants.
  3. St. Joseph Plantation - This working sugar cane plantation has been in operation since the 1830s and offers guided tours of the mansion and grounds. Visitors can also learn about the process of making sugar and sample some of the plantation’s products.

Natural Attractions of Vacherie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Maurepas Swamp Wildlife Management Area - This 107,000-acre swamp is home to a variety of plant and animal species, including alligators, snakes, and egrets. Visitors can explore the swamps and bayous by hiking or paddling a canoe or kayak.
  2. Mississippi River Overlook - This scenic overlook offers stunning views of the Mississippi River and the surrounding countryside. Visitors can relax on a bench and watch the river flow by, or hike one of the nearby trails to explore the area further.
  3. St. James Wildlife Refuge - This wildlife refuge is home to a variety of waterfowl, including ducks and geese. Visitors can hike along the trails that wind through the refuge and catch glimpses of the birds in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vacherie

  1. Bonnet Carre Spillway Trail - This 6.7-mile trail is great for hiking or biking and offers panoramic views of the wetlands and swamps along the Mississippi River.
  2. Woodland Trail - This 4.5-mile trail winds through the woods and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside and nearby ponds.
  3. Barataria Preserve Trails - Located near nearby Marrero, these trails offer visitors the chance to explore the swamps and bayous of southern Louisiana. Visitors can hike or bike the trails and see alligators, snakes, and a variety of bird species.

Best Things to Do in Vacherie: Activities and Tours

  1. Cajun Pride Swamp Tours - This tour company offers guided boat tours of the swamps and bayous around Vacherie. Visitors can learn about the local wildlife and Cajun history while enjoying the beautiful scenery.
  2. Airboat Adventures - This tour company offers high-speed airboat tours of the swamps near Vacherie. Visitors can see alligators, snakes, and a variety of other wildlife up close.
  3. Bayou Teche Brewing - This local brewery offers tours of its facilities and has a tasting room where visitors can sample a variety of craft beers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vacherie

Day 1: Tour Oak Alley Plantation and enjoy lunch at the onsite restaurant. Day 2: Explore the swamps and bayous around Vacherie with a guided boat tour. Day 3: Visit Laura Plantation and learn about the history of the Creole people who lived and worked there. Day 4: Take a hike on one of the nearby trails and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 5: Visit St. Joseph Plantation and learn about the process of making sugar. Day 6: Take a high-speed airboat tour of the swamps and bayous around Vacherie. Day 7: Relax at one of Vacherie’s many parks or visit Bayou Teche Brewing for a craft beer tasting.

Food and Drink Scene of Vacherie

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Crawfish étouffée - A local favorite, this dish features crawfish smothered in a rich, flavorful sauce made with onions, celery, and peppers.
  2. Boudin - This sausage is a staple of Cajun cuisine and is made with pork, rice, and a blend of Cajun spices.
  3. Jambalaya - This hearty dish features rice, chicken, sausage, and vegetables, and is seasoned with a blend of Cajun spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Hurricane cocktail - This sweet, fruity cocktail is a New Orleans classic and is typically made with rum, passion fruit juice, and grenadine.
  2. Abita beer - This local beer is brewed in neighboring Covington and comes in a variety of flavors, including Amber, Turbo Dog, and Purple Haze.

Where to Stay in Vacherie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Oak Alley Plantation - This historic plantation offers onsite accommodations in historic cottages and is a great option for visitors who want to stay in a unique and historic setting.
  2. St. Joseph Plantation Bed and Breakfast - This working sugar cane plantation has a small bed and breakfast with three guest rooms, each with its own unique decor and historic charm.
  3. Best Western La Place Inn - This hotel is located just a short drive from Vacherie and offers comfortable accommodations and amenities like a pool and fitness center.

What to Buy in Vacherie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hot sauce - Louisiana is famous for its hot sauce, and visitors to Vacherie can take home a bottle of hot sauce from a local producer like Tabasco or Louisiana Hot Sauce.
  2. Cajun seasoning - Another staple of Louisiana cuisine, Cajun seasoning can be found at local grocery stores and gift shops.
  3. Handmade crafts - Many local artisans sell handmade crafts like pottery, jewelry, and textiles at farmers markets and craft fairs around Vacherie.

Local Culture and Customs of Vacherie

Vacherie is steeped in Louisiana culture, with a rich blend of French, Creole, and Cajun influences. Visitors to Vacherie can experience this unique culture through the town’s historic plantation homes, traditional cuisine, and vibrant music scene.

Best Time to Visit Vacherie

The best time to visit Vacherie is in the fall or winter, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. The summer can be hot and humid, and hurricanes are a possibility during this time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vacherie

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including Cajun and Creole dishes like gumbo, jambalaya, and crawfish étouffée.
  • Visit the local plantations to learn about Louisiana’s rich history and culture.
  • Take a scenic drive along River Road to see some of the area’s historic plantation homes.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the heat and humidity in the summer months.
  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring the swamps and bayous.
  • Leave without trying at least one Cajun or Creole dish.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vacherie

Vacherie is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal safety precautions like locking their vehicles and securing their valuables.

Health in Vacherie

Visitors to Vacherie should be aware of the heat and humidity in the summer months and take steps to stay cool and hydrated. Mosquitoes are also common in the area, so visitors should take precautions like using insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved shirts and pants.

Getting to Vacherie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vacherie

The closest airport to Vacherie is Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport, which is about 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Vacherie

Greyhound bus service is available in nearby Gonzales, which is about 30 minutes away from Vacherie.

Other Options

Car rental is a popular way to get around Louisiana, and visitors to Vacherie can rent a car at the airport or in nearby cities like New Orleans or Baton Rouge.

How to Get Around in Vacherie

The best way to get around Vacherie is by car. Visitors can rent a car or drive their own vehicle to explore the town and surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Vacherie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. New Orleans - Just a short drive away, New Orleans is one of the most vibrant and unique cities in the United States, with a rich cultural heritage and delicious cuisine.
  2. Baton Rouge - The state capital of Louisiana, Baton Rouge is home to a variety of museums, galleries, and historic sites.
  3. Lafayette - Located in the heart of Cajun country, Lafayette is known for its vibrant music scene and delicious local cuisine.

Conclusion

Vacherie, Louisiana is a hidden gem in the heart of Acadiana, where visitors can experience Louisiana’s rich history and culture in a peaceful and scenic setting. With its historic plantations, natural beauty, and unique cuisine, Vacherie is a must-see destination for anyone traveling to Louisiana.
