Introduction to LaPlace

Located in the River Parishes of Louisiana, LaPlace is a hidden gem for travelers seeking a mix of culture, adventure, and relaxation. Known for its Creole and Cajun cuisine, historical landmarks, and outdoor activities, LaPlace is the perfect destination for those who want to experience the true essence of Southern hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in LaPlace

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cajun Pride Swamp Tours

If you’re looking to see Louisiana’s natural beauty up close, Cajun Pride Swamp Tours is the perfect attraction for you. Take a guided tour through the swamps and witness the wildlife, flora, and fauna unique to this region.

San Francisco Plantation

San Francisco Plantation is a stunning example of the Greek Revival architecture of the mid-1800s. This historic site is a great opportunity to learn about Louisiana plantation culture and the sugar industry’s impact on the state.

St. John the Baptist Catholic Church

Experience the iconic beauty of Gothic architecture at St. John the Baptist Catholic Church. This church, built-in 1923, welcomes visitors to admire its intricate stained-glass windows, classic statues, and quiet serenity.

Natural Attractions of LaPlace: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bonnet Carre Spillway

Enjoy a relaxing day at the Bonnet Carre Spillway, where you can walk or bike along the riverside and capture breathtaking views of Lake Pontchartrain. Spot various types of wildlife while taking in the splendid nature that surrounds you.

Frenier Landing Boat Launch

Frenier Landing Boat Launch is an ideal spot for fishing enthusiasts, boaters, or anyone seeking a calming place for a picnic with water views. Gaze out into the lake, watch the boats pass by, and soak up the stunning sunsets.

Delta Marina

Delta Marina is a convenient spot for fishermen and boaters looking to embark on a waterfront adventure. It has all the essential amenities, including boat rentals, a bait shop, an RV park, and a restaurant with authentic Cajun cuisine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of LaPlace

LaPlace offers several biking and hiking trails that suit people of all fitness levels. The possibilities are endless, from scenic riverside trails to dense woodlands. Check out Percy Marone Park, which has over five miles of paved trails and multiple rest stations with scenic views of Bayou Segnette.

Best Things to Do in LaPlace: Activities and Tours

LaPlace Farmers Market

Join in and visit the LaPlace Farmers Market, which operates all year round. It’s a great place to find fresh produce, homemade crafts, and even grab a bite at the food trucks. Explore the market and learn more about the local culture and cuisine, and maybe try some delicacies not found elsewhere.

Mardi Gras Museum

Visit the Mardi Gras Museum to learn about LaPlace’s history of the carnival celebrations. This museum is filled with vibrant and intricately detailed costumes, masks, and parades floats. It’s impossible to leave this fascinating museum without learning something new about the history and traditions of Louisiana’s Mardi Gras.

Week-Long Itinerary for LaPlace

Day 1: Explore the town center of LaPlace and check out the local shops and eateries. Day 2: Take a tour to the historic San Francisco Plantation, located just 10 minutes outside of LaPlace. Day 3: Catch a glimpse of nature on the Cajun Pride Swamp Tour. Day 4: Rent kayaks or canoes and spend the day paddling on Lake Maurepas. Day 5: Visit the Delta Marina, rent a boat, and try your hand at fishing or crabbing. Day 6: Take a guided tour at the Mardi Gras Museum and learn about the history and traditions of carnival celebrations. Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at the Bonnet Carre Spillway picnicking, sunbathing, and pursuing various outdoor activities.

Food and Drink Scene of LaPlace

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to LaPlace is complete without trying some classic Cajun and Creole dishes. Be sure to indulge in savory gumbo, spicy jambalaya, and shrimp etouffee. Andouille sausage, boudin, and crawfish beignets are must-try delicacies here.

Local Beverages

Grab a refreshing glass of Southern Sweet Tea to beat the Louisiana heat or try one of the local beers. Abita Brewing Company is a fan favorite and is based in the nearby town of Covington, LA.

Where to Stay in LaPlace: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Some of the best places to stay in LaPlace include Hampton Inn, Best Western LaPlace Inn, and Comfort Inn LaPlace. These hotels provide comfortable rooms, excellent service, and amenities such as free Wi-Fi, a complimentary breakfast, and a gym.

What to Buy in LaPlace: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Satisfy your souvenir needs and bring home something unique to remember your trip to Louisiana. Catch some local flavor by bringing home a jar of Tabasco hot sauce and Tony Chachere’s Famous Creole Seasoning blend. Don’t forget to stop by the LaPlace Farmer’s Market to stock up on fresh produce and locally made crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of LaPlace

LaPlace is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, and its unique commercialized Creole culture is famous worldwide. Visitors can immerse themselves in this culture and explore the various customs and history of these states.

Best Time to Visit LaPlace

The best time to visit LaPlace falls between April to October, as it’s a sunny and warm season. This period offers numerous outdoor activities, refreshing cool drinks, and mouthwatering seasonal food. The upcoming fall also brings beautiful colors and an overall incredible experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of LaPlace

Do:

  • Try out the local foods and beverages.
  • Tip generously, as it’s a part of the Southern hospitality culture.
  • Visit the local attractions.
  • Be polite and friendly to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen and mosquito spray.
  • Make unnecessary noise in public places, such as cemeteries.
  • Wear anything disrespectful to local culture or religious sites.
  • Litter or leave any trash.

Safety and Health

Safety in LaPlace

LaPlace is generally a safe destination to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions. Do not leave your belongings unattended, always lock your doors and be aware of your surroundings. Check local news and weather forecasts to prepare for potential risks like hurricanes.

Health in LaPlace

Visitors to LaPlace must always stay hydrated as temperatures average in the high 80s- low 90s degrees Fahrenheit. Apply sunscreen and insect repellent regularly, as it is also a humid region. Stay cognizant of potential health risks while enjoying outdoor activities such as hiking and swimming.

Getting to LaPlace: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near LaPlace

The nearest airports are the Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport (MSY) and Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport (BTR). Both airports offer rental cars, shuttle services, and public transportation to LaPlace.

Bus Stations Near LaPlace

The Greyhound bus company has regular services to and from LaPlace. You can find their station at the LaPlace Bus Station (122 Joe Parquet Circle, LaPlace, LA 70068).

Other Options

Renting a car or using ride-sharing apps such as Uber or Lyft is a convenient option for those looking to move around in LaPlace.

How to Get Around in LaPlace

Walking or biking is a popular way to get around in LaPlace, especially if you’re exploring the town center. It’s also possible to rent vehicles, such as cars, motorcycles, and boats. Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are available, but you may need to wait longer to get a ride in a small town like LaPlace.

Where to Go Next from LaPlace: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

LaPlace provides excellent access to other popular Louisiana destinations. Don’t miss out on visiting the vibrant New Orleans, which is just a 30-minute drive from LaPlace. Baton Rouge, the state capital, is only an hour’s drive away, where you can explore Louisiana’s ultimate historical and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

LaPlace is a Southern sweetheart, with an effortless blend of nature, culture, food, and history. The town is rich in diversity and traditions, and its charm can captivate any traveler. So pack your bags, grab your sun hats, and head to LaPlace to explore the best of Louisiana’s River Parishes!
