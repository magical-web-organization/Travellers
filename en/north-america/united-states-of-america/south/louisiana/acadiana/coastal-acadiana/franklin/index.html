Introduction to Franklin

Located in Coastal Acadiana in the southern United States, Franklin boasts a rich history and breathtaking scenery that make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a taste of Louisiana’s unique culture. From exploring historic museums and natural attractions to indulging in delicious local cuisine and shopping for souvenirs, there’s something for everyone in Franklin.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Evangeline Memorial Park - A beautiful park dedicated to the famous Cajun heroine, Evangeline
  • The Chateau de la Riviere-House of Memories - A historic mansion turned museum that depicts the lives of Franklin’s residents in the 19th century
  • Louisiana Shrimp and Petroleum Festival Building and Museum - A museum dedicated to two of Louisiana’s largest industries: shrimping and oil

Natural Attractions of Franklin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Fausse Pointe State Park - A nature lover’s paradise with a diverse range of flora and fauna, perfect for hiking, camping, and fishing
  • Bayou Teche National Wildlife Refuge - A sprawling wetland preserve home to dozens of endangered species, including the American alligator and the Louisiana pine snake
  • Weeks Island Preserve - A unique salt dome island that boasts incredible geological formations, rare bird species, and a fascinating history as a former mine

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin

  • Franklin Canal Bike Trail - A scenic trail that runs alongside the Franklin Canal, perfect for avid bikers and hikers
  • Lake Fausse Pointe State Park Trails - A network of trails that wind through the park’s diverse landscapes, ranging from swamp to hardwood forest

Best Things to Do in Franklin: Activities and Tours

  • Louisiana Shrimp and Petroleum Festival - A lively annual festival that celebrates the history and culture of Franklin’s two largest industries
  • Atchafalaya Basin Swamp Tours - A guided tour of the world’s largest freshwater swamp, giving visitors the opportunity to spot alligators, birds, and other wildlife up close
  • Cypress Bayou Casino - A popular casino and entertainment complex that offers numerous games, dining options, and live performances

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin

Day Activities
1 Visit Evangeline Memorial Park, Louisiana Shrimp and Petroleum Festival Building and Museum, and Lake Fausse Pointe State Park
2 Explore the Chateau de la Riviere-House of Memories and take a Cypress Bayou Casino
3 Hike the Franklin Canal Bike Trail and try Cajun cuisine at a local restaurant
4 Go on an Atchafalaya Basin Swamp Tour and visit the Bayou Teche National Wildlife Refuge

6 Take a historical walking tour of Franklin’s downtown district and shop for souvenirs in local boutiques

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gumbo - A hearty stew made with seafood, sausage, and rice that is a staple of Cajun cuisine
  • Jambalaya - A spicy rice dish that often includes chicken, sausage, and vegetables
  • Crawfish Etouffee - A creamy, crawfish-based dish served over rice
  • Boudin - A traditional pork and rice sausage that is a unique Louisiana specialty

Local Beverages

  • Abita Beer - A Louisiana-based brewery that offers a wide range of craft beers
  • Rougaroux Rum - A unique rum made from Louisiana sugarcane that is distilled in Thibodaux, just a short drive from Franklin
  • Sazerac - A classic New Orleans cocktail made with rye whiskey, bitters, and absinthe

Where to Stay in Franklin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Forest Inn - A comfortable hotel with spacious rooms and an on-site restaurant
  • Comfort Inn & Suites - A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast
  • Chateau Saint Denis - A boutique hotel located in historic Natchitoches, about an hour from Franklin

What to Buy in Franklin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cajun Spices - A blend of herbs and spices that is essential to Southern cooking
  • Handmade Pottery - Beautiful pieces of pottery made by local artisans
  • Cypress Knees - Unique and interesting pieces of wood that come from the knees of cypress trees

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin

Franklin is located in the heart of Acadiana, a region known for its unique Cajun culture. Visitors to Franklin can expect to experience the music, food, and language of the Cajun people and should embrace the laid-back, relaxed pace of life that is common in Southern Louisiana.

Best Time to Visit Franklin

The best time to visit Franklin is in the fall when the weather is mild and the humidity is low. This is also the time when many of the town’s festivals and cultural events take place, including the Louisiana Shrimp and Petroleum Festival and the Bayou Teche Black Bear Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin

Do:

  • Try Cajun and Creole cuisine
  • Take a swamp tour to see alligators and other wildlife up close
  • Embrace the relaxed pace of life in Southern Louisiana

Don’t:

  • Forget insect repellent when visiting natural areas
  • Take photographs of local residents or their property without their permission
  • Pass up an opportunity to try boudin or crawfish

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin

Franklin is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should take precautions when exploring natural areas, such as wearing insect repellent and being aware of poisonous snakes and alligators.

Health in Franklin

Visitors to Franklin should be aware of the high humidity and heat during the summer months and take steps to stay hydrated. It’s also important to protect against insect bites in order to prevent the spread of diseases like West Nile virus and Zika virus.

Getting to Franklin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Franklin

  • Lafayette Regional Airport - Located about an hour from Franklin and offers flights to and from several major U.S. cities
  • Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport - Located about two hours from Franklin and offers flights to and from destinations worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Franklin

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Lafayette - Located about an hour from Franklin and offers connections to destinations throughout the United States.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing service to reach Franklin from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Franklin

The most convenient way to get around Franklin is by car, as the town’s attractions are spread out over a large area. Visitors can also rent bikes at Lake Fausse Pointe State Park to explore the park’s trails.

Where to Go Next from Franklin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Orleans - Located about two hours from Franklin, New Orleans is a vibrant city known for its music, food, and culture.
  • Lafayette - Located about an hour from Franklin, Lafayette is a bustling college town with a rich Cajun culture.
  • Baton Rouge - Located about two hours from Franklin, Baton Rouge is the state capital and home to many historic museums and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Franklin is a hidden gem in Louisiana that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Cajun culture and natural beauty. From exploring historic museums and natural attractions to indulging in delicious local cuisine and shopping for souvenirs, there’s something for everyone in Franklin. Plan your visit today to experience the best that this charming town has to offer.
