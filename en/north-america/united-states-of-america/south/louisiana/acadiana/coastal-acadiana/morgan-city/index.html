Introduction to Morgan City

Located in Coastal Acadiana, Louisiana, Morgan City is a charming city known for its rich history and natural beauty. This small town sits on the banks of the Atchafalaya River and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Whether you’re looking to hike the local trails, dive into the local culture, or simply soak up the scenic views, Morgan City has something to offer everyone. Read on to discover the top attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, and local culture and customs of Morgan City.

List of Places to Visit in Morgan City

To make the most of your visit to Morgan City, be sure to check out these top-rated attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • International Petroleum Museum and Exposition: This museum showcases oil industry history, technology, and rescue equipment.
  • Brownell Memorial Park and Carillon Tower: Take a guided tour and learn about the park’s significance as a WWII memorial, the stunning views from the top of the tower are priceless.
  • Tiffany Art Glass Display: This museum exhibits and sells handmade art glass including the famously beautiful stained glass works by Louis Comfort Tiffany.
  • St. Mary Parish Library: This public library houses a vast collection of books and historical documents related to regional history.

Natural Attractions of Morgan City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Atchafalaya Basin: This is the largest swamp in the United States, which offers visitors the opportunity to hike, bike, fish, or photograph wildlife.
  • Dr. J.H. Aucoin Marina & Bayou Vista Paddling Trail: Rent kayaks and paddles and explore the calm waters of Bayou Vista.
  • Lake End Park: This park features hiking trails, boat ramps, picnic areas and offers a gorgeous view of the sunsets over Lake Palourde.
  • West St. Mary Civic Center Farmers Market: Celebrate the local culture, foods, and goods of the community.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morgan City

Morgan City boasts excellent trails for hikers and bikers of all levels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Lake Palourde Bike Trail: This 3.5-mile trail winds through Lake Palourde, offers beautiful lake views, and access to bird watching stands.
  • Franklin Bike Trail: This 3.75-mile trail runs parallel to Bayou Teche, offering lovely views of the bayous and the surrounding area.
  • Atchafalaya Basin Bridge: Enjoy the scenic nature trails long the Morgan City to Lafayette ends of the bridge.

Best Things to Do in Morgan City: Activities and Tours

  • Swamp Tours: Take a boat tour of the region’s swamps and learn about the local wildlife.
  • Charter Fishing: For avid anglers, the Morgan City experience is incomplete without a day of fishing on the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: This is a popular activity among outdoor enthusiasts, as kayaking and canoeing is one of the best ways to explore the area’s bayous and marshes.
  • Golf Courses: There are several fantastic golf courses in the area, including the Atchafalaya course.

Week-Long Itinerary for Morgan City

Day 1: Visit the International Petroleum Museum, learn about the history of the oil industry, then explore Lake Palourde Bike Trail.

Day 2: Take a Swamp Tour of the Atchafalaya Basin, then enjoy authentic Cajun cuisine from the area.

Day 3: Take a Kayaking and Canoeing trip through the calm waters of Bayou Vista, then catch a sunset at Lake End Park.

Day 4: Visit the Brownell Memorial Park and Carillon Tower, one of the most significant WWII memorials, followed by a chartered fishing trip in the Gulf of Mexico.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at the beautiful Atchafalaya course.

Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Tiffany Art Glass Display, then watch a live performance at the Teche theatre.

Day 7: Visit the St. Mary Parish Library, access the historical documentation that details the local culture and history.

Food and Drink Scene of Morgan City

Morgan City features several outstanding restaurants and bars that visitors don’t want to miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Platter: This Cajun/Creole dish is locally sourced and consists of various fresh catch seafood, including shrimp, oysters, and catfish.
  • Gumbo: A traditional New Orleans dish, which is made with a dark roux and features seafood, Andouille sausage, and rice.
  • Po’ boy: This sandwich is filled with fried seafood, such as shrimp, oysters, or catfish, and dressed with mayonnaise, lettuce, and tomatoes.
  • Jambalaya: A spicy rice dish that features sausage, vegetables, and seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Abita Beer: A popular Louisiana beer brewer, that produces sour beers, such as the strawberry harvest lager, Amber Ale, and more.
  • Cajun Bloody Mary: A spicy, thick, and hearty drink made with vodka or gin, tomato juice, and a generous mix of spices.

Where to Stay in Morgan City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Morgan City has several excellent places to stay for those visiting the region.

  • Holiday Inn: A centrally located hotel that offers excellent amenities such as a pool, fitness room, and 24-hour business centre.
  • Plantation Inn: A historic boutique-style hotel that offers elegant rooms with Jacuzzis.
  • OYO Hotel Morgan City Baldwin: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable and straightforward accommodations.

What to Buy in Morgan City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Creole Tomatoes: These tomatoes are grown in the rich Louisiana soil and are packed with flavour. They are fantastic for making salads, salsa, or even sandwiches.
  • Hot Sauce: Louisiana Hot Sauce is world-renowned, so bring some back to friends and family for a kick of spicy flavour.
  • Cajun Spices: This mix of spices is the backbone of Louisiana cuisine. With a container of this spice mix, you can add a taste of Cajun cooking to any dish at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Morgan City

Like much of Coastal Louisiana, Morgan City is home to a rich and vibrant culture steeped in history and tradition. Here are a few fun facts about the city’s culture and customs:

  • Cajun/Creole Dialect: Many of the locals speak in their dialect, which is a variation of French and African languages called “Patois.”
  • Music and Dance: Visitors may find themselves tapping their feet to the beat of a lively Cajun or Zydeco tune, popular genres of music that are quintessentially southern Louisiana.
  • Festivals and Celebrations: Morgan City hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Cypress Sawmill Festival, the Shrimp and Petroleum Festival, and the Louisiana Fur and Wildlife Festival.

Best Time to Visit Morgan City

The best time to visit Morgan City is from November to April when the weather is mild, and the humidity is comparatively low. This time frame is ideal for outdoor adventures and exploring the city’s beautiful natural attractions. Summer months can get very hot and humid, making outdoor activities uncomfortable, but September to November months are more favourable for sports fishers who come to take on Tuna, Mahi-Mahi, Red Snapper, and many other fish.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morgan City

Do:

  • Try the local seafood - Morgan City seafood is fresh, locally sourced, and incredibly delicious!
  • Embrace the local culture - From Cajun cuisine to Zydeco music, Morgan City is steeped in culture, so take the time to learn and appreciate it.
  • Visit the museums - Morgan City is rich in history, so take some time to explore the area’s museums and historical sites to learn about its past.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for directions - The locals are friendly and are there to help so that you can explore the city with ease.
  • Don’t miss the seafood festivals - Morgan City’s annual seafood festivals are a must-attend event.
  • Don’t litter - Morgan City’s natural beauty is one of its biggest attractions, so please help keep it clean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morgan City

Morgan City is a relatively safe city, but visitors should use common sense and take precautions such as:

  • Being aware of your surroundings in unfamiliar areas
  • Locking your car doors and not leaving valuables visible
  • Avoiding travelling alone at night
  • Watching your step - the city’s hiking and biking trails can be tricky to navigate in some areas.

Health in Morgan City

Visitors to Morgan City should ensure that they have travel insurance and are up-to-date on all necessary vaccinations. Because it is a region with high humidity, it is essential to hydrate regularly to avoid dehydration and drink legibly treated and boiled water to avoid any water-borne disease.

Getting to Morgan City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Morgan City is easy to access via various transportation modes.

Airports Near Morgan City

  • Lafayette Regional Airport: This airport is approximately 75 miles from Morgan City and is serviced by several major airlines, such as American Airlines, Delta, and United.
  • Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport: Located around 100 miles from Morgan City, and is a hub for various carriers around the globe.

Bus Stations Near Morgan City

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound bus station is in Lafayette.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Rental cars can be hired from most airports in the region as well as from major car rental companies.

How to Get Around in Morgan City

Although most of Morgan City’s main attractions are either within walking or biking distance, renting a car during your stay is highly recommended as some attractions are situated across Louisiana bayous and drive-through nature trails.

Where to Go Next from Morgan City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region after visiting Morgan City, consider these nearby destinations:

  • Lafayette: This city is known for its unique food culture, music, and festivals and is located about an hour’s drive from Morgan City.
  • New Orleans: This famous city, known for its Jazz music, French-Creole cuisine, and Mardi-Gras festivals, is a 2-hour drive from Morgan City.
  • Baton Rouge: The capital city of Louisiana, Baton Rouge, is located about an hour and a half from Morgan City and is home to several historic landmarks, recreational sites and cultural centres.

Conclusion

Morgan City is a beautiful and peaceful destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture vultures. The locals’ hospitality, stunning landscapes, quaint restaurants and bars will make your visit enjoyable, fun, and memorable. Whether it’s exploring cultural and historical sites or enjoying the natural beauty surrounding the area, Morgan City is a must-visit destination for any traveller seeking a unique and genuine experience.
