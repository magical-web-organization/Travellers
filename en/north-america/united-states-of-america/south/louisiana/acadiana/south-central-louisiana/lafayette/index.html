Introduction to Lafayette

Located in South Central Louisiana, in the heart of Acadiana, Lafayette is a vibrant city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known for its Cajun and Creole heritage, Lafayette boasts an eclectic mix of French, African, and Native American influences that make it a fascinating and unique destination.

In this comprehensive tour guide for Lafayette, you’ll discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local customs and culture to help you make the most of your trip to this charming southern city.

List of Places to Visit in Lafayette

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vermilionville: This living history museum showcases the lifestyles and crafts of the Acadian, Native American, and Creole cultures that once thrived in the region.
  • Acadian Cultural Center: Learn about the history and culture of Acadiana and the Cajun people at this National Park Service site.
  • Alexandre Mouton House: Visit the former home of former Louisiana Governor Alexandre Mouton, which has been restored to its 1800s splendor.
  • Lafayette Science Museum: Explore the interactive exhibits and planetarium shows at this museum dedicated to science and technology.

Natural Attractions of Lafayette: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Atchafalaya Basin: The largest swamp in North America is a haven for wildlife and offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and birdwatching.
  • Lake Martin: This serene lake is a popular spot for kayaking, fishing, and birdwatching, with plenty of scenic hiking trails.
  • Jean Lafitte National Historical Park and Preserve: This park offers miles of trails through wetlands, forested areas, and along the Bayou Vermilion, as well as a visitor center showcasing the region’s history and culture.
  • Cypress Island Preserve: Nature lovers will appreciate the views of cypress trees, wildlife, and birds that inhabit this beautiful preserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lafayette

  • Bayou Vermilion Trail: This scenic trail follows the Bayou Vermilion for 10 miles and passes by parks, neighborhoods, and historic sites.
  • Acadiana Park Nature Station Trail: This easy, two-mile trail is perfect for families and offers views of wildflowers, wildlife, and native plants.
  • Pack and Paddle Paddle Trail: Kayakers and canoers will love the peaceful waterways of Lake Martin and the Atchafalaya Basin, with plenty of opportunities to spot alligators and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Lafayette: Activities and Tours

  • Cajun Food Tours: Discover the flavors of Cajun and Creole cuisine on a walking or driving tour of the city.
  • Festival International de Louisiane: Join the locals for this annual five-day cultural celebration, featuring live music, food, and arts and crafts.
  • Louisiana Hot Air Balloon Championship: Watch a colorful display of hot air balloons launching into the sky during this exciting event held each spring.
  • Zydeco Dance Lessons: Learn the unique steps to this lively dance at a local dance hall or festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Lafayette

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crawfish étouffée: A classic Cajun dish made with crawfish, roux, and vegetables, served over rice.
  • Boudin: A spicy sausage made with pork, rice, and vegetables, often served with crackers and hot sauce.
  • Gumbo: A hearty soup made with seafood or meat, vegetables, and roux, served over rice.
  • Jambalaya: A one-pot meal made with rice, meat, and vegetables, often seasoned with Creole spices.

Local Beverages

  • Abita Beer: A popular Louisiana-based craft brewery known for its range of beers, including the classic Abita Amber and seasonal varieties.
  • Café au Lait: A strong coffee mixed with hot milk, often served with beignets.
  • Hurricane: A fruity rum-based cocktail that originated in New Orleans and is a staple of Louisiana bar culture.

Where to Stay in Lafayette: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Courtyard Lafayette South: A modern hotel with an outdoor pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.
  • T’Frere’s House: A charming guesthouse with beautifully-appointed rooms and a lush, park-like setting.
  • Travelodge Lafayette: A budget-friendly option with clean, comfortable rooms and easy access to downtown Lafayette.

What to Buy in Lafayette: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mardi Gras Beads: Colorful necklaces thrown during Mardi Gras celebrations, available year-round at souvenir shops.
  • Hot Sauce: An ubiquitous condiment in Louisiana, with a wide variety of local brands and flavors available.
  • Cajun Seasoning: A blend of spices, including paprika, garlic, and cayenne pepper, used to Cajun-style dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Lafayette

Lafayette is known for its joie de vivre, or love of life, which is evident in its vibrant music, food, and cultural scene. Cajun and Creole traditions, including zydeco music, dance, and cuisine, are an important part of the local culture.

Casual dress is common in Lafayette, but it’s customary to dress up for special occasions, such as Mardi Gras or a wedding. It’s also considered polite to greet people with a friendly “bonjour” or “hello” when entering a room or public space.

Best Time to Visit Lafayette

The best time to visit Lafayette is in the fall or spring, when temperatures are mild and festivals and events are in full swing. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lafayette

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including crawfish, gumbo, and jambalaya.
  • Do attend a live music event or take a zydeco dance lesson.
  • Do visit the natural attractions, such as Lake Martin and the Atchafalaya Basin.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t ignore local customs or culture.
  • Don’t forget to tip at restaurants and for other services.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lafayette

Lafayette is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take typical precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight. It’s also advisable to stay in well-lit areas at night and to avoid walking alone.

Health in Lafayette

Lafayette has several hospitals and medical facilities, including the University Hospital and Clinics at Lafayette and Lafayette General Medical Center. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and to carry any necessary medications or prescriptions with you.

Getting to Lafayette: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lafayette

  • Lafayette Regional Airport: Located just three miles southeast of downtown Lafayette, this small airport has daily flights to Houston and Atlanta.

Bus Stations Near Lafayette

  • Greyhound: Located in the heart of downtown Lafayette, Greyhound offers daily bus service to and from major cities throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The Amtrak station in nearby New Iberia, just 20 miles from Lafayette, provides daily train service to New Orleans, Baton Rouge, and beyond.
  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at the Lafayette Regional Airport and in the downtown area.

How to Get Around in Lafayette

The best way to get around Lafayette is by car, but there are also taxis and ride-sharing services available. Biking is becoming increasingly popular, with several bike rental and share options in the city.

Where to Go Next from Lafayette: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Orleans: Just a two-hour drive from Lafayette, New Orleans offers a rich mix of culture, history, and nightlife.
  • Lake Charles: About an hour’s drive west of Lafayette, Lake Charles is known for its casinos, outdoor recreation, and charming downtown area.
  • St. Martinville: The birthplace of Cajun culture, St. Martinville boasts historic landmarks, great food, and a serene setting along the Bayou Teche.

Conclusion

Lafayette, Louisiana, is a destination unlike any other, with its blend of French, African, and Native American influences, vibrant cultural scene, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a foodie looking to explore Cajun and Creole cuisine, a music lover in search of zydeco rhythms, or an outdoor enthusiast seeking adventure in the swamps and wetlands, Lafayette has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your perfect trip to this unique and exciting southern city.
