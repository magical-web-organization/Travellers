Introduction to St. Martinville

St. Martinville is a small French-influenced town in Acadiana, South Central Louisiana. The town is known for its historically rich French culture, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. The town is also home to some important cultural attractions, including museums and historical places, and offers numerous outdoor adventures.

Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in history, enjoy outdoor recreational activities, or experience local culture and customs, St. Martinville has something to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Martinville

Day 1: Explore the Longfellow-Evangeline State Historic Site and learn about the French culture and heritage of the area.

Day 2: Take a boat ride on the Bayou Teche, and explore the beautiful natural scenery this town has to offer.

Day 3: Visit the Acadian Memorial and learn about the history of the Acadian people, and their journey to Louisiana.

Day 4: Rent a bike and take a ride on the Bayou Teche Bikeway, which runs through the town’s charming neighborhoods and historic district.

Day 5: Explore the beautiful Lake Martin Wildlife Refuge, and enjoy its diverse wildlife and ecosystems.

Day 6: Attend the famous Breaux Bridge Crawfish Festival and immerse yourself in the local culture and customs.

Day 7: Take a guided swamp tour and discover the unique beauty of the Atchafalaya Basin

Local Culture and Customs of St. Martinville

St. Martinville has a rich French heritage, and the town’s residents speak a unique dialect of French, called Cajun French. The town is also known for its traditional music, including Cajun, Zydeco, and Jazz.

Best Time to Visit St. Martinville

The best time to visit St. Martinville is during the Spring and Fall seasons, when the weather is mild and the town celebrates various festivals, including the Breaux Bridge Crawfish Festival.
