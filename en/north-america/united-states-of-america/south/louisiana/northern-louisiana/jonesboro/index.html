Introduction to Jonesboro

Nestled in Northern Louisiana, Jonesboro offers travelers a charming retreat with its scenic beauty, historic sites, and Southern hospitality. Whether you are looking for a peaceful getaway or an outdoor adventure, Jonesboro has something for everyone. In this article, we will guide you through the must-see attractions, local culture, and practical tips to make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Jonesboro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jackson Parish Museum: Discover the history of the region through exhibits on Native American culture, early settlers, and notable people from the area.
  • Louisiana Political Museum and Hall of Fame: Learn about Louisiana’s political legacy through exhibits on governors, state officials, and influential figures.
  • Jimmie Davis Tabernacle: Attend a gospel concert or tour the childhood home of Jimmie Davis, Louisiana’s two-time governor and country music icon.
  • Piney Hills Heritage Museum: Explore the Cajun and Creole culture in Jonesboro through exhibits on the region’s food, music, and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Jonesboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Caney Lake Recreation Area: Rent a boat or a kayak and enjoy fishing or a scenic ride on the 5,000-acre Caney Lake.
  • Kisatchie National Forest: Hike through the 604,000-acre forest and discover rare species of plants and animals.
  • Castor Creek Scenic Area: Take a leisurely walk or bike ride on the 8-mile multi-use trail and enjoy the beautiful views of Castor Creek.
  • Lincoln Parish Park: Camp out or have a picnic under the shady oaks and pine trees at this park that offers fishing, hiking trails, and a disc golf course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jonesboro

  • Clarks Creek Trail: A 2-mile trail through the Kisatchie National Forest that features panoramic views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Lincoln Parish Park Trail: A 7-mile loop trail that offers a moderate hike through the woods and past the lake.
  • Castor Creek Bike Trail: An 8-mile multi-use path along Castor Creek that is suitable for beginner to intermediate bikers.

Best Things to Do in Jonesboro: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the J-Swirl Art Gallery and Studio to see the work of local artists and participate in a pottery class.
  • Take a guided tour of Chatham High School, the birthplace of the Louisiana High School Athletic Association.
  • Attend the Louisiana Peach Festival in June and enjoy local food, music, and festivities celebrating the area’s peach harvest.
  • Explore the Pecanland Mall, the largest shopping center in Northeast Louisiana, offering a variety of shops, restaurants, and entertainment options.

Food and Drink Scene of Jonesboro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boudin sausage: A Cajun specialty made with pork, rice, and seasonings.
  • Fried catfish: A Southern favorite served with hush puppies and coleslaw.
  • Crawfish étouffée: A rich stew made of crawfish, roux, and vegetables, served over rice.
  • Peach cobbler: A sweet and fluffy dessert made with fresh Louisiana peaches.

Local Beverages

  • Abita beer: A popular Louisiana beer that comes in a variety of flavors, including Amber, Purple Haze, and Strawberry Lager.
  • Sazerac: A classic New Orleans cocktail made with rye whiskey, Peychaud’s Bitters, and absinthe.
  • Hurricane: A fruity rum-based cocktail that is a staple of Mardi Gras and other Louisiana celebrations.

Where to Stay in Jonesboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jonesboro Inn and Suites: A comfortable and affordable hotel with modern amenities and a central location.
  • Dubach Inn: A charming bed and breakfast with a historic ambiance and a warm welcome.
  • Lakeside Lodge: A family-friendly resort with a cozy cabin and a beautiful view of Caney Lake.

What to Buy in Jonesboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Louisiana pecans: A delicious and healthy snack that can be found in many local shops and farmers’ markets.
  • Mardi Gras beads: Colorful necklaces that are traditionally thrown during the annual Mardi Gras festivities.
  • Homemade jams and jellies: Made from fresh Louisiana fruits, these preserves are a great way to take a taste of Jonesboro back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Jonesboro

Jonesboro is a welcoming and hospitable town with a strong sense of community. Locals take pride in their Southern heritage and are happy to share their traditions with visitors. Respectful behavior and good manners are appreciated, and a friendly smile or a kind word can go a long way in making new friends.

Best Time to Visit Jonesboro

The best time to visit Jonesboro is in the spring (March through May) or fall (September through November), when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the foliage is at its best. Summer can be hot and humid, with occasional thunderstorms and hurricanes, while winter can be chilly and rainy, with occasional snow or ice. The Louisiana Peach Festival in June is a highlight of the summer season and a great time to enjoy local food, music, and fun.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jonesboro

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take plenty of water and sunscreen when going outdoors.
  • Enjoy the Southern cuisine and local beverages.
  • Be friendly and talk to locals.

Don’t:

  • Assume stereotypes or prejudices about the area and its people.
  • Litter or damage the natural or cultural landmarks.
  • Ignore warnings or advice from locals or authorities.
  • Be disrespectful or rude to others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jonesboro

Jonesboro is a safe and peaceful town with low crime rates and friendly neighbors. However, travelers should be aware of the risks and take precautions when dealing with natural hazards or emergencies. It is a good idea to have a first-aid kit, a map, and a communication device when going outdoors, and to follow the regulations and rules of the parks and forests.

Health in Jonesboro

Jonesboro has a good healthcare system and several clinics and hospitals for emergencies or general health issues. It is recommended that travelers have a medical insurance and consult their doctor about the medical conditions and precautions before visiting.

Getting to Jonesboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jonesboro is accessible by several means of transportation, including:

Airports Near Jonesboro

  • Monroe Regional Airport (MLU): A regional airport with daily flights to major hubs, such as Dallas and Houston.
  • Shreveport Regional Airport (SHV): A larger airport with domestic and international flights to several destinations.

Bus Stations Near Jonesboro

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: A national bus company that has a station in Ruston, LA, about 20 miles from Jonesboro.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in Jonesboro and surrounding areas.
  • Private shuttle: Some hotels and resorts offer private shuttle services to and from the airport or bus station.

How to Get Around in Jonesboro

The best way to get around Jonesboro is by car, as most attractions and amenities are spread out and not well connected by public transportation. However, there are some taxi companies and ride-sharing services available for those without a car.

Where to Go Next from Jonesboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shreveport: A vibrant city with casinos, museums, and a lively music scene.
  • Natchitoches: A quaint town known for its historic architecture, delicious food, and charming Christmas festival.
  • Monroe: A cultural center that offers museums, galleries, parks, and a riverfront entertainment district.

Conclusion

Jonesboro is a hidden gem in Northern Louisiana that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and friendly hospitality. We hope this travel guide has inspired you to visit and explore this charming town and discover its many treasures. Safe travels!
