Introduction to Bossier City

Located in Northern Louisiana, Bossier City is a bustling community that offers southern hospitality, rich history, and bountiful charms of life. Originally known as Bennett Bayou, the area transformed itself into one of Louisiana’s most vibrant cities. Bossier City offers plenty of opportunities to explore, from adventurous outdoor activities to upscale shopping centers that incorporate culture and history. Known as the home for Barksdale Air Force Base, Bossier City welcomes visitors from different parts of the world alike.

List of Places to Visit in Bossier City

Here are some of the top attractions you should visit in Bossier City.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Barksdale Global Power Museum: Home to many artifacts of American airpower, this museum includes an astonishing collection of historic aviation memorabilia.
  • Louisiana Boardwalk Outlets: Located on the banks of the Red River, this outlet mall has more than 60 stores offering top designer products.
  • Sci-Port Discovery Center: Providing space to children and adults alike, Sci-Port Development Center encompasses science and technology exhibits, learning displays, an IMAX Dome theater, and an earthquake simulator.
  • Bossier Arts Council Gallery: This historical building of the Central Fire Station houses the Bossier Arts Council with events exhibiting local art and culture in a vivid manner.
  • Red River National Wildlife Refuge: Red River Wildlife invigorates with wildlife and landscape views, a great place to stroll the trails and view birds and animals native to Louisiana.

Natural Attractions of Bossier City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Red River: A great way to enjoy the outdoors, Riverfront vistas and lengthy sidewalks provide a great way to bring the family together for a leisure outdoor activity.
  • The Gardens of the American Rose Center: Boasting over 500 areal roses and lands arranged in various garden courtyard settings, this mesmerizing garden the country’s largest park for roses.
  • Black Bayou Lake National Wildlife Refuge: Explore this vast area’s diverse fauna and flora, this wildlife refuge awakens with its views of swampy areas and varying wildlife.
  • Cypress Nature Study Center: A peaceful space to relax and expand, the center offers learning opportunities with opportunities to explore cypress swamps followed by trekking on boardwalks and trails.
  • Bossier City Farmers Market: A unique shopping experience provided entirely by local farmers and retail outlet owners showcasing local produce, arts, and craftsmen.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bossier City

Bossier City’s trails and parks offer lengthy bike paths and walking trails boasting lovely scenery and ideal for families and those seeking solitude. Some of the city’s best trails include:

  • Red River Bike-Walking Trail: Almost 2 miles of the scenic trail is constructed above the Red River, offering panoramic views of the lush surroundings.
  • North Bossier Park: This 40.7-acre park is equipped with playgrounds, soccer fields, baseball diamonds, tennis and basketball courts, and picnic areas, including a difficult 9-hole disc golf course with a length of 1.3 miles.
  • Cypress Black Bayou Bike Trailhead: Experience the serene black water bayou by venturing on this easy trailhead measuring around 3 miles.

Best Things to Do in Bossier City: Activities and Tours

From gaming to sampling local cuisine, Bossier City has a unique variety of things to do and places to see. Some of the most popular activities and tours include:

  • Horseshoe Casino: A dominant casino destination in the area provides a luxury bedroom with premium poker room services an array of table games and slots to indulgently engage in.
  • Highland Jazz and Blues Festival: a concert event held annually to uplift your spirits and enjoyable performance from the Jazz to Blues genres.
  • Tours to Plantations: Explore the tale of how southern Chicot State Park formed with distinct food, music, and primary practices of that era turned present for all visitors to gain a glimpse into the lifestyle of Louisiana history.
  • Louisiana State Exhibit Museum: Housing a compelling array of historical artifacts, taxidermy exhibits, and the musical history of Louisiana, to name a few.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bossier City

Day 1: Tour around city and historical visit to the Barksdale Global Power Museum. Day 2: Explore nature and admire the scenic beauty of Red River National Wildlife Refuge, building to indulge Louisiana’s beauty. Day 3: Embark on a biking adventure on the Red River Bike-Walking Trail and shop at Bossier City Farmers Market, among other attractions. Day 4: Tour Cypress Black Bayou Bike Trailhead - a mandatory tourist location for bikers and nature enthusiasts. Day 5: Shop at Louisiana Boardwalk Outlet for designer clothes and an amazing range of custom products. Day 6: Explore the history of Louisiana State Exhibit Museum through artifacts and interactive displays. Day 7: Visit the Sci-Port Development Center and take a guided tour to learn about science, technology, and Louisiana’s role in space exploration.

Food and Drink Scene of Bossier City

Bossier City offers an exciting food and drink scene for both tourists and locals. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crawfish: This tasty and spicy treat, typical in the New Orleans cuisine, is available at various seafood restaurants in Bossier City.
  • Boudin Balls: A juicy, seasoned combination of sausage rice with crispy fried crust, this popular dish appears on multiple restaurant menus in Louisiana.
  • Jambalaya: A mouth-watering dish with rice, sausage, chicken, seafood, and vegetables, this traditional Cajun dish is found in most food eateries in Bossier City.

Local Beverages

  • Pecan Beer: It’s a top-notch, delicious beer made from pecans and roasted sweet potatoes found primarily in Bossier City’s breweries.
  • Hurricane: A trademark beverage of Louisiana, it is a blend of fruit-flavored sugar rum and juice.

What to Buy in Bossier City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bossier City is famous for its unique souvenirs, local games, and many other items. Here are some souvenirs to bring back home:

  • Gumbo pot: A specially made pot used to cook gumbo prevalent in Louisiana, it is a must-buy souvenir.
  • Jazz Fest Posters: These specially curated, limited edition posters from the Jazz and Blues Festivals held annually are highly collectible.
  • Louisiana Hot Sauce: This brand-exclusive hot sauce is made primarily in Louisiana and its adjoining areas using local ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Bossier City

Best Time to Visit Bossier City

The ideal time to visit Bossier City is from March to June or September to November, with moderate temperatures and manageable crowds.

Getting to Bossier City: Transportation Options and logistics

Here are some transportation options to get to Bossier City:

Bus Stations near Bossier City

  • Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Shreveport, around 8 miles away from Bossier City.

Other Options
