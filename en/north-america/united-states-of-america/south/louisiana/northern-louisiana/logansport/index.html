Introduction to Logansport

Logansport is a small town located in northern Louisiana, part of the United States. The town has a population of just over 1,500 people and has a rich history and culture. Logansport is known for its friendly locals, beautiful scenery, and a variety of outdoor activities. With so much to offer, this town is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the southern charm of Louisiana.

List of Places to Visit in Logansport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Logansport is a town with a rich history and culture. Some of the top museums and historical places that should be on your list include:

  • The Logansport Museum: The museum features exhibits on the town’s past, including Native American artifacts, a collection of old farming equipment, and more.
  • The Sabine Parish Museum: The museum is housed in the former San Miguel de Linares de los Adaes, which was a Spanish fort built in 1721. The museum features exhibits on the history of the fort and the Sabine Parish area.
  • Fort Jesup State Historic Site: The site features a reconstructed 19th-century fort and a visitor center that offers visitors information on the history of the fort and the area.

Natural Attractions of Logansport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Logansport is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery and offers a variety of outdoor activities. Some of the top natural attractions in the area include:

  • Toledo Bend Reservoir: The reservoir is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and swimming. It is also surrounded by national forests, making it a great place for hiking and camping.
  • Hodges Gardens State Park: The park features over 700 acres of gardens, forests, and waterfalls. It is a great place for hiking, camping, and picnicking.
  • Kisatchie National Forest: The forest covers over 604,000 acres and offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, fishing, and hunting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Logansport

If you are looking for some outdoor adventures, Logansport offers a variety of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the top trails in the area:

  • Kisatchie Bayou Loop Trail: This 5.8-mile trail offers scenic views of the Kisatchie Bayou and is suitable for both hiking and biking.
  • Backbone Trail: The Backbone Trail is a 7.7-mile hike that features beautiful wildflowers and scenic views of the surrounding forest.
  • South Loop Trail: This 13.6-mile trail is a popular hiking and biking route that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Logansport: Activities and Tours

Logansport offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top things to do in the area:

  • Toledo Bend Lake Tours: Take a boat tour of Toledo Bend Reservoir and enjoy the scenic views of the lake and surrounding forests.
  • Birding in Kisatchie National Forest: The forest is home to more than 200 species of birds, making it a great destination for birdwatching.
  • Sabine River Bottom Wildlife Tours: Take a guided tour of the Sabine River Bottoms and spot some of the area’s wildlife, including deer, wild hogs, and alligators.

Week-Long Itinerary for Logansport

If you are planning to visit Logansport for a week, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Logansport Museum and learn about the town’s history.
  • Day 2: Take a hike on the Kisatchie Bayou Loop Trail.
  • Day 3: Explore Toledo Bend Reservoir and enjoy some fishing or swimming.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of the Sabine River Bottom Wilderness Area.
  • Day 5: Visit the Hodges Gardens State Park and take a picnic.
  • Day 6: Visit the Sabine Parish Museum and learn about the Spanish fort and history of the area.
  • Day 7: Take a scenic drive through Kisatchie National Forest and stop for a hike on the Backbone Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Logansport

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Logansport has a variety of delicious options to choose from. Here are some dishes you must try:

  • Gumbo: A hearty stew made with sausage, chicken, shrimp, and okra served over rice.
  • Boudin: A spicy sausage made from pork and rice.
  • Jambalaya: A rice dish made with sausage, chicken, and spices

Local Beverages

Logansport is known for its sweet tea and craft beers. Some popular breweries to try include:

  • Crying Eagle Brewing Company: A family-owned brewery that offers a variety of craft beers.
  • Great Raft Brewing: A local brewery that offers a variety of seasonal craft beers.

Where to Stay in Logansport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Logansport offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Best Western Plus DeSoto Inn & Suites: A modern hotel with a pool and fitness center.
  • Starlite Motel: A cozy motel with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Logansport Guest House: This guesthouse offers a historic feel and is located in the heart of the town.

What to Buy in Logansport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Logansport, there are a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some popular items include:

  • Louisiana hot sauce
  • Handmade pottery
  • Local honey

Local Culture and Customs of Logansport

Logansport is a town rich in culture and traditions. Some local customs and traditions include:

  • Mardi Gras celebrations in February or March
  • Louisiana-style cooking and cuisine
  • Southern hospitality and welcoming locals

Best Time to Visit Logansport

If you are planning a trip to Logansport, the best time to visit is during the spring or fall months when temperatures are mild and the scenery is beautiful. Summer months can be hot and humid, so best avoided.

Do’s and Don’ts of Logansport

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including gumbo, boudin, and jambalaya.
  • Take a hike or bike ride through Kisatchie National Forest.
  • Explore the historic attractions, including the Logansport Museum and Sabine Parish Museum.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent when enjoying the outdoors.
  • Skip the chance to interact with the friendly locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Logansport

Logansport is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in sight.

Health in Logansport

Visitors to Logansport should take precautions to protect themselves from the hot and humid southern climate, including drinking plenty of water and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Logansport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Logansport

The nearest airport to Logansport is the Shreveport Regional Airport (SHV), which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Logansport

There is no bus station in Logansport, but visitors can take a Greyhound bus to nearby Mansfield.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or hire a ride-sharing service to get around Logansport and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Logansport

The best way to get around Logansport is by car. Visitors can also take hikes or bike rides to explore the natural scenery.

Where to Go Next from Logansport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Logansport is surrounded by a variety of interesting destinations and excursions. Some of the top options include:

  • Shreveport: A larger city located about an hour’s drive away, known for its casinos and nightlife.
  • Piney Woods Wine Trail: A trail that takes visitors on a tour of local wineries and vineyards.
  • Natchitoches: A historic town located about an hour’s drive away, known for its Creole cuisine and charming architecture.

Conclusion

Logansport is a charming town with a rich history and culture, beautiful natural scenery, and a variety of activities and attractions for visitors. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, exploring history and culture, or just relaxing in a small town, Logansport should be on your travel list.
