Introduction to Northern Louisiana

Northern Louisiana boasts a rich history and culture that attracts tourists from around the world. From its beautiful natural scenery to its delicious regional cuisine, there’s something for everyone here. Located in the southern United States, this region offers a unique blend of French, Spanish, and African-American influences.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Louisiana

Here’s a list of some must-visit places in Northern Louisiana:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Louisiana State Exhibit Museum: Showcases the history of Louisiana with a diorama collection of key events and places.
  • Shreveport Water Works Museum: Acting as both a museum and botanic garden the water works museum features a variety of flora and fauna to enjoy in the picturesque gardens.
  • Toledo Bend Reservoir: A sprawling man-made lake on the Sabine River, that provides water, hydro-electric power, and outdoor recreational opportunities for the region.
  • Chennault Aviation and Military Museum: Discovers the history of military aviation in the USA and features exciting exhibits, plus a gift shop selling aviation souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Northern Louisiana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kisatchie National Forest: Surrounded by the skyscraping pines of the Kisatchie National Forest, every nature lover will delight in this sprawling forest with its hiking and camping opportunities.
  • Black Bayou Lake National Wildlife Refuge: Home to a variety of wildlife, this nature reserve provides kayaking and bird watching for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Sci-Port Discovery Center: Engaging for all ages, discover interactive exhibits and a fun space center, an arcade and even an on-site cafe for a day trip full of memorable fun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Louisiana

Northern Louisiana provides various scenic biking and hiking trails. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Bossier City Bike Trail: A 1.9-mile trail along the Red River that provides an excellent view of the city skyline.
  • Chemin-A-Haut State Park Trail: A 2.2-mile trail offering stunning views of rugged outcrops, a natural waterfall, and fabulous fishing opportunities.
  • Shreveport Riverfront Trail: A 5 mile trail from Stoner Avenue to the Shreveport-Barksdale Bridge that passes parks, art displays, and running water.

Best Things to Do in Northern Louisiana: Activities and Tours

  • Louisiana Boardwalk: An outlet mall situated along the Red River. A great place for shopping and dining, with a Ferris wheel for the kids to enjoy and events held throughout the year.
  • Red River National Wildlife Refuge: An outdoor lover’s paradise, explore the trails and enjoy activities like birdwatching, hunting, and fishing.
  • Gators and Friends Adventure Park and Exotic Zoo: Experience over 150 alligators and a variety of exotic animals at the Gators and Friends Adventure Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Louisiana

Here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your Northern Louisiana trip:

  • Day 1 and 2: Start your trip with a visit to the Louisiana State Exhibit Museum and Shreveport Water Works Museum to learn about the state’s history.
  • Day 3 and 4: Take a trip to the Toledo Bend Reservoir for outdoor recreation.
  • Day 5: Visit the Sci-Port Discovery Center to experience hands-on interactive exhibits.
  • Day 6: Explore the vast Kisatchie National Forest by hiking or camping.
  • Day 7: Shop at the Louisiana Boardwalk and stop at the Red River National Wildlife Refuge to end your tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Louisiana

Northern Louisiana is known for its delicious cuisine, with various unique flavors and dishes to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Red Beans and Rice: A classic Southern dish made with beans, rice, and seasoning.
  • Crawfish Etouffee: A flavorful dish made with crawfish, vegetables, and a roux-based sauce served over rice.
  • Po’ Boy Sandwiches: A classic New Orleans sandwich made with fried seafood, lettuce, tomato, and mayo on French bread.

Local Beverages

Don’t miss out on trying the local beverages:

  • Sazerac: A classic New Orleans cocktail, made with rye whiskey, absinthe, and Peychaud’s Bitters.
  • Daiquiris: The famous frozen daiquiris from Louisiana come in a variety of flavors and are sold in drive-thru establishments across the state.

Where to Stay in Northern Louisiana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Northern Louisiana to suit all budgets. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Margaritaville Resort Casino: A luxury casino resort with an outdoor pool, spa, and various shops and restaurants.
  • Greenwood Bed and Breakfast: A beautiful historic B&B featuring southern charm mixed with modern amenities.
  • Holiday Inn Downtown Shreveport: A comfortable and affordable hotel in the heart of downtown.

What to Buy in Northern Louisiana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Louisiana Hot Sauce: A must-have for any traveler to Louisiana, try various brands and flavors available throughout the region.
  • Pralines: These sweet treats are made with pecans and caramel and are available in various shops and bakeries across the state.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Louisiana

Northern Louisiana culture and customs are influenced by French, Spanish, and African-American heritages. The area is steeped in history and traditions, and residents take pride in their unique heritage. Respect for southern hospitality is crucial, and visitors should also show manners when addressing the locals.

Best Time to Visit Northern Louisiana

The best time to visit Northern Louisiana is during the fall (September-November) when the weather is mild, and the foliage is beautiful. Spring (March-May) is also an excellent time to visit. Summer in the region can be hot and humid, and winter can get very chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Louisiana

Do:

  • Say “please” and “thank you” to locals.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities.
  • Enjoy the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Make fun of the local customs or accents.
  • Litter or disrespect the natural environment.
  • Overlook the state’s history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Louisiana

Northern Louisiana is a relatively safe region, but as with any tourist destination, visitors should stay alert and aware of their surroundings. Local law enforcement, tourist police, and hotel staff are available to assist with any safety concerns.

Health in Northern Louisiana

Visitors should take proper precautions when venturing into outdoor activities and environments. It is best to pack sunscreen, bug spray, hats, and proper footwear when hiking or camping.

How to Get Around in Northern Louisiana

The best way to get around Northern Louisiana is by car. There are various car rental companies available at the airports and in major cities. Alternatively, visitors can also use ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft or take advantage of local taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Northern Louisiana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Orleans, Louisiana: Head southeast to experience the iconic culture, music, and cuisine of New Orleans.
  • Dallas, Texas: Head west to experience the big city lights and shopping of Dallas.
  • Jackson, Mississippi: Get a taste of traditional southern charm with various historic buildings and culinary delights.

Conclusion

From natural scenery to historic attractions and delicious cuisine, Northern Louisiana has something for everyone. Use this travel guide to help plan an unforgettable trip and explore all the wonders that the region has to offer.
