Introduction to Jefferson

Nestled in Jefferson Parish, Greater New Orleans, South Louisiana, Jefferson is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a mix of lively city life and serene natural beauty. This charming location is home to a myriad of tourist attractions, from historical landmarks and museums to outdoor adventures and scenic views. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything Jefferson has to offer and share all the insider tips and local knowledge you need for an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Jefferson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll love the cultural landmarks of Jefferson. From museums and historical sites to art galleries and architectural wonders, there’s something for everyone.

  • Jefferson Historical Museum: This award-winning museum showcases the rich history of Jefferson and its role in shaping South Louisiana culture. It features authentic artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays, making it a great place for all ages.
  • The Mardi Gras Museum of Imperial Calcasieu: This museum displays the world-renowned costumes and paraphernalia of Mardi Gras. It has an impressive collection of costumes and artifacts from past years’ celebrations, all helping to preserve the tradition of Mardi Gras for future generations.
  • Mississippi River Heritage Park: The park overlooks the mighty Mississippi River and features several historic buildings, including the Old U.S. Mint and the adjacent Canal Street Ferry Terminal Buildings. Visitors can tour the buildings and learn about their history and importance to the area.
  • Magazine Street: This busy street offers several antique shops, art galleries, clothing boutiques, and specialty stores. Visitors can spend hours browsing through the various shops and restaurants that this unique street has to offer.

Natural Attractions of Jefferson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers won’t be disappointed in Jefferson. With its lush vegetation, waterways, and wildlife, it offers a range of outdoor adventures and scenic views that make it a great destination for outdoor activities. Some of the best natural attractions in the area include:

  • City Park: This 1,300 acre park is one of the largest urban parks in the country and is home to several attractions, including a botanical garden, amusement parks, golf courses, and a sculpture garden. Visitors can wander through the park and take in the beauty of the gardens and natural scenery.
  • Jean Lafitte National Historical Park and Preserve: This park and preserve is home to a diverse variety of plants and animals that live in the swamp. Visitors can take walking tours, boat tours and kayak/canoe trips as well as wildlife observation and fishing.
  • Bayou Segnette State Park: Located in Westwego, Louisiana, Bayou Segnette State Park is a beautiful park that features two nature trails, a fishing pier, picnic areas, and playgrounds for children. Visitors can hike, bike, or partake in water activities, including swimming and fishing during their stay in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jefferson

One of the best ways to explore Jefferson’s beautiful natural scenery is through hiking and biking. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • Lafitte Greenway: This 2.6-mile bike and pedestrian path connects the neighborhoods of the Faubourg Tremé, Mid-City, and Lakeview. It features beautiful scenery, including oak trees and blooming flowers, as well as local public art.
  • Tammany Trace: This 31-mile path runs through five towns in St. Tammany Parish, from Covington to Slidell. The path is perfect for hiking, biking, skating, and horseback riding.
  • Bonnabel Boat Launch Trail: This scenic 1.5-mile trail meanders through the woods and along the edge of the lake. It features several bird-watching and fishing areas, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Jefferson: Activities and Tours

From sightseeing tours to adrenaline-packed adventures, there’s no shortage of fun activities to partake in while in Jefferson. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • New Orleans Ghost Tour: This tour takes you through the spookiest and most haunted sites of New Orleans. Guides regale you with tales of horror, hope, and the inexplicable spirits that roam the streets of the city.
  • Swamp Tours: Several tour companies offer trips through the swamps and bayous surrounding the city, including alligator spotting and learning about the diverse ecosystems of Louisiana’s marshlands.
  • Natchez Steamboat Jazz Dinner Cruise: Savor delicious cuisine, listen to the soothing tunes of a New Orleans jazz band, and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Mississippi river when you take a Natchez Steamboat Jazz Dinner Cruise. It’s a unique experience that you wouldn’t want to miss.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jefferson

To get the best of Jefferson, we suggest the following week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival, check into hotel, stroll around Magazine Street to shop and dine.
  • Day 2: Visit the Jefferson Historical Museum early in the morning, followed by lunch in a nearby café and visit Jean Lafitte National Historical Park.
  • Day 3: Rent a kayak and explore Bayou St. John for a few hours, then enjoy dinner at a local seafood restaurant.
  • Day 4: Take a New Orleans ghost tour in the evening.
  • Day 5: Spend the day in City Park, exploring the gardens and museums.
  • Day 6: Embark on a swamp tour in the morning. In the evening, take a Jazz Dinner Cruise.
  • Day 7: Depart from Jefferson.

Food and Drink Scene of Jefferson

No matter what your taste buds are, Jefferson offers a wide range of delicious foods and drinks that are sure to satisfy your appetite. From classic Louisiana dishes like gumbo and jambalaya to trendy cocktail bars, here are some of the best culinary destinations in the area.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gumbo: A local favorite, this savory soup is made with seafood or sausage and served over rice.
  • Po-Boys: This classic sandwich features Louisiana-style bread with either fried oysters, shrimp, or catfish, lettuce, and mayo.
  • Beignets: These fluffy fried doughnuts are served with powdered sugar on top, and will be a sure hit with visitors.

Local Beverages

  • Hurricane: A classic cocktail made with rum, juice, and various other mixers.
  • Abita Beer: A Louisiana-based brewery that offers several different types of beer.

Where to Stay in Jefferson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jefferson offers a variety of accommodations for visitors that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Jefferson:

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel New Orleans Airport: This 3-star hotel offers complimentary airport shuttle service and is located near top attractions such as City Park and the New Orleans Museum of Art.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham: Located on Baronne Street, this 3-star hotel offers easy access to the famous French Quarter and numerous restaurants.
  • Auberge NOLA Hostel: Located in the Lower Garden District, this hostel has a great community atmosphere with plenty of amenities for guests.

What to Buy in Jefferson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that visitors should consider purchasing while in Jefferson:

  • Mardi Gras beads: These colorful beads are perfect souvenirs from your visit to Jefferson.
  • Coffee with Chicory: A traditional New Orleans blend that you can take back home and enjoy for months to come.

Local Culture and Customs of Jefferson

Like in any other place, it’s important for visitors to understand and respect the local culture and customs of Jefferson. The best way to do so is by learning about their traditions and history. Here are some of the noteworthy customs and cultural practices of Jefferson:

  • Mardi Gras: Jefferson is home to one of the most popular Mardi Gras celebrations in the country. The tradition involves parades, floats, costumes, and celebrations throughout the city.
  • Jazz Music: Jefferson is also known worldwide for its Jazz music. Visitors can enjoy live music at various venues throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit Jefferson

The best time to visit Jefferson is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the city is less crowded. The months of March to May and September to November are the ideal time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jefferson

Do:

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave the city without trying local jazz music.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jefferson

For the most part, Jefferson is considered a safe city for tourists. However, visitors should always be cautious of pickpockets in busy areas and should avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Jefferson

Visitors should be aware of the high humidity and heat during the summer months, and should stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when outdoors. It’s also a good idea to bring insect repellent, as mosquitoes are prevalent in the area.
