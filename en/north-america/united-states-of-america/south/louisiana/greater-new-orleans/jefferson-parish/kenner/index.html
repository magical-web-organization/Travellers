Kenner, a small city in Jefferson Parish, Greater New Orleans, is a hidden gem of Louisiana. This travel guide will give you a complete overview of what to expect when visiting Kenner. From cultural attractions to natural wonders, outdoor activities to food and drink, this guide has everything to make your trip unforgettable.

Introduction to Kenner

Kenner, Louisiana, also known as the city of progress, is a small and thriving town in Greater New Orleans. Its location on the Mississippi River has made it a hub for commerce and transportation, which can be seen in its rich history. Kenner is an excellent destination if you want to escape the hustle and bustle of the big city and enjoy a more laid-back environment.

List of Places to Visit in Kenner

There are plenty of places to visit in Kenner, and here is a comprehensive list to make the most of your trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kenner City Hall: A beautiful building that showcases Kenner’s culture and history.
  • Rivertown museums and cultural centers: A collection of museums and cultural centers that shed light on Louisiana’s history and culture.
  • LaSalle’s Landing: A memorial park dedicated to French explorer Robert de LaSalle, who landed near Kenner in 1682.
  • St. Augustine Catholic Church and Cemetery: A historic Catholic Church and cemetery that is one of the oldest in Louisiana.
  • The Esplanade Mall: A shopping mall with many shops, restaurants, and entertainment options.

Natural Attractions of Kenner: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Mississippi River: A strong force that has shaped Kenner’s history and culture.
  • Kenner Boat Launch: A great place to launch your boat or watch the boats pass by.
  • Laketown Park: A waterfront park with a fishing pier, a playground, and a walking trail.
  • Butterfly Garden: A beautiful garden filled with vibrant butterfly species.
  • Kenner Planetarium and Science Museum: A great place to learn about the universe and SPACE.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kenner

Kenner offers great opportunities to explore nature through its many hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Lafreniere Park: A large park with many trails that offers a peaceful and green environment.
  • Bonnett Carre Spillway: A popular biking route with scenic views of the spillway.
  • The Levee Trail: A 2.5-mile trail that follows the river levee with breathtaking views of the Mississippi River.

Best Things to Do in Kenner: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities and tours to enjoy in Kenner. Here are some of the best:

  • The Swamp Tour: A must-do tour that takes you through the wild swamps of Louisiana.
  • The Airboat Tour: Another exciting way to explore the swamps with a high-speed boat ride.
  • Jazz Cruises on the Mississippi River: A romantic way to enjoy live music, dinner, and breathtaking waterfront views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kenner

If you plan to stay in Kenner for a week, here’s an ideal itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Rivertown to discover Kenner’s history and culture.
  • Day 2: Explore nature in Lafreniere Park.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a swamp tour in the morning and have a jazz cruise in the evening.
  • Day 4: Take a bike ride on Bonnett Carre Spillway.
  • Day 5: Visit the Butterfly Garden and the Kenner Science Museum.
  • Day 6: Go shopping at the Esplanade Mall and visit St. Augustine Catholic Church and Cemetery.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind in Laketown Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Kenner

Kenner is known for its delicious Southern cuisine, and here are some of the best places to eat and drink in Kenner.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cajun and Creole Recipes: Gumbo, Jambalaya, Crawfish Etouffee, Red Beans and Rice, and Po-boys.
  • Louisiana Seafood: Crabs, Shrimps, Oysters, and Catfish.

Local Beverages

  • Hurricanes and Daiquiris: A sweet and refreshing cocktail that is perfect for a hot Southern day.
  • Abita Beer: A local brewery that makes some of Louisiana’s best beers.

Where to Stay in Kenner: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Kenner, and here are some of the best:

  • The Hilton New Orleans Airport: A luxurious hotel with top-notch amenities and excellent service.
  • Crowne Plaza New Orleans Airport Hotel: A modern hotel that is budget-friendly and conveniently located near the airport.
  • La Quinta Inn and Suites by Wyndham New Orleans Airport: A hotel that offers great value for money and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Kenner: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • New Orleans Spices and Sauces: A great way to bring the flavor of New Orleans home.
  • Mardi Gras Beads: A fun and colorful souvenir to remind you of your Louisiana trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Kenner

Kenner has a rich and vibrant culture that is reflected in its music, art, and cuisine. It is a community that values family, faith, and good times. The locals are friendly and hospitable, making it an excellent destination for travelers.

Best Time to Visit Kenner

The best time to visit Kenner is between January and May and between September and December when the weather is mild and less humid. However, if you want to experience the excitement of Mardi Gras, plan your trip in February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kenner

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Kenner is known for its delicious Southern food, so make sure to try as much of it as you can.
  • Be Friendly: Locals are friendly and hospitable, so be sure to return the gesture.
  • Respect the Local Culture: Kenner has a unique and vibrant culture, so be respectful of it.

Don’t:

  • Take Pictures of People Without Permission: It is considered rude to take pictures of people without their consent.
  • Be Disrespectful: It is essential to be respectful of the locals and their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kenner

Kenner is a safe city to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions.

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: As with any city, it’s best to avoid walking alone at night.
  • Carry Your Valuables Cautiously: Avoid flashing your valuables in public places.

Health in Kenner

Louisiana is known for its rich and flavorful cuisine, and it’s easy to indulge in delicious food. However, it is essential to practice moderation and stay hydrated to avoid health issues.

Getting to Kenner: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kenner is easily accessible via different modes of transportation.

Airports Near Kenner

  • Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport: Located five miles west of Kenner, this airport is the primary gateway to the city.

Bus Stations Near Kenner

  • Jefferson Transit E2 Bus Line: A public transit system that runs through most of the city.

Other Options

Kenner is accessible via car and taxi.

How to Get Around in Kenner

Public transportation is the best way to get around Kenner. There are buses and trolleys that run throughout the city, and taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Kenner: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from Kenner. Here are some of the best:

  • New Orleans: Explore the vibrant city of New Orleans.
  • Baton Rouge: Take a day trip to Baton Rouge, the capital of Louisiana.
  • Lafayette: A charming city that offers a unique combination of Cajun and Creole culture.

Conclusion

Kenner, Louisiana, is a hidden gem that offers a perfect combination of history, culture, food, and natural beauty. We hope this travel guide helps you in planning your Kenner trip and makes it unforgettable.
