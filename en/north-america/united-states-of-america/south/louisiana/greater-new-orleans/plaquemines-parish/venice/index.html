Introduction to Venice

Located in Plaquemines Parish in Greater New Orleans, Venice is a charming small town filled with history and attractions. Many people visit this area for its natural beauty and outdoor activities like fishing and hiking. It’s a perfect getaway for anyone who wants to escape city life and enjoy some peace and quiet in nature. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, stay, eat and more in Venice.

List of Places to Visit in Venice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fort Jackson

Fort Jackson, one of the most significant forts in the state’s history, was built in the early 19th century. It played an essential part in defending Louisiana from foreign invasions in the 19th and 20th centuries. Visitors can learn about the fort’s history through a guided tour.

Venice Historic District

The Venice Historic District is a beautiful area with many historic buildings, homes, and businesses that have been around since the early 1900s. Visitors can take a self-guided tour to explore the district’s history, visit the high school museum and a WWII memorial museum.

Natural Attractions of Venice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Venice Marina

The Venice Marina is an excellent destination for those who love fishing. Located along the Mississippi River delta, this marina is the start of some world-class fishing trips. Visitors can rent fishing boats, book fishing tours, or just enjoy the serene natural beauty of the area.

Delta National Wildlife Refuge

The Delta National Wildlife Refuge is a perfect destination for wildlife lovers. Visitors can see a wide range of species, including alligators, birds, and fish. The Refuge provides information on the Delta’s ecosystem and the animals that inhabit the area and offer a variety of outdoor activities like hiking, fishing and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Venice

Bohemia Spillway Trail

The Bohemia Spillway Trail is a beautiful hiking trail ideal for anyone looking for an adventure into the wilderness. The 5.4-mile trail goes through freshwater marshes, accented by cattails and ancient cypress trees covered in Spanish moss.

Best Things to Do in Venice: Activities and Tours

Fishing Tour

Venice is an excellent location for fishing, and visitors can join a guided fishing tour to make the most of their experience. There are many charters available in the area, which cater to both amateurs and experienced anglers.

Boat Tour

For those looking for a more relaxed and leisurely activity, Venice also offers boat tours. Visitors can explore the Mississippi River delta and the surrounding marshes while observing local wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Venice

Must-Try Dishes

Grits and Grillades

Grits and Grillades, a traditional Creole dish, is a must-try in Venice. The dish consists of slow-cooked beef tenderloin served over creamy grits.

Local Beverages

Bloody Mary

The Bloody Mary is a signature drink of Venice, Louisiana. It’s prepared with local hot sauce and spices, making it a must-try drink for those who love spicy cocktails.

Where to Stay in Venice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Venice RV Park

The Venice RV Park is located near the Venice Marina and provides a peaceful atmosphere for travelers. Along with daily and weekly rates, visitors can take advantage of facilities like laundry and shower.

River Palm Guest House

The River Palm Guest House is a cozy accommodation option near the Delta National Wildlife Refuge. The guesthouse features excellent views and personalized attention from the hosts.

What to Buy in Venice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home souvenirs of Venice’s unique culture and history by purchasing items like handmade crafts, specialty hot sauce, and Creole groceries from local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Venice

Venice has a rich history of Creole and Cajun cultures that influence the local customs. Visitors can immerse themselves in local customs during Mardi Gras celebrations, which are significant events in the area.

Best Time to Visit Venice

The best time to visit Venice is during the spring and fall seasons, as the weather is mild and pleasant. During these seasons, visitors can also enjoy festivals like the Venice Gumbo Festival and the Mardi Gras parade.

Do’s and Don’ts of Venice

Do:

  • Do try Grits and Grillades, Bloody Mary, and other local cuisine.
  • Do experience the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in natural areas like the Delta National Wildlife Refuge.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and mosquito repellent when visiting outdoor recreational areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Venice

Venice is a safe destination for travelers, but general precautions like locking doors and car windows should be taken. Visitors are also advised to avoid walking alone in secluded areas at night.

Health in Venice

Visitors should be aware of the local wildlife, especially snakes and alligators found in the area. Sunscreen and mosquito repellent are recommended while hiking or doing outdoor activities.

Getting to Venice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Venice

The nearest airport to Venice is the Louis Armstroairportr New Orleans International Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away from Venice. From there, visitors can take a rental car to get to Venice.

Bus Stations Near Venice

The Greyhound station in New Orleans is the closest bus station to Venice. From here, visitors can take a rental car to get to Venice.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Venice by driving from nearby cities or taking a taxi.

How to Get Around in Venice

Venice has limited public transportation options, so visitors are advised to rent a car. There are car rental companies located at the Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport.

Where to Go Next from Venice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Orleans

New Orleans, known as the “Big Easy” is located about 90 miles away from Venice. Visitors can explore the city’s unique culture, live music venues and delicious food scene.

Grand Isle

Grand Isle, a barrier island located in Louisiana’s southeastern corner, is another excellent destination for beach, fishing and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Venice, Louisiana is an excellent location to escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy a peaceful and leisurely time in nature. With its charming history, beautiful scenery, and friendly people - Venice is a perfect destination for travelers looking to experience Louisiana’s unique culture and traditions.
