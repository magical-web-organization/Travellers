Introduction to Plaquemines Parish

Plaquemines Parish is a fascinating destination that offers a unique blend of Louisiana’s rich history, lush natural scenery, and vibrant culture. Located in Greater New Orleans, in the southern part of Louisiana, Plaquemines Parish is a true gem of the region, full of charm and surprises.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore everything that Plaquemines Parish has to offer, from its culturally important museums and historical places to its breathtaking outdoor adventures and scenic views.

So let’s pack our bags and get ready for an unforgettable journey through Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana.

List of Places to Visit in Plaquemines Parish

Plaquemines Parish has an abundance of attractions to explore, including culturally important museums, historic sites, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and more. Here are some of the top places to visit in Plaquemines Parish:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plaquemines Parish Museum: This fascinating museum showcases the history of Plaquemines Parish, with exhibits showcasing Native American artifacts, early Spanish colonial life, and the importance of the Mississippi River to the area.

  • Fort Jackson: Located on the east bank of the Mississippi River, Fort Jackson is a historical landmark that played a critical role in both the War of 1812 and the Civil War. The fort offers guided tours and hosts special events throughout the year.

  • Woodland Plantation: Built in 1834, Woodland Plantation is a beautifully restored historic home that offers a glimpse into the antebellum South. The home’s architecture and furnishings reflect the wealthy sugar cane plantation era of the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Plaquemines Parish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Venice: This small fishing town is known as the “tuna capital of the world” and offers excellent sport fishing opportunities in the Gulf of Mexico.

  • Bayou Segnette State Park: This sprawling park features beautiful wetlands, waterways, and woodlands. It’s an ideal destination for hiking, birdwatching, and kayaking. There are also plenty of picnic areas and camping facilities for overnight stays.

  • The English Turn: This scenic loop of the Mississippi River offers breathtaking views of the river and its surrounding landscape. It’s a peaceful and serene place that’s perfect for a leisurely drive or a relaxing stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plaquemines Parish

Plaquemines Parish is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering plenty of opportunities for biking, hiking, and exploring the region’s unique natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Plaquemines Parish:

  • Woodland Trail: This 5-mile trail meanders through Woodland Plantation’s magnificent 19th-century gardens, providing an immersive and relaxing walk through the area’s beautiful scenery.

  • Belle Chasse Parkway: This 23-mile road offers a picturesque cycling route along the shore of the Mississippi River, with stunning views of the river and surrounding wetlands.

  • Barataria Preserve: This beautiful nature preserve features a network of trails that allows visitors to explore the bayous and wetlands of Plaquemines Parish. The trails range from easy to moderate difficulty and offer excellent opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

Best Things to Do in Plaquemines Parish: Activities and Tours

Plaquemines Parish offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Plaquemines Parish:

  • Delta Charter Fishing: Plaquemines Parish is home to some of the best sport fishing in the world, and Delta Charter Fishing is the perfect way to experience it. With experienced guides and top-notch equipment, you can catch a variety of fish, including redfish, speckled trout, and flounder.

  • Creole Queen Historic Cruise: This 2-hour cruise takes you along the Mississippi River, providing an insightful and unforgettable tour of New Orleans’ history and culture. You’ll see historic sites like the French Quarter, Jackson Square, and the Garden District and learn about the city’s fascinating past.

  • Louisiana Crawfish Company Tour: Crawfish is a traditional delicacy in Louisiana, and this tour takes you behind the scenes of Louisiana Crawfish Company’s crawfish farming operation. You’ll learn about the history and process of crawfish farming and even get to sample some of the delicious boiled crawfish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plaquemines Parish

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Plaquemines Parish, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at one of the area’s fantastic hotels or guesthouses.

  • Day 2: Explore the culturally important attractions, starting with a visit to the Plaquemines Parish Museum. Next, head to Fort Jackson for a guided tour of this historic landmark. Finish off the day with a visit to Woodland Plantation.

  • Day 3: Head to Venice for a day of sport fishing in the Gulf of Mexico.

  • Day 4: Spend the day exploring the beautiful natural attractions of Plaquemines Parish, starting with a visit to Bayou Segnette State Park. Next, take a drive along the scenic English Turn.

  • Day 5: Rent a bike and take the Belle Chasse Parkway cycling route, stopping at local food stands for some authentic Louisiana cuisine.

  • Day 6: Take a Louisiana Crawfish Company tour to learn about the area’s crawfish farming traditions.

  • Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Plaquemines Parish

No trip to Louisiana is complete without indulging in the area’s famous local cuisine and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of Plaquemines Parish:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jambalaya: This flavorful dish consists of rice, meat, and vegetables cooked together in a delicious blend of spices.

  • Crawfish Etouffee: This Cajun classic is a staple of Louisiana’s Creole cuisine. It features crawfish smothered in a rich, seasoned roux and served over rice.

  • Gumbo: Gumbo is a hearty stew that typically includes meat or seafood, okra, and the “holy trinity” of vegetables (onions, celery, and bell peppers). It’s often served over rice.

Local Beverages

  • Hurricane Cocktail: This sweet and potent cocktail is made from rum, fruit juice, and syrup. It’s a New Orleans classic and a must-try while visiting Plaquemines Parish.

  • Abita Beer: This Louisiana beer is brewed with artesian spring water and local ingredients, giving it a distinct and delicious flavor.

  • Sazerac: This classic cocktail was invented in New Orleans and features rye whiskey, absinthe, bitters, and sugar for a complex and flavorful drink.

What to Buy in Plaquemines Parish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Plaquemines Parish is home to a variety of unique and authentic Louisiana products that make excellent souvenirs. Here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Pralines: These sweet treats are a Louisiana classic, made from sugar, cream, and pecans.

  • Hot Sauce: Louisiana is known for its spicy cuisine, and hot sauce is a must-have for any spice lover. Look for locally made hot sauces for a unique taste.

  • Artisan Crafts: There are many talented artisans in Plaquemines Parish who create beautiful and unique crafts. Look for handcrafted pottery, jewelry, and other items to bring home as a souvenir of your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Plaquemines Parish

Plaquemines Parish has a rich and diverse culture that reflects its unique history and heritage. From its Creole and Cajun roots to its proximity to the Mississippi River, Plaquemines Parish’s culture is a fascinating blend of influences.

Some of the customs and traditions that you may encounter while visiting Plaquemines Parish include:

  • Mardi Gras: This famous New Orleans celebration is a must-see for anyone visiting Plaquemines Parish in the winter. The festivities include parades, music, and lots of colorful costumes and decorations.

  • Crawfish Boils: A crawfish boil is a traditional Louisiana event that typically involves boiling crawfish along with other seasonings and vegetables. It’s a fun and social activity that’s enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

  • Voodoo: Voodoo is a unique and fascinating spiritual tradition that has its roots in West Africa. It’s still practiced by some in Louisiana, and you may encounter voodoo symbols and practices during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Plaquemines Parish

The best time to visit Plaquemines Parish is during the fall and winter months (October to February) when the weather is milder and the crowds are smaller. This is also the peak season for Louisiana’s famous Mardi Gras celebrations, which are a must-see if you’re in the area during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plaquemines Parish

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Plaquemines Parish:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Louisiana’s cuisine is famous for its unique and flavorful dishes. Make sure to try some of the local specialties while you’re in Plaquemines Parish.

  • Respect the local culture: Plaquemines Parish has a rich and diverse culture that’s worth exploring. Make sure to respect the customs and traditions of the local community.

  • Be prepared for the weather: Louisiana can be hot and humid, especially during the summer months. Make sure to bring lightweight, breathable clothing and stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Harm or disturb wildlife: Plaquemines Parish is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including alligators, egrets, and other birds. Make sure to keep a safe distance and avoid disturbing or harming the animals.

  • Ignore local warnings and advice: Plaquemines Parish is prone to flooding and other natural hazards. Make sure to pay attention to local warnings and advice and take necessary precautions.

  • Litter or pollute: Louisiana’s natural beauty is one of its biggest attractions. Make sure to take care of the environment and avoid littering or polluting.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are important considerations when traveling, and Plaquemines Parish is no exception. Here are some tips to help you stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Plaquemines Parish

  • Watch out for wildlife: Keep a safe distance from alligators, snakes, and other wildlife.

  • Be aware of flooding risks: Plaquemines Parish is prone to flooding, especially during hurricane season. Stay informed and take necessary precautions.

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night: Avoid walking alone at night and stay in well-lit areas.

Health in Plaquemines Parish

  • Stay hydrated: Louisiana can be hot and humid, so make sure to drink plenty of water and other fluids to avoid dehydration.

  • Wear sunscreen: Protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays by wearing sunscreen and a hat when outdoors.

  • Be aware of food allergies: Louisiana’s cuisine is full of delicious and unique flavors, but make sure to be aware of your food allergies before trying new dishes.

Where to Go Next from Plaquemines Parish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
