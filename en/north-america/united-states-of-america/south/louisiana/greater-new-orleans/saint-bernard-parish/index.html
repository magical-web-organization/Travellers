Introduction to Saint Bernard Parish

Saint Bernard Parish is located in Greater New Orleans, in Louisiana, in the United States of America. It is known for its rich history and vibrant culture that attracts tourists from all around the world. This beautiful parish is home to stunning natural beauty, museums, historical sites, outdoor adventures, delicious food and drink, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Saint Bernard Parish

Here are some of the must-visit places in Saint Bernard Parish:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chalmette Battlefield: This historical site marks the location of the Battle of New Orleans during the War of 1812. Visitors can explore the battlefield, see the exhibits, and learn about this significant event in American history.

  • Docville Farm: This 18th-century farm offers a glimpse into the region’s history and agricultural practices. Visitors can take a guided tour of the farm, see the farm animals, and enjoy the scenic nature trails.

  • Los Islenos Museum Complex: This museum complex showcases the rich heritage of the Canary Islanders who settled in Saint Bernard Parish in the 18th century. Visitors can learn about the culture and traditions of these settlers through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and traditional crafts.

Natural Attractions of Saint Bernard Parish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bayou Sauvage National Wildlife Refuge: This expansive wildlife refuge is home to over 340 bird species, alligators, otters, and other wildlife species. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and birdwatching while exploring the scenic beauty of the refuge.

  • Jean Lafitte National Historical Park and Preserve: This park offers visitors an opportunity to explore the swamps and bayous of Louisiana. Visitors can take guided tours, go on canoe or kayak tours, and enjoy the stunning scenery and wildlife of the park.

  • Shell Beach: This charming fishing village offers visitors the chance to see the traditional fishing boats and taste fresh seafood. Visitors can take guided tours of the village and learn about the history of the fishing industry in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint Bernard Parish

Saint Bernard Parish boasts many scenic trails for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Bayou Bienvenue Wetland Triangle Trail: This scenic trail winds through the Bayou Bienvenue Wetland Triangle and offers visitors the opportunity to see various bird species and other wildlife species.

  • St. Bernard State Park Trail: This trail takes visitors to the scenic beauty of the St. Bernard State Park. It offers breathtaking views of the Mississippi River and is perfect for hiking and biking.

  • Violet Canal Greenway Trail: This trail connects Violet, Meraux, and Chalmette and offers visitors scenic views of the Mississippi River. This trail is ideal for biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Saint Bernard Parish: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Saint Bernard Parish:

  • Swamp and Bayou Tour: Take a guided boat tour of the swamps and bayous of Louisiana and explore the stunning natural beauty and wildlife of the region.

  • Fishing Trip: Go fishing with a local guide and learn about the fishing industry in the region. Visitors can taste fresh seafood and learn about the traditional fishing methods used in Saint Bernard Parish.

  • Taste the Local Cuisine: Taste the delicious local cuisine of Saint Bernard Parish, including Crawfish Etouffee, jambalaya, gumbo, and po’ boy sandwiches.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint Bernard Parish

Saint Bernard Parish is known for its delicious cuisine and local drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crawfish Etouffee: This classic Cajun dish features crawfish cooked in a roux-based sauce and served over rice.

  • Jambalaya: This hearty dish is made with rice, meat, and vegetables, and is flavored with spices.

  • Gumbo: This stew-like dish is made with a roux base and features seafood, sausage, and vegetables.

  • Po’ Boy Sandwich: This sandwich is made with fried seafood or roast beef and is served on a baguette.

Local Beverages

  • Hurricane Cocktail: This sweet and fruity cocktail is made with rum, passion fruit juice, and citrus.

  • Daiquiri: This classic drink is made with rum, lime juice, and sugar and is available in various flavors.

  • Abita Beer: This locally brewed beer comes in a variety of flavors and is a must-try in Saint Bernard Parish.

What to Buy in Saint Bernard Parish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Saint Bernard Parish:

  • Canary Islanders Crafts: Visitors can buy traditional crafts made by the Canary Islanders, such as lacework, wood carvings, and pottery.

  • Hot Sauce: Louisiana is famous for its hot sauce, and visitors can find a range of locally made sauces in Saint Bernard Parish.

  • Local Art: Visitors can buy unique artworks by local artists, including paintings, sculptures, and prints.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Bernard Parish

Saint Bernard Parish is known for its rich culture and traditions, influenced by the early settlers of the region. Visitors can see the local customs through the festivals and events held throughout the year, including the Los Islenos Fiesta and the Battle of New Orleans reenactment.

Best Time to Visit Saint Bernard Parish

The best time to visit Saint Bernard Parish is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be chilly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint Bernard Parish

Saint Bernard Parish is generally safe for visitors. However, visitors should take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as locking their cars and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Saint Bernard Parish

Visitors should carry insect repellent while exploring the natural areas of Saint Bernard Parish to protect themselves from mosquito bites. It is also recommended to stay hydrated and use sunscreen while outside.

Getting to Saint Bernard Parish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Saint Bernard Parish by air or road. The nearest airport is the Louis Armstrong International Airport in New Orleans. Visitors can also take a Greyhound bus or drive to the parish.

Airports Near Saint Bernard Parish

  • Louis Armstrong International Airport: This airport is located in New Orleans and is a 30-minute drive from Saint Bernard Parish.

Bus Stations Near Saint Bernard Parish

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station is located in New Orleans and offers connections to Saint Bernard Parish.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from Louis Armstrong International Airport or New Orleans.

How to Get Around in Saint Bernard Parish

Visitors can hire a car, take a taxi, or use the local bus service to get around Saint Bernard Parish.
