Introduction to Chalmette

Chalmette is a charming suburb located in Saint Bernard Parish, in Greater New Orleans, in Louisiana. With its rich culture, historical landmarks, and natural wonders, Chalmette is a hidden gem waiting to be explored by travelers. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Chalmette, from the best places to visit to the top things to do.

List of Places to Visit in Chalmette

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Chalmette has plenty of museums and historical sites to explore, including:

  • Chalmette Battlefield and National Cemetery: This national park commemorates the Battle of New Orleans and honors the men who fought and died there.
  • De La Ronde Ruins: The ruins of a former 18th-century plantation home, De La Ronde is a fascinating glimpse into Chalmette’s colonial past.
  • Los Islenos Museum Complex: This museum complex celebrates the Canary Islander heritage of Chalmette’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Chalmette: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chalmette is home to some of Louisiana’s most breathtaking wilderness areas, including:

  • Bayou Bienvenue Wetland Triangle: This 23,000-acre wetland is perfect for exploring by kayak or boat tour.
  • Chalmette National Cemetery: Not just a historical landmark, this cemetery is also a peaceful and beautiful outdoor space.
  • Jean Lafitte National Historical Park and Preserve: This park is home to six distinct wilderness areas, each with its own unique ecosystem.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chalmette

For those who love the great outdoors, Chalmette has a variety of hiking and biking trails to choose from, including:

  • The Chalmette Battlefield Walking Trail: This 2.5-mile loop trail winds through the site of the Battle of New Orleans.
  • The Woodlands Conservancy Trail: This 850-acre nature reserve has over 8 miles of hiking and biking trails.
  • The Mississippi River Trail: Part of the longest bike trail in the United States, this section of the trail offers stunning views of the Mississippi River and access to several Chalmette landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Chalmette: Activities and Tours

Chalmette has a diverse array of activities and tours to suit any traveler, including:

  • Swamp tours: Explore the Bayou Bienvenue Wetland Triangle on a guided swamp tour.
  • Distillery tours: Sample some of Louisiana’s best spirits on a tour of the Atelier Vie distillery.
  • Fishing charters: Charter a boat and catch some of the Gulf of Mexico’s best seafood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chalmette

Day 1: Visit the Chalmette Battlefield and National Cemetery, followed by a hike on the battlefield walking trail.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the natural wonders of the Bayou Bienvenue Wetland Triangle.

Day 3: Take a guided swamp tour and visit the Los Islenos Museum Complex.

Day 4: Visit the De La Ronde Ruins and explore the Woodlands Conservancy Trail.

Day 5: Take a distillery tour at Atelier Vie and go on a fishing charter in the Gulf of Mexico.

Day 6: Visit Jean Lafitte National Historical Park and Preserve, and finish the day with a sunset stroll along the Mississippi River Trail.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of Chalmette’s delicious Cajun cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Chalmette

Must-Try Dishes

Chalmette is known for its delicious Cajun cuisine, with some dishes that must be tried including:

  • Jambalaya: A spicy rice dish with meat and vegetables.
  • Gumbo: A soup-like dish made with seafood, sausage, or chicken and thickened with okra or filé powder.
  • Po’boys: Louisiana’s answer to the sub sandwich, po’boys are served on French bread with a variety of fillings, including roast beef, fried seafood, and fried chicken.

Local Beverages

Chalmette is also known for its local beverages, including:

  • Abita Beer: This Louisiana staple is brewed with pure artesian spring water.
  • Hurricane Cocktails: A fruity, rum-based cocktail that’s perfect for a night out on the town.
  • Chicory Coffee: This New Orleans classic is made with a blend of coffee and roasted chicory root.

Where to Stay in Chalmette: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chalmette has a variety of options for accommodations, including:

  • Best Western Plus Chalmette Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and a free breakfast each morning.
  • Paris Oaks Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a delicious homemade breakfast.
  • Magnolia Mansion: This beautiful mansion offers luxurious suites and easy access to all the best that Chalmette has to offer.

What to Buy in Chalmette: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to pick up some of these local favorites before you leave:

  • Cajun spices and seasonings: Bring home a taste of Louisiana with some of Chalmette’s famous spice blends.
  • Hand-crafted pottery: Many local artists create beautiful hand-crafted pottery that makes for a unique and unforgettable souvenir.
  • Mardi Gras beads and decorations: Chalmette is just a short drive from New Orleans, so make sure to bring home some Mardi Gras memorabilia to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Chalmette

Known for its friendly locals and vibrant culture, Chalmette is a melting pot of cultures that offers a unique perspective on Louisiana life. Be sure to learn about the local customs and traditions before your trip to fully immerse yourself in the Chalmette experience.

Best Time to Visit Chalmette

The best time to visit Chalmette is from October to April when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. But, if you want to experience the vibrant culture of Chalmette, then head there during Mardi Gras season in February to experience the biggest and most exciting festival in Louisiana.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chalmette

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including jambalaya, gumbo, and po’boys.
  • Do visit the Chalmette Battlefield and National Cemetery to learn about the history of the town.
  • Do go on a swamp tour to explore Louisiana’s unique wilderness.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any of the natural wonders of Chalmette.
  • Don’t skip out on trying a famous Hurricane cocktail.
  • Don’t forget to bring cash for tours and small businesses that may not accept credit or debit cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chalmette

Chalmette is a relatively safe town, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when traveling. Stay in well-lit areas at night, and avoid walking alone if possible.

Health in Chalmette

Louisiana has a very humid climate, so be sure to drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn. Always check for ticks and other insects if you’re exploring the outdoors, and make sure to bring any necessary medications with you on your trip.

Getting to Chalmette: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chalmette

The closest airport to Chalmette is Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport, which is located about 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Chalmette

Greyhound has a bus stop in New Orleans, which is about 10 miles away from Chalmette.

Other Options

The easiest way to get to Chalmette is by car, and you can rent a car at the airport or in downtown New Orleans.

How to Get Around in Chalmette

The best way to get around Chalmette is by car, but there are also several taxi and rideshare services available.

Where to Go Next from Chalmette: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chalmette is located just a short drive from New Orleans, so make sure to check out the famous French Quarter and Bourbon Street during your stay. You can also head to nearby destinations like Gulfport and Biloxi, Mississippi, or take a trip to Baton Rouge to explore more of Louisiana’s rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Chalmette is a one-of-a-kind destination that offers something for every traveler, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts and foodies. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to this charming Louisiana town.
