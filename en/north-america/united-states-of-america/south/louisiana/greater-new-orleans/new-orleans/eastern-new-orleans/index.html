Introduction to Eastern New Orleans

Eastern New Orleans is a vibrant neighborhood located in the southeastern corner of the city. Home to dozens of historical and cultural attractions, as well as some of the best food and drink in the city, Eastern New Orleans is a must-visit destination for any traveler. This comprehensive guide will outline everything you need to know to plan your trip, from where to stay to what to do and see.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern New Orleans

Eastern New Orleans is a diverse neighborhood with a wide range of attractions to suit any interest. Here are some of the must-see spots:

  • Longue Vue House and Gardens

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern New Orleans

For outdoor enthusiasts, Eastern New Orleans has plenty of options for hiking and biking. One popular route is the Tammany Trace Trail, a 31-mile rail-to-trail path that runs through Covington, Abita Springs, Mandeville, and Lacombe.

Best Things to Do in Eastern New Orleans: Activities and Tours

  • Swamp tours
  • Riverboat cruises
  • Live music shows
  • Cooking classes
  • Walking tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern New Orleans

Day 1: Visit the Chalmette Battlefield and take a riverboat cruise on the Mississippi River. Day 2: Explore the New Orleans Jazz Museum and catch a live music show in the French Quarter. Day 3: Take a swamp tour and learn about the wildlife and ecology of the area. Day 4: Visit Mardi Gras World and learn about the history and tradition of the famous New Orleans celebration. Day 5: Spend the day hiking or biking the Tammany Trace Trail. Day 6: Visit Longue Vue House and Gardens and enjoy the beautiful grounds. Day 7: Relax on Lake Pontchartrain and enjoy the beaches and water activities.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern New Orleans

Eastern New Orleans has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of African American, Creole, and Cajun traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including the use of “y’all” as a common form of address and the celebration of Mardi Gras as a major cultural event.

Best Time to Visit Eastern New Orleans

The best time to visit Eastern New Orleans is from February to May, when the weather is mild and the city is alive with Mardi Gras celebrations. However, visitors can enjoy Eastern New Orleans year-round, with plenty of indoor and outdoor activities available in any season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern New Orleans

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take part in Mardi Gras celebrations
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrespect the environment
  • Speak loudly in quiet areas
  • Be disrespectful to local customs or traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern New Orleans

Eastern New Orleans is generally safe, but visitors should exercise caution in areas with high crime rates. It is also important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when outdoors, as the weather can be hot and humid.

Health in Eastern New Orleans

Visitors to Eastern New Orleans should be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as Zika virus and West Nile virus. It is recommended to wear mosquito repellent and protective clothing when outdoors, particularly during peak mosquito season from June to October.

How to Get Around in Eastern New Orleans

Public transportation options in Eastern New Orleans are limited, so visitors may wish to rent a car or use a rideshare service to get around. Biking and walking are also good options for shorter distances.
