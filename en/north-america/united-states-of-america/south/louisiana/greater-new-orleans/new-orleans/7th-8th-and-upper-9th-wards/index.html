Introduction to 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

Located in the Greater New Orleans area of Louisiana, 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards are known for their vibrant and diverse cultural scenes. These neighborhoods played a significant role in the city’s history and are essential to understanding the rich cultural heritage of New Orleans. The area is also famous for its fantastic food scene, live music, and outdoor experiences.

List of Places to Visit in 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Backstreet Cultural Museum: A small museum featuring exhibits on the African American cultural traditions of New Orleans. It is home to a collection of Mardi Gras Indian costumes, second line parade memorabilia, and jazz funeral artifacts.

  2. Lower Ninth Ward Living Museum: A museum dedicated to preserving the history of the Lower Ninth Ward community, which suffered significant damage during Hurricane Katrina. Visitors can learn about the history, culture, and traditions of this vibrant neighborhood.

  3. St. Augustine Catholic Church: The first African American Catholic Church in the country and a significant landmark in the Civil Rights Movement. It’s a beautiful example of Gothic Revival architecture.

Natural Attractions of 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mississippi River Levee: A perfect spot for a scenic stroll or a bike ride along the river.

  2. Bayou Bienvenue: A beautiful wetland area with plenty of wildlife and hiking trails.

  3. Crescent City Park: A lovely park with a breathtaking view of the Mississippi River and the city skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

  1. Dauphine Bike Lane: A designated bike lane that runs through the neighborhoods and provides a unique way to explore the city.

  2. The Lafitte Greenway: A 2.6-mile walking and biking trail that connects Mid-City to the French Quarter, passing through the 7th and 8th Wards.

  3. Bonnabel Boat Launch: A scenic launch point for kayaks and canoes, located on Bonnabel Boulevard.

Best Things to Do in 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards: Activities and Tours

  1. Food Tours: Experience some of the best food New Orleans has to offer by taking a culinary tour. 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards are home to some of the best Creole and soul food restaurants in the city.

  2. Music Tours: Known as the birthplace of jazz, these neighborhoods are a must-visit for any music lover. Take a tour that focuses on the music history of the area and the many famous jazz musicians who played here.

  3. Art Tours: These neighborhoods have a rich history of art and culture. Take a tour of some of the local galleries and museums to see some of the best examples of the local art scene.

Food and Drink Scene of 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Gumbo: A classic New Orleans dish made with seafood, sausage, and okra, cooked in a thick broth and served with rice.

  2. Jambalaya: A dish made with rice, meat, and vegetables, cooked in a spicy sauce.

  3. Po-boys: A traditional New Orleans sandwich made with French bread, filled with fried seafood, roast beef, or chicken.

Local Beverages

  1. Sazerac: A classic cocktail made with rye whiskey, Peychaud’s bitters, and absinthe.

  2. Hurricane: A fruity cocktail made with rum and passion fruit juice, served in a signature hurricane glass.

  3. Daiquiri: A popular frozen cocktail made with rum, ice, and fruit flavors.

Where to Stay in 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Auld Sweet Olive Bed and Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast in the heart of the 7th Ward that offers a cozy and intimate experience.

  2. The Frenchmen Hotel: Located in the heart of Faubourg Marigny, one of New Orleans’ most vibrant neighborhoods, this hotel offers an excellent location and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Mardi Gras Indian Crafts: These crafts, including intricate beading and sewing, are a significant part of the African American cultural heritage.

  2. Second-Line Handkerchiefs: Colorful handkerchiefs waving in the air are a common sight in the Second Line parades.

Local Culture and Customs of 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

These neighborhoods have a rich history of African American and Creole culture, which is reflected in their food, music, and art scenes. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including etiquette when attending Second Line parades.

Best Time to Visit 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

The best time to visit these neighborhoods is from February to May, during the city’s festival season. The weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy Mardi Gras, Jazz Fest, and other cultural events.

Safety and Health

Safety in 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

Visitors should exercise caution when walking alone at night, as crime rates are higher in some areas. Avoid leaving valuables in cars and keep an eye on belongings at all times.

Health in 7th, 8th, and Upper 9th Wards

Visitors should stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun’s rays during the hot summer months. It’s also a good idea to bring along insect repellent when exploring the natural areas.
