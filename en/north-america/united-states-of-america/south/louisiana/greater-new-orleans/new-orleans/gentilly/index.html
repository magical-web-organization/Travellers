Gentilly, located in Greater New Orleans in Louisiana, is a historic neighborhood that offers a mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. From museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Gentilly. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and travel tips for your trip to Gentilly.

Introduction to Gentilly

Gentilly is a diverse neighborhood in the northern part of New Orleans, bordered by Lake Pontchartrain to the north and the French Quarter to the south. The area is known for its historical buildings, beautiful parks, and vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Gentilly

Gentilly offers a range of attractions and activities to explore. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Gentilly:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dillard University: A historical black university that offers tours of its campus and features a museum that highlights the contributions of African Americans to the arts, sciences, and humanities.

  2. St. Augustine Church: A Roman Catholic church, founded in 1841, that is the oldest African American parish in the city of New Orleans.

  3. Musicians’ Village: A community created by Habitat for Humanity and jazz musicians Harry Connick Jr. and Branford Marsalis to provide affordable housing for musicians.

Natural Attractions of Gentilly: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bayou Sauvage National Wildlife Refuge: A 24,000-acre refuge with marshes and woodlands that provide habitat for migratory birds, alligators, and other wildlife.

  2. Joe W. Brown Memorial Park: A park that offers playgrounds, tennis courts, and picnic areas, as well as a scenic lake for fishing and boating.

  3. UNO Lakefront Trail: A 3.7-mile trail that runs along Lake Pontchartrain and offers scenic views of the lake and the city skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gentilly

Gentilly offers several scenic hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Bayou Sauvage Ridge Trail: A 5-mile loop trail that offers views of the marshes and woodlands of the Bayou Sauvage National Wildlife Refuge.

  2. Dillard University Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that runs through the Dillard University campus and provides views of the university’s historic buildings and green spaces.

  3. UNO Lakefront Trail: A 3.7-mile trail along Lake Pontchartrain that is great for walking, jogging, or biking.

Best Things to Do in Gentilly: Activities and Tours

Gentilly offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Gentilly:

  1. New Orleans Jazz Tour: A tour that explores the history of jazz music in New Orleans, including a visit to the Jazz Museum at Dillard University.

  2. Kayaking on Bayou St. John: A kayaking tour that takes you through the historic Bayou St. John and provides views of city landmarks, such as City Park and the New Orleans Museum of Art.

  3. Creole Cuisine Tour: A tour that explores the history and culture of Creole cuisine in New Orleans with visits to local restaurants and markets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gentilly

If you have a week to spend in Gentilly, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit St. Augustine Church and explore the history of African American culture in New Orleans.

Day 2: Embark on the New Orleans Jazz Tour and visit the Jazz Museum at Dillard University.

Day 3: Relax at Joe W. Brown Memorial Park and enjoy the natural scenery.

Day 4: Kayak on Bayou St. John and take in the breathtaking views of the city.

Day 5: Experience the local Creole cuisine on a food tour.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, such as the French Quarter or the National World War II Museum.

Day 7: Enjoy a serene walk along UNO Lakefront Trail and end the day with a sunset picnic.

Food and Drink Scene of Gentilly

Gentilly offers a unique culinary experience with a blend of Creole and other cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Gumbo: A seafood and sausage stew that is a staple of Creole cuisine.

  2. Jambalaya: A rice dish with meat, seafood, and vegetables that is a fusion of Spanish and French cuisine.

  3. Red Beans and Rice: A classic Southern dish made with red beans, rice, and sausage.

Local Beverages

  1. Hurricane: A sweet rum cocktail that was invented in New Orleans and is popular at the city’s famous bars, such as Pat O’Brien’s.

  2. Abita Beer: A local brewery that offers a variety of beers that capture the unique flavors of Louisiana.

  3. Chicory Coffee: A coffee made with roasted chicory root, which is popular in New Orleans and has a nutty flavor.

What to Buy in Gentilly: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home from Gentilly, here are some suggestions:

  2. Spices and Seasonings: New Orleans is known for its flavorful cuisine, so bring home some of the local seasonings and spices that add those unforgettable tastes to your dishes.

  3. Mardi Gras Beads: The city is synonymous with Mardi Gras, so be sure to bring home some of the famous Mardi Gras beads as a memento.

Local Culture and Customs of Gentilly

Gentilly is a diverse and welcoming neighborhood that celebrates its heritage and traditions. Here are some aspects of local culture and customs you should know:

  1. Mardi Gras: Residents of Gentilly enthusiastically celebrate Mardi Gras with parades, music, and parties that last for days.

  2. Jazz Music: Jazz music is an important part of the city’s culture, and you can hear live music on many street corners and in many venues around the city, especially in Gentilly.

  3. Southern Hospitality: Gentilly residents are known for their hospitality and warmth, so expect to be greeted with open arms throughout your stay.

Best Time to Visit Gentilly

The best time to visit Gentilly is from March to May or from September to November when the weather is mild and there are fewer tourists. The city is crowded during Mardi Gras, which occurs in February or March each year, so consider planning your trip outside of this period if you prefer quieter and more affordable travel.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gentilly

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Gentilly:

Do:

  1. Try local cuisine: New Orleans is famous for its flavorful cuisine, so be sure to indulge in some delicious Creole dishes.

  2. Respect local customs: Gentilly is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, so be mindful of local customs and always show respect.

  3. Tip generously: Tipping is a common practice in the service industry, so be sure to leave a generous gratuity when dining out or receiving other services in Gentilly.

Don’t:

  1. Litter: Help preserve the natural beauty of Gentilly by disposing of your trash properly.

  2. Be disrespectful: Avoid making negative comments about local customs or traditions, and always show respect for others.

  3. Forget to wear sunscreen: Louisiana is known for its hot and sunny weather, so be sure to wear sunscreen to protect yourself from harmful UV rays.

Safety and Health

Gentilly is a generally safe and friendly neighborhood, but it’s always important to take precautions during your travels. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Gentilly

  1. Stay alert: Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert for any signs of suspicious activity.

  2. Use common sense: Avoid walking alone late at night, and always lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodations.

  3. Be prepared for emergencies: Keep important documents, such as your passport and travel insurance, in a safe place and always have a plan for emergencies.

Health in Gentilly

  1. Stay hydrated: Louisiana is known for its hot and humid weather, so be sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

  2. Be prepared for allergies: Louisiana is known for its allergenic flora and fauna, so bring allergy medicine if necessary.

  3. Practice good hygiene: Wash your hands frequently and avoid touching your face to prevent the spread of illnesses.
