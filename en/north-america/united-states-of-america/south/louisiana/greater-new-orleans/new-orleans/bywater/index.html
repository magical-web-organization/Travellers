Introduction to Bywater

Bywater is a neighborhood in Greater New Orleans, Louisiana, in the Southern region of the United States. It is known for its creative and bohemian vibe, with a rich history and diverse cultural scene. With its cobblestone streets, colorful architecture, and proximity to the Mississippi River, Bywater offers a unique experience for travelers looking for an alternative to the touristy areas of New Orleans.

List of Places to Visit in Bywater

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Orleans African American Museum - This museum showcases the history and culture of African Americans in New Orleans through exhibits and educational programs.
  • The Historic New Orleans Collection - A museum and research center that celebrates the history and culture of New Orleans through art, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • The Music Box Village - An interactive sculpture garden that features musical structures and installations with performances and events.

Natural Attractions of Bywater: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Crescent Park - A 1.4-mile riverfront park that offers stunning views of the Mississippi River, with walking and biking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.
  • The Schiro Garden - A community garden that offers a peaceful oasis in the middle of the city, with a variety of plants and flowers to admire.
  • The Bywater Country Club - A private club that offers a swimming pool, shuffleboard, and other outdoor activities for members and guests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bywater

  • The Lafitte Greenway - A 2.6-mile pedestrian and bike path that runs from the French Quarter to Mid-City, passing through the heart of Bywater along the way.
  • The Mississippi River Trail - A 10-mile bike path that runs along the levee on the river’s edge, providing stunning views of the water and skyline.
  • The Bayou St. John Trail - A 3-mile bike and running path that winds through several New Orleans neighborhoods, including Bywater, and offers a great way to explore the city’s green spaces.

Best Things to Do in Bywater: Activities and Tours

  • Mardi Gras World - A tour that takes you behind the scenes of Mardi Gras, showing you how the floats are made and giving you a taste of the carnival atmosphere.
  • Ghost and Vampire Tours - A spooky way to explore the history and legends of New Orleans, with tours that take you to haunted houses, cemeteries, and other creepy places.
  • Street Art Walking Tour - This tour takes you through Bywater’s streets, showing you the colorful murals, graffiti art, and street art that make the neighborhood so unique.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bywater

Food and Drink Scene of Bywater

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gumbo - A hearty stew of meat, seafood, and vegetables, and a staple of New Orleans cuisine.
  • Po’ Boys - Sandwiches filled with meat or seafood and served on French bread. Favorites include oyster, shrimp, and roast beef.
  • Beignets - Deep-fried doughnuts covered in powdered sugar, served hot and fresh from the fryer.

Local Beverages

  • Hurricane - A fruity rum cocktail that packs a punch and is a classic New Orleans drink.
  • Sazerac - A whiskey cocktail with a dash of absinthe that is said to be the oldest cocktail in America.
  • Abita Beer - A local brewery that produces a variety of beers, including the popular Abita Amber.

Where to Stay in Bywater: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Guest House - A cozy bed and breakfast located in Bywater, with quaint guest rooms and a communal courtyard.
  • The Not So Hostel - A hostel that offers private and shared rooms, with a communal kitchen and lounge area.
  • The Maison de Macarty - A historic guesthouse that offers a luxurious experience, with elegant rooms and a beautiful courtyard garden.

What to Buy in Bywater: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art - Bywater is known for its vibrant art scene, with many artists working in various media. Shop for paintings, sculptures, and other artworks directly from local artists.
  • Vintage Clothing - Take a stroll through the many vintage shops in Bywater, where you can find unique and retro clothing pieces.
  • Hot Sauce - New Orleans-style hot sauce is a must-try for spice lovers. Pick up a bottle from a local shop or market to bring home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Bywater

Bywater is known for its free-spirited and artistic community, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. The neighborhood has a diverse population that includes artists, musicians, and people from all walks of life.

Best Time to Visit Bywater

The best time to visit Bywater is between February and May, when the weather is mild and pleasant. This is also the time of year when many of the city’s festivals and events take place, including Mardi Gras and JazzFest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bywater

Do:

  • Do explore the local art and music scenes, which are some of the best in the city.
  • Do try the local cuisine, especially gumbo, po’ boys, and beignets.
  • Do be respectful of the local community and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the streets.
  • Don’t visit areas that are known to be unsafe or dangerous.
  • Don’t touch or disturb any religious or cultural artifacts you may come across.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bywater

Bywater is a relatively safe neighborhood, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. It is recommended that visitors travel in groups, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dark or unfamiliar areas. As with any urban area, it is important to be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Bywater

Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance, as medical care can be expensive in the United States. It is recommended that visitors carry any necessary medications with them, and that they take precautions to prevent mosquito bites, as mosquito-borne illnesses such as Zika and West Nile Virus have been reported in Louisiana.

Getting to Bywater: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bywater

The nearest airport to Bywater is Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport, which is located about 15 miles away. Visitors can take a taxi or shuttle from the airport to Bywater.

Bus Stations Near Bywater

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown New Orleans, about 3 miles away from Bywater. Visitors can take a taxi or ride-share service from the station to their accommodations in Bywater.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bywater by car, taking Interstate 10 to the Esplanade Avenue exit, which leads directly into the neighborhood. However, parking can be difficult in Bywater, so visitors may prefer to use public transportation or ride-share services once they arrive.

How to Get Around in Bywater

Bywater is a walkable neighborhood, with many attractions and restaurants located within a short distance of one another. Visitors can also use bike share services, like Blue Bikes, or take taxis or ride-share services like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Bywater: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • French Quarter - Just across the Mississippi River, the French Quarter is a popular destination for visitors to New Orleans, with its lively atmosphere, historic landmarks, and world-famous nightlife.
  • Garden District - A historic neighborhood located uptown, the Garden District is known for its grand mansions, beautiful gardens, and quiet residential streets.
  • Bayou Sauvage National Wildlife Refuge - A nature reserve located just outside the city, the Bayou Sauvage is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including alligators, birds, and fish.

Conclusion

Bywater is a unique and vibrant neighborhood in New Orleans, with a rich history, diverse culture, and plenty of sights and activities to explore. Whether you’re looking for an alternative to the touristy areas of the city or simply want to experience the local art, music, and cuisine, Bywater is definitely worth a visit.
