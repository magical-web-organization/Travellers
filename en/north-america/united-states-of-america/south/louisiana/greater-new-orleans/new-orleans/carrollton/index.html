Introduction to Carrollton

Carrollton is a charming neighborhood located in Greater New Orleans, Louisiana, in the southern United States. This historic district is known for its picturesque streets lined with 19th-century architecture and incredible natural scenery. With so much to see and do, Carrollton is an ideal destination for travelers of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Carrollton

If you’re planning a trip to Carrollton, there are plenty of exciting attractions and fun things to do. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carrollton Courthouse: Dating back to 1855, this beautiful courthouse is a prime example of 19th-century architecture and serves as an important landmark for the district.
  • Camellia Grill: Opened in the 1940s, this classic diner is famous for its delicious food and retro atmosphere. Don’t leave without trying one of their famous pies!
  • The Historic New Orleans Collection: Located on Royal Street in the heart of the French Quarter, this museum showcases the city’s rich cultural heritage through its extensive collection of art and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Carrollton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Audubon Park: This sprawling park boasts over 350 acres of lush greenery, scenic waterways, and beautiful walking trails.
  • Magazine Street: This bustling thoroughfare is lined with charming boutiques, trendy cafes, and some of the best shopping in the city.
  • Audubon Zoo: Home to a vast array of exotic animals from around the world, the Audubon Zoo is a must-visit for animal lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carrollton

If you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of Carrollton, there are plenty of great hiking and biking trails to choose from. Some of our favorites include:

  • The Mississippi River Trail: This scenic route follows the Mississippi River and offers breathtaking views of downtown New Orleans and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Carrollton: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or simply having fun, Carrollton has something for everyone. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • The Southern Food and Beverage Museum: This museum offers a fascinating look into the history and culture of Southern cuisine, including cooking demonstrations, exhibits, and tastings.
  • A Swamp Tour: Take a thrilling airboat ride through the bayous and swamps of Louisiana, filled with alligators, turtles, and other wild creatures.
  • The National WWII Museum: This museum tells the story of the Second World War through interactive exhibits and multimedia presentations, and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carrollton

If you’re planning to spend a week in Carrollton, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Tour the Carrollton Courthouse and surrounding historic district
Day 2 Visit Audubon Park and explore the beautiful walking trails
Day 3 Take a swamp tour and discover the wildlife of Louisiana
Day 4 Spend the day shopping on Magazine Street and enjoying the local cafes and restaurants
Day 5 Visit the National WWII Museum and learn about the history of the Second World War
Day 6 Take a day trip to nearby Baton Rouge to visit the Louisiana State Capitol
Day 7 Relax and unwind in Audubon Zoo or Camellia Grill

Food and Drink Scene of Carrollton

When it comes to food and drink, Carrollton has no shortage of delicious options to choose from. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Po’Boy Sandwiches: A classic New Orleans dish, po’boy sandwiches are filled with anything from fried shrimp to roast beef and served on a traditional French bread roll.
  • Beignets: Deep-fried doughnuts covered in powdered sugar, beignets are a staple of New Orleans cuisine.
  • Gumbo: A spicy stew made with chicken, sausage, and seafood, gumbo is a must-try for anyone visiting Carrollton.

Local Beverages

  • Hurricanes: This fruity cocktail is a New Orleans classic, made with rum, fruit juice, and plenty of ice.
  • Sazeracs: Considered the official cocktail of New Orleans, the Sazerac is made with rye whiskey, sugar, and bitters.

Where to Stay in Carrollton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carrollton has a wide range of accommodation options to choose from, whether you’re looking for luxury hotels or budget-friendly hostels. Some of our top picks include:

  • The Columns Hotel: A beautifully restored 19th-century mansion, The Columns Hotel offers elegant rooms, a stunning courtyard, and a prime location in the heart of the district.
  • The Garden District House: This beautiful guesthouse is perfect for travelers on a budget, with clean, comfortable rooms and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere.
  • The Pontchartrain Hotel: With spectacular views of downtown New Orleans, a rooftop bar, and luxurious rooms, The Pontchartrain Hotel is the perfect place to unwind after a long day of exploring.

What to Buy in Carrollton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home a unique souvenir or gift from your trip to Carrollton, there are plenty of great options to choose from. Some of the most popular include:

  • Local Artwork: Carrollton is home to a thriving arts scene, so be sure to check out some of the local galleries and studios for beautiful paintings, sculptures, and other artwork.
  • Mardi Gras Beads and Trinkets: New Orleans is famous for its legendary Mardi Gras celebrations, so bring home some colorful beads and trinkets to remember your trip.
  • Hot Sauce: With its signature blend of spices and heat, Louisiana hot sauce makes for a great souvenir or gift for anyone who loves spicy food.

Local Culture and Customs of Carrollton

Carrollton is known for its friendly, welcoming atmosphere and vibrant local culture. Some customs and traditions you may encounter during your visit include:

  • Mardi Gras: This festive celebration takes place each year in February or March and is one of the biggest parties in the world, filled with parades, costumes, and lots of good food and drink.
  • Second Lines: A beloved local tradition, second lines are parades of brass bands and revelers that take place in the streets of New Orleans on Sundays, celebrating life and community.
  • Laissez les bons temps rouler: This Cajun saying, which means “let the good times roll,” embodies the laid-back, fun-loving spirit of New Orleans and its residents.

Best Time to Visit Carrollton

The best time to visit Carrollton depends on your preferences and travel plans. If you’re looking to experience the excitement of Mardi Gras, plan your trip for February or March. If you prefer quieter, less crowded streets, consider visiting during the fall or winter months. Overall, the weather is pleasant year-round, with mild winters and hot, humid summers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carrollton

If you’re planning a trip to Carrollton, there are a few things to keep in mind to ensure you have a safe and enjoyable experience:

Do:

  • Try the Food: Carrollton is known for its delicious cuisine, so be sure to sample as much as you can while you’re here.
  • Be Respectful: The locals in Carrollton are friendly and welcoming, so be sure to return the favor by being courteous and polite.
  • Stay Safe: While Carrollton is generally a safe neighborhood, it’s always a good idea to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night.

Don’t:

  • Forget the Bug Spray: Louisiana is home to a variety of biting insects, so be sure to pack plenty of bug spray and wear long sleeves and pants when hiking or exploring outdoors.
  • Drink Too Much: New Orleans is a party city, but it’s important to drink in moderation and stay aware of your limits.
  • Skip the Sunscreen: With its hot, humid climate, Carrollton can be hard on your skin. Be sure to apply sunscreen regularly and wear a hat and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.

Safety and Health

While Carrollton is generally a safe and welcoming neighborhood, it’s always a good idea to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Carrollton

  • Stay Aware: Be aware of your surroundings at all times, particularly at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Use Common Sense: Avoid walking alone at night, and don’t carry large amounts of cash or valuables with you.
  • Watch Your Step: The streets of Carrollton can be uneven and poorly lit in some areas, so wear sturdy shoes and watch your step.

Health in Carrollton

  • Stay Hydrated: With its hot, humid climate, it’s important to drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated and avoid heat exhaustion or heatstroke.
  • Protect Yourself from Mosquitoes: Louisiana is home to many mosquitos, so be sure to wear insect repellent and cover up to avoid bites.
  • Stay Sanitized: Wash your hands frequently, practice good hygiene, and wear a mask to protect yourself against illness and disease.

Getting to Carrollton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Carrollton is easy, with plenty of transportation options available for travelers of all types. Here are some of the best ways to get to Carrollton:

Airports Near Carrollton

  • Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport: Located just 15 minutes away from Carrollton by car, this airport offers direct flights to many major cities in the United States and around the world.

Bus Stations Near Carrollton

  • Greyhound New Orleans Bus Station: Located just 10 minutes away from Carrollton by car, the Greyhound bus station offers daily service to destinations across the United States.

Other Options

  • Ridesharing Services: Popular ridesharing services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in Carrollton and offer a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Carrollton

Once you’ve arrived in Carrollton, there are plenty of great transportation options to help you get around the city. Some of our top picks include:

  • Walking: With its picturesque streets and charming architecture, walking is a great way to explore the historic district of Carrollton.
  • Biking: Carrollton offers many scenic biking trails and bike rental services for those who want to explore the city on two wheels.
  • Public Transit: The New Orleans Regional Transit Authority offers buses, streetcars, and ferries to help you get around the city quickly and easily.

Where to Go Next from Carrollton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Louisiana and the surrounding areas, there are plenty of great destinations to choose from. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • The Garden District: Just a short walk from Carrollton, the Garden District is home to some of the most beautiful homes and gardens in the city.
  • Baton Rouge: Louisiana’s capital city is just a short drive from Carrollton and offers a wealth of history and culture, including the Louisiana State Capitol, the Shaw Center for the Arts, and much more.
  • Tomb of the Unknown Soldier: Located in nearby Metairie, this historic site offers a moving tribute to the servicemembers who have given their lives in service to their country.

Conclusion

With its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and vibrant local culture, this charming neighborhood has something to offer everyone. So why wait?
