Introduction to Elkins

Elkins is a picturesque city situated in Potomac Highlands, in the southern region of West Virginia, in the United States of America. Known for its scenic beauty, friendly people, and rich cultural heritage, Elkins is an ideal destination for those seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

With its abundance of natural attractions, outdoor activities, and world-class museums, Elkins offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Elkins

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Elkins boasts an array of culturally significant attractions that offer visitors a glimpse into the region’s rich history. Here are some of the most popular museums and historical places to visit in Elkins:

  • The Elkins Depot Welcome Center is a former train station that has been converted into a museum. It serves as the perfect starting point for visitors to learn about the city’s history and culture.
  • The West Virginia Railroad Museum displays steam and diesel locomotives and railroad memorabilia that reflect the region’s history.
  • The Randolph County Historical Society Museum showcases the heritage of the area and has a vast collection of artifacts, including antique furnishings, clothing, and photographs.

Natural Attractions of Elkins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Elkins is nestled in the midst of the Allegheny Mountains, offering breathtaking views and an abundance of outdoor activities. Nature lovers can indulge in a plethora of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and rafting. Some of the natural attractions in Elkins are:

  • The Monongahela National Forest, which spans over 921,000 acres and is home to breathtaking mountains, rivers, and wildlife.
  • The Otter Creek Wilderness Area, a stunning natural area with miles of trails for hikers, bikers, and photographers.
  • The Shavers Fork of the Cheat River, known for its thrilling whitewater rafting experiences amidst beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elkins

Elkins is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. The city offers several picturesque trails, each suitable for different fitness levels. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Elkins:

  • The Allegheny Highlands Trail is a 25-mile-long trail that runs from Elkins to Parsons and is perfect for biking or hiking.
  • The Bickle Knob Observation Tower Trail is a short but steep hike that rewards visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The North Fork Mountain Trail offers some of the most stunning views of rock formations, lush forests, and the valley.

Best Things to Do in Elkins: Activities and Tours

Elkins offers numerous activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the top things to do in Elkins:

  • Take a scenic train ride on the Durbin & Greenbrier Valley Railroad that passes through some of West Virginia’s most beautiful landscapes.
  • Visit the American Mountain Theater, a world-class music venue, for thrilling performances from talented musicians and singers.
  • Attend the Augusta Heritage Center, a cultural institute that offers music and dance workshops year-round.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elkins

To make the most of your visit to Elkins, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that allows you to explore the best attractions:

Day 1: Visit the Elkins Depot Welcome Center and explore the city’s history.

Day 2: Take a scenic train ride on the Durbin & Greenbrier Valley Railroad.

Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Monongahela National Forest.

Day 4: Visit the Randolph County Historical Society Museum and learn about the region’s heritage.

Day 5: Go whitewater rafting on the Shavers Fork of the Cheat River.

Day 6: Hike along the North Fork Mountain Trail.

Day 7: Attend a concert or show at the American Mountain Theater.

Food and Drink Scene of Elkins

Elkins is home to some of the best eateries in West Virginia, where you can savor traditional Appalachian dishes and local delights. Don’t miss these must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls, a local specialty made with Italian bread, pepperoni, and cheese.
  • Ramp Burgers, a tasty burger that features ramps, a type of wild onion highly popular in West Virginia.

Local Beverages

  • West Virginia wines and craft beers are well known for their unique flavor and authenticity.
  • Moonshine, a distilled alcoholic beverage made from locally grown corn and wheat.

Where to Stay in Elkins: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Elkins has a range of accommodation options catering to all budgets and preferences. Whether you opt for luxury hotels or locally managed hostels or guesthouses, there is something for everyone.

  • Graceland Inn & Conference Center is an ideal choice for those seeking a comfortable stay with a touch of history and elegance.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites is a modern and comfortable hotel that offers a range of amenities for your leisure and comfort.
  • Elkins Inn & Suites is a locally owned guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodation and a warm ambiance.

What to Buy in Elkins: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Elkins offers many opportunities to take home unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Handmade quilts and crafts made by local artisans.
  • Fresh produce, including fruits, vegetables, and honey, from local farmers’ markets.
  • Appalachian pottery and wooden crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Elkins

Elkins prides itself on its deep cultural connections and customs, shaped by its Appalachian roots. Some customs you might encounter in Elkins include:

  • Music and dance are integral parts of the local culture, so don’t be surprised if you come across impromptu performances in public places.
  • Locals are courteous and friendly, so a smile and a greeting go a long way.

Best Time to Visit Elkins

Elkins is a year-round destination, but each season offers something unique. For warm weather activities, visit during the summer months (June to August). To experience the autumn foliage and festivals, visit in the fall (September to November). For winter sports and holiday festivities, visit during the winter (December to February).

Do’s and Don’ts of Elkins

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photos of the stunning scenery and architecture.
  • Have a taste of local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural habitats.
  • Trespass into private property.
  • Speak loudly in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Elkins can have a stress-free and healthy trip by following some simple safety and health tips.

Safety in Elkins

Elkins is a safe city, but always be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Some safety tips include:

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Don’t go on isolated trails alone.
  • Always carry a map and a GPS device while exploring.

Health in Elkins

The city of Elkins has made deliberate efforts to maintain and promote healthy living standards for residents and visitors alike. To stay healthy while traveling in Elkins, keep in mind:

  • Always carry sunscreen, insect repellent, and a first aid kit.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids.
  • Be aware of any allergies or illnesses and carry the necessary medication with you at all times.

Getting to Elkins: Transportation Options and Logistics

Elkins is easily accessible by road, rail, and air. Here are some ways to get to Elkins:

Airports Near Elkins

The closest airports to Elkins are:

  • North Central West Virginia Airport, approximately 40 miles away
  • Yeager Airport, approximately 100 miles away

Bus Stations Near Elkins

The closest bus stations to Elkins are:

  • Elkins Depot Welcome Center, which offers bus service to neighboring towns and cities.
  • Barbour County Intermodal Facility, approximately 15 miles away.

Other Options

Elkins is also accessible by car, train, and bike. The city has an excellent road network, and train services connect it to other parts of the country.

How to Get Around in Elkins

Elkins is a walkable city, and many of its attractions are within walking distance, but other transportation options include:

  • Car rentals
  • Taxi services
  • Biking rentals

Where to Go Next from Elkins: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Elkins is conveniently located to some of West Virginia’s most exciting destinations. Here are some popular excursions from Elkins:

  • Canaan Valley: The stunning valley is located approximately 40 miles away from Elkins and is known for its skiing, snowboarding, and outdoor adventures.
  • Lewisburg: A historic and charming town located approximately 90 miles away from Elkins, known for its shopping and outdoor adventures.
  • Blackwater Falls State Park: Located approximately 30 miles away from Elkins, the state park features stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Elkins is home to an array of attractions and activities for nature lovers, culture enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike, making it an ideal destination for your next vacation. So, pack your bags and prepare to get lost in the enchanting beauty of West Virginia’s Potomac Highlands.
