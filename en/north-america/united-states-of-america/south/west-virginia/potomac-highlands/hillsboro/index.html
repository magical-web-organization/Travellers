Introduction to Hillsboro

Located in the Potomac Highlands of West Virginia, Hillsboro is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventure. Whether you are a history buff, an avid hiker, or simply looking for a scenic getaway, Hillsboro has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hillsboro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pearl S. Buck Birthplace Museum: The birthplace of the Pulitzer Prize-winning author Pearl S. Buck has been converted into a museum that showcases her life and works.
  • Droop Mountain Battlefield State Park: The site of the last Civil War battle in West Virginia, this state park features a museum and a self-guided tour of the battlefield.
  • Pocahontas County Opera House: A historic building that has been restored into a popular cultural hub, the opera house hosts concerts, plays, and other live performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Hillsboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Watoga State Park: Spread over 10,000 acres, Watoga State Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with hiking trails, fishing spots, swimming areas, and more.
  • Cranberry Glades Botanical Area: A unique ecosystem that features rare flora and fauna, the Cranberry Glades offer a serene and picturesque setting for hikers and nature lovers.
  • Highland Scenic Highway: A 43-mile long scenic highway that passes through Monongahela National Forest, Highland Scenic Highway is a must-do drive for visitors to Hillsboro.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hillsboro

  • Greenbrier River Trail: A 77-mile long trail that follows the path of the Greenbrier River, the Greenbrier River Trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Cranberry Wilderness Area: A rugged and remote area that features over 35 miles of hiking trails, the Cranberry Wilderness Area offers a challenging hike and stunning views to the more adventurous visitors.

Best Things to Do in Hillsboro: Activities and Tours

  • Tea Time at the Pearl S. Buck Birthplace Museum: Visitors can enjoy a quintessential Southern-style tea along with a guided tour of the museum and a book discussion group.
  • Greenbank Observatory: Home to one of the world’s largest fully steerable radio telescopes, the Greenbank Observatory offers visitors a chance to learn about astronomy and space science through tours and programs.
  • Highland County Maple Festival: Held annually in March, the festival showcases the maple syrup making process and offers visitors maple flavored treats, live music, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Hillsboro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni rolls: A West Virginia staple, pepperoni rolls are soft buns filled with slices of spiced pepperoni.
  • Ramp dishes: A seasonal delicacy in West Virginia, ramps are wild onions that are used in a variety of dishes including soups, stews, and omelets.
  • Trout: Freshly caught trout is a popular dish in Hillsboro and can be prepared in various ways including baked, grilled, or blackened.

Local Beverages

  • West Virginia wines: The state of West Virginia boasts over 20 wineries that offer a variety of wines made from locally grown fruits and grapes.
  • Moonshine: A strong distilled liquor that is a part of Appalachian culture, moonshine is often flavored with fruits and spices and can be found at local distilleries.

Where to Stay in Hillsboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Route 39 RV Park: A family-friendly campground with RV sites, cabins, and tent camping options.
  • The Old Clark Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast that features individually decorated rooms and modern amenities.
  • Snowshoe Mountain Resort: A popular ski resort that offers a variety of accommodation options including lodge rooms, condos, and townhouses.

What to Buy in Hillsboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup: West Virginia is known for its high-quality maple syrup, and visitors can buy bottles of the golden liquid as souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: Local artisans in Hillsboro create a range of handicrafts including pottery, quilts, and wooden toys that make for unique and authentic souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hillsboro

  • Hospitality: People of Hillsboro are known for their warm and welcoming nature, and visitors can expect to be treated with genuine kindness and hospitality.
  • Southern dialect: The locals speak with a distinct Southern dialect that is infused with Appalachian slang and idioms.

Best Time to Visit Hillsboro

The best time to visit Hillsboro is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall are also good times to visit when the weather is mild, and the fall foliage offers a spectacular sight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hillsboro

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the numerous outdoor activities and scenic vistas.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the natural surroundings by littering or damaging the environment.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals by using offensive language or behavior.
  • Ignore the safety guidelines for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hillsboro

Hillsboro is a safe and welcoming place, but visitors should be vigilant and take the usual safety precautions including locking car doors, not leaving valuables unattended, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Hillsboro

Visitors should carry a first aid kit and insect repellent when hiking or camping. Those with allergies or medical conditions should carry their medications and consult their doctor before embarking on any outdoor activity.

Getting to Hillsboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hillsboro

The nearest major airport to Hillsboro is Greenbrier Valley Airport (LWB), which is about 45 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Hillsboro

The closest bus stations to Hillsboro are located in nearby towns such as Lewisburg and Marlinton.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Hillsboro on the scenic US Route 219 or take Amtrak to either White Sulphur Springs or Hinton and then drive or take a bus to Hillsboro.

How to Get Around in Hillsboro

The best way to get around Hillsboro is by car or by bike, although walking is also an option for exploring the town center.

Where to Go Next from Hillsboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Snowshoe Mountain Resort: A popular ski resort that offers downhill skiing, snowboarding, and cross-country skiing in the winter, and mountain biking, golfing, and hiking in the summer.
  • Seneca Rocks: A towering rock formation that attracts hikers, rock climbers, and nature lovers from all over the world.
  • New River Gorge National River: A scenic area with deep gorges, hiking trails, and whitewater rapids, the New River Gorge offers a variety of outdoor activities including rafting, kayaking, and fishing.

Conclusion

A trip to Hillsboro is the perfect getaway for those who love natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventure. With its stunning landscape, warm hospitality, and range of activities, Hillsboro promises a memorable vacation for all.
