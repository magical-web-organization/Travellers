Introduction to Snowshoe

Snowshoe, nestled in the Potomac Highlands of West Virginia in the southern region of the United States, is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking for adventure. From hiking and biking to skiing and snowboarding, Snowshoe offers a wide range of activities for visitors to enjoy.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Snowshoe. We’ll cover the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, activities, dining, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Snowshoe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cass Scenic Railroad State Park offers tours of the historic steam engine-powered train that was used to transport lumber and logs through the mountains. The park also features a museum, gift shop, and several hiking trails.
  • The Pearl S. Buck Birthplace Museum is a tribute to the Nobel Prize winner and Pulitzer Prize-winning author who was born in nearby Hillsboro, WV. The museum offers self-guided tours of the homestead and exhibits on Buck’s life and accomplishments.

Natural Attractions of Snowshoe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Shavers Lake is a 7-acre lake located in Snowshoe’s Shavers Fork area and is great for fishing and boating in the summer. The lake area also has picnic facilities and hiking trails.
  • Snowshoe Mountain has 60 beautiful mountain biking trails from beginner to advanced levels. Visitors can also try out one of the many hiking trails in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Snowshoe

  • Allegheny Trail is a 330-mile hike that passes through several states including West Virginia, Maryland, and Pennsylvania. It is recommended for those with intermediate to advance hiking skills.
  • Snowshoe Basin Crawl offers hikers a out-and-back trail that gives panoramic views of Cheat Mountain, Castle Rock, and the valley below.

Best Things to Do in Snowshoe: Activities and Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Snowshoe

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Pocahontas County’s Famous Pepperoni Rolls.
  • The Fiddlehead Restaurant serves up a delightful breakfast and has many seasonal menu options throughout the day.

Local Beverages

  • Enjoy some locally brewed options at Big Timber Brewery, located just a few miles from Snowshoe.

Where to Stay in Snowshoe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Snowshoe’s famed Slopeside Suites offers tourists the most luxurious accommodation.
  • The Inn at Snowshoe is also a popular option for visitors looking for a more budget-friendly stay.

What to Buy in Snowshoe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Stop by Elk River Touring, a locally owned shop, where you can pick up outdoor gear and apparel before hitting the trails.
  • The Raven Trading Company showcases Appalachian arts and crafts from West Virginia artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Snowshoe

Snowshoe is a small town with deep-rooted traditions. The locals are known for their friendliness, love of their land and its bountiful natural resources. Visitors to Snowshoe are welcome if they are respectful of the locals and their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Snowshoe

Winter months are when Snowshoe is at its best. The ski season starts in early December and continues until late March. However, summer months also bring great hiking and beautiful scenery to the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Snowshoe

Do:

  • Do pack for all seasons.
  • Do respect the locals and their traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack water and snacks when exploring the area.
  • Don’t litter or leave any trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Snowshoe

Visitors should be prepared for extreme weather conditions and changeable weather while participating in outdoor activities. Visitors should ensure they are prepared with appropriate gear and supplies.

Health in Snowshoe

Visitors should be prepared with necessary vaccination and medication requirements before traveling to the area. Visitors with pre-existing respiratory, cardiovascular, or other health conditions should consult a healthcare professional before making the journey.

Getting to Snowshoe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Snowshoe

  • Charlottesville-Albemarle Airport, Virginia (CHO) is about a two-hour drive from Snowshoe.
  • Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport, Virginia (ROA) is about a three-hour drive away from Snowshoe.

Bus Stations Near Snowshoe

  • The Greyhound Bus Station located in Charleston, WV is the closest Greyhound bus station to Snowshoe.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also drive to Snowshoe through several scenic routes while also having the option of car rentals.

How to Get Around in Snowshoe

Renting a car provides ease of access and lack of restrictions to visitors when exploring the area. Otherwise, there are taxi services available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Snowshoe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Take a scenic drive through the nearby Monongahela National Forest.
  • The small town of Lewisburg, WV has a charming downtown filled with shopping, dining, and antiques.

Conclusion

Snowshoe is a fantastic destination for visitors looking for adventure and natural beauty. With a wide range of activities to enjoy, from skiing and snowboarding to hiking and cycling, travelers can experience the beauty of West Virginia landscapes and culture. Make sure to use this comprehensive travel guide for the ultimate Snowshoe experience.
