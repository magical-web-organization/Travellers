Introduction to Romney

Romney, located in the Potomac Highlands of West Virginia, is a small town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. This charming town, situated in the South of the United States, offers visitors a unique blend of old-world charm and outdoor adventure.

Romney served as a crossroads in the Civil War, and its rich heritage is reflected in its historic buildings and museums. The town is also surrounded by the majestic Potomac Highlands, which offer many opportunities for outdoor recreation. With a wide range of attractions, activities, and great food, Romney is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Romney

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hampshire County Courthouse: Built in 1922, this courthouse is a National Historic Landmark that played a pivotal role in the Civil War. Visitors can take a guided tour of the courthouse and learn about its fascinating history.
  • Taggart Hall: This historic building was once a Civil War hospital and is now home to the Hampshire County Historical Society Museum. Visitors can learn about the history of Romney and Hampshire County through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Indian Mound Cemetery: This cemetery is the final resting place of Confederate soldiers, as well as local residents who played important roles in the town’s history. Visitors can take a self-guided walking tour of the cemetery and learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Romney: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • C&O Canal National Historical Park: The C&O Canal is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and kayaking. The park covers 184.5 miles, with trails running through the historic canal towpath and scenic views of the Potomac River.
  • Rocky Gap State Park: This park is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and camping. Visitors can also enjoy hiking trails that offer scenic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Lost River State Park: Located just a short drive from Romney, this park offers hiking trails, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can also explore the park’s unique geological features, including the stunning rock formations of the “Cranny Crow overlook.”

Hiking/Biking Routes of Romney

Romney is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with a variety of trails for all skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  • C&O Canal Towpath: This 184.5-mile trail runs along the historic canal towpath and offers picturesque views of the Potomac River.
  • Savage River State Forest: This forest offers a variety of hiking and mountain biking trails, with stunning views of the Savage River Reservoir.
  • Green Ridge State Forest: Located just a short drive from Romney, this forest offers scenic hiking trails, including the 16-mile Western Maryland Rail Trail.

Best Things to Do in Romney: Activities and Tours

  • Romney Carriage Tours: Visitors can take a guided carriage tour of the town and learn about its history, while enjoying the scenic views.
  • Potomac Eagle Scenic Railroad: This train ride offers stunning views of the Potomac River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Fort Mill Ridge Civil War Trenches: Visitors can explore these Civil War trenches and learn about their role in the Battle of Romney.

Food and Drink Scene of Romney

Must-Try Dishes

  • West Virginia Hot Dogs: These hot dogs are topped with creamy coleslaw and chili sauce, and are a local favorite.
  • Frosty Root Beer: This local beverage is a refreshing treat on a hot day.

Local Beverages

  • Brews and Barrels: This brewery offers a variety of craft beers, as well as tours and tastings.
  • Hampshire County Co-Op: Visitors can purchase locally produced wines, ciders, and meads at this co-op.

  • The Inn at Abbott Farm Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a beautifully restored farmhouse and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Romney

Romney is a friendly and welcoming town, where visitors can experience the warm hospitality of West Virginia locals. The town has a rich history, and visitors are encouraged to take an interest in the town’s past and learn about its local customs.

Best Time to Visit Romney

Romney can be enjoyed year-round, with each season offering its own unique attractions. Spring and fall offer comfortable temperatures and beautiful scenery, while summer is the perfect time for outdoor recreation. Winter visitors can enjoy skiing and snowshoeing in the nearby mountains.

Safety and Health

Safety in Romney

Romney is a safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their cars and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Romney

There are no major health concerns in Romney. Visitors are advised to take standard health precautions, such as drinking bottled water and wearing sunscreen.

The town is small enough to be explored on foot, but visitors may also want to consider renting a bike or car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are not widely available in Romney.

Conclusion

Romney, West Virginia is a hidden gem, offering a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re interested in exploring local museums, hiking scenic trails, or simply enjoying the town’s friendly hospitality, Romney is the perfect destination for your next trip.
