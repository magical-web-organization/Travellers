Introduction to Davis

Nestled in the heart of the Potomac Highlands in West Virginia, Davis is a charming town that is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Known for its scenic views, rich culture, and outdoor adventures, Davis attracts visitors from all over the world.

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed vacation, Davis is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Davis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tucker County Historical Society Museum: The museum has a collection of artifacts and memorabilia that traces the history of Tucker County from the early pioneer days to the present.

  • Blackwater Falls State Park: One of the most photographed locations in West Virginia, Blackwater Falls State Park is home to the stunning Blackwater Falls and offers a variety of outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and fishing.

  • Canaan Valley Resort State Park: This park offers a range of outdoor activities like skiing, snowboarding, golfing, and hiking. It is also equipped with a conference center, lodge, and restaurant.

Natural Attractions of Davis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dolly Sods Wilderness Area: This stunning landscape is a popular destination for hikers and backpackers due to its rugged terrain and diverse vegetation.

  • Monongahela National Forest: This forest is home to a variety of wildlife and offers outdoor activities like camping, hiking, and fishing.

  • Seneca Rocks: Seneca Rocks is a popular destination for rock climbers and hikers. The rock formations offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Davis

  • Allegheny Trail: This trail offers hiking and biking opportunities for visitors who want to explore the stunning landscapes of the Potomac Highlands.

  • Blackwater Canyon Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking and biking and offers some of the best views of the Blackwater Canyon.

  • Canaan Mountain Backcountry: This backcountry destination offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that wind through wildflowers, hardwood forests, and rocky terrain.

Best Things to Do in Davis: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing at Canaan Valley Resort State Park: Canaan Valley Resort State Park is a popular destination for skiers and snowboarders due to its challenging terrain.

  • Mountain Biking at Snowshoe Mountain Resort: Snowshoe Mountain Resort offers some of the best mountain biking trails in the country.

  • Whitewater Rafting on the Cheat River: The Cheat River offers a variety of rapids for adventurous visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of Davis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: This West Virginia specialty is a delicious combination of soft bread and spicy pepperoni. It’s perfect for a quick snack or a light meal.

  • Rainbow Trout: Freshly caught rainbow trout is a must-try dish in Davis.

  • Appalachian Fried Chicken: This dish features crispy fried chicken that is coated in a blend of herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Mountain State Brewing Co.: This local brewery offers a variety of beer that is made using locally sourced ingredients.

  • Sweet Dreams Winery: This winery offers a variety of wines made using locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Davis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Canaan Valley Resort State Park: This hotel offers comfortable accommodations and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Timberline Four Seasons Resort: This resort is perfect for visitors who are looking for a relaxing getaway. It offers a range of cabins and lodges with modern amenities.

  • The Billy Motel: This trendy motel is located in the heart of Davis and offers comfortable accommodations with a retro feel.

What to Buy in Davis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Davis is known for its unique pottery, which is made using local clay and traditional techniques.

  • Local Honey: The honey produced in Davis is known for its rich flavor and high quality.

  • Appalachian Crafts: Local artisans sell a variety of handmade crafts that reflect the unique culture and history of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Davis

Davis has a rich culture that is rooted in its history and natural beauty. Locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to engage with the community. The mountainous landscape has played a significant role in shaping the culture of the region, and visitors can expect to encounter a deep appreciation for nature and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Davis

The best time to visit Davis is from May to October when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a popular time to visit as the leaves change color and the landscape transforms into a stunning tapestry of gold and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Davis

Do:

  • Do explore the local culture and history through museums and historical landmarks.

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities like hiking and biking.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the natural areas and be sure to dispose of trash properly.

  • Don’t disturb the wildlife.

  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather and terrain.

Safety and Health

Safety in Davis

Davis is a safe destination for tourists, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors are advised to practice caution while exploring the natural areas and to stay within designated trails.

Health in Davis

The healthcare facilities in Davis are limited, and visitors are advised to bring their medications and medical supplies while traveling. Visitors should also take measures to prevent tick bites and other health risks associated with outdoor activities.

Getting to Davis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Davis

  • Yeager Airport (CRW): The airport is located in Charleston and offers flights to major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Davis

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The bus station is located in Elkins, which is about 25 miles from Davis.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car in nearby cities like Pittsburgh and drive to Davis.

  • Shuttle Service: Shuttle services are available from airports and bus stations to Davis.

How to Get Around in Davis

The best way to get around Davis is by car. Visitors can rent a car or bring their own. Biking and hiking are also popular ways to explore the town and the surrounding natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Davis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Harpers Ferry National Historical Park: This park is located in West Virginia and is known for its scenic views and historical landmarks.

  • Shenandoah National Park: This park is located in Virginia and offers a range of outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and fishing.

  • Pittsburgh: This vibrant city is located in Pennsylvania and is known for its rich history, culture, and art scene.

Conclusion

Davis is a charming town that offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures, culture, and history. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed vacation, Davis is the perfect destination. With its stunning natural beauty, friendly locals, and rich culture, Davis is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors.
