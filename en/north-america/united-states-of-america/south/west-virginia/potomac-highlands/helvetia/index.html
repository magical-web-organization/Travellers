Introduction to Helvetia

Located in the Potomac Highlands of West Virginia, Helvetia is a charming little town with a rich history and cultural significance. Helvetia was first settled by Swiss immigrants in the mid-19th century, and its unique heritage and traditions have been preserved to this day. The town is small, with a population of less than 60 people, but its friendly and welcoming community is a big draw for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Helvetia

There is a lot to see and do in Helvetia, and we’ve compiled a list of the top attractions and activities that you shouldn’t miss during your visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the history and heritage of Helvetia, these attractions are a must-visit:

  • Helvetia Museum and Cultural Center: Located in the heart of town, this museum showcases the history and culture of Helvetia through exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • Kultur Haus Helvetia: This historic building was once a general store, but it’s now a community center where locals gather to celebrate their Swiss traditions and culture.
  • Saint Boniface Catholic Church: This beautiful church was built by Swiss immigrants in 1883 and is still used today for religious services.

Natural Attractions of Helvetia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Helvetia is nestled in the mountains, surrounded by natural beauty. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions and scenic viewpoints:

  • Kumbrabow State Forest: This beautiful state forest offers hiking trails, fishing, camping, and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Tea Creek Trail: This popular hiking trail takes you through the stunning Tea Creek Wilderness Area, with opportunities for bird watching and wildlife spotting.
  • Shavers Mountain Fire Tower: This historic fire tower offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and is a popular spot for photographers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Helvetia

There are many hiking and biking trails in and around Helvetia. Here are some of the most popular:

  • West Fork Trail: This 23-mile trail follows the West Fork of the Greenbrier River and is great for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.
  • Allegheny Trail: This 330-mile trail runs through some of the most scenic parts of West Virginia and takes you through Helvetia and other nearby towns.
  • Tea Creek Mountain Trail: This 13.7-mile trail offers challenging terrain and stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Helvetia: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Helvetia:

  • Swiss Wine Tasting Tour: Helvetia is famous for its Swiss-style wines, and you can sample them at local wineries like Forks of Cheat Winery and Lambert’s Vintage Wines.
  • Swiss Folk Dance and Music Festival: Every year, Helvetia hosts a festival to celebrate its heritage with traditional Swiss dancing, music, and food.
  • Guided Tours: You can take a guided tour of the town to learn more about its history and Swiss traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Helvetia

Helvetia is known for its delicious Swiss cuisine and homemade wine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fondue: A classic Swiss dish made with melted cheese and served with bread and vegetables.
  • Rösti: A Swiss potato dish that’s similar to hash browns.
  • Raclette: Another Swiss cheese dish that’s served melted and scraped onto potatoes or bread.

Local Beverages

  • Swiss-style Wines: Helvetia’s wineries produce delicious Swiss-style wines, including Riesling, Chardonnay, and Cabernet Franc.
  • Homemade Schnapps: Helvetia is also famous for its homemade fruit schnapps, which is available at local restaurants and shops.

Where to Stay in Helvetia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a few options for accommodation in Helvetia, including:

  • The Guesthouse: This historic guesthouse is located in the heart of town and offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Helvetia Bunkhouse: This rustic hostel is perfect for budget travelers and hikers.
  • Private Vacation Rentals: There are a few private vacation rentals available in Helvetia, including cabins and cottages.

What to Buy in Helvetia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some ideas:

  • Handmade Crafts: Helvetia has a vibrant community of artists and craftsmen, and you can find handmade pottery, jewelry, and other crafts at local shops and galleries.
  • Swiss-style Foods: You can bring home some Swiss-style cheeses, wines, and other specialty foods from local shops like the Helvetia General Store and the Mountain People’s Co-Op.

Local Culture and Customs of Helvetia

Helvetia has a rich cultural heritage, and locals are proud of their Swiss traditions. Some of the customs and practices you may encounter include:

  • Swiss Dress: Many locals still wear Swiss-style clothing for special events and festivals.
  • Swiss Music and Dance: Locals love to celebrate their heritage through traditional Swiss music and dance.
  • Community Events: Helvetia has a strong sense of community, and events like barn dances, potluck dinners, and festivals are popular and well-attended.

Best Time to Visit Helvetia

The best time to visit Helvetia depends on your interests. If you want to see the town in full festival mode, come during May Day or the Swiss Folk Dance and Music Festival in September. Fall is also an excellent time to visit Helvetia, when the leaves on the trees turn red and gold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Helvetia

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try some Swiss-style food and wine.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the town’s history and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Leave trash or litter behind when hiking or exploring outdoor areas.
  • Disrespect locals or their property.

Safety and Health

Helvetia is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should be aware of a few things:

Safety in Helvetia

  • Be cautious when hiking or exploring outdoor areas, and always let someone know where you’re going and when to expect you back.
  • Follow all safety guidelines and rules when visiting Green Bank Observatory and other nearby attractions.

Health in Helvetia

  • Lyme disease is present in the area, so visitors should take precautions when hiking or spending time in wooded areas.
  • Bring sunscreen and bug spray, as ticks and mosquitoes can be a nuisance during the warmer months.

Getting to Helvetia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Helvetia is located in a remote area of West Virginia, so it’s best to have a car to get around. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Helvetia

  • Greenbrier Valley Airport: This small airport is located in Lewisburg, West Virginia, about an hour and a half from Helvetia.

Bus Stations Near Helvetia

  • Greyhound: The closest Greyhound station is in Elkins, West Virginia, about 30 minutes from Helvetia.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: You can rent a car at Greenbrier Valley Airport or at various locations in nearby towns like Elkins.

How to Get Around in Helvetia

Helvetia is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to venture further afield, a car is necessary. There are no public transportation options in the area.

Where to Go Next from Helvetia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cass Scenic Railroad State Park: Located about an hour and a half from Helvetia, this park offers scenic train rides and hiking trails.
  • Seneca Rocks: This iconic West Virginia landmark is about an hour from Helvetia and offers hiking trails, rock climbing, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Canaan Valley Resort State Park: About an hour and a half from Helvetia, this park offers skiing, hiking, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

With its rich heritage, stunning natural beauty, and friendly locals, Helvetia is a hidden gem in West Virginia. Whether you’re interested in history, hiking, or just soaking up small-town charm, there’s something for everyone in Helvetia.
