Introduction to Franklin

Nestled in the heart of Potomac Highlands, in West Virginia, Franklin is a charming small town boasting sublime natural beauty. Its rolling hills, sprawling forests, and pristine rivers offer many opportunities for outdoor adventures. Besides, it is a treasure trove of cultural and historical landmarks that tell fascinating stories of the town’s past. This town may have a small population of only 721 people, but it is big on hospitality and welcomes visitors with open arms.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Franklin, highlighting the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay and what to eat, drink and take home.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Franklin’s Heritage Hall Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into Franklin’s past, showcasing artifacts, antiques, and photographs.
  • Cedar Creek Civil War Battlefield: It is a must-visit destination for history buffs, commemorating the battle fought on October 19, 1864, between the Union and Confederate armies.
  • Pendleton County Historical Society Museum: Located in the old Jail building, this museum is a treasure trove of historic Pendleton County artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Franklin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seneca Rocks: Known as the largest rock climbing destination in the region, Seneca Rock is a majestic natural landmark that towers above the surrounding valley. However, if you’re not feeling brave enough to climb, make sure to take the hike up to the observation platform for a breathtaking view.
  • Spruce Knob: The highest peak in the state, Spruce Knob is a popular destination for hikers, offering several trails of varying difficulty levels.
  • Smoke Hole Caverns: These spectacular limestone caves, located in the Spruce Knob-Seneca Rocks National Recreation Area, boast impressive stalactites and stalagmites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin

Franklin offers ample opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the town’s breathtaking natural beauty. Some of the best hikes and bike routes include:

  • North Fork Mountain Trail: This trail offers challenging terrain with stunning views of the valley.
  • Seneca Creek Trail: It is an excellent hiking spot for families and nature lovers, which is a well-maintained route with small waterfalls and gorgeous scenic views.
  • Allegheny Highlands Trail: This trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride, with smooth and level terrain following the South Branch River.

Best Things to Do in Franklin: Activities and Tours

  • Smoke Hole Resort: This rustic resort offers activities like fishing, tubing, and kayaking.
  • Franklin Farmers Market: Open every Saturday morning, it’s an excellent place for fresh and locally produced food and artisanal goods.
  • Seneca Caverns: Guided tours enable you to discover the hidden underground treasures beneath Franklin’s surface.

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin

Day 1: Visit Pendleton County Historical Society Museum, Franklin’s Heritage Hall Museum, and Pendleton County Public Library.

Day 2: Take a hike up Seneca Rocks to take in the stunning panoramic views.

Day 3: Embark on a scenic drive through the Monongahela National Forest and visit Spruce Knob.

Day 4: Go fishing or kayaking on the South Branch River.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby towns like Circleville, Onego, or Brandywine.

Day 6: Explore Smoke Hole Caverns and Seneca Caverns.

Day 7: Visit Cedar Creek Battlefield Historical Park and then enjoy your evening with the locals in downtown Franklin.

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried chicken and biscuits: A Southern classic that gets a West Virginia spin in Franklin.
  • Apple butter: Locally made apple butter is a must-try.
  • Fresh trout: Franklin is known for its delicious trout dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Sample local brews at the Franklin Mercantile or Tapped Out Taproom.
  • Whiskey: Bourbon is West Virginia’s most famous drink, so make sure to try a local whiskey.

Where to Stay in Franklin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Guesthouse Inn: A charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of the town.
  • Smoke Hole Resort: A rustic resort offering cabins and outdoor activities.
  • Cabins at Seneca Rocks: A perfect accommodation option for hikers and outdoor adventurers.

What to Buy in Franklin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally produced honey and maple syrup are the perfect edible souvenirs.
  • Pottery and other hand-crafted items made by local artisans make for excellent souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin

Locals in Franklin are friendly, welcoming, and often conservative. They take pride in their rich heritage and are always happy to share their love and knowledge of the town with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Franklin

The best time to visit Franklin is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin

Do:

  • Respect locals and their customs.
  • Take time to appreciate Franklin’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin

Franklin is a small town where crime is rare. However, it’s always advisable to take the necessary safety precautions when visiting any new place.

Health in Franklin

The nearest hospital is in nearby Harrisonburg, Virginia. Make sure always to carry any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Franklin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Franklin

  • Shenandoah Valley Regional Airport (SHD)
  • Washington Dulles International Airport (IAD)

Bus Stations Near Franklin

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal- Harrisonburg, VA
  • Young’s Bus Service- Moorefield, WV

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available at Dulles airport or Harrisonburg, VA.

How to Get Around in Franklin

The best way to get around Franklin is by car since public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Franklin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dolly Sods Wilderness: A stunning, remote wilderness area with gorgeous views and epic trails for those who want to have some peace of mind.
  • The Green Bank Observatory: A haven for astronomers and space lovers, this observatory features the largest steerable radio telescope on the planet.

Conclusion

Franklin is a hidden gem of West Virginia, with its rich history and beautiful natural attractions. Whether you’re an outdoor adventurer, history buff, or just seeking some relaxation away from the hustle and bustle of city life, Franklin has something to offer for everyone. Make sure to visit this beautiful town and experience the charms of rural West Virginia for yourself.
