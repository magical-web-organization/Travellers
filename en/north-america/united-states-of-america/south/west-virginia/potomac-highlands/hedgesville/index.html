Introduction to Hedgesville

Nestled in the scenic Potomac Highlands of West Virginia, Hedgesville is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and small-town charm. With a population of just over 300 people, this town is a perfect destination for a relaxing getaway or a weekend trip. From hiking and biking trails to cultural attractions and local dining, Hedgesville offers something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Hedgesville

Hedgesville is home to a rich Appalachian culture, which is reflected in the town’s artisanal products, local cuisine, and traditions like ramp hunting and baking. Visitors can expect a warm welcome and down-to-earth hospitality from locals.

Best Time to Visit Hedgesville

The best time to visit Hedgesville is during spring (March to May) and fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are at their best. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hedgesville

Do:

  • Check out local wineries and breweries
  • Explore the outdoors and hiking/biking trails
  • Sample local cuisine, including West Virginia hot dogs, pepperoni rolls, and ramp dishes

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Leave traces of litter while hiking/trailblazing
  • Disrespect wildlife in the area

Safety and Health

Safety in Hedgesville

Hedgesville is a generally safe town. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions while exploring the outdoors, such as carrying ample water and notifying someone of their itinerary.

Health in Hedgesville

There are medical facilities in the nearby towns of Martinsburg and Berkeley Springs. Visitors are advised to have adequate travel insurance while in the area.

Getting to Hedgesville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hedgesville

  • Hagerstown Regional Airport (25 miles)
  • Eastern WV Regional Airport (32 miles)
  • Washington Dulles International Airport (67 miles)

Bus Stations Near Hedgesville

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Martinsburg (11 miles)

Other Options

  • Driving: Hedgesville is located on Route 9, approximately 11 miles west of Martinsburg and 17 miles east of Berkeley Springs.
  • Train: Amtrak Stations are available in Harpers Ferry (23 miles) and Martinsburg (11 miles).

How to Get Around in Hedgesville

The best way to get around Hedgesville is by car, as public transportation options are limited.
