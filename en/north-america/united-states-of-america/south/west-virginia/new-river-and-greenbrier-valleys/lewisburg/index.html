Introduction to Lewisburg

Located in the New River and Greenbrier Valleys of West Virginia, Lewisburg is a historic town with a vibrant cultural scene and stunning natural surroundings. The town is known for its charming downtown area, rich history, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in exploring history and culture, enjoying outdoor activities, or simply relaxing in a tranquil environment, Lewisburg has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lewisburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Greenbrier: This historic hotel has welcomed guests since 1778 and is known for its luxurious amenities, fascinating history, and world-class golf courses. The hotel has hosted numerous presidents and celebrities, and it played a significant role in American history.
  • Carnegie Hall: This historic building was built in 1902 and now serves as a cultural center for the community. It features exhibitions, concerts, and workshops, and it hosts several events throughout the year.
  • Lost World Caverns: This fascinating underground cave system features impressive rock formations, underground lakes, and unique wildlife. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the geology and history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Lewisburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Greenbrier River Trail: This 78-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail follows the scenic Greenbrier River and offers stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains and the surrounding forests.
  • Monongahela National Forest: This vast wilderness area covers over 900,000 acres and offers endless opportunities for outdoor recreation. Visitors can hike, camp, fish, hunt, and explore the stunning landscapes of the forest.
  • Lewisburg Farmers Market: This weekly market is held during the summer months and offers an incredible selection of local produce, baked goods, and crafts. Visitors can sample the local flavors and meet the farmers and artisans of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewisburg

  • Greenbrier River Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. It follows the Greenbrier River and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests. The trail is 78 miles long and features several access points.
  • Midland Trail: This scenic route runs through Lewisburg and offers a glimpse into the town’s past. Visitors can explore the historic buildings, landmarks, and cultural sites along the way.
  • Allegheny Trail: This challenging trail covers over 330 miles and offers breathtaking views of the Appalachian Mountains. It passes through several state parks, forests, and wildlife management areas.

Best Things to Do in Lewisburg: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Tour of Lewisburg: This spooky tour takes visitors on a journey through the town’s haunted past. Visitors will learn about the ghostly legends and eerie stories of Lewisburg.
  • Taste of the Town Tour: This delicious tour offers visitors the chance to sample the local flavors and specialties of Lewisburg. Visitors will taste artisanal cheeses, chocolates, and local craft beer.
  • Craft Beer Tour: This tour takes visitors to the local breweries and taprooms of Lewisburg. Visitors can sample a variety of craft beers and learn about the brewing process.

Food and Drink Scene of Lewisburg

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: This West Virginia classic is a savory and spicy treat. It features a soft roll stuffed with pepperoni and cheese, and it’s perfect for a quick snack or lunch.
  • Ramp Recipes: Ramps are a wild onion that grow in the Appalachian Mountains. They have a strong flavor and are used in many local dishes, including ramp soup, ramp pesto, and ramp butter.
  • Homemade Jams: Many local farms in the area produce homemade jams with fresh fruits and berries. Visitors can taste the flavors of the region and even bring home some jars as souvenirs.

Local Beverages

  • Greenbrier Valley Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers and ciders. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and sample some of the brewery’s specialties.
  • Smooth Ambler Spirits: This craft distillery produces award-winning whiskies, gins, and vodkas. Visitors can take a tour of the distillery and learn about the distillation process.
  • Hill and Holler Pizza: This local restaurant offers a variety of local beers and ciders on tap. Visitors can enjoy a delicious pizza and a cold beer in a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.

Where to Stay in Lewisburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Greenbrier: This historic hotel is known for its luxurious amenities, world-class golf courses, and fascinating history. It offers several dining options, a spa, and a casino.
  • Home-Towne Suites Lewisburg: This budget-friendly hotel offers spacious and comfortable suites with fully equipped kitchens. It’s a great option for families and long-term stays.
  • The Country Inn of Lewisburg: This charming inn features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a garden. It’s located in downtown Lewisburg and offers easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Lewisburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Soap: Many local artisans in the area produce homemade soap with natural ingredients. Visitors can find a variety of scents and textures and support local businesses.
  • Pottery: Local potters create beautiful and functional pieces with local clay. Visitors can find unique and one-of-a-kind items, such as mugs, bowls, and vases.
  • Quilts: Many local quilters create intricate and colorful quilts using traditional techniques. Visitors can find beautiful and warm blankets to take home and use as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewisburg

Lewisburg is known for its friendly and welcoming community, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, such as the importance of family and community, the love of the outdoors, and the appreciation of local arts and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Lewisburg

The best time to visit Lewisburg is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are colorful. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and snowy. However, each season offers unique and exciting activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewisburg

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Engage with the local community and support local businesses.
  • Attend cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural resources.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Be insensitive or disrespectful to the local history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewisburg

Lewisburg is generally a safe and welcoming community. Visitors should take common sense precautions, such as locking doors and windows, keeping valuables out of sight, and being aware of their surroundings. In case of an emergency, visitors should dial 911 for assistance.

Health in Lewisburg

Visitors to Lewisburg should have a basic health insurance plan. It’s important to stay hydrated and use sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors. Visitors should also be aware of any allergies or health conditions and carry any necessary medications.

How to Get Around in Lewisburg

The best way to get around Lewisburg is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car or bike from several local rental companies, or they can bring their own. The town is also walkable, and many attractions and restaurants are within walking distance from downtown.

Where to Go Next from Lewisburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fayetteville: Located about an hour from Lewisburg, Fayetteville is known for its outdoor adventures and stunning landscapes. Visitors can go rafting, rock climbing, hiking, and biking in the nearby New River Gorge.
  • Charleston: The state capital of West Virginia is about an hour and a half from Lewisburg. Visitors can explore the historic downtown area, visit museums and galleries, and enjoy local cuisine and music.
  • Snowshoe Mountain: This ski resort is about two hours from Lewisburg and offers winter sports and outdoor activities year-round. Visitors can ski, snowboard, snowshoe, hike, mountain bike, and ride scenic chairlifts.

Conclusion

Lewisburg is a fascinating and charming town with a rich history and vibrant cultural scene. Visitors can explore its historic buildings and landmarks, hike its scenic trails, and enjoy its delicious local cuisine and craft beer. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxation, Lewisburg has something for everyone.
