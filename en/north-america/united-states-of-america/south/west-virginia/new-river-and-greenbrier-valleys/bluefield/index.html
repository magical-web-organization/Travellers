Introduction to Bluefield

Located in New River and Greenbrier Valleys, in West Virginia, in the southern region of the United States of America, Bluefield is a small city known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage. It is home to a number of museums, historical sites, and scenic attractions, making it an ideal tourist destination for those looking to explore the wonders of the Appalachian Mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bluefield

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including pepperoni rolls and fried pickles.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities, such as hiking and ATV adventures.
  • Attend a local festival to experience the unique culture and customs of Bluefield.
  • Visit the museums and historical sites to learn about the rich cultural heritage of Bluefield.

Don’t:

  • Miss the opportunity to buy locally-produced souvenirs and gifts.
  • Disrespect the local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bluefield

Bluefield is a relatively safe city, but visitors should always take basic safety precautions, such as locking their car doors and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Bluefield

Visitors to Bluefield should take precautions to avoid tick and mosquito bites, as they can carry diseases such as Lyme disease and West Nile virus. It is also important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when engaging in outdoor activities.
