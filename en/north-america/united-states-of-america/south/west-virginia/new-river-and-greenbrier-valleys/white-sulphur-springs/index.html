Introduction to White Sulphur Springs

White Sulphur Springs, located in New River and Greenbrier Valleys of West Virginia, is a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from all over the world. This charming town is famous for its natural beauty, rich history, cultural heritage, and friendly people.

Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, White Sulphur Springs has something special for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we will provide you with valuable information on the best places to visit, hiking/biking routes, things to do, where to stay, and local culture. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore this amazing destination in West Virginia.

List of Places to Visit in White Sulphur Springs

There are several exciting places to visit in White Sulphur Springs that offer a perfect blend of natural beauty and rich history. Here is a list of must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Greenbrier Resort: This stunning resort has been a top attraction in White Sulphur Springs for decades. It offers world-class amenities, culminating in a fascinating history dating back to the 1700s. Take a tour of the resort to learn about its significance in American history.
  • Lost World Caverns: This natural wonder is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Take a guided tour of the caves and see the unique rock formations and underground lakes.
  • The Salt Sulphur Springs Historic District: This historic district is home to many well-preserved buildings that showcase the architecture and lifestyle of the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of White Sulphur Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Greenbrier River Trail: This 78-mile-long trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail meanders through some of West Virginia’s most stunning landscapes, including lush green forests, sparkling rivers and streams.
  • Beartown State Park: This state park is home to striking rock formations, deep canyons, and verdant forests. It’s an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love to hike, bike or simply enjoy the outdoors.
  • The Allegheny Mountains: These majestic mountains offer breathtaking vistas, stunning rock formations, and golden sunsets. If you love nature, this is a destination that you can’t miss.

Hiking/Biking Routes of White Sulphur Springs

White Sulphur Springs offers several scenic routes for hiking and biking, which cater to all levels of experience. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • The Greenbrier River Trail: This 78-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail follows the Greenbrier River and passes through some of the most scenic and picturesque landscapes in West Virginia.
  • The Lewisburg to White Sulphur Springs Trail: This 3.9-mile trail is perfect for beginners and offers great views of the Allegheny Mountains.
  • The Greenbrier River Trail to Keeney Knob Trail: This 16-mile trail offers spectacular views of the river and a challenging hike for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in White Sulphur Springs: Activities and Tours

White Sulphur Springs offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in this charming town:

  • Experience the history and luxury of the Greenbrier Resort by taking a tour of the property.
  • Take a thrilling adventure into the Lost World Caverns and explore this natural wonder.
  • Saddle up and take a horseback ride through the stunning Allegheny Mountains.
  • Indulge in the fine dining scene by trying some of the local cuisine with a gourmet food tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for White Sulphur Springs

If you’re planning on spending a week in White Sulphur Springs, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Salt Sulphur Springs Historic District and explore the preserved 19th-century architecture. Day 2: Take a day trip to the Beartown State Park and hike through the unique rock formations. Day 3: Embark on the Greenbrier River Trail and enjoy nature at its best. Day 4: Take a tour of the Greenbrier Resort and experience luxury and history rolled into one. Day 5: Enjoy some local cuisine with a gourmet food tour of the area. Day 6: Take a horseback ride through the Allegheny Mountains and enjoy the stunning vistas. Day 7: Explore the Lost World Caverns and experience the hidden wonders of White Sulphur Springs.

Food and Drink Scene of White Sulphur Springs

White Sulphur Springs boasts a fantastic food and drink scene, with a wide variety of restaurants and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biscuits and Gravy: This staple Southern breakfast dish consists of fluffy biscuits smothered in a rich gravy and is served at many local restaurants.
  • Pepperoni Rolls: This handheld snack originated in West Virginia and consists of a roll stuffed with pepperoni and cheese. One of the area’s must-try foods.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes: A Southern delight, these fried green tomatoes are a must-try at any local restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Moonshine: This locally brewed alcohol is a must-try for those who love a strong drink.
  • West Virginia Wine: The rich soil and perfect climate in West Virginia create ideal growing conditions for wine grapes. Several local wineries offer tours and tastings.

What to Buy in White Sulphur Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

White Sulphur Springs offers several unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for those looking to bring a piece of their trip home with them. Here are some top picks:

Local Culture and Customs of White Sulphur Springs

White Sulphur Springs is known for its friendly and welcoming locals who embody the spirit of West Virginia. The area’s residents are proud of their heritage, and visitors can celebrate this through the destination’s cuisine, events, and attractions.

Best Time to Visit White Sulphur Springs

The best time to visit White Sulphur Springs is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the area’s natural beauty is in full bloom. The fall months are also a great time to visit when the foliage turns into a beautiful array of colors.

Safety and Health

Safety in White Sulphur Springs

White Sulphur Springs is a welcoming and safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should be aware of the surroundings and take necessary safety precautions when hiking or exploring the area’s outdoor attractions.

Health in White Sulphur Springs

The town has several health clinics and hospitals in the area. It is advisable to carry health insurance and be aware of certain health risks like ticks, poison ivy, and other allergies if you’re sensitive to nature.

Getting to White Sulphur Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

White Sulphur Springs can be accessed through several modes of transportation. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near White Sulphur Springs

The nearest airport to White Sulphur Springs is the Greenbrier Valley Airport, which is approximately 11 miles away.

Bus Stations Near White Sulphur Springs

The nearest bus stations to White Sulphur Springs are the Greyhound Bus Station in Lewisburg and the Southeastern Stages Bus Station in Ronceverte.

Other Options

If driving, the area is accessible by using Interstate 64 East or West, requiring a detour at Exit 175 at White Sulphur Springs into the town.

How to Get Around in White Sulphur Springs

White Sulphur Springs is a small town with limited public transportation. Renting a car, ride-sharing services, or taking a taxi is the best option to explore the town and its attractions.

Where to Go Next from White Sulphur Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located in the heart of Appalachia, White Sulphur Springs is an ideal destination for exploring the surrounding natural areas and attractions. Here are some other nearby destinations and excursions:

  • The Greenbrier River Trail continues into neighboring towns and offers stunning scenery all the way to Marlinton.
  • Lewisburg, a charming historic town just 10 miles from White Sulphur Springs has great dining experiences and has a close proximity to the Lost World Caverns.
  • Fayetteville, located just 40 miles from White Sulphur Springs, is a well-known hub for whitewater rafting, rock climbing, and other outdoor-based activities.

Conclusion

White Sulphur Springs offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage that tourists of all interests can explore and enjoy. With its stunning outdoor attractions, rich history, incredible food and drink scene, and friendly locals welcoming visitors with warmth and hospitality, the town is an ideal destination for a dream vacation.
