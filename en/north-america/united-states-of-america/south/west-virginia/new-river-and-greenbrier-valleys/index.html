Introduction to New River and Greenbrier Valleys

Nestled in the southwestern corner of West Virginia, New River and Greenbrier Valleys are two of the state’s most scenic and treasured destinations. Known for its rugged beauty, the area boasts an abundance of opportunities for outdoor recreation, cultural experiences, and relaxation. Whether you’re looking to take a leisurely stroll along the Greenbrier River Trail, explore the rich history of the New River Gorge, or simply soak up the natural beauty, there is something for everyone in these valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New River and Greenbrier Valleys

New River and Greenbrier Valleys offer a plethora of hiking and biking trails for different skill levels and interests. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Endless Wall Trail
  • Glade Creek Trail
  • Greenbrier River Trail
  • Meadows of Dan Trail

Best Things to Do in New River and Greenbrier Valleys: Activities and Tours

  • White-water rafting in New and Gauley Rivers
  • Rock climbing and rappelling in New River Gorge
  • Horseback riding in Greenbrier State Forest
  • Fishing and hunting
  • Scenic driving

Week-Long Itinerary for New River and Greenbrier Valleys

Day 1: Arrival and Visit to Tamarack: The Best of West Virginia. Day 2: Explore Beckley Exhibition Coal Mine and take a scenic drive to the New River Gorge. Day 3: Take a white-water rafting adventure on Gauley River. Day 4: Relax at Greenbrier resort and enjoy the luxurious amenities it offers. Day 5: Hike the Endless Wall Trail and visit the National Radio Astronomy Observatory. Day 6: Take a horseback riding tour in Greenbrier State Forest or enjoy fly fishing in one of the rivers. Day 7: Departure.

Local Culture and Customs of New River and Greenbrier Valleys

The Appalachian culture heavily influences New River and Greenbrier Valleys. Visitors can experience a rich music tradition, including Bluegrass and Old Time music. Also, local crafts and artwork are deeply rooted in the Appalachian culture.

Best Time to Visit New River and Greenbrier Valleys

The best times to visit are in the spring and fall. However, summers can be busy with white-water rafting, hiking and other activities with families and groups looking for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of New River and Greenbrier Valleys

Do:

  • Engage and interact with locals. They are friendly and welcoming.
  • Leave no trace. Respect nature and keep the trails clean and safe.
  • Enjoy and take advantage of the natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disregard the need for appropriate safety gear and precautions while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Litter or cause damage to the natural environment.
  • Expect luxury accommodations.

Safety and Health

Safety in New River and Greenbrier Valleys

While New River and Greenbrier Valleys are generally safe, outdoor activities can pose risks of injury. Visitors should take necessary precautions including proper gear and proceeding with caution.

Health in New River and Greenbrier Valleys

Visitors should take caution to avoid tick bites and check for signs of Lyme disease. In addition, visitors should be aware of heat-related illnesses while participating in outdoor activities during summer months.

Getting to New River and Greenbrier Valleys: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New River and Greenbrier Valleys

Nearby airports include Yeager Airport in Charleston and Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near New River and Greenbrier Valleys

Greyhound buses stop in Beckley, West Virginia, which is a short drive from many of the area’s attractions.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars
  • Taxi Services
  • Private Shuttles

How to Get Around in New River and Greenbrier Valleys

The best way to get around these valleys is by car as public transportation is limited. Additionally, bikes are an excellent option for exploring the Greenbrier River Trail, and other areas with designated biking routes.
