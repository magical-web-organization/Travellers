Introduction to Welch

Nestled in the New River and Greenbrier Valleys of West Virginia, in the Southern region of the United States, lies the charming city of Welch. This small city boasts a rich history, stunning outdoor landscapes, and plenty of cultural attractions. Despite its small size, Welch has plenty to offer visitors, making it a great destination for those looking to experience the beauty and culture of West Virginia.

List of Places to Visit in Welch

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The McDowell County Courthouse is a must-visit for those interested in the history of Welch. Built in 1892, the courthouse is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and features beautiful architecture. Another important historical site is the Welch National Guard Armory, which was built in 1938.

For those looking to learn about the region’s coal-mining past, the Coal Heritage Trail is a great place to start. This 300-mile trail takes visitors through the heart of West Virginia’s coal country and offers a glimpse into the history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Welch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for stunning natural beauty, Welch delivers. The Pinnacle Rock State Park, located just fifteen miles from the city, offers some of the most breathtaking views in the region. The park offers hiking trails, a picnic area, and a playground for families. For more hiking trails, visitors can head to the nearby Panther State Forest. The forest offers over 4,000 acres of hiking, camping, and fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Welch

The Coal Heritage Trail is a great option for bikers, offering a 13-mile paved trail that winds through the mountains. Additionally, the local ATV trails offer a great opportunity to explore the wooded terrain on four wheels.

Best Things to Do in Welch: Activities and Tours

For a truly unique experience, check out the ATV tours offered by Southern WV ATV Tours. These guided tours take visitors through some of the most beautiful parts of the surrounding mountains. For a different kind of adventure, consider booking a whitewater rafting trip on the nearby New River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Welch

Day 1: McDowell County Courthouse, Welch National Guard Armory Day 2: Pinnacle Rock State Park, lunch at Daniel’s in Bluefield, ATV tour with Southern WV ATV Tours Day 3: Coal Heritage Trail, stop for coffee at the Left Fork Grille in Whitman Day 4: Panther State Forest, lunch at the Historic Kimball Hotel Day 5: Break day – visit the Welch Community Pool and local shops Day 6: Welsh National Cemetery, Guided fishing tour at Pipestem Resort State Park Day 7: Whitewater Rafting on the New River

Food and Drink Scene of Welch

Must-Try Dishes

Welch has a rich culinary history, and visitors should definitely try a few of the local specialties. For breakfast, head to the Wagon Wheel Restaurant for their famous biscuits and gravy. For lunch, grab a hotdog at the Harkins Drive-Inn. For dinner, head to King Tut Drive-Inn for classic West Virginia favorites like burgers and milkshakes.

Local Beverages

West Virginia is known for its craft beer, and Welch is no exception. Be sure to check out the local Craft Brewed Tap House for a great selection of West Virginia beers.

Where to Stay in Welch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For a comfortable and affordable stay in Welch, check out the Holiday Inn Express. For a more unique experience, consider staying at one of the many bed and breakfasts in the area. A few great options include the Welch Bed and Breakfast and the Brier Inn.

What to Buy in Welch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a truly unique souvenir, consider picking up a piece of coal jewelry. These pieces are made from coal mined from the surrounding mountains and make for a great keepsake. Additionally, local crafts like pottery and hand-carved wooden bowls are available at many of the local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Welch

Welch has a strong sense of community and a deep connection to its coal-mining roots. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the local customs and to appreciate the rich history and traditions of the area.

Best Time to Visit Welch

The best time to visit Welch is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Additionally, the fall foliage in the surrounding mountains is truly stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Welch

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the surrounding mountains and natural beauty.
  • Experience the local culture and try the local food.
  • Appreciate the rich history and traditions of Welch.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs or culture.
  • Ignore the natural beauty of the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Welch

Welch is a safe city, but visitors should still take normal precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Lock cars and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Welch

Visitors should be prepared for the outdoor activities and bring appropriate gear. Additionally, it’s recommended to use sunscreen and bug spray when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Welch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Welch

The closest airport to Welch is the Beckley Raleigh County Memorial Airport, located about 45 miles from the city.

Bus Stations Near Welch

The closest bus station to Welch is the Greyhound station in Bluefield, located about 15 miles from the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Welch, as the city is easily accessible from major highways in the region.

How to Get Around in Welch

Having a car is the easiest way to get around Welch and the surrounding areas. However, visitors can also use public transportation or book guided tours for some of the outdoor adventures.

Where to Go Next from Welch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some great nearby destinations include the historic town of Bramwell, where visitors can take a walking tour of the beautiful Victorian mansions. Additionally, the city of Bluefield is just a short drive away and offers plenty of shopping and dining options.

Conclusion

Welch, West Virginia offers visitors a unique and charming destination with plenty of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious local food. Whether you’re looking for a quiet getaway in the beautiful mountains or a more adventurous trip, Welch has something for everyone.
