Introduction to Weirton

Weirton, a small town located in the Ohio Valley in West Virginia, offers visitors a mix of rich history, outdoor adventures, local culture, and welcoming hospitality. It was founded in 1909 by the Weirton Steel Company and named after its founder, Ernest T. Weir. Today, Weirton is a thriving community that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the rich history of the Ohio Valley, along with plenty of activities and attractions to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Weirton

If you’re planning a trip to Weirton, there are several places you should add to your itinerary. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Weirton Area Museum and Cultural Center: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Weirton area, from prehistoric times to the present. Exhibits include artifacts from the early days of the steel industry, as well as local art and photography.
  • Mary H. Weir Public Library: Built in 1908, this historic library is a beautiful example of the Beaux-Arts architecture. Visitors can take a self-guided tour of the library and its extensive collection.

Natural Attractions of Weirton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Panhandle Trail: This 29-mile trail stretches from Weirton to Carnegie, Pennsylvania, and offers stunning views of the Ohio River and Pittsburgh skyline. Visitors can hike, bike, or even horseback ride on the trail.
  • Tomlinson Run State Park: This 1,398-acre park is located just a short drive from Weirton and features hiking trails, fishing ponds, a swimming pool, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Weirton

Weirton and its surrounding areas offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of our favorite routes:

  • The Three Rivers Heritage Trail: This 24-mile trail runs along the banks of the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio Rivers and offers stunning views of Pittsburgh.
  • The Montour Trail: This 60-mile trail runs through several counties in Pennsylvania and ends in Clairton. It offers a mix of scenic views and challenging hills.

Best Things to Do in Weirton: Activities and Tours

Weirton offers visitors plenty of activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of our top choices:

  • Wintry River Adventure Tours: These tours take visitors on a scenic boat ride down the Ohio River, with stops at local wineries and historical landmarks.
  • Weirton Ghost Tour: This spooky tour takes visitors through the town’s most haunted buildings and sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Weirton

Weirton’s food and drink scene is a mix of classic American cuisine and local favorites. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: A West Virginia classic, these rolls feature pepperoni baked into a soft roll and are available at most local bakeries.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes: A Southern classic, these tangy tomatoes are often served with a spicy aioli.

Local Beverages

  • West Virginia Wine: Local wineries, such as Forks of Cheat Winery and Lambert’s Vintage Wines, offer a variety of wines made with local grapes.
  • West Virginia Beer: Breweries such as Wheeling Brewing Company and Parkersburg Brewing Company offer a variety of locally brewed beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Weirton

Weirton is known for its welcoming hospitality and pride in its local history and culture. Visitors should respect the town’s historical and cultural landmarks and engage with locals to learn more about the area’s unique history.

Best Time to Visit Weirton

The best time to visit Weirton is during the fall when the trees are changing colors, and the weather is mild. Visitors can enjoy the scenic drives and outdoor activities without the crowds of the summer months.

Weirton is a small town that is best explored on foot, especially for visitors staying in the town center. However, for those looking to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car or taking a private transportation service is recommended.
