Introduction to Hurricane

Located in the Metro Valley region of West Virginia, Hurricane is a small but vibrant city that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. With a population of around 7,000 people, Hurricane is a welcoming and friendly community that prides itself on its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and close-knit community.

List of Places to Visit in Hurricane

Here are some of the top places to visit in Hurricane, divided by cultural and natural attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Heritage Farm Museum and Village: A living history museum that features authentic Appalachian buildings and exhibits that showcase the area’s rich heritage.
  • Blenko Glass Company: A glass-making factory that offers tours and sells hand-crafted glassware.
  • The Red Caboose Regional Artisan Center: A co-op of local artisans who sell handmade crafts and artwork.

Natural Attractions of Hurricane: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Teays Valley Nature Preserve: A 680-acre nature preserve that offers hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Ridenour Lake: A scenic lake that offers fishing, boating, and picnicking opportunities.
  • Putnam County Park: A 156-acre park that offers hiking trails, camping sites, and playgrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hurricane

Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Hurricane:

  • The Ridenour Lake Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that circles Ridenour Lake and offers beautiful views of the water and surrounding landscape.
  • The Kanawha Trace Trail: A 31-mile trail that passes through Hurricane and offers challenging terrain and beautiful views of the area’s hills and valleys.
  • The Teays Valley Trail: A 5-mile trail that runs along the Teays River and offers scenic views and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Hurricane: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top activities and tours to take in Hurricane:

  • West Virginia Wine Tours: A tour company that offers guided tours of local wineries and vineyards.
  • New River Gorge Bridge Walk: A guided tour that takes visitors across the New River Gorge Bridge and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • White Water Rafting: Experience the thrill of white water rafting on the nearby New River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hurricane

Day 1: Start your trip with a tour of the Heritage Farm Museum and Village.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the natural beauty of Hurricane at Teays Valley Nature Preserve and Ridenour Lake.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Blenko Glass Company and learn about the art of glass-making.

Day 4: Experience the thrill of white water rafting on the nearby New River.

Day 5: Sample the local food scene at popular restaurants like La Roca Mexican Restaurant or Smith’s Restaurant.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive through the Kanawha Valley or visit a nearby winery for a tasting.

Day 7: End your trip with a visit to the Red Caboose Regional Artisan Center and pick up some hand-crafted souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Hurricane

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: A West Virginia specialty, these rolls are filled with spicy pepperoni and cheese.
  • Coal Miners Pasta: A hearty dish made with pasta in a rich tomato sauce, sausage, and peppers.
  • Ramp Recipes: Ramps are a wild onion that is a West Virginia delicacy. Try them in a variety of dishes, including ramp soup, ramp frittatas, and ramp pesto.

Local Beverages

  • Moonshine: Try some of the locally distilled moonshine for a true West Virginia experience.
  • Sweet Tea: A southern classic, sweet tea is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot summer days.
  • West Virginia Wine: Sample some of the local wines at the nearby wineries.

Where to Stay in Hurricane: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Hurricane:

  • Hampton Inn Winfield/Teays Valley: A modern hotel located near the area’s top attractions.
  • Sleep Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to the highway.
  • Airbnbs: Hurricane has a variety of vacation rentals available on Airbnb, including cozy guesthouses and cabins in the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Hurricane: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to take home from Hurricane:

  • Blenko Glass: Purchase hand-crafted glassware and vases from the Blenko Glass Company.
  • Appalachian Handicrafts: The Red Caboose Regional Artisan Center has a variety of handmade crafts and artwork for sale.
  • Moonshine: Bring home a bottle of locally distilled moonshine for a truly unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Hurricane

Hurricane is a welcoming and friendly community that values its rich cultural heritage. Visitors should respect the area’s history and traditions and make an effort to learn about the community’s unique customs and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Hurricane

The best time to visit Hurricane is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing colors. Summer can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hurricane

Do:

  • Do make an effort to learn about the local culture and history.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including pepperoni rolls and ramp dishes.
  • Do take advantage of the area’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t trespass on private property or disturb wildlife.
  • Don’t litter or damage the area’s natural resources.
  • Don’t disrespect the local community or its traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hurricane

Hurricane is generally a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should take standard safety precautions when exploring the area’s parks and natural attractions. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid hiking alone in remote areas.

Health in Hurricane

Visitors to Hurricane should take standard health precautions, including keeping up-to-date on routine vaccinations and wearing insect repellent to prevent tick bites.

Getting to Hurricane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hurricane

The nearest major airport to Hurricane is Yeager Airport in Charleston, West Virginia, which is about a 40-minute drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Hurricane

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus station in nearby Charleston, West Virginia, which offers connections to other cities throughout the region.

Other Options

Hurricane is easily accessible by car and is located near several major highways, including Interstate 64. Visitors can also take Amtrak trains to nearby cities and rent a car to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Hurricane

The best way to get around Hurricane is by car, as many of the area’s attractions and natural areas are spread out and not easily accessible on foot. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in nearby Charleston.

Where to Go Next from Hurricane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to check out after visiting Hurricane:

  • New River Gorge National River: Located about two hours south of Hurricane, the New River Gorge offers stunning views and world-class outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Kanawha State Forest: A nature preserve located about 30 minutes from Hurricane that offers hiking, biking, and camping opportunities.
  • Charleston, West Virginia: The state capital is located about 25 minutes from Hurricane and offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.

Conclusion

From its rich history and cultural attractions to its beautiful natural landscapes and outdoor adventures, Hurricane has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to explore the area’s top attractions or simply relax and soak in the local culture, Hurricane is a must-visit destination in West Virginia.
