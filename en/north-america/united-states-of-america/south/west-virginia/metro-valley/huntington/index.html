Introduction to Huntington

Nestled in the heart of Metro Valley in West Virginia, Huntington is a charming city that boasts a unique blend of urban and natural attractions backed by a rich cultural heritage. The city is known for its historical landmarks, picturesque landscapes, and mouth-watering cuisine. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit, things to do, and eat in Huntington.

List of Places to Visit in Huntington

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, Huntington has something to offer for everyone. Here’s a list of must-visit destinations in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Huntington Museum of Art
  • Heritage Farm Museum and Village
  • The Marshall University Museum
  • Ritter Park Rose Garden
  • Joan C. Edwards Performing Arts Center

Natural Attractions of Huntington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beech Fork State Park
  • Camden Park
  • Harris Riverfront Park
  • Guyandotte River

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntington

For adventure enthusiasts, Huntington offers a plethora of hiking and biking options. Here are some of the top trails you can explore:

  • The Huntington Greenway Trail
  • The Guyandotte Trail
  • The Ritter Park Trails
  • The Beech Fork Lake Trails

Best Things to Do in Huntington: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re on a solo trip or visiting with your family, Huntington has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Take a historic walking tour of downtown Huntington
  • Go fishing at Beech Fork Lake
  • Visit the Huntington Museum of Art
  • Attend a concert at the Pullman Square
  • Explore the Heritage Farm Museum and Village
  • Take a bike tour of the city

Food and Drink Scene of Huntington

No trip to Huntington is complete without trying out the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in the city:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The famous hot-dog topped with spicy meat sauce at Stewart’s Original Hot Dogs
  • The wood-fired pizza at Black Sheep Burrito and Brews
  • West Virginia-style pulled pork sandwich at Hillbilly Hot Dogs
  • The home-style fried chicken at Tudor’s Biscuit World

Local Beverages

  • Craft beers from the Huntington Tri-State Ale Trail
  • Moonshine and other spirits at Smooth Ambler
  • Freshly brewed coffee at Appalachian Coffee Company
  • Apple cider and donuts at Camden Park

Local Culture and Customs of Huntington

Huntington is a welcoming and diverse community with a rich cultural heritage. Some of the customs and traditions that are popular in the city include:

  • The annual Heritage Farm Fall Festival
  • Outdoor concerts and festivals at Pullman Square
  • Museums and historical landmarks honoring local history
  • The Tri-State Arts Association street art murals

Best Time to Visit Huntington

Huntington is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is warm and sunny. Fall is also a great time to visit when the leaves begin to change colors.
