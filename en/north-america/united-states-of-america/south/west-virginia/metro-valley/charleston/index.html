Introduction to Charleston

Charleston, located in Metro Valley, West Virginia, is a charming city that offers a good mix of outdoor adventure, history, and local culture. The city is famous for its scenic views, museums, and delicious cuisine. Whether you are traveling solo, with friends or family, Charleston is a perfect destination for a relaxing, yet adventurous vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Charleston

Charleston has several places to visit that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here’s a list of some must-visit attractions in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Virginia State Museum: The museum tells the story of West Virginia’s cultural heritage, from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • State Capitol Building: The iconic building represents West Virginia and its governance system.
  • Clay Center for the Arts and Sciences: It is a vast complex that features a planetarium, art museum, and a performance hall.

Natural Attractions of Charleston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kanawha State Forest: Ideal for hiking and camping, Kanawha State Forest offers over 25 miles of well-marked trails.
  • Hawks Nest State Park: The park offers breathtaking views of the New River Gorge and is a great destination for a picnic.
  • Coonskin Park: The park provides a serene natural setting, fishing, boating, playgrounds, picnic areas, a golf course for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charleston

Charleston has a variety of scenic hiking and biking trails for adventurers that connect the city’s beautiful parks and natural areas. Some popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Charleston Loop Trail: This five-mile trail loops around the city’s historic district, connecting many historical points of interest.
  • Kanawha State Forest Trail: The forest is home to several hiking trails, with Black Bear being one of the most scenic. The trail is approximately 4 miles long in total.
  • Little Creek Park Trail: The trail is 3 miles long and winds through the wooded hills of the park.

Best Things to Do in Charleston: Activities and Tours

Charleston is home to many unique experiences that visitors can enjoy. Here are a few options:

  • Avampato Discovery Museum: The museum offers a range of interactive displays and exhibits that provide a hands-on learning experience for visitors of all ages.
  • Mountain Quest: This adventure company provides treetop challenge courses, zip lines, and game contests.
  • Kanawha River Tour: The tour provides impressive views as you cruise along the river, getting a close-up look at some of the city’s best landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Charleston

If you are planning to spend a week in Charleston, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the State Capitol Building and The West Virginia State Museum
2 Explore the Clay Center for Arts and Sciences in the morning and hike at Kanawha State Forest in the afternoon
3 Take a Kanawha River Tour and have lunch at one of the nearby restaurants
4 Visit the Avampato Discovery Museum, then hike the Little Creek Park Trail
5 Drive to Hawks Nest State Park and enjoy a picnic and hiking
6 Experience the treetop challenge courses and game contests at Mountain Quest
7 Check out the local restaurants and cafes and do some souvenir shopping before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Charleston

Charleston’s food and drink scene is well known for its fusion of southern and local flavor, which blends perfectly with the local culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages while in Charleston:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: A traditional West Virginia snack made with bread and pepperoni, originating in the coal mines of the state.
  • Country Fried Steak: A dish made with steak coated in seasoned flour, fried, and served with gravy.
  • West Virginia Hot Dog: A hot dog with chili, mustard, and coleslaw, a popular local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Blenheim Ginger Ale: Produced in South Carolina with natural flavors and spicy ginger, it is known throughout the South for its robust flavor and distinct heat.
  • West Virginia Moonshine: A locally made corn liquor that has been made for centuries and is a staple drink of the region.
  • Mountain State Brewery: A family-owned and operated brewery specializing in handcrafted brews with a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Charleston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Charleston offers visitors a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are a few popular places to stay:

  • Charleston Marriott Town Center: A luxurious hotel with an excellent location, comfortable rooms, and exceptional dining facilities.
  • Brass Pineapple Bed & Breakfast: A cozy and comfortable guesthouse offering personalized attention from the friendly hosts in a convenient location.
  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham: A budget-friendly hotel with basic amenities and a comfortable stay in the heart of Charleston.

What to Buy in Charleston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Charleston, there are several souvenirs and local products that you should consider bringing home:

  • Blenko Glass: A handmade glassware store that has been crafting glass pieces since 1893. The store offers an array of products, from vases and bowls to unique decorative pieces.
  • Hand-Crafted Jewelry: Local artisans make hand-crafted jewelry that is unique and original. Several stores offer some of the best pieces around Charleston.
  • Handmade Quilts and Pottery: Several local artisans and stores offer hand-made quilts and pottery that make for a perfect gift to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Charleston

Charleston is rich in both history and culture, and the locals are known for their southern hospitality. Visitors should be aware of some of the local customs, such as being polite and courteous, dressing modestly in churches, and saying “please” and “thank you” often.

Best Time to Visit Charleston

Charleston’s climate makes it an ideal destination to visit year-round. However, the best time to visit is during fall, between September and November, when the weather is pleasant, and the foliage is a sight to behold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charleston

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine during your visit.
  • Do be polite and courteous to locals.
  • Do dress modestly in churches and adhere to dress codes, if applicable.
  • Do visit historical places and museums.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to locals or their customs.
  • Don’t litter or smoke in public places.
  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen and insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charleston

Charleston is generally a safe city to visit. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as keeping valuables safe in hotels and not leaving valuable items in cars.

Health in Charleston

Visitors should stay hydrated, avoid eating undercooked meats, and wearing the appropriate sunscreen and insect repellent, especially during summer.

How to Get Around in Charleston

Charleston is an easily walkable city, but visitors can also use local taxis or ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft. Visitors can also hire a bike or use the city’s bike-sharing system to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Charleston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Charleston is the perfect starting point to explore the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations:

  • Moab: A vibrant city in Utah, famous for its national parks, red rock formations, and outdoor activities.
  • Pittsburgh: Pittsburgh is a fascinating city in Pennsylvania, known for its museums, sports, and great food culture.
  • Washington DC: Washington DC is the capital city of the United States and home to many iconic landmarks, museums, and festivals.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Charleston is a unique city with a variety of attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers. With its rich cultural heritage, outdoor activities, delicious food, and welcoming people, Charleston is one of the must-visit destinations in the United States. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore Charleston and create lasting memories.
