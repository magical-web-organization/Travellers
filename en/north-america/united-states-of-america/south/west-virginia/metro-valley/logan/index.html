Introduction to Logan

Nestled in the heart of West Virginia’s Metro Valley, Logan is a small city that boasts big attractions. Known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, Logan is a top destination for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking to hit the hiking trails, explore historical sites, or sample local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Logan.

List of Places to Visit in Logan

Here are some must-see attractions to add to your Logan itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chief Logan State Park: One of the most popular attractions in Logan, Chief Logan State Park offers a range of recreational activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and camping. The park is also home to the Museum in the Park, which highlights the history, heritage, and culture of West Virginia.
  • Hatfield-McCoy Trails: Spanning over 700 miles of trails, the Hatfield-McCoy Trails offer one of the best ATV experiences in the country. With plenty of scenic landscapes and challenging terrains, the trails are a must-visit for adventure seekers.
  • Coalfield Jamboree: This performing arts center showcases local talent and hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including live music, comedy shows, and theater performances.

Natural Attractions of Logan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Holly River State Park: Located just outside of Logan, Holly River State Park is a nature lover’s paradise. Featuring over 8,000 acres of natural beauty, the park offers trails for hiking, fishing, picnicking, and more.
  • The Guyandotte River: This scenic river is perfect for kayaking, fishing, and swimming, with plenty of spots to stop and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.
  • Logan Wildlife Management Area: Known for its abundant wildlife and stunning views, Logan Wildlife Management Area is a great place to hunt, hike, or simply explore the great outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Logan

If you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of Logan on foot or bike, here are some popular routes to try:

  • Little Coal River Trail: This 13-mile trail runs along the Little Coal River and is perfect for hiking, mountain biking, or horseback riding.
  • Ridge Top Trail: With scenic views of Logan and the surrounding mountains, the Ridge Top Trail is a popular hiking destination.
  • Old Man’s Cave Trail: Located in the nearby Hocking Hills region of Ohio, this trail offers stunning waterfalls, rock formations, and forest scenery.

Best Things to Do in Logan: Activities and Tours

To make the most of your visit to Logan, check out these top activities and tours:

  • Hatfield McCoy ATV Tours: Explore the Hatfield-McCoy Trails on a guided ATV excursion, with options for all skill levels.
  • Whitewater Rafting: The nearby Gauley River is one of the top whitewater rafting destinations in the country, with options for beginners and experienced rafters alike.
  • History and Heritage Tour: Learn about Logan’s rich cultural heritage on a guided tour of the city’s top historical sites and museums.

Week-Long Itinerary for Logan

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Logan, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Chief Logan State Park and Museum in the Park Day 2: The Guyandotte River (kayaking or swimming) and Coalfield Jamboree Day 3: Hatfield-McCoy Trails ATV Tour and Holly River State Park Day 4: Hiking the Ridge Top Trail and exploring Logan’s downtown area Day 5: Whitewater Rafting on the Gauley River and visiting local farms for produce Day 6: Horseback riding at Little Coal River Trail and checking out local shops and restaurants Day 7: History and Heritage Tour of Logan

Food and Drink Scene of Logan

No trip to Logan is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: A West Virginia specialty, these rolls are filled with pepperoni and cheese and make for the perfect on-the-go snack.
  • Country Fried Steak: A classic Southern dish, this hearty steak is breaded and fried to perfection.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: A breakfast staple, biscuits and gravy feature fluffy biscuits smothered in a rich and flavorful gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Sassafras Tea: Made from the roots of the sassafras plant, this sweet tea is a refreshing and unique treat.
  • West Virginia Craft Beer: With a growing craft beer scene, West Virginia is home to a variety of local breweries, including Bridge Brew Works and Greenbrier Valley Brewing Company.
  • Sweet Tea: A Southern favorite, sweet tea is a staple of any meal in Logan.

Where to Stay in Logan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly options to luxurious accommodations, Logan has something for everyone. Here are some top choices:

  • Chief Logan Lodge: Located within Chief Logan State Park, this lodge offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to the park’s recreational activities.
  • Holiday Inn Express: A popular chain hotel, this option is convenient for travelers looking for reliable accommodations.
  • The Guest House Inn on Courthouse Square: This historic inn offers elegant rooms and a central location in Logan’s downtown area.

What to Buy in Logan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To remember your trip to Logan, here are some top souvenir and local product options:

  • Handmade Crafts and Artwork: Logan is home to many skilled artisans who create unique and beautiful crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and paintings.
  • Appalachian Foods and Spices: From homemade jams and jellies to locally sourced honey and maple syrup, the food scene in Logan is rich with Appalachian flavors.
  • Outdoor Gear and Accessories: Whether you’re looking for camping supplies, fishing gear, or hunting equipment, Logan’s outdoor shops have you covered.

Local Culture and Customs of Logan

With its deep roots in Appalachian culture and history, Logan has a unique identity that is worth exploring. Some aspects of local culture to be aware of include:

  • Music and the Arts: Logan is home to many talented musicians, writers, and visual artists who draw inspiration from the region’s cultural heritage.
  • Hospitality and Generosity: Southern hospitality is alive and well in Logan, with locals known for their welcoming and generous nature.
  • Religion and Faith: Christianity is a prominent part of life in Logan, with many churches and religious institutions throughout the city and surrounding areas.

Best Time to Visit Logan

While Logan is beautiful year-round, the best time to visit depends on your interests. For outdoor recreation, spring and fall offer mild temperatures and colorful foliage. Summer is ideal for water sports and outdoor activities, while winter is perfect for skiing and snow sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Logan

To make the most of your Logan experience, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Logan, from hiking trails to fishing spots.
  • Do soak up the local culture and try new foods, drinks, and experiences.
  • Do dress in layers, as weather can change quickly in the mountains.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the environment in any way. Leave no trace behind.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t underestimate the power of nature; always be prepared and stay safe while exploring.

Safety and Health

As with any travel destination, it’s important to take precautions and be aware of potential safety and health concerns in Logan.

Safety in Logan

  • Be cautious when driving on winding mountain roads and keep an eye out for wildlife.
  • Avoid hiking or camping alone and make sure to tell someone your plans before heading out.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings in unfamiliar areas and avoid flashing large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Logan

  • Be aware of the potential for tick-borne illnesses when spending time outdoors.
  • Stay hydrated and take breaks in the shade to avoid heat exhaustion or dehydration.

Getting to Logan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you’re traveling by air or car, Logan is easily accessible from major cities in the region. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Logan

  • Yeager Airport (CRW) in Charleston, WV (about 50 miles from Logan)
  • Tri-State Airport (HTS) in Huntington, WV (about 80 miles from Logan)

Bus Stations Near Logan

  • Greyhound Bus Lines (located in Charleston, WV)
  • Tri-State Transit Authority (with routes throughout the surrounding areas)

Other Options

  • Car rentals, taxi services, and ride-sharing apps like Uber are also available in and around Logan.

How to Get Around in Logan

While having your own car offers the most flexibility for exploring Logan and the surrounding areas, there are other transportation options available:

  • Taxis and ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are available in Logan.
  • Bike rentals and guided tours are also available through local outfitters like Hatfield McCoy Trails.

Where to Go Next from Logan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip and explore more of West Virginia and the surrounding region, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Charleston, WV: The state capital is just a short drive from Logan and offers a range of cultural attractions, including the State Capitol and the Clay Center for the Arts and Sciences.
  • Hocking Hills, OH: Located about two hours from Logan, this scenic area features stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and rock formations.
  • Gatlinburg, TN: For a more leisurely excursion, head south to Gatlinburg, TN, a charming mountain town known for its stunning views and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Logan is a hidden gem in the heart of West Virginia, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Logan has something for everyone. By following this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to make the most of your visit to Logan and experience all that this charming city has to offer!
