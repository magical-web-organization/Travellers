Kingwood, located in Mountains and Lakes Country, in West Virginia, in South, in the United States of America, is a hidden gem that boasts of natural beauty, rich history, and stunning scenery. If you’re planning to visit this charming city, our comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your trip. From exploring cultural attractions to discovering outdoor adventures, here’s everything you need to know about Kingwood.

Introduction to Kingwood

Nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains, Kingwood is a small town with a population of just over 2,600 people. It’s known for its charming downtown area lined with unique shops and eateries, as well as its proximity to natural wonders like Cheat River and the Allegheny Mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Kingwood

Here are some of the must-visit places in Kingwood:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Preston County Historical Society: This museum is known for showcasing the history of Preston County, including Civil War artifacts, early settler exhibits, and more.
  2. Arthurdale Heritage: This National Historic District showcases the homestead community that was created during the New Deal era.
  3. Kingwood Railroad Station: This station has been beautifully restored and is a great place to snap some pictures and learn about the town’s history with the railroads.

Natural Attractions of Kingwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cheat River: This river is a popular destination for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing.
  2. Coopers Rock State Forest: This state forest has over 12,000 acres of hiking trails, overlooks, and breathtaking views.
  3. Krepps Park: This park is a great place to enjoy a picnic or take a leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingwood

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Kingwood has plenty of options. Some of the best routes include:

  1. Cheat River Trail: This trail takes you along the Cheat River and is a great option for hiking or biking.
  2. Krepps Park Trail: This trail is a scenic loop through the park and is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride.
  3. Coopers Rock Trail: This trail is a challenging hike through the state forest and offers stunning views of the surrounding nature.

Best Things to Do in Kingwood: Activities and Tours

Whether you prefer indoor or outdoor activities, Kingwood has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do in Kingwood:

  1. West Virginia Wine Tour: Take a guided tour of the local wineries and vineyards.
  2. Antiquing: Browse through the antique shops in downtown Kingwood.
  3. Horseback Riding: Enjoy the scenic views of Kingwood while horseback riding.
  4. Fishing: Head to Cheat River for some of the best trout fishing in the area.

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for your trip to Kingwood:

Food and Drink Scene of Kingwood

Kingwood has a small but vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pepperoni Rolls: A West Virginia staple, this dish consists of a pepperoni-filled roll that’s perfect for a quick snack.
  2. Country Fried Steak: This hearty dish is a favorite among locals, served with gravy and mashed potatoes.
  3. Sausage Gravy and Biscuits: This classic breakfast dish is a must-try while in Kingwood.

Local Beverages

  1. West Virginia Wine: The local wineries offer a range of delicious wines, including whites, reds, and blushes.
  2. Mountain Moonshine: This locally-made moonshine is a popular choice among visitors.

Where to Stay in Kingwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kingwood offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Inn At Stonecliffe: Located in the heart of downtown Kingwood, the Inn At Stonecliffe offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  2. Sleep Inn & Suites: This newly-built hotel offers modern amenities and is located near Cheat River.
  3. Lakeview Golf Resort & Spa: This resort offers breathtaking views of Cheat Lake and is perfect for a luxurious getaway.

What to Buy in Kingwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Kingwood

Kingwood is a friendly, inviting town where visitors are welcomed with open arms. Some local customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  1. Hunting and Fishing: Hunting and fishing are a big part of the local culture, so be respectful and ensure you have any necessary permits or licenses if you choose to partake.
  2. Small-Town Hospitality: Visitors to Kingwood can expect to be greeted with warm, small-town hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Kingwood

The best time to visit Kingwood depends on what kind of experience you’re looking for. Spring and fall offer mild temperatures and vibrant foliage, making it a great time to visit for outdoor activities. Summer is perfect for taking advantage of the nearby river and lake, while winter offers a cozy getaway complete with skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kingwood

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Kingwood:

Do:

  1. Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in the area, including hiking, fishing and kayaking.
  2. Do try the local cuisine and drinks, including pepperoni rolls and West Virginia wine.
  3. Do connect with the friendly locals and embrace the small-town hospitality.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment while enjoying outdoor activities.
  2. Don’t be intimidated by the small size of the town - there are plenty of things to do and see.
  3. Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for exploring the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Kingwood is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of some safety and health hazards:

Safety in Kingwood

  1. Be aware of natural hazards, such as slippery conditions on hiking trails or strong currents in the river.
  2. Exercise caution while driving on winding mountain roads.

Health in Kingwood

  1. Be prepared for insect bites and ticks - make sure to bring bug spray and check for ticks after spending time outdoors.
  2. Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun while enjoying outdoor activities.

Getting to Kingwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kingwood is accessible by car, plane, and bus.

Airports Near Kingwood

  1. Morgantown Municipal Airport: This airport is located about 30 minutes from Kingwood and offers flights from several major airlines.
  2. Pittsburgh International Airport: This airport is located about 90 minutes from Kingwood and offers flights from major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Kingwood

  1. West Virginia Coach: This bus station is located in nearby Morgantown and offers connections to several other cities.
  2. Greyhound: This bus station is located in Uniontown, Pennsylvania, about 30 minutes from Kingwood.

Other Options

  1. Car Rentals: Rent a car from a major provider, such as Enterprise or Budget, to get around Kingwood and the surrounding areas.
  2. Uber: Uber is available in and around Kingwood, providing a convenient option for getting around town.

How to Get Around in Kingwood

Kingwood is a small town and is easily walkable. However, for those who prefer to drive, there are car rental options available and Uber is available in and around town.

Where to Go Next from Kingwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to expand their trip beyond Kingwood, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Blackwater Falls State Park: This park is located about an hour east of Kingwood and offers stunning waterfalls and hiking trails.
  2. Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania: This nearby city is just 90 minutes away and offers urban attractions like museums, sports, and dining options.

Conclusion

Kingwood, West Virginia, is a charming small town with plenty to offer visitors. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, you’ll find it all here. Book your trip today and discover everything Kingwood has to offer.
