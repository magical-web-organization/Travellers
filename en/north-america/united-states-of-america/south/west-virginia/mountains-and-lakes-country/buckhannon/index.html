Introduction to Buckhannon

Nestled in the heart of Mountains and Lakes Country, Buckhannon is a charming city located in West Virginia’s South. Surrounded by picturesque mountains and sprawling forests, Buckhannon is a nature lover’s paradise. Its small-town charm, rich history, and cultural heritage make it a popular destination for travelers. Whether you are a nature enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, Buckhannon has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Buckhannon

Here are some of the must-visit places in Buckhannon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Upshur County Historical Society - A treasure trove of historical artifacts and exhibits that provide a glimpse into Upshur County’s rich history.
  • West Virginia Wildlife Center - A wildlife sanctuary that is home to various species of animals native to West Virginia.
  • Theater West Virginia - A theatrical organization that produces plays and musicals throughout the year in Buckhannon and other cities in West Virginia.

Natural Attractions of Buckhannon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buckhannon River - A scenic river that is perfect for kayaking, fishing, and swimming.
  • Audra State Park - A beautiful park that offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and camping facilities.
  • Tomlinson Run State Park - A sprawling park that features hiking trails, fishing ponds, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buckhannon

Buckhannon is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • West Virginia Route 20 Trail - A 23-mile trail that follows the route of the old West Virginia Southern Railroad.
  • Buckhannon River Trail - A 2.5-mile trail that runs along the Buckhannon River and offers scenic views.
  • French Creek Rail Trail - A 14.5-mile trail that follows the route of the old West Virginia Northern Railroad.

Best Things to Do in Buckhannon: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do and tours to take in Buckhannon:

  • Buckhannon Ghost Tour - A spooky tour of Buckhannon’s most haunted spots.
  • Mountain Trail Rides - A horseback riding tour that takes you through the breathtaking mountain trails of West Virginia.
  • Buckhannon Antique Mall - A rustic mall that has a collection of antiques, collectibles, and locally-made crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Buckhannon

Day 1: Visit Upshur County Historical Society and West Virginia Wildlife Center

Day 2: Hike Audra State Park and Buckhannon River Trail

Day 3: Take the Buckhannon Ghost Tour

Day 4: Explore the French Creek Rail Trail and Tomlinson Run State Park

Day 5: Visit Theater West Virginia and Mountain Trail Rides

Day 6: Have a picnic at Buckhannon River and browse Buckhannon Antique Mall

Day 7: Relax and unwind at your accommodation before heading out to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Buckhannon

Buckhannon offers a diverse culinary scene that revolves around local ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls - A West Virginian specialty that consists of a bread roll with pepperoni baked inside
  • Tudor’s Biscuit World - A breakfast joint that serves delicious biscuits with gravy, sausage, and eggs.
  • Smoke on the Water BBQ - A local restaurant that serves mouthwatering BBQ dishes.

Local Beverages

  • West Virginia Wine - West Virginia’s wine industry is growing and thriving, thanks to its unique terroir and varietals.
  • Mountain State Brewing Co. - A local brewery that produces craft beer using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Buckhannon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodation options in Buckhannon:

  • Bicentennial Inn - A historic hotel that offers comfortable and spacious rooms and suites.
  • Colonial Hotel - A boutique hotel that has a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.
  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham Buckhannon - A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Buckhannon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Buckhannon include:

  • Handmade Crafts - There are several craft stores in Buckhannon that sell locally made crafts and artisanal products.
  • Maple Syrup - West Virginia has a thriving maple syrup industry, and Buckhannon is no exception. You can find several stores that sell locally sourced maple syrup.
  • Coal Art - Buckhannon is located in the heart of Coal Country, and you can find several stores that sell handcrafted coal art items such as figurines and ornaments.

Local Culture and Customs of Buckhannon

Buckhannon has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditions, festivals, and events. Some of the notable cultural events that take place throughout the year include:

  • Strawberry Festival - A celebration of West Virginia’s strawberry harvest that takes place every June.
  • West Virginia Italian Heritage Festival - A festival that celebrates West Virginia’s Italian heritage and culture with food, music, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Buckhannon

The best time to visit Buckhannon is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is vibrant. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buckhannon

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Buckhannon by hiking, biking or kayaking.
  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the pepperoni rolls and locally-sourced maple syrup.
  • Do attend one of the many festivals and events that take place throughout the year in Buckhannon.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or customs.
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buckhannon

Buckhannon is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take appropriate precautions while traveling, such as avoiding dark and isolated areas at night.

Health in Buckhannon

Buckhannon has a good healthcare system with several hospitals and clinics. It is always advisable to carry basic medication and consult a doctor if you fall sick while traveling.

Getting to Buckhannon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Buckhannon is easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Here are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Buckhannon

  • North Central West Virginia Airport (CKB) - Located 25 miles from Buckhannon, it is the closest airport
  • Pittsburgh International Airport (PIT) - Located 120 miles from Buckhannon

Bus Stations Near Buckhannon

  • Buckhannon Bus Station - Located in the heart of the city, it is serviced by several local and intercity bus operators.

Other Options

  • Cars - Buckhannon is easily accessible by car, and there are several car rental services available.
  • Trains - Amtrak operates several routes that pass through West Virginia, and the closest station to Buckhannon is located in Clarksburg.

How to Get Around in Buckhannon

Buckhannon is a small city and is easily navigable on foot. However, if you need to travel further, there are several transportation options available, including taxis and local buses.

Where to Go Next from Buckhannon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Buckhannon is a hidden gem in West Virginia that is waiting to be discovered. With its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture, Buckhannon is the perfect destination for travelers who want to experience the best of what West Virginia has to offer. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Buckhannon has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in Buckhannon.
