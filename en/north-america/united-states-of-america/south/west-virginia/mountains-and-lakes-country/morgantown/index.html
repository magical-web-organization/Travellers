Morgantown is a charming city nestled in the Mountains and Lakes Country of West Virginia, South, in the United States of America, North America. This town is a perfect destination for nature lovers looking for outdoor adventures and scenic views along with cultural experiences. In this travel guide, we will introduce you to the list of places to visit, best things to do, local culture, and safety tips that make Morgantown an ideal location for your next vacation.

Introduction to Morgantown

Morgantown, founded in 1772, is a city encapsulated by the stunning Appalachian Mountains. It is the home of the renowned West Virginia University and the birthplace of the pepperoni roll. The town is a fusion of a college town and a historic industrial city that makes it unique and diverse. Morgantown is a perfect destination for adventure, culture, and food enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Morgantown

Morgantown has many historical landmarks, green spaces, and cultural attractions that make it a fantastic place for sightseeing. Below is a list of places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Virginia University Coliseum: Home to sporting events and musical performances.
  • West Virginia Botanic Garden: A garden with different species of plants from around the world.
  • Core Arboretum: A green space filled with wildflowers and walking trails.
  • Morgantown History Museum: Showcases the city’s rich history, from prehistoric Native Americans to present-day.

Natural Attractions of Morgantown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coopers Rock State Forest: Hiking trails, rock climbing, and stunning views of the Cheat River Gorge.
  • Cheat Lake: A haven for fishing and water sports, including swimming, kayaking, and boating.
  • Mountaineer Field at Milan Puskar Stadium: Home of the West Virginia Mountaineers football team, with a great view of the city from the upper level.
  • Deckers Creek Trail: A 19-mile rail-trail that runs along the scenic Deckers Creek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morgantown

Morgantown has many outdoor activities to offer, and hiking and biking are some of the most popular ones. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking:

  • Cheat River Trail: A 10.6-mile trail that provides an excellent biking experience, along the Cheat River.
  • White Park Trail System: A multi-use hiking and biking trail system with many loop options.
  • Monongahela River Trail: A 20+ mile trail that runs from Morgantown to Prickett’s Fort State Park.

Best Things to Do in Morgantown: Activities and Tours

Morgantown has many exciting things to do, and below are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Whitewater Rafting in Cheat River: Experience the rush of the rapids while rafting through the beautiful Cheat River.
  • WVU Mountaineer Field Tour: A guided tour of the football stadium that gives you an opportunity to walk on the field, visit the locker rooms, and understand the stadium’s history.
  • Tuscan Sun Spa and Salon: Get pampered and rejuvenated with different spa treatments ranging from facials to different types of massage.
  • Farmers Market and Street Fair Tour: Explore different farmers’ markets and street fairs with a guided tour led by a local.

Food and Drink Scene of Morgantown

Morgantown is a heaven for foodies with a wide range of dishes and drinks to indulge in. Below is a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Roll: A local favorite, this pastry is filled with pepperoni and cheese, baked to perfection.
  • Country Club Bakery’s Fruit Pies: A pie made with fresh and local fruits, and the crust that is perfectly crispy.
  • Creole Soul Cafe’s Jambalaya: A Louisiana-style dish with various meats and seafood mixed with a rice pilaf.

Local Beverages

  • Mountain State Brewing Company’s Coal Miner’s Daughter Stout: A dark and creamy stout that is made using locally roasted coffee beans.
  • Black Bear Burritos’ Margaritas: Made with fresh and local ingredients, this margarita is a perfect way to cool down on a hot day.
  • Tin 202’s Moscow Mule: A drink made with spicy ginger beer, vodka, and fresh lime juice.

Where to Stay in Morgantown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Morgantown offers a wide range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Morgantown:

  • Euro-Suites Hotel: An all-suite luxury hotel located near the West Virginia University campus.
  • The Scholar Hotel Morgantown: A recently renovated hotel located in the heart of the downtown area.
  • Hostel Chestnut: A hostel located in the downtown area with different types of accommodation, including private rooms and shared dorms.

What to Buy in Morgantown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Morgantown has a variety of souvenirs and local products that you can take home with you. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy:

  • Woodburned Signs: Handcrafted signs that feature scenes from the area and quotes from famous West Virginians.
  • Blenko Glass: A family-owned and operated glass company that produces handmade glassware.
  • Tamarack: The Best of West Virginia: A unique store that offers different types of products handmade by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Morgantown

West Virginia’s local culture is rich in traditions, including bluegrass music, train whistles, and coal mining. In Morgantown, the local culture is a fusion of college town and industrial heritage. The town is famous for its hospitality, and locals are always ready to help visitors. Morgantown has a reputation for its passion for sports, music, and good food.

Best Time to Visit Morgantown

Morgantown has its charm in every season, but the best time to visit is from mid-August to mid-October when the weather is mild, and the fall colors are at their peak. Spring is also a beautiful time to visit with blooming flowers and greenery everywhere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morgantown

Do:

  • Do wear comfortable shoes when exploring the city’s dark and narrow streets.
  • Do try out the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Do take time to talk to locals and understand their culture and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t carry valuables while exploring the city.
  • Don’t litter in parks and green spaces.
  • Don’t forget to tip waitstaff at restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morgantown

Morgantown is a safe city, but it is always essential to take precautions while traveling. Here are some tips to make your stay in Morgantown safe and enjoyable:

  • Always be aware of your surroundings.
  • Do not leave valuables in plain sight in your car.
  • Lock your doors and windows when leaving your hotel room or car.

Health in Morgantown

Morgantown has good quality drinking water, clean air, and good healthcare facilities. If you are traveling to West Virginia from an area with high COVID-19 numbers, it is recommended to bring a negative COVID-19 test result. Vaccination is also a good idea.

Getting to Morgantown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Morgantown can be accessed by air, bus, and car.

Airports Near Morgantown

  • Morgantown Municipal Airport is the closest airport, which is approximately 5 miles from downtown Morgantown.
  • Pittsburgh International Airport is approximately 80 miles north of Morgantown.

Bus Stations Near Morgantown

  • Greyhound Bus Station is in downtown Morgantown.

Other Options

  • Amtrak train services connect to the city of Pittsburgh, which is 75 miles north of Morgantown.

How to Get Around in Morgantown

Morgantown has a well-connected transit system, including buses and taxis. Below are some popular transportation options:

  • Mountain Line Transit Authority: Provides public transportation throughout the city.
  • Uber and Lyft: Available in Morgantown.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Morgantown.

Where to Go Next from Morgantown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Morgantown is located in an excellent position for making excursions to other nearby attractions. Some of the popular destinations are:

  • West Virginia State Capitol: A beautiful building in Charleston that showcases the state’s history and architecture.
  • Harpers Ferry National Historical Park: A historic town situated at the intersection of the Shenandoah and Potomac rivers.
  • New River Gorge National Park and Preserve: A stunning national park filled with outdoor activities and scenic views.

Conclusion

Morgantown is the perfect destination for those who love adventure, culture, and good food. The city is rich in natural beauty and history, making it a gem offering endless options to visitors. We hope this travel guide was helpful in planning your next Morgantown excursion. Enjoy your stay!
