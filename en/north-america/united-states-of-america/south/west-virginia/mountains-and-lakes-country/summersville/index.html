Introduction to Summersville

Summersville, located in the Mountains and Lakes Country region of West Virginia, is a picturesque city known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. From pristine lakes to rugged mountains, Summersville is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The city also has a rich history and a vibrant culture, making it an all-round destination for travelers. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Summersville.

List of Places to Visit in Summersville

Summersville has no shortage of attractions and places to visit. From museums to scenic parks, here are the top places to add to your Summersville itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carnifex Ferry Battlefield State Park: This historical park commemorates the Civil War battle that took place here, where Union soldiers thwarted Confederate attempts to take control of the area. Visitors can explore the museum, take a guided tour, or hike the trails.

  • Nicholas County Veterans Memorial Park: This park is dedicated to honoring the brave men and women who have served in the Armed Forces. The centerpiece of the park is a striking monument featuring the names of Nicholas County veterans, along with a museum displaying military memorabilia.

  • Summersville Lake Lighthouse: This unique attraction is a replica of a 19th-century lighthouse and offers stunning views of the Summersville Lake. Visitors can take guided tours, climb to the top for even better views, and even stay overnight in the lighthouse keeper’s quarters.

Natural Attractions of Summersville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Summersville Lake: With over 60 miles of shoreline, the Summersville Lake is a favorite spot for boating, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can also hike around the lake, enjoy picnics, and watch stunning sunsets.

  • New River Gorge National Park and Preserve: Located just a short drive from Summersville, this national park and preserve offers a variety of outdoor adventures, including whitewater rafting, rock climbing, and hiking. Visitors can also explore the New River Gorge Bridge, one of the longest steel-arch bridges in the world.

  • Gauley River National Recreation Area: This area is a paradise for adrenaline junkies, with class V rapids for rafting and kayaking. Visitors can also enjoy hiking trails, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Summersville

Summersville is a haven for hikers and bikers, with miles of trails offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and the Summersville Lake. Here are some of the best routes to explore.

  • Long Point Trail: This 3.5-mile trail is perfect for a beginner hike, with breathtaking views of the lake and a scenic overlook at the end.

  • Battleship Rock Trail: This 2.7-mile trail takes you to a towering rock formation that resembles a battleship. Along the way, you’ll enjoy beautiful views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

  • Summersville Lake Retreat Trail: This easy 1.7-mile trail is perfect for families and offers a leisurely walk through dense forest, along with beautiful views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in Summersville: Activities and Tours

Summersville offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors, including water sports, cultural tours, and outdoor adventures. Here are the top things to do in Summersville.

  • Whitewater Rafting/Kayaking: The New River and the Gauley River offer some of the best whitewater rafting and kayaking in the country. Visitors can take guided tours or rent equipment from local outfitters.

  • Summersville Lake Boat Tours: Take a relaxing boat tour of the Summersville Lake and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the beautiful mountains of Summersville on horseback, with guided tours available for all skill levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Summersville

To truly experience the best of Summersville, we recommend a week-long itinerary that takes you through all the top attractions and activities. Here’s a sample itinerary to get you started.

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Carnifex Ferry Battlefield State Park and Nicholas County Veterans Memorial Park
Day 2 Enjoy water sports at the Summersville Lake
Day 3 Take a guided tour of the New River Gorge National Park and Preserve

Food and Drink Scene of Summersville

Summersville offers a diverse culinary scene, with everything from traditional Southern dishes to farm-to-table cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: These rolled-up sandwiches filled with spicy pepperoni and cheese are a West Virginia classic.

  • Ramp Recipes: Ramps, also known as wild leeks, are a spring delicacy in West Virginia. Try them pickled, sautéed, or in soups.

  • Apple Dumplings: These sweet treats are made with fresh apples, cinnamon, and a flaky pastry crust.

Local Beverages

  • Breweries: Check out local breweries like Mountaineer Brewing Company and Weathered Ground Brewery for craft beers and ciders.

  • Wine: Summersville is close to several wineries, including Kirkwood Winery and Watts Roost Vineyard.

Where to Stay in Summersville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Summersville offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top options to consider.

  • Resort at Glade Springs: This luxurious resort offers everything from golf courses to a spa to family-friendly activities.

  • Summersville Lake Retreat & Lighthouse: This unique accommodation option allows you to stay in the keeper’s quarters of the Summersville Lake Lighthouse.

  • Sleep Inn Summersville: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities like a fitness center and a continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Summersville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Summersville offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or mementos of your trip. Here are some top options to consider.

  • Handmade Crafts: Shop for handmade crafts like quilts, wood carvings, and pottery at local shops and markets.

  • Maple Syrup: West Virginia is famous for its maple syrup, which you can buy at local farms and markets.

  • Moonshine: Take home a bottle of locally distilled moonshine, a West Virginia specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Summersville

Summersville has a rich culture that’s deeply rooted in its history and natural surroundings. Locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the laid-back and relaxed pace of life here. Respect for nature is an important part of the local culture, and visitors are expected to follow Leave No Trace principles when exploring the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Summersville

Summersville is a year-round destination, with something to offer in every season. The best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Summer is peak season, with the warmest weather and the most activities and events. Spring and fall offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds, making them good times to enjoy outdoor adventures. Winter is ideal for skiing and other snowy activities, with several nearby ski resorts offering great options for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Summersville

Here are some important things to keep in mind when visiting Summersville.

Do:

  • Respect nature and follow Leave No Trace principles.

  • Engage with locals and learn about the local culture and history.

  • Be prepared for outdoor adventures with the right gear and equipment.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural or cultural sites.

  • Trespass on private property.

  • Ignore safety warnings or advice from locals and guides.

Safety and Health

Summersville is a safe and healthy destination for travelers. Here are some important things to know.

Safety in Summersville

  • Summersville has a low crime rate, but visitors should take basic precautions like locking their cars and securing their valuables.

  • Follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor adventures like whitewater rafting and hiking, and always use proper equipment and gear.

Health in Summersville

  • Visitors should be prepared for hot and humid weather in the summer and cold temperatures in the winter.

  • As with any outdoor destination, visitors should be aware of ticks and take precautions like wearing long pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Summersville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Summersville is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus. Here are some transportation options to consider.

Airports Near Summersville

  • Yeager Airport in Charleston, WV (approximately 1.5 hours’ drive)

  • Greenbrier Valley Airport in Lewisburg, WV (approximately 1.5 hours’ drive)

Bus Stations Near Summersville

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Charleston, WV (approximately 1.5 hours’ drive)

  • Megabus Station in Pittsburgh, PA (approximately 3 hours’ drive)

Other Options

  • Amtrak rail service to Charlottesville, VA or Washington, DC (approximately 3 hours’ drive)

  • Car rental services available at nearby airports and bus stations.

How to Get Around in Summersville

Summersville is a small city that’s easy to navigate on foot or by car. Bike rentals are also available at several local outfitters.

Where to Go Next from Summersville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Summersville is located in a beautifully scenic part of West Virginia, making it an ideal home base for exploring the nearby area. Here are some top destinations and excursions to consider.

  • Snowshoe Mountain Resort: This popular ski resort is located about 2 hours’ drive from Summersville and offers a range of winter activities like skiing, snowboarding, and snowmobiling.

  • Canaan Valley Resort State Park: This park is located about 2.5 hours’ drive from Summersville and offers year-round outdoor adventures like skiing, hiking, and mountain biking.

  • Beckley Exhibition Coal Mine & Youth Museum: This educational museum and coal mine tour is located about 45 minutes’ drive from Summersville and offers a fascinating glimpse into the history of coal mining in the area.

Conclusion

Summersville is a hidden gem in the Mountains and Lakes Country region of West Virginia, offering something for everyone. With its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich history and culture, Summersville is a top destination for travelers who want to explore the best of what West Virginia has to offer. Plan your trip today, and discover the magic of Summersville!
