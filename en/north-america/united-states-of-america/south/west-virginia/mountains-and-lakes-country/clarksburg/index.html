Introduction to Clarksburg

Clarksburg is a charming city located in the Mountains and Lakes Country in West Virginia, in the southern part of the United States of America. It is known for its rich history, picturesque scenery, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Clarksburg has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Clarksburg and make the most of your time here.

List of Places to Visit in Clarksburg

Clarksburg has a variety of attractions, from museums and historical places to scenic views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit places in Clarksburg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clarksburg History Museum
  • Waldomore Mansion
  • Robinson Grand Performing Arts Center
  • Louis A. Johnson VA Medical Center

Natural Attractions of Clarksburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clarksburg

Clarksburg is known for its scenic hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and lakes. Some of the popular hiking/biking trails in Clarksburg are:

  • North Bend Rail Trail
  • Coastal Virginia Trail
  • Tygart Valley River Trail

Best Things to Do in Clarksburg: Activities and Tours

Aside from the hiking/biking trails, Clarksburg has many activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Clarksburg:

  • Take a scenic drive on Route 50
  • Visit a local winery or brewery
  • Attend a performance at the Robinson Grand Performing Arts Center
  • Participate in the Annual Italian Heritage Festival

Week-Long Itinerary for Clarksburg

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Clarksburg, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Clarksburg and check-in at your accommodation. Visit the Clarksburg History Museum and Waldomore Mansion.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive on Route 50 and visit the Nathan Goff Jr. Park.

Day 3: Hike/bike on the North Bend Rail Trail and visit the Veterans Memorial Park.

Day 4: Participate in the Annual Italian Heritage Festival and taste some local food.

Day 5: Visit the Robinson Grand Performing Arts Center and Louis A. Johnson VA Medical Center.

Day 6: Visit a local winery or brewery and enjoy some local beverages.

Day 7: Depart from Clarksburg.

Local Culture and Customs of Clarksburg

The people of Clarksburg are friendly and welcoming. They take pride in their heritage and traditions, which are influenced by their Italian and Irish ancestry. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs, such as the importance of family and community.

Best Time to Visit Clarksburg

The best time to visit Clarksburg is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are many outdoor activities. The Italian Heritage Festival, held in August, is also a must-see event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clarksburg

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the pepperoni rolls and Italian sausage.
  • Explore the natural attractions, such as the North Bend Rail Trail and Veterans Memorial Park.
  • Learn about the local history and culture by visiting museums and historical places.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural sites.
  • Don’t park in restricted areas.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clarksburg

Clarksburg is generally a safe city, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in secluded areas. It’s also important to lock your car doors and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Clarksburg

Clarksburg has several medical facilities, including the Louis A. Johnson VA Medical Center, where visitors can receive medical care if needed.

How to Get Around in Clarksburg

Clarksburg is a small city, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer not to walk, there are several transportation options available, such as taxis and rental cars.
