Introduction to Shinnston

Nestled in the heart of Mountains and Lakes Country in West Virginia, Shinnston is a small town with a rich history and natural beauty. This charming town with a population of just over 2000 offers visitors a chance to unwind, explore, and immerse in the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Shinnston

  • Shinnston City Park
  • Greaser’s Scooter Museum
  • Covered Bridge

  • Fido’s Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bice-Ferguson Museum

The Bice-Ferguson Museum highlights the history of Shinnston and the surrounding area. The museum displays artifacts from the prehistoric era, the early settlement of the town, the civil war, and the industrial revolution. There is something for everyone in this museum, from history buffs to kids who like to learn new things.

Greaser’s Scooter Museum

Greaser’s Scooter Museum is a quirky attraction in Shinnston highlighting the history of scooters and motorcycles. The museum features vintage scooters, motorcycles, and memorabilia from the 1940s to the present day.

Covered Bridge

The Covered Bridge is a picturesque attraction located at the end of Pike Street and has been around since 1892. The bridge was destroyed during a flood in 1985 and rebuilt in 1991, and it now stands as a testament to the history and resilience of the town.

Natural Attractions of Shinnston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shinnston City Park

Shinnston City Park is a beautiful spot for a picnic, a game of frisbee or a stroll. The park features a playground, picnic areas, basketball courts, a walking trail, and softball fields.

John W. Floyd Memorial Field

The John W. Floyd Memorial Field is located in North Central West Virginia and provides visitors with an ideal location for outdoor recreation. The field is mostly used for baseball and softball games and offers fantastic views of the city and surrounding hills.

West Fork River Trail

The West Fork River Trail is a beautiful hiking and biking trail that passes through rural West Virginia. The trail winds past stunning rock formations and scenic vistas and is a great place to observe wildlife and nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shinnston

West Fork River Trail

The West Fork River Trail is a 17.5-mile trail that follows the course of the West Fork River from its headwaters to its confluence with the Tygart Valley River. The trail offers a mix of easy and challenging terrain and passes through tunnels, bridges, and historic sites.

Fido’s Park

Fido’s Park is the perfect spot for pet lovers; it is a fenced dog park in Shinnston that provides visitors the chance to enjoy the outdoors and their pet’s company simultaneously.

Best Things to Do in Shinnston: Activities and Tours

Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast or a family looking for some fun, Shinnston offers something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in this charming town

  • Visit the Bice-Ferguson Museum
  • Take a walk or bike ride on the West Fork River Trail
  • Check out the Covered Bridge
  • Enjoy a picnic at Shinnston City Park
  • Go on a tour of Greaser’s Scooter Museum

Week-Long Itinerary for Shinnston

Day 1-2: Visit the historical attractions: Bice-Ferguson Museum, Covered Bridge.

Day 3: Enjoy the outdoors at Shinnston City Park.

Day 4-6: Take a hike or bike ride on the West Fork River Trail

Day 7: Visit Greaser’s Scooter Museum and go on one last ride through Shinnston’s beautiful countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Shinnston

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to eating in Shinnston, there are some must-try local dishes that visitors should try.

  • Pepperoni Roll - A savory bread roll filled with pepperoni and cheese, the Pepperoni Roll is a West Virginia specialty that was invented by coal miners in the early 1900s.

Local Beverages

While in Shinnston, visitors should try the locally-brewed beer, which is made from locally-sourced grains.

Where to Stay in Shinnston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shinnston has a few options for visitors looking for accommodation, including a Bed & Breakfast inn and a few chain hotels situated near the highway. There are limited options, hence visitors may want to consider accommodation options in nearby cities such as Bridgeport and Clarksburg.

What to Buy in Shinnston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shinnston does not offer many shopping options, but the Covered Bridge Artisans has a great collection of souvenirs, antiques and art crafted by local artists and craftsman.

Local Culture and Customs of Shinnston

Shinnston has a close-knit community of friendly and welcoming people, and visitors can experience the town’s culture and customs through the museums, events and the annual Shinnston Frontier Days Celebration.

Best Time to Visit Shinnston

Spring and summer months from April to October are the best times to visit Shinnston. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views, outdoor adventures, and events such as the Frontier Days Celebration, which happens in September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shinnston

Do:

  • Enjoy the scenic beauty and outdoor activities of the town.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or on hiking trails.
  • Disrespect the historical sites and artifacts in the museums.
  • Speak rudely or make derogatory comments about the local residents or the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shinnston

Shinnston is a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to face any significant safety issues. However, it is advisable to keep an eye on personal belongings.

Health in Shinnston

Shinnston does not have any major health issues that visitors should be concerned with. However, it is always good to carry a small first-aid kit that can handle minor emergencies.

Getting to Shinnston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shinnston

The closest airport to Shinnston is North Central West Virginia Airport, located about 15 miles from the city.

Bus Stations Near Shinnston

The nearest bus station to Shinnston is the Greyhound station in Clarksburg, located 6 miles from the city.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Shinnston, as the city is just off the Interstate-79.

How to Get Around in Shinnston

The best way to get around Shinnston is by car or bike. Visitors can also use charter services or shuttle buses to get around the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Shinnston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations to explore after Shinnston are:

  • Valley Falls State Park, 12.1 miles from Shinnston.
  • Tygart Lake State Park, 20 miles from Shinnston.
  • Salem International University, 6.3 miles from Shinnston.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a small-town charm and outdoor adventure, Shinnston is an excellent destination to consider. The town’s rich history and natural beauty offer visitors a chance to relax, learn, and unwind, making it a perfect place to visit in West Virginia.
