Introduction to Clay

Nestled in the heart of Mountains and Lakes Country, Clay is a quaint town located in West Virginia, South of the United States. With its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals, Clay is the perfect destination for travelers who are looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through all the must-see places and things to do in Clay, so you can plan your trip with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Clay

Whether you enjoy learning about history, exploring the outdoors, or trying new foods, Clay has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clay County Museum: This charming museum is home to a fascinating collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the rich history and culture of Clay County. From antique farm equipment to historic photographs, there’s plenty to see and learn at this local favorite.
  • Clay County Courthouse: Built in 1901, this beautiful courthouse is a stunning example of Romanesque architecture. Make sure to snap a photo of the iconic clock tower while you’re there.
  • Clay County Veterans Memorial: Pay your respects to the brave men and women who have served in the armed forces at this touching memorial, which features a statue of a soldier and a list of names of local veterans.

Natural Attractions of Clay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kanawha Forest: If you’re looking for a peaceful retreat in the great outdoors, look no further than Kanawha Forest. With over 9,000 acres of pristine wilderness, this park offers ample opportunities for hiking, fishing, and camping. Be sure to check out the stunning views of the Kanawha River from the park’s overlooks.
  • Diana Wildlife Management Area: Wildlife enthusiasts won’t want to miss a visit to Diana Wildlife Management Area, which is home to an impressive variety of flora and fauna. Keep your eyes peeled for sightings of white-tailed deer, black bear, and wild turkeys as you explore the park’s hiking trails.
  • Clay County Park and Pool: Cool off on a hot summer day at Clay County Park and Pool, a family-friendly destination that features a large swimming pool, picnic areas, and playgrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clay

For those who love to hike or bike, Clay is a paradise. Whether you’re an experienced trail blazer or a beginner, you’ll find plenty of trails to suit your needs. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Kanawha Trace: This 31-mile trail features a mix of challenging climbs and easy terrain, making it a great option for intermediate to advanced hikers and bikers. As you wind your way through the forest, you’ll be treated to stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Poca River Trail: If you’re looking for a more leisurely stroll, check out the Poca River Trail, a 1.7-mile loop that meanders through the park-like setting of Clay County Park. With its flat terrain and shaded paths, this trail is ideal for families with young children or anyone who wants to take a leisurely walk in nature.

Best Things to Do in Clay: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the outdoors, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Clay. Here are some top picks:

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the rolling hills and forests of Clay County on horseback with a guided tour from Equine Adventures. With experienced guides and well-trained horses, this is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the area.
  • Kayaking: Take to the water and explore the scenic beauty of the Elk River with a kayaking tour from Elk River Touring Center. With options for both beginners and experienced kayakers, this is a fun and unique way to see the area.
  • Farm Tours: Get a taste of rural life in West Virginia with a farm tour from local outfit Mountain Harvest Farm. Learn about the challenges and joys of farming while enjoying the fresh air and scenic views of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Clay

No trip to Clay is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: These delicious snacks are a West Virginia specialty, featuring soft bread stuffed with spicy pepperoni. Try them at a local bakery like Shaffer’s or Gino’s.
  • Burgers at Pizza Hut: It might sound strange to order a burger at a pizza place, but trust us–the burgers at Clay’s Pizza Hut are legendary. Thick, juicy, and topped with all the classic fixings, this is a meal you won’t soon forget.
  • Cherry Delight Cake: For a sweet treat, try the Cherry Delight Cake at Tudor’s Biscuit World. With its layers of fluffy cake, sweet cherries, and creamy icing, this dessert is a fan favorite.

Local Beverages

  • West Virginia Beer: Fans of craft beer will find plenty to love in West Virginia, which has a thriving microbrewery scene. Be sure to try a local brew or two while you’re in town.
  • Apple Cider: Take a tour of a local cider mill like Bear Wallow Farms and sample some of the delicious apple cider and other seasonal treats.

Where to Stay in Clay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For visitors looking for accommodations in Clay, there are several options to choose from. Here are some top picks:

  • Sleep Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and convenient access to Clay’s downtown area.
  • Oak Tree Inn: This charming bed and breakfast boasts cozy rooms, a beautiful garden, and a homey atmosphere.
  • Elk River Inn and Cabins: This rustic retreat offers cozy cabins, a hot tub, and stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.

What to Buy in Clay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some top picks:

  • Handmade Pottery: Clay is known for its beautiful handmade pottery, which makes for a great souvenir or gift. Check out local shops like Country Treasures and Bear Run Inn for some unique finds.
  • Local Honey: Stock up on delicious, locally sourced honey at places like Casey’s Country Store and Mountain Harvest Farm.
  • Antiques and Collectibles: For those who love to hunt for treasures, Clay is home to several antique shops and flea markets. Check out places like Hummingbird Antiques and Casey’s Country Store for some unique finds.

Local Culture and Customs of Clay

Clay is home to a friendly and welcoming community that takes pride in its rural roots and laid-back lifestyle. Visitors should be prepared to slow down and enjoy the simple pleasures of life, from sipping on sweet tea on a front porch to taking a leisurely stroll through the countryside.

Best Time to Visit Clay

The best time to visit Clay is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful. Summer is also a popular season for visitors, thanks to the warm weather and abundance of outdoor activities.

Safety in Clay

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings
  • Use caution when driving on rural roads
  • Stay on designated trails when hiking or exploring the outdoors

Health in Clay

  • Make sure to pack sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities
  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot weather
  • Check for ticks and other insects when spending time outdoors

Getting to Clay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clay is located in a rural area of West Virginia, but getting there is still relatively easy. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Clay

The nearest major airport is Yeager Airport, located in Charleston, West Virginia, about 50 miles from Clay. From there, visitors can rent a car or arrange for a shuttle service to take them to Clay.

Bus Stations Near Clay

Greyhound bus service is available in nearby Summersville, West Virginia, about 30 miles from Clay. From there, visitors can rent a car or arrange for a shuttle service to take them to Clay.

Other Options

For visitors who prefer to drive, Clay is easily accessible via route 4 or route 16, which both run through the town.

How to Get Around in Clay

While driving is the easiest way to get around in Clay, visitors who prefer not to rent a car can also use taxi services or bike rentals to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Clay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors who want to continue their West Virginia adventure after leaving Clay, there are several nearby destinations to consider. Here are some top picks:

  • Summersville Lake: Located about 30 miles from Clay, Summersville Lake is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and other water activities.
  • New River Gorge Bridge: Located about 60 miles from Clay, the New River Gorge Bridge is a stunning example of engineering and offers incredible views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Pocahontas County: Located about 70 miles from Clay, Pocahontas County boasts a picturesque small town vibe and plenty of wilderness to explore.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat in nature or an immersive cultural experience, Clay has something to offer. With its friendly locals, unique cuisine, and abundance of natural beauty, Clay is the perfect destination for travelers who want to experience the best that West Virginia has to offer.
