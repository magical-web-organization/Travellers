Introduction to Fairmont

Fairmont is nestled in the Mountains and Lakes Country of West Virginia, in the southern region of the United States. This charming city offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, outdoor activities, local culture, and history. Whether you want to hike in the mountains, admire the scenic views, learn about the local culture, or savor the delicious food, Fairmont has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Fairmont

If you are planning a trip to Fairmont, here are the top places that you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mannington District Historical Museum: Take a step back in time and explore the history of Mannington District through artifacts, documents, and photographs.

  • Pricketts Fort State Park and Museum: Learn about the life of the early settlers in the Appalachian Mountains with reenactments, exhibits, and tours.

  • Marion County Historical Society Museum: Discover the rich history of Marion County through artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Fairmont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valley Falls State Park: Enjoy a day of hiking, fishing, and picnicking in this beautiful park, which boasts several waterfalls, scenic trails, and lush vegetation.

  • West Fork River Rail-Trail: Walk, bike, or jog this trail, which offers stunning views of the river, bridges, and wildflowers.

  • Prickett’s Fort State Park: Take a nature walk, watch wildlife, or paddle along the Monongahela River in this beautiful park, which features dense forest, rolling hills, and scenic river views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fairmont

Fairmont is an outdoor paradise, offering several hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best options:

  • Valley Falls State Park Trail: This 2.5-mile trail offers a moderate level of difficulty and offers picturesque views of four waterfalls.

  • West Fork River Rail-Trail: This 23-mile trail is perfect for biking, walking, or jogging and offers stunning views of the river, bridges, and wildflowers.

  • Heston Farm Trail: This 5-mile trail is great for hiking and offers panoramic views of the rolling hills, forests, and streams.

Best Things to Do in Fairmont: Activities and Tours

Fairmont offers several fun activities and tours to make your trip memorable. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Underground Railroad Museum Tour: Discover the history of the Underground Railroad Movement and take a guided tour of the Underground Railroad Museum in Fairmont, which features interactive exhibits and artifacts.

  • Salvage Yards and Antique Store Tour: If you love antiques and vintage collectibles, take a tour of the salvage yards and antique stores in Fairmont. These places offer unique finds and great bargains.

  • Riverboat Cruise: Enjoy a relaxing cruise along the Monongahela River and learn about the history and ecosystem of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fairmont

Planning your trip to Fairmont? Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit:

Day 1: Visit Prickett’s Fort State Park and Museum, and take a hike in the beautiful natural surroundings.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Underground Railroad Museum, and explore local antique shops and salvage yards.

Day 3: Visit Valley Falls State Park, and enjoy a day of hiking, fishing, and picnicking.

Day 4: Explore the Marion County Historical Society Museum and learn about the local history and culture.

Day 5: Take a riverboat cruise and enjoy the scenic views of Monongahela River.

Day 6: Discover the natural beauty of Heston Farm Trail and enjoy the panoramic views.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food scene of Fairmont.

Food and Drink Scene of Fairmont

Fairmont offers a diverse food scene for travelers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls: This iconic West Virginia dish is a must-try. It consists of a soft roll filled with spicy pepperoni and is a favorite snack of locals.

  • Hot Dogs: Fairmont has a rich hot dog culture, with several stands and restaurants offering unique toppings.

  • Buffalo Chicken Pizza: This pizza is a local favorite, with a crispy crust, spicy buffalo sauce, and lots of chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Mountain Moonshine: This local beverage is brewed using traditional methods and offers a taste of the Appalachian Mountains.

  • Blenheim Ginger Ale: This spicy ginger ale is a favorite of locals and offers a refreshing drink on hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Fairmont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fairmont offers several lodging options for travelers. Here are some of the best options:

  • Historic Clarion Inn: This restored historic hotel offers comfortable rooms and is close to many attractions.

  • Hampton Inn: This hotel offers modern amenities, free breakfast, and is a great choice for families.

  • Hostel on Main: This affordable hostel offers dorm and private rooms, and is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Fairmont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and gifts to bring back home? Here are some local products that you should consider:

  • Pepperoni Rolls: Bring home a taste of West Virginia with this iconic dish.

  • Craft Beer: Fairmont has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries offering unique flavors.

  • Handmade Crafts: Marion County has several local artisan shops offering handmade crafts, pottery, and folk art.

Local Culture and Customs of Fairmont

Fairmont has a rich local culture, with friendly locals and a laid-back lifestyle. Here are some customs and etiquette that you should keep in mind:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: West Virginians are proud of their heritage and are likely to be offended if their traditions and customs are not respected.

  • Be polite and friendly: West Virginians are known for their hospitality and warmth, so be sure to greet locals with a smile and a friendly hello.

Best Time to Visit Fairmont

The best time to visit Fairmont is from May to October, when the weather is pleasant, and there are several outdoor activities and events. However, if you want to avoid crowds, visit in the shoulder season, from March to April or November to December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fairmont

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Fairmont:

Do:

  • Respect nature: West Virginia has a beautiful natural landscape, so make sure to keep it clean and respect the environment.

  • Try local cuisine: West Virginia has a unique food scene, so be sure to try the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter: West Virginia has strict laws against littering, so don’t throw garbage anywhere.

  • Be rude to locals: West Virginians are known for their friendly and hospitable nature, so be respectful and polite.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy in Fairmont:

Safety in Fairmont

  • Watch out for wildlife: West Virginia has a diverse wildlife population, which includes bears, wolves, and snakes. Always be aware of your surroundings and avoid disturbing wildlife.

  • Take precautions while hiking: Wear sturdy shoes, carry enough water, and avoid hiking alone.

Health in Fairmont

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially during hot summer days.

  • Protect yourself from ticks and mosquitoes: Use insect repellent and wear protective clothing while hiking or camping.

Getting to Fairmont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fairmont is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Fairmont

  • North Central West Virginia Airport: This airport is located in Bridgeport, West Virginia, about 20 miles from Fairmont.

  • Pittsburgh International Airport: This airport is located about 80 miles from Fairmont and offers several domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Fairmont

  • Greyhound Bus Station, Clarksburg: This station is located about 20 miles from Fairmont and offers several bus routes to neighboring cities.

  • Mega Bus Station, Pittsburgh: This station is located about 80 miles from Fairmont and offers several bus routes to neighboring cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: There are several car rental options available at both airports and bus stations.

  • Taxi service: Several taxi companies offer affordable rates to Fairmont and neighboring cities.

How to Get Around in Fairmont

Fairmont is a small city, and many attractions are within walking distance. However, if you need transportation, here are some options:

  • Public transportation: Fairmont offers a public bus service that runs throughout the city.

  • Taxi service: You can hail a taxi on the street or book one through a local taxi company.

  • Bike rental: You can rent a bike or scooter from one of several rental companies in Fairmont.

Where to Go Next from Fairmont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to extend your trip and explore nearby destinations, here are some possibilities:

  • Buckhannon: Explore this charming historic town, which boasts several artisan shops, restaurants, and museums.

  • Canaan Valley Resort State Park: This beautiful park is located about 90 miles from Fairmont and offers several winter activities such as skiing and snowboarding.

  • Harpers Ferry: Visit this historic town, which played a significant role in the civil war and has several historical landmarks and museums.

Conclusion
