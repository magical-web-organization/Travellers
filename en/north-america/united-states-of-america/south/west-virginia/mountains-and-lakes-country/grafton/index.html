Introduction to Grafton

Grafton is an idyllic town nestled in the heart of Mountains and Lakes Country, located in the southern part of West Virginia, United States of America. It is a town steeped in history, with a rich cultural heritage, and breathtaking natural beauty. Grafton is the ideal getaway destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture seekers alike. Here, we present a comprehensive guide to help you make the most out of your visit to Grafton.

List of Places to Visit in Grafton

Grafton boasts numerous attractions perfect for tourists. Here’s a list of must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • B&O Railroad Depot and Museum - It’s a historical depot, now converted into a museum, offering a glimpse into Grafton’s railway history.
  • Anna Jarvis Birthplace Museum - Anna Jarvis is known for founding Mother’s Day. Her birthplace museum tells the story of her life and contributions to society.
  • Tygart Valley Homesteads Museum - The Homesteads Museum documents West Virginia’s largest New Deal project.

Natural Attractions of Grafton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valley Falls State Park - A leisurely walk in the middle of the forest, revealing a beautiful waterfall.
  • Heston Farm Winery - A beautiful winery, located in a serene valley, offering amazing views and wines.
  • Dorsey’s Knob Park - A hiking trail leading to the park’s summit, offering panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grafton

If you’re looking for hiking and biking trails in Grafton, take your pick among the following:

  • North Bend Rail Trail
  • Allegheny Highlands Trail

Best Things to Do in Grafton: Activities and Tours

  • West Virginia Botanic Garden - The garden offers a serene atmosphere and bountiful nature to visitors.
  • Grafton National Cemetery - A peaceful and serene cemetery, site of approximately 2900 graves of Union soldiers.
  • Grafton City Hospital - Offers one of the best architectural tours in the country

Week-Long Itinerary for Grafton

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Grafton:

Food and Drink Scene of Grafton

Grafton’s culinary scene is rich, featuring a mix of traditional, local fare and contemporary cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Rolls - A classic local pastry filled with peppers and pepperoni originated in West Virginia.
  • Freshwater Fish - Trout, bass, and catfish are commonly served.

Local Beverages

  • West Virginia Wine - Wineries such as Heston Farm offer amazing wine, with Heston Vidal Blanc being the most well known.
  • West Virginia Ale - Microbreweries such as Bridge Brew Works offer amazing beers, with their Long Point Lager being their most popular brew.

Where to Stay in Grafton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grafton offers a diverse range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Mountaineer Casino Hotel - A luxurious hotel in Grafton, offering numerous amenities and gambling opportunities.
  • Hampton Inn - An affordable and comfortable hotel located near the center of the town.
  • Lost River Inn - A charming and quaint guesthouse, ideal for visitors who prefer a more intimate and cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Grafton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Grafton back home with you by purchasing the following souvenirs:

  • Handmade Crafts - Grafton boasts a thriving local arts and crafts scene, with artisans selling handcrafted pottery, jewelry, and clothing.
  • Local Wines and Beers - Heston Farm, Bridge Brew Works, and other breweries/wineries offer unique and delicious beverages, available for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Grafton

Grafton’s culture is heavily influenced by its rich history, natural beauty, and welcoming, friendly inhabitants. Visitors to Grafton can expect to encounter a laid-back, relaxed atmosphere and warm hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Grafton

Grafton is a destination worth visiting at any time of the year, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. The summer months (June to August) offer ideal weather conditions for outdoor activities, while the autumn season (September to November) is perfect for those who want to experience the vibrant fall foliage. Winter months offer opportunities for skiing and viewing the breathtaking snowclad mountains, covered with snow.

Safety and Health

Grafton is a relatively safe and healthy destination to visit. Here are some general safety tips to follow:

Safety in Grafton

  • Stay in well-lit, populated areas after dark
  • Avoid carrying large sums of cash when going out
  • Keep your belongings close and within sight at all times.

Health in Grafton

  • Since there are no notable health concerns in Grafton, you are just expected to stay hydrated to prevent heat-related illnesses.
