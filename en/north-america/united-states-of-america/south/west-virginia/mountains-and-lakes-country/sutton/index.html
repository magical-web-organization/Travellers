Introduction to Sutton

Sutton is a charming town located in the Mountains and Lakes Country of West Virginia, in the southern region of the United States. It’s an ideal destination for visitors looking for a peaceful retreat in the heart of nature. The town is surrounded by mountains and lakes, making it a perfect spot for outdoor activities and scenic views. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the must-visit places, activities, and local delicacies of Sutton and provide you with all the essential information you need to plan a perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Sutton

Sutton has something for everyone, from history buffs to outdoor adventurers. Here are some of the must-visit places in Sutton that you can’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Braxton County Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of Braxton County and features artifacts, photos, and documents from the past.

  • Flatwoods Factory Outlet Stores: A popular shopping center with a varied selection of stores and restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Sutton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sutton Lake: A picturesque lake that provides hours of fun and adventure, including fishing, boating, and swimming.

  • Sutton Dam: A beautiful and historic dam that’s open for visitors who enjoy hiking the trails or picnicking in the surrounding areas.

  • Burnsville Lake Wildlife Management Area: A wildlife reserve where visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sutton

Sutton is an outdoor lover’s paradise, thanks to its stunning mountains and lakes. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Sutton that are worth exploring:

  • Sutton Lake Trail: A scenic trail that circles the lake, offering beautiful views of the water and surrounding mountains.

  • Holly River State Park: This park offers numerous trails that vary in difficulty, from easy walks to more challenging hikes. There are also biking trails for those who prefer two wheels.

  • Stonewall Jackson Lake State Park: This park offers over 18 miles of hiking trails that wind through the forest and along the lake.

Best Things to Do in Sutton: Activities and Tours

Sutton has a lot to offer its visitors, no matter their interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Sutton:

  • Take a scenic drive along Route 19: This road passes through some of West Virginia’s most stunning scenery, including mountains, forests, and picturesque towns.

  • Go fishing in Sutton Lake: The lake is stocked with various types of fish, including bass and catfish, making it an excellent spot for fishing.

  • Visit the West Virginia State Fair: Held every August in Lewisburg, about an hour’s drive from Sutton, the state fair features livestock shows, carnival rides, and local food vendors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sutton

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Sutton, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Sutton and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring the town’s historical sites, including the Braxton County Historical Society Museum.

Day 2: Go hiking at Holly River State Park and explore the nearby town of Webster Springs.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive along Route 19 to Pocahontas County and visit the Cass Scenic Railroad State Park.

Day 4: Spend the day fishing and boating on Sutton Lake.

Day 5: Explore the Burnsville Lake Wildlife Management Area and take a picnic lunch in the park.

Day 6: Visit the town of Buckhannon and its historic courthouse.

Day 7: Attend the West Virginia State Fair in Lewisburg before heading back to Sutton.

Food and Drink Scene of Sutton

Sutton’s food scene is all about local cuisine, with plenty of restaurants offering traditional Appalachian dishes. Here are some must-try local delicacies:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni rolls: This local treat is a warm, soft, bread roll, filled with spicy pepperoni.

  • Biscuits and sausage gravy: A classic Appalachian breakfast dish that’s hearty and filling.

  • Fried chicken: You can find this beloved southern dish at many local restaurants around Sutton.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: A classic southern drink, sweet tea is a refreshing option for hot summer days.

  • Local beer: Sutton has a great craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering their unique brews.

Where to Stay in Sutton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sutton has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Sutton Inn: A budget-friendly hotel located close to the town’s main attractions.

  • Quality Inn: A mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as a swimming pool and fitness center.

  • Sutton Days Inn & Suites: A top-tier hotel that offers luxurious amenities such as a spa and indoor pool.

What to Buy in Sutton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some of the best places to shop in Sutton:

  • Sutton Antique Mall: A popular shopping destination offering antiques, collectibles, and vintage items.

  • Sutton Smokehouse: A local favorite offering house-made smoked meats and cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Sutton

Sutton is a friendly and welcoming town that values its strong community spirit. Visitors are welcomed with open arms, and locals are always ready to share their stories and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sutton

The best time to visit Sutton is in the fall when the autumn foliage is at its peak. The weather is cool and comfortable, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Summer is also a popular time to visit Sutton due to the warm weather and abundance of water activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sutton

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture.

  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities and make sure to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind in public areas.

  • Trespass on private land or engage in activities that could harm the environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sutton

Sutton is a relatively safe town, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables locked up and being aware of your surroundings. When engaging in outdoor activities, make sure to bring plenty of water and stay on designated trails.

Health in Sutton

Make sure to pack any necessary medications, sunscreen, and insect repellent when visiting Sutton, as there may not be many pharmacies or convenience stores nearby.

Getting to Sutton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sutton is easily accessible by car via Route 19. The closest major airports are in Charleston and Morgantown, both approximately two hours away. Bus service is also available via Greyhound and Megabus.

Airports Near Sutton

  • Yeager Airport in Charleston, WV (approx. 113 miles from Sutton)

  • Morgantown Municipal Airport in Morgantown, WV (approx. 139 miles from Sutton)

Bus Stations Near Sutton

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Charleston, WV (approx. 118 miles from Sutton)

  • Megabus Station in Morgantown, WV (approx. 142 miles from Sutton)

Other Options

  • Private car rental is available at both Charleston and Morgantown airports.

How to Get Around in Sutton

The best way to get around Sutton is by car. There are no public transportation options in the town, and many of the best attractions are spread out throughout the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Sutton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more places to explore after Sutton, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Snowshoe Mountain: A popular ski resort located about two hours away from Sutton.

  • Seneca Rocks: A stunning rock formation located in Monongahela National Forest, about two and a half hours from Sutton.

  • New River Gorge Bridge: A scenic bridge that spans the New River Gorge and is about two hours away from Sutton.

Conclusion

Sutton is a hidden gem in West Virginia, offering visitors the perfect blend of history, nature, and local charm. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Sutton, from where to stay to what to see and do. So pack your bags, hit the road, and get ready to experience the beauty of Mountains and Lakes Country in West Virginia.
