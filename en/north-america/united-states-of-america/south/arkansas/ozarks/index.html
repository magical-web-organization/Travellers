If you’re looking for a nature-lover’s paradise, the Ozarks in Arkansas should be on your travel bucket list. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and outdoor adventures, this destination has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide to Ozarks, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip.

Introduction to Ozarks

The Ozarks is a region spanning across Arkansas, Missouri, and Oklahoma. But when people say “the Ozarks,” they typically mean the Ozark Mountains in northwestern Arkansas. The region is home to many natural wonders and culturally important attractions that make it a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Ozarks

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art: This world-renowned museum features a collection of American art from the colonial era to the present day. The museum also boasts stunning trails and outdoor sculptures.
  • Ozark Folk Center State Park: Visit this state park to explore the Ozarks cultural heritage. Watch artisans demonstrate traditional trades such as blacksmithing, pottery, and weaving.
  • Blanchard Springs Caverns: This underground wonderland features three levels of spectacular cave formations, including a waterfall and a massive chamber called “The Cathedral.”

Natural Attractions of Ozarks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buffalo National River: The first National River in the United States, the Buffalo River is a major draw for visitors looking to hike, canoe, and fish. The river also offers spectacular views of towering bluffs and cascading waterfalls.
  • Hawksbill Crag: Located within the Ozark National Forest, this iconic rock formation offers breathtaking vistas of the surrounding mountainside.
  • Roark Creek Trail: This 2-mile trail takes hikers through a shaded forest and alongside a peaceful creek, culminating in a stunning waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ozarks

Hiking and biking are popular activities in the Ozarks, thanks to its rugged terrain and stunning landscapes. Some of the best routes include:

  • Ozark Highlands Trail: This 218-mile trail takes hikers through the heart of the Ozarks, from Lake Fort Smith State Park to the Buffalo River Wilderness Area.
  • Sylamore Creek Trail: This 14-mile trail is a favorite among hikers and mountain bikers alike, featuring lush forests, sparkling streams, and beautiful waterfalls.
  • Mount Magazine State Park: This park is home to several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding Ouachita Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ozarks: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the beauty of the Ozarks from above on a hot air balloon ride.
  • Zip Lining: Get your adrenaline pumping by zipping through the treetops in Ozark National Forest.
  • UTV Adventure Tours: Explore the Ozarks from a rugged utility vehicle with a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ozarks

Day 1: Arrive in Eureka Springs and explore the charming Victorian architecture and unique shops.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking or mountain biking on the Ozark Highland Trail.

Day 3: Drive to Mountain View to visit the Ozark Folk Center State Park and watch artisans at work.

Day 4: Take a scenic drive and explore Blanchard Springs Caverns.

Day 5: Kayak or canoe down the Buffalo National River and experience its natural beauty in a unique way.

Day 6: Visit the Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art and take in the stunning outdoor art installations and trails.

Day 7: Enjoy a hot air balloon ride to see the Ozark Mountains from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Ozarks

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried catfish: a classic Ozarks dish, often served with hushpuppies and coleslaw
  • Biscuits and gravy: a hearty breakfast dish featuring fluffy biscuits smothered in creamy gravy
  • Barbecue: the Ozarks is known for its excellent barbecue, featuring slow-cooked meats and tangy sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Ozark Beer Company: This brewery in Rogers, AR, produces a range of craft beers using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Keels Creek Winery: This family-owned winery in Eureka Springs offers tastings and tours of their vineyards.

Where to Stay in Ozarks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Crescent Hotel and Spa: A historic hotel located in Eureka Springs, known for its ghost stories and stunning views of the Ozarks.
  • The Lodge at Copperhead: A rustic lodge in Blairsville, Georgia, offering easy access to hiking and outdoor activities.
  • KOA Camping: A budget-friendly option for visitors looking to camp in the heart of the Ozarks.

What to Buy in Ozarks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ozark Handmade Soaps: These artisanal soaps are handcrafted using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Ozark Forest Mushrooms: These mushrooms are foraged from the Ozarks forests and are a delicious and unique edible souvenir.
  • Ozark Mountain Honey: This local honey is harvested from bees that pollinate Ozark wildflowers and is known for its rich flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Ozarks

The Ozarks region has a rich cultural history that is still visible in its architecture, traditions, and language. Visitors will encounter hospitable locals who are proud of their heritage and culture.

Best Time to Visit Ozarks

The Ozarks is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from late spring to early fall. During this time, visitors can enjoy mild temperatures and participate in outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and kayaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ozarks

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the Ozarks National Forest and Buffalo National River
  • Do try the local cuisine, including fried catfish and biscuits and gravy
  • Do visit the Ozark Folk Center State Park to learn about the history and culture of the region

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind when visiting the natural attractions
  • Don’t underestimate the rugged terrain and bring proper hiking or outdoor gear
  • Don’t forget to tip servers at restaurants and bars, as this is customary in the United States

Safety and Health

Safety in Ozarks

Visitors to the Ozarks should be aware of the terrain and bring appropriate hiking or outdoor gear. It’s also important to stay on designated trails and avoid venturing off into unfamiliar territory. Visitors should also take precautions against ticks and other insects that may carry diseases.

Health in Ozarks

Visitors should take usual precautions to prevent contracting COVID-19. Additionally, any outdoor activities carry inherent risks. Visitors should ensure they carry adequate health insurance coverage prior to their visit.

Getting to Ozarks: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ozarks

  • Northwest Arkansas National Airport
  • Springfield-Branson National Airport
  • Little Rock National Airport

Bus Stations Near Ozarks

  • Greyhound stations in Springdale, Fayetteville, and Little Rock

Other Options

  • Rent a car from any of the airports and enjoy a scenic drive to Ozarks.

How to Get Around in Ozarks

The best way to get around in the Ozarks is by car. There are plenty of car rental options available at the airports and in the larger towns.

Where to Go Next from Ozarks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hot Springs National Park: A short drive from Ozarks, this national park features natural hot springs and bathing experiences.
  • Branson, MO: A popular tourist destination known for its live music and entertainment venues.
  • Little Rock, AR: The capital city of Arkansas, known for its rich Civil War history and museums.

Conclusion

The Ozarks is a stunning destination perfect for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers. We hope this tour guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most out of your time in this beautiful region.
