Introduction to Heber Springs

Located in the heart of the Ozarks in Arkansas, Heber Springs is a quaint town that boasts scenic beauty, history, and outdoor adventures. With its charming downtown area and proximity to Greer’s Ferry Lake, this is an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Heber Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs, there is a variety of museums and landmarks to explore in Heber Springs. The list includes:

  • Cleburne County Historical Society - Discover the history of Heber Springs and its surroundings through displays, exhibits, and artifacts.
  • Heber Springs Sports Hall of Fame - This museum showcases the achievements of the local athletes and sports teams of Heber Springs.
  • John F. Kennedy Memorial Garden - This tranquil garden is a tribute to President Kennedy and offers a quiet place to sit and reflect.

Natural Attractions of Heber Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Heber Springs is a destination for nature lovers, thanks to its abundance of outdoor activities and scenic beauty. Some of the best natural attractions to add to your itinerary include:

  • Greer’s Ferry Lake - This man-made lake is a paradise for lovers of fishing, swimming, boating, and water sports.
  • Sugar Loaf Mountain - This towering mountain offers a beautiful hike to the top that rewards you with sweeping views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Swinging Bridge - An iconic landmark of Heber Springs, this 1920s-era bridge spans over the Little Red River and is an excellent spot for taking photos or relaxing in the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Heber Springs

Heber Springs has no shortage of scenic trails and routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • First Electric Cooperative Trail - This multi-use trail runs for 8 miles through the picturesque countryside, crossing the Little Red River and offering gentle inclines.
  • Greer’s Ferry Lake Trail - Stretching over 20 miles total, this paved trail offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding forests.
  • Sylamore Trail - For a challenging hike amid stunning scenery, try this 26-mile trail. Note that the terrain is rugged, but the views are worth it.

Best Things to Do in Heber Springs: Activities and Tours

When you’re not exploring the natural beauty of Heber Springs, there are plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy. Here are some of the must-try experiences:

  • Scenic River Tours - Take a guided kayak or canoe tour, and explore the river and lake by water.
  • Waterfalls Tour - Heber Springs is home to several stunning waterfalls, and a guided tour will take you to the most picturesque spots.
  • Golfing at the Red Apple Inn - With its stunning setting and challenging course, this is a golfer’s paradise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Heber Springs

If you’re planning a week-long stay in Heber Springs, you’ll want to add these destinations to your itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Greer’s Ferry Lake
2 Go hiking or biking on Sugar Loaf Mountain

Food and Drink Scene of Heber Springs

When it comes to food and drink, Heber Springs may surprise you with its selection of unique and delicious offerings.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish - Freshly caught local catfish is fried to perfection and served hot and crispy.
  • BBQ Ribs - Tender, smoky, and slathered in sauce, ribs are a staple of Southern cuisine.
  • Fried Pie - Heber Springs is famous for its fried pies, which are essentially pockets of fruit and pastry deep-fried to crispy perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Arkansas Craft Beer - With several breweries in the region, you’re sure to find a locally-made beer to satisfy your taste buds.
  • Sweet Tea - No Southern meal is complete without a tall glass of sweet tea, a refreshing and flavorful iced tea that is sweetened with sugar.

Where to Stay in Heber Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Heber Springs offers several options for lodging, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Red Apple Inn and Country Club - With its picturesque setting on the lake and top-notch amenities, this resort offers the perfect blend of relaxation and activity.
  • Holiday Inn Express Heber Springs - Located near the downtown area and offering affordable rates and comfortable amenities, this hotel is an ideal choice for families and budget-conscious travelers.
  • Riverside Resort and Canoe Rentals - For a unique and rustic experience, consider staying in one of the cabins at this resort, and enjoy access to various outdoor activities, including canoeing and kayaking.

What to Buy in Heber Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Heber Springs, be sure to look for unique and locally-made products, such as:

  • Handmade Jewelry - Local artisans craft unique and beautiful pieces of jewelry featuring gemstones, rocks, and other natural materials found in the region.
  • Honey and Jams - Made with natural ingredients and local produce, these sweet treats make tasty and thoughtful gifts.
  • Pottery - Local potters create stunning pieces of pottery, from decorative pieces to functional items such as bowls and mugs.

Local Culture and Customs of Heber Springs

Heber Springs is a friendly and welcoming town, where visitors will find a relaxed pace of life and a strong connection to their roots. Some local customs to be aware of include:

  • Southern Hospitality - You’ll be greeted with warm smiles and a welcoming attitude wherever you go.
  • Respect for Nature - Locals place a high value on protecting the natural beauty of the region, and visitors are encouraged to do the same.

Best Time to Visit Heber Springs

The best time to visit Heber Springs is during the summer months when the weather is warm, and the lake and river are perfect for swimming and water sports. For leaf peepers, the fall months offer stunning foliage, and winter features beautiful snowscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Heber Springs

Do:

  • Explore the Outdoors - Heber Springs is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, so take advantage of all the hiking, biking, and kayaking opportunities.
  • Visit the Local Attractions - From history museums to local eateries, there is plenty to discover in Heber Springs.
  • Try the Local Cuisine - Southern cooking is famous worldwide for a reason, so be sure to indulge in the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Respect the natural beauty of Heber Springs by disposing of trash responsibly and keeping the surroundings clean.
  • Trespass on Private Property - Always seek permission before entering private land or properties.
  • Drive Recklessly - Be mindful of speed limits and driving safety, particularly on winding roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Heber Springs

Heber Springs is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always prudent to take basic safety precautions. Some tips to consider include:

  • Be aware of weather forecasts - Tornadoes and thunderstorms can occur in the region, so stay informed about local weather conditions.
  • Watch out for wildlife - While rare, black bears and mountain lions live in the Ozarks, so take appropriate measures to protect yourself when traveling in the more remote areas.

Health in Heber Springs

There are no major health concerns to worry about when traveling to Heber Springs. The nearest hospital is the Baptist Health Medical Center located in Heber Springs.

Getting to Heber Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Heber Springs

  • Little Rock National Airport - Located about 75 miles from Heber Springs, this is the nearest major airport with flights to several major U.S. cities.
  • Memphis International Airport - Located 140 miles from Heber Springs, this airport is slightly farther but offers more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Heber Springs

There are no bus stations serving Heber Springs. The nearest Greyhound station is located in Little Rock, about 80 miles away.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Heber Springs, Interstate 40 runs through the town, making it easy to reach from both the east and west. Additionally, several car rental companies serve the area.

How to Get Around in Heber Springs

The best way to get around in Heber Springs is by car. Taxis and ride-hailing services are not prevalent in the area.

Where to Go Next from Heber Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Heber Springs, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Mountain View - A charming small town known for its bluegrass music scene and stunning Ozark scenery, located about 50 miles away.
  • Hot Springs - A renowned destination for its natural hot springs and historic bathhouses, located about 70 miles away.
  • Little Rock - The capital of Arkansas, located about 75 miles away, offers a vibrant downtown area and several cultural attractions.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and variety of activities and attractions, Heber Springs is a gem of a destination that is perfect for exploring the Ozarks and relaxing in laid-back Southern style.
