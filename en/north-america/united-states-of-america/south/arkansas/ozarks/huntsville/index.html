Introduction to Huntsville

Nestled in the heart of the Ozarks in Arkansas, Huntsville is a hidden gem that offers the perfect combination of natural beauty and cultural heritage. The small town, with a population of just over 2,500, provides visitors with an opportunity to explore the breathtaking vistas of the Ozark Mountains and immerse themselves in the rich history and traditions of the region.

Whether you’re an avid hiker looking to conquer new trails, a history buff eager to learn about the area’s past, or a foodie seeking local delicacies, Huntsville has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions and activities that make Huntsville a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Huntsville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Madison County Courthouse

As one of the most iconic buildings in the region, the Madison County Courthouse is a must-visit for history and architecture buffs. Built in 1900, it features a unique mix of Romanesque and Victorian architectural styles. Visitors can tour the building and learn about its rich history, which dates back to the Civil War era.

Withrow Springs State Park

Located just a few miles outside of Huntsville, Withrow Springs State Park offers a unique opportunity to learn about the Native American and Civil War history of the area. The park features a visitor center with exhibits about the history of the Ozarks and the area’s natural resources.

Natural Attractions of Huntsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ozark National Forest

As one of the most stunning natural areas in the region, the Ozark National Forest offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. Visitors can hike or bike hundreds of miles of trails, take a leisurely scenic drive, or explore the crystal-clear streams and rivers that wind through the forest.

War Eagle Mill

War Eagle Mill is a historic gristmill that is one of the most photographed and painted landmarks in the state. Visitors can take a guided tour of the mill to learn about the history of milling, browse the gift shop, and sample some of the delicious products made on site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntsville

Ozark Highlands Trail

The Ozark Highlands Trail is a 165-mile hiking trail that runs through the Ozark National Forest. The trail offers stunning views of the forest, as well as opportunities to see wildlife and explore unique landmarks like rock formations and waterfalls.

Slaughter Pen Trail System

The Slaughter Pen Trail System is a network of trails that are perfect for mountain biking enthusiasts. With over 20 miles of bike-specific trails, riders can enjoy a wide range of terrains and difficulty levels.

Best Things to Do in Huntsville: Activities and Tours

Botanical Garden of the Ozarks

The Botanical Garden of the Ozarks is a stunning 42-acre garden that features a wide variety of plants and natural features. Visitors can take a guided tour, attend workshops and events, or simply stroll through the gardens on their own.

Arkansas Air and Military Museum

The Arkansas Air and Military Museum is home to over 40 aircraft, as well as a collection of military artifacts and memorabilia. Visitors can explore the exhibits, take a guided tour, or even book a flight in one of the historic planes.

Food and Drink Scene of Huntsville

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Catfish

As a classic southern dish, Fried Catfish is a must-try when visiting Huntsville. The dish is typically served with hushpuppies, coleslaw, and tartar sauce.

Possum Pie

Possum Pie is a unique dessert that is a staple of southern cuisine. The pie features a creamy filling made with vanilla pudding, cream cheese, and whipped cream, topped with chocolate shavings and toasted pecans.

Local Beverages

Ozark Beer Company

Ozark Beer Company is a local brewery that features a wide variety of craft beers. Visitors can sample the beers on site or take a tour of the brewery to learn about the brewing process.

Keels Creek Winery

Keels Creek Winery is a locally owned winery that offers a range of award-winning wines. Visitors can sample the wines in the tasting room, attend events and workshops, or take a tour of the vineyards.

Where to Stay in Huntsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn at Huntsville

The Inn at Huntsville is a historic hotel that offers luxury accommodations in the heart of downtown. The rooms are elegantly decorated and feature modern amenities like free Wi-Fi and cable TV.

Colton House Hotel

The Colton House Hotel is a charming bed and breakfast that offers cozy accommodations in a historic setting. The rooms are decorated with antique furnishings and offer a unique glimpse into the history of the area.

What to Buy in Huntsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handcrafted Pottery

Handcrafted pottery is a staple of the Ozarks region, and Huntsville is home to several pottery studios where visitors can purchase unique and beautiful pieces.

Local Honey

Huntsville is home to several local honey producers, offering a delicious and healthy souvenir to bring home. Visitors can purchase jars of honey in local shops and at farmers’ markets throughout the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Huntsville

Huntsville has a rich culture and tradition that is deeply rooted in the natural resources and history of the area. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local museums and landmarks to learn more about the region’s past and present culture.

Best Time to Visit Huntsville

The best time to visit Huntsville is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most stunning. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huntsville

Do:

  • Do explore the rich history and culture of the area
  • Do take advantage of the natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural resources of the area
  • Don’t disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions, especially during hiking and outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Huntsville

Overall, Huntsville is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take common sense measures to stay safe.

Health in Huntsville

Visitors to Huntsville should take precautions against ticks and other insects when spending time outdoors. It’s also important to stay hydrated and protect against sun exposure during outdoor activities.

Getting to Huntsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huntsville

The nearest airport to Huntsville is Northwest Arkansas Regional Airport (XNA), which is located approximately 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Huntsville

Greyhound offers bus service to several cities throughout the region, including Fayetteville, which is located approximately 20 miles from Huntsville.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from either the airport or bus station to reach Huntsville.

How to Get Around in Huntsville

The best way to get around Huntsville is by car. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hike some of the trails, depending on their preference.

Where to Go Next from Huntsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fayetteville

Just a short drive from Huntsville, Fayetteville is a lively college town that offers a wide range of cultural attractions, outdoor recreation opportunities, and dining and shopping options.

Eureka Springs

Located approximately 35 miles from Huntsville, Eureka Springs is a charming Victorian-era town that is known for its historic homes, eclectic shops, and picturesque setting in the Ozark Mountains.

Conclusion

Huntsville offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure that is sure to delight visitors of all ages and interests. With its stunning vistas, historic landmarks, and delicious cuisine, Huntsville is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking an authentic Arkansas experience.
