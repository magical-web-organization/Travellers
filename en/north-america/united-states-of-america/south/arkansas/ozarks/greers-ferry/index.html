Introduction to Greers Ferry

Nestled in the heart of the Ozarks in Arkansas, Greers Ferry is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its scenic beauty and outdoor adventures, the small town of Greers Ferry attracts travelers who want a unique experience. The town is located in the southern part of the United States and is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Keep reading to plan your next trip to Greers Ferry and explore everything it has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Greers Ferry

When visiting Greers Ferry, there are plenty of places to add to your itinerary. From culturally significant museums to outdoor adventures, here are some top attractions to consider visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Heber Springs Area Museum & Archives: This museum houses fascinating exhibits on the history of Heber Springs and the Greers Ferry Lake area.
  • William Carl Garner Visitor Center: Learn about the construction of the Greers Ferry Dam and view exhibits on the White River and the local area’s flora and fauna.
  • Eden Isle Marina: A hub for water-based activities, the marina offers rental boats and guided tours of Greers Ferry Lake.

Natural Attractions of Greers Ferry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Greers Ferry Lake: Spanning over 40,000 acres, this man-made lake is perfect for boating, fishing, and water sports.
  • Bridal Veil Falls: A stunning waterfall located in the Ozark National Forest that cascades down a rocky bluff and spills into a pool at the bottom.
  • Sugar Loaf Mountain: This 690-foot mountain offers breathtaking views of the Greers Ferry Lake and the surrounding area. Hiking trails are available for all experience levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greers Ferry

Greers Ferry is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Take a look at some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • Little Red River Trail: A 20-mile paved trail that winds through the heart of Greers Ferry, passing through various parks and historic sites and offering scenic views of the river.
  • Dam Site Trail: An easy 1.5-mile trail that offers fantastic views of the Greers Ferry Dam and its surroundings.
  • Devil’s Knob-Bluff Trail: A moderate 3.5-mile hiking trail that offers panoramic views of Greers Ferry Lake and Sugar Loaf Mountain.

Best Things to Do in Greers Ferry: Activities and Tours

Greers Ferry has a range of activities and tours on offer, making it easy to customize your travel itinerary. Here are some popular options:

  • Fishing: Greers Ferry Lake is renowned for its abundance of fish and is a popular spot for anglers.
  • Boating and Water Sports: Spend a day on the lake and rent a boat, go water skiing, wakeboarding, or tubing.
  • Hot Springs Day Trip: Take a day trip to nearby Hot Springs National Park and soak in a natural hot mineral bath.

Food and Drink Scene of Greers Ferry

No travel experience is complete without experiencing the local cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Catfish: A popular dish in the southern region, try local catfish for a taste of the area’s culture and cuisine.
  • Arkansas-style Barbecue: Barbecue is a staple in Arkansas, and Greers Ferry has some of the best ribs and brisket in the state.
  • Fried Pickles: A unique side dish, fried pickles are a local favorite in Greers Ferry.

Local Beverages

  • Local craft beer: The region has a growing craft beer scene, with local breweries offering unique flavors and brews.
  • Sweet Tea: A southern classic, sweet tea can be found in many cafes and restaurants in Greers Ferry. It’s the perfect drink to cool down on a hot day.

Local Culture and Customs of Greers Ferry

The locals of Greers Ferry are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature. They take pride in the area’s natural heritage and work hard to preserve it. Make sure to respect their customs and traditions; greet people with a smile, and be polite and courteous.

Best Time to Visit Greers Ferry

The best time to visit Greers Ferry is during spring and fall when the temperatures are mild, and the leaves change color, creating a picturesque landscape. Summer is the peak season and is a great time to enjoy water-based activities such as boating and water sports. Winter is also a beautiful time to visit but be prepared for colder temperatures.
