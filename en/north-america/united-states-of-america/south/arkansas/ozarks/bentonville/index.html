Introduction to Bentonville

Bentonville is a charming city located in the heart of the Ozarks in Northwest Arkansas. This small city is known for being the birthplace of Walmart and as the headquarters for the retail giant. However, Bentonville is also home to a vibrant cultural scene, exciting outdoor activities, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-packed trip, Bentonville has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bentonville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bentonville is rich in history and has several museums and historical sites that are well worth visiting. The Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art is a world-class institution that houses a stunning collection of American artwork from the Colonial era to contemporary pieces. Another must-visit museum is the Museum of Native American History that comprises exhibits that highlight Native American culture and artifacts from across North America. You can also take a trip to the Peel Mansion Museum and Heritage Gardens to learn about the history and architecture of the South.

Natural Attractions of Bentonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bentonville is located in the heart of the Ozarks, which means there are plenty of natural attractions to explore. The Ozark National Forest is just a short drive away from Bentonville and is an ideal place to hike, bike, and camp. Another attraction that’s worth exploring is the Lake Bentonville Park, which is an ideal spot for swimming, kayaking, and fishing. You can also take a scenic drive along the Pig Trail Scenic Byway to see the beautiful Ozark Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bentonville

One of the most popular activities in Bentonville is hiking and cycling. The city has several notable trails that are perfect for both beginners and experienced hikers. The Razorback Regional Greenway is a 36-mile multi-use trail that’s perfect for cycling, running, or walking. You can also take a hike up to the Hobbs State Park-Conservation Area, which houses over 12,000 acres of Ozark woodland and is ideal for hiking, exploring, or camping. Other trails to explore include the Slaughter Pen Trail and the Coler Mountain Bike Preserve.

Best Things to Do in Bentonville: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and cycling, Bentonville has several other activities and tours that are worth checking out. The 21c Museum Hotel offers guided tours that take visitors through its contemporary galleries and artfully designed spaces. You can also take a brewery tour at the Bentonville Brewing Company and learn about the brewing process of some of the city’s best craft beers. Another fun activity is the Ozark Escape Room, where you’ll have to solve puzzles and clues to escape from a locked room.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bentonville

Day 1: Visit Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art and take a guided tour of the gardens. Day 2: Hike or bike along the Razorback Regional Greenway. Day 3: Explore downtown Bentonville and grab lunch at a local restaurant. Day 4: Take a guided brewery tour at Bentonville Brewing Company. Day 5: Go kayaking or swimming at Lake Bentonville Park. Day 6: Visit the Museum of Native American History. Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Bentonville

Must-Try Dishes

Bentonville has a thriving food scene that showcases locally sourced ingredients and Southern cuisine. Some must-try dishes include the Ark-Mex barbecue tacos at Yeyo’s Mexican Grill, the dry-rubbed ribs at Wright’s Barbecue, and the fried chicken at Bentonville Butcher and Deli.

Local Beverages

In addition to its delicious food scene, Bentonville offers a variety of local brews and cocktails to enjoy. The Bentonville Brewing Company is a popular spot to try local craft beers, while the Onyx Coffee Lab offers a variety of specialty coffee blends. For a night out, visit The Hive restaurant, which has an impressive cocktail menu and live music every night.

Where to Stay in Bentonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bentonville has an array of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. If you’re looking for upscale lodging, The 21c Museum Hotel is a popular choice that offers contemporary accommodations and a prime location. For budget-friendly options, consider the American Inn Bentonville or the Four Points by Sheraton Bentonville.

What to Buy in Bentonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bentonville has several souvenir shops and boutiques where you can find unique gifts and locally made products. Some of the top places to check out include the Walmart Museum, Crystal Bridges Museum Store, and the Downtown Bentonville Square.

Local Culture and Customs of Bentonville

Bentonville is a city that prides itself on its Southern hospitality and friendly locals. It’s customary to greet people with a smile and engage in small talk, even with strangers. Tipping is also expected in restaurants and for other services, so be sure to have cash on hand.

Best Time to Visit Bentonville

The best time to visit Bentonville is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is pleasant, and the foliage is at its peak. The summer months can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bentonville

Do:

  • Try the local craft beers and Southern cuisine.
  • Visit the Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art.
  • Take a hike or bike ride through the Ozarks.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the downtown Bentonville Square.
  • Forget to tip at restaurants and for other services.
  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothes suitable for hiking and outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bentonville

Bentonville is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to take necessary precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables in a safe place and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Bentonville

Bentonville has several hospitals and medical centers should you require medical attention. It’s also a good idea to pack sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the city’s natural attractions.

Getting to Bentonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bentonville

The closest airport to Bentonville is the Northwest Arkansas National Airport (XNA), which is located just 17 miles from the city. Several major airlines operate flights to and from XNA, including American Airlines and Delta Air Lines.

Bus Stations Near Bentonville

The Greyhound bus station is located in nearby Fayetteville, which is just a short drive away from Bentonville.

Other Options

You can also drive to Bentonville, as the city is located near major highways, including I-49 and US-71.

How to Get Around in Bentonville

The best way to get around Bentonville is by car or bike. Several rental car companies operate in the city, including Avis and Hertz. You can also rent a bike from one of the city’s bike share stations.

Where to Go Next from Bentonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Northwest Arkansas, there are several exciting destinations nearby. The charming college town of Fayetteville is just a short drive away and is home to the University of Arkansas and a thriving arts scene. If you’re interested in exploring the outdoors, head to the Buffalo National River, which is just a few hours’ drive from Bentonville.

Conclusion

Bentonville is a hidden gem located in the heart of the Ozarks that offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and Southern charm. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical sites, hiking in the Ozarks, or trying the local brewery scene, Bentonville has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-prepared to plan the perfect trip to Bentonville.
