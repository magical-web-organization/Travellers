Introduction to Springdale

Located in the heart of the Ozarks in Arkansas, Springdale is a charming city that provides visitors with a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and exciting outdoor adventures. The city has a welcoming atmosphere that makes it the perfect destination for solo travelers, couples, and families alike.

List of Places to Visit in Springdale

There’s an array of exciting places to explore in Springdale, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or simply enjoy learning about the past, then Springdale has got you covered! Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Springdale:

  • Shiloh Museum of Ozark History: This museum’s mission is to collect, preserve, and exhibit artifacts, photographs, and documents related to the Northwest Arkansas Ozarks. The museum has several galleries that showcase the history and culture of the Ozarks.

  • Arkansas & Missouri Railroad: Take a ride on the Arkansas and Missouri Railroad and enjoy a scenic tour through the Ozark Mountains. This railway dates back to 1986 and has been providing transportation services to locals and tourists in Northwest Arkansas ever since.

  • Siloam Springs Museum: Explore the history of Siloam Springs, a city located just 18 miles from Springdale, at this small but impressive museum. Exhibits cover the city’s Indian heritage, pioneers, and subsequent growth and change over the years.

Natural Attractions of Springdale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are looking to explore some of the Ozarks’ natural beauty, there’s no shortage of outdoor adventures to experience in Springdale:

  • Ozark National Forest: This 1.2 million-acre national forest is located just a few miles from Springdale and offers visitors the opportunity to hike, fish, camp, and hunt among breathtaking views of the forested mountains and deep valleys.

  • Lake Wedington: Located a little over ten miles from the city limits lies this beautiful lake. Surrounded by the wilderness, Lake Wedington provides visitors with fishing, kayaking, paddle boarding, and swimming experiences that will have you feeling totally refreshed.

  • Fayetteville Ale Trail: If you’re a fan of beer, then this trail is sure to be right up your alley. The trail takes visitors on a tour of local breweries in the Fayetteville area, with tasty brews and good food to be had along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Springdale

Springdale’s natural beauty provides ample opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes to try:

  • The Razorback Greenway: This 36-mile paved trail runs through the Ozarks, connecting six cities in the region, including Springdale. It’s perfect for bikers and hikers who want to explore the area’s natural beauty.

  • Lake Fayetteville: This 5.5-mile loop around Lake Fayetteville is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a medium-difficulty hike. Half of the trail follows the lakeshore, while the other half connects you to the Razorback Greenway.

  • Devil’s Den State Park: Just a short drive from Springdale, this state park has miles of hiking trails, ranging from easy to strenuous, offering beautiful views of the surrounding Ozarks.

Best Things to Do in Springdale: Activities and Tours

Springdale is a city that offers something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Springdale:

  • Botanical Garden of the Ozarks: This 86-acre botanical garden houses native and exotic plants, butterfly and hummingbird gardens, an Ozark garden, and more. It’s perfect for a leisurely stroll with the family or for a romantic, flower-filled date.

  • Ozark Escape Room: Solve puzzles, decipher clues, and race against the clock as you make your way out of escape rooms with your friends or family. The escape rooms are themed rooms, making the experience even more fun and memorable.

  • Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art: This phenomenal museum hosts an extensive collection of American art from the colonial era up to the present day. The museum has over 120 acres of scenic Ozark forest that you can explore while checking out outdoor exhibits.

Food and Drink Scene of Springdale

Springdale has a range of restaurants that serve both local and international cuisines as well as bars and breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: A Southern specialty, fried catfish is a must-try dish when you’re in Springdale. Try it at places like AQ Chicken House and Herman’s Ribhouse.

  • Smoked Pulled Pork: Smoky and delicious, Arkansas is famous for its barbecue. Smoked pulled pork is a must-try dish at places like Wright’s Barbecue and Smokin Joe’s.

  • Crispy Fried Chicken: You’ll never forget the taste of crispy fried chicken from Springdale’s hot spots, such as Rick’s Bakery and Penguin Ed’s Bar-B-Que.

Local Beverages

  • Mountain Beer: Ozark Beer Company and Saddlebock Brewery are known for their unique craft beers and ciders. Try an Ozark Mountain Beer or their seasonal beer for a truly Arkansas experience.

  • Sweet Tea: In this southern state, sweet tea is a staple. Made with tea, sugar, and lemon, it’s a refreshing choice for a hot day.

  • Bourbon: Enjoy a drink or tasting tour at the Rock Town Distillery, which makes its award-winning bourbon, whiskey, gin, and rum.

Where to Stay in Springdale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Springdale has a range of accommodation options to suit different tourists’ needs and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Springdale:

  • SpringHill Suites by Marriott: This hotel is a top pick for tourists looking for a modern, comfortable stay. It offers a complimentary breakfast, indoor pool, and fitness center.

  • Inn at the Mill: This bed and breakfast boasts a beautiful garden and cozy rooms with period antiques. It’s just a short drive away from popular attractions like the Botanical Garden of the Ozarks.

  • Super 8 Motel: If you’re looking for a budget-friendly stay, the Super 8 Motel checks all boxes with free breakfast, outdoor pool, and quality amenities.

What to Buy in Springdale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Springdale without some unique souvenirs or local products to remind you of your trip. Here’s what you should consider buying:

  • Handmade Pottery: Local pottery shops, such as Ozark Folkways in Winslow, offer unique pieces made by the locals. Get a piece to celebrate your visit.

  • Ozark Honey: Take home some all-natural, raw Ozark honey, which is said to have numerous health benefits. Try out local brands like Wildflower Valley Bees or Rogers Apiaries.

  • Wooden Cutting Boards: The famous Ozark Mountain Cutting Boards handcraft beautiful cutting boards made from native hardwoods like maple and cherry.

Local Culture and Customs of Springdale

When travelling to Springdale, it’s important to be mindful of local customs and traditions. Springdale is a friendly, accepting, and welcoming city, and visitors are expected to show respect to the locals’ way of life, religious beliefs, and celebratory practices.

Best Time to Visit Springdale

The best time to visit Springdale is typically during the fall season from September to November. The temperature is mild, and the forested mountains are boasting fall colors. You’ll also experience fewer crowds and avoid swarming mosquitoes. Springdale’s spring and early summer can also be an excellent time to visit with relatively mild temperatures and blooming wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Springdale

Here are some dos and don’ts you need to keep in mind during your visit to Springdale.

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and products
  • Stay hydrated and carry mosquito repellent during outdoor activities
  • Plan ahead and book tours in advance

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Dress inappropriately for religious or ceremonial places
  • Be disrespectful to locals or other tourists
  • Forget to tip generously where and when due

Safety and Health

Safety in Springdale

Springdale is generally a safe city with the normal precautions required when traveling to any urban area. However, tourists should be vigilant while visiting tourist sites, parks, and municipalities. Some tips to stay safe include:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded areas
  • Avoid dark alleys or poorly lit areas at night
  • Learn about the cultural values and local customs before your trip
  • Research and stay updated on local weather, disturbances that might affect your trip

Health in Springdale

Springdale is not a high-risk area as far as diseases are concerned. Visitors must observe general hygiene practices, such as washing hands, staying hydrated, and paying attention to personal health. It’s best to carry appropriate insect repellent, sunscreen, and any necessary medication.

Getting to Springdale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Springdale

The Northwest Arkansas Regional Airport (XNA) serves the region and is approximately 16 miles from Springdale. It’s an easy drive along the highway or a convenient coach and cab ride into the city limits.

Bus Stations Near Springdale

Greyhound and Megabus both have stops at several locations near Springdale. Sprinfield, MO Greyhound bus station and nearby Boltbus stop are popular options.

Other Options

Some other options for getting to and from Springdale include:

  • Driving: Springdale is easily accessible via the I-49 for those travelling by car. Once you arrive, the city has ample parking, both on the streets and garages.

  • Ride sharing: Uber and Lyft ride-sharing services in Springdale are easily available and safe.

  • Cycling: The Ozark Greenway trail system connects with cities in the area and a good option for those looking for an adventurous or recreational cycling trip.

How to Get Around in Springdale

Springdale is a relatively small city, making for a simple transportation process to navigate. Visitors can choose from several transport options, including:

  • Driving a rental car: Visit local rental agencies like Enterprise in the city to rent a car.

  • Public transport: Ozark Regional Transit bus service is the best public transportation option in Springdale.

  • Cycling: There are several places and rental agencies inside and close to city limits where visitors can hire bicycles for local trips.

Where to Go Next from Springdale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, then explore the nearby destinations, such as:

  • Eureka Springs: A beautiful, spa town with Victorian architecture situated about 45 minutes from Springdale.

  • Bentonville: This city is just 20 minutes away from Springdale and has a host of interesting attractions such as the Walmart Museum and the Amazeum for kids.

  • Buffalo National River: This river is perfect for canoeing, kayaking, and scenic floating. A little over two hours from Springdale, it’s an excellent day trip.

Conclusion

Springdale, Arkansas, is a charming city that will enchant you with its natural beauty, tourist attractions, friendly locals, and unique culture. This comprehensive guide should provide you with everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Springdale. Happy traveling!
