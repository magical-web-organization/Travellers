Introduction to Little Rock

Little Rock, the state capital of Arkansas, is a city full of history, culture, and natural beauty. This southern city lies in central Arkansas and is known for its vibrant food and music scenes, beautiful parks, and numerous museums and historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Little Rock

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Little Rock boasts several museums and historical landmarks that are sure to interest any traveler.

Clinton Presidential Library and Museum

The Clinton Presidential Library and Museum is the largest presidential library in the country. It contains over two million photographs, documents, and artifacts from the Clinton presidency, as well as temporary exhibits. Don’t miss the full-scale replica of the Oval Office.

Little Rock Central High School National Historic Site

Little Rock Central High School played a crucial role in the history of civil rights in the United States. Visitors can take guided tours of the school, which was the site of the Little Rock Nine crisis in 1957.

Arkansas State Capitol

The Arkansas State Capitol is home to the state government and offers guided tours of the building. The exterior of the building is stunning with its massive pillars and bronze doors, and visitors are sure to appreciate the beautiful stained glass interior.

Natural Attractions of Little Rock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Little Rock is surrounded by natural beauty and offers outdoor activities for every type of traveler.

Pinnacle Mountain State Park

Pinnacle Mountain State Park is a favorite destination for hiking enthusiasts, offering several trails with varying degrees of difficulty. It also offers stunning views of the surrounding Ouachita Mountains.

Big Dam Bridge

The Big Dam Bridge is the longest pedestrian and bicycle-specific bridge in North America. Spanning the Arkansas River, this bridge provides panoramic views of the river and the Little Rock skyline.

Arkansas River Trail

The Arkansas River Trail is an 88-mile trail that spans from Little Rock to Conway, Arkansas. The trail is a popular spot for hiking, jogging, and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Rock

Little Rock is a haven for cyclists and hikers, thanks to its numerous parks and trails that wind through the city and surrounding regions.

The River Trail

The River Trail provides over 17 miles of trails through the city of Little Rock and North Little Rock. The trail loops through natural forests, riverside parks, and famous landmarks such as the Clinton Library and the Big Dam Bridge.

Ouachita National Forest

Ouachita National Forest is a vast expanse of wilderness that covers large parts of Arkansas and Oklahoma. The forest has hundreds of miles of trails, including the epic Ouachita Trail that is 223 miles long.

Pinnacle Mountain State Park

Pinnacle Mountain State Park has a variety of hiking trails, ranging from easy to strenuous, with some offering stunning views of the Ouachita Mountains and the Arkansas River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Little Rock: Activities and Tours

Little Rock has plenty of activities and tours for travelers of all interests and ages.

Riverboat Sightseeing Tour

A Riverboat Sightseeing Tour is an excellent way to get inquisitive about the beauty and history of Little Rock. The boat tours take you along the Arkansas River and offer stunning views of the city, with a guide who shares information on the area’s history and notable sights.

Arkansas River Trail Bike Tour

The Arkansas River Trail Bike Tour is a perfect way to get some exercise while taking in the beautiful natural surroundings of Little Rock. Bikes, helmets, and guides are provided on these tours, which can also be customized based on the participants’ fitness levels.

Food Tour

A food tour is a great way to explore Little Rock’s vibrant food scene. The tours take you to famous restaurants and markets, allowing you to sample some of the best local cuisine and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Little Rock

Little Rock has a vibrant food and beverage scene that showcases the Southern cuisine and hospitality.

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Catfish

Fried catfish is a Southern dish made with catfish fillets coated in cornmeal flour and served with coleslaw, hush puppies, and tartar sauce.

Barbecue

Barbecue is a staple of Southern cuisine, and Little Rock offers some of the best barbecue in the region. Pulled pork, ribs, and brisket are favorites, and each restaurant offers its own unique take on the classic dish.

Fried chicken

Fried chicken is another staple of Southern cuisine, and Little Rock offers some of the best fried chicken in the country. Chicken is breaded and fried until crispy, and is often served with mashed potatoes, macaroni and cheese, and green beans.

Local Beverages

Beer

Little Rock is home to several breweries that offer a range of beers, from light and crisp lagers to dark and malty stouts. Some popular breweries include Lost Forty Brewing, Stone’s Throw Brewing, and Rebel Kettle Brewing.

Whiskey

Arkansas is known for its whiskey, and Little Rock has several distilleries that offer tours and tastings. Rock Town Distillery, the first legal distillery in Arkansas after prohibition, is one of the most renowned.

Where to Stay in Little Rock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Little Rock offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s budget and preferences.

Hotels

Little Rock boasts dozens of hotels ranging from mid-range to luxury. Some popular options include the Capital Hotel, the Little Rock Marriott, and the DoubleTree by Hilton Little Rock.

Hostels

Little Rock also has a few hostels that offer economical accommodations for budget travelers. Some popular options include the HI Little Rock Firehouse Hostel & Museum and the Little Rock Guesthouse.

Guesthouses

Guesthouses are another option for travelers who want a more unique accommodation experience. Many guesthouses are situated in historic buildings and offer personalized service. Some popular options include the Empress of Little Rock and The Baker House Bed & Breakfast.

What to Buy in Little Rock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Little Rock has several souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and mementos of your trip.

Hot Sauce

Little Rock is known for its spicy food and several specialty shops sell locally made hot sauces and spice blends.

Arkansas Peaches

Arkansas is famous for its peaches, and visitors can find them at local farmers’ markets and roadside stands.

Local Culture and Customs of Little Rock

Southern hospitality is a part of the Little Rock culture and visitors can expect warm and welcoming treatment at most places.

Best Time to Visit Little Rock

The best time to visit Little Rock is in the spring or fall when temperatures are mild and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Little Rock

Do:

  • Visit the Clinton Presidential Library and Museum
  • Explore the natural beauty of Pinnacle Mountain State Park
  • Attend a live music and food festival

Don’t:

  • Visit Little Rock during the hot and humid summer months
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Little Rock

Like any city, there are areas of Little Rock where tourists should exercise caution. Visitors should avoid walking alone at night or in areas they are unfamiliar with.

Health in Little Rock

Little Rock has several medical facilities, including the UAMS Medical Center, which provides world-class medical services.

Getting to Little Rock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Little Rock

The Bill and Hillary Clinton National Airport is the primary airport of Little Rock and offers daily nonstop flights from major U.S. cities.

Bus Stations Near Little Rock

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in downtown Little Rock, which provides service to several major cities in the region.

Other Options

Little Rock is also accessible by car via several major highways, including I-30 and I-40.

How to Get Around in Little Rock

Little Rock has several transportation options for visitors, including car rental, taxis, and public transportation.

Public Transportation

The Rock Region Metro operates a reliable bus system that covers most of the city’s major attractions and neighborhoods.

Taxis and Rideshare

Taxis and rideshare services such as Uber and Lyft are widely available in Little Rock.

Car Rental

Several car rental companies, including Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise, offer rental services at the airport and throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Little Rock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Little Rock serves as a gateway to several exciting destinations in the region, including:

Hot Springs, Arkansas

Hot Springs National Park is located just an hour southwest of Little Rock. The park is known for its natural hot springs, hiking trails, and historic bathhouses.

Memphis, Tennessee

Memphis, the birthplace of rock ‘n’ roll and home to Beale Street, Graceland, and the National Civil Rights Museum, is located just two hours southeast of Little Rock.

Conclusion

Little Rock is a city full of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re sampling the local cuisine, exploring museums and historical landmarks, or hiking through breathtaking natural landscapes, Little Rock offers something for every type of traveler. Plan your trip today and experience the best that Little Rock has to offer.
