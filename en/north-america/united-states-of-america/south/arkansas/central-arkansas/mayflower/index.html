Introduction to Mayflower

Welcome to Mayflower, a small town in Central Arkansas that is known for its charming atmosphere and outdoor adventures. Named after the Mayflower ship that brought the Pilgrims to America, this town has a fascinating history and is steeped in Southern culture and hospitality. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through all the top things to do and see in Mayflower and give you insider tips to help you plan the perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Mayflower

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, Mayflower has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top attractions in Mayflower that you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mayflower Museum: Located in the heart of downtown, this museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Mayflower and its role in American history.

  • The Mayflower Cemetery: This cemetery is the final resting place of many of the town’s founders and early residents, including members of the Mayflower Compact.

  • The Faulkner County Museum: Just a short drive from Mayflower, this museum tells the story of Faulkner County and its role in Arkansas history.

Natural Attractions of Mayflower: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Conway: This serene lake offers fishing, boating, and other water activities.

  • Cypress Bayou Wildlife Management Area: This outdoor oasis offers hiking, birdwatching, and nature photography opportunities.

  • Woolly Hollow State Park: This park has hiking trails that wind through the forest, as well as a lake for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mayflower

Mayflower is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes in and around the town:

  • The Lake Conway Loop: This 21-mile route takes you around Lake Conway and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Two Rivers Park Trail: Located just a short drive from Mayflower, this trail features 10 miles of paved paths through lush forests and along the Arkansas River.

  • The Ouachita National Recreation Trail: This 223-mile trail winds through the Ouachita Mountains and is perfect for multi-day hikes or bike rides.

Best Things to Do in Mayflower: Activities and Tours

From guided tours to hands-on experiences, Mayflower has plenty to offer visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in town:

  • The Mayflower History Tour: Take a guided tour through downtown Mayflower and learn about the town’s history and traditions.

  • The Woolly Hollow State Park Adventure Course: Test your skills on this treetop adventure course, which features ziplines, rope bridges, and other obstacles.

  • Fishing and Boating on Lake Conway: Rent a boat and spend the day fishing or exploring the lake’s coves and inlets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mayflower

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Mayflower, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Mayflower Museum and the Mayflower Cemetery
2 Hike the Lake Conway Loop and have a picnic by the lake
3 Take a guided tour of downtown Mayflower and try some local cuisine
4 Rent a boat and spend the day exploring Lake Conway
5 Visit the Faulkner County Museum and go shopping in Conway
6 Tackle the Woolly Hollow State Park Adventure Course
7 Take a day trip to the nearby city of Little Rock for sightseeing and shopping

Food and Drink Scene of Mayflower

No trip to Mayflower would be complete without sampling some of the town’s delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: This Southern classic is a staple on menus throughout Mayflower.

  • Pulled Pork BBQ Sandwiches: Slow-cooked and smoky, these sandwiches are a local favorite.

  • Sweet Potato Pie: Made with fresh sweet potatoes and warm spices, this dessert is a true taste of the South.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot summer days.

  • Mayflower Moonshine: Made with locally sourced ingredients, this moonshine is a popular choice among visitors.

Where to Stay in Mayflower: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mayflower has a variety of accommodations to fit every budget and travel style. Here are a few options to consider:

  • The Holiday Inn Express: This hotel is conveniently located near downtown Mayflower and offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

  • The Angel at Rose Hall: This bed and breakfast is just a short drive from Mayflower and offers luxurious rooms and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Cabins at Petit Jean State Park: If you’re looking for a rustic escape, consider staying in one of the cabins at nearby Petit Jean State Park.

What to Buy in Mayflower: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Mayflower home with you by picking up some locally made souvenirs and products. Here are a few ideas:

  • Mayflower Moonshine: Pick up a bottle or two to share with friends back home.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Browse the selection of unique, locally made jewelry at one of the town’s artisan shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Mayflower

Mayflower is known for its warm hospitality and Southern charm. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and manners, including saying “yes ma’am” and “no sir” when speaking to elders. Additionally, Mayflower is a dry town, so visitors should be aware of local laws regarding alcohol consumption.

Best Time to Visit Mayflower

Mayflower is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall when temperatures are mild and the foliage is at its peak. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mayflower

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mayflower:

Do:

  • Show respect for local customs and manners.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take in the natural beauty of the area by hiking, biking, or boating.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public or violate local laws.

  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

  • Ignore safety warnings or guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

While Mayflower is a safe destination, visitors should take normal precautions when traveling. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Mayflower

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

  • Keep valuables out of sight and in a secure location.

  • Follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Mayflower

  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot summer months.

  • Apply sunscreen and insect repellent as needed.

  • Carry any necessary medications, and be aware of local healthcare options.

Getting to Mayflower: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mayflower is located about 30 minutes from Little Rock, Arkansas, and is easily accessible by car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Mayflower

  • Little Rock National Airport

Bus Stations Near Mayflower

  • Central Arkansas Transit Authority

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at Little Rock National Airport or in nearby Conway.

How to Get Around in Mayflower

Mayflower is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by car. Bicycles are also a great option for exploring the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Mayflower: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Petit Jean State Park: Located just a short drive from Mayflower, this state park offers hiking, camping, and stunning views of the Arkansas River.

  • Little Rock, Arkansas: Just a 30-minute drive from Mayflower, Little Rock offers a variety of cultural attractions and dining options.

Conclusion

Mayflower is a charming town with a rich history, beautiful scenery, and plenty of outdoor adventures to enjoy. Whether you’re looking for a day trip or a week-long vacation, Mayflower is definitely worth a visit. Remember to respect local customs and safety guidelines, and enjoy everything this Southern gem has to offer.
