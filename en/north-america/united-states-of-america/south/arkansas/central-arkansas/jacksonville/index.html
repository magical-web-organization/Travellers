Introduction to Jacksonville

Jacksonville is a charming city in Central Arkansas, United States, known for its rich culture, natural beauty, and outdoor recreation options. The city is located just 15 miles from downtown Little Rock and offers a perfect balance of modern amenities and southern hospitality. Jacksonville is a great place to visit with family, friends, or solo, as it has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you on a virtual tour of Jacksonville and help you plan your trip to this beautiful destination.

List of Places to Visit in Jacksonville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jacksonville has a rich history and is home to many museums and historical sites that are worth exploring.

Jacksonville Museum of Military History

The Jacksonville Museum of Military History is a must-visit for history buffs and those interested in learning about the military history of the United States. The museum features exhibits on various wars and conflicts, including World War I, World War II, and the Vietnam War. Visitors can view artifacts, uniforms, weapons, and more.

Mammoth Spring State Park

Mammoth Spring State Park is located just outside of Jacksonville and is known for its natural beauty and rich history. The park features a stunning 10-acre spring-fed lake, walking trails, picnic areas, and a playground. Visitors can also explore the historic Mammoth Spring Depot, which houses a museum dedicated to the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Jacksonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jacksonville is home to many natural wonders and outdoor recreation options that are perfect for those who love the great outdoors.

Burns Park

Burns Park is the largest municipal park in the United States and is located just outside of Jacksonville. The park features 1,700 acres of wooded land, lakes, hiking and biking trails, a fishing lake, and a golf course.

Arkansas River Trail

The Arkansas River Trail is a 17-mile trail that connects downtown Little Rock with North Little Rock and offers stunning views of the Arkansas River. Visitors can walk, run, or bike the trail and enjoy the beautiful scenery along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jacksonville

Jacksonville is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, as it offers many trails and routes to explore.

The Sylamore Trail

The Sylamore Trail is a 50-mile trail that winds through the Ozark National Forest and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The trail is perfect for hikers and mountain bikers and is open year-round.

The River Trail

The River Trail is a 15-mile trail that runs along the Arkansas River and connects downtown Little Rock with North Little Rock. The trail is perfect for hikers, runners, and cyclists and offers stunning views of the river and the city skyline.

Best Things to Do in Jacksonville: Activities and Tours

Jacksonville offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests.

Little Rock Central High School National Historic Site

The Little Rock Central High School National Historic Site is a must-visit for those interested in the civil rights movement. Visitors can tour the school and learn about its important role in the integration of public schools in the United States.

Little Rock Zoo

The Little Rock Zoo is a fun destination for families and animal lovers. The zoo features over 700 animals from around the world, including lions, tigers, bears, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Jacksonville

Must-Try Dishes

Catfish

Catfish is a Southern staple and is a must-try dish when visiting Jacksonville. The fish is typically fried and served with hushpuppies, coleslaw, and tartar sauce.

BBQ

BBQ is a popular cuisine in Arkansas, and there are many great BBQ restaurants in Jacksonville. Some of the must-try dishes include pulled pork, ribs, and brisket.

Local Beverages

Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a classic Southern beverage and is a popular drink in Jacksonville. This refreshing drink is made by brewing black tea and sweetening it with sugar.

Local Beer

Jacksonville is home to many great craft breweries, and visitors can try local beer at one of the many bars and restaurants in the city.

Where to Stay in Jacksonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Quality Inn

The Quality Inn is a great budget-friendly option for those looking for a comfortable and convenient place to stay in Jacksonville.

Courtyard by Marriott Little Rock North

The Courtyard by Marriott is a great option for those looking for a more upscale hotel experience. The hotel offers a fitness center, indoor pool, and on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Jacksonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Artwork

Jacksonville is home to many talented artists, and visitors can find unique and beautiful artwork at local galleries and markets.

Arkansas Honey

Arkansas is known for its honey, and visitors can find jars of this sweet treat at local farmers’ markets and specialty stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Jacksonville

Jacksonville is known for its warm southern hospitality and friendly residents. Visitors should expect to be greeted with a smile and a friendly conversation wherever they go.

Best Time to Visit Jacksonville

The best time to visit Jacksonville is during the fall and spring months when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jacksonville

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Jacksonville by hiking or biking one of the many trails in the area.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including BBQ and catfish.
  • Do visit the museums and historical sites to learn about the rich history of the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray when exploring the outdoors.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask the locals for recommendations on where to eat and what to see.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the museums and parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jacksonville

Jacksonville is a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions when traveling. It is important to lock car doors and not leave valuables visible inside vehicles.

Health in Jacksonville

Visitors to Jacksonville should be aware of ticks and take proper precautions when hiking or spending time outdoors. It is also important to stay hydrated and use sunscreen when spending time in the sun.

Getting to Jacksonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jacksonville

The closest airport to Jacksonville is the Bill and Hillary Clinton National Airport in Little Rock, which is located about 18 miles away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Jacksonville

The closest bus station to Jacksonville is the Central Arkansas Transit Authority (CATA) transit center in North Little Rock, located about 12 miles away from the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Jacksonville or rent a car from the airport.

How to Get Around in Jacksonville

The best way to get around Jacksonville is by car, as the city is spread out and most attractions are not within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Jacksonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Little Rock

Little Rock is the capital city of Arkansas and is located just 15 miles from Jacksonville. Visitors can explore the River Market District, tour the Arkansas State Capitol, or visit the Little Rock Zoo.

Hot Springs National Park

Hot Springs National Park is a beautiful natural wonder located about 80 miles from Jacksonville. Visitors can hike the trails, soak in the hot springs, or take a scenic drive through the park.

Conclusion

Jacksonville is a beautiful destination in Arkansas that offers a perfect blend of outdoor recreation, history, culture, and southern charm. Whether you’re interested in exploring the great outdoors or learning about the history of the area, Jacksonville has something for everyone. Use our guide to plan your trip to this amazing destination, and have a wonderful time exploring all that it has to offer.
