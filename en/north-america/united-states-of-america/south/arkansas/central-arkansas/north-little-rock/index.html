North Little Rock is a charming city in Central Arkansas, located in the South of the United States of America. With its fascinating history, abundance of natural attractions, and hospitable locals, this city is an excellent destination for travelers seeking a unique experience. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to North Little Rock.

Introduction to North Little Rock

North Little Rock is located across the Arkansas River from Little Rock, the capital of Arkansas. This city has a rich history that dates back to the 1800s. Today, North Little Rock is a thriving community that offers visitors a blend of modern convenience and southern charm.

List of Places to Visit in North Little Rock

North Little Rock has plenty of exciting places to visit, whether you are looking for cultural attractions, scenic views, or outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit places in North Little Rock:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North Little Rock has a wealth of culturally important attractions, including museums and historical places. These sites offer visitors a glimpse into the city’s rich history and heritage. Here are some of the most popular:

  • William J. Clinton Presidential Library: This world-class facility houses artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the 42nd President of the United States, Bill Clinton. Visitors can explore the presidential exhibits, see the president’s Oval Office replica, and take a stroll around the serene grounds overlooking the Arkansas River.
  • Old Mill: The Old Mill is an authentic replica of a 19th-century water-powered grist mill located in T.R. Pugh Memorial Park. It is surrounded by beautiful outdoor gardens and a landscaped pond, which make it an excellent spot for a relaxing walk or picnic.
  • Arkansas Inland Maritime Museum: This museum is home to two floating naval vessels, the USS Razorback submarine and the USS Hoga tugboat. Visitors can learn about their significant role in World War II and the Korean War, and explore the inside of the submarine for an unforgettable experience.

Natural Attractions of North Little Rock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Little Rock is a paradise for nature lovers, with a range of scenic views and outdoor activities to enjoy. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in North Little Rock:

  • Emerald Park: This park is a beautiful natural area that offers stunning views of the Arkansas River. Visitors can walk the nature trails, have a picnic, or play a game of disc golf while enjoying the scenic views.
  • Burns Park: Burns Park is a massive municipal park that features a golf course, soccer fields, tennis courts, and miles of walking and biking trails. Visitors can also enjoy fishing and boating at the park’s two lakes.
  • Rockwater Marina: With its prime location on the Arkansas River, Rockwater Marina is an excellent spot for riverfront dining, boating, and fishing. Visitors can enjoy a relaxing dinner cruise or rent a kayak or paddleboard to explore the river at their own pace.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Little Rock

North Little Rock is a mecca for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with numerous trails that offer spectacular views of the city and surrounding areas. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore in North Little Rock:

  • Over the River Trail: This paved trail spans from North Little Rock to Little Rock over the Arkansas River. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the river and surrounding areas from the pedestrian and bike bridges.
  • Riverfront Park Trail: This scenic trail is located in the heart of downtown Little Rock and offers visitors a leisurely stroll or bike ride along the Arkansas River. The trail also provides stunning views of the Little Rock skyline.
  • Bells of Peace Trail: This trail is an easy one-mile loop that takes visitors through the serene wooded area of the Arkansas River. It is an excellent spot for a peaceful hike or a romantic picnic.

Best Things to Do in North Little Rock: Activities and Tours

In addition to its many natural and cultural attractions, North Little Rock also offers a range of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in North Little Rock:

  • Arkansas Riverboat Cruises: Arkansas Riverboat Cruises offers a variety of riverboat rides, including dinner cruises, sunset tours, and sightseeing tours. Visitors can relax and take in the stunning views of the Arkansas River while enjoying a delicious meal or drink.
  • Pulaski Pedaler: The Pulaski Pedaler is a unique way to see the city while getting some exercise. This 15-passenger pedal party bike allows visitors to pedal around town while enjoying cold drinks, music, and fun with friends.
  • Big Rock Mini Golf & Fun Park: Big Rock Mini Golf & Fun Park is a great place to spend a fun afternoon with the family. Visitors can enjoy a round of mini-golf, go-karting, laser tag, and other exciting activities.

Food and Drink Scene of North Little Rock

North Little Rock offers a unique food and drink scene, with many local restaurants and bars serving up southern comfort food and regional specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried catfish: This is a classic southern dish, and North Little Rock has some of the best catfish in the region. Try Catfish City & BBQ Grill or Flying Fish for some of the most famous recipes.
  • Pimento cheese: This spread made from cheddar cheese and pimento peppers is a southern staple. Try it at the Southern Gourmasian or Maddie’s Place.
  • BBQ: Arkansas-style BBQ is known for its distinct combination of sweet and tangy flavors. Try it at Whole Hog Cafe or Lindsey’s BBQ.

Local Beverages

  • Diamond Bear Brewery: This brewery offers a range of locally brewed beers, including the famous Presidential IPA. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and enjoy a tasting of their delicious beers.
  • Roxy’s Wine & Spirits: Roxy’s is a local winery that offers wine tastings and bottles to purchase. Visitors can also enjoy a delicious charcuterie board with their wine flights.

Where to Stay in North Little Rock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Little Rock has plenty of excellent accommodations for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best options:

  • Wyndham Riverfront Little Rock: This hotel offers stunning views of the Arkansas River and is located in the heart of the city. It features a rooftop pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.
  • Little Rock Marriott: This upscale hotel offers luxurious amenities, including a full-service spa, indoor pool, and on-site restaurant. It is conveniently located near the William J. Clinton Presidential Library.
  • Baker House Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a historic home with comfortable rooms and delicious breakfast offerings.

What to Buy in North Little Rock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Little Rock has several unique shops and boutiques that offer a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • River Market District: This area is home to several shops and boutiques selling everything from locally made crafts to high-end fashion. Visitors can also enjoy the River Market Farmer’s Market on Saturdays.
  • Argenta Bead Company: This shop offers a range of unique beads, jewelry-making supplies, and handmade jewelry.
  • Loblolly Creamery: This local ice cream shop offers handcrafted ice cream made from locally sourced ingredients. Visitors can also purchase ice cream-themed souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of North Little Rock

North Little Rock is a city steeped in southern charm and hospitality. Visitors should expect friendly locals, a laid-back atmosphere, and plenty of southern hospitality. When it comes to customs, visitors should remember to be respectful and courteous to locals, and avoid sensitive topics such as politics and religion in conversation.

Best Time to Visit North Little Rock

The best time to visit North Little Rock is from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and tourist crowds are low. Summer (June to August) can be hot and humid, and winter (December to February) can be cold and damp.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Little Rock

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting North Little Rock:

Do:

  • Try the local Southern cuisine.
  • Take a scenic drive to enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Visit historic sites to learn about the city’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to locals or ignore their southern hospitality.
  • Ignore traffic laws and regulations.
  • Leave valuables in an unattended car.

Safety and Health

North Little Rock is generally safe for visitors, but of course, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in North Little Rock

  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times, especially in more crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night or in isolated areas.
  • Keep valuables in a secure location.

Health in North Little Rock

  • Stay hydrated during the hot summer months.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to avoid sunburn and mosquito bites.
  • Seek medical attention if you feel unwell or have any health concerns.

Getting to North Little Rock: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Little Rock is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus.

Airports Near North Little Rock

  • Bill and Hillary Clinton National Airport (LIT) is the primary airport serving North Little Rock and Little Rock, located about 15 minutes away from the city center.
  • Memphis International Airport (MEM) is also an option, located about two hours away from North Little Rock.

Bus Stations Near North Little Rock

  • The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown Little Rock, just across the river from North Little Rock.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or take a shuttle service from the airports to North Little Rock.

How to Get Around in North Little Rock

While North Little Rock is a relatively small city, visitors will still need a way to get around. Here are some transportation options:

  • Car rental: Rental cars are widely available and offer the most flexibility in terms of travel.
  • Bus: The Rock Region Metro Transit is the local public transportation system, with several bus lines that cover North Little Rock and Little Rock.
  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft: Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are available in North Little Rock and provide a reliable transportation option.

Where to Go Next from North Little Rock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Little Rock is located in the heart of a beautiful region of Arkansas, and visitors have plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Here are some of the best excursions to take from North Little Rock:

  • Hot Springs: Located about an hour-and-a-half’s drive from North Little Rock, Hot Springs is a historic spa town with natural hot springs and beautiful architecture.
  • Eureka Springs: This charming town is located in the Ozark Mountains and offers visitors a glimpse into Arkansas’ unique culture and history.
  • Bentonville: Home to the Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art, Bentonville is a must-visit destination for art lovers.

Conclusion

North Little Rock is an excellent destination for travelers looking to experience the unique blend of southern charm, natural beauty, and rich history. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or great food and drink, North Little Rock has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this charming city.
