Introduction to Lonoke

Nestled in the heart of Central Arkansas, Lonoke is a small town with a big heart. Known for its rich history and beautiful landscapes, it is the perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Lonoke has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lonoke

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Depot Museum: This museum, located in the heart of Lonoke, showcases the town’s history, including its role in the Civil War and the growth of the railroad system.

  • The Lonoke County Courthouse: The courthouse, built in 1928, is a beautiful example of neoclassical architecture and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

  • The White House: This historic home, built in 1914, is the former residence of the town’s founder, William Lonoke Brown.

Natural Attractions of Lonoke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lonoke City Park: This park, located on the banks of the Lonoke Creek, offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of town. Visitors can enjoy picnic areas, walking trails, fishing, and bird watching.

  • Mount Nebo State Park: Just a short drive from Lonoke, this state park offers breathtaking views of the Arkansas River Valley, hiking trails, and camping areas.

  • Bayou Meto Wildlife Management Area: This wildlife sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, ducks, and geese. Visitors can enjoy hunting, fishing, and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lonoke

  • The Lonoke Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile trail meanders along the Lonoke Creek and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

  • The Bayou Meto Wildlife Management Area Trail: This 10-mile trail winds through the Bayou Meto Wildlife Management Area and offers a unique opportunity to see Arkansas wildlife up close.

  • The Mount Nebo State Park Trail System: This system consists of multiple hiking trails of varying difficulty levels that take visitors through the beautiful landscapes of the Arkansas River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Lonoke: Activities and Tours

  • Lonoke Farmer’s Market: This farmer’s market, held every Saturday morning, is a great place to buy fresh, locally-grown produce and handmade crafts.

  • Lonoke County Fair: Held annually in September, the fair offers carnival rides, games, live music, and a variety of exhibits.

  • Ghost Tours of Lonoke: This tour, held in the fall, takes visitors on a spooky journey through the town’s haunted past.

Food and Drink Scene of Lonoke

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: A staple of Southern cuisine, fried catfish can be found at many local eateries in Lonoke.

  • Barbecue: Arkansas is known for its delicious barbecue, and Lonoke is no exception. Be sure to try the local barbecue during your stay.

  • Pecan Pie: Made with locally-grown pecans, Lonoke’s pecan pie is a sweet treat that should not be missed.

Local Beverages

  • Diamond Bear Brewing Company: Located in nearby North Little Rock, this brewery offers a variety of craft beers made with Arkansas-grown ingredients.

  • Wine Country: Just a short drive from Lonoke, this area is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Lonoke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Plus Lonoke Hotel: This hotel is conveniently located off of Highway 167 and offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as a fitness center and free breakfast.

  • Knights Inn Lonoke: This budget-friendly option offers basic amenities such as free Wi-Fi and breakfast.

  • Airbnb: Several Airbnb options are available in Lonoke, offering visitors a more personalized and affordable stay.

What to Buy in Lonoke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Local artisans sell their handmade goods at the Lonoke Farmer’s Market and other local shops.

  • Locally-Grown Produce: Grab some fresh fruits and veggies at the farmer’s market to bring home and enjoy.

  • Pecans: Arkansas is known for its pecans, and Lonoke is no exception. Stock up on locally-grown pecans to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Lonoke

Lonoke has a rich history and a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s history and traditions and take the time to get to know the locals.

Best Time to Visit Lonoke

The best time to visit Lonoke is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the landscapes are at their most beautiful. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and dreary.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lonoke

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the town’s history and natural beauty.

  • Do respect the town’s traditions and culture.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s natural areas.

  • Don’t disrespect the town’s history or culture.

  • Don’t forget to tip your servers and service workers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lonoke

Lonoke is generally a safe town to visit. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

Health in Lonoke

Medical facilities and pharmacies are available in Lonoke and nearby areas. Visitors should have adequate health insurance and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Lonoke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lonoke

  • Clinton National Airport: Located in Little Rock, this airport is about 25 miles from Lonoke.

Bus Stations Near Lonoke

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Little Rock, this station is about 25 miles from Lonoke.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at the Clinton National Airport and in Little Rock.

How to Get Around in Lonoke

The best way to get around Lonoke is by car. While the town is small and walkable, many of the area’s top attractions are outside of town and require a vehicle to reach.

Where to Go Next from Lonoke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Little Rock: The state capital is just a short drive from Lonoke and offers plenty of attractions, including the Clinton Presidential Library and the River Market District.

  • Hot Springs: This famous spa town is just over an hour’s drive from Lonoke and offers a variety of hot springs and spas to relax and rejuvenate.

Conclusion

Lonoke is a hidden gem in Central Arkansas, offering a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and Southern charm. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a peaceful getaway, Lonoke has something to offer for everyone.
