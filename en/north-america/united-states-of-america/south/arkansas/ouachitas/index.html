Introduction to Ouachitas

Located in the south of Arkansas, USA, Ouachitas is a range of low mountains that stretches across parts of Oklahoma and Arkansas. The region is named after the Ouachita River, which flows through it. Surrounded by the stunning Ouachita National Forest, the area is a nature lover’s paradise with a wide range of outdoor activities and breathtaking scenic views. In addition, Ouachitas has also a rich cultural heritage with many interesting museums, historical landmarks, and a lively local culture. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, history, or food, Ouachitas has something for everyone. This comprehensive tour guide will take you through all the top attractions and provide you with valuable information to make the most out of your Ouachitas trip.

List of Places to Visit in Ouachitas

To make your trip to Ouachitas unforgettable, you should visit these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hot Springs National Park - located in downtown Hot Springs, this park offers a unique combination of natural hot springs and historic bathhouses.
  • Gangster Museum of America - situated in Hot Springs, this museum showcases the city’s notorious history as a gangster haven in the 1930s and 1940s.
  • Washita Battlefield National Historic Site - learn about the story of the Cheyenne people and the attack by the US Army in 1868, which took place on this site, located in Cheyenne.
  • Arkansas Museum of Natural Resources - located in Smackover, this museum features the state’s oil and gas industry’s history and heritage, including an oil rig tour.

Natural Attractions of Ouachitas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ouachita National Forest - spread over 1.8 million acres, this forest offers many places for hiking, fishing, camping, biking, and other outdoor activities.
  • Talimena National Scenic Byway - this scenic drive offers a breathtaking view of Ouachita Mountains from Mena to Talihina.
  • Lake Ouachita - known for its crystal-clear waters and a variety of recreational activities such as boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Ouachita River - ideal for canoeing and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ouachitas

Ouachitas offers a wide range of hiking and biking routes for hiking enthusiasts, ranging from short and easy paths to more challenging trails:

  • Ouachita National Recreation Trail - a 223-mile trail that cuts through the Ouachita Mountains, offering stunning views and varying levels of difficulty.
  • Womble Trail - a 38-mile biking trail that winds through Ouachita National Forest.
  • Lake Ouachita Vista Trail - this 24-mile trail offers stunning views of Lake Ouachita, and it’s ideal for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Ouachitas: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to shopping, here are some must-do activities and tours in Ouachitas:

  • Hot Springs Bathhouse Dinner and Tour - get a glimpse of the thermal bathing circuit, and enjoy an elegant dinner.
  • Ouachita Mountain ATV Tour - explore Ouachita Mountains on an all-terrain vehicle.
  • Ouachita River Cruise - enjoy a scenic cruise tour of Ouachita River.
  • Ouachita Art Trails - a collection of galleries and studios where you can see local artists working and buy their unique artworks and crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Ouachitas

Ouachitas is known for its unique blend of Southern and Appalachian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish - a regional favorite, served in many local restaurants.
  • Biscuits and Gravy - a savory and hearty breakfast dish made with buttermilk biscuits and sausage gravy.
  • Southern Pecan Pie - a rich, nutty pie that is a regional specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Arkansas Wine - the area is known for producing some exceptional wines, including muscadine, a sweet and fruity wine.
  • Sweet Tea - a Southern staple, sweet tea is a refreshing drink that has gained popularity across the country.
  • Local Beer - Ouachitas has several microbreweries that brew unique craft beers.

Where to Stay in Ouachitas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a budget-friendly place, Ouachitas has many accommodation options to choose from.

  • Arlington Resort Hotel and Spa - this historic hotel in downtown Hot Springs offers elegant rooms and world-class spa services.
  • Mena Mountain Resort - located on the Talimena Drive, this resort offers stunning views of Ouachita Mountains, and comfortable log cabins.
  • Red Roof Inn Hot Springs - a budget-friendly option near the downtown area.

What to Buy in Ouachitas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To take a piece of Ouachitas home with you, here are some souvenir ideas and local products:

  • Handcrafted Jewelry - made by local artisans using natural stones, crystals, and metals.
  • Homemade Jams and Jellies - made from local fruits and berries, these jams and jellies are a delicious way to remember your trip.
  • Arkansas Pine Crafts - locally made wooden crafts, including bowls, decorative items, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Ouachitas

Ouachitas has a unique blend of Southern and Appalachian cultures. Locals are known for their friendliness and hospitality, and visitors should expect a warm welcome wherever they go. In addition, the area has a rich history, and many museums and landmarks celebrate this heritage.

Best Time to Visit Ouachitas

The best time to visit Ouachitas is in the spring and fall, when the weather is pleasant, and the trees change colors. The summer months are also popular, but they can be hot and humid. In the winter, the area can experience snow and ice, which can impact accessibility to some of the outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ouachitas

Do:

  • Respect nature and local customs.
  • Be friendly and polite to locals.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and tours.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural habitats.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their culture.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing or attire.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ouachitas

Ouachitas is generally a safe area for travelers, but it’s always best to practice common sense and take precautions while exploring. Here are some safety tips:

  • Always lock your vehicles and secure your valuables.
  • Avoid walking alone in unlit and isolated areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay cautious in unfamiliar territory.
  • Stay up-to-date with weather conditions and be prepared for sudden changes.

Health in Ouachitas

Ouachitas has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and urgent care centers. Visitors should also ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before arriving.

Getting to Ouachitas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ouachitas

The closest airport to Ouachitas is Bill and Hillary Clinton National Airport (LIT) in Little Rock. The airport is approximately 60 miles from Hot Springs, one of the main destinations in the area.

Bus Stations Near Ouachitas

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in Hot Springs, Arkansas. Jefferson Lines buses also serve the Hot Springs location.

Other Options

Driving is the best way to get around the area, with several scenic drives offering stunning views and easy accessibility to several landmarks and attractions.

How to Get Around in Ouachitas

There are several ways to get around Ouachitas, including driving, biking and walking. Many hotels offer shuttle services to nearby attractions and activities.

Where to Go Next from Ouachitas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ouachitas is an excellent launching point for exploring other areas and attractions in Arkansas and surrounding states.

  • Little Rock - Arkansas’ capital city is located only 60 miles away and offers many historical sites, great food, and unique cultural attractions.
  • Dallas - located three hours away, Dallas is a vibrant city known for its shopping, dining, and arts and music scene.
  • Oklahoma City - located only four hours, Oklahoma City is a bustling metro known for its museums, parks, and cultural institutions.
