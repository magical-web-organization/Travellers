Introduction to Paris

Paris, located in the Ouachitas of Arkansas in the southern region of the United States of America in North America, is a charming town known for its outdoor adventures, natural beauty, and culturally significant attractions. Whether you’re a foodie, history buff, art lover, hiker, or just looking for a relaxing vacation, Paris offers something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you make the most of your trip to Paris.

List of Places to Visit in Paris

Paris boasts a range of attractions for visitors to see and experience. We have divided the top attractions into two categories: Culturally Important Attractions and Natural Attractions of Paris.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Paris is rich in history and culture, as evidenced by the many museums and historical places located in and around the town. Here are some of the top places to visit:

  • Eiffel Tower: One of the most iconic landmarks in Paris, the Eiffel Tower offers breathtaking views of the city and is a must-visit attraction.
  • Paris Historic District: Featuring historic homes and buildings dating back to the late 1800s and early 1900s, the Paris Historic District is a great place to explore the town’s history and architecture.
  • Logan County Museum: This museum offers exhibits and collections that highlight the history and culture of Logan County.
  • Mount Magazine State Park: With its stunning views of the surrounding mountains, Mount Magazine State Park is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers.
  • Mount Bethel Winery: Take a break from all the outdoor adventures to enjoy some delicious local wines at this winery.

Natural Attractions of Paris: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those looking to get outside and explore nature, Paris is the perfect destination. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in Paris:

  • Mount Magazine: The highest point in Arkansas, Mount Magazine offers some of the most stunning views in the state.
  • Mount Nebo State Park: Home to scenic overlooks, hiking trails, and campsites, Mount Nebo State Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Petit Jean State Park: This park offers opportunities for camping, hiking, fishing, and more, all amidst beautiful natural scenery.
  • Blue Mountain Lake: With clear waters and abundant fish, Blue Mountain Lake is a popular destination for fishing and boating.
  • Aux Arc Park: Featuring the Arkansas River, Aux Arc Park offers great opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and other water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paris

If hiking and biking are your passion, Paris won’t disappoint. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Paris:

  • Petit Jean State Park: With over 20 miles of hiking trails, Petit Jean State Park offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and exploring. The Cedar Falls Trail is a must-visit, as it leads to a stunning 95-foot waterfall.
  • Mount Magazine State Park: The Signal Hill Trail at Mount Magazine State Park is a popular route for biking, hiking, and horseback riding. Offering beautiful scenery and challenging terrain, this trail is ideal for experienced riders and hikers.
  • Sugar Creek Loop Trail: Located in Mount Nebo State Park, the Sugar Creek Loop Trail is a scenic trail that offers great views of the surrounding mountains and valley.

Best Things to Do in Paris: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, Paris offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Paris:

  • Paris Farmers Market: Open every Saturday from April to October, the Paris Farmers Market offers fresh produce, homemade goods, and crafts from local vendors.
  • Paris Art and Wine Walk: Taking place in June, the Paris Art and Wine Walk is a popular event that features wine tastings, live music, and local artists showcasing their work.
  • Pleasant Grove Trail Rides: Located near Paris, Pleasant Grove Trail Rides offers guided horseback tours through the Ouachita Mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Paris

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Paris, here’s a recommended itinerary to make the most of your visit:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Paris Historic District
Day 2 Visit Mount Magazine State Park
Day 3 Hike the Cedar Falls Trail at Petit Jean State Park
Day 4 Take a wine tour at Mount Bethel Winery

Day 7 Take a scenic drive through the Ouachita Mountains

Food and Drink Scene of Paris

No trip to Paris is complete without sampling the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paris Pickles: Made from cucumbers and onions, these pickles are a local favorite.
  • Fried Catfish: A staple in Southern cuisine, fried catfish is a must-try when in Paris.
  • Hog Jowls: A flavorful and unique dish, hog jowls are a traditional Southern appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Mount Bethel Winery’s Wine: The winery produces award-winning wines made from locally grown grapes.
  • Paris Coffee Roastery Coffee: Known for their handcrafted coffee, Paris Coffee Roastery is a great place to stop for a caffeine fix.
  • Paris Soda Shoppe Sodas: This local soda shop offers a variety of handcrafted sodas, including their famous root beer.

Where to Stay in Paris: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Paris offers a range of lodging options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Paris:

  • Days Inn by Wyndham Paris: Located near downtown Paris, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • The Lodge at Mount Magazine: Situated within Mount Magazine State Park, The Lodge offers stunning views and excellent dining options.
  • Montagne Center: This bed and breakfast inn features a relaxing atmosphere and comfortable rooms.
  • Paris RV Park: Ideal for campers and RVers, Paris RV Park offers full hookups and easy access to local attractions.
  • Budgetway Inn: Offering budget-friendly rooms, this hotel is great for travelers on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Paris: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Paris offers a range of souvenirs and local products to bring home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some of the best things to buy in Paris:

  • Paris Pickles: These locally made pickles are a great souvenir and a delicious snack.
  • Blue Mason Jars: These iconic blue jars are a great way to bring a piece of Paris home with you.
  • Mount Bethel Wine: A bottle of Mount Bethel wine is the perfect gift for wine enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Paris

Paris is rich in culture and tradition, and it is important for visitors to respect and embrace local customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Southern Hospitality: Paris is known for its warm and welcoming hospitality, so be prepared for friendly locals and excellent service.
  • Respectful Dress: When visiting churches or other religious sites, it’s recommended to dress modestly out of respect.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in Paris and is usually around 15-20% for restaurant service.

Best Time to Visit Paris

The best time to visit Paris is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing. Summer can be hot and busy, so expect crowds at popular attractions. Winter can be chilly, but the holiday season is a great time to see the town decorated for Christmas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paris

To ensure you have the best trip possible, here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Experience Local Cuisine: Don’t miss out on the delicious local food and beverages in Paris.
  • Plan for Outdoor Adventures: Paris offers beautiful natural scenery and a range of activities and tours to enjoy.
  • Visit Historical Sites: Paris is rich in history and culture, so be sure to visit the many museums and historical places in town.

Don’t:

  • Skip the Eiffel Tower: This iconic landmark is a must-visit attraction when in Paris.
  • Forget Sunscreen: When hiking or spending time outdoors, be sure to apply sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun.
  • Litter: Always dispose of trash properly and leave natural areas as you found them.

Safety and Health

As with any travel destination, it’s important to be aware of safety and health concerns when visiting Paris.

Safety in Paris

  • Wildlife Encounters: While hiking or exploring natural areas, be aware of local wildlife, including snakes and bears.
  • Be Aware of Weather Conditions: Paris can experience severe weather, including thunderstorms and tornadoes, so be sure to check the weather forecast before heading outdoors.

Health in Paris

  • Stay Hydrated: With hot temperatures in the summer months, it’s important to stay hydrated to avoid heat-related illnesses.
  • Wear Insect Repellent: When hiking or spending time outdoors, wear insect repellent to avoid ticks and other biting insects.

Getting to Paris: Transportation Options and Logistics

Paris is located in the southern region of Arkansas, making it easily accessible by car. Here are some transport options to consider:

Airports Near Paris

  • Fort Smith Regional Airport: Located about 70 miles south of Paris, this airport offers domestic flights to various locations.

Bus Stations Near Paris

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in nearby Russellville, this bus station offers routes throughout the country.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: With Paris located in a rural area, car rental is a popular option for getting around.
  • Taxi Service: Several local taxi services are available in Paris for convenient transportation.

How to Get Around in Paris

While having a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding areas, there are also options for getting around town:

  • Walking/Biking: Paris is a small town, making it easy to explore on foot or bike.
  • Car Rental: If you prefer driving, car rental is available in Paris.
  • Taxi Service: Local taxi services are also available for getting around town.

Where to Go Next from Paris: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Ouachita National Forest: Located nearby, Ouachita National Forest offers scenic hiking trails and outdoor adventures.
  • Hot Springs National Park: Home to hot springs and bathhouses, Hot Springs National Park is a great place to relax and unwind.
  • Little Rock: Arkansas’ capital city, Little Rock is a great place to explore history, culture, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Paris, Arkansas is a unique destination that offers a range of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you’re inspired to visit Paris and make the most of your trip. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Paris has something for everyone.
