Introduction to Pocahontas

Pocahontas is a scenic city nestled in the heart of Randolph County, Arkansas, in the southern region of the United States. The town is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals. Pocahontas is the perfect getaway destination for those looking to experience the charm of a small southern town while still enjoying the outdoors. It’s not only a great place to visit, but also a wonderful place to live.

List of Places to Visit in Pocahontas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Randolph County Heritage Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the history of Pocahontas and Randolph County through artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays.
  • Old Davidsonville State Park: This state park, located just outside of Pocahontas, is a must-visit for history buffs. It was once a thriving river port and is now an archaeological site with historic foundations and artifacts.
  • Davidsonville Historic State Park: Another historic site just outside of Pocahontas, this park features a beautiful 1830s courthouse, a historic tavern, and stunning views of the Black River.

Natural Attractions of Pocahontas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Black River: A beautiful river that flows through Pocahontas, perfect for kayaking, fishing, and swimming.
  • Rocky Creek Trail: A popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Ozark Mountains and surrounding forests.
  • Mud Creek Nature Trail: A scenic hiking and biking trail that meanders through the woods and along Mud Creek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pocahontas

Pocahontas is a great place to explore the great outdoors. Whether you prefer hiking or biking, there are plenty of options to suit your preferences. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Rocky Creek Trail: This 3.5-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking and offers stunning views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Mud Creek Nature Trail: This 2.5-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking and offers scenic views of the creek and surrounding forests.
  • Black River Trail: This 5-mile trail runs along the Black River and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding nature.

Best Things to Do in Pocahontas: Activities and Tours

  • Black River Outfitters: Take a guided tour down the Black River with Black River Outfitters and experience the beauty of Pocahontas from the water.
  • The Old Skillet: Enjoy a delicious meal at this iconic Pocahontas restaurant that has been serving up southern comfort food for over 45 years.
  • Randolph County Fair: Visit Pocahontas during the Randolph County Fair (held annually in September) and enjoy carnival rides, live music, and delicious fair food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pocahontas

Day 1: Explore the Black River on a guided tour with Black River Outfitters. Day 2: Visit the Randolph County Heritage Museum and learn about the history of Pocahontas and the surrounding area. Day 3: Hike the Rocky Creek Trail for stunning views of the Ozark Mountains. Day 4: Visit Old Davidsonville State Park and explore the historic foundations and archaeological artifacts. Day 5: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Pocahontas at one of the many parks and trails. Day 6: Enjoy southern comfort food at The Old Skillet. Day 7: Attend the Randolph County Fair (held annually in September) for carnival rides, live music, and delicious fair food.

Food and Drink Scene of Pocahontas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: Deep-fried and seasoned catfish is a local favorite and can be found at many restaurants in Pocahontas.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: A southern staple that is a must-try for breakfast lovers.
  • Pimento Cheese: A creamy and cheesy spread that is perfect for sandwiches.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A southern classic that is perfect for hot summer days.
  • Locally Brewed Beer: Pocahontas is home to several breweries that serve up delicious craft brews.

Where to Stay in Pocahontas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Days Inn & Suites: A comfortable hotel located just minutes from downtown Pocahontas.
  • Cedar Lodge Motel: A cozy motel with affordable rates and easy access to local attractions.
  • Airbnb: For a more unique and personalized experience, consider staying in an Airbnb in Pocahontas.

What to Buy in Pocahontas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Arkansas-made crafts and jewelry
  • Local honey
  • Pecans and other southern treats

Local Culture and Customs of Pocahontas

Pocahontas is known for its friendly residents and welcoming atmosphere. When visiting, be sure to embrace the southern hospitality and take time to explore the town’s rich history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Pocahontas

The best time to visit Pocahontas is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pocahontas

Do:

  • Be respectful of the town’s rich history and cultural traditions.
  • Embrace the Southern hospitality and kindness of the locals.
  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s speed limits and traffic laws.
  • Be disrespectful or rude to the locals.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pocahontas

Pocahontas is a safe and welcoming town. However, it is always important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Be sure to lock your car and secure your valuables, especially when visiting parks and other outdoor areas.

Health in Pocahontas

Pocahontas is home to several medical facilities, including the Five Rivers Medical Center and Saint Bernards Medical Center. Be sure to bring any necessary medication and consult with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Pocahontas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pocahontas

  • Jonesboro Municipal Airport: Located approximately 60 miles from Pocahontas.
  • Memphis International Airport: Located approximately 125 miles from Pocahontas.

Bus Stations Near Pocahontas

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in nearby Jonesboro.
  • Mammoth Spring Bus Stop: Serves several bus lines and is located approximately 30 miles from Pocahontas.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at both Jonesboro Municipal Airport and Memphis International Airport.
  • Taxis and Rideshare Services: Taxis and rideshare services are available throughout the area.

How to Get Around in Pocahontas

The best way to get around Pocahontas is by car. However, the town is small enough that walking is also a viable option.

Where to Go Next from Pocahontas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jonesboro, Arkansas: Located just 60 miles from Pocahontas, Jonesboro is a bustling college town with a lively arts and culture scene.
  • Little Rock, Arkansas: The capital city of Arkansas is just a few hours’ drive from Pocahontas and offers a wealth of history, culture, and entertainment.
  • Branson, Missouri: This popular vacation destination is just a couple hours’ drive from Pocahontas and offers world-class entertainment, shopping, and dining.

Conclusion

Pocahontas is a hidden gem in the southern United States, offering breathtaking natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality. With plenty of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious food, Pocahontas is the perfect getaway destination for travelers looking for a little bit of everything.
