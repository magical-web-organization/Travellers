Introduction to Lake Village

Located in Delta, Arkansas, Lake Village is a charming southern city that offers an authentic taste of the south. Home to a rich history, stunning landscapes, and southern hospitality, Lake Village offers an idyllic vacation destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit and things to do in Lake Village, Arkansas, to help you plan an unforgettable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Village

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lake Chicot State Park: Visit the oldest state park in Arkansas, featuring a 22-mile lake, campsites, trails, and picnic areas.

  • Lakeport Plantation: Explore the restored antebellum plantation house that offers a glimpse into the life of plantation owners and enslaved individuals.

  • Boyd House: Discover the oldest wooden structure in Lake Village, which was the former residence of the city’s founder.

Natural Attractions of Lake Village: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Chicot: Take a boat ride or a hike around the state’s largest natural lake, offering gorgeous views and abundant wildlife.

  • Mississippi River State Park: Explore the stunning landscapes of the Mississippi Delta region with hiking trails, camping sites, and water activities.

  • Delta Heritage Trail State Park: Discover the rich history and natural beauty of Arkansas with a scenic bike ride along the Delta Heritage Trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Village

Lake Village is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with its numerous biking and hiking trails. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Mississippi River Trail: Take a scenic bike ride along the Mississippi river, starting from Craighead Forest Park and ending at downtown Helena.

  • Lost Forty Trail: This 1.7-mile trail is perfect for hiking enthusiasts, with spectacular views of the forest canopy and native trees.

  • Lakeport Plantation Trail: Explore the lush forests and the cotton fields of Lakeport plantation on this 3-mile trail.

Best Things to Do in Lake Village: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Lake Village is a renowned fishing destination, with abundant species of fish like crappie, catfish, and bream. Visitors can hire guides for fishing tours in the nearby lakes and rivers.

  • Duck Hunting: Take a guided tour to experience the thrill of duck hunting in the Mississippi flyway.

  • Cultural Tours: Explore the rich cultural heritage of Lake Village with guided tours to plantations, museums, and historical places.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Village

Day 1: Arrive in Lake Village, check-in to a hotel, and take a leisurely stroll around Lake Chicot State Park.

Day 2: Go on a fishing trip in Lake Chicot and have a picnic lunch at the park.

Day 3: Explore the historic Boyd House and learn about the city’s beginnings.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of Lakeport Plantation and immerse yourself in the antebellum lifestyle.

Day 5: Take an adventurous bike ride along the Mississippi River Trail and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 6: Visit the Mississippi River State Park to experience the Delta’s diverse wildlife.

Day 7: Spend the day indoor, checking out local arts and crafts shops for some souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Village

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crawfish and Shrimp Boil: A classic Louisiana style dish, offers a perfect balance of spices, seafood, and boiled potatoes and corn.

  • Fried Catfish: A Southern staple, fried catfish served with hush puppies and coleslaw is a must-try.

  • BBQ Ribs: Juicy and tender pork ribs with a smoky flavor, served with mac and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple, sweet tea is a refreshing drink that complements any meal.

  • Margaritas: Head to the local bars to try Margaritas, a popular cocktail in Arkansas.

Where to Stay in Lake Village: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Days Inn Lake Village: Affordable and comfortable accommodation, located close to Lake Chicot State Park.

  • The Lakeport Plantation: Enjoy a historic stay at the restored antebellum plantation.

  • Delta Resort and Spa: Luxurious accommodation with excellent facilities and amenities, located near the Mississippi River.

What to Buy in Lake Village: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cotton Textiles: The region is famous for producing high-quality cotton, and visitors can purchase bed linens, towels, and other cotton products.

  • Handmade Crafts: Local craftsmen produce handmade quilts, baskets, pottery, and woodworks that make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Village

The southern hospitality, delicious cuisine, and rich history of Lake Village make it unique. The locals are friendly, and visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by attending cultural festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Lake Village

The best time to visit Lake Village is from March to May and September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the crowd size is manageable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Village

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and beverages to get a taste of the southern flavors.

  • Do take a guided tour to learn more about the region’s history and cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to check the weather before heading out for outdoor activities.

  • Don’t litter the natural surroundings, and respect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Village

Although Lake Village is a safe destination, visitors should take precautions while exploring the natural surroundings and remote areas.

Health in Lake Village

Visitors should carry insect repellent and take precautions against the mosquitoes and ticks in the region.

Getting to Lake Village: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Village

The nearest airport is Greenville Mid-Delta Airport (GLH), located in Greenville, Mississippi. The airport is around 25 miles from Lake Village.

Bus Stations Near Lake Village

Bus services are available from Memphis and Little Rock to Greenville, Mississippi, which is the nearest bus station to Lake Village.

Other Options

Lake Village is accessible by car, and visitors can rent cars from nearby cities like Memphis and Little Rock.

How to Get Around in Lake Village

The best way to get around Lake Village is by car, with rental cars and taxis available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Lake Village: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Greenville, Mississippi: Discover the blues music scene and explore the history of he Mississippi Delta region.

  • Memphis, Tennessee: Visit the music capital of the world and explore the iconic Beale Street.

Conclusion

Lake Village is a charming southern city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From outdoor adventures to cultural tours, there is something for everyone in Lake Village. Plan your vacation to Lake Village today and immerse yourself in the southern hospitality and rich culture of this charming city.
