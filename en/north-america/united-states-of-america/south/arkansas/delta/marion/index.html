Introduction to Marion

Located in the Delta region of Arkansas, Marion is a small but charming city with a rich history and a vibrant culture. It is the county seat of Crittenden County and has a population of around 12,000 people. Founded in the mid-19th century, Marion played a significant role in the American Civil War as the site of the Battle of Marion. Today, it is a bustling community that offers plenty of unique experiences for travelers, from exploring its museums and historic landmarks to enjoying the great outdoors. In this travel guide to Marion, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, local cuisine, and other things to see and do in this hidden gem of the South.

List of Places to Visit in Marion

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Crittenden County Museum: Learn about the city’s history, from its pre-Civil War era to the present day, through exhibits, photos, and artifacts.
  • Sultana Disaster Museum: Commemorating one of the deadliest maritime disasters in US history, the museum features displays and interactive exhibits.
  • The Heritage House Museum: This historic home turned museum offers visitors a glimpse into life in Marion during the early 20th century.
  • The Civil War Trail: Follow the trail to retrace the steps of Union and Confederate troops during the Battle of Marion.

Natural Attractions of Marion: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wapanocca National Wildlife Refuge: Hiking, birdwatching, and fishing opportunities are available within the 5,484-acre wildlife refuge.
  • Sultana Memorial Trail: Enjoy picturesque views of the Mississippi River as you hike or bike along this 18-mile trail.
  • Blackfish Lake: A serene retreat located within the expansive US Forest Service St. Francis National Forest, offering opportunities for fishing, camping, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marion

  • Rusty Relics Trail: A 2.2-mile trail that leads you through Marion’s historic downtown area, with stops at notable landmarks along the way.
  • Southland Park Gaming & Racing Trail: A 1.5-mile trail running alongside the Mississippi River and ending at Southland Park Gaming & Racing for some entertainment.

Best Things to Do in Marion: Activities and Tours

  • Southland Park Gaming & Racing: Featuring live greyhound racing, slot machines, and table games, this facility provides entertainment for visitors over 21 years of age.
  • Fireworks Extravaganza: Enjoy Marion’s renowned fireworks display during the annual 4th of July celebration at Patriot’s Park.
  • Crittenden County Fair: A family-friendly event that includes carnival rides, livestock exhibits, live music and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Marion

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried catfish: A classic Southern dish made with locally sourced catfish fillets, that are battered and fried.
  • BBQ Ribs: You can’t go wrong with a delicious slab of slow-cooked, smoky BBQ ribs served with southern-style sides.
  • Banana Pudding: A classic southern dessert made with sweet vanilla custard, whipped cream, and sliced bananas.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A refreshing southern drink made with brewed tea and plenty of sugar.
  • Muddy Water: A cocktail made with whiskey and the iconic Arkansas mud.

Where to Stay in Marion: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Marion: A popular chain hotel with comfortable rooms, fitness center, and indoor pool.
  • Comfort Inn Marion: An affordable option with free breakfast, on-site parking, and an outdoor pool.
  • Landers Roadhouse: A cozy bed and breakfast with beautiful gardens and personalized service.

What to Buy in Marion: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crittenden County memorabilia: Check out the gift shops at the museums to buy souvenirs ranging from postcards to tee shirts.
  • Delta-made crafts: Browse unique crafted souvenirs and artifacts made by locals who have lived in the Delta for generations.

Local Culture and Customs of Marion

The people of Marion are friendly, welcoming and have a strong connection with the neighboring Delta region. Respect for Southern hospitality, cuisine, and traditions are the cultural aspects that mean a lot to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Marion

Fall and Spring are considered the best seasons to visit Marion. Temperatures are pleasant, and the sights and colors are simply breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marion

Do:

  • Respect local culture and traditions: The people of Marion take pride in their unique Southern identity, so be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine: Indulge in the local cuisine and try the authentic southern dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter: The residents of Marion take pride in keeping the city clean and tidy. Respect their efforts by not littering.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marion

Marion is a safe city to travel to, just be mindful of your surroundings and follow basic safety precautions, such as locking your doors and gates when out and about.

Health in Marion

There are several urgent care centers and hospitals located throughout Marion, and travelers are advised to have health insurance before visiting the city.

Getting to Marion: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marion

  • Memphis International Airport (MEM): Located just 30 miles south of Marion, this airport provides daily non-stop flights to major cities in the US and around the world.

Bus Stations Near Marion

  • Memphis Central Station: Accessible through Amtrak and serviced by several bus lines.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies are located in Marion and nearby Memphis.

How to Get Around in Marion

Having a private vehicle is the best option to explore Marion, but travelers can also choose other options such as walking, biking, taxis, and ridesharing apps like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Marion: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Memphis, Tennessee: Just a half-hour drive from Marion, Memphis is known for its legendary music heritage, thriving food scene, and iconic landmarks like Graceland and Beale Street.
  • Little Rock, Arkansas: Less than two hours southwest of Marion, Little Rock is Arkansas’s largest city and the perfect destination for art, history, and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Hot Springs, Arkansas: A bit further, but definitely worth driving for, this picturesque city is best known for its natural hot springs and historic Bathhouse Row.

Conclusion

Marion may be a small city, but it boasts plenty of cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, and local flavors, making it a unique destination for travelers who are looking for an authentic Southern experience. By using this travel guide, now you’ve got a better idea of the best things to do and see in Marion, Arkansas, and the surrounding areas.
