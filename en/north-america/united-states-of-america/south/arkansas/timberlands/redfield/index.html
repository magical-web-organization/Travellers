Introduction to Redfield

Located in the heart of Timberlands, Redfield is a small town in Arkansas, United States, that’s often overlooked by tourists. But if you’re looking for a quaint and quiet escape from the city, Redfield is well worth the visit.

Not only does Redfield have a rich history dating back to the early 1900s, but it’s also surrounded by natural beauty that makes it an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking trails to scenic drives, Redfield has everything you need for an unforgettable trip.

In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top things to do in Redfield, the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Redfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, Redfield has several attractions that you won’t want to miss.

Redfield Depot Museum

Built in 1906, the Redfield Depot Museum is one of the oldest standing train depots in Arkansas. It’s been restored to look like it did in the early 1900s, complete with antique furniture and train memorabilia.

Redfield City Hall and Opera House

The Redfield City Hall and Opera House is a historic building that was built in 1923. It served as a city hall until the 1980s and is now used as a venue for community events and performances.

Natural Attractions of Redfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Redfield is surrounded by natural beauty, making it an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Bayou Meto Wildlife Management Area

The Bayou Meto Wildlife Management Area covers over 33,000 acres and is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, turkey, and waterfowl. Fishing, hunting, hiking, and wildlife viewing are popular activities here.

Old River Lake Wildlife Management Area

Old River Lake Wildlife Management Area is another popular spot for outdoor activities. It’s a 2,000-acre lake that’s perfect for fishing, kayaking, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redfield

If you’re looking to get some exercise and immerse yourself in nature, Redfield has several hiking and biking trails to choose from.

Tricia Keith Trail

The Tricia Keith Trail is a 2-mile trail that starts in Redfield City Park and winds through the forest. It’s a relatively easy hike, making it a great option for families.

Arkansas River Trail

The Arkansas River Trail is a 34-mile paved trail that runs along the Arkansas River. It’s perfect for biking, jogging, or walking, and it offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Redfield: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing, Redfield has a variety of other activities and tours to offer.

Redfield Farmers Market

Every Saturday, from May to October, Redfield hosts a farmers market where locals sell fresh produce, handmade crafts, and baked goods.

Redfield Vineyards and Winery

Redfield Vineyards and Winery is a family-owned winery that makes a variety of wines from locally grown grapes. They offer tours and tastings, and they also have a gift shop where you can purchase bottles to take home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Redfield

If you’re planning to spend a week in Redfield, we recommend the following itinerary.

Day Activity
1 Visit the Redfield Depot Museum and walk around the historic downtown area
2 Explore Bayou Meto Wildlife Management Area and go fishing or wildlife viewing
3 Take a scenic drive on the Arkansas River Trail
4 Go kayaking or birdwatching at Old River Lake Wildlife Management Area
5 Visit the Redfield City Hall and Opera House and attend a performance or event
6 Hike the Tricia Keith Trail and have a picnic in Redfield City Park
7 Visit Redfield Vineyards and Winery and enjoy a tasting

Food and Drink Scene of Redfield

Food and drink in Redfield is all about down-home Southern cooking and fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Catfish

Fried catfish is a classic Southern dish, and you can find some of the best at local restaurants like Jerry’s Fish House and Fish Net Family Restaurant.

Smoked Meat

If you’re a meat lover, you can’t leave Redfield without trying some of the famous smoked meat. Smokin’ Ember BBQ and Boudreaux’s Grill & Bar are both excellent options.

Local Beverages

Sassafras Tea

Sassafras tea is a local favorite made from the sassafras root. It’s a sweet, refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot summer days.

Craft Beer

Redfield is home to several craft breweries, including White River Brewing Company and Lost 40 Brewing. Stop by for a tasting and take home a few bottles as souvenirs.

Where to Stay in Redfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Although Redfield is a small town, there are several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Super 8 by Wyndham

Super 8 by Wyndham is a budget-friendly hotel that offers clean, comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

The Governor’s Inn Bed and Breakfast

For a more upscale experience, The Governor’s Inn Bed and Breakfast is a historic mansion that offers cozy rooms and a gourmet breakfast.

What to Buy in Redfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, Redfield has several options.

Arkansas Craft Guild

The Arkansas Craft Guild is a non-profit organization that promotes local artisans. They have a gift shop where you can purchase handmade pottery, jewelry, textiles, and more.

Redfield Antiques and Collectibles

Redfield Antiques and Collectibles is a great place to find unique vintage and antique items, including furniture, decor, and collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of Redfield

Redfield has a small-town, friendly feel, and locals are always happy to welcome visitors. Southern hospitality is prevalent here, so expect to be greeted with a smile wherever you go.

Best Time to Visit Redfield

The best time to visit Redfield is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its best. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold with occasional snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redfield

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the fried catfish and smoked meat.

  • Do visit the local winery and craft breweries.

  • Do explore the outdoor attractions, like the Bayou Meto Wildlife Management Area and the Arkansas River Trail.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public areas.

  • Don’t be rude to locals or other visitors.

  • Don’t ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redfield

Redfield is a safe town, and crime rates are low. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard safety precautions like keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Redfield

For medical emergencies, there is a hospital in nearby Pine Bluff, about a 15-minute drive from Redfield.

Getting to Redfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Redfield

The closest airport to Redfield is the Bill and Hillary Clinton National Airport, which is about a 35-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Redfield

There are several bus stations in nearby cities like Little Rock and Pine Bluff that offer transportation to Redfield.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Redfield is easily accessible from several major highways, including I-40 and I-530.

How to Get Around in Redfield

Redfield is a small town, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Redfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring the surrounding area, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider.

Little Rock

The capital city of Arkansas, Little Rock, is only a 25-minute drive from Redfield. There you can visit the Clinton Presidential Library, explore the River Market District, or take a stroll through the Arkansas Arboretum.

Hot Springs National Park

Hot Springs National Park is about a 2-hour drive from Redfield. There you can soak in the natural hot springs or hike the scenic trails.

Conclusion

Redfield is a hidden gem that’s well worth the visit. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures, or just want to enjoy some Southern hospitality and cuisine, Redfield has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and get ready to experience one of Arkansas’s best-kept secrets!
