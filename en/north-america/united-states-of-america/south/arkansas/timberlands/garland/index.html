Introduction to Garland

Garland is a charming town located in Timberlands, nestled in the southern part of Arkansas, USA. With a population of just over 2,500, Garland offers visitors a slice of small-town charm with all the amenities of a bustling city. The town is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, and there is always something to do, no matter what your interests are.

In this travel guide, we will give you insider tips on what to see and do in Garland, where to stay, and where to eat. We will also share some important information about the town’s culture, customs, and safety.

List of Places to Visit in Garland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Garland has a rich history and culture that visitors can explore through its museums and historical attractions.

The Garland County Historical Society Museum

The Garland County Historical Society Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. This museum houses exhibits that explore the local history of Garland and its impact on Arkansas’s development over the years. It also maintains an extensive archive of historical documents and photographs useful for research.

Oaklawn Racing Casino Resort

If you are interested in gambling or horse racing, the Oaklawn Racing Casino Resort is a perfect place to visit. The resort offers all kinds of entertainment, including a casino, horse racing, and eateries. The casino features hundreds of slot machines, table games, and a poker room that meet diverse gaming interests.

Natural Attractions of Garland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Garland has no shortage of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore.

Garvan Woodland Gardens

Garvan Woodland Gardens is an enchanting 210-acre botanical garden situated on Lake Hamilton. The gardens feature native plants, shrubs, trees, and seasonal flowers displayed in various gardens, including the Rose Garden, Asian Garden, and Rock and Sand Garden. Visitors will also enjoy hiking trails, waterfalls, and the stunning Anthony Chapel.

Lake Ouachita State Park

Lake Ouachita State Park is an excellent place for outdoor recreation, with plenty of activities available to enjoy year-round, such as fishing, swimming, boating, hiking, and wildlife observation. It’s also a great place to relax in one of the park’s many RV stalls and campsites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Garland

Garland’s natural scenery offers visitors the chance to explore the great outdoors with hiking and biking trails suitable for all skill levels.

Hot Springs Creek Greenway Trail

Hot Springs Creek Greenway Trail is a scenic three-mile paved path that follows the creek through the heart of downtown Garland. The trail is ideal for biking, walking, jogging, or rollerblading. Enjoy the fantastic views of the tree-lined shady area and the tranquil creek.

Ouachita National Forest

Ouachita National Forest offers miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails that traverse the rugged terrain. The forest has over 700 miles of trails, accessed from multiple entry points, making it easy to find one that suits your level of expertise.

Best Things to Do in Garland: Activities and Tours

Garland’s diverse attractions and natural beauty offer visitors a range of experiences to enjoy.

Mountain Harbor Resort

Mountain Harbor Resort is an excellent place to relax and unwind for families with chidren of all ages. Visitors will enjoy an array of activities offered at the resort such as fishing, kayaking, tennis courts, biking, hiking or swimming along Little Mazarn Creek.

Maxwell Blade Theatre of Magic

If you’re looking for something unusual, Maxwell Blade Theatre of Magic is the premier magic theatre show in Garland and one of the town’s top tourist destinations. The performance features a combination of illusions, comedy, and music, making it a great night out for all ages.

Food and Drink Scene of Garland

Garland is known for its southern-style cuisine and eating-out culture.

Must-Try Dishes

McClard’s BBQ

For some finger-licking-good barbecue, head to McClard’s BBQ, a family-owned business since the 1920s. McClard’s BBQ combines classic barbecue recipes with unique house-made barbecue sauces that make your taste buds dance in happiness.

501 Prime

501 Prime is a high-end steakhouse serving up premium cuts of beef and seafood. The restaurant has a stylish, upscale ambiance, perfect for a dinner-date night.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer From Superior Bathhouse Brewery and Distillery

Superior Bathhouse Brewery and Distillery is located in the heart of Hot Springs National Park. It’s the only brewery in the world that gives hot spring mineral water beers. Whether you are a craft beer enthusiast or just love unique experiences, the brewery is an ideal stop while in Garland.

Where to Stay in Garland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Garland offers a variety of accommodations, suitable for different budgets and preferences.

The Waters Hotel

The Waters Hotel is located downtown and combines sophistication with a charming historic feel. The boutique hotel’s guest rooms feature upscale decor and modern amenities for a comfortable and unforgettable stay in Garland.

Holiday Inn Express & Suites

Holiday Inn Express & Suites is a popular hotel near Lake tech that caters to business travellers and family vacationers. The hotel offers an indoor pool, free Wi-Fi, a fitness center, and a hot breakfast that make it a great place to relax while exploring the area’s attractions.

What to Buy in Garland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Garland without taking home some souvenirs that remind you of your trip.

Bathhouse Soapery

Bathhouse Soapery is a local store that creates handmade natural soaps, shampoos, and body care products. The store’s scents are inspired by the hot springs destination and the natural beauty of Garland.

Local Culture and Customs of Garland

Garland’s culture and customs have a southern charm and hospitality that will make you feel at home away from home. The town keeps the southern hospitality traditions alive and is friendly to visitors and residents alike.

Best Time to Visit Garland

The best time to visit Garland is during the spring and fall when the weather is cooler and the crowds are thinner. Spring is an excellent time to see the flowers in bloom, and the fall foliage is breathtaking in October and November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Garland

Do:

  • Do attend one of the many events held in Garland throughout the year.
  • Do enjoy the food and drinks scene, from BBQ to craft beer.
  • Do respect the locals and their customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter! Please keep the parks and outdoor areas clean for everyone’s enjoyment.
  • Don’t miss a trip to the downtown for shopping and culture.
  • Don’t forget to hike/bike the scenic trails.

Safety and Health

Garland is generally safe and healthy to travel to, but visitors should take some necessary precautions.

Safety in Garland

  • Always be cautious. Do not leave valuables in plain sight in vehicles.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid areas that seem suspect.
  • Keep an eye on the weather forecast, particularly during tornado season.

Health in Garland

  • Visitors should drink plenty of water, particularly during the summer season.
  • Apply the right sunscreen while outdoors in the sun for extended periods.
  • Carry insect repellent and protect yourself from mosquito bites.

Getting to Garland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Garland is easy to reach with several transportation options at visitors’ disposal.

Airports Near Garland

  • Bill and Hillary Clinton National Airport (LIT) is the nearest primary airport to Garland, and about a 45-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Garland

  • The Greyhound Bus Terminal serves the Garland area and nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Rental cars and taxis are readily accessible in Garland.

How to Get Around in Garland

The best way to get around Garland is by renting a car. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Garland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Garland is situated in the heart of Arkansas, with several fantastic destinations within reach for a day trip or weekend getaway.

Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art

Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art is a must-visit attraction in Bentonville. It’s home to an impressive collection of American artworks, including works by Warhol, Pollock, and Rockwell. The museum’s unique architecture is also worth paying attention to.

Hot Springs National Park

Hot Springs National Park is just a 20-minute drive from Garland, making it an easy day-trip destination. The park has several natural hot springs that visitors can bathe in or drink from making it a more comfortable nature experience.

Conclusion

Garland is a charming town that offers visitors a fantastic combination of history, culture, and natural beauty. With the town’s rich heritage, excellent dining scene, and diverse range of attractions, there is plenty of activities and sights to see and experience for visitors of all ages. Plan your trip well, and it’s guaranteed to be an unforgettable experience.
