Introduction to Alma

Welcome to Alma, a charming small town located in the heart of the River Valley region of Arkansas. Known for its natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals, Alma is a great destination for travelers looking to explore a quieter, more laid-back side of the South. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or just relaxing, Alma has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Alma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alma Performing Arts Center: This state-of-the-art center hosts a range of performances, from live music to stage productions.
  • Drennen-Scott Historic Site: This restored 1836 home and grounds offers a look back into the history of the region.
  • Alma City Museum: Learn about Alma’s fascinating past, including its role in the Trail of Tears and the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Alma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alma City Lake: This beautiful lake offers boating, fishing, and picnicking opportunities.
  • Crawford County Conservation District: Hiking trails and scenic views abound in this natural area.
  • Mulberry River: Go for a swim, take a scenic drive, or go kayaking down this picturesque river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alma

  • Alma Aquatic Park Trail: This 2.5-mile trail offers a beautiful view of the Alma Aquatic Park and a chance to see local wildlife.
  • Mulberry River Trail: Follow this trail along the river and through scenic wooded areas.
  • Frisco Trail: This 7-mile trail follows the historic Frisco railway and offers beautiful views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Alma: Activities and Tours

  • Alma Scenic Drive: Take in the beauty of the River Valley on a leisurely drive through the area.
  • Mountainburg Railroad Historical Museum: Learn about the history of the railway and the role it played in the development of the region.
  • Alma Farmers Market: Browse local produce and goods at this weekly farmers market.

Food and Drink Scene of Alma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Okra: A Southern staple, fried okra is a must-try dish in Alma.
  • Barbecue: Get your fill of slow-cooked meats and tangy, smoky sauces at one of the many barbecue joints around town.
  • Pecan Pie: Satisfy your sweet tooth with a slice of this classic Southern dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: Cool off with a glass of sweet tea, a Southern favorite.
  • Craft Beer: Check out the local brewery, Ozark Beer Company, for a taste of the region’s best craft beer.
  • Arkansas Wine: Sip on a glass of Arkansas wine at Post Winery, located just a short drive from Alma.

Where to Stay in Alma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Comfort Inn: A comfortable, affordable option with easy access to the highway and local sights.
  • Alma Heritage Inn Bed & Breakfast: Stay in style at this historic bed and breakfast, located right in the heart of town.
  • Mulberry Mountain Lodging and Events: For a more unique experience, book a stay in one of their yurts, cabins, or tipis.

What to Buy in Alma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Honey: Stock up on local honey, a delicious souvenir and great gift.
  • Crafts: Check out the many local craft fairs and markets for unique, hand-crafted gifts.
  • Ozark Stone Jewelry: These one-of-a-kind pieces made from local stones make for a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Alma

Alma is a friendly, welcoming town, so be prepared to make some new friends during your visit. Southern hospitality is alive and well in Alma, so don’t be surprised if locals strike up a conversation with you.

Best Time to Visit Alma

The best time to visit Alma is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alma

Do:

  • Do try local barbecue and other Southern specialties.
  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of the River Valley.
  • Do visit local museums and historical sites to learn about the region’s past.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals.
  • Don’t forget to try some local honey or other unique local products.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alma

Alma is a safe town, but as with any destination, it’s always important to remain aware of your surroundings and take precautions to protect your valuables.

Health in Alma

There are several medical facilities in Alma, including a hospital and multiple clinics, should you require medical attention during your visit.

Getting to Alma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alma

  • Fort Smith Regional Airport: Located just 20 miles from Alma.

Bus Stations Near Alma

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The nearest Greyhound station is located in Fort Smith, about 20 miles from Alma.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: A rental car is a great way to explore the River Valley at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Alma

The best way to get around Alma is by car. Many of the local sites and attractions are spread out, so having your own transportation is highly recommended.

Where to Go Next from Alma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Devil’s Den State Park: Just a short drive from Alma, Devil’s Den offers hiking trails, camping, and stunning views.
  • Fort Smith: Located just 20 miles from Alma, Fort Smith offers museums, historic sites, and a bustling downtown area.
  • Ozark National Forest: Explore the natural beauty of the region with a visit to the Ozark National Forest.

Conclusion

Alma, Arkansas offers a fascinating glimpse into Southern life and an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the River Valley. With something to offer everyone, Alma is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to get off the beaten path and experience a different side of the South.
