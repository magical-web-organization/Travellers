Introduction to Fort Smith

Nestled in River Valley, in Arkansas, United States of America, Fort Smith is a historic and scenic destination that attracts visitors from all over the world. The city was established as a military outpost in 1817 and played a significant role in American history as a gateway to the Wild West. Today, Fort Smith is an ideal location to discover the legacy of the Old West and appreciate the natural beauty of the region.

Whether you are visiting Fort Smith for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation, this comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your itinerary. From culturally significant museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Fort Smith has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Smith

Below is a list of must-visit places in Fort Smith that will leave you awestruck with its beauty and history.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Fort Smith National Historic Site: Discover the history of a military outpost and learn about the law and order of the Wild West with an interactive exhibit. Don’t miss the the Trail of Tears exhibit that focuses on the forcible removal of Native Americans from their lands to Oklahoma.

  2. Fort Smith Museum of History: Explore the rich history of Fort Smith and the surrounding areas with a collection of artifacts, photographs, and archives.

  3. The Clayton House: Built in 1882, this restored Victorian mansion is now a museum and represents the life of William Henry Harrison Clayton, a prominent lawyer, and congressman of Arkansas.

Natural Attractions of Fort Smith: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Janet Huckabee Arkansas River Valley Nature Center: A paradise for nature lovers, the center offers exhibits, trails, and activities for learning and recreational purposes.

  2. Ben Geren Regional Park: Enjoy a day out with family with stunning views and a range of activities including golf, frisbee golf, cricket, and volleyball.

  3. Massard Prairie Battlefield Park: This battlefield park preserves the site of a Civil War conflict allowing visitors to walk the trails and learn about the battle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Smith

  1. Riverfront Trail: A verdant trail along the Arkansas River with plenty of scenic views.

  2. The Lake Loop: Ideal for hiking, mountain biking, and trail running, and here it’s possible to see the wildlife and vegetation native to River Valley.

  3. Park at West End: With three different skill levels, this biking trail is ideal for riders of all ages and experience levels.

Best Things to Do in Fort Smith: Activities and Tours

  1. Fort Smith Trolley Museum: Hop-on an old streetcar and enjoy a tour of downtown Fort Smith with historic landmarks.

  2. Live Music Scene: Fort Smith hosts many events such as Old Fort Days Rodeo, Fort Smith Riverfront Blues Festival, and many regional and national music artists to showcase its rich musical heritage.

  3. Painting with a Twist: Enjoy a creative and relaxing experience with friends, family, or colleagues.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Smith

Day 1 - Explore Fort Smith National Historic Site and the Trail of Tears exhibit

Day 2 - Visit the Fort Smith Museum of History and Clayton House

Day 3 - Spend a day at Janet Huckabee Arkansas River Valley Nature Center

Day 4 - Take a Bike ride in the Ben Geren Regional Park

Day 5 - Hike the Lake Loop

Day 6 - Attend an old Fort Days Rodeo or Fort Smith Riverfront Blues Festival

Day 7 - Relax with Painting with a Twist or enjoy an Arkansas-themed dinner

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Smith

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried Catfish: A Southern-style staple, Fort Smith serves the best fresh Catfish in the region.

  2. Chicken Fried Steak: A classic comfort dish, you can find the best Chicken Fried Steak in town at Dixie Cafe.

Local Beverages

  1. Fort Smith Brewing Company: Get a taste of locally brewed beer and enjoy the atmosphere with friends and family at Fort Smith Brewing Company.

  2. Sweet Bay Coffee: A cozy and welcoming environment to enjoy coffee, tea, and other beverages in Fort Smith.

Where to Stay in Fort Smith: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Home2 Suites by Hilton Fort Smith: Offers spacious rooms equipped with modern amenities such as refrigerator, microwave, and stove.

  2. Days Inn & Suites by Wyndham Fort Smith: A budget-friendly option for visitors who prioritize convenience, this hotel is located close to Fort Smith airport and other attractions.

  3. Inn Towne Lodge: A hidden gem in Fort Smith with clean and cozy rooms, perfect for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Fort Smith: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Arkansas Wine: Vineyards located around Fort Smith produce some of the state’s best wines.

  2. Artwork: Visit local galleries to buy unique and innovative artwork to add to your collection.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Smith

Fort Smith takes pride in its heritage and punctuality. Locals are friendly and welcoming, and it is common to exchange greetings and compliments at short meetings and mini-holidays.

Best Time to Visit Fort Smith

The best time to visit Fort Smith is from March to May when the temperature is pleasant, and the flowers are in full bloom. Moreover, Autumn is another great option to plan a trip to Fort Smith and witness colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Smith

Do:

  1. Greet the locals with a smile and enjoy their hospitality.

  2. Try new foods, explore wildlife, and scenic views.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or pollute any of the natural attractions in Fort Smith.

  2. Be disrespectful to locals and their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Smith

Fort Smith is a safe destination and has a low crime rate, but visitors should be cautious and take their belongings after their spending day out.

Health in Fort Smith

Fort Smith has a well-functioning healthcare system, and travelers and visitors can rely on them for medical emergencies. However, you must consult with your medical expert before traveling and don’t forget your medication if needed.

Getting to Fort Smith: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Smith

Fort Smith Regional Airport is the closest airport to town and located just five miles away.

Bus Stations Near Fort Smith

Greyhound operates routes and stops in Fort Smith.

Other Options

Car rentals and Ubers are also available to move around the town.

How to Get Around in Fort Smith

The best way to explore Fort Smith is by car or on bike, public transportation in Fort Smith is not very efficient and frequent.

Where to Go Next from Fort Smith: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Devil’s Den State Park: An ultimate natural relaxation and adventure spot, located just 30 miles south of Fort Smith.

  2. Eureka Springs: A beautiful Victorian-era nation gem points situated approximately 80 miles from Fort Smith.

Conclusion

Fort Smith, Arkansas, is an excellent vacation destination, packed with natural beauty, historic sites, and attractions for visitors to enjoy. This comprehensive guide has everything you need to know to plan your trip to Fort Smith, from the best places to stay and eat to the top things to see and do, ensuring that you experience the best of River Valley’s beautiful location.
