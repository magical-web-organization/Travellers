Introduction to Van Buren

Van Buren is a charming city located in the heart of the River Valley, in Arkansas, South USA. It’s a small town with a big personality. Historically significant and rich in culture, Van Buren is full of interesting attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious food and drink options. With friendly locals, a relaxed atmosphere, and plenty of things to see and do, Van Buren is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the area.

List of Places to Visit in Van Buren

If you’re planning a trip to Van Buren, here is a list of places you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • King Opera House: Built in 1880, the King Opera House is a beautifully restored theater in the heart of downtown Van Buren. Catch a show or take a tour of this iconic landmark.
  • Van Buren Historic District: Take a stroll through the Van Buren Historic District and admire the picturesque Victorian buildings that line the streets. It’s like stepping back in time.
  • Drennen-Scott Historic Site: Visit the Drennen-Scott Historic Site to learn about the role Van Buren played in the Civil War. This museum and cultural center offers a glimpse into the city’s fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Van Buren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Fort Smith State Park: Just 20 miles from Van Buren, this beautiful state park offers hiking trails, fishing, camping, and stunning views of the Ozark Mountains.
  • Lee Creek Reservoir: If you’re looking for a serene spot to go fishing or enjoy a picnic, be sure to visit Lee Creek Reservoir. It’s a beautiful spot to soak up the natural beauty of Van Buren.
  • Chad Colley Boulevard Trail: This biking and hiking trail winds through the heart of Van Buren and offers scenic views of the city’s historic architecture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Van Buren

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Van Buren is the perfect destination for you. From easygoing hikes to challenging bike rides, here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Butterfield Hiking Trail: This scenic 15-mile trail winds through the stunning Ozark National Forest and includes views of beautiful waterfalls and rock formations.
  • Chad Colley Boulevard Trail: As mentioned above, this paved trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or afternoon stroll.
  • Lee Creek Reservoir Trail: This 4.5-mile hiking and biking trail encircles Lee Creek Reservoir and offers beautiful views of the water and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Van Buren: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring the city’s many attractions, here are some other things to do in Van Buren:

  • Van Buren Ghost Walk: Take a spooky tour through downtown Van Buren and hear tales of the city’s haunted past.
  • Crawford County Fair: Held annually in September, the Crawford County Fair is a fun-filled celebration of the area’s agricultural heritage.
  • Breeden’s Orchard: Visit this charming orchard to pick your own apples, enjoy a scenic hayride, or sample some delicious apple cider.

Week-Long Itinerary for Van Buren

If you’re planning to spend a week in Van Buren, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

1 Explore the Van Buren Historic District

7 End your trip with a relaxing day at Lake Fort Smith State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Van Buren

No trip to Van Buren is complete without sampling some of the city’s delicious food and drink options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: The Ozarks are known for their delicious barbecue, and Van Buren is no exception. Be sure to try the pulled pork at Crawdad’s Patio Grill or the ribs at Rib Room Bar-B-Que.
  • Catfish: For a true taste of the South, head to the Catfish Hole for some crispy fried catfish.
  • Mexican cuisine: Van Buren has a thriving Mexican food scene, and you won’t want to miss the delicious fare at La Huerta or El Taco Loco.

Local Beverages

  • Ozark Beer Company: This local microbrewery offers a variety of delicious craft beers.
  • Wiederkehr Wine Cellars: Take a short drive to nearby Wiederkehr Wine Cellars for a tasting of their award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Van Buren: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a charming guesthouse, there are plenty of great options for lodging in Van Buren. Here are a few top picks:

  • Mount Magazine Lodge: This scenic lodge is located just a short drive from Van Buren and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Hampton Inn Van Buren: If you’re looking for a comfortable and convenient hotel option, the Hampton Inn Van Buren is a great choice.
  • Cedar House Inn & Yurts: For a unique and memorable stay, book a yurt at Cedar House Inn. These eco-friendly accommodations are situated on a beautiful farm and offer a relaxing retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city.

What to Buy in Van Buren: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Van Buren, here are some great options:

  • Handmade Jewelry: Shop for beautiful handmade jewelry at Angie’s Jewelry & Gifts or Originals by Kathy.
  • Antiques: Explore the many antique shops in Van Buren for a chance to discover vintage treasures.
  • Fresh Produce: Visit the Van Buren Farmers Market for fresh, locally grown produce to bring home and enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Van Buren

Van Buren is known for its warm and welcoming culture. Locals are friendly and always happy to share their love for the city with visitors. When visiting Van Buren, it’s important to respect the area’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Van Buren

The best time to visit Van Buren is during the spring or fall months. The weather is mild, and the city’s outdoor attractions are at their most beautiful during these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Van Buren

When visiting Van Buren, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Take a stroll through the Van Buren Historic District.
  • Visit the Drennen-Scott Historic Site to learn about the area’s Civil War history.
  • Spend a day hiking or biking the scenic trails in and around Van Buren.
  • Try the delicious BBQ and Mexican food in town.
  • Bring home some unique souvenirs and locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the area’s cultural heritage.
  • Skimp on the sunscreen and bug spray when exploring outdoor attractions.
  • Forget to tip your servers at restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

When visiting Van Buren, it’s important to stay safe and healthy. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Van Buren

  • Always lock your car and remove valuables when parking.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when exploring unfamiliar areas.
  • Follow all safety guidelines when hiking, biking, or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Van Buren

  • Be sure to stay hydrated during hot weather.
  • Apply sunscreen regularly to avoid sunburn.
  • Protect yourself against ticks and other insects when hiking and exploring outdoor areas.

Getting to Van Buren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Van Buren is easily accessible by car or bus. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Van Buren

  • Fort Smith Regional Airport (FSM): Located just 15 miles from Van Buren, Fort Smith Regional Airport offers flights to several major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Van Buren

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound operates a bus station in nearby Fort Smith, which is just a short drive from Van Buren.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car at the Fort Smith Regional Airport or in downtown Fort Smith.

How to Get Around in Van Buren

Van Buren is a small city, and many attractions are within walking distance. However, if you prefer to drive, there are several rental car options available in town.

Where to Go Next from Van Buren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the River Valley area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Fort Smith: Just a short drive from Van Buren, Fort Smith is home to several interesting museums, including the Fort Smith National Historic Site.
  • Oklahoma City: If you’re up for a longer road trip, head to Oklahoma City to explore the city’s vibrant arts scene and delicious food options.
  • Hot Springs: Known for its natural hot springs and soothing spas, Hot Springs is a great destination for a relaxing day trip.

Conclusion

Van Buren is a hidden gem in the River Valley region. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or simply enjoying delicious food and drink, this charming city has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that Van Buren has to offer!
