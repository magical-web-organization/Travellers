Introduction to Ridgeland

Welcome to Ridgeland, a charming town located in Golden Corner, South Carolina. This hidden gem is a perfect holiday destination for families, couples, and solo travelers who want to explore the natural beauty of South Carolina. Ridgeland offers a perfect combination of history, culture, and outdoor adventures that will make your trip unforgettable. In this travel guide, we provide you with an in-depth understanding of the town, from the best places to visit to the local customs and tips to make your stay enjoyable.

List of Places to Visit in Ridgeland

Ridgeland offers a variety of attractions and activities to explore. Some of the top places to add to your list are:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Morris Center for Lowcountry Heritage
  • Sergeant Jasper County Park
  • Blue Heron Nature Center
  • Zion Cemetery and Baynard Mausoleum

Natural Attractions of Ridgeland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jasper County Farmers Market
  • Great Swamp Sanctuary
  • Cypress Wetlands
  • Tillman Sand Ridge Heritage Preserve

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ridgeland

Ridgeland offers an abundance of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the popular trails to explore are:

  • Blueway Trail
  • Jasper County Linear Trail
  • Sargeant Jasper Park Trail

Best Things to Do in Ridgeland: Activities and Tours

Ridgeland has numerous activities and tours for tourists of all ages and interests. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Blue Collards & Cocktails
  • Adventure’s Rentals of Hilton Head
  • May River Excursions
  • Salt Marsh Discovery

Week-Long Itinerary for Ridgeland

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ridgeland, consider the following itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Lowcountry Heritage Trail and Morris Public Library

Day 2: Tour Sargeant Jasper Park and lunch at Blue Heron Nature Center

Day 3: Take a ride on the Lowcountry Driving Tour and visit KingsTree Village

Day 4: Visit the Jasper County Farmers Market and then enjoy a hike along the Cypress Wetlands

Day 5: Rent a Kayak from May River Excursions

Day 6: Visit the Frazier Memorial Gardens and enjoy the beautiful views

Day 7: End the trip with a bike ride around the Jasper County Linear Trail

Local Culture and Customs of Ridgeland

Ridgeland has a friendly, welcoming culture that visitors should embrace. Some essential local customs to remember include:

  • Saying “yes, ma’am” and “no sir” to elders
  • Being respectful of the southern traditions and customs
  • Embracing the hospitality of the people

Best Time to Visit Ridgeland

The best time to visit Ridgeland is in the spring and fall seasons. The weather is comfortable, and there are plenty of outdoor activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ridgeland

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts of Ridgeland:

Do:

  • Make use of public spaces and parks
  • Take time to appreciate the rural scenery
  • Enjoy Southern cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas
  • Make negative comments about the southern culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Ridgeland

Ridgeland is generally safe for tourists. However, it is advisable to take necessary precautions, such as walking in well-lit areas and avoiding isolated spots at night.

Health in Ridgeland

You should take safety measures against mosquito bites and stay hydrated to avoid heat exhaustion while exploring Ridgeland’s natural attractions.
