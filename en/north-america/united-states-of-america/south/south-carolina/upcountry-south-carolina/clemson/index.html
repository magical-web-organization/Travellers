Introduction to Clemson

Located in Upcountry South Carolina, Clemson is a small university town that attracts tourists due to its picturesque surroundings, rich culture, and outdoor activities. It is home to Clemson University, a top-ranked public research institution, which has a significant influence on the town’s unique character and cultural offerings. In this comprehensive tour guide to Clemson, we will explore the top attractions and things to do and see in and around this charming town. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Clemson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Hill Plantation: Once the home of John C. Calhoun, Fort Hill Plantation is now part of Clemson University and open for tours. Visitors can learn about the history of the region, the life of Calhoun, and the African American history of the area.
  • Hanover House: This historical house is located on the National Register of Historic Places and is a museum of domestic life in the late 19th century.
  • Bob Campbell Geology Museum: This museum is another attraction located on the Clemson University campus that features displays of geological formations and fossils.

Natural Attractions of Clemson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Hartwell: Clemson is situated at the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains and provides easy access to Lake Hartwell, a popular spot for water sports such as boating, kayaking, and swimming. The lake’s marinas, boat ramps, and picnic areas make it an ideal spot for a day trip.
  • South Carolina Botanical Garden: This beautiful garden is spread over 295 acres and features diverse habitats, flowers, and plants, as well as a Butterfly Garden, Children’s garden, and Wildflower meadow. Guided tours and educational programs are also offered year-round.
  • Issaqueena Falls and Stumphouse Tunnel: Issaqueena Falls, located just outside Clemson, is a picturesque waterfall with a height of 200 feet and is a popular hiking spot. Visitors can also explore Stumphouse Tunnel, a 1,600-foot-long historic railway tunnel that is partially completed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clemson

  • Table Rock State Park: This state park is located 30 miles from Clemson, is a popular spot for hiking and offers picturesque views, trails of varying lengths, and adventurous routes suitable for beginners as well as experienced hikers.
  • Clemson Experimental Forest: This forest is ideal for biking and has several trails ranging from easy to hard difficulty levels.
  • The Doodle Trail: This bike and walking trail is a converted railway line and offers scenic views while traversing through the town’s neighborhoods, old railways, and lakes.

Best Things to Do in Clemson: Activities and Tours

  • Death Valley: Clemson is proud of its football stadium, nicknamed Death Valley and rightly so; with its seating capacity of 81,500 people, it is one of the largest on-campus football stadiums in the country, and it is a must-do tour while visiting the campus.
  • Clemson’s Farmer Market: This market is a chance to meet locals, grab a bite, and buy fresh produce and handicrafts. It is open every Tuesday through from May until September, providing an insight into the town’s friendly and welcoming community.
  • Sipping & Strolling Wine Tour: This custom tour of three Upcountry South Carolina vineyards offers a chance to tour boutique vineyards, sample wine, and enjoy a scenic drive through the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clemson

Day 1: Arrival+ exploring Clemson University Campus, Death Valley Tour. Day 2: Clemson’s Farmer Market, South Carolina Botanical Garden tour, exploring the Fort Hill Plantation. Day 3: Issaqueena Falls and Stumphouse Tunnel, Table Rock State Park (hiking). Day 4: Bob Campbell Geology Museum, biking at Clemson Experimental Forest. Day 5: Sipping & Strolling Wine Tour. Day 6: Lake Hartwell (water sports, picnic), exploring Hanover House. Day 7: Relaxation day.

Food and Drink Scene of Clemson

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Smokin’ Pig: This BBQ spot, located in nearby Pendleton, is a fan favorite and is known for its mouth-watering pulled pork, ribs, and smoked turkey.
  • BGR Burger Joint: A local hotspot, BGR is perfect for when you’re craving something savory and filling. Their burgers are hand-ground, grilled to order, and served on a locally made bun.
  • The Chocolate Moose: A local gem, The Chocolate Moose is a must-visit for sweets lovers. They have 15 flavors of homemade ice cream, cakes, pies, and cookies.

Local Beverages

  • Carolina Bauernhaus Brewery and Winery: This charming brewery and winery is a must-visit for anyone who enjoys craft beer and wine. They specialize in sour and fruit beers and use locally sourced ingredients.
  • Wine House at Lake Keowee: The Wine House offers an extensive selection of wine, craft beer, and spirits as well as curated charcuterie boards, making it an ideal spot for an evening out.
  • All In Coffee Shop: This trendy coffee shop serves locally roasted coffee beans alongside a unique selection of baked goods, sandwiches, breakfast foods, and teas.

Where to Stay in Clemson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Abernathy: A boutique, stylish hotel situated in the heart of downtown Clemson, The Abernathy offers 41 beautifully appointed guest rooms and suites.
  • Hampton Inn Clemson: The Hampton Inn Clemson provides comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast daily. Perfect for families and groups, it’s located just minutes from Clemson University.
  • The Inn at Patrick Square: This luxury inn located just outside Clemson features 30 well-appointed guestrooms and suites with contemporary furnishings. Guests can enjoy complimentary breakfast, fitness facilities, and 24-hour reception and security.

What to Buy in Clemson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Clemson University Merchandise: A visit to Clemson is incomplete without picking up a memento or two from the university’s souvenir shop. From t-shirts, water bottles, hats, and license plates to other knick-knacks, there’s something for everyone.
  • Pedal Chic: If you’re a biking enthusiast, stop by Pedal Chic for cycling shirts, shorts, hand-made cycling caps, and bandanas.
  • All In Coffee Shop: Their ‘All In’ mugs are the perfect souvenir to bring back home and remind you of your favorite coffee shop back in Clemson.

Local Culture and Customs of Clemson

Clemson is a small, friendly town known for its southern hospitality and is incredibly proud of its sports team, Clemson Tigers. Clemson residents are known to be very supportive of their local community; when you visit, you’re sure to experience the town’s warm welcome. Clemson is also a place steeped in history, and visitors will encounter different traditions, architectural styles, ethnicities, and cultures that intermingled in the region over time.

Best Time to Visit Clemson

Fall would be the best time to visit Clemson if you enjoy cooler weather. The autumn months in Clemson are usually sunny and have perfect temperatures for outdoor activities such as hiking and touring the campus. The town’s football season also begins in September, attracting thousands of sports lovers. Clemson’s farmers’ markets, fall festivals, and events during this time of year, make it the perfect season to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clemson

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do bring comfortable clothes for outdoor activities.
  • Do visit the Clemson University campus.
  • Do experience the local culture by attending a football game.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t drive in areas marked for pedestrians.
  • Don’t be rude to Clemson residents.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clemson

Clemson is generally a safe town with low crime rates. However, travelers should take the usual precautions, such as keeping valuables safe at all times, especially in crowded areas. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night, especially in less-traveled areas.

Health in Clemson

Clemson has several medical facilities and hospitals, including the Clemson University Redfern Health Center. It’s always recommended the basic precautions against the spread of COVID-19 are followed, making sure that masks are worn and hands washed regularly while maintaining social distancing.

Getting to Clemson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clemson

  • Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport: Located approximately 40 minutes southeast of Clemson.
  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport: Located approximately 120 miles southwest of Clemson.

Bus Stations Near Clemson

  • Clemson Area Transit: This local bus service provides travel to and from downtown Clemson and the university.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental car services are available from both airports and are the most convenient way of traveling.
  • Taxis and Ride Shares: Several taxi and ride-share companies operate in Clemson and provide door-to-door services.

How to Get Around in Clemson

Clemson is a small town where many attractions, restaurants, and hotels are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use the Clemson Area Transit to get around the area. The local bus service is affordable and has convenient stops throughout the town, making it an ideal mode of transportation for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Clemson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Greenville: Just 45 minutes from Clemson is Greenville, a beautiful town with the famous Falls Park, museums, restaurants, and shopping centers to explore.
  • Asheville: Asheville is 1.5 hours north of Clemson, and it’s a vibrant city famous for its art scene, breweries, and the beautiful Blue Ridge Parkway.
  • Atlanta: The bustling city of Atlanta, about 2 hours south of Clemson, is a perfect destination for a day trip. Tour the Georgia Aquarium, World of Coca Cola, and Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site.

Conclusion

Clemson is a charming town in South Carolina that provides a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural amenities, and outdoor activities. This comprehensive tour guide has hopefully given you a good sense of what you can expect to see and do when you visit the town. From visiting historical museums, hiking through natural wonders, trying out local cuisine, and relaxing at boutique hotels – Clemson has something for everyone.
