Introduction to Greenville

Greenville is a charming city located in Upcountry South Carolina, in the southern region of the United States. Known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions and thriving food scene, Greenville is a popular vacation destination for tourists.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best of Greenville, including top attractions, must-try dishes, local culture and customs, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Greenville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Greenville County Museum of Art: This museum features a rotating collection of American art, with exhibits ranging from paintings and sculptures to contemporary pieces.
  2. Upcountry History Museum: This museum explores the history of the Upcountry region, with exhibits that highlight the cultural heritage of the area.
  3. Peace Center: This performing arts center hosts a variety of shows and concerts throughout the year, including Broadway productions, classical music concerts, and comedy shows.

Natural Attractions of Greenville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Falls Park on the Reedy: This picturesque park is home to the majestic Reedy River Falls, as well as scenic walking trails, gardens, and public art installations.
  2. Paris Mountain State Park: This state park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and canoeing, as well as breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  3. Swamp Rabbit Trail: This 22-mile trail is perfect for cycling or walking, and offers scenic views of the city and the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenville

  1. Jones Gap State Park: This park offers over 20 miles of hiking trails, with spectacular views of waterfalls and ancient forests.
  2. Table Rock State Park: This park offers several hiking trails, including a challenging climb to the summit of Table Rock mountain.
  3. Greenville Health System Swamp Rabbit Trail: This popular trail is perfect for biking or walking, and connects Greenville with several nearby communities.

Best Things to Do in Greenville: Activities and Tours

  1. Greenville Zoo: This small zoo is home to a variety of animals, including giraffes, lions, and red pandas.
  2. Greenville Foodie Tour: This guided tour takes you to some of the city’s best restaurants and artisanal food shops, giving you a taste of Greenville’s thriving culinary scene.
  3. Greenville Craft Beer Tour: This tour takes you to some of the city’s best craft breweries, with tastings and insider insights into Greenville’s beer culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenville

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shrimp and grits: This Lowcountry dish is a southern favorite, made with creamy grits and sautéed shrimp, often served with a side of collard greens.
  2. BBQ: Greenville is known for its BBQ, with slow-cooked pork and tangy sauces being the local specialty.
  3. Biscuits and gravy: This traditional breakfast dish features flaky biscuits and creamy gravy, often served with a side of bacon.

Local Beverages

  1. Sweet tea: This southern staple is a refreshing drink made with black tea and sweetened with sugar.
  2. Craft beer: Greenville has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries crafting unique and delicious beers.
  3. Moonshine: This high-proof liquor is a southern tradition, made from distilled corn and often flavored with fruit or spices.

Where to Stay in Greenville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hyatt Regency Greenville: This downtown hotel offers luxurious accommodations, with a rooftop pool and bar overlooking the city.
  2. Swamp Rabbit Inn: This charming guesthouse offers affordable and comfortable rooms, with a central location near attractions and restaurants.
  3. The Westin Poinsett, Greenville: This historic hotel offers elegant rooms and suites, with a prime location near attractions and shopping.

What to Buy in Greenville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Artisanal food products: From artisanal cheeses to locally roasted coffee, Greenville has a thriving foodie culture that is reflected in its products.
  2. Craft beer: Many of Greenville’s local breweries offer cans and growlers to take home.
  3. Handmade crafts: Greenville has a thriving arts and crafts community, with local artisans creating unique and beautiful works.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenville

Greenville is a friendly and welcoming community, with a strong sense of southern hospitality. Visitors should expect to be greeted with warmth and kindness, and should reciprocate these gestures.

Best Time to Visit Greenville

The best time to visit Greenville is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the city is alive with outdoor activities, festivals, and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenville

Do:

  1. Take advantage of Greenville’s outdoor activities and scenic views.
  2. Explore the city’s thriving food and drink scene.
  3. Show respect for local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Be overly critical of southern culture and traditions.
  2. Disrespect locals or their property.
  3. Leave trash or litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenville

Greenville is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take common-sense precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Greenville

Greenville has several excellent medical facilities, including Greenville Memorial Hospital and St. Francis Hospital. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance to cover the cost of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Greenville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greenville

Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport (GSP) is the nearest airport to Greenville, located just 13 miles from the city center. Other nearby airports include Charlotte-Douglas International Airport (CLT) and Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL).

Bus Stations Near Greenville

Greyhound Bus Lines has a bus station in Greenville, with regular service to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for getting around Greenville and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Greenville

Greenville is a walkable city, with many attractions located within walking distance of one another. Taxis and rideshare services are also available, as are rental cars and bikes.

Where to Go Next from Greenville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Asheville, NC: This bustling mountain city is home to a variety of art galleries, shops, and restaurants, as well as the famous Biltmore Estate.
  2. Charleston, SC: This historic city is known for its beautiful architecture, stunning gardens, and charming streets.
  3. Atlanta, GA: This thriving metropolis offers visitors a variety of cultural attractions, shopping destinations, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

With its unique combination of southern hospitality, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary delights, Greenville is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the southern United States. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Greenville, and that you will have a wonderful time exploring all that this charming city has to offer.
