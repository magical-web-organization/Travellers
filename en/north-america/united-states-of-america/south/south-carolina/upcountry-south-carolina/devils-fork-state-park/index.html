If you’re looking for a scenic getaway, Devils Fork State Park in Upcountry South Carolina is the perfect destination. This stunning natural area boasts crystal-clear Lake Jocassee, 18 miles of hiking and mountain biking trails, as well as a wide range of outdoor recreational activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventurer, or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in Devils Fork State Park.

Introduction to Devils Fork State Park

Devils Fork State Park is located in Upcountry South Carolina, in the southern region of the United States. It covers a total area of 622 acres and is situated near the border with North Carolina. The park is home to the crystal-clear Lake Jocassee, which is surrounded by towering mountains and lush forests.

Visitors to Devils Fork State Park can enjoy a wide range of activities, including hiking, mountain biking, camping, fishing, boating, and swimming. The park also offers several guided tours and cultural events throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Devils Fork State Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The historic Poinsett Bridge, built in the early 1800s, is one of the oldest surviving bridges in South Carolina and is just a short drive from Devils Fork State Park.
  2. The Oconee Station State Historic Site is a restored 1792 trading post that served as a military outpost during the American Revolution.
  3. The Walhalla Fish Hatchery is a popular attraction that offers a close-up look at the life cycle of trout and other freshwater fish.

Natural Attractions of Devils Fork State Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Jocassee, which covers a total area of 7,500 acres, is one of the most scenic lakes in the United States and a top destination for boating, kayaking and fishing.
  2. The Foothills Trail, which runs for 77 miles from Oconee State Park to Table Rock State Park, offers some of the best hiking and mountain biking in the region.
  3. The Laurel Fork Falls Trail is a short and easy hike that takes visitors to a spectacular waterfall tucked away in the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Devils Fork State Park

  1. The Bear Cove Trail is a 2.5-mile loop trail that offers stunning views of Lake Jocassee and the surrounding mountains.
  2. The Natural Bridge Trail is a 1.5-mile trail that takes visitors to an impressive natural rock bridge over a rushing creek.
  3. The Jocassee Gorges Trail is a 5.3-mile trail that meanders through forests and across streams, offering gorgeous views of the lake and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Devils Fork State Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a guided boat tour of Lake Jocassee to see waterfalls, hidden coves, and rare wildlife.
  2. Rent a pontoon boat or kayak to explore the lake on your own.
  3. Go fishing for rainbow trout, bass, and other freshwater species.
  4. Attend a cultural event or festival, such as the annual South Carolina Square Dance Convention.

Week-Long Itinerary for Devils Fork State Park

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive at Devils Fork State Park and set up camp. Take a sunset hike on the Natural Bridge Trail.
Day 2 Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the coves and waterfalls of Lake Jocassee.
Day 3 Take a guided boat tour of the lake and see some of the rare wildlife that lives in the area.
Day 4 Hike the Bear Cove Trail and explore the nearby town of Walhalla.
Day 5 Take a day trip to the nearby Table Rock State Park and hike to the top of Table Rock Mountain.
Day 6 Go fishing for rainbow trout or bass in Lake Jocassee.
Day 7 Attend a cultural event or festival, such as the annual South Carolina Square Dance Convention before leaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Devils Fork State Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shrimp and grits, a classic Southern dish that features fresh shrimp over creamy grits.
  2. Fried green tomatoes, crispy green tomatoes served with a tangy, spicy dipping sauce.
  3. Barbecue, a Southern staple with various meat options and regional styles of sauces.

Local Beverages

  1. Sweet tea, a Southern classic that is served fresh and sweetened with cane sugar.
  2. Local craft beers and ciders, including the unique flavors of the New South Brewing Co.

Where to Stay in Devils Fork State Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The park offers several campgrounds and cabins for visitors looking to stay in the heart of the park.
  2. The nearby town of Walhalla offers several charming hotels and bed and breakfasts.

What to Buy in Devils Fork State Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade crafts from local artisans, such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  2. Local honey and Maple Syrup, unique and popular gifts from the nearby foothills.

Local Culture and Customs of Devils Fork State Park

  1. Southerners pride themselves on their hospitality, so expect friendly smiles and warm welcomes from the locals.
  2. Respect the culture and traditions of the region and its residents. Inclinations to recklessness or offensiveness will be met with coldness.

Best Time to Visit Devils Fork State Park

The best time to visit Devils Fork State Park is during the spring and fall months when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are slim.

Do’s and Don’ts of Devils Fork State Park

Do:

  1. Follow the park’s rules and regulations.
  2. Respect the environment and wildlife.
  3. Carry out all of your trash and leave the park as you found it.

Don’t:

  1. Feed the wildlife or leave food out in the open.
  2. Litter or damage the natural environment.
  3. Bring pets to the park (not allowed in certain areas).

Safety and Health

Safety in Devils Fork State Park

  1. Bring sunscreen, water, and insect repellent when participating in outdoor activities.
  2. Wear appropriate shoes and clothing for hiking and other outdoor activities.
  3. Check the weather forecast and be prepared for sudden changes in temperature or severe weather.

Health in Devils Fork State Park

  1. Be aware of the risk of tick-borne diseases in the area.
  2. Avoid drinking untreated water from natural sources.
  3. Bring necessary medication and first aid kits.

How to Get Around in Devils Fork State Park

  1. Hiking trails connect most of the main attractions in Devils Fork State Park.
  2. Renting a bike is also a great option to explore the park on wheels.
  3. Private cars are not allowed in certain areas of the park.
