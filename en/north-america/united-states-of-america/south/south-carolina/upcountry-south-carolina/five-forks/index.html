A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Five Forks, South Carolina

Located in Upcountry South Carolina, Five Forks is a charming town that boasts natural beauty, rich history, and a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a culture buff, Five Forks offers something for everyone. Read on to discover the best things to do, places to visit, food to eat, and where to stay in Five Forks.

Introduction to Five Forks

Five Forks, a peaceful residential town, is located in Greenville County, South Carolina. The town got its name from the convergence of five roads at a single point, hence the name Five Forks. The town is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and close proximity to many other fascinating attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Five Forks

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Greenville County Museum of Art: A must-visit museum for art lovers that houses a collection of American art, specifically that of the South.

  • Mossy Creek Missionary Baptist Church & Town Hall: One of the most iconic landmarks of Five Forks, and a fascinating example of Greek Revival architecture.

  • Historic Price House: Built-in 1825, this listed property museum highlights the lives of the early settlers of Greenville.

Natural Attractions of Five Forks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Standing Stone Trail: A scenic hiking trail that takes you through the majestic foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

  • Fox Run Country Club: Enjoy a game of golf at this picturesque 18-hole golf course that offers picturesque landscape views and challenging holes.

  • Lake Cooley Outdoor Recreation Area: Explore hiking trails and wildlife while enjoying outdoor activities all year long.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Five Forks

  • Sandy Flat Trail: A 1.5 mile hike with stunning views of Paris Mountain and the blue ridge mountains.

  • Laurel Creek Trail: 5-mile hike through a wooded area that leads you to a scenic creek.

  • Doodle Trail: A flat, easy 7.5-mile biking trail that runs from Easley to Pickens.

Best Things to Do in Five Forks: Activities and Tours

  • Southeast Helicopter Tour: A scenic helicopter tour that offers breathtaking views of Greenville and the surrounding areas.

  • Greenville Food Tour: Explore the culinary scene of Greenville with a guided food tour around the city.

  • Glowgolf Miniature Golf: Enjoy a round of indoor golf in a unique glow-in-the-dark setting.

Food and Drink Scene of Five Forks

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ - Indulge in delicious barbecue dishes at the Smoke on the Water restaurant that offers amazing views of the Reedy River.

  • Fried Okra - Try the crispy, deep-fried okra from Stax Omega Diner & Bakery.

  • Shrimp and Grits - Enjoy classic South Carolina cuisine at the five forks cafe which serves delicious shrimp and grits.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - Savor the taste of handcrafted beers at the brewery and tasting room of Quest Brewing Company.

  • Wine - Visit City Scape Winery, a family-owned winery that offers a beautiful setting in Piedmont.

Where to Stay in Five Forks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Greenville: A luxurious hotel that offers scenic views, an elegant on-site restaurant, and idyllic settings.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms, a pool, and free breakfast.

  • Sheraton Greenville: A centrally located hotel with well-equipped rooms, an outdoor pool, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Five Forks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Items - Get your hands on artisan-made crafts, pottery, and jewelry from the farmers’ markets, local shops, and retailers in Five Forks.

  • Local Food Products - Bring back home some signature hot sauces and spices made by local manufacturers in Five Forks.

Local Culture and Customs of Five Forks

The local culture of Five Forks reflects Southern hospitality and a love for the outdoors. You’ll find that residents are welcoming and helpful, and community events are a cornerstone of the town. It’s customary to say “yes, ma’am” and “no, sir,” and to hold doors open for others.

Best Time to Visit Five Forks

The best time to visit Five Forks is during the spring and fall seasons when the temperature is pleasant, and the natural beauty of the town is in full display.

Do’s and Don’ts of Five Forks

Do:

  • Embrace the Outdoors - Explore the natural beauty of Five Forks by hiking, biking, or fishing.

  • Visit the Farmers’ Markets - Get your hands on locally sourced produce, crafts, and souvenirs from the farmers’ markets in Five Forks.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Respect the natural beauty of Five Forks by keeping the town clean and disposing of trash responsibly.

  • Disrespect Local Customs - Show consideration for local customs and etiquette, such as using honorifics like “yes, ma’am” and “no, sir.”

Safety and Health

Safety in Five Forks

Five Forks is generally a safe town with a low crime rate. As with any destination, be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions.

Health in Five Forks

  • Medical Facilities - The closest hospitals are Greenville Memorial Hospital and St. Francis Hospital, both of which are easily accessible by car.

Getting to Five Forks: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Five Forks

The nearest airport to Five Forks is the Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport (GSP), which is just a 15-minute car ride away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Five Forks

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus service from Greenville, which is about 11 miles away from Five Forks.

Other Options

Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are available options for getting around Five Forks.

How to Get Around in Five Forks

While Five Forks is a small town, renting a car is the most practical choice for getting around and exploring its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Five Forks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Paris Mountain State Park: A beautiful park located only 30 minutes away from Five Forks is a popular camping destination, with more than 180 campsites.

  • Table Rock State Park: Less than an hour’s drive from Five Forks lies this beautiful state park that features beautiful trails with stunning views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

Conclusion

Five Forks may be a small town, but it is packed with incredible sights and experiences meant to delight visitors of all ages. With its charming natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality offered by its residents, Five Forks is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the true charm of South Carolina.
