Introduction to Taylors

Located in Upcountry South Carolina, Taylors is a charming town with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions, trying out new food, or simply relaxing in a peaceful setting, Taylors has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Taylors

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Taylors Mill, an iconic former textile mill that has been transformed into a thriving community hub with shops, restaurants, and art galleries
  • The Greer Heritage Museum, which provides a fascinating insight into the history of the area
  • The Children’s Museum of the Upstate, a fun-filled interactive museum that’s perfect for families

Natural Attractions of Taylors: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Robinson, a beautiful reservoir that offers boating, fishing, and hiking opportunities
  • Paris Mountain State Park, a stunning nature reserve with miles of hiking and biking trails
  • The Swamp Rabbit Trail, a popular recreational trail that runs through Taylors and its surrounding towns

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taylors

  • The Paris Mountain State Park Trail System, featuring over 15 miles of trails for various skill levels
  • The Swamp Rabbit Trail, a 22-mile paved trail that runs through Taylors and connects to other nearby towns
  • The Lake Robinson Nature Trail, a scenic 0.5-mile loop around the lake

Best Things to Do in Taylors: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided kayaking tour of Lake Robinson
  • Explore local breweries and wineries on a tasting tour
  • Attend a concert or festival at the Taylors Mill

Week-Long Itinerary for Taylors

Day Activity
1 Explore the Taylors Mill and surrounding art galleries

Food and Drink Scene of Taylors

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue, including the famous pulled pork sandwiches at Henry’s Smokehouse
  • Southern-style comfort food, such as fried chicken and biscuits at Mama’s Kitchen
  • Sweet treats, like the homemade ice cream at The Pink Mama’s Ice Cream Parlor

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer from the local breweries, like Birds Fly South Ale Project and Brewery 85
  • Award-winning wines from nearby vineyards, such as Cityscape Winery
  • Freshly brewed coffee from Due South Coffee Roasters

Where to Stay in Taylors: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Embassy Suites by Hilton Greenville Golf Resort and Conference Center, a luxurious option with excellent amenities
  • The Westin Poinsett Greenville, a historic hotel in the heart of downtown
  • The Swamp Rabbit Inn, a cozy bed and breakfast with a laid-back atmosphere

What to Buy in Taylors: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally made pottery and ceramics from the artists at the Taylors Mill
  • Crafts and gifts from the Taylors Farmers Market
  • Fresh produce and other goods from nearby farms

Local Culture and Customs of Taylors

Taylors has a friendly, welcoming culture with a strong sense of community pride. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including supporting local businesses and being mindful of the natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Taylors

The best time to visit Taylors is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the natural beauty of the area is at its best. Summertime can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and wet.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taylors

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area by hiking, biking, or kayaking
  • Try the local cuisine, including the famous barbecue and sweet treats
  • Support local businesses and craftspeople

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Ignore the opportunity to learn about the area’s rich history and culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Taylors

Taylors is a safe and friendly town, with a low crime rate and welcoming locals. Visitors should take normal precautions when it comes to personal safety, such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Taylors

Visitors to Taylors should ensure they have the necessary health insurance and vaccinations before arriving. The town has several medical facilities and hospitals in case of emergency.

Getting to Taylors: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taylors

The closest airport to Taylors is the Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport, which is just a short drive away. Other nearby airports include the Charlotte Douglas International Airport and the Asheville Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near Taylors

Taylors is not directly served by bus stations, but it can be reached via buses that run through nearby towns such as Greenville and Spartanburg.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Taylors and the surrounding area, as public transportation is limited. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Taylors

The best way to get around Taylors is by car or bike. The town is small enough to explore on foot, and there are several bike rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Taylors: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Greenville, a vibrant city with great shopping, dining, and cultural attractions
  • Spartanburg, a historic town with a lively arts scene
  • Asheville, a scenic mountain town with excellent hiking and outdoor adventures

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and welcoming community, Taylors is the perfect destination for anyone seeking a peaceful, relaxing vacation in the heart of South Carolina’s Upcountry. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, you’re sure to find plenty of activities and attractions to keep you entertained!
