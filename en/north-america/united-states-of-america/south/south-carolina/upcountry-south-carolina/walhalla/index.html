Introduction to Walhalla

Nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains, Walhalla is a charming town in the Upcountry region of South Carolina. Walhalla, which means “Valhalla” in German, was founded in 1850 by German settlers and has a rich heritage that is reflected in its historic architecture, museums, and festivals. This travel guide will show you the best things to do, places to stay, and local culture of Walhalla, South Carolina.

List of Places to Visit in Walhalla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oconee Heritage Center: Learn about the history and culture of the Upcountry region at this museum that features exhibits on Native American artifacts, military history, and the region’s agricultural and textile industries.

  • Stumphouse Tunnel: Built in the 1850s as part of a failed railroad project connecting Charleston to the Midwest, this unfinished tunnel now serves as a popular hiking and picnic spot.

  • Walhalla Civic Auditorium: This historic auditorium hosts musical performances, plays, and other cultural events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Walhalla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Issaqueena Falls: This 100-foot waterfall is located just outside of Walhalla and can be viewed from a short hike.

  • Chau Ram County Park: This park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and a scenic overlook of the Chauga River.

  • Chattooga Belle Farm: This family-owned farm offers tours, tastings, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Don’t miss their apple brandy!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walhalla

  • Stumphouse Mountain Bike Park: This park offers miles of mountain biking trails with varying levels of difficulty for all skill levels.

  • Foothills Trail: This 77-mile hiking trail starts near Walhalla and leads through the beautiful wilderness of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

  • Yellow Branch Falls Trail: This 3-mile hike leads to a stunning 50-foot waterfall and is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Walhalla: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Tour: This spooky tour takes you on a guided walk through some of Walhalla’s haunted spots and historic cemeteries.

  • Wine Tasting: Take a scenic drive to nearby vineyards like Chattooga Belle Farm or Carolina Vineyards to enjoy local wine tastings.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: With several nearby rivers and lakes, Walhalla is the perfect destination for a kayaking or canoeing adventure. Rent kayaks or canoes at Lake Hartwell or the Chattooga River Outpost.

Food and Drink Scene of Walhalla

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: Walhalla has some excellent barbecue restaurants like The Spotted Pig and Creekside BBQ.

  • German Cuisine: For a taste of Walhalla’s German heritage, visit places like Black Forest Bakery and Cafe or Old Europe Restaurant.

  • Southern Food: For classic Southern cuisine, try Stumphouse Cafe or Blue Marble Deli and Bakery.

Local Beverages

  • Breweries: Visit breweries like Carolina Bauernhaus, WESTBROOK Brewing Company, and Oconee Brewing Company for craft beers made with local ingredients.

  • Wine: Don’t miss the opportunity to try local wine at Chattooga Belle Farm or Carolina Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Walhalla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Tillman: This hotel in nearby Clemson offers comfortable rooms and is perfect for families.

  • Mountain Rest Cabins: This group of cabins in nearby Mountain Rest offers a peaceful, rustic stay in the woods.

  • Blue Bear Mountain Lodge: This cozy lodge in nearby Cashiers, NC, boasts stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Walhalla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: The Walhalla Art Works co-op features handcrafted pottery by local artisans.

  • Honey: The Oconee Beekeepers Association sells honey made from the bees of local beekeepers.

Local Culture and Customs of Walhalla

Walhalla has a rich German heritage, which can be seen in its architecture, festivals, and cuisine. Visitors should try to experience the Oktoberfest celebration held annually in October and visit historic sites like the Lutheran Church of the Ascension.

Best Time to Visit Walhalla

The best time to visit Walhalla is during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the weather is mild. The town also hosts several festivals and events during this time, like the Walhalla Oktoberfest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walhalla

Do:

  • Do explore the outdoors and natural wonders of Walhalla.
  • Do immerse yourself in the town’s rich local culture and German heritage.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable outdoor gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Walhalla

Walhalla is a safe community, but visitors should take common sense precautions like locking doors and valuables in cars.

Health in Walhalla

Visitors should check their health insurance coverage before traveling and take caution when hiking or enjoying outdoor activities.

Getting to Walhalla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Walhalla

The closest airport to Walhalla is Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport, which is approximately 81 miles away. Charlotte Douglas International Airport is also a viable option and is about 139 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Walhalla

The closest Greyhound bus stations are in Anderson and Seneca, both about 20 miles away from Walhalla.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Walhalla via Highways 28 and 183 or take a scenic drive on the Cherokee Foothills Scenic Highway.

How to Get Around in Walhalla

Visitors can easily get around Walhalla by car or on foot, with some bike paths available as well.

Where to Go Next from Walhalla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Clemson: Just a short drive from Walhalla, Clemson is home to the beautiful Clemson University and several museums and parks.

  • Lake Jocassee: This stunning lake offers swimming, fishing, hiking, and camping opportunities and is just a short drive from Walhalla.

  • Asheville: A popular destination in nearby North Carolina, Asheville is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, as well as its stunning mountain views.

Conclusion

Walhalla, South Carolina, is a charming destination with plenty to offer visitors in terms of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring local museums, or trying the local cuisine, Walhalla has something for everyone.
