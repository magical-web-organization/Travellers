Introduction to Orangeburg

Located in Santee Cooper Country, Orangeburg is a city in South Carolina that boasts a rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural beauty. From visiting museums and historical places to exploring outdoor adventures and scenic views, Orangeburg offers something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Orangeburg, we’ll highlight the top places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, local culture and customs, safety and health concerns, transportation options, and nearby destinations. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Orangeburg

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or an adventure seeker, Orangeburg has plenty to offer. Here are some of the top places to visit in Orangeburg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. South Carolina State University Museum of Art: This museum is home to a collection of African American art, civil rights exhibits, and contemporary art.

  2. Edisto Memorial Gardens: These stunning gardens feature over 4,000 rose bushes, along with camellias, azaleas, and a butterfly garden.

  3. Orangeburg County Historical Society: The Orangeburg County Historical Society offers exhibits and displays on the rich history of Orangeburg county, including artifacts from the Civil War era and the antebellum period.

  4. Santee National Wildlife Refuge: This 15,000-acre refuge provides opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, fishing, and hunting.

Natural Attractions of Orangeburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Congaree National Park: This beautiful park is located just a short drive from Orangeburg and offers hiking trails, canoeing, and camping opportunities.

  2. Lake Marion: South Carolina’s largest lake, Lake Marion offers fishing, boating, and scenic views.

  3. Four Holes Swamp: This stunning wetland area is home to an abundance of wildlife, including alligators, otters, and deer.

  4. Elloree Heritage Museum & Cultural Center: This museum and cultural center showcases the history and culture of the Elloree community, including antiques, furniture, photographs, and art.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orangeburg

Orangeburg offers several hiking and biking opportunities for those who love to explore the outdoors. Here are a few notable routes:

  • The Edisto River Blue Trail: This 66-mile blueway offers opportunities for paddling, fishing, and camping.

  • The Palmetto Trail: This 500-mile trail takes hikers and bikers through some of South Carolina’s most beautiful landscapes. The Orangeburg-to-Clemson section is particularly stunning.

  • The Santee Canal Trail: This 7-mile trail offers scenic views of the historic Santee Canal and provides opportunities for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Orangeburg: Activities and Tours

  1. Horseback Riding: Saddle up and explore local trails on horseback with Sidekick Farms, located just outside Orangeburg.

  2. Fishing: With its proximity to Lake Marion and the Edisto River, Orangeburg offers some of the best freshwater fishing in South Carolina.

  3. Golfing: Orangeburg has several golf courses, including Hillcrest Golf Course and the Orangeburg Country Club.

  4. Ghost Tours: Take a guided tour of Orangeburg’s haunted sites with Old South Walking Tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Orangeburg

Orangeburg’s food and drink scene is a delicious blend of Southern comfort food and Lowcountry cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lowcountry Boil: A classic Southern dish that features shrimp, sausage, corn, and potatoes boiled together in a flavorful broth.

  2. Fried Chicken: Crispy, golden, and downright delicious, fried chicken is a Southern staple.

  3. Gullah Rice: This dish originated with the Gullah people, who lived along the coast of South Carolina. Gullah rice is made with rice, tomatoes, and shrimp, and it’s full of flavor.

  4. Pimento Cheese: A classic Southern spread made with cheddar cheese, mayonnaise, and pimentos.

Local Beverages

  1. Sweet Tea: A Southern staple, sweet tea is made with black tea and plenty of sugar.

  2. Palmetto Ale: Brewed by the Palmetto Brewing Company, this beer is a South Carolina favorite.

  3. Firefly Sweet Tea Vodka: This vodka is made with real tea leaves and is perfect for sipping on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Orangeburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Orangeburg has several options for lodging, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are a few notable options:

  1. Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott: This hotel features comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a fitness center.

  2. Thee Matriarch Bed & Breakfast: This elegant bed and breakfast is located in a historic home built in 1896 and offers modern comfort and luxury.

  3. Econo Lodge Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel offers basic amenities, including free Wi-Fi and a continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Orangeburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home from Orangeburg, here are some suggestions:

  • Boiled Peanuts: A Southern snack that you won’t find anywhere else.

  • Pottery: Orangeburg is known for its beautiful pottery, including handcrafted bowls, plates, and vases.

  • Palmetto Cheese: This delicious spread is made with cheddar cheese, cream cheese, and diced jalapenos.

Local Culture and Customs of Orangeburg

Orangeburg is a city with a rich history and culture. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect the history of Orangeburg and its people.

  • Southern hospitality is a Southern tradition. Be courteous and polite to everyone you meet.

  • The pace of life in Orangeburg is slower than in urban areas. Relax and enjoy the beauty of the city.

Best Time to Visit Orangeburg

The best time to visit Orangeburg is in the spring or fall when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orangeburg

Do:

  • Visit local museums and historical sites.

  • Try Southern cuisine and local beverages.

  • Explore the outdoor adventures and natural beauty of Orangeburg.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local culture and customs.

  • Ignore safety and health concerns.

  • Be impolite to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orangeburg

Orangeburg is a generally safe city, but as with any city, there are some precautions to take:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables secure.

  • Lock your car doors and windows.

  • Avoid confrontations with strangers.

Health in Orangeburg

Orangeburg has several medical facilities, including Regional Medical Center and Orangeburg County Community of Care. Be sure to carry medical insurance and necessary medications with you.

Getting to Orangeburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Orangeburg

  • Columbia Metropolitan Airport: Located approximately 40 miles from Orangeburg, this airport serves several major airlines.

  • Charleston International Airport: Located approximately 70 miles from Orangeburg, this airport serves several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Orangeburg

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in downtown Orangeburg, this bus station provides service to several major cities.

Other Options

  • Driving: Orangeburg is located off of I-26, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Orangeburg

Orangeburg is easily navigable by car, and several car rental agencies are available. However, there is no public transportation system in Orangeburg, so having a car is necessary to get around.

Where to Go Next from Orangeburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore nearby destinations from Orangeburg, here are a few ideas:

  • Charleston, South Carolina: Known for its historic charm and Southern hospitality, Charleston is approximately 70 miles from Orangeburg.

  • Columbia, South Carolina: The state capital, Columbia, is approximately 40 miles from Orangeburg.

  • Myrtle Beach, South Carolina: Approximately 150 miles from Orangeburg, Myrtle Beach is known for its stunning beaches, boardwalk, and amusement parks.

Conclusion

Orangeburg is a city with a diverse culture, rich history, and stunning natural beauty.
