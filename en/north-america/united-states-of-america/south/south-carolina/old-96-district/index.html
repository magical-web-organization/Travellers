Introduction to Old 96 District

Old 96 District is a beautiful region in South Carolina that offers a perfect mix of outdoor adventures and cultural attractions. Located in the south of the state, Old 96 District is a great destination for people who are looking to experience the authentic southern hospitality and explore the natural beauty of the region. With plenty of museums, historical sites, hiking and biking trails, and charming towns, Old 96 District has something for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Old 96 District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old 96 District is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich history and cultural heritage. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Greenwood Museum: This museum showcases the history and heritage of the Greenwood community with exhibits, tours, and special events.
  • Abbeville Opera House: This historic building is a must-visit for theater lovers. Built in 1908, this iconic theater has been beautifully restored and hosts musical performances, plays, and other events.
  • Ninety Six National Historic Site: This site tells the story of the Revolutionary War in Old 96 District. Visitors can hike trails and see the original star fort used during the war.

Natural Attractions of Old 96 District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Old 96 District is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty that visitors can explore and admire. From stunning waterfalls to peaceful lakes and parks, this region has it all. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Lake Greenwood State Park: This beautiful park features a large lake where visitors can go fishing, boating, and kayaking. The park also has hiking trails, picnic areas, and camping sites.
  • Calhoun Falls State Park: This park located on the shores of Lake Russell boasts hiking trails, fishing, and camping. A must-visit spot is the spectacular 75-foot Calhoun Falls waterfall.
  • Sumter National Forest: This forest offers miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails along with amazing views of waterfalls and canyons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old 96 District

Old 96 District is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails suitable for all levels of experience. Whether you are a seasoned hiker or just starting, Old 96 District has something for you. Some of the top routes include:

  • Bartram Trail: This 110-mile trail, named after naturalist William Bartram, passes through Old 96 District and offers spectacular views of the Nantahala National Forest and the Appalachian Mountains.
  • Lake Greenwood State Park Nature Trail: This easy 2.1-mile trail winds through the hardwood forest and offers beautiful views of Lake Greenwood.
  • Hickory Knob State Park Trails: With over 7 miles of hiking and biking trails, Hickory Knob State Park offers a perfect opportunity to explore the beauty of Old 96 District.

Best Things to Do in Old 96 District: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Old 96 District offers visitors plenty of other fun activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Old 96 District include:

  • Visit local wineries: Old 96 District is home to several award-winning wineries, such as Carolina Vineyards and Winery and City Scape Winery. Take a wine tasting tour and learn about the local wines.
  • Go fishing: With several lakes and streams, Old 96 District is a great spot for fishing enthusiasts. Lake Greenwood and Calhoun Falls State Park are popular locations to catch catfish and largemouth bass.
  • Take a ghost tour: Old 96 District is rich in history and has its share of local ghost stories. Take a ghost tour of places like Abbeville Opera House and hear about the supernatural experiences of the locals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Old 96 District

With so many things to see and do, it can be tough to fit everything in a short trip. Here is a week-long itinerary that covers the best of Old 96 District:

Day 1: Arrive in Greenwood and explore the downtown area, visit the Museum, and dine in one of the local restaurants.

Day 2: Take a hiking trail in Sumter National Forest and then visit the Ninety Six National Historic Site to see the old star-shaped fort.

Day 3: Go fishing or kayaking at Lake Greenwood State Park and then take a wine tasting tour at Carolina Vineyards and Winery.

Day 4: Visit Abbeville Opera House for a musical performance and enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 5: Explore Hickory Knob State Park and take a biking trail through the hardwood forest, then go on a ghost tour.

Day 6: Go hiking at Bartram Trail and enjoy scenic views of the Appalachian Mountains, followed by relaxing at the Calhoun Falls State Park waterfall.

Day 7: Spend the day at Lake Russell and enjoy fishing, hiking, swimming, and camping.

Food and Drink Scene of Old 96 District

Old 96 District has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a wide range of local restaurants, bakeries, and breweries. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: South Carolina is known for its barbecue, and Old 96 District has plenty of great BBQ joints. Try the pulled pork sandwich at Brewer’s BBQ or the ribs at Carolina Barbecue.
  • Catfish: With so many water bodies, it’s no surprise that catfish is a local delicacy. Head to the Mill House or Village Grill in Greenwood for some delicious catfish.
  • Pimento Cheese: A classic southern dish, you can find pimento cheese sandwiches at many local restaurants in Old 96 District.

Local Beverages

  • Muscadine wine: Made from locally grown grapes, muscadine wine is a must-try for wine lovers. Visit City Scape Winery for a tasting.
  • Craft beer: Old 96 District has several breweries and pubs that serve excellent craft beer. Try the Rail Line Brewery in Greenwood or the Eagles Nest Brewery in West Union.

Where to Stay in Old 96 District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old 96 District has plenty of options for accommodations that cater to every budget and preference. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy bed and breakfast, Old 96 District has it all. Some of the best options include:

  • The Inn on the Square: This hotel in the heart of Greenwood offers comfortable rooms with great amenities and is walking distance from local attractions.
  • The Johnston House: A charming Bed and Breakfast in Abbeville with excellent reviews from visitors who enjoy its pristine garden and workshops on offer.
  • Lakeview Motel: Located in McCormick next to the historical Railroad Depot Museum, Lakeview Motel is perfect for a weekend break near the lake.

What to Buy in Old 96 District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Old 96 District has plenty of unique and special products that make great souvenirs and gifts. Some of the best local products include:

  • Handcrafted pottery: From functional pieces to art pieces, Old 96 District is home to many talented potters. Visit The Purple Possum for pottery crafted on-site or visit the Abbeville Art Center for a wider range of pottery.
  • Hand-turned articles made of wood: Artisan vendors specialize in setting up market booths and offers one-of-a-kind experiences with handmade baskets or small boxes as some popular items.
  • Carolina Moon Distillery’s flavored moonshine: Offering traditional and unique flavor options, Carolina Moon Distillery’s moonshine is a must-try for those looking to take the taste of Old 96 District back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Old 96 District

Old 96 District is known for its warm and welcoming southern hospitality. Don’t be surprised if people you meet in Old 96 District strike up a conversation at the grocery store or offer to show you around town. It is customary to be friendly and courteous, and small talk is a great way to get to know the locals.

Best Time to Visit Old 96 District

The best time to visit Old 96 District is in the spring or fall when the weather is pleasant, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Temperatures are cooler, and there are fewer crowds, making it easier to explore museums and other cultural sites.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old 96 District

Do:

  • Be courteous to the locals and engage in small talk.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, especially the BBQ and muscadine wine.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Old 96 District by exploring the hiking and biking trails.
  • Take a ghost tour and learn about the region’s rich history and folklore.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural beauty of Old 96 District.
  • Don’t miss out on the chance to explore the local museums and historical sites.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old 96 District

Old 96 District is a safe destination overall, but visitors should exercise caution while hiking or camping in remote areas and keep valuables safe. Keep hydrated and sunscreen applied while outdoors to avoid sunburn on some of the more lengthy hikes along the trails.

Health in Old 96 District

The government obliges food handling establishments in the area to maintain hygiene standards and ensure patrons’ safety and well-being. Visitors should follow the recommended sanitation guidelines and take precautions to avoid insect bites when enjoying the outdoors.

Getting to Old 96 District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Old 96 District is accessible by road and air, with major airports such as Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport, just an hour and a half away by car. There are also bus and taxi services available in the area. Rental vehicles are available at airport locations at competitive rates servicing Old 96 District’s various scenic locations.

Airports Near Old 96 District

  • Columbia Metropolitan Airport: 1 hr 45 min away by car.
  • Charlotte Douglas International Airport: 2 hr 20 min away by car.

Bus Stations Near Old 96 District

Other Options

How to Get Around in Old 96 District

Getting around Old 96 District is easy, and visitors have plenty of options, from rental cars and taxis to bikes and walking tours. Bikes and scooters are offered by several rentals throughout the district, and a ride on the “Heritage Trail” Trolley offers a narrated experience of Greenwood’s history.

Where to Go Next from Old 96 District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
