Introduction to Laurens

Nestled in the heart of the Old 96 District in South Carolina, the charming town of Laurens offers a unique blend of rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty. The town is situated in the southern part of the state, surrounded by rolling hills and lush forests, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies alike.

List of Places to Visit in Laurens

Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s rich history or adventure outdoors, Laurens has plenty of sights and attractions to keep you entertained. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Laurens County Museum - The museum features exhibits on the rich history and culture of Laurens County, showcasing artifacts from the region’s Native American and colonial past, the Civil War, and more.
  • Historic Laurens County Courthouse - Built in 1838, the courthouse is a beautiful example of Greek Revival architecture and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Red Barn Studio Museum - Housed in a charming red barn, the museum features rotating exhibits of local and regional art.

Natural Attractions of Laurens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Rabon - This 567-acre lake is a haven for fishing, boating, and hiking, with several trails surrounding the lake offering stunning views of the water and surrounding hills.
  • Little River Park and Nature Trail - This 30-acre park features a nature trail along the Little River, which is a popular spot for hiking, bird-watching, and picnicking.
  • Musgrove Mill Historic Site - This state park is the site of a Revolutionary War battle and features hiking trails, picnic areas, and a museum.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laurens

Laurens offers several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts of all levels. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Lake Rabon Trail - This 8.5-mile trail circles the entire lake, offering stunning views of the water and surrounding hills.
  • Laurens Rail Trail - This 2-mile trail follows the path of an old railroad line through the heart of downtown Laurens, offering a glimpse into the town’s history and culture.
  • Musgrove Mill State Historic Site Trails - The park features several trails of varying lengths and difficulty levels, offering hikers and bikers the chance to explore the beauty and history of the area.

Best Things to Do in Laurens: Activities and Tours

Laurens offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Laurens Farmers Market - Open every Saturday from May through October, the market features local vendors selling fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.
  • Laurens County Speedway - The speedway hosts weekly races featuring local drivers, making it a popular spot for racing enthusiasts.
  • Southern Fried Cruisers Cruise-In - This monthly event features classic cars from around the region, offering visitors a glimpse into the history and culture of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Laurens

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Laurens, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Laurens

No trip to Laurens is complete without trying some of the town’s locally sourced, Southern-style cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Water Cornbread - This traditional Southern dish is made with cornmeal and hot water, then fried to crispy perfection.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes - Sliced green tomatoes that are breaded and fried, served with a side of remoulade sauce.
  • BBQ Pulled Pork - Slow-cooked pork shoulder that’s smothered in a tangy, vinegar-based BBQ sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A Southern classic, sweet tea is made with black tea and plenty of sugar, served over ice.
  • Strawberry Lemonade - Freshly squeezed lemonade with pureed strawberries for a sweet and tangy twist.

Where to Stay in Laurens: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Laurens offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top options:

  • Inn on the Square - This historic hotel is located in the heart of downtown Laurens and offers charming rooms and suites.
  • The Red Roof Inn - This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to downtown Laurens.
  • Laurens Bed and Breakfast - This charming B&B offers cozy rooms and a homey atmosphere, with a patio overlooking the garden.

What to Buy in Laurens: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir or local product to bring home from Laurens, here are some of the top options:

  • Handmade Crafts and Decor - Laurens has a thriving arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans creating unique handmade items.
  • Local Honey and Jams - Many local farmers offer fresh, locally sourced honey and homemade jams at the Laurens Farmers Market.
  • Southern Cookbooks - Bring home a taste of Southern cooking with a cookbook featuring classic recipes from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Laurens

Laurens is a friendly, welcoming town that values community and tradition. Southern hospitality is alive and well here, with locals often going out of their way to make visitors feel at home. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s history and traditions, including its role in the Civil War and its contributions to Southern cuisine and culture.

Best Time to Visit Laurens

The best time to visit Laurens is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the foliage is at its most vibrant. Summertime can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laurens

When visiting Laurens, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Embrace Southern hospitality and be friendly to locals.
  • Explore the town’s rich history and culture.
  • Try some of the town’s famous Southern cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the town’s history or traditions.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Be rude or confrontational to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laurens

Laurens is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should still take normal safety precautions, such as locking their car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Laurens

Laurens has several clinics and pharmacies to meet visitors’ medical needs. It’s also important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Laurens: Transportation Options and Logistics

Laurens is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area. The nearest airport is Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport, located about 30 miles away. Visitors can also take a bus to nearby Greenwood or Clinton and then take a taxi or rental car to Laurens.

Airports Near Laurens

  • Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport - Located about 30 miles from Laurens.

Bus Stations Near Laurens

  • Greenwood Greyhound Station - Located about 20 miles from Laurens.
  • Clinton Bus Station - Located about 15 miles from Laurens.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars - There are several rental car companies in the area, including Enterprise and Hertz.

How to Get Around in Laurens

Laurens is a small town, and many of the top attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the area. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Laurens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the South, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Greenville - This vibrant city is located about an hour away and offers a thriving arts scene, award-winning restaurants, and stunning outdoor spaces.
  • Columbia - South Carolina’s capital city is located about 90 minutes away and offers a rich history, a variety of cultural attractions, and a lively nightlife.
  • Charleston - This historic city is located about three hours away and offers some of the best shopping, dining, and sightseeing in the South.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Laurens has something for everyone. With its warm Southern hospitality, charming small-town atmosphere, and stunning natural beauty, it’s no wonder that visitors return to Laurens time and time again.
