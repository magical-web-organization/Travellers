Your Complete Travel Guide to Greenwood, South Carolina

Located in Old 96 District of South Carolina, Greenwood is a small city known for its impressive cultural attractions, natural beauty, and thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or a trip through history and art, Greenwood has something to offer for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Greenwood.

Introduction to Greenwood

Greenwood is a small city of around 24,000 people located in the western part of South Carolina. Founded in 1824, Greenwood has a rich history and several cultural attractions. It’s also surrounded by natural beauty, with several lakes and state parks in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Greenwood

If you’re planning a trip to Greenwood, these are the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Greenwood Museum: Located in the downtown area, this museum offers a deep dive into the history and culture of Greenwood, showcasing local artifacts and exhibits.
  • Emerald Farm: An eco-farm located on the outskirts of Greenwood, visitors can interact with farm animals and enjoy the scenic views.
  • The Museum and Railroad Historical Center: Formerly the Greenwood Freight Depot, it contains historic artifacts and exhibits of the railroad and its social and economic impact on Greenwood.

Natural Attractions of Greenwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Greenwood State Park: The park has 900 acres of land and provides activities such as boating, fishing, and hiking, and also has a camping area.
  • Ninety-Six National Historic Site: A popular spot for hiking, walking trails, and exploration of Revolutionary War battlegrounds.
  • Paris Mountain State Park: Located just a few miles from Greenwood, this park features hiking, swimming and fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenwood

Greenwood provides numerous biking and hiking routes for adventure enthusiasts to explore. Here are some of the top options for you to consider:

  • Rail-to-Trail: A 3.6-mile trail runs between Emerald Farm and town featuring a nature walk and hiking trails to enjoy.
  • Lake Greenwood State Park: RV and camping facilities, hiking trails, boating, fishing and a scenic nature experience for visitors. The Loop Trail is one of the park’s highlights, offering scenic views of the lake shoreline.
  • 96 Creekside Trail: An easy 2-mile trail runs alongside the Ninety-Six Creek connecting to the historic brickyard that dates back to the 1700s.

Best Things to Do in Greenwood: Activities and Tours

Greenwood offers a wide range of activities for visitors to enjoy. Some of the top things to do in Greenwood include:

  • Visit the farmer’s market: Experience the amazing selection of locally grown fresh fruits, vegetables, and handcrafted goods.
  • Greenwood Community Theatre: Experience the local performing arts scene by attending a show.
  • Golf at The Links at Stoney Point: A world-class golf course, offering beautiful scenic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greenwood

If you’re planning a trip to Greenwood, this itinerary provides a lot of fun and excitement while also allowing time to relax and rejuvenate.

Day 1: Explore the museums and landmarks like the Greenwood Museum and Railroad Historical Center.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Lake Greenwood State Park, hitting the lake trails.

Day 3: Enjoy the farmer’s market in the morning and then head over to The Links at Stoney Point for an afternoon golfing round.

Day 4: Hike the scenic 96 Creekside Trail followed by an early dinner in downtown Greenwood.

Day 5: Visit Ninety-Six National Historic Site and learn about Revolutionary War battlegrounds and the history of Ninety-Six.

Day 6: Take a leisurely day trip to Paris Mountain State Park.

Day 7: Visit Emerald Farm, interact with farm animals, and take a relaxed nature walk coupled with a picnic lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenwood

Greenwood is a city with a diverse selection of dining options, with an abundance of locally-made and organically-grown foods. Here are some of the top food and drink experiences not to be missed:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lowcountry Fish and Grits: A staple cuisine in the South and an excellent dish in Greenwood. Served in almost all restaurants, and variations come with okra, tomatoes, or collard greens.
  • Pimento Cheese and Crackers: A local favorite in most eateries as a snack or appetizer.
  • Shrimp and grits: This famous southern staple is a must-try in Greenwood.

Local Beverages

  • Hunter Gatherer Brewery: This brewery provides unique and fresh craft beers from South Carolina’s native ingredients.
  • City Scape Winery: This winery offers unique flavors, including its signature wine, Black and Blueberry sipping wine.

Where to Stay in Greenwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greenwood offers a wide range of options when it comes to accommodations - from affordable hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the top options for visitors to Greenwood include:

  • The Inn on the Square: This hotel situated in the heart of Greenwood and offers modern amenities and elegant suites.
  • Red Roof Inn: A budget hotel with affordable prices, the Red Roof Inn provides excellent value for money in Greenwood.

What to Buy in Greenwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While visiting Greenwood, make sure to bring back some of the locally-made products. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to take home include:

  • Indigo Soul: A diverse Shop with a good mix of clothing, jewelry, and home goods handcrafted in and around Greenwood.
  • Greenwood-themed apparel: Most local shops sell branded t-shirts, hats, and scarves with the name and slogan of the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenwood

Greenwood residents are hospitable, and visitors should endeavor to be respectful and follow local customs. Residents appreciate it when visitors say ‘please’ and ‘thank you.’ Dressing casually is generally acceptable, although residents dress appropriately for events like church services. People in Greenwood enjoy participating in local events and festivals, and visitors are welcome to join in the fun.

Best Time to Visit Greenwood

Greenwood has a fairly moderate climate throughout the year, but the best time to visit is between March and May or September and November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are lower. During these months, visitors can enjoy hiking and other outdoor activities with more ease.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenwood

It’s always important to respect and follow local customs when traveling. Here are some essential do’s and don’ts when visiting Greenwood:

Do:

  • Learn about the history and culture of Greenwood.
  • Participate in local events and festivals.
  • Be courteous and respectful to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural environment.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public areas.

Safety and Health

While there are no major safety concerns when visiting Greenwood, it’s always better to be prepared. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Greenwood

  • Make sure to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions while hiking or biking in the natural areas.
  • Use common sense and be aware of your belongings in crowded, urban areas.

Health in Greenwood

  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach issues.
  • Apply sunscreen when going outdoors to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Greenwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Greenwood is conveniently located within driving distance of several major cities in South Carolina. Here are the best transportation options to get to Greenwood:

Airports Near Greenwood

  • Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport: The largest airport in the region, around 60 miles northwest of Greenwood.
  • Columbia Metropolitan Airport: located around 60 miles south of Greenwood.

Bus Stations Near Greenwood

  • Greyhound Lines: This bus transportation service has a station located close to historic downtown Greenwood.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: There are various options companies in the area with a range of vehicle types to select from.

How to Get Around in Greenwood

Greenwood is a small town, so most visitors find walking or cycling sufficient to explore most of the attractions in town. Taxis, ride-hailing services, and rental cars are also available if you need to go further outside the town or across the state.

Where to Go Next from Greenwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your trip from Greenwood, there are some fantastic nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring.

  • Columbia, the capital of South Carolina, is a two-hour drive from Greenwood and is home to several cultural attractions and Southern cuisine.
  • Greenville, located just an hour from Greenwood, offers many street events, a solid coffee and brewery scene, and lots of fun outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Greenwood offers incredible cultural attractions and natural beauty that is attractive to visitors who want to explore and enjoy the urban and natural adventures in the South. This comprehensive travel guide provides all the necessary information for visitors to experience everything that Greenwood has to offer. Plan your itinerary accordingly and have an unforgettable experience!
