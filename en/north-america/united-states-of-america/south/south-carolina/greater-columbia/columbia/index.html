Introduction to Columbia, South Carolina

Columbia is a vibrant city in South Carolina that offers a plethora of experiences for travelers. The city boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and a wide range of activities and tours for anyone looking to explore. In this guide, we’ll cover all the must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, local food and drinks, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Columbia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • South Carolina State House: The iconic and stunning building of South Carolina State House is a must-visit. Take a guided tour to learn about the building’s history and significance in the state.
  • South Carolina State Museum: The largest museum in the state offers exhibits on everything from art and history to natural science.
  • Columbia Museum of Art: This museum highlights both international and reginal artists including Monet, all the way to contemporary art.
  • Robert Mills House and Gardens: This historic landmark, built in 1823, is a historic mansion that offers guided tours and beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Columbia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Congaree National Park: Explore one of the few remaining old-growth forests with guided hikes, canoe trips along the Congaree River, or boardwalk trails
  • Riverbanks Zoo and Garden: See the diverse animals and the dynamically crafted botanical gardens, including a waterfall and a Kiwi exhibit!
  • Harbison State Forest: Enjoy the stunning views of the forest with hiking and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbia

  • Saluda Dam Walk: trail surrounds the Saluda Lake Dam 8.1 mile round-trip hike or bike path.
  • Three Rivers Greenway: 12-mile stretch of the West Columbia area along the Saluda, Broad, and Congaree Rivers for walking, jogging, bike riding, or inline skating.
  • Harbison State Forest Trails: A seasoned biker and hiker’s favorite with a grand total of 18 miles and two different loops, it’s a great way to enjoy the outdoors.

Best Things to Do in Columbia: Activities and Tours

  • Escape Rooms: Columbia is home to a few escape room experiences, where you have 60 minutes to solve puzzles and decipher clues to make it out alive.
  • Segway Tours: Tour the city on a Segway to get a new perspective and cover more ground!
  • Craft breweries and a distillery: Columbia is home to River Rat Brewery, WECO Bottle and Biergarten, Swamp Cabbage Brewery, and Copper Horse Spirits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbia

Day Activity
1 Visit the South Carolina State House and South Carolina State Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Columbia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and Grits: A local favorite, often made with local shrimp and creamy grits that make for a flavorful meal.
  • South Carolina Barbecue: South Carolina-style barbecue has mustard-based sauce instead of tomato-based, with a range of flavors that vary from region to region.
  • Fried Okra: A popular Southern side dish that pairs perfectly with meals.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: South Carolinians love sweet iced tea, and you can typically find it everywhere.
  • Craft Beer: Columbia is home to several craft breweries, including River Rat Brewery, WECO Bottle and Biergarten, and Swamp Cabbage Brewery.
  • Mint Julep: While not exclusive to South Carolina, you’ll likely have a chance to sample this classic Southern cocktail in Columbia.

Where to Stay in Columbia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Graduate Columbia: A boutique-style hotel that offers bright, retro decor and is located in the heart of the city.
  • Hyatt Place Columbia/Downtown/The Vista: A modern hotel that overlooks the Congaree River, with access to top landmarks and museums.
  • The Wayside Inn Bed & Breakfast: A restored home that offers a romantic setting near the USC campus.

What to Buy in Columbia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Palmetto Pendant: The Palmetto Pendant is a beautiful piece of jewelry that you can find in many stores throughout the city.
  • Grits: Find local, stoneground grits at places like Adluh Flour.
  • South Carolina State Flag Merchandise: While the state flag is a familiar sight to most, you can take home souvenirs from boutique stores like Soda City Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbia

South Carolina is known for its Southern culture, charm, hospitality, and politeness. It’s polite to say “yes, ma’am” or “no, sir” when speaking to people. Southerners love their food and often gather around the table with friends and family, paired with sweet tea. Football is also a big part of the local culture in Columbia, with the University of South Carolina Gamecocks football team drawing huge crowds during the season.

Best Time to Visit Columbia

The best time to visit Columbia is from March to May or from September to November. The temperatures are mild, making outdoor activities enjoyable. You can also visit during the summer months, but expect hot, humid, and crowded conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbia

Do:

  • Dress modestly for historical places of worship
  • Always be polite and courteous when talking to locals
  • Try local food and beverages

Don’t:

  • Avoid leaving your valuables in plain sight in your vehicle
  • Don’t litter, be respectful of the environment by disposing of your waste responsibly
  • Don’t overindulge in alcohol in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbia

Columbia is generally a safe city, but you should take common-sense precautions such as not leaving valuables in plain sight in your vehicle and staying in well-lit areas at night. For emergencies, dial 911.

Health in Columbia

The tap water in Columbia is safe to drink. Mosquitoes can be a common problem in the summer months, so bring bug repellent, especially if you plan to go on outdoor adventures.

Getting to Columbia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Columbia

  • Columbia Metropolitan Airport: Located 8 miles southwest of the city.

Bus Stations Near Columbia

  • Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Columbia.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The Columbia Metropolitan airport is serviced by Amtrak with stops throughout other major metropolitan areas.

How to Get Around in Columbia

  • Car: The city is easily navigable by car, and parking areas are abundant throughout the city.
  • Public Transportation: The Central Midlands Regional Transit Authority offers local transit service throughout Columbia.
  • Ride-Sharing: Both Uber and Lyft operate in Columbia, South Carolina.

Where to Go Next from Columbia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Charleston: A charming city with a rich history, beaches, and great food that’s just a two-hour drive from Columbia.
  • Myrtle Beach: A famous coastal destination with white sandy beaches and plenty of attractions, just a three-hour drive from Columbia.
  • Charlotte, North Carolina: Known for its impressive skyline, great local breweries, and excellent sporting events for both professional and collegiate sports.

Conclusion

Columbia, South Carolina, offers a perfect blend of history, natural beauty, and a broad range of activities and tours for any traveling adventurers. With so much to explore, a week-long itinerary begins with the South Carolina State House and South Carolina State Museum and ends with local boutiques and stores for souvenirs. Regardless of your itinerary, experience local food and drink like sweet tea and mint juleps along the way. Safe travels and enjoy your trip!
