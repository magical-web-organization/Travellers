Introduction to Congaree National Park

Congaree National Park is a breathtaking natural wonder located in Greater Columbia in South Carolina, United States of America, in North America. Offering over 27,000 acres of untouched wilderness, Congaree National Park features a diverse range of flora and fauna, including the largest high-ground hardwood swamp in the world.

This guide aims to provide you with an in-depth exploration of Congaree National Park, including a list of the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, must-try dishes, cultural customs, safety tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Congaree National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Harry Hampton Visitor Center

The Harry Hampton Visitor Center is the main entrance for visitors to Congaree National Park. It features interactive exhibits on the park’s natural and cultural history, a film about the park’s unique ecosystem, and a shop where you can purchase park-related merchandise.

The Boardwalk Trail

The Boardwalk Trail is a mile-long elevated boardwalk that takes visitors through a primeval forest. Along the way, visitors can see a variety of plants and animals, including the park’s signature bald cypress trees and the endangered red-cockaded woodpecker.

Cedar Creek Canoe Trail

The Cedar Creek Canoe Trail is a 15-mile water trail that allows visitors to paddle through the heart of Congaree National Park. Along the way, visitors can see majestic cypress trees, towering tupelo trees, and diverse wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Congaree National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bluff Trail

The Bluff Trail is a 7.5-mile trail that takes visitors through a variety of ecosystems, including oak-hickory forest, bottomland hardwood forest, and floodplain.

Weston Lake Loop Trail

The Weston Lake Loop Trail is a 4.4-mile trail that takes visitors through the heart of the park. Along the way, visitors can see diverse wildlife, including white-tailed deer and yellow-bellied sapsuckers.

Congaree River Blue Trail

The Congaree River Blue Trail is a 50-mile water trail that takes visitors along the Congaree River. Along the way, visitors can see a variety of natural and cultural sites, including the Old Columbia Dam and Congaree National Monument.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Congaree National Park

Bluff Trail

The Bluff Trail is a 7.5-mile trail that takes visitors through a variety of ecosystems, including oak-hickory forest, bottomland hardwood forest, and floodplain. It is one of the most popular trails in Congaree National Park, featuring stunning views of the Congaree River and diverse wildlife.

Sims Trail

The Sims Trail is a 4.6-mile trail that takes visitors through a variety of ecosystems, including bottomland hardwood forest and freshwater marsh. Along the way, visitors can see a variety of plants and animals, including wildflowers and bobcats.

Rock Hop Trail

The Rock Hop Trail is a 2.4-mile trail that takes visitors through a scenic forested area. Along the way, visitors can see a variety of trees, including hardwood and pine.

Best Things to Do in Congaree National Park: Activities and Tours

Guided Canoe Tours

Guided canoe tours are a great way to explore the park’s waterways and see diverse wildlife up close. Tours are led by experienced guides who can provide information about the park’s natural and cultural history.

Ranger-Led Programs

Ranger-led programs offer visitors an opportunity to learn more about the park’s natural and cultural history. Programs include guided hikes, campfire talks, and night sky programs.

Astronomy Programs

Astronomy programs offer visitors an opportunity to explore the night sky and learn about the park’s natural history. Programs include stargazing parties and telescope viewing.

Located just a short drive from Columbia, South Carolina, Congaree National Park is an easy addition to any week-long itinerary. Here’s our suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Congaree National Park:

Local Culture and Customs of Congaree National Park

Southern Hospitality

South Carolina is known for its warm and welcoming culture. Visitors to Congaree National Park can expect to be welcomed with open arms.

Gullah Culture

Gullah culture is a unique blend of African and American traditions. Visitors to the area surrounding Congaree National Park can learn more about Gullah culture by visiting local museums and cultural sites.

  • Follow Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Congaree National Park

Visitors to Congaree National Park should always exercise caution while hiking and paddling. Be sure to have a map and plenty of water, and be prepared for changing weather conditions.
