Introduction to Greater Columbia

Located in the heart of South Carolina, Greater Columbia is a vibrant and bustling city with a rich history and culture. From its beautiful parks and natural attractions to its world-class museums and historic landmarks, there is no shortage of things to see and do in this exciting destination. In this travel guide, we will explore all that Greater Columbia has to offer, from top tourist attractions to hidden gems.

List of Places to Visit in Greater Columbia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The culture and history of Greater Columbia can be seen in the many museums and historical places scattered throughout the city. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • South Carolina State Museum: The state’s largest museum is dedicated to telling the story of South Carolina from prehistoric times to the present day.

  • Columbia Museum of Art: This museum boasts an impressive collection of European and American art, as well as works by regional artists.

  • South Carolina State House: The State House is a beautiful example of Greek Revival architecture and the historic center of Columbia.

  • Robert Mills House and Gardens: This historic home was designed by Robert Mills, the architect of the Washington Monument, and features lush gardens and period furnishings.

Natural Attractions of Greater Columbia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Greater Columbia offers a variety of options to get back to nature. Here are some of the best natural attractions:

  • Congaree National Park: This national park is home to the largest intact tract of old-growth bottomland hardwood forest in the country.

  • Saluda Shoals Park: Located on the banks of the Saluda River, this park features hiking trails, river access, and picnic areas.

  • Lake Murray: This man-made lake is perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming, with over 500 miles of shoreline to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greater Columbia

The Greater Columbia area offers many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails to experience:

  • Three Rivers Greenway: The Three Rivers Greenway is a system of linear parks located along the Broad, Saluda, and Congaree Rivers.

  • Harbison State Forest: This 2,200-acre forest offers over 20 miles of trails for hikers and mountain bikers.

  • Palmetto Trail: This 500-mile trail runs through the state of South Carolina and offers a variety of terrains and landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Greater Columbia: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural attractions and historic sites, Greater Columbia offers a variety of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Riverbanks Zoo and Garden: This award-winning zoo is home to over 2,000 animals and features beautiful botanical gardens.

  • Brewery and Distillery Tours: Greater Columbia is home to several breweries and distilleries that offer tours and tastings, including Columbia Craft Brewing Company and Copper Horse Distilling.

  • Segway Tours: Explore the city in a unique way with a Segway tour of downtown Columbia.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greater Columbia

For those planning a week-long trip to Greater Columbia, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the South Carolina State Museum and explore the Columbia Canal and Riverfront Park.
  • Day 2: Hike the trails at Harbison State Forest or visit the Columbia Museum of Art.
  • Day 3: Take a brewery or distillery tour and have lunch at one of the city’s many restaurants.
  • Day 4: Visit the historic Robert Mills House and Gardens and stroll through Finlay Park.
  • Day 5: Spend the day at Lake Murray, boating or fishing.
  • Day 6: Explore Congaree National Park and take a nature walk.
  • Day 7: Visit Riverbanks Zoo and Garden and have dinner in the Vista district.

Food and Drink Scene of Greater Columbia

Must-Try Dishes

The Greater Columbia area offers a diverse array of dining options, with Southern cuisine being the highlight. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Shrimp and Grits: A classic Southern dish of sautéed shrimp and creamy grits.
  • Fried Chicken: A Southern staple that is crisp, juicy, and full of flavor.
  • Pimento Cheese: A spread made with cheddar cheese, pimento peppers, and mayonnaise, often used as a dip or spread for sandwiches.

Local Beverages

In addition to the many restaurants, Greater Columbia is home to several local breweries and distilleries. Be sure to try these local drinks:

  • River Rat Brewery: This popular brewery serves up a variety of craft beers, including their signature Broad River Red Ale.
  • Copper Horse Distilling: Visit this distillery for a tasting of their award-winning bourbon and whiskey.

Where to Stay in Greater Columbia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greater Columbia offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Hampton Inn Columbia - Historic District: Located in the heart of downtown, this hotel is within walking distance to many attractions.
  • Columbia Marriott: This upscale hotel offers luxurious amenities, including a spa and rooftop bar.
  • HI Columbia Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorm-style rooms and a communal kitchen for guests.

What to Buy in Greater Columbia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of Greater Columbia, there are many unique souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some ideas:

  • Palmetto Cheese: This popular brand of pimento cheese spread is made in South Carolina.
  • Sweetgrass Baskets: Handmade by local artisans from sweetgrass, these baskets are a Southern tradition.
  • South Carolina BBQ Sauce: Stock up on this delicious sauce made from mustard and vinegar, a popular condiment in the state.

Local Culture and Customs of Greater Columbia

The people of Greater Columbia are known for being warm and friendly. Common customs and traditions in the region include:

  • Respect for History: With many historic sites and landmarks, the people of Greater Columbia hold a deep respect for their local history.
  • Hospitality: Southern hospitality is alive and well in Greater Columbia, with locals welcoming visitors with open arms.
  • Love of Food: The food scene in Greater Columbia is a huge part of the local culture, with an emphasis on comfort foods and Southern cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Greater Columbia

The best time to visit Greater Columbia is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greater Columbia

Do:

  • Do visit the many historical landmarks and museums in the region.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including barbecue and fried chicken.
  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore safety precautions, especially when hiking or biking in natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers and bartenders.
  • Don’t be shy about asking for recommendations from locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greater Columbia

Overall, Greater Columbia is a safe place to visit, with a relatively low crime rate. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take precautions when traveling alone or at night. In particular, visitors to natural areas should be mindful of wildlife and terrain hazards.

Health in Greater Columbia

For travelers from outside the United States, it is important to have travel medical insurance that will cover emergency medical care. It is also important to stay hydrated, especially when spending time outdoors in hot weather.

Getting to Greater Columbia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greater Columbia

The nearest major airport to Greater Columbia is the Columbia Metropolitan Airport (CAE), which is serviced by several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Greater Columbia

Greyhound bus lines offers daily service to the Greater Columbia area from multiple locations across the country.

Other Options

For those driving to Greater Columbia, major highways that serve the region include I-20, I-26, and I-77.

How to Get Around in Greater Columbia

The best way to get around Greater Columbia is by car, although public transportation options are available. Ride-share services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Greater Columbia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Greater Columbia is ideally situated for exploring other destinations in the region. Here are some nearby places to consider:

  • Charleston, SC: Known for its historic charm and beautiful beaches, Charleston is just two hours away.
  • Blue Ridge Mountains: Just a few hours northwest, the Blue Ridge Mountains offer scenic views and outdoor adventures.
  • Myrtle Beach, SC: One of America’s favorite beach destinations, Myrtle Beach is three hours from Greater Columbia.

Conclusion

Whether you are interested in history and culture, natural attractions, or outdoor adventures, Greater Columbia has something for everyone. With its welcoming locals, vibrant dining scene, and myriad of activities, Greater Columbia is a destination not to be missed.
