Introduction to Lexington

Located in Greater Columbia, South Carolina, Lexington is a charming town that boasts a rich history and breathtaking natural sceneries. As the county seat, Lexington offers plenty of facilities and amenities that cater to both locals and tourists. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or quaint small-town vibes, Lexington is a destination that won’t disappoint.

List of Places to Visit in Lexington

There are plenty of things to do and see in Lexington, and here are some of the must-visit places that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lexington County Museum - Travel back in time with a visit to this fascinating living history museum that features historic buildings, artifacts, and exhibits that showcase the region’s heritage.
  • The Old Mill Antique Mall - Located in a historic mill, this antique mall offers a wide array of vintage items that reflect both local and international history.
  • The Lexington County Courthouse - This elegant courthouse boasts a neoclassical design and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Natural Attractions of Lexington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Peachtree Rock Heritage Preserve - This natural area is home to unique rock formations, waterfalls, and hiking trails that lead you through breathtaking landscapes.
  • The Saluda River - Go kayaking or canoeing on the tranquil waters of this picturesque river that offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and wildlife.
  • The Congaree National Park - Just a short drive away from Lexington, this national park is a treasure trove of biodiversity and offers hiking trails, fishing spots, and guided tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lexington

If you’re in the mood for a more active adventure, Lexington offers several hiking and biking routes that cater to different skill levels. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • The Lake Murray Dam Walkway - Enjoy scenic views of the lake and city skyline as you walk or bike across this dam that connects Lexington to Columbia.
  • The Three Rivers Greenway - This 12-mile paved trail runs along the Congaree, Saluda, and Broad rivers and offers stunning views of the natural landscapes and wildlife.
  • The Harbison State Forest Trails - With over 20 miles of trails, this forest is a favorite among hikers and mountain bikers due to its diverse terrain and beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Lexington: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring cultural and natural attractions, Lexington offers a wide array of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Lexington County Peach Festival - This annual event features live music, food vendors, crafts, and a peach-themed parade that showcases the region’s seasonal abundance.
  • The Wine Tastings at Mercer House Estate Winery - Savor local wines and enjoy beautiful scenery with a visit to this winery that offers guided tastings and tours.
  • The Horseback Riding Tours at Whispering Pines Farm - Immerse yourself in nature by embarking on a horseback riding tour that showcases Lexington’s natural landscapes and wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Lexington

Lexington offers a rich and diverse food and drink scene that showcases local flavors and ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Lexington-style BBQ - This type of barbecue features pork that is slow-cooked over hickory wood and served with a tangy mustard-based sauce.
  • The Shrimp and Grits - This classic Southern dish features shrimp cooked in a savory gravy and served over creamy grits.
  • The Fried Chicken - This crispy and juicy comfort food is a staple in many Lexington restaurants and diners.

Local Beverages

  • The Muscadine Wine - This wine is made from locally-grown muscadine grapes and offers a unique flavor that reflects the region’s terroir.
  • The Peach Tea - This refreshing iced tea is brewed with locally-sourced peaches and offers a sweet and aromatic taste.
  • The Craft Beers - Lexington boasts several breweries and taprooms that offer a wide array of locally-crafted beers that cater to different tastes and preferences.

What to Buy in Lexington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Lexington

Lexington has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, food, art, and events. Some of the most notable cultural events and customs in the town include:

  • The Lexington County Peach Festival - This annual festival celebrates the region’s peach harvest with a range of food, music, and cultural events.
  • The Historic Downtown Lexington - This area boasts several historic buildings and landmarks that reflect Lexington’s history and architectural styles.
  • The Southern Hospitality - Lexington is known for its friendly and welcoming locals who are always ready to share their stories and recommendations with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Lexington

Lexington enjoys a mild and pleasant climate throughout most of the year, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the seasons and their highlights:

  • Spring (March - May) - This season offers mild temperatures, blooming flowers, and lively outdoor events, such as the Lexington County Spring Fair.
  • Summer (June - August) - This season is perfect for outdoor adventures, water sports, and cultural festivals, such as the Lexington County Peach Festival.
  • Fall (September - November) - This season offers cooler temperatures, colorful foliage, and seasonal flavors, such as pumpkin spice and apple cider.
  • Winter (December - February) - This season offers occasional snowfalls, cozy indoor activities, and holiday-themed events, such as the Lexington County Christmas Parade.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lexington

To fully enjoy your stay in Lexington, it’s important to be aware of some of the do’s and don’ts of the culture and customs. Here are some tips that can help:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine. Lexington offers a rich and diverse food scene that is worth exploring and savoring.
  • Do be respectful to the locals and their customs. Southern hospitality is a real thing, and most locals appreciate visitors who show politeness and respect.
  • Do use sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors. The sun and bugs can be intense during some seasons, so it’s important to protect yourself.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural landscapes. Lexington’s scenic views and outdoor attractions are some of its main highlights, so it’s important to preserve them for future generations.
  • Don’t use offensive language or behavior. Lexington is a friendly and welcoming town, but any form of discrimination or prejudice is not tolerated.
  • Don’t forget to tip if you receive good service. Tipping is a customary practice in most restaurants, bars, and other hospitality venues.

Safety and Health

Lexington is generally a safe and healthy town to explore, but it’s important to take some precautions and be aware of the following:

Safety in Lexington

  • Watch out for traffic. Although Lexington is a walkable and bikeable town, some roads and intersections can be busy, so it’s important to pay attention and follow traffic rules.
  • Be aware of natural hazards. Some outdoor attractions, such as hiking trails and rivers, can present some risks and dangers, so it’s important to be prepared and follow safety guidelines.
  • Take care of your belongings. Although Lexington has a low crime rate, it’s still important to keep an eye on your valuables and belongings, especially in crowded areas or public spaces.

Health in Lexington

  • Stay hydrated. The Southern heat and humidity can be intense, so it’s important to drink plenty of water and other fluids to avoid dehydration.
  • Protect yourself from sun and bug bites. Long sleeves, hats, sunscreen, and insect repellent are essential, especially during the peak seasons.
  • Pack any necessary medication or supplies. If you have any allergies, chronic conditions, or special needs, make sure to bring all the necessary medication and supplies with you.

Getting to Lexington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lexington is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane, and here are some details on each transportation option:

  • Driving to Lexington - Lexington is located near several major highways, including I-20 and I-26, and offers plenty of parking facilities and rental car services.
  • Taking a Bus to Lexington - Several bus companies, such as Greyhound and Megabus, offer regular services to Columbia, which is located a short drive from Lexington.
  • Flying to Lexington - The Columbia Metropolitan Airport is the closest airport to Lexington and offers direct flights from several major cities, including Atlanta, Charlotte, and Dallas.

Airports Near Lexington

  • Columbia Metropolitan Airport - Located 9 miles away from Lexington, this airport offers direct flights to several major cities in the USA.

Bus Stations Near Lexington

  • Greyhound - Columbia Bus Station - Located 14 miles away from Lexington, this station offers regular services to several destinations in the USA.

Other Options

  • Uber and Lyft - Both Uber and Lyft offer services in Lexington and are a convenient and affordable way to get around.
  • Renting a Car - Several car rental services, such as Avis, Budget, and Enterprise, are available in Lexington and can be used to explore the town and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Lexington

Lexington is a small and walkable town, but it also offers several transportation options for visitors who prefer to explore the town and its surroundings. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Walking - Most of Lexington’s attractions are located within walking distance of each other, and the town offers plenty of sidewalks and pedestrian-friendly areas.
  • Biking - Lexington offers several bike rental services and bike-friendly routes that cater to different levels and preferences.
  • Driving - If you prefer to explore the town and its surroundings at your own pace, renting a car is a popular option that offers flexibility and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Lexington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lexington offers plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Columbia - Located just a short drive away from Lexington, Columbia is the state capital and offers plenty of cultural, natural, and culinary attractions.
  • Lake Murray - This serene lake is located just a few miles west of Lexington and offers plenty of recreational activities, such as boating, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Charleston - Located a 2-hour drive away from Lexington, Charleston is a historic and charming city that offers a rich mix of Southern hospitality, architecture, and culture.

Conclusion

Lexington is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and Southern hospitality. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or quaint small-town vibes, Lexington is a destination that won’t disappoint.
