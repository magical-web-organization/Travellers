Thoroughbred Country is a charming region in South Carolina, known for its sprawling farms, picturesque countryside, and rich culture and history. Here, you’ll find plenty of things to do, see, and experience, whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie. In this guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local tips to help you plan your trip to Thoroughbred Country.

Introduction to Thoroughbred Country

Located in the heart of South Carolina, Thoroughbred Country is a region that spans several counties, including Aiken, Edgefield, and Saluda. The region is known for its vast horse farms, where thoroughbred horses are bred and trained. A visit to Thoroughbred Country allows you to experience both the beauty of the countryside and the rich traditions and culture of the area’s people.

List of Places to Visit in Thoroughbred Country

With so much to see and do in Thoroughbred Country, it can be challenging to narrow down your itinerary. Here are some of our top picks for places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thoroughbred Country has a rich history, as evidenced by the numerous museums and historic sites in the area. Some of these include:

  • Aiken Thoroughbred Racing Hall of Fame and Museum: This museum pays tribute to the region’s equestrian heritage, showcasing the history of horse racing and breeding.

  • Redcliffe Plantation State Historic Site: This historic home was built in the early 19th century and is now open to the public as a museum.

  • Old Edgefield District Genealogical Society Library and Archives: This library is an excellent resource for those interested in tracing their family history.

Natural Attractions of Thoroughbred Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thoroughbred Country is home to many breathtaking natural attractions, including:

  • Hitchcock Woods: This sprawling forest is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  • Edisto River: A trip down the Edisto River offers kayaking and tubing enthusiasts a chance to enjoy the scenic views and the great outdoors.

  • Horseshoe Lake: This tranquil lake is an excellent spot for fishing or picnicking and is home to a variety of wildlife, including egrets and herons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thoroughbred Country

With its rolling hills and winding roads, Thoroughbred Country is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of our favorite hiking and biking trails in the region:

  • Canal Trail: This trail is a 7.5-mile stretch that runs along the old towpath of the Augusta Canal. It offers scenic views of the historic waterway.

  • Hitchcock Woods Trail: With over 70 miles of trails, Hitchcock Woods is a popular destination for hikers and bikers alike.

  • Graniteville Trail: This trail crosses the beautiful Horse Creek and offers views of scenic waterfalls and historic structures.

Best Things to Do in Thoroughbred Country: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in horse racing or wine tasting, Thoroughbred Country has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Aiken Trolley Tour: This tour takes you around Aiken and its historic districts, showcasing the town’s beautiful architecture and fascinating history.

  • Thoroughbred Country Wine Trail: This self-guided tour takes you through some of the region’s top wineries, each offering unique varietals and beautiful settings.

  • Steeplechase Races: Thoroughbred Country is known for its horse racing, and the Aiken Trials and Aiken Steeplechase are two of the region’s most popular events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thoroughbred Country

To help you make the most of your trip to Thoroughbred Country, here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the most popular attractions in the region:

Day Activity

Food and Drink Scene of Thoroughbred Country

Thoroughbred Country is home to a range of dining options, from casual cafes to upscale restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages you should sample on your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: This Southern classic is a must-try in Thoroughbred Country, and local favorite spots include the Union Street Deli and Ole Timey Meats.

  • Grits: No Southern breakfast is complete without a bowl of grits, and the Chicken Biscuit at TakoSushi’s Sunday Brunch is sure to satisfy.

  • Boiled Peanuts: These salty snacks are a Southern tradition, and you can find them at roadside stands throughout the region.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: This refreshing beverage is a quintessential Southern drink and can be found at most restaurants throughout Thoroughbred Country.

  • Belle Isle Moonshine: This locally distilled moonshine comes in a variety of flavors, including Honey Habanero and Blood Orange.

Where to Stay in Thoroughbred Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thoroughbred Country has a range of accommodation options, from cozy bed and breakfasts to larger hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Willcox: This historic hotel in downtown Aiken offers upscale rooms and amenities, including a full-service spa.

  • Carriage House Inn: Located in Aiken’s historic district, this charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a gourmet breakfast.

  • Saluda Shoals Park: This park offers campsites with full hookup and primitive camping options.

What to Buy in Thoroughbred Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thoroughbred Country has no shortage of locally made goods and souvenirs to take back home with you. Here are some of the best products to look out for:

  • Silver Bluff Farms Grass-fed Beef: This farm produces high-quality beef that you can purchase at local markets throughout the region.

  • Pottery by Jim and Barbara Latham: These local potters create beautiful, handcrafted pieces that make for unique souvenirs.

  • Thoroughbred Trail Mix: This delicious snack mix is made by Southern Gourmet, a local company that uses ingredients sourced from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Thoroughbred Country

To get a taste of local culture in Thoroughbred Country, we recommend checking out some of the following events:

  • Aiken’s Makin’: This annual arts and crafts show takes place in downtown Aiken and features hundreds of local vendors.

  • Hopelands Summer Concert Series: Enjoy live music in the beautiful Hopelands Gardens in Aiken every Monday evening during the summer months.

Best Time to Visit Thoroughbred Country

Thoroughbred Country is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re interested in horse racing, plan to visit during the Aiken Trials in March or the Aiken Steeplechase in April. Otherwise, fall and spring are beautiful times to visit, with milder temperatures and colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thoroughbred Country

When visiting Thoroughbred Country, keep in mind these do’s and don’ts to make the most of your trip:

Do:

  • Dress for the Weather: Thoroughbred Country can be warm and humid in the summer months, so pack accordingly.

  • Take a Horseback Riding Lesson: If you’re a beginner to horse riding, don’t miss the chance to take a lesson with one of Thoroughbred Country’s many experienced trainers.

Don’t:

  • Miss the Local Festivals: Thoroughbred Country is home to numerous festivals and events throughout the year, so make sure to check out what’s happening during your visit.

  • Overestimate Your Hiking Abilities: Many of Thoroughbred Country’s hiking trails have steep inclines, so make sure to choose a trail that matches your fitness level.

Safety and Health

While Thoroughbred Country is a generally safe area, it’s always important to take proper safety precautions during your stay. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Thoroughbred Country

  • Always lock your car doors and keep valuables out of plain sight.

  • If hiking, make sure to bring enough water and avoid trails after heavy rainfall.

Health in Thoroughbred Country

  • For those with allergies, be aware that Thoroughbred Country is home to many types of plants and animals.

  • To avoid tick-borne illnesses, make sure to use insect repellent and check for ticks after spending time outdoors.

Getting to Thoroughbred Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thoroughbred Country is easily accessible by car and has several nearby airports. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Thoroughbred Country

  • Augusta Regional Airport: This airport is about 30 miles from Aiken and offers nonstop flights to several cities.

  • Columbia Metropolitan Airport: This airport is about 60 miles from Aiken and offers nonstop flights to several major cities.

Bus Stations Near Thoroughbred Country

  • Greyhound Bus: This bus line has multiple stations in the region, including one in Aiken.

Other Options

  • Renting a Car: If flying into a nearby airport, renting a car is an easy and convenient way to get around Thoroughbred Country.

How to Get Around in Thoroughbred Country

While having a car is the easiest way to explore Thoroughbred Country, there are other transportation options available. These include:

  • Taxi Services: Several taxi services operate in the region, including Aiken Yellow Cab and Aiken Limo.

  • Rideshare Services: Lyft and Uber both operate in Thoroughbred Country.

Where to Go Next from Thoroughbred Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, consider visiting one of these nearby destinations:

  • Congaree National Park: Located in Columbia, South Carolina, this stunning national park is known for its towering cypress trees and beautiful swamps.

  • Savannah, Georgia: Only a few hours’ drive from Thoroughbred Country, Savannah offers charming architecture, delicious food, and plenty of southern charm.

Conclusion

Thoroughbred Country offers visitors a chance to experience the rich culture and beautiful natural scenery of South Carolina. Whether you’re interested in horse racing, hiking, or simply exploring historic sites, this region has something for everyone. With our guide, you’ll be able to plan a memorable trip to Thoroughbred Country and create memories that will last a lifetime.
