Introduction to Aiken

Located in Thoroughbred Country, Aiken is a charming city in the heart of South Carolina. Known for its lush gardens, historic downtown area, and equestrian heritage, Aiken is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a mix of culture and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re interested in visiting museums, hiking through scenic trails, or indulging in local cuisine, Aiken has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Aiken

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aiken County Historical Museum: Housed in a former mansion, this museum features exhibits on the history of Aiken.
  • Aiken Thoroughbred Racing Hall of Fame and Museum: Discover the history of horse racing in Aiken at this museum, which showcases memorabilia and artifacts from the sport.
  • Redcliffe Plantation State Historic Site: Located nearby in Beech Island, this historic site features a plantation house and slave quarters, offering a glimpse into the antebellum period.

Natural Attractions of Aiken: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hopelands Gardens: A serene 14-acre garden in the heart of Aiken, Hopelands Gardens features fountains, ponds, and walking paths.
  • Aiken State Park: Set on the banks of the Edisto River, Aiken State Park offers hiking trails, fishing, and camping.
  • Hitchcock Woods: As one of the largest urban forests in the country, this 2100-acre woodland is a haven for hikers, horseback riders, and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aiken

Aiken is known for its picturesque trails, perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • North Augusta Greenway: This 7.5-mile paved trail connects the town of North Augusta to the Savannah River.
  • Aiken County Trails: These trails span over 60 miles, passing through scenic landscapes, historic sites, and natural areas.
  • The Palmetto Trail: A long-distance trail that stretches over 350 miles, the Palmetto Trail passes through Aiken and offers views of the state’s diverse ecology.

Best Things to Do in Aiken: Activities and Tours

  • Aiken Trolley Tours: See the city’s historic homes and landmarks aboard a trolley tour.
  • Aiken Antique Mall: Shop for antique and vintage items at this treasure trove of unique finds.
  • Horseback Riding: Embrace Aiken’s equestrian culture with a horseback riding tour at one of the nearby stables.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aiken

Day 1: Visit the Aiken County Historical Museum and explore the city’s downtown area. Day 2: Take a hike in Hitchcock Woods or go fishing at Aiken State Park. Day 3: Explore Hopelands Gardens and take a trolley tour of the city. Day 4: Visit the Aiken Thoroughbred Racing Hall of Fame and Museum and take a horseback riding tour. Day 5: Take a day trip to Redcliffe Plantation State Historic Site. Day 6: Go on a shopping spree at the Aiken Antique Mall and enjoy dinner at one of the city’s restaurants. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the city’s parks and scenic views, or take a cycling tour of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Aiken

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: A southern staple, try the crispy fried chicken at local restaurants.
  • Shrimp and Grits: A Lowcountry dish, this creamy and savory meal is a must-try when in Aiken.
  • Pimento Cheese Sandwich: A delicious southern sandwich made with pimento cheese spread.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A refreshing and sweet drink that is a staple in the south.
  • Wine Country: Aiken is home to three wineries and vineyards, where you can sample local wines.

Where to Stay in Aiken: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Willcox: A historic hotel in the heart of Aiken, The Willcox offers luxurious rooms and fine dining.
  • The Guest House at Houndslake: This charming guesthouse features comfortable rooms and access to a golf course.
  • The Carriage House Inn: A quaint bed and breakfast located in a restored 19th-century house.

What to Buy in Aiken: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Horse-related souvenirs: Aiken’s equestrian heritage means there are plenty of horse-related souvenirs to choose from, such as horse figurines or riding gear.
  • Locally-made crafts: Aiken is home to many talented artisans, whose works range from pottery to jewelry.
  • Sweets and Treats: Stock up on locally-made chocolates and sweets to bring home as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Aiken

Aiken has a culture rooted in the traditions of the south, including a love for horse racing and a passion for the outdoors. Residents are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and it’s common courtesy to say “hello” or “howdy” when greeting someone.

Best Time to Visit Aiken

Aiken’s temperate climate makes it an ideal destination for year-round travel. Spring and fall are especially beautiful, with blooming gardens and changing leaves. Summer can be hot and humid, while winters are mild but can occasionally bring ice and snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aiken

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites to learn about Aiken’s rich history.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities, such as hiking and horseback riding.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially southern staples like fried chicken and shrimp and grits.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural areas.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Forget to say “hello” or “howdy” when greeting someone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aiken

Aiken is a relatively safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions such as locking car doors and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Aiken

Aiken has several medical facilities and clinics, as well as pharmacies and grocery stores for purchasing any necessary health and wellness items.

Getting to Aiken: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aiken

The nearest airports to Aiken are Augusta Regional Airport (AGS) and Columbia Metropolitan Airport (CAE), both located within an hour’s drive.

Bus Stations Near Aiken

The Greyhound bus station is located in Augusta, Georgia, just a short drive from Aiken.

Other Options

Aiken is easily accessible by car via I-20, which passes through the city.

How to Get Around Aiken

Aiken is a walkable city, but there are also options for public transportation, such as the Aiken Trolley and bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Aiken: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Augusta, Georgia: Located just across the border, Augusta is home to beautiful gardens, historic sites, and the iconic Masters golf tournament.
  • Columbia, South Carolina: The state’s capital offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums and theaters.
  • Charleston, South Carolina: A two-hour drive from Aiken, Charleston is a charming coastal town known for its southern hospitality and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Aiken is a beautiful destination that offers something for everyone, whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a charming southern town. With this comprehensive guide to Aiken, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip and make lasting memories.
