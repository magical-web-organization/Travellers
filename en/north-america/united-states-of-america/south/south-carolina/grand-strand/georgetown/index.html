Georgetown, located in Grand Strand, is a beautiful coastal city in South Carolina. Known for its rich history, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, Georgetown is the perfect destination for a relaxing vacation. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Georgetown.

Introduction to Georgetown

Georgetown is a quaint coastal city with a population of approximately 9,000 people. Founded in 1729, Georgetown is one of the oldest cities in South Carolina and has a rich history. The city is located on the banks of Winyah Bay, which provides visitors with stunning scenic views. Georgetown is also known for its beautiful beaches, outdoor adventures, and delicious seafood.

List of Places to Visit in Georgetown

Georgetown has a variety of attractions that are perfect for history buffs and nature lovers alike. Here are some of the top places to visit in Georgetown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kaminski House Museum: This historic site was built in 1769 and is now a museum that showcases the history and culture of Georgetown.
  • South Carolina Maritime Museum: This museum offers exhibits on the history of seafaring in South Carolina, including an antique boat collection and a working wooden boat shop.
  • Georgetown County Museum: Visitors can learn about the county’s history through exhibits that highlight Native American artifacts, plantation life, and the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Georgetown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hobcaw Barony: A 16,000-acre nature preserve that runs from the Waccamaw River to the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can explore the wetlands, salt marshes, and forests on guided tours.
  • Brookgreen Gardens: This beautiful sculpture garden and wildlife preserve features over 2,000 works of art, including sculptures of animals, plants, and human figures.
  • Huntington Beach State Park: This beach park is a perfect spot for swimming, fishing, and birdwatching. Visitors can also hike the nature trails or take a guided tour of Atalaya Castle, a National Historic Landmark.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Georgetown

For those seeking outdoor adventures, Georgetown has several hiking and biking trails to explore, including:

  • Waccamaw Neck Bikeway: This bike trail runs through Georgetown and several other coastal towns, offering views of the Waccamaw River and the Intracoastal Waterway.
  • Lewis Ocean Bay Heritage Preserve: This nature preserve has eight miles of hiking trails that run through a forest of pine trees and wetlands.
  • East Bay Park Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking and birdwatching and offers views of Winyah Bay and the Georgetown Lighthouse.

Best Things to Do in Georgetown: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Georgetown, including:

  • Fishing Charter Tours: Visitors can book fishing charters that leave from Georgetown Harbor and explore the waters around Georgetown.
  • Georgetown Lighthouse Tour: This tour allows visitors to climb to the top of the historic Georgetown Lighthouse, which offers stunning views of Winyah Bay.
  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take boat tours of the Winyah Bay and the Intracoastal Waterway, where they can see dolphins and other wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Georgetown

If you’re planning to spend a week in Georgetown, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Georgetown

The food and drink scene in Georgetown is centered around seafood. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • She-Crab Soup: A creamy soup made with crab meat, roe, and heavy cream.
  • Fried Shrimp: Fresh shrimp breaded and fried to perfection.
  • Crab Cakes: A local specialty made with a mix of crab meat, spices, and bread crumbs.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A classic Southern beverage made with sweetened black tea.
  • Palmetto Amber Ale: A locally brewed beer with a slightly sweet flavor.
  • Firefly Sweet Tea Vodka: A vodka infused with sweet tea, a favorite local beverage.

Where to Stay in Georgetown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Georgetown has several options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top choices:

  • The Harbor House Bed & Breakfast: A historic bed and breakfast located in downtown Georgetown, with views of the harbor.
  • Quality Inn & Suites: An affordable hotel located near the airport and several attractions in Georgetown.
  • The Greyhound Flats Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in a historic building in downtown Georgetown.

What to Buy in Georgetown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Georgetown offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that are perfect for taking home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Sweetgrass Baskets: Handwoven baskets made by local artisans.
  • Georgetown Lighthouse Souvenirs: T-shirts, hats, and other souvenirs featuring the Georgetown Lighthouse.
  • Local Artwork: Paintings, photographs, and sculptures by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Georgetown

Georgetown has a strong Gullah Geechee culture, which is a unique African-American culture that originated in the Lowcountry of South Carolina. Visitors can learn more about this culture by attending events like the annual Gullah Geechee Festival and the Gullah Geechee Cultural Heritage Corridor Commission events.

Best Time to Visit Georgetown

The best time to visit Georgetown is from May to October when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the peak tourist season and prices tend to be higher. To avoid the crowds and enjoy lower prices, consider visiting in the shoulder season from April to May or September to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Georgetown

Do:

  • Do take a guided tour of the city to learn about its rich history.
  • Do try the local seafood and sweet tea.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t swim in areas where signs indicate dangerous conditions.
  • Don’t litter or disturb the wildlife in the nature preserves.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Georgetown

Georgetown is a safe city, but it’s always important to be cautious when exploring a new place. Visitors should keep their valuables secured and avoid walking alone at night. Check the weather and any warnings on swimming or visiting the nature preserves before embarking.

Health in Georgetown

Georgetown’s healthcare system is equipped to handle most emergencies. Visitors with prescriptions should bring extra medicine or prescription in case a refill is needed on their trip.

Getting to Georgetown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Georgetown

  • Myrtle Beach International Airport: 30 miles from Georgetown
  • Charleston International Airport: 90 miles from Georgetown

Bus Stations Near Georgetown

  • Georgetown Bus Station: 335 Hazard St, Georgetown, SC 29440

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to rent a car and drive into Georgetown.

How to Get Around in Georgetown

Visitors can explore downtown Georgetown on foot. However, to explore the surrounding natural areas and attractions, it’s best to rent a car or use a ride-sharing app such as Lyft or Uber.

Where to Go Next from Georgetown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Georgetown is situated near several other popular destinations, including:

  • Myrtle Beach: Located 30 miles from Georgetown, Myrtle Beach is known for its beautiful beaches, amusement parks, and golf courses.
  • Charleston: A historic city known for its antebellum architecture, museums, and Southern cuisine, Charleston is located 90 miles from Georgetown.
  • Pawleys Island: A quiet beach town located 17 miles from Georgetown, Pawleys Island offers a relaxing respite from the bustle of the city.

Conclusion

Georgetown, South Carolina is a beautiful destination with a rich history, stunning scenery, and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re ready to plan your trip and experience all that Georgetown has to offer.
