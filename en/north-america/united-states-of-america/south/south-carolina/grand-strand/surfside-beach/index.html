Surfside Beach Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour of South Carolina’s Hidden Gem

Surfside Beach is a charming coastal town that boasts stunning beaches, rich cultural heritage, and a laid-back atmosphere. Located in the heart of Grand Strand, South Carolina, this hidden gem is often overlooked by tourists who flock to more popular destinations in the area. But if you’re looking for a place that’s off the beaten path, with fewer crowds, and plenty of activities, Surfside Beach is the place to be.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Surfside Beach, showcasing all the must-visit places, hidden gems, local cuisines, and tips to make your trip easy and worth-remembering.

Local Culture and Customs of Surfside Beach

Surfside Beach has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its Southern roots. When visiting, keep in mind these local customs:

  • Respect local traditions and cultural practices.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious and historical sites.
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Surfside Beach

The best time to visit Surfside Beach is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. July and August are peak tourist seasons and can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surfside Beach

Do:

  • Respect the environment, and dispose of trash in designated areas.
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun.
  • Be courteous and friendly to locals and other visitors.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural and historic sites.
  • Disturb or harm wildlife.
  • Ignore warning signs on beaches and outdoor areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surfside Beach

Surfside Beach is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s still essential to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings.
  • Lock your doors and windows at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.

Health in Surfside Beach

There are no major health concerns to worry about when visiting Surfside Beach. If you have any pre-existing medical condition or require medical attention, head to Grand Strand Medical Center or Tidelands Waccamaw Community Hospital.

Getting to Surfside Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Surfside Beach is easily accessible by air, bus, or car.

Airports Near Surfside Beach

The nearest airport to Surfside Beach is the Myrtle Beach International Airport, located just 7 miles away. Other nearby airports include Florence Regional Airport and Charleston International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Surfside Beach

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Myrtle Beach, just a few miles from Surfside Beach. Other bus operators include Southeastern Stages and the Trans-Bridge Lines.

Other Options

You can also drive to Surfside Beach via highways like Route 17 or US 501. When in town, getting around is easy via rental cars, taxis, shuttle services or bicycles.
