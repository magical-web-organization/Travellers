Introduction to Murrells Inlet

Located in the Grand Strand of South Carolina, Murrells Inlet is a small, quaint fishing village with a rich history and natural beauty. It is known for its marsh boardwalk, delicious seafood, and exciting outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a quiet getaway or an adventure-packed vacation, Murrells Inlet has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Murrells Inlet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Atalaya Castle: This stunning historic castle was built in 1931 by Anna Huntington, the wife of American sculptor Archer Huntington. The castle is now a part of the Huntington Beach State Park and offers guided tours.
  • Brookgreen Gardens: This 9,100-acre sculpture garden is home to over 2,000 works of art and offers tours of the historic rice plantations.
  • Georgetown County Museum: Located in nearby Georgetown, this museum features exhibits that showcase the history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Murrells Inlet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Huntington Beach State Park: This park offers hiking trails, birdwatching, fishing, and beach access. It’s home to a variety of wildlife, including alligators and loggerhead sea turtles.
  • Murrells Inlet Marshwalk: This boardwalk offers breathtaking views of the saltwater marshes and is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Waccamaw Neck Bikeway: This 18-mile trail offers a scenic route for hiking and biking through the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Murrells Inlet

  • Waccamaw Neck Bikeway: This 18-mile trail passes through several parks and nature reserves and offers scenic views of Murrells Inlet.
  • Huntington Beach State Park Trails: This state park offers several hiking trails that take you through the woods, along the beach, and around the lagoons.

Best Things to Do in Murrells Inlet: Activities and Tours

  • Inlet Adventure Mini Golf: This mini-golf course is located in the heart of Murrells Inlet and offers a fun and challenging experience for all ages.
  • Capt. Dick’s Marina: This marina offers boat rentals, fishing charters, and eco-tours.
  • Crazy Sister Marina: This marina offers a variety of water activities, such as jet ski rentals, dolphin watching tours, and parasailing.

Food and Drink Scene of Murrells Inlet

Must-Try Dishes

  • She-crab Soup: This creamy soup is a local specialty made with crab meat and crab roe.
  • Fried Seafood Platter: This platter offers a variety of fresh seafood, such as shrimp, oysters, and clams, that are lightly battered and fried to perfection.
  • Lowcountry Boil: This hearty dish of shrimp, sausage, potatoes, and corn on the cob is a staple in southern cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: This cold, refreshing tea is a southern classic and pairs perfectly with any meal.
  • Local Craft Beer: Murrells Inlet has a growing craft beer scene, with several breweries offering unique and delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Murrells Inlet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inlet Sports Lodge: This boutique hotel has upscale rooms and suites and offers access to a private golf course.
  • Ellington at Wachesaw Plantation East: This resort features spacious villas and is located near several golf courses and beaches.
  • The Inlet View at Oyster Bay: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms with stunning views of the inlet.

What to Buy in Murrells Inlet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artwork: Murrells Inlet is home to many talented artists and artisans, and you can find unique pieces at the local galleries.
  • Seafood Spices: Bring home the flavors of Murrells Inlet by purchasing seafood spices that are used in local restaurants.
  • Gullah Sweetgrass Baskets: These baskets are made by the Gullah people, who are descendants of West African slaves and are unique to the Lowcountry region of South Carolina.

Local Culture and Customs of Murrells Inlet

Murrells Inlet has a rich history and culture. The Gullah people have had a significant influence on the region, and their art, music, and cuisine are celebrated. The local culture also revolves around the fishing and seafood industries, and the annual Blessing of the Inlet ceremony is an important event.

Best Time to Visit Murrells Inlet

The best time to visit Murrells Inlet is from March to May and from September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months can be hot and humid, but offer plenty of water activities and outdoor adventures. The winter months are generally quiet, but can be a good option for a peaceful getaway.

Do’s and Don’ts of Murrells Inlet

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Try the local seafood and traditional southern dishes.
  • Engage with the local art and culture.

Don’t:

  • Feed or harass wildlife.
  • Forget to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated in the hot summer months.
  • Ignore the significance of Gullah culture and history.

Safety and Health

Safety in Murrells Inlet

Murrells Inlet is generally a safe destination, but it’s important to be aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Use common sense and follow basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables secured and locking your car doors.

Health in Murrells Inlet

The water in Murrells Inlet is generally safe for swimming, but it’s important to note that there are sharks and jellyfish in the area. It’s important to check for any beach advisories before swimming, and to take precautions against sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Murrells Inlet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Murrells Inlet

  • Myrtle Beach International Airport: This airport is located about 14 miles north of Murrells Inlet and is served by several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Murrells Inlet

  • Myrtle Beach Greyhound Station: This station is located in Myrtle Beach, about 12 miles north of Murrells Inlet.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Murrells Inlet is easily accessible by car, and there are several car rental options available.
  • Private Transfer: Private transfer options are also available for those who prefer more personalized transportation.

How to Get Around in Murrells Inlet

The best way to get around Murrells Inlet is by car or bike. There are also several local taxi and rideshare services available, such as Uber and Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Murrells Inlet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Georgetown: This charming town is located about 15 miles south of Murrells Inlet and is known for its historic district and waterfront restaurants.
  • Myrtle Beach: This popular beach destination is located about 12 miles north of Murrells Inlet and offers plenty of attractions and entertainment options.
  • Charleston: This beautiful and historic city is located about 95 miles south of Murrells Inlet and is a must-visit destination for those interested in southern culture and history.

Conclusion

Murrells Inlet is a hidden gem in the Grand Strand of South Carolina, offering a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural significance, and exciting activities. From outdoor adventures to indulging in local cuisine, Murrells Inlet has something to offer for everyone.
