Introduction to St. George

Located in the Greater Charleston area, on the southern coast of South Carolina, St. George is a charming town steeped in history and surrounded by natural beauty. Founded in 1758, the town has a rich colonial history and is well-known for its historic architecture and cultural landmarks. Visitors to St. George can expect to discover many hidden gems in this peaceful and picturesque part of South Carolina.

List of Places to Visit in St. George

Whether you’re looking for a cultural experience, outdoor adventure, or simply want to relax and soak up the local atmosphere, St. George has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best places to visit in St. George:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. George is home to many historical sites and museums that offer visitors a glimpse into the town’s past. Here are some must-see cultural attractions:

  • The St. George Museum: A local museum that displays artifacts and exhibits about the town’s early history and culture.
  • Warren House: A historic home built in the Greek Revival style in the early 1800s.
  • Dorchester Academy: A former school for African Americans that played an important role in the Civil Rights Movement.

Natural Attractions of St. George: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Surrounded by natural beauty, St. George offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor exploration and adventure. These are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • SC Canal and Wildlife Preserve: A scenic area with hiking trails, boating, and fishing opportunities.
  • Givhans Ferry State Park: A place to relax, picnic, hike, and paddle and is also known for its serene beauty.
  • Edisto River Canoe and Kayak Trail: A beautiful river that runs through St. George, perfect for kayaking or canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. George

St. George has several great hiking and biking routes that offer visitors the chance to explore the area’s natural beauty. Popular routes include:

  • Sandhills Greenway Trail: A paved trail that is great for biking or walking and is 5.2 miles long in total.
  • Francis Beidler Forest: A serene and peaceful trail that winds through a natural cypress swamp.

Best Things to Do in St. George: Activities and Tours

St. George offers a variety of fun and unique activities that visitors can enjoy. These are some of the best things to do in St. George:

  • Blackwater Outdoor Adventures: Rent kayaks, canoes, and stand-up paddleboards and explore the Edisto River.
  • Mattrix Party Rentals: Rent inflatables, bounce houses, and other party equipment for a fun gathering with family or friends.
  • Lowcountry Guided Birding Day Trips: Take a guided birdwatching tour and explore the diverse bird population in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. George

If you have a week to spend in St. George, here is a sample itinerary that includes some of the best activities and attractions:

Day 1: Arrive in St. George and check into your hotel or Airbnb. Day 2: Take a walking tour of St. George’s historic district and visit some of the town’s museums and landmarks. Day 3: Explore the Edisto River by kayak or canoe and enjoy a picnic lunch at one of the river’s scenic spots. Day 4: Visit Givhans Ferry State Park for a day of hiking and picnicking. Day 5: Take a scenic drive to nearby Charleston and explore the city’s historic architecture and attractions. Day 6: Visit the SC Canal and Wildlife Preserve for a day of fishing and hiking. Day 7: Relax in St. George and visit some of the town’s local restaurants and shops before departing for home.

Food and Drink Scene of St. George

St. George is known for its delicious southern cuisine. Visitors are encouraged to try some of the local dishes and drinks, such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gullah Gumbo: This stew is a staple of South Carolina’s low country and is made with Okra, shrimp, and a secret blend of spices.
  • Low-Country Boil: A seafood stew containing shrimp, crab, corn, and potatoes.
  • Fried Chicken: A southern classic, St. George’s fried chicken is juicy and flavorful.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A southern staple, sweet tea is available in most restaurants.
  • Palmetto Ale: A local beer made in nearby Charleston.

Where to Stay in St. George: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. George has several options for accommodation, from budget-friendly options to high-end hotels. Here are some of the most popular places to stay in St. George:

  • Hampton Inn St. George: A comfortable and convenient hotel that offers a full breakfast and has a swimming pool.
  • Americas Best Value Inn: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location.
  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: A high-end hotel that offers a variety of amenities and a fitness center.

What to Buy in St. George: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. George offers many unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home to remember their stay. Here are some ideas for what to buy in St. George:

  • Gullah Geechee Sweetgrass Baskets: These unique woven baskets are made by descendants of former slaves and are a South Carolina tradition.
  • Palmetto Cheese: A local favorite, Palmetto Cheese is a blend of cheddar and pimento peppers that is perfect for snacking.
  • Yellow Jasmine Soap: A locally-made soap that incorporates the sweet aroma of yellow jasmine, a South Carolina native plant.

Local Culture and Customs of St. George

St. George has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its history and geography. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect local customs, such as:

  • The Gullah Culture: A unique blend of African and American cultural traditions that has a strong influence in South Carolina’s low country.
  • Southern Hospitality: Southerners are known for their graciousness and hospitality, so visitors should expect to be welcomed warmly by locals.
  • Conservative Attitudes: St. George is a conservative community, so visitors should be respectful of local customs and etiquette.

Best Time to Visit St. George

The best time to visit St. George is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. George

Visitors to St. George should keep the following tips in mind:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Respect local customs and traditions, such as the Gullah culture.
  • Get Outdoors: Take advantage of the area’s natural beauty and explore the river or hiking trails.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Savor the flavors of southern cuisine and try some of the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the town clean and beautiful by disposing of trash properly.
  • Disrespect Historical Sites: Be respectful when visiting historical sites and museums.
  • Overpack: Pack lightly and bring only what you need for your trip.

Safety and Health

While St. George is a generally safe and healthy place to visit, visitors should keep the following tips in mind:

Safety in St. George

  • Watch for Ticks: Ticks are prevalent in wooded areas, so visitors should wear insect repellent and check for ticks after spending time outdoors.
  • Be Aware of Wildlife: If exploring the outdoors, be mindful of local wildlife such as snakes and alligators.
  • Lock Doors and Windows: As in any town, be sure to lock doors and windows when leaving your accommodation or car.

Health in St. George

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially during hot summer months.
  • Protect Skin from Sun: Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect your skin from the sun.
  • Pack Medications: Be sure to pack any necessary medications, and bring a copy of your prescription just in case.

Getting to St. George: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to St. George have several transportation options to choose from:

  • Charleston International Airport: Located approximately 50 miles from St. George, Charleston’s international airport offers flights from major US cities and international destinations.
  • Bus Service: Greyhound and Southeastern Stages operate bus service to St. George’s bus station.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from the airport or a local rental agency to explore the area’s attractions.

How to Get Around in St. George

Visitors can get around St. George by car or by bicycle. Several local companies also offer bicycle rental services for visitors who want to explore the area’s natural beauty on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from St. George: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. George is located in a beautiful part of South Carolina, close to several other scenic destinations. Visitors may want to consider these nearby excursions:

  • Charleston: A historic and cultural capital of South Carolina, Charleston is a must-visit destination that offers many attractions.
  • Myrtle Beach: Known for its white sandy beaches, Myrtle Beach is a popular destination for sun-seeking tourists.
  • Beaufort: A charming coastal town that is known for its picturesque architecture and scenic views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Conclusion

St. George’s unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture make it a must-visit destination in South Carolina. From hiking and kayaking to exploring local museums and historical landmarks, there is something for everyone to enjoy in this charming town. Visitors should come hungry, ready to experience the best of southern cuisine, and be prepared to explore the beautiful surroundings of one of South Carolina’s hidden gems.
