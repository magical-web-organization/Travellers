Introduction to Hartsville

Welcome to Hartsville, a charming city located in Pee Dee Country, South Carolina. Known for its friendly community and abundance of outdoor activities, Hartsville is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a laid-back getaway. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, there’s something for everyone in Hartsville.

List of Places to Visit in Hartsville

Here are some of the top attractions and must-see places in Hartsville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coker University - Founded in 1908, Coker University is a private, liberal arts institution that offers a range of undergraduate and graduate degrees. The campus features a blend of historic and modern architecture, making it a popular spot for photography enthusiasts.
  • Hartsville Museum - Located in the historic train depot, the Hartsville Museum showcases the history of the town through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Lawton Park - This park is home to two important historical sites: the Jacob Kelley House and the Lawton House. The Kelley House is a restored 18th-century farmhouse while the Lawton House is a museum featuring a collection of Civil War artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Hartsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pride Park - This park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including a playground, tennis courts, and a walking trail.
  • Little Pee Dee State Park - Just a short drive from Hartsville, Little Pee Dee State Park is a nature lover’s paradise. The park offers hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and a tranquil river perfect for kayaking and canoeing.
  • Kalmia Gardens - This botanical garden features a diverse collection of native and exotic plants, as well as a pond, walking trails, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hartsville

Hartsville is a great place for both hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some routes to explore:

  • Coker University Nature Trail - This 1.5-mile trail winds through the campus of Coker University and offers beautiful views of the surrounding trees and wildlife.
  • Prestwood Nature Trail - This 1.3-mile trail is located in Prestwood Lake Park and offers scenic views of the lake and its resident wildlife.
  • Hartsville Rail Trail - This 7.3-mile trail starts in downtown Hartsville and follows the path of an old rail line. The trail offers a mix of urban and natural scenery and is suitable for both hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Hartsville: Activities and Tours

Looking for some fun things to do in Hartsville? Here are some suggestions:

  • Artfully Yours - This art studio offers a range of classes in painting, pottery, and other crafts. It’s a great activity for couples or families looking for a creative outlet.
  • Hartsville Farmers Market - Open from March to November, the Hartsville Farmers Market offers a variety of fresh produce, baked goods, and other local products.
  • Wine Tasting at Duplin Winery - Just a short drive from Hartsville, Duplin Winery offers a variety of wine tastings and tours. It’s a great way to try some local wines and learn about the winemaking process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hartsville

If you have a week to spend in Hartsville, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Coker University and take a nature walk on the Coker University Nature Trail.
2 Explore the Hartsville Museum and Lawton Park.
3 Hike the Hartsville Rail Trail in the morning, and spend the afternoon at Little Pee Dee State Park.
4 Take a pottery class at Artfully Yours and grab lunch at the Hartsville Farmers Market.
5 Visit Kalmia Gardens and enjoy a picnic lunch.
6 Go kayaking on the Little Pee Dee River or take a wine tasting tour at Duplin Winery.
7 Relax at Pride Park and take in the beautiful views.

Food and Drink Scene of Hartsville

Hartsville boasts a variety of dining options, from Southern comfort food to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and grits - This classic Southern dish can be found at many local restaurants, including Mr. B’s Seafood and Grill.
  • Fried green tomatoes - Another Southern staple, fried green tomatoes are a must-try dish at any restaurant in Hartsville.
  • Pimento cheeseburger - Hartsville’s own Venus Pie Pizzeria is known for their pimento cheeseburger, which features a juicy beef patty topped with creamy pimento cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea - A Southern classic, sweet tea can be found at almost every restaurant in Hartsville.
  • Firefly vodka - Made in nearby Charleston, Firefly vodka is a popular drink among locals and visitors alike.
  • Kalmia Gardens tea - This soothing herbal tea is made from the plants grown at Kalmia Gardens and is available for purchase at their gift shop.

Where to Stay in Hartsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hartsville offers a variety of accommodations, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some options:

  • The Mantissa Executive Suites and Spa - This boutique hotel offers luxurious suites, an on-site spa, and a rooftop bar with stunning views of the city.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Hartsville - A popular choice for budget-conscious travelers, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • The Hartsville Hostel - This hostel offers budget-friendly accommodations in a shared dormitory or private room setting.

What to Buy in Hartsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for something to bring home from your trip to Hartsville? Here are some locally made products and souvenirs to consider:

  • Kalmia Gardens tea or honey - These soothing teas and local honey are made from the plants and bees at Kalmia Gardens.
  • Pottery from Artfully Yours - Take home a handcrafted piece of pottery made in Hartsville.
  • Local artwork - The Black Creek Arts Council offers a variety of locally made art, including paintings, prints, and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Hartsville

Hartsville is known for its friendly community and southern hospitality. When visiting, be sure to show respect for local customs and traditions, such as saying “yes, ma’am” or “no, sir” when speaking to elders.

Best Time to Visit Hartsville

The best time to visit Hartsville is from March to May or from September to November when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hartsville

Do:

  • Do try the Southern food and local beverages.
  • Do explore the local art scene and visit the Hartsville Museum.
  • Do take advantage of the abundance of outdoor activities, such as hiking and kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to show respect for the local culture and customs.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the parks and outdoor areas.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for recommendations or help from locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hartsville

Hartsville is a safe community, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, keep your valuables secure, and follow any posted safety guidelines at natural attractions.

Health in Hartsville

There are several medical facilities in Hartsville, including Carolina Pines Regional Medical Center and Hartsville Urgent Care. It’s recommended to have travel insurance and to check any health advisories before your trip.

Getting to Hartsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hartsville

The closest airport to Hartsville is the Florence Regional Airport, located about 30 miles away. Myrtle Beach International Airport is also a viable option, located about 70 miles southeast of Hartsville.

Bus Stations Near Hartsville

The closest Greyhound bus station to Hartsville is located in Florence, about 20 miles away.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors to Hartsville, as it provides more flexibility and freedom to explore the surrounding areas. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

How to Get Around in Hartsville

The best way to get around in Hartsville is by car or bike. Many of the attractions and outdoor areas are not within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Hartsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hartsville is located in a prime location for day trips and excursions. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Florence, SC - Located about 20 miles away, Florence is known for its historic downtown and abundance of cultural events and festivals.
  • Columbia, SC - The state capital of South Carolina is located about 80 miles southeast of Hartsville and offers a variety of museums, outdoor activities, and shopping.
  • Myrtle Beach, SC - Located about 70 miles southeast of Hartsville, Myrtle Beach is a popular beach destination with activities such as golfing, shopping, and amusement parks.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or an outdoor adventure, Hartsville has something for everyone. From the beautiful parks and outdoor areas to the rich history and culture, Hartsville is a gem of the South that is not to be missed.
