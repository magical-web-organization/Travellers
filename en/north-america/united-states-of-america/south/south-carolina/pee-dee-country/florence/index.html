Introduction to Florence

Located in Pee Dee County, South Carolina, Florence is a charming town with a fascinating history, a lively culture, and endless outdoor adventures. Florence is situated in the heart of Pee Dee Country, just a short drive away from the Atlantic Ocean and the Great Smoky Mountains. With its rich history, diverse culture, and scenic beauty, Florence is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Southern experience.

List of Places to Visit in Florence

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Florence has something to offer. Some of the top attractions include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Florence County Museum: This museum showcases the history, art, and culture of Pee Dee County and its people.
  • Florence National Cemetery: This cemetery serves as the final resting place for soldiers who fought in various American wars.
  • War Between the States Museum: This museum features an extensive collection of artifacts, documents, and other items related to the American Civil War.
  • William H. Johnson House: This historic home was the birthplace of William H. Johnson, a renowned African American artist.

Natural Attractions of Florence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jeffries Creek Park: This park offers hiking trails, picnic shelters, and scenic views of the creek.
  • Lynches River County Park: This park offers kayaking, fishing, and hiking opportunities, as well as a nature center and a playground.
  • Florence Rail Trail: This twenty-mile trail is perfect for biking, jogging, and nature walks.
  • Ebenezer Park: This park features a lake, a playground, and camping facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Florence

  1. Pee Dee River Blue Trail: This 76-mile trail runs from the town of Cheraw to the Winyah Bay, offering scenic views of the river, wildlife, and historic sites.
  2. City Center Trail: This trail is a one-and-a-half-mile loop that offers a glimpse of Florence’s historic downtown area.
  3. Lynches River County Park Trail: This trail is a seven-mile loop that offers scenic views of the river and the park.

Best Things to Do in Florence: Activities and Tours

  • Florence Food Tour: This tour takes you on a culinary journey through Florence’s historic downtown area, where you can sample Southern cooking and learn about the city’s rich food culture.
  • Florence Ghost Tour: This tour takes you through the city’s most haunted locations, where you can hear tales of ghosts, spirits, and other paranormal activities.
  • Segway Tour of Florence: This tour takes you on a fun and unique ride through the city’s historic downtown area and other landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Florence

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and grits: This classic Southern dish is a combination of creamy grits and succulent shrimp, often topped with a savory bacon gravy.
  • Fried chicken: This iconic dish is a staple in Southern cuisine, and Florence has many restaurants that serve it up hot and crispy.
  • Barbecue: No trip to the Pee Dee region would be complete without trying the local barbecue, which is slow-cooked over wood and seasoned with a tangy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: This quintessential Southern beverage is an iced tea that’s sweetened with sugar and served with lemon.
  • Cheerwine: This soda is a beloved Southern classic, with a fruity and fizzy flavor that’s hard to resist.
  • Craft beer: Florence has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries that offer unique and flavorful brews.

Where to Stay in Florence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Florence: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Florence’s downtown area, and offers elegant rooms and suites.
  • Red Roof Inn: This budget-friendly hotel is conveniently located near the city’s major attractions, and offers comfortable rooms and free breakfast.
  • Country Inn & Suites: This hotel is located near the airport, and offers spacious rooms and suites with all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Florence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Palmetto roses: These delicate flowers are made from palmetto fronds, and are a popular souvenir among visitors to South Carolina.
  • Peanut brittle: This sweet and crunchy treat is a Southern classic, and Florence has several shops that sell it fresh and delicious.
  • Local crafts: Florence has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and you can find everything from handmade pottery to one-of-a-kind jewelry in the local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Florence

Florence is a friendly and welcoming community, where Southern hospitality is a way of life. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and traditions, and to experience the unique culture of the Pee Dee region.

Best Time to Visit Florence

The best time to visit Florence is from March to May and from September to November, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Florence

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, and discover the unique flavors of the Pee Dee region.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for walking and hiking, as Florence has many beautiful outdoor trails and parks.
  • Do take the time to explore the local shops and markets, and discover the handmade crafts and souvenirs that make Florence special.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural environment, as Florence is home to many scenic parks and outdoor destinations.
  • Don’t drive too fast or recklessly on the roads, as safety is a top priority in Florence.
  • Don’t forget to be respectful of the local customs and traditions, and to treat the community with kindness and courtesy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Florence

Florence is generally a safe and friendly community, but visitors should take precautions to stay safe, such as locking their cars and valuables, and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Florence

Visitors to Florence should ensure that they have the necessary vaccinations and medications before traveling, and should take steps to protect themselves from mosquitos and other insects.

Getting to Florence: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Florence

  • Florence Regional Airport: This airport serves the Florence area, and offers flights to several major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Florence

  • Florence Greyhound Station: This bus station is located in downtown Florence, and offers daily bus service to several major destinations.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in downtown Florence, and explore the city and surrounding area at their own pace.
  • Taxi service: Several taxi companies operate in the Florence area, offering convenient transportation for visitors.

How to Get Around in Florence

Visitors to Florence can get around by car, bike, or foot, depending on their preferences and needs. The city has several reliable taxi companies, as well as a network of bike trails and pedestrian paths.

Where to Go Next from Florence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many exciting destinations and excursions near Florence, including:

  • Myrtle Beach: This popular beach town is just a short drive from Florence, and offers miles of pristine shoreline, shopping, and dining.
  • Columbia: South Carolina’s capital city is just an hour’s drive from Florence, and offers historic landmarks, museums, and gardens.
  • Asheville: This charming mountain town is just a few hours’ drive from Florence, and offers scenic beauty, craft breweries, and delicious foods.

Conclusion

Florence is a hidden gem in the Pee Dee region of South Carolina, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re seeking a relaxing vacation or an active getaway, Florence has something to offer. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your perfect trip to Florence and discover all that this charming Southern town has to offer.
