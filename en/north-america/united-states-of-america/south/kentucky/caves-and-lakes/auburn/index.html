Introduction to Auburn

Nestled in the Caves and Lakes region of Kentucky, Auburn is a small but charming town known for its rich cultural and natural attractions. From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Auburn offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Auburn is the perfect destination for your next getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Auburn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Auburn is home to several museums and historical places where visitors can learn about the town’s rich past. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • The Auburn Museum: Housed in a historic log cabin, the Auburn Museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the town’s history and heritage.

  • The Logan County Courthouse: Built in 1870, the Logan County Courthouse is a beautiful example of Italianate architecture and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

  • The Octagon Hall Museum: This octagonal-shaped house was built in 1863 and is now a museum that showcases the life and history of the Shakers and the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Auburn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Auburn is surrounded by natural beauty and offers several outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Lake Malone State Park: This beautiful park features a 788-acre lake, hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and a variety of water sports.

  • The Russellville Walking Trail: A 3.5-mile scenic trail that meanders through historic neighborhoods, downtown Russellville, and countryside.

  • Shakertown at South Union: Located just a few miles from Auburn, Shakertown at South Union is a historic village that showcases the culture and way of life of the Shakers. Visitors can explore restored buildings, gardens, and exhibits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auburn

Auburn and its surrounding areas offer several hiking and biking trails that will take you through beautiful natural landscapes and historical sites. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Tailwater Trail: A 1.5-mile paved trail that winds along the banks of the Barren River and offers scenic views of the river and surrounding forests.

  • The Auburn Walking Trail: A 1.2-mile trail that takes you through historic Auburn and offers views of historic sites, buildings, and natural beauty.

  • The Shaker Village Loop Trail: A 3.5-mile loop trail that takes you through Shakertown at South Union and offers scenic views of the village, river, and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Auburn: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Auburn offers several other activities and tours that visitors will enjoy.

  • Wine Tasting on the Wine Trail: Auburn is located on the Kentucky Wine Trail, which features several wineries that offer tastings, tours, and beautiful views of the countryside.

  • Horseback Riding at Rock’n B Ranch: Located just outside of Auburn, Rock’n B Ranch offers horseback riding for all skill levels and tours of their beautiful ranch.

  • Zipline Adventures: The Edge Adventures Zipline Course offers a thrilling way to see the beautiful forests and natural beauty of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auburn

Day 1: Visit the Auburn Museum and Logan County Courthouse.

Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Lake Malone State Park and the Russellville Walking Trail.

Day 3: Discover the history and way of life of the Shakers at Shakertown at South Union.

Day 4: Hike or bike the Tailwater Trail and enjoy the beautiful views of the Barren River.

Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour of the Kentucky Wine Trail and enjoy the scenic countryside.

Day 6: Horseback riding at Rock’n B Ranch.

Day 7: Zipline adventure at The Edge Adventures Zipline Course.

Food and Drink Scene of Auburn

Auburn has a growing food and drink scene with several local establishments that offer unique dishes and local flavors. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwich at Southern Yankee BBQ: This delicious sandwich features slow-cooked pulled pork with a sweet and tangy BBQ sauce.

  • Hot Brown at Claudia’s Tea Room: This classic Kentucky dish features turkey, bacon, tomato, and Mornay sauce on Texas toast.

  • Burgers at Xtreme Burgers and Wings: Xtreme Burgers and Wings offers a variety of delicious burgers with unique toppings and flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon at MB Roland Distillery: Located just a few miles from Auburn, MB Roland Distillery offers a variety of bourbon and distillery tours.

  • Craft Beers at Back Alley Brewery: Back Alley Brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are brewed on-site in their historic building.

Where to Stay in Auburn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auburn offers several options for accommodation that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • The Belmont Inn Bed and Breakfast: This beautifully restored historic home offers cozy and comfortable rooms, delicious breakfast, and friendly service.

  • The Auburn Hotel: This newly renovated hotel offers modern amenities, comfortable rooms, and a convenient location downtown.

  • The Cabin at Lake Malone: This rustic cabin offers beautiful views of the lake, full kitchens, and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Auburn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Auburn offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts or mementos of your trip. Some of the best options include:

  • Shaker-inspired Crafts and Gifts at Shakertown at South Union: Visitors can browse a variety of handmade crafts and gifts inspired by the Shaker way of life.

  • Wine from the Kentucky Wine Trail: Kentucky produces a variety of world-class wines, and visitors can bring home a bottle or two from one of the wineries on the Kentucky Wine Trail.

Local Culture and Customs of Auburn

Auburn is a small town with a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should expect southern hospitality and a relaxed pace of life. Residents are proud of their history and heritage and enjoy sharing it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Auburn

The best time to visit Auburn is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the natural beauty of the area is at its most vibrant. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auburn

Do:

  • Embrace the relaxed pace of life and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

  • Visit the local museums and historical places to learn about the town’s rich history and heritage.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, especially the bourbon and wine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack insect repellent for outdoor adventures.

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations or directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Auburn

Auburn is a safe and friendly town with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions when traveling, such as locking doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Auburn

Visitors should take precautions for tick and mosquito bites, as these insects can carry diseases that can be harmful to humans. It’s also important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Auburn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Auburn

The closest airport to Auburn is the Nashville International Airport, located approximately 70 miles south of Auburn. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to Auburn.

Bus Stations Near Auburn

The closest bus station to Auburn is the Greyhound Bus Station in Bowling Green, located approximately 40 miles north of Auburn.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Auburn by car or bike via the historic US Route 68, which offers beautiful scenic views of the countryside.

How to Get Around in Auburn

Most of the attractions in Auburn are within walking or biking distance of downtown. However, visitors may need a car to reach some of the more remote natural attractions and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Auburn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Auburn is a great starting point for exploring the Caves and Lakes region of Kentucky. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Located approximately 40 miles east of Auburn, Mammoth Cave National Park is the world’s longest known cave system and offers guided tours and beautiful natural landscapes.

  • The Corvette Museum: Located approximately 50 miles north of Auburn, the Corvette Museum is dedicated to the history and evolution of the iconic American sports car.

  • Bowling Green: Located approximately 40 miles north of Auburn, Bowling Green is a thriving city with a vibrant downtown, rich history, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Auburn is a small town with a rich history and culture, beautiful natural landscapes, and a growing food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Auburn offers something for everyone. So pack your bags and plan your getaway to this charming small town in the heart of Kentucky.
