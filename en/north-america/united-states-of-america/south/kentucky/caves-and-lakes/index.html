Introduction to Caves and Lakes

Caves and Lakes is a region located in the southern part of Kentucky, in the United States of America. The region is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, primarily characterized by a network of underground caves and pristine lakes. These caves and lakes provide the perfect blend of adventure, nature, and beauty, making them an ideal destination for travelers looking for an escape into the wilderness.

In this guide, we have compiled a list of the best things to do, places to explore, and local experiences that must not be missed when visiting Caves and Lakes, Kentucky. From cultural landmarks and outdoor adventures to local cuisine and souvenirs, this guide has got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Caves and Lakes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mammoth Cave National Park: One of the most popular attractions in Caves and Lakes, Mammoth Cave National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world’s longest known cave system.
  • Dinosaur World: A family-friendly attraction, Dinosaur World is a park with over 150 life-sized sculptures of dinosaurs.
  • National Corvette Museum: A mecca for car enthusiasts, the National Corvette Museum displays the history of the iconic American car.
  • Abraham Lincoln Birthplace National Historical Park: The birthplace of the 16th U.S. President, Abraham Lincoln, is a fascinating place to visit for history buffs.
  • Historic RailPark & Train Museum: Housed in a restored 1925 train station, this museum features exhibits and interactive displays highlighting the history of passenger train travel.

Natural Attractions of Caves and Lakes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lost River Cave: This underground cave system is perfect for guided tours, hiking, kayaking, and zip-lining adventures.
  • Lake Cumberland: The largest lake in the region, Lake Cumberland offers swimming, boating, and fishing opportunities.
  • Nolin Lake State Park: A beautiful park with hiking trails, picnic areas, and boating facilities.
  • Red River Gorge: A paradise for hikers and rock climbers, this natural wonder has over 100 natural stone arches and spectacular views.
  • Green River Lake: A serene lake perfect for kayaking, fishing or just enjoying the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caves and Lakes

  • Mammoth Cave Railroad Bike and Hike Trail: A 9-mile trail that runs between Mammoth Cave National Park and the Green River Ferry Boat Ramp.
  • Lost River Cave Trail System: A network of hiking and biking trails that run through the Lost River Cave and Valley.
  • Green River Lake State Park Trails: A system of trails ranging from easy to moderate difficulty levels that offer stunning views of the lake and forest.
  • Levi Jackson Wilderness Road State Park Trails: A collection of trails that offer a glimpse into the region’s history as an early pioneer settlement area.

Best Things to Do in Caves and Lakes: Activities and Tours

  • Mammoth Cave Adventure Tours: A variety of cave tours, ranging from easy walks to crawling adventures, are available at Mammoth Cave National Park.
  • Zipline and Kayak Tours at Lost River Cave: This tour company offers a variety of outdoor activities, including zip-lining, kayaking, and paddle-boarding.
  • Lake Cumberland Boat Rentals: Rent a boat and explore the stunning beauty of Lake Cumberland at your own pace.
  • Horseback Riding at Red River Gorge: Enjoy the beauty of the Red River Gorge on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Big South Fork Scenic Railway: An excursion train ride through the Big South Fork River valley, offering scenic views and a glimpse into history.

Food and Drink Scene of Caves and Lakes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kentucky Hot Brown: An open-faced sandwich that originated in Kentucky, made with turkey, bacon, tomato, and Mornay sauce on bread, topped with cheese and broiled.
  • Burgoo: A traditional stew made with meats and vegetables, often served at local festivals.
  • Fried Chicken: A Southern staple, fried chicken is a must-try when in Kentucky.
  • Derby Pie: A pie made with chocolate and walnuts, traditionally served during the Kentucky Derby.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: Kentucky is known as the Bourbon Capital of the World, and the state produces some of the best bourbon on the planet.
  • Ale-8-One: A locally produced soft drink that has been a Kentucky favorite since the 1920s.
  • Wine: Kentucky is home to numerous wineries, producing a variety of wines using locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Caves and Lakes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mammoth Cave Hotel: Located within Mammoth Cave National Park, this hotel offers convenient access to the park’s attractions.
  • Lost River Cave Cottages: These cozy cottages are located within walking distance of the Lost River Cave and offer comfortable accommodations amid beautiful surroundings.
  • Green River Lake State Park Lodge: A rustic lodge situated on the bank of Green River Lake, offering spectacular views and outdoor recreational opportunities.
  • Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: A historic lodge nestled in a forested area overlooking the Cumberland River, offering guest rooms and cabins.

What to Buy in Caves and Lakes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bourbon: A bottle of Kentucky bourbon makes an excellent souvenir or gift.
  • Mammoth Cave National Park Souvenirs: Shop for t-shirts, hats, postcards, and other souvenirs at the park’s gift shop.
  • Local Handmade Crafts: Many artisans and crafters create beautiful handmade items that capture the spirit of Kentucky and Caves and Lakes.
  • Ale-8-One: A six-pack of Ale-8-One is a delicious and unique item to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Caves and Lakes

Kentucky and Caves and Lakes are known for their Southern hospitality and warm welcome to visitors. The region has a rich history, and the locals are proud of their heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and customs, and take the time to learn about the region’s history and its people.

Best Time to Visit Caves and Lakes

The best time to visit Caves and Lakes is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is changing. Summer months can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caves and Lakes

Do:

  • Do take a guided tour of Mammoth Cave National Park.
  • Do visit the local museums and historical landmarks.
  • Do sample the local cuisine, especially the iconic Kentucky Hot Brown.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Don’t enter caves without a guide.
  • Don’t neglect to check the weather forecast before outdoor activities.
  • Don’t forget to tip waitstaff and service employees.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caves and Lakes

Visitors should exercise caution and follow all safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities, particularly those involving water or heights. Make sure to stay hydrated and wear appropriate clothing and gear.

Health in Caves and Lakes

Visitors should be aware of tick and mosquito-borne illnesses and take appropriate measures to protect themselves. Bring insect repellent and wear long pants and sleeves when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities.

Getting to Caves and Lakes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caves and Lakes

  • Nashville International Airport (BNA): Located 90 miles from Mammoth Cave National Park.
  • Louisville International Airport (SDF): Located 110 miles from Mammoth Cave National Park.
  • Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport (CVG): Located 150 miles from Mammoth Cave National Park.

Bus Stations Near Caves and Lakes

  • Greyhound Bus Station Bowling Green: Located 30 miles from Mammoth Cave National Park.
  • Greyhound Bus Station Elizabethtown: Located 60 miles from Mammoth Cave National Park.

Other Options

  • Driving: Visitors can drive to Caves and Lakes via Interstate 65.
  • Rental Car: Rental car companies are available at both Nashville and Louisville airports.

How to Get Around in Caves and Lakes

The best way to get around Caves and Lakes is by car, as public transportation is not readily available. Various rental car companies can be found at Nashville and Louisville airports.

Where to Go Next from Caves and Lakes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Louisville, Kentucky: Home to the Kentucky Derby, Louisville is a vibrant city with plenty of culinary and cultural experiences to offer.
  • Nashville, Tennessee: Known as the Country Music Capital of the World, Nashville is a lively city with a rich musical history.
  • Cincinnati, Ohio: A gateway to the Midwest, Cincinnati is a charming city with an impressive skyline and plenty of art and culture.

Conclusion

Caves and Lakes, Kentucky is a nature lover’s paradise, with its underground caves, pristine lakes, and stunning natural beauty. The region also offers a wealth of cultural experiences, fantastic local cuisine, and plenty of outdoor adventures to keep visitors busy for days. Whether you’re looking for a family-friendly vacation, outdoor activities, or a romantic getaway, Caves and Lakes has something for everyone.
