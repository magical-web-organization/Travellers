Introduction to Morgantown

Morgantown, a small town nestled in the heart of Caves and Lakes region in Kentucky, is a perfect getaway for nature enthusiasts and history buffs. Known for its scenic beauty, adventurous hiking trails, and rich history, Morgantown attracts travelers from across the globe.

List of Places to Visit in Morgantown

Below is a list of top-rated attractions that you shouldn’t miss during your visit to Morgantown.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Butler County Courthouse: Built-in 1859, Butler County Courthouse is a historic landmark that stands as a testament to the county’s rich and complex history. The architecture and design of the courthouse are remarkable, and it is one of the most well-preserved courthouses in the state.

  • The Armory: The Armory is a historic military building that was used as a drill hall and meeting place for the National Guard in the early 1900s. Today, it serves as a community center and event venue.

  • A.M. Brown’s Store Museum: This museum is a restored early 1900s general store that provides visitors with a glimpse into the past. The store contains original merchandise and fixtures from the turn of the century.

Natural Attractions of Morgantown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Green River: The Green River is a beautiful waterway that runs through Morgantown. It provides a perfect setting for kayaking, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Located just a short drive from Morgantown, Mammoth Cave is the largest cave system in the world. Visitors can take a guided tour through the cave’s vast underground chambers and learn about the unique geology and history of the area.

  • Nolin Lake: This pristine lake is a popular destination for water activities such as swimming, boating, and fishing. Nolin Lake State Park offers campgrounds, cabins, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morgantown

Morgantown is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Kentucky. Check out these popular routes:

  • Green River Ferry Trail: This easy 2.5-mile trail winds along the Green River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Russellville Walking Trail: This 3-mile paved trail winds through the heart of Russellville and provides scenic views of the town’s historic landmarks.

  • Shanty Hollow Lake Trail: This moderate 3.5-mile trail takes you through dense forests and past a charming lake.

Best Things to Do in Morgantown: Activities and Tours

  • Kentucky Down Under Adventure Zoo: This unique attraction offers visitors a chance to get up close and personal with Australian animals such as kangaroos, wallabies, and emus.

  • Morgantown City Park: The park has a playground, picnic area, and a walking trail. It is an excellent spot for family outings or a peaceful nature walk.

  • The Peacock Playhouse: This 1930s-style theater hosts plays, concerts, and other performances throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Morgantown

If you’re planning to spend a week in Morgantown, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Green River Ferry Trail in the morning, and visit Butler County Courthouse in the afternoon.
2 Take a guided tour of Mammoth Cave National Park.
3 Spend the day at Nolin Lake, fishing and swimming.
4 Visit Kentucky Down Under Adventure Zoo in the morning, and go hiking at Shanty Hollow Lake Trail in the afternoon.
5 Spend the day at Morgantown City Park, pack a picnic and enjoy a relaxing day in nature.
6 Attend a performance at The Peacock Playhouse.
7 Explore the armory and A.M. Brown’s Store Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Morgantown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kentucky Hot Brown: A classic Kentucky dish, consisting of turkey and bacon on toasted bread, covered in Mornay sauce and broiled until bubbly.

  • Burgoo: A hearty stew made with a variety of meats and vegetables, often served at local festivals and gatherings.

  • Spoonbread: A cornmeal-based bread that is light and fluffy in texture, often served as a side dish with savory meals.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: The state’s signature beverage, Kentucky bourbon is produced in distilleries throughout the region and can be enjoyed at local bars and restaurants.

  • Ale-8-One: A regional soft drink that has been produced in Winchester, KY since 1926.

Where to Stay in Morgantown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Morgantown to suit every traveler’s needs and budget.

  • Butler Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in a historic home in downtown Morgantown and offers comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

  • Nolin Lake State Park Campground: This budget-friendly option offers campsites and cabins overlooking the beautiful Nolin Lake.

  • Log Cabin Inn Bed and Breakfast: This cozy B&B is located in nearby Bowling Green, KY, and offers guests a tranquil and relaxing retreat.

What to Buy in Morgantown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • A.M. Brown’s Store Museum: The store offers original merchandise and fixtures from the early 1900s, including vintage candy and soda.

  • Kentucky Bourbon: Take home a bottle of the state’s signature beverage as a memento of your trip to Morgantown.

  • Local Artisan Crafts: The region is known for its talented artisans, who produce unique handmade items such as pottery, woven baskets, and woodcarvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Morgantown

Morgantown’s culture and customs are deeply rooted in the region’s history and natural beauty. Visitors are encouraged to explore and appreciate the town’s historic landmarks and scenic landscapes.

Best Time to Visit Morgantown

The best time to visit Morgantown is during the fall when the leaves change colors, and the weather is mild. Spring is also a great time to visit when the wildflowers are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morgantown

Do:

  • Take time to appreciate the town’s historic landmarks and natural beauty.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Engage with the friendly locals, who are always happy to share their knowledge and recommendations.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Disrespect the town’s history and cultural heritage.

  • Engage in any illegal activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morgantown

Morgantown is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still exercise caution and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Morgantown

Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical insurance and take necessary precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and insect repellent, when outdoors.

Getting to Morgantown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morgantown

  • Nashville International Airport: Located 90 miles south of Morgantown, Nashville International Airport is the closest major airport to Morgantown.

  • Louisville International Airport: Located 112 miles northwest of Morgantown, Louisville International Airport is another option for visitors to the region.

Bus Stations Near Morgantown

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Bowling Green, KY, the Greyhound Bus Station offers daily bus service to and from major US cities.

Other Options

  • Driving: Morgantown is easily accessible by car, with Interstate 65 running through the region.

How to Get Around in Morgantown

The best way to get around Morgantown is by car. Rental cars are available at both Nashville and Louisville airports.

Where to Go Next from Morgantown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bowling Green, KY: Located just 20 miles south of Morgantown, Bowling Green is home to the National Corvette Museum and Lost River Cave.

  • Owensboro, KY: Located 70 miles west of Morgantown, Owensboro is known for its bluegrass music and delicious barbecue.

Conclusion

Morgantown is a hidden gem in the heart of Kentucky’s Caves and Lakes region, offering visitors a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re looking to explore the area’s unique heritage or simply enjoy a peaceful getaway in nature, Morgantown is sure to leave a lasting impression.
