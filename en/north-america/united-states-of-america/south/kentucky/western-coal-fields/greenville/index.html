Introduction to Greenville

Nestled in the Western Coal Fields of Kentucky, Greenville is a charming small town that boasts a rich history and gorgeous natural beauty. Despite its small size, Greenville is home to many cultural attractions, natural wonders, and fun activities that are worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Greenville

Whether you are a history lover who wants to explore museums and historical places, an outdoor enthusiast seeking scenic views and adventures, or a foodie searching for local dishes, Greenville has something for everyone. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Greenville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Muhlenberg County Jail Museum: This museum showcases the history of the local jail and features exhibits on infamous inmates, escape attempts, and more.
  • Everly Brothers Monument and Childhood Home: Discover the birthplace of rock-and-roll legends, the Everly Brothers. The home has been converted into a museum that showcases artifacts and memorabilia from the duo’s career.
  • Dr. Roland B. Wilson Museum: This museum highlights the history of medicine and medical practices throughout the years, as well as the life and work of Dr. Wilson, a local doctor who dedicated his life to serving his community.

Natural Attractions of Greenville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rough River Dam State Resort Park: Explore acres of natural beauty, hiking trails, and water activities at this state park that features a large lake and a variety of recreational amenities.
  • Paradise Park: Discover the beauty of nature with picturesque landscapes and nature trails featuring local flora and fauna.
  • Greenville City Park: A great spot for a picnic, this park is home to playgrounds, picnic areas, and a beautiful lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenville

  • Rough River Dam State Resort Park Trails: Take a hike or bike ride through miles of beautiful trails that wind through the park’s gorgeous landscape.
  • Shores Lake Trail: This scenic trail follows along the beautiful Shores Lake.
  • Paradise Park Nature Trails: Enjoy the beauty of nature on the park’s winding trails that weave through dense forests and rolling hills.

Best Things to Do in Greenville: Activities and Tours

  • Greenville Art Guild Gallery: Visit the local artists and explore the work of the talented creatives.
  • Moseley Memorial Music Museum: This museum showcases the history of music and the local community’s musical contributions over the years.
  • Felix E. Martin Jr. Hall: Attend a live performance or concert in a beautifully restored historic theater.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greenville

Day 1: Visit the Muhlenberg County Jail Museum, Everly Brothers Monument and Childhood Home, and Dr. Roland B. Wilson Museum.

Day 2: Explore Paradise Park, Shores Lake Trail, and Greenville City Park.

Day 3: Hike or bike Rough River Dam State Resort Park Trails and picnic near the lake.

Day 4: Visit the Greenville Art Guild Gallery and Moseley Memorial Music Museum.

Day 5: Go fishing or boating around the Rough River Lake.

Day 6: Attend a live performance at the Felix E. Martin Jr. Hall.

Day 7: Go on a scenic drive through the countryside and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenville

No trip to Greenville is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgoo: A thick and hearty stew made with a variety of meats and vegetables, this local dish is a must-try.
  • Barbecue: Greenville has some of the best barbecue in the country, and you won’t be disappointed by the juicy, smoky meats.
  • Moonlite Bar-B-Q Inn: This iconic restaurant has been around since 1963 and serves up some of the best barbecue in town.

Local Beverages

  • Ale-8-One: A local soda that has been enjoyed in Kentucky since 1926.
  • Bourbon: Kentucky is the birthplace of bourbon, and Greenville has several distilleries that offer tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Greenville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and regular events for guests to enjoy.
  • Greenville Guest House: An affordable and cozy option with comfortable rooms and free parking.
  • Lake Malone Bed and Breakfast: A charming and romantic getaway that offers beautifully decorated rooms and scenic views of the lake.

What to Buy in Greenville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Everly Brothers Memorabilia: Take home a souvenir from the Everly Brothers Museum or stop by a local antique shop to find rare memorabilia.
  • Handmade Crafts: Many local artisans sell handmade crafts like jewelry, woodwork, and pottery.
  • Moonlite Bar-B-Q Sauce: Take home a bottle of the famous barbecue sauce from the Moonlite Bar-B-Q Inn.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenville

Greenville is a small town with a tight-knit community. Visitors should expect to be greeted with warm southern hospitality and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Greenville

The best time to visit Greenville is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenville

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the local cultural attractions and natural wonders.
  • Do embrace the southern hospitality and friendly locals.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t leave trash or litter behind in parks and natural areas.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations or directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenville

Greenville is a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors should still take the necessary precautions to keep their belongings and themselves safe.

Health in Greenville

There are several medical facilities in Greenville, and visitors should ensure they have the necessary insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Greenville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Greenville is easily accessible by car, but visitors can also arrive via the following options:

Airports Near Greenville

  • Owensboro-Daviess County Regional Airport (OWB) is located 50 miles from Greenville and offers both domestic and international flights.
  • Nashville International Airport (BNA) is located 111 miles from Greenville and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Greenville

  • Greyhound Bus Station is located in Madisonville, which is 20 miles from Greenville.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive via private or rented vehicles, or by taking a taxi service available in nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Greenville

The best way to get around Greenville is by car, as most attractions and destinations are spread out. However, visitors can also rent bikes or take a taxi service for short trips.

Where to Go Next from Greenville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Located 85 miles from Greenville, this world-famous park features the world’s largest cave system.
  • Louisville: A lively city located 115 miles from Greenville, Louisville is home to the Kentucky Derby, Muhammad Ali Museum, and much more.
  • Nashville: This vibrant city is located 117 miles from Greenville and is famous for its music scene, museums, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Greenville is a charming town that has something for everyone. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, Greenville has plenty of attractions and activities to explore. Plan your perfect trip with this comprehensive tour guide, and discover all that Greenville has to offer.
