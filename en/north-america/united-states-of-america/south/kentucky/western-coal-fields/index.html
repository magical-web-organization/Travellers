Introduction to Western Coal Fields

Located in the southern region of Kentucky, the Western Coal Fields cover a vast area and are often overlooked by tourists. However, this region offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions that will leave visitors in awe.

Western Coal Fields are named after the primary source of employment in the area - coal mining. The region is also known for its important role in American history, serving as a key location during the Civil War.

List of Places to Visit in Western Coal Fields

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kentucky Coal Museum

The Kentucky Coal Museum is located in Benham and offers a fascinating glimpse into the coal mining industry that fueled the region’s economy for decades. Visitors can explore the exhibits and artifacts and learn about the challenges faced by coal miners through the years.

Civil War Fort at Columbus-Belmont State Park

The Columbus-Belmont State Park’s Civil War fort was the site of a strategic battle during the Civil War and is now an important historical landmark. Visitors can explore the fort and learn about the soldiers who fought for their beliefs.

The National Quilt Museum

If you’re interested in textiles and crafts, you won’t want to miss the National Quilt Museum in Paducah. The museum features unique and inspiring exhibits that showcase quilt-making through the years.

Natural Attractions of Western Coal Fields: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mammoth Cave National Park

Mammoth Cave National Park is perhaps the most well-known attraction in Western Coal Fields, and for good reason. Exploring the underground cave system is an unforgettable adventure. Visitors can also enjoy hiking and horseback riding in the park’s above-ground trails.

Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

This state park offers a range of outdoor activities, such as fishing, hiking, and boating. The stunning scenery of the lake against the rolling hills of the region provides an excellent backdrop for your adventure.

Natural Bridge State Resort Park

Another popular outdoor attraction in Western Coal Fields is Natural Bridge State Resort Park, which features a natural stone arch that visitors can hike to and explore. The park also offers rock climbing, camping, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western Coal Fields

For those who love to explore on foot or by bike, Western Coal Fields offer plenty of options.

Dawkins Line Rail Trail

The Dawkins Line Rail Trail offers a unique opportunity to explore the region’s history on a 18-mile track through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the area.

Pine Mountain State Resort Park

Pine Mountain State Resort Park offers a range of hiking trails from easy to difficult, with each trail offering stunning scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Western Coal Fields: Activities and Tours

Explore the Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area

Located on the border of Tennessee and Kentucky, the Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and even an elk and bison prairie.

Take a Bourbon Tour

Kentucky is known for its bourbon, and Western Coal Fields are no exception. Visitors can take a tour of the many distilleries in the area, including the award-winning Buffalo Trace distillery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Western Coal Fields

Day 1: Visit the Kentucky Coal Museum and learn about the region’s mining history

Day 2: Explore Mammoth Cave National Park and take a guided cave tour

Day 3: Take a bourbon tour of the area’s distilleries

Day 4: Hike the trails at Pine Mountain State Resort Park

Day 5: Visit the National Quilt Museum in Paducah

Day 6: Take a scenic drive through the Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

Food and Drink Scene of Western Coal Fields

Must-Try Dishes

The Hot Brown sandwich

Originally created by Chef Fred Schmidt in the 1920s at the Brown Hotel in Louisville, the Hot Brown sandwich has become a Kentucky staple. The dish consists of turkey and bacon on toast, smothered in a creamy Mornay sauce.

Burgoo

Burgoo is a stew-like dish that typically includes a mix of meats and vegetables. The dish is often served at social events and is a popular choice during the state’s annual Derby festivities.

Local Beverages

Kentucky Bourbon

Bourbon is perhaps the most well-known beverage of Kentucky. The state is home to several major distilleries, including Maker’s Mark and Jim Beam. Visitors can take tours and learn about the history and process of bourbon making.

Ale-8-One

A regional beverage, Ale-8-One, is often described as a cross between ginger ale and lemon-lime soda. The beverage has been produced in Winchester, Kentucky, since the 1920s and is available at many local restaurants and stores.

Where to Stay in Western Coal Fields: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Boone Tavern Hotel

Located in Berea, the Boone Tavern Hotel offers an excellent choice for those who want a luxurious stay with a historical touch. The hotel was originally built in 1909 and combines elegant modern amenities with a rich history.

Rough River Dam State Resort Park

For those who want a more natural setting, the Rough River Dam State Resort Park offers cabins and camping options near the beautiful Rough River Lake.

What to Buy in Western Coal Fields: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handcrafted items

Western Coal Fields are home to many artisans who create handcrafted items such as pottery, quilts, and jewelry.

Bourbon

Whether gifted to friends or kept for yourself, bringing a bottle of Kentucky bourbon home is a must-do.

Local Culture and Customs of Western Coal Fields

The people of Western Coal Fields are proud of their history and culture. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s mining history and take the time to learn about the region’s past.

Best Time to Visit Western Coal Fields

The best time to visit Western Coal Fields is during the early fall, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its peak. The region can be hot and humid during the summer months, and snowfall can be heavy during the winter months.

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the outdoors

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Western Coal Fields

Visitors should be aware of the standard safety guidelines when exploring outdoor areas, such as staying aware of the terrain and checking the weather report before embarking on any hikes or activities.

Health in Western Coal Fields

Visitors should be prepared for outdoor activities and the potential of exposure to ticks and other creatures that can cause harm. Always dress appropriately and be aware of your surroundings.

Getting to Western Coal Fields: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Western Coal Fields

The nearest airports to Western Coal Fields include Nashville International Airport and Louisville International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Western Coal Fields

Greyhound Bus Line serves the region. The nearest station to Western Coal Fields is in Bowling Green.

Other Options

Driving to Western Coal Fields is the preferred method of transportation for many visitors. There are also several rental car options available.

How to Get Around in Western Coal Fields

A car is highly recommended for getting around the region, as public transportation is limited, and some attractions are in rural areas.

Where to Go Next from Western Coal Fields: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Louisville

Louisville, Kentucky’s largest city, is about 2 hours away from Western Coal Fields and is a great destination for food, history, and culture.

Nashville

Only 2 hours away from Western Coal Fields, Nashville offers a unique mix of music, history, and southern charm.

Conclusion

Western Coal Fields are a hidden gem that offers visitors an opportunity to explore the region’s rich history, stunning natural scenery, and unique culture. With so much to see and do, this area of Kentucky should be at the top of any traveler’s list.
