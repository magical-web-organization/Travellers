Introduction to Gilbertsville

Gilbertsville is a small town located in the Western Waterlands region of Kentucky, United States of America. The town is known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich history, and outdoor recreational activities. It is the perfect destination for those looking for a peaceful and relaxing vacation in the outdoors. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Gilbertsville with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Gilbertsville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park

Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park is a beautiful park that sits on the shores of Kentucky Lake. The park is home to a variety of activities, including hiking, fishing, golfing, and boating. Visitors can also enjoy horseback riding, mini-golfing, and swimming at the park’s beach. The park has a variety of accommodations, including cabins and campsites.

Kentucky Opry

The Kentucky Opry is a must-visit destination for country music lovers. The venue hosts a variety of music shows, including country, gospel, and bluegrass. The shows feature talented musicians, singers, and dancers who will entertain you with their impressive performances.

Badgett Playhouse

Badgett Playhouse is another great destination for music lovers. The venue hosts a variety of shows, including Broadway-style musicals, tribute shows, and comedy performances. The shows feature talented musicians, singers, and actors who will keep you entertained throughout the performance.

Natural Attractions of Gilbertsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area

The Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area is a beautiful park that spans over 170,000 acres. The park is home to a variety of activities, including hiking, fishing, hunting, and camping. Visitors can also enjoy boating, swimming, and horseback riding at the park. The park is also home to a variety of wildlife, including bald eagles, deer, and bison.

Kentucky Lake

Kentucky Lake is the perfect destination for water sports enthusiasts. The lake is the largest man-made lake in the eastern United States and offers a variety of activities, including boating, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can also enjoy water skiing, jet skiing, and tubing at the lake.

Pisgah Bay

Pisgah Bay is a beautiful and secluded bay on Kentucky Lake. The bay is the perfect destination for those looking for a peaceful and relaxing getaway. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, and kayaking at the bay. The bay is also home to a variety of wildlife, including bald eagles, ospreys, and otters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gilbertsville

Canal Loop Trail

The Canal Loop Trail is a 3.5-mile trail that loops around the Kentucky Dam. The trail offers stunning views of the lake and the dam and is the perfect destination for hikers and bikers.

Hematite Trail

The Hematite Trail is a 2.5-mile trail that offers beautiful views of Kentucky Lake. The trail is surrounded by beautiful forests and is the perfect destination for hikers and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Gilbertsville: Activities and Tours

Kentucky Lake Jet Ski Tours

Kentucky Lake Jet Ski Tours offer visitors a unique way to explore the lake. Visitors can choose from a variety of tours, including sunset tours, wildlife tours, and sightseeing tours. The tours are led by experienced guides who will show you the best spots on the lake.

Pisgah Point Trail Ride

The Pisgah Point Trail Ride is a horseback riding tour that takes visitors through the beautiful forests of Kentucky Lake. The tour is led by experienced guides who will take you on a scenic tour of the area.

The Kentucky Hot Brown is a traditional southern dish that originated in Louisville. The dish is made with turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce and is served open-faced on toast.
