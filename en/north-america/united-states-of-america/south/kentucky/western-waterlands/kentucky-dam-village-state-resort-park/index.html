Introduction to Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park

Located in the Western Waterlands region of Kentucky, United States of America, Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park is a stunning lakeside retreat that promises an abundance of cultural and natural attractions, delicious food and drink, and excellent accommodation options. The park is situated on the north shore of Kentucky Lake, the largest man-made lake in the eastern United States, and offers a beautiful landscape that every traveler will love to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Nature Station

Set in the woods near Kentucky Lake, The Nature Station is a great place for families to learn about Kentucky’s wildlife. Here, visitors will find live animals, interactive exhibits, and ranger-led programs that are both educational and engaging.

The Kentucky Dam

Built in the 1930s, the Kentucky Dam is one of the largest hydroelectric dams in the world. Visitors can take a tour of the dam, which includes a visit to the observation deck, where they can learn about the history of the dam and how it works.

The Arboretum

Spread across 94 acres, The Arboretum features over 1,500 trees and shrubs. Visitors can take a guided tour or explore the park on their own, immersing themselves in the stunning natural surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Kentucky Lake

The Kentucky Lake is a prime destination for boating, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can rent pontoon boats, jet skis, and other watercraft, or relax on the sandy beaches.

The Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area

Owned by the federal government, The Land Between the Lakes is a sprawling forested area that offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and birding. Visitors can also take a scenic drive through the park, stopping at overlooks to enjoy sweeping views of the surrounding landscape.

The Eggners Ferry Bridge

Spanning the Tennessee River, the Eggners Ferry Bridge is a stunning piece of engineering that offers sweeping views of the river and the surrounding countryside. Visitors can walk or bike across the bridge, taking in the breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park

Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park offers a range of hiking and biking trails to suit all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Cool Hollow Trail

  • The Overlook Trail

Best Things to Do in Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park: Activities and Tours

Boat Tour of the Lake

Visitors can take a boat tour of the Kentucky Lake, enjoying stunning views of the surrounding landscape while learning about the history of the area.

Fishing

The Kentucky Lake is home to a variety of fish species, including bass, crappie, catfish, and bluegill. Visitors can fish from the banks or take a guided fishing trip.

Golfing

Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park offers an 18-hole golf course that promises a challenging and enjoyable game.

Food and Drink Scene of Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park

Must-Try Dishes

Kentucky Hot Brown

A Kentucky staple, Hot Brown is an open-faced sandwich that consists of roast turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce served over bread.

Burgoo

A hearty stew, burgoo contains meat (usually beef or chicken), potatoes, and vegetables. Visitors can find it at many local restaurants, especially during the Kentucky State Fair.

Local Beverages

Kentucky Bourbon

Kentucky is famous for its bourbon. Visitors can take a tour of the Kentucky Bourbon Trail, which includes several of the state’s leading distilleries, including Maker’s Mark, Jim Beam, and Wild Turkey.
