Introduction to Newport

Nestled in the Northern Ohio River Region of Kentucky, Newport is a charming city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Here, you’ll find a range of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and exciting things to do and see. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Newport.

List of Places to Visit in Newport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Newport offers visitors a glimpse into its rich cultural and historical past with its many museums and historical places. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Newport Aquarium: This world-renowned aquarium features a diverse range of marine life, including sharks, rays, and penguins.
  • The Thompson House: A beautifully-preserved Victorian mansion that offers tours and events throughout the year.
  • Southgate House Revival: A historic music venue that has hosted legendary acts like Johnny Cash, Jerry Lee Lewis, and Elvis Costello.
  • Tower Park: A scenic park that’s home to the World Peace Bell and the Newport Memorial Hall, honoring veterans of all wars.

Natural Attractions of Newport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will appreciate Newport’s stunning natural attractions, from tranquil parks to scenic trails. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore in Newport:

  • The Purple People Bridge: A pedestrian-only bridge spanning the Ohio River, offering stunning views of the city and its surroundings.
  • General James Taylor Park: A serene waterfront park that’s perfect for picnics, hiking, and fishing.
  • East Row Historic District: A beautiful, tree-lined residential area that’s perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • Newport Municipal Beach: A picturesque beach on the banks of the Ohio River, offering swimming, fishing, and picnicking opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newport

Newport boasts a variety of scenic trails and routes that are perfect for hiking or biking. Here are some of the top trails to explore:

  • Riverfront Trail: A scenic trail that follows the banks of the Ohio River, offering stunning views of the water and the city.
  • Love Run Trail: A peaceful trail that winds through the woods, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding nature.
  • Licking River Greenway and Trail: A scenic trail that follows the Licking River, offering wildlife viewing opportunities and a glimpse into Newport’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Newport: Activities and Tours

From exciting tours to fun-filled activities, Newport has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Kentucky Bourbon Trail: Take a tour of the famous Kentucky Bourbon Trail and sample some of the best bourbons in the world.
  • Newport Gangster Tour: Learn about Newport’s seedy past on this fascinating tour of the city’s gangster history.
  • World Peace Bell: Visit the World Peace Bell, the largest free-swinging bell in the world, and learn about peace and justice.
  • Newport on the Levee: A popular entertainment complex that’s home to a wide range of shops, restaurants, and attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newport

Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to the Newport Aquarium, followed by a stroll along the Purple People Bridge.

Day 2: Take a relaxing hike or bike ride on the Riverfront Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the Ohio River.

Day 3: Explore Newport’s rich history at The Thompson House and Tower Park, stopping for a picnic lunch in between.

Day 4: Get your adrenaline pumping with a visit to the Newport Gangster Tour, followed by an evening of live music at the Southgate House Revival.

Day 5: Head out for a day trip to the Kentucky Bourbon Trail and enjoy a tasting of some of the best bourbons in the world.

Day 6: Take a leisurely stroll through the East Row Historic District, stopping for coffee or lunch at one of the charming cafes or bistros in the area.

Day 7: End your trip with a visit to the World Peace Bell, followed by a relaxing afternoon at Newport Municipal Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Newport

Must-Try Dishes

Newport is home to a vibrant food scene, offering a range of delicious dishes that are sure to delight your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Goetta: A popular regional dish made from pork and steel-cut oats, typically served for breakfast.
  • Hot Brown: A Kentucky classic, this open-faced sandwich features turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce, baked until golden brown.
  • Fried Chicken: Kentucky-style fried chicken is a must-try, with its crispy exterior and juicy, flavorful meat.

Local Beverages

Kentucky is also known for its bourbon, and Newport has no shortage of places to enjoy a glass of this delicious spirit. Here are some must-visit spots:

  • The Party Source: A massive liquor store that offers an incredible selection of bourbons and other spirits.
  • New Riff Distilling: A distillery that offers tours and tastings, where you can sample their award-winning bourbons.
  • Bourbon Haus 1841: A bourbon bar with over 400 varieties to choose from, as well as an impressive menu of cocktails and small plates.

Where to Stay in Newport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Newport offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and lifestyle. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Hotel Covington: A luxurious boutique hotel housed in a historic building, featuring stylish rooms and amenities including a rooftop bar and restaurant.
  • Aloft Newport on the Levee: Located in the heart of Newport, this trendy hotel offers modern rooms and easy access to a range of attractions.
  • Shively Court Bed & Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast housed in a beautifully-restored 19th-century home, offering comfortable and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Newport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home from Newport? Here are some must-visit shops:

  • Newport on the Levee: This shopping complex is home to a variety of shops offering everything from clothing and accessories to souvenirs and gifts.
  • Rookwood Pottery Company: A pottery company that produces handcrafted pottery and other ceramics, featuring designs inspired by the local area.
  • Art on the Levee Gallery: A gallery that showcases the work of local artists and artisans, including paintings, sculptures, and other works of art.

Local Culture and Customs of Newport

Newport is a friendly and welcoming city, with a rich and diverse cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local culture and customs, including the vibrant music scene, historic architecture, and delicious cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Newport

The best time to visit Newport is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. Summer is also a popular time to visit, but be prepared for hot and humid weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newport

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including Kentucky-style fried chicken, hot browns, and goetta.
  • Do take a stroll along the Purple People Bridge for stunning views of the Ohio River.
  • Do explore Newport’s rich cultural and historical heritage, including the many museums and historic sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent, especially if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of Newport’s outdoor attractions.
  • Don’t drink and drive. Always have a designated driver or use a ride-sharing service.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newport

Newport is a safe city, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors are advised to practice common sense safety precautions, such as staying aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Newport

Newport has several hospitals and medical centers, including St. Elizabeth Healthcare and The Christ Hospital Outpatient Center. Visitors are advised to have adequate medical insurance and take precautions to stay healthy, such as staying hydrated and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Newport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Newport

The closest airport to Newport is the Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport, located about 15 minutes away by car. The Dayton International Airport is also within driving distance, located about 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Newport

The Greyhound Bus Terminal in Cincinnati is located about 15 minutes away from Newport by car. The Megabus also offers service to Cincinnati, with a stop at the University of Cincinnati.

Other Options

Newport is easily accessible by car, with major highways such as I-71, I-75, and I-275 passing through the area. Alternatively, visitors can take the Southbank Shuttle, a trolley-style bus that connects Newport and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Newport

Newport is a walkable city, with many attractions within easy walking distance of each other. Visitors can also take advantage of the Southbank Shuttle, which connects Newport with other nearby cities.

Where to Go Next from Newport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newport is surrounded by other exciting destinations, including the cities of Cincinnati and Covington. Visitors can also explore the stunning natural beauty of the Kentucky countryside, including the Red River Gorge and Mammoth Cave National Park.

Conclusion

Newport is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in this charming Kentucky city. Plan your trip today and discover all that Newport has to offer!
