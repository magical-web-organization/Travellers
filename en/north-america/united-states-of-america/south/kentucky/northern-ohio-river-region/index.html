Introduction to Northern Ohio River Region

Located in the southern part of Kentucky State, the Northern Ohio River Region is a scenic and historically rich destination. The region is situated along the Ohio River, known for its beautiful waterfront views and outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, and amazing food and drink of the region. We will also provide tips for transportation, safety, and health, making your visit to Northern Ohio River Region a trip to remember.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Ohio River Region

Here is a list of some of the best places to visit in Northern Ohio River Region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or simply love to learn about the culture of a place, the Northern Ohio River Region doesn’t disappoint. Here are some culturally significant attractions worth visiting:

  • National Underground Railroad Museum: Located in Maysville, the National Underground Railroad Museum is dedicated to telling the story of the slave trade and the peaceful resistance movements that led to the abolitionist cause. The museum features interactive exhibits and showcases more than 3,000 artifacts and works of art.
  • Old Fort Harrod State Park: Located in Harrodsburg, Old Fort Harrod is a replica of the original fort built in 1774. Visitors can take guided tours and participate in historical reenactments.
  • Shaker Village of Pleasant Hill: Explore the Shaker way of life at the Shaker Village of Pleasant Hill. This living history museum includes 34 restored buildings and features daily tours and demonstrations.

Natural Attractions of Northern Ohio River Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Northern Ohio River Region is home to the beautiful Ohio River and lush greenery, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Check out these natural wonders:

  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Located in Cave City, Mammoth Cave National Park features over 400-miles of cave systems that you can explore. Visitors can also go hiking, camping, and canoeing.
  • Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area: This recreation area is situated between Kentucky Lake and Lake Barkley and offers endless opportunities for outdoor activities such as fishing, camping, hiking, and more.
  • Natural Bridge State Resort Park: This park features a natural bridge that is a great spot for hiking, scenic overlooks, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Ohio River Region

Northern Ohio River Region is a haven for hikers and cyclists because of its beautiful landscapes. Here are some of its best hiking and biking routes:

  • Buckhorn Trail: The Buckhorn Trail is a moderate-to-difficult hiking trail located in Buckhorn Lake State Resort Park. It is about 6 miles long and takes you to beautiful mountainous views and waterfalls.
  • Great Escapes Trail: This is a family-friendly hike located in Mammoth Cave National Park that takes you on a 3-mile trail through some of the park’s unique attractions.
  • Kentucky Bourbon Trail Cycling Tour: This tour takes you through beautiful scenery, historic distilleries, and bourbon-producing areas of Kentucky.

Best Things to Do in Northern Ohio River Region: Activities and Tours

No trip to Northern Ohio River Region is complete without experiencing some of its amazing activities. Here are some of the best things that you should do when you are there:

  • Take a distillery tour: Visit some of the world-renowned distilleries in the region, like Jim Beam Bourbon, Wild Turkey Distillery, and Four Roses Distillery. Take a tour and get to taste some of the world’s finest bourbons.
  • Visit Newport Aquarium: Located in Newport, Kentucky, Newport Aquarium is a must-visit attraction for animal lovers. With thousands of aquatic animals, including penguins, sharks, and alligators, make sure to include this in your itinerary.
  • Take a riverboat tour: Immerse yourself in the picturesque views of the Ohio River by taking a riverboat tour. Enjoy dinner while taking in the views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Ohio River Region

Here’s an example week-long itinerary for Northern Ohio River Region:

Day 1: Visit the National Underground Railroad Museum, then head to Mammoth Cave National Park for hiking and exploring. Day 2: Take a distillery tour and enjoy a bourbon tasting. Day 3: Visit the Newport Aquarium and take a stroll along the riverfront. Day 4: Go to Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area for fishing, camping, and hiking. Day 5: Head to Shaker Village of Pleasant Hill for a living history experience. Day 6: Visit Old Fort Harrod State Park for a historical reenactment tour. Day 7: Take a riverboat tour and indulge in some spa treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Ohio River Region

Northern Ohio River Region offers a plethora of delicious cuisine and local beverages. Here’s what you need to know about its food and drink scene:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Hot Brown: The Hot Brown is an open-faced sandwich topped with sliced turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce.
  • Burgoo: Burgoo is a stew commonly made with beef, pork, chicken, and vegetables.
  • Derby pie: Derby pie is a chocolate and nut-filled pie that is a staple dessert in Kentucky.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: Kentucky bourbon is world-renowned. Some of the popular bourbon distilleries in the region include Jim Beam, Wild Turkey, and Maker’s Mark.
  • Ale-8-One: Ale-8-One is a regional ginger ale that was invented in Winchester, Kentucky.
  • Wine: The Northern Ohio River Region has a budding wine industry with various wineries, like the Lovers Leap Vineyards and Winery.

Where to Stay in Northern Ohio River Region: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Ohio River Region has a range of options for accommodation. Here are some of the best options:

  • 21c Museum Hotel Lexington: This hotel is in Lexington and features contemporary art and luxurious amenities.
  • The Brown Hotel: Located in Louisville, this hotel is a historic landmark and is famous for its Hot Brown sandwiches.
  • The Corner House Bed and Breakfast: Located in Harrodsburg, The Corner House is a charming bed and breakfast with beautiful views and unique furnishings.

What to Buy in Northern Ohio River Region: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Northern Ohio River Region is filled with amazing options. Here are some of the things you should consider buying:

  • Bourbon: As previously mentioned, Kentucky bourbon is world-renowned. Don’t forget to grab a bottle for your home bar or as a gift for someone.
  • Kentucky horse race memorabilia: Horse racing is a big part of the culture in Northern Ohio River Region. Look for souvenirs such as Derby hats and jockey figurines.
  • Handcrafted souvenirs: Northern Ohio River Region is known for its arts and crafts, such as handmade pottery, glass, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Ohio River Region

Northern Ohio River Region has a rich culture that is deeply rooted in traditions. Here are some customs you should know:

  • Horse racing: Kentucky is known as the horse capital of the world and is home to the famous Kentucky Derby.
  • Bourbon: The Northern Ohio River Region is known for its world-renowned bourbon.
  • Bluegrass Music: The region is known for its traditional bluegrass music, with the annual International Bluegrass Music Association held in Raleigh, NC.

Best Time to Visit Northern Ohio River Region

The best time to visit Northern Ohio River Region is during the summer months, from June to August. During this season, the outdoor activities are in full swing, and the weather is pleasant for exploring the natural attractions. Fall months from September to November offer cooler temperatures and beautiful autumn foliage.
