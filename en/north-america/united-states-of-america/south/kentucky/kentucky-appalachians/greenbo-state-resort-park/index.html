Introduction to Greenbo State Resort Park

Located in the heart of the Kentucky Appalachians region, Greenbo State Resort Park is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This 3,300-acre park offers stunning views of Kentucky’s rolling hills, lush forests, and peaceful lake. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking for a quiet getaway, Greenbo State Resort Park has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Greenbo State Resort Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Greenbo Lake State Park Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of the area with exhibits on local wildlife, Native American tribes, and the pioneer era.
  • Greenup County War Memorial: A beautiful monument commemorating the lives of Greenup County residents who have served in the armed forces.
  • Carter Caves State Resort Park: Just a short drive away from Greenbo, visit the caves and explore the underground beauty.

Natural Attractions of Greenbo State Resort Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Greenbo Lake: The star attraction of the park, this serene lake is perfect for fishing, boating, swimming, and camping. Rent a paddle boat, kayak or canoe at the marina to explore the crystal-clear waters.
  • Licking River: This is a peaceful river that runs through the park. You can kayak or canoe to the river.
  • Hiking Trails: Greenbo State Resort Park has over 25 miles of hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenbo State Resort Park

  • The Overlook Trail: This 1.5-mile trail leads to an overlook that offers stunning views of Greenbo Lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • The Pioneer Trail: This 2-mile trail takes visitors past historic landmarks such as the old pioneer cemetery and the remains of an old grist mill.

Best Things to Do in Greenbo State Resort Park: Activities and Tours

  • Camping: Greenbo State Resort Park offers over 200 campsites for tents and RVs.
  • Fishing: Greenbo Lake is stocked with a variety of fish species including largemouth bass, catfish, and bluegill.
  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can explore the park’s scenic trails on horseback.
  • Sightseeing Cruises: Take a scenic tour of the lake on the SeaArk with interpretive nature narration.
  • Golfing: Greenbo State Resort Park has a challenging 18-hole golf course that spans over 6,000 yards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greenbo State Resort Park

Day 1: Arrive at Greenbo State Resort Park and set up camp. Take a dip in the lake and prepare for an adventurous week.

Day 2: Explore the park’s hiking trails and take in the stunning views. Stop at the park museum and learn about the history of Greenbo and Kentucky Appalachians.

Day 3: Spend the day fishing or boating on Greenbo Lake. Have a picnic and relax by the lake shore.

Day 4: Take a sightseeing cruise of the lake and enjoy the scenic views. Afterward, enjoy a round of golf at the park’s golf course.

Day 5: Discover Licking River and its natural beauty by kayaking or canoeing. Go on a horseback ride to experience the park’s trails in a unique way.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Carter Caves State Resort Park and explore the area’s underground caves.

Day 7: Relax at the campsite, soak in the sun and enjoy the serene atmosphere of Greenbo State Park before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenbo State Resort Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kentucky Hot Brown: This open-faced sandwich is made with turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce.
  • Fried Chicken: Crispy and juicy, fried chicken is a must-try in Kentucky and Greenbo.
  • Kentucky Bourbon: Sip on the most famous drink of Kentucky while enjoying the view of the lake.

Local Beverages

  • Ale-8-One: A refreshing soda pop that is a Kentucky classic.
  • Kentucky Bourbon: Bourbon expertly distilled at local Kentucky distilleries.

Where to Stay in Greenbo State Resort Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Camping: Greenbo State Resort Park offers various camping options such as tent sites and RV hookups.
  • Lodge: The lodge offers cozy accommodations with beautiful views of the lake.

What to Buy in Greenbo State Resort Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Greenbo State Resort Park

Kentucky is known for its Southern hospitality and friendly people. Locals take pride in their state’s history, traditions, and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Greenbo State Resort Park

The best time to visit Greenbo State Resort Park is during the spring and summer months when outdoor activities are in full swing, and the weather is warm and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenbo State Resort Park

Do:

  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellant.
  • Respect the wildlife and environment.
  • Follow park rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitat.
  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Ignore park warnings or signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenbo State Resort Park

  • Always carry a map and know your location.
  • Take sufficient water and food with you during hiking, kayaking or any other outdoor activity.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and shoes.

Health in Greenbo State Resort Park

  • Check for ticks after any outdoor activity.
  • Bring any medical supplies you might need.
  • Apply sunblock and take shade breaks to prevent heat exhaustion or sunstroke.

Getting to Greenbo State Resort Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greenbo State Resort Park

  • Tri-State/Milton J. Ferguson Field: The closest airport is about 35 miles away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Greenbo State Resort Park

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greenup KY, about 15 miles from the park.

Other Options

  • Car Rental Services: Renting a car is the easiest way to access the park and explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Greenbo State Resort Park

The best way to get around Greenbo State Resort Park is by walking or biking. Hiking trails and cycling routes offer visitors a chance to explore the park’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Greenbo State Resort Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ashland, KY: about 20 miles away, this city on the Ohio River offers great restaurants, shopping, beautiful parks, and Ashland Amusement Park.
  • Huntington, WV: Visit the local museums, parks, and botanical gardens. Huntington is about 30 miles from Greenbo.
