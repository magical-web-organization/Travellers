Introduction to Catlettsburg

Located in the heart of the Kentucky Appalachians, Catlettsburg is a charming city that boasts a rich history and a vibrant culture. With a population of just over 1,800 people, this small city offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures, scenic views, cultural attractions, and culinary delights. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Catlettsburg has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Catlettsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to history and culture, Catlettsburg has no shortage of attractions to offer. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places in the city:

  • The Catlettsburg Heritage Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the city’s history and showcasing its cultural heritage. Visitors can learn about the city’s early settlers, its development over the years, and its unique customs and traditions.

  • The Kentucky Highlands Museum & Discovery Center: This museum houses over 10,000 artifacts that tell the story of the Appalachian region. Visitors can explore exhibits on early industry, transportation, coal mining, and more.

  • The Boyd County Public Library: This beautiful library is located in a historic building that dates back to the early 1900s. It offers a vast collection of books and resources that cater to readers of all ages and interests.

Natural Attractions of Catlettsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Catlettsburg has plenty of natural attractions that are worth exploring. Here are some of the top outdoor destinations in the city:

  • Greenbo Lake State Resort Park: This picturesque park offers over 3,000 acres of natural beauty, including a 300-acre lake, hiking trails, camping sites, and more.

  • Grayson Lake State Park: This park is located just a short drive from Catlettsburg and is a popular destination for boating, fishing, swimming, and camping.

  • The Russell Fork River: This scenic river is perfect for kayaking, rafting, and other water sports. It’s surrounded by beautiful forests and rock formations that make for stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Catlettsburg

Catlettsburg offers some excellent hiking and biking trails that offer spectacular views of the city’s natural beauty. Here are some of the top routes to consider:

  • The Greenbo Lake Trail: This 23-mile trail offers breathtaking views of Greenbo Lake and takes you through some of the most scenic parts of the park.

  • The Dawkins Line Rail Trail: This 18-mile trail was once a railroad line and offers some unique views of the surrounding countryside. It’s perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  • The Pine Mountain Trail: This trail is located just a short drive from Catlettsburg and offers some of the best hiking in the state. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Catlettsburg: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Catlettsburg, here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • Horseback Riding at Greenbo Lake: This is a great way to explore the park’s natural beauty and get up close with some of Kentucky’s most magnificent horses.

  • Kentucky Bourbon Trail Tour: This tour takes you through some of the state’s best distilleries, giving you a taste of Kentucky’s famous bourbon.

  • Catlettsburg Haunted House Tour: This tour takes you through some of the city’s most haunted places and offers a spooky and thrilling experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Catlettsburg

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Catlettsburg, here’s a sample itinerary that you might find useful:

Day 1: Visit the Kentucky Highlands Museum & Discovery Center and the Catlettsburg Heritage Museum.

Day 2: Explore the Greenbo Lake State Resort Park, go horseback riding, and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 3: Take a tour of the Russell Fork River and go kayaking or rafting.

Day 4: Visit the Boyd County Public Library and attend a reading or workshop.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the Pine Mountain Trail and go hiking.

Day 6: Take a Kentucky Bourbon Trail Tour and enjoy some tastings.

Day 7: Relax and unwind in one of Catlettsburg’s many parks or outdoor spaces.

Food and Drink Scene of Catlettsburg

When it comes to food and drink, Catlettsburg has plenty to offer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kentucky Fried Chicken: This iconic dish originated in Kentucky and remains a must-try while you’re in the state.

  • Hot Brown: This sandwich is a Louisville original and consists of turkey, bacon, tomatoes, and Mornay sauce, all broiled to perfection.

  • Burgoo: This is a traditional Kentucky stew that’s made with a variety of meats and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: Kentucky is famous for its bourbon, and Catlettsburg is no exception. You can taste some of the state’s best whiskey on the Kentucky Bourbon Trail Tour.

  • Ale-8-One: This ginger-flavored soft drink is a favorite among locals and is a great way to cool off on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Catlettsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Catlettsburg, whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse or a luxury hotel. Here are some of the top options to consider:

  • The Ashland Inn & Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and a central location, making it the perfect choice for travelers on a budget.

  • The Ramada Limited: This hotel offers spacious rooms and plenty of amenities, including an indoor pool and a fitness center.

  • The Cottages at Greenbo Lake: These cozy cottages are located inside the Greenbo Lake State Resort Park and offer stunning views of the surrounding nature.

What to Buy in Catlettsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Catlettsburg, here are some of the top options:

Local Culture and Customs of Catlettsburg

Catlettsburg has a unique culture and heritage that’s worth exploring. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Friendliness: The people of Catlettsburg are known for their friendliness and hospitality, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a local.

  • Horse racing: Horse racing is a popular pastime in Kentucky, and Catlettsburg is no exception. You might want to catch a race while you’re in town.

  • Fall Festivals: Fall is a great time to visit Catlettsburg and attend one of its many fall festivals, which celebrate local traditions, culture, and food.

Best Time to Visit Catlettsburg

The best time to visit Catlettsburg depends on your preferences. If you’re looking for mild weather and plenty of outdoor activities, then the summer months are a great choice. If you want to catch a horse race or attend a fall festival, then the fall months are ideal. In general, spring and autumn are great for mild temperatures and fewer crowds.
