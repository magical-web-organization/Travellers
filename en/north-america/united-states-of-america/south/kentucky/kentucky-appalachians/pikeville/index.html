Welcome to Pikeville, a hidden gem of the Kentucky Appalachians, situated in the south of the United States of America and located in the heart of the Bluegrass State. This serene town, surrounded by scenic mountains and rich cultural heritage, is a delight to visit. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure enthusiast, Pikeville has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve covered everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Pikeville, Kentucky.

Introduction to Pikeville

Pikeville is a small town nestled in the Appalachian Mountains, home to breathtaking natural beauty, cultural history, and friendly locals. Known as the “City that Moves Mountains,” Pikeville is a peaceful town that’s perfect for experiencing southern hospitality and the warmth of community. Pikeville offers stunning views of the Appalachian hills, endless outdoor activities, local cuisine, and southern charm that make it a hidden gem in Kentucky.

List of Places to Visit in Pikeville

There are many things to see and do in Pikeville. Here are the top tourist attractions that you don’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Pike County Courthouse Built in 1962, The Pike County Courthouse is a famous landmark of the town. It’s an iconic building is known for its unique design and architecture, including a clock tower that is visible from most parts of Pikeville.

  2. Hatfield-McCoy Feud Sites Take a fascinating tour of the Hatfield-McCoy Heritage Trail, which showcases the history and sites of the infamous family feud. The tour includes many historic landmarks, including the home of Devil Anse Hatfield, the McCoy cabins, and the 1888 courthouse.

  3. Eastern Kentucky Exposition Center The Eastern Kentucky Exposition Center is a sports and concert venue in Pikeville. It hosts various events throughout the year, including concerts, trade shows, and community events.

Natural Attractions of Pikeville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Breaks Interstate Park Breaks Interstate Park is a beautiful natural park in Pikeville spanning over 4,600 acres. Here, visitors can admire stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains from the Pinnacle Overlook, hike on the trails, zip line, fish, kayak or canoe on the Russell Fork River.

  2. Fishtrap Lake Fishtrap Lake is a serene lake offering breathtaking scenery and various water activities like boating, swimming, and fishing.

  3. Bob Amos Park Bob Amos Park is Pikeville’s largest park and offers hiking trails, a playground, soccer fields, baseball diamonds, and a pond. It’s an excellent spot for picnics, enjoying nature, and outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pikeville

Pikeville offers various hiking and biking trails that are perfect for adventure seekers. Some of the most popular trails are:

  1. Dawkins Line Rail Trail The Dawkins Line Rail Trail is a premier rail-trail that spans over 18 miles. It features magnificent views of the Appalachian Mountains and breathtaking Kentucky countryside.

  2. Pine Mountain State Resort Park Pine Mountain State Resort Park is a beautiful state park that features over 20 miles of hiking trails. Visitors can view the spectacular sights from the Raven Rock Trail, which offers a panoramic view of the surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Pikeville: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Pikeville:

  1. Elkhorn City Heritage Trail The Elkhorn City Heritage Trail is a must-do experience for history buffs, nature lovers, and outdoor enthusiasts alike. Explore the rich heritage and breathtaking beauty of this picturesque trail by hiking or biking.

  2. Tour of the Trails Take a guided tour of the trails with local guides, who will show you the best routes, wildlife, and vegetation in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Pikeville

Southern cuisine is often described as comfort food, and Pikeville’s culinary scene is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried Chicken It’s the quintessential southern dish and a must-try at Pikeville’s various restaurants and eateries.

  2. Hot Brown A Kentucky classic dish made with turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce.

  3. Bourbon Balls These delicious treats are made with bourbon whiskey, pecans, and chocolate and are the perfect souvenir to take back home.

Local Beverages

  1. Bourbon Bourbon is the most popular drink in Kentucky, and Pikeville has many distilleries that produce the finest quality bourbon.

  2. Ale-8-One Ale-8 -One is a beloved soft drink that originated in Kentucky and is now famous nationwide.

Local Culture and Customs of Pikeville

Pikeville has a rich cultural heritage, identified by its warm-hearted folks, delicious cuisine, and panoramic Appalachian views. Residents here take pride in their heritage, and tourists can expect warm hospitality and a pleasant smile wherever they go.

Best Time to Visit Pikeville

The best time to visit Pikeville is during the summer, from June to August, when the weather is sunny, warm, and ideal for outdoor activities. Fall is another fantastic season to visit, from September to November, as visitors can admire the foliage of the Appalachian Mountains.
