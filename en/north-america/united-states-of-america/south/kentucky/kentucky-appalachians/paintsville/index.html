Introduction to Paintsville

Paintsville is a beautiful small city located in the heart of Kentucky’s Appalachian Mountains. It is surrounded by lush green forests, rolling hills, and picturesque landscapes. This city boasts of friendly locals, rich history, and vibrant culture offering a mix of outdoor adventures, historic sites, and delicious local cuisine. Visitors to Paintsville can enjoy scenic drives through the mountains, hiking and biking on the Paintsville Lake State Park trail, or simply exploring the quaint downtown area with its charming boutiques and restaurants.

List of Places to Visit in Paintsville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Paintsville has a rich cultural history that can be uncovered by visiting some of its museums and historic sites. The Mayo Mansion is a beautiful mansion-turned-museum that is dedicated to the life and work of the Mayo family, who were influential in the growth of the region in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The Mountain HomePlace is a living history farm that provides a glimpse into the life of Appalachian families in the 1800s. Additionally, the Paintsville Historical Museum showcases the rich cultural history of Johnson County.

Natural Attractions of Paintsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Paintsville is surrounded by some of the most beautiful landscapes in the state. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and fishing at the Paintsville Lake State Park. Paintsville Lake, located on the outskirts of the city, is perfect for boating and kayaking with its calm waters and scenic views. Another stunning natural attraction in the area is the Jenny Wiley State Resort Park, which features scenic views and plenty of outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paintsville

The Paintsville Lake State Park Trail offers 12 miles of picturesque hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the mountains. The trail is suitable for all skill levels and offers a great way to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in Paintsville: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Paintsville can choose from a wide range of activities and tours. Take a tour of the Mayo Mansion or the Paintsville Historical Museum to learn about the city’s rich history. Visitors can also go fishing in the lake or take a scenic drive through the Appalachian Mountains. A visit to the Mountain HomePlace offers the opportunity to experience daily life in the Appalachian region.

Food and Drink Scene of Paintsville

Must-Try Dishes

Paintsville is known for its delicious Southern cuisine. Stop by Billy Ray’s Restaurant for their famous Kentucky Hot Brown and other Southern specialties. Linda’s Restaurant is a local favorite for homestyle cooking and down-home charm. Be sure to try the hot biscuits and gravy at Classic Diner.

Local Beverages

Paintsville has a thriving craft brewery scene. Visit Akasha Brewing Company for a taste of their locally brewed beer. The Wine Cellar at Stone Crest Vineyard is a great place to try local wines while enjoying the beautiful scenery.

Where to Stay in Paintsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Paintsville offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. For a luxurious stay, book a room at the Ramada Hotel and Conference Center or the Highlands Plaza Hotel. For a more budget-friendly option, opt for the Days Inn or Comfort Suites. Additionally, there are several charming bed and breakfasts in the area that offer a more personalized experience.

What to Buy in Paintsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Paintsville can take home a taste of the local region by purchasing some of the area’s local produce and crafts. Stop by Northeast Kentucky Beekeepers Association to buy honey or pick up some handmade soaps and candles from Paintsville Herb Cottage. Additionally, the Adams Old House and Country Store offers a range of antiques and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Paintsville

Paintsville is a welcoming and friendly city that values its cultural heritage. Visitors can experience the traditions and customs of the Appalachian region by attending local music festivals or taking part in traditional crafts workshops. It is important to note that the locals value their privacy and it is best to ask for permission before taking photographs or videos.

Best Time to Visit Paintsville

The best time to visit Paintsville is from March to May when the weather is pleasant and the flowers are in bloom. The fall months of September to November also offer beautiful foliage and cooler temperatures making it an ideal time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paintsville

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Take plenty of photographs of the beautiful scenery.

Don’t:

  • Intrude on the locals’ privacy or take pictures without permission.
  • Visit off-limits areas on private property.
  • Litter in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paintsville

Paintsville is a safe city but it is advisable to take normal precautions such as locking your vehicle and not leaving valuables in plain sight. Additionally, visitors should be cautious when hiking or exploring the mountains and should adhere to park rules and regulations.

Health in Paintsville

Visitors to Paintsville should drink bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach upsets. It is also advisable to bring sunscreen and bug spray when exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Paintsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paintsville

The nearest airport to Paintsville is Tri-State Airport in Huntington, West Virginia, which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Paintsville

Greyhound Bus Lines and Trailways have bus stations in nearby cities such as Ashland, KY and Prestonsburg, KY, which are about a half-hour’s drive away.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car to explore the region at their leisure. Ride-hail companies such as Lyft or Uber are not available in Paintsville.

How to Get Around in Paintsville

The best way to get around Paintsville is by car. Most of the attractions and accommodation options are located outside of the city and a car will provide the flexibility to explore the surrounding area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Paintsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Paintsville can explore several nearby destinations. Pikeville is a nearby city known for its rich heritage and unique culture. The Breaks Interstate Park offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is just a short drive away. Additionally, nearby Ashland offers a range of shops and restaurants for visitors to explore.

Conclusion

Paintsville, Kentucky, is a perfect destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and anyone looking for a glimpse into Southern culture. This comprehensive guide will help plan the perfect trip to Paintsville, providing information on where to stay, what to do, and how to get there. Don’t forget to stop by one of the local restaurants for a taste of the delicious Southern cuisine!
