Introduction to Ashland

Nestled in the heart of the Kentucky Appalachians, Ashland is a charming city with a rich history and plenty of natural beauty to explore. It is located in the south of the United States and is an excellent destination for those looking for a relaxing getaway.

Ashland is known for its stunning green spaces, natural beauty, and historical attractions. Visitors can explore museums, hike through nature reserves, and take in the breathtaking views of the Ohio River. There’s something for everyone in Ashland, whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply enjoying the local food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Ashland

Here are some of the top places to visit in Ashland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ashland, The Henry Clay Estate: This National Historic Landmark was the former home of statesman Henry Clay. Visitors can take guided tours of the house and gardens to learn about the legacy of one of America’s most influential politicians.

  • Paramount Arts Center: This beautiful theater hosts a variety of performances, including concerts, plays, and comedy shows. The interior is stunning, and it’s a must-visit spot for arts and culture enthusiasts.

  • Highlands Museum and Discovery Center: This museum features exhibits that highlight the history of Ashland and the surrounding region. Here, visitors can learn about the area’s natural history, Appalachian culture, and more.

Natural Attractions of Ashland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Central Park: This beautiful green space is the perfect spot for a picnic or a game of frisbee. There are walking trails, a playground, and plenty of space to relax and enjoy nature.

  • Carter Caves State Resort Park: This park is located just outside of Ashland and offers hiking, cave tours, and other outdoor activities. The park’s natural beauty is stunning, and it’s a great destination for families and outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Ohio River: One of the most scenic spots in Ashland is the Ohio River. Visitors can take a riverboat tour or simply sit back and enjoy the views of the water and surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashland

For those who love the outdoors, Ashland has several excellent hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the best include:

  • Greenbo Lake State Resort Park Trails: This park offers over 25 miles of hiking and biking trails through beautiful forests and along the lakefront. There are trails for all skill levels, from beginner to advanced.

  • Poage Landing Trail: This 3-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride. It goes through green spaces and offers stunning views of the Ohio River.

  • Jesse Stuart Nature Preserve Trail: This trail is a bit more challenging, with steep inclines and rocky terrain, but it’s worth it for the views. The trail goes through the forest and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Ashland: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Ashland that cater to various interests. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Kentucky Bourbon Trail Tour: Learn about the history, production, and culture of bourbon on this guided tour. You’ll visit several distilleries and taste some of the world’s best bourbons.

  • Ashland Ghost Walk: Take a spooky guided tour through Ashland’s haunted spots, complete with chilling tales of local lore and legends.

  • Beech Bend Amusement Park & Splash Lagoon: This amusement park is perfect for families with thrill rides, water slides, and a lazy river. There’s something for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashland

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring Ashland:

Day 1: Visit the Ashland, The Henry Clay Estate, and Paramount Arts Center

Day 2: Explore Central Park and take a riverboat tour of the Ohio River

Day 3: Hike the Jesse Stuart Nature Preserve Trail

Day 4: Visit the Highlands Museum and Discovery Center

Day 5: Tour a bourbon distillery on the Kentucky Bourbon Trail

Day 6: Spend the day at Beech Bend Amusement Park & Splash Lagoon

Day 7: Relax at Carter Caves State Resort Park

Food and Drink Scene of Ashland

Ashland has a thriving food and drink scene, and there are many must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • ‘Deviled’ Eggs: This local spin on deviled eggs is made with mustard and relish, giving them a unique flavor.

  • Bourbon Burgoo: This hearty stew is made with chunks of meat, vegetables, and Bourbon.

  • Hot Brown: A classic Kentucky dish, the Hot Brown is an open-faced sandwich that includes turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Ale-8-One: This ginger and citrus-flavored soda is a Kentucky classic.

  • Bourbon: Kentucky is famous for its Bourbon, and there are many excellent distilleries to visit in Ashland.

  • Kentucky Common Beer: This beer is a local favorite, and it’s known for its refreshing taste and light color.

Where to Stay in Ashland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several great options for accommodations in Ashland. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Ashland Plaza Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Ashland and offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites Ashland: This hotel is perfect for business travelers or families, with modern rooms and suites.

  • The Ashland Inn: This charming guest house offers a cozy and affordable option for travelers who want to experience the local culture.

What to Buy in Ashland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring back from Ashland include:

  • Bourbon: Bring back a bottle of Kentucky Bourbon to share with friends and family.

  • Handcrafted items: From pottery to quilts to jewelry, there are many local artisans who create beautiful handcrafted items.

  • Ale-8-One merchandise: If you love the local soda, pick up a t-shirt or hat with the Ale-8-One logo.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashland

Ashland is known for its friendly locals and welcoming culture. Visitors should expect a slower pace of life and a strong sense of community. It’s important to be respectful of local customs, such as waving and saying hello to strangers on the street.

Best Time to Visit Ashland

The best time to visit Ashland is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the scenery is stunning. Summer can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashland

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Ashland:

Do:

  • Say hello and wave to locals.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs.

  • Ignore safety rules when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

  • Leave trash or litter in parks or green spaces.

Safety and Health

Ashland is a safe and welcoming community, but it’s always important to stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions when traveling. Here are a few tips for staying safe and healthy while in Ashland:

Safety in Ashland

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep valuables out of sight and lock car doors when parked.

  • Follow safety rules when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Ashland

  • Make sure you have all necessary vaccinations before traveling.

  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot weather.

  • Always apply sunscreen and wear appropriate clothing to protect against the sun.

Getting to Ashland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ashland is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Ashland

The closest airports to Ashland are:

  • Tri-State Airport (HTS) in Huntington, West Virginia (30 minutes away)

  • Blue Grass Airport (LEX) in Lexington, Kentucky (2 hours away)

Bus Stations Near Ashland

Greyhound has a bus station located in Ashland at:

  • 12979 US Route 60, Ashland, KY 41102

Other Options

Ashland is also easily accessible by car. It’s located just off of Interstate 64, and there are plenty of rental car options available.

How to Get Around in Ashland

Ashland is a relatively small city, and many of the top attractions are within walking distance. However, if you need to get around, there are several transportation options available, including:

  • Taxis

  • Rental cars

  • Public transportation

Where to Go Next from Ashland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Kentucky and the surrounding area, here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Lexington, Kentucky: Known for its horse racing and bourbon distilleries, Lexington is a beautiful city with a lot to offer.

  • Huntington, West Virginia: Just across the state line, Huntington is a great destination for history buffs, with several museums and historical landmarks to explore.

  • Red River Gorge: Located about two hours from Ashland, Red River Gorge is a stunning natural area that’s great for hiking and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Ashland is a charming and welcoming city that’s perfect for those looking for a relaxing getaway or outdoor adventure. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and thriving food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone in Ashland.
