Introduction to London

Located in the heart of Daniel Boone Country, in Kentucky, USA, London is a charming city that is steeped in history and surrounded by natural beauty. It offers a blend of rustic and modern appeal, making it an ideal destination for travelers from all walks of life. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there is something for everyone to enjoy in London.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at the must-see attractions, best things to do, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and tips for getting around and staying safe. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in London

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

London has a rich history that dates back centuries. Here are some of the top museums and historical landmarks that you shouldn’t miss:

  • The Levi Jackson Wilderness Road State Park: Explore the rich history of the iconic path, the Wilderness Road, that was used by settlers before the advent of modern transportation.
  • The London Downtown Murals: These stunning murals capture the essence of London’s history and culture, and are a must-visit for anyone interested in art and history.
  • The Laurel County African American Heritage Center: Discover the legacy of African Americans in London and Kentucky and their contributions to the local society and culture.
  • The District Court of Laurel County: This classic courthouse building has stood at the heart of London for centuries and is a great example of local history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of London: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

London is blessed with a scenic landscape that offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor exploration. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • The London Country Club: This beautiful golf course offers stunning views and a chance to play a round of golf while enjoying the natural beauty of London.
  • The Levi Jackson Wilderness Road State Park Lake: This serene lake is perfect for fishing, swimming, and just relaxing.
  • The Wildcat Adventures and Off-road Adventure Park: Get your adrenaline pumping with a wild ride offers ATV trails and nature’s cliff-lined amphitheater for obstacle course fun.
  • Manchester Music Hall & the Railbird festival: This music venue and annual festival boast the natural beauty of London to create an unforgettable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of London

London offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Kentucky. Here are some of the top trails to explore:

  • The Sheltowee Trace National Recreation Trail: This 308-mile trail takes you through some of the most beautiful natural areas in the state and leads you to the Daniel Boone National Forest.
  • The Daniel Boone National Forest: This vast and scenic forest offers miles of hiking trails for both beginners and experienced hikers, including the Rough Trail and the Cumberland River Trail. You may also enjoy fishing along the Cumberland River or a bird watching adventure on the Laurel River.
  • The London-Laurel County Wellness Park: Enjoy a trail and outdoor fitness equipment for a mid-hike workout at this scenic park perfect for a picnic.

Best Things to Do in London: Activities and Tours

From art galleries to bourbon distilleries, there is no shortage of exciting activities to enjoy in London. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a sip of native spirits: Visit the Jar Fly Brewing Company and try some of the locally-brewed beers. Sip on the aged whiskey Bourbon and Rye spirits available at various distilleries throughout the region.
  • Experience nature at its best: Plan a trip to the eventful 2022 Railbird festival a music, art, food, and culture festival that combines food, music, art, and nature.
  • Shop local: Visit the flea markets and antique stores in downtown London to find unique treasures and secondhand items from local collectors.
  • Explore the city: Discover architecture from 100-year-old buildings to modernism with a self-guided walking tour through London’s neighbourhoods.

Week-Long Itinerary for London

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for exploring London and its surroundings:

  • Day 1: Arrive in London and spend the day exploring the history and culture of downtown area.
  • Day 2: Take a hike through the beautiful national parks that surrounds London.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a day of leisure at the London-Laurel County Wellness Park and other breathtaking natural attractions.
  • Day 4: Sample local brewed spirits and tour local breweries.
  • Day 5: Embark on a scenic road trip exploring the nearby towns and sights.
  • Day 6: Enjoy an afternoon of antique shopping and exploring the local cafes and dining experiences.
  • Day 7: Depart from London

Food and Drink Scene of London

London has a food and drink scene that reflects its rich local cuisine and culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to indulge in:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgoo: A traditional Kentucky stew made with various meats and vegetables served over rice or potatoes.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: This savory breakfast dish features biscuits topped with gravy made with sausage and milk.
  • Fried Chicken and Cornbread: This classic southern dish features crispy fried chicken served with warm cornbread.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon and Rye whiskey: London is known for its excellent distilleries that produce some of the best bourbon and rye whiskey in the world.
  • Kentucky-Style Coffee: A classic coffee is perfect for caffeine lovers, and you might want to add a bit of native brandy to warm you up.

Where to Stay in London

London offers a variety of accommodations to suit the needs and budget of every traveler, from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  • Comfort Suites London: This hotel offers a central location with excellent amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center.
  • Super 8 London: A great budget-friendly option that is conveniently located near all the top attractions in London.
  • Renfro Valley KOA Holiday: Get away from the city and camp in this beautiful park for an unforgettable experience amidst natural beauty.

What to Buy in London: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back home, London has plenty of options for you to choose from. Here are some popular local products to consider:

  • Kentucky Bourbon: Bring back a bottle or two of London’s world-famous bourbon and rye for a taste of true southern charm.
  • Local Crafts and Pottery: Bring back some local crafts and pottery to decorate your home or as gifts for loved ones. Pottery is a lasting relic of the area’s Appalachian culture and history.
  • Personalized Souvenirs: Get a personalized keepsake from one of London’s many souvenir shops for a more memorable experience.

Local Culture and Customs of London

London locals are known for their warmth and hospitality. As a visitor, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions, such as being kind and courteous to everyone, and dressing appropriately into the Appalachian atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit London

The best time to visit London is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the leaves change colors. Summers tend to be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and icy.

Do’s and Don’ts of London

Do:

  • Do explore the local culture and history through the various museums and historical sites throughout the city.
  • Do try out local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment, respecting the Appalachian mountains and their beauty is paramount.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Don’t photograph without permission as some spots might be special to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in London

London is a safe city to visit, however, it is always good to take precautions while traveling.

Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert while exploring the city.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure.
  • Obey all traffic rules and regulations when driving or walking.
  • Check the local weather forecast for potentially severe weather patterns.

Health in London

Make sure to pack all necessary prescriptions with you when traveling to London. Weather changes, like humidity, might affect how allergic you are to certain environmental allergens.

Getting to London: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near London

  • London-Corbin Airport: This airport is a regional airport which means flights might be limited, but if looking to come from a nearby location, it’s a great option plus private planes for those owners.
  • Blue Grass Airport: This airport is located 77 miles away from London but offers a greater variety of flights and international options.

Bus Stations Near London

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This station is located in downtown London.

Other Options

  • Renting a Car: Rent or bring your own car to explore London at leisure.
  • Railway: London KY does not have a railway service but is available in surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in London

The easiest way to get around London is by car or by renting your own car. Many of the major attractions are located within a few miles of each other, making it easy to explore the city on foot, too.

Where to Go Next from London: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

London is surrounded by some of the most beautiful natural wonders and cities in Kentucky. Here are some nearby destinations to explore:

  • Daniel Boone National Forest: This breathtaking forest offers miles of hiking trails and stunning views.
  • Renfro Valley: This entertainment center hosts some of the biggest names in country music and is a must-visit destination for all music lovers.
  • Berea: Explore this historic town with an artsy culture and artisan shops for crafts and souvenirs.

Conclusion

London is an amazing city that offers an abundance of natural and cultural attractions, delicious local cuisine, and plenty of outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-packed vacation, London has something for everyone. Use our comprehensive guide to plan your next trip to London and make the most out of your visit.
