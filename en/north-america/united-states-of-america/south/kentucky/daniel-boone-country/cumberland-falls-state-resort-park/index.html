Introduction to Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Located in Daniel Boone Country, Kentucky, Cumberland Falls State Resort Park is one of the hidden gems of America’s beautiful South. Named the “Niagara of the South,” Cumberland Falls is a natural wonder with its 68-foot waterfall, stunning river views, and a wealth of outdoor activities. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, history buff, adventure seeker, or a family looking for a relaxing vacation, Cumberland Falls State Resort Park offers something for everyone. Let’s dive into the guide and plan your visit to this beautiful park.

List of Places to Visit in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum lovers will appreciate the Cumberland Museum, which provides a rich look at the Native American heritage, geologic details, and the history of the park, from early settlements to the present day. Another great place to visit is Barthell Coal Mining Camp, a restored mining camp from the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cumberland Falls is the main attraction in the park. A visit allows for a breathtaking view of the falls and a chance to take in the beautiful surroundings. Take a hike to Eagle Falls, a beautiful, secluded waterfall with a scenic hike leading to it, and Moonbow Trail, which allows you to experience awe-inspiring views of the Cumberland River.

For an adrenaline rush, try out bouldering at the Dog Slaughter Falls Trailhead or whitewater rafting with Sheltowee Trace Adventure Resort. Fishing and boating are also popular pastimes in the Cumberland River. The park also offers horseback riding, golfing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Cumberland Falls State Resort Park has several scenic trails to explore the beauty of nature. Moonbow Trail, which takes about 3 miles to reach the falls, is the most popular hiking trail in the park.

Hikers can also explore the Dog Slaughter Falls Trail, which goes through forests and rock formations to a scenic waterfall. Bikers have the option to bike the partially paved Cumberland River Bike Trail or the Sheltowee Trace National Recreation Trail.

Best Things to Do in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: Activities and Tours

Cumberland Falls State Resort Park provides a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Take a guided horse trail tour or a naturalist-led hike and learn about the flora and fauna of the area. Night hikes are also available, allowing you to see the park in a whole new light.

Visitors can attend the Cumberland Falls Storytelling Festival, take a ghost walk with the park ranger, or participate in the Full Moon Paddle. Don’t miss out on the Laser Light Show, which electrifies the falls with an explosion of color and music regularly.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Need help planning your week of adventure at Cumberland Falls State Resort Park? Plan to spend a day exploring the falls with hiking and bouldering activities, then head out on a horse trail tour the next day. Take a naturalist-led hike to Eagle Falls before spending an evening around a campfire. Take a day trip to visit Barthell Coal Mining Camp, followed by a day of biking and golfing. End the week with a Full Moon Paddle on the river and witness the spectacular sight of a moonbow.

Food and Drink Scene of Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Must-Try Dishes

At Cumberland Falls State Resort Park, you must try the famous Kentucky hot brown, a hearty dish originating from the Brown Hotel in Louisville. The dish comprises oven-baked turkey on white bread with bacon, smothered in Mornay sauce. Visitors can also enjoy other Kentucky specialties, like the burgoo, a thick stew made with beef, pork, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Kentucky is well-known for its bourbon. While at Cumberland Falls State Resort Park, sample some famous brands of the South, such as Woodford Reserve or Maker’s Mark. Local craft beer and wine are also available at the park.

Where to Stay in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodations are abundant in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park, ranging from cabins to campsites to hotels. Visitors can choose to stay at the Dupont Lodge, featuring 64 rooms, a restaurant, and a lounge. For something more rustic, there are several cabins with varying amenities, including a hot tub, fire pit, or fireplace.

For campers, the park has several campgrounds with different facilities. Horse camping is also available, and guests can bring their horses or use the park’s stables.

What to Buy in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Cumberland Falls State Resort Park home with you by browsing through the park’s many gift shops. From books to clothing to unique souvenirs, there’s something for everyone. You can also purchase local honey, hand-made soaps, or Appalachian crafts in several stores located within the park.

Local Culture and Customs of Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Kentucky is known for its unique cuisine, history, cultural traditions, and southern hospitality. Cumberland Falls State Resort Park embodies all of these qualities, and visitors can experience the best of traditional Kentucky hospitality and local culture during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

The best time to visit Cumberland Falls State Resort Park is during the Spring and Autumn months when the weather is mild, and the colorful foliage provides picturesque scenery. The park holds several events throughout the year, including Fourth of July fireworks and Christmas activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Do:

  • Respect the environment by not littering.
  • Bring plenty of water and comfortable shoes for hiking.
  • Observe local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Feed wildlife.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Remove natural or man-made objects.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Cumberland Falls State Resort Park is a relatively safe and secure park, and visitors should always keep an eye on their valuables. If hiking or exploring in less-traveled areas, it’s always best to let someone know where you’re going and when you plan on returning.

Health in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Visitors should take extra precautions to protect themselves from mosquito and tick bites, as the area is known for tick-borne illnesses and the prevalence of mosquitoes. Always bring insect repellent and use it regularly.

Getting to Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

The nearest airport to the park is Blue Grass Airport In Lexington, which is approximately a 1.5-hour drive from the park.

Bus Stations Near Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

The closest bus station to Cumberland Falls State Resort Park is in Corbin, which is approximately 15.7 km from the park, and several taxi services can take visitors to the park.

Other Options

Visitors could also take a train to the Cumberland Gap National Park Station, which is located approximately 30 minutes from the park. However, visitors will need to arrange their transportation from the station to the park.

How to Get Around in Cumberland Falls State Resort Park

Hiking and biking are popular modes of transport within the park. Visitors can rent bikes at the park, and electric golf carts are also available for rent. Visitors can take a trolley ride to the Cumberland Falls outlook to experience awe-inspiring views of the park.

Where to Go Next from Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re inspired for more outdoor adventures, several other great parks and attractions are in the surrounding area of Cumberland Falls State Resort Park. These include Kentucky Horse Park, Big South Fork National River and Recreation Area, and the Mammoth Cave National Park.

Conclusion

Cumberland Falls State Resort Park has something for everyone, from enjoying the natural beauty of the park to experiencing its rich cultural heritage. Plan your trip with our comprehensive guide, and you won’t be disappointed. Don’t forget to take plenty of pictures as memories of the beautiful park and your experiences while visiting.
