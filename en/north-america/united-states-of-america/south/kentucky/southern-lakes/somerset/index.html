Introduction to Somerset

Located in the heart of Southern Lakes, Kentucky, Somerset is a charming city known for its natural beauty, vibrant culture, and friendly people. It is surrounded by stunning lakes, rolling hills, and lush forests, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Somerset is proud of its rich history, which is reflected in its many museums, galleries, and historical landmarks. Visitors can also explore the vibrant food and drink scene, shop for unique souvenirs, and experience the warm hospitality of the locals.

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing retreat or an exciting adventure, Somerset has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll share the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to buy in Somerset.

List of Places to Visit in Somerset

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Somerset is home to a variety of museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into its rich heritage and fascinating culture. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • The SomerSplash waterpark: A perfect place to cool off during summers, this waterpark offers a variety of slides, pools, and other water attractions for all ages.

  • The Mill Springs Battlefield and Visitor Center: This historical site is a must-visit for history buffs. It is the largest battlefield in Kentucky and showcases the history of the Civil War.

  • The Lake Cumberland State Resort Park: This state park offers beautiful views of the lake, hiking trails, fishing, boating, and other outdoor activities.

  • The Master Musicians Festival: This music festival is one of the best in Kentucky and features national and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Somerset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Somerset is known for its stunning natural beauty, which is reflected in its many outdoor attractions and scenic spots. Here are some of the top natural attractions of Somerset:

  • Daniel Boone National Forest: This breathtaking forest is home to many hiking trails, camping sites, and stunning waterfalls.

  • The Big South Fork National River and Recreation Area: This vast park offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, kayaking, and horseback riding.

  • The Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: Known as the “Niagara of the South,” this stunning waterfall is a must-see for nature lovers.

  • The Lake Cumberland: The main attraction of the city, the Lake Cumberland is a 65,530-acre reservoir that offers a range of water sports and activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somerset

Somerset is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and hiking/biking is one of the best ways to explore its natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes of Somerset:

  • The Sheltowee Trace Trail: This 320-mile trail offers stunning views of the forest, waterfalls, and other natural wonders.

  • The Lake Cumberland State Resort Park Trails: This park offers a range of hiking and biking trails for all levels.

  • The Big South Fork National River and Recreation Area Trails: This park offers a variety of trails through the forest, river, and scenic overlooks.

Best Things to Do in Somerset: Activities and Tours

Apart from outdoor activities and sightseeing, Somerset offers a range of other things to do and experience. Here are some of the top activities and tours to try in Somerset:

  • The Lake Cumberland Winery Tour: This tour offers wine tastings and vineyard tours in the beautiful setting of Lake Cumberland.

  • The Bee Rock Overlook: Located in the Daniel Boone National Forest, this scenic overlook offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and cliffs.

  • The Waitsboro Hills Golf Course: This 18-hole golf course offers stunning views of the lake and hills and is perfect for golf enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Somerset

For those planning a week-long trip to Somerset, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Mill Springs Battlefield and Visitor Center and go on a boat tour of Lake Cumberland.

Day 2: Explore the Daniel Boone National Forest and take a hiking/biking trail or enjoy some adventure sports.

Days 3-4: Visit the Big South Fork National River and Recreation Area and go hiking, camping, or kayaking.

Day 5-6: Explore the Lake Cumberland State Resort Park and take part in water activities such as boating, fishing, and swimming.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene of Somerset.

Food and Drink Scene of Somerset

The food and drink scene of Somerset is a highlight of any trip to this charming city. From local favorites to international cuisine, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Somerset:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgoo: This traditional Kentucky stew is made with beef, pork, chicken, and vegetables and is served with cornbread.

  • Hot Brown: This classic dish is a turkey and bacon loaded dish topped off with cheese sauce.

  • Bourbon Balls: These sweet treats are made with bourbon-infused chocolate and are one of the most popular souvenirs from Kentucky.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Julep: This iconic drink is made with bourbon, sugar, water, and fresh mint and is a must-try in Somerset.

  • Ale-8-One: This local soft drink is unique to Kentucky and has been a favorite since 1926.

Where to Stay in Somerset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Somerset offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, here are some of the best places to stay in Somerset:

  • The Hampton Inn Somerset: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, and free breakfast.

  • The Quality Inn & Suites Somerset: This hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and free breakfast.

  • The Tee Dee’s Inn: This guesthouse offers cozy rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Somerset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Somerset, here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Bourbon: Kentucky is known for its bourbon, and Somerset has some of the best distilleries in the state.

  • Kentucky Derby Hats: These iconic hats are a must-have souvenir from Kentucky.

  • Handmade Crafts: Somerset is home to many talented artisans, and you’ll find a range of unique handmade crafts in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Somerset

Somerset is proud of its southern heritage, and its culture is reflected in its food, music, and art. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Somerset

The best time to visit Somerset is from spring to fall when the weather is milder, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somerset

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Do take part in outdoor activities and explore the natural wonders.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Don’t be disrespectful to the locals or their culture.

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Somerset

Somerset is a safe city, but travelers should take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on personal belongings and avoiding walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Somerset

The health facilities in Somerset are of a high standard, and there are pharmacies and hospitals throughout the city. However, travelers should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Somerset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Somerset

The nearest airports to Somerset are Lexington-Blue Grass Airport (LEX) and Nashville International Airport (BNA). Both airports are well-connected and offer regular flights to major cities in the U.S.

Bus Stations Near Somerset

Greyhound Bus Lines and Southeastern Stages are the two primary bus lines that operate in Somerset.

Other Options

Rental cars and private taxis are available in Somerset for those wishing to explore the city at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Somerset

The best way to explore Somerset is by car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Somerset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The neighboring cities of Bowling Green and Lexington are popular destinations to explore from Somerset. Mammoth Cave National Park is also a must-visit attraction and is located just a short drive from Somerset.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or unique cuisine, Somerset has something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality, it’s a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
