Introduction to Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

Located in Southern Lakes, Kentucky, Lake Cumberland State Resort Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and history buffs. The park boasts over 1,200 miles of shoreline, making it one of the largest man-made lakes in the country. Visitors can hike through miles of trails, enjoy water-based activities, explore natural caves, or simply relax in beautiful surroundings.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cumberland Falls State Resort Park: One of the most iconic natural attractions of Kentucky, the Cumberland Falls is famous for its moonbows, a natural optical phenomenon that occurs during full moons. Visitors can also hike along the trails, fish, or picnic at the park.

  • The Mill Springs Battlefield Museum: Located near the Cumberland River, the Mill Springs Battlefield Museum marks the site where Union forces fought against Confederate soldiers in 1862. The museum features exhibits and artifacts related to the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Lake Cumberland State Resort Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Cumberland: A popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming, Lake Cumberland is surrounded by beautiful scenery and natural wildlife. Visitors can rent boats, kayaks, or jet skis, or simply enjoy a lazy day at the beach.

  • The Eagle Falls Trail: One of the most popular hiking trails in the park, the Eagle Falls Trail offers stunning views of the Cumberland River and its waterfalls. The trail is moderate in difficulty level.

  • The Dupont Lodge: Built in the 1930s, the Dupont Lodge is a beautiful example of rustic architecture. Visitors can enjoy a meal at the restaurant, grab a drink at the bar, or simply relax by the fireplace.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

  • The Moonbow Trail: A popular 1.5 mile trail that leads visitors to the Cumberland Falls. The trail offers scenic views of the river and the falls.

  • The Eagle Falls Trail: A moderate 3.5 mile trail that offers amazing views of the river and its waterfalls.

  • The Lick Creek Trail: A 12 mile trail that offers scenic views of the lake and the park’s natural wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Lake Cumberland State Resort Park: Activities and Tours

  • Boating and Fishing: Lake Cumberland is a popular destination for boating and fishing. Visitors can rent boats, kayaks, or jet skis, or join a guided fishing tour.

  • Cave Tours: Lake Cumberland State Resort Park offers guided tours to the Natural Arch Cave and the Barthell Coal Mine.

  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can go on a guided horseback ride through the park’s trails.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

Day 1: Arrive at Lake Cumberland State Resort Park. Check-in at Dupont Lodge.

Day 2: Visit the Cumberland Falls State Resort Park. Join a guided moonbow tour after sunset.

Day 3: Explore historic Mill Springs Battlefield Museum. Enjoy a picnic lunch by the river.

Day 4: Go on a guided cave tour of Natural Arch Cave.

Day 5: Rent a boat and spend the day on Lake Cumberland. Enjoy swimming and watersports.

Day 6: Go on a guided horseback ride through the park’s trails.

Day 7: Relax at the lodge. Enjoy a farewell dinner at the restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgoo: A traditional Kentucky stew made with beef or pork, vegetables, and spices.

  • Hot browns: A local dish made with turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce.

  • Bourbon Balls: A sweet treat made with bourbon, chocolate, and pecans.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: A popular local spirit, Kentucky Bourbon is made with at least 51% corn and matured in charred oak barrels.

  • Ale 8 One: A local soda made with ginger and citrus flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

The people of Lake Cumberland are known for their southern hospitality and reverence for tradition. Respect for nature and the outdoors is a common cultural value, as is the love for local food and drink.

Best Time to Visit Lake Cumberland State Resort Park

The best time to visit Lake Cumberland State Resort Park is in the spring and fall when the weather is cooler and the crowds are thinner.
