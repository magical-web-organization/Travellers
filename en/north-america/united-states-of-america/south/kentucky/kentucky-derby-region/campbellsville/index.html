Introduction to Campbellsville

Located in the Kentucky Derby Region, Campbellsville is a small town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. It is the county seat of Taylor County and boasts an attractive downtown area filled with boutiques, antique shops, and great dining options. Campbellsville is an excellent destination for anyone looking to experience a quintessential southern charm and hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Campbellsville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Green River Lake State Park

Green River Lake State Park is one of the top attractions in Campbellsville, featuring spectacular views of the lake. The park is a perfect spot for outdoor activities, including boating, fishing, and hiking. It is home to a variety of wildlife, making it a great place for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.

Campbellsville Historical Society Museum

The Campbellsville Historical Society Museum is an excellent destination for anyone interested in the history of the town. It features exhibits of the town’s history, including the famous Civil war battle fought in the region. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, photos, and documents that highlight the town’s development over the years.

Civil War Sites

The town of Campbellsville played a significant role during the Civil War, making it a perfect destination for history buffs. The Confederate Monument, Battle for Tebbs Bend Civil War Reenactment, and the cemetery for the Union soldiers who died is a must-visit site for those interested in learning about the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Campbellsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Green River Lake

Green River Lake is one of the most popular attractions in the area, featuring beautiful views, a marina, and a recreational area. The lake is perfect for swimming, boating, jet-skiing, and fishing, and boasts of over 8,200 acres of water for visitors to explore.

The Bourbon Trail

The Bourbon Trail, located within driving distance of Campbellsville, is a must-visit attraction for anyone who enjoys bourbon. The trail offers visitors a chance to explore the history of bourbon in Kentucky by touring a variety of distilleries, enjoying tastings, and learning about the complicated process of making bourbon.

Mammoth Cave National Park

Mammoth Cave National Park is situated a short drive away from Campbellsville and is a perfect destination for anyone looking for outdoor adventures. The park, which is home to the world’s longest-known cave system, offers visitors a chance to explore the caves and enjoy activities such as hiking, canoeing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campbellsville

Campbellsville boasts of scenic hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the town’s natural beauty. The Green River Lake Trail is a well-maintained hiking trail that offers visitors the chance to see breathtaking views of the lake and surrounding forests. The loop trail is approximately 2.3 miles long and is ideal for hikers of all experience levels.

Best Things to Do in Campbellsville: Activities and Tours

Campbellsville Ghost Tour

Take a lantern-lit walking tour through historic downtown Campbellsville and learn about the town’s haunted past. The Ghost Tour is an excellent way to learn more about Campbellsville’s rich history and legends while enjoying a spine-tingling experience.

Campbellsville University Sports

Campbellsville University is known for its excellent sports programs, featuring basketball, football, and baseball teams that compete in various leagues. Attending a live game is a great way to experience the town’s lively college spirit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Campbellsville

Day 1: Explore the town’s history with a visit to the Campbellsville Historical Society Museum and the Civil War sites. Day 2: Enjoy a day of outdoor activities at Green River Lake State Park. Day 3: Go on a Bourbon Trail tour and learn about the art of bourbon making. Day 4: Take a guided ghost tour through downtown Campbellsville. Day 5: Explore Mammoth Cave National Park and enjoy a day of hiking, kayaking, and canoeing. Day 6: Watch a Campbellsville University sports game. Day 7: Spend the day shopping at the local boutiques and antique shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Campbellsville

Must-Try Dishes

Campbellsville is known for its delicious southern-style cooking, and visitors must try some of the town’s special dishes:

  • Fried chicken and biscuits
  • Hot browns
  • Burgoo
  • Derby pie

Local Beverages

Kentucky is famous for its bourbon, and Campbellsville is no exception. Visitors can enjoy the local bourbon at the various distilleries on the Bourbon Trail or experience the town’s live music scene while enjoying cocktails at one of the town’s many bars.

What to Buy in Campbellsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home a piece of Campbellsville with them by purchasing souvenirs and local products unique to the town. Some of the popular items to buy include handcrafted pottery, homemade jams and jellies, and handmade quilts.

Local Culture and Customs of Campbellsville

Campbellsville is a town steeped in southern hospitality, and visitors can expect to be welcomed with warmth and friendliness. The town values its history, and visitors are encouraged to respect the various civil war sites and local events that celebrate the town’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Campbellsville

The best time to visit Campbellsville is during the summer months when visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as swimming, boating, and fishing. The fall months are also a great time to visit, as the trees start to turn, and the town’s beauty is highlighted by the colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campbellsville

Do:

  • Respect the town’s rich history and heritage.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the town’s outdoor attractions.
  • Experience the local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Damage or vandalize any of the town’s historical sites.
  • Forget to be respectful to the locals and their businesses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Campbellsville

Campbellsville is a safe town, and visitors can enjoy their trip without any significant safety concerns. However, visitors are encouraged to take normal precautions, such as keeping valuables hidden and following safety guidelines at outdoor attractions.

Health in Campbellsville

Campbellsville has various medical facilities that cater to residents and visitors alike. Visitors are encouraged to ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage for their trip.

Getting to Campbellsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Campbellsville

The Louisville International Airport (SDF) is the nearest major airport located approximately 75 miles north of Campbellsville. Visitors may also opt to use the Lexington Blue Grass Airport (LEX), which is 85 miles north.

Bus Stations Near Campbellsville

Campbellsville does not have a bus station, but visitors can use the Greyhound bus service to travel to the nearest station in Elizabethtown (25 miles away). From Elizabethtown, visitors can take a cab or rent a car to reach Campbellsville.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Campbellsville using the Interstate 65 that runs north-south and connects the town to Nashville and Louisville.

How to Get Around in Campbellsville

Campbellsville is a small town and is easily accessible on foot, but visitors may also want to rent a car to explore the nearby attractions.
