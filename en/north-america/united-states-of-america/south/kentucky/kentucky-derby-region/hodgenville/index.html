Introduction to Hodgenville

Located in the heart of the Kentucky Derby Region, Hodgenville is a small city known for its rich history and natural beauty. The city features several museums and historical sites, as well as hiking and biking trails, and scenic views. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Hodgenville has plenty of activities to offer. This travel guide provides a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Hodgenville.

List of Places to Visit in Hodgenville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Abraham Lincoln Birthplace National Historical Park

One of the most famous landmarks in Hodgenville, the Abraham Lincoln Birthplace National Historical Park is a site that honors the birth and early life of Abraham Lincoln. The park includes a memorial building, a visitor center, and a 19th-century cabin.

Lincoln Museum

Located in downtown Hodgenville, the Lincoln Museum is a museum dedicated to the life and legacy of Abraham Lincoln. The museum features several exhibits, including artifacts and memorabilia from Lincoln’s life.

The Lincoln Legacy Museum

The Lincoln Legacy Museum is located on the historic campus of Lincoln Memorial University in Harrogate, Tennessee, and it chronicles the life and legacy of President Abraham Lincoln.

Natural Attractions of Hodgenville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brier Creek Trail

This 2.5-mile trail is a popular hiking and biking destination that offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape. The trail is open year-round and is suitable for all fitness levels.

Buffalo Creek

Buffalo Creek is a popular spot for fishing and kayaking. The creek is known for its calm waters and scenic surroundings.

Freeman Lake Park

Located just outside Hodgenville, Freeman Lake Park is a 170-acre park that offers hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, playgrounds, and a free public boat ramp. The lake is also a popular spot for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hodgenville

Buffalo Creek Trail

This 2.25-mile trail is a popular destination for hiking and biking. The trail offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape and is suitable for all fitness levels.

Lincoln Heritage Trail

The Lincoln Heritage Trail is a 13-mile trail that connects the Abraham Lincoln Birthplace National Historical Park and the Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial in Indiana. The trail features scenic views and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Hodgenville: Activities and Tours

Visit the Abraham Lincoln Birthplace National Historical Park

Visit the birthplace of Abraham Lincoln and explore the memorial building and 19th-century cabin that are part of the National Historical Park.

Take a Walking Tour of Downtown Hodgenville

Explore the historic downtown area and visit local shops, restaurants, and museums.

Enjoy the Outdoors at Freeman Lake Park

Visit Freeman Lake Park and enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, boating, and picnicking.

Food and Drink Scene of Hodgenville

Must-Try Dishes

Burgoo

Burgoo is a classic Kentucky stew made with a variety of meats and vegetables. It’s a hearty and delicious dish that is a must-try when visiting Hodgenville.

Hot Brown Sandwich

The Hot Brown Sandwich is a Kentucky classic. It’s an open-faced sandwich that typically consists of turkey, bacon, tomato, and Mornay sauce.

Local Beverages

Bourbon

Kentucky is famous for its bourbon, and Hodgenville is no exception. Several local distilleries offer tours and samples of their products.

Ale-8-One

Ale-8-One is a ginger and citrus-flavored soft drink that is a favorite among locals in Hodgenville and throughout Kentucky.

Where to Stay in Hodgenville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lincoln Jamboree Guesthouse

Located in the heart of Hodgenville, the Lincoln Jamboree Guesthouse is a charming and affordable option for travelers. The guesthouse features four bedrooms and can accommodate up to ten people.

La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Hodgenville

The La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Hodgenville is a modern and comfortable hotel located just outside of downtown Hodgenville. The hotel offers a variety of amenities, including free breakfast, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Hodgenville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lincoln Souvenirs

Many local shops in Hodgenville sell souvenirs related to Abraham Lincoln, including books, keychains, and t-shirts.

Bourbon and Ale-8-One

Several local stores offer a variety of bourbons and Ale-8-One soft drinks that make great souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Hodgenville

Hodgenville is a small city known for its friendly locals and rich history. Visitors should be respectful of the city’s cultural heritage and should follow local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hodgenville

The best time to visit Hodgenville is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. Summer can get hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy, so plan accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hodgenville

Hodgenville is a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid accidents or theft. Don’t leave valuables in plain sight in cars, and be aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking.

Health in Hodgenville

There are several medical clinics and hospitals in and around Hodgenville. Visitors should make sure they have health insurance and access to medical care if needed.

Getting to Hodgenville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hodgenville

The Louisville International Airport is the closest airport to Hodgenville. It’s about an hour’s drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Hodgenville

There are several bus stations located near Hodgenville, including the Louisville Greyhound Station and the Elizabethtown Greyhound Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from the airport to Hodgenville.

How to Get Around in Hodgenville

The best way to get around Hodgenville is by car or bike. There are several rental car companies located in nearby cities, and bike rentals are available at several local shops.

Where to Go Next from Hodgenville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mammoth Cave National Park

Located about an hour’s drive from Hodgenville, Mammoth Cave National Park is home to the longest cave system in the world. Visitors can take guided tours of the cave and explore the surrounding park.

Churchill Downs

Located in Louisville, Churchill Downs is home to the Kentucky Derby, one of the most famous horse races in the world. Visitors can take tours of the racetrack and see the famous Churchill Downs Museum.

Conclusion

Hodgenville is a small city with a rich history and natural beauty. Visitors can explore historical sites and museums, hike and bike on scenic trails, and enjoy local cuisine and beverages. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Hodgenville has plenty to offer.
