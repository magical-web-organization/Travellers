Introduction to Paris

Located in the Bluegrass Region of Kentucky, Paris is a charming and idyllic city that offers visitors a perfect blend of modern amenities and rural appeal. Known for its rolling hills, horse farms, and rich cultural heritage, Paris has something to offer everyone, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Paris

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hopewell Museum and Mansion

The Hopewell Museum and Mansion is a historic home built in 1814, which now serves as a museum, with exhibits on Kentucky’s famous thoroughbred horse industry, Civil War history, and local art and ceramics.

Duncan Tavern Historic Center

Duncan Tavern, built-in 1788, is one of the most important historical homes in Kentucky, housing a museum dedicated to the era of early settlers in the region.

Cane Ridge Meeting House

Cane Ridge Meeting House is one of the most significant landmarks in the history of the Christian Church. The church holds great significance as it was the birthplace of the Restoration movement, which aimed to unite all Christians on the basis of the Bible alone.

Natural Attractions of Paris: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stoner Creek

Stoner Creek is a beautiful stream that runs through Bourbon County and is considered a Class I waterway. The creek is also a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming during the summer months.

Thoroughbred Heritage Horse Farm Tours

Experience the beauty of the Bluegrass Region and visit some of the most renowned thoroughbred horse farms in the world.

Paris-Bourbon County YMCA

The YMCA offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking trails, swimming pools, and fitness centers, making it a great place to spend a day for any outdoor enthusiast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paris

With its rolling hills and stunning scenery, Paris and the Bluegrass Region offer some of the best hiking and biking trails around. The following are a few routes to explore:

West Sixth Farm

The West Sixth Farm Bike Trail is a 5.5-mile-long trail that goes through the heart of the West Sixth Farm, giving visitors an unmatched riding experience through beautiful scenery.

Fort Boonesborough State Park

Located just outside of Paris in Richmond, the Fort Boonesborough State Park Trails are a must-visit for any outdoor enthusiast. The park offers several trails, all varying in difficulty and offering a unique perspective of the local landscape.

Bourbon County Trail

The Bourbon County Trail is a 50-mile-long trail that starts in Paris and goes through the beautiful landscape of the Bluegrass Region. It offers a variety of terrains, including paved roads, dirt roads, and gravel paths.

Best Things to Do in Paris: Activities and Tours

Bourbon County Distillery Tours

Those who have an interest in the art of whiskey making, the Bourbon County Distillery tour should definitely be on their list. Learn about the history of the whiskey industry, see how it is made, and sample some of the finest whiskeys in the region.

Salt River Outfitters

The Salt River Outfitters offers various activities, including kayaking, canoeing, and tubing, making it an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts looking for adventure.

Bluegrass Scenic Railway & Museum

The Bluegrass Scenic Railway & Museum is a leading attraction for all rail fans. Visitors can take a tour of the museum that tells the history of railways in the Bluegrass Region and then hop on a train to explore some of the region’s scenic beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Paris

Day 1: Visit the Hopewell Museum and Mansion, Duncan Tavern Historic Center and Cane Ridge Meeting House

Day 2: Go hiking on the Bourbon County Trail

Day 3: Visit the Bourbon County Distillery

Day 4: Experience the West Sixth Farm Bike Trail and visit the Bluegrass Scenic Railway & Museum

Day 5: Tour the local horse farms

Day 6: Go kayaking, canoeing, and tubing at Salt River Outfitters

Day 7: Relax at the Paris-Bourbon County YMCA

Food and Drink Scene of Paris

Must-Try Dishes

Burgoo

Burgoo is a hearty stew that originated in Kentucky and is typically made with a variety of meats and vegetables, giving it a unique flavor.

Hot Browns

Hot Browns are an open-faced sandwich topped with turkey, bacon, and a creamy sauce. It is traditionally eaten for breakfast or lunch

Local Beverages

Bourbon Whiskey

Bourbon Whiskey is a well-known staple of the Kentucky area and is produced in Bourbon County and the surrounding region.

Ale-8-One

Ale-8-One, a regional soft drink, is a ginger and citrus-flavored soda that is popular throughout Kentucky.

Where to Stay in Paris: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Campbell House

The Campbell House is an elegant boutique hotel that offers guests comfort, luxury, and style. It has a rich history as it was built in 1951 and is known for exceptional service with modern amenities.

The Kastle Inn

The Kastle Inn is a budget-friendly option for those visiting Paris. It offers comfortable rooms, a convenient location, and reasonable rates, making it popular among budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Paris: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kentucky Bourbon

One of the most popular souvenirs to take back from Paris would be a bottle of Kentucky Bourbon. The rich history and unique taste of the spirit make it a perfect gift for anyone.

Horse Racing Memorabilia

Horse Racing Memorabilia is another popular souvenir to take back home from Paris, primarily if one plans to visit some of the famous horse farms or attend any of the racing events.

Local Culture and Customs of Paris

Paris is a community with rich cultural heritage. Residents of Paris are proud of their heritage, tradition, and culture. A handshake is a common greeting in Paris, and it is customary to use ‘‘sir’’ and ‘‘ma’am’’ when addressing people in formal settings.

Best Time to Visit Paris

The best time to visit Paris is from mid-March until mid-November when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable. Summer and fall are peak seasons, so it’s essential to make reservations in advance to ensure a pleasant trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paris

Do:

  • Wear comfortable shoes and dress appropriately for the season
  • Treat others with respect and be polite
  • Carry cash as not all places will accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the area
  • Make excessive noise or engage in reckless behavior
  • Ignore local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Paris

Paris is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid becoming a victim of crime. This includes staying aware of their surroundings, not leaving valuables in plain sight, and locking doors and windows when leaving a place.

Health in Paris

Visitors to Paris should ensure they have up-to-date medical insurance, and travelers should take measures to protect themselves against mosquito bites, ticks, and other harmful insects.

Getting to Paris: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paris

The nearest major international airport is Blue Grass Airport, Lexington, which is just twenty minutes from Paris. Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport and Louisville International Airport are also within a two-hour drive from Paris.

Bus Stations Near Paris

The Greyhound bus station in Lexington is the nearest bus station to Paris, which is roughly fifteen minutes away.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Paris from the nearby airports to make the journey more comfortable and enjoyable.

How to Get Around in Paris

Visitors can easily get around in Paris using public transportation, including buses and taxis. Biking is also a popular way to explore the area, and bike rentals are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Paris: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lexington

Lexington is only a short drive from Paris and is known for its beautiful horse farms, excellent local cuisine, and passionate sports fans.

Red River Gorge

Red River Gorge is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and offers hiking, rock climbing, swimming, and other adventures.

Louisville

Louisville is a vibrant city with a rich history and culture, and a range of attractions and activities such as the Louisville Mega Cavern.

Conclusion

Paris is a picturesque city with a rich history, home to a vibrant culture. From its outdoor beauty to the depths of its cultural heritage, there’s something in Paris for everyone. Our comprehensive guide on Paris will help you plan your visit and make it an unforgettable journey. Whether you’re looking for nature, history, or culture, Paris has so much to discover.
