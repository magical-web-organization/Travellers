Introduction to Mount Sterling

Mount Sterling is a small city located in the Bluegrass Region of Kentucky, in the southern part of the United States. The town is steeped in history, with many museums, landmarks, and historic buildings. Mount Sterling is known for its superb outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Sterling

Here are some of the best places to visit in Mount Sterling, Kentucky:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old Town Walking Tour

Take a walking tour of the historic district, including the beautiful courthouse and the historic homes.

Montgomery County Historical Society

Learn about the history and heritage of Montgomery County, including the Civil War and the Underground Railroad.

Gateway Regional Arts Center

Buildings that were once abandoned are now transformed into galleries and shops. You’ll find exhibitions of local art, pottery, and sculptures as well as spaces for live performances.

Natural Attractions of Mount Sterling: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Red River Gorge

Take a day trip to the Red River Gorge, part of the Daniel Boone National Forest. Take a hike or a nature walk or swim in the crystal-clear water.

Natural Bridge State Park

This scenic overlook is located in the Daniel Boone National Forest surrounded by beautiful mountains. Take a hike or ride the Skylift to the top for breathtaking views.

Cave Run Lake

Spend a day fishing, boating, and enjoying the sunshine in this recreational area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Sterling

Trails of the Red River Gorge

Plan your hike or bike ride along one of the many trails in the Red River Gorge. They’re well-marked and range from easy to strenuous.

Salt Lick Creek Trail

Explore 9.7 miles of looped trails in Daniel Boone National Forest near Salt Lick Creek and the Red River.

Best Things to Do in Mount Sterling: Activities and Tours

Zipline Tour

Enjoy an exhilarating ride through the trees on a zipline tour through Red River Gorge and the Daniel Boone National Forest.

Bourbon Trail

Take a tour of one of the numerous bourbon distilleries in the Bluegrass Region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Sterling

Day 1: Old Town Walking Tour and Montgomery County Historical Society

Day 2: Red River Gorge

Day 3: Natural Bridge State Park

Day 4: Salt Lick Creek Trail

Day 5: Zipline Tour

Day 6: Bourbon Trail Tour

Day 7: Cave Run Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Sterling

Must-Try Dishes

Burgers at Rooster’s

Try the famous burgers at Rooster’s, which consistently ranks as one of the best burger places in Kentucky.

Steak at the Cattleman’s Roadhouse

Enjoy a steak cooked to perfection at this popular restaurant.

Local Beverages

Bourbon at Town Branch Distillery

Take a tour of the Town Branch Distillery and try their flagship product, Kentucky Bourbon.

Wine at Jean Farris Winery and Bistro

Treat yourself to some Kentucky wine at Jean Farris Winery and Bistro, which offers a relaxing atmosphere and excellent food.

Where to Stay in Mount Sterling: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ramada by Wyndham Mount Sterling

This hotel offers modern, spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a free hot breakfast.

Hometown BnB

This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Mount Sterling: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kentucky Bourbon

Bring back the taste of Kentucky with a bottle of bourbon from a local distillery.

Handmade Pottery

Visit the Gateway Regional Arts Center to find unique, handcrafted pottery from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Sterling

Kentucky is known for its horse racing and bourbon, and Mount Sterling is no exception. Be sure to take part in the local culture by enjoying a glass of bourbon or watching a horse race.

Best Time to Visit Mount Sterling

The best time to visit Mount Sterling is in the fall when the leaves are changing and the temperatures are cool. The town also boasts a number of events throughout the year, such as the Gateway to the Gorge Festival in September, and the Honey Bee Festival in October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Sterling

Do:

  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.

  • Try the local bourbon and beer.

  • Visit the Gateway Regional Arts Center.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind when hiking.

  • Bring pets to the natural attractions.

  • Smoke indoors in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Sterling

Mount Sterling is a safe town, but be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Mount Sterling

Bring sunscreen and plenty of water when hiking and exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Mount Sterling: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Sterling

The nearest airport is theBlue Grass Airport in Lexington, which is approximately 39 miles from Mount Sterling.

Bus Stations Near Mount Sterling

Greyhound and Megabus offer bus service to a station in Lexington, where you can connect to Mount Sterling via local transit.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, rent a car and travel to Mount Sterling by Interstate 64.

How to Get Around in Mount Sterling

The best way to get around Mount Sterling is on foot, by bike, or by renting a car.

Where to Go Next from Mount Sterling: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lexington

Lexington is the “Horse Capital of the World” and it boasts a great nightlife and shopping scene.

Louisville

Louisville is known for its bourbon and is home to the famous horse race, the Kentucky Derby.

Conclusion

Mount Sterling offers a unique combination of history, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and local culture. With a plethora of things to do and see, this small town is a must-visit destination in the Bluegrass Region of Kentucky.
