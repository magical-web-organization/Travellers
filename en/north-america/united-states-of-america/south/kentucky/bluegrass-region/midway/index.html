Your Ultimate Guide to Midway, Kentucky

Located in the heart of the Bluegrass Region in South Kentucky, Midway is a picturesque town that boasts rich history, natural beauty, and exciting activities. With so much to offer, it’s no wonder that Midway is becoming a popular tourist destination. In this guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Midway.

List of Places to Visit in Midway

Here are some of the best places to visit in Midway:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Midway Museum and Gallery: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Midway. It has exhibits that showcase the town’s railroad heritage, and its influence on the Kentucky horse industry.
  • Wallace Station: This historic building was once a train station and now serves as a restaurant and country store. It’s a must-visit spot for history buffs and foodies alike.
  • Weisenberger Mill: Established in 1865, this water-powered mill is one of the oldest mills that is still in operation today. It produces a variety of flours and cornmeals that you can purchase at the mill.

Natural Attractions of Midway: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sally Brown Nature Preserve: This preserve is a popular spot for hiking, birdwatching, and observing wildlife. It spans over 200 acres and has several trails that are suitable for different skill levels.
  • Kentucky Horse Park: If you’re a horse-lover, you can’t miss the Kentucky Horse Park. It’s a working horse farm and a world-famous equestrian facility that has hosted several international competitions.
  • Daniel Boone National Forest: This forest is located just outside Midway and is a great spot for camping, fishing, hiking, and exploring nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Midway

Midway is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Legacy Trail: This 12-mile trail that runs from Lexington to Midway is excellent for hiking, biking, and jogging.
  • Town Branch Trail: This trail is part of the Lexington trail system and runs through downtown Lexington and into the countryside. It’s a lovely trail for biking, hiking, and walking.
  • Kentucky Horse Park Trail: This trail is a 7.5-mile loop around the Kentucky Horse Park. It’s a scenic trail that is perfect for horseback riding, hiking, and biking.

Best Things to Do in Midway: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Midway:

  • Bourbon trail tours: Midway is located near several world-famous bourbon distilleries, including Woodford Reserve and Buffalo Trace. You can take part in guided tours and learn about the history of bourbon-making in Kentucky.
  • Horseback Riding: Midway offers several horseback riding trails and tours that allow you to explore the beautiful countryside.
  • Tasting tours: You can take part in wine, cheese, and chocolate tasting tours that are available at various locations in Midway, such as the Equus Run Vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Midway

Here’s a sample 7-day itinerary for Midway:

Day 1 Visit the Midway Museum and Gallery, Wallace Station, and Weisenberger Mill
Day 2 Explore the Sally Brown Nature Preserve and enjoy outdoor activities
Day 3 Take a tour of the Kentucky Horse Park and enjoy horseback riding
Day 4 Go on a Bourbon trail tour and visit Woodford Reserve and Buffalo Trace
Day 5 Explore the Daniel Boone National Forest and go camping
Day 6 Take part in a wine, cheese, and chocolate tasting tour and enjoy local cuisine
Day 7 Visit nearby attractions, such as the Lexington Opera House or the Mary Todd Lincoln House

Food and Drink Scene of Midway

Midway has a great food and drink scene that offers something for every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgoo: This is a traditional Kentucky stew made with different meats, vegetables, and spices. It’s a must-try dish when in Kentucky.
  • Hot Brown: This is a sandwich made with toast, turkey, bacon, and Mornay sauce. It was invented in Louisville and has become a Kentucky staple.
  • Kentucky Fried Chicken: This famous fast-food chain originated in Kentucky and is known for its signature fried chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: Kentucky is synonymous with bourbon, and Midway is conveniently located near several famous bourbon distilleries. You can take part in guided tours and tastings to learn more about this iconic drink.
  • Ale-8-One: This is a ginger-and-citrus-flavored soft drink that was invented in Kentucky and is popular in the Bluegrass Region.
  • Mint Julep: This is a bourbon-based cocktail that is traditionally served at the Kentucky Derby, but it’s also available at various local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Midway: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Midway has several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that offer comfortable stays. Here are some options:

  • The Brown-Forman Room: Located in the historic Old Town District, this bed-and-breakfast offers luxury accommodations and is close to several attractions.
  • Midway Hospitality Suite: This private studio suite is located in the heart of Midway and is walking distance to various dining and shopping options.
  • Elkhorn Campground: Located in Frankfort, just a 20-minute drive from Midway, this campground offers RV and tent sites, cabins, and several outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Midway: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Midway has several unique local products that you can buy as souvenirs or gifts. Here are some options:

  • Bourbon: Kentucky bourbon is a popular souvenir that you can buy at various liquor stores and gift shops in Midway and the surrounding area.
  • Horse Decor: Kentucky is famous for its horses, and you can find several horse-themed decor items, such as art, figurines, and mementos.
  • Bourbon Barrel Products: You can buy various products made from bourbon barrels, such as furnishings, decor, and even food items.

Local Culture and Customs of Midway

Kentucky is known for its hospitality, and Midway is no exception. The town has a charming small-town atmosphere, and locals are friendly and welcoming. When visiting Midway, it’s essential to respect local culture and customs. Here are some tips:

  • Dress Code: Kentucky is a relatively conservative state, and it’s best to dress modestly when visiting Midway and other small towns in the area.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in restaurants, bars, and other service industries. A 15-20% tip is standard.
  • Southern Hospitality: Be prepared for friendly greetings and hospitable locals who may strike up a conversation with you.

Best Time to Visit Midway

The best time to visit Midway is during the spring and fall when the temperature is mild and the foliage is beautiful. However, Midway has something to offer year-round. Summers are warm, and winters are cold, but snow is sporadic.

Do’s and Don’ts of Midway

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Midway:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Be respectful of local culture and traditions, such as dressing modestly and being courteous to locals.
  • Explore Nature: Midway is home to several natural attractions, such as the Sally Brown Nature Preserve and the Daniel Boone National Forest. Take advantage of these outdoor activities.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Kentucky has a unique and delicious cuisine that is worth trying during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Drink and Drive: Kentucky has strict DUI laws, and it’s not worth taking the risk. Use taxis, ride-share services, or public transportation.
  • Be Rude: Southerners are known for their hospitality, so it’s essential to be courteous and polite during your visit.
  • Litter: Midway is located in a natural setting, and littering is not only disrespectful but harmful to the environment.

Safety and Health

Midway is a relatively safe town, but, as always, it’s essential to take precautions to stay safe and healthy during your trip.

Safety in Midway

  • Crime: Midway has a low crime rate, but it’s still essential to take necessary precautions, such as locking doors and windows and avoiding unsafe areas at night.
  • Traffic Safety: Kentucky has strict traffic laws, and it’s essential to obey speed limits and traffic signs.

Health in Midway

  • Medical Services: Midway has several medical clinics and pharmacies for basic healthcare needs. For emergencies, the nearest hospital is Frankfort Regional Medical Center.

Getting to Midway: Transportation Options and Logistics

Midway is located just off I-64, and there are several transportation options for reaching the town.

Airports Near Midway

The nearest airport to Midway is the Blue Grass Airport in Lexington, which is 16 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Midway

The nearest bus stations to Midway are Greyhound Stations in Lexington and Frankfort.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: There are several car rental options available at the Blue Grass Airport and in downtown Lexington.
  • Taxis and Ride-Share Services: Taxis and ride-share services are available in Midway and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Midway

The best way to get around Midway is by car or bicycle. Several car rental companies are available in Lexington and the surrounding area, and there are several bike rental options available in Midway.

Where to Go Next from Midway: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Kentucky, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Lexington: Just a 20-minute drive from Midway, Lexington is a vibrant city that offers several cultural and entertainment options.
  • Louisville: Louisville is about 70 miles from Midway and is famous for its bourbon, food, art, and music scene.
  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Located about 150 miles from Midway, Mammoth Cave National Park is home to the world’s longest cave system and offers several outdoor activities, such as hiking, camping, and wildlife observation.

Conclusion

Midway, Kentucky, is a charming town that has something for everyone. From its rich history and natural beauty to its outdoor adventures and dining scene, Midway is a perfect destination for a memorable trip. Use this guide to plan your visit and create lasting memories.
