Introduction to Frankfort

Frankfort is a charming city in the heart of Kentucky’s Bluegrass Region, known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. Established in the late 1700s, Frankfort played a pivotal role in shaping Kentucky’s culture and economy. Today, the city offers a unique blend of old and new, with well-preserved historic sites and modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Frankfort

When it comes to attractions, Frankfort has a lot to offer. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor adventures, you’ll find plenty of options here. Here’s a list of some of the top places to visit in Frankfort:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kentucky State Capitol: The Kentucky State Capitol is a stunning architectural masterpiece and a must-see destination when in Frankfort. Visitors can enjoy free guided tours of the building and its grounds.

  • Kentucky Historical Society: The Kentucky Historical Society is dedicated to preserving and sharing the state’s rich history. It showcases a diverse collection of exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.

  • Old State Capitol: The Old State Capitol is a historic building that served as the seat of Kentucky’s government from 1830 to 1910. Today, it houses a museum with exhibits on the state’s political history and civil rights movement.

Natural Attractions of Frankfort: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cove Spring Park: Cove Spring Park is a 240-acre park that offers an array of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and picnicking. Visitors can also explore the park’s historic structures and gardens.

  • Salato Wildlife Education Center: The Salato Wildlife Education Center is a 100-acre wildlife center that features exhibits of Kentucky’s native wildlife, including elk, bison, and eagles. Visitors can also hike nature trails and fish in the stocked lake.

  • Kentucky River: The Kentucky River is a scenic waterway that flows through Frankfort, offering kayaking, fishing, and other water sports. Visitors can also take a relaxing boat tour while enjoying the stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frankfort

Frankfort is home to several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Capital City Trail: The Capital City Trail is a 14-mile paved trail that runs through Frankfort, linking several parks and historic sites.

  • Buckley Wildlife Sanctuary: Buckley Wildlife Sanctuary is a 374-acre nature reserve that offers hiking trails, birdwatching, and fishing.

  • Peaks Mill Road: Peaks Mill Road is a scenic route that offers spectacular views of the Kentucky River and surrounding hills, ideal for cycling.

Best Things to Do in Frankfort: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling on your own or with a group, Frankfort has plenty of activities and tours to suit everyone’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Frankfort:

  • Bourbon Tours: Kentucky is known as the bourbon capital of the world, and Frankfort is no exception. Visitors can take a tour of some of the state’s top distilleries and learn about the history of bourbon-making.

  • Thoroughbred Tours: Horse racing is a big part of Kentucky’s heritage, and Frankfort is home to several horse farms and racing facilities that offer tours for visitors.

  • Art Walks: Frankfort is home to a vibrant arts scene, with several galleries and studios showcasing the work of local artists. Visitors can take a self-guided art walk to explore the city’s creative side.

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors interested in exploring the best of Frankfort:

  • Explore Downtown Frankfort’s shops, restaurants, and art galleries.

  • Visit the Kentucky Historical Society and learn about the state’s rich history.

  • Explore the Old State Capitol and its collection of exhibits on the state’s political history and civil rights movement.

  • Visit the Salato Wildlife Education Center and see Kentucky’s native wildlife.

  • Explore Cove Spring Park and enjoy hiking, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Explore Downtown Frankfort’s galleries and studios on a self-guided art walk.

Day 6: Kentucky River Boat Tour

  • Enjoy a scenic cycling route along Peaks Mill Road, taking in the stunning views of the Kentucky River and surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Frankfort

Frankfort is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with several restaurants and bars serving up delicious local cuisine and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Browns: A classic Kentucky dish, hot browns feature turkey, cheese, and bacon served on a bed of bread and covered in a rich sauce.

  • Fried Chicken: Kentucky is known for its fried chicken, and Frankfort offers several top-notch spots to try it.

  • Burgoo: Burgoo is a thick stew made with a variety of meats and vegetables, a staple of Kentucky cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon: Kentucky is the home of bourbon, and Frankfort offers several distilleries where visitors can sample some of the best.

  • Ale-8-One: Ale-8-One is a locally-produced soda that has been a Kentucky favorite for over 90 years.

  • Wine: Frankfort is home to several wineries that produce some of the best Kentucky wines.

Where to Stay in Frankfort: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Frankfort offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some top picks:

  • Capital Plaza Hotel: The Capital Plaza Hotel is a historic hotel with a prime location in Downtown Frankfort, offering cozy rooms and great views.

  • Bluegrass Inn: The Bluegrass Inn is a cozy, budget-friendly option with free breakfast, Wi-Fi, and parking.

  • The Meeting House: The Meeting House is a charming bed and breakfast with spacious rooms and a lovely garden.

What to Buy in Frankfort: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home from Frankfort? Here are some top picks:

  • Bourbon: Kentucky bourbon is a popular local product, and visitors can buy bottles to take home or as gifts.

  • Horse-related Gifts: Horse racing is an integral part of Kentucky’s culture, and visitors can find all kinds of horse-related souvenirs and gifts in Frankfort.

  • Local Art: Frankfort is home to many talented artists, and visitors can find unique pieces of art to bring home as a souvenir of their trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Frankfort

Frankfort is a friendly and welcoming city, with a diverse mix of people and cultures. Visitors should take note of the following customs and cultural norms:

  • Southern Hospitality: Frankfort is part of the South, known for its friendly and hospitable people.

  • Respect for History and Tradition: Frankfort has a rich history, and visitors are expected to show respect for historic sites and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Frankfort

The best time to visit Frankfort is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is beautiful. However, Frankfort is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy its attractions and activities throughout the year.

Getting to Frankfort: Transportation Options and Logistics

Frankfort is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some of the top transportation options:

Airports Near Frankfort

  • Louisville International Airport: This airport is located 58 miles from Frankfort.

  • Blue Grass Airport: This airport is located 20 miles from Frankfort.

Bus Stations Near Frankfort

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This station is located in Lexington, 20 miles from Frankfort.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in Downtown Frankfort.

  • Taxi and Ride Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in Frankfort and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Frankfort

Frankfort is a walkable city, and most attractions are within easy walking distance. Visitors can also use taxis, rental cars, and ride-sharing services to get around.

Where to Go Next from Frankfort: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Frankfort is located in the heart of Kentucky, making it a great base for exploring the state’s other attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out:

  • Lexington: Lexington is known as the horse capital of the world and is just a 30-minute drive from Frankfort.

  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Mammoth Cave National Park is a two-hour drive from Frankfort, offering spectacular natural beauty and underground cave tours.

  • Churchill Downs: Home of the Kentucky Derby, Churchill Downs is just an hour’s drive from Frankfort.

Conclusion

Frankfort is a hidden gem in the heart of Kentucky, offering a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring historic landmarks, enjoying outdoor adventures, or savoring local cuisine and drinks, Frankfort is a destination worth adding to your travel list.
