Berea, located in the Bluegrass Region of Kentucky, is a hidden gem for travelers looking to experience the beauty of the Southern United States. This vibrant and charming town offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and small-town charm. From its deep cultural roots to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Berea has something for everyone. This travel guide will provide you with all the essential information you need to make the most of your trip to Berea.

Introduction to Berea

Founded in 1855, Berea is a small town in Kentucky that holds a rich history and deep cultural heritage. The town is home to Berea College, a liberal arts college that has been offering free education to students from Appalachia for over 150 years. The town’s commitment to education, the arts, and social justice can still be seen today in the many cultural and historical attractions that dot its landscape. Berea is also known for its thriving artisan community, which produces some of the finest crafts in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Berea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Berea College - Explore the stunning campus that includes the Boone Tavern Hotel, the Log House Craft Gallery, the Berea College Crafts, and the Student Crafts.
  • Kentucky Artisan Center - A state-of-the-art center featuring regional artisan crafts, an art gallery, and events for visitors to enjoy.
  • Old Town Artisan Village - A beautiful place that showcases the creative works of local artists and craftsmen.
  • Indian Fort Theatre and Museum - Take a journey through history and be wowed by the museum’s collection of artifacts, photos, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Berea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pinnacles Hiking Trails - Accessible all year round, Pinnacles Park is a hike that offers stunning views of the Kentucky terrain.
  • The Berea City Parks - A perfect place to relax or have a picnic, this park system offers a lot of outdoor recreation options.
  • Boone Trace Trail - This trail is a great representation of the Appalachian history and takes you on a journey to the past.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berea

  • The Pinnacles
  • Anglin Falls
  • Jackson and Coyle Falls
  • Lily Mountain Trail
  • The Owsley Fork
  • The Boone Trace Trail
  • The Kentucky River Trail

Best Things to Do in Berea: Activities and Tours

  • Craft Workshops - Make your own souvenirs from workshops such as woodworking, glass-blowing, weaving, jewelry-making, or painting.
  • Farmers Market - Experience Berea’s local food scene by visiting the Farmers’ Market.
  • Berea’s Historic Walking Tour - Take a tour of Berea’s most historic sites, including the Old Town Artisan village.
  • Celebrate Berea Festival - A festival that celebrates Berea’s rich history, music, arts, and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Berea

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Browns - A local specialty dish, similar to an open-faced sandwich with turkey and bacon, smothered in a cheese sauce.
  • Burgoo - A Kentucky-staple stew with a variety of meats, veggies, and secret ingredients.
  • Bourbon Balls - A candy made with bourbon, chocolate and pecans.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon - Kentucky’s signature drink.
  • Barrel-Ay Ciderworks - Berea’s first craft cidery.

Where to Stay in Berea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Boone Tavern Hotel - An elegant hotel that has been the center of Berea’s social and political life for over a century.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites - Modern accommodations close to Berea College and local attractions.
  • Silver Cliff Inn Bed and Breakfast - A charming and cozy retreat with home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in Berea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crafts - handcrafted jewelry, glassware, pottery, baskets, woodwork & more.
  • Bourbon - Locally-produced bourbon from one of the Kentucky distilleries.
  • Moonshine and Wines - A unique opportunity to taste local bourbon, moonshine, and wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Berea

Berea is a town that highly values its culture and heritage, and as such, visitors should respect its local customs and traditions. Berea is known for being a welcoming town, so visitors will usually find themselves easily accepted and welcomed into the community.

Best Time to Visit Berea

The best times to visit Berea are in the fall and spring when the weather is pleasant, and the fall foliage is at its peak. However, summer is also a great time to visit if you want to enjoy the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Berea

Do:

  • Respect the history and culture of the town.
  • Support local business by buying locally-grown produce and artisan goods.
  • Participate in local events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Discard litter or leave your trash behind.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Disrespect the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Berea

Berea is a safe town, but caution should always be taken when traveling. Visitors should avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night, but this is a general safety recommendation for all destinations.

Health in Berea

Visitors should make sure to pack a good supply of insect repellent to guard against ticks and mosquito bites. Ensure to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, as temperatures can go up in the summer months.

Getting to Berea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Berea

  • Blue Grass Airport in Lexington, Kentucky, is the closest airport to Berea - located about an hour away.

Bus Stations Near Berea

  • Greyhound Bus Station is available in Richmond, Kentucky, which is just a few miles north of Berea.

Other Options

  • The drive by car from Lexington is roughly 50 minutes.
  • Berea can also be reached by train through the Amtrak station in Richmond, Kentucky.

How to Get Around in Berea

Berea is a small town that can easily be explored by walking or biking. For places that are a few miles away, a car can be rented, or there’s UBER/Lyft service.

Where to Go Next from Berea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lexington - home to the Kentucky Horse Park, bourbon distilleries, and horse races.
  • Daniel Boone National Forest - popular among hikers and nature enthusiasts.
  • Louisville - famous for Bourbon, Muhammad Ali, and the Kentucky Derby.
