Introduction to Jacksonville

Jacksonville is a small city located in North Carolina’s Coastal Plain region. It is known for its rich history, outdoor activities, and beautiful natural attractions. Whether you’re exploring the city or venturing out into the surrounding areas, Jacksonville has plenty to offer for travelers of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Jacksonville

Here are some of the must-see attractions and destinations in Jacksonville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lejeune Memorial Gardens: A national landmark honoring Marine Corps history and fallen soldiers.
  • The Montford Point Marine Museum: A museum dedicated to the first African American Marines.
  • Lynwood Park Zoo: A small, family-friendly zoo with free admission.

Natural Attractions of Jacksonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Onslow Pines Park: A large park that offers picnic areas, fishing, and hiking trails.
  • Cedar Point Tideland Trail: A scenic trail that winds through a salt marsh and offers beautiful views of wildlife and plant life.
  • Hammocks Beach State Park: A beautiful beach and park located on Bear Island. The park also offers hiking trails, kayaking, and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jacksonville

If hiking or biking is your thing, Jacksonville offers some great trails and routes:

  • Rails to Trails: A 5-mile paved trail that starts in Jacksonville and offers beautiful views of the area.
  • Jacksonville-Camp Lejeune Rail Trail: A 16-mile trail that offers a great challenge for experienced hikers and bikers.
  • Jones Island Loop Trail: A 3-mile trail that offers a smooth and easy hike with stunning views of the area.

Best Things to Do in Jacksonville: Activities and Tours

From water activities to cultural experiences, here are some of the best things to do in Jacksonville:

  • Go Fishing: Jacksonville has some of the best fishing spots in North Carolina. Try your luck at New River, Stones Creek, or Sneads Ferry.
  • Experience Marine Corps History: Take a tour of the Marine Corps base and learn about the rich history of the area.
  • Relax on the Beach: Spend a day at one of the many beautiful beaches in the area, including Topsail Beach and Surf City Beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jacksonville

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring Jacksonville and the surrounding areas:

Day Activity
1 Visit Lejeune Memorial Gardens and The Montford Point Marine Museum.
2 Go fishing at Stones Creek.
3 Explore Hammocks Beach State Park and go kayaking.
4 Take a tour of the Marine Corps base.
5 Spend a day at the beach.
6 Hike the Jones Island Loop Trail.
7 Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Jacksonville

No trip to Jacksonville is complete without trying some of the delicious local cuisine and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • North Carolina BBQ: Pulled pork with a tangy vinegar-based sauce is a local favorite.
  • Shrimp and Grits: A classic Southern dish that pairs perfectly with fresh seafood.
  • Fried Chicken: A staple of Southern cuisine, you can’t go wrong with this crispy classic.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Jacksonville has a growing craft beer scene, with several local breweries offering unique and tasty brews.
  • Sweet Tea: A classic Southern drink, sweet tea is refreshing and perfect for sipping on a hot day.
  • Locally Roasted Coffee: Jacksonville has several local coffee shops that roast their own beans, offering a delicious and unique coffee experience.

Where to Stay in Jacksonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly options to luxurious accommodations, here are some of the top places to stay when visiting Jacksonville:

  • Courtyard by Marriott Jacksonville: A centrally located hotel that offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • Home2 Suites by Hilton Jacksonville: A budget-friendly option that offers modern rooms and a convenient location.
  • Airbnb: If you’re looking for a more unique experience, there are several great Airbnb options in Jacksonville and the surrounding areas.

What to Buy in Jacksonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or unique products to bring home, here are some of the best options:

  • Marine Corps Merchandise: The Marine Corps is an important part of the Jacksonville community, and there are several shops that offer Marine Corps merchandise and souvenirs.
  • Local Artwork and Crafts: Jacksonville has a thriving arts scene, with several local artists and artisans selling unique and beautiful creations.
  • Locally Made Food Products: From jams and jellies to hot sauces and seasonings, there are several local food products that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Jacksonville

Jacksonville is a military town, so it’s important to be respectful of the military community and its traditions. The city also has a strong Southern culture, with a love of food, music, and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Jacksonville

The best time to visit Jacksonville is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summer can be crowded and hot, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jacksonville

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Jacksonville:

Do:

  • Respect the Marine Corps and the military community.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.
  • Explore the natural attractions and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Ignore Marine Corps customs and traditions.
  • Disrespect the local culture and Southern hospitality.
  • Forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jacksonville

Jacksonville is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially if you’re exploring the city at night. Be sure to stay in well-lit areas and avoid walking alone.

Health in Jacksonville

Make sure to stay hydrated when exploring the outdoors, and be aware of ticks and other pests that can carry diseases. The sun can be intense, so be sure to wear sunscreen and protect yourself from the heat.

Getting to Jacksonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jacksonville

  • Albert J. Ellis Airport (OAJ): Located about 20 minutes from downtown Jacksonville, OAJ offers flights to several major cities, including Charlotte and Atlanta.

Bus Stations Near Jacksonville

  • Amtrak Station: Located in nearby New Bern, the Amtrak Station offers train service to several major cities, including New York, Philadelphia, and Washington, D.C.

Other Options

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in nearby Wilmington, the Greyhound Bus Station offers bus service to several major cities, including Raleigh, Charlotte, and Atlanta.

How to Get Around in Jacksonville

The best way to get around Jacksonville is by car. While the city does have a limited bus system, a car will give you more flexibility when exploring the area.

Where to Go Next from Jacksonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of North Carolina and the surrounding areas, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Wilmington: A beautiful historic city located on the coast, Wilmington offers great food, shopping, and beaches.
  • Outer Banks: Just a few hours drive from Jacksonville, the Outer Banks offer some of the most beautiful beaches on the East Coast.
  • Raleigh: The capital of North Carolina, Raleigh offers great food, culture, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or just relaxing on the beach, Jacksonville has something for everyone. With its beautiful natural attractions, unique local cuisine, and thriving arts scene, this North Carolina gem is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
