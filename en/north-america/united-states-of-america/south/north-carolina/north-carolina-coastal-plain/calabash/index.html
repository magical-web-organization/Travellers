Introduction to Calabash

Located in the North Carolina Coastal Plain, Calabash is a small town known for its unique seafood cuisine and laid-back lifestyle. The town got its name from the calabash gourd, which was used to make drinking cups in the past. With a population of just over 2,000, Calabash is a peaceful town with plenty to offer visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Calabash

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing on the beach, Calabash has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Calabash:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Calabash Fire Department Museum - This museum showcases the history of the Calabash Fire Department and its contributions to the community.
  • Museum of Coastal Carolina - Located in nearby Ocean Isle Beach, this museum features exhibits on the region’s natural and cultural history.
  • Ingram Planetarium - Also located in Ocean Isle Beach, this planetarium offers educational shows and exhibits on astronomy.

Natural Attractions of Calabash: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sunset Beach - This pristine beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing.
  • Calabash River - Rent a kayak or boat and explore the peaceful waters of the Calabash River.
  • Calabash Fishing Fleet - Take a fishing charter and catch some of the freshest seafood in town.
  • Bird Island Reserve - Just a short drive away, this nature reserve is home to a variety of bird species and offers stunning views of the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calabash

Calabash is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Waccamaw Neck Bikeway - This 10-mile trail meanders through scenic woodlands and offers serene views of the Waccamaw River.
  • Ingram Planetarium Trail - This short trail leads to the Ingram Planetarium and offers a taste of the area’s natural beauty.
  • Bird Island Trail - This 1.5-mile trail takes you through the Bird Island Reserve and offers excellent bird-watching opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Calabash: Activities and Tours

  • Calabash Seafood Tour - Take a guided tour of the local seafood restaurants and sample some of the freshest seafood in town.
  • Battleship North Carolina Tour - Located in nearby Wilmington, this historic battleship offers self-guided tours and a glimpse into the state’s military history.
  • Wine Tasting Tour - Visit the local wineries and taste some of the best native wines the area has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calabash

Day 1 - Arrive in Calabash and spend the day exploring Sunset Beach.

Day 2 - Take a fishing charter and catch some of the freshest seafood in town.

Day 3 - Visit the Museum of Coastal Carolina and learn about the history of the region.

Day 4 - Spend the day hiking or biking on one of the area’s scenic trails.

Day 5 - Take a wine tasting tour and sample some of the best local wines.

Day 6 - Explore the nearby town of Wilmington and tour the Battleship North Carolina.

Day 7 - Relax on the beach and soak up the laid-back atmosphere of Calabash.

Food and Drink Scene of Calabash

Calabash is known for its unique seafood cuisine. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calabash Seafood Platter - This platter includes a variety of seafood, including shrimp, clams, oysters, and fish, all lightly battered and fried.
  • Crab Cakes - These delicious cakes are made with fresh crab meat and are a local specialty.
  • Hushpuppies - Deep-fried balls of cornmeal, these are the perfect accompaniment to any seafood dish in Calabash.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A Southern staple, sweet tea is a refreshing beverage perfect for a hot day.
  • Muscadine Wine - Made from native muscadine grapes, this wine is unique to the region and is a must-try for wine lovers.
  • Craft Beer - Calabash has several local breweries that offer a variety of craft beers to choose from.

Where to Stay in Calabash: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calabash has several options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly to upscale:

  • The Sunset Inn - This charming inn is located just a short walk from Sunset Beach and offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Comfort Inn - This budget-friendly option offers clean and comfortable rooms and is located near several local attractions.
  • Brunswick Plantation and Golf Resort - This upscale resort features luxurious rooms and suites, as well as a golf course and spa.

What to Buy in Calabash: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products that are unique to Calabash and make perfect gifts for loved ones or mementos of your trip:

  • Calabash Gourd - A symbol of the town’s history, a calabash gourd makes for a unique and meaningful souvenir.
  • Handmade Pottery - Several local artists create beautiful pottery that reflects the natural beauty of the area.
  • Fresh Seafood - Take some of the fresh seafood home with you and recreate the unique Calabash flavor in your own kitchen.

Local Culture and Customs of Calabash

Calabash has a laid-back and friendly culture. Visitors should be aware of the town’s reverence for its history and its love of fresh seafood.

Best Time to Visit Calabash

The best time to visit Calabash is in the mild spring or fall months, when temperatures are comfortable and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and humid, but offer the best beach weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calabash

Here are some helpful tips to ensure a great trip to Calabash:

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood cuisine.
  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities in the area.
  • Do take time to explore the nearby towns and attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the town’s nature reserves.
  • Don’t forget to tip your tour guide or server.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calabash

Calabash is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should take usual precautions such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Calabash

Visitors should be aware of the hot and humid weather in the summer months and take precautions such as drinking plenty of water and wearing sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Calabash: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calabash

The closest airport to Calabash is Myrtle Beach International Airport, which is roughly 35 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Calabash

The closest bus station to Calabash is in Wilmington, which is about a 45-minute drive away.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or shuttle from the airport to get to Calabash.

How to Get Around in Calabash

The best way to get around Calabash is by car. The town is relatively small and easy to navigate, and there are several car rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Calabash: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calabash is conveniently located near several other popular destinations in the South:

  • Myrtle Beach, SC - Just a short drive away, Myrtle Beach offers a wide variety of attractions, including beaches, amusement parks, and shopping.
  • Wilmington, NC - This historic town is located about an hour away and offers a glimpse into the region’s colonial past.
  • Charleston, SC - Just a few hours away, Charleston is known for its beautiful architecture, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene.

Conclusion

A trip to Calabash is the perfect way to relax and enjoy the natural beauty and unique culture of the South. From fresh seafood to scenic hikes, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Planning your trip ahead of time with this comprehensive travel guide will help ensure a memorable and enjoyable experience.
