Introduction to Goldsboro

Located in the Coastal Plain region of North Carolina, Goldsboro is a charming city that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. Known for its history, this city is home to several museums and historical sites that will take you back in time. Whether you’re a foodie, an outdoor enthusiast, or a history buff, Goldsboro has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Goldsboro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wayne County Museum

Founded in 1949, the Wayne County Museum is dedicated to preserving the history and heritage of Goldsboro and Wayne County. Housed in a restored 1927 courthouse, this museum features exhibits on military history, Native American artifacts, and more.

Goldsboro Bridge Battlefield

This battlefield was the site of a Civil War battle in 1862. Today, you can visit this historic site and see the reconstructed earthen fortifications. You can also attend living history events and reenactments.

W.A. Foster Center

The W.A. Foster Center is a historic African American school that has been converted into a cultural center. It features exhibits on the history of African Americans in Goldsboro and Wayne County.

Natural Attractions of Goldsboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cliffs of the Neuse State Park

This state park is located just southeast of Goldsboro and offers numerous outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and boating. You can also admire the breathtaking views of the cliffs and the Neuse River.

Seymour Johnson Air Force Base

Seymour Johnson Air Force Base is home to the 4th Fighter Wing, and it offers a variety of attractions and activities for visitors, including aircraft displays, an aviation museum, and a golf course.

Busco Beach and ATV Park

This park is a popular destination for off-roading enthusiasts. You can ride your ATV, dirt bike, or UTV on over 2,000 acres of trails and terrain, or take a dip in one of the park’s swimming areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goldsboro

Cliffs of the Neuse State Park

The park has over 10 miles of hiking trails that offer scenic views of the river and the cliffs. You can also bring your mountain bike and ride the multi-use trails.

Wayne County Trails

Wayne County has several trails that are perfect for hiking and biking. The Eastern Carolina Greenway Trail is a 3.8-mile trail that connects downtown Goldsboro to Berkeley Boulevard. The Mountains to Sea Trail is also accessible from Goldsboro and offers over 1,000 miles of hiking trails across North Carolina.

Best Things to Do in Goldsboro: Activities and Tours

Ghosts of Goldsboro Tour

This spooky tour takes you on a journey through the historic downtown area to explore the city’s haunted history.

Goldsboro Brew Works Tour

Visit the Goldsboro Brew Works to see how their craft beer is made, learn about the history of beer brewing in Goldsboro, and enjoy a tasting.

Wine Tasting at Hinnant Family Vineyards

Located just outside of Goldsboro, Hinnant Family Vineyards offers wine tastings and tours of their facility, which produces a wide variety of wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Goldsboro

Must-Try Dishes

Barbecue

North Carolina is known for its barbecue, and Goldsboro is no exception. Some popular barbecue spots include Wilber’s Barbecue, Grady’s Barbecue, and B’s Barbecue.

Southern Classics

Goldsboro has plenty of restaurants that serve classic Southern dishes like fried chicken, biscuits and gravy, and collard greens. Check out The Country Squire or Andy’s for some good old-fashioned Southern cooking.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

Goldsboro Brew Works is a must-visit for craft beer lovers. They offer a variety of brews that are made on-site, including IPAs, pilsners, and stouts.

Wine

Hinnant Family Vineyards is a local winery that produces a variety of wines, including Chardonnay, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Muscadine.

Where to Stay in Goldsboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

Hampton Inn Goldsboro

This hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and an outdoor pool.

Best Western Plus Goldsboro

This hotel features spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant.

Hostels

Unfortunately, there are no hostels in Goldsboro.

Guesthouses

Grantham House

This bed and breakfast is located in a historic home in nearby Grantham. It features four guest rooms and serves a homemade breakfast.

Magic Cottages at Goldsboro

These charming cottages are located on a 44-acre farm. They offer a variety of amenities, including a hot tub and a fire pit.

What to Buy in Goldsboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wayne County Honey

Pick up a jar of local honey from Wayne County Beekeepers Association. It’s a great souvenir that will remind you of your trip to Goldsboro every time you enjoy it with your morning toast.

Pickles

Goldsboro is known for its pickles, and the Mt. Olive Pickle Company is located just a few miles from downtown. They offer a variety of pickle products that can be a great addition to your pantry.

Local Culture and Customs of Goldsboro

Goldsboro is a friendly and welcoming community. Be sure to greet people with a smile and a “hello.”

Best Time to Visit Goldsboro

The best time to visit Goldsboro is in the fall when the weather is mild and the autumn leaves are at their peak. Spring is also a great time to visit when the flowers are blooming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goldsboro

Do:

  • Do visit the Cliffs of the Neuse State Park and Seymour Johnson Air Force Base.
  • Do try the local barbecue and pickles.
  • Do be friendly and say “hello” to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t disturb the wildlife.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goldsboro

Goldsboro is a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to practice common sense safety precautions, such as locking your car and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Goldsboro

There are several hospitals and healthcare facilities in Goldsboro, including Wayne UNC Health Care and Carolina Family Health Centers. Be sure to bring any necessary medications with you on your trip.

Getting to Goldsboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Goldsboro

The closest airport to Goldsboro is Raleigh-Durham International Airport, which is located about 85 miles away. You can rent a car or take a shuttle bus for transportation to Goldsboro.

Bus Stations Near Goldsboro

Greyhound buses stop in nearby Kinston, which is about 20 miles from Goldsboro. You can take a taxi or rent a car to get to Goldsboro.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Goldsboro is easily accessible from several major highways, including I-95 and US-70.

How to Get Around in Goldsboro

The best way to get around Goldsboro is by car. There are several car rental options available, or you can use a ride-sharing service like Uber.

Where to Go Next from Goldsboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wilmington

Wilmington is a coastal city located about 120 miles southeast of Goldsboro. It offers a variety of attractions, including the Battleship North Carolina and the Riverwalk.

Raleigh

Raleigh, the capital city of North Carolina, is located about 60 miles northwest of Goldsboro and has plenty of attractions, including the North Carolina State Capitol and the North Carolina Museum of Art.

Conclusion

Goldsboro offers a unique blend of history, outdoor adventures, and Southern charm. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking in state parks, or enjoying some local barbecue, Goldsboro has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming city has to offer.
