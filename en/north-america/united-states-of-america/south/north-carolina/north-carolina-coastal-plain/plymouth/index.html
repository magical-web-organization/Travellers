Introduction to Plymouth

Plymouth is a small town located in North Carolina Coastal Plain, in North Carolina, in the southern part of the United States of America. It is a historic town that was founded in the early 1700s and has rich history and beautiful natural landscapes. Plymouth is a great place to visit for anyone who loves history, enjoys outdoor activities, and wants to experience local culture and cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Plymouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roanoke River Lighthouse and Maritime Museum

Located in downtown Plymouth, this museum is a must-visit for anyone who loves maritime history. The Roanoke River Lighthouse and Maritime Museum is housed in a beautifully restored 1886 lighthouse and features exhibits on the history of the Roanoke River and its importance to the region’s economy.

Port O’ Plymouth Museum

This museum showcases the history of Plymouth and the surrounding region. Featuring exhibits on Native American history, the Civil War, and the town’s industrial past, Port O’ Plymouth Museum is a great place to learn about the area’s rich history.

Natural Attractions of Plymouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pettigrew State Park

Pettigrew State Park is a beautiful natural area located just outside of Plymouth. It features miles of hiking trails, scenic views of the Roanoke River, and opportunities for fishing and boating. Visitors can also explore the park’s historic buildings, including a gristmill and a sawmill.

Roanoke River National Wildlife Refuge

This wildlife refuge is home to a variety of species, including egrets, herons, and otters. Visitors can explore the refuge on hiking trails and can also go fishing and boating in the Roanoke River. If you’re lucky, you may even spot an endangered red-cockaded woodpecker!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plymouth

Riverwalk

The Riverwalk is a beautiful 1.5-mile path that winds along the Roanoke River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It’s a great place to go for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

Pettigrew State Park

Pettigrew State Park offers miles of hiking trails through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the region. Whether you’re looking for a short hike or a longer trek, you’ll find plenty of options at Pettigrew State Park.

Best Things to Do in Plymouth: Activities and Tours

Strolling and Shagging on the Riverwalk

Every summer, Plymouth hosts a series of concerts and events on the Riverwalk. Visitors can enjoy live music and dancing, as well as delicious food and drinks from local vendors.

Ghost Walk

Take a guided tour of Plymouth’s historic district and discover the town’s haunted past. This spooky tour takes you through some of the town’s most historic and haunted buildings and is sure to give you a chill!

Week-Long Itinerary for Plymouth

Food and Drink Scene of Plymouth

Must-Try Dishes

Eastern-Style Barbecue

North Carolina is famous for its barbecue, and Plymouth is no exception. Be sure to try the eastern-style barbecue, which is a local specialty.

Sweet Potato Biscuits

Sweet potatoes are a staple in North Carolina cuisine, and sweet potato biscuits are a must-try when visiting Plymouth.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

North Carolina is becoming known for its craft beer scene, and Plymouth is no exception. Be sure to try some of the local brews while you’re in town.

Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a southern tradition and is a refreshing beverage to enjoy on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Plymouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn on the Roanoke

Located on the banks of the Roanoke River, The Inn on the Roanoke is a charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable accommodations and stunning views.

Comfort Inn

Located in downtown Plymouth, the Comfort Inn offers modern and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Plymouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pottery

North Carolina is known for its pottery, and Plymouth is a great place to find unique handmade pieces.

Sweet Potato Products

Sweet potatoes are grown in abundance in North Carolina, and you can find products made from this delicious vegetable all over Plymouth. Be sure to try some sweet potato jam or sweet potato pie!

Local Culture and Customs of Plymouth

Plymouth is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors should be sure to respect local customs and traditions. Southern hospitality is alive and well in Plymouth, and visitors can expect a warm welcome wherever they go.

Best Time to Visit Plymouth

The best time to visit Plymouth is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the scenery is beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, but there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plymouth

Do:

  • Do try the eastern-style barbecue and sweet potato biscuits.
  • Do take a guided tour of the historic district.
  • Do enjoy the natural beauty of Pettigrew State Park and the Roanoke River National Wildlife Refuge.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be disrespectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t miss the chance to try some local craft beer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plymouth

Plymouth is a safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Be sure to lock your car and keep valuables out of sight. Keep your wallet and phone in a secure pocket when walking in busy areas.

Health in Plymouth

North Carolina is prone to hot and humid weather, so be sure to bring plenty of water and protect yourself from the sun. Be aware of ticks during outdoor activities and check yourself for ticks after spending time outside.

Getting to Plymouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plymouth

The closest airport to Plymouth is Pitt-Greenville Airport, which is located approximately 45 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Plymouth

The nearest bus station is in Greenville, which is approximately 45 miles away.

Other Options

There are many rental car companies available at Pitt-Greenville Airport, and the drive to Plymouth is simple and straightforward.

How to Get Around Plymouth

Plymouth is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available for those who need them.

Where to Go Next from Plymouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Outer Banks

The Outer Banks are a popular destination for beach lovers and offer some of the best beaches on the east coast. Be sure to visit the iconic lighthouses and enjoy some fresh seafood while you’re there.

Greenville

The city of Greenville is just a short drive from Plymouth and offers a variety of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions. Be sure to visit the Greenville Museum of Art and the uptown district for a taste of the local art scene.

Conclusion

Plymouth is a charming and historic town that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or local cuisine, you’re sure to have a great time in Plymouth. Plan your visit today and start exploring all that this beautiful town has to offer!
