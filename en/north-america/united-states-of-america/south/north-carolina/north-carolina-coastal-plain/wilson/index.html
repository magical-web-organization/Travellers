Introduction to Wilson

Located in the heart of North Carolina’s Coastal Plain, Wilson is a stunning city that has something to offer to every type of traveler. Known for its rich cultural heritage, Wilson boasts a thriving food and drink scene, incredible natural landscapes, and a warm southern hospitality that makes visitors feel right at home. Whether you’re looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the big city, explore the great outdoors, or discover the local culture, Wilson is a must-visit destination that should be on every traveler’s radar.

List of Places to Visit in Wilson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Imagination Station - A popular science museum that offers interactive exhibits, hands-on activities, and live demonstrations for visitors of all ages.
  • Oliver Nestus Freeman Roundhouse Museum - A historic railroad station that now houses exhibits focused on African American history and culture.
  • Wilson County Public Library - A stunning classical revival style building that houses a vast collection of books, magazines, and other literary materials.

Natural Attractions of Wilson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Wilson - A beautiful man-made lake surrounded by lush green forests and rolling hills. Perfect for fishing, boating, and relaxing on the beach.
  • Sugar Hill Pines Golf Course - A popular destination for golf enthusiasts due to its breathtaking landscapes and challenging courses.
  • Whirligig Park and Museum - A one-of-a-kind outdoor sculpture park that features colorful and whimsical whirligigs created by acclaimed local artist Vollis Simpson.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilson

  • Greenway Trail System - A network of paved trails that wind through the city’s parks and natural areas. Great for hiking, biking, and jogging.
  • Wilson Botanical Gardens - A stunning collection of gardens and nature preserves that offers a range of hiking trails for visitors to explore.

Best Things to Do in Wilson: Activities and Tours

  • Brewmasters Walking Tour - A guided tour that takes you through the city’s historic downtown district and introduces you to some of the best craft breweries and taprooms in Wilson.
  • Southern Charm Bike Tour - A leisurely cycling tour that explores the city’s charming neighborhoods, historic landmarks, and hidden gems.
  • Mall at Turtle Creek - A premier shopping destination that offers a range of high-end retailers, boutiques, and dining options.

Food and Drink Scene of Wilson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Eastern North Carolina-style Barbecue - A local delicacy featuring juicy, slow-cooked pulled pork that’s been doused in a tangy vinegar-based sauce.
  • Boiled Peanuts - A classic southern snack that’s made by boiling raw peanuts until soft and tender.
  • Pimento Cheese - A creamy spread made from cheddar cheese and diced pimento peppers that’s great on crackers, sandwiches, and burgers.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - Wilson is home to several award-winning breweries and taprooms, serving up a range of unique and flavorful craft beers.
  • Sweet Tea - A southern staple, sweet tea is a refreshing drink made by brewing black tea and adding sugar.
  • Cherry Bounce - A traditional North Carolina drink made by steeping sour cherries in moonshine or whiskey.

Where to Stay in Wilson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson - A family-friendly hotel known for its spacious rooms, free breakfast, and heated indoor pool.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Wilson - A popular choice for business travelers, with spacious suites, modern amenities, and conference facilities.
  • The Wilson House - A charming guesthouse located in the heart of downtown Wilson, with cozy rooms, friendly staff, and a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Wilson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sweet Potato Biscuits - A classic southern treat made with sweet potatoes, flour, and butter.
  • Handmade Pottery - A range of unique and beautifully crafted pottery items that make for great souvenirs.
  • Wilson’s Own Hot Sauce - A spicy and flavorful sauce that’s made with fresh peppers and locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilson

Wilson is a welcoming city with a rich history and unique cultural heritage. Southern hospitality is a way of life here, and locals are known for their friendly and outgoing demeanor. When visiting Wilson, it’s important to be respectful of the local customs and traditions, and to embrace the laid-back and easy-going lifestyle that makes this city so special.

Best Time to Visit Wilson

The best time to visit Wilson is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy. However, regardless of the time of year, there’s always something fun and exciting happening in Wilson, so it’s a great destination to visit year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilson

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and cuisine.
  • Explore the city’s many parks and outdoor attractions.
  • Take in a live performance at the historic Edna Boykin Cultural Center.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a local - they’re always happy to share their knowledge and experiences with visitors.
  • Don’t forget to pack plenty of sunscreen and bug spray if you plan to spend time outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wilson

Wilson is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate and a strong sense of community. However, as with any destination, visitors should remain vigilant and take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and securing your valuables.

Health in Wilson

There are several medical centers and hospitals located in Wilson, so visitors can rest assured that they will be able to access high-quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Wilson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wilson

The nearest airport to Wilson is the Raleigh-Durham International Airport (RDU), located approximately one hour away by car. Other nearby airports include the Pitt-Greenville Airport (PGV) and the Coastal Carolina Regional Airport (EWN).

Bus Stations Near Wilson

Wilson is well-connected by bus, with several bus companies offering regular service to and from the city. The Greyhound Bus Terminal is located in downtown Wilson.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Wilson by car, with easy access to major highways and interstates. Amtrak also offers train service to Wilson, with the Wilson Station located in the heart of downtown.

How to Get Around in Wilson

Visitors can easily get around Wilson by car or by using the city’s public transit system, which includes several bus routes and a paratransit service for those with mobility challenges.

Where to Go Next from Wilson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Raleigh - The state capital of North Carolina, located approximately 45 minutes away by car from Wilson.
  • Greenville - A charming college town located approximately 30 minutes away by car from Wilson.
  • Atlantic Beach - A stunning seaside destination located approximately two hours away by car from Wilson.

Conclusion

Wilson is a hidden gem of a city that offers a wealth of opportunities for adventure, exploration, and relaxation. From its sprawling natural landscapes to its rich cultural heritage and vibrant food and drink scene, Wilson has something to offer to every type of traveler. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Wilson is a destination that should not be missed.
