Introduction to Kure Beach

Kure Beach is a hidden gem in the Coastal Plain of North Carolina with beautiful natural attractions, rich history, and cultural significance. The town is located on a narrow strip of land between the Atlantic Ocean and the Cape Fear River, making it an ideal destination for water sports enthusiasts and beachgoers. Kure Beach is renowned for its pristine beaches, lush greenery, colonial-era history, and maritime culture. With a plethora of exciting activities and jaw-dropping sights, Kure Beach offers an unparalleled vacation experience for travelers of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Kure Beach

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Fisher State Historic Site: The site of the largest land-sea battle during the American Civil War and a significant Confederate fortification with a museum and tours available all year round.
  • North Carolina Aquarium at Fort Fisher: A must-visit attraction that features over 2,500 aquatic creatures, outdoor exhibits, touch tanks, and live animal encounters.
  • Kure Beach Fishing Pier: A historic fishing pier that extends 710 feet into the Atlantic Ocean, perfect for fishing, strolling, and taking in the breathtaking views.
  • Battleship North Carolina: A historic warship that was commissioned in 1941 and played a significant role in World War II, now a museum open for tours.

Natural Attractions of Kure Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Carolina Beach State Park: A beautiful park that boasts campsites, hiking trails, kayaking, and fishing in the Cape Fear River.
  • Kure Beach: A beautiful beach that is perfect for sunbathing, walking, and swimming in the clear waters.
  • North End Surf School: A great place to learn to surf or paddleboard along the North Carolina coast.
  • Zeke’s Island Reserve: A stunning nature preserve accessible only by boat with hiking trails and beautiful views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kure Beach

  • Island Greenway: A 1.7-mile-long trail that connects Kure Beach, Carolina Beach, and Fort Fisher for biking, jogging, or strolling.
  • Fort Fisher State Recreation Area: Nine miles of pristine beach perfect for bird watching, hiking, and fishing.

Best Things to Do in Kure Beach: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Take a guided sunset or moonlit tour in the Cape Fear River or rent a kayak and go exploring on your own.
  • Fishing: Kure Beach offers a wealth of fishing opportunities, from the pier to deep sea fishing charters.
  • Beachcombing: Discover various seashells and fossils along the beach and nature trails
  • Surfing: Take a surf lesson or rent a board and hit the waves with the local surf schools.

Food and Drink Scene of Kure Beach

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Kure Beach and the surrounding area is renowned for its seafood, including shrimp, crab, and fresh fish.
  • Low-Country Boil: A traditional Southern dish that is a must-try.
  • Barbeque: Savor the flavorful barbeque with classic Southern sides like coleslaw and cornbread.

Local Beverages

  • Wilmington Brewing Company: A local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers.
  • Defy Gravity: A trendy rooftop bar and restaurant that serves cocktails, wine, and beer.

Where to Stay in Kure Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Courtyard by Marriott Carolina Beach: A beachfront hotel that offers stunning views, a pool, and on-site dining.
  • Camper’s Inn RV Park: A great option for those who prefer self-contained accommodation in the outdoors.
  • Airbnb: Look for private condos available for rent all around Kure Beach and surrounding areas.

What to Buy in Kure Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Seashells: Get your hands on some unique and beautiful seashells that can be found only in the Kure Beach area.
  • Handmade Crafts: The local shops offer unique art and crafts that make great gifts and souvenirs.
  • North Carolina BBQ Sauce and Spices: Take home some of the locally made barbeque sauce, spices, and rubs to recreate the flavors of Kure Beach at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kure Beach

  • Maritime Culture: Kure Beach has a rich maritime history with a strong connection to the ocean and beach culture. Respect the beaches and nature while you visit.
  • Hospitality: Southerners are famous for their hospitality, so expect to receive a warm welcome to Kure Beach.

Best Time to Visit Kure Beach

The best time to visit Kure Beach is between March and May and September through November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. The peak tourist season is during the summer months of June through August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kure Beach

Do:

  • Respect the beaches and nature.
  • Follow the rules and regulations while fishing or surfing.
  • Try the local seafood and specialties.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the parks.
  • Camp in undesignated areas or build fires on the beach.
  • Ignore warning signs on the shore.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kure Beach

Like all beach destinations, safety is always a top priority in Kure Beach. Always swim within designated areas and follow the rules and regulations while on the beach. Be aware of rip currents and other hazards, and never swim alone.

Health in Kure Beach

Kure Beach is safe health-wise and there are no particular health precautions necessary to take when visiting. However, it is always advisable to use sunscreen and insect repellent to avoid sunburn and bug bites.

Getting to Kure Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kure Beach

  • Wilmington International Airport: 20 miles from Kure Beach

Bus Stations Near Kure Beach

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound station is in Wilmington, 16 miles from Kure Beach

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Renting a car is an option for those who wish to have more freedom to explore the surrounding areas of Kure Beach.

How to Get Around in Kure Beach

  • Walking: Kure Beach is a walkable town, especially if you’re staying close to the beach or town center.
  • Biking: Bicycles are available for rent at several locations, such as Pleasure Island Rentals, and the Island Greenway is a great place for cycling.
  • Trolleys: The Island Hopper Trolley system is an affordable and convenient option for getting around Kure Beach and neighboring towns.

Where to Go Next from Kure Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wilmington: A charming port city with a beautiful historic district, vibrant nightlife, and lovely riverfront views.
  • Myrtle Beach: A popular vacation destination that offers a wide range of attractions, such as water parks, amusement parks, and shopping centers.
  • Brunswick Island: A group of picturesque coastal towns that offer gorgeous beaches, excellent golf courses, and fresh seafood.

Conclusion

Kure Beach offers a wealth of exciting activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. From pristine beaches to beautiful parks and museums, there is something for everyone in Kure Beach. Whether you’re looking to escape the busy city and relax on the beach, explore the rich history and culture of the town, or indulge in local cuisine and beverages, Kure Beach won’t disappoint. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to this stunning coastal destination.
