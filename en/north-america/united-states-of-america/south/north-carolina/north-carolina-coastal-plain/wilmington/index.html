Introduction to Wilmington

Wilmington is a vibrant city located in the North Carolina Coastal Plain, in the southern part of the United States of America. Known for its historic downtown, stunning beaches, and rich cultural heritage, Wilmington is a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. This city is home to numerous world-class museums and galleries, award-winning restaurants and breweries, and top-of-the-line accommodation options.

List of Places to Visit in Wilmington

Whether you’re a history buff or a nature lover, Wilmington has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions you should definitely include in your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cameron Art Museum: With over 6,000 works of art, the Cameron Art Museum is a must-visit attraction for all art lovers. The museum’s collection focuses on American art and features works by local artists.
  • Battleship North Carolina: Explore the battleship that served during World War II and learn about the lives of the sailors who served on it.
  • Bellamy Mansion Museum: Visit the Bellamy Mansion Museum, a historic antebellum mansion that gives a glimpse into the life of wealthy Southern families in the mid-19th century.
  • Thalian Hall: Catch a show at Thalian Hall, one of the oldest and most beautiful theaters in the country.

Natural Attractions of Wilmington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wilmington Riverwalk: Take a stroll along the riverfront and enjoy the stunning views of the Cape Fear River. Stop by for lunch at one of the many waterfront restaurants.
  • Wrightsville Beach: Relax on the pristine beaches of Wrightsville Beach and soak up the sun. Take a surfing lesson or paddleboarding session for some fun water activities.
  • Airlie Gardens: Visit the beautiful Airlie Gardens, a 67-acre public garden that features over 100,000 azaleas, camellias, and other native plants.
  • Carolina Beach State Park: Hike through the scenic trails in the Carolina Beach State Park and take in the breathtaking views of the Cape Fear River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilmington

Wilmington is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Explore the city’s natural beauty by hiking or biking through these amazing trails.

  • Gary Shell Cross-City Trail: This 15-mile trail takes you through some of Wilmington’s best parks and neighborhoods.
  • Hugh MacRae Park: Walk or ride through Hugh MacRae Park’s scenic trails and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.
  • River to Sea Bikeway: This 20-mile trail takes you from Wilmington’s riverfront to Wrightsville Beach and offers stunning views of the coast.

Best Things to Do in Wilmington: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Walk of Old Wilmington: Take a spooky tour of Wilmington’s haunted houses and cemeteries and learn about the city’s colorful history.
  • Cape Fear Segway Tours: Explore the city on a Segway and learn about its history and culture.
  • Wilmington Brew Cruise: Take a beer-tasting tour of the city’s top breweries and brewpubs.
  • Wilmington Water Tours: Take a sunset cruise along the Cape Fear River and enjoy the city’s stunning views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wilmington

Looking to spend a week in Wilmington? Here’s a sample itinerary for your trip:

  • Day 1: Visit the Battleship North Carolina and the Cameron Art Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a surfing lesson at Wrightsville Beach and explore the Wrightsville Beach Loop.
  • Day 3: Hike through the Carolina Beach State Park and visit the Fort Fisher State Historic Site.
  • Day 4: Take the Ghost Walk of Old Wilmington and visit the Bellamy Mansion Museum.
  • Day 5: Explore downtown Wilmington on a Segway and take a sunset cruise on the Cape Fear River.
  • Day 6: Visit Airlie Gardens and enjoy a picnic lunch.
  • Day 7: Take a beer-tasting tour of the city’s top breweries and brewpubs.

Food and Drink Scene of Wilmington

Wilmington is known for its diverse food scene and top-of-the-line breweries and wineries. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • North Carolina BBQ: Try some authentic North Carolina-style barbecue, which features slow-roasted pork, vinegar-based sauce, and a variety of sides.
  • Seafood: Sample the freshest seafood at one of Wilmington’s many seafood restaurants.
  • Boiled Peanuts: Try this Southern snack featuring fresh, boiled peanuts seasoned with spices.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Visit one of Wilmington’s top breweries, such as Wilmington Brewing Company and Flytrap Brewing, and try a variety of craft beers.
  • Wine: Visit the Duplin Winery and sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Spirits: Try some of the award-winning spirits from End of Days Distillery and Blue Shark Vodka.

Where to Stay in Wilmington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wilmington offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are some of the top-rated options.

  • Hotel Ballast: Located on the riverfront, this luxurious hotel offers stunning views and world-class amenities.
  • Graystone Inn: This historic bed and breakfast offers elegant rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Rodeway Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable accommodations and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Wilmington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs to bring back home? Here are some of the best local products to buy in Wilmington.

  • Blue Crab: Buy fresh blue crab from one of Wilmington’s seafood markets and cook it up for a delicious meal.
  • Brookstone Soaps: These locally made soaps are crafted using natural ingredients and are perfect for a pampering self-care routine.
  • Wilmington T-Shirts: Grab a Wilmington T-shirt from a local gift shop as a memento of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilmington

Wilmington has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its diverse festivals and events. The city is also known for its Southern hospitality and friendly locals.

Best Time to Visit Wilmington

The best time to visit Wilmington is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and the crowds are thin. Summer months can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilmington

Do:

  • Do try the authentic North Carolina barbecue.
  • Do explore the city’s historic downtown on foot.
  • Do take a sunset cruise on the Cape Fear River.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in the parks.
  • Don’t forget to tip your waitstaff at restaurants.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wilmington

Wilmington is generally a safe city to visit, but it’s best to be cautious in unfamiliar areas and to stay alert at night. Lock your car doors and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Wilmington

Wilmington has a decent healthcare system, but it’s advisable to purchase travel insurance before your trip. Remember to pack any necessary medications and to stay hydrated in the hot weather.

Getting to Wilmington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wilmington

The Wilmington International Airport is the main airport serving the city. It offers domestic flights to cities like Atlanta, Charlotte, and New York.

Bus Stations Near Wilmington

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown Wilmington and offers regular bus services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Wilmington is easily accessible by car and offers ample parking options for tourists.

How to Get Around in Wilmington

The best way to get around Wilmington is by car. The city also offers public transportation options, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Wilmington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to explore nearby areas? Here are some of the best destinations to visit from Wilmington.

  • Asheville: Explore the vibrant city of Asheville, located in the picturesque Blue Ridge Mountains.
  • Charleston: Visit the historic city of Charleston, known for its charming architecture and rich cultural heritage.
  • Myrtle Beach: Relax on the stunning beaches of Myrtle Beach, located just a short drive from Wilmington.

Conclusion

Wilmington is a must-visit city on the East Coast, offering stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and top-of-the-line food and drink scene. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Wilmington, North Carolina.
