Introduction to Elizabeth City

Elizabeth City is a charming city located in the North Carolina Coastal Plain in the southern United States. The city is situated on the banks of the Pasquotank River, which adds to its scenic beauty. Elizabeth City is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and food scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Elizabeth City has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Elizabeth City

If you’re visiting Elizabeth City, don’t miss out on these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Albemarle: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Albemarle region, including the Native American tribes, colonial settlers, and the Civil War.
  • Elizabeth City State University Planetarium and G.R. Little Theater: This state-of-the-art planetarium and theater offers a wide range of educational shows and performances.
  • Historic downtown Elizabeth City: Stroll through the charming streets of Elizabeth City’s historic downtown and marvel at the beautifully preserved 19th-century architecture.

Natural Attractions of Elizabeth City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dismal Swamp State Park: This park offers over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as canoe and kayak rentals to explore the Great Dismal Swamp.
  • Pasquotank River: Take a scenic boat tour or rent a kayak to explore the peaceful waters of the Pasquotank River.
  • Elizabeth City Waterfront: Enjoy a walk along the riverfront, try fishing for stripped bass, or soak in the beauty of an Elizabeth City sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elizabeth City

Elizabeth City is home to several beautiful trails that offer breathtaking views of its natural surroundings. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Elizabeth City include:

  • Elizabeth City State University Trail: This 1.8-mile trail is perfect for a scenic walk or jog around the university’s campus.
  • Dismal Swamp State Park Trail: The park’s 14-mile trail is ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts looking for a challenge.
  • Newbold-White House Trail: This scenic trail is a must-visit for history buffs and offers a glimpse into the colonial life of the region.

Best Things to Do in Elizabeth City: Activities and Tours

Elizabeth City has something for everyone when it comes to activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Elizabeth City include:

  • Elizabeth City Ghost Walk: Take a spooky tour of Elizabeth City’s haunted locations.
  • TCOM Airship Tour: Take a tour of the world’s only in-flight tested airship and learn about its aviation history.
  • Arts of the Albemarle Gallery: This gallery showcases the works of local artists and offers an insight into the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elizabeth City

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Elizabeth City, use this itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Museum of the Albemarle and explore downtown Elizabeth City.
Day 2 Take a boat tour of the Pasquotank River and enjoy a sunset on the waterfront.
Day 3 Go hiking or biking at the Dismal Swamp State Park and enjoy a picnic.
Day 4 Learn about aviation history with the TCOM Airship Tour.
Day 5 Take a scenic drive to nearby Edenton and explore its historic downtown.
Day 6 Enjoy local art at Arts of the Albemarle Gallery and watch a performance at G.R. Little Theater.
Day 7 Relax and unwind at Elizabeth City’s waterfront and enjoy some local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Elizabeth City

Whether you’re in the mood for some classic Southern dishes or want to try something new, Elizabeth City has an exceptional food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carolina-style BBQ: Slow-cooked pork with a tangy vinegar-based sauce.
  • Seafood Gumbo: A Southern staple made with fresh seafood and warm spices.
  • Chowder Fries: A local favorite, these fries are topped with creamy clam chowder.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Elizabeth City is home to several craft breweries, including Ghost Harbor Brewing and The Blind Pelican Brewery.
  • Sweet Tea: A classic Southern beverage, perfect for cooling down on a hot day.
  • Wine: Try some of North Carolina’s finest wines at Vineyards on the Scuppernong or Sanctuary Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Elizabeth City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a budget-friendly option, Elizabeth City has plenty of accommodation choices to suit all preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Elizabeth City include:

  • The Pond House Inn: Situated on the Pasquotank River, this scenic inn offers comfortable rooms and breakfast made from fresh, locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Hampton Inn Elizabeth City: This modern hotel offers all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay at an affordable price.
  • The Culpepper Inn: This historic guesthouse offers charming rooms and a delicious breakfast in a beautifully restored 1930s mansion.

What to Buy in Elizabeth City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for something to bring back home to remind you of your trip to Elizabeth City, check out these local products and souvenirs:

  • Handcrafted pottery: Several local artists make beautiful handmade pottery that can make an excellent souvenir or gift.
  • BBQ sauce and spices: Bring some of Elizabeth City’s unique flavors to your kitchen with local BBQ sauces and spice blends.
  • North Carolina wines: Take back a piece of North Carolina’s rich wine-making heritage with some delicious local wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Elizabeth City

Elizabeth City has a rich cultural heritage that is still celebrated today. Here are some of the local customs and traditions that you might experience during your visit:

  • Southern hospitality: Elizabeth City residents are known for their warm and friendly nature, so don’t be surprised if people strike up a conversation with you!
  • Military appreciation: The town has a strong military presence, with the US Coast Guard’s largest base in the city. Expect to encounter this theme in various ways during your stay.
  • Colonial heritage: Elizabeth City was founded by colonists in the 1700s, and the city’s history and architecture still reflect this heritage today.

Best Time to Visit Elizabeth City

The best time to visit Elizabeth City is during the fall and spring months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are minimal. However, if you’re a fan of festivals, you might want to plan your visit around one of the city’s many annual celebrations, such as the International Street Fair and the Potato Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elizabeth City

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Elizabeth City:

Do:

  • Do take a peaceful boat ride on the Pasquotank River. It’s a wonderful way to reconnect with nature.
  • Do meet the locals and enjoy the Southern hospitality of Elizabeth City.
  • Do try the local Southern cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Keep Elizabeth City’s natural beauty intact by keeping the area clean.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather and activities you have planned.
  • Don’t forget to take some bug spray when going to the state park or other outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elizabeth City

Elizabeth City is a relatively safe city with a very low crime rate. However, like any other city, it’s important to take some safety precautions during your visit. Here are some tips:

  • Always lock your car doors when parking in Elizabeth City.
  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash or valuables when walking around the city.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking in secluded areas at night.

Health in Elizabeth City

Elizabeth City has several hospitals and medical centers that provide excellent healthcare services. However, it’s important to take precautions to stay healthy during your visit. Here are some tips:

  • Stay hydrated and carry a water bottle with you at all times during the summer months.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and sunscreen when spending time outdoors.
  • Don’t forget to take insect repellent when heading to outdoor adventures.

Getting to Elizabeth City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Elizabeth City

The nearest major airport to Elizabeth City is Norfolk International Airport, which is about 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Elizabeth City

The closest bus stations are in Norfolk and Virginia Beach, both of which are around 70 miles away from Elizabeth City.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Elizabeth City, the city is located on Highway 17 between Norfolk, VA and Wilmington, NC. Additionally, Amtrak offers train services to and from the nearby town of Hertford.

How to Get Around in Elizabeth City

Elizabeth City is a relatively small city, and most of the attractions are easily accessible by foot. However, if you prefer to drive, several car rental companies operate in the area. To explore the surrounding areas, you can use taxi services or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Elizabeth City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time left during your visit, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you might want to consider:

  • Edenton: This charming town known for its colonial architecture is just a 30-minute drive from Elizabeth City.
  • Nags Head: Head to this picturesque beach town just an hour’s drive from Elizabeth City for some sun, sand, and surf.
  • Wanchese: Visit this fishing village just over an hour’s drive from Elizabeth City to learn about the local fishing industry and enjoy some fresh seafood.

Conclusion

Elizabeth City is a hidden gem that offers an excellent mix of culture, history, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. This comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your visit to this charming Southern city. Don’t forget to follow the do’s and don’ts to ensure you have a safe and enjoyable trip!
