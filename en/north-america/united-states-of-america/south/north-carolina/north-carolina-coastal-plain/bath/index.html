Introduction to Bath

Bath is a charming small town located in the North Carolina Coastal Plain, in the south of the United States. It is known for being the oldest town in North Carolina, founded in 1705, and therefore has a rich history and culture to explore. Bath is also surrounded by beautiful waterways, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Bath

Here are the top places to visit in Bath, organized by type of attraction:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bath Historic Site: A collection of historic buildings, including St. Thomas Church, Palmer-Marsh House, and Bonner House, that offer visitors a glimpse into the town’s colonial past.
  • Historic Bath Cemetery: A peaceful cemetery filled with graves dating back to the 18th century, including those of prominent Bath residents such as Batholomew Thatcher and John Lawson.
  • N.C. Maritime Museum: This museum showcases the area’s maritime history, including displays of ships, model boats, tools, and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Bath: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bath Creek: Offering stunning views, this calm creek is perfect for kayaking, paddleboarding, and boating. You can also stroll along the paved path that runs beside it.
  • Goose Creek State Park: A 1,400-acre park with hiking trails, fishing spots, picnic areas, and a visitor center where you can learn about the local flora and fauna.
  • Bonner Point: A popular fishing spot with a pier located on the Pamlico River’s western shore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bath

  • Jack’s Island Nature Trail: A 1.5-mile loop trail that takes you through a beautiful wooded area and offers stunning views of Bath Creek and the Pamlico River.
  • Goose Creek State Park Trails: The park’s trails consist of six hiking trails ranging from 0.2 miles to 3.3 miles in length. Biking is also allowed on some of the extensive trails.

Best Things to Do in Bath: Activities and Tours

  • Historic Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of Bath’s historic sites and attractions and learn about the town’s colonial history.
  • Boating and Fishing: Rent a kayak or motorboat and explore the tranquil waterways of Bath or try your hand at fishing in any of the nearby creeks or the Pamlico River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bath

Day 1-2: Explore Bath’s Historic Sites and Museums Day 3: Visit Goose Creek State Park for Hiking and Picnicking Day 4: Go Boating or Fishing on the Pamlico River Day 5: Take a Historic Tour of the Area by Bike Day 6-7: Relax, enjoy Bath’s local cuisine, shop for unique items to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Bath

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carolina Barbeque: Bath has many family-owned barbecue establishments that are famous for their unique slow-roasted meat and vinegar-based sauce.
  • North Carolina Oysters: This regional delicacy can be bought from local seafood vendors or enjoyed at restaurants throughout town.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: There are several breweries in Bath, including the Tipsy Bear Brewery, which offers beers made on-site.
  • Wine: The Pungo Creek Mills Winery and Salty Dawg Smokehouse offers a unique experience of sipping wines while mesmerized by the surrounding beauty.

Where to Stay in Bath: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • NC Eco-Farm: An eco-farm with cabins, glamping experiences, and campsites. A unique experience in the countryside.
  • The Inn on Bath Creek: A romantic getaway location that promises ultimate luxury and tranquility of the Pamlico River.

What to Buy in Bath: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally made Jewelry: Bath is home to many skilled artisans who create beautiful jewelry from seashells, glass, pottery, and precious metals.
  • Handmade Pottery: Visit local pottery studios to find one-of-a-kind pieces that showcase the unique creativity of the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Bath

Bath’s local culture is rooted in its colonial past, and visitors can experience it through tours of historic sites and museums. The town’s residents are also known for their friendliness and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Bath

The best time to visit Bath is between late spring and early fall if you enjoy comfortable weather, sunshine, and outdoor activities. The summers are hot and humid, so it’s best to plan your outdoor activities in the morning and evenings or stick to indoor activities if visiting at this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bath

Do:

  • Do explore the historic sites and museums to learn about Bath’s colonial history.
  • Do bring your sun protection, insect repellent, and hydration pack if spending time outside in the summer.
  • Do try the local seafood and BBQ.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to take your trash with you while in nature or the countryside.
  • Don’t overstep the boundaries when visiting private properties with or without proper signs or markings on them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bath

Bath is generally considered a safe town, but visitors should still practice caution when walking alone at night.

Health in Bath

Bath has a hospital facility, along with a few pharmacies and local clinics, so if you require any medical assistance, you can get help easily and quickly.

Getting to Bath: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bath

The closest airport to Bath is Coastal Carolina Regional Airport in New Bern, North Carolina, located approximately 30 miles away from Bath.

Bus Stations Near Bath

The nearest Greyhound bus station is in Washington, which is about 15 miles away. The local town council offers arrangements for pick-up and drop-off points for visitors arriving via bus or private transportation.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Bath, and several highways link the town to other cities in North Carolina such as Greenville, Washington, and New Bern.

How to Get Around in Bath

The town is small enough to navigate on foot, but to explore the surrounding areas, you would require a car or bike rental as there is no reliable public transportation available.

Where to Go Next from Bath: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Greenville, NC: A college town located only an hour from Bath. Famous for their annual Pirate Fest!
  • New Bern, NC: A charming and historic city, located about 30 miles from Bath.
  • Chocowinity, NC: Enjoy camping and hiking at Cypress Landing RV Park. located just 14 kilometers from Bath.

Conclusion

Bath is a great destination filled with fun and exciting activities that cater to every traveler’s preference. From its rich history and culture to its outdoor adventures and scenic views, Bath offers something for everyone. Be sure to add Bath to your travel bucket list and experience its natural beauty and unique charm.
