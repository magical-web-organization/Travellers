Introduction to Lumberton

Nestled in the heart of North Carolina Coastal Plain, Lumberton is a charming city blessed with southern hospitality, friendly atmosphere, and a rich history. With its scenic parks, vibrant culture, and diverse outdoor activities, Lumberton is an ideal destination for travelers looking for a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or cultural explorer, Lumberton has something for everyone. This travel guide highlights some of the best attractions, activities, and places to stay and eat, to help you plan the perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Lumberton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Robeson County History Museum

Located in the heart of Lumberton, the Robeson County History Museum is a must-see for history buffs and cultural enthusiasts. The museum showcases the rich history and cultural heritage of the Robeson County area, through an impressive collection of artifacts, exhibits, and historical documents.

African American Cultural Center

A visit to the African American Cultural Center is a great way to learn about the rich African American history and cultural heritage of the region. The center showcases the art, music, stories, and traditions of African American communities in the region, through exhibits, events, and performances.

Exploration Station

The Exploration Station is an interactive science center that offers an exciting and educational experience for visitors of all ages. The center features a range of hands-on exhibits and activities that explore science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) topics, making it an ideal destination for families and kids.

Natural Attractions of Lumberton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Luther Britt Park

Located on the banks of Lumber River, Luther Britt Park is a stunning natural area that offers a range of outdoor activities and scenic views. The park features hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, picnic areas, and playgrounds, making it an ideal destination for families and outdoor enthusiasts.

Lumber River State Park

If you love kayaking, canoeing, and fishing, Lumber River State Park is the perfect destination for you. The park features a 115-mile long blackwater river that is ideal for paddling and fishing, along with a range of hiking trails, camping areas, and picnic facilities.

Jones Lake State Park

If you’re looking for a peaceful and serene escape, Jones Lake State Park is the perfect destination. The park is located around a pristine freshwater lake, and features hiking trails, a boat ramp, and picnic areas perfect for spending a relaxing day in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lumberton

Cypress Bend Trail

This scenic trail runs along the banks of Lumber River for 3.5 miles, offering panoramic views of the water and surrounding forests. The trail is ideal for hiking and biking, and is suitable for all skill levels.

River Walk Trail

The River Walk Trail is a 1.5-mile route that winds through Luther Britt Park, offering beautiful views of Lumber River and the surrounding woodlands. The trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride, and is accessible year-round.

Best Things to Do in Lumberton: Activities and Tours

Downtown Lumberton Ghost Walk Tour

The Downtown Lumberton Ghost Walk Tour is a unique and fun way to explore the city’s history and culture, and learn about the local legends and hauntings. The tour takes visitors on an entertaining ghost walk, with knowledgeable guides who share the stories of the area’s past and present.

Lumberton Farmer’s Market

Visiting the Lumberton Farmer’s Market is a must-do for foodies and lovers of fresh produce. The market features a variety of local produce, meats, seafood, and handmade crafts, making it an ideal destination for shopping local and supporting the community.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lumberton

Day 1: Visit the Robeson County History Museum and explore downtown Lumberton.

Day 2: Spend the day at Lumber River State Park, hiking, fishing, kayaking or canoeing.

Day 3: Go on a walking tour of historic sites in Lumberton, including the Lumberton Commercial Historic District and the African American Cultural Center.

Day 4: Visit the Exploration Station and spend some time exploring hands-on exhibits that focus on STEM topics and science education.

Day 5: Hike the Cypress Bend Trail or take a bike ride along the River Walk Trail.

Day 6: Explore the local food scene and visit the Lumberton Farmer’s Market to buy some local produce and sample some delicious food.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and visit local shops and boutiques to find unique and authentic gifts to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Lumberton

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Lumberton is known for its southern-style comfort food, including fried chicken, barbecue, collard greens, and sweet tea. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Barbecue pork ribs
  • Fried chicken and waffles
  • Mac and cheese
  • Cornbread

Local Beverages

Lumberton has a thriving beer and wine scene, with several breweries and wineries in the area. Some of the local brews and wines include:

  • Dirty Hoe Brown Ale from Railhouse Brewery
  • Pineapple Cilantro Cider from Monkey Wrench

Where to Stay in Lumberton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Holiday Inn Lumberton

The Holiday Inn Lumberton offers comfortable and affordable accommodations, along with great amenities like an outdoor pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.

Comfort Suites

The Comfort Suites is a great option for families and business travelers, offering spacious suites with separate living areas, sleeper sofas, and kitchenettes.

What to Buy in Lumberton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Lumberton has a range of options to choose from. Some of the best products to bring home include:

  • Handmade crafts and artworks from local artisans
  • Peach preserves and jams from local farmers
  • Sweet tea and other local beverages

Local Culture and Customs of Lumberton

Lumberton has a deep cultural heritage, with influences from Native American, African American, and southern traditions. Visitors can experience this unique culture through local festivals and events, including the Lumbee Homecoming Festival, the Blueberry Festival, and the Labor Day PowWow.

Best Time to Visit Lumberton

The best time to visit Lumberton is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the foliage is at its peak. The summer season can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and snowy, making it difficult to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lumberton

Do:

  • Explore the local food scene and try some southern-style comfort food
  • Attend local festivals and events to experience the unique culture and traditions of Lumberton
  • Visit the parks and natural areas to enjoy the scenic beauty of the region

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in plain sight in your car while parked
  • Litter or damage natural areas and parks
  • Ignore road signs and safety warnings

Safety and Health

Safety in Lumberton

Like any city, Lumberton has its share of safety concerns, including petty crime and theft. Visitors should take basic precautions like locking their car doors and being aware of their surroundings, especially when walking at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Lumberton

Lumberton has several healthcare facilities and hospitals, including Southeastern Regional Medical Center, which provides emergency and acute care services to the local community.

Getting to Lumberton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lumberton

The nearest airport to Lumberton is Fayetteville Regional Airport, located about 30 miles north of the city.

Bus Stations Near Lumberton

Greyhound Bus Lines provides bus service to Lumberton through its station located in the city center.

Other Options

Lumberton is easily accessible by car, with major highways including I-95, US-301, and US-74 running through the area.

How to Get Around in Lumberton

There are several transportation options in Lumberton, including:

  • Rental cars
  • Taxis and rideshare services
  • Buses and shuttles
  • Biking and walking

Where to Go Next from Lumberton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fayetteville

Located about 26 miles north of Lumberton, Fayetteville is a historic city with a range of cultural attractions and outdoor activities. Visitors can explore the city’s history at the Museum of the Cape Fear Historical Complex, or enjoy outdoor adventures at Cape Fear River Trail or Lake Rim Park.

Raleigh

Located about 90 miles north of Lumberton, Raleigh is the capital of North Carolina and an urban center that has much to offer visitors. From its vibrant arts scene to its many parks and museums and world-class dining, Raleigh is a great destination for a day trip or extended stay.

Conclusion

Lumberton is a hidden gem in North Carolina, with a rich cultural heritage, scenic natural areas, and outdoor activities galore. Whether you’re a history buff, culture enthusiast, or outdoor adventurer, Lumberton has something to offer. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your perfect trip to Lumberton and discover all this charming city has to offer.
