Introduction to Colington

Located in the Outer Banks of North Carolina, Colington is a quaint coastal town that offers visitors a mix of breathtaking natural scenery and rich historical and cultural attractions. Surrounded by water on all sides, Colington boasts a number of recreational activities, including hiking and biking trails, outdoor adventures, and water sports. Visitors can also explore the town’s museums, historical sites, and local cuisine, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all interests.

Local Culture and Customs of Colington

  • Colington is known for its laid-back lifestyle and focus on community.
  • Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and customs, including the importance of fishing and outdoor activities.
  • The area has a rich history, including the first flight by the Wright Brothers, and visitors should take time to learn about the local heritage.

Best Time to Visit Colington

The best time to visit Colington is during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall when the weather is mild, and crowds are smaller. Summer is the busiest time of year and can be crowded, especially on weekends.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colington

Do:

  • Take time to explore the natural scenery and hiking trails.
  • Try the local seafood and craft beer.
  • Learn about the local history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural areas or historic sites.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colington

  • Visitors should be aware of the dangers of rip currents and strong tides when swimming or boating.
  • Be cautious when driving on the narrow, winding roads in the area.

Health in Colington

  • It is recommended to bring insect repellent, as mosquitoes and other insects can be present in outdoor areas.

Getting to Colington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colington

  • Norfolk International Airport (ORF), located approximately 90 miles north of Colington.
  • Raleigh-Durham International Airport (RDU), located approximately 170 miles southwest of Colington.

Bus Stations Near Colington

  • Greyhound Bus Lines in Elizabeth City, located approximately 15 miles west of Colington.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also arrive by car or bike, using the Route 158 and Route 12 highways.

How to Get Around in Colington

The easiest way to get around Colington is by car or bike, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can also explore the downtown area on foot.

Where to Go Next from Colington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Head south to the beach towns of Nags Head and Kill Devil Hills.
  • Visit the historic town of Edenton, located approximately 40 miles west of Colington.
  • Take a ferry to Ocracoke Island to explore its natural beauty and unique local culture.

Conclusion

Colington is a hidden gem in the Outer Banks region, offering visitors a mix of natural beauty, historical significance, and local charm. With a wide range of activities, attractions, and cultural experiences, Colington is a must-visit destination in North Carolina’s Coastal Plain region.
