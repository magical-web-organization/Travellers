A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Hatteras, North Carolina

If you’re looking for the perfect combination of sun, sand, and surf, Hatteras is the place to be. Located in the Outer Banks region of North Carolina, Hatteras boasts a stunning coastline with an array of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures and scenic views, and delicious local cuisine. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Hatteras, covering everything from the top sights and activities to the best places to stay and the must-try dishes.

List of Places to Visit in Hatteras

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Graveyard of the Atlantic Museum

For a deep dive into the history of Hatteras, head to the Graveyard of the Atlantic Museum. It’s dedicated to preserving the history of shipwrecks and rescues in the region. Located in a restored 19th-century lifesaving station, the museum features fascinating exhibits showcasing the maritime history of Hatteras and the surrounding area.

Cape Hatteras Lighthouse

Cape Hatteras Lighthouse is arguably the most iconic landmark in Hatteras. It’s the tallest brick lighthouse in the country and has an interesting history - it’s been moved a number of times due to erosion. You can climb the lighthouse to get panoramic views of the coastline.

Natural Attractions of Hatteras: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hatteras Island

Hatteras Island is a 42-mile stretch of beach that’s perfect for a relaxing day out. You can swim, sunbathe, or rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the clear waters. The island is also home to a variety of wildlife, including bald eagles and sea turtles.

Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge

Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge is a must-visit spot for nature lovers. The refuge is home to hundreds of species of birds, including peregrine falcons and snowy egrets. You can explore the different trails, watch the bird migrations, or head to the beach for some amazing views of the Atlantic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hatteras

Cape Hatteras National Seashore

The Cape Hatteras National Seashore is a popular spot for hiking and biking. There are over 70 miles of trails that will take you through the island’s wildlife reserves, dunes, and forests. You can catch a glimpse of the wild horses that roam freely on the beaches, or explore the historic villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Hatteras: Activities and Tours

Outer Banks Ferry

The Outer Banks Ferry is an exciting way to see the region from a different perspective. The ferry offers a scenic ride across the Pamlico Sound, taking you from Hatteras to Ocracoke Island. The journey takes around an hour each way, and you’ll get stunning views of the coastline and the lighthouses along the way.

Fishing Charters

Fishing charters are a great way to explore the waters around Hatteras. You can hire a charter and head out to sea with an experienced guide. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced angler, you can catch some of the best fish the region has to offer, including tuna, marlin, and mahi-mahi.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hatteras

Day 1 Check-in and head to the beach
Day 2 Explore Graveyard of the Atlantic Museum and nearby historical sites
Day 3 Visit Cape Hatteras Lighthouse and hike the nearby trails
Day 4 Take the Outer Banks Ferry to Ocracoke Island
Day 5 Spend the day kayaking, paddleboarding, or fishing
Day 6 Head to Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge and go bird-watching
Day 7 Enjoy some of the local cuisine before checking out

Food and Drink Scene of Hatteras

Must-Try Dishes

Hatteras-style Clam Chowder

Hatteras-style clam chowder is a seafood lovers’ dream come true. This rich and creamy soup is made with fresh clams, bacon, potatoes, onions, and celery. The addition of hot sauce gives it a slightly spicy kick.

Shrimp and Grits

Shrimp and grits are a Southern classic, and Hatteras does it right. The shrimp are cooked to perfection, and the grits are creamy and buttery. It’s the perfect comfort food after a long day out in the sun.

Local Beverages

Carolina Pale Ale

If you’re a beer lover, make sure to try some of the local brews. Carolina Pale Ale is a popular beer made with hops and barley grown in North Carolina. It’s light, refreshing, and perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.

Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a Southern classic, and you can’t visit Hatteras without trying it. It’s a refreshing, ice-cold drink made with tea and sugar. It’s the perfect accompaniment to any meal.

Where to Stay in Hatteras: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hatteras Village Motel

The Hatteras Village Motel is a historic motel that’s been updated with modern amenities. It’s located just steps from the beach and offers comfortable rooms and suites.

The Inn on Pamlico Sound

The Inn on Pamlico Sound is an upscale bed and breakfast that offers stunning views of the water. It’s located on the southern end of Hatteras Island and has luxurious rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Hatteras: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hatteras Island Surprises

Hatteras Island Surprises is a gift shop that sells a range of souvenirs, including t-shirts, hats, and postcards. They also sell local products, such as handmade soaps and candles.

Lee Robinson’s General Store

Lee Robinson’s General Store is a historic store that’s been around since 1948. They sell a range of items, including clothing, kitchenware, and locally-made jams and jellies.

Local Culture and Customs of Hatteras

Hatteras has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its food, music, and art. The locals are known for their warm hospitality and their love of the sea.

Best Time to Visit Hatteras

The best time to visit Hatteras is during the spring and fall months. The temperatures are mild, and the crowds are smaller. If you’re looking for a beach vacation, the summer months are the busiest, but there’s still plenty of room to spread out and enjoy the sand and surf.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hatteras

Do:

  • Explore the different beaches along the coastline.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the seafood.
  • Take a fishing charter.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Ignore the weather warnings or advisories.
  • Touch or disturb the wildlife - it’s important to respect their natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hatteras

Hatteras is a safe destination, but it’s still important to take precautions, especially when you’re out on the water. When swimming, make sure to stay within the designated areas, and always wear sunscreen and a hat.

Health in Hatteras

If you’re visiting from outside the area, make sure to pack any necessary medications or medical supplies. There are medical facilities and pharmacies in the area, but it’s always better to be prepared.

Getting to Hatteras: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hatteras

Norfolk International Airport is the closest major airport to Hatteras, located about two and a half hours away. The Dare County Regional Airport is also nearby, but it primarily caters to private flights.

Bus Stations Near Hatteras

Greyhound buses stop at Elizabeth City, which is about an hour and a half from Hatteras by car. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi.

Other Options

If you’re driving from the north, you can take the Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel. It’s a picturesque drive that takes you through the Chesapeake Bay and across the Atlantic Ocean.

How to Get Around in Hatteras

The best way to get around Hatteras is by car. You can rent a car from one of the major rental companies or bring your own. There are also taxis and ride-sharing services available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Hatteras: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wright Brothers National Memorial

The Wright Brothers National Memorial is a must-visit spot for anyone interested in aviation history. It’s located about two hours away from Hatteras and is home to the site of the first powered flight.

Roanoke Island

Roanoke Island is a charming destination that’s located about an hour and a half from Hatteras. It’s home to the Lost Colony Outdoor Drama, a popular show that tells the story of the settlers who disappeared from the island in the late 1500s.

Conclusion

Hatteras is a hidden gem that’s worth exploring. From the natural beauty of the beaches and wildlife reserves to the cultural attractions and delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or explore the history and culture of the region, Hatteras is sure to delight.
