Introduction to Kitty Hawk

Located in the scenic Outer Banks region of North Carolina, Kitty Hawk is a quaint beach town with a rich history and natural beauty. The town is famous for being the site of the Wright Brothers’ first flight and is known for its beautiful beaches, thrilling outdoor activities, and unique local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Kitty Hawk

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Kitty Hawk, separated into two categories: culturally important attractions and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wright Brothers National Memorial: This monument celebrates the achievements of the Wright Brothers and their historic first flight in 1903.
  2. Monument to a Century of Flight: This monument commemorates the 100th anniversary of the Wright Brothers’ flight and other aviation milestones.
  3. Aycock Brown Welcome Center: This center has exhibits on the town’s history and serves as an information hub for visitors.
  4. Outer Banks Center for Wildlife Education: This museum offers exhibits on local wildlife and interactive displays.
  5. Dare County Arts Council: This arts center features local artists and galleries.

Natural Attractions of Kitty Hawk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jockey’s Ridge State Park: This park has the tallest sand dune on the East Coast, perfect for hiking and sandboarding.
  2. Kitty Hawk Woods Coastal Reserve: This reserve offers hiking and birdwatching opportunities.
  3. Kitty Hawk Beach: This beach is known for its natural beauty and is great for swimming, surfing, and sunbathing.
  4. Kitty Hawk Bay: This bay is perfect for kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding.
  5. Bodie Island Lighthouse: This lighthouse is a beautiful sight and offers stunning views of the surrounding area from the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kitty Hawk

Kitty Hawk has plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include the Kitty Hawk Woods Coastal Reserve Trail, the Beach Road Trail, and the multi-use path that runs along NC-12.

Best Things to Do in Kitty Hawk: Activities and Tours

  1. Learn to hang glide: Kitty Hawk Kites offers hang gliding lessons for beginners.
  2. Go fishing: Kitty Hawk Fishing Charters offers both inshore and offshore fishing trips.
  3. Take a dolphin watching tour: Carolina Coast offers tours that give you a chance to see dolphins up close.
  4. Take a sunset sail: Nor’Banks Sailing offers kayaking and sailing tours at sunset.
  5. Visit a vineyard: Sanctuary Vineyards is a local vineyard that offers wine tastings and tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kitty Hawk

Day 1: Visit the Wright Brothers National Memorial and Jockey’s Ridge State Park in the morning, followed by surfing or SUPing at Kitty Hawk Beach in the afternoon.

Day 2: Take a hang gliding lesson with Kitty Hawk Kites in the morning and go fishing with Kitty Hawk Fishing Charters in the afternoon.

Day 3: Take a dolphin watching tour with Carolina Coast in the morning and visit the Outer Banks Center for Wildlife Education in the afternoon.

Day 4: Take a sunset sail with Nor’Banks Sailing and then have dinner at The Saltbox Cafe.

Day 5: Visit the Bodie Island Lighthouse in the morning and then go on a wine tour at Sanctuary Vineyards in the afternoon.

Day 6: Hike or bike along the Kitty Hawk Woods Coastal Reserve Trail in the morning and take a guided tour of the town’s historic homes in the afternoon.

Day 7: Spend your last day soaking up the sun at Kitty Hawk Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Kitty Hawk

Kitty Hawk has a rich food and drink scene, with plenty of options for both foodies and casual diners. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Outer Banks Clam Chowder: This creamy chowder is a local specialty.
  2. Hatteras-style Po’ Boy: This sandwich features fried seafood and is a staple of the Outer Banks.
  3. Steamed Blue Crabs: This local delicacy is often served at seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  1. North Carolina Craft Beer: Try a local beer from local breweries such as Outer Banks Brewing Station and Lost Colony Brewery.
  2. Sweet Trolley Iced Tea: This tea is a refreshing and sweet drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Kitty Hawk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kitty Hawk has plenty of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to guesthouses. Some of the best options include The Beachwoods Resort, Days Inn & Suites by Wyndham, and Sea Vista Motel.

What to Buy in Kitty Hawk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kitty Hawk is known for its stunning seascapes, and beach-themed gifts are a popular souvenir. You can also find beautiful Outer Banks-themed artwork and local seafood products, such as Carolina-style BBQ sauce and Outer Banks’ famous blue crabs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kitty Hawk

Kitty Hawk is known for its charming Southern hospitality and relaxed beach culture. Visitors are expected to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly in public areas and avoiding loud noise after 10 pm.

Best Time to Visit Kitty Hawk

The best time to visit Kitty Hawk is during the summer months from May to September when the weather is warm and the beaches are bustling with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kitty Hawk

Do:

  1. Do visit the Wright Brothers National Memorial to learn about the first flight.
  2. Do try local seafood dishes, such as the Hatteras-style Po’ Boy and the Outer Banks Clam Chowder.
  3. Do take advantage of the natural beauty of the Outer Banks by exploring the beaches, hiking trails, and sand dunes.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly and not littering on the beach.
  2. Don’t forget to apply sunscreen regularly, as the sun can be intense in Kitty Hawk.
  3. Don’t forget to book accommodation and activities in advance, as popular options can sell out quickly during peak travel season.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kitty Hawk

Kitty Hawk is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking rental cars and bikes and keeping valuables out of sight. Visitors should also be aware of rip currents in the ocean and follow lifeguard instructions.

Health in Kitty Hawk

Kitty Hawk has excellent medical facilities and urgent care centers available, but visitors should still take precautions against ticks, mosquitos, and other insects by wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Kitty Hawk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kitty Hawk

Norfolk International Airport is approximately 70 miles north of Kitty Hawk and is the closest major airport. Raleigh-Durham International Airport is further away, approximately 200 miles west of Kitty Hawk.

Bus Stations Near Kitty Hawk

There are no bus stations in Kitty Hawk, but visitors can take the Greyhound bus to nearby cities like Nags Head and then rent a car or take a taxi to Kitty Hawk.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Kitty Hawk and take the ferry from Nags Head to nearby Roanoke Island.

How to Get Around in Kitty Hawk

The best way to get around in Kitty Hawk is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Kitty Hawk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Nags Head: This neighboring town is known for its beautiful beaches and surfing.
  2. Roanoke Island: This island is home to the Lost Colony, an outdoor theater that tells the story of the first English colony in America.
  3. Ocracoke Island: This island is accessible only by ferry and is known for its remote beaches and wild horses.

Conclusion

Kitty Hawk is a beautiful and unique beach town that offers plenty of opportunities for adventure, relaxation, and exploration. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and charming local culture, there is something for everyone in Kitty Hawk.
