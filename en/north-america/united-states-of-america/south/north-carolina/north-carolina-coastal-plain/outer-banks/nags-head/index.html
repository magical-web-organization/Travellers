Nags Head Travel Guide - A Must-Read Before You Go

Located in the Outer Banks region of North Carolina, Nags Head is a beautiful and popular vacation destination. Home to stunning beaches, diverse wildlife, and a rich history, Nags Head has something for everyone, whether you are a beach lover, an adrenaline seeker, or a history buff. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through all the essential things you need to know to plan your trip to Nags Head. From places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, to safety tips – we’ve got you covered!

List of Places to Visit in Nags Head

Nags Head boasts of numerous attractions that cater to different age groups and interests. Below are some of the top places to visit in Nags Head.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bodie Island Lighthouse: A historic lighthouse that has stood on the Outer Banks since the 1870s. Visitors can climb the lighthouse to enjoy breathtaking views of the ocean and the surrounding area.
  • Wright Brothers National Memorial: This fascinating facility celebrates the site where Orville and Wilbur Wright made their first plane flight. The visitors center has exhibits that cover the history and science of flight, and visitors can get on a replica of the original flying machine.
  • The Lost Colony: A production that dramatizes the story of the first English settlement in America established in 1587. The show takes place in an outdoor theater on Roanoke Island, and the performance involves music, drama, and dance.

Natural Attractions of Nags Head: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jockey’s Ridge State Park: A sandy park that has the highest natural sand dune system in the eastern US, which makes for incredible panoramic views. Visitors can fly kites, take hang-gliding lessons, go hiking or observe the sunset.
  • Cape Hatteras National Seashore: Known for its gorgeous beaches, this national park is a must-visit when in Nags Head. The seashore stretches over 70 miles, providing visitors with ample opportunities to swim, sunbathe, surf, or fish.
  • Alligator River National Wildlife Refuge: A hidden gem that covers over 152,000 acres, offering visitors a chance to see some of the areas’ indigenous plants and animals. Visitors can enjoy bird-watching, kayaking, fishing, or hiking the nature trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nags Head

Nags Head has more than enough scenic locations for hiking and biking activities. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Cape Hatteras National Seashore: Offers a 30-mile multi-use trail, efficient for bike enthusiasts who love the scenic views of the Atlantic Ocean
  • Roanoke Island: Historically significant site, the roads provide an adequate route for cycling enthusiasts who are interested in observing landmarks and natural surroundings
  • Jockey’s Ridge State Park: A massive park with over 400 acres of natural sand dunes, suitable for biking and trail hiking with a fantastic view of the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Nags Head: Activities and Tours

Aside from the spots listed above, there are many more exciting things to see and do while in Nags Head. Here are some ideas for your itinerary:

  • Fishing tours: Nags Head has plenty of opportunities for fishing enthusiasts. Book a charter with one of the many local fishing guides for deep-sea fishing tours that guarantee a fun-filled day out at sea.
  • Corolla Wild Horse Tours: Located on the Currituck Outer Banks, the Corolla Wild Horse Tours are a fascinating way to get to know the area’s wild Mustang horses. The tours take visitors to sites where they can see and learn about these magnificent animals.
  • Manteo Water Adventures: Take a sunset cruise with this water adventure company for an experience with superb clear views of the area’s marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nags Head

Here is a rough itinerary on how to spend a week in Nags Head.

Day 2 Visit Bodie Island Lighthouse, then head to the beach for some sun, sea, and sand

Food and Drink Scene of Nags Head

Nags Head is well known for its seafood restaurants, and you’ll find a range of delicious local dishes to try during your stay.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Outer Banks-style Clam Chowder: A creamy and savory soup that comprises of chopped clams, potatoes, and herbs.
  • Crab cakes: A popular dish, made with fresh crab meat, served with a side of hush puppies or fries.
  • Shrimp and Grits: A classic Southern fare with shrimp seasoned with lip-smacking spices served with cheesy grits.

Local Beverages

  • Breweries: The OBX Brewing Station and Lost Colony Brewery and Café provide visitors with locally brewed beer options.
  • Wines: Saint Paul Mountain Vineyards offers guests vineyard views and wine tastings that keep visitors delighted.

Where to Stay in Nags Head: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nags Head has several options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to campsites and cottages. Here are a few recommendations:

  • The Sanderling Resort: A luxurious oceanfront resort that offers panoramic views of the ocean, multiple pools, and spa services.
  • Comfort Inn South Oceanfront: Comfortable and affordable accommodation that’s ideal for families. The hotel is right on the beach, with plenty of restaurants and bars nearby.
  • Cape Hatteras Motel: A reasonably-priced motel with spacious rooms, right next to the beach. The property also has an outdoor pool, a BBQ area and outdoor seating to provide visitors with the ultimate vacation experience.

What to Buy in Nags Head: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nags Head is home to several shops and boutiques selling souvenirs and local products. Here are some things you might consider buying:

  • Shells and seastars: Take back shells, seastars, and sea urchins from Nags Head beaches - the best way to remember a trip to the coast!
  • Jewelry and handmade crafts: Visit The Cotton Gin, which offers a wide array of unique handmade products, from jewelry to pottery items.
  • T-Shirts and Sweatshirts: Take home trendy t-shirts and sweatshirts from the dedicated beach shops in Nags Head.

Local Culture and Customs of Nags Head

Nags Head has a rich history and culture that is evident in the local customs and traditions. The locals are known for their love for fishing, and seafood is a staple in the community. Keep a lookout for the annual events such as Kite Festival, Brewtag, OBX Marathon, and running events that showcase the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Nags Head

The best time to visit Nags Head is from May to September when temperatures range from 70°F (21°C) to 90°F (32°C). These months offer perfect beach weather, ideal for swimming or sun-bathing. October to April are generally colder months, and temperatures go as low as 60°F(15°C). These months are perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities such as hiking, with the seashore being less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nags Head

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat when going out to enjoy the sun.
  • Attend the annual events and festivals to learn more about the local culture.
  • Respect the wildlife and avoid littering in the national parks.

Don’t:

  • Never leave valuables unattended on the beach; Nags Head Beaches attract thieves.
  • Don’t swim on beaches that are not lifeguarded; riptides and other hazards can be dangerous.
  • Avoid fishing without a valid fishing license to avoid legal complications.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nags Head

Nags Head is generally safe for tourists, but travelers should be careful and exercise caution when exploring the beaches or parks. However, always be alert, especially at night, and avoid areas that are deemed unsafe. Additionally, make sure you always supervise children when swimming, as the unpredictable ocean currents can be dangerous.

Health in Nags Head

The health facilities in Nags Head are adequate, and you should be able to get help without significant issues. Medical emergencies should be reported immediately to the required authorities.

Getting to Nags Head: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nags Head is accessible by road, air, or sea. Below are the various ways to get to Nags Head conveniently:

Airports Near Nags Head

  • Norfolk International Airport: Located just two hours away, this airport provides an affordable flight option, connecting many cities worldwide.
  • Raleigh-Durham International Airport: Just four-hour drive to Nags Head, this airport has several daily arrivals from the major cities worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Nags Head

  • Elizabeth City Bus Station: A twenty-minute drive to Nags Head serves as an affordable option for travelers.
  • ECU Transit Station: A reliable station that receives regular interstate bus services

Other Options

Driving to Nags Head is comfortable, and you can rent cars from airports or at the various rental car facilities near Nags Head.

How to Get Around in Nags Head

Renting a car is a popular choice in Nags Head to get around the different attractions and explore the beaches and seashore. There are also bike rentals available in Nags Head, allowing visitors to explore the area at their leisure. Additionally, visitors can use the Dare County buses that come with low fares to avoid traffic and parking issues in crowded areas.

Where to Go Next from Nags Head: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nags Head is conveniently located, providing visitors with chances to explore other interesting locations nearby, be sure to check out:

  • Hatteras Island: A barrier island that offers visitors more options for swimming and outdoor activities like fishing and hiking.
  • Ocracoke Island: Accessible only via a ferry ride, this undisturbed island has preserved its natural beauty and old architecture, making it a perfect destination to relax and rejuvenate.

Conclusion

With numerous activities to engage in, fantastic sightseeing opportunities, and cultural events to partake in, Nags Head is an ideal vacation destination that promises to offer visitors an unforgettable experience. Remember to carry your sunscreen, book your accommodation early and purchase a valid fishing tours license to avoid trouble when planning your trip to Nags Head.
