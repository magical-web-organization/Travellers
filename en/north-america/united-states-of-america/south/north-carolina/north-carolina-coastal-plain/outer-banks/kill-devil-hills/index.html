Introduction to Kill Devil Hills

Kill Devil Hills is a town located in the Outer Banks region of North Carolina Coastal Plain. It is famous for its stunning beaches and historical landmarks, making it a popular vacation destination for families and solo travelers alike. Apart from its scenic views and outdoor adventures, the area is rich in cultural history, offering various museums and historical sites for visitors to explore. In this travel guide, we will take you through a comprehensive tour of Kill Devil Hills, highlighting must-visit places, outdoor activities, food, accommodations, culture and customs, safety, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Kill Devil Hills

If you are planning to visit Kill Devil Hills, you should not miss these top-rated attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wright Brothers National Memorial

The Wright Brothers National Memorial is a tribute to the Wright Brothers, who are credited with the first controlled, powered, and sustained flight of a heavier-than-air craft. The site includes a visitor center with exhibits, galleries, and a reconstructed model of the Wright Brothers’ gliders and airplanes. You can also climb up to the top of the hill to see the granite monument dedicated to the Wright brothers and enjoy a panoramic view of the city.

Roanoke Island Festival Park

Roanoke Island Festival Park is a living history museum that offers visitors a chance to step back in time to the 16th century. The park has several exhibits and interactive displays showcasing the lives and traditions of Native Americans and European settlers. It also has several outdoor stages where you can enjoy live music, theater, and dance performances.

Natural Attractions of Kill Devil Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jockey’s Ridge State Park

Jockey’s Ridge State Park is the largest active sand dune system in the Eastern United States. It offers 426-acres of land for hiking, picnicking, and exploring. You can also go hang gliding or kite flying, or simply enjoy the breathtaking views of the sunset over the Atlantic Ocean.

Nags Head Woods Ecological Preserve

Nags Head Woods Ecological Preserve is a protected area consisting of 1,400-acres of forest, marshland, and wetlands. It is home to over 50 species of birds and various mammals, including deer and raccoons. Visitors can hike the trails, go bird watching, or participate in guided nature tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kill Devil Hills

Kill Devil Hills offers several hiking and biking trails for nature enthusiasts, including:

  • The Wright Brothers National Memorial Trail

  • The Nature Trail at the Nags Head Woods Ecological Preserve

Local Culture and Customs of Kill Devil Hills

Kill Devil Hills is a laid-back city, and residents are friendly and welcoming to tourists. The area has a deep-seated history, and it is essential to be respectful of local culture and customs. One particular custom is wearing shoes inside the house, which is considered rude by some locals.

Best Time to Visit Kill Devil Hills

The best time to visit Kill Devil Hills is during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. The months of March to May and September to November offer optimal conditions for outdoor activities and exploring the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kill Devil Hills

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try local cuisine and drinks
  • Bring sunscreen and bug spray
  • Wear comfortable shoes for hiking, biking, and exploring

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or in the parks
  • Walk on the dunes or remove seashells and rocks
  • Swim alone in the ocean
  • Leave valuables in unattended vehicles

Safety and Health

Safety in Kill Devil Hills

Kill Devil Hills is a relatively safe area, but it is always essential to take precautions. Avoid swimming alone in the ocean and stay aware of the dangerous rip currents. When hiking, stay on the authorized trails and beware of wildlife such as snakes, spiders, and ticks.

Health in Kill Devil Hills

If you plan to spend a lot of time outdoors in Kill Devil Hills, it’s crucial to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays and insects. Bring sunscreen, a hat, and bug spray and stay hydrated during outdoor activities. In case of a medical emergency, the Outer Banks Hospital is located in Nags Head, around 10 minutes away from Kill Devil Hills.

Getting to Kill Devil Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kill Devil Hills is located about 90 miles south of Norfolk, Virginia. The area is serviced by several airports and bus stations, making it accessible for travelers.

Airports Near Kill Devil Hills

  • Norfolk International Airport
  • Newport News/Williamsburg International Airport
  • Raleigh-Durham International Airport

Bus Stations Near Kill Devil Hills

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Elizabeth City, NC
  • Currituck Travel on the Outer Banks

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, you can take Route 64 East to Route 158 South.

How to Get Around in Kill Devil Hills

The best way to navigate Kill Devil Hills is by car, bike, or foot. Several bike rental shops are available in the area, but it’s essential to be careful while biking on the roads. Uber and Lyft services are also available in Kill Devil Hills.
