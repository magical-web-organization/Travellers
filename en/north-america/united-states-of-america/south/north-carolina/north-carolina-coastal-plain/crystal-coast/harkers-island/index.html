Introduction to Harkers Island

Located in the Crystal Coast region of North Carolina’s Coastal Plain, Harkers Island is a hidden paradise waiting to be explored. With its unspoiled natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality, this island makes for a great escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

The island is easily accessible by car, with plenty of parking space available. Once here, you can explore the island’s sandy beaches, go fishing, kayaking or boating, hike the beautiful trails or simply relax and enjoy the stunning sunsets.

List of Places to Visit in Harkers Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs, Harkers Island offers a glimpse into the past with its many historic sites and museums. Here are a few must-visit places:

  • Core Sound Waterfowl Museum and Heritage Center: This museum celebrates the art and culture of the region’s waterfowl hunting heritage. You can learn about the techniques used by the hunters and see the beautiful carvings of the birds.

  • Harkers Island Visitor Center: The center has exhibits on the history and culture of the island, along with information on the local flora and fauna.

  • Rachel Carson Reserve: This natural reserve is home to various bird species and is named after the famous biologist and environmentalist, Rachel Carson.

Natural Attractions of Harkers Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Harkers Island has an abundance of natural beauty, waiting to be explored. Here are some popular natural attractions:

  • Cape Lookout National Seashore: This protected area stretches over 56 miles of the Outer Banks’ southernmost barrier islands and features unspoiled beaches, dunes, and marshes. Here you can go hiking, swimming, fishing and boating.

  • Hammocks Beach State Park: Located just a short distance inland, this park features hiking trails, picnic areas, and beautiful sandy beaches.

  • Shackleford Banks: This small island off the coast is home to wild horses that have roamed the area for centuries.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harkers Island

Harkers Island has some great hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s natural beauty. The most popular trails include:

  • Harkers Island Trail: A 2.4-mile loop trail that takes you through the woods and offers scenic views of the Core Sound.

  • Cedar Point Tideland Trail: A 1.1-mile trail that winds through the salt marsh and offers views of the sound.

Best Things to Do in Harkers Island: Activities and Tours

Harkers Island offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few:

  • Deep-Sea Fishing: Take a chartered fishing boat and enjoy a day of deep-sea fishing for tuna, marlin, and other species.

  • Stand-Up Paddleboarding: Enjoy a guided tour of the area’s scenic waterways while paddling on a stand-up paddleboard.

  • Sunset Dolphin Cruise: Take a relaxing boat ride and watch the dolphins play in the sunset-drenched waters of the Core Sound.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harkers Island

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip to Harkers Island:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the island’s beaches and enjoy water sports.
Day 2 Visit the Core Sound Waterfowl Museum and Heritage Center.
Day 3 Take a hiking or biking tour of the island’s trails.
Day 4 Charter a deep-sea fishing boat.
Day 5 Take a guided paddleboarding tour.
Day 6 Visit the Rachel Carson Reserve and Shackleford Banks.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the island’s natural beauty before leaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Harkers Island

Harkers Island has a rich food and drink scene with several local hotspots worth checking out.

Must-Try Dishes

  • North Carolina Barbecue: This slow-cooked pulled pork dish is a local favorite and is served with vinegar-based sauce.

  • Hush Puppies: These deep-fried balls of cornmeal are a popular side dish.

  • Soft-Shell Crab Sandwich: Enjoy a sandwich made with the freshest local crab, served on a bun with lettuce and tomato.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Try one of the many locally brewed craft beers at the Island Time Tavern.

  • Sweet Tea: This refreshing drink is a Southern classic and can be found throughout the island.

Where to Stay in Harkers Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available on Harkers Island, ranging from hotels to bed and breakfasts. Here are a few recommended options:

  • Harkers Island Lodge: A comfortable hotel with waterfront views and a swimming pool.

  • The Castle Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms, a delicious breakfast, and a beautiful view of the sound.

  • White Point Motel: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Harkers Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-Carved Decoys: These stunning wooden carvings of various waterfowl species are made by local artisans and can be found at the Core Sound Waterfowl Museum and Heritage Center.

  • Local Honey: This delicious honey is made from the hives found throughout the island.

  • North Carolina Barbecue Sauce: Bring home a bottle of the famous vinegar-based barbecue sauce to enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Harkers Island

Harkers Island has a rich and vibrant local culture that is deeply rooted in its history and natural surroundings. The islanders are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature. Visitors should respect the island’s natural beauty and preserve its ecological balance.

Best Time to Visit Harkers Island

The best time to visit Harkers Island is in the spring and fall when the weather is milder, and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and windy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harkers Island

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the island.

  • Take part in the local culture and traditions.

  • Book your accommodation in advance, especially during peak season.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their traditions.

  • Ignore safety warnings and signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harkers Island

Harkers Island is a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of possible hazards in outdoor activities such as boating, swimming, and hiking.

Health in Harkers Island

Medical facilities are available on the mainland, but visitors should bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Harkers Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harkers Island

The closest airport to Harkers Island is the Coastal Carolina Regional Airport, located in New Bern, which is 35 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Harkers Island

The nearest Greyhound Bus station is located in New Bern, which is 35 miles away from Harkers Island.

Other Options

Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car from New Bern to Harkers Island.

How to Get Around in Harkers Island

Most visitors to Harkers Island prefer to drive their own vehicles to explore the island’s attractions. Bicycles and boats are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Harkers Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beaufort: This charming, historic town is located just a short drive away from Harkers Island and offers excellent restaurants, shopping, and sightseeing.

  • Atlantic Beach: This popular beach town is located just a few miles from Harkers Island and offers plenty of water sports, shopping, and dining options.

Conclusion

Harkers Island is a hidden gem in North Carolina that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. From beautiful beaches to historic sites and delicious local cuisine, this island should be on every traveler’s bucket list. Plan a trip to Harkers Island and experience the best that North Carolina has to offer!
