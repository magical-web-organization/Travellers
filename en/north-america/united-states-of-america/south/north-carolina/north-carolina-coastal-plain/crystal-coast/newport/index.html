Introduction to Newport

Located in the Crystal Coast of North Carolina, Newport is a charming town popular for its rich history, outdoor adventures, and stunning landscapes. Whether you are a history buff looking to explore the area’s museums and historical sites, or a nature lover seeking scenic vistas and outdoor activities, Newport has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay to help you plan the perfect trip to Newport, North Carolina.

List of Places to Visit in Newport

A trip to Newport is incomplete without visiting some of its most popular attractions. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Newport.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Newport Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history of Newport, NC through exhibits and artifacts, including Native American culture, colonial life, and early industries of the town.

  • Fort Macon State Park: Built in the mid-19th century, Fort Macon offers an insight into the state’s history during the Civil War. Visitors can explore the restored fort, enjoy the beach, and hike down the scenic trails.

Natural Attractions of Newport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Croatan National Forest: Covering over 160,000 acres, Croatan National Forest offers several biking and hiking trails, wildlife viewing opportunities, and swimming spots. The forest is only a short drive from Newport and is perfect for a day trip.

  • Emerald Isle Beach: This stunning beach is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying water sports such as surfing and paddleboarding. Visitors can also hike and bike on the nearby trails or go fishing along the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newport

Newport and its surrounding areas are full of amazing hiking and biking trails that range from easy to challenging.

  • Neusiok Trail: This 21-mile trail winds through the woodlands, swamps, and wetlands of Croatan National Forest. It includes several camping sites for overnight stays, and hikers can also observe the diverse flora and fauna of the region.

  • Bogue Banks Bike Path: This 11-mile path runs parallel to the beautiful beaches of Emerald Isle and includes several scenic overlooks and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Newport: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling solo or with family and friends, there is always something to do in Newport. Here are some of the best things to do in Newport.

  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Newport offers numerous opportunities to explore its waterways, including Bogue Sound and Newport River, via kayaks and paddleboards. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the coastline while paddling down the tranquil waters.

  • Fishing Charters: Take a guided fishing charter and explore the waters around Newport. Skilled guides can provide insights into the best fishing spots and techniques depending on the season.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newport

Here’s a sample itinerary for spending a week in Newport:

Food and Drink Scene of Newport

Newport offers a diverse culinary scene with dishes that combine fresh seafood and Southern cooking. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carolina-style Barbecue: A classic Carolina barbecue is a must-try dish during your trip to Newport. Restaurants serve pulled pork cooked over wood and served with a tangy vinegar-based sauce.

  • Crab Cakes: Newport’s seafood is a staple in the local cuisine, and crab cakes are a perfect representation of it. Battered and fried to perfection, they make for a delicious appetizer or main course.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Newport has several local breweries that produce their own craft beer with unique flavors. Some of the popular ones include Mill Whistle Brewing, Shortway Brewing, and Bogue Sound Distillery.

  • Sweet Tea: A refreshing glass of sweet tea is a staple in Southern cuisine, and Newport is no exception. Sweet tea pairs well with almost any meal and is a perfect thirst quencher.

Where to Stay in Newport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Newport has accommodations that cater to every budget and style. From luxurious resorts to cozy bed and breakfasts, here are some of the top places to stay in Newport.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Atlantic Beach: This hotel is located a short drive from Newport and offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

  • The Inn at Pine Knoll Shores: Located in Pine Knoll Shores, this bed and breakfast offers beautiful ocean views, private beach access, and a continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Newport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Newport has various local shops that offer unique souvenirs and products. Here are some of the best places to shop for souvenirs.

  • Buddy’s Crab House and Oyster Bar: Located in Atlantic City, Buddy’s Crab House and Oyster Bar offers a variety of fresh, local seafood. They also sell unique coastal-themed souvenirs, such as T-shirts, hats, and mugs.

Local Culture and Customs of Newport

Newport is known for its welcoming locals who take pride in their town’s history and natural beauty. Visitors are expected to be respectful and courteous towards the locals.

Best Time to Visit Newport

The best time to visit Newport is during the late spring and early fall when the weather is pleasant and the crowds are thinner. However, if you plan to visit during the peak season, book your accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newport

Do:

  • Respect the environment and dispose of any rubbish responsibly.

  • Carry water, sunscreen, and mosquito repellent when exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local flora and fauna.

  • Ignore safety rules when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newport

Newport is a safe town for travelers. However, travelers should take the usual precautions, such as locking your car and guarding your belongings when exploring crowded areas.

Health in Newport

Newport has several medical facilities that provide healthcare services to travelers. In case of an emergency, dial 911.

Getting to Newport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Newport is located an hour’s drive from Raleigh-Durham International Airport and is easily accessible via several modes of transportation. Here are some of the best ways to get to Newport:

Airports Near Newport

  • Raleigh-Durham International Airport: Located in Raleigh, NC, this airport is serviced by several major airlines and offers daily flights to several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Newport

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in nearby Jacksonville, this bus station offers bus services to several cities in North Carolina.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Rent a car and drive down to Newport. This option provides more flexibility and is ideal for exploring the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Newport

Newport is a walkable town, and visitors can easily explore the main attractions on foot. However, renting a car or a bike may be necessary for exploring other parts of the town.

Where to Go Next from Newport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newport is located near several popular destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best places to visit near Newport.

  • Beaufort: A charming seaside town with historic landmarks, delicious seafood, and a relaxed atmosphere.

  • Morehead City: A bustling coastal city with miles of beaches, exciting water sports, and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and diverse culinary scene, Newport has something for every traveler. Follow our comprehensive travel guide to explore the best of what Newport, North Carolina, has to offer.
