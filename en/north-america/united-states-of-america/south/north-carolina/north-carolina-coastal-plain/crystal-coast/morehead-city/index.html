Introduction to Morehead City

Morehead City, located in Crystal Coast, is a small coastal city in North Carolina Coastal Plain, South. Known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and rich history, Morehead City offers a unique travel experience for visitors. The city is home to several tourist attractions including museums, historical sites, hiking and biking trails, outdoor adventures, and more. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and how to get around in Morehead City, along with other important information for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Morehead City

Morehead City boasts a variety of tourist attractions, including culturally important landmarks, natural attractions with scenic views, and outdoor adventures. We’ve compiled a comprehensive list of must-visit places in Morehead City to make the most of your trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. History Place - Visitors can learn about the city’s rich history at the museum and see artifacts from the past.
  2. Morehead City Waterfront Museum - This museum is dedicated to the history of Morehead City, and coastal life and history.
  3. The North Carolina Maritime Museum - Boasts the maritime history of the state with exhibits on sea creatures and beach ecology.
  4. Fort Macon State Park - A historic U.S fort that dates back to the 19th century, offers guided tours and scenic views.

Natural Attractions of Morehead City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Atlantic Beach - One of the best beaches in North Carolina, Atlantic Beach is known for its pristine shoreline and turquoise waters.
  2. Radio Island Beach - This beach offers a calm and peaceful environment perfect for relaxation and long beach walks.
  3. Shackleford Banks - This undeveloped barrier island provides stunning views of its horses and unspoiled beaches.
  4. Crystal Coast Ecotours - You can take a guided tour through the island and explore barrier islands, estuaries, and marshes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morehead City

Hikers and bikers can find several scenic trails in Morehead City. From easy nature walks to moderate bike rides, here are some highly recommended routes.

  1. The Neusiok Trail - This is a 20.6-mile trail, known for its remote, scenic views and wildlife sightings.
  2. The Fort Macon Trail - This trail offers stunning views of the beach, dunes, and historic fort.
  3. Emerald Loop Trail - This is a 2.8-mile loop, offering picturesque landscapes with several lookout points and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Morehead City: Activities and Tours

Morehead City offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained throughout your trip, from water sports to cultural experiences.

  1. Alligator River National Wildlife Refuge – This is an ideal place for bird watching and hiking trails that go through the forest, also known for alligators breeding.
  2. Charter Fishing - Experience saltwater fishing in the deep-water or near-shore locations of the Atlantic Ocean with experienced captains.
  3. Crystal Coast Lady Cruises - This breathtaking tour offers scenic views of the Crystal Coast area from a boat with refreshments.

Week-Long Itinerary for Morehead City

If you’re planning to spend a week in Morehead City, here’s an itinerary that will help you make sure you make the most of your time.

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Morehead City, check into your hotel, and take a walk along the waterfront
Day 2 Explore Morehead City Waterfront Museum, and head over to Fort Macon State Park.
Day 3 Visit The North Carolina Maritime Museum and take a relaxing walk around the beach
Day 4 Go on a fishing charter from Atlantic Beach and make the most of adventure
Day 5 Take a guided tour of new islands, estuaries, and marshes through Crystal Coast Ecotours
Day 6 Visit the famous Crystal Coast Lady Cruises and take a tour down the pristine coastline
Day 7 Spend the day at the Radio Island Beach followed by your last dinner in Morehead City

Food and Drink Scene of Morehead City

For foodies, Morehead City has lots of enticing dining options, with a focus on seafood dishes. From traditional to modern cuisine, you’ll find an array of restaurants, bars, and breweries.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Calabash Seafood - This deep-fried seafood platter is a must-try, with crispy seafood accompanied by fries and coleslaw.
  2. Shrimp and grits - This is a southern favorite dish, usually made with shrimp served over cheese grits.
  3. Oysters on the Half Shell - Fresh oysters, shucked and served on the half shell, are a specialty of many seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  1. Sweet Tea - This is a southern classic, made with black tea and sweetened with sugar.
  2. Craft Beer - You will find several breweries in Morehead City with a focus on craft beer varieties.

Where to Stay in Morehead City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious beach resorts to budget-friendly guest houses, Morehead City has a variety of accommodations to suit your preferences and budget.

  1. The DoubleTree by Hilton - Luxurious beachfront hotel with all the amenities you need for a comfortable and exceptional stay.
  2. Bask Hotel at Big Rock Landing - This is an excellent option for those who prefer smaller, boutique hotels.
  3. Holiday Inn Express & Suites Morehead City - Budget-friendly, offering great value for money, Comfortable stay, and premium amenities.

What to Buy in Morehead City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Morehead City offers several unique and locally made items that make for perfect souvenirs. Here are some must-buy things from the city.

  1. Souvenir T-shirts and Magnets - You’ll find a variety of souvenir shops selling t-shirts, magnets, and other items with Morehead City and North Carolina Coastal Plain designs.
  2. Crystal Coast Seafood - Bring home souvenirs of the best seafood from seafood retail shops in the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Morehead City

Morehead City has a rich culture that reflects the history and traditions of its people. Some customs and etiquette that visitors should be mindful of when visiting the city includes dressing appropriately, respecting the history and heritage, and following basic courtesy while interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Morehead City

The best time to visit Morehead City is from May to October when the temperatures are warm and the city hosts various events and festivals related to the beach and its culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morehead City

Do:

  1. Wear sunscreen when visiting the beach and outdoor areas.
  2. Respect the city’s culture and heritage.
  3. Follow beach rules and regulations, including no littering.

Don’t:

  1. Litter- You can face up to $100 fine for litter on public beaches.
  2. Feed the horses on Shackleford Banks.
  3. Wait until the last minute to book hotels or activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morehead City

Morehead City is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take basic safety precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, always keep valuables close, be aware of your surroundings, and look both ways before crossing the street.

Health in Morehead City

While visiting Morehead City, it’s important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen for protection from the sun.

Getting to Morehead City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Morehead City is easily accessible via several transportation options.

Airports Near Morehead City

The closest airport to Morehead City is Coastal Carolina Regional Airport located in New Bern, North Carolina. Other nearby airports include:

  1. Pitt-Greenville Airport - 92 miles away
  2. Wilmington International Airport -118 miles away

Bus Stations Near Morehead City

The local bus station in Morehead City, known as the Morehead City Greyhound Bus Terminal, is easy to navigate for travelers coming in and out of the city.

Other Options

You can also travel via trains – nearest being in New Bern Amtrak Station around 35 miles away.

How to Get Around in Morehead City

Getting around Morehead City is easy and convenient. Public transportation includes the Morehead City Trolley and buses, and many attractions are within walking distance. You can also rent a car or bike to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Morehead City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Morehead City is just a short drive from several other cities, making it an ideal base for further exploring.

  1. Beaufort - A nearby historic town known for its fine antebellum architecture and charming waterfront, also home of the North Carolina Maritime Museum.
  2. Hammocks Beach State Park - This island is only accessible by ferry and is a peaceful getaway with stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Morehead City is a vibrant coastal city, offering something for everyone, from beach enthusiasts to history lovers. The city’s rich history, natural attractions, and vibrant culture make it an ideal destination for a vacation. With the tips and recommendations mentioned in this travel guide, you’re all set to have an unforgettable Morehead City experience.
