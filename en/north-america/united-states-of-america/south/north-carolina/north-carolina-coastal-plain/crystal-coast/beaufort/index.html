Introduction to Beaufort

Welcome to Beaufort, a charming town located in the Crystal Coast, North Carolina. Surrounded by water on three sides, Beaufort is famous for its picturesque waterfront, historic sites, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful beach vacation, a relaxing retreat, or an adventure-packed trip, Beaufort has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to do, places to stay, and food to eat in Beaufort.

List of Places to Visit in Beaufort

Here are some must-visit places in Beaufort:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Educate yourself on the history and culture of the town by visiting some of these historic sites:

  • North Carolina Maritime Museum
  • Beaufort Historic Site and Old Burying Ground
  • Beaufort Arsenal
  • Old Town Historic District
  • The Carteret County Courthouse

Natural Attractions of Beaufort: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Get up close and personal with the stunning natural beauty surrounding Beaufort:

  • Shackleford Banks
  • Rachel Carson Reserve
  • Cape Lookout National Seashore
  • NC Aquarium at Pine Knoll Shores
  • Fort Macon State Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beaufort

If you’re in the mood for some exercise and scenic views, you’ll find several hiking and biking trails in Beaufort:

Local Culture and Customs of Beaufort

Beaufort has a rich history that’s reflected in its architecture, culture, and traditions. It’s a small town that values community, hospitality, and southern charm.

Best Time to Visit Beaufort

The best time to visit Beaufort is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild, and the tourist crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beaufort

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture
  • Visit historic sites
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Explore the natural beauty of Beaufort

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Ignore the safety rules on the water
  • Be disrespectful to the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Beaufort

Beaufort is a relatively safe place to visit. Like any other coastal town, take precautions when swimming and enjoy the water safely.

Health in Beaufort

Beaufort has excellent medical facilities, and visitors are advised to have insurance coverage for medical expenses.

Getting to Beaufort: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beaufort

The closest airport to Beaufort is the Coastal Carolina Regional Airport in New Bern, which is approximately 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Beaufort

Greyhound Bus Lines has stops in Morehead City, which is just across the bridge from Beaufort.

Other Options

Beaufort is an easy town to navigate by car, bike, or foot, so renting a car or bike is a popular option. Local taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

How to Get Around in Beaufort

Beaufort is a small town with few traffic issues, so getting around is easy. Visit the historic district and waterfront areas on foot or bike. For attractions that are further away, a rental car or taxi service is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Beaufort: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Take day trips from Beaufort to nearby destinations like:

  • Harkers Island
  • Morehead City
  • Atlantic Beach
  • Ocracoke Island
  • New Bern

Conclusion

Beaufort is a hidden gem of the Crystal Coast, with its combination of history, culture, and natural beauty. There is no shortage of things to do and see in this charming town. Whether it’s your first visit or fiftieth, Beaufort will leave a lasting impression.
