If you’re looking for a small town in North Carolina with a big heart, Laurinburg is the perfect destination. Located in the Coastal Plain region of North Carolina, Laurinburg offers a rich history, cultural landmarks, natural beauty, and an array of outdoor activities.

Introduction to Laurinburg

Laurinburg is a city in Scotland County, North Carolina, with a population of just over 15,000 people. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, as well as its historic buildings and landmarks. The town is also home to St. Andrews University, a private liberal arts and sciences university.

List of Places to Visit in Laurinburg

There are plenty of things to do and see in Laurinburg. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • John Blue House and Heritage Center: A historic house and museum that showcases the history and culture of Scotland County.
  • Scottish Heritage Center: A museum that celebrates the history and heritage of Scotland County.
  • Storytelling and Arts Center of the Southeast: A cultural center where visitors can learn about the history and folklore of the region.

Natural Attractions of Laurinburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • James L. Morgan Recreation Complex: A park with baseball and soccer fields, a playground, and a walking trail.
  • Chalk Banks: A scenic stretch of the Lumber River, popular for kayaking and fishing.
  • Wagram Springs Nature Preserve: A park with 400 acres of hardwood forests, wetlands, and meadows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laurinburg

Exploring Laurinburg on foot or on a bicycle is a great way to discover the town’s natural beauty. Check out these hiking and biking routes:

  • Browning Creek Trail: A scenic trail through hardwood forests, along a creek.
  • John Blue Nature Trail: A 2.5-mile trail that winds through pine forests and open fields.
  • Lumber River State Park: A park with over 115 miles of hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Laurinburg: Activities and Tours

  • St. Andrews University: Take a campus tour of this picturesque liberal arts and sciences university.
  • Laurinburg-Maxton Airport: Go on a hot air balloon ride with Carolina BalloonFest.
  • 4D Farm: Visit this family-owned farm for hayrides, corn mazes, and fun activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Laurinburg

One of the best ways to experience Laurinburg’s local culture is through its food and drink scene.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork barbecue: A North Carolina classic, served with vinegar-based sauce.
  • Southern-style fried chicken: Crispy and juicy, served with sides like coleslaw and mac and cheese.
  • Sweet potato casserole: A savory and sweet side dish made with mashed sweet potatoes, butter, cinnamon, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: A classic Southern beverage, sweetened with sugar and served chilled.
  • Cheerwine: A cherry-flavored soda that originated in North Carolina.
  • Craft beer: Check out local breweries like Hugger Mugger Brewing Company and Squire’s Pub & Brewhouse.

Where to Stay in Laurinburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Laurinburg offers a variety of accommodations for all budgets:

  • Hampton Inn Laurinburg: A popular chain hotel with comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • Scotland Inn: A budget hotel with clean rooms and a friendly staff.
  • The Inn at Aberdeen: A cozy bed and breakfast with beautifully decorated rooms.

What to Buy in Laurinburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lumber River Trading Post: A gift shop that sells local handmade crafts and souvenirs.
  • The Berry Patch: A farmers’ market that sells local produce, jams, and jellies.
  • S&H Farm Supply: A store that sells seeds and gardening supplies.

Local Culture and Customs of Laurinburg

Laurinburg is a welcoming and friendly town that values community and tradition. Visitors should expect to encounter traditional Southern hospitality and a close-knit community.

Best Time to Visit Laurinburg

The best time to visit Laurinburg is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laurinburg

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the town’s parks and waterways.
  • Visit the town’s cultural landmarks and museums.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local culture and traditions.
  • Leave litter in the parks or natural areas.
  • Speed on the country roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laurinburg

Laurinburg is a safe town, but visitors should still take precautions like locking their cars and keeping their valuables safe.

Health in Laurinburg

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in and around Laurinburg, including Scotland Memorial Hospital and Betsy Johnson Hospital.

Getting to Laurinburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Laurinburg is located off Interstate 74 in eastern North Carolina, making it easily accessible by car.

Airports Near Laurinburg

  • Fayetteville Regional Airport (FAY): About 30 miles from Laurinburg.
  • Raleigh-Durham International Airport (RDU): About 95 miles from Laurinburg.

Bus Stations Near Laurinburg

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located about 3 miles from Laurinburg in nearby Maxton, NC.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station is located in Hamlet, NC, about 15 miles from Laurinburg.

How to Get Around in Laurinburg

The best way to get around in Laurinburg is by car or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Laurinburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pinehurst: A town known for its world-class golf courses and resort.
  • Wilmington: A historic coastal town with beautiful beaches and a vibrant downtown.
  • Raleigh: The state capital, with museums, shopping, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Laurinburg is a hidden gem of eastern North Carolina, offering visitors the chance to experience Southern charm, natural beauty, and history. Plan your trip today and discover all that this vibrant town has to offer!
