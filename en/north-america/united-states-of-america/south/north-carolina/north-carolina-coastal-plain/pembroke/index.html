Introduction to Pembroke

Nestled in the North Carolina Coastal Plain, Pembroke is a quaint town that offers plenty of things to see and do for travelers seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and historical exploration. The community is rich in Native American culture and history, and it features a vast array of museums, hiking, and biking trails, and scenic views. Pembroke is a haven for nature lovers, history buffs, and foodies who enjoy exploring new destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Pembroke

Whether you’re looking for a cultural, historical, outdoor experience, or a combination of all three, Pembroke has something for you. Here are some of the top places to visit in and around the town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Southeast American Indian: This museum celebrates the rich culture and traditions of the Lumbee tribe and other Native American tribes. Visitors can explore exhibits on Native American history, art, and customs.

  • Lumbee Homecoming: This annual event is one of the largest Native American gatherings in the country. Held each summer, the Lumbee Homecoming features a parade, pageants, and traditional music and dance performances.

  • Givens Performing Arts Center: This performing arts center regularly hosts concerts, plays, and lectures by local and national artists.

Natural Attractions of Pembroke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lumber River State Park: One of the best places to visit in Pembroke for nature lovers, this park is home to a variety of wildlife and provides visitors with opportunities for hiking, canoeing, and fishing.

  • Indian Heritage Trail: This scenic trail, located alongside the Lumbee River, stretches for 3.5 miles and provides visitors with opportunities for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

  • Carolina Horse Park: For equestrian enthusiasts, the Carolina Horse Park is a must-visit destination. Here, visitors can watch horse shows, take riding lessons, and explore the park’s hiking trails and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pembroke

Pembroke is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced adventurer, there’s a trail that suits your interests and abilities. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in and around Pembroke:

  • Indian Heritage Trail: A beautiful trail located alongside the Lumbee River and stretches for 3.5 miles.

  • Lumber River State Park: The park offers several trails that provide hikers and bikers with the chance to immerse themselves in nature while getting some exercise.

  • Pembroke Greenway Trail: This scenic route runs along the university campus and features a boardwalk that provides views of the surrounding forests and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Pembroke: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to explore in Pembroke. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, you’ll find something that suits your interests. Here are a few must-do activities to try in Pembroke:

  • Take a guided tour of the Museum of the Southeast American Indian.

  • Explore the Lumbee Homecoming, an annual event that attracts thousands of visitors each year.

  • Go hiking or biking on one of the many trails in and around Pembroke.

Food and Drink Scene of Pembroke

Pembroke is a small town that is home to a variety of local restaurants and bars that cater to different tastes and preferences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lumbee Fried Bread: This delicious treat is a staple of the Lumbee tribe’s diet. It is usually served with honey or butter and can be found in local restaurants and food trucks.

  • Homemade Sweet Potato Fries: This dish is a local favorite and can be found in many of the town’s restaurants.

  • Brunswick Stew: A traditional Southern dish made with vegetables, meat, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beers: Many of the restaurants and bars in Pembroke offer a range of craft beers from local breweries.

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple that can be found in most restaurants in town.

What to Buy in Pembroke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring home, Pembroke has plenty to offer. Here are some of the top products to buy in Pembroke:

Local Culture and Customs of Pembroke

The Lumbee tribe is an essential part of the culture and customs of Pembroke. Visitors should take the time to learn about the tribe’s history, traditions, and contributions to the town’s society. It is essential to respect the culture and customs of the community and show sensitivity towards their beliefs and values.

Best Time to Visit Pembroke

The best time to visit Pembroke is from April to June and September to November. The temperatures during these months are mild and pleasant, and there are fewer crowds compared to the peak summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pembroke

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the Lumbee tribe and other Native American tribes.

  • Take the time to learn about the history and culture of the town.

  • Treat the environment with care and kindness when hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Lumbee tribe and their customs and beliefs.

  • Leave trash behind when hiking or biking.

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
