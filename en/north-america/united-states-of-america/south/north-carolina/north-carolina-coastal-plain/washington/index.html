Introduction to Washington

Washington, located in the North Carolina Coastal Plain region of North Carolina, is a charming town with a rich history and a vibrant culture. Known for its beautiful natural attractions, Washington is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. From scenic hikes to scenic bike rides, there are plenty of opportunities to explore and appreciate the beauty of Washington.

List of Places to Visit in Washington

Washington is home to several attractions that are worth visiting. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Washington:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The North Carolina Estuarium - A museum dedicated to the ecology and history of the Estuarine System, with interactive exhibits, aquariums, and educational programs.

  • The Turnage Theatre - An intimate performing arts venue that offers a range of live performances, from musicals to plays to concerts.

  • The Historic Bath Site - A National Historic Landmark that features a recreated colonial village and the oldest church in North Carolina.

  • The Doc & Merle Watson Museum - A museum featuring exhibits and performances celebrating the life and music of Doc Watson and his son Merle, both North Carolina natives and renowned musicians.

Natural Attractions of Washington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Goose Creek State Park - offers various hiking trails, fishing and picnic facilities, as well as stunning views of Goose Creek.

  • Roanoke River National Wildlife Refuge - A vast array of flora and fauna species in an untouched natural wilderness. Perfect for bird-watching, hiking, and kayaking.

  • Pamlico River (Inner Banks Waterway) - perfect for fishing, kayaking, and enjoying the beautiful waterside views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Washington

Washington is known for its vast and beautiful natural landscapes. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the area:

  • The Hackney Trail – a serene woodland trail that passes multiple bodies of water and parks, covering a distance of approximately 5 miles, is perfect for serene picnics and family fun.

  • The South Creek Trail - A shady and peaceful trail that follows along South Creek; an excellent spot for fishing, kayaking, and nature tours.

Best Things to Do in Washington: Activities and Tours

Looking for things to do in Washington? Here are some of the top activities and tours to put on your itinerary:

  • Washington City Tour - A chance to learn about the history and culture of Washington while taking in its many sights.
  • Paddleboarding/Kayaking - A great opportunity to explore Washington from a unique perspective, especially with the numerous waterways and natural scenery.
  • Fishing Trips - Pamlico River is home to an abundance of fish species, and having the chance to catch them is a must-try experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Washington

If you’re planning to spend a week in Washington, you’re in for an unforgettable experience. Here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Washington

Washington offers a range of culinary delights that will satisfy any foodie. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carolina BBQ - a staple dish of North Carolina, especially during summer and fall seasons when everyone gathers to have barbeques.

  • Hushpuppies and seafood – These are classic American dishes made with cornmeal and seafood- creating a unique fusion, making it a must-try dish.

Local Beverages

  • Eastern North Carolina Wine Trail - Wineries like Ironclad Brewery, Occasions Winery and Bunn Vineyards offer a great wine-tasting experience, and have grown proficient over the years.

  • Brewery Tour - Mother Earth Brewing offers brewery tours that will enlighten you about the brewing process, evolution, and history of craft beer.

Where to Stay in Washington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Washington has a range of options for accommodation that will meet all your needs. Here are the top places to stay in Washington:

  • The Inn on Gray - Bed & breakfast type of accommodation that offers comfortable and enchanting guestrooms, along with a scrumptious complimentary breakfast.

  • The Pamlico House Bed & Breakfast - A must-visit Bed & Breakfast catering to guests, serving breakfast without interruptions while providing all the necessary amenities.

What to Buy in Washington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs to take home? Here are some suggestions:

  • Washington Scented Soaps - Local artisans create handmade soaps with the signature scent of Washington that you can bring home.

  • Local Art and Crafts - The town is famous for many natural resources, like local textiles, pottery, and other trinkets.

Local Culture and Customs of Washington

Washington has a diverse culture shaped by its rich history, natural beauty, and the warmth of its residents. Laid-back and friendly, residents of Washington are proud of their town’s heritage and the natural splendor around them. They’re welcoming and always happy to share their knowledge and love for the town with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Washington

The best time to visit Washington is either late spring or early summer, around May to June. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the flora and fauna are in full bloom. Fall can also be an excellent time to visit, as the autumn foliage makes for a picturesque landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Washington

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Washington:

Do:

  • Plan your trip early to avoid any last-minute rush and ensure availability at the best places.

  • Respect the local community and their traditions.

  • Enjoy the scenic trails, riverside views and the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural attractions in any way.

  • Disrespect any locals or their customs.

  • Break any rules or regulations.

Safety and Health

Washington is generally a safe town, but it’s always crucial to take precautions:

Safety in Washington

  • Stay aware and cautious of your surroundings at all times.

  • Secure your valuables and always lock up your accommodation.

  • Be careful while hiking or biking steep trails, keep your distance from wild animals and stay on marked trails at all times.

Health in Washington

It’s essential to stay hydrated and wear appropriate gear while exploring Washington. Beware of bugs and insect bites and consult a doctor if necessary. Also, be sure to follow all COVID-19 guidelines and restrictions to ensure your health and safety.

Getting to Washington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to help you plan your trip to Washington:

Airports Near Washington

The nearest airport to Washington is the Coastal Carolina Regional Airport, about 35 miles away in New Bern, NC.

Bus Stations Near Washington

The town is well-connected to several bus stations:

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Greenville, North Carolina - around 25 miles away from Washington.

  • All Points Bus Station - Located at Jacksonville, NC, around 35 miles away from Washington.

Other Options

A rental car or taxi may be the best option to travel to Washington from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Washington

The best way to explore Washington is by foot or bike, but driving is also an option. Bike rentals are available in the town for visitors who wish to explore the region surrounding Washington.

Where to Go Next from Washington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to explore nearby destinations? Here are some places to visit after Washington:

  • New Bern - 50 miles away and known for its rich history and colonial architecture.

  • Hatteras National Seashore - 150 miles away and known for its clear waters, exciting water sports and pristine white sand.

Conclusion

Washington is a perfect destination to appreciate the charm of small towns and the serene beauty of nature. The place offers something for everyone, whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or a foodie. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Washington, with all the essential information to make the most of your visit.
