Introduction to Southport

Southport is a charming, seaside town located in the North Carolina Coastal Plain, in the southeastern region of the United States. Known for its picturesque waterfront views, this town is a popular tourist destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy some peaceful relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Southport

Southport offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Johnston-Southport Museum and Visitors Center: Learn about the history of Fort Johnston and the town of Southport.

  • North Carolina Maritime Museum at Southport: This museum showcases the maritime heritage of the region and features exhibits on local shipwrecks and the seafood industry.

  • Old Brunswick County Jail: Built in 1904, this jail was used until 1971 and is now a museum depicting the conditions of prisoners at the time.

Natural Attractions of Southport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oak Island Beach: A beautiful spot for swimming, kayaking, or just relaxing on the sand.

  • Swamp Park: This zipline adventure park offers a thrilling way to explore the surrounding wetlands.

  • Southport Waterfront Park: Stroll along the picturesque riverfront, and enjoy the stunning views of the Cape Fear River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southport

Those looking to explore the outdoors will be pleased with the various hiking and biking routes available in Southport. Here’s a list of popular routes:

  • Brunswick Nature Park Trail: A popular hiking and biking trail that is well-maintained and offers scenic views.

  • Green Swamp Preserve: A 15,000-acre nature preserve that is home to a variety of wildlife.

  • Fort Fisher State Recreation Area: This area offers both hiking and biking trails, as well as stunning beaches.

Best Things to Do in Southport: Activities and Tours

Southport offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here’s a list of some of the best:

  • Carolina Beach State Park Kayak Tour: A two-hour guided kayak tour of the Carolina Beach State Park.

  • Southport Downtown Ghost Walk: A two-hour guided walking tour of Southport’s most haunted spots.

  • Bald Head Island Golf Course: A world-class golf course located on Bald Head Island, accessible by ferry.

Food and Drink Scene of Southport

Southport offers a variety of dining options, from seafood to BBQ. Here’s a list of must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and Grits: This classic Southern dish can be found on many menus throughout Southport.

  • Carolina BBQ: North Carolina is known for its BBQ, and Southport is no exception.

  • Calabash Seafood: Calabash-style seafood is a local specialty, and is known for its lightly battered fried seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Fear Wine: This local winery produces a variety of wines, using grapes grown in North Carolina.

  • Wrightsville Beach Brewing: This brewery offers a range of craft beers, perfect for a relaxing afternoon by the riverfront.

Where to Stay in Southport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southport offers a range of accommodations, from budget-friendly options to luxurious hotels. Here’s a list of some popular options:

  • The Spouter Inn Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in the heart of Southport, and offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

  • Hampton Inn Southport: This hotel is located right on the riverfront, and offers stunning views of the Cape Fear River.

  • Airlie Gardens: This historic guesthouse is located just outside of Southport, and is known for its beautiful gardens and peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Southport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southport has a variety of unique shops and stores, offering a range of souvenirs and local products. Here’s a list of popular options:

  • Southport Trading Company: This store offers a range of local products, including jams, jellies, and hot sauces.

  • Old Southport Village Shoppes: This collection of stores offers a range of unique gifts and souvenirs, including handmade jewelry and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Southport

Southport is a friendly town, known for its Southern charm and laid-back atmosphere. When visiting, it’s important to be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Southport

Southport can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and there are fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southport

Do:

  • Do explore the outdoors: Southport is known for its beautiful parks and nature reserves, so be sure to take advantage.

  • Do try the local cuisine: Southport is known for its delicious seafood and BBQ, so be sure to indulge.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Keep Southport’s natural beauty intact by disposing of trash properly.

  • Don’t rush: Southport is a laid-back town, so take your time and enjoy the slow pace.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southport

Southport is a safe town, but as with any destination, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Be aware of your surroundings, and be cautious when walking alone at night.

Health in Southport

Southport has several medical facilities, including Brunswick Novant Medical Center, and several urgent care clinics.

Getting to Southport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southport

The closest major airport to Southport is Wilmington International Airport, located about 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Southport

Greyhound Bus Lines has a bus station in Wilmington, which is located about 30 minutes away from Southport.

Other Options

Southport is easily accessible by car. If traveling from the north, take I-95 south to US-17, and follow US-17 south to Southport. If traveling from the south, take I-95 north to I-40, and then take US-17 south to Southport.

How to Get Around in Southport

Southport is a small town, and is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. For those who prefer other modes of transportation, there are several car rental companies in the area, as well as a local taxi service.

Where to Go Next from Southport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southport is located near several other popular destinations, including Wilmington, Myrtle Beach, and Bald Head Island.

Conclusion

Southport, North Carolina, offers a peaceful oasis for those looking to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the area. With its charming downtown, scenic waterfront, and range of outdoor activities, Southport has something to offer everyone. So why not pack your bags and come enjoy this charming coastal town for yourself?
