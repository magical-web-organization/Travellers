Boone is a small town nestled in the mountains of North Carolina, offering a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions. With a population of less than 20,000, Boone offers a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere for tourists looking to getaway. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your visit to Boone, including a list of places to visit, hiking/biking routes, activities and tours, where to stay, and more!

Introduction to Boone

Boone is a small town located in the western part of North Carolina. Its elevation of over 3,000 feet above sea level makes it a refreshing destination for tourists looking to escape the heat of the city. Boone has a rich history, with roots dating back to the 18th century. The town itself is situated in the Appalachian Mountains, which offers plenty of outdoor activities for tourists visiting the area.

List of Places to Visit in Boone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, Boone has plenty to offer in the form of museums and historical places. Here are a few of the top options:

  • The Appalachian Cultural Museum: This museum offers a comprehensive look at the cultural heritage of the Southern Appalachians. You can learn about traditional crafts, music, and folkways, as well as explore exhibits on local history.
  • The Hickory Ridge Living History Museum: This museum is a living history museum that presents the heritage and culture of the Southern Appalachians through hands-on experiences, exhibits, and workshops.
  • Daniel Boone Native Gardens: This garden is a beautiful tribute to the Native American cultures that once flourished in the area. Walking through the garden, you’ll experience both the beauty and history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Boone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Boone is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities. Here are some of the top options to consider during your stay:

  • Blue Ridge Parkway: This scenic highway provides breathtaking views of the Appalachian Mountains. You can take a drive along the 469-mile parkway, or stop at one of the many overlooks and hiking trails.
  • Grandfather Mountain: Visitors to Grandfather Mountain can enjoy hiking, wildlife exhibits, and stunning views from the Mile High Swinging Bridge.
  • Linville Gorge and Falls: Hiking trails through the gorge offer stunning views of rugged terrain and the Linville River, as well as the famous Linville Falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boone

Boone offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels, from easy family-friendly trails to challenging routes for experienced hikers. Whether you want a leisurely stroll or a challenging hike, Boone has something for everyone. Here are some popular routes:

  • Tweetsie Trail: This 10-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk, and passes through some of the most scenic parts of Boone.
  • Moses Cone Park: This park offers over 25 miles of carriage trails with easy to moderate hikes through peaceful woods and beautiful meadows.
  • Rough Ridge Trail: This trail is a popular, moderate hike that takes you through scenic forests and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Boone: Activities and Tours

Boone offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained during your stay. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, there is something here for everyone:

  • Zipline tours: Fly through the trees with one of the many zipline tours in the area.
  • Skiing and snowboarding in winter: Boone’s slopes are perfect for skiing and snowboarding in the winter months.
  • Downtown Boone: Wander through the quaint streets of Downtown Boone, with its unique shops and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boone

Day 1: Explore the Appalachian Cultural Museum and Daniel Boone Native Gardens.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive on the Blue Ridge Parkway, stopping at overlooks and hiking trails along the way.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Grandfather Mountain, and cross the Mile High Swinging Bridge for stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Day 4: Bike the Tweetsie Trail or hike the Moses Cone Park Carriage Trails for a peaceful and scenic day.

Day 5: Go on a zipline tour for a thrilling adventure in the trees.

Day 6: Visit Downtown Boone for a unique shopping and dining experience.

Day 7: Take a relaxing day at your hotel and enjoy the amenities or visit a local spa for some pampering.

Food and Drink Scene of Boone

Boone has a range of dining options to suit any taste. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: North Carolina is known for its barbecue, and Boone is no exception.
  • Biscuits and gravy: Another Southern staple, be sure to try biscuits and gravy at one of Boone’s local diners.
  • Sweet potato fries: A regional specialty, try them at one of the many restaurants in Downtown Boone.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Boone has several breweries, including Booneshine Brewing Co. and Lost Province Brewing Co., where you can try local craft beers.
  • Cider: Boone also has several cideries, including Doc’s Cider and Lost Province Cider Co.

Where to Stay in Boone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Boone has a range of accommodations to suit any budget or preference. Here are a few options:

  • Mast Farm Inn: This historic inn offers cozy rooms and suites on a scenic property.
  • The Horton Hotel & Rooftop Lounge: This chic boutique hotel is located in Downtown Boone and offers a rooftop lounge with stunning views.
  • Valle Crucis Log Cabin Rentals: Experience the rustic charm of log cabin rentals in the heart of the mountains.

What to Buy in Boone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Boone has a range of specialty shops where you can find unique gifts and souvenirs to bring home with you. Here are a few options:

  • Mast General Store: This historic store offers a selection of local crafts, clothing, and souvenirs.
  • Appalachian Antiques: This antique store has an extensive collection of vintage items and mountain memorabilia.
  • Stick Boy Bread Company: Pick up some freshly-baked bread or pastries from this local bakery.

Local Culture and Customs of Boone

Boone is a small town with a rich cultural history. The locals are known for their hospitality and friendliness. When visiting, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Boone

The best time to visit Boone is during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the air is crisp and cool. However, Boone is a year-round destination, with plenty of activities and events to enjoy no matter the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boone

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the scenic hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disturb wildlife in their natural habitat.

Safety and Health

When visiting Boone, safety and health should always be a priority. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Boone

  • Follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of the weather and any potential hazards.
  • Exercise caution when driving on mountain roads.

Health in Boone

  • Bring bug spray to avoid ticks and mosquitoes.
  • Stay hydrated, especially when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of any allergies and bring necessary medications.

Getting to Boone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Boone is located in the western part of North Carolina, approximately two hours from Charlotte and three hours from Raleigh. Here are some options for getting to Boone:

Airports Near Boone

  • Charlotte Douglas International Airport
  • Asheville Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Boone

  • Boone Cabs
  • Greyhound Bus Lines

Other Options

  • Driving: Boone is easily accessible by car, with several highways running through the area.
  • Train: The nearest Amtrak station is in Charlottesville, Virginia, approximately two hours from Boone.

How to Get Around in Boone

Boone is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several taxi and shuttle services available if needed.

Where to Go Next from Boone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore more of the area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Asheville: This nearby city is known for its vibrant art scene and rich history.
  • Blowing Rock: This charming town is home to the famous Blowing Rock attraction.
  • Linville Falls: Enjoy hiking trails with stunning views of the falls and surrounding mountains.

Conclusion

Boone is a gem of a town, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. From scenic drives on the Blue Ridge Parkway to zipline tours through the trees, there is something for everyone in Boone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and make the most out of your time in this charming mountain town.
