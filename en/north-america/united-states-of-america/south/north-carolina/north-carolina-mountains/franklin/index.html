Introduction to Franklin

Nestled in the North Carolina Mountains, Franklin is a charming town known for its natural beauty and small-town charm. With its scenic mountains, stunning waterfalls, and rich cultural heritage, Franklin is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture seekers alike. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, and safety and health tips for Franklin.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the rich heritage and history of Franklin, make sure to visit these culturally important attractions:

  • The Scottish Tartans Museum: showcases the history and culture of Scottish tartans, kilts, and clans.
  • Macon County Historical Museum: exhibits artifacts and documents that date back to the early 19th century, showcasing the history of Franklin and Macon County.
  • The Cowee Mountain Ruby Mine: offers visitors the chance to experience the thrill of gem mining and discover rubies, sapphires, and other precious stones.

Natural Attractions of Franklin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love outdoor adventures, Franklin has plenty of natural attractions to offer. Here are some of the top outdoor destinations to visit:

  • The Nantahala National Forest: offers visitors the chance to hike, bike, fish, and camp in a stunning mountain landscape.
  • The Appalachian Trail: runs through Franklin, and offers hikers a challenging 2,200-mile trek from Georgia to Maine.
  • The Cullasaja Falls: a stunning waterfall near Franklin, perfect for hiking and taking scenic photographs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin

Franklin is a hiker’s paradise, thanks to its proximity to the Appalachian Trail, Nantahala National Forest, and other natural areas. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes to check out:

  • Appalachian Trail: a challenging, 2,200 mile trek from Georgia to Maine with a section that runs through Franklin.
  • Bartram Trail: a 100 mile trail that winds through the Nantahala National Forest and showcases some of the best views in the area.
  • Siler Bald Trail: a moderate 4.2-mile hike to a summit with breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Franklin: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Franklin. Here are some of the best:

  • Gem Mining: experience the thrill of discovering beautiful rubies and sapphires at one of Franklin’s gem mines.
  • Kayaking and Rafting: take a thrilling trip down the Nantahala River, which is perfect for kayaking and rafting.
  • Scenic Drives: drive along the winding mountain roads, taking in stunning views of the forests, lakes, and waterfalls.

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin

If you’re planning to visit Franklin for a week, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Scottish Tartans Museum and the Macon County Historical Museum

3 Take a scenic drive through the Nantahala National Forest

7 Wind down with a relaxing spa day at one of Franklin’s day spas

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin

Must-Try Dishes

Franklin has plenty of delicious Southern cuisine to try. Here are a few of the must-try dishes:

  • Fried Chicken: try a classic Southern dish at one of Franklin’s many restaurants.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: a hearty breakfast dish that is a staple of the South.
  • BBQ: Franklin is known for its flavorful and tender BBQ meats.

Local Beverages

In addition to delicious food, Franklin has some great local beverages to try, such as:

  • Craft Beer: Franklin has several local breweries that offer a wide variety of craft beers.
  • Apple Cider: Franklin is known for its fresh, locally-sourced apple cider.
  • Moonshine: take a tour of Franklin’s local moonshine distilleries and sample some of the best flavors in the area.

Where to Stay in Franklin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Franklin has plenty of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from upscale hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Franklin Terrace Bed and Breakfast: a historic bed and breakfast with modern amenities and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Hampton Inn: a popular chain hotel that offers amenities such as free breakfast and a pool.
  • Sapphire Inn: a budget-friendly option that offers clean, comfortable rooms and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Franklin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Franklin, you’ll want to bring home some souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best:

  • Gemstones: bring home a piece of Franklin’s gem mining heritage with a beautiful ruby or sapphire.
  • Moonshine: Franklin’s local moonshine distilleries produce some of the best flavors around.
  • Local Crafts: visit the many artisan shops and pick up handmade pottery, woodcrafts, and other beautiful local artworks.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin

Franklin has a rich cultural history that includes traditions and customs that are unique to the area. Some of the prominent cultural practices include:

  • Music: traditional country music and bluegrass concerts can be seen at many local venues.
  • Southern Hospitality: the people of Franklin are known for their friendliness and welcoming attitude towards visitors.
  • Gem Mining: Franklin’s gem mining history is a unique and important part of the town’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Franklin

The best time to visit Franklin is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the natural beauty of the area is at its peak. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be unpredictable with snow and ice.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin

Do:

  • Do hike or bike the Appalachian Trail for a stunning mountain experience.
  • Do visit the gem mining facilities and try your luck digging for rubies and other precious stones.
  • Do take in the local culture by attending a traditional country music concert.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the area.
  • Don’t ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities like hiking and rafting.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers when dining out in Franklin.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin

While Franklin is generally a safe town, visitors should take precautions when venturing out into the mountains. Here are some tips:

  • Don’t hike alone; always go with a group or a guide.
  • Check the weather forecast before heading out on a hike or other outdoor adventure.
  • Always wear proper safety gear for outdoor activities like kayaking and rafting.

Health in Franklin

As with any travel destination, visitors to Franklin should take steps to ensure their health and safety. Here are some tips:

  • Apply sunscreen and insect repellent when outdoors, and stay hydrated.
  • Be aware of the local flora and fauna - poisonous snakes, spiders, and plants are common in the area.
  • If you do experience any health concerns, seek medical attention at a local hospital or clinic.

Getting to Franklin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Franklin is easy, thanks to its good road connections to other cities in the area. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Franklin

The nearest major airports to Franklin are the Asheville Regional Airport and the Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Franklin

The closest bus station to Franklin is the Greyhound station in Asheville.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Franklin is located off of US Highway 64 and North Carolina Highway 28, which offer easy access to other cities in the area.

How to Get Around in Franklin

The best way to get around Franklin is by car, as many of the natural and cultural attractions are spread out throughout the area. If you’re staying in downtown Franklin, you can also walk to many of the nearby shops, restaurants, and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Franklin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other destinations in the area, here are some nearby towns and cities to consider:

  • Asheville, North Carolina: a bustling city with great shops, restaurants, and outdoor activities.
  • Cherokee, North Carolina: home to the Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians and the Harrah’s Cherokee Casino.
  • Greenville, South Carolina: a vibrant city famous for its food scene and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Franklin, North Carolina Mountains is a must-see destination for lovers of nature, history, and culture. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm Southern hospitality, Franklin offers visitors a unique and unforgettable travel experience. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to Franklin and make the most of your time in this charming town.
