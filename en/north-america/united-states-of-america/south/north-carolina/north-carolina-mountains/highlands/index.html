Introduction to Highlands

Highlands is a charming mountain town located in the western region of North Carolina. Surrounded by breathtaking landscapes, Highlands is a perfect destination for those seeking outdoor adventures, scenic views, and a glimpse into local culture and history. Whether you want to go hiking and biking, explore cultural attractions, or indulge in local cuisine and products, Highlands has something for everyone.

In this travel guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive itinerary for your trip to Highlands, including a list of places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local customs and safety tips. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Highlands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the history and culture of the area, make sure to visit these important cultural attractions in Highlands:

  • Highlands Historical Society Museum: Located in the Historic Highlands House, the museum showcases the rich history of Highlands from Native American settlements to modern-day.
  • The Bascom: A visual arts center that exhibits local and international artists.
  • Highlands Playhouse: A community theater that hosts musicals, plays, and film screenings.

Natural Attractions of Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Highlands is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are plenty of activities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some must-visit natural attractions in Highlands:

  • Whiteside Mountain: Known for its stunning panoramic views and hiking trails.
  • Dry Falls: A 65-foot waterfall that visitors can walk behind.
  • Glen Falls: A triple waterfall with trails for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Sunset Rock: An iconic spot to view sunsets and enjoy the scenic beauty of Highlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Highlands

Highlands offers a plethora of hiking and biking opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular trails:

  • Whiteside Mountain Trail: A 2.5-mile hike with scenic views of the surrounding area.
  • Shortoff Mountain Trail: A 6.4-mile hike with views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
  • Highlands Greenway Trail: A 2.1-mile paved trail for walking, running, or biking.
  • Kelsey Trail: A 2.6-mile hike that passes by waterfalls and scenic overlooks.

Best Things to Do in Highlands: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking trails, Highlands offers several unique activities and tours that are worth exploring. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Highlands Food and Wine Festival: A four-day festival in November that features culinary events, wine tastings, and live entertainment.
  • Highlands Village Square Art & Craft Show: A two-day event in August featuring local artists and artisans.
  • Wildwater Ziplines: A zipline tour with six ziplines and several sky bridges.
  • Highlands Aerial Park: An adventure park with ropes courses, ziplines, and other activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Highlands

For a memorable week in Highlands, follow this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Highlands and explore the town’s historic sites and museums.
  • Day 2: Go hiking at Whiteside Mountain or check out the nearby waterfalls.
  • Day 3: Plan a rafting trip or zip-lining adventure in the morning, and walk the Highlands Greenway Trail in the afternoon.
  • Day 4: Take a scenic drive on the Blue Ridge Parkway and visit the Cherokee Indian Reservation.
  • Day 5: Explore the arts and crafts vendors at Highlands Village Square Art & Craft Show.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a round of golf at Old Edwards Golf Club or visit Highlands Botanical Garden.
  • Day 7: Relax at a local spa or take a cooking class at one of the area’s culinary schools.

Food and Drink Scene of Highlands

Highlands is a culinary destination with a robust food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trout: A staple in southern cuisine, trout is a delicious freshwater fish found in local rivers and streams.
  • Fried green tomatoes: This southern classic features lightly breaded and fried green tomatoes.
  • BBQ: North Carolina is famous for its BBQ, and Highlands is no exception.

Local Beverages

  • Moonshine: A distilled spirit that was once illegal, moonshine is now produced legally in the area and can be found at several local distilleries.
  • Craft beer: Highlands has several microbreweries that produce excellent craft beer.
  • Wine: The area boasts several vineyards that produce award-winning wines, and Highlands hosts an annual Food and Wine Festival.

Where to Stay in Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Highlands has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some top choices:

  • Old Edwards Inn: A luxury hotel with a spa, golf course, and restaurants.
  • Half-Mile Farm: A charming bed and breakfast with a swimming pool and beautiful gardens.
  • Highlands Inn Lodge: A budget-friendly option with air-conditioned rooms and complimentary breakfast.
  • Airbnb: Highlands has several Airbnb accommodations that range from cozy tiny homes to luxurious villas.

What to Buy in Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home some local souvenirs or products, be sure to check out these places:

  • Highlands Chamber of Commerce Visitor Center: The Visitor Center has several souvenirs, including T-shirts, coffee mugs, and postcards.
  • Mountain Fresh Grocery: A local grocery store with fresh produce, meats, and cheeses.
  • House of Flags: A store that sells American and international flags, including North Carolina’s state flag.

Local Culture and Customs of Highlands

Highlands is home to a friendly and welcoming community with a strong cultural identity. Here are some cultural customs and etiquette tips to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Southern hospitality is a hallmark of the area, so be prepared for friendly greetings and warm welcomes.
  • If you’re visiting in the summer months, be prepared for hot and humid weather.
  • Respecting local customs and traditions, such as attending church and participating in community events, is highly appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Highlands

The best time to visit Highlands is during the fall or spring months when the weather is cooler and less humid. The town’s fall foliage is a beautiful sight to behold, and springtime brings with it colorful blooms and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Highlands

Do:

  • Do take advantage of outdoor activities and explore the beautiful scenery.
  • Do check out local festivals and events.
  • Do indulge in the area’s culinary delights and local produce.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Highlands

Highlands is a safe town, but it’s always best to take precautions when exploring the outdoors. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • When hiking, make sure to stay on designated trails and avoid going off-trail.
  • Wear appropriate footwear and clothing for outdoor adventures.
  • Be aware of wildlife, including bears and rattlesnakes, and follow guidelines for safely encountering them.

Health in Highlands

Highlands has several medical facilities in the area, including Highlands-Cashiers Hospital and Mission Health Highlands. Be sure to pack any necessary prescription medication and sunscreen to protect against the sun’s harmful rays.

Getting to Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Highlands

The nearest airports to Highlands are:

  • Asheville Regional Airport (AVL)
  • Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport (GSP)

Bus Stations Near Highlands

The closest bus station is the Greyhound Bus Station in Asheville, which is approximately 60 miles away from Highlands.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient option for traveling within Highlands, but there are also several taxi and car services available in the area.

How to Get Around in Highlands

Driving is the best way to get around Highlands, but several hiking and biking trails are accessible from the town center, and walking is a viable option for exploring the town’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels after Highlands, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Asheville, North Carolina: A city known for its art scene, breweries, and outdoor activities.
  • Cashiers, North Carolina: A small mountain town with several hiking trails and outdoor adventures.
  • Nantahala National Forest: A stunning natural area with hiking and biking trails, waterfalls, and lakes.

Conclusion

Highlands, North Carolina, is a hidden gem in the western region of the state. With its natural beauty, outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and culinary delights, Highlands has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip and discover everything this charming town has to offer. Happy travels!
