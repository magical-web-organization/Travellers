Introduction to Weaverville

Nestled in the North Carolina Mountains is the quaint town of Weaverville. If you’re seeking a serene getaway or outdoor adventure, Weaverville is a must-visit destination. This charming town boasts a rich history, stunning scenery, and a variety of activities to suit every kind of traveler. In this comprehensive Weaverville travel guide, we’ll cover the best places to visit, things to do, and insider travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Weaverville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Weaverville Art Safari: One of the most popular annual events in Weaverville, Art Safari is a self-guided tour of artist studios and galleries in the area. Meet the artists, view their works, and participate in workshops and demonstrations.
  • Reems Creek Golf Club: This public golf course is situated in the stunning Blue Ridge Mountains, offering scenic views and challenging terrain.
  • The Vance Birthplace: This historic site commemorates the birthplace of Civil War Governor Zebulon Vance. Explore the restored farmstead and learn about the history of the Vance family and their role in North Carolina’s past.
  • The Barnwell House: This historic house museum displays artifacts and furnishings from the Victorian era, giving visitors a glimpse into life in Weaverville during the mid-1800s.

Natural Attractions of Weaverville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Ridge Parkway: Take a scenic drive along this famous roadway and experience breathtaking views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.
  • Jack’s Knob Trail: This moderate hiking trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.
  • Reems Creek Valley: Enjoy a leisurely stroll or bike ride along the paved greenway that runs through the picturesque Reems Creek Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Weaverville

Weaverville and its surrounding areas are home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in North Carolina. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Mount Mitchell Trail: This challenging 5.6-mile trail climbs to the summit of Mount Mitchell, the highest peak in the eastern United States.
  • Craggy Pinnacle Trail: This moderate trail offers stunning views of the Blue Ridge Mountains from the summit of Craggy Pinnacle.
  • French Broad River Greenway: This 2.8-mile paved trail follows the picturesque French Broad River and is great for walkers, runners, and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Weaverville: Activities and Tours

  • Weaverville Tailgate Market: Shop for local produce, crafts, and artisanal goods at this vibrant outdoor market.
  • The Blue Ridge Craft Trails: Discover the wealth of handmade arts and crafts that North Carolina’s Blue Ridge Mountains has to offer by joining one of the guided tours.
  • Asheville Urban Trail: Explore Asheville’s rich history and architecture on this self-guided walking tour through the city’s downtown area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Weaverville

Day 1: Spend the day exploring downtown Weaverville, including the historic Barnwell House and bookstores.

Day 2: Enjoy a round of golf at Reems Creek Golf Club, followed by a hike along the nearby Jack’s Knob Trail.

Day 3: Visit the Vance Birthplace and learn about North Carolina’s Civil War history.

Day 4: Take a scenic drive along the famous Blue Ridge Parkway.

Day 5: Shop for local produce and handmade crafts at the Weaverville Tailgate Market.

Day 6: Experience the Asheville Urban Trail and discover Asheville’s rich history and architecture.

Day 7: Head out to explore the nearby towns of Black Mountain and Mars Hill.

Food and Drink Scene of Weaverville

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Glass Onion: This Weaverville staple serves up classic Southern comfort food like fried chicken and mac and cheese.
  • Well-Bred Bakery: Known for their homemade bread and pastries, this bakery also serves sandwiches and deli salads.
  • Zebulon Artisan Ales: Sample craft beers made on-site at this Weaverville brewery.

Local Beverages

  • Juicebox: This juice and smoothie bar is also a great spot for coffee and light breakfast.
  • Mack Daddy Coffee Roasters: Get your caffeine fix and enjoy roasted in-house coffee.

Where to Stay in Weaverville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bridgeton House: This charming bed and breakfast is located downtown and features hand-carved wood details and antiques.
  • The Mountain Inn and Suites: Affordable and conveniently located, this hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms with mountain views.

What to Buy in Weaverville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Weaverville Art Safari souvenirs: Pick up unique handmade arts and crafts from the Weaverville Art Safari event.
  • Brown’s Pottery: This family-owned pottery shop has been creating unique, handcrafted pottery for over 30 years.

Local Culture and Customs of Weaverville

Weaverville is known for its strong sense of community, love of the arts, and appreciation for nature. Be sure to check out local events like Art Safari, and make time to explore the surrounding natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Weaverville

Weaverville is a year-round destination, but fall is especially beautiful with the colorful foliage and cooler temperatures. Summer is also popular for outdoor activities, while winter offers skiing and snowboarding opportunities in nearby mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Weaverville

Do:

  • Do enjoy the outdoor activities from hiking to golfing.
  • Do visit the local coffee shops and bakeries.
  • Do take part in the local events, festivals, and markets.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in natural areas.
  • Don’t be noisy and respect your neighbors and other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Weaverville

Weaverville is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always a good idea to exercise caution when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, and take steps to protect your belongings and avoid unsafe areas.

Health in Weaverville

No special precautions are needed for health in Weaverville, but visitors should still be mindful of sun exposure and stay hydrated during hot weather.

Getting to Weaverville: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Weaverville: The nearest airport is Asheville Regional Airport (AVL), which is about 25 miles from Weaverville.
  • Bus Stations Near Weaverville: Greyhound Bus Lines serves the Asheville area, and there are several bus stops in Weaverville.
  • Other Options: Visitors can also arrive in Weaverville by car or by taking a scenic drive through the Blue Ridge Mountains.

How to Get Around in Weaverville

The best way to get around Weaverville is by car, as many of the area’s attractions are spread out. However, visitors can also bike or hike to local destinations. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Weaverville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asheville: The vibrant city of Asheville is a short drive away and offers great dining, shopping, and entertainment options.
  • Mars Hill and Black Mountain: Two nearby towns known for their beautiful natural settings, historic downtown areas, and quirky local shops.

Conclusion

Weaverville is a true gem in the North Carolina Mountains, offering stunning natural scenery, rich history, and a vibrant cultural scene. Use this Weaverville travel guide to plan your visit and ensure that you don’t miss out on any of the town’s must-visit attractions. Happy travels!
