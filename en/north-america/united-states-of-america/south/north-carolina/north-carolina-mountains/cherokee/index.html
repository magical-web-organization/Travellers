Introduction to Cherokee

Nestled in the heart of the North Carolina Mountains, Cherokee is a small town steeped in Native American history and culture. The Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians has called this place home for centuries and today, visitors can explore the rich traditions and heritage of this unique community. With natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural museums, and exciting activities, Cherokee offers a perfect blend of entertainment, relaxation, and education to its visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Cherokee

There are a ton of attractions to explore in Cherokee. Listed below are the top places to visit organized by categories:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Cherokee Indian: This museum showcases the 11,000-year history of Cherokee, including the tribe’s pre-Colonial era, Trail of Tears, and modern-day life. The museum has interactive exhibits, artifacts, and a theater room with short films about Cherokee history and culture.
  • Oconaluftee Indian Village: Located in a recreated 18th-century Cherokee village setting, this attraction offers demonstrations, workshops, and performances which highlight the culture of Cherokee people.
  • Qualla Arts and Crafts Mutual: This historic cooperative was established in 1946 as an attempt to preserve the traditional crafts and skills of Cherokee craftsmen and women. Today, the co-op offers handmade crafts and art representing traditional and contemporary Cherokee culture.

Natural Attractions of Cherokee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: With over 800 square miles of natural beauty, the park is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. Offering several trails for hiking, camping, and rock climbing, the park also has picnic areas and scenic drives.
  • Mingo Falls: A stunning 120-foot waterfall, Mingo Falls is one of the most popular natural attractions in Cherokee. Visitors can reach Mingo Falls via a short hike along a scenic path.
  • Blue Ridge Parkway: A 469-mile stretch of road through the Appalachians, the parkway provides some of the most beautiful vistas in the entire region. Visitors can enjoy scenic drives, visit natural attractions, and stop at overlooks for a panoramic view of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cherokee

Cherokee is a treasure trove of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Mile High Swinging Bridge Trail: Offering breathtaking views of the Great Smoky Mountains, this 0.6-mile trail leads to the iconic Mile High Swinging Bridge.
  • Kephart Prong Trail: With 4.2 miles of wooded trail, this hiking route offers a moderate challenge and leads to beautiful scenic views and a waterfall.
  • Tsa’lagi Trails: This trail system offers something for everyone, from beginners to seasoned hikers. With over 30 miles of trails, visitors can opt for a short or long hike, and can choose from levels of difficulty that suits their experience and ability.

Best Things to Do in Cherokee: Activities and Tours

Cherokee offers an abundance of fun-filled activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Cherokee:

  • Rafting in the Nantahala River: This thrilling activity is perfect for river rafting enthusiasts. Challenging rapids provide a thrilling experience while admiring the stunning scenery!
  • Cherokee Bonfire Experience: One of the unique experiences you can have while in Cherokee is gathering around a bonfire for storytelling, music, and s’mores. This is a great activity for the family to wind down and relax after a busy day of exploring.
  • Cherokee Indian Reservation Qualla Arts and Crafts Mutual Tour: Visitors can go on a guided tour of this historic cooperative and engage with the craftsmen and women who are keeping traditional arts and crafts alive.

Food and Drink Scene of Cherokee

Foodies rejoice! Cherokee has a food scene that’s worth exploring. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Cherokee:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry bread: A classic of Cherokee cuisine, Fry Bread is fried dough served with honey, jam, or powdered sugar.
  • Pork barbecue: Pork Barbecue is a dish made of slow-cooked pork with flavorful sauce and is a staple of Southern cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Tsali Notch Vineyard: Cherokee’s locally-grown wine is produced at Tsali Notch Vineyard. Visitors can join a wine-tasting tour and sample some of the vineyard’s best wines.

Where to Stay in Cherokee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cherokee offers a variety of accommodations for different budgets and tastes. Some of the popular options are:

  • Harrah’s Cherokee Hotel and Casino: A luxurious hotel with amenities such as a spa, pool, and casino.
  • Happy Holiday Campground: A budget-friendly option for camping enthusiasts, Happy Holiday Campground offers RV and tent camping sites with basic facilities.
  • Smoky Mountain Inn & Suites: A mid-range hotel with spacious rooms and scenic views. The hotel also has a heated indoor pool, Jacuzzi, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Cherokee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cherokee is a great place to do some shopping and take home some unique souvenirs. Here are some local products you can buy in Cherokee:

  • Qualla Arts and Crafts Mutual: The historic cooperative offers handmade crafts representing traditional Cherokee culture, including jewelry, pottery, and crafts.
  • John C. Campbell Folk School Craft Shop: Visitors can purchase traditional handmade Appalachian crafts, including baskets, quilts, and woodworks.

Local Culture and Customs of Cherokee

The Cherokee tribe has a rich and fascinating history, heritage, and culture that visitors can explore and learn about. Cherokee values cultural preservation, and visitors are expected to respect their customs. Dress code is casual, and it’s considered respectful to ask permission before taking photographs of cultural objects or events.

Best Time to Visit Cherokee

Cherokee is a year-round destination, with something special to offer in every season. However, the best time to visit Cherokee is in the fall, from September to November, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is spectacular. spring and summer are also great times to explore the town, with warm temperatures perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cherokee

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Cherokee:

Do:

  • Respect the Cherokee community’s customs and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes suitable for outdoor activities.
  • Tip servers at restaurants and other service workers.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of tribal members or ceremonies without permission.
  • Litter or disturb wildlife in natural areas.
  • Overstep boundaries or engage in disrespectful behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cherokee

Cherokee is a safe and peaceful community, and visitors generally face no problems regarding safety. However, it’s always advisable to exercise common sense, and visitors should avoid wandering in unfamiliar places at night.

Health in Cherokee

Cherokee has access to hospitals and clinics that offer standard medical services. It’s always a good idea to keep personal medication if you have any pre-existing health conditions while travelling.

Getting to Cherokee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cherokee is well-connected, and there are several ways to get to Cherokee.

Airports Near Cherokee

  • Asheville Regional Airport: 58 miles from Cherokee, it’s the nearest commercial airport to Cherokee.
  • McGhee Tyson Airport: 86 miles from Cherokee, it is another option for visitors.

Bus Stations Near Cherokee

  • Great Smoky Mountains Railroad: Located in Bryson City, 14 miles away from Cherokee. Visitors can take connecting bus services from nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from nearby cities or airports and drive to Cherokee.
  • Taxi and Shuttle Services: Visitors can hire a taxi or shuttle service from nearby cities or airports.

How to Get Around in Cherokee

The best way to explore Cherokee is by driving. Car rentals are available in the vicinity, and visitors can also opt for golf carts or bikes to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Cherokee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of places to explore near Cherokee. Some of the popular destinations are:

  • Gatlinburg, Tennessee: A charming town located 45 minutes away, Gatlinburg offers outdoor activities, entertainment, and local culture.
  • Bryson City: Located 14 miles away, Bryson City offers visitors scenic drives, waterfalls, and outdoor recreation.
  • Maggie Valley: A vintage mountain town located 20 miles away from Cherokee, has antique shops, wineries, and ski resorts.

Conclusion

Cherokee is a beautiful and vibrant town with something to offer visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you are here to explore the natural beauty, learn about Cherokee heritage, or enjoy outdoor activities, Cherokee has plenty to offer. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip and make the most of their time in Cherokee.
