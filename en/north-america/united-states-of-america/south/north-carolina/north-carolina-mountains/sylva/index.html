Introduction to Sylva

Nestled in the North Carolina Mountains, Sylva is a charming town that offers plenty of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious food. With its proximity to the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Pisgah National Forest, and Nantahala National Forest, Sylva is a popular destination for nature lovers.

However, Sylva also has a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its museums, art galleries, and historic landmarks. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an action-packed adventure, Sylva has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Sylva

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jackson County Green Energy Park: A former landfill turned into an eco-friendly art park, Jackson County Green Energy Park is a must-visit attraction in Sylva. The park features working studios for glassblowing, blacksmithing, and pottery, along with outdoor sculptures and a playground made of recycled materials.
  • Jackson County Courthouse: Built in 1914, the Jackson County Courthouse is a stunning neoclassical building that houses the Jackson County Public Library and the Jackson County Historical Association.
  • Mountain Heritage Center: Located on the campus of Western Carolina University, the Mountain Heritage Center is a museum that showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the Southern Appalachians.

Natural Attractions of Sylva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: With its breathtaking views and abundant wildlife, the Great Smoky Mountains National Park is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the United States. Located just a short drive from Sylva, the park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, camping, and wildlife watching.
  • Pisgah National Forest: Known for its waterfalls, scenic drives, and challenging hiking trails, Pisgah National Forest is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. The forest also offers opportunities for mountain biking, rock climbing, and horseback riding.
  • Nantahala National Forest: With over 500,000 acres of forest, Nantahala National Forest is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The forest is home to the Nantahala River, which is popular for whitewater rafting and kayaking, as well as numerous hiking trails and scenic overlooks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sylva

  • Blue Ridge Parkway: One of the most scenic drives in America, the Blue Ridge Parkway is a 469-mile road that stretches from Virginia to North Carolina. The parkway offers numerous hiking trails and scenic overlooks, including the popular Graveyard Fields Trail.
  • Panthertown Valley: Known as the “Yosemite of the East,” Panthertown Valley is a 6,300-acre wilderness area that offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains. The area is known for its waterfalls, rock formations, and diverse wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Sylva: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting: With its proximity to the Nantahala River, Sylva is a popular destination for white water rafting and kayaking. There are several outfitters in the area that offer guided tours and rentals.
  • Craft Beer Tasting: Sylva has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries and taprooms in the area. Some of the most popular breweries include Innovation Brewing, Sneak E Squirrel, and Balsam Falls Brewing Company.
  • Zip-Lining: For a thrilling adventure, visitors can go on a zip-line tour of the Nantahala Gorge. The tour offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Food and Drink Scene of Sylva

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: Sylva is known for its delicious barbecue, with several restaurants in the area offering pulled pork, brisket, and ribs.
  • Sweet Potato Fries: A popular side dish in the area, sweet potato fries are a must-try for anyone visiting Sylva.
  • Moonshine: Sylva has a rich moonshine history, and visitors can taste a variety of flavors at the Elevated Mountain Distilling Company.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Sylva has several breweries and taprooms in the area, including Innovation Brewing, Sneak E Squirrel, and Balsam Falls Brewing Company.
  • Wine: Located in the neighboring town of Dillsboro, the Jackson County Vineyard offers a variety of locally sourced wines.
  • Moonshine: Visitors can taste a variety of moonshine flavors at the Elevated Mountain Distilling Company.

Where to Stay in Sylva: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cabin on the Creek: Located just outside of Sylva, Cabin on the Creek offers cozy cabins that are perfect for a romantic getaway or a family vacation.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: Conveniently located in the heart of Sylva, the Holiday Inn Express & Suites offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as an indoor pool and fitness center.
  • Sylva Inn: A locally owned motel that offers affordable rooms and easy access to the town’s restaurants and shops.

What to Buy in Sylva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Sylva has several local potters and ceramicists in the area, and visitors can purchase their handmade wares at the Jackson County Green Energy Park or the Jackson County Farmers Market.
  • Moonshine: Elevated Mountain Distilling Company offers a variety of moonshine flavors that make for a unique and delicious souvenir.
  • Local honey: Visitors can purchase locally sourced honey at the Jackson County Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Sylva

Sylva is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors are encouraged to explore the area’s rich history and culture. The town is known for its bluegrass music and moonshine history, and visitors can attend local festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Sylva

The best time to visit Sylva is in the fall, when the leaves are changing colors and the weather is cool and crisp. However, each season offers its own unique charms, from the spring wildflowers to the summer waterfalls.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sylva

Sylva is a relatively safe town, with low levels of crime and a friendly community. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions when exploring the area’s natural sites, such as wearing appropriate footwear and obeying park rules and regulations.

Health in Sylva

Sylva has several medical facilities in the area, including Harris Regional Hospital and MedWest Urgent Care. Visitors should bring any necessary medication and stay hydrated when exploring the area’s outdoor sites.

Getting to Sylva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sylva

  • Asheville Regional Airport: Located 50 miles east of Sylva, Asheville Regional Airport is the closest airport to the town.
  • Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport: Located 100 miles south of Sylva, Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport offers additional flight options.

Bus Stations Near Sylva

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Asheville, the Greyhound Bus Station offers bus services to and from Sylva.
  • Smoky Mountain Transit Authority: The Smoky Mountain Transit Authority offers bus services between Sylva and neighboring towns.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car from the Asheville Regional Airport or in downtown Sylva.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Several taxi and ride-sharing services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Sylva

The easiest way to get around Sylva is by car or on foot. Several local companies offer car rentals or visitors can bring their own vehicle. Downtown Sylva is quite walkable, and many of the town’s attractions and restaurants are within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Sylva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asheville: Located just 50 miles east of Sylva, Asheville is a vibrant city that offers a variety of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious food.
  • Cherokee: A history-rich town located just 20 miles west of Sylva, Cherokee is the home of the Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians and offers a variety of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Brevard: Located 40 miles south of Sylva, Brevard is known for its waterfalls, live music, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Sylva, North Carolina, is a hidden gem that offers plenty of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages. With its rich history, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food, Sylva is a must-visit destination in the North Carolina Mountains.
