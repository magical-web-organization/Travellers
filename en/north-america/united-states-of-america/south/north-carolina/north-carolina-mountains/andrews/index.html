Introduction to Andrews

Andrews is a charming, small town in North Carolina’s picturesque mountains, surrounded by lush forests and stunning natural scenery. This hidden gem is known for its serene hiking trails, scenic views, and vibrant local culture. Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventure seeker, or a history buff, Andrews has something to offer for everyone. In this guide, we will take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay, ensuring you have a memorable trip to Andrews.

List of Places to Visit in Andrews

When it comes to visiting Andrews, there are plenty of sights and attractions that shouldn’t be missed. Here are some of the top places to include on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Andrews Art Museum

Housed in a historic building in downtown Andrews, this museum showcases local and regional art exhibitions throughout the year. Admission is free, and the museum also offers hands-on art workshops and classes for all ages.

Andrews Heritage Museum

This museum is a must-visit for history lovers, offering a glimpse into the town’s rich cultural and industrial heritage. Visitors can explore exhibits on Cherokee culture, railroads, moonshine, and more.

Natural Attractions of Andrews: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Andrews Creek Trails

For those who love hiking, Andrews offers a wide range of trails with varying difficulty levels. Andrews Creek Trails, located in the Nantahala National Forest, offers picturesque views of creeks and waterfalls along the way.

Snowbird Backcountry Area

This is a great destination for snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, and hiking in the winter. The area’s scenic views, including the stunning Snowbird Mountains, make this backcountry area a must-visit location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Andrews

Andrews has a variety of hiking and biking routes, all with different lengths and difficulties to cater to all types of visitors. Here are some of the best trails in and around Andrews:

Fires Creek Rim Trail

The Fires Creek Rim Trail is a moderate 5.4-mile hike that offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and the nearby Fires Creek. This trail is known for its wildflowers and is dog-friendly.

Tsali Recreation Area

Located in the nearby town of Bryson City, Tsali Recreation Area offers plenty of trails that cater to hikers and mountain bikers. The trail system is made up of four loops, with each loop offering different levels of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Andrews: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities and tours to do in Andrews, catering to all interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

Nantahala River Rafting

The Nantahala River, located a short drive from Andrews, offers some of the best rafting adventures in the Southeast. Rafting trips usually last around two hours and include Class II-III rapids.

Gem Mining

If you’re traveling with kids, gem mining is a great way to spend a fun day in Andrews. You can rent mining supplies and dig through dirt to find and take home beautiful gems, fossils, and crystals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Andrews

If you’re planning to spend a week in Andrews, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most out of your trip:

Day 1: Visit Andrews Heritage Museum and Andrews Art Museum in downtown Andrews.

Day 2: Explore the Andrews Creek Trails and enjoy the stunning waterfalls and creeks along the way.

Day 3: Take a day trip to nearby Cherokee to learn about the history and culture of the Cherokee tribe.

Day 4: Go whitewater rafting on the Nantahala River.

Day 5: Hike the Fires Creek Rim Trail or Snowbird Backcountry Area for scenic views and nature photography.

Day 6: Spend a day gem mining with family and friends.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local culture in downtown Andrews, including food, drinks, and souvenir shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Andrews

Andrews has a unique food scene with plenty of local ingredients and flavors on offer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

Moonshine Cake

This delicious cake is made with locally-sourced moonshine, giving it a unique and authentic flavor.

Appalachia Cheese Steak

The Appalachia Cheese Steak is a local favorite, made with freshly caught trout, mushrooms, onions, and cheese.

Local Beverages

Brewed Awakenings

This popular coffee shop in downtown Andrews is famous for its freshly brewed coffee, specialty drinks, and homemade pastries.

Andrews Brewing Company

Andrews Brewing Company is a must-visit destination for beer lovers, offering a wide range of locally brewed craft beers.

Where to Stay in Andrews: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, Andrews has plenty of options to cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in Andrews:

Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham

This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities, including free Wi-Fi, breakfast, and a fitness center.

Andrews Valley View Bed & Breakfast

Andrews Valley View Bed & Breakfast offers stunning mountain views and cozy rooms. The bed and breakfast also serves delicious homemade breakfasts every morning.

What to Buy in Andrews: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Andrews has plenty of unique, locally made souvenirs and products that make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the best places to shop for souvenirs:

Andrews Art Museum Gift Shop

The Andrews Art Museum Gift Shop offers a selection of locally made art, pottery, and jewelry that make great souvenirs.

Calaboose Cellars

Calaboose Cellars is a local winery that produces high-quality wines made from North Carolina grapes. This is a great place to pick up a bottle of wine to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Andrews

Andrews has a warm and welcoming local culture, known for its hospitality and love for the outdoors. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including showing appreciation for the natural environment and local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Andrews

The best time to visit Andrews is during the fall, from September to November, when the weather is mild, and the foliage turns into a stunning array of colors. Spring and summer are also great seasons to visit, with plenty of outdoor activities and festivals on offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Andrews

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the natural environment, including keeping trails and parks clean.
  • Support local businesses and buy locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trails and parks dirty.
  • Disrespect local culture or traditions.
  • Ignore safety guidelines for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

When visiting Andrews, it’s essential to prioritize safety and health. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Andrews

While Andrews is generally safe, visitors should take precautions when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. Be aware of wildlife, carry plenty of water and food, and plan for unexpected weather changes.

Health in Andrews

It’s always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before traveling to Andrews. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, including wearing sunscreen and bug spray, staying hydrated, and following COVID-19 guidelines during their trip.

Getting to Andrews: Transportation Options and Logistics

Andrews is accessible by car and public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Andrews:

Airports Near Andrews

The nearest airports to Andrews are Asheville Regional Airport and Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport, both about a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Andrews

Greyhound offers limited bus service to Andrews. The closest terminal is located in Sylva, approximately 30 minutes from Andrews.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Andrews, take a taxi, or rent a car from nearby rental agencies.

How to Get Around in Andrews

Andrews is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use a taxi or rental car to get around, or rent a bike to explore the town’s scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Andrews: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Andrews is located in a beautiful region known for its natural beauty and cultural attractions. Here are some nearby destinations that are worth visiting:

Asheville

Asheville, located about a two-hour drive from Andrews, is a vibrant city known for its arts and culture scene, unique architecture, and stunning views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Great Smoky Mountains National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, located about an hour from Andrews. The park offers miles of scenic hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Andrews is a charming mountain town that offers plenty of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and warm hospitality. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Andrews with ease, ensuring you don’t miss out on any of the town’s top sights, activities, and experiences.
