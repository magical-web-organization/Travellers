Introduction to Mars Hill

Nestled in the beautiful North Carolina Mountains, Mars Hill is a small town in Madison County, North Carolina, United States. With a population of just over 1,700 people, Mars Hill is a hidden gem for travelers seeking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy some peace and tranquility amidst breathtaking natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Mars Hill

No matter your interests, there’s something for everyone in Mars Hill. Here are some of the top places you must visit when in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Liston B. Ramsey Center for Regional Studies: This center is located on the campus of Mars Hill University and offers visitors the opportunity to learn about the history and culture of western North Carolina through exhibits and various events.

  • Rural Heritage Museum: Featuring exhibits on everything from moonshine to agriculture, the Rural Heritage Museum offers a peek into the unique history and culture of Madison County.

  • Mars Hill Depot: A former train depot that has been converted into a museum showcasing the history of the Mars Hill community.

Natural Attractions of Mars Hill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bailey Mountain Bike Park: An adrenaline-pumping experience for mountain bikers, this park has over 100 acres of terrain featuring downhill trails, jump lines, and more.

  • Pisgah National Forest: This national forest is a must-visit for nature lovers and offers opportunities for hiking, camping, fishing, and more amidst stunning natural beauty.

  • Blue Ridge Parkway: A scenic drive that offers breathtaking views of the mountains and is a great way to take in the fall foliage in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mars Hill

Mars Hill boasts some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. Here are a few of the top routes:

  • Appalachian Trail: The iconic Appalachian Trail runs through the Mars Hill area, offering hikers breathtaking views of the mountains and a chance to explore some of the region’s most stunning wilderness.

  • Mars Hill University Trail System: This system of trails is located on the campus of Mars Hill University and offers a variety of hiking and biking options for visitors of all experience levels.

  • Roan Mountain: A great day hike, Roan Mountain offers a stunning ascent through evergreen forest and opens up to spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Mars Hill: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: Take a ride on horseback through the beautiful mountains with Sandy Bottom Trail Rides.

  • Fishing: Go fishing in the pristine waters of local rivers and streams with Southern Appalachian Anglers.

  • Guided Tours: Take a guided tour with Appalachian GhostWalks or High Country Walking Tours to learn about the history and culture of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mars Hill

Day 1: Arrive in Mars Hill and take a leisurely hike along the Mars Hill University Trail System.

Day 2: Explore the museums and historical sites in town, including the Rural Heritage Museum and Mars Hill Depot.

Day 3: Head out to Bailey Mountain Bike Park for a day of mountain biking excitement.

Day 4: Take a guided tour with Appalachian GhostWalks or High Country Walking Tours to learn more about the unique history and culture of the area.

Day 5: Spend the day fishing or hiking in the beautiful wilderness of Pisgah National Forest.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive on the Blue Ridge Parkway and enjoy the beautiful fall foliage.

Day 7: Depart Mars Hill, but not before indulging in some of the town’s delicious local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Mars Hill

From classic southern cuisine to modern farm-to-table restaurants, Mars Hill has something for every foodie. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: Get the quintessential southern dish at Pritchard’s Kitchen.

  • Mac and Cheese: Try the mouth-watering mac and cheese at Stackhouse Restaurant or Madisons Wood Fired Grill.

  • Moonshine: Sample some of the region’s famous moonshine at Two Wolves Distillery.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Mars Hill boasts several great breweries, including Mad Co. Brew House and Wicked Weed Brewing.

  • Apple Cider: Get a taste of the region with some fresh apple cider from the local orchards.

Where to Stay in Mars Hill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mars Hill has a range of convenient accommodations for travelers of all types. Here are some top options:

  • Mars Hill University Lodging: The university offers affordable accommodations for travelers in town.

  • Brightwaters Vacation Rentals: Experience true southern hospitality with a stay in these cozy vacation rentals.

  • Comfort Inn Mars Hill: A comfortable hotel that is close to all of the main attractions in town.

What to Buy in Mars Hill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Mars Hill, make sure to pick up some unique souvenirs and local products to take home with you. Here are a few top options:

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Pick up some unique pottery from local artists at the Mad Co. Pottery Gallery.

  • Local Honey: Get some fresh and delicious honey from Morgan Branch Apiaries.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Check out the unique handmade jewelry at Artisun Gallery.

Local Culture and Customs of Mars Hill

Mars Hill is a friendly, welcoming community that values its unique history and culture. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, including:

  • Southern Hospitality: Expect to be greeted with warm smiles and friendly conversation at every turn.

  • Religious Diversity: Mars Hill is home to several different religious communities, including Baptist, Methodist, and Presbyterian.

Best Time to Visit Mars Hill

The best time to visit Mars Hill is in the fall, when the leaves change color and the crisp mountain air makes for perfect hiking weather. However, the town is lovely to visit year-round, with a variety of activities available in all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mars Hill

Do:

  • Do be courteous and friendly to everyone you meet.

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities and natural beauty of the area.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind on the trails or at parks.

  • Don’t make assumptions about the customs and traditions of the local community without first seeking to understand them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mars Hill

Mars Hill is a safe community with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still remain aware of their surroundings and take basic safety precautions.

Health in Mars Hill

Mars Hill is home to several medical facilities, including Mars Hill Medical Center and Blue Ridge Regional Hospital. Visitors should bring any necessary medications and have current medical insurance.

Getting to Mars Hill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mars Hill is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the region. Additionally, there are several airports and bus stations nearby for those traveling from farther away.

Airports Near Mars Hill

  • Asheville Regional Airport: Approximately 30 miles from Mars Hill.

  • Greenville-Spartanburg International Airport: Approximately 90 miles from Mars Hill.

Bus Stations Near Mars Hill

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal (Asheville): Approximately 25 miles from Mars Hill.

  • Trailways Bus Station (Johnson City, TN): Approximately 50 miles from Mars Hill.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental agencies operate in the Asheville area, making it easy to rent a car and drive to Mars Hill.

  • Taxi or Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services operate in the region, but can be expensive for longer distances.

How to Get Around in Mars Hill

Mars Hill is a relatively small town that is easily walkable. However, for those who prefer to drive, several car rental agencies operate in the Asheville area.

Where to Go Next from Mars Hill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asheville, North Carolina: Home to the famous Biltmore Estate, Asheville is a vibrant city with plenty of restaurants, bars, and shops to explore.

  • Black Mountain, North Carolina: A charming mountain town known for its arts and crafts scene.

  • Great Smoky Mountain National Park: A must-visit for nature lovers, this national park straddles the border between North Carolina and Tennessee and offers stunning views and a variety of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Mars Hill is a hidden gem in the North Carolina Mountains, offering visitors beautiful natural scenery, exciting outdoor activities, and unique cultural experiences. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor adventurer, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.
