Are you planning a trip to the Piedmont Triad region in North Carolina? Look no further than Burlington, a hidden gem nestled in the heart of the state. With a rich history, vibrant culture, and breathtaking natural scenery, Burlington offers something for every type of traveler. In this guide, we’ll highlight the top attractions, food and drink destinations, local customs, and much more, so you can plan an unforgettable trip to this charming city.

Introduction to Burlington

Burlington is a city in Alamance County, North Carolina, with a population of around 54,000. Located in Piedmont Triad, it is part of the larger Piedmont region and offers easy access to other major cities like Raleigh and Charlotte. Burlington has a rich textile history and was once known as the “Hosiery Center of the South”. Today, it is a diverse city with a mix of cultural, natural, and adventurous opportunities that make it a great destination for both locals and tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Burlington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Burlington has a rich history, and visitors can experience it firsthand through a variety of excellent museums and historical sites.

Alamance Battleground State Historic Site

Discover the historic Alamance Battleground, where the Revolutionary War Battle of Alamance took place in 1771. Visitors can explore the site, which features a museum, trails, and a restored farmstead.

Textile Heritage Museum

Learn about Burlington’s history as the “Hosiery Center of the South” at the Textile Heritage Museum. This museum features exhibits on textile manufacturing and the development of the hosiery industry in North Carolina.

Burlington City Park

Spend a day at the beautiful Burlington City Park, a 75-acre park with a lake, playgrounds, picnic shelters, and walking trails. The park also features a train ride, carousel, and a mini golf course.

Natural Attractions of Burlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Experience the natural beauty of Burlington by exploring its scenic parks and adrenaline-pumping outdoor activities.

Haw River Trail

Go hiking, kayaking, or fishing along the Haw River Trail, which spans over 70 miles and features stunning views of the river and surrounding wilderness.

Cedarock Park

Explore the great outdoors at Cedarock Park, a 500-acre park with hiking trails, fishing ponds, playgrounds, and an equestrian center.

Elon University Environmental Center

Take in the natural beauty of Elon University’s Environmental Center, a 750-acre reserve with miles of hiking trails, wetlands, and birdwatching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burlington

Burlington has plenty of hiking and biking options for outdoor enthusiasts looking to get some exercise and take in the local scenery.

Company Mill Loop Trail

Explore the beautiful Company Mill Loop Trail in Eno River State Park. This 5-mile trail takes you around the river and features stunning views of the surrounding forest.

Burlington City Bicycle Route

Take a leisurely ride around the city on the Burlington City Bicycle Route, a 13-mile loop that takes you through the city’s residential neighborhoods, downtown, and parks.

Graham-Mebane Lake Trail

This 7-mile trail is perfect for hiking or biking, with plenty of scenic views of the lake and surrounding woodlands.

Best Things to Do in Burlington: Activities and Tours

From historical tours to adrenaline-pumping adventures, Burlington offers a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy.

Brewery Tours

Take a tour of the local breweries and sample some of the best craft beer in North Carolina. Burlington Beer Works, The Blend, and The Lab are just a few of the breweries worth visiting.

Free Walking Tours

Explore downtown Burlington on a free walking tour. Learn about the city’s history, architecture, and local culture from knowledgeable guides.

Go-Karting and Laser Tag

For a fun day out with family or friends, head to the RushHour Karting and RushHour Laser Tag. This indoor amusement center offers go-karting, laser tag, and arcade games.

Food and Drink Scene of Burlington

Burlington’s food and drink scene is a mix of traditional Southern cooking and modern fusion dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Lexington-style BBQ

North Carolina is known for its BBQ, and Burlington is no exception. Head to Hursey’s Bar-B-Q for some tasty Lexington-style BBQ.

Chicken and Dumplings

Try this traditional Southern dish at The Sweet Shoppe.

Banana Pudding Shake

Indulge in this yummy milkshake at Cook Out, a fast-food chain founded in North Carolina.

Local Beverages

Cheerwine

This beloved cherry-flavored soda was first created in North Carolina in 1917. Burlington is a great place to try it, as it is available everywhere in the city.

Craft Beer

Burlington is home to several craft breweries, including Burlington Beer Works, which serves a variety of delicious IPAs, stouts, and ales.

Where to Stay in Burlington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Burlington has a range of accommodations to suit any budget, from historic inns to upscale hotels.

Historic Inns

The Acorn Inn

This historic 1800s home is now a cozy bed and breakfast. The Acorn Inn features five unique rooms with antique furnishings and modern amenities.

Budget Accommodations

Red Roof Inn

This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and convenient access to local attractions.

Upscale Hotels

Courtyard by Marriott Burlington

This upscale hotel features a fitness center, indoor pool, and on-site restaurant. The Courtyard by Marriott is located just minutes from downtown Burlington.

What to Buy in Burlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Burlington offers plenty of souvenirs to help you remember your trip, including local food items and crafts.

Local Food Products

Cheerwine

Bring home a six-pack of this delicious cherry-flavored soda, which can only be found in North Carolina.

Moravian Cookies

These thin, crisp cookies are a North Carolina favorite and can be found at local shops like The Old Mill of Guilford.

Local Crafts

Burlington Basket Company

This local basket maker produces high-quality hand-woven baskets that make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Burlington

Burlington has a relaxed and welcoming culture, with a mix of Southern hospitality and Northeastern charm.

Best Time to Visit Burlington

Burlington is a great destination year-round, but the best time to visit is in the fall or spring when the weather is cooler and the scenery is most picturesque.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burlington

Do:

  • Do try the local BBQ and craft beer.
  • Do explore the city’s history through its museums and historical sites.
  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to try the Cheerwine, a local North Carolina favorite.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burlington

Burlington is a safe city, but visitors should still take normal safety precautions, such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Burlington

Visitors should take care of their health while traveling to Burlington. Be sure to stay hydrated, use sunscreen, and take precautions against ticks and other outdoor pests.

Getting to Burlington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burlington

The closest major airport to Burlington is Piedmont Triad International Airport (GSO), located in Greensboro, about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Burlington

Greyhound Lines offers bus service to Burlington, with the nearest station located in Greensboro.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby cities like Greensboro or Raleigh.

How to Get Around in Burlington

Burlington is a walkable city, but visitors can also use taxis, ride-share services, or rent a car to get around.

Where to Go Next from Burlington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Burlington is a great jumping-off point for exploring other nearby destinations in North Carolina.

Greensboro

Head to Greensboro, located just 30 miles away, and check out its attractions like the International Civil Rights Center and Museum and Greensboro Science Center.

Asheboro

Explore Asheboro, a charming town located about an hour from Burlington. Don’t miss the North Carolina Zoo, one of the largest zoos in the world.

Raleigh

Visit the state’s capital city, Raleigh, located about an hour and a half from Burlington. Check out its museums, parks, and historic sites.

Conclusion

Burlington is a unique and exciting destination with something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking for natural beauty, cultural attractions, or adventurous activities, Burlington has it all. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this charming city.
