Introduction to Winston-Salem

Winston-Salem is a vibrant city located in Piedmont Triad, North Carolina. This city is well-known for its art, culture, cuisine, and beautiful landscapes. Winston-Salem is a great destination for families, solo travelers, and couples. This guide will help you discover the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Winston-Salem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Reynolda House Museum of American Art

Reynolda House Museum of American Art is a beautiful museum located on the Reynolda estate. The museum showcases American art from the colonial period to the present-day. Some of the notable artists featured in the museum include Mary Cassatt, Georgia O’Keeffe, and Grant Wood.

Old Salem Museums & Gardens

Old Salem Museums & Gardens is a living history museum that showcases Moravian culture. Visitors can experience the authentic 18th century buildings, gardens, and craft demonstrations. The museum also hosts festivals and events throughout the year.

Winston-Salem State University

Winston-Salem State University is a historically black university that was established in 1892. The university is known for its strong academic programs and athletic teams. Visitors can attend sporting events and explore the beautiful campus.

Natural Attractions of Winston-Salem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pilot Mountain State Park

Pilot Mountain State Park is a beautiful park located just outside Winston-Salem. The park offers several scenic trails, rock climbing opportunities, and camping facilities. The summit of Pilot Mountain offers stunning views of the Piedmont Triad.

Bethabara Park

Bethabara Park is a historic park that was established by Moravian settlers in 1753. The park features a nature trail, herb garden, and a reconstructed 18th century village.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Winston-Salem

Winston-Salem offers several hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the popular trails include Salem Lake Trail, Muddy Creek Greenway, and Tanglewood Park Trail.

Best Things to Do in Winston-Salem: Activities and Tours

Tanglewood Park

Tanglewood Park is a great place to spend a day with family and friends. The park features a golf course, horseback riding, fishing, and picnic areas. Visitors can also explore the beautiful gardens and walkways.

Carolina History & Haunts Ghost Tour

The Carolina History & Haunts Ghost Tour is a fun and spooky way to explore Winston-Salem’s history. The tour takes you to some of the most haunted locations in the city, and you will learn about the city’s past while having a great time.

Week-Long Itinerary for Winston-Salem

Day 1: Visit Reynolda House Museum of American Art, and walk through the beautiful Reynolda Gardens.

Day 2: Explore Old Salem Museums & Gardens, and take a Moravian history tour.

Day 3: Hike at Pilot Mountain State Park, and enjoy the scenic views from the summit.

Day 4: Visit Winston-Salem State University, and attend a sporting event or concert.

Day 5: Take a Carolina History & Haunts Ghost Tour, and explore some of the most haunted locations in the city.

Day 6: Spend the day at Tanglewood Park, and enjoy the golf course, horseback riding, and other activities.

Day 7: Visit Bethabara Park, and learn about the history of the Moravian settlers in Winston-Salem.

Food and Drink Scene of Winston-Salem

Must-Try Dishes

Krispy Kreme Doughnuts

Krispy Kreme Doughnuts is a Winston-Salem-based chain that offers delicious, fresh doughnuts. The original glazed doughnut is a must-try.

Texas Pete

Texas Pete is a hot sauce that is produced in Winston-Salem. This sauce is a staple in Southern cuisine and can be found in almost every restaurant in the city.

Local Beverages

Foothills Brewing

Foothills Brewing is a local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers. The brewery has a taproom where visitors can sample the beers and learn about the brewing process.

Raffaldini Vineyards & Winery

Raffaldini Vineyards & Winery is a beautiful winery located just outside Winston-Salem. The winery offers wine tastings, vineyard tours, and a beautiful outdoor patio.

Where to Stay in Winston-Salem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kimpton Cardinal Hotel

Kimpton Cardinal Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of downtown Winston-Salem. The hotel offers spacious rooms, a rooftop bar, and a fitness center.

Brookstown Inn

Brookstown Inn is a historic hotel that was once a textile factory. The hotel features unique rooms and suites, a complimentary breakfast, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Winston-Salem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moravian Cookies

Moravian Cookies are a traditional cookie that has been baked in Winston-Salem for over 200 years. These cookies make a great souvenir or gift.

Salem Baking Company

Salem Baking Company creates delicious crackers, cookies, and snacks that are made in Winston-Salem. Visitors can purchase these treats at local grocery stores and gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Winston-Salem

Winston-Salem has a strong Moravian culture, and visitors can experience this culture through their cuisine, architecture, and festivals. The Moravian Christmas Lovefeast is a unique and beautiful tradition that should not be missed.

Best Time to Visit Winston-Salem

The best time to visit Winston-Salem is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is beautiful. However, Winston-Salem has events and festivals throughout the year, so there is always something to do.

Do’s and Don’ts of Winston-Salem

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including Moravian cookies and Texas Pete hot sauce.
  • Explore the Moravian history and culture through the museums and architecture.
  • Attend a festival or event.
  • Visit Pilot Mountain State Park for scenic views and outdoor adventure.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local cuisine, it’s unique and delicious.
  • Disrespect the Moravian culture and traditions.
  • Miss out on the outdoor opportunities, Winston-Salem has plenty of beautiful parks and trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Winston-Salem

Winston-Salem is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions to stay safe. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Winston-Salem

Several hospitals and medical facilities are located in Winston-Salem. Visitors should bring their necessary medication and contact their medical provider if necessary.

Getting to Winston-Salem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Winston-Salem

Piedmont Triad International Airport (GSO) is the nearest airport to Winston-Salem. The airport is approximately 25 minutes from downtown.

Bus Stations Near Winston-Salem

Greyhound Bus Lines and Carolina Trailways offer bus service to Winston-Salem. The stations are located in downtown Winston-Salem.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Winston-Salem using major highways such as I-40, I-85, and I-77.

How to Get Around in Winston-Salem

Winston-Salem has a public transportation system called WSTA, which offers bus service throughout the city. However, the best way to get around is by car.

Where to Go Next from Winston-Salem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

High Point

High Point is a small city located just 20 minutes from Winston-Salem. This city is known for its furniture industry and offers several furniture stores and outlets for visitors to explore.

Asheville

Asheville is a beautiful mountain city located approximately 2 hours from Winston-Salem. The city offers art, music, and culture, as well as several outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking.

Conclusion

Winston-Salem is a beautiful city with plenty to do and see. Whether you’re interested in art, history, or outdoor adventure, Winston-Salem has something to offer. This guide will help you explore Winston-Salem and make the most of your trip.
