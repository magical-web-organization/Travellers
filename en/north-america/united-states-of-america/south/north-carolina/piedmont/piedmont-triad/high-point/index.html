Introduction to High Point

Located in the Piedmont Triad region of North Carolina, High Point is a charming city known for its furniture industry and its role in the civil rights movement. With a population of over 110,000, the city offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a great destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in High Point

Here’s a list of must-visit places in High Point:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • International Civil Rights Center & Museum: This museum chronicles the civil rights movement and the sit-ins that took place in Greensboro in 1960. Visitors can take a tour through the museum and experience the powerful exhibits.
  • High Point Museum: Learn about the history and culture of High Point at this museum, which features exhibits on furniture design, textiles, and more.
  • Mendenhall Plantation: This historic plantation dates back to the late 18th century and offers a look into the life and times of the region’s early inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of High Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pilot Mountain State Park: Located just 30 minutes from High Point, Pilot Mountain State Park offers plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and camping. The park’s most notable feature is its namesake mountain, which offers stunning views from the top.
  • High Point City Lake Park: This 340-acre park features a beautiful lake and plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities, such as boating, fishing, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of High Point

High Point offers some great hiking and biking routes, here are some of the best:

  • Bicentennial Greenway: This 6.5-mile trail connects High Point to Greensboro and offers a mix of urban and natural scenery.
  • High Point Greenway: This 4.5-mile trail runs along the outskirts of the city and offers a mix of wooded areas and open fields.

Best Things to Do in High Point: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in High Point:

  • Furniture Shopping: High Point is often referred to as the furniture capital of the world, so it’s no surprise that furniture shopping is a must-do activity here. Visit the High Point Market to see the latest and greatest in furniture design.
  • Wine Tasting: Check out the wineries in the surrounding area for a taste of local wines. Wineries like Grove Winery & Vineyards and Childress Vineyards offer tastings and tours.
  • Take a Food Tour: High Point has a vibrant dining scene, so consider taking a food tour to discover some of the best spots in the city.

Food and Drink Scene of High Point

High Point has a diverse food and drink scene. Here’s what you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: North Carolina is known for its barbecue, so make sure to try some of the local offerings while in High Point.
  • Pimento Cheese: This Southern classic is a must-try in High Point. Try it on a sandwich or as a dip.
  • Sweet Potato Fries: High Point is located in the sweet potato belt, so it’s no surprise that sweet potato fries are a local specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: High Point has a growing craft beer scene, so be sure to try some of the local brews at breweries like Brown Truck Brewery and Incendiary Brewing Company.
  • Wine: As mentioned earlier, there are several wineries in the surrounding area that offer tastings and tours.
  • Sweet Tea: This Southern classic is a must-have in High Point. Try it with a dash of lemon for a refreshing twist.

Where to Stay in High Point: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in High Point:

  • Proximity Hotel: This eco-friendly hotel located in Greensboro offers a luxurious stay with a focus on sustainability.
  • Courtyard by Marriott High Point: Located in the heart of High Point, this hotel offers comfortable and convenient accommodations.
  • J.H. Adams Inn: This historic hotel in High Point features charming rooms and a restaurant serving Southern cuisine.

What to Buy in High Point: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs and local products in High Point:

  • Furniture: As mentioned earlier, High Point is the furniture capital of the world, so furniture shopping is a must. Check out the High Point Market for the latest and greatest in furniture design.
  • Sweet Potato Products: Since High Point is located in the sweet potato belt, there are plenty of sweet potato products to try and bring home, such as sweet potato butter and sweet potato pancakes.
  • Pottery: North Carolina is known for its pottery, and High Point has several great shops and studios selling beautiful handmade pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of High Point

High Point is renowned for its furniture industry and as a hub of civil rights history. Visitors are encouraged to appreciate the region’s cultural diversity, rich history, and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit High Point

The best time to visit High Point is during the spring (March to May) and the fall (September to November). The weather is milder, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of High Point

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting High Point:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, particularly the sweet potato dishes and barbecue.
  • Do visit the International Civil Rights Center & Museum to learn about the region’s important role in the civil rights movement.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the area by hiking in the nearby state parks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave High Point without trying some of the local craft beer or wine.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray if you plan on spending time outdoors.
  • Don’t forget to be respectful of the region’s history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in High Point

High Point is a safe city, but visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors, not leaving valuables unattended, and being aware of surroundings.

Health in High Point

Healthcare facilities in High Point are plentiful with several hospitals and medical centers. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before arriving.

Getting to High Point: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options and logistics for getting to High Point:

Airports Near High Point

  • Piedmont Triad International Airport (GSO): Located about 15 miles from High Point, this airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near High Point

  • Greyhound: There is a Greyhound bus station located in High Point.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The High Point Amtrak station is served by the Crescent train line, which runs between New York and New Orleans.
  • Car Rental: Car rentals are available at the airport and in the city.

How to Get Around in High Point

The easiest way to get around High Point is by car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from High Point: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting High Point:

  • Greensboro: Located just 20 miles from High Point, Greensboro offers a wide variety of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • Winston-Salem: Winston-Salem, the largest city in the Piedmont Triad region, is just 30 miles north of High Point and is known for its arts and culture scene.
  • Uwharrie National Forest: This beautiful national forest is located just 40 miles east of High Point and offers plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking and camping.

Conclusion

High Point offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a great destination for any traveler. With its beautiful state parks, rich history, and vibrant food scene, there’s something for everyone in this charming North Carolina city.
