Introduction to Thomasville

Located in the heart of North Carolina’s Piedmont Triad, Thomasville is a charming city with a rich history, cultural attractions, and a thriving food scene. Often referred to as the “Chair Town” due to its long-standing history in furniture manufacturing, Thomasville has much more to offer than just chairs. In this guide, we will explore the city’s top attractions, dining options, and outdoor adventures to help you plan your next trip to Thomasville.

List of Places to Visit in Thomasville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thomasville is steeped in history, and there are many museums and historical landmarks to explore. The Old Salem Museum and Gardens provide a glimpse into colonial life while also showcasing the largest collection of Moravian art and artifacts in the country. The Thomasville History Center houses exhibits that detail the city’s past, including its connection to the furniture industry. Other significant museums and historical sites include:

  • The North Carolina Museum of History
  • The Greensboro Historical Museum
  • The High Point Museum

Natural Attractions of Thomasville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty to love in Thomasville. There are several parks such as the Finch Park, which offers miles of walking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas. For those looking for a more challenging hike, the Uwharrie National Forest is a short drive away and boasts over 50 miles of trails. Bald Eagle Wildlife Preserve is also a fantastic spot for nature lovers to explore the local wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thomasville

Thomasville has several beautiful hiking and biking trails. The one-mile Kennel Road Trail is perfect for a short, scenic walk. The adjacent High Point Greenway also provides miles of walking or biking paths through the heart of the city and 11 miles of scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Thomasville: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums and outdoor activities, there are other unique experiences to be had in Thomasville. The Higher Ground Hot Air Balloon Company offers balloon rides, giving visitors an amazing aerial view of the region. The Big Chair Walking Tour takes curious visitors to see the world-famous Thomasville chair, which stands at over 30 feet tall. The tour also includes several historic sites in the downtown area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thomasville

Day 1: Explore Downtown Thomasville

Start your trip to Thomasville by exploring the downtown area. Take a walk on the Big Chair Walking Tour and visit the historic sites such as The Thomasville History Center. Spend the rest of the day enjoying the local dining scene.

Day 2: Visit the Museums

Spend day two exploring the numerous museums in and around the city, such as Old Salem Museum and Gardens. Learn about the area’s history at North Carolina Museum of History.

Day 3: Enjoy Finch Park

Spend the day enjoying the nature of Finch Park, where you can walk the scenic trails, bring a picnic, or have a game of frisbee golf.

Day 4: Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride

View the stunning North Carolinian horizon as you float up in a hot air balloon with the Higher Ground Hot Air Balloon Company.

Day 5: Discover Uwharrie National Forest

Hike through the trails at Uwharrie National Forest or visit the Bald Eagle Wildlife Preserve.

Day 6: Take Another Walking Tour or Visit the Furniture Market

Take your pick from another Big Chair Walking Tour or browse the latest trending colors and design furniture in the area’s famous High Point Furniture Market

Day 7: Go to the Farmer’s Market

On your last day, visit the local farmer’s market to pick up some fresh fruits and vegetables as a souvenir to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Thomasville

Must-Try Dishes

Thomasville has a thriving food scene inspired by Southern Cuisine. The best dishes to try include:

  • Lexington-style BBQ
  • Fried Chicken and Waffles
  • Pimento Cheese

Local Beverages

Thomasville also has a range of local beverages such as:

  • Cheerwine soda
  • Craft beers from Southern Home and Small Batch Beer
  • Tea from Elizabeth & Olive

Where to Stay in Thomasville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thomasville has several accommodation options to make your stay comfortable. Some of the best options are:

  • Comfort Inn
  • Microtel Inn and Suites
  • Best Western Plus

What to Buy in Thomasville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thomasville may be known for its famous furniture pieces, but there are also other locally made products to bring home, including Cheerwine, Moravian cookies, and even handcrafted leather goods from Murdock Leather.

Local Culture and Customs of Thomasville

The people of Thomasville are warm, welcoming and proud of their heritage. Be sure to embrace the slow pace of life and make the most of your trip to Thomasville.

Best Time to Visit Thomasville

The best time to visit Thomasville is in the Spring and Fall seasons where you can enjoy the area’s scenic beauty in pleasant temperatures, full of blooming nature.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thomasville

Do:

  • Embrace the slow pace of the city and enjoy the Southern hospitality.
  • Visit the furniture market for latest and vintage furniture designs.
  • Try local cuisine such as Lexington-style BBQ and Fried Chicken and Waffles.
  • Explore the museums and historical places with interest.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be disrespectful of Southern manners or culture.
  • Don’t litter in parks or natural areas.
  • Don’t expect urban entertainment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thomasville

Thomasville is a safe city, but a general awareness is necessary when enjoying the city’s outdoor activities, such as walking or hiking trails.

Health in Thomasville

Thomasville has many healthcare options, but it is crucial for international travellers to have appropriate health insurance before visiting.

Getting to Thomasville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thomasville

The closest airport to Thomasville is the Piedmont Triad International Airport (PTIA), which is approximately 33 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Thomasville

The closest bus service is provided by the Greyhound Bus Station in High Point, North Carolina, just 10 miles from Thomasville.

Other Options

Car rentals are the most common way to navigate the Marion area.

How to Get Around in Thomasville

Thomasville is best navigated by car. Many hotels offer shuttle services, and car rentals and cabs are available from the airport. Uber and Lyft ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Thomasville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thomasville is located in close proximity to several other cities and towns, including:

  • High Point
  • Greensboro
  • Winston-Salem

Each of the mentioned areas has something unique to offer and are just a short drive away.

Conclusion

Thomasville is a unique city full of history, culture, and nature ideal for travellers looking to explore beautiful landscapes and enjoy soulful Southern culture. Plan your trip to this charming city for a memorable experience!
