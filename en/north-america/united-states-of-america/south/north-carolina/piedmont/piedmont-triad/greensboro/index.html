Introduction to Greensboro

Greensboro, located in the Piedmont Triad region of North Carolina, is a vibrant and historic city with a lot to offer. The city is home to a diverse population and rich cultural heritage, as well as numerous outdoor attractions and exciting things to see and do.

List of Places to Visit in Greensboro

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor recreation, or shopping, Greensboro has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Greensboro:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • International Civil Rights Center & Museum: Located in the historic Woolworth building, this museum focuses on the struggle for civil rights in America and the Greensboro lunch counter sit-ins that helped spark the movement.
  • Greensboro History Museum: Explore Greensboro’s rich history and heritage through exhibits and artifacts in this modern museum.
  • Weatherspoon Art Museum: Part of the University of North Carolina at Greensboro, this museum features a collection of over 17,000 works of modern and contemporary art and hosts exhibitions and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Greensboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tanger Family Bicentennial Garden: This 7-acre garden features a variety of plants, sculptures, fountains, and a koi pond.
  • Country Park: Offering over 800 acres of scenic trails, a lake, and a variety of recreational activities, this park is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Piedmont Environmental Center: Located just outside the city, this nature preserve features over 375 acres of woodlands, wetlands, and fields with miles of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greensboro

  • Atlantic & Yadkin Greenway: An extensive network of scenic trails for biking, walking, and hiking.
  • Lake Brandt Trail: A 7.5-mile trail that loops around the lake and offers scenic views.
  • Rich Fork Preserve Trail: A 1.75-mile trail that winds through a wooded area and is great for bird-watching.

Best Things to Do in Greensboro: Activities and Tours

  • Crafted - The Art of the Taco: Featuring a creative menu of gourmet tacos and margaritas, this popular spot is known for its unique flavors and fun atmosphere.
  • Greensboro Science Center: A great attraction for families with kids, this science museum features hands-on exhibits, an aquarium, and a zoo.
  • Carolina Theatre: This historic theater hosts concerts, live events, and movies throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greensboro

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Greensboro, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit the International Civil Rights Center & Museum and have dinner at Crafted.
2 Go hiking at Rich Fork Preserve Trail and have lunch at Sticks & Stones.

Food and Drink Scene of Greensboro

Greensboro has a lively food and drink scene with a variety of culinary options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: North Carolina is known for its delicious slow-smoked BBQ, and there are plenty of great spots to try it in Greensboro, such as Stamey’s BBQ and Pig Pounder Brewery.
  • Biscuits: Southern-style biscuits are a staple in Greensboro, and you can find them at many places, including Smith Street Diner and Biscuitville.
  • Sweet Potatoes: This staple crop of North Carolina is featured in many dishes, such as sweet potato fries from Hops Burger Bar and sweet potato biscuits from Smith Street Diner.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Greensboro has a thriving craft beer scene, with numerous local breweries such as Natty Greene’s Brewing Co. and Pig Pounder Brewery.
  • Sweet Tea: This classic Southern beverage can be found at many restaurants throughout Greensboro, such as Dame’s Chicken and Waffles and Smith Street Diner.
  • Cheerwine: This soda, made in nearby Salisbury, NC, is a regional favorite and can be found at many grocery stores and restaurants throughout Greensboro.

Where to Stay in Greensboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greensboro has a variety of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some top options:

  • Proximity Hotel: A modern and luxurious hotel located close to downtown Greensboro.
  • Greensboro Marriott Downtown: A comfortable and convenient option for those looking to stay in the heart of downtown.
  • Battleground Inn Greensboro: A budget-friendly motel located close to several parks and outdoor attractions.

What to Buy in Greensboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and locally made products to bring home from Greensboro, here are some top options:

  • Cobble Hill: This boutique gift shop offers a range of unique and locally made products, such as jewelry, home decor, and accessories.
  • Gate City Candy Company: This local candy shop has been in business for over 100 years and offers a variety of sweets and treats.
  • Reconsidered Goods: A thrift store and craft center that offers recycled and repurposed goods made by local artists and artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Greensboro

Greensboro is a diverse city with a rich cultural heritage. The city has a strong African American history and is home to several important civil rights landmarks, including the International Civil Rights Center & Museum. The local arts and music scene is also vibrant, with many venues and events showcasing local talent.

Best Time to Visit Greensboro

The best time to visit Greensboro is in the spring from March to May or in the fall from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild and comfortable, and there are several festivals and outdoor events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greensboro

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Greensboro:

Do:

  • Explore downtown Greensboro, where you can find many shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.
  • Visit some of the city’s parks and natural attractions, such as Country Park and the Tanger Family Bicentennial Garden.
  • Try some of the local cuisine, such as BBQ, biscuits, and sweet tea.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the city’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Neglect to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greensboro

Greensboro is generally a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your doors, not leaving valuables in your car, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Greensboro

The city has good healthcare facilities, including several hospitals and clinics. If you need medical assistance, dial 911 for emergencies or seek care at a local hospital or clinic.

Getting to Greensboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Greensboro is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Piedmont Triad International Airport: Located just outside of the city, this airport offers flights to several major destinations across the US and abroad.
  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greensboro is served by several bus lines, including Greyhound, which offers daily service to many nearby cities and states.
  • Car Rentals: Several car rental companies, including Hertz and Enterprise, are located at the airport and throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Greensboro

The best way to get around Greensboro is by car, as the city is spread out and many attractions are located outside of downtown. There are also several taxi services and rideshare options available, such as Lyft and Uber.

Where to Go Next from Greensboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in the area, consider visiting some of these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Asheboro: This small town located about 30 miles south of Greensboro is home to the North Carolina Zoo, one of the largest wildlife parks in the world.
  • Winston-Salem: This city located about 30 miles west of Greensboro is known for its arts and culture scene, including several museums and galleries.
  • Blue Ridge Mountains: The stunning Blue Ridge Mountains are located a few hours west of Greensboro and offer breathtaking hikes, scenic drives, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Greensboro, North Carolina is a great destination for travelers of all ages and interests. With its rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural beauty, Greensboro offers a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or culinary scene, you’ll find plenty to see and do in this vibrant Southern city.
