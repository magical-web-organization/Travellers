Introduction to Apex

Located in the Research Triangle of North Carolina’s Piedmont region, Apex is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of outdoor adventures to explore. Named the “Peak of Good Living,” Apex is a great destination for families, couples, and solo travelers looking to experience Southern hospitality, local culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re planning a weekend escape or week-long vacation, this travel guide to Apex will help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Apex

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re looking to discover the history of Apex and the surrounding region, make sure to visit the following attractions:

  • Apex Historical Society Museum: Located in the historic Train Depot, this museum is dedicated to preserving Apex’s history and showcases artifacts from the town’s early years.
  • Halle Cultural Arts Center: This center is home to rotating exhibits, theater productions, and workshops that celebrate the arts and culture of Apex.
  • Salem Street Historic District: Walk through the heart of Apex and explore the beautiful Victorian-style homes and buildings that have been preserved from the 1800s and early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Apex: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, Apex has plenty to offer, including:

  • Jordan Lake State Recreation Area: This 14,000-acre lake is perfect for fishing, swimming, boating, and camping. With several hiking trails and picnic areas, there’s something for everyone.
  • Crowder District Park: This park has over 30 acres of natural beauty, including trails, playgrounds, and a fishing pond.
  • American Tobacco Trail: This 22-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. With scenic views and wildlife sightings, it’s a must-see for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apex

Apex has a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. Some of the best routes include:

  • Apex Nature Park: This park has over five miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through wooded areas and along creeks.
  • Beaver Creek Greenway: This 2.8-mile trail connects Apex to nearby Cary and offers beautiful views of the Beaver Creek.
  • Jordan Lake State Recreation Area: With over 21 miles of hiking trails, this park offers plenty of options for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Apex: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions, Apex has several fun activities and tours to experience, including:

  • Jordan Lake Tours: Take a guided tour of Jordan Lake and learn about the natural history and wildlife of the area.
  • Peak City Bike Tours: Explore Apex on two wheels with a guided bike tour that takes you through the historic downtown and surrounding countryside.
  • Apex Farmers Market: Shop local and enjoy fresh produce, baked goods, and artisan crafts at this weekly farmers market.

Week-Long Itinerary for Apex

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Apex, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the Salem Street Historic District and enjoy lunch at one of the many local eateries.
  • Day 2: Visit the Apex Historical Society Museum and enjoy an evening theater production at Halle Cultural Arts Center.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Jordan Lake State Recreation Area, hiking, fishing, and boating.
  • Day 4: Take a guided bike tour of Apex and nearby countryside and enjoy lunch at a local brewery.
  • Day 5: Visit Crowder District Park and enjoy a picnic lunch along the fishing pond.
  • Day 6: Shop local at the Apex Farmers Market and enjoy an evening pint at one of the many local breweries.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Raleigh, exploring museums, shopping districts, and the local food scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Apex

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Apex is complete without trying these delicious local dishes:

  • BBQ: North Carolina is famous for its BBQ, and Apex has several great places to try this Southern staple.
  • Fried Chicken: From local diners to upscale restaurants, Apex has plenty of delicious fried chicken options.
  • Sweet Potatoes: This Southern staple is a must-try, whether served baked, fried, or mashed.

Local Beverages

Apex has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries that offer delicious brews and friendly atmospheres. Some must-try breweries include:

  • Brüeprint Brewing Company: This family-owned brewery serves up a variety of delicious beers, including seasonal and limited-release options.
  • Southern Peak Brewery: With over 20 beers on tap and rotating food trucks, this brewery is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike.
  • Aviator Brewing Company: This award-winning brewery offers tours and tastings, as well as a full menu of delicious pub fare.

Where to Stay in Apex: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Apex has several options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hyatt Place Raleigh West: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to downtown Apex and nearby cities.
  • Holiday Inn Express Apex: With a prime location and affordable rates, this hotel is a great option for budget-conscious travelers.
  • Airbnb: With several local hosts offering unique and comfortable accommodations, Airbnb is a great way to experience the local culture and community of Apex.

What to Buy in Apex: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, consider the following options:

  • Handmade Crafts: From pottery to textiles, Apex has a thriving community of local artisans who create one-of-a-kind crafts and products.
  • Local Foods: From barbecue sauces to sweet potato treats, Apex has several local food and beverage options that make great souvenirs and gifts.
  • Peak of Good Living Merchandise: Show your love for Apex with t-shirts, hats, and other merchandise that celebrates the town’s nickname, “Peak of Good Living.”

Local Culture and Customs of Apex

Apex is known for its friendly and welcoming community, and visitors should be prepared to experience Southern hospitality and charm. Some local customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Hospitality: Apex locals are known for their welcoming nature, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation or offer assistance.
  • History and Tradition: With a rich history dating back to the 1800s, Apex residents take pride in preserving the town’s heritage and traditions.
  • Religious Faith: Many Apex residents are deeply committed to their religious faith, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Apex

The best time to visit Apex is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the colors of the surrounding nature are at their peak. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy, making shoulder seasons the best time to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apex

Do:

  • Do try the local BBQ and other Southern cuisine.
  • Do explore Jordan Lake State Recreation Area and other natural areas.
  • Do engage with locals and enjoy the Southern hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip at restaurants and cafes.
  • Don’t ignore the local history and cultural attractions.
  • Don’t underestimate the heat and humidity during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apex

Apex is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety, including:

  • Locking car doors and keeping valuables out of sight.
  • Staying aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking.
  • Knowing the location of emergency services and hospitals in case of an emergency.

Health in Apex

Apex has several medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care clinics, that are equipped to handle emergency and non-emergency medical needs. Visitors should also take precautions to protect themselves from ticks and mosquitoes, which can carry diseases.

Getting to Apex: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apex

The closest airport to Apex is Raleigh-Durham International Airport (RDU), which is located approximately 25 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Apex

The Chapel Hill Transit and Triangle Transit have several bus routes that connect to Apex and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Renting a car or taking a taxi or ride-sharing service are also good options for getting to and around Apex.

How to Get Around in Apex

The easiest way to get around Apex includes renting a car or taking a taxi or ride-sharing service. For those looking to explore the town on foot or by bike, several paved trails connect to the town’s parks and natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Apex: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Apex is located in a prime location for exploring other nearby towns, cities, and attractions. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Durham: Home to several prestigious universities, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • Raleigh: The state capital has plenty of historic sites, shopping districts, and food and drink options.
  • The North Carolina Coast: Several beautiful beaches and coastal towns are within a few hours’ drive of Apex.

Conclusion

Apex is a hidden gem in the heart of North Carolina’s Piedmont region, offering a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and Southern charm. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and local breweries, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Use this travel guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in Apex.
