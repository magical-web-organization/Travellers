Introduction to Smithfield-Selma

Smithfield-Selma is a small town located in Johnston County, North Carolina. The town’s history dates back to the early 18th century when the area was inhabited by Native Americans. Smithfield-Selma was originally called Smithfield and was incorporated in 1777. The town was named after John Smith, one of the town’s early settlers. In 1969, the town was merged with the neighboring town of Selma, and the name was changed to Smithfield-Selma.

List of Places to Visit in Smithfield-Selma

There are many places to visit in Smithfield-Selma. Here is a list of some of the top attractions in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ava Gardner Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and career of one of Hollywood’s most iconic actresses, Ava Gardner. The museum houses a collection of items that belonged to the actress, including costumes, photographs, and memorabilia.
  • Johnston County Heritage Center: The center is housed in a former courthouse and features exhibits that tell the story of the county’s history.
  • Bentonville Battlefield State Historic Site: This site was the site of a Civil War battle and offers visitors the opportunity to learn about the history of the conflict.

Natural Attractions of Smithfield-Selma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Neuse River Trail: The trail offers visitors the opportunity to hike, bike, and horseback ride along the scenic Neuse River.
  • Howell Woods Environmental Learning Center: The center offers visitors a chance to explore the area’s diverse flora and fauna through guided hikes, fishing, and birdwatching.
  • Smithfield Community Park: The park features a playground, walking trails, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smithfield-Selma

Smithfield-Selma is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in town:

  • Buffalo Creek Greenway: The greenway offers excellent hiking and biking opportunities, with several trailheads located throughout the town.
  • Neuse River Greenway Trail: The trail follows the Neuse River for 33 miles and is perfect for hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • Smithfield-Selma Rail Trail: This trail follows the route of the former Norfolk Southern Railway and is ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Smithfield-Selma: Activities and Tours

When in Smithfield-Selma, there are plenty of activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in town:

  • Visit Smithfield Farmers Market: The market features local produce, crafts, and baked goods.
  • Go fishing: Smithfield-Selma is home to several excellent fishing spots, including the Neuse River and the Howell Woods Environmental Learning Center.
