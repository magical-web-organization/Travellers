Introduction to Durham

Durham is a city located in the Piedmont region of North Carolina in the United States. Durham is part of the Research Triangle, a hub for high-tech industries in the southeastern United States. Durham has a rich history and culture, boasting several museums, historical sites, great outdoor experiences, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Durham

Durham offers a variety of attractions for visitors. Here are some of the top places to visit in Durham:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Duke University Chapel

The Duke University Chapel is located at the heart of Duke’s West Campus. It boasts neo-Gothic architecture, stained glass windows, and a grand pipe organ. Visitors can attend Christian services, concerts, and other events.

The Museum of Life and Science

The Museum of Life and Science is a family-friendly destination that offers interactive exhibits on science and nature. The museum has two indoor buildings and an outdoor park with over 84 acres of exhibits, animals, and playgrounds.

The Nasher Museum of Art at Duke University

Located on the Duke University campus, the Nasher Museum of Art focuses on contemporary and modern art. It features both permanent and rotating exhibits from artists around the world.

Natural Attractions of Durham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eno River State Park

Eno River State Park is a scenic park with over 30 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. Visitors can also kayak, fish, or swim in the river. The park also has several historic structures, including a restored 19th-century mill.

Sarah P. Duke Gardens

The Sarah P. Duke Gardens is a stunning 55-acre oasis located at Duke University. Visitors can stroll through the gardens and enjoy the variety of plants, flowers, and sculptures.

The American Tobacco Trail

The American Tobacco Trail is a 22-mile trail that connects Durham to the neighboring town of Cary. The trail is ideal for hiking, biking, or jogging, and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Durham

Durham has several great hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the top routes:

The Carolina Tobacco Trail

The Carolina Tobacco Trail is a 22.6-mile trail that runs from Durham to Wake County. The trail passes through several historic towns and landmarks, including the Chatham County Courthouse and the American Tobacco Trail.

The Al Buehler Trail

The Al Buehler Trail is a 3.1-mile loop that encircles the Duke University Golf Course. The trail is ideal for walking, jogging, or biking, and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and landscapes.

The Duke Forest Trail

The Duke Forest Trail is a network of over 7,000 acres of forestland owned by Duke University. The trails offer a variety of options for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Durham: Activities and Tours

Durham offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Durham:

Durham Bulls Athletic Park

The Durham Bulls Athletic Park is home to the Durham Bulls, a minor league baseball team. Visitors can enjoy a game while indulging in some of the best pub fare in the area.

The Durham Performing Arts Center

The Durham Performing Arts Center is a state-of-the-art theater that attracts world-renowned performers and productions. The center hosts a variety of shows, including musicals, plays, and concerts.

The Tobacco Road Tour

The Tobacco Road Tour is a guided bus tour that takes visitors through the region’s tobacco history. Visitors will learn about the rich history of tobacco and its profound impact on the region’s economy and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Durham

Here is a one-week itinerary to explore Durham:

Weekday Activity
Monday Visit the Eno River State Park and explore some of the hiking trails.
Tuesday Tour the Museum of Life and Science and learn about the science behind the exhibits.
Wednesday Stroll through the Sarah P. Duke Gardens and enjoy the tranquil beauty of the lush flora and foliage.
Thursday Explore the local food and drink scene with a Durham Tasting Tour.
Friday Visit the Nasher Museum of Art and appreciate modern artwork and sculptures.
Saturday Take a guided tour of the Duke University campus and learn about the university’s rich history and culture.
Sunday Take a leisurely bike ride on The American Tobacco Trail and enjoy the scenic views along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Durham

Must-Try Dishes

BBQ

Durham is known for its BBQ - pulled pork and brisket are particularly popular. Some of the best spots include The Pit, Backyard BBQ Pit, and Bullock’s Bar-B-Cue.

Fried Chicken

Fried Chicken is a favorite comfort food in Durham. Dame’s Chicken & Waffles and Rose’s Meat Market and Sweet Shop are some of the top recommended spots.

Sweet Potato Pie

Sweet Potato Pie is a dessert local to the area and an absolute must-try. Some of the best places to try it are Loaf, Scratch Bakery, and The Parlour.

Local Beverages

Fullsteam Brewery

Fullsteam Brewery offers a range of craft beers served in a relaxed and welcoming environment with food trucks visiting daily.

Ponysaurus Brewing Company

Ponysaurus Brewing Company serves a selection of hand-crafted beers in a modern and inviting taproom and beer garden.

Where to Stay in Durham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

21c Museum Hotel Durham

21c Museum Hotel Durham offers luxury accommodations in a historic building renovated as a contemporary art museum.

Washington Duke Inn & Golf Club

Washington Duke Inn & Golf Club is a luxury hotel located on the Duke University Campus. It boasts high-end amenities, a spa, and a golf course.

The Durham Hotel

The Durham Hotel offers a blend between modern and vintage, with an emphasis on industrial design and excellent service.

What to Buy in Durham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bull City Olive Oil

Bull City Olive Oil is a local shop selling artisanal olive oils and imported balsamic vinegars. Many products are unique to the area and make for excellent souvenirs to bring back home.

Runaway Clothing

Runaway Clothing is a local shop selling trendy and stylish Durham-inspired merchandise, from t-shirts and hats to enamel pins and stickers.

Local Culture and Customs of Durham

Durham is a diverse and accepting community. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s customs and local traditions and enjoy the area’s unique culture.

Best Time to Visit Durham

The best time to visit Durham is during the spring or fall, as the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. September and October are popular months when the weather is pleasant, and the leaves change colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Durham

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and try BBQ, sweet potato pie, and fried chicken.
  • Do take a guided tour to gain a better understanding of the history and culture of the area.
  • Do visit the outdoor attractions and enjoy the natural beauty of Durham.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Durham prides itself on maintaining a clean and beautiful environment.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the local community and its traditions.
  • Don’t visit during the summer months when temperatures and humidity can be high.

Safety and Health

Safety in Durham

Durham is generally a safe place to visit, as long as visitors take normal precautions. Visitors should be mindful of their surroundings, not leave valuable items visible in cars, and lock doors and windows when leaving a residence.

Health in Durham

Visitors should bring all the necessary medications and inform their doctor of their travel plans. Visitors should also be careful when hiking or participating in outdoor activities and wear appropriate clothing and sun protection.

Getting to Durham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Durham

The Raleigh-Durham International Airport is the closest airport to Durham. It offers a wide range of domestic and international flights and is only a 20-minute drive from Durham.

Bus Stations Near Durham

The Durham Transit Center is downtown and accessible to riders on several local routes. The Greyhound Bus Station is located right outside of downtown Durham.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Durham through the Amtrak train station, located in downtown Durham.

How to Get Around in Durham

The best way to get around Durham is by car, bike, or public transportation. The city offers several bus routes through GoDurham Transit and a trolley that runs through downtown. Bike racks are available on all buses for easy transport, and rental bike services are also accessible.

Where to Go Next from Durham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chapel Hill

Just 10 miles west of Durham, Chapel Hill is home to the University of North Carolina and boasts a beautiful campus, top-rated restaurants, and a busy cultural calendar.

Raleigh

Only 25 miles south-west of Durham, Raleigh offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, including museums, art galleries, and live entertainment.

Conclusion

With its vibrant history, culture, and natural beauty, Durham offers a unique experience to visitors. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and local cuisine, Durham is sure to satisfy any traveler’s interests.
