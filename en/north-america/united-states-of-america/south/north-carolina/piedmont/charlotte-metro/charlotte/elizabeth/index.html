Introduction to Elizabeth

Elizabeth is a charming and historic neighborhood located in Charlotte, North Carolina. It is named after the first neighborhood school, which was established in 1891 and named after Queen Elizabeth. Today, Elizabeth is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, offering a unique blend of historic sites, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and a vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Elizabeth

Here are some of the top places to visit in Elizabeth:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roseland Cemetery and Elizabeth Cemetery: These two historic cemeteries are located next to each other and are the final resting places of many important figures in Charlotte’s history, including Dr. Annie Alexander, the first licensed female physician in North Carolina, and Thomas Wolfe, the famous American author.
  • VanLandingham Estate: This beautiful mansion was built in 1913 and has been a part of Charlotte’s history ever since. Today, it serves as a popular event venue and bed and breakfast, but visitors come to see its exquisite architecture and lush gardens.
  • The Mint Museum: This museum is dedicated to the art and culture of the American South and features a collection of over 34,000 objects, including paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts.

Natural Attractions of Elizabeth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Independence Park: This beautiful park is located in the heart of Elizabeth and features winding pathways, picnic areas, and a large pond. It is a popular spot for locals to relax and enjoy the outdoors.
  • Little Sugar Creek Greenway: This 19-mile greenway runs through Elizabeth and provides a scenic path for walking, running, and biking. It is a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • The Duke Mansion: This historic mansion was built in 1915 and sits on four and a half acres of beautiful gardens. Visitors can take a guided tour of the mansion or stroll through the gardens on their own.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elizabeth

The Little Sugar Creek Greenway is the perfect place to go for a hike or bike ride in Elizabeth. This greenway runs through the neighborhood and provides a scenic path for walking, running, and biking. The trail is paved and offers a safe and enjoyable way to explore the neighborhood and its surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Elizabeth: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Elizabeth:

  • Take a guided tour of the historic VanLandingham Estate and learn about its fascinating history.
  • Visit the Mint Museum and admire the art and culture of the American South.
  • Explore the Little Sugar Creek Greenway on foot or by bike and take in the beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Elizabeth

Elizabeth has a vibrant food and drink scene, featuring a wide range of options for every taste and budget.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: North Carolina is known for its barbecue, and Elizabeth has some of the best around. Try the pulled pork sandwich from Midwood Smokehouse for an authentic North Carolina experience.
  • Fried Chicken: Price’s Chicken Coop is a Charlotte institution and serves up some of the best fried chicken in the city.
  • Burgers: The Diamond Restaurant has been serving up classic burgers and hot dogs since 1945.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Elizabeth is home to several outstanding breweries, including Legion Brewing and Wooden Robot Brewery.
  • Sweet Tea: No trip to the South is complete without a refreshing glass of sweet tea. Try the sweet tea at Sunflour Bakery for a local twist on this classic beverage.

Where to Stay in Elizabeth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Elizabeth offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the top places to stay in Elizabeth:

  • The Duke Mansion: This historic mansion turned bed and breakfast offers luxury accommodations and beautiful gardens.
  • The VanLandingham Estate: This historic mansion turned event venue and bed and breakfast is a Charlotte landmark.
  • The Morehead Inn: This boutique hotel offers stylish accommodations in a historic setting.

What to Buy in Elizabeth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Elizabeth, here are some ideas:

  • Local Honey: The Charlotte Regional Farmers Market sells local honey that is a sweet and delicious souvenir.
  • Artisanal Goods: The Elizabeth neighborhood is home to several artisanal shops, including Green with Envy and Julia’s Cafe and Books, which offer a selection of unique gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Elizabeth

Elizabeth has a rich history and a strong local culture. Some of the customs and traditions that you may encounter during your visit include:

  • Southern Hospitality: The people of Charlotte are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and you can expect to be greeted warmly wherever you go.
  • Barbecue: North Carolina-style barbecue is a local favorite and a must-try during your visit.
  • Historic Architecture: Elizabeth is home to several historic homes and buildings, and many of them have been lovingly restored and preserved to reflect the area’s rich history.

Best Time to Visit Elizabeth

The best time to visit Elizabeth is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Winter can be cold and dreary, and summers can be hot and humid, so plan accordingly.

Getting to Elizabeth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Elizabeth

The closest airport to Elizabeth is Charlotte Douglas International Airport, which is located just a few miles away. From there, you can take a taxi, shuttle, or rental car to get to Elizabeth.

Bus Stations Near Elizabeth

The Greyhound bus station is located in downtown Charlotte, just a few miles from Elizabeth. From there, you can take a taxi or public transportation to get to your destination.

Other Options

Elizabeth is also easily accessible by car, and there are several parking options available throughout the neighborhood.

How to Get Around in Elizabeth

Elizabeth is a walkable neighborhood, and most attractions are located within easy walking distance. If you prefer to bike or drive, there are several rental options available throughout the neighborhood.
