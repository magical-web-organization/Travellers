Introduction to University City

Located in the Charlotte Metro area of North Carolina, University City is a bustling and vibrant area known primarily for its collegiate atmosphere. Home to the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, the area is also home to numerous cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local eateries. Whether you’re a student, researcher, or just a curious traveler, there’s plenty to see and do in University City.

List of Places to Visit in University City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • UNC Charlotte Botanical Gardens: Explore over seven acres of gardens and woodlands, including a Japanese Garden, native Carolinian plants, a greenhouse, and a garden store.
  • Mint Museum of Craft & Design: Featuring contemporary craft and design, the Mint Museum is known for its unique glass collection and a robust itinerary of rotating exhibitions.
  • Historic Rosedale Plantation: Guided tours of this preserved antebellum plantation provide insight into Charlotte’s history. Guests learn about the Beatties, a family that owned the land for more than 100 years, and about the significance of livestock, farming, and slavery in the antebellum South.
  • Levine Museum of the New South: A nod to Charlotte’s past can be found at this museum which detials the state’s history from the end of the Civil War to the present day.

Natural Attractions of University City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Reedy Creek Park and Nature Preserve: This outdoor space offers many opportunities for picnics, hiking, and fishing. There are several trails, including one that’s paved, and a boardwalk through a forested wetland.
  • Highland Creek Greenway Trail: This paved trail offers scenic views of the surrounding area and is suited for walkers, runners, and cyclists.
  • McAlpine Creek Park: This 100-acre park has a disc golf course, a large lake with fishing access, and numerous trails for hiking and walking.
  • Robbins Park: Found just outside the University City area and into Cornelius, this park showcases a peaceful lake plus trails around the nature preserve. Perfect for spending an afternoon immersed in North Carolina nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of University City

  • Rocky River Trail: This trail spans two different parks and stretches for nearly eight miles, making it ideal for a day-long excursion.
  • Clark’s Creek Nature Preserve Trail: This preserve offers a relatively short (1.1-mile) hike, as well as the chance to see wildlife such as deer, wild turkeys, and birds of prey.
  • Mallard Creek Greenway: A paved trail that runs for over two miles and connects UNC Charlotte with nearby neighborhoods. Buzzing with sights and sounds of nature, visitors can also get plenty of exercise in by walking along the trail.

Best Things to Do in University City: Activities and Tours

  • Charlotte Motor Speedway: One of the top motor racing facilities in the country and home to NASCAR, Charlotte Motor Speedway offers behind-the-scenes tours, as well as driving experiences and special events throughout the year.
  • Charlotte Brews Cruise: Discover some of the city’s best and most creative breweries while taking in the beautiful sights of the University City area with this tour.
  • Sky High Sports Trampoline Park: Get some air while playing dodgeball, jumping on a giant trampoline, and performing gravity-defying gymnastics maneuvers at this trampoline park.
  • Discovery Place: This is a state-of-the-art science museum with unique hands-on experiences such as an aquarium and planetarium. This is also a great place to take the entire family.

Week-Long Itinerary for University City

Day Activity
1 Visit the UNC Botanical Gardens, then stop by one of University City’s local breweries such as Wooden Robot or Resident Culture.
2 Take a walk through Historic Rosedale Plantation, then explore the shops and local eats at UNC Charlotte’s student-driven Popp Martin Student Union.
3 Head to the Mint Museum of Craft & Design before taking a walk on the Highland Creek Greenway Trail.
4 Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the serene waters of Lake Norman, one of North Carolina’s most popular lakeside getaways.
5 Marvel at the Charlotte skyline from behind home plate at Truist Field while watching a Charlotte Knights game.
6 Visit the NASCAR Hall of Fame, one of Charlotte’s top attractions.
7 End your trip with some retail therapy at Concord Mills, a massive shopping mall with several eateries.

Food and Drink Scene of University City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: From traditional Carolina-style to smoked meats and brisket, barbecue is a North Carolina staple. The city is home to several award-winning joints, including Smoke Pit BBQ and Bubba’s BBQ.
  • Burgers: Known for serving up some of the best burgers in the city, Shake Shack is a must-visit when in University City.
  • Southern Classics: Grab a Southern favorite like chicken and waffles at Famous Toastery or chicken pot pie at Nantucket Grill.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The University City area is home to plenty of local breweries, including Wooden Robot Brewery, NoDa Brewing Company, and Birdsong Brewing Company, each offering unique and flavorful craft beers.
  • Wine: Sip on locally sourced wines at the urban-chic Corkbuzz in SouthPark Mall.

Where to Stay in University City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Charlotte University Place: This hotel offers guests a prime location in the heart of University City with spectacular views of Lake Norman.
  • Residence Inn Concord: Just thirty minutes from the city center, this hotel boasts an outdoor pool, hot tub, and on-site restaurant.
  • Hyatt Place Charlotte Airport/Tyvola Road: Located near Charlotte Douglas International Airport, this hotel offers spacious suites, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in University City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sports Souvenirs: Head to Truist Field or Spectrum Center for Charlotte sports-related merchandise.
  • UNC Charlotte Gear: Show off your 49ers pride with a t-shirt, hoodie, or hat from the university bookstore.
  • Locally Made Soaps and Candles: Check out local boutiques such as Savory Moments or 523 Handmade for organic, handmade soaps and eco-friendly candles.

Local Culture and Customs of University City

  • University Life: Home to the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, the area exudes a true college-town atmosphere, with arts and culture events catering to the university’s faculty, staff, and students.
  • NASCAR and Motorsports: As one of the largest NASCAR hosts, the Queen City embodies a love of high-octane motorsports.
  • Southern Hospitality: North Carolinians know how to make visitors feel at home and are ready to offer smiles and assistance to those visiting their city.

Best Time to Visit University City

The best time to visit University City is during the fall and spring, as the weather is mild and the area isn’t too crowded. The summer months can be hot and humid, whereas the winter months can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of University City

Do:

  • Visit Local Breweries: Sample some of the unique and flavorful craft beers that the area has to offer.
  • Explore Nature: Spend time outdoors exploring the beautiful lakes, forests, and parks that University City has to offer.
  • Embrace Southern Culture: Try local dishes, attend local events, and visit historical sights to embrace the southern charm and unique culture of the city.

Don’t:

  • Skip Parking Regulations: Be sure to park in designated areas to avoid towing fines.
  • Disrespect Local Customs: North Carolinians are friendly and hospitable, so be sure to treat locals with respect and kindness.
  • Underestimate Traffic: Charlotte’s traffic can be heavy during rush hour, so plan your travels accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in University City

Overall, University City is a relatively safe area, but visitors should still take precautions, such as staying alert and locking doors and windows.

Health in University City

Visitors should apply sunscreen and stay hydrated when venturing outdoors, especially during the summer months. North Carolina has many allergens, so be sure to check pollen forecasts and bring appropriate medication if needed.

Getting to University City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near University City

  • Charlotte Douglas International Airport (CLT): Located about 20 minutes away from University City by car or shuttle.

Bus Stations Near University City

  • Charlotte Transportation Center: Offers bus service around and within the Charlotte area.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car from the airport is a popular option for travelers wanting to explore the area.
  • Rideshare Services: Uber and Lyft both operate within the Charlotte area.

How to Get Around in University City

  • CATS Bus: The local bus service (Charlotte Area Transit System) offers multiple routes within University City and throughout Charlotte.
  • Light Rail: The LYNX Blue Line runs from UNC Charlotte to South End in Charlotte, making several stops along the way.
  • Biking and Walking Trails: Many University City residents enjoy walking, jogging, or biking as a way to commute in the area.

Where to Go Next from University City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Concord: Just a 25-minute drive from University City, this quiet town has an exciting motorsports scene with attractions like Charlotte Motor Speedway and the NASCAR Hall of Fame.
  • Asheville: A two-hour drive from University City, Asheville is a beautiful mountain city with many popular attractions, including The Biltmore Mansion, Grove Park Inn, and River Arts District.
  • Winston-Salem: Known for its history and architecture, Winston-Salem is a three-hour drive from the University City area. Visitors can enjoy tours of old tobacco factories and historic buildings in Old Salem.

Conclusion

University City is a fantastic destination to visit for those looking to experience a college town with a dose of Southern hospitality. From scenic trails to local breweries, there’s something for everyone in this lively and vibrant neighborhood.
