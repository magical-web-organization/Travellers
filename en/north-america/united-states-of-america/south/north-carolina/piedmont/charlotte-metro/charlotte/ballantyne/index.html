Introduction to Ballantyne

Ballantyne is a picturesque neighborhood located in Charlotte Metro, Piedmont, North Carolina. Known for its upscale vibe, golf courses, and green spaces, this charming destination attracts travelers seeking a relaxing retreat with convenient access to city amenities and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, a culture seeker, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Ballantyne has something to offer. In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a list of must-visit attractions, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, week-long itinerary, food and drink scene, accommodations, what to buy, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Ballantyne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ballantyne has a rich history and cultural heritage that you can experience by visiting its museums and historical sites. Here are some of the most significant attractions:

  • The Ballantyne Hotel and Lodge: This luxury hotel offers world-class amenities, spa treatments, golf courses, and dining options. It also features an art collection with more than 50 original paintings, including works by Matisse, Warhol, and Botero.
  • Carolina History and Haunts: Take a ghost tour and explore the history and legends of Charlotte and its surrounding areas.
  • Charlotte Museum of History: This museum showcases the city’s and region’s history, from the Native American culture to modern-day Charlotte.
  • Levine Museum of the New South: This museum offers exhibits and events illustrating the history of the Carolina Piedmont since the civil war to the present day.
  • President James K. Polk State Historic Site: Discover the life and legacy of the 11th US President, who was born in Pineville, near Ballantyne.

Natural Attractions of Ballantyne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ballantyne is home to many parks, trails, and outdoor spaces that offer a change of scenery and a chance to unwind. Here are some of the most impressive natural attractions:

  • Ballantyne Corporate Park: A 535-acre green space, ideal for a stroll, bike ride, or family picnic.
  • Four Mile Creek Greenway: A 2.3-mile paved trail that runs from McAlpine Creek Park to Matthews Township Parkway, with scenic views of the natural surroundings.
  • Reedy Creek Park: A 146-acre park with playgrounds, picnic areas, and sports fields, and trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • McDowell Nature Center and Preserve: A 1108-acre nature reserve with hiking trails through the wetlands, forests, and Lake Wylie shoreline.”,
  • Anne Springs Close Greenway: a 2,100-acre nature preserve offering hiking, horseback riding, fishing, camping, mountain biking, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ballantyne

Ballantyne is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy cycling, jogging, and hiking. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Ballantyne:

  • XCLT Trail: A 5.4 mile asphalt trail that winds around the Ballantyne Corporate Park and Four Mile Creek Greenway.
  • Little Sugar Creek Greenway: A 19-mile paved trail that runs through the Charlotte metropolitan area, offering public art displays and recreational activities.
  • McAlpine Creek Greenway Trail: A 8.2-mile paved trail that runs from South Charlotte Middle School to East 7th Street near downtown Charlotte.

Best Things to Do in Ballantyne: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking, biking, and visiting museums, Ballantyne has many attractions and activities to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Golfing: Ballantyne has several world-class golf courses, including the Ballantyne Country Club and the Golf Club at Ballantyne.
  • Carowinds: A theme park with roller coasters, water rides, live entertainment, and more, perfect for a fun family outing.
  • Escape Rooms: Challenge your problem-solving skills and teamwork by visiting popular escape rooms like Breakout Games Charlotte and Codescape.
  • Treetop Adventures: An outdoor adventure park offering climbing, zip-lining, and obstacle courses among the treetops.
  • Painting with a Twist: Unleash your inner artist and enjoy a painting session with friends and family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ballantyne

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ballantyne, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Ballantyne, check-in at Ballantyne Hotel, explore the art collection, and have dinner in Gallery Restaurant.
  • Day 2: Visit the Levine Museum of the New South, have lunch at the museum cafe, explore Charlotte’s Uptown, and have dinner at the Fig Tree Restaurant.
  • Day 3: Tee off at Ballantyne Country Club Golf Course, have lunch at the golf club, visit the President James K. Polk State Historic Site in Pineville, and have dinner at Baku.
  • Day 4: Go on a ghost tour with Carolina History and Haunts in Charlotte, have lunch at the Queen City Q, and shop at SouthPark Mall.
  • Day 5: Visit Reedy Creek Park and McDowell Nature Center and Preserve, hike the trails, picnic, and have dinner at Fahrenheit Restaurant.
  • Day 6: Take a painting class at Painting with a Twist, have lunch at Leroy Fox Ballantyne, and visit the Treetop Adventures Park in Indian Trail.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to Lake Norman State Park, hike the trails, picnic, and have dinner at Kindred Restaurant in Davidson.

Food and Drink Scene of Ballantyne

Ballantyne offers a diverse and exciting dining scene, with plenty of options for foodies. Whether you’re in the mood for Southern comfort food, international cuisine, or craft beer, you’ll find something to savor in Ballantyne. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and Grits: a Southern classic, made with creamy grits, shrimp, bacon, and herbs.
  • Fried Chicken: a crispy and juicy comfort food, often served with mashed potatoes or collard greens.
  • Barbecue: North Carolina is known for its barbecue, with several regional styles. Try the pulled pork with vinegar-based sauce for a classic taste.
  • Pimento Cheese: a creamy and tangy spread, made with shredded cheddar cheese, pimentos, and spices. Often served with crackers or vegetables.
  • Sweet Potato Pie: a sweet and spiced dessert, made with sweet potatoes, cinnamon, and nutmeg.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Ballantyne has several craft breweries, producing delicious beers with unique flavors. Be sure to try beers from Sugar Creek Brewing, Olde Mecklenburg Brewery, and Legion Brewing.
  • Sweet Tea: a Southern staple, sweet tea is a refreshing and sweetened iced tea ubiquitous at most restaurants.
  • Cider: Charlotte grows many apple orchards, so hard cider is very popular in the region. Check out Red Clay Ciderworks in Charlotte.

Where to Stay in Ballantyne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ballantyne has several accommodation options to suit every budget and style. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Ballantyne Hotel and Lodge: A luxury hotel with elegant rooms, dining options, spa, golf courses, and fitness center.
  • Staybridge Suites Charlotte Ballantyne: A cozy and affordable hotel with spacious suites, free breakfast, outdoor pool, and fitness center.
  • The Golf Club at Ballantyne: A stylish and elegant accommodation, located near the golf courses, and offering high-end amenities and dining options.

What to Buy in Ballantyne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts from your trip to Ballantyne, you can find some unique and authentic options in local shops and markets. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • Ballantyne Village: A shopping center with boutiques, specialty stores, restaurants, and a movie theater, perfect for a day of shopping and entertainment.
  • SouthPark Mall: A massive and popular shopping mall, featuring many renowned luxury brands and department stores.
  • Charlotte Regional Farmers Market: Open Wednesday through Sunday, each season. The Farmers Market features fresh produce, arts, crafts, plants, and vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Ballantyne

Ballantyne has a distinct culture and lifestyle, influenced by its history, geography, and social environment. Here are some of the key aspects of Ballantyne culture and customs:

  • Southern Hospitality: Locals are known for their warm and welcoming attitudes towards visitors, making visitors feel right at home.
  • Golf Culture: Ballantyne takes its golf culture seriously, attracting golfers from all over the world who come to experience the world-class courses.
  • BBQ Culture: North Carolina is known for its barbecue, and Ballantyne is no exception. There are many restaurants, roadside stands, and festivals dedicated to barbecue.
  • Rich History: Ballantyne has a long and multifaceted history, reflected in its museums, historic sites, and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Ballantyne

The best time to visit Ballantyne is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is moderate, and the outdoor activities are enjoyable. Summer is also an excellent time to visit Ballantyne, but the weather can be hot and humid. Winter can be cold with occasional snowfalls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ballantyne

Do:

  • Do Bicycling and Hiking: Ballantyne is an excellent place for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Eat Barbecue: Ballantyne is known for its delicious barbecue dishes, so don’t miss out on trying some local favorites.
  • Take a Ghost Tour: Discover the haunting legends and stories of the region’s history with a ghost tour from Carolina History and Haunts.
  • Shop and Sightseeing: Visit local shops for souvenirs of crafts, art and explore Ballantyne’s cultural history with museums and historical sights.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the Grist Mill: Visit the restored 19th-century Grist Mill in nearby Pineville, NC.
  • Don’t forget to Tip: Tipping is customary in the US for wait staff, bartenders, and other service staff.
  • Don’t Speed: Observe and obey the speed limit on Ballantyne roads and highways.
  • Don’t be Rude: Observe good manners and civility when interacting with locals or staff.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ballantyne

Ballantyne is generally safe for travelers, but as with any other place, it’s essential to take safety precautions. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use common sense: Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night. Keep your valuable items hidden securely.
  • Watch for traffic: Be careful when crossing the streets and obey traffic rules.
  • Don’t trust strangers: Be cautious when approached by strangers, especially in unpopulated areas or unlit streets.

Health in Ballantyne

Healthcare is widely available in Ballantyne, with many clinics and hospitals in the region. The city has excellent medical facilities, and emergency services are widely available.

Getting to Ballantyne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ballantyne

Charlotte Douglas International Airport (CLT) is the nearest major airport to Ballantyne, located about 25 miles (40 km) away. It is the sixth-busiest airport in the world, serving over 161 destinations.

Bus Stations Near Ballantyne

Public transportation in Ballantyne is a suburban area. CATS provides transit services to Charlotte-Mecklenburg counties. Multiple bus routes and Lynx Light Rail allow you to explore many areas within the region.

Other Options

Ballantyne is situated near several major interstates: I-485, I-77, and I-85 corridors. Getting around in Ballantyne is easy, with plenty of car rental agencies, ride-sharing apps, and taxis available.

How to Get Around in Ballantyne

Ballantyne is a sprawling suburban area, and having a car to explore the area is your best bet. However, if driving is not an option, you can use public transportation or arrange a ride-share or taxi service.
