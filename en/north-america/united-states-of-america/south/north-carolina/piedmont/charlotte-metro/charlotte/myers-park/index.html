Introduction to Myers Park

Nestled in the heart of Charlotte Metro, Myers Park is a beautiful neighborhood known for its tree-lined streets, exquisite architecture, and charming atmosphere. Established in the early 1900s, Myers Park is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and boasts a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its stunning museums, historical landmarks, and art galleries.

List of Places to Visit in Myers Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mint Museum: Located on Randolph Road, The Mint Museum is one of the largest museums in the Southeast. With an impressive collection of American, European, and African art, this museum is a must-visit for art enthusiasts.
  • The Duke Mansion: In the heart of Myers Park lies the elegant Duke Mansion, a historic home that has been restored and transformed into a bed and breakfast and event venue. Tourists can take a stroll through the beautiful gardens, or dine in their on-site restaurant.
  • The James K. Polk State Historic Site: A stone’s throw from Myers Park lies this historic home of former president James K. Polk. Visitors can tour the site to learn about his presidency and admire the beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of Myers Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Freedom Park: Freedom Park is a large, urban park that offers visitors a range of activities, from walking trails and picnic areas to tennis courts and a beautiful lake.
  • Reedy Creek Park: Located a short drive from Myers Park, Reedy Creek Park is a 146-acre park located in the middle of nature that offers hiking trails, bike paths, and outstanding scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Myers Park

Charlotte is a bike-friendly city, with an extensive system of trails and bike lanes that wind through Myers Park. Some of the most popular routes include the Little Sugar Creek Greenway, which runs through Myers Park and into Uptown, and the Cross Charlotte Trail, which runs from Pineville to the University area.

Best Things to Do in Myers Park: Activities and Tours

Some of the best activities and tours to do in Myers Park include:

  • Segway Tours: Take a Segway tour to explore the city and experience the beautiful landmarks.
  • Food Tours: Visit the unique restaurants and local breweries in Myers Park to sample delicious dishes and drinks.
  • Farmers Market: Visit the Myers Park Farmers Market to explore the vast array of locally grown produce and crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Myers Park

Must-Try Dishes

Myers Park offers a fantastic range of culinary delights that will tantalize your taste buds. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • The Duke Mansion’s Southern Breakfast: A sumptuous breakfast of classic Southern-style dishes.
  • Barrington’s’s Crab Cake: An exquisite appetizer made with local crabmeat and served with a remoulade sauce.
  • Flour Shop’s Pasta Dish: A mouth-watering selection of house-made pasta dishes that are sure to satisfy your taste buds.

Local Beverages

Myers Park is famous for its craft beer scene, with numerous breweries that create unique and flavorful beers.

Where to Stay in Myers Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Myers Park offers a great range of lodging options, from luxurious hotels to charming guesthouses.

  • The Duke Mansion: An elegant bed and breakfast located in the heart of Myers Park.
  • The Morehead Inn: A historic personification, this Victorian inn offers a romantic and relaxing retreat.

What to Buy in Myers Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for the perfect souvenir, Myers Park offers a selection of unique and local products that are sure to delight.

  • Charlotte-Southern Kitchen’s Chopped BBQ Sauce: A delicious sauce that’s perfect for grilling and roasting.
  • Charlotte’s own Booneshine: A unique and flavorful craft beer that’s a must-try.

Local Culture and Customs of Myers Park

Myers Park is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood that celebrates a range of cultural traditions and customs. One fabled part of the neighborhood is Fisher Farm Park, which is a living museum great for those who want to discover Myers Park’s culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Myers Park

Spring to fall is the best time to visit Myers Park. The autumn leaves make the neighborhood particularly enchanting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Myers Park

Do:

  • Visit the Myers Park Farmers Market

Don’t:

  • Ignore historical sites and buildings

Safety and Health

Safety in Myers Park

Myers Park is generally a safe neighborhood, but it’s always essential to be vigilant, lock up car doors and avoid poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Myers Park

For medical care, tourists may visit the nearby Novant Health Presbyterian Medical Center for any necessary healthcare needs.

Getting to Myers Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Myers Park

  • Charlotte/Douglas International Airport (CLT) - 7 miles

Bus Stations Near Myers Park

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - 0.2 miles
  • Charlotte Transportation Center - 3 miles

Other Options

Charlotte is a walkable city with several transportation options, including bike-sharing systems such as B-Cycle, taxis, and ride-share services like Lyft and Uber.

How to Get Around in Myers Park

Charlotte’s bike-sharing system, B-Cycle, has several bike stations around the neighborhood, making it an affordable and eco-friendly way to explore.
