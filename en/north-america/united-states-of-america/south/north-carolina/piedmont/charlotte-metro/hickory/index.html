Introduction to Hickory

Located in the Charlotte Metro area in the Piedmont region of North Carolina, lies the charming city of Hickory. The city is known for its breathtaking nature, outdoor activities, rich history and culture, and fabulous Southern cuisine and hospitality. Visitors to this city can expect a delightful blend of modern amenities and old-world charm, making it an ideal destination for any traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, exciting things to do, amazing hiking/biking routes, where to eat, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Hickory

Hickory offers a range of exciting places for visitors to explore, from museums and historical landmarks to scenic outdoor locations. Here are some places you shouldn’t miss on your visit to Hickory:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hickory is steeped in history and culture, offering a range of museums and historical places, including:

  • The Hickory Museum of Art: Located in the heart of Downtown Hickory, the Hickory Museum of Art is a treasure trove of art and culture, with a collection featuring works from globally renowned artists.
  • The Catawba Science Center: Perfect for families with children, this center has interactive exhibits on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.
  • The Harper House/Hickory History Center: A must-visit for history buffs, the Hickory History Center features exhibits detailing the history of the city and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Hickory: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hickory is blessed with natural beauty and offers an abundance of outdoor activities and stunning views, including:

  • Lake Hickory: The perfect spot for outdoor enthusiasts, offering activities such as boating, fishing, and kayaking, and miles of majestic shoreline for those looking to relax.
  • Hickory Ridge Living History Museum: Travel back in time with a visit to this open-air living museum featuring restored historical structures from the 18th and 19th centuries.
  • Bakers Mountain Park: A popular spot for hikers, this park offers miles of hiking trails with breathtaking views of the Piedmont region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hickory

Hickory is a popular destination for hikers and bikers, offering a range of trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Lake Hickory Trail: A 4.2-mile paved trail that circles Lake Hickory, offering stunning views of the lake’s shoreline and surrounding nature.
  • The Hickory Greenway: A series of paved trails winding through the city, passing many of Hickory’s cultural attractions and scenic spots.
  • The Bakers Mountain Park Trails: Offering various trails of varying lengths and difficulty levels, these trails provide incredible natural views.

Best Things to Do in Hickory: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Hickory offers a range of other exciting activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Hickory:

  • Hickory Crawdads Baseball Game: Catch a game of baseball with the local team, the Hickory Crawdads, at L. P. Frans Stadium.
  • Wine Tasting: Explore the Hickory Wine Trail and enjoy tastings at some of the best wineries in the region.
  • Hickory Motor Speedway: Witness the fastest half-mile track in the world at the Hickory Motor Speedway, a popular destination for racing fans.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hickory

Day 1: Visit the Hickory Museum of Art and spend an evening at a local brewery.

Day 2: Visit the Catawba Science Center followed by a leisurely stroll along the Hickory Greenway.

Day 3: Go hiking at the Bakers Mountain Park and enjoy the stunning views of the Piedmont region.

Day 4: Spend the day in the outdoors at the Lake Hickory and indulge in water sports.

Day 5: Explore the Hickory Ridge Living History Museum to learn about Hickory’s fascinating heritage.

Day 6: Watch a game of baseball with the Hickory Crawdads team at L.P. Frans Stadium.

Day 7: Take a relaxing wine tasting tour on the Hickory Wine Trail to end the week on a high note.

Food and Drink Scene of Hickory

No visit to Hickory is complete without indulging in its delectable Southern cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: This Southern classic is a must-try while in Hickory, with establishments such as Chicken in the Basket and Chicken Box serving up delicious interpretations of the dish.
  • BBQ: Hickory has a long history with BBQ, and there are several places in the city offering this Southern favorite.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: A unique breakfast item, biscuits and gravy are a Southern staple that visitors should try.

Local Beverages

  • Hickory Nut Gorge Brewery: Offering locally brewed beer with unique flavors and tastes, the brewery is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike.
  • Wine Tasting on the Hickory Wine Trail: Hickory and the surrounding region are known for their wine offerings; tours of the best vineyards in the region can be booked.

Where to Stay in Hickory: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hickory offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some options to consider:

  • The Hilton Garden Inn: Offering modern amenities, a convenient location, and excellent service, the Hilton Garden Inn is popular with business travelers and vacationers.
  • The Fairfield Inn and Suites by Marriott: A budget-friendly option that offers clean rooms, free breakfast, and an indoor pool.
  • The Courtyard by Marriott: Located in the heart of the city’s downtown area, this hotel is highly recommended for its proximity to many of Hickory’s best attractions.

What to Buy in Hickory: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hickory offers a range of unique and local products that make for perfect souvenirs, including:

  • Hickory Furniture: Known for its premium furnishings, Hickory offers buyers the opportunity to purchase unique and authentic Southern-style furniture to take home.
  • Hickory Nut Gap Farm Products: This family-owned farm offers organic and grass-fed beef, pork, and other products that are perfect for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Hickory

Hickory’s cultural identity is shaped by its history and its location in the South. Visitors to Hickory can expect genuine Southern hospitality and are encouraged to explore the city’s unique customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hickory

Hickory is a year-round destination, with each season offering different attractions and experiences. However, the best time to visit Hickory is from September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the leaves are in full fall colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hickory

Do:

  • Visit Lake Hickory and enjoy the many outdoor activities it has to offer.
  • Indulge in Southern cuisine at local eateries.
  • Spend time exploring Hickory’s unique cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Miss out on the unique hiking trails offered in the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hickory

Hickory is a safe city for visitors. However, as with any travel destination, visitors should take precautions and look out for their safety and belongings, especially when exploring the city’s downtown areas.

Health in Hickory

Hickory offers several healthcare facilities for visitors requiring medical assistance, including the Frye Regional Medical Center and the Catawba Valley Medical Center.

Getting to Hickory: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hickory

The nearest airport to Hickory is the Charlotte-Douglas International Airport, located approximately 50 miles southeast of the city. Visitors can hire a car with various rental companies or take a taxi or shuttle service to get to Hickory.

Bus Stations Near Hickory

Greyhound Bus Lines have a station in Hickory, making it an ideal option for those traveling on a budget.

Other Options

Hickory is easily accessible by car, with several highways connecting the city to neighboring states and cities.

How to Get Around in Hickory

Hickory offers several transportation options for visitors, including rental cars, taxis, and ride-share services such as Uber and Lyft. Visitors can also navigate the city using public transit with the Hickory Greenway offering numerous bike paths and walking trails.

Where to Go Next from Hickory: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Hickory, there is much more to discover in the surrounding area, including:

  • Asheville: This charming city offers visitors the chance to explore the natural beauty of the Blue Ridge Mountains, while also offering a range of cultural attractions.
  • Charlotte: Home to various cultural attractions and sporting events, Charlotte is a vibrant city that has something for everyone.
  • The Great Smoky Mountains National Park: A short drive from Hickory, this national park is visited for its breathtaking views and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Hickory is a Southern gem in North Carolina, offering visitors an ideal mix of modern amenities and old-world charm. From its natural beauty and outdoor activities to its museums and historical attractions, there is much to explore in Hickory. With this guide as your companion, you’ll have everything you need to make the most out of your visit to this wonderful city.
