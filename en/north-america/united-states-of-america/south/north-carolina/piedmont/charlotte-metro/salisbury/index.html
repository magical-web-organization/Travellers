Introduction to Salisbury

Salisbury is a hidden gem in North Carolina, offering a perfect blend of history, culture, and adventure. This charming city located in the Piedmont region promises tourists a glimpse of the past, with its 19th-century architecture and well-preserved heritage sites. At the same time, it also offers the thrill of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, camping, and fishing. There’s plenty to explore in this vibrant city, from its natural attractions to its cultural hotspots that make it a perfect holiday destination.

List of Places to Visit in Salisbury

If you’re wondering what to do in Salisbury, here are some places you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Rowan Museum - This museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It showcases the rich history of Rowan County, from the colonial period to the present day. The museum campus includes six historic buildings, including the 1854 courthouse.

  2. North Carolina Transportation Museum - The museum offers visitors a fascinating glimpse into North Carolina’s transportation history with exhibits on aviation, railroads, and automotive transportation. It also houses a collection of antique vehicles, full-size locomotives, and a historic train station that dates back to 1896.

  3. Historic Salisbury Foundation - The foundation offers guided tours of the city’s historic sites, including the Salisbury Confederate Prison, listed on the National Register of Historic Places and built-in 1861.

Natural Attractions of Salisbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dan Nicholas Park - This park covers over 400 acres and has something for everyone. There’s a lake for fishing and boating, hiking/biking trails, a campground, a carousel, and much more.

  2. Gold Hill Mines Historic Park - This park, once a thriving gold mining town, offers a glimpse into the past with its old mine shafts and eerie ruins. The park is also ideal for hiking and picnicking.

  3. Hurley Park - This 16-acre park is a botanical haven, featuring an extensive collection of plants, walking trails, and scenic views of Goose Creek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salisbury

If you’re an adventure lover, Salisbury has a lot to offer:

  1. The Salisbury Greenway - This 3.2-mile greenway connects several city parks and is perfect for walking, jogging, and biking.

  2. Dan Nicholas Park - The park has several trails for hiking and mountain biking.

  3. Gold Hill Mines Historic Park - Trails wind throughout the park, promising an exciting hiking experience over varying terrain.

Best Things to Do in Salisbury: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for activities and tours to do in Salisbury, here are our recommendations:

  1. Day trip to Lexington - Just 20 minutes away from Salisbury, Lexington is known as the BBQ capital of North Carolina and has a distinct southern charm.

  2. Zip Line Tour - Head to Treetop Adventures for the adrenaline-pumping experience of ziplining through the trees.

  3. Salisbury Ghost Walk - This guided walking tour takes you through the city’s dark alleys and haunted buildings, making it a fun and spooky experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salisbury

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Salisbury, here’s our suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Salisbury

When it comes to food, Salisbury offers a diverse and vibrant scene. Here are some local dishes and beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Salisbury Steak - This dish features a juicy burger patty smothered in gravy. It’s said to have originated in Salisbury, North Carolina.

  2. Cheerwine - It’s a cherry-flavored soda that’s popular in the Carolinas, and it pairs well with any dish.

  3. BBQ - Lexington-style BBQ is a must-try if you’re in Salisbury. It’s slow-cooked pork shoulder served with a tangy vinegar sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer - Salisbury has several local breweries and taprooms. Choose from a variety of craft beers, ranging from pale ale to stout.

  2. Wine - Rowan County is known for its vineyards and wineries. The county’s rolling hills produce some of the best wines in North Carolina.

Where to Stay in Salisbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Salisbury has a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and needs.

  1. Holiday Inn Express & Suites - A modern hotel with spacious rooms, a pool, and a fitness center.

  2. The Knob Hill Inn - A charming guesthouse with cozy rooms and a beautiful garden.

  3. The Charlotte Inn - This historic inn is perfect if you’re looking for a unique experience. It’s a short walk from downtown Salisbury, giving you easy access to restaurants and shops.

What to Buy in Salisbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Pottery - The Catawba Indian pottery has been a staple of Rowan County for centuries. The pottery makes for an excellent souvenir or gift.

  2. Antiques - The city has several antique shops that sell unique items such as vintage furniture, pottery, and glassware.

  3. Cheerwine - Take home a bottle of this local soda as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Salisbury

Salisbury prides itself on its historical and cultural heritage. The city is steeped in tradition, making it a unique place to visit. Be sure to show respect for local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Salisbury

The best months to visit Salisbury are from March to May and September to November – the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salisbury

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Take part in outdoor activities like hiking and fishing.

  3. Chat with the friendly locals.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage historic sites.

  2. Trespass on private property.

  3. Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salisbury

Salisbury is generally a safe city, but visitors are advised to take precautionary measures like avoiding remote areas after dark and not carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Salisbury

There are no health concerns specific to Salisbury. Visitors should follow general precautions such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated during outdoor activities.

Getting to Salisbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salisbury

Salisbury Regional Airport is the closest airport to the city. The airport is located just three miles northeast of downtown Salisbury.

Bus Stations Near Salisbury

Salisbury has a Greyhound bus station that provides transportation to several cities in the northeast and south.

Other Options

A rental car is an ideal option if you want to explore the area around Salisbury.

How to Get Around in Salisbury

Getting around Salisbury is relatively easy. You can walk within the city center, and there are several bike rental options available. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Salisbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby places worth considering after your visit to Salisbury:

  1. Charlotte - Just an hour’s drive from Salisbury, Charlotte is North Carolina’s largest city. It’s known for its museums, vibrant nightlife, and sports events.

  2. Asheville - Known for its mountain vibes and beautiful landscapes, this is the perfect destination for a relaxing weekend getaway.

  3. Winston-Salem - Known as the City of Arts and Innovation, Winston-Salem has a thriving arts and culture scene. It’s a must-visit destination for art lovers.

Conclusion

Salisbury has a long and rich history, stunning natural scenery, top-quality food, and a vibrant cultural scene. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you a good idea of what to expect when visiting Salisbury and that it helps you plan a memorable trip to this charming city in the heart of North Carolina.
