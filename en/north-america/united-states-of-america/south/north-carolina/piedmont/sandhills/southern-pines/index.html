Southern Pines Travel Guide: Discover the Best of Sandhills, North Carolina

Located in Sandhills, in the Piedmont region of North Carolina, Southern Pines is a charming town that offers visitors a glimpse into the natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and rich history of the South. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Southern Pines has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the town’s must-see attractions, hiking trails, activities and tours, local food and drinks, accommodations, souvenirs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Pines

Here are some of the best places to visit in Southern Pines:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shaw House and Gardens: This 1820 Federal-style home was once home to Charles Cameron, a prominent businessman and politician. Today, the house serves as a museum, allowing visitors to experience life in the 19th-century South.
  • Tufts Archives: This archives center showcases the history of Southern Pines, from its founding in the late 1800s to modern times. It is also the home of the Shaw House, and it offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the South.
  • Weymouth Center for the Arts and Humanities: This cultural center and historic mansion is dedicated to the preservation and promotion of Southern literature, arts, and culture. It is a popular venue for concerts, lectures, and other cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Southern Pines: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandhills Horticultural Gardens: This 32-acre botanical garden features more than 5,000 varieties of plants, including rare and endangered species from around the world. It is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Weymouth Woods Sandhills Nature Preserve: This 900-acre preserve is home to one of the last remaining longleaf pine forests in North Carolina. It offers visitors a chance to hike, bike, and explore the unique ecosystem of the Sandhills region.
  • Reservoir Park: This family-friendly park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and picnicking. It is also home to a miniature train ride and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Pines

Southern Pines is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are our top picks:

  • The Carolina Horse Park: This 250-acre equestrian center features more than 150 miles of scenic trails, perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding.
  • Southern Pines Bike Trails: This network of trails spans across multiple parks and greenways, offering visitors a chance to explore the town’s natural beauty on foot or on wheels.
  • Weymouth Woods Sandhills Nature Preserve: This nature preserve features several hiking trails, ranging from short, easy strolls to more challenging hikes that offer breathtaking views of the Sandhills region.

Best Things to Do in Southern Pines: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Southern Pines offers an array of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Horseback Riding: Southern Pines is known for its equestrian culture, and there are several local farms that offer horseback riding lessons and tours.
  • Golfing: The town is home to several world-renowned golf courses, including Pinehurst No. 2, which has hosted several U.S. Open Championships.
  • Wine Tasting: The area around Southern Pines is home to several wineries and vineyards, offering visitors a chance to taste local wines and learn about the region’s viticulture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Pines

Southern Pines may be a small town, but there’s plenty to see and do. Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day 1: Explore the Shaw House and Gardens and the Tufts Archives to learn about the town’s history.

Day 2: Spend the day at the Sandhills Horticultural Gardens, followed by a picnic in Reservoir Park.

Day 3: Hike the trails at Weymouth Woods Sandhills Nature Preserve and attend a concert or lecture at the Weymouth Center for the Arts and Humanities.

Day 4: Take a horseback riding lesson or tour local farms.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at one of the area’s world-renowned golf courses.

Day 6: Go on a wine tour and tasting excursion.

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing and taking in the town’s laid-back Southern charm.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Pines

Southern Pines offers visitors a chance to savor the flavors of the South, from classic BBQ to locally brewed beer. Here are our top picks for must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • NC BBQ: Pork is slow-cooked over hickory wood and served with a vinegar-based sauce.
  • Fried Chicken: A Southern staple, served with biscuits and gravy.
  • Sweet Potato Pie: A classic Southern dessert, made with sweet potatoes, cinnamon, and nutmeg.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A classic Southern drink, sweetened to perfection.
  • Craft Beer: Several local breweries offer a variety of beer styles, from IPA to stout.
  • Bourbon: Kentucky may be the bourbon capital of the world, but Southern Pines has its fair share of distilleries that produce top-notch bourbon.

Where to Stay in Southern Pines: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Pines offers a range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to quaint guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Carolina Hotel: This luxurious hotel offers world-class amenities, including a spa, restaurants, and a golf course.
  • The Jefferson Inn: This historic inn offers charming rooms and top-notch service.
  • Airbnb: Southern Pines has several Airbnb options, offering visitors a chance to stay in unique and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Southern Pines: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southern Pines is known for its equestrian culture, and there are several local shops that sell horse-related souvenirs and products. Visitors can also pick up locally made crafts, such as pottery and quilts.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Pines

Southern Pines is known for its laid-back, Southern charm. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and etiquette, such as saying “ma’am” and “sir” when addressing locals.

Best Time to Visit Southern Pines

The best time to visit Southern Pines is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. The town can get crowded during the summer months, when many tourists come to enjoy the golf courses and outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Pines

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including classic Southern dishes and locally brewed beer.
  • Do take time to explore the town’s rich history and culture.
  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities, including hiking and horseback riding.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to be respectful of local customs and etiquette.
  • Don’t litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Pines

Southern Pines is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their doors and not leaving valuables visible in their cars.

Health in Southern Pines

Southern Pines has several medical facilities, including the FirstHealth Moore Regional Hospital. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as ticks and venomous snakes.

Southern Pines is a small town, and the downtown area is easily walkable. Visitors can also rent bikes from several local shops to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Southern Pines: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southern Pines is an excellent base for exploring the Sandhills region and the broader Piedmont area of North Carolina. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:
