Introduction to Sevierville

Sevierville is a charming city located in Eastern Tennessee, United States. It is fondly known as the hometown of Dolly Parton, and her influence can be seen all over the city. Sevierville is a blend of the old and new, offering visitors a taste of Southern hospitality, adventure, and scenic beauty.

The city is located in an area that is popular for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, fishing, and white-water rafting. It is also home to several restaurants, shops, and museums. Sevierville is an excellent destination for solo travelers, couples, and families seeking a Southern-style getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Sevierville

Sevierville has several places to visit that are perfect for spending a day or an afternoon. Here are some top-rated attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dolly Parton Statue: Located in the heart of downtown Sevierville, this statue pays homage to the city’s most famous native, Dolly Parton.

  2. Sevier County Courthouse: This historic courthouse dates back to 1896 and is known for its beautiful architecture.

  3. Floyd Garrett’s Muscle Car Museum: This museum houses an impressive collection of American classic muscle cars and race cars.

Natural Attractions of Sevierville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Great Smoky Mountains National Park: The park is located a short drive away from Sevierville and offers over 800 miles of hiking trails, scenic drives, and stunning vistas.

  2. Douglas Lake: The lake is perfect for fishing, boating, and swimming.

  3. Forbidden Caverns: These caverns offer a unique underground experience and a chance to see beautiful rock formations, underground waterfalls, and natural chimneys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sevierville

Sevierville has several hiking and biking routes that attract outdoors enthusiasts. Here are some popular options:

  1. Laurel Falls Trail: This trail is one of the most popular hikes in the Smoky Mountains. It is a moderate hike that leads visitors to a beautiful waterfall.

  2. Gatlinburg Trail: This trail is a leisurely, easy hike that starts in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park and ends in the town of Gatlinburg.

  3. Cove Creek Loop Trail: This trail is also located inside the Great Smoky Mountains National Park and offers fantastic views of the area.

Best Things to Do in Sevierville: Activities and Tours

  1. Dollywood: Owned by Dolly Parton, this theme park offers rides, live music, and shows that showcase the culture of the region.

  2. Zipline Tours: Several companies offer zipline tours that allow visitors to see the beautiful scenery from a bird’s eye view.

  3. Horseback Riding: Visitors can explore the beautiful countryside of Sevierville riding a horse and learn about the region’s history and wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sevierville

Day 1: Visit Dollywood and experience the thrill of the rides and attractions.

Day 2: Explore the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.

Day 3: Enjoy a day of hiking or mountain biking at one of the various routes.

Day 4: Discover Sevierville’s history at the Sevier County Courthouse and Floyd Garrett’s Muscle Car Museum.

Day 5: Go shopping and try some local cuisine at one of the many shops and restaurants in downtown Sevierville.

Day 6: Relax and unwind at one of the area’s lakes, such as Douglas Lake.

Day 7: Take in the natural wonder of the Forbidden Caverns.

Food and Drink Scene of Sevierville

Must-Try Dishes

  1. BBQ Ribs and Pulled Pork: Sevierville is known for its delicious BBQ, and visitors won’t be disappointed.

  2. Fried Catfish: This Southern classic is a must-try, especially when served with hushpuppies and coleslaw.

  3. Sweet Potato Pie: Made with local sweet potatoes, this dessert is a staple of Southern cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Moonshine: The area around Sevierville has become known for its locally distilled moonshine. Visitors can take tours of distilleries and sample different flavors.

  2. Craft Beer: Several microbreweries around the city offer tasty brews that are perfect for unwinding after a day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Sevierville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sevierville has a range of accommodation options for different budgets and preferences. Some top-rated options include:

  1. Wilderness at the Smokies: This resort features indoor and outdoor water parks, a spa, and multiple dining options.

  2. Hampton Inn Sevierville: This hotel offers modern amenities and a great central location.

  3. Blue Mountain Mist Country Inn & Spa: This bed and breakfast is perfect for couples looking for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Sevierville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Moonshine: Visitors can purchase locally distilled moonshine in various flavors to take home and share with friends.

  2. Crafts: The region is famous for its handmade crafts, including pottery, wood crafts, and textiles.

  3. Souvenirs: Many shops in downtown Sevierville offer great options for souvenirs such as t-shirts, magnets, and postcards.

Local Culture and Customs of Sevierville

Sevierville is a city that embraces its Southern roots and traditions. Visitors should expect to encounter a friendly, welcoming population that takes pride in their heritage. Local customs include:

  1. Saying “y’all”: This is a common phrase used in the South as an informal way of addressing a group of people.

  2. Embracing traditional Southern cuisine: Visitors should try local classics like fried chicken, grits, and cornbread.

  3. Appreciating traditional music: Country, bluegrass, and gospel music are all important parts of Sevierville’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Sevierville

The best time to visit Sevierville is during the fall (late September to early November) when the leaves change color and the weather is mild. The spring (March to May) is also a great time to visit, as the wildflowers bloom. Summer (June to August) can be crowded, and the heat and humidity can make outdoor activities uncomfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sevierville

Do:

  1. Explore the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.

  2. Try local BBQ, moonshine, and sweet potato pie.

  3. Say “hello” and “thank you” - Southerners appreciate good manners!

Don’t:

  1. Ignore traffic laws - the police take their enforcement very seriously.

  2. Be disrespectful of locals or their traditions.

  3. Litter or harm the environment - the beauty of Sevierville relies on everyone’s responsibility to keep it clean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sevierville

Sevierville is generally a safe city, but visitors should still be cautious, especially at night. Some areas, such as downtown, can be crowded, so it’s essential to be aware of your surroundings and keep belongings secure.

Health in Sevierville

Sevierville has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care centers, in case of emergencies. Visitors should bring personal medications, such as allergy medication or inhalers, if needed.

Getting to Sevierville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sevierville

  1. McGhee Tyson Airport: Located in Knoxville, Tennessee, McGhee Tyson is the nearest international airport, 30 miles from Sevierville.

  2. Asheville Regional Airport: This airport is another option for visitors flying from other parts of the US or internationally. It is approximately 70 miles from Sevierville.

Bus Stations Near Sevierville

Greyhound Bus Lines offers transportation to the Knoxville Bus Station, which is approximately 30 miles from Sevierville.

Other Options

Sevierville is approximately 200 miles from Atlanta, Georgia, and a four-hour drive from Nashville, Tennessee. Cars are available for rent from all major airports, bus stations, and cities.

How to Get Around in Sevierville

Sevierville has plenty of options for getting around, including rental cars, taxis, and buses. However, driving is the most convenient way to explore the area, particularly outside of downtown.

Where to Go Next from Sevierville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Gatlinburg: A cozy mountain town just 15 miles from Sevierville, Gatlinburg is famous for its skiing, shopping, and entertainment.

  2. Pigeon Forge: Located only four miles from Sevierville, Pigeon Forge offers more entertainment options, including Dolly Parton’s Stampede dinner show.

  3. Asheville: Head south to Asheville, North Carolina, for its great food, breweries, and music scene.

Conclusion

Sevierville, Tennessee, offers visitors a unique blend of Southern hospitality, history, beauty, and adventure. Whether travelers are seeking outdoor exploration, cultural experiences, or just a great vacation destination the city has it all. From classic Southern cuisine to natural wonders, Sevierville has something for everyone.
