Introduction to Eastern Tennessee

Nestled in the southern part of the United States, Eastern Tennessee is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Known for its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and adventurous spirit, this region offers an array of attractions and activities for travelers of all types. Whether you’re looking to explore the Great Smoky Mountains, learn about the history of the Civil War, or simply relax and soak up the local culture, Eastern Tennessee has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Tennessee

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Eastern Tennessee that should not be missed in your next trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dollywood Theme Park: This iconic amusement park is a must-visit attraction for the whole family, with thrilling rides, live music shows, and classic Southern food.
  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: One of the most visited national parks in the country, the Great Smoky Mountains offer breathtaking views of lush forests, rolling hills, and stunning waterfalls.
  • Chattanooga Choo Choo: Take a tour of this historic train station turned hotel, and enjoy exhibits on the history of rail travel in America.
  • Civil War attractions: The Eastern Tennessee region was a crucial site during the Civil War, with many battles fought here. Visit important sites such as the Allendale Mansion, the Andrew Johnson National Historic Site, and the Battle of Franklin Trust.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Tennessee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Appalachian Trail: The iconic 2,200-mile hiking trail that stretches from Georgia to Maine passes through Eastern Tennessee, giving hikers access to some of the most stunning views and landscapes in the country.
  • Cumberland Gap National Historical Park: Located in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, this park offers over 80 miles of hiking trails, bird watching, and other outdoor activities.
  • Rocky Top Wine Trail: This scenic trail takes you through picturesque vineyards and wineries, offering tastings and tours of the local wines of Eastern Tennessee.
  • Tuckaleechee Caverns: These underground caves are over 20 million years old and offer guided tours that take visitors through awe-inspiring chambers and formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Tennessee

Eastern Tennessee offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the country, allowing visitors to explore the natural beauty and wildlife of the region. Here are some of the top trails to try:

  • AT Trail: The Appalachian Trail offers some challenging sections of the trail in Eastern Tennessee, but the amazing views make it worth the effort.
  • James White Parkway: This beautiful bike trail runs along the Tennessee River and provides stunning views of Knoxville’s skyline.
  • Big Ridge State Park Loop: This scenic trail takes hikers through rolling hills, historic structures, and diverse woodlands.
  • Natchez Trace Parkway: This iconic road offers cyclists over 400 miles of uninterrupted road through stunning scenery and cultural landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Tennessee: Activities and Tours

Eastern Tennessee offers an array of activities and tours for travelers who want to experience the local culture and history. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Moonshine Tasting: Eastern Tennessee is known for its moonshine, and visitors can taste test various flavors at local distilleries, including Ole Smoky Moonshine and Tennessee Legend Distillery.
  • Oak Ridge Secret City: In Oak Ridge, visitors can explore the sites of the Manhattan Project and learn about the history of atomic weapons and their impact on the world.
  • Market Square: Located in downtown Knoxville, Market Square is home to a thriving local community, featuring restaurants, shops, and farmers markets.
  • Ziplining and Rappelling: The Smoky Mountains offer adventurous activities such as zip-lining and rappelling, providing breathtaking views and an adrenaline rush.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Tennessee

Eastern Tennessee offers a variety of culinary experiences, ranging from traditional Southern dishes to innovative farm-to-table cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Chicken: A spicy twist on traditional fried chicken, hot chicken is a popular dish in Eastern Tennessee.
  • BBQ: Eastern Tennessee is famous for its BBQ, especially pulled pork and ribs.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: A staple Southern breakfast dish, biscuits and gravy is a hearty meal that will keep you going all day.

Local Beverages

  • Moonshine: A distilled spirit made from corn, moonshine is a popular local beverage in Eastern Tennessee.
  • Sweet tea: A classic Southern drink, sweet tea is a refreshing and sugary drink.
  • Craft beer: Eastern Tennessee has a thriving craft beer scene, with local breweries such as Blackberry Farm Brewery, Crafty Bastard Brewery, and Fanatic Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Eastern Tennessee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastern Tennessee offers a range of accommodation options to match any style and budget. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Blackberry Farm: A luxurious, award-winning country inn and farm in the Smoky Mountains.
  • The Guest House at Graceland: Elvis Presley’s former home-turned-hotel, offering stylish rooms and a range of amenities.
  • Lost Creek Cabins: Nestled in the mountains, these charming cabins offer a rustic escape with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Eastern Tennessee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eastern Tennessee has a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home as a memory of your trip. Here are some options:

  • Moonshine: Purchase some locally-made moonshine as a gift or to enjoy back home.
  • Pottery: Eastern Tennessee is known for its pottery, with many artisans and studios offering handmade pieces.
  • Handmade Crafts: Eastern Tennessee has a thriving arts and crafts community, offering handmade cards, quilts, and other unique items.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Tennessee

Eastern Tennessee is steeped in rich cultural heritage and traditions, and visitors can experience these traditions by attending local festivals and events such as the Gatlinburg Craftsmen’s Fair, the Smoky Mountains Songwriters Festival, and the National Cornbread Festival in South Pittsburg.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Tennessee

The best time to visit Eastern Tennessee is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is colorful. However, the region is beautiful in all seasons, each bringing its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Tennessee

Do:

  • Do try traditional Southern dishes such as hot chicken, BBQ, and biscuits and gravy.
  • Do take a hike or bike ride through the Great Smoky Mountains.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes to explore the many parks and trails in the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the steep terrain of the region.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray when exploring the outdoors.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations and tips.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Tennessee

Eastern Tennessee is generally safe, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as locking car doors and securing valuables. Visitors should also be aware of weather changes and potential natural hazards in the region, such as flash floods and steep terrain.

Health in Eastern Tennessee

Visitors should always carry enough medication with them and consult a healthcare provider before travel. The region also has an abundance of allergens and pollen, which can make it difficult for visitors with allergies.

Getting to Eastern Tennessee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastern Tennessee is easily accessed via air, bus, and car.

Airports Near Eastern Tennessee

  • Knoxville’s McGhee Tyson Airport (TYS): Located in Alcoa, TN.
  • Tri-Cities Airport (TRI): Located in Blountville, TN.
  • Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport (CHA): Located in Chattanooga, TN.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Tennessee

  • Downtown Knoxville Bus Station, Knoxville, TN.
  • Greyhound Bus Station, Chattanooga, TN.
  • Nashville Bus Station, Nashville, TN.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available at most airports and in main cities.
  • Amtrak trains and buses provide transportation options from major cities.

How to Get Around in Eastern Tennessee

The most convenient way to get around Eastern Tennessee is by car. However, visitors can also use public transport such as buses, taxis, and ride-sharing apps to travel between cities and tourist sites.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Tennessee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nashville, TN: The capital of Tennessee, Nashville is a vibrant city known for its live music scene, BBQ, and honky-tonk bars.
  • Atlanta, GA: Located about 3 hours south of Eastern Tennessee, Atlanta offers visitors a range of cultural attractions such as the Georgia Aquarium and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site.
  • Asheville, NC: A picturesque mountain town nestled in the Blue Ridge Mountains, Asheville offers a range of outdoor activities, breweries, and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Eastern Tennessee is a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect trip to this inviting and exciting region of the United States. Whether you’re looking for fine dining, mountain views, or a relaxing getaway, Eastern Tennessee has it all.
