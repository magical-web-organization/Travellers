Introduction to Gatlinburg

Nestled in the heart of the Great Smoky Mountains, Gatlinburg offers a unique travel experience for adventurers and nature lovers. This charming town boasts unmatched natural beauty, fascinating cultural landmarks, exciting outdoor adventures, and mouth-watering cuisines. Gatlinburg is an ideal destination for families, groups of friends, solo travelers looking to explore nature and enjoy the relaxed mountain lifestyle.

In this comprehensive tour guide to Gatlinburg, we share everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to this magical destination.

List of Places to Visit in Gatlinburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gatlinburg has a rich cultural history, and there’s no better way to experience it than visiting the town’s museums and historical landmarks. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Gatlinburg include:

  • Gatlinburg Skylift Park
  • Gatlinburg Space Needle
  • Hollywood Star Cars Museum
  • Arrowmont School of Arts and Crafts
  • Gatlinburg Arts & Crafts Community
  • Salt and Pepper Shaker Museum

Natural Attractions of Gatlinburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gatlinburg is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, and the place is perfect for exploring the outdoors. The town is home to fascinating wildlife, serene waterfalls, and winding trails that offer an unforgettable experience to visitors. Some of the must-see natural attractions in Gatlinburg include:

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park
  • Roaring Fork Motor Nature Trail
  • Gatlinburg Scenic Overlook
  • Ober Gatlinburg Ski Area and Amusement Park
  • Gatlinburg Trail
  • Cades Cove

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gatlinburg

Gatlinburg provides an extensive network of hiking and biking trails for all fitness levels. With over 800 miles of trails, visitors can explore the scenic beauty of the Great Smoky Mountains. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Gatlinburg include:

  • Alum Cave Bluffs Trail
  • Andrews Bald Trail
  • Rainbow Falls Trail
  • Roaring Fork Motor Nature Trail
  • Sugarland Mountain Trail
  • Cades Cove Loop Road

Best Things to Do in Gatlinburg: Activities and Tours

Gatlinburg offers an extensive range of activities to keep visitors entertained. From adventure sports to family-friendly activities, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the must-do things in Gatlinburg:

  • White water rafting
  • Zipline tours
  • Fishing and fly fishing
  • Horseback riding
  • Snow tubing
  • Mini-golf
  • Moonshine tasting
  • Ghost tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Gatlinburg

With so many things to see and do in Gatlinburg, visitors should plan a minimum of a week-long itinerary to see all the best that the town has to offer. Here’s an example of how you can spend seven days in Gatlinburg:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore downtown Gatlinburg, visit the Gatlinburg Space Needle, and check out the salt and pepper shaker museum.
Day 2 Take a scenic drive around the Great Smoky Mountains National Park and visit Clingmans Dome.
Day 3 Go hiking on the Rainbow Falls trail and visit the Gatlinburg Trail.
Day 4 Take a tour of the Arrowmont School of Arts and Crafts, and visit the Hollywood Star Cars Museum.
Day 5 Spend the day skiing and enjoying the amusement park at Ober Gatlinburg.
Day 6 Go fishing on the Pigeon River, and enjoy moonshine tastings in downtown Gatlinburg.
Day 7 Take a guided tour of the town and visit the Gatlinburg Arts & Crafts Community.

Food and Drink Scene of Gatlinburg

Must-Try Dishes

Gatlinburg is a haven for foodies that offers a wide range of culinary options. Sample the following local dishes and staple fare during your visit:

  • Fried catfish
  • Pancake stacks
  • Biscuits and gravy
  • Country ham
  • Pecan pie
  • Fried chicken
  • BBQ ribs

Local Beverages

Gatlinburg has a flourishing craft beer, distillery and winery scene. Make sure to try these local drinks while in town:

  • Tennessee whiskey
  • Local craft beers
  • Moonshine
  • Wines from the Smoky Mountain Winery

Where to Stay in Gatlinburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gatlinburg has an extensive range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, guest houses, and cabins. Here are some of the options:

  • Edgewater Hotel

What to Buy in Gatlinburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gatlinburg is known for its vibrant artistic scene. Visitors can buy souvenirs at local shops such as:

  • Handmade pottery and wooden utensils
  • Arts, crafts, and jewelry at the Gatlinburg Arts & Crafts Community
  • Souvenir t-shirts and hats
  • Customized scents at Aunt Mahalia’s Candies

Local Culture and Customs of Gatlinburg

Gatlinburg has a rich cultural history, and visitors can experience the town’s unique lifestyle and customs by participating in community events, taking guided tours and visiting cultural landmarks. It’s essential to take cultural sensitivities into account and to be respectful of the town’s customs during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Gatlinburg

Gatlinburg is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preference. The town sees snowy winters, stunning fall foliage, and temperate summers. Summer (June-August) is the peak season, while the spring and fall shoulder seasons offer mild weather with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gatlinburg

Do:

  • Respect the town’s cultural norms and customs
  • Engage in outdoor activities and adventures in the Great Smoky Mountains
  • Try the local foods, wines, and beers
  • Shop at the local artisans’ shops

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas
  • Disrespect the natural environment or wildlife
  • Ignore local customs or social etiquette

Safety and Health

Safety in Gatlinburg

Gatlinburg is a safe destination for visitors. However, it’s essential to always take standard safety precautions while exploring the town, including:

  • Keep valuables safe and locked away during outdoor activities
  • Be mindful of traffic and follow pedestrian rules
  • Wear protective gear during outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and skiing.

Health in Gatlinburg

While Gatlinburg doesn’t have a hospital, there are several healthcare facilities, including urgent care centers and pharmacies, located throughout town in case of emergency.

Getting to Gatlinburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gatlinburg

The nearest airport is McGhee Tyson Airport (TYS) in Knoxville, Tennessee, which is 42 miles away from Gatlinburg. Alternatively, travelers can fly into Nashville International Airport or Atlanta Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, both of which are within driving distance.

Bus Stations Near Gatlinburg

Greyhound Lines and other bus service providers offer bus services from the major cities across the US that stop in Knoxville or Sevierville, both of which are a short drive away from Gatlinburg.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Gatlinburg by renting a car and driving through scenic highways, including The Blue Ridge Parkway, The Smoky Mountains National Park, and more.

How to Get Around in Gatlinburg

Gatlinburg is a small town, and the best way to get around is on foot. However, visitors can also move around town via trolleys, taxis, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Gatlinburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
