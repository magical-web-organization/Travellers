Introduction to Farragut

Located in Eastern Tennessee, Farragut is a charming town that offers incredible outdoor adventures, exciting activities, and breathtaking views. Known for its rich history and warm Southern hospitality, Farragut is a perfect destination for those who want to explore the beauty of Tennessee. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover all of the must-see places, things to do, food to try, and where to stay in Farragut.

List of Places to Visit in Farragut

No tour guide to Farragut would be complete without highlighting some of the incredible places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

As you explore Farragut’s history, you will learn about its rich past and discover incredible museums and historical sites.

  • Admiral Farragut Statue: This statue pays homage to Admiral David Farragut, the first Admiral of the United States Navy.
  • Farragut Museum: Located in the Farragut Town Hall, this museum offers a glimpse into the town’s past and history.
  • Fort Dickerson Park: This historical landmark was a stronghold of the Union during the Civil War and now offers hiking trails.

Natural Attractions of Farragut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Farragut is blessed with abundant nature, and the town’s natural beauty is mesmerizing. Here are some of the best outdoor spots to explore.

  • Concord Park: A stunning park with a boat launch, picnic shelters, playground, and a paved greenway trail for walking, jogging, or biking.
  • Haw Ridge Park: This park is an outdoor lover’s paradise, with over 28 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and bird watching.
  • Melton Hill Lake: This beautiful lake offers a panoramic view and excellent fishing opportunities. Spend a relaxing day fishing or boating, then enjoy the breathtaking sunset views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Farragut

Farragut’s natural beauty is best explored on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in town.

  • Grigsby Chapel Greenway: A 2.1-mile asphalt trail, this greenway is ideal for walking, jogging, or biking.
  • Concord Park Trail: This 2-mile paved trail is great for hikers, bikers, and joggers, offering a view of the lake and the beautiful scenery of the park.
  • Northshore Greenway Trail: This 3.5-mile paved trail connects several parks, including Anchor Park, Carl Cowan Park, and Mayor Bob Leonard Park.

Best Things to Do in Farragut: Activities and Tours

Farragut offers plenty of fun activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the must-try things to do in town.

  • Farragut Farmers Market: Every Wednesday between May and September, locals and tourists gather to shop for fresh produce, local crafts, and wares.
  • Knoxville Food Tours: Experience Knoxville’s culinary scene by joining a food tour that will take you on a culinary adventure through some of the city’s best restaurants.
  • Sky High Sports: Have fun with your friends or family and jump on trampolines, play dodgeball, and enjoy other fun activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Farragut

If you have a week to spare and want to explore Farragut, here’s a suggested itinerary.

Day 1: Visit Farragut Museum and the Admiral Farragut Statue
Day 2: Take a morning hike at Haw Ridge Park, followed by an afternoon stroll at Concord Park
Day 3: Enjoy a day of fishing at Melton Hill Lake, then catch the beautiful sunset
Day 4: Take a Knoxville food tour and explore some of the best culinary offerings in the city
Day 5: Ride your bike on the Northshore Greenway Trail
Day 6: Shop at the Farragut Farmers Market and visit the Fort Dickerson Park
Day 7: Try some fun activities, including jumping on trampolines at Sky High Sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Farragut

Farragut offers a diverse range of culinary delights, from Southern-style fare to exotic international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

When visiting Farragut, don’t miss out on these delicious local dishes:

  • Fried Chicken: A Southern classic, fried chicken is a must-try dish that you won’t forget.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: Start your day with this hearty breakfast that will keep you full all day.
  • BBQ: As a Southern town, Farragut offers some of the best BBQ dishes, including pulled pork, ribs, and beef brisket.

Local Beverages

For non-alcoholic drinks, try some sweet tea, a Southern classic, or sample some of the locally produced apple cider. Or, if you prefer something stronger, don’t miss out on experiencing a Tennessee whiskey tour.

Where to Stay in Farragut: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Farragut offers some of the best lodging options that cater to all budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to consider:

  • Home2 Suites by Hilton Knoxville West: This hotel is conveniently located near the attractions, including the Farragut Museum and the Admiral Farragut Statue.
  • Westgate Smoky Mountain Resort & Spa: If you prefer a luxury stay, choose this resort with an outdoor pool, spa, and spacious suites.
  • Sleep Inn & Suites: For a budget-friendly option, book this hotel that offers free Wi-Fi and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Farragut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Farragut has several options for purchasing souvenirs and local products. Here are some fantastic places to check out:

  • Turkey Creek: This outdoor shopping center houses several popular brand stores and local boutiques that sell souvenirs and local products.
  • The Village Exchange: A farmer’s market type store that specializes in local produce, meats, and handmade goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Farragut

Southern hospitality is a way of life in Farragut, and visitors can expect warm and friendly interactions with the locals. Manners are important in the South, so use phrases like “please” and “thank you.”

Best Time to Visit Farragut

The best time to visit Farragut is between spring and fall, from March to November. This time of year offers a mild climate with beautiful foliage. However, if you prefer cold weather and winter activities, visit between December and February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Farragut

Here are some essential guidelines to follow when visiting Farragut.

Do:

  • Do enjoy the downtown area and explore the unique shops.
  • Do try the delicious southern fried chicken.
  • Do respect the locals and Southern hospitality
  • Do experience the town’s natural beauty by hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t forget to thank people that helped you or provided you service.
  • Don’t be disrespectful towards the local culture.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Farragut can expect a safe and healthy environment during their stay.

Safety in Farragut

Farragut is known for being a safe place to visit. However, it is always wise to take necessary precautions, such as securing valuables and locking your car and hotel room doors.

Health in Farragut

Farragut has several pharmacies and a hospital in case of an emergency. Visitors should always stay hydrated, use sunscreen, and practice social distancing if required.

Getting to Farragut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Farragut is located between Knoxville and Oak Ridge and is easily accessible via car or plane.

Airports Near Farragut

The closest airport to Farragut is the McGhee Tyson Airport in Knoxville, which is just a 20-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Farragut

The Knoxville Bus Station is the only bus station in the area. It is located in the downtown area and offers services from various bus companies, including Greyhound and Megabus.

Other Options

Taxis, Ubers, and rental cars are all viable options for getting around Farragut.

How to Get Around in Farragut

To get around Farragut, renting a car is the best option. However, biking or walking is also a great way to explore the town’s unique charm.

Where to Go Next from Farragut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Farragut is in proximity to several excellent destinations and excursions worth exploring, including Knoxville and Oak Ridge.

Conclusion

Farragut is a beautiful town bursting with Southern charm and natural beauty, perfect for visitors looking for an escape. With plenty of activities, incredible food, and excellent lodging options to choose from, there’s no doubt that Farragut should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
