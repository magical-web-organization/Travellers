Introduction to Kodak

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Tennessee lies the charming town of Kodak, a hidden gem for travelers seeking scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. Kodak offers visitors a blend of natural beauty and rich history, making it a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Tennessee. From hiking and biking trails to museums and historic sites, Kodak has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Kodak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kodak is home to many fascinating museums and historic sites, each offering a glimpse into the area’s rich cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions in Kodak include:

  • Tennessee Museum of Aviation: This museum features a collection of vintage aircraft and interactive exhibits that highlight the history of aviation in Tennessee. Visitors can take a guided tour of the museum or even book a flight on one of the airplanes.

  • Bush’s Visitor Center: Bush’s Visitor Center is a great place to learn about the history of Bush’s Beans, one of the most popular brands of canned beans in the United States. Visitors can explore exhibits about the company’s founders, the manufacturing process, and even taste some of the beans.

  • Sevierville Historic Walking Tour: This self-guided walking tour takes visitors through downtown Sevierville, showcasing some of the area’s most historic buildings and landmarks. Visitors can download a free map from the Sevierville Chamber of Commerce website.

Natural Attractions of Kodak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Kodak offers a wealth of outdoor activities and breathtaking views. Some of the top natural attractions in Kodak include:

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: Located just a short drive from Kodak, this national park boasts some of the most stunning mountain scenery in the country. Visitors can hike, bike, fish, and camp in the park, or simply take a scenic drive through the mountains.

  • Douglas Lake: Douglas Lake is a sprawling reservoir that offers visitors ample opportunities for boating, fishing, and swimming. The lake is also surrounded by parks and hiking trails, making it a popular spot for picnicking and nature walks.

  • Forbidden Caverns: This underground cave system is a unique natural wonder that visitors can explore on a guided tour. Along the way, visitors can see underground waterfalls, crystal formations, and even a few animal bones.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kodak

Kodak is a hiker’s paradise, with miles of trails winding through the mountains and forests. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Kodak include:

  • Cove Creek Trail: This 5.8-mile trail is a moderate hike that takes visitors through beautiful forests and along creeks and streams. The trail is also open to mountain bikers.

  • Ramsey Cascades Trail: This strenuous 8-mile hike takes visitors on a steep climb to see one of the tallest waterfalls in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park. The trail features stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Riverwalk Greenway: This paved trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride, with scenic views of the French Broad River and downtown Sevierville.

Best Things to Do in Kodak: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the natural beauty of Kodak, visitors can also enjoy a variety of activities and guided tours. Some of the best things to do in Kodak include:

  • Ziplining: Several companies in Kodak offer zipline tours through the forest canopy, giving visitors a unique perspective on the area’s natural beauty.

  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can experience the thrill of horseback riding through the mountains with several local outfitters.

  • Tennessee Smokies Minor League Baseball: Located in nearby Knoxville, the Tennessee Smokies Baseball team offers visitors a chance to catch America’s favorite pastime in action.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kodak

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to Kodak:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Kodak and explore the local area, including the Tennessee Museum of Aviation and Bush’s Visitor Center.

  • Day 2: Go on a hiking adventure in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, followed by a relaxing evening in one of Kodak’s cozy lodges.

  • Day 3: Experience the thrill of ziplining through the forest, followed by a picnic lunch at Douglas Lake.

  • Day 4: Take a scenic drive through the mountains, stopping at scenic overlooks and historic landmarks.

  • Day 5: Go on a guided horseback ride through the mountains, followed by a visit to the Sevierville Historic Walking Tour.

  • Day 6: Explore the Forbidden Caverns, followed by an evening of minor league baseball at the Tennessee Smokies stadium.

  • Day 7: Depart from Kodak and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Kodak

Kodak may be a small town, but it has plenty of great options for food and drink. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pulled Pork BBQ: Tennessee is famous for its BBQ, and Kodak is no exception. Visitors can enjoy delicious pulled pork BBQ sandwiches at several local restaurants.

  • Fried Green Tomatoes: This Southern-style dish features sliced green tomatoes breaded and fried, served with a side of ranch or remoulade sauce.

  • Biscuits and Gravy: A Southern breakfast staple, biscuits and gravy is served at many local diners and cafes in Kodak.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: Sweet tea is a Southern classic, and visitors to Kodak can enjoy a cold glass with their meals or while relaxing on a porch or balcony.

  • Moonshine: Tennessee is known for its moonshine, and visitors to Kodak can sample this potent beverage at several local distilleries. Just be sure to drink responsibly!

Where to Stay in Kodak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kodak has several great options for accommodations, whether you’re looking for a cozy lodge or a budget-friendly hostel. Some of the top places to stay in Kodak include:

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott Kodak: This modern hotel is located just off Interstate 40 and offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a fitness center.

  • All Season Suites: This all-suite hotel offers spacious accommodations, an outdoor pool, and a complimentary hot breakfast.

  • Dumplin Valley Farm RV Park and Campgrounds: For visitors who prefer camping, this picturesque RV park and campground offers a range of amenities, including a swimming pool, laundry facilities, and a fishing pond.

What to Buy in Kodak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Kodak has plenty to offer. Some of the best things to buy in Kodak include:

  • Moonshine and other spirits: Several local distilleries produce high-quality moonshine and other spirits that make for great gifts and souvenirs.

  • Handmade crafts and pottery: Kodak is home to several talented artisans who create beautiful handmade crafts and pottery.

  • Huckleberry jam: Huckleberries are a local specialty in Kodak, and visitors can find delicious huckleberry jam and other food products at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kodak

Kodak is a friendly, welcoming town that values its rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Kodak should respect local customs and traditions, including:

  • Southern hospitality: Southerners are known for their hospitality, and visitors to Kodak can expect a warm welcome wherever they go.

  • Respect for the outdoors: Kodak is home to some of the most beautiful natural scenery in the country, and visitors should show respect for the environment and follow the “leave no trace” principle.

Best Time to Visit Kodak

The best time to visit Kodak is during the spring (March-June) and fall (September-November), when temperatures are milder and the foliage is at its peak. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy, although the area is still beautiful in its own right.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kodak

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the natural beauty of Kodak by going on a hike or exploring the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.

  • Do sample the local food and drink, including BBQ, sweet tea, and moonshine.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions, including Southern hospitality and respect for the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave trash or other debris in the natural areas around Kodak.

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray if you plan to spend time outdoors.

  • Don’t ignore safety guidelines for outdoor activities like hiking and ziplining.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kodak

Kodak is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions like locking their cars and securing their valuables. When exploring the outdoors, visitors should also be aware of potential dangers like wildlife or rapidly changing weather conditions.

Health in Kodak

Kodak has several medical clinics and pharmacies to serve visitors in case of illness or injury. Visitors should also be aware of potential health hazards like tick-borne illnesses or poison ivy when spending time in the great outdoors.

Getting to Kodak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kodak is conveniently located just off Interstate 40, making it easily accessible by car. Some other transportation options for visitors include:

Airports Near Kodak

  • McGhee Tyson Airport: Located just 30 minutes from Kodak, McGhee Tyson Airport is the closest airport for visitors flying in from out of state.

  • Asheville Regional Airport: Located about 2 hours from Kodak, Asheville Regional Airport is another option for visitors flying into the area.

Bus Stations Near Kodak

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in Knoxville, about 30 minutes from Kodak.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from several major car rental companies located at McGhee Tyson Airport or in downtown Knoxville.

  • Taxis and Rideshare Services: Visitors can also use services like Uber or Lyft for transportation to and from Kodak.

How to Get Around in Kodak

Most visitors to Kodak will need a car to get around and explore the area’s natural beauty. However, there are also several taxi and rideshare services available for those who don’t want to drive.

Where to Go Next from Kodak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kodak is located near several other popular destinations in Eastern Tennessee, including:

  • Gatlinburg: Gatlinburg is a charming mountain town located just 30 minutes from Kodak, known for its hiking trails, ski slopes, and local shops and eateries.

  • Pigeon Forge: Pigeon Forge is another popular destination just a short drive from Kodak, with attractions like Dollywood and the Great Smoky Mountains Wheel.

  • Knoxville: The city of Knoxville is located about 30 minutes from Kodak, offering visitors a wide range of cultural events and activities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover or a history buff, Kodak has something to offer for every kind of traveler. With its natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals, Kodak is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Tennessee.
