Introduction to Knoxville

Welcome to Knoxville, a charming city located in the eastern part of Tennessee, United States. With its rich history, beautiful natural surroundings, and lively cultural scene, Knoxville offers plenty of things to see, do, and experience for travelers of all tastes and preferences.

Founded in 1791, Knoxville has served as an important center for business, education, and recreation for centuries. Today, it is home to around 190,000 residents and welcomes thousands of visitors each year, drawn by its unique blend of Southern charm, Appalachian beauty, and urban sophistication.

In this comprehensive tour guide for Knoxville, we’ll take you through all the must-visit attractions and activities in the city, as well as provide recommendations for where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to make the most of your time here. So, let’s get started!

Knoxville is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Third Creek Greenway
  • Will Skelton Greenway
  • Bicentennial Greenbelt Park
  • House Mountain State Natural Area
  • Concord Park Trails

Best Things to Do in Knoxville: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Knoxville Zoo
  • Go whitewater rafting on the Pigeon River
  • Catch a game at the University of Tennessee’s Neyland Stadium
  • Take a walking tour of Knoxville’s historic downtown
  • Attend a live performance at the Tennessee Theatre
  • See a concert at the Bijou Theatre

Local Culture and Customs of Knoxville

In Knoxville, as in the rest of the South, hospitality and manners are highly valued. It’s common to say “yes, ma’am” and “no, sir” to show respect, and greeting strangers on the street is expected. The city has a rich music scene, with many live shows and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Knoxville

The best time to visit Knoxville depends on your interests and preferences. Spring and fall offer mild weather and beautiful foliage, making them great for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Summer can be hot and humid, but it’s also the season for many festivals and events. Winter can be quite cold, but it’s a good time to enjoy indoor attractions and cultural activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Knoxville

Knoxville is generally a safe city, but it’s still important to take basic safety precautions while traveling. Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas, keep your valuables close to you, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Knoxville

Knoxville has a modern healthcare system and several hospitals and clinics throughout the city. If you need medical attention, seek out the closest facility or call 911 in case of an emergency.
