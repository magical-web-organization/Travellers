Elizabethton Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Elizabethton, TN

Elizabethton is a small town located in Eastern Tennessee, nestled along the Appalachian Mountains. Known for its abundance of natural beauty, Elizabethton is a perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With a long history, the town also features a rich cultural heritage, an exciting food scene, and warm hospitality that will make any trip memorable. In this comprehensive tour guide for Elizabethton, we’ll explore the town’s top attractions, activities, food, lodging, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Elizabethton

Here are some of the top places to visit in Elizabethton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Carter Mansion: A prime example of 18th-century Vernacular Georgian architecture, this historic site showcases Tennessee’s rich heritage and is a must-visit destination in Elizabethton.
  2. Sycamore Shoals State Historic Park: Site of significant events that shaped the state’s history, this park features hiking trails, a visitor center, and an interactive exhibit about the region’s history.

Natural Attractions of Elizabethton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Roan Mountain State Park: Featuring more than 2,000 acres of lush, diverse nature trails, Roan Mountain offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation, including hiking, camping, fishing, and picnicking.
  2. Watauga Lake: Surrounded by the Appalachian Mountains, Watauga Lake is an ideal place to relax, swim, fish, or take a scenic drive.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elizabethton

Elizabethton has some of the best hiking and biking trails in Tennessee. Here are some of the top routes:

  1. Elizabethton Linear Trail: This 2.3-mile paved trail winds along the Doe River, offering scenic views of the town, and is perfect for cyclists, runners, or walkers.
  2. Rhododendron Trail: This 1-mile trail is located in Sycamore Shoals State Park and offers views of the Watauga River.

Best Things to Do in Elizabethton: Activities and Tours

There’s always something to do in Elizabethton, whether you’re a history buff or an adventurous outdoor enthusiast. Here are a few activities to consider:

  1. Jonesborough Walking Tour: Take a guided tour of Jonesborough, the oldest town in Tennesse. Learn about its unique history, architecture, and culture.
  2. Fly Fishing: The Watauga River is known for its trophy trout, and there are several fly fishing guides that offer trips for anglers of all skill levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elizabethton

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Elizabethton:

Day Activity
1 Visit Carter Mansion and Sycamore Shoals State Historic Park for a dose of history
2 Explore Roan Mountain for some hiking and breathtaking views
3 Take a scenic drive around Watauga Lake
4 Enjoy fly fishing on the Watauga River
5 Head to Jonesborough for a walking tour of the town and to check out the local art scene
6 Take a leisurely walk through the downtown of Elizabethton and grab a drink at one of the local breweries or bars
7 Spend a day at a spa or relax in nature at Doe River Gorge.

Food and Drink Scene of Elizabethton

Elizabethton has a variety of restaurants and bars that offer local cuisines, and award-winning brews. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Elizabethton:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Barbecue - Elizabethton is known for its barbecue joints, offering slow-cooked ribs, chicken, and pulled pork
  2. Fried Green Tomatoes - A Southern favorite, served at many local restaurants

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer - Elizabethton is home to multiple breweries, each with their unique flavors and brews.
  2. Sweet Iced Tea - A Southern classic, sweet iced tea is a refreshing beverage and is available in many of the local restaurants.

Where to Stay in Elizabethton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Elizabethton offers a range of accommodations, from hotels and motels to quaint bed and breakfasts. Here are a few places to consider:

  1. The River Spires: This bed and breakfast is situated in the heart of Elizabethton. The River Spires offer comfortable and luxurious rooms, and a delicious breakfast.
  2. Hampton Inn: The Hampton Inn is a budget-friendly hotel located near downtown Elizabethton and offers free breakfast and parking.

What to Buy in Elizabethton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Elizabethton offers a variety of local products, such as handmade crafts, art, and country-style decor. Here are some places to look for souvenirs:

  1. Elk Mills Store: Located in a historic, 2-story building, Elk Mills Store offers an impressive selection of antiques and collectibles, including vehicles and farming equipment.
  2. Elizabethton Farmers Market: This weekly farmers market offers locally produced goods, such as fresh fruits, vegetables, and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Elizabethton

Elizabethton’s culture is deeply influenced by the South, with its music, art, food, and traditions. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are always welcomed with open arms. Cultural events occur throughout the year, such as the annual Christmas Parade, Fourth of July festivities and the Fiddlers and Fiddleheads Festival in May.

Best Time to Visit Elizabethton

The best time to visit Elizabethton is from May to October when the weather is mild and the nature is in full bloom. August is the hottest month, and September and October are the best time for autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elizabethton

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Try local foods and drinks
  3. Engage with locals and explore charming downtown area.

Don’t:

  1. Litter
  2. Visit private property without permission
  3. Skip tipping at restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elizabethton

Elizabethton is generally a safe destination for travelers, but you should be aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Standard safety precautions, such as locking car and hotel room doors, are recommended.

Health in Elizabethton

Elizabethton has a reliable healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics in the area. Be sure to have sufficient travel insurance, and consult your doctor about any necessary vaccinations or health concerns before traveling.

Getting to Elizabethton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Elizabethton

The Tri-Cities Regional Airport, located 20 miles from Elizabethton, is the nearest airport.

Bus Stations Near Elizabethton

The nearest bus station to Elizabethton is the Greyhound Station in Johnson City, located 12 miles south of the town.

Other Options

Car rentals are also available at the Tri-Cities Regional Airport and in downtown Elizabethton.

How to Get Around in Elizabethton

Elizabethton is a small town, and most attractions are within a short driving distance. You can rent a car, use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft, or use a taxi service. Biking and walking around the downtown and nearby areas are also recommended.

Where to Go Next from Elizabethton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Great Smoky Mountains: With its vast expanse of forests, mountain ranges, and scenic trails, the Great Smoky Mountains is a perfect destination for outdoor adventure lovers.
  2. Asheville: Asheville, located in North Carolina, is a charming city known for its rich history, art scene, and beautiful architecture.

Conclusion

Elizabethton is an ideal destination for those looking for a mix of outdoor adventure, history, and culture. With its stunning natural beauty, warm hospitality, and rich history, the town promises an unforgettable experience. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan an exciting trip to Elizabethton in Eastern Tennessee.
