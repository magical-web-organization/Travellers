Introduction to Chattanooga

Chattanooga is a charming city located in Eastern Tennessee, filled with rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture. Known as the “Scenic City,” Chattanooga offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, and gastronomic delights. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventurous traveler, or a foodie, Chattanooga has something exciting for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Chattanooga

If you’re planning a trip to Chattanooga, here’s a list of must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tennessee Aquarium: Explore the world of marine life and witness the beauty of the underwater world at this top-notch aquarium that boasts a range of interactive exhibits and over 12,000 animals.
  • Creative Discovery Museum: This is an excellent place for families with little children, with innovative exhibits and hands-on activities that spark creativity and discovery.
  • The Hunter Museum of American Art: Situated atop a stunning bluff with views of the Tennessee River, this museum showcases an extensive collection of American art from the Colonial period to contemporary times.
  • Chattanooga National Cemetery: This is an active cemetery that serves as a final resting place for soldiers who fought in the Civil War and other conflicts.

Natural Attractions of Chattanooga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lookout Mountain: This is a natural wonder filled with hiking trails, scenic viewpoints, and adventure sports like zip-lining. Be sure to check out Rock City and Ruby Falls while you’re here.
  • Chickamauga and Chattanooga National Military Park: This park is a living history museum that commemorates the Civil War battles that took place in Chattanooga. Take a guided tour or hike along the scenic trails to experience the beauty and history of the area.
  • Raccoon Mountain Caverns: Explore an underground wonderland of stalactites, stalagmites, and subterranean waterfalls at this limestone cave system.
  • Tennessee River Gorge: Kayak or paddleboat along the beautiful Tennessee River while enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chattanooga

Chattanooga is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with several exciting hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few of the most popular ones:

  • Stringer’s Ridge: This is a popular hiking trail with panoramic views of the city and river.
  • Lookout Mountain: This has some of the best mountain biking routes, ranging from easy to challenging.
  • Signal Point: This is a beginner-friendly hike that offers stunning views of the Tennessee River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Cloudland Canyon State Park: This has over 30 miles of hiking trails that lead to breathtaking waterfalls, canyons, and panoramic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Chattanooga: Activities and Tours

Chattanooga is a city that offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to every interest and age group. Here are some of the best things to do in Chattanooga:

  • Riverboat Cruise on the Tennessee River: Take a scenic cruise along the Tennessee River while enjoying live music and a delicious meal.
  • Rock Climbing at High Point Climbing and Fitness: This is a great place to test your strength and agility while enjoying panoramic views of the city.
  • Moon River Kayak Tours: Paddle along the serene waters of the Tennessee River while exploring the local flora and fauna.
  • The Chattanooga Ghost Tour: This is a spooky and fun way to learn about the haunted history of Chattanooga.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chattanooga

Here’s a week-long itinerary that highlights the best of Chattanooga:

Day 1: Tennessee Aquarium and Chattanooga Choo Choo

Day 2: Creative Discovery Museum and Lookout Mountain

Day 3: Chickamauga and Chattanooga National Military Park and Ruby Falls

Day 4: High Point Climbing and Fitness and Walnut Street Bridge

Day 6: Signal Point and Cloudland Canyon State Park

Day 7: Rock City and Chattanooga Ghost Tour

Food and Drink Scene of Chattanooga

Chattanooga has a thriving food and drink scene, with something to suit every palate and budget. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chattanooga Choo Choo: This is a local favorite, consisting of toasted bread, scrambled eggs, cheese, and ham.
  • Chattanooga-style Chili: This is a unique version of chili that includes spaghetti and often served with cheese on top.
  • BBQ: Chattanooga has some of the best BBQ in the region, with mouth-watering pulled pork and ribs.

Local Beverages

  • Chattanooga Whiskey: This is a locally distilled whiskey that is a must-try for whiskey lovers.
  • Tennessee Brew Works: This is a popular brewery that offers a wide range of beers and delicious pub food.
  • Moon Pie Float: This unique float is made with the iconic Southern snack Moon Pie and ice-cream.

Where to Stay in Chattanooga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chattanooga has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some popular ones:

  • The Read House: This is a historic hotel located in downtown Chattanooga that offers luxurious accommodations and excellent amenities.
  • Crash Pad: This is a budget-friendly hostel that caters to outdoor enthusiasts and has a lively vibe.
  • Mayor’s Mansion Inn: This is a beautiful Victorian mansion that has been converted into a bed and breakfast.

What to Buy in Chattanooga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chattanooga offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and mementos. Here are some of the top things to buy in Chattanooga:

  • Moon Pies: These makes for a perfect snack or gift, and you can find a variety of flavors like banana, coconut, and chocolate.
  • Local Art: Chattanooga has a thriving art scene, and you can find beautiful paintings, sculptures, and other artworks by local artists.
  • Tennessee Whiskey: The state of Tennessee is known for its quality whiskey, and Chattanooga offers several distilleries you can visit and purchase bottles from.

Local Culture and Customs of Chattanooga

Chattanooga is a friendly and welcoming city that celebrates its Southern heritage and culture. Some customs to bear in mind when visiting include:

  • Southern Hospitality: The locals are known for their graciousness and charm, so be prepared to be greeted with a warm smile wherever you go.
  • Casual Dressing: Chattanooga has a relaxed and laid-back vibe, so casual dressing is the norm.
  • Respect for History: Chattanooga takes great pride in its role in American history, so visitors are expected to show respect for historical landmarks and monuments.

Best Time to Visit Chattanooga

The best time to visit Chattanooga is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the city holds several festivals and events. The winter months can be quite cold, while the summers can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chattanooga

Do:

  • Do try the local food and beverages: Chattanooga offers several unique and delicious dishes and beverages that you won’t find elsewhere in the country.
  • Do explore the outdoors: Chattanooga has some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in the country, so make sure to take advantage of the hiking, biking, and adventure sports options.
  • Do visit historical landmarks: Chattanooga played a crucial role in the Civil War, and the city has several landmarks and monuments that commemorate this period of history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Chattanooga takes great pride in its cleanliness and natural beauty, so visitors are expected to dispose of trash responsibly.
  • Don’t disrespect historical landmarks: Chattanooga has several historical landmarks that are of great significance to the locals, so visitors are expected to show respect and reverence towards them.
  • Don’t forget to tip: Tipping is a customary practice in Chattanooga, so be sure to leave a generous tip for good service.

Safety and Health

Chattanooga is a safe city with a low crime rate, but visitors should still exercise caution and take necessary precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Chattanooga:

Safety in Chattanooga

  • Stay in well-lit areas after dark: Chattanooga has several safe and vibrant neighborhoods, but visitors should still avoid walking in poorly lit areas after dark.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and report any suspicious activity to the authorities.
  • Watch out for wildlife: Chattanooga is home to several species of wildlife, including snakes and bears, so visitors should take necessary precautions when hiking or exploring the outdoors.

Health in Chattanooga

  • Stay hydrated: Chattanooga can be quite hot and humid during the summer months, so visitors should drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated.
  • Use sunscreen: The sun can be quite strong in Chattanooga, so visitors should use sunscreen to protect their skin from sunburn and skin damage.
  • Beware of allergies: Chattanooga has high pollen counts during the spring and fall months, so visitors with allergies should take necessary precautions and carry appropriate medication.

Getting to Chattanooga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chattanooga is well-connected with several transportation options, making it easy to reach the city from different parts of the country.

Airports Near Chattanooga

  • Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport: This is the closest airport to the city, located just 15 minutes from downtown Chattanooga.

Bus Stations Near Chattanooga

  • Greyhound: This is the most popular bus service in Chattanooga, with frequent trips to and from the city.

Other Options

  • Train: Amtrak offers daily train service to Chattanooga from cities like Atlanta, Nashville, and New York.
  • Car: Chattanooga is easily accessible by car, with several major highways leading to the city.

How to Get Around in Chattanooga

Chattanooga has several transportation options that make it easy to get around the city, including:

  • Free Electric Shuttle: This shuttle service runs through downtown Chattanooga and is a convenient and free way to get around the city.
  • Bike Rentals: Chattanooga has several bike rental companies, including Bike Chattanooga, that offer a convenient and eco-friendly way to explore the city.
  • Car Rentals: Visitors can easily rent a car from one of the several car rental companies located in Chattanooga.

Where to Go Next from Chattanooga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more places to explore from Chattanooga, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: This beautiful national park is located just two hours from Chattanooga and provides breathtaking scenery and wildlife.
  • Nashville: Known as the “Music City,” Nashville is just two hours from Chattanooga and offers a range of cultural attractions, historic landmarks, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Atlanta: The bustling city of Atlanta is just two hours from Chattanooga and offers everything from world-class museums and shopping to delicious food and music.

Conclusion

Chattanooga is a city that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, and gastronomic delights. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture, Chattanooga is a must-visit city for any traveler. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Chattanooga, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience.
