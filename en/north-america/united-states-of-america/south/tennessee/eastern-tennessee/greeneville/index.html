Greeneville, situated in Eastern Tennessee, is a culturally rich and naturally diverse town that attracts visitors from all around the world. From the historical landmarks and museums to the outdoor adventures, Greeneville has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more. So, let’s dive into the vibrant world of Greeneville.

Introduction to Greeneville

Greeneville is the second oldest town in Tennessee, established in 1783. It has a deep connection to the state’s history and was the hometown of former US president Andrew Johnson. The town boasts a rich cultural heritage, friendly locals, and stunning natural beauty. During its over 230 years of history, the town has grown and become a popular tourist destination in East Tennessee.

List of Places to Visit in Greeneville

No trip to Greeneville is complete without exploring the top attractions that define the town. Here are some must-sees:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Andrew Johnson National Historic Site: A must-visit for history lovers, the site commemorates the life and legacy of the 17th President of the United States, Andrew Johnson.
  • Nathanael Greene Museum: This museum showcases the years of history and heritage of Greeneville to visitors.
  • Dickson-Williams Mansion: This beautiful historic mansion was constructed in 1815 and is a local treasure.

Natural Attractions of Greeneville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Davy Crockett Birthplace State Park: Visit the birthplace of the legendary frontiersman, Davy Crockett, and explore hiking trails, fishing, and camping.
  • Cherokee National Forest: An outdoor enthusiasts’ paradise, the forest offers endless opportunities for hiking, camping, and water sports.
  • Andrew Johnson National Cemetery: This cemetery is located on the side of a picturesque hill offering a panoramic view of the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greeneville

Greeneville has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Some popular trails include:

  • Margarette Falls Trail: A moderately challenging trail that leads to a stunning waterfall.
  • Paint Creek Corridor Trail: A 16.5-mile trail that passes through a lush green forest, several waterfalls, and scenic viewpoints.
  • Horse Creek Recreation Area: A hiking and mountain biking trail that winds through lush woods.

Best Things to Do in Greeneville: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and sightseeing, Greeneville offers a wealth of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best:

  • Autumn Ridge Golf Course: A premier golf course with breathtaking views of the mountains.
  • Lazy Llama Campground: A unique outdoor experience camping with llamas which makes it an unforgettable trip.
  • Art on the Square: This annual event provides an opportunity to browse and buy a wide selection of works from local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greeneville

Here’s a suggested itinerary that covers the best of Greeneville in a week:

Day Activity
1 Visit Andrew Johnson National Historic Site, Nathanael Greene Museum, and the Dickson-Williams Mansion
2 Explore Davy Crockett Birthplace State Park and the Cherokee National Forest
3 Hike to Margarette Falls Trail and enjoy the panoramic views of the Andrew Johnson National Cemetery
4 Go mountain biking and hiking at the Paint Creek Corridor Trail
5 Visit historical and art sites like the City Garage Car Museum, Doak House Museum, or Greene County Partnership Gallery, and then play golf at Autumn Ridge Golf Course
6 Go camping with llamas at Lazy Llama Campground
7 Shop for souvenirs and local products at the Greeneville Farmer’s Market and then attend the annual Art on the Square event

Food and Drink Scene of Greeneville

Explore Greeneville’s vibrant food and drink scene that will tantalize your taste buds. Here are some of the must-tries:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: Tennessee is famous for its mouthwatering barbecue, and Greeneville is no exception.
  • Fried Chicken: Crispy and juicy fried chicken is a local favorite.
  • Southern-style Biscuits: Served with butter or gravy, these delicious biscuits are a must-have.

Local Beverages

  • Tennessee Whiskey: Home to famous distillers, Tennessee is known for its whiskey.
  • Craft Beer: Greeneville boasts several local breweries that offer a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Greeneville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greeneville offers various accommodation options for every budget and taste. Some popular options are:

  • General Morgan Inn: Built in 1884 and renovated in 2006, this historic hotel offers luxury and comfort.
  • Knotty Pine Lodge: Situated near the Cherokee National Forest, it offers cozy cabins and rooms.
  • Emmanuel’s Guest House: Located in the heart of Greeneville, this guest house offers a unique experience of Southern hospitality.

What to Buy in Greeneville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Greeneville with you by shopping at local stores and markets. Here are some popular items to bring home:

  • Handcrafted jewelry: Explore unique handcrafted jewelry made by local artisans.
  • Homemade jams, jellies, and fudge: Stop by Greeneville Farmer’s Market to pick up some locally made treats.
  • Tennessee Whiskey: Gift bottles of Tennessee whiskey to your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Greeneville

Greeneville is known for its Southern hospitality, friendly locals, and rich heritage. One should take care to show respect for local cultures and customs.

Best Time to Visit Greeneville

The best time to visit Greeneville is during summer and fall from June to November when the weather is mild, and the town hosts several events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greeneville

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit historical landmarks and museums.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Be disrespectful towards locals or their culture.
  • Skip the opportunity to learn about the town’s history.

Safety and Health

Greeneville is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should be mindful of natural hazards while exploring the outdoors.

Safety in Greeneville

  • Do not leave valuables in your car.
  • Keep an eye on your surroundings at all times.
  • Know the location of emergency services, including hospitals and police stations.

Health in Greeneville

  • Be cautious when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and always carry water.
  • Consult a physician before exploring if you have any medical conditions.

How to Get Around in Greeneville

The best way to get around Greeneville is by car. Several rental agencies are available at the airport, or you can hire taxis and ride-sharing services. A few travel companies also offer bicycles for hire.
