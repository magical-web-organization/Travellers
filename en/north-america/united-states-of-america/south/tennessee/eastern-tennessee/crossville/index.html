Introduction to Crossville

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Tennessee, Crossville is a quaint town that attracts visitors from around the country with its natural beauty, rich history, and southern hospitality. Located just a short drive from Nashville, Knoxville, and Chattanooga, Crossville is a great destination for a day trip or a week-long getaway. This comprehensive travel guide will highlight the best things to do, see, and experience in Crossville.

List of Places to Visit in Crossville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Military Memorial Museum

Located in downtown Crossville, the Military Memorial Museum is a must-visit for history buffs and military enthusiasts alike. The museum features an extensive collection of military artifacts and displays, including weapons, uniforms, and memorabilia from various conflicts throughout American history.

Cumberland Homesteads Tower Museum

Step back in time at the Cumberland Homesteads Tower Museum, which showcases life during the Great Depression era. The museum is housed in one of the original buildings constructed in Crossville as part of President Roosevelt’s New Deal program, and offers visitors a glimpse into life in a small Tennessee farming community during the 1930s.

Natural Attractions of Crossville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cumberland Mountain State Park

Spanning over 1,500 acres, Cumberland Mountain State Park offers endless opportunities for outdoor recreation. Visitors can hike over 10 miles of trails, fish in Byrd Lake, swim at the park’s beach, or simply relax and enjoy the stunning mountain vistas.

Obed Wild and Scenic River

Located just a short drive from Crossville, the Obed Wild and Scenic River is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The river offers world-class kayaking and fishing, while the surrounding area is home to miles of hiking trails, rock climbing routes, and stunning waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crossville

Cumberland Trail State Park

Stretching over 300 miles, the Cumberland Trail State Park offers a diverse range of hiking options for visitors of all skill levels. Some of the most popular sections of the trail in Crossville include the Black Mountain section and the Obed Wild and Scenic River section.

Fairfield Glade Trail System

The Fairfield Glade Trail System offers over 50 miles of hiking and biking trails, with options ranging from easy strolls to challenging climbs. This network of trails winds through the stunning hills and valleys of central Tennessee, offering breathtaking views and unforgettable experiences.

Best Things to Do in Crossville: Activities and Tours

Golfing

Crossville is known as the “Golf Capital of Tennessee,” and for good reason – the town is home to over a dozen championship-level golf courses. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, Crossville’s golf scene is sure to impress.

Wine Tasting

Tennessee may not be the first place you think of when it comes to wine, but Crossville is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours. Visitors can sample locally-produced wines and learn about the history and process of winemaking in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Crossville

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Crossville is complete without sampling the town’s famous southern cuisine. For a truly authentic experience, be sure to try some of these local favorites:

  • Fried chicken
  • Biscuits and gravy
  • Pimento cheese
  • Fried green tomatoes
  • Sweet tea

Local Beverages

Tennessee may be known for its bourbon, but Crossville also offers a range of locally-produced beers and spirits. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Calfkiller Brewing Company
  • Chestnut Hill Winery
  • Tennessee Legend Distillery

What to Buy in Crossville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rock City Gardens Birdhouse

These hand-painted birdhouses are made from local wood and feature intricate designs inspired by the iconic Rock City Gardens.

Local Artwork and Crafts

Crossville is home to many talented local artists and craftspeople, and there are several galleries and shops where visitors can purchase unique and beautiful pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Crossville

Crossville is a small town with a rich history and a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s traditions and customs, and take the time to learn about the region’s unique culture.

Best Time to Visit Crossville

The best time to visit Crossville is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most vibrant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winter temperatures can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crossville

Do:

  • Shop at local businesses and support the community
  • Take the time to learn about Crossville’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions and customs
  • Drive recklessly or disobey traffic laws

Safety and Health

Safety in Crossville

Crossville is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take basic precautions such as locking car doors and avoiding unfamiliar areas after dark.

Health in Crossville

Crossville has several medical facilities, including Cumberland Medical Center and Crossville Medical Group, which provide a range of services for visitors and residents.

Getting to Crossville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Crossville

The closest airport to Crossville is the Knoxville McGhee Tyson Airport, which is located approximately one hour and fifteen minutes away by car. Other nearby airports include Nashville International Airport and Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport.

Bus Stations Near Crossville

Crossville does not have a bus station, but there are several Greyhound stations located in nearby towns such as Cookeville and Knoxville.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Crossville, as the town is located just off of several major highways, including I-40 and US-70.

How to Get Around in Crossville

The easiest way to get around Crossville is by car, as the town is spread out and public transportation options are limited. Several car rental companies have locations in the area, and taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Crossville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Crossville is located in the heart of Eastern Tennessee, and there are several nearby destinations that are worth a visit, including:

  • Chattanooga, TN
  • Knoxville, TN
  • Nashville, TN

Conclusion

Crossville may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of experiences and attractions for visitors to explore. From outdoor adventures and scenic vistas to historic landmarks and rich cultural traditions, Crossville is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Eastern Tennessee.
