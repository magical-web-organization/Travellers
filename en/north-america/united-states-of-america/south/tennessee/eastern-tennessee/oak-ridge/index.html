Introduction to Oak Ridge

Located in Eastern Tennessee, Oak Ridge is a city with a rich history shaped by the United States government, which initiated a top-secret project to create the first atomic bomb during World War II. This history is still palpable in Oak Ridge, with numerous landmarks and museums dedicated to the Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the Manhattan Project. However, Oak Ridge also boasts a vibrant natural and cultural scene, with plenty of outdoor activities, locally-inspired food, and unique shopping experiences. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through everything Oak Ridge has to offer, and provide you with insider tips to make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Oak Ridge

If you’re wondering what to see in Oak Ridge, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here’s a brief list of some of the city’s top attractions and destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • American Museum of Science and Energy – a fascinating museum dedicated to science, energy, and national security
  • Oak Ridge History Museum – featuring exhibits and artifacts on the city’s history and the Manhattan Project
  • Y-12 National Security Complex – a National Nuclear Security Administration facility where visitors can take guided tours

Natural Attractions of Oak Ridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Haw Ridge Park – a beautiful 800-acre park nestled along the Clinch River, perfect for hiking, biking, and bird watching
  • Melton Hill Lake – a scenic lake popular for recreational activities like boating, kayaking, and fishing
  • Greenways and Trails – a network of trails that runs through the city, offering scenic views of the surrounding mountains and forests

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oak Ridge

One of the main draws of Oak Ridge is its abundance of outdoor activities, particularly its hiking and biking routes. Here are a few routes to consider:

  • North Boundary Trail – a nine-mile trail that offers scenic views of the Clinch River Valley and passes by several historic sites
  • Greenways and Trails – a network of trails that spans over 85 miles throughout the city, offering varying levels of difficulty for hikers and bikers
  • Haw Ridge Park – offers over 30 miles of biking and hiking trails through woodlands and along the shores of the Clinch River

Best Things to Do in Oak Ridge: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Oak Ridge. Here are some top activities and tours:

  • Secret City Scavenger Hunt – a two-hour scavenger hunt that takes you through the city’s landmarks and history
  • Oak Ridge Ghost Tour – a two-hour walking tour that exposes you to the spooky side of the city’s history
  • Oak Ridge Bicycle Adventure – a five-hour guided bike tour that takes you through scenic routes in the city’s outskirts

Week-Long Itinerary for Oak Ridge

If you’re planning to spend a week in Oak Ridge, here’s a possible itinerary to consider:

Day Activities
1 Visit the American Museum of Science and Energy, stroll along Blankenship Field, and explore Oak Ridge History Museum
2 Hike or bike the North Boundary Trail and explore the nearby Historic Jackson Square
3 Take a guided tour of the Y-12 National Security Complex and visit one of the city’s many art galleries.
4 Go kayaking or fishing at Melton Hill Lake and have a picnic at Haw Ridge Park
5 Take a day trip to either the Great Smoky Mountains National Park or to Knoxville, Tennessee.
6 Play a round of golf at the Oak Ridge Country Club and explore the local food and drink scene
7 Visit the University of Tennessee Arboretum and enjoy a scenic drive through the backroads of Oak Ridge

Food and Drink Scene of Oak Ridge

Oak Ridge’s food scene is a mix of classic American fare, Southern cuisine, and international flavors. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Fried Chicken – a spicy Southern style of fried chicken, popular at local establishments like Southern Diner and Big Ed’s Pizza
  • Brunswick Stew – a hearty Southern stew made with vegetables, meat, and sometimes barbecue sauce, a popular specialty at Dean’s Restaurant
  • Burgers and Fries – classic American fare available at numerous eateries throughout the city

Local Beverages

  • Proton IPA – a hoppy, citrusy India Pale Ale brewed by the local craft brewery, Clinch River Brewing
  • Marble Slab Milkshake – a creamy vanilla milkshake blended with homemade ice cream and pieces of marble slab candy, available at Razzleberry’s Ice Cream Lab

Where to Stay in Oak Ridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse, a budget-friendly hostel, or a luxury hotel, Oak Ridge has accommodation options to suit everyone’s needs. Some top picks include:

  • Staybridge Suites Knoxville Oak Ridge – a modern hotel with spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center
  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Oak Ridge - Knoxville – a stylish hotel with an on-site restaurant, bar, and fitness center
  • WestGate Lodging – featuring cabins, apartments, and cottages with kitchens and laundry facilities, ideal for families or extended stays

What to Buy in Oak Ridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to remember your visit to Oak Ridge? Consider these local products:

  • Smoky Mountain Mead – a locally produced, honey-based wine available for purchase at the Carolina Honey Bee Company
  • Oak Ridge Heritage T-Shirt – a comfortable, fun t-shirt branded with an iconic photo of Oak Ridge National Laboratory, available throughout the city’s souvenir shops
  • Handmade Jewelry – artisan-made jewelry featuring unique designs inspired by the city’s history and nature, available at various local boutiques

Local Culture and Customs of Oak Ridge

Oak Ridge’s rich history and unique cultural heritage have shaped its local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind when visiting Oak Ridge include:

  • Respect for National Security – given Oak Ridge’s history and continued prominence in national security matters, visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and sensitive to any restrictions or security concerns
  • Southern Hospitality – Oak Ridge locals are known for their warm and welcoming nature, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation and ask for recommendations or tips!
  • Love of Nature – Oak Ridge residents take great pride in their natural surroundings, so be respectful of the city’s green spaces and outdoor areas

Best Time to Visit Oak Ridge

The best time to visit Oak Ridge depends on your interests and preferences. Summer is the most popular time for outdoor activities, with warm temperatures and sunny skies. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and cooler temperatures. Winter can be chilly, but it’s also a great time for indoor activities like museum visits and cozy meals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oak Ridge

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Oak Ridge:

Do:

  • Visit the Museums – Oak Ridge’s museums provide fascinating glimpses into the city’s history and culture.
  • Enjoy the Outdoors – With miles of scenic hiking and biking routes, Oak Ridge is an outdoor lover’s paradise.
  • Eat Local Food – Whether you’re looking for Southern staples or international cuisine, Oak Ridge has plenty of delicious eateries to explore.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on Restricted Areas – Given Oak Ridge’s history as a top-secret government facility, it’s important to respect any designated restricted areas.
  • Litter in Public Spaces – Help keep Oak Ridge’s natural spaces beautiful by disposing of waste properly.
  • Overlook Local Boutiques – While Oak Ridge has plenty of popular chain stores, don’t miss out on the unique, one-of-a-kind items available at local boutiques and artisan shops.

Safety and Health

If you’re wondering about safety and health while in Oak Ridge, here are a few things to keep in mind:

Safety in Oak Ridge

Overall, Oak Ridge is a relatively safe city, but it’s important to take general safety precautions, like locking your car and securing valuables.

Health in Oak Ridge

The city’s health care system is solid, with several large hospitals and clinics in the area. However, visitors should still take standard precautions, like staying hydrated and wearing sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Oak Ridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oak Ridge is located approximately 25 miles west of Knoxville, Tennessee, making it relatively easy to get to by car or public transportation. Here are a few options:

Airports Near Oak Ridge

  • McGhee Tyson Airport – Located about 30 minutes away in Knoxville, this is the closest airport to Oak Ridge.

Bus Stations Near Oak Ridge

  • Greyhound Bus Station, Knoxville – Located about 25 miles east of Oak Ridge in Knoxville, this is the closest bus station to the city.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals – With several major car rental companies available at McGhee Tyson Airport and in nearby Knoxville, renting a car is a popular option for visitors to Oak Ridge
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing – Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in the area

How to Get Around in Oak Ridge

Getting around Oak Ridge is easy, with plenty of taxi and ride-sharing services, as well as public transportation options such as the Oak Ridge Circulator and the Knoxville Area Transit System. Visitors can also opt to rent a car if they plan on exploring the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Oak Ridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Oak Ridge, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park – Located about an hour’s drive south of Oak Ridge, this is one of the most popular national parks in the country, known for its scenic vistas and outdoor recreational activities.
  • Knoxville, Tennessee – Only a short drive away, Knoxville is a vibrant city with plenty of cultural attractions, unique shopping, and delicious food and drink.

Conclusion

Oak Ridge, Tennessee, is a fascinating city with a rich history and vibrant cultural and outdoor scene. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums, hiking its scenic trails, or indulging in its unique culinary scene, there’s something for everyone in Oak Ridge.
