Introduction to Cleveland

Cleveland, located in the eastern part of Tennessee, in the southern region of the United States, is a small city with plenty of charm and attractions. Known for its breathtaking natural beauty and outdoor activities, Cleveland also offers a rich cultural experience with its museums, historical sites, and food scene. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a history buff, Cleveland has something for everyone. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Cleveland

Cleveland has a plethora of attractions to offer. From museums and historical sites to outdoor activities and scenic views, here is a list of must-visit places in Cleveland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum Center at 5ive Points - This museum showcases the history and culture of Cleveland and the surrounding region through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and artwork.
  2. Red Clay State Historic Park - This park marks the location of the last Cherokee council grounds before the Trail of Tears, and offers historical exhibits, trails, and cultural programs.
  3. Craigmiles Hall - This beautifully restored building was constructed in 1838 and offers a glimpse into the city’s early history.
  4. Tennessee Wesleyan University - Visit this small liberal arts college to see its collection of art and artifacts from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Cleveland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hiwassee River - Go kayaking, tubing, or rafting on this scenic river that offers stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains.
  2. Ocoee River - This river is known for its Class III and IV rapids and is a popular spot for white-water rafting and kayaking.
  3. Cherokee National Forest - Experience the natural beauty of Cleveland with a hike through this forest, which offers trails for all skill levels.
  4. Lookout Mountain - Take a drive up Lookout Mountain and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cleveland

Cleveland is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and offers plenty of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Chickamauga and Chattanooga National Military Park - This park offers over 30 miles of hiking trails, with options ranging from easy to strenuous.
  2. Benton Falls Trail - This 3.5-mile trail leads hikers to a 65-foot waterfall, offering a beautiful and peaceful escape from the city.
  3. Cloudland Canyon State Park - This park offers over 30 miles of hiking trails, with stunning views of waterfalls, canyons, and caves.
  4. Cleveland/Bradley County Greenway - This paved trail is perfect for biking or jogging and runs for 4.8 miles through the heart of Cleveland.

Best Things to Do in Cleveland: Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural beauty and cultural attractions, Cleveland offers plenty of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Zipline Adventure - Experience the thrill of zipping through the trees at Adventure Park Zip Lines.
  2. Ocoee Adventure Center - This center offers white-water rafting, kayaking, and tubing adventures on the Ocoee River.
  3. Cleveland Speedway - Catch a race or attend an event at this local speedway
  4. Tennessee Aquarium - This aquarium in nearby Chattanooga features a variety of marine life and interactive exhibits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cleveland

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Cleveland, incorporating some of the top attractions and activities:

Day 1: Visit Museum Center at 5ive Points and explore the city’s downtown area. Day 2: Hike the Benton Falls Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 3: Take a zipline adventure at Adventure Park Zip Lines and enjoy a sunset dinner at Bald Headed Bistro. Day 4: Visit Red Clay State Historic Park and learn about the Trail of Tears. Day 5: Enjoy a day of kayaking or tubing on the Hiwassee River. Day 6: Explore the natural beauty of Cloudland Canyon State Park through hiking and sightseeing. Day 7: Take a tour of Tennessee Wesleyan University and enjoy a relaxing day at the Cleveland/Bradley County Greenway.

Food and Drink Scene of Cleveland

Cleveland offers a variety of dining options, ranging from trendy cafes and breweries to classic Southern comfort food. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Moon Pie Burger at Bald Headed Bistro - This juicy burger is topped with moon pie bacon, peanut butter, and hot pepper jelly.
  2. Fried Green Tomatoes at Catch Bar and Grill - This southern classic is a must-try when visiting Cleveland.
  3. Hot Chicken at Stack Southern Bistro - This spicy dish is a Nashville classic with a Cleveland twist.
  4. BBQ at Jenkins Deli - This local deli serves up some of the best smoked pork and chicken in town.

Local Beverages

  1. Coca-Cola - Cleveland is home to one of Coca-Cola’s bottling plants, and visitors can enjoy a cold Coke from the original glass bottle.
  2. Microbreweries - Cleveland boasts several local breweries, including Mash and Hops and Bald Headed Bistro.
  3. Sweet Tea - This southern staple is available at almost every restaurant and cafe in Cleveland.

Where to Stay in Cleveland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cleveland has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to upscale hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Cleveland State Community College Hotel - This newly renovated hotel offers modern accommodations at an affordable price.
  2. Douglas Inn and Suites - This family-owned hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at a reasonable rate.
  3. Applegate Inn - This bed and breakfast is a charming option for those who prefer a more personal touch.
  4. Home2 Suites by Hilton - This upscale hotel offers extended stay suites with a fully equipped kitchen and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Cleveland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Cleveland, be sure to bring back some souvenirs and local products to remember your trip. Here are some of the best items to bring home:

  1. Moon Pies - These Southern treats are available at almost every grocery store and gas station in Cleveland.
  2. Pottery - Cleveland is known for its pottery, and visitors can find a variety of handcrafted pieces at local shops.
  3. Honey - Cleveland has several local beekeepers, and visitors can bring home a jar of fresh, local honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Cleveland

Cleveland, like many Southern cities, has a strong focus on family, community, and hospitality. Visitors are often greeted with a warm smile and friendly conversation.

Best Time to Visit Cleveland

The best time to visit Cleveland is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cleveland

Do:

  1. Explore the natural beauty of Cleveland
  2. Try the local cuisine
  3. Visit the city’s museums and historical sites
  4. Take a tour of Tennessee Wesleyan University

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the city’s history and culture
  2. Be rude to locals
  3. Leave trash in natural areas
  4. Drive recklessly or speed

Safety and Health

Safety in Cleveland

Cleveland is a relatively safe city, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions.

Health in Cleveland

Cleveland has several medical facilities, including Tennova Healthcare and Cleveland Family Medicine. Visitors should ensure they have proper health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Cleveland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cleveland is easily accessible by car or plane. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Cleveland

  1. Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport - This airport is located about 30 miles from Cleveland and offers several major airlines.
  2. Knoxville McGhee Tyson Airport - This airport is located about 90 miles from Cleveland and offers several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Cleveland

  1. Cleveland Greyhound Bus Station - This station is located in the heart of the city and offers several routes to nearby destinations.

Other Options

  1. Rental Cars - Several rental car companies are available at the Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport.
  2. Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services - Uber and Lyft are available in Cleveland.

How to Get Around in Cleveland

Cleveland is a small city and is easily walkable. However, visitors may prefer to rent a car or use a ride-sharing service to reach attractions outside of the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Cleveland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cleveland is located in the heart of the scenic Tennessee Valley, offering plenty of nearby destinations for day trips or weekend getaways. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  1. Chattanooga - Known for its aquarium, rock climbing, and scenic views, Chattanooga is located about 30 miles from Cleveland.
  2. Knoxville - Home to the University of Tennessee, Knoxville offers a variety of cultural attractions and outdoor activities and is located about 90 miles from Cleveland.
  3. Smoky Mountains - Explore the scenic beauty of the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, located about 100 miles from Cleveland.
  4. Nashville - This musical city is located about 150 miles from Cleveland and offers a lively nightlife and iconic attractions.

Conclusion

Cleveland has plenty to offer visitors, from its outdoor activities and natural beauty to its rich cultural history and delicious cuisine. By following this travel guide, you can plan a trip that is sure to be a memorable and enjoyable experience.
