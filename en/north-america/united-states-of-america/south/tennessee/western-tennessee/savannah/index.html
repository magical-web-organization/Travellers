Introduction to Savannah

Savannah is located in Western Tennessee, United States, and is known for its rich history, charming architecture, and natural beauty. It’s a popular destination for solo travelers, couples, families, and history buffs. In this guide, we’ll provide a detailed overview of the best places to visit, top things to do, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to get around in Savannah.

List of Places to Visit in Savannah

Savannah has a lot to offer, whether you want to explore historical landmarks, enjoy scenic views, or indulge in outdoor adventures. Here is a list of the best places to visit in Savannah:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shiloh National Military Park: This national park is known for the famous Civil War battle that took place on April 6-7, 1862. Visitors can explore the battlefield and learn about the history of the war.
  • Johnsonville State Historic Park: This park commemorates the Civil War battle that took place on November 4-5, 1864. Visitors can learn about the history of the park through guided tours and a museum exhibit.
  • Pickwick Landing State Park: Located on the shores of Pickwick Lake, the state park offers a range of outdoor recreational activities like fishing, boating, and swimming.

Natural Attractions of Savannah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Natchez Trace Parkway: A scenic roadway that stretches 444 miles from Natchez, MS, to Nashville, TN. It’s a popular destination for cyclists, hikers, and nature enthusiasts.
  • Tennessee River: The Tennessee River is a large river that stretches 652 miles from Knoxville, TN, to Paducah, KY. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and hiking among the scenic views and wildlife.
  • Chickasaw State Park: This park is known for its scenic beauty, featuring a man-made lake, campgrounds, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savannah

Savannah is home to several beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Natchez Trace Parkway: A popular cycling route that offers stunning views of the countryside along a scenic roadway.
  • Shiloh National Military Park: Visitors can explore the battlefield on a guided walking tour or follow the self-guided trails to learn about the history of the war.
  • Pickwick Landing State Park: The state park offers several hiking trails, including the 1.5-mile lake trail that offers stunning views of Pickwick Lake.

Best Things to Do in Savannah: Activities and Tours

Savannah has something for everyone, whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, enjoying outdoor adventures, or indulging in local cuisine. Here are some of the best things to do in Savannah:

  • Visit the Shiloh National Military Park: Learn about the Civil War and the famous battle that took place here.
  • Explore the Pickwick Landing State Park: Enjoy a range of outdoor recreational activities like fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • Take a scenic drive along the Natchez Trace Parkway: Enjoy stunning views of the countryside and explore historic landmarks along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Savannah

Savannah is famous for its Southern cuisine, which features dishes like fried chicken, biscuits and gravy, and sweet tea. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Savannah:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried chicken: A Southern classic, fried chicken is a must-try dish in Savannah.
  • Biscuits and gravy: This dish features fluffy biscuits smothered in creamy gravy, and is a staple at many Southern restaurants.
  • Pecan pie: A delicious dessert made with pecans, sugar, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: A classic Southern beverage that’s perfect for hot summer days.
  • Tennessee whiskey: A type of whiskey that is distilled in Tennessee and has a distinctive flavor.

Where to Stay in Savannah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Savannah has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Savannah:

  • The Peabody Memphis: A luxury hotel located in downtown Memphis that offers elegant rooms and suites, a rooftop bar, and a spa.
  • Grand Hyatt Nashville: A modern hotel located in the heart of downtown Nashville that features comfortable rooms and suites, a fitness center, and a rooftop bar.
  • HI Memphis Hostel: An affordable hostel located in downtown Memphis that offers shared and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a lounge area.

What to Buy in Savannah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Savannah has a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts and mementos. Here are some of the best things to buy in Savannah:

  • Moon pies: A Southern treat made with chocolate and marshmallow that’s popular in Savannah.
  • Southern-made hot sauces: A range of hot sauces made with Southern ingredients like peaches, peppers, and bourbon.
  • Handcrafted pottery: Unique pottery pieces made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Savannah

Savannah has a rich culture and heritage that’s influenced by its Southern roots, history, and natural beauty. Here are some of the local customs and traditions you should be aware of when visiting:

  • Southern hospitality: Visitors can expect to be greeted warmly and treated with kindness and respect.
  • Church culture: Religion plays an important role in Southern culture, and visitors may find that Sunday mornings are reserved for church services.
  • Respect for nature: A love for nature and the outdoors is deeply ingrained in Southern culture, and visitors are encouraged to respect the natural beauty of the region.

Best Time to Visit Savannah

The best time to visit Savannah is in the fall or spring, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be chilly. If you’re planning to visit during the summer, be prepared for high temperatures and crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savannah

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts of Savannah:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Sample classic Southern dishes like fried chicken, biscuits and gravy, and sweet tea.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions: Be respectful of the local culture and traditions, including church services and a love for nature.
  • Take a walking tour of Savannah: Explore the city’s charming architecture and historic landmarks on foot.

Don’t:

  • Forget to wear sunscreen: The sun can be strong in Savannah, so be sure to wear sunscreen and protect your skin.
  • Be disrespectful: Avoid making derogatory comments about the local culture or heritage.
  • Litter or harm the natural environment: Respect the natural beauty of the region and avoid littering or harming the local environment.

Safety and Health

When visiting Savannah, it’s important to take safety and health precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Safety in Savannah

Savannah is generally a safe destination for visitors, but it’s still important to take precautions to avoid theft and other crimes. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay in well-lit areas: Stick to well-lit areas and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Use a money belt or secure purse: Keep your money and valuables secure to avoid theft.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Stay alert and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist attractions.

Health in Savannah

Savannah has a range of medical facilities and emergency services to ensure your health and safety. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration in hot and humid weather.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Be aware of common illnesses: Be aware of common illnesses like heatstroke and insect bites, and take precautions to avoid them.

Getting to Savannah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Savannah is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some of the transportation options and logistics to consider when planning your trip:

  • Airports Near Savannah: The nearest airport is Memphis International Airport, which is located about 120 miles from Savannah. Other nearby airports include Nashville International Airport and Jackson International Airport.
  • Bus Stations Near Savannah: Greyhound is the main bus company that serves Savannah, with a station located downtown.
  • Other Options: Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi/Uber to explore Savannah and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Savannah

Savannah has a range of transportation options that make it easy to get around. Here are some of the best ways to get around in Savannah:

  • Walking: Explore the city’s charming architecture and historic landmarks on foot.
  • Bicycling: Rent a bike and explore Savannah’s beautiful parks and green spaces.
  • Public Transportation: The Memphis Area Transit Authority (MATA) operates buses that serve downtown Savannah and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Savannah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savannah makes a great base for exploring nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some of the best places to go next from Savannah:

  • Memphis: Visit the birthplace of rock and roll and explore the city’s famous BBQ scene.
  • Nashville: Discover the “Music City” and enjoy live music or explore the famous Country Music Hall of Fame.
  • Great Smoky Mountains: Explore the natural beauty of the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, located a few hours’ drive from Savannah.

Conclusion

Savannah is a charming city that offers a rich blend of history, natural beauty, and Southern hospitality. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, enjoying outdoor adventures, or indulging in local cuisine, Savannah has something for everyone.
