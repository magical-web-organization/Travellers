Introduction to Hermitage

Located in Central Tennessee, Hermitage is a historic city in the South region of the United States. The city boasts an incredible mix of history, culture, and natural beauty that attracts visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life, you’re sure to find something that captures your interest in Hermitage.

List of Places to Visit in Hermitage

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Hermitage:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Hermitage: This is the former home of America’s seventh president, Andrew Jackson, and one of the most visited historic sites in the country. Visitors can explore the mansion, grounds, and museum to learn more about Jackson and his time as president.
  • Nashville Shores Lakeside Resort: This waterpark is a popular attraction for families and those looking to beat the heat during the summer months.
  • Andrew Jackson’s Tomb: Located on the grounds of the Hermitage, this is the final resting place of Andrew Jackson and his wife Rachel.
  • The Hermitage Arboretum: This peaceful oasis is located on the grounds of the Hermitage and is home to a diverse collection of trees and plants.

Natural Attractions of Hermitage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • J. Percy Priest Lake: This beautiful lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • Long Hunter State Park: With over 2,600 acres of natural beauty, Long Hunter State Park is a perfect destination for hiking, camping, and picnicking.
  • Stones River Greenway: This 10-mile trail is perfect for biking, running, or strolling through nature.
  • The Cedars of Lebanon State Park: This state park features hiking trails, picnic areas, and a campground for visitors to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hermitage

Hermitage offers some excellent hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Stones River Greenway: This 10-mile trail is perfect for biking, running, or strolling through nature.
  • Radnor Lake State Park: This park offers several hiking trails through the forest and around the lake.
  • Percy Priest Lake: Enjoy a scenic bike ride along the lake or take a more challenging route through the hills.

Best Things to Do in Hermitage: Activities and Tours

Hermitage offers a variety of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Take a tour of the Hermitage: Explore the historic mansion, grounds, and museum to learn more about Andrew Jackson and his time as president.
  • Visit Nashville Shores Lakeside Resort: This water park is a popular attraction for families and those looking to beat the heat during the summer months.
  • Go fishing or boating on Percy Priest Lake: With several marinas and ramps, it’s easy to get out on the water and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  • Take a hike at Long Hunter State Park: With over 2,600 acres of natural beauty, Long Hunter State Park is a perfect destination for hiking, camping, and picnicking.
  • Explore the local food scene: From traditional Southern cuisine to international flavors, Hermitage has something for everyone.

Food and Drink Scene of Hermitage

Hermitage offers plenty of options for foodies and beverage enthusiasts. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot chicken: A Nashville delicacy, hot chicken is a spicy fried chicken dish that’s become a regional favorite.
  • Meat and three: A traditional Southern meal that includes your choice of meat (usually fried chicken, catfish, or pork chops) and three sides (such as mashed potatoes, green beans, and macaroni and cheese).
  • Biscuits and gravy: This classic breakfast dish features homemade biscuits smothered in creamy sausage gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: A Southern staple, sweet tea is a refreshing drink made with black tea and lots of sugar.
  • Craft beer: Nashville has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries located in and around Hermitage.
  • Whiskey: Tennessee is known for its bourbon and whiskey, and several distilleries offer tours and tastings for visitors to enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Hermitage

Tennessee has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in the city of Hermitage. Visitors should be aware of the following customs and traditions:

  • Southern hospitality: The people of Hermitage are known for their friendliness and welcoming nature, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you.
  • Respect for history: Hermitage is a city with deep historical roots, and visitors should show respect for the city’s many historic landmarks and monuments.
  • Love of country music: Tennessee is known as the birthplace of country music, and you’ll find plenty of opportunities to enjoy live music in Hermitage.

Getting to Hermitage: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options available for getting to Hermitage. Here are some options:
