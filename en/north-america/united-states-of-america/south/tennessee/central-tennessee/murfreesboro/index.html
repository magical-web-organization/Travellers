Introduction to Murfreesboro

Located in Central Tennessee, Murfreesboro is steeped in history and outdoor adventure. The city is home to Middle Tennessee State University and boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, as well as a variety of museums and cultural attractions. From hiking trails to local breweries, Murfreesboro has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Murfreesboro

  • Cannonsburgh Village
  • Oaklands Mansion
  • Bradley Academy Museum
  • Stones River National Battlefield
  • Discovery Center
  • Murfreesboro Greenway System

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Murfreesboro is home to a variety of museums and historical places that showcase the city’s rich heritage. Here are a few must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Cannonsburgh Village: This recreated pioneer village gives visitors a glimpse into life in the early 19th century. With over 20 buildings, including a general store, a gristmill, and a schoolhouse, there’s plenty to explore.

  • Oaklands Mansion: This historic mansion was once home to one of Murfreesboro’s most prominent families. Today, visitors can take guided tours and learn about the history of the mansion and its former residents.

  • Bradley Academy Museum: This museum is housed in an old school and focuses on African American history in Murfreesboro and the surrounding area. Exhibits include artifacts and photographs from throughout the region’s history.

  • Stones River National Battlefield: This historic battlefield was the site of a major Civil War battle in 1862. Visitors can take self-guided tours and learn about the history of the battle and its significance in the war.

Natural Attractions of Murfreesboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Murfreesboro is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, and there are plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure. Here are a few top natural attractions:

  • Murfreesboro Greenway System: This system of trails and parks is perfect for hiking, running, biking, or just enjoying the outdoors. With multiple access points throughout the city, it’s easy to explore.

  • Barfield Crescent Park: This 430-acre park offers hiking and biking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas. It’s also home to a variety of wildlife, including deer and foxes.

  • Stones River Greenway: This paved trail follows the banks of the Stones River and offers beautiful views of the water and surrounding forests and meadows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Murfreesboro

  • Murfreesboro Greenway Trail: This trail runs for more than 12 miles and offers a variety of surfaces, from paved to gravel. It’s perfect for walking, running, or biking and passes through some of the city’s most beautiful natural areas.

  • Gateway Island Trail: This easy, half-mile trail is perfect for families or anyone looking for a short, scenic walk. It circles a small pond and offers views of the nearby wetlands and forests.

  • Cason Trail: This moderate four-mile trail circles Cason Lane Park and offers views of the surrounding fields and forests. It’s perfect for hikers and bikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Murfreesboro: Activities and Tours

  • Batey Farms: This family-owned farm offers a variety of activities, including pumpkin patches in the fall and berry-picking in the summer. There’s also a playground, petting zoo, and farm store.

  • Murfreesboro Ghost Tours: Take a spooky stroll through the city’s historic downtown and learn about its haunted history. Tours are offered year-round.

  • Hop Springs: This local brewery and beer garden offers tastings, live music, and a variety of events throughout the year. It’s the perfect spot to relax and enjoy a cold beer with friends.

Week-Long Itinerary for Murfreesboro

Day 1: Explore Downtown Murfreesboro, including the Square and Cannonsburgh Village. Day 2: Visit the Oaklands Mansion and take a tour of the museum. Day 3: Spend the day hiking or biking on the Murfreesboro Greenway Trail. Day 4: Visit the Stones River National Battlefield and learn about the history of the Civil War. Day 5: Take a tour of the Bradley Academy Museum and learn about the African American history of Murfreesboro. Day 6: Enjoy a day at Barfield Crescent Park, including hiking, picnicking, or playing on the playground. Day 7: Sample local beers at Hop Springs Brewery and Beer Garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Murfreesboro

Murfreesboro has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a variety of restaurants and bars to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot chicken: A spicy fried chicken dish that’s a Nashville classic.
  • Meat-and-three: A traditional Southern meal with a protein and three sides.
  • Barbecue: Tennessee is known for its barbecue, and Murfreesboro is no exception.

Local Beverages

  • Jack Daniel’s: This world-renowned whiskey is distilled in Lynchburg, just a short drive from Murfreesboro.
  • Tennessee cider: Hard apple cider is a popular local drink.
  • Craft beer: Murfreesboro has several breweries that offer a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Murfreesboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Embassy Suites by Hilton: Located in the heart of downtown, this hotel offers spacious suites and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Baymont by Wyndham: This budget-friendly hotel is located near MTSU and offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • The Sweat House: This historic guesthouse offers cozy rooms and a unique glimpse into Murfreesboro’s past.

What to Buy in Murfreesboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tennessee whiskey: Jack Daniel’s is just one of several distilleries in the area that offer tours and tastings.
  • Handmade crafts: Local artisans create a variety of handmade crafts, including pottery and jewelry.
  • Civil War memorabilia: Stones River National Battlefield has a gift shop that offers a variety of Civil War-themed souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Murfreesboro

Murfreesboro is a Southern city with a strong sense of community and hospitality. Visitors should be prepared for friendly locals and a laid-back, welcoming atmosphere. The area also has a rich history, and visitors should take time to learn about the city’s past and its ongoing cultural contributions.

Best Time to Visit Murfreesboro

The best time to visit Murfreesboro is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Murfreesboro

Do:

  • Visit the Stones River National Battlefield to learn about the history of the area.
  • Enjoy the outdoors on the Murfreesboro Greenway Trail or at Barfield Crescent Park.
  • Sample local beers at Hop Springs Brewery and Beer Garden.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Leave trash behind on hiking or biking trails.
  • Drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Murfreesboro

Murfreesboro is generally a safe city, but visitors should still take basic precautions. Keep your valuables secured and don’t leave them unattended, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Murfreesboro

Visitors to Murfreesboro should take common-sense precautions when it comes to their health. Stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and be on the lookout for ticks and other pests when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Murfreesboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Murfreesboro

  • Nashville International Airport: Located about 45 minutes northwest of Murfreesboro, this is the nearest major airport.

Bus Stations Near Murfreesboro

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: There is a Greyhound station in downtown Murfreesboro.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient option for getting around Murfreesboro and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Murfreesboro

Murfreesboro has a number of transportation options, including:

  • Murfreesboro Greenway Trail: This system of trails and parks is perfect for hiking, running, biking, or just enjoying the outdoors.
  • Public transportation: Murfreesboro has a bus system that offers service throughout the city.
  • Ridesharing: Uber and Lyft are both available in Murfreesboro.

Where to Go Next from Murfreesboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nashville: Just 45 minutes away, Nashville is a must-visit destination for music lovers and anyone looking for a taste of Southern cuisine and culture.
  • Lynchburg: This small town is home to the Jack Daniel’s distillery and offers tours and tastings of the famous Tennessee whiskey.
  • Chattanooga: About an hour and a half from Murfreesboro, Chattanooga is another scenic Tennessee destination with a variety of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Murfreesboro is a charming Southern city with a rich history and plenty to see and do. From its outdoor adventures to its museums and cultural attractions, there’s something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoors enthusiast, a trip to Murfreesboro is sure to be a memorable experience.
