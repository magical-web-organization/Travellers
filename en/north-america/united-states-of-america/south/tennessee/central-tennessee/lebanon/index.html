Introduction to Lebanon

Lebanon is a charming city located in Central Tennessee, United States of America. It is a perfect mix of natural beauty and cultural heritage. The city offers a plethora of activities for tourists, including hiking and biking routes, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you are a history buff or an adventure seeker, Lebanon has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lebanon

Here is a list of some of the top attractions that you should visit in Lebanon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Crackerneck Wildlife Management Area - This area is known for its beautiful wildlife, including deer, ducks, and turkeys, and is an excellent spot for hunting and fishing.
  • Tuckers Gap Event Center - A cultural center that hosts many events including concerts, theatrical performances, and art exhibitions.
  • Wilson County Courthouse - The courthouse is a must-visit place for its awe-inspiring architecture, and it has played a crucial role in Lebanon’s history.

Natural Attractions of Lebanon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cumberland River Bicentennial Trail - The hiking and biking trail runs along the Cumberland River and offers stunning views of the Cumberland Mountains.
  • Fiddlers Grove Historic Village - This historic village is located in the Fiddlers Grove Nature Conservatory and features walking trails, a lake, and beautiful gardens.
  • Old Hickory Lake - A perfect spot for swimming, fishing, boating, or simply relaxing on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lebanon

Lebanon is home to numerous hiking and biking routes, with many of them running through beautiful nature reserves.

Some of the top hiking and biking trails in Lebanon include:

  • Jones Mill Mountain Bike Trail - A singletrack trail that offers a challenging ride with numerous twists and turns.
  • Long Hunter State Park Hiking Trails - The park has a variety of hiking trails through its beautiful wooded areas, scenic lakes, and rolling hills.
  • Cedar Forest Wildlife Management Area - A beautiful spot for hiking and biking, offering stunning views of the nearby river and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Lebanon: Activities and Tours

Lebanon offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Whether you’re interested in history or adventure sports, Lebanon has something to offer for everyone.

Some of the top things to do in Lebanon include:

  • Lebanon Ghost Tour - A guided tour that explores the city’s rich history and its haunted locations.
  • Southern Yankee Beer Company - A brewery located in Lebanon that offers beer tastings and tours.
  • Cedars of Lebanon State Park - A state park that boasts of beautiful trails, picnic areas, and a pool.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lebanon

If you are planning to spend a week in Lebanon, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Lebanon

Lebanon offers a wide range of local cuisine and beverages that you must try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Chicken - This Nashville speciality can be found in many restaurants in Lebanon.
  • Meat and Three - A traditional Southern meal that includes a meat, three side dishes, and a biscuit or cornbread.
  • Biscuits and Gravy - A classic Southern breakfast dish that you can find in many local diners.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - A Southern staple, you can find sweet tea in many local restaurants in Lebanon.
  • Jack Daniel’s Tennessee Whiskey - A world-famous whiskey that is produced in nearby Lynchburg.
  • Craft Beer - Lebanon has a thriving craft beer scene, with many breweries offering tastings and tours to visitors.

Where to Stay in Lebanon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lebanon offers a range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Some of the top hotels in Lebanon are:

  • Comfort Suites - A budget-friendly hotel located close to many attractions and restaurants.
  • Holiday Inn Express - A popular mid-range hotel known for its comfortable rooms and excellent customer service.
  • Hampton Inn and Suites - A high-end hotel located close to downtown Lebanon.

What to Buy in Lebanon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, Lebanon has many great options. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Jack Daniel’s Tennessee Whiskey - A world-famous whiskey produced in nearby Lynchburg.
  • Lebanon-Nashville Rails to Trails Book - A book that showcases the beautiful trails in the area.
  • Lebanon Farmer’s Market - A market that offers fresh produce, honey, and other locally produced goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Lebanon

Lebanon has a rich cultural heritage that visitors should be aware of. Some of the customs and traditions include:

  • Southern Hospitality - People in Lebanon tend to be warm and welcoming to visitors.
  • Manners and Respect - It’s important to be courteous and respectful to others, especially in public places.
  • Religious Traditions - Lebanon has a predominantly Christian population, and visitors should be respectful of their customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Lebanon

The best time to visit Lebanon is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lebanon

Here are some things that you should and should not do during your visit to Lebanon:

Do:

  • Do be respectful and courteous to locals and other visitors.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including hot chicken and meat-and-three dishes.
  • Do visit the historic Wilson County Courthouse and other cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places or on hiking trails.
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to others.
  • Don’t visit restricted areas, such as private property or wildlife reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lebanon

Lebanon is a safe city overall, but visitors should take the usual precautions. It’s best to avoid walking alone at night in less populated areas.

Health in Lebanon

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before visiting Lebanon. It’s also important to protect against tick-borne illnesses when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Lebanon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lebanon is easily accessible by air, road, and bus.

Airports Near Lebanon

  • Nashville International Airport - Nashville, Tennessee (25 miles from Lebanon)

Bus Stations Near Lebanon

  • Greyhound - Nashville, Tennessee (25 miles from Lebanon)

Other Options

Lebanon is also accessible by car, with several major highways and interstates passing through the city.

How to Get Around in Lebanon

The best way to get around in Lebanon is by car. Many attractions are spread out, and public transportation is limited. Some visitors also opt for bike rentals to explore the city and its scenic trails.

Where to Go Next from Lebanon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lebanon is close to many other exciting destinations and day trip options. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Nashville, Tennessee - A vibrant city known for its music scene, museums, and historical sites.
  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park - A beautiful national park located in the Appalachian Mountains.
  • Chattanooga, Tennessee - A city famous for its outdoor activities, including hiking, rock climbing, and mountain biking.

Conclusion

Lebanon, Tennessee, is a hidden gem that offers a mix of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or local cuisine, Lebanon has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and explore the best of this charming city.
