Introduction to Dickson

Nestled in the heart of Central Tennessee, Dickson is a small city rich in culture and natural beauty. Home to a number of museums, natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, and delectable local cuisine, the city offers something for everyone. Whether you’re traveling solo, as a couple, or bringing the whole family along, Dickson is the perfect destination for a relaxing, rejuvenating getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Dickson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Clement Railroad Hotel Museum: This restored historic hotel showcases the history of the city and the railroad that played a vital role in its growth.
  • The War Memorial Building: A cultural treasure of Dickson, the War Memorial Building is known for showcasing the local arts scene and hosting numerous events throughout the year.
  • Loretta Lynn’s Ranch: Just a short drive from Dickson, this famous ranch is the birthplace of the country music icon Loretta Lynn and offers guided tours of her childhood home and museum.

Natural Attractions of Dickson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Montgomery Bell State Park: Known for its hiking trails and serene lake, this park is the perfect spot for a day trip or nature getaway.
  • Turnbull Creek Trail: This trail winds through the scenic forests of Dickson and offers stunning views of the local landscape.
  • Piney River Trail: A relatively easy hike, this trail follows the Piney River and is popular among families for its scenic vistas and charming picnic spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dickson

  • Hickman Springs Trail: This 1.8-mile trail follows the scenic Hickman Springs Lake and offers a peaceful, refreshing hike.
  • Dickson City Lake Trail: An easy 1.5-mile loop, this trail boasts serene lake views and is perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • Piney River Greenway Trail: A popular trail among bikers, this 9-mile path follows the Piney River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Dickson: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Dickson Farmers Market: If you’re visiting on a Saturday, make sure to check out the local farmers market for fresh produce and handmade crafts.
  • Go Fishing: Dickson boasts several lakes and rivers perfect for a day of fishing, including Hickman Springs Lake and Piney River.
  • Take a Historic Walking Tour: Explore the city’s rich history with a guided walking tour of its historic downtown area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dickson

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the War Memorial Building and explore the local arts scene
Day 2 Take a hike along Turnbull Creek Trail
Day 3 Explore the Clement Railroad Hotel Museum and enjoy a picnic at Montgomery Bell State Park
Day 4 Go fishing at Piney River and enjoy a scenic drive along the Piney River Greenway Trail
Day 5 Take a guided tour of Loretta Lynn’s Ranch
Day 6 Go for a leisurely bike ride along Dickson City Lake Trail
Day 7 Visit the Dickson Farmers Market and take a historic walking tour of downtown Dickson

Food and Drink Scene of Dickson

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: Dickson is known for its delicious BBQ, especially at places like Daddy Billy’s and R&D BBQ.
  • Country-style cooking: You can’t leave Dickson without trying some classic southern dishes, such as fried chicken and corn bread, at Marcy Jo’s Mealhouse or Carl’s Perfect Pig.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Enjoy a locally-brewed craft beer at the local Monkey’s Tail Brewery, known for its unique blends of hops and malts.

Where to Stay in Dickson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Dickson: With a central location and a complimentary hot breakfast, Hampton Inn is a great choice for travelers looking for a comfortable, affordable stay.
  • Best Western Executive Inn: This hotel boasts an outdoor pool, free Wi-Fi, and is located near popular attractions like the Clement Railroad Hotel Museum.
  • The Alleys Inn: This charming guesthouse offers a cozy, inviting atmosphere and is located near downtown Dickson.

What to Buy in Dickson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey: Pick up a jar of locally-sourced honey from the Dickson Farmers Market.
  • Handmade crafts: Browse the local selection of handmade crafts, from pottery to quilts, at the weekly farmers market.

Local Culture and Customs of Dickson

  • Southern hospitality: Visitors to Dickson will quickly notice the friendly, welcoming nature of the locals.
  • Respect for history: Dickson is home to a number of cultural and historical attractions that are greatly respected and celebrated by the local community.

Best Time to Visit Dickson

The best time to visit Dickson is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and damp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dickson

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the city’s outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the history and culture of the city.
  • Disrespect the natural environment by littering or damaging trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dickson

Dickson is a relatively safe and low-crime city. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions and use common sense, especially when exploring less-traveled areas and hiking trails.

Health in Dickson

Dickson is home to a number of medical facilities and clinics, including TriStar Horizon Medical Center and Dickson Medical Associates. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medications with you on your trip.

Getting to Dickson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dickson

  • Nashville International Airport (BNA): Located approximately 40 miles east of Dickson, Nashville International Airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Dickson

  • Greyhound: Located just one mile from downtown Dickson, the Greyhound bus station offers bus service to a number of cities throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a popular option for exploring Dickson and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Dickson

The best way to get around Dickson is by car, as many attractions are spread out and not easily accessible by public transportation. Some hotels offer shuttles to popular destinations, and taxi and ride-share services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Dickson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nashville, TN: Just a short drive from Dickson, Nashville offers a vibrant cultural scene, live music, and a thriving food and drink culture.
  • Mammoth Cave National Park: Located in nearby Kentucky, Mammoth Cave National Park is known for its stunning cave tours and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural hotspots, delicious local cuisine or relaxing accommodations, Dickson has something for you. We hope you found our travel guide helpful for planning your next trip to this charming Tennessee city.
