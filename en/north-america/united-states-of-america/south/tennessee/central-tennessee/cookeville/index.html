Introduction to Cookeville

Cookeville Tennessee is a small, yet, vibrant city located in the heart of the Upper Cumberland. Surrounded by rolling hills, breathtaking natural sights, and cultural attractions, Cookeville provides an exceptional experience that’s hard to forget. It has something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts seeking adventure in the Upper Cumberland region, to culture lovers looking to delve into the history of Tennessee.

Cookeville is named after Richard Fielding Cooke, who is considered the “Father of Cookeville.” The city’s downtown area preserves its rich cultural heritage and includes locally-owned shops, restaurants, and cafes.

List of Places to Visit in Cookeville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cookeville History Museum

The Cookeville History Museum chronicles the history of Cookeville and the Upper Cumberland. The museum features exhibits that tell the story of the region, from the Native American settlements to present-day Cookeville.

Cummins Falls State Park

Cummins Falls State Park is one of the most scenic attractions in Tennessee. It’s known for its natural waterfalls, swimming holes, and hiking trails. The park offers a unique and memorable experience for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

Tennessee Tech University

Tennessee Tech University is a public university that serves as the academic hub for the area. It offers a wide range of programs, including Business, Education, and Engineering, among others. Its campus features many cultural and artistic events and beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of Cookeville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Burgess Falls State Park

Burgess Falls State Park is a must-see destination in Cookeville and Tennessee. It features four waterfalls, trails, and fishing opportunities. Visitors can enjoy picnics, hiking, and fishing in a wonderful natural setting.

Standing Stone State Park

Standing Stone State Park is another beautiful state park near Cookeville. It spans over 11,000 acres and features hiking trails, boating, fishing, and camping opportunities. The park is home to Standing Stone Lake, which offers canoeing and kayaking opportunities.

The Appalachian Center for Craft

The Appalachian Center for Craft celebrates the artistry and craftsmanship of the region. It offers workshops, studios, and exhibitions. The center is a valuable and enriching cultural destination for visitors and residents interested in the arts and crafts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cookeville

Burgess Falls State Park Trail

The Burgess Falls State Park Trail is a moderate hike that takes visitors to the park’s main attraction: the waterfalls. It’s a scenic route that offers views of the Upper Cumberland region’s beauty.

Cumberland Mountain State Park Trail

The Cumberland Mountain State Park Trail is a challenging hike that visitors with hiking experience would love. It’s a long route that offers stunning views of the mountains, streams, and wildlife.

City Lake Natural Area Trail

City Lake Natural Area Trail is a relatively easy and short hiking trail located near Tennessee Technological University. It’s ideal for beginners or families visiting Cookeville and provides an opportunity to explore the natural beauty that the city has to offer.

Best Things to Do in Cookeville: Activities and Tours

Tennessee Distilling Company

Tennessee Distilling Company is a small-batch distillery located in Cookeville. It features a tasting room where visitors can sample a range of spirits, including their flagship brand, “Chase Distributing.” Visitors can sign up for a whiskey-making class or tour. It’s an excellent destination for whiskey enthusiasts.

Art Prowl

Art Prowl is an annual art event in Cookeville that attracts artists and art lovers from all over the region. It features live music, food trucks, and exhibitions of paintings, sculptures, ceramics, and more. It’s an ideal cultural destination for art enthusiasts.

Upper Cumberland Wine Trail

The Upper Cumberland Wine Trail takes visitors on a tour of the best wineries in the region, including DelMonaco Winery & Vineyards, Highland Manor Winery, and Holly Ridge Winery. It’s a great way to experience the region’s rich wine culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cookeville

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Cookeville History Museum, Tennessee Tech University, and enjoy a meal in one of the downtown restaurants.
Day 2 Hike to Burgess Falls State Park and explore the nearby City Lake Natural Area.
Day 3 Go on the Upper Cumberland Wine Trail and visit some of the best wineries in the region.
Day 4 Visit the Cummins Falls State Park and enjoy a swim in one of the swimming holes.
Day 5 Explore the Appalachian Center for Craft and participate in one of its workshops or classes.
Day 6 Hike the challenging Cumberland Mountain State Park Trail and see the stunning views.
Day 7 Visit the local markets and buy some souvenirs. Spend the rest of the day relaxing and taking in the beauty of Cookeville.

Food and Drink Scene of Cookeville

Must-Try Dishes

Southern fried chicken

Southern fried chicken is a must-try dish when in Cookeville. It’s a staple in Southern cuisine and usually served with mashed potatoes and gravy.

Barbecue

Barbecue is also a staple in Southern cuisine and served in most restaurants in Cookeville. It’s a type of slow-cooked meat that’s soaked in a rich, smoky sauce.

Local Beverages

Sweet tea

Sweet tea is the traditional beverage in Cookeville and throughout the South. It’s a refreshing drink served over ice and infused with sugar and fresh tea leaves.

Craft beer

Cookeville has a growing craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering unique and delicious beer. Some of the best breweries include Red Silo Brewing Company, Jig Head Brewing Company, and Hix Farm Brewery.

Where to Stay in Cookeville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many different options for accommodation in Cookeville, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Hotels

The Country Inn & Suites by Radisson and Hampton Inn Cookeville are both excellent options for visitors looking for convenient and comfortable accommodation in the heart of Cookeville.

Hostels

For budget travelers, the Red Roof Inn Cookeville is a popular option. It offers basic amenities and a central location.

Guesthouses

Cookeville also has a variety of guesthouses and bed and breakfasts, including the beautiful and charming DelMonaco Bed & Breakfast and The Garden Inn Bed & Breakfast.

What to Buy in Cookeville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Potter’s Place

Potter’s Place is a ceramics studio that offers handmade pottery, mugs, and bowls. Visitors can purchase one-of-a-kind pieces as a unique souvenir.

Appalachian Center for Craft Shop

The Appalachian Center for Craft Shop offers unique and beautiful pottery, jewelry, and textiles from local artists. It’s a great place to find a special souvenir that captures the essence of Cookeville.

Local Culture and Customs of Cookeville

One of the unique aspects of Cookeville’s culture is its love for the outdoors. Residents and visitors alike enjoy hiking, fishing, and camping in the beautiful natural surroundings of the Upper Cumberland region. Cookeville is also known for its Southern hospitality, with locals eager to welcome visitors to the area.

Best Time to Visit Cookeville

The best time to visit Cookeville is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. This allows for optimal outdoor activities and exploration of the region’s natural parks and outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cookeville

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in the area.
  • Do take the time to explore and learn about Cookeville’s rich cultural history and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather; make sure to dress appropriately when exploring the outdoors.
  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and people of Cookeville.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cookeville

Cookeville is a relatively safe city, and visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as being aware of their surroundings and taking care of their belongings.

Health in Cookeville

Travelers should bring any medications they might need during their stay in Cookeville and know the location of the nearest hospital or emergency medical services beforehand.

Getting to Cookeville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cookeville

The closest airports to Cookeville are Nashville International Airport (BNA) and Huntsville International Airport (HSV).

Bus Stations Near Cookeville

Visitors traveling by bus can take the Greyhound bus to Cookeville from various cities in the region, including Nashville, Chattanooga, and Knoxville.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Cookeville by car or by train using Amtrak’s City of New Orleans line, which stops in nearby Livingston.

How to Get Around in Cookeville

The best way to get around Cookeville is by car or by bike, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also walk around the downtown area and take advantage of the city’s bike share program.

Where to Go Next from Cookeville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chattanooga

Chattanooga, located roughly 100 miles south of Cookeville, is a vibrant city that’s known for its art and culture scene. It features many outdoor activities and attractions such as the Tennessee Aquarium and Rock City.

Nashville

Nashville is approximately 70 miles west of Cookeville and features many cultural landmarks including Ryman Auditorium, the Country Music Hall of Fame, and the Grand Ole Opry. It’s a must-visit destination for country music fans.

Conclusion

Cookeville, Tennessee, is a unique and vibrant city that offers something to every type of traveler. Whether you’re a nature lover, culture enthusiast, foodie, or adventurer, Cookeville has everything you need to make your visit to the Upper Cumberland region an unforgettable experience.
