Introduction to Monteagle

Nestled in the heart of Central Tennessee, Monteagle is a charming mountain town situated in the Appalachian Mountains. Providing an ideal holiday destination for those looking to escape the hustle-bustle of city life, Monteagle is just a 90-minute drive from Nashville and 2-hour drive from Chattanooga making it easily accessible for a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Monteagle

Monteagle has a host of attractions to visit that are sure to add an exciting experience to your trip. Here is a list of the top destinations that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Smokehouse

The Smokehouse is a historic restaurant in Monteagle that has been serving the town for over 50 years. Established in 1960, it offers delicious southern cuisine like pulled pork, brisket, beef ribs, and banana pudding. This restaurant is a must-visit for anyone looking for a taste of Southern hospitality.

The Monteagle Sunday School Assembly

Founded in 1882, the Monteagle Sunday School Assembly is a historic non-profit organization that offers cultural and educational events for all ages. The Assembly pitches its tent every summer for eight weeks for lectures, sacrosanct assemblies, recitals, concerts, and more.

Natural Attractions of Monteagle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Fiery Gizzard Trail

The Fiery Gizzard Trail is considered one of the best hiking trails in the US and is known for its stunning waterfalls. It is an absolute must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts as it features rocky outcroppings, lush forests, and panoramic vistas.

The Grundy Forest Trailhead

If you’re looking for a great place to see some wildlife in Monteagle, The Grundy Forest Trailhead is the spot. You can expect to see deer, turkey, owls, and even eagles as you trek through the woods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monteagle

No trip to Monteagle is complete without trekking through some of the scenic hiking and biking routes. These are some of the best routes to explore:

The Stone Door Trail

This trail is a 1.6-mile hiking path with a heavy elevation change, making it only for experienced hikers. The trail includes panoramic views of the woods and waterfalls that are truly magnificent.

The Mountain Goat Trail

For a leisurely bike ride, Monteagle’s Mountain Goat Trail is perfect. This 6.5-mile trail is a paved path that passes through the beautiful nature surrounding Monteagle.

Best Things to Do in Monteagle: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, Monteagle has plenty of unique activities and tours to offer its visitors. Here are a few of the must-do activities:

A Visit to The Clingmans Dome

For stunning panoramic views of the Appalachian Mountains, a visit to the Clingmans Dome should be on your itinerary. The spectacular observation tower gives you 360-degree views of the mountains, which is definitely worth the climb up the steep incline.

The Tennessee Aquarium

If you’re in the mood for an interactive activity, the Tennessee Aquarium should be your destination. It is the largest fresh-water aquarium in the world and home to over 12,000 animals. It offers a range of activities like exploring the habitats, seeing wildlife up-close, and engaging with touch tanks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monteagle

If you’re planning a longer stay in Monteagle, here is a suggested itinerary for your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in the town, explore the surrounding nature trails.

Day 2: Visit The Smokehouse, and head out to Grundy Forest Trailhead.

Day 3: Take a bike ride on the Mountain Goat Trail.

Day 4: Go on Clingmans Dome’s observation tower.

Day 5: Visit the Tennessee Aquarium.

Day 6: Explore the Monteagle Sunday School Assembly events and performances.

Day 7: Depart Monteagle.

Food and Drink Scene of Monteagle

As the smokehouse is the primary destination for visitors, Monteagle also has a bustling food and drink scene that you must try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages that the town offers:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southern Fried Chicken: Known for its crispy exterior and tender meat, you can have a taste during your stay.

  • Flaky Biscuits With Gravy: Monteagle is famous for its homemade biscuits and smooth gravy that makes mornings delightful.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: It’s a staple of southern cuisine and served best at most restaurants in Monteagle.

  • Craft Beer: One of the emerging trends of the town’s nightlife is craft beer. You can enjoy fresh local beer at High Point Pub or Ziggys Bar.

Where to Stay in Monteagle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monteagle has a range of accommodation options for your stay, from luxury hotels to affordable bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the must-stay options:

  • Monteagle Inn is a luxurious hotel with high-end amenities and lush gardens.

  • The Edgeworth Inn is a 1930’s mansion turned bed and breakfast, boasting spacious rooms and antique furniture.

What to Buy in Monteagle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Monteagle, the following souvenirs and locally made products are worth picking up:

  • Handmade Pottery: One of the specialties of the region is pottery. Tennessee Clay Works has an excellent collection of handmade pottery that you can bring as gifts or souvenirs.

  • Local Jams and Jellies: Visit the Dutch Maid Bakery, a local shop that offers homemade jams and jellies made using fresh ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Monteagle

Monteagle is famous for its southern hospitality and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the town’s customs, including manners, greetings, and even the slow-paced lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Monteagle

The best time to visit Monteagle is between April and November when the weather is favorable for outdoor activities like hiking and cycling. The fall season is a highlight when the foliage is outstanding, so autumn is a particular time you should consider visiting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monteagle

Do:

  • Indulge in southern hospitality, from sitting on porches in rocking chairs to friendly small talk with strangers.

  • Visit the Tennessee State parks and natural landmarks in the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrupt the landscapes and natural habitats.

  • Ignore friendly greetings or not respond courteously when engaging with locals.

Safety and Health

Monteagle is a safe town to visit with no significant health risks. However, visitors should still take the necessary precautions for safety and health.

Safety in Monteagle

  • Always lock doors and windows when staying in unknown local accommodation.

  • Keep valuables out of sight when leaving your car in public parking lots.

Health in Monteagle

  • Visitors with allergies to pollen or other natural elements should be prepared with necessary medication.

Getting to Monteagle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Monteagle is conveniently located near interstates, making it an easy-to-reach destination for visitors.

Airports Near Monteagle

  • Nashville International Airport – located about an hour and a half away from Monteagle.

Bus Stations Near Monteagle

  • Greyhound bus station in Chattanooga is just two hours away from Monteagle.

Other Options

  • You can also rent a car from the Nashville or Chattanooga Airport to drive to Monteagle.

How to Get Around in Monteagle

Monteagle is a walkable town but exploring nearby attractions like the State parks will require a car. You can rent a car, and there are also taxi services available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Monteagle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chattanooga is a great next destination from Monteagle. It’s just an hour and a half away and is home to majestic waterfalls, art museums, and other historical landmarks.

  • Nashville is another option not too far away, just an hour and a half drive through some lovely countryside. You can take a stroll through the Music Row, visit the Country music Hall of Fame, and enjoy the nightlife of the city.

Conclusion

Monteagle is a charming mountain town that will make for a unique, relaxing, and memorable vacation. With a plethora of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and local cuisine, you’re sure to have a great time exploring the town’s many destinations.
