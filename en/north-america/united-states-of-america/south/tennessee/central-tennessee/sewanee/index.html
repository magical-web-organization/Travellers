Introduction to Sewanee

Nestled in the foothills of the Cumberland Plateau, Sewanee is a charming town with a rich history, a vibrant cultural scene, and breathtaking natural beauty. Founded in 1857 and home to the University of the South, this idyllic community offers visitors a plethora of sights and activities to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in history, art, outdoor adventures, or gastronomy, Sewanee is the perfect destination for you. Join us as we explore the best that this picturesque town in Tennessee has to offer.

Local Culture and Customs of Sewanee

Sewanee has a strong sense of community and hospitality, with a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through various festivals and events throughout the year. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sewanee

The best time to visit Sewanee is during the fall, when the foliage is at its most beautiful and the temperature is mild. Spring and summer are also great times to visit, with plenty of outdoor activities and events on offer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sewanee

Sewanee is a safe community, but visitors are advised to take common-sense precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

Health in Sewanee

Visitors should be aware of the risks of tick-borne illnesses in the area and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing insect repellent and checking for ticks after spending time outdoors.
