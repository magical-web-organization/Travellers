Introduction to Cowan

Cowan is a charming town located in Central Tennessee in the United States of America. This small town has a population of about 1,700, and it is known for its rich history, scenic views and outdoor activities. Whether you want to explore the town’s cultural landmarks or enjoy hiking and biking routes, Cowan has something to offer to every traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide for Cowan, we will help you plan your next visit to this beautiful town.

List of Places to Visit in Cowan

If you are planning to visit Cowan, you should not miss out on the following places.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cowan Railroad Museum: Cowan has a rich railroad history as the Nashville, Chattanooga and St. Louis (NC&StL) Railway, which was built in 1850, runs through the town. Visit Cowan Railroad Museum to learn more about the town’s railroad history.
  • Cowan Depot: Built in 1904, the Cowan Depot is an iconic landmark of the town.

Natural Attractions of Cowan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Old Stone Fort State Park: Located just outside Cowan is the Old Stone Fort State Park. This park is named after a 2000-year-old Native American structure and offers excellent hiking trails, picnic areas, and camping spots.
  • Fiery Gizzard Trail: The 12.5-mile long Fiery Gizzard Trail is one of the most popular hiking trails in Tennessee and is known for its scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cowan

Cowan is known for its outdoor activities and offers several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Duck River Trail: This 13-mile trail is perfect for biking and hiking and offers stunning views of the Duck River.
  • Walls of Jericho: The Walls of Jericho is a 6.5-mile long trail that leads to a stunning waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Cowan: Activities and Tours

Cowan offers several activities and tours, including:

  • Chattanooga Balloon Company: Take a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful landscape of Tennessee.
  • Painted Peak Brewing Co: Visit this local brewery to taste the best beer in Cowan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cowan

Day 1: Cowan Railroad Museum Day 2: Walls of Jericho trail Day 3: Old Stone Fort State Park Day 4: Fiery Gizzard Trail Day 5: Chattanooga Balloon Company Day 6: Painted Peak Brewing Co Day 7: Relax and Explore Downtown Cowan

Food and Drink Scene of Cowan

Cowan has a unique food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Country Fried Steak: This classic Southern dish is a must-try in Cowan.
  • Pecan Pie: Pecan pie is a famous dessert in Tennessee and is a must-try in Cowan.

Local Beverages

  • Jack Daniel’s Whiskey: Jack Daniel’s Distillery is located in nearby Lynchburg. Try the famous whiskey in Cowan.
  • Craft Beer: Cowan has several breweries that produce excellent craft beer.

Where to Stay in Cowan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Western Gateway Motel: This motel is a popular choice for tourists as it is located in the heart of the town.
  • Big Cedar Lodge: This lodge is located in the Old Stone Fort State Park and offers excellent accommodation options.

What to Buy in Cowan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Moon Pies: Moon Pies were invented in Tennessee and are a popular souvenir to take back home.
  • Jack Daniel’s Whiskey: Buy a bottle of the famous whiskey from a local store.

Local Culture and Customs of Cowan

Cowan has a strong Southern culture, and residents are known for their hospitality and friendliness. It is customary to say “hello” to people you pass by on the street.

Best Time to Visit Cowan

The best time to visit Cowan is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cowan
