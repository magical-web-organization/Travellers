Introduction to Smyrna

Located in Central Tennessee, Smyrna is a charming town with plenty of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. With its vibrant food, arts, and music scenes, Smyrna is a must-visit for anyone traveling to this part of Tennessee.

List of Places to Visit in Smyrna

If you’re planning a trip to Smyrna, make sure to check out these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sam Davis Home and Museum is a great place to learn about the Civil War and the life of Sam Davis, one of Tennessee’s most famous Confederate soldiers.
  • The Historic Sam Davis Battlefield is another historical site that visitors won’t want to miss, with plenty of exhibits and artifacts tracing the history of Tennessee and the American South.
  • The Lane Motor Museum features rare and unusual vehicles from around the world, including microcars, amphibious vehicles, and more.

Natural Attractions of Smyrna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stones River National Battlefield is a must-see destination for history buffs and nature lovers alike. This park features miles of hiking trails, picnic areas, and wildlife viewing opportunities, along with a visitor center and museum.
  • Sharp Springs Park is a popular spot for outdoor activities in Smyrna, with amenities like a swimming pool, playground, hiking trails, and more.
  • Lee Victory Recreation Park is another great destination for outdoor fun, with facilities for sports like baseball, disc golf, and tennis.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smyrna

Smyrna’s natural beauty makes it a great destination for hiking and biking. Some of the area’s top trails include:

  • The Stones River Greenway is a multi-use trail that spans 10 miles along the river and through historic sites.
  • The Smyrna Blueway Trail is a scenic route that winds through wetlands and woodland areas near the lake.

Best Things to Do in Smyrna: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, there are plenty of things to keep you busy in Smyrna. Some of the top activities include:

  • Visiting the Smyrna Bowling Center, a retro-style bowling alley that features laser tag, an arcade, and more.
  • Sampling the local cuisine at restaurants like the popular Slick Pig BBQ and the seafood-focused Captain D’s.
  • Taking a tour of local craft breweries, such as the Black Abbey Brewing Company and Mayday Brewery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Smyrna

Here’s a possible week-long itinerary for exploring Smyrna and its surroundings:

Day 1: Arrive in Smyrna and explore the town’s Historic Downtown District and local shops.

Day 2: Visit the Sam Davis Home and Museum and the Historic Sam Davis Battlefield, then enjoy a scenic hike or bike ride along the Stones River Greenway.

Day 3: Spend the day at Sharp Springs Park, enjoying its many sports facilities and natural beauty.

Day 4: Take a tour of the Lane Motor Museum and try some local cuisine at Slick Pig BBQ.

Day 5: Head to Lee Victory Recreation Park and try your hand at disc golf or tennis, then cool off with a swim at the park’s pool.

Day 6: See some live music at one of Smyrna’s many venues, such as the Depot District Amphitheater or the Smyrna Event Center.

Day 7: End your trip with a visit to the Stones River National Battlefield and its many hiking trails and historical sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Smyrna

Smyrna has plenty of great options for food and drink, with a focus on Southern cuisine and craft beer. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot chicken is a spicy Nashville classic that can also be found in Smyrna at places like Prince’s Hot Chicken Shack.
  • Biscuits and gravy are a Southern staple, and you can find some of the best at the local breakfast spot, Taste of Italy.
  • Fried catfish is another local favorite, served up fresh at restaurants like the Catfish House.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer is a big draw in Smyrna, with local breweries like the Black Abbey Brewing Company and Mayday Brewery.
  • Moonshine is another Southern specialty, and you can try some at the Old Smokey Moonshine Distillery in nearby Gatlinburg.

Where to Stay in Smyrna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Smyrna offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget or travel style. Some of the top choices include:

  • The Smyrna Inn, a budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions and restaurants.
  • The Hyatt Place Nashville/Downtown, a stylish and modern hotel in the heart of Nashville with easy access to Smyrna and other nearby destinations.
  • The Inn at Opryland, a charming guesthouse in a historic building with plenty of amenities and a great location.

What to Buy in Smyrna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Smyrna, be sure to check out these local shops and products:

  • The Smyrna Outdoor Market, a seasonal market that features fresh produce, handmade crafts, and locally made goods.
  • The Smyrna Candle Company, which produces handmade candles in unique scents ranging from clean linen to buckeye.
  • The Blue Porch, a local gift shop that carries handmade jewelry, pottery, and other souvenir items.

Local Culture and Customs of Smyrna

Smyrna is a welcoming and friendly town with a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and attitudes, and take time to appreciate the town’s Southern charm and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Smyrna

The best time to visit Smyrna is during the spring and fall, when temperatures are moderate and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Smyrna

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including hot chicken, biscuits and gravy, and fried catfish.
  • Explore the town’s natural beauty, with hikes, bike rides, and visits to parks like Sharp Springs and Lee Victory.
  • Take in the local culture, from the music and arts scenes to the historic sites and museums.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of Southern customs and traditions, including manners and social norms.
  • Expect fast-paced city life, as Smyrna is a more laid-back town that values its slower pace of life.
  • Overlook the natural beauty of the area, which includes both the lake and surrounding forests.

Safety and Health

Safety in Smyrna

Smyrna is a generally safe town, with low crime rates and a welcoming environment. Visitors should still exercise caution and common sense, and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Smyrna

While Smyrna does not have any major health concerns, travelers should still take steps to protect themselves from the sun, dehydration, and insect bites while enjoying the town’s outdoor activities.

Getting to Smyrna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Smyrna is easily accessible by car, with several major highways and interstates connecting it to nearby cities like Nashville and Chattanooga. Visitors can also fly into Nashville International Airport, which is just a short drive away.

Airports Near Smyrna

  • Nashville International Airport (BNA)

Bus Stations Near Smyrna

  • Greyhound Bus Lines, located in nearby Nashville

Other Options

  • Car rentals, which are available at Nashville International Airport and other nearby locations.

How to Get Around in Smyrna

While having a car is the most convenient option for getting around Smyrna, there are also several transportation options available, including ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft and local taxis.

Where to Go Next from Smyrna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Tennessee, there are plenty of great destinations within a short drive of Smyrna. Some of the top options include:

  • Nashville, the state capital and a hub of live music, food, and drink.
  • Chattanooga, a historic town with plenty of natural beauty, including Lookout Mountain and the Tennessee River.

Conclusion

With its scenic beauty, cultural attractions, and warm Southern hospitality, Smyrna is a hidden gem for visitors to Tennessee. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, this charming town has something for everyone.
