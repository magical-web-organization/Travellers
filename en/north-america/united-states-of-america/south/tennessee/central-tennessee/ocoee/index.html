Introduction to Ocoee

Nestled in the mountains of central Tennessee, Ocoee is a small town that packs a punch when it comes to outdoor adventures. Known for its whitewater rafting, hiking trails, and fishing spots, Ocoee is a must-visit destination for adventure-seekers looking to explore the great outdoors.

In addition to its natural beauty, Ocoee is home to a vibrant local culture that visitors are sure to love. From museums and historical sites to local food and drink, there’s plenty to discover in Ocoee.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll share everything you need to know to plan your trip to Ocoee.

List of Places to Visit in Ocoee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ocoee Exhibition Coal Mine and Museum: Explore a former coal mine and learn about the region’s mining history.

  • Museum Center at 5ive Points: Discover the history and culture of the Ocoee region through a variety of exhibits and programs.

  • Cherokee Removal Memorial Park: Pay tribute to the Cherokee people who were forced to leave their homes in the 1830s.

Natural Attractions of Ocoee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ocoee River: Brave the rapids on a whitewater rafting trip down the Ocoee River.

  • Chilhowee Mountain: Hike the trails and take in stunning views of the mountains and valleys below.

  • Parksville Lake: Enjoy a relaxing day of fishing, swimming, or boating on this scenic lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocoee

  • Benton MacKaye Trail: This 300-mile trail runs through several states, including Tennessee, and offers stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains.

  • Chestnut Mountain Trail: This moderate hike offers beautiful views of the Ocoee River and the surrounding mountains.

  • Tanasi Trail System: These interconnected trails range in difficulty and offer stunning views of the Ocoee River Gorge.

Best Things to Do in Ocoee: Activities and Tours

  • Zip Line Canopy Tours: Soar through the treetops on a high-flying adventure.

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic countryside on horseback.

  • Fly Fishing: Perfect for anglers of all skill levels, Ocoee offers some of the best fly fishing in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Ocoee

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: A Southern classic, served up with hush puppies and cole slaw.

  • BBQ Ribs: Slow-cooked to perfection, these ribs are a local favorite.

  • Pimento Cheese: A creamy spread made with sharp cheddar cheese and red peppers, perfect for snacking.

Local Beverages

  • Moonshine: Made in nearby distilleries, this potent drink is a must-try for those looking to experience Ocoee’s local culture.

  • Craft Beer: Ocoee is home to several breweries, offering a wide variety of unique and flavorful brews.

Where to Stay in Ocoee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ocoee River Inn: Offering comfortable rooms and a convenient location near outdoor adventures.

  • Ocoee Mist Farm Bed and Breakfast: Enjoy a peaceful stay in a historic farmhouse, with delicious homemade breakfast included.

  • Hiwassee River Cabins: Stay in a cozy cabin overlooking the river, complete with a hot tub and outdoor grill.

What to Buy in Ocoee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Moonshine: Take home a bottle of the local spirit to remember your trip.

  • Handmade Crafts: Ocoee is home to many talented artists and crafters, offering unique and beautiful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ocoee

Ocoee is a welcoming and friendly community, with a strong sense of Southern hospitality. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs, such as saying “y’all” and taking things at a relaxed pace.

Best Time to Visit Ocoee

The best time to visit Ocoee is during the spring and fall, when temperatures are mild and outdoor activities are at their best. However, summer is also a popular time to visit, especially for rafting and other water activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ocoee

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities and natural beauty of Ocoee.

  • Do try the local food and drink, especially the moonshine and BBQ.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray.

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ocoee

Ocoee is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should still exercise caution and take basic safety precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Ocoee

Visitors should take note that the rural location of Ocoee means that medical facilities may be limited and emergency response times may be longer than in more urban areas. It’s a good idea to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you.

Getting to Ocoee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocoee

  • Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport (CHA): Located approximately 35 miles south of Ocoee, this airport offers several domestic and international flights.

  • Knoxville McGhee Tyson Airport (TYS): Approximately 90 miles north of Ocoee, this airport offers a variety of domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Ocoee

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in nearby Cleveland, approximately 20 miles north of Ocoee.

  • Southeast Tennessee Human Resource Agency: Offers transportation services for seniors and people with disabilities.

Other Options

Visitors may also choose to rent a car or hire a taxi or rideshare service for transportation to and around Ocoee.

How to Get Around in Ocoee

A car or bicycle is recommended for getting around Ocoee, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors should also be prepared for some winding and hilly roads when navigating the area.

Where to Go Next from Ocoee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: Located approximately 70 miles to the east, this stunning national park offers hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

  • Chattanooga: Just a short drive south of Ocoee, Chattanooga is a vibrant city with plenty to see and do, including the Tennessee Aquarium and the historic Chattanooga Choo Choo.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, Ocoee has something to offer everyone. From whitewater rafting to hiking to enjoying local food and drink, Ocoee is a hidden gem in the heart of Tennessee. Plan your trip today and discover all that Ocoee has to offer.
