Introduction to Clarksville

Clarksville is a city in Central Tennessee that sits along the banks of the Cumberland River. It is the fifth largest city in the state and is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Clarksville

Clarksville is packed with things to see and do, from museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Customs House Museum and Cultural Center: This museum showcases the history of Clarksville and its surrounding areas, with exhibits on art, science, and history.

  • Roxy Regional Theatre: A historic theater that has been operating since 1947, it features live performances, movies, and events.

  • Fort Defiance Civil War Park & Interpretive Center: This historical site features an interpretive center and a preserved fort from the Civil War era.

Natural Attractions of Clarksville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dunbar Cave State Park: This park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, fishing opportunities, and guided tours of the cave and its geology.

  • Mossy Ridge Trail: A scenic hiking trail located in the nearby Percy Warner Park, offering views of the surrounding hills and forests.

  • Clarksville Greenway: A pedestrian and bike trail that stretches for 5 miles and offers beautiful views of the Cumberland River.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Clarksville

  • Bicentennial Greenway: This multi-use trail stretches for nearly 9 miles and offers a great opportunity for hiking, biking, and jogging.

  • North Ford Street Mountain Bike Park: This park offers over 4 miles of trails specifically designed for mountain biking.

  • Red River Trail: This trail follows the Red River and offers scenic views and opportunities for fishing and kayaking.

Best Things to Do in Clarksville: Activities and Tours

  • Downtown Artists’ Co-Op: A local art gallery featuring the work of over 20 artists from the Clarksville area.

  • Wetland Walkway Boardwalk: A mile-long boardwalk offering views of the surrounding wetlands and wildlife.

  • Rivers and Spires Festival: An annual festival featuring live music, vendors, carnival rides, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clarksville

Day 1: Tour the Customs House Museum and Cultural Center, followed by a live performance at the Roxy Regional Theatre.

Day 2: Visit Fort Defiance Civil War Park and Interpretive Center, followed by a hike at Dunbar Cave State Park.

Day 3: Explore the Clarksville Greenway and enjoy a picnic lunch at one of its many picturesque spots.

Day 4: Take a mountain biking excursion at North Ford Street Mountain Bike Park, followed by a stroll along the Wetland Walkway Boardwalk.

Day 5: Visit the Red River Trail and enjoy a kayaking trip along the river.

Day 6: Attend the Rivers and Spires Festival and enjoy live music, carnival rides, and local food and drink.

Day 7: Visit any of the places you missed earlier in the week, and enjoy a relaxing evening in one of Clarksville’s local establishments.

Food and Drink Scene of Clarksville

Clarksville offers a diverse culinary scene with a mix of traditional southern cuisine and international flavors. Here are some dishes and local beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Chicken Sandwich: A southern classic made with fried chicken and topped with hot sauce.

  • Meat & Three: A classic southern meal that consists of meat (fried chicken, country ham, or roast beef) served with three side dishes (typical options include green beans, corn, mac and cheese, sweet potatoes, etc.).

  • Pho: A Vietnamese soup typically made with rice noodles, beef broth, and varying meat toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Tennessee whiskey: A must-try for whiskey lovers, Tennessee is home to several renowned whiskey distilleries such as Jack Daniel’s and George Dickel.

  • Clarksville’s own Blackhorse Brewery: This local brewery offers craft beers and pub fare.

Where to Stay in Clarksville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clarksville has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and needs, from budget-friendly hotels to luxury options. Some recommended accommodations include:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Clarksville: A popular hotel with contemporary rooms and an on-site restaurant.

  • Riverview Inn: A historic hotel located in downtown Clarksville with river views and a full-service restaurant.

  • Quality Inn Exit 4: A budget-friendly hotel located near the interstate and offering free breakfast and Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Clarksville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Clarksville-made crafts and art: Visit Downtown Artists’ Co-Op and discover unique handmade crafts and art made by local artists.

  • Tennessee Whiskey: Pick up a bottle of Tennessee’s famous whiskey from Jack Daniel’s Distillery or George Dickel Distillery.

  • Handmade jams: Discover a variety of handmade jams and jellies made from locally grown and harvested fruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Clarksville

Clarksville has a rich cultural heritage rooted in the traditions and customs of southern United States. The locals are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and visitors are always welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Clarksville

The best time to visit Clarksville is between April and October when the weather is warmer and many outdoor activities are available. Fall is particularly beautiful, as the trees turn into shades of red, orange, and yellow. Summers in Clarksville can be hot and humid, so be sure to pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clarksville

Do:

  • Explore the local food scene and try classic Southern dishes.

  • Visit the many museums and cultural attractions that Clarksville has to offer.

  • Take a trip down the Cumberland River and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray if you plan on spending time outdoors.

  • Ignore local customs and traditions of southern United States.

  • Disregard the natural beauty of Clarksville’s many parks and trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clarksville

Clarksville is generally a safe city, but take precautions as you would in any other city. Always be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when traveling alone at night.

Health in Clarksville

Healthcare facilities are located throughout Clarksville, from general practitioners to specialty clinics. It’s important to have proper travel insurance when visiting the United States, as healthcare costs can be quite high.

Getting to Clarksville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clarksville

Clarksville is served by the Nashville International Airport (BNA), located about 48 miles away from the city center. There are several shuttle and taxi services available between the airport and Clarksville.

Bus Stations Near Clarksville

Clarksville does have a Greyhound bus station, located at 206 South Third Street.

Other Options

Clarksville is easily accessible by car, with several highways running through the city and many rental car services available. There are also several ride-sharing services operating in Clarksville, such as Uber and Lyft.

How to Get Around in Clarksville

Clarksville is a car-dependent city, but there are several public transportation options available such as the Clarksville Transit System buses, taxis, and several rental car companies. Biking is also popular, with several bike rental programs and bike paths available.

Where to Go Next from Clarksville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nashville, TN: Located around 50 miles South of Clarksville, Nashville is famous for its country music scene, nightlife, and rich history.

  • Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area: Located about an hour away from Clarksville and stretching across Western Kentucky and Tennessee, this area offers opportunities for outdoor recreation, hiking, and camping.

Conclusion

Clarksville is a city of contrasts, offering a blend of rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a food lover, there’s something for you in Clarksville. Plan your visit today and discover all that this city and the surrounding areas have to offer.
