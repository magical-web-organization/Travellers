Introduction to Mount Juliet

Nestled in the heart of Central Tennessee, Mount Juliet is a charming city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its rich heritage, outdoor adventures, and vibrant food scene, Mount Juliet has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Juliet

If you are planning a trip to Mount Juliet, these are the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Charlie Daniels Park: A beautiful park that has several hiking trails, picnic areas, and sports facilities.
  • The Mount Juliet Museum: Houses artifacts and memorabilia that highlight the city’s history.
  • The Cedars Of Lebanon State Park: A historical state park with 900-acres of land that’s perfect for picnicking, hiking, and camping.

Natural Attractions of Mount Juliet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stones River National Battlefield: A beautiful park with hiking trails, picnic areas, and war memorials.
  • The Bellwood Quarry: A stunning lake with hiking trails, picnic areas, and fishing spots.
  • Long Hunter State Park: With over 2,600-acres of land, this state park is home to many hiking trails, swimming spots, and camping sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Juliet

  • Couchville Lake Loop Trail: A nice hike that takes you around Couchville Lake.
  • Stones River Greenway: A paved trail along the Stones River that is perfect for biking and walking.
  • Cedar Glade Trail: A short hiking trail that takes you through a wildflower-filled meadow.

Best Things to Do in Mount Juliet: Activities and Tours

  • Sky High Adventures: An amusement park with go-karts, bumper boats, and mini-golf.
  • Rose Mont Plantation: A beautiful historic home that offers tours to visitors.
  • Kenny Perry’s Country Creek Golf Course: An 18-hole golf course with stunning views of the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Juliet

Day 1: Hike the Couchville Lake Loop Trail and have a picnic at the Cedars Of Lebanon State Park.

Day 2: Visit The Mount Juliet Museum and learn about the city’s history, followed by a round of mini-golf at Sky High Adventures.

Day 3: Take a tour of Rose Mont Plantation and explore the beautiful gardens.

Day 4: Biking on the Stones River Greenway and have lunch at a local restaurant.

Day 5: Tee off at Kenny Perry’s Country Creek Golf Course and enjoy the scenery.

Day 6: Explore Stones River National Battlefield, followed by a relaxing picnic at the Bellwood Quarry.

Day 7: Relax in Long Hunter State Park and enjoy the beautiful views.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Juliet

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot chicken: A spicy chicken dish that is a local specialty.
  • Barbeque ribs: Mouth-watering ribs that are slow-cooked and seasoned to perfection.
  • Fried catfish: A southern classic that is crispy and delicious.

Local Beverages

  • Tennessee whiskey: A locally-distilled whiskey that is world-famous.
  • Sweet tea: A southern favorite that is refreshing and perfect for hot days.
  • Craft beer: Several local breweries offer unique and delicious beer options.

Where to Stay in Mount Juliet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Courtyard by Marriott: A comfortable and modern hotel that offers great amenities.
  • Quality Inn: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites: A well-rated hotel that offers great value for money.

What to Buy in Mount Juliet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tennessee whiskey: A souvenir that you can’t go wrong with.
  • Local honey: A delicious and healthy souvenir that supports the local economy.
  • Handmade crafts: Several local stores offer unique and beautiful handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Juliet

Mount Juliet is a friendly and welcoming city, with strong ties to its southern heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including Southern hospitality and politeness.

Best Time to Visit Mount Juliet

The best time to visit Mount Juliet is in the Fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the leaves change colors. Spring (March to May) is another great time to visit if you want to enjoy the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Juliet

Do:

  • Embrace the city’s southern hospitality and politeness.
  • Enjoy the local food and drink.
  • Explore the great outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or their customs.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Juliet

Mount Juliet is a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding poorly-lit areas at night.

Health in Mount Juliet

Visitors to Mount Juliet should take standard health precautions, such as avoiding tap water and staying up to date on vaccinations.

Getting to Mount Juliet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Juliet

  • Nashville International Airport (BNA): The closest airport to Mount Juliet, located about 10 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Mount Juliet

  • Greyhound: 615-254-7285.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies are available at Nashville International Airport.
  • Taxi or rideshare: Several taxi and rideshare companies operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Mount Juliet

The best way to get around Mount Juliet is by car or bike. Several car rental companies are available, and several biking paths are available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Mount Juliet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nashville: The “Music City” is just a 20-minute drive from Mount Juliet.
  • Lebanon: A charming small town that’s home to the Fiddler’s Grove Historical Village and the Cracker Barrel headquarters.

Conclusion

Mount Juliet is a wonderful city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and enjoying southern cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Mount Juliet.
