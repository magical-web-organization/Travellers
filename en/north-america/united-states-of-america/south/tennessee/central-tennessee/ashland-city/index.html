Introduction to Ashland City

Located in Central Tennessee, Ashland City is a charming town that offers visitors a wealth of exciting attractions and activities. From historic museums to scenic hiking routes, there is something for everyone in this vibrant community. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan the perfect visit to Ashland City.

List of Places to Visit in Ashland City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cheatham County Courthouse – An iconic landmark in Ashland City, this beautiful building dates back to 1938 and boasts an impressive art deco design.
  • Ashland City Masonic Lodge #604 – Explore the history of Freemasonry in Ashland City with a visit to this historic building.
  • The Bell Witch Cave – This eerie cave was home to the infamous Bell Witch, a ghostly figure that haunted a local family in the early 19th century.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Riverbluff Park – Experience the beauty of the Cumberland River with a visit to this stunning park. Featuring hiking trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds, it’s a perfect spot for families.
  • Sycamore Creek Park – This expansive park is perfect for nature lovers, with its acres of forested land, fishing spots, and hiking trails.
  • Cheatham Lake – A popular destination for boating, swimming, and fishing, this lake offers visitors breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashland City

  • Bowie Nature Park – With over 700 acres of natural beauty to explore, this park is a must-visit for hikers and bikers alike.
  • The Cumberland River Bicentennial Trail – This scenic walking and biking path stretches for 6.7 miles along the Cumberland River, offering beautiful views of the water and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Ashland City: Activities and Tours

  • Vertical Challenge – Experience the thrill of climbing with a visit to Vertical Challenge, Ashland City’s premier indoor climbing facility.
  • The Escape Game Nashville – Located just a short drive from Ashland City, this immersive escape room experience will put your problem-solving skills to the test.
  • Nashville Night Tour with Dinner – Take a guided tour of the best attractions and landmarks in Nashville, then enjoy a delicious Southern-style dinner at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashland City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Chicken – A Nashville staple, this fried chicken dish is seasoned with fiery spices that will make your taste buds sing.
  • Sweet Tea – This Southern classic is the perfect accompaniment to any meal.
  • Pecan Pie – End your meal on a sweet note with a slice of this delicious pie.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer – Ashland City is home to several breweries that offer up a wide variety of beers, from IPAs to stouts.
  • Whiskey and Bourbon – No visit to Tennessee is complete without sampling some of the state’s famous whiskey and bourbon.

Where to Stay in Ashland City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Ashland City – This modern hotel is conveniently located near the town center.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham Ashland City – This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • Airbnb – For a more authentic experience, consider renting a room or apartment through Airbnb.

What to Buy in Ashland City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sycamore Creek Soap Company – This local business offers a wide selection of handmade soaps and skincare products.
  • The Mill District – This shopping destination is home to several local boutiques that offer everything from handmade jewelry to vintage clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashland City

Ashland City is known for its warm hospitality and Southern charm. Visitors should be prepared to encounter friendly locals who are happy to share their love for the town with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Ashland City

The best time to visit Ashland City is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the autumn leaves are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashland City

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors – Ashland City is surrounded by natural beauty, so be sure to take advantage of the many hiking trails and parks in the area.
  • Sample the local cuisine – From hot chicken to pecan pie, there are plenty of delicious Southern dishes to try in Ashland City.

Don’t:

  • Forget your bug spray – Mosquitoes and other pests can be a nuisance in the Tennessee countryside, so make sure to come prepared with insect repellent.
  • Overpack – Ashland City is a small town, so there’s no need to bring more than you really need.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashland City

Ashland City is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, particularly when exploring isolated areas or at night.

Health in Ashland City

Visitors to Ashland City should be aware of the risk of tick-borne diseases such as Lyme disease and Rocky Mountain spotted fever. Be sure to wear long pants and use insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Ashland City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ashland City

  • Nashville International Airport – Located 25 miles from Ashland City, this is the nearest major airport.

Bus Stations Near Ashland City

  • Greyhound Bus Line – This bus line services the nearby town of Clarksville, which is just a short drive from Ashland City.

Other Options

  • Rental Car – Renting a car is a convenient option for visitors who want to explore all that Ashland City has to offer.

How to Get Around in Ashland City

The best way to get around Ashland City is by car. While the town is small enough to explore on foot, having a car will allow you to venture out to nearby attractions and destinations with ease.

Where to Go Next from Ashland City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nashville – Just a short drive from Ashland City, Nashville is known for its vibrant music scene, museums, and historic landmarks.
  • Franklin – This charming town is home to numerous art galleries, boutiques, and restaurants.
  • Clarksville – Located just 20 miles from Ashland City, this town has a rich history and a vibrant downtown area.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to plan your next visit to Ashland City. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or authentic Southern cuisine, this charming town has it all.
