Introduction to Lewisburg

Nestled in the heart of Central Tennessee, Lewisburg is a charming southern town full of history, outdoor adventure, and friendly locals. Founded in 1837, the town has a rich cultural heritage and small-town vibe that draws visitors from around the region.

Whether you’re exploring the many museums and historical landmarks, hiking through scenic wilderness, or sampling the delicious local cuisine, Lewisburg offers something for every type of traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you plan the perfect trip to this hidden gem in the South.

List of Places to Visit in Lewisburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marshall County Historical Museum

This small museum highlights the history of Marshall County, from prehistoric times to the present day. Exhibits cover the Civil War era, local industries, and the contributions of influential residents.

Hazelburn Historic House

Built in the 1830s, Hazelburn is a beautiful antebellum mansion that has been meticulously restored and preserved. Visitors can take a guided tour of the home and learn about life in the Old South.

Tennessee Walking Horse National Museum

Learn about the history and culture of the Tennessee Walking Horse breed at this fascinating museum. Exhibits include interactive displays, historical artifacts, and information on the breed’s impact on the local economy.

Natural Attractions of Lewisburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Henry Horton State Park

Located just a short drive from Lewisburg, Henry Horton State Park offers over 1,100 acres of pristine wilderness and recreational opportunities. Visitors can hike, fish, swim, camp, and more in this beautiful natural oasis.

Rock Creek Park

Explore miles of hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic vistas at this popular park. Rock Creek is a great spot for bird watching, fishing, and taking in the stunning beauty of Central Tennessee.

Elk River Dam

Visit the Elk River Dam and take in the stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside. The dam is a popular spot for fishing and boating, as well as picnicking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewisburg

Henry Horton State Park Trails

Henry Horton State Park offers several hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. The 5.5 mile Hickory Ridge Trail offers stunning views of the park and surrounding area, while the 3.5 mile Wilhoite Mill Trail winds through scenic pine forests and past historic sites.

The Lost Creek Overlook Trail

Located in nearby Russell Hill Wildlife Management Area, the Lost Creek Overlook Trail is a 2.5 mile loop that offers breathtaking views of the Lewisburg area. The trail is moderate in difficulty and is a great option for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Lewisburg: Activities and Tours

The Escape Game Nashville

Located just a short drive from Lewisburg, The Escape Game Nashville offers a thrilling and immersive experience for visitors. Solve puzzles, decipher clues, and beat the clock to escape from one of several themed rooms.

Lewisburg Ghost Walk

Explore the town of Lewisburg after dark and hear about the many ghostly legends and haunts that are said to linger in the area. This walking tour is a fun and spooky way to experience the town’s rich history and folklore.

Food and Drink Scene of Lewisburg

Must-Try Dishes

Country Ham Biscuits

A southern breakfast classic, country ham biscuits are made from flaky biscuits and thin slices of salt-cured ham. Local shops serve up some of the best in the region.

Fried Chicken

Lewisburg is famous for its delicious fried chicken. From crispy and juicy to spicy and savory, you’ll find the perfect plate of chicken at any number of local restaurants.

Local Beverages

Sweet Tea

A classic southern drink, sweet tea is a staple in Lewisburg. Served cold and sweetened to perfection, it’s the perfect way to cool down on a hot summer day.

Coca-Cola

Lewisburg is one of the few places in the world where Coca-Cola is still bottled in glass bottles. The local bottling plant offers behind-the-scenes tours and a chance to sample some old-fashioned Coke.

Where to Stay in Lewisburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hampton Inn Lewisburg

This modern hotel offers spacious rooms and a host of amenities, including a fitness center, outdoor pool, and complimentary breakfast. It’s conveniently located near downtown and many local attractions.

Walking Horse Lodge Lewisburg

This cozy lodge offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a restaurant, lounge, and outdoor pool. It’s a great choice for travelers looking for a home away from home in a beautiful setting.

What to Buy in Lewisburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Walking Horse Souvenirs

Pick up a souvenir of your visit to Lewisburg by shopping for Walking Horse-themed gifts and memorabilia. T-shirts, hats, and other items are widely available throughout town.

Local Artwork

Browse local galleries and art shops for unique and original pieces by regional artists. From paintings and sculptures to pottery and jewelry, there’s something for every artistic taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewisburg

Lewisburg is a friendly and welcoming community that takes great pride in its regional heritage and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to explore the many museums, historic sites, and cultural events that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Lewisburg

The best time to visit Lewisburg depends on your preferred activities and weather preferences. Summer is a popular time for outdoor adventure and festivals, while fall offers stunning foliage and cooler temperatures. Spring brings blooming flowers and mild weather, and winter is perfect for cozying up by the fire with a cup of hot cocoa.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewisburg

Do:

  • Explore the town’s many museums and historic sites
  • Try the local cuisine, including fried chicken and sweet tea
  • Participate in outdoor activities like hiking and fishing

Don’t:

  • Forget to wear sunscreen and take other precautions against the sun
  • Leave valuables in your car or in plain sight

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewisburg

Lewisburg is a safe town with a friendly and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors should take basic precautions like locking car doors and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lewisburg

There are several healthcare providers located throughout town, including a hospital, urgent care centers, and primary care clinics. Visitors should have health insurance in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Lewisburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewisburg

The nearest major airport is Nashville International Airport, located about an hour’s drive from Lewisburg. Several smaller regional airports are also located within a short drive.

Bus Stations Near Lewisburg

Greyhound and other bus lines serve Nashville, which is the closest major city. From Nashville, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle or taxi to Lewisburg.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Lewisburg from other parts of Tennessee or nearby states. The town is located just off of Interstate 65 and is easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Lewisburg

The best way to get around in Lewisburg is by car. Several rental car agencies are located in town and at nearby airports, and taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lewisburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nashville

Just an hour’s drive from Lewisburg, Nashville is a city known for its music scene, history, and Southern hospitality. Visitors can explore the many museums, galleries, and stages that make up Music City, or simply enjoy the city’s vibrant culture and nightlife.

Franklin

Located about 45 minutes from Lewisburg, Franklin is a charming and historic town that’s known for its picturesque Downtown Square, unique shops, and delicious restaurants. Visitors can also explore the Civil War history that’s so deeply rooted in the area.

Conclusion

Lewisburg is a hidden gem in the heart of Central Tennessee. With its rich history, abundant natural beauty, and friendly locals, it’s a perfect destination for travelers of all ages and interests. Use this guide to plan your visit and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
