Introduction to Dover

Dover is a small town in Central Tennessee, located in the South region of the United States of America. It is known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and historical significance. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Dover has something to offer for everyone. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, outdoor activities, local culture, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Dover

Dover is home to several culturally important attractions and natural wonders. In this section, we will explore the must-visit places in Dover.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Donelson National Battlefield: This historic battlefield is the site of the first major Union victory of the Civil War. It features a visitor center, museum, and over six miles of hiking trails.
  • Stewart County Visitor Center: This visitor center offers information about the local area, and has exhibits on the history of Stewart County.
  • Surrender House- Bennett Place State Historic Site: This site is the location where Confederate General John B. Hood surrendered his Confederate forces to Union General George Thomas on April 12, 1865.

Natural Attractions of Dover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area: This 170,000-acre park is a popular destination for outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, boating, and camping. It is home to elk and bison herds, and has several scenic overlooks.
  • Cross Creeks National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge is home to over 15,000 acres of wetlands, woodland, and grasslands. It offers bird watching, hiking, and fishing opportunities.
  • Fort Donelson Lake: This lake is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and swimming. It offers over 15 miles of shoreline and several boat ramps.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dover

Dover is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • North/South Trail: This 24-mile hiking and biking trail connects the North and South sections of the Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area. It offers scenic views of the lake and the surrounding woods.
  • Fort Donelson National Battlefield Hiking Trails: These hiking trails offer visitors the opportunity to explore the historic battlefield and see the surrounding countryside. The trails range from easy to challenging, with options for all skill levels.
  • Blue Trail: This 4.5-mile trail is located in Cross Creeks National Wildlife Refuge. It offers scenic views of the wetlands and the nearby lake.

Best Things to Do in Dover: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Fort Donelson Lake and the Cumberland River offer plenty of fishing opportunities for anglers of all skill levels.
  • Boating: Fort Donelson Lake and the Cumberland River are both popular destinations for boating enthusiasts.
  • Civil War Reenactments: Several reenactment events are held throughout the year at Fort Donelson National Battlefield.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dover

If you are planning a week-long trip to Dover, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Dover

Dover offers a variety of dining options, from Southern comfort food to international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nashville hot chicken: This spicy fried chicken dish is a Tennessee classic. Try it at The Old Feed Store Restaurant in Dover.
  • Country ham: This salty, cured ham is a Southern delicacy. Sample it at The Cabin Restaurant in Dover.
  • Fried catfish: This Southern-style seafood dish is a favorite in Dover. Get it at Catfish Kitchen in nearby Erin.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: This Southern classic is a staple in Dover. You can find it at most restaurants in town.
  • Tennessee whiskey: Jack Daniel’s and George Dickel distilleries are both located within an hour’s drive of Dover.

Where to Stay in Dover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dover offers several lodging options for travelers, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Dover:

  • Dover Inn Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean rooms and friendly service.
  • Paris Landing State Park Inn: This lakeside inn is located about 30 minutes from Dover and offers comfortable rooms and beautiful views.
  • Kelly’s Place: This charming bed and breakfast is located in nearby Erin and offers comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Dover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey: Stewart County is known for its delicious honey, which can be found at various farmers’ markets and stores throughout Dover.
  • Handmade crafts: The Stewart County Arts and Crafts Guild offers a variety of handmade crafts, including pottery, quilts, and paintings.
  • Tennessee whiskey: Bring home a bottle of Tennessee whiskey from the Jack Daniel’s or George Dickel distilleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Dover

Dover has a rich cultural history, influenced by Southern traditions and the area’s Civil War history. Southerners tend to be very friendly and welcoming, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you in Dover.

Best Time to Visit Dover

The best time to visit Dover is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dover

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Dover.
  • Learn about the area’s Civil War history.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the Southern classics.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Ignore safety warnings or guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dover

Dover is a safe destination for travelers. As with any outdoor activity, it is important to be aware of your surroundings and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Dover

There are no major health concerns specific to Dover. It is always a good idea to bring insect repellent and sunscreen when exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Dover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dover is easily accessible by car or bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Dover

  • Nashville International Airport (BNA): This airport is located about 90 minutes from Dover and offers flights from several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Dover

  • Greyhound Station in Clarksville, TN.
  • Jefferson Lines in Hopkinsville, KY.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: Rental cars are available at Nashville International Airport.
  • Private transportation: Taxicabs and ride-share services such as Uber and Lyft are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Dover

The best way to get around Dover is by car. The town is small, and most attractions are within a short driving distance of each other. Bicycles and walking are also good options for exploring the town.

Where to Go Next from Dover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dover is located near several other great destinations in the South. Here are some places to consider visiting after exploring Dover:

  • Nashville, TN: Located about 90 minutes from Dover, Nashville is famous for its lively music scene, delicious food, and Southern hospitality.
  • Land Between the Lakes National Recreation Area: This large park is located adjacent to Dover and offers plenty of outdoor activities for visitors.
  • Mammoth Cave National Park: This park is located about two hours from Dover in Kentucky and features the world’s longest known cave system.

Conclusion

Dover is a charming destination in Central Tennessee that offers something for every traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or delicious Southern cuisine, Dover has it all. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful town.
