Introduction to Nashville

Nicknamed “Music City,” Nashville is the capital city of Tennessee and a top destination for music lovers worldwide. With a booming food and drink scene, rich history and culture, and diverse natural attractions, Nashville allows visitors to experience the best of both worlds. This Southern gem offers an endless list of things to do and see, so here’s a comprehensive guide to help you make the most of your trip to Nashville.

List of Places to Visit in Nashville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum

As one of Nashville’s most visited attractions, the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum is a must-see for all music enthusiasts. The museum celebrates the history of country music and its legends through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive experiences.

Andrew Jackson’s Hermitage

Located just east of Nashville, the Hermitage was the plantation of seventh U.S. president Andrew Jackson. The tour offers visitors a glimpse into the life and times of Jackson, as well as unique insight into American history.

The Parthenon

The Parthenon in Nashville is a full-scale replica of the original Parthenon in Athens. Visitors can wander through the museum and admire the impressive collection of art, including a colossal statue of Athena.

Natural Attractions of Nashville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Radnor Lake State Park

For nature lovers, Radnor Lake State Park is a must-visit. This urban oasis offers hiking trails, wildlife sightings, and stunning views of the city skyline.

Cheekwood Estate & Gardens

Cheekwood Estate & Gardens is a perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of Nashville. The gardens feature a seasonal rotation of colorful blooms, and the estate offers exhibitions celebrating American and contemporary art.

The Cumberland River Pedestrian Bridge

A stroll over the Cumberland River Pedestrian Bridge offers visitors stunning views of the Nashville skyline. It’s the perfect spot for a photo-op and a peaceful walk along the riverfront.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nashville

Percy Warner Park

Percy Warner Park is the perfect destination for any hiker or biker. With over 10 miles of trails, visitors can explore the park’s diverse terrain, from steep wooded hillsides to open grassy fields.

Shelby Bottoms Greenway

Located just outside the city, Shelby Bottoms Greenway is a favorite among local bikers and hikers. The flat, paved trail winds along the Cumberland River and offers outstanding views of the city skyline.

Warner Parks Nature Center

The Warner Parks Nature Center offers visitors a chance to explore the diverse flora and fauna of Tennessee’s wilderness. The center has interactive exhibits and nature walks, making it ideal for families with children.

Best Things to Do in Nashville: Activities and Tours

Music City Hop-On Hop-Off Trolley Tour

The Music City Hop-On Hop-Off Trolley Tour is an excellent option for first-time visitors to Nashville. The tour covers over 100 points of interest and allows visitors to hop off and explore at their own pace.

Ryman Auditorium Backstage Tour

Ryman Auditorium is one of Nashville’s most iconic music venues and has hosted famous artists like Johnny Cash and Dolly Parton. The backstage tour offers visitors an in-depth look into the history of this legendary building and the artists who have performed there.

Southern Belle Riverboat Cruise

A ride on the Southern Belle Riverboat offers visitors a unique perspective of the city skyline. The paddle-wheel boat cruises down the Cumberland River while passengers take in the sights and sounds of Nashville.

Food and Drink Scene of Nashville

Must-Try Dishes

Nashville Hot Chicken

A signature dish of Nashville, Hot Chicken is a spicy fried chicken served on a piece of white bread with a side of pickles to cool the heat.

Meat and Three

A traditional Southern dish, Meat and Three features a choice of meat served with three sides, often including vegetables like collard greens or macaroni and cheese.

Biscuits and Gravy

A classic American breakfast, Biscuits and Gravy consists of a warm, flaky biscuit topped with creamy white gravy.

Local Beverages

Jack Daniel’s Whiskey

Located about an hour outside of Nashville, Jack Daniel’s Distillery offers visitors a chance to tour the facility and taste their legendary whiskey.

Goo Goo Clusters

A southern favorite, Goo Goo Clusters are a blend of peanuts, caramel, and marshmallow covered in milk chocolate.

Craft Beer

Nashville boasts a burgeoning craft beer scene, with a multitude of breweries offering unique brews for every palate.

Where to Stay in Nashville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nashville has a wide range of accommodation options suitable for all budgets.

For the budget-conscious traveler, hostels like Nashville Downtown Hostel and Music City Hostel offer comfortable, clean, and affordable accommodation in the heart of the city.

For those looking for something more luxurious, boutique hotels like the Thompson Nashville or the Noelle Hotel offer a unique and elegant experience.

What to Buy in Nashville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Goo Goo Clusters

As previously mentioned, Goo Goo Clusters are a favorite local treat and make for an excellent souvenir or gift to bring back home.

Hatch Show Print

Located inside the Country Music Hall of Fame, Hatch Show Print is America’s oldest letterpress poster shop. Visitors can purchase unique and handmade prints as a reminder of their trip to Nashville.

Music Memorabilia

Music buffs will find an abundance of stores selling vinyl records, CDs, and other music memorabilia.

Local Culture and Customs of Nashville

Nashville has a vibrant and diverse culture that blends traditional Southern hospitality with a modern, cosmopolitan feel. Travelers should be prepared to experience music, food, and culture unique to the city and its people.

Best Time to Visit Nashville

The best time to visit Nashville is during spring and fall when the temperature is mild, and Nashville’s natural beauty is at its peak. Summer can be quite hot and humid, but it’s an excellent time to catch a music festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nashville

Do:

  • Visit the City’s Music Venues
  • Try Nashville Hot Chicken
  • Take a Riverboat Cruise

Don’t:

  • Carry Large Backpacks through Crowded Areas
  • Be Disrespectful to Local Customs
  • Litter or Leave Trash in Public Areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Nashville

Nashville is considered a relatively safe city for travelers, but incidents of petty crime can occur. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take standard safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables in parked cars, especially at night.

Health in Nashville

Nashville has excellent medical facilities, and travelers should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage when visiting the city.

Getting to Nashville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nashville

The Nashville International Airport is the primary airport serving the city and is located about ten miles from downtown.

Bus Stations Near Nashville

The Nashville Greyhound bus station is located a few blocks away from downtown and offers daily connections to major cities throughout the region.

Other Options

A rental car is a great way to explore Nashville and the surrounding area. Several rental car companies serve the airport and downtown area.

How to Get Around in Nashville

Public Transportation

Nashville has a reliable public transportation system, including buses and light rail. The Music City Circuit offers free transportation around the downtown area.

Ride-Sharing and Taxis

Ride-sharing and taxis are a popular and convenient way to travel around the city, especially for those visiting for the first time.

Biking and Walking

Nashville is a great city for walking and biking, with several bike paths and pedestrian-friendly areas.

Where to Go Next from Nashville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Franklin

Located about twenty miles south of Nashville, Franklin is a charming historic town with excellent dining and shopping options.

Mammoth Cave National Park

Located about two hours from Nashville, Mammoth Cave National Park offers visitors a chance to explore one of the world’s most extensive cave systems.

The Jack Daniel’s Distillery

Located about an hour south of Nashville, the Jack Daniel’s Distillery offers visitors a chance to tour the facility and taste their legendary whiskey.

Conclusion

Nashville is a city with something for everyone. From the vibrant music and cultural scene to the stunning natural attractions and mouth-watering cuisine, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in Music City.
