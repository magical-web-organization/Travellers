If you’re looking for a peaceful, charming vacation spot in Central Tennessee, Hendersonville is the perfect destination. Surrounded by picturesque landscapes and rich cultural heritage, this small city in Sumner County offers a variety of options for a delightful and unforgettable experience.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Hendersonville, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs to keep in mind. So, pack your bags, and let’s dive into the beauty of Hendersonville.

Introduction to Hendersonville

Located in Central Tennessee, Hendersonville is a small city with a population of around 58,000 people. It’s named after William Henderson, its first postmaster, and has a rich history dating back to the American Revolutionary War.

The city boasts a charming downtown area with shops, restaurants, and bars, along with numerous outdoor activities and historic landmarks that showcase the city’s vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Hendersonville

Hendersonville is full of attractions that should be on your bucket list while visiting. Check out this list of must-see spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hendersonville is a historic city, with many attractions that showcase its rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-visit historical landmarks and museums:

  • Monthaven Arts and Cultural Center: Located in the renovated Monthaven Mansion, this museum features a variety of art exhibits, cultural events, and educational programs.

  • Rock Castle State Historic Site: This restored mansion highlights the history of Tennessee, offering tours and educational programs throughout the year.

  • Trinity Music City: Owned by the gospel singer Randy Travis, this music venue offers a unique experience to fans of all ages by hosting musical shows with gospel, bluegrass, and country artists.

Natural Attractions of Hendersonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you enjoy exploring nature, Hendersonville has plenty to offer with its scenic views, lakes, and parks. Make sure you don’t miss:

  • Bledsoe Creek State Park: A land of nature lovers, this park features 57 campsites, hiking trails, fishing, and boating.

  • Old Hickory Lake: A 22,500-acre lake that borders many communities within the Nashville metropolitan area. It offers boating, fishing, and breathtaking views of the waterfront.

  • Sanders Ferry Park: This park has waterfront views and offers visitors a playground, pavilions, and a popular fishing spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hendersonville

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Hendersonville has various hiking and biking trails that let you explore the beauty of Mother Nature. Here are some worth checking out:

  • Drakes Creek Park: A popular spot for walking and hiking in Hendersonville, it features a 1.5-mile nature trail with scenic views.

  • Stones River Greenway: A paved trail that features picturesque views, connecting Stones River National Battlefield to Percy Priest.

Best Things to Do in Hendersonville: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are loads of activities to indulge in Hendersonville, and here are some favorites:

  • Dixie Caviar Boat Tours and Charters: Enjoy a private tour of Old Hickory Lake and Dixie Caviar’s upscale atmosphere.

  • Hendersonville Strike and Spare Family Fun Center: For indoor family fun, this center offers bowling, arcade games, laser tag, and more.

  • Live Music Nights: Explore the town’s music scene at different venues that feature live music on different nights of the week.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hendersonville

If you’re planning to stay in Hendersonville for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you maximize your stay.

Day 1: Arrive in Hendersonville, check in at your hotel, and explore the downtown area.

Day 2: Visit Monthaven Arts and Cultural Center, followed by a sunset view at Old Hickory Lake.

Day 3: Go hiking or biking on the Drakes Creek trail, and visit the Hendersonville Strike and Spare Family Fun Center.

Day 4: Take a boat tour of Old Hickory Lake and dine at Dixie Caviar.

Day 5: Go shopping at the Streets of Indian Lake, and visit the Rock Castle State Historic Site.

Day 6: Spend a day exploring natural parks like Bledsoe Creek State Park, and Sanders Ferry Park.

Day 7: Visit Trinity Music City and watch a gospel or bluegrass musical show.

Food and Drink Scene of Hendersonville

Hendersonville’s dining scene caters to all tastes and budgets, featuring an extensive selection of local cuisine with delightful flavors and contemporary atmosphere.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Chicken Sandwiches: A local delicacy of the Nashville region, featuring spicy fried chicken and a soft bread bun with toppings.

  • Barbecue: Hendersonville’s barbecue scene features Southern-style BBQ, cooked low and slow over hickory wood.

  • Hot Fudge Cake: A classic dessert dish, featuring a warm chocolate cake topped with a generous scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Hatch Show Print: A local craft brewery that offers a variety of beer flavors to indulge.

  • Jack Daniel’s Distillery: Take a tour at this iconic distillery and taste-test some of their finest whiskeys.

Where to Stay in Hendersonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hendersonville offers different types of accommodations ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Hyatt Place Nashville/Hendersonville: A modern hotel with luxury amenities, perfect for family and business travelers.

  • Comfort Inn: A comfortable and affordable option for families on a budget.

  • Hendersonville Guesthouse: A unique experience offering vacation homes and cabanas for those looking for a different accommodation experience.

What to Buy in Hendersonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some souvenirs to take back home, here are some local products that perfectly represent the city’s culture:

  • Pottery: Handmade stoneware inspired by the local flora, fauna, and music scene.

  • Moon Pies: A well-known southern snack, this chocolate-covered marshmallow and graham cracker treats make for the perfect snack.

Local Culture and Customs of Hendersonville

Hendersonville is a Southern city with a unique charm, and its locals exhibit warm hospitality with a good dose of hospitality. It may be useful to remember these customs while here:

  • Acknowledgment: Polite acknowledgement of people passing by is commonly observed in local culture.

  • Food Etiquette: Southern dining culture is based on warmth and hospitality, and it is customary to offer guests second servings and meals.

Best Time to Visit Hendersonville

The best time to visit Hendersonville is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hendersonville

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience in Hendersonville, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and partake in the local festivals and events.

  • Carry a light jacket to counter unexpected fluctuations in temperature.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.

  • Don’t disrespect local culture and customs.

  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen and insect repellent while engaging outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Hendersonville is generally considered a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Hendersonville

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded places.

  • Keep your belongings in a secure place.

Health in Hendersonville

  • Wear comfortable and appropriate clothing for outdoor activities to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

  • Carry bug spray to protect yourself from insects while exploring outdoor activities.

Getting to Hendersonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are various ways to get to Hendersonville, here are some suggestions:

Airports Near Hendersonville

  • Nashville International Airport (BNA): 16 miles south of Hendersonville.

Bus Stations Near Hendersonville

  • Greyhound Bus station: Nashville, 19 miles south of Hendersonville.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Consider renting a car to explore Hendersonville and its surroundings with ease.

  • Taxis: Book a taxi to take you to Hendersonville from the airport.

How to Get Around in Hendersonville

Renting a car is recommended for exploring Hendersonville and its surrounding areas- however, if you prefer public transportation, the Nashville MTA bus system has regular services that connect Hendersonville to other parts of Nashville.

Where to Go Next from Hendersonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hendersonville is surrounded by many cities and towns worth exploring, such as:

  • Nashville: Boasting plenty of museums, music venues, and southern restaurants, Nashville is a perfect destination for a quick getaway.

  • Franklin: Known for its preserved Civil War-era buildings, charming downtown area, and quirky shops, this small city is a must-visit.

Conclusion

Hendersonville has a lot to offer and is packed with a variety of opportunities to discover. From history and culture to outdoor adventures, cuisine, and shopping, this destination is perfect for a stress-free and memorable vacation experience.
