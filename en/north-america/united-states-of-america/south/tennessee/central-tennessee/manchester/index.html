Introduction to Manchester

Located in central Tennessee, Manchester boasts stunning natural landscapes, vibrant history, and exciting activities for locals and visitors alike. Founded in 1836, the city played a key role in the Civil War and was home to famous musician Bon Iver. However, it’s most well-known for hosting the annual Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival. In this travel guide, we’ll explore all that Manchester has to offer, including must-see attractions, food and drink experiences, and helpful tips for getting around.

List of Places to Visit in Manchester

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Manchester is steeped in history and culture, with numerous fascinating museums and historical sites to explore. Here are a few must-see destinations to check out:

  • The Old Stone Fort Museum: This ancient stone structure was used by Native Americans for shelter in prehistoric times and served as a military fortification during the Civil War.

  • The Coffee County Museum: Learn about local history at this museum, which features exhibits on the city’s agricultural past and civil war artifacts.

  • The Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival: Each June, Manchester comes to life with this world-famous festival, which draws thousands of fans from around the globe.

Natural Attractions of Manchester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Manchester is home to some of the most stunning natural scenery in Tennessee. Get out and explore the great outdoors with these adventures:

  • Tims Ford State Park: This park offers scenic views of the Tims Ford Lake, hiking trails, and fishing opportunities.

  • Cumberland Caverns: This underground cave system provides visitors with a glimpse into Tennessee’s unique geology and offers guided tours.

  • Rutledge Falls: This breathtaking waterfall located in Tullahoma is a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manchester

If you love hiking or biking, Manchester offers several stunning trails:

  • Short Springs State Natural Area: This area has over 12 miles of hiking trails that wind through waterfalls, forests, and stunning rock formations.

  • Greenway Trail: This three-mile paved trail takes visitors along the Duck River and through the heart of Manchester.

  • Pelham Mountain Trail: This 3.5-mile strenuous hike provides stunning views of the surrounding mountains and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Manchester: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Manchester offers plenty of other exciting activities and tours:

  • Beechcraft Heritage Museum: This fascinating museum showcases the history of Beechcraft airplanes and offers guided tours.

  • Oak Barrel Tours: Take a tour of local craft breweries and distilleries to learn about the craft beer and spirits scene in Manchester.

  • Mountain Goat Market: Visit this local market to sample fresh produce and artisanal goods from Tennessee farmers.

Food and Drink Scene of Manchester

### Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Manchester is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Country Ham: Dry-cured ham cooked and smoked to perfection, served with biscuits and gravy.

  • Hot Chicken Sandwich: Fried chicken coated with a spicy sauce that will leave your palate feeling pleasantly satisfied.

  • Southern Fried Catfish: Served with coleslaw and hush puppies, this dish is a staple of southern cuisine.

### Local Beverages

Tennessee is known for its craft beers, distilled spirits, and wines, and Manchester doesn’t disappoint. Here are some of the local beverages to try:

  • Jack Daniel’s: Tennessee’s own world-famous whiskey is distilled in Lynchburg and is a must-try.

  • Turtle Anarchy Brewery: Sample a selection of locally-made craft beers at this popular brewery.

  • Beans Creek Winery: Stop by this winery to sample award-winning selections of Tennessee wine.

Where to Stay in Manchester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manchester has plenty of options for accommodations to fit any budget. Here are a few popular choices:

  • Holiday Inn Express: This well-known chain offers comfortable accommodations with plenty of amenities.

  • The Ambassador Inn and Luxury Suites: This locally-owned hotel features luxurious suites with views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Top of the Bight: This cozy bed and breakfast is perfect for travelers who are looking for a more intimate experience.

What to Buy in Manchester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Complete your Manchester experience by taking home some locally made souvenirs and products. Here are some popular items to look out for:

  • Jack Daniel’s Whiskey: Purchase a bottle of this world-famous whiskey to take home as a souvenir.

  • Bush Brothers Beans: A local staple ever since the company’s founding in 1908, the bush beans are a must-have.

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Visit the local pottery shops to purchase unique, handmade pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Manchester

Manchester locals are known for their welcoming hospitality. It is common courtesy to always offer a smile and polite greeting to others. Southern manners, such as holding doors for others and being prompt, are also valued in the region.

Best Time to Visit Manchester

The best time to visit Manchester is during spring, summer, and fall. The weather is mild, and outdoor activities are open and in full swing. If you’re visiting for Bonnaroo, be sure to make hotel reservations months in advance due to high demand.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manchester

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural scenery and outdoor activities in the area.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer scene.
  • Embrace Southern hospitality and politeness.

Don’t:

  • Discard litter in public spaces.
  • Be disrespectful of locals, be open and accepting of any cultural differences you might encounter.
  • Forget to respect outdoor spaces and wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manchester

Manchester is generally a safe city, but it’s always important to exercise caution in unfamiliar areas. Here are a few tips to stay safe in Manchester:

  • Always lock your car and hotel room doors.
  • Avoid walking through less-populated areas, particularly at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and attentive to people around you

Health in Manchester

Manchester has plenty of medical facilities available in case of an emergency. The Manchester-Coffee County hospital is one of the most recommended. Remember to stay hydrated and protected from the sun if you’re spending time outdoors.

Getting to Manchester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manchester can be conveniently accessed through multiple transportation options:

  • Airports: The closest international airports are Nashville International Airport (BNA) and Chattanooga Metropolitan Airport (CHA), both approximately one hour’s drive away from Manchester.
  • Bus Stations: Greyhound/Megabus provides scheduled bus services across the US, including pickup/drop off points in Manchester.
  • Other Options: Car rentals are available at both Nashville International and Chattanooga Metropolitan airports.

How to Get Around Manchester

Manchester is fairly easy to navigate, getting around is possible by car with rental or rideshare apps. Manchester is relatively small, making walking, and cycling popular options. Depending on your location or destination, taking public transportation such as the Coffee County Public Transit system is available.

Where to Go Next from Manchester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time and are looking for something to do outside of Manchester, consider visiting any of these nearby destinations:

  • Beechcraft Corporation Legacy Museum in Tullahoma
  • Great Stage Park, home of Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival.
  • Old Stone Fort State Archaeological Park

Conclusion

Manchester, Tennessee has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or simply enjoying Southern hospitality, Manchester is the perfect destination. With various activities, delicious food and drinks, welcoming locals, and beautiful natural scenery, Manchester is a charming, must-visit city in Tennessee. So pack your bags, take this comprehensive guide, and come explore all that Manchester has to offer!
