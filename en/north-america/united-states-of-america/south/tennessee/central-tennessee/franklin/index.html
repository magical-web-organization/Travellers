Introduction to Franklin

Nestled in Central Tennessee, Franklin is the perfect blend of historic charm and modern amenities. Once the site of brutal Civil War battles, this small town has overcome its tumultuous past and is now a vibrant and culturally-rich destination for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carnton. This historic plantation was used as a field hospital during the Civil War and is now a museum that showcases the town’s past.
  • Lotz House. This Civil War-era house is open for tours and offers a glimpse into what life was like during the war.
  • Franklin Theatre. This beautifully restored 1930s movie theater now hosts live performances and events.
  • Carter House. Another Civil War-era mansion that was turned into a field hospital during the war, now visitors can explore the house and learn about the harrowing events that took place here.

Natural Attractions of Franklin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Natchez Trace Parkway. This scenic drive takes visitors through beautiful forests and past historic sites.
  • Pinkerton Park. Perfect for families, this park features walking trails, a playground, and a creek for wading.
  • Winstead Hill Park. This park was the site of a significant Civil War battle and now offers visitors picturesque views of the town.
  • Harpeth River State Park. Hiking, fishing, and canoeing opportunities abound in this beautiful state park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin

  • Cool Springs Multi-Use Trail. This paved trail is perfect for bikers and walkers alike, and runs for six miles between Brentwood and Franklin.
  • Natchez Trace Parkway. The parkway also features several hiking trails, including the scenic Garrison Creek Trail and the adventurous Hidden Lake Trail.
  • Timberland Park. This park offers several stunning hiking trails that wind through the woods and offer beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Franklin: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Downtown Franklin. With its quaint shops, restaurants, and historic architecture, downtown Franklin is a must-see.
  • Take a Ghost Tour. Franklin is said to be one of the most haunted towns in America, making it the perfect place to take a spooky ghost tour.
  • Visit Leiper’s Fork. This charming town is just a short drive from Franklin and is known for its scenic beauty and eclectic artist community.
  • Experience the Franklin Wine Festival. Every year, this festival brings together wine lovers from around the country to sample the best vintages and enjoy live music and food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin

Day Activity
1 Explore Downtown Franklin, including the Franklin Theatre
2 Visit the Carter House and Carnton for a full day of Civil War history
3 Take a hike in Harpeth River State Park and explore the Pinkerton Park
4 Biking on the Cool Springs Multi-Use Trail, followed by a ghost tour of Franklin
5 Spend the day in Leiper’s Fork, hiking and shopping
6 Sample wine at the Franklin Wine Festival
7 Enjoy a picnic in Winstead Hill Park and take a scenic drive along Natchez Trace Parkway

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Chicken. A spicier take on traditional fried chicken, hot chicken is a must-try dish in Franklin.
  • Biscuits and Gravy. A southern classic, biscuits and gravy is a filling and delicious breakfast option.
  • Pimento Cheese. A cheesy spread that’s perfect for crackers or sandwiches, pimento cheese is a Franklin specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Jack Daniel’s Whiskey. This famous Tennessee whiskey is made just a short drive from Franklin and is a must-try for whiskey lovers.
  • Arrington Vineyards Wine. This local vineyard produces award-winning wines that are sure to please any palate.

Where to Stay in Franklin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Harpeth Hotel. This luxurious hotel is located in downtown Franklin and offers stunning views of the town and the Harpeth River.
  • Drury Plaza Hotel. Perfect for families, this hotel features an indoor pool and hot tub, free breakfast, and a convenient location.
  • The Franklin Guest House. This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of downtown Franklin and offers comfortable and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Franklin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery. Franklin is home to several talented potters who create unique and beautiful ceramic pieces.
  • Handmade Soap. Several local shops make their own all-natural soap, using ingredients like honey, lavender, and peppermint.
  • Tennessee Whiskey. Jack Daniel’s isn’t the only Tennessee whiskey out there - be sure to sample some of the lesser-known varieties as well.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin

Franklin is a welcoming and friendly town, where Southern hospitality reigns supreme. Visitors should be prepared to say “y’all” and shake hands with strangers - socializing is a big part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Franklin

The best time to visit Franklin is during the fall, from late September to early November, when the trees are ablaze with color and the weather is mild. Spring is also a good time to visit, with blooming flowers and warm temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin

Do:

  • Do try the hot chicken - it’s a Franklin specialty.
  • Do take a hike in one of the local parks - the views are stunning.
  • Do make time to explore downtown Franklin - it’s a treasure trove of shops and restaurants.

Don’t:

  • Don’t skip out on the museums and historical sites - there’s so much history to explore here.
  • Don’t forget to take a ghost tour - Franklin is said to be one of the most haunted towns in America.
  • Don’t leave without sampling some Tennessee whiskey.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin

Overall, Franklin is a safe place to visit. However, as with any town, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions when walking around at night.

Health in Franklin

Franklin has several excellent hospitals and medical facilities, including Williamson Medical Center and Saint Thomas Medical Partners. Visitors should also make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Franklin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Franklin

  • Nashville International Airport. Located just 20 miles north of Franklin, this airport is the most convenient for visitors arriving by air.

Bus Stations Near Franklin

  • Greyhound Bus Lines. There is a Greyhound station located in Nashville, just a short drive from Franklin.

Other Options

  • Rental Car. Renting a car is one of the easiest ways to get around Franklin and the surrounding area.
  • Taxi or Ride Share. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in Franklin.

How to Get Around in Franklin

The best way to get around Franklin is by car - either your own rental car or a taxi. Biking is also a popular option, with several bike-friendly trails and routes throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Franklin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nashville. Just a short drive north of Franklin, Music City USA offers visitors plenty of live music, great food, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Mammoth Cave National Park. A two-hour drive from Franklin, Mammoth Cave is the world’s longest known cave system and offers visitors the chance to explore its beautiful rock formations and underground rivers.
  • Chattanooga. Two hours southeast of Franklin, Chattanooga offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing.

Conclusion

Franklin is a beautiful and historic town that is sure to charm visitors with its blend of old and new. With its picturesque landscapes, rich culture, and delicious food, it’s no wonder that Franklin is becoming a popular destination for travelers from around the world.
