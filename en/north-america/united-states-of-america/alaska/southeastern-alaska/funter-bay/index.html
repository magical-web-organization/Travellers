Introduction to Funter Bay

Funter Bay is a small community located in Southeastern Alaska, part of the larger state of Alaska in the United States of America. The area is known for its stunning natural beauty, rugged coastal landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path travel destination, Funter Bay should be at the top of your list.

List of Places to Visit in Funter Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Funter Bay Historical Society Museum is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in local culture and history. The museum has displays on the Tlingit people, early industry in Funter Bay, and the way of life for early settlers in the area.

Natural Attractions of Funter Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Funter Bay is home to some of the most beautiful scenery in Alaska. Take a hike through the Tongass National Forest to explore the lush, green landscape, or take a kayak tour of the Kowee Channel to see the area from a different perspective.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Funter Bay

Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Funter Bay include the Seavy Loop Trail, the Funter Bay Point Trail, and the Kowee Island Trail. Each of these routes offers breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness, as well as the opportunity to spot local wildlife like bald eagles, black bears, and harbor seals.

Best Things to Do in Funter Bay: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and kayaking, there are a number of other activities and tours available in Funter Bay. Take a fishing charter out into the bay to try and catch some of the area’s famous halibut or salmon, or sign up for a guided wildlife photography tour to capture the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Funter Bay

Day 1: Arrive in Funter Bay and check-in to your hotel or guesthouse. Spend the day exploring the Funter Bay Historical Society Museum and learning about the area’s rich cultural heritage.

Day 2: Take a kayak tour of the Kowee Channel to see the area from a different perspective. Keep an eye out for local wildlife like harbor seals and sea otters.

Day 3: Go on a fishing charter to try and catch some of the area’s famous halibut or salmon. If you’re lucky, you might even spot some whales breaching in the distance.

Day 4: Take a hike through the Tongass National Forest to explore the lush, green landscape. Make sure to bring your camera to capture the stunning views.

Day 5: Sign up for a guided wildlife photography tour to capture the natural beauty of the region. From bald eagles to black bears, there’s plenty of local wildlife to photograph.

Day 6: Spend the day exploring the local shops and markets, picking up souvenirs and local products to take home with you.

Day 7: Depart Funter Bay and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Funter Bay

Must-Try Dishes

When visiting Funter Bay, make sure to try some of the local seafood. Fresh halibut and salmon are both popular choices, as well as locally harvested crab and clams.

Local Beverages

Alaska is known for its craft beer scene, and Funter Bay is no exception. Make sure to try some of the local brews from the Forbidden Peak Brewery, or sample some of the locally produced mead from Albino Moose Meadery.

Where to Stay in Funter Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a number of accommodation options available in Funter Bay, ranging from hotels and guesthouses to camping sites and RV parks. Some of the most popular options include the Funter Bay Lodge, the Taku Harbor Lodge, and the Raven’s Roost Bed and Breakfast.

What to Buy in Funter Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Funter Bay, make sure to pick up some locally made souvenirs and products to take home with you. Some popular choices include locally harvested honey, handcrafted jewelry made from local materials, and handmade knitwear.

Local Culture and Customs of Funter Bay

The Tlingit people have lived in the Funter Bay area for thousands of years, and their culture and traditions continue to play an important role in the community today. Visitors should take the time to learn about the Tlingit people and their way of life, as well as the history of the area’s early settlers and the industries that have shaped the region over the years.

Best Time to Visit Funter Bay

The best time to visit Funter Bay is between May and September, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are at their best. Visitors should be prepared for rain, however, as the area can be quite wet during this time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Funter Bay

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Tlingit people.
  • Take the time to explore the natural beauty of the region.
  • Try the local seafood and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter behind when visiting the area’s outdoor attractions.
  • Disrupt local wildlife or their habitats.
  • Ignore the area’s weather warnings and advice from locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Funter Bay

Funter Bay is a generally safe destination, but visitors should still be cautious when participating in outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking. Always stay on designated trails and follow any posted safety information.

Health in Funter Bay

There are no major health concerns in Funter Bay, but visitors should still take precautions to stay healthy and avoid any potential health risks. Make sure to bring any necessary medications with you, and be prepared for the area’s changeable weather.

Getting to Funter Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Funter Bay

The nearest major airport to Funter Bay is the Juneau International Airport, which is located about 25 miles to the northwest.

Bus Stations Near Funter Bay

There are no major bus stations near Funter Bay, as the area is primarily served by small boat and plane transportation.

Other Options

Visitors to Funter Bay can also arrive by boat or kayak, or by hiking or biking on the Tongass National Forest trails.

How to Get Around in Funter Bay

The best way to get around in Funter Bay is by foot or bike. Many of the area’s attractions and outdoor activities are located within walking or biking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Funter Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations and excursions from Funter Bay include the Mendenhall Glacier Visitor Center, the Alaska State Capitol building in Juneau, and the Glacier Bay National Park and Preserve.

Conclusion

Funter Bay is a truly unique travel destination, offering stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a laid-back atmosphere that’s perfect for getting away from it all. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just soaking up the local atmosphere, Funter Bay is sure to leave a lasting impression.
