Introduction to Juneau

Juneau, the capital city of Alaska, is situated in Southeastern Alaska and is surrounded by the scenic Tongass National Forest. It is a bustling city known for its breathtaking natural beauty, rich local culture, and exciting adventure activities. This comprehensive travel guide will give you all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Juneau.

List of Places to Visit in Juneau

Here is a list of the must-visit attractions in Juneau:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alaska State Capitol Building
  • Juneau-Douglas City Museum
  • Last Chance Mining Museum
  • Shrine of St. Therese

Natural Attractions of Juneau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mendenhall Glacier
  • Mount Roberts Tramway
  • Tracy Arm Fjord
  • Whale Watching Tour

Hiking/Biking Routes of Juneau

Juneau boasts some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in the world. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Mount Juneau Trail
  • Perseverance Trail
  • Treadwell Ditch Trail
  • Eaglecrest Ski Area

Best Things to Do in Juneau: Activities and Tours

Juneau offers plenty of exciting activities and tours that cater to every interest. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing
  • Helicopter Tours
  • Salmon Fishing
  • Glacier Trekking

Week-Long Itinerary for Juneau

Here is an itinerary for a week-long trip to Juneau:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Downtown Juneau and visit Alaska State Capitol Building
Day 2 Visit Mendenhall Glacier and hike on Mount Juneau Trail
Day 3 Take a whale watching tour and visit Juneau-Douglas City Museum
Day 4 Trek to Taku Glacier and take a helicopter tour
Day 5 Explore the scenic Tracy Arm Fjord
Day 6 Enjoy salmon fishing and visit Last Chance Mining Museum
Day 7 take a leisurely stroll on Perseverance Trail and visit Shrine of St. Therese

Local Culture and Customs of Juneau

Juneau is known for its rich local culture and customs, deeply rooted in native Alaskan heritage. Here are some things to keep in mind about the local customs:

  • Respect the wildlife and environment
  • Follow the guidelines of the local tribal culture
  • Tipping is customary at restaurants and bars

Best Time to Visit Juneau

The best time to visit Juneau is during the summer months between May and September, when the weather is favorable and days are long. However, Juneau is a year-round destination that offers unique experiences in every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Juneau

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Juneau:

Do:

  • Explore the scenic surroundings
  • Respect the local culture and wildlife
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Ignore safety guidelines while on an adventure tour

Safety and Health

Juneau is generally a safe city for visitors. However, it is important to take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Safety in Juneau

  • Avoid venturing into secluded or unlit areas at night
  • Keep track of your belongings while in crowded areas
  • Approach adventure tours with caution

Health in Juneau

  • Carry necessary medication and first-aid supplies
  • Drink only bottled or filtered water
  • Make sure to carry insect repellent while outdoors

How to Get Around in Juneau

Juneau is a walkable city, and many attractions are within walking distance. However, for travel outside the city, you can rent a car, take a public bus, or hire a taxi.

Conclusion

Juneau is a unique travel destination that offers something for everyone. With its natural beauty, rich culture, and exciting adventure activities, Juneau is a must-visit destination on your Alaska travel itinerary. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Juneau and make it a memorable experience.
