Introduction to Gustavus

Gustavus is a small town located in Alaska’s southeastern region, situated on the northern edge of the Inside Passage. This unincorporated community is home to a population of under 500 people, making it a perfect destination for travelers seeking a quiet getaway. Gustavus is surrounded by endless wilderness that is just waiting to be explored, making it a prime destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

Local Culture and Customs of Gustavus

Gustavus is home to the Tlingit natives, who have a rich and intriguing culture. Tlingit art and craftwork are widely available throughout the town, showcasing their traditional designs and motifs.

Best Time to Visit Gustavus

The best time to visit Gustavus is during the summer months, from May to September. This period offers the best weather and optimal conditions for outdoor activities, such as hiking, kayaking, and canoeing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gustavus

Gustavus is a relatively safe destination with low crime and incident rates. However, precautions should be taken while hiking, kayaking or canoeing, especially if you are unfamiliar with the territory.

Health in Gustavus

Visitors traveling to Gustavus should receive the necessary vaccinations, such as tetanus and measles, before their trip. The local medical facilities in Gustavus are limited, so taking preventative measures against injuries and illnesses is highly recommended.
