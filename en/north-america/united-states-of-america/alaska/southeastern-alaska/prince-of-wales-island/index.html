Introduction to Prince of Wales Island

Prince of Wales Island is a picturesque island in Southeast Alaska, surrounded by stunning natural beauty. With an area of over 2,600 square miles, it is the fourth-largest island in the United States. Known for its pristine forests, abundant wildlife, and rich history, Prince of Wales Island is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and history buffs. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous vacation, there is something for everyone on Prince of Wales Island.

List of Places to Visit in Prince of Wales Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Totem Bight State Historical Park: This park features one of the largest collections of totem poles in the world, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Tlingit and Haida people.
  • Craig Museum: Located in the town of Craig, this museum offers a glimpse into the history of Prince of Wales Island, with exhibits on the island’s logging, fishing, and mining industries.
  • Kasaan Cultural Tour: Take a guided tour of the historic village of Kasaan, where you can see totem poles, a clan house, and a longhouse, and learn about the Tlingit culture and history.

Natural Attractions of Prince of Wales Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Capitan Cave: Explore the largest known cave in Alaska, featuring an underground river and stunning rock formations.
  • Thorne Bay Ranger District: This area offers numerous hiking trails, fishing spots, and campgrounds, as well as beautiful scenery and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Whale Pass: This small community is known for its fishing, hunting, kayaking, and wildlife watching, including sightings of whales, bears, and eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prince of Wales Island

Prince of Wales Island offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for all levels of experience. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Beaver Falls Trail: This easy, 1.5-mile trail leads to a beautiful waterfall and is accessible for all skill levels.
  • Karta River Trail: This 6-mile trail winds through scenic forests and offers the chance to see wildlife such as bears, wolves, and eagles.
  • Windy Bay Trail: This 4.5-mile trail features stunning views of the bay and the surrounding forest, and is great for intermediate hikers.

Best Things to Do in Prince of Wales Island: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Prince of Wales Island is a renowned fishing destination, with abundant opportunities to catch salmon, halibut, and other species in its numerous rivers, lakes, and ocean waters.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore the island’s beautiful bays and waterways by kayak or canoe, and see wildlife such as sea otters, seals, and whales up close.
  • Bear Viewing: Take a guided tour to see bears in their natural habitat, with opportunities to observe them fishing for salmon or feeding on berries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prince of Wales Island

Day 1: Arrive on Prince of Wales Island Day 2-3: Explore the natural attractions of Thorne Bay Ranger District and El Capitan Cave Day 4: Visit the Craig Museum and take a Kasaan Cultural Tour Day 5-6: Hike the island’s scenic trails or go fishing and kayaking Day 7: Depart from Prince of Wales Island

Food and Drink Scene of Prince of Wales Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Halibut: This delicious fish is a local specialty, and can be prepared in a variety of ways, from grilled to smoked.
  • Spruce Tip Jelly: Made from the new growth of Sitka spruce trees, this jelly has a unique flavor and is a favorite among locals.
  • Salmon Candy: This sweet and savory salmon jerky is a popular snack on the island.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan Craft Beer: Prince of Wales Island is home to several craft breweries, offering a variety of unique and flavorful beers.
  • Birch Syrup: Made from the sap of birch trees, this syrup has a rich, nutty flavor and is a popular ingredient in cocktails and desserts.

Where to Stay in Prince of Wales Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pioneer Inn: Located in the town of Craig, this historic hotel offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local seafood and other dishes.
  • My Alaska Hostel: This hostel in Klawock offers budget-friendly accommodations for travelers, with shared kitchen and living spaces.
  • Log Cabin Resort: This rustic resort in Thorne Bay features cozy cabins and a restaurant overlooking the bay.

What to Buy in Prince of Wales Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Totem Poles: Prince of Wales Island is known for its impressive totem poles, and visitors can purchase miniature versions as souvenirs.
  • Birch Products: Birch bark baskets, bowls, and other handmade items are a popular souvenir and a great way to support local artisans.
  • Smoked Salmon: Bring home a taste of the island with locally smoked salmon, available from numerous markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Prince of Wales Island

The Tlingit and Haida cultures have a strong presence on Prince of Wales Island, and visitors should be respectful of their traditions and customs. One important practice is the “potlatch,” a ceremonial feast and gift-giving event that is an essential part of their culture.

Best Time to Visit Prince of Wales Island

The best time to visit Prince of Wales Island is in the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild and the natural attractions are at their best. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain and cool temperatures, even in the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prince of Wales Island

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Practice responsible fishing and wildlife viewing.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter behind.
  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.
  • Ignore safety guidelines for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prince of Wales Island

Visitors to Prince of Wales Island should be aware of the potential for wildlife encounters, including bears and moose. It is important to follow safety guidelines for hiking and camping, and to be prepared with bear spray and bear-resistant food storage containers.

Health in Prince of Wales Island

Travelers should be aware of the risk of tick-borne illnesses and take precautions such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent. It is also important to bring any necessary medication or medical supplies, as healthcare facilities on the island are limited.

Getting to Prince of Wales Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prince of Wales Island

The island is served by two airports: Klawock Airport and Craig Seaplane Base. Both offer flights from Juneau and other Alaskan cities.

Bus Stations Near Prince of Wales Island

There are no bus stations on Prince of Wales Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the island by ferry from nearby Ketchikan, or by private boat or seaplane.

How to Get Around in Prince of Wales Island

Renting a car or ATV is the easiest way to get around the island, but visitors can also use the public bus system or book guided tours for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Prince of Wales Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ketchikan: This charming waterfront town is known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and outdoor activities, and is just a short ferry ride from Prince of Wales Island.
  • Misty Fjords National Monument: This majestic wilderness area is located near Ketchikan and features towering cliffs, waterfalls, and pristine lakes.
  • Glacier Bay National Park: This stunning national park is accessible by boat from Gustavus, offering the chance to see glaciers, whales, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

Conclusion

Prince of Wales Island is a hidden gem of Southeast Alaska, offering breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a wide range of outdoor activities and experiences. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous vacation, this scenic destination has something to offer. Plan your trip today and discover the best of Prince of Wales Island!
