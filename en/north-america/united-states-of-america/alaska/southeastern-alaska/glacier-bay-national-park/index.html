Introduction to Glacier Bay National Park

Glacier Bay National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Southeast Alaska on the west coast of the state’s panhandle. Encompassing over 3.3 million acres, the park is home to numerous glaciers, fjords, and wildlife species. It is considered one of the last untouched wilderness areas in the world, and visitors can experience breathtaking views, pristine wilderness, and unique cultural and historical attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Glacier Bay National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Huna Tribal House: A traditional Tlingit clan house located in Bartlett Cove, which serves as a cultural center for the Huna Tlingit people who have lived in Glacier Bay for thousands of years.
  • Glacier Bay National Park Visitor Center: A museum and interpretive center about the park’s natural and cultural history, as well as the area’s Tlingit history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Glacier Bay National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Margerie Glacier: A stunning glacier located in the Tarr Inlet, which is part of the larger Glacier Bay.
  • Bartlett Cove: A popular destination for kayaking, fishing, and hiking. Visitors can also take whale-watching tours from here.
  • Gustavus: A small town in the park, known for its scenic beauty and wildlife, including bears, moose, and wolves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glacier Bay National Park

  • Forest Loop Trail: A 1.5-mile loop trail that takes hikers through the lush forest of Bartlett Cove.
  • Bartlett Lake Trail: A 7-mile round-trip hike that takes hikers to a serene and beautiful alpine lake.

Best Things to Do in Glacier Bay National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the park’s stunning waterways and glaciers.
  • Whale watching: Whale watching tours from Bartlett Cove offer visitors the chance to see humpback whales, killer whales, and other marine life.
  • Helicopter Tours: Helicopter tours offer stunning views of the park’s glaciers and mountains.
  • Fishing: Visitors can fish for salmon and halibut in the park’s waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Glacier Bay National Park

  • Day 1-2: Explore Bartlett Cove, take a whale watching tour, and visit the Huna Tribal House.
  • Day 3-4: Hike the Margerie Glacier trail and explore the Gustavus area.
  • Day 5-6: Take a kayak tour and helicopter tour of the park.
  • Day 7: Relax and take in the park’s stunning scenery before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Glacier Bay National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alaskan King Crab: A local delicacy, served fresh in many restaurants and seafood markets in the area.
  • Halibut: Another local favorite, halibut is served in many restaurants and can be caught during fishing tours.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan beer: Local breweries like Alaskan Brewing Company offer a range of craft beers.
  • Glacier Bay National Park Tea: A tea blend made with local plants, which can be purchased at the Glacier Bay National Park Visitor Center.

Where to Stay in Glacier Bay National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Glacier Bay Country Inn: A rustic bed and breakfast located in the heart of Gustavus.
  • Glacier Bay Lodge: A hotel located near Bartlett Cove, offering easy access to many of the park’s attractions.

What to Buy in Glacier Bay National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native Art: Traditional Tlingit art and crafts can be found at the Huna Tribal House and the Glacier Bay National Park Visitor Center.
  • Glacier Bay Seal Oil: A traditional Tlingit food, which can be purchased at the Glacier Bay National Park Visitor Center.

Local Culture and Customs of Glacier Bay National Park

  • The Tlingit people have lived in the area for thousands of years and have a rich culture and history. Visitors should respect and learn about their culture while in the park.

Best Time to Visit Glacier Bay National Park

The best time to visit Glacier Bay National Park is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is mild and most attractions are open to visitors. However, visitors should be prepared for rain, as the area is known for its wet weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glacier Bay National Park

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and keep a safe distance from animals.
  • Pack appropriately for the weather and terrain.
  • Stay on designated trails and campsites.

Don’t:

  • Approach or feed wildlife.
  • Litter or leave trash in the park.
  • Disturb historical or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Glacier Bay National Park

  • Visitors should be aware of potential hazards, such as crevasses, steep drops, and unstable ice, when hiking on glaciers and other natural formations.

Health in Glacier Bay National Park

  • Visitors should bring adequate supplies of any necessary medications and be prepared for potential medical emergencies. Medical services are limited in the area.

Getting to Glacier Bay National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Glacier Bay National Park

  • Gustavus Airport: Located in the park, this small airport offers seasonal flights from Juneau and other Alaskan cities.

Bus Stations Near Glacier Bay National Park

  • None.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also access the park via ferry from nearby towns and cities or by driving to Haines or Skagway and taking a ferry or small plane to Gustavus.

How to Get Around in Glacier Bay National Park

  • Visitors can explore the park by kayak, on foot, or by small plane or helicopter.

Where to Go Next from Glacier Bay National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Juneau: The capital city of Alaska, located south of Glacier Bay.
  • Haines and Skagway: Two historic towns on the coast of Alaska, both known for their scenic beauty and rich history.

Conclusion

Glacier Bay National Park offers visitors a unique and breathtaking experience of Alaskan wilderness, culture, and history. From stunning glaciers and fjords to traditional Tlingit art and food, the park has something for everyone. Visitors should not miss the chance to explore this incredible destination.
