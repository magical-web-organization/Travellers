Introduction to Hyder

Nestled in the heart of the Tongass National Forest, Hyder is a small town in Southeastern Alaska that offers visitors the opportunity to experience the natural beauty of the Last Frontier. Known as the “Gateway to Alaska,” Hyder is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the rugged wilderness of Southeast Alaska.

List of Places to Visit in Hyder

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hyder is home to a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its many museums and historical places. The following are some of the top destinations for those interested in learning about the town’s history:

  • Fish Creek Wildlife Observation Site - The Fish Creek Wildlife Observation Site is one of the best places in the world to observe grizzly bears in the wild. This site is also home to the annual salmon run, which is a must-see event for nature enthusiasts.
  • Hyder’s Historic Cannery - Hyder’s historic cannery is a testament to the town’s rich fishing history. Visitors can tour the cannery and learn about the town’s commercial fishing industry.
  • Hyder Pioneer Cemetery - The Hyder Pioneer Cemetery is a historical site that tells the story of Hyder’s early pioneers. Visitors can learn about the town’s founding families and view the graves of some of Hyder’s first settlers.

Natural Attractions of Hyder: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hyder is home to some of the most beautiful natural scenery in Alaska. Outdoor enthusiasts will find a wealth of opportunities for adventure, including:

  • Salmon Glacier - The Salmon Glacier is a breathtaking sight that is considered to be one of the most beautiful glaciers in North America. Visitors can take a guided tour of the glacier or hike to its peak for panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Bear Viewing - Grizzly bears are a common sight in Hyder, and visitors can observe them in their natural habitat at the Fish Creek Wildlife Observation Site.
  • Misty Fjords National Monument - The Misty Fjords National Monument is one of the most picturesque locations in Alaska. Visitors can take a boat tour of the fjords or view them from the air on a seaplane tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hyder

Hyder is a paradise for hikers and mountain bikers. The following are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Salmon Glacier Trail - This challenging hike takes visitors to the top of the Salmon Glacier for spectacular views of the surrounding area.
  • Cascade Creek Trail - This moderate hike takes visitors through a lush forest and offers views of a beautiful waterfall.
  • Abbott Ridge Trail - This difficult hike takes visitors along a steep ridge for panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Hyder: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Hyder for visitors of all ages and interests. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Fishing Charters - Hyder is known for its world-class fishing, and visitors can book a charter to try their luck at catching salmon and halibut.
  • Wildlife Tours - Visitors can take a guided tour to observe grizzly bears, bald eagles, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Glacier Tours - Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the Salmon Glacier and other nearby glaciers.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Misty Fjords National Monument

Day 3: Hike the Salmon Glacier Trail

Day 4: Go fishing or take a wildlife tour

Day 5: Visit Hyder’s historic cannery

Day 6: Take a seaplane tour of the surrounding area

Day 7: Depart from Hyder

Food and Drink Scene of Hyder

Must-Try Dishes

Hyder is known for its fresh seafood and hearty meals. Some of the must-try dishes in the area include:

  • Halibut Fish and Chips - A classic Alaska meal that features fresh halibut and crispy French fries.
  • Salmon Chowder - Made with fresh salmon and vegetables, this hearty chowder is a staple in the area.
  • Bison Burger - For those looking for a taste of the local game, a bison burger is a must-try.

Local Beverages

Hyder is a small town, so there aren’t many options for local beverages. However, visitors can enjoy a cold beer or glass of wine at the local tavern.

Where to Stay in Hyder: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hyder is a small town, so there aren’t many options for accommodations. The following are some of the top places to stay in the area:

  • Hyder Hostel - This budget-friendly hostel offers clean accommodations and a shared kitchen for guests.
  • Compromise Guestrooms - Located in an historic building, the Compromise Guestrooms offer comfortable accommodations and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Sea Breeze Lodge - For those looking for a more upscale experience, the Sea Breeze Lodge offers beautiful ocean views and a luxurious atmosphere.

What to Buy in Hyder: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Hyder can find a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the top items include:

  • Salmon and Halibut - Fresh salmon and halibut can be purchased from local fish markets for visitors to take home and prepare.
  • Souvenir T-Shirts - Visitors can find a variety of T-shirts and other souvenirs featuring the town’s name and unique wildlife.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry - Local jewelry makers create unique pieces featuring locally sourced materials like jade and shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Hyder

Hyder is a small town with a unique culture and customs. Visitors should respect the following norms:

  • Recycling - Recycling is an important part of the local culture. Visitors should take care to properly dispose of their waste.
  • Fishing Regulations - Fishing regulations are strictly enforced, and visitors should make sure to obtain the proper permits before fishing in the area.
  • Wildlife Viewing - Visitors should always observe wildlife from a safe distance and avoid disturbing the animals.

Best Time to Visit Hyder

The best time to visit Hyder is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and the days are long. Visitors can take advantage of the many outdoor activities and tours available in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hyder

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs
  • Do take care to properly dispose of your waste
  • Do observe wildlife from a safe distance

Don’t:

  • Don’t disturb the wildlife
  • Don’t fish without obtaining the proper permits
  • Don’t litter or vandalize the natural beauty of the area

Safety and Health

Safety in Hyder

Hyder is a safe town with few crime-related issues. Visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other small town, such as locking their car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Hyder

Hyder is a remote location, so visitors should make sure to bring any necessary medications with them. There is a small medical clinic in town for minor injuries and illnesses, but visitors with serious medical issues will need to travel to the nearest hospital.

Getting to Hyder: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hyder

The closest airport to Hyder is the Ketchikan International Airport, which is located approximately 75 miles away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to Hyder.

Bus Stations Near Hyder

There are no bus stations in Hyder. Visitors can take a bus from Ketchikan to nearby towns like Wrangell and Petersburg, but will need to arrange for transportation to Hyder from there.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Hyder by ferry or private boat.

How to Get Around in Hyder

The town of Hyder is small enough to walk or bike around. Rental cars are available, but visitors should be aware that the road to Hyder is unpaved and can be rough in places.

Where to Go Next from Hyder: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Hyder can explore the surrounding area by taking a ferry or seaplane to nearby towns like Ketchikan, Wrangell, and Petersburg. These towns offer a variety of cultural attractions and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Visitors to Hyder will find a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventure, and cultural heritage. From hiking to fishing, there’s something for everyone in this remote Alaskan town.
