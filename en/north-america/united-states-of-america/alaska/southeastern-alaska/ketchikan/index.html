Are you ready to experience the natural wonders of Southeastern Alaska? Look no further than Ketchikan, known as the “Salmon Capital of the World.” With its rich Native culture, stunning scenery, and diverse range of activities, Ketchikan is an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore all that Alaska has to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Ketchikan.

Introduction to Ketchikan

Ketchikan is a small city with a population of around 14,000 people. It is located on Revillagigedo Island in Southeastern Alaska, and can only be accessed by boat or plane. The city is surrounded by water and forests, making it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors to Ketchikan can learn about the region’s rich Native culture, catch salmon and halibut in the fishing grounds, or explore the numerous indoor and outdoor attractions the city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ketchikan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ketchikan is home to several museums and historical sites that showcase the region’s Native and pioneer cultures. These include the:

  • Totem Bight State Historical Park
  • Southeast Alaska Discovery Center
  • Saxman Native Village

Natural Attractions of Ketchikan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ketchikan boasts vast stretches of pristine wilderness, breathtaking views, and plenty of outdoor adventures. Popular natural attractions in the area include:

  • Misty Fjords National Monument
  • Tongass National Forest
  • Creek Street
  • Bear Viewing at Anan Creek

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ketchikan

Ketchikan offers a diverse range of hiking and biking trails through forests, waterfronts, and mountains. Whether you’re an experienced hiker looking for a challenge or a casual visitor seeking a leisurely walk, there’s a trail for you. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Deer Mountain Trail
  • Rainbird Trail
  • Ward Lake Trail
  • Carlanna Lake Trail

Best Things to Do in Ketchikan: Activities and Tours

Ketchikan is home to a wide range of activities that cater to every interest. Whether you’re interested in a scenic boat tour, a thrilling zip line adventure, or a relaxing spa treatment, Ketchikan has it all. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Alaskan Rainforest Sanctuary
  • Ziprider zip line adventure
  • Alaska Canopy Adventures
  • Southeast Sea Kayaks

Week-Long Itinerary for Ketchikan

To help you get the most out of your visit, we’ve prepared a week-long itinerary that covers some of the best attractions and experiences Ketchikan has to offer:

Day 1: Explore Downtown Ketchikan & Red Light District​

Day 2: Tongass National Forest and Creek Street​

Day 3: Misty Fjords National Monument​

Day 4: Fishing Tour at Clover Pass​

Day 6: Totem Pole Carving & Ketchikan Museums​

Day 7: Kayak around Ketchikan Coastline & Bear Viewing at Anan Creek​

Food and Drink Scene of Ketchikan

When it comes to food, Ketchikan has a diverse range of options, from local seafood to international cuisine. For a true taste of Alaska, be sure to try the following must-try dishes:

  • Salmon and halibut dishes
  • Crab cakes
  • Alaska king crab legs

Don’t forget to pair your meal with a local beverage such as Alaskan Brewing Company’s beer, or a Moscow Mule from The New York Cafe.

Where to Stay in Ketchikan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ketchikan has a wide range of accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the most popular places to stay in Ketchikan include:

  • Cape Fox Lodge
  • Inn at Creek Street
  • Black Bear Inn

What to Buy in Ketchikan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ketchikan has no shortage of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some top picks include:

  • Hand-carved totem poles
  • Native art and jewelry
  • Alaskan smoked salmon
  • Local craft beer

Local Culture and Customs of Ketchikan

Ketchikan has a rich Native culture that is celebrated throughout the city. Visitors can experience local customs and traditions by attending events such as the annual Pow Wow, or by visiting local museums and cultural centers.

Best Time to Visit Ketchikan

The best time to visit Ketchikan is during the summer months, from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild, and the days are long, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ketchikan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Tip generously
  • Be prepared for wet weather

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife
  • Leave trash behind
  • Overlook the importance of proper outdoor gear

Safety and Health

Safety in Ketchikan

Ketchikan is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the wilderness or walking around at night. We recommend visitors to:

  • Keep an eye on their valuables
  • Be aware of their surroundings
  • Stay in well-lit areas and avoid walking alone at night

Health in Ketchikan

Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications and basic first-aid supplies with them. Ketchikan has a hospital and various clinics for medical care, but it’s always best to be prepared.

Getting to Ketchikan: Transportation Options and Logistics

The only way to reach Ketchikan is via boat or plane. Ferry services are available from Juneau and Prince Rupert, while Ketchikan International Airport is served by Alaska Airlines, Delta Air Lines, and United Airlines.

Airports Near Ketchikan

  • Ketchikan International Airport (KTN)

Bus Stations Near Ketchikan

  • Ketchikan Gateway Borough Bus Service

Other Options

  • Alaska Marine Highway ferry service from Juneau or Prince Rupert

How to Get Around in Ketchikan

Ketchikan is a small city and only a few miles long, so getting around is relatively easy on foot. However, visitors looking to explore further afield may need to consider renting a car or taking a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Ketchikan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ketchikan is an excellent starting point for those looking to explore the wider Southeastern Alaska region. Nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Juneau, Alaska’s capital city
  • Sitka, the former capital of Russian America
  • Wrangell, a scenic, natural paradise

Conclusion

Ketchikan is an ideal destination for those looking to explore all that Southeastern Alaska has to offer. With its rich Native culture, stunning scenery, and diverse range of activities, visitors to Ketchikan are sure to find something they love. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Ketchikan.
