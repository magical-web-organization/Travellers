Introduction to Sitka

Located in the southeastern part of Alaska, Sitka is a beautiful city that boasts a rich mix of Russian and Native American culture. Nestled on the west coast of Baranof Island, it’s a city surrounded by the stunning views of mountains, crystal-clear waters, and lush green forests. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a lover of history and culture, Sitka has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Sitka

Start your journey in Sitka by visiting the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sitka has a rich history, and the following museums and historical places are must-visit destinations:

  • Russian Bishop’s House: The Russian Bishop’s House is one of the oldest and most impressive buildings in Sitka. It offers a glimpse into the city’s Russian past and is a great place to learn about the history and culture of Alaska’s indigenous people.

  • Sitka National Historical Park: This park features artifacts, exhibits, and totem poles that showcase the Tlingit culture and the Russian-American period that took place in Sitka.

  • Baranof Castle Hill State Historic Site: Located at the heart of Sitka, this historic site is where Russia formally handed Alaska over to the United States in 1867. It’s a great place to learn about the history of the city while enjoying the stunning views.

Natural Attractions of Sitka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sitka has a vast array of natural attractions that offer some of the most stunning views you’ll find in Alaska. Don’t miss these spots:

  • Mount Edgecumbe: Take a ferry to this dormant volcano, located on Kruzof Island, for a breathtaking view of the surrounding area.

  • Tongass National Forest: This national forest offers hiking trails, fishing spots, and other outdoor activities that allow you to experience the beauty of Sitka.

  • Sitka Sound: This area is perfect for a wildlife viewing tour. You can see whales, sea otters, sea lions, and various seabirds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sitka

Sitka is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a range of trails for all levels of enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Indian River Trail: This trail, located just outside of Sitka, offers a pleasant hike through a dense forest alongside a river and waterfall.

  • Gavan Hill Trail: This route provides challenging terrain and amazing views of Sitka Sound, the surrounding islands, and the town itself.

  • Cross Trail: A perfect trail for mountain bike enthusiasts, Cross Trail, is one of the most popular trails in Sitka with its steep and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Sitka: Activities and Tours

Sitka offers a range of activities and tours that cater to everyone’s interests. Here are some of the best activities to consider:

  • Sea Kayaking Tour: You can explore the shorelines, coves, and caves of Sitka Sound while enjoying the stunning vistas and wildlife during this tour.

  • Whale Watching Excursion: Take a cruise or tour to see the wildlife diversity of the area, including humpback and killer whales, Dall’s porpoises, and Steller’s sea lions.

  • Fishing and Crabbing Tours: Fishing and crabbing tours are among the most sought-after activities in Sitka. You can catch salmon and trout in freshwater streams or try king crab and Dungeness crab in Sitka’s coastal waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sitka

Here’s an itinerary that covers all the must-visit destinations in Sitka:

Day 1 Visit the Sitka Historical Museum and Walk through Old Harbor

Food and Drink Scene of Sitka

Sitka has a thriving food scene that showcases Alaska’s fresh seafood, wild berries, and locally sourced ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Alaskan King Crab: This type of crab is taken fresh from the waters around Sitka and brings a sweet and delicate flavor when cooked to perfection.

  • Sourdough pancakes: Made from sourdough starters passed down from generation to generation, Sitka’s sourdough pancakes are a must-try.

  • Salmon: Various types of salmon are available in Sitka, including chinook, sockeye, and coho among others. It’s usually served grilled or smoked.

Local Beverages

  • Sitka Spruce tip tea: This tea, made from the new growth of Sitka spruce needles, has a mild, citrusy flavor and is packed with vitamin C.

  • Alaska-brewed beer: Alaska has some of the best microbreweries in the world, and Sitka is no exception.

Where to Stay in Sitka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sitka offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Aspen Hotel of Sitka: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with a view of the harbor as well as modern amenities such as a fitness center and free wifi.

  • Sitka Hotel: This recently renovated hotel is located in downtown Sitka and offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

  • Sitka Wild Rose Guest House: This cozy guest house offers a home-away-from-home experience with clean and comfortable accommodations and beautiful gardens.

What to Buy in Sitka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring back a piece of Sitka home with you or a souvenir for your loved ones, make sure to check out these top items:

  • Alaskan Native arts and crafts: Sitka is home to many artists that specialize in making traditional and contemporary native art and crafts.

  • Smoked salmon: Sitka smoked salmon is a local delicacy that you can take home and enjoy long after your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Sitka

Sitka is a city with a vibrant cultural heritage that dates back to its Tlingit and Russian roots. Visitors can experience the city’s unique culture through events like the Sitka Summer music festival or by visiting a local Tlingit village.

Best Time to Visit Sitka

The best time to visit Sitka is during the summer months from May to August, when the weather is the warmest and pleasant. Winters in Sitka can be cold and snowy, but it’s still a beautiful time for outdoor winter sports and a chance to experience the northern lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sitka

Make sure to adhere to these do’s and don’ts when visiting Sitka:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and indigenous people’s traditions.

  • Dress warmly and appropriately for the weather.

  • Follow bear safety guidelines.

Don’t:

  • Touch, feed, or approach wildlife.

  • Leave any trash in public parks, beaches, or trails.

  • Ignore local signs and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sitka

Sitka is generally a safe city, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Keep the following safety tips in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or outdoor activities.

  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight in crowded areas.

Health in Sitka

Sitka has a hospital and medical clinics that offer basic healthcare services. It’s important to have travel insurance to cover any medical expenses that may arise during your visit.

Getting to Sitka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sitka

The Sitka Rocky Gutierrez Airport is the only airport that services Sitka and offers daily flights to major cities in Alaska and some cities outside of the state.

Bus Stations Near Sitka

There are no bus stations in Sitka as the city is not accessible by road. Visitors can take a ferry from Juneau or use a private charter boat to reach the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive at Sitka by using the Alaska Marine Highway, a comprehensive ferry system that connects many coastal Alaskan cities.

How to Get Around in Sitka

The best way to get around Sitka is by walking or renting a bike. There are no taxis or ride-sharing services in the city, but visitors can use the public transit system or hire a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Sitka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby cities within reach of Sitka include Juneau, Ketchikan, and Skagway, each of which offers unique experiences to visitors, making them a worth-visit destination.

Conclusion

Sitka is one of the most charming cities in Alaska, with its rich culture, beautiful natural views, and a wide range of activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether it’s exploring the history or hiking on the trails, Sitka is a must-visit city that should be on everyone’s bucket list.
