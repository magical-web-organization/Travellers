Shaktoolik, a remote village located in Arctic Alaska, is an adventurous destination for those who want to explore the Arctic wilderness. With its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Shaktoolik attracts tourists from across the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink, accommodations, local culture, and safety tips to help you plan your memorable trip to Shaktoolik.

Introduction to Shaktoolik

Shaktoolik is a small Inupiaq Eskimo village that lies on the eastern shoreline of Norton Sound on the Seward Peninsula. The village has a total area of 33.0 square miles, with a population of about 250. Shaktoolik is rich in cultural heritage, and you can experience the native traditions and customs of the Inupiaq Eskimo people.

List of Places to Visit in Shaktoolik

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Inupiat Heritage Center: A museum dedicated to preserving and showcasing the art, culture, and history of the Inupiaq people.
  • Old Shaktoolik: Visit the historic site of the old village that dates back to the 1800s. Learn about the history and traditions of the Inupiaq people.

Natural Attractions of Shaktoolik: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shaktoolik Bay: Enjoy stunning views of the Norton Sound and the Shaktoolik Bay. Go fishing for Arctic grayling, Dolly Varden, and whitefish in the Bay.
  • Shaktoolik River: Go rafting or kayaking on the Shaktoolik River while admiring the stunning views and spotting wildlife such as bears, moose, and caribou.
  • Cape Denbigh: Hike to Cape Denbigh to experience panoramic views of the Norton Sound and witness the historic site of an ancient Inupiaq village.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shaktoolik

  • Shaktoolik River Trail: A scenic hiking trail along the Shaktoolik River, where you can spot wildlife such as bears, moose, and caribou.

Best Things to Do in Shaktoolik: Activities and Tours

  • Aurora sightings: Enjoy the breathtaking views of the Northern Lights while staying in Shaktoolik.
  • Dog sledding: Experience the traditional method of transportation for the Inupiaq people in the Arctic.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shaktoolik

Day 1: Arrive in Shaktoolik and check-in to your hotel. Day 2: Visit the Inupiaq Heritage Center and learn about the history and traditions of the Inupiaq people. Day 3: Go fishing in Shaktoolik Bay. Day 4: Take a tour of the Old Shaktoolik village. Day 5: Hike to Cape Denbigh and enjoy the scenic views of the Norton Sound. Day 6: Go dog sledding and experience the traditional method of transportation in the Arctic. Day 7: Depart Shaktoolik and head to nearby destinations such as Nome or Kotzebue.

Food and Drink Scene of Shaktoolik

Must-Try Dishes

  • Agutuk: an Inupiaq dish made with meat, fish, and vegetables.
  • Muktuk: a traditional Inupiaq food made with whale blubber and skin.

Local Beverages

  • Akutaq: a type of ice cream made with animal fats, berries, and fish.

Where to Stay in Shaktoolik: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shaktoolik Lodge: A comfortable and affordable hotel offering stunning views of the Norton Sound.

What to Buy in Shaktoolik: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Inupiaq arts and crafts, such as ivory carvings and beadwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Shaktoolik

The Inupiaq culture is centered around subsistence hunting and fishing. Visitors can participate in traditional activities such as dog sledding, ice fishing, and cultural performances.

Best Time to Visit Shaktoolik

The best time to visit Shaktoolik is from December to March, when you can experience the Northern Lights and enjoy winter activities such as dog sledding and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shaktoolik

Do:

  • Respect the traditions and customs of the Inupiaq people.
  • Be prepared for the cold and bring appropriate clothing and gear.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or its habitat.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shaktoolik

Shaktoolik is a remote village, and it is important to take precautions when exploring the wilderness. Always carry a map, make sure your equipment is in good condition, and inform others of your itinerary.

Health in Shaktoolik

Shaktoolik has limited medical facilities, so visitors should take the necessary precautions and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Shaktoolik: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shaktoolik

  • Shaktoolik Airport, which offers limited commercial flights.

Other Options

  • Charter flights and helicopter services are available from nearby cities such as Nome or Kotzebue.

How to Get Around in Shaktoolik

The best way to get around Shaktoolik is by walking or dog sled.

Where to Go Next from Shaktoolik: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nome: A historic gold rush town with a vibrant mix of Alaska Native and Western culture, located about 100 miles from Shaktoolik.
  • Kotzebue: A community located on the shore of the Chukchi Sea, known for its scenic beauty, traditional Inupiaq culture, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Shaktoolik offers its visitors a unique experience in the Arctic region. With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and traditional lifestyle, this village is a must-visit for anyone who wants to explore the Arctic wilderness. Follow our comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Shaktoolik and make unforgettable memories.
