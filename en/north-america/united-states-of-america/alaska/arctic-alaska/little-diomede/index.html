Introduction to Little Diomede

Little Diomede, also known as Ignaluk, is a small island in the Bering Strait. It is one of the two Diomede Islands - the other being Big Diomede, which belongs to Russia. Little Diomede has a population of around 100 residents who are mostly Inupiaq Eskimos. The island is accessible only by helicopter or small planes and has no roads connecting its villages. A visit to Little Diomede is truly a unique experience.

List of Places to Visit in Little Diomede

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Little Diomede Cultural Center: Here, visitors can learn about the island’s history and culture, view traditional clothing and artifacts, and participate in cultural activities.
  • Saint Lawrence Island Eskimo Heritage Museum: This museum showcases the art and culture of indigenous people who live in and around the Bering Sea.
  • Cemetery Cove: This is the final resting place of many Inupiaq Eskimos. Visitors can pay their respects and learn about the island’s history while taking a walk around the cemetery.

Natural Attractions of Little Diomede: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peak of the Arctic: The tallest point on Little Diomede offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding sea and ice.
  • Pinnacle Rock: This landmark serves as Little Diomede’s mascot and can be seen from most parts of the island. Visitors can take a hike to get a closer look.
  • Devil’s Thumb: This is a unique rock formation that is said to have mystical powers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Diomede

While there are no official hiking or biking trails on Little Diomede, visitors can explore the island’s rugged terrain. Guides are available for hire to help visitors navigate.

Best Things to Do in Little Diomede: Activities and Tours

  • Northern Lights Viewing: Little Diomede is located in the auroral oval, making it a prime spot for viewing the northern lights.
  • Traditional Eskimo Dancing: Visitors can participate in a traditional dance show and learn about the Inupiaq culture.
  • Eskimo Blanket Toss: A thrilling and unique experience where participants are tossed into the air on a blanket.

Food and Drink Scene of Little Diomede

Must-Try Dishes

  • Akutuq: This traditional Eskimo dish is a dessert made from mashed berries and animal fat.
  • Muktuq: A popular dish made from sliced raw whale blubber served with soy sauce and onions.
  • Fish and Seal Soup: A hearty soup made with local fish and seal meat.

Local Beverages

  • Akutaq Juice: A refreshing drink made from mashed berries, sugar, and water.
  • Inupiaq Tea: A traditional herbal tea made from local plants.

Where to Stay in Little Diomede: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are no hotels or hostels on Little Diomede. Visitors can stay in one of the island’s guesthouses or with a local family.

What to Buy in Little Diomede: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Inupiaq Handicrafts: Little Diomede is known for its intricate woven baskets and ivory carvings.
  • Local Snacks: Visitors can bring home locally made fish jerky and akutuq.

Local Culture and Customs of Little Diomede

The residents of Little Diomede are Inupiaq Eskimos, and their culture and heritage are prominent throughout the island. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, which include a deep connection to the land and sea and a strong sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Little Diomede

The best time to visit Little Diomede is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is mild, and the sun shines almost 24 hours a day. Visitors can also watch traditional whale hunts during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Little Diomede

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Hire a guide to explore the rugged terrain.
  • Try local dishes and attend cultural events.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Take photos of residents without permission.
  • Litter on the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Little Diomede

Little Diomede has limited medical services, so visitors should take precautions to prevent illness and injury. The island is also remote and has no roads connecting its villages, so visitors should take care when exploring.

Health in Little Diomede

Visitors should bring any necessary medication as there are no pharmacies on the island. It is also recommended to have travel medical insurance before arrival on the island.

Getting to Little Diomede: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Little Diomede

There is no direct flight to Little Diomede, visitors must fly to Nome and take a helicopter or small plane from there to the island.

Bus Stations Near Little Diomede

There are no bus stations near Little Diomede.

Other Options

Visitors can take a boat tour to Little Diomede during the summer months. However, due to weather conditions, boat travel is not always reliable.

How to Get Around in Little Diomede

Little Diomede has no road network, so visitors must explore the island by foot, snowmobile, or four-wheeler. Guides are available for hire to help visitors navigate the island.

Where to Go Next from Little Diomede: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations such as Nome, Alaska, or venture to Russia’s Big Diomede Island. Boat tours and helicopter charters are available.

Conclusion

Little Diomede is a unique and stunning destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure. The local culture, natural beauty, and adventurous outdoor activities make Little Diomede a destination worth exploring. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip and make the most of their visit to Little Diomede.
