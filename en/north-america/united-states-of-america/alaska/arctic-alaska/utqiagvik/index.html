Introduction to Utqiagvik

Utqiagvik, formerly known as Barrow, is the most northerly city in the United States of America. It is located in Arctic Alaska on the coast of the Arctic Ocean that looks out at the horizon where the sun doesn’t set for 84 days. Known as the “top of the world,” Utqiagvik is an excellent tourist destination for outdoor enthusiasts, thrill-seekers, and people seeking a unique cultural experience.

List of Places to Visit in Utqiagvik

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Utqiagvik has a rich Alaska Native history, and you can experience that at the following museums and historical places:

  1. Ilisagvik College: This college is the only higher education institution in the North Slope region. Visitors can learn about traditional Inupiaq culture and take part in hands-on activities.

  2. Inupiat Heritage Center: This center showcases the Inupiaq Eskimo culture and heritage through exhibits, artwork and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Utqiagvik: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Utqiagvik is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, boasting numerous scenic outdoor attractions.

  1. The Arctic Ocean: The sea that surrounds Utqiagvik is a marvel to behold. Visitors can take a stroll along the beach, hunt for whale remnants and experience the stunning Northern Lights.

  2. National Petroleum Reserve – Alaska: Explore one of the nation’s largest natural wilderness areas spread over 23 million acres of pristine wilderness.

  3. Pigniq area: It is a parkous area where visitors can observe the village of Utqiagvik from the top of a hill. This location is picturesque during midnight sun and looks fantastic during Northern-sky watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Utqiagvik

Utqiagvik provides incredible opportunities to immerse yourself in nature and experience breathtaking views while hiking or biking.

Nalukataq trail

The Nalukataq Trail, is a 21-mile trail that begins in the National Petroleum Reserve – Alaska. The trail leads visitors to the beach community of Utqiagvik where they can experience the true essence of the North Slope.

Best Things to Do in Utqiagvik: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Utqiagvik.

Whaling

Whaling is a prominent tradition and livelihood for the people of Utqiagvik. During the whale hunting season, visitors can witness the hunting ritual by taking part in a whale hunt tour.

Aurora Watching

Utqiagvik offers an outstanding opportunity to observe the captivating Northern Lights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Utqiagvik

Here is an ideal week-long itinerary for Utqiagvik:

Day 1: Arrive at Utqiagvik, explore the town Day 2: Experience the traditional Inupiaq culture at Ilisagvik College. Day 3: Go on a whale hunt tour. Day 4: Take a hike on Nalukataq Trail. Day 5: Visit the Inupiat Heritage Center. Day 6: Watch Northern Lights Day 7: Depart Utqiagvik

Food and Drink Scene of Utqiagvik

Must-Try Dishes

Utqiagvik is known for unique dishes made from locally sourced ingredients. Here are must-try foods in the city.

  1. Muktuk – raw whale skin and blubber.

  2. Akutaq – dessert made from low bush cranberries, sugar, and shortening mixed with nuts or fish.

Local Beverages

Try out local drinks like fitkik and akutuq beers, which are unique beverages you won’t find anywhere else.

Where to Stay in Utqiagvik: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

There are several decent options for lodging in Utqiagvik. From hotels to guesthouses, visitors have different options to choose from.

  1. Utqiagvik Inn: A popular cozy stay known for its small-town charm.

  2. King Eider Inn: Located in the heart of the city’s business district, this sophisticated stay provides every essential amenity a traveler’s need.

What to Buy in Utqiagvik: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products that visitors should consider buying:

  1. Caribou antlers

  2. Walrus tusk

Local Culture and Customs of Utqiagvik

The Inupiaq Eskimo culture is vibrant, and the people are proud of their heritage. Visitors can learn about the culture by visiting museums and attending local events.

Best Time to Visit Utqiagvik

The best time to visit Utqiagvik is during the summer months of June and July when the sun shines bright, and the town offers a range of outdoor recreational activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Utqiagvik

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and culture.

  2. Engage with the locals and learn about their ways of life.

Don’t:

  1. Intrude upon people’s privacy.

  2. Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Utqiagvik

Utqiagvik is a safe city, so visitors shouldn’t be worried about their well-being. However, it’s always best to take necessary safety precautions.

Health in Utqiagvik

Visitors should carry medication, as medical facilities in the city are limited.

Getting to Utqiagvik: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Utqiagvik

The Wiley Post-Will Rogers Memorial Airport is the primary airport located in the city.

Bus Stations Near Utqiagvik

There are no bus services in Utqiagvik.

Other Options

The cheapest way of getting to Utqiagvik is by taking a ferry from Juneau.

How to Get Around in Utqiagvik

Visitors can travel locally by taking bus, taxi, or bike rides.

Where to Go Next from Utqiagvik: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visit the city of Deadhorse, which is home to several oil-producing companies. The town is located east of Utqiagvik and can be accessed by plane.

Conclusion

Utqiagvik is a perfect destination filled with unique experiences that visitors will remember for a lifetime. From whaling to Northern Lights viewing, visitors get a chance to immerse themselves in the traditions and culture of the Inupiak. Enjoy local dishes, purchase souvenirs, and stay safe while having a great time in Utqiagvik.
