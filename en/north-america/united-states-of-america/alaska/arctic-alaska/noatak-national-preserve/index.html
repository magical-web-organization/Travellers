Introduction to Noatak National Preserve

Located in the pristine wilderness of Arctic Alaska, Noatak National Preserve is a remarkable destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Covering over 6.6 million acres, this expansive preserve is home to stunning scenic beauty, diverse wildlife, and a rich cultural history. Visitors to Noatak National Preserve can experience incredible outdoor adventures, learn about ancient cultures, and witness some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the world.

List of Places to Visit in Noatak National Preserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Noatak National Preserve is rich in cultural history, and several landmarks and museums offer visitors insight into the ancient cultures that once inhabited the area.

  • Inuit Culture and Historical Site: Explore the remnants of Inuit dwellings and hunt sites from 500 years ago, preserved by the permafrost.

  • Kotzebue Sound: This site is where Alutiiq, Siberian Yupik, and Inupiaq people have exchanged ideas and goods for over 1,000 years. Visitors can see the preserved sites where these events took place.

Natural Attractions of Noatak National Preserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Noatak National Preserve is a haven for adventure lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With exciting activities and breathtaking scenery, there is no shortage of attractions to explore.

  • Noatak River: Several outfitters offer guided floats on the Noatak, which is the primary means of transportation through the park. As you paddle down the river, keep an eye out for wildlife like bears, moose, and caribou.

  • Brooks Range: The park is located in the Brooks Mountain Range, which offers ample opportunities for hiking, camping, and mountaineering. The Arrigetch Peaks are a popular destination for climbers, offering stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noatak National Preserve

  • The 26-Mile Trail: A popular backcountry trail that starts near the park headquarters in Kotzebue. This trail offers panoramic views of the Noatak River, the Brooks Range, and the surrounding wilderness.

  • Hiking in the Browne Mountains: A challenging but rewarding hike that takes visitors through areas with some of the park’s best wildlife sighting opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Noatak National Preserve: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Watching: Visitors can witness the diverse wildlife in the park, including grizzly bears, wolves, caribou, and moose. Outfitters provide guided tours for visitors to ensure their safety while they explore the park.

  • Snowmobiling: In the winter, the park transforms into a winter wonderland and is accessible by snowmobile.

Week-Long Itinerary for Noatak National Preserve

  • Day 1: Arrive at the park’s main office in Kotzebue, Alaska. Spend the day exploring the local area and getting acclimated to the park.

  • Days 2-4: Take a guided river tour down the Noatak River, explore historical sites, and take part in wildlife watching tours.

  • Days 5-6: Embark on a hiking trip to explore the rugged terrain of the Browne Mountains and the Arrigetch Peaks.

  • Day 7: Wrap up the trip with a peaceful day of fishing, kayaking, or simply relaxing in the beautiful natural surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Noatak National Preserve

Must-Try Dishes

  • Muktuk: A traditional Inuit dish consisting of whale blubber and skin.

  • Akutaq: A sweet and creamy dessert made from Eskimo ice cream, a mixture of whipped animal fat with berries and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Birch Beer: A flavorful, non-alcoholic drink made from birch sap.

  • Arctic Wine: Made from local berries, such as blueberries or cranberries, and fermented in traditional ways.

Where to Stay in Noatak National Preserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Noatak National Preserve Visitors Center: Provides information on the park, camping permits, and maps. The park also provides remote campsites for those who want to camp in the wilderness.

  • Kotzebue: This small town has several hotels and lodges that offer visitors accommodations during their stay.

What to Buy in Noatak National Preserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Inuit Carvings: Crafts made by Inuit artists, including carvings of bears, otters, and seals.

  • Local Art: Paintings, sculptures, and jewelry from local artists are available for sale in the park’s gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Noatak National Preserve

The park is situated in the heart of Inupiaq territory, and visitors can learn about the local culture by talking with the local Iñupiaq people and attending cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Noatak National Preserve

The best time to visit Noatak National Preserve is during the summer months, when temperatures are milder. The long days provide ample opportunities for outdoor activities and wildlife watching.

Do’s and Don’ts of Noatak National Preserve

Do:

  • Hire a guide: Rely on a guide that has been certified by the park to get a better knowledge of the park and to ensure your safety.

  • Pack appropriately: Plan for all weather conditions when packing for your trip.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Respect the environment and leave the park as you found it.

  • Feed the Wildlife: It is strictly prohibited to feed the wildlife in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Noatak National Preserve

  • Bear Safety: Visitors should be aware of bears in the park and take precautions to avoid attracting them. Carry bear spray, make noise to avoid surprising bears, and store food properly.

  • River Safety: Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with river travel and ensure they have proper gear when doing so.

Health in Noatak National Preserve

Visitors to the park should be in good physical condition and bring their own medicine and supplies, as there are no medical facilities in the park.

Getting to Noatak National Preserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Noatak National Preserve

  • Ralph Wien Memorial Airport: The closest airport to the park is located in its main office in Kotzebue, Alaska.

Bus Stations Near Noatak National Preserve

  • N/A

Other Options

  • Boat: Visitors can also travel by boat from Kotzebue to the park’s headquarters.

How to Get Around in Noatak National Preserve

  • Hiking: Most of the park’s attractions are accessible by hiking.

  • River Travel: The Noatak River is primarily used for transportation within the park.

Where to Go Next from Noatak National Preserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kotzebue: The nearby town of Kotzebue has several cultural and historical attractions, as well as restaurants and shopping opportunities.

Conclusion

Noatak National Preserve is an unforgettable destination that promises a unique mix of outdoor adventure, cultural history, and breathtaking scenery. Whether you’re hiking through rugged mountain terrain or exploring the park’s rich cultural heritage, Noatak National Preserve is sure to leave you with memories that will last a lifetime. So plan your trip today and discover the beauty of this incredible Arctic wonderland.
