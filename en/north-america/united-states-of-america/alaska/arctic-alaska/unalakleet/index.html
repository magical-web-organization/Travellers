Introduction to Unalakleet

Located in Arctic Alaska, Unalakleet is a small town known for its rich heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. The town lies on the west coast of Alaska, along the Norton Sound, and is accessible by air or boat. The community center of Unalakleet serves as a hub for locals and visitors alike, providing access to numerous historical and cultural attractions, as well as outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.

The indigenous Iñupiat people have inhabited the lands of Unalakleet for thousands of years, and their heritage is reflected in the town’s art, architecture, and traditions. Visitors can experience this rich cultural legacy by exploring local museums and historical sites, or by participating in traditional activities such as whaling festivals, Eskimo dances, and fish camps. Unalakleet is also an excellent base camp for exploring the surrounding wilderness, with ample opportunities for hiking, fishing, and hunting.

List of Places to Visit in Unalakleet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iñupiat Heritage Center: This museum showcases the history, art, and cultural traditions of the Iñupiat people. Visitors can view exhibits on traditional crafts, hunting and fishing practices, and the history of colonialism and resistance.

  • Old Saint Joseph’s Catholic Church: Built in 1902, this church is a landmark of Unalakleet’s early history. Visitors can view the original wooden frame structure, as well as an exhibit on the history of Catholicism in Alaska.

  • Sheldon Jackson School: This former boarding school for indigenous children now houses a museum displaying artifacts from the school’s history, as well as exhibits on the Iñupiaq culture.

Natural Attractions of Unalakleet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serpentine Hot Springs: A cluster of natural hot springs located in the wilderness outside of Unalakleet. Visitors can soak in the gentle warm waters while observing the Northern Lights.

  • Norton Sound: A vast and picturesque shallow basin that borders Unalakleet. Visitors can observe the marine wildlife, including seals, sea otters, and whales.

  • Kaltag Portage Trail: This historic trail runs between Unalakleet and Kaltag, a 60-mile route through the wilderness that was once used by indigenous peoples for hunting and trade. Visitors can hike or bike along the trail and observe the region’s diverse flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Unalakleet

Unalakleet offers hikers and bikers ample opportunities for striking views and exhilarating adventures. Some of the top routes to explore include:

  • Blueberry Hill: A short hike up Blueberry Hill offers panoramic vistas of Unalakleet and its surroundings, including the Norton Sound and the Yukon River.

  • Serpentine Hot Springs Trail: A challenging hike through the wilderness that ends at the natural hot springs.

  • Kaltag Portage Trail: A 60-mile trail connecting Unalakleet and Kaltag through the wilderness, providing opportunities for hiking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Unalakleet: Activities and Tours

Unalakleet provides plenty of activities and tours to ensure a memorable trip, including:

  • Fishing in the Norton Sound: The Norton Sound is known for its abundant marine wildlife, and visitors can take a boat tour to fish for salmon, halibut, and other species.

  • Whaling Festival: An annual festival that celebrates the cultural heritage of the Iñupiat people and their relationship with the ocean. Visitors can participate in traditional activities, such as skin boat races and communal feasts.

  • Eskimo Dancing: The traditional dance of the Iñupiat people, which is inspired by the natural world and the processes of hunting and fishing. Visitors can attend a performance or take part in a workshop to learn the steps.

Week-Long Itinerary for Unalakleet

Day 1: Arrive in Unalakleet, check into a local hostel or guesthouse, and spend the day exploring the town center, including the Iñupiat Heritage Center and the Old Saint Joseph’s Catholic Church.

Day 2: Embark on a guided hike or bike ride along the Kaltag Portage Trail, observing the diverse flora and fauna of the region. Optional hot springs soak on the return trip.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of the Norton Sound, observing marine wildlife and fishing for salmon and other species. Optional visit to local fish processing plant to learn about the processing techniques.

Day 4: Participate in an Eskimo dance workshop or attend a performance to learn about the traditional dance of the Iñupiat people.

Day 5: Visit the Sheldon Jackson School to learn about the history of colonialism and resistance in Alaska, including the Iñupiat struggle for self-determination.

Day 6: Enjoy a day of leisure, exploring town museums, shops, and restaurants, or taking part in cultural activities such as fish camps or berry picking.

Day 7: Depart from Unalakleet, taking time for a final soak in the Serpentine Hot Springs before returning home.

Food and Drink Scene of Unalakleet

Must-Try Dishes

Unalakleet is known for its fresh and locally-sourced ingredients, including fish, berries, and game. Some of the top dishes to try while in Unalakleet include:

  • Salmonberry Jam: A sweet and tangy spread made from locally sourced salmonberries. Often served with toast or crackers.

  • Moose Steak: A hearty and savory dish made from locally hunted moose. Often served with potatoes and vegetables.

  • Smoked Salmon: A popular dish made from locally caught salmon, which is smoked and seasoned before serving. Often served on a cracker or with cream cheese.

Local Beverages

Unalakleet has a thriving craft beer scene, with numerous local breweries offering unique and flavorful beers. Some of the top breweries to visit include:

  • Unalakleet Brewing Company: A family-owned brewery that produces a wide range of beers, including a popular IPA and a seasonal pumpkin ale.

  • Norton Sound Brewing Company: A small brewery located in nearby Nome, known for its creative and experimental beers, including a sour cranberry ale and a smoked porter.

Where to Stay in Unalakleet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Unalakleet provides a range of options for accommodation, to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • Unalakleet River Lodge: A cozy and rustic lodge located along the Unalakleet River, offering amenities such as a bar, restaurant, and fishing tours.

  • Aurora Inn: A comfortable and modern hotel located in the town center, offering amenities such as free wifi, on-site laundry, and a breakfast buffet.

  • Unalakleet Hospitality: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of town, offering a communal kitchen, shared bathrooms, and access to local tours and activities.

What to Buy in Unalakleet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Unalakleet offers a range of unique and locally-made souvenirs, including:

  • Iñupiat Art: Traditional crafts such as carvings, beaded jewelry, and woven baskets can be found in local shops and art galleries.

  • Berry Jams: Locally made jams and jellies made from wild berries and fruits in the region

  • Local Necessities: Practical items such as traditional clothing, food products, and skin boots are ideal for those seeking a one-of-a-kind gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Unalakleet

Unalakleet has a diverse and rich cultural heritage that is reflected in the town’s art, music, and traditions. The indigenous Iñupiat people have inhabited these lands for thousands of years, and their customs and practices continue to be a vital part of the local culture. Some of the customs and traditions that visitors can observe in Unalakleet include:

  • Whaling Festivals: An annual celebration that honors the Iñupiat’s relationship with the ocean and their successful harvest of the bowhead whale.

  • Eskimo Dancing: A traditional dance form that is inspired by hunting, fishing, and the natural world. The dance is often accompanied by drums and chants.

  • Fish Camps: A communal gathering place where locals and visitors can enjoy fishing, cooking, and sharing stories and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Unalakleet

The best time to visit Unalakleet is during the summer months, from May to September, when temperatures are mild and the daylight hours are long. This is the ideal time for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and boating. The fall and winter months offer other unique experiences such as aurora-chasing and dog-sledding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Unalakleet

Do:

  • Respect local customs and cultural traditions.
  • Support local businesses and artisan products.
  • Explore the wilderness and participate in outdoor adventures such as hiking and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect traditional practices such as whaling and subsistence hunting.
  • Leave trash or litter behind in the wilderness or town areas.
  • Take photograph without asking for permission or paying a fair tourism fee.

Safety and Health

Safety in Unalakleet

Unalakleet is generally a safe and welcoming community; however, visitors should take some precautions to ensure their safety, including:

  • Be aware of wildlife such as bears and moose when hiking or biking in the wilderness.
  • Carry a personal locator beacon in case of emergencies.
  • Avoid traveling alone in remote areas.

Health in Unalakleet

Visitors should be aware that the isolation of Alaska makes emergency medical care more difficult to access. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary prescription medications and to have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Unalakleet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Unalakleet is accessible primarily by air or boat, with no major roads connecting the town to other communities.

Airports Near Unalakleet

The Unalakleet Airport is served several times daily from Anchorage by major airlines.

Boats and Ferries to Unalakleet

The St. Lawrence Island, Stebbins, and Koyukuk River Villages provide regular ferry service to Unalakleet.

How to Get Around in Unalakleet

The most convenient way of getting around Unalakleet is by foot, and most of the accommodations and attractions are easily accessible on foot. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the surrounding wilderness or hire a local tour guide for bespoke experiences.

Where to Go Next from Unalakleet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Unalakleet provides a great jumping-off point for exploring the surrounding areas, including:

  • Nome: A historic gold-rush town that offers unique cultural experiences and outdoor adventures such as exploring abandoned mines and gold panning.

  • Koyukuk River Villages: A network of native villages and towns along the Koyukuk River, offering cultural and wilderness experiences such as dog mushing, hunting, and fishing.

Conclusion

Unalakleet is a unique and vibrant town that offers a wealth of cultural and natural experiences for visitors. With its rich heritage and stunning natural beauty, Unalakleet is the ideal destination for those seeking to explore the history and diversity of Arctic Alaska. Whether it’s hiking, fishing, or sampling the local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Unalakleet. Plan your trip today to experience the best of this unique community!
