Introduction to North Pole

Located in Interior Alaska, in the United States of America, North Pole is a winter wonderland that lives up to its name. The town is known for its year-round Christmas decorations and festive atmosphere. But there’s more to North Pole than just holiday spirit. From natural wonders and outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and local cuisine, this town has a variety of things to offer travelers. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip to North Pole.

List of Places to Visit in North Pole

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Santa Claus House - This iconic attraction is a must-visit for anyone traveling to North Pole. It’s a massive Christmas-themed store that’s open year-round, with hundreds of souvenirs, decorations, and gifts. You can also meet Santa and his elves, take photos, and mail letters from the North Pole post office.
  • Alaska House Art Gallery - This gallery showcases the artworks of Alaskan artists, including paintings, sculptures, and jewelry. You can also buy souvenirs, such as traditional Native Alaskan carvings.
  • El Dorado Gold Mine - This historical site takes you back to the gold rush era and offers guided tours of a 100-year-old gold mine and a lively panning experience. You can also learn about the mining history of the region.

Natural Attractions of North Pole: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chena River State Recreation Area - This vast wilderness park encompasses over 250,000 acres of forests, lakes, and rivers. You can hike, fish, kayak, camp, and observe wildlife, such as moose, eagles, and bears.
  • Aurora Ice Museum - This unique museum is entirely made of ice and snow and showcases intricate ice sculptures and chandeliers. You can also take an ice carving class or enjoy a cocktail at the ice bar.
  • Ski Land - This ski resort is the perfect destination for winter sports enthusiasts. You can ski, snowboard, or sled down the slopes while enjoying the majestic scenery of the Alaska Range.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Pole

Angel Rocks Trail

This 3.5-mile trail is located in the Chena River State Recreation Area and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys. It’s a moderate hike with a few steep sections, but the reward is worth it. You’ll pass by rock formations, alpine meadows, and mountain streams. The trail is also suitable for mountain biking.

Granite Tors Trail

This 7.3-mile trail is another fantastic option for hiking and biking. It’s located in the same park as the Angel Rocks Trail and offers a more challenging terrain. You’ll hike through forests, over hills, and around the Granite Tors rock formations. The views of the interior Alaska landscape will take your breath away.

Best Things to Do in North Pole: Activities and Tours

  • Northern Lights Tour - North Pole is one of the best places in the world to see the Northern Lights, also known as the Aurora Borealis. You can take a guided tour to witness this natural phenomenon and learn about the science behind it.
  • Dog Sledding Tour - Dog mushing is a traditional Alaskan activity that’s still practiced today. You can take a dog sledding tour and experience the thrill of being pulled by a team of huskies.
  • Hot Springs Tour - Alaska has many hot springs, and one of them is located near North Pole. You can take a tour of the Chena Hot Springs and soak in the natural pools while admiring the Northern Lights.

Food and Drink Scene of North Pole

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Alaska Salmon - This fish is a delicacy in Alaska and is served in various forms, such as grilled, smoked, or baked. You can enjoy it at a local fish restaurant or catch it yourself and cook it over a campfire.
  • Reindeer Sausage - Reindeer meat is a staple in Alaska and is often used in sausages. You can try this unique dish at a local eatery or buy it at a grocery store.
  • Birch Syrup - Birch trees are abundant in Alaska, and their sap is used to make a syrup that’s similar to maple syrup but with a distinct taste. You can use it as a topping for pancakes or waffles.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan Beers - Alaska has a thriving craft beer scene, and many breweries offer tours and tastings. You can try local favorites like Alaska Amber or Alaskan White Ale.
  • Arctic Fire - This spicy cocktail is made with locally distilled vodka, jalapenos, and grapefruit juice. It’s a popular drink in North Pole’s bars and restaurants.
  • Alaskan Coffee - This coffee is made with locally roasted beans and is known for its smooth, rich taste. You can buy it at a specialty coffee shop or a local grocery store.

Where to Stay in North Pole: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Aurora Express Bed and Breakfast - This bed and breakfast is housed in a train car and offers cozy rooms with unique decor. It’s located near the Chena River and is a short drive from North Pole’s attractions.
  • Pike’s Waterfront Lodge - This lodge is located in Fairbanks, which is a 15-minute drive from North Pole. It offers comfortable rooms with great views of the Chena River and the Northern Lights.
  • SpringHill Suites by Marriott Fairbanks - This hotel is located in Fairbanks and offers spacious suites with modern amenities. It’s a short drive from North Pole’s attractions and is a convenient option for families.

What to Buy in North Pole: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Christmas Ornaments - North Pole is known for its Christmas decorations, and you can buy unique ornaments at Santa Claus House or Alaska House Art Gallery.
  • Native Alaskan Artwork - Many local artists create traditional Native Alaskan artworks, such as carvings or beadwork. You can buy them at Alaska House Art Gallery or other local shops.
  • T-shirts and Hoodies - North Pole is a fun and quirky town, and you can find many T-shirts and hoodies with clever designs and slogans that capture its spirit.

Local Culture and Customs of North Pole

The residents of North Pole take their Christmas traditions seriously, and you can see it in the year-round decorations and events. However, they also value their wilderness and Alaskan heritage and embrace activities like dog mushing and gold panning. It’s important to respect their customs and culture and learn about them during your visit.

Best Time to Visit North Pole

The best time to visit North Pole is during the winter months, from November to March, when the Northern Lights are most visible, and the town is transformed into a Christmas wonderland. However, if you prefer milder weather and outdoor activities, the summer months, from June to August, are also a great option.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Pole

Do:

  • Dress in layers and bring warm clothes, even during the summer months.
  • Respect the wildlife and follow the park rules when hiking or camping.
  • Tip your tour guides and restaurant servers.

Don’t:

  • Touch or feed the wild animals.
  • Leave trash or litter in the parks and trails.
  • Overdo it with the eggnog.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Pole

North Pole is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s important to follow basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and not leaving valuables in plain sight. Also, be aware of the weather conditions, which can be harsh during the winter months, and prepare accordingly.

Health in North Pole

North Pole has a few medical clinics and pharmacies, but for serious health issues, it’s recommended to go to Fairbanks, which has larger hospitals and medical facilities. It’s also important to stay hydrated and protect your skin from the cold and sun.

Getting to North Pole: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Pole

The closest airport to North Pole is Fairbanks International Airport, which is a 15-minute drive from the town. Car rentals, taxis, and shuttle buses are available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near North Pole

The Alaska Railroad offers passenger trains that stop at North Pole on their route from Fairbanks to Anchorage. Greyhound buses also stop at Fairbanks, which is a short drive from North Pole.

Other Options

Driving to North Pole is also a popular option, especially if you want to explore other parts of Alaska. The town is located on the Richardson Highway, which connects Fairbanks to Valdez.

How to Get Around in North Pole

The best way to get around North Pole is by car, as the town is sprawling and the attractions are spread out. Car rentals are available at the airport and in Fairbanks. You can also take a taxi or book a tour that includes transportation.

Where to Go Next from North Pole: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Denali National Park - This iconic national park is located three hours south of North Pole and offers breathtaking views of the Alaska Range and wildlife sightings, such as grizzly bears and caribou.
  • Valdez - This coastal town is located six hours south of North Pole and offers scenic views of glaciers and fjords. You can also go fishing, kayaking, or hiking.
  • Nome - This remote town is located six hours west of North Pole and offers a glimpse into Alaska’s gold rush history and traditional Native Alaskan culture. You can also witness the Iditarod dog sled race, which starts in Nome.

Conclusion

North Pole is a unique and charming town that offers something for everyone. Whether you want to experience the holiday spirit, witness the Northern Lights, or explore the Alaskan wilderness, this town will not disappoint. Plan your trip to North Pole and get ready for an unforgettable adventure.
