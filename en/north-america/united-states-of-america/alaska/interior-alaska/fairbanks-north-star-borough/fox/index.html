Introduction to Fox

Nestled in the heart of the Interior Alaska region, Fox is a small and charming community that is often overlooked by tourists. However, those who make the effort to visit will discover a world of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. Ensconced by panoramic views of snow-capped mountains, pristine forests, and crystal-clear lakes, Fox offers an enchanting getaway from the bustling urban life. In this travel guide, we have curated a list of places to visit, things to do, and tips to make the most of your trip to Fox.

List of Places to Visit in Fox

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

Alaska House Art Gallery

Opened in 1985, the Alaska House Art Gallery is a quaint gallery that showcases some of the finest works of local artists. The gallery is housed in a traditional log cabin that has been beautifully restored, and the exhibits focus on themes of nature, wildlife, and Alaskan lifestyles.

Morris Thompson Cultural and Visitors Center

Opened in 2009, the Morris Thompson Cultural and Visitors Center is a hub for tourists and locals alike. The center features interactive exhibits, workshops, and performances that celebrate the culture, history, and traditions of Alaska’s indigenous people.

Natural Attractions of Fox: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chena River State Recreation Area

Sprawling over 254,000 acres of pristine wilderness, the Chena River State Recreation Area is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers a range of activities, including fishing, hiking, camping, and wildlife watching. The park’s main attraction is the Chena Hot Springs, which is a natural geothermal pool that remains warm throughout the year, even during winters.

Angel Rocks Trailhead

The Angel Rocks Trailhead is a popular hiking destination that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The 3.5-mile trail takes you through lush forests, rocky terrains, and panoramic vistas. The trail is moderate in difficulty, making it accessible for hikers of all levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fox

Chena River State Recreation Area

The Chena River State Recreation Area offers a range of hiking trails that cater to different difficulty levels. The Angel Rocks Trail, Granite Tors Trail, and the Chena Dome Trail are some of the most popular trails for hikers. The trails offer exceptional views of the park’s diverse ecosystem, wildlife, and geological formations.

Tanana Valley State Forest

The Tanana Valley State Forest is a vast expanse of forest that offers a range of biking trails that cater to different levels of difficulty. The trails offer spectacular views of the surrounding landscape, and visitors can spot wildlife such as moose, bears, birds, and foxes.

Best Things to Do in Fox: Activities and Tours

Aurora Borealis Viewing

One of the most spectacular natural phenomena in Alaska is the Aurora Borealis, or the Northern Lights. Fox and the surrounding areas offer some of the best viewing opportunities for the Aurora. Visitors can take guided tours that take them to the best viewing spots and offer insights into the science behind the Northern Lights.

Fishing on Chena River

Chena River is home to a wide variety of fish species, including Alaska salmon, Arctic graylings, and northern pikes. Visitors can experience the thrill of fishing by taking guided fishing tours that offer equipment, licenses, and expert guidance.

Food and Drink Scene of Fox

Must-Try Dishes

Sour toe cocktail

A unique and odd delicacy of Alaska, the sour toe cocktail is a drink that contains a mummified human toe. The toe is typically served in a shot glass, and the challenge is to swallow the drink without touching the toe with your lips. Note that the toe is sanitized after each use and is safe to consume.

Reindeer sausage

A popular street food, reindeer sausage is a must-try dish in Fox. The sausage is made of tender and juicy reindeer meat, and is typically served with a side of mustard and sauerkraut.

Local Beverages

Alaskan beer

Alaska is known for its craft beers that are brewed with pristine glacial water and local ingredients. Visitors can enjoy a range of flavorful and unique beers at the local breweries in Fox.

Where to Stay in Fox: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rivers Edge Resort

Located on the banks of the Chena River, Rivers Edge Resort is a luxurious and rustic resort that offers comfortable rooms, cabins, and suites. The resort is known for its impeccable service, scenic views, and a range of activities such as fishing, hiking, and hot springs.

HooDoo Brewing Company Hostel

For budget-conscious travelers, the HooDoo Brewing Company Hostel offers a cozy and affordable stay. The hostel is located above the popular HooDoo Brewing Company, where guests can enjoy a range of beers and socialize with locals.

What to Buy in Fox: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ivory carvings

Ivory carvings are a popular souvenir item in Alaska, and Fox is no exception. Visitors can find a range of intricately carved ivory sculptures, jewelry, and art pieces that reflect the region’s indigenous culture and heritage.

Birch syrup

A unique and delicious souvenir item, birch syrup is a popular ingredient in local cuisine. Visitors can buy bottles of birch syrup from local stores and syrup farms.

Local Culture and Customs of Fox

Fox has a rich cultural heritage that is closely tied to the region’s indigenous people. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are expected to respect their traditions, beliefs, and customs. Visitors should dress appropriately for the weather and avoid littering or damaging the local ecosystem.

Best Time to Visit Fox

The best time to visit Fox is from late May to early September when the weather is mild, and the days are long. Summers offer a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching. However, visitors who want to experience the Northern Lights must visit during winters.

Do’s and Don’ts in Fox

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities
  • Follow the park rules and regulations at Chena River State Recreation Area
  • Try local dishes and beverages
  • Bring insect repellent and sunscreen

Don’t:

  • Harm or disturb the local wildlife
  • Litter or damage the ecosystem
  • Ignore park warnings and safety precautions
  • Propagate false stereotypes or assumptions about Alaskan culture
  • Forget to pack warm clothing and appropriate gear for outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Fox

Visitors should take necessary precautions while hiking, fishing, or engaging in other outdoor activities. They should bring appropriate gear, follow park safety guidelines, and be aware of their surroundings. Visitors should also exercise caution while driving on icy roads and avoid driving under the influence of alcohol.

Health in Fox

It is essential to carry necessary medication and first aid kits while traveling to Fox. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of hypothermia, frostbite, and other cold-related illnesses during winters. Visitors should bring warm clothing and dress in layers to stay warm in cold weather.

Getting to Fox: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fox

The Fairbanks International Airport is the nearest airport to Fox, located approximately 20 miles away. Visitors can hire local taxis, shuttle services, or rent a car from the airport to reach Fox.

Bus Stations Near Fox

The Alaska Railroad Corporation offers train and bus services that connect to Fairbanks and other parts of Alaska. Visitors can take the bus services that run from the Fairbanks bus depot to Fox and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Fox from other parts of Alaska or Canada. Fox is located on the Steese Highway and can be reached by road from Fairbanks or Anchorage.

How to Get Around in Fox

Visitors can explore Fox and its surroundings by car rental, taxi, or shuttle services. They can also rent bikes or go on foot to experience the natural beauty of the region.

Where to Go Next from Fox: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Denali National Park

Located approximately 2.5 hours away from Fox, Denali National Park is a world-renowned destination that offers stunning views of the Denali mountain, wildlife, and hiking trails.

North Pole

A charming and festive town located approximately 15 miles away from Fox, North Pole is known for its Christmas-themed décor, gift shops, and Santa Claus House.

Conclusion

Fox is a hidden gem in Alaska that offers a wide range of natural, cultural, and adventurous experiences. The town’s rustic charm, warm hospitality, and scenic beauty make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking to explore Alaska’s hidden treasures. From hiking and fishing to Aurora viewing and local cuisine, Fox offers a memorable vacation that is unique and unforgettable.
