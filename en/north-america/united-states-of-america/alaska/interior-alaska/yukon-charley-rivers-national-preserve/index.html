Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve Travel Guide

Located in Interior Alaska, in the United States of America, the Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve is a haven for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Spread over an area of 2.5 million acres, the preserve is an ode to the rugged beauty of Alaska. From snow-capped mountains to rushing rivers and fascinating wildlife, the preserve offers a little something for everyone. Let’s dive into an ultimate travel guide to Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve.

Introduction to Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

Established in 1978, the preserve is named after the two rivers that flow through it, the Yukon River and the Charley River. The preserve offers a diverse range of activities that cater to different interests. Visitors can explore historical sites, hike or bike through the wilderness, enjoy scenic views, and engage in outdoor adventures like fishing, hunting, and camping.

List of Places to Visit in Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

The preserve has several culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities. Here is a list of places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eagle Historic District: A town that was once a booming center of trading during Alaska’s gold rush. Today, visitors can explore its rich history and culture by visiting its museums and old buildings.
  • Fortymile Historic District: A historic mining town, once the largest town in interior Alaska, that still stands in the preserve. Visitors can explore the old gold mines, town buildings, and cemeteries.
  • Yukon-Charley Rivers Visitor Center: Located in Eagle, visitors can stop here to get information about the preserve’s history and plan their trip. There are several exhibits, including a full-sized replica of a miner’s cabin.

Natural Attractions of Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yukon River: A 2,300-mile-long river that is the lifeline of Alaska. Visitors can take a guided river tour or paddle on their own to explore the river’s beauty and wildlife.
  • Charley River: A stunning and remote wilderness, excellent for floating and fishing.
  • Steese National Conservation Area: Scenic vistas, rich wildlife, and a haven for hikers and bikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

The preserve offers several hiking and biking routes that cater to different levels of expertise. Here are a few popular routes:

  • Charley River Overlook Trail: A 14-mile-long strenuous hike that offers scenic views of the Charley River and the preserve’s mountains.
  • Coal Creek Trail: A 3-mile-long moderate hike that offers scenic views of the coal mines and the surrounding nature.
  • Lower Eagle Trail: A 3.5-mile-long moderate hike that brings visitors to the Eagle River and offers stunning views of the Eagle Historic District.

Best Things to Do in Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve: Activities and Tours

The preserve offers several activities that cater to different interests. Here are the best things to do:

  • Wildlife Viewing: Visitors can spot caribou, moose, black and brown bears, gray wolves, and many other wild animals.
  • Fishing: The preserve has several species of fish, including salmon, arctic grayling, northern pike, and Dolly Varden Char, which are excellent for sportfishing.
  • Camping: There are several campsites in the preserve for visitors who want to engage in a rustic adventure.
  • Rafting: The preserve has several rivers that offer excellent opportunities for white water rafting.

To make the most of your trip to the preserve, here is a week-long itinerary:

Day 6 White water rafting in the preserve

Food and Drink Scene of Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

The preserve’s food and drink scene is as rugged as its nature. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salmon: Freshly caught salmon is a delicacy that visitors should not miss.
  • Moose Meat: A local favorite, moose meat is served in various forms, including burgers, meatloaf, and sausages.
  • Cloudberry Jam: A sweet, tangy jam made from local berries is a great way to start your day.

Local Beverages

  • Birch Beer: A sweet, non-alcoholic soda made from birch sap.
  • Spruce Tip Tea: A warm drink made from the new growth of spruce trees is perfect for chilly nights.
  • Alaskan Amber: A locally brewed beer that pairs well with the rustic nature of the preserve.

Where to Stay in Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The preserve offers several options to stay, including:

  • Camping: The preserve has several campsites for visitors.
  • Hotels: There are several hotels in the nearby town of Eagle.
  • Guesthouses: Some local hosts provide guesthouses for visitors who want a more rustic experience.

What to Buy in Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy several locally made products and souvenirs, including:

  • Birch Bark Baskets: Handmade baskets made from birch bark are excellent souvenirs.
  • Wood Carvings: Visitors can buy local wood carvings, including wildlife figurines and jewelry boxes.
  • Furs and Pelts: Local trappers sell furs and pelts from the preserve’s wildlife.

Local Culture and Customs of Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

The preserve is home to several indigenous communities, including Athabascan and Tlingit. Visitors should respect their traditions and learn about their culture by visiting the nearby villages.

Best Time to Visit Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

The best time to visit is from May to September, when the preserve is accessible and there is plenty of daylight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and follow the preserve’s guidelines to avoid affecting their habitat.
  • Leave no trace and pack out all your trash.
  • Carry bear spray and take precautions to stay safe in the wilderness.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife or get too close to them.
  • Leave the designated trails and paths.
  • Smoke or light fires outside of the designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

The preserve offers several outdoor adventures that can be dangerous if visitors are not careful. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Always check the weather and trail conditions before heading out.
  • Carry a map, compass, and a GPS device while exploring the preserve’s wilderness.
  • Take a guided tour for activities like rafting and fishing.

Health in Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

Visitors should carry a first aid kit and have good health before engaging in strenuous activities in the preserve’s wilderness.

Getting to Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

The preserve is remote and accessible only by air or water. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

  • Fairbanks International Airport
  • Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport

Bus Stations Near Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

  • Alaskan Bus Lines
  • Alaska Land Transportation

Other Options

Visitors can also charter a taxi or take a private car from nearby towns like Fairbanks or Anchorage.

How to Get Around in Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve

Visitors can explore the preserve by hiking, biking, rafting, or by taking guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby towns like Eagle or take a guided tour to nearby attractions like the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.

Conclusion

The Yukon - Charley Rivers National Preserve is a stunning destination that offers a range of experiences for visitors. Whether you want to explore its natural beauty, engage in outdoor adventures, or simply enjoy its rugged charm, the preserve has something for everyone. Plan a trip and indulge in the true essence of Alaska’s wilderness.
