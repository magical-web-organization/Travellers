Introduction to Chicken

Located in Interior Alaska, Chicken is a small community known for its gold rush history and outdoor adventures. The town got its name when early residents couldn’t spell the word “ptarmigan” correctly and opted for a simpler name instead. Today, Chicken is a popular destination for visitors looking to explore the stunning natural beauty of Alaska.

List of Places to Visit in Chicken

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chicken Gold Camp & Outpost: This historic gold mining camp features guided tours and gold panning experiences.
  • Downtown Chicken: Check out the unique shops, restaurants, and accommodations in the small downtown area.
  • Fortymile River: Follow the gold rush trail along the scenic Fortymile River.
  • Taylor Highway: This scenic highway leads to some of the most remote and beautiful parts of Alaska.

Natural Attractions of Chicken: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chicken Creek: Go hiking, fishing, or gold panning along the creek that runs through the town.
  • Chicken BLM: The Bureau of Land Management offers a variety of outdoor activities like camping, hiking, and wildlife viewing in the area.
  • Chicken Ridge: Take a hike or drive up to Chicken Ridge for stunning panoramic views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Yukon-Charley Rivers National Preserve: This vast wilderness area offers outdoor activities like hiking, camping, fishing, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chicken

  • Fortymile River Trail: Follow the historic gold rush trail along the Fortymile River.
  • Upper and Lower Chicken Loop Trails: These trails offer moderate hiking and biking options and are accessible all year round.
  • American Summit Trail: This challenging trail leads to a breathtaking view of the surrounding wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Chicken: Activities and Tours

  • Gold Panning: Learn about the town’s gold rush history and try your hand at panning for gold.
  • River Rafting: Take a thrilling rafting trip down the Fortymile River.
  • Northern Lights Viewing: Enjoy the spectacular Aurora Borealis display during certain times of the year.
  • Dog Sledding: Experience a true Alaskan adventure by going dog sledding in the snow-covered wilderness.

Food and Drink Scene of Chicken

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alaska King Crab Legs: Enjoy fresh and locally caught king crab legs, served with garlic butter and lemon.
  • Reindeer Sausage: A unique Alaskan specialty, reindeer sausage is a must-try dish for adventurous eaters.
  • Wild Salmon: Sample locally caught wild salmon, cooked in a variety of ways.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan Beer: Alaska is known for its locally brewed beers, with many breweries located throughout the state.
  • Fireweed Honey Mead: Try this sweet and floral honey wine, made with Alaskan fireweed.
  • Chaga Tea: A popular drink in Alaska, chaga tea is made from a type of mushroom and is believed to have many health benefits.

Where to Stay in Chicken: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chicken Gold Camp & Outpost: This historic camp offers a variety of accommodations, including cabins, tents, and RV sites.
  • Chicken Creek RV Park: This RV park offers full hookups and easy access to Chicken Creek.
  • Chicken Motel & RV Park: This motel and RV park offers affordable accommodations and all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Chicken: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gold Rush Memorabilia: Pick up a piece of Alaska’s gold rush history to remember your trip.
  • Native Artwork: Browse a variety of handmade Native artwork, including traditional carvings and beadwork.
  • Alaskan Salmon: Bring home some delicious, freshly caught salmon from one of the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Chicken

The local culture of Chicken is heavily influenced by its gold rush history and the wilderness that surrounds it. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s heritage and the natural beauty of the area. It’s recommended to dress in layers and be prepared for changing weather conditions, as well as carrying bear spray while hiking or exploring the wilderness.

Best Time to Visit Chicken

The best time to visit Chicken is during the summer months, from June to August, when temperatures are warmer and the days are longer. However, winter visitors will be treated to the spectacular Northern Lights display and a unique Alaska winter experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chicken

Do:

  • Do carry bear spray and be aware of your surroundings when hiking or exploring.
  • Do try the local beer and cuisine for a true Alaskan experience.
  • Do respect the town’s gold rush history and cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t approach wildlife, including moose and bears.
  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing and proper gear for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chicken

Visitors should be aware of the dangers associated with the wilderness and wild animals in the area. It’s recommended to carry bear spray, stay on designated trails, and avoid camping near rivers or lakes. Mosquitoes and other insects can also be a nuisance in the summer months, so bring insect repellent and protective clothing.

Health in Chicken

Visitors should be aware of the risks of foodborne illnesses from consuming raw or undercooked meat, fish, or shellfish. It’s also recommended to get a tetanus shot before participating in outdoor activities and to carry a first aid kit for emergencies.

Getting to Chicken: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • By Air: The closest major airport is Fairbanks International Airport, located about 165 miles from Chicken.
  • By Car: Chicken is accessible by car via the Taylor Highway, which connects the town to Tok, Alaska and the Alaska Highway.
  • By Bus: There are several bus companies that provide transportation to and from Chicken, including the Alaska Bus Company and the Tok Transportation Company.

Airports Near Chicken

  • Fairbanks International Airport (FAI): Located about 165 miles from Chicken, this is the closest major airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Chicken

  • Tok Transportation Company: Offers daily scheduled bus service to and from Chicken.
  • Alaska Bus Company: Provides transportation to and from Chicken, as well as other destinations throughout Alaska.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available in Fairbanks and Tok.
  • Private Transportation: Visitors can arrange for private transportation to and from Chicken.

How to Get Around in Chicken

Visitors can easily explore the town on foot or by bike. Rental cars and RVs are also available for those wanting to explore the surrounding wilderness areas.

Where to Go Next from Chicken: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tok, Alaska: Located about 90 miles from Chicken, this small town offers outdoor activities and stunning views of the Alaska Range.
  • Dawson City, Yukon: This historic town is located across the Canadian border and is known for its gold rush history and cultural attractions.
  • Wrangell-St. Elias National Park: This vast wilderness area offers outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing and is located about 160 miles from Chicken.
