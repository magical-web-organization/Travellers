Introduction to Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve

Located in Southcentral Alaska, Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve is the largest national park in the United States. Sprawling over 13.2 million acres, the park is home to four mountain ranges, glaciers, rivers, and vast wilderness. Visitors can explore the natural wonders, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures that Wrangell-St. Elias has to offer.

Best Time to Visit Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve

The best time to visit Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve is during the summer months from June to August when the days are longer, and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and keep a safe distance.
  • Carry bear spray and know how to use it.
  • Stay on designated trails to minimize impact on the environment.

Don’t:

  • Approach or feed the wildlife.
  • Litter or leave trash in the park.
  • Get too close to glaciers or icebergs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve

  • Carry a first aid kit and know basic wilderness first aid.
  • Dress in layers and bring appropriate gear.
  • Check weather and trail conditions before embarking on a hike.

Health in Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.
  • Apply sunscreen and protect yourself from UV rays.
  • Watch out for signs of altitude sickness if you plan to hike the high peaks.
