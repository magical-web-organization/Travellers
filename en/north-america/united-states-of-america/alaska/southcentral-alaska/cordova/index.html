Introduction to Cordova

Located in Southcentral Alaska, Cordova is a picturesque seaside town known for its stunning natural beauty and diverse wildlife. Tucked away in the heart of the Chugach Mountains, Cordova offers breathtaking views of towering glaciers, pristine forests, and crystal-clear waters. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, Cordova has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at all the must-see attractions, best places to stay, and delicious local food that you won’t want to miss.

List of Places to Visit in Cordova

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cordova is steeped in rich history and culture that dates back centuries. If you’re interested in learning more about the town’s past, check out these top attractions:

  • Cordova Historical Museum: offers a glimpse into the local history with displays on Cordova’s native people, fishing industry, and natural wonders.
  • The Copper River Delta Historical Society museum: Discover the history and significance of the Copper River Delta.
  • Alaganik Slough Archaeological District: showcases the ancient history of the Eyak people.

Natural Attractions of Cordova: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cordova is famous for its stunning natural scenery and abundant wildlife. Whether you’re looking for a scenic hike or a chance to spot some of Alaska’s most iconic animals, here are a few top attractions worth checking out:

  • Sheridan Glacier: a popular destination for hiking and exploring the stunning landscape.
  • Prince William Sound: one of the most beautiful natural wonders of the world known for its stunning fjords, glaciers, and wildlife.
  • The Copper River Delta: a vast and diverse landscape that is home to a wide range of birds and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cordova

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Cordova offers ample opportunity for hiking and biking. Here are a few of the top routes you won’t want to miss:

  • Heney Ridge Trail: offers stunning sweeping views of the town, harbor, and mountains.
  • Humpback Lake Trail: a popular hiking route that takes you through the beautiful forest to Humpback Lake.
  • Power Creek Trail: a challenging but rewarding trail that offers views of the Copper River Delta and the town of Cordova.

Best Things to Do in Cordova: Activities and Tours

Want to experience Cordova like a local? Check out these top activities and tours:

  • Wildlife Tours: offers a chance to spot some of Alaska’s most iconic animals, such as brown bears, humpback whales and bald eagles.
  • Fishing Tours: a popular activity in Cordova, thanks to its abundant salmon runs and diverse seafood.
  • Sea Kayaking: explore the picturesque bays and inlets of Cordova from the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cordova

Whether you have a week or a weekend, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you plan your Cordova trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Cordova; visit the Cordova Historical Museum and the Copper River Delta Historical Society museum.
  • Day 2: Visit the Alaganik Slough Archaeological District and hike the Heney Ridge Trail.
  • Day 3: Take a wildlife tour of Prince William Sound.
  • Day 4: Sea kayaking along the coastline and exploring the scenic views of the town.
  • Day 5: Go fishing in the Copper River, one of the most popular fishing destinations in Alaska.
  • Day 6: Hike the Sheridan Glacier Trail or Power Creek Trail.
  • Day 7: Explore the local food scene, souvenir shops, and relax in the small-town atmosphere before departing Cordova.

Food and Drink Scene of Cordova

Cordova boasts a lively culinary scene that is sure to please any foodie. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Copper River Salmon: Cordova is renowned for its salmon harvest and offers plenty of restaurants offering fresh, locally caught seafood.
  • Clam Chowder: A hearty soup made with local clams, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Halibut: Another popular seafood dish in Cordova, served either fried or grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan Brewing: A local craft brewery that offers a range of beers, from light, refreshing IPAs to darker, robust stouts.
  • Kachemak Bay Brewing: Serves a variety of craft beers made from locally grown hops.

Where to Stay in Cordova: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cordova offers a variety of accommodations for every budget, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are a few top choices:

  • Cordova Rose Lodge: This elegant hotel features modern amenities, stunning views, and professional services.
  • Orca Adventure Lodge: Offers cozy cabins with full meals and guided tours included in your stay.
  • The Reluctant Fisherman Inn: A historic bed and breakfast that offers charming rooms, delicious home-cooked breakfasts, and beautiful views.

What to Buy in Cordova: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect keepsake to remind you of your Cordova trip? Here are a few popular souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Local Seafood: Freshly caught salmon and other seafood are popular souvenirs in Cordova.
  • Native Crafts: Cordova is home to a community of Tlingit artists who create beautiful crafts, including woven baskets, carved masks, and intricate jewelry.
  • Local Art: The town is home to many talented artists who create works inspired by the natural beauty of the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Cordova

To fully experience Cordova, it’s important to understand the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to know before your visit:

  • Cordova is a tight-knit community where locals take pride in their history, culture, and traditions.
  • Many residents rely on the fishing industry and form a deep connection with the ocean and its wildlife.
  • The town celebrates many festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Cordova Iceworm Festival and the Copper River Shorebird Festival.

Best Time to Visit Cordova

The best time to visit Cordova depends on your travel preferences. If you’re looking for lush greenery and mild weather, visit during the summer months (June-August). However, if you’re interested in seeing wildlife such as humpback whales and bald eagles, visit during the fall (September-November). The winter months (December-February) offer stunning snowy landscapes and vibrant Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cordova

Here are a few “Do’s” and “Don’ts” to keep in mind during your Cordova trip:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Dress in layers and bring waterproof gear to prepare for the unpredictable weather.
  • Try the local seafood and craft beers.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or litter in the natural setting.
  • Approach or feed the wildlife.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cordova

Cordova is a small, safe community, and visitors rarely experience any serious crime. However, it’s always important to take precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure and locking your doors at night.

Health in Cordova

Cordova has a medical clinic that offers basic healthcare services, but for serious medical issues, you will need to travel to Anchorage. It’s recommended that visitors pack their own supply of over-the-counter medication, as it may not be readily available in Cordova.

Getting to Cordova: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cordova is only accessible by boat, plane, or ferry. Here are the different transportation options to consider:

  • Cordova Municipal Airport: offers daily flights to Anchorage and other nearby cities.
  • Alaska Marine Highway: a ferry service with a regular route that visits different towns throughout Alaska.
  • Charter flights and private boats: For visitors arriving from outside of Alaska, charter flights or boats may be a convenient option.

Airports Near Cordova:

How to Get Around in Cordova

Cordova is a small town, so most places are easily accessible by foot or bike. However, if you prefer, here are a few other transportation options:

  • Car rental: local car rental agencies offer cars and trucks for rent.
  • Taxi services: a few taxi companies offer short trips around town.

Where to Go Next from Cordova: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Cordova, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out:

  • Anchorage: Alaska’s largest city is just a short plane ride from Cordova and offers many cultural attractions and outdoor activities.
  • Katmai National Park: home to a large population of brown bears and beautiful landscapes.
  • Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve: a vast wilderness area that includes some of the highest peaks in North America.

Conclusion

Cordova is a beautiful and vibrant town that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re interested in hiking, wildlife tours, or simply relaxing by the waterfront, Cordova is the perfect destination for your next Alaskan getaway. Plan your trip today and enjoy all that this stunning town has to offer.
