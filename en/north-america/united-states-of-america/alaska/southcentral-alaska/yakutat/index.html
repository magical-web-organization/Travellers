Introduction to Yakutat

Nestled in Southcentral Alaska near the Gulf of Alaska, Yakutat is a remote town that offers a unique Alaskan experience. It welcomes visitors to experience its natural beauty, from glaciers to pristine rivers. Yakutat is also known for its cultural heritage, which is shaped by the town’s indigenous Tlingit and Ahtna tribal communities.

List of Places to Visit in Yakutat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Yakutat Tlingit Tribe Cultural Center

Visit the Yakutat Tlingit Tribe Cultural Center for an immersive look at the culture and lifestyle of the indigenous Tlingit community. The center displays artifacts and traditional crafts, and hosts dance performances and cultural classes.

2. Situk River State Recreation Site

The Situk River is a popular spot for salmon fishing, and you can rent equipment or find local guides at the Situk River State Recreation Site. The center also provides a comprehensive history of the river and the town itself.

Natural Attractions of Yakutat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

3. Hubbard Glacier

One of the top attractions in Yakutat is the Hubbard Glacier, which is the largest tidewater glacier in North America. A boat tour will take you up close to the glacier, giving you an unforgettable view of the icy expanse from the water.

4. Russell Fjord Wilderness Area

The Russell Fjord Wilderness Area features vast mountain ranges, crystal clear waterways, and magnificent glaciers. Take a guided tour van or go for a hike on one of the area’s numerous trails for a close look at this pristine wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yakutat

5. Yakutat Mountain Bike Tours

Yakutat Mountain Bike Tours is a popular provider that offers an excellent selection of mountain biking tours, and hiking adventures that include exploring the nearby glaciers, archeological sites, and scenic landscapes. This activity is perfect for those who are looking for a bit of an adventure in Yakutat.

Best Things to Do in Yakutat: Activities and Tours

6. Yakutat Charter Boat Company

Fishing enthusiasts should not miss the chance to tour the scenic sea of Yakutat with Yakutat Charter Boat Company. The guides are experienced fishermen and experts in the area, and can help you land the legendary salmon, halibut, and trout that inhabit the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Yakutat

Must-Try Dishes

7. Halibut cheeks

Halibut cheeks, typically served as a starter, feature the most tender, moist and flavorful of fish parts. They are often lightly breaded and fried, served with a side of tangy tartar sauce and sliced lemon.

8. Smoked Salmon

Alaska is well-known for its smoked salmon, and while in Yakutat, it is a must-try local specialty. The fish is first cured with salt and sugar before being smoked over alder wood to create a classic flavor.

Local Beverages

9. Alaskan Brewing Co.

For beer lovers, a stop at the Alaskan Brewing Co. brewery and tasting room is a must-do. Sample a wide variety of beverages, including their classic Alaskan Amber Lager and limited edition brews.

Where to Stay in Yakutat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

10. Glacier Bear Lodge

Located south of Yakutat in Gustavus, Glacier Bear Lodge is a great option for travelers who want a comfortable basecamp for their Alaska adventure. The lodge features cozy guest rooms and cabins as well as a restaurant and bar that serves fresh seafood and locally brewed beer.

What to Buy in Yakutat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

11. Native arts and crafts

Yakutat is known for its indigenous Tlingit and Ahtna communities, who produce intricate native arts and crafts. Visitors can find items such as hand-woven baskets, jewelry, hand-carved masks, and clan totem poles as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Yakutat

Yakutat’s cultural heritage is significant to understanding the town’s unique identity. It is important to respect the local customs and traditions when traveling to these indigenous communities. Visitors are encouraged to participate in cultural classes and engage with the people of the community respectfully.

Best Time to Visit Yakutat

The best time to visit Yakutat is during the summer months of June through August. This period offers the most daylight hours, warmer temperatures, and the opportunity to experience the region’s natural beauty at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yakutat

Do:

  • Respect the native tribes and their customs.
  • Dress warmly and prepare for harsh weather conditions.
  • Try the local seafood and beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in public places.
  • Approach wildlife or stray from marked hiking trails.
  • Ignore warning signs or alerts about dangerous weather or water conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yakutat

Emergency services in Yakutat are limited, so it is essential to plan and act responsibly throughout your visit. It is critical to be aware of the potential risks of outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing, and to follow appropriate guidelines and rules.

Health in Yakutat

Travelers to Yakutat should understand the potential health risks associated with outdoor activities. Proper equipment and safety measures are necessary for activities such as hiking, fishing, and kayaking.

Getting to Yakutat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yakutat

Yakutat airport is served by Alaska Airlines, which offers daily flights from Anchorage, Juneau, and Sitka. A small number of charter airlines also operate in Yakutat.

Bus Stations Near Yakutat

There are no bus stations in Yakutat or nearby Gustavus. Travelers can, however, hire a car to explore the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to travel to Yakutat via the Alaska Marine Highway, which operates ferry service to various ports along the coast and within the Inside Passage.

How to Get Around in Yakutat

Yakutat is small enough to explore on foot or bike in the summer months. Visitors can also opt to rent a car from an airport rental agency or use a taxi or shuttle service to get around.

Where to Go Next from Yakutat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

12. Juneau

The state capital of Juneau is a popular destination from Yakutat. Visitors can explore the city’s rich history, visit the Mendenhall Glacier, and go on whale watching tours.

13. Glacier Bay National Park

Located southwest of Yakutat, the Glacier Bay National Park offers wildlife, adventure, stunning scenery, and an opportunity to experience the power of glaciers firsthand.

Conclusion

With its unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, Yakutat provides a one-of-a-kind experience for visitors. From the Hubbard Glacier and Russell Fjord Wilderness Area to the Situk River State Recreation Site and Yakutat Tlingit Tribe Cultural Center, visitors can explore the community’s fascinating history and landscape.
