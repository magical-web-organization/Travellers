Introduction to Anchorage

Located in Southcentral Alaska, Anchorage is the largest city in the state and a gateway to the state’s stunning wilderness. Situated at the foot of the Chugach Mountains and on the shores of the Cook Inlet, the city is a blend of urban amenities and outdoor recreation, promising something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Anchorage

If you’re planning a trip to Anchorage, there are plenty of attractions worth visiting. Below is a list of some of the must-see places to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Anchorage Museum: The largest museum in Alaska, the Anchorage Museum features exhibits on the state’s art, history, and culture.
  • Alaska Native Heritage Center: Learn about Alaska’s indigenous people and their history through interactive exhibits, dance performances, and crafts at this cultural center.
  • Alaska Railroad Depot: A National Historic Landmark, the depot is an important part of Anchorage’s history and serves as a hub for train travel throughout the state.

Natural Attractions of Anchorage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chugach State Park: The state’s third-largest park, Chugach is a wilderness playground right outside Anchorage’s door, offering hiking, biking, and skiing trails, fishing, and camping.
  • Tony Knowles Coastal Trail: A beautiful 11-mile trail along the coast of Cook Inlet, perfect for biking, walking, or running.
  • Alaska Botanical Garden: This 110-acre garden showcases the native plants of Alaska, with gardens and trails for exploration.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anchorage

Anchorage prides itself on its outdoor recreation opportunities, and there are many trails available for hikers and bikers in and around the city.

  • Flattop Mountain Trail: A challenging hike to the peak of Flattop Mountain, offering panoramic views of the city and surrounding wilderness.
  • Kincaid Park Trails: Over 60 miles of trails for hiking, biking, skiing, and snowshoeing through this large park.
  • Eagle River Nature Center Trails: Beautiful hikes through the Eagle River Valley, with different trails for different skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Anchorage: Activities and Tours

Looking for unique things to do in Anchorage? Check out these activities and tours:

  • Dog Sledding Tours: Experience Alaska’s state sport with an exhilarating dog sledding tour of the wilderness.
  • Whale Watching Tours: Cruise the waters of Cook Inlet and see humpback whales, orcas, and other marine life.
  • Aurora Hunting Tours: Catch a glimpse of the northern lights on a guided tour outside the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Anchorage

Anchorage’s food scene is a mix of traditional Alaska Native cuisine, fresh seafood, and hearty pub fare.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild game: Moose, elk, and caribou are staples of Alaska cuisine.
  • Salmon: Fresh and plentiful, salmon is a must-try when visiting Anchorage.
  • Reindeer sausage: A local specialty, reindeer sausage is commonly served in breakfast dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Alaska craft beer: Locally brewed beer is a popular drink choice for locals and visitors alike.
  • Alaska cocktails: Try a cocktail featuring locally-distilled spirits.

Where to Stay in Anchorage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anchorage offers a variety of accommodations for every budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Downtown Anchorage Hotels

  • Hotel Captain Cook: A luxury hotel in the heart of downtown, with spectacular views of the city and nearby mountains.
  • Anchorage Marriott Downtown: A Hilton hotel, located within walking distance of downtown shopping and dining.

Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Bent Prop Inn and Hostel: A budget option for travelers looking for a hostel experience.
  • Arctic Adventure Hostel: Another hostel option, conveniently located near several popular hiking trails.

What to Buy in Anchorage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home from Anchorage? Check out these ideas:

  • Alaska Native artwork: From jewelry to carvings and paintings, you can find beautiful Alaska Native artwork throughout the city.
  • Wildlife souvenirs: Moose antlers, bear claws, and other animal souvenirs can be found in many gift shops.
  • Alaska smoked salmon: A delicious edible souvenir, perfect for bringing home as a gift or snack.

Local Culture and Customs of Anchorage

Alaska has a rich history and unique culture, including a deep connection to the state’s natural resources and remote wilderness areas.

Best Time to Visit Anchorage

The best time to visit Anchorage is during the summer months (May through August), when the weather is the warmest and the days are the longest. However, winter is also a great time to visit for winter sports and the chance to see the northern lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anchorage

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities: Hiking, biking, skiing, and other outdoor activities are a big part of the Anchorage lifestyle.
  • Learn about the local culture: Alaska Native culture and history are an important part of the state’s identity and well worth exploring.
  • Try the local cuisine: From wild game to fresh seafood, Alaska has a unique and delicious culinary scene.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather: Even in the summer months, temperatures can be cool and weather can change quickly.
  • Disrespect the wildlife: Anchorage is home to many wild animals, and it’s important to maintain a safe distance and respect their habitat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anchorage

While Anchorage is generally safe for tourists, it’s always important to take necessary precautions.

  • Watch out for wildlife: Moose and other animals can wander into urban areas, so be aware of your surroundings.
  • Be prepared for the weather: Alaska weather can be unpredictable, so pack accordingly.

Health in Anchorage

While Anchorage has high-quality medical facilities, it’s always a good idea to take certain health precautions when traveling.

  • Stay hydrated: The dry Alaskan air can leave you dehydrated, so drink plenty of water.
  • Avoid raw or undercooked seafood: While Alaska’s seafood is delicious, consume it only when it is fully cooked.

Getting to Anchorage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anchorage

  • Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport (ANC): The main airport serving the city, with flights from major US cities as well as several international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Anchorage

  • Anchorage Transit Center: Serves local and regional bus routes.

Other Options

  • Alaska Railroad: A scenic train ride that offers views of the state’s stunning wilderness.
  • Ferry: A unique way to see Alaska’s coastline, with service to several cities from Anchorage.

How to Get Around in Anchorage

Anchorage is a fairly easy city to navigate, with several transportation options.

  • Rental cars: Available at the airport and throughout the city.
  • Anchorage People Mover: The city’s public transit system, with several bus routes throughout the city.
  • Taxi and ride-sharing services: Both taxi and ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft are available in Anchorage.

Where to Go Next from Anchorage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve seen all Anchorage has to offer, there are several nearby destinations worth exploring, including:

  • Denali National Park and Preserve: A stunning national park with hiking trails, wildlife, and breathtaking views.
  • Kenai Peninsula: A popular destination for fishing, hiking, and other outdoor activities.
  • Seward: A charming coastal town with a busy harbor and access to Kenai Fjords National Park.

Conclusion

From outdoor adventure to cultural exploration, Anchorage promises a wealth of experiences for travelers. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide and discover everything Alaska’s largest city has to offer.
