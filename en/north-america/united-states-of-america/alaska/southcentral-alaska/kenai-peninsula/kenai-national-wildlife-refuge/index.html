Introduction to Kenai National Wildlife Refuge

Kenai National Wildlife Refuge is one of the most beautiful and ecologically diverse wildlife refuges in the world. It covers over 2 million acres of pristine Alaskan wilderness, including rugged mountain ranges, vast tundra, and forests. The refuge is home to a wide variety of wildlife that includes grizzly bears, wolves, moose, and bald eagles. Visitors to the Kenai National Wildlife Refuge can explore the vast wilderness through various adventurous activities, including hiking, biking, and boating.

Visitors to the Kenai National Wildlife Refuge are advised to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some safety tips:
