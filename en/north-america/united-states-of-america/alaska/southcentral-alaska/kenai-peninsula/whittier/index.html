Introduction to Whittier

Nestled in the Kenai Peninsula of Southcentral Alaska, Whittier is a hidden gem that’s just waiting to be explored. This small but charming town is accessible only by boat or through the renowned Anton Anderson Memorial Tunnel, which takes visitors through the heart of a pristine mountain. Whittier is known for its untouched beauty and incredible natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or returning to Alaska, Whittier is a must-visit destination that will leave you in awe.

List of Places to Visit in Whittier

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Whittier is rich in history, and its museums and historical sites are a testament to that. Here are some places that you must visit to experience Whittier’s history:

  • Whittier Museum
  • Buckner Building
  • Whittier Harbor

Natural Attractions of Whittier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whittier’s natural beauty is unparalleled, and there are plenty of opportunities to explore the outdoors. From hiking and biking trails to scenic boat tours, Whittier has something for everyone. Don’t miss out on these natural attractions:

  • Portage Pass Trail
  • Portage Glacier
  • Prince William Sound

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whittier

Whittier is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and hiking and biking are two of the best ways to explore this beautiful town. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Portage Pass Trail
  • Blackstone Bay Trail
  • Begich Boggs Visitor Center Trail

Best Things to Do in Whittier: Activities and Tours

Whittier offers a range of activities that cater to all ages and interests. From boat tours to bird watching, here are some of the best things to do in Whittier:

  • Explore Prince William Sound on a glacier and wildlife cruise
  • Take in the views of the glaciers at Portage Glacier State Park
  • Watch the incredible wildlife at the Begich Boggs Visitor Center

Week-Long Itinerary for Whittier

Whittier has so much to offer, and a week-long trip is the perfect way to experience it all. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Whittier

Must-Try Dishes

Whittier may be a small town, but its food scene is not to be underestimated. Here are some of the must-try dishes to sample while in Whittier:

  • Halibut fish and chips at the Lazy Otter Cafe
  • Crab cakes at the Varly’s Swiftwater Seafood Cafe
  • Fresh Alaskan salmon at the Inn at Whittier Restaurant

What to Buy in Whittier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Whittier

Whittier has a small but close-knit community that is proud of the town’s history and culture. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind as you explore Whittier:

  • Respect for nature and natural resources is strongly emphasized in Whittier.
  • Hunting and fishing are central to the local culture, but visitors should be aware of local regulations and conservation efforts.
  • The town has a strong military history and a significant presence of active-duty and retired military personnel.

Best Time to Visit Whittier

The best time to visit Whittier is during the summer months between May and September when the weather is milder and the natural attractions are in full bloom. However, the winter months between October and April offer skiing and snowboarding opportunities for the adventurous traveler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whittier

Do:

  • Respect the natural resources and wildlife in Whittier.
  • Follow local customs and regulations, especially in regards to hunting and fishing.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and tours that Whittier has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm natural resources in any way.
  • Ignore local regulations and customs.
  • Underestimate the weather in Whittier.

Safety and Health

Safety in Whittier

Whittier is generally a safe place to visit with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities and natural disasters such as earthquakes.

Health in Whittier

Whittier has limited healthcare facilities, and visitors are advised to carry adequate medical insurance and take precautions to avoid health risks such as dehydration and hypothermia.

Getting to Whittier: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Whittier

The nearest major airport to Whittier is Anchorage International Airport, which is approximately 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Whittier

Whittier is accessible by bus from Anchorage, which takes about 1.5 hours.

Other Options

Whittier is also accessible by boat from Valdez and Cordova.

How to Get Around in Whittier

Whittier is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and shuttle services are also available for visitors who need transportation.

Where to Go Next from Whittier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Whittier is truly a hidden gem that offers visitors the opportunity to explore Alaska’s untouched beauty and rich cultural heritage.
