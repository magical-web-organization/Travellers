Introduction to Valdez

Valdez is a small coastal city nestled in the Chugach Mountains in Southcentral Alaska. Known for its stunning natural surroundings, Valdez attracts outdoor enthusiasts from around the world. With a population of just over 4,000 residents, Valdez is a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of larger cities.

List of Places to Visit in Valdez

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Valdez has a rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore in the city. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • The Valdez Museum and Historical Archive: This museum chronicles the history of Valdez, from the founding of the city to the 1964 earthquake and beyond.
  • The Old Town Valdez Site: Located on Galena Drive, this site was once the location of the original Valdez settlement before it was destroyed in the 1964 earthquake. Visitors can explore the remnants of the town, which include old buildings and a cemetery.
  • Prince William Sound Regional Citizens’ Advisory Council: This council is dedicated to promoting safe shipping practices and protecting the environment in Prince William Sound. Visitors can learn about their initiatives and the Exxon Valdez oil spill of 1989, which had a significant impact on the region.
  • Maxine & Jesse Whitney Museum: Located on the campus of Prince William Sound College, this museum showcases the work of Maxine and Jesse Whitney, two Alaskan artists who captured the beauty of the state through their paintings and drawings.

Natural Attractions of Valdez: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Valdez is a haven for nature lovers, with outdoor activities and scenic views aplenty. Some of the top natural attractions in Valdez include:

  • Worthington Glacier: This stunning glacier is located just a short drive from Valdez and is a must-visit for anyone who is interested in glaciers and ice fields. Visitors can hike to the glacier and even go ice climbing on its walls.
  • Prince William Sound: This sound is home to a variety of marine life, including whales, sea otters, and seals. Visitors can take a scenic boat tour to see these animals up close and learn about the ecosystem of the sound.
  • Keystone Canyon: This canyon is home to a number of picturesque waterfalls, including Bridal Veil Falls and Horsetail Falls. Visitors can hike to the waterfalls and take in the stunning views along the way.
  • Valdez Glacier Lake: This glacial lake is a popular spot for kayaking and canoeing. Visitors can paddle around the lake and take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valdez

Valdez is a hiking and biking paradise, with trails for all skill levels. Some of the best routes include:

  • Mineral Creek Trail: This easy trail is just over 2 miles long and is perfect for families with children. The trail follows Mineral Creek and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Solomon Gulch Trail: This moderate trail is just over 4 miles long and takes hikers along the coast of Prince William Sound. Along the way, visitors can see salmon spawning in Solomon Gulch Creek.
  • Shoup Bay Trail: This difficult trail is over 5 miles long and offers challenging terrain for experienced hikers. The trail takes visitors to Shoup Glacier and offers stunning views of the glacier and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Valdez: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Valdez, whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation. Some of the top activities and tours to check out include:

  • Helicopter Tours: Visitors can take a helicopter tour of the surrounding mountains and glaciers, offering a bird’s eye view of the stunning landscape of Valdez.
  • Day Cruises: Visitors can take a day cruise along the coast of Prince William Sound, offering the chance to see whales, seals, sea otters, and other marine life up close.
  • Salmon Fishing: Valdez is known for its salmon fishing, and visitors can join a guided fishing tour to try their hand at catching their own dinner.
  • Snowboarding and Skiing: Valdez has plenty of snowfall during the winter months, making it a popular destination for snowboarders and skiers looking for a challenging and scenic experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valdez

Day 1: Explore the Valdez Museum and Historical Archive and the Old Town Valdez Site. Day 2: Take a helicopter tour of the surrounding mountains and glaciers and have lunch at a local restaurant. Day 3: Hike the Solomon Gulch Trail and have dinner at one of Valdez’s seafood restaurants. Day 4: Take a day cruise along the coast of Prince William Sound, then have dinner at a local brewery. Day 5: Go salmon fishing in the morning and spend the afternoon kayaking on Valdez Glacier Lake. Day 6: Take a scenic drive to Worthington Glacier and explore the area on foot. Day 7: Go snowboarding or skiing at the Alyeska Resort and have dinner at a local steakhouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Valdez

Must-Try Dishes

Valdez is known for its fresh seafood, and visitors shouldn’t leave without trying some of these local dishes:

  • Halibut: This white fish is a staple of Alaskan cuisine and can be served in a variety of ways, including grilled, seared, or fried.
  • Crab Legs: Alaskan king crab legs are a delicacy, and visitors can find them on menus at local restaurants.
  • Salmon: Fresh salmon can be found on menus throughout Valdez, and visitors shouldn’t miss the chance to try it.

Local Beverages

Valdez has a burgeoning craft brewery scene, with local breweries offering a variety of unique and refreshing beers. Visitors can also try Alaskan wines and spirits, including birch syrup and honey vodka.

Where to Stay in Valdez: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valdez has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Some top options include:

  • The Best Western Valdez Harbor Inn: This hotel is located in the heart of Valdez and offers stunning views of the harbor.
  • The Totem Inn: This hotel is located just outside of Valdez and is perfect for visitors who want to explore the surrounding wilderness.
  • The Valdez Adventure Lodge: This lodge offers a range of accommodations, from private cabins to hostel-style rooms, and is popular with outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Valdez: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Valdez can bring home a variety of unique and locally made souvenirs, including:

  • Alaskan Native art and jewelry: Valdez has a number of art galleries and shops that sell authentic Alaskan Native art and jewelry.
  • Local foods and beverages: Visitors can bring home a taste of Valdez by purchasing locally made beers, wines, and spirits, as well as Alaskan smoked salmon and other seafood products.
  • Outdoor gear: With so many outdoor activities in Valdez, visitors may want to pick up some gear or clothing to help them make the most of their adventures.

Local Culture and Customs of Valdez

Valdez has a proud history and culture, and visitors can learn more about it by attending events and festivals throughout the year. Some popular events include:

  • The Valdez Gold Rush Days: This festival celebrates Valdez’s mining history and includes a parade, live music, and other family-friendly activities.
  • The Last Frontier Theatre Conference: This conference, held annually in Valdez, celebrates playwrights and theatrical performances from around the world.
  • The Valdez Fish Derbies: These derbies, held throughout the year, celebrate the tradition of fishing in Valdez and offer visitors the chance to participate in the fun.

Best Time to Visit Valdez

The best time to visit Valdez depends on your interests and travel style. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and adventures, the summer months (June to August) offer plenty of daylight and mild temperatures. If you’re interested in winter sports, the winter months (December to February) offer plenty of snowfall and opportunities for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valdez

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the stunning natural surroundings and participate in outdoor activities.
  • Do try locally made foods and beverages, including fresh seafood and Alaskan craft beer.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs, including attending festivals and events throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather in Valdez, which can be unpredictable and change quickly.
  • Don’t litter or harm the environment, which is so important to the Valdez way of life.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities, as conditions can be challenging.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valdez

Valdez is a safe city, but visitors should take precautions when participating in outdoor activities. This includes:

  • Checking weather conditions before heading out on hikes or other outdoor expeditions.
  • Telling someone where you’re going and when you expect to return.
  • Carrying proper safety gear, such as bear spray and a first-aid kit.

Health in Valdez

Valdez has a small medical center, but visitors with serious health concerns should seek medical attention in Anchorage. It’s also important to be prepared for the weather, which can be cold and unpredictable, by layering clothing and bringing appropriate gear.

Getting to Valdez: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Valdez can reach the city by car, bus, or plane. Some transportation options include:

Airports Near Valdez

  • Valdez Airport: This small airport offers flights from Anchorage and other surrounding areas.

Bus Stations Near Valdez

  • Alaska Railroad: The Alaska Railroad offers trains from Anchorage to the port town of Whittier, where visitors can take a ferry or shuttle to Valdez.
  • The Alaska Marine Highway: The Alaska Marine Highway offers ferry service to Valdez from other ports in Alaska.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Visitors can rent a car in Anchorage and drive to Valdez, which takes approximately 5 hours.

How to Get Around in Valdez

Valdez is a small town and walking or biking are great ways to get around. Taxis are also available, and some accommodations offer shuttle services or bike rentals for guests.

Where to Go Next from Valdez: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Valdez is located near a number of other popular destinations in Alaska, including:

  • Anchorage: Alaska’s largest city is just a 5-hour drive from Valdez and offers a range of cultural and outdoor activities.
  • Denali National Park: This park is home to North America’s tallest mountain and is a popular destination for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts.
  • Fairbanks: This northern city is known for its aurora borealis viewing opportunities and unique cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Valdez is a stunning and unique destination for travelers looking to experience Alaska’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. From hiking and skiing to fishing and exploring museums, there are plenty of activities to keep visitors entertained. With this comprehensive guide, visitors to Valdez can plan their trip with confidence and make the most of their time in this beautiful part of Alaska.
