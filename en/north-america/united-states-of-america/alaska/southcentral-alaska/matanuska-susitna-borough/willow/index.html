Introduction to Willow

Located in Matanuska-Susitna Borough in Southcentral Alaska, Willow is a small town that offers stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. From hiking trails to historical sites, there are plenty of things to see and do in this charming destination. In this travel guide, we’ve highlighted the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Willow.

List of Places to Visit in Willow

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Willow Community Center and Library

The Willow Community Center and Library is the perfect place to learn about the history and culture of Willow. It has exhibits on local wildlife, Native Alaskan traditions, and the history of the town. The library also has a wide selection of books on Alaska’s history, culture, and nature.

Talkeetna Ranger Station

The Talkeetna Ranger Station is where mountaineers and hikers check-in before heading to Denali National Park. Inside, you’ll find information about climbing and hiking permits, safety tips, maps and souvenirs.

Nancy Lake State Recreation Area

Located just west of Willow, the Nancy Lake State Recreation Area offers an array of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and canoeing. Wander around the park and discover the beauty of Alaska’s vast wilderness.

Natural Attractions of Willow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hatcher Pass

Hatcher Pass boasts magnificent views of glaciers, mountains, and valleys, and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Take a hike on one of the trails and witness the incredible natural beauty of Alaska.

Hurricane Turn Train

The train between Fairbanks and Talkeetna is one of Alaska’s most scenic routes. The Hurricane Turn Train runs on a “flag-stop” system, so be sure to wave at the conductor if you need to get on or off.

Willow Swamp Trail

Willow Swamp Trail is a great place to explore Alaska’s flora and fauna. It is accessible for hikers of all skill levels, and the trail is well-maintained.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Willow

Hatcher Pass

Hatcher Pass provides endless opportunities for hikers and mountain bikers. It has trails for all skill levels, so whether you’re a beginner or an advanced hiker or biker, you’ll find a trail that suits you.

Pioneer Ridge Trail

The Pioneer Ridge Trail offers stunning views of the Matanuska Valley, Knik Glacier, and the Chugach Mountains. It is a challenging hike, but the view from the top is worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Willow: Activities and Tours

Anchorage City Tour

An Anchorage city tour is a great way to explore all that this city has to offer. You’ll see landmarks like the Tony Knowles Coastal Trail, Alaska Native Heritage Center, and Anchorage Museum.

Northern Lights Viewing

The Northern Lights are a beautiful natural phenomenon that can be viewed in Willow during the winter months. Take a Northern Lights viewing tour and witness this incredible spectacle for yourself.

Food and Drink Scene of Willow

Must-Try Dishes

Salmon

Alaska is known for its wild-caught salmon, and Willow is no exception. Sample this delicious fish in a variety of dishes.

Reindeer Sausage

Reindeer Sausage is a local delicacy that can be found in restaurants across Willow and the surrounding areas. It’s a must-try for foodies.

Local Beverages

Alaskan Beer

Alaska has a vibrant craft beer scene, and many local breweries can be found in the Willow area. Sample a range of delicious brews made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Willow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alaska Creekside Cabins

Alaska Creekside Cabins offers cozy cabins with everything you need for a comfortable stay. Each cabin comes with a kitchenette, private bathroom, and stunning views.

Willow Creek Bed and Breakfast

Willow Creek Bed and Breakfast is a charming place to stay in Willow. It is located near the parks and hiking trails, and offers comfortable rooms with private bathrooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Willow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alaskan Crafts

Alaskan crafts are a popular souvenir of Willow. Look for items made from natural materials, such as birch bark or caribou antler, or pick up a piece of artwork created by a local artist.

Local Culture and Customs of Willow

Alaska has a rich Native Alaskan culture, and visitors to Willow can learn more about it at the Willow Community Center and Library. It is important to respect the local culture and practices.

Best Time to Visit Willow

The best time to visit Willow is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is warmer and the days are longer. However, if you’re interested in viewing the Northern Lights, winter is the best time to go.

Do’s and Don’ts of Willow

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and the activity.
  • Take advantage of the natural beauty of Willow.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disturb the wildlife or natural habitats.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Willow

When visiting Willow, it is important to be aware of potential hazards, such as wildlife encounters, challenging hiking trails, and unpredictable weather conditions. Always be prepared and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Willow

While traveling to Willow, it is important to consider the elements of the environment, such as higher altitude or colder weather, to prevent altitude sickness, hypothermia, and frostbite. Bring appropriate clothing and take care of your physical activity.

Getting to Willow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Willow

The Anchorage International Airport is the closest airport to Willow, located about 80 miles south. There are car rental and taxi service available from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Willow

Matanuska-Susitna Borough bus service presently does not operate in Willow currently. The nearest bus station is in Anchorage.

Other Options

Driving is the easiest way to get around in Willow. A rental car is recommended as there is little public transportation within the town limits.

How to Get Around in Willow

The best way to get around Willow is by car, as there is little public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Willow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palmer

Palmer is a quaint town located just a short drive from Willow. It is famous for its Matanuska Glacier, local arts scene, and abundance of outdoor recreational activities.

Denali National Park

Denali National Park is one of Alaska’s most famous attractions and is home to the tallest peak in North America, Mount Denali. The park offers stunning natural beauty, hiking trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Willow, Alaska, offers an exciting mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and natural wonders. This comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your visit by highlighting the best things to see and do, where to stay and eat, and important safety and cultural considerations.
