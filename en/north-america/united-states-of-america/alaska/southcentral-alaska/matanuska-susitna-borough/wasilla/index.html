Introduction to Wasilla

Wasilla is a charming town located in the Matanuska-Susitna Borough of Southcentral Alaska. It is surrounded by breathtaking mountain ranges, pristine lakes, and stunning glaciers. The town is best known for its rich history, interesting culture, and a wide range of outdoor activities, making it an ideal destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Wasilla

Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Wasilla has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained. Here are some of the top places to visit in Wasilla:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iditarod Trail Sled Dog Race Headquarters and Museum: Learn about the history and culture of the famous Iditarod Trail Sled Dog Race, which is held annually in the town.
  • Alaska Museum of Transportation and Industry: Explore the history of transportation in Alaska through the wide range of exhibits.
  • Wasilla Museum and Visitors Center: Discover the fascinating history and culture of Wasilla through the various exhibits and displays.

Natural Attractions of Wasilla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hatcher Pass: Enjoy scenic views of the surrounding mountain ranges and valleys while hiking or biking in this beautiful area.
  • Nancy Lake State Recreation Area: Admire the natural beauty of the region through various outdoor activities, such as fishing, kayaking, and hiking.
  • Matanuska Glacier: See the stunning Matanuska Glacier up close and explore the surrounding area through hiking or glacier trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wasilla

Wasilla has a wide range of hiking and biking trails, catering to different levels of expertise. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Pioneer Ridge Trail: A 7-mile round trip trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers.
  • Bodenburg Butte Trail: A 2.5-mile round trip trail that offers panoramic views of the valley and surrounding mountains.
  • Palmer-Moose Creek Trail: A 12-mile round trip trail that runs along the scenic Matanuska River.

Best Things to Do in Wasilla: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Wasilla:

  • Dog sledding tour: Experience the thrill of riding a sled pulled by a team of huskies through the snow-covered terrain.
  • Glacier trekking tour: Explore the stunning Matanuska Glacier up close through a guided trekking tour.
  • Fishing tour: Go on a fishing tour and catch salmon, halibut, trout, and other popular species.

Food and Drink Scene of Wasilla

Wasilla has a burgeoning food and drink scene that is a testament to the region’s diverse culture and history. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salmon Bake: A popular dish made with grilled or baked salmon served with a variety of sides.
  • Reindeer sausage: A local specialty made from reindeer meat that is served in various forms, from hot dogs to burgers.
  • Fried halibut: A seasoned and battered halibut fillet, deep-fried to golden brown.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan Brewing Company Beers: A wide range of beers made by the Alaskan Brewing Company, including the famous Alaskan Amber and the Alaskan Summer Ale.
  • Kassik’s Brewery Beers: A range of beers made using traditional Alaskan ingredients such as birch sap.
  • Alaskan root beer: A non-alcoholic soda made using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Wasilla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wasilla has a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Some of the popular options include:

  • Grand View Inn and Suites: A luxurious hotel offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Bent Prop Inn and Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable rooms and common areas.
  • Pioneer Ridge Bed and Breakfast: A charming guesthouse that offers a personalized experience to its guests.

What to Buy in Wasilla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home from Wasilla:

  • Alaskan art and crafts: From locally made jewelry to hand-carved wooden figurines, Wasilla has plenty of unique art and crafts to bring home.
  • Smoked salmon: A popular local delicacy that makes for a unique and tasty souvenir.
  • Alaskan honey, jams, and preserves: Made using locally sourced ingredients, these products offer a taste of Alaskan flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Wasilla

Wasilla has a rich culture and history that is reflected in its local customs and traditions. The residents of Wasilla are proud of their heritage and often celebrate it through cultural festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Wasilla

The best time to visit Wasilla is during the summer months of May to September when the weather is pleasant, and the daylight hours are long. During these months, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and glacier trekking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wasilla

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Wasilla:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions.
  • Carry bear spray and other necessary equipment for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural surroundings.
  • Approach wildlife under any circumstances.
  • Ignore warning signs and guidelines for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

While Wasilla is a safe destination, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Wasilla

  • Be aware of the presence of wildlife, including bears and moose.
  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions.
  • Carry bear spray and other necessary equipment for outdoor activities.

Health in Wasilla

  • Drink bottled or purified water.
  • Carry medication and any necessary medical supplies.
  • Get vaccinated for illnesses common in the region, such as tetanus and hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Wasilla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wasilla is located about an hour’s drive from Anchorage, Alaska. Here are some transportation options to reach Wasilla:

  • Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport: The nearest airport to Wasilla, located about 45 miles away.
  • Glenn Highway: A scenic drive that connects Anchorage to Wasilla.
  • Alaska Railroad: A convenient and comfortable way to reach Wasilla from Anchorage.

Airports Near Wasilla

  • Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport: Located about 45 miles from Wasilla.

Bus Stations Near Wasilla

  • Park’s Highway Express: Offers daily bus service between Anchorage and Wasilla.

Other Options

  • Drive: Wasilla is about an hour’s drive from Anchorage.
  • Alaska Railroad: Offers scenic train rides between Anchorage and Wasilla.

How to Get Around in Wasilla

While Wasilla is a small town, visitors will need transportation to explore its many attractions. Some options include:

  • Rental cars: A convenient option for exploring the town and its surrounding areas.
  • Taxis: Available for short journeys within the town.
  • Buses: Offered by the Matanuska-Susitna Borough and serve various destinations in and around Wasilla.

Where to Go Next from Wasilla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Wasilla:

  • Anchorage: Alaska’s largest city, known for its vibrant culture and natural beauty.
  • Denali National Park: Home to the majestic Mount Denali and a diverse range of wildlife species.
  • Talkeetna: A charming town located at the base of Mount Denali, known for its authentic Alaskan experiences.

Conclusion

Wasilla is a charming town with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a wide range of outdoor activities and cultural attractions. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan a perfect itinerary for your visit to this unique Alaskan destination.
