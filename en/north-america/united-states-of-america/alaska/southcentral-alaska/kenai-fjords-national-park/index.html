Introduction to Kenai Fjords National Park

Nestled in Southcentral Alaska in the United States of America, Kenai Fjords National Park is a natural wonder. Covering over 669,984 acres of land and water, the park features stunning glaciers, snow-capped mountains, and abundant marine wildlife.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the captivating terrain of this rugged beauty. We’ll cover all the must-see attractions and share insider tips to help you make the most of your time at Kenai Fjords National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kenai Fjords National Park

If you’re looking to experience the great outdoors to its fullest, the hiking and biking trails are a must-try when exploring Kenai Fjords National Park.

  • Harding Icefield Trail (8.2 miles)
  • Exit Glacier Trail (1.8 miles)
  • Tonsina Point Trail (3.5 miles)
  • Caines Head Trail (4 miles)
  • Lost Lake Trail (15 miles)

Do’s and Don’ts of Kenai Fjords National Park

When visiting Kenai Fjords National Park, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and keep a safe distance.
  • Pack appropriate gear and clothing for the weather.
  • Follow the Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Approach wildlife too closely.
  • Disturb the natural habitat.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Kenai Fjords National Park, it’s essential to keep safety and health in mind.

Safety in Kenai Fjords National Park

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Learn about bear safety and precautions.
  • Carry bear spray with you when exploring.

Health in Kenai Fjords National Park

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.
  • Protect your skin from the sun and wear appropriate clothing.
  • Pack any necessary medication or medical equipment.
