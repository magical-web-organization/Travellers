Introduction to Glennallen

Glennallen is a charming small town nestled in the heart of Southcentral Alaska. Located at the confluence of the Copper and Klutina Rivers, Glennallen is a gateway to some of the most stunning natural landscapes in the world. The town is also home to a rich history and a vibrant local culture, making it a great place to explore and experience the real Alaska.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Glennallen. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, from local cuisine to accommodations, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Glennallen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Copper Valley Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Copper River Valley. The exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and documents from the area’s early days.
  • Ahtna Cultural Center: Learn about the history and traditions of the Ahtna people, one of the Native Alaskan tribes. The center features exhibits, demonstrations, and cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Glennallen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wrangell-St. Elias National Park: The largest national park in the U.S., Wrangell-St. Elias is a wilderness wonderland of glaciers, mountains, and wildlife. The park offers hiking trails, kayaking, fishing, and much more.
  • Copper River Delta: This massive delta offers a unique habitat for migratory birds and local wildlife. The area is also great for hiking and fishing.
  • Keystone Canyon: This stunning canyon features waterfalls, wildlife, and breathtaking views. The area is great for hiking and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glennallen

  • Liberty Falls Trail: This is an easy 1.5-mile hike with stunning views of Liberty Falls. The trail is perfect for families and beginner hikers.
  • The Goat Trail: This challenging 4.3-mile hike offers breathtaking views of the Wrangell Mountains. The trail is best for experienced hikers.
  • Cordova Glacier Trail: This 5-mile hike takes you to the top of Cordova Glacier for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. The trail is best for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Glennallen: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Glennallen is a great place for fishing, with the Copper River being one of the best salmon fishing spots in Alaska. You can also go fly fishing or ice fishing in the winter.
  • Glacier Tours: Take a guided tour to explore the stunning glaciers of the Wrangell-St. Elias National Park.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the Copper River Valley on horseback with local guides.

Food and Drink Scene of Glennallen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alaskan King Crab: This local delicacy is a must-try when in Alaska. The crab is sweet, succulent, and abundant in the waters around Glennallen.
  • Reindeer Sausage: Try this unique sausage made from locally sourced reindeer meat. It’s a staple of Alaskan cuisine.
  • Wild Berries: Alaska is home to a variety of wild berries, including blueberries, raspberries, and salmonberries. Try some fresh berries or enjoy them in jams and pies.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan Beer: Alaska has a thriving craft beer scene, and many of the breweries source their ingredients locally. Try a local IPA or porter.
  • Glacier Ice Cocktail: This refreshing cocktail is made with glacier ice, fresh lemon juice, and vodka. Enjoy it at one of Glennallen’s local bars.

Where to Stay in Glennallen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Old Town Copper Center Inn: This historic inn has been welcoming guests since 1943. The rooms are cozy and comfortable, and the on-site restaurant serves delicious local cuisine.
  • Copper River Princess Wilderness Lodge: This lodge is located in the heart of Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The lodge offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and guided tours.
  • Birch Grove Guest House: This guesthouse is located in a peaceful neighborhood and offers affordable accommodations with a shared kitchen and living area.

What to Buy in Glennallen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native Alaskan Art: The Ahtna people are known for their beautiful handmade baskets and beadwork. Visit local shops and galleries to purchase unique Alaskan art.
  • Local Honey: Alaskan honey is unique and delicious, thanks to the abundance of wildflowers in the area. Purchase some local honey to take home or give as a gift.
  • Birch Syrup: This unique syrup is made from the sap of birch trees and has a sweet, smoky flavor. Purchase some local birch syrup to drizzle over pancakes or waffles.

Local Culture and Customs of Glennallen

Glennallen is home to the Ahtna people, one of the Native Alaskan tribes. The Ahtna people have a rich history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn and respect their traditions. When visiting Glennallen, it’s important to be mindful of the environment and to practice Leave No Trace principles.

Best Time to Visit Glennallen

The best time to visit Glennallen is during the summer months, from mid-May to mid-September. This is when the weather is best for outdoor activities, and many of the local attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glennallen

Do:

  • Respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Learn about and respect the traditions of the Ahtna people.
  • Practice Leave No Trace principles when exploring the wilderness.

Don’t:

  • Approach wildlife too closely.
  • Ignore trail closures or warnings.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Glennallen

Glennallen is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the wilderness. Always be aware of your surroundings and the potential risks, such as wildlife encounters or changing weather conditions.

Health in Glennallen

Glennallen has a small medical clinic and pharmacy, but visitors should come prepared with any necessary medications and medical supplies. It’s also important to drink plenty of water and use sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Glennallen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Glennallen

  • Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport: This is the closest major airport to Glennallen, located about 3 hours away by car.
  • Gulkana Airport: This small regional airport is located about 20 minutes from Glennallen and offers limited commercial flights.

Bus Stations Near Glennallen

  • Copper Valley Transit: This local bus service offers transportation within the Copper River Valley, including Glennallen, Valdez, and Paxson.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the area at your own pace.
  • Private Shuttles: Private shuttles offer transportation to and from Glennallen and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Glennallen

Glennallen is a small town and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. However, a car or private shuttle is necessary for exploring the surrounding areas and national parks.

Where to Go Next from Glennallen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Valdez: This coastal town is located about 90 minutes from Glennallen and offers stunning views of glaciers and wildlife.
  • Denali National Park: This world-famous national park is located about 4 hours from Glennallen and offers breathtaking views of the highest peak in North America.
  • Anchorage: Alaska’s largest city is located about 3 hours from Glennallen and offers a wealth of cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Glennallen is a hidden gem in the heart of Southcentral Alaska. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant local culture, it’s a great place to experience the real Alaska. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Glennallen and make unforgettable memories in one of the most stunning destinations in the world.
