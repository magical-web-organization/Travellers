Introduction to Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is a scenic wilderness area located in southwestern Alaska. The park was established in 1980 to protect its diverse ecosystems, including the rugged Chigmit Mountains, glaciers, forests, tundra, and lakes. Lake Clark covers approximately 4 million acres, and it is among the least visited national parks in the United States. As a result, visitors who make the trek to this remote destination are rewarded with solitude and the opportunity to experience authentic Alaskan wilderness.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Although Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is primarily focused on nature conservation, it is also home to several culturally significant attractions. For example, the park is home to several Native American tribes who have lived in the area for generations. Visitors can explore the cultural heritage of these tribes at the following museums and historical places:

  • Dena’ina Ełnena: This living history center showcases the culture, history, and arts of the Dena’ina people, who have inhabited Lake Clark for thousands of years.
  • Telaquana Trail: This historic trail was once used by the Dena’ina tribe to travel to Bristol Bay for fishing and trade.
  • Twin Lakes Cannery: This abandoned cannery offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of early 20th-century Alaskan fishermen.

Natural Attractions of Lake Clark National Park and Preserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The park offers a wide range of activities, including hiking, fishing, camping, boating, and wildlife viewing. Some of the most popular natural attractions in the park include:

  • Lake Clark: The park’s namesake body of water is a stunning turquoise lake that stretches for more than 40 miles. Visitors can explore the lake and its surrounding wilderness by boat, kayak, or floatplane.
  • Alagnak Wild River: This designated wild and scenic river flows through pristine wilderness and offers some of the best fishing in the park.
  • Telaquana Lake: This remote alpine lake is a popular destination for backpackers and hikers, offering breathtaking views and opportunities for fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

Lake Clark National Park and Preserve boasts over 100 miles of hiking trails, ranging from easy day hikes to multi-day backpacking trips. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the park include:

  • Tanalian Trail: This 7.5-mile trail leads hikers through forests and along the shoreline of Cook Inlet, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers.
  • Twin Lakes Trail: This 10-mile-long trail offers a moderately challenging hike through forested terrain, past several pristine lakes and beautiful waterfalls.
  • Chitna Pass Trail: This strenuous 32-mile trail takes hikers through some of the park’s most remote wilderness areas, offering stunning views of the Chigmit Mountains and opportunities for wildlife spotting.

Best Things to Do in Lake Clark National Park and Preserve: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and outdoor adventures, there are several must-do activities and tours in Lake Clark National Park and Preserve. Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie or a nature lover, there’s something for everyone in this spectacular wilderness area.

  • Bear Viewing Tours: Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is home to one of the largest populations of brown bears in the world. Visitors can take guided tours to observe these majestic creatures in their natural habitat.
  • Fishing: The park’s lakes and rivers offer some of the best fishing in Alaska, with opportunities to catch salmon, trout, and grayling.
  • Flightseeing Tours: Visitors can take scenic flights over the park to see its vast wilderness areas, towering mountains, and glaciers from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

To make the most of your visit to Lake Clark National Park and Preserve, consider the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive at Anchorage and take a domestic flight to Port Alsworth.

Day 2: Explore Port Alsworth, visit the Dena’ina Ełnena living history center and go on a guided fishing tour.

Day 3: Hike the Tanalian Trail, enjoy a picnic lunch on the beach, and observe wildlife along the way.

Day 4: Take a flightseeing tour over Lake Clark and the surrounding wilderness areas, enjoying bird’s eye views of glaciers, mountains, and wildlife.

Day 5: Go on a bear viewing tour to observe brown bears in their natural habitat, enjoying their playful antics from a safe distance.

Day 6: Explore the Alagnak Wild River by rafting or kayaking, taking in the stunning views and fishing for salmon and trout.

Day 7: Depart from Port Alsworth and return to Anchorage.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors to Lake Clark National Park and Preserve can enjoy a range of local Alaska cuisine, including:

  • Wild salmon: Served grilled, baked, smoked, or as sushi.
  • Halibut: This white, flaky fish is a local favorite, often served breaded and deep-fried.
  • Birch syrup: This unique syrup is made from birch tree sap and is a popular ingredient in local confections and baked goods.

Local Beverages

  • Alaskan beer: The state is home to several craft breweries, with offerings ranging from hoppy IPAs to rich stouts.
  • Wild berry cocktails: Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is home to several wild berries, including blueberries, raspberries, and huckleberries, which are often used in cocktails and other drinks.

Where to Stay in Lake Clark National Park and Preserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is a remote wilderness area, with limited options for accommodation. Visitors can choose from a few hotels, guesthouses, and hostels in the nearby town of Port Alsworth, or opt for camping or cabin rentals in the park itself. Some popular accommodation options include:

  • Lake Clark Lodge: This rustic lodge offers comfortable guest rooms and cabins, as well as meals and guided tours.
  • Nondalton Lodge: This historic lodge offers basic rooms and cabins, as well as guided tours and fishing trips.
  • Campgrounds: There are several established campgrounds in the park, offering primitive campsites, pit toilets, and picnic tables.

What to Buy in Lake Clark National Park and Preserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Lake Clark National Park and Preserve can bring home a range of unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Handcrafted Alaska Native art: From intricate beadwork to carved masks and totems, there are several galleries in the park and nearby towns where visitors can purchase authentic Alaska Native art.
  • T-shirts and hats: Several park gift shops offer a range of T-shirts and hats featuring the park’s logo and wildlife.
  • Birch syrup and other local food products: Visitors can purchase birch syrup, jams, jellies, and other local food products from several shops in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is home to several Native American tribes, who have lived on the land for thousands of years. Visitors should respect their culture and customs, including:

  • Leave no trace: Visitors should pack out all trash and keep the park clean and pristine.
  • Respect wildlife: Visitors should keep at least 100 yards distance from bears and other wildlife, and never approach or feed them.
  • Respect private property: Some areas of the park are owned by private landowners, and visitors should respect their property and privacy.

Best Time to Visit Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

The best time to visit Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is during the summer months, from May to September. This is when the weather is most pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 50°F to 70°F and the park’s wildlife is most active. However, visitors should be aware that the summer months are also the most crowded and expensive.

Safety and Health

Lake Clark National Park and Preserve is a remote wilderness area, and visitors should take appropriate safety precautions to avoid accidents and emergencies. This includes:

  • Carry bear spray: Visitors hiking or camping in bear country should carry and know how to use bear spray.
  • Avoid solo hiking: Visitors should avoid hiking alone, especially in remote areas of the park.
  • Be aware of weather conditions: The weather in Lake Clark National Park and Preserve can be unpredictable and severe, and visitors should check weather forecasts and be prepared for extreme conditions.

Health in Lake Clark National Park and Preserve

Visitors to Lake Clark National Park and Preserve should take appropriate health precautions, including:

Getting to Lake Clark National Park and Preserve: Transportation Options and Logistics
