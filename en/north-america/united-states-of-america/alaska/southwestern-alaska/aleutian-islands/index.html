Introduction to Aleutian Islands

The Aleutian Islands are a chain of islands located in southwestern Alaska that stretch over 1,200 miles. The islands are home to a diverse range of wildlife, including sea otters, whales, and numerous bird species. The region’s unique landscape, rich history, and local culture make it an ideal destination for adventurous travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Aleutian Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aleutian World War II Visitor Center: This museum provides insight into the Aleutian Campaign during World War II, which is integral to the history of the region.
  • Unalaska Museum of the Aleutians: Learn about the history of the Aleutian Islands, including the native culture and Russian influence.
  • Holy Ascension Cathedral: This historic Russian Orthodox church was built in 1825 and is considered to be one of the most important landmarks in the region.

Natural Attractions of Aleutian Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bering Sea: Take a boat tour to spot whales, sea lions, seals, puffins, and other sea birds that call the region home.
  • Mount Ballyhoo: Hike to the top of this mountain for stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Shishaldin Volcano: This active volcano is one of the most picturesque attractions in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aleutian Islands

The Aleutian Islands offer a range of hiking and biking trails, suitable for all skill levels. The following are some popular routes:

  • Bogoslof Island: This volcanic island offers a challenging hike that rewards visitors with stunning views of the surrounding sea.
  • Dutch Harbor: This area offers several easy-to-moderate hikes, including trails to scenic vistas and abandoned World War II bunkers.
  • Atka Island: Explore this remote island’s unique volcanic landscape on a guided hiking tour.

Best Things to Do in Aleutian Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Unalaska – Akutan Ferry Tour: Take a ferry ride between Unalaska and Akutan, where you’ll be able to spot whales and sea lions.
  • Russian Heritage Tour: Experience the region’s Russian heritage by exploring historic landmarks and cultural sites.
  • Bering Sea Crab Fishermen’s Tour: Go behind the scenes of one of the most dangerous and lucrative jobs in the world by joining a Bering Sea crab fishing tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Aleutian Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bering Sea King Crab: The Aleutian Islands are home to some of the world’s most luscious king crab.
  • Salmon: Freshly caught salmon is a staple of the Aleutian Islands, and there are numerous preparations to try.
  • Reindeer Sausage: A common snack food in the region, reindeer sausage is a dish that’s uniquely Alaskan.

Local Beverages

  • Kodiak Island Brewing Company: This company brews popular local beers, including “The Lazy Otter” and “Brother Francis ESB”.
  • Alaskan Brewing Company: Try the “Alaskan Amber Ale,” which is known for its unique blend of malt and hops.

Where to Stay in Aleutian Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Aleutian Hotel: Located in Unalaska, this hotel is a popular choice for travelers. It offers comfortable rooms and stunning sea views.
  • Island House: This charming guesthouse is located in Dutch Harbor and offers cozy rooms with a homely feel.
  • Eagle Bay Inn: Located in Unalaska, this inn offers private rooms, a communal kitchen, and shared bathrooms.

What to Buy in Aleutian Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bering Sea Crab: Bring home fresh, local crab that’s been caught in the region’s waters.
  • Eskimo Art: The Aleutian Islands are home to several talented artists and craftspeople who make intricate carvings, masks, and other pieces.
  • Local Honey: The Aleutian Islands are home to several beekeepers who have been producing delicious, high-quality honey for years.

Local Culture and Customs of Aleutian Islands

The Aleutian Islands are home to a rich and diverse culture, shaped by both indigenous and Russian influences. Visitors should respect the traditions and customs of the local people, which include fishing, hunting, and subsistence living.

Best Time to Visit Aleutian Islands

The Aleutian Islands are accessible year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is milder.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aleutian Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress in layers, as the weather can change quickly.
  • Bring appropriate outdoor gear, including raincoats and hiking boots.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disturb the local wildlife.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public areas.
  • Don’t take souvenirs that are protected by law, such as eagle feathers and sea otter fur.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aleutian Islands

Travelers should be aware of the potentially hazardous weather conditions in the Aleutian Islands and should take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Aleutian Islands

Visitors should bring any necessary medication and be prepared for the lack of medical facilities in the region. It’s also important to avoid drinking untreated or unfiltered water.

Getting to Aleutian Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aleutian Islands

  • Unalaska Airport: Located on Amaknak Island, this airport is the main point of entry for travelers to the Aleutian Islands.
  • Adak Airport: Located on Adak Island, this airport offers flights to and from Anchorage.

Bus Stations Near Aleutian Islands

There are no bus stations in the Aleutian Islands as the islands are only accessible by boat or plane.

Other Options

  • Ferry: The Alaska Marine Highway provides service from Homer to Unalaska.
  • Chartered Flight: Multiple companies offer chartered flights to the Aleutian Islands.

How to Get Around in Aleutian Islands

The best way to get around the Aleutian Islands is by boat or plane. There are no roads that connect the islands, so visitors will need to rely on water or air transportation.

Where to Go Next from Aleutian Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kodiak Island: Travel to Kodiak Island to experience more of Alaska’s unique natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Kenai Peninsula: Explore the Kenai Peninsula’s glaciers, mountains, and wildlife.
  • Anchorage: Visit Anchorage to experience Alaska’s urban side and to explore nearby natural attractions.

Conclusion

The Aleutian Islands offer a unique travel experience that can’t be found anywhere else in the world. From its rich history to its stunning natural beauty, the region is sure to captivate the hearts of adventurous travelers. Plan your trip today and experience all that the Aleutian Islands have to offer.
