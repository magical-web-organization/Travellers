Introduction to Unalaska

Unalaska is a beautiful destination located in the Aleutian Islands in southwestern Alaska. It is a perfect destination for those who love outdoor adventures, scenic beauty and cultural attractions. The city of Unalaska is home to approximately 4,000 people and is situated on the island of Unalaska, which is part of the larger Aleutian Islands. This comprehensive tour guide will help you explore the best places, things to do, local culture and customs, where to stay, what to buy, and more in Unalaska.

List of Places to Visit in Unalaska

Unalaska has a variety of attractions no matter what your interests are. Among the most popular are its culturally important attractions, museums, historical places, natural attractions, outdoor adventure sports, scenic landscapes, hiking and biking trails, activities and tours. Here are the top attractions you shouldn’t miss during your visit to Unalaska:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Aleutians: This museum showcases the history of the Aleutian Islands. Exhibitions include artifacts, photographs, and art.

  • Aleutian World War II Visitor Center: Learn about the Aleutian campaign of World War II and see exhibits such as warplanes, artifacts, and more.

  • Russian Orthodox Church of the Holy Ascension: The oldest standing Russian Orthodox Church in America, dating back to 1825. It is a notable feature of the skyline and a testament to Unalaska’s heritage

Natural Attractions of Unalaska: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bunker Hill: Climb this hill at the eastern end of Unalaska Bay for incredible panoramic views of Dutch Harbor and the surrounding islands.

  • Kelty Beach: A great spot for beachcombing or watching vessels come and go at the port.

  • Across-the-Way Productions: Where you can book a tour to encounter the scenic beauty and wildlife of the Aleutians.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Unalaska

  • Pyramid Mountain: A short hike with rewarding views over the city.

  • Morris Cove Trail: This 3.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the harbor, surrounding mountains, and rolling green hills.

  • Mount Ballyhoo: A 1.5-mile hike with amazing views of Dutch Harbor and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Unalaska: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Unalaska is known for its world-renowned fishing opportunities such as salmon and halibut fishing.

  • Boat tours: Embark on an unforgettable sea adventure to watch whales, seals, and sea otters.

  • Bird Watching: Discover Unalaska’s unique and diverse bird inhabitants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Unalaska

Day 1: Arrive and Explore Unalaska City

  • Visit City Hall and the Russian Orthodox Church.
  • Eat at one of the local restaurants, and get to know the city’s residents.

Day 2: Discover History and Culture

  • Visit the Museum of the Aleutians and the World War II Visitor Center.
  • Learn about the Aleutian Campaign and the island’s rich history.

Day 3: Outdoor Adventure

  • Hike Pyramid Mountain or Mount Ballyhoo.
  • Explore Morris Cove Trail or take a boat tour around the islands.

Day 4: Wildlife Adventure and Beach Combing

  • Head out on a sea tour to watch marine life.
  • Head to Kelty Beach for some beachcombing.

Day 5: Fishing Adventure

  • Go on a fishing trip for salmon and halibut for an unforgettable experience

Day 6: Specific Local Culture and Customs

  • Participate in a native culture dance practice.
  • Try local cuisine like Aleutian seafood or reindeer sausage

Day 7: Departure

  • Spend a final day exploring the sights at your own leisure
  • Take the time to experience any missed or favorite activities

Food and Drink Scene of Unalaska

Unalaska has a thriving food and drink scene. The freshest seafood, like crab and salmon, is readily available here. You can enjoy many local delicacies with unique seasoning and preparation styles. Here are some popular must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Sausage: This gourmet sausage is a local specialty you can find at a lot of cafes and restaurants in Unalaska.

  • Seafood Chowder: Made with fresh local fish, seafood chowder is one of the most popular dishes in Unalaska. It is served in many cafes, restaurants, and eateries.

  • Halibut Tacos: Packed with fresh halibut, lettuce, cabbage, and toppings, this dish is a favorite of many locals and is becoming more common in restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Bering Sea Brew: This is an ale brewed in Unalaska and can find at local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Unalaska: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Unalaska has many different lodging options ranging from hotels and motels to hostels and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Grand Aleutian Hotel: This is one of the best places to stay in Unalaska for luxury and comfort. It is located in the heart of the city and offers great amenities.

  • Harbor View Inn: Another great option for comfortable accommodation with scenic views of the harbor.

  • City of Unalaska Lodge: A cozy and comfortable option that is popular among budget travellers.

What to Buy in Unalaska: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Unalaska offers many local souvenirs that you can take back home. Some popular items include:

  • Artifacts: You can find Aleutian artifacts such as dolls, carvings, and clothing in local shops.

  • Native art and crafts: Unalaska is home to many creative people who produce handmade crafts like baskets and earrings that make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Unalaska

Unalaska is home to the Unangan people, who have lived in the Aleutian Islands for thousands of years. Their rich history, culture, and traditions are prevalent throughout the island. The City of Unalaska has a strong connection with Russian history as well. You will get the chance to experience Native Culture and its highlights include unique traditions such as Aleutian Culture Center, Aleutian Music Experience, and Native dance.

Best Time to Visit Unalaska

Summer and Fall (June to November) are the best seasons to visit Unalaska. The weather is milder, and outdoor activities are more accessible than in the winter which is harsh and volatile. However, visitors should be prepared for the unpredictable weather conditions in the Aleutians.

Do’s and Don’ts of Unalaska

Do:

  • Respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Visit museums and cultural sites to learn about the city’s history and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t remove artifacts or disturb the wildlife.
  • Don’t enter private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Unalaska

Unalaska is a safe destination for visitors, however, like any place you have to be cautious and always be aware of your surrounding. Bear safety is a must if you go on any hiking trails in the area. Visitors should be aware of unpredictable weather patterns frequently occur in the Aleutian Islands, so plan accordingly.

Health in Unalaska

Like most remote islands in Alaska, medical resources in Unalaska are limited. If you need medical attention or emergency help, the best way is to call 911 or visit branch medical clinics. Make sure to bring basic medication and first aid kits if needed.

Getting to Unalaska: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Unalaska

  • Unalaska Airport (Dut Umnak Pass Airport) is located on the island of Unalaska, approximately 4 miles northeast of the city.

Other Options

  • Boat: You can access Unalaska by boat with the Alaska Marine Highway system.

How to Get Around in Unalaska

The best way to get around in Unalaska is by rental car, ride-sharing, or taxis. Bicycle rentals are also available. There are limited opportunities for walking in Unalaska due to the remote location and harsh weather conditions.

Where to Go Next from Unalaska: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Unalaska serves as a gateway to other destinations in the Aleutian Islands. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Adak island, 675 km southwest of Dutch Harbor. A unique military base and world-class bird watching location
  • Akutan, located approximately 35 km east of Unalaska, and hardly visited by tourists.

Conclusion

Unalaska is a unique destination offering visitors the opportunity to experience the rich history and culture of the Aleutian Islands. With an abundance of outdoor activities, beautiful landscapes, local cuisine, and comfortable lodging options, Unalaska is an experience that everyone should have on their bucket list.
