Introduction to Aniakchak National Monument

Aniakchak National Monument and Preserve is a remote, wild, and stunningly beautiful national park located in southwestern Alaska. The park is home to the Aniakchak volcano, one of the most active and least visited volcanoes in the world. Aniakchak National Monument offers visitors unparalleled opportunities for hiking, backpacking, fishing, and wildlife watching in a rugged and pristine wilderness setting.

Natural Attractions of Aniakchak National Monument: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aniakchak Caldera: A breathtaking volcanic crater made from collapsed magma chamber.
  • Surprise Lake: A stunning lake located at the bottom of the caldera offering panoramic views.
  • The Gates: A narrow and steep canyon with flowing river behind the lake.
  • The Ukak River: Popular for fly fishing for trout and salmon.
  • Tsuiokpak Lake: A remote lake only accessible by floatplane and offers great fishing and camping spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aniakchak National Monument

  • The Aniakchak River Trail: A 23 mile trail for hiking and backpacking through the Aniakchak caldera and the Alaska Peninsula.
  • The Ukak River Trail: A 13 mile trail for hiking and backpacking through lush rainforests and along the river.

Best Things to Do in Aniakchak National Monument: Activities and Tours

  • Hiking and Backpacking: Enjoy multiple trails with varying difficulty levels.
  • Fishing: Catch several freshwater fish species like trout, dolly varden, and salmon in the Ukak River and Surprise Creek.
  • Wildlife viewing: Spot Brown Bears, Moose, Caribou, Wolves, And Fox.

Local Culture and Customs of Aniakchak National Monument

  • The best way to show respect for the environment and the local culture is to follow Leave No Trace principles and carry out all trash.

Best Time to Visit Aniakchak National Monument

  • The best time to visit Aniakchak National Monument is during the summer months (June to August) when the park is accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aniakchak National Monument

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles
  • Pack appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities
  • Obtain appropriate fishing licenses

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife
  • Litter or leave trash behind
  • Venture into unmarked trails without a guide

Safety and Health

Safety in Aniakchak National Monument

  • There are no safety concerns except for the active volcano and it is recommended to follow guide recommendations and trail warnings.

Health in Aniakchak National Monument

  • It is recommended to bring your own first aid kit and recommended medications. There are no medical facilities in the park.
