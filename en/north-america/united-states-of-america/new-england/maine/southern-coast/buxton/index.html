Introduction to Buxton

Buxton is a picturesque town in southern Maine that attracts visitors with its scenic beauty and rich history. The town is a perfect getaway to explore the great outdoors. Its lush forests, pristine lakes, and winding streams offer ample opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and other outdoor activities. Moreover, Buxton’s museums and historical sites offer a glimpse into the town’s past, adding to its charm.

List of Places to Visit in Buxton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Narramissic Farm

Visiting the Narramissic Farm is like stepping into Maine’s past. This 19th-century farmstead is an ideal place to discover how the early settlers of the region lived and worked. The farm offers informative tours of the historic buildings, and visitors can explore the beautifully maintained grounds.

Buxton Historical Society

The Buxton Historical Society is a must-visit landmark for those interested in the history of Maine. The Society showcases artifacts and documents from Buxton’s past. It’s a great place to learn about the town’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Buxton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bradbury Mountain State Park

Bradbury Mountain State Park is a great place to spend the day hiking and exploring the outdoors. The park offers a variety of hiking trails, picnic areas, and opportunities for bird watching. Visitors can also enjoy the scenic views from the summit of Bradbury Mountain.

Saco River

The Saco River offers a wonderful opportunity to get outdoors and enjoy the water. Visitors can explore the river by canoe, kayak, or tubing. The river also has scenic views and is a great place for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buxton

Narragansett Trail

The Narragansett Trail is a popular hiking and biking trail in Buxton. The trail runs for eight miles through the beautiful forested areas of the region. It provides breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and is a leading spot for bird watching.

Buxton Community Trail

The Buxton Community Trail is another popular hiking and biking trail in Buxton. The 1.8-mile trail takes visitors through beautiful wooded areas and over streams, making it a perfect day hike for beginners and families.

Best Things to Do in Buxton: Activities and Tours

Fishing in Pleasant River

Pleasant River is a great spot for fishing. Visitors can catch a variety of fish, including brook trout, brown trout, and smallmouth bass. The river is also beautiful and offers a tranquil setting for fishing.

Wine Tasting at Oyster River Winegrowers

Oyster River Winegrowers is a gem hidden in Maine’s countryside. The winery provides a knowledgeable guide to help visitors taste a variety of high-quality wines. Its tranquil environment and breathtaking views make it an ideal place to unwind and enjoy some wine.

Food and Drink Scene of Buxton

Must-Try Dishes

Maine Lobster

Maine is known for its fresh lobster. Visitors must try steamed lobsters with melted garlic butter, a delicacy that can be found in most local eateries.

Whoopie Pies

Whoopie pies are a local specialty, and Buxton is home to some of the best! These delicious cakes filled with cream are a perfect way to end your meal or snack on-the-go.

Local Beverages

Maine Microbrews

Maine provides micro-breweries with locally grown hops, resulting in sweet and refreshing beer. Visitors can taste a variety of micro-brewery beer on every corner of the state.

Coffee Roasted in Maine

Buxton has some great local coffee shops that roast their beans in-house. Visitors can enjoy a fresh cup of coffee brewed in Maine.

Where to Stay in Buxton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

RunoftheMill Bed & Breakfast

This charming and unique bed and breakfast has an excellent location in Buxton, close to all tourist attractions. The historic home of the mill owner is now converted into a state-of-the-art B&B with a beautifully landscaped garden.

River View Resort

River View Resort is a family-friendly Buxton location. It offers visitors a colorful range of lodgings for any family size and caters to all types of travelers. The resort offers well-furnished cabins and small apartments, making it ideal for families and groups.

What to Buy in Buxton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wild Maine Blueberry Jams and Spreads

Maine is known for its blueberries, which are present in jams, spreads, muffins, and even marmalades! This local product makes for a perfect souvenir and a great addition to home-cooked meals.

Handcrafted Pottery

Buxton offers exquisite, handmade pottery that is perfect for home décor or gifts. The pottery is crafted by artisans who take pride in their work and create beautiful, unique pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Buxton

Buxton locals are friendly and welcoming. The town’s close-knit community is known for its love for the outdoors, and residents avidly participate in outdoor activities. Visitors are greeted with a warm smile and are sure to get a taste of the state’s friendliness.

Best Time to Visit Buxton

Visitors can thrive in Buxton all year-round. Summer is ideal for outdoor adventurer enthusiasts, fall for leaf peeping, winter for skiing, and spring for fishing and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buxton

Do:

  • Respect the local community and their culture
  • Be Prepared for Outdoor Adventures
  • Take in the Scenery

Don’t:

  • Hesitate to try the local dishes and drinks.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or pollute the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buxton

Buxton is a safe town with a friendly atmosphere. However, visitors must take standard safety precautions, like locking cars and securing personal belongings when out on day trips.

Health in Buxton

Visitors should carry personal hygiene and health kits, which include face masks, hand sanitizers, and necessary medication. They should also avoid consuming contaminated water and food.

Getting to Buxton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buxton

The nearest international airports are the Boston Logan International Airport in Boston, and the Portland International Jetport in Portland, Maine.

Bus Stations Near Buxton

The Concord Coach Lines bus is available, providing direct access to the city from Boston.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Buxton, as rental cars and taxis are widely available.

How to Get Around in Buxton

The best way to get around Buxton is by car or bike rental. However, visitors can also explore the town on foot.

Where to Go Next from Buxton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lake Region

The Lake Region is an excellent place to visit after Buxton. It offers visitors a stunning view of Sebago Lake, which is a popular spot for boating, swimming, and fishing.

Portland

Explore the vibrant city of Portland. Walk around the Old Port, visit the Maine Maritime Museum, and end the day waterfront, exploring the local art galleries and eateries.

Conclusion

Buxton is a hidden gem in Southern Maine that has a lot to offer visitors. Visitors can explore the town’s natural beauty, rich history, culture, and enjoy outdoor activities or unwind with some locally brewed beer or a glass of wine. It’s time to plan your trip and experience a unique and authentic Maine experience!
