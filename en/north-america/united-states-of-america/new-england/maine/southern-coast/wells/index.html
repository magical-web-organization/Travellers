Introduction to Wells

Welcome to Wells, a charming town nestled in the southern coast of Maine. With its stunning natural attractions, rich history, and vibrant culture, Wells is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. From hiking and biking to museums and local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Wells.

List of Places to Visit in Wells

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Wells:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wells Reserve at Laudholm: This 2,250-acre site preserves the local ecosystem and offers visitors a chance to explore nature trails, salt marshes, and beaches.
  • Wells Auto Museum: A fascinating collection of over 80 vintage automobiles and motorcycles.
  • Ogunquit Museum of American Art: Showcases American modern art, with a focus on artists from Maine and New England.
  • Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge: A sanctuary for migratory birds and other wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Wells: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wells Beach: A six-mile stretch of white sand and crystal clear water, perfect for sunbathing, swimming and surfing.
  • Cliff Walk: A scenic walking trail that runs along the rocky coastline, offering amazing views of the ocean and the coastline.
  • Mount Agamenticus: A 690-foot peak that offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, with hiking trails and picnic areas.
  • The Kayak Shack: Located on the tidal Ogunquit River, this kayak rental shop offers guided tours and equipment rentals for exploring the local waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wells

Wells boasts an extensive network of scenic bike paths and hiking trails that take you through its natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Eastern Trail: This 65-mile trail runs from Kittery to South Portland, offering stunning views of the Maine coast and countryside.
  • Wells Reserve at Laudholm: With 7 miles of nature trails, this is a perfect spot for hiking, birdwatching, and exploring the local wildlife.
  • Mount Agamenticus: A network of trails takes you through forests, fields, and rocky summits, with stunning views of the ocean.

Best Things to Do in Wells: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours to enjoy in Wells. Here are some of the best:

  • Wells Beach: Relax on the sandy beach or take a dip in the ocean.
  • Ogunquit Playhouse: A historic theater that presents Broadway-quality musicals and plays.
  • Maine Foodie Tours: Explore the local cuisine with this guided food tour that highlights the best of Maine’s farm-to-table dining.
  • Wells Harbor: Enjoy a scenic boat tour of the harbor, or rent a kayak or paddleboard to explore the local waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wells

Day 1: Explore the natural beauty of Wells with a visit to Wells Reserve at Laudholm and Mount Agamenticus. Day 2: Visit the Wells Auto Museum and Ogunquit Museum of American Art. Day 3: Relax on Wells Beach and take a scenic walk along the Cliff Walk. Day 4: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the local waterways from the Kayak Shack. Day 5: Enjoy a food tour of the local cuisine with Maine Foodie Tours. Day 6: Take in a Broadway-quality show at the Ogunquit Playhouse. Day 7: Visit the Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge to observe migratory birds and other wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Wells

Wells is known for its farm-to-table cuisine, highlighting locally-sourced ingredients with a focus on fresh seafood. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic Maine dish that features fresh lobster meat served on a buttered and toasted bun.
  • Blueberry Pie: A delicious dessert made with locally-sourced Maine blueberries.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy soup made with fresh clams and local vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: A local brewery that features a variety of craft beers, including their popular Pumpkinhead Ale.
  • Allen’s Coffee Flavored Brandy: A Maine specialty, incorporating locally roasted coffee beans mixed with brandy.

Where to Stay in Wells: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wells is home to many accommodations, from beachside hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best:

  • Village by the Sea: A seaside resort with spacious suites and indoor and outdoor pools.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Wells-Ogunquit: A modern hotel with free breakfast, located close to Wells Beach.
  • Ogunquit River Inn: A charming guesthouse that offers panoramic views of the local waterways.

What to Buy in Wells: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Wells include:

  • Lobster-themed kitchenware: Local shops offer everything from lobster-shaped oven mitts, to lobster claw bottle openers.
  • Maine maple syrup: A sweet and authentic Maine treat that’s perfect on pancakes or as a souvenir.
  • Sea Glass Jewelry: A unique keepsake made from the mounds of seaglass that wash up on the beach.

Local Culture and Customs of Wells

Wells has a strong sense of community, and visitors can experience this through a variety of events and festivals. One such festival is the Wells Harborfest, which takes place in July and features local music, food, and craft vendors. It’s also worth noting that Maine is known for its emphasis on sustainability and environmental stewardship, so take care to respect local wildlife and ecosystems.

Best Time to Visit Wells

The best time to visit Wells is between May and September when the weather is mild and sunny, and the beach is at its most enjoyable. The fall months are also a great time to visit, with fewer crowds and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wells

Do:

  • Try the lobster: It’s a Maine specialty, and the local cuisine is not to be missed.
  • Explore the natural beauty: There are countless opportunities for outdoor adventures, so take advantage of them.
  • Respect the locals: Mainers are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so be sure to reciprocate that kindness.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Maine emphasizes environmental sustainability, so be mindful of your waste and dispose of it properly.
  • Ignore local customs: Take care to respect local traditions and customs, particularly when it comes to environmental conservation.
  • Underestimate the weather: Maine can experience some extreme weather conditions, particularly in the winter months. Be prepared for whatever weather may come your way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wells

Wells is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should always take caution in crowded areas, particularly during peak tourist season. Be sure to keep your valuables secure and remain mindful of surroundings.

Health in Wells

Wells has a variety of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should take care to monitor their health and seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Wells: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wells

The closest airport to Wells is the Portland International Jetport, located 30 miles north. Alternatively, Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is located 80 miles south and provides additional flight options.

Bus Stations Near Wells

The Wells Transportation Center is served by the Concord Coach, which offers daily bus service to major cities throughout New England.

Other Options

Wells is also accessible by car, with several major highways passing through the area. Visitors can also take a train to the nearby towns of Wells or Saco, Maine, which are both served by Amtrak’s Downeaster train line.

How to Get Around in Wells

Wells is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking or biking distance of each other. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, and car rentals are easily accessible from the local airport or nearby cities.

Where to Go Next from Wells: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wells is situated in an ideal location that makes it easy to explore other nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Portland: Maine’s largest city is located just 45 minutes north of Wells and offers a variety of cultural attractions and historical landmarks.
  • Ogunquit: Just 10 minutes south of Wells, Ogunquit is another charming coastal town with a variety of art galleries and museums.
  • Kennebunkport: Located 20 miles north of Wells, Kennebunkport is a picturesque coastal town that is home to several historic landmarks and presidential homes.

Conclusion

Wells is a beautiful and welcoming town that offers visitors a wealth of natural beauty, world-class cuisine, and cultural experiences. We hope this guide has provided you with the information you need to plan your next trip to this charming destination.
