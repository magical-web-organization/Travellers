Introduction to Biddeford

Biddeford is a beautiful city located on the Southern Coast of Maine in New England, United States of America, in North America. Biddeford was originally a mill town, but today it is a bustling city with a rich history, natural beauty, and cultural significance. This city has so much to offer visitors, from museums and historical sites to natural beauty and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Biddeford

Biddeford is a place where you can enjoy the natural beauty of the coast, explore its modern landmarks, and learn about its rich history. With so much to offer, here is our list of top places to visit in Biddeford.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Saco Museum - The Saco Museum is a cultural center that offers exhibitions, educational programs, and special events. It has a collection of art and artifacts from the region’s past and the world beyond, showcasing the history, culture, and art of the surrounding area.

  2. McArthur Public Library - McArthur Public Library is a beautiful, historic library that offers a wide range of books, resources, and programs to the public. It is a perfect destination for book lovers who wish to learn more about the history of Biddeford and the surrounding area.

  3. Nanopack Indian Museum - The Nanopack Indian Museum is an independent museum created to provide insight into the cultures, history, and traditions of the people of Southern Maine. The museum offers guided tours, educational programs, and exhibits to the general public.

Natural Attractions of Biddeford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Fortunes Rocks Beach - A 4 mile-long sandy beach with dramatic cliffs, perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and surfing.

  2. Biddeford Pool - A beautiful saltwater pool that is perfect for swimming, paddleboarding, and kayaking.

  3. East Point Sanctuary - A nature preserve that offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and trails for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biddeford

Biddeford offers several hiking and biking trails for nature enthusiasts. Here are a few top choices:

  1. Cliff Walk - A scenic coastal walk that runs for more than a mile and offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and rocky coastline.

  2. Eastern Trail - A 65-mile multi-use trail that runs from South Portland to Kittery. It is suitable for biking, hiking, and most other outdoor activities.

  3. Hills to Sea Trail - A 47-mile trail that starts at the ocean in Biddeford and ends in Unity. It is perfect for hiking or biking to explore the natural beauty and history of Biddeford and nearby towns.

Best Things to Do in Biddeford: Activities and Tours

Biddeford offers plenty of things to do, including whale watching, kayaking, lobster boat tours, and more. Here are our recommendations for the best things to do in and around Biddeford:

  1. Whale Watching - Captain Joe’s Whale Watch offers a 4-hour whale watching adventure that promises sightings of humpback whales, fin whales, and dolphins.

  2. Kayaking - Southern Maine Kayak Rentals offers kayak rentals to explore the serene Biddeford Pool or the more challenging Saco River.

  3. Lobster Boat Tours - Come aboard Nick’s Chance Lobster Boat Tours to get an up-close look at the lobster fishing process and learn about Maine’s fishing traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biddeford

Come to Biddeford for a week-long vacation to fully experience everything this city and the southern coast of Maine have to offer. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Spend the day exploring Biddeford and nearby towns. Visit the museums and historical sites, then head to the beach or natural reserves for some outdoor adventure.

Day 2: Take a whale watching boat tour and see the majestic whales and other marine life in the Atlantic Ocean.

Day 3: Go kayaking at the Biddeford Pool or visit the scenic East Point Sanctuary.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Portland, Maine. Visit the Portland Head Light and explore the city’s famous Old Port.

Day 5: Visit neighboring towns like Kennebunkport, which offers shopping, dining, and historic sites.

Day 6: Take a lobster boat tour and explore the local marine life and fishing traditions.

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at the beach and taking in the natural beauty of the Biddeford region.

Food and Drink Scene of Biddeford

Biddeford offers a variety of cuisines, from fresh seafood to farm-to-table restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Biddeford:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Roll - A sandwich made with fresh Maine lobster, buttered hot dog bun, and served with coleslaw and chips. The best ones are available at the Ugly Tuna in Saco, which is just a few minutes away from Biddeford.

  2. Fish and Chips - A classic Maine dish, made with fresh fish caught daily and served with fries and tartar sauce. The best ones are available at Huot’s Seafood Restaurant and the Run of the Mill.

  3. Blueberry Pie - A signature dish of Maine made with fresh blueberries and a flaky crust. The best ones are available at Ruby’s Homemade Treats.

Local Beverages

  1. Maine Craft Beer - Maine is famous for its craft beer industry, which includes famous breweries such as Allagash Brewing Company, Shipyard Brewing Company, and Geary’s Brewery.

  2. Coffee - Maine is home to many coffee roasters, including Rock City Coffee Roasters and Bard Coffee.

  3. Apple Cider Donuts - A locally made apple cider donut is a must-try in Maine. The best ones are available at Smiling Hill Farm.

Where to Stay in Biddeford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Biddeford and its surrounding towns offer a variety of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are our top recommendations:

  1. The Inn at English Meadows - A boutique hotel located in Kennebunk, just 10 minutes from Biddeford. The Inn offers luxurious rooms and exceptional service.

  2. The Higgins Beach Inn - A historic beachfront inn located on Higgins Beach. It offers a relaxing and tranquil retreat with beautiful views of the ocean.

  3. Airbnb - You can find a variety of accommodations on Airbnb, including guesthouses, cottages, and apartments.

What to Buy in Biddeford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Biddeford combines traditional Maine craftsmanship with the vibrant creative energy of its artisans, producing unique gifts and souvenirs. Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  1. Hand-made pottery from CJ’s Pottery - CJ’s Pottery is a studio and shop that features the work of local artist Chris Davis, who creates functional and decorative pottery.

  2. Step Stone Creations - A locally owned gift shop that offers a variety of souvenirs and unique gifts, including handcrafted jewelry, vintage postcards, and hand-poured candles.

  3. Salvage BBQ Sauce - Bring home the flavors of Biddeford with the Salvage BBQ Sauce, which uses locally sourced, farm-fresh ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Biddeford

Biddeford is a culturally rich city with a blend of French-Canadian and Americana influences. Biddeford has a strong mill history and is proud of its industrial past. One of the notable cultural customs of Biddeford is the Franco-American Festival, which celebrates the French-Canadian heritage of the city.

Best Time to Visit Biddeford

The best time to visit Biddeford is during the summer months between June and August, when the weather is warm, and the coastal activities are in full swing. This is an ideal time to enjoy the beaches, hiking, and other outdoor activities. However, the fall months from September to November also offer beautiful foliage, food festivals, and other cultural events.

  2. Follow Leave No Trace principles and respect the natural environment.

  3. Try the local seafood and maple syrup.

  2. Don’t forget to tip your servers at restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biddeford

Biddeford is a safe city to visit, but like all cities, it’s important to keep in mind general safety tips. Be aware of your surroundings and belongings, and take precautions while hiking trails and exploring natural reserves.

Health in Biddeford

Biddeford has several healthcare facilities in the area, and it’s never a bad idea to bring a small first aid kit on your travels.

Getting to Biddeford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Biddeford is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some transportation options to get to Biddeford:

Airports Near Biddeford

  1. Portland International Jetport - Located in Portland, Maine, and only 20 minutes from Biddeford.

  2. Logan International Airport - Located in Boston, Massachusetts, and approximately 90 minutes from Biddeford.

Bus Stations Near Biddeford

  1. Concord Coach Lines - Offers daily bus service to Biddeford from Portland, Boston, New Hampshire, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  1. Driving - Biddeford is accessible via Interstate 95, which runs through Maine.

  2. Rental Cars - Several car rental companies such as Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise have rental locations in Portland and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Biddeford

The best way to get around Biddeford is by car, although bike rentals are available as well. Walking and hiking are great ways to explore the nearby natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Biddeford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Biddeford is a great base to explore Southern Maine. Here are some nearby destinations:

  2. Portland - A bustling city with a historic Old Port, top-rated restaurants, and craft breweries.

  3. Old Orchard Beach - A popular beach town with an amusement park, boardwalk, and a long sandy beach.
