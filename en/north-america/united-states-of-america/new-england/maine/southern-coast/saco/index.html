Introduction to Saco

Saco is a charming town located in the Southern Coast of Maine. Known for its scenic beauty, historical landmarks, and outdoor activities, Saco offers a perfect blend of culture and nature. Whether you’re looking to explore museums, go hiking, or taste delicious local cuisine, Saco has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, activities, and travel tips for Saco.

List of Places to Visit in Saco

From museums to natural wonders, Saco has a lot to offer tourists. Here are some of the must-see attractions in Saco:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, here are a few Saco attractions you should definitely put on your list:

  • Saco Museum: Founded in 1866, Saco Museum is one of the oldest museums in Maine. The museum houses a vast collection of art and artifacts, including American decorative arts, early Maine history, and Native American artifacts.
  • The Run of the Mill Public House & Brewery: Located in the historic Pepperell Mill Campus, the Run of the Mill is a local brewpub with an extensive beer selection and an interesting backstory.
  • Dyer Library: Built in 1889, the Dyer Library is a historic library where you can find a great book to read and learn about local history.

Natural Attractions of Saco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love spending time outdoors, Saco is a paradise. Here are a few natural attractions that are worth visiting:

  • Ferry Beach State Park: Located in Saco, Ferry Beach State Park is a popular attraction among tourists who enjoy hiking, picnicking, and exploring the beach.
  • Saco Heath Preserve: One of Maine’s rarest inland systems, the Saco Heath Preserve offers breathtaking views of wildlife, scenic trails, and boardwalks.
  • Cascade Falls: A nearby scenic spot with a stunning waterfall that offers an ideal picnic and photo-op spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saco

Hiking and biking are some of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Saco. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Eastern Trail: A beautiful 65-mile stretch of paved trails that connect South Portland to Kittery, with scenic views of Maine’s natural beauty.
  • Saco River Trail: A scenic bike trail that offers a picturesque view of woods, meadows, and wetlands.
  • Ocean Park: A scenic walk or bike trail that takes you to mountains and coastal wonders to the north of Saco.

Best Things to Do in Saco: Activities and Tours

Saco offers many exciting activities and tours for tourists. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Saco River Canoe and Kayak: Take a canoe or kayak and paddle down the Saco River while enjoying the scenic view of nature.
  • Funtown Splashtown USA: A popular amusement park that features several exciting rides, including a roller coaster and a water park.
  • Saco Farmers’ Market: Every Saturday from June through October, you can visit the farmers’ market for locally grown produce, live music, and a fun community atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saco

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Saco, here’s an itinerary you may find useful:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Saco Museum, explore the historic Pepperell Mill Campus, and have dinner at the Run of the Mill Public House & Brewery.
Day 2 Hike or bike the Eastern Trail, take in the scenic view, and have lunch at a nearby restaurant.
Day 3 Visit the Dyer Library, explore local shops and galleries, and have dinner at a seafood restaurant.
Day 4 Go canoeing or kayaking down the Saco River, have a picnic at Cascade Falls, and visit other nearby scenic spots.
Day 5 Visit the Saco Farmers’ Market, explore the Saco Heath Preserve, and have lunch at a scenic eatery.
Day 6 Spend the day at Funtown Splashtown USA with your family, and have dinner at a kid-friendly restaurant.
Day 7 Take a day trip to nearby Portland and enjoy its many attractions, from art galleries and museums to breweries and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Saco

Saco offers a diverse range of local dishes and beverages that you should try during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: A traditional Maine dish, lobster rolls are served on a hotdog bun and stuffed with fresh, juicy lobster meat.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy, hearty soup made with clams, potatoes, and bacon.
  • Whoopie Pies: A popular dessert consisting of two pieces of chocolate cake sandwiched with a creamy filling.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: A local brewery that offers a vast selection of craft beer, including its signature Pumpkinhead Ale.
  • Maine Root: A local soda company that produces organic, ethically sourced drinks with unique flavors, like Maine Root Ginger Brew.

Where to Stay in Saco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saco offers various accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hampton Inn Saco: A hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a heated indoor pool, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Saco Bay Rentals: A rental company that offers various vacation rentals, including cottages and apartments, located near the beach.
  • Ferry Beach Park Association: A non-profit organization that manages a campground and rental cabins in Ferry Beach State Park.

What to Buy in Saco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saco offers many unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some of the top options:

  • Saco Farmers’ Market: Purchase fresh, locally grown produce, honey, and dairy products.
  • Furbish: A local gift shop that sells handmade candles, pottery, and other unique items.
  • Bean Group: A local shop that provides an eco-friendly selection of souvenirs, including locally crafted jewelry and chocolate.

Local Culture and Customs of Saco

Saco is a friendly, relaxed town where locals take pride in their unique lifestyle. A few things to keep in mind while navigating Saco’s local culture are:

  • Saco values environmental conservation and sustainability is observed.
  • Respect the locals and their culture and they will treat you with the same respect.
  • Be prepared to enjoy slower-paced routines, especially during the off-peak season.

Best Time to Visit Saco

The best time to visit Saco is during the summer months, from June to August. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and visitors can enjoy the town’s outdoor activities. However, Fall is another great season to visit for its foliage sightings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saco

Saco is a safe place with low crime rates. Ensure to keep your belongings secure and lock the doors of your car and accommodations.

Health in Saco

Saco has adequate healthcare facilities that provide good-quality healthcare services. It is recommended to have travel insurance coverage to be on the safe side.

Getting to Saco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saco is easily accessible from various nearby cities within driving range, making it ideal for a quick weekend getaway.

Airports Near Saco

  • Portland International Jetport: Located just 20 miles away from Saco and offers domestic and international flights throughout the year.

Bus Stations Near Saco

  • Portland Transportation Center: A significant transit station that offers both bus and train services to various destinations.
  • Concord Coach Lines: Operates bus services to several locations with Saco for quick transit and is a budget-friendly option.

Other Options

Car rentals and Uber/Lyft services are also viable options within the region.

How to Get Around in Saco

Saco is easily walkable, and biking is an enjoyable way to explore this tranquil paradise. Visitors may use rental cars, cabs, or take the public transportation option as well.

Where to Go Next from Saco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Saco:

  • Portland, Maine: Located just 30 minutes away, Portland is a city with diverse cultural and natural attractions, including art galleries, museums, breweries, and restaurants.
  • Kennebunkport, Maine: A charming town located just 20 minutes away from Saco, Kennebunkport offers beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and picturesque landscapes.
  • Boston, Massachusetts: Located just a 2-hour drive away from Saco, Boston is a bustling city with several historical landmarks, museums, and dining options to explore.

Conclusion

Saco is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. With an array of activities, outdoor adventures, and scenic beauty, there is something for everyone in this friendly town. We hope this travel guide helps you plan your trip to Saco and discover the many wonders this serene haven has to offer.
