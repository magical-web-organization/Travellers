Introduction to Kennebunk

Located in Southern Coast, Maine, Kennebunk is a small coastal town known for its serene beaches, charming New England architecture, and friendly locals. The town is divided into two parts: Kennebunk and Kennebunkport, which are separated by the Kennebunk River. People from all over the world flock to this picturesque town to relax and unwind amidst nature. Whether you want to soak in the sun, explore the rich history, indulge in outdoor activities, or simply indulge in the local cuisine, Kennebunk has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kennebunk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brick Store Museum: Established in 1936, the Brick Store Museum is the perfect place to explore the rich history and culture of Kennebunk. The museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits, including the town’s architectural heritage, marine history, and Native American culture.
  • Wedding Cake House: This magnificent Victorian house is a masterpiece of its kind and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. It was built-in 1826 and is famous for its distinctive white exterior, which resembles a wedding cake.
  • First Families Kennebunkport Museum: Located in the heart of Dock Square, Kennebunkport, this museum takes you on a journey through the town’s vibrant history and culture. The museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the fascinating stories of the town’s first families.

Natural Attractions of Kennebunk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kennebunk Beaches: The town boasts of three beautiful beaches - Gooch’s Beach, Mother’s Beach, and Middle Beach - all of which offer stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean. These beaches are perfect for sunbathing, fishing, surfing, or simply taking a leisurely walk.
  • Franciscan Monastery: A peaceful retreat that offers panoramic views of the Kennebunk River, the Franciscan Monastery is a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the town. The beautifully landscaped gardens, walking trails, and religious shrines make it an ideal spot for meditation and contemplation.
  • Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge: Spread over 5000 acres, the Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge is a paradise for nature lovers. The refuge is home to a wide variety of birds, plants, and animals, including the endangered New England cottontail rabbit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kennebunk

Kennebunk offers multiple hiking and biking routes that are perfect for exploring the town’s natural beauty. Here are a few routes you can explore:

  • Bridle Path Trail: This 1.8-mile trail is a scenic route that takes you through the woods, past a waterfall, and across a stream.
  • Eastern Trail: This 65-mile trail runs from Kittery to South Portland and offers stunning views of the coastline and marshland.
  • Mount Agamenticus: This 692-foot mountain offers panoramic views of the surrounding forests, marshes, and ocean.

Best Things to Do in Kennebunk: Activities and Tours

  • Lobster Boat Tour: A must-do when in Kennebunk, the lobster boat tour takes you on a voyage to catch lobsters. You’ll learn about Maine’s lobster industry and even get to taste freshly caught lobsters.
  • Shopping: Kennebunk has an excellent shopping scene, with many antique and boutique stores selling unique items. Dock Square, in particular, is known for its collection of artisanal shops.
  • Whale Watching Tour: From May to October, you can take a whale watching tour from Kennebunk and spot whales, dolphins, and porpoises.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kennebunk

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Kennebunk:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the town’s historical and cultural attractions, including the Brick Store Museum, Wedding Cake House, and First Families Kennebunkport Museum.
  • Day 3-4: Go hiking or biking and explore the natural beauty of Kennebunk.
  • Day 5-6: Take a lobster boat tour and whale watching tour and indulge in some shopping in Dock Square.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beaches and watch the sunset over the Atlantic Ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Kennebunk

Must-Try Dishes

Kennebunk is known for its seafood cuisine, here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Maine Lobster: Indulge in the state’s famous crustacean, which is available in many forms, including lobster rolls, lobster bisques, and broiled lobster tails.
  • Clam Chowder: This creamy soup is a Maine classic and is made with fresh clams, potatoes, onions, and milk.
  • Whoopie Pies: A dessert staple in Maine, Whoopie pies are made of two chocolate cakes filled with icing.

Local Beverages

  • Allen’s Coffee Brandy: This is a favorite among locals and is often referred to as Maine’s unofficial state beverage.
  • Maine Craft Beer: The state has a thriving craft beer scene, and many local breweries offer unique brews that are a must-try.

Where to Stay in Kennebunk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kennebunk has many options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are a few top-rated options:

  • The White Barn Inn and Spa: This luxurious hotel is one of the town’s most popular and is known for its elegant decor, top-notch service, and excellent spa facilities.
  • The Kennebunk Inn: This historic inn is located in the heart of Kennebunk and is known for its cozy rooms and excellent restaurant.
  • The Lodge at Turbat’s Creek: This peaceful retreat is perfect for a relaxed and intimate getaway and is located in a peaceful wooded area near the Kennebunk River.

What to Buy in Kennebunk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saltwater Taffy: This sweet treat is a Kennebunk classic and is available in many flavors and colors.
  • Blueberry Jam: Maine is known for its blueberries, and you can find locally made blueberry jam in many stores.
  • Pottery: Artisans in Kennebunk create beautiful pottery, and you can find unique pieces to bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kennebunk

Maine locals are known for their friendly demeanor and laid-back attitude, so visitors should always be respectful and courteous. Additionally, Kennebunk has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should take the time to learn about the town’s history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kennebunk

The best time to visit Kennebunk is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm, and the beaches and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season (September-November) is also a great time to visit and offers stunning foliage views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kennebunk

Do:

  • Experience the local seafood cuisine and craft beer scene.
  • Take a stroll on the town’s beaches.
  • Explore the natural beauty and go hiking or biking.
  • Learn about the town’s rich history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kennebunk

Kennebunk is a relatively safe town, but visitors should always take precautions, including locking their cars and homes and keeping valuables hidden from sight.

Health in Kennebunk

Kennebunk has a few medical facilities in case of illness or injury, including the Southern Maine Health Care- Walk-In Clinic.

Getting to Kennebunk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kennebunk

  • Portland International Jetport (26 miles away)
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (75 miles away)

Bus Stations Near Kennebunk

  • South Station Bus Terminal, Boston (80 miles away)
  • Transportation Center, Portland (25 miles away)

Other Options

Kennebunk can also be reached by car or train. Amtrak’s Downeaster train services Boston and Portland, both of which are a short drive from Kennebunk.

How to Get Around in Kennebunk

Kennebunk is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance. Trolleys and buses are also available, and bikes can be rented from many local shops.

Where to Go Next from Kennebunk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ogunquit, Maine: This charming coastal town is known for its stunning beaches and vibrant arts scene and is located just 15 miles south of Kennebunk.
  • Portsmouth, New Hampshire: This historic port city is located just across the border from Maine and is known for its lively nightlife, excellent seafood restaurants, and historic architecture.
  • Boston, Massachusetts: A world-class city located just two hours south of Kennebunk, Boston is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and excellent food scene.

Conclusion

Kennebunk is a gem in Maine’s Southern Coast, offering visitors a chance to unwind and relax amidst natural beauty and rich history. Whether you want to bask in the sun, indulge in the local cuisine, or explore the cultural and natural attractions, Kennebunk has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of Kennebunk.
