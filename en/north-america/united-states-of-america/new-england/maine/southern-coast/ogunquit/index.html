Introduction to Ogunquit

Ogunquit, a quaint coastal town in Southern Maine, is renowned for its natural beauty, scenic views, sandy beaches, and vibrant cultural scene. The name “Ogunquit” comes from the Abenaki word “Makwahkiak,” which means “beautiful place by the sea.” This picturesque seaside town has attracted artists, writers, and travelers for over a century, and it continues to charm visitors with its laid-back, relaxing atmosphere. In this tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit in Ogunquit, the must-try dishes and local beverages, the top hiking and biking routes, the best time to visit, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Ogunquit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ogunquit Museum of American Art: The OMAA features a world-class collection of American artworks, including paintings, drawings, sculptures, and prints.
  • The Marginal Way: This scenic one-mile cliff walk along the rugged coastline offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the rocky shoreline.
  • Perkins Cove: A historic fishing village located at the mouth of the Ogunquit River, Perkins Cove is home to traditional lobster boats, boutiques, and restaurants.
  • Ogunquit Playhouse: Referred to as “America’s Foremost Summer Theatre,” the Ogunquit Playhouse has been producing Broadway-quality shows since the 1930s.
  • Nubble Lighthouse: Located in nearby York, Maine, the Nubble Lighthouse is one of the most photographed lighthouses in America.

Natural Attractions of Ogunquit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ogunquit Beach: This three-mile stretch of fine sand and surf is the perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing.
  • Footbridge Beach: A quieter stretch of beach located at the mouth of the Ogunquit River, Footbridge Beach is ideal for fishing, kayaking, and picnicking.
  • Wells National Estuarine Research Reserve: This 2,250-acre nature reserve is home to diverse wildlife, including migratory birds, seals, and fish. Visitors can explore the beach, tidal pools, and hiking trails.
  • Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge: This 5,265-acre refuge protects several seaside habitats and provides a haven for endangered species like the piping plover and the least tern.
  • Mount Agamenticus: Located in nearby York, Maine, Mount Agamenticus offers panoramic views of the coast and the surrounding woodlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ogunquit

  • The Marginal Way: One of the most popular walking and hiking trails in Ogunquit, the Marginal Way offers stunning coastal views and easy-to-moderate walking terrain.
  • Mount Agamenticus Trails: Mount Agamenticus has over 10 miles of well-marked hiking trails of varying difficulty levels, ranging from easy to strenuous. Mountain biking is also permitted on some trails.
  • Eastern Trail: The Eastern Trail is a 65-mile scenic trail that runs from South Portland, Maine, to Kittery, Maine. The Ogunquit section of the trail offers picturesque views of beaches, marshes, and woodlands.

Best Things to Do in Ogunquit: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Several local outfitters offer kayaking tours of the Ogunquit River and the nearby Wells Reserve.
  • Fishing: The Ogunquit River is home to a wide variety of fish, including striped bass, bluefish, and mackerel. Local fishing charters offer half-day and full-day trips.
  • Whale Watching: Head to the nearby town of Kennebunkport for a whale watching tour from April to October.
  • Shopping: Ogunquit Center offers a wide variety of locally-owned shops, boutiques, and galleries.
  • Wine Tasting: The nearby town of Kennebunkport has several wineries and vineyards that offer wine tastings and tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ogunquit

Day Activities
1 Explore the beach and the Marginal Way trail.
2 Visit the Ogunquit Museum of American Art and Perkins Cove.
3 Take a kayaking tour of the Ogunquit River or fish in the nearby waters.

5 Visit the Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge or the Wells National Estuarine Research Reserve.

7 Relax and enjoy the natural scenery or explore the local shops and galleries in Ogunquit Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Ogunquit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Ogunquit is famous for its fresh Maine lobster, which can be savored in a buttery, toasted roll or simply steamed with lemon and butter.
  • Clam Chowder: This classic New England dish is a creamy blend of potatoes, onions, clams, and cream.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is known for its sweet, plump blueberries, which are often used in pies, muffins, and pancakes.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: Located in nearby Portland, Maine, Shipyard is a popular brewery that serves a wide variety of craft beers, including the popular Pumpkinhead Ale.
  • Spirits of Maine Distillery: This distillery in Biddeford, Maine, offers tours and tastings of its craft spirits, including vodka, gin, and whiskey.
  • Coffee Roasters of the Kennebunks: Located in nearby Kennebunk, Maine, this coffee roaster offers locally-roasted, organic coffee blends.

Where to Stay in Ogunquit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Beachmere Inn: A historic oceanfront hotel located right on the beach, the Beachmere Inn offers luxurious rooms, suites, and cottages with stunning ocean views.
  • The Admiral’s Inn: A restored 1950s motel, the Admiral’s Inn offers uniquely decorated rooms, an outdoor pool, and a popular restaurant and lounge.
  • The Gazebo Inn Ogunquit: A cozy, family-owned inn located just steps from the beach, the Gazebo Inn offers comfortable, affordable rooms with free breakfast and Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Ogunquit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lobster-themed Souvenirs: From lobster aprons to lobster-shaped cutting boards, Ogunquit offers a wide variety of lobster-themed souvenirs to take home.
  • Blueberry Products: Blueberry jams, syrups, and salsas make great gifts and are a delicious reminder of your visit to Ogunquit.
  • Local Artwork and Crafts: Ogunquit is renowned for its vibrant arts scene, and you can find a wide variety of locally-made artwork, jewelry, and pottery in shops and galleries throughout the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Ogunquit

Ogunquit is a friendly, welcoming community with a relaxed, casual atmosphere. People in Ogunquit tend to be easy-going, outdoorsy, and environmentally conscious. To fit in and show respect to the local customs:

  • Dress casually and comfortably, with layers in case of changes in weather.
  • Tips are appreciated at restaurants, bars, and hotels.
  • Respect the natural scenery and wildlife, and follow the Leave No Trace principles when exploring the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Ogunquit

The best time to visit Ogunquit is from May to October when the weather is mild, the beaches are open, and the shops and restaurants are in full swing. June, July, and August tend to be the busiest and most crowded months, while September and October offer cooler temperatures, lower prices, and fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ogunquit

Do:

  • Take a walking tour of the historic Perkins Cove village.
  • Try some of the local seafood and craft beers.
  • Bring sunscreen, water, and comfortable walking shoes.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Take any items or stones from the beaches.
  • Drink and drive or bike after consuming alcohol.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ogunquit

Ogunquit is a relatively safe community, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should take standard precautions to protect their valuables and stay safe, such as locking doors, using a hotel safe, and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Ogunquit

Ogunquit has several medical clinics and pharmacies that can treat minor injuries and illnesses. For more serious medical emergencies, the nearest hospital is Southern Maine Health Care in nearby Biddeford.

Getting to Ogunquit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ogunquit

The nearest international airport to Ogunquit is Boston Logan International Airport (BOS), which is located approximately 70 miles south of Ogunquit. From Boston, visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use a shuttle service to reach Ogunquit.

Bus Stations Near Ogunquit

The nearest bus station to Ogunquit is the Wells Transportation Center, which is served by several local and regional bus lines, including Concord Coach Lines and Greyhound. From the Wells Transportation Center, visitors can take a taxi or shuttle service to reach Ogunquit.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Ogunquit by car via Interstate 95 or by train via the Amtrak Downeaster, which has a station in nearby Wells.

How to Get Around in Ogunquit

Ogunquit is a small town and is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use the Ogunquit Trolley, which runs from late May to mid-October and provides service to several local attractions. Car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Ogunquit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kennebunkport: A charming coastal town located approximately 15 miles south of Ogunquit, Kennebunkport offers quaint shops, restaurants, and picturesque views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Portland: Located approximately 45 miles north of Ogunquit, Portland is the largest city in Maine and offers a vibrant arts scene, top-rated restaurants, and historic architecture.
  • White Mountains: Located approximately 70 miles northwest of Ogunquit, the White Mountains offer stunning views, hiking trails, and skiing opportunities.

Conclusion

Ogunquit, Maine is a quintessential coastal town that offers visitors an ideal mix of scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and friendly local culture. Whether you want to lounge on the beach, explore the trails of Mount Agamenticus, or savor some fresh Maine lobster, Ogunquit is the perfect destination for a relaxing, rejuvenating vacation.
