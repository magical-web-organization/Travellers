Introduction to Old Orchard Beach

Old Orchard Beach is one of the most popular beach towns in Maine. Its seven-mile stretch of sandy beach, a famous landmark of Pine Point, attracts thousands of visitors every year. Located in Southern Coast, in Maine, in New England, in United States of America, in North America, Old Orchard Beach is a perfect destination for families and solo travelers alike. From beach activities to hiking trails and tasty seafood, there’s something for everyone in Old Orchard Beach.

List of Places to Visit in Old Orchard Beach

Discover the best places to visit and things to do in Old Orchard Beach.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old Orchard Beach offers a glimpse into the rich history and culture of Maine. Here are some must-visit attractions to explore:

  • The Harmon Museum: Learn about the local history and heritage of Old Orchard Beach through a collection of photographs, artifacts, and memorabilia.
  • The Palace Playland Amusement Park: One of Maine’s most popular amusement parks is a historic landmark of the city. Ride the Ferris wheel, carousel, and thrill ride called Adrenaline.
  • Dunegrass Golf Club: Hit the links at this high-end resort-style golf course that’s been top-rated by Golf Digest for its scenic beauty.

Natural Attractions of Old Orchard Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking trails to breathtaking natural wonders, Old Orchard Beach is packed with great outdoor experiences.

  • Old Orchard Beach State Park: This state park is home to some of the best beaches and surrounding scenery in the area. Beachcombing, bird watching and playing sports in the soft sand are all popular activities.
  • Scarborough Marsh: Kayak, canoe or paddleboard through Maine’s largest salt marsh to see the diverse flora and fauna that live here.
  • Eastern Trail: This 65-mile trail is perfect for walking, jogging and biking and offers scenic views of wooded areas, wetlands and beautiful escapes from nearby cities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old Orchard Beach

Old Orchard Beach offers some of the best hiking trails for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • The Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge is home to over 300 bird species and is located in the nearby city of Wells. Take a hike to see the diverse flora and fauna that call home to the Refuge.
  • Saco Heath Preserve: This beautiful nature reserve offers a boardwalk trail across the famous bog. Visitors can take a tour to learn about its rich history and see the unique flora and fauna that grow here.

Best Things to Do in Old Orchard Beach: Activities and Tours

Looking for things to do besides soaking up the sun on the beach? Old Orchard Beach is packed with activities and tours for all ages.

  • Explore the historical downtown of Old Orchard Beach with its many boutiques, souvenir stores and trendy restaurants.
  • Take a scenic trolley tour around Old Orchard Beach to discover the landmarks and the best attractions of the city.
  • Try fishing! Book a charter or bring your own gear and try your luck at catching the famous Maine lobsters.

Food and Drink Scene of Old Orchard Beach

Discover the food and drink scene of Old Orchard Beach.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maine Lobster: A trip to Maine is not complete without trying the famous Maine Lobster.
  • Clam Chowder: Rich, creamy and filled with fresh clams, this dish is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is famous for its blueberries, so don’t forget to try a slice of freshly baked blueberry pie.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Craft Beer: Maine is home to many great craft breweries that offer rich and flavorful beer.
  • Moxie: Maine’s very own soft drink that has a polarizing set of lovers and haters. Give it a try and decide for yourself.

Where to Stay in Old Orchard Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old Orchard Beach offers many great accommodation options for all types of travelers.

  • The Beachwood Farmhouse Inn: A 19th-century farmhouse that has been turned into a charming bed and breakfast just walking distance from the beach.
  • The Sea Cliff House Motel: A family owned motel with oceanfront views, pools, and many other great amenities.
  • The Marcotte Motor Court: A great budget hotel with a kitchenette, barbeque grills and other fantastic amenities

What to Buy in Old Orchard Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring back some souvenirs and local products to remember your trip to Old Orchard Beach.

  • Maine-made jewelry and knick-knacks, like key chains and magnets, from local beachfront shops.
  • Blueberry jam or honey made from locally harvested blueberries.
  • Hand-made pottery and glassware made by Maine artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Old Orchard Beach

Maine culture is unique and filled with traditional practices and customs. Lobster boils, blueberry festivals, and historic landmarks play a significant part in the local culture. Most people in Maine tend to be incredibly friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Old Orchard Beach

The best time to visit Old Orchard Beach is during the summer, between June and September, when the weather is warmest, and activities are plentiful. However, the fall months of October and November are also great seasons to visit, as the weather is crisp and the scenic landscape is stunning.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old Orchard Beach

Old Orchard Beach is a relatively safe city, but it is always wise to take precautions when exploring a new city or beach destination. Watch out for rip tides and follow all safety guidelines when participating in water activities.

Health in Old Orchard Beach

Where to Go Next from Old Orchard Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Old Orchard Beach is located in Southern Coast, in Maine, in New England, in United States of America, in North America, and there are many great nearby destinations to explore.

  • Portland: Just 15 miles north, Portland is a bustling city and a hub of culture and arts with many attractions, including the famous Portland Head Light Lighthouse.
