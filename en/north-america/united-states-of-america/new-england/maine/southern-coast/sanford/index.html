Sanford is a beautiful town located in Southern Maine, known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures. From museums to hiking trails, cultural landmarks to quaint shops, and much more, Sanford has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll find plenty to love in this charming town. In this comprehensive tour guide for Sanford, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to this amazing destination.

List of Places to Visit in Sanford

Sanford has plenty of exciting places to visit, ranging from cultural landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the top spots to check out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sanford-Springvale Historical Society Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the history of Sanford, showcasing everything from Native American artifacts to the town’s industrial past. If you’re a history buff, you won’t want to miss this museum.
  • The Twisted Iron Customs Museum: For those interested in cars and motorcycles, this museum is a must-visit. You’ll find an impressive collection of classic cars, vintage motorcycles, and other rare vehicles.
  • The Goodall Mansion: Built in 1892, this Victorian-style mansion is now a museum and showcases the history of the Goodall family, who were prominent industrialists in the area.

Natural Attractions of Sanford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mousam River Wildlife Sanctuary: This 51-acre wildlife sanctuary is a nature lover’s paradise. You’ll find plenty of hiking trails, bird-watching opportunities, and stunning views of the Mousam River.
  • Sanford Rail Trail: This converted rail line is now a popular spot for biking, jogging, and walking. You’ll enjoy scenic views of the surrounding countryside as you make your way along the trail.
  • Vaughan Woods State Park: This 250-acre park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and picnicking. You’ll find several beautiful waterfalls, as well as stunning views of the surrounding forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sanford

If you love hiking or biking, Sanford has plenty of options for you. Some of the top routes include:

  • The Sanford Rail Trail: This 5-mile trail stretches from Sanford to Kennebunk, offering stunning views of the countryside as you make your way along. It’s a popular spot for biking, walking, and jogging.
  • Mousam River Wildlife Sanctuary Loop Trail: This 1.5-mile trail takes you through the heart of the Mousam River Wildlife Sanctuary, offering plenty of opportunities for bird-watching and wildlife viewing.
  • Bauneg Beg Mountain Trail: If you’re up for a challenge, the Bauneg Beg Mountain Trail is a 3.6-mile hike that takes you to the summit of Bauneg Beg Mountain. From the top, you’ll enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Sanford: Activities and Tours

Sanford offers plenty of exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few of the top options:

  • Lobster Boat Tour: If you’re interested in learning about the local fishing industry, a lobster boat tour is a must. You’ll get an up-close look at how lobster is caught and learn about the history of the industry.
  • Sanford Food Tours: This tour takes you on a culinary journey through Sanford, stopping at some of the top restaurants and cafes in town. You’ll enjoy delicious food and learn about the local culinary scene.
  • Sanford Art Walk: If you’re interested in art, the Sanford Art Walk is a great way to explore the local art scene. You’ll visit local galleries, meet artists, and learn about their work.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Sanford, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Sanford

Sanford has a vibrant food and drink scene, offering plenty of delicious options for visitors to enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A Maine classic, the lobster roll is a must-try when visiting Sanford. You’ll find plenty of delicious options in town.
  • Clam Chowder: Another classic Maine dish, clam chowder is a hearty and delicious option for seafood lovers.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is known for its blueberries, and you won’t want to miss the chance to try this local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer Company: This popular craft brewery offers a variety of delicious beers, including IPAs, stouts, and more.
  • Cellardoor Winery: If you’re a wine lover, don’t miss the chance to visit Cellardoor Winery, where you’ll find a variety of award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Sanford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sanford offers plenty of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the top options include:

  • Sanford Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to all the top attractions in town.
  • The Colonial Inn: If you’re looking for a more luxurious experience, the Colonial Inn offers spacious rooms and plenty of amenities.
  • Yachtsman Lodge & Marina: For those looking for a waterfront experience, the Yachtsman Lodge & Marina is a great option, offering stunning views of the Mousam River.

What to Buy in Sanford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sanford is home to plenty of unique shops and boutiques, offering everything from handmade crafts to locally made food products. Some of the top options include:

  • Mousam Valley Mushrooms: This family-owned farm offers a variety of delicious mushrooms, including shiitake, oyster, and maitake.
  • Farmers Markets: Sanford has several farmers markets throughout the town, offering a variety of locally made goods, including honey, jams, and baked goods.
  • Maine Antique Mall: For those interested in antiques and vintage items, the Maine Antique Mall is a must-visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Sanford

Sanford has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Native American, French, and English influences. The town is known for its strong sense of community, and visitors are always welcomed with open arms. When visiting Sanford, be sure to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sanford

The best time to visit Sanford is from late spring to early fall when the weather is warm and sunny. The town is especially beautiful in the fall when the leaves change color and the foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sanford

Do:

  • Do explore the town by bike or on foot, as Sanford is a great town to explore on foot or bike.
  • Do try the local seafood and drinks, as Maine is known for its delicious seafood and craft beer.
  • Do show respect for local customs and traditions, as Sanford is a friendly and welcoming town.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, as Sanford values its natural beauty and cleanliness.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive, as Sanford is a peaceful and relaxing town.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather if visiting in the winter, as it can get quite cold.

Safety and Health

Sanford is a safe and healthy town to visit, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in Sanford

  • Always lock your car and secure your valuables when out and about in town.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking or biking at night.
  • Follow all posted safety rules when hiking or enjoying other outdoor activities.

Health in Sanford

  • Make sure you have all necessary vaccinations and medications before traveling to Sanford.
  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.

Sanford is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several taxi and car rental services available for those who need transportation.

Where to Go Next from Sanford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning a longer trip to the area, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring. Some of the top options include:

  • Kennebunkport: This charming coastal town is located just a short drive from Sanford and is known for its beautiful beaches and quaint shops.
  • Portland: This thriving city is located just a short drive from Sanford and offers plenty of cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and theaters.
  • Acadia National Park: Located approximately 165 miles from Sanford, Acadia National Park is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering stunning views of the Maine coast.

Conclusion

Sanford is an amazing destination, offering visitors the chance to explore its rich history, natural wonders, and vibrant community. With plenty of options for accommodations, dining, and activities, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. So whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a longer trip, be sure to include Sanford on your itinerary.
