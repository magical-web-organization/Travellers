Introduction to Kennebunkport

Kennebunkport is a charming coastal town located in Southern Maine, in the New England region of the United States of America. This small town has a rich history, breathtaking natural beauty, and a thriving food and drink scene that makes it perfect for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.

This comprehensive guide will take you through all of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, where to eat and drink, and essential safety and travel tips for your visit to Kennebunkport.

List of Places to Visit in Kennebunkport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kennebunkport has a rich history, and there are many museums and historical places that you can visit to learn more about the town’s past. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Kennebunkport include:

  • The Brick Store Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Kennebunks, and it has exhibits on everything from Native American tribes to modern-day life in the area.

  • The Nott House: This historic home was built in the 1850s and has been restored to its former glory. Visitors can take a guided tour of the house to learn about its history and the people who lived there.

  • The Seashore Trolley Museum: This museum has the largest collection of trolley cars in the world, and visitors can take a ride on a restored trolley through the Maine countryside.

Natural Attractions of Kennebunkport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kennebunkport is known for its stunning natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures that you can have during your visit. Some of the best natural attractions in Kennebunkport include:

  • Goose Rocks Beach: This beautiful beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and beachcombing. The sand is soft and white, and the views of the Atlantic Ocean are breathtaking.

  • Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge is home to a variety of wildlife, including migratory birds, turtles, and fish. Visitors can take guided tours or hike on their own through the refuge.

  • Kennebunkport Conservation Trust: This organization maintains over 2,300 acres of land in the Kennebunks, and visitors can hike, bike, or cross-country ski on the many trails that crisscross the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kennebunkport

For those who love outdoor activities, hiking and biking around Kennebunkport is a must. The area has plenty of beautiful trails and routes to explore. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Kennebunkport include:

  • The Eastern Trail: This 65-mile trail runs from Kennebunk to South Portland and offers stunning views of the Maine countryside.

  • The Kennebunk Bridle Path: This 6-mile trail follows the old railroad line and offers beautiful views of the coast.

  • Mousam Way Trail: This 5-mile trail runs from Kennebunk to Sanford and offers a quiet, peaceful hike through the woods.

Best Things to Do in Kennebunkport: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and enjoying the natural beauty of Kennebunkport, there are plenty of other things to do during your visit. Some of the best activities and tours in Kennebunkport include:

  • Sailing Excursions: There are several companies in Kennebunkport that offer sailing excursions, including sunset sails and lobster boat tours.

  • Wine Tastings: The Kennebunkport Wine Company offers tastings of their award-winning wines in a beautiful setting.

  • Art Galleries: Kennebunkport is home to many art galleries featuring works by local artists and artisans.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kennebunkport

To make the most of your visit to Kennebunkport, we recommend spending at least a week exploring the town and its surroundings. Here is a sample itinerary to help you plan your visit:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in to your hotel. Take a stroll through Kennebunkport’s downtown and enjoy a seafood dinner at one of the many local restaurants.

Day 2: Enjoy a morning hike on one of the local trails and visit The Brick Store Museum in the afternoon.

Day 3: Take a sailing excursion around the coast of Kennebunkport and enjoy a lobster dinner at a local restaurant in the evening.

Day 4: Visit Goose Rocks Beach and enjoy a picnic lunch on the sand.

Day 5: Spend the morning exploring the Kennebunkport Conservation Trust trails and visit The Nott House in the afternoon.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Portland, Maine, about a 30-minute drive away, and visit some of the city’s art galleries and shops.

Day 7: Spend the day at Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge, taking a guided tour and enjoying a picnic lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Kennebunkport

When it comes to food and drink, Kennebunkport has a lot to offer, especially when it comes to seafood. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Kennebunkport include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: This classic Maine dish is a must-try when you visit Kennebunkport. The best lobster rolls can be found at The Clam Shack and The Lobster House.

  • Chowder: New England clam chowder is a regional specialty, and there are plenty of restaurants in Kennebunkport that serve a delicious bowl.

  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is known for its amazing blueberries, and you can find some of the best blueberry pie in Kennebunkport at Mabel’s Lobster Claw.

Local Beverages

  • Allagash Brewing Company: This local brewery is located in Portland, Maine, and offers tours and tastings of their award-winning beers.

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: This brewery is known for their pumpkin beer, which is a must-try if you visit Kennebunkport during the fall season.

Where to Stay in Kennebunkport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kennebunkport has a variety of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s needs and budget. Some of the best places to stay in Kennebunkport are:

  • Captain Lord Mansion: This historic inn has been welcoming guests since the early 1800s and offers beautiful rooms and excellent service.

  • The White Barn Inn: This luxury hotel is a Relais & Châteaux property and has been named one of the best hotels in the world by Travel + Leisure magazine.

  • The Kennebunkport Inn: This stylish hotel is located in the heart of downtown Kennebunkport and offers beautiful rooms and a great restaurant.

What to Buy in Kennebunkport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a piece of Kennebunkport, there are plenty of local products and souvenirs to choose from. Some of the best things to buy in Kennebunkport include:

  • Blueberry Jam: Made from locally-grown blueberries, this jam is a delicious souvenir to bring home.

  • Sea Salt: Maine sea salt is renowned for its flavor, and you can find it at several shops in Kennebunkport.

  • Lobster Rope Doormat: Made from recycled lobster rope, these doormats are a unique and environmentally-friendly souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kennebunkport

Kennebunkport has a rich culture that is influenced by its history as a fishing village. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and there are a few customs to keep in mind during your visit.

  • Respect the Ocean: The ocean is an integral part of life in Kennebunkport, and it’s important to treat it with respect. Always follow posted beach rules, and never litter on the beach or in the water.

  • Fishing Tradition: Many local families make their living from fishing, and it’s important to respect this tradition. If you see a fishing boat at dock, don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation with the fishermen.

Best Time to Visit Kennebunkport

The best time to visit Kennebunkport depends on what you want to do and see during your visit. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and events, the summer months of June through August are the best time to visit. Fall is also a great time to visit and see the beautiful autumn foliage. If you’re looking to avoid crowds, consider visiting in the spring, from April to May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kennebunkport

Do:

  • Explore the Outdoors: Kennebunkport is known for its natural beauty, and there are plenty of opportunities to get outside and enjoy it.

  • Try the Seafood: Maine is known for its seafood, and Kennebunkport is no exception. Be sure to try some of the local dishes during your visit.

  • Learn About the History: Kennebunkport has a rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Kennebunkport is a small town, and it’s essential to keep it clean and free of litter.

  • Ignore Beach Rules: There are posted rules for beaches in Kennebunkport, and it’s important to follow them for your safety and the safety of others.

  • Overpack: Kennebunkport is a casual town, and you won’t need to dress up for most activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kennebunkport

Kennebunkport is a safe town, but it’s important to take basic safety precautions during your visit. Always lock your car and hotel room, and keep an eye on your belongings on the beach or in crowded areas.

Health in Kennebunkport

Kennebunkport is a healthy town, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and fresh seafood. However, it’s always a good idea to pack sunscreen and insect repellent for your outdoor adventures.

Getting to Kennebunkport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kennebunkport is easily accessible by car or plane, and there are several transportation options available for visitors. Some of the best options include:

Airports Near Kennebunkport

  • Portland International Jetport: This airport is located in Portland, Maine, about a 30-minute drive from Kennebunkport.

  • Boston Logan International Airport: This airport is located in Boston, Massachusetts, about a 90-minute drive from Kennebunkport.

Bus Stations Near Kennebunkport

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus company offers service from Boston to Portland, with a stop in Kennebunkport.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies have locations at Portland International Jetport.

  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and Uber are available in Kennebunkport for shorter trips.

How to Get Around in Kennebunkport

Kennebunkport is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by bike. Several companies also offer trolley tours of the town, which is a great way to see the sights.

Where to Go Next from Kennebunkport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kennebunkport is located in Southern Maine, which is a beautiful region with plenty of other destinations to explore. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Portland, Maine: This coastal city is known for its great food and drink scene, as well as its historic architecture.

  • Acadia National Park: This spectacular national park is located about a 3-hour drive from Kennebunkport and offers breathtaking views of the Maine coast.

Conclusion

Kennebunkport is a beautiful, welcoming town with plenty to offer visitors of all interests. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or seafood, you’re sure to find something to love about this charming coastal town.
