Waterboro, located in Southern Coast, Maine, is a charming town rich in cultural heritage and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore historical landmarks, tackle outdoor adventures, or simply relax in a cozy bed and breakfast, Waterboro offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve put together a list of must-see attractions, local cuisine, and insider tips to help you make the most of your visit.

Introduction to Waterboro

Waterboro is a picturesque town in Southern Maine known for its quaint village, stunning lakes, and peaceful way of life. Although it’s a small town, Waterboro offers a wide range of activities and attractions that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Waterboro has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Waterboro

While Waterboro may be a small town, it’s packed with interesting sights and fun activities. Here are some of the top attractions you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Waterboro Historical Society Museum - This museum, located in downtown Waterboro, offers a glimpse into the town’s past. The museum’s collection features items such as antique furniture, old photographs, and vintage artifacts.
  • The Old Red Church - Built in 1804, this historic church is a must-see for architecture and history buffs. Its distinctive red paint makes it easy to spot from the road.
  • Nason’s Beach - This beach was established in the early 1900s as a popular lake resort, and it remains a beloved local attraction to this day. Visitors can enjoy swimming, boating, picnicking, and other fun activities.
  • Waterboro Public Library - This library was constructed in 1903 and is a beautiful example of Richardsonian Romanesque architecture. It houses a collection of over 30,000 books, including many rare and antique volumes.

Natural Attractions of Waterboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ossipee Hill Summit - Hike to the top of Ossipee Hill and take in breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. This trail also passes by an abandoned fire tower with panoramic vistas.
  • Mt. Cutler Trail - This moderate hiking trail leads to the summit of Mt. Cutler, offering stunning views of the surrounding forest and hills.
  • Shapleigh Woods Wildlife Management Area - This 181-acre nature preserve offers hiking trails, angling opportunities, and birdwatching. It’s a great place to experience Maine’s natural beauty.
  • Little Ossipee Lake - With crystal-clear waters, a sandy beach, and plenty of fish to catch, Little Ossipee Lake is the perfect place for swimming, paddling, and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waterboro

Waterboro is a perfect destination for hiking enthusiasts. Check out these popular hiking and biking routes:

  • Waterboro Barrens Preserve - This 369-acre preserve boasts a beautiful mix of meadows, wetlands, and upland forest. It offers hiking, biking, birdwatching, and picnicking opportunities.
  • West Road Trail - This easy, 2.5-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely hike or bike ride. It winds through the woods and past several small ponds and streams.
  • Mount Agamenticus Trails - Located just a 20-minute drive from Waterboro, these trails offer a series of mountain biking loops that cover different terrains and difficulty levels.

Best Things to Do in Waterboro: Activities and Tours

Wondering what to do in Waterboro? Here are some fun activities and tours to check out:

  • Horseback Riding at Castle in the Clouds - Take a trail ride through the scenic woods and fields surrounding Castle in the Clouds, a beautiful mansion located in the nearby town of Moultonborough, New Hampshire.
  • Day Trip to Old Orchard Beach - Just a short drive from Waterboro, Old Orchard Beach offers a classic Maine beach experience with its long stretch of golden sand, lively boardwalk, and amusement park.
  • Paint Your Own Pottery at Potter’s Place - Get creative and make your own unique pottery at this fun studio. Choose from a wide variety of items to paint, including mugs, plates, figurines, and more.
  • Barn Quilt Trail Tour - Embark on a self-guided tour of the charming barn quilts that adorn barns throughout the Waterboro area. These colorful, eye-catching quilts are a unique local tradition.

Food and Drink Scene of Waterboro

From classic lobster rolls to unique craft brews, Waterboro offers plenty of tasty treats. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll at Waterhouse’s Kitchen Connection - This popular restaurant serves up fresh, delicious lobster rolls that are a favorite among locals and tourists alike.
  • Blueberry Pie from Jordan’s Store - Located just a short drive from Waterboro, Jordan’s Store is known for its mouthwatering blueberry pies made from locally grown berries.
  • Maple Syrup from Maple Moon Farm - Take home a bottle of Maple Moon Farm’s award-winning maple syrup, which is tapped, boiled, and bottled right on the farm.

Local Beverages

  • Banded Horn Brewing Company - This local brewery offers a variety of unique craft beers that are brewed on-site. Try the Pepperell Pilsner or the Jolly Woodsman IPA.
  • White Rock Distilleries - Located in the nearby town of Lewiston, White Rock Distilleries produces a variety of high-quality spirits, including vodka and gin.

Where to Stay in Waterboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer a cozy bed and breakfast or a luxury hotel, Waterboro has plenty of lodging options to choose from. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Lakefront Inn and Motel - This budget-friendly motel offers comfortable rooms and a scenic location right on Little Ossipee Lake.
  • The Cutty Sark Motel - This charming motel offers cozy rooms with knotty pine walls and a vintage feel. It’s just a short walk from downtown Waterboro.
  • The Waterboro House - This beautifully restored bed and breakfast offers five unique guest rooms and a delicious breakfast served daily. It’s located in the heart of downtown Waterboro.

What to Buy in Waterboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taking home a piece of Waterboro is a great way to remember your trip. Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Pottery from Potter’s Place - Take home a one-of-a-kind piece of pottery that you made yourself, or purchase a pre-made piece at this popular studio.
  • Lobster Boat T-Shirt from Jordan’s Store - Show off your love for Waterboro’s seafood with a fun t-shirt featuring a lobster boat.
  • Blueberry Jam from Limerick Market - This small market in the nearby town of Limerick offers delicious, locally made blueberry jam and other tasty treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Waterboro

Waterboro is a friendly and close-knit community that values traditions and a laid-back lifestyle. Visitors to Waterboro should be respectful of the local culture and take the time to appreciate the town’s history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Waterboro

The best time to visit Waterboro depends on your personal preferences. Summer is the most popular season for tourists, with warm weather and plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. Fall is a great time to visit if you want to experience Maine’s famous foliage, while winter offers opportunities for skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waterboro

Here are a few tips to help you make the most of your trip to Waterboro:

Do:

  • Enjoy the local seafood, especially lobster and clams.
  • Take advantage of the area’s many hiking trails and outdoor activities.
  • Be respectful of the town’s history and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the town’s natural resources.
  • Ignore safety rules and guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Be disrespectful or dismissive of the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your trip to Waterboro:

Safety in Waterboro

  • Always wear a life jacket when participating in water activities.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings when hiking or camping in the woods, and be prepared for potential wildlife encounters.
  • Follow traffic laws when driving, and be aware of the challenges that come with rural roads.

Health in Waterboro

  • Stay hydrated during outdoor activities, especially in hot weather.
  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Be aware of ticks during the summer months and take preventative measures such as wearing long sleeves and pants, applying insect repellent, and checking for ticks frequently.

Getting to Waterboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Waterboro is located about 90 minutes from Portland, Maine, and just over two hours from Boston, Massachusetts. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Waterboro

  • Portland International Jetport (PWM) - Located in Portland, Maine, about 45 minutes from Waterboro.
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT) - Located in Manchester, New Hampshire, about two hours from Waterboro.

Bus Stations Near Waterboro

  • Concord Coach Lines - This bus line serves Portland, Maine, which is the closest major city to Waterboro.
  • Greyhound Lines - This bus line has stops in Portland, Lewiston, and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars - Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Maine, especially if you plan to explore beyond Waterboro.
  • Taxi Service - Several local taxi companies offer transportation within Waterboro and to nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Waterboro

While Waterboro is a small town, having a car is recommended for getting around and exploring the surrounding area. Some hotels and bed and breakfasts offer shuttle services to nearby attractions, and there are several taxi companies in town.

Where to Go Next from Waterboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waterboro is conveniently located near several other popular destinations in Maine and New Hampshire. Here are some nearby excursions to consider:

  • Portland, Maine - Just an hour’s drive from Waterboro, Portland offers a vibrant arts scene, top-notch restaurants, and fun attractions like the Portland Head Light.
  • White Mountains, New Hampshire - The White Mountains are a beautiful destination for hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities. They’re located about two hours from Waterboro.
  • Kennebunkport, Maine - Another charming seaside town, Kennebunkport offers abundant shopping, dining, and sightseeing opportunities. It’s located just 30 minutes from Waterboro.

Conclusion

Waterboro, Maine, is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of small-town charm, natural beauty, and cultural heritage. With our insider tips and comprehensive travel guide, you can plan the ultimate trip to Waterboro and experience all that this charming town has to offer.
