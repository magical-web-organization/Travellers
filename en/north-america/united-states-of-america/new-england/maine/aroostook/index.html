Introduction to Aroostook

Aroostook is a county located in the northern part of Maine, in New England, USA. With its vast forests, rolling hills, and pristine lakes, this destination offers visitors a wonderful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Aroostook County is home to many charming small towns that exude the unique culture of northern Maine. With friendly locals and a laidback atmosphere, Aroostook has everything you need to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of this region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aroostook

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Aroostook, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Aroostook County Historical & Art Museum and the Fort Kent Blockhouse.
  • Day 2: Explore the Allagash Wilderness Waterway and go canoeing or kayaking.
  • Day 3: Go hiking in Baxter State Park or Bradbury Mountain State Park.
  • Day 4: Visit local farms and experience farm-to-table cuisine.
  • Day 5: Take an ATV tour in the Frenchville Trail System.
  • Day 6: Go snowmobiling or cross-country skiing.
  • Day 7: Relax in one of Aroostook’s charming small towns and enjoy some local food and drinks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aroostook

Aroostook County is generally a safe destination, but it’s always important to take the necessary precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Keep your valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when out in nature.
  • Follow safety guidelines for activities such as ATV tours and snowmobiling.

Health in Aroostook

Visitors to Aroostook County should be aware of the risk of Lyme disease, which is transmitted by ticks. Be sure to wear appropriate clothing and use insect repellent when out in nature.
