Introduction to Caribou

Caribou is a charming town situated in Aroostook, Northern Maine, in New England, United States of America. The town is referred to as the “most northeastern” town in the United States, making it a unique destination for both domestic and international tourists. Caribou provides visitors with a perfect blend of history, outdoor activities, culture, and scenic beauties. The town is known for its significant potato farming, and agriculture is the primary industry.

List of Places to Visit in Caribou

Caribou offers numerous places where visitors can explore to learn more about its rich history and enjoy the stunning beauty of nature. Here are some of the top places to visit in Caribou:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Caribou Historical Center: The Caribou Historical Center houses a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that chronicle the town’s history, beginning from the early trading period to the present day. Visitors can also learn about famous celebrities born in Caribou, such as Ross McElwee and Shaun Graham.

2. Nylander Museum of Natural History: The Nylander Museum of Natural History is a fantastic place to visit for lovers of nature and history. The museum boasts of over 10,000 specimens of plants, animals, and minerals, with many dating back to the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Caribou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Aroostook State Park: The Aroostook State Park is located on the south shore of Quoggy Jo Lake and offers visitors opportunities to participate in several outdoor activities such as fishing, hiking, paddle boating, camping, and swimming.

2. Gouldsboro State Park: The Gouldsboro State Park is a favorite destination for tourists interested in exploring nature, stargazing at night, or hiking along the Ocean Path Trail. Visitors can also enjoy picnics, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caribou

Caribou has various hiking and biking trails suitable for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Caribou:

  • The Nordic Heritage Sport Club: The Nordic Heritage Sport Club has over 20 miles of year-round trails suitable for hiking and biking activities.

  • Aroostook Valley Trail: The Aroostook Valley Trail covers approximately 28 miles of paved and unpaved trails, ideal for hiking, biking, running, snowmobiling, cross-country skiing, and snowshoeing.

Best Things to Do in Caribou: Activities and Tours

Caribou offers a wide variety of activities and tours to enjoy, and visitors can tailor their tour to their individual preferences. Here are some top-selected activities and tours in Caribou:

  • Aroostook Agricultural Tour: The Aroostook Agricultural Tour is a two-hour self-guided driving tour that takes visitors to some of the top farming sites in Caribou, such as potato fields, cornfields, and dairy farms.

  • Historic Walking Tour of Caribou: The historic walking tour of Caribou is an excellent way for visitors to explore the town’s historical sites, including the Cahill House, the Nylander Museum, and Collins Street.

Food and Drink Scene of Caribou

The food and drink scene in Caribou is a mix of traditional, local cuisine, and modern food options suitable for diverse palates. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Caribou:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Acadian Poutine: This local dish consists of crispy fries, gravy, and cheese curds.

  • Maine Lobster Roll: This traditional Maine Street is popular throughout the state and features succulent lobster meat served in a buttery roll.

Local Beverages

  • Northern Maine Brewing Company: The Northern Maine Brewing Company is a local brewery that produces excellent craft beers, including the popular Hophead IPA.

  • Potato Vodka: The potato vodka is made from locally-grown potatoes and is sold under various brand names, including Cold River and Maine Distilleries.

Where to Stay in Caribou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Caribou has an excellent selection of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses suitable for varying travel budgets. Some of the top-rated options include:

  • Caribou Inn & Convention Center: This upscale hotel offers visitors comfortable rooms, an onsite restaurant, and a fitness center.

  • Budget Travel Inn: The Budget Travel Inn is an affordable option with amenities such as free Wi-Fi and continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Caribou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Caribou can purchase souvenirs and local products to bring home to their loved ones. Here are some of the top items to buy in Caribou:

  • Potatoes: Caribou is known for its potato farming, and visitors can purchase different varieties of potatoes, including the famous Caribou Russets.

  • Handcrafted Gifts: Visitors can purchase handcrafted gifts, including pottery, baskets, and clothing, from local artisans and shops around Caribou.

Local Culture and Customs of Caribou

Caribou is a small town where people value respect, honesty, and simplicity. Visitors to Caribou are encouraged to respect the customs and traditions of the locals, including:

  • Greeting People: It is customary to greet people politely and introduce oneself before engaging with them.

  • Dressing Appropriately: Visitors should dress modestly and appropriately when visiting public places such as churches and museums.

Best Time to Visit Caribou

The best time to visit Caribou is during summer (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Getting to Caribou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Caribou is well-connected by various modes of transportation. Here are some transportation options available to get to Caribou:

Airports Near Caribou

  • Northern Maine Regional Airport: This airport is located in Presque Isle, approximately 12 miles from Caribou and offers flights to several domestic cities.

Bus Stations Near Caribou

Greyhound buses operate services to Presque Isle from some cities and visitor can connect from there to Caribou.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or drive to Caribou, especially from nearby cities and states.

How to Get Around in Caribou

Visitors can get around Caribou using various modes of transportation such as:

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent cars from rental companies at the airport and other locations throughout the town.

  • Bike Rental: Visitors can rent bikes from local shops and use them to explore the town and its environs.

Where to Go Next from Caribou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can extend their trip by exploring nearby destinations and attractions such as:

  • Maplewood Farm: Visitors can tour this 500-acre farm, learn about maple syrup production, and have some samples.

Conclusion

Caribou is an exciting destination with numerous attractions suitable for varying interests and tastes.
