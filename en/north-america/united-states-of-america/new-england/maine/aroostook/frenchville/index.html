Introduction to Frenchville

Frenchville is a charming town located in the Aroostook County of Maine. Founded in 1870, the town has a rich history and offers visitors a serene environment, delicious foods, and amazing scenic views. Whether you want to experience outdoor adventures, immerse yourself in Frenchville’s culture and customs, or just relax and unwind, this small town has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Frenchville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Northern Maine Museum of Science

Located in the neighboring town of Presque Isle, The Northern Maine Museum of Science is a fascinating science museum that offers hands-on exhibits on the human body, space, and much more.

2. St. Luce Catholic Church

Built-in 1902, the St. Luce Catholic Church is a charming church that serves the town’s French-speaking community.

3. Fort Kent Blockhouse

The Fort Kent Blockhouse is one of the many historical sites located in nearby Fort Kent. The Blockhouse was built-in 1839 during the Aroostook War as a defensive measure against British troops.

Natural Attractions of Frenchville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Baker Brook Falls

Baker Brook Falls is a beautiful waterfall located in nearby Madawaska. The waterfall is about 30 feet tall, and the trail leading to it is an easy hike.

2. Lake Josephine

Lake Josephine is a popular spot for boating and fishing. The lake is home to many fish species, such as brook trout, salmon, and pike.

3. Long Lake

Long Lake is a beautiful lake spanning over 11 miles, making it perfect for boating, kayaking, and swimming. The lake is also home to many campsites, making it an ideal spot for a weekend getaway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frenchville

If you love outdoor adventures, then Frenchville is the perfect destination for you. There are many hiking and biking trails in and around Frenchville, offering visitors a chance to explore its natural beauty. Here are a few trails to check out:

1. Eagle Lake Trail

The Eagle Lake Trail is a 42-mile trail that starts in Fort Kent and ends in Eagle Lake. The trail passes through forests, streams, and lakes, offering hikers and bikers magnificent views of the wilderness.

2. Madawaska Trail

The Madawaska Trail is a beautiful 14-mile trail that offers stunning views of Mount Katahdin. The trail is an easy hike and is perfect for beginners and families.

Best Things to Do in Frenchville: Activities and Tours

1. Canoeing and Kayaking

With so many lakes and rivers in and around Frenchville, canoeing and kayaking are popular activities among visitors. There are many companies that rent canoes and kayaks or offer guided tours, making it convenient for visitors who want to experience the beauty of the area’s waterways.

2. Snowmobiling

Winter sports enthusiasts will love Frenchville’s snowmobiling trails. There are many trails throughout the area, offering snowmobilers scenic views of the snow-covered wilderness.

Week-Long Itinerary for Frenchville

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long visit to Frenchville:

Day 1 Visit the Northern Maine Museum of Science
Day 2 Explore the Baker Brook Falls and Madawaska Trail
Day 3 Enjoy kayaking or canoeing on Long Lake
Day 4 Take a guided tour of the Fort Kent Blockhouse
Day 5 Spend the day at the Eagle Lake Trail
Day 6 Snowmobile on the trails around Frenchville
Day 7 Go shopping at local shops and markets

Food and Drink Scene of Frenchville

Must-Try Dishes

Frenchville has a unique and delicious food scene, with many dishes influenced by the French-Canadian communities that have been in the area for generations. Here are some must-try dishes:

1. Ploye

Ploye is a type of pancake made with buckwheat flour and water, served with butter and maple syrup.

2. Tourtiere

Tourtiere is a meat pie made with pork, beef, and potatoes, seasoned with cinnamon, cloves, and allspice.

3. Sugar Pie

Sugar pie is a dessert made with a rich, sweet filling of brown sugar, cream, and butter, baked in a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

1. Maple Syrup

Maine is known for its maple syrup, which is produced in many local farms around Frenchville. You can try it in your coffee or pancakes.

2. Blueberry Ale

Blueberries are a staple in Maine, and many breweries in the area make delicious blueberry ale.

What to Buy in Frenchville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Potato-inspired Souvenirs

Potatoes are a staple crop in Maine, and many souvenir shops in the area sell potato-themed products like magnets, keychains, and t-shirts.

Local Maple Syrup

You can purchase maple syrup from many local farms around Frenchville. It makes a tasty souvenir to take home and enjoy long after your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Frenchville

Frenchville’s culture and customs are strongly influenced by the French-Canadian communities that have been in the area for generations. Visitors to the area can expect to experience a unique blend of French and North American traditions. A popular event is the Frenchville Acadian Festival, which celebrates the town’s French-Canadian heritage.

Best Time to Visit Frenchville

The best time to visit Frenchville is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities like kayaking, hiking, and fishing are in season. Winter sports like snowmobiling are popular from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Frenchville

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Take advantage of the area’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter the scenic views.
  • Be loud and rowdy in public areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Frenchville

Frenchville is a safe town with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors should be cautious when hiking or snowmobiling and should always follow safety guidelines.

Health in Frenchville

There are healthcare services available in the area, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies.

Getting to Frenchville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Frenchville

The nearest airport to Frenchville is the Northern Maine Regional Airport, which is located in Presque Isle, about 45 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Frenchville

The Concord Coach Lines bus station is located in Presque Isle, and Greyhound has a stop in Houlton, both are approximately an hour drive from Frenchville.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent option for those who want to explore the surrounding areas around Frenchville. Taxis and Ubers are not available in the area.

How to Get Around in Frenchville

The best way to get around Frenchville is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent cars from nearby airports or take taxis in the area.

Where to Go Next from Frenchville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fort Kent

Fort Kent is a town located just 25 miles north of Frenchville and offers visitors beautiful scenic views of the Saint John River valley.

Acadia National Park

Acadia National Park is a beautiful national park located in Bar Harbor, Maine, and is about a three-hour drive from Frenchville. The park offers visitors beautiful views of the ocean, lush forests, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Frenchville, Maine, is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and delicious foods. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or an action-packed adventure, this small town in northern Maine will not disappoint.
