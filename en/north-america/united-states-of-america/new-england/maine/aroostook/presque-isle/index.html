Introduction to Presque Isle

Nestled in Aroostook County in Maine, Presque Isle is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural attractions, making it a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore the beautiful North American region of New England. Home to bustling shopping centers, top-notch restaurants, and unbeatable outdoor recreation opportunities, Presque Isle has become known as the commercial hub of Northern Maine.

List of Places to Visit in Presque Isle

When planning your trip to Presque Isle, there are plenty of places that you cannot miss. Here is a list of the top attractions and must-see destinations in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to delve into Presque Isle’s fascinating history is by visiting its museums and exploring its landmarks. Some of the top culturally significant attractions include:

  • Aroostook State Park: With its numerous hiking trails, scenic views, and wide range of outdoor activities, Aroostook State Park is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts visiting the area.
  • The Northern Maine Museum of Science: A fun and interactive museum that explores the natural history of the state of Maine and beyond.
  • The Presque Isle Historical Society: The perfect place to learn about the history of Presque Isle and its surrounding areas. With exhibits exploring the agricultural, military, and social history of the region, this fantastic museum is a must-visit.
  • The Nylander Museum of Natural History: With its impressive collections of birds, minerals, and mammals, this excellent museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the region’s natural history.

Natural Attractions of Presque Isle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Explore the great outdoors in Presque Isle and take in its stunning natural wonders. Here are a few attractions to add to your itinerary:

  • The majestic Aroostook River is one of the most breathtaking sights in the region. The waters offer fantastic opportunities for kayaking, canoeing, and fishing, while its banks offer scenic hiking and mountain biking trails.
  • The Maine Solar System Model: This fascinating site boasts a scaled-down version of the solar system, with planets represented by huge sculptures located throughout the town.
  • The Nordic Heritage Center: The perfect destination for skiing enthusiasts, with its cross-country skiing trails and snowshoeing activities.
  • The Big Rock Ski Area: Located just a few miles outside of Presque Isle, the Big Rock Ski Area offers fantastic opportunities for skiing and snowboarding in the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Presque Isle

As previously noted, Presque Isle offers numerous outdoor recreational activities, including hiking and biking. Here are just a few of our favorite routes:

  • The Nordic Heritage Center: With 20-plus miles of well-groomed trails, this center offers an exciting and rewarding experience for hikers and bikers.
  • The Aroostook River Trail: With approximately 25 miles of trails and picnic areas, this is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The trail follows the Aroostook River for most of its journey, offering picturesque views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Presque Isle Trails: This vast network of trails is perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels, with its many scenic views and interesting landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Presque Isle: Activities and Tours

There are countless things to do in Presque Isle, from exploring the town’s museums and galleries to fishing, skiing, and mountain biking. Here are a few of our must-not-miss activities and tours:

  • Husson University Nature Trails: This beautiful nature reserve located on the campus of Husson University offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of Presque Isle - perfect for a peaceful walk or run.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: Take a leisurely paddle down the Aroostook River, enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape along the way.
  • Snowmobiling: With miles of groomed trails, Presque Isle is a paradise for snowmobile enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Presque Isle

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week’s stay in Presque Isle:

Day 1 Arrive in town and check-in to your accommodations. Spend the afternoon exploring the museums and galleries in town.
Day 2 Visit Aroostook State Park and take in its natural wonders.
Day 3 Explore the Nordic Heritage Center on skis, snowshoes, or mountain bikes. Enjoy an afternoon snack at one of the nearby cafes and restaurants.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Big Rock Ski Area and enjoy the skiing/snowboarding opportunities.
Day 5 Take a leisurely paddle down the Aroostook River and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
Day 6 Rent a snowmobile and take to the trails for an adrenaline-fueled adventure.
Day 7 Spend the day skiing or snowshoeing at the nearby trails and parks. Enjoy a farewell dinner at one of the nearby restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Presque Isle

When it comes to food and drink, Presque Isle does not disappoint. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This classic Canadian dish can be found in most of the town’s restaurants and is a must-try.
  • Lobster: Fresh from Maine’s coasts, Presque Isle’s lobster dishes are not to be missed.
  • Maine blueberry pie: These delicious pies, made from Maine’s famous blueberries, can be found in many of the town’s bakeries and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Maine beer: With numerous microbreweries located in and around the town, Maine’s craft beer scene is thriving.
  • Apple cider: Made from locally grown apples, Presque Isle’s apple cider is famous throughout the region.
  • Mocha coffee: The town’s cafes and coffee shops offer excellent chocolate and coffee drinks.

Where to Stay in Presque Isle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Presque Isle boasts a variety of accommodations, ranging from excellent hotels to affordable hostels. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • The Northeastland Hotel: This charming hotel boasts comfortable rooms and modern amenities and is located in the heart of town.
  • Hampton Inn Presque Isle: With well-appointed rooms and top-notch service, the Hampton Inn is perfect for those looking for a modern and convenient place to stay.
  • Caribou Inn & Convention Center: Located just a few miles outside of town, the Caribou Inn & Convention Center offers everything from spa services to access to nearby snowmobile trails.

What to Buy in Presque Isle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect memento to bring home from your trip to Presque Isle? Here are a few ideas:

  • Maple syrup: With its proximity to Canada, Presque Isle is known for its delicious maple syrup.
  • Handcrafted pottery: The town’s galleries and museums feature fantastic handcrafted pottery and ceramics by local artists.
  • Blueberry jam: Made from Maine’s famous blueberries, this jam is the perfect way to savor your memories of Presque Isle.

Local Culture and Customs of Presque Isle

Presque Isle has a rich and unique culture that reflects its deep history and diverse community. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting the town:

  • The town’s people are exceptionally friendly and welcoming. When exploring the town, don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation with the locals.
  • The town celebrates numerous cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Potato Blossom Festival and the Northern Maine Fair.
  • Maine’s outdoor recreation opportunities are in high demand, so be sure to book ahead if you plan on participating in any activities.

Best Time to Visit Presque Isle

The best time to visit Presque Isle is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and dry and the town’s various outdoor activities are in full swing. In the winter months, Presque Isle transforms into a winter wonderland, with various skiing, snowshoeing, and snowmobiling opportunities, making it a perfect destination for cold-weather enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Presque Isle

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Presque Isle:

Do:

  • Be respectful of the town’s people and their customs.
  • Bring warm clothing if you’re visiting during the fall or winter months.
  • Take your time exploring the town’s many attractions.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in public places.
  • Ignore the town’s rich cultural history.
  • Disrespect the town’s natural areas by not following its designated trails and paths.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Presque Isle, it is essential to keep your safety and health in mind. Here is some important information to keep in mind:

Safety in Presque Isle

Presque Isle is an exceptionally safe town, with little to no violent crime. However, it is worth noting that Maine has some of the highest rates of Lyme disease in the country, so be sure to take appropriate precautions if you plan on spending a lot of time outdoors.

Health in Presque Isle

Presque Isle has several medical and healthcare facilities, with Aroostook Medical Center being the most notable.

Getting to Presque Isle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Presque Isle is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are a few options to consider:

Airports Near Presque Isle

The closest airport to Presque Isle is the Northern Maine Regional Airport, which is located just a few miles outside of town.

Bus Stations Near Presque Isle

Presque Isle is serviced by Concord Coach Lines, which runs a bus service throughout the state of Maine.

Other Options

If driving, Presque Isle is accessible via I-95, with parking available throughout the town.

How to Get Around in Presque Isle

Exploring Presque Isle is best done by car or bike, with bike rentals available in the town. Taxis are also available, but their service may be limited.

Where to Go Next from Presque Isle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Presque Isle, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Baxter State Park: A stunning nature reserve located a few hours to the south of Presque Isle.
  • Bangor: This charming city is the perfect destination for those who enjoy shopping and a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Acadia National Park: One of the East Coast’s most beautiful national parks, located a few hours to the south of Presque Isle.

Conclusion

Presque Isle is a charming and unique town that offers something for everyone, from natural wonders to exciting outdoor activities and fascinating cultural events. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adrenaline junkie, Presque Isle is a must-visit destination that is sure to leave a lasting impression.
