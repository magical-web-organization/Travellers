Introduction to Limestone

Located in Aroostook County, in Maine, New England, Limestone is a charming town that boasts stunning natural landscapes, a rich history, and a unique local culture. Originally a center for limestone quarrying, Limestone is now a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone who appreciates small-town charm.

List of Places to Visit in Limestone

From museums and historical attractions to natural wonders and outdoor adventures, Limestone offers a variety of attractions that cater to different interests and preferences.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Limestone is home to several museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the town’s past.

  • Limestone Historical Society Museum - Discover the town’s history through the exhibits that showcase old photographs, artifacts, and documents.
  • Aroostook County Historical & Art Museum - This museum features the cultural and historical heritage of the Aroostook County.
  • Loring Military Heritage Center - Explore the history of Loring Air Force Base, which played a crucial role in the Cold War.

Natural Attractions of Limestone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Limestone offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

  • Aroostook State Park - Hike, fish, swim, or picnic in this beautiful state park that features 800 acres of forests, lakes, and beaches.
  • Goulding Creek Trail - This trail offers a peaceful hike through the woods and along the creek.
  • Limestone Mountain - Hike up the mountain for spectacular views of the town and surrounding areas.
  • Bradbury Mountain State Park - This park is perfect for hiking, biking, wildlife watching, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Limestone

Limestone has a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer something for everyone, from easy walks to challenging treks.

  • Aroostook Valley Trail - This 28-mile trail stretches from Presque Isle to New Sweden, passing through Limestone.
  • Limestone Trail System - This network of trails provides options for hiking and mountain biking for all skill levels.
  • Caribou Bog Walk - This boardwalk trail offers an easy walk through a beautiful wetland area.

Best Things to Do in Limestone: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Limestone offers a variety of activities and tours that are sure to keep visitors entertained.

  • Canoeing and Kayaking - Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the Aroostook River or the Aroostook State Park.
  • Fishing - Cast a line and try your luck at catching salmon, trout, or bass.
  • Snowmobile Tours - In the winter, explore the snow-covered countryside with a snowmobile tour.
  • Sledding - Head to Little Rock Ski Area for some fun in the snow.

Week-Long Itinerary for Limestone

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Limestone.

Day 1 - Arrive in Limestone, visit the Limestone Historical Society Museum and Aroostook County Historical & Art Museum. Day 2 - Go hiking in the Aroostook State Park, have a picnic, and swim in the lake. Day 3 - Take a guided tour of the Loring Military Heritage Center. Day 4 - Go mountain biking in the Limestone Trail System. Day 5 - Visit the Bradbury Mountain State Park for a day of hiking and wildlife watching. Day 6 - Go fishing in the Aroostook River or just relax in town. Day 7 - Go on a snowmobile tour in the winter or enjoy some sledding at the Little Rock Ski Area.

Food and Drink Scene of Limestone

Limestone has a vibrant food and drink scene, which reflects the local culture and history.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ployes - a traditional pancake-like dish made with buckwheat flour.
  • Maine Lobster - Fresh lobster is a must-try delicacy in Limestone.
  • Moose Steak - Game meat enthusiasts will find moose steak on menus in town.

Local Beverages

  • Allen’s Coffee Brandy - This local brandy is a staple of Maine’s drinking culture.
  • Maine Beer - Sample the local craft beers, such as Shipyard Brewing Company’s Export Ale and Allagash Brewing Company’s White Ale.

Where to Stay in Limestone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lodge, hostels, and guesthouses are all available in Limestone.

  • The Inn on Main - A bed and breakfast in a centrally located, historic building.
  • Caribou Inn & Convention Center - A larger hotel just a few miles away in Caribou.
  • Northern Door Inn - A family-owned motel in the heart of Limestone.

What to Buy in Limestone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Limestone without picking up a few souvenirs or local products.

  • Potatoes - Aroostook County is known for its potato crops, so grab some fresh potatoes to take home.
  • Maple Syrup - The sweet syrup produced from the maple trees in Maine forests is worth savoring.
  • Limestone T-Shirts - A way to show off your visit and local pride.

Local Culture and Customs of Limestone

Residents of Aroostook County are known for their strong work ethic, friendliness, and love of outdoor activities. Like other New Englanders, Mainers are self-reliant, practical, and independent.

Best Time to Visit Limestone

Limestone is a year-round destination with distinct seasons.

  • Summer - The warm weather and outdoor activities make summer a popular time to visit.
  • Fall - The foliage is beautiful during the autumn months.
  • Winter - The town is transformed into a winter wonderland, great for sledding, skiing, and snowmobiling.
  • Spring - The weather is mild, and outdoor activities are just starting to open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Limestone

Here are some helpful tips to follow during your visit to Limestone.

Do:

  • Be mindful of wildlife in the state parks, especially moose and black bears.
  • Respect the locals and their way of life.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities available, such as hiking and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural environment.
  • Forget your camera, there are plenty of amazing photo opportunities around Limestone.
  • Ignore the weather, it can be unpredictable throughout the year.

Safety and Health

Limestone is a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions when enjoying the outdoor activities.

Safety in Limestone

  • When hiking, stay on the marked trails and adhere to the posted warnings.
  • Be cautious of ticks and apply insect repellent to expose skin.
  • Make sure you have the proper gear and supplies, especially in the winter months.

Health in Limestone

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Check with your doctor before traveling to ensure that you are up to date on vaccinations.
  • Be mindful of COVID-19 protocols and follow local guidelines.

Getting to Limestone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Limestone

  • Northern Maine Regional Airport, Presque Isle - 9 miles away

Bus Stations Near Limestone

  • Concord Coach Lines, Presque Isle - 10 miles away

Other Options

  • Drive - Limestone is easily accessible by car, with Route 1 providing access to the town from the south.

How to Get Around in Limestone

Limestone is a small town that is easily walkable or bikeable, but having a car can be helpful depending on your itinerary.

Where to Go Next from Limestone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Caribou - A town known for its culinary scene, located just a few miles to the west.
  • Fort Kent - A town located along the Canadian border, known for its history and scenery.
  • Baxter State Park - A 209,000-acre wilderness area that is home to Mount Katahdin, Maine’s highest peak.

Conclusion

Limestone is a hidden gem in Maine that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Whether you’re in town for a day or a week, you won’t run out of things to do and see. Plan your trip today and experience the natural beauty and unique culture of Limestone.
