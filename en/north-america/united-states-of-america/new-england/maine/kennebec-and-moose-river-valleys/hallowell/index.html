Introduction to Hallowell

Hallowell, located in the Kennebec and Moose River Valleys region, in Maine, United States of America, is a charming small town with a big history. Known as the “City of Divine Bounties,” Hallowell was settled by Europeans in the 18th century, and today, it has become a popular destination for visitors who want to immerse themselves in Maine’s rich culture and scenic beauty.

In this comprehensive guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, activities to do, foods to try, and everything else you need to know to plan your trip to Hallowell.

List of Places to Visit in Hallowell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Maine State Museum - Explore the natural and cultural history of Maine through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and artwork.
  • The Maine State House - Visit the seat of the Maine State Legislature and admire its neoclassical architecture and impressive dome.
  • Old Fort Western - A National Historic Landmark, this fort was built in 1754 and is Maine’s oldest surviving wooden fort.

Natural Attractions of Hallowell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Kennebec River - Take a relaxing boat ride or kayak excursion down the beautiful Kennebec River.
  • The Vaughan Woods & Historic Homestead - Enjoy a peaceful walk in nature while learning about the history of this stunning estate.
  • Hallowell Reservoir - Go fishing, boating, or take a walk along the trails that surround this beautiful lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hallowell

  • Rails-to-Trails - This 6-mile trail runs from Augusta to Hallowell and is perfect for walking, biking, and even cross-country skiing.

Best Things to Do in Hallowell: Activities and Tours

  • Hallowell Ghost Walks - Discover the history and legends of Hallowell through a guided ghost walk.
  • Hallowell Wood Pellet Factory Tour - Learn about the green technology of making wood pellets for heating and hot water supply.
  • Hallowell Farmer’s Market - Sample the delicious local produce grown in and around Hallowell.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hallowell

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Maine State Museum and the Maine State House
Day 2 Explore historic Old Fort Western and take a river boat tour

Food and Drink Scene of Hallowell

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls - A Maine specialty, lobster rolls are the perfect way to enjoy fresh seafood.
  • Blueberry Pie - Maine is known for its blueberries, and the pie is a must-try.
  • Whoopie Pies - A delicious local dessert consisting of two chocolate cakes with a cream filling.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Craft Beer - Hallowell has several local breweries that produce quality craft beer.
  • Moxie - A unique soda with a polarizing taste, it’s a must-try for adventurous drinkers.
  • Allen’s Coffee Brandy - A Maine favorite, this coffee-flavored brandy is delicious in cocktails or on its own.

Where to Stay in Hallowell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Maple Hill Farm Inn and Conference Center - A farmhouse-turned-inn, with beautiful rooms, an indoor pool, and acres of gardens and agricultural fields for outdoor activities.
  • B&B at the Osprey - A quirky and comfortable B&B located in an 1850s Victorian home.
  • Huber’s Boutique Hotel - A charming hotel located in the heart of downtown Hallowell, with beautifully appointed rooms and a lively bar scene.

What to Buy in Hallowell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maine Blueberry Jam - A delicious and local way to start your day.
  • Maine-Inspired Artwork - Find beautiful paintings, sculpture, and prints made by local artists in Hallowell’s galleries.
  • Maine Lobster and Crab - Bring a taste of Maine home by purchasing fresh seafood at Hallowell’s supermarkets and farmers’ markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Hallowell

Maine, and Hallowell in particular, is known for its friendly and welcoming community. Visitors are encouraged to take part in local customs and activities, be polite, and always say “please” and “thank you.”

Best Time to Visit Hallowell

The best time to visit Hallowell is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and the town is bustling with outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hallowell

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the historic and cultural landmarks in Hallowell.
  • Do try the local food and craft beer scene.
  • Do engage with the friendly Hallowell community.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for outdoor activities and walking tours.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for recommendations or directions from Hallowell locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hallowell

Hallowell is a safe and welcoming town, with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking doors and windows.

Health in Hallowell

Hallowell has several medical centers and pharmacies, and visitors are advised to have health insurance coverage for any unforeseen emergencies.

Getting to Hallowell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hallowell

  • Augusta State Airport - a regional airport located 6 miles northeast of Downtown Hallowell

Bus Stations Near Hallowell

  • Concord Coach Lines - offers daily bus service from Boston, Portland, and Bangor to Augusta
  • Greyhound Bus Lines - connects to Augusta from various locations in the US

Other Options

  • Car Rental Services - available at the Augusta State Airport and in neighboring towns like Gardiner and Augusta.

How to Get Around in Hallowell

The best way to get around Hallowell is by car, bike or foot. The compact downtown area is easy to navigate on foot, while cars and bikes are best for exploring the surrounding scenic areas.

Where to Go Next from Hallowell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Augusta - The capital of Maine and located just minutes from Hallowell, Augusta offers museums, art galleries and delicious seafood.
  • Gardiner - Just 4 miles northeast of Hallowell, Gardiner is known for its vibrant cultural scene and historic architecture.
  • Acadia National Park - A beautiful national park located 2.5 hours from Hallowell, Acadia offers breathtaking views, hiking trails, and a stunning coastline.

Conclusion

Hallowell is a small town with a big personality, and a visit here offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re here to explore the museums and landmarks, hike the scenic trails, or immerse yourself in the local culture, Hallowell is sure to delight and inspire.
