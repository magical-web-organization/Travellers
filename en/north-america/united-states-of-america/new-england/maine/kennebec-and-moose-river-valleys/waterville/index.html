Introduction to Waterville

Waterville is a beautiful city located in Kennebec and Moose River Valleys, Maine, the northeastern state of the United States of America. The city is known for its scenic views, rich history, and cultural significance. With a population of over 16,000 people, Waterville is home to several famous landmarks, including Colby College, Waterville Opera House, and the Maine International Film Festival.

In this comprehensive Waterville travel guide, we will walk you through the city’s must-see attractions, best things to do, iconic landmarks, local culture, and customs. We will also touch on transportation options, the best time to visit, and safety tips. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Waterville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The city of Waterville offers a rich cultural experience with several iconic landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Colby College Museum of Art: This museum features over 10,000 works of art from ancient times to the contemporary period. The museum holds special exhibitions and events throughout the year and is open to the public all year round.

  • Waterville Opera House: This theater is an iconic landmark of Waterville, known for hosting musical shows, concerts, and theater performances. The theater also screens classic films, and special events are hosted during the year, including the Maine International Film Festival.

  • Redington Museum: This museum showcases Waterville’s rich history and culture with exhibits focusing on early settlement, farming, and commerce in the area.

Natural Attractions of Waterville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Waterville’s natural landscape is breathtaking and offers a wide range of outdoor activities. Here are some of the top natural attractions of Waterville:

  • Kennebec River: The river is a perfect place for water activities, including kayaking, rafting, and fishing.

  • Messer Pond: This pond is a perfect location for fishing, swimming, and boating, and offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Lithgow Public Library: This library is an excellent place for nature lovers, located in a serene environment, with hiking trails and beautiful gardens surrounding it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waterville

Waterville offers several hiking and biking trails that provide picturesque views of the city’s scenic landscape. Here is a list of the top routes:

  • The Kennebec River Rail Trail: This trail offers scenic views along the Kennebec River, covering a distance of 6.5 miles from Augusta to Gardiner.

  • The Viles Arboretum Trail System: This trail covers over 150 acres of forests, streams, and gardens, offering visitors year-round activities, including hiking, skiing, snowshoeing, and birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Waterville: Activities and Tours

Waterville has a variety of activities and tours for all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Waterville:

  • Maine International Film Festival: The festival is held annually in July and is a celebration of independent films, classics and offers an opportunity to meet and interact with filmmakers.

  • Colby College Concert Series: The concert series features world-renowned musicians and singers who perform at the Waterville Opera House.

  • Waterville Farmers Market: The market is open every Thursday, offering a variety of locally grown produce, fresh seafood, baked goods, and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Waterville

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your visit to Waterville:

Day 1: Colby College Museum of Art and Alfond Youth and Community Center.

Day 2: Kennebec River Rail Trail, Kennebec Chocolates, and Viles Arboretum.

Day 3: Redington Museum and Waterville Opera House.

Day 4: Waterville Farmers Market and Messer Pond.

Day 5: Quarry Road Trails and local food tour.

Day 6: Maine International Film Festival and local shopping.

Day 7: Fishing in the Kennebec River and Waterville Public Library.

Food and Drink Scene of Waterville

Must-Try Dishes

Waterville has a rich culinary culture and offers a wide range of dishes that are worth trying:

  • Lobster Roll: This is a classic seafood dish made from fresh Maine lobster served on a grilled roll.

  • Poutine: This is a French-Canadian dish that consists of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is famous for its wild blueberries, and you must try the classic blueberry pie during your visit.

Local Beverages

Waterville is known for its locally brewed beer and delicious coffee. Visitors can enjoy the refreshing beverages at several cafes and breweries throughout the city. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Berry Fruit Farm: This is a local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

  • Selah Tea Cafe: This cafe offers a variety of hot and cold tea blends, smoothies, and sandwiches.

  • Bard Coffee: Bard Coffee is a popular coffee shop that brews various coffee blends with natural ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Waterville

When visiting Waterville, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Tipping: The customary tipping percentage in Waterville restaurants is 18-20% of the total bill.

  • Behavior in Public: It’s essential to act respectfully and maintain good behavior in public areas.

  • Educate Yourself: Learn about the local history and culture by visiting the Redington Museum and other cultural landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Waterville

The best time to visit Waterville is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy Waterville during the fall and winter seasons, where they can experience the beauty of the changing colors of the leaves and enjoy winter sports like skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waterville

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Tipping is customary in Waterville restaurants, so ensure that you tip appropriately.
  • Visit the iconic landmarks and natural landscapes of the city.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended, especially in cars or public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waterville

Waterville is a safe city for visitors, but as in any other city, it is essential to exercise caution and take necessary safety measures. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Always keep your belongings safe and secure.
  • Don’t walk alone at night in isolated areas.
  • Adhere to traffic and public utility signs and instructions to avoid accidents.

Waterville is known for its excellent health services and medical facilities, with hospitals and clinics located throughout the city. In case of a medical emergency, call 911.

Getting to Waterville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Waterville

The nearest airport to Waterville is Augusta State Airport, which is located 14 miles away from the city. Other airports in the area include Portland International Jetport and Bangor International Airport, which are both located within a two-hour drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Waterville

The Concord Coach Lines bus station is located in Augusta, just 16 miles from Waterville. Buses run daily from Portland, Boston, and New York City.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Waterville using the highway 95 that runs through Maine, or they can use a private car or taxi service to navigate the city.

How to Get Around in Waterville

Visitors can enjoy Waterville’s natural landscape by biking or hiking. Waterville also offers public transportation, including buses and taxi services, to get around the city easily.

Where to Go Next from Waterville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waterville’s central location makes it an ideal base for day trips and excursions. Some of the nearby destinations that you can visit during your stay include Augusta, Belfast, and Camden.

Conclusion

Waterville is a beautiful city located in Maine that offers a rich cultural experience, picturesque views, and exciting outdoor adventures. From visiting iconic landmarks to exploring natural landscapes, Waterville has something for everyone.
