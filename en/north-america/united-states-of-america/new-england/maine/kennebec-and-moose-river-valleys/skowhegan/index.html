Introduction to Skowhegan

Located in Kennebec and Moose River Valleys in Maine, Skowhegan is a scenic town that offers visitors a rich cultural experience and endless outdoor adventures. This charming town is home to museums, historic landmarks, art galleries, and natural attractions that are sure to captivate visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re a history buff, art lover, or nature enthusiast, Skowhegan has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Skowhegan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Skowhegan History House Museum and Research Center: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into the town’s history, with exhibits on the evolution of Skowhegan from Native American settlements to modern times.

  2. Margaret Chase Smith Library: Named after Maine’s first female Senator, this library features exhibits and artifacts related to the life and career of Senator Smith. The museum also holds events and lectures throughout the year.

  3. Maine Grain Alliance and the Somerset Grist Mill: Visiting this historic mill and bakery will give you an opportunity to see how organic grains are locally grown and transformed into delicious bread and pastry.

Natural Attractions of Skowhegan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Skowhegan State Fair: This annual fair runs for 10 days and offers visitors a unique blend of livestock shows, live music, carnival rides, and great food. It’s a must-visit if you’re in Skowhegan during the months of August and September.

  2. Wesserunsett Arts Council: This arts council provides visitors with an opportunity to explore art exhibitions, participate in art workshops, and attend musical performances by local and renowned artists.

  3. Lake George Regional Park: With over 300 acres of parkland and 3200 feet of shoreline, Lake George offers visitors an opportunity to boat, fish, swim, and hike. The park features well-maintained hiking trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skowhegan

  1. Maine Appalachian Trail: Consider taking the Maine part of the Appalachian trail and get started on some great hiking or biking routes. Explore gorges, rivers, forests, and waterfalls while experiencing the beauty of Maine on foot.

  2. Kennebec River: Experience Maine’s most beautiful river by canoeing the Kennebec! With rapids, scenic shores, and a rich history, it’s an adventure like no other.

Best Things to Do in Skowhegan: Activities and Tours

  1. Riverwood RV Park: Enjoy a fun-filled day exploring the outdoors at the Riverwood RV park. Take a dip in the heated pool, go fishing, or explore the scenic forests and riverbanks.

  2. Skowhegan Drive-In Theater: Take a step back in time and experience classic movies at one of the few remaining drive-in theaters in Maine. Showcasing new releases along with old time favorites, this is a great activity for families.

Week-Long Itinerary for Skowhegan

Day 1: Visit Skowhegan History House Museum and Research Center, followed by a stroll around Coburn Park.

Day 2: Explore the art exhibitions and cultural programs at Wesserunsett Arts Council.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive through the Kennebec River Valley and stop for lunch at Lake George Regional Park.

Day 4: Visit Margaret Chase Smith Library and enjoy a live concert by a local artist in Coburn Park.

Day 5: Enjoy a day at Riverwood RV park, with activities such as fishing, boating and exploring the beauty of the park.

Day 6: Explore the natural beauty of Maine by hiking or biking the Maine Appalachian Trail.

Day 7: Enjoy a movie night at Skowhegan Drive-In Theater.

Food and Drink Scene of Skowhegan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Roll: This classic Maine dish must be on your list of must-try dishes when in Skowhegan. Launched some time ago in Maine it quickly became the Maine food culture symbol.

  2. Cider Donuts: An apple cider donut is a Skowhegan icon. Made fresh daily, they are a must-try for any sweet-toothed visitor.

  3. Blueberry pie: A classic Maine dessert, you can’t leave Skowhegan without trying this delicious dessert at least once.

Local Beverages

  1. Maine Craft Beers: Sample some of Maine’s best craft beers at Barrel’s Community Market.

  2. Maine Fruit Wines: Maine is a great place to try fruit wines, and Stetson Winery is the perfect place to do so.

Where to Stay in Skowhegan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Heritage House Bed and Breakfast: This Victorian-era home features luxurious rooms, homemade breakfast, and a picturesque location along the Kennebec River.

  2. Route 2 Motel: This motel features comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and easy access to Skowhegan’s major attractions.

What to Buy in Skowhegan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Maine-made Crafts: Visit Maine-made Crafts for locally made and unique arts and crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and prints.

  2. Maple Syrup and Honey: Treat yourself to some locally produced maple syrup and honey from Skowhegan Farmer’s Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Skowhegan

Skowhegan has a strong sense of local culture and community. Visitors are often struck by the friendliness of the locals, who are happy to share their town’s rich history and cultural traditions. A significant aspect of the local culture is the appreciation of nature and outdoor activities, which are an integral part of Skowhegan’s identity.

Best Time to Visit Skowhegan

The best time to visit Skowhegan is during the summer months of June, July, and August when the weather is pleasant, and most outdoor activities are accessible. However, visitors may also enjoy the fall foliage season that lasts from September to October, which provides stunning views of New England’s colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skowhegan

Do:

  1. Do make an effort to interact with the friendly locals.

  2. Do try local craft beers, and wines

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or harm the local environment.

  2. Don’t forget to carry insect repellent when going out to enjoy nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Skowhegan

Skowhegan is generally a safe place for visitors. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions against theft or personal safety

Health in Skowhegan

Visitors traveling to Skowhegan should take precautions such as carrying sunscreen, bug repellent, and hand sanitizer. It is advisable to check with your doctor if any immunizations are necessary before embarking on your trip.

Getting to Skowhegan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Skowhegan by car or bus from nearby towns and cities. Rental cars are also available from nearby airports.

Airports Near Skowhegan

  1. Portland International Jetport – 90 miles away

  2. Bangor International Airport – 60 miles away

Bus Stations Near Skowhegan

  1. Concord Coach Lines: Provides bus services to and from Skowhegan, including Boston and New York.

Other Options

There are several private shuttle services, car rentals, and ride-sharing services available for transportation to Skowhegan.

How to Get Around in Skowhegan

The best way to get around Skowhegan is by car. Visitors can rent cars or take taxis to visit local attractions.

Where to Go Next from Skowhegan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Sugarloaf Mountain: One of America’s best ski resorts, Sugarloaf Mountain is just under a two-hour drive from Skowhegan.

  2. Acadia National Park: Located three hours away, Acadia National Park offers visitors stunning ocean scenery, wildlife sightings, and miles of hiking trails.

Conclusion

Skowhegan, Maine, offers visitors a wealth of cultural and natural attractions that will leave them captivated and enriched. Visitors can enjoy museums, art exhibitions, outdoor adventures, and local food and drink that reflects the town’s cultural uniqueness and rich history. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer trip, Skowhegan is a charming town that offers something for everyone.
