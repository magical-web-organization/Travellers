Introduction to The Forks

Located in the Kennebec and Moose River Valleys of Maine, The Forks is a small town that serves as a gateway to some of the best outdoor adventures in New England. Surrounded by stunning landscapes and home to world-class activities, The Forks is a must-visit destination for any nature lover, outdoor enthusiast, or adventure seeker.

List of Places to Visit in The Forks

If you’re planning a trip to The Forks, here’s a list of places you must visit to experience the best the town has to offer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Northern Outdoors Adventure Resort: A popular resort that offers various outdoor activities, including white-water rafting, hiking, ATV tours, and snowmobiling.
  • Maine Sport Outfitters: A store that sells outdoor gear and clothing for all seasons.
  • Moxie Outdoor Adventures: An adventure company that offers white-water rafting, moose tours, fishing, and ATV tours.

Natural Attractions of The Forks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kennebec River: A popular destination for white-water rafting and kayaking.
  • Moxie Falls: A stunning 90-feet waterfall located in the Kennebec Valley region.
  • Moosehead Lake: A massive lake that offers various water activities, including boating, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Moxie Mountain: A popular hiking destination that offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Forks

The Forks is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in New England. Here are some popular routes you can explore:

  • The Appalachian Trail: A 2,200-mile trail that starts in Georgia and ends in Maine. The Forks is a popular destination for hikers who are looking to explore a section of the trail.
  • Moxie Bald Mountain: A 5.6-mile round trip trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Kennebec River Rail Trail: A 6.5-mile trail that follows the Kennebec River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in The Forks: Activities and Tours

  • White-water Rafting: The Kennebec and Dead Rivers offer some of the best white-water rafting experiences in New England.
  • ATV Tours: Explore the trails and backroads of Maine with an ATV tour.
  • Fly-Fishing: The Kennebec River is a popular destination for fly-fishing enthusiasts.
  • Snowmobiling: The Forks is also a popular winter destination and offers various snowmobile tours.

Food and Drink Scene of The Forks

If you’re a foodie, The Forks won’t disappoint. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A traditional Maine dish that consists of a lobster salad served in a hot dog bun.
  • Blueberry Pie: A delicious dessert made with fresh Maine blueberries.
  • Gifford’s Famous Ice Cream: A local ice cream company that offers various flavors of ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Allagash White: A Belgian-style wheat beer brewed in Portland, Maine.
  • Maine Craft Distilling: A local distillery that produces various spirits, including rum, gin, and whiskey.
  • Maine Root: A local soda company that offers organic sodas made with natural ingredients.

Best Time to Visit The Forks

The Forks is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Here’s a breakdown of the best times to visit:

  • Summer (June-August): Best for outdoor activities and water adventures.
  • Fall (September-November): Best for hiking, foliage viewing, and hunting.
  • Winter (December-February): Best for winter sports and activities.
  • Spring (March-May): Best for fishing and spring hiking.
