Introduction to Jackman

Nestled in the heart of the Kennebec and Moose River Valleys, in Maine, Jackman is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Surrounded by the stunning beauty of the White Mountains National Forest, Jackman offers breathtaking views of mountains, lakes, and rivers. This small town is a perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful and relaxing getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. With a population of around 900, Jackman is a tight-knit community with a rich history and a vibrant culture that will leave you mesmerized.

List of Places to Visit in Jackman

If you are planning a trip to Jackman, there are many amazing places to visit. We have divided these places into two categories for your convenience:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jackman-Moose River Region Historical Society: Learn about the history of this beautiful region through a collection of artifacts, documents, and photographs.
  • Parlin Pond Blockhouse: Visit the only remaining blockhouse in Maine from the time of the Aroostook War in 1839.
  • Attean Overlook: Take a scenic hike to the Attean Overlook and enjoy the magnificent view of the Moose River Valley.

Natural Attractions of Jackman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Wood Lake: Go fishing, swimming, boating, or camping at this beautiful lake located just a few miles outside of Jackman.
  • Moxie Falls: Located in the nearby town of West Forks, this 90-foot waterfall is one of the most spectacular natural attractions in Maine.
  • Mount Kineo: Take a challenging hike to the summit of Mount Kineo and enjoy panoramic views of the Moosehead Lake region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jackman

Jackman is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The stunning natural scenery of the area is best explored on foot or two wheels. Below are some of the best trails Jackman has to offer:

  • Pleasant Pond Mountain: A moderately difficult 4.6-mile hiking trail that offers some of the best scenic views in the region.
  • Arnold Trail: A 12-mile multi-use trail that follows the route of Benedict Arnold’s historic expedition to Quebec in 1775. Perfect for biking and hiking.
  • Bald Mountain Trail: A popular 1.5-mile hiking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Jackman: Activities and Tours

Jackman offers a variety of activities and tours that are suitable for all ages and interests. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Jackman:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore the scenic lakes and rivers of Jackman while paddling through calm waters.
  • Snowmobiling: Enjoy the winter wonderland of Jackman by snowmobiling on over 200 miles of groomed trails.
  • Wildlife Watching Tours: Spot some of the most elusive wildlife in North America, including moose, deer, and black bears, on a guided wildlife watching tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jackman

If you are planning to spend a week in Jackman, we have put together an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in this beautiful town:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Jackman-Moose River Region Historical Society, explore the town, and dine at one of the local restaurants.
Day 2 Hike the Bald Mountain Trail in the morning, have a picnic lunch at Big Wood Lake, and go fishing in the afternoon.
Day 3 Take a wildlife watching tour in the morning, have lunch at the Attean Overlook, and visit Moxie Falls in the afternoon.
Day 4 Explore the Arnold Trail on a bike, have lunch at the Parlin Pond Blockhouse, and relax in the afternoon.
Day 5 Kayak or canoe on the Moose River and have a picnic lunch on the riverbank.
Day 6 Snowmobile on the trails around Jackman, have lunch at the Polaris Pit Stop, and relax in the afternoon.
Day 7 Take a guided tour of Mount Kineo, have lunch at the clubhouse, and enjoy the rest of the day at leisure.

Food and Drink Scene of Jackman

Jackman is a small town, but it has a surprisingly diverse food and drink scene. From classic American diners to fine dining restaurants, Jackman has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maine Lobster Roll: A classic sandwich made with fresh Maine lobster meat, served on a buttered and grilled bun.
  • Blueberry Pie: A delicious dessert made with locally grown wild blueberries.
  • Poutine: A Canadian classic made with French fries, gravy, and cheese curds.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer: The state has a thriving craft beer scene, with some of the best breweries located in and around Portland.
  • Blueberry Wine: This sweet and fruity wine is made with locally grown blueberries.
  • Allen’s Coffee Brandy: A uniquely Maine drink, Allen’s Coffee Brandy is the most popular liquor in the state.

Where to Stay in Jackman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jackman has a wide range of accommodation options that cater to different tastes and budgets. Whether you prefer a cozy bed and breakfast, a rustic cabin in the woods, or a modern hotel, Jackman has something for you. Here are some of the best places to stay in Jackman:

  • Jackman Maine Cabins: Cozy log cabins located in the heart of the town.
  • ATV Resort at Jackman: A family-friendly resort that offers ATV tours and rentals.
  • The Birches Resort: A year-round resort located on Moosehead Lake, offering a variety of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Jackman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, Jackman has plenty of options. Here are some of the best places to shop in Jackman:

  • Moose River Trading Post: A gift shop that offers a wide variety of locally made products, including maple syrup, pottery, and jewelry.
  • Beehive Gifts: A gift shop that specializes in handmade items, including candles, soaps, and knitted goods.
  • The Shed Antler Shop: A store that offers unique antler-based products, including lamps, knives, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Jackman

Jackman is a tight-knit community that values family, friendliness, and hard work. The locals are known for their warm hospitality and love of the outdoors. The town has a rich history and a vibrant culture that is deeply rooted in the natural beauty of the surrounding area. Visitors are advised to be respectful of the local culture and customs, and to always seek permission before entering private property.

Best Time to Visit Jackman

The best time to visit Jackman depends on your preferences. Summer is the peak season, with warm weather, long days, and a variety of outdoor activities. Fall is also a popular time, with stunning foliage and a quieter atmosphere. Winter is perfect for snowmobiling and skiing, while spring offers a beautiful display of wildflowers and blooming trees. No matter when you decide to visit, Jackman offers something special for every season.

Safety and Health

Jackman is a safe and friendly town with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to follow basic safety precautions, especially when participating in outdoor activities. Here are some important safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Jackman

  • Always wear a helmet when participating in any type of motorized activity, including snowmobiling and ATV riding.
  • Carry a map and a communication device when hiking or biking in remote areas.
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes, especially during winter.

Health in Jackman

  • Always carry a first aid kit when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of ticks and take precautions to avoid tick bites, especially during summer.
  • Drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Jackman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jackman is located in a remote area of Maine, which makes transportation a bit challenging. Here are some ways to get to Jackman:

Airports Near Jackman

  • Bangor International Airport: The closest major airport, located about 2.5 hours from Jackman.
  • Portland International Jetport: Located about 3.5 hours from Jackman.

Bus Stations Near Jackman

  • Concord Coach Lines: Offers bus service to nearby towns, including Farmington and Skowhegan.

Other Options

  • Rental car: If you are flying into a nearby airport, renting a car is the most convenient way to get to Jackman.
  • Private shuttle service: There are several private shuttle services that offer transportation to and from Jackman.

How to Get Around in Jackman

Jackman is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. If you want to explore the surrounding area, renting a car or an ATV is the best option. Snowmobiling is also a popular way to get around during winter.

Where to Go Next from Jackman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time and want to explore more of Maine, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Moosehead Lake: The largest lake in Maine, located about an hour and a half from Jackman.
  • Acadia National Park: A national park known for its stunning beauty and diverse wildlife, located about 4 hours from Jackman.
  • Colby College Museum of Art: A world-class museum that features over 10,000 works of art, located about 2 hours from Jackman.
