Introduction to China

As the world’s most populous country and one of its oldest civilizations, China is steeped in rich history, culture, and natural beauty. From the towering peaks of the Himalayas to the bustling streets of Shanghai, China offers a diverse range of experiences for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in China

China is vast and diverse, with countless cities, attractions, and natural wonders to explore. Some of the must-visit places in China include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Great Wall of China, one of the world’s greatest engineering feats
  • The Forbidden City, the imperial palace complex in the heart of Beijing
  • The Terracotta Army, an army of life-size terracotta sculptures constructed to protect the tomb of the first emperor of China
  • The Summer Palace, a beautiful garden palace in Beijing
  • The Shanghai Museum, showcasing over 120,000 Chinese artifacts from prehistoric to modern times

Natural Attractions of China: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Yangtze River, the longest river in Asia, with stunning gorges and beautiful scenery
  • Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, known for its towering pillars of sandstone and breathtaking views
  • Mount Everest, the highest peak in the world, and the Himalayan mountain range that stretches over 1,500 miles across China
  • The Guilin and Yangshuo regions, featuring gorgeous karst landscapes and the Li River, perfect for hiking, trekking, or cycling

Hiking/Biking Routes of China

China offers plenty of opportunities for hikers and bikers to explore its beautiful landscapes. Some popular routes include:

  • The Great Wall Trek, a challenging hike and scenic route along the ancient walls of China
  • Mount Huangshan, one of the most famous peaks in China, with scenic hiking trails and stunning vistas
  • The Lijiang River bike tour, a scenic and leisurely bike ride through the picturesque Yangshuo countryside

Best Things to Do in China: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to adventure sports, China has plenty to offer travelers. Some activities and tours worth checking out include:

  • The Peking Opera, a traditional Chinese art form that combines singing, dancing, and acrobatics
  • Kung Fu classes, where visitors can learn about the ancient martial art from a master instructor
  • Cooking classes, where travelers can learn to make traditional Chinese dishes like dumplings, baozi, and hot pot
  • The Karakoram Highway, one of the world’s highest and most spectacular roads, featuring breathtaking views of the Himalayas

Week-Long Itinerary for China

To make the most of your trip to China, we recommend spending at least a week exploring some of the country’s top destinations. Here’s a sample itinerary to get you started:

Day 1 Beijing: The Forbidden City and Tiananmen Square
Day 2 Beijing: The Great Wall of China
Day 3 Xian: The Terracotta Warriors
Day 4 Chengdu: The Giant Pandas and Sichuan Cuisine
Day 5 Lijiang and Yangshuo: Scenic Landscapes and Outdoor Activities
Day 6 Shanghai: The Bund and Nanjing Road shopping
Day 7 Shanghai: The Yu Garden and Shanghai Museum

Food and Drink Scene of China

Chinese cuisine is one of the world’s most popular and diverse, with a wide range of savory flavors and unique regional specialties. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peking duck, a roasted duck dish served with pancakes, scallions, and a sweet and savory sauce
  • Dumplings, a staple of Chinese cuisine that come in a variety of shapes, fillings, and cooking methods
  • Hot pot, a communal meal where diners cook raw meats, seafood, and vegetables in a simmering broth
  • Kung Pao chicken, a spicy and stir-fried dish that originated in Sichuan province
  • Mapo tofu, a spicy and silken tofu dish that originated in Chuan cuisine

Local Beverages

  • Tea, the national drink of China, with a variety of types, such as green tea, black tea, and Dragon Well tea from Hangzhou
  • Baijiu, a strong Chinese liquor made from grains like wheat and barley
  • Tsingtao beer, one of China’s most popular beer brands, originating in Qingdao city

Where to Stay in China: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

China has a wide range of accommodation options available, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • The Ritz Carlton, a luxury hotel chain with locations in Beijing, Shanghai, and other major cities
  • The Mandarin Oriental, another luxury hotel chain with locations in Chengdu, Guangzhou, and other cities
  • The W Hostels, a stylish and hip hostel chain with locations in Beijing, Shanghai, and Xian
  • The China Homestay Network, a network of guesthouses and homestays throughout China

What to Buy in China: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

China is famous for its silk, porcelain, jade, and other traditional handicrafts, which make for great souvenirs and gifts. Some other popular items to buy in China include:

  • Tea and tea sets from Hangzhou
  • Chinese calligraphy supplies and artwork
  • Silk scarves, dresses, and fabrics from Suzhou
  • Chinese-made electronics and gadgets from Shenzhen

Local Culture and Customs of China

Chinese culture is known for its long history, rich traditions, and diverse ethnic groups. Visitors should be aware of some cultural norms and customs when traveling in China, such as:

  • Respect for elders and authority figures is important in Chinese culture.
  • Removing shoes inside homes and some restaurants, especially in the northern part of the country, is customary.
  • Tipping is not customary in China, instead it can be considered rude.

Best Time to Visit China

The best time to visit China depends on a variety of factors, such as the region you plan to visit, your activities of choice, and your tolerance for crowds and weather conditions. Spring (April to May) and fall (September to October) are generally considered the best seasons to visit most parts of China.

Do’s and Don’ts of China

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to China, be sure to follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Practice basic Mandarin phrases and manners
  • Carry cash, as many places don’t accept credit cards
  • Keep your passport and important documents in a safe and secure place
  • Use designated smoking areas

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of military personnel or installations - this is strictly prohibited.
  • Eat or drink tap water before it is boiled, as water-borne diseases are common
  • Be disrespectful of Chinese culture and traditions

Safety and Health

While China is a relatively safe and healthy destination, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in China

  • Beware of pickpockets and scammers in crowded areas and public transportation
  • Keep your valuables secure and stay alert in unfamiliar areas
  • Familiarize yourself with emergency numbers in China, such as 110 for police and 120 for ambulance

Health in China

  • Take out medical insurance before your trip and carry medicines necessary to avoid food and waterborne illnesses.
  • Avoid street food and raw ingredients to prevent food poisoning or gastrointestinal problems.
  • Consult with a doctor if you plan on hiking or trekking in remote areas.

Getting to China: Transportation Options and Logistics

China is accessible by air, land, and sea, with many transport options available for travelers. Some popular options include:

Airports Near China

  • Beijing Capital International Airport, the busiest airport in China, with direct flights to major cities around the world
  • Shanghai Pudong International Airport, a major hub for international flights to China and other Asian destinations
  • Hong Kong International Airport, another major hub for travel to and from China

Bus Stations Near China

  • The Beijing Central Bus Station, a major hub for inter-regional and international buses
  • The Shanghai Long-Distance Bus Terminal, offering services to most provinces and major cities across China
  • The Guangzhou Provincial Bus Terminal, one of the largest bus stations in southern China

Other Options

  • Train travel in China is also popular, boasting the world’s largest network of high-speed trains.
  • Some visitors opt to take cruises along the Yangtze River, which offer stunning views of the countryside and relaxing amenities on board.

How to Get Around in China

Transportation options vary across the cities, but most visitors rely on a mix of taxis, public transportation buses and subway systems, or car rentals if going to non-urban places.

Where to Go Next from China: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

China is surrounded by multiple countries that make for excellent post-trip destinations. Popular destinations to travel to after China include:

  • Vietnam, with unique cultural heritage sites, bustling cities, and beautiful beaches
  • Mongolia, cherished for its nomadic lifestyle and breathtaking steppes, as well as Gobi Desert
  • Japan, with its delicious cuisine, arts and crafts and, modern technology and architecture
  • South Korea, with splendid mountains, historic temples, and delectable cuisine

Conclusion

China has something to offer for everyone, from rich cultural experiences to breathtaking natural landscapes. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient history, trying delicious food, or hiking through stunning mountains, China offers an unforgettable experience. By following the tips and recommendations outlined in this guide, you’re sure to make the most of your trip to this fascinating country.
