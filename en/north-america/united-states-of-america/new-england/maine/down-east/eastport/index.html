Introduction to Eastport

Located in the Down East region of Maine, Eastport is a picturesque coastal city known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful natural surroundings. Boasting stunning views of the Bay of Fundy and the Canadian island of Campobello, this charming town offers a wide range of attractions and activities that cater to visitors of all ages and interests.

Whether you’re a history buff looking to explore the city’s historic sites, a nature lover hoping to embark on outdoor adventures, or a foodie eager to sample some of Eastport’s delicious cuisine, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your visit to this spectacular destination.

List of Places to Visit in Eastport

If you’re planning a trip to Eastport, you won’t want to miss out on these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Tides Institute & Museum of Art: Located in the heart of downtown Eastport, this museum showcases the history and arts of the surrounding region.
  • The Eastport Historic District: Take a self-guided walking tour through the historic district and admire the charming 19th-century architecture.
  • The Raye’s Mustard Mill Museum: Discover the fascinating history behind America’s oldest stone-ground mustard mill and sample some of the delicious products for yourself.
  • The Peavey Memorial Library: Built in the early 1900s, this stunning library features beautiful stained glass windows and a collection of rare books.

Natural Attractions of Eastport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Quoddy Head State Park: Explore the rugged coastline, hike scenic trails, and marvel at the iconic red-and-white striped lighthouse.
  • Moose Island: Located in the heart of Eastport, this island offers incredible views of the town’s historic waterfront and bustling harbor.
  • Cobscook Bay State Park: Boasting over 888 acres of lush forests and tidal estuaries, this park is a prime destination for hiking, camping, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastport

If you’re looking to explore Eastport’s natural beauty on foot or by bike, be sure to check out these popular routes:

  • Quoddy Head State Park Loop: This 5-mile loop takes hikers and bikers around the park’s stunning coastline and through lush forests.
  • Mud Hole Trail: Located in the Cobscook Bay State Park, this 7-mile trail winds through tidal flats and marshes, offering incredible views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Eastport and Shackford Head State Park Circuit: This 11-mile route takes visitors through charming coastal towns, stunning natural landscapes, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Eastport: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, Eastport has something to offer every type of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Whale Watching Tours: Hop aboard a boat tour and observe majestic humpback whales and porpoises in their natural habitat.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take a guided or self-guided tour and explore the area’s scenic bays and waterways.
  • Beachcombing and Clamming: Eastport is home to several secluded beaches that are perfect for beachcombing and clamming.
  • Music and Art Festivals: Throughout the year, Eastport hosts several lively music and art festivals that showcase the town’s vibrant culture and creative spirit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastport

Day 1: Arrive in Eastport and check into your accommodations. Spend the afternoon exploring the town’s charming downtown area.

Day 2: Visit the Tides Institute & Museum of Art and the Eastport Historic District.

Day 3: Enjoy a whale watching tour or kayaking excursion.

Day 4: Embark on a scenic hike through Quoddy Head State Park.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Lubec and explore its historic downtown area and stunning coastal scenery.

Day 6: Visit the Raye’s Mustard Mill Museum and the Peavey Memorial Library.

Day 7: Spend the day beachcombing and clamming along one of Eastport’s secluded beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastport

No trip to Eastport would be complete without sampling some of the area’s delicious cuisine and local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: Eastport is known for its fresh, succulent lobster, and its lobster rolls are a local favorite.
  • Fried Clam Strips: These crispy, golden-brown clams are a staple of New England cuisine.
  • Smoked Seafood: Eastport is home to several smokehouses that specialize in smoked fish and other seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Eastport is home to a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries offering unique, flavorful brews.
  • Blueberry Wine: Maine is famous for its juicy, plump blueberries, and many local wineries offer flavorful blueberry wines.

Where to Stay in Eastport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastport offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Some top options include:

  • The Kilby House Inn: This charming inn is located in the heart of downtown Eastport and features comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • The Eastland Motel: This budget-friendly motel is located just outside of town and offers clean, comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.
  • The Shackford Head State Park Campground: For a more rustic experience, consider camping at this beautiful state park campground.

What to Buy in Eastport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect Eastport souvenir to bring home? Here are some ideas:

  • Raye’s Mustard: Take home a jar of America’s oldest stone-ground mustard, made right here in Eastport.
  • Local Art and Crafts: Eastport is home to several talented artists and craftsmen, and you’ll find a variety of unique, handmade items in local shops and galleries.
  • Seafood and Smoked Fish: If you’re a foodie, consider taking home some smoked seafood or fresh-caught fish for a delicious taste of Eastport.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastport

Eastport is known for its friendly, welcoming community, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs. Here are a few tips to help you make the most of your visit to Eastport:

  • Respect Local Traditions: Eastport has a rich history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect local traditions and customs.
  • Embrace the Outdoors: With its stunning coastline and lush forests, Eastport is a prime destination for outdoor adventures, so be sure to take advantage of the area’s natural beauty.
  • Support Local Businesses: Eastport is home to many small, locally owned businesses, so be sure to shop and dine at local establishments to support the local economy.

Best Time to Visit Eastport

The best time to visit Eastport depends on what you’re looking for. During the summer months (June through August), the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor festivals and events to enjoy. In the fall, visitors can take advantage of the stunning foliage and harvest festivals, while the winter months offer a chance to enjoy the town’s quiet, cozy charm.

Safety and Health

When visiting Eastport, it’s important to keep safety and health in mind. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Eastport

  • Always wear appropriate footwear and be mindful of slippery rocks and unstable terrain when hiking or exploring the coastline.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables out of sight to deter theft.
  • Familiarize yourself with emergency procedures and contact information for local authorities and medical facilities.

Health in Eastport

  • Stay hydrated and avoid overexertion in hot weather.
  • Use insect repellent and protective clothing to avoid tick bites.
  • Be sure to pack any necessary medications and first aid supplies.

Getting to Eastport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastport is located in the northeastern corner of Maine and is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Eastport:

Airports Near Eastport

  • Bangor International Airport (BGR)
  • Portland International Jetport (PWM)

Bus Stations Near Eastport

  • Concord Coach Lines
  • Greyhound Bus Lines

Other Options

  • Private Transportation Services: Several private transportation companies offer shuttle and taxi services to and from Eastport.

How to Get Around in Eastport

Eastport is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, a car or bicycle can be useful for exploring the surrounding area and reaching more remote destinations. Taxis and private transportation services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Eastport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore the area, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Lubec: This charming town is located just across the bridge from Eastport and offers stunning coastal scenery and a rich maritime history.
  • Acadia National Park: Located about 2 hours south of Eastport, this iconic national park boasts rugged coastline, towering mountains, and lush forests.
  • Bar Harbor: This quaint seaside town is located about 2 hours south of Eastport and offers charming downtown shops, restaurants, and galleries.

Conclusion

From its stunning natural beauty to its rich history and vibrant culture, Eastport is a destination that truly has it all.
