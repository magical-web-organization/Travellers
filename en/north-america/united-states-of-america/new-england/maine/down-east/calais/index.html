Introduction to Calais

Calais is a charming city in Washington County, Maine, situated near the Canadian border. Known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures, Calais is a must-visit destination for tourists. The city’s name is derived from the French word “calais,” which means “a boat harbor.” The city was incorporated in 1809 and was one of the earliest settlements in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Calais

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calais is a treasure trove for history buffs, with several museums and historical places to explore. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • St. Croix Island International Historical Site: This site is believed to be the location of the first French settlement in North America. It is also the site of an important archeological discovery of the 1980s.
  • Holmes Cottage Museum: This museum portrays the life of 19th-century families in Calais. The cottage was home to a wealthy sea captain and has preserved his life and family history.
  • Washington County Courthouse: This courthouse serves as a witness to the county’s history and the legal system of the time.

Natural Attractions of Calais: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calais is a nature lover’s paradise, with breathtaking views, hiking/biking trails, and scenic drives. These are some popular natural attractions:

  • Reversing Falls Park: Two sections are separated by a narrow gorge that creates the reversing falls. Visitors can enjoy hiking, picnicking, and amazing views.
  • Black’s Beach: A short walk from downtown, this beach is the perfect spot for a swim or a cold drink while taking in the spectacular view.
  • Calais Marsh Boardwalk: Experience the beauty of the marsh without getting wet. There’s an accessible boardwalk for you to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calais

Calais is a great place for hiking and biking, with various scenic trails for all fitness levels. Here are some we recommend:

  • Downeast Sunrise Trail: Explore the scenic trail that connects with Machias, Calais, and Eastport, finishing in Ayers Junction.
  • Bold Coast Trails: Follow the coast and experience the rugged beauty of Downeast Maine.
  • Moosehorn National Wildlife Refuge: Enjoy trails for all abilities, wildlife watching, and nature for all.

Best Things to Do in Calais: Activities and Tours

Calais offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and age groups. These activities include:

  • Whale Watching: Embark on a whale watching tour to see humpbacks, minke, and fin whales in their natural habitat.
  • Kayaking: Experience nature in a unique way by taking a guided kayaking tour along the St. Croix River.
  • Fishing: Find some of the world’s best salmon fishing locations on the St. Croix and nearby streams.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calais

Here’s our proposed week-long itinerary for Calais:

  • Day 1: Explore historic landmarks and museums in Downtown Calais, such as Holmes Cottage, the Washington County Courthouse, and the St. Croix Island International Historical Site.
  • Day 2: Visit the Reversing Falls Park and the Calais Marsh Boardwalk for scenic views of the Downeast coast.
  • Day 3: Go kayaking along the St. Croix River and enjoy nature’s beauty.
  • Day 4: Take a whale watching tour to witness the majesty of humpbacks, minke, and fin whales.
  • Day 5: Hike the Bold Coast Trail and see scenic views of the coast.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day at Black’s Beach for swimming, sunbathing, or a cold drink.
  • Day 7: Go fishing on the St. Croix or nearby streams, one of the world’s best salmon fishing locations.

Food and Drink Scene of Calais

Calais food and drink scene has a little bit of everything: plenty of seafood, award-winning pies, and unique local brews. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A popular seafood dish featuring fresh lobster meat tucked into a buttery, grilled roll.
  • Wild Blueberry Pie: A local favorite pie made with fresh Downeast Maine blueberries.
  • Burgers and Fries: A classic American dish, but you may not want to miss the local twist. The patties are often made from local beef.

Local Beverages

  • Blueberry Ale: A beer brewed locally featuring the taste of Downeast blueberries.
  • Maple Syrup: A locally produced, natural sweetener used in ice cream, coffee, and desserts with distinctive flavor.

Where to Stay in Calais: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calais has some excellent lodging options catering to a range of budgets and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • The Hotel: A beautiful historic hotel with an antique charm that features modern amenities like free Wi-Fi and air conditioning.
  • Airbnb: Many Airbnb options are also available, including independent cottages, apartments with stunning Downeast views.
  • Motel: Calais has a range of motels available in the city for those who want a comfortable stay while traveling on a budget.

What to Buy in Calais: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Calais, take home some souvenirs and local products to share with friends and family. Here are some unique items you shouldn’t miss:

  • Maple Syrup: A natural sweetener that’s locally produced and served as a topping for pancakes or a sweetener in your coffee.
  • Blueberry Jam: A popular jam made with fresh Downeast Maine blueberries that are mixed with sugar and pectin to make a yummy spread.
  • Hand-Crafted Artifacts: The city is known for its hand-crafted artifacts that are unique and authentic.

Local Culture and Customs of Calais

Calais has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its history. The Downeast coast is known for its strong community and work ethic, and it’s evident in the local culture. Here are some cultural norms to keep in mind:

  • Friendliness: Mainers are well-known for their friendliness, and it’s no different in Calais. People are always willing to help, so don’t hesitate to ask for assistance.

Best Time to Visit Calais

The best time to visit Calais is during summers between June and August when the weather is pleasant and the city comes alive. The city celebrates its heritage with an array of events, including the International Festival of Toys, the Blueberry Festival, and the International Homecoming Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calais

Do:

  • Take a kayak tour: It’s the best way to explore the wilderness while keeping an eco-friendly approach.
  • Explore historic landmarks and museums: A great way to learn about the city’s heritage.
  • Take lots of photographs: Don’t forget to take your camera as Calais, Maine has some stunning landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas: Calais, Maine is a beautiful city, so respect nature and keep it clean.
  • Leave anything valuable in the car: It is essential to take your valuable or lock up valuable items when leaving your car.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calais

Calais is a safe city for tourists and has a low crime rate. However, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings safe. It’s also crucial to follow safety protocols to prevent accidents during outdoor activities like hiking or kayaking.

Health in Calais

Calais has several health facilities, including pharmacies, hospitals, and doctors’ offices. However, it’s important to have travel health insurance and take precautions accordingly during the Covid-19 pandemic.

Getting to Calais: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calais is situated near the Canadian border and can be accessed via car or bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Calais

The closest airport to Calais is Bangor International Airport, located approximately 112 miles south of the city.

Bus Stations Near Calais

Concord Coach Lines offers bus services from Bangor and other nearby cities to Calais and other destinations.

Other Options

You can take a car to get to Calais, or you can also rent a car and drive to the city.

How to Get Around in Calais

Calais is easily navigable by car. However, if you’re looking to explore the city at a more leisurely pace, consider using a bike or walking around as the city is small and charming.

Where to Go Next from Calais: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calais is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding areas of Downeast Maine, including Eastport, Machias, and Lubec. However, If you are looking for nature, then visit Acadia National Park, which is 140 miles southwest of Calais.

Conclusion

Calais, Maine, is the perfect destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure enthusiasts. From scenic hiking trails and historic museums to unique shopping experiences and a variety of food and drink options, Calais has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable journey in Calais.
