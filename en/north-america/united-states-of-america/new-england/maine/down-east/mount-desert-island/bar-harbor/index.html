Introduction to Bar Harbor

Nestled amid the breathtaking beauty of Mount Desert Island, Bar Harbor is a charming town in Down East Maine that’s home to stunning vistas, abundant wildlife, and a host of attractions for all ages. This picturesque destination has something for everyone — from hiking and exploring to soaking up new experiences in the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Bar Harbor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Abbe Museum: Discover the fascinating history and culture of Maine’s native Wabanaki people, who have lived in the area for over 11,000 years.
  • Bar Harbor Historical Society Museum: Discover the rich and storied past of Bar Harbor through exhibits, photographs, and interactive displays that showcase the town’s origins as a fishing and shipbuilding hub in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Bar Harbor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Acadia National Park: Explore the stunning natural scenery of Acadia National Park, which boasts over 120 miles of scenic hiking trails, pristine lakes and ponds, and incredible views of the rugged coast and surrounding islands.
  • Cadillac Mountain: Drive or hike to the highest peak on the East Coast and enjoy unparalleled views of the sunrise and sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bar Harbor

  • Jordan Pond Path: This easy, 3.2-mile hike is perfect for families and takes you around the beautiful Jordan Pond, where you can enjoy views of the surrounding mountains and wooded areas.

  • Eagle Lake Trail: Take a stroll through pristine forestland and enjoy the tranquility of this 6-mile loop trail, which is great for all levels of hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Bar Harbor: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tour: Head out to the ocean and get up close and personal with some of the world’s largest and most majestic creatures on a whale watching tour.
  • Kayaking Tours: Explore the secluded coves, inlets, and bays of Mount Desert Island on a kayaking tour that combines adventure and relaxation in equal measure.

Food and Drink Scene of Bar Harbor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Sample the local seafood for which Maine is renowned by indulging in the classic lobster roll, which is typically loaded with succulent chunks of lobster meat and served in a buttery, toasted roll.
  • Blueberry Pie: Savor the sweetness of Maine’s legendary blueberries by enjoying a slice of the local blueberry pie, which features a flaky, buttery crust and a mouthwatering filling that’s made from fresh-picked blueberries.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer: Sip on some locally crafted brews from some of Maine’s finest microbreweries, such as Bar Harbor Brewing Company, Shipyard Brewing Company, and Allagash Brewing Company.
  • Lobster Bloody Mary: Add some local flair to your brunch by trying a lobster bloody mary, which offers a distinctive twist on the classic cocktail and can be found at many of the area’s bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Bar Harbor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bar Harbor Inn & Spa: Experience luxury accommodations in the heart of the downtown area with stunning views of Frenchman Bay and access to indoor and outdoor pools, a spa, and gourmet dining.
  • Acadia Yurts & Wellness Center: Embrace nature in these eco-friendly yurts, which offer breathtaking views of the Maine coast and come equipped with a full kitchen, private bathroom, and outdoor shower.
  • Wonder View Inn & Suites: Enjoy comfortable and affordable lodging with panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean and close proximity to the area’s best attractions.

What to Buy in Bar Harbor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blueberry Jam: Take home a taste of Maine by picking up a jar of locally made blueberry jam, which pairs perfectly with fresh-baked bread or English muffins.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Support local artisans and bring home a beautiful piece of handcrafted pottery, which often features designs inspired by the stunning natural scenery of the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Bar Harbor

Maine has a rich and distinctive culture that’s shaped by its unique geography, history, and people. Here are a few customs and traditions that you may encounter during your visit to Bar Harbor:

  • Lobstering: The lobster industry is an integral part of Maine’s economy and way of life, and many locals are intimately connected to it through family or work.
  • Acadian Culture: The traditional culture of French Acadians is still celebrated and preserved in many parts of Maine, including Bar Harbor, where you can attend fairs and festivals that feature French-speaking singers, dancers, and other cultural icons.

Best Time to Visit Bar Harbor

The best time to visit Bar Harbor is during the fall (September to November), when the weather is mild, the crowds are smaller, and the leaves on the area’s trees turn brilliant shades of red, orange, and yellow. However, the summer (June to August) is a popular time to visit, too, thanks to the warm temperatures and abundance of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bar Harbor

Do:

  • Explore Acadia National Park: With over 120 miles of hiking trails, pristine lakes and ponds, and breathtaking views, Acadia National Park is a must-visit attraction for anyone visiting Bar Harbor.

Don’t:

  • Forget your sunscreen: Even on overcast days, the sun’s rays can be intense, so be sure to protect your skin with a high-SPF sunscreen.
  • Litter or damage the local wildlife or flora: Maine’s natural beauty is one of its greatest treasures, so be a responsible visitor and help to protect it by avoiding littering or damaging the local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bar Harbor

Bar Harbor is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should exercise the same caution as they would in any other tourist town. Here are a few tips to stay safe:

  • Be aware of traffic: Bar Harbor’s narrow streets can be congested at times, so pedestrians and cyclists should be cautious when crossing roads or navigating through traffic.
  • Watch your step: The rocky terrain at Acadia National Park can be challenging for visitors to navigate, so be sure to wear sturdy shoes with good traction and watch your step on slippery or uneven surfaces.

Health in Bar Harbor

Like any destination, visitors to Bar Harbor should take steps to stay healthy during their trip. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Stay hydrated: The weather in Maine can be unpredictable, so be sure to bring plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Pack your first aid kit: It’s always a good idea to pack a small first aid kit with basic supplies such as bandages, pain relievers, and antiseptic wipes.

Getting to Bar Harbor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bar Harbor

  • Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport: This regional airport is located just 12 miles from the town center and offers seasonal flights to several major cities in the Northeast, including Boston, New York, and Philadelphia.

Bus Stations Near Bar Harbor

  • Bangor Bus Station: Located about 50 miles from Bar Harbor, the Bangor Bus Station serves several major bus lines, including Greyhound and Concord Coach Lines.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Many visitors to Bar Harbor choose to drive their own car or rent a car to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Bar Harbor

The best way to get around Bar Harbor is on foot, as the downtown area is quite small and easy to navigate. Bicycles and trolleys are also popular transportation options, and taxi and shuttle services are available for longer trips or visits to areas outside of the town center.

Where to Go Next from Bar Harbor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland, Maine: Located just 2.5 hours south of Bar Harbor, Portland is a charming seaside city that’s famous for its vibrant food and drink scene, historic architecture, and active waterfront.
  • Rockland, Maine: This scenic coastal town is less than 2 hours south of Bar Harbor and features a thriving art scene, stunning views of the coast, and some of Maine’s best seafood.

Conclusion

No matter when you visit, Bar Harbor is a breathtakingly beautiful destination that’s full of adventure, history, and local charm. Just be sure to take time to savor all that this unique corner of Maine has to offer, and soak in the natural beauty and rich culture that make it such a special place.
