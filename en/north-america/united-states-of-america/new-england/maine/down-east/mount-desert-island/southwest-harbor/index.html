Introduction to Southwest Harbor

Located in Mount Desert Island, Down East Maine, Southwest Harbor is a hidden gem nestled between the mountains of Acadia National Park and the coast of Maine. With its charming Main Street, scenic harbor views, and welcoming community, Southwest Harbor offers a relaxing and authentic Maine experience for travelers seeking to escape the hustle and bustle of larger tourist destinations. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, eat, and stay in Southwest Harbor, as well as local culture and customs, safety tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Southwest Harbor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wendell Gilley Museum: Discover the artistry of woodcarving and the work of Wendell Gilley, a Southwest Harbor artist and bird carver.
  • Southwest Harbor Historical Society Museum: Explore the history of Southwest Harbor and Mount Desert Island through interactive exhibits and artifacts.
  • St. Sauveur Church: Admire the beauty of this 19th-century Romanesque church designed by John Calvin Stevens.
  • Sumner Memorial High School: Visit the birthplace of former U.S. Secretary of State and Nobel Peace Prize winner, Elihu Root.

Natural Attractions of Southwest Harbor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Acadia National Park: With over 49,000 acres of natural beauty, Acadia National Park offers hiking trails, scenic drives, and stunning views of the Maine coastline.
  • Echo Lake Beach: Take a dip in the crystal clear waters of Echo Lake, surrounded by the mountains of Acadia National Park.
  • Beech Mountain Trail: Hike to the top of Beech Mountain for a panoramic view of Mount Desert Island and the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Bass Harbor Head Lighthouse: Admire the iconic red and white striped lighthouse perched on a cliff overlooking the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southwest Harbor

Southwest Harbor offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts of all levels. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Acadia Mountain Trail: This moderate hike offers stunning views of Somes Sound and the surrounding mountains.
  • Wonderland Trail: This easy hike takes you through a section of Acadia National Park, with great views of the ocean and rocky coastline.
  • Carriage Roads: Take a leisurely bike ride on the scenic 45-mile network of Carriage Roads, built by John D. Rockefeller Jr. in the early 20th century.
  • Jordan Pond Path: This flat, easy trail loops around the peaceful Jordan Pond, offering views of the surrounding mountains and the famous Jordan Pond House.

Best Things to Do in Southwest Harbor: Activities and Tours

  • Whale watching: Take a boat tour from Southwest Harbor and witness the majestic humpback, fin, and minke whales in their natural habitat.
  • Kayaking: Explore the rugged coastline of Acadia National Park and spot wildlife such as seals, dolphins, and bald eagles.
  • Lobster boat tour: Learn about the Maine lobster fishing industry and catch a glimpse of lobster traps in action on a guided boat tour.
  • Bar Harbor and Acadia National Park tour: Discover the highlights of Bar Harbor and Acadia National Park on a guided bus tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southwest Harbor

Day 1: Explore the town center of Southwest Harbor, including the Wendell Gilley Museum and the Southwest Harbor Historical Society Museum.

Day 2: Hike the Acadia Mountain Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the coastline.

Day 3: Take a whale watching tour and spot humpback whales, minke whales, and dolphins in their natural habitat.

Day 4: Bike the Carriage Roads and admire the beauty of Acadia National Park.

Day 5: Take a lobster boat tour and learn about the Maine lobster fishing industry, then enjoy a fresh lobster dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 6: Kayak along the scenic coastline of Acadia National Park and spot wildlife such as seals, otters, and bald eagles.

Day 7: Relax at Echo Lake Beach and take in the natural beauty of Mount Desert Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Southwest Harbor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll: This classic Maine dish features fresh lobster meat served on a buttered and toasted roll.
  • Blueberry pie: Made from Maine’s famous wild blueberries, this sweet treat is a must-try dessert.
  • Clam chowder: A hearty soup made with fresh clams, potatoes, and cream, this dish is a New England classic.
  • Maple syrup: Maine is known for producing some of the best maple syrup in the world, and it’s a delicious addition to pancakes, waffles, and more.

Local Beverages

  • Maine craft beer: With over 150 craft breweries in Maine, there’s no shortage of options for beer lovers.
  • Blueberry wine: Made from Maine’s wild blueberries, this fruity wine is a unique and delicious local specialty.
  • Moxie soda: A New England favorite, this unique soda has a distinct, slightly bitter flavor that you’ll either love or hate.

Where to Stay in Southwest Harbor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southwest Harbor offers a range of accommodations to fit any traveler’s budget and preferences, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  • Asticou Inn: This historic inn offers elegant rooms and suites with ocean or garden views, as well as on-site dining and a spa.
  • Harbor Ridge: This cozy bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms with private bathrooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.
  • Quietside Inn: This charming motel features affordable rooms with beautiful views of the Acadia Mountains and a convenient location near the town center.
  • The Drydock Inn: This boutique inn offers stylish rooms with modern amenities, as well as a rooftop deck with panoramic views of the harbor.

What to Buy in Southwest Harbor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lobster gear: Take home a piece of Maine with a lobster-themed t-shirt, hat, or kitchen accessory.
  • Blueberry products: Stock up on blueberry jam, syrup, and other tasty treats made from Maine’s famous wild blueberries.
  • Handmade crafts: Support local artisans and take home a unique souvenir such as pottery, jewelry, or woodcarvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Southwest Harbor

Maine is known for its friendly people, scenic beauty, and love of seafood. Visitors to Southwest Harbor should be prepared to slow down and enjoy the simple pleasures of life, such as a leisurely boat ride or a fresh lobster dinner. It’s also important to respect the local environment and wildlife, and follow the Leave No Trace principles when hiking and exploring.

Best Time to Visit Southwest Harbor

The best time to visit Southwest Harbor is from late spring through early fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. Peak tourist season is from July to September, so travelers who prefer smaller crowds should consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of May or October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southwest Harbor

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood, especially lobster.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Acadia National Park.
  • Do respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Do slow down and enjoy the simple pleasures of life.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and insect repellent when hiking or spending time outside.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the trails or beaches.
  • Don’t miss out on the chance to try blueberry pie or other local specialties.
  • Don’t rush through your visit - take the time to savor the beauty of Southwest Harbor and Acadia National Park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southwest Harbor

Southwest Harbor is a safe and welcoming community, but travelers should take common sense precautions such as locking doors and not leaving valuables in their cars. When hiking or exploring the outdoors, it’s important to be prepared with proper gear and to follow the Leave No Trace principles.

Health in Southwest Harbor

Travelers to Southwest Harbor should be aware of tick-borne illnesses such as Lyme disease, and take precautions such as wearing long pants and using insect repellent when hiking in wooded areas. It’s also important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outside.

Getting to Southwest Harbor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southwest Harbor

  • Bangor International Airport (BGR): 45 miles away
  • Portland International Jetport (PWM): 154 miles away
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT): 220 miles away

Bus Stations Near Southwest Harbor

  • Concord Coach Lines: Offers daily bus service from Boston, Portland, and other Maine locations to Bar Harbor, just a short drive from Southwest Harbor.

Other Options

  • Rental car: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the area, and there are several rental car agencies located near the airports.
  • Ferry: The CAT ferry connects Yarmouth, Nova Scotia to Bar Harbor, Maine during the summer months.

How to Get Around in Southwest Harbor

Southwest Harbor is a small town, and most attractions and accommodations are within walking or biking distance of each other. There are also taxi and shuttle services available, as well as bike rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Southwest Harbor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bar Harbor: Just a short drive from Southwest Harbor, Bar Harbor offers a vibrant downtown area with shopping, restaurants, and nightlife.
  • Mount Desert Island: Explore the rest of Mount Desert Island, including the Park Loop Road, Sand Beach, and Cadillac Mountain.
  • Blue Hill: A charming small town with local galleries and shops, located just a short drive from Southwest Harbor.
  • Portland: This vibrant coastal city is a great destination for food lovers and history buffs, with excellent restaurants and museums.

Conclusion

Southwest Harbor is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a relaxing and authentic Maine experience. With its natural beauty, friendly community, and range of attractions and activities, there’s something for everyone in this charming seaside town.
