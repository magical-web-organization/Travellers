Castine Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Castine, Maine

Located in the Hancock County of Down East Maine, Castine is a charming town with a rich history and beautiful scenic views. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or just relaxing in quaint surroundings, Castine has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and helpful tips to make the most of your trip to Castine.

Introduction to Castine

Castine is a small town in Maine that is known for its rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. The town was founded in 1613 and has served as a strategic location for American, British, and French forces throughout its history. Today, Castine is a popular tourist destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Castine

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich history or enjoying its beautiful natural surroundings, Castine has plenty to offer visitors. Here are some of the must-visit places in Castine:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castine Historical Society: The Castine Historical Society is a non-profit organization that is dedicated to preserving the town’s rich history. The society operates a museum in the historic Abbott School building, which houses exhibits on the town’s history and hosts events throughout the year.

  • Fort George: Fort George is a historic fort that was built by the British in 1779 during the American Revolution. Today, the fort serves as a museum that offers visitors a glimpse into Castine’s military history.

  • Wilson Museum: The Wilson Museum is a local history museum that is located in a historic house in Castine. The museum houses exhibits on the town’s maritime history, natural history, and Native American history.

Natural Attractions of Castine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wadsworth Cove: Wadsworth Cove is a beautiful natural harbor that is located in Castine. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and swimming in the harbor’s clear waters.

  • Eaton’s Point Beach: Eaton’s Point Beach is a popular beach that is located in the heart of Castine. The beach offers visitors a chance to swim, sunbathe, and enjoy the beautiful views of the Penobscot Bay.

  • Castine Harbor: Castine Harbor is a picturesque natural harbor that is known for its beautiful views and calm waters. Visitors can enjoy sailing, fishing, and kayaking in the harbor or just relax on the shore and take in the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Castine

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Castine has plenty of options for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Hollingsworth Trail: The Hollingsworth Trail is a scenic hiking trail that is located in the heart of Castine. The trail offers stunning views of the Penobscot Bay and is a great way to enjoy the town’s natural beauty.

  • Castine Bike Loop: The Castine Bike Loop is a popular route for cyclists. The loop takes visitors on a scenic tour of the town and offers beautiful views of the harbor and surrounding countryside.

  • Wardwell Preserve: The Wardwell Preserve is a 500-acre nature preserve that is located just outside of Castine. The preserve offers visitors a chance to explore the area’s natural beauty and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Castine: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s natural and cultural attractions, Castine also offers a variety of activities and tours. Here are some of the top things to do in Castine:

  • Castine Kayak Adventures: Castine Kayak Adventures offers visitors a chance to explore the town’s waterways and natural beauty by kayak. The company offers guided tours and rentals for visitors of all skill levels.

  • Castine Cruises: Castine Cruises offers visitors a chance to explore the town’s history and natural beauty by boat. The company offers a variety of tours, including a scenic harbor tour and a historic fort tour.

  • Castine Arts Association: The Castine Arts Association is a non-profit organization that is dedicated to promoting the arts in the town. The association hosts events and exhibits throughout the year, including the annual Castine Plein Air Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Castine

Castine has a vibrant food and drink scene that is centered around local seafood and traditional New England cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Castine is known for its fresh lobster, and a lobster roll is a must-try dish for visitors. The lobster roll is traditionally served on a buttered bun with mayo and celery.

  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is known for its blueberries, and Castine is no exception. A slice of blueberry pie is the perfect way to end a meal in Castine.

  • Steamers: Steamers are a type of clam that is found in the cold waters off the coast of Maine. Steamers are traditionally served in a broth of water and butter and are a popular appetizer in Castine.

Local Beverages

  • Allen’s Coffee Brandy: Allen’s Coffee Brandy is a popular local beverage that is made in Maine. The brandy is often mixed with milk or used as a flavoring in desserts.

  • Maine Beer: Maine is home to a thriving craft beer scene, and there are several local breweries in Castine and the surrounding area. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of beers, from IPAs to stouts, at these local breweries.

Where to Stay in Castine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Castine has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Castine:

  • Pentagoet Inn: The Pentagoet Inn is a historic inn that is located in the heart of Castine. The inn offers comfortable rooms, a full restaurant, and beautiful views of the harbor.

  • Castine Harbor Lodge: The Castine Harbor Lodge is a boutique hotel that is located on the water’s edge. The hotel offers beautifully appointed rooms, a restaurant, and easy access to the town’s attractions.

  • Castine Inn: The Castine Inn is a historic inn that is located within walking distance of the town’s attractions. The inn offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and beautiful views of the harbor.

What to Buy in Castine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Castine is home to several shops and boutiques that offer unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best places to shop in Castine:

  • Castine Variety: Castine Variety is a local convenience store that also sells souvenirs and local products, including T-shirts, hats, and postcards.

  • Main Street Mercantile: Main Street Mercantile is a local boutique that sells Maine-made items and unique gifts. Visitors can find everything from locally made soap to artisan jewelry at this charming store.

  • Mystery Cove Book Shop: Mystery Cove Book Shop is a local bookstore that also sells souvenirs and gifts. The shop specializes in books on Maine and maritime history.

Local Culture and Customs of Castine

Castine has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its traditions and customs. Visitors should be mindful of the following customs:

  • Respect for History: Castine is a town that is steeped in history, and visitors should show respect for the town’s traditions and cultural heritage.

  • Community Spirit: Castine is a small town that values community spirit and neighborliness. Visitors should be friendly and respectful to locals and should be mindful of their impact on the community.

Best Time to Visit Castine

The best time to visit Castine is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the town is alive with activity. However, visitors should be prepared for crowds and higher prices during this time. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, as the leaves change color and the town takes on a peaceful, serene feel.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Castine should be aware of the following safety and health considerations:

Safety in Castine

Castine is a safe town, and visitors should not encounter any major safety concerns. However, visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and should take normal precautions.

Health in Castine

Visitors to Castine should be aware of the risk of tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease. Visitors should wear protective clothing and use insect repellent when hiking or spending time in wooded areas.

Getting to Castine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Castine can reach the town by a number of transportation options. Here are some of the most common options:

Airports Near Castine

The closest airport to Castine is the Bangor International Airport, which is located approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Castine

There are no bus stations in Castine, but visitors can take a bus to Bangor and then rent a car or take a taxi to Castine.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Castine by car or by taking the ferry from nearby Bar Harbor.

How to Get Around in Castine

Castine is a small town that is easily navigable by foot or bike. However, visitors who want to explore the surrounding area may want to rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Castine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Castine is located in a beautiful area of Maine that is home to several other charming towns and natural attractions. Here are some of the nearby destinations that visitors can explore:

Conclusion

Castine is a beautiful town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure.
