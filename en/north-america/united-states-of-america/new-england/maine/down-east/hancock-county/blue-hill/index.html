Blue Hill is a charming town located in Hancock County, Down East Maine. It is an ideal destination for people looking for a quiet vacation spot with plenty of natural beauty to explore. This comprehensive travel guide to Blue Hill will give you a detailed itinerary to help you plan your trip.

Introduction to Blue Hill

Blue Hill is a picturesque town located on the coast of Maine. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, friendly locals, and quaint New England charm. There are plenty of activities to do in the area, making it an excellent destination for families, couples, or solo travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Hill

Blue Hill is a small town, but it has a lot to offer visitors. Here are some of the best places to visit in Blue Hill:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jonathan Fisher House: This is a historic house that belonged to a famous local artist and preacher. It is now a museum showcasing his life and works.
  • Blue Hill Public Library: A quaint library that is well-known for its extensive collection of books and community events.
  • Blue Hill Historical Society: A museum that celebrates the history and culture of Blue Hill.

Natural Attractions of Blue Hill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Hill Falls: A picturesque waterfall that is located just outside of town. It is a great spot for a picnic or a hike.
  • Holbrook Island Sanctuary State Park: A beautiful park that offers hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and beach access.
  • Blue Hill Heritage Trust Preserves: A series of nature preserves with hiking trails that offer picturesque views of the Maine coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Hill

Blue Hill is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • Disc Golf Course: A scenic disc golf course that offers stunning views of the Maine coast.
  • Blue Hill Mountain: A popular hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Sunrise Trail: A scenic biking trail that runs from Ellsworth to Eastport.

Best Things to Do in Blue Hill: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking Tours: Take a guided tour of the coastline with one of the local kayaking companies.
  • Whale Watching: Take a trip out into the ocean to spot whales and other marine life.
  • Blue Hill Wine Shop Tasting: Visit the Blue Hill Wine Shop to sample some of the best local wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blue Hill

Day 1: Arrive in Blue Hill and spend the day exploring the town.

Day 2: Take a guided kayaking tour of the coast.

Day 3: Hike up Blue Hill Mountain for stunning views of the area.

Day 4: Take a whale watching tour in the morning, and spend the afternoon at Holbrook Island Sanctuary State Park.

Day 5: Visit the Jonathan Fisher House and the Blue Hill Historical Society.

Day 6: Explore the Blue Hill Heritage Trust Preserves.

Day 7: Depart from Blue Hill and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Hill

Blue Hill has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some of the best places to eat and drink in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster: Fresh lobster is a staple of Maine cuisine.
  • Blueberry Pie: Blueberries are abundant in Maine, and the pie is a must-try.
  • Clam Chowder: A classic New England dish that is a must-try in Maine.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Maine is known for its craft beer scene, and there are plenty of local breweries to visit in the area.
  • Blueberry Wine: Blueberries are also used to make wine, which is a popular local drink.

Where to Stay in Blue Hill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blue Hill has a range of accommodation options, from traditional hotels to charming guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Blue Hill:

  • Blue Hill Inn: A historic inn that offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  • Pentagoet Inn: A charming inn that is located near the waterfront.
  • Blue Hill Lodge: A guesthouse that offers affordable rooms and a laid-back vibe.

What to Buy in Blue Hill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blueberry Products: Blueberries are a staple of Maine, and there are plenty of blueberry products to bring home, such as jams, pies, and muffins.
  • Handmade Crafts: Blue Hill is home to many talented artisans who make handmade crafts, such as pottery and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Hill

Blue Hill is a small town that values community and culture. It is important to be respectful of local customs, such as being friendly and courteous, and supporting local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Blue Hill

The best time to visit Blue Hill is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. The fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the changing leaves and crisp air.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Hill

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Support local businesses.
  • Be friendly and courteous to locals.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the local customs and culture.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be rude to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blue Hill

Blue Hill is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is still essential to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring natural areas.

Health in Blue Hill

Blue Hill has good healthcare facilities, but it is still important to pack any necessary medications and travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Blue Hill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blue Hill

  • Bangor International Airport: Located approximately 50 miles from Blue Hill.

Bus Stations Near Blue Hill

  • Concord Coach Lines: Offer daily bus service to and from Bangor.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is an excellent option for exploring the area at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Blue Hill

The best way to get around Blue Hill is by car or bike. There are also taxis and ride-sharing services available.

Where to Go Next from Blue Hill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Acadia National Park: A stunning national park located approximately 40 miles from Blue Hill.
  • Bar Harbor: A charming town that is known for its lobster and waterfront views.
  • Castine: A historic town that is known for its beautiful architecture and maritime history.

Conclusion

Blue Hill is a hidden gem in Maine that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, charming New England culture, and outdoor adventure. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next vacation to Blue Hill and explore all that this beautiful town has to offer.
