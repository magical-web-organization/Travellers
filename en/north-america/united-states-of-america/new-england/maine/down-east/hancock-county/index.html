Introduction to Hancock County

Located in Down East, Maine, Hancock County is a hidden gem that boasts stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine. The county is home to several quaint towns and villages, each with its own unique charm and character. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover the best things to do, see, and eat in Hancock County. From exploring historical landmarks to indulging in local cuisine, you’ll have a memorable experience in Hancock County.

List of Places to Visit in Hancock County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Acadia National Park

Acadia National Park is one of the most popular attractions in Hancock County, and it’s not hard to see why. The park spans over 47,000 acres of breathtaking natural beauty, including forests, mountains, lakes, and coastline. You can spend hours hiking, biking, kayaking, or simply relaxing and taking in the views.

Abbe Museum

The Abbe Museum is a must-visit attraction for history lovers. The museum features exhibits that explore the history and culture of Maine’s Native American communities, including the Wabanaki Confederacy. You can learn about the traditional practices, beliefs, and art of these communities through the museum’s interactive exhibits.

Woodlawn Museum

The Woodlawn Museum is a beautifully preserved historic estate that dates back to the early 1800s. The estate comprises a mansion, gardens, and a carriage house. You can take a guided tour of the mansion, which features period furnishings and artwork. The gardens are also worth exploring, with their beautiful landscaping and water features.

Natural Attractions of Hancock County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Desert Island

Mount Desert Island is home to several scenic spots that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. One of the most popular spots is Cadillac Mountain, which is the highest point along the North Atlantic seaboard. You can drive, hike, or bike to the top of the mountain and enjoy panoramic views of the island.

Schoodic Peninsula

The Schoodic Peninsula is a hidden gem that offers a quieter and more secluded experience than Mount Desert Island. The peninsula features stunning coastal vistas, scenic hiking trails, and abundant wildlife. You can take a leisurely drive along the Schoodic Loop Road and stop at the various overlooks for stunning views.

Blue Hill Peninsula

The Blue Hill Peninsula is a charming coastal region that features several picturesque towns and villages. The peninsula offers a mix of outdoor activities, such as hiking and kayaking, as well as cultural attractions such as art galleries and museums. You can explore the towns of Blue Hill, Brooklin, and Stonington for a taste of local life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hancock County

Hancock County is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with endless trails and routes to explore. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the county:

  • Acadia National Park: The park offers over 120 miles of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging mountain hikes.
  • Downeast Sunrise Trail: The trail spans over 87 miles and connects several towns and villages in Hancock County. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.
  • Great Pond Mountain Conservation Trust: The Trust features several hiking trails that offer panoramic views of Great Pond and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Hancock County: Activities and Tours

Whale Watching Tours

Hancock County is a popular destination for whale watching, with several companies offering tours. During the tour, you’ll have the chance to see humpback, finback, and minke whales in their natural habitat. You may also spot dolphins, porpoises, and seals.

Lobster Boat Tours

Maine is known for its fresh lobster, and a lobster boat tour is a great way to experience this local delicacy. During the tour, you’ll learn about lobster fishing and have the chance to see the traps being hauled up from the water. You can even sample fresh lobster on the boat.

Bar Harbor Ghost Tour

If you’re looking for a spooky and entertaining experience, the Bar Harbor Ghost Tour is a must-try. The tour takes you on a guided walk through the town, where you’ll hear stories about local ghosts and hauntings. It’s a fun way to learn about the town’s history and folklore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hancock County

Day 1-2: Explore Acadia National Park, go hiking or biking, and visit the Jordan Pond House for a famous popover.

Day 3: Take a whale watching tour and spot some marine life.

Day 4: Visit the Abbe Museum and learn about Maine’s Native American communities.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive along the Schoodic Peninsula and stop at the various overlooks for stunning views.

Day 6: Explore the charming towns of Blue Hill and Stonington.

Day 7: Relax on one of the county’s stunning beaches, such as Sand Beach or Echo Lake Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Hancock County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Served on a freshly baked bun, this Maine classic is a must-try.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is known for its wild blueberries, and blueberry pie is a favorite dessert.
  • Whoopie Pie: A classic Maine treat, whoopie pies are made with two soft chocolate cakes filled with a sweet cream filling.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer: The state has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries in Hancock County, such as Atlantic Brewing Company and Bar Harbor Brewing Company.
  • Blueberry Wine: Maine’s wild blueberries also make for a delicious wine. Try the blueberry wine at Bartlett Maine Estate Winery.
  • Moxie: Maine’s official state drink is an acquired taste, but worth a try. Moxie is a distinctive soda with a bitter, herbal flavor.

Where to Stay in Hancock County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bar Harbor Grand Hotel

The Bar Harbor Grand Hotel is a luxurious option for travelers seeking comfort and elegance. The hotel features spacious rooms with modern amenities and stunning views of the harbor. It also has a rooftop bar and a restaurant on-site.

Acadia Yurts and Wellness Center

For a unique and eco-friendly experience, check out the Acadia Yurts and Wellness Center. The center features comfortable yurts, each with a private deck and outdoor shower. The center offers yoga classes and wellness activities to guests.

Castlemaine Inn

The Castlemaine Inn is a charming bed and breakfast that dates back to 1886. The inn features beautifully appointed rooms and a delicious breakfast served each morning. It’s located in the heart of Bar Harbor and within walking distance to shops, restaurants, and the harbor.

What to Buy in Hancock County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wild Blueberries: Buy fresh or preserved wild blueberries at a local farmer’s market or grocery store.
  • Maine-made Crafts: Hancock County is home to several artisans who create beautiful pottery, woodwork, and jewelry.
  • Lobster-themed Souvenirs: From t-shirts to kitchen towels, you’ll find a variety of lobster-themed souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hancock County

Maine is known for its friendly and laid-back culture, where manners and politeness are highly valued. It’s common to see locals greeting each other and holding doors open for others. The seafood industry is also an integral part of the culture, and many locals work in lobster fishing or clamming.

Best Time to Visit Hancock County

The best time to visit Hancock County is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is warm, and the days are long. However, if you prefer fewer crowds and cooler temperatures, consider visiting in the fall months of September and October. The fall foliage is stunning, and there are several fall festivals and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hancock County

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the area and leave no traces behind.
  • Try the local seafood, especially the lobster.
  • Explore the county’s charming towns and villages for a taste of local life.
  • Drive carefully and be aware of wildlife crossing the roads.

Don’t:

  • Take rocks or shells from the beaches.
  • Leave trash or litter behind.
  • Disturb wildlife or feed them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hancock County

Hancock County is a safe and friendly place to visit, with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to take precautions, such as locking your car and securing your valuables.

Health in Hancock County

Hancock County has several medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care centers. It’s recommended to have travel insurance when visiting the area.

Getting to Hancock County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hancock County

The nearest airport to Hancock County is Bangor International Airport, located about 45 minutes away by car. The airport offers several daily flights from major cities, including New York City and Boston.

Bus Stations Near Hancock County

Concord Coach Lines offers daily bus service to Bar Harbor and other towns in Hancock County. The bus station in Bangor is the closest station to the county.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Hancock County, especially if you plan to explore the area’s rural roads and scenic routes.

How to Get Around in Hancock County

Hancock County is a rural area, and public transportation options are limited. Renting a car is highly recommended for exploring the area. Alternatively, there are several taxi and shuttle services available.

Where to Go Next from Hancock County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Portland

Portland is the largest city in Maine, located about 3 hours south of Hancock County. The city is known for its vibrant arts scene and delicious food.

Acadia National Park

Acadia National Park spans over 47,000 acres, and there’s always more to explore. Consider spending additional time in the park to discover hidden gems and lesser-known trails.

Camden

Camden is a picturesque coastal town located about 2 hours north of Hancock County. The town features beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and a charming downtown area.

Conclusion

Hancock County offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in this Maine gem. Plan your visit using this comprehensive travel guide, and you’ll have a memorable experience in Hancock County.
