Introduction to Trenton

Trenton is a picturesque town located in Hancock County, Down East, Maine, in the northeastern reaches of the United States. Situated on the Mount Desert Island Bridge, the town of Trenton offers a stunning view of Acadia National Park. Trenton’s rustic charm and proximity to natural wonders make it an ideal vacation destination.

List of Places to Visit in Trenton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Acadia Air Tours: This is the perfect way of seeing Maine’s spectacular scenery from above. You can fly over the rocky coastline and get an unforgettable birds-eye view of Trenton and its scenic surroundings.
  • Acadia National Park: The park is ranked among the top ten most visited national parks in America, and features 49,000 acres of natural beauty offering amazing scenic views and recreational activities like hiking, camping, fishing, and boating.
  • Abbe Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Wabanaki, the indigenous people of Maine dating back to 10,000 years ago, through art, artifacts, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Trenton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bar Harbor Whale Watch: Trenton is the gateway to the Bar Harbor Whale Watch, which offers amazing opportunities to encounter humpback whales, fin whales, dolphins, and porpoises in their natural habitat.
  • Maine Coast Heritage Trust: This trust offers a comprehensive guide to hiking trails in the region. There are several trails through forests, rocky outcroppings, and coastal stretches that offer breathtaking views of Maine’s coastline and wildlife.
  • Trenton Bridge Lobster Pound: Fleming’s Cozy Cottage is an authentic seafood joint located near the lobster pound which provides an excellent opportunity for lobster lovers to enjoy some tasty lobster rolls while taking in the spectacular views of the mouth of Bass Harbor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trenton

  • Beech Hill Cliffs: This hiking route offers stunning views of the coast and the surrounding hills. Along the route, you’ll find wildflowers and wildlife as well as uncommon mosses and lichens.
  • Hancock Point: This biking route is perfect for cycling or walking with views of Blue Hill Bay, Frenchman Bay and the unspoiled beauty of Acadia’s mountainous interior with stops along the way to appreciate land and marine life.
  • Thuya Garden: This leisurely stroll through the lush gardens of the Thuya Estate is highly recommended in the fall, when the leaves turn a fiery red and orange.

Best Things to Do in Trenton: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take a guided kayaking tour in Acadia National Park or explore the sea caves of Mount Desert Island. Kayaks and canoes can also be rented on a hourly or daily basis from the Trenton area.
  • Acadia 2-Day Camping Adventure: For those who wish to spend their nights under the stars in Acadia National Park, a camping adventure is the way to go. A 2-day camping adventure offers an opportunity to hike the trails and watch the night sky among the mountains.
  • Acadia by Air: A helicopter tour offers an unforgettable experience as it provides a unique perspective of Acadia National Park and the surrounding area by air.

Food and Drink Scene of Trenton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: Trenton’s seafront offers a variety of lobster rolls that are delectable and affordable.
  • Clam Chowder: This traditional dish of the Maine coast is a creamy and hearty soup made with clams and potatoes.
  • Blueberries: Trenton is known for its production of blueberries, which are harvested from late July to mid-August.

Local Beverages

  • Bar Harbor Brewing Company: This is a popular local brewery that produces a variety of craft beers.
  • Atlantic Brewing Company: This is another local brewery that produces a range of seasonal beers.

Where to Stay in Trenton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bar Harbor Grand Hotel: This hotel is located just minutes from the entrance to Acadia National Park and is one of the best options for budget travelers.
  • Bar Harbor Inn & Spa: This is a more luxurious option that offers a stunning view of the bay and has an on-site spa.
  • Acadia Gateway Motel: This is a cozy motel with vintage charm and is located in the heart of Trenton.

What to Buy in Trenton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blueberry Jams and Syrups: This is one of the most popular local products in Trenton and can be found in most grocery stores.
  • Handmade Crafts: There are several gift shops featuring handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Trenton

The people of Trenton and Maine, in general, are known for their friendliness and hospitality towards visitors. The locals are proud of their history and culture and are happy to share their knowledge and stories with visitors. It is customary to tip waitstaff and bartenders, and it is considered polite to hold open doors and say “thank you” and “please.”

Best Time to Visit Trenton

The best time to visit Trenton is from late June to late September when the weather is warm, and the sun shines. This is the peak tourist season and the park and surrounding towns are buzzing with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trenton

Do:

  • Explore the Acadia National Park
  • Try the lobster rolls and other seafood dishes
  • Enjoy the scenic driving tours along the coast
  • Interact with the locals and learn about their culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Ignore safety rules and guidelines, such as not venturing off marked trails

Safety and Health

Safety in Trenton

Trenton and the surrounding town of Bar Harbor have a low crime rate. However, visitors should take care to safeguard their belongings when visiting busy tourist areas. It is also important to be aware of natural hazards, such as cliffs and sudden tides in coastal areas.

Health in Trenton

There are several hospitals and clinics in Bar Harbor in case of medical emergencies. Visitors are advised to take precautions against tick-borne diseases like Lyme disease by wearing long sleeves and pants when hiking in wooded areas and using insect repellent.

Getting to Trenton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trenton

  • Bangor International Airport: Approximately a 45-minute drive from Trenton. Served by Delta, American, and United airlines.
  • Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport: Just ten minutes from downtown Trenton. Offers seasonal flights to Boston and other nearby destinations.

Bus Stations Near Trenton

  • Concord Coach Lines: This company operates daily bus services between Trenton and Boston, with several stops along the way.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Several national car rental companies offer services from the airports and downtown areas

How to Get Around in Trenton

Trenton is a small town, and most of the popular destinations are within a short driving distance. Visitors can also hire taxis, ride-sharing companies, or bikes to get around.

Where to Go Next from Trenton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bangor: This nearby city is easily accessible from Trenton and offers plenty of opportunities to shop and dine. Visitors can also take a tour of its historic district.
  • Bar Harbor: This coastal town is just minutes from Trenton and features several waterfront restaurants and hiking trails around its harbor.
  • Mount Desert Island: This island offers even more opportunities to explore Acadia National Park, as well as whale-watching tours and scenic drives.

Conclusion

A visit to Trenton offers a unique opportunity to explore the great outdoors and discover the rich culture and traditions of Maine. From hiking and biking to savoring lobster rolls and enjoying the beautiful scenery, Trenton offers a memorable experience for all types of travelers.
