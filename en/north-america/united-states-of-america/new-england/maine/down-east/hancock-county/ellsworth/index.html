Introduction to Ellsworth

Ellsworth, located in Hancock County, is a charming city situated in Down East region of Maine, bordering Acadia National Park. This maritime community has a rich history and a variety of natural attractions that make it a true gem in Maine’s heart.

List of Places to Visit in Ellsworth

There are many things to do and places to visit in and around Ellsworth, so here’s a list to get you started:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ellsworth’s history is intertwined with shipbuilding, marine resources and colonial settlements. Its numerous historic attractions let you explore the area’s storied past.

  • Woodlawn Museum: A historic estate of the Black family which was built in 1801. Woodlawn is now a local museum and is a popular wedding spot in Maine.
  • Ellsworth Public Library: Dating back to 1903, the Ellsworth Public Library has some exceptional local history, genealogy, and maritime collections.
  • Stanwood Homestead Museum and Sanctuary: The traditional woodlands and fields of this homestead provide a peaceful atmosphere for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Ellsworth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Ellsworth is unparalleled, and it is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lover.

  • Acadia National Park: Located just about 10 miles from Ellsworth is the world-renowned Acadia National Park. The park has a host of recreational and educational activities that include hiking, biking, kayaking, and exploring the wildlife.
  • Tidal Falls: The reversing tidal currents between the narrow strait are the main attraction of Tidal Falls. Visitors frequently visit to see their amazing strength and witness the great depth of water rushing through the gates.
  • Birding Trails: Ellsworth has many prime birding trails that are home to an impressive array of species. There are over 60,000 acres of protected lands along with many water sources that provide sustenance for birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellsworth

Ellsworth is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with many trails to explore and adventures that will take you deep into Maine’s local wilderness. Here are a few examples:

  • Eagle Lake Trail: A four-mile wilderness hike and biking trail that passes through lush granite formations, brooks and serene natural settings of Acadia National Park.
  • Great Pond Mountain 23 miles of stunning trails are waiting for you at Great Pond Mountain. The hiking trails highlight unique rock formations and fantastic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Blackwoods Campgrounds: One of the most popular destinations in Acadia National Park, Blackwoods Campground has an extensive trail system with sights that feature natural features such as Acadia’s quiet lakes, rocky shorelines, and high mountain peaks.

Best Things to Do in Ellsworth: Activities and Tours

Ellsworth is a tourist-friendly destination, and there are a variety of things to see and do. Here are some of the most popular activities within the area:

  • Bar Harbor Whale Watching: The view of the open ocean from a whale watching boat is something that will never leave you. Bar Harbor is also home to some of the most beautiful viewpoints in Maine.
  • Penobscot Bay History Center Tour: Visitors can learn about the maritime history of Penobscot Bay through exhibits and hands-on activities.
  • Bar Harbor Lobster Tour: The authentic lobstering experience is one thing visitors should not miss, which includes a ride on a real lobster boat with a seasoned captain.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellsworth

The Ellsworth food scene is filled with excellent local restaurants, cafes, and bakeries. The food encompasses a range of styles and dishes, which promises to satisfy all appetites. Here are a few options considered a must-try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maine Lobster: A succulent and sweet delicacy that every visitor to Maine should try to satisfy their seafood cravings.
  • Blueberry Pie: Made with freshly picked, Maine blueberries in flaky crust, topped with a crispy-sugared mass.
  • Whoopie Pies: A classic Maine dessert consisting of total rich whipped filling placed between two soft cookie cakes.

Local Beverages

  • Schoodic Brewing Company: Offers a broad selection of crafted beer that can be enjoyed in the taproom or outside in the beer garden.
  • Cobb’s Microbrewery: A family-owned brewery that uses 100% natural organic ingredients sourced locally to produce hand-crafted high-quality ales and beers.
  • Cat’s Meow Coffee Bar: Specialty drinks, espressos, lattes, and pastries from Cat’s Meow Coffee Bar are a great way to start your day.

Where to Stay in Ellsworth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ellsworth has an excellent range of accommodation options for budget-friendly travelers or those looking for luxurious stays. Be sure to book early during the high season to ensure availability.

  • Comfort Inn Ellsworth - Bar Harbor: A seasonally open hotel with great value, comfort, and convenience.
  • Hampton Inn Ellsworth: An upscale guest room with modern amenities nestled in a beautiful green landscape.
  • Mount Desert Islander Inn: Affordable motel with friendly hosts, and a great location for exploring Maine’s outdoor attractions.

What to Buy in Ellsworth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ellsworth is a haven for unique and fun shopping; you’ll find everything from local handmade crafts, antiques, and souvenirs to rare finds and memorabilia of the city and its surroundings. For unique mementos of your trip the following locations are must-visits:

  • Sylvester’s Cove Soaps: A locally-owned soap and skincare company has a range of bath bombs, scrubs, and salves that make perfect gifts or souvenirs.
  • Cherrystone Design: A unique boutique shop selling exquisite handmade jewelry, ceramics, glassware, and other art items by regional artists.
  • Ellsworth Antiques Show: A must-attend annual event for antique admirers where they can find rare and unusual home furnishings like furniture, clocks, and silverware.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellsworth

  • Be respectful of Maine’s local culture and its natural resources.
  • Always use the outdoors safely and understand the rules and regulations in the state parks and other recreational sites.
  • Maine has an older and more culturally significant population. As a visitor, be courteous, aware, and respect the local community.

Best Time to Visit Ellsworth

The best times to visit Ellsworth are from April to November. The summer months, from May to September, are popular and busy, while fall, from October to November, is a quieter yet enjoyable season, with cooler temperatures and beautiful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellsworth

Do:

  • Try a lobster meal when dining out.
  • Respect Maine’s natural resources and stay polite with other visitors and locals.
  • Visit Acadia National Park for an unforgettable hiking and sightseeing experience.

Don’t:

  • Litter or dump trash while enjoying Ellsworth’s natural beauty.
  • Disrespect cultural heritage and traditions.
  • Pick protected wildflowers or rocks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ellsworth

  • Ellsworth is generally safe for visitors, but as with many destinations, it is always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.
  • Always wear a life jacket when participating in any water or boating activities.
  • Be sure to pack appropriate clothing and gear when hiking and exploring the outdoors in Maine.

Health in Ellsworth

  • Medical facilities are readily available in Ellsworth and surrounding areas.
  • As with most travel, it is always best practice to carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medications that you may need.

Getting to Ellsworth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ellsworth

  • Bangor International Airport, 25 miles from Ellsworth with direct flights from most major hubs in the US.

Bus Stations Near Ellsworth

  • Concord Coach Lines and Greyhound Lines have services that stop at the bus transfer station in Ellsworth.

Other Options

  • Driving is the most convenient and common way of reaching Ellsworth.
  • Consider joining a railway tour after taking the scenic coach towards the region.

How to Get Around in Ellsworth

The best way to explore Ellsworth is by car, specifically, a rental car. This will provide you the freedom to explore the city and its surroundings at your own pace and convenience.

Taxi services are readily available, but they can be expensive. There are also port and shuttle services from the airport that provide scheduled rides.

Where to Go Next from Ellsworth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby towns and villages in the vicinity of Ellsworth are perfect for a memorable and charming day trip, so we’ve listed a few of our favorites:

  • Bar Harbor: Known for its cultured history, seaside locations, and World-class Bear watching, Bar Harbor is just 10 miles from Ellsworth.
  • Belfast: Right in the heart of Maine’s Mid Coast, Belfast is a thriving artisan town that is famous for dining and outdoor activities.
  • Castine: The historical settlement of Castine and its architectural heritage, its scenic views, and outdoors make it an excellent location to explore.

Conclusion

Ellsworth is a beautiful and peaceful destination where you can escape the hustle and bustle of the city while enjoying the scenic nature and cultural heritage of Maine. We hope this comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip and make it a memorable one. Safe travels!
