Introduction to Winter Harbor

Winter Harbor is a hidden gem located in Hancock County, in Down East Maine. This small coastal town is known for its natural beauty, rich history, and charming local culture. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and art galleries, there is no shortage of things to do in Winter Harbor. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, the top things to do, and where to stay and eat in Winter Harbor.

List of Places to Visit in Winter Harbor

Winter Harbor is filled with unique attractions and must-visit destinations. Here are some of the top places to see in Winter Harbor:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Winter Harbor Historical Society Museum: This museum is the perfect place to learn about the town’s local history and culture. The exhibits showcase the area’s fishing, maritime, and military heritage.
  • Schoodic Institute: This research center and nature center offers a range of educational programs, lectures, and guided tours focusing on environmental science and sustainability.
  • Grindstone Neck of Maine: This exclusive neighborhood located on the eastern side of the Schoodic Peninsula is a testament to the Gilded Age, with several beautiful mansions and gardens to explore.

Natural Attractions of Winter Harbor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Acadia National Park: This national park is a must-visit destination in Winter Harbor. It offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and kayaking. Be sure to check out Cadillac Mountain - the highest point on the east coast of the United States.
  • Frenchman Bay Conservancy: This non-profit organization has been working to conserve the natural beauty of the Downeast region since 1987. The Osgood Trail and the Indian Point Blagden Preserve are two popular hiking spots in Winter Harbor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Winter Harbor

One of the best ways to experience the natural beauty of Winter Harbor is by hiking or biking. Here are some popular routes:

  • Schoodic Peninsula Loop Road: This scenic 8.3-mile loop takes you through the heart of Schoodic Peninsula, offering stunning coastal views and wildlife spotting opportunities.
  • Beech Mountain Trail: This moderately difficult 1.6-mile hike takes you to the summit of Beech Mountain, where you can enjoy panoramic views of Frenchman Bay and Mount Desert Island.

Best Things to Do in Winter Harbor: Activities and Tours

Winter Harbor offers a range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Lobster Boat Tour: Experience the local fishing culture on a lobster boat tour, where you can learn about lobster fishing and sample some fresh and delicious seafood.
  • Art Galleries and Studios: Winter Harbor is home to several art galleries and working studios, showcasing the work of local artists and artisans. Be sure to visit the Tidal Falls Preserve to find a collection of beautiful driftwood sculptures.
  • Kayaking Tours: Explore the stunning coastline of Winter Harbor by kayaking in the crystal-clear waters of Frenchman Bay. Several tour operators offer guided tours for all experience levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Winter Harbor

Winter Harbor has a thriving food and drink scene, with several excellent restaurants and bars serving up fresh and delicious seafood and local craft beer. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: You can’t visit Maine without trying one of its most famous dishes. Winter Harbor has several restaurants where you can enjoy a classic lobster roll, including Chase’s Daily and Fisherman’s Galley.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is known for its wild blueberries, and several restaurants in Winter Harbor offer blueberry pie as a dessert option.
  • Clam Chowder: A warm bowl of clam chowder is the perfect comfort food on a chilly day. Try some excellent chowder at Fisherman’s Galley or The Pickled Wrinkle.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Winter Harbor has several microbreweries and craft beer bars where you can try a range of local brews, including Atlantic Brewing Company and Airline Brewing Company.
  • Maine Blueberry Wine: Maine’s wild blueberries are also used to make some excellent wine. Bartlett Maine Estate Winery and Maine Mead Works are two local wineries you can check out.

Where to Stay in Winter Harbor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Winter Harbor has a range of accommodation options to choose from, whether you prefer a luxury hotel or a cozy bed and breakfast. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Colony: This luxury hotel is located on Grindstone Neck and offers stunning views of Frenchman Bay. It has several amenities, including a restaurant, bar, and spa.
  • The Captain Nickels Inn: This historic bed and breakfast was built in 1874 and is located in the heart of Winter Harbor. It has several comfortable rooms and a beautiful front porch.
  • The Inn at Schoppee Farm: This eco-friendly bed and breakfast is located on a farm and has several unique rooms and cottages. It offers a farm-to-table breakfast for guests to enjoy.

What to Buy in Winter Harbor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Winter Harbor has several unique shopping destinations where you can buy souvenirs and local products to take home. Here are some of the top options:

  • Winter Harbor 5 and 10: This old-fashioned variety store has been in operation for over 75 years and sells a range of gifts, toys, and souvenirs.
  • The Maine Grind: This coffee shop and gift shop offers several local products, including handcrafted jewelry, pottery, and artwork.
  • Winter Harbor Lobster Co-op: If you want to bring home some local seafood, the Winter Harbor Lobster Co-op is the perfect place to buy fresh lobster and other seafood products.

Local Culture and Customs of Winter Harbor

Winter Harbor has a rich local culture and history, shaped by its location and its residents. The town has a strong fishing heritage, and many locals depend on the sea for their livelihoods. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs, including the importance of sustainable fishing practices and environmental conservation.

Best Time to Visit Winter Harbor

Winter Harbor is at its best from June to September when the weather is warm and festivals are in full swing. However, visitors can enjoy Winter Harbor’s natural beauty and charm year-round. The winter months are quieter but offer a chance to see the stunning landscape covered in snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Winter Harbor

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Winter Harbor:

Do:

  • Support local businesses and artisans by shopping locally.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities. Layers are recommended as temperatures can vary throughout the day.
  • Follow sustainable and responsible tourism practices, including leaving no trace and respecting wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment by picking flowers or disturbing wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs and cultural practices.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Winter Harbor

Winter Harbor is generally a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as locking doors and windows at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Winter Harbor

Visitors should be aware of potential health issues when visiting Winter Harbor, particularly those related to outdoor activities, such as tick-borne illnesses and sunburn. It is important to take precautions, such as wearing protective clothing and using insect repellent.

Getting to Winter Harbor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Winter Harbor is located in Down East Maine and is accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some of the top transportation options:

Airports Near Winter Harbor

  • Bangor International Airport: This airport is located about an hour and a half from Winter Harbor and has several direct flights from major U.S. cities.
  • Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport: This airport is located about 40 minutes from Winter Harbor and offers scheduled commercial flights as well as private charters.

Bus Stations Near Winter Harbor

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus company offers daily service to and from Bangor, with several stops in between, including Ellsworth, Winter Harbor’s closest town.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several major car rental companies have locations at Bangor International Airport and the Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport.
  • Taxi and Shuttle Services: Several local taxi and shuttle services offer transportation to and from Winter Harbor.

How to Get Around in Winter Harbor

Winter Harbor is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors may also choose to rent bikes or cars to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Winter Harbor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Winter Harbor is located within easy driving distance of several other must-visit destinations in Down East Maine. Here are some top options:

  • Bar Harbor: This famous coastal town is located about an hour’s drive from Winter Harbor and is known for its stunning natural beauty and vibrant local culture.
  • Blue Hill: This charming town is located about a half-hour’s drive from Winter Harbor and offers several art galleries, cafes, and shops.
  • Acadia National Park: While Winter Harbor is located within Acadia National Park, there are several other entrance points to the park that visitors can explore, including Mount Desert Island and Acadia’s main visitor center.

Conclusion

Winter Harbor is a hidden gem on the coast of Maine, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural history. From exploring museums and hiking trails to indulging in local seafood and craft beer, there is something for everyone in Winter Harbor. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming coastal town has to offer.
