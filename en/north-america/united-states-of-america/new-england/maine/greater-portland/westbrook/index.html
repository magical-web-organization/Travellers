Introduction to Westbrook

Westbrook, a city situated in Greater Portland, Maine, is a fascinating destination for travelers seeking to explore the rich history and natural beauty of the state. Located in the Northeastern region of the United States, Westbrook boasts an array of cultural and natural attractions that cater to all kinds of visitors, making it an ideal place for a vacation.

In this guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit places and activities in Westbrook, including hiking and biking routes, museums, historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, local cuisines and drinks, hotels, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Westbrook

From museums to natural attractions, Westbrook is home to a variety of locations that cater to different interests. Below are some of the places that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Maine Irish Heritage Center: A museum that showcases the significant contributions of the Irish to Maine’s heritage and culture.
  • Tate House Museum: A historically significant landmark that offers visitors a glimpse into the life of one of the earliest families to settle in Westbrook.
  • Portland Museum of Art: A cultural center that houses an impressive collection of American and European art.

Natural Attractions of Westbrook: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saccarappa Falls: This popular waterfall in the heart of downtown Westbrook is a must-visit destination that offers picturesque views and hiking trails.
  • Riverton Trolley Park: A park located along the Presumpscot River that serves as a perfect destination for picnics and outdoor recreation.
  • Highland Lake: One of the most beautiful and largest lakes in the region, offering opportunities for activities like boating, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westbrook

Westbrook is home to an extensive network of trails that cater to hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Here are some of the most popular destinations for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Riverton Rail Trail: A picturesque, well-maintained biking trail that runs along the Presumpscot River.
  • Whitten Hill Trail: A trail that meanders through lush forests and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Pisgah Hill Preserve: This preserve located near Westbrook offers hiking trails that lead to the summit, providing panoramic views of the entire region.

Best Things to Do in Westbrook: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Westbrook offers a variety of activities and tours that are worth adding to your itinerary. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Visit the Westbrook Farmers’ Market: A great place to visit to sample fresh and delicious local produce and crafts.
  • Take a Duck Boat Tour: Jump on board the Duck boat for a sightseeing tour of Portland’s most famous landmarks and attractions.
  • Enjoy Wine Tasting at Cellardoor Winery: A destination for wine lovers, where they can taste a variety of locally made wines in an amazing setting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westbrook

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring Westbrook, Maine:

Day Activity
1 Visit Saccarappa Falls and the Maine Irish Heritage Center
2 Hike Whitten Hill Trail and take a duck boat tour in Portland
3 Explore the Tate House Museum and take a picnic break at Riverton Trolley Park
4 Visit the Portland Museum of Art and enjoy a wine tasting at Cellardoor Winery
5 Kayak or paddleboard on Highland Lake and visit the Westbrook Farmers’ Market for fresh produce
6 Hike Pisgah Hill Preserve and enjoy a relaxing lunch at Smiling Hill Farm
7 Take a day trip to the nearby coastal town of Kennebunkport and enjoy scenic views and attractions

Food and Drink Scene of Westbrook

Westbrook offers visitors many opportunities to enjoy the local cuisine, which is a blend of traditional Maine and international flavors. Here are some local dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Savor a traditional Maine lobster roll, served at many local restaurants in Westbrook.
  • Clam Chowder: This creamy and rich soup made from fresh clams is a popular dish in Maine.
  • Blueberry Pie: A sweet and fruity dessert made from fresh, wild Maine blueberries.

Local Beverages

  • Allagash Beer: A famous craft beer that is brewed locally and enjoyed throughout the region.
  • Moxie: A soft drink that is made in Maine and is known for its unique and unusual flavor.
  • Maple Syrup: A sweet and syrupy syrup that is made from fresh Maine maple sap and is a popular addition to many dishes.

Where to Stay in Westbrook: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Westbrook offers visitors many lodging options, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Westbrook:

  • Westbrook Inn Bed & Breakfast: A cozy B&B that offers guests comfortable and affordable rooms and a delicious homemade breakfast every morning.
  • Hilton Garden Inn Portland Airport: A luxury hotel located near the airport, with amenities like a fitness center, restaurant, and bar.
  • HI Portland - Maine: A hostel that offers budget-friendly accommodations for travelers seeking to connect with other travelers and explore the city together.

What to Buy in Westbrook: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From local crafts to delicious foods, Westbrook is home to many unique and authentic souvenirs that make perfect gifts or reminders of your trip. Here are some products you should look for:

  • Maine Lobster Candy: These sweet and delicious candies are shaped like Maine lobsters and are a popular souvenir.
  • Handmade Pottery: Many local artisans sell beautiful pottery that is unique to the region and make excellent gifts.
  • Maine Maple Syrup: A quintessential Maine treat that is treasured by locals and visitors alike.

Local Culture and Customs of Westbrook

The people of Westbrook are known for their warmth and friendliness towards visitors. Maine is also a state steeped in history and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Maine’s Lobster Culture: Maine is famous for its lobster, which is a part of the state’s identity and culture. Don’t leave without trying a lobster roll or a full lobster meal.
  • Seasonal Events: Westbrook and the surrounding areas host many seasonal events like apple picking, maple syrup festivals, and fall foliage tours. Make sure to check the calendar to find out about upcoming events.
  • Environmental Awareness: Mainers care deeply about their state’s environment and natural resources. Be sure to follow the “Leave No Trace” principle and respect the natural beauty around you.

Best Time to Visit Westbrook

The best time to visit Westbrook is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the region is alive with outdoor activities. However, Maine’s countryside is stunning in the fall, with foliage and pristine beaches a site to behold. Prices and crowds tend to be lower in the winter, although some outdoor activities may be limited.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westbrook

When visiting Westbrook, it is essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Embrace the “mainah” accent and ways of life.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing and kayaking.
  • Sample some of Maine’s famous seafood and local craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrespect the environment.
  • Be inconsiderate of others, including visitors and locals.
  • Ignore traffic rules or regulations.

Safety and Health

Maine is known for its safety, with locals and visitors feeling safe and secure in the state. Nevertheless, it is still essential to take some precautions to ensure your safety.

Safety in Westbrook

  • Be careful driving on rural roads, where wildlife and some rural traffic may pose hazards.
  • Avoid areas that may make you feel unsafe, and be cautious at night.
  • Be ​prepared for sudden changes in weather conditions.

Health in Westbrook

  • Be aware of the sun and apply sunscreen to avoid sunburns and skin cancer.
  • Stay hydrated during hot weather.
  • If you’re hiking on a trail or in the woods, make sure to wear appropriate clothing to avoid contact with ticks and other insects.

Getting to Westbrook: Transportation Options and Logistics

Westbrook is easily accessible by car and public transportation. Below are some of the ways to get to Westbrook:

Airports Near Westbrook

  • Portland International Jetport: Located only six miles away from Westbrook and provides direct flights to many major cities.

Bus Stations Near Westbrook

  • Concord Coach Lines: A bus service that connects Portland with many other cities in Maine and beyond.
  • Greater Portland Metro: Offers transit service throughout the Portland metropolitan area.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available at the Portland International Jetport.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft are available in Westbrook most hours of the day.

How to Get Around in Westbrook

The best way to get around Westbrook is by car, bike, or foot. Westbrook has an extensive network of trails, bike routes and sidewalks, which makes getting around more accessible, but if you don’t want to drive around, there are some options like:

  • Greater Portland Metro: Provides bus service throughout the Portland metropolitan area, including Westbrook.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft offer transportation services in Westbrook.

Where to Go Next from Westbrook: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Westbrook makes a great base for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Freeport: A quaint coastal town known for its shopping and outlet stores.
  • Portland: Maine’s intellectual and cultural center, buzzing food and drink scene, and many other attractions.
  • Kennebunkport: A charming coastal town that offers a variety of outdoor attractions and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Westbrook’s rich history, natural beauty, and welcoming community make it a must-visit destination for anyone seeking to explore the state of Maine. Use our comprehensive guide to make sure to see and experience all the best attractions, activities, and local culture that Westbrook has to offer.
