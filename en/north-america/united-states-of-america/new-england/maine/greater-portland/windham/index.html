Introduction to Windham

Located in Greater Portland, Windham is a charming town situated in the heart of Maine in New England. The town has a population of approximately 18,000 people, and it is known for its stunning natural scenery, excellent outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage. Windham was first settled in the 1730s, and it played an important role in the American Revolution. Today, Windham is a popular tourist destination, and it attracts visitors from all over the world who are interested in its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Windham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Windham Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Windham and the surrounding area. Visitors can explore a range of exhibits, including artifacts from the town’s early settlers, displays about the industrial revolution, and much more.
  • Greater Portland Landmarks: This organization offers tours of some of the most historic and culturally significant buildings in the Greater Portland area, including several in Windham.

Natural Attractions of Windham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sebago Lake State Park: This stunning park offers visitors the chance to hike, bike, fish, swim, and enjoy a range of other outdoor activities. The park also has several picnic areas and a playground for children.
  • Little Falls Preserve: This beautiful nature reserve features hiking trails and stunning waterfalls. The preserve is a great spot for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windham

Windham is home to several excellent hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Hedgehog Mountain Trail: This 3.8-mile loop trail is suitable for hikers of all skill levels, and it offers stunning views of the White Mountains and the surrounding countryside.
  • Sebago to the Sea Trail: This 28-mile trail connects Sebago Lake to Casco Bay, and it passes through some of the most beautiful parts of Windham.

Best Things to Do in Windham: Activities and Tours

  • Paddleboarding on Sebago Lake: Visitors can rent paddleboards and explore the beautiful waters of Sebago Lake on their own or take a guided tour.
  • Horseback Riding: Several local companies offer guided horseback rides through some of the most scenic areas of Windham.

Week-Long Itinerary for Windham

Day 1: Explore Windham Historical Society Museum and the surrounding area. Day 2: Spend the day at Sebago Lake State Park hiking, swimming, and picnicking. Day 3: Take a guided horseback ride through the beautiful countryside of Windham. Day 4: Visit Little Falls Preserve and enjoy a hike through the beautiful nature reserve. Day 5: Rent a paddleboard and explore the waters of Sebago Lake. Day 6: Take a tour of some of the historic buildings in Windham offered by Greater Portland Landmarks. Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Windham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: Windham is located close to the coast, and there is no shortage of fresh seafood. Visitors should not miss out on trying a traditional Maine lobster roll.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is famous for its wild blueberries, and pie made from these delicious berries is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard Brewery: This famous local brewery offers a range of craft beers, including the popular “Summer Ale.”
  • Cumberland County Winery: This local winery offers a range of wines made from locally sourced fruits and berries, including blueberries and apples.

Where to Stay in Windham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham Windham: This hotel is conveniently located near several of the town’s top attractions and offers comfortable, affordable accommodations.
  • The Lodge at Turbat’s Creek: This charming guesthouse is located in a quiet, scenic area of Windham and offers visitors a cozy, homey feel.

What to Buy in Windham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Crafts and Artwork: Windham is home to several talented artists and craftspeople, and visitors can find a range of unique, locally made products in the town’s shops and galleries.
  • Maple Syrup: Maine is known for its delicious maple syrup, and visitors should not miss the chance to bring home a bottle of this sweet treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Windham

Windham is known for its friendly, welcoming locals who are always eager to share their love of their town with visitors. The area has a long and rich cultural heritage, and visitors can learn a lot about early settlers and life in Maine during the industrial revolution by exploring the town’s museums and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Windham

The best time to visit Windham is between May and October when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, swimming, and a range of other outdoor activities during this time, and the fall foliage is particularly beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Windham

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace.
  • Follow local customs and be respectful of the local culture.
  • Try some of the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety rules or warnings when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Windham

Windham is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take basic safety precautions.

Health in Windham

There are several medical clinics and hospitals in Windham, so visitors should have no trouble finding medical care if they need it.

Getting to Windham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Windham

The closest airport to Windham is Portland International Jetport, which is approximately 20 miles away from the town and offers several transportation options, including rental cars, taxis, and shuttles.

Bus Stations Near Windham

The closest bus station to Windham is the Portland Transportation Center, which is approximately 20 miles away and offers bus connections throughout Maine and the surrounding area.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Windham or take a train to Portland and then rent a car or take a taxi to the town.

How to Get Around in Windham

The best way to get around Windham is by car, although visitors can also walk or bike to some of the town’s top attractions.

Where to Go Next from Windham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland: The vibrant city of Portland is just a short drive away and offers visitors a range of cultural attractions, great restaurants, and exciting nightlife.
  • Acadia National Park: Nature lovers will not want to miss the chance to visit the stunning Acadia National Park, which is located approximately four hours north of Windham.

Conclusion

Windham is a beautiful, welcoming town that is perfect for visitors who want to experience the best of Maine’s natural scenery, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. With its stunning forests, mountains, lakes, and rivers, Windham has something to offer visitors of all ages and interests. So book your trip today, and discover the magic of Windham for yourself!
