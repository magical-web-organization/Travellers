Introduction to Portland

Located in Greater Portland in Maine, USA, Portland is a charming coastal city boasting a vibrant culture, rich history, and breathtaking natural attractions. Known as the “Forest City,” Portland is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, and history buffs alike. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Portland.

List of Places to Visit in Portland

No visit to Portland is complete without a trip to its must-see attractions. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Portland Museum of Art: This art museum features contemporary and historical art exhibits, as well as a sculpture garden.

  • Victoria Mansion: Also known as the Morse-Libby Mansion, this old-fashioned house museum showcases 19th-century architecture and design.

  • Wadsworth-Longfellow House: This historic home was the birthplace of Henry Wadsworth Longfellow, a famous poet. The museum offers guided tours and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Portland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Portland Head Light: One of the most photographed lighthouses in the country, Portland Head Light sits atop a rocky shoreline overlooking the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Casco Bay Islands: A group of islands off the coast of Portland, Casco Bay is home to beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and charming coastal villages.

  • Eastern Promenade: This scenic park offers stunning views of Casco Bay and the Portland skyline, as well as walking and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portland

For outdoor enthusiasts, Portland offers plenty of hiking and biking trails with stunning scenery. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Back Cove Trail: This 3.5-mile loop trail offers scenic views of the Back Cove and downtown Portland.

  • Fore River Sanctuary: This 85-acre nature preserve features hiking trails and wildlife observation opportunities.

  • Cape Elizabeth Shore Trail: This 1.25-mile trail follows the rocky coastline of Cape Elizabeth, offering scenic views of the Atlantic Ocean and Portland Head Light.

Best Things to Do in Portland: Activities and Tours

From boat tours to foodie adventures, Portland offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Maine Foodie Tours: This walking tour takes you through Portland’s foodie scene, offering a taste of local cuisine along the way.

  • Casco Bay Lines Ferry Tour: This ferry tour takes you through Casco Bay, providing scenic views of the islands and coastline.

  • Cycling Culinary Adventure: This tour combines two of Portland’s favorite activities: biking and food.

Food and Drink Scene of Portland

Portland is known for its culinary scene, with plenty of local dishes and beverages to try. Here are our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic Maine dish, the lobster roll is a hot dog bun filled with fresh lobster meat.

  • Clam Chowder: Another classic New England dish, clam chowder is a creamy soup made with clams and potatoes.

  • Blueberry Pie: Made from local blueberries, this sweet pie is a Maine favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Allagash Brewing Company: This local brewery produces Belgian-style beers, including their famous Allagash White.

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: A popular Maine brewery, Shipyard offers a variety of beers, including their signature Pumpkinhead ale.

Where to Stay in Portland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels, Portland offers plenty of options for accommodations. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Portland Harbor Hotel: Located in the Old Port, this luxurious hotel offers stunning views of Portland Harbor.

  • Black Elephant Hostel: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Portland’s arts district.

  • Inn at St. John: A charming guesthouse located in a historic building, the Inn at St. John offers affordable accommodations in a central location.

What to Buy in Portland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of Portland, here are some of our top picks for local products and souvenirs:

  • Maine Blueberry Jam: Made from local blueberries, this sweet jam is a great souvenir.

  • Bean Boots: A classic Maine product, Bean Boots are waterproof, durable boots perfect for outdoor adventures.

  • Portland Head Light Postcard: A scenic postcard featuring one of Portland’s most iconic attractions.

Local Culture and Customs of Portland

Portland is a friendly and welcoming city with a rich culture and history. Here are a few customs and practices to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Tipping: It is customary to leave a 15-20% tip at restaurants and cafes.

  • Appropriate Dress: While Portland is generally a casual city, dress appropriately for the occasion. Beach attire is not appropriate for most restaurants and museums.

Best Time to Visit Portland

The best time to visit Portland is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and festivals happening. Fall is another great time to visit, with stunning foliage and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portland

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Portland:

Do:

  • Do try local seafood: Portland is known for its fresh seafood, so make sure to try some local dishes.

  • Do visit the lighthouses: Portland is home to several scenic lighthouses that are worth a visit.

  • Do explore the outdoors: From hiking to kayaking, Portland offers plenty of outdoor activities for nature lovers.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Keep Portland beautiful by disposing of trash properly.

  • Don’t be rude: Portlanders are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so be respectful of others.

  • Don’t forget your sunscreen: Even on cloudy days, the sun can be intense, so make sure to bring sunscreen.

Safety and Health

Portland is a safe and healthy city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Here are a few tips:

Safety in Portland

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: While Portland is generally safe, it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night.

  • Secure your belongings: Keep your personal belongings secure, especially when in crowded areas.

Health in Portland

  • Drink plenty of water: Stay hydrated, especially during the summer months when temperatures can get high.

  • Pack appropriate clothing: Bring layers to stay comfortable in changing weather conditions.

Getting to Portland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Portland is easily accessible by plane, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Portland

  • Portland International Jetport: Located just a few miles from downtown Portland, the Portland International Jetport offers flights from several major airlines.

  • Boston Logan International Airport: Located about two hours from Portland, Boston Logan International Airport is a larger airport with more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Portland

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus company offers daily service to several cities in Maine and New Hampshire.

  • Greyhound Lines: Greyhound offers daily service to Portland from several major cities along the East Coast.

Other Options

  • Train: Amtrak offers daily service to the Portland Transportation Center.

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies are available at the Portland International Jetport and throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Portland

Portland is a walkable and bike-friendly city, but there are also several public transportation options available, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Portland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Portland is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in Maine and New England. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Freeport: Known for its outlet shopping, Freeport is located just 20 minutes from Portland.

  • Acadia National Park: Located about three hours from Portland, Acadia National Park is a must-see destination for nature lovers.

  • Boston: Located just two hours from Portland, Boston is a popular destination for a day trip or weekend getaway.

Conclusion

With its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culinary scene, Portland is a destination that shouldn’t be missed. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit, and get ready for a memorable experience in one of America’s most charming cities.
