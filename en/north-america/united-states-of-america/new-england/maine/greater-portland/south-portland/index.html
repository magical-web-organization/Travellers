Introduction to South Portland

Located in Greater Portland, Maine, South Portland is a charming city that offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. The city is home to several museums that showcase Maine’s rich history, as well as beautiful parks and trails that are perfect for hiking, biking and exploring. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll highlight the best things to see and do in South Portland, where to eat and drink, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in South Portland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South Portland is rich in history and heritage and offers tourists a range of museums and historical sites to explore.

Maine Narrow Gauge Railroad Museum

The Maine Narrow Gauge Railroad Museum showcases the trains that transported people and goods throughout Maine’s history. Visitors can ride on the narrow gauge train, view exhibits on the history of railroads in Maine, and explore the museum’s extensive collection of antique train cars.

Spring Point Ledge Light

The Spring Point Ledge Light is a lighthouse that was built in 1897. The lighthouse is open for tours and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Portland Harbor Museum

The Portland Harbor Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the maritime history of Maine. The museum showcases the history of Portland’s working waterfront, fishing industry, and port-related industries.

Natural Attractions of South Portland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South Portland is home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in Maine. Whether it’s hiking, biking, or simply enjoying the scenic views, there’s something for everyone in South Portland.

Bug Light Park

Bug Light Park is a waterfront park that offers stunning views of Casco Bay and the Portland skyline. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the tree-lined paths, have a picnic, or go fishing.

Greenbelt Walkway

The Greenbelt Walkway is a 3.5-mile trail that connects several parks and green spaces along the Fore River in South Portland. It’s a great place for a walk or a bike ride.

South Portland Greenbelt

The South Portland Greenbelt is a 7.5-mile trail that runs along the coastline and offers stunning views of the harbor and surrounding islands. It’s perfect for hiking, biking, or birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Portland

South Portland is a paradise for hikers and bikers alike. Here are the best hiking and biking routes to explore in the area.

Eastern Trail

The Eastern Trail is a 65-mile trail that runs from Kittery to South Portland. The section that runs through South Portland offers stunning views of the marshland and coastline.

Fort Williams Park Loop

The Fort Williams Park Loop is a 3-mile loop that offers breathtaking ocean views and a chance to explore the ruins of the old fort.

South Portland Greenbelt Trail

The South Portland Greenbelt Trail is a 7.5-mile trail that runs along the coastline, through woods and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in South Portland: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in South Portland, there’s plenty to keep you busy. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try while you’re in town.

Casco Bay Islands

Take a ferry ride to explore the Casco Bay Islands. The islands offer a variety of activities, including beachcombing, hiking, and exploring historic sites.

Maine Foodie Tours

Maine Foodie Tours are a great way to explore the city’s culinary scene. The tours take visitors to local food markets, restaurants, and bakeries to sample some of the best dishes in the area.

Casco Bay Lines

Casco Bay Lines offer scenic boat tours that offer views of the harbor, lighthouses, and rugged coastline.

Food and Drink Scene of South Portland

Maine is known for its seafood, and South Portland is no exception. Here are some of the best places to eat and drink in the area.

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Rolls

Lobster rolls are a Maine classic. Be sure to try one while you’re in South Portland.

Fried Seafood

South Portland has many excellent places to enjoy fried seafood, from fish and chips to scallops and clams.

Blueberry Pie

Maine is famous for its blueberries, and blueberry pie is a must-try dessert in South Portland.

Local Beverages

Maine Beer Company

Maine Beer Company is a local brewery that produces some of the best beer in the state. Be sure to stop by for a tasting.

Shipyard Brewing Company

Shipyard Brewing Company is another local brewery that offers a range of delicious beers and ales.

Where to Stay in South Portland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Portland offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets.

Best Budget Option: Black Elephant Hostel

The Black Elephant Hostel is a great budget option for solo travelers or those on a tight budget. The hostel offers shared and private rooms and is located in the heart of South Portland.

Best Mid-Range Option: Courtyard by Marriott Portland Airport

The Courtyard by Marriott Portland Airport is a great mid-range option for families or business travelers. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.

Best Luxury Option: The Press Hotel

The Press Hotel is a luxury hotel that offers a unique experience for visitors. The hotel is located in a restored 1923 newspaper building and offers stylish rooms and excellent dining options.

What to Buy in South Portland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, there are plenty of options in South Portland.

Maine-Made Products

Maine is known for its artisanal products, including pottery, clothing, and food items. Be sure to explore the local markets and shops to find unique gifts and souvenirs.

Lobster and Seafood

South Portland is home to several seafood markets where you can buy fresh lobster and other seafood to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of South Portland

South Portland is known for its friendly people and laid-back lifestyle. When visiting, be sure to respect the local culture and customs, including:

  • Tipping waitstaff and bartenders is customary
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities you’ll be doing
  • Be mindful of noise levels in public places, especially at night
  • Always ask permission before taking photos of locals

Best Time to Visit South Portland

The best time to visit South Portland is between June and August when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with stunning foliage and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Portland

Do:

  • Do try the seafood
  • Do explore the local hiking and biking trails
  • Do visit the museums and historical sites
  • Do respect the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind while hiking or visiting parks
  • Don’t take souvenirs from the beaches or parks
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to locals

Safety and Health

Safety in South Portland

South Portland is generally a safe city to visit. However, like any urban area, it’s important to take precautions to stay safe, especially at night. Walk in well-lit areas, be aware of your surroundings, and don’t leave valuables in your car or unattended in public places.

Health in South Portland

South Portland has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care centers. Be sure to bring any necessary medication with you, and be prepared for the weather conditions.

Getting to South Portland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Portland

The closest airport to South Portland is the Portland International Jetport, which is located just 10 minutes from the city center.

Bus Stations Near South Portland

South Portland is served by several bus stations, including the South Portland Bus Station and the Portland Transportation Center.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great way to explore the area, and there are several car rental companies located in South Portland. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

How to Get Around in South Portland

South Portland is a small city that is easily walkable. Bikes are also a popular mode of transportation, and there are several bike rental companies in the area. Taxis and rideshare services are also available, or you can rent a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from South Portland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth is a beautiful seaside town located just a few miles from South Portland. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, lighthouses, and historic forts.

Portland

Portland is just a short drive from South Portland and offers visitors a mix of history, culture, and nightlife. Be sure to explore the Old Port area, the Portland Museum of Art, and the Eastern Promenade.

Freeport

Freeport is a shopper’s paradise, with dozens of outlet stores and quaint shops offering everything from high-end fashion to outdoor gear.

Conclusion

South Portland, Maine, is a charming city that offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether it’s exploring the city’s museums and historical sites, hiking and biking along the South Portland Greenbelt, or enjoying fresh seafood and local beer, there’s something for everyone in South Portland. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Maine.
