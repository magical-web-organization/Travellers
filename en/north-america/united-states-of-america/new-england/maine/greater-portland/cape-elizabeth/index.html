Introduction to Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth is a picturesque town in Greater Portland, Maine, known for its pristine beaches, historic landmarks, and vibrant food scene. Located on the coast of the Atlantic Ocean, this town offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Its craggy shorelines, scenic hiking trails, and charming lighthouses make it a popular destination among travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Cape Elizabeth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cape Elizabeth is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the town’s rich historic past. The following are some of the top culturally important attractions to visit:

  • Portland Head Light: One of the most photographed lighthouses in the country, Portland Head Light is a striking landmark and a remnant of Cape Elizabeth’s maritime history. Visitors can tour the lighthouse and its museum, which features exhibits about the history of the area.

  • Fort Williams Park: A historical park on the site of an old military fortification, Fort Williams Park is a scenic spot with breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can also check out the park’s military museum and explore the remains of the old fort.

  • South Portland Historical Society Museum: This local museum showcases the history of the town and its surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Cape Elizabeth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cape Elizabeth is blessed with natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Two Lights State Park: A picturesque park with rocky shorelines, Two Lights State Park offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and is a popular spot for picnicking, hiking, and fishing.

  • Crescent Beach State Park: A beautiful beach with crescent-shaped dunes, Crescent Beach State Park is a great place to surf, swim, or simply bask in the sun.

  • Great Pond: A remote and tranquil spot, Great Pond is perfect for kayaking, canoeing, and paddle boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth has a vast network of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the town’s vicinity. Here are some of the top trails:

  • Fort Williams Park Trail: A scenic trail that meanders along the coastline of Fort Williams Park, offering breathtaking views of Portland Head Light and the Atlantic Ocean. It is an easy trail suitable for all ages.

  • Cliff Trail: A moderate hiking trail that runs along the rocky coast and offers panoramic views of the ocean.

  • Greenbelt Walkway: A family-friendly trail that connects all the parks in Cape Elizabeth, offering a scenic view of the town.

Best Things to Do in Cape Elizabeth: Activities and Tours

Cape Elizabeth offers various activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s interests. Here are some of the must-do activities:

  • Beach Hopping: Cape Elizabeth has several beaches, each with its unique charm. Spend the day jumping from one beach to another, taking in the beautiful views.

  • Lighthouse Tour: Cape Elizabeth is home to several iconic lighthouses, like the Portland Head Light and Cape Elizabeth Light. Take a guided tour to learn about these landmarks’ history and take in the stunning views.

  • Whale Watching: Take a whale watching tour to spot humpback whales or dolphins in their natural habitat.

Food and Drink Scene of Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth has a fabulous food and drink scene that is a mix of traditional coastal Maine food and modern fusion cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: A quintessential Maine dish made with fresh lobster meat, served in a buttered bun with mayo and celery.

  • Fried Clams: A local favorite, fried clams are served with tartar sauce and lemon wedges.

  • Blueberry Pie: A classic dessert made with locally sourced blueberries.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer: The craft beer scene in Maine is vibrant, with several breweries offering unique flavors. Must-try beers include the Maine Lunch IPA and the Allagash White Belgian-Style Wheat Beer.

  • Blueberry Wine: Made with locally sourced blueberries, blueberry wine is a sweet and fruity wine with an unusual flavor.

Where to Stay in Cape Elizabeth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cape Elizabeth has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Inn by the Sea: A luxury beach resort with ocean-facing suites, a spa, and an award-winning restaurant.

  • Higgins Beach Inn: A charming and historic inn located steps away from Higgins Beach, offering cozy rooms and a traditional Maine atmosphere.

  • Cape Elizabeth Home: A budget-friendly guest house with a shared kitchen, located in the quiet residential neighborhood of Cape Elizabeth.

What to Buy in Cape Elizabeth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cape Elizabeth has several unique souvenirs and local products that travelers can bring home as mementos of their trip. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Lobster-Related Souvenirs: Cape Elizabeth is known for its fresh and delicious lobster, so buy some lobster-themed souvenirs, such as magnets, T-shirts, or stuffed animals.

  • Blueberry Jam: Made with locally sourced blueberries, blueberry jam is a perfect gift for foodies and those with a sweet tooth.

Local Culture and Customs of Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth’s culture is a mix of traditional maritime culture and modern-day liberal attitudes. The town is known for its laid-back and friendly locals who are always willing to help visitors.

Best Time to Visit Cape Elizabeth

The best time to visit Cape Elizabeth is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is pleasant, and there are several outdoor activities to enjoy. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and milder temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cape Elizabeth

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Treat the town’s landmarks and attractions with care.
  • Take advantage of the beautiful natural scenery and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s natural resources.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Make loud noises in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth is a safe and welcoming town. However, visitors should exercise caution while exploring the natural attractions and always follow the park’s rules and regulations.

Health in Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth has several hospitals and medical centers for emergencies. Visitors should always carry a first aid kit and stay hydrated when exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Cape Elizabeth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cape Elizabeth

  • Portland International Jetport: Located just 20 minutes from Cape Elizabeth, the Portland International Jetport is the nearest airport.

Bus Stations Near Cape Elizabeth

  • Concord Coach Lines: Concord Coach Lines offers bus services to Portland, Maine, which is just a 10-minute drive from Cape Elizabeth.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car and drive to Cape Elizabeth from nearby cities like Boston or Portland.

How to Get Around in Cape Elizabeth

Cape Elizabeth has several transportation options, including rental cars, bikes, and public transportation. However, the town is best explored by bike or car to enjoy the scenic views and natural beauty comfortably.

Where to Go Next from Cape Elizabeth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cape Elizabeth is conveniently located near several stunning destinations, making it an ideal base to explore Maine and beyond. Here are some of the nearest excursions:

  • Portland: Portland is Maine’s largest city, located just a 10-minute drive from Cape Elizabeth. Explore the city’s historical landmarks, restaurants, and bars.

  • Kennebunkport: A charming seaside town, located just an hour’s drive from Cape Elizabeth. Stroll around the town’s streets, buy souvenirs, and enjoy fresh seafood.

  • Acadia National Park: A natural wonderland, located a few hours drive from Cape Elizabeth. Explore the park’s rugged coastline, mountains, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Cape Elizabeth is a gem in the crown of Maine’s travel destinations. With so much to see and do, visitors cannot get enough of this charming town. We hope this comprehensive tour guide helps you plan your trip to this extraordinary place. Happy travels!
