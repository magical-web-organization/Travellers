Introduction to Bridgton

Located in Greater Portland, in Maine, in New England, in the United States of America, in North America, Bridgton is a charming town known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. With a population of just over 5,000 people, Bridgton is a popular destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy some peace and quiet in the great outdoors.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs in Bridgton, Maine.

List of Places to Visit in Bridgton

Bridgton is home to a variety of attractions, including museums, historical places, natural wonders, and more. Here are some of the top places to visit in Bridgton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rufus Porter Museum: This museum celebrates the life and work of Rufus Porter, a prominent 19th-century American painter, inventor, and founder of Scientific American magazine.

  • Bridgton Historical Society Museum: Built in 1806, this museum houses a collection of historical artifacts, photos, and documents that tell the story of Bridgton’s past.

  • Narramissic Farm: This historic farm offers visitors a glimpse into the life of the 19th-century Maine farming family that lived there.

Natural Attractions of Bridgton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shawnee Peak Ski Resort: This popular ski resort offers over 40 trails for skiing and snowboarding, as well as breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Moose Pond: This 17-mile-long lake is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and swimming.

  • Pleasant Mountain: With miles of hiking trails and stunning views of the White Mountains, Pleasant Mountain is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridgton

Bridgton is a hiker’s paradise, with dozens of trails weaving through the surrounding mountains and forests. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Bridgton:

  • Bald Pate Mountain Preserve: This popular hiking and biking destination offers stunning views of western Maine and the White Mountains.

  • Bridgton Highlands Country Club: This golf course and hiking trail offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation.

  • Bradbury Mountain State Park: With miles of hiking and biking trails and stunning views of Casco Bay, Bradbury Mountain State Park is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Bridgton: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and skiing, there are plenty of other things to see and do in Bridgton. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Bridgton Drive-In: This vintage drive-in movie theater is the perfect place to catch a flick and enjoy some old-fashioned entertainment.

  • Maine Craft Distilling: Tour this local distillery and learn about the process of crafting high-quality spirits.

  • Hiram Blake Camp: This historic camp offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, swimming, fishing, boating, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bridgton

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Bridgton, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Rufus Porter Museum, Bridgton Historical Society Museum, Narramissic Farm

Day 2: Pleasant Mountain, Shawnee Peak Ski Resort

Day 3: Moose Pond, Bridgton Drive-In

Day 4: Maine Craft Distilling, Highland Lake Resort

Food and Drink Scene of Bridgton

Bridgton has a variety of dining options for visitors to enjoy, from classic American fare to local seafood and craft beers. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Bridgton:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England dish, the lobster roll at Standard Gastropub is a must-try for seafood lovers.

  • Shepherd’s Pie: Made with locally-sourced ingredients, the shepherd’s pie at Black Horse Tavern is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

  • Maple Bacon Donut: This sweet and savory pastry from Bridgton’s Best Donuts is a must-try for dessert lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their popular Lunch IPA.

  • Cellardoor Winery: Sample a variety of award-winning wines at this local vineyard and tasting room.

  • Stirring the Mash Whiskey Distillery: Tour this local distillery and try some of their handcrafted whiskies.

Bridgton has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast or a luxurious hotel. Here are some of the top places to stay in Bridgton:

  • The Inn at Crystal Lake: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.

Getting to Bridgton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bridgton is easily accessible by car, as it is located off Route 302 in southern Maine. Here are some transportation options to consider when traveling to Bridgton:

Airports Near Bridgton

  • Portland International Jetport (PWM): Located just 45 minutes away from Bridgton, the Portland International Jetport is the closest major airport to Bridgton.

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT): Located about 2 hours away from Bridgton, the Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is another option for visitors traveling to the area.

Bus Stations Near Bridgton

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus line offers daily service to and from Portland, making it easy for visitors to get to and from Bridgton.

  • Greyhound: Greyhound also offers daily service to Portland, with connections to other major cities throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in Portland and drive to Bridgton.

  • Private car service: Arrange for a private car service or taxi to pick you up and bring you to Bridgton from the airport or surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Bridgton

Public transportation is limited in Bridgton, so renting a car is the easiest way to get around. Alternatively, visitors can also use taxis or ride-sharing services to get to and from their destination.
