Introduction to Gorham

Nestled in the heart of Maine’s Greater Portland region, the town of Gorham is a charming New England destination that offers something for every type of traveler. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and delicious local cuisine, Gorham has it all.

Founded in 1764, Gorham is steeped in history and charm. It’s located just 10 miles west of Portland, Maine’s largest city, which makes it an easy day trip or a convenient base for exploring the surrounding areas. Whether you’re interested in taking a nature hike, learning about local history, or trying some delicious lobster, Gorham won’t disappoint.

List of Places to Visit in Gorham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gorham Historical Society & Railroad Museum: Housed in a restored 1862 train depot, this museum features exhibits on Gorham’s history and railroad heritage.

  • Narragansett Schoolhouse: This well-preserved one-room schoolhouse dates back to 1852 and offers a glimpse into the past.

  • South Gorham Civil War Monument: Located in the town center, the monument honors Gorham residents who fought in the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Gorham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Babb’s Mountain: This scenic 500-acre conservation area has hiking trails and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • White Rock Preserve: This 120-acre preserve has hiking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and a viewpoint overlooking the Presumpscot River.

  • Sebago to the Sea Trail: This 28-mile trail runs from Sebago Lake to Casco Bay and passes through Gorham. It’s ideal for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gorham

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or just looking for a leisurely stroll, Gorham has plenty of beautiful trails to explore. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Rochester Falls Trail: This easy 1.2-mile trail leads to a scenic waterfall and is perfect for families with kids.

  • Libby Hill Forest Trails: This extensive network of trails offers opportunities for hiking, biking, cross-country skiing, and snowshoeing. The trails vary in difficulty and length, so there’s something for everyone.

  • Narragansett Trail: This 5-mile trail winds through woods, fields, and wetlands and is ideal for birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Gorham: Activities and Tours

  • Maine Beer Company: Take a tour of this popular craft brewery and sample some of their award-winning beers.

  • Gorham Bike and Ski: Rent a bike or cross-country skis and explore the scenic trails in the area.

  • Fore River Sanctuary: This 85-acre nature preserve has hiking trails, picnic areas, and a salt marsh.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gorham

Day 1: Explore Gorham’s downtown area, including the historical society museum and local shops and restaurants.

Day 2: Take a hike on one of Gorham’s beautiful trails, such as the Sebago to the Sea Trail or the Rochester Falls Trail.

Day 3: Visit nearby Portland and explore its shops, restaurants, and museums.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Kennebunkport, a charming coastal town known for its beaches and picturesque homes.

Day 5: Visit Cape Elizabeth and see Portland Head Light, one of Maine’s most iconic lighthouses.

Day 6: Go biking or cross-country skiing on one of Gorham’s scenic trails, such as Libby Hill Forest or Narragansett Trail.

Day 7: Enjoy some local cuisine at one of Gorham’s restaurants, such as The Frog and Turtle or The Great Lost Bear.

Food and Drink Scene of Gorham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster: No trip to Maine would be complete without trying this classic dish.

  • Cider Donuts: These sweet treats are a New England specialty, and the ones at Pumpkin Valley Farm are particularly delicious.

  • Fish and Chips: Gorham House of Pizza serves up this classic dish with a Maine twist, using locally caught seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer Company: This popular brewery in Freeport offers a wide range of delicious craft beer.

  • Allagash Brewing Company: Located in Portland, this brewery is known for its Belgian-style beer.

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: This Portland-based brewery is famous for its iconic pumpkin ale.

Where to Stay in Gorham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gorham Motor Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to downtown Gorham.

  • PineCrest Inn: This historic inn dates back to 1810 and has 12 charming rooms and a full-service restaurant.

  • English Meadows Inn: This elegant bed and breakfast has seven beautifully appointed rooms and is located in nearby Kennebunkport.

What to Buy in Gorham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lobster-themed souvenirs: From t-shirts to keychains, you’ll find plenty of fun lobster-themed souvenirs in Gorham.

  • Maine-made maple syrup: Maine is known for its delicious maple syrup, and you can find plenty of locally made options in Gorham.

  • Handcrafted pottery: Some of Maine’s most talented artisans are based in Gorham, and their handcrafted pottery makes a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Gorham

Gorham residents are friendly and welcoming, and the town has a strong sense of community. Locals are proud of their town’s history and are happy to share their knowledge with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Gorham

The best time to visit Gorham is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, thanks to the stunning foliage displays. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it’s a great time to enjoy winter sports like skiing and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gorham

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s rich history and take advantage of the outdoor activities.

  • Do try some local cuisine, from lobster and seafood to cider donuts and craft beer.

  • Do take a day trip to nearby Portland or Kennebunkport.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any of the natural attractions.

  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather, especially if you’re taking part in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gorham

Gorham is a safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. As always, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when traveling.

Health in Gorham

Gorham has several medical facilities, including Mercy Gorham Primary Care and PrimeCare. Remember to pack any necessary medications and travel insurance for your trip.

Getting to Gorham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gorham

  • Portland International Jetport: Located just 11 miles east of Gorham, this airport offers flights to major US cities.

Bus Stations Near Gorham

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus line offers service from Portland’s transportation center to cities throughout Maine and New Hampshire.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: Rentals cars are available at the Portland airport and in downtown Portland.

How to Get Around in Gorham

Gorham is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot or by bike. If you’re planning to visit nearby areas, a rental car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Gorham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland: Just 10 miles east of Gorham, Portland is a must-visit destination known for its vibrant food and drink scene, fascinating history, and beautiful coastal landscapes.

  • Kennebunkport: This picturesque coastal town is just a short drive from Gorham and is known for its beautiful beaches, picturesque homes, and charming downtown area.

Conclusion

Gorham, Maine is a charming destination that offers a little bit of everything. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or craft beer, you’ll find something to love in this beautiful New England town. Plan your trip today and get ready for an unforgettable adventure.
