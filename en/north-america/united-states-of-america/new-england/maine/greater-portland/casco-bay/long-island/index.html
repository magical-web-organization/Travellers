Introduction to Long Island

Long Island is a quaint island situated in Casco Bay of Greater Portland, Maine, USA. It is known for its picturesque towns, sandy beaches, and unique culture. It is an ideal destination for tourists looking for outdoor festivities, beautiful sceneries, and wildlife sightings.

List of Places to Visit in Long Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bailey Island Historical Society

It is a non-profit organization dedicated to preserving Bailey Island’s history through the collection of some artifacts, images, and oral history recordings. The museum’s ground floor contains photos and memorabilia of the island’s commercial fishing industry, while the second floor is dedicated to artifacts and information on the establishment of Bailey Island cottages.

Cundy’s Harbor Library

The library exhibits literary works and history of Long Island, and it also holds occasional events such as guest speakers and author readings.

Natural Attractions of Long Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Driftwood Beach State Park

This 44-acre island features sprawling beaches and hiking trails. It is also an excellent destination for birdwatching, whale watching, and sea kayaking.

Mackerel Cove

This scenic beach offers a calm and serene environment, with crystal clear water and pristine beaches. It is an excellent destination for swimming, paddling, and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Island

Long Island Trail Network

The Long Island Trail Network offers an extensive system of hiking, biking, and walking trails that provide stunning views of the island’s wildlife, birds, and flora.

Best Things to Do in Long Island: Activities and Tours

Casco Bay Mailboat Run

An exciting way to explore the Casco Bay by hopping on board a ferry that drops off mail and supplies to the local community.

Island Lobstermen Boat Tour

The tour offers an immersive experience of the local lobster fishing industry. Guests are taken on a working lobster boat for a demonstration of pulling up lobster traps and exploring the island’s picturesque coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Island

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Explore the Mackerel Cove area and visit the Bailey Island Historical Society.
Day 2 Take part in the Casco Bay Mailboat Run and tour the island’s famous coves.
Day 3 Hop on a kayak or paddleboard to explore the island’s hidden beaches and the ocean coast.
Day 4 Take the Island Lobstermen Boat Tour and learn about the local lobster fishing industry.
Day 5 Visit the Cundy’s Harbor Library and rent a bicycle to explore the Long Island Trail Network.
Day 6 Take a scenic boat tour on the Casco Bay and enjoy sunset views along the coastline.
Day 7 Go whale watching on a nearby island or fishing for striped bass and mackerel.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Island

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Rolls

A classic New England dish, the lobster roll is a must-try when in Long Island. A hot dog bun stuffed with succulent and fresh lobster meat, mayonnaise, and butter.

Clam Chowder

Another New England classic, clam chowder, is a rich seafood stew made with fresh clams, diced potatoes, and a dash of cream.

Local Beverages

Maine Beer Company

Located in the nearby town of Freeport, Maine Beer Company boasts an impressive collection of locally brewed craft beers.

Island Lobster Co.

A refreshing sip of Island Lobster Co.’s sparkling wine is an excellent way to toast to your visit to Long Island.

Where to Stay in Long Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chebeague Island Inn

Located just a ferry ride away, the Chebeague Island Inn offers luxury accommodation with fantastic ocean views.

Long Island Inn

The Long Island Inn offers rooms with stunning views of the scenic island and boasts an on-site restaurant and bar.

Island View Cottages

For those looking for a more rustic experience, Island View Cottages offer cozy and affordable apartments and cottages with a view of Casco Bay.

What to Buy in Long Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sea Salt

Long Island’s unique marine environment has always been favorable for the production of artisanal sea salt.

Lobster

Lobster is a northeastern icon. One of the best things you can do is contact Island Lobster Co. and bring home the lobster you caught with them!

Local Culture and Customs of Long Island

Long Island has a strong sense of community and holds various festivals year-round. It is advised to respect the locals’ customs and participate in everything that the island has to offer.

Best Time to Visit Long Island

The best time to visit Long Island is from June to September as the island enjoys a pleasant climate during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Island

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Participate in local festivities and events.
  • Explore the Island Trail Network.

Don’t:

  • Litter in sensitive areas.
  • Smoke or make loud noise on the beaches, nature reserves, and parks.
  • Park in restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Island

The island has a low crime rate, and it is generally safe to explore on foot, bike, or golf cart. However, it’s still important to lock your bikes and valuables in your accommodation or vehicle.

Health in Long Island

The nearest medical facilities are available in Portland, so it is advised to bring any necessary medication along with you. Be mindful of hygiene, stay hydrated, and apply sunscreen frequently.

Getting to Long Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Long Island

The closest airport to Long Island is the Portland International Jetport, which is a 30-minute drive from Casco Bay.

Bus Stations Near Long Island

The Concord Coach Lines Bus Terminal is 30 minutes from Long Island and offers various routes to Boston, New York, and other destinations.

Ferry Services

The Chebeague Transportation Company offers ferry services to Long Island from Portland, and the Casco Bay Ferry offers service schedules between surrounding islands.

How to Get Around in Long Island

Vehicles such as cars and motorcycles are not permitted on the island, but visitors can rent bikes and golf carts for transportation around the island.

Where to Go Next from Long Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Portland, Maine

With an abundance of restaurants, art galleries, and museums to refresh you, Portland, Maine, is a great place to begin your New England journey.

Conclusion

Long Island is a beautiful and secluded destination that offers tranquil beaches, hiking trails, delectable seafood, and a sustainable marine environment. The island is ideal for visitors looking for a serene environment away from the hustle and bustle of city life.
