Discovering Chebeague Island: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Chebeague Island is a hidden gem located in Casco Bay, in Greater Portland, Maine, USA. It’s a perfect place for those seeking adventure, natural beauty, and a glimpse of traditional New England life. From historic landmarks to scenic views, there is something for everyone on Chebeague Island. In this travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, where to eat, stay, and buy local souvenirs. We’ll also share some safety tips, cultural notes, and transportation options to help plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Chebeague Island

Chebeague Island has many attractions for visitors, from museums to natural wonders. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chebeague Island Historical Society Museum: Located in the Island Hall, the museum showcases the history of Chebeague Island through photographs and artifacts.
  • Chebeague Island Inn: This historic inn dates back to the 1920s and is an excellent example of traditional New England architecture.
  • Stone Wharf: A wharf where local fishermen unload their catch of lobsters and other seafood. It’s also a great place to view the sunset with a cold brew in hand.

Natural Attractions of Chebeague Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chebeague Island Boat Yard: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the beautiful Casco Bay from the water.
  • Chebeague Island Cemetery: Take a walk through the oldest cemetery in Maine, which dates back to the 1700s.
  • Hamilton Beach: A small, secluded beach on the eastern side of the island, Hamilton Beach offers calm waters and beautiful views of Casco Bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chebeague Island

Chebeague Island has several scenic routes for hiking and biking:

  • Cliff Trail: A 2.5-mile trail that takes you through forests and offers views of the surrounding islands and ocean.
  • East Shore Trail: A 5.5-mile trail that takes you along the eastern shore of the island, passing by beaches and rocky outcroppings.
  • North Ridge Trail: A 1-mile trail that takes you to the highest point of the island, offering amazing views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Chebeague Island: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do on Chebeague Island, such as:

  • Sailing Tours: Explore the beauty of Casco Bay on a sailing trip, available through several local companies.
  • Fishing Trips: Join a local fisherman and experience the thrill of catching lobsters or other seafood.
  • Golfing: Enjoy the beautiful scenery while playing a round of golf at the Chebeague Island Golf Course.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chebeague Island

If you’re planning on staying longer, here’s a suggested itinerary for a week on Chebeague Island:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Chebeague Island Historical Society Museum and learn about the island’s history

5 Enjoy a round of golf at the Chebeague Island Golf Course

Food and Drink Scene of Chebeague Island

Chebeague Island has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many restaurants offering fresh seafood and other local delicacies.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A local delicacy, available at many island restaurants including the Chebeague Island Inn and the Chebeague Island Boat Yard.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy soup made with fresh clams, potatoes, and other vegetables.
  • Blueberry Pie: A traditional dessert made with fresh blueberries, sugar, and flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer: Craft breweries on the mainland and nearby islands offer a variety of beers, from IPAs to stouts.
  • Cider: Made with local apples, cider is a refreshing drink on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Chebeague Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chebeague Island has several options for accommodations, from historic inns to guesthouses.

  • Chebeague Island Inn: A historic inn with ocean views, fine dining, and elegant rooms.
  • The Chebeague Island Boat Yard: A guesthouse with direct access to the Casco Bay and amenities such as a boat rental and a beach bar.
  • Airbnb Rentals: Several rental options, from small apartments to large homes, available throughout the island.

What to Buy in Chebeague Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chebeague Island offers several unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  • Lobster Traps: Locally made traps, perfect for decoration or as a gift.
  • Blueberry Jam: Made with fresh local blueberries, this delicious jam is a perfect souvenir to take home.
  • Sweaters and Beanies: Warm and cozy apparel made with natural wool, a perfect gift for a loved one or for oneself.

Local Culture and Customs of Chebeague Island

Chebeague Island has a unique culture rooted in traditional New England life. The islanders are proud of their heritage and welcoming to visitors. Some customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect the islanders: Chebeague Island has a tight-knit community, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions.
  • Slow down: The island moves at a slower pace than the mainland, so visitors should enjoy the relaxed atmosphere and take their time.

Best Time to Visit Chebeague Island

The best time to visit Chebeague Island is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm, and the island has several activities and festivals during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chebeague Island

Do:

  • Visit the local landmarks: Chebeague Island has several unique attractions that offer a glimpse into its culture and history, such as the Chebeague Island Historical Society Museum and Stone Wharf.
  • Try the seafood: Chebeague Island is known for its fresh seafood, so visitors should try some of the local delicacies such as lobster and clams.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Visitors should be respectful of the island’s natural beauty and dispose of their waste properly.
  • Trespass: Visitors should respect private property and avoid trespassing on land that is not open to the public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chebeague Island

Chebeague Island is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions:

  • Be aware of wildlife: Chebeague Island has several wild animals such as ticks and mosquitos, so visitors should protect themselves from bites.
  • Dress for the weather: Chebeague Island’s weather can be unpredictable, so visitors should wear appropriate clothing and footwear.

Health in Chebeague Island

Chebeague Island has a first-aid station and several pharmacies and clinics on the mainland.

Getting to Chebeague Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chebeague Island is accessible by ferry or water taxi from several locations, including Portland, Yarmouth, and Cumberland. Some options include:

Airports Near Chebeague Island

  • Portland International Jetport: Located 20 miles from Chebeague Island, the airport offers daily flights to several locations.

Bus Stations Near Chebeague Island

  • Portland Transportation Center: Located 13 miles from Chebeague Island, the bus station offers daily services to several locations in Maine and beyond.

Other Options

  • Water Taxi: A private boat transfer available from several locations, including Portland.

How to Get Around in Chebeague Island

Chebeague Island is best explored on foot, by bike, or by renting a golf cart. Visitors can also rent cars from the mainland and bring them on the ferry.

Where to Go Next from Chebeague Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chebeague Island is a perfect starting point for exploring the natural and cultural attractions of Maine. Nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Portland: A vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage, located 20 miles from Chebeague Island.
  • Freeport: A shopper’s paradise, known for its outlet stores and boutiques, located 30 miles from Chebeague Island.
  • Acadia National Park: A majestic national park with spectacular views, located 130 miles from Chebeague Island.

Conclusion

Chebeague Island offers a unique New England experience, with its historic landmarks, natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene.
