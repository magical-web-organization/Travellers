Introduction to Peaks Island

Peaks Island is a picturesque island in Casco Bay, just a few miles off the coast of Portland, Maine. This island is the epitome of the beauty that Maine has to offer. The beauty of the beaches and natural wonders is what draws tourists to its shores.

List of Places to Visit in Peaks Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Peaks Island has a rich history, and there are many culturally important attractions that visitors can explore. While visiting the island, be sure to check out the following places:

  • Fifth Maine Regiment Museum: This museum showcases the history of the US Fifth Maine Infantry Regiment and their role in the Civil War.
  • Umbrella Cover Museum: This unique museum is dedicated to the humble umbrella cover, with an extensive collection of decorated covers.
  • The Island Inn: Built in 1905, this historic hotel has been serving guests for over a century.

Natural Attractions of Peaks Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Peaks Island is simply breathtaking, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to experience it to the fullest. Here are some places to visit:

  • Casco Bay Islands: Take a ferry ride to explore the other beautiful islands of Casco Bay.
  • Backshore Beach: Enjoy the scenic views and sunsets while strolling down the sandy shores of this peaceful beach.
  • Battery Steele: Explore the WWII bunker that lies beneath the island. It is a unique adventure that you will not forget.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peaks Island

Peaks Island offers numerous hiking and biking routes that provide a chance to explore the beautiful landscapes of the island. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Battery Steele Trail
  • The Backshore Cove Trail
  • The Centennial Loop Trail

Best Things to Do in Peaks Island: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy on the island. Some of the best things to do in Peaks Island are:

  • Take a lobster boat tour to learn about the Maine lobster fishing tradition.
  • Rent a kayak and paddle around Casco Bay Islands and see the beautiful sunset.
  • Observe wildlife on a whale watching tour and see the beauty of the sea creatures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peaks Island

Here is a week-long itinerary for Peaks Island:

Day 1: Explore the island on a bike tour and pick up a picnic lunch from the local market.

Day 2: Visit the Fifth Maine Regiment Museum and learn about the island’s military history.

Day 3: Take a day trip to another island in Casco Bay.

Day 4: Rent a kayak for a few hours and paddle along the shoreline.

Day 5: Relax on Backshore Beach and enjoy the sunset over the ocean.

Day 6: Attend a local music concert or art show in town.

Day 7: Go on a tour of the Umbrella Cover Museum, or take a ferry ride to Portland to explore the city.

Where to Stay in Peaks Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peaks Island has plenty of options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options are:

  • The Island Inn: This historic hotel offers guests a charming and comfortable stay with breathtaking views of the ocean.
  • Peaks Island House: A beautiful guesthouse offering a peaceful escape from the mainland.
  • The Inn on Peaks Island: This cozy inn offers visitors comfortable accommodation in the heart of the island.

What to Buy in Peaks Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peaks Island has something unique and special for everyone. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home:

  • Handcrafted artisan jewelry
  • Local Maine jams and jellies
  • Paintings and photographs by local artists

Local Culture and Customs of Peaks Island

Peaks Island is a tight-knit community that values its unique and peaceful way of life. Visitors are always welcome, but it is important to respect the customs of the locals and treat the island with care.

Best Time to Visit Peaks Island

The best time to visit Peaks Island is from May to October when the weather is mild and enjoyable. The fall months are particularly beautiful, with colorful foliage and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peaks Island

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local community.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the island with responsible behavior
  • Try the local cuisine and enjoy a cozy and peaceful stay on the island.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on the island.
  • Disturb or harm wildlife in any way.
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior towards locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peaks Island

Peaks Island is a safe destination, but it is important to take the following precautions:

  • Always wear a life jacket while engaging in water activities.
  • Follow the local safety guidelines and laws when operating a bicycle or vehicle.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay within your comfort zone while exploring the wild landscapes.

Health in Peaks Island

Peaks Island has a small medical clinic that can provide basic medical care if needed. Visitors should bring any necessary medication and always practice good hygiene practices.

Getting to Peaks Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Peaks Island via ferry from Portland. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Peaks Island

  • Portland International Jetport( PWM): The closest airport to Peaks Island is the Portland International Jetport

Bus Stations Near Peaks Island

  • Concord Coach Lines: Offers a bus service from Portland to Boston

Other Options

  • Taxis: There are taxis that offer transportation to and from the ferry terminal in Portland.

How to Get Around in Peaks Island

The best way to explore Peaks Island is by foot, bike or golf cart rentals which are available on the Island. Golf carts are particularly popular among visitors who want to explore the island at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Peaks Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peaks Island is an ideal starting point to explore other breathtaking destinations in Maine. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore:

  • Portland: Just a ferry ride away, Portland is a vibrant city with plenty of shopping, dining, and outdoor activities.

  • Acadia National Park: A few hours drive away, this stunning National Park is a must-visit for nature lovers.

  • Kennebunkport: A scenic coastal town that offers a perfect seaside escape.
