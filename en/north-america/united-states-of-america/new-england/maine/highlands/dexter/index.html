Introduction to Dexter

Nestled in the Highlands region of Maine, Dexter is a fascinating destination full of natural and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventure or learning about history and culture, Dexter has something for everyone. With its charming small-town vibe, travelers can enjoy an authentic Maine experience.

List of Places to Visit in Dexter

If you’re planning a trip to Dexter, here’s a list of must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Abbott Museum: Located in nearby Monson, the Abbott Museum features exhibits showcasing the town’s history from pre-colonial era to present day.
  • The Dexter Historical Society: This local institution explores the regional history of Dexter, including the role of the woolen mill that helped build the town.
  • The Fireman’s Museum: Located inside the town hall, The Fireman’s Museum features old-fashioned firefighting gear and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Dexter: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Wassookeag: This picturesque lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, kayaking, and boating.
  • Mount Katahdin: One of the highest points in Maine, Mount Katahdin offers breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Dexter Town Forest: Great for hiking, the forest offers a stunning view of the nearby ridge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dexter

Dexter is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise with beautiful trails for all experience levels.

  • The Pinecone Trail: A four-mile trail that weaves through the forest and offers a stunning view of Lake Wassookeag.
  • The Packard Trail: A 9-mile trail that winds through dense forest and offers several challenging climbs.
  • The Village Loop Trail: A beginner-friendly trail that is perfect for family hiking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Dexter: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do while you’re in Dexter:

  • Take a ride on the Moosehead Lake Steamship Katahdin.
  • Tour the local farms and learn about the region’s agricultural heritage.
  • Attend the annual Maine Whoopie Pie Festival in nearby Dover-Foxcroft for a delicious treat.

Food and Drink Scene of Dexter

With a focus on farm-to-table cuisine, Dexter has an emerging food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster rolls: A traditional Maine sandwich prepared with fresh lobster meat.
  • Blueberry pie: Blueberries are a local staple in Maine and you can try it in a pie form.
  • Whoopie pies: A Maine dessert institution, whoopie pies are two chocolate cakes filled with a sweet creamy filling.

Local Beverages

  • Maine beer: Craft breweries are a big deal in Maine, so be sure to try a local brew or two.
  • Apple cider: Maine is also known for its apple orchards and cider production.

Where to Stay in Dexter: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some accommodation options in Dexter:

  • The Brewster Inn: A charming bed and breakfast with an on-site restaurant offering a variety of local options.
  • The Four Winds Inn: A budget-friendly option located right in the heart of town.
  • The Galloping Goose: An elegant guesthouse overlooking the lake, perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Dexter: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for something to bring back home? Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Maine blueberries and maple syrup: Maine is known for its blueberries and maple syrup, so grab a jar of each to take home.
  • Handmade pottery: There are several local studios where you can purchase unique and beautiful pottery.
  • Locally made woolen products: Since Dexter’s history began with woolen mill, you can look for some locally and sustainably made woolen hats, mittens, bags, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Dexter

Maine is well-known for its friendly and laid-back culture. When you’re in Dexter, expect to see its signature hospitality and love of the outdoors. People are always up for socializing over a campfire or attending local fairs and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Dexter

The best time to visit Dexter is during the summer months between late May and early October, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and nature is at its prime. But if you love winter sports like skiing, sledding, and snowmobiling, then the winter months are perfect for you.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dexter

Here are some practical do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while you’re in Dexter:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and the local customs.
  • Embrace the spirit of the town and connect with local people.
  • Take advantage of the natural beauty and get outside.

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause any harm to the environment.
  • Be rude or dismissive to locals.
  • Overlook the little shops and family-run restaurants in favor of big chains.

Safety and Health

When it comes to Dexter, there are some things to keep in mind in regards to safety and health.

Safety in Dexter

Dexter is a relatively safe town with very low crime rates. However, as with any destination, use common sense and take precautions like locking your doors and safeguarding your belongings.

Health in Dexter

As a general rule, it’s always a good idea to pack any essential medications and first aid supplies you may need. Also, be mindful of drinking water, where you eat, and make use of sun protection if you plan to spend time outside.

Getting to Dexter: Transportation Options and Logistics

If you’re looking to visit Dexter, here are some transportation options to keep in mind:

Airports Near Dexter

  • Bangor International Airport: About one hour away from Dexter.

Bus Stations Near Dexter

  • The Concord Trailways bus line runs from Boston to Bangor, with a stop in Dexter.

Other Options

  • Driving: Dexter is easily accessible from the nearby highway, Route 95.
  • Cycling: The region’s bike paths are spacious, and cycling can be a great way to get around.

How to Get Around in Dexter

If you’re looking to move around within Dexter, here are some transportation options to keep in mind:

  • Walking: Downtown Dexter is small enough to explore on foot.
  • Biking: There are bike rentals available, and exploring Dexter on two wheels can help you explore further.
  • Car rental companies: If you prefer to have more freedom and flexibility, rental companies are available.

Where to Go Next from Dexter: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to check out after visiting Dexter:

  • Acadia National Park: Located a couple of hours’ drive away, it’s an excellent spot for outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Sebec Lake: Located nearby, Sebec Lake is a popular destination for boating, swimming, and fishing.

Conclusion

Dexter is a hidden gem in Maine’s Highlands region that’s well worth a visit. With plenty of natural and cultural attractions, along with a thriving food and drink scene, there is something here for everyone.
