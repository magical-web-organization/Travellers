Introduction to Patten

Nestled in the Highlands of Maine, Patten is a small town known for its picturesque scenery and charming rural environment. Its proximity to Baxter State Park and the Katahdin Woods and Waters National Monument makes it an ideal base for outdoor enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve rounded up the best things to do, places to visit, and tips for planning your trip to Patten.

List of Places to Visit in Patten

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lumberman’s Museum: This museum chronicles the history of the logging industry in Maine, including artifacts, videos, and photographs.
  • Patten Free Library: A historic library with a collection of more than 40,000 volumes, including rare books and manuscripts.
  • The North Maine Woods: A privately-owned, road-less wilderness spanning over 3.5 million acres, the North Maine Woods offers an authentic glimpse into Maine’s untamed wilderness.

Natural Attractions of Patten: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Katahdin Woods and Waters National Monument: A 87,500-acre national monument with miles of hiking trails, camping sites, and opportunities for wildlife viewing.
  • Baxter State Park: A vast wilderness area with over 200,000 acres of forests, peaks, lakes, and streams.
  • Mount Katahdin: The highest mountain in Maine, with several trails leading to the summit and breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Patten

  • Saint John Valley Heritage Trail: A 14-mile biking and hiking trail that passes through rolling farmland and charming small towns.
  • Baxter State Park Trails: A network of over 200 miles of trails of varying difficulty that lead to scenic overlooks, waterfalls, and camping sites.
  • Katahdin Woods and Waters National Monument Trails: With over 80 miles of trails, this national monument offers something for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Patten: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: With numerous lakes and rivers in the area, Patten is an ideal destination for paddling.
  • Fishing: Opportunities for fishing abound in the area, with brook trout, salmon, and bass just a few of the species available.
  • Moose Watching: Patten is home to a large population of moose and guided tours are available year-round.

Week-Long Itinerary for Patten

Day 1: Hike to the summit of Mount Katahdin Day 2: Explore the North Maine Woods Day 3: Bike the Saint John Valley Heritage Trail Day 4: Whitewater rafting on the Penobscot River Day 5: Visit Lumberman’s Museum and learn about Maine’s logging industry Day 6: Canoe or kayak along the Allagash Wilderness Waterway Day 7: Visit Baxter State Park and hike to one of the waterfalls

Food and Drink Scene of Patten

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maine Lobster Roll: Fresh Maine lobster meat served on a buttery roll.
  • Blueberry Pie: Made with locally grown Maine blueberries.
  • Poutine: A Canadian dish with French fries topped with gravy and cheese curds.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer Company: A craft brewery located in Freeport, Maine.
  • Allagash Brewing Company: A Belgian-style brewery located in Portland, Maine.
  • Bar Harbor Brewing Company: A brewery located in Bar Harbor, Maine, that specializes in English-style ales.

Where to Stay in Patten: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Young House Bed and Breakfast: A charming 1890s Victorian house located in the heart of Patten.
  • The Crow’s Nest Campground: A family-friendly campground located on the banks of the Patten Stream.
  • The Appalachian Trail Lodge: A rustic lodge located near the Baxter State Park entrance.

What to Buy in Patten: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lobster Traps: A unique souvenir that also makes for interesting home decor.
  • Maine Blueberry Products: From blueberry jam to blueberry wine, there are many local products featuring Maine’s famous berries.
  • Handmade Crafts: From pottery to woodworking, Maine is home to many talented artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Patten

Maine is known for its lobster industry, and lobster is an important part of the local culture. Visiting one of the many lobster shacks in the area is a must-do activity for anyone visiting Patten.

Best Time to Visit Patten

The best time to visit Patten is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the area’s many outdoor attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Patten

Do:

  • Enjoy the local scenery and wildlife.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.
  • Try the local lobster.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore warnings about unsafe conditions or areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Patten

Patten is a safe town, but visitors should exercise caution when hiking in remote areas and be aware of the potential for wildlife encounters.

Health in Patten

Visitors should be aware of ticks and the potential for Lyme disease, and take appropriate precautions when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Patten: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Patten

The nearest major airport to Patten is Bangor International Airport, which is approximately 75 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Patten

The nearest bus station to Patten is the Greyhound station in Houlton, Maine, which is approximately 45 miles away.

Other Options

Car rentals are available at both Bangor and Portland airports, as well as in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Patten

Having a car is the easiest method of transportation in Patten. However, biking and hiking offer great opportunities to explore the area on foot.

Where to Go Next from Patten: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bar Harbor, Maine: A charming coastal town with a vibrant arts scene and access to Acadia National Park.
  • Rangeley, Maine: A lakeside town known for its scenic beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Quebec City, Canada: A charming, European-style city located just across the Canadian border.

Conclusion

Patten is a hidden gem in the heart of Maine’s Highlands, offering visitors a chance to experience the state’s natural beauty and rich history. From hiking and biking trails to charming B&Bs and award-winning breweries, there’s something for everyone in Patten.
