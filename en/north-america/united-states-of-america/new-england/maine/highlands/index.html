Introduction to Highlands

Nestled in the scenic region of New England, Highlands, Maine is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Surrounded by mountains, rolling hills, and picturesque landscapes, Highlands offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life. A destination that is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and friendly locals, Highlands is a must-visit place for anyone looking for a relaxing and rejuvenating vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Highlands

Highlands boasts a plethora of attractions and activities. Whether you prefer museums and historical sites, outdoor adventures, or scenic views, this travel guide has got you covered.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Highlands Museum: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the history and culture of Highlands. Visitors can explore exhibits on the region’s agricultural heritage, textile mills, and paper-making industries.
  • Maine Forest and Logging Museum: This museum offers insights into the history of forestry and logging in Maine. Visitors can learn about the tools and techniques used in the industry and take a guided tour of the nearby forests.
  • Bicentennial Park: This park is a celebration of the 200th anniversary of Highlands’ founding. It features a granite monument commemorating the town’s history and a walking trail with benches and picnic tables.

Natural Attractions of Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Acadia National Park: This park is a haven for hikers and bikers, featuring more than 120 miles of trails, coastal views, and refreshing waterfalls.
  • Echo Lake Beach: This pristine beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. The crystal-clear waters and the surrounding hills make it a breathtaking spot.
  • Jordan Pond: This pond is renowned for its crystal-clear waters and Native American history. Visitors can hike around the pond, take a boat ride, or simply enjoy a picnic by the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Highlands

Highlands offers a wide range of trails and routes for hikers and bikers. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Cadillac Mountain: This trail is a challenging but rewarding experience. It offers magnificent views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Precipice Trail: This trail is a must-visit for adventurous hikers. It features a steep ascent on narrow trails with ladders and breathtaking views.
  • Carriage Roads: These roads are a popular biking destination, offering scenic views of the park and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Highlands: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Highlands offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best things to do in Highlands are:

  • Whale watching tours: Tour operators offer excursions to see the gentle giants of the sea in their natural habitat.
  • Kayaking and canoeing: The lakes and rivers of Highlands offer breathtaking routes for kayaking and canoeing.
  • Golfing: Highlands is home to some of the best golf courses in the region. Visitors can enjoy a round of golf while taking in the stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Highlands

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Highlands, here’s a sample itinerary that covers most of the highlights:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Highlands Museum and Bicentennial Park
Day 2 Explore Acadia National Park, hike Cadillac Mountain
Day 3 Visit Maine Forest and Logging Museum, go kayaking
Day 4 Relax by the Echo Lake Beach, go whale watching
Day 5 Visit Jordan Pond, take a boat tour
Day 6 Go golfing, bike the Carriage Roads
Day 7 Take a guided tour of the Bar Harbor region

Food and Drink Scene of Highlands

Highlands boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with numerous restaurants and taverns offering a range of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster rolls: No trip to Maine is complete without trying the iconic lobster rolls. They’re a local delicacy and can be found in most restaurants in Highlands.
  • Blueberry pie: Blueberries are a staple in Maine cuisine, and the blueberry pie is a must-try dessert.
  • Clam chowder: This hearty soup is a classic New England dish that is popular in Highlands.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Highlands is home to several microbreweries that offer a range of craft beers. Some of the popular breweries include Atlantic Brewing Company and Bar Harbor Brewing Company.
  • Blueberry soda: This refreshing drink is made with real blueberries and is a popular local beverage.

Where to Stay in Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to charming bed and breakfasts, and affordable hostels, Highlands offers accommodation options to suit every budget and taste.

Luxury hotels

  • West Street Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Bar Harbor and offers ocean view rooms, spa services, and on-site dining options.
  • Harborside Hotel and Marina: This hotel offers waterfront rooms, indoor and outdoor pools, and several dining options.

Bed and breakfasts

  • Saltair Inn Waterfront Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers stunning views of the Frenchman Bay, antique furniture, and homemade breakfasts.
  • Moseley Cottage Inn and Town Motel: This historic inn offers cozy rooms and is just a short walk from downtown Bar Harbor.

Hostels

  • The Atlantic Eyrie Lodge: This hostel offers affordable rooms, stunning views of Frenchman’s Bay, and easy access to Acadia National Park.
  • Bar Harbor Grand Hotel: This hotel offers budget-friendly accommodations, comfortable rooms, and an on-site bar and grill restaurant.

What to Buy in Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Highlands is known for its handmade crafts, local produce, and artisanal goods. Some of the best souvenirs to buy in Highlands include:

  • Blueberry jams and syrups: made from the fresh, plump berries grown in Maine.
  • Pottery and handcrafted items: Highlands has a thriving community of artists and craftsmen, creating unique handmade items that make great souvenirs.
  • Lobster-themed merchandise: From t-shirts to mugs, there is no shortage of lobster-themed souvenirs in Highlands.

Local Culture and Customs of Highlands

Highlands has a vibrant community that values its history, culture, and traditions. Visitors are expected to be respectful of the local customs and etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Highlands

Highlands is a year-round destination, with each season offering its unique charm. The best time to visit Highlands is between May and October when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Highlands

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Observe designated hiking and biking trails.
  • Attend local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Highlands

Highlands is a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions.

Health in Highlands

Medical facilities are readily available in Highlands. Visitors should carry health insurance and take necessary precautions against tick-borne diseases.

Getting to Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Highlands is easily accessible by air, road, and bus.

Airports Near Highlands

  • Bangor International Airport: A 50-minute drive from Highlands.
  • Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport : A 15-minute drive from Highlands.

Bus Stations Near Highlands

  • Concord Coach Lines
  • Greyhound

Other Options

  • Car rentals
  • Private taxis and shuttles

How to Get Around in Highlands

Highlands is a small town that is best explored on foot, bike, or car. Car rentals are available in downtown Bar Harbor.

  • White Mountains: A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts in New England, known for its hiking trails, ski resorts, and scenic routes.

Conclusion

Highlands, Maine, is a haven for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and those looking for a relaxing vacation. Its picturesque landscapes, vibrant culture, and friendly locals make it a must-visit destination in New England. Take some time to explore the region’s outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and unique culture and create memories that will last a lifetime.
