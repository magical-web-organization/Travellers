Orono, located in the Highlands of Maine, is a charming town in the heart of New England. It’s known for its stunning natural vistas, rich history, and welcoming locals. If you’re planning a trip to Orono, this comprehensive guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs to know before you go.

Introduction to Orono

Orono is a small town situated on the banks of the Penobscot River in central Maine. It is a quaint and charming town with a rich history and beautiful natural surroundings. The town is home to the University of Maine, which gives it a youthful and vibrant atmosphere. Visitors to Orono will love exploring the town’s many parks, museums, and historic landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Orono

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Orono has a rich history and many important landmarks and museums that reflect its past. Some of the must-visit museums and attractions in Orono include:

  • The Hudson Museum at the University of Maine: This museum features exhibits on the history and culture of Indigenous peoples, including art, artifacts, and multimedia displays.
  • Orono Historical Society and Museum: This museum showcases the history of Orono, Maine, and includes displays on the town’s early settlers, industrial history, and more.
  • Black Bear Brewing Company: Take a tour of this microbrewery that produces some of the region’s best beer.
  • The Maine Forest and Logging Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the importance of the forestry industry in Maine.

Natural Attractions of Orono: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Orono is known for its natural beauty and stunning views. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or just want to enjoy the scenery, there’s something for everyone in Orono. The top natural attractions in Orono include:

  • Orono Bog Boardwalk: This 1-mile-long boardwalk takes visitors through a pristine peat bog and offers amazing views of unique plant and animal life.
  • Penobscot River: Experience the beauty of this majestic river by taking a boat tour, kayaking, or fishing.
  • Hirundo Wildlife Refuge: A perfect location for birdwatchers and nature lovers to explore.
  • Acadia National Park: A one-hour drive from Orono, Acadia National Park is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts visiting Maine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orono

Orono has a variety of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • University of Maine Trail System: A network of trails that crisscross the University of Maine campus and surrounding areas, offering stunning views of the Penobscot River.
  • Orono Bog Boardwalk: A 1-mile looped trail, which provides a unique perspective on the Bog and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Orono: Activities and Tours

Orono offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy, including:

  • Guided kayaking tours of the Penobscot River.
  • Fishing trips in areas surrounding Orono.
  • Guided tours of the University of Maine campus.
  • Brewery Tours at the Black Bear Brewing Company
  • Shopping local artisan products and visiting local farmer’s markets

Week-Long Itinerary for Orono

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Orono, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Hudson Museum and grab lunch at one of the downtown restaurants.
  • Day 2: Explore the University of Maine trails and take a tour of the campus.
  • Day 3: Take a guided kayaking tour of the Penobscot River and enjoy lunch at a nearby restaurant.
  • Day 4: Visit the Maine Forest and Logging Museum and then take a scenic drive to Acadia National Park.
  • Day 5: Go fishing or take a shopping trip at the local farmer’s markets
  • Day 6: Explore Orono Bog Boardwalk and Wildlife Refuge, then enjoy dinner at one of Orono’s local restaurants.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind by the river or visit the Black Bear Brewing Company for a tour of this microbrewery.

Food and Drink Scene of Orono

If you’re a foodie or a beer lover, you’ll love Orono’s food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in the area:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Maine Lobster: There’s nothing like fresh Maine lobster.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is known for its blueberries, and you can’t leave without trying a slice of blueberry pie.
  • Maple Syrup: Maine is one of the biggest maple syrup producers in the U.S.
  • Whoopie Pies: A New England staple, these chocolate cake sandwiches with cream filling.
  • Fresh seafood: Maine’s coast provides some of the freshest seafood available.

Local Beverages:

  • Coffee at Bagel Central: Enjoy a cup of coffee at this cozy coffee shop in downtown Orono.
  • Beer at the Black Bear Brewing Company: Take a tour of this microbrewery that produces some of the region’s best beer.
  • Maine craft beer: Maine is known for its craft beer scene, with Breweries like Orono Brewing Company, Foundation, and Maine Beer Company at the helm.
  • Local ciders and spirits: Maine is also home to a growing cider scene, with producers such as Maine Mead Works and Portland Cider Co.

Where to Stay in Orono: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Orono offers a variety of accommodations for tourists, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • Black Bear Inn & Conference Center: This hotel offers comfortable lodging in the heart of Orono.
  • Orono Commons: This affordable guesthouse is a great option for budget travelers.
  • The University Inn: Located in the heart of the University of Maine campus and offering spectacular views of the Orono landscape.

What to Buy in Orono: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some suggestions:

  • Local artisan crafts such as pottery and textiles.
  • Specialty jams and maple syrups from local producers.
  • Maine-made blueberry products, such as muffin mixes and jams.

Local Culture and Customs of Orono

In Orono, locals are friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of community. Visitors should observe the usual customs of being polite and respectful. Personal space is highly valued and it is not customary to engage in conversation with complete strangers.

Best Time to Visit Orono

The best time to visit Orono is during the fall months (September-November) when the changing foliage is at its peak. The winters are perfect for those looking to engage in winter sports, such as cross-country skiing. Maine’s summers offer not only outdoor activities, but also a variety of festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orono

Do:

  • Travel responsibly: Be respectful of natural areas and always follow the Leave No Trace principles adopted by Orono.
  • Dress for the weather: Maine’s climate is unpredictable so Always check weather conditions beforehand and plan accordingly.
  • Be polite and courteous: Mainers are friendly and welcoming people, so follow suit and be polite.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute natural areas: Maine is known for its pristine natural beauty, so be sure to keep it that way.
  • Be disrespectful: Treat others as you would like to be treated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orono:

Orono is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise the usual precautions when travelling in uncharted areas and not leave valuables unattended.

Health in Orono:

Visitors should prepare for the weather and dress suitably. Protection from insect bites (particularly ticks) and sun exposure is encouraged.

Getting to Orono: Transportation Options and Logistics

Orono is located approximately 10 miles northeast of the city of Bangor, Maine, and getting there is most easily done by car.

Airports Near Orono

  • Bangor International Airport (BGR): Located just 10 miles west of Orono.

Bus Stations Near Orono

  • Concord Coach Lines: Offers daily service to and from Orono.

Other Options

  • Driving by car: This is the most commonly used means of transportation to get to Orono.

How to Get Around in Orono

Many of the town’s attractions are located within walking or biking distance, so travelers can easily explore on foot.

Where to Go Next from Orono: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For travelers who have extra time to explore, some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Acadia National Park: This large National Park is around 1.5 hours from Orono.
  • Bar Harbor: Bar Harbor is another popular tourist destination located close to Acadia National Park.
  • Portland: At around a two hour drive, Portland is a vibrant city with history, culture, and amazing food

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a quaint and charming town to escape the troubles of everyday life, look no further than Orono. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and friendly locals, this is a destination sure to capture the hearts of travelers of all ages. There is an abundance of activities, restaurants, and accommodations available that will make your trip to Orono unforgettable.
