Introduction to Greenville

Nestled in the heart of Maine’s North Woods, Greenville is a small town known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and outdoor adventures. With Moosehead Lake as its centerpiece, Greenville offers visitors miles of pristine forests, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking mountain vistas. This sleepy town may be small, but it boasts a variety of attractions and activities that make it a popular destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Greenville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moosehead Marine Museum: Located on the shores of Moosehead Lake, the Moosehead Marine Museum houses a collection of historic boats and exhibits that showcase the region’s maritime history.
  • Moosehead Historical Society & Museums: This museum complex includes several historic buildings, including an 1830s-era schoolhouse, a blacksmith shop, and a museum dedicated to the history of the region.
  • Shirley Historical Society Museum: The Shirley Historical Society Museum is housed in a former church and features exhibits on the town’s history, including maps, artifacts, and photographs.

Natural Attractions of Greenville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moosehead Lake: One of Maine’s largest lakes, Moosehead Lake offers visitors miles of unspoiled shoreline and crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming, fishing, boating, and kayaking.
  • Mount Kineo: Located on a peninsula in Moosehead Lake, Mount Kineo is a popular day hike for visitors to Greenville. The five-mile trail to the summit offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding forest.
  • Gulf Hagas: Known as the “Grand Canyon of Maine,” Gulf Hagas is a breathtaking gorge that offers visitors miles of hiking trails, waterfalls, and dramatic landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenville

  • Little Moose Mountain Trail: This seven-mile trail takes hikers through dense forest and offers stunning views of Moosehead Lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Big Moose Mountain Trail: This eight-mile trail leads hikers to the summit of Big Moose Mountain, where they can take in panoramic views of Moosehead Lake and the surrounding forest.
  • Indian Hill Trail: A three-mile loop trail that takes hikers to the top of Indian Hill for panoramic views of Moosehead Lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Greenville: Activities and Tours

  • Moose Safaris: Guided moose safaris are a popular activity in Greenville, with numerous tour operators offering visitors the chance to spot these majestic animals in their natural habitat.
  • Canoe Trips: Canoe trips on Moosehead Lake offer visitors the chance to explore the lake’s pristine shoreline and remote islands.
  • Scenic Flights: Scenic flights over Moosehead Lake and the surrounding forest offer visitors a bird’s-eye view of one of Maine’s most stunning landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Lobster: Maine is known for its delicious lobster, and Greenville is no exception. Try it steamed or in a lobster roll.
  • Whoopie Pies: These delicious chocolate cakes filled with creamy frosting are a favorite dessert in Maine.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is the blueberry capital of the world, and visitors to Greenville should not miss the chance to savor a slice of fresh blueberry pie.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Craft Beers: Maine is home to numerous craft breweries, many of which can be found near Greenville. Try a local IPA or porter.
  • Wild Blueberry Wine: Made from Maine’s famous blueberries, wild blueberry wine is a unique and delicious local beverage.

Where to Stay in Greenville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Blair Hill Inn: This historic inn offers luxurious and elegant accommodations with stunning views of Moosehead Lake.
  • The Lodge at Moosehead Lake: This rustic lodge is located on Moosehead Lake and offers guests a variety of cozy rooms and suites.
  • Greenville Inn at Moosehead Lake: This charming inn is located in the heart of Greenville and features comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Greenville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Moosehead Lake Souvenirs: Take home a souvenir of your trip to Moosehead Lake, including t-shirts, mugs, and other keepsakes.
  • Local Crafts: Greenville is home to numerous artisans and craftsmen who create unique and beautiful products, including pottery, jewelry, and woodworking.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenville

Greenville has a rich history and a strong connection to the natural world. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s natural resources and cultural heritage, and follow local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Greenville

The best time to visit Greenville is from early June to late September, when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing colors. Moose watching is especially good during this time, as the moose are more visible during the cooler months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenville

Do:

  • Respect the natural resources and cultural heritage of the town
  • Try the local food and drink scene
  • Take a guided moose safari

Don’t:

  • Damage natural resources or remove artifacts
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Approach moose or other wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenville

Greenville is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves and their property. Be sure to lock car doors and secure valuables when parking or leaving your vehicle. Visitors should also be cautious when hiking or exploring the wilderness, as there are potential dangers such as wildlife and hazardous terrain.

Health in Greenville

Greenville has several medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics, but visitors should bring any necessary prescription medication with them. It is also a good idea to bring insect repellent and sunscreen to protect against ticks and the sun’s harmful rays.

Getting to Greenville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greenville

  • Bangor International Airport (BGR): Located 91 miles from Greenville, Bangor International Airport offers flights to several major cities in the United States.
  • Portland International Jetport (PWM): Located 207 miles from Greenville, Portland International Jetport offers flights to several major cities in the United States and Canada.

Bus Stations Near Greenville

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound operates daily bus service to Bangor, which is approximately 91 miles from Greenville.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Several car rental companies, such as Budget and Hertz, operate in Bangor and Portland and offer rentals for the duration of visitors’ stays.

How to Get Around in Greenville

Visitors to Greenville will need a car to get around the area, as public transportation options are limited. Hiking and biking trails are available for those who prefer to explore on foot or by bike. Guided tours are also available for those who prefer a more structured experience.

Where to Go Next from Greenville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Baxter State Park: This stunning wilderness area is located approximately 100 miles from Greenville and offers visitors miles of hiking trails and breathtaking views.
  • Acadia National Park: This iconic national park is located approximately 170 miles from Greenville and offers visitors stunning coastal vistas, hiking trails, and numerous other attractions.

Conclusion

Greenville, Maine is a hidden gem that combines natural beauty, rich history, and outdoor adventure. With numerous attractions and activities, visitors are sure to have a memorable trip to this beautiful town. Follow the local customs and traditions, respect the natural resources and cultural heritage, and enjoy the local food and drink scene for an unforgettable trip to Greenville.
