Introduction to Bangor

Located in the Highlands of Maine, Bangor offers a great combination of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Situated on the Penobscot River, it is the third-largest city in the state and a popular destination for tourists visiting Maine. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will be exploring the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and much more in Bangor, Maine.

List of Places to Visit in Bangor

There’s no shortage of places to visit in Bangor, whether you’re interested in history, art, or outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions - Museums and Historical Places

  • Bangor Museum and Center for History: This museum is dedicated to preserving and interpreting the history of Bangor and the surrounding area. It features interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs that tell the story of the city’s growth and development over the years.

  • Cole Land Transportation Museum: This museum showcases the history of land transportation in Maine, with exhibits ranging from vintage cars and trucks to horse-drawn carriages and military vehicles.

  • Stephen King’s House: Fans of the famous horror writer will want to visit his imposing Victorian mansion in nearby Bangor. Although the house is not open to the public, fans can take photos from the street.

Natural Attractions of Bangor - Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Acadia National Park: Just a short drive from Bangor, Acadia National Park is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers miles of hiking trails, scenic drives, and breathtaking views of the Maine coast.

  • Penobscot River: The largest river in Maine, the Penobscot River is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and birdwatching. You can also take a scenic drive over the Penobscot Narrows Bridge for amazing views of the river and the surrounding countryside.

  • Bangor City Forest: This 650-acre park is located within the city limits and features miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as picnic areas and a scenic overlook.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bangor

Bangor is a great place to explore on foot or by bike, with many beautiful trails and scenic routes to choose from. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Bangor City Forest Trails: With over 10 miles of trails through woodlands and wetlands, this is a great option for hikers and mountain bikers looking to explore the natural beauty of Bangor.

  • Orrington Bog Walk: A 1-mile boardwalk trail through a wetland habitat provides visitors with a unique way to experience nature.

  • Bangor and Aroostook Trail: This multi-use recreational trail spans 61 miles from Houlton to Presque Isle, with a section passing through the heart of Bangor.

Best Things to Do in Bangor - Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Bangor, whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Paul Bunyan Statue: No trip to Bangor is complete without visiting the 31-foot-tall statue of legendary lumberjack Paul Bunyan downtown.

  • Hollywood Casino: For a fun evening out, head to the Hollywood Casino in nearby Bangor. The casino has over 1,000 slot machines and table games, as well as live entertainment and restaurants.

  • Bangor Brews Cruise: Beer lovers will enjoy this guided tour of the best breweries and taprooms in Bangor.

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Bangor:

Food and Drink Scene of Bangor

Bangor’s food and drink scene is growing and vibrant, catering to both locals and visitors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Maine is famous for its lobster, and Bangor has no shortage of excellent lobster rolls. We recommend trying one at the famous Fisherman’s Catch restaurant.

  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is also known for its blueberries, and you can find them in everything from pancakes to ice cream. A slice of blueberry pie from Frank’s Bakery is a must-try.

  • Potato Donut: These delicious donuts are a Maine tradition and a great way to start your day. Try one from Bagel Central.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: This Portland-based brewery is known for its award-winning craft beers. Check out the tasting room in Bangor for a sample.

  • Maine Mead Works: For something a little different, try some mead at this Bangor-based meadery. They offer tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Bangor - Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bangor offers a variety of accommodations to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hollywood Casino Hotel & Raceway: This hotel is connected to the Hollywood Casino and offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a restaurant and bar.

  • The Charles Inn: Located in downtown Bangor, this boutique hotel offers stylish rooms and suites in a historic setting.

  • University Inn Academic Suites: This hotel is located near the University of Maine and offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a restaurant and outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Bangor - Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products to bring home, there’s no shortage of options in Bangor. Here are some of the best:

  • Maine-made Maple Syrup: Maine is known for its maple syrup, and you can find plenty of it in Bangor. Try some from the nearby Cedar Swamp Farm.

  • Wild Blueberry Jam: Delicious and healthy, wild blueberry jam from Maine is a must-try. Get some at the Bangor Farmers Market.

  • Stephen King Memorabilia: Fans of the famous horror writer will want to check out the merchandises available at the nearby Stephen King’s House.

Local Culture and Customs of Bangor

Bangor has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Native American, French, and English influences. The city is also known for its strong tradition of hospitality and friendliness towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Bangor

The best time to visit Bangor is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the vibrant colors of the changing leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bangor

Do:

  • Do visit the local museums and historical sites to learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

  • Do make time for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and kayaking.

  • Do try the local cuisine, including lobster rolls, blueberry pie, and potato donuts.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the local environment.

  • Don’t forget to tip your servers and bartenders.

  • Don’t forget to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bangor

Bangor is generally a safe city for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to take general precautions when traveling, such as keeping your valuables secure and avoiding unlit or isolated areas at night.

Health in Bangor

Bangor has several excellent medical facilities, including Eastern Maine Medical Center and St. Joseph Hospital. If you require emergency medical attention, call 911.

Getting to Bangor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bangor

Bangor International Airport is the main airport serving the city, with flights from major airlines including Delta, American, and United.

Bus Stations Near Bangor

The Concord Coach Lines and Greyhound offer bus services in Bangor. Bangor bus terminal is located in the heart of downtown Bangor.

Other Options

Bangor is easily accessible by car via I-95, with several rental car companies located at Bangor International Airport and downtown.

How to Get Around in Bangor

The easiest way to get around Bangor is by car, as the city is spread out. However, there are also taxi services and a bus network operated by Community Connector.

Where to Go Next from Bangor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from when visiting Bangor. Here are some options:

  • Bar Harbor, Maine: Just an hour and a half drive from Bangor, Bar Harbor is a popular destination for vacationers looking to explore Acadia National Park and the surrounding area.

  • Portland, Maine: Head south from Bangor to reach Portland, a charming city known for its historic districts, excellent restaurants, and picturesque harbor.

  • Baxter State Park: This massive wilderness park is located a few hours north of Bangor and offers some of the most spectacular scenery in Maine.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, Bangor has something for everyone. With its rich heritage and natural beauty, it’s a must-visit destination for tourists exploring Maine.
