Introduction to Cumberland County

Located in southern Maine, Cumberland County covers an area of around 835 square miles and has a population of nearly 300,000 people. The county is home to several popular beach towns, including Scarborough, Cape Elizabeth, and Old Orchard Beach, as well as Maine’s largest city, Portland. Cumberland County is also renowned for its picturesque lighthouses, beautiful parks, and historic landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Cumberland County

Cumberland County has several must-visit attractions that showcase the area’s natural beauty and rich heritage. Here are some of the top attractions in the county:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maine Historical Society and Museum: This museum features several exhibits that highlight the rich cultural history of the state of Maine.
  • Victoria Mansion: Located in the heart of Portland, this historic landmark is a must-visit for anyone interested in architecture and design.
  • Portland Museum of Art: This art museum features an extensive collection of American and European art.
  • Wadsworth-Longfellow House: Explore the childhood home of one of America’s most renowned poets, Henry Wadsworth Longfellow.
  • Portland Observatory: This historic tower offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Cumberland County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Casco Bay Islands: Take a ferry ride to explore the picturesque islands in Casco Bay, including Peaks Island and Great Diamond Island.
  • Mackworth Island: The Mackworth Island Loop Trail is a picturesque hiking trail that offers stunning views of the coast.
  • Bradbury Mountain State Park: This park is a popular destination for hiking and picnicking, with over 800 acres of forests, meadows, and streams.
  • Crescent Beach State Park: Relax on the sandy beach or explore the tranquil trails that traverse this beautiful state park.
  • Portland Head Light: This iconic lighthouse is one of the most photographed lighthouses in the world, and for good reason!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cumberland County

Cumberland County is home to several stunning hiking and biking routes that showcase the county’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Eastern Promenade Trail: This scenic trail offers sweeping views of Casco Bay and the city of Portland.
  • Casco Bay Trail: This 22-mile trail takes you through several picturesque towns, including Cape Elizabeth and Scarborough.
  • Bradbury Mountain State Park Trails: The park has several hiking trails that range in difficulty, including the Summit Trail, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Portland Trails: The Portland Trails network has over 70 miles of trails that traverse the city and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Cumberland County: Activities and Tours

There’s a lot to see and do in Cumberland County, from exploring historic landmarks to sampling local cuisine. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Maine Foodie Tours: Sample the best of Portland’s food scene on this culinary tour.
  • Portland Schooner Co.: Take a scenic sailing tour of Casco Bay aboard a traditional wooden schooner.
  • Portland Observatory Tour: Learn about Portland’s rich maritime history on a tour of this historic tower.
  • Allagash Brewing Company Tour: Take a tour of this popular brewery and sample some of their award-winning beer.
  • Old Port Historic Walking Tour: Explore the historic brick buildings and cobblestone streets of Portland’s Old Port district.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cumberland County

Cumberland County has so much to offer that it’s hard to fit everything into just a few days. Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you get the most out of your visit:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic landmarks and museums of Portland, including the Wadsworth-Longfellow House and Portland Museum of Art.
  • Day 2: Take a scenic tour of Casco Bay and visit several of the picturesque islands, like Peaks Island and Great Diamond Island.
  • Day 3: Hike the trails of Bradbury Mountain State Park, followed by a visit to Allagash Brewing Company for a beer tasting.
  • Day 4: Visit Cape Elizabeth to see the iconic Portland Head Light and explore the nearby beaches.
  • Day 5: Take a culinary tour of Portland’s vibrant food scene or explore the local farmer’s markets.
  • Day 6: Go on a scenic bike tour of the Eastern Promenade Trail followed by a visit to the Victoria Mansion.
  • Day 7: Relax on the sandy beaches of Old Orchard Beach or take a whale watching tour from the nearby port.

Food and Drink Scene of Cumberland County

Cumberland County has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the area’s rich culinary traditions. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: This classic Maine dish features fresh lobster meat served on a toasted bun with mayonnaise and lemon.
  • Clam Chowder: This creamy soup is a classic New England dish made with clams, potatoes, and cream.
  • Whoopie Pies: This dessert consists of two chocolate cake rounds sandwiched together with marshmallow filling.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Craft Beer: Cumberland County is home to several popular breweries, including Allagash Brewing Company, Bissell Brothers, and Rising Tide.
  • Allen’s Coffee Brandy: This is Maine’s official state drink and a popular mixer in cocktails.
  • Moxie: This iconic soft drink has a unique flavor that is beloved by locals.

What to Buy in Cumberland County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cumberland County is home to several unique shops and boutiques that offer locally made products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best places to shop in Cumberland County:

Local Culture and Customs of Cumberland County

Maine’s culture is influenced by its coastal location, Yankee ingenuity, and proud literary tradition. Maine is known for its hospitality, and locals are generally welcoming and laid-back. Some local customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Tipping: In Maine, it is customary to tip servers 15-20% at restaurants and bartenders $1 per drink.
  • Lobster Boils: Lobster boils are a popular Maine tradition and often comprise a backyard gathering and a spread of lobster, corn, and potatoes.
  • Outdoor Activities: With its abundance of natural beauty, Cumberland County is a great place to be outdoors, and many locals enjoy hiking, kayaking, and camping.

Best Time to Visit Cumberland County

The best time to visit Cumberland County is typically from May to October, when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are at their peak. September and October are great months to visit as the fall foliage is breathtaking, making it a photographer’s paradise. Winter can be cold and snowy, but the warmth and charm of the local community make it worth a visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cumberland County

To make the most of your visit to Cumberland County, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the local food scene and try as many local dishes as possible.
  • Visit the local markets to sample fresh, locally made foods and products.
  • Hike or bike some of the county’s scenic trails.
  • Visit local landmarks and museums to learn about the rich cultural history of the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind when exploring the outdoors.
  • Disturb wildlife or natural habitats while exploring the outdoors.
  • Forget to tip your servers and bartenders at restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Cumberland County is generally a safe and healthy destination, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when exploring new places. Here are some safety and health tips for Cumberland County:

Safety in Cumberland County

  • Lock your car door when exploring urban areas and safeguard your valuables.
  • Be cautious when exploring nature, watch your step, and avoid climbing over railings or retaining walls.
  • Wear a life vest when boating or kayaking.

Health in Cumberland County

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring outdoors.
  • Check for ticks when hiking and camping in the forested areas.
  • Be aware of food allergies and dietary restrictions when sampling local cuisine.

Getting to Cumberland County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cumberland County is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus. Here are some transportation options for getting to Cumberland County:

Airports Near Cumberland County

  • Portland International Jetport is the primary airport serving Cumberland County and is located in Portland.

Bus Stations Near Cumberland County

  • Portland Transportation Center is the primary bus station serving the county.

Other Options

  • Several major highways and interstates run through the county, making it accessible by car from other parts of Maine and neighboring states.

How to Get Around in Cumberland County

Cumberland County is best explored by car, but there are several other transportation options available. Here are the best ways to get around Cumberland County:

  • Car rental: Several major car rental companies operate out of Portland International Jetport.
  • Taxi and ride-sharing: Lyft and Uber are available in Portland and several other towns in the county.
  • Bicycle: The Portland Trails network has over 70 miles of off-road trails that are perfect for biking.
  • Public transportation: Several bus lines operate in and around Portland and throughout the county.

Where to Go Next from Cumberland County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cumberland County is a great home base for exploring other parts of Maine. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Kennebunkport: This charming coastal town is located about 30 miles south of Portland and features picturesque beaches, historic landmarks, and top-notch seafood.
  • Acadia National Park: Located about three hours from Portland, this stunning national park offers incredible hiking trails, scenic drives, and breathtaking vistas.
  • Boothbay Harbor: This picturesque coastal town is located about an hour and a half from Portland and features charming boutiques, cafes, and galleries.

Conclusion

Cumberland County is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the best of Maine’s natural and cultural attractions.
