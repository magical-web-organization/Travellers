Introduction to Androscoggin County

Tucked in the Lakes and Mountains region of Maine, USA, Androscoggin County is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Named after the Androscoggin River that flows through the county, this area is rich in history, culture, and scenic beauty. Visitors who venture to this county can enjoy exploring historic towns, hiking through pristine wilderness, and savoring delicious local cuisine.

In this comprehensive guide, we have compiled everything you need to know to plan your trip to Androscoggin County – from the best attractions to visit, to must-try dishes, to a week-long itinerary. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!

List of Places to Visit in Androscoggin County

Androscoggin County is home to a plethora of attractions, from cultural landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bates College Museum of Art: Located in Lewiston, this museum boasts an impressive collection of contemporary art and rotating exhibitions.

  • Franco-American Heritage Center: Housed in a historic church, this Lewiston center is dedicated to preserving and promoting the history and culture of the Franco-American community.

  • Auburn Riverwalk: Take a stroll along the Androscoggin River on this scenic path that passes by historic buildings and landmarks.

Natural Attractions of Androscoggin County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Range Ponds State Park: Hiking, swimming, picnicking, and fishing are popular activities in this state park, which offers picturesque views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Thorncrag Bird Sanctuary: Home to more than 130 bird species, this Lewiston sanctuary is ideal for birdwatching and hiking.

  • Androscoggin Riverlands State Park: This park offers opportunities for hiking, canoeing, fishing, and wildlife watching on more than 2,700 acres of wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Androscoggin County

Androscoggin County is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Mt. Apatite Trail: This 6-mile trail in Auburn features a scenic quarry, a historic granite quarry operation, and stunning vistas.

  • Bradbury Mountain State Park: This park boasts numerous hiking and biking trails of varying difficulty levels, as well as breathtaking views of the surrounding hillsides.

  • Poland Spring Preservation Park: Located in Poland, this park offers trails that wind through tranquil forests and past historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Androscoggin County: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other fun things to do in Androscoggin County. Here are some must-try activities:

  • Discover local breweries: Androscoggin County is home to several craft breweries, including Baxter Brewing Co. in Lewiston and Side By Each Brewing in Auburn. Take a tour and taste some of the region’s best beers.

  • Go on a fishing trip: With several rivers and ponds in the county, Androscoggin County is a prime destination for fishing enthusiasts.

  • Explore historic homes and buildings: Lewiston and Auburn are home to many beautifully preserved historic buildings and homes, including the Governor’s Mansion and the Auburn City Hall.

Week-Long Itinerary for Androscoggin County

For those planning a longer stay, we recommend this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore downtown Lewiston, including the Bates College Museum of Art and the Franco-American Heritage Center.

  • Day 2: Visit Range Ponds State Park and take a swim or hike around the park.

  • Day 3: Go on a tour of the Baxter Brewing Co. followed by a stroll along the Auburn Riverwalk.

  • Day 4: Visit Thorncrag Bird Sanctuary and go on a rare bird sighting.

  • Day 5: Spend a day at the Androscoggin Riverlands State Park and enjoy scenic views.

  • Day 6: Visit the Governor’s Mansion in Augusta, head back to Auburn and have dinner in one of the local food spots.

  • Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products to bring home, and explore the historic downtown area of Auburn and Lewiston.

Food and Drink Scene of Androscoggin County

No trip to Androscoggin County is complete without indulging in the exceptional local cuisine and craft drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic Maine dish, a simple yet delicious sandwich with fresh lobster meat, served with butter on a hot dog bun.

  • Poutine: A dish made of crispy fries, cheese curds, and gravy, a specialty of Franco-American cuisine in the area.

  • Wild Maine Blueberry Pie: A dessert made from fresh blueberries, sugar, and a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee By Design: A locally-owned coffee shop, serving some of the best coffee blends in the county.

  • Bear Bones Beer: This Lewiston brewery offers a wide range of handcrafted beers and ciders inspired by local flavors.

  • Mariner Beverages: A one-stop-shop for all your beverage needs, from craft beers to fine wines.

Where to Stay in Androscoggin County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Androscoggin County offers a range of accommodation options for every budget and travel style. Here are some top recommended places to stay:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Auburn Riverwatch: Located in Auburn, this hotel offers scenic views of the Androscoggin River, a pool and a hot tub, and a fitness center.

  • Poland Spring Resort: This historic resort in Poland offers access to golf courses, tennis courts, and plenty of outdoor activities.

  • The Wolf Cove Inn: Located in Poland, this cozy bed and breakfast offers lakeshore rooms, fireplaces, and homemade breakfast served every morning.

What to Buy in Androscoggin County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Androscoggin County home with you by picking up some of these locally-made souvenirs:

  • Wild Maine Blueberries: Buy fresh and preserved blueberries, which are unique to Maine and are believed to have many health benefits.

  • Maple Syrup: Not exclusive to the county but available in plenty, Maine’s famous maple syrup is a classic souvenir.

  • Locally-made Crafts: Visit some local craft shops, and you will find locally-made candles, pottery, and other handcrafted items - perfect for unique gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Androscoggin County

The area has a rich history, influenced by the Abenaki Native American tribe, early French connection, and English settlements. Although the area has changed over time, it still celebrates and remembers both its historic and cultural roots. The people have long-standing traditions, religious beliefs, and customs influenced by both French and New England cultures, making it a unique area to visit.

Best Time to Visit Androscoggin County

Androscoggin County is a year-round destination, although summer months are the busiest and tend to bring higher prices. Spring and Fall can be a good time to visit with fewer crowds, beautiful foliage, and mild temperatures. The winters can be harsh in the area, but it offers the opportunity for winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Androscoggin County

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling to Androscoggin County:

Do:

  • Do hike and explore the area’s natural beauty.

  • Do bring comfortable shoes and clothes, as there are many opportunities for outdoor activities.

  • Do support the local businesses and farmer’s markets.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural surroundings.

  • Don’t cut or remove live vegetation.

  • Don’t trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Androscoggin County is a safe area to visit, but it’s essential to follow general precautions while traveling. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Androscoggin County

  • Stay within open, well-lit areas.
  • Secure valuables in lockboxes in hotels or secure them in your car.
  • Bring identification documents and emergency contact numbers.
  • Be prepared with navigation tools to avoid getting lost.

Health in Androscoggin County

  • Stay hydrated and bring plenty of water while engaging in physical activities.
  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and other medical necessities.

Getting to Androscoggin County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Androscoggin does not have a dedicated airport, but several nearby airports can be utilized as connection points. Here are the possible transportation options:

Airports Near Androscoggin County

  • Portland International Jetport: Located about 45 miles from Androscoggin, it is the nearest airport available.

  • Bangor International Airport: Located about 100 miles from Androscoggin, it is a regional airport for Maine.

Bus Stations Near Androscoggin County

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal in Lewiston: Go on the Greyhound website, make a reservation, and purchase tickets for Maine travels.

Other Options

-Rent a Car: Multiple car rental options are available at the airports, making it possible to drive to Androscoggin County.

How to Get Around in Androscoggin County

The area is best experienced by car, and renting one is the best option to get around in Androscoggin County if you plan to explore. Public transportation like buses is available in Auburn and Lewiston during the daytime.

Where to Go Next from Androscoggin County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Maine offers numerous experiences for tourists, so you might want to extend your visit to explore the local regions. Here are some nearby destinations:

  • Brunswick: Located about 45 miles from Androscoggin County, it is a charming coastal town home to Bowdoin College and historic Fort Andross.

  • Portland: Located about 45 miles from Androscoggin County, it is Maine’s largest city, offering excellent food, culture, and sightseeing options.
