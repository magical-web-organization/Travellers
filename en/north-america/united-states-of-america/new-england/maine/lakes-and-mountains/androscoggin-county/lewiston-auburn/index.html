Introduction to Lewiston–Auburn

Located in Androscoggin County, in the heart of Maine’s Lakes and Mountains region, Lewiston–Auburn is a charming, vibrant city that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and modern amenities. The area is home to over 100,000 people and has a diverse culture that includes a mix of French-Canadian, African-American, and Irish-American heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Lewiston–Auburn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bates College Museum of Art: A contemporary art museum that showcases the works of local artists and also features a collection of ancient Greek art and Egyptian artifacts.
  • Franco-American Heritage Center: A cultural center and museum that highlights the history and contributions of French-Canadians in Maine.
  • Auburn Riverwalk: A beautiful trail that runs along the Androscoggin River and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Androscoggin Historical Society: A museum that provides a fascinating look into the history of the Androscoggin River valley, including exhibits on early settlers, industry, and transportation.
  • The Great Falls Balloon Festival: The annual festival in August features hot air balloons filled with color and pattern.
  • Basilica of Saints Peter and Paul: The basilica displays fine French Canadian-style stained glass, the largest in Maine, including a rare rose window with Gothic tracery.

Natural Attractions of Lewiston–Auburn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Apatite Park: A 345-acre park with stunning views of Lewiston–Auburn, hiking and biking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Thompson Lake: A stunning lake that offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and swimming in the summer, and ice fishing and skating in the winter.
  • Lost Valley Ski Area: Great trails for skiers and snowboarders. The area is home to many ski races, events, and competitions
  • Great Falls: Located on the Androscoggin River between Lewiston and Auburn, the Great Falls are a series of rushing cascades that offer some of the best views in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewiston–Auburn

  • Mount Apatite Park Trails: The trails of Mount Apatite Park offer visitors a great opportunity to hike or bike along scenic paths with breathtaking views.
  • Simard Payne Memorial Park Trails: With over 2.5 miles of trails that meander through thick forests, open fields, and rolling hills, Simard Payne Memorial Park Trails are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Lewiston–Auburn: Activities and Tours

  • Maine Beer Co. Tours: Learn about the brewing process and sample some of the finest beer in Maine during a tour of the Maine Beer Co. brewery.
  • Maine Wildlife Park: The Maine Wildlife Park is a great place for families with kids. You can observe and learn about native Maine animals such as moose, black bear, bald eagles and more.
  • Pirates Cove Adventure Golf: A fun-filled miniature golf course that is great for families. The course features pirates, waterfalls, caves, and challenging holes that will delight golfers of all ages.

Food and Drink Scene of Lewiston–Auburn

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: A staple of Maine cuisine, lobster rolls are a must-try when visiting Lewiston–Auburn. You can find them at many local seafood restaurants.
  • Poutine: A beloved Quebec dish, poutine is a delicious combination of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy, and can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Baked Beans: A Maine tradition that is typically served with brown bread and hot dogs – a classic meal for any frugal family.

Local Beverages

  • Maine Beer Co.: A local brewery that offers a wide range of artisanal, high-quality beers in an environmentally responsible manner.
  • Tree Spirits: An apple-based distillery that specializes in hard ciders, spirits, and wines. Visitors can enjoy tastings and tours at the distillery.

Where to Stay in Lewiston–Auburn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Garden Inn Auburn Riverwatch: Located on the banks of the Androscoggin River, this hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding area, an indoor pool, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Hampton Inn Lewiston-Auburn: Located in downtown Lewiston, this hotel is within walking distance of many attractions, including the Androscoggin Historical Society Museum and the Franco-American Heritage Center.
  • Poland Spring Resort: Located just a short drive from Lewiston–Auburn, this resort offers a wide range of amenities, including a golf course, spa, and outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Lewiston–Auburn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: A classic Maine souvenir, maple syrup can be found at many local shops and farmers markets.
  • Local Art: The Bates College Museum of Art is a great place to find original works by local Maine artists.
  • Blueberry Jam: Maine is well-known for its blueberries, and one of the best ways to enjoy them is in the form of delicious blueberry jam.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewiston–Auburn

Lewiston–Auburn is proud of its Franco-American heritage, and visitors can learn more about local culture and customs by attending events such as the La Kermesse Franco-Americaine, an annual festival that celebrates French-Canadian culture with music, dance, and food.

Best Time to Visit Lewiston–Auburn

The best time to visit Lewiston–Auburn is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are abundant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewiston–Auburn

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine: Maine is known for its seafood and unique local dishes like lobster rolls and blueberry jam.
  • Do visit local museums and attractions: Lewiston–Auburn has a rich history and culture.
  • Do bring layers: The weather in Maine can be unpredictable, so bring warm and cool clothing options.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Help keep this beautiful area clean and litter-free.
  • Don’t follow GPS blindly: Some GPS routes may lead you down unpaved roads or remote highways, so use a paper map to double-check directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewiston–Auburn

Lewiston–Auburn is a safe place for visitors. However, visitors should take routine precautions such as locking their car doors, watching their belongings and be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Lewiston–Auburn

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before travelling to Lewiston–Auburn and carry their health insurance information with them while travelling.

Getting to Lewiston–Auburn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewiston–Auburn

The closest airport to Lewiston–Auburn is Portland International Jetport, located about 35 miles south.

Bus Stations Near Lewiston–Auburn

Concord Coach Lines provides daily bus service to Lewiston–Auburn from many locations throughout Maine and New Hampshire.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Lewiston–Auburn by car or train.

How to Get Around in Lewiston–Auburn

Visitors can get around Lewiston–Auburn by car, bike, or walking.

Where to Go Next from Lewiston–Auburn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland: Maine’s largest city and a cultural hub of the state. It’s located about 35 miles south of Lewiston–Auburn.
  • Freeport: A shopper’s paradise with over 170 upscale outlets and boutique stores, located about 35 miles east of Lewiston–Auburn.
  • Acadia National Park: Located about three hours north of Lewiston–Auburn, Acadia National Park offers some of the most breathtaking scenery in the state.

Conclusion

Lewiston–Auburn is a beautiful and unique destination to explore. From scenic vistas to delicious food and drink, there is something here for everyone. Come visit and experience everything this charming area has to offer.
