Introduction to Mexico

Mexico is one of the most popular tourist destinations in North America. It has a rich culture, captivating history, and stunning natural landscapes that offer visitors a unique and memorable experience. The country is known for its vibrant cuisine, colorful festivals, and friendly locals. Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie looking for adventure or a traveler seeking to immerse yourself in the local culture, Mexico has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Mexico

Mexico is a country with a deeply rooted culture and traditions. Family and religion are very important to Mexicans, and hospitality and warm welcome are a common thing among locals. Mexicans love to celebrate and organize different fiestas throughout the year, among which Christmas and Day of the Dead are the most significant ones.

Best Time to Visit Mexico

The best time to visit Mexico is between December and April, when the country experiences dry and sunny weather. However, it is worth noting that some tourist destinations in Mexico, such as Cancun, are especially busy during peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mexico

Do:

  • try Mexican street food
  • learn basic Spanish phrases
  • respect local customs
  • negotiate prices in local markets
  • carry small change for tipping

Don’t:

  • touch artwork inside churches or museums
  • wear revealing clothing in public places
  • take unauthorized taxis

Safety and Health

Safety in Mexico

Mexico has a reputation for being an unsafe destination for tourists. However, while there are areas of the country that should be avoided due to drug-cartel violence, most tourist destinations are safe to visit. It’s always wise to stay aware of your surroundings and take basic precautions.

Health in Mexico

Mexico has a good reputation for medical care, and many private hospitals and clinics offer quality care. However, it’s recommended to get travel insurance that can cover medical expenses while in Mexico.
