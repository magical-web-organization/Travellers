Introduction to Andover

Nestled in the heart of Oxford County, in the Lakes and Mountains region of Maine, you’ll find the charming town of Andover. From its gorgeous natural surroundings to its rich cultural and historical significance, Andover is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring New England.

List of Places to Visit in Andover

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural enlightenment, or simply a taste of small-town charm, Andover has something to offer everyone. Here are just a few of the top attractions you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Andover Historical Society: Learn about the town’s rich history through exhibits of artifacts and photographs at this small museum, housed in a historic church building.
  • The Richardson Hollow Schoolhouse: Take a step back in time at this impeccably preserved one-room schoolhouse, which dates back to the 19th century.
  • The East Andover Village Preserving Society: Discover the historic buildings and artifacts that tell the story of East Andover’s development from a farming community to a thriving mill town.

Natural Attractions of Andover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Appalachian Trail: Hike a portion of the world-famous AT as it winds through over 280 miles of Maine wilderness, passing through Andover.
  • Grafton Notch State Park: This stunning park features roaring waterfalls, miles of hiking trails, and stunning views of the White Mountains.
  • The Androscoggin River: Fish for trout or take out a kayak or canoe to explore the winding river that runs through Andover.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Andover

Andover is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with countless trails and routes to explore. Some top options include:

  • The Appalachian Trail
  • The Grafton Loop Trail
  • The Androscoggin River Trail

Best Things to Do in Andover: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities to keep you busy during your stay in Andover. Some top options include:

  • Kayaking or canoeing on the Androscoggin River
  • Fishing for trout or bass in Mirror Pond
  • Snowmobiling or cross-country skiing in the winter months
  • Taking a scenic drive along Route 5

Week-Long Itinerary for Andover

Day 1: Arrive and check into your accommodations. Day 2: Explore the Andover Historical Society and Richardson Hollow Schoolhouse. Day 3: Hike a portion of the Appalachian Trail or take a scenic drive on Route 5. Day 4: Spend the day fishing or canoeing on the Androscoggin River. Day 5: Explore Grafton Notch State Park. Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Bethel, Maine, for shopping and dining. Day 7: Depart Andover.

Food and Drink Scene of Andover

No trip to Andover would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are a few must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster rolls from East Andover General Store
  • Blueberry pie from Goodwin’s Mills General Store
  • Maple syrup from Hidden Valley Nature Center

Local Beverages

  • Andover Brewing Company’s craft beers
  • Freshly brewed coffee from The Little Red Hen Bakery

Where to Stay in Andover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite its small size, Andover offers a variety of lodging options to suit every traveler’s needs and budget. Some top picks include:

  • The Perennial Inn: This historic bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms with period decor and all the modern amenities you could need.
  • The Lockehaven Lodge: This charming lodge sits on the shore of beautiful Rangeley Lake and offers cozy rooms and stunning views.
  • East Andover Village Inn: This 1860s-era inn offers unique decor, homemade breakfasts, and cozy common areas for relaxing after a day of exploring.

What to Buy in Andover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to take a piece of Andover home with you? Here are some top souvenirs and local products to look for:

  • Maple syrup and other local products from the Hidden Valley Nature Center gift shop
  • Handmade pottery from local artist Sandy Black
  • Handmade jewelry and accessories from Andover’s annual Crafts and Gifts Fair

Local Culture and Customs of Andover

Andover residents are proud of their town’s rich history and natural beauty. They’re also known for their hospitality and friendliness toward visitors. To fully immerse yourself in local culture, consider attending one of Andover’s many annual events, such as the 4th of July Parade or the Crafts and Gifts Fair.

Best Time to Visit Andover

Fall is a particularly beautiful time to visit Andover, as the surrounding forests explode into vibrant shades of orange, red, and yellow. Winter is also a popular time, as the town offers a variety of snow sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Andover

Do:

  • Embrace the town’s natural beauty by spending time outdoors and participating in outdoor activities.
  • Take the time to learn about the town’s rich history and cultural significance.
  • Sample the local cuisine, particularly the seafood and maple syrup.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s natural areas or historic buildings.
  • Disrespect the town’s residents or their way of life.
  • Skip out on local events or festivals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Andover

Andover is a small, safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings while walking or hiking.

Health in Andover

While Andover doesn’t have a hospital within town limits, there are nearby medical facilities for emergencies and routine healthcare needs.

Getting to Andover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Andover

Portland International Jetport is the closest major airport to Andover, located approximately 90 miles to the southeast.

Bus Stations Near Andover

The nearest bus station to Andover is located in Bethel, approximately 15 miles to the south.

Other Options

Driving to Andover is also a popular option, as it allows visitors to take in the stunning New England scenery along the way.

How to Get Around in Andover

Walking or biking is a great option for exploring Andover’s downtown area. For longer distances or to reach nearby attractions, you may want to consider renting a car or taking a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Andover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The White Mountains of New Hampshire, located to the west of Andover
  • The historic town of Bethel, Maine, located to the south of Andover
  • The stunning Acadia National Park, located to the north of Andover

Conclusion

With its unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and small-town charm, Andover is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Lakes and Mountains region of Maine. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural enlightenment, or simply a relaxing escape, Andover offers something for everyone.
