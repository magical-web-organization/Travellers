Introduction to Roxbury

Located in the Lakes and Mountains region of Oxford County, Maine, Roxbury is a quaint rural town that offers a unique blend of rugged outdoor adventure and small-town charm. With a population of just over 400, Roxbury is perfect for those looking for a quiet retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Roxbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Roxbury Historical Society Museum – This small museum is a treasure trove of the town’s history, showcasing artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Roxbury’s past.
  2. Hiram Davis Monument – Erected in honor of Hiram Davis, a local hero who fought and died in the Civil War, this monument is an important landmark in the town.
  3. Andover Road Covered Bridge – This picturesque covered bridge is a must-visit attraction for lovers of history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Roxbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Roxbury Pond – This scenic pond is a popular spot for fishing, swimming, and boating.
  2. Ellis River Trail – This easy hiking trail winds through the heart of Roxbury, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  3. Roxbury Gap – For more experienced hikers, the Roxbury Gap offers a challenging trek with spectacular views from the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roxbury

  1. Devil’s Den Trail – This moderate-level trail winds through the forest, offering a mix of moderate and steep terrain with breathtaking views of the surrounding scenery.
  2. Swift River Trail – This easy trail follows the Swift River, offering a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of town.
  3. White-tail Deer Trail – This easy-to-moderate trail takes hikers through the woods and around Beaver Pond, offering scenic views and a chance to spot local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Roxbury: Activities and Tours

  1. Roxbury ATV Riders Club – If you’re looking for an adrenaline rush, join the Roxbury ATV Riders Club to explore miles of rugged terrain on an ATV.
  2. Sunday River Brewing Company – Take a tour of this award-winning brewery and enjoy a tasting of their delicious craft beers.
  3. Lakeside Theater – Catch a movie or live performance at this charming local theater.

Week-Long Itinerary for Roxbury

Food and Drink Scene of Roxbury

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Roll – Roxbury is located near the coast, so it’s no surprise that lobster is a local favorite. Try it in a classic lobster roll for a true Maine experience.
  2. Blueberry Pie – Maine is known for its wild blueberries, so it’s only fitting that you try a slice of homemade blueberry pie during your visit.
  3. Clam Chowder – Another classic New England favorite, clam chowder is a hearty and delicious dish that will warm you up on chilly evenings.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer – Maine is home to many excellent breweries, including Sunday River Brewing Company, which is located right in Roxbury.
  2. Apple Cider – Maine is also known for its delicious apple cider, which is perfect for sipping on a crisp fall day.
  3. Maine Blueberry wine – For those who prefer their beverages with a bit of sweetness, try a glass of Maine blueberry wine.

Where to Stay in Roxbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Roxbury Motel – This cozy motel offers comfortable rooms and excellent service, making it a great choice for those who prioritize comfort and convenience.
  2. Saddleback Motor Lodge – Located just a short drive from Roxbury, the Saddleback Motor Lodge offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  3. Private cabin rentals – For those who want a rustic experience, consider renting a private cabin in the woods. There are many options available in and around Roxbury.

What to Buy in Roxbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Blueberry Jam – Take home a jar of delicious blueberry jam, made from locally grown blueberries.
  2. Handmade Crafts – Roxbury is home to many talented artisans who create unique handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  3. Maple Syrup – Maine is known for its delicious maple syrup, and Roxbury is no exception. Bring home a bottle or two as a tasty souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Roxbury

Roxbury is a small, close-knit town where community is important. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and take the time to get to know their neighbors, who are always happy to welcome newcomers.

Best Time to Visit Roxbury

The best time to visit Roxbury is during the fall, when the leaves change color and the crisp air makes for the perfect hiking weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roxbury

Do:

  1. Take the time to explore the natural beauty of Roxbury.
  2. Try the local cuisine, including seafood and blueberry products.
  3. Be respectful of local customs and take the time to get to know your neighbors.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural environment.
  2. Ignore local customs or traditions.
  3. Leave without trying a slice of homemade blueberry pie!

Safety and Health

Safety in Roxbury

Roxbury is a safe town, but visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as locking their cars and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Roxbury

Visitors should bring any medications they need with them, as there are limited medical facilities in town.

Getting to Roxbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roxbury

The nearest major airport is Portland International Jetport, located about 80 miles from Roxbury.

Bus Stations Near Roxbury

Greyhound offers bus service to the nearby town of Rumford, which is about 10 miles from Roxbury.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore the area or take a taxi from the nearest city.

How to Get Around in Roxbury

The best way to get around Roxbury is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Roxbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Rangeley Lakes – This scenic region is just a short drive from Roxbury and offers a wealth of outdoor adventures, including fishing, hiking, and skiing.
  2. Bath – Known for its historic downtown and excellent seafood, Bath is a great place to spend a day or two.
  3. Acadia National Park – This must-see national park is located about 3 hours from Roxbury and offers stunning views, hiking trails, and more.

Conclusion

Roxbury, Maine is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of outdoor adventure, small-town charm, and local culture. With its natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and friendly community, Roxbury is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Maine experience. Plan your trip today and discover all that Roxbury has to offer!
