Introduction to Bethel

Nestled in Oxford County in the Lakes and Mountains of Maine, Bethel is a picturesque town offering visitors a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures. With scenic views that could rival those along the coast, Bethel is a year-round tourist destination that attracts visitors from far and wide.

In this ultimate guide to Bethel, we’ll take you through all the must-visit places, best things to do, and local customs to keep in mind while traveling to this charming town. From outdoor activities to cultural events, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Bethel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bethel’s rich history is reflected in its many cultural attractions, including museums and historical sites that serve as a window into the town’s past.

One such place is the Bethel Historical Society Museum, which houses an extensive collection of artifacts from the 18th and 19th centuries. Visitors can learn about the town’s history and view exhibits that explore topics including farming, logging, and the railroad.

Another must-visit spot is the Dr. Moses Mason House, an 1813 Federal-style mansion that’s now a museum. The house offers guided tours that highlight the history of the family who owned the house and their contributions to the town.

Natural Attractions of Bethel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There’s no shortage of outdoor activities in Bethel, with the town’s natural scenery offering the perfect backdrop for hiking, biking, and skiing.

One of the town’s most popular destinations is Grafton Notch State Park, where visitors can hike scenic trails, spot wildlife, and take in stunning views of waterfalls and mountains. The park is especially beautiful during fall, when the leaves change color and create a breathtaking patchwork of reds and oranges.

Another natural gem is Sunday River, a ski resort that offers over 135 trails for skiers and snowboarders of all levels. Aside from skiing, visitors can enjoy snowmobiling, snowshoeing, and even ziplining.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bethel

Bethel is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous trails and paths that offer stunning scenery and a chance to get close to nature. Here are some top hiking and biking routes to try:

  • Mount Will Trail: This moderate 3.3-mile hike rewards visitors with views of the town below.
  • Bethel Pathway: A paved 2.5-mile trail that’s perfect for biking and walking, offering scenic views of the Androscoggin River.
  • Step Falls Preserve: A family-friendly hike that leads to a series of waterfalls and natural pools.

Best Things to Do in Bethel: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and skiing, there are plenty of other things to do in Bethel that are worth checking out. Some highlights include:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride with Maine Balloon Rides for an unforgettable view of the town and surrounding mountains.
  • Check out Gould Academy’s Bingham Hall Performing Arts Center for a variety of cultural events, including concerts, plays, and art exhibits.
  • Visit the nearby town of Norway for their famous Oxford County Fair, held in late September every year and featuring rides, games, and classic fair food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bethel

For those planning an extended stay in Bethel, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival and orientation. Take a walk along Bethel Pathway and get to know the town center.
  • Day 2: Visit the Bethel Historical Society Museum and Dr. Moses Mason House.
  • Day 3: Spend a day at Sunday River, enjoying skiing or snowboarding.
  • Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning and spend the afternoon snowmobiling.
  • Day 5: Explore Grafton Notch State Park with a scenic hike.
  • Day 6: Binge on shopping and dining, checking out the town’s local boutiques and restaurants.
  • Day 7: Departure from Bethel.

Food and Drink Scene of Bethel

Must-Try Dishes

After a long day of outdoor adventures, treat yourself to some of Bethel’s delicious local cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England dish that’s a staple on many of the town’s menus.
  • Wild Maine Blueberry Pancakes: Made with the state’s famous blueberries, these pancakes are a breakfast favorite.
  • Maine Whoopie Pie: A sweet treat made of two rounds of chocolate cake with sweet cream filling.

Local Beverages

Bethel’s local breweries and wineries are worth checking out for those looking for a drink. Some popular options include:

  • Sunday River Brewing Company: This brewery offers a variety of seasonal beers and an extensive food menu.
  • Maine Distilleries: Try their Alchemy Gin or Blueshine Blueberry Moonshine for a taste of local flavors.
  • Root Cellar Winery: This local winery offers tastings of their homemade wines, including a variety of fruity reds and whites.

Where to Stay in Bethel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bethel offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxury resorts. Here are some top picks:

  • The Chapman Inn: A historic bed and breakfast located in the heart of the town, offering Victorian-style rooms and a communal hot tub.
  • The Bethel Inn Resort: This luxury resort offers a golf course, spa services, and fine dining options.
  • The Holidae House Bed & Breakfast: A cozy spot with wood-burning fireplaces and an outdoor hot tub.

What to Buy in Bethel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking to take home a piece of Bethel, there are plenty of local products available for purchase. Some top picks include:

  • Wild Blueberries: Maine is famous for its wild blueberries, and visitors can take home jams, spreads, and syrups made from the berries.
  • Locally Crafted Pottery: Several local potters offer handmade bowls, vases, and other pieces that are unique to the area.
  • Maine-made Maple Syrup: Bethel visitors can take home a bottle of this liquid gold, produced by nearby farms during syrup season.

Local Culture and Customs of Bethel

The town of Bethel prides itself on a friendly and welcoming atmosphere, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress in layers: The weather in Bethel can be unpredictable, so it’s always a good idea to have layers handy.
  • Say hello: It’s customary to greet people with a friendly smile and hello.
  • Leave your worries at home: Bethel is all about relaxation, so take some time to slow down and enjoy the town’s slower pace.

Best Time to Visit Bethel

The best time to visit Bethel depends on the traveler’s interests. For those who love the outdoors, summer and fall are ideal months, with plenty of hiking, biking, and water activities. Winter offers skiing and snowmobiling opportunities, while spring offers a quieter time for visitors to enjoy the town’s local culture and food scene.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bethel

Do:

  • Try the local blueberries and maple syrup.
  • Respect the town’s natural resources, such as the hiking trails and waterfalls.
  • Embrace the slower pace of life.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s natural resources.
  • Overpack, as the weather can range from hot to cold in the same day.
  • Ignore the town’s local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bethel

Bethel is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Some safety tips include:

  • Lock car doors when parked.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or walking alone.
  • Keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Bethel

Bethel is a healthy town, with plenty of outdoor activities and fresh air. Those with allergies should be aware of pollen season in the spring, and visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in weather.

Getting to Bethel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bethel

The nearest major airport to Bethel is Portland International Jetport, which is about a two-hour drive away. Another option is Bangor International Airport, which is roughly two and a half hours from the town.

Bus Stations Near Bethel

Greyhound and Concord Coach Lines run buses to Portland and Augusta, which are both within a reasonable driving distance to Bethel.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car from one of the major rental companies, or take a taxi from the airport to Bethel.

How to Get Around in Bethel

Bethel is a small town that’s easily walkable, but visitors can also rent bikes or take a taxi if they prefer. There is also a shuttle service that stops at major attractions in the area, such as Sunday River and Grafton Notch State Park.

Where to Go Next from Bethel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Maine has plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring, such as:

  • Kennebunkport: A charming coastal town with lighthouses, beaches, and plenty of seafood restaurants.
  • Portland: Maine’s largest city offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern amenities, with award-winning restaurants and beautiful waterfront views.

Conclusion

Bethel, Maine is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy nature, local culture, and outdoor adventures. With plenty of things to do and see, this charming town is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Maine.
