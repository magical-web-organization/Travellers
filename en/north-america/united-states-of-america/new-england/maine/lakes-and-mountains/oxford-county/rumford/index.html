Introduction to Rumford

Nestled in Oxford County in the beautiful region of Lakes and Mountains in the heart of Maine, Rumford is a charming town that boasts a unique blend of outdoor adventure, rich history, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or an action-packed vacation, Rumford has something for everyone.

This ultimate guide to Rumford will help you navigate the town and explore its many hidden gems, from its breathtaking natural scenery to its vibrant arts scene and delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Rumford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rumford is rich in history and culture, and it offers many opportunities to learn about the past. Some of the most notable cultural attractions in Rumford include:

  • The Maine Paper & Heritage Museum: This museum celebrates the rich history of Maine’s paper industry, which has played a significant role in the local economy for over a century.

  • The Rumford Historical Society: This organization hosts a museum filled with artifacts, documents, and photographs detailing Rumford’s unique heritage.

  • The Black Mountain of Maine Ski Museum: This museum celebrates the rich history of skiing in the region and offers exhibits on the evolution of ski technology, the people behind the sport, and more.

Natural Attractions of Rumford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rumford is also a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with breathtaking natural scenery and plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and more. Some of the must-see natural attractions in Rumford include:

  • The White Mountains: Located just a short drive away from Rumford, the White Mountains offer some of the best hiking and biking trails in New England. Don’t miss the opportunity to hike or drive up Mt. Washington, the highest peak in the Northeast.

  • Grafton Notch State Park: This park is a nature lover’s paradise, with stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and scenic vistas. Be sure to check out Screw Auger Falls and Mother Walker Falls.

  • The Androscoggin River: The Androscoggin River flows through Rumford and provides plenty of opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and other water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rumford

Rumford is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with many trails that offer stunning views of the mountains and the surrounding countryside. Here are a few of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Black Mountain Trail: This trail is a 2.5-mile loop that offers stunning views of the White Mountains. It’s suitable for hikers of all skill levels.

  • The Rumford Whitecap Mountain Trail: This 5.8-mile trail is a moderate hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

  • The Bethel Village Trails: This network of trails offers over 30 miles of hiking and biking routes through the forests and fields of Bethel.

Best Things to Do in Rumford: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of the region, there are many other fun and exciting things to do in Rumford. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Take a guided tour of the Maine Paper & Heritage Museum to learn about the history of the paper industry in Maine.

  • Go snowshoeing or cross-country skiing on one of the many trails in the area, or hit the slopes at the Black Mountain of Maine Ski Resort.

  • Take a scenic drive along Route 2, which offers stunning views of the White Mountains and the Androscoggin River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rumford

If you’re planning to spend a week in Rumford, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Rumford and take a guided tour of the Maine Paper & Heritage Museum.

  • Day 2: Take a hiking or biking trip to Black Mountain or Rumford Whitecap Mountain.

  • Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Grafton Notch State Park, including Screw Auger Falls and Mother Walker Falls.

  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Mt. Washington and experience the stunning views from the summit.

  • Day 5: Visit the Rumford Historical Society and learn about the town’s unique heritage and culture.

  • Day 6: Take a scenic drive along Route 2 and stop at some of the region’s most picturesque spots.

  • Day 7: Enjoy some outdoor activities, such as kayaking, fishing, or snowshoeing, depending on the season.

Food and Drink Scene of Rumford

Must-Try Dishes

Rumford may be a small town, but it’s home to some delicious culinary delights that are sure to please any foodie. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Maine lobster rolls: Made with fresh-caught Maine lobster served on a buttered and toasted roll, this classic New England dish is a must-try.

  • Whoopie pies: A classic Maine dessert, whoopie pies are made up of two soft chocolate cake cookies with a fluffy white filling in the middle.

  • Blueberry pancakes: Made with fresh Maine blueberries, these fluffy pancakes are a breakfast staple in Rumford.

Local Beverages

Rumford is also home to a thriving local beverage scene, with many breweries, wineries, and distilleries in the area. Some of the best places to sample local beverages include:

  • Black Mountain of Maine: This ski resort offers a range of craft beers and other beverages, all made on-site.

  • Sunday River Brewing Company: Located in nearby Bethel, this brewery offers a range of delicious brews, including their popular Sunday River Ale.

  • Cellardoor Winery: Located in nearby Lincolnville, this winery produces a range of award-winning wines using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Rumford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rumford has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels. Here are a few top picks:

  • The Rumford House Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B offers comfortable rooms in a historic Victorian mansion.

  • Riverside Inn & Restaurant: This recently renovated hotel offers comfortable rooms with stunning views of the Androscoggin River.

  • The Bethel Inn Resort: Located just a short drive from Rumford in nearby Bethel, this historic property offers luxurious accommodations and a range of amenities, including a golf course and spa.

What to Buy in Rumford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Rumford is complete without picking up some souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the best places to shop for souvenirs include:

  • Black Mountain of Maine gift shop: This gift shop offers a range of souvenirs and gifts, including t-shirts, mugs, and ski gear.

  • Maine Made & More gift shop: Located in nearby Rangeley, this shop specializes in locally made products, including handcrafted jewelry, pottery, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Rumford

Rumford is a friendly town with a strong sense of community and pride in its heritage. Visitors can expect to be welcomed with open arms and treated with warmth and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Rumford

The best time to visit Rumford depends on your interests and the activities you want to enjoy. If you’re interested in winter sports such as skiing and snowshoeing, then the best time to visit is during the winter months. If you prefer warmer weather and outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking, then the spring, summer, and fall months are ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rumford

Do:

  • Take advantage of the town’s many natural attractions, from hiking trails to the Androscoggin River.

  • Try some of the delicious local cuisine, including whoopie pies and Maine lobster rolls.

  • Respect the town’s history and heritage, and take the time to learn about the region’s unique culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural environment.

  • Disrespect local customs or cultural sensitivities.

  • Ignore safety warnings and precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rumford

Rumford is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still take basic precautions to ensure their safety. These include:

  • Locking car doors and homes when unattended.

  • Being aware of your surroundings when hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities.

  • Following safety guidelines when skiing or snowboarding.

Health in Rumford

Rumford is home to a range of medical facilities, including several hospitals and medical centers. Visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their health, such as washing their hands regularly and staying hydrated when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Rumford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rumford

The nearest major airport to Rumford is Portland International Jetport, which is about 87 miles south of Rumford. Other nearby airports include Augusta State Airport and Bangor International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Rumford

There are no major bus stations in Rumford, but visitors can take a Greyhound bus to Augusta or Portland and rent a car or take a taxi to Rumford.

Other Options

The best way to get to Rumford is by car, as there are many scenic routes and attractions to explore in the surrounding countryside. Visitors can rent a car at any of the major airports or in downtown Portland or Augusta.

How to Get Around in Rumford

The best way to get around Rumford is by car or bike, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can rent a car at any of the major airports or in downtown Portland or Augusta, or they can bring their own bikes and explore the many scenic trails in the area.

Where to Go Next from Rumford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rumford is located in the heart of Maine’s Lakes and Mountains region, which offers many other exciting destinations and attractions to explore. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • The village of Bethel, which offers a range of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

  • The Rangeley Lakes region, which is home to some of the best fishing and boating in Maine.

  • The city of Portland, which offers a range of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions, including the Portland Museum of Art and the Old Port district.

Conclusion

From the natural beauty of Grafton Notch State Park to the rich history and culture of the Maine Paper & Heritage Museum, Rumford offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie looking for delicious local cuisine, this friendly town is sure to delight and inspire you.
