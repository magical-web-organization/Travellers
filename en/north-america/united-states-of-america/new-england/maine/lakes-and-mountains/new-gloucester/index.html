Introduction to New Gloucester

Located in Lakes and Mountains region, New Gloucester offers a perfect combination of rugged natural beauty, fascinating history, and modern amenities. This small town in Maine is home to some of the most beautiful lakes, peaks and forests in the state.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the town’s top attractions, activities, accommodations, local culture, customs, and cuisines.

List of Places to Visit in New Gloucester

Here are some of the must-visit places in New Gloucester:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, consider visiting the following museums and historical places in New Gloucester:

  • Sabbathday Lake Shaker Village: Learn about the unique community of Shakers, their history, and their way of life. You can take a guided tour of the grounds and buildings, including the herb garden and the old barn.
  • New Gloucester History Barn: This barn is a local treasure, housing a quirky collection of antique tools, documents, and curiosities that reflect the town’s history.
  • Thompson’s Orchard: Indulge in some apple-picking and take a tour of the orchard to learn about its history and production techniques.

Natural Attractions of New Gloucester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

New Gloucester is a nature lover’s heaven with an abundance of beautiful landscapes and outdoor activities. Top nature spots in New Gloucester include:

  • Pineland Farms: Go horseback riding, mountain biking, hiking, and snowshoeing at this 5,000-acre nature reserve. You can also learn about farming and agriculture at the Education Center.
  • Intervale Preserve: This 51-acre preserve offers a scenic trail that follows the Piscataqua River. It is a great spot for hiking, bird watching, and picnicking.
  • Bryant Pond: This is a popular ice fishing spot in the winter, and an excellent place for kayaking and fishing in the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Gloucester

New Gloucester is an adventure lover’s paradise with numerous hiking and mountain biking trails scattered throughout the town. Some top trails include:

  • Mount Pisgah: This is a challenging 3.5-mile round trip hike that rewards you with panoramic views of the White Mountains and nearby lakes.
  • Bradbury Mountain: This mountain is a popular spot for hiking, trail running, and mountain biking. The summit’s observation tower provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Auburn Riverwalk: This is a 1.6-mile trail that follows the Androscoggin River and provides an excellent opportunity for cycling, walking, and bird watching.

Best Things to Do in New Gloucester: Activities and Tours

Looking for unique things to do in New Gloucester? Here are some options:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride over New Gloucester and the surrounding areas.
  • Go on a horse-drawn carriage ride through the town.
  • Take a guided fishing trip on one of the nearby lakes or rivers.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Gloucester

Wondering how to spend a week in New Gloucester? Consider this itinerary:

Day 1: Sabbathday Lake Shaker Village Day 2: Pineland Farms Day 3: Mount Pisgah Day 4: Auburn Riverwalk Day 5: Bradbury Mountain Day 6: Thompson’s Orchard Day 7: Intervale Preserve

Food and Drink Scene of New Gloucester

New Gloucester offers a wide range of dining options, from classic American comfort food to globally-inspired cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll: You can find some of the best lobster rolls in Maine in New Gloucester.
  • Whoopie pies: This sweet treat is a Maine classic, made with chocolate cake and a sweet, creamy filling.
  • Apple pie: With Thompson’s Orchard in town, New Gloucester is a great place to indulge in some apple pie.

Local Beverages

  • Allen’s Coffee Brandy: This is a Maine staple and a popular choice for mixing with hot cocoa or in “Moose Milk” cocktails.
  • Maine beer: New Gloucester is home to several breweries, including Norumbega Cidery and Maine Beer Company.

Where to Stay in New Gloucester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Gloucester offers a variety of options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some top choices include:

  • Pineland Farms: This working farm offers several guest cottages that are perfect for families or groups.
  • Black Bear Inn & Conference Center: This is a cozy inn that offers comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • The Lodge at Norway Lake: This luxury lodge is situated on the shore of Norway Lake and offers a spa, fitness center, and lakeside restaurant.

What to Buy in New Gloucester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For unique souvenirs and gifts, consider buying the following locally-made products in New Gloucester:

  • Pottery and ceramics from Maine Grind Pottery.
  • Hand-painted glassware from New Gloucester’s Artisan Village.
  • Maple syrup and honey from local producers.

Local Culture and Customs of New Gloucester

New Gloucester has a rich cultural heritage and a unique way of life. Some customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Be respectful of the Shaker community and their religious practices if you visit the Sabbathday Lake Shaker Village.
  • Take off your shoes before entering a private home.
  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.

Best Time to Visit New Gloucester

The best time to visit New Gloucester is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. September and early October offer stunning fall foliage views.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Gloucester

Here are some things to do and avoid when visiting New Gloucester:

Do:

  • Explore the town’s natural beauty.
  • Attend the local farmers’ market for fresh, locally-grown produce.
  • Visit the Shaker Village for a unique and enlightening experience.

Don’t:

  • Be rude to the locals.
  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Ignore traffic rules while driving.

Safety and Health

New Gloucester is generally a very safe town, but it is always important to take some necessary precautions.

Safety in New Gloucester

  • Take care while hiking and wear appropriate footwear.
  • Be aware of wildlife while exploring the natural areas.
  • Do not swim in unknown bodies of water.

Health in New Gloucester

  • Wear sunscreen when outdoors.
  • Bring bug spray during the summer months.
  • Stay hydrated during outdoor activities.

Getting to New Gloucester: Transportation Options and Logistics

New Gloucester is situated 25 miles northwest of Portland. Here are some transportation options for getting to New Gloucester:

  • Fly into Portland International Jetport and take a cab or rental car to New Gloucester.
  • Take the Concord Coach Lines bus to Greyhound Bus Station in Portland and then transfer to a local bus or taxi to New Gloucester.
  • Drive to New Gloucester.

Airports Near New Gloucester

  • Portland International Jetport (PWM)

Bus Stations Near New Gloucester

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Portland)

Other Options

  • Amtrak train (Brunswick station)

How to Get Around in New Gloucester

Car rental is the most convenient way to get around in New Gloucester. Some popular rental car companies in the area include Avis, Enterprise, and Hertz. Biking and walking are great options for exploring the town’s nature trails and other attractions.

Where to Go Next from New Gloucester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are looking to explore more of Maine, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Portland: This coastal city is a foodie’s paradise with a bustling arts scene and scenic harbor.
  • Freeport: This town is known for its outlet shopping and proximity to the beautiful Wolfe’s Neck Woods State Park.
  • Rangeley Lakes: A serene and beautiful resort area that offers plenty of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

New Gloucester offers something for every type of traveler, from history buffs to nature lovers. With an array of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and affordable accommodations, this hidden gem is a must-visit destination in Maine. Plan your visit today and experience the natural beauty and unique culture of New Gloucester.
