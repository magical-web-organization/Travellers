Introduction to Standish

Located in the beautiful Lakes and Mountains region of Maine, Standish is a charming small town that’s rich in history and natural beauty. The town is situated in New England, United States of America, and is known for its stunning lakes, picturesque mountains, and lush forests. With a population of just over 10,000, Standish offers visitors a peaceful and laid-back atmosphere that’s perfect for anyone who wants to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Standish

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Standish has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions to visit on your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Standish Historical Society Museum: Explore the rich history of Standish and the surrounding area through exhibits and artifacts that depict the town’s early years. The museum is located in the old town hall on Route 35.
  • Ossipee Trail Arts Center: This non-profit organization hosts art exhibits, workshops, and performances by local artists throughout the year.
  • Museums of the Bethel Historical Society: These museums feature a wide range of exhibits, including the history of the town, its people, and its industries.
  • The Brick Store Museum: Located just outside Standish in nearby Kennebunk, this museum offers visitors a glimpse into Maine’s heritage through art, artifacts, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Standish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sebago Lake: Maine’s second-largest lake is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and swimming. The lake boasts crystal-clear waters and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Harris Conservation Area: This 200-acre preserve includes forests, wetlands, and grasslands that are home to a variety of bird species and other wildlife. Visitors can hike the trails to explore the area’s natural beauty.
  • Songo River: This river provides an excellent kayaking and canoeing spot, and is surrounded by forests and wildlife.
  • Bradbury Mountain State Park: Located just a short drive from Standish, Bradbury Mountain State Park is a beautiful natural area that offers hiking trails and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Standish

Standish is home to some of Maine’s best hiking and biking trails. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Sebago to Sea Trail: This 26-mile trail connects Sebago Lake to the ocean, passing through forests, farmland, and urban areas.
  • Harris Farm Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is an easy hike that offers views of the nearby hills and forests.
  • Ossipee Hill Trail: This 6-mile round-trip trail takes visitors to the top of Ossipee Hill, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Saco Heath Preserve: This 900-acre preserve features a boardwalk trail through a beautiful heath ecosystem.

Best Things to Do in Standish: Activities and Tours

  • Standish Village Greenway: This 1.5-mile walking trail winds through the heart of Standish. It’s a great way to explore the town and get some exercise at the same time.
  • Fishing: Sebago Lake and the Saco River offer some of the best freshwater fishing opportunities in Maine.
  • Boating: Whether you’re looking for a leisurely cruise or an adrenaline-filled day on the water, Sebago Lake has something for everyone.
  • Golfing: Standish is home to a number of excellent golf courses, including Bridgton Highlands Golf and Tennis, and Point Sebago Golf Resort.

Week-Long Itinerary for Standish

Day 1: Arrive in Standish and explore the town’s historical sites, including the Standish Historical Society Museum and the Ossipee Trail Arts Center.

Day 2: Spend the day outdoors exploring the Harris Conservation Area and the Sebago Lake region.

Day 3: Take a scenic hike on the Ossipee Hill Trail, followed by a leisurely afternoon at Songo River.

Day 4: Enjoy a round of golf at one of Standish’s excellent golf courses, followed by a relaxing afternoon on Sebago Lake.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Bradbury Mountain State Park for some hiking and panoramic views.

Day 6: Spend the day boating or fishing on Sebago Lake.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a visit to the Brick Store Museum in Kennebunk, followed by a wonderful meal at one of Standish’s great restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Standish

Standish offers visitors a great selection of local cuisine, from seafood to regional specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic Maine dish that’s not to be missed. The meat is often served with mayonnaise, butter, or both, in a toasted brioche bun.
  • Blueberry Pie: Made with fresh Maine blueberries, this pie is a delicious way to end any meal.
  • Fried Clams: Fresh clams are fried to perfection and served with tartar sauce and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Allagash White: This Belgian-style wheat beer is brewed by the Allagash Brewing Company in nearby Portland, and is a favorite among locals.
  • Moxie: A regional soda that’s been around since 1884, Moxie has a distinctively bitter and sweet flavor that’s an acquired taste.
  • Maine Beer Company IPAs: This craft brewery, located in Freeport just minutes from Standish, is known for its delicious IPAs.

Where to Stay in Standish: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Standish has a variety of accommodation options, from cozy B&Bs to luxury resorts. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Point Sebago Golf Resort: This family-friendly resort offers cabins, RV sites, and hotel rooms, as well as golf courses, boating, and swimming.
  • The White Barn Inn & Spa: Located just a short drive from Standish in Kennebunkport, this luxury resort offers upscale accommodations and world-class amenities.
  • Lakeview Inn: With its beautiful lakeside location and charming decor, this B&B is the perfect place to relax and unwind.

What to Buy in Standish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maine Blueberry Products: From jams and jellies to blueberry wine, you’ll find a wide variety of products made from Maine’s famous blueberries.
  • Handmade Crafts: Explore the local art scene and pick up some unique handmade crafts, such as pottery or jewelry.
  • Maine Maple Syrup: Maple syrup is a staple in Maine cuisine, and makes for a delicious souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Standish

Mainers are known for their friendly and down-to-earth attitude, and Standish is no exception. Visitors can expect warm hospitality and a laid-back atmosphere wherever they go.

Best Time to Visit Standish

The best time to visit Standish is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the outdoor attractions are in full swing. However, fall is also a great time to visit, when the leaves change color and the crowds thin out.

Do’s and Don’ts of Standish

Do:

  • Take in the natural beauty of Sebago Lake and the surrounding forests.
  • Try some of the local foods, such as lobster and blueberry pie.
  • Explore the town’s rich history through its museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs or act disrespectfully towards locals.
  • Miss out on the chance to experience the town’s outdoor activities and scenic views.

Safety and Health

Safety in Standish

Standish is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing valuables.

Health in Standish

Visitors to Standish should take precautions to prevent tick and mosquito bites, such as wearing long sleeves and insect repellent. Additionally, they should be aware of the risk of Lyme disease and other tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Standish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Standish

The nearest airport to Standish is Portland International Jetport, which is located approximately 26 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Standish

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Portland, approximately 25 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Standish by car, or by train via the Amtrak Downeaster to the nearby cities of Portland or Wells.

How to Get Around in Standish

The best way to get around Standish is by car. This will allow visitors to explore the town’s attractions at their own pace and access the surrounding natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Standish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland: Just a short drive from Standish, Portland is a vibrant city that offers plenty of cultural attractions and great food.
  • Kennebunkport: This charming coastal town is just a short drive from Standish, and is known for its stunning scenery and historical attractions.
  • Bar Harbor: While a bit further away, Bar Harbor is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts, thanks to its proximity to Acadia National Park.

Conclusion

Standish is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience Maine’s natural beauty and charm. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this quaint town. With its stunning lakes and mountains, rich history, and friendly locals, it’s no wonder that Standish has become a popular tourist destination in recent years.
