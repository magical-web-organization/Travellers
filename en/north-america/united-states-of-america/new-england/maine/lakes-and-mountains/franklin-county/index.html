Introduction to Franklin County

Franklin County is a picturesque county located in the Lakes and Mountains region of Maine, in the United States of America. It is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. The county is home to many charming towns and villages, each offering a unique charm and character. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Franklin County.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Stanley Museum

The Stanley Museum is a must-visit attraction for those interested in the history of transportation. The museum showcases the famous Stanley Steamer, a steam-powered car invented by Francis and Freelan Stanley, who were born and raised in Kingfield, Maine. The museum has an impressive collection of vintage cars, photographs, and artifacts related to the Stanley brothers and the automobile industry.

Maine Huts & Trails

Maine Huts & Trails is a network of backcountry eco-lodges and trails that offer visitors a unique outdoor experience. The trail system covers over 80 miles, connecting four eco-lodges, and is open for hiking, biking, snowshoeing, and cross-country skiing. The eco-lodges offer comfortable accommodation and wholesome meals made from locally-sourced ingredients.

Maine State Building

The Maine State Building, located in Farmington, was built in 1935 as part of Franklin D. Roosevelt’s New Deal. The building served as a showcase of Maine’s natural resources and craftsmanship. Today, it houses the University of Maine at Farmington, and visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the building’s history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Franklin County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Blue State Park

Mount Blue State Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park covers over 8,000 acres and has miles of hiking trails, fishing ponds, and campsites. Visitors can also enjoy swimming in the park’s two beaches, canoeing, boating, and picnicking.

Sandy River and Rangeley Lakes Railroad

The Sandy River and Rangeley Lakes Railroad is a historic narrow-gauge railroad that offers visitors a scenic ride through the woods and fields of western Maine. The train runs along the Sandy River and passes through several historic towns, providing visitors with an opportunity to see the beauty of Franklin County’s countryside.

Height of Land

Height of Land is a scenic overlook located on Route 17, offering visitors a breathtaking view of the Rangeley Lakes and the surrounding mountains. It’s a great spot for photographers, hikers, and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin County

Franklin County has an abundance of hiking and biking trails, from easy strolls to challenging hikes. Here are some of the best routes for hikers and bikers:

Hiking Routes

Tumbledown Mountain Trail

The Tumbledown Mountain Trail is a moderate-to-difficult hike that rewards hikers with stunning views of the Tumbledown Mountain, including a deep gully known as “The Cradle.” The trail covers over four miles and is best for experienced hikers.

Bald Mountain Trail

The Bald Mountain Trail is an easy-to-moderate hike that offers views of the surrounding mountains and hills. The trail is about two miles long and is suitable for all skill levels.

Fly Rod Crosby Trail

The Fly Rod Crosby Trail is a 45-mile-long trail that takes hikers through the woods and fields of western Maine. The trail is named after Cornelia “Fly Rod” Crosby, a famous Maine outdoorswoman and writer. The trail is divided into several sections, making it easy for hikers to do shorter hikes.

Biking Routes

Sandy River Rail Trail

The Sandy River Rail Trail is a 14.5-mile-long trail that follows the Sandy River and passes through several small towns, including Farmington and Phillips. The trail is relatively flat and is suitable for all skill levels.

Rangeley Lakes Trail Center

The Rangeley Lakes Trail Center is a popular destination for mountain bikers and offers over 50 miles of single-track trails. The trails vary in difficulty, making it suitable for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Franklin County: Activities and Tours

Snowmobiling

Franklin County is a premier destination for snowmobiling, with over 300 miles of groomed trails. Visitors can rent snowmobiles and explore the county’s scenic beauty during the winter months.

Fishing

Franklin County is home to several lakes, rivers, and streams that offer excellent fishing opportunities. Visitors can catch trout, salmon, bass, and other fish species.

Canoeing and Kayaking

Franklin County has several lakes and rivers that are perfect for canoeing and kayaking. The calm waters of Rangeley Lake and the Kennebec River provide a relaxing experience for paddlers of all levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin County

Must-Try Dishes

Maine Lobster

Maine lobster is a must-try dish when visiting Franklin County. Lobster shacks and restaurants throughout the county offer fresh and delicious lobster dishes, from classic lobster rolls to lobster tail dinners.

Blueberry Pie

Maine blueberries are famous for their sweet, juicy flavor, and are often used in traditional blueberry pies. Visitors can find blueberry pies in many restaurants, diners, and bakeries throughout the county.

Local Beverages

Maine Beer

Maine has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries located in Franklin County. Be sure to try some of the local brews, such as the Maine Beer Company’s IPA or the Tumbledown Brewing’s Scotch Ale.

Maine Maple Syrup

Maine is known for its delicious and high-quality maple syrup, which is produced in the county’s sugarhouses. Visitors can tour the sugarhouses and try the syrup in many breakfast places, diners, and cafes.

Where to Stay in Franklin County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Franklin County offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from cozy guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in the county:

The Inn on Winter’s Hill

The Inn on Winter’s Hill is a charming bed and breakfast located in Kingfield, Maine. The inn has seven beautifully decorated rooms and offers a full breakfast each morning.

Sugarloaf Mountain Resort

Sugarloaf Mountain Resort is a world-renowned ski resort located in Carrabassett Valley, Maine. The resort has over 1,400 acres of skiable terrain and offers a range of accommodations, including hotels, condos, and rental homes.

Rangeley Lake Resort

Rangeley Lake Resort is a lakeside resort located in Rangeley, Maine. The resort has cozy cabins and cottages and offers a range of activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking.

What to Buy in Franklin County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Franklin County is known for its local products and crafts, including maple syrup, handmade pottery, and wool products. Visitors can find these products in many shops and markets throughout the county, including:

Farmington Farmers Market

The Farmington Farmers Market is a farmers’ market held every Saturday during the summer months. The market features local farmers and vendors selling fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

Maine Made & More

Maine Made & More is a gift shop located in Farmington that specializes in selling products made by local artisans. Visitors can find a range of products, including jewelry, pottery, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin County

Franklin County has a rich history and a vibrant culture. The county is home to several festivals and events that celebrate its heritage, including:

Rangeley Lakes Lumberjack Festival

The Rangeley Lakes Lumberjack Festival is held every August and celebrates the county’s logging heritage. The festival features lumberjack competitions, axe throwing, and live music.

Phillips Old Home Days

Phillips Old Home Days is an annual event held in July that celebrates the town’s history and culture. The event features parades, live music, and a vintage car show.

Best Time to Visit Franklin County

The best time to visit Franklin County depends on the visitor’s preference. The county is beautiful year-round, with each season offering a unique experience. Summer is the most popular season for outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and boating. Fall is known for its stunning foliage and is an excellent time for leaf-peeping. Winter is ideal for snow sports, such as skiing, snowshoeing, and snowmobiling. Spring is a quieter time, with fewer tourists, making it a great time to explore the county’s cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin County

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Take care of the wilderness and leave no trace.
  • Try the local cuisine and products.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the locals or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin County

Franklin County is a safe and friendly place to visit. Visitors should be aware of the following safety precautions:

  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Be cautious when driving on rural roads, especially during winter when roads can be icy and snow-covered.
  • Be aware of black bears, which are common in the county, and take necessary precautions.

Health in Franklin County

Visitors should be aware of the following health precautions when visiting Franklin County:

  • Lyme disease is prevalent in the county, especially during the summer months. Visitors should wear long-sleeved clothing and use insect repellent to avoid tick bites.
  • The county has several hospitals and healthcare facilities, and visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage.

Where to Go Next from Franklin County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Franklin County is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

Portland

Portland is Maine’s largest city and is known for its vibrant food and drink scene, historic architecture, and scenic waterfront. Visitors can tour the city’s art galleries, visit its many breweries and restaurants, or explore its historic Old Port district.

Acadia National Park

Acadia National Park, located in eastern Maine, is a stunning natural wonder that boasts miles of hiking trails, beautiful beaches, and wildlife. Visitors can explore the park’s many attractions, including Cadillac Mountain, Jordan Pond, and Thunder Hole.

Kennebunkport

Kennebunkport is a charming coastal town in southern Maine known for its picturesque harbor, sandy beaches, and boutique shops. Visitors can stroll along Dock Square, enjoy fresh seafood, or take a scenic boat tour.

Conclusion

Franklin County is a hidden gem in Maine that offers visitors a unique and authentic experience. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or delicious food, Franklin County has something for everyone. With its scenic beauty, rich history, and friendly locals, Franklin County is a destination that should not be missed.
