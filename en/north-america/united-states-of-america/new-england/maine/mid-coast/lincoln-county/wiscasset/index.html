Introduction to Wiscasset

Located in charming Lincoln County, Wiscasset is a picturesque town in the Mid Coast region of Maine. Known for its historic downtown district, charming coastal scenery, and abundance of outdoor activities, Wiscasset is the perfect destination for those looking for a quintessential Maine experience.

Explore the town’s quaint shops and eateries, or head outdoors to hike, fish, or take in the stunning views along the coast. In this guide, we’ll take a look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay and eat in Wiscasset.

List of Places to Visit in Wiscasset

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historic Sites

  • Castle Tucker: A historic manor house that dates back to 1807, Castle Tucker is a shining example of Federal-style architecture. Tour the mansion and gardens and learn about the influential Tucker family who called this house home.
  • Lincoln County Historical Association: Discover the rich history of Wiscasset and Lincoln County through exhibits and collections at this museum, located in the old county jail.
  • Nickels-Sortwell House: Visit this stunning Federal-style mansion, once owned by a successful shipbuilder, and learn about Wiscasset’s rich maritime history.

Natural Attractions of Wiscasset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coastal Maine Botanical Gardens: Wander through stunning gardens and trails with breathtaking views of the rugged Maine coast. Kids will enjoy the children’s garden, and everyone can take in the beauty of the seasonal flowers and foliage.
  • Montsweag Preserve: Hike the trails and take in the scenery of this 860-acre nature preserve. With over 10 miles of hiking trails and a variety of wildlife to see, this is a top destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Sheepscot River: Take a scenic drive along Route 1 to gaze at the stunning views of the Sheepscot River. Bring a picnic or stop at a seafood restaurant to enjoy the fresh catch of the day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wiscasset

  • Wiscasset and Woolwich Bridge: Bike or walk along this historic bridge, which spans the Sheepscot River and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Whiskeag Trail: Located in nearby Bath, this 6.5-mile hiking trail offers views of the Kennebec River and passes by historic forts and scenic vistas.
  • Dodge Point Preserve: Located in Newcastle, this preserve has over 500 acres of forests, meadows, and trails perfect for exploring on foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Wiscasset: Activities and Tours

  • Wiscasset Village Farmers Market: Browse local produce, baked goods, crafts and more at this popular outdoor market. Open every Wednesday from May to October.
  • Maine Art Gallery: Located in Wiscasset’s historic village, this art gallery showcases the work of local Maine artists.
  • Cape Line Adventures: Take a guided kayaking tour along the coast and view the lobster boats and seals in the area. Great for beginners or those with experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Wiscasset

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maine Lobster: No trip to Wiscasset is complete without trying the local lobster. Head to Red’s Eats or Sarah’s Cafe for the ultimate lobster roll.
  • Blueberry Pancakes: Start your day with a stack of fluffy blueberry pancakes at any of the local diners.
  • Chowder: Warm-up with a bowl of creamy clam chowder from Sea Basket Restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: Sip on a local brew at the Shipyard Tasting Room.
  • Frosty’s Donuts: Enjoy an artisanal donut and an espresso from this popular local establishment.
  • Rising Tide Co-Op: Pick up a bottle of locally sourced wine or beer from the co-op’s impressive selection.

Where to Stay in Wiscasset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sneakaway Inn: This boutique bed and breakfast in Wiscasset offers charming rooms, comfortable amenities, and a locally sourced breakfast buffet each morning.
  • Wiscasset Woods Lodge: A budget-friendly option located just outside of town with cozy rooms and a pool on site.
  • Coveside Bed-and-Breakfast: This stunning waterfront inn offers beautiful views of the Sheepscot River and a gourmet breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Wiscasset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maine Blueberry Jam: Stock up on the local staple and bring it home to enjoy on toast or with cheese and crackers.
  • Lobster-themed Gifts: Bring home a lobster-themed souvenir such as a tea towel, coffee mug, or keychain to remind you of your trip.
  • Maine-made Crafts: Browse the local shops for handmade pottery, glassware, or jewelry from Maine artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Wiscasset

Wiscasset is known for its rich maritime history, and locals take pride in supporting small businesses and preserving the town’s unique character. It’s common to see residents and visitors alike walking around town, stopping for a chat with acquaintances or taking in the views along the coast.

Best Time to Visit Wiscasset

Wiscasset is a four-season destination, each with its own charm. The warm summer months are popular for outdoor activities and events, while the fall foliage and winter snow offer stunning scenery and cozy activities. Spring is the perfect time to visit the Coastal Maine Botanical Gardens, where the seasonal flowers are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wiscasset

Do:

  • Take a bike ride along the Wiscasset and Woolwich Bridge
  • Support local businesses by shopping and dining locally
  • Take in the stunning views along the Sheepscot River

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural beauty of the area
  • Forget to pack layers, as Maine weather can be unpredictable
  • Be loud and disruptive in residential areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Wiscasset

Wiscasset is a safe and welcoming town, with little crime. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and lock their cars when leaving valuables inside.

Health in Wiscasset

Wiscasset does have a hospital and urgent care center nearby, as well as several pharmacies. Visitors should pack any necessary medications and consult their doctors before traveling.

Getting to Wiscasset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wiscasset

  • Knox County Rgnl Airport (RKD), 27 miles
  • Portland International Jetport (PWM), 50 miles
  • Bangor International Airport (BGR), 83 miles

Bus Stations Near Wiscasset

  • Concord Coach Lines: Service to Damariscotta and Rockland, both of which are within a 30-minute drive of Wiscasset.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Maine and see all that Wiscasset has to offer.

How to Get Around in Wiscasset

While having a car is the easiest way to explore the area, visitors can also bike or walk through town or take a taxi or Uber for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from Wiscasset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Camden: Just 32 miles north, this charming coastal town is known for its scenic harbor, boutique shops, and local restaurants.
  • Portland: The state’s largest city is just a one-hour drive south and offers an abundance of cultural attractions, museums, and shopping.
  • Acadia National Park: This iconic park is just three hours north of Wiscasset and offers stunning scenic drives, coastal walks, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

From historic mansions to outdoor adventures, Wiscasset offers something for everyone. Embrace the town’s small-town charm and support local businesses while taking in the stunning coastal views. Whether you’re looking for a romantic weekend away or a family vacation, Wiscasset promises to offer the quintessential Maine experience.
