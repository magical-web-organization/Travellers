Introduction to Whitefield

Welcome to Whitefield, a charming town in Lincoln County, Maine. Located in Mid Coast, Whitefield is a small town with a big personality. As you explore this beautiful region, you’ll discover a wealth of cultural, historical, and natural attractions that will keep you busy for days. From hiking and biking to museums and local cuisine, Whitefield has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll show you the best things to see and do in Whitefield, as well as tips on where to stay and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Whitefield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town of Whitefield has a rich history, and there are many museums and historical sites to visit. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Sheepscot General Store and Farm: This historic store dates back to the early 19th century and is a great place to buy local produce and baked goods.
  • The David Getchell Sr. Memorial Bridge: This locally famous bridge was built in 1939 and spans the Sheepscot River.
  • Whitefield Historical Society: The society maintains a number of sites, including the recently restored 1804 Methodist Meetinghouse, the Ballstown Schoolhouse, and the Googins Hall Schoolhouse.

Natural Attractions of Whitefield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whitefield is a nature lover’s paradise, and there are many outdoor activities and scenic landscapes to explore. Here are some must-visit destinations:

  • Sheepscot River: This river winds its way through Whitefield and is a scenic spot to go kayaking or canoeing.
  • Togus Pond: This beautiful pond is surrounded by woods, and it’s a great spot for a picnic or to watch the sunset.
  • Chickawaukee Lake: This lake is popular among swimmers and anglers alike, with plenty of opportunities for boating and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whitefield

Whitefield is a great place to explore on foot or by bike, with many trails and routes to choose from. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Whitefield Heritage Trail: This scenic trail takes you through the historic district of Whitefield, with stops at the town hall, historical society, and other sites.
  • Sheepscot Valley Conservation Association Trail Network: This network of trails covers over 10,000 acres of land along the Sheepscot River and is great for hikers and bikers of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Whitefield: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Whitefield. Here are some ideas:

  • Maine State Music Theatre: This professional theater company in Brunswick offers high-quality musical productions during the summer season.
  • Damariscotta River Cruises: Take a boat tour to discover the history and natural beauty of the Damariscotta River.
  • Maine Winery Guild Tour: Take a tour of local wineries and taste some of the best wines in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Whitefield

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Whitefield, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

Day Activity
Monday Visit the Whitefield Historical Society and go for a hike on the Whitefield Heritage Trail.
Tuesday Rent a kayak and explore the Sheepscot River. Have lunch at the Sheepscot General Store.
Wednesday Take a scenic drive to Coastal Maine Botanical Gardens in Boothbay Harbor.
Thursday Tour some of the local wineries, such as Cellardoor Winery and Sweetgrass Winery.
Friday Take a boat tour of the Damariscotta River and enjoy a seafood dinner at the Newcastle Publick House.
Saturday Go for a bike ride on the Sheepscot Valley Conservation Association Trail Network.
Sunday Relax at Togus Pond and have a picnic lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Whitefield

Must-Try Dishes

Whitefield is known for its local cuisine, and there are many traditional dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Lobster Roll: This classic Maine dish is a must-try, and you’ll find it on the menu at many local seafood restaurants.
  • Blueberry Pie: Maine is famous for its blueberries, and a slice of blueberry pie is the perfect dessert after a traditional Maine meal.
  • Whoopie Pie: This beloved dessert consists of two chocolate cakes with a creamy filling.

Local Beverages

Maine is also known for its craft breweries and wineries. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Allagash Brewery: This famous brewery in Portland offers tours and tastings of its award-winning Belgian-style beers.
  • Maine Mead Works: This Portland-based meadery uses honey from local beekeepers to create unique flavors like lavender and blueberry.
  • Downtown & Coastal Wine Trail: This trail takes you to some of the best wineries in the area for tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Whitefield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whitefield has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and travel styles. Here are some options:

  • The Sheepscot Inn: This cozy inn offers comfortable rooms and a great location in the heart of Whitefield.
  • Whitefield Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and comfortable rooms with basic amenities.
  • Airbnb: For a more authentic experience, consider renting a room or apartment through Airbnb.

What to Buy in Whitefield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Whitefield:

  • Blueberry Products: Bring home some blueberry jam, blueberry wine, or other blueberry-themed products from local stores like Maine Coast Book Shop & Cafe.
  • Local Crafts: Discover handmade pottery, jewelry, and other crafts at local stores like Sheepscot General Store and Gifts of the Wyrd.
  • Fresh Produce: Check out the farmer’s markets in Whitefield and nearby towns for fresh fruits and vegetables, as well as homemade jams and other preserves.

Local Culture and Customs of Whitefield

Maine has a strong fishing and maritime heritage, and tradition is an important part of local customs and culture. Many towns along the coast celebrate festivals and events that pay homage to the state’s maritime roots.

Best Time to Visit Whitefield

The best time to visit Whitefield is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are many outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy during this time. Fall is also a great time to visit for those who enjoy colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whitefield

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and blueberry products.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Whitefield by hiking, biking, or boating.
  • Do visit the historic sites and museums to learn more about the town’s history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the area.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t visit in the winter if you don’t enjoy cold weather or snow.

Safety and Health

Safety in Whitefield

Whitefield is a safe town, but visitors should still take the usual precautions when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or biking on remote trails. Watch out for ticks during the summer months, as Lyme disease is prevalent in the area.

Health in Whitefield

Maine has a high rate of tick-borne diseases, so visitors should take care to protect themselves from ticks when spending time outdoors. Use insect repellent and wear light-colored clothing with long sleeves and pants. Be cautious of the water quality in lakes and rivers when swimming or boating.

Getting to Whitefield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Whitefield

The closest airport to Whitefield is Augusta State Airport, which is about a 30-minute drive from the town. Portland International Jetport is about a 90-minute drive.

Bus Stations Near Whitefield

Concord Coach Lines operates a bus that stops in Damariscotta, a nearby town, which is about a 20-minute drive from Whitefield.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to rent a car to explore the area, as there is limited public transportation. Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Whitefield

While a car is necessary to explore the surrounding areas, Whitefield is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several tour companies that offer bus or boat tours of the area.

Where to Go Next from Whitefield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whitefield is located in Mid Coast Maine, a region known for its scenic beauty and coastal towns. Here are some nearby destinations to consider visiting:

  • Boothbay Harbor: This charming town is located about 30 miles from Whitefield and is known for its picturesque harbor and botanical gardens.
  • Rockland: This coastal town is about an hour’s drive from Whitefield and is famous for its art galleries and restaurants.
  • Portland: Maine’s largest city is about a 90-minute drive from Whitefield and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, dining options, and shopping.

Conclusion

Whitefield, Maine, is a small town with a lot to offer its visitors. From historic buildings and museums to beautiful natural landscapes and outdoor activities, Whitefield has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has given you some ideas for your next trip to this charming New England town. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an active adventure, Whitefield is the perfect destination.
