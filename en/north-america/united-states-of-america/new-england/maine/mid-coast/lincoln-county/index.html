Introduction to Lincoln County

Located in Mid Coast Maine, Lincoln County is known for its stunning coastal scenery, historic sites, and outdoor adventures. The county spans over 700 square miles, and is home to about 35,000 residents. Visitors to Lincoln County can enjoy a variety of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local customs to help you plan your trip to Lincoln County.

List of Places to Visit in Lincoln County

Whether you’re interested in history, art, or outdoor recreation, Lincoln County has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Maine Maritime Museum

Located in Bath, this museum showcases Maine’s rich maritime history through exhibits, historic boats, and interactive displays. Visitors can take a boat tour of the Kennebec River or explore the Percy & Small Shipyard.

Boothbay Railway Village

This living history museum in Boothbay features historic buildings, antique cars, and a working narrow-gauge railway. The village, which dates back to the 1850s, hosts special events and educational programs throughout the year.

Pemaquid Point Lighthouse

One of Maine’s most photographed lighthouses, Pemaquid Point dates back to 1827. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for stunning views of the rocky coastline.

Natural Attractions of Lincoln County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Damariscotta Lake

Located in Jefferson, this freshwater lake offers plenty of opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing. Visitors can also hike the nearby trails in the surrounding forests.

Bradbury Mountain State Park

Located in nearby Pownal, this state park is a popular destination for hiking and camping. Visitors can climb to the summit of Bradbury Mountain for panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Coastal Maine Botanical Gardens

This 270-acre botanical garden in Boothbay Harbor features stunning gardens, fountains, and sculptures. Visitors can explore the gardens, attend special events, or take a guided tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lincoln County

For outdoor enthusiasts, Lincoln County has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

Boothbay Harbor Land Trust

This nonprofit land trust maintains over 30 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and skiing. The trails offer stunning views of the coast and surrounding forests, and are open year-round.

Hidden Valley Nature Center

Located in Jefferson, this nature center features over 25 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing. Visitors can explore the forests, ponds, and streams, and attend educational programs and workshops.

Rachel Carson Salt Pond Preserve

Located in New Harbor, this coastal preserve features miles of trails for walking, hiking, and birdwatching. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the ocean, salt marshes, and rocky outcroppings.

Best Things to Do in Lincoln County: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Lincoln County. Here are some of our top picks:

Boothbay Harbor Lobster Boat Tour

Experience the Maine lobster industry up close with a lobster boat tour in Boothbay Harbor. Visitors can learn about lobster trapping, see the local wildlife, and even help haul in the catch.

Puffin Watching Tours

Visitors to the area can take a boat tour to see puffins, eagles, and other seabirds along Maine’s coast. Tours depart from Boothbay Harbor and New Harbor, and run from May to October.

Antique Shopping

Lincoln County is famous for its antique shops, which offer everything from fine art to vintage clothing to furniture. Visitors can browse the shops in Boothbay Harbor, Wiscasset, and other small towns and villages in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lincoln County

For visitors with more time to spend in the area, here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Lincoln County:

Day 1: Maine Maritime Museum and Bath Iron Works Day 2: Coastal Maine Botanical Gardens and Boothbay Harbor Day 3: Pemaquid Point Lighthouse and Damariscotta Day 4: Hiking in Hidden Valley Nature Center and lunch in Jefferson Day 5: Boothbay Harbor Land Trust and afternoon sail Day 6: Antique shopping in Wiscasset and seafood dinner in New Harbor Day 7: Rachel Carson Salt Pond Preserve and farewell lunch in Boothbay Harbor

Food and Drink Scene of Lincoln County

Maine is known for its fresh seafood, farm-to-table cuisine, and craft beer. Here are some of the best places to eat and drink in Lincoln County:

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Roll

No trip to Maine is complete without trying a lobster roll, and Lincoln County has plenty of options. Consider trying one at Lobster Dock in Boothbay Harbor or Muscongus Bay Lobster in Round Pond.

Clam Chowder

Another classic Maine dish, clam chowder is best enjoyed overlooking the ocean. Try the version at McSeagull’s in Boothbay Harbor or Moody’s Diner in Waldoboro.

Blueberry Pie

Maine is known for its wild blueberries, and blueberry pie is a classic dessert. Try the pie at Moody’s Diner or the Blueberry Hill Farmstand in Jefferson.

Local Beverages

Maine Craft Beer

Lincoln County is home to several craft breweries, including Boothbay Craft Brewery, Oxbow Brewing Company, and Rising Tide Brewing Company. Try a flight at one of the tasting rooms in the area.

Maine Wines

Maine is not usually known for its wine, but there are several wineries in the area worth visiting. Try the blueberry wine at Savage Oakes Vineyard in Union or the Riesling at Sweetgrass Farm Winery and Distillery in Union.

Where to Stay in Lincoln County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From historic inns to waterfront resorts, Lincoln County offers a variety of lodging options to suit every taste and budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

The 1812 Farm Bed and Breakfast

This historic inn in Bristol features four rooms with private baths and plenty of New England charm. The inn is located just a short drive from Pemaquid Point Lighthouse and other local attractions.

Linekin Bay Resort

This waterfront resort in Boothbay Harbor offers a variety of accommodations, from traditional rooms to private cabins. The resort also features a restaurant, fitness center, and activities like kayaking and sailing.

Topside Inn

Located in Boothbay Harbor, this historic bed and breakfast offers stunning views of the harbor and surrounding islands. The inn features 20 rooms and suites with luxurious amenities like fireplaces and whirlpool tubs.

What to Buy in Lincoln County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From lobster-themed kitchenware to handmade soaps to craft beer, there are plenty of unique local products to take home from Lincoln County. Here are some of our top picks:

Maine-Made Products

The Maine Made Marketplace in Portland offers a wide variety of products made by local artisans, including pottery, jewelry, and clothing. Visitors can also find locally made products at farmers markets and craft fairs throughout the area.

Lobster Kitchenware

For a fun and functional souvenir, consider picking up some lobster-themed kitchenware, like a lobster butter dish or a lobster bib. Look for these items at gift shops in Boothbay Harbor and other coastal towns.

Craft Beer

Maine is known for its craft beer scene, and there are plenty of local breweries to choose from. Consider taking home a six-pack or two from one of the tasting rooms in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln County

Mainers are known for their love of seafood, outdoor activities, and independent spirit. Visitors to Lincoln County should be prepared for cool coastal weather, and should also be respectful of local traditions and customs. Take time to chat with the friendly locals, and be sure to try some of the local cuisine and craft beer.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln County

The best time to visit Lincoln County depends on your interests and preferences. Summer is the busiest season, with warm weather, festivals, and plenty of outdoor activities. Fall is a quieter time of year, but offers stunning foliage and harvest festivals. Winter is the least busy season, but offers snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, and other winter sports. Spring is a great time to visit for birdwatching and seeing wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln County

Do:

  • Visit local farmers markets and farm stands for fresh produce
  • Take a boat tour to see the local wildlife and marine life
  • Try the lobster and blueberry dishes
  • Explore the local hiking and biking trails

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches or trails
  • Ignore traffic laws or speed limits on the winding coastal roads
  • Forget to pack warm layers and rain gear, especially in the spring and fall
  • Close off to chance encounters with friendly locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Lincoln County

Lincoln County is a safe area with low crime rates, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Be aware of your surroundings, secure your valuables, and practice safe driving on the winding coastal roads. Swimmers and boaters should also be aware of changing tides and rough seas.

Health in Lincoln County

Lincoln County has several hospitals and medical clinics for visitors in need of medical attention. Travelers should also be aware of tick-borne illnesses like Lyme disease and take preventative measures like wearing insect repellent.

Getting to Lincoln County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lincoln County

The closest major airport to Lincoln County is Portland International Jetport, which is about an hour’s drive away. Other nearby airports include Bangor International Airport and Boston Logan International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lincoln County

Local bus service is limited in Lincoln County, but visitors can take the Concord Coach Lines bus to nearby cities like Portland and Boston.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lincoln County by car via the Maine Turnpike, or by train via Amtrak’s Downeaster, which stops in nearby Brunswick.

How to Get Around in Lincoln County

The easiest way to get around Lincoln County is by car, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can rent a car at the airports or in the nearby towns of Brunswick or Augusta.

Where to Go Next from Lincoln County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lincoln County is located in a beautiful part of Maine with plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Here are some suggestions for nearby excursions:

Portland

Maine’s largest city, Portland, is about an hour’s drive from Lincoln County. Visitors can explore the historic Old Port district, visit the Portland Museum of Art, or take a food tour of the city’s many excellent restaurants.

Acadia National Park

Located about three and a half hours from Lincoln County, Acadia National Park offers stunning coastal scenery, hiking trails, and outdoor activities. Visitors can also explore the nearby towns of Bar Harbor and Northeast Harbor.

Cape Cod

Located about five hours south of Lincoln County, Cape Cod is a popular vacation destination known for its beaches, seafood, and charming seaside towns.

Conclusion

Lincoln County is a hidden gem of Maine, with plenty of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in history, hiking, or lobster, this area has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Lincoln County and discover all that this coastal Maine county has to offer.
