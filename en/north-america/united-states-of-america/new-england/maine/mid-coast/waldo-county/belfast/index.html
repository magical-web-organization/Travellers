Introduction to Belfast

Belfast is a charming coastal town situated in Waldo County in the Mid Coast region of Maine, in New England, United States of America. Belfast is home to beautiful parks and gardens, fabulous restaurants, unique museums, and stunning natural landscapes, making it an ideal destination for those who love the outdoors, culture, and history. In this comprehensive travel guide to Belfast, we’ll cover all the must-visit places, things to do, and insider tips for making your trip to Belfast unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Belfast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Penobscot Bay History Center

Penobscot Bay History Center is a history museum dedicated to preserving the history of Penobscot Bay and the surrounding communities. The museum has an extensive collection of artifacts, photographs, archives, and manuscripts. The exhibits showcase the maritime heritage of the region, including the fishing industry and shipbuilding.

Belfast Historical Society and Museum

The Belfast Historical Society and Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts, documents, and photographs, celebrating the town’s rich history. The exhibits showcase the history of Belfast from its Native American origins to the present day.

Fort Knox State Historic Site

Fort Knox State Historic Site is a well-preserved fortification from the mid-19th century. The fort was constructed to protect the Penobscot River area against potential British attacks. Visitors can take a guided tour of the fort and learn about its fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Belfast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Belfast City Park

Belfast City Park is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The park is home to a beautiful beach, picnic areas, a playground, and a gazebo, making it the perfect spot for a family outing. The park also has trails for hiking and biking, providing stunning views of Penobscot Bay.

Belfast Harbor walk

Belfast Harbor Walk is a scenic trail that takes visitors along the Belfast waterfront. The trail is perfect for a relaxing walk, jog, or bike ride, providing stunning views of the harbor, the boats, and the local wildlife.

Moose Point State Park

Moose Point State Park is a 186-acre park located in nearby Searsport. The park is home to a beautiful shoreline, a picnic area, and hiking trails. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of Penobscot Bay and Mount Battie.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belfast

Belfast offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some popular trails to explore:

  • Little River Community Trail
  • Belfast Rail Trail
  • Hills to Sea Trail
  • Moose Point State Park trails

Best Things to Do in Belfast: Activities and Tours

Belfast Farmers’ Market

Belfast Farmers’ Market is a vibrant and bustling market, offering a range of local produce, artisan crafts, and delicious baked goods. The market is open year-round on Fridays, providing a taste of local flavor.

Belfast Art Walk

Belfast Art Walk is a monthly event that showcases the work of local artists and artisans. The event takes place on the fourth Friday of every month from May to October, providing a unique opportunity to experience the vibrant arts and culture scene in Belfast.

Lobster Boat Tour

A Lobster Boat Tour is a fun and unique way to experience the local lobster industry. Visitors can learn about the lobstermen’s daily routine, and even have the chance to catch their own lobster.

Food and Drink Scene of Belfast

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Roll

Lobster Rolls are a must-try dish in Belfast. Freshly caught lobsters are served in a hot dog bun, topped with butter and spices, providing a delicious and savory flavor.

Blueberry Pie

Blueberry Pie is another local favorite. Maine is known for its blueberries, and the pie is made with fresh, locally sourced berries, providing a sweet and tasty treat.

Local Beverages

Maine Craft Beer

Maine Craft Beer is a fan favorite, with local breweries offering a range of flavors and styles to choose from.

Maine-Made Wine

Maine-Made Wine is another local favorite, made from locally grown grapes and fruit, providing a unique taste of Maine.

Where to Stay in Belfast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarratine Club

Tarratine Club is a charming and historic bed and breakfast, located in the heart of Belfast. The inn features beautiful period decor, comfortable rooms and suites, and a delicious breakfast.

Fireside Inn & Suites

Fireside Inn & Suites is a great option for those looking for a comfortable stay, with amenities such as a heated indoor pool, fitness center, and an on-site restaurant.

Yankee Clipper Motel

Yankee Clipper Motel is a budget-friendly option, located just minutes from downtown Belfast. The motel features comfortable and clean rooms, with easy access to all the attractions in Belfast.

What to Buy in Belfast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maine Made Crafts

Maine Made Crafts are a unique and authentic souvenir to bring home, showcasing the talent and creativity of local artisans and craftspeople.

Lobster-Related Gifts

Lobster-Related Gifts are another popular souvenir, with a range of items such as lobster traps, bibs, and even lobster-themed clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Belfast

Visitors to Belfast will find locals friendly and welcoming. It’s common courtesy to greet locals with a smile and a friendly hello. Tipping is customary in restaurants and bars, with a standard rate of 15-20% for good service.

Best Time to Visit Belfast

The best time to visit Belfast is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Fall is also a great time to visit Belfast, with the gorgeous foliage providing a picture-perfect setting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belfast

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife
  • Pack for all types of weather, as the climate can be unpredictable
  • Try the lobsters and other locally sourced seafood

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural environment
  • Take rocks or other natural souvenirs from the parks or beaches
  • Leave valuables in your car

Safety and Health

Safety in Belfast

Belfast is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings, particularly in areas with higher vehicle and foot traffic. Locking up your car and valuables is always recommended.

Health in Belfast

Belfast has excellent medical facilities, with Waldo County General Hospital providing top-notch care for both visitors and locals. Visitors should ensure they have health insurance coverage before their trip.

Getting to Belfast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belfast

The closest major airport to Belfast is Bangor International Airport, approximately 45 minutes away. Portland International Jetport is also within driving distance, approximately 2 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Belfast

Concord Coach Lines provides regular bus service to the area from Boston and other major cities in Maine.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Belfast, with the town being located off of US Route 1.

How to Get Around in Belfast

Belfast is a walkable town, with many attractions within walking distance from the downtown area. Visitors can also rent bicycles or cars to explore the parks and natural attractions in and around Belfast.

Where to Go Next from Belfast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Acadia National Park

Acadia National Park is a must-see destination in Maine, offering stunning landscapes, hiking and biking trails, and wildlife watching opportunities. The park is approximately 2.5 hours away from Belfast.

Camden

Camden is a charming coastal town, known for its picturesque harbor, fabulous restaurants, and unique boutiques. Camden is approximately 30 minutes away from Belfast.

Conclusion

Belfast, Maine is a charming coastal town, with plenty of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to Belfast, from exploring the local history and culture to hiking and biking in the beautiful natural landscapes. Don’t forget to try the local lobsters, and take home a souvenir crafted by a local artisan. Enjoy your trip to Belfast!
