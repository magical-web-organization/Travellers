Introduction to Searsport

Located in Waldo County, in the Mid Coast region of Maine, Searsport is a picturesque coastal town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture and scenic beauty. With its rich maritime history and natural charm, Searsport is the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Maine experience.

List of Places to Visit in Searsport

Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast or simply seeking a tranquil coastal escape, Searsport has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Searsport has a rich history that is well-preserved in its many museums and historical sites. A few must-visit places include:

  • Penobscot Marine Museum: offers insight into the town’s maritime history through exhibits on boatbuilding, lobstering, and fishing.
  • Maine Maritime Academy Museum: showcases models of historic ships and charts of Maine’s coast
  • Moose Point State Park: features a scenic coastal trail, picnic areas, and a beach.

Natural Attractions of Searsport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Searsport’s natural beauty offers a variety of outdoor activities for all ages, including:

  • Sears Island: a popular spot for hiking, nature walks, and enjoying stunning views of the Penobscot Bay.
  • Belfast Rail Trail: a 2.3-mile trail for walking, running, and biking.
  • Bayside Village: a quaint, charming village with stunning views of the bay and a small beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Searsport

Searsport has some of Maine’s most scenic hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Sears Island Loop Trail: A 6-mile trail that offers stunning views of Penobscot Bay.
  • Moose Point State Park Trail: A 1.2-mile loop trail that offers views of the rocky coastline.

Best Things to Do in Searsport: Activities and Tours

From exploring maritime history to enjoying the natural beauty of the Maine coast, Searsport has many activities and tours. A few must-do activities include:

  • Penobscot Bay History Center: A great place to learn about the region’s maritime history.
  • Searsport Pines Golf Course: A beautiful 9-hole golf course surrounded by pine trees.
  • Heron Ridge Yoga and Meditation Center: Offers yoga, meditation, and spiritual wellness classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Searsport

Day 1: Visit the Penobscot Marine Museum and the Maine Maritime Academy Museum.

Day 2: Hike the Sears Island Loop Trail and take a picnic.

Day 3: Explore the shops and cafes of Downtown Searsport.

Day 4: Take a sunset cruise tour on the Penobscot Bay.

Day 5: Visit a local winery or brewery in nearby Lincolnville.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive along the Maine coast and visit Bayside Village.

Day 7: Relax at the beach in Moose Point State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Searsport

Searsport offers plenty of options when it comes to dining and drinking, from seafood to local brews.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: A Maine classic, fresh lobster meat served on a toasted hot dog bun.
  • Blueberry Pie: A Maine staple, made with fresh Maine blueberries.
  • Haddock Chowder: A seafood chowder made with fresh haddock and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard Brewing Company: A local brewery that produces a variety of craft beers.
  • Savage Oakes Vineyard and Winery: Offers tastings of local wines.

Where to Stay in Searsport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Searsport has accommodations to fit all budgets, from camping to luxury hotels. A few recommended places to stay include:

  • Homeport Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast with ocean views.
  • Captain Nickels Inn: A luxurious historic mansion with nine rooms.
  • Searsport Shores Ocean Camping: A family-friendly oceanfront campground.

What to Buy in Searsport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Searsport is known for its maritime history and local handcrafted products. A few things to consider bringing home include:

  • Woodcarvings: Hand-carved wooden figures that depict the local wildlife and maritime themes.
  • Maine Blueberry Jam: A sweet and tangy spread made with fresh Maine blueberries.
  • Local Artwork: Many local artists create paintings, sculptures, and pottery that feature the beautiful Maine coast.

Local Culture and Customs of Searsport

Maine is known for its friendly, laid-back culture, and Searsport is no exception. Visitors are encouraged to engage with the locals and enjoy the town’s slow-paced way of life.

Best Time to Visit Searsport

The best time to visit Searsport is from June to August when the weather is warm, and the town comes alive with outdoor concerts, festivals, and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Searsport

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and beer/wine.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Searsport, including the Penobscot Bay.
  • Do engage with the friendly locals and ask for recommendations.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing if visiting in the off-season.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the beaches or hiking trails.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help or directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Searsport

Searsport is a safe destination, but visitors are encouraged to follow standard safety precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Searsport

Searsport has several medical facilities, including a hospital and walk-in clinics. Visitors should also take precautions against tick-borne illnesses if hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Searsport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Searsport

The closest airport to Searsport is the Bangor International Airport, which is about 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Searsport

Concord Coach Lines operates a bus service that stops in Belfast, which is a 15-minute drive from Searsport.

Other Options

Visitors may also drive to Searsport or take a taxi or rideshare from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Searsport

Searsport is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Car rentals, taxis, and rideshare services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Searsport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Searsport is located in the heart of Maine’s Mid Coast region, which offers plenty of nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Bar Harbor: Known for Acadia National Park and charming coastal towns.
  • Camden: A beautiful seaside town known for its picturesque harbor and locally-owned boutiques.
  • Rockland: A vibrant art community known for its galleries and museums.

Conclusion

With its rich maritime history, scenic views, and friendly locals, Searsport is the perfect destination for anyone seeking an authentic Maine experience. From exploring the town’s historic sites to enjoying the natural beauty of the coast, Searsport has something for everyone.
