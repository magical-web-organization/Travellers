Introduction to St. Johnsbury

St. Johnsbury is a cozy little town located in Vermont’s Northeast Kingdom, in New England, United States of America. The town is renowned for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, cultural treasures, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are a history buff, culture seeker, nature lover, or adventure junkie, there are countless reasons to visit St. Johnsbury.

List of Places to Visit in St. Johnsbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Johnsbury is a town that is steeped in rich history, and one of the best ways to explore this history is by visiting its museums and historical places. Here are some of the main culturally important attractions to visit:

  • Fairbanks Museum & Planetarium: a natural history museum that houses a rich collection of artifacts and contains one of the oldest and largest planetariums in the country.
  • St. Johnsbury Athenaeum: a historic library and art gallery that features works by famous artists like Albert Bierstadt, Winslow Homer, and James McNeill Whistler.
  • Old Stone House Museum: an eighteenth-century Georgian house that houses a vast collection of artifacts and memorabilia dating back to the early days of St. Johnsbury.

Natural Attractions of St. Johnsbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St. Johnsbury is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, and there is no shortage of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. Here are some of the natural attractions to visit:

  • Dog Mountain: a 150-acre park featuring trails, ponds, and picnic areas. It is also home to the famous Dog Chapel, a spiritual retreat for dogs and their owners.
  • Burke Mountain: a famous ski resort located in the heart of the Green Mountains. It offers an array of winter sports activities, including skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing.
  • Mount Pisgah: a scenic hike that offers stunning views of St. Johnsbury and the neighboring towns.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Johnsbury

St. Johnsbury is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in Vermont. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Lamoille Valley Rail Trail: a 93-mile trail that runs from St. Johnsbury to Swanton. It is great for hiking, biking, cross-country skiing, and snowmobiling.
  • The Kingdom Trail: a 100-mile trail system that is perfect for mountain biking, hiking, and trail running. It offers stunning views of the Green Mountains and the hills of the Northeast Kingdom.
  • Mount Hor and Tower Trail: a scenic hike that leads to the Mount Hor fire tower and provides panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in St. Johnsbury: Activities and Tours

St. Johnsbury offers an array of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in St. Johnsbury:

  • Brewery Tours: Visit one of Vermont’s best breweries and sample some of the finest craft beers in town.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Take a trip to Burke Mountain Resort and experience some of Vermont’s best skiing and snowboarding.
  • St. Johnsbury Farmers’ Market: Shop for locally sourced food, plants, and crafts at the weekly Farmers’ Market every Saturday.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Johnsbury

Here is a week-long itinerary that covers the best things to see and do in St. Johnsbury:

  • Day 1: Arrive in St. Johnsbury and take a stroll around the town.
  • Day 2: Visit the Fairbanks Museum & Planetarium and the St. Johnsbury Athenaeum.
  • Day 3: Take a hike on Mount Pisgah.
  • Day 4: Explore Dog Mountain and the neighboring towns.
  • Day 5: Take a tour of a local brewery.
  • Day 6: Go skiing or snowboarding at Burke Mountain Resort.
  • Day 7: Shop for locally sourced food at the St. Johnsbury Farmers’ Market.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Johnsbury

St. Johnsbury offers a range of culinary delights, from farm-to-table restaurants to local breweries. Here are some of the dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup: Vermont is famous for its maple syrup, and St. Johnsbury is no exception. Try the maple syrup pancakes at the St. Johnsbury House.
  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese: Visit the Vermont Cheese House and sample some of the best cheddar cheese in town.
  • Local Beef and Pork: Visit the Joe’s Brook Farm and sample some of the locally-raised beef and pork.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Visit Kingdom Brewery or Red Barn Brewery and sample some of the finest craft beers in town.
  • Cider: Visit Cold Hollow Cider Mill and try their famous cider.

Where to Stay in St. Johnsbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Maplewood Lodge: a luxurious guesthouse that offers spacious rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in St. Johnsbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Johnsbury is home to a range of local products and souvenirs that you can take home to commemorate your trip. Here are some of the best items to buy:

  • Handmade Crafts: Shop for handmade crafts like pottery, jewelry, and textiles at the local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Johnsbury

St. Johnsbury is a town that values its culture and traditions. Here are some of the local customs that you should be aware of:

  • Dress Code: St. Johnsbury is a laidback town, so casual attire is acceptable at most places.
  • Tipping: Tipping is the norm in St. Johnsbury, and the standard tip is 15% of the total bill.
  • Language: English is the primary language spoken in St. Johnsbury.

Best Time to Visit St. Johnsbury

The best time to visit St. Johnsbury is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is mild and the town is bustling with activities. Winter is also a great time to visit, especially if you are interested in skiing and snowboarding. Spring and fall can be quieter, but the town’s natural beauty is at its peak during these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Johnsbury

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting St. Johnsbury:

Do:

  • Explore the town on foot to get a true sense of its charm and character.
  • Sample locally sourced food and drinks.
  • Respect the town’s natural beauty and leave no trace.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural landscapes.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.
  • Disturb wildlife or tamper with nature.

Safety and Health

St. Johnsbury is a safe and healthy town to visit, but it is always good to be prepared and take the necessary precautions. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in St. Johnsbury

  • Lock your car and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight.
  • Stick to well-lit and populated areas, especially at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in St. Johnsbury

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors.
  • Be aware of ticks and other insects that may transmit diseases.

Getting to St. Johnsbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Johnsbury is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near St. Johnsbury

  • Burlington International Airport: located 2 hours from St. Johnsbury.
  • Manchester Airport: located 2.5 hours from St. Johnsbury.
  • Boston Logan International Airport: located 3 hours from St. Johnsbury.

Bus Stations Near St. Johnsbury

  • Concord Coach Lines: connects St. Johnsbury to cities like Boston and New York City.
  • Vermont Translines: connects St. Johnsbury to cities like Burlington and Montpelier.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Rent a car from one of the local car rental agencies and drive to St. Johnsbury.
  • Private Shuttle: Book a private shuttle from the airport or bus station to St. Johnsbury.

Where to Go Next from St. Johnsbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
