Introduction to Northeast Kingdom

Northeast Kingdom is a sprawling region covering the northeast corner of Vermont. Its beautiful landscape of rolling hills, glimmering lakes, lush forests, and picturesque small towns makes it a charming and relaxing destination for nature and culture lovers. The region is also home to some of the best maple syrup, farm-to-table restaurants, and craft breweries in the country.

List of Places to Visit in Northeast Kingdom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fairbanks Museum & Planetarium: This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the natural history and science of Vermont and the world. The museum features exhibits on astronomy, weather, ecology, and ethnology, and also has a planetarium that offers immersive star shows.
  • Old Stone House Museum: This quintessential Vermont museum houses eight buildings on 55 acres of scenic land. Visitors can learn about life in the 19th century and explore exhibits on local history, art, and crafts.
  • Bread and Puppet Theater Museum: This unique museum features the famous Bread and Puppet Theater, a politically and socially outspoken theater group that has been performing in the Northeast Kingdom since the 1960s. The museum houses a collection of puppets, masks, and posters used in their performances.

Natural Attractions of Northeast Kingdom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Willoughby: This stunning glacial lake is surrounded by mountains and forests and offers visitors opportunities for kayaking, swimming, fishing, or simply enjoying the view. In the winter, the lake is also a popular destination for ice fishing and cross-country skiing.
  • Jay Peak Resort: This year-round resort is known for its excellent skiing and snowboarding trails, but also has an indoor waterpark, a golf course, and an ice-skating rink. Visitors can take the aerial tramway to the summit of the mountain for panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • White Mountains National Forest: Although this vast forest stretches across New Hampshire and Maine, the Vermont side of it offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, camping, and scenic drives. Visitors can explore the pristine wilderness trails and cascading waterfalls of the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeast Kingdom

  • Kingdom Trails: This extensive network of trails covers nearly 100 miles of terrain across the Northeast Kingdom. The trails are perfect for hiking, biking, or snowshoeing, and offer picturesque views of the forests, rivers, and mountains of the area.

Best Things to Do in Northeast Kingdom: Activities and Tours

  • Flume Gorge: The 800-foot-long gorge is a natural wonder that features towering granite walls, cascading waterfalls, and a covered bridge. Visitors can take a guided tour of the gorge and its surroundings or explore on their own.
  • Ben & Jerry’s Factory Tour: Although not technically in the Northeast Kingdom, this delicious tour is located only an hour away and is well worth the visit. Visitors can take a guided tour of the ice cream factory, sample different flavors, and learn about the company’s history and values.
  • Farm Tours: The Northeast Kingdom is home to some of the best farm-to-table restaurants and artisanal food producers in the country. Visitors can take guided tours of these local farms and learn about their sustainable practices, sample their delicious products, and even buy some to bring home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northeast Kingdom

Day 1 Visit the Fairbanks Museum & Planetarium, and explore the nearby St. Johnsbury Athenaeum, a historic library with beautiful architecture and artwork
Day 2 Hike the trails of Burke Mountain and ride the famous Kingdom Trails bike park
Day 3 Take a scenic drive through the White Mountains National Forest and stop for a picnic at one of the many overlooks
Day 4 Visit Lake Willoughby and take a kayaking tour of the lake
Day 5 Tour the Vermont Maple Museum and learn about the history and production of maple syrup
Day 6 Explore the Old Stone House Museum and nearby historic towns like Craftsbury and Greensboro
Day 7 Relax at the local spas and finish the trip with a cold craft beer at one of the many local breweries

Food and Drink Scene of Northeast Kingdom

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sweet and savory crepes: These thin pancakes are a local specialty, filled with fresh fruits, eggs, cheeses, and meats.
  • Vermont Fiddleheads: These edible ferns are a seasonal delicacy in the Northeast Kingdom, usually served steamed or stir-fried with butter or olive oil.
  • Maple Syrup: The region is famous for its maple syrup, which is used in many local dishes and can be bought in local farms and markets.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The Northeast Kingdom has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries offering tours and tastings. Some of the most popular local breweries include Hill Farmstead, Kingdom Brewing, and Jasper Hill Farm.
  • Apple Cider: The region is also known for its fresh and natural cider, which can be found in local orchards like Burtt’s Apple Orchard and Champlain Orchards.

Where to Stay in Northeast Kingdom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Burke Mountain Hotel & Conference Center: This year-round resort offers premium lodging, dining, and activities, including ski and bike tours, golf courses, and swimming pools.
  • Willoughvale Inn & Cottages: This charming lakeside inn offers cozy, comfortable rooms and cottages, as well as access to the lake kayaks and canoes.
  • Wildflower Inn: This welcoming family-owned inn offers spacious rooms decorated with regional themes, as well as delicious breakfasts and dinners at the onsite restaurant.

What to Buy in Northeast Kingdom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: The region’s most famous product is also the most popular souvenir, with many local farms and markets selling bottles of pure Vermont maple syrup.
  • Handmade Crafts: The Northeast Kingdom has a vibrant arts and crafts scene, with many local artists and artisans offering a variety of handmade products, from pottery and jewelry to textiles and woodwork.
  • Ice Cider: Made from apples that are naturally frozen on trees in the winter, ice cider is a sweet and flavorful beverage that is unique to the Northeast Kingdom.

Local Culture and Customs of Northeast Kingdom

The Northeast Kingdom is known for its strong sense of community, resilience, and tradition. The region has a rich history of farming, logging, and craftsmanship, and many of these cultural values are still prominent today. Some of the most popular local events and festivals that reflect these traditions include:

  • Bread and Puppet Theater Performances: This iconic theater group performs in various locations around the Northeast Kingdom and the world, using giant puppets and masks to promote social and political change.
  • Northeast Kingdom Fall Foliage Festival: This annual event celebrates the beauty and diversity of the region’s fall foliage, featuring local foods, crafts, and live music.
  • Lyndonville Annual Snowflake Festival: This winter festival is a highlight of the region’s snow season, offering ice skating, sledding, bonfires, and hot cocoa.

Best Time to Visit Northeast Kingdom

The Northeast Kingdom is a year-round destination, offering different attractions and activities depending on the season. The fall foliage season, from September to October, is one of the most popular times to visit, with panoramic views of the changing leaves. The winter season, from December to March, is perfect for skiing, snowboarding, snowshoeing, and other winter sports. The summer season, from June to August, is ideal for hiking, biking, swimming, and kayaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northeast Kingdom

Do:

  • Take advantage of the region’s many outdoor activities and natural attractions.
  • Support local farmers, artisans, and businesses by buying locally produced products.
  • Respect the environment by following Leave No Trace principles and staying on designated trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural areas by not following established rules or regulations.
  • Disrupt the peace and quiet of the local communities by making loud noises or disturbing the wildlife.
  • Underestimate the weather conditions and prepare accordingly, especially during winter or outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northeast Kingdom

The Northeast Kingdom is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates and welcoming communities. Visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as locking valuables in cars or keeping an eye on personal belongings.

Health in Northeast Kingdom

The region has top-notch healthcare facilities, including several hospitals and medical clinics. Visitors should also be aware of the higher elevation and potential for severe weather conditions, especially during winter. Drinking plenty of water and dressing properly for the weather are important for maintaining good health.

Getting to Northeast Kingdom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northeast Kingdom

The closest major airports to the Northeast Kingdom are the Burlington International Airport, located in Burlington, and the Boston Logan International Airport, located about a 3-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Northeast Kingdom

Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Vermont Translines, offer direct or connecting bus services to cities like Boston and New York. The closest bus station is located in St Johnsbury.

Other Options

Other transportation options include driving on scenic routes like Interstate 91 or using ride-sharing or taxi services. Rental cars are also available in Burlington and other major cities in Vermont.

How to Get Around in Northeast Kingdom

The best way to get around the Northeast Kingdom is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited outside the main towns. Visitors can also book private or guided tours from local companies or resorts.

Where to Go Next from Northeast Kingdom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Burlington: This vibrant college town is only a 50-minute drive away from the Northeast Kingdom and offers a variety of cultural and outdoor attractions, including a lively downtown area, a scenic waterfront, and numerous parks and museums.
  • White Mountains National Forest: This vast forest extends into neighboring New Hampshire and offers even more opportunities for hiking, driving, and exploring the natural landscape.
  • Montreal: This cosmopolitan metropolis is located only 2 hours away from the Northeast Kingdom and offers a unique blend of European and North American culture, with world-class dining, arts, and entertainment.

Conclusion

The Northeast Kingdom is a hidden gem in Vermont, offering visitors a relaxing and authentic New England experience. With its natural beauty, cultural richness, and delicious food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone to enjoy and explore.
