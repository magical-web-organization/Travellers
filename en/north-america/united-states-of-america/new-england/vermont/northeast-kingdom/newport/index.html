Newport, located in the Northeast Kingdom of Vermont, is a charming town that offers visitors a glimpse into the natural beauty of New England. Surrounded by mountains and pristine lakes, Newport offers a serene and picturesque setting, making it a perfect destination for those seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a relaxing getaway. In this comprehensive tour guide, we have compiled a list of the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Newport.

Introduction to Newport

Newport is a small, yet lively, town in the Northeast Kingdom region of Vermont. The town is surrounded by the rolling green hills and mountains of the region, and is located on the shore of Lake Memphremagog. The town boasts breathtaking natural beauty, outdoor recreation opportunities, and a rich cultural scene, making it a must-visit destination for travelers of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Newport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Goodrich Memorial Library: a historic library dating back to 1899.
  • The MAC Center for the Arts: showcases the works of local artists and holds regular exhibitions and events.
  • The Haskell Opera House: a historic theater and opera house located on the Vermont-Quebec border.

Natural Attractions of Newport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Memphremagog Ski Touring Foundation: a prime location for cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and hiking in the winter.
  • Jay Peak Resort: a popular ski destination in the winter and a hiking spot in the summer.
  • Lake Memphremagog: a pristine lake and a perfect spot for kayaking, fishing, or simply enjoying the stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newport

  • The Newport Bike Path: a 7.5-mile paved trail that travels through the town and along the shore of Lake Memphremagog.
  • The Long Trail: a 272-mile trail that traverses the entirety of Vermont and passes through Newport.

Best Things to Do in Newport: Activities and Tours

  • A guided tour of Newport’s historic downtown district, filled with quaint shops, local restaurants, and charming architecture.
  • A scenic boat tour of Lake Memphremagog, which offers stunning views of the mountains and surrounding landscape.
  • A visit to the Newport State Airport, where visitors can watch planes take off and land and learn about the history of aviation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newport

Day 1: Visit the Goodrich Memorial Library and hike the Newport Bike Path. Day 2: Take a guided tour of Newport’s historic downtown district, followed by a visit to the MAC Center for the Arts. Day 3: Go skiing or snowshoeing at the Memphremagog Ski Touring Foundation or Jay Peak Resort. Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Lake Memphremagog, either by boat or kayak. Day 5: Spend the day hiking along the Long Trail. Day 6: Visit the Haskell Opera House and take a scenic drive through the surrounding countryside. Day 7: Relax at a local spa or hot springs and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Newport

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple glazed donuts
  • Ice cider

Local Beverages

  • Vermont craft beer
  • Ice cider

Where to Stay in Newport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Newport City Inn and Suites: a budget-friendly hotel with a convenient location in downtown Newport.
  • The East Side Restaurant and Pub: a charming guesthouse with a relaxed, cozy atmosphere.
  • The Inn at Buck Hollow Farm: a luxurious bed and breakfast set in a scenic, rural setting.

What to Buy in Newport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup and maple candy
  • Vermont cheddar cheese
  • Handmade crafts and artwork from local artists

Local Culture and Customs of Newport

  • Vermont is known for its “dairy culture,” with farming and agriculture playing a significant role in the state’s economy and local identity.
  • Residents of Newport are known for their friendly and welcoming attitude toward visitors.
  • Many locals are involved in the arts scene, with regular exhibitions and performances taking place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Newport

The best time to visit Newport is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and outdoor activities are plentiful. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the foliage in the surrounding hills and forests is stunning and vibrant during this time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newport

Do:

  • Take advantage of the natural beauty of the area by exploring the hiking trails and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Sample the local food and drink, including Vermont cheddar cheese, maple syrup, and ice cider.
  • Take the time to explore the town’s unique cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack for both warm and cooler weather, as temperatures can fluctuate throughout the day and during different seasons.
  • Don’t litter or disrespect the natural beauty of the area.
  • Don’t forget to ask locals for recommendations on the best places to eat, drink, and explore.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newport

Newport is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors and securing valuables when leaving their accommodation.

Health in Newport

Visitors to Newport should be aware of the potential for sunburn and insect bites during the summer months. It is also a good idea to bring a first aid kit for minor injuries or illnesses.

Getting to Newport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Newport

  • Burlington International Airport: approximately 1.5 hours away by car.
  • Montreal-Trudeau International Airport: approximately 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Newport

  • Greyhound: the nearest station is in St. Johnsbury, approximately 30 minutes away by car.

Other Options

  • Rent a car: this is the most practical option for exploring the surrounding area and natural attractions.
  • Take a taxi or rideshare service from the nearest airport or bus station.

How to Get Around in Newport

Visitors can easily explore the town center by foot, but a car or bike rental is recommended for exploring the surrounding area and outdoor attractions.

Where to Go Next from Newport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Burlington: a charming city on the eastern shore of Lake Champlain, known for its vibrant arts scene and local food and drink.
  • Montreal: a bustling, cosmopolitan city with a rich history and culture, located just across the Vermont-Canadian border.

Conclusion

Newport, Vermont, offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor recreation opportunities. From hiking and skiing to exploring the town’s galleries and museums, visitors are sure to find something to suit their interests and preferences. So pack your bags and head to Newport to experience the best that Vermont has to offer!
