Westmore, Vermont, located in the picturesque Northeast Kingdom region, is a hidden gem known for its natural beauty, charming small-town atmosphere, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or simply a relaxing getaway, this quaint town has something to offer for everyone.

Introduction to Westmore

Westmore is a small town located in the Orleans County of Vermont. It is situated in the Northeast Kingdom region of the state, which is known for its scenic beauty consisting of hills, forests, lakes, and rivers. Westmore town is named after the British Earl of Westmoreland.

List of Places to Visit in Westmore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the history and culture of Westmore, be sure to visit some of the following museums and historical landmarks:

  • Old Stone House Museum: This museum is located in nearby Brownington and features exhibits on local history and architecture.

  • Peterkin Camp and Conference Center: This camp offers accommodations as well as a variety of cultural and educational programs for visitors of all ages.

  • Bread and Puppet Museum: This museum in nearby Glover celebrates the art of puppetry and presents engaging performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Westmore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Westmore is known for its rugged beauty that offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation and adventure, including:

  • Lake Willoughby: This pristine glacial lake is surrounded by steep cliffs and forested slopes, with public beaches and boat rentals available during the summer months.

  • Bald Mountain Trail: One of the most popular hiking trails in the area, the Bald Mountain Trail offers breathtaking views of Lake Willoughby and the surrounding mountains at the summit.

  • Mount Pisgah: This nature reserve features a network of trails that allow visitors to explore the forested slopes of the mountain and enjoy stunning views of Lake Willoughby.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westmore

Westmore is a playground for hikers and bikers, offering a vast selection of scenic trails and routes. Here are some of the best:

  • Long Trail: This hiking trail passes through Westmore and offers challenging terrain and stunning views of the Green Mountains.

  • Darling Hill Road: This scenic route is popular among bikers, featuring rolling hills and picturesque farmland with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Westmore: Activities and Tours

Westmore offers a variety of activities and tours to keep you entertained during your visit, including:

  • Fishing: Lake Willoughby is known for its excellent fishing, with a variety of fish such as lake trout, salmon, and bass available.

  • Paddling: Rent a kayak or canoe to explore the calm waters of Lake Willoughby.

  • Horseback riding: Several farms in the area offer guided trail rides through the scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westmore

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Westmore:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Westmore, check into your hotel or rental property, and explore the town.
Day 2 Hike the Bald Mountain Trail and take in the stunning views of Lake Willoughby.
Day 3 Go fishing or paddling on Lake Willoughby.
Day 4 Visit the Old Stone House Museum and learn about the history of the area.
Day 5 Take a horseback ride at a local farm.
Day 6 Visit the Bread and Puppet Museum in Glover.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the scenery before departing Westmore.

Food and Drink Scene of Westmore

Westmore has a surprisingly diverse food scene, offering fresh local ingredients and delicious regional specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup: Vermont is famous for its high-quality maple syrup, which can be found at local farmers’ markets throughout the area.

  • Cabot Cheese: This award-winning cheese is produced by the Cabot Creamery Cooperative and can be found at many local shops and markets.

Local Beverages

  • Hill Farmstead Brewery: This world-renowned craft brewery is located in nearby Greensboro and offers tours and tastings.

  • Citizen Cider: This local cidery produces a variety of delicious hard ciders using locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Westmore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Westmore offers a variety of accommodations to suit any budget and preference, including:

  • Westmore Motel: This motel offers clean, comfortable rooms with beautiful views of Lake Willoughby.

  • Willoughby Haven: This lakeside inn offers spacious suites and cozy cabins with private decks and hot tubs.

  • Airbnb: There are many unique and affordable vacation rentals available on Airbnb, including cozy cabins, lakeside cottages, and modern apartments.

What to Buy in Westmore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back from Westmore:

  • Maple Syrup: Vermont produces some of the best maple syrup in the world, so be sure to bring back a bottle or two to enjoy at home.

  • Cabot Cheese: This award-winning cheese is a Vermont staple and makes for a delicious souvenir.

  • Handmade crafts: Westmore is home to several talented artisans and crafters, so consider picking up some locally-made pottery, jewelry, or textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Westmore

Westmore is known for its strong sense of community and rural way of life. Residents are proud of their town’s natural beauty and cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to take part in local traditions and events.

Best Time to Visit Westmore

The best time to visit Westmore depends on your interests and preferences. The summer months are ideal for outdoor activities and festivals, while the fall foliage season offers stunning views of the changing leaves. Winter sports enthusiasts will enjoy the scenic ski slopes and snowmobile trails during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westmore

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.

  • Support local businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources.

  • Ignore posted signs and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westmore

Westmore is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Westmore

Westmore has several medical facilities and pharmacies available within a short drive of the town. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for ticks and Lyme disease when hiking in wooded areas.

Getting to Westmore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westmore

The nearest major airport is Burlington International Airport, which is about 80 miles south of Westmore.

Bus Stations Near Westmore

The nearest major bus station is in Montpelier, which is about 55 miles southwest of Westmore.

Other Options

Visitors may also choose to drive or rent a car to get to Westmore.

How to Get Around in Westmore

Westmore is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding countryside and visiting nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Westmore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Westmore is located within driving distance of many popular Vermont destinations, including:

  • Stowe: This charming ski town is known for its picturesque streets, gourmet restaurants, and luxury resorts.

  • Lake Champlain: This scenic lake is ideal for sailing, fishing, and watersports, and is also home to several historic sites and museums.

  • Burlington: Vermont’s largest city offers a vibrant arts and culture scene, as well as shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or a little of both, Westmore is an ideal destination for your next vacation. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming community, this charming town is sure to leave a lasting impression.
