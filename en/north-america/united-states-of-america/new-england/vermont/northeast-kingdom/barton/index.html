Introduction to Barton

Nestled in the heart of Vermont’s Northeast Kingdom, Barton is a charming small town known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreation opportunities, and rich history. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or a bit of both, there’s something for everyone in this idyllic corner of New England.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to make the most of your visit to Barton, from the top attractions to the best local food and drinks, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Barton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Orleans County Historical Society Museum: This fascinating museum offers a glimpse into the history of Orleans County, with exhibits covering everything from Native American history to the region’s agricultural and industrial heritage.
  • Crystal Lake State Park: This beautiful park features a sandy beach, picnic areas, and miles of hiking trails, all set against the backdrop of stunning views of Crystal Lake.
  • Lake Willoughby: Known as the “Lake Lucerne of America,” Lake Willoughby is a stunningly beautiful glacial lake surrounded by mountains, making it the perfect spot for hiking, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Great Vermont Corn Maze: As one of the largest corn mazes in the country, this is the perfect place to get lost in the beauty of Vermont’s natural landscape.

Natural Attractions of Barton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bread and Puppet Theatre: Located in nearby Glover, this legendary theater company offers performances, workshops, and festivals featuring giant puppets, masks, and political satire.
  • Burke Mountain Resort: This popular ski resort is known for its challenging terrain and stunning views of the surrounding mountains, making it a must-visit for adventure seekers and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Jay Peak Resort: Another top ski resort in the area, Jay Peak offers a variety of outdoor activities in addition to skiing, such as golfing, hiking, and mountain biking.
  • Barton River: This tranquil river is the perfect spot for kayaking, canoeing, or just relaxing by the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barton

Barton Bike Trail

This beautiful bike trail meanders through the heart of Barton, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside and a chance to experience the region’s natural beauty up close.

Willoughby State Forest

Featuring miles of hiking trails through pristine forests and scenic vistas, Willoughby State Forest is a great spot for a day hike or overnight camping trip.

Best Things to Do in Barton: Activities and Tours

  • Green Mountain Railroad: Hop aboard this vintage train for a scenic journey through the heart of the Northeast Kingdom, featuring stunning views of Vermont’s beautiful landscapes.
  • Maple Syrup Tours: No visit to Vermont is complete without sampling some of the state’s famous maple syrup, and these tours give visitors a chance to learn about the syrup-making process while tasting some of the best syrup in the world.
  • Dog Mountain: This unique spot is a must-visit for dog lovers, with its sprawling fields, ponds, and hiking trails designed for dogs and their owners to enjoy together.
  • Jay Peak Ice Arena: If you’re visiting Barton in the winter months, be sure to check out the Jay Peak Ice Arena for ice skating, hockey games, and much more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barton

Day 1 Explore the town of Barton and visit the Orleans County Historical Society Museum.
Day 2 Hike the trails at Willoughby State Forest and enjoy a picnic lunch by Lake Willoughby.
Day 3 Bike the Barton Bike Trail and stop for lunch at The Parson’s Corner.
Day 4 Enjoy a day of skiing at Burke Mountain Resort.
Day 5 Take a Maple Syrup Tour and visit a local sugarhouse.
Day 6 Spend the day at Jay Peak Resort, skiing, snowboarding, or relaxing at the resort spa.
Day 7 Take a scenic train ride on the Green Mountain Railroad.

Food and Drink Scene of Barton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: Nothing compares to the sweetness of pure Vermont maple syrup, which is the perfect addition to pancakes, waffles, or even as a glaze for roasted meats.
  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese: This creamy, tangy cheese is a Vermont staple and is perfect for snacking, grilling, or melting on top of a burger.
  • Vermont Maple Cream Pie: A decadent dessert made with rich maple cream and a flaky crust, this is the perfect way to cap off a meal in Barton.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Craft Beer: Vermont is famous for its craft beer scene, and breweries like Hill Farmstead and The Alchemist are popular stops for beer lovers visiting the area.
  • Vermont Cider: Whether you prefer sweet or dry, Vermont’s cider scene is second to none, with many local orchards producing delicious artisanal ciders.

Where to Stay in Barton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crystal Lake State Park

Featuring campsites overlooking the lake and cabins nestled in the woods, Crystal Lake State Park is a great option for those looking to immerse themselves in nature.

Crystal Lake Falls Bed and Breakfast

This charming bed and breakfast offers beautiful rooms with stunning views of Crystal Lake and the surrounding mountains, as well as a delicious homemade breakfast served each morning.

What to Buy in Barton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: A must-buy for anyone visiting Vermont, pure maple syrup is a delicious reminder of your trip to Barton.
  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese: Stock up on some of Vermont’s famous cheddar cheese to enjoy at home, or bring back as gifts for family and friends.
  • Vermont Crafts: From hand-carved wooden bowls to hand-knitted sweaters, Vermont is home to many talented artisans and crafters who produce beautiful and unique products.

Local Culture and Customs of Barton

Vermonters are known for their hospitality and down-to-earth nature, so don’t be surprised if you strike up a conversation with a local during your visit. Respect for nature and the environment is also a core value in Vermont, so be sure to practice Leave No Trace principles when enjoying the great outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Barton

While each season has its own charm, the best time to visit Barton is in the fall, when the foliage is at its peak. If you’re visiting in the winter months, be sure to pack warm clothing and be prepared for snow and ice on the roads and trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barton

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and practice Leave No Trace principles.
  • Sample some of Vermont’s famous local products, such as maple syrup and cheddar cheese.
  • Take time to explore the region’s rich history and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment in any way.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or fellow travelers.
  • Trespass on private property or areas that are off-limits to visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barton

Vermont is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the same safety precautions as they would in any other unfamiliar area, including locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Barton

Barton is home to several medical clinics and hospitals, so visitors should have no trouble finding medical care if needed. However, it’s always a good idea to travel with basic first aid supplies, just in case.

Getting to Barton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barton

The nearest airports to Barton are Burlington International Airport and Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, both of which are about two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Barton

There are no bus stations in Barton, but visitors can take a bus to nearby Newport or St. Johnsbury and then rent a car or take a taxi to Barton.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Barton, as the town is located near several major highways. Rental cars are available at the nearby airports and in the town of St. Johnsbury.

How to Get Around in Barton

The best way to get around Barton is by car, as many of the top attractions are spread out across the region. However, visitors can also bike or walk along the Barton Bike Trail or take a scenic train ride on the Green Mountain Railroad.

Where to Go Next from Barton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stowe: This charming mountain town is known for its ski resorts, hiking trails, and cozy bed and breakfasts.
  • Montpelier: As the capital of Vermont, Montpelier is home to many historic monuments and cultural attractions, as well as several top-notch restaurants and breweries.
  • Burlington: Located on the shores of Lake Champlain, Burlington is a bustling college town known for its vibrant music and arts scene, as well as its beautiful parks and outdoor spaces.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Barton offers plenty of things to see and do that are sure to create lasting memories. From the town’s rich history to its beautiful natural landscapes and delicious local food and drinks, Barton truly has something for everyone. So pack your bags, hit the road, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in one of the most charming corners of New England.
