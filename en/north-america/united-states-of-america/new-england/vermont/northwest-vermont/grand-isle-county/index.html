Introduction to Grand Isle County

Grand Isle County is situated in northwest Vermont in the United States. Grand Isle County is located between the Adirondack Mountains of New York and the Green Mountains of Vermont. To the west is Lake Champlain and to the east is the Green Mountains. Grand Isle County is the second smallest county in Vermont, but it is big on natural beauty and outdoor recreation activities. This travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know about planning your trip to Grand Isle County, Vermont.

List of Places to Visit in Grand Isle County

If you’re visiting Grand Isle County, you’ll find plenty of places to visit and things to do to keep you busy.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Lake Champlain Maritime Museum: This museum offers various exhibits and interactive displays depicting the lives of people who lived along Lake Champlain.

  2. Grand Isle Art Works: There are plenty of opportunities to browse through art galleries, and Grand Isle Art Works is one of the best.

  3. South Hero Historical Society: This museum provides visitors with an insight into the rural, agricultural way of life that had existed in Grand Isle County.

Natural Attractions of Grand Isle County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. North Hero State Park: Enjoy a picnic at the scenic park while gazing at the fantastic views of the Lake Champlain while relaxing with family and friends.

  2. Alburg Dunes State Park: This is an ideal place for swimming along the sandy beach, windsurfing and water skiing.

  3. Grand Isle State Park: Hiking, biking, swimming, and picnicking are popular activities here. Bring your pets to run along the sandy shores and amazing parks and trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grand Isle County

Grand Isle County boasts some of the most beautiful scenic byways and byways just ideal for indulging in nature. Some of the hiking, biking, and walking routes are:

  • The South Hero Recreation Path: This trail is a 4.5-mile paved route for walkers, runners, and bikers, with stunning views of the lake and coastline.

  • The Missisquoi Valley Rail Trail: A 26-mile-long trail for hiking, biking, and horseback riding with plenty of opportunities to take a rest and enjoy the natural beauty along the way.

  • Island Line Trail: This trail in Burlington is a converted rail trail that extends 14 miles to the Colchester Causeway, offering stunning views of the water.

Best Things to Do in Grand Isle County: Activities and Tours

After a day enjoying the great outdoors, it’s time to take part in some activities and tours.

  1. Champlain Islands Farmers Market: One of the popular attractions in Grand Isle County is the Champlain Islands Farmers Market, which offers locally sourced produce to its guests from the nearby farms and vineyards.

  2. Lake Champlain Fishing Adventures: This is a recreational fishing adventure on Lake Champlain for Bass, Lake Trout, Salmon and more.

  3. Kayaking and Watersports: These activities can be found on various bays and coastline throughout Grand Isle County.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grand Isle County

If you’re planning to spend a week in Grand Isle County, here is a suggested itinerary to make your planning easier:

Food and Drink Scene of Grand Isle County

Vermont has a lot to offer when it comes to food and drinks, and Grand Isle County is no exception.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Vermont Maple Syrup: There are plenty of maple syrup options to be found in Grand Isle.

  2. Invitation to Tea: Enjoy a range of specialty teas accompanied by scones and sandwiches on North Hero Island.

  3. Fiddleheads: A local favorite seasonal vegetable dish in springtime.

Local Beverages

  1. Vermont Craft Breweries: With over 50 craft breweries in Vermont, there is no shortage of beer options for beer lovers.

  2. Vermont Wines: There are vineyards in Grand Isle County and nearby regions to produce wines, ciders, and spirits.

Where to Stay in Grand Isle County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grand Isle County has several accommodation options to fit any lifestyle and budget.

  1. Lake Champlain House Bed and Breakfast: A cozy bed and breakfast located on the shores of Lake Champlain in the town of Alburg.

  2. Green Cottage on Lake Champlain: A Small Green Cottage with beach access to Lake Champlain. It’s located in Grand Isle with beautiful sights of its surroundings.

  3. Whitcomb House: A grand 1800s historic Guesthouse with many rooms to accommodate people in the Grand Isle area.

What to Buy in Grand Isle County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Grand Isle County will find a variety of souvenirs to take home, along with many locally made products to bring back memories of their trip.

  1. Maple Syrup: You can buy maple syrup at any nearby stores in Grand Isle or directly purchase them at the nearby Maple Sugarhouses.

  2. Vermont Cheese: Vermont is known as the cheese capital of New England, and Grand Isle County also produces cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Grand Isle County

The locals of Grand Isle County are friendly and hospitable. However, being a rural county, make sure to drive safely and not litter in the parks and roadways.

Best Time to Visit Grand Isle County

The best time to visit Grand Isle County is during the warmer months of May through October when you’ll have plenty of opportunities to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grand Isle County

Do:

  • Do Respect the natural beauty of Grand Isle County.

  • Do Travel in groups and carry emergency supplies, including a first aid kit.

  • Do Support local businesses and buy locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Don’t throw litter on the trails, parks, and roadways.

  • Don’t disturb wildlife.

  • Don’t trespass on private property.

## Safety and Health

Safety in Grand Isle County

Grand Isle County is a safe place to visit as long as visitors follow basic safety practices. Visitors should avoid getting too close to water bodies alone and should explore the parks during daylight hours, avoiding the trails and parks during the night.

Health in Grand Isle County

Visitors can find medical assistance at the Grand Isle Health Center or the University of Vermont Medical Center in Burlington.

Getting to Grand Isle County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grand Isle County

The nearest airport is the Burlington International Airport, which is 35 miles south of Grand Isle County.

Bus Stations Near Grand Isle County

Vermont Translines offers transportation from Burlington to Grand Isle County.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option may be best for those looking to explore Grand Isle County more freely.

How to Get Around in Grand Isle County

Walking, biking, and driving are the best options for getting around Grand Isle County. Consider renting a bicycle or car for a more pleasant experience.

Where to Go Next from Grand Isle County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip to explore nearby destinations within Vermont like Burlington, Stowe, and Montpelier.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or a peaceful retreat, Grand Isle County is the perfect destination. Its natural beauty, food and drink scene and rich cultural attractions make it a must-visit place on your Vermont trip itinerary. Plan ahead for the best chance to enjoy all that the area has to offer.
