Introduction to Shelburne

Located in the northwestern part of Vermont, Shelburne is a small town that boasts of a rich historical and cultural heritage. With its natural beauty, well-preserved historical sites, and charming downtown, the town attracts visitors all year round. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just strolling in picturesque streets, Shelburne has something to offer to everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Shelburne, you’ll find everything you need to know to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this lovely town. From the top places to visit, to the best food and drink spots, to the cultural customs and safety tips, read on to discover all the exciting things that Shelburne has in store for you.

List of Places to Visit in Shelburne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shelburne Museum: This is a must-visit for any first-timer in Shelburne. The museum showcases a vast collection of Americana and folk art, including historic buildings such as a covered bridge and a lighthouse, and an array of exhibits featuring quilts, carriages, and other antiques.

  • The Ticonderoga: This beautifully restored steamboat is a National Historic Landmark, featuring intricate woodwork, brass fixtures, and a breathtaking view of Lake Champlain. Guided tours are available, offering an insight into the vessel’s fascinating history.

  • Webb Gallery: Housed in an 1820s building, Webb Gallery features a diverse collection of contemporary art, sculpture, jewelry, and ceramics.

  • Fisk Farm: A local landmark since the 1800s, Fisk Farm is now a living history museum with exhibits depicting the daily life of rural Vermont in the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Shelburne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shelburne Farms: A working farm that spans over 1,400 acres, Shelburne Farms offers visitors a chance to explore its many hiking trails, working farms, and award-winning restaurants, with stunning views of Lake Champlain.

  • Mt. Philo State Park: The highest point in Chittenden County, Mt. Philo State Park offers spectacular views of the Adirondack Mountains, Lake Champlain, and the Green Mountains. Hiking trails, picnic areas, and a playground make it a perfect spot for a family day out.

  • LaPlatte Nature Park: This 240-acre preserve features a network of trails through fields, woods, and wetlands, making it a popular spot for bird-watching, walking, and picnics.

  • Lake Champlain Maritime Museum: This museum on the shores of Lake Champlain teaches visitors about the history and ecology of the lake. Its collection includes a replica of a Revolutionary War gunboat and hands-on exhibits about steamboats and shipwrecks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shelburne

  • The Loop: This 4.5-mile trail circles the Shelburne village and offers stunning views of the Adirondacks and Lake Champlain.

  • Brewer Path: A 1.5-mile nature trail that winds through meadows and woods, ending at a scenic overlook of Lake Champlain.

  • LaPlatte River Trail: This 2.4-mile trail follows the LaPlatte River through woods and fields, and is a favorite of bird-watchers.

Best Things to Do in Shelburne: Activities and Tours

  • Burlington Brew Tour: A four-hour guided tour of Burlington’s best craft breweries, featuring tastings and behind-the-scenes tours.

  • Vermont Bike Tours: A family-friendly guided bike tour of Shelburne, with stops at local landmarks, farms, and wineries.

  • Shelburne Vineyard Tour: A guided tour of the vineyard, with tastings and a chance to learn about the winemaking process.

  • Ariel’s Floats: A river tubing adventure on the Winooski River, featuring scenic views and plenty of water fun.

Food and Drink Scene of Shelburne

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: A staple of Vermont cuisine, this sweet delight is the perfect topping for pancakes, waffles, or even on its own.

  • Craft Beer: With plenty of microbreweries in the area, the beer scene in Shelburne is thriving. Be sure to try some local favorites like Fiddlehead IPA or Zero Gravity Conehead Wheat IPA.

  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine: Shelburne’s restaurants proudly feature locally-sourced ingredients, including fresh produce and dairy from nearby farms.

  • Apple Cider Donuts: A staple of the fall season, these sweet treats can be found at local bakeries and farm stands across town.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Shelburne Vineyard is known for its award-winning wines, featuring locally-sourced grapes and a spectacular view of the Adirondacks.

  • Cider: From traditional off-dry to fruit-infused, cider lovers can find a variety of options at local breweries and cider mills.

  • Coffee: With artisanal roasters and cozy cafes, Shelburne’s coffee scene has something for everyone. Be sure to try the seasonal flavors at Vermont Coffee Company.

Where to Stay in Shelburne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Inn at Shelburne Farms: Located on the grounds of Shelburne Farms, this historic inn offers a stunning view of Lake Champlain, farm-to-table dining, and elegant rooms and suites.

  • Folly Hill Inn Bed & Breakfast: A Victorian-style bed and breakfast in the heart of Shelburne, providing cozy rooms, home-cooked breakfast, and a quiet atmosphere.

  • Heart of the Village Inn: This charming inn is just a short walk away from the Shelburne Museum, and features modern amenities, such as free Wi-Fi, in a historic setting.

What to Buy in Shelburne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: Bring home a bottle of the purest maple syrup in the world, straight from Vermont’s sugaring season.

  • Craft Beer: Stock up on the local microbrews to enjoy back home, or share with friends and family.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artists create unique pieces featuring Vermont’s natural beauty, such as Lake Champlain or fall foliage.

  • Stoneware and Pottery: Vermont artisans craft beautiful, functional pieces using local clay and featuring unique designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Shelburne

Shelburne’s residents take pride in their natural surroundings, local history, and artisanal craftsmanship. Visitors are encouraged to take the time to learn about these local customs, respect the environment, and support the small businesses that make Shelburne such a special place.

Best Time to Visit Shelburne

Shelburne’s natural beauty can be appreciated year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. For outdoor adventurers, the summer and early fall offer the most opportunities for hiking, biking, and watersports. For cultural enthusiasts, the Shelburne Museum’s peak season is from May to October, with special events and exhibits throughout. And for those looking for a cozy winter getaway, Shelburne’s picturesque downtown and local winter sports offer a charming winter retreat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shelburne

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment when exploring the hiking trails and parks.

  • Support local businesses by shopping at small boutiques, farmers’ markets, and cafes.

  • Take the time to learn about Shelburne’s history and cultural heritage, including the Champlain Valley and the Underground Railroad.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on the trails or in public areas.

  • Ignore traffic laws when bicycling, especially in downtown areas.

  • Visit during hunting season without taking proper precautions and wearing safety gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shelburne

Shelburne is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take basic precautions such as keeping valuables out of sight, locking doors and windows when leaving their accommodation, and being aware of their surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Shelburne

Shelburne has a range of medical facilities for residents and visitors, including a hospital, urgent care center, and a variety of doctors and specialists. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as ticks and sun exposure, and take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Shelburne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shelburne

  • Burlington International Airport: Located just 20 minutes north of Shelburne, this airport services several major airlines and offers direct flights to major hubs like New York City, Boston, and Chicago.

Bus Stations Near Shelburne

  • Burlington Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Burlington, this station offers daily service to several major cities in the Northeast and beyond.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several major car rental companies operate out of nearby airports and downtown Burlington.

  • Bicycle Rental: Several local bike shops offer rental services for visitors looking to explore Shelburne on two wheels.

How to Get Around in Shelburne

Shelburne is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the downtown area on foot. Bicycle and car rentals are also available for those looking to venture further afield. Local taxis and ride-sharing services are available, but may be limited outside the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Shelburne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Burlington: Just a short drive north, Burlington is Vermont’s largest city and features a thriving arts scene, vibrant nightlife, and fun outdoor activities.

  • Stowe: A picturesque mountain town about an hour east of Shelburne, Stowe is home to several ski resorts, charming boutiques, and a distillery trail.

  • Montreal: Across the Canadian border and a two-hour drive north, Montreal features a unique blend of French and Quebecois culture, including historic landmarks, museums, and delicious cuisine.

Conclusion

No matter what your interests, Shelburne, Vermont is a charming destination that provides something for everyone. From its natural beauty to its intriguing history and lively food scene, there’s always something new to discover. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip, and get ready to explore this picturesque town on the shores of Lake Champlain.
