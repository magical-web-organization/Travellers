Explore Williston, Vermont: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Introduction to Williston

Nestled in Chittenden County, in the northwest part of Vermont, Williston is a charming small town known for its beautiful natural scenery, diverse outdoor recreational activities, and rich local culture. Located in New England region of the United States of America, Williston offers an ideal vacation destination for nature enthusiasts, adventure travelers, history buffs, and foodies. Here is everything you need to know about exploring Williston, Vermont.

List of Places to Visit in Williston

Whether you’re looking for culturally important attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, or delicious food, Williston has something for everyone. Here is a list of top places to visit in Williston that you should not miss on your trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vermont Teddy Bear Factory: A popular tourist attraction, the Vermont Teddy Bear Factory features a teddy bear-making process tour, store, and outdoor picnic spot.
  • North Star Underground Railroad Museum: This museum offers an interactive experience of the Underground Railroad network, where abolitionists helped slaves escape to freedom during the 19th century.
  • Shelburne Museum: Located in nearby Shelburne, this museum showcases American art and design, folk art, and historical exhibits, including 39 buildings spread over 45 acres.

Natural Attractions of Williston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Muddy Brook Wetland Reserve: A hidden gem in Williston, this reserve offers a 2.2-mile hiking trail through wetlands and forests, where visitors can spot various bird species and wildlife.
  • Catamount Outdoor Family Center: This family-owned outdoor center offers an extensive network of trails for hiking, mountain biking, running, Nordic skiing, snowshoeing, and sledding.
  • Mt. Mansfield State Forest: Located in nearby Underhill, Mt. Mansfield is the highest peak in Vermont, with towering cliffs and stunning alpine vistas. Visitors can hike the famous Long Trail or take the gondola ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Williston

Williston is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with miles of scenic trails that wind through forests, lakes, and wetlands. Here is a list of some popular routes that you can explore:

  • Williston Community Park: A 2-mile multi-use trail that circles a lake and offers scenic views of the Adirondack Mountains.
  • Perry Hill Trails: A network of over 20 miles of singletrack trails for hiking and mountain biking, through rolling hills and dense forests.
  • Mud Pond Country Park: A 260-acre park with hiking trails, a pond, a meadow, and a historic sawmill site.

Best Things to Do in Williston: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Williston has several activities and tours that offer unique experiences for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Williston:

  • Vermont Brewery Tours: Discover the craft beer scene of Vermont with a guided brewery tour, where you can sample some of the finest artisanal beers in the state.
  • Vermont Farm Tours: Visit local farms and learn about the sustainable agriculture practices, and sample delicious farm-to-table food and drink.
  • Hot air balloon rides: Soar above the picturesque beauty of the Champlain Valley with a hot air balloon ride, and experience stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Williston

Williston features a diverse food and drink scene, with some of the freshest and most delicious locally-sourced dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont classics: Try some of the regional specialties, such as maple syrup, Vermont cheddar cheese, apple cider donuts, and farm-fresh produce.
  • Pizza: Head to Folino’s Pizza or Tala Cafe & Bakery for some of the best pies in town.
  • Seafood: Enjoy some fresh and flavorful seafood at South of the Border Seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Green Mountain Coffee Roasters: Sample some of the finest locally roasted coffee beans at the Green Mountain Coffee Roasters Café.
  • Vermont wine and cider: Explore the region’s wine and cider scene with a tasting at Shelburne Vineyard, Boyden Valley Winery, or Citizen Cider.

Where to Stay in Williston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Williston offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay in Williston:

  • The Essex Resort & Spa: A luxury hotel and spa featuring guest rooms and suites with stunning views, indoor and outdoor pools, and fine dining options.
  • Sonesta ES Suites Burlington: A family-friendly hotel with spacious suites, free breakfast, fitness center, and outdoor pool.
  • Williston Village Inn: A historic Inn with cozy rooms, complimentary breakfast, and easy access to hiking trails and cultural attractions.

What to Buy in Williston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Williston, don’t forget to take home some of the souvenirs and local products as a memory of your trip. Here are some items that you must shop for:

  • Maple Syrup and Honey: Vermont is renowned for its maple syrup and honey, and you can find a variety of flavored syrups and honey at local stores and farms.
  • Handmade Crafts and Gifts: Visit places like Artisans Hand, where you can find unique and handmade crafts, jewelry, and art by local artists.
  • Farm Produce: Get freshly-picked and organic farm produce, such as berries, tomatoes, sweet corn, and pumpkins, at local farmers’ markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Williston

Vermonters are known for their friendly and welcoming attitude, so make sure to strike up a conversation and make new friends. Vermont is also proud of its natural beauty and conservation efforts, so please respect the environment and take care of the surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Williston

The best time to visit Williston is from June to October when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is especially beautiful in Vermont, with stunning foliage colors and harvest festivals. However, winter can also be a great time to visit for skiing and snowsports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Williston

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Williston has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region, so make sure to get out and enjoy them.
  • Indulge in local cuisine: Vermont has a rich food culture, so don’t miss trying some of the regional specialties, such as maple syrup and cheddar cheese.
  • Check out the cultural attractions: From museums to historic sites, Williston has a lot to offer for history and culture lovers.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip: Tipping is customary in Vermont, so don’t forget to tip your servers and drivers.
  • Litter in the parks: Vermont is proud of its natural beauty, so please respect the environment and keep the parks clean.
  • Disrespect local customs: Show respect for the local culture and customs, and ask for permission before taking photos of people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Williston

Williston is a relatively safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always advisable to take standard precautions, such as locking your doors, not leaving valuables in your car, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Williston

Vermont has a high-quality healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in and around Williston. Make sure to have adequate travel insurance, and carry the necessary medications and first-aid supplies with you.

Getting to Williston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Williston is easily accessible by air, road, and public transportation.

Airports Near Williston

The Burlington International Airport is the nearest airport, located just 10 miles from Williston. You can also fly into Montpelier or Plattsburgh International airports, both within a 1-hour drive.

Bus Stations Near Williston

The Greyhound and Megabus stations are located in nearby Burlington, just 8 miles from Williston. Local bus services are available in the region as well.

Other Options

If you are driving, Williston can be reached via the I-89 highway and Route 2. If you prefer to travel by train, take the Amtrak to nearby Essex Junction station.

How to Get Around in Williston

The best way to get around Williston is by car or bike, as the town is spread out and public transportation options are limited. There are several car rental agencies in the area, and bike rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Williston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Williston is a great base to explore the rest of the state. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore:

  • Burlington: Vermont’s largest city, known for its vibrant arts scene, coffee shops, and restaurants.
  • Stowe: A charming New England town, famous for its ski resorts, hiking trails, and historic sites.
  • Lake Champlain Islands: A series of islands on Lake Champlain, offering a scenic drive, bike ride, or boating trip with breathtaking views.

Conclusion

Williston, Vermont may be a small town, but it offers a big experience for travelers seeking adventure, culture, and culinary delights. With its stunning natural beauty, abundant outdoor activities, and rich history and culture, Williston has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and get ready for an unforgettable Vermont experience!
