Introduction to South Burlington

South Burlington is a beautiful city located in Chittenden County, Northwest Vermont, in New England. It is known for its vibrant culture, natural beauty, and friendly locals. This city is a hub for outdoor activities, shopping, and dining. Whether you are a solo traveler, a group of friends or a family vacation, South Burlington has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in South Burlington

South Burlington is a small city, but it boasts a variety of attractions that draw visitors from all around. Here’s a list of must-visit places during your stay in this charming city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shelburne Museum

Shelburne Museum is a must-visit destination when in South Burlington. This museum boasts a vast collection of art and artifacts related to American history, including folk art, quilts, and textiles. The museum also features a beautiful garden that visitors can explore.

Ethan Allen Homestead Museum

Ethan Allen Homestead Museum is another culturally important attraction in South Burlington. This museum is dedicated to Ethan Allen, Vermont’s founding father. Visitors can explore the museum’s exhibits and learn about Allen’s life and legacy.

Natural Attractions of South Burlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Red Rocks Park

Red Rocks Park is a natural gem located on the shores of Lake Champlain. This park is famous for its stunning sunsets and panoramic views of the lake and the Adirondack Mountains. Visitors can enjoy hiking, picnicking, swimming, and fishing while taking in the breathtaking views.

Burlington Greenway

The Burlington Greenway is a trail that runs along the Lake Champlain shoreline and offers stunning views of the Adirondack Mountains. Visitors can enjoy biking, walking, or rollerblading on this scenic trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Burlington

South Burlington has hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

Mount Philo State Park

Mount Philo State Park is a popular destination for hikers and bikers. The park features a one-mile trail that takes visitors to the summit of Mount Philo. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of Lake Champlain and the Adirondack Mountains from the top of the mountain.

Burlington Bike Path

The Burlington Bike Path is a 7.5-mile trail that runs along Lake Champlain’s waterfront. The trail is perfect for biking, jogging and taking in the scenic views.

Best Things to Do in South Burlington: Activities and Tours

South Burlington offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best:

Lake Champlain Sunset Cruise

A Lake Champlain Sunset Cruise is a popular activity for tourists. Visitors can enjoy the stunning sunset over Lake Champlain while enjoying local cuisine and drinks.

Vermont Teddy Bear Company Tour

The Vermont Teddy Bear Company Tour is a unique tour, giving visitors an inside look at how these popular teddy bears are made. Visitors can see the teddy bear production process and even make their own bears to take home.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Burlington

If you’re planning a week-long trip to South Burlington, here’s a recommended itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Shelburne Museum and Ethan Allen Homestead Museum Day 2: Explore the Burlington Greenway and Lake Champlain Day 3: Hike or bike Mount Philo State Park Day 4: Take a day trip to Stowe and enjoy the skiing and outdoor activities Day 5: Visit the Ben & Jerry’s Factory Tour Day 6: Enjoy a Lake Champlain Sunset Cruise and try out local cuisine Day 7: Visit Burlington’s Church Street Marketplace for shopping and dining

Food and Drink Scene of South Burlington

South Burlington’s food scene is diverse, offering a variety of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

maple syrup

Maple syrup is the epitome of Vermont cuisine. It is used in everything from breakfast foods to desserts.

Vermont Cheddar Cheese

Vermont Cheddar Cheese is another must-try dish while in South Burlington. This cheese is handmade and aged, giving it a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

Vermont Craft Beer

Vermont is known for its craft beer scene, and South Burlington is no exception. There are several breweries in the area that offer a variety of beer options.

Vermont Maple Syrup Liqueur

Vermont Maple Syrup Liqueur is a popular drink that visitors can find in local bars and restaurants. It is made with locally sourced maple syrup and has a rich, sweet flavor.

Best Western Plus Windjammer Inn & Conference Center is a popular choice for visitors in South Burlington. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a lovely garden.

Lang House on Main Street

Lang House on Main Street is a bed and breakfast that promises its guests a comfortable stay with cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in South Burlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find a variety of souvenirs and local products in South Burlington. Here are some recommendations:

Vermont Maple Syrup

Vermont Maple Syrup is a popular souvenir that visitors can take home. Visitors can find it in local farms and shops throughout South Burlington.

Ben & Jerry’s Ice Cream

Ben & Jerry’s Ice Cream is another local favorite that visitors can take home. Visitors can visit the Ben & Jerry’s Factory Tour and enjoy a scoop or two while learning about how the ice cream is made.

Local Culture and Customs of South Burlington

South Burlington boasts a rich cultural heritage, including Native American, French, and English histories. The city celebrates its history and traditions through cultural events, festivals, and museums.

Best Time to Visit South Burlington

The best time to visit South Burlington is during the months of May to October when the weather is warm and pleasant. These months offer an excellent opportunity to explore the city’s outdoor destinations and scenic views.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Burlington
