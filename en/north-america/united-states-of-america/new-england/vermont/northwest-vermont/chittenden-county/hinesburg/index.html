Introduction to Hinesburg

Hinesburg is a small town situated in the heart of Chittenden County in Northwest Vermont. It is surrounded by picturesque mountains and scenic views, making it a perfect tourist destination. This town is known for its rich culture, beautiful landscapes, and a fascinating history that dates back to centuries ago.

In this article, we will guide you through the best places to visit, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, and other travel tips for a perfect trip to Hinesburg, Vermont.

List of Places to Visit in Hinesburg

Here’s a comprehensive list of places you should definitely visit in Hinesburg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hinesburg Historical Society Museum - This museum showcases the town’s rich history and allows visitors to see how the community has grown and changed over time.

  • Champlain Valley Alpacas - A unique attraction that offers visitors a chance to see alpacas in their natural habitat, and visitors can purchase alpaca fiber products.

  • Hinesburg Artist Series - This famous art series showcases the works of renowned artists from all over the world. Here, visitors can learn about the culture and art of Hinesburg.

Natural Attractions of Hinesburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Camel’s Hump State Park - This park is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. It offers various hiking trails, camping options, and stunning views of the mountains.

  • Hinesburg Town Forest - This forest offers visitors an opportunity to go on a peaceful hike while enjoying the beauty of nature.

  • Vermont Bicycle Tours - For bikers, this tour agency offers guided bike tours through Hinesburg and surrounding areas. Here, you can experience Hinesburg’s natural beauty while getting in some exercise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hinesburg

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure, Hinesburg has various hiking and biking routes for you to enjoy. Here are some of the best scenic routes:

  • Williston-Burlington Bike Path - This bike path offers a scenic route through the town of Hinesburg and leads you through some of the most beautiful scenery of Vermont.

  • Hinesburg Town Forest - It offers various hiking routes that are very easy to navigate and offers stunning views of the mountains.

  • Camel’s Hump State Park - Known for its hiking trails, this park provides a unique opportunity for visitors to enjoy the beauty of nature while experiencing the adrenaline of hiking.

Best Things to Do in Hinesburg: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Hinesburg, besides hiking and biking. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Vermont Zip Line Adventure Tours - For those looking for a more adventurous tour, this tour agency provides zip-lining tours through the lush greenery of Hinesburg.

  • Hinesburg Homebrew Festival - If you’re a beer enthusiast, this beer festival is for you. Here, you can taste excellent craft beer from local breweries.

  • Hinesburg Pumpkin Chuckin’ Festival - For an event that is both unique and exciting, head to the Hinesburg Pumpkin Chuckin’ Festival, where participants launch pumpkins through the air using various machines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hinesburg

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for a perfect trip to Hinesburg:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Hinesburg Historical Society Museum
2 Go hiking in Camel’s Hump State Park
3 Visit Champlain Valley Alpacas and learn about alpaca farming
4 Go biking with Vermont Bicycle Tours
5 Explore the Hinesburg Town Forest
6 Go zip-lining with Vermont Zip Line Adventure Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Hinesburg

Hinesburg is known for its vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Creamee - A frozen cream dessert made of local Vermont maple syrup.

  • Pho Noodle Soup - A Vietnamese soup made with rice noodles, broth, and meat.

  • Vermont Maple Syrup Donut - A donut made with Vermont’s famous maple syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Cider Company - This company produces fresh, hand-crafted hard cider made from local Vermont apples.

  • Hill Farmstead Brewery - This award-winning brewery produces high-quality craft beer made with locally sourced ingredients.

  • Shelburne Vineyard - Produces award-winning wines, and visitors can tour the vineyard and enjoy some wine tasting.

Where to Stay in Hinesburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best recommendations for places to stay in Hinesburg:

  • Sleepy Hollow Inn and B&B - A beautiful inn that offers excellent accommodations and breakfast.

  • ** Willard Street Inn** - A cozy bed and breakfast with lovely gardens and stunning views of the city.

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Burlington Vermont - A modern hotel with luxurious amenities.

What to Buy in Hinesburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Hinesburg:

  • Maple Products - Maple syrup, maple sugar, and maple candies are some of the best gifts you can take back home from Hinesburg.

  • Handmade Pottery - There are several local artists who sell beautifully crafted pottery made of locally sourced materials.

  • Alpaca Fiber Products - You can purchase products made from locally sourced alpaca fiber, including hats, scarves, and blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of Hinesburg

Vermont has a strong and unique culture that visitors can experience in Hinesburg. Here are a few things that are unique to the area:

  • Farm-to-Table Food Culture - Hinesburg is known for its farm-to-table culinary culture, sourcing ingredients locally and organically.

  • Vermont Maple Syrup - Vermont is famous for its maple syrup, and almost every local restaurant and cafe uses it in their dishes.

  • Abundance of Hiking Trails - Vermont has an extensive network of hiking trails, and Hinesburg offers an excellent starting point for accessing some of the best trails.

Best Time to Visit Hinesburg

The best time to visit Hinesburg is during the months of May through October, where visitors can experience the true beauty of Vermont’s natural scenery, and enjoy the outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hinesburg

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings and the rules of the hiking trails.

  • Take a tour of the local breweries and vineyards.

  • Sample the local cuisine, including dishes made with maple syrup.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the hiking trails or in public spaces.

  • Drive under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

  • Ignore traffic rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hinesburg

Hinesburg is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. Visitors should be mindful of their belongings and avoid keeping valuables in their cars.

Health in Hinesburg

Hinesburg has a good healthcare system in place, and visitors can get prompt medical attention if needed. The best way to stay healthy is by staying hydrated, following the rules of the hiking trails, and taking necessary precautions against tick-borne diseases.

Getting to Hinesburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some options for getting to Hinesburg:

Airports Near Hinesburg

  • Burlington International Airport - The closest airport to Hinesburg and is served by various airlines.

  • Montréal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport - Located about 2 hours from Hinesburg and is served by several international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Hinesburg

  • Greyhound Bus Station is conveniently located in Burlington and offers daily services from several major cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a good option for visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas.

  • Taxi or Shuttle Services - Several companies offer taxi or shuttle services for visitors who do not want to rent a car.

How to Get Around in Hinesburg

Visitors can get around Hinesburg by renting a car, taking taxis or shuttle services. Hiking and biking are also great options for getting around the town, especially during the summer months.

Where to Go Next from Hinesburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you plan to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations you can visit from Hinesburg:

  • Burlington - A vibrant city located 20 minutes drive from Hinesburg. You can visit various breweries, museums, and art galleries, stroll along the waterfront, and dine at local restaurants.

  • Lake Champlain Islands - A group of islands located north of Burlington, offering breathtaking views of the lake and a perfect destination for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing.

  • Mad River Valley - A valley located near Hinesburg, and it is known for its vibrant outdoor recreational activities such as skiing, hiking, and biking.

Conclusion

Hinesburg is a small town that offers plenty of outdoor activities, rich culture, and beautiful landscapes. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, history buff, foodie, or just looking for a peaceful getaway, Hinesburg has something for everyone.
