Introduction to Winooski

Nestled in the northwest of Vermont, Winooski is a charming city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Its name, which is derived from the Abenaki name for “onion”, is a nod to the onion-shaped gorge that runs through the city and into the Winooski River.

As you explore Winooski, you’ll discover a variety of attractions and activities that cater to both outdoor enthusiasts and culture-seekers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve highlighted some of the must-visit places, trails, and landmarks that define Winooski’s rich history and stunning landscape.

List of Places to Visit in Winooski

Here’s a rundown of some of the best places to visit in Winooski, grouped by their respective categories:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Keen Hall, one of the oldest standing structures in Winooski and the former home of the city’s first mayor
  • Champlain Mill, a historic mill that has been transformed into a thriving hub of shops, restaurants, and businesses
  • Winooski Blockhouse, a small military fortification that played a key role in the American Revolution
  • The Winooski Historical Society and Museum, which houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that celebrate the city’s rich history

Natural Attractions of Winooski: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Winooski Falls, a stunning set of cascades that can be seen from several overlooks
  • Salmon Hole Park, a picturesque park that boasts trails, picnic areas, and swimming holes
  • Ethan Allen Homestead, a former home of the famous Revolutionary War hero that offers tours and hiking trails
  • Gilbrook Nature Area, a tranquil woodland that’s perfect for birdwatching and hiking

Hiking/Biking Routes of Winooski

Winooski is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of scenic trails and bike routes that provide an immersive experience in nature. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Winooski include:

  • The Winooski Valley Park District, which offers over 1,000 acres of pristine natural beauty and miles of well-marked trails
  • The Colchester Causeway, a former railroad bed turned scenic bike path that offers stunning views of Lake Champlain
  • The Burlington Greenway, a scenic paved trail that stretches over 8 miles along the waterfront of Lake Champlain

Best Things to Do in Winooski: Activities and Tours

Winooski offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to visitors of all ages and interests. Some of the best things to do in Winooski include:

  • Take a brewery tour at one of the city’s many craft breweries, such as Four Quarters Brewing or Zero Gravity Craft Brewery
  • Attend a local festival or event, such as the Winooski Farmers Market or the Rotary Duck Race
  • Explore the local art scene by visiting galleries and studios, such as SEABA Center or Frog Hollow Gallery

Local Culture and Customs of Winooski

Winooski has a rich culture and customs that are deeply rooted in Vermont’s history and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Winooski celebrates several festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Winooski Farmers Market and Rotary Duck Race

Getting to Winooski: Transportation Options and Logistics

Winooski is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including air, bus, and car. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Winooski

  • Burlington International Airport (BTV) is the closest airport to Winooski, located just 4 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Winooski

  • Greyhound Lines offers several bus routes that run through Winooski and surrounding areas.

Other Options
