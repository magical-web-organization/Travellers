Introduction to St. Albans

Nestled in the picturesque landscape of Franklin County, Vermont, St. Albans is a charming town that boasts of intriguing local culture, notable historical sites, and endless outdoor adventures. It is a great travel destination for couples, families, and solo travelers looking to unwind and explore the natural and cultural beauty of Vermont. This comprehensive guide showcases the best places to visit in St. Albans, the top activities, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in St. Albans

Whether you are drawn to cultural attractions or prefer outdoor adventures, St. Albans has something for everyone. Here are the best places to visit in St. Albans:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Albans Museum: Housed in a beautifully restored 1861 Greek Revival-style building, the St. Albans Museum showcases the town’s unique history and culture through engaging exhibits and artifacts.

  • Hunter Farm: Discover Vermont’s rural roots at the Hunter Farm, a historic farmstead that features a museum, agricultural artifacts, and walking trails.

  • Railroad Museum: History buffs will enjoy the Railroad Museum, which showcases the state’s rich railroad history and features antique railcars and engines.

Natural Attractions of St. Albans: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aldis Hill: Discover St. Albans’ natural beauty at Aldis Hill, which offers scenic hiking trails, stunning views of Lake Champlain, and picnic spots.

  • Hard’ack Recreation Area: This popular recreation area offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, mountain biking, snowshoeing, and skiing.

  • Lamoille Valley Rail Trail: Explore the stunning Vermont landscape while hiking, biking, or snowmobiling on the Lamoille Valley Rail Trial, a scenic 93-mile trail that winds through forests, fields, and small towns.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Albans

St. Albans is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, featuring miles of well-marked hiking and biking trails. Here are the best routes to explore:

  • St. Albans Bay Trail: This scenic, 3-mile trail winds through a nature preserve and offers stunning views of Lake Champlain.

  • Cotton Brook Loop Trail: Enjoy a moderate hike on this 3.6-mile trail that passes a beaver pond and beautiful forested areas.

  • Shelburne Bay Trail: Explore the gorgeous natural landscape of Vermont on this 7.4-mile trail that passes through wetlands, forests, and along the shores of Lake Champlain.

Best Things to Do in St. Albans: Activities and Tours

From exploring the outdoors to experiencing local culture, there is no shortage of things to do in St. Albans. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • St. Albans Ghost Walk Tour: Get spooked on this fascinating 1.5-hour walking tour through the town’s haunted sites and spooky history.

  • Missisquoi River Paddle: Experience the tranquility of Vermont’s pristine rivers on this guided paddle tour of the Missisquoi River.

  • Night Sky Photography Tour: Take the perfect shot of the stunning Vermont night sky on this photography tour that includes a professional guide and state-of-the-art equipment.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Albans

If you are planning a longer stay in St. Albans, here is a week-long itinerary that covers the best attractions and activities:

Day 1: Visit St. Albans Museum and Hunter Farm Day 2: Hike Aldis Hill and enjoy a picnic Day 3: Take the St. Albans Ghost Walk Tour Day 4: Bike Cotton Brook Loop Trail and visit the Railroad Museum Day 5: Paddle on the Missisquoi River Day 6: Explore Hard’ack Recreation Area and enjoy a night sky photography tour Day 7: Visit Shelburne Bay Trail and enjoy a local brew

Food and Drink Scene of St. Albans

St. Albans is renowned for its farm-to-table cuisine and local breweries. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Glazed Pork: This local delicacy features succulent pork loin glazed with Vermont maple syrup.

  • Lake Champlain Perch: Savor the fresh flavors of Lake Champlain by trying the local perch, fried to perfection and served with tartar sauce.

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: No trip to St. Albans is complete without trying the famous Vermont maple syrup, drizzled on pancakes or waffles.

Local Beverages

  • Switchback Brewing Company: This popular local brewery features delicious craft beers made with Vermont-grown hops.

  • Dead Creek Brewery: Enjoy a pint of locally crafted beer and savor the unique flavors of Vermont.

  • Snow Farm Vineyard: Relax with a glass of estate-grown Vermont wine at this family-owned vineyard.

Where to Stay in St. Albans: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Albans offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Inn at Grace Farm: Enjoy luxurious accommodations and scenic views at this award-winning bed-and-breakfast, located on a historic farm.

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites: Located in the heart of St. Albans, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location.

  • Hostelling International Burlington: Budget-conscious travelers will appreciate this clean and comfortable hostel located in nearby Burlington.

What to Buy in St. Albans: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of St. Albans’ unique culture with these souvenirs and local products:

  • Maple Syrup: Bring home a bottle of Vermont’s famous maple syrup to savor the delicious flavors of the state.

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Purchase beautiful, handmade pottery crafted by local artisans at the St. Albans Museum.

  • Vermont Cheese: Sample and bring home some of the best cheeses in Vermont, available at local cheese shops and farmers’ markets.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Albans

St. Albans boasts a unique culture and friendly locals. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect for Nature: Vermonters take pride in their natural environment, so be sure to leave no trace and respect the flora and fauna.

  • Friendly Nature: St. Albans locals are famously friendly and welcoming, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation and ask for recommendations.

  • Love of Maple: Vermonters take their maple syrup seriously, so be sure to savor this local delicacy while visiting the state.

Best Time to Visit St. Albans

The best time to visit St. Albans is during the summer months when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities. Alternatively, fall is a beautiful time of year to view the changing foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Albans

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting St. Albans:

Do:

  • Enjoy the Outdoors: St. Albans is renowned for its natural beauty, so be sure to take advantage of the hiking and biking trails.

  • Support Local Businesses: Vermonters take pride in their locally sourced products, so support the local businesses and farmers’ markets.

  • Embrace the Culture: Take advantage of the local tours and cultural experiences to truly immerse yourself in the unique Vermont culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Vermonters are dedicated to protecting their environment, so be sure to dispose of your waste properly and leave no trace.

  • Miss the Maple Syrup: Don’t leave Vermont without trying the famous maple syrup!

  • Overlook the History: St. Albans boasts a rich historical legacy, so be sure to take advantage of the museums and historical sites.

Safety and Health

St. Albans is a safe and healthy destination that offers excellent medical facilities and emergency services. Here are a few important things to keep in mind:

Safety in St. Albans

  • Wildlife Awareness: Be sure to keep your distance from wildlife, especially during the summer and fall months when the chance of encountering bears is higher.

  • Driving Safety: Vermont roads can be challenging, especially during the winter months when snow and ice are common. Be sure to practice safe driving habits and follow posted speed limits.

  • Water Safety: Lake Champlain is a popular attraction, but be sure to practice safe swimming habits and avoid swimming alone or in unsafe areas.

Health in St. Albans

  • Medical Facilities: St. Albans offers excellent medical facilities, including Northwestern Medical Center.

  • Emergency Services: In case of an emergency, dial 911 for immediate assistance.

  • Ticks: Ticks are common in Vermont, especially during the summer months. Be sure to check for ticks after spending time outdoors and to use insect repellent.

Getting to St. Albans: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Albans is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are the best transportation options:

Airports Near St. Albans

  • Burlington International Airport: Located 30 minutes away from St. Albans, this airport offers daily flights to major cities, including New York, Boston, and Washington D.C.

  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport: Located 90 minutes away from St. Albans, this airport offers international flights to major cities, including London, Paris, and Dublin.

Bus Stations Near St. Albans

  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers daily bus service from New York, Boston, and Montreal to the nearby town of Swanton, just 10 minutes away from St. Albans.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for those who want to explore Vermont’s natural beauty at their own pace.

  • Amtrak: Amtrak offers train service to Essex Junction, just 25 minutes away from St. Albans.

How to Get Around in St. Albans

St. Albans is a walkable town that is easily explored on foot. Additionally, car rentals, taxis, and rideshares are readily available for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from St. Albans: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Albans is the perfect starting point for exploring other charming Vermont towns and natural attractions, including:

  • Burlington: Explore the vibrant college town of Burlington, home to eclectic shops, restaurants, and breweries.

  • Shelburne: Visit the quaint town of Shelburne to tour the historic Shelburne Museum and enjoy scenic hiking trails.

  • Lake Champlain Islands: Explore the idyllic island towns and picturesque scenery of Lake Champlain.

Conclusion

St. Albans is a hidden gem in the picturesque landscape of Vermont, offering an abundance of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. This comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to explore the best of St. Albans and to experience the unique culture of Vermont. So pack your bags, hit the road, and discover the magic of St. Albans for yourself!
