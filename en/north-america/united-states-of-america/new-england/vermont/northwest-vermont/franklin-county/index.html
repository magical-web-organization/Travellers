Introduction to Franklin County

Franklin County is a picturesque region in Northwest Vermont, located in the state’s North Country. It offers a wide variety of natural sites, cultural landmarks, and other attractions that make it a great destination for travelers. The county is home to several small towns and villages, each with its own unique charm and personality.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin County

Franklin County offers many exciting and worthwhile places to visit, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The St. Albans Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of St. Albans and the surrounding area, including the railroad and its impact on the area.
  • Missisquoi National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge is a great place to observe a variety of birds and other animals.
  • Enosburg Falls Opera House: Built-in 1892, this historic opera house is still in use today, offering a wide range of musical and theatrical events.
  • The Vermont Maple Museum: Located in nearby Rutland, this museum celebrates the rich history and cultural importance of maple syrup in Vermont.

Natural Attractions of Franklin County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Champlain: A beautiful scenic lake that offers plenty of opportunities for swimming, boating, fishing, and other water-based activities.
  • The Long Trail: This hiking trail passes through the county and offers some challenging and rewarding hikes.
  • Bakersfield Wildlife Management Area: This area is perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring the outdoors. It offers beautiful views of the mountains and the valley.
  • Jay Peak Resort: This resort offers a variety of outdoor activities including skiing, snowboarding, mountain biking, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin County

Franklin County offers several great hiking and biking routes. These include:

  • The Long Trail: This 272-mile hiking trail offers some challenging and rewarding hikes.
  • The Missisquoi Valley Rail Trail: This 26-mile biking and hiking route follows an old rail line, providing scenic views and easy access to several small towns in the area.

Best Things to Do in Franklin County: Activities and Tours

In addition to the outdoor activities mentioned above, there are many other things to do in Franklin County. Here are some of the best:

  • The Vermont Maple Festival: Held annually in nearby St. Albans, this festival celebrates the state’s most famous product – maple syrup.
  • The Franklin County Field Days: This fair is held every August and features agricultural displays, craft exhibits, and carnival rides.
  • The Jay Peak Pump House: This is an indoor waterpark that offers a variety of slides, wave pools, and other fun water-based activities.
  • The Apple Fest: Held every October, this festival celebrates the area’s apple harvest with live music, food vendors, and craft fairs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin County

Day 1: Arrive in St. Albans and visit the St. Albans Museum. Day 2: Explore Lake Champlain and try your hand at fishing or boating. Day 3: Hit the trails and hike the Long Trail. Day 4: Take a bike ride along the Missisquoi Valley Rail Trail. Day 5: Go to Jay Peak and try your hand at skiing or snowboarding. Day 6: Visit the Vermont Maple Museum. Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a visit to the Enosburg Falls Opera House.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin County

Franklin County is a rural area with a strong sense of community. It is known for its arts and crafts, small-town fairs and festivals, and traditional New England charm.

Best Time to Visit Franklin County

The best time to visit Franklin County is in the summer (June to August) when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are at their best. The fall (September to November) is also a great time to visit as the foliage is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin County

Do:

  • Take advantage of the natural beauty and explore the outdoors.
  • Attend local fairs and festivals.
  • Be respectful of the area’s customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local residents or their property.
  • Ignore the area’s history and cultural significance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin County

Franklin County is a relatively safe area, but travelers should take common sense precautions against theft and other crimes. Hiking and other outdoor activities can also pose a risk, so be sure to bring appropriate gear and stay on designated trails.

Health in Franklin County

Franklin County has several medical facilities, including North Country Hospital in Newport and Northwestern Medical Center in St. Albans. However, travelers should always carry medical insurance and be prepared for emergencies.

Travelers to Franklin County can rent a car or use public transportation, including buses and trains. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.
