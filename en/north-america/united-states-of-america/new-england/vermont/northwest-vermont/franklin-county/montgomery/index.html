Introduction to Montgomery

Montgomery is a charming town in Franklin County, located in the picturesque Northwestern region of Vermont. Known for its scenic landscapes, world-class outdoor adventures, and warm local culture, Montgomery has something for everyone. Plan your next trip to Montgomery and experience its unique blend of history, nature, and arts.

List of Places to Visit in Montgomery

Montgomery offers a wide variety of attractions for all types of travelers. Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Montgomery has something for you. Here is a list of top places to visit in Montgomery:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Montgomery Historical Society and Museum - Learn about the town’s rich history and local culture through a collection of exhibits and artifacts.

  2. Montgomery Center for the Arts - A community arts center that hosts cultural events, art classes, and exhibitions.

  3. Montgomery Covered Bridge - Built in 1885, this picturesque wooden covered bridge is one of the last remaining covered bridges in Vermont.

Natural Attractions of Montgomery: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jay Peak Resort - Vermont’s premier ski and snowboarding destination, Jay Peak Resort offers a variety of winter sports activities as well as a year-round indoor water park.

  2. Hazen’s Notch - A popular destination for hiking and camping, Hazen’s Notch offers scenic views, trails, and campsites.

  3. Trout River Falls - A hidden gem of Montgomery, this waterfall is located in the heart of the Green Mountains and offers stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montgomery

Montgomery offers some of the best hiking and biking routes, suitable for all experience levels. Here are some of the top trails to explore:

  1. Long Trail - The longest hiking trail in Vermont, spanning over 272 miles, offers breathtaking views of the Green Mountains.

  2. Hazen’s Notch Trail - A moderate trail that leads to the top of Hazen’s Notch and presents panoramic views of the valley below.

  3. Montgomery Town Trail - A beginner-friendly path which presents visitors with stunning vistas of Montgomery center and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Montgomery: Activities and Tours

Montgomery offers a diverse range of activities and tours, including:

  1. Snowshoeing - Enjoy the winter wonderland of Vermont with guided snowshoeing tours at Jay Peak Resort.

  2. ATV Tours - Experience the thrill of an ATV tour through the backcountry of Vermont with All Terrain Adventures.

  3. Brewery Tours - Sample some of Vermont’s finest microbreweries with guided tours such as the Vermont Brewery Tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montgomery

Here is a week-long itinerary for Montgomery that will help you make the most of your trip:

Day Activities
1 Visit Montgomery Historical Society and Museum, followed by Trout River Falls.
2 Explore Hazen’s Notch Trail and the surrounding beauty.
3 Ski or snowboard at Jay Peak Resort, or visit the indoor water park.
4 Take a guided snowshoeing tour at Jay Peak Resort, or explore the town on your own.
5 Join a brewery tour to sample some of Vermont’s finest microbreweries.
6 Visit Montgomery Center for the Arts and attend a cultural event.
7 Relax and unwind with a scenic drive through the local countryside, before departing home.

Food and Drink Scene of Montgomery

Montgomery has something to offer for every taste bud. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Vermont Maple syrup - A local specialty, Vermont maple syrup can be enjoyed in a variety of forms, from pouring to glazing.

  2. Trapp Family Lodge Goulash - A hearty traditional Austrian goulash dish served at the Trapp Family Lodge.

  3. Vermont cheddar cheese - Vermont cheddar cheese can be found in various forms, from sharp to aged, and can be enjoyed alone or paired with crackers or bread.

Local Beverages

  1. Vermont craft beer - Home to more than 50 craft breweries, Vermont has become a popular destination for beer enthusiasts.

  2. Pure Vermont maple syrup - Enjoy a sweet cup of maple syrup, a traditional Vermont staple.

  3. Hard cider - Vermont’s cider industry has been growing in popularity, with various flavors and types available.

Where to Stay in Montgomery: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montgomery offers a variety of accommodations to choose from, ranging from cozy bed and breakfasts to high-end resorts. Here are some top-rated options:

  1. Phineas Swann Bed and Breakfast - A charming Victorian-style bed and breakfast, located within walking distance of local shops and attractions.

  2. Jay Peak Resort - A premier ski resort featuring a variety of accommodations options, including hotel rooms, condominiums, and cottages.

  3. The Inn at Buck Hollow Farm - A restored farmhouse maintaining its authentic feel with cozy guestrooms with modern amenities and an outdoor hot tub.

What to Buy in Montgomery: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montgomery offers various local products, from maple syrup to handmade crafts. Here are some souvenirs to consider buying:

  1. Vermont Maple Syrup - Bring home some of the state’s famous maple syrup, available in specialty stores and gift shops.

  2. Pottery and Crafts - Visit small artisan shops and galleries to find unique handmade crafts to bring back home.

  3. Local wine - With a growing number of wineries in Vermont, purchasing a bottle or two of local wine to bring home is both unique and delicious.

Local Culture and Customs of Montgomery

Montgomery has a deep-rooted local culture, with hospitality and friendliness being hallmarks of its residents. Vermont in general consumes a lot of locally-sourced foods and beverages and shopping local is highly encouraged.

Best Time to Visit Montgomery

Montgomery’s beautiful landscape can be enjoyed all year round, with something different to offer. Summer is perfect for hiking, while winter is ideal for skiing and snowboarding. Spring brings the famous maple syrup season and Autumn offers a colorful foliage view, making it a prefered season for visitors all over the across the United States.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montgomery

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling to Montgomery:

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine, including maple syrup and cheddar cheese.

  2. Respect the local culture and customs.

  3. Shop for locally-sourced products.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or pollute the natural environment.

  2. Take scenic trails or areas outside of identified trails as it can cause severe environmental degradation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montgomery

Montgomery is a very safe town to explore with low crime rates. Be mindful of wildlife since Vermont is known for its black bear population.

Health in Montgomery

Montgomery is a small rural town with limited medical services. The nearest medical facility located in St. Albans. Carry an adequate supply of medications and check-in for any chronic or life-threatening medical concerns with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Montgomery: Transportation Options and Logistics

Montgomery is located approximately 70 miles (113 km) East of Burlington, Vermont. Here are some ways to reach Montgomery:

Airports Near Montgomery

The nearest airport to Montgomery is the Burlington International Airport (BTV) located about an hour and a half from the town.

Bus Stations Near Montgomery

Montgomery is primary accessible by car, but there are public transportation available to make travel easier. Local buses travels through St. Albans.

Other Options

Montgomery is accessible via rental car or car rentals from anywhere in Vermont and the surrounding states. The scenic Vermont Route 100 is the major road that runs through Montgomery.

How to Get Around in Montgomery

Montgomery has no public transport facilities except for occasional Uber Service. Rental cars are available for hire, including bicycles and scooters.

Where to Go Next from Montgomery: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montgomery is in proximity to several other famous Vermont towns. Here are some top destinations and excursions:

  1. Burlington - An hour and a half drive from Montgomery, Burlington is famous for its college-town vibe, local culture, and lakefront festivals.

  2. Stowe - Known for its quintessential Vermont charm, Stowe is a popular destination for skiing, snowboarding, and hiking.

  3. Ben & Jerry’s Factory - Located in Waterbury, the Ben & Jerry’s factory tour is the perfect excursion for ice cream lovers.

Conclusion

Montgomery offers an unforgettable and unique experience, with a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, culture, and adventure. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide and make the most of your time in Montgomery. Travel responsibly, appreciate every moment, and leave the town better than how you found it.
