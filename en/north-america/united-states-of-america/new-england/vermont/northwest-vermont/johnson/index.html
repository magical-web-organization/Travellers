Introduction to Johnson

Nestled in the heart of the Green Mountains in Northwest Vermont, Johnson is a charming town that offers a unique mix of art, culture, and outdoor adventure. Boasting a picturesque landscape of rolling hills, lush forests, and glistening lakes, Johnson is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway in the countryside or an action-packed adventure, Johnson has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Johnson.

List of Places to Visit in Johnson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Johnson Historical Society Museum - Housed in a historic 19th-century building, the Johnson Historical Society Museum showcases the rich history of the town through various exhibits and artifacts, including maps, photographs, and antique farming tools.
  • Vermont Studio Center - One of the largest artist residency programs in the country, the Vermont Studio Center is a haven for artists and writers from around the world. Visitors can take a guided tour of the center’s studios, galleries, and workshops and see some of the best contemporary art produced in New England.

Natural Attractions of Johnson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sterling Pond Trail - A scenic and peaceful hike that leads you to one of the highest natural ponds in Vermont, Sterling Pond Trail is a must-visit destination for hikers and nature lovers. With stunning views of the surrounding mountains, the trail is an ideal spot for a picnic or a peaceful afternoon getaway.
  • Lamoille Valley Rail Trail - A popular destination for cyclists and runners, the Lamoille Valley Rail Trail follows the scenic Lamoille River and offers picturesque views of the countryside. With a wide, well-maintained trail, the rail trail is an ideal choice for a leisurely bike ride or a morning jog.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Johnson

  • Mount Mansfield - The highest peak in Vermont, Mount Mansfield is a favorite destination for hikers and climbers. With several trails that range from easy to difficult, visitors can choose the perfect trail to suit their level of fitness and experience.
  • Johnson State College Disc Golf Course - An 18-hole disc golf course located on the campus of Johnson State College, the course offers a fun and challenging experience for players of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Johnson: Activities and Tours

  • Fly Fishing on the Lamoille River - With abundant brook, rainbow, and brown trout, the Lamoille River is a popular spot for fly fishing enthusiasts. Visitors can take guided tours and lessons from expert fly fishing guides and enjoy a peaceful day on the river.
  • Kayaking on Lake Eden - With crystal-clear waters and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, Lake Eden is a favorite destination for kayakers and boaters. Visitors can rent kayaks and paddleboats and explore the lake at their own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Johnson

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Johnson:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Johnson Historical Society Museum and explore the town’s quaint shops and cafes.
Day 2 Hike the Sterling Pond Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch overlooking the pond.
Day 3 Visit the Vermont Studio Center and take a guided tour of the center’s studios and galleries.
Day 4 Enjoy a round of disc golf at the Johnson State College Disc Golf Course.
Day 5 Go fly fishing on the Lamoille River and enjoy a peaceful afternoon on the water.
Day 6 Rent kayaks and paddleboats and explore the scenic Lake Eden.
Day 7 Take a scenic drive through the Green Mountains and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Food and Drink Scene of Johnson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup - Vermont is famous for its maple syrup, and Johnson is no exception. Visitors can sample the local maple syrup and enjoy the sweet, rich flavor that has made Vermont famous.
  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese - Another iconic Vermont product, Vermont cheddar cheese is a favorite of foodies and cheese lovers. Visitors can buy locally-made cheddar cheese from Johnson’s farmer’s markets and local stores.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Microbrews - Vermont is known for its craft beer scene, and Johnson is no exception. Visitors can try a variety of locally-brewed beers at Johnson’s many bars and breweries.
  • Cold Hollow Cider Mill - Located just a short drive from Johnson, the Cold Hollow Cider Mill is a popular destination for visitors looking to sample Vermont’s famous cider.

Where to Stay in Johnson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Governor’s House in Hyde Park - A historic bed and breakfast located just a short drive from Johnson, The Governor’s House in Hyde Park offers a charming, old-world charm and luxurious accommodations for visitors.
  • The Sterling Ridge Resort - A rustic, yet luxurious resort located in the heart of the Green Mountains, The Sterling Ridge Resort offers visitors a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

What to Buy in Johnson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vermont Maple Syrup - A perfect gift for foodies and syrup lovers, Vermont maple syrup is a sweet and delicious souvenir that visitors can bring home.
  • Vermont Crafts and Artwork - Visitors can find a variety of locally-made crafts and artwork at Johnson’s many cultural attractions, including the Vermont Studio Center and the Johnson Historical Society Museum.

Local Culture and Customs of Johnson

  • Respect for Nature - Vermonters have a deep appreciation for the natural beauty of their state and visitors are expected to respect the environment and wildlife.
  • Small-Town Hospitality - Johnson is known for its friendly, welcoming residents who are happy to share their town with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Johnson

The best time to visit Johnson is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and the natural beauty of the Green Mountains is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Johnson

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment
  • Explore the town’s cultural attractions
  • Sample the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb wildlife
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Leave without trying Vermont’s famous maple syrup

Safety and Health

Safety in Johnson

Johnson is a safe and welcoming town with low crime rates, but visitors should take appropriate precautions, especially when hiking or biking in the surrounding mountains.

Health in Johnson

Visitors should be aware of the risks of tick bites and take precautions to avoid them, including wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Johnson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Johnson

  • Burlington International Airport - Located just over an hour’s drive from Johnson, Burlington International Airport is the closest major airport to the town.
  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport - Located just over two hours’ drive from Johnson, Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International airport is a popular gateway for international visitors.

Bus Stations Near Johnson

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Montpelier - Located less than an hour’s drive from Johnson, the Greyhound Bus Station in Montpelier is a popular destination for visitors traveling by bus.
  • Megabus Station in Burlington - Located just over an hour’s drive from Johnson, the Megabus Station in Burlington is a convenient option for visitors traveling by bus.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or take a taxi from the nearby airports or bus stations to Johnson.

How to Get Around in Johnson

The best way to get around Johnson is by car or bike. Cars can be rented from the nearby airports or bus stations, and bikes can be rented from local rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Johnson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stowe - A charming mountain town located just a short drive from Johnson, Stowe is a popular destination for skiing, hiking, and shopping.
  • Burlington - A vibrant college town located on the shores of Lake Champlain, Burlington offers visitors a variety of cultural attractions, shops, and restaurants.

Conclusion

With its breathtaking natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventures, Johnson is a perfect destination for visitors looking to explore the best of Vermont. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat in the countryside or an action-packed adventure, Johnson has something for everyone. So pack your bags, hit the road, and discover the charms of Northwest Vermont in Johnson.
