Introduction to Burlington

Burlington, nestled in the heart of Champlain Valley, is one of the most picturesque cities in the Northeastern United States. This vibrant and bustling city is known for its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and abundance of fun and exciting activities.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or simply looking for a peaceful retreat, Burlington has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, exciting things to do, hidden gems, and practical tips that will make your Burlington visit unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Burlington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shelburne Museum: Visiting the Shelburne Museum is like taking a step back in time. Located a short drive from Burlington, this museum has something for everyone, including a vast collection of Americana, folk art, quilts, and historic buildings that showcase the history and culture of Vermont.

  • Ethan Allen Homestead Museum: Discover the legacy of one of Vermont’s most famous patriots at the Ethan Allen Homestead Museum. The museum offers guided tours of the house and grounds that are full of history and rich in detail, with knowledgeable guides who will bring Ethan Allen’s story to life.

  • University of Vermont Morgan Horse Farm: The UVM Morgan Horse Farm is a unique experience that both adults and children will enjoy. Here, you can learn about the history and breeding of the iconic Morgan horse, take a guided tour of the farm, or even go for a ride on a horse.

Natural Attractions of Burlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Church Street Marketplace: The Church Street Marketplace is a historical pedestrian-only outdoor mall located in the heart of Burlington. The charming street is lined with locally owned specialty shops, restaurants, and cafes, all with unique offerings for both locals and tourists to enjoy.

  • Waterfront Park: For stunning views of the lake, head to the Burlington Waterfront. This park offers a beautiful bike path along the lake, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride. Along the way, you can stop for ice cream or lunch at one of the park’s many food vendors.

  • The Adirondack Mountains: Located just across the lake, the Adirondack Mountains offer adventurous visitors a chance to hike or bike through beautiful wilderness areas with stunning views and world-class trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burlington

  • The Burlington Greenway: This 7.5-mile paved trail runs along the lakefront and is suitable for cyclists and walkers of all abilities.

  • The Causeway: A unique and scenic cycling experience, the Causeway bike path runs over Lake Champlain on a railroad causeway that connects to the Champlain Islands.

Best Things to Do in Burlington: Activities and Tours

  • Vermont Lake Monsters Baseball: Spend a relaxing afternoon at the ballpark cheering on the Vermont Lake Monsters, the local minor league baseball team.

  • Burlington Brew Tours: Burlington is known for its excellent microbreweries, and a brewery tour is a fun and informative way to explore the city’s craft beer scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burlington

Day 1: Explore the Church Street Marketplace, lunch at a local cafe, and bicycle along the Burlington Greenway. Day 2: Visit the Shelburne Museum and Ethan Allen Homestead Museum. Day 3: Go hiking in the Adirondack Mountains. Day 4: Take a guided horseback tour at the UVM Morgan Horse Farm. Day 5: Spend the day relaxing at Waterfront Park, perhaps renting a paddleboard or kayak. Day 6: Take a Burlington Brew Tour and sample local craft beers. Day 7: Catch a game with the Vermont Lake Monsters and indulge in some tasty ballpark food.

Food and Drink Scene of Burlington

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese: Vermont is known for producing some of the best cheddar cheese in the world. Make sure you try some during your stay.

  • Maple Syrup: Another Vermont specialty, delicious maple syrup should be on anyone’s food bucket list while in Burlington.

  • Ben & Jerry’s Ice Cream: The popular ice cream chain started their business in Burlington and has a factory here, now an iconic destination for visitors to the city.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Burlington is a beer lover’s dream with its numerous award-winning craft breweries and taprooms, including the famed Magic Hat Brewing Company.

  • Vermont Cider: Complimenting the beer scene, Vermont offers a plethora of hard ciders for visitors to enjoy.

Where to Stay in Burlington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Vermont: Located in the heart of downtown Burlington, this luxurious and modern hotel offers stylish suites, a farm-to-table restaurant, and access to a range of outdoor activities.

  • Lang House: This charming bed and breakfast in a beautifully restored historic home, Lang House offers comfortable rooms and excellent homemade breakfasts.

  • North Beach Campground: Located just outside of downtown Burlington, North Beach Campground is an excellent option for those interested in camping and enjoying the natural surroundings.

What to Buy in Burlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: A popular souvenir, this sweet treat is perfect for bringing home to family and friends.

  • Local art: Burlington has a thriving arts scene, and you will find local art and crafts ranging from paintings and prints to ceramics and jewelry.

  • Vermont cheese: Get a taste of Vermont’s famous dairy industry with a selection of local cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Burlington

Burlington’s culture is best defined as laid-back and relaxed, with a focus on the outdoors and the appreciation of nature. The locals are friendly, welcoming, and always eager to share their love of Vermont’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Burlington

The best time to visit Burlington is during the summer months, from June to August. The warm and sunny weather is perfect for outdoor activities, and the lake is at its most inviting. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the changing leaves creating a colorful and breathtaking backdrop throughout the Champlain Valley.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burlington

Do:

  • Take a brewery tour: Burlington has a thriving craft beer scene that is well worth exploring.

  • Get active outdoors: With countless hiking and biking trails, a beautiful waterfront, and the nearby Adirondack Mountains, Burlington is a dream destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep Burlington’s natural beauty clean and pristine by disposing of trash responsibly.

  • Be impolite: The locals are friendly and laid-back, so follow suit and treat others as you would like to be treated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burlington

Burlington is a relatively safe city, and violent crime is rare. However, like any urban area, it is always wise to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, particularly after dark or on isolated trails.

Health in Burlington

The air quality and water in Burlington are generally good, and the city has several hospitals and medical clinics, so visitors shouldn’t have any issues accessing medical care if needed.

Getting to Burlington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burlington

  • Burlington International Airport: The closest airport; located just 10 mins from Downtown Burlington.

  • Plattsburgh International Airport: Another option in the Burlington area.

Bus Stations Near Burlington

Megabus and Greyhound Bus both operate out of the Burlington Bus Terminal, located downtown.

Other Options

Amtrak train service also connects Burlington to major cities including New York, Boston, and Montreal.

How to Get Around in Burlington

Burlington is a highly pedestrian-friendly city with a well-connected bike path. Taxis, Lyft, and public buses are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Burlington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Montreal: Located just 1.5 hours north of Burlington, Montreal is an easy and exciting day trip destination with its rich French-Canadian culture.

  • Stowe: Hailed as the “Ski Capital of the East,” Stowe offers outdoor adventures, breathtaking vistas, and a relaxed Vermont vibe.

Conclusion

Burlington is a fantastic destination for anyone looking to explore the natural beauty of Vermont while also experiencing unique culture and history. This comprehensive travel guide should help you make the most of your visit by introducing you to the top things to do, places to eat, and exciting experiences that await you in beautiful Burlington, Vermont.
