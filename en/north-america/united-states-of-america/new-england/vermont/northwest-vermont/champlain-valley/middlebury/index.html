Introduction to Middlebury

Nestled in the Champlain Valley of Northwest Vermont, Middlebury is a quaint and picturesque town filled with rich history and natural beauty. Home to Middlebury College, the town offers a unique blend of youthful energy and old-time charm. Visitors can explore cultural attractions, enjoy outdoor adventures, and indulge in delicious food and drink.

List of Places to Visit in Middlebury

Whether you’re into history, nature, or a bit of both, Middlebury has a variety of sights to see. Below, we’ve curated a list of the top places to visit in Middlebury:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Middlebury College Museum of Art: Housed in the impressive Mahaney Arts Center, the museum features an extensive collection of American and European art, including works by Pablo Picasso and Rembrandt.
  • Henry Sheldon Museum: This museum celebrates Middlebury’s rich history with exhibits on the town’s early settlers and famous residents like poet Robert Frost.
  • The Vermont Folklife Center: Dedicated to preserving the state’s cultural heritage, the center hosts exhibits, films, and events on traditional music, crafts, and storytelling.

Natural Attractions of Middlebury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Middlebury Trail Network: This network of over 20 miles of trails is perfect for hiking or biking. The trails meander through lush forests and open fields, offering stunning views of the Adirondack Mountains.
  • Otter Creek Gorge: Located just outside of town, this natural wonder is a beautiful spot for a picnic, hike, or swim.
  • Rattlesnake Cliffs: Hike up to these towering cliffs for a breathtaking view of the Champlain Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Middlebury

Middlebury is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of well-maintained trails. Here are some top picks for hiking or biking:

  • Battell Woods: This peaceful forest is perfect for a leisurely hike or bike ride.
  • Robert Frost Interpretive Trail: Take a stroll through this serene wooded area while reading some of Frost’s famous poems.
  • Snake Mountain: With an elevation gain of over 1,000 feet, this hike offers an excellent workout with stunning views of Lake Champlain as a reward.

Best Things to Do in Middlebury: Activities and Tours

Middlebury has a variety of activities and tours suitable for every type of traveler. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Middlebury Food Tour: Sample some of the best local cuisine while learning about the town’s food history on this guided tour.
  • Platinum Pursuits: This adventure company offers guided kayak and paddleboard tours on nearby lakes and rivers.
  • Vermont Knolls Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take to the skies and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the stunning Champlain Valley.

Week-Long Itinerary for Middlebury

For travelers with a week to spare, we’ve put together an itinerary that includes some of Middlebury’s top attractions:

Day 1: Explore the town’s museums and cultural attractions, including the Middlebury College Museum of Art and the Henry Sheldon Museum.

Day 2: Spend the day outdoors, hiking the Middlebury Trail Network and enjoying a picnic at Otter Creek Gorge.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the Champlain Valley, then dine at one of Middlebury’s top eateries, like Tourterelle or Two Brothers Tavern.

Day 4-5: Embark on a kayak or paddleboard tour of nearby lakes and rivers with Platinum Pursuits.

Day 6: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Robert Frost Interpretive Trail, then enjoy a locally-made cheese plate and beer at American Flatbread.

Day 7: Hike up Snake Mountain for an incredible view of the Champlain Valley, then relax with a spa treatment at the Middlebury Inn.

Food and Drink Scene of Middlebury

Middlebury’s food and drink scene is as diverse as it is delicious. From farm-to-table cuisine to craft cocktails, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont Cheese Plate: Sample some of the state’s delicious cheese with homemade crackers and local honey.
  • Maple Glazed Pork Belly: This sweet and savory dish is a local favorite, featuring pork belly glazed with Vermont maple syrup.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: Made with locally-sourced apples, these donuts are a delicious treat.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Beer: The state’s craft beer scene is booming, with breweries like Otter Creek Brewing and Drop-In Brewing Company calling Middlebury home.
  • Vermont Cider: Sample some of the state’s famous hard cider at Woodchuck Cider House or Champlain Orchards.
  • Ginger Libation: This exotic beverage is made with local ginger and honey, creating a sweet and spicy flavor.

Where to Stay in Middlebury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Middlebury has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and style. Here are some top picks:

  • Middlebury Inn: This historic inn dates back to 1827 and offers boutique guest rooms, a full-service spa, and an award-winning restaurant.
  • Jackson House Inn: This elegant bed and breakfast features luxurious rooms, serene gardens, and a gourmet breakfast.
  • Grapevine Cottage: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Middlebury and features cozy rooms with private bathrooms.

What to Buy in Middlebury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For travelers looking to bring home a piece of Middlebury, there are a variety of local products and souvenirs to choose from. Here are some top picks:

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: This sweet and delicious syrup is a staple of Vermont’s culinary scene.
  • Middlebury College Apparel: Show off your Panther pride with some school-branded gear.
  • Vermont Crafts: From handmade pottery to hand-woven textiles, Vermont’s artisanal crafts are worth shopping for.

Local Culture and Customs of Middlebury

Middlebury’s culture is deeply rooted in its history and natural beauty. Vermonters are known for their strong sense of community, and visitors should expect a warm and welcoming environment. When visiting, it’s important to respect the town’s natural resources and support local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Middlebury

Middlebury is a four-season destination, with each offering its own unique experiences. Summer and fall are popular times for outdoor activities, while winter offers skiing and snowshoeing opportunities. Spring brings beautiful foliage and maple syrup season. Ultimately, the best time to visit Middlebury depends on your interests and what you’d like to experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middlebury

To ensure a pleasant stay in Middlebury, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the town’s natural resources and wildlife by leaving no trace.
  • Support local businesses by shopping at boutiques and dining at locally-owned restaurants.
  • Dress appropriately for the season and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources.
  • Overlook Middlebury’s quieter, slower pace of life.

Safety and Health

Middlebury is a safe, friendly town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always take basic safety precautions and be aware of their surroundings.

Safety in Middlebury

  • Keep valuables out of sight and lock your car and accommodations.
  • Practice caution when hiking or biking off-trail.
  • Follow local weather reports and adjust outdoor activities accordingly.

Health in Middlebury

  • Middlebury has excellent medical facilities for emergencies.
  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.

Getting to Middlebury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Middlebury is accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Middlebury:

Airports Near Middlebury

  • Burlington International Airport: located 40 miles away.
  • Rutland Southern Vermont Regional Airport: located 32 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Middlebury

  • Greyhound Station: located in Burlington, 40 miles away.
  • Yankee Trails World Travel: located in Bennington, 60 miles away.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: stations located in nearby Essex Junction and Rutland.
  • Car rental: available at Burlington and Rutland airports.

How to Get Around in Middlebury

Middlebury is a compact town that’s easy to navigate on foot. However, a car may be necessary for exploring the area outside of town. Taxis and rideshare services like Uber are also available.

Where to Go Next from Middlebury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Middlebury is located in a beautiful region of Vermont, with plenty of exciting destinations within a short drive. Here are some top picks:

  • Burlington: A thriving city located on the shores of Lake Champlain, with a bustling downtown area and great shopping.
  • Lake Champlain Islands: A beautiful region with beaches, wineries, and scenic drives.
  • Green Mountain National Forest: Explore the natural beauty of Vermont’s largest national forest, offering hiking, camping, and cross-country skiing.

Conclusion

Middlebury is a charming and unforgettable destination that offers a perfect blend of history, nature, and culture. With a variety of attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious food and drink, visitors are sure to fall in love with this picturesque Vermont town.
