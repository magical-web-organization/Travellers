    • Introduction to Jericho

    Nestled in the heart of Champlain Valley in Northwest Vermont, Jericho is a serene and charming town with plenty to offer for travelers seeking a peaceful and rejuvenating vacation. Founded in 1763, Jericho has a rich history that is reflected in its quaint architecture, intriguing museums, and cultural landmarks.

    In this travel guide, we will take you on an immersive journey through the best of Jericho, exploring its captivating landscapes, culturally significant landmarks, and local food and drink scene. Whether you’re an outdoor adventurer, history buff, or foodie, Jericho has something for everyone.

    List of Places to Visit in Jericho

    Here is a list of must-visit places in Jericho:

    Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

    Jericho boasts a rich culture and history that is beautifully preserved in its museums and historical landmarks. Don’t miss the following attractions:

    • Old Red Mill Museum: Housed in an 1820s grain mill, this museum showcases the rich history of Jericho through its exhibits on agriculture, industry, and transportation.
    • Jericho Historical Society & Emmons House Museum: Take a step back in time and explore the life of early pioneers in Jericho through this museum’s collections of artifacts, photographs, and documents.

    Natural Attractions of Jericho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

    Jericho is known for its stunning natural landscapes and picturesque scenery. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions to explore:

    • Mills Riverside Park: With its serene river views, hiking trails, and picnic areas, this park offers a perfect escape for nature lovers.
    • Mt. Mansfield State Forest: With its winding trails, streams, and waterfalls, this forest is a hiker’s paradise.

    Hiking/Biking Routes of Jericho

    Jericho is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, offering some of the best hiking and biking trails in Vermont. Here are some of our favorites:

    • Browns River Trail: This 3-mile trail in Mills Riverside Park offers a scenic walk along the banks of the Browns River.
    • Jericho Settlers Trail: This 7.1-mile trail passes through some of Jericho’s most historic landmarks, including the Old Red Mill and Jericho Center Village.

    Best Things to Do in Jericho: Activities and Tours

    Jericho offers plenty of fun and unique activities for tourists to enjoy. Here are some top picks:

    • Jericho Country Store: Locals and tourists alike love this historic country store, which features a café, gift shop, and daily live music performances.
    • Jericho Corn Maze: Get lost in the winding corridors of Vermont’s largest corn maze, which features over ten acres of mazes and twists.

    Week-Long Itinerary for Jericho

    Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Jericho:

    Day Activity
    1 Explore Jericho Historical Society & Emmons House Museum
    2 Take a scenic hike in Mt. Mansfield State Forest
    3 Tour Old Red Mill Museum followed by a picnic in Mills Riverside Park
    4 Lunch at Jericho Country Store, followed by cycling along Jericho Settlers Trail
    5 Visit Underhill State Park and hike Mount Mansfield
    6 Spend a leisurely day browsing local boutiques and shops in Jericho Center
    7 Relax and unwind with a visit to a local spa or salon

    Food and Drink Scene of Jericho

    Jericho is a foodie’s dream destination, offering a variety of quality eateries and watering holes. Here’s our guide to the must-try dishes and local beverages:

    Must-Try Dishes

    • Vermont Maple Syrup: Jericho is known for its world-renowned maple syrup. Don’t forget to try it with your pancake breakfast!
    • Pumpkin Ravioli: A classic dish made with locally sourced pumpkins

    Local Beverages

    • Vermont Cider: This crisp, refreshing apple cider is a local favorite and is often served at Jericho’s pubs and taverns.
    • Vermont Craft Beer: Vermont has a vast selection of local breweries that produce some of the best craft beers in the region.

    Where to Stay in Jericho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

    Jericho has a host of accommodation options that cater to a range of budgets and preferences. Here are our top picks:

    • The Mansfield Inn: A charming bed and breakfast that offers cozy accommodation in a serene setting.
    • The Essex: A luxury spa resort located just 15 minutes from Jericho, with beautiful views and world-class amenities.

    What to Buy in Jericho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

    If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, here are some must-buy items:

    • Vermont Maple Syrup: A classic souvenir from Jericho, this local specialty is a must-try for all foodies.
    • Handmade Soaps and Candles: Browse local boutiques and shops in Jericho Center for handmade soaps and candles.

    Local Culture and Customs of Jericho

    Jericho residents are known for their warm hospitality and friendly demeanor. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind while exploring Jericho:

    • Respect the Outdoors: Vermonters take great pride in their natural landscapes and expect visitors to take care of them as well.
    • Say Hello: It’s common courtesy to greet the locals with a polite “hello” or “hi” when passing by on the street.

    Best Time to Visit Jericho

    The best time to visit Jericho is during the fall or winter season when the foliage is at its most beautiful, and snow blankets the town. However, any time of year, Jericho is a charming destination with plenty to offer.

    Do’s and Don’ts of Jericho

    • Do: Respect the local customs and traditions and take care of the natural landscapes.

    • Don’t: Litter or leave trails and parks in a messy condition.

    Safety and Health

    Safety in Jericho

    Jericho is a relatively safe and peaceful town. However, it’s essential to take the usual precautions while exploring the parks and forests.

    Health in Jericho

    Jericho has top-class medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, which provide immediate medical attention in case of an emergency.

    Getting to Jericho: Transportation Options and Logistics

    Airports Near Jericho

    The nearest airport to Jericho is Burlington International Airport, located just 30 minutes’ drive away.

    Bus Stations Near Jericho

    Greyhound Bus Lines have a bus stop in Burlington from where you can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Jericho.

    Other Options

    Renting a car or hiring a taxi is the most convenient way to reach Jericho.

    How to Get Around in Jericho

    Jericho is a small town; therefore, the best way to get around is by car or bike. Most major attractions are easily accessible by car or cycling.

    Where to Go Next from Jericho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

    • Burlington: Located just 15 miles from Jericho, Burlington is a bustling town with plenty of activities to offer, including dining, shopping, and cultural attractions.
    • Stowe: A charming mountain town just 45 minutes from Jericho, offering stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities.

    Conclusion

    Jericho is a hidden gem in the heart of Vermont that offers a beautiful blend of natural beauty and rich culture. This comprehensive guide is sure to help you plan an unforgettable trip to this tranquil town, which will leave you feeling rejuvenated and refreshed.
