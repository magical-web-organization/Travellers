Introduction to Norwich

Welcome to Norwich, a peaceful town located in Southern Vermont, nestled in the heart of New England, United States of America. This picturesque place offers a small-town vibe, surrounded by breathtaking landscapes, and outdoor adventures that make it a perfect destination for people of all ages. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know before visiting Norwich, including the best spots for hiking, the must-visit cultural attractions, iconic dishes, and more. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Norwich

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and cultural attractions, Norwich won’t disappoint. Three museums will keep you hooked with their exceptional exhibits.

  1. Montshire Museum of Science: Montshire is a nationally recognized science museum that has won the hearts of all age groups. The interactive exhibits and exciting events will keep you entertained for hours.

  2. Dan & Whit’s General Store: Named as one of “America’s Favorite Shops,” Dan & Whit’s General Store has been running since 1946. Choose from a wide range of local products, vintage items, and the famous Norwich T-shirt collection.

  3. The President Calvin Coolidge State Historic Site: Explore the village of Plymouth Notch, where the U.S. President Calvin Coolidge was born, lived, and took the oath of office. This place will take you back to the 19th century and provide a glimpse into Vermont’s rural life back then.

Natural Attractions of Norwich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Get ready to be mesmerized by the scenic views, breathtaking fall foliage, and a range of outdoor activities at Norwich’s natural attractions.

  1. Mount Ascutney State Park: Located nearby, Mount Ascutney offers miles of hiking trails, skiing adventures, splendid views from the summit, and much more.

  2. Connecticut River: Take a serene boat ride on Connecticut River, which divides Vermont and New Hampshire. You’ll see some of the best views of Vermont’s valleys, riverbanks, and wildlife.

  3. King Arthur Flour café and baking classes: Nestled in the picturesque Norwich, King Arthur Flour cafe is a recipe for country paradise. You’ll find an endless array of baking essentials, including products unique to Vermont. You could also sign up for their expert baking classes to learn from the best.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Norwich

For adventure enthusiasts, Norwich is a paradise. Listed below are the best hiking and biking routes to explore Norwich’s natural beauty.

  1. Gile Mountain Trail: Climb your way to the top of Gile Mountain and capture some stunning panoramic views.

  2. Mount Cube Trail: An extensive 7.6-mile hike that is perfect for experienced hikers. Enjoy the sights and sounds of the wilderness.

  3. Appalachian Trail: A great spot for long-distance hikers, the Appalachian Trail is more than 2,000 miles long and stretches from Maine to Georgia. The Vermont stretch of the trail will leave you breathless with its stunning views and epic wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Norwich: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a culture lover, or a foodie, Norwich has something for everyone. Listed below are a few things you must do when in Norwich.

  1. Attend a Norwich Farmers Market: Get the taste and feel of Vermont by visiting the Norwich Farmers Market, which sells fresh produce and bakery items all year round.

  2. Explore Norwich’s history on a guided tour: A two-hour guided tour of Norwich will take you through the streets of historic Vermont, exploring the town’s history and culture.

  3. Visit the Simon Pearce Glass Factory: Simon Pearce Glass Factory offers a one-of-a-kind experience, offering guided tours of the glass-blowing process and a gourmet restaurant featuring local ingredients and craft cocktails.

Week-Long Itinerary for Norwich

Day 1: Visit the Montshire Museum of Science Day 2: Explore the Connecticut River Day 3: Go hiking at Mount Cube or Gile Mountain Trail Day 4: Visit King Arthur Flour Cafe or take a baking class Day 5: Visit the President Calvin Coolidge State Historic Site Day 6: Attend a Norwich Farmers Market and shop for souvenirs at Dan & Whit’s General Store Day 7: Go skiing, snowboarding, or snowshoeing on Mount Ascutney

Food and Drink Scene of Norwich

Must-Try Dishes

Norwich is a heaven for food enthusiasts, offering you an array of dining options. Listed below are must-try dishes:

  1. MapleCreme Donut: A famous donut from “The Donut Dude,” which is an independent donut maker that travels to the local farmers’ markets.

  2. Vermont Cheddar Cheese: Vermont cheddar cheese is world-renowned for its strong flavor, velvety texture, and sharp bite.

  3. Maple Syrup: Vermont is the top producer of maple syrup in the United States, and it’s no surprise why it’s a must-try dish. Try some freshly prepared on a stack of buttermilk pancakes.

Local Beverages

Vermont’s craft brew industry is flourishing, and Norwich is home to several breweries that offer various beer styles. Below are a few recommendations:

  1. Harpoon Brewery & Beer Hall: A staple of Vermont’s craft beer scene.

  2. Jasper Murdoch’s Ale House: A Vermont pub that serves classic beer and cocktails made with local spirits.

  3. King Arthur Baking Company Cafe: Enjoy your morning coffee with a side of freshly baked scones in the beautiful garden of Norwich’s famed bakery.

Where to Stay in Norwich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Norwich offers an array of options to choose from when it comes to accommodation. Below are a few options:

  1. The Norwich Inn: A 1797 inn that has been converted into a famous hotel, offering comfortable rooms, great food, and local beer.

  2. The Windsor Mansion Inn: Located in a stunning valley, Windsor Mansion Inn is the perfect retreat from city life.

  3. Norwich Farmhouse Inn: A bed and breakfast that offers eight beautifully appointed rooms, each with private baths, fireplaces, and individual AC.

What to Buy in Norwich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to pack some Vermont’s traditional goodies to bring it back with you and cherish your memories. Some of the options are:

  1. Vermont Crafts: Vermont crafts such as pottery, handmade wooden toys, and hand-knitted woolen slippers.

  2. Vermont Cheese: A variety of Vermont’s famed cheddars, made from cow, goat, or sheep’s milk, is available at the local stores.

  3. Vermont Maple Syrup: Vermont Maple syrup is world-renowned for its rich flavor and versatile use in various dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Norwich

Norwich residents, commonly known as Norwichers, are proud of their history, tradition and are always happy to share it with visitors. Make sure to be respectful of the city’s history, traditions, and culture during your stay.

Best Time to Visit Norwich

Norwich is appealing all year round, but the best time to visit is during fall (September to November), when fall foliage displays its most beautiful colors. However, you might need to brace yourself for some crowds during this time. Spring (March to early June) is an excellent time to visit for those who love flowers and outdoor adventures while avoiding the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Norwich

Do:

  1. Respect nature and wildlife in Norwich.
  2. Explore the downtown area of Norwich and enjoy its local shops and attractions.
  3. Try local cuisine and products from any of the independent farmers’ markets.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public places.
  2. Intrude on personal spaces or private properties.
  3. Ride on ski trails or marked backcountry trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Norwich

Norwich is a safe and friendly town. However, it’s essential to be careful and aware of your surroundings as it’s a rural area.

Health in Norwich

Norwich has a small medical center and local healthcare providers if you need them. Be sure to carry all necessary medications and check with your insurance company before your trip.

Getting to Norwich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Norwich

The closest airport to Norwich is the Lebanon regional airport, 14 miles away. Alternatively, Boston Logan International Airport, 125 miles south of Norwich, is an excellent option for long-distance travelers.

Bus Stations Near Norwich

The best way to reach Norwich by bus is through Greyhound or Vermont Transit, with bus stations in White River Junction, less than ten minutes away.

Other Options

Norwich does have a small Amtrak station in White River Junction, with service daily to Washington, D.C., and New York City. A rental car is the most convenient option for getting around the local area.

How to Get Around in Norwich

Norwich is a small town, and the best way to get around the city is on foot or with a bike. If you have to travel longer distances, it’s best to rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Norwich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Woodstock: 15 minutes away by car is Woodstock, which is home to some historic covered bridges, museums, lovely restaurants, and boutique shops.

  2. Quechee: Just a 15-minute drive from Norwich, Quechee is known for its picturesque waterfalls and antique shops.

  3. Hanover: Hanover, New Hampshire, is conveniently just a few minutes drive from Norwich. Enjoy a scenic riverside walk, visit indoor markets, and explore some of the art museums located in this college town.

Conclusion

With its charming downtown, outdoor recreation opportunities, and cultural attractions, Norwich Vermont attracts visitors looking for rest, relaxation, and a little bit of adventure. Whether you’re here to shop, eat, hike, or relax, Norwich has something special to offer everyone. Use this comprehensive Norwich guide to plan your next trip to Southern Vermont, United States of America.
