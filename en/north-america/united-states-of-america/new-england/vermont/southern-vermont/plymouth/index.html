Introduction to Plymouth

Nestled in the southern region of Vermont, Plymouth boasts a rich history as the birthplace of Calvin Coolidge, the 30th President of the United States. Today, Plymouth is a vibrant destination that offers a charming blend of cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. From hiking to skiing to exploring museums and historic sites, there’s something for everyone in this quaint town. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you plan your visit to Plymouth.

List of Places to Visit in Plymouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Calvin Coolidge State Historic Site: Visit the birthplace and childhood home of President Calvin Coolidge and learn about his life and legacy.
  • Plymouth Notch: Step back in time and explore this picturesque village that was once the thriving homestead of the Coolidge family.
  • The President Calvin Coolidge Museum and Education Center: Discover the history of President Coolidge’s life and presidency through engaging exhibits and interactive displays.
  • The Plymouth Cheese Factory: Sample locally-made cheese and learn about the history of cheese-making in Vermont at this historic factory.

Natural Attractions of Plymouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Echo Lake State Park: Enjoy swimming, fishing, and hiking with stunning views of the Green Mountains at Echo Lake State Park.
  • Buttermilk Falls: Hike through beautiful woods to reach the scenic Buttermilk Falls, a popular destination for nature lovers.
  • Okemo Mountain: Hit the slopes and enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities at Okemo Mountain Resort.
  • Coolidge State Forest: Explore over 21,000 acres of forest trails and wildlife in this beautiful state park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plymouth

  • Calvin Coolidge Historic Site Trail: This easy 1.2-mile round-trip hike takes you to the birthplace and cemetery of Calvin Coolidge.
  • Buttermilk Falls Trail: This 1.8-mile trail leads to a breathtaking waterfall and is a great choice for families.
  • Echo Lake Trail: This 5-mile round-trip hike offers beautiful views of Echo Lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Plymouth: Activities and Tours

  • Snowmobile Tours: Explore the stunning landscape of Plymouth on a snowmobile tour, available during the winter season.
  • Farm and Artisan Tours: Visit local farms and learn about Vermont’s agricultural history on a guided tour.
  • Plymouth Folk and Blues Festival: Attend the annual festival and enjoy live music, local food, and craft vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Plymouth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: Try this sweet treat on pancakes, waffles, or French toast.
  • Cabot Cheese: Sample the award-winning cheese from the Plymouth Cheese Factory.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: These delicious treats are a fall favorite and can be found at local farm stands and bakeries.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Craft Beer: Sip on a cold one from one of Vermont’s many craft breweries.
  • Cider: Enjoy a glass of locally-made cider, a refreshing and cozy drink, especially during the fall months.
  • Maple Latte: Savor the flavor of maple with this delicious and unique coffee drink.

Where to Stay in Plymouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Echo Lake Inn: This charming inn features cozy rooms and an on-site restaurant serving farm-to-table fare.
  • Okemo Mountain Resort: Stay slope-side at this popular resort featuring luxurious accommodations and plenty of winter and summer activities.
  • The Inn at Water’s Edge: This cozy bed and breakfast offers beautiful views of Echo Lake and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Plymouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: Bring home a bottle of this sweet treat to enjoy for breakfast or as a gift for friends and family.
  • Wooden Cutting Boards: Pick up a beautiful handcrafted cutting board made by a local artisan.
  • Vermont Made Crafts and Jewelry: Browse local shops for unique crafts and jewelry made by Vermont artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Plymouth

Vermont’s culture is heavily influenced by its rural roots and farming heritage. It is known for its commitment to small businesses, local food, and sustainable living. The state is also home to a thriving arts community, with galleries and music venues throughout the state.

Best Time to Visit Plymouth

The best time to visit Plymouth is in the fall, when the trees change colors and the weather is mild. This is also when many festivals and events take place in the area. Winter is a popular time for skiing and other winter sports, while summer offers plenty of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plymouth

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Visit during the fall to experience the beautiful foliage.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Disrespect historical and cultural landmarks.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plymouth

Vermont is generally a safe state, but it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when traveling. The state does experience harsh weather conditions during the winter, so be prepared for snow and ice if visiting during the colder months.

Health in Plymouth

There are several medical facilities in the region, including hospitals and urgent care centers. It’s important to have adequate travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Plymouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plymouth

  • Rutland Southern Vermont Regional Airport: Located just 20 miles from Plymouth, this airport offers daily flights from Boston and New York City.
  • Burlington International Airport: This larger airport is located 90 miles from Plymouth and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Plymouth

  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers daily bus service to Rutland, Vermont, which is just a short drive from Plymouth.
  • Megabus: Megabus offers daily service to Burlington, Vermont, which is a bit farther away from Plymouth.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for exploring Vermont at your own pace.
  • Train: There are no direct trains to Plymouth, but you can take the Amtrak to Rutland, Vermont and rent a car or take a bus from there.

How to Get Around in Plymouth

The best way to get around Plymouth and the surrounding area is by car. There are also several taxi and shuttle services available, as well as bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Plymouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Woodstock, Vermont: Just a short drive from Plymouth, this charming town is known for its beautiful covered bridges and historic architecture.
  • The Green Mountains: Explore the mountains and rugged terrain of Vermont’s Green Mountains.
  • Manchester, Vermont: Enjoy shopping and fine dining in this quaint New England town.

Conclusion

Plymouth, Vermont is a charming destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. Whether you’re interested in hiking, skiing, or exploring museums and historic sites, there’s something for everyone in this picturesque town. Plan your visit today and discover all that Plymouth has to offer!
