White River Junction is a charming town located in Southern Vermont, nestled in the heart of New England. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture, it has become a popular destination for travelers seeking to explore the best of what this region has to offer. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through some of the best things to do, see, and experience in White River Junction, as well as where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

Introduction to White River Junction

Originally a bustling railroad hub, White River Junction has transformed itself into a cultural and artistic hub in recent years. The town still retains its historic charm, exemplified by its beautiful Victorian buildings and other architecturally significant structures. Today, White River Junction is known for its vibrant cultural scene, art galleries, museums, shops, restaurants, and more.

List of Places to Visit in White River Junction

Whether you’re interested in architecture, history, or outdoor adventures, visiting White River Junction is guaranteed to provide countless hours of entertainment and discovery. Below, we’ve compiled a list of places you should visit while in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Main Street Museum: This quirky museum is dedicated to preserving and sharing the history of White River Junction and beyond. It offers a wide variety of exhibitions and programming, including art shows, live music, film screenings, and more.

  • The Center for Cartoon Studies: This world-renowned cartooning school has put White River Junction on the map as a hub of graphic storytelling. Visitors can view exhibitions and purchase comics and graphic novels in its gallery.

  • The Tip Top Building: This beautifully restored historic former bakery building houses multiple artist studios, galleries, and creative businesses, including Northern Stage theater.

Natural Attractions of White River Junction: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hartford Town Forest: This beautiful forest offers miles of hiking and mountain biking trails, as well as stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Quechee Gorge State Park: Located just outside of town, this popular park features Vermont’s deepest gorge, as well as hiking trails, picnic areas, and more.

  • Montshire Museum of Science: Surrounded by beautiful forests and trails, this museum is dedicated to exploring the mysteries of the natural world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of White River Junction

White River Junction and its surrounding areas have some of the most picturesque hiking and biking trails that will leave you spellbound. Some of the best trails to visit include:

  • Northern Rail Trail: This trail spans 58 miles across central New Hampshire and is popular among hikers and bikers alike.

  • The Appalachian Trail: Just a short drive from White River Junction, the Appalachian Trail offers hikers an incredible experience through some of the Northeast’s most scenic landscapes.

  • Bill Ballard Trail: This trail is located within the Hartford Town Forest and features a great mix of single-track and double-track trails that are perfect for mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in White River Junction: Activities and Tours

White River Junction is jam-packed with activities and tours suitable for the whole family. Below are some of the best things you can do:

  • Take a Brewery Tour: Vermont is known for its delicious craft beer, and many local breweries offer tours of their facilities.

  • Visit the Local Farmers’ Market: The Upper Valley Farmers’ Market is a great place to shop for local produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

  • Explore the Art Scene: Art enthusiasts will love strolling through the many galleries and studios situated in White River Junction, including the Center for Cartoon Studies Gallery and the AVA Gallery.

Week-Long Itinerary for White River Junction

To make the most of your visit to White River Junction, we recommend spending at least a week in town. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of White River Junction

White River Junction boasts a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of local delights to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup: Vermont is known for its delicious maple syrup, and you can find it used in everything from pancakes to cocktails.

  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese: Vermont cheddar cheese is world-famous for its sharp flavor and creamy texture.

  • Ben & Jerry’s Ice Cream: White River Junction is just a short drive from the Ben & Jerry’s factory in Waterbury, where you can take a tour and sample some of their famous ice cream flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Craft Beer: Vermont has become known as a hub of craft beer, with breweries like Long Trail and Harpoon offering tours and tastings of their delicious brews.

  • Apple Cider: Vermont is also famous for its apple cider, which you can find at local farmers’ markets and cideries.

  • Maple Lattes: For a delicious caffeine fix, try a maple latte, made with locally sourced maple syrup.

Where to Stay in White River Junction: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

White River Junction has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some top picks:

  • Hilton Garden Inn: This hotel is located in the heart of town and offers clean, comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a fitness center and swimming pool.

  • The Hotel Coolidge: This historic hotel has been in operation since 1924 and offers charming guest rooms and a range of amenities, including an on-site restaurant and bar.

  • The Shire Woodstock: This stylish hotel is situated just a short drive from White River Junction and offers spacious guest rooms, an on-site spa, and a restaurant serving farm-to-table cuisine.

What to Buy in White River Junction: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in White River Junction, there are a few locally made products that you absolutely shouldn’t miss:

  • Maple Syrup: As mentioned earlier, Vermont maple syrup is world-renowned for its rich, delicious flavor.

  • Potters’ Crafts: The Upper Valley is home to several talented potters whose work can be found in many of the local shops and galleries.

  • Handmade Soaps and Lotions: Many local artisans make their own soaps and lotions, often using natural and organic ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of White River Junction

Vermonters are known for their warm hospitality and love of the outdoors. When visiting White River Junction, you can expect to encounter friendly locals who are passionate about their community, their history, and their culture. Be sure to respect the local customs and traditions, such as being mindful of the environment and avoiding littering or damaging natural areas.

Best Time to Visit White River Junction

The best time to visit White River Junction is during the fall season when the foliage is at its most vibrant. Alternatively, summer is an excellent time to explore the outdoors, while winter presents the perfect opportunity for skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of White River Junction

Do:

  • Explore the vibrant art scene on Main Street.
  • Visit the local farmers’ markets to sample delicious local produce.
  • Take a hike in the beautiful Hartford Town Forest.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Disrespect the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in White River Junction

White River Junction is a relatively safe town, with low levels of crime and a welcoming community. However, visitors should be cautious when exploring natural areas, as wildlife can be unpredictable. Always follow the posted safety signs and guidelines when in parks and natural areas.

Health in White River Junction

White River Junction has several excellent medical facilities, including the Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center and the White River Junction VA Medical Center. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance when traveling to this region.

Getting to White River Junction: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near White River Junction

The closest major airports to White River Junction are Burlington International Airport (BTV) and Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT), both approximately a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near White River Junction

White River Junction has a bus station that is serviced by Greyhound and Vermont Transit bus lines. The bus station is conveniently located in the town center, making it easy to reach by foot or taxi.

Other Options

White River Junction is also accessible by car, with Interstate 89 providing easy access to the town from nearby cities such as Boston and Montreal.

How to Get Around in White River Junction

White River Junction is a walkable town, with most attractions and amenities located within a short distance of the town center. For those who prefer not to walk, there are several taxi, Uber, and Lyft services available in the area.

Where to Go Next from White River Junction: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

White River Junction is situated in the heart of New England, making it an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations. Some popular places to visit include:

  • Killington Ski Resort: This world-class ski resort is just a short drive away and offers skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts the opportunity to carve up the slopes.

  • Hanover, NH: Located just across the river from White River Junction, Hanover is home to Dartmouth College and offers plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural experiences.

  • Woodstock, VT: This picturesque town is a short drive away and is home to the Billings Farm & Museum, as well as several art galleries, shops, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, White River Junction has something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your visit to this charming town and that you enjoy your stay in beautiful Southern Vermont.
