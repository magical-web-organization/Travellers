Introduction to Mount Snow

Nestled in the beautiful Green Mountains of southern Vermont, Mount Snow draws outdoor enthusiasts and culture seekers year-round. This picturesque area is known for its stunning natural scenery, rich history and culture, and thrilling sports and activities. Whether you’re looking to hit the slopes, hike through scenic trails, or immerse yourself in local history and culture, Mount Snow has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Snow

Looking for places to visit in Mount Snow? Here are some of the top attractions and must-see spots in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Vermont Museum of Mining and Minerals: The museum explores the rich mining history and mineral treasures that can be found in Vermont. Visitors can see stunning gemstones, fossils, and minerals, and learn about the science behind how they are formed.

  • The Historic Wilmington Walking Tour: Take a self-guided tour of Wilmington, a charming village known for its historic architecture, shops, and restaurants. Visit the local landmarks, including the Memorial Hall Center for the Arts and the Wilmington Town Hall.

  • The Molly Stark Byway: Discover the history of southwestern Vermont as you drive along the scenic Molly Stark Byway, a 48-mile route that takes you through picturesque villages, rolling hills, and stunning countryside.

Natural Attractions of Mount Snow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Snow: Hit the slopes at Mount Snow, one of the top skiing and snowboarding destinations on the East Coast. With over 600 acres of trails and over 100 ski runs, there is something for skiers and snowboarders of all levels.

  • Hiking and Biking Trails: Explore the scenic trails around Mount Snow on foot or by bike. Hike the trails in Green Mountain National Forest or pedal your way through the picturesque villages of Wilmington and Dover.

  • Lake Whitingham: Take in breathtaking views of the Green Mountains as you relax on the shores of Lake Whitingham. This beautiful lake offers opportunities for swimming, boating, kayaking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Snow

Get ready to hit the trails in Mount Snow! Here are some of our favorite hiking and biking routes:

  • Dover Town Trail: This easy 2-mile trail takes you through the heart of Dover. Enjoy beautiful views of the valley as you walk.

  • Haystack Mountain Trail: This moderate 3-mile trail is perfect for hikers looking for a challenge. Enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains from the summit.

  • Hogback Mountain Trail: This 2-mile trail is perfect for experienced hikers looking for a challenge. Enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys as you climb to the summit.

Best Things to Do in Mount Snow: Activities and Tours

Looking for some fun things to do in Mount Snow? Here are some of the top activities and tours in the area:

  • Snow Sports: Hit the trails at Mount Snow for skiing and snowboarding. The resort also offers other winter sports, such as snowshoeing and tubing.

  • Farm-to-Table Food Tours: Explore the local food scene with a farm-to-table food tour. Discover delicious local produce, cheeses, and meats, and learn about the unique food culture in Vermont.

  • Scenic Drives: Take a leisurely drive through the stunning countryside of Vermont. Explore the Molly Stark Byway, the Route 100 byway, or the Scenic Route 9.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Snow

Looking to explore the local food and drink scene? Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample in Mount Snow:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont Maple Syrup: Bring home a bottle of this sweet local treat, perfect for pancakes, waffles, and other breakfast favorites.

  • Cheddar Cheese: Vermont is known for its delicious cheddar cheese. Try a local variety, such as Cabot or Grafton Village Cheese.

  • Ben & Jerry’s Ice Cream: While it’s not made in Mount Snow, this famous Vermont ice cream brand is a must-try when visiting the region.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Craft Beer: Sample some of the local brews from Vermont’s many craft breweries, such as Long Trail Brewing Company, Magic Hat Brewing Company, and Harpoon Brewery.

  • Vermont Cider: Try some of the delicious ciders made by local Vermont cider companies, such as Citizen Cider and Woodchuck Cider.

Where to Stay in Mount Snow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Looking for accommodations in Mount Snow? Here are some top options:

  • Mount Snow Grand Summit Hotel: This upscale hotel is located right at the base of Mount Snow, offering easy access to skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

  • Deerhill Inn: This charming bed-and-breakfast in West Dover is known for its cozy rooms, delectable breakfasts, and picturesque views of the Green Mountains.

  • The Wilmington Inn: This historic inn in the heart of Wilmington offers comfortable rooms, outstanding dining options, and easy access to local attractions.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Snow

Want to immerse yourself in the local culture? Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of in Mount Snow:

  • Respect for Nature: Vermonters have a deep respect for the natural world. When exploring the outdoors, make sure to clean up after yourself and follow responsible hiking and camping practices.

  • Slow-Paced Lifestyle: Life in Vermont is slower-paced, with a focus on community and connection. Take the time to explore the local culture and connect with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Mount Snow

The best time to visit Mount Snow depends on your interests. Winters are perfect for skiing and snowboarding, while summers are ideal for hiking, camping, and water sports. Fall is a beautiful time to see the foliage, and spring offers opportunities for birdwatching and gardening.
