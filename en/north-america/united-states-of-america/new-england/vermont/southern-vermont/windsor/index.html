Introduction to Windsor

Windsor, Vermont is a quaint and charming town located in southern Vermont, in the New England region of the United States. Known as the birthplace of Vermont, this picturesque town is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty.

Visitors can explore the culturally important attractions such as the American Precision Museum and the Old Constitution House, or experience the natural beauty of Mount Ascutney and Lake Runnemede.

Here’s a comprehensive tour guide to Windsor, Vermont.

List of Places to Visit in Windsor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

American Precision Museum

Explore the history of manufacturing in America through the exhibits featuring machines, tools, and precision instruments that were produced in America.

Old Constitution House

Visit the birthplace of Vermont and explore the history of Vermont, including the drafting of the constitution.

Natural Attractions of Windsor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Ascutney

Enjoy hiking trails, mountain biking, and snowshoeing on the trails in Mount Ascutney State Park.

Lake Runnemede

Fish on the alpine lake, hike on trails that circle the lake, and picnic at Lake Runnemede.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windsor

Mount Ascutney State Park

A popular trail is the Windsor Trail, with an elevation gain of approximately 2,811 feet, which culminates in stunning views of the Connecticut River Valley.

Paradise Park

A hiking loop of moderate difficulty, with seasonal wildflowers and stunning views of Mount Ascutney.

Best Things to Do in Windsor: Activities and Tours

Culinary Tours of Vermont

A cultural experience in which visitors learn about Vermont’s culinary heritage and visit artisan food producers.

The Cornish-Windsor Bridge

Take a walk to the longest wooden bridge in the United States, offering spectacular views and photo opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Windsor

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the American Precision Museum and take a stroll on the Cornish-Windsor Bridge.
Day 2 Hike Mount Ascutney and explore its beautiful trails.
Day 3 Visit the Old Constitution House and then enjoy a picnic lunch at Lake Runnemede.
Day 4 Take a horse-drawn carriage ride through the countryside.
Day 5 Enjoy a culinary tour of Vermont and savor the unique tastes of the area.
Day 6 Visit local art galleries showcasing Vermont-makers.
Day 7 Explore Paradise Park and hike the trails with seasonal wildflowers.

Food and Drink Scene of Windsor

Must-Try Dishes

Vermont Maple Syrup

Vermont’s signature product, locally made maple syrup, is an essential ingredient in many of the dishes in the area.

Apple Cider Donuts

A delicious sweet pastry, made from the seasonal apple cider produced locally.

Local Beverages

Cold Hollow Cider Mill

A popular spot to enjoy the local apple cider, cider donuts, and freshly made apple pies.

Silo Distillery

A micro-distillery that offers a range of spirits, including vodka, gin, and whiskey.

Where to Stay in Windsor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Snapdragon Inn

A historic inn with luxurious rooms and scenic views, located downtown Windsor.

Hartness House

A Victorian mansion with a lovely restaurant and beautiful gardens, conveniently situated near local attractions.

What to Buy in Windsor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Artisanal Cheese

Local cheese produced just a few miles from downtown Windsor and served in restaurants throughout Vermont.

Vermont Maple Syrup

Bring home a taste of Vermont with a bottle of its signature maple syrup.

Local Culture and Customs of Windsor

Vermont takes pride in its small-town charm, where locals cherish their community and care for the environment. Visitors should take care not to litter or harm the natural beauty of the area while exploring.

Best Time to Visit Windsor

The best time to visit Windsor is from May to October, during the warmer months. The fall foliage is particularly stunning during late September and early October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Windsor

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Take care of the environment by keeping the area clean and following local regulations.
  • Hike, bike, and explore the rural roads and small villages in Vermont.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Violate any local laws or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Windsor

Windsor is a safe town, and visitors should take regular safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows.

Health in Windsor

The area outside of Windsor has some dense forest which may contain ticks. Visitors should take care to wear protective clothing and use insect repellent when venturing out into the woods and regularly check themselves for ticks.

Conclusion

Windsor, Vermont is the perfect destination for those looking for a charming and picturesque New England town. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of Mount Ascutney and Lake Runnemede, explore the region’s cultural heritage at the American Precision Museum or the Old Constitution House, and savor Vermont’s unique cuisine and beverages.
