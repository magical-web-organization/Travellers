Introduction to Southern Vermont

Nestled in the heart of New England, Southern Vermont is a scenic destination offering a wealth of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage. With foliage bursting into brilliant hues of red, gold, and orange in fall, snow-capped mountains in winter, blooming tulips in spring, and lush greenery in summer, Southern Vermont is a year-round delight for visitors. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you plan your trip to Southern Vermont, exploring the best places to visit, things to do, local culture, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Vermont

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a lover of art, history, and culture, Southern Vermont offers a range of museums and historical places that are sure to delight. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • The Bennington Museum - Featuring world-class art, regional history, and a notable collection of Grandma Moses artwork.
  • The American Museum of Fly Fishing - The perfect attraction for all fishing enthusiasts, this museum is dedicated to preserving the art and history of fly fishing.
  • Hildene: The Lincoln Family Home - Visit the home of Robert Todd Lincoln, the son of President Abraham Lincoln, and explore the restored 1905 Georgian Revival mansion and picturesque gardens.

Natural Attractions of Southern Vermont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Vermont is home to stunning natural attractions that offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. From hiking and biking trails to waterfalls and state parks, here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • Mount Equinox - Hike the 5.2-mile trail to the summit of Mount Equinox, Vermont’s highest peak, for stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Manchester Riverwalk - Stroll along this scenic walkway that runs alongside the Battenkill River and enjoy the gorgeous views of the waterfalls, bridges, and surrounding nature.
  • Stratton Mountain Resort - Enjoy skiing, snowshoeing, and other outdoor activities at this popular ski resort, known for its beautiful scenery and extensive trail system.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Vermont

Southern Vermont offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails, offering something for everyone from beginners to experienced hikers. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Long Trail - Spanning the entire length of Vermont, this 272-mile trail is the oldest long-distance hiking trail in the country.
  • Green Mountain National Forest - With over 900 miles of hiking trails spread across its 400,000-acre expanse, this national forest is a paradise for hikers and nature lovers.
  • Mount Snow Bike Park - Featuring 10 miles of lift-serviced downhill terrain for bikers of all skill levels, Mount Snow Bike Park is a thrilling adventure for adrenaline junkies.

Best Things to Do in Southern Vermont: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling alone or with family, Southern Vermont offers a host of exciting activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Southern Vermont:

  • Bromley Adventure Park - Enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including zip lines, mountain coasters, alpine slides, and more at this popular amusement park.
  • Southern Vermont Natural History Museum - Explore the natural history of Vermont and the Adirondack Mountains at this fascinating museum, which features an extensive collection of fossils, minerals, and live animal exhibits.
  • Vermont Country Store - Experience the charm of a bygone era at this iconic general store, which features all kinds of unique locally made products and vintage goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Vermont

If you’re planning to spend a week in Southern Vermont, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Vermont

Must-Try Dishes

Southern Vermont is known for its farm-to-table cuisine featuring fresh, locally-sourced ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese - Made from the milk of local cows, Vermont Cheddar Cheese is a delicious and quintessential Vermont specialty.
  • Maple Syrup - Vermont produces more than 40% of the country’s maple syrup, and it’s a delicious addition to pancakes, ice cream, and more.
  • Apple cider donuts - Made with locally-sourced apples and cider, these warm and spicy donuts are a fall favorite in Southern Vermont.

Local Beverages

Vermont is home to an array of craft breweries, cideries, and wineries that offer delicious and unique beverages. Some of the popular local beverages to try include:

  • Harpoon Brewery - Offering a variety of craft beers, Harpoon Brewery is a must-visit for beer lovers.
  • Champlain Orchards - This cidery produces a range of hard ciders made from locally-sourced apples and offers tastings and tours.
  • Boyden Valley Winery - Offering a range of locally grown and produced wines, Boyden Valley Winery is a great place to relax and enjoy a glass of wine amidst stunning scenery.

Where to Stay in Southern Vermont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Vermont offers a variety of accommodation options, from quaint B&Bs to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay in Southern Vermont:

  • The Equinox Golf Resort & Spa - This luxurious resort in Manchester is the perfect place to relax and unwind amidst stunning natural beauty.
  • The Governor’s Inn - Located in the heart of Ludlow, this cozy and charming inn offers comfortable rooms and easy access to top ski resorts.
  • Hostel Tevere - Offering affordable accommodation in a stunning location near the Mad River, Hostel Tevere is the perfect choice for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Southern Vermont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Vermont is known for its unique local products and specialties, making it the perfect place to shop for souvenirs. Here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Maple Syrup - A classic Vermont souvenir and favorite topping for pancakes, waffles, and more.
  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese - Made from the milk of local cows, Vermont Cheddar Cheese is a staple of Vermont cuisine.
  • Handmade Crafts - Southern Vermont is home to a range of artisans and crafters, who produce everything from pottery and jewelry to woodworking and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Vermont

Vermonters are known for their friendly, laidback, and independent nature. The state also has a strong sense of community, with many festivals and events celebrating local culture and traditions. Some of the popular festivals and events that are unique to Southern Vermont include:

  • The Vermont Summer Festival - An equestrian event held every summer in Manchester, featuring competitions, exhibitions, and more.
  • The Vermont Country Store Weston Christmas lights - A beloved holiday tradition where the small town of Weston is transformed into a magical winter wonderland.
  • The Rock River Artists Open Studio Tour - A chance to visit the studios of local artists and see their work up close.

Best Time to Visit Southern Vermont

The best time to visit Southern Vermont largely depends on what you want to do and see. For skiing and other winter sports, mid-December to early March is the best time to go. For fall foliage, late September to early October is peak season. In general, late spring and early summer are the best times to visit if you want to avoid crowds and enjoy mild weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Vermont

Do:

  • Sample the local cuisine, including Vermont Cheddar Cheese, maple syrup, and apple cider donuts.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the state by hiking, biking, or skiing.
  • Attend a local festival or event to experience Vermont culture and traditions firsthand.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  • Leave valuables unattended in your car or hotel room.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Vermont

Southern Vermont is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always important to be vigilant and take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows and avoiding secluded areas at night.

Health in Southern Vermont

Southern Vermont has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals, urgent care centers, and pharmacies. However, it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications and travel insurance when traveling to a new place.

Getting to Southern Vermont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Vermont

The nearest major airports to Southern Vermont include Albany International Airport (ALB) in Albany, NY (67 miles), Bradley International Airport (BDL) in Windsor Locks, CT (87 miles), and Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT) in Manchester, NH (104 miles).

Bus Stations Near Southern Vermont

The nearest major bus stations to Southern Vermont include Greyhound Bus Lines in Albany, NY (67 miles), Peter Pan Bus Lines in Springfield, MA (75 miles), and Vermont Translines in Brattleboro, VT (15 miles).

Other Options

Driving to Southern Vermont is also an option, as the state is well-connected to major highways and interstates, making it easy to navigate and explore once you arrive.

How to Get Around in Southern Vermont

Car rentals and taxis are widely available in Southern Vermont, making it easy to get around town and explore the surrounding countryside. Uber and Lyft are also available in larger towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Southern Vermont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Boston, Massachusetts - Just a three-hour drive from Southern Vermont, Boston is a world-class city with rich history, culture, and cuisine.
  • Lake George, New York - Located just over an hour’s drive south of Southern Vermont, Lake George offers stunning scenery, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions.
  • White Mountains, New Hampshire - This mountain range is just two hours away from Southern Vermont and offers breathtaking scenery, hiking trails, and skiing.

Conclusion

Southern Vermont is a stunning destination with something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re a lover of history, culture, nature, or adventure, you’re sure to find plenty to do and see in this beautiful part of New England. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip and explore the best of Southern Vermont.
