Introduction to Ludlow

Located in Southern Vermont, Ludlow is a charming town known for its scenic views, outdoor activities, and quaint New England architecture. The town has a rich history, with evidence of pre-colonial Native American settlements and influences from early European settlers. Today, Ludlow is a popular destination for tourists looking to discover authentic Vermont experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Ludlow

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Black River Academy Museum - Originally a schoolhouse built in 1835, the museum now houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing Ludlow’s rich history.

  • Fletcher Farm School for the Arts and Crafts - A non-profit arts and crafts school that offers classes and workshops, as well as a rotating gallery of locally made crafts.

  • Buttermilk Falls - A beautiful waterfall and park with trails and picnic areas.

Natural Attractions of Ludlow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Okemo Mountain Resort - A popular ski destination in the winter, Okemo also offers year-round activities such as hiking, mountain biking, and scenic gondola rides.

  • Lake Rescue - A picturesque lake offering boating, fishing, and swimming in the summer months.

  • Camp Plymouth State Park - A scenic state park with hiking trails, campgrounds, and a sandy beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ludlow

Ludlow offers several popular hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Healdville Trail - A moderate hike that leads to stunning views of Okemo Mountain and the surrounding countryside.

  • Hawks Mountain Trail - A challenging hike that rewards hikers with panoramic views of the Green Mountains.

  • Ludlow Valley Trail - An easy, scenic trail that winds through Ludlow’s historic district and along the Black River.

Best Things to Do in Ludlow: Activities and Tours

  • Cheese Making Workshop - Learn to make cheese from scratch at Plymouth Artisan Cheese, a local creamery that has been making cheese since 1890.

  • Vermont Country Store - A must-visit store that offers a wide range of locally made products, from maple syrup to handmade crafts.

  • Bike Rental - Rent a bike and explore Ludlow’s scenic trails at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ludlow

Day 1: Arrive in Ludlow and explore the historic district, including the Black River Academy Museum and the Buttermilk Falls.

Day 2-4: Spend days hiking, biking, and enjoying the scenic views at Okemo Mountain Resort and Camp Plymouth State Park.

Day 5: Take a cheese making workshop at Plymouth Artisan Cheese and visit the Vermont Country Store.

Day 6: Rent a bike and explore Ludlow’s scenic trails, including the Ludlow Valley Trail.

Day 7: Depart Ludlow.

Food and Drink Scene of Ludlow

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup - Vermont is known for its delicious maple syrup, and Ludlow has several local producers worth checking out.

  • Ben & Jerry’s Ice Cream - While not made in Ludlow, Ben & Jerry’s ice cream is a Vermont institution and can be found at many local shops and restaurants.

  • Craft Beer - Vermont has a thriving craft beer scene, and Ludlow is no exception. Check out Big Eyes Bakery and Cafe for a selection of local beers.

Local Beverages

  • Maple Syrup - In addition to its delicious taste, maple syrup is also the perfect ingredient for a variety of cocktails and coffee drinks.

  • Craft Beer - As mentioned, Ludlow has a great selection of local craft beers.

Where to Stay in Ludlow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jackson Gore Inn at Okemo Mountain Resort - Offering spacious rooms and easy access to the resort’s amenities.

  • The Pettigrew Inn - A charming bed and breakfast in the heart of Ludlow’s historic district.

  • The Combes Family Inn - A family-run inn with comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ludlow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup - A classic Vermont souvenir, Ludlow has several local producers to choose from.

  • Locally Made Crafts - Fletcher Farm School for the Arts and Crafts is a great place to find handmade gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ludlow

Ludlow is known for its friendly, welcoming residents and its appreciation for the natural beauty of the area. The town is proud of its history and local traditions, including maple sugaring in the spring and fall festivals celebrating the changing of the leaves.

Best Time to Visit Ludlow

The best time to visit Ludlow depends on your preferences. Summer is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and swimming, while winter offers world-class skiing and snowboarding at Okemo Mountain Resort. Fall is a popular time to visit, with the changing of the leaves creating a beautiful backdrop for scenic drives and hikes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ludlow

Do:

  • Do respect the area’s natural beauty by practicing Leave No Trace principles while hiking and exploring.

  • Do try the local maple syrup, craft beer, and other Vermont specialties.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.

  • Don’t take or disturb artifacts or objects from historical and cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ludlow

Ludlow is a safe town, but visitors should take the same precautions they would in any small town. Lock car doors and keep valuables out of sight, and be aware of your surroundings when walking at night.

Health in Ludlow

Ludlow has a hospital and several medical clinics, as well as a pharmacy and several grocery stores with a selection of basic health products.

Getting to Ludlow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ludlow

  • Rutland Southern Vermont Regional Airport (RUT), located approximately 25 miles from Ludlow.

  • Albany International Airport (ALB), located approximately 80 miles from Ludlow.

Bus Stations Near Ludlow

  • Greyhound Bus Lines stops in Springfield, VT, approximately 20 miles from Ludlow.

  • Vermont Translines offers bus service to Rutland, approximately 25 miles from Ludlow.

Other Options

Ludlow is easily accessible by car via major highways including Interstate 91 and Route 103. Visitors can also take Amtrak to Rutland or White River Junction, both approximately 25 miles from Ludlow.

How to Get Around in Ludlow

Ludlow is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, but visitors may also rent bikes or drive. There are several taxi and car rental services available for longer trips outside the town.

Where to Go Next from Ludlow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manchester - A picturesque town known for its outlet shopping and historic architecture, located approximately 30 miles from Ludlow.

  • Woodstock - A charming New England town with a historic downtown area, located approximately 45 miles from Ludlow.

  • Killington - A popular ski and mountain biking destination, located approximately 25 miles from Ludlow.

Conclusion

Ludlow offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience, whether they are seeking outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or small-town charm. With its rich history, friendly residents, and beautiful natural scenery, Ludlow is the perfect destination for anyone seeking an authentic Vermont experience.
