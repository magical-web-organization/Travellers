Introduction to Barre

Barre, a small city in Central Vermont, is known for its distinctive granite industry that has been an integral part of the city’s culture and history. The city also boasts several cultural and natural attractions, including museums and historical places, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and a thriving food and drink scene. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Barre, Vermont.

List of Places to Visit in Barre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rock of Ages - This granite quarry and visitor center offers guided tours to visitors, which tell the history of granite quarrying in the region.
  • Vermont Historical Society Museum - The museum features several exhibits that showcase the state’s rich history and culture.
  • Thunder Road International Speedbowl - This stock car racing track has been home to several racing events since its establishment in 1960.

Natural Attractions of Barre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Millstone Hill Touring Center - This outdoor adventure center offers several activities, including hiking, biking, and skiing, among others, and boasts beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Spruce Mountain Fire Tower - Visitors can climb this tower and enjoy panoramic views of Barre City and its surroundings.
  • Barre Town Forest - This forest offers several trails for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities, including a scenic loop around Perry Pond.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barre

  • Barre Heritage Trail - This 3.2-mile trail passes through the city’s historic downtown area, several neighborhoods, and parks.
  • VAST Trail - This snowmobiling trail is used for hiking and biking during the off-season and runs consecutively through many towns and cities in Vermont.
  • North Branch Trails - These trails offer several options for hiking, biking, and skiing, and range in difficulty from easy to strenuous.

Best Things to Do in Barre: Activities and Tours

  • Barre Opera House - This performing arts center offers several events, including plays, concerts, and other performing arts shows.
  • Hope Cemetery - This historic cemetery has several unique features, including granite sculptures and architecture.
  • Central Vermont Arts Center - This arts center features several exhibits, workshops, and classes on art and the creative process.

Food and Drink Scene of Barre

Must-Try Dishes

  • Creemies - A Vermont-style soft-serve ice cream that’s a must-try for visitors.
  • Pho Siagon Vietnam Restaurant - This restaurant offers several Vietnamese dishes, including banh mi, pho, and bubble tea.
  • K’s Corner Cafe - This restaurant offers several breakfast and lunch options, including sandwiches, soups, and salads.

Local Beverages

  • Switchback Brewing - This brewery offers several craft beers for visitors.
  • Green Mountain Coffee - Visitors can taste several coffee blends made by the renowned coffee company in Barre’s local cafes.
  • Citizen Cider - This cidery offers several flavors of cider, including traditional, apple, pear, and maple.

Where to Stay in Barre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hollow Inn and Motel - This motel offers several room options and amenities, including a pool and a game room.
  • Maplewood Lodge - This guesthouse offers several cozy vacation homes and suites.
  • Highland Lodge - This lodge offers several rooms and cabins, a restaurant, and several other amenities for visitors.

What to Buy in Barre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vermont Maple Syrup - Visitors can buy several grades of maple syrup produced by Barre’s maple farms.
  • Granite Souvenirs - Visitors can buy several souvenirs made from granite, including coasters, cutting boards, and more.
  • Vermont Cheese - Visitors can buy several cheese varieties, including cheddar, goat, and sheep cheese made by local farms.

Local Culture and Customs of Barre

Barre’s culture is heavily influenced by the granite industry, which has provided employment and shaped the city’s culture over the years. Visitors should expect to see several sculptures, architecture, and historical sites centered around granite in the city.

Best Time to Visit Barre

The best time to visit Barre is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and conducive for several outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and more. Additionally, the fall months (September to November) offer stunning foliage views and milder weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barre

Do:

  • Visit the city’s granite quarry and learn about the history of granite quarrying.
  • Taste Vermont’s famous maple syrup and cheese.
  • Respect the culture and history of the city, especially in terms of granite.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage historical sites and sculptures.
  • Ignore safety precautions while hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barre

Barre is generally a safe city; however, visitors should remain vigilant and take necessary precautions against pickpockets and petty crimes in crowded areas.

Health in Barre

Barre has several good medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, which provide quality healthcare services to residents and visitors alike.

Getting to Barre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barre

  • Burlington International Airport - 40 miles away from Barre.
  • Lebanon Municipal Airport - 68 miles away from Barre.

Bus Stations Near Barre

  • Greyhound Barre Downtown Stop - This bus station is in the city’s downtown area and offers several services to surrounding towns and cities.
  • Vermont Translines - This bus station is four miles away from the city center and offers several services to several towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Barre through several scenic routes or take a train or ferry from surrounding towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Barre

Visitors can get around Barre through several transportation options, including buses, taxis, and rental cars, among others. Additionally, visitors can also explore the city on foot or bikes.

Where to Go Next from Barre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Montpelier - The state capital of Vermont is just 12 miles away from Barre and offers several cultural and natural attractions, including museums, hiking trails, and shopping centers.
  • Waterbury - This town is 18 miles away from Barre and is the home to several breweries, including the renowned Alchemist Brewery.
  • Stowe - This town is 30 miles away from Barre and offers several year-round activities, including skiing, hiking, and biking trails.

Conclusion

Barre, Vermont, is a city steeped in history, culture, and stunning natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy several cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and a thriving food and drink scene in the city. A week-long itinerary, several transportation options, and nearby destinations make Barre the perfect destination for a vacation or a day trip.
