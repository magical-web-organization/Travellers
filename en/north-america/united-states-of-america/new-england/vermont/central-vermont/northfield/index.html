Introduction to Northfield

Northfield is a beautiful and serene town located in Central Vermont, nestled in the picturesque countryside of the New England region of the United States. Known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant cultural scene, Northfield attracts visitors from all over the world who come to explore its many attractions and experience its unique charm.

From hiking and biking to sightseeing and cultural exploration, there’s something for everyone in Northfield. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a week-long vacation, this guide will help you make the most of your time in this charming Vermont town.

List of Places to Visit in Northfield

Here are the top places to visit in Northfield, organized by category:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Red Water Brook Covered Bridge: This historic bridge dates back to 1872 and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Norwich University: The birthplace of the Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (ROTC), this prestigious university is the oldest private military college in the country.
  • Goodrich Memorial Library: Housed in a gorgeous Victorian-era building, this library is one of the many examples of Northfield’s rich architectural heritage.
  • Northfield Historical Society: Dedicated to preserving the town’s rich history and cultural heritage, the Northfield Historical Society has a fascinating collection of artifacts and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Northfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Northfield Falls: These stunning waterfalls are a must-see for nature lovers and photographers.
  • Dog River Recreation Area: Featuring a beautiful river and miles of hiking trails, this recreation area is a great place to explore the great outdoors.
  • Cox Brook Falls: Another breathtaking waterfall, Cox Brook Falls can be reached by hiking the nearby trail.
  • Goulet Farm: This idyllic farm offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, along with picturesque red barns and farmhouses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northfield

The hiking and biking scene in Northfield is one of the town’s biggest draws, thanks to its beautiful natural landscapes and scenic trails. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Norwich University Trails: These scenic trails wind through the lush grounds of Norwich University, providing hikers and bikers with lovely views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Mill Trail: This easy hiking trail meanders along the Mill Brook, offering hikers a relaxing and tranquil escape from the busy town center.
  • Paine Mountain Trail: For more experienced hikers, the Paine Mountain Trail offers a challenging climb and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Northfield: Activities and Tours

For those looking to explore Northfield’s many attractions, here are some of the best things to do:

  • Guided Tours: Learn about Northfield’s rich history and cultural heritage with a guided tour of the town. Options include walking tours, bus tours, and more.
  • Winter Sports: Northfield is a great destination for winter sports enthusiasts, with nearby ski resorts like Sugarbush and Mad River Glen offering some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the region.
  • Visiting Local Breweries: Northfield is home to a number of small breweries that offer tastings and tours, giving visitors a chance to sample some of the town’s best craft beers.

Food and Drink Scene of Northfield

The food and drink scene in Northfield is a standout feature of the town, with a variety of restaurants and cafes offering everything from classic American fare to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup: Vermont is famous for its maple syrup, and Northfield is no exception. Try some of the delicious maple syrup-glazed bacon or pancakes.
  • Mac and Cheese: Made with local Vermont cheddar cheese, Northfield’s take on this classic dish is sure to please.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: Made with freshly pressed apple cider, these donuts are a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Craft Beer: Northfield is home to several small breweries that offer a range of delicious craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and more.
  • Apple Cider: Freshly pressed apple cider is a local favorite, and can be found at many of Northfield’s cafes and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Northfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northfield offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few options:

  • The Inn at the Round Barn: This romantic bed and breakfast features cozy rooms and luxurious amenities, including an on-site spa.
  • The Northfield Inn: With affordable rooms and a great location in the heart of Northfield, the Northfield Inn is a popular choice for budget-conscious travelers.
  • The Falls Inn & Tavern: This historic inn offers charming guest rooms and an on-site tavern that serves up delicious meals and craft beer.

What to Buy in Northfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northfield is known for its artisanal products and locally made goods, making it a great destination for souvenir shopping. Here are some great products to bring home:

  • Maple Syrup: As mentioned earlier, Vermont is famous for its maple syrup, and Northfield has plenty of varieties to choose from.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Local potters create beautiful and unique pieces that make great gifts or souvenirs.
  • Handmade Soap: Made with natural ingredients, Northfield’s handmade soap is perfect for anyone looking to pamper themselves.

Local Culture and Customs of Northfield

Northfield’s culture is largely centered around its rich history and natural surroundings, with an emphasis on sustainability and community. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the town’s laid-back vibe and take part in its many cultural events and community activities.

Best Time to Visit Northfield

The best time to visit Northfield is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most vibrant. Winter is also a great time to visit for the winter sports scene, while summer can be quite warm but offers plenty of outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northfield

Do:

  • Embrace the town’s natural beauty and explore its many hiking and biking trails.
  • Sample some of the local craft beers and delicious Vermont cuisine.
  • Take part in Northfield’s many cultural events and community activities.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for the season and activities you plan to do.
  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas or on hiking trails.
  • Disrespect the town’s rich cultural heritage or architecture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northfield

Northfield is generally a safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming community. Visitors should exercise common sense and take typical precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables in hotel rooms.

Health in Northfield

Northfield has several medical facilities and clinics, as well as a local pharmacy. Visitors should bring any necessary medication or supplies with them, and be prepared for weather-related health issues like sunburn or heat exhaustion during the summer months.

Getting to Northfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northfield is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some options for getting to Northfield:

Airports Near Northfield

  • Burlington International Airport: Located about 45 minutes from Northfield, the Burlington airport offers a variety of flights from major airlines.
  • Lebanon Regional Airport: Located about 45 minutes from Northfield, the Lebanon airport offers regional flights from Cape Air.

Bus Stations Near Northfield

  • Montpelier Greyhound Station: Located about 15 minutes from Northfield, the Montpelier station offers bus service to major cities across the country.
  • Burlington Greyhound Station: Located about 45 minutes from Northfield, the Burlington station offers a similar range of bus services.

Other Options

For those traveling by car, Northfield is easily accessible from major highways like Interstate 89. For those coming from nearby cities like Boston or New York, driving may be the most convenient option.

How to Get Around in Northfield

Northfield is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. For those who need transportation, there are several taxi services and car rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Northfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you may want to consider after your visit to Northfield:

  • Montpelier: The capital city of Vermont, Montpelier is home to a number of cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.
  • Stowe: Known for its scenic vistas and popular ski resort, Stowe offers a variety of outdoor activities and tourist attractions.
  • Burlington: Vermont’s largest city, Burlington is a vibrant and cosmopolitan destination with a range of cultural and culinary offerings.

Conclusion

We hope you’ve enjoyed this comprehensive travel guide to Northfield, Vermont. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, skiing, or exploring the town’s rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone in this charming New England town. Come for a day or stay for a week – but be sure to take the time to discover everything that Northfield has to offer!
