Introduction to Stowe

Nestled in the Green Mountains of Central Vermont, Stowe is a picturesque town that attracts nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. Offering an array of outdoor activities, breathtaking scenery, a rich cultural heritage, and a welcoming community, Stowe is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Stowe, providing all the necessary information and insights to make your trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Stowe

Stowe has many attractions to offer for all kinds of travelers. Whether you’re interested in museums and historical places or outdoor adventures and scenic views, Stowe has something for everyone. Here’s a list of some of the top places to visit in Stowe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vermont Ski and Snowboard Museum
  • Stowe Historical Society Museum
  • Trapp Family Lodge Mozart Room
  • Helen Day Art Center

Natural Attractions of Stowe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Mansfield
  • Smugglers Notch State Park
  • Bingham Falls
  • Stowe Recreation Path

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stowe

Stowe is famous for its hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Stowe:

  • Long Trail/ Vermont’s highest peak, Mount Mansfield
  • Stowe Pinnacle Trail
  • Waterbury Trail
  • Cady Hill Forest

Best Things to Do in Stowe: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Stowe offers a range of activities and tours that are perfect for those looking for a unique and exciting experience. Here are some of the best things to do in Stowe:

  • Zip-lining at Stowe Mountain Resort
  • Dog sledding with Peace Pups Dogsledding
  • Horseback riding at Gentle Giants Carriage Rides and Sleighs
  • Hot air balloon rides with Above Reality Hot Air Balloon Rides

Week-Long Itinerary for Stowe

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Stowe, here’s a suggested itinerary that includes all the top attractions and activities:

Day Activity
1 Hike up Mount Mansfield
2 Explore Stowe Village and visit the Vermont Ski and Snowboard Museum

5 Go dog sledding with Peace Pups Dogsledding
6 Explore Smugglers Notch State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Stowe

Stowe is a foodie’s paradise, offering an array of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you should not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vermont cheese plate
  • Apple cider donuts
  • Poutine (fries with cheese curds and gravy)
  • Lobster Bisque

Local Beverages

  • Vermont craft beers from The Alchemist, Lawson’s Finest Liquids, and Hill Farmstead Brewery
  • Freshly pressed apple cider from Cold Hollow Cider Mill
  • Maple syrup and maple whiskey from Stowe Maple Bourbon

What to Buy in Stowe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local artisanal cheese

Local Culture and Customs of Stowe

Stowe is a small town with a proud and unique culture. The locals are known for their hospitality, outdoor lifestyle, and love of the arts. When visiting Stowe, it’s important to be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Stowe

Stowe is a popular destination all year round, with each season offering its unique beauty and activities. If you’re looking to experience the autumn foliage, September and October are the best months. For winter sports and activities, December to February is the ideal time to visit. Summer in Stowe is a great time for hiking, biking, and exploring the outdoors, with June to August being the busiest months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stowe

When visiting Stowe, it’s essential to know the local customs and etiquette to make your trip unforgettable. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and the local wildlife
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their way of life
  • Take plenty of photos of the breathtaking scenery

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment
  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their customs
  • Ignore the warnings and safety guidelines for outdoor activities
  • Get too close to wild animals or disturb their habitat

Safety and Health

Safety in Stowe

Stowe is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s important to take precautions when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, such as checking the weather forecast, wearing appropriate gear, and carrying a map and compass.

Health in Stowe

Stowe has a well-developed healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics in the area. However, it’s always a good idea to carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medication with you when traveling.

Getting to Stowe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Stowe is located in Central Vermont, approximately 45 miles east of Burlington International Airport. There are several transportation options available for getting to Stowe:

Airports Near Stowe

  • Burlington International Airport (BTV) (45 miles)
  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS) (200 miles)
  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport (YUL) (120 miles)

Bus Stations Near Stowe

  • Greyhound Bus Station (44 miles)
  • Megabus Bus Station (44 miles)

Other Options

  • Rent a car from Burlington Airport
  • Hire a taxi or shuttle service to Stowe

How to Get Around in Stowe

Stowe is a small town that is easy to navigate by foot or bicycle. Several rental car services are also available in the area, making it convenient for tourists to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Stowe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stowe is surrounded by several charming towns and natural attractions that are worth visiting. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Montpelier, Vermont’s capital city (30 miles)
  • Lake Champlain and Burlington, Vermont’s largest city (45 miles)
  • The Ben & Jerry’s Factory in Waterbury, Vermont (12 miles)
  • The Cabot Creamery in Cabot, Vermont (30 miles)

Conclusion

Stowe is a magical destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or delicious cuisine, Stowe will not disappoint.
