Introduction to Jeffersonville

Jeffersonville is a small town in central Vermont, known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreation, and historic charm. The town is surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests, and sparkling streams, making it an ideal destination for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers. But there’s more to Jeffersonville than just outdoor adventures; the town also boasts a rich cultural heritage, with many museums, galleries, and historical sites to explore. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, read on to discover the best things to do, places to stay, and local culture of Jeffersonville.

List of Places to Visit in Jeffersonville

As one of the top destinations in Vermont, here are some of the must-visit places in Jeffersonville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Smugglers’ Notch Distillery: Take a tour of the award-winning distillery and learn about the history and craft of making spirits. You can also sample the products and purchase a bottle to take home.
  • Johnson State Historic Site: Explore the restored home of William Samuel Johnson, a prominent figure in early American politics. The site also features a museum, gardens, and walking trails.
  • Vermont Folklife Center: The center is dedicated to preserving and sharing the traditions and culture of Vermont through exhibits, workshops, and performances.

Natural Attractions of Jeffersonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Smugglers’ Notch Resort: The resort offers year-round activities, including skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and zip-lining. Enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Sterling Pond: Hike to the top of Sterling Mountain, where you’ll find a pristine alpine pond. The hike is challenging but worth the effort for the breathtaking views.
  • Lamoille Valley Rail Trail: This 93-mile trail runs through Jeffersonville and surrounding towns. Enjoy a leisurely bike ride or walk along the scenic route.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jeffersonville

Jeffersonville is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with miles of trails to explore. Here are a few of the best:

  • Sterling Pond Trail: This challenging 2.9-mile trail takes you to the top of Sterling Mountain, where you’ll find a pristine alpine pond.
  • Hellbrook Trail: This strenuous hike is not for the faint of heart, but it rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Lamoille Valley Rail Trail: This 93-mile trail runs through Jeffersonville and surrounding towns, offering a leisurely biking or walking route through the scenic countryside.

Best Things to Do in Jeffersonville: Activities and Tours

  • Smugglers’ Notch Resort: The resort offers year-round activities, including skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and zip-lining. Enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Brewery Tour: Take a tour of the local breweries and sample some of Vermont’s best craft beers.
  • Scenic Drives: Explore the winding back roads of Jeffersonville and enjoy the panoramic views of the mountains and valleys.

Food and Drink Scene of Jeffersonville

Vermont is known for its farm-to-table cuisine and craft beverages, and Jeffersonville is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup: Vermont produces some of the best maple syrup in the world, so be sure to try it on pancakes, waffles, or even ice cream.
  • Farm-to-Table Fare: Many restaurants in Jeffersonville source their ingredients from local farms, so be sure to try the fresh, seasonal dishes.
  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese: Vermont is famous for its cheddar cheese, made from local milk and aged to perfection. Try it on a sandwich or with crackers.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Vermont has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering tours and tastings.
  • Cider: Vermont is also known for its hard cider, made from locally grown apples.
  • Maple Syrup: Maple syrup isn’t just for pancakes. Try it in a cocktail, coffee, or even on ice cream for a unique Vermont twist.

Where to Stay in Jeffersonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jeffersonville offers a variety of accommodations, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious resorts. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Smugglers’ Notch Resort: This award-winning resort offers a range of accommodations, from mountain lodges to condominiums. Enjoy year-round activities and stunning mountain views.
  • The Cambridge House: This charming bed and breakfast features six guest rooms and homemade breakfasts made from local ingredients.
  • Sterling Ridge Resort: This rustic resort offers cozy cabins and cottages, with access to hiking and biking trails, a swimming pool, and a hot tub.

What to Buy in Jeffersonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Vermont with these local products and souvenirs:

  • Maple Syrup: Vermont is known for its exceptional maple syrup, and you can buy it in many local shops and farms.
  • Vermont Cheddar Cheese: Bring home a block of aged cheddar cheese, made from local milk and aged to perfection.
  • Artisan Crafts: Vermont is home to many talented artisans, and you can find their work in local galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Jeffersonville

Vermonters are known for their independent spirit, love of nature, and commitment to community. Respect the local culture by being friendly and polite, supporting local businesses, and enjoying the natural beauty of the state.

Best Time to Visit Jeffersonville

Jeffersonville is beautiful year-round, but each season offers its own charms. Visit in the fall for stunning foliage, in the winter for skiing and snow sports, in the spring for wildflowers and maple sugar season, or in the summer for outdoor adventures and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jeffersonville

Do:

  • Do enjoy the outdoors and appreciate the local natural beauty.
  • Do try the local food and drink, especially the craft beers and maple syrup.
  • Do support local businesses, including farmers, artisans, and small shops.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t ignore safety rules and guidelines, especially while enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jeffersonville

Jeffersonville is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s important to take precautions while enjoying outdoor activities. Be sure to wear appropriate clothing and gear, follow trail markers and safety guidelines, and be aware of changes in weather conditions.

Health in Jeffersonville

Vermont is known for its clean air and water, and visitors can enjoy fresh, locally sourced food and beverages. However, it’s important to take precautions against tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease, by wearing insect repellent and checking for ticks after outdoor activities.

Getting to Jeffersonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jeffersonville

The nearest airport to Jeffersonville is Burlington International Airport, which is about 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Jeffersonville

There are no bus stations in Jeffersonville, but Greyhound and Megabus both offer service to nearby Burlington.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Vermont, especially if you plan to explore the surrounding towns and countryside. Jeffersonville is also bike-friendly, with many local trails and bike rental options available.

How to Get Around in Jeffersonville

The best way to get around Jeffersonville is by car or bike. There are also local shuttle services and taxi companies available.

Where to Go Next from Jeffersonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more to do in Vermont, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Stowe: This charming town is known for its skiing, shopping, and dining, as well as its stunning fall foliage.
  • Burlington: Vermont’s largest city offers a bustling downtown area, a beautiful waterfront, and a variety of cultural attractions.
  • Ben & Jerry’s Factory: Take a tour of the famous ice cream factory and try some of their signature flavors.

Conclusion

Jeffersonville is a hidden gem in Vermont, offering a diverse array of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local charm. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Jeffersonville has something for everyone. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and discover all that this beautiful town has to offer.
