Introduction to Morrisville

Nestled in the heart of Central Vermont, Morrisville is a charming town that welcomes visitors with its breathtaking natural beauty and vibrant culture. With access to some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the country, as well as world-class hiking and biking trails, Morrisville is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. In addition, this quaint town is known for its rich history, lively arts scene, and friendly locals. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural immersion, or a relaxing getaway, Morrisville has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Morrisville

If you’re planning a trip to Morrisville, be sure to check out these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rock Art Brewery: Located on the outskirts of Morrisville, this award-winning craft brewery is known for its creative and flavorful brews. Stop by for a tour and tasting, or enjoy a pint in the cozy tasting room.
  • Vermont Ski and Snowboard Museum: This small but impressive museum showcases the history of skiing and snowboarding in Vermont, from its early beginnings to the modern era.
  • Stowe Historical Society Museum: This museum features exhibits on the history of Stowe and the surrounding area, including early settlement, agriculture, transportation, and tourism.
  • Copley Hospital: A not-for-profit community hospital, Copley has been serving the residents of Morrisville and the surrounding area for over 100 years. Visitors are welcome to take a tour and learn about the history and operations of this vital community institution.

Natural Attractions of Morrisville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Mansfield: At 4,393 feet, Mount Mansfield is the highest peak in Vermont and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Hiking trails of varying difficulty levels are available for visitors to explore.
  • Lamoille Valley Rail Trail: This 93-mile trail runs through Morrisville and is perfect for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing. It passes through picturesque countryside, including farmland, forests, and wetlands.
  • Smugglers’ Notch State Park: This park is known for its rugged beauty, with steep cliffs, boulder-strewn streams, and dense forests. Visitors can hike, bike, or simply enjoy the scenery.
  • Green River Reservoir: This pristine reservoir is perfect for canoeing, paddleboarding, and kayaking. With over 19 miles of shoreline, it’s also a popular spot for fishing, swimming, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morrisville

Morrisville is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in the country. Here are a few of the most popular:

  • Stowe Pinnacle Trail: This moderate 3.7-mile hike offers stunning views of the Green Mountains and the village of Stowe.
  • Catamount Trail: This 300-mile trail runs the length of Vermont, passing through Morrisville on its way from Canada to Massachusetts. It’s ideal for backcountry skiing, snowshoeing, hiking, and mountain biking.
  • Lamoille Valley Rail Trail: As mentioned above, this trail offers 93 miles of scenic beauty, perfect for bicycling, walking, and cross-country skiing.

Best Things to Do in Morrisville: Activities and Tours

Looking for more things to do in Morrisville? Check out these popular activities and tours:

  • Stowe Mountain Resort: Just a few miles from Morrisville, this world-class ski resort offers some of the best skiing and snowboarding on the East Coast. In the summer, it’s also a popular spot for hiking, mountain biking, and zip-lining.
  • Morrisville Farmers’ Market: Held every Saturday from May to October, this lively market features fresh produce, baked goods, artisan crafts, and live music.
  • Canoe/Kayak Tours: Several local outfitters offer guided tours of the nearby waterways, including the Lamoille River and Green River Reservoir.
  • Fly Fishing: The rivers and streams around Morrisville are known for their excellent trout fishing. Several local guides offer fly fishing trips for anglers of all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Morrisville

No trip to Morrisville would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are a few must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple syrup: Vermont is famous for its maple syrup, and Morrisville is no exception. Try it on pancakes, waffles, or ice cream.
  • Vermont cheese: With its rolling hills and lush pastures, Vermont produces some of the best cheese in the country. Sample some at a local cheese shop or farmer’s market.
  • Apple cider donuts: These delicious treats are a fall staple in Vermont. Visit a local orchard to try them fresh.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Vermont is home to over 50 craft breweries, including several in and around Morrisville. Try a pint at Rock Art Brewery, Lost Nation Brewing, or The Alchemist.
  • Hard cider: Vermont is also known for its hard cider, with several local producers offering unique and flavorful varieties.
  • Maple syrup: In addition to its culinary uses, maple syrup is also a popular ingredient in cocktails and other alcoholic beverages.

Where to Stay in Morrisville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Morrisville offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Here are a few options:

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites: A modern and comfortable hotel located just outside of Morrisville, with easy access to Stowe and other nearby attractions.
  • The Farmhouse Tap & Grill: This popular restaurant also offers cozy guest rooms upstairs, each decorated in a rustic farmhouse style.
  • The Riverbend Lodge: Located at the base of Stowe Mountain Resort, this lodge offers ski-in/ski-out access and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Airbnb: For a more authentic local experience, consider renting a private room or entire home on Airbnb.

What to Buy in Morrisville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home from Morrisville? Here are a few local products to consider:

  • Handcrafted artisan goods: Check out the Morrisville Farmers’ Market or local craft fairs for unique and handmade items like jewelry, pottery, and textiles.
  • Vermont cheese and other gourmet foods: Stock up on some of Vermont’s delicious cheeses and other locally made gourmet foods to take home as gifts or for your own enjoyment.

Local Culture and Customs of Morrisville

Morrisville is a welcoming and friendly community, with a proud New England heritage. Visitors should be aware of the following local customs and cultural practices:

  • Small town etiquette: Morrisville is a small town, and locals tend to be polite and friendly. Be sure to say “hello” and “thank you” to everyone you interact with, and be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Winter sports culture: Morrisville is an active and outdoorsy community, with a strong ski and snowboard culture in the winter. Even if you’re not an experienced skier or snowboarder, be sure to take advantage of the many winter sports activities in the area.
  • Maple syrup obsession: As mentioned above, Vermonters take their maple syrup very seriously. Don’t be surprised if every other shop or restaurant you visit offers maple syrup as a topping or ingredient.

Best Time to Visit Morrisville

Morrisville is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Here’s a breakdown by season:

  • Winter: Morrisville is a winter wonderland, with some of the best skiing and snowboarding on the East Coast. If you’re a winter sports enthusiast, this is the time to visit. Average temperatures range from 10-30 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Spring: Vermont’s spring is short but sweet, with maple sugaring season in full swing in March and April. Average temperatures range from 35-60 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Summer: Morrisville is a popular summer destination, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities. Average temperatures range from 60-80 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Fall: Vermont’s fall foliage season is legendary, with brilliant displays of red, orange, and gold leaves. This is also a great time for hiking and apple picking. Average temperatures range from 45-65 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morrisville

When visiting Morrisville, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the wilderness: Morrisville is home to some of the most beautiful natural areas in the country. Respect the environment by leaving no trace, following trail guidelines, and avoiding sensitive areas.
  • Support local businesses: Morrisville is a tight-knit community with many great local businesses. Support them by shopping at local stores, eating at local restaurants, and attending local events.
  • Keep an open mind: Morrisville is a unique and quirky place with its own customs and traditions. Keep an open mind, and be willing to try new things and meet new people.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute: Morrisville prides itself on its clean and pristine environment. Don’t litter or pollute, and be mindful of your impact on the environment.
  • Ignore the weather: Vermont weather can be unpredictable and change rapidly. Be prepared for all kinds of weather, and check the forecast regularly.
  • Assume everyone is a tourist: Morrisville is a popular destination, but it’s also home to many year-round residents. Don’t assume everyone you meet is a tourist, and be respectful of the local community.

Safety and Health

Morrisville is a safe and healthy place to visit, with access to quality medical care and emergency services. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Safety in Morrisville

  • Wildlife: Morrisville is home to a variety of wildlife, including black bears, moose, and coyotes. Be aware of your surroundings and follow appropriate safety guidelines when encountering wildlife.
  • Winter sports safety: If you plan to ski or snowboard in Morrisville, be sure to wear appropriate safety gear and follow guidelines for safe winter sports practices.
  • Bicycle safety: If you plan to bike in Morrisville, be sure to wear a helmet and follow the rules of the road.

Health in Morrisville

  • Medical care: Morrisville has a few medical clinics and hospitals, including Copley Hospital, which offers a range of medical services.
  • Tick-borne illnesses: Lyme disease and other tick-borne illnesses are a concern in Vermont. Be sure to take precautions, such as wearing long pants and sleeves, using insect repellent, and checking for ticks after spending time outdoors.

Getting to Morrisville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Morrisville is a small town, but it’s easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are a few options:

Airports Near Morrisville

  • Burlington International Airport: Located about 45 minutes from Morrisville, this airport offers flights to several major US cities, including New York, Boston, and Chicago.
  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport: Located about 2 hours from Morrisville, this airport offers flights to several international destinations, as well as domestic flights within Canada.

Bus Stations Near Morrisville

  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers bus service to several nearby towns, including Montpelier, St. Albans, and Burlington.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental agencies operate out of Burlington International Airport, making it easy to rent a car for your stay in Morrisville.
  • Private car service: Several private car services are available in the area, offering comfortable transportation to and from Morrisville and surrounding towns.

How to Get Around in Morrisville

Morrisville is a small town, and is easily navigable on foot or by bicycle. However, if you need transportation, there are a few options:

  • Car rental: Several car rental agencies operate out of Burlington International Airport, making it easy to rent a car for your stay in Morrisville.
  • Local taxis: Several local taxi companies operate in the Morrisville area, providing affordable and convenient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Morrisville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re visiting Morrisville, consider taking a day trip or two to nearby attractions. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Stowe: Just a few miles from Morrisville, Stowe is a picturesque New England town with a lively arts scene, great restaurants, and world-class skiing and snowboarding.
  • Burlington: Located about 45 minutes from Morrisville, Burlington is a vibrant college town on the shore of Lake Champlain. It’s home to several museums, galleries, and breweries, as well as a thriving music and arts scene.
  • White Mountains National Forest: Just over the border in New Hampshire, the White Mountains offer some of the most spectacular scenery in New England, with hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.
