Introduction to Erving

Erving is a charming town located in the heart of Pioneer Valley, Massachusetts. This picturesque destination is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, including the Connecticut River and the lush forests of the Berkshire Mountains. Erving is the perfect place to relax and unwind, reconnect with nature, and explore the rich history and culture of New England.

List of Places to Visit in Erving

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Erving Historical Society Museum

The Erving Historical Society Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. This small museum showcases the town’s history, from its early settlers to its industrial past. The museum’s highlight is its collection of artifacts related to the town’s paper mills, which were once the backbone of Erving’s economy.

Millers River Environmental Center

The Millers River Environmental Center offers a variety of attractions for nature enthusiasts. The center features exhibits on the plants, animals, and geology of the Millers River watershed, as well as the history of local Native American tribes. Visitors can also take part in guided nature walks, workshops, and educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Erving: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Erving State Forest

Erving State Forest is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The forest covers over 1,800 acres and features miles of hiking trails, fishing spots, swimming areas, and picnic grounds. Visitors can also enjoy stunning views of the nearby mountains and valleys.

Millers River

Millers River is a beautiful spot for kayaking, fishing, and swimming. The river is known for its clean water and abundant wildlife, including bald eagles, ospreys, and beavers. Visitors can rent kayaks and canoes from local outfitters and explore the river at their own pace.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Erving

Pocumtuck Ridge Trail

The Pocumtuck Ridge Trail is a 22-mile hiking and biking trail that winds through the hills of Pioneer Valley. The trail offers stunning views of the Connecticut River and the surrounding countryside. The path is well-marked and easy to navigate, making it a great choice for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Erving: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding at Saddle Up Stables

Saddle Up Stables offers guided horseback riding tours that take visitors through the scenic countryside surrounding Erving. The tours are suitable for all skill levels and offer a unique perspective on the area’s natural beauty.

Brewery Tour at Abandoned Building Brewery

The Abandoned Building Brewery is a local craft brewery that offers guided tours of its facilities. Visitors can learn about the brewing process and taste some of the brewery’s award-winning beers.

Food and Drink Scene of Erving

Must-Try Dishes

New England Clam Chowder

New England Clam Chowder is a classic dish that is found on many local menus. This creamy soup is made with fresh clams, potatoes, and onions, and is sure to warm you up on a chilly day.

Lobster Roll

Lobster Roll is another New England staple. This delicious sandwich is made with fresh lobster meat, mayonnaise, and celery, and is served on a buttered roll.

Local Beverages

Samuel Adams Beer

Samuel Adams beer is a local favorite and can be found in many bars and restaurants throughout Erving. This popular beer is brewed in Boston and comes in a variety of styles, including lagers, ales, and IPAs.

Apple Cider

Apple cider is a seasonal favorite in New England. This refreshing beverage is made with freshly squeezed apples and is perfect for sipping on a cool fall day.

Where to Stay in Erving: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn at Clark’s

The Inn at Clark’s is a charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities. The inn is located near many of Erving’s top attractions and is the perfect place to relax after a busy day of sightseeing.

The Lake House Guest Cottages of the Berkshires

The Lake House Guest Cottages of the Berkshires offers cozy cottages with stunning views of the nearby lake. The cottages are fully equipped with kitchens, living rooms, and bedrooms, and are the perfect home away from home.

What to Buy in Erving: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maple Syrup

Maple syrup is a local favorite and can be found in many gift shops throughout Erving. This delicious syrup is made from the sap of local maple trees and is perfect for drizzling over pancakes or waffles.

Handmade Jewelry and Crafts

Handmade jewelry and crafts are a great way to bring a piece of Erving home with you. Local artisans create unique jewelry, pottery, and art that make the perfect souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Erving

Erving, like many New England towns, has a rich history and culture that is deeply rooted in its industrial past. Visitors can learn about the town’s history and customs by visiting museums and historical sites, attending local events, and sampling the local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Erving

The best time to visit Erving is during the fall, when the trees are changing colors and the weather is cool and crisp. This is also the best time to sample seasonal favorites like apple cider and pumpkin pie.

Safety and Health

Safety in Erving

Erving is a safe destination, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

Health in Erving

Erving has several medical clinics and hospitals that offer emergency and non-emergency medical care. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them and be aware of local pharmacies and medical facilities.

Getting to Erving: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Erving

The nearest major airport to Erving is Bradley International Airport, which is located in Hartford, Connecticut, about 50 miles south of Erving.

Bus Stations Near Erving

The nearest bus station to Erving is the Greyhound bus station in Greenfield, Massachusetts, which is about 12 miles from Erving.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Erving by car, bike, or on foot. Erving is conveniently located near several major highways, including Interstate 91 and the Massachusetts Turnpike.

How to Get Around Erving

The best way to get around Erving is by car. Visitors can rent cars from several local rental agencies and explore the area at their own pace. There are also several taxi services and rideshare companies available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Erving: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pioneer Valley

Pioneer Valley is a beautiful region that is located just south of Erving. This area is known for its rolling hills, historic towns, and vibrant arts scene.

Berkshire Mountains

The Berkshire Mountains are located about 50 miles west of Erving and offer stunning vistas, vibrant culture, and quaint towns.

Conclusion

Erving is a charming destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, you’re sure to find plenty to explore in this beautiful New England town.
