Introduction to Amherst

Welcome to Amherst, a small town located in Pioneer Valley, Massachusetts. Known for its rich cultural offerings and stunning natural beauty, Amherst is a must-visit destination. Full of history and charm, the town offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and cozy accommodations.

List of Places to Visit in Amherst

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich history or taking in the scenic views, Amherst has something for everyone. Here is a list of top places to visit in Amherst:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amherst is home to several museums and historical sites that offer visitors a glimpse into the town’s rich history and culture. Here are our recommendations:

  • Emily Dickinson Museum: Visit the home of one of America’s most celebrated poets and take a guided tour through her house, where she wrote nearly all of her poems.
  • Amherst College Museum of Natural History: Explore the museum’s collections of animals, fossils, and cultural artifacts.
  • The Eric Carle Museum of Picture Book Art: This family-friendly museum showcases picture book art and provides hands-on experiences for children.

Natural Attractions of Amherst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Amherst’s natural beauty is one of its highlights, with many parks and nature reserves perfect for outdoor activities. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Mount Norwottuck: Hike the trails of this mountain for some stunning vistas of Pioneer Valley.
  • Puffers Pond: Enjoy a sunny day on the beach or kayaking in the pond.
  • Amethyst Brook Conservation Area: Take a peaceful walk on the trails surrounded by wildlife, streams, and trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amherst

Amherst has a variety of hiking and biking routes for visitors to enjoy. Here are the top routes:

  • Robert Frost Trail: This trail takes you through woodlands, meadows, and streams, with historical and literary landmarks in the way.
  • Amherst Rail Trail: This bike-friendly trail follows the former Boston and Maine rail line and spans from Amherst to Northampton.

Best Things to Do in Amherst: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the town’s many attractions, Amherst offers visitors a range of activities and tours. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Amherst Farm Winery Tour: Take a tour of the winery and cider house and sample some of the award-winning wines, ciders, meads and with farm-to-table meals.
  • Ghost Tours of Amherst: A tour of the town is great, but the night became even more exciting with this spooky ghost tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amherst

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Amherst:

Day 1 Visit the Emily Dickinson Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Amherst

Amherst offers many dining options, from casual cafes to fine dining. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast Beef Sandwich: A local favorite to try– roast beef layered with horseradish mayo, caramelized onions, and melted cheese.
  • Pesto Pasta: Simple and delicious with fresh pesto, tomatoes, and parmesan.
  • Blueberry and Cream Cheese Pie: Perfectly baked crust filled with blueberries and cream cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Amherst Ale: Beer brewed by the Amherst Brewing Company with distinctive flavor thanks to local hops and grains.
  • Maple Syrup: Mass-produced syrup available locally using native maple trees.

Where to Stay in Amherst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amherst has a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are our top picks:

  • The Lord Jeffery Inn: A luxurious option located in the heart of Amherst.

  • Amherst Inn: A bed and breakfast that once welcomed Ralph Waldo Emerson, and known for its historic charm.

  • Allen House Victorian Inn: A hostel in the heart of Amherst, perfect for budget-friendly travelers.

What to Buy in Amherst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products to take a piece of Amherst home with you:

  • Hand-Crafted Pottery: Get individually handcrafted pottery from the North Leverett pottery studio.

  • Handmade Paper Products: Hampshire College Book Arts Workshop creates products such as journals, cards, and other notebooks with handmade paper.

Local Culture and Customs of Amherst

Amherst has its own unique customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Tipping: Tipping is usual in America, and generally expected in the service industry with 20% as the standard tip percentage.

  • Laws: In Massachusetts, smoking is not allowed in restaurants, bars and workplaces.

Best Time to Visit Amherst

The best time to visit Amherst is in the fall (September-November), when the colors are picturesque. The temperatures are usually mild in September and October, and the crowds are not as thick as they are during the peak tourist season of summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amherst

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Amherst:

Do:

  • Do Respect the Town: Keep the environment clean and green by disposing of all litter appropriately.
  • Do Take a Tour: A great way to explore the town is to take a guided tour.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Park Anywhere: Because of the town’s dense population, parking spaces are minimal. Pay attention to the parking signs and regulations.

Safety and Health

Travelers should not encounter any health or safety issues while in Amherst. However, here are some tips to ensure a safe and healthy visit:

Safety in Amherst

  • Lock your belongings: Theft can occur, and it is better to be safe than sorry. Lock valuables in your vehicle and hotel room.

  • Carry emergency numbers or any medication: Keep a list of emergency numbers with you or any medication you need to take in the event of an emergency.

Health in Amherst

  • Sunscreen: During the summer months it is recommended to apply a suitable amount of sunscreen to reduce your chances of getting skin damage from the sun.

  • Stay Hydrated: During outdoor activities and touring, it is important to stay hydrated and drink enough water.

Getting to Amherst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Amherst is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options. Here are some suggestions:

Airports Near Amherst

  • Bradley International Airport: Located about an hour and a half away by car, Bradley International Airport in Hartford, Connecticut, is the nearest airport with domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Amherst

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: This bus service provides travel to Amherst with stops in nearby cities, including Boston and New York City.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: If you’re flying into Boston or Hartford, consider renting a car for your trip to Amherst.

How to Get Around in Amherst

While public transportation options are limited in Amherst, visitors can take advantage of taxi services, ride-sharing apps, and bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Amherst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Amherst, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Northampton: A beautiful town with many eclectic shops, dining options, and art galleries.

  • The Berkshires: A beautiful rural region great for a scenic drive with various cultural activities like museums and music festivals.

Conclusion

Amherst is a lively town that offers a rich blend of history, culture, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing vacation, it is an ideal destination. Use our comprehensive guide when planning your trip to Amherst, and be sure to explore all the town has to offer.
