Introduction to Hancock

Hancock is a charming town located in the heart of Berkshires in Western Massachusetts. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures, Hancock offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you are looking to explore the area’s history, indulge in outdoor activities or simply relax and unwind, Hancock promises to leave you spellbound.

List of Places to Visit in Hancock

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hancock Shaker Village: This living history museum showcases the life and culture of the Shaker community, known for their simplicity, craftsmanship, and innovation.
  • Naumkeag: This Gilded Age estate boasts stunning gardens, breathtaking views of the Berkshires, and a meticulously preserved 44-room mansion.
  • Tanglewood: This iconic music venue is renowned for its summer concerts featuring the Boston Symphony Orchestra and world-famous artists.
  • Norman Rockwell Museum: This museum celebrates the life and work of the beloved American illustrator, famous for his realistic and nostalgic depictions of American life.
  • Berkshire Theatre Group: This historic theatre group presents a wide range of performances, from classic plays to contemporary productions.

Natural Attractions of Hancock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jiminy Peak Mountain Resort: This four-season resort offers skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and thrilling outdoor activities for all ages.
  • Bash Bish Falls: This stunning waterfall located in Mount Washington State Forest is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and hikers.
  • Pittsfield State Forest: This 11,000-acre forest features over 30 miles of hiking trails, panoramic views of the Berkshires, and serene lakes for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hancock

For outdoor enthusiasts, Hancock offers miles of hiking and biking trails through its scenic forests, mountains, and valleys. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Berkshire Natural Resources Council Trails: This network of trails encompasses over 50 miles of hiking and biking trails through the natural areas of the Berkshires.
  • Taconic Crest Trail: This challenging 35-mile trail runs along the ridgeline of the Taconic Mountains, offering spectacular views of the Berkshires and Hudson Valley.
  • Ashuwillticook Rail Trail: This flat and easy 11-mile trail follows the former railroad bed from Lanesborough to Adams, offering stunning views of Cheshire Lake.

Best Things to Do in Hancock: Activities and Tours

  • Take a scenic drive along the Mohawk Trail, one of the oldest scenic byways in the country, featuring breathtaking vistas, charming towns, and historic landmarks.
  • Go rafting on the Deerfield River, a favorite destination for whitewater enthusiasts of all levels.
  • Visit the local farms and wineries and taste the fresh produce and award-winning wines of the region.
  • Join a guided tour of the Berkshires, led by knowledgeable local guides, who will take you off the beaten path and show you hidden gems and insider tips.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hancock

Day 1: Explore the cultural attractions of Hancock, including the Hancock Shaker Village, Norman Rockwell Museum, and Berkshire Theatre Group.

Day 2: Hike the scenic trails of Pittsfield State Forest, followed by a visit to Naumkeag to see its stunning gardens.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive along the Mohawk Trail, stopping at historic landmarks, charming towns, and scenic vistas along the way.

Day 4: Experience the outdoor adventures of Jiminy Peak Mountain Resort, including skiing, snowboarding, or thrilling mountain rides.

Day 5: Go rafting on the Deerfield River, followed by a visit to the local farms and wineries for a taste of the region’s fresh produce and award-winning wines.

Day 6: Join a guided tour of the Berkshires, led by knowledgeable local guides, who will take you off the beaten path and show you hidden gems and insider tips.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at your accommodation, enjoying the serene beauty of the Berkshires and reflecting on your memorable journey through Hancock.

Food and Drink Scene of Hancock

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: This classic New England dish is a must-try, featuring fresh lobster meat served on a buttered roll.
  • Clam Chowder: Another New England classic, clam chowder is a creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, and other vegetables, served with crackers or bread.
  • Apple Pie: This iconic dessert is a true taste of the Berkshires, featuring fresh apples baked in a flaky crust and served with vanilla ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Berkshire Brewing Company: This award-winning brewery offers a wide range of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers, made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Furnace Brook Winery: This family-owned winery produces high-quality wines from locally grown grapes, including Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and Cabernet Franc.
  • Barrington Coffee Roasting Company: This specialty coffee roaster sources its beans from small-scale farmers around the world, offering a unique taste of each region.

Where to Stay in Hancock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hancock offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Some of the popular options include:

  • Wyndham Bentley Brook: This ski-in/ski-out resort features spacious suites with full kitchens, fireplaces, and mountain views, as well as on-site amenities such as a fitness center, indoor and outdoor pools, and a spa.
  • Vacation Village in the Berkshires: This family-friendly resort offers cozy villas with fully equipped kitchens, fireplaces, and balconies, as well as on-site amenities such as an indoor pool, hot tub, and game room.
  • Shaker Mill Inn: This romantic inn features elegantly appointed rooms with antique furnishings, fireplaces, and whirlpool tubs, as well as a garden and a gourmet breakfast served each morning.

What to Buy in Hancock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery: The Berkshires are known for their talented artisans, who create beautiful pottery and ceramics using local clay and traditional techniques.
  • Handcrafted Furniture: Another specialty of the region is furniture made by skilled craftsmen using locally sourced wood, featuring unique designs and high-quality craftsmanship.
  • Maple Syrup: The Berkshires are home to many maple farms, producing some of the finest maple syrup in the world, which you can bring home as a sweet reminder of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Hancock

Hancock is a small town with a strong sense of community, where people value simplicity, nature, and tradition. Visitors are welcomed with warmth and hospitality, and are encouraged to respect the natural environment and local customs.

Best Time to Visit Hancock

Hancock is a year-round destination, with each season offering its unique charm and activities. Summer and fall are the peak seasons, with pleasant weather, vibrant foliage, and a wide range of outdoor activities and cultural events. Winter is a popular time for skiing and winter sports, while spring is a time for new beginnings and fresh blooms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hancock

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and follow Leave-No-Trace principles.
  • Explore the local culture and learn about the history and traditions of the region.
  • Support local businesses and artisans by purchasing locally made products and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitat of wildlife.
  • Disrespect local customs or cultural practices.
  • Cause unnecessary noise or disturbance, especially in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hancock

Hancock is a safe and welcoming town, with a low crime rate and a strong sense of community. Visitors are advised to take normal precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables, especially in crowded areas and at night.

Health in Hancock

Hancock has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, that offer high-quality medical services for residents and visitors alike. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications and to follow healthy habits, such as staying hydrated and getting enough rest and exercise.

Getting to Hancock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hancock is located about 130 miles from Boston, and can be reached by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Hancock

  • Albany International Airport (ALB): This is the closest airport to Hancock, located about 45 miles away, offering flights to many destinations in the US and abroad.
  • Bradley International Airport (BDL): This airport is located about 70 miles away, offering flights to many domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Hancock

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: This bus company offers service to nearby towns and cities, including Pittsfield and Albany, as well as connections to major cities such as New York and Boston.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient option for visitors who want to explore the Berkshires at their own pace and access remote areas without relying on public transportation.
  • Private transportation: Some hotels and resorts offer shuttle service or private transportation for their guests, including airport transfers and guided tours.

How to Get Around in Hancock

The best way to get around Hancock is by car, as public transportation is limited and may not reach all areas of interest. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use ride-sharing services to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Hancock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hancock is located in the heart of the Berkshires, a region known for its natural beauty and cultural attractions. Some of the nearby destinations worth visiting include:

  • Great Barrington: This charming town is home to many galleries, shops, and restaurants, as well as the historic Mahaiwe Performing Arts Center.
  • Lenox: This picturesque town is known for its beautiful mansions, including The Mount, the former home of Edith Wharton, as well as Tanglewood and Shakespeare & Company.
  • Williamstown: This vibrant college town is home to the world-renowned Clark Art Institute and the Williams College Museum of Art, as well as the popular hiking spot, Mount Greylock.

Conclusion

Hancock is a hidden gem in the Berkshires, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. Whether you are looking to immerse yourself in the local culture, indulge in outdoor adventures, or simply unwind in a serene and scenic environment, Hancock promises to leave you with unforgettable memories and a desire to return. Plan your visit today and discover all that Hancock has to offer!
