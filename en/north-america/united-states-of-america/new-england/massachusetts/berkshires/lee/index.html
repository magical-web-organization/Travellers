Introduction to Lee

Nestled in the Berkshires, a picturesque region in western Massachusetts, Lee is a charming town where the past meets the present. Founded in the early 18th century, this quaint community boasts a rich history, diverse culture, and scenic beauty that attract visitors from near and far. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural events, or simply some R&R, Lee offers something for every type of traveler. In this tour guide, we will explore some of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs of Lee.

List of Places to Visit in Lee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Lee Historical Society & Museum: This museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It showcases the town’s fascinating past through exhibits and artifacts, including photographs, paintings, and documents.

  2. The Colonel John Ashley House: Built in 1735, this historic mansion is a testament to the town’s colonial heritage. Take a guided tour to learn about the house’s history and inhabitants.

  3. The Berkshire Scenic Railway Museum: Located in the restored Lee station on the Housatonic Railroad, this museum is dedicated to preserving the history of railroading in the Berkshires. Visitors can admire an impressive collection of vintage locomotives and rail cars.

Natural Attractions of Lee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Beartown State Forest: This 10,000-acre forest is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Hike on the park’s trails, admire the breathtaking views, or go camping to enjoy the pristine wilderness.

  2. October Mountain State Forest: With over 16,000 acres of rolling hills and wild forests, this park is a paradise for hikers, mountain bikers, and nature lovers. Don’t miss the opportunity to observe wildlife and explore the park’s picturesque trails.

  3. Laurel Hill Association: This privately-owned park offers scenic views of Laurel Hill and nearby Lake Laurel. Take a leisurely stroll and immerse yourself in the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lee

Lee and the surrounding region offer a multitude of hiking and biking opportunities for visitors of all skill levels. Some of the most popular trails include the Appalachian Trail, the Beartown State Forest Trail, and the Kennedy Park Trail. These trails offer stunning views of the town and its rugged terrain, as well as access to secluded forests and sparkling lakes.

Best Things to Do in Lee: Activities and Tours

  1. Experience the arts: Attend the annual Lee Founders Weekend Festival in August, featuring a variety of artistic and cultural events.

  2. Explore the town: Take a walking tour of Lee’s charming streets and historic sites, such as the Congregational Church and the Lee Library.

  3. Enjoy the outdoors: Rent a bike, kayak, or paddleboard from one of the local outfitters and explore the scenic beauty of the Berkshires.

Food and Drink Scene of Lee

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Rolls: This Maine classic is a must-try at any seafood shack in town.

  2. Clam Chowder: Warm up with a rich and creamy bowl of clam chowder at a cozy restaurant.

  3. Apple Pie: Indulge in a delicious slice of homemade apple pie, made with locally sourced apples.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Sip on a refreshing locally brewed beer at a charming brewery or gastropub.

  2. Wine: Sample award-winning wines at one of the nearby vineyards or wineries.

Where to Stay in Lee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lee offers a range of attractive and affordable lodging options, from charming bed and breakfasts to luxurious resorts. Some of the most popular accommodations include:

  1. The Yankee Inn: This cozy inn offers comfortable rooms and suites, a heated outdoor pool, and a complimentary breakfast buffet.

  2. The Black Swan Inn, an Ascend Collection Hotel: This elegant hotel features spacious suites, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast each morning.

  3. The Chambery Inn: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms, a wine bar, and a garden patio.

What to Buy in Lee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Artwork: Browse the local art galleries and boutiques for unique and eye-catching pieces of art.

  2. Antiques: Explore the charming antique stores and pick up a vintage piece to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lee

Lee has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its diverse history and strong sense of community. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

  1. Respect the local environment and natural resources.

  2. Be mindful of the town’s historic sites and buildings.

  3. Embrace the town’s friendly and inclusive culture.

Best Time to Visit Lee

Lee’s beauty and attractions are on display year-round, with each season offering unique delights. The summer months are the most popular, due to the warm weather and numerous outdoor events. The fall foliage season is also a popular time to visit, as the Berkshires become awash in vibrant colors. Winter brings skiing and other winter sports, while spring is ideal for hiking and enjoying the blooming flora.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lee

Do:

  1. Sample the local cuisine and beverages.

  2. Respect the town’s historic buildings and landmarks.

  3. Embrace the natural beauty of the Berkshires.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or disrespect the environment.

  2. Disrespect the town’s cultural heritage.

  3. Be loud or disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lee

Lee is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise caution when hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities, and be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Lee

Lee has several medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling, and take necessary precautions to avoid illness or injury.

Getting to Lee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lee is easily accessible by car, bus, or train.

Airports Near Lee

The nearest airports to Lee are Albany International Airport (ALB) and Bradley International Airport (BDL), both located within a two-hour drive.

Bus Stations Near Lee

Peter Pan Bus Lines and Greyhound both provide bus service to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available for visitors who prefer not to drive.

How to Get Around in Lee

Lee is a walkable town, and visitors can easily explore on foot. Alternatively, rental cars, bicycles, and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Great Barrington

Located just 10 miles south of Lee, Great Barrington is a vibrant town known for its arts scene, eclectic shops, and delicious restaurants.

Stockbridge

Just a short drive north of Lee lies the charming town of Stockbridge, known for its quaint New England charm and historic attractions.

Conclusion

Lee, Massachusetts, is a charming town steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, Lee offers something for everyone. From its scenic hiking trails and historic sites to its charming boutiques and cozy hotels, this town is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic New England experience. We hope this guide has inspired you to plan your next trip to Lee and explore all this wonderful town has to offer.
