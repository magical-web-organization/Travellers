Introduction to Pittsfield

Pittsfield is a charming town located in the heart of the Berkshire Mountains in Massachusetts. It is a popular destination for those seeking outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and scenic beauty. From hiking and biking to exploring the town’s rich history and vibrant arts scene, there’s something for everyone in Pittsfield.

List of Places to Visit in Pittsfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Berkshire Museum: This museum features a diverse collection of art, history, and natural history exhibits.
  • Herman Melville’s Arrowhead: The home of the famous writer, and the setting for his novel Moby Dick.
  • Oliver Wendell Holmes House: The childhood home of the famous author and physician.
  • Hancock Shaker Village: This 19th-century village provides a glimpse into the daily life of the Shaker community.

Natural Attractions of Pittsfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Greylock: At 3,491 feet, Mount Greylock is the highest peak in Massachusetts, providing stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Onota Lake: This large lake offers opportunities for swimming, fishing, boating, and picnicking.
  • Pittsfield State Forest: This forest offers hiking, biking, camping, and cross-country skiing activities.
  • Canoe Meadows Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary has over 200 acres of fields, wetlands, and forest, with hiking trails and bird-watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pittsfield

  • Ashuwillticook Rail Trail: This 11-mile trail is popular among bikers and runners, with scenic views of the Cheshire Reservoir and White Oak Hill Ridge.
  • Housatonic River Walk: This trail is located along the Housatonic River and provides a scenic way to explore the town.
  • Burbank Park: This park has a 1.5-mile trail around the lake, with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Pittsfield: Activities and Tours

  • Barrington Stage Company: This award-winning theater company showcases Broadway-quality productions throughout the year.
  • Steel Rail Half Marathon: This annual race takes place in May, with scenic views of the Berkshire Mountains.
  • Folk Music Festival: This annual festival takes place in September and is a celebration of folk music and dance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pittsfield

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the Berkshire Museum and downtown area

Food and Drink Scene of Pittsfield

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Cider Donuts: These delicious donuts are a Berkshire County staple and can be found at local bakeries and farm stands.
  • Beef Wellington: This classic dish is served at 19th-century mansion-turned-restaurant, Wheatleigh.
  • Blueberry Pancakes: Fresh blueberries are a popular ingredient in many breakfast dishes throughout the Berkshires.

Local Beverages

  • Berkshire Brewing Company: This brewery produces a variety of craft beers, including the famous Steel Rail Extra Pale Ale.
  • Whiskey Sour: This classic cocktail is made with local whiskey from the Berkshire Mountain Distillers.

Where to Stay in Pittsfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel on North: This boutique hotel is located in the heart of the downtown district, with modern amenities and a rooftop bar.
  • The Porches Inn: This unique hotel features retro-chic rooms and an outdoor pool.
  • Ramblewild: This eco-friendly resort is located near the Pittsfield State Forest and offers a variety of accommodations, including treehouses and cabins.

What to Buy in Pittsfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Berkshire Mountain Bakery Bread: This bakery produces artisanal bread using local ingredients.
  • Berkshire Blue Cheese: This award-winning cheese is made using pasteurized cow’s milk.
  • Hancock Shaker Village Crafts: Handcrafted items made by local Shaker artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Pittsfield

  • Hiking and Outdoor Activities: The residents of Pittsfield take pride in their outdoor lifestyle and are happy to share their love of hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of the area.
  • Arts and Culture: Pittsfield is home to many artists, musicians, and writers, making it a hub for creativity and expression.
  • Agriculture and Farming: The Berkshires are known for their thriving agriculture industry, with many local farmers markets and farm-to-table restaurants throughout the region.

Best Time to Visit Pittsfield

The best time to visit Pittsfield is during the fall months of September-November when the foliage is at its peak and the weather is cool and crisp. Spring and summer are also great times to visit for outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pittsfield

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the many hiking and outdoor activities available in the area.
  • Do explore the town’s rich history and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos without asking permission, especially in private homes and gardens.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the Berkshires.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pittsfield

Pittsfield is a safe town, but as with any place, it is important to take basic safety precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Keep your belongings close and avoid leaving valuables in your car.

Health in Pittsfield

There are several hospitals and medical centers in the area, including Berkshire Medical Center and Fairview Hospital. Travelers should also be aware of ticks and take precautions against Lyme disease.

Getting to Pittsfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pittsfield

  • Albany International Airport: Located about an hour’s drive from Pittsfield.
  • Bradley International Airport: Located about an hour and a half’s drive from Pittsfield.
  • Logan International Airport: Located about two and a half hours’ drive from Pittsfield.

Bus Stations Near Pittsfield

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: Offers bus service to Pittsfield from various locations throughout the Northeast.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: There are several car rental companies located in Pittsfield.
  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station is located in Albany.
  • Uber/Lyft: Ride-sharing services are available in Pittsfield.

How to Get Around in Pittsfield

The best way to get around in Pittsfield is by car. There are several rental car companies located in the area. Biking and walking are also popular options for exploring the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Pittsfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lenox: A charming town located just 7 miles east of Pittsfield, known for its music and arts scene.
  • Stockbridge: A picturesque town known for its Norman Rockwell Museum and beautiful countryside.
  • Great Barrington: A historic town located 20 miles south of Pittsfield, known for its boutique shops and restaurants.

Conclusion

Pittsfield is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Massachusetts. With its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant arts scene, there’s truly something for everyone in this charming town located in the heart of the Berkshires.
