Introduction to Adams

Nestled in the stunning Berkshires region of Massachusetts, Adams is a picturesque New England town known for its fascinating history, beautiful natural surroundings, and charming small-town ambiance. Originally founded as a farming community, Adams developed into a thriving industrial center in the 19th century, powered by the Hoosic River that runs through town. Today, it’s a popular destination for visitors who come to enjoy the diverse range of indoor and outdoor activities in and around the area.

List of Places to Visit in Adams

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Susan B. Anthony Birthplace Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the legacy of women’s suffrage leader Susan B. Anthony, who was born in Adams in 1820. The museum features exhibits on Anthony’s life, as well as on the broader women’s rights movement in America.
  • Hoosic River Revival Heritage Trail: This 6-mile trail connects several historical sites in Adams, including the Susan B. Anthony Birthplace Museum, the Quaker Meeting House, and the Ashuwillticook Rail Trail.
  • St. Stanislaus Kostka Church: This beautiful Roman Catholic church is built in the Polish Cathedral style and features stunning stained glass windows and artwork.
  • Thunderbolt Ski Museum: Located at the base of the Thunderbolt Ski Trail, this museum celebrates the history of skiing in the Berkshires and features exhibits on the Thunderbolt Ski Race, which takes place annually in Adams.

Natural Attractions of Adams: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Greylock State Reservation: This beautiful state park covers over 12,000 acres and includes the highest peak in Massachusetts, Mount Greylock. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, and stunning views of the surrounding Berkshires.
  • Ashuwillticook Rail Trail: This 13-mile paved trail runs along the Hoosic River and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside. Perfect for biking, walking, and jogging.
  • Natural Bridge State Park: This park features a picturesque and unique natural bridge formation, which was created by thousands of years of glacial meltwater.
  • Windsor Lake: This beautiful lake is an excellent spot for swimming, fishing, and picnicking in the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Adams

  • Greylock Glen Trail: This moderate-level trail is a 4-mile loop that passes through lush forests and offers stunning views of Mount Greylock.
  • Cheshire Harbor Trail: This 2-mile trail is a popular destination for hikers and bikers alike, offering stunning views of Greylock and the surrounding countryside.
  • Ashuwillticook Rail Trail: This easy trail is perfect for bikers and walkers looking for a scenic route along the Hoosic River.

Best Things to Do in Adams: Activities and Tours

  • Savoy Mountain State Forest: This state park features over 50 miles of hiking trails, as well as opportunities for camping, fishing, and winter sports like cross-country skiing and snowshoeing.
  • Bas Ridge Golf Course: This 9-hole golf course offers beautiful views of the Berkshire Mountains and is open to the public.
  • Thunderbolt Ski and Race School: This ski school offers lessons and programs for skiers of all levels, as well as the annual Thunderbolt Ski Race in February.

Week-Long Itinerary for Adams

Day Activity
1 Visit the Susan B. Anthony Birthplace Museum and explore the Hoosic River Revival Heritage Trail
2 Take a scenic drive or bike ride along the Ashuwillticook Rail Trail and enjoy a picnic at Windsor Lake
3 Hike to the top of Mount Greylock and enjoy the stunning views from the summit
4 Visit the Thunderbolt Ski Museum and take a lesson at the Thunderbolt Ski and Race School
5 Enjoy a round of golf at Bas Ridge Golf Course and explore the shops and restaurants in downtown Adams
6 Take a day trip to nearby North Adams and visit the Massachusetts Museum of Contemporary Art (MASS MoCA)
7 Relax and unwind at Savoy Mountain State Forest, with opportunities for hiking, fishing, and camping.

Food and Drink Scene of Adams

Must-Try Dishes

  • Classic New England Clam Chowder: This hearty soup is a staple of New England cuisine, and is especially delicious at local restaurants like Desperado’s and Bounti-fare.
  • Burgers at Freight Yard Pub: This local watering hole serves up delicious burgers and other pub fare, with live music on weekends.
  • Famous Sundaes at Scooter’s: This ice cream parlor is known for its massive sundaes, made with homemade ice cream and a wide range of toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Berkshire Mountain Distillers: This local distillery produces award-winning craft spirits, including gin, whiskey, and vodka.
  • Bright Ideas Brewing: This craft brewery is located in nearby North Adams and offers a wide range of beers in a cozy taproom.

Where to Stay in Adams: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tennessee Mountain Cabin: This charming guesthouse offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains, as well as outdoor amenities like hiking trails and fire pits.
  • Topia Inn: This eco-friendly inn features luxurious rooms with down pillows and robes, as well as yoga classes and spa treatments.
  • Holiday Inn Berkshires: This comfortable hotel offers a central location and amenities like an indoor pool and fitness center.

What to Buy in Adams: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery at the Eclipse Mill Gallery: This local gallery features unique pottery and other crafts made by local artists.
  • Maple Syrup from Ioka Valley Farm: This family-owned farm produces delicious maple syrup and other maple products.
  • Vintage Finds at Eleven Vintage: This local store offers a wide range of vintage and retro items, including clothing, furniture, and décor.

Local Culture and Customs of Adams

Adams is a small, friendly town with a rich cultural history and a strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s natural surroundings and to engage with the locals to learn more about the area’s unique history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Adams

The best time to visit Adams is in the fall, when the leaves change colors and the temperatures are mild. However, visitors can enjoy the town’s charms year-round, with plenty of outdoor activities in the summer and winter sports in the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Adams

Do:

  • Do explore the beautiful natural surroundings of Adams, including Mount Greylock and the Hoosic River.
  • Do learn about the town’s history and culture at local museums and historical sites, like the Susan B. Anthony Birthplace Museum.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, including classic New England dishes and craft spirits.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural surroundings of Adams.
  • Don’t underestimate the importance of the town’s cultural history, especially when it comes to women’s rights and suffrage.
  • Don’t forget to respect the locals and engage with the community to learn more about the rich traditions of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Adams

Adams is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. Visitors should take standard safety precautions when exploring the natural surroundings of the area, including hiking and biking trails.

Health in Adams

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in nearby Pittsfield, offering a wide range of services for visitors to the area. Visitors should also take care to stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun and other environmental hazards during outdoor activities.

Getting to Adams: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Adams

The nearest airport to Adams is Albany International Airport, located 45 miles away in Albany, New York. Visitors can also fly into Bradley International Airport, located 75 miles away in Hartford, Connecticut.

Bus Stations Near Adams

Peter Pan Bus Lines operates bus services to nearby Pittsfield and North Adams from several major cities in the northeast, including Boston and New York City.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Adams, with easy access to major highways like I-90 and Route 7.

How to Get Around in Adams

The best way to get around Adams is by car, with several rental car companies operating in the area. Visitors can also take advantage of local bike rental options, or explore the town on foot.

Where to Go Next from Adams: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • North Adams: This nearby town is home to MASS MoCA, as well as a charming downtown area with shops and restaurants.
  • Williamstown: This historic college town is home to Williams College and the Sterling and Francine Clark Art Institute.
  • Pittsfield: This city is the largest in the Berkshires region and offers a wide range of cultural and recreational activities.

Conclusion

Adams is a charming New England town with a rich cultural history and stunning natural surroundings. Whether you’re interested in exploring the area’s museums and historical sites, discovering the best hiking and biking trails, or simply relaxing and experiencing the local cuisine and culture, Adams has something to offer every visitor. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your visit, and we wish you a wonderful stay in this beautiful part of Massachusetts!
