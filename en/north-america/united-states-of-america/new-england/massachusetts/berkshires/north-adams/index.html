Introduction to North Adams

Nestled in the picturesque Berkshires, North Adams is a charming town in western Massachusetts that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures. Once known for its industrial mills and factories, this former mill town has transformed into a vibrant hub for art, culture, and outdoor recreation.

Its location near the Appalachian Trail and Mount Greylock, the highest peak in Massachusetts, provides ample opportunities for hiking, biking, skiing, and other outdoor activities. The town is also home to several museums, galleries, and music festivals that showcase the region’s rich cultural heritage.

Whether you’re a history buff, an art enthusiast, or an outdoors lover, North Adams has something in store for everyone. This comprehensive tour guide covers all the must-see attractions, hidden gems, and insider tips to help you plan your trip to North Adams, Massachusetts.

List of Places to Visit in North Adams

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North Adams is steeped in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that you should not miss during your visit:

1. MASS MoCA

MASS MoCA (Massachusetts Museum of Contemporary Art) is one of the largest centers for contemporary visual and performing arts in the country. It features more than 250,000 square feet of gallery space and hosts a rotating roster of exhibitions, performances, and events throughout the year.

2. The Clark Art Institute

The Clark Art Institute is a world-renowned museum and research center that houses a stunning collection of European and American art from the Renaissance to the early twentieth century. The museum also features beautiful gardens, a library, and a research center.

3. The Susan B. Anthony Birthplace Museum

The Susan B. Anthony Birthplace Museum is the childhood home of the suffragist and women’s rights activist Susan B. Anthony. It is a National Historic Landmark that offers a glimpse into her life and legacy.

Natural Attractions of North Adams: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Adams is surrounded by picturesque landscapes, hiking trails, and spectacular views. Here are some of the natural attractions you should explore:

1. Mount Greylock State Reservation

Mount Greylock State Reservation is a natural paradise that offers scenic views, hiking trails, and outdoor recreation opportunities for all skill levels. It features the highest peak in Massachusetts, numerous waterfalls, and panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

2. Natural Bridge State Park

Natural Bridge State Park is a unique geological formation that features a natural marble arch, a historic marble quarry, and a scenic gorge. It is a perfect destination for hiking, picnicking, and nature photography.

3. Western Gateway Heritage State Park

Western Gateway Heritage State Park is a historic site that showcases the industrial and cultural heritage of North Adams. It features an exhibit hall, a visitor center, and beautiful gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Adams

North Adams is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes:

  1. The Appalachian Trail

The Appalachian Trail runs through North Adams, offering hikers a chance to explore the scenic beauty of the Berkshires. The section of the trail that runs through North Adams features several stunning viewpoints and mountain vistas.

  1. The Cascade Trail

The Cascade Trail is a popular hiking route that offers stunning views of natural waterfalls and scenic vistas. The trail is easy to moderate, making it accessible for hikers of all skill levels.

  1. The Ashuwillticook Rail Trail

The Ashuwillticook Rail Trail is a scenic bike trail that runs for 11 miles through beautiful forests, fields, and wetlands. It features rest areas, picnic areas, and scenic overlooks along the way.

Best Things to Do in North Adams: Activities and Tours

North Adams offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

  1. Take a Tour of MASS MoCA

MASS MoCA offers several tours of its galleries, exhibitions, and performing arts venues. These tours provide insights into contemporary art, architecture, and culture.

  1. Visit the North Adams Farmers Market

The North Adams Farmers Market is held every Saturday during the summer and fall months. It offers fresh produce, artisanal food, live music, and a lively atmosphere.

  1. Take a Scenic Drive on the Mohawk Trail

The Mohawk Trail is a scenic drive that offers stunning mountain views, historic landmarks, and charming small towns. It is a perfect way to explore the natural beauty of Western Massachusetts.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Adams

Day 1: Visit MASS MoCA and explore its galleries and exhibitions. Have lunch at Lickety Split Cafe, an iconic local eatery.

Day 2: Hike the Appalachian Trail and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visit the North Adams Farmers Market in the afternoon.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Clark Art Institute and enjoy its majestic gardens. Have dinner at Gramercy Bistro, a farm-to-table restaurant.

Day 4: Visit Natural Bridge State Park and explore its unique geological formations and scenic gorge. Have lunch at PUBLIC eat+drink, a trendy gastropub.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive on the Mohawk Trail and explore the charming towns and natural beauty of the Berkshires. Stop by at the historic hairpin turn, known as the “Hail to the Sunrise Statue”

Day 6: Visit the Susan B. Anthony Birthplace Museum and learn about the life and legacy of this iconic women’s rights activist. Have dinner at the Freight Yard Pub, a local favorite.

Day 7: Hike the Cascade Trail and enjoy its breathtaking views of the natural waterfalls and mountain vistas. Have lunch at Trail House Kitchen & Bar, a cozy restaurant with a charming outdoor patio.

Food and Drink Scene of North Adams

North Adams boasts a vibrant food and drink scene that features local produce, craft beer, and artisanal cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. The BLT at Lickety Split Cafe: A classic sandwich made with crispy bacon, lettuce, and tomato, served on toasted bread and paired with homemade chips and pickles.

  2. The Lobster Roll at Jack’s Hot Dog Stand: A mouth-watering sandwich made with fresh lobster meat, celery, and mayo, served on a toasted bun.

  3. The Chicken Pot Pie at Gramercy Bistro: A savory pie made with chicken, vegetables, and a flaky crust, served with roasted potatoes and gravy.

Local Beverages

  1. The Steel Rail Extra Pale Ale at Berkshire Brewing Company: A crisp and refreshing beer with hints of citrus, hoppy bitterness and a smooth finish.

  2. The Widow’s Walk Porter at Bright Ideas Brewing: A dark and rich beer with notes of coffee, chocolate, and caramel.

  3. The Hard Cider at Headwater Cider: A crisp and refreshing cider made with apples grown in the Berkshires.

Where to Stay in North Adams: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Adams offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  1. The Porches Inn at MASS MoCA: A boutique hotel that offers comfortable rooms, beautiful gardens, and easy access to MASS MoCA and downtown North Adams.

  2. Tourists: A quirky hotel that features vintage Airstream trailers, cozy cabins, and communal spaces for socializing and relaxing.

  3. The Holiday Inn Berkshires: A family-friendly hotel that offers spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and easy access to major attractions and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in North Adams: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Adams is a great place to find unique souvenirs, artisanal products, and local crafts. Here are some of the best shops and markets to check out:

  1. MASS MoCA Store: A gift shop that offers a wide range of art-inspired gifts, books, and accessories.

  2. Tunnel City Coffee: A local coffee roaster that offers specialty blends, teas, and coffee-related gifts.

  3. Berkshire Emporium & Antiques: A vintage shop that offers a selection of unique furniture pieces, antiques, and collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of North Adams

North Adams is a friendly and welcoming town that values community, art, and culture. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of local customs and traditions, and to participate in community events and activities.

Best Time to Visit North Adams

North Adams is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your personal preferences and interests. Here is a breakdown of the seasons in North Adams:

Spring (March to May):

Spring is a beautiful season in North Adams, with mild temperatures, blooming flowers, and outdoor festivals. It is a great time to visit if you enjoy hiking, biking, and exploring nature.

Summer (June to August):

Summer is the busiest and most popular season in North Adams, with warm temperatures, lively festivals, and outdoor activities. It is a great time to visit if you enjoy live music, art shows, and outdoor recreation.

Fall (September to November):

Fall is the most picturesque season in North Adams, with colorful foliage, harvest festivals, and crisp temperatures. It is a great time to visit if you enjoy scenic drives, hiking, and cozy fall activities.

Winter (December to February):

Winter is a quiet season in North Adams, with cold temperatures, snow, and winter sports. It is a great time to visit if you enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Adams

North Adams is generally a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should exercise caution and follow basic safety guidelines, especially when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in North Adams

North Adams has several hospitals, clinics, and health care providers that offer a range of medical services to residents and visitors.

Getting to North Adams: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Adams is easily accessible by car, bus, or train.

Airports Near North Adams

The nearest airports to North Adams are Albany International Airport (ALB) and Bradley International Airport (BDL), both around 45-60 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near North Adams

The nearest bus stations to North Adams are located in Williamstown and Pittsfield, both of which are served by the Berkshire Regional Transit Authority (BRTA).

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to North Adams by train, with the Amtrak station located in Pittsfield, or by car, with several major highways and scenic routes leading into the town.

How to Get Around in North Adams

North Adams is a walkable town, but visitors can also use the BRTA bus system or taxis to get around. Car rentals are also available for those who prefer to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from North Adams: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Adams is surrounded by several charming towns, scenic routes, and outdoor destinations that are perfect for day trips or weekend getaways. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Williamstown: A charming college town that features the famous Williams College, a historic theater, and several museums.

  • Lenox: A picturesque town that is home to Tanglewood, the summer home of the Boston Symphony Orchestra.

  • Bennington: A small town in Vermont that features historic sites, art galleries, and popular outdoor activities.
