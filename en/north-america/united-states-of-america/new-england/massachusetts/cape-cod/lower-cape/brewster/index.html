Introduction to Brewster

Brewster is a picturesque town located in the Lower Cape of Cape Cod, Massachusetts, in New England, United States of America. It was founded in 1803 and named after Elder William Brewster, who was a Pilgrim leader and one of the passengers on the Mayflower. Brewster is known for its historical significance, beautiful beaches, and natural scenery, which attracts countless visitors every year. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best attractions, outdoor adventures, food scene, accommodations, and nearby destinations of Brewster.

List of Places to Visit in Brewster

Brewster has a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking nature, and plenty of outdoor activities that appeal to tourists of all ages. Whether you’re a history enthusiast, beach lover, or an adventurer, there is something for everyone in this charming town. In this section, we will highlight the top places to visit in Brewster.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brewster has a deep-rooted history that is preserved in its museums and historical landmarks. These attractions offer visitors a glimpse of the town’s fascinating past and the people who lived here.

  • The Brewster Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the town’s history through exhibits, artifacts, and documents. Visitors can learn about the town’s founding, the local fishing industry, and iconic landmarks like the windmill.
  • Crosby Mansion: This historic mansion is situated on a hill overlooking Cape Cod Bay and was built in 1888. Visitors can tour the mansion, which is filled with original furnishings and artworks, and take in the stunning views of the bay.
  • Nickerson State Park: This park offers visitors a glimpse of the area’s natural beauty and has historical significance too. Visitors can explore the Civilian Conservation Corps Museum, which tells the story of the job-creating program during the Great Depression.

Natural Attractions of Brewster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brewster has some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in the region, making it a prime spot for outdoor adventures and scenic views. From hiking and biking to kayaking and fishing, there is no shortage of outdoor activities to enjoy in Brewster.

  • Cape Cod Rail Trail: This 22-mile paved trail runs through Brewster and offers visitors a scenic route for cycling, running, or walking. The trail passes through charming towns, beautiful beaches, and picturesque landscapes.
  • Nickerson State Park: This park covers 1,900 acres of forests, freshwater ponds, and trails. Visitors can hike, bike, camp, boat, or swim in one of the eight ponds in the park.
  • Cape Cod Museum of Natural History: This museum is dedicated to educating visitors about the natural beauty of Cape Cod. Visitors can explore the indoor exhibits and outdoor trails, which showcase the area’s stunning wildlife, marine life, and flora.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brewster

As mentioned earlier, the Cape Cod Rail Trail is a popular route for cycling and hiking through Brewster. However, there are many other scenic routes that visitors can explore, depending on their fitness level and preferences.

  • Cape Cod National Seashore: This beautiful stretch of preserved coastline offers visitors several trails for hiking and biking. Visitors can explore the dunes, woodlands, and tidal pools while taking in the ocean views.
  • Punkhorn Parklands: This scenic park covers 876 acres and offers visitors a variety of hiking trails and fishing opportunities. The park’s many lakeside trails provide visitors with stunning views of the area’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Brewster: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums, historical landmarks, and natural attractions, Brewster offers visitors plenty of activities to keep them entertained throughout their stay. From guided tours to hands-on workshops, there is always something to do in this vibrant town.

  • Fishing Charters: Brewster is known for its excellent fishing, and visitors can take part in guided fishing charters or rent equipment to try their luck on their own.
  • Art Workshops: Visitors can try their hand at painting, pottery, or glassblowing at one of the many art workshops held in Brewster.
  • Whale Watching Tours: Brewster is close to several whale migration routes, and visitors can take a guided tour to see these magnificent creatures up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brewster

To make the most of your visit to Brewster, we recommend spending at least a week exploring the town and its surrounding areas. Here is a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Brewster Historical Society Museum and Crosby Mansion
Day 2 Hike or bike the Cape Cod Rail Trail
Day 3 Explore the Cape Cod Museum of Natural History and Nickerson State Park
Day 4 Take a fishing charter or rent equipment on one of Brewster’s beautiful beaches
Day 5 Attend an art workshop or try out a new restaurant

Food and Drink Scene of Brewster

Brewster has a vibrant food and drink scene that emphasizes fresh seafood and local produce. Visitors can sample traditional New England dishes or try something new at one of the many restaurants, cafes, and breweries in town.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: No trip to Cape Cod is complete without trying a cup or bowl of creamy clam chowder, made with fresh clams and potatoes.
  • Lobster Roll: Brewster is known for its delicious lobster rolls, which are typically served hot or cold with a side of coleslaw and fries.
  • Cape Cod Bay Scallop: These succulent scallops are harvested from the waters surrounding Cape Cod and are a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

Brewster has several breweries, wineries, and coffee shops that offer visitors a taste of the local flavors.

  • Cape Cod Beer: This local brewery produces a range of craft beers, including IPA, lagers, and stouts. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery, sample the products, and purchase souvenirs.
  • Truro Vineyards: Located nearby in Truro, this family-owned winery produces award-winning wines from local grapes. Visitors can enjoy a wine tasting, tour the vineyard, or attend one of the many events held on the property.
  • The Brewster Coffee House: This cozy coffee shop serves delicious coffee made from locally roasted beans, as well as pastries and sandwiches.

Where to Stay in Brewster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brewster has several options for accommodation, ranging from comfortable hotels to cozy guesthouses. Whether you’re traveling on a budget or looking for a luxurious getaway, there is something for everyone in Brewster.

  • Brewster by the Sea: This charming bed and breakfast features 9 guest rooms and suites and is located just a short walk from the beach. It offers guests a complimentary breakfast, afternoon tea, and wine and cheese in the evenings.
  • The Mansion at Ocean Edge Resort & Golf Club: This luxurious resort offers guests a variety of accommodation options, including hotel rooms, suites, and villas. It features a private beach, 18-hole golf course, pool, and spa.
  • Brewster Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel features 6 private rooms and is located in a quiet residential area. It offers guests a shared kitchen, common room, and unlimited coffee and tea.

What to Buy in Brewster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home a piece of Brewster’s unique charm by purchasing souvenirs and locally-made products. From handmade jewelry to fresh seafood, there is something for everyone.

  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Many local artists create jewelry from the beautiful sea glass that washes up on Brewster’s shores.
  • Cape Cod Chips: This potato chip company was founded in Hyannis, Massachusetts, and is now a popular snack around the country. Visitors can purchase a bag of chips at many local stores.
  • Fresh Seafood: Visitors can take home some fresh seafood, such as Cape Cod Bay scallops, lobsters, or oysters, to prepare at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Brewster

Brewster has a rich culture and heritage that is embodied in its architecture, traditions, and social customs. Visitors can gain a better understanding of the local culture by attending one of the many festivals or events held throughout the year.

  • Brewster in Bloom: This annual festival celebrates the arrival of spring in Brewster and features a parade, arts and crafts show, and live music.
  • Brewster Historical Society Heritage Days: This festival celebrates Brewster’s history and features a parade, antique car show, and historic tours.
  • Brewster Conservation Day: This event focuses on the area’s natural beauty and conservation efforts and features educational exhibits, guided walks, and outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Brewster

Brewster is a popular destination year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. The summer months of June to August are the busiest and offer visitors warm weather, plenty of outdoor activities, and events. Spring and fall are less crowded and offer cooler weather, beautiful foliage, and quieter beaches. Winter is also a good time to visit for those who enjoy winter sports or want to attend holiday events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brewster

Do:

  • Do explore the area’s natural beauty through hiking, biking, or kayaking.
  • Do take a guided tour of a museum or historical landmark to learn about Brewster’s rich history.
  • Do try the local seafood, especially the clam chowder and Cape Cod Bay scallops.
  • Do attend one of the many festivals or events held throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute the area’s natural resources.
  • Don’t disturb wildlife or damage the environment.
  • Don’t smoke on the beach or in public areas.
  • Don’t drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brewster

Brewster is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety and security. It is advisable to lock car doors and not to leave valuables unattended. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and follow the usual safety precautions, especially at night.

Health in Brewster

Brewster has several medical facilities that can provide visitors with medical assistance if needed. Visitors should have travel insurance and bring a basic first-aid kit on their trip. It is also essential to protect against tick-borne diseases, such as Lyme disease, by wearing insect repellent and checking for ticks after spending time outdoors.

Getting to Brewster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brewster is accessible by several means of transportation, including plane, bus, and car.

Airports Near Brewster

  • Boston Logan International Airport: This airport is located 90 miles northwest of Brewster and is the closest international airport.
  • Barnstable Municipal Airport: This airport is located 15 miles south of Brewster and offers regional flights to nearby cities.

Bus Stations Near Brewster

  • Plymouth & Brockton Street Railway Co.: This bus company provides daily service to Brewster from several nearby cities, including Boston and Hyannis.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby cities or airports.

How to Get Around in Brewster

Brewster is a relatively small town and is easy to navigate on foot, bike, or car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Brewster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brewster is located within driving distance of several nearby destinations that are worth exploring.

  • Provincetown: This charming seaside town is located 30 miles north of Brewster and is known for its art galleries, shops, and restaurants. Visitors can take a whale watching tour or explore the dunes.
  • Nantucket Island: This beautiful island is located 30 miles south of Brewster and is accessible by ferry. Visitors can explore the beaches, lighthouses, and museums.
  • Hyannis: This bustling harbor town is located 20 miles south of Brewster and is the main transportation hub for the Cape Cod region. Visitors can explore the John F. Kennedy Hyannis Museum or take a ferry to Martha’s Vineyard.

Conclusion

Brewster is a hidden gem in the Cape Cod region, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its pristine beaches to its charming museums and historical landmarks, Brewster has something for everyone. We hope that this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to this beautiful town and make the most of your stay.
