Introduction to Osterville

Located in Mid Cape, Cape Cod, Massachusetts, Osterville is a charming village that offers visitors a taste of quintessential New England. Known for its historical landmarks, natural beauty, and outdoor activities, Osterville is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the region.

List of Places to Visit in Osterville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Osterville Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and arts of Osterville and its surrounding areas. Visitors can learn about the village’s colonial past, its role in the maritime industry, and its famous residents, such as John F. Kennedy.

  2. Armstrong-Kelley Park: This park houses the oldest saltworks in America. It also features a nature trail and a museum that displays the history of salt making in the area.

  3. Wianno Club: This exclusive private club was established in 1916 and has hosted many famous guests, including presidents and celebrities. Visitors can admire the club’s picturesque architecture and enjoy its private beach.

Natural Attractions of Osterville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dowses Beach: This beautiful beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing. It also offers stunning views of the Nantucket Sound.

  2. Joshua’s Pond Conservation Area: This peaceful nature reserve has hiking trails, wildlife viewing opportunities, and a serene pond for swimming and fishing.

  3. Cape Cod Lavender Farm: This serene lavender farm features picturesque gardens and a charming gift shop where visitors can purchase lavender products.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Osterville

  1. Osterville Village Library Trail: This short and easy trail starts from the Osterville Village Library and offers beautiful views of the natural surroundings.

  2. Sandy Neck Trail: This moderate trail starts at Sandy Neck Beach and leads to a historic lighthouse, offering stunning views of the Nantucket Sound.

  3. Cape Cod Rail Trail: This 22-mile trail starts in Dennis and passes through Osterville on its way to Wellfleet. It is a popular route for biking, hiking, and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Osterville: Activities and Tours

  1. Osterville Farmers Market: This weekly market is held on Fridays from June to September and features local produce, crafts, and live music.

  2. Cape Cod Kayak: This outdoor adventure company offers guided kayaking tours of the surrounding waterways, including the Cotuit Bay and the Mashpee River.

  3. Cape Cod Winery: This local winery offers tours and tastings of their award-winning wines, as well as a picturesque picnic area and gift shop.

Food and Drink Scene of Osterville

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Roll: This classic New England dish is a must-try when visiting Osterville. Enjoy it at any of the local seafood restaurants or food trucks.

  2. Cape Cod Clam Chowder: Another classic dish that showcases the region’s fresh seafood. Make sure to try it at any of the local diners or seafood shacks.

  3. Cranberry Pie: This delicious dessert is made with locally grown cranberries and is a sweet and tart treat that visitors shouldn’t miss.

Local Beverages

  1. Cape Cod Beer: This local brewery specializes in crafting small-batch beers with local ingredients, such as cranberries and oysters.

  2. Naukabout Beer Company: This brewery offers a wide selection of craft beers with unique flavors, such as their Blueberry Blonde and Oyster Stout.

  3. Dirty Water Distillery: This distillery creates high-quality spirits using Cape Cod ingredients, such as their award-winning vodka made with locally grown potatoes.

Where to Stay in Osterville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sea Mist Resort: This family-friendly resort offers a variety of accommodations, such as hotel rooms, suites, and cottages, as well as amenities, such as indoor and outdoor pools, tennis courts, and a restaurant.

  2. The Red Horse Inn: This historic bed and breakfast offers charming rooms with antique furnishings, a daily breakfast, and a serene garden for relaxation.

  3. Captain David Kelley House: This beautiful inn features luxurious rooms with modern amenities, as well as a daily breakfast, afternoon tea, and outdoor hot tub for relaxation.

What to Buy in Osterville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Cape Cod Cranberries: Bring home a bag of locally grown cranberries or a jar of cranberry jam or sauce.

  2. Wooden Lobster Traps: These decorative items are a unique souvenir that showcases the region’s maritime history.

  3. Osterville T-Shirts: Purchase a stylish t-shirt that highlights your visit to Osterville and its local attractions.

Local Culture and Customs of Osterville

Osterville is proud of its New England heritage and values its community spirit and charm. Visitors should respect the local culture by learning about its history and traditions and by supporting the local businesses and events.

Best Time to Visit Osterville

The best time to visit Osterville is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and outdoor attractions are open. However, the spring and fall seasons offer cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, making it an ideal time for nature lovers and hikers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Osterville

Do:

  1. Take a guided tour of Osterville and its surroundings to learn about its history and culture.

  2. Purchase local products and support the community’s businesses.

  3. Respect the natural surroundings and wildlife and follow the rules and regulations of the parks and beaches.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural surroundings or historical landmarks.

  2. Ignore the safety rules and guidelines of the outdoor activities and adventures.

  3. Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Osterville

Osterville is generally a safe and welcoming community. However, visitors should be aware of the potential risks and follow the safety rules and guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities, such as swimming, hiking, and kayaking. They should also take precautions against ticks and other insects and avoid leaving valuables in their cars or unattended on the beaches and parks.

Health in Osterville

Osterville has several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should also take precautions against the sun and stay hydrated during the summer months.

Getting to Osterville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Osterville

  1. Barnstable Municipal Airport (HYA): This is the closest airport to Osterville, located about 10 miles away.

  2. Boston Logan International Airport (BOS): This major airport is located about 80 miles away from Osterville.

Bus Stations Near Osterville

  1. Cape Cod Regional Transit Authority: This bus company operates service to Osterville and the surrounding areas.

  2. Plymouth & Brockton Street Railway Co.: This bus company offers service from Boston to Osterville and the Cape Cod region.

Other Options

  1. Rental Cars: Visitors can rent a car at any of the nearby airports or in Osterville to explore the region at their own pace.

  2. Taxis and Ride-Sharing: Visitors can use local taxi services or popular ride-sharing apps, such as Uber and Lyft, to get around Osterville and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Osterville

Visitors can easily explore Osterville and its sights by foot or bike. Rental cars and taxis are also available for those who prefer to travel farther.

Where to Go Next from Osterville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Hyannis: This nearby town is home to the John F. Kennedy Museum, the Cape Cod Potato Chip Factory, and a bustling main street with shops and restaurants.

  2. Cape Cod National Seashore: This scenic area offers pristine beaches, hiking trails, and stunning views of the ocean.

  3. Martha’s Vineyard: This charming island is only a short ferry ride away and offers picturesque towns, beautiful beaches, and unique shops and restaurants.

Conclusion

Osterville is a beautiful village in Cape Cod that offers visitors a taste of local New England charm, history, and natural beauty. From its historic landmarks and museums to its picturesque beaches and hiking trails, Osterville has something to offer for every kind of traveler. Plan your trip to Osterville today and experience the best of New England!
