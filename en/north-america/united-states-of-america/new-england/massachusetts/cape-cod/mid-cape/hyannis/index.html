Hyannis, located in the Mid Cape area of Cape Cod in Massachusetts, is known for its rich history and stunning natural beauty. Once a sleepy little town, Hyannis has evolved into one of the most visited places on Cape Cod, thanks to its fabulous beaches, great restaurants, and exciting attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide to Hyannis, we’ll cover all of the must-see attractions, best hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations. So, let’s dive in!

Introduction to Hyannis

Hyannis is a vibrant coastal town that has several attractions, from its stunning beaches to its unique museums, historic sites, and many outdoor activities. The town also has a rich history, with a strong connection to President John F. Kennedy and his family. Whether you’re a solo traveler or a family looking for a fun-filled vacation, Hyannis has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hyannis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. John F. Kennedy Hyannis Museum - dedicated to preserving and promoting the legacy of the Kennedy family, offering engaging exhibits and interactive displays.
  2. Cape Cod Maritime Museum - located on the Hyannis Harbor, highlighting the Cape’s nautical history and marine technology.
  3. Toad Hall Classic Sports Car Museum - featuring cars from the 50s, 60s, and 70s, showcasing an era significant to automotive history.
  4. Cape Cod Baseball League Hall of Fame - celebrating the history of Cape Cod baseball and its players that have moved on to professional baseball careers.
  5. Sturgis Library - the oldest library building in the United States that has been in continuous use.

Natural Attractions of Hyannis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kalmus Beach - well-known for windsurfing and great for sunbathing.
  2. Craigville Beach - one of the finest beaches in New England, stretching over 4 miles with soft white sand.
  3. Hyannis Harbor - the focal point of the town with great views of boats and ferries departing and arriving.
  4. Kennedy Memorial Park - located on Lewis Bay, providing stunning views of Nantucket Sound.
  5. Hyannis Port Beach - a lovely small beach, surrounded by beautiful beach homes with stunning ocean views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hyannis

  1. The Cape Cod Rail Trail - a paved trail that covers 22 incredible miles, running from Dennis to Wellfleet.
  2. The Shining Sea Bikeway - an 11-mile paved trail that runs along the coast from North Falmouth to Woods Hole.
  3. Cape Cod Canal Bikeway - an 8.5-mile trail that is mainly flat and perfect for cycling, jogging, and walking.

Best Things to Do in Hyannis: Activities and Tours

  1. Cape Cod Scenic Tours - offering customized tours of Cape Cod with comfortable vans and informative guides.
  2. Hyannis Whale Watcher Cruises - provide a chance to get up close and personal with whales, dolphins, and other marine life.
  3. Hyannis Main Street Shops - the charming main street of the town filled with local shops and boutiques, selling souvenirs, art pieces, and handcrafted items.
  4. Cape Cod Duckmobiles - the perfect activity to entertain children, driving visitors on an exciting land and water tour.
  5. Cape Cod Central Railroad - a truly unique and relaxing way to enjoy Cape Cod with the restored railway carriages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hyannis

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the JFK Hyannis Museum and get a taste of the town’s rich history.
Day 2 Spend the day on Kalmus Beach, windsurfing, or just soaking up the sun.
Day 3 Take a scenic tour of Cape Cod with Cape Cod Scenic Tours.
Day 4 Visit the Cape Cod Maritime Museum.
Day 5 Cruise the waters around Hyannis with Hyannis Whale Watcher Cruises.
Day 6 Take a bike ride on the picturesque Cape Cod Rail Trail.
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs, arts, and craft on the charming Hyannis Main Street Shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Hyannis

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Clam Chowder - a classic creamy soup made from Atlantic Ocean Clams, potatoes, onions, and heavy cream.
  2. Lobster Roll - featuring chunks of sweet, succulent lobster meat tossed in mayo served on a buttery, toasted roll.
  3. Fried Clams - fresh and crispy lightly battered clams fried to perfection.
  4. Seafood Stew - filled with fresh clams, mussels, scallops, and shrimp, simmering in an aromatic tomato-based broth.
  5. Oysters - the town’s seafood gem, served fresh, and chilled.

Local Beverages

  1. Cape Cod Beer - an independent brewery located in Hyannis, offering a range of locally crafted beers.
  2. Truro Vineyards + South Hollow Spirits - home to a range of wines and spirits, located on Cape Cod’s Lower Cape.

Where to Stay in Hyannis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. SeaCoast Inn - located in the center of Hyannis, offering comfortable and affordable accommodation.
  2. The Green Harbor Resort - beautiful beachfront property, featuring an outdoor pool, children’s playground, and multiple dining options.
  3. The Pelham House Resort - a landmark property located in the heart of the village with commanding water views.
  4. The Hyannis Inn - budget-friendly accommodation, perfect for budget-conscious travelers.
  5. Cape Cod Ocean View - comfortable and stylish waterfront accommodation, featuring private balconies, fire pits, and a beach area.

What to Buy in Hyannis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Cape Cod Potato Chips - we must admit, you haven’t tasted potato chips until you’ve had Cape Cod Potato Chips, the company’s factory sits in nearby Sandwich.
  2. Salt Water Taffy - delicious and fun, this traditional Cape Cod sweet can be found at multiple shops or farmer’s markets.
  3. Handcrafted Jewelry - made by local artisans showcasing their skills and artistic side.
  4. Nautical Decoratives - these items have been a beach home staple for years, so why not bring a little bit of the seaside feeling back home with you?

Local Culture and Customs of Hyannis

The people of Hyannis are friendly and welcoming to all visitors. The town is known for its strong connection to the Kennedy family and history, and their legacy is felt throughout the area. The town is deeply rooted in fishing and whaling, and visitors will appreciate the availability of fresh seafood in restaurants and local markets.

Best Time to Visit Hyannis

The summer months of June to August are the peak tourist season in Hyannis, and the weather is mild and sunny. However, the shoulder season of September to November offers pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and discounted prices, making it an excellent time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hyannis

Do:

  1. Visit the JFK Hyannis Museum and learn about the Kennedy family history.
  2. Try the seafood and chowder.
  3. Walk the streets of Hyannis and explore the local shops and boutiques.
  4. Experience the stunning natural beauty of the coastline.
  5. Be respectful of locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the delicate marine ecosystem.
  2. Ignore safety guidelines around seals and other marine life.
  3. Miss out on outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hyannis

Hyannis is generally safe, but it’s always crucial to take precautions when visiting a new place. Keep an eye on belongings, especially in crowded areas, and avoid isolated areas at night.

Health in Hyannis

Hyannis has several medical facilities, including the Cape Cod Hospital, Barnstable County Health and Environment, and various clinics.

Getting to Hyannis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hyannis

  1. Boston Logan International Airport - 70 miles away from Hyannis.
  2. T.F. Green Airport - 75 miles away from Hyannis.

Bus Stations Near Hyannis

  1. Hyannis Transportation Center - located in the center of Hyannis and serviced by Peter Pan Bus Lines and Plymouth & Brockton Street Railway Company.

Other Options

  1. Driving - Hyannis is easily accessible and is only a couple of hours’ drive from Boston and Cape Cod.
  2. Ferries - Hyannis has several ferry services provided by the Steamship Authority.

How to Get Around in Hyannis

  1. Walk - the town is small and walkable, perfect for leisurely exploring.
  2. Bike - with an excellent selection of bike rental shops scattered throughout the town, cruising Cape Cod just got easier.
  3. Car - rental and taxi services are also available for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Hyannis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Martha’s Vineyard - This classic New England island has something for everyone, from lighthouses to art galleries.
  2. Nantucket Island - Enjoy stunning views of Quaint New England villages, pristine beaches, and world-class cuisine.
  3. Provincetown - Famous for its art galleries, vibrant nightlife, and incredible whale-watching opportunities, Provincetown is a nearby must-visit destination.

Conclusion

Hyannis, Massachusetts, is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling around Cape Cod or New England. The town is rich in history, has beautiful beaches and natural scenery, delicious cuisine, and great activities for visitors of all ages. With our comprehensive guide, you’ll be equipped with everything you need to have an unforgettable trip to Hyannis.
