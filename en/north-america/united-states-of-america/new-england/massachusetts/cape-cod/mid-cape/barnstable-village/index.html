Introduction to Barnstable Village

Barnstable Village is a charming historic town located in Mid Cape, Cape Cod, Massachusetts. Founded in 1639, this quaint village is known for its rich maritime history, well-preserved 18th and 19th-century architecture, and breathtaking natural beauty.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a place to unwind, Barnstable Village has something to offer. In this guide, we’ll explore the must-see attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and everything in between.

List of Places to Visit in Barnstable Village

Make the most of your trip by visiting these top attractions in Barnstable Village:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Barnstable Historical Society Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, this museum houses numerous exhibits on the town’s fascinating past.

  • Sturgis Library: Established in 1867, this iconic library is the oldest in Cape Cod and a must-see for book lovers.

  • Coast Guard Heritage Museum: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the history and legacy of the US Coast Guard.

Natural Attractions of Barnstable Village: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandy Neck Beach: With six miles of stunning coastline, this beach is a great spot for swimming, fishing, and beachcombing.

  • Barnstable Harbor: Take a leisurely stroll along the harbor and enjoy the picturesque views of boats, sailboats, and yachts.

  • Cape Cod Rail Trail: Built on a former railroad bed, this 22-mile trail is a perfect destination for biking, walking, and running.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barnstable Village

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, don’t miss out on these scenic hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Great Marsh Trail: A two-mile trail that winds through sand dunes, salt marshes, and woodland areas.

  • Old King’s Highway Trail: This 14.9-mile route is perfect for biking, offering stunning views of historic homes, quaint villages, and beautiful landscapes.

  • Millway Beach Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that takes you along the beach and through some wooded areas along the salt marshes.

Best Things to Do in Barnstable Village: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of fun activities in Barnstable Village. Whether you’re traveling solo or with family, here are some of the top things to do:

  • Whale Watching Tours: Take a thrilling whale-watching tour to see humpback whales, dolphins, and seals up close.

  • Kayaking in Barnstable Harbor: Explore the scenic shores of Barnstable Harbor on a guided kayak tour.

  • Visit Cape Cod Brewery: Take a tour of the Cape Cod Brewery to learn about the brewing process and sample some of their famous craft beers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barnstable Village

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Barnstable Village, here’s a rough itinerary to help you make the most of your vacation:

Day 1: Check out the historical sites in town, including the Barnstable Historical Society Museum and Sturgis Library.

Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of the area, by walking the Great Marsh Trail and visiting Sandy Neck Beach.

Day 3: Take a whale-watching tour and kayaking trip to explore the stunning coastline.

Day 4: Visit the Coast Guard Heritage Museum and enjoy a craft beer at the Cape Cod Brewery.

Day 5: Spend the day biking along the Old King’s Highway Trail, taking in the gorgeous views of the quaint villages and historic homes.

Day 6: Visit Millway Beach and take a scenic boat tour along the harbor.

Day 7: Enjoy your last day in Barnstable Village by discovering the hidden gems in town and taking a relaxing walk along the harbor.

Food and Drink Scene of Barnstable Village

Barnstable Village is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of options to suit every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A Cape Cod staple, this dish is a must-try for seafood lovers. Try the one at The Dolphin Restaurant in Barnstable Village.

  • Clam Chowder: This creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, and spices is a hearty and delicious meal. Check out the version at The Barnstable Restaurant and Tavern.

  • Cranberry Bread: Made with fresh cranberries, this sweet bread is a beloved Cape Cod specialty. Get a loaf at Barnstable Market.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Cod Beer: This local brewery offers a wide variety of beer, including Cape Cod Red and Beach Blonde Ale.

  • Truro Vineyards: Visit this vineyard for a taste of their award-winning wines, including their famous Cabernet Franc.

  • Cape Cod Coffee: Roasted locally, Cape Cod Coffee is a popular choice among caffeine lovers.

Where to Stay in Barnstable Village: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury stay or a budget-friendly hostel, here are some of the best places to stay in Barnstable Village:

  • The Seabreeze Inn: This charming inn offers cozy rooms and beautiful views of the harbor.

  • Lamb and Lion Inn: A historic and elegant property that offers upscale amenities and personalized service.

  • Cape Cod Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers, this hostel offers basic amenities and a social atmosphere.

What to Buy in Barnstable Village: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Barnstable Village without bringing home some local souvenirs and products. Some popular items include:

  • Cape Cod Cranberry Products: From cranberry sauce to dried cranberries, these products make great gifts for friends and family.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: You’ll find plenty of local artisans selling beautiful and unique jewelry, including necklaces, earrings, and bracelets.

  • Cape Cod Wind Chimes: These beautiful chimes made from seashells and driftwood make for the perfect memento of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Barnstable Village

Barnstable Village is a town with rich cultural traditions and a proud maritime heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect local traditions, including:

  • Conservation efforts to protect the natural environment.

  • The importance of the seafood industry to the local economy.

  • The historic architecture of the town and its role in the town’s cultural identity.

Best Time to Visit Barnstable Village

The best time to visit Barnstable Village is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with activity. However, if you prefer a quieter vacation, consider visiting during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barnstable Village

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Barnstable Village:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.

  • Take some time to learn about the town’s rich history and cultural traditions.

  • Try the local seafood and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitats of local wildlife.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Overdo it at the local bars and breweries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barnstable Village

Barnstable Village is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise common sense and take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Locking doors and windows of your accommodation and rental vehicles.

  • Avoiding unfamiliar or unlit areas after dark.

  • Following all safety guidelines during outdoor activities.

Health in Barnstable Village

Barnstable Village is a relatively healthy place with several medical facilities nearby, including Cape Cod Hospital and Barnstable County Department of Health. Visitors should also take precautions against tick bites, which can carry Lyme disease.

Getting to Barnstable Village: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barnstable Village

The closest airport to Barnstable Village is Barnstable Municipal Airport, located four miles north of the town. Other nearby airports include Boston Logan International Airport and Providence Airport.

Bus Stations Near Barnstable Village

The main bus station serving Barnstable Village is the Hyannis Transportation Center, located three miles east of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Barnstable Village by car, with several major highways, including Route 6 and the Mid-Cape Highway, passing nearby.

How to Get Around in Barnstable Village

Barnstable Village is a walkable town, and visitors can easily navigate on foot or by bike. Cabs and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Barnstable Village: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re done exploring Barnstable Village, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Provincetown: Located at the tip of Cape Cod, Provincetown is known for its vibrant arts scene, beautiful beaches, and historic landmarks.

  • Nantucket Island: Take a ferry to this charming island, known for its beaches, lighthouses, and quaint streets lined with boutiques and cafes.

  • Martha’s Vineyard: This picturesque island is a favorite among tourists, known for its stunning views, quaint towns, and peaceful nature trails.

Conclusion

Barnstable Village is a beautiful and charming getaway destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing by the beach, this town is a must-see on any New England itinerary.
