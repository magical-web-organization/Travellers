Introduction to Bourne

Nestled in Upper Cape region of Massachusetts, Bourne is a serene town with spectacular natural beauty and rich history. It is an idyllic getaway for those seeking a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city. Bourne is home to various beautiful beaches, dense woodlands, and serene waterways that allow visitors to indulge in outdoor activities all year round. This comprehensive tour guide provides a list of places to visit in Bourne, hiking/biking routes, local culture, food and drink scene, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Bourne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bourne Historical Society and Aptucxet Trading Post Museum: This museum showcases the local history and houses various artifacts of the Old Bourne, including Native American items, historical documents, and maritime exhibits.
  • National Marine Life Center: Visit the marine animal hospital, observe seals in rehabilitation, and learn about the center’s marine conservation efforts.
  • Buzzards Bay Discovery Center: Discover the local wildlife, swim with seals, and learn various marine species through interactive activities.

Natural Attractions of Bourne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canal Park: This park offers scenic views of the Cape Cod Canal that connects Cape Cod Bay to Buzzards Bay. Visitors may relax by the canal or walk along the manicured paths while watching boats pass by.
  • Scusset Beach State Reservation: Enjoy swimming, fishing, and sunbathing at this beautiful sandy beach. It has picnic tables, restrooms, and changing rooms, ideal for family outings.
  • Bourne Scenic Park: This waterfront park offers a fantastic view of the Cape Cod Canal, where one can indulge in fishing, birdwatching, and hiking. There is also a playground for children and picnic areas for lunch.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bourne

  • Cape Cod Canal bike path: Experience the scenic views of Cape Cod Canal on a 7-mile paved bike path that stretches from Bourne to Sandwich. It is a popular spot for joggers, walkers, and cyclists.
  • Cape Cod Rail Trail: This 25.5-mile old railway track trail spans from South Dennis to South Wellfleet. It is a scenic path through woodlands, ponds, and hills.
  • Monks Park Trail: This is a 1.5-mile natural trail that runs along Cataumet pond. This easy and scenic trail is ideal for a leisurely walk through the woods.

Best Things to Do in Bourne: Activities and Tours

  • Horseneck Alpacas: Enjoy an interactive experience with alpacas at this beautiful farm. The visitors may take a farm tour, learn about the alpacas’ habitat, and have a chance to feed and pet them.
  • Gray Gables Market: Check out this charming market that sells various local food and drink products, unique souvenirs, and homemade delicacies.
  • Bourne Farm: This working farm features various trails for hiking, picnicking, and exploring the local wildlife. There are also guided tours of the property, including the farm animals, barns, and garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Bourne

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll: A classic New England fast food sandwich that uses fresh lobster meat, lemon juice, and mayonnaise stuffed in a toasted bun.
  • Fried Clams: Fresh clams coated in cornflake crumbs and fried golden brown served with tartar sauce and fries.
  • Cape Cod Creamery Ice Cream: Sample the sweet handmade, locally-sourced ice cream such as the popular Beachcomber Bar made with dark chocolate, coconut, almonds, and caramel.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Cod Beer: Handcrafted beer made with high-quality ingredients to create a delicious flavor, such as the popular Cape Cod Red Ale, Beach Blonde Ale, and IPA.
  • Nobska Farms Wine: Locally sourced fruit and grapes from Bourne used in all the wines for a unique taste, such as the popular cranberry dessert wine and Cabernet Franc red blend.

Where to Stay in Bourne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sea Crest Beach Hotel: This beach-front hotel boasts comfortable and luxurious rooms, indoor and outdoor pool, and various dining options.
  • The Inn at Cataumet: Stay in one of the charming rooms at this inn, featuring ornate woodwork, cozy décor, and a fireplace. They offer a complimentary continental breakfast.
  • Cape Cod Bourne Bed and Breakfast: Nestled in a serene neighborhood, this charming bed and breakfast features comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a big garden to relax in.

What to Buy in Bourne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gray Gables Market: Browse through the variety of unusual and unique products at this market, including local wine and craft beer, artisanal cheese, and chocolates.
  • Stuffed Quahogs: This local delicacy is a small clam shell stuffed with bread crumbs, Italian herbs, and chopped quahogs.
  • Cape Cod Jewelry: A beautiful souvenir option is the locally made Cape Cod-themed jewelry, featuring the iconic shape of the peninsula and various seashell designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bourne

The local culture of Bourne is heavily influenced by its maritime history. The town has deep roots in the fishing industry, and it is reflected in its food scene, museums, and architecture. Visitors also get a glimpse of the Native American heritage at Aptucxet Trading Post Museum. Locals are friendly and often wave to strangers passing by. It is customary to greet people with a smile and a nod.

Best Time to Visit Bourne

Bourne is lovely to visit all year round, but summers are the most popular due to its beach scene and outdoor activities. The warm weather from June to August brings crowds and higher prices. The fall season, September to November, offers mild weather, beautiful foliage, and fewer crowds. The winter season, December to February, is less touristy but offers opportunities for ice-skating, skiing, and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bourne

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and natural surroundings.
  • Treat people with kindness and respect.
  • Leave beaches and parks clean.

Don’t:

  • Litter on public beaches or parks.
  • Smoke on the beach.
  • Take sand or stones from the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bourne

Bourne is generally a safe town, but visitors should always take necessary precautions and stay aware of their surroundings. Be cautious while walking or hiking in areas with high wildlife activity. Keep valuables safe and locked when parked on the beach.

Health in Bourne

Visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid sunburns and dehydration during hot and sunny weather. Tick-borne diseases, such as Lyme disease, are prevalent in areas with high grass or wooded areas, protect yourself before venturing out in those areas.

Getting to Bourne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bourne is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Bourne

  • Barnstable Municipal Airport, located in the town of Hyannis, is about 23 miles from Bourne.

Bus Stations Near Bourne

  • Plymouth & Brockton Bus Company runs regular bus services from Boston to Bourne.

Other Options

  • Wood’s Hole Ferry Terminal connects visitors from Martha’s Vineyard with Bourne via ferry services.

How to Get Around in Bourne

The best way to explore Bourne is by car or bike. The local bike rental services are also available for a convenient way to cycle around the town.

Where to Go Next from Bourne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Falmouth: A picturesque coastal town known for its seafood, beaches, and shopping scene, located 20 miles east of Bourne.
  • Martha’s Vineyard: A popular summer destination, located 25 miles south of Bourne, with lovely beaches, seafood restaurants, and shopping options.
  • Nantucket: An island town known for its historical attractions, rolling sand dunes, and lighthouses, located 49 miles southeast of Bourne.

Conclusion

Bourne is a charming and beautiful town in Massachusetts that has something for everyone. From the stunning beaches to local culture, visitors can experience the best of New England in Bourne. This comprehensive tour guide gives them all the information they need to plan a perfect trip to Bourne. Don’t forget to enjoy the local cuisine, explore the natural beauty, and relax in the welcoming town of Bourne.
