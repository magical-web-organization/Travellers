Introduction to Sandwich

Located in the Upper Cape region of Cape Cod in Massachusetts, Sandwich is a charming historic town that is rich in culture and natural beauty. Founded in 1637, it is one of the oldest towns in the United States and its historic district is teeming with museums, galleries, and antique shops. Sandwich boasts beautiful beaches, scenic hiking trails, and numerous parks and gardens. It is a perfect destination for history buffs, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone looking for a relaxing getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Sandwich

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sandwich Glass Museum: This museum showcases the history of the glass industry in Sandwich and features exhibits and live glassblowing demonstrations.

  • Thornton W. Burgess Museum: Dedicated to the author and naturalist Thornton Burgess, this museum features exhibits on Burgess’ life and books, as well as live animals and gardens.

  • Hoxie House: Built in 1675, the Hoxie House is one of the oldest houses on Cape Cod and now serves as a museum of early Cape Cod life.

  • Heritage Museums and Gardens: This extensive museum is home to an impressive collection of art, American history exhibits, and beautiful outdoor gardens.

Natural Attractions of Sandwich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandwich Boardwalk: This scenic boardwalk stretches out into the sea and offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.

  • Shawme-Crowell State Forest: This park encompasses over 700 acres of forest and offers hiking and biking trails, wildlife viewing, and a picnic area.

  • Sandwich Beaches: Sandwich is known for its beautiful beaches, including Town Neck Beach, East Sandwich Beach, and Scusset Beach.

  • Green Briar Nature Center and Jam Kitchen: This nature center offers guided walks and educational programs on Cape Cod’s wildlife and habitats, as well as homemade jams and jellies made on site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandwich

  • Cape Cod Canal Bikeway: This 7.9-mile paved trail goes along the Cape Cod Canal and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding area.

  • Sandwich Heritage Trail: This 1.5-mile loop trail takes you through Sandwich’s historic district and past some of its most notable historic sites, including the 1675 Hoxie House.

  • Sandy Neck Trail: This 4-mile round trip trail takes you through the dunes of Sandy Neck Beach and offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Sandwich: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour from the Sandwich Marina and spot humpback whales, dolphins, and other marine life.

  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and explore the waterways around Sandwich, including the Great Marsh.

  • Cranberry Bog Tours: Learn about cranberry farming in the area with a guided tour of a local bog.

  • Art Galleries: Explore the numerous art galleries in Sandwich, many of which showcase the work of local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sandwich

Day Activities
1 Visit the Sandwich Glass Museum and take a stroll along the boardwalk.
2 Explore the Hoxie House and Thornton W. Burgess Museum.
3 Take a hike in the Shawme-Crowell State Forest and visit the Green Briar Nature Center.
4 Relax on the beach and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

7 Shop for souvenirs and explore the town’s antique shops and galleries.

Food and Drink Scene of Sandwich

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England dish, the lobster roll is a must-try in Sandwich. Try one at Fisherman’s View or The Pilot House.

  • Clam Chowder: Another New England classic, clam chowder is a creamy soup made with clams and potatoes. Try it at The Dan’l Webster Inn or The Belfry Inn and Bistro.

  • Quahog: This shellfish is a staple of Rhode Island and can be found in many restaurants in Sandwich. Try them stuffed with breadcrumbs and baked.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Cod Beer: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including the popular Cape Cod Red.

  • Truro Vineyards: Just a short drive from Sandwich, Truro Vineyards offers wine tastings and tours of their winery and vineyard.

Where to Stay in Sandwich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Dan’l Webster Inn and Spa: This historic inn features elegant rooms, on-site dining, and a relaxing spa.

  • The Belfry Inn and Bistro: This unique bed and breakfast offers rooms in a former church and a delicious farm-to-table restaurant.

  • Sandwich Lodge and Resort: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Sandwich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Glassware from the Sandwich Glass Museum gift shop

  • Jams and jellies from the Green Briar Jam Kitchen

  • Antique and vintage finds from the numerous shops in town

Local Culture and Customs of Sandwich

Sandwich is known for its rich history and culture, with numerous museums and historic sites throughout the town. The area also has strong ties to the fishing industry, and seafood is a staple of the local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Sandwich

The best time to visit Sandwich is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the beaches are bustling with activity. However, spring and fall offer cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, making it a great time to explore the town’s museums and natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandwich

Do:

  • Visit the historic sites and museums in town

  • Try the local cuisine, including seafood and cranberries

  • Explore the outdoor attractions, including the beaches and hiking trails

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural areas

  • Ignore traffic laws while biking or driving

  • Forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent

Safety and Health

Safety in Sandwich

Sandwich is a safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should always use caution and common sense when exploring the area.

Health in Sandwich

Sandwich has several medical facilities, including the Sandwich Medical Center and Cape Cod Hospital. Visitors should also be aware of ticks and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing long sleeves and using insect repellent.

Getting to Sandwich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sandwich

  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS): 75 miles away

  • T.F. Green Airport (PVD): 75 miles away

Bus Stations Near Sandwich

  • Plymouth and Brockton Street Railway Company: Offers bus service from Boston and other nearby areas

  • Cape Cod Regional Transit Authority: Offers local and regional bus service throughout Cape Cod

Other Options

  • Ferry: There is ferry service from Boston to Provincetown, about 45 minutes from Sandwich

How to Get Around in Sandwich

The best way to get around Sandwich is by car, as many of the town’s attractions are spread out. Biking is also a great option, and there are several rental shops in town. Public transportation is limited, but the Cape Cod Regional Transit Authority offers bus service throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Sandwich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Falmouth: This picturesque town is known for its beaches, biking trails, and historic sites.

  • Martha’s Vineyard: Take a ferry to this popular island destination, known for its beautiful scenery, quaint towns, and delicious seafood.

  • Provincetown: Located at the tip of Cape Cod, Provincetown is a vibrant arts community with museums, galleries, and theaters.

Conclusion

Sandwich, Massachusetts is a charming and historic town with something for everyone. From museums and hiking trails to beautiful beaches and delicious seafood, it is a perfect vacation destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Cape Cod. Plan your trip today and discover all that Sandwich has to offer!
