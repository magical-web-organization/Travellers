Introduction to Falmouth

Located in Upper Cape, Cape Cod, Massachusetts, Falmouth is a beautiful seaside town that offers a perfect blend of history, natural beauty and culture. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover or simply looking for a relaxing vacation by the water, Falmouth has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Falmouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Falmouth Museums on the Green

Falmouth Museums on the Green offer a glimpse into the town’s rich history and heritage. The museums consist of two historic homes and an outdoor garden, showcasing the colonial era of Falmouth.

Highfield Hall and Gardens

Highfield Hall and Gardens is a beautiful historic mansion with 6 acres of stunning gardens. You can take a tour of the mansion and learn about its history or simply stroll through the gardens enjoying the beauty and serenity.

Woods Hole Science Aquarium

The Woods Hole Science Aquarium is a popular attraction for families with kids. This small aquarium features a variety of local marine life, including lobsters, sea turtles, and local fish.

Natural Attractions of Falmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shining Sea Bikeway

The Shining Sea Bikeway is a 10.7-mile bike trail with stunning views of the coastline. The trail runs through salt marshes, cranberry bogs, and offers panoramic views of Vineyard Sound.

Old Silver Beach

Old Silver Beach is a popular beach in Falmouth with crystal clear water and brown sugar sand. The beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, lounging, and building sandcastles.

Ashumet Holly Wildlife Sanctuary

The Ashumet Holly Wildlife Sanctuary is a 45-acre nature preserve with walking trails and over 70 species of native plants. The sanctuary is also home to a variety of bird species, making it a great spot for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Falmouth

Falmouth is a biker’s paradise, with beautiful coastal scenery and flat terrain. The Shining Sea Bikeway mentioned earlier is a must-ride, but there are other great options as well. Additionally, there are some great hiking trails in Falmouth. The Beebe Woods/ Peterson Farm Conservation Area is one such destination offering five miles of walking trails.

Best Things to Do in Falmouth: Activities and Tours

Boat Cruise

A boat cruise is a great way to explore the waters of Falmouth. You can choose from a variety of options, including a sunset cruise, a whale watching tour or a fishing trip.

Shopping

Falmouth is a great destination for shopping. The town has several boutiques, antique shops, and gift shops, making it a great spot for souvenirs and unique finds.

Golf

Falmouth has multiple golf courses, including The Cape Club, a championship course with spectacular views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Falmouth

Day 1: Explore the Museums and Historical Sites in Falmouth Day 2: Spend a day at Old Silver Beach enjoying swimming, sunbathing, and beach games. Day 3: Rent bikes and ride the Shining Sea Bikeway Day 4: Take a boat cruise and enjoy the sunset and the water views. Day 5: Go shopping and buy some local souvenirs. Day 6: Play a round of golf at one of the golf courses. Day 7: Relax at your hotel or spend a leisurely day hiking at the Beebe Woods/Peterson Farm Conservation Area.

Food and Drink Scene of Falmouth

Must-Try Dishes

Seafood is definitely a must-try in Falmouth, with fresh local seafood served in many restaurants. Some signature dishes include lobster rolls, clam chowder, and fish and chips. Additionally, the town is home to several vineyards and wineries that offer tours and tastings, making it a great destination for wine lovers.

Local Beverages

If you love beer, you won’t want to miss the Bad Martha Beer Company. They offer unique beers such as their Cranberry Kolsch and their Toasted Coconut Porter, which are quite popular among visitors.

Where to Stay in Falmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Falmouth has several great options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. A few notable options include:

Shoreway Acres Inn and Cape Cod Resort

The Shoreway Acres Inn and Cape Cod Resort is a charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Falmouth. It has a beautiful outdoor pool and is just minutes away from some of the town’s best attractions.

The Captain’s Manor Inn

The Captain’s Manor Inn is a sophisticated and elegant bed and breakfast known for its exceptional hospitality. The inn is located in a historic mansion and offers luxurious rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Falmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Falmouth, be sure to browse the town’s many specialty shops, art galleries, and boutiques. Some popular items include locally made pottery, handmade jewelry, and handsewn textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Falmouth

The locals of Falmouth are known for their laid-back attitude and friendly nature. The town has a rich history of seafaring and is proud of its maritime heritage. Additionally, Falmouth has a vibrant arts community, and you can find art galleries featuring local artists and frequent art festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Falmouth

The best time to visit Falmouth is from late May to early September when the weather is warm and the beaches are open. Additionally, fall is a great time to visit when the foliage is at its peak, and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Falmouth

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and wines.
  • Do explore the town’s history and heritage.
  • Do come prepared for outdoor activities like swimming and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t expect the nightlife scene to be as lively as in some of the larger cities.
  • Don’t forget your sunscreen and bug spray when exploring the outdoors.
  • Don’t hesitate to ask locals for recommendations or help if you need it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Falmouth

Falmouth is a very safe place to visit. The town has a low crime rate, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any safety concerns. However, as with any tourist destination, visitors should be vigilant and keep their belongings close.

Health in Falmouth

Falmouth has several medical facilities, including Falmouth Hospital, making it a suitable destination for those with health concerns.

Getting to Falmouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Falmouth

The closest airport to Falmouth is Barnstable Municipal Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away. Another viable option is Boston’s Logan International Airport which is around 75 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Falmouth

Falmouth is serviced by Plymouth & Brockton Street Railway Co., which offers regular bus services to Boston, New York City, and Providence.

Other Options

If you prefer, you can also drive to Falmouth, or use ride-share services like Uber or Lyft to get around town.

How to Get Around in Falmouth

Falmouth is relatively small, and many attractions are within walking distance. However, biking is a popular activity, and you can easily rent bicycles from several shops in town. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Falmouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Falmouth is a great base to explore the Cape Cod region. Some nearby destinations include:

  • Martha’s Vineyard
  • Nantucket Island
  • Plymouth
  • Hyannis

Conclusion

Falmouth is an appealing and laid-back seaside town with a rich heritage and plenty to offer visitors of all tastes and preferences. From stunning beaches to cultural attractions, Falmouth truly is a gem on Cape Cod that’s calling out to be explored.
