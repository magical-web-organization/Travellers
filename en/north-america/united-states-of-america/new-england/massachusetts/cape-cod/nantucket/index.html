Introduction to Nantucket

Nantucket is an idyllic island located in Cape Cod, Massachusetts. It is a popular destination for those seeking a quiet, laid-back retreat from the bustling metropolis. Nantucket boasts 82 miles of pristine beaches, a charming historic district, and picturesque landscapes that will take your breath away.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the best of Nantucket. Whether you are a culture aficionado, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, there is something for everyone on this enchanting island.

List of Places to Visit in Nantucket

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nantucket Whaling Museum: Take a trip back in time to learn about Nantucket’s rich history as the whaling capital of the world. Explore artifacts, paintings, and exhibitions that showcase the island’s unique maritime heritage.

  • The Oldest House and Garden: Step into one of the oldest houses on Nantucket and marvel at its 17th-century architecture. The house’s immaculately-maintained gardens will also capture your heart.

  • Nantucket Lightship Basket Museum: See the intricate, woven baskets that have been a part of Nantucket’s cultural heritage for more than 150 years at this fascinating museum.

  • Brant Point Lighthouse: Located at the northern end of Nantucket, this lighthouse is an iconic landmark that offers stunning views of the harbor and surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Nantucket: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sankaty Head Light: Located on the eastern end of Nantucket, the lighthouse offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean. Standing atop a 90-foot bluff, visitors can explore the grounds surrounding the lighthouse and take in the sweeping panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  • Madaket Beach and Tom Nevers Beach: These two popular beaches are perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying a myriad of water sports.

  • Great Point Light: Accessible only by beach vehicles, Great Point Light is a must-visit for those who enjoy off-road adventure.

  • Coskata-Coatue Wildlife Refuge: Located on Nantucket’s northeast shore, this expansive nature preserve offers miles of hiking trails, pristine beaches, and stunning scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nantucket

Nantucket is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with more than 30 miles of trails that span the island’s idyllic beaches, dunes, and woodlands. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Polpis Harbor Trail: A scenic trail that follows the shores of beautiful Nantucket Harbor.

  • Masquetuck Reservation Trail: A meandering trail that passes through the rugged coastline of Nantucket’s eastern shore.

  • Highlands Trail: A challenging trail that rewards hikers with expansive views of the Atlantic Ocean and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Nantucket: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Nantucket’s calm waters are perfect for kayaking, and several tour companies offer excursions that allow you to explore the island’s scenic waterways.

  • Fishing: Nantucket’s waters are teeming with marine life, making it a prime spot for fishing enthusiasts. You can go out on a charter boat or try your luck at one of the island’s many fishing locations.

  • Sunset Sail: End your day with a stunning sunset sail around the harbor aboard a traditional Nantucket boat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nantucket

Day 1: Explore downtown Nantucket and visit the Whaling Museum.

Day 2: Spend the day at Madaket Beach and take a sunset sail in the evening.

Day 3: Hike to Great Point Light and explore the Coskata-Coatue Wildlife Refuge.

Day 4: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Polpis Harbor Trail and visit the Oldest House and Garden.

Day 5: Enjoy a day of fishing or kayaking.

Day 6: Take in the scenic views along the Masquetuck Reservation Trail.

Day 7: End your trip with a trip to Sankaty Head Lighthouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Nantucket

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Nantucket is known for its fresh, locally-caught seafood, and the lobster roll is a must-try.

  • Quahog Chowder: A rich, creamy clam chowder that is a local favorite.

  • Oysters: Nantucket’s waters are home to world-renowned oysters, which are a must-try for seafood enthusiasts.

Local Beverages

  • Nantucket Wine: Nantucket has a vibrant wine scene, with several vineyards located on the island.

  • Cisco Brewers: A local brewery that offers tours and tastings of their award-winning craft beers.

Where to Stay in Nantucket: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • 76 Main: A boutique hotel located in downtown Nantucket, known for its chic decor and comfortable rooms.

  • The Beachside: A charming guesthouse located just steps from the beach and downtown Nantucket.

  • HI Nantucket Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel featuring dorm-style rooms and communal spaces.

What to Buy in Nantucket: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Nantucket Baskets: These hand-woven baskets are one of Nantucket’s most famous exports and make the perfect souvenir.

  • Gray Lady Ale: A locally brewed beer that is well-regarded by beer enthusiasts.

  • Nantucket Lighthouse Ornaments: A perfect way to remember the stunning lighthouses of Nantucket.

Local Culture and Customs of Nantucket

Nantucket has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. The island is also known for its friendly locals and relaxed, laid-back atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Nantucket

The best time to visit Nantucket is between May and October when the weather is warmer, and tourist attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nantucket

Do:

  • Try the fresh seafood, especially the lobster rolls and oysters.
  • Explore the many hiking and biking trails that span the island.
  • Treat the locals with respect and kindness.

Don’t:

  • Park your car in prohibited areas; Nantucket strictly enforces parking laws.
  • Pick the wildflowers that dot the island.
  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches or public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nantucket

Nantucket is a safe destination, but visitors should exercise caution when swimming, especially in rough seas. Rip currents are common, and swimmers should always check the beach conditions before entering the water.

Health in Nantucket

Nantucket has world-class medical facilities, including several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. It is always advisable to consult a doctor before traveling and to purchase travel insurance.

Getting to Nantucket: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nantucket

  • Nantucket Memorial Airport: Located on the south side of the island, Nantucket Memorial Airport offers daily flights to several major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Nantucket

  • Hyannis Bus Terminal: The Hyannis Bus Terminal, located on the mainland, offers daily bus services to Nantucket.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Visitors can also take a ferry from Hyannis or Martha’s Vineyard to Nantucket.

How to Get Around in Nantucket

Nantucket is a small island that is easily navigable by bike, car or on foot. A public transportation system also operates on the island.

Where to Go Next from Nantucket: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Martha’s Vineyard: Catch a ferry from Nantucket to the nearby island of Martha’s Vineyard, renowned for its charming towns, beaches, and picturesque scenery.

  • Provincetown: A historic, seaside town located in Cape Cod, Provincetown is known for its thriving artist community, beautiful beaches, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Nantucket is a hidden gem that offers visitors a glimpse into the quaint, relaxed lifestyle of coastal New England. From beaches to historic landmarks, culinary delights to outdoor adventures, Nantucket has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now ready to plan a memorable trip to this enchanting island.
