Introduction to Martha’s Vineyard

Martha’s Vineyard is a beautiful island located south of Cape Cod in Massachusetts, USA. Known for its scenic beauty, marina life, and cultural heritage, Martha’s Vineyard offers an unforgettable vacation experience. From historic lighthouses to sandy beaches and vineyards, Martha’s Vineyard has something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Martha’s Vineyard

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Martha’s Vineyard Museum

Explore the cultural history of Martha’s Vineyard at the Martha’s Vineyard Museum. The museum showcases the cultural heritage and history of the island and its residents through multimedia exhibits and interactive displays.

2. Edgartown Lighthouse

The Edgartown Lighthouse, built in 1828, is one of the most iconic landmarks on the island. Climb to the top of the lighthouse for breathtaking views of the island and the Atlantic Ocean.

3. Aquinnah Cliffs

The Aquinnah Cliffs are a geological wonder and natural beauty on the island. The cliffs are made of colorful clay and are believed to be over 100 million years old.

4. Gay Head Light

The Gay Head Light is one of the most photographed lighthouses in the world. Located at the westernmost point of Martha’s Vineyard, it offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding beaches.

5. Oak Bluffs

Oak Bluffs is a charming town known for its colorful, gingerbread houses, quaint shops, and restaurants. Stroll through the streets to experience the town’s unique architecture and vibrant culture.

Natural Attractions of Martha’s Vineyard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Menemsha Beach

Menemsha Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches on Martha’s Vineyard. The beach is surrounded by rolling hills and offers stunning views of the sunset over the Atlantic Ocean.

2. State Beach

State Beach is a long stretch of sandy beach on the south side of the island. It is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or simply relaxing in the sun.

3. Chappy Island

Chappy Island is a small island located off the eastern coast of Martha’s Vineyard. The island is perfect for hiking, biking, and birdwatching, with several scenic trails and viewpoints.

4. Felix Neck Wildlife Sanctuary

Felix Neck Wildlife Sanctuary is a nature reserve on the island with over 350 acres of diverse habitat, including forests, meadows, ponds, and salt marshes. The sanctuary offers several nature trails, guided tours, and educational programs for visitors.

5. Mytoi Garden

Mytoi Garden is a serene and tranquil Japanese-style garden on the island. The garden has a small pond, walking trails, and several species of trees and plants. It is a perfect spot for a peaceful stroll and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Martha’s Vineyard

1. Martha’s Vineyard Trail

The Martha’s Vineyard Trail is a scenic, 44-mile bike loop around the island. The trail passes through several scenic sites, including beaches, lighthouses, and nature reserves. The trail is suitable for cyclists of all levels.

2. State Forest

Martha’s Vineyard State Forest is a 5,300-acre forest on the island with several hiking and biking trails. The forest is home to several species of plants and wildlife, including oak trees, blueberries, and white-tailed deer.

Best Things to Do in Martha’s Vineyard: Activities and Tours

1. Sunset Sail

Experience the beauty of the sunset over the Atlantic Ocean with a sunset sail around the island. Several tour operators offer sunset sails with champagne and hors d’oeuvres.

2. Vineyard Artisans Festival

The Vineyard Artisans Festival is a summer event on the island showcasing local handmade arts and crafts. The festival features over 70 local artisans and crafters, live music, and food trucks.

3. Fly-Fishing

Martha’s Vineyard is a popular destination for fly-fishing enthusiasts, with several tour operators offering guided fishing trips around the island. Popular fish species in the waters around the island include striped bass, bluefish, and false albacore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Martha’s Vineyard

Day 1: Arrive at Martha’s Vineyard, check into your hotel, and explore the town of Oak Bluffs.

Day 2: Take a bike tour of the island, including State Beach, Gay Head Light, and the Martha’s Vineyard Trail.

Day 3: Visit the Martha’s Vineyard Museum, and learn about the history and culture of the island.

Day 4: Hike the trails of Martha’s Vineyard State Forest, and enjoy a picnic lunch amidst the scenic beauty.

Day 5: Take a sunset sail around the island, and experience the beauty of the sunset over the Atlantic Ocean.

Day 6: Explore Edgartown and visit the Edgartown Lighthouse. Enjoy a seafood lunch at Menemsha Beach.

Day 7: Relax on the beach and soak up the sun, or take a day trip to nearby Nantucket.

Food and Drink Scene of Martha’s Vineyard

Must-Try Dishes

1. Lobster Roll

The lobster roll is a staple in Martha’s Vineyard, with several restaurants serving this delicious seafood delicacy. The lobster is usually served in a toasted bun with butter and mayo.

2. Clam Chowder

Clam chowder is a classic New England dish and is especially popular on Martha’s Vineyard. The chowder is made with clams, potatoes, onions, and cream or milk.

3. Fried Clams

Fried clams are a favorite snack food in Martha’s Vineyard. The clams are coated in batter and fried until crispy.

Local Beverages

1. Martha’s Vineyard Wine

Martha’s Vineyard produces several types of wine, including Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Merlot, and Cabernet Franc. The island has several vineyards and wineries, offering tastings and tours to visitors.

2. Island Ale

Island Ale is a popular beer brewed on the island by the Martha’s Vineyard Ale Works. The beer is made with local ingredients, such as barley and hops, and is available at most local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Martha’s Vineyard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Harbor View Hotel

The Harbor View Hotel, located in Edgartown, is one of the most luxurious hotels on the island. The hotel boasts a stunning oceanfront view, a private beach, and several fine dining options.

2. The Island Inn

The Island Inn is a family-friendly hotel located in Oak Bluffs, with a pool, gardens, and several outdoor activities.

3. The Dockside Inn

The Dockside Inn is a budget-friendly hotel in Oak Bluffs, with clean and comfortable rooms, and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Martha’s Vineyard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Island-made jewelry

Martha’s Vineyard has several jewelry stores selling locally made jewelry. The jewelry is often made with beach glass, seashells, and sterling silver.

2. Handmade ceramics

Several local potters make and sell their ceramics on Martha’s Vineyard. The ceramics are often inspired by the island’s natural beauty and color palette.

Local Culture and Customs of Martha’s Vineyard

Martha’s Vineyard has a rich and diverse culture, influenced by its native Wampanoag population, colonial history, and maritime heritage. The island’s residents are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and are proud of their island’s unique culture and identity.

Best Time to Visit Martha’s Vineyard

The best time to visit Martha’s Vineyard is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the island comes alive with festivals and events. However, the shoulder seasons of spring and fall offer a quieter and more laid-back atmosphere, with mild weather and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Martha’s Vineyard

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural environment and wildlife.
  • Try the local seafood and wine.
  • Attend a festival or event to experience the island’s culture.
  • Rent a bike or take a hike to explore the island’s scenic beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches or in public areas.
  • Ignore the local customs and culture.
  • Overcrowd or disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Martha’s Vineyard

Martha’s Vineyard is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a friendly and welcoming community. However, visitors should exercise caution and common sense, especially when swimming and boating in the ocean.

Health in Martha’s Vineyard

Martha’s Vineyard has several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should take precautions against tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease, by wearing long sleeves and pants, using insect repellent, and performing tick checks after spending time outdoors.

Where to Go Next from Martha’s Vineyard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nantucket

Nantucket is a beautiful island located just south of Martha’s Vineyard. It is known for its stunning beaches, historic architecture, and world-class restaurants.

Cape Cod

Cape Cod is a popular vacation destination located just north of Martha’s Vineyard. It is known for its scenic lighthouses, sandy beaches, and charming seaside towns.

Conclusion

Martha’s Vineyard is a beautiful and unique vacation destination, offering visitors a glimpse into the island’s rich culture and scenic beauty. Whether you prefer outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply relaxing on the beach, Martha’s Vineyard has something for everyone. Plan your visit today, and experience the natural beauty and warm hospitality of Martha’s Vineyard.
