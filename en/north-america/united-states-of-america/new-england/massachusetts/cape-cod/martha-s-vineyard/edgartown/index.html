Introduction to Edgartown

Nestled on the eastern coast of Martha’s Vineyard, Edgartown is a charming town with a rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. This serene town is renowned for its elegant old homes, scenic coastal vistas, and quintessential New England charm.

List of Places to Visit in Edgartown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Edgartown’s history dates back to the 17th century, resulting in a rich heritage that is evident in its numerous museums and historical places.

  • The Martha’s Vineyard Museum: This museum offers a comprehensive overview of the island’s rich history, including exhibits about whaling, shipbuilding, and the island’s early settlers.

  • Vincent House Museum: Built in 1672, the Vincent House is one of the oldest houses on Martha’s Vineyard and now serves as a museum, showcasing the early colonial era.

Natural Attractions of Edgartown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Edgartown is not just a cultural and historical hub, but the town also offers an abundance of natural beauty and outdoor adventures.

  • South Beach: South Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches on Martha’s Vineyard, offering miles of unspoiled coastline with pristine sand dunes and crystal-clear water.

  • Felix Neck Wildlife Sanctuary: This amazing wildlife sanctuary located in Edgartown protects the diverse flora and fauna of the island and offers visitors a unique opportunity to observe and understand the ecosystems of Martha’s Vineyard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edgartown

Edgartown is surrounded by natural beauty, and the best way to explore the island’s hidden gems is on foot or by bike.

  • Cape Poge Wildlife Refuge Trail: This two-mile trail will take you through the pristine ecosystems of Martha’s Vineyard’s northeast corner, boasting stunning ocean views, dunes, and estuaries.

  • Edgartown Great Pond Trail: This trail will lead you through the secluded landscape surrounding the great pond and lush woodland, making it an excellent option for nature enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Edgartown: Activities and Tours

Edgartown is bursting with activities and tours that cater to different interests and age groups.

  • Duke’s County Historical Society Walking Tours: These guided tours provide visitors with an intricate overview of Edgartown’s historical architecture, giving you a unique ‘behind the scenes’ glimpse of this town’s captivating history.

  • Unguided Winery Bike Tour: Rent a bike and take a self-guided tour through the countryside, with stops at some of the island’s picturesque vineyards like Chilmark Chocolates and Grey Barn Farm.

Week-Long Itinerary for Edgartown

Here is an itinerary for a week-long stay in Edgartown:

Day 1: Arrive in Edgartown and explore the town’s historic architecture.

Day 2: Spend the day at South Beach exploring ocean trails and soaking up the sun.

Day 3: Rent a bike and visit the charming Martha’s Vineyard farms, including Grey Barn Farm, Chilmark Chocolates, and West Tisbury Farmers’ Market.

Day 4: Take a guided walking tour of Edgartown’s historic homes and famous landmarks, including the Vincent House, Dr. Daniel Fisher House, and the Old Whaling Church.

Day 5: Book a whale watching tour and witness the majestic marine mammals.

Day 6: Enjoy some time at Felix Neck Wildlife Sanctuary, hiking, and spotting wildlife.

Day 7: Depart from Edgartown and head to nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Edgartown

Edgartown’s food scene is a mixture of traditional New England cuisine, fresh seafood with a modern twist, and locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Bisque: Thick and creamy soup made with lobster and garden-fresh vegetables.

  • Clam Chowder: A creamy soup with clams, potatoes, vegetables, and plenty of herbs and spices.

  • Seafood Platter: This is the quintessential dish of coastal Massachusetts, featuring a mix of freshly fried clams, scallops, shrimp and local fish with tartar sauce on the side.

Local Beverages

  • Island-made beer: Sample the locally brewed beers at Offshore Ale Company, Edgartown Brewery, and Bad Martha Farmer’s Brewery, etc.

  • Wine: Visit the island’s wineries, like Chilmark Chocolates, for locally crafted wine with vineyards that provide stunning views of the countryside.

Where to Stay in Edgartown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Edgartown offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious suites to budget-friendly hostels.

  • Harbor View Hotel: This iconic hotel is located on the waterfront and offers luxurious rooms and suites, a spa, access to a private beach, and excellent dining options.

  • Ashley Inn: This bed and breakfast, located in the heart of Edgartown, offers cozy rooms, a tranquil outdoor patio, and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Edgartown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Edgartown is known for its unique shopping experience, with a range of independent boutiques and shops.

  • Martha’s Vineyard Bracelet: This handmade bracelet, featuring multi-colored beads, charm, and an “M.V.” pendant, is a perfect souvenir for those who love this island.

  • Grey Barn Farm Cheese: Cheese lovers can bring home some award-winning cheeses made by Grey Barn Farm, a local farm located in Chilmark.

Local Culture and Customs of Edgartown

  • Respect for the environment and wildlife is a crucial aspect of Edgartown’s culture, making it a perfect destination for eco-tourism enthusiasts.

  • Locals are friendly and hospitable, so visitors should not hesitate to start a conversation or ask for advice or recommendations.

Best Time to Visit Edgartown

The best time to visit Edgartown is during the summertime, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edgartown

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and environment by not littering, disturbing the animals, or damaging any natural resources.

  • Explore the island’s coastline with a rented kayak or stand-up paddleboard.

Don’t:

  • Don’t visit Edgartown during the off-season, as most businesses and attractions may not be open.

  • Don’t be afraid to try the local delicacies and cuisine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edgartown

Edgartown is a safe and secure place to travel with minimal risks.

  • Be aware of the weather conditions and check forecasts before going out to the sea.

  • Respect other drivers and pedestrians on the road.

Health in Edgartown

Edgartown has healthcare facilities for visitors in case of emergencies.

  • Always apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Edgartown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edgartown

The nearest airports to Edgartown are the Martha’s Vineyard Airport and the Boston Logan International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Edgartown

The nearest bus stations to Edgartown are the Martha’s Vineyard Bus Terminal and the Woods Hole Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can take the ferry from Woods Hole, Massachusetts, to Martha’s Vineyard, and Edgartown is easily accessible from other parts of the island by bus or bike.

How to Get Around in Edgartown

The best way to get around in Edgartown is on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent cars from local car rentals.

Where to Go Next from Edgartown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oak Bluffs: A short distance from Edgartown, this town features vintage architecture, unique boutiques, and a variety of restaurants.

  • Aquinnah Cliffs: Visitors can witness the amazing cliffs and beautiful beaches on the southwestern tip of Martha’s Vineyard.

Conclusion

Edgartown, located on Martha’s Vineyard, offers a perfect mix of nature and culture, making it a must-visit destination for travelers to Massachusetts. With its luxurious accommodations, award-winning cuisine, and plethora of outdoor activities and tours, Edgartown will undoubtedly leave every visitor with unforgettable memories.
