Introduction to Aquinnah

Situated on the westernmost point of the island of Martha’s Vineyard in Massachusetts, Aquinnah is a charming coastal town that offers scenic views and a rich cultural history. The town was originally inhabited by the Wampanoag tribe, one of the Native American groups who resided in this area for over 10,000 years. Today, Aquinnah is known for its natural beauty and unique attractions, including historic sites, hiking trails, and beautiful beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Aquinnah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Aquinnah Cultural Center: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Wampanoag people, who have lived in this region for centuries. Exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and art displays.

  • Gay Head Lighthouse: One of the most famous landmarks in Martha’s Vineyard, the Gay Head Lighthouse stands at the edge of the Gay Head Cliffs, overlooking the Atlantic Ocean. Built in 1799, the lighthouse has been restored and is open to visitors during the summer.

Natural Attractions of Aquinnah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Menemsha Hills: Hiking enthusiasts will enjoy the trails in Menemsha Hills, which offer breathtaking views of the Elizabeth Islands and the surrounding coastline.

  • Aquinnah Cliffs: Rising up to 150 feet above the sea, the Aquinnah Cliffs are a stunning natural wonder that provide a panoramic view of the sea.

  • Moshup Beach: One of the most popular beaches in Aquinnah, Moshup Beach boasts clear blue waters and powdery white sands. Visitors can relax on the beach and swim in the clean sea waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aquinnah

Aquinnah offers various hiking and biking routes that provide breathtaking views of its natural wonders. Popular routes include the Menemsha Hills trail and the Aquinnah Cliffs trail. Visitors can rent bikes from local shops or take guided tours to explore the trails.

Best Things to Do in Aquinnah: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Charters: Aquinnah is known for its fishing charters that take visitors on excursions to the nearby waters where they can catch a variety of fish.

  • Vineyard Transit Authority: A popular way to explore Martha’s Vineyard is by taking a bus tour, which is offered by Vineyard Transit Authority. Visitors can choose from various routes that highlight the best attractions in Martha’s Vineyard.

  • Sunset Tours: Aquinnah is known for its magnificent sunsets, and visitors can take sunset tours to enjoy them. These tours usually include visits to scenic spots like the Menemsha Hills or the Aquinnah Cliffs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aquinnah

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Aquinnah:

  • Day 1: Visit the Aquinnah Cultural Center and explore the Menemsha Hills trail
  • Day 2: Take a fishing charter and enjoy a sunset tour
  • Day 3: Visit the Gay Head Lighthouse and Moshup Beach
  • Day 4: Bike tour of the Aquinnah Cliffs trail
  • Day 5: Vineyard Transit bus tour of Martha’s Vineyard
  • Day 6: Relax on Moshup Beach and take a swimming lesson
  • Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and enjoy the local food scene

Food and Drink Scene of Aquinnah

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Aquinnah is known for its fresh seafood, including oysters, clams, lobsters, and crabs. Visitors can try local specialties at the various seafood restaurants in town.

  • Clam Chowder: A classic New England dish, clam chowder is a must-try when in Aquinnah.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Several vineyards are located in Martha’s Vineyard, producing award-winning wines that visitors can enjoy at local wineries.

  • Beer: Craft breweries in Martha’s Vineyard offer a variety of locally brewed beers that are popular with visitors.

Where to Stay in Aquinnah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous accommodation options available in Aquinnah, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Popular choices include:

  • The Outermost Inn: A luxury inn situated right on the beach, the Outermost Inn offers stunning views of the sea and the Aquinnah Cliffs.

  • The Beach Plum Inn: A charming bed and breakfast located in a historic farmhouse, the Beach Plum Inn is known for its cozy rooms and exceptional service.

  • The Duck Inn: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable dormitory-style accommodation, the Duck Inn is popular with backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Aquinnah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artwork: Aquinnah is known for its vibrant contemporary art scene, and visitors can find unique paintings, sculptures, and other works of art in local galleries.

  • Jewelry: Many local artisans specialize in creating unique jewelry pieces using shells, sea glass, and other natural materials found in Aquinnah.

Local Culture and Customs of Aquinnah

The Wampanoag people have a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn about their traditions and customs. Respectful behavior is expected when visiting cultural sites or attending events, and visitors are encouraged to support local artists and artisans by purchasing their work.

Best Time to Visit Aquinnah

The best time to visit Aquinnah is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild and the days are long. The town can get crowded during this time, so visitors are advised to book accommodations and tours well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aquinnah

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Wampanoag people.

  • Try local seafood delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Pick flowers in the Menemsha Hills or the Aquinnah Cliffs.

  • Litter on the beaches or in the town center.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aquinnah

Aquinnah is a safe town but visitors should take standard safety precautions such as not leaving valuables in plain sight in their cars and locking doors when leaving a rental.

Health in Aquinnah

Aquinnah has a medical center which can provide urgent care and other medical attention as needed. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications with them and check with their doctor for any travel vaccines needed.

Getting to Aquinnah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aquinnah

The nearest airport to Aquinnah is Martha’s Vineyard Airport, located about 15 miles away. Logan International Airport in Boston is the closest major airport, with connecting flights to Martha’s Vineyard Airport.

Bus Stations Near Aquinnah

The Vineyard Transit Authority offers bus services to Aquinnah from various parts on Martha’s Vineyard.

Other Options

Other transportation options include renting a car or taking a taxi from Martha’s Vineyard Airport or port.

How to Get Around in Aquinnah

In Aquinnah, visitors can rent bicycles to explore the town and its surrounding areas. Taxis are also available for hire. Alternatively, visitors can opt to rent a car for more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Aquinnah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Martha’s Vineyard:

Visitors can explore other towns and beaches on Martha’s Vineyard, such as Edgartown, Oak Bluffs, and Vineyard Haven.

Cape Cod:

A popular destination for beachgoers, Cape Cod is located just a short ferry ride away from Martha’s Vineyard and offers numerous attractions including lighthouses, museums, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Aquinnah is a charming coastal town with unique cultural and natural attractions that are sure to appeal to visitors. From its fascinating museums and historic sites to its scenic hiking trails and beautiful beaches, there’s something for everyone in this little town. Visitors will also enjoy the local food scene, unique shopping opportunities and its proximity to other popular destinations like Martha’s Vineyard and Cape Cod.
