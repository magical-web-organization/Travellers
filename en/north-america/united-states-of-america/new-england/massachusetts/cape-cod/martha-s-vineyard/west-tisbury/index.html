Introduction to West Tisbury

West Tisbury is a charming town located in the western part of Martha’s Vineyard, the idyllic island in Cape Cod, Massachusetts. With a population of just over 2,500, West Tisbury is the perfect destination for travelers seeking tranquility, natural beauty, and a rich cultural heritage. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll help you plan your visit to West Tisbury, highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in this picturesque New England town.

List of Places to Visit in West Tisbury

West Tisbury offers a diverse range of attractions, from cultural landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Martha’s Vineyard Museum: The museum showcases the history, art, and culture of Martha’s Vineyard, with a collection of over 30,000 objects, including photographs, paintings, and artifacts.
  • Alley’s General Store: Established in 1858, Alley’s General Store is a living museum that preserves the charm and nostalgia of the bygone era. You’ll find everything here from penny candy to hardware.
  • West Tisbury Library: The library houses a vast collection of books, including a special children’s section, and regularly hosts events and workshops.

Natural Attractions of West Tisbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Long Point Wildlife Refuge: The refuge is a paradise for nature lovers, with pristine beaches, hiking trails, and spectacular views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Sepiessa Point Reservation: This conservation area features serene walking trails, a freshwater pond, and abundant wildlife.
  • Iroquois Beach: This quiet beach is a great spot to relax and enjoy stunning ocean vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Tisbury

West Tisbury is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of scenic trails and paths. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Menemsha Hills Reservation: This 211-acre reserve offers hiking trails that lead to stunning views of the Vineyard Sound and the Elizabeth Islands.
  • Gilbert Park: This park has a network of trails perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring the beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Lambert’s Cove Beach: The beach is surrounded by a wooded area, and you can follow the trail through the forest to reach the beach.

Best Things to Do in West Tisbury: Activities and Tours

From kayaking to wine tasting, there’s no shortage of fun things to do in West Tisbury. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Kayak MV: Take a kayak tour of the scenic Tisbury Great Pond and explore the rich biodiversity of the ecosystem.
  • Martha’s Vineyard Wine Trail: Embark on a self-guided tour of the island’s wineries and taste local wines with breathtaking views.
  • Farmers’ Market: The West Tisbury Farmers’ Market is open every Wednesday and Saturday from June to October and features local produce, artisanal foods, and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Tisbury

If you’re planning a week-long trip to West Tisbury, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Explore West Tisbury village and visit Alley’s General Store, West Tisbury Library, and Farmers’ Market
2 Hike the trails at Menemsha Hills Reservation and enjoy the views
3 Rent a bike and ride the scenic trails at Gilbert Park
4 Take a kayak tour of Tisbury Great Pond with Kayak MV
5 Visit the Martha’s Vineyard Museum and learn about the island’s history
6 Relax on the beaches of Iroquois Beach and Lambert’s Cove Beach
7 Embark on a wine-tasting tour of the Martha’s Vineyard Wine Trail

Food and Drink Scene of West Tisbury

West Tisbury boasts a vibrant culinary scene, with a range of options for foodies. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll: This classic New England dish is a must-try, and West Tisbury has some of the freshest and most delicious lobster rolls around.
  • Fried clams: Another New England classic, fried clams are crispy and succulent, and you’ll find them at most seafood restaurants in West Tisbury.

Local Beverages

  • Island Brewery beer: The local brewery produces a range of artisanal beers, each with its unique flavor and character.
  • Island-made wines: Vineyards dot the island, and many produce award-winning wines that reflect the island’s unique terroir.

Where to Stay in West Tisbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Tisbury offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Lambert’s Cove Inn and Restaurant: This charming inn features stylish rooms, an award-winning restaurant, and a serene setting surrounded by nature.
  • The Beach Plum Inn: The inn offers luxurious cabins with breathtaking views of the sea, as well as a farm-to-table restaurant that celebrates local cuisine.
  • The Outermost Inn: This inn is located on a bluff overlooking the Atlantic Ocean and offers elegant rooms, as well as a gourmet restaurant serving locally sourced fare.

What to Buy in West Tisbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

West Tisbury offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make perfect mementos of your visit. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Handmade soaps: Several vendors at the Farmers’ Market offer handmade soaps, infused with herbs and flowers from the island.
  • Vintage postcards: Alley’s General Store has a collection of vintage postcards that depict the island’s history and charm.
  • Local jams and jellies: Many farmers and producers offer homemade jams and jellies made with local fruits and berries.

Local Culture and Customs of West Tisbury

West Tisbury has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its Native American, African American, and European American communities. The town is known for its vibrant arts scene, with many artists and writers calling it their home. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions and to engage with the community in a respectful and meaningful way.

Best Time to Visit West Tisbury

The best time to visit West Tisbury is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and trails are open. The fall is also a good time to visit, with cooler temperatures and colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Tisbury

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife, and avoid littering or damaging the trails and beaches.
  • Engage with the local community and support local businesses.
  • Take the time to explore the island’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Take anything from the beaches or natural reserves, as it’s illegal and harms the ecosystem.
  • Ignore safety rules when hiking or biking, and always check the weather and trail conditions.
  • Be disrespectful to the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Tisbury

West Tisbury is a safe and friendly town, with low crime rates and a close-knit community. However, visitors should always take the necessary precautions and avoid leaving valuables unattended or walking alone at night.

Health in West Tisbury

West Tisbury offers excellent medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication and to have adequate travel health insurance.

Getting to West Tisbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

West Tisbury is located on Martha’s Vineyard and can be reached by ferry, plane, or car.

Airports Near West Tisbury

The nearest airports to West Tisbury are Martha’s Vineyard Airport and Boston Logan International Airport, which offer domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near West Tisbury

The Vineyard Transit Authority operates buses that connect West Tisbury to the rest of the island and the mainland.

Other Options

There are also several ferry services that operate between Martha’s Vineyard and Woods Hole, Falmouth, and New Bedford.

How to Get Around in West Tisbury

West Tisbury is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, bike, or car. Rental cars, bikes, and mopeds are available on the island, and several taxi and shuttle services operate between the town and other destinations.

Where to Go Next from West Tisbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West Tisbury is a great base for exploring the rest of Martha’s Vineyard and nearby destinations. Some of the popular excursions include:

  • Oak Bluffs: This vibrant town is known for its colorful architecture, historic carousel, and bustling harbor.
  • Aquinnah: This coastal town is home to the majestic Aquinnah Cliffs and the Gay Head Lighthouse.
  • Nantucket: This island is a popular destination for its quaint charm, picturesque beaches, and historic sites.

Conclusion

West Tisbury is a beautiful and peaceful town that offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and heritage. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has provided you with valuable information and inspiration for your visit to this New England gem. So put on your walking shoes, pack your sense of adventure, and get ready to explore the rich sights, sounds, and flavors of West Tisbury.
