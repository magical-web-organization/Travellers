Introduction to Chilmark

Chilmark is a charming town located in Martha’s Vineyard, an island situated off the coast of Cape Cod, Massachusetts. Known for its pristine beaches, rolling hills, and charming countryside, Chilmark is the perfect escape for travelers looking to relax and immerse themselves in nature. This town is famous for being a favorite destination for intellectuals and artists, and you will find several art galleries and bookstores here.

List of Places to Visit in Chilmark

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chilmark has a rich history that is reflected in its many museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit places in Chilmark include:

  • The Chilmark Historical Society: This organization preserves and promotes the history of Chilmark through exhibits, events, and educational programs.
  • The Martha’s Vineyard Museum: Housed in a 19th-century building, this museum showcases the history of Martha’s Vineyard through a collection of artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits.
  • The Martha’s Vineyard Hebrew Center: This synagogue is considered to be one of the most beautiful buildings on the island, with its striking architecture and incredible artwork.

Natural Attractions of Chilmark: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chilmark has no shortage of natural wonders to explore. From beaches and nature trails to scenic views and wildlife sanctuaries, this town has something for everyone. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Chilmark include:

  • Menemsha Beach: This stunning beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. You can also enjoy stunning views of the sunset from here.
  • Lucy Vincent Beach: This beach boasts rocky cliffs, soft sand, and crystal clear water, making it one of the most picturesque beaches in the area.
  • Waskosim’s Rock Reservation: This nature reserve features trails that meander through lush green forests, rocky outcroppings, and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Chilmark Pond Preserve: This wildlife sanctuary is a haven for birdwatchers, with over 100 species of birds calling it home.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chilmark

Chilmark has plenty of routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Menemsha Hills Reservation: This hiking trail offers stunning views of the Elizabeth Islands and Martha’s Vineyard Sound. On a clear day, you can see all the way to Noman’s Land, a small island located south of Martha’s Vineyard.
  • The Sepiessa Point Reservation Trail: This relatively easy hiking trail takes you through a beautiful oak forest and offers stunning views of the sparkling blue water of the Tisbury Great Pond.
  • The Vineyard Sound Loop: This biking route takes you through the towns of Chilmark, Aquinnah, and West Tisbury, showcasing the stunning natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Chilmark: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking to unwind or have an adventure, Chilmark has something for everyone. Some of the best things to do in Chilmark include:

  • Fishing: Chilmark is home to some of the best fishing spots on Martha’s Vineyard. You can rent a boat or go on a fishing charter to catch bluefish, striped bass, and more.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: The calm waters of Chilmark’s ponds make for great kayaking and canoeing opportunities. You can rent a kayak or canoe from a local outfitter and explore the region’s waterways.
  • Farm Tours: Chilmark is known for its many farms, and you can go on a tour to learn about the region’s agricultural practices and sample fresh produce.
  • Art Galleries: Chilmark is home to several art galleries that showcase local and international artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chilmark

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Chilmark, consider the following itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the historic Chilmark town center, including the Chilmark General Store, Chilmark Community Church, and the Chilmark Historical Society.

Day 2: Visit Lucy Vincent Beach and enjoy a leisurely picnic. In the evening, head to Menemsha Beach to watch the sunset.

Day 3: Take a hike on the Menemsha Hills Reservation Trail and soak in the stunning views. In the evening, enjoy dinner at one of the many seafood restaurants in the area.

Day 4: Visit the Martha’s Vineyard Museum and the Martha’s Vineyard Hebrew Center. In the evening, explore one of Chilmark’s art galleries.

Day 5: Go fishing or take a fishing charter to catch some of the region’s famous fish. In the evening, enjoy a cocktail at the Beach Plum Inn, which offers stunning views of the ocean.

Day 6: Take a farm tour and sample fresh produce from the region’s many farms. In the evening, enjoy a concert at the Chilmark Community Center.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy your last day in Chilmark. Take a scenic drive or hike to soak in all the natural beauty of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Chilmark

Must-Try Dishes

Chilmark is famous for its fresh seafood, but it also offers a variety of other delicious dishes. Some of the must-try dishes in Chilmark include:

  • Lobster Roll: This classic New England dish is a must-try in Chilmark. You can find it at many of the seafood restaurants in the area.
  • Kale Salad: Chilmark is known for its farm-to-table cuisine, and kale salad is a popular dish made using fresh and locally sourced ingredients.
  • Bluefish: This flavorful fish is a staple in Chilmark and is often served grilled or smoked.

Local Beverages

Chilmark is home to several local breweries and wineries. Some of the must-try beverages in Chilmark include:

  • Bad Martha Farmer’s Brewery: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers, including its flagship Martha’s Vineyard Ale.
  • Chilmark Chocolates and Vineyard Sweets: These local chocolatiers offer a range of delicious chocolates and sweets that pair perfectly with a glass of wine.

Where to Stay in Chilmark: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chilmark has a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Chilmark:

  • Beach Plum Inn: This luxury inn offers stunning ocean views and is located within walking distance of Menemsha Beach.
  • The Hob Knob Inn: This elegant hotel is housed in a 19th-century Victorian mansion and boasts beautiful gardens and unparalleled hospitality.
  • The Shiverick Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Chilmark and is perfect for travelers looking for a cozy and comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Chilmark: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chilmark is famous for its artisanal products and locally sourced goods. Some of the must-buy items in Chilmark include:

  • Chilmark Chocolates and Vineyard Sweets: These local chocolatiers offer a range of delicious chocolates and sweets that make for great souvenirs.
  • Handmade Soaps and Skincare Products: Chilmark is known for its natural skincare products made using ingredients sourced from the island’s farms.
  • Handmade Pottery and Ceramics: Chilmark is home to several local potters and ceramicists, and their work can be found in many of the region’s art galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Chilmark

Chilmark is a charming town with a unique culture and heritage. Here are some customs and traditions you should know before visiting:

  • Respect for the Environment: Chilmark is known for its pristine natural beauty, and visitors are expected to respect the environment and refrain from littering or damaging the flora and fauna.
  • Slow Pace of Life: Chilmark is a small town that moves at a slow pace. Visitors are encouraged to relax and soak in the stunning natural beauty while enjoying leisurely walks or hikes.
  • Community Involvement: Community events and programs play an important role in Chilmark’s culture, and visitors are welcome to participate in these events.

Best Time to Visit Chilmark

The best time to visit Chilmark is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are bustling with activity. However, Chilmark is also beautiful during the fall when the leaves change colors, and the vineyards are yielding their harvest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chilmark

Do:

  • Respect the environment and local customs.
  • Try the fresh seafood and locally sourced produce.
  • Take a hike or bike ride to explore the stunning natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be loud, rude, or disrespectful towards locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chilmark

Chilmark is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take normal precautions to protect their personal belongings and avoid walking alone in isolated areas, particularly at night.

Health in Chilmark

Chilmark is a safe and healthy destination. However, visitors should be aware of tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease, which are common in the area. Make sure to wear appropriate clothing and insect repellent when hiking or spending time in wooded areas.

Getting to Chilmark: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chilmark is located on Martha’s Vineyard, which is accessible via ferry or plane from the mainland.

Airports Near Chilmark

The nearest airports to Chilmark are the Martha’s Vineyard Airport and the Boston Logan International Airport. Visitors can fly into Boston and take a connecting flight to Martha’s Vineyard or take a ferry from Woods Hole to Martha’s Vineyard.

Bus Stations Near Chilmark

There are several bus stations in the region, but visitors are advised to rent a car to explore the area fully.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private shuttle or taxi from the airport to Chilmark or rent a bike or kayak to explore the town and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Chilmark

The best way to get around Chilmark is to rent a car, bike, or kayak. Visitors can also use the public bus system or take a taxi or shuttle to get around the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Chilmark: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chilmark is surrounded by several other charming towns, making it an excellent starting point for further excursions.

  • Aquinnah: This coastal town is located just a few miles from Chilmark and is famous for its stunning red cliffs and lighthouse.
  • Edgartown: This picturesque town is known for its historic architecture, museums, and art galleries.
  • Oak Bluffs: This vibrant town is famous for its gingerbread cottages, beaches, and lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Chilmark is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and history. With a rich tradition of agriculture, fishing, and art, this town is the perfect destination for travelers looking to unwind and immerse themselves in a slower pace of life.
