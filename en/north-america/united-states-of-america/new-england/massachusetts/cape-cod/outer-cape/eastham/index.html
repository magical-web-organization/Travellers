Introduction to Eastham

Nestled in the Outer Cape region of Cape Cod, Massachusetts, Eastham is a charming town on the Atlantic Ocean with a rich cultural and historical heritage. Visitors to Eastham have a lot to explore, from natural wonders and scenic routes to cultural attractions and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Eastham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Swift-Daley House Museum: Visitors can explore the history and traditions of Eastham’s residents, with a collection of historical artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the story of the town’s past.
  • Cape Cod National Seashore Visitor Center: Learn about the ecology and natural history of the Cape Cod region, and pick up maps of the area’s hiking and biking trails.
  • Nauset Lighthouse and Coast Guard Station: A well-known landmark located in the Cape Cod National Seashore, the Nauset Lighthouse dates back to 1877, and the station offers a glimpse into the Eastham’s maritime history.
  • Salt Pond Visitor Center: Exhibits and films showcase the natural and cultural history of the region, with interactive displays for visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Eastham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • First Encounter Beach: The site of the first meeting between Native Americans and the Pilgrims in 1620, this picturesque beach is also popular for its dunes and surfing.
  • Cape Cod Rail Trail: This 22-mile paved trail passes through Eastham and offers a scenic route for hikers and bikers, with several points of interest, including Nickerson State Park.
  • Fort Hill: Hikers can explore scenic trails that offer stunning views of the ocean, the Salt Pond, and the surrounding marshlands, with opportunities for birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastham

Eastham is home to several scenic routes for hiking and biking, including:

  • Nauset Marsh Trail: This easy 1.5-mile trail follows a boardwalk through salt marshes, offering beautiful views of the Nauset Marsh and Atlantic Ocean.
  • Red Maple Swamp Trail: A 1.5-mile loop trail through a dense red maple swamp, this route offers a look at the diverse wildlife and fauna of the region.
  • Fort Hill Trail: A 1.5-mile loop trail that climbs up to a scenic overlook with views of both the ocean and the salt pond.

Best Things to Do in Eastham: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Rent a kayak or paddleboard for a peaceful tour of the oak and pine-lined streams of the Cape Cod National Seashore, or explore the tidal pools and estuaries of the region.
  • Fishing Trips: Charter a boat for deep-sea fishing, or cast your line in the salt ponds or on the jetties for a chance to catch some of the Atlantic Ocean’s finest fish.
  • Whale-Watching Tours: Experience the majesty of the ocean’s largest mammals on a whale-watching tour from nearby Provincetown.
  • Scenic Drives: Take a scenic drive along the Cape Cod National Seashore for beautiful views of the Atlantic Ocean, the dunes, and the marshlands.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastham

Day 1: Explore the Cape Cod National Seashore Visitor Center, hike the Nauset Marsh Trail, and end the day with a sunset on First Encounter Beach.

Day 2: Rent a bike and explore the Cape Cod Rail Trail, stopping at Nickerson State Park for a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Take a whale-watching tour from Provincetown, and finish the day with a seafood dinner in Eastham.

Day 4: Visit the Salt Pond Visitor Center, then go kayaking or paddleboarding on the Nauset Marsh, or fishing on the jetties.

Day 5: Hike the Fort Hill Trail and tour the Swift-Daley House Museum to learn more about Eastham’s history and traditions.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive along the Cape Cod National Seashore, stopping at the Nauset Lighthouse and Coast Guard Station for some photo opportunities.

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day on First Encounter Beach, swimming in the Atlantic Ocean or surfing the waves.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A quintessential East Coast dish, try it at Arnold’s Lobster & Clam Bar with a side of clam chowder.
  • Wellfleet Oysters: Freshly shucked at area restaurants like Mac’s Seafood and taken straight from the bay.
  • Cape Cod Potato Chips: sample Cape Cod Potato Chips.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Cod Beer: Enjoy a locally brewed beer at Cape Cod Beer Company, which offers a range of seasonal flavors.
  • Wine: Eastham has several vineyards where visitors can sample wine, including Truro Vineyards and First Crush Winery.

Where to Stay in Eastham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Penny House Inn and Spa: This charming bed and breakfast offers upscale rooms and suites with fireplaces and private balconies, as well as a full-service spa.
  • Cove Bluffs Motel: This family-friendly motel offers spacious rooms and suites with easy access to the Cape Cod Rail Trail.
  • HI-Eastham Hostel: This hostel offers affordable dorm rooms, private rooms, and camping facilities.

What to Buy in Eastham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sea Glass and Shells: Collect some from the beaches of Eastham to create a unique souvenir.
  • Artwork: Visit the numerous local galleries and artist studios for a unique piece of artwork.
  • Cape Cod Potato Chips: The delicious chips can be found in the snack aisles of local grocery stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastham

Eastham has a rich history and tradition, from its settlement in the 1600s to its vibrant fishing and maritime industry in the 19th and 20th centuries. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s heritage and culture, including its conservation efforts to protect its delicate ecosystem and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Eastham

The best time to visit Eastham is during the summer months when the climate is warmer and there are plenty of outdoor activities available. The shoulder seasons of spring and fall offer quieter days, cooler temperatures, and colorful foliage. Winter is best avoided due to lower temperatures and fewer activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastham

Do:

  • Respect the natural and cultural heritage of the region
  • Dress in layers for changing weather conditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm wildlife
  • Step off marked trails or disturb protected areas
  • Swim in unguarded areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastham

Eastham is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should still practice general safety precautions, such as locking up valuables and being aware of their surroundings when walking or biking at night.

Health in Eastham

Visitors to Eastham should be aware of the risks of sunburn and heat exhaustion during the summer months, and take precautions such as wearing sunscreen and drinking plenty of water. The town has several medical facilities, including the Outer Cape Health Services and Cape Cod Hospital in nearby Hyannis.

Getting to Eastham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Access to Eastham is available through several transportation options:

Airports Near Eastham

  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS): Approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes by car from Eastham.
  • Providence T.F. Green International Airport (PVD): Approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes by car from Eastham.

Bus Stations Near Eastham

  • Hyannis Transportation Center: Served by several bus lines, including Peter Pan and Plymouth & Brockton, approximately 30 minutes from Eastham.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies are located at both Boston Logan International Airport and Providence T.F. Green International Airport.
  • Uber/Lyft: Ride-hailing services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Eastham

Getting around Eastham is best done by car, as the region is spread out and many of the top attractions are outside of the town center. Biking along the Cape Cod Rail Trail is also an option, as well as using ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Eastham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Provincetown: A vibrant and eclectic town located at the tip of Cape Cod, known for its colorful architecture, art galleries, and entertainment scene. Approximately a 35-minute drive from Eastham.
  • Chatham: A quaint and picturesque town with beaches, historic lighthouses, and world-class cuisine. Approximately a 25-minute drive from Eastham.
  • Boston: A bustling metropolis with a rich history, cultural attractions, and an impressive food and drink scene. Approximately a 2-hour drive from Eastham.

Conclusion

Eastham is a beautiful destination with much to offer visitors, from its rich history and cultural attractions to its natural wonders and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide in hand, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to this Cape Cod gem.
