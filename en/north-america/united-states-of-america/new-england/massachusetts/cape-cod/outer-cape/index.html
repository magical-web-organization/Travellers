Introduction to Outer Cape

Nestled in the Northeastern region of the United States of America, the Outer Cape of Cape Cod, Massachusetts, is an outstanding vacation destination for those seeking relaxation, adventure, and an authentic seaside experience. The Cape Cod National Seashore on the Atlantic coast spans over 40,000 acres and offers visitors an idyllic combination of sun-kissed beaches, striking sand dunes, and verdant flora, as well as opportunities for hiking, swimming, and bird-watching, amongst others.

List of Places to Visit in Outer Cape

Here’s a list of the top attractions to explore in Outer Cape:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pilgrim Monument and Provincetown Museum: This 252-foot-tall granite tower commemorates the first landing of the Pilgrims on American soil and gives visitors amazing views of the coast.
  • The Whydah Pirate Museum: Dive into the world of pirates as you learn the fascinating history of the world’s only pirate treasure, the Whydah Gally.
  • Eastham Windmill: Explore one of the most scenic windmills in Cape Cod, a classic 1680 saltbox-style building that features original millstones and an interesting museum.

Natural Attractions of Outer Cape: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Cod National Seashore: Comprising of more than 40,000 acres of beaches, dunes, forests, and ponds, Cape Cod National Seashore offers hikers, swimmers, and bird-watchers with an abundance of opportunities to explore nature.
  • Marconi Beach: Experienced surfers will love this beach with its strong waves and spectacular views. Besides surfing, you can sunbathe, go for a stroll, and experience unparalleled views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Nauset Lighthouse: Perched on the dunes of the Outer Cape, Nauset Lighthouse is the perfect spot for observing the surreal beauty of the sunrise or sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Outer Cape

For nature lovers, Outer Cape offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the US:

  • Province Lands Trail: A scenic 5.25-mile looped trail that takes you through Outer Cape’s diverse landscapes of sand dunes, swamplands, and forests.
  • Nauset Marsh Trail: A picturesque 2.5-mile trail that overlooks Nauset Marsh and the Atlantic Ocean. Highly recommended for birdwatchers.
  • Cape Cod Rail Trail: Scoot, cycle, or skate along the 22-mile paved trail that stretches from Dennis to Wellfleet. The route takes you through several Cape Cod towns, state parks, and nature reserves.

Best Things to Do in Outer Cape: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling solo or as a group, Outer Cape offers activities and tours for everyone:

  • Whale Watching: With over 12 different species of whales visiting the region annually, Outer Cape is a prime location to view these stunning creatures in their natural habitat.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore miles of intricate waterways, crystal-clear ponds, and beautiful beaches. Take a scenic tour with a group or explore it solo.
  • Sunset Sail: Relax and enjoy a picturesque sunset sail in a traditional wooden sailboat. Watch the sun paint the sky with various colors and reflect on the glass-like water before your eyes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Outer Cape

We recommend the following itinerary to explore Outer Cape in a week:

Food and Drink Scene of Outer Cape

Outer Cape’s food and drink scene offers a blend of traditional flavors and modern cuisine with a coastal twist.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic Outer Cape dish, rich with fresh lobster served on a buttered and toasted Massachusetts-style hotdog roll.
  • Blueberry Pancakes: Try some of the best blueberry pancakes for breakfast, made with fresh blueberries grown locally.
  • Clam Chowder: A piping hot bowl of clam chowder, chock-a-block with chunky potatoes, onions, and juicy clams that is perfect any time of the year.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Cod Beer: A smooth and refreshing craft beer made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Cape Cod Organic Cranberry Juice: Enjoy the goodness of tart cranberries in this healthy beverage.

Where to Stay in Outer Cape: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious beachfront resorts to budget-friendly hostels, Outer Cape offers a wide array of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences.

  • Chatham Bars Inn: A luxurious resort experience with a private beach and excellent dining options.
  • Crowne Pointe Historic Inn and Spa: A boutique hotel in Provincetown that offers modern amenities and a soothing atmosphere.
  • HI Truro Hostel: A budget-friendly option with a cozy environment, located within walking distance of Cape Cod National Seashore.

What to Buy in Outer Cape: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Nantucket Basket: Take home an authentic handmade basket crafted by skilled artisans using traditional tools and techniques.
  • Miniature Lighthouse: A miniature replica of the Nauset Lighthouse to decorate your bookshelf or display cabinet.
  • Cape Cod Potato Chips: Try the crunchy and utterly satisfying potato chips made from fresh Cape Cod potatoes.

Local Culture and Customs of Outer Cape

The Outer Cape culture is strongly influenced by its maritime roots. Here are a few quintessential customs and norms:

  • Respect the local fishing and farming industries: Outer Cape is home to several fishing and farming communities, and it is essential to be respectful of their traditions and livelihoods.
  • Give way to walkers and runners: When biking or driving, give way to pedestrians and runners, especially on small local roads.
  • Be mindful of the beaches: Protect the shoreline by following the beach’s rules, staying off the dunes, and collecting your trash.

Best Time to Visit Outer Cape

The peak season is from June to September when the weather is warm, and there are ample opportunities for outdoor activities. However, if you prefer fewer crowds and pleasant weather, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of May and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Outer Cape

Do:

  • Explore the beaches and nature reserves: Outer Cape is renowned for its pristine beaches, dunes, and nature reserves. Spend ample time exploring these landscapes and connecting with nature.
  • Try the seafood: Outer Cape offers some of the freshest and most delicious seafood dishes. Do try the lobster rolls, clam cakes, and other local dishes.
  • Pack appropriate clothing: The weather can be unpredictable in Outer Cape, and packing appropriate clothing is essential. Bring comfortable hiking shoes, hats, sunscreen, and bug sprays.

Don’t:

  • Plan too much: Avoid cramming up your itinerary with too many activities. Instead, take your time to explore the area at a slower pace and appreciate its beauty.
  • Go off the beaten track alone: While exploring, it is recommended to stick to the well-traveled paths and avoid venturing out alone in remote areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Outer Cape

  • Be mindful of rip currents: Stay safe in the water by being aware of the tide changes and avoid going into the water during high tides or rip currents.
  • Follow traffic rules: Pay attention to the local traffic rules when biking or driving, wear helmets, and stay visible on the road.

Health in Outer Cape

  • Lyme Disease: Tick bites are common in Outer Cape, and the best way to avoid getting infected with Lyme disease is to wear protective clothing and use tick repellents when venturing out in the wild.
  • Sun Protection: The sun can be fierce in Cape Cod; stay safe by wearing sunscreen and a hat.

Getting to Outer Cape: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Outer Cape

  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS): This international airport is about 2 hours away from Outer Cape by car.

Bus Stations Near Outer Cape

  • Plymouth/Brockton Street Railway Company: This bus company connects Boston’s South Station with locations within Cape Cod.

Other Options

  • Hy-Line Cruises: Enjoy a scenic ferry ride and reach the ports of Provincetown, Martha’s Vineyard, and Nantucket.
  • CapeFLYER: Travel to Cape Cod by train through CapeFLYER, which runs a seasonal train service from Boston’s South Station.

How to Get Around in Outer Cape

The best way to explore Outer Cape is by car, bike, or bus. Car rental services are available at Boston Logan International Airport or in most hotel lobbies. Local buses are also an affordable option and traverse through many Cape Cod towns.

Where to Go Next from Outer Cape: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plymouth: A historic town that has a deep connection to the Pilgrims and the Mayflower.
  • Boston: A vibrant and cosmopolitan city with rich history, stunning architecture, and diverse dining scene.

Conclusion

Outer Cape is a dazzling vacation destination that has much to offer visitors in terms of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and vibrant culture. With a plethora of activities, tours, and attractions to choose from, you are sure to have a memorable vacation experience in this stunning region of New England.
