Introduction to Barnstable

Located in the stunning Cape Cod region of Massachusetts, Barnstable boasts beautiful beaches, serene natural landscapes, and a rich historical and cultural heritage. From the quaint villages of Hyannis and Osterville to the bustling town center of Barnstable Village, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in the local history, explore the great outdoors, or indulge in delicious local cuisine, Barnstable has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Barnstable

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Coast Guard Heritage Museum: Featuring exhibits on the history of the Coast Guard and its search and rescue missions, this museum is a must-visit for any history buff.
  • The Barnstable Historical Society: Discover the town’s rich history through its extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing life in Barnstable over the centuries.
  • The Cahoon Museum of American Art: Showcasing the works of local artists, this museum is a must-visit for art enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Barnstable: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandy Neck Beach: One of the town’s most popular attractions, this beach offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean, miles of sand dunes, and great fishing opportunities.
  • Barnstable Harbor: Take a whale watching tour, go fishing, or simply take in the stunning views of the harbor.
  • Cape Cod Rail Trail: This 22-mile trail winds through the Cape’s scenic landscapes, offering a great way to explore the area on foot or by bike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barnstable

  • Barnstable Great Marsh Trail: Explore the town’s beautiful marshland on this easy, three-mile trail.
  • Bass River Trail: This scenic trail takes you through the heart of Cape Cod, offering stunning views of local wildlife and the surrounding marshes.

Best Things to Do in Barnstable: Activities and Tours

  • Cape Cod Brewery Tour: Take a tour of the town’s famed brewery and sample some of the best local beers.
  • Sailing Excursion: Set sail aboard a historic sailboat and explore the Cape’s beautiful waters.
  • Hyannis Food Tour: Sample the town’s best local cuisine on this guided tour of the village of Hyannis.

Food and Drink Scene of Barnstable

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A local specialty, this delicious sandwich is filled with fresh lobster meat and served with fries.
  • Clam Chowder: Made with fresh local clams, this soup is a staple of New England cuisine.
  • Cape Cod Potato Chips: These crispy, kettle-cooked chips are a favorite snack in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Cod Beer: This locally brewed beer is a must-try for any beer enthusiast.
  • Cape Cod Winery: Sample some of the region’s best wines on a visit to this local winery.

Where to Stay in Barnstable: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Belfry Inn and Bistro: Located in the heart of town, this charming inn offers comfortable rooms and delicious meals.
  • The Seadar Inn: Situated right on the beach, this inn offers stunning ocean views and easy access to the town’s best attractions.
  • The Compass Rose Inn: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of Barnstable Village, offering easy access to the town’s best museums and historical sites.

What to Buy in Barnstable: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cape Cod Jewelry: These beautiful pieces of jewelry are handcrafted by local artisans and make for a great souvenir.
  • Cape Cod Potato Chips: These locally made chips are a favorite snack of the region and make for a great gift.
  • Cape Cod Lavender Farm Products: This local lavender farm sells a variety of handmade products, from soaps to candles, all made with locally grown lavender.

Local Culture and Customs of Barnstable

Barnstable is a small town with a proud maritime heritage. Residents take great pride in their town’s history and culture, and visitors are always welcome to explore and learn more about the area.

Best Time to Visit Barnstable

The best time to visit Barnstable is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the beaches are buzzing with activity. However, the town’s fall foliage is also a popular attraction and the winter months offer the opportunity for snow sports and cozy nights by the fire.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barnstable

Do:

  • Explore the town’s maritime heritage by visiting its many museums and historical sites.
  • Indulge in some of the region’s best local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take advantage of the town’s many outdoor adventures, from hiking to fishing to sailing.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s rich history and cultural heritage, as it’s an important part of the local identity.
  • Leave without trying at least one of the town’s famous lobster rolls or bowls of clam chowder.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barnstable

Barnstable is a safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables in a safe place and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Barnstable

Barnstable has a number of medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors should ensure that they have health insurance that covers them while traveling and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Barnstable: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barnstable

  • Barnstable Municipal Airport: This small airport is located just a few miles from the town center and offers flights to and from Boston and Nantucket.

Bus Stations Near Barnstable

  • Plymouth & Brockton Street Railway Co.: This bus company offers regular service to and from Boston and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Visitors can also take a ferry from Boston or New York City to nearby Provincetown and then drive or take a bus to Barnstable.

How to Get Around in Barnstable

The most convenient way to get around Barnstable is by car, as there is limited public transportation in the town. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the town’s many scenic trails and paths.

Where to Go Next from Barnstable: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Provincetown: This charming seaside town is just a short drive or ferry ride from Barnstable, and offers vibrant nightlife, great food, and unique shopping opportunities.
  • Boston: The historic city of Boston is just a few hours’ drive away and offers world-class museums, arts, and culture.
  • Nantucket: Take a ferry to this scenic island and explore its pristine beaches, charming towns, and beautiful landscapes.

Conclusion

Barnstable offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. From exploring the town’s historical sites to hiking its scenic trails and indulging in delicious local cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in this charming New England town. With its stunning beaches, picturesque harbors, and quaint villages, Barnstable is the perfect destination for anyone looking to experience the beauty and charm of Cape Cod.
