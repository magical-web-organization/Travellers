Introduction to Hudson

Located in MetroWest, Eastern Massachusetts, Hudson is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of history, nature and culture. Known for its beautiful natural attractions, vibrant art scene and delectable food, Hudson is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a break from city life.

List of Places to Visit in Hudson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Assabet Village Co-op Market - a community-owned market that offers fresh local produce, organic foods and natural wellness products.
  2. Hudson History Museum - showcases the town’s rich history with exhibits spanning the Revolutionary War period to the modern era.
  3. The Cellar Contemporary - an art gallery featuring modern, contemporary art by local artists.
  4. Affordable Chic - a vintage shop that offers a wide range of antiques, home decor, jewelry and clothing.

Natural Attractions of Hudson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Assabet River Rail Trail - a 5.6-mile trail that runs alongside the scenic Assabet River and is ideal for hiking, biking and fishing.
  2. Hudson Farmers Market - a lively marketplace that features locally sourced, organic produce, baked goods, cheeses and more.
  3. Assabet Reservoir - a beautiful reservoir that offers boating, hiking and fishing opportunities.
  4. Lake Boon - a popular destination for swimming, boating, water skiing and other water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hudson

  1. Assabet River Rail Trail - a paved biking and hiking route that runs 5.6 miles from downtown Hudson to the South Acton commuter rail station.
  2. Assabet Wildlife Refuge Trail - a hiking trail that meanders through forests, meadows and wetlands, and is a great place for bird watching.
  3. Owls Head Town Forest Trail - a 2.5-mile hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Assabet River and the surrounding wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Hudson: Activities and Tours

  1. Go on a Brewery Tour - visit Hudson’s two breweries, Medusa Brewing Company and Ground Effect Brewing Company, for a fun beer-tasting experience.
  2. Explore Hudson’s Art Scene - visit The Cellar Contemporary and The Horseshoe Studio Gallery for a glimpse into the town’s vibrant art scene.
  3. Go on a Food Tour - Taste the local flavors with the Hudson Food Tour, which takes you on a culinary journey of the town’s best restaurants and cafes.
  4. Attend a Hudson Farmer’s Market - a popular attraction that offers local produce, music and entertainment.

Food and Drink Scene of Hudson

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Clam Chowder - a creamy and hearty soup made with fresh clams and potatoes.
  2. Lobster Roll - a traditional New England dish made of fresh lobster meat served on a buttered roll.
  3. Blueberry Pie - a classic dessert made with fresh blueberries and a buttery pastry crust.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer - Hudson is home to two breweries, Medusa Brewing Company and Ground Effect Brewing Company, where you can sample a wide range of craft beers.
  2. Cider - Try local ciders at Lookout Farm Brewing and Cider Co, located a few miles from Hudson.
  3. Wine - A visit to Nashoba Valley Winery, located nearby to Hudson can be a perfect way to try the local wines.

Where to Stay in Hudson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Marriott Courtyard Boston Marlborough - located a few miles away, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations and an on-site fitness center.
  2. Residence Inn Boston Marlborough - Perfect for family trips, this hotel provides spacious rooms equipped with full kitchens and a breakfast buffet.
  3. Hampton Inn Boston Marlborough - Enjoy a comfortable stay with complimentary breakfast and a 24-hour fitness center.

What to Buy in Hudson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hudson Honey - made with local honeybees, this is a perfect souvenir that reflects the town’s natural beauty.
  2. Assabet Village Co-op Market products - a wide selection of natural wellness products, spices, herbs and artisan food products.
  3. Art by Local Artists - visit The Cellar Contemporary and The Horseshoe Studio Gallery to pick up a unique piece of art.

Local Culture and Customs of Hudson

Hudson is a friendly community, and visitors are encouraged to engage with the locals. The community is passionate about supporting local businesses and promoting sustainable and eco-friendly practices. Holidays and events are celebrated with zest. Be sure to witness the famous “Hudson Days” celebration, usually held in August, showcasing music, food and local art.

Best Time to Visit Hudson

The best time to visit Hudson is from May through October when the weather is warm and festivals and events are in full swing. To avoid crowds and enjoy lower prices, consider visiting during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hudson

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drink scene.
  • Do visit the Hudson History Museum and the art galleries of Hudson.
  • Do engage with the locals and attend community events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter as Hudson promotes cleanliness and littering is a punishable offense.
  • Don’t drive under the influence as in MA, drunk driving laws are strict.
  • Don’t miss out on the breathtaking natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hudson

Hudson is generally a safe place to visit. However, do take precautions to protect your valuables during tours and outdoor excursions. Also, get health and travel insurance before traveling.

Health in Hudson

Healthcare facilities are easily accessible in Hudson. However, visitors should carry necessary medicines and follow COVID safety protocols.

Getting to Hudson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hudson

  • Boston Logan International Airport is approximately 35 miles away from Hudson
  • T.F. Green Airport is approximately 60 miles away from Hudson.

Bus Stations Near Hudson

Greyhound Bus Line and Peter Pan Bus Lines, both offer service from New York, Boston to Hudson.

Other Options

Rental Cars, Rideshare options, and private car services are available as well.

How to Get Around in Hudson

Rental cars, taxi’s and ride-shares are readily available but renting a bike in warmer months is an ideal way to explore Hudson and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Hudson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Boston - Massachusetts’ capital city is a must-visit for history buffs with top attractions, such as the Freedom Trail and Faneuil Hall.
  2. Worcester - Located about 15 miles away, Worcester is a thriving city known for its culture, arts, parks, and recreational activities.
  3. Concord - Just 20 miles away, it is home to several historic sites, including Ralph Waldo Emerson’s house and Louisa May Alcott’s Orchard House.

Conclusion

Visiting Hudson, Massachusetts is an ideal way to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy a perfect blend of history and nature. The town offers something for every kind of traveler, and we hope this guide helps you make the most of your visit to Hudson.
