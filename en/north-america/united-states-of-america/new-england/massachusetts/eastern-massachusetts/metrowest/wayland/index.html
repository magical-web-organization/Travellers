Introduction to Wayland

Located in Eastern Massachusetts, Wayland is a vibrant town that is a great destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and adventurous travelers. This charming New England town is home to many cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures that are just waiting to be explored. Whether you are looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Wayland has something for everyone. In this guide, we will share with you the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Wayland.

List of Places to Visit in Wayland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Wayland Museum & Historical Society: This museum showcases the history of Wayland through exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • The Samuel Haven House: This historical house is a well-preserved example of a mid-19th century farmstead.
  • Stone’s Public House: This historic restaurant/bar was once a popular gathering place for the leaders of the American Revolution, and it still serves great food and drinks to this day.

Natural Attractions of Wayland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wayland Town Beach: This beautiful beach is a perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Bentley Memorial Library: Take a relaxing stroll around the library’s well-tended gardens.
  • Wayland Rail Trail: This trail is great for walking, cycling or just enjoying the views of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wayland

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Wayland offers a variety of trails suited for different skill levels. Below are a few options:

  • Wayland Town Center Route: This easy 1.5 mile route showcases the town’s historic buildings and beautiful neighborhoods.
  • Greenway Trail: This trail is a 4.5-mile, moderately challenging hike that offers some beautiful views of the surrounding nature.
  • Bondar Memorial Park Trail: This trail offers a peaceful hike and takes you through wooded areas and over streams.

Best Things to Do in Wayland: Activities and Tours

  • Art Exhibitions at the Wayland Art Gallery: The gallery features a variety of art exhibitions throughout the year.
  • Wayland Farmers’ Market: This local market offers a great chance to sample local produce and products.
  • Wine Tasting at Wayland Wine and Spirits: This store offers wine tasting events, great selection of wines, and other beverages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wayland

Day Activity
1 Visit the Wayland Museum & Historical Society and learn about the town’s history

Food and Drink Scene of Wayland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: Nothing screams New England more than the creamy clam chowder served at the Stone’s Public House.
  • Lobster Roll: The Wayland Market has fresh seafood and amazing lobster rolls that are a must-try.
  • Pizza: Tenzo Japanese Gastropub has a unique pizza menu that combines Japanese flavors with classic Italian pizza.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Wayland has a robust beer scene with breweries like Jack’s Abby and Lookout Farm which offer locally brewed craft beers.
  • Cocktails: Stone’s Public House has an excellent range of inventive cocktails made with fresh ingredients.
  • Wine: Wayland Wine and Spirits has a great selection of wines from around the world, and often host wine tasting events with local vineyards.

Where to Stay in Wayland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at Hastings Park: This upscale hotel is perfect for those looking for a comfortable stay in Wayland.
  • The Westin Waltham Boston: Located in the nearby Waltham, this hotel offers luxury accommodations and is a great option for those looking for a more urban experience.
  • Airbnb: There are also many affordable Airbnb and guesthouse options available in Wayland.

What to Buy in Wayland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wayland Farmers Market: The farmers market is a great place to buy local produce, fresh baked goods, and homemade crafts made by local artisans.
  • Wayland Wine and Spirits: Bring home a bottle of local wine or craft beer purchased from the Wayland Wine and Spirits store.
  • Gift Shops: Wayland has a few gift shops that offer locally made souvenirs such as Wayland-themed t-shirts, postcards, and keychains.

Local Culture and Customs of Wayland

Wayland is a small town with friendly people, and the community is tight-knit. The town has a deep attachment to its history, and residents are proud and passionate about preserving and celebrating it. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the town’s history and follow local customs.

Best Time to Visit Wayland

The best time to visit Wayland is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and the foliage is stunning. Summer is also a great time to visit for water activities and outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wayland

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings and the community.
  • Try local foods and drinks.
  • Follow hiking and biking regulations and guidelines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural surroundings.
  • Engage in any disrespectful behavior towards the historical sites.
  • Ignore traffic laws; speed in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Wayland is generally a safe town to visit, but visitors should still take precautions and stay aware of their surroundings, especially when outdoors. Visitors should also note that ticks carrying Lyme disease are prevalent in the area, and it is important to take necessary precautions when hiking or spending time outdoors.

How to Get Around in Wayland

The best way to explore Wayland is by car, bike, or on foot. The town is relatively small and easy to navigate. Biking or walking the rail trail is a great way to see the natural beauty of Wayland.

Where to Go Next from Wayland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Boston: A quick 30-minute drive or train ride you can explore the famous city of Boston including the Freedom Trail, museums, and great food.
  • Concord: This historic New England town is located only 10 minutes away from Wayland. Home to some of the most important sites of Revolutionary War history.
  • Worcester: A short drive from Wayland, Worcester has a thriving art scene, excellent restaurants, and beautiful parks.

Conclusion

Wayland is a charming town with its rich history, natural beauty, and scenic trails. It is a great destination for those looking for an active getaway or a peaceful retreat. We hope this guide gives you a better understanding of this lovely town and helps you plan your next trip to Wayland, Massachusetts.
