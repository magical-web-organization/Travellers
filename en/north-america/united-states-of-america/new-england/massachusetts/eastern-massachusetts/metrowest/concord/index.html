Introduction to Concord

Located in MetroWest, Eastern Massachusetts, Concord is a charming town that is full of history, culture, and adventure. This small town is known for its involvement in the Revolutionary War and literary history with famous writers including Henry David Thoreau and Ralph Waldo Emerson. Today, Concord is a popular tourist destination with something to offer to every traveler, whether it’s exploring museums, hiking trails or shopping in boutiques.

List of Places to Visit in Concord

There are plenty of places to visit in Concord, ranging from culturally important attractions to natural wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Concord is home to several museums and historical sites showcasing early American history. The most popular attractions include:

  • Concord Museum: This museum offers an insight into the history of Concord, including fascinating exhibits on Thoreau, Emerson, and the Revolutionary War.
  • The Old Manse: Built-in 1770, the Old Manse is a National Historic Landmark and home to a literary family, including Nathaniel Hawthorne.
  • Louisa May Alcott’s Orchard House: This is a preserved historic house that was once home to the author of Little Women, Louisa May Alcott.
  • Minute Man National Historic Park: This park captures the history of the colonial period leading up to the start of the American Revolution, including Paul Revere’s famous ride.

Natural Attractions of Concord: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Concord offers beautiful natural spaces, including parks, lakes, and hiking trails. Some of the most popular natural wonders in Concord are:

  • Walden Pond State Reservation: This park is renowned for its scenic views, offering crystal-clear water surrounded by trees and a sand beach.
  • Great Meadows National Wildlife Refuge: This is a popular birdwatching spot, offering miles of trails through fields, forests, and wetlands.
  • Canoeing and kayaking on the Concord River: Take in the beautiful views of the river and surroundings while enjoying kayaking or canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Concord

Concord offers several hiking and biking trails through natural landscapes and historic sites. Some popular trails include:

  • Minute Man National Historical Park Trail: This is a 5-mile stretch through various sites related to the Revolutionary War.
  • Sudbury River Trail: This trail takes you through the scenic wetlands and forests along the Sudbury River.
  • Battle Road Trail: This is a 6-mile hiking trail that offers an immersive experience into colonial history.

Best Things to Do in Concord: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Concord. Some popular ones are:

  • Concord River Cruise: Cruise along the Concord River and explore nearby islands.
  • Walking Tours: A guided walking tour is an excellent way to explore the town while learning about its history, culture, and architecture.
  • Dinner at 80 Thoreau: Savor delicious farm-to-table cuisine while taking in the cozy ambiance at 80 Thoreau.

Food and Drink Scene of Concord

The local food and drink scene is another reason to visit Concord. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New England Clam Chowder: A creamy and hearty soup made with clams, potatoes, and cream.
  • Lobster Roll: A classic sandwich made with fresh lobster meat and mayonnaise.
  • Blueberry Muffins: A light and delicious muffin made with fresh blueberries.

Local Beverages

  • Concord Grape Juice: This refreshing drink is made with the local Concord grapes that were first cultivated in the town.
  • Jack’s Abby Craft Lagers: A brewery that offers a wide range of beers, including Haymaker, Framingham Lager, and Hoponious Union.

Where to Stay in Concord: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Concord, ranging from luxurious hotels to affordable guesthouses.

  • Concord’s Colonial Inn: A charming historic inn in a prime location in the town center.
  • Residence Inn Boston Concord: An upscale hotel offering comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • North Bridge Inn: An elegant guesthouse with cozy rooms and delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Concord: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Concord, the birthplace of Henry David Thoreau, Louisa May Alcott, and Ralph Waldo Emerson is known for its literary association, and you can bring some of it home with you. Some popular souvenirs and local products include:

  • Books and Memorabilia: Buy books and other memorabilia from the Concord Bookshop and Orchard House.
  • Concord Grape Products: Get grape juice, jam, and other products made with local Concord grapes from Verrill Farm’s Market.
  • Craft and Art from Locals: Grab some unique and handmade crafts and art pieces from the Emerson Umbrella Center for the Arts.

Local Culture and Customs of Concord

Concord is a small town that has a rich cultural history, and visitors must respect the customs and traditions of the town. Some local customs to keep in mind include staying quiet in public places such as cemeteries, and respecting the locals’ privacy and space.

Best Time to Visit Concord

The best time to visit Concord is during the fall and spring seasons when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. The peak season runs from June to August, and the weather can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Concord

Here are some tips for visitors to stay safe and respectful in Concord:

Do:

  • Visit the historical sites and museums to learn about the town’s history and culture.
  • Take a guided walking tour to explore the town while learning about its history, culture, and architecture.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the town’s monuments, cemeteries, and memorials.
  • Walk in narrow or winding roads without proper attire or light.
  • Disturb the wildlife in natural reserves and parks.

Safety and Health

Concord is a safe town for visitors. However, visitors must be cautious and follow simple safety measures such as locking their car doors and securing their belongings.

Safety in Concord

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Keep your belongings, including your car keys, in a safe and secure place.
  • Follow the local laws and regulations.

Health in Concord

Concord offers excellent medical facilities, including Emerson Hospital and Minute Clinic. Visitors must stay vigilant against any medical emergencies.

Getting to Concord: Transportation Options and Logistics

Concord is easily accessible by road, train, or plane.

Airports Near Concord

The closest international airports to Concord are:

  • Logan International Airport
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Concord

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines
  • Concord Coach Lines

Other Options

  • Driving: Visitors can rent a car from Boston and drive to Concord within an hour.
  • Train: The town is easily accessible by train, with the Concord station located on the Fitchburg Line.

How to Get Around in Concord

Concord is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, visitors can also rent a bike, car, or use public transportation to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Concord: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Concord is surrounded by several exciting destinations and attractions, including:

  • Boston: Boston, the largest city in Massachusetts, is just 20 miles away from Concord.
  • Lexington: Lexington, famous for being the site of the first battle of the American Revolution, is only a 15-minute-drive from Concord.
  • Salem: Salem, also known as the Witch City, is less than an hour’s drive from Concord.

Conclusion

Concord, once home to the literary elite and a critical site in the Revolutionary War, is now a charming spot for tourists. The town provides visitors an insight into America’s past while offering a combination of culture, art, and adventure. With its natural beauty, diverse cuisine, and stunning historical sites, it’s the perfect destination for anyone looking to have a unique cultural experience.
