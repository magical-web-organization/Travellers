Groton is a charming town located in the heart of the Merrimack Valley in Eastern Massachusetts. Nestled in the rolling hills of Massachusetts, it is renowned for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich colonial history. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing country getaway or an exciting outdoor adventure, Groton has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you a rundown of the best places to visit, things to do, and tips to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Groton.

Introduction to Groton

Located just 30 miles northwest of Boston, Groton boasts a rich colonial history that can be seen in its many historic landmarks and museums. The town was first settled by Europeans in the early 17th century and played an important role in the American Revolution. Today, it is a popular tourist destination renowned for its scenic countryside, rolling hills, and charming New England architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Groton

From historic landmarks and museums to scenic hiking trails and wineries, Groton has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained throughout your trip. Here are some of the must-visit spots in Groton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Groton Historical Society Museum: This small museum showcases the town’s rich colonial history and features exhibits on the American Revolution, colonial life, and local artifacts.

  • Whitney Tavern: Built in 1793, this historic tavern was once a popular gathering place for early Americans, including John Adams and John Hancock.

  • Shepley Hill Cemetery: One of the oldest cemeteries in the town, it features graves dating back to the 17th century and offers a glimpse into the town’s colonial history.

Natural Attractions of Groton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Groton Town Forest: Spanning over 1,000 acres, this forest offers miles of hiking trails, scenic vistas, and a tranquil escape from the bustle of the city.

  • Nashua River Rail Trail: This 12-mile trail meanders through the town’s scenic countryside and offers a great opportunity for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

  • Nashoba Valley Winery: A picturesque winery set amidst rolling hills and scenic vistas, it features some of the best locally grown grapes, wine tastings, and tours of the vineyard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Groton

Groton boasts some of the best hiking and biking routes in Eastern Massachusetts, offering a range of scenic trails, quiet country roads, and off-road routes for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Groton:

  • Midstate Trail: This 95-mile hiking trail bisects the town and offers stunning vistas, rocky terrain, and challenging climbs.

  • Groton Town Forest: With over 1,000 acres of forest, this area is a popular destination for hiking, mountain biking, and birdwatching.

  • Nashua River Rail Trail: This 12-mile trail stretches from Ayer to Dunstable, offering scenic views of the Nashua River and a gentle ride for biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Groton: Activities and Tours

From wine tasting to historical tours, Groton offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to explore:

  • Tour historic sites: Groton is home to many historic landmarks, including the 1793 Whitney Tavern, the Major John Buttrick House, and the Groton School.

  • Visit a winery: The Nashoba Valley Winery is a picturesque setting offering tours of the vineyards, tastings, and delicious local cuisine.

  • Experience outdoor adventures: With miles of scenic trails and natural areas, Groton offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploring the outdoors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Groton

If you’re planning to spend a week in Groton, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day Activities
Day 1 Tour the Groton Historical Society Museum, follow it up with a hike in the Groton Town Forest and a wine tasting at the Nashoba Valley Winery.
Day 2 Explore the Nashua River Rail Trail by bike or on foot, take a scenic drive through the countryside and visit the Shepley Hill Cemetery.
Day 3 Visit the Major John Buttrick House and enjoy a leisurely walk through the world-famous Fruitlands Museum in nearby Harvard.
Day 4 Take in the scenic views of the rolling hills of East Boston and enjoy a wine tasting at the Nashoba Valley Winery.
Day 5 Hike the challenging Midstate Trail, take a guided tour of the historic Whitneys Tavern, and catch a show at the Groton Community Theater.
Day 6 Take a scenic drive to the picturesque town of Ayer, and explore the Nashua River Watershed for some incredible opportunities for exploring the outdoors.
Day 7 Relax with a day of sightseeing in Boston, including the famous Freedom Trail and the Museum of Fine Arts.

Food and Drink Scene of Groton

Groton is home to a burgeoning food and drink scene, with a variety of locally-sourced cuisine and beverages on offer. Here are some of the best places to eat and drink in Groton:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: This New England classic is a staple on almost every menu in Groton, and is always served with a side of oyster crackers.

  • Burgers: Groton is home to some of the best burger joints in Eastern Massachusetts, with classic burger bars and gourmet burger joints on every corner.

  • Seafood: Being a coastal town, Groton is known for its fresh seafood offerings. Lobster, crab, and shrimp are particularly tasty in this region.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Nashoba Valley Winery offers some of the best locally-made wine in Eastern Massachusetts.

  • Beer: Nashoba Valley Brewery is one of the best locally made craft breweries in the region, and are soon to have a dedicated tap room of their very own.

  • Cider: Locally bottled ciders are popular in Groton, which are made by pressing fresh apples and fermenting the juice into a tart, refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Groton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or a cozy bed and breakfast, Groton offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are a few of the best places to stay in Groton:

  • The Groton Inn: An elegant, boutique hotel located in the heart of Groton, offering spacious guestrooms and suites overlooking the countryside.

  • The Deerfield Inn: A charming bed and breakfast set in a 150-year-old farmhouse, offering comfortable guestrooms and a hearty home-cooked breakfast.

  • AirBnB: If you prefer a more personal touch, there are many AirBnB listings in Groton, providing an opportunity to experience local life with the help of locals themselves.

What to Buy in Groton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Groton boasts a thriving local arts and crafts scene, with an array of local products on offer, such as:

  • Hand-blown Glass: A variety of beautiful glassworks are available in Groton that showcase the talent of the town’s local artisans.

  • Locally Made Crafts: From handmade wooden boxes to crocheted scarves, Groton is home to dozens of craft stores, selling unique souvenirs made by local artists.

  • Fruit Jams and Preserves: Groton is home to many farms, so locally-made fruit jams and preserves are widely available and make for great gifts to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Groton

Groton is a small, charming town with a rich history and a vibrant local culture. Here are some things to keep in mind about the local culture and customs:

  • Friendliness: Groton is home to friendly locals, who are always willing to lend a hand to visitors.

  • Love for the outdoors: As a town with a lot of natural scenery and attractions, Groton is home to many outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Appreciation for history: Groton has a rich colonial history that is celebrated throughout the town, with many museums, historical landmarks, and events commemorating this.

Best Time to Visit Groton

The best time to visit Groton is during the fall months of September and October when the foliage is at its peak, and the colors of the trees are breathtaking. The weather is mild, and there are many seasonal activities to enjoy, such as apple picking, pumpkin picking, and hayrides. Other good times to visit are in the summer months of June to August when the temperatures are warm and sunny, and the town is alive with events and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Groton

If you’re planning a trip to Groton, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect nature: Groton is home to many natural attractions, so always be respectful of the environment and treat it with care.

  • Tip generously: It is customary to leave a 15% to 20% tip for good service in restaurants and cafes.

  • Engage with locals: Groton is home to many friendly and helpful locals who can offer valuable insights and tips about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Always dispose of your trash responsibly and keep the environment clean.

  • Miss out on historical sites: Groton is home to many historical landmarks and museums, so make sure to take advantage of these.

  • Drive too fast: Rural roads in Groton can be narrow and winding, so always drive carefully and obey traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Groton is a very safe town, with low crime rates, but just like in other towns, taking care of yourselves and being vigilant is always important.

Safety in Groton

  • Weather Hazards: Natural hazards such as snow, ice, and high winds can occur during the fall-to-winter months, so take care when driving, hiking, or biking, particularly along rural or poorly-maintained routes.

  • Wildlife: Be cautious about encountering wildlife such as black bears, coyotes, and skunks. Keep garbage cans tightly sealed, avoid littering, and keep a look out whilst trekking or exploring.

Health in Groton

  • Ticks: Ticks are active throughout the spring-to-summer months, so wear long-sleeved shirts and pants when hiking or biking in wooded areas, and apply insect repellent when necessary.

  • COVID-19: Groton, like most towns in Massachusetts, has mandatory face mask policies in place. Be sure to wipe your hands regularly and get vaccinated if you have not already.

Getting to Groton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Groton is easily accessible from many major cities in the Northeast, including Boston and New York City. Here’s a rundown of the best transportation options to get to Groton:

Airports Near Groton

The nearest major airport is Logan International Airport in Boston (BOS), which is about 45 miles away from Groton.

Bus Stations Near Groton

There are several bus stations near Groton, including the MBTA Bus Station in Ayer and the Peter Pan Bus station in Lowell.

Other Options

If you’re driving from other parts of the Northeast, you can reach Groton via the I-495 or US 3. The town is easily accessible by car, with plenty of free parking available in town.

How to Get Around in Groton

Groton is a small town, and many attractions are easily accessible on foot or bike. If you prefer a car, there are plenty of rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Groton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Groton is located in a beautiful part of Massachusetts, making it an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some nearby attractions worth visiting:

  • Boston: Located just 45 miles away from Groton, Boston is a must-visit destination and accessible via public transportation, and by car if you prefer to drive.

  • Worcester: The second largest city in Massachusetts, Worcester is located just an hour’s drive from Groton.

  • Cape Cod: The beautiful and idyllic Cape Cod is located only two hours drive from Groton and is well worth a visit.

Conclusion

Groton is a hidden gem of Eastern Massachusetts, with a rich colonial history, outdoor adventures, and a beautiful countryside to explore. Whether you’re looking for a quiet country escape or an exciting outdoor adventure, Groton offers something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and discover the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs to experience that will make your trip an unforgettable one!
