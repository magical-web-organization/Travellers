Introduction to Westford

Welcome to Westford, a charming town located in the Merrimack Valley in Eastern Massachusetts. Known for its picturesque landscapes and rich history, Westford is a perfect destination for both adventure enthusiasts and history buffs. With a wide range of attractions, activities and outdoor adventures, Westford has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Westford

Check out our list of must-see destinations in Westford, categorized by culture and nature.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Westford Museum and Historical Society - This museum houses a collection of artifacts and documents dating back to the town’s founding in 1729. Visitors can learn about the town’s agricultural, industrial, and social history through exhibits, video presentations, and contemporary art shows.

  • Nashoba Valley Ski Area - This historic winter resort is located in the heart of Westford. It offers skiing and snowboarding for all skill levels, as well as snow tubing and zip lining.

Natural Attractions of Westford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Forge Pond Park - This 50-acre park offers swimming, fishing, and non-motorized boating. Visitors can also enjoy picnicking and hiking along the park’s trails.

  • Stone Arch Bridge - This 25-foot granite bridge is a highlight of the historic Jamestown Road Trail. Built in 1824, it spans Stony Brook and offers a stunning view of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westford

Westford is home to several hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to difficult. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Nashoba River Rail Trail - This 11-mile trail passes through the towns of Ayer, Groton, Pepperell, and Dunstable. It offers scenic views of the Nashua River and is suitable for walkers, runners, and cyclists of all levels.

  • Boves Conservation Land - This 77-acre park features a 2-mile loop trail that takes hikers through varied terrain. Visitors can enjoy views of the pond and wildlife while walking on the accessible trail.

Best Things to Do in Westford: Activities and Tours

Westford has a plethora of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained:

  • Kimball Farm - This family-fun destination offers mini-golf, bumper boats, batting cages, and other activities. It also features a country store, gift shop and ice cream stand.

  • Westford Farmer’s Market - Held every Tuesday during the summer and fall, this market features locally grown produce, baked goods, artisanal crafts and live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westford

For those planning to visit Westford for a week, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Westford Museum and Historical Society
2 Hike along the Jamestown Road Trail and visit the Stone Arch Bridge
3 Ski at Nashoba Valley Ski Area
4 Go boating or fishing at Forge Pond Park
5 Take a bike ride on the Nashoba River Rail Trail

7 Shop at Westford Farmer’s Market

Food and Drink Scene of Westford

Westford has a variety of dining options, ranging from casual to upscale. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder - A New England classic, this creamy soup is often made with clams, potatoes, and bacon.

  • Lobster Roll - This sandwich is made with chunks of fresh lobster meat, mayonnaise, and celery, served on a grilled bun.

Local Beverages

  • Samuel Adams Boston Lager - This popular beer is brewed in Boston and is known for its rich, full-bodied taste.

  • Cranberry Juice - Massachusetts is the second-largest producer of cranberries in the country, and the juice is a refreshing local favorite.

Where to Stay in Westford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Westford has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hampton Inn and Suites - This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a heated indoor pool.

  • The Inn at Robbins Brook - This boutique hotel offers suites with a fireplace and a kitchenette. Guests can also enjoy a complimentary breakfast and fitness center.

What to Buy in Westford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to buy in Westford:

  • Westford Pottery - Handmade pottery from local artisans.

  • Maple Syrup - Raw and organic maple syrup from local farmers.

Local Culture and Customs of Westford

Westford is a small town with a rich history and strong community values. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs, including the importance of agriculture and outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Westford

The best time to visit Westford is during the fall, when the leaves change color and the weather is mild. The town also hosts several festivals and events during this time, including the Westford Apple Blossom Festival and the Lowell Winterfest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westford

Do:

  • Visit the Westford Museum and Historical Society to learn about the town’s rich history.
  • Explore the area’s hiking and biking trails for a fun and active vacation.
  • Purchase locally made products like pottery and maple syrup.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s natural areas.
  • Ignore the agricultural traditions and significance of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westford

Westford is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should still follow standard safety precautions, such as locking their car and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Westford

There are several medical facilities in Westford, including Emerson Hospital and Circle Health Urgent Care.

Getting to Westford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Westford is easily accessible by car or public transportation:

Airports Near Westford

  • Boston Logan International Airport - This airport is approximately 35 miles east of Westford.

Bus Stations Near Westford

  • Lowell Regional Transit Authority - This bus station is located in nearby Lowell and provides access to Boston and surrounding towns.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals - Several car rental companies are located at Boston Logan International Airport.
  • Taxi/Uber - Taxis and rideshare services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Westford

The best way to get around Westford is by car. The town is spread out, and many attractions are not within walking distance. There are also several car rental companies in the area.

Where to Go Next from Westford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Westford is located near several other popular destinations in Massachusetts, including:

  • Boston - This historic city is located less than an hour’s drive from Westford, and offers shopping, dining, and historical attractions.

  • Salem - This coastal town is known for its witchcraft trials and spooky attractions.

Conclusion

With its rich history, diverse natural beauty, and charming community, Westford is a must-visit destination in Eastern Massachusetts. From hiking and biking trails to locally made crafts, there is something for everyone in this quaint town. Book your trip today and discover all that Westford has to offer.
