Introduction to Ashby

Ashby is a small town situated in Merrimack Valley, in Eastern Massachusetts, in New England, in the United States of America. The town was named after Lord Ashby, a resident of Newbury, and was settled in the mid-17th century. Today, Ashby is a charming town with a population of around 3,000, and is an excellent destination for those seeking nature and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Ashby

Ashby may be small in size but it boasts a variety of attractions that visitors will surely enjoy. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Ashby:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ashby Free Public Library - This public library boasts a great selection of books, periodicals, and other resources for visitors who want to learn more about the town and its history.

  • Officer Charles K. Martin Memorial Park - This park is a serene and peaceful place that honors the memory of police officer Charles K. Martin, a resident of Ashby.

  • Museum of Natural History - This museum showcases the natural wonders of Ashby and its surrounding areas. Visitors can see a variety of exhibits showcasing the flora and fauna of the region and learn about the geological and ecological history of the area.

  • Ashby Historical Society and Museum - This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the town’s rich history. Visitors can learn about the town’s development, its people and key events that have shaped it over the years.

Natural Attractions of Ashby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Watatic Mountain State Wildlife Area - This wildlife area features a variety of trails and hikes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness and the nearby Mount Monadnock.

  • Mount Watatic - This mountain offers excellent hiking and walking routes that lead to the summit, where visitors can enjoy amazing panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  • Ashby State Forest - This forest features several hiking and biking trails that offer opportunities to explore the wilderness in all its natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashby

Ashby offers great hiking and biking routes that are ideal for adventure enthusiasts. Here are a few of Ashby’s top trails:

  • Mount Watatic Trail - This trail follows the ridgeline of Mount Watatic and offers beautiful views of the rolling hills and valleys of the surrounding area.

  • The Ashby Rail Trail - This multi-use trail follows the former rail bed of the Boston and Maine Railroad, and offers great views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Watatic Mountain Loop - This loop trail offers amazing views of Mount Monadnock and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Ashby: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking routes, Ashby offers a variety of other things to do for visitors. Here are some of the top activities and tours to enjoy in Ashby:

  • Visit the Ashby State Forest - This forest offers visitors a chance to explore the wilderness in all its natural glory, whether it’s hiking, camping, or picnicking.

  • Horseback Riding - Ashby boasts several stables that offer guided horseback rides through the countryside.

  • Fishing and Boating - The town’s pristine lakes, ponds, and rivers offer plenty of opportunities for fishing and boating.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashby

Even though Ashby is a small town, it has a vibrant food and drink scene that is worth exploring. Here are some of the top places to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ashby Ale House - This pub serves classic pub food, such as burgers, sandwiches, and fish and chips, that are a must-try.

  • Thurston’s Cafe - This cafe serves classic breakfast and lunch dishes, such as omelets, sandwiches, and salads, using locally-sourced ingredients.

  • The Ashby Grille and Cafe - This cafe serves homemade comfort food such as pizza, burgers, and fries, that you won’t want to miss.

Local Beverages

  • Nashoba Valley Winery - This winery is located just outside of Ashby and specializes in local wines, spirits, and ciders.

  • The Gardner Ale House - This pub serves locally-brewed craft beer that’s sure to please beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Ashby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ashby may be small, but it offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, including:

  • Ashby Bed and Breakfast - A quaint bed and breakfast located in the heart of Ashby that offers comfortable rooms and a delicious homemade breakfast.

  • Ashby Aspetuck Lodge - A modest lodge situated in the Ashby State Forest that offers visitors the chance to stay in the middle of the wilderness.

  • Chocksett Inn and Restaurant - This inn is located in nearby Sterling and offers charming rooms and a highly-rated restaurant.

What to Buy in Ashby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here’s a list of the top places to shop:

  • The Ashby Farmers Market - This market offers locally-grown produce, meats, and artisanal cheeses.

  • The Flower Hive - This florist offers beautiful floral arrangements made with locally-grown flowers.

  • The Ashby Pumpkin and Apple Farm - This farm offers visitors the chance to pick their own apples, pumpkins, and holiday trees.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashby

Ashby is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors are always treated with kindness and respect. It’s important to note that the town is relatively conservative, and visitors should avoid discussing topics such as politics or religion unless they are comfortable with the local values.

Best Time to Visit Ashby

The best time to visit Ashby is between May and October when the weather is pleasant and the town is at its liveliest. During the winter months, the town is relatively quiet, and many of the outdoor attractions may not be accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashby

Here are some tips to help visitors navigate the local customs and etiquette in Ashby:

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

  • Take advantage of the natural beauty of the area by hiking, fishing, or boating.

  • Shop at local farmers’ markets and stores to support the local economy.

Don’t:

  • Engage in heated political or religious discussions.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment in any way.

  • Be overly loud or disruptive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Ashby is a safe town, and visitors can rest assured that their well-being is a top priority. Here are some tips to stay healthy and safe during your visit:

Safety in Ashby

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, particularly if you’re hiking or camping in the wilderness.

  • Be mindful of wildlife and avoid interaction with wild animals at all times.

  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Ashby

  • Drink plenty of water, especially during the warmer months.

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors to avoid sunburn.

  • Check for ticks and other insects, particularly if you’re spending time in wooded areas.

Getting to Ashby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ashby is located in Eastern Massachusetts, and there are several ways to get here:

Airports Near Ashby

  • Boston-Logan International Airport - This airport is located approximately one and a half hours away from Ashby.

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport - This airport is located approximately an hour away from Ashby.

Bus Stations Near Ashby

  • Greyhound - This station is located in nearby Fitchburg, approximately 15 minutes away from Ashby.

  • Peter Pan - This station is located in nearby Gardner, approximately 20 minutes away from Ashby.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals - There are several car rental companies available in the area, including Hertz, Enterprise, and Avis.

  • Taxi and ride-sharing services - Uber and Lyft are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Ashby

The best way to get around Ashby is by car, as the town is relatively spread out and many of the top attractions are located outside of town.

Where to Go Next from Ashby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ashby is located in the heart of Eastern Massachusetts and is a great starting point for exploring nearby destinations, including:

  • Boston - One of the oldest cities in the United States, Boston is located approximately an hour and a half away from Ashby and boasts a rich cultural and historical heritage.

  • Cape Cod - Located approximately two hours away from Ashby, Cape Cod is a beautiful destination known for its scenic beaches and charming seaside towns.

Conclusion

Ashby, Massachusetts is a charming town that offers a wealth of natural beauty and outdoor adventures for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re hiking Mount Watatic, exploring the Ashby Museum of Natural History, or sampling the local food and drink scene, Ashby is sure to captivate you with its charm and friendliness.
