Introduction to Amesbury

Nestled in Merrimack Valley, in eastern Massachusetts, Amesbury is small, but gorgeous town that offers an amalgamation of history, culture, and scenic beauty. It is located at the confluence of the Powow and Merrimack rivers, adding to its natural charm. Amesbury’s rich history dates back to the 17th century, and it has played a significant role in the American revolution and 19th-century industrialization.

List of Places to Visit in Amesbury

From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures, Amesbury has something for every traveler. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lowell’s Boat Shop Museum: This museum exhibits handmade wooden boats and offers guided tours for visitors. It was established in 1793 and is the oldest operating boat shop in America.

  • Amesbury Carriage Museum: Visitors can explore the town’s rich history of carriage-making in this museum. It showcases different types of carriages, tools and the Carriage Maker’s Shop.

  • Macy-Colby House: Built-in 1654, this house serves as a museum and cultural center. It exhibits antique furniture and other objects that offer a glimpse into the colonial life of Amesbury’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Amesbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Gardner Beach: This lake offers ample opportunities for swimming, fishing, and boating. The sandy beach and shaded areas provide a relaxing escape from the daily routine.

  • Powow River and Falls: The Powow River runs through Amesbury and offers gorgeous views and tranquil spots for picnicking, kayaking, and hiking.

  • Newburyport Marshes: Just a short drive away from Amesbury, Newburyport Marshes offer walking trails, birdwatching, and stunning salt marsh views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amesbury

Amesbury is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some routes you can take to experience the natural beauty of the town:

  • Woodsom Farm: This 400-acre farm offers winding trails that are perfect for hiking and mountain biking.

  • Powow Hill: This 313-foot hill offers a scenic hiking trail that provides stunning views of Amesbury and its surrounding areas.

  • Amesbury Riverwalk: This paved trail runs for 1.3 miles and is suitable for walking, biking, and rollerblading. It follows the Powow River and offers scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Amesbury: Activities and Tours

  • Brewery Tours: Amesbury has a few breweries where visitors can take a tour and try craft beers. Some popular breweries include BareWolf Brewing, True North Ale Company, and Riverwalk Brewing.

  • Farm Visits: A few farms in Amesbury offer tours where you can learn about organic farming and taste fresh produce. Cedar Rock Gardens and Cider Hill Farm are popular among tourists.

  • Zip-Lining: Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of Amesbury while zip-lining through the forest at the Amesbury Sports Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amesbury

Day 1: Visit the Amesbury Carriage Museum and Lowell’s Boat Shop Museum.

Day 2: Explore the town’s outdoor attractions, including the Powow River and Falls, and the Newburyport Marshes.

Day 3: Take a hike on the Woodsom Farm trails and enjoy the natural beauty.

Day 4: Have a relaxing day at Lake Gardner Beach.

Day 5: Visit nearby Plum Island for a guided tour of Parker River Wildlife Reservation and lighthouses.

Day 6: Take a brewery tour at BareWolf Brewing Company and unwind in the evening.

Day 7: Visit the Cedar Rock Gardens and pick your fresh produce.

Food and Drink Scene of Amesbury

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: Amesbury is known for its clam chowder, which is a popular dish in the area. The Market Square Bakehouse and the Rudder are a few places you can try it.

  • Lobster Rolls: Fresh lobster rolls are found in plenty in the town. Good places to try them are the Windward Grille and Ristorante Molise.

  • Apple Cider Donuts: Cider Hill Farm is famous for its apple cider donuts, made fresh daily.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: As mentioned before, BareWolf Brewing, True North Ale Company, and Riverwalk Brewing are popular breweries in the town that offer unique craft beers.

  • Wine: Mill River Winery is located in Amesbury and offers local wines made from grapes grown in the Merrimack Valley region.

Where to Stay in Amesbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For tourists looking for places to stay in Amesbury, the city offers a range of options between bed and breakfasts, hotels, motels, and vacation rentals with affordable rates.

Some popular options include The Inn at Ring’s Island, Hampton Inn, and Fairfield Inn.

What to Buy in Amesbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wooden Boats: Lowell’s Boat Shop produces handmade wooden boats that are made in the traditional Amesbury style. They make for an excellent souvenir to take back home.

  • Farm-fresh Produce: Visit Cedar Rock Gardens and Cider Hill Farm for organic produce like fruits, vegetables, and even honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Amesbury

Amesbury’s history heavily influences the town’s culture. It is known for its carriage-making industry and has artifacts and museums that showcase it.

Best Time to Visit Amesbury

The best time to visit Amesbury is from late spring to early autumn when the weather is pleasant, and the town’s outdoor attractions are in full bloom. The fall foliage is especially stunning and brings in many tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amesbury

Do:

  • Try the clam chowder and lobster rolls.

  • Experience the town’s outdoor attractions and hiking trails.

  • Take a tour of the breweries and enjoy the local craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the outdoor spaces or trails.

  • Ignore the town’s rich history and cultural significance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amesbury

Amesbury is a relatively safe town with low crime rates compared to other towns in the area. However, it is always wise to take necessary safety precautions like locking your car and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Amesbury

Amesbury has a decent healthcare system. The Anna Jaques Hospital located in nearby Newburyport, has an emergency department and offers full service medical and surgical care.

Getting to Amesbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amesbury

The closest international airports to Amesbury are Logan International Airport in Boston and Manchester-Boston Regional Airport in New Hampshire, which are 45 minutes and 60 minutes away, respectively.

Bus Stations Near Amesbury

Amesbury has no bus terminal of its own, but travelers can take a bus to Newburyport and then change to Amesbury using local services provided by the MVRTA bus company.

Other Options

Visitors can take Amtrak trains to Boston, which is about 30 miles from Amesbury or drive themselves to the town.

How to Get Around in Amesbury

The easiest way for travelers to get around town and its neighboring areas is by renting a car. Some other options include taxis and rideshare services like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Amesbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newburyport: This picturesque seaside town is only a 15-minute drive away from Amesbury and is known for its charming streets, beaches, and art galleries.

  • Salem: Famous for the Salem Witch Trials in the 17th century, Salem is an hour’s drive from Amesbury and offers visitors a glimpse into the region’s colonial past.

  • Boston: The city of Boston is around a 45-minute drive from Amesbury and offers visitors a chance to experience the city’s rich history and cultural landmarks, museums, and diverse cuisine.

Conclusion

Amesbury may be a small town, but it packs a punch when it comes to offering visitors a unique and rich experience. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, the town has something for everyone, making it an excellent destination for a day trip or even a week-long vacation.
