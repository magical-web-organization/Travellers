Introduction to Methuen

Methuen is a city located in Merrimack Valley in Eastern Massachusetts, bordered by the towns of Haverhill, Lawrence, and Andover. Known for its rich history and natural attractions, Methuen offers a unique travel experience for visitors. This guide will provide you with comprehensive information to plan your trip and explore the city’s cultural and natural attractions, food and drink scene, accommodations, transportation options, safety and health, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Methuen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-see attractions in Methuen is the Nevins Memorial Library, a historic building listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The library offers a vast selection of books and resources for visitors to enjoy.

The Methuen Memorial Music Hall is another cultural landmark in the city, featuring a historic pipe organ that is over 150 years old. Visitors can enjoy live music and performances throughout the year at this historic venue.

The Tenney Gatehouse is a restored historic home that offers visitors a glimpse into the life of Methuen’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Methuen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the best natural attractions in Methuen is the Spicket River Greenway, a scenic walking and biking trail that winds through the city’s parks and natural areas.

Forest Lake and Lake Massapoag are both popular destinations for swimming, fishing, and boating in the summer months.

Methuen Rail Trail is a popular spot for hiking, jogging, or cycling. The 2.5-mile trail runs from Pleasant Valley Street to Barker Street and offers scenic views along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Methuen

Besides the Methuen Rail Trail, visitors can explore the Willowdale State Forest that offers more than 40 miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails.

Best Things to Do in Methuen: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some outdoor activities, check out the activities offered by Lawrence Biking and Hiking Trails. They offer guided tours, rentals, and even lessons for visitors of all skill levels.

Family-friendly activities in Methuen include Altitude Trampoline Park, a giant indoor trampoline park, and Jay Gee’s Ice Cream & Fun Center, which offers mini-golf, bumper cars, and other fun activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Methuen

Must-Try Dishes

For some local cuisine, try out Blackstrap BBQ, a popular spot for authentic barbecue, or Nick’s Nest, and indulge in their famous hot dogs.

For Italian cuisine, check out Toscana’s Ristorante or Scola’s Restaurant & Wine Bar. The seafood lovers can head to The Fish Tale, offering fresh seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer enthusiasts can head to the Navigation Brewing Company, which offers a variety of unique and delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Methuen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options available for accommodations in Methuen, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. The Days Hotel by Wyndham Methuen MA offers affordable accomodation and is located in the heart of the city. For luxurious options, The Stonehedge Inn & Spa is a great option, featuring a spa where visitors can unwind and relax.

What to Buy in Methuen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For local souvenirs and products, head to Methuen Village Shops, a historic shopping destination featuring local stores, designer shops, and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Methuen

Methuen’s culture is deeply rooted in its rich history, natural beauty, and diverse communities. Visitors should respect and embrace the local customs and traditions while exploring the city.

Best Time to Visit Methuen

The best time to visit Methuen is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is pleasant and there are plenty of outdoor activities available for visitors to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Methuen

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Try out the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural attractions.
  • Disrespect the local communities and their traditions.
  • Forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Methuen

Methuen is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night in areas that are not well-lit.

Health in Methuen

Visitors should stay hydrated, especially during outdoor activities in the summer months. It’s recommended to carry a water bottle with them. Visitors should also pack bug spray and sunscreen to prevent insect bites and sunburn.

Getting to Methuen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Methuen

Methuen is located approximately 28 miles from Boston Logan International Airport and can be reached via car or public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Methuen

The Methuen Loop is the main transportation hub for buses, and there are several bus companies that operate in the area, including Greyhound and Peter Pan Bus Lines.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, hail a taxi, or use ridesharing services to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Methuen

Walking and biking are the best ways to explore Methuen’s natural beauty and cultural landmarks, while public transportation and rental cars are also available for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Methuen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby towns of Haverhill and Lawrence and check out their attractions, local cuisine and cultural landmarks. Boston, the capital city of Massachusetts, is located only 30 miles from Methuen and offers plenty of unique attractions, museums, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Methuen is a unique and charming city that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities.
