Introduction to North Andover

Located in Merrimack Valley, Eastern Massachusetts, North Andover is a charming year-round destination with plenty of recreational activities, cultural attractions, and historical landmarks. Visitors can explore the town’s colonial roots by strolling down tree-lined streets, admiring historic homes, and visiting local museums. North Andover offers something for everyone, from foodies to nature lovers, history buffs to adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in North Andover

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stevens-Coolidge Place - This historic estate features manicured gardens and a Georgian Revival mansion
  • Parson Barnard House - A historic house museum built in 1715 that showcases 18th-century life and architecture
  • Merrimack Valley Textile Museum - Learn about the region’s textile industry through exhibits, machinery, and artifacts
  • Addison Gallery of American Art - A free art museum featuring a collection of over 20,000 American art pieces
  • North Andover Historical Society - A nonprofit organization dedicated to preserving North Andover’s history through exhibits, outreach, and education.

Natural Attractions of North Andover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harold Parker State Forest - A 3,000-acre natural reserve featuring camping, swimming, hiking, and fishing
  • Weir Hill Reservation - A 194-acre hilltop park with hiking trails and stunning views of Lake Cochichewick
  • Stevens Pond - A popular spot for swimming, fishing, and picnicking, with a public beach and boat launch
  • Merrimack River - Go kayaking, canoeing, or fishing on the historic river
  • Ward Reservation - Take in the scenic views from atop Holt Hill, the highest point in Essex County.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Andover

  • The Bay Circuit Trail - A 200-mile trail that traverses 37 towns and passes through North Andover, offering great views of wetlands, forests, and scenic countryside
  • The Stevens-Coolidge Place Trail - A relaxing walk through gorgeous gardens and forest trails
  • The Weir Hill Trail - A two-mile loop trail through hills, fields, and woods.

Best Things to Do in North Andover: Activities and Tours

  • North Andover Historical Society’s Walking Tours - Join a guided walking tour of North Andover’s historic houses, churches, and landmarks
  • Canobie Lake Park - Just a short drive from North Andover, this amusement park has something for everyone, from thrilling roller coasters to family-friendly rides and games
  • Smuttynose Brewing Company - Take a guided brewery tour and sample some of the best craft beers in New England
  • North Andover Farmers Market - Browse a wide variety of fresh produce, baked goods, artisanal crafts, and live music
  • The Stevens Estate - This restored 19th-century mansion offers guided tours, wedding venues, and event spaces.

Food and Drink Scene of North Andover

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder - A New England classic, made with fresh clams, cream, and potatoes
  • Lobster Roll - A sandwich filled with sweet, succulent lobster meat
  • Apple Cider - Freshly pressed apple cider is a popular fall treat in North Andover
  • Roast Beef Sandwich - Thinly sliced beef piled high on a bun with horseradish mayo, a local favorite
  • Pumpkin Pie - A quintessential autumn dessert, best enjoyed with a dollop of whipped cream on top.

Local Beverages

  • Merrimack Ales - A local brewery offering a variety of craft beers such as IPA’s, porters, and stouts
  • Nestle Waters - Located in North Andover, the spring water is believed to originate from the nearby hills
  • Cider Hill Farm - Enjoy a glass of hard cider or visit their farm stand to purchase fresh produce.

Where to Stay in North Andover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Courtyard by Marriott Boston Andover - A contemporary hotel offering modern amenities, an indoor pool, and an on-site restaurant
  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Boston - Andover - A welcoming hotel with stylish rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant
  • North Andover AirBnb - A great option for more budget-conscious visitors, with many rooms available for rent in the area.

What to Buy in North Andover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • North Andover Farmers Market - Pick up locally made crafts, baked goods, and produce
  • The Leather Shop of North Andover - Browse a variety of leather goods, including wallets, belts, and bags
  • Steven’s Estate Gift Shop - Find unique souvenirs and gifts like handmade soaps, candles, and home goods.

Local Culture and Customs of North Andover

North Andover is a close-knit community with a strong sense of pride in its rich history and natural beauty. Visitors should be respectful of this heritage and take the time to learn about the town’s unique culture and customs. The town is home to a mix of nationalities, and visitors should be aware of multicultural customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit North Andover

The best time to visit North Andover is during the fall months when the leaves change colors and the air is crisp. This is also the time for apple picking and pumpkin picking. Winter in North Andover can be quite cold and snowy, while spring and summer are mild and offer plenty of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Andover

Do:

  • Take advantage of the wonderful hiking and biking trails
  • Sample some of the locally brewed craft beers
  • Learn about the town’s rich history and heritage at local museums and landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces
  • Ignore local traffic laws
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Andover

North Andover is a relatively safe town, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. Visitors should always take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.

Health in North Andover

North Andover is home to several medical establishments. In case of an emergency, dial 911. For non-emergent medical needs, consult works in nearby Urgent Care Centers.

Getting to North Andover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Andover

  • Boston Logan International Airport is less than an hour drive from North Andover.
  • Manchester Airport is an alternative that is located just 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near North Andover

  • Merrimack Valley Regional Transit offers bus service to various locations in North Andover.

Other Options

  • Several car rental companies operate in North Andover
  • Taxis and ride-share services like Uber and Lyft are readily available.

How to Get Around in North Andover

A rental car or personal vehicle is the best way to get around North Andover. There are plenty of parking lots and garages in town, making it easy to park and walk around. Buses and trains are also available via MBTA commuter rail.

Where to Go Next from North Andover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Boston - Visit the famous Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum, walk the Freedom Trail, or catch a game at Fenway Park. Boston is just a short drive away from North Andover.
  • Apple picking and pumpkin picking - Enjoy a fun time of picking your very own fruit and vegetable at any of the nearby orchards and farms located around North Andover.

Conclusion

North Andover is a picturesque town offering a blend of natural beauty, historical landmarks, and outdoor adventures. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history and culture, enjoy the local food scene, and take in the stunning scenery. Whether you’re travelling solo or with family and friends, North Andover has something for everyone.
