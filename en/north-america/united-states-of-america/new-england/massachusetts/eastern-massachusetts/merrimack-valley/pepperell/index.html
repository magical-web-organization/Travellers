Introduction to Pepperell

Located in Merrimack Valley and Eastern Massachusetts, Pepperell is a picturesque small town that offers a unique mix of history, culture, and nature. Pepperell, officially incorporated in 1775, has a rich history dating back to the colonial era and played a vital role in the American Revolution. The town has preserved several historical landmarks that provide a glimpse into its past. Pepperell is also home to many natural attractions such as Nashua River, Pepperell Pond, and Varnum Brook that offer visitors an opportunity to indulge in outdoor adventures. The following guide covers the best things to do, see, and eat in Pepperell.

List of Places to Visit in Pepperell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lawrence Library: Built-in 1883, Lawrence Library is a historical landmark and an exceptional example of Victorian architecture. The library houses a vast collection of books and offers community programs and events for all ages.

  • Pepperell Covered Bridge: Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, Pepperell Covered Bridge is a 401-foot-long, 10-foot-wide wooden bridge over Nashua River. It is one of the few remaining covered bridges in Massachusetts.

  • Butterfly Place: Located in nearby Westford, Butterfly Place is a tropical garden housed in a glass conservatory. The garden is home to thousands of exotic butterflies and offers a unique nature experience for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Pepperell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nashua River Rail Trail: The trail is a 12.5-mile-long paved trail that runs from Ayer to Nashua. It offers scenic views of the river, forests, and farmlands and provides an excellent opportunity for hiking, biking, and inline skating.

  • Pepperell Pond: This small pond is a great spot for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing. The pond is surrounded by scenic forests, and a hiking trail leads to the adjacent Varnum Brook Falls.

  • Varnum Brook Trail: A 1.5-mile-long trail that follows Varnum Brook to its source. The trail offers stunning views of the brook, a small waterfall, and a pond.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pepperell

  • Rail Trail: As mentioned earlier, the Nashua River Rail Trail is a popular spot for hiking and biking in Pepperell. The trail is suitable for all skill levels and is accessible year-round.

  • Rocky Hill Wildlife Sanctuary: A 1.7-mile-long trail that winds through meadows, forests, and wetlands. The trail offers excellent views of the Nashua River and is well-marked with interpretive signs.

  • Pratt Conservation Area: The area features four miles of hiking trails that wind through mixed forests, wetlands, and streams. The trails offer views of the surrounding hills and forests.

Best Things to Do in Pepperell: Activities and Tours

  • Farmers Market: Held every Saturday from June to October, the Farmers Market offers visitors an opportunity to sample fresh produce, artisanal products, and meet local farmers and makers.

  • Apple Orchards: Pepperell has several apple orchards, most notably, the George Hill Orchards and the Kimball Fruit Farm. Visitors can pick their apples, enjoy fresh cider and donuts, and take a hay ride.

  • Tiki Trail: A mini-golf course with a tropical theme. The course features challenging holes designed to resemble a Hawaiian island.

Food and Drink Scene of Pepperell

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Available at several seafood restaurants in Pepperell, the lobster roll is a classic New England dish that should not be missed.

  • Blueberry Pancakes: Many of the town’s breakfast spots serve fluffy blueberry pancakes made with fresh blueberries, a must-try dish for breakfast lovers.

  • Apple Cider Donuts: A seasonal treat available at the town’s apple orchards, apple cider donuts are a delicious fall treat.

Local Beverages

  • Pepperell Sling: A signature cocktail made with local gin and cranberry juice, the Pepperell Sling is a refreshing drink to try.

  • Nashoba Valley Winery: Located just outside of Pepperell, Nashoba Valley Winery produces award-winning wines, spirits, and ciders. Visitors can sample their products at the winery’s tasting room.

Where to Stay in Pepperell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • SpringHill Suites by Marriott: Located in nearby Devens, SpringHill Suites by Marriott offers spacious rooms and modern amenities such as a fitness center and an indoor pool.

  • The Inn on the Horse Farm: A charming bed and breakfast located in Pepperell, the Inn on the Horse Farm features cozy rooms and a farm-to-table breakfast.

What to Buy in Pepperell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Apple Products: Pepperell is famous for its apple orchards, and visitors can bring home fresh apples, apple cider, and apple cider donuts.

  • Handmade Soaps: Several local artisans create handmade soaps using all-natural ingredients. These make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Pepperell

Pepperell is a small town with a strong community spirit. Residents actively participate in community events, such as the Pepperell Fall Festival and the Christmas tree lighting ceremony. Visitors are welcomed with warmth and hospitality and are encouraged to participate in these events.

Best Time to Visit Pepperell

The best time to visit Pepperell is in the fall when the trees turn to vibrant shades of red, yellow, and orange, and the town’s orchards are in full swing. Spring and summer are also great times to visit, with warm temperatures and numerous outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pepperell

Do:

  • Visit the Farmers Market: The Pepperell Farmers Market is an excellent place to experience the town’s community spirit and sample local products.

  • Respect Nature: Pepperell is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty. Visitors should take care not to litter or disturb the town’s wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Visitors should not leave trash on hiking trails or in public places.

  • Ignore Traffic Rules: Pepperell has several winding roads, and visitors should follow traffic rules and drive slowly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pepperell

Pepperell is a safe and welcoming town with low crime rates. Visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking their cars and keeping their valuables safe.

Health in Pepperell

Pepperell has several medical clinics and hospitals nearby. Visitors should ensure they have health insurance and carry any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Pepperell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pepperell

  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS): Located approximately 50 miles away, Boston Logan International Airport is the nearest major airport to Pepperell.

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT): Located approximately 25 miles away, Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is a smaller airport that offers flights to several destinations.

Bus Stations Near Pepperell

  • Concord Coach Lines: Provides bus service from Boston, Maine, and New Hampshire to Nashua, located approximately 15 miles from Pepperell.

Other Options

  • Car Rental Services: Several car rental services are available at Boston Logan International Airport and Manchester-Boston Regional Airport.

  • Amtrak Train Service: Amtrak offers train service to nearby cities such as Boston and Worcester.

How to Get Around in Pepperell

Most of the town’s attractions are located within a short driving distance of each other and can be explored by car. Biking is also an excellent way to explore the town’s scenic trails and natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Pepperell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lowell: Located approximately 15 miles away, Lowell is a historic mill town that offers several cultural and natural attractions, such as Lowell National Historical Park and Merrimack River.

  • Nashua: Located approximately 10 miles away across the New Hampshire border, Nashua is a vibrant city that offers several shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Pepperell, Massachusetts, is a small town that offers visitors a unique mix of history, culture, and nature. With numerous cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, Pepperell is an excellent destination for a short trip or an extended stay. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Pepperell has something to offer for everyone.
