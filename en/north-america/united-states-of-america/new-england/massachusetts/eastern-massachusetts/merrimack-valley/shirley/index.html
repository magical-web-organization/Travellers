Introduction to Shirley

Nestled in the Merrimack Valley of Eastern Massachusetts and surrounded by the beautiful sights of New England, Shirley is a small but vibrant town known for its historic charm and scenic beauty. With a rich cultural heritage and a variety of outdoor attractions, there’s something for everyone in Shirley.

Whether you’re a history buff, an adrenaline junkie, a foodie, or just looking to relax and enjoy the scenery, Shirley is the perfect destination for your next getaway.

In this travel guide, we’ll give you an insider’s look at the best things to see and do in Shirley, along with tips for getting around, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Shirley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Shirley Meeting House: A historic gathering place and community center that has been beautifully restored and offers a glimpse into the town’s past.
  • The Bull Run Restaurant: This historic building has served as a tavern and stagecoach stop since the 18th century, and now hosts live performances and events year-round.
  • The Phoenix Building: Originally built as a hotel in the 19th century, this beautifully restored building now houses a variety of shops and restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Shirley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oxbow Wildlife Sanctuary: With over 200 acres of protected land and dozens of hiking trails, Oxbow is the perfect place to get up close and personal with nature.
  • Shirley Shaker Village: This living history museum immerses visitors in the daily life of the town’s Shaker community, including authentic workshops, gardens, and tours.
  • The Nashua River Rail Trail: This scenic, 12-mile trail is a favorite of hikers, bikers, and runners, and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shirley

Shirley is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails winding through its forests, hills, and meadows. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Midstate Trail: Running from New Hampshire to Rhode Island, this 95-mile trail passes through Shirley and offers some of the most challenging and rewarding hiking in the region.
  • Mount Watatic: Rising over 1,800 feet above sea level, this peak is a favorite of hikers and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Horse Hill Nature Preserve: Featuring over 300 acres of preserved land, Horse Hill offers a variety of trails for hikers, bikers, and equestrians, along with stunning views of ponds, forests, and meadows.

Best Things to Do in Shirley: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Apple Orchards: Shirley is home to several orchards that offer tours, tastings, and other activities throughout the year.
  • Attend a Concert at the Bull Run: From local musicians to nationally-renowned acts, the Bull Run hosts a variety of concerts and events that showcase Shirley’s thriving arts scene.
  • Take a Ghost Tour: Explore the history and hauntings of Shirley with a spooky, lantern-lit tour through the town’s most haunted spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shirley

Day 1: Explore Shirley’s historic downtown, visit the Shirley Meeting House, and dine at the Bull Run Restaurant.

Day 2: Take a scenic hike along the Midstate Trail or explore the Nashua River Rail Trail on a bike ride.

Day 3: Spend a day at Oxbow Wildlife Sanctuary, taking in the natural beauty and wildlife of the area.

Day 4: Immerse yourself in the history of Shirley with a visit to the Shaker Village and the Phoenix Building.

Day 5: Take a ghost tour of Shirley and learn about the town’s haunted past.

Day 6: Visit a local apple orchard and sample some of Shirley’s delicious cider and apple-themed treats.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenery of Shirley, perhaps taking a leisurely walk through Horse Hill Nature Preserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Shirley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Cider Donuts: A New England classic, these sweet treats are a must-try when visiting Shirley’s apple orchards.
  • Lobster Rolls: Fresh seafood is a staple of the New England diet, and Shirley is no exception. Sample some delicious lobster rolls at one of the town’s seafood spots.
  • Clam Chowder: Another classic New England dish, clam chowder is a hearty and warming soup that’s perfect for a chilly day in Shirley.

Local Beverages

  • Apple Cider: Made from the fresh apples grown in Shirley’s orchards, this sweet and spicy cider is a favorite of locals and visitors alike.
  • Craft Beer: Shirley is home to several breweries that offer a variety of delicious, locally-made beers.

Where to Stay in Shirley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Phoenix Inn: Located in a beautifully restored historic building, this inn offers elegant accommodations and easy access to Shirley’s downtown area.
  • The Ayer Motor Inn: Just a short drive from Shirley, this family-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to nearby attractions.
  • The Hidden Hills Retreat: This charming B&B boasts stunning views of the countryside and offers cozy, rustic rooms that are perfect for a peaceful getaway to Shirley.

What to Buy in Shirley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Apple Cider: Bring home a taste of Shirley’s orchards with a bottle of their famous apple cider.
  • Shaker Crafts: The Shaker Village offers a wide variety of handcrafted items made by local artisans, including baskets, furniture, and pottery.
  • Maple Syrup: Made from the sap of sugar maples in nearby forests, maple syrup is a delicious and uniquely New England souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Shirley

Shirley is a friendly and welcoming town that takes pride in its history and natural beauty. Visitors should respect the town’s historic sites and natural areas, and be sure to sample the delicious local cuisine that makes Shirley such a special place.

Best Time to Visit Shirley

The best time to visit Shirley depends largely on your interests. Spring and fall are popular times for outdoor activities and enjoying the stunning fall foliage, while summer is ideal for swimming and other water activities. Winter is a popular time for skiing and snowshoeing in nearby areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shirley

Do:

  • Respect the town’s historic sites and natural areas.
  • Sample the delicious local cuisine, especially the apple-themed treats and seafood.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and exploring the nearby forests and hills.

Don’t:

  • Damage or remove any plants, animals, or other natural resources.
  • Ignore safety warnings or guidelines, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Disturb the peace and quiet of Shirley’s residents, especially during nighttime hours.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shirley

Shirley is a safe and welcoming town with relatively low crime rates. Visitors should take basic precautions to protect their personal belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Shirley

Shirley does not have its own hospital or medical center, but nearby towns offer a variety of medical services. Visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications and be aware of any potential allergies or health issues when dining out.

Getting to Shirley: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT)

Bus Stations Near Shirley

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: Located in nearby Fitchburg, MA.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: There are several car rental companies in the area, including Avis and Budget.
  • Uber/Lyft: Both Uber and Lyft operate in the Shirley area.

How to Get Around in Shirley

The best way to get around Shirley is by car or bike, as many of the town’s attractions and outdoor areas are spread out. Walking is a good option for exploring the town’s historic downtown area, and there are several bike rental companies in the area for visitors who prefer to explore on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Shirley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Concord, MA: Just a 15-minute drive from Shirley, Concord is a historic town famous for its literary and revolutionary history.
  • Boston, MA: The bustling metropolis of Boston is just an hour’s drive from Shirley, and offers a wide variety of cultural attractions, dining options, and shopping opportunities.
  • Wachusett Mountain State Reservation: A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, this state park offers skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter activities, along with hiking, biking, and camping in the summer months.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or simply looking to relax and enjoy the beauty of New England, Shirley has something for everyone. With its charming small-town atmosphere, rich cultural heritage, and stunning natural beauty, Shirley is the perfect destination for your next getaway.
