Introduction to North Attleborough

Welcome to North Attleborough, a charming town located in Eastern Massachusetts in the Bristol-Norfolk County region. With plenty of parks, historical sites, locally-owned shops, and top-notch restaurants, North Attleborough is ideal for those seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in North Attleborough

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re an art or history buff, you’ll enjoy visiting the following museums and historical sites in North Attleborough:

  • Whiting Field: This historical baseball field is known for its connection to the classic 1989 film “Field of Dreams.”
  • Old Colony Historical Society: Explore North Attleborough’s rich history at the Old Colony Historical Society, a museum that boasts exhibits on agriculture, industry, and the importance of the local commuter train system.
  • Attleboro Area Industrial Museum: Take a step back into the Industrial Revolution at the Attleboro Area Industrial Museum, where you can learn about the town’s production of jewelry and other products.

Natural Attractions of North Attleborough: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Attleborough is home to several natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • World War I Memorial Park and Zoo: This 45-acre park features several playgrounds, a duck pond, and a 12-animal zoo, making it an ideal spot for a family-friendly outing.
  • The F. Gilbert Hills State Forest: Hike, bike, or horseback ride through the F. Gilbert Hills State Forest, which spans over 1000 acres of land that overlooks the Blackstone Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Attleborough

If you’re looking to add some adventure to your trip, North Attleborough has several hiking and biking routes to choose from:

  • The Ten Mile River Greenway Trail: This 3.6-mile trail is ideal for a peaceful bike ride or jog. The route follows the scenic Ten Mile River, with plenty of picnic areas along the way.
  • Rhode Island North-South Trail: This 77-mile trail starts in Charlestown and ends in Gloucester. While the trail mostly covers rocky terrain, the views of nature and wildlife more than make up for the challenging trek.

Best Things to Do in North Attleborough: Activities and Tours

North Attleborough has plenty of activities to keep visitors entertained, from sports games to yoga classes:

  • DeMarinis Family Ice Rink: Catch a local hockey game or participate in recreational skating at DeMarinis Family Ice Rink.
  • Attleboro Farmer’s Market: Visit the Attleboro Farmer’s Market to pick up some locally-sourced produce on Saturdays between June and October.
  • Yoga at the YMCA: The Attleboro YMCA offers yoga classes to help visitors unwind and de-stress during their trip.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Attleborough

Monday: Explore the Old Colony Historical Society and learn about the area’s rich history.

Tuesday: Go hiking on the F. Gilbert Hills State Forest trail.

Wednesday: Take a break from hiking and relax at the Whiting Field park.

Thursday: Visit the Sixteen Acres Garden Center to pick up some plants for souvenirs.

Friday: Take a day trip to nearby Providence, Rhode Island, to enjoy the local cuisine and culture.

Saturday: Shop for fresh local produce at the Attleboro Farmer’s Market.

Sunday: Play a game of ice hockey at the DeMarinis Family Ice Rink and enjoy some classic American cuisine afterwards.

Food and Drink Scene of North Attleborough

Must-Try Dishes

North Attleborough is famous for American cuisine, with plenty of classic dishes to try:

  • Deli Sandwich at Anthony’s North End Deli: Try out the classic Italian deli sandwiches at Anthony’s North End Deli, which come with a range of fresh meats and cheeses.
  • Burgers at Buster’s Sports Bar and Grill: Enjoy some classic American pub food at Buster’s Sports Bar and Grill, with specialty burgers and sandwiches on the menu.
  • Doughnuts at Atkins Farm: Get a taste of North Attleborough’s local cuisine at Atkins Farm, known for their fresh, warm apple cider doughnuts.

Local Beverages

North Attleborough also offers a range of locally-brewed beverages to sample:

  • Sons of Liberty Beer and Spirits Co.: Taste Sons of Liberty’s locally-brewed beer and spirits, with a range of tours and tastings available.
  • Twin Rivers Wine Co.: Enjoy a glass of locally-produced wine from Twin Rivers Wine Co.’s outdoor patio while taking in the picturesque scenery.

Where to Stay in North Attleborough: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Attleborough has several accommodation options to choose from, depending on your budget and preferences:

  • Holiday Inn Express: This hotel offers basic amenities and is located close to several North Attleborough attractions.
  • The Stone House: Stay in a classic New England B&B at The Stone House, which offers cosy accommodations in a historic setting.

What to Buy in North Attleborough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home? North Attleborough has several shops and boutiques where you can purchase locally-made products:

  • Sixteen Acres Garden Center: Pick up some seeds or plants from the Sixteen Acres Garden Center to take home and grow.
  • Ward’s Berry Farm: Shop for fresh produce, such as strawberries and blueberries, at Ward’s Berry Farm from June until October.

Local Culture and Customs of North Attleborough

North Attleborough has a friendly, community-centric culture that values independence and self-sustainability. It’s common to see locally-owned small businesses throughout the town, and visitors are encouraged to support these establishments during their stay.

Best Time to Visit North Attleborough

The best time to visit North Attleborough is during the summer months, between June and September, when the weather is warm and local events are in full swing. Fall is another great time to visit, with the changing leaves offering picturesque scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Attleborough

Do:

  • Do support locally-owned small businesses during your stay.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces.
  • Don’t disregard historical markers and plaques when exploring the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Attleborough

North Attleborough is considered a safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and avoid travelling alone at night.

Health in North Attleborough

North Attleborough has several clinics and hospitals, such as Sturdy Memorial Hospital, in case of medical emergencies during your trip.

Getting to North Attleborough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Attleborough

  • T.F. Green Airport in Warwick, Rhode Island is 20 miles away from North Attleborough.
  • Logan International Airport in Boston is 40 miles away from North Attleborough.

Bus Stations Near North Attleborough

Greyhound Bus Station and Peter Pan Bus Lines both operate out of Providence, Rhode Island, which is only a 15-minute drive from North Attleborough.

Other Options

The nearby Mansfield Train Station offers services that connect visitors to Boston, Providence, and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in North Attleborough

Visitors can rent a car or use rideshare services to get around North Attleborough. Additionally, visitors can take the Commuter Rail train to explore North Attleborough and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from North Attleborough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Attleborough is located near several popular destinations in Massachusetts and Rhode Island:

  • Providence, Rhode Island: Explore the bustling city of Providence, known for its diverse culture and arts scene.
  • Boston, Massachusetts: Take a weekend trip to Boston to experience the city’s rich history and culture.
  • Wrentham, Massachusetts: Shop at the Wrentham Outlets, offering popular brands and discounted prices.

Conclusion

North Attleborough is an ideal destination for visitors seeking a peaceful escape and a chance to experience American culture and history. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, local cuisine, or historical sites, North Attleborough has something for every type of traveler.
