Introduction to Medfield

Medfield is a charming town located in Bristol-Norfolk County, Eastern Massachusetts. It is situated in the heart of Massachusetts, making it an ideal location to explore other places in the state. The town has a rich history dating back to the 17th century, which you can still see in its well-preserved historical buildings and museums. Medfield offers many attractions for visitors, including outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Medfield

Here are the top places to visit in Medfield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Peak House - Built-in 1651, this historic house is one of the oldest in Medfield. It served as a hospital during King Philip’s War, and now operates as a museum showcasing the town’s history.
  • The Dwight-Derby House - Another historic house turned museum, showcasing Medfield’s heritage and the day-to-day life of its 17th-century inhabitants.
  • The Medfield Historical Society and Museum - Visit this museum to learn more about the town’s history through a range of exhibits and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Medfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rocky Woods Reservation - A great place for hiking and trail running enthusiasts, with over 6 miles of trails through beautiful woodlands and hills.
  • Hales Pond and the Choate Park - The perfect spot for swimming or just relaxing on the beach.
  • Baxter Park - A peaceful park perfect for a family picnic or a quiet afternoon stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medfield

Medfield is an active town, attracting plenty of outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Dover Greenway Trail -A 6-mile path connecting Medfield to the neighboring town of Dover and surrounding areas.
  • Shattuck Reservation Trail - A 1.6mile stone dust trail, making it handicapped accessible.

Best Things to Do in Medfield: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling alone, with your family, or in a group, there are plenty of things to do in Medfield:

  • The Zullo Gallery - Check out the art scene in Medfield by visiting this modern art gallery.
  • Medfield Farmers’ Market - This weekly event showcases local produce, baked goods, artisanal crafts, and more.
  • Brett & Jane - A small family-run business, offering their farm’s meat, eggs and local milk from their farm.

Week-Long Itinerary for Medfield

Here is a week-long itinerary for Medfield:

Day 1 - Arrive in Medfield and check in to your accommodation Day 2 - Take a walking tour of the town’s historic sites, including Peak House and The Medfield Historical Society and Museum. Day 3- Visit the local conservation areas like Rocky Woods Reservation and Shattuck Reservation. Day 4 - Spend the day at Hales Pond and the Choate Park for swimming and relaxation. Day 5 - Check out some local shops along Main Street like Tess & Carlos Boutique and pick up some souvenirs. Day 6 - Visit The Zullo Gallery for a dose of contemporary art, followed by a visit to Brett & Jane for farm-fresh goods and a picnic. Day 7 - Spend your last day in Medfield leisurely enjoying a good book at Baxter Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Medfield

Don’t miss out on some of the delicious local foods and beverages when in town.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Medfield Pasta & Cheese – A company which makes some of the best cheese and pasta in the area.

Local Beverages

  • The Sugar Shoppe – this place serves a good cup of coffee and delicious pastries. Don’t miss their famous ‘Medfield Madness’ latte!

Where to Stay in Medfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Medfield offers visitors several accommodation options depending on their budget and preference.

  • The Medfield House - A cozy and well-maintained guesthouse.
  • The Harding House - A historic building turned to a bed and breakfast.
  • The Charles River Inn - A welcoming family-run motel.

What to Buy in Medfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The Little Shop of Olive Oils - This little store offers unique blends of olive oils and vinegars that are a must-try.
  • The Toy Box - A family-owned toy store with a great selection of classic toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Medfield

Medfield is home to a tight-knit and friendly community, where you can still feel the remnants of its past. One of the most popular ways to stay connected is participation in community events.

Best Time to Visit Medfield

The best time to visit Medfield is from May to September when the weather is warm and welcoming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medfield

Do:

  • Do visit the historic sites and museums to learn about the town’s rich history.
  • Do check out the local farmers’ market for fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal crafts.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry a reusable bag when visiting the farmers’ market.
  • Don’t litter in the beautiful conservation areas.

Safety and Health

Medfield is a pretty safe town with a low-crime rate but, like anywhere, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Medfield

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Medfield

  • Health-wise, visitors should carry necessary medications, to make sure medical attention can be received at the local Medfield hospital (https://www.steward.org/medfield)

Getting to Medfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Medfield is easy with several transportation options to choose from.

Airports Near Medfield

  • Boston Logan International Airport - located 22 miles from Medfield is the best bet for air travellers.

Bus Stations Near Medfield

  • The near bus service station is Northeastern University.

Other Options

  • Private Car Services such as Uber are the best options for comfortable transportation around Medfield.

How to Get Around in Medfield

Walking and cycling are ideal ways to get around town though private cars such as Uber are still recommended.

Where to Go Next from Medfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Medfield is an excellent starting point for exploring the wider Massachusetts area.

  • Take the drive to Gillette Stadium in Foxborough, the home of the New England Patriots, for a game or concert.
  • Head South to Wrentham Village Premium Outlets, one of the largest outlet malls in New England.

Conclusion

Medfield is a welcoming town that will leave a lasting impression. With an abundance of historical sites, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and accommodations, visitors will have a memorable experience in this charming destination. Plan your trip in advance. We hope this travel guide has been a helpful tool in discovering the best of Medfield!
