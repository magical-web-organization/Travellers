Introduction to Canton

Located in Eastern Massachusetts, Canton is a charming town worth exploring. Famous for its rich culture, historical landmarks, and breathtaking views, Canton provides visitors with a unique New England experience. This travel guide will help you explore the best things to do in Canton, including top-rated attractions, local restaurants, hiking and biking routes, and cultural events.

List of Places to Visit in Canton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Canton Viaduct: Known as the world’s longest and tallest masonry structure. It was built in 1834 and still carries trains across the Neponset River.
  • Museum of American Bird Art: This museum showcases artwork, artifacts, and scientific specimens that portray the history and species of birds in America.
  • The Eustis Estate Museum: This historic house and landscape preserves the stories of American History through architecture, artwork, and archive documents.

Natural Attractions of Canton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Hills Reservation: The largest park in Eastern Massachusetts filled with 125 miles of trails, rolling hills, and panoramic views of the Boston skyline.
  • Wampatuck State Park: Massachusetts state park with 3,500 acres of forest, marshes, ponds, and wildlife, perfect for hiking and camping.
  • Borderland State Park: A beautiful landscape area filled with adventure opportunities, including hiking trails, fishing spots, and wildlife discovery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Canton

  • Blue Hills Reservation: Skyline Trail: One of the most popular trails in Blue Hills Reservation, hiking across eight peaks, including the Great Blue Hill, providing travelers with amazing photo opportunities of the Boston skyline.
  • Ponkapoag Trail: This trail surrounds the beautiful Ponkapoag Pond, providing a tranquil and serene experience.
  • Wollomonopoag Trail: Explore ancient glacial landforms while hiking splendid trails surrounded by lush forestry.

Best Things to Do in Canton: Activities and Tours

  • The Trailside Museum: Offers interactive and educational exhibits on local wildlife, interactive exhibits, and a live animal room with creatures you don’t typically see in the wild.
  • Tidmarsh Wildlife Sanctuary: A perfect place for birding and exploring their habitat. Visitors will also enjoy the scenic views of the wetland.

Food and Drink Scene of Canton

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Town Spa Pizza: A delicious pizza spot known for its famous thin-crust pizza and unforgettable pizza salad.
  • The Coffee Corner: A cozy place filled with locals serving delicious clam chowder, bacon-wrapped scallops, and other seafood specialties.

Local Beverages

  • Trillium Brewing Company: A local brewery with a taproom and plenty of craft beers that are worth tasting.
  • Artifact Cider Project: This spot offers visitors with local cider tasting, tours, and a chance to learn about their fermentation process.

Where to Stay in Canton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Boston Canton: The new extended-stay hotel, near University Campus, provides visitors with comfortable suites and an indoor pool.
  • Residence Inn Boston Canton: The perfect location close to attractions, including the Museum of American Bird Art, the hotel offers spacious guestrooms and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • Courtyard by Marriott Boston Stoughton: A modern hotel with exceptional amenities and spectacular views of Boston, including a fitness center, business center, and complimentary Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Canton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Boston Jams and Jellies: A perfect culinary souvenir, offering a range of flavored jams and jellies, including pumpkin pie, cranberry, blueberry, and strawberry jam.
  • Kane’s Donuts: A famous donut store that offers traditional handmade donuts with a modern twist.

Local Culture and Customs of Canton

Canton celebrates its culture through various events throughout the year, including the Canton Friendship Festival, Canton Fall Fair, and St. Patrick’s Day Parade. The town’s locals value their heritage and celebrate it through music, dance, and cultural arts.

Best Time to Visit Canton

The best time to visit Canton is during spring (April-June) or fall (September-November), when the weather is mild, and the scenery is outstanding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canton

Do:

  • Wear comfortable shoes as there will be walking involved while exploring various attractions in Canton.
  • Experience the culture and try local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment or trails.
  • Photograph strangers without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Canton

Canton is a safe community. However, it’s always wise to practice common sense and be aware of your surroundings when exploring new places.

Health in Canton

Canton has several medical facilities available, including Good Samaritan Medical Center and other local clinics. It’s important to be diligent with your medication and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Canton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Canton

  • Logan International Airport: Boston’s primary airport is 20 miles and about 35 minutes from Canton.

Bus Stations Near Canton

  • MBTA Bus Route: Provides access to Canton at multiple stops, delivering all around the town.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a perfect option for visitors if they prefer total independence while traveling to the town.

How to Get Around in Canton

  • Walking and biking are popular ways to get around in Canton.
  • Car rental is also a great option for visitors who want to cover more ground quickly.

Where to Go Next from Canton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Boston: Only a 20-mile drive (30 minutes) from Canton is the world-famous city, with its rich history and culture, beautiful parks, and fresh seafood.
  • Plymouth: A 45-mile drive (50 minutes) to the southeast is where the iconic Plymouth Rock resides, commemorating the arrival of the Pilgrims, and other historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Canton, Massachusetts, is a beautiful town that offers visitors unique experiences, including hiking trails, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re looking to discover natural beauty, history, or culture, Canton has something for everyone.
