Introduction to Sharon

Sharon is a quaint town situated in Eastern Massachusetts, in the United States of America. The town is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. Sharon offers a perfect blend of urban sophistication and rural tranquility.

List of Places to Visit in Sharon

Sharon has a variety of places to discover and explore. Here are some of the top attractions to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moose Hill Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to several protected wildlife species and offers stunning views of the natural surroundings.
  • Sharon Historical Society and Museum: It’s a Local history museum that explores the history of the town from its founding to the present day.
  • Plimoth Plantation: A living history museum that offers a unique look at the Pilgrim way of life in the 17th century.
  • Harvard Museum of Natural History: The museum showcases an extensive collection of natural specimens, including dinosaur skeletons, minerals, and gemstones.

Natural Attractions of Sharon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Borderland State Park: The park offers over 20 miles of hiking trails, numerous fishing spots, and a beautiful lake ideal for swimming.
  • Sharon Dog Park: This park provides a fantastic off-leash area for dogs and offers several exercise stations for dogs and owners alike.
  • Massachusetts Audubon Society: The Audubon manages more than 20 wildlife sanctuaries throughout Massachusetts, which offers a nature trail and fantastic opportunities for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sharon

Sharon is a hiker and biker-friendly town, with plenty of trails for everyone. Here are some of the famous Hiking/Biking routes:

  • The Bay Circuit Trail: The trail runs for 230 miles throughout Massachusetts, and Sharon is one of the locations. The area is accessible and offers a stunning view of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Warner Trail: This trail runs from Sharon to Cumberland in Rhode Island and offers a variety of terrains for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Sharon: Activities and Tours

Sharon has several activities and tours that ensure visitors never run out of things to do. Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in Sharon:

  • Cooking Class: Attend a cooking class at the Signature Finishing Touches in Sharon and learn how to prepare delicious meals.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a horseback riding tour along the trails of Borderland State Park and enjoy the natural scenery.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit the Vinea Wines for a wine tasting tour and experience a wide range of wines from around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sharon

For visitors who want to spend a week in Sharon, here is a seven-day itinerary to make the most of their time:

Day Itinerary

Food and Drink Scene of Sharon

Sharon has a diverse food and drink scene that caters to all tastes and preferences.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A sandwich made with fresh lobster meat is one of the famous dishes in the area.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy soup made from clams, potatoes, and onions is a must-try.
  • Fish and chips: A classic dish of battered fish served with chips and tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Harpoon Brewery: It’s a famous brewery in the Boston area that offers a vast array of beers for beer aficionados.
  • Samuel Adams Brewery: A brewery with local ties that offers a unique range of beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Sharon

Sharon has a rich culture with many traditions passed down through generations. Some of the customs and traditions include:

  • Festivals: The town commemorates every occasion with festivals, fairs, and parades.
  • Sports: Sharon is known for its strong sports culture and has some of the best sports facilities in the region.
  • Arts: Sharon is home to several arts organizations, including dance groups, visual arts groups, and music groups.

Best Time to Visit Sharon

The best time to visit Sharon is from April to October when the weather is pleasant and most of the outdoor activities are available. The fall season is especially famous for its foliage; the whole town is ablaze in a sea of colors of fall leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sharon

  • Be adventurous and try new activities.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Sharon

Sharon is generally a safe town to visit, but visitors should take precautions like any other place.

  • Always be aware of your surroundings, especially after dark.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Observe any safety signs or warnings in the tourist sites.

Health in Sharon

Sharon has many medical facilities that provide high-quality healthcare to both residents and visitors.

  • Drink plenty of water during physical activities.
  • Protect yourself from the sun by wearing sunscreen and a hat.
  • Use insect repellent to protect yourself from ticks and mosquitos.

Where to Go Next from Sharon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions from Sharon, which include:

  • Plymouth: It’s a historic town located 30 miles south of Sharon, famous for the Pilgrims Landing and numerous outdoor activities.
  • Cape Cod: A picturesque town located 90 miles from Sharon and offers beautiful beaches, whale watching, and historic sites.

Conclusion

Sharon is a hidden gem in Massachusetts, offering something for everyone. With its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions, Sharon is the perfect place to visit. Plan your visit well, and make the most of the town’s offerings.
