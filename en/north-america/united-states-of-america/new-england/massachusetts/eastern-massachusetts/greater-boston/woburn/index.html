Introduction to Woburn

Welcome to Woburn, a charming city just 10 miles north of Boston, Massachusetts. Originally settled in 1640, Woburn is rich in history and culture. From its beautiful parks to its delicious restaurants, there is something for everyone in this New England gem. This comprehensive travel guide will introduce you to all the best places to visit, eat, stay, and more during your trip to Woburn.

List of Places to Visit in Woburn

No matter what your interests, there are plenty of things to see and do in Woburn. From museums to outdoor adventures, here is a list of some of the must-visit destinations in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baldwin House. Step back in time at the Baldwin House, a historical home that dates back to 1661. This well-preserved house is now a museum, and visitors can take guided tours to learn about the history of the area.

  • Woburn Public Library. Take a break from the outdoors and spend some time at the Woburn Public Library, an impressive building with beautiful architecture and a vast collection of books.

  • Winn Memorial Library. Don’t miss the Winn Memorial Library, a small but charming museum dedicated to the history of Woburn.

Natural Attractions of Woburn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horn Pond. Horn Pond is a popular destination for hiking and fishing, and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Horn Pond Mountain. For more experienced hikers, take a trek up Horn Pond Mountain and enjoy panoramic views of the city and beyond.

  • Holland Street Park. Perfect for a picnic or a walk, Holland Street Park is a peaceful spot to relax and take in the natural beauty of Woburn.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Woburn

Woburn is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. From leisurely walks to challenging treks, there are routes for every level of adventurer. Below are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Woburn:

  • Horn Pond Trail. The Horn Pond Trail is an easy hiking trail that circles the beautiful Horn Pond. Perfect for beginners and families with young children.

  • Shawsheen River Trail. The Shawsheen River Trail is a scenic route that winds alongside the beautiful Shawsheen River. Great for bikers and hikers alike.

  • Middlesex Fells Reservation. For experienced hikers, the Middlesex Fells Reservation offers an extensive network of trails with stunning views and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Woburn: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Woburn. Here are some of the best:

  • Boston Brew Tours. Take a tour of some of Boston’s best breweries and taste some of the city’s finest beer.

  • Escape the Room. Put your problem-solving skills to the test at Escape the Room, an interactive game where you must solve clues to escape a locked room.

  • SkyVenture Indoor Skydiving. Experience the thrill of skydiving without the plane at SkyVenture Indoor Skydiving, an indoor skydiving facility that simulates the real thing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Woburn

To make the most out of your trip to Woburn, we’ve created a sample week-long itinerary that hits all the best destinations in the city:

Food and Drink Scene of Woburn

Woburn has a delicious food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties and international cuisine to enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New England Clam Chowder. Woburn is in the heart of New England, which means you can’t leave without trying the famous New England Clam Chowder.

  • Boston Cream Pie. This dessert is a Boston classic and can be found at many restaurants and bakeries in Woburn.

  • Fried Clams. Another local specialty, fried clams are a delicious seafood dish that can be found at many seafood restaurants in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Samuel Adams Beer. Samuel Adams is a well-known beer from Boston, and you can find it at many local bars and restaurants in Woburn.

  • Cranberry Juice. Massachusetts is the largest producer of cranberries in the United States, and you can find locally made cranberry juice at many stores and restaurants in Woburn.

Where to Stay in Woburn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Crowne Plaza Boston-Woburn. This luxurious hotel has an indoor pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.

  • Red Roof Inn PLUS+ Boston-Woburn. This budget-friendly hotel is pet-friendly and offers free breakfast and parking.

What to Buy in Woburn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Woburn has plenty of unique shops and boutiques where you can buy local souvenirs and products. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Woburn Farmers’ Market. The Woburn Farmers’ Market is open on Saturdays from June to October, and offers fresh produce, local crafts, and artisanal food products.

  • Duck Mill. This historic mill building has been converted into a shopping center, with shops selling everything from antiques to clothing.

  • Krause’s Homemade Candy. Treat yourself to some delicious homemade candy from Krause’s, a local candy shop with a variety of sweet treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Woburn

Woburn is a friendly and welcoming city, with a rich history and culture. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, but can expect a warm reception from locals. Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation and learn more about the city and its people.

Best Time to Visit Woburn

The best time to visit Woburn is during the fall or spring, when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. If you’re interested in skiing or winter sports, the winter months can also be a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Woburn

Do:

  • Take a tour of the historical homes and museums in the city.
  • Explore the beautiful natural scenery and go hiking or biking.
  • Try local specialties like New England Clam Chowder.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Woburn is generally a safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Woburn:

Safety in Woburn

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas or parks.

  • Keep your valuables stored securely and out of sight.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and report any suspicious behavior to the authorities.

Health in Woburn

  • Stay hydrated during the hot summer months.

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.

  • Speak with your healthcare provider before traveling to Woburn to ensure that you are up-to-date on all necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Woburn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Woburn is conveniently located just 10 miles north of Boston, and is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. Here are some transportation options and logistics for traveling to Woburn:

Airports Near Woburn

  • Boston Logan International Airport. Located just 11 miles south of Woburn, Boston Logan International Airport is the closest airport to the city.

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport. Located about 50 miles north of Woburn, Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is another option for travelers.

Bus Stations Near Woburn

  • Anderson Regional Transportation Center. This bus station is located in Woburn and offers buses to many destinations in the area.

  • Boston South Station. Located in downtown Boston, Boston South Station offers buses to many destinations in Massachusetts and beyond.

Other Options

  • Car Rental. Renting a car is a great way to explore the city and surrounding areas.

  • Train. Amtrak and the MBTA both offer train service to Woburn.

How to Get Around in Woburn

Woburn is a small city, and most destinations are easily accessible on foot, bike, or car. Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are also available for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Woburn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Woburn is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Boston. Just 10 miles south of Woburn, Boston is a must-visit destination with its rich history, great food and drink scene, and beautiful architecture.

  • Salem. Located about 20 miles north of Woburn, Salem is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of museums, shops, and restaurants.

  • Cape Cod. Take a day trip to Cape Cod and enjoy the beautiful beaches, lighthouses, and seafood.
