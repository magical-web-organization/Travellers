Introduction to Somerville

Tucked away in the heart of Greater Boston, Somerville is a charming city that boasts a unique combination of historical and modern attractions. Home to a diverse community, the city offers a plethora of things to see and do for all travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Somerville

From museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and delicious culinary experiences, Somerville has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Somerville Museum: This quaint museum offers exhibits on the city’s rich history, culture, and art.

  • Prospect Hill Monument: Climb to the top of this monument to get a stunning view of the Boston skyline.

  • Powder House Square: This historical spot was built in 1704 and is now a popular meeting place in Somerville.

Natural Attractions of Somerville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Foss Park: Located in the heart of the city, this park offers picnic areas, a playground, and walking trails.

  • Mystic River Reservation: This beautiful park is situated on the Mystic River and offers walking trails, kayaking, and hiking.

  • Alewife Brook Reservation: Home to a diverse ecosystem, this park is perfect for nature enthusiasts and birdwatchers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somerville

  • Somerville Community Path: This 2.5 mile path is perfect for a scenic bike ride or jog through the city.

  • Mystic River Greenway: This 6-mile long trail runs along the Mystic River and offers stunning views of the city and nature.

  • Middlesex Fells Reservation: This beautiful park has over 100 miles of trails and is ideal for hiking and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Somerville: Activities and Tours

  • ArtBeat Festival: Held annually in July, this festival celebrates the arts with live music, theater, dance, and arts & crafts booths.

  • Aeronaut Brewing Company: Check out this popular brewery that offers tours and tastings of their craft beer.

  • Somerville Theatre: This charming theater screens both old and new films and is a must-see for film buffs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Somerville

  • Day 1: Explore the city by renting a bike and biking along the Somerville Community Path. End the day with a pint at Aeronaut Brewing Company.

  • Day 2: Spend the day in nature at Mystic River Reservation and enjoy hiking or kayaking. End the day with dinner at Sarma, a popular Middle Eastern restaurant.

  • Day 3: Visit the Somerville Museum and learn about the city’s rich history. Afterwards, visit Prospect Hill Monument for a breathtaking view of the city.

  • Day 4: Take a tour of the Harvard campus located nearby. Afterwards, head to the Brattle Theatre and catch an indie movie.

  • Day 5: Stroll through the beautiful Foss Park and have a picnic lunch. Afterwards, visit the popular Union Square Farmer’s Market.

  • Day 6: Explore the Middlesex Fells Reservation with a guided hike followed by a late lunch at Redbones BBQ.

  • Day 7: Spend the day exploring Harvard Square and shopping at the local boutiques. End the day with a delicious meal at Oleana, a Mediterranean restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Somerville

Being a melting pot of cultures, Somerville boasts a diverse culinary scene with a variety of restaurants and eateries. Here are some dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fluffernutter Sandwich: This iconic sandwich is made with marshmallow cream and peanut butter, and is a favorite amongst locals.

  • Lobster Roll: Feast on a buttery lobster roll at one of the seafood restaurants in the area.

  • Cannoli: Treat yourself to a classic Italian cannoli from a local bakery.

Local Beverages

  • Sam Adams Beer: Boston’s famous brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are popular amongst beer enthusiasts.

  • Harpoon Brewery: This local brewery is known for its beers and tours.

  • Iced Coffee: Bostonians take their coffee seriously, and iced coffee is a must-try during the summer months.

Where to Stay in Somerville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly to luxury stays, Somerville has many accommodation options that cater to the different needs of travelers. Some popular choices include:

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly option with all the necessary amenities.

  • The Row Hotel: A boutique hotel located in a renovated firehouse in the heart of Union Square.

  • The Inn at Harvard: Located near Harvard Square, this hotel is an ideal choice for those wanting to explore the university campus.

What to Buy in Somerville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring back home, Somerville has a variety of options:

  • Taza Chocolate: This local chocolatier offers unique and delicious chocolate bars that are made from scratch.

  • Union Square Farmer’s Market: Every Saturday, the market offers a variety of fresh produce, handmade crafts, and baked goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Somerville

Somerville has a diverse community and values inclusivity and equality. While the city is known to be progressive and liberal, it’s important to respect the cultural differences and customs of residents.

Best Time to Visit Somerville

The best time to visit Somerville is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are at their peak. The city also offers a variety of festivals and events during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somerville

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take a walking tour of the city to learn about its rich history.

  • Be respectful of the cultural differences of the residents.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.

  • Ignore the rules and regulations of public parks and facilities.

  • Engage in disrespectful behavior towards residents and visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Somerville

Somerville is generally considered a safe city, but it’s always important to take necessary precautions when traveling. Keep a watchful eye on your personal belongings and valuables, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Somerville

Somerville has a range of medical facilities and hospitals for emergency and non-emergency healthcare needs. It’s important to have adequate travel insurance and be aware of any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Somerville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some options to get to Somerville:

Airports Near Somerville

  • Boston Logan International Airport: This airport is just 6 miles away from Somerville and offers a wide range of domestic and international flights.

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: This airport is located in New Hampshire and is approximately 44 miles away from Somerville.

Bus Stations Near Somerville

  • Amtrak: offers daily train service to Boston’s South Station from various cities across the U.S.

  • Greyhound: offers bus service to Boston’s South Station and Bus Terminal from various cities across the U.S.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: If driving is your preferred mode of transportation, car rentals are readily available at the airport and in the city.

  • Uber/Lyft: These ride-hailing services are widely available in the city.

How to Get Around in Somerville

Somerville is a walkable city and also offers public transportation options such as bus and train services. Biking is also a popular mode of transportation with bike rental stations located throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Somerville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cambridge: Home to Harvard University and MIT, Cambridge is just a stone’s throw away from Somerville and offers plenty of attractions to explore.

  • Boston: Head to the historic city of Boston and explore famous landmarks such as the Freedom Trail, Fenway Park, and the Boston Public Library.

  • Cape Cod: Enjoy a day trip to the scenic Cape Cod and explore the lighthouses, beaches, and seafood restaurants.

Conclusion

Somerville may be a small city, but it offers a host of activities and attractions to explore. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or nature lover, Somerville has something to offer for everyone. With this guide in hand, you’ll surely enjoy your trip to the heart of Greater Boston!
