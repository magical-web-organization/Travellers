Introduction to Brookline

Brookline is a charming and affluent town located just outside of Boston, Massachusetts. This picturesque community boasts diverse historical and cultural landmarks, delightful dining options, and endless outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Brookline

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • John F. Kennedy National Historic Site: This is the childhood home of the 35th US President, and the site where he launched his political career. Visitors can explore the restored house, watch films, and see exhibits about JFK’s life and legacy.
  • Frederick Law Olmsted National Historic Site: This is the former home and office of Frederick Law Olmsted, the father of American landscape architecture. The house now serves as a museum dedicated to his life and work.
  • Larz Anderson Auto Museum: This museum showcases over 40 vintage cars and highlights American auto design and culture from 1899 to the present day.
  • The Coolidge Corner Theatre: This historic theater has been operating since 1933 and features independent, foreign and classic films, as well as lectures and educational events.

Natural Attractions of Brookline: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Larz Anderson Park: This 64-acre park boasts beautiful gardens, walking paths, and a large pond. Visitors can picnic, play sports, and explore the park’s three museums, including the aforementioned auto museum.
  • Brookline Reservoir: This body of water located in the heart of Brookline offers a beautiful spot for jogging, walking, and picnicking.
  • Amory Park: This lovely urban-green space offers benches from which to enjoy views, walking paths, and a small playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brookline

Brookline is home to numerous parks and trails that allow visitors to enjoy the outdoors while getting some exercise.

  • The Emerald Necklace: This is a 1,100-acre chain of parkland that starts in Boston and runs through Brookline. The park has over 10 miles of biking trails, paths for walking or jogging, and picnic areas.
  • The Hammond Pond Reservation: This 180-acre reservation is a peaceful retreat with miles of walking and hiking trails. There is also a small pond great for fishing.

Best Things to Do in Brookline: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Walking and Trolley Tours: Visitors can take guided tours of Brookline’s sights and landmarks, including JFK’s birthplace and the Olmsted House.
  • Brookline Food and Culture Walking Tour: This two-hour tour offers the chance to taste some of the best local cuisine while learning about the area’s history and culture.
  • Fenway Park Tours: Sports fans can visit this legendary baseball stadium and learn about its historic past by taking a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brookline

Here is a suggested itinerary for visitors planning a week-long stay in Brookline.

Day 1 - Visit the John F. Kennedy National Historic Site and the Larz Anderson Auto Museum.

Day 2 - Explore the Olmsted National Historic Site and spend the afternoon in the Emerald Necklace.

Day 3 - Take a trolley tour of Brookline’s cultural landmarks and stop for lunch at one of the gourmet food trucks that frequent the area.

Day 4 - Spend the day outdoors, hiking trails in the Hammond Pond Reservation or lounging in Larz Anderson Park.

Day 5 - Visit the Coolidge Corner Theater to watch an indie or foreign film, then head for dinner at one of the many popular eateries on Harvard Street.

Day 6 - Take a day trip into Boston and visit some of the city’s famous sights, such as the Freedom Trail or the USS Constitution Museum.

Day 7 - Attend a ballgame at Fenway Park and enjoy a beer and a Fenway Frank hotdog.

Food and Drink Scene of Brookline

Must-Try Dishes

Brookline has a diverse and exciting food scene, with a range of restaurants and cafes serving everything from classic American burgers to international cuisine. Here are some local specialties worth a try:

  • Bagels from Kupel’s Bakery: This family bakery has been making delicious bagels since 1940. Try the sesame or everything bagels with cream cheese.
  • Chicken and Rice from Pasha: This Middle Eastern restaurant serves up a juicy chicken and rice dish packed with flavors such as garlic, turmeric, and sumac.
  • Burger from the Regal Beagle: This neighborhood bar has been serving delicious burgers made with local grass-fed beef since 2011.
  • Ice Cream at JP Licks: This local chain of ice cream shops has been a Brookline staple for over 30 years. Try any of their unique flavors like Blueberry Cheesecake or Sweet Cream Biscuits and Jam.

Local Beverages

Brookline has many local cafes and bars where visitors can enjoy a variety of beverages. Here are some must-try drinks:

  • Coffee from KooKoo Cafe: This local café is famous for its smooth espresso and lattes.
  • Craft Beer from Brookline’s Publick House: This bar has a wide selection of beers, many of which are local. Try the Trillium Brewing Company’s brew.
  • Tea from Tea Bar by EvyTea: This local tea shop features a selection of handcrafted, organic loose-leaf teas.

Where to Stay in Brookline: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brookline offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some popular choices:

  • The Inn at Brookline: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Brookline, and features a fitness center, an on-site restaurant, and a lounge.
  • The Coolidge Corner Guest House: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the vibrant Coolidge Corner neighborhood and is walking distance from many great dining options.
  • The New England Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is great for travelers on a budget. It’s located in a Victorian house in the heart of Brookline’s Coolidge Corner.

What to Buy in Brookline: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vintage Collectibles: Brookline is home to several antique stores, which are great for finding unique souvenirs or gifts. Try Brookline Antiques and The Coolidge Corner Theater Store.
  • Local Foods: Take home some of the local specialty foods mentioned above, such as bagels from Kupel’s Bakery or chicken and rice from Pasha.
  • Brookline memorabilia: Look for Brookline postcards, maps or posters to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Brookline

Brookline is known for its active community and strong commitment to education. Many families move here for it’s top-performing public school system. Brookline residents take pride in their diversity and welcoming spirit. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local community and to explore the cultural landmarks that make Brookline unique.

Best Time to Visit Brookline

The best time to visit Brookline is from May to October when the weather is warm and mostly sunny, and when most outdoor attractions and parks are open. Fall is especially scenic due to the colorful leaves of the trees around town. Alternatively, a visit in the winter months can afford a chance to enjoy snow sports and the Brookline parks under their lovely snow covers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brookline

Do:

  • Do visit Brookline’s historic sites, parks, and beaches.
  • Do try out local cuisine and food culture, such as bagels from Kupel’s Bakery or chicken and rice from Pasha.
  • Do explore the community and enjoy the relaxed atmosphere.

Don’t:

  • Don’t neglect to explore the natural settings, like the famed Emerald Necklace, during your trip.
  • Don’t take away souvenirs that could hurt the environment or damaged historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brookline

Brookline is a safe and friendly town, making violent crime uncommon. Visitors should exercise common sense and caution, particularly in the evening hours.

Health in Brookline

Brookline boasts several high-quality medical facilities, such as the Longwood Medical Area, Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, and Boston Children’s Hospital.

Getting to Brookline: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brookline

The nearest airport to Brookline is Boston Logan International Airport, located just 6 miles from Brookline and offers several public transportation options.

Bus Stations Near Brookline

Several bus companies commonly travel to Boston, making it easy to get to Brookline on a bus. Greyhound and Peter Pan both have terminals located in Boston. Once there, several bus and train lines serve Brookline’s neighborhoods.

Other Options

Brookline is a walking and biking friendly town, and visitors are encouraged to walk, bike or use the town’s public transportation, like the T subway train or the numerous buses with varying routes, to move around.

How to Get Around in Brookline

Walking or biking are popular ways to get around in Brookline, and it’s quite easy to explore the town on foot or by bike. Additionally, Brookline’s public transportation system is also efficient and accessible for visitors to use throughout their stay. The subway, bus lines and taxis are all viable options.

Where to Go Next from Brookline: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Boston: Take a quick trip into the City of Boston and explore everything it has to offer, from the Freedom Trail to Fenway Park.
  • Cape Cod: Cape Cod, known for its quaint villages, sandy beaches and whale watching opportunities. Excursions to Boston Harbor and Cape Cod can be easily booked with any number of local tourist sites, taxis or shuttles.
  • Newport: A short drive away, Newport, Rhode Island is a beautiful coastal town that offers plenty of history, nature, and culture.

Conclusion

Brookline is an idyllic town with lots to offer for visitors looking for a little bit of everything: from history to cuisine, stunning natural beauty, and culture. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can feel confident planning a trip to this lovely community without missing any of the highlights. Get ready to experience the beauty and charm of Brookline!
