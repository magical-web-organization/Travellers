Introduction to Malden

Malden is a bustling city in Greater Boston, located in Eastern Massachusetts. It is a fantastic destination for tourists who love to explore urban areas but also enjoy natural attractions. Malden is a diverse city with rich history and culture, reflected in the variety of eateries, galleries, and boutiques in its neighborhoods.

With so much to see and do in Malden, here is a comprehensive guide to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Malden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Malden has numerous museums, art galleries, and historical places where tourists can discover the city’s diverse cultural history.

  • Malden Public Library: A historic library with an extensive collection of books, newspapers, and magazines that offer insight into the city’s past.
  • Beebe Estate: A beautiful historical mansion that was built in 1828 and now hosts events and exhibitions
  • The Fellsmere Park: A public park that is a perfect place for picnics and a walk. The park has an amphitheater that hosts concerts and music events
  • Malden Historical Society: A nonprofit organization that promotes the city’s history and runs events to raise awareness about the city’s preservation and conservation.

Natural Attractions of Malden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Malden has several natural attractions that offer visitors an opportunity to experience the beauty of nature.

  • Middlesex Fells Reservation: A vast forested nature reserve that has an array of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and trail running
  • Fellsmere Pond: A popular spot for fishing and boating. The calm waters of the pond are a perfect escape from the urban hustle and bustle.
  • Waitt’s Mount Park: A public park that offers stunning views over Boston’s skyline and is an excellent place for a picnic or a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malden

For outdoor enthusiasts, Malden is a paradise. With its vast natural reserve, hiking and biking offer visitors a great way to explore and discover the city’s stunning natural environment.

  • Skyline Trail: A 7.5-mile trail that traverses the rocky terrain of the Middlesex Fells Reservation and offers panoramic views of the Boston skyline
  • Tri-Mountain Loop Trail: A 2.8-mile trail that loops around three peaks in the Middlesex Fells Reservation, offering stunning views of the lake and a challenging trek over rugged terrain
  • Bell Rock Reservation Trail: An easy and flat trail that offers visitors a peaceful walk through lush forest. It is accessible year-round, and often used by families with pets.

Best Things to Do in Malden: Activities and Tours

Malden offers visitors an array of activities that will keep them engaged and entertained.

  • Malden Farmers Market: A perfect spot to sample fresh local produce, baked goods, and handicrafts of the region. The market is open on Saturdays between June and October
  • Boda Borg: An interactive gaming facility where visitors are placed in physical and mental challenges that allow them to see if they can conquer them.
  • Boston Tall Ship Adventure: Sail on historic ship, Roseway, and experience New England’s scenic coast in an interactive tour from Boston’s Harbor

Week-Long Itinerary for Malden

Malden is a city that offers visitors a vast range of activities that cater to diverse interests. Visitors who plan on spending a week in Malden can break their itinerary down into the following:

Day 1: Explore Malden by taking a walk through the city, visiting local boutiques and eateries. Day 2: Discover Malden’s history by visiting Beebe Estate, Malden Public Library, and the Malden Historical Society Day 3: Hike the Skyline Trail in the Middlesex Fells Reservation Day 4: Take a boat on Fellsmere Pond for fishing and boating Day 5: Take part in the interactive challenges at Boda Borg Day 6: Visit the Fellsmere Park and have a picnic and see a concert Day 7: Relax by taking a leisurely walk in the Waitt’s Mount Park

Food and Drink of Malden

Malden offers guests a range of food and drinks that represent the diversity of the city.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken and Rice Guys: This food truck specializes in middle-eastern style grilled chicken with rice and an array of sauces, it won’t disappoint.
  • All Seasons Table: A restaurant that offers an exciting and unique blend of Chinese, Japanese, and Thai cuisine
  • Pisa Pizza: Try their famous Buffalo Chicken Pizza, it is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Idle Hands Craft Ales: Offering a range of delicious locally brewed beers, this is one of Malden’s best-kept secrets
  • Mystic Brewery: A place to taste delicious craft beers with a New England twist
  • Kung Fu Tea: A Taiwanese-style bubble tea shop that is perfect for a refreshing pick-me-up on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Malden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malden offers visitors a wide range of accommodations that cater to diverse interests and budgets.

  • Four Points by Sheraton Boston Logan Airport Revere: This hotel offers guests a comfortable stay that is located near Revere and Boston’s skyline. The hotel is also close to Logan International Airport.
  • The Row Hotel at Assembly Square: A boutique hotel that is perfect for visitors who want to experience luxury and comfort during their stay. The hotel is located near stores, including the Assembly Row Mall, offering plenty of options for shopping and dining.
  • Boston YMA: A youth hostel that is cheap and perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Malden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Malden has several local gift shops where visitors can buy authentic souvenirs and local products that represent the city’s culture and heritage.

Some of the best places to shop include:

  • Tedeschi’s Florist & Gifts: A shop that offers visitors a range of beautiful locally made gifts
  • Downtown CBD: A shop that sells locally produced clothing, accessories, and home décor
  • Sweet Mimi’s Candy Shop: A candy store that offers delicious varieties of chocolates and gummies.

Local Culture and Customs of Malden

Malden is a diverse city that embraces and celebrates cultural and ethnic diversity. Tourists will find friendly locals who will welcome them with their famous New England hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Malden

The best time to visit Malden is between the months of May and October when the weather is milder, and visitors can fully experience the city’s outdoor attractions and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malden

Do:

  • Explore Malden’s diverse neighborhoods
  • Visit the Fellsmere Park for a picnic
  • Use public transport such as the subway to avoid traffic and save time
  • Venture out of Malden to explore nearby attractions such as Boston and Salem.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas or parks
  • Be rude to locals, the New England hospitality is contagious
  • Ignore the possibility of getting lost when hiking trails in Middlesex Fells Reservation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malden

Malden is a safe city to visit with low crime rates. However, visitors should take appropriate precautions to ensure their safety.

  • Avoid unsafe areas, specifically at night time
  • Lock vehicles and room doors
  • Carry a light for night-time adventures and hikes

Health in Malden

There are several medical facilities, such as Malden’s Lawrence Memorial Hospital, in Malden that are well-equipped to attend to any emergency medical needs that may arise during a visit.

Getting to Malden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Malden is easily accessible and well connected to other cities in Massachusetts and the rest of the USA.

Airports Near Malden

Logan International Airport is located approximately 8 miles from Malden and is easily accessible by taxi, bus or train.

Bus Stations Near Malden

Malden has several bus stations that are located within close proximity of the city center.

Other Options

For visitors who prefer driving, Malden is easily accessible from the surrounding areas via the Interstate 93 freeway.

How to Get Around in Malden

Malden is compact and offers several modes of transportation, including public transport and taxis.

The subway is cost-effective and efficient and will get visitors around the city with ease. Visitors who prefer to explore the city on foot can expect a pleasant experience with plenty of footpaths and sidewalks.

Where to Go Next from Malden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations that visitors can explore during their stay in Malden include:

  • Boston: A vibrant and scenic city with rich cultural and historical heritage
  • Salem: A city with a unique blend of historic landmarks and modern attractions.
  • Lexington and Concord: A great place to discover American Revolution history.

Conclusion

Malden is a vibrant and diverse city that has something for everyone. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, this guide offers visitors a comprehensive overview of the city. Follow this guide to ensure a memorable trip to Malden.
