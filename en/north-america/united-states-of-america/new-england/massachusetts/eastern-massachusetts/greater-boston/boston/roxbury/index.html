Introduction to Roxbury

Roxbury is a neighborhood in Boston, situated in Greater Boston, Eastern Massachusetts. It is one of the oldest and most diverse neighborhoods in Boston. Roxbury has a rich cultural and historical background dating back to the 17th century. The neighborhood offers a unique blend of historic sites, outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and vibrant dining options. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an adventure lover, there’s something for everyone to explore in Roxbury.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roxbury has a rich cultural heritage, and you can witness it through its museums and historical places. The following are the main cultural attractions one should check out while in Roxbury:

Massachusetts Historical Society

The Massachusetts Historical Society has an extensive collection of artifacts, manuscripts, and books that showcase the state’s history. It is the oldest historical society in the United States, and exhibits include American paintings, photographs, historical prints, and other significant historical items.

The Shirley-Eustis House

The Shirley-Eustis House is a restored Georgian-style mansion built-in 1747, showcasing the lifestyle of wealthy inhabitants during the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries. The museum presents period furnishings, artwork, and decorative pieces, and there is a garden with an orchard on the grounds.

The Mapparium

The Mary Baker Eddy Library has an extraordinary three-story stained-glass globe created in 1935, called The Mapparium. It provides a multimedia show on world geography, history, and world issues and is a world-renowned attraction.

Roxbury Heritage State Park

Roxbury Heritage State Park is a great attraction for history buffs, located on the former site of the Roxbury carpet mill. It provides tours to several historic buildings, including the Dillaway-Thomas House and the Eliot Burying Ground.

Natural Attractions of Roxbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Roxbury has several natural attractions, including parks, gardens, and wildlife reservations to explore. Here are some of the outdoor activities in Roxbury:

Franklin Park Zoo

Franklin Park Zoo covers 72 acres and showcases over 220 species. The zoo has several exhibits, including the Tropical Forest with lowland gorillas and Caribbean flamingos. The children’s zoo provides interactive features such as farm animals, a petting zoo, and an animatronic dinosaur exhibit.

Southwest Corridor Park

Southwest Corridor Park is a beautiful park that runs from the South End to Forest Hills. The park consists of bike paths, tennis courts, playgrounds, and dog parks. With an excellent view of the skyline, it is one of the most beautiful parks in Boston.

Highland Park

Highland Park offers an exceptional trail through 82 acres of greenery that shows a panoramic view of Boston skyline. It also has an arboretum, playgrounds, and picnic tables where you can relax and enjoy outdoor activities with family and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roxbury

Roxbury provides a great opportunity for hiking and biking enthusiasts to explore the scenic beauty of its parks. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Roxbury:

Southwest Corridor Park Trail

The bike trail stretches over a distance of four miles from Ruggles Street to Forest Hills. It passes through beautiful landscapes, and you can enjoy a picnic stop or a visit to the nearby urban farms.

The Emerald Necklace Trail

The Emerald Necklace Trail covers 1.5 miles of off-road biking trails that let you explore the greenery and beauty of the city wildlife. The trail links Back Bay Fens, Riverway, and Olmsted Park with Jamaica Pond.

Best Things to Do in Roxbury: Activities and Tours

Roxbury offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to people of varying interests. The following are some of the best things to do in Roxbury:

Black Heritage Trail Tours

The Black Heritage Trail tour is an exciting introduction to the significant people and places in African American history. The trail is a meaningful historical journey through the stories and struggles of Roxbury’s black heritage.

Boston Chocolate Tasting Tour

Take a fun-filled Boston chocolate tour and taste the best sweets and chocolates available in the city. The tour provides a chocolate history lesson, chocolate making, and chocolate tastings of nine different flavors at three locations.

Local Culture and Customs of Roxbury

Bostonians value respect, politeness, and honesty. It is essential to use the terms ‘Please’ and ‘Thank You’ in communication. Tipping is expected in Boston, even if the service was not exceptional.

Best Time to Visit Roxbury

The best time to visit Roxbury is from May to October. The weather is mild, and many outdoor activities are available. Additionally, several festivals, concerts, and other events occur during this time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roxbury

Roxbury is generally safe, and most visitors do not face any criminal risks. However, it is advisable to be cautious around areas with a high crime rate.

Health in Roxbury

Roxbury has a good health care infrastructure. Hospitals like Boston Medical Centre and prominent medical centres are located nearby. It is also essential to wear sunscreen and carry water while out and about.

Where to Go Next from Roxbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fenway Park

Fenway Park is one of the must-visit attractions in Boston, located a few miles from Roxbury. It is the oldest baseball stadium in the USA, home to Boston Red Sox baseball games. Visitors can take a tour of the park and explore its history.

Boston Common

Boston Common is a beautiful park located in downtown Boston, a few miles from Roxbury. The park offers several activities, including ice skating, yoga classes and events like Shakespeare in the park.
