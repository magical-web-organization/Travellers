Introduction to Charlestown

Located in Greater Boston, Charlestown is a quaint neighborhood that offers a glimpse into the early days of America. Home to numerous historical sites, museums, and outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in this charming area.

List of Places to Visit in Charlestown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bunker Hill Monument: This impressive granite obelisk stands 221 feet tall and commemorates the first major battle of the American Revolution. Visitors can climb the 294 steps to the top of the monument for panoramic views of Boston.
  • USS Constitution Museum: Dedicated to the history of the USS Constitution, the world’s oldest commissioned warship afloat.
  • The Warren Tavern: A historic tavern frequented by American revolutionaries such as Paul Revere and George Washington.
  • The Paul Revere House: The former home of Paul Revere is now a museum that showcases life during the Revolutionary War era.
  • The Charlestown Navy Yard: The former home of the Boston Naval Shipyard and now hosts a variety of attractions including the USS Constitution Museum, the Boston National Historical Park Visitor Center, and the Charlestown Navy Yard waterfront.

Natural Attractions of Charlestown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bunker Hill: A historic park that offers great views of the city and a perfect place for a picnic.
  • The Charles River: Offers kayaking and boating opportunities and has a well-maintained park system that runs along the river.
  • The Charlestown Community Garden: A charming community garden that is open to the public and a great place to relax and enjoy the greenery.
  • The Freedom Trail: A 2.5-mile walking trail that showcases Boston’s rich history and chronicles the American Revolution. The trail passes through Charlestown and leads visitors to various historical sites and attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charlestown

Charlestown is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. The Freedom Trail is perfect for those who want a leisurely walk through history, while Bunker Hill offers a slightly more elevated experience. Additionally, the Charles River offers plenty of opportunities for kayaking and boating.

Best Things to Do in Charlestown: Activities and Tours

  • The Freedom Trail: A 2.5-mile walking trail that showcases Boston’s rich history and chronicles the American Revolution. The trail passes through Charlestown and leads visitors to various historical sites and attractions.
  • Boston Sunset Cruise: A relaxing way to enjoy the scenic views of Boston Harbor, complete with a champagne toast and light snacks.
  • Upham’s Corner Farmers Market: A seasonal farmers market that offers fresh produce, local food, and live entertainment.
  • Plough and Stars: A traditional Irish pub that features live music and is a popular spot among locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Charlestown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England dish featuring freshly picked lobster mixed with mayonnaise and served in a bun.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, and bacon, and served with oyster crackers.
  • Boston Cream Pie: A fluffy sponge cake filled with pastry cream and topped with chocolate sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sam Adams Beer: A Boston-based beer brand that is available in a range of styles and flavors.
  • Harpoon Brewery: A local brewery that offers a wide variety of beers, including seasonal and limited edition options.
  • Dunkin’ Donuts Coffee: A Massachusetts-based chain of coffee shops that serves hot and iced coffee as well as specialty drinks and doughnuts.

Where to Stay in Charlestown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Charlestown offers a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • The Residence Inn by Marriott Boston Harbor on Tudor Wharf: A hotel that features studio and one-bedroom suites with fully equipped kitchens and complimentary breakfast.
  • The AC Hotel Boston North: A modern hotel that offers stylish guest rooms, a fitness center, and a rooftop bar.
  • The Bunker Hill Bed and Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse that is walking distance to many historical sites in Charlestown.

What to Buy in Charlestown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Charlestown has a variety of souvenir shops where visitors can purchase unique and authentic gifts to bring back home.

  • Old Sully’s Proprietor’s Reserve: A whiskey created by the Charlestown-based Sully family.
  • Charlestown Candle Company: All-natural, hand-poured candles made in small batches in Charlestown.
  • Bunker Hill Cheese Company: Gourmet cheese shop that offers a variety of artisanal products made from local ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Charlestown

Charlestown is a friendly and welcoming community, with a strong sense of pride in its history and culture. It is important to respect the area’s historical significance and to be mindful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Charlestown

The best time to visit Charlestown is from April through October, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The fall season is especially beautiful when the trees change color and the air is crisp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charlestown

Do:

  • Visit the historical sites and museums in Charlestown.
  • Take a stroll along the harborwalk.
  • Indulge in local delicacies like lobster rolls and clam chowder.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage historical sites.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing while visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Be disrespectful of local traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charlestown

Charlestown is a safe community, but visitors should always take basic precautions like being aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables out of sight. Tipping is customary in the service industry, so it’s important to be prepared to leave a small tip for services such as dining, or tours.

Health in Charlestown

Charlestown has several pharmacies and urgent care clinics. For emergencies and serious health issues, the Massachusetts General Hospital is a well-regarded facility with many good reviews from patients.

Getting to Charlestown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Charlestown

The closest airport to Charlestown is the Boston Logan International Airport. This airport is approximately 15 minutes by car or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Charlestown

The two bus terminals near Charlestown are the South Station Bus Terminal and the Alewife Bus Station.

Other Options

Charlestown is easily accessible by car, bike, or by utilizing ride-sharing apps. Additionally, there are several water taxis available to travel to destinations around the city.

How to Get Around in Charlestown

Charlestown can easily be explored on foot or by utilizing public transportation. The MBTA (Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority) offers bus and subway routes throughout the city and the surrounding areas. Biking and ride-sharing apps are also popular options.

Where to Go Next from Charlestown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boston, Cambridge, and Somerville are nearby destinations that offer plenty of attractions and activities to explore. The coastal communities of Marblehead and Gloucester are also just a short drive away and offer beautiful views of the ocean.

Conclusion

Charlestown is a charming neighborhood in Boston with plenty of historical sites, outdoor activities, and delicious food options. It’s a great destination for visitors who want to experience the rich history and culture of the New England area. With this comprehensive tour guide, travelers can easily plan their trip to Charlestown and create lasting memories.
