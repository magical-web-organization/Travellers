Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Watertown boasts a rich history that is well represented in its museums and historical sites. Here are a few must-see attractions for history buffs:

The Perkins School for the Blind

Founded in 1829, The Perkins School for the Blind is among the oldest schools for the blind in the United States and is a leader in educating those with visual and hearing disabilities. The campus is home to the Howe Building, which houses a museum that showcases the history of the school.

The Moses Greely Parker House

Built in 1873, The Moses Greely Parker House is a Victorian-style mansion that was once the home of one of Watertown’s most prominent families. Today, the house serves as a museum and a venue for art exhibitions.

The Edmund Fowle House and Museum

The Edmund Fowle House and Museum is a historic home built in 1730 and is notable for its role in the American Revolution. It was used by the Massachusetts Provincial Congress as its headquarters from April to July 1775.

The Armenian Library and Museum of America

The Armenian Library and Museum of America offers a unique perspective on Armenian history and culture. The museum’s exhibits feature pieces of artwork, manuscripts, and artifacts that showcase the Armenian diaspora.

Natural Attractions of Watertown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Watertown’s natural beauty offers tourists a chance to explore and enjoy the outdoors. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Watertown:

Charles River Greenway

The Charles River Greenway is a scenic path that runs along the Charles River and offers visitors an opportunity to explore the riverside scenery and enjoy the outdoors. The greenway loops around Watertown Square and is perfect for a relaxing stroll or a bike ride.

Mount Auburn Cemetery

Mount Auburn Cemetery is a historic cemetery that features beautiful gardens, majestic trees, and magnificent landscapes. The cemetery is more than just a burial ground and is also a popular destination for birdwatching and outdoor enthusiasts.
