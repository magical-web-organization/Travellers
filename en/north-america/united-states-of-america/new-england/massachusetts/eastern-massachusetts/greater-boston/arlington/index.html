Introduction to Arlington

Arlington is a charming town located in eastern Massachusetts, just six miles northwest of downtown Boston. Known for its picturesque neighborhoods, rich history, vibrant local culture, and stunning natural beauty, Arlington has been a popular tourist destination for years.

Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in history, explore the outdoors, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Arlington. In this travel guide, we’ve put together a comprehensive list of the best places to visit, activities to do, and things to see in Arlington, so you can plan your trip with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Arlington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arlington Historical Museum

Located in the historic Jason Russell House, the Arlington Historical Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in local history. The museum features exhibits on the town’s colonial and Revolutionary War-era past, as well as displays on local industry and culture.

Cyrus E. Dallin Art Museum

Dedicated to the works of celebrated American sculptor Cyrus E. Dallin, the Cyrus E. Dallin Art Museum is a stunning showcase of the artist’s work. The museum features a collection of over 100 sculptures, including many of Dallin’s most famous works.

Park Circle Historic District

Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, the Park Circle Historic District is a beautiful residential area that boasts an impressive collection of well-preserved Victorian homes. Visitors can take a self-guided walking tour of the area to admire the stunning architecture and learn about the history of the town.

Natural Attractions of Arlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Menotomy Rocks Park

Located on the banks of the Mystic River, Menotomy Rocks Park is a popular gathering place for locals and visitors alike. The park covers over 30 acres and offers a variety of recreational opportunities, including hiking trails, picnic areas, and sports fields.

Arlington Great Meadows

Arlington Great Meadows is a stunning nature preserve that covers over 180 acres of wetlands, fields, and forests. Visitors can explore the area’s numerous trails, observe a variety of local wildlife, and enjoy the peace and quiet of nature.

Spy Pond

Located just a stone’s throw from downtown Arlington, Spy Pond is a tranquil retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors can go boating, fishing, or simply relax by the water’s edge and enjoy the stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arlington

Alewife Brook Reservation

Located just a few minutes from downtown Arlington, Alewife Brook Reservation is a beautiful natural area that features over 120 acres of wetlands, ponds, and forests. The park’s trails are perfect for hiking, jogging, and biking, and offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Minuteman Bikeway

The Minuteman Bikeway is a paved trail that stretches over 11 miles from Cambridge to Bedford, passing right through Arlington along the way. The trail is perfect for biking, jogging, or simply taking a leisurely stroll, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Arlington: Activities and Tours

Dinner and a Movie

Arlington’s Capitol Theatre hosts regular movie screenings along with a menu that features classic American fare.

Arlington Farmers Market

Open throughout the growing season, the Arlington Farmers Market boasts a wide variety of fresh, locally sourced produce, baked goods, and crafts. Visitors can sample fresh vegetables, indulge in artisanal cheeses, and shop for unique handmade gifts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arlington

Day 1: Start your trip by exploring the Arlington Historical Museum and learning about the town’s colonial past. Afterward, head to the Park Circle Historic District for a self-guided walking tour of the area’s stunning Victorian architecture.

Day 2: Spend a day outdoors by exploring the trails and natural beauty of Arlington Great Meadows and Spy Pond.

Day 3: Take a leisurely bike ride down the Minuteman Bikeway, stopping off in Lexington to explore the historic Battle Green and visit the Lexington Green Historical Society.

Day 4: Take a seat at the Capitol Theatre and enjoy a classic American meal while watching a movie.

Day 5: Give your feet a break and explore the Mystic Valley Parkway via your car.

Day 6: Unwind with a visit to the Cyrus E. Dallin Art Museum, where you can admire the works of this celebrated American sculptor.

Day 7: End your trip with a visit to the Arlington Farmers Market, where you can sample fresh local produce and shop for unique handmade gifts.

Food and Drink Scene of Arlington

Must-Try Dishes

Buffalo Chicken Pizza at Za

Za is known for its delicious, crispy crust pizza and they make mouth-watering buffalo chicken pizza. Locals rave about it.

Juicy Lucy at The Local Fare

A treat of a burger from The Local Fare awaits you with the Juicy Lucy - fried patty stuffed with American cheese along with some tater tots, makes for a great meal.

Local Beverages

Mystic Brewery

Come for the beer, stay for the scenery. Mystic Brewery is tucked away down a long alley, making it a hidden gem of a spot for sipping delicious brews.

Where to Stay in Arlington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Apple Tree Inn

Located a stone’s throw from downtown Arlington, the Apple Tree Inn is a charming bed and breakfast that offers cozy rooms, a complimentary breakfast, and easy access to the town’s many attractions.

Mystic Hotel

Just a short drive from downtown Arlington, the Mystic Hotel offers spacious rooms, an indoor swimming pool, and easy access to the Mystic Valley Parkway and other local attractions.

What to Buy in Arlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Book Rack

Arlington’s Book Rack offers a vast selection of old and new books for all interests. Grab a few and take them home to enjoy.

The Artful Heart Gallery

The Artful Heart Gallery is home to a wide range of unique and handcrafted gifts, from jewelry and pottery to paintings and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Arlington

Arlington is a friendly and welcoming town, where visitors are always made to feel at home. One of Arlington’s most important annual events is the Arlington Town Day festival, which takes place every September and features food, music, and fun for the whole family.

Best Time to Visit Arlington

Arlington is a beautiful destination year-round, but the best time of the year to visit is during the fall, when the leaves turn to fiery oranges and yellows, and the air is crisp and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arlington

Do:

  • Explore the local history by visiting the Arlington Historical Museum and the Park Circle Historic District.
  • Take a stroll around Spy Pond and enjoy the natural beauty of Arlington.
  • Attend the annual Arlington Town Day festival in September.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural areas of Arlington. It’s important to maintain the beauty of these natural areas for generations to come.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arlington

Arlington is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Arlington

Arlington has a wide range of medical facilities, including several hospitals and clinics, and visitors can access quality healthcare if needed.

Getting to Arlington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arlington

Boston Logan International Airport is the closest airport to Arlington, just a short drive away.

Bus Stations Near Arlington

Greyhound Bus Lines and Peter Pan Bus Lines both offer service to Boston, which is just a short drive away from Arlington.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Arlington by taking the subway from Boston, or by driving via Interstate 95.

How to Get Around in Arlington

Arlington is a small town and is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use local bus service to get around the town and outlying areas.

Where to Go Next from Arlington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boston

Just six miles away from Arlington, Boston is a vibrant city that’s known for its historic landmarks, stunning architecture, and rich cultural scene.

Concord

Located just a short drive from Arlington, Concord is a charming New England town that’s famous for its literary heritage, including the writings of Henry David Thoreau and Ralph Waldo Emerson.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, local culture, or simply a relaxing retreat, Arlington has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip with ease and make the most of your time in this beautiful New England town.
