Introduction to Reading

Located in Greater Boston, Eastern Massachusetts, Reading is a small town that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. With its picturesque streets, charming neighborhoods, and natural beauty, Reading is a destination that offers something for everyone.

Whether you’re looking to soak in the local culture, explore the great outdoors, or indulge in delicious food and drinks, Reading has it all. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best of what Reading has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Reading

Here are some of the top places to visit in Reading:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Parker Tavern: This historic tavern was built in 1694 and is one of the oldest buildings in Reading. It offers a glimpse into the town’s rich history and is open for tours.
  • Reading Public Library: Originally built in 1884, the Reading Public Library is a stunning example of Richardsonian Romanesque architecture and houses a collection of over 200,000 volumes.
  • Pleasant Street Historic District: This preserved neighborhood features homes from the 18th and 19th centuries and is a perfect example of New England architecture.

Natural Attractions of Reading: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Birch Meadow: This park features a lake, playground, and walking trails. It’s the perfect spot for a picnic or an afternoon hike.
  • Killam-Mackenzie Farm: A working farm that has been in operation for over 200 years, Killam-Mackenzie Farm offers hayrides, a corn maze, and other family-friendly activities.
  • Rahanis Park: With its baseball fields, tennis courts, and playgrounds, Rahanis Park is a hub for outdoor recreation in Reading.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reading

Reading offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking. Some of the top options include the Ipswich River Wildlife Sanctuary, which features over 12 miles of trails, and the Town Forest, which is a popular spot for mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Reading: Activities and Tours

  • Reading Fall Street Faire: This annual event features live music, food vendors, and local artisans selling their wares.
  • Reading Lions Club Car Show: This car show, held each August, features classic cars from all eras.
  • Walk through Downtown Reading: Stroll through the quaint downtown area and browse local shops and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Reading

Day 1: Explore the historical sites and museums in Reading, including Parker Tavern and the Reading Public Library.

Day 2: Spend time outdoors at Birch Meadow, with a picnic or a hike.

Day 3: Take a hayride and other family-friendly activities at Killam-Mackenzie Farm.

Day 4: Play some sports and enjoy the playgrounds at Rahanis Park.

Day 5: Enjoy the local culture with a stroll through Downtown Reading and try some of the local food and drinks.

Day 6: Go on a bike ride through the Town Forest or one of Reading’s other scenic bike routes.

Day 7: Catch a show or concert at the Parker Middle School Auditorium.

Local Culture and Customs of Reading

Reading is known for its love of sports, with many residents avid fans of the Red Sox, the Bruins, and the Patriots. The town is also home to a thriving arts community, with local theaters and musical performances.

Best Time to Visit Reading

The best time to visit Reading is during the fall, when the town comes alive with vibrant foliage and fun fall events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reading

Do:

  • Try the local seafood and other delicious dishes at the town’s restaurants.
  • Take a stroll through Downtown Reading and check out the local shops.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Reading’s parks and trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any natural habitats while exploring the outdoors.
  • Ignore the town’s rich history and cultural attractions.

Getting to Reading: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Reading

Logan International Airport is the closest airport to Reading, located just 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Reading

The Lowell Regional Transit Authority offers bus service to Reading from nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to rent a car or take a taxi or ride-share service to get around Reading and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Reading

The town is small enough to navigate on foot or by bike, but visitors can also opt to rent a car or use ride-share or taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Reading: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider from Reading, including:

  • Salem, MA: Just a short drive from Reading, Salem is known for its rich history and witch trials.
  • Boston, MA: Just a quick train or Uber ride away, Boston offers endless entertainment options, including museums, sports, and dining.

Conclusion

Reading is a charming New England town that offers something for everyone, from natural beauty and outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a week-long vacation, there’s plenty to explore and enjoy in Reading, Massachusetts.
