Introduction to Beverly

Nestled on the coast of Massachusetts, Beverly is a charming town with a unique mix of New England history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture. It’s located in the North Shore region and is a popular destination for tourists seeking a relaxing getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Beverly

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cabot - a beautifully restored performing arts theater that features movies, live music, comedy shows, and more.
  • The Salem Witch Museum - located just 15 minutes from Beverly, this museum offers an in-depth look at the Salem Witch Trials.
  • The Beverly Historical Society & Museum - documents and preserves the rich history of Beverly, Massachusetts.
  • Endicott College’s Rose Performance Hall - an iconic venue for musical performances in the town.

Natural Attractions of Beverly: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Independence Park - a stunning oceanfront park that’s perfect for picnics, relaxing, or strolling.
  • Lynch Park - a beautiful park with rose gardens, a beach, tennis courts, and playgrounds.
  • Bass Haven Beach - a popular beach where visitors can swim, fish, or just sunbathe.
  • Beverly Commons - a vast open space ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beverly

  • Danvers Rail Trail - a 4.5-mile trail that passes through Beverly and connects to Danvers.
  • Beverly Commons - a 200-acre nature reserve with over 10 miles of trails, perfect for hiking, walking, and biking.

Best Things to Do in Beverly: Activities and Tours

  • The Beverly Farmer’s Market - open from June to October, this market features locally grown produce, baked goods, and artisanal products.
  • A in the Game Dining Escape Room - an interactive game where you have to solve puzzles to get out of the room within a given time frame.
  • North Shore Music Theatre - a classic theater that showcases Broadway shows, musicals, and concerts.

Food and Drink Scene of Beverly

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll - a New England summer classic served with chips and a pickle
  • Steamed Clams - a local specialty with a unique flavor
  • Chowder - another New England classic, creamy and filling

Local Beverages

  • Ipswich Ale Brewery - one of Massachusetts’ original craft breweries, it offers beer lovers a place to indulge in unique brews.
  • The Cottage - famous for their cocktail menu and live music.

Where to Stay in Beverly: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Wylie Inn and Conference Center at Endicott College - situated on the oceanside and surrounded by verdant greenery, this inn offers an idyllic escape.
  • The Cabot Lodge - located right in downtown Beverly, this sophisticated lodge is ideal for those seeking a central location.
  • Harbor Light Inn - a cozy inn near the ocean with an in-house spa and top-rated on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Beverly: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beverly Salt Co. - a gourmet salt company that makes sea salt from the local waters.
  • Beverly Farms Candy Company - this artisanal candy store offers unique flavors with old-school charm.

Local Culture and Customs of Beverly

  • The community is known for being friendly and welcoming to visitors.
  • Locals take pride in their rich history and unique traditions.

Best Time to Visit Beverly

The best time to visit Beverly is between May and September. The summer months are ideal for outdoor activities, like swimming, hiking, and biking, while fall offers beautiful foliage and a quieter town. Winter is also an option for those who wish to ski and snowboard.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beverly

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take a moment to soak in the natural beauty of the town.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the natural attractions.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beverly

Beverly is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always best to practice general safety measures such as locking up your belongings and avoiding unknown areas at night.

Health in Beverly

Beverly is home to Beverly Hospital, a full-service community hospital offering a variety of medical care services. Medical tourists can rely on the hospital for emergency care and other medical needs.

Getting to Beverly: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beverly

  • Boston Logan International Airport - 20 miles south of Beverly, is the closest major airport.

Bus Stations Near Beverly

  • Beverly Depot - This commuter rail station offers access to the North Shore area, including Salem, Ipswich, and Rockport.

Other Options

  • Driving: The town is located off of Routes 1A and 127, both of which are connected to major highways.
  • Uber/Lyft is available in Beverly

How to Get Around in Beverly

  • Car rentals and taxi services are available in the town.
  • The Beverly Depot rail station offers commuter rail services.

Where to Go Next from Beverly: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Salem, Massachusetts - home to the Salem Witch Museum, is just a 15-minute drive from Beverly.
  • Gloucester, Massachusetts - a charming fishing town ideal for seafood lovers and home to several historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Beverly is a picturesque destination that has it all - history, stunning natural scenery, and lively culture. It’s a must-visit town for anyone looking for relaxation, outdoor adventures, or a combination of both. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Beverly, MA.
