Introduction to Gloucester

Nestled on the rocky coastline of Cape Ann in Massachusetts, Gloucester is a quintessential New England town known for its fishing heritage, stunning natural beauty, and rich culture. Founded in 1623, Gloucester has evolved into an eclectic and vibrant community where visitors can experience the history and charm of an old-world fishing village while enjoying the modern amenities of a seaside resort town.

List of Places to Visit in Gloucester

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s culture and history or seeking outdoor adventure, Gloucester offers many opportunities for visitors to experience New England’s natural beauty and rich heritage.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cape Ann Museum: Learn about the town’s cultural and artistic heritage at this museum.
  • Hammond Castle Museum: Tour the medieval-style castle built by inventor John Hays Hammond Jr.
  • Gloucester Fishermen’s Memorial: Honor Gloucester’s fishing legacy at this monument to lost fishermen.
  • Sargent House Museum: Discover the rich history of Gloucester’s colonial era at this restored 18th-century mansion.

Natural Attractions of Gloucester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Good Harbor Beach: Enjoy a day on the beach, with a view of the Twin Lights of Thacher Island.
  • Halibut Point State Park: Hike the trails and take in the breathtaking views of the rocky coastline.
  • Stage Fort Park: Explore the historic fort and picnic in the park while taking in the views of Gloucester Harbor.
  • Annisquam Lighthouse: Take a scenic drive through the scenic village of Annisquam to this historic lighthouse.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gloucester

  • Dogtown Common: Explore the abandoned settlement and walk the winding trails through this expansive forest.
  • Halibut Point Reservation: Hike the wooded paths to the stunning rocky coastline.
  • Cape Ann Trail Stewards: Hike or bike the many trails around Cape Ann, including the Essex Greenbelt.

Best Things to Do in Gloucester: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tours: See whales, dolphins, and other sea life on one of the many whale-watching tours available in Gloucester.
  • Schooner Cruises: Take a romantic sail on a historic schooner schooner and enjoy the stunning sunset.
  • Art Walk: Explore the town’s artistic offerings during the monthly self-guided art walk.
  • Fishing Charters: Charter a boat and try your hand at fishing for lobster, cod, or tuna in the waters off Gloucester.

Food and Drink Scene of Gloucester

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: Enjoy fresh lobster in a bun with a traditional New England recipe.
  • Clam Chowder: Sample the town’s famous clam chowder, a creamy soup filled with fresh clams and potatoes.
  • Fish and Chips: Keep it simple and try the classic British-American dish that Gloucester is famous for.
  • Fried Calamari: Try the fresh calamari fried to perfection and served with marinara sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Cape Ann Brewing Company: Taste the local brews and enjoy live music and food trucks on the patio.
  • Ryan & Wood Distilleries: Take a tour and sample the handcrafted spirits made from locally sourced ingredients.
  • Turtle Alley Chocolates: Grab a cup of hot cocoa, an espresso, or a piece of homemade chocolate at this local establishment.

Where to Stay in Gloucester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Beauport Hotel: Enjoy stunning views and comfortable accommodations at this upscale oceanfront hotel.
  • Cape Ann Motor Inn: Stay right on the beach at this simple, budget-friendly motel.
  • Addison Choate Inn: Relax in a cozy room or suite in this elegant, historic inn.

What to Buy in Gloucester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lobster Memorabilia: Take home anything from lobster-themed kitchen accessories to t-shirts, mugs, and hats.
  • Fishermen’s Wives Bracelets: Buy a bracelet crafted by the local fishermen’s wives and support the town’s fishing community.
  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Browse local artisan shops for one-of-a-kind pieces made from sea glass found on Gloucester’s beaches.

Local Culture and Customs of Gloucester

  • Fishing heritage: Learn about Gloucester’s proud fishing legacy and the challenges faced by local fishermen today.
  • Gloucester Schooner Festival: Join the Labor Day weekend festivities and watch schooners race across the harbor.
  • St. Peter’s Fiesta: Celebrate the town’s patron saint each June with a series of events, including a carnival, parade, and seine boat race.

Best Time to Visit Gloucester

The best time to visit Gloucester is during the summer months, from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town comes alive with festivals and events. However, visiting in the off-season can be a peaceful and intimate experience, with lower rates on accommodations and fewer crowds to contend with.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gloucester

Do:

  • Take a whale-watching trip
  • Visit beaches like Good Harbor and Wingaersheek
  • Shop on Main Street in the quaint shops and galleries
  • Try the seafood at one of the town’s many seafood restaurants

Don’t:

  • Touch live lobsters on display in town
  • Fish without a license
  • Litter on the beach or in town
  • Ignore parking signs and regulations

Safety and Health

Safety in Gloucester

Gloucester is a relatively safe and welcoming town. However, visitors should exercise caution and avoid leaving valuables unattended in vehicles or on the beach. It is also important to respect the ocean and the power of the waves. Consult lifeguards and beach officials for the latest beach conditions.

Health in Gloucester

Gloucester offers several pharmacies and medical centers, including Addison Gilbert Hospital, for routine or emergency care. Visitors should also bring sunscreen and bug spray and be mindful of ticks when hiking in wooded areas.

Getting to Gloucester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gloucester

  • Boston Logan International Airport: (BOS) Located about 37 miles away from Cape Ann, this airport offers direct flights to major US and international cities.

Bus Stations Near Gloucester

  • MBTA Gloucester Station: (GLO) Located in downtown Gloucester and offering commuter rail service from Boston.
  • Greyhound: Located in nearby Boston, this bus station offers connections to several other major cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available at Logan International Airport or in downtown Boston. Visitors driving to Gloucester will find several parking lots conveniently located throughout downtown Gloucester and around the town’s major attractions.

How to Get Around in Gloucester

Gloucester is a relatively small town that is easy to navigate by foot or bike. For longer distances, public transportation is available with the MBTA and Cape Ann Transportation Authority. Car rental services are also available for those who prefer to have their own transportation.

Where to Go Next from Gloucester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Salem: Only 20 miles away from Gloucester, Salem is famous for its history of witchcraft, museums, and quaint shops.
  • Boston: A short 45-minute drive, Boston is a must-visit destination for history, culture, and entertainment.
  • Rockport: This charming seaside town is located only 3 miles from Gloucester and offers beautiful scenery and a bustling arts community.

Conclusion

Gloucester offers an unforgettable experience for those seeking history, culture, and natural beauty. From exploring the town’s fishing heritage to relaxing on the beach and enjoying fresh seafood, Gloucester is the perfect destination for a New England getaway. Whether you’re traveling solo, with family, or with a group of friends, Gloucester has something for everyone.
