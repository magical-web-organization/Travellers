Introduction to Ipswich

Ipswich is a charming town located in the North Shore region of Eastern Massachusetts. Founded in 1634, Ipswich has a rich history that can be explored through its well-preserved colonial homes, historic sites, and museums. The town is also known for its beautiful natural scenery, including sandy beaches, marshes, and forests. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or just a relaxing getaway, Ipswich has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ipswich

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ipswich is home to several museums and historical sites that provide insight into the town’s past and culture. The Ipswich Museum is a great starting point for visitors to learn about the town’s history and view artifacts from the Ipswich area. Other museums and historical sites include:

  • Castle Hill on the Crane Estate: A historic mansion and estate with beautiful gardens and sweeping views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Choate Bridge and the Ipswich Riverwalk: An iron truss bridge that spans the Ipswich River and provides a scenic walk along the riverbanks.
  • Whipple House Museum: A colonial-era home that showcases Ipswich’s history and serves as a local history museum.
  • Old Burying Ground: A cemetery dating back to the 1600s with many notable historic figures buried there.

Natural Attractions of Ipswich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ipswich is a great place to explore the outdoors, with many parks, beaches, and trails to enjoy. Here are some natural attractions worth visiting:

  • Crane Beach: A popular beach with miles of soft sand and stunning ocean views.
  • Ipswich River Wildlife Sanctuary: A 2,000-acre nature reserve with over 12 miles of trails to explore.
  • Appleton Farms: A working farm with over 1,000 acres of fields, forests, and farmhouses to explore.
  • Bradley Palmer State Park: A 721-acre park with hiking trails, fishing spots, and picnic areas.
  • Great Marsh: A vast salt marsh habitat that is home to a variety of rare and endangered species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ipswich

Ipswich is a great place for hiking and biking, with many trails and paths to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • The Ipswich Riverwalk: A leisurely walk along the Ipswich River that passes through the heart of Ipswich.
  • The Bay Circuit Trail: A 200-mile hiking and biking trail that runs through Ipswich and connects to many other trails in the area.
  • Bradley Palmer State Park: A popular spot for hiking and mountain biking, with over 20 miles of trails to explore.
  • Willowdale State Forest: A 2,200-acre forest with miles of hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Ipswich: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Ipswich. Here are some of the best:

  • Take a guided tour of Castle Hill on the Crane Estate.
  • Experience an authentic clam bake on Crane Beach.
  • Visit one of the local farms for apple or berry picking.
  • Attend a seasonal event, such as the Ipswich Ale Harvest Fest or the Ipswich Chowderfest.
  • Enjoy a scenic boat tour of the Ipswich River and explore the local wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ipswich

If you’re planning a week-long stay in Ipswich, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Ipswich Museum and the Old Burying Ground.

Day 2: Visit Castle Hill on the Crane Estate and enjoy a tour of the mansion and gardens.

Day 3: Spend the day at Crane Beach and take a guided tour of the dunes.

Day 4: Take a bike ride along the Ipswich Riverwalk and visit the Whipple House Museum.

Day 5: Spend the day at Appleton Farms and enjoy hiking and picnicking.

Day 6: Explore the Ipswich River Wildlife Sanctuary and take a boat tour with a local guide.

Day 7: Relax with a scenic drive through the Great Marsh and enjoy the local wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Ipswich

Ipswich offers a variety of delicious dishes and drinks that visitors can try. Here are some must-try items:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ipswich Clams: A local favorite, Ipswich clams are sweet and tender, and can be enjoyed fried, steamed, or baked.
  • Lobster Rolls: A classic New England dish, made with fresh lobster meat, mayo, and sometimes celery or lemon juice.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, and sometimes bacon or other ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Ipswich Ale: A local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and pilsners.
  • Mill River Winery: A local winery that produces a variety of wines, including red, white, and rosé.

Where to Stay in Ipswich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ipswich offers several options for lodging, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • Ipswich Inn Bed and Breakfast: A charming inn with a cozy atmosphere and a central location.
  • The Inn at Castle Hill: A historic inn located on the Crane Estate, with luxurious rooms and beautiful views.
  • Essex Street Inn: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Ipswich, with modern amenities and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Ipswich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ipswich offers many souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a memory of their stay. Here are some ideas:

  • Ipswich Clams: Fresh or canned, Ipswich clams make a unique and tasty gift for foodies.
  • Local Spirits: Ipswich offers a variety of local spirits, including rum, whiskey, and gin.
  • Ipswich Pottery: Handcrafted pottery made by local artisans is a great way to support the local community.

Local Culture and Customs of Ipswich

Ipswich has a rich culture and history, which can be seen in the town’s architecture, museums, and festivals. Visitors should respect the town’s historic sites and take care not to damage or litter the local environment.

Best Time to Visit Ipswich

The best time to visit Ipswich is during the summer months, when the town is full of events and festivals, and the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s attractions during the fall and spring months, when the weather is milder and the crowds are smaller.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ipswich

Ipswich is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly community. Visitors should take normal precautions to avoid petty theft and exercise caution when swimming or boating in the local area.

Health in Ipswich

Ipswich has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should take normal precautions to avoid common illnesses and stay hydrated during hot weather.

Getting to Ipswich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ipswich is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Ipswich:

Airports Near Ipswich

  • Logan International Airport (BOS): The closest major airport, located 30 miles south of Ipswich.

Bus Stations Near Ipswich

  • Ipswich Bus Station: Located in the center of town, with connections to Boston and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Drive: Ipswich is located on the intersection of several major highways, including I-95 and US-1.
  • Train: Ipswich is served by the Newburyport/Rockport Line of the MBTA Commuter Rail.

How to Get Around in Ipswich

Ipswich is a small town that is easily walkable or biked. Visitors can also take the local bus or arrange for a taxi or car rental if needed.
