Introduction to Salisbury

Salisbury is a small coastal town located in the North Shore region of Eastern Massachusetts. It is situated on the northern border of Boston with the Atlantic Ocean to the east, Amesbury to the north, and Newburyport to the south. With its pristine beaches, historical landmarks, art and culture, and outdoor recreational opportunities, Salisbury is a fantastic destination for travelers of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Salisbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salisbury has a rich history that can be explored by visiting its various landmarks and museums. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  1. Maudslay State Park: A 19th-century estate transformed into a public park which features gardens, walking trails, and picnicking areas.
  2. Rings Island Historic District: A historic district on the island that marks one of the earliest European settlements in North America.
  3. Salisbury Point Ghost Trail: A historic walking trail that showcases the town’s rich colonial and maritime history.
  4. Custom House Maritime Museum: A museum that features exhibits on the region’s maritime heritage, including shipbuilding and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Salisbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salisbury’s natural beauty is a big draw for travelers. Here are some of the must-see natural attractions:

  1. Salisbury Beach State Reservation: A long and beautiful stretch of pristine beach perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and surfing.
  2. Sandy Point State Reserve: A natural beach reserve with hiking trails and birdwatching opportunities.
  3. Plum Island Wildlife Refuge: A protected refuge and home to over 800 species of birds, mammals, and plants.
  4. Merrimack River: A great spot for fishing, kayaking, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salisbury

Salisbury offers several hiking and biking trails for travelers who enjoy outdoor adventures. Some of the most popular trails include:

  1. Old Eastern Marsh Trail: A 1.4-mile trail located on the salt marshes near the Merrimack River.
  2. Rings Island Trail: A 2.5-mile trail that meanders through the woods and along the river’s edge.
  3. Maudslay State Park Trail: A 4.5-mile trail that winds through the park’s gardens, fields, and along the marsh.

Best Things to Do in Salisbury: Activities and Tours

Salisbury has a range of exciting activities and tours to offer travelers. Here are some ideas to add to your itinerary:

  1. Take a whale watching tour: Whale watching tours from Newburyport offer a unique experience to observe whales and other marine life in their natural habitat.
  2. Visit the Salisbury Beach Boardwalk: The boardwalk offers amusement rides, games, and great eats.
  3. Kayaking on the Merrimack River: Rent a kayak and paddle through the river’s many channels and islands.
  4. Take a sunset dinner cruise: Relax while enjoying dinner and drinks on a sunset cruise on the Merrimack River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salisbury

A week-long itinerary for Salisbury could include:

  • Day 1: Visit Salisbury Beach State Reservation and the Salisbury Beach Boardwalk.
  • Day 2: Explore the historical district and custom house maritime museum.
  • Day 3: Spend the day hiking or biking on one of Salisbury’s beautiful trails.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Newburyport and explore its historic waterfront.
  • Day 5: Take a whale watching tour or enjoy a sunset dinner cruise on the Merrimack River.
  • Day 6: Visit Plum Island Wildlife Refuge and Sandy Point State Reserve.
  • Day 7: Relax and take in the beauty of Maudslay State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Salisbury

Must-Try Dishes

Salisbury offers a range of delicious cuisine and local delicacies. Some must-try dishes include:

  1. Clam chowder: A classic New England dish that can be found in many local establishments.
  2. Lobster roll: Fresh lobster served on a buttered roll, a must-try when visiting the area.
  3. Fried clams: Freshly breaded and fried in oil, served with tartar sauce, and freshly squeezed lemon.
  4. Steamer clams: Served with broth for dipping, these clams are a popular local delicacy.

Local Beverages

Salisbury and the surrounding area offer several local beverages worth trying. Some popular options include:

  1. Ipswich Ale: A local beer featuring a range of craft brews, including a staple IPA.
  2. Turkey Shore Distilleries: Tour the distillery and sample locally sourced and produced spirits.
  3. Newburyport Brewing Company: Another local brewery with a range of seasonal and specialty beers.

Where to Stay in Salisbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Salisbury offers several options for accommodation, including:

  1. Blue Boar Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast located near Salisbury Beach.
  2. Garrison Inn Boutique Hotel: A beautifully restored historic Inn located in the nearby town of Newburyport.
  3. Holiday Inn Express: A convenient and affordable hotel located near the highway.

What to Buy in Salisbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salisbury is an excellent destination to find unique local products and souvenirs. Some popular options to bring home include:

  1. Sea Glass Jewelry: Local artisans create beautiful jewelry pieces out of recycled sea glass found on local beaches.
  2. Artwork by Local Artists: Salisbury and surrounding areas are home to many talented artists, and their work can be purchased in local galleries.
  3. Locally Made Sauces and Spices: Take home the flavors of Salisbury with locally made sauces and spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Salisbury

Salisbury is a welcoming and friendly community, and visitors should respect its local culture and customs. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress appropriately for the season and weather.
  • Observe social etiquette and treat locals and other travelers with respect.
  • Follow all beach rules and regulations.
  • Be aware of the tides and currents when swimming or boating.

Best Time to Visit Salisbury

Salisbury is a great destination year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months when temperatures are warm, and outdoor activities are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salisbury

Do:

  • Do take a walk along the Salisbury Beach State Reservation.
  • Do try the local seafood and clam chowder.
  • Do plan a visit to one of the local breweries or distilleries.
  • Do hike or bike along one of the many trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beach.
  • Don’t skip the historical landmarks and museums.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray.
  • Don’t miss out on the local arts scene.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salisbury

Salisbury is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should still exercise caution and take necessary safety measures, such as:

  • Use caution when swimming, particularly in ocean waters.
  • Follow all beach rules and regulations for safe swimming.
  • Be aware of the tides and currents when swimming and boating.

Health in Salisbury

Visitors to Salisbury should take the necessary precautions to ensure their health. Remember to:

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect from the sun’s rays.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of fluids.
  • Bring bug spray to protect against ticks and other insects.

Getting to Salisbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salisbury

The closest airport to Salisbury is Boston Logan International Airport, located approximately 45 miles south of Salisbury. Other options include Manchester-Boston Regional Airport in New Hampshire or Portland International Jetport in Maine.

Bus Stations Near Salisbury

There is no bus station located in Salisbury, but visitors can take advantage of nearby stations in Amesbury or Newburyport.

Other Options

Salisbury can also be reached by train via the Boston North Station, which is located in downtown Boston.

How to Get Around in Salisbury

Salisbury is a small town, and many of the attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also take advantage of local bus services or rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Salisbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Salisbury is surrounded by many nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  1. Newburyport: A nearby historic town with a bustling waterfront and many shops and restaurants.
  2. Plum Island: A beautiful island only a few miles away known for its wildlife refuge.
  3. Parker River National Wildlife Refuge: A scenic wildlife refuge located around 13 miles south of Salisbury that offers a chance to observe local wildlife.
  4. Salem: A historic town that is famous for the Salem witch trials and the Peabody Essex Museum.

Conclusion

Salisbury is a fantastic destination for travelers looking to enjoy a blend of rich history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Plan your next trip to Salisbury and experience all that this vibrant coastal town has to offer.
