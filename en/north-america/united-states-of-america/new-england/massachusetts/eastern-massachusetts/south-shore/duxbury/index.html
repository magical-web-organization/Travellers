Introduction to Duxbury

Nestled in South Shore, Eastern Massachusetts, Duxbury is a picturesque coastal town that boasts a rich history, scenic views, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. If you’re planning a trip to Massachusetts and are looking for a charming, off-the-beaten-path destination, Duxbury should definitely be on your list.

List of Places to Visit in Duxbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

History buffs should definitely spend some time exploring these culturally important attractions in Duxbury:

  • The Nathaniel Winsor Jr. House: This historic 18th-century home now serves as a museum, showcasing the life and times of Nathaniel Winsor Jr., a wealthy merchant and politician from Duxbury.
  • King Caesar House: Built in the 1800s, the King Caesar House is a stunning example of Federal-style architecture. It now houses the Duxbury Rural and Historical Society, and offers guided tours of the property and surrounding gardens.
  • The Bradford House and Garden: This historic home, dating back to the early 1700s, is a National Historic Landmark and one of the oldest homes in Duxbury. Visitors can tour the house and gardens to learn about the history of the Bradford family and their impact on the town and surrounding region.

Natural Attractions of Duxbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love spending time outdoors, Duxbury offers plenty of natural attractions to explore:

  • Duxbury Beach: With miles of pristine sandy beach and clear blue water, Duxbury Beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
  • Myles Standish Monument State Reservation: This park is home to the iconic Myles Standish Monument, a 116-foot tall granite structure that offers stunning views of the surrounding area. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, fishing, and camping in the park.
  • Duxbury Bay: Rent a kayak or take a guided tour of Duxbury Bay to explore the beautiful coastline and spot local wildlife like seals, herons, and ospreys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Duxbury

For outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the natural beauty of Duxbury on foot or bike, here are some top routes to consider:

  • The Bay Circuit Trail: This 200-mile trail passes through Duxbury and offers hikers the chance to explore a wide variety of landscapes, from coastal wetlands to wooded forests.
  • Duxbury Beach Park: Take a leisurely bike ride down the beach or explore the park’s nature trails by foot to take in the scenic views and spot local wildlife.
  • Myles Standish State Forest: This expansive forest boasts miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for camping and fishing.

Best Things to Do in Duxbury: Activities and Tours

Looking for some fun activities and tours to enjoy during your visit to Duxbury? Here are some ideas:

  • Duxbury Farmer’s Market: Head to the Duxbury Farmer’s Market on Sundays to sample local produce, baked goods, and crafts from around the region.
  • Duxbury Wine & Spirits Tasting Room: Sample a variety of locally produced wines and spirits at this cozy tasting room in the heart of downtown Duxbury.
  • Duxbury Art Association: Check out the latest exhibitions and take an art class at the Duxbury Art Association, which features works from local artists and craftsmen.

Food and Drink Scene of Duxbury

Must-Try Dishes

Foodies won’t be disappointed with the delicious local cuisine that Duxbury has to offer. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Lobster Rolls: Fresh lobster meat piled high on a buttery, toasted roll is a classic New England dish that you won’t want to miss.
  • Clam Chowder: Duxbury clam chowder is a local specialty, made with fresh clams and potatoes in a creamy, savory broth.
  • Blueberry Muffins: Duxbury is known for its juicy, plump blueberries, which are used to make some of the best muffins around.

Local Beverages

To wash down your delicious Duxbury dishes, be sure to try some of the local beverages:

  • Craft Beer: Duxbury boasts several craft breweries, including Mayflower Brewing Company, which offers tours and tastings of their delicious brews.
  • Cape Cod Cranberry Juice: Sip on some locally made cranberry juice, a tart and refreshing beverage that is a staple in New England.
  • Wine: Duxbury Wine & Spirits offers a selection of locally produced wines that are well worth tasting.

Where to Stay in Duxbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse or a luxurious hotel room, Duxbury has plenty of options for accommodations:

  • The Duxbury House: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Duxbury and features cozy rooms that are decorated in a classic New England style.
  • The Winsor House Inn: For a more upscale experience, consider the Winsor House Inn, a charming bed and breakfast that features elegant rooms and luxurious amenities.
  • The Mirbeau Inn & Spa: Located just outside of Duxbury, the Mirbeau Inn & Spa offers a luxurious spa experience, as well as elegant guestrooms and fine dining.

What to Buy in Duxbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Duxbury without picking up some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Blueberry Jam: Made from fresh Duxbury blueberries, this jam is a delicious way to bring a taste of Duxbury home with you.
  • Handmade Pottery: Check out the Duxbury Art Association’s gift shop for some beautiful, handmade pottery and other artisanal crafts.
  • Duxbury Bay Maritime Products: From nautical-themed t-shirts to locally made soaps and candles, Duxbury Bay Maritime Products has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Duxbury

Duxbury is a town that values its history and traditions, and visitors should be aware of some of the local customs:

  • Etiquette: When visiting Duxbury, be sure to dress modestly and politely interact with locals. Respectful behavior is highly valued in the town.
  • History: Duxbury has a rich history, and visitors should take time to explore the many museums and historic sites that showcase this heritage.
  • Sports: Duxbury residents love their sports, and visitors should try to catch a local game or match to get a taste of the town’s passion for athletics.

Best Time to Visit Duxbury

The best time to visit Duxbury depends on your preferences and interests. For ideal beach weather, visit during the summer months. Fall is a great time to explore the town’s beautiful foliage and enjoy outdoor activities in comfortably cool weather. Winter brings snow and chilly temperatures, but also the chance to enjoy cozy indoor activities like wine tastings and museum visits.

Do’s and Don’ts of Duxbury

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Duxbury by taking advantage of the many hiking, biking, and kayaking options in the area.
  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the seafood dishes that Duxbury is known for.
  • Do respect the town’s history and traditions by visiting its many museums and historic sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment while enjoying outdoor activities in Duxbury.
  • Don’t skip the chance to explore the nearby towns and attractions that are just a short drive away from Duxbury.
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing for the season, as the weather in New England can be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Duxbury

Duxbury is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming community. Still, visitors should take care to follow standard safety precautions like staying aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Duxbury

Duxbury has a high-quality healthcare system, and visitors should have no trouble finding medical care if needed. It is always a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you on your trip, and to stay hydrated and well-rested to stay healthy while exploring Duxbury.

Getting to Duxbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Duxbury

The closest airport to Duxbury is Logan International Airport in Boston, which is approximately a 45-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Duxbury

The Plymouth and Brockton Street Railway Company operates bus service to nearby towns like Plymouth, which is just a short drive from Duxbury.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Duxbury and the surrounding area. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, but may be less reliable in the smaller town.

How to Get Around in Duxbury

Driving is the easiest way to get around Duxbury, and visitors should consider renting a car to explore the town and its surrounding area. Biking and walking are also popular options for getting around town, especially in the summer months when the weather is pleasant.

Where to Go Next from Duxbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Duxbury makes a great base for exploring some of the nearby towns and attractions in the area, like:

  • Plymouth: Just a short drive from Duxbury, Plymouth is a historic New England town that features attractions like Plymouth Rock and the iconic Mayflower II ship replica.
  • Boston: Known for its rich cultural heritage and unique local cuisine, Boston is just a 45-minute drive from Duxbury and offers plenty of opportunities for sightseeing and exploration.
  • Cape Cod: Head south from Duxbury to explore the stunning beaches, quaint towns, and maritime history of Cape Cod.

Conclusion

Duxbury may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine that is sure to delight visitors looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Massachusetts. From hiking and kayaking to wine tasting and museum visits, there is something for everyone in Duxbury.
