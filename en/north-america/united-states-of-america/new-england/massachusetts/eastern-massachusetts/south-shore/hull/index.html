Introduction to Hull

Hull, located in South Shore in Eastern Massachusetts, is a small town with big surprises. Known as a seaside community, Hull is home to sandy beaches, breathtaking harbor views, and a rich maritime history.

From kayaking in the Hull Bay to sailing in Hull Gut, this town is made for anyone who loves the ocean. Take a trip to Hull and discover its hidden gems.

List of Places to Visit in Hull

There is no shortage of places to visit in Hull. Here’s a list of must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Revere Park: A fort built during the American Revolution to protect Boston Harbor. The park is now a popular spot for hiking, picnics with stunning waterfront views, and a historic site tour.
  • The Emily Fuller Drew Museum: A charming museum showcasing the numerous artifacts of Hull’s history and art. Make sure to visit the museum’s quaint gift shop.
  • Hull Lifesaving Museum: A museum dedicated to the United States Lifesaving Service, which was created during the 19th century to rescue stranded seafarers.

Natural Attractions of Hull: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nantasket Beach: A two-mile-long beach known for its fine sand and pristine water quality. Take a stroll on the beach or try out one of the many water activities offered here.
  • World’s End: An iconic park offering stunning views of Hingham Bay. It’s a popular place to go hiking or biking on its many trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hull

Hull has many hiking trails and bike routes to choose from. Here are some of the best routes to try out:

  • Pemberton Point Trail: A scenic trail that allows you to see some of Hull’s beautiful shorelines and salt marshes. The total distance is 1.6 miles, and it’s an easy trail.
  • The Weir River Estuary Trail: A moderate-level hike through the beautiful Weir River Estuary. The trail is 3.4 miles long and is popular among bird watchers.
  • Hull Coast Walk: A 7-mile-long walk offering breathtaking views of Hull Bay and the Boston skyline. This trail is moderately challenging.

Best Things to Do in Hull: Activities and Tours

  • Paragon Carousel: A carousel that has been entertaining visitors since 1928. Kiddos and adults alike will love this nostalgic ride.
  • Sunset Cruise from Hull Harbor: Enjoy a relaxing cruise along the Hull Harbor, sipping on a drink while admiring the stunning sunset view.
  • Nantasket Beach Mini Golf: A fun pastime for kids and adults. The mini-golf course features a Victorian theming and oceanic obstacles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hull

Day 1: Arrive in Hull, check into hotel, explore the Hull Gut for a sunset view Day 2: Rent bikes from Nantasket Kayaks and bike along the Hull Coast Walk trail. Have lunch at Barefoot Bobs, dinner at Nantasket Beach Resort and catch a live music performance. Day 3: Head out to Nantasket Beach and take surf lessons, go for drinks and appetizers at Paragon Grill. Day 4: Go on Emily Fuller Drew Museum and Hull Lifesaving Museum Tours. Have lunch at Toast or C-Note. Day 5: Hike the Weir River Estuary Trail, In the evening, take the Sunset Cruise from Hull Harbor. Day 6: Try out kayaking and paddleboarding at Hull Bay. Make a quick trip to the historic Fort Revere Park. Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a round of mini golf and a walk along the shore.

Food and Drink Scene of Hull

From seafood-shacks to fancy restaurants, Hull offers a diverse dining scene.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Clam Strip Sandwich: Fresh clam strips, breaded and fried until golden brown, then served in a soft roll with lettuce and tartar sauce, served at Schooner’s Restaurant.
  • Lobster Salad Roll: Enjoy a lobster salad mixed with light mayonnaise and served in a buttered roll at Bridgeman’s Restaurant.
  • Crab Rangoon: Crispy cream cheese and crab fried wontons at Mei Wei Restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Mayflower Brewing Company: A brewery known for its award-winning craft beer. Try out their signature “Thanksgiving Ale”.
  • Nantasket Coffee: A local coffee shop serving freshly brewed coffee, espresso drinks, and baked pastries.

Where to Stay in Hull: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hull offers different options for accommodation. Some of the most notable ones are:

  • The Nantasket Beach Resort: A luxurious resort located right on Nantasket Beach. Features a bar, spa, fitness center, and rooftop pool.
  • The Beacon Luxury Waterfront B&B: A picturesque bed and breakfast located on Bay Street. Enjoy a magnificent waterfront view and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Sunset Bay Inn: A cozy guest house located on Bay Street. Features a garden, terrace, and communal fireplace.

What to Buy in Hull: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hull T-shirts and other memorabilia from the Hull Lifesaving Museum Gift Shop.
  • A jar of homemade jam or honey from Fresh Start Farm stand at the Hull Farmers Market.
  • Sea glass jewelry and coastal-themed home decor from Hull’s Seaside Street-Made Shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Hull

Hull’s tight-knit community is known for its kindness and ingenuity. If you ever have any questions or trouble while in town, just ask a local and they’ll be happy to give you advice.

Best Time to Visit Hull

The best time to visit Hull is from late spring through early fall. During these months, visitors can fully enjoy the town’s beaches and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hull

Do:

  • Try some of the local seafood dishes, especially lobster rolls and clam chowder.
  • Visit the local farmers market and indulge in fresh fruits, vegetables, and baked goods.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities and trails.

Don’t:

  • Spoil the town’s natural beauty by littering or defacing public property.
  • Overestimate the town’s small size. There’s plenty of things to see and do, so be sure to plan your itinerary before visiting.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hull

Hull is a generally safe destination. However, visitors should be aware of water safety, as open-water swimming carries some risks. Make sure to pay attention to posted swim advisories and beach conditions.

Health in Hull

Hull has a small hospital and clinics for any medical emergency. Visitors should be aware of the COVID-19 safety protocols in place in Hull.

Getting to Hull: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hull

  • Boston Logan International Airport: 18.6 miles, 30 minutes drive away.
  • T.F. Green Airport: 58.9 miles, 70 minutes drive away.

Bus Stations Near Hull

  • Quincy Center Station: 6.8 miles, 20 minutes drive away.
  • Hingham Station: 7.2 miles, 15 minutes drive away.

How to Get Around in Hull

The best way to get around Hull is by car, bike, or by walking. There are bike and kayak rentals available in Nantasket Beach. Also, visitors can hail a taxi or ride-share service for convenience.

Where to Go Next from Hull: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plymouth, MA: A 30-minute drive from Hull. Home to Plymouth Rock, one of America’s most iconic landmarks.
  • Boston, MA: A 40-minute drive from Hull. Known as one of the country’s most historic and culturally varied cities, Boston’s famed cobblestone streets and bustling markets deserve a visit.

Conclusion

Hull may be a tiny town, but it offers an array of activities and places for visitors to enjoy. From the friendly locals to the stunning beaches, Hull delivers the quintessential New England experience. Come visit Hull and discover its charm for yourself.
