Introduction to Weymouth

Weymouth is a charming town located in South Shore, Eastern Massachusetts. It’s known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture. The town has something for everyone, from outdoor adventurers to history buffs, foodies to culture enthusiasts. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a week-long vacation, Weymouth is a great destination that you won’t want to miss.

List of Places to Visit in Weymouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Abigail Adams Birthplace - Visit the birthplace of the wife of President John Adams and mother of President John Quincy Adams, a famed patriot and women’s rights activist.
  • Old South Union Church - Founded in 1751, this church is a fine example of colonial architecture and a cauldron of over 250 years of local and national history.
  • Weymouth Historical Society Museum - Discover the town’s rich history with exhibits ranging from Wessagusset to the present, with carefully curated collections from the personal and public spheres.

Natural Attractions of Weymouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Webb Memorial State Park - A scenic park by the Hingham Bay, with views of the Boston skyline and options for boating and fishing.
  • Great Esker Park - Enjoy a leisurely stroll along a glacial esker rising 65 feet above the wetlands of Great Pond, with a mix of historic and nature-inspired trails.
  • Whitman’s Pond - A picturesque pond located in East Weymouth, featuring a beach area, picnic tables, basketball hoops, and rentals for boats and kayaks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Weymouth

  • The Quincy Bay Pathway Trail - A scenic trail along the bay, featuring views of the harbor, beaches, and downtown Boston skyline.
  • The Shipyards Trail - A 1.5-mile trail that connects the Webb Memorial State Park to the waterfront’s town landing, with stunning views of the Boston skyline, Quincy Bay, and Weymouth Fore River.
  • The Wompatuck State Park Trail - A multi-use trail with options for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, with a hidden cave and several interpretive hiking loops.

Best Things to Do in Weymouth: Activities and Tours

  • Dinosaur Trackway at Grape Island - See over 150 dinosaur tracks on an island north of Boston Harbor that served as dinosaur homes 200 million years ago.
  • Launch! Kayak Weymouth - Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the Hingham Bay, or take their guided tours around Webb Memorial State Park and Weymouth’s rich history.
  • Weymouth Landing Farmer’s Market - Explore the market with locally sourced foods, artisan crafts, and one of the best lamb gyro stands in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Weymouth

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Abigail Adams Birthplace, Old South Union Church, and snack on beach fare from the Hingham Lobster Pound.
Day 2 Biking up the Shipyards Trail to the Shipyard Brewery for an afternoon brew and some food trucks.
Day 3 Enjoy the Dinosaur Trackway, followed by a date night at The Town Wharf.
Day 4 Rent kayaks, canoes, or paddleboards from Webb’s and cool off with some popsicles.
Day 5 Shop at Weymouth Landing Farmer’s Market and head over to South Shore Living for some coastal decorations.
Day 6 Do a hike at Wompatuck State Park and enjoy the natural beauty of the park.
Day 7 Take a day trip to Boston or Plymouth, or just spend a lazy afternoon at Whitman’s Pond.

Food and Drink Scene of Weymouth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls at Hingham Lobster Pound - A staple for anyone on the South Shore, Hingham Lobster Pound’s rolls are packed full of fresh local lobster.
  • The Fried Clam Strip Dinner at Weymouth House of Pizza - A classic clam strip meal with crispy fries.
  • The Curry at Taco Loco - This Mexican restaurant has a twist - their cult favorite curries represent what happens when Mexican and Indian cuisine come together.

Local Beverages

  • Shipyard’s Beer - The brewery is located on the historic Hingham Shipyard and offers local, craft beer that epitomizes the seaside essence of Weymouth.
  • Harbor Sweets - Gourmet chocolates that are handmade in Weymouth with Swiss chocolate.

Where to Stay in Weymouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Weymouth Inn - This bed and breakfast is located in a beautiful Victorian mansion with a relaxing atmosphere.
  • The Inn at Crystal Cove - Located just outside of Weymouth in Winthrop, this hotel features beautiful accommodations and stunning views of Boston Harbor.
  • Stonecroft Country Inn - This charming Inn offers historic accommodations with modern amenities, providing a unique lodging option for those seeking to explore the south shore.

What to Buy in Weymouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Harbor Sweets chocolates - These famous handmade delicacies are a must-try.
  • Souvenirs from Weymouth Historical Society Museum - Bring home a piece of the town’s rich history with gifts from the museum’s store.
  • Saltwater taffy from Solera Candy Co. - The perfect seaside treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Weymouth

Weymouth is known for its vibrant and friendly community. Some customs include respecting the local water conservation codes, not smoking in public areas and starting conversations with strangers.

Best Time to Visit Weymouth

The best time to visit Weymouth is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and the town is buzzing with activity. However, visiting during other seasons can have its own unique charms. Fall foliage for example turns the local color palette into deep oranges and yellows.

Do’s and Don’ts of Weymouth

Do:

  • Be friendly and respectful to locals
  • Enjoy the natural surroundings
  • Try local foods and drinks
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen, especially during the summer months

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment
  • Smoke in public areas where it is prohibited
  • Park in non-permitted areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Weymouth

Weymouth is a relatively safe town, but it’s always important to take precautions when exploring a new place. Stay alert and take care when out on trails or in natural areas.

Health in Weymouth

Weymouth is home to a couple of hospitals such as South Shore Hospital or Beth Israel Deaconess Hospital, so receiving medical attention shouldn’t be a problem.

Getting to Weymouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Weymouth

The closest airport to Weymouth is Boston Logan International Airport, just a 30-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Weymouth

Buses connect Weymouth to Boston and other regional areas through Greyhound and Peter Pan.

Other Options

The Weymouth area is also accessible by car and train with MBTA’s Red Line, once at Wood Island, one can take bus 225 to get to Weymouth.

How to Get Around in Weymouth

Weymouth is a town best explored by car as it’s spread out with many neighborhoods and trails to explore. That being said, there is a public transportation system available including bus routes that connect Weymouth to Boston, Quincy and other suburbs.

Where to Go Next from Weymouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plymouth - Just a short drive south of Weymouth, Plymouth is known for its rich history and picturesque waterfront.
  • Boston - A 20-minute drive from Weymouth, Boston is a must-visit destination with plenty of things to see and do.
  • Cape Cod - South of Boston, this famous vacation destination is perfect for a day trip or longer.

Conclusion

Weymouth is a great destination for people looking for a mix of natural beauty, history, and culture. With plenty of outdoor activities, landmark attractions, and delicious cuisine, there’s always something to see and do in this charming town. Whether you’re interested in relaxing by the beach or exploring the local history, Weymouth is the perfect destination for a memorable New England adventure.
