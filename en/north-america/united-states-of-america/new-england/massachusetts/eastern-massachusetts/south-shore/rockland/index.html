Introduction to Rockland

Rockland, located in South Shore, Eastern Massachusetts, United States, is a charming small town with a vibrant history. Established in 1673, the town still bears heritage from its early days. The town boasts an array of historical landmarks and attractions, including museums, cultural centers, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore and highlight the best things to do, the top places to stay, and the best time to visit beautiful Rockland.

List of Places to Visit in Rockland

Rockland has much to offer, and here is a list of places to visit when in town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rockland Memorial Library: This stunning brick library is a popular attraction that offers numerous programs, activities, and events.
  • Rockland Historical Society: The historical society houses a collection of artifacts, documents, and photographs that depict the town’s history, including its shipbuilding heritage.
  • Union Congregational Church: Rockland’s Union Congregational Church is a stunning piece of architecture with a vibrant history.

Natural Attractions of Rockland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spring Gate Farm: This is the perfect place for a family picnic, or to take a relaxing stroll through the beautiful gardens.
  • Wompatuck State Park: Wompatuck Park is ideal for hiking, biking, and exploring nature. This park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering numerous activities and adventures, including camping.
  • Thayer Homestead: A 200-year-old colonial-era farmstead is a must-see for history buffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rockland

Rockland is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are many hiking and biking trails to explore. The Wompatuck State Park offers an extensive network of trails that are suitable for all levels. There is also the Abington Branch of the Old Colony Rail Trail, which travels through Rockland.

Best Things to Do in Rockland: Activities and Tours

  • Rockland Golf Course: This traditional golf course is popular with locals and tourists alike.
  • Paintball Bay: This is an exhilarating, fast-paced adventure for all ages.
  • Horseback riding: The Equestrian Center offers horseback riding lessons and trail rides through the gorgeous Rockland countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rockland

Day 1: Start with a visit to the Rockland Memorial Library, then head to the Union Congregational Church and Museum.

Day 2: Explore Wompatuck State Park, and enjoy a hike or bike ride.

Day 3: Take a farm tour at Spring Gate Farm, and enjoy a picnic in the beautiful gardens.

Day 4: Tour Thayer Homestead, a fascinating opportunity to explore a colonial-era farmstead.

Day 5: Discover the local art scene at the Rockland Art Center and the Studio on Webster.

Day 6: Learn about Rockland’s shipbuilding heritage at the Hull Lifesaving Museum.

Day 7: Visit the Rockland Historical Society for a deep dive into the town’s rich heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Rockland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England dish with a Rockland twist.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy soup made with local clams and vegetables.
  • Blueberry pie: A sweet pastry treat made with locally grown blueberries.

Local Beverages

  • Mayflower Brewing Company: This award-winning brewery offers a large selection of craft beers.
  • Widowmaker Brewing: Another excellent brewery, Widowmaker, offers unique and delicious brews.

Where to Stay in Rockland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rockland has several excellent accommodations options for visitors, including:

  • The Comfort Inn: This hotel has great amenities, including a heated indoor pool and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Best Western: The Best Western is a perfect option if you are looking to stay in the heart of Rockland.
  • The Holiday Inn Express: The Holiday Inn Express is an excellent option if you are looking for a more luxurious stay.

What to Buy in Rockland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery: The Studio on Webster offers exquisite handmade pottery that makes a great souvenir.
  • Specialty foods: Stock up on delicious artisanal jams, relishes, and pickles at Spring Gate Farm.

Local Culture and Customs of Rockland

Rockland is a small town with a friendly community. Residents take pride in their town’s history and are always happy to share their stories.

Best Time to Visit Rockland

The best time to visit Rockland is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, the fall foliage season from late September to early November is also a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rockland

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow all the COVID-19 guidelines and protocols.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rockland

Rockland is a relatively safe town, but visitors are advised not to leave their valuables unattended.

Health in Rockland

Rockland has several hospitals and medical facilities. The town also has a dedicated medical center, the Rockland Medical Center.

Getting to Rockland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rockland is easily accessible by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Rockland

  • Boston Logan International Airport: Boston Logan is the closest international airport to Rockland.

Bus Stations Near Rockland

  • Rockland MBTA Station: The Rockland MBTA Station offers intercity bus services to Boston.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental companies such as Enterprise Rent-a-Car, Avis, and Hertz offer services in Rockland.

How to Get Around in Rockland

The best way to get around Rockland is by car, and several car rental companies offer their services in town. Alternatively, you can also explore the town on foot or hire a bike.

Where to Go Next from Rockland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plymouth: You can take a day trip to explore Plymouth, a town famous for its rich history and beautiful waterfront.
  • Boston: Boston, the capital city of Massachusetts, is just a short drive or train ride from Rockland.

Conclusion

Rockland is a charming town filled with rich history, culture, and nature. From its outdoor adventures to its museums and historical landmarks, there is something for everyone in Rockland.
