Introduction to Bridgewater

Located in South Shore, in Eastern Massachusetts, Bridgewater is a charming town that offers visitors a mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Founded in 1656, Bridgewater boasts a rich history that’s preserved in numerous museums and historical sites that dot its landscape. If you’re looking for scenic views and outdoor activities, Bridgewater won’t disappoint either. From hiking trails to bike routes, you can enjoy the town’s natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Bridgewater

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bridgewater State University Art Gallery- this museum features works from local and national artists and regularly hosts exhibitions and events.
  • Bridgewater Historical Commission Museum- a must-visit for history buffs, this museum has several exhibits showcasing the town’s history from colonial times to present day.
  • Old Bridgewater Historical Society Museum- this museum has a vast collection of artifacts dating back to the town’s founding years, including original documents, tools, and furniture.
  • Bridgewater Veterans’ Memorial Park- this park pays tribute to Bridgewater residents who served in various wars, including the Revolutionary War and World War II.

Natural Attractions of Bridgewater: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bridgewater Town Common- the heart of Bridgewater, this park also features a gazebo and bandstand where locals host events during the summer months.
  • Hockomock Swamp Wildlife Management Area- if you’re a nature enthusiast, you’ll love exploring this 16,950-acre wilderness area, home to several hiking and biking trails, including the 9-mile long Bay Circuit Trail.
  • Sachem Rock Farm- although not open to the public, this 200-acre farm is an excellent spot for scenic views and picture taking.
  • Bridgewater Farm Supply Conservation Land- home to a 1.5-mile trail, this conservation land is a great spot for nature hikes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridgewater

  • Bay Circuit Trail- spanning 200 miles, the Bay Circuit Trail passes through Bridgewater and offers fantastic views of the town’s natural attractions.
  • Beaver Dam Brook Trail- a 3-mile hiking trail known for its scenic views and woodland setting.
  • Bridgewater Rail Trail- perfect for biking enthusiasts, this 3.2-mile-long trail runs from Bridgewater State University to the nearby town of Halifax.

Best Things to Do in Bridgewater: Activities and Tours

  • Lynnwood Cafe Music Series- if you love live music, head to Lynnwood Cafe, a local hotspot that features local and national artists.
  • Bridgewater Triangle Tour- if you’re into paranormal activity, this guided tour explores the fascinating history of the Bridgewater Triangle, a region famous for its strange events, ghosts, and unexplained occurrences.
  • Paint Nite- a fun activity for both adults and kids, Paint Nite is a guided painting class where you can create your masterpiece while sipping on your favorite drink.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bridgewater

Day 1: Visit Bridgewater Historical Commission Museum and Bridgewater State University Art Gallery.

Day 2: Explore the Hockomock Swamp Wildlife Management Area and go on a nature hike or bike ride.

Day 3: Visit Bridgewater Veterans’ Memorial Park and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 4: Take a guided Bridgewater Triangle tour.

Day 5: Go on a day trip to Plymouth and visit Plymouth Rock and the Mayflower II.

Day 6: Visit the Old Bridgewater Historical Society Museum and end the day at Lynnwood Cafe for a live music performance.

Day 7: Head to Sachem Rock Farm for scenic views and a photo opportunity.

Food and Drink Scene of Bridgewater

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza from The Bridgewater House of Pizza- known for its delicious thin-crust pizza, this is a local favorite.
  • Oncore Club at The Corner Market Deli- a classic deli sandwich that’s perfect for a quick lunch.
  • BBQ Chicken Wings from The Charlie Horse Restaurant- a fan favorite, these wings are crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside.

Local Beverages

  • Bridgewater State University’s student-produced beer- brewed by the university’s students, this beer is available at the campus pub.
  • Cranberry wine from Running Brook Vineyards- located in nearby North Dartmouth, this winery produces several award-winning fruit wines, including cranberry wine.
  • The Pulpit Rock Brewing Company’s IPA- located in nearby Decorah, The Pulpit Rock Brewing Company is a popular craft brewery that produces several excellent IPAs.

Where to Stay in Bridgewater: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bridgewater Inn- a budget-friendly motel that’s conveniently located near downtown Bridgewater.
  • Extended Stay America- this hotel is ideal for extended stays and features several amenities, including a fully equipped kitchen.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Boston/Stoughton- located in nearby Stoughton, this hotel is perfect for families and offers a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Bridgewater: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Honey from Bridgewater Farm Supply- this famous farm supplies some excellent honey that’s perfect for bringing home.
  • Bridgewater State University Merchandise- represent the town by buying apparel from the local university’s bookstore.
  • Antique Furniture from Olde Marquee Antiques & Collectables- located in nearby Raynham, this antique store has several unique pieces that are worth a visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Bridgewater

Bridgewater residents are welcoming and passionate about local events and activities. If you’re visiting during the summer months, be sure to attend the Bridgewater Summer Festival, a community event that features live music, food, and games for all ages.

Best Time to Visit Bridgewater

The best time to visit Bridgewater is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re interested in paranormal activity, October is an excellent time to visit, as the Bridgewater Triangle is said to be at its spookiest during this month.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bridgewater

Do:

  • Try the pizza from Bridgewater House of Pizza.
  • Visit the Hockomock Swamp Wildlife Management Area and go on a nature hike.
  • Participate in the Bridgewater Summer Festival.

Don’t:

  • Litter in any of the town’s parks or natural attractions.
  • Smoke in any public place. Bridgewater is a smoke-free town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bridgewater

Bridgewater is a safe town with low crime rates. However, do take the usual precautions such as not leaving valuables in your car and keeping your belongings safe while exploring the town’s natural attractions.

Health in Bridgewater

There are several health facilities located in Bridgewater. The Bridgewater Veterinary Clinic provides veterinary services to pets, while Signature Healthcare Bridgewater Primary Care provides health services to residents.

Getting to Bridgewater: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bridgewater

  • Logan International Airport- located in Boston, this is the closest international airport.
  • T.F. Green Airport- located in Warwick, Rhode Island, this airport is a great alternative to avoid the crowds at Logan airport.

Bus Stations Near Bridgewater

  • Bridgewater State University Bus Station- located on the university’s campus, this bus station services several local and regional routes.
  • Brockton Area Transit Authority- located in nearby Brockton, this regional transportation service provides bus services throughout Plymouth and Bristol counties.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars- several car rental companies operate in the town, including Hertz and Enterprise.
  • Taxi Services- several cab services operate in the town, including Bridgewater Taxi and Yellow Cab of Brockton.

How to Get Around in Bridgewater

Bridgewater is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bike. There are several bike rentals available in the town that you can take advantage of if you don’t have your bike.

Where to Go Next from Bridgewater: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plymouth- located just a 30-minute drive from Bridgewater, Plymouth is known for its history, including Plymouth Rock and the Mayflower II.
  • Boston- the state capital of Massachusetts, Boston is a bustling city that’s rich in history, culture, and food.
  • Cape Cod- a scenic peninsula located southeast of Massachusetts, Cape Cod is famous for its beaches, lighthouses, and seafood.

Conclusion

Bridgewater is a charming town with something for every type of traveler. From its cultural attractions to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, you’re sure to have a great time exploring this town. So what are you waiting for? Pack your bags and head to Bridgewater for a memorable vacation!
