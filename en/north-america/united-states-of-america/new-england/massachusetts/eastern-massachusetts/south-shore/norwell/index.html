Introduction to Norwell

Located in the South Shore region of Eastern Massachusetts, Norwell is a charming and historic town known for its picturesque landscapes and vibrant culture. Founded in 1636, Norwell was once a farming and shipbuilding community but has since transformed into a bustling suburban town with a strong sense of community.

List of Places to Visit in Norwell

If you’re planning a trip to Norwell, there’s no shortage of unique and exciting places to check out. Here are our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Norwell Historical Society Museum: Learn about Norwell’s rich history and view unique artifacts at this small, but informative, museum.
  • Glastonbury Abbey: This former Benedictine monastery is now a peaceful retreat center and offers daily tours of the grounds.
  • The First Parish Church: Visit the oldest church in town to see beautiful stained glass windows and learn about the church’s important role in Norwell’s history.

Natural Attractions of Norwell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Norwell Farms: This community farm offers a wide variety of crops and hosts events such as yoga classes and farm dinners.
  • Norwell Conservation Trust: Explore over 1000 acres of protected natural space and scenic walking trails at one of the many trust-owned properties throughout town.
  • Jacobs Pond: Fish, boat, or simply enjoy the picturesque views at this local pond, where fishing licenses are available for purchase.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Norwell

Norwell is home to several beautiful hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Norwell’s Rail Trail: Take a scenic 2.5 mile walk or bike ride along this converted former railroad bed, which offers plenty of beautiful views of the town and nearby woodlands.
  • Norwell’s Great Pond Road Trail: This trail takes you on a peaceful walk through the woods and around Norwell’s scenic Great Pond.
  • Cushing Trail: This trail features beautiful stone bridges, streams, and lush greenery on a 1.5 mile loop that makes for a great morning or afternoon hike.

Best Things to Do in Norwell: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun and unique activities to enjoy while visiting Norwell. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Norwell Kayak Rentals: Rent a kayak for a day and explore the beautiful North River, which flows through Norwell.
  • Norwell Summer Fest: Join the community for a day of live music, food, and entertainment at this annual festival in the summer months.
  • Norwell Art Association Shows: Attend one of the many art shows hosted by the Norwell Art Association and see stunning works of art from local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Norwell

Day 1: Arrive in Norwell and explore the town center, including the historic First Parish Church.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking on one of Norwell’s many trails, such as the Norwell Rail Trail or Cushing Trail.

Day 3: Rent a kayak or go fishing on the North River, taking in beautiful views of the town and surrounding nature.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Boston, only a 30-minute drive away, to explore the city’s rich history and cultural attractions.

Day 5: Enjoy a relaxing day at Norwell Farms, exploring the farm’s fields, attending a farm-to-table dinner, or taking a yoga class.

Day 6: Tour some of Norwell’s popular cultural attractions, such as the Norwell Historical Society Museum and Glastonbury Abbey.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a visit to the Norwell Art Association to view works from local artists before departing for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Norwell

Norwell has a vibrant food and drink scene with a variety of locally owned and operated restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips from The Tinker’s Son: This popular Irish pub in Norwell is known for its delicious fish and chips.
  • Lobster Roll from The Clam Box: Enjoy fresh, local seafood at this iconic Norwell spot.
  • Chicken Parmesan from Aroma Pizza and Grill: This family-owned restaurant serves up delicious Italian classics, including a mouth-watering Chicken Parmesan.

Local Beverages

  • Beer from Barrel House Z: This local brewery offers a wide variety of unique, craft beers in a cozy taproom atmosphere.
  • Cider from Stormalong Cider: Sip on refreshing, locally-made cider at Stormalong’s taproom, with beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Where to Stay in Norwell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Norwell is a small town, there are still a few excellent accommodations for visitors to choose from:

  • The James Homestead Bed and Breakfast: This historic home-turned-B&B is known for its comfortable and inviting guest rooms, and plentiful breakfast.
  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: This chain hotel offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and a fitness center, all within a short drive of Norwell’s popular attractions.

What to Buy in Norwell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fresh Produce from Norwell Farms: Take home a basket of fresh, locally-grown produce from Norwell Farms.

Local Culture and Customs of Norwell

Norwell is known for its strong sense of community and civic pride. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions while in town, including attending local events and festivals, and supporting small businesses and local farms.

Best Time to Visit Norwell

The best time to visit Norwell is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and many of Norwell’s popular outdoor attractions are in full swing. Additionally, the fall months offer beautiful foliage and plenty of seasonal harvest festivals to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Norwell

Do:

  • Attend local events and festivals, such as Norwell Summer Fest and fall harvest festivals.
  • Explore Norwell’s many natural and cultural attractions, such as the Norwell Rail Trail and the Glastonbury Abbey.
  • Support local businesses and farms by shopping at farmers markets and purchasing locally-made products.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions, such as attending church on Sundays and supporting local farms and businesses.
  • Litter or disrespect natural spaces or attractions, always leaving them better than you found them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Norwell

Norwell is a safe and friendly town, with low crime rates. Visitors should take general safety precautions while exploring Norwell’s natural spaces, such as staying on marked trails and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Norwell

Norwell has a variety of healthcare facilities, including dentist and medical offices and a nearby hospital. Visitors should also take general health precautions while traveling, such as staying hydrated, practicing good hygiene, and wearing appropriate clothing and sunscreen when outdoors.

Getting to Norwell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Norwell

The closest major airport to Norwell is Boston Logan International Airport (BOS), located approximately 25 miles away. Additionally, T.F. Green airport located in Providence, Rhode Island is only about 40 miles away and is another viable option.

Bus Stations Near Norwell

The closest major bus station to Norwell is the Brockton Area Transit Authority station, located approximately 10 miles away.

Other Options

Norwell is easily accessible by car, with multiple major highways nearby, including Interstate 95 and Route 3. Visitors can also utilize ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft, as well as traditional taxi services.

How to Get Around in Norwell

While Norwell is primarily a driving town, visitors can also explore the town’s many natural and cultural attractions by bike or on foot. Additionally, ride sharing and taxi services are available for those without a car.

Where to Go Next from Norwell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Norwell has plenty to offer, visitors may also want to explore nearby destinations such as:

  • Plymouth: Only a 20-minute drive away, Plymouth is known for its historic landmarks and beautiful waterfront area.
  • Cape Cod: Located just south of Norwell, Cape Cod offers plenty of natural attractions, charming towns, and beaches to explore.
  • Boston: Only a 30-minute drive away, Boston is a must-visit destination for history buffs and culture enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Norwell is a beautiful and charming town that offers unique cultural and outdoor experiences for visitors. With a strong sense of community and plenty of natural beauty to explore, Norwell is the perfect destination for anyone looking for a relaxing and immersive New England experience.
