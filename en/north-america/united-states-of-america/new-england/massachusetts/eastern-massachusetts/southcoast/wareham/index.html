Introduction to Wareham

Located in SouthCoast, Eastern Massachusetts, Wareham is a charming town surrounded by natural beauty. It is situated in Plymouth County and is a part of New England in the United States of America. Wareham has plenty to offer visitors, from historic sites to natural attractions to outdoor adventures. This guide will highlight the best things to do in Wareham and provide you with all the information to plan a perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Wareham

Wareham has something for everyone, whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor activities, or food. Here are the top places to visit in Wareham:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Multi-platinum songwriter, guitarist and record producer John Cephas House: Visit the historic home of John Cephas, a blues musician who lived in Wareham in the 1930s. This house is now listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Wareham Historical Society Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the town’s past by showcasing the artifacts, including furniture, clothing, and documents that date back to the 18th century.
  • Lyman Reserve: Lyman Reserve is a beautiful natural area that offers a glimpse into Wareham’s past as a Native American settlement. The reserve has several trails for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife observation.

Natural Attractions of Wareham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Onset Beach: Onset Beach is a charming stretch of sand that offers excellent swimming, fishing, and boating opportunities. If you’re interested in water sports, Onset Beach is a must-visit.
  • Cape Cod Canal: This man-made canal offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape, making it a popular spot for hiking and biking. You can also take a cruise to explore the canal or watch the boats pass by.
  • Agawam River: The Agawam River is a picturesque stream that runs through Wareham. The river is home to several species of fish and offers excellent opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wareham

Wareham is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Wrentham State Forest: This park boasts several miles of trails that take visitors through dense forests, past waterfalls, and around scenic ponds.
  • Myles Standish State Forest: Myles Standish State Forest has a vast network of trails that run through marshes, pine forests, and ponds. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, horseback riding, and camping.
  • Great Neck Wildlife Sanctuary: The Great Neck Wildlife Sanctuary has several trails that traverse through marshes, woodlands, dunes, and banks. The trails offer stunning views of the water and are ideal for birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Wareham: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Wareham has several rivers and ponds that offer perfect conditions for kayaking and canoeing. Among the popular options are Agawam River, Buzzards Bay, and Onset Bay.
  • Fishing: Wareham is known for its excellent fishing opportunities. Anglers can enjoy freshwater fishing in ponds or saltwater fishing at Onset Beach and Buzzards Bay.
  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the Cape Cod Canal, Buzzards Bay, and Vineyard Sound. Several tour operators offer both group and private tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wareham

Day 1: Visit the Wareham Historical Society Museum and take a walking tour of the town center.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking the Wrentham State Forest.

Day 3: Explore the Cape Cod Canal by bike or boat tour.

Day 4: Take a kayaking tour of the Agawam River.

Day 5: Relax at Onset Beach, swim in the ocean, and enjoy some fresh seafood.

Day 6: Visit Myles Standish State Forest for more hiking and camping.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a boat tour of Vineyard Sound and Buzzards Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Wareham

No trip to Wareham is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: With its location on Buzzards Bay, Wareham offers plenty of fresh seafood options. Lobster rolls, fried clams, and raw oysters are local favorites.
  • Cranberries: Massachusetts is known for its cranberries, and Wareham is no exception. You can try cranberry sauce, juice, or even a martini.
  • Clam Chowder: New England Clam Chowder is a creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, onions, and bacon. It is a classic New England dish and a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Wareham has several craft breweries that offer locally brewed beer. Some popular options are Buzzards Bay Brewing and Mayflower Brewing Company.
  • Cranberry Wine: Cranberry wine is a unique local beverage made with locally grown cranberries. Visit the Westport Rivers Vineyard & Winery to try it.
  • Cocktails: Try some of the creative cocktails offered at Wareham’s local bars and restaurants, featuring cranberry juice and local beer.

Where to Stay in Wareham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wareham has plenty of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s needs and budget.

  • TownePlace Suites by Marriott Wareham Buzzards Bay: This hotel is located close to Onset Beach and has an indoor pool, fitness center, and hot tub.
  • Rosewood Motel: This affordable motel is in an excellent location, and it offers rooms with air conditioning, a refrigerator, and TV.
  • The Atlantic Motel: The Atlantic Motel offers guests free Wi-Fi, parking, and air-conditioning. It is located close to many restaurants and bars.

What to Buy in Wareham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cranberry Products: Massachusetts is known for its cranberries, and Wareham is no exception. You can buy cranberry juice, sauce, or jam as souvenirs.
  • Saltwater Taffy: Saltwater taffy is a classic candy that originated along the Jersey Shore. You can find this delicious treat in many of the candy shops in Wareham.
  • Handmade Crafts: Visit Wareham’s local artisan markets to discover handmade jewelry, ceramics, and other crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Wareham

  • Festivals: Wareham has several festivals throughout the year, including the Wareham Oyster Festival and the Cranberry Harvest Festival.
  • Manners: People in Wareham are friendly and welcoming. It is customary to say “please” and “thank you” and hold doors open for others.
  • Religion: Wareham is a predominantly Christian town, but there are several other religions represented in the community.

Best Time to Visit Wareham

The best time to visit Wareham is from May to October when the weather is mild, and the outdoor attractions are open. The peak season is from June to August, so expect crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wareham

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites: Learn about Wareham’s rich history and culture.
  • Try local cuisine: Wareham has plenty of fresh seafood and locally grown produce to try.
  • Explore the outdoors: Hiking, kayaking, and swimming are popular activities in Wareham.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Wareham’s natural attractions are among its main attractions, so it’s essential to keep the town clean.
  • Forget sunscreen and bug repellent: The sun can be strong, and mosquitoes are common during the summer months.
  • Trespass: Wareham has several private properties, so it’s essential to respect the property of others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wareham

Wareham is a relatively safe town, but it is essential to take usual precautions, such as locking car doors and staying alert in unfamiliar areas. Be cautious while swimming or boating in the water and pay attention to tide changes.

Health in Wareham

Wareham has several medical facilities, including Southcoast Health Urgent Care, Beth Israel Deaconess Hospital, and Tobey Hospital. Be sure to have health insurance or travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Wareham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wareham

The nearest airport to Wareham is New Bedford Regional Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away. Boston Logan International Airport is also close by, located 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Wareham

The Bonanza Bus Lines has a stop in Wareham, connecting travelers to various destinations within the state.

Other Options

The nearest train station is in Middleborough, which is approximately 13 miles away. Several car rental companies are located in Wareham, making it a convenient option for those who prefer to drive.

How to Get Around in Wareham

The best way to get around Wareham is by car. Several car rental companies offer rental cars, or you can bring your vehicle. Biking, walking, or kayaking are also viable options for exploring the town.

Where to Go Next from Wareham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wareham is situated close to several other popular destinations, including:

  • Boston: Boston is just a short drive away from Wareham and boasts many historical sites, museums, and attractions.
  • Cape Cod: Cape Cod is known for its picturesque towns, stunning beaches, and boating opportunities.
  • Martha’s Vineyard: This picturesque island off the coast of Cape Cod is accessible by ferry and offers stunning beaches and a relaxed atmosphere.

Conclusion

Wareham is a hidden gem in Eastern Massachusetts. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or food lover, there is plenty to see and do in Wareham. This guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful town.
