Introduction to Dartmouth

Located in the Southcoast region of Eastern Massachusetts, Dartmouth is a charming town with a rich history, stunning natural surroundings, and a rich culture. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, avid shopper, or a foodie, you’ll love what Dartmouth has to offer. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with a detailed overview of the town’s top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and what to eat and drink.

List of Places to Visit in Dartmouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dartmouth has a rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore. Some of the must-see attractions include:

  • New Bedford Whaling Museum: Explore the fascinating history of the whaling industry and learn about the town’s connection to this once-thriving industry.
  • Dartmouth Heritage Trail: Take a self-guided tour through some of Dartmouth’s oldest and most historic landmarks.
  • Rotch-Jones-Duff House and Garden Museum: Discover the stunning Greek Revival architecture and rich history of one of the town’s most beautifully preserved historic homes.

Natural Attractions of Dartmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dartmouth boasts some of the most stunning natural scenery in all of Massachusetts. Here are some of the top attractions that nature lovers shouldn’t miss:

  • Dartmouth Natural Resources Trust: Hike through miles of beautiful trails, explore unspoiled forests and wetlands, and enjoy breathtaking views of the river and ocean.
  • Demarest Lloyd State Park: Relax on a quiet beach, go swimming, or explore the local wildlife in this beautiful state park.
  • Allens Pond Wildlife Sanctuary: Take a guided tour or explore the sanctuary’s many hiking trails and see some of the incredible local wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dartmouth

Dartmouth has a variety of scenic trails perfect for hiking and biking. Some of the best routes include:

  • Dartmouth Natural Resources Trust: With over 15 miles of trails, this is the perfect place for hiking or biking in Dartmouth.
  • Shining Sea Bikeway: Explore the stunning coastline of Cape Cod on this popular bike route that runs all the way from Falmouth to Woods Hole.

Best Things to Do in Dartmouth: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in shopping, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, Dartmouth has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in town:

  • Go Shopping: Browse local boutiques and artisan markets for unique souvenirs and gifts.
  • Explore the Dartmouth Waterfront: Take a romantic stroll along the boardwalk, enjoy a picnic in the park, or try your hand at fishing in the bay.
  • Take a Vineyard Tour: Visit local wineries and vineyards, sample delicious wines, and learn about the local winemaking tradition.

Food and Drink Scene of Dartmouth

Must-Try Dishes

Dartmouth is home to a wide range of unique and delicious dishes that you won’t find anywhere else. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Shellfish Chowder: Made with fresh local clams and scallops, this hearty chowder is a local favorite.
  • Lobster Roll: Made with fresh lobster meat and served on a buttered, toasted bun, this classic New England dish is a must-try.
  • Mudslides: A delicious mixture of Kahlua, Bailey’s Irish Cream, and vodka, this sweet cocktail is the perfect way to end a day of exploring.

Local Beverages

Dartmouth is home to a number of local breweries and distilleries, producing some of the finest craft beers and spirits around. Here are some of the best beverages to try:

  • Buzzard’s Bay Brewing: Sample a variety of locally brewed beers, from refreshing ales to complex stouts.
  • Coastal Wine Trail: Visit local wineries and vineyards, and sample some of the finest wines in Massachusetts.
  • Shoveltown Brewery: Enjoy a cold one at this local brewery, featuring a rotating selection of seasonal and specialty brews.

Where to Stay in Dartmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dartmouth has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Delano Homestead Bed and Breakfast: Stay in a beautifully restored 19th-century home, with all the modern amenities you need for a comfortable stay.
  • Hampton Inn by Hilton: Enjoy modern rooms, a complimentary breakfast, and easy access to all the top attractions in town.
  • Holiday Inn Express: Relax in spacious rooms and enjoy free breakfast, parking, and a convenient location near downtown Dartmouth.

What to Buy in Dartmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dartmouth is home to a wide range of unique shops and markets, selling everything from handmade crafts to artisanal foods. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Dartmouth Natural Resources Trust: Purchase maps, books, and other items to fully enjoy your experience on the trust’s trails.
  • The Bridge Crafts Center: Browse locally made crafts, including pottery, weaving, and glass art.
  • Lees Market: Shop for artisanal foods, local produce, and other unique food items to take home with you.

Best Time to Visit Dartmouth

Dartmouth experiences four distinct seasons, each with its own unique charms. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with warm weather perfect for enjoying the beach and outdoor activities.
