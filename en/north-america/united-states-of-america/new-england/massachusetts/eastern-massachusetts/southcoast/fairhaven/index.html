Introduction to Fairhaven

Located in SouthCoast, Eastern Massachusetts, Fairhaven is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of rich history, natural beauty, and cultural vibrancy. Once a thriving whaling center, Fairhaven is home to a variety of attractions, ranging from museums and historical sites to natural wonders.

In this comprehensive Fairhaven travel guide, we’ll explore all the top attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, where to stay, and where to eat. Additionally, we’ll provide you with some valuable travel tips, do’s and don’ts, and essential information on how to get there and around.

List of Places to Visit in Fairhaven

Fairhaven is packed with a wide array of attractions that cater to visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some must-visit places in Fairhaven:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Phoenix State Reservation: A historic fort located on the eastern banks of the Acushnet River.
  • The Millicent Library: A beautiful, historic library dating back to 1893, which is home to an extensive collection of books, photos, and artifacts.
  • The Fairhaven Town Hall: A historic building built in 1894, and famous for its clock tower.
  • Whitfield-Manjiro Friendship House Museum: A unique museum built-in a 19th-century building that showcases the relationship between two cultures — American and Japanese.

Natural Attractions of Fairhaven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nasketucket Bay State Reservation: A scenic bay that offers hiking, kayaking, fishing, and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Cushman Park: A picturesque park with a playground and walking trails.
  • Fort Taber Park: A historic fort that dates back to the Civil War, which now offers recreational opportunities like hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fairhaven

Fairhaven is an excellent destination to enjoy hiking and biking, thanks to its varied terrain and many trails. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Phoenix Bike Trail: A 3.5-mile bike trail connecting Fairhaven and New Bedford.
  • Fort Phoenix State Reservation: A loop trail encompassing the historic fort and beautiful Nasketucket Bay.
  • Rogers/Banta-Davis Conservation Area: A picturesque hiking and biking trail that follows the Slocum River.

Best Things to Do in Fairhaven: Activities and Tours

Fairhaven offers an incredible array of activities and tours for visitors of all interests. Here are some must-do activities in Fairhaven:

  • Visit the New Bedford Whaling Museum, where you can get a glimpse of the town’s whaling past.
  • Take a walking tour of the historic district, with the Fairhaven Office of Tourism.
  • Enjoy a scenic Sunset Bay Cruise, which takes visitors on a cruise to explore the town’s coastline and harbor.

Food and Drink Scene of Fairhaven

Fairhaven boasts a diverse and vibrant food and drink scene, offering everything from casual dining to fine dining experiences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Fairhaven:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England delicacy, available at several local seafood restaurants in Fairhaven.
  • Clam Chowder: A creamy and hearty seafood soup that’s as popular in Fairhaven as it is in Boston.
  • Portuguese Sweet Bread: A traditional bread made with eggs, sugar, flour, and milk, sold at several local bakeries in Fairhaven.

Local Beverages

  • Portuguese Wine and Beer: Try a locally brewed beer or a glass of wine at one of the Portuguese restaurants in Fairhaven.
  • Coffee Milk: A Rhode Island and southeastern Massachusetts staple, made with coffee-flavored syrup mixed with milk.

Where to Stay in Fairhaven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fairhaven offers plenty of options for accommodation, from budget-friendly to luxury hotels, hostels, inns, and guesthouses. Here are some top-rated options:

  • Delano Homestead: A beautiful 19th-century inn with elegant rooms and a patio garden.
  • Hampton Inn New Bedford/Fairhaven: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and a heated indoor pool.
  • Seaport Inn & Marina: A waterfront hotel with a restaurant, a pool, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Fairhaven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fairhaven is the perfect destination to find unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some of the best options:

  • Portuguese Ceramics: Beautiful hand-painted ceramic plates, bowls, and vases, available at local pottery shops in Fairhaven.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Local artisan jewelry made from silver, gold, and other materials, available at several local shops and galleries.
  • Sea Glass: Beautiful pieces of naturally worn and tumbled glass found on the beaches of Fairhaven, perfect for making jewelry or home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Fairhaven

Fairhaven has a rich cultural heritage, with a deep connection to Portuguese culture. The town celebrates several cultural events and festivals throughout the year, including the Feast of the Blessed Sacrament, which is the largest Portuguese feast in the world, and the Portuguese-American Festival. The town is also known for its beautiful architecture, historic landmarks, and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Fairhaven

The best time to visit Fairhaven is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is pleasant, and most of the outdoor activities and attractions are open for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fairhaven

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Fairhaven:

Do:

  • Take a walking tour of the historic district.
  • Try the local seafood and Portuguese cuisine.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on beaches or parks.
  • Park illegally in any designated areas.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Fairhaven is generally a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to stay safe and healthy while exploring the town and its attractions.

Safety in Fairhaven

  • Be aware of the tides and currents when swimming or boating in the harbor or bay.
  • Lock your cars and keep valuable items secure.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.

Health in Fairhaven

  • Check for ticks when spending time outdoors, as they can transmit Lyme disease.
  • Take precautions against mosquito bites, as they can carry diseases like West Nile virus and Eastern equine encephalitis.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.

Getting to Fairhaven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fairhaven is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Fairhaven

  • T.F. Green Airport: Located in Warwick, Rhode Island, approximately 30 miles from Fairhaven.
  • Logan International Airport: Located in Boston, Massachusetts, approximately 60 miles from Fairhaven.

Bus Stations Near Fairhaven

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: Offers bus services to and from Boston, Providence, and other nearby cities.
  • Greyhound: Offers bus services to and from New York and other destinations.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Several rental car agencies are available at the airports and cities in the area.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Lyft and Uber operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Fairhaven

Fairhaven is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. However, for those who prefer not to walk, rental cars, taxis or ride-sharing services, and bicycles are available.

Where to Go Next from Fairhaven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fairhaven is an excellent base to explore the nearby areas and destinations. Here are some recommendations:

  • Martha’s Vineyard: A beautiful island located off the coast of Massachusetts, known for its quaint towns, pristine beaches, and excellent seafood.
  • Cape Cod: A stunning peninsula that features numerous beaches, lighthouses, and charming coastal towns.
  • Providence: A beautiful city known for its arts, culture, and excellent restaurants.

Conclusion

Fairhaven is a hidden gem of SouthCoast, Massachusetts, with a rich history, beautiful natural landscapes, and unique cultural heritage. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures, hiking, and biking trails, Fairhaven has something for everyone.
