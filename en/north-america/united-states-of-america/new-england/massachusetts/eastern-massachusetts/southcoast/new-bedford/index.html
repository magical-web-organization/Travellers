Introduction to New Bedford

Located in the SouthCoast region of Eastern Massachusetts, New Bedford is a charming and historic city that offers visitors a plethora of things to do and see. The city is renowned for its whaling history, which is deeply rooted in its culture and heritage. In addition to its rich history, New Bedford boasts natural beauty, adventurous outdoor activities, and a flourishing food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, New Bedford has something to offer that is bound to capture your heart and keep you coming back for more.

Local Culture and Customs of New Bedford

New Bedford has a vibrant and diverse culture, which is heavily influenced by its Portuguese and Whaling heritage. Portuguese is the second most commonly spoken language in the city, and the Portuguese Feast of the Blessed Sacrament is one of the most significant events in the calendar.

Best Time to Visit New Bedford

New Bedford is a year-round destination, thanks to its moderate climate. However, the best time to explore the city is during the summer months, between June and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city comes alive with exciting events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Bedford

Do:

  1. Explore the Whaling Museum and other local museums
  2. Take a Whale Watching Tour
  3. Visit Buttonwood Park
  4. Try the local seafood dishes
  5. Attend the Portuguese Feast of the Blessed Sacrament

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public spaces
  2. Swim in unguarded areas
  3. Leave valuables in your car
  4. Ignore pedestrian crosswalks
  5. Disrespect local customs and traditions
