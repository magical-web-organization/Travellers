Introduction to Somerset

Welcome to Somerset, Massachusetts, a charming town located in the SouthCoast region of Eastern Massachusetts. This quaint community is nestled between the Taunton River and Mount Hope Bay, providing visitors with a scenic backdrop for their travel experience. Rich in history, Somerset is filled with attractions and activities that cater to travelers of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Somerset

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing vacation, Somerset has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your Somerset itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Somerset Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the town’s rich history through exhibits on Native American culture, early settlers, and local industry.

  • Old Town Hall Museum: Housed in a historic building from the 1800s, this museum features exhibits on Somerset’s maritime history and traditional crafts.

  • Pierce Beach & Playground: This public beach and playground is popular with locals and visitors alike. It features picnic areas, a boardwalk, and stunning views of the bay.

Natural Attractions of Somerset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taunton River: Take a river cruise or go kayaking on the Taunton River to experience the natural beauty of Somerset.

  • Buffington Park: This park offers miles of hiking trails, a playground, and a fishing pond.

  • Somerset Berkley Regional High School Hawk Observatory: Stargazers will love this observatory, which features state-of-the-art telescopes and breathtaking views of the night sky.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somerset

Somerset is home to several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are a few of the best routes:

  • Slades Ferry Trail: This 3-mile trail runs along the Taunton River and offers stunning views of the water.

  • Buffington Park Loop: Go for a hike or a jog on this 3-mile trail that winds through wooded areas and features a fishing pond.

  • Bike Path: This 6-mile paved trail runs through Somerset and neighboring towns, offering cyclists a safe and scenic ride.

Best Things to Do in Somerset: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions of Somerset, there are also a variety of activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Somerset Open Air Market: This market offers locally-made goods and live entertainment.

  • Somerset Country Club: Practice your swing or enjoy a round of golf at this beautiful golf course.

  • Somerset Motorhome and Camping Resort: This resort offers a range of recreational activities, including swimming, fishing, and boating.

Week-Long Itinerary for Somerset

Day 1: Visit the Somerset Historical Society Museum and Old Town Hall Museum.

Day 2: Explore the Taunton River on a river cruise or kayaking tour.

Day 3: Spend a day at Pierce Beach and Playground.

Day 4: Take a hike on the Slades Ferry Trail or Buffington Park Loop.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at the Somerset Country Club.

Day 6: Discover the local flavor at the Somerset Open Air Market.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the amenities at the Somerset Motorhome and Camping Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of Somerset

Somerset may be a small town, but it is not lacking in culinary offerings. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: This New England favorite is a must-try when visiting Somerset. The chowder at LePage’s Seafood and Grille is a local favorite.

  • Lobster Roll: Fresh lobster meat served on a buttery roll is a classic New England dish. The lobster rolls at The Lobster Pot are highly recommended.

  • Portuguese Cuisine: Somerset has a strong Portuguese community, which means you’ll find plenty of delicious Portuguese restaurants to try. The chourico and peppers at Sagres Restaurant come highly recommended.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The Fall River/Somerset area has a burgeoning craft beer scene. Check out the brews on tap at Slackman’s Brewery.

  • Coffee: For a cozy coffee shop experience, head to Carry Out Cafe and enjoy a specialty brew with a breakfast sandwich.

Where to Stay in Somerset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Somerset offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some of the top options:

  • Somerset Motorhome and Camping Resort: This resort offers campsites and RV hookups.

  • Comfort Inn & Suites Fall River-Somerset: This hotel features an indoor pool and hot tub.

  • Borden House Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in a historic home and offers a cozy and unique lodging experience.

What to Buy in Somerset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Somerset, be sure to bring home some local souvenirs and products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Handmade Pottery: Browse the selection of unique pottery and ceramics at Four Corners Arts Center.

  • Local Honey: Pick up some local honey from Long Acre Farm to take home and enjoy.

  • Vintage Finds: Browse the antique shops and flea markets in town for one-of-a-kind vintage treasures.

Local Culture and Customs of Somerset

Somerset is a tight-knit community with a proud history and rich culture. Visitors can expect to find a friendly and welcoming atmosphere. Portuguese culture is especially prevalent, with many festivals and events celebrating Portuguese heritage throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Somerset

The best time to visit Somerset depends on your travel style and preferences. Summer is the peak tourist season, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. If you’re looking for a quieter and more affordable trip, consider visiting in the spring or fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somerset

Do:

  • Do visit the local seafood restaurants and try the fresh fish and shellfish.

  • Do participate in the local festivals and events, such as the Portuguese Holy Ghost Festival.

  • Do take advantage of the stunning natural surroundings and explore the hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the beaches or in parks.

  • Don’t ignore traffic laws or walk in the bike lanes.

  • Don’t venture off the hiking trails, as it can be dangerous and harmful to the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Somerset

Somerset is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take common-sense safety precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving personal belongings unattended in public areas.

Health in Somerset

Somerset has several healthcare providers, including a hospital, urgent care centers, and primary care physicians. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to Somerset.

Getting to Somerset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Somerset is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Somerset

  • T.F. Green Airport: This airport is located in Warwick, Rhode Island and is approximately 22 miles from Somerset.

  • Logan International Airport: This airport is located in Boston, Massachusetts and is approximately 52 miles from Somerset.

Bus Stations Near Somerset

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: This bus station is located in Fall River, approximately 4 miles from Somerset.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from several providers, such as Enterprise Rent-A-Car or Avis, for easy transportation around Somerset.

How to Get Around in Somerset

For the most part, visitors will need a car to get around Somerset and the surrounding areas. However, there are a few options for public transportation, such as local bus routes and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Somerset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newport, Rhode Island: Visit the famous mansions and enjoy the coastal scenery in this picturesque town, located approximately 30 miles from Somerset.

  • Cape Cod, Massachusetts: Explore the beaches, lighthouses, and charming towns of Cape Cod, located approximately 50 miles from Somerset.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and local flavor, Somerset is a hidden gem in Massachusetts that is worth a visit. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be sure to make the most of your time in this charming New England town.
