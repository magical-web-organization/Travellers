Located in the heart of Central Massachusetts, Brookfields is a charming town that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the region’s history and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Brookfields has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and more in Brookfields, Massachusetts.

Introduction to Brookfields

Brookfields is a quaint town in Worcester County that comprises five distinct villages, including East Brookfield, West Brookfield, North Brookfield, Brookfield, and Warren. The town boasts a rich history dating back to colonial times, evident in its many historical homes and landmarks. Visitors will also marvel at Brookfields’ pristine natural beauty, courtesy of its several conservation areas and parks.

List of Places to Visit in Brookfields

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Brookfields will be a fascinating place to explore. The town has several museums, galleries, and historical landmarks, each offering a glimpse into the town’s past. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Brookfields:

  • The Brookfields Historical Society’s Old Town Hall: A historic building that now houses a museum showcasing Brookfields’ history and artifacts.

  • The Merriam-Gilbert Public Library: Originally a handsome home built in 1840, this building houses both the town’s library and the Brookfield Historical Society’s collections.

  • The Salem Cross Inn: A historic restaurant located in a restored 1705 post and beam farmhouse. The inn serves delicious food and beverages and offers tours.

Natural Attractions of Brookfields: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brookfields is home to several conservation areas, parks, and hiking trails, which allow visitors to appreciate the area’s natural beauty. Here are some must-visit natural attractions in Brookfields:

  • The Quaboag River: This winding river offers a serene escape from the busy downtown area and is perfect for canoeing or kayaking.

  • Oakham State Forest: Located in the northeast corner of Brookfields, this forest has over eight miles of hiking trails. Guests can also enjoy hunting, fishing, and camping.

  • Tantiusques: A conservation area that includes 57 acres of hiking trails and a historic granite quarry.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brookfields

Brookfields is a paradise for hikers, bikers, and outdoor enthusiasts. The town boasts several parks, trails, and conservation areas that offer different levels of difficulty. Here are some top hiking/biking routes in Brookfields:

  • The Brookfields Rail Trail: A paved trail that follows the abandoned railroad bed of Boston and Maine Railway.

  • The Welsh Preserve at Wauwinet: A beautiful conservation area that has over two miles of hiking trails, freshwater ponds, and forests.

  • The Ware River Watershed: A popular area among hikers for its beautiful vistas, waterfalls, and remote pools.

Best Things to Do in Brookfields: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, Brookfields offers several other activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some top things to do in Brookfields:

  • Take a Salem Cross Inn Tour: Guests can step back in time with an authentic, historic tour of this restaurant, which also includes a hayride and traditional New England-dinner.

  • Visit North Brookfield’s Memorial Town Hall: This National Historic Register Building located at North Brookfield’s Bowertown Road is used by town agencies, organizations, and for special events.

  • Attend a Concert at the Brookfields’ Bandstand: Head to Brookfield Common on a Saturday night (June-mid-Aug) for free concerts at the historic bandstand.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brookfields

If you’re planning to spend a week in Brookfields and the surrounding areas, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the Brookfields Rail Trail and dine at Salem Cross Inn.
  • Day 2: Visit the Brookfields Historical Society’s Old Town Hall, followed by an afternoon picnic at North Brookfield’s Memorial Town Hall.
  • Day 3: Spend the day hiking at Oakham State Forest, then relaxing at the Welsh Preserve at Wauwinet.
  • Day 4: Go kayaking on the Quaboag River, followed by dinner at one of the area’s seafood restaurants.
  • Day 5: Visit Tantiusques Conservation Area and take a day trip to one of the nearby towns.
  • Day 6: Attend a concert at the Brookfield’s Bandstand.
  • Day 7: Go on a scenic drive throughout central Massachusetts.

Food and Drink Scene of Brookfields

Must-Try Dishes

Brookfield’s food scene is a mix of traditional New England fare and contemporary cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Lobster Roll: A classic dish to try when exploring the nearby coastal towns like Manchester-by-the-Sea.
  • Flatbreads: Salem Cross Inn serves artisan flatbreads and pizza cooked right in front of you in their beehive oven.
  • Shepherd’s Pie: The Inn at Brookfield Farm in North Brookfield is known for its locally sourced ingredients, available in dishes like their shepherd’s pie.

Local Beverages

Brookfields is home to several local wineries, breweries, and cideries, making it the perfect spot for those looking for a drink. Here are some must-try local beverages:

  • Hard Cider: Try one of the area’s secrets of hard cider, like Ragged Hill Cider.
  • Craft Beer: Brookfields has several breweries to choose from, like Oakholm Brewing and Kelsey Hill Brewing.

Where to Stay in Brookfields: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brookfields has several options for lodging, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • The Old Sturbridge Inn & Reeder Family Lodges: A historic inn that offers luxurious amenities, a perfect blend of old and new.
  • Salem Cross Inn: The Inn offers guest rooms and suites, all with private bathrooms and decorated in period style.
  • The Nutmeg Inn: A charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Brookfields, featuring comfortable antique rooms and delicious homemade breakfasts.

What to Buy in Brookfields: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Brookfields is known for its various local and handmade items that you can take home. Here are some top things to buy in Brookfields:

  • Handmade Soaps and Candles: The local Soap & Stonewickery shop sells unique, handmade soap bars and candles.
  • Antiques: Shop for authentic antiques at the Cedar Chest Antiques and Garage Sale on Route 9.

Local Culture and Customs of Brookfields

Brookfields is a small-town community that values its rich history, natural beauty, and unique traditions. If you’re visiting Brookfields, it’s essential to respect the town’s cultural customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Brookfields

The best time to visit Brookfields is during the summer months when the weather is beautiful and ideal for outdoor activities. Fall is also an excellent time to visit and appreciate the gorgeous foliage landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brookfields

Do:

  • Respect the town’s customs and traditions.
  • Experience the area’s natural beauty and wildlife with cautions.
  • Treat the locals with respect and courtesy.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitat of Brookfields.
  • Trespass onto private property.
  • Disrespect or damage any of the town’s historical landmarks or buildings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brookfields

Brookfields is a safe town with virtually no crime. Visitors should exercise standard caution and safety when exploring the town’s natural habitats.

Health in Brookfields

Visitors should check with their physicians and insurance providers about necessary immunizations and medical coverage. There is no hospital in Brookfields, so in the case of an emergency, go to the closest medical facility.

Getting to Brookfields: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brookfields is easily accessible by road. Visitors often drive from Boston, which takes approximately an hour. Alternatively, you can fly into Bradley International Airport, located 45 minutes from Brookfields.

Airports Near Brookfields

  • Bradley International Airport, Hartford, Connecticut (55 mi).
  • Worcester Regional Airport, Worcester, Massachusetts (30 mi).

Bus Stations Near Brookfields

  • Worcester Greyhound Station, Worcester, Massachusetts (23 mi).
  • Southbridge MA Bus Terminal, Southbridge, Massachusetts (18 mi).

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Brookfields via train, with the Amtrak Service available from Worcester, Massachusetts.

How to Get Around in Brookfields

The best way to get around Brookfields is by driving, walking, or cycling. Brookfields has several local transportation companies, including taxi and limousine services, which offer services for local travel.

Where to Go Next from Brookfields: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Springfield: home to the revolutionary Basketball Hall of Fame, and an hour’s drive from Brookfields.
  • Boston: a bustling city known for its rich history, Boston is an hour’s drive from Brookfields.

Conclusion

Brookfields, Massachusetts, is a charming, small-town destination that offers visitors a rare glimpse into New England’s rich history and natural beauty. From exploring the town’s historical landmarks to navigating its various conservation areas and hiking trails, there’s always something to see and do in Brookfields.
