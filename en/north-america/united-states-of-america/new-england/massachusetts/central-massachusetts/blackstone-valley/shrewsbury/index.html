Introduction to Shrewsbury

Shrewsbury is a town located in Central Massachusetts near the Blackstone Valley and is historically significant in Massachusetts. The town has a rich history dating back to the early 1700s when it was first incorporated.

List of Places to Visit in Shrewsbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ward Hill Ski Area Museum The Ward Hill Ski Area Museum is located on Prospect Street in downtown Shrewsbury and is a must-see attraction for skiing enthusiasts. The museum is dedicated to preserving the history of skiing in New England and the United States.

  • Shrewsbury Historical Society The Shrewsbury Historical Society is located on Main Street in Shrewsbury and is home to a collection of historical artifacts and documents pertaining to the town’s past. The society operates a museum that is open to the public and hosts various events and exhibits throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Shrewsbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Quinsigamond Lake Quinsigamond is a freshwater lake that stretches across the border between Worcester and Shrewsbury. The lake is popular for boating, fishing, and swimming and is home to several parks and recreational areas.

  • Dean Park Dean Park is a large recreational area located in the heart of Shrewsbury and features several miles of hiking and biking trails, playgrounds, and athletic fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shrewsbury

Shrewsbury is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the surrounding countryside. Some of the most popular trails include the Lake Quinsigamond Trail, the Green Hill Park Loop, and the Dean Park Trails.

Best Things to Do in Shrewsbury: Activities and Tours

  • EcoTarium The EcoTarium is a museum that features exhibits on the natural world, astronomy, and ecology and is located in Worcester, a short drive from Shrewsbury.

  • Worcester Art Museum The Worcester Art Museum is located in Worcester and is home to a collection of over 35,000 art objects including paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts.

  • Shrewsbury Farmers Market The Shrewsbury Farmers Market is held weekly during the summer months and features a variety of fresh produce, crafts, and other locally made goods.

Food and Drink Scene of Shrewsbury

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrewsbury Sizzler The Shrewsbury Sizzler is a classic American sandwich, consisting of steak or chicken cooked on a griddle with peppers, onions, and cheese, and served on a long roll.

  • Coney Island Hot Dog The Coney Island Hot Dog is a classic hot dog that is served with chili, mustard, and onions. It’s a staple in Shrewsbury and the surrounding area.

Local Beverages

  • Wormtown Brewery Wormtown Brewery is a locally owned and operated brewery that produces a variety of craft beers. The brewery is located in Worcester, a short drive from Shrewsbury.

  • Greater Good Imperial Brewing Co. The Greater Good Imperial Brewing Co. is a craft brewery located in Worcester that produces a variety of high-quality beers.

Where to Stay in Shrewsbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shrewsbury is home to several hotels and guesthouses that cater to travelers looking for a comfortable place to stay. Some of the most popular options include:

  • The Lodge Hotel The Lodge Hotel is a boutique hotel located in the heart of Shrewsbury that offers stylish accommodations, on-site dining, and easy access to local attractions.

  • Holiday Inn Express The Holiday Inn Express is a budget-friendly hotel located just off of Route 9 in Shrewsbury. The hotel offers comfortable accommodations, a hot breakfast, and easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Shrewsbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Shrewsbury include:

  • Shrewsbury Maple Syrup Shrewsbury is known for its high-quality maple syrup, which can be found at several local farms and markets.

  • Handcrafted Pottery Shrewsbury is home to several artisans who create beautiful handcrafted pottery. Look for pottery shops and galleries in the downtown area.

Local Culture and Customs of Shrewsbury

Shrewsbury has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, museums, and traditions. The town is known for its vibrant arts community and its commitment to preserving its history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Shrewsbury

The best time to visit Shrewsbury is during the fall when the leaves are changing colors and the air is crisp. The town hosts several fall events and festivals, including the popular Shrewsbury Harvest Festival.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shrewsbury

Shrewsbury is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from theft and other crimes. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.

Health in Shrewsbury

There are several hospitals and medical clinics in the area that provide high-quality healthcare services to residents and visitors alike. If you become ill or injured during your stay in Shrewsbury, seek medical attention as soon as possible.

Getting to Shrewsbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shrewsbury

The closest airport to Shrewsbury is Worcester Regional Airport, which is serviced by several airlines and offers flights to a number of domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Shrewsbury

The Worcester Regional Transit Authority operates several bus routes that serve Shrewsbury and the surrounding area. The closest bus station is in downtown Worcester.

Other Options

Visitors to Shrewsbury can also get around by car or taxi. Rental cars are available at the Worcester airport and several other locations throughout the area.

How to Get Around in Shrewsbury

Shrewsbury is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are several parks and recreational areas in the town that are connected by hiking and biking trails.
