Introduction to Boroughs

Located in the Blackstone Valley region of Central Massachusetts, Boroughs is a small yet culturally and naturally rich town. Home to several museums, historical sites, and beautiful parklands, Boroughs is a perfect destination for anyone looking for a peaceful vacation amidst scenic surroundings.

In this comprehensive tour guide to Boroughs, we have covered everything you need to know to plan your trip, including the best places to visit, things to do, food to eat, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Boroughs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boroughs Historical Society Museum: Located in the heart of the town, the museum provides a glimpse into the town’s history. The exhibits include photographs, artifacts, and documents dating back to the early 1800s.

  • John H. Chaffee Blackstone River Valley National Heritage Corridor Visitor Center: The visitor center provides information about the Blackstone River Valley region’s history and culture. The center also houses a gift shop where you can purchase souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Boroughs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Davidian Bros. Farm: The farm features a greenhouse, seasonal produce, and a pumpkin patch. Visitors can take a hayride around the farm during the fall season.

  • Riverside Park: The park is situated along the banks of the Blackstone River and offers breathtaking views of the waterfalls. Visitors can also take a leisurely walk around the park, fish, and have a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boroughs

  • Blackstone River Bikeway: The trail stretches for 48 miles from Worcester, Massachusetts, to Providence, Rhode Island, through several small towns, including Boroughs. The trail is ideal for biking, walking, or jogging.

  • Purgatory Chasm State Reservation: The chasm features a unique rock formation that provides opportunities for hiking and rock climbing. The trail loops around the chasm, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Boroughs: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The Blackstone River offers several opportunities for water sports, including canoeing and kayaking. Visitors can rent equipment from nearby vendors or participate in guided tours.

  • Boroughs Art Center: The center offers art classes and workshops for all ages, including painting, pottery, and jewelry making.

Food and Drink Scene of Boroughs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: A regional specialty, the clam chowder at Depot Street Tavern is a must-try.

  • Roast Beef Sandwich: The roast beef sandwich at Cornell’s Irish Pub is a customer favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The Taproom at Medusa Brewing Company offers a variety of craft beers brewed on-site.

  • Wine: Visitors can sample local wines at nearby Nashoba Valley Winery.

Where to Stay in Boroughs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guest Houses

  • Boroughs Bed and Breakfast: The bed and breakfast offers a cozy and comfortable stay with easy access to nearby attractions.

  • Courtyard by Marriott: The Courtyard by Marriott is situated in nearby Milford and offers comfortable and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Boroughs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Davidian Bros. Farm Stand: Visitors can purchase fresh produce, jams, and baked goods from the farm stand.

  • Boroughs Historical Society Gift Shop: The gift shop offers a variety of locally-made souvenirs, including postcards and t-shirts.

Local Culture and Customs of Boroughs

Boroughs is a small town with a friendly community that values its history and culture. While the town is modern and progressive, visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Boroughs

The best time to visit Boroughs is from May to October when the weather is pleasant, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can enjoy Boroughs’ beauty throughout the year.
