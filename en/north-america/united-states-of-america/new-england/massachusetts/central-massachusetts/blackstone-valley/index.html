Introduction to Blackstone Valley

Nestled in the heart of Central Massachusetts, Blackstone Valley is a historic and picturesque region that offers visitors a glimpse into New England’s rich past and natural beauty. Known as the birthplace of the American Industrial Revolution, this area is home to charming towns, lush forests, scenic waterways, and some of the finest museums and cultural attractions in the region.

From exploring Revolutionary War sites to hiking through scenic trails, enjoying local cuisine to soaking in the charm of quintessential New England towns, Blackstone Valley has something for every traveler. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or simply in search of a relaxing getaway, this travel guide will help you make the most of your trip to Massachusetts’ historic heartland.

List of Places to Visit in Blackstone Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Blackstone River Valley National Heritage Corridor - This national park offers visitors a chance to explore the birthplace of the American Industrial Revolution. With over 400 miles of scenic trails, historic sites, and cultural attractions, the park is a must-see for history buffs and nature enthusiasts alike.
  • Old Sturbridge Village - This living museum takes visitors back to New England in the 1830s. With costumed interpreters, authentic period buildings, and working farms, Old Sturbridge Village offers a glimpse into everyday life in early America.
  • Worcester Art Museum - Known for its extensive collection of American art, the Worcester Art Museum also features works from Europe, Asia, and ancient civilizations. Highlights include the Medieval Galleries, the Renaissance Court, and the Lucy Truman Aldrich Collection of Chinese Export Porcelain.
  • EcoTarium - This family-friendly science museum is dedicated to exploring the natural world. Visitors can interact with live animals, explore hands-on exhibits, and discover the wonders of the cosmos in the planetarium.

Natural Attractions of Blackstone Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Purgatory Chasm State Reservation - This unique natural landmark features a series of rock formations that make for a challenging but rewarding hike. Visitors can explore the chasm’s narrow passageways, climb its steep ledges, and take in stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Wachusett Mountain State Reservation - With its scenic trails, panoramic views, and rugged terrain, Wachusett Mountain offers hiking, camping, and outdoor recreation opportunities suitable for all skill levels.
  • Blackstone River State Park - This park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and canoeing. Visitors can also enjoy the park’s scenic picnic areas and nature trails.
  • West Hill Dam - Located in Uxbridge, this impressive structure was built by the Army Corps of Engineers to control flooding along the Blackstone River. Visitors can hike the trails around the dam and take in panoramic views of the surrounding valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blackstone Valley

Blackstone Valley offers a diverse range of hiking and biking trails, with options suitable for beginners and seasoned adventurers alike. These are some of the most popular routes:

  • Southern New England Trunkline Trail - This former rail corridor is now a multi-use path that runs for 22 miles through scenic forests and along the banks of the Blackstone River.
  • West Hill Dam Trail - This 3-mile loop trail takes hikers through the woods and along the shore of a picturesque reservoir.
  • Blackstone River Greenway - When completed, this 48-mile paved trail will link the towns of Worcester and Providence, providing breathtaking views of the Blackstone River and its surrounding wildlife.
  • Purgatory Chasm Loop Trail - This scenic 2.5-mile hike takes visitors through the unique rock formations of Purgatory Chasm State Reservation.

Best Things to Do in Blackstone Valley: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, as a couple, or with your family, Blackstone Valley offers plenty of fun activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of the best:

  • Blackstone Valley Explorer River Boat Tours - This narrated boat tour takes visitors along the scenic Blackstone River, offering a unique perspective on the region’s history and natural beauty.
  • Horseback Riding at Cornerstone Ranch - Located in Princeton, MA, this ranch offers scenic trail rides suitable for all ages and skill levels.
  • Worcester Bravehearts Baseball Games - Catch a game with the local minor league team at the Hanover Insurance Park at Fitton Field.
  • Farm Visits and Food Tastings at Agriturismo - This farm in Uxbridge offers farm-to-table tours, culinary workshops, and tastings featuring locally sourced ingredients.
  • Paddle Boarding in the Blackstone River Valley - With several rental and tour options available, visitors can paddle board along the Blackstone River while enjoying stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blackstone Valley

To make the most of your trip to Blackstone Valley, consider following this suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Worcester

  • Visit the Worcester Art Museum
  • Explore the Canal District’s restaurants, cafes and shops
  • Catch a Worcester Bravehearts baseball game

Day 2: Blackstone River Valley National Heritage Corridor

  • Visit the River Bend Farm Visitor Center and go for a hike
  • Tour one of the historic mills in the area, such as the Slater Mill in Pawtucket, RI
  • Visit the Museum of Work and Culture in Woonsocket, RI

Day 3: Uxbridge and Milford

  • Go horseback riding at Cornerstone Ranch
  • Visit the Tufts veterinary school’s teaching farm in Grafton
  • Sample locally-sourced products at the Agriturismo farm in Uxbridge

Day 4: Worcester County

  • Hike up Wachusett Mountain
  • Visit the Higgins Armory Museum (closed, but a collection is available the Worcester Art Museum)
  • Go on a craft beer tour in Worcester

Day 5: Southbridge-Sturbridge Area

  • Explore the living history museum at Old Sturbridge Village
  • Go for a hike in Purgatory Chasm State Reservation
  • Have lunch at the Salem Cross Inn in West Brookfield

Day 6: Douglas and Sutton

  • Visit the Douglas State Forest and take one of its hiking trails
  • Explore the Manchaug Pond Conservation Area in Sutton
  • Have dinner at the Duck in Millville, a well-known seafood restaurant

Day 7: Providence, RI

  • Visit the RISD Museum in Providence
  • Explore the city’s historic Federal Hill neighborhood
  • Enjoy local cuisine at one of the many excellent restaurants in the area

Food and Drink Scene of Blackstone Valley

No trip to Blackstone Valley is complete without indulging in some of the area’s delicious local cuisine. From seafood to farm-to-table fare, the region offers a diverse range of dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder - A New England classic, this hearty soup is made with creamy broth, fresh clams, potatoes, and spices.
  • Lobster Roll - This iconic sandwich is made with fresh lobster meat and served on a buttered roll.
  • Johnny Cakes - A type of cornmeal pancake that is popular in the region, especially in Rhode Island.
  • Baked Beans - Made with molasses and pork, baked beans are a traditional New England side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Cranberry Juice - Blackstone Valley is known for its cranberry bogs, and cranberry juice is a popular local beverage.
  • Harpoon IPA - A locally brewed beer that is a favorite among craft beer enthusiasts.
  • Nashoba Valley Winery Wine - This award-winning winery produces a variety of delicious wines made from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Blackstone Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blackstone Valley has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget, from luxurious hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts. Here are some suggestions:

  • Harrington House Bed and Breakfast - Located in Milford, this cozy B&B offers comfortable accommodations and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Beechwood Hotel - This elegant hotel in Worcester offers luxury accommodations and fine dining at its award-winning restaurant, Ceres Bistro.
  • DoubleTree by Hilton - Westborough - This full-service hotel features an indoor pool, fitness center and signature warm chocolate chip cookie welcome.

What to Buy in Blackstone Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to take a piece of Blackstone Valley home with them, there are plenty of local products to choose from. Whether you’re looking for crafts, food or drink, here are some recommendations:

  • Cranberry Products - From cranberry juice to cranberry sauce, the area’s signature fruit is a popular local product.
  • Pottery and Ceramics - The region is home to many talented potters and ceramic artists, producing everything from mugs and bowls to decorative vases.
  • Maple Syrup - The area’s maple syrup is a delicious and popular local product.
  • Woven Textiles - Blackstone Valley has a rich textile history, and hand-woven wool blankets and rugs are a popular souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Blackstone Valley

Blackstone Valley is known for its rich cultural heritage, reflected in everything from its architecture and art to its food and festivals. Residents take pride in their history and traditions, and visitors are encouraged to celebrate and appreciate the area’s unique cultural identity. Popular local customs include:

  • Clambakes - A time-honored New England tradition, clambakes are a popular summertime activity in Blackstone Valley.
  • Fall Festivals - The region is famous for its vibrant fall foliage, and fall festivals celebrating the season are popular throughout the area.
  • Town Common Concerts - Many of the towns in Blackstone Valley offer free summer concerts on their town commons.

Best Time to Visit Blackstone Valley

Blackstone Valley is a year-round destination, with something to offer visitors in every season. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with stunning foliage and fall festivals. Winter can be cold and snowy, but offers opportunities for skiing and other winter sports. Spring is a quieter time to visit, with mild weather and blooming flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blackstone Valley

Do:

  • Take advantage of the region’s outdoor activities and natural beauty.
  • Try the local cuisine, including seafood and other regional specialties.
  • Take time to explore the region’s rich cultural heritage and history.

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone speaks English. Many residents of the region are bilingual, and may prefer to speak in another language.
  • Forget to pack appropriate clothing for the season. New England weather can be unpredictable, so be prepared for anything.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blackstone Valley

Blackstone Valley is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take the same precautions they would in any unfamiliar place. Here are some tips:

  • Keep valuables out of sight and securely locked away.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert, especially in crowded areas.
  • Follow posted safety warnings and guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Blackstone Valley

Blackstone Valley has excellent medical facilities and resources, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should be aware of any specific health risks related to their travel plans and take appropriate precautions. It is also advisable to carry a basic first aid kit and any necessary medications.

Getting to Blackstone Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Blackstone Valley is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Blackstone Valley

  • Worcester Regional Airport - About 20 miles east of Blackstone Valley, this airport offers flights to several destinations in the US.
  • T. F. Green Airport - Located about 50 miles south in Rhode Island, T. F. Green offers flights to many destinations in the US and Canada.

Bus Stations Near Blackstone Valley

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines - Offers regular bus service to Worcester and other destinations throughout New England.
  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Offers regular service to Providence and other major Northeastern cities.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Service - Provides trains service to Worcester and other destinations throughout the Northeast.
  • Rental Cars - Several car rental agencies operate in the area, providing visitors with flexible transportation options.

How to Get Around in Blackstone Valley

Public transportation is limited in Blackstone Valley, so visitors may want to consider renting a car or using ride-share services to get around. Taxi services and private car services are also available, but may be more expensive. Biking and walking are also popular options, especially in urban areas and parks.

Where to Go Next from Blackstone Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blackstone Valley is a great starting point for exploring other attractions in the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Boston - About an hour’s drive east of Blackstone Valley, Boston is a world-class city with rich history, great food, and cultural attractions.
  • Cape Cod - A scenic two-hour drive southeast, Cape Cod offers beautiful beaches, charming towns, and plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Newport, RI - About an hour’s drive south of Blackstone Valley, Newport is a picturesque seaside town with a rich history and stunning mansions.
  • Providence, RI - Just a 30-minute drive southeast of Blackstone Valley, Providence is a vibrant city with excellent restaurants, entertainment venues, and cultural attractions.
  • Mount Greylock State Reservation - Located about 90 minutes west, this beautiful state park offers stunning views of the Massachusetts countryside and excellent hiking and outdoor recreation opportunities.
