Introduction to Milford

Milford is a charming town located in the heart of Blackstone Valley, Central Massachusetts in the United States of America. Known for its picturesque beauty, cultural events, and unique experiences, Milford is a hidden gem that must be explored.

This guide will take you through the places to visit, things to do, how to get around Milford, where to stay, and more. Come along as we explore all that Milford has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Milford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Milford Historical Commission and Museum

The Milford Historical Commission and Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. The museum showcases Milford’s rich cultural and historical heritage. Here, you can explore exhibits on notable figures, including John F. Kennedy and Mary Baker Eddy, and the many contributions made by Milford citizens.

Women’s Pavilion and Town Hall

The Women’s Pavilion and Town Hall is a prime example of historic architecture in Milford. The building houses the Town Clerk’s office, the Historical Commission, and the Women’s Pavilion exhibit, which highlights the many contributions of women to the local community and beyond.

Natural Attractions of Milford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Milford Bike Trail

The Milford Bike Trail is a 1.3-mile paved path ideal for cycling, walking, and jogging. The trail begins at Louisa Lake and ends at Central Street.

Southwick’s Zoo

Southwick’s Zoo is located just a few miles from Milford and is an excellent destination for animal lovers. Considered as one of the best zoos in New England, the zoo features exotic animals, adventure pathways, and interactive animal encounters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milford

Milford Bike Trail

The Milford Bike Trail is a great option for biking enthusiasts. The trail takes you through the heart of the town’s natural beauty and scenic views.

Conservation Areas

Milford boasts several conservation areas, including the Dilla Street Conservation Area, which offers a 0.4-mile walking trail with scenic views of the wetlands. The Moses Taft Conservation Area is also a great option for nature lovers and features several hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Milford: Activities and Tours

Tour the Milford Town Library

The Milford Town Library claims to have the oldest public library in the United States, dating back to 1868. The library features a wide array of books, including rare books dating back to the 1800s.

Visit the Milford Labor Day Parade

The Milford Labor Day Parade is a popular event among both locals and visitors. The parade features food vendors, arts and crafts, and live entertainment, making it an ideal event for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milford

Day Plan
1 Visit the Milford Historical Commission and Museum and Women’s Pavilion.
2 Hike the Dilla Street Conservation Area.
3 Take a bike ride on the Milford Bike Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Milford

Must-Try Dishes

Clam Chowder

Clam chowder is one of Milford’s signature dishes. Many local restaurants serve a delicious and authentic version of it.

Lobster Rolls

Milford’s lobster rolls are famous throughout the region. Several restaurants and food trucks serve this delicacy, making it a must-try dish for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

Milford is home to several distilleries that produce high-quality craft beer. The HOPPED UP BREWING COMPANY is not to be missed and offers a wide range of beer options for all tastes.

Craft Spirits

Ryan & Wood produces high-quality craft spirits, gin, and rum. The tasting room offers visitors the opportunity to try several different spirits and learn about the fermentation process.

Where to Stay in Milford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Hotels

DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Boston - Milford

Located at 11 Beaver Street, the DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel is a luxurious hotel that offers several amenities, including a fitness center, an outdoor pool, and an in-house restaurant.

Courtyard by Marriott Boston Milford

Located at 10 Fortune Boulevard, the Courtyard by Marriott features spacious and comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a 24-hour fitness center.

Budget Accommodations

Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites Boston-Milford

Located at 50 Fortune Boulevard, the Holiday Inn Express offers budget-friendly accommodation close to all of Milford’s major attractions.

What to Buy in Milford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Milford boasts several shops that sell locally produced items, including candles, handcrafted jewelry, and pottery. If you’re looking for souvenirs, consider visiting the Milford Craft Show, which features several vendors selling locally made products.

Local Culture and Customs of Milford

Milford boasts several annual cultural events, including the Milford Christmas Parade, held every December. The parade features live entertainment, food vendors, and festive decorations, making it a must-visit event if you’re in Milford around the holiday season.

Best Time to Visit Milford

The best time to visit Milford is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milford

Do:

  • Respect the cultural heritage and history of the town.
  • Enjoy the scenic beauty of Milford.
  • Explore the hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Disrespect the locals and their beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Milford

Milford is a relatively safe town to visit. However, visitors are advised to stay vigilant and take precautions, especially when hiking or exploring the outdoors.

Health in Milford

Milford has several medical facilities, including the Milford Regional Medical Center, which offers a wide range of medical services.

Getting to Milford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Milford

The nearest major airport to Milford is Logan International Airport in Boston.

Bus Stations Near Milford

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus depot in Milford at 3 Central Street.

Other Options

Milford is easily accessible by car, and visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to get around the town.

How to Get Around in Milford

Milford’s downtown area is easily walkable, making it an ideal location for exploring by foot. Visitors looking to explore the outskirts of town can rent a bike or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Milford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hopkinton State Park

Located about 20 miles from Milford, Hopkinton State Park is an excellent destination for hiking, fishing, and swimming.

Boston

Located about 35 miles from Milford, Boston is one of the most iconic cities in the United States. Visitors can explore historic sites, sample delicious food, and enjoy cultural events.

Conclusion

Milford offers visitors an authentic and unique New England experience. With an array of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and a vibrant food and drink scene, Milford must be on every traveler’s itinerary.
