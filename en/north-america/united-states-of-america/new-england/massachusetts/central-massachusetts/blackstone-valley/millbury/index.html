Introduction to Millbury

Located in Central Massachusetts, in the Blackstone Valley region, Millbury is a quaint town that is full of history and natural beauty. The town was established in 1813 and was originally known for its mills and factories. Today, Millbury is a popular destination for tourists looking to explore the town’s rich history and scenic landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Millbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Asa Waters Mansion

Built in 1826, the Asa Waters Mansion is a historic mansion that features 17 rooms, including a ballroom and formal parlor. The mansion was once home to Asa Waters, a successful businessman and philanthropist. Today, visitors can tour the mansion and learn about its history and significance to Millbury.

Millbury Historical Society Museum

Located in the former Blacksmith Shop of Jabez Upham, the Millbury Historical Society Museum features exhibits that showcase the town’s history and culture. Visitors can explore artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the story of Millbury’s past.

Natural Attractions of Millbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Blackstone River and Canal Heritage State Park

Blackstone River and Canal Heritage State Park is a beautiful park that features trails for hiking and biking, as well as scenic views of the Blackstone River. The park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and it is a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Millbury.

Waters Farm

Waters Farm is a historic farm that features 130 acres of fields, woods, and ponds. Visitors can explore the farm’s gardens, orchards, and barns, and they can learn about the history of agriculture in Millbury. The farm also hosts events and programs throughout the year, including a farmers’ market and a harvest festival.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Millbury

Millbury is home to several great hiking and biking routes, including the Blackstone River and Canal Heritage State Park trail. This trail runs for 11 miles along the Blackstone River, and it features scenic views of the river and surrounding landscape. Another popular route is the Waters Farm Trail, which runs for 1.5 miles and explores the farm’s fields, woods, and ponds.

Best Things to Do in Millbury: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Millbury, from exploring historic mansions and museums to hiking and biking in the great outdoors. Visitors can also take part in guided tours of the town and its surrounding areas, or they can attend festivals and events throughout the year. The town is also a great place to shop for antiques and other unique items.

Getting to Millbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Millbury

The nearest airport to Millbury is Worcester Regional Airport, which is located about 7 miles away. Other nearby airports include Logan International Airport in Boston and Bradley International Airport in Hartford.

Bus Stations Near Millbury

Greyhound Bus Lines and Peter Pan Bus Lines both have stations in Worcester, which is located about 5 miles away from Millbury. The town is also accessible by taxi or rideshare services like Uber and Lyft.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore Millbury and the surrounding areas, or they can take a scenic train ride on the Blackstone Valley Explorer.

How to Get Around in Millbury

The best way to get around Millbury is by car or bike. Visitors can also take local buses or taxis to get around town and to nearby attractions and destinations.
