Charlton is a charming town located in South County, in Central Massachusetts, and is part of the picturesque New England region of the United States. Home to a variety of cultural and natural attractions, this destination is perfect for those looking to explore quaint towns and stunning outdoor spaces. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore all the best things to do, see, eat, and experience in Charlton.

Introduction to Charlton

Charlton is a town rich in history and culture. The town was originally settled in the early 18th century and was known for its farming and manufacturing industries. Today, Charlton has evolved into a destination that has something to offer for everyone. Whether you are interested in exploring museums, hiking through forests, eating local cuisine, or shopping for souvenirs, Charlton has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Charlton

  • Old Sturbridge Village
  • The Salem Cross Inn
  • Charlton Orchards and Farm
  • Capen Hill Nature Sanctuary
  • Westville Lake Recreation Area
  • Tree House Brewing Company
  • Opacum Land Trust

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Charlton has a rich history that is well-preserved in its museums and historical places. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Charlton:

  • Old Sturbridge Village: This living museum offers visitors a glimpse of life in the 19th century. With more than 200 acres of historical buildings and gardens, visitors can explore the daily life of New Englanders during this time period.
  • The Salem Cross Inn: This iconic landmark is a fully restored 1705 farmhouse and is now a restaurant serving classic New England cuisine.
  • Westville Dam Recreation Area: This man-made lake and the surrounding terrain is ideal for boating, fishing, and hiking.

Natural Attractions of Charlton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Charlton’s natural beauty is hard to miss. Here are some of the top places to explore nature in Charlton:

  • Capen Hill Nature Sanctuary: This expansive wildlife sanctuary offers hiking trails for all skill levels, a nature center, and various educational programs.
  • Opacum Land Trust: This conservation area is home to a variety of rare plant and animal species. Visitors can explore the trails, watch wildlife, and bird watch.
  • Westville Lake Recreation Area: This man-made lake and the surrounding terrain is ideal for boating, fishing, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charlton

Charlton is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Charlton:

  • Capen Hill Nature Sanctuary: This wildlife sanctuary offers a variety of hiking trails for all skill levels. These trails offer scenic views and wildlife watching opportunities.
  • Westville Dam Recreation Area: This man-made lake and the surrounding terrain is ideal for boating, fishing, and hiking. It offers a variety of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Charlton: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Charlton that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Charlton:

  • Tree House Brewing Company: One of the most popular breweries in the state, Tree House Brewing Company is known for its delicious craft beer.
  • Charlton Orchards and Farm: This family-owned farm offers a variety of fruit picking and outdoor activities, including hayrides and pumpkin patches.
  • Old Sturbridge Village: This living museum offers visitors a glimpse of life in the 19th century.

Week-Long Itinerary for Charlton

Here is a comprehensive itinerary to make the most out of your week-long stay in Charlton:

Day 1: Explore the town and visit Charlton Orchards and Farm.

Day 2: Visit the Old Sturbridge Village and learn about New England’s history.

Day 3: Take a hike in the Capen Hill Nature Sanctuary, and have lunch at the Salem Cross Inn.

Day 4: Enjoy a beer-tasting at the famous Tree House Brewing Company.

Day 5: Spend the day fishing, hiking, or boating at the Westville Lake Recreation Area.

Day 6: Visit the Opacum Land Trust and observe the local flora and fauna.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the town’s local shops before leaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Charlton

The food and drink scene in Charlton is a testament to the town’s rich history and culture. Here are some highlights:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster rolls: Lobster rolls are a classic New England dish, and Charlton offers some of the best.
  • The Salem Cross Inn’s turkey dinner: This iconic meal is a testament to the restaurant’s history and quality.
  • Apple cider donuts: Charlton’s many orchards produce some of the freshest apple cider and apple cider donuts in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Tree House Brewing Company: This craft brewery is famous for its top-quality beer and popular tasting room.
  • Charlton Orchards and Farm: The farm has a tasting room with a variety of apple ciders and juices.

Where to Stay in Charlton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Charlton offers a variety of options for accommodation, from luxurious hotels to more budget-friendly options. Here are some of the best places to stay in Charlton:

  • The Publick House Historic Inn: This historic inn offers spacious accommodations and excellent amenities.
  • Red Roof Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable and clean rooms at a reasonable price.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham Sturbridge: This hotel offers comfortable accommodations at a more affordable price point.

What to Buy in Charlton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Charlton offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great keepsakes. Here are some of the best things to buy in Charlton:

  • Apple cider donuts: Freshly made donuts from the local orchards are a must-try.
  • Maple syrup: Maple syrup is a New England staple and Charlton offers some of the best.
  • Handmade crafts: Explore the local craft shops to find handcrafted souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Charlton

Charlton is known for its friendly and welcoming environment. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Charlton

The best time to visit Charlton is from May to October when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charlton

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take the time to explore the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charlton

Charlton is a safe destination. Visitors should take standard safety precautions such as not leaving valuables in plain sight and locking doors and windows.

Health in Charlton

Visitors should bring any necessary medication as healthcare facilities may be limited in the area.

Getting to Charlton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Charlton

  • Worcester Regional Airport (ORH): Approximately 38 minutes away.
  • Bradley International Airport (BDL): Approximately 56 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Charlton

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: The nearest bus station is in Sturbridge, MA, approximately five minutes away from Charlton.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from Worcester or Springfield airports.

How to Get Around in Charlton

Charlton is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. However, car rentals are available for visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Charlton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Charlton is a great jumping-off point for a variety of nearby destinations. Here are some of the best day trips from Charlton:

  • The Berkshires: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes away.
  • Boston: Approximately 1 hour 15 minutes away.
  • Mystic Seaport: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes away.

Conclusion

Charlton offers a perfect getaway for those looking to explore New England’s rich history and natural beauty. With plenty to see and do, visitors are sure to enjoy this charming destination.
