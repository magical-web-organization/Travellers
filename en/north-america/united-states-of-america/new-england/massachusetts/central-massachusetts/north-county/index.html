Introduction to North County

North County is a picturesque region situated in Central Massachusetts. It is a popular destination for outdoors enthusiasts, cultural tourists, and anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors to North County will discover charming towns, scenic hiking trails, fascinating historical sites, and delicious local cuisine. In this guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, things to do, and practical tips for traveling in North County.

List of Places to Visit in North County

North County offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions for visitors to explore. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fitchburg Art Museum - The Fitchburg Art Museum features an extensive collection of American art, including works by local artists and emerging talents. Highlights include the museum’s European collection and its impressive Asian art collection.

  • Fort Devens Museum - The Fort Devens Museum provides a fascinating glimpse into the history of the US Army base that was located here from 1917 to 1996. Exhibits include military artifacts, photographs, and interactive displays.

  • Gardner Museum - The Gardner Museum is housed in a stunning historic building and features a wide range of exhibits, including contemporary art, photography, and decorative arts.

Natural Attractions of North County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wachusett Mountain State Reservation - Wachusett Mountain is a popular destination for hiking, skiing, and snowboarding. Visitors can take in stunning views of the surrounding countryside from the mountain’s summit.

  • Doyle Conservation Center - Doyle Conservation Center is a 170-acre nature reserve that offers excellent hiking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and a chance to see local wildlife up close.

  • Leominster State Forest - Leominster State Forest is a great place to go for a picnic, bike ride, or nature walk. The park’s trails wind through stunning woodlands, wetlands, and open fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North County

North County is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Mid-State Trail - The Mid-State Trail is a scenic hiking trail that runs for 92 miles through Central Massachusetts. The trail passes through rugged wilderness areas, charming towns, and historic sites.

  • Wachusett Mountain Loop Trail - The Wachusett Mountain Loop Trail is a challenging 3.9-mile loop that takes hikers to the summit of Wachusett Mountain.

  • North Central Pathway - The North Central Pathway is a 7.5-mile paved bike trail that winds through the picturesque countryside of North County. The trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or an afternoon walk.

Best Things to Do in North County: Activities and Tours

North County offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all interests. Here are some recommendations:

  • Apple Picking at Sholan Farms - Sholan Farms is a beautiful orchard that offers apple picking in the fall. Visitors can also enjoy hayrides, pumpkin picking, and live music.

  • Wachusett Mountain Ski Area - Wachusett Mountain Ski Area is a popular ski resort that offers skiing, snowboarding, and tubing in the winter. In the summer, visitors can enjoy scenic chairlift rides and hiking.

  • Farm Tours at Red Apple Farm - Red Apple Farm is a family-owned farm that offers tours and tastings of their artisanal products, including cider, donuts, and baked goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for North County

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to North County:

Day 1: Visit the Fitchburg Art Museum and explore the city’s charming downtown area.

Day 2: Hike the Wachusett Mountain Loop Trail, then head to Sholan Farms for apple picking.

Day 3: Take a tour of the Fort Devens Museum, then head to Doyle Conservation Center for a nature walk.

Day 4: Rent bikes and ride the North Central Pathway, then visit Red Apple Farm for a farm tour and cider tasting.

Day 5: Tour the Gardner Museum, then explore the nearby scenic town of Princeton.

Day 6: Visit Leominster State Forest for a day of picnicking and hiking.

Day 7: Enjoy a scenic chairlift ride at Wachusett Mountain Ski Area, then head to a local restaurant for dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of North County

North County offers a unique and delicious food and drink scene that draws inspiration from its local farms, orchards, and breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Cider Donuts - These delicious treats are a fall staple in North County. Try them fresh from the orchard at Red Apple Farm.

  • Lobster Roll - North County is located near the coast, so you can enjoy fresh seafood. Try a classic lobster roll at a local seafood restaurant.

  • Burger at Rye & Thyme - This popular gastropub in Leominster is known for its tasty burgers made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Wachusett Brewing Company - This award-winning brewery in Westminster offers brewery tours and tastings of its signature beers, including Larry DIPA and Country Pale Ale.

  • Nashoba Valley Winery - Located in Bolton, this winery produces over 30 different wines from local grapes. Take a tour and sample some of their award-winning wines.

  • Redemption Rock Brewery - Specializes in small-batch, artisanal beer with a taproom for tastings.

Where to Stay in North County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North County offers a variety of options for accommodations, ranging from cozy bed and breakfasts to modern hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Taj Boston - Located in Boston, this historic hotel offers luxury accommodations and stunning views of the city skyline.

  • The International Golf Club and Resort - This beautiful resort in Bolton features an award-winning golf course, upscale accommodations, and gourmet dining.

  • Bullock Lodge Bed and Breakfast - This charming bed and breakfast in Phillipston is located in a historic 19th-century farmhouse. Guests can enjoy a complimentary breakfast and cozy accommodations.

What to Buy in North County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North County offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make for perfect gifts or keepsakes. Here are some ideas:

  • Apple Cider - Bring home a jug of fresh-pressed apple cider from Red Apple Farm.

  • Maple Syrup - North County is known for its delicious maple syrup. Pick up a bottle from a local farm or farmer’s market.

  • Handmade Crafts - North County is home to many talented artisans who create handmade wood carvings, pottery, and other crafts. Check out some of the local craft fairs to find unique treasures.

Local Culture and Customs of North County

North County is a welcoming and friendly community that values its local history, outdoor recreation, and rich agricultural traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the region’s cultural customs and traditions, including a love for nature and a passion for fresh, locally grown food.

Best Time to Visit North County

The best time to visit North County depends on your interests and preferences. The fall is a popular season for apple picking and leaf-peeping, while the winter is perfect for skiing and snowboarding. Spring is a great time to enjoy nature walks and outdoor festivals, while the summer offers a wide range of outdoor activities and concerts. No matter when you visit, North County’s unique beauty and charm will leave a lasting impression.

Do’s and Don’ts of North County

Here are some important do’s and don’ts for visitors to North County:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors - North County has some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Massachusetts. Take advantage of its countless hiking trails and scenic views.

  • Try the local food and drink scene - North County is famous for its fresh apples, cider, and maple syrup, as well as its craft beer and award-winning wines.

  • Respect the local culture - North County is a unique and welcoming community that values its heritage and traditions. Be respectful of the region’s customs and practices.

Don’t:

  • Litter - North County is known for its pristine natural beauty, and visitors should do their part to keep it that way. Be sure to properly dispose of any trash or recyclables.

  • Trespass on private property - North County is home to many private residences and farms, and visitors should always respect the boundaries of these properties.

  • Ignore safety guidelines - Whether you’re hiking, skiing, or participating in any other outdoor activity, always follow safety guidelines and be aware of your surroundings.

Safety and Health

Visitors to North County should take precautions to ensure their safety and health during their trip. Here are some important tips:

Safety in North County

  • Stay on marked trails - When hiking or exploring the outdoors, always stick to marked trails and paths.

  • Be vigilant of wildlife - North County is home to a variety of wildlife, including black bears, coyotes, and eastern timber rattlesnakes. Be aware of your surroundings and always keep a distance from animals.

  • Follow safety guidelines on the water - If you plan to swim or boat on one of North County’s many lakes or rivers, be sure to follow all safety guidelines and wear a life jacket.

Health in North County

  • Take precautions against tick-borne illnesses - North County is known for its population of ticks that can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease. Be sure to wear protective clothing and use insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors.

  • Be aware of air quality alerts - During the summer months, North County may experience air quality warnings due to high levels of ozone pollution. Visitors with respiratory issues should be aware of these alerts and plan accordingly.

  • Maintain good hygiene practices - As with any travel destination, visitors should practice good hygiene habits, including regular handwashing and avoiding contact with sick individuals.

Getting to North County: Transportation Options and Logistics

North County is located in Central Massachusetts and is easily accessible by car, bus, or train.

Airports Near North County

  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS)
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT)
  • Worcester Regional Airport (ORH)

Bus Stations Near North County

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Peter Pan Bus Lines

Other Options

  • Amtrak train service runs from Boston and Worcester to nearby cities such as Fitchburg and Leominster.

How to Get Around in North County

The best way to get around North County is by car. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the many bike trails in the area. Public transportation options are limited, although there are some bus routes and a rail line that runs from Fitchburg to Boston.

Where to Go Next from North County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in North County, consider visiting some nearby destinations:

  • Boston - Located just over an hour away, Boston is a world-class city that offers a wealth of museums, historical sites, and cultural attractions.

  • Cape Cod - Cape Cod is a popular vacation spot that offers beautiful beaches, charming towns, and delicious seafood.

  • Providence - Providence is a vibrant city in nearby Rhode Island that offers a fun food and nightlife scene, as well as historic architecture and museums.

Conclusion

North County is a hidden gem in Central Massachusetts that offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor recreation, and farm-to-table cuisine. Visitors to the region will discover charming towns, scenic hiking trails, and a warm and welcoming community. Whether you’re seeking a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, North County is the perfect destination.
