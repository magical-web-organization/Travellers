Introduction to Gardner

Gardner is a small town located in North County, Central Massachusetts. Nestled in the heart of the state, Gardner has a rich history and offers plenty of attractions for visitors looking for a break from the city. Outdoor enthusiasts will be thrilled with the abundance of natural beauty and opportunities for hiking and biking, while culture buffs will enjoy the town’s museums and historic sites. In this guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see in Gardner and offer tips for making the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Gardner

If you’re planning a trip to Gardner, there are plenty of places to explore and things to experience. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gardner has a rich history that’s reflected in its many cultural attractions. From museums to historical sites, there’s plenty to explore.

  • Gardner Museum: This local museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the town and its people. Exhibits cover everything from the town’s manufacturing past to its cultural heritage.

  • Heywood-Wakefield Historic District: Take a stroll through this historic district to explore 19th and 20th-century architecture. The district is home to several Victorian-style mansions, as well as the Wakefield Memorial Building, which once housed the town’s public library.

  • Vietnam Veterans Memorial: This touching memorial is located on Central Street and honors those from Gardner who served and died during the Vietnam War.

Natural Attractions of Gardner: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whether you’re looking for a challenging hike or a leisurely stroll, Gardner offers plenty of opportunities to enjoy the great outdoors. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Dunn State Park: This state park covers over 100 acres and offers plenty of trails for hiking and biking. Visitors can also enjoy fishing in the park’s pond.

  • Mount Watatic: This scenic mountain is located just outside of Gardner and offers a challenging hike to the summit. At the top, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Millers River: This picturesque river runs through Gardner and offers opportunities for kayaking, fishing, and picnicking along its banks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gardner

Gardner is a haven for hikers and bikers, with plenty of trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the top options:

  • Midstate Trail: This 95-mile trail runs from Rhode Island to New Hampshire, passing through Gardner along the way. The trail offers a variety of challenging terrain and scenic views.

  • Dunn State Park Trail: This easy-to-moderate loop trail winds through Dunn State Park and offers plenty of opportunities to enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

  • Mount Watatic Trail: This challenging trail takes hikers to the top of Mount Watatic and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside along the way.

Best Things to Do in Gardner: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours available in Gardner. Here are some of the top options:

  • Wachusett Mountain Ski Area: During the winter months, visitors can enjoy skiing and snowboarding at this popular ski area located just a short drive from Gardner.

  • Gardner Ale House Brewery Tour: Take a tour of this local brewery and learn about the beer-making process, then enjoy a tasting of their delicious brews.

  • Gardner Cinema: This local cinema offers both mainstream and arthouse films, as well as live performances and special events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gardner

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Gardner, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Gardner Museum and Heywood-Wakefield Historic District
Day 2 Hike the Mount Watatic Trail
Day 3 Take a brewery tour and enjoy a tasting at Gardner Ale House

Day 5 Picnic and fish by the Millers River
Day 6 Take in a movie or live performance at Gardner Cinema

Food and Drink Scene of Gardner

Gardner has plenty of options for hungry and thirsty visitors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Cider Donuts: A New England classic, these delicious donuts are a must-try when visiting Gardner.

  • Fish and Chips: Gardner is located just a short drive from Massachusetts’ coastal cities, making it a great place to enjoy some delicious fish and chips.

  • Barbecue: Gardner has a number of great barbecue spots, offering everything from pulled pork to brisket.

Local Beverages

  • Gardner Ale House Beer: This local brewery offers a number of delicious craft beers that are a must-try when visiting the area.

  • Apple Cider: Made from locally grown apples, this cider is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for the fall season.

  • Cranberry Juice: Massachusetts is one of the top cranberry-growing states in the country, and visitors to Gardner can enjoy locally made cranberry juice.

Where to Stay in Gardner: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gardner has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Colonial Hotel: This historic hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as an on-site restaurant and spa.

  • Rodeway Inn: This affordable hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

  • Gardner Ale House: This local brewery also offers rooms for rent, making it a great option for beer lovers visiting the area.

What to Buy in Gardner: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from your trip to Gardner, here are some top options:

  • Pottery and Ceramics: Gardner has a thriving pottery and ceramics community, and visitors can purchase locally made pieces from a variety of galleries and studios.

  • Apples and Cranberries: Massachusetts is famous for its apples and cranberries, and visitors to Gardner can purchase these locally grown fruits at farmers’ markets and roadside stands.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Gardner is home to several talented jewelry makers, and visitors can purchase unique and beautiful pieces made from local materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Gardner

Gardner is a small town with a strong sense of community. Visitors can expect a warm welcome and a slower pace of life than they might be used to in larger cities. The town has a rich history that’s celebrated through its museums, historic sites, and cultural attractions. Local customs include enjoying the outdoors, supporting small businesses, and participating in community events.

Best Time to Visit Gardner

The best time to visit Gardner is during the fall, when the foliage is at its peak and the weather is cool and crisp. However, there’s something to see and do in Gardner year-round, so visitors can plan their trip based on their interests.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gardner

Do:

  • Explore the local museums and historic sites.

  • Take advantage of the beautiful outdoors by hiking, biking, or fishing.

  • Try the local beer and cider.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the area.

  • Ignore the town’s history and culture.

  • Be rude to locals or tourists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gardner

Gardner is a generally safe town, but visitors should take basic safety precautions such as locking their cars and securing their belongings. Hikers and bikers should also take care on trails and be prepared for changes in weather conditions.

Health in Gardner

Gardner has a number of medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them and be prepared for changes in weather conditions.

Getting to Gardner: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gardner is located in Central Massachusetts and is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Gardner

  • Worcester Regional Airport: Approximately 30 minutes by car from Gardner, this regional airport offers flights to several major US cities.

  • Logan International Airport: Approximately an hour and a half by car from Gardner, this major airport offers flights to destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near Gardner

  • Peter Pan Bus: This bus company offers service to several nearby cities, including Worcester and Boston.

  • Greyhound Bus: This national bus company offers service to several major US cities.

Other Options

  • Train: Amtrak offers train service to nearby cities, including Worcester and Boston.

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from several major car rental companies located in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Gardner

Gardner is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Rental cars are also available for visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Gardner: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Central Massachusetts, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Wachusett Mountain State Reservation: This nearby state park offers hiking trails, skiing, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Fitchburg Art Museum: This nearby museum offers a collection of American and European art, as well as exhibitions and events.

  • Harvard, Massachusetts: This nearby town is home to a charming downtown area and several local farms offering delicious produce.

Conclusion

Gardner is a hidden gem in Central Massachusetts, offering visitors a chance to explore the town’s rich history and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring local museums, or simply relaxing in a cozy bed and breakfast, Gardner has something to offer. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip and make the most of your time in this North County town.
