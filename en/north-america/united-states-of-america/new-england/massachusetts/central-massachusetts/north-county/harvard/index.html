Introduction to Harvard

Harvard is a small town located in North County, in Central Massachusetts. It is best known for being home to Harvard University, one of the world’s most prestigious and oldest universities. The town is steeped in American history, with many notable figures attending the university or living in the area. Whether you’re interested in history, academia, or just want to explore the beautiful New England countryside, Harvard has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Harvard

Harvard has many cultural and natural attractions to explore, including museums and historical places, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and more. Here are some must-visit places in Harvard:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harvard University: As one of the world’s most prestigious universities, Harvard is a must-visit destination. Take a stroll through Harvard Yard, the heart of the university, and visit some of the famous libraries, museums, and monuments on campus.
  • Fruitlands Museum: This museum is dedicated to the Transcendentalist movement and features a collection of transcendentalist art, Native American artifacts, and more. Located on a hilltop, it offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Glessner House Museum: A National Historic Landmark, the Glessner House Museum displays the artwork and furnishings of Henry Hobson Richardson, one of the great American architects. It’s a fascinating chance to see an example of late 19th century architecture.

Natural Attractions of Harvard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fruitlands Museum Trail: The trails at the Fruitlands Museum lead through beautiful woodlands and fields, featuring stunning views of the Nashua River Valley. Enjoy the landscape as you hike or bike throughout the trail.
  • Bowers Springs Conservation Area: Hike or bike the trails of Bowers Springs Conservation Area to see unique flora and fauna and take a look at Nashoba Brook.
  • Oxbow National Wildlife Refuge: Covering 1,665 acres, this scenic area is home to many species of wildlife and offers fantastic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harvard

Harvard offers a variety of hiking and biking trails suitable for all skill levels. The trails weave through beautiful woodlands, fields, and mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding scenery. Here are some of the best trails to explore in Harvard:

  • Harvard Trails: The Harvard trails consist of over 150 miles of trails, suitable for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. They take you through some of the most scenic areas of the state.
  • The Bay Circuit Trail: A 200-mile trail through 37 towns and cities in Massachusetts, the Bay Circuit Trail offers some of the best hiking and biking opportunities in the state. It’s a great way to explore the natural beauty of Massachusetts.
  • Nashua River Rail Trail: This paved trail provides a great way to explore the Nashua River Valley and the surrounding woodland. It’s perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a walk with your furry friend.

Best Things to Do in Harvard: Activities and Tours

Harvard has something for everyone, from historical tours to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best things to do in Harvard:

  • Harvard Historical Tours: Take a guided tour through historic Harvard University, discovering the art, architecture, and famous landmarks.
  • Nashoba Valley Winery: Take a tour of the winery and sample some of the best wines in the area. Don’t forget to visit the gift shop to purchase a bottle of your favorite wine!
  • Skydive Pepperell: For those who love adventure, skydiving is an experience not to be missed. Take in the stunning vistas of the Nashua River Valley and the Blue Hills while experiencing the freedom of skydiving.

Food and Drink Scene of Harvard

Harvard has a thriving food and drink scene, with many local eateries and bars to explore. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England dish, the lobster roll is a must-try when visiting Harvard. It consists of a buttery toasted bun piled high with fresh lobster meat.
  • Boston Cream Pie: Originating in Boston, this dessert consists of layers of sponge cake filled with custard cream and topped with chocolate ganache.
  • Clam Chowder: Another classic New England dish, clam chowder is a hearty soup made with clams, potatoes, onions, and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Sam Adams Boston Lager: This popular beer was first brewed in Boston in the 1980s and is now available nationwide.
  • Cranberry Juice: Massachusetts is the second-largest producer of cranberries in the US, making it the perfect spot to try some fresh cranberry juice.
  • Boston Cocktail: A classic cocktail, the Boston is made with gin, apricot brandy, triple sec, and lime juice.

Where to Stay in Harvard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Harvard, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Harvard:

  • The Inn at Harvard: The Inn at Harvard is a classic New England establishment located in the heart of Harvard Square. It offers luxurious rooms and suites with all the amenities you’d expect from a high-end hotel.
  • Harvard Square Hotel: The Harvard Square Hotel is a modern and chic hotel located in the center of Harvard Square. It offers comfortable rooms and suites with all the necessary amenities.
  • Harvard Hostel: For budget travelers, the Harvard Hostel is a great option. It offers affordable dorm rooms and private rooms with a shared bathroom. The hostel is located just a short walk from Harvard Square.

What to Buy in Harvard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Harvard is home to many local shops and boutiques, offering unique and handmade souvenirs and local products. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Harvard University Apparel: Show off your love for Harvard with t-shirts, hoodies, and more featuring the university logo.
  • Walden Pond Books: This independent bookstore specializes in transcendentalist literature and gifts. It’s a great place to find unique and thoughtful gifts for book lovers.
  • Harvard General Store: The Harvard General Store is a small shop selling locally made gifts and souvenirs. It’s a great place to find handmade candles, pottery, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Harvard

Harvard is a friendly and welcoming town, with a rich history and strong academic culture. Some customs and traditions to be aware of when visiting Harvard are:

  • Academic culture: As a town heavily influenced by Harvard University, academic culture is prevalent in Harvard. It’s not uncommon to see students reading and working in local cafes and parks.
  • New England charm: Harvard is located in New England, an area known for its friendly and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors can expect to receive a warm welcome and experience the charm of the region.
  • Transcendentalism: Harvard has a strong connection to the Transcendentalist movement, a philosophical and literary movement that emerged in the 19th century. Many landmarks and museums in Harvard are dedicated to this movement.

Best Time to Visit Harvard

The best time to visit Harvard is during the fall and spring months when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. The fall months offer stunning leaf-peeping opportunities, and the spring months are a great time to explore the surrounding landscape. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harvard

Do:

  • Do take a historical tour of the town to learn about its rich history and culture.
  • Do book your accommodation in advance, especially during peak travel season.
  • Do try out the local food and drink scene, including lobster rolls, clam chowder, and Boston cream pie.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing for the weather, as it can change quickly.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray if exploring the outdoors in the summer months.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers in restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harvard

Harvard is a safe and welcoming town, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions, such as staying aware of their surroundings, especially at night, and securing their belongings.

Health in Harvard

Harvard has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should make sure to have adequate health insurance and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Harvard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Harvard is located in Central Massachusetts, making it easily accessible from surrounding areas. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Airports: The closest airports to Harvard are Boston Logan International Airport and Worcester Regional Airport.
  • Bus: The Peter Pan Bus Lines and Greyhound both offer regular bus service to Harvard.
  • Train: Amtrak has a station located in nearby Worcester, offering regular train service to Boston, New York, and other nearby locations.

How to Get Around in Harvard

Harvard is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. However, for those traveling longer distances, renting a car or taking a taxi may be necessary.

Where to Go Next from Harvard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harvard is located in the heart of New England, making it a great starting point for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Boston: A short drive or train ride away, Boston is a historic city with plenty to see and do. Be sure to visit the Freedom Trail, Boston Common, and the USS Constitution Museum.
  • Cape Cod: Just a short drive from Harvard, Cape Cod is a beautiful area known for its beaches, lighthouses, and seafood.
  • Worcester: Located just 30 minutes west of Harvard, Worcester is a vibrant city with plenty of museums and cultural attractions to explore.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, academia, or outdoor adventures, Harvard has something for everyone. With plenty of cultural and natural attractions, as well as delicious food and drink options and cozy accommodation, a trip to Harvard is not to be missed. Plan your trip today and experience all that this charming New England town has to offer.
