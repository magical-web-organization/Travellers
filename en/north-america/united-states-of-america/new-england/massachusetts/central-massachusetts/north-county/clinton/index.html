Introduction to Clinton

Located in Central Massachusetts, Clinton is a charming town with a rich history and natural beauty. Situated in North County, Clinton is an ideal destination for those looking to explore the great outdoors while experiencing small-town life in New England.

List of Places to Visit in Clinton

Whether you’re interested in learning about local history or taking in the beauty of nature, Clinton has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Clinton.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Russian Icons: The Museum of Russian Icons houses the largest collection of Russian icons in North America, with over 1,000 icons spanning six centuries.

  • The Clinton Historical Society: The Clinton Historical Society is dedicated to preserving and promoting Clinton’s unique history through exhibits, programs, and events.

  • The Sweeney Memorial Museum: The Sweeney Memorial Museum, located in the historic Theodore Sweeney House, offers visitors a glimpse into life in Clinton in the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Clinton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wachusett Reservoir: The Wachusett Reservoir is a popular destination for fishing, kayaking, and hiking.

  • Monadnock-Sunapee Greenway Trail: The Monadnock-Sunapee Greenway Trail is a 50-mile hiking trail that passes through Clinton and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Berlin State Forest: Berlin State Forest is home to miles of hiking and mountain biking trails, as well as a spring-fed pond for swimming and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clinton

The town of Clinton is surrounded by beautiful nature trails and scenic bike routes, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Clinton include:

  • The Wachusett Reservoir Loop: The Wachusett Reservoir Loop is a 7-mile trail that offers stunning views of the reservoir and surrounding landscape.

  • The Lancaster Greenway: The Lancaster Greenway is a 4.5-mile trail that passes through Clinton and offers views of historic mills and scenic forests.

  • The Nashua River Rail Trail: The Nashua River Rail Trail is a 12-mile trail that runs through several towns, including Clinton, and is perfect for walking, biking, and rollerblading.

Best Things to Do in Clinton: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other fun and exciting activities to enjoy in Clinton. Some of the best things to do in Clinton include:

  • Take a guided tour of the Museum of Russian Icons to learn about the history and significance of Russian icons.

  • Visit the Wachusett Reservoir for hiking, fishing, kayaking, and scenic views.

  • Take a walk through the Clinton Greenway Conservation Area to enjoy the natural beauty of the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clinton

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Russian Icons and take a guided tour.

Day 2: Explore the Wachusett Reservoir and enjoy a day of hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

Day 3: Visit the Clinton Historical Society and learn about the town’s unique history.

Day 4: Take a bike ride on the Lancaster Greenway and enjoy the scenic beauty of Central Massachusetts.

Day 5: Kayak on the Nashua River and take in the natural beauty of the area.

Day 6: Enjoy a day of shopping and dining at the many restaurants and shops located in downtown Clinton.

Day 7: Relax at the Berlin State Forest and enjoy a picnic or swim in the spring-fed pond.

Food and Drink Scene of Clinton

Clinton offers a range of dining options, from classic New England seafood to ethnic cuisine. Here are some of the top dishes and local beverages to try while in Clinton.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster rolls: A classic New England dish, lobster rolls are a must-try when in Clinton.

  • Polish pierogies: Clinton has a large Polish community, and pierogies are a local specialty.

  • Clam chowder: Another New England classic, clam chowder is a staple at many Clinton restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Wachusett Brewery: The Wachusett Brewery is a local favorite, producing a range of craft beers.

  • Nashoba Valley Winery: The Nashoba Valley Winery offers a range of award-winning wines and is a popular destination for wine tastings.

Where to Stay in Clinton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clinton offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy bed and breakfasts to modern hotels. Here are some top options for where to stay in Clinton:

  • The Berlin Lodge: The Berlin Lodge is a charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Clinton, offering comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

  • The Clinton Motel: The Clinton Motel offers affordable and comfortable accommodations, perfect for budget-conscious travelers.

  • The Colonial Hotel: The Colonial Hotel is a modern, full-service hotel located just outside of Clinton, offering a range of amenities and luxurious accommodations.

What to Buy in Clinton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From locally-made crafts to specialty foods, there are many great souvenirs to bring home from Clinton. Here are some top options:

  • Russian icons and crafts from the Museum of Russian Icons gift shop

  • Polish pierogies and other ethnic foods from local markets

  • Locally-made pottery and other crafts from the Clinton Farmers’ Market

Local Culture and Customs of Clinton

Clinton’s rich history and cultural diversity are reflected in its customs and traditions. The town is known for its vibrant ethnic festivals, including the Clinton Polish Festival and the Irish Music Festival.

Best Time to Visit Clinton

The best time to visit Clinton is in the fall, when the leaves change color and the weather is cool and crisp. Summer is also a great time to visit for outdoor activities, while winter offers opportunities for snowshoeing and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clinton

Do:

  • Visit the Museum of Russian Icons and take a guided tour

  • Explore the natural beauty of the Wachusett Reservoir and other outdoor attractions

  • Attend one of the town’s many cultural festivals

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions

  • Overlook the town’s many natural attractions

  • Forget to try the local cuisine

Safety and Health

Safety in Clinton

Clinton is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take basic safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their belongings.

Health in Clinton

Clinton has several medical facilities, including Clinton Hospital and a CVS MinuteClinic, for visitors in need of medical attention.

Getting to Clinton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clinton

The nearest airport to Clinton is Boston Logan International Airport, located approximately 50 miles east.

Bus Stations Near Clinton

There are several bus stations located near Clinton, including the Worcester Regional Transit Authority and the Peter Pan Bus Lines.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Clinton by car, with several major highways passing through the area, including Interstate 495 and Route 2.

How to Get Around in Clinton

The town of Clinton is small and easily walkable, with several parks, trails, and restaurants located within walking distance of downtown.

Where to Go Next from Clinton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clinton is located in the heart of Central Massachusetts and is surrounded by many other great destinations and attractions. Here are some nearby destinations to consider for a day trip or weekend getaway:

  • Worcester: Located just a short drive from Clinton, Worcester is a bustling city with a thriving arts scene, great restaurants, and many cultural attractions.

  • Concord: Concord is a historic town located just over an hour’s drive from Clinton, known for its role in the American Revolution and as the home of several famous writers, including Louisa May Alcott.

  • Boston: Boston is located approximately 50 miles east of Clinton and is known for its rich history, great food, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring local history, taking in natural beauty, or simply enjoying small-town life in New England, Clinton has something for everyone. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural attractions, Clinton is a destination that’s not to be missed.
