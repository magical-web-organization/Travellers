Introduction to Waterbury

Located in Litchfield Hills, Connecticut, Waterbury is a city that seamlessly blends its rich industrial history with gorgeous natural landscapes. A few hours’ drive from Boston and New York City, Waterbury attracts visitors with its vibrant downtown, museums, nature reserves, and great food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Waterbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Waterbury has a wealth of cultural and historical destinations waiting to be explored. Some of the must-visit museums and historical sites in Waterbury include:

  • The Mattatuck Museum: This art and history museum houses a permanent collection of over 30,000 objects ranging from contemporary art to ancient artifacts.

  • The Timexpo Museum: This museum showcases the history of Timex watches, which have been made in Waterbury for over a century. The museum features exhibits on everything from watchmaking to timekeeping devices.

  • The Palace Theater: A beautifully restored theater with an elaborate interior, the Palace Theater is an iconic landmark in Waterbury. Catch a show here or take a guided tour to admire its stunning art deco architecture.

  • The Holy Land USA: This bizarre yet fascinating park features miniature versions of holy sites from around the world, including Bethlehem, Jerusalem, and Golgotha. While it may be in disrepair, it’s worth a visit for the unique experience.

Natural Attractions of Waterbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Surrounded by rolling hills, Waterbury is a great place to explore Connecticut’s natural beauty. Some top outdoor destinations in the area include:

  • West Rock Ridge State Park: A favorite among hikers, this park boasts several trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Fulton Park: This beautiful urban park is the perfect spot for a picnic, walk, or relaxing in the sun. It also features a lake with paddleboat rentals.

  • Black Rock State Park: Located in nearby Thomaston, this park offers a range of activities, including camping, swimming, and fishing. Visitors can also hike around the park’s scenic trails.

  • Hop Brook Lake: Built by the Army Corps of Engineers, this lake offers excellent fishing opportunities for trout, bass, and bluegill. Surrounding the lake are hiking trails, picnic areas, and a beach where visitors can swim and sunbathe.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waterbury

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will find plenty to keep them busy in and around Waterbury. Some popular trails in the area include:

  • Mattatuck Trail: A 36-mile trail that winds through some of the most scenic parts of Litchfield Hills. The trail begins at the Massachusetts border and ends near Waterbury.

  • Naugatuck State Forest: With over 5,000 acres of forest and trails, the Naugatuck State Forest is a great place for hiking, biking, and camping. The forest also features several streams and ponds for fishing and swimming.

  • River Trail: This paved trail runs along the Naugatuck River and is perfect for biking and walking. Visitors can take in the views of the river and surrounding hills while enjoying a leisurely ride or stroll.

Best Things to Do in Waterbury: Activities and Tours

With its mix of culture, history, and natural beauty, Waterbury has something for everyone. Some top things to do in the area include:

  • Tour the Timexpo Museum: Learn about the history of Timex watches and see rare timekeeping devices up close.

  • Visit the Mattatuck Museum: Admire the permanent and temporary exhibits showcasing the art and history of the region.

  • Go for a Hike: Explore the area’s beautiful parks and nature reserves on foot.

  • Catch a Show at the Palace Theater: Experience a live performance or participate in a guided tour of this stunning historic theater.

  • Take a Food Tour: Discover Waterbury’s culinary scene with a guided tour that includes a variety of tastings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Waterbury

Day 1: Start with a visit to the Mattatuck Museum, followed by a hike on the Mattatuck Trail.

Day 2: Take in a show at the Palace Theater and dine at one of the city’s top restaurants.

Day 3: Explore the history of watchmaking at the Timexpo Museum, followed by a leisurely bike ride along the River Trail.

Day 4: Spend the day at Naugatuck State Forest, swimming in the ponds, hiking the trails, and grilling up some lunch.

Day 5: Walk around and explore the city center; visit the Silas Bronson Library, the Brass Mill Center Mall, and grab a drink at one of the city’s bars or pubs.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive around Litchfield Hills and stop by the enchanting village of Kent for some shopping and dining.

Day 7: Learn about the unique and historical Holy Land USA, followed by a picnic in Fulton Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Waterbury

Must-Try Dishes

Waterbury is home to delicious and diverse food options. With Italian influence, the food scene in Waterbury is unique and rich in flavors. Some popular foods in the city include:

  • Peking Duck: A specialty at Golden Palace, this dish is a crispy, succulent treat.

  • New Haven-style Pizza: It’s thin, crispy, and incredibly mouth-watering. Frank Pepe Pizzeria Napoletana makes some of the best pizza in the city.

  • Hot Dogs: Take a bite out of a classic New England-style hot dog at Frankie’s Hot Dogs. The chili dog is a particular favorite.

Local Beverages

Waterbury is known for its drinking scene, which features craft beer, wine, cider, and spirits. Some top places to try are:

  • Brass Works Brewing Company: Offering something different at every brew, stop by this brewery to try their wide selection of craft beers.

  • Averill Farm: The oldest family-owned orchard in Connecticut offers a range of fruit wines, cider, and hard cider.

Where to Stay in Waterbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Waterbury has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, inns, and guesthouses. Some top picks include:

  • The Mayflower Grace: This luxury hotel is a retreat from urban life, offering luxurious accommodations complemented by fine dining, fitness facilities, and a full-service spa.

  • Courtyard Marriott: Located in downtown Waterbury, this hotel features modern rooms, an indoor pool, fitness center, and event space.

  • Waterbury Big Apple Hostel: A great choice for budget travelers, Waterbury Big Apple Hostel offers affordable dorm and private rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a lounge area.

What to Buy in Waterbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souvenir shopping in Waterbury is a must, with its unique shopping center locations all around the city. Here are some items visitors should not miss:

  • Watch: With a long history of watchmaking, Timex watches can be purchased at the Timexpo Museum.

  • Pottery: Artist Carole Huber is known for her beautiful pottery work, which can be bought at the Artists & Artisans gallery in Main Street.

  • Local Cheese: Just a few miles north of the city is Arethusa Farm, where they make artisanal cheeses that can be purchased at their farm shop or local market.

Local Culture and Customs of Waterbury

Waterbury is a melting pot of cultures and ethnicities, and its people are proud of their heritage. Visiting historic sites and art galleries is recommended to get a grasp of the art and music scene in this city.

Best Time to Visit Waterbury

Visit Waterbury in summer when the weather is at its most beautiful. Fall is another ideal time to visit as autumn foliage colors are spectacular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waterbury

Do:

  • Visit the Mattatuck or Timexpo museums to learn about the history and culture of the city.

  • Enjoy a hike at the Naugatuck State Forest or walk around downtown Waterbury.

  • Shop for locally-made pottery, artisanal cheese, and Timex watches.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or cultures, as the city is home to people from diverse backgrounds.

  • Ignore the natural beauty that surrounds Waterbury, as the area is home to beautiful parks, lakes, and hiking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waterbury

Waterbury is considered a safe city with low violent crime rates. Visitors are advised to take precautions when walking in parks after dark or leaving valuable items in their car. It’s always better to park your car in safe areas.

Health in Waterbury

Waterbury has plenty of hospitals and clinics in the city for any health-related emergency. Visitors should consider getting health insurance before traveling to any foreign places.

Getting to Waterbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Waterbury is easily accessible via plane, train, or car. Some top transportation options include:

  • Airports Near Waterbury: Bradley International Airport and John F. Kennedy Airport are the nearest to the city.

  • Bus Stations Near Waterbury: Peter Pan Bus Lines provides local bus services in the city.

  • Other Options: Visitors traveling by car can take the Interstate 84 and follow signs to Waterbury.

How to Get Around in Waterbury

Waterbury is relatively small, and it is easy to navigate around the city by car. The area is quite walkable, and the city provides public transportation options, including buses that run between areas outside of downtown or rural places in Litchfield Hills.

Where to Go Next from Waterbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waterbury is close to several other interesting places to visit. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Lake Compounce Amusement Park: Just 25 minutes away near Bristol, this amusement park is one of the oldest in the country and offers rides and attractions for all ages.

  • Litchfield Hills Wine Trail: Located in the surrounding countryside, this trail showcases some of Connecticut’s best wineries and vineyards.

  • Historic New Haven: This city is about an hour’s drive away and offers visitors the chance to explore Yale University, visit the museums, and savor some of the best pizza in the country.

Conclusion

Waterbury is a beautiful, vibrant, and unique city that offers something for everyone. From its museums, historic sites, natural beauty to its culinary scene, there’s plenty to discover and enjoy in this Connecticut city.
