Introduction to Kent

Located in Litchfield Hills, in Connecticut, Kent is a charming small town in New England, United States. Known for its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant arts scene, Kent is a popular destination for travelers seeking a mix of adventure and culture. This picturesque town is surrounded by rolling hills, and the Housatonic River flows through it, making it a perfect spot for outdoor activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, history enthusiast, or culture buff, Kent has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kent

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Kent Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of Kent, the surrounding area, and the lives of its residents, from the colonial era to the present day.
  • Sloane-Stanley Museum and Kent Iron Furnace: Learn about the history of iron-making in Kent and the impact it had on the town’s growth and development.
  • Morrison Gallery: This contemporary art gallery showcases the works of emerging and established artists.

Natural Attractions of Kent: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kent Falls State Park: Home to Kent Falls, this park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the waterfall and the surrounding woods.
  • Macedonia Brook State Park: This park has miles of hiking trails, stunning views, and campsites perfect for an overnight stay.
  • Bulls Bridge: This historic bridge on the Housatonic river provides a scenic backdrop for photos and a spot for fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kent

  • The Appalachian Trail: For experienced hikers, the Appalachian Trail runs through Kent providing challenging hikes through scenic vistas.
  • Kent Land Trust: Trail maps and access can be found on this land trust website that cover the many hiking options in Kent.
  • Kent Bike Tours: Biking enthusiasts will love exploring Kent by bike on a guided tour that will take you to the best spots in town.

Best Things to Do in Kent: Activities and Tours

  • Housatonic River Boat Tours: Take a ride on a boat along the Housatonic River to see the stunning scenery and wildlife in the area.
  • Kent Art Walk: Stroll through the streets of Kent to see the art galleries and shops showcasing local artists and crafts.
  • Connecticut Antique Machinery Association Museum: This museum displays the industrial heritage of Connecticut with exhibits on machines, power sources, and tools.

Food and Drink Scene of Kent

Must-Try Dishes

  • Connecticut Lobster Roll: Try this classic New England dish at Bulls Bridge Inn.
  • Kent Pizza Garden: Savor the local flavors at this famous pizzeria in town.
  • Ice Cream at Ferris Acres Creamery: Enjoy handmade ice cream in an idyllic setting right outside of town.

Local Beverages

  • Housatonic River Brewing: Stop by for a beer at this local brewery which is famous for its craft beers.
  • Kent Coffee and Chocolate Company: Enjoy specialty coffee and hand-crafted chocolates at this cozy café.
  • Sunset Meadow Vineyards: Indulge in wine tastings and scenic views at this local vineyard.

Where to Stay in Kent: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Fife ‘n Drum Inn: This historic, elegant inn offers luxuriously appointed rooms and a fine dining experience.
  • The Inn at Kent Falls: This charming bed and breakfast overlooking the Kent Falls State Park offers stunning views and cozy rooms.
  • Cornwall Inn and Lodge: This one-of-a-kind lodge is located on an old country road in the foothills of the Berkshires and offers exceptional lodgings and unique rooms.

What to Buy in Kent: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artwork and crafts from local galleries and shops.
  • Honey and maple syrup from local farms.
  • Locally sourced specialty food items from shops like the Kent Market and Davis IGA.

Local Culture and Customs of Kent

Kent embraces its New England heritage through its local festivals, including the Fourth of July celebration, Autumn Fest, and the Agricultural Fair. Visitors should also be aware of the town’s conservation efforts aimed at preserving the natural beauty of Kent and its surrounding areas.

Best Time to Visit Kent

The best time to visit Kent is from May to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. Fall is the most popular time of year when the foliage is at its peak, and temperatures are comfortable. Summer is an excellent time to visit for outdoor activities and festivals, while winter is ideal for skiing and winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kent

Do:

  • Take advantage of the natural beauty and go hiking, boating or biking.
  • Visit the art galleries and shops to see the work of local artists and artisans.
  • Experience local culture through festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore conservation and preservation efforts to protect Kent and its surroundings.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kent

Kent is a safe town, and visitors can explore without major concerns. However, visitors should take usual safety precautions such as locking car doors, watching belongings and being aware of surroundings.

Health in Kent

There is no specific health risk in Kent, and the town has a medical clinic in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Kent: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kent

The nearest airport is Bradley International Airport in Hartford. From the airport, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Kent, which is approximately a 90 minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Kent

Visitors can take a bus from Hartford Bus Station to Kent, which takes approximately 2 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can drive from nearby major cities like New York City and Boston. Alternatively, visitors can take the Amtrak to Waterbury or Metro North Commuter Railroad to New Haven and then take a taxi or rental car to Kent.

How to Get Around in Kent

Visitors can drive or bike around Kent easily to visit the different attractions. Parking is available in town, but some of the more popular parks have limited parking so plan accordingly.

Where to Go Next from Kent: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Waramaug State Park: This state park is perfect for a day-trip for boating, swimming or hiking and is just a short drive from Kent.
  • Mohawk Mountain Ski Area: Perfect for winter sports enthusiasts, this ski area is just a short drive from Kent.
  • Lime Rock Park Racetrack: Just a few miles away, Lime Rock Park is a great place to catch a car race for those interested in motorsports.

Conclusion

Kent offers a serene and unique experience for visitors through its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Plan your trip accordingly and explore all of what Kent has to offer. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences or just to relax and unwind, Kent has something for everyone.
