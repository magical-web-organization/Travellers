Introduction to Norfolk

Located in Litchfield Hills, Connecticut, Norfolk is a charming town in New England. Known for its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions, this quaint town offers a plethora of experiences for visitors. Norfolk is surrounded by rolling hills, jagged cliffs, and dense forests, which make it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. In this guide, we will help you discover the best places to visit, dine, sleep, hike, and explore in Norfolk, Connecticut.

List of Places to Visit in Norfolk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Norfolk Historical Society Museum: This museum chronicles the town’s rich history and showcases artifacts related to early settlers, industry, and agriculture.
  • Historical Society of Goshen Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Goshen and the surrounding areas. Visitors can explore exhibits related to the town’s early settlers, industrial heritage, and Civil War history.
  • Infinity Hall & Bistro: The Infinity Hall is a historic music hall that presents live music performances. Its adjacent bistro serves farm-to-table cuisine cooked using locally sourced ingredients.

Natural Attractions of Norfolk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Mountain Forest: This 6,000-acre forest is perfect for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can hike, bike, fish, and camp here.
  • Dennis Hill State Park: This park offers stunning views of the Litchfield Hills and the Housatonic River Valley. It is an ideal place for picnicking, hiking, and birdwatching.
  • Tobey Pond Recreation Area: This is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Norfolk

  • The Appalachian Trail: The Appalachian Trail runs through Norfolk, offering hikers a chance to explore the town’s natural beauty.
  • Campbell Falls State Park Reserve: This park features a 100-foot waterfall and offers a scenic hiking trail to the top of the falls.
  • People’s State Forest: This forest offers a wide range of hiking trails, from easy walks to challenging climbs. The trails lead visitors through dense forests, along rivers, and over rocky ridges.

Best Things to Do in Norfolk: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Norfolk Farmers Market: This weekly farmers market offers fresh produce, locally made food items, and artisanal crafts.
  • Tour the Litchfield Distillery: This distillery produces award-winning spirits using locally grown grains. Visitors can take a tour, learn about the distillation process, and sample the products.
  • Attend a Concert at the Infinity Music Hall & Bistro: This music hall is renowned for its eclectic lineup of musical acts and outstanding sound quality.

Week-Long Itinerary for Norfolk

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Norfolk Historical Society Museum and the Historical Society of Goshen Museum
Day 2 Hike the Campbell Falls State Park Reserve and picnic at Dennis Hill State Park
Day 3 Explore the Great Mountain Forest and go fishing at Tobey Pond Recreation Area

Day 5 Attend a concert at the Infinity Music Hall & Bistro
Day 6 Go on a scenic drive through the Litchfield Hills and visit local farms
Day 7 Relax and unwind at your hotel or explore Norfolk at your leisure

Food and Drink Scene of Norfolk

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: This classic New England dish is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Connecticut-style Pizza: This pizza is characterized by its thin, crispy crust and fresh toppings.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: These sweet treats are made using fresh apple cider and topped with cinnamon sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Norfolk is home to several excellent craft breweries, including the Black Hog Brewery and the Kent Falls Brewing Company.
  • Connecticut Wine: Visitors can try locally produced wines at the Sunset Meadow Vineyards and the Hopkins Vineyard.

Where to Stay in Norfolk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Manor House Inn: This elegant bed and breakfast offers luxurious accommodations and delicious breakfast.
  • Blackberry River Inn: This historic inn is located in a picturesque setting and offers cozy guest rooms and a warm fireplace.
  • The Inn at Iron Masters: This charming inn is the perfect place to relax after a day of exploring Norfolk.

What to Buy in Norfolk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Norfolk is known for its delicious maple syrup, which is harvested in the surrounding forests.
  • Pottery: Visitors can find unique handcrafted pottery items made by local artisans.
  • Antiques: Norfolk and the surrounding areas are home to several antique shops and markets, where visitors can find one-of-a-kind items.

Local Culture and Customs of Norfolk

Norfolk has a rich cultural heritage, with a variety of festivals and events hosted throughout the year. The town is known for its love of music, and visitors can experience live performances at the Infinity Music Hall & Bistro. Norfolk also has a vibrant arts scene, with galleries and museums showcasing local artists.

Best Time to Visit Norfolk

The best time to visit Norfolk is during the fall, when the leaves change color and the town is at its most beautiful. The weather is also mild and pleasant during this time. Spring and summer are also good times to visit, as the town is bustling with outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Norfolk

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the town by hiking, biking, or kayaking.
  • Visit the local farmers market and try the fresh produce.
  • Attend live music performances at the Infinity Music Hall & Bistro.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places or on hiking trails.
  • Ignore safety guidelines while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Norfolk

Norfolk is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and take appropriate safety precautions, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Norfolk

Norfolk has several healthcare facilities, including the Charlotte Hungerford Hospital and the Sharon Hospital. Visitors should also take precautions against tick-borne illnesses, which are prevalent in the area.

Getting to Norfolk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Norfolk

The closest major airport to Norfolk is Bradley International Airport, which is located about 1 hour and 20 minutes away by car. Other nearby airports include the Westchester County Airport and the Albany International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Norfolk

The closest bus station to Norfolk is located in Torrington, about 20 minutes away by car. Visitors can also take buses from Hartford or New York City to Torrington and then travel to Norfolk by car or taxi.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Norfolk by driving or by taking a train to a nearby station and then renting a car.

How to Get Around in Norfolk

The best way to explore Norfolk is by car. Visitors can rent a car at nearby airports or bus stations. Bicycles are also a popular way to get around town, with several rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Norfolk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Litchfield: This charming town is known for its historic architecture, quaint boutiques, and excellent restaurants.
  • Berkshires: Located in Massachusetts, the Berkshires are known for their scenic beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities.
  • New York City: Visitors can take a day trip to New York City, which is about 2 hours away by car or train.

Conclusion

Norfolk is a hidden gem in Connecticut that offers a variety of experiences for visitors. From natural beauty to historic landmarks to cultural attractions, this town has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, attend live music performances, or enjoy delicious local cuisine, you won’t be disappointed in Norfolk.
