Introduction to Sherman

Sherman is a quaint town located in Litchfield Hills in Connecticut. It’s known for its beautiful natural surroundings, outdoor activities, and rich history that dates back to the 18th century. Whether you’re traveling solo, as a couple, or with your family, Sherman has something to offer everyone. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Sherman.

List of Places to Visit in Sherman

Sherman is a small town, but it has plenty of attractions and landmarks worth visiting. Here are some of the best places to visit in Sherman:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Old Store Museum & Gift Shop: This museum showcases the history of Sherman and allows visitors to experience the town’s past through a collection of artifacts, photographs, and stories.
  • The Sherman Historical Society: The Sherman Historical Society is dedicated to preserving and promoting the history of Sherman. The museum features artifacts, photographs, and documents, allowing visitors to learn about the town’s rich heritage.
  • Holy Trinity Orthodox Church: This historic Orthodox Church was built in 1897 and is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful interior.

Natural Attractions of Sherman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Squantz Pond State Park: This beautiful state park is a popular spot for swimming, boating, fishing, and hiking. The park features a beach, picnic areas, a playground, and various hiking trails.
  • Appalachian Trail: The Appalachian Trail runs through Sherman and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and even horseback riding.
  • Candlewood Lake: Candlewood Lake is the largest lake in Connecticut and offers plenty of opportunities for boating, swimming, fishing, and other water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sherman

Sherman is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Sherman:

  • Appalachian Trail: The Appalachian Trail is a 2,190-mile trail that runs from Georgia to Maine. The trail passes through Sherman and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Shepaug Dam: The Shepaug Dam is a popular spot for fishing and hiking, with several scenic trails to choose from.
  • White Memorial Conservation Center: This nature preserve features over 40 miles of hiking trails that wind through forests, fields, and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Sherman: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with kids or looking for a romantic getaway, Sherman has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Sherman:

  • Wine Tasting at White Silo Winery: The White Silo Winery offers a variety of wines to taste, along with stunning views of the surrounding vineyards.
  • Animal Adventures at Action Wildlife: Action Wildlife is home to over 300 animals from around the world, including lions, tigers, bears, and more. Visitors can take a guided tour of the facility and interact with the animals up close.
  • Fishing at Candlewood Lake: Candlewood Lake is a prime spot for fishing, with several species of fish to catch, including bass, trout, and walleye.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sherman

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Sherman, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Old Store Museum & Gift Shop, the Sherman Historical Society, and Holy Trinity Orthodox Church
2 Hike the Appalachian Trail or explore Shepaug Dam
3 Spend the day at Squantz Pond State Park, swim, boat or hike
4 Go wine tasting at White Silo Winery and have lunch at their onsite café
5 Take a tour at the Action Wildlife park and explore the surrounding hiking trails or take a picnic lunch
6 Rent a boat at Candlewood Lake and enjoy the day’s activities
7 Enjoy local flavors and experience the food and drink scene in Sherman before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Sherman

Sherman may be a small town, but it still boasts an impressive selection of restaurants and bars. Here’s what to try in Sherman’s food and drink scene:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Connecticut Lobster Rolls: Lobster rolls are a must-try in Connecticut and Sherman has some of the best. Enjoy fresh, tender lobster meat in a buttery roll, traditionally served with fries and coleslaw.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: Enjoy freshly made, warm apple cider donuts served here for breakfast and brunch, and even some markets, and convenience stores carry them for sale throughout the day.
  • Farm-To-Table Cuisine: Enjoy fresh, locally sourced ingredients, and innovative dishes at the local farm-to-table restaurants - a treat for food lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Connecticut Wines: The state of Connecticut has a thriving wine industry, and Sherman is no exception. Sample award-winning wines from local vineyards like White Silo Winery.
  • Connecticut Beers: Try some of the locally brewed beers at the namesake brewery in New Milford Brewing Company, and lively atmosphere at the Bull’s Bridge Inn.

Where to Stay in Sherman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sherman has a variety of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sherman:

  • The White Hart Inn: This historic inn is located in the heart of downtown Sherman and offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • The Guesthouse at Woodward House: This guesthouse offers eight beautifully designed rooms with all amenities, providing a quiet getaway in a peaceful countryside setting.
  • The Mayflower Inn & Spa: This luxury hotel and spa is set in a woodland setting and boasts beautifully appointed rooms and dining options.

What to Buy in Sherman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many things you can buy in Sherman to take home as a souvenir or give as a gift. Here are some of the best things to buy in Sherman:

  • Local Honey: The Connecticut countryside is home to a variety of flowers, giving the local honey a unique and delicious flavor.
  • Artisanal Jams: Sherman has several jam makers in town who sell their jams at the local farmer’s market. These jams are made from local fruits and make for a delicious gift.
  • Pottery and Crafts: Sherman has a thriving arts community, with many artists and artisans creating beautiful pottery, crafts, and unique gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Sherman

Sherman is a friendly town with a strong sense of community. Residents take pride in preserving the town’s history while also embracing the present. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the town’s small-town charm and customs, like stopping to talk with locals and admiring the natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Sherman

The best time to visit Sherman is during the summer, which is the peak tourist season in town. During this time, you can take advantage of outdoor activities like swimming, boating, and hiking in the beautiful New England weather. However, the fall season, particularly in October, offers stunning foliage to see and the local fairs are eclectic.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sherman

  • Do eat local and sample the local food and drink scene of Sherman.
  • Do take time to chat with the locals to learn more about the town’s history and culture.

  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t ignore local customs, show respect towards the residents and town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sherman

Sherman is a safe town to visit, with a low crime rate and a friendly community. Visitors are still advised to take standard precautions, including locking doors, and not leaving valuables visible in vehicles.

Health in Sherman

Sherman is home to quality healthcare facilities, and the town is generally accessible for individuals with disabilities. Health needs shouldn’t be a concern while visiting this town.

Getting to Sherman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sherman is located in Connecticut in the New England region of the United States. There are several options to reach Sherman.

Airports Near Sherman

The nearest major airport to Sherman is Bradley International Airport in Hartford. From the airport, visitors can either rent a car or take a shuttle, bus and/or taxi to the final destination.

Bus Stations Near Sherman

The nearest Greyhound station is in Bridgeport, Connecticut which is at a distance of 45 minutes by car. Another option to consider is Litchfield Transit that runs multiple routes to/from Sherman daily.

Other Options

Driving your own car either from New York City, Boston or any other nearby towns, taking in the New England scenery is an amazing option to consider while planning your trip to Sherman.

How to Get Around in Sherman

To navigate within Sherman, visitors can either rent a car, bike, or if preferred opt for private shuttle services or taxis that operators within the area. Many attractions are within short driving distance loated less than 30 minutes away.

Where to Go Next from Sherman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to explore more of New England? Sherman is a great starting or ending point to explore the beautiful surrounding towns and cities within Connecticut and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Kent: Known for its charming downtown, covered bridges, and natural beauty
  • Litchfield: A historic town known for its stunning churches, antique shops, and art galleries.
  • Cooperstown: Known for the National Baseball Hall of Fame, the Farmers’ Museum, and the Fenimore Art Museum.
