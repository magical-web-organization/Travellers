Introduction to Cornwall

Cornwall is a charming town located in Litchfield Hills, Connecticut, in the New England region of the United States. Known for its significant historical landmarks and breathtaking natural beauty, Cornwall is the perfect getaway for travelers looking to soak up the rich history and scenic views of New England.

List of Places to Visit in Cornwall

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cornwall Historical Society - Immerse yourself in the town’s rich history at the Cornwall Historical Society. Exhibits include artifacts and documents that showcase Cornwall’s colonial and industrial past.
  • Sculpture Grounds - Visitors can admire outdoor sculptures set within the beautiful landscape of the Connecticut hills. Works of art are frequently added to the collection, making this an excellent destination for art enthusiasts.
  • Sharon Audubon Center - A nature center that provides educational programs, exhibits, and live animal demonstrations. Visitors can hike through wetlands, forests, and meadows and observe local birds and wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Cornwall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mohawk State Forest - For a taste of the great outdoors, Mohawk State Forest offers miles of hiking trails through the woodlands. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of autumn foliage, streams, waterfalls, and the Housatonic River.
  • West Cornwall Covered Bridge - Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, the West Cornwall Covered Bridge is a charming 172-foot-long bridge that offers impressive views of the river. The bridge is especially beautiful in the fall when the leaves change color.
  • Cornwall Hollow Conservation Area - A wonderful spot where visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and picnicking amidst charming scenery. The park’s highlights include a scenic pond and several walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cornwall

Cornwall offers some of the most beautiful hiking trails and scenic pathways in New England. Here are some must-visit hiking and biking routes in Cornwall:

  • Mohawk Trail - A 5.5 mile loop trail that is excellent for hikers of all skill levels. The trail’s highlights include waterfalls, scenic viewpoints, and colorful foliage in the fall.
  • Cornwall Trail - A 4.5 mile out-and-back trail that is perfect for hiking and is accessible year-round. The trailhead is located near the top of Cream Hill Road, and visitors can enjoy picturesque views of the Housatonic River.
  • Housatonic Meadows State Park Trail - The flat, hard-packed trail is ideal for biking and hiking. The trailhead is located at the northern end of Housatonic Meadows State Park.

Best Things to Do in Cornwall: Activities and Tours

Cornwall offers visitors plenty of activities to enjoy, and here are some of the best things to do when visiting Cornwall:

  • Lime Rock Park - A motorsports race track that hosts several car and motorcycle races throughout the year.
  • Housatonic River Outfitters - Fishing enthusiasts can enjoy fly fishing in the nearby Housatonic River, known for its trout and bass fishery.
  • Housatonic Railroad Historical Society Museum - For a unique experience, visitors can explore the history of the railroads in the area. The museum features model trains, exhibits, and artifacts, including an antique caboose.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cornwall

Here’s a week-long itinerary for your trip to Cornwall:

Day 1: Arrive and settle in at your accommodations. Spend the afternoon strolling through Cornwall Hollow Conservation Area.

Day 2: Embark on a morning hike on the Mohawk Trail. In the afternoon, visit the West Cornwall Covered Bridge and the Sculpture Grounds.

Day 3: Spend the day at Mohawk State Forest, hiking or simply enjoying the scenic beauty the place has to offer.

Day 4: Explore the Cornwall Historical Society, followed by a visit to the Sharon Audubon Center.

Day 5: Take a bike ride or hike through the Housatonic Meadows State Park Trail. Visit the Housatonic Railroad Historical Society Museum in the evening.

Day 6: Immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Cornwall with a visit to the Housatonic River Outfitters.

Day 7: Depart Cornwall.

Food and Drink Scene of Cornwall

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty - A traditional dish consisting of a crumbly pastry filled with beef and vegetables. Some places also offer a vegetarian version.
  • Apple Cider Donuts - A sweet treat to enjoy while exploring the hills and valleys of Cornwall in the fall.

Local Beverages

  • New England Hard Cider - A refreshing local beverage available at the Tapped Apple, a cider tasting room in the region.
  • Connecticut Wine - From stony soil to sweet reds, the state has many vineyards, such as Sunset Meadow outside Cornwall.

Where to Stay in Cornwall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cornwall offers accommodation options for all budgets and preferences, from historic inns to cozy bed and breakfasts.

  • Cornwall Inn - A charming historic inn with cozy rooms and great breakfast services.
  • The Cornwall Farm Market Guesthouse - A comfortable and affordable option for travelers seeking a more rural retreat.
  • The Tollgate Hill Inn - A luxurious experience with an outdoor pool, tennis courts, and elegant antique decor.

What to Buy in Cornwall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cornwall Chocolate Shop - A visit to this quirky shop is a must to a chocoholic. Check out their award-winning chocolate truffles or try their hand-dipped apple cider donuts.
  • A Signature Piece from a Local Artisan - The town is deeply rooted in art culture, and many local boutiques sell beautiful handmade ceramics, sculptures, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Cornwall

Cornwall is a charming small town filled with rich culture and history. It is known for its inspiring art scene that spans across the local artists, shops, and galleries.

Best Time to Visit Cornwall

Cornwall is a beautiful destination all year round, but the best time to visit is during the fall season, from September to November. The warm orange and golden hues of autumn cover the hills, and the leaves turn into an array of colors, making it the perfect time for hiking and scenic drives.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cornwall

Cornwall is a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of the natural hazards that can pose a significant risk while exploring the outdoors. Be cautious when hiking near cliff edges and be aware of potential wildlife encounters in rural areas.

Health in Cornwall

Visitors should always carry insect repellent and be careful while walking in tick-prone regions. Lyme disease is prevalent in rural Connecticut, and it is vital to protect oneself.

Getting to Cornwall: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cornwall

The nearest airport to Cornwall is Bradley International Airport, located 81 miles away, near Hartford.

Bus Stations Near Cornwall

There is no bus station in Cornwall, but bus services are available in nearby towns like Torrington, and Sharon.

Other Options

Visitors can opt for a car rental or ride-sharing services to travel to Cornwall comfortably.

How to Get Around in Cornwall

While a car is essential to explore the nooks and crannies of Cornwall, bicycling and walking is another popular way to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Cornwall: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cornwall’s prime location makes it a great base for exploring nearby places like Salisbury, Lakeville, and Falls Village, each with unique attractions to explore.

Conclusion

Cornwall is a charming town in the heart of New England and is perfect for travelers looking to experience the beauty and history of her.
