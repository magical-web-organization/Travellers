Introduction to Barkhamsted

Barkhamsted is a charming town located in Litchfield Hills, Connecticut, in New England, United States. The town is known for its beautiful natural surroundings, historical sites, and cultural attractions.

Barkhamsted has an area of 38 square miles and a population of around 3,600 residents. The town has a rich history, dating back to the 18th century. Barkhamsted was originally inhabited by Native American tribes, including the Algonquin and Mohican tribes. Later, European settlers arrived and established a community in the area. Barkhamsted became a town in 1779.

List of Places to Visit in Barkhamsted

Barkhamsted offers numerous attractions that cater to a wide range of interests. Here’s a list of places to visit in Barkhamsted:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Squire’s Tavern Museum: This historical site is a well-preserved 18th-century tavern that highlights the early history of the town.
  • The Barkhamsted Center for the Arts: A community-based arts center that hosts cultural events, exhibits, and workshops.
  • Barkhamsted Historical Society: This institution preserves and promotes the history and cultural heritage of Barkhamsted.

Natural Attractions of Barkhamsted: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • People’s State Forest: A 4,000-acre forest that offers hiking trails, swimming, fishing, and picnicking.
  • American Legion State Forest: An ideal place for camping, fishing, hunting, hiking, and mountain biking.
  • Saville Dam: This site offers stunning views of the Barkhamsted Reservoir and is ideal for picnicking and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barkhamsted

Barkhamsted provides abundant hiking and biking opportunities in its natural surroundings. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Barkhamsted:

  • Ratlum Mountain Loop Trail: A 4.2-mile hiking trail that offers scenic views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  • Tunxis Trail: A 50-mile long-distance hiking trail that passes through Barkhamsted’s lush forest and scenic ridges.
  • Farmington River Rail Trail: An 18.2-mile bike trail that starts in Simsbury and passes through the town of Barkhamsted.

Best Things to Do in Barkhamsted: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local farmer’s market and buy local produce
  • Attend a cultural performance at the Barkhamsted Center for the Arts
  • Go on a family picnic at People’s State Forest
  • Take a guided historical tour of the town
  • Try your hand at fly fishing on the Farmington River

Week-Long Itinerary for Barkhamsted

Day 1: Visit the Squire’s Tavern Museum and take a historical tour of the town. Day 2: Hike the Tunxis Trail and enjoy the natural surroundings of the forest. Day 3: Go fly fishing on the Farmington River. Day 4: Explore People’s State Forest and attend a local farmer’s market to shop for local produce. Day 5: Take a scenic drive around the town and enjoy the fall foliage. Day 6: Visit the Barkhamsted Historical Society and learn about the town’s rich cultural heritage. Day 7: Relax and unwind at the American Legion State Forest.
