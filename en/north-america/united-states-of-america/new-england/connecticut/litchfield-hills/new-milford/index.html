Introduction to New Milford

Nestled in the picturesque Litchfield Hills of Connecticut, New Milford is a charming town that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With scenic landscapes, natural beauty, and a rich cultural heritage, New Milford has something for everyone. This comprehensive tour guide will help you explore the best sights, activities, accommodations, and local insights in and around New Milford.

List of Places to Visit in New Milford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Maxx: A vibrant community center that hosts a wide range of programs and events for all ages, including concerts, art shows, theater productions, and more.
  • Housatonic Valley Association: A non-profit organization dedicated to preserving the natural beauty and resources of the region through various educational programs and conservation initiatives.
  • The Silo: A cultural center that features an art gallery, cooking school, and gift shop, where you can browse unique handmade items by local artisans.
  • New Milford Historical Society & Museum: An excellent place to learn about the town’s history and heritage, with exhibits featuring artifacts, photographs, and documents dating back to the colonial era.
  • Merwinsville Hotel Restoration: An impressive example of 19th-century architecture, this historic hotel has been carefully restored and now functions as a community center for events and education programs.

Natural Attractions of New Milford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Candlewood Lake: A popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming, with over 60 miles of shoreline and several public parks for picnicking and hiking.
  • Harrybrooke Park: A beautiful park with walking trails, a pond, gardens, and a historic mansion that offers tours and events throughout the year.
  • Bear Mountain Reservation: A scenic spot with hiking trails, streams, waterfalls, and stunning views of the surrounding valleys and hills.
  • Clatter Valley Park: A peaceful nature preserve with walking trails, meadows, and wildlife habitats.
  • Kent Falls State Park: A nearby park with a picturesque waterfall, picnic areas, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Milford

New Milford is a hiker’s paradise with plenty of scenic trails and breathtaking views. Some popular routes include:

  • The Lovers Leap State Park Trail: This trail follows the Housatonic River and offers stunning views of the valley from atop Lover’s Leap.
  • Steep Rock Preserve: A scenic park with over 5 miles of hiking trails that wind through forests, fields, and along the Shepaug River.
  • Appalachian Trail: The famous long-distance trail crosses through New Milford, with some of the most scenic sections starting around the Bulls Bridge area.
  • Lake Waramaug State Park Trail: This 3.2-mile loop trail circles Lake Waramaug and offers plenty of spots to enjoy the scenic beauty of the lake and surrounding woods.
  • The Still River Greenway: A paved 1-mile trail perfect for biking, walking, or jogging, which provides excellent views of the Still River and surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in New Milford: Activities and Tours

  • Go kayaking or canoeing on the Housatonic River or Candlewood Lake.
  • Visit a local farmer’s market or farm stand to sample fresh produce and organic meats.
  • Take a guided horseback riding tour through the countryside.
  • Attend one of New Milford’s many festivals or fairs, such as the annual Village Fair Days or the Litchfield Jazz Festival.
  • Catch a movie at the historic Bank Street Theater.
  • Go wine tasting at one of the local vineyards or breweries.
  • Visit a nearby farm and pick your own fruit or vegetables.
  • Take a scenic drive through the Litchfield Hills and enjoy the fall foliage.
  • Go fishing for bass, trout, and other types of fish in one of the many nearby streams.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Milford

If you’re planning a week-long trip to New Milford, here’s a perfect itinerary that will allow you to explore the best sights and activities in the area:

Day 1: Arrive in New Milford and check into your accommodation. Take a stroll down Bank Street and enjoy shopping, dining, and drinks at the local restaurants and bars.

Day 2: Visit the New Milford Historical Society & Museum and learn about the town’s history and heritage. Afterward, go for a leisurely hike in Harrybrooke Park and have a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Spend the day exploring the Litchfield Hills, starting with a scenic drive through the region’s picturesque villages and towns. Visit Kent Falls State Park and take a hike to the waterfall.

Day 4: Rent a kayak or canoe and paddle down the Housatonic River. Afterward, visit The Maxx community center in downtown New Milford, where you can participate in a variety of programs and activities.

Day 5: Go on a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards and enjoy a gourmet lunch at one of the excellent restaurants in the area.

Day 6: Visit one of the nearby farms and pick your own fruit or vegetables. Take a leisurely hike through the Bear Mountain Reservation and enjoy the stunning views of the countryside.

Day 7: Spend your final day in New Milford sampling local delicacies and shopping for souvenirs at The Silo. Depart in the evening, taking with you memories that will last a lifetime.

Food and Drink Scene of New Milford

Must-Try Dishes

One of the best ways to experience the local culture is through its cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes in New Milford:

  • Connecticut-style lobster rolls: Fresh local lobster is served on a buttered, toasted bun, with a sprinkle of lemon juice and chives.
  • Shepaug sausage and cheese: A delicious appetizer made with local sausage and artisanal cheeses.
  • Grilled cheese with tomato soup: A comforting classic, made with locally sourced bread and artisanal cheeses.
  • Maple-glazed pork chops: A savory dish that incorporates local maple syrup and herbs for a delicious and hearty meal.
  • Blueberry crumb pie: Made with fresh, organic blueberries and a buttery crumb topping, this dessert is a true local specialty.

Local Beverages

New Milford has a thriving craft beer and wine scene, with several breweries and vineyards in the surrounding region. Some of the popular beverages to try include:

  • Wine from Hopkins Vineyard: A family-owned vineyard that offers a variety of award-winning wines, including chardonnay, merlot, and cabernet sauvignon.
  • Beer from Witchdoctor Brewing Company: A local brewery that specializes in small-batch, unique craft beers like The Jig Is Up IPA and The Cure Witbier.
  • Cider from Averill Farm: This family-owned orchard produces crisp, refreshing cider made from locally grown apples.
  • Cocktails from the Iron Rail: A cozy local bar that serves up delicious cocktails made with fresh, artisanal ingredients.

Where to Stay in New Milford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Milford offers a variety of accommodations that are perfect for every budget and taste. Here are some top choices:

  • Homestead Inn: This charming bed and breakfast offer luxurious rooms, gourmet breakfast, and a serene atmosphere that’s perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • The Litchfield Inn: Located near the town center, this historic inn offers tastefully decorated rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • The Red House: A cozy guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms, an outdoor hot tub, and a scenic setting that’s perfect for nature lovers.
  • Candlewood Lake Motel: A budget-friendly motel that offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a convenient location near the lake and downtown.

What to Buy in New Milford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whenever you’re traveling, it’s always fun to bring back unique souvenirs and local products that you can’t find elsewhere. Here are some of the most popular souvenirs and products to bring back from New Milford:

  • Handcrafted pottery and ceramics from the Silo.
  • Apple cider and maple syrup from the nearby farms.
  • Artwork and handicrafts from the local artisans’ shops and galleries.
  • Antique and unique finds at one of New Milford’s many vintage and antiques shops.
  • Gourmet cheeses from Arethusa Farm Dairy, located in the nearby town of Litchfield.

Local Culture and Customs of New Milford

New Milford is a welcoming and friendly town, with a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its events, festivals, and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect local traditions and customs, especially during festivals and religious events.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather, as temperatures can vary widely throughout the year.
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants and bars.
  • Take some time to explore the local art scene and appreciate the work of local artists and artisans.

Best Time to Visit New Milford

New Milford is delightful year-round, with each season offering its unique charms. However, the best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests. Here’s what to expect throughout the year:

  • Spring: Mild temperatures and blooming flowers make it a perfect time for outdoor activities and events.
  • Summer: Warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities make it a popular time for tourists.
  • Fall: The foliage season is truly spectacular in New Milford, with stunning hues of red, orange, and gold.
  • Winter: Though temperatures can be chilly, the winter season offers the chance for memorable winter activities like skiing, skating, snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Milford

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many wonderful outdoor activities and natural beauty of New England.
  • Embrace local culture and customs and be respectful to others.
  • Explore the local art and culture scene that the area has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitat and resources of the area.
  • Disrespect any of the cultural or religious events in the area.
  • Ignore the local food scene and not try the fresh, locally sourced meals that are available.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Milford

New Milford is a safe and peaceful town, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s always essential to take common-sense precautions to ensure your safety:

  • Always lock your car and hotel room door.
  • Avoid deserted areas at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking.

Health in New Milford

New Milford has excellent medical facilities and services, including several hospitals and clinics. However, it’s always a good idea to carry necessary medications and insurance cards, as well as follow regular health precautions like washing hands regularly and booking any medical appointments based on one’s need.

Getting to New Milford: Transportation Options and Logistics

New Milford is easily accessible from every possible direction and offers several transportation options, including:

Airports Near New Milford

  • Bradley International Airport (BDL): Located just under 50 miles away from New Milford, Bradley International Airport in Windsor Locks is the nearest major airport.
  • Westchester County Airport (HPN): Situated around 35 miles away from New Milford, Westchester County Airport in White Plains is another alternative.

Bus Stations Near New Milford

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: Peter Pan Bus Lines offer coach bus service for various locations.
  • Housatonic Area Regional Transit (HART): HART offers local bus service within the area, including Connecticut’s Northwest Corner.

Other Options

New Milford is also accessible by car, with I-84 and Route 7 passing through the town. If you’re coming from New York or other nearby cities, then you can also opt for Amtrak or Metro-North Railways trains, both of which make stops in nearby towns like Danbury or Bridgeport.

How to Get Around in New Milford

Getting around New Milford is easy, with several transportation options available.

  • Rent a car or bike to explore the town and surrounding countryside at your pace.
  • Take a taxi or ride-hailing app service for convenient and efficient transportation.
  • Utilize the HART bus service for travel within the area.
  • Walk or hike if you are up for it, or explore the town by foot for a more immersive experience.

Where to Go Next from New Milford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While New Milford has many excellent attractions, there are plenty of other exciting destinations, within a driving distance, to explore in Connecticut:

  • Litchfield Hills: Home to several quaint towns, scenic roads, and parks.
  • Lake Waramaug: A stunning lake and its surrounding towns, offering various outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Danbury: A vibrant city filled with shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Waterbury: A city with rich arts and culture scene, famous for its brass industry.
