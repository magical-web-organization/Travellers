Discover Avon, CT - A Travel Guide

If you’re looking for a peaceful getaway where you can relax and rejuvenate amidst the scenic beauty of the Connecticut River Valley, Avon in Connecticut, USA, is just the place for you. From cultural and historical landmarks to vast natural landscapes, this town has something for every type of traveler. This comprehensive travel guide will tell you everything you need to know for a trip to Avon.

List of Places to Visit in Avon

Whether you’re a nature lover or a history enthusiast, Avon has plenty of places to explore. Here are some of the top places you don’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Avon Historical Society Museum: This small museum showcases the history of Avon and its surrounding areas with interesting artifacts and exhibits.

  • Pine Grove Schoolhouse Museum: This beautifully restored school building from the late 19th century offers a glimpse into what school life in Avon was like over a century ago.

Natural Attractions of Avon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Talcott Mountain State Park: This park boasts over 500 acres of pristine natural beauty, with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. Here, hikers can enjoy 12 miles of trails and picnic areas.

  • Farmington River Trail: This scenic trail follows the Farmington River for 18.2 miles and is a popular spot for hiking and biking.

  • Fisher Meadows Recreation Area: A popular spot for jogging, hiking, cycling, fishing, and bird watching, this well-maintained recreation area offers spectacular views of the meadows and hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avon

There are several hiking and biking routes in Avon that cater to all abilities and fitness levels. Here are some of the most notable ones:

  1. Talcott Mountain State Park Trail: This 1.3-mile trail is a moderate hike that offers stunning views of Hartford and the surrounding areas.

  2. Farmington Canal Heritage Trail: This 84-mile trail was once a canal system that was converted into a multi-use trail for hikers, bikers, and joggers. It starts in New Haven and runs all the way to Northampton, Massachusetts.

  3. Fisher Meadows Trail: This 2.7-mile easy trail runs along the banks of the Farmington River, offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside, and is suitable for all ages and abilities.

Best Things to Do in Avon: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Avon has plenty of other activities to offer, such as:

  • Balloon rides with the Aer Blarney Balloons company offer a bird’s-eye view of the beautiful surroundings.

  • Farmington River Tubing, suitable for all skill levels and a great way to spend a relaxing day on the water while enjoying scenic views.

  • A visit to the nearby Living Museum, which shows how early American settlers lived and worked through demonstrations and exhibits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avon

Avon has so much to explore that it can be difficult to decide what to see and do. Here’s our suggested itinerary for a week-long stay:

Day 1: Arrive and Explore Downtown Avon for nearby museums and historical places.

Day 2: Visit The Pine Grove Schoolhouse and then Talcott Mountain State Park.

Day 3: Hiking on the Farmington River Trail.

Day 4: Drive to The Living Museum

Day 5: Enjoy a balloon ride with Aer Balbrney Balloons Company.

Day 6: Relax at Fisher Meadows Recreation Area.

Day 7: Departure from Avon.

Food and Drink Scene of Avon

Whether you want to taste local flavors and drinks or indulge in international cuisine, Avon has plenty of options to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepe’s Pizza: A Connecticut classic, this celebrated pizzeria has been serving pies for almost a century.

  • Barbecue from Bear’s Smokehouse: This award-winning, family-owned barbecue restaurant boasts flavorful dishes with homemade sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Local beers at the Avon Country Deli and Wine and Spirit.

  • Locally produced cider from the Walden Hill Cidery.

Where to Stay in Avon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avon has several accommodation options for every type of budget. Here are some recommendations for where to stay:

  • Avon Old Farms Hotel: A historic hotel with a classic, grand feel and up-to-date amenities.

  • The Simsbury Inn: A boutique hotel situated on the lush hillside overlooking the Farmington River Valley.

  • Airbnb: For a more personal and intimate experience, you can opt for renting from the numerous local Airbnb options.

What to Buy in Avon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From locally sourced foods to eclectic wares, Avon offers premium products that reflect its history and culture. Here are some of the best products for souvenirs from Avon:

  • Smith’s Acres: They sell locally sourced and organic home-grown produce at their farm store.

  • Avon Village Market: A local store that sells Hartford- and Avon-made products including clothing, crafts, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Avon

The people of Avon blend both the New England tradition and contemporary sophistication, resulting in a culture characterized by warm hospitality, down-to-earth simplicity, and a strong sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Avon

The best time to visit Avon is from June to August, when the weather is mild and the days are long. The fall season is also a delightful time with classic fall colors on the leaves. Winter offers great opportunities for snow sports, but travel can be difficult due to inclement weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avon

Do:

  • Respect local customs when you’re in public places.

  • Keep the surroundings clean, which is locally emphasized, especially in parks and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Do not smoke in public places as it is banned throughout Connecticut.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avon

Avon is a peaceful small town but it is advisable to always stay cautious, especially when on the trails. Don’t venture out too far alone especially at night.

Health in Avon

Medical facilities are available throughout Avon. In case of emergencies, dial 911.

Getting to Avon: Transportation Options and Logistics

The Bradley International Airport is the nearest, and it is located around 20 miles away from Avon. There are no public transport options available in Avon. Visitors can easily rent cars or hail taxis to get around.

Airports Near Avon

  • Bradley International Airport (20 miles)

Bus Stations Near Avon

  • Hartford Bus Station (14 miles)

Other Options

  • Rent a car
  • Taxi

How to Get Around in Avon

The easiest way to explore Avon is by car or bike. However, if you are in Avon without your own mode of transport, you can hire a taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Avon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hartford: The capital city of Connecticut which is renowned for its architecture, parks, and culture.

  • Litchfield County: With vibrant art troupes and stunning forests, a perfect spot for a serene vacation.

Conclusion

Avon is an ideal destination for those who want to enjoy natural beauty, experience local culture, and witness the rich history of America. With various options for outdoor activities, culturally important events, great food and drink, and relaxed and friendly locals, your vacation in Avon is sure to be memorable.
