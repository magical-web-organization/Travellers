Introduction to Windsor Locks

Located in the beautiful Connecticut River Valley, Windsor Locks is a charming town in Connecticut that offers a wide range of things to see and do. This guide will take you through all the highlights of Windsor Locks, including its cultural attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking trails, top things to do, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Windsor Locks

If you’re planning a trip to Windsor Locks, there’s a lot to explore. Here’s a list of some of the must-visit places in this town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New England Air Museum: A museum devoted to the history of aviation, the New England Air Museum is home to over 80 aircraft and an extensive collection of engines, equipment, and artifacts.

  • Windsor Locks Canal State Park Trail: A must-visit spot for those interested in the history of the Connecticut River Valley. The trail follows the path of the canal between Windsor Locks and Suffield.

  • Windsor Historical Society and Museum: A museum that houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits relating to the history of Windsor Locks and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Windsor Locks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scantic River State Park: An ideal place to get away from the hustle and bustle of daily life and enjoy a peaceful walk or picnic. The park offers hiking trails, fishing spots, and scenic views.

  • Northwest Park: Spanning over 473 acres, Northwest Park is a beautiful natural space with miles of hiking trails, a maple sugar house, a nature center, and an animal barn.

  • Connecticut River: The longest river in New England, the Connecticut River is a great spot for boating, fishing, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windsor Locks

Windsor Locks is a beautiful place to explore on foot or by bike. Here are some top hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • Canal Trail: A 4.3-mile trail that follows the route of the canal, taking visitors through scenic landscapes and past historical landmarks.

  • Windsor Locks Canal State Park Trail: An easy 2.5-mile trail that is perfect for a leisurely hike or bike ride. It offers views of the canal and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Windsor Locks: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Windsor Locks, here are some of our top picks:

  • Connecticut Trolley Museum: Board a historic trolley and take a ride through the beautiful scenery of Connecticut.

  • Zip Line: Strap on a harness and glide through the air on a zip-line, enjoying beautiful views of the Connecticut River Valley.

  • CT Beer Trail: The Connecticut Beer Trail is a self-guided tour of the state’s breweries, and Windsor Locks is home to several. Hop from brewery to brewery, sampling local craft beers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Windsor Locks

If you’re planning on spending a week in Windsor Locks, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Windsor Locks Canal State Park and explore the town

Food and Drink Scene of Windsor Locks

Windsor Locks has a great selection of restaurants and bars, offering everything from classic American fare to international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

When visiting Windsor Locks, there are a few dishes you should definitely try:

  • New England Clam Chowder: A classic dish that is a must-try in the area.

  • Lobster Roll: A delicious sandwich made with fresh lobster meat, usually served with fries and coleslaw.

Local Beverages

Connecticut has a thriving craft beer scene, and Windsor Locks is no exception. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Two Roads Brewing Company: A brewery that offers a wide range of craft beers, including IPAs, lagers, and stouts.

Where to Stay in Windsor Locks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Windsor Locks has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Sheraton Hartford Hotel at Bradley Airport: This hotel is conveniently located at the Bradley International Airport, making it a great choice for those with early or late flights.

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hartford - Bradley Airport: This hotel boasts a free airport shuttle service, along with a fitness center, indoor pool, and restaurant.

  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Hartford/Windsor Locks: An all-suite hotel that offers kitchenettes and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Windsor Locks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Windsor Locks, there are a few souvenirs and local products that you should consider bringing home:

  • Connecticut maple syrup: A local delicacy that can be found at farmer’s markets and specialty shops.

  • Connecticut wine: Connecticut has a thriving wine industry, with several wineries located in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Windsor Locks

Connecticut is a friendly and welcoming state. When visiting, keep in mind the following customs and cultural norms:

  • Tipping: In restaurants, it is customary to leave a tip of around 15-20% of the total bill.

  • Friendliness: Connecticut residents are generally friendly and welcoming, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation with a local.

Best Time to Visit Windsor Locks

The best time to visit Windsor Locks is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is beautiful. Winter can be quite cold, and summers can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Windsor Locks

Here are some quick do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Windsor Locks:

Do:

  • Enjoy the beautiful outdoors and nature spots.

  • Try local food and drink.

  • Take advantage of the town’s historical and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Ignore local customs or cultural norms.

  • Overstay your welcome in public places or parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Windsor Locks

Connecticut is generally a safe place to visit, with low levels of violent crime. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take practical precautions, such as locking your car and hotel room door and staying in well-lit areas after dark.

Health in Windsor Locks

Connecticut has good medical facilities, and the tap water is safe to drink. It’s recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Windsor Locks: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Windsor Locks

  • Bradley International Airport: This airport is located in Windsor Locks, making it the most convenient option for travelers.

Bus Stations Near Windsor Locks

  • Windsor Locks Bus Stop: Located in the town’s center, this bus stop is serviced by Peter Pan Bus Lines and Greyhound.

Other Options

  • Driving: Windsor Locks is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area.

How to Get Around in Windsor Locks

Visitors to Windsor Locks can get around by car, bike, or on foot. The town’s compact size makes it easy to explore on foot, and biking is a great way to get around the area’s scenic trails.

Where to Go Next from Windsor Locks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Windsor Locks is a great hub for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Hartford, CT: The state capital is just a short drive away and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions.

  • Springfield, MA: Just over the border in Massachusetts, Springfield is home to a variety of museums, parks, and attractions.

Conclusion

Windsor Locks is a beautiful town in Connecticut that offers a great mix of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and local cuisine. Whether you’re looking to explore historical landmarks, hike scenic trails, or sample craft beers, there’s something for every traveler in Windsor Locks.
