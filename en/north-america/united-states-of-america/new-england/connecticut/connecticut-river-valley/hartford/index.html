Introduction to Hartford

Located in the Connecticut River Valley of New England, Hartford is the capital city of the state of Connecticut in the United States of America. It is a city rich in history and culture, with a thriving arts scene, unique architecture, and natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Hartford, along with insider tips and local recommendations.

List of Places to Visit in Hartford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mark Twain House & Museum: The former home of the famous American author Mark Twain, this historical landmark is now a museum dedicated to his life and works. Visitors can explore the stunning 19th-century mansion and learn about Twain’s impact on American literature.
  • Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art: With over 50,000 American and European works of art, the Wadsworth Atheneum is Connecticut’s oldest public art museum. It features a diverse collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts spanning over 5,000 years of art history.
  • Connecticut State Capitol: Completed in 1879, the Connecticut State Capitol is a magnificent architectural masterpiece featuring a stunning gold leaf dome. Visitors can take a guided tour of the building and learn about its history and significance to the state.

Natural Attractions of Hartford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bushnell Park: Located in the heart of downtown Hartford, Bushnell Park is a beautiful green space featuring a historic carousel, the Soldiers and Sailors Memorial Arch, and a tranquil pond. It is the perfect place for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.
  • Elizabeth Park: Known for its famous rose garden, Elizabeth Park is a stunning 101-acre park boasting colorful botanical gardens, walking trails, and scenic vistas of the Hartford skyline.
  • Riverfront Recapture: With 148 acres of parkland along the Connecticut River, Riverfront Recapture offers a variety of outdoor activities, from boating and fishing to hiking along its scenic trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hartford

  • Park River Trail: This 3-mile trail follows the path of the historic Park River and offers stunning views of the city and surrounding nature.
  • Farmington Canal Heritage Trail: Stretching over 80 miles from New Haven to Northampton, MA, this trail crosses through Hartford and features scenic vistas, historic landmarks, and a variety of outdoor activities.
  • East Coast Greenway: A 3,000-mile trail from Maine to Florida, the East Coast Greenway passes through Hartford and offers a variety of hiking and biking options, with beautiful views of the river and surrounding parks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Hartford: Activities and Tours

  • Connecticut Science Center: With over 165 hands-on exhibits, live science demonstrations, and a state-of-the-art 3D theater, the Connecticut Science Center offers fun and educational activities for visitors of all ages.
  • Harriet Beecher Stowe Center: Learn about the life and legacy of Harriet Beecher Stowe, the author of “Uncle Tom’s Cabin,” at her former home turned museum. Visitors can take a guided tour, participate in interactive exhibits, and attend special events and programs.
  • Hartford Flavor Company: Take a tour of this local distillery and taste their award-winning line of artisanal botanical spirits, featuring unique flavors like lavender, rose, and chai tea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hartford

Day 1 Explore downtown and visit the historic Connecticut State Capitol and Bushnell Park.
Day 2 Visit the Mark Twain House & Museum and Riverfront Recapture.
Day 3 Head to Elizabeth Park and explore its beautiful botanical gardens.
Day 4 Take a hike along the Park River Trail followed by a visit to the Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art.
Day 5 Discover Hartford’s local food scene with a visit to the Hartford Farmers Market and lunch at one of the city’s best restaurants.
Day 6 Take a tour of the Harriet Beecher Stowe Center and enjoy a cocktail at the Hartford Flavor Company.
Day 7 Spend the day exploring nearby attractions, such as the Mystic Seaport or Foxwoods Resort Casino.

Food and Drink Scene of Hartford

Must-Try Dishes

  • Connecticut-style lobster rolls: This regional specialty is made with warm, buttered lobster meat and served on a New England-style hot dog bun.
  • Steamed cheeseburger: A unique local delicacy, steamed burgers are made by cooking a burger patty in a steamer and served with a side of melted cheese.
  • New Haven-style pizza: Although technically not in Hartford, this famous style of pizza is worth the short drive to nearby New Haven. Known for its thin, crispy crust and unique toppings, it is widely regarded as some of the best pizza in the world.

Local Beverages

  • Connecticut beer: With over 100 breweries and counting, Connecticut is home to a thriving craft beer scene. Some notable local breweries include Two Roads Brewing Company, New England Brewing Company, and Stubborn Beauty Brewing Company.
  • Connecticut wine: Surprisingly, Connecticut is also home to a growing wine industry, with over 40 wineries and vineyards throughout the state. Some popular local wineries include Gouveia Vineyards, Hopkins Vineyard, and Stonington Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Hartford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Goodwin Hotel: A luxury boutique hotel located in downtown Hartford, the Goodwin Hotel features stylish rooms, on-site dining, and a rooftop bar with stunning city views.
  • Hartford Marriott Downtown: Conveniently located in the heart of the city, this modern hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.
  • HI Hartford Hostel: Affordable and centrally located, this hostel offers private and shared rooms, a fully equipped kitchen, and a variety of activities and events for guests.

What to Buy in Hartford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Connecticut-made products: Discover unique handcrafted goods made by local artisans and makers at the Hartford Artisans Weaving Center, the Farmington Valley Arts Center, and the Connecticut Art Trail.
  • Mark Twain memorabilia: The Mark Twain House & Museum’s gift shop offers a variety of Twain-related books, clothing, and souvenirs.
  • Hartford Whalers merchandise: Although the beloved hockey team is no longer in existence, fans can still show their support with merchandise from local retailers such as Hartford Prints! and Raggs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hartford

Hartford is known for its vibrant arts and cultural scene, with a variety of theaters, galleries, and venues showcasing local talent and international acts. The city is also home to a diverse population, including many college students, professionals, and families. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and always ask for permission before taking photos or videos of individuals or private property.

Best Time to Visit Hartford

The best time to visit Hartford is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. The summer months can be hot and humid, but offer a variety of outdoor events and festivals. The winter months can be snowy and cold, but also offer opportunities for winter sports and holiday festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hartford

Do:

  • Explore the city on foot and take in the unique architecture and historic landmarks.
  • Visit local museums and historical sites to learn about the city’s rich history and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine, such as steamed cheeseburgers and Connecticut-style lobster rolls.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car or unattended in public areas.
  • Take photos or videos of individuals or private property without permission.
  • Underestimate the importance of dressing appropriately for the weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hartford

Like any city, visitors to Hartford should be aware of their surroundings and take common sense precautions to ensure their safety. It is recommended to avoid walking alone at night and to stay in well-lit areas. Theft and car break-ins can occur in crowded areas or parking lots, so keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Hartford

Hartford is home to several hospitals and medical centers, offering a variety of medical services to visitors in case of emergency. It is recommended to pack any necessary medications or prescriptions when traveling and to ensure that all vaccinations are up to date.

Getting to Hartford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hartford

The closest airport to Hartford is Bradley International Airport (BDL), located approximately 15 miles north of downtown Hartford. Other nearby airports include Hartford-Brainard Airport (HFD) and Tweed New Haven Regional Airport (HVN).

Bus Stations Near Hartford

Hartford is served by several bus companies, including Greyhound, Peter Pan, and Megabus. The city’s primary bus station is the Downtown Hartford Bus Station, located in the heart of the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Hartford by train, with Amtrak stations located in nearby New Haven and Springfield, MA. Additionally, several major highways, including I-84 and I-91, run through Hartford, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Hartford

Hartford is a walkable city, with many of its top attractions located within a short distance of each other. Visitors can also use local buses, provided by CT Transit, or ride-hailing services like Uber or Lyft for longer distances. Bicycles are also available for rent through services like Lime and Zagster.

Where to Go Next from Hartford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mystic Seaport: Located approximately 70 miles southeast of Hartford, the Mystic Seaport is a maritime museum featuring historic ships, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • Newport, RI: Known for its stunning mansions and picturesque coastline, Newport is located approximately 90 miles east of Hartford.
  • Berkshire Mountains: Located approximately 60 miles northwest of Hartford, the Berkshire Mountains offer a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.

Conclusion

Hartford is a city full of history, culture, and natural beauty, with something to offer visitors of all interests. Whether you’re exploring downtown, hiking along beautiful trails, or tasting local cuisine, there is always something new to discover in Hartford. Plan your trip today and experience all that this unique and vibrant city has to offer!
