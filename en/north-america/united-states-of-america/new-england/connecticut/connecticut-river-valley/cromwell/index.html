Introduction to Cromwell

Located in the heart of the Connecticut River Valley in Connecticut, Cromwell is a small town with a cozy, old-school charm. Prominent for birdwatching, hiking, golfing, and relaxation, Cromwell features a variety of outdoor activities. This picturesque town is also home to some of Connecticut’s more famous attractions, making it a great destination for history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Cromwell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cromwell Historical Society Museum: Located in the historic Stevens-Fisher Farmhouse, the Cromwell Historical Society Museum is a must-visit attraction. The museum tells the story of the town’s past and displays artifacts that date back to the 1800s.
  • Old County Jail: A historical landmark from the 1800s, this building worked as a prison for almost two centuries.

Natural Attractions of Cromwell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cromwell Meadows Wildlife Management Area: A haven for bird enthusiasts, Cromwell Meadows Wildlife Management Area, is home to several species of native species of birds. Visitors can go on hiking trails and birding expeditions, and can also bring along their fishing gear for the nearby Salmon River.
  • River Highlands State Park: With picturesque views of the Connecticut River, River Highlands State Park is a great place to hike or picnic with friends and family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cromwell

  • River Highlands State Park Trail: This 1.5-mile-long trail meanders through woods and along the river, rewarding hikers with stunning views of the park.
  • Mattabesset Trail: A 50-mile-long trail that runs from New Haven to the Massachusetts state line with several sections nearby in Cromwell.

Best Things to Do in Cromwell: Activities and Tours

  • Brownstone Exploration and Discovery Park: An adventure park and a destination for extreme sports, with activities such as Cliff Jumping, zip-lining.
  • TPC River Highlands: A famous 18-hole golf course designed by iconic American Golfer, Bobby Jones. With a stunning view of the Connecticut River, TPC is regarded as a tour-worthy experience.
  • Skydive Danielson: For high-adrenaline enthusiasts, SkyDive Danielson offers tandem jumps.

Food and Drink Scene of Cromwell

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: A classic dish, Cromwell does them right too, featuring warm, buttered lobster on a toasted bun.
  • New England Clam Chowder: A hearty staple in the Northeastern United States, featuring potatoes, cream and clams cooked in a tasty broth.

Local Beverages

  • Connecticut Craft Breweries: Several breweries are available to visitors that offer traditional beers with refreshing twists.

Where to Stay in Cromwell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quality Inn- Cromwell: Basic amenities with a convenient location and modest prices.
  • Courtyard by Marriott Hartford Cromwell: A perfect staycation spot, featuring a grand pool, pleasant decor, and luxury amenities.

What to Buy in Cromwell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Connecticut-made maple syrup and honey: Quick to grab, brings a little local flavor to any dish.
  • Connecticut artisanal crafts: visit the Cromwell Farmer’s Market (during season) some of the fantastic crafts made by talented, passionate locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Cromwell

Cromwell has long been known for its traditions such as the Mushroom Festival, a culinary event that features a mushroom cookoff and bands.

Best Time to Visit Cromwell

The best time of year to visit Cromwell is in the late spring through early fall. The warm weather offers the perfect climate for outdoor activities and hikes in the area. Winter can be cold and rough, but the holiday season sees the town light up with festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cromwell

Do:

  • Respect local customs and keep the environment intact while visiting.
  • Dress appropriately when enjoying the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or take anything from the natural environments.
  • Don’t be loud or disrespectful in public places or inside private establishments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cromwell

Cromwell is a safe town with low crime rates. Basic safety measures like carrying a working cell phone and traveling with someone are recommended while going on hikes.

Health in Cromwell

There are several medical facilities available for visitors in Cromwell and the surrounding area, including Cromwell Medical Center and Middlesex Hospital.

Getting to Cromwell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cromwell

  • Bradley International Airport (BDL)
  • Sikorsky Memorial Airport (BDR)

Bus Stations Near Cromwell

  • Greyhound station in Hartford and Middletown.

Other Options

  • Cromwell is reachable via rental car.
  • Taxis and Ubers are available too.

How to Get Around in Cromwell

Once in Cromwell, it’s best to have a rental car. Still, taxis and Uber can be used to get around.

Where to Go Next from Cromwell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Haven: Visit Yale University, the Peabody Museum of Natural History, and the New Haven Green.
  • Mystic: Explore the famous aquarium and historic seaport.

Conclusion

Cromwell has a lot to offer for visitors of all ages. With its blend of historical landmarks and scenic natural views, Cromwell is a perfect destination for relaxing getaways and exploring Connecticut.
