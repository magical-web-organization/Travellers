Introduction to Old Saybrook

Old Saybrook is a charming small town located in the Connecticut River Valley region of Connecticut, in New England, United States of America. Famous for its picturesque New England scenery, rich history, and nautical charm, it is a popular destination for tourists seeking a quiet yet active vacation.

Old Saybrook was officially settled in 1635 and was named after the English town of the same name. It has a long and fascinating history, which is evident in its well-preserved colonial homes, museums, and historical sites.

With its beautiful beaches, nature reserves, cultural attractions, and variety of outdoor activities, Old Saybrook is a perfect getaway for families, couples, and solo travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Old Saybrook

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

General William Hart House

The General William Hart House is a must-visit destination for history buffs. It is the oldest structure in Old Saybrook, and its architecture reflects the typical homes built by early English colonists.

The Connecticut River Museum

The Connecticut River Museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the Connecticut River Valley. Housed in an 1878 steamboat warehouse, it features exhibits and interactive displays that highlight the river’s role in shaping the region.

The Katharine Hepburn Cultural Arts Center

The Katharine Hepburn Cultural Arts Center is a performing arts venue that showcases music, theater, and movies. The center’s building also houses a museum dedicated to the life and career of Hollywood actress Katharine Hepburn, a longtime resident of Old Saybrook.

Natural Attractions of Old Saybrook: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Harvey’s Beach

Harvey’s Beach is a beautiful, clean, and family-friendly beach that offers a wide range of activities such as swimming, sunbathing, and beach volleyball. Visitors can also explore the nearby salt marshes and enjoy scenic sunsets.

South Cove Wildlife Preserve

South Cove Wildlife Preserve is a 22-acre nature reserve that boasts of stunning views of the Long Island Sound. Visitors can hike the trails, observe various wildlife and migratory birds, and enjoy fishing at the cove.

Fenwick Golf Course

Fenwick Golf Course is a public, nine-hole golf course that offers breathtaking views of the Long Island Sound. With the availability of cart rentals, it is the perfect spot for golf enthusiasts to practice while enjoying the scenic surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old Saybrook

The Tidal Marsh Trail

The Tidal Marsh Trail is a 1.2-mile trail located in the South Cove Wildlife Preserve. It is a great option for visitors who want to take a leisurely walk and appreciate scenic views of the Long Island Sound.

The Old Saybrook Greenway

The Old Saybrook Greenway is a 1.6-mile trail that connects Old Saybrook’s Town Green with the town’s Industrial Park. It is ideal for cycling, walking, and jogging and passes through several parks and scenic spots.

The Ragged Rock Creek Trail

The Ragged Rock Creek Trail is a 3.5-mile trail that follows the Ragged Rock Creek through woodlands, wetlands, and shoreline. It is a relatively easy trek with scenic overlooks and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Old Saybrook: Activities and Tours

Kayak the Connecticut River

Kayaking the Connecticut River is among the most popular activities in Old Saybrook. Rentals are available for visitors to explore the river’s scenic beauty and observe various islands, coves, and wildlife.

Tanger Outlets at Foxwoods

Tanger Outlets at Foxwoods is a great spot for shopping enthusiasts, located less than 25 miles away from Old Saybrook. It features several high-end shops and brand outlets.

Essex Steam Train and Riverboat Ride

Essex Steam Train and Riverboat Ride offer visitors a unique experience that includes a steam train ride through scenic countryside and a cruise on the Connecticut River. It is perfect for families and nature enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Old Saybrook

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Roll

Old Saybrook is located near the shore, which makes it an excellent destination for seafood lovers. A must-try dish is the famous New England Lobster Roll, which features fresh chunks of lobster meat served in a toasted bun with melted butter or mayonnaise.

Clam Chowder

Another popular dish in Old Saybrook is the Clam Chowder, made with freshly harvested clams and vegetables such as potatoes and onions. Visitors have the option to choose between the creamy and clear broth versions.

Local Beverages

Beer

Beer enthusiasts have a variety of local breweries to choose from, including Steady Habit Brewing Company, Dockside Brewery, and Outer Light Brewing Company. Each brewery provides an array of beer flavors and styles to taste and enjoy.

Wine

Visitors can sample Connecticut’s wine scene at the CT Wine Trail, which features several wineries located near Old Saybrook. Some of the popular stops include Chamard Vineyards, Arrigoni Winery, and Priam Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Old Saybrook: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old Saybrook has several accommodation options available for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

Luxury and Mid-Range Hotels

The Saybrook Point Inn & Spa is a luxurious waterfront property that offers breathtaking views of the Long Island Sound. Other top-rated hotels include the Water’s Edge Resort & Spa and the Deacon Timothy Pratt Bed & Breakfast.

Budget-Friendly Guesthouses and Hostels

For budget-conscious travelers, options include the Pier Blue Guesthouse and the Super 8 by Wyndham Old Saybrook.

What to Buy in Old Saybrook: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Old Saybrook can bring home a variety of souvenirs and local products. Popular items include

  • Old Saybrook t-shirts and sweatshirts
  • Handmade jewelry and pottery
  • Local honey and maple syrup
  • Antique and vintage items
  • Handmade soap and candles

Local Culture and Customs of Old Saybrook

Old Saybrook has a rich cultural heritage, which is evident in its architectural landmarks, museums, and festivals. The town celebrates its seafaring history and nautical traditions and is home to various seafood festivals and fairs. The locals are known for their friendliness and hospitality, and visitors will always be welcomed with a smile and a warm greeting.

Best Time to Visit Old Saybrook

The best time to visit Old Saybrook is during the summer months, from June through August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are several outdoor activities and events to enjoy. However, visitors can also explore Old Saybrook during the fall, when the foliage is vibrant and breathtaking, or during the winter season, when several festive celebrations take place.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old Saybrook

Old Saybrook is generally a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should exercise proper precautions such as locking doors and windows when leaving their accommodations and being aware of their surroundings while exploring the town.

Health in Old Saybrook

Visitors to Old Saybrook should ensure that they have reliable medical insurance to cover any emergencies. They should also carry essential medicines and contact their physician before traveling to obtain any necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Old Saybrook: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Old Saybrook

The nearest airport to Old Saybrook is Bradley International Airport, located about 50 miles away. Visitors can also consider flying into John F. Kennedy International Airport or LaGuardia Airport, which are both located about 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Old Saybrook

Old Saybrook has a bus station located downtown, offering easy transportation options for visitors traveling from nearby towns or cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Old Saybrook by train; the Amtrak station is located in neighboring Westbrook, CT, and offers convenient and comfortable transportation options.

How to Get Around in Old Saybrook

Old Saybrook is a small town, and visitors can quickly get around on foot or by bicycle. For those who prefer to drive, car rentals are available from several companies, and taxis and rideshare services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Old Saybrook: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Old Saybrook can also consider exploring other nearby towns and cities. Some popular options include:

  • Essex, a small town that boasts of historic sites and a unique shopping and dining scene
  • Mystic, home to the famous Mystic Aquarium and several other popular attractions
  • Newport, a picturesque coastal town located in Rhode Island that is known for its historic mansions and yachting culture

Conclusion

Old Saybrook is a hidden gem in Connecticut River Valley, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. With its beautiful beaches, nature reserves, cultural attractions, and variety of outdoor activities, it is a perfect destination for anyone seeking a peaceful yet exciting vacation. Whether it’s kayaking along the Connecticut River, exploring the local museums or savoring the delicious seafood, there’s something for everyone in Old Saybrook.
