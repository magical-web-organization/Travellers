Windsor, a historic town located in the Connecticut River Valley of Connecticut, boasts beautiful scenery, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. It is considered to be the first English settlement in Connecticut, and visitors can immerse themselves in the town’s rich history while exploring the various historical sites and museums. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Windsor, providing information on the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat, stay, and shop in the town.

Introduction to Windsor

Windsor is a small town located in the north-central region of Connecticut. It has a population of just over 28,000 and is known for its rich history and beautiful natural scenery. Windsor was founded in 1633 by a group of English settlers led by Captain John Mason. Its name was inspired by the town of Windsor, England. Today, the town is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and charming atmosphere.

List of Places to Visit in Windsor

Here is a list of the best places to visit in Windsor:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Windsor Historical Society: Explore the town’s rich history and culture at the Windsor Historical Society. The museum features various exhibits and collections, including artifacts and documents from the town’s founding to the present day.
  • The Connecticut Trolley Museum: The Connecticut Trolley Museum showcases the history of trolley cars in the state. Visitors can take a ride on a vintage trolley car and explore the museum’s collection of trolley cars and related artifacts.
  • The Oliver Ellsworth Homestead: The Oliver Ellsworth Homestead is the former home of the Founding Father and Chief Justice of the United States Supreme Court, Oliver Ellsworth. The home has now been turned into a museum, featuring exhibits on Ellsworth’s life and achievements.

Natural Attractions of Windsor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Northwest Park: Northwest Park is a sprawling nature reserve that features hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views. It is also home to a wildlife sanctuary, where visitors can spot various species of birds and animals.
  • Windsor Meadows State Park: Windsor Meadows State Park is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and hiking. It is known for its picturesque views of the Connecticut River and surrounding countryside.
  • Paddle Adventure Mystic: Paddle Adventure Mystic offers tours and rentals of kayaks and paddleboards. Visitors can explore the scenic waterways of the region and soak up the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windsor

Windsor has several hiking and biking routes that offer beautiful views of the town’s natural surroundings. Some of the best routes include:

  • Windsor River Trail: This scenic trail follows the Connecticut River and passes through wetlands, meadows, and woodlands.
  • Case Mountain Recreation Area: The Case Mountain Recreation Area features over 600 acres of scenic landscape, including trails for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.
  • Penwood State Park: Penwood State Park is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking. The park’s trails offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Windsor: Activities and Tours

Windsor offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to a range of interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Windsor:

Windsor Art Center

The Windsor Arts Center offers a wide range of art exhibits, workshops, and events. Visitors can attend art shows, concerts, and lectures throughout the year.

Windsor Farmers Market

The Windsor Farmers Market offers a variety of fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products. It is held from May through October, and visitors can enjoy live music and other entertainment while shopping.

Tasting Tours

The Connecticut Trolley Museum offers tasting tours that showcase the region’s local breweries, vineyards, and distilleries. Visitors can sample locally made wines, beers, and spirits while learning about the area’s local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Windsor

Windsor has a diverse food and drink scene, with a range of options from casual to fine dining. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Connecticut-style lobster roll: A classic New England-style lobster roll, served warm with butter and lemon juice.
  • Pizza at Vito’s By The Park: Vito’s By The Park serves some of the best pizza in town, with a range of creative and delicious toppings.
  • Chimichanga at La Paloma Sabanera: La Paloma Sabanera is a local coffee shop that also serves delicious Mexican food. Their chimichangas are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Connecticut craft beer: Connecticut has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors can sample some of the best local brews at bars and pubs throughout Windsor.
  • Wine at Gouveia Vineyards: Gouveia Vineyards is a beautiful winery located just outside of Windsor. Visitors can sample a range of their award-winning wines while enjoying the scenic views.

Local Culture and Customs of Windsor

Windsor has a rich cultural history, dating back to its founding by English settlers in the 17th century. The town celebrates this history through various cultural events and festivals, including the Windsor Shad Derby Festival and the Windsor Chamber of Commerce Chili Challenge.

Best Time to Visit Windsor

The best time to visit Windsor is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Visitors can enjoy the colorful foliage in the fall, or the blooming flowers and trees in the spring.

Getting to Windsor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Windsor is easily accessible by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Windsor

  • Bradley International Airport is located just 10 miles south of Windsor. It offers non-stop flights to several major domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Windsor

  • The Peter Pan Bus Lines station is located in downtown Hartford, just a short drive from Windsor. It offers bus service to several major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi for transportation to and around Windsor.

How to Get Around in Windsor

Windsor is a small town and many of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use public transportation, rental cars, or taxis to get around.
