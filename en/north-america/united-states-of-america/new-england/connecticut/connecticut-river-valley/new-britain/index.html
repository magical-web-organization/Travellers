Introduction to New Britain

Nestled in the picturesque Connecticut River Valley, New Britain is a charming city with a vibrant cultural scene and natural beauty. Known as the “Hardware City,” New Britain has a rich industrial history and is home to some of the world’s top manufacturers such as Stanley Black & Decker. The city is also famous for its excellent museums, lively downtown, and numerous outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, art lover, or nature enthusiast, New Britain has plenty to offer. This comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your visit to this wonderful city.

List of Places to Visit in New Britain

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

New Britain is home to several world-class museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the city’s rich history and culture. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit in New Britain:

  • The New Britain Museum of American Art: This impressive museum houses over 12,000 works of American art from the seventeenth century to the present day. The museum’s collections include paintings, sculptures, photographs, and decorative arts.

  • The New Britain Industrial Museum: Located in the heart of downtown, this museum takes visitors through the city’s fascinating industrial history. The museum’s exhibits showcase the tools, machines, and products that made New Britain one of the world’s leading manufacturing centers.

  • The Polish American Foundation of Connecticut Museum and Library: This museum, located in a historic Polish neighborhood, celebrates the city’s Polish heritage. Visitors can view exhibits on Polish culture, history, and traditions, including a replica of a traditional Polish village.

  • The New Britain Youth Museum: This fun and interactive museum is designed for children and families. The museum’s exhibits focus on science, history, art, and culture.

Natural Attractions of New Britain: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

New Britain is blessed with a wealth of natural beauty, including parks, forests, and lakes. There are plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore and admire the stunning landscapes. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in New Britain:

  • Walnut Hill Park: This beautiful park, located in the heart of the city, boasts stunning views of the surrounding hills. The park features rolling lawns, wooded groves, walking trails, and a picturesque pond.

  • Hungerford Park: This sprawling park covers over 200 acres and offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and picnicking. The park is also home to an animal sanctuary with a variety of exotic and domestic animals.

  • West Peak: This scenic peak, located in the traprock ridges of the Hanging Hills, offers panoramic views of the city and beyond. Visitors can hike to the summit via the popular Blue Trail or the more challenging Ragged Mountain Trail.

  • Pinnacle Rock State Park: This small but stunning park features a towering rock formation that affords breathtaking views of the city and surrounding countryside. The park is popular for hiking, picnicking, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Britain

New Britain is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with miles of scenic trails and paths that wind through forests, along rivers, and up mountains. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore in New Britain:

  • The Farmington Canal Heritage Trail: This popular 80-mile trail runs from New Haven to Northampton, Massachusetts, passing through New Britain. The trail follows the route of the historic Farmington Canal and features scenic views of the Connecticut River Valley.

  • Metacomet Trail: This 62-mile trail follows the ridgeline of the Metacomet Ridge, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The trail runs through New Britain’s popular Hanging Hills and is a favorite among hikers and rock climbers.

  • The East Coast Greenway: This 3,000-mile trail stretches from Florida to Maine, passing through New Britain along the way. The trail offers a mix of on-road and off-road paths and is popular with both hikers and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in New Britain: Activities and Tours

New Britain offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in New Britain:

  • Go on a Stanley Black & Decker Factory Tour: This popular factory tour allows visitors to see firsthand how some of the world’s best tools and products are made. The tour includes a visit to the company’s world headquarters, a stop at the tool museum, and a look at the company’s research and development labs.

  • Visit Lake Compounce: This popular amusement park is one of the oldest in the country and features a range of rides, attractions, and shows. The park also has a waterpark and is a popular destination for families.

  • Attend a New Britain Bees Baseball Game: This minor league baseball team plays at the New Britain Stadium and offers an affordable and enjoyable evening of entertainment.

  • Take a Art Walk Tour of Downtown: This self-guided tour showcases the public art installations and murals throughout the city’s downtown district. Visitors can walk, bike or take a bus to explore and appreciate the artworks.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Britain

If you’re planning a week-long trip to New Britain, here’s a suggested itinerary that includes the top attractions and activities:

Day 1: Visit the New Britain Museum of American Art and explore the city’s downtown district.

Day 2: Hike the Blue Trail in the Hanging Hills and enjoy the views from West Peak.

Day 3: Take a tour of the Stanley Black & Decker factory and tool museum.

Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Walnut Hill Park, Hungerford Park, and the New Britain Youth Museum.

Day 5: Attend a New Britain Bees game at New Britain Stadium.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive to nearby destinations like Hartford or Lake Compounce.

Day 7: Take a Art Walk Tour of Downtown, and shop for souvenirs in the city’s unique stores

Food and Drink Scene of New Britain

New Britain is a melting pot of cultures, and its food and drink scene reflects this diversity. Visitors can sample a variety of cuisines and flavors, from classic American fare to ethnic delicacies. Here are some recommendations:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Polish Plate: This classic dish features traditional Polish favorites like kielbasa, pierogi, and stuffed cabbage.

  • Italian Sub at Grappler’s Grill: Grapplers Grill is a beautifully restored vintage diner near Downtown which offers old-style diner grub with modern servings.

  • Hot Dogs at Capitol Lunch: Serving their famous steamed hot dogs since 1929 with their special meat sauce, Capitol Lunch is a New Britain staple.

  • Pizza at Roma or Star: New Britain is famous for its pizza, with both Roma and Star having been voted the best pizza in CT multiple times.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Drink local with Connecticut’s local breweries with Two Phase, Kinsmen, Five Churches Brewing, and Alvarium Brewery are some of the top-rated spots.

  • Apple Cider: Made with locally grown apples, Apple cider, and apple cider donuts from Rogers Orchard are must-try while here in the fall season.

Where to Stay in New Britain: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Britain offers a range of options for lodging, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some suggestions:

  • The Red Roof Inn: Conveniently located off Route 9, Red Roof Inn is a budget-friendly option that features spacious rooms and modern amenities.

  • The Grand Hotel at Cheshire Square: This upscale hotel is located in the heart of downtown and features luxurious rooms and suites, an on-site restaurant and bar, and access to a fitness center.

  • The American Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located just outside the city and offers a cozy and comfortable stay.

  • Hostel New Britain: This affordable and well-maintained hostel is designed for backpackers and budget travelers and features dormitory-style rooms and communal facilities.

What to Buy in New Britain: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

New Britain is home to a wealth of unique and locally-made products that make for excellent souvenirs and gifts. Here are some ideas:

  • Stanley Tools: Stanley Black & Decker manufactures some of the world’s best tools and products, including power tools, hand tools, and hardware. Visitors can purchase Stanley products at the company’s research and development store.

  • Polish Pottery: New Britain’s large Polish community means that visitors can find a wealth of beautiful Polish pottery and ceramics at stores like Ursula’s store.

  • City-themed Merchandise: Souvenir shops in the downtown area offer a choice of New Britain-themed shirts, mugs, magnets, and other items.

Local Culture and Customs of New Britain

New Britain is a diverse and welcoming community, and visitors can experience the local culture and customs firsthand during their visit. Here are some tips:

  • Embrace the City’s Diversity: New Britain is home to a range of ethnic groups, including Polish, Italian, and Latino populations. Visitors are encouraged to explore the city’s unique neighborhoods and appreciate the local customs and traditions.

  • Attend a Cultural Event: New Britain hosts a range of cultural events throughout the year, including Polish festivals, Italian festivals, and Hispanic celebrations. Visitors are encouraged to attend these events to experience the local culture and art.

  • Appreciate the Art: New Britain is known for its vibrant art scene, with many public art installations and murals throughout the downtown area. Visitors are encouraged to take a self-guided Art Walk Tour to appreciate the local art scene.

Best Time to Visit New Britain

New Britain is a year-round destination, with each season bringing a unique set of experiences and attractions. Spring and fall are the best times to visit, with milder temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities. Summer can be hot and humid, but there are plenty of outdoor concerts and festivals to enjoy. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it is a magical time to explore the city’s winter markets, holiday events, and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Britain

Do:

  • Explore The walking trails and pathways in New Britain’s natural areas like the Hungerford, Walnut Hill Park and the Hanging Hills.

  • Attend a sporting event at the New Britain Baseball Stadium, which includes a Double-A Boston Red Sox team called the New Britain Bees..

  • Visit carefully selected monuments and locations of cultural and historical significance, like the New Britain Industrial Museum and the Polish American Foundation of Connecticut Museum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended in your car when parked on the streets.

  • Don’t litter New Britain’s beautiful natural parks and recreational areas. Please use bins when available.

  • Don’t forget to check out the local store hours. Many of them can vary with the season.

Safety and Health

New Britain is a safe and healthy place to visit, but visitors should take precautions like in any new destination. Here are some tips:

Safety in New Britain

  • Carry a lock when exploring the city’s outdoor areas and trails and park your car in a well-lit area.

  • Don’t wander around alone at night if you’re not familiar with the neighborhood.

  • Follow precautionary guidelines during the Pandemic times.

Health in New Britain

  • New Britain has a variety of hospitals and medical facilities, including the Hospital of Central Connecticut, New Britain General Campus and the Veterans Affairs Hospital.

  • Use sunscreen and warm clothes when exploring the natural attractions in New Britain to avoid compounding weather-related ailments.

Getting to New Britain: Transportation Options and Logistics

New Britain is easily accessible from nearby airports and major highways, with several transportation options available. Here are some suggestions:

Airports Near New Britain

  • Bradley International Airport: Located in Windsor Locks, Bradley Airport is the closest major airport to New Britain, served by domestic and international airlines.

  • Tweed New Haven Regional Airport: This small airport is located in New Haven and offers flights to several nearby cities.

Bus Stations Near New Britain

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound provides service from both Hartford and New Haven to New Britain, with multiple daily trips.

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: Peter Pan offers several trips from Boston to New Britain each day.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: New Britain is served by the Amtrak train station, which provides direct service to major cities like Boston, New York City, and Washington D.C.

  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Several taxi and ride-sharing services operate in New Britain, providing convenient transportation to visitors.

How to Get Around in New Britain

New Britain is easy to navigate, with several modes of transportation available. Here are some suggestions:

  • Walking: Many of New Britain’s top attractions are located within walking distance of each other, especially in the downtown area.

  • Biking: New Britain has several well-maintained bike paths and trails, making it easy to explore the city on two wheels.

  • Public Transportation: CT Transit operates a local bus service throughout New Britain, making getting around easy and affordable.

  • Driving: New Britain is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the city. Parking is available at most attractions and downtown areas.

Where to Go Next from New Britain: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Britain is situated in the heart of the Connecticut River Valley, making it the perfect base to explore nearby destinations. Here are some suggestions for day trips and excursions:

  • Hartford: The capital city of Connecticut is just a short drive from New Britain and offers a wealth of attractions, including the Mark Twain House, the Connecticut Science Center and the Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art.

  • Mount Southington: This popular ski resort is located just outside of New Britain and offers 14 trails, night skiing, and snowboarding.

  • Lake Compounce: This popular amusement park, the oldest in the country, features more than 50 rides, attractions, and shows.

Conclusion

New Britain is a hidden gem in the Connecticut River Valley with a rich history, impressive museums, and outdoor beauty. The city offers visitors a range of activities, attractions, and lodging options, making it an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or week-long vacation. Now, explore the wonderfully diverse city of New Britain.
