Introduction to Ellington

Nestled in the picturesque Connecticut River Valley, Ellington is a charming town in Tolland County, Connecticut. The town’s rich history dates back to the 1700s, and it has since grown into a thriving community of approximately 16,000 residents. Ellington offers visitors a range of things to do, including hiking on scenic trails, exploring museums and historical landmarks, and enjoying local food and drink.

List of Places to Visit in Ellington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ellington Historical Society - This nonprofit organization is dedicated to preserving the history of Ellington. Visitors can take a guided tour of the Nellie McKnight Museum, which showcases artifacts and exhibits from the town’s past.
  • Crystal Lake Historical Society Museum - This museum features exhibits and memorabilia related to the history of Crystal Lake and the surrounding area.
  • Nathan Hale Homestead - Located just a short drive from Ellington in Coventry, this historic home was once the residence of Revolutionary War hero Nathan Hale. Guided tours of the homestead are available.
  • Tolland County Agricultural Center - This sprawling complex hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including the annual Tolland County 4-H Fair.

Natural Attractions of Ellington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shenipsit State Forest - This 7,000-acre forest offers hiking, camping, fishing, and mountain biking opportunities.
  • Willington Hill Preserve - This 633-acre preserve features a variety of scenic trails that wind through forests and open fields.
  • Crystal Lake - This 200-acre lake is a popular destination in the summer for swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Hockanum River Trail - This 15-mile trail follows the Hockanum River through several towns, including Ellington.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellington

  • Shenipsit Trail - A 50-mile trail that passes through Shenipsit State Forest and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Ellington Ridge Loop Trail - An easy 1.6-mile loop trail that meanders through forests and over streams.
  • Hockanum River Trail - A 15-mile trail that follows the Hockanum River through several towns, including Ellington.

Best Things to Do in Ellington: Activities and Tours

  • Go on a brewery tour - The town has several breweries, including the popular Cold Creek Brewery, which offers tours and tastings.
  • Attend an outdoor concert - The Ellington Summer Concert Series features live music performances in various parks throughout the summer.
  • Visit the farmers market - The Ellington Farmers Market runs from June to October and offers fresh produce, baked goods, and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ellington

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Ellington’s museums and historical landmarks, including the Nellie McKnight Museum and the Crystal Lake Historical Society Museum.
Day 2 Hike the Ellington Ridge Loop Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch at one of the scenic overlooks.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellington

Must-Try Dishes

  • New England clam chowder - A hearty soup made with clams, potatoes, and cream.
  • Lobster roll - A popular New England specialty consisting of fresh lobster meat served on a toasted hot dog bun.
  • Apple cider doughnuts - Made with fresh apples and warm spices, these doughnuts are a fall favorite in Connecticut.

Local Beverages

  • Connecticut wine - The state has a growing wine industry, with several vineyards located within a short drive of Ellington.
  • Connecticut craft beer - The town has several breweries, including Cold Creek Brewery and Yankee Cider Company.
  • Apple cider - Made with fresh apples from local orchards, this seasonal beverage is a delicious fall treat.

Where to Stay in Ellington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites - This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Crystal Lake Inn - A charming bed and breakfast located near Crystal Lake.
  • Camping in Shenipsit State Forest - The forest has several camping options for those who enjoy sleeping under the stars.

What to Buy in Ellington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup - Connecticut is known for its maple syrup, which is harvested in the spring.
  • Local honey - The town has several apiaries that produce honey using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Handmade crafts and jewelry - The Ellington Farmers Market and local craft fairs offer a range of handmade goods from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellington

The people of Ellington are known for their friendly nature and welcoming spirit. The town has a strong sense of community, and residents often come together for events and festivals throughout the year. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and customs, especially when attending cultural events or visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Ellington

The best time to visit Ellington is in the fall, when the leaves are ablaze with vibrant colors and local orchards are harvesting apples. The summers are also a popular time to visit, as the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellington

Do:

  • Do take a scenic drive through the Connecticut River Valley to enjoy the stunning fall foliage.
  • Do try some of the local craft beer or wine.
  • Do rent a kayak or go fishing at one of the many lakes and ponds in the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment while hiking or camping.
  • Don’t trespass on private property or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray if spending time outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ellington

Ellington is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take typical precautions, such as locking their car doors and being aware of their surroundings after dark.

Health in Ellington

There are several medical facilities in the area, including Rockville General Hospital in nearby Vernon. Visitors should also bring any necessary medications with them, as some pharmacies may have limited hours.

Getting to Ellington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ellington

  • Bradley International Airport - Located approximately 25 miles south of Ellington, Bradley International Airport is the closest major airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Ellington

  • Tolland Citibus - This local bus service provides transportation within the town of Tolland and connects to other nearby towns.
  • Peter Pan Bus Lines - This bus service connects to major cities, including Boston and New York City, with a stop in nearby Hartford.

Other Options

  • Driving - Ellington is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area.
  • Biking - The town has several bike-friendly roads and trails for those who prefer to travel on two wheels.

How to Get Around in Ellington

The town is best explored on foot or by bike, with several scenic trails and quiet roads. Driving is also a popular option, with several parking lots available throughout the town. Taxis and ridesharing services are limited in the area.

Where to Go Next from Ellington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hartford, Connecticut - Located just 20 miles southwest of Ellington, Hartford is Connecticut’s capital city and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Springfield, Massachusetts - Located approximately 30 miles north of Ellington, Springfield is home to the Basketball Hall of Fame and several other museums and cultural landmarks.
  • Sturbridge, Massachusetts - This charming New England town is located approximately 40 miles northeast of Ellington and is known for its historic village and living history museum.

Conclusion

Ellington, Connecticut offers visitors a range of activities and attractions, from hiking and camping in picturesque state parks to exploring museums and enjoying local food and drink. With its strong sense of community and welcoming locals, Ellington is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of New England.
