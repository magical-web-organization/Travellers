Introduction to Vernon

Nestled in the Connecticut River Valley of Connecticut, Vernon is a charming town that boasts of scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and a rich history. It is conveniently located in the heart of New England, making it an ideal stopover for travelers exploring the region. The town is home to several museums, historic landmarks, hiking and biking trails, and more. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Vernon.

List of Places to Visit in Vernon

If you’re planning a trip to Vernon, here’s a list of places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New England Civil War Museum: The museum exhibits a vast collection of Civil War artifacts and memorabilia.
  • Vernon Historical Society Museum: The museum is a tribute to the history and heritage of Vernon.
  • Rockville Historic District: The district is a preserved area that showcases the architecture and lifestyle of the olden times.
  • Old Talcott Mill: The mill is an ancient industrial complex that houses a range of artistic and cultural institutions.

Natural Attractions of Vernon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valley Falls Park: The park boasts of stunning waterfalls, hiking and biking trails, and fishing spots.
  • Hockanum River Linear Park Trail: The trail is perfect for walking, running, and biking and offers scenic river views.
  • Rails-to-Trails Park: The park is a converted railway line and is perfect for hiking, biking, and picnics.
  • Air Line State Park Trail: The trail passes through stunning forest areas, wetlands, and bridges and is ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vernon

Vernon is home to many hiking and biking trails, offering breathtaking views and natural beauty. Some popular trails include:

  • Vernon Rails-to-Trails Park: The trail is 1.2 miles long and is perfect for a quick hike, bike, or run.
  • Hockanum River Trail: This trail is 10.3 miles long and offers scenic views of the Hockanum River.
  • Valley Falls Park Trail: The trail covers 5.5 miles and takes hikers through the stunning waterfalls and natural beauty of the Valley Falls Park.

Best Things to Do in Vernon: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for fun activities to do in Vernon, here’s what you can try:

  • Kayaking or canoeing on the Hockanum River
  • Fishing for trout at Valley Falls Park
  • Biking on the Rails-to-Trails Park
  • Taking a historical or cultural tour of the town

Week-Long Itinerary for Vernon

Day 1: Visit the New England Civil War Museum and explore the Rockville Historic District.

Day 2: Take a leisurely stroll on the Rails-to-Trails Park and discover the history of the Old Talcott Mill District.

Day 3: Hike or bike on the beautiful trails of Valley Falls Park and enjoy a picnic.

Day 4: Visit the Vernon Historical Society Museum to learn about the town’s heritage and culture.

Day 5: Enjoy a kayaking or canoeing adventure on the Hockanum River.

Day 6: Explore the natural beauty of the Hockanum River Linear Park Trail.

Day 7: Visit the Air Line State Park Trail and marvel at the beauty of the forest areas and bridges.

Food and Drink Scene of Vernon

Vernon has a variety of places to eat and drink, with a focus on local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages when in town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster rolls from Rein’s New York Style Deli
  • Steamed cheeseburgers from Ted’s Restaurant
  • Fresh seafood and sushi from Feng Asian Bistro

Local Beverages

  • Connecticut Wine Trail: Vernon is conveniently located near several wineries and vineyards, such as Jones Winery and Connecticut Valley Winery.
  • Craft Beer Scene: Local breweries such as Back East Brewing and Tankerhoosen Brewery offer a unique and flavorful experience.

  • Motel 6 Vernon
  • Red Roof Inn Hartford- Vernon

What to Buy in Vernon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some options:

  • Art and crafts at the Vernon Farmers’ Market
  • Local honey and maple syrup at Maple Hill Farms
  • Connecticut wines and craft beers at local wineries and breweries

Local Culture and Customs of Vernon

Vernon has a rich history and culture that reflects in its architecture, museums, and historic landmarks. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and the food and drink scene embraces the flavors of the region.

Best Time to Visit Vernon

The best time to visit Vernon is during the summer (June to August) when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall (September to October) is also a good time as the foliage turns to a beautiful hue of red and gold, making it a great time for hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vernon

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the town by not littering or harming the environment.
  • Visit the museums and historic landmarks to learn about the town’s history and heritage.
  • Sample local cuisine and beverages to get a true taste of Vernons flavors.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or disturb residents by being loud or obnoxious.
  • Leave valuables unattended while exploring the town.
  • Disrespect locals or their customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vernon

Vernon is a generally safe town, but as with any travel, it pays to stay vigilant. Some safety tips include:

  • Lock your car and don’t leave valuables in sight.
  • Keep your belongings close to you when exploring the town.
  • Watch for uneven terrain or potential hazards while on the trails.

Health in Vernon

There are no major health concerns in Vernon, but it’s always best to take necessary precautions. Some tips include:

  • Stay hydrated and wear proper sunscreen while hiking or biking.
  • Avoid tick bites by wearing long-sleeved clothes and using insect repellent.
  • If you have a medical emergency, seek help immediately from the nearest hospital or call 911.

Getting to Vernon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Vernon is conveniently located in the heart of New England and is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Vernon

  • Bradley International Airport (BDL) is located 22 miles southwest of Vernon and is the closest major airport.

Bus Stations Near Vernon

  • Vernon Transit Center offers daily bus service to major cities and towns in Connecticut.

Other Options

  • Amtrak train service is available between Hartford and New Haven.

How to Get Around in Vernon

Vernon is a small town, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Taxis are available, and there is a local bus service that connects different parts of the town.

Where to Go Next from Vernon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Hartford, Connecticut: The state capital is less than a half-hour drive from Vernon and offers several cultural and historical attractions.
  • New England States: The town is conveniently located near several New England states, including Massachusetts and Rhode Island, making it an ideal base for exploring the region.
  • Mystic, Connecticut: The charming seaside town is located an hour from Vernon and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Vernon, Connecticut, offers a charming mix of natural beauty, rich history, and outdoor adventures. It’s an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore the Connecticut River Valley and the surrounding New England states. From museums and historic landmarks to scenic hiking trails and local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Vernon. Pack your bags and get ready for a memorable trip!
