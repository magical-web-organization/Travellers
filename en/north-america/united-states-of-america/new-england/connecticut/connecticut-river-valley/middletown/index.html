Introduction to Middletown

Located in the beautiful Connecticut River Valley, Middletown is a charming city known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and vibrant culture. Founded in 1650, Middletown is home to historic architecture, museums, and a lively arts and music scene. With its natural beauty, outdoor adventures and picturesque surroundings, Middletown is a popular destination for nature lovers, hikers, and cyclists. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do, see, eat, and stay in Middletown.

List of Places to Visit in Middletown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wesleyan University: Known for its liberal arts curriculum, Wesleyan is one of the top-ranked universities in the country. The campus features beautiful architecture, art galleries, and museums, including the Davison Art Center and the Wesleyan University Museum of Art.

  • Middlesex County Historical Society: Housed in a historic mansion, this museum showcases the rich history of Middlesex County from 1700 to the present day. Exhibits include period furnishings, clothing, art, and artifacts.

  • General Mansfield House: Built in 1819, this historic mansion served as the home of General Joseph K.F. Mansfield, a hero of the Civil War. The house is open for guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Middletown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wadsworth Falls State Park: Spread over 285 acres, this state park features waterfalls, hiking trails, picnic areas, fishing, and swimming.

  • Middletown Nature Gardens: This serene oasis features walking trails, gardens, and wooded areas. Visitors can enjoy nature walks, bird watching, and picnicking.

  • Mattabesset Trail: This 50-mile hiking trail runs through Middletown and offers stunning views of the Connecticut River Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Middletown

  • Airline Trail: This 50-mile trail runs through Middletown and is perfect for hiking, cycling, and horseback riding. The trail offers scenic views of farmland, woods, and streams.

  • Mattabesset Bike Trail: This 10-mile bike trail runs through the heart of Middletown and offers stunning views of the Connecticut River.

Best Things to Do in Middletown: Activities and Tours

  • Copper Hill Golf Club: This 18-hole golf course offers scenic views of the Connecticut River Valley and rolling hills.

  • Kidcity Children’s Museum: This interactive museum is a great place for families to explore and learn through hands-on exhibits and activities.

  • Connecticut River Expeditions: This riverboat tour offers scenic views of the Middletown area and its natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Middletown

Day 1: Visit Wesleyan University and Davison Art Center.

Day 2: Explore Middletown Nature Gardens and Wadsworth Falls State Park.

Day 3: Spend the day golfing at Copper Hill Golf Club.

Day 4: Take a tour of historic General Mansfield House and the Middlesex County Historical Society.

Day 5: Hike the Mattabesset Trail and enjoy the scenic views of the Connecticut River Valley.

Day 6: Visit the Kidcity Children’s Museum for some family fun.

Day 7: Take a riverboat tour with Connecticut River Expeditions and enjoy breathtaking views of Middletown.

Food and Drink Scene of Middletown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: With its proximity to the coast, Middletown is known for its fresh seafood dishes such as clams, lobster, and scallops.

  • Pizza: Middletown is home to some of the best pizza joints in the state, including Eli Cannon’s Tap Room and First and Last Tavern.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Middletown is home to several breweries, including Stubborn Beauty Brewing Company, which offers a variety of craft beers.

  • Wine: Connecticut is known for its vineyards and wineries, and Middletown is home to several such as Gouveia Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Middletown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Inn at Middletown: This historic hotel is located in the heart of downtown Middletown and features luxurious rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Wesley Inn & Suites: This family-owned hotel is located close to Wesleyan University and features comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Middletown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Connecticut Wine: Bring home a bottle of locally produced wine from one of the nearby vineyards.

  • Handcrafted Items: Visit local craft stores such as Blue Moon Artisans for unique, handcrafted items made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Middletown

Middletown is a bustling city with a thriving arts and music scene. Visitors can enjoy live music, concerts, and theater performances throughout the year. The city is also home to several festivals such as the Middletown Farmers Market and the Riverfront Music Festival, which showcase the local food and culture.

Best Time to Visit Middletown

The best time to visit Middletown is during the fall (September to November) when foliage is at its peak, and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middletown

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Middletown offers a wide range of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and picnicking at the state parks.

  • Support local businesses: Middletown has a vibrant local business community, so make sure to explore the city’s restaurants, cafes, and shops.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Respect the environment by disposing of your trash properly.

  • Speeding: Be mindful of the speed limits and traffic rules when driving on the roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Middletown

Middletown is a safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise caution and follow usual safety precautions.

Health in Middletown

Middletown has several hospitals, clinics, and healthcare facilities in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Middletown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Middletown

  • Bradley International Airport (BDL): Located in Windsor Locks, CT, Bradley International Airport is the closest airport to Middletown.

Bus Stations Near Middletown

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: This bus service operates several routes to and from Middletown.

Other Options

  • Train: Amtrak and Metro-North Railroad both operate train services to and from Middletown.

How to Get Around in Middletown

Middletown is a walkable city, and visitors can explore downtown and nearby attractions on foot. Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are also available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Middletown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hartford: The state capital of Connecticut is located just 15 miles north of Middletown and offers several attractions such as the Mark Twain House and Museum.

  • New Haven: This historic coastal city is located approximately 35 miles south of Middletown and is home to several museums, galleries, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Middletown is a wonderful destination in Connecticut that offers a mix of nature, history, and culture. From scenic hikes to historic mansions, visitors can explore the city’s unique charm and beauty. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a longer stay, Middletown has something to offer for everyone.
