Introduction to Greenwich

Located in Connecticut’s affluent Fairfield County, Greenwich is a hidden gem that boasts luxurious estates, rich history, and natural beauty. As a part of New England, this town has a charming character with colonial-style architecture, scenic backdrops, and warm hospitality. Greenwich offers a lot in terms of recreation, culture, and cuisine. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, the tastiest foods to try, the most scenic hiking trails, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Greenwich

Greenwich is a town full of surprises, with numerous attractions that cater to every interest. Here are some of the best places to visit in Greenwich.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bruce Museum of Arts and Science: Located in Greenwich’s historic district, this museum houses an impressive collection of art and science exhibits.
  • Bush-Holley House: One of the oldest homes in Greenwich, Bush-Holley House is a historical landmark that dates back to 1730. The house now serves as a museum, showcasing the town’s history and its cultural evolution.
  • Greenwich Historical Society: Featuring multiple exhibits and events year-round, the Historical Society celebrates the rich history of the town, and offers educational programs for visitors of all ages.
  • Putnam Cottage: Known as the “Knapp Tavern” during the American Revolution, this historical cottage marks the site of a strategic meeting that influenced the outcome of the war.
  • The Hyatt House Bed and Breakfast: Built in 1868, the Hyatt House is now a quaint B&B that retains its Victorian charm with antique furnishings, and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Natural Attractions of Greenwich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Audubon Greenwich: This 285-acre sanctuary houses numerous birds, butterflies, and other wildlife species. Visitors can hike, canoe, and attend educational programs.
  • Greenwich Point: A favorite destination among beach lovers, this park offers a long stretch of sandy beach, picnic areas, and walking trails that overlook the Long Island Sound.
  • Mianus River Park: Spread over 391 acres of lush woodlands, Mianus River Park offers miles of hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and scenic vistas.
  • Great Captain Island: One of the most fascinating places in Greenwich, this island is accessible by ferry and boasts scenic views, a lighthouse, and a wildlife sanctuary.
  • Pomerance Park: a 230-acre park with hiking trails, streams and a pond that is surrounded by lush greenery and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenwich

Greenwich’s natural beauty is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are the best hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • Greenwich Audubon Trails: Audubon Greenwich offers over 7 miles of hiking trails that take visitors through forests, wetlands, and meadows.
  • Mianus River State Park: The park has hiking trails that span across 400 acres, leading to scenic views of the Mianus River and the surrounding forests.
  • Spruce Mountain: a 45-minute climb up the mountain rewards hikers with a panoramic view of Greenwich.
  • Greenwich Point Park: Cyclists and hikers can enjoy the park’s scenic vistas and the coastal breeze while walking or biking for miles.

Best Things to Do in Greenwich: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours visitors can take advantage of when visiting Greenwich.

  • Cruise along the Long Island Sound: Take a scenic boat tour to see Greenwich from the water, and enjoy the fresh ocean breeze.
  • Equestrian experiences at Conyers Farm: Explore the Conyers Farm’s scenic trails on horseback and admire Greenwich’s natural beauty from a unique perspective.
  • Fall foliage tour: A drive through the town’s scenic routes during autumn is an unforgettable experience, with beautiful colors painting the town.
  • Wine Tasting at local winery: Sip the finest wines produced in local wineries in and around Greenwich.
  • Summer Outdoor Music Events: enjoy jazz, folk, rock and classical music in a variety of outdoor locations

Food and Drink Scene of Greenwich

Greenwich’s dining scene is diverse and offers something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll: The most iconic dish in Greenwich, this seafood staple is served up in numerous restaurants around town.
  • Pizza: Known for its deliciously crispy crust and fresh toppings, a slice from a renowned pizza joint in Greenwich is a must-try.
  • Cocktail Oyster: Fresh oysters served with a spicy cocktail sauce are a popular appetizer in Greenwich.
  • Ravioli: Handmade ravioli stuffed with ricotta and spinach, topped with marinara sauce is a local favorite.
  • New York-style Pastrami Sandwich: A Greenwich deli staple, you can’t leave town without trying the mouth-watering grilled pastrami sandwich.

Local Beverages

  • Cocktails: Riverside’s Polpo Restaurant is known for its creative and carefully crafted cocktails, while the views from the Water’s Edge at Greenwich are a perfect accompaniment to your drinks.
  • Wine: Known for its great vineyards Greenwich offers a great selection of local wines with top picks being Centennial Ardelle and White Birch Vineyards.
  • Beer: Frank Pepes Pizzeria and tavern for great local beer choices like the Glory Haze.

Where to Stay in Greenwich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The J House Greenwich: A trendy hotel with a spa, fitness center, and a rooftop bar, The J House Greenwich is a favorite among travelers.
  • Delamar Greenwich Harbor: Situated on the waterfront, this luxurious hotel offers stunning views of Greenwich Harbor, with numerous amenities including a spa and pool.
  • Hyatt Regency Greenwich: With a prime location near Stamford and Greenwich attractions, this hotel offers modern amenities including a fitness center, pool, and spa.
  • Airbnb: There are several affordable Airbnb options in Greenwich with great access to local amenities and attractions.

What to Buy in Greenwich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Greenwich Avenue: Greenwich’s high-end retail area has luxury boutiques and designer stores, offering a shopping experience like no other.
  • Greenwich Farmers Market: A weekly event where visitors can purchase local produce and hand-made crafts.
  • Juliska: Greenwich’s own ceramic and glassware company offers a variety of home items with bespoke designs.
  • The Gifted Monkey: A gift and souvenir shop with quirky items that embody the character of the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenwich

Greenwich has a rich history and is known for its appreciation of luxury and high-end living, with affluent lifestyles being the norm in this town. The town is friendly and welcoming to visitors that respect local culture and customs. Residents pride themselves on being both stylish and understated, and it’s important to dress neat and tidy, even in casual settings.

Best Time to Visit Greenwich

Greenwich is a great place to visit year-round. However, the best time to visit is during the fall, from September through November, as the foliage changes and the weather is cooler, making it perfect for outdoor activities. Spring is also a great time to visit, with the blooming of various flowers at Greenwich’s beautiful gardens.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenwich

Here are some tips to make the most out of your visit while respecting the local culture.

Do:

  • Respect residents’ privacy.
  • Tidy up after yourself in restaurants and other public settings.
  • Make a reservation at high-end restaurants and cafes.
  • Follow pedestrian rules and traffic signals.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of private homes or estates without prior permission.
  • Speak too loudly in public areas.
  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Dress too informally in upscale settings.

Safety and Health

Greenwich is a safe destination, with low crime rates. The town’s health system is exceptional, with quality healthcare facilities available in case of an emergency.

Safety in Greenwich

  • Stick to well-lit and well-traveled areas.
  • Secure your belongings in public areas.
  • Follow traffic rules when driving or walking.

Health in Greenwich

  • There are many healthcare facilities and hospitals available in the town. The Greenwich Hospital is known to be one of the best in Connecticut.

Getting to Greenwich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greenwich

  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA): 20 miles away from Greenwich, LaGuardia is accessible via taxi, bus, or rental car.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK): 40 miles away from Greenwich, JFK is accessible via taxi, bus, or rental car.
  • Westchester County Airport (HPN): Just 7 miles away from Greenwich, Westchester County Airport is the closest airport to the town and is easily accessible by taxi and rental car.

Bus Stations Near Greenwich

  • Greenwich Bus Station: Located on the corner of Lewis and Chapel Street, Greenwich Bus Station is the town’s central station with many daily intercity bus services between New Haven and New York City.

Other Options

  • Metro-North Railroad: Connecticut’s commuter rail system is a great option to visit Greenwich. The ride from New York’s Grand Central Station takes around 50 minutes and is a smooth and scenic journey.

How to Get Around in Greenwich

Greenwich has an excellent transport system, with many affordable options available:

  • Bicycle: Cycling is a great mode of transportation as the terrain is relatively flat and easy to navigate. You can rent bicycles from local shops and explore Greenwich at your own pace.
  • Car: Renting a car is a convenient option for exploring Greenwich and neighboring towns.
  • Public Transportation: The local bus service and Metro-North Railroad are convenient, cost-effective, and eco-friendly ways of traveling the town.

Where to Go Next from Greenwich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Greenwich, here are some nearby destinations to include in your itinerary:

  • Westport: Known for its beautiful beaches and upscale shopping scene, Westport is a charming town just 15 miles from Greenwich.
  • Stamford: A vibrant city just 15 miles away from Greenwich, Stamford is home to great museums, waterfront parks and top-notch restaurants.
  • New Canaan: Located about 10 miles northeast of Greenwich, New Canaan is a picturesque town featuring many architectural landmarks designed by acclaimed architects such as Frank Lloyd Wright.
  • Norwalk: Known for its maritime history, Norwalk is another charming Connecticut town, with a great museum describing the history of lighthouses in America.

Conclusion

Greenwich is a haven that offers many opportunities for visitors to explore its rich history, scenic beauty, and luxurious lifestyle. With its natural reserves, charming architecture, excellent dining options, and vibrant cultural scene, Greenwich is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, history buff, or a foodie, Greenwich has something for you. We hope this guide has helped you plan your next visit to this charming Connecticut town.
