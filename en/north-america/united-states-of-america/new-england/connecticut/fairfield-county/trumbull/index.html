Introduction to Trumbull

Nestled in Fairfield County, in Connecticut, Trumbull offers its visitors an unparalleled New England experience. If you’re looking for a calming getaway with a small-town feel, Trumbull is the perfect destination. The picturesque landscape, vibrant culture, and historical sites make it an excellent choice for a weekend trip. Whether you’re traveling with family or friends, this quaint town offers plenty of activities and experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Trumbull

Here’s a comprehensive list of places to visit when you’re in Trumbull.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Trumbull is steeped in rich history, and its museums and historical landmarks pay homage to its past. Check out these must-see cultural attractions:

  1. The Trumbull Historical Society Museum - This museum is home to artifacts that depict Trumbull’s history, ranging from the colonial era to the present.
  2. The Little Red Schoolhouse - This schoolhouse takes you on a trip to yesteryear, with tours available by appointment.
  3. The Nichols Fountain - Installed in 1907, the fountain is a historic landmark in Trumbull and an instagrammable spot.

Natural Attractions of Trumbull: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trumbull’s natural landscapes offer a refreshing break from the humdrum of city life. The scenic beauty of this town is bound to take your breath away. Here are our top picks for the natural attractions:

  1. Twin Brooks Park - This park is spread over 243 acres, and it is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and picnicking.
  2. Pequonnock River Valley State Park - This state park is an excellent spot for nature enthusiasts and hikers. Enjoy the picturesque trails, streams, and forested landscapes.
  3. Sports Hill Farm - The farm offers apple picking, vegetable picking, pony rides, and hayrides for families to escape from busy life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trumbull

Trumbull has scenic biking and hiking trails that offer breathtaking views and allow visitors to explore the town’s natural beauty. Here’s a list of the top hiking/biking routes in Trumbull:

  1. Pequonnock River Trail - This eight-mile trail rewards visitors with fantastic views of the river and its surrounding greenery. The trail is an easy-to-moderate level, and it provides a great opportunity for nature lovers to spend a relaxing afternoon.
  2. Indian Ledge Park - Walk the Indian Ledge Park Trail and enjoy a picnic on the grass or take a hike with friends through the beautiful surroundings.
  3. Tashua Recreation Area - The Tashua Recreation Area Trail is a well-maintained, easy hike, perfect for families with small children.
  4. Rails-to-Trails - This trail is ideal for bike riders of all levels and abilities. Visitors can bike for 21 miles and take in the beautiful countryside along the way.

Best Things to Do in Trumbull: Activities and Tours

Trumbull offers several fun activities and tours to make your trip unforgettable. Here are some of the best things to do when you’re in Trumbull:

  1. Take a tour of Weir Farm National Historical Park - The house of the famous painter J. Alden Weir is a mandatory stop for all art lovers who visit Trumbull.
  2. Visit Beardsley Zoo - Enjoy a family day out at Beardsley Zoo and see unique animals such as Amur tigers, giant anteaters, and red pandas.
  3. Attend the annual Arts and Cultural Festival - The Trumbull Arts Festival is an excellent opportunity for visitors to see performances by talented musicians, dancers, and artists in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Trumbull

Trumbull’s food scene is a mix of famous and lesser-known restaurants that offer an array of flavors and cuisines. Here are our must-tries dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Great Indian Restaurant - offers some of the best Indian food in the area.
  2. Trumbull Kitchen - a popular seafood restaurant that offers an extensive menu.
  3. Brewport - a unique brewery that serves delicious pizzas.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking for a sip of something local, check out these local beverages:

  1. Two Roads Brewing Company - This brewery offers beer and food and welcomes visitors for a tour of the facility.
  2. Aspetuck Brew Lab - If you’re a beer lover, Aspetuck Brew Lab should be on your list to check out. The brewery has an outdoor seating area, and visitors can enjoy food trucks on weekends.

What to Buy in Trumbull: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors love taking souvenirs from their trips, and Trumbull has no shortage of cool things to bring back. Here are our top picks:

  1. Dare to Dream Farm - A farm on Main Street that offers delicious honey and beeswax products.
  2. The Farm - Gift Shop - The perfect place to grab a little gift for the family, which has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Trumbull

Trumbull has some unique customs that truly define its spirit. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  1. Trumbull loves its football - High school football is a big deal around here and is an excellent way to support the community.
  2. Trumbull Day - Trumbull Day is an annual event hosted in the fall at Indian Ledge Park. It includes music, amusement rides, food trucks, and various other forms of entertainment.

Best Time to Visit Trumbull

Trumbull is lovely year-round, but the best time to visit is during the fall season (September to November). The weather is comfortable, and New England’s infamous autumn foliage adds a pop of color to the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trumbull

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to help your trip go smoothly:

Do:

  • Do plan ahead so you can make the most of your time in Trumbull.
  • Do enjoy the beautiful nature and outdoor adventures that Trumbull has to offer.
  • Do take time to learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack your rain jacket and waterproof shoes.
  • Don’t forget to book your hotel in advance.
  • Don’t drive too fast along the winding roads, as they can be treacherous on rainy days.

Safety and Health

Trumbull is a safe town, but you should always be on alert, especially at night. Here are a few tips for staying safe and healthy during your trip.

Safety in Trumbull

  • Always be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.
  • Keep your money and valuables in safe places.
  • Secure your belongings in your hotel and car.

Health in Trumbull

  • Always stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Wear sunscreen and bug spray to protect yourself from the sun and bugs.
  • Carry any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Trumbull: Transportation Options and Logistics

Trumbull is accessible through various transportation modes. Here are some options:

Airports Near Trumbull

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport is 60 miles west of Trumbull.
  • LaGuardia Airport is 55 miles west of Trumbull.

Bus Stations Near Trumbull

  • Greyhound Bus provides service to Bridgeport, which is only a short drive from Trumbull.

Other Options

  • The best option is to drive to Trumbull. From New York City, it’s only a 75-minute drive.

How to Get Around in Trumbull

Trumbull is a small town, and getting around is easy. If you prefer walking or biking, there are plenty of trails to enjoy. However, renting a car will make it more convenient to explore the different attractions and landmarks.

Where to Go Next from Trumbull: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trumbull has several neighboring towns and cities that you can explore. Here are a few places where you can extend your adventure:

  • New Haven - Explore the town’s rich history, Yale University, the Peabody Museum, and its challenging restaurants and bars.
  • Westport - A very traditional New England town famous for its arts scene, good boutiques, and restaurants.
