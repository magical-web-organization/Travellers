Introduction to Ridgefield

Located in Fairfield County, Connecticut, Ridgefield is a picturesque town in New England that is famous for its rich history, natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Nestled amidst the rolling hills of the Berkshire Mountains, the town offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, giving visitors a chance to unwind, relax, and rejuvenate.

List of Places to Visit in Ridgefield

Ridgefield has a lot to offer to its visitors, from museums and historical sites to natural attractions and scenic views. Here are some of the must-visit places in Ridgefield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Keeler Tavern Museum and Garden
  • The Aldrich Contemporary Art Museum
  • Ridgefield Historical Society
  • The Ridgefield Playhouse
  • Weir Farm National Historic Site

Natural Attractions of Ridgefield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seth Low Pierrepont State Park
  • Hemlock Hills/Pinoak Ridge Natural Area
  • Bennett’s Pond State Park
  • Pine Mountain

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ridgefield

Ridgefield is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with many scenic trails that offer stunning views of the town’s natural beauty. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Norwalk River Valley Trail
  • The Ridgefield Loop Trail
  • The Pine Mountain Trail
  • Hemlock Hills Trail

Best Things to Do in Ridgefield: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other exciting things to do in Ridgefield. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride with Balloons Over the Rainbow
  • Go horseback riding at Lee’s Riding Stables
  • Explore the town on a Segway tour with Wheeler Park Segway Tours
  • Go fishing and boating at Lake Mamanasco
  • Attend a live performance or concert at the Ridgefield Playhouse

Week-Long Itinerary for Ridgefield

Day 1: Start your trip by exploring Ridgefield’s cultural attractions, beginning with a visit to Keeler Tavern Museum and Garden and the nearby Ridgefield Historical Society. After lunch, head to The Aldrich Contemporary Art Museum and end the day by watching a live performance at The Ridgefield Playhouse.

Day 2: Take it easy today and enjoy Ridgefield’s outdoor adventures. Start with a scenic hike at Pine Mountain followed by a visit to Seth Low Pierrepont State Park. Spend the evening fishing and boating at Lake Mamanasco.

Day 3-4: For the next two days, explore Ridgefield’s vibrant downtown area, shop for souvenirs, and dine at local eateries. Take a Segway tour with Wheeler Park Segway Tours, visit Weir Farm National Historic Site, and experience a hot air balloon ride with Balloons Over the Rainbow.

Day 5: Today is all about natural attractions. Start with a leisurely hike at Hemlock Hills Trail and then visit Bennett’s Pond State Park afterward.

Day 6: Go horseback riding at Lee’s Riding Stables and end the day with a live performance at The Ridgefield Playhouse.

Day 7: Spend your last day hiking and biking on the scenic Ridgefield Loop Trail, taking in majestic views of Ridgefield’s undulating landscapes. End your trip by sampling some of the local products and dining at one of the town’s fine restaurants.

What to Buy in Ridgefield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ridgefield is known for its locally made craft beers, wines, and specialty foods, including cheese from Arethusa Farm. For souvenirs, check out the town’s many unique shops, including Books on the Common, a popular independent bookstore.

Local Culture and Customs of Ridgefield

Ridgefield is a town with deep roots in American history, and it prides itself on maintaining its traditional values. The town has a strong sense of community and is known for its welcoming locals. It’s important to dress appropriately when visiting museums or historic buildings.

Best Time to Visit Ridgefield

The best time to visit Ridgefield is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are at their best. Spring (April to May) is also a great time to visit, when the town is surrounded by blooming trees and flowers. Fall is a popular time, too, because of the town’s beautiful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ridgefield

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting museums or historic buildings
  • Enjoy the town’s local foods and beverages
  • Explore Ridgefield’s hiking and biking trails
  • Be respectful to locals and fellow visitors

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces
  • Disturb wildlife in natural areas
  • Ignore traffic rules and laws

Safety and Health

Safety in Ridgefield

Ridgefield is a very safe town, with a low crime rate.

Health in Ridgefield

Ridgefield has access to excellent healthcare facilities, including Danbury Hospital and New York-Presbyterian.

How To Get Around in Ridgefield

There are plenty of transportation options in Ridgefield, from walking and biking to renting a car or taking a taxi or ride-sharing service.
