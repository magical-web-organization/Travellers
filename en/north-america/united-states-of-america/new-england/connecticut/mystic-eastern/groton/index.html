Introduction to Groton

Groton, located in the Mystic-Eastern region of Connecticut, is a town filled with rich history and scenic views. It is known for its naval heritage, and the town is home to the United States Navy’s primary submarine base, the Naval Submarine Base New London. Groton is also the location of the Mystic Seaport Museum, which exhibits the region’s maritime history and houses historical ships.

Aside from its Naval roots, Groton is also an excellent place for outdoor activities. With parks and ocean beaches, Groton is perfect for hiking, biking, swimming, and fishing. In this tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and some cultural dos and don’ts of Groton.

List of Places to Visit in Groton

Here is a list of must-visit spots within Groton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mystic Seaport Museum: It is the largest maritime museum in the United States and houses historical ships and an extensive collection of maritime artifacts.

  2. Submarine Force Museum: It is the home of the US Navy’s submarine force, featuring submarine exhibits and artifacts.

  3. Fort Griswold Battlefield State Park: It is a state park that features a restored Revolutionary War fortification and houses museum exhibits about the American Revolution.

  4. Avery-Copp House: It is a historic house built-in 1808, featuring antique furniture and decorative art.

Natural Attractions of Groton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bluff Point State Park: It is a coastal reserve and state park covering 800 acres of open space filled with hiking trails, wildlife, and scenic views of Long Island Sound.

  2. Haley Farm State Park: With its stunning views of the Connecticut River and nearby islands, the park is an ideal spot for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

  3. Esker Point Beach: Located on a beautiful sand spit, it is a perfect place for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Groton

Apart from the state parks, Groton has plenty of jogging and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the notable options include:

  1. Noank Village: It is a village that offers scenic coastal views on its 2.5-mile loop trail.

  2. Mystic River Trail: It is a two-mile loop trail that passes picturesque Mystic River Bascule Bridge and leads to beautiful views of the river.

  3. Shennecossett Road Loop: It is a three-mile loop trail that offers scenic views of the Thames River, with the historic Shennecossett Golf Course nearby.

Best Things to Do in Groton: Activities and Tours

  1. Mystic Whaler Cruises: Go on a boat tour on a 110-foot wooden tall ship for a unique maritime and historical experience.

  2. Explore Mystic Village: It offers unique boutiques, restaurants, and specialty shops, reflecting the town’s maritime history.

  3. Take a Nature Walk: Visit the Allyn Preserve, featuring beautiful gardens filled with various species of flora and fauna that thrive in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Groton

Here is a week-long itinerary that you can follow to make the most out of your trip to Groton.

Day Activities
1 Visit Mystic Seaport Museum and enjoy dinner in Downtown Mystic.
2 Spend the day exploring the Bluff Point State Park and go picnicking at Haley Farm State Park.
3 Go for a nature walk in Allyn Preserve and explore Avery-Copp House in the evening.
4 Take a boat tour on a Mystic Whaler Cruise and explore Mystic Village.
5 Hike the Noank village trail to view the scenic coastal views, and spend the evening at Esker Point beach.
6 Take a trip to Fort Trumbull State Park and explore its museum exhibits.
7 Spend the day sailing in the Long Island Sound or exploring the town’s naval history.

Food and Drink Scene of Groton

Groton, with its coastal charm, has plenty of food and drink options. The town offers everything from beach-style food to top-notch seafood restaurants.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Rolls: This local delicacy is a must-try while in Groton. Try them at Abbott’s Lobster in the Rough.

  2. Clam chowder: Take a visit to Go Fish for some of the best clam chowder in town.

  3. Burger: Grab a burger at Gold Star Burgers - serving up some of the best burgers in town.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: Head to Outer Light Brewing Company to explore the local brewing scenes and try a local beer.

  2. Wine: Maugle Sierra Vineyards, located on top of a hill in Ledyard, offers both wine and stunning views of the Connecticut countryside.

Where to Stay in Groton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Groton offers a range of lodging options from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  1. Hilton Mystic: This hotel is located within easy reach of many attractions. The hotel has an on-site restaurant, bar, and outdoor pool.

  2. Fitch Claremont Vineyard B&B: This upscale bed and breakfast offer a unique experience with its on-site vineyard and wine tasting rooms.

  3. Mystic Marriott Hotel & Spa: This luxurious hotel is perfect for leisure and business travelers. It features an on-site spa, restaurant, and lounge.

What to Buy in Groton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Groton has plenty of unique items to take home as souvenirs. Some of the popular options include:

  1. Mystic Knotwork: They make nautical accessories and decorations.

  2. Coastal Gourmet: Famous for its gourmet gift baskets, this local shop offers a range of artisanal foods and products.

  3. Harvest Home Farmers Market: This market showcases locally produced foods and crafts from different artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Groton

The locals in Groton are friendly and welcoming. Being a naval town, residents hold a strong connection with the town’s naval history and are proud of their heritage. It is advisable to dress casually while visiting Groton and be respectful towards the town’s naval heritage and culture.

Best Time to Visit Groton

The best time to visit Groton is during the summer months from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and the town is buzzing with tourists and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Groton

Do:

  • Do explore the maritime history of the town by visiting Mystic Seaport Museum and the Submarine Force Museum.

  • Do dine at the town’s seafood restaurants for the best local cuisine experience.

  • Do take a boat ride to explore the coastal beauty of the town.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces. Keep the town clean and tidy.

  • Don’t disrespect the town’s local culture and customs.

  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen and a water bottle, especially when planning outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Groton

Groton is a safe town to visit with a low crime rate. However, it is advisable to take standard precautions while visiting any new place.

Health in Groton

Groton has access to some top-quality health facilities, including Lawrence + Memorial Hospital and Yale-New Haven Health’s Westerly Hospital.

Getting to Groton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Groton

The closest airport to Groton is Bradley International Airport, which is about 60 miles away. Other options include LaGuardia and JFK International Airport, both located in New York City and approximately 120 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Groton

The town is serviced by Greyhound and Peter Pan bus services that provide connections to New York and Boston.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Groton as the town is accessible through I-95. Rental cars and taxis are available at the airport and bus stations.

How to Get Around in Groton

Groton is a small town, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance. However, visitors can use taxis or rental cars to move around the town.

Where to Go Next from Groton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Groton is ideally situated between Boston and New York – two of the United State’s most iconic cities. Visitors can explore Boston’s historical sites, and New York’s museums, and entertainment scenes.

In addition, several popular Destinations are located within driving distance of Groton, such as Newport, Rhode Island, and Cape Cod, Massachusetts.

Conclusion

Groton is a spectacular blend of maritime history, scenic views, and outdoor activities. We hope that this comprehensive tour guide will aid you in exploring everything that Groton has to offer. From museums to parks, beaches to scenic trails – Groton has something for everyone, making it an ideal vacation destination.
