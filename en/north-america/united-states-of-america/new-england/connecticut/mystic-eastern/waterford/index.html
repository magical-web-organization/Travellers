Introduction to Waterford

Waterford is a small town located in the Mystic-Eastern region of Connecticut, situated in New England, the northeastern region of the United States of America. The town was founded in 1801 and has a rich historical and cultural heritage. Waterford offers a wide range of activities for all types of travelers, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and excellent food scene.

In this guide, we will explore the best things to do in Waterford, places to visit, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Waterford

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Waterford:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Millstone Farm and Vineyard: A local farm producing wine and craft beer, with a tasting room, a retail shop, and a variety of farm-to-table dining options.
  • Governor Samuel Huntington Homestead: A historic home and museum featuring the life and times of Samuel Huntington, the signer of the Declaration of Independence and one of the first governors of Connecticut.
  • Harkness Memorial State Park: A large estate featuring a 19th-century mansion, beautiful gardens, and a public beach on the Long Island Sound.

Natural Attractions of Waterford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Waterford Beach Park: A local beach offering stunning views of the Long Island Sound, swimming, fishing, beach volleyball, and more.
  • Oswegatchie Hills Nature Preserve: A vast preserve with hiking trails, bird-watching opportunities, and many scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Jordan Cove Historic District: A picturesque historic district featuring scenic views of the Jordan Cove and the surrounding marshland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waterford

Waterford has an array of hiking and biking trails:

  • The Great Neck Trail: A beautiful 2.5-mile trail that winds through the woods and along the shores of the Long Island Sound.
  • The Oswegatchie Hills Nature Preserve Trail: A 5.5-mile hiking trail that takes visitors through a variety of ecosystems, waterfalls, and stunning views of the area.
  • The Waterford Heritage Trail: A 3.2-mile multi-use trail that offers biking, hiking, and walking along the scenic historic railway line.

Best Things to Do in Waterford: Activities and Tours

  • Mystic Seaport Museum: A nearby museum that offers a unique look at America’s maritime history with interactive exhibits, costumed interpreters, and historic vessels.
  • Essex Steam Train and Riverboat: A scenic train and riverboat experience through the Connecticut River Valley with breathtaking views.
  • Ocean Beach Park: A local amusement park featuring rides, a boardwalk, food vendors, and a popular public beach on the Long Island Sound.

Week-Long Itinerary for Waterford

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for those visiting Waterford:

Day 1: Arrive and relax at Waterford Beach Park. Day 2: Explore Millstone Farm and Vineyard, followed by Harkness Memorial State Park. Day 3: Visit Governor Samuel Huntington Homestead and Jordan Cove Historic District. Day 4: Take a scenic train ride on the Essex Steam Train and Riverboat. Day 5: Enjoy Mystic Seaport Museum and a food tour of Mystic. Day 6: Go on a hike at the Oswegatchie Hills Nature Preserve Trail. Day 7: Visit Ocean Beach Park and indulge in the local food scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Waterford

Waterford has a fantastic food scene, offering a wide range of options for all types of visitors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A local delicacy with fresh lobster meat served on a toasted roll with melted butter.
  • Clam Chowder: A rich soup made with clams, potatoes, and cream.
  • Steamed Mussels: Freshly caught mussels steamed in a flavorful broth with garlic and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Enjoy local craft beer at the Millstone Farm and Vineyard tasting room.
  • Connecticut Wines: Try wine produced at the Millstone Farm and Vineyard.
  • Local Coffee: Savor a cup of locally roasted coffee at Common Grounds Coffee House.

Where to Stay in Waterford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Waterford has many accommodation options that suit different tastes and budgets, including:

  • Springhill Suites by Marriott Waterford Mystic: A contemporary hotel featuring spacious suites, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.
  • Niantic Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast that provides a laid-back atmosphere, comfortable rooms, and a great breakfast.
  • Wallace Suites: A lovely guesthouse with rustic decor, spacious rooms, and picturesque views.

What to Buy in Waterford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mystic Knotwork: Buy unique handmade sailor bracelets and nautical décor at Mystic Knotwork.
  • Millstone Farm and Vineyard Products: Take home a bottle of locally crafted wine or beer from Millstone Farm and Vineyard.
  • Mystic Seaport Store: Purchase maritime treasures like maps, sailing rope, and books on the history of sailing at Mystic Seaport Store.

Local Culture and Customs of Waterford

Waterford has a rich cultural heritage and celebrates several annual festivals and events such as the Annual Waterford Fair, the Mystic Eats festival, and the Mystic Outdoor Art Festival.

Best Time to Visit Waterford

The best time to visit Waterford is during the shoulder season of spring or fall when the crowds are lesser, and the scenery is magnificent. Summer is also an excellent time to explore the town with plenty of outdoor festivals, activities and it offers beach time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waterford

Here are some helpful Do’s and Don’ts of Waterford:

Do:

  • Visit the beaches and enjoy the sun and water.
  • Explore the town on bikes or by foot.
  • Experience local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches or any other public areas.
  • Be disrespectful towards the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waterford

Waterford is a safe town with low crime rates, and visitors are unlikely to face any safety issues.

Health in Waterford

Waterford has several healthcare facilities, including urgent care centers, hospitals, and specialty clinics.

Getting to Waterford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors traveling to Waterford can use several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Waterford

  • T. F. Green Airport (PVD), Warwick, IS 63 miles away (75 minutes)
  • Bradley International Airport (BDL), Hartford, IS 62 miles away (about 70 minutes)

Bus Stations Near Waterford

  • Greyhound Bus Lines (New London, CT): 5 miles away (13 minutes)
  • Peter Pan (New London, CT): 5 miles away (13 minutes)

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Rent a car at one of the airports to explore the town and nearby attractions.
  • Train Station: Take a train to New London with Amtrak and get a rental car, taxi, or public transportation to Waterford.

How to Get Around in Waterford

The best way to get around Waterford is by car, taxi, or bikes.

Where to Go Next from Waterford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mystic, Connecticut: Just a short drive away and offering wonderful seaside views.
  • Newport, Rhode Island: Another great destination known for its stunning coastal views and architecture.

Conclusion

Waterford is a fascinating and charming destination for visitors to the Mystic-Eastern region of Connecticut. Whether visitors come for the outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or the food and drink scene, Waterford has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has been helpful in planning your visit to this lovely town.
