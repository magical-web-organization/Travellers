Introduction to Putnam

Putnam is a charming town located in the Mystic-Eastern region of Connecticut, United States. Known for its rich history, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning natural beauty, Putnam is a must-visit destination for any traveler staying in Connecticut.

List of Places to Visit in Putnam

If you’re planning a trip to Putnam, here are some of the top attractions and places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Putnam Historical Society and Museum
  • Cargill Falls Mill Site & Park
  • The Bradley Playhouse

Natural Attractions of Putnam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rotary Park
  • Owen Bell Park
  • Harrisville Golf Course

Hiking/Biking Routes of Putnam

Putnam has many beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding natural beauty. Some of the top routes include:

  • Air Line State Park Trail
  • Quinebaug River Trail
  • Putnam River Trail

Best Things to Do in Putnam: Activities and Tours

Putnam has a variety of fun activities and tours for visitors of all ages, including:

  • Exploring the local arts scene at the Spring Street Gallery
  • Visiting the Putnam Farmers’ Market
  • Taking a scenic hot-air balloon ride

Week-Long Itinerary for Putnam

Day 1: Visit the Putnam Farmers’ Market, explore the Putnam Historical Society and Museum, and catch a show at the Bradley Playhouse.

Day 2: Hike the Air Line State Park Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch at Owen Bell Park.

Day 3: Take a hot-air balloon ride and enjoy stunning aerial views of the town.

Day 4: Visit the Cargill Falls Mill Site & Park and enjoy a picnic lunch by the waterfall.

Day 5: Explore the town’s local arts scene and visit the Spring Street Gallery.

Day 6: Play a round of golf at the Harrisville Golf Course.

Day 7: Take a leisurely bike ride along the Quinebaug River Trail and enjoy the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Putnam

Putnam has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the best of Connecticut’s local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam chowder from the Putnam Lodge of Elks No. 574
  • Lobster roll from The Courthouse Bar & Grille
  • Pizza from The Crossings Restaurant & Brew Pub

Local Beverages

  • Beer from The Crossings Restaurant & Brew Pub
  • Wines from the nearby Sharpe Hill Vineyard
  • Cider from B.F. Clyde’s Cider Mill

Local Culture and Customs of Putnam

Putnam is a town with a deep appreciation for arts and culture. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local arts scene and attend community events to get a sense of the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Putnam

The best time to visit Putnam is during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the town hosts its annual Fall Festival. However, the town is beautiful year-round and has something to offer visitors in every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Putnam

Do:

  • Explore the town’s local arts scene
  • Visit the Putnam Farmers’ Market
  • Take a scenic bike ride along the Quinebaug River Trail

Don’t:

  • Engage in littering or other forms of disrespectful behavior
  • Ignore the town’s social calendar and miss out on community events
  • Overlook the local restaurants and cuisine

Safety and Health

Safety in Putnam

Putnam is a safe town for visitors. However, travelers should take basic precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their cars and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Putnam

Putnam has several medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics, for travelers in need of medical attention.

Getting to Putnam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Putnam

The closest airport to Putnam is Bradley International Airport in Windsor Locks, CT. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle to Putnam.

Bus Stations Near Putnam

The closest bus station to Putnam is in Hartford, CT, which is about an hour’s drive away.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Putnam or take a train to the nearby city of Worcester, MA, and rent a car from there.

How to Get Around in Putnam

The best way to get around Putnam is by car or bike. Visitors can also walk to many of the town’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Putnam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Putnam is located in the heart of the Mystic-Eastern region of Connecticut, which has many other attractions and destinations nearby, including:

  • The Mystic Seaport Museum
  • The Mashantucket Pequot Museum and Research Center
  • The Goodwin State Forest

Conclusion

Putnam is a beautiful and charming town that offers something for every type of traveler. Visitors can explore the town’s rich cultural heritage, enjoy stunning natural beauty, and indulge in local cuisine. Whether you’re staying for a weekend or a week, Putnam is sure to leave you with lasting memories and a desire to return.
