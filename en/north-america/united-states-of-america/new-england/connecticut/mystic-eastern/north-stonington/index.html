Introduction to North Stonington

Nestled in the picturesque region of Mystic-Eastern in the state of Connecticut, North Stonington is a charming town that offers adventurers an array of natural attractions, culturally significant landmarks, and exciting outdoor activities. With its rich historic background, this destination provides visitors with a one-of-a-kind experience that will leave them coming back for more. In this guide, we delve into the must-visit places, the best activities to engage in, where to stay, and what to buy when in North Stonington.

List of Places to Visit in North Stonington

As one of the most historically rich towns in Connecticut, North Stonington offers visitors a wide range of museums and historical landmarks to explore, along with some beautiful outdoor spaces to enjoy. Here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jonathan Edwards’ Birthplace

North Stonington carries a wealth of intriguing history, none more so than a visit to Jonathan Edwards’ Birthplace - an hour-long guided tour around the homestead of one of the most prominent names in Religious Studies in American history, where he preached and wrote most of his sermons. You’ll learn about his life and legacy, as well as the colonial lifestyle of the time.

Wheeler Library

The oldest library in Stonington county, Wheeler Library is a historic landmark that was established in 1903. They host exhibitions showing local artists, giving insight into the town community’s creative arts scene. Visitors of all ages are welcome to visit and enjoy the library’s charm.

Natural Attractions of North Stonington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pachaug State Forest

This park covers over 28,000 acres in the eastern part of Connecticut, North Stonington contains some of the forest’s best stations, including Green Fall Pond. The Forest offers an array of activities ranging from mountain biking, hiking, camping, and fishing, making it an indoor activity destination.

Lantern Hill

At an elevation of 491 feet, Lantern Hill is a hiking haven with a stunning view of Fishers Island Sound. The hill features a seven-mile trail where you can enjoy panoramic views of the intricate landscape from the summit. The peak of the hill is a great spot to have a picnic with friends and family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Stonington

Apart from the trails mentioned above on Lantern Hill and Pachaug State Forest, there are bike trails to several possible spots. North Stonington creates such terrain where the land is suitable for a comfortable bike ride. The roads within North Stonington don’t get too busy in terms of traffic, making cycling comfortable.

Best Things to Do in North Stonington: Activities and Tours

The town of North Stonington is a one-stop destination for adventurers who love outdoor activities and those who enjoy learning about history or enjoy the arts. Here are the suggestions for the best things to engage in while you’re here:

Winery Tours and Tastings

North Stonington is home to several vineyards that offer wine tastings and tours, giving visitors an opportunity to sample some of the area’s best wines. Stonington Vineyards is a great option as they grow nine different types of grapes and have an incredible landscape, making for a perfect day trip.

Visit the Jonathan Edwards Winery Tasting Room

Take a moment to enjoy the fruitful wine at the Jonathan Edwards tasting room, located in downtown Mystic.

Ross’ Clam Shack & Marketplace

Take a break from sightseeing and savor the town’s finest seafood at Ross’ Clam Shack and Marketplace. Famous for its lobster rolls, Ross’ Clam Shack provides visitors a classic New England seafood experience with a wharf-style vibe.

For visitors looking to schedule a week-long stay in North Stonington, here’s an ideal itinerary to provide an action and excitement-packed trip:

Food and Drink Scene of North Stonington

North Stonington offers food enthusiasts a range of dining options, coupled with many of Connecticut’s best wines. The local cuisine merges with authentic seafood, giving it a unique flare for foodies. Here’s some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Rolls

One of the traditional and most loved local dishes, the Lobster Roll, is a must-try for anyone experiencing North Stonington. Quick tip: try Ross’ Clam Shack and Marketplace’s famous lobster roll.

Apple Cider Donuts

Visit one of the local orchards and get hand-made Apple Cider Donuts. These mouth-watering snacks are a fan favorite and a sweet way to end an adventure-filled day.

Local Beverages

Stone Acres Farm Sangria

A signature drink at the Stone Acres Farm with a unique blend of white wine, sliced fruit, and a kick of brandy, it’s the perfect refreshment for visitors seeking a good time.

Jonathan Edwards Winery Chardonnay

Visit the winery and try the Chardonnay. It has a buttery flavor, making it the perfect wine to top off your meal at a fine North Stonington restaurant.

Local Culture and Customs of North Stonington

North Stonington is an excellent representation of authentic New England culture. Some of the customs observed here include Remembrance Day, Thanksgiving Day, and the Connecticut Light Fest and Christmas Tree Festival.

Best Time to Visit North Stonington

Summer and fall make the best time to visit North Stonington, as the weather is optimal and all the fantastic outdoor activities are open.

Safety and Health

It is important to keep in mind your safety when exploring a new town. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in North Stonington

North Stonington is a small and safe town with low crime rates. Beachgoers should take necessary precautions when going on long walks, as the sand gets hot during summer months.

Health in North Stonington

It is always important to stay healthy when traveling, especially during a pandemic. Make sure to stay updated on COVID-19 protocols and wear a mask. Most businesses comply with CDD guidelines in North Stonington.

Getting to North Stonington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are various transportation options to North Stonington:

Airports Near North Stonington

Bradley International Airport and T.F. Green Airport are the nearest airports to North Stonington, situated about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near North Stonington

The New London Bus Station is the nearest metrobus station with regular schedules, with a bus ride of about thirty minutes away.

Other Options

Rental cars can be found at nearby airports, or reserve a taxi or ride-share like Uber.

How to Get Around in North Stonington

There are multiple transport options around North Stonington, with some activities being conveniently located in the town; it’s possible to take walking as an option. In instances where driving becomes necessary, public transportation, taxis, or rental vehicles can effectively do so.

Where to Go Next from North Stonington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Stonington provides visitors numerous options on where to visit after spending time in the town. Here are some destinations nearby:

Mystic Aquarium

Located a few miles away in the neighboring town of Mystic, CT, Mystic Aquarium is a perfect spot to visit with family and friends. The aquarium is home to over 300 species of aquatic animals.

Foxwoods Resort Casino

Just a few minutes away, Foxwoods Casino presents a fascinating experience for visitors with a range of table games, video gaming, and entertainment options.
