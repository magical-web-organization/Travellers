Introduction to Storrs

Storrs, located in the heart of Connecticut, is a small, charming town known for its breathtaking natural beauty, rich history, and unique cultural experiences. Home to the University of Connecticut’s flagship campus, Storrs is a vibrant and bustling college town that offers a plethora of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s museums and historical sites, taking in the scenic views, or enjoying outdoor adventures like hiking and biking, Storrs has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Storrs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • University of Connecticut Museum of Natural History - This museum houses collections of dinosaurs, rocks and minerals, and biological specimens from around the world.
  • Ballard Institute and Museum of Puppetry - The museum is dedicated to the preservation and promotion of puppetry as an art form.
  • Mansfield Historical Society Museum - The museum showcases the history of Mansfield and the surrounding area and features exhibits on local industries, including textiles and agriculture.
  • Gilbert H. Hood Museum of Art - This museum houses a diverse collection of American and European art from the ancient to contemporary periods.

Natural Attractions of Storrs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spring Valley Trails - Spring Valley Trails is a 3.5-mile trail system that gives hikers and bikers access to forests and fields on the edge of campus.
  • Mansfield Hollow State Park - The park offers a range of activities including hiking trails, fishing, mountain biking, horseback riding, and boating.
  • Fenton River - The Fenton River Trail is a 1.2-mile trail that follows the river through a forested area.
  • UConn Forest - The UConn Forest features 2,200 acres of rolling hills, rocky ridges, and forests with over 30 miles of multi-use trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Storrs

Storrs is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in Connecticut, with scenic routes that are perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Here are some of the popular trails to check out:

  • Spring Valley Trails: 3.5 miles
  • East Brook Trail Loop: 3.5 miles
  • Fenton River Trail: 1.2 miles
  • Horsebarn Hill Loop: 2.5 miles
  • Nipmuck Trail: 12.5 miles

Day 1: Explore the Uconn Campus and visit the Ballard Institute and Museum of Puppetry. Day 2: Visit the Mansfield Historical Society Museum and hike the Spring Valley Trails. Day 3: Land a catch or enjoy a picnic at Mansfield Hollow State Park. Day 4: Take a bike tour along the Fenton River Trail and enjoy lunch at a local brewery. Day 5: Go on a scenic drive through the countryside and visit the Gilbert H. Hood Museum of Art. Day 6: Visit the UConn Forest and hike the Nipmuck Trail.
