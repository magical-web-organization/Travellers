Introduction to Stonington

Located in the charming Mystic-Eastern region of Connecticut, Stonington is a picturesque coastal town with a rich cultural history and scenic views. With its intimate size and historic architecture, Stonington is a charming destination that offers endless opportunities to explore and enjoy the great outdoors. Whether you’re an art lover, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Stonington has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Stonington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stonington is home to several culturally significant attractions that offer a glimpse into its fascinating history. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • The Old Lighthouse Museum: Built in the 19th century, this iconic lighthouse stands as a testament to Stonington’s maritime history. Visitors can climb up the tower to enjoy panoramic views of the ocean.

  • Stonington Historical Society: The society’s museum houses a collection of artifacts and memorabilia that bring the town’s past to life. Visitors can learn about the town’s whaling history and the lives of its past inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of Stonington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stonington’s stunning natural surroundings make it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for a hike, a bike ride, or a leisurely stroll, the town has plenty to offer. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Barn Island Wildlife Management Area: Covering more than 1,000 acres of marshland and forest, this area is a haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers. The park has several hiking trails and kayaking opportunities.

  • Stonington Borough: With its picturesque harbor and intimate size, the Stonington Borough is the perfect place to stroll around and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stonington

Stonington is home to several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the town’s natural beauty. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • The Stonington Borough trail: Beginning at Stonington Point, this 1.5-mile trail takes walkers along the scenic harbor and through the borough’s charming streets.

  • Lantern Hill Trail: This 3-mile trail is perfect for hiking and takes you to the top of Lantern Hill, from where you can enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Stonington: Activities and Tours

Stonington offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Mystic Seaport Museum: Located nearby, this fascinating museum offers visitors a glimpse into the life of 19th-century sailors. You can take a tour of the museum’s historic ships, watch a maritime demonstration, or explore the exhibits and galleries.

  • Saltwater Farm Vineyard: This winery is a charming place to relax and enjoy a glass of wine amidst beautiful surroundings. The venue also hosts live music events and yoga classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stonington

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Stonington, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Stonington and explore the Stonington Borough.

Day 2: Visit the Old Lighthouse Museum and take a stroll around Stonington Point.

Day 3: Go hiking at Barn Island Wildlife Management Area followed by a picnic lunch.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Mystic and visit Mystic Seaport Museum.

Day 5: Explore the art galleries and boutiques in downtown Stonington.

Day 6: Go wine-tasting at Saltwater Farm Vineyard.

Day 7: Relax and soak up the sun at Stonington’s beautiful beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Stonington

Stonington has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers everything from seafood to farm-to-table cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll: Stonington is famous for its delicious, freshly caught seafood, and the lobster roll is a must-try dish.

  • Clam chowder: Another classic New England seafood dish to try is the clam chowder served in many of the town’s restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Whaler’s Brewing Company: This microbrewery serves up a variety of locally crafted beers.

  • Stonington Vineyards: The winery produces a range of award-winning wines that can be enjoyed on-site or brought home as a souvenir.

Where to Stay in Stonington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stonington offers several accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Inn at Stonington: This historic inn overlooks the harbor and offers luxurious accommodations and a spa.

  • The Whaler’s Inn: This charming hotel is located in the heart of downtown Stonington and boasts cozy guest rooms.

What to Buy in Stonington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring back home, Stonington has plenty to offer. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • Stonington-themed merchandise: You can find a range of souvenirs, such as t-shirts, hats, and mugs, that are adorned with the town’s name and imagery.

  • Local honey: Stonington has several local honey producers that offer unique and delicious varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Stonington

Stonington has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, galleries, and museums. The town is home to several artists and creative professionals, and visitors can experience this vibrant local culture through various events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Stonington

The best time to visit Stonington is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. The fall season (September-November) is also a beautiful time to visit, as the autumn colors make the town even more picturesque.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stonington

Do:

  • Visit the Old Lighthouse Museum for breathtaking views of the ocean.

  • Go hiking at Barn Island Wildlife Management Area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the local wildlife.

  • Don’t swim in areas where it is prohibited.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stonington

Stonington is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always wise to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Stonington

Stonington has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, where travelers can seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Stonington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stonington

The closest airports to Stonington are T. F. Green Airport in Providence, Rhode Island (45 miles away) and Bradley International Airport in Windsor Locks, Connecticut (61 miles away).

Bus Stations Near Stonington

The nearest bus stations are in nearby New London and Mystic, which are both served by Greyhound and Peter Pan.

Other Options

For those who prefer to drive, Stonington is located just off of Interstate 95.

How to Get Around in Stonington

Stonington’s small size means that it’s easy to get around on foot or by bike. The town also has several taxi and car rental services for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Stonington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stonington’s proximity to several other charming New England towns makes it a great starting point for more exploration. Some nearby destinations worth exploring include Mystic, Newport, and Boston.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an adventure-packed trip, Stonington has something to offer. This charming coastal town is perfect for those who want to experience the best of New England’s natural beauty, culture, and history. With its outdoor adventures, delicious food and drink scene, and a vibrant cultural landscape, Stonington is a truly unforgettable destination.
