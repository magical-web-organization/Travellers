Introduction to Union

Union is a small town located in Mystic-Eastern, Connecticut, in the New England region of the United States of America. Although it is one of the smallest towns in Connecticut, Union is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Union, including the best attractions, things to do, where to stay, and travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Union

Below is a list of the top attractions and places to visit in Union:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bigelow Brook Farm
  • Union Agricultural Society Fairgrounds

Natural Attractions of Union: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mashapaug Pond
  • Nipmuck State Forest

Hiking/Biking Routes of Union

Union offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Connecticut. Below are some of the most popular trails:

  • Nipmuck Trail
  • Shenipsit Trail

Best Things to Do in Union: Activities and Tours

Union offers a variety of activities and tours for all ages. Below are some of the best things to do in Union:

  • Pick your own fruits and vegetables at Bigelow Brook Farm
  • Attend the Union Agricultural Society Fair
  • Canoeing, kayaking or fishing on Mashapaug Pond

Week-Long Itinerary for Union

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Union:

Day 1 Explore Mashapaug Pond and hike the Nipmuck Trail
Day 2 Visit the Bigelow Brook Farm and pick some fresh fruits
Day 3 Go on a scenic drive to nearby attractions such as the Roseland Cottage in Woodstock, and Mark Twain House in Hartford
Day 4 Canoe or kayak on Mashapaug Pond
Day 5 Attend the Union Agricultural Society Fair
Day 6 Hike the Shenipsit Trail
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of Union

Where to Stay in Union: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Union has limited options for accommodation, with no hotels or hostels in the town. However, there are a few guesthouses and inns in the nearby towns of Woodstock and Storrs that offer a comfortable stay. Some of the popular options include:

  • Inn at Woodstock Hill
  • Nathan Hale Inn

What to Buy in Union: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Union is known for its agricultural products, including fresh fruits and vegetables. Visitors can also purchase locally made handicrafts and souvenirs from the Union Agricultural Society Fair. Some popular souvenirs include:

  • Maple syrup
  • Farm-fresh fruits and vegetables
  • Handcrafted leather goods

Local Culture and Customs of Union

Union has a rich agricultural heritage that has been preserved and celebrated throughout the years. The town is known for its annual agricultural fair, which showcases the best of Union’s farming community. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions while visiting Union.

Best Time to Visit Union

The best time to visit Union is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities. Fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit, with stunning foliage and annual agricultural fair held in September.

Safety and Health

Safety in Union

Union is generally a safe town, with very low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take standard precautions, such as locking their car doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Union

There are no major health concerns in Union. However, visitors are advised to carry insect repellent during the summer months to protect against ticks and mosquitoes.

Getting to Union: Transportation Options and Logistics

Union is a small town in Connecticut and is best accessed by car. Below are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Union

  • Bradley International Airport (BDL) - Located about 40 miles south of Union

Bus Stations Near Union

  • Willimantic Bus Station - Located about 15 miles east of Union

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available at Bradley International Airport.

How to Get Around in Union

Union is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding areas.

Conclusion

Union is a small but culturally rich town in Connecticut, with a strong agricultural heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, there is something for everyone in Union. So, plan your trip, pack your bags, and get ready to explore all that Union has to offer.
