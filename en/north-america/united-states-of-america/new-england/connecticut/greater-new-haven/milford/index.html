Introduction to Milford

Milford, located in Greater New Haven, Connecticut, is a charming coastal town boasting beautiful scenery, rich history, and a thriving community. Dating back to 1639, Milford is one of the oldest towns in the United States and has played a significant role in the region’s development. Today, visitors can explore landmarks, museums, outdoor adventures, and a vibrant food scene. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best attractions, activities, food, accommodations, and travel tips to help you plan your next trip to Milford.

List of Places to Visit in Milford

Milford has a plethora of things to see and do, from cultural and historical landmarks, to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here is a list of must-visit places in Milford:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Milford Historical Society: This museum offers a glimpse into Milford’s past through exhibits, collections, and artifacts.
  • The Mary Taylor Memorial United Methodist Church: The church dates back to 1828 and is a popular attraction for its iconic steeple and stained-glass windows.
  • The Museum of Fine Arts: This museum showcases fine arts, pottery, sculpture, and paintings from various periods in history.

Natural Attractions of Milford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Silver Sands State Park: This state park boasts a scenic beach, boardwalks, and trails, along with opportunities for bird watching, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Charles E. Wheeler Salt Marsh and Wildlife Management Area: This wildlife reserve is home to numerous bird species and offers visitors the chance to experience the serene beauty of the marshland.
  • Wadsworth State Park: This park features hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the Connecticut River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milford

Milford has numerous hiking and biking trails, offering visitors the chance to experience the natural beauty of the area. Here are a few of the best routes to explore:

  • The Farm River State Park Trail: This trail is 16 miles long and runs from East Haven to North Branford, taking visitors through diverse landscapes and beautiful sights.
  • The Gulf Beach Boardwalk: This boardwalk is a popular spot for walking, jogging, and biking, offering views of the Long Island Sound.
  • The Housatonic Rail Trail: This 3.5-mile trail runs from the New Haven border to the Walnut Beach area of Milford, with stunning views of the Housatonic River.

Best Things to Do in Milford: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Milford has something to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Kayaking Tours: Take a kayak tour with one of the local outfitters to explore the scenic views of the Long Island Sound.
  • Historical Tours: Join a walking tour to learn more about Milford’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: Visit the local vineyards for a wine tasting tour that showcases the area’s unique terroir.

Food and Drink Scene of Milford

Milford has a diverse food scene that showcases the area’s seafood, as well as other local and international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A New England specialty, the lobster roll is a must-try when visiting Milford.
  • Clam Chowder: This creamy soup is a local favorite, made with fresh clams and potatoes.
  • Pizza: Milford is known for its pizza, with many local pizzerias serving up classic pies.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Milford has a thriving beer scene, with many local breweries offering unique brews.
  • Wine: Visit the local vineyards and try some of the area’s delicious wines.

Where to Stay in Milford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Milford has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets, from bed and breakfasts to luxury hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Milford: This hotel is located in a prime location near the beach and offers a fitness center, indoor pool, and a restaurant.
  • Hyatt Place Milford: This hotel features spacious rooms, a 24-hour fitness center, and a coffee/cocktail bar.
  • The Queen’s Accents: This bed and breakfast is a charming option for those looking for a cozy and comfortable stay in Milford.

What to Buy in Milford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Milford has a range of locally made products and souvenirs that are perfect for taking home as a reminder of your trip. Some of the best options include:

  • Artisanal Soaps: Made with natural ingredients, these soaps are a popular choice among visitors.
  • Local Art: Visit the local galleries and shops to purchase unique works of art.
  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Milford is known for its abundance of sea glass, which is often made into beautiful jewelry by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Milford

Milford has a rich cultural history, shaped by its colonial roots, maritime heritage, and artistic community. Visitors should note that the town values its local traditions and encourages respect for the area’s landmarks and natural resources.

Best Time to Visit Milford

The best time to visit Milford is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the beach scene is lively. However, visitors can enjoy Milford’s attractions year-round, with the fall and spring seasons offering mild weather and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milford

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Milford:

Do:

  • Explore Milford’s cultural and natural landmarks, including its museums, parks, and beaches.
  • Try the local seafood and other specialties, such as pizza and fine wines.
  • Respect the area’s cultural heritage and landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural resources of Milford, including its beaches, parks, and conservation areas.
  • Disrespect the area’s cultural heritage and landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Milford

Milford is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions while exploring the area. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Lock your car and belongings while at the beach or other public areas.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone after dark.
  • Follow the lifeguards’ instructions while at the beach, and be cautious of rip currents and other hazards.

Health in Milford

Milford has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, for emergencies or health concerns. Visitors should also take precautions to protect themselves from sunburn and dehydration while enjoying the outdoor activities.

Getting to Milford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Milford is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Milford

  • Bradley International Airport: Located in Windsor Locks, Connecticut, Bradley International Airport is the closest airport to Milford, at 70 miles away.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: JFK Airport is located in New York, approximately 58 miles from Milford.

Bus Stations Near Milford

  • Milford Transit: The local bus system offers connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Train: Take the Metro-North train to the Milford station, which connects to New York City and other nearby towns.
  • Car: Milford is located near major highways, such as Interstate 95 and Route 15.

How to Get Around in Milford

Milford is a walkable town, with many attractions located within a short distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bikes or take public transportation to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Milford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Milford is located near many popular destinations in Connecticut and New England, making it a great base for exploring the area. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • New Haven: Located just 12 miles away, New Haven is home to Yale University, museums, theaters, and a bustling food scene.
  • Hammonasset Beach State Park: This state park is located 30 minutes away in Madison and offers a beautiful beach, trails, and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Mystic Seaport: This maritime museum is located 40 miles away in Mystic, Connecticut, and offers visitors the chance to explore historic ships and learn about maritime history.

Conclusion

Milford, Connecticut, offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural experiences. From its scenic beaches to its charming town center, Milford’s attractions have something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, arts and culture, or delicious cuisine, Milford is a must-visit destination.
