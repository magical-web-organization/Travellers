Introduction to East Haven

Located in Greater New Haven, East Haven is a small town in Connecticut, known for its historic buildings, stunning natural landscapes, and friendly locals. The town is easily accessible and located just a few miles east of New Haven, making it an ideal destination for a quick getaway or a longer vacation.

List of Places to Visit in East Haven

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pardee-Morris House: This historic home was built in 1750 and is now open to the public as a museum. Visitors can learn about the history of East Haven and the daily life of colonial families.

  • The Shore Line Trolley Museum: Located in nearby Branford, the Shore Line Trolley Museum features vintage trolleys, engines, and other forms of mass transit from the early 20th century.

  • The Yale University Art Gallery: This world-famous art museum is located in nearby New Haven and contains over 200,000 works of art from all over the world, including ancient art, contemporary art, and everything in between.

Natural Attractions of East Haven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lighthouse Point Park: This public park is located on the Long Island Sound and offers visitors a sandy beach, picnic areas, swimming, fishing, bird watching, and hiking trails.

  • East Haven Town Beach: A great place to enjoy a day of sun and sand, this beach is located in the heart of East Haven and offers everything from swimming to kayaking to beach volleyball.

  • Farm River State Park: Located on the east side of East Haven, this park offers hiking trails, fishing, and amazing views of the lower Connecticut River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Haven

  • The East Haven Greenway: This scenic trail runs for 1.5 miles along an old railroad bed and offers stunning views of Long Island Sound and the surrounding natural landscape.

  • The Farm River Trail: This trail starts in Farm River State Park and runs for two miles through beautiful woodland and along the Farm River.

  • The Quinnipiac River Trail: Starting in nearby New Haven, this trail follows the Quinnipiac River for over 14 miles and offers a great opportunity to see wildlife and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in East Haven: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the historic Pardee-Morris House: A great way to learn about the history of the area and the daily life of colonial families.

  • Visit local farms: East Haven is home to several local farms, including the Bishop’s Orchards Farm Market, which is known for its fresh-picked produce and homemade baked goods.

  • Go fishing: There are many great spots to fish in East Haven, including the Farm River, the shoreline, and several local lakes.

Food and Drink Scene of East Haven

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza: East Haven is known for its delicious and authentic pizza. Try a slice at Pepe’s Pizza or Modern Apizza.

  • Lobster Roll: Fresh and delicious lobster rolls can be found at several local restaurants, including Lobster Shack and Lenny’s Indian Head Inn.

  • Clam Chowder: Also known as “New England Clam Chowder”, this creamy soup is a must-try when visiting the area.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Visit Gouveia Vineyards in nearby Wallingford for a taste of local wines.

  • Beer: Try the local ale at Overshores Brewing or Thimble Island Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in East Haven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The East Haven Beach House: Enjoy stunning views of the Long Island Sound from this beautiful beachfront property.

  • The Red Roof Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to all the attractions in East Haven.

  • The Lighthouse Point Bed and Breakfast: Stay in a charming 19th-century lighthouse keeper’s home while enjoying the beauty of Lighthouse Point Park.

What to Buy in East Haven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fresh-picked produce: Visit a local farm market to purchase fresh fruits and vegetables.

  • Lobster and seafood: Bring home some fresh seafood from one of the local seafood markets.

Local Culture and Customs of East Haven

East Haven is a small town with a strong sense of community. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors will feel at home in no time. East Haven has a rich history, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the area’s past and appreciate the town’s unique culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit East Haven

The best time to visit East Haven is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the beauty of the area during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Haven

Do:

  • Respect the area’s natural environment and wildlife.
  • Take the time to learn about the area’s history and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local landmarks or cultural customs.
  • Be excessively loud or disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Haven

East Haven is a relatively safe area, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in East Haven

Visitors should be aware of the risk of tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease, which are common in the area. Visitors should take precautions such as wearing long-sleeved shirts and pants, using insect repellent, and checking for ticks after spending time outdoors.

Getting to East Haven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Haven

The closest airports to East Haven are:

  • Bradley International Airport: Located in Windsor Locks, Connecticut, about 53 miles northwest of East Haven.
  • Tweed New Haven Regional Airport: Located in New Haven, Connecticut, about 3 miles west of East Haven.

Bus Stations Near East Haven

Several bus companies serve the New Haven area, including:

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Peter Pan Bus Lines
  • Megabus

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to East Haven using their personal vehicles or rental cars. The town is easily accessible from several major highways, including I-95 and I-91.

How to Get Around in East Haven

The best way to get around East Haven is by car. Visitors can also use taxis or ride-sharing services such as Uber or Lyft to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from East Haven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Haven: This nearby city is home to Yale University, world-class museums, and great restaurants.

  • Hartford: The capital of Connecticut, Hartford is known for its historic landmarks, beautiful parks, and vibrant arts scene.

  • Martha’s Vineyard: This scenic island off the coast of Massachusetts is a popular destination for beach-goers, foodies, and wine-lovers.

Conclusion

East Haven is a beautiful and welcoming town that offers something for everyone. From stunning natural landscapes to rich cultural heritage, visitors to East Haven will leave with memories that will last a lifetime.
