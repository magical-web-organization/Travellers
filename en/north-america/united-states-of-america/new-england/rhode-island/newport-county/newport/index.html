Introduction to Newport

Newport, located in Newport County, Rhode Island, is a charming coastal city that offers a perfect blend of history, nature, and culture. Founded in 1639, Newport is famous for its stunning architecture, historical landmarks, natural beauty, and lively entertainment scene. The city is known for hosting the America’s Cup and is a cultural hub. Visitors can enjoy beautiful beaches, scenic views, and outdoor adventures, along with tasting local cuisine, visiting museums and mansions, and immersing themselves in the local culture. In this guide, we’ve rounded up the best things to do and see in Newport, along with tips on where to stay, what to eat and buy, and how to stay safe and healthy during your trip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newport

Newport is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to stay safe. Here are some safety tips:

  • Keep valuables out of sight in public spaces
  • Do not leave belongings unattended on the beach or parks
  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas
  • Use life jackets and safety equipment while boating or sailing
  • Be aware of rip currents and dangerous surf conditions at beaches

Health in Newport

Newport has good healthcare facilities, including hospitals and medical centers. Visitors should take necessary precautions to stay healthy, including frequent hand washing, staying hydrated, and using sunscreen.
