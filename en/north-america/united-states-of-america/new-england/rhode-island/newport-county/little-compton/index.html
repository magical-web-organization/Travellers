Little Compton is a charming, picturesque town nestled in Newport County, Rhode Island. It is renowned for its natural beauty and colonial architecture, making it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a slice of history and adventure. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you plan your perfect vacation in Little Compton with suggestions on the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay and eat.

Introduction to Little Compton

Located on the southeastern coast of Rhode Island, Little Compton is a rural community dotted with rolling hills, pristine beaches, and fertile farmlands. With a population of fewer than 4,000 people, it has remained relatively untouched by tourism and retains its charm and character.

List of Places to Visit in Little Compton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wilbor House Museum: This colonial-era house, built-in 1692, documents the town’s history and features exhibits on early American life.
  2. Sakonnet Point Lighthouse: Standing tall since 1884, the lighthouse is a popular spot for photography and offers stunning views of the Sakonnet River and the Atlantic Ocean.
  3. Little Compton Community Center: A former gristmill that has been transformed into a cultural center that hosts exhibitions, performances, and events.

Natural Attractions of Little Compton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Goosewing Beach Preserve: A pristine beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and birdwatching.
  2. Simmons Mill Pond Management Area: an 842-acre forested preserve, featuring a pond, hiking trails and wildlife watching opportunities.
  3. South Shore Beach: a quiet, uncrowded, and family-friendly beach that is perfect for windsurfing, kayaking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Compton

For those who are looking for an adventure, Little Compton offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular include:

  1. Wilbur Woods: A 45-acre forest with hiking trails that wind through the woods and wetlands, and offer scenic views of the Sakonnet River.
  2. Tiverton Four Corners: A 6-mile paved bike path that passes through the serene countryside of Tiverton and Little Compton.

Best Things to Do in Little Compton: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a historic walking tour of the town and admire the colonial architecture and landmarks.
  2. Visit FarmCoast New England, a community initiative that highlights the region’s rich agricultural history.
  3. Take a sunset sail on the bay, and enjoy a romantic evening under the stars.
  4. Attend the Summer Concert series at the Sakonnet Vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Little Compton

Day 1-2: Explore the town by taking a historic walking tour, visit the Wilbor House Museum and Little Compton Community Center.

Day 3-4: Spend your days soaking up the sun and swimming at Goosewing Beach Preserve and South Shore Beach.

Day 5-6: Take an adventure and hike or bike through Simmons Mill Pond Management Area and Wilbur Woods.

Day 7: Visit the Sakonnet Point Lighthouse and enjoy a sunset sail on the bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Little Compton

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Rolls: Filled with fresh lobster meat and served with a side of fries and coleslaw, the lobster rolls are a local delicacy.
  2. Clam Chowder: A creamy soup made with chopped clams, potatoes, and seasonings that will warm your soul.

Local Beverages

  1. Newport Vineyards: A family-owned vineyard that offers wine tasting and tours.
  2. The Sakonnet Vineyards: A scenic winery that also offers excellent cheese and meat platters.

Where to Stay in Little Compton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Stone House: A historic inn with elegant rooms and fantastic waterfront views.
  2. Point Pleasant Inn & Resort: A charming bed & breakfast that exudes the comfort and warmth of a family home.

What to Buy in Little Compton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Little Compton Honey: Locally sourced and undeniably sweet, this honey is a must-buy.
  2. Handcrafted Gifts: Visit the many farmers’ markets in the area to find organic soaps, maple syrup, and handcrafted pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Little Compton

Locals in Little Compton take pride in their agricultural heritage and farming culture. As you walk through the town, you’ll see small family farms, roadside stands selling fresh produce, and the famous FarmCoast Market. It’s essential to respect the area’s agricultural traditions, and local customs, such as waving to passing cars and saying thank-you to farmers.

Best Time to Visit Little Compton

The best time to visit Little Compton is from June through September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are open for swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Little Compton

Do:

  1. Say “hello” to locals and strike up a conversation.
  2. Visit the farmers’ markets and buy fresh local produce.
  3. Respect nature and wildlife when hiking or biking through the forests or along the beaches.

Don’t:

  1. Litter on beaches or hiking trails.
  2. Ignore road safety measures, as most roads in Little Compton are narrow, with hidden driveways.
  3. Trespass on private property, as the area is dotted with farms and farmland.

Safety and Health

Safety in Little Compton

Little Compton is a safe and friendly town, with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

Health in Little Compton

Little Compton residents have access to excellent medical care, with several hospitals and clinics located within a short distance. Visitors are advised to ensure they have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Little Compton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Little Compton

The closest airport to Little Compton is T.F. Green Airport, located about 35 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Little Compton

Greyhound Bus Lines and Peter Pan Bus Lines operate services to Providence, where visitors can take a local bus or taxi to Little Compton.

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Little Compton by rental car or private taxi.

How to Get Around in Little Compton

The best way to get around Little Compton is by car, which allows visitors to explore every corner of the town and the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Little Compton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Newport: A historic city with mansions, beaches, and shopping, located less than an hour’s drive from Little Compton.
  2. Block Island: A scenic island with beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and historic landmarks, located just a short ferry ride away.

Conclusion

Little Compton is a hidden gem in the heart of Rhode Island, offering visitors an authentic experience of colonial America and scenic natural beauty. Plan your ideal vacation with our comprehensive guide, and discover everything this charming town has to offer.
