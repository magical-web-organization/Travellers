Introduction to Tiverton

Located in Newport County, Rhode Island, Tiverton is a beautiful coastal town nestled between the Sakonnet River and Mount Hope Bay. With its rich history, natural beauty, and diverse culture, Tiverton has become a popular destination for tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Tiverton

From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there are plenty of places to visit and things to do in Tiverton.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Four Corners Arts Center: Located in the heart of downtown Tiverton, this art center features exceptional exhibitions and events all year round.
  2. The Little Compton Historical Society: Learn about Tiverton’s rich history and heritage, including the area’s connections to the Revolutionary War.
  3. The Tiverton Historical Society Museum: Featuring exhibits that focus on the town’s early settlement to the 19th century textile industry.

Natural Attractions of Tiverton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Weetamoo Woods and Pardon Gray Preserve: Take a hike or bike ride amid the tranquil forests and wetlands of these preserves that offer breathtaking views of the Sakonnet River.
  2. The Sakonnet River: Take a boat ride or try your hand at fishing in this majestic river with stunning views.
  3. The Grinnell’s Beach: Enjoy some sunbathing, swimming, and beach games at this lovely beach on Mount Hope Bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tiverton

Tiverton has great hiking and biking trails, which take visitors through the beautiful, natural terrain. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • The Tiverton Trail: This 5.5-mile trail is ideal for seasoned hikers. The route takes you through the lush Weetamoo Woods and offers spectacular views of the Sakonnet River.
  • The Pardon Gray Preserve Loop Trail: This scenic 1.5-mile trail offers splendid views of the Sakonnet River and the Pardon Gray Preserve’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Tiverton: Activities and Tours

  1. Tiverton Farmers Market: Visit the farmers’ market, which takes place on Saturdays, and sample some of the finest produce from local farmers.
  2. Coastal Roasters: Stop by Coastal Roasters for a cup of artisan coffee and delicious baked goods.
  3. Boat tours on the Sakonnet River: Enjoy a leisurely boat tour around the Bay to witness incredible views of the natural surroundings.
  4. Kayaking on the Sakonnet River: Rent a kayak and paddle your way through the serene waters of the Sakonnet River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tiverton

Day 1: Visit the Tiverton Historical Society Museum and learn about Tiverton’s rich history and heritage.

Day 2: Take a bike ride through the Pardon Gray Preserve and take in the Natural Beauty of the area.

Day 3: Explore Weetamoo Woods and enjoy a natural escape unlike any other.

Day 4: Go fishing in the Sakonnet River and discover the many species of fish that call this river home.

Day 5: Visit the Coastal Roasters and relax with a cup of coffee while admiring the view around the Bay.

Day 6: Take a boat tour around the Sakonnet River and witness the stunning natural beauty.

Day 7: Visit Grinnell’s Beach for some beach relaxation, swimming, and tanning.

Food and Drink Scene of Tiverton

Tiverton is home to a variety of restaurants that serve a diverse range of dishes, as well as local beverages and famous Rhode Island seafood.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stuffies: A delicious clam chowder delicacy stuffed with breadcrumbs, onions, celery and peppers.
  2. Lobster bisque: An undeniable Lighthouse seafood must-try dish filled with lobster meat for an excellent flavor.
  3. Grilled shrimp skewers: A healthy seafood dish grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: Visit the local breweries for the popular Newport Storm beer and other craft brews.
  2. Wine: Discover locally-made fine wines that match your palate when you embark on vineyard tours in the area.

Where to Stay in Tiverton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Stone House: A cozy and elegant hotel with a wonderful view of the Bay.
  2. The Atlantic House: Offers guests a warm and welcoming experience, with world-class serenity and comfort.
  3. Borden Brook Reservoir: A peaceful and serene guest house close to the natural wonders of Tiverton.

What to Buy in Tiverton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. The Olive Oil Shop: Browse the Olive Oil Shop for specialty olive oils and balsamic vinegar flavors that you cannot find anywhere else.
  2. The Cheese Wheel: Cheese lovers can indulge in a vast array of artisanal cheese flavors at the Cheese Wheel that would create a pleasing taste on your taste buds.

Local Culture and Customs of Tiverton

Visitors to Tiverton, Rhode Island, should be aware of its rich history and cultural diversity. Tiverton has a close-knit community who pride themselves on the town’s history and heritage. Local residents are hospitable, friendly, and willing to help visitors with any queries to make them feel at home.

Best Time to Visit Tiverton

Peak season for tourism in Tiverton is during summer from June to August, when visitors can indulge in the beach life and bask in the sun rays. The fall season, September to November, is the perfect time to visit for the picturesque foliage views, and mild weather for outdoor activities. During winter, the weather is chilly and quiet in Tiverton, and skiing is a popular activity if snowfall is good.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tiverton

Do:

  • Do take a boat tour on the Sakonnet River and enjoy the stunning natural scenery.
  • Do join a hiking or biking trail for a full experience of Tiverton’s natural terrain.
  • Do try the locally produced beer and fine wine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t overlook Tiverton’s history and heritage when exploring its natural beauty and outdoor activities offerings.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen, bug spray, and other outdoor essentials for daytime outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tiverton

Tiverton is a safe town overall. Take precautions such as locking car doors and windows and not leaving valuables in your car, especially in parking lots.

Health in Tiverton

Health risks in Tiverton are minimal. The town has an excellent healthcare system with top-rated hospitals within driving distance.

Getting to Tiverton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tiverton

Visitors can fly to T. F. Green Airport, just a 30-minute drive from Tiverton. Boston’s Logan International Airport is another option since Tiverton is a 90-mile drive from Boston.

Bus Stations Near Tiverton

There are many bus stations in Newport, a 15-minute drive from Tiverton.

Other Transportation Options

A car rental can be an excellent option for those who prefer a comfortable trip with multiple stops and detours.

How to Get Around in Tiverton

The best mode of transportation in Tiverton is driving. Rental cars can be hired, or visitors can use their personal cars, if any.

Where to Go Next from Tiverton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tiverton’s neighboring towns, Little Compton, and Portsmouth, have many attractions, such as natural preserves and farms to explore that make for great day trip options.

Conclusion

Tiverton is a must-visit destination for tourists seeking outdoor activities, natural beauty and serene seascapes, rich history, cultural diversity, and great food and drink scenes. Plan a trip today to explore all that Tiverton and Rhode Island offers!
