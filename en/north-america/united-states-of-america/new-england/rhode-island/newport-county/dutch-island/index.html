The Ultimate Guide to Dutch Island: A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Dutch Island is a small 100-acre island in Rhode Island that offers tourists an unparalleled opportunity to enjoy nature and learn about its history. Located in the heart of Narragansett Bay, Dutch Island is home to historical landmarks and a rich culture that has influenced the region since the 17th century. In this guide, we’ll take you through all you need to know about the island, from where to go and what to see to where to stay and how to get there.

Introduction to Dutch Island

Dutch Island is a part of Newport County in Rhode Island, in New England, in the United States of America, in North America. The island was named after Dutch explorer Adriaen Block who first visited the island in 1614. The island played an important role in the American Revolutionary War, serving as a strategic defense point against the British. Today, it is a popular spot for tourists visiting Rhode Island.

List of Places to Visit in Dutch Island

There are several must-visit places on Dutch Island that offer a glimpse into its history and natural beauty. Here are some of the top attractions on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Greble: Fort Greble was established in 1863 and was used as a coastal defense post during the Civil War. Today, visitors can tour the fort and learn about its history.
  • Dutch Island Lighthouse: The Dutch Island Lighthouse is an important historical landmark that has stood on the island since 1826. Visitors can climb the lighthouse and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Dutch Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wildlife Viewing: Dutch Island is home to a variety of wildlife including osprey, deer, and bald eagles. Visitors can enjoy wildlife viewing on hikes and walks around the island.
  • Beaches: Dutch Island has several beaches where visitors can relax and enjoy views of the coasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dutch Island

Dutch Island offers several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s natural beauty. The trails range from easy to moderate difficulty, making them accessible to visitors of all ages and fitness levels. Some of the top hiking and biking trails on the island include:

  • The Dutch Island Loop Trail: This hiking trail is approximately four miles long and takes visitors around the entire island. It offers stunning views of the surrounding areas and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • The Beach Walk: The Beach Walk is a one-mile flat walk along the coast that offers visitors stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Dutch Island: Activities and Tours

Dutch Island offers several exciting activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some of the top things to do on the island include:

  • Guided Tours: Visitors can enjoy guided tours of the island’s historical landmarks and natural attractions.
  • Fishing: Dutch Island is a popular spot for recreational fishing, with visitors angling for striped bass, bluefish, and blackfish.
  • Kayaking: Visitors can rent a kayak and explore the island’s calm and tranquil waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dutch Island

To make the most of your time on Dutch Island, we have a specially curated week-long itinerary for you. Here’s what your week could look like on the island:

Day 1: Arrive on Dutch Island, settle into your accommodation, and take a stroll along the beaches.

Day 2: Enjoy a guided tour of the Dutch Island Lighthouse and the Fort Greble Historical Site.

Day 3: Take a hike on the Dutch Island Trail Loop, and enjoy stunning views of the island and the surrounding areas.

Day 4: Spend a day fishing around the island, and try your hand at catching striped bass, bluefish, and blackfish.

Day 5: Rent a kayak and explore the calm and tranquil waters around the island.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Block Island and enjoy scenic views of the coast and the Atlantic Ocean.

Day 7: Relax on the beaches and take one final walk around the island before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Dutch Island

Dutch Island offers visitors some delicious food and drink options, allowing them to indulge in the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: Rhode Island is famous for its clam chowder, and visitors to Dutch Island can savor this delicacy in most of the local eateries.
  • Lobster Rolls: The lobster rolls on Dutch Island are a must-try for seafood lovers. They’re loaded with fresh lobster meat and served in warm brioche buns.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Rhode Island has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors to Dutch Island can indulge in the local brews.
  • Coffee Milk: Coffee milk is Rhode Island’s official state drink, and visitors to Dutch Island can enjoy this sweet drink in most local eateries.

Where to Stay in Dutch Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dutch Island offers several accommodation options for visitors, depending on their budget and preferences. Here are some top accommodation options on the island:

  • Dutch Island Cottage: This historic cottage offers visitors a chance to stay on the island, with stunning views of the surrounding areas.
  • Hostelling International: Hostelling International is a popular choice for budget travelers. The hostel offers dorm-style accommodation and shared bathrooms.
  • Newport Beach Hotel and Suites: This luxurious hotel is situated in nearby Newport and offers visitors a chance to indulge in luxury accommodation.

What to Buy in Dutch Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dutch Island offers visitors several unique souvenirs and local products to take home. Here are some top items to buy in Dutch Island:

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Visitors can buy locally made pottery that showcases the island’s unique designs and aesthetics.
  • Quahog Shells: Quahog shells are a popular souvenir from Rhode Island. Visitors can find these shells in most local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Dutch Island

Rhode Island has a rich culture that is reflected in its food, art, and lifestyle. Visitors to Dutch Island can experience this culture through the island’s festivals, events, and local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Dutch Island

The best time to visit Dutch Island is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and pleasant. Visitors can enjoy the beaches, hikes, and outdoor activities during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dutch Island

Safety and Health

Safety in Dutch Island

Dutch Island is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take precautions when hiking or swimming around the island.

Health in Dutch Island

Rhode Island has several medical facilities that offer quality healthcare services to visitors. Visitors are also advised to carry adequate medical insurance before traveling to the island.

Getting to Dutch Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dutch Island is only accessible by boat. Visitors can take a ferry from nearby Newport or Jamestown to the island. Private boat rentals are also available for those who prefer to travel on their own.

Airports Near Dutch Island

T.F. Green Airport in Providence is the closest airport to Dutch Island, located approximately 26 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Dutch Island

The closest bus station to Dutch Island is in Newport, approximately 3 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Dutch Island by private boat or water taxi.

How to Get Around in Dutch Island

Visitors can explore the island on foot or rent a bike or kayak to explore the area on their own.
