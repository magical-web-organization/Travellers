Introduction to Portsmouth

Located in Newport County, Rhode Island, Portsmouth is a small town on the island of Aquidneck. Steeped in history dating back to the 1600s, Portsmouth offers a glimpse into the early days of the United States. Visitors can explore the town’s many historical sites, museums, and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Portsmouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coggeshall Farm Museum: Experience what life was like on an early 18th-century farm by touring this living history museum.
  • The Portsmouth Historical Society Museum: Learn about the town’s history and see a collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  • The Green Animals Topiary Garden: See a whimsical collection of topiary figures in a beautiful garden setting.
  • Glen Manor House: Take a guided tour of this magnificent French-style chateau and learn about its past.

Natural Attractions of Portsmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sakonnet Greenway Trail: Hike or bike through the scenic countryside on this 10-mile multi-use trail.
  • Sandy Point Beach: Enjoy swimming, kayaking, or fishing at this beautiful coastal beach.
  • The Norman Bird Sanctuary: Hike through the acres of protected land and see a variety of plants, animals, and birds.
  • The Mount Hope Bay Viewpoint: Drive to this scenic viewpoint for panoramic views of the bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portsmouth

Portsmouth offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the town. Some popular routes include:

  • The Sakonnet Greenway Trail (10 miles)
  • The Miantonomi Park trail (1.5 miles)
  • The Portsmouth Park trail (2 miles)

Best Things to Do in Portsmouth: Activities and Tours

  • Island Tour of Newport and Portsmouth: Take a guided tour of both Newport and Portsmouth to see the best sights of both towns.
  • Kayaking Adventure: Rent a kayak and explore the Sakonnet River.
  • Wine Tasting at Greenvale Vineyards: Sip wine and enjoy live music at this scenic vineyard.
  • Ghost Tours of Newport and Portsmouth: Take a spooky night-time tour and hear stories of haunted buildings and ghost sightings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Portsmouth

Day 1: Explore Portsmouth Historical Society Museum and Coggeshall Farm Museum Day 2: Take a hike on the Sakonnet Greenway Trail Day 3: Visit The Green Animals Topiary Garden and enjoy a picnic lunch Day 4: Spend the day at Sandy Point Beach Day 5: Take a guided tour of Glen Manor House Day 6: Rent a kayak and explore the Sakonnet River Day 7: Visit The Norman Bird Sanctuary

Food and Drink Scene of Portsmouth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Try a classic New England delicacy by indulging in a fresh lobster roll.
  • Clam Chowder: Sample a traditional bowl of creamy clam chowder, perfect for a chilly day.
  • Quahogs: Rhode Island’s official shellfish offer a deliciously briny taste.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer: Enjoy a cold brew from Rhode Island’s famous beer company.
  • Newport Vineyards: Visit this local vineyard for a wine tasting and scenic views.

Where to Stay in Portsmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gurney’s Resort: This waterfront resort features luxurious rooms, a spa, and excellent dining options.
  • The Vanderbilt Grace: Stay in a beautiful mansion-turned-hotel complete with a rooftop bar and modern amenities.
  • The Newport Beach Hotel & Suites: Enjoy oceanfront views and easy access to nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Portsmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rhode Island Souvenirs: Pick up a t-shirt or mug with the state’s iconic anchor emblem or flag.
  • Fresh Seafood: Bring home some of the town’s fresh local seafood, such as lobster or quahogs.

Local Culture and Customs of Portsmouth

Known for its maritime history and strong ties to the sea, Portsmouth celebrates its local culture and customs with events like the Newport International Boat Show and the Newport Kite Festival. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local community by attending these events and participating in traditional activities like clam bakes or sailing excursions.

Best Time to Visit Portsmouth

The best time to visit Portsmouth is in the summer months of June, July, and August when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the fall foliage in September and October or the holiday celebrations in December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portsmouth

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and wine.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Portsmouth by taking a hike on one of the town’s many trails.
  • Do visit the town’s historical museums and attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t overlook the town’s natural attractions in favor of its historical sites.
  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to try a traditional lobster roll from a local restaurant.

Safety and Health

Safety in Portsmouth

Portsmouth is generally a safe town, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially when visiting natural areas like beaches or trails. Keep valuables out of sight and lock all car doors.

Health in Portsmouth

There are several medical centers and hospitals in the Newport area, including Newport Hospital and St. Anne’s Hospital. Bring any necessary medications and plan for emergency medical care if needed.

Getting to Portsmouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Portsmouth

The closest major airport to Portsmouth is T.F. Green Airport in Providence, Rhode Island, which is about 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Portsmouth

The nearest bus station is in Newport, Rhode Island, which is about 10 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Portsmouth

Visitors can easily get around Portsmouth by car or bicycle. There is also a local bus system, the Rhode Island Public Transit Authority (RIPTA), which offers service throughout Aquidneck Island.

Where to Go Next from Portsmouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newport, Rhode Island: Just a short drive away, Newport offers a variety of attractions, including the famous Newport Mansions and stunning ocean views.
  • Providence, Rhode Island: The state’s capital city is just 45 minutes away by car and offers a thriving culinary and arts scene.
  • Fall River, Massachusetts: Located just across the waterway, Fall River offers a variety of historic and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Portsmouth, Rhode Island, offers a unique blend of natural beauty and historical charm. From hiking and kayaking to exploring museums and historical sites, visitors will find plenty to see and do in this charming coastal town. And with nearby attractions like Newport and Providence, there’s always something new to discover.
