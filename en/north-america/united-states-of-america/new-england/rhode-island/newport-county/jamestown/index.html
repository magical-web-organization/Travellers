Introduction to Jamestown

Nestled in Newport County, Rhode Island, Jamestown is a charming coastal town that boasts of natural beauty, historical charm, and lively culture. Known for its stunning beaches, picturesque lighthouses, and delicious food, Jamestown offers an unforgettable travel experience to all its visitors. Whether you are a history buff, adventure seeker or a foodie, Jamestown has got something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Jamestown

Here’s a list of places that you must visit during your trip to Jamestown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beavertail Lighthouse Museum: This iconic lighthouse is located at the southernmost tip of Jamestown and offers sweeping views of the ocean.
  • Jamestown Windmill: Built in 1787, this historic windmill is an impressive structure that offers a glimpse into the town’s agricultural past.
  • The Newport Bridge: Take a walk on this iconic bridge that connects Jamestown with Newport and offers stunning views of Narragansett Bay.
  • Watson Farm: This 265-acre farm showcases the agrarian history of Jamestown and offers scenic views of the island and the bay.

Natural Attractions of Jamestown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beavertail State Park: This 153-acre park boasts rocky beaches, hiking trails, and fishing spots that offer breathtaking views of the ocean and Rhode Island.
  • Fort Getty Park: This popular recreational spot is perfect for picnics, camping, and scenic walks.
  • Dutch Harbor Boat Yard: This working boat yard is ideal for those who love to watch boats getting built, renovated or repaired.
  • Mackerel Cove: This picturesque beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the stunning views of the bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jamestown

Jamestown offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to experience the natural beauty of the town.

  • Fort Wetherill State Park: This 61-acre park offers several hiking trails that lead to the fort and the beach.
  • Dutch Island: This historic island offers miles of hiking trails that offer stunning views of the bay.
  • Jamestown Reservoir: This serene lake is perfect for a quiet walk or a peaceful bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Jamestown: Activities and Tours

Jamestown offers several activities and tours that allow visitors to experience the best of the town.

  • Discover Jamestown Tour: This tour offers a guided walk through the town’s historic neighborhoods and landmarks.
  • Newport Harbor Cruise: Take a scenic cruise from Jamestown to Newport and enjoy the stunning views of Newport’s harbor.
  • Fishing Charters: Jamestown is home to several fishing charters that offer visitors a chance to catch some of the freshest seafood in Rhode Island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jamestown

Here’s a week-long itinerary that includes some of the best places to visit in Jamestown:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Beavertail Lighthouse Museum and Beavertail State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Jamestown

Jamestown offers a delicious food and drink scene that is sure to satisfy any foodie’s palate.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stuffies: This local favorite is a stuffed quahog that is packed with clams, breadcrumbs, and spices.
  • Lobster Rolls: Jamestown is famous for its freshly caught lobsters that are served in a warm, buttery roll.
  • Chowder: This creamy and rich clam chowder is a local specialty that is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer: This local beer is a Rhode Island classic that is perfect for a sunny afternoon.
  • Newport Vineyards Wine: This award-winning vineyard offers delicious wines that are made from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Jamestown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jamestown offers several accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences.

  • Conanicut Marina & Suites: This waterfront hotel offers spacious rooms with stunning views of the bay.
  • Jamestown Fish: This renovated historic building offers charming guest rooms that are located above a local seafood restaurant.
  • Serenity Inn: This bed and breakfast is located in a tranquil setting that offers peace and relaxation.

What to Buy in Jamestown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home souvenirs or local products from Jamestown, then here are a few things to consider:

  • Jamestown Windmill Souvenir: Bring home a souvenir from Jamestown’s iconic windmill that is located in the town’s center.
  • Local Seafood: Pick up some fresh seafood from one of the local seafood markets and enjoy it at home.
  • Local Art: Jamestown is home to several local artists who offer beautiful paintings, sculptures, and crafts that are inspired by the town’s natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Jamestown

Jamestown is a tight-knit community that values its cultural heritage and natural resources. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s history, culture, and environment.

Best Time to Visit Jamestown

The best time to visit Jamestown is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jamestown

Here are a few things to consider so you can make the most of your visit to Jamestown:

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history, culture, and environment.
  • Enjoy the local seafood and drinks.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the town’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s land or property.
  • Ignore the town’s cultural traditions and customs.
  • Disturb the local wildlife or marine ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jamestown

Jamestown is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Jamestown

Visitors to Jamestown should ensure that they have proper health insurance and take necessary precautions, such as using sunblock to protect themselves from the sun and keeping themselves hydrated during the hot summer months.

Getting to Jamestown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jamestown can be reached by air, bus, ferry, or car.

Airports Near Jamestown

  • T.F. Green Airport, Providence, Rhode Island (PVD) (25 miles)
  • Logan International Airport, Boston, Massachusetts (BOS) (75 miles)

Bus Stations Near Jamestown

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines (8 miles)

Other Options

  • Ferry: The Jamestown Newport Ferry operates from Newport to Jamestown and offers stunning views of the bay.
  • Road: Jamestown can be reached via Route 138, which connects Newport and mainland Rhode Island.

How to Get Around in Jamestown

Jamestown is a walkable town with several biking and hiking trails. Visitors can also use local taxis, shuttle buses, or rental cars to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Jamestown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can consider:

  • Newport, Rhode Island: This historic coastal town is known for its stunning mansions, beautiful beaches, and lively culture.
  • Providence, Rhode Island: This vibrant city is known for its vibrant arts scene, delicious food, and rich history.
  • Block Island, Rhode Island: This enchanting island is famous for its stunning scenery, unique wildlife, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Jamestown is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and lively culture. From exploring historical landmarks to enjoying delicious seafood and wine, Jamestown has got something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the best of Jamestown, Rhode Island.
