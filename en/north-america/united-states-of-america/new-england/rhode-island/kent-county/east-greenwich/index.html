Introduction to East Greenwich

Located in Kent County, Rhode Island, East Greenwich is a small coastal town with a rich history and picturesque scenery. It is situated in the heart of New England and is known for its beautiful beaches, natural parks, and cultural attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide, you will find everything you need to know to plan your trip to East Greenwich.

List of Places to Visit in East Greenwich

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Varnum Armory Museum

Located in the heart of East Greenwich, the Varnum Armory Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. This museum features exhibits on the history of the Rhode Island militia and offers guided tours of the historic armory.

Greenwich Odeum

The Greenwich Odeum is a beautifully restored theater and performing arts center in East Greenwich. The venue hosts a wide variety of cultural events, including live music, theater productions, and film screenings.

Natural Attractions of East Greenwich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Goddard State Park

Goddard State Park is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves the outdoors. This beautiful park features hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, swimming beaches, and a golf course. It also offers stunning views of the Narragansett Bay.

East Greenwich Town Beach

East Greenwich Town Beach is a popular destination for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. The beach features a concession stand, restrooms, and plenty of parking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Greenwich

Goddard Memorial State Park

Goddard Memorial State Park is a great place for hiking, biking, and exploring the great outdoors. The park features miles of trails that wind through the forest and offer breathtaking views of the bay.

East Coast Greenway

The East Coast Greenway is a 3,000-mile trail that runs from Maine to Florida, passing through East Greenwich along the way. This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in East Greenwich: Activities and Tours

Sailing Tours

Take a sailing tour of the Narragansett Bay and explore the beautiful coastline of Rhode Island. Several companies offer sailing tours from East Greenwich, and most tours include a scenic tour of the bay, a chance to see local wildlife, and the opportunity to learn about the region’s history.

Fishing Charters

Charter a fishing boat and try your luck at catching some of the region’s famous seafood. Fishing charters are available from several companies in East Greenwich, and most tours include all the necessary equipment and instruction.

Food and Drink Scene of East Greenwich

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Roll

East Greenwich is known for its delicious seafood, and a must-try dish is the classic New England lobster roll. Many restaurants in the area serve up their own version of this iconic dish.

Fried Clams

Another East Greenwich classic is the fried clam. This seafood dish is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

Newport Vineyards

Newport Vineyards is a popular destination for wine lovers in the East Greenwich area. The vineyard offers tours and tastings of their award-winning wines.

Whalers Brewing Company

For beer enthusiasts, Whalers Brewing Company is a must-visit destination. This local brewery offers tours and tastings of their craft beers.

Where to Stay in East Greenwich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greenwich Bay Inn

The Greenwich Bay Inn is a cozy and affordable option for visitors to East Greenwich. The hotel features comfortable rooms with all the amenities you need for a relaxing stay.

The Vangeline House Bed and Breakfast

For a more charming and intimate stay, consider the Vangeline House Bed and Breakfast. This historic B&B features cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in East Greenwich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olive Oil

East Greenwich is known for its high-quality, locally produced olive oil. Several shops in the area offer samples and bottles for purchase.

Handmade Crafts

The downtown area of East Greenwich is home to several boutique shops that sell locally made crafts and souvenirs. These make for great gifts for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of East Greenwich

East Greenwich has a strong sense of community and is known for its charming, small-town feel. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions and should be willing to engage with the friendly locals.

Best Time to Visit East Greenwich

The best time to visit East Greenwich is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. The town is bustling with activity during this time, and there are plenty of outdoor events and festivals to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Greenwich

Do:

  • Explore the historic downtown area of East Greenwich
  • Take a sailing tour of the Narragansett Bay
  • Try the local seafood

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions
  • Leave trash or litter on the beaches or in public areas
  • Miss out on the stunning natural scenery of the area

Safety and Health

Safety in East Greenwich

East Greenwich is a safe and welcoming community, but as with any destination, visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety. This includes being aware of your surroundings and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in East Greenwich

There are several hospitals and medical facilities located in and around East Greenwich, making it easy to find quality healthcare if needed.

Getting to East Greenwich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Greenwich

The nearest major airport to East Greenwich is T.F. Green Airport, located just 15 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near East Greenwich

Several bus stations are located in and around East Greenwich, making it easy to travel to and from the town by bus.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors to East Greenwich, as this gives you the freedom to explore the area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in East Greenwich

The downtown area of East Greenwich is easily walkable, but visitors who want to explore further afield can rent a bike or a car.

Where to Go Next from East Greenwich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newport

Just a short drive from East Greenwich is the historic town of Newport, famous for its mansions, waterfront views, and charming downtown area.

Providence

The capital city of Rhode Island, Providence, is located just a short drive from East Greenwich. This vibrant city is known for its arts and culture scene, historic landmarks, and delicious food.

Conclusion

East Greenwich is a beautiful and charming destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, the outdoors, or just good food and drink, you’re sure to find something to love in this picturesque New England town. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of East Greenwich.
