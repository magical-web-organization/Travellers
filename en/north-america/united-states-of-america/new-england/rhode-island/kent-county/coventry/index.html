Introduction to Coventry

Coventry is a small, peaceful town located in Kent County, Rhode Island, in the United States of America. It is situated in the heart of New England, surrounded by pristine natural beauty and boasting a slow-paced way of life that appeals to travelers seeking a retreat from the hustle and bustle of city living.

Coventry’s historic downtown area is home to numerous shops, restaurants, and cafes, offering visitors a chance to indulge in local food and drink, as well as explore local culture and traditions. The town is also a great place to get outdoors, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or just looking for a quiet getaway, Coventry is the perfect destination for your next trip.

List of Places to Visit in Coventry

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nathanael Greene Homestead: The Nathanael Greene Homestead is a historic house museum that was home to General Nathanael Greene, one of George Washington’s most trusted generals. Visitors can take guided tours of the house and grounds, as well as learn about Greene’s life and legacy.
  • Coventry Center Historic District: The Coventry Center Historic District is a picturesque neighborhood that features several historic buildings, including the Old Coventry Town Hall and the General Rice House. Visitors can explore the area on foot and learn about Coventry’s history.
  • Museum of Primitive Art and Culture: The Museum of Primitive Art and Culture showcases the art and artifacts of indigenous cultures from around the world. Visitors can view exhibits on everything from Oceania to Native American cultures.

Natural Attractions of Coventry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pachaug State Forest: Pachaug State Forest is a massive natural area with extensive hiking and mountain biking trails. Visitors can also enjoy fishing, boating, and camping in the forest.
  • Big River Management Area: The Big River Management Area is a popular destination for hiking, birdwatching, and fishing. The area is renowned for its scenic vistas and abundant wildlife.
  • Frosty Drew Observatory: The Frosty Drew Observatory is a popular astronomical observation site that offers visitors an opportunity to view the night sky through telescopes. The site also offers educational programs and lectures on astronomy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coventry

  • Washington Secondary Bike Path: The Washington Secondary Bike Path is a 19-mile long biking and hiking trail that passes through Coventry and several other towns in Rhode Island. The trail is a great way to explore the natural beauty of the area and get some exercise.
  • Tillinghast Pond Management Area: Tillinghast Pond Management Area is a popular destination for hiking, fishing, and birdwatching. Visitors can explore the area on foot or by bike using the numerous trails that crisscross the preserve.

Best Things to Do in Coventry: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on Johnson’s Pond: Johnson’s Pond is a beautiful freshwater lake that is perfect for kayaking and canoeing. Visitors can rent kayaks and other paddlecraft from several outfitters around the lake.
  • Horseback Riding with Dame Farm and Orchard: Dame Farm and Orchard offers visitors a chance to explore the area on horseback. The farm offers guided trail rides, lessons, and other equestrian activities.
  • Haunted History Tours: Coventry is said to be one of the most haunted towns in New England, with a rich history of paranormal activity. Several tour companies offer guided ghost tours of the town, highlighting some of its most famous and eerie sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Coventry

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rhode Island Clam Chowder: This hearty soup is made with fresh clams, potatoes, onions, and a creamy broth. It’s a classic New England dish that you shouldn’t miss.
  • Stuffies: Stuffies are baked stuffed clams that are a Rhode Island specialty. They are typically made with a mixture of chopped clams, breadcrumbs, herbs, and spices, and they are a must-try if you’re a seafood fan.
  • Coffee Milk: Coffee milk is Rhode Island’s official state drink, and it’s made by mixing sweetened coffee syrup with milk. It’s a delicious and unique way to enjoy coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer: Narragansett Beer is a classic New England brewery that has been in business since 1890. Their flagship beer, Narragansett Lager, is a crisp, refreshing brew that’s perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Dave’s Coffee Syrup: Dave’s Coffee Syrup is a locally-made coffee syrup that is used to make coffee milk. It’s made from freshly roasted coffee beans and sweetened with natural ingredients.

Where to Stay in Coventry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Stagecoach House Inn: The Stagecoach House Inn is a charming bed and breakfast located in Coventry’s historic downtown area. The inn features six individually decorated rooms, each with its own unique personality.
  • The Break Hotel at Narragansett Beach: The Break Hotel is a boutique hotel located in nearby Narragansett. The hotel features stylish rooms and a rooftop terrace with stunning ocean views.
  • Campgrounds in the Area: There are several campgrounds located within a short drive of Coventry, including the Burlingame State Park Campground and the Whispering Pines Campground.

What to Buy in Coventry: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rhode Island Sea Glass Jewelry: Rhode Island Sea Glass Jewelry is made from naturally tumbled sea glass and is a unique and beautiful souvenir to bring home.
  • Forgotten Farms Cheese: Forgotten Farms Cheese is a small-batch cheese made from locally-sourced milk. The cheese is aged for several months, resulting in a rich and flavorful product.
  • Crazy Cakes: Crazy Cakes are a local pastry made from buttery shortbread, baked with sprinkles and chocolate chips, and then topped with a sweet glaze.

Local Culture and Customs of Coventry

Coventry is a friendly, welcoming community that values its small-town way of life. Visitors should observe local customs, such as saying “hello” to strangers and showing respect for the natural beauty of the area. Coventry residents take great pride in their heritage, and visitors should take the time to learn about the town’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Coventry

The best time to visit Coventry is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural beauty of the area is at its peak. However, visitors should be aware that this is also the peak tourist season, and the town can be crowded at this time. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, as the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coventry

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Do sample local food and drink.
  • Do learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to locals or their culture.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coventry

Coventry is a safe town, but visitors should still take basic precautions to ensure their safety. It’s a good idea to lock your car and keep valuables out of sight, especially when parking in public areas. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Coventry

Coventry has several medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. If you require medical attention during your stay, seek assistance from one of these facilities.

How to Get Around in Coventry

The best way to get around Coventry is by car. While there is a limited public bus service in the area, having your own vehicle gives you more freedom to explore the natural beauty of the area. If you don’t have your own transportation, car rentals are available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Coventry: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newport, Rhode Island: Located just over an hour’s drive from Coventry, Newport is a historic seaside town known for its beautiful mansions and stunning ocean views.
  • Block Island, Rhode Island: Block Island is a small, picturesque island off the coast of Rhode Island that is a popular destination for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and boating.
  • Providence, Rhode Island: Providence is Rhode Island’s capital city and a cultural hub featuring museums, art galleries, and historic architecture.

Conclusion

Coventry, Rhode Island is a picturesque and peaceful town that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of New England and learn about its rich history and culture. Whether you’re looking to get outdoors, sample local food and drink, or just enjoy some peace and quiet, Coventry is the perfect destination for your next trip.
