A Comprehensive Travel Guide to West Warwick, Rhode Island

West Warwick is a small town located in Kent County, Rhode Island. It is known for its historic landmarks, picturesque countryside, and charming hospitality. In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your visit to this hidden gem, including the must-visit places, local cuisine, accommodation options, and safety tips.

Introduction to West Warwick

West Warwick was established in the late 18th century as part of Warwick, Rhode Island. The town has a rich history, and its artifacts and landmarks are still visible today. Most of these landmarks date back to the Revolutionary War era, and they tell the stories of the town’s brave history. West Warwick’s surroundings are decorated with rustic buildings and farmlands, making it an ideal destination for those looking for peace and tranquility.

List of Places to Visit in West Warwick

Here are the top places to visit in West Warwick:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arctic Village & Museum: The museum showcases the history of the Arctic, including the Native American and Inuit cultures. Live part of their reality by living in an igloo.
  • Pawtuxet Valley Preservation and Historical Society Museum: This museum is located in the center of the town and highlights the town’s historic past.
  • The Knight Estate: A large Colonial Revival home with 80 acres of lush grounds, which is a hotspot for weddings and events.
  • Clouds Hill Victorian House Museum: This Victorian house museum features the life and history of a Providence family.

Natural Attractions of West Warwick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Riverpoint Conservation Area: A great place to explore a myriad of trails both by walking and biking, the conservation area offers varied terrain.
  • Phenix Pond: A great place for fishing and boating.
  • Crow’s Nest Nature Preserve: Bird watchers and hiking enthusiasts adore the natural beauty of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Warwick

West Warwick has numerous hiking and biking paths throughout 20 parks and public spaces. Here are some of the most noteworthy spots:

  • Trestle Trail: The trail is an excellent option for a bike ride, as well as for walking and jogging.
  • Washington Secondary Bike Path: This path connects Coventry and Cranston, making it perfect for a longer cycling trip.
  • Burkhardt Park: This park offers a 1.4-mile round-trip loop, perfect for walking or running.

Best Things to Do in West Warwick: Activities and Tours

  • Escape the Room Games: A fun and challenging group activity.
  • The Boombox Babes Nightclub: Experience West Warwick’s nightlife at this trendy club.
  • Rustic Drive-In: The drive-in movie theater is a fun, nostalgic experience for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Warwick

Day 1: Arrival in West Warwick

Day 2: Guided historical tour of the town and the Knight Estate.

Day 3: Visit Clouds Hill Victorian House Museum and go on a nature walk in the Riverpoint Conservation Area.

Day 4: Spend the day fishing and boating in Phenix Pond.

Day 5: Take a bike ride along the Washington Secondary Bike Path and try an escape the room game.

Day 6: Go to a movie at the Rustic Drive-In.

Day 7: Depart from West Warwick.

Food and Drink Scene of West Warwick

West Warwick is not a large town, but it has a diverse food scene. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza strips: A taste of Rhode Island’s distinct cuisine, these strips are delicious long slices of pizza topped with tomato sauce (not cheese!).
  • Clam Cakes: A Rhode Island treat, these fried balls of dough are filled with clams.
  • Stuffies: Another tried-and-true Rhode Island dish, this is a baked quahog, a type of clam, mixed with breadcrumbs and spices.

Local Beverages

West Warwick has no shortage of local breweries and wine bars. Some options include:

  • Proclamation Ale Company: A popular local microbrewery, known for its beer-making process and durability.
  • JTT Winery: This family-owned winery is located in the countryside and offers wine tastings and tours of its property.

Where to Stay in West Warwick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Warwick has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities, including a pool and a gym.
  • Pleasant View Inn: With ocean views and historic architecture, this inn is perfect for a comfortable and beautiful stay.
  • NYLO Hotel Warwick: This chic hotel has a stylish vibe and comfortable accommodations to make your stay memorable.

What to Buy in West Warwick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenir options from West Warwick:

  • Bags of Coffee: The coffee from West Warwick’s The Coffee Exchange is world-renowned.
  • Quahog shells: A beautiful shell that makes a great decoration or ashtray (traditionally in Rhode Island culture).
  • Local crafts: Artisans in West Warwick sell local crafts and products in shops.

Local Culture and Customs of West Warwick

The people of West Warwick are friendly and hospitable. It’s advised to show respect to the local customs and be good guests. Rhode Island is well known for breaded seafood and hot dogs!

Best Time to Visit West Warwick

The best time to visit West Warwick is from May to September when the weather is warm. Fall is a great time to visit West Warwick to witness the changing colors of the leaves. The winter months can be quite cold and snowy, but some great winter activities can also be done.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Warwick

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, like clam cakes and stuffed quahogs.
  • Visit the museums to learn about the town’s history.
  • Take advantage of the beautiful natural scenery and go hiking or cycling.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind when visiting the parks and wildlife preserves.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Warwick

West Warwick is a safe town, with low crime rate. It is still advised to take the regular safety precautions like securing your valuables and being mindful of surroundings.

Health in West Warwick

It is safe to drink tap water in West Warwick and the town has several quality medical facilities. It’s advisable to take COVID-19 precautions during the pandemic and adhere to safety protocols.

Getting to West Warwick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some options for getting to West Warwick:

Airports Near West Warwick

  • T. F. Green Airport in Warwick is the nearest major airport.

Bus Stations Near West Warwick

  • Providence Bus Terminal in Providence

Other Options

  • Travel by car or train to Boston (about 1.5 hrs)

How to Get Around in West Warwick

The best way to get around West Warwick is by car or bike. Parking is available, and roads are well maintained. It is advised to follow standard traffic guidelines and wear helmets for cycling.

Where to Go Next from West Warwick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newport: This historic city is located just 25 miles from West Warwick and is packed with mansions, waterfront, dining and shopping opportunities, perfect for a weekend getaway.
  • Providence: The state capital and largest city of Rhode Island is just a short drive from West Warwick and is ideal for exploring history, art, and cuisine.

Conclusion

West Warwick is a hidden gem showcasing an array of historical and natural attractions, local cuisine, and accommodation options in Kent County, Rhode Island. It is a perfect destination for individuals and families looking for a peaceful and relaxed vacation.
