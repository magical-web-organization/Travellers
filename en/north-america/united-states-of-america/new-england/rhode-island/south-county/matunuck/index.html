Introduction to Matunuck

Welcome to Matunuck, a charming coastal town in South County, Rhode Island. This quiet hamlet is known for its pristine beaches, delicious seafood, and relaxing ambiance. As you explore the area, you’ll find a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. This travel guide will take you on an exciting journey through Matunuck and highlight the best things to see and do.

List of Places to Visit in Matunuck

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, there’s plenty to discover in Matunuck. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Sites

  • The Old Mountain Field Cemetery: This historic cemetery dates back to the 1600s and is the final resting place of many early Settlers.

  • Matunuck Oyster Bar: This award-winning restaurant is one of the top seafood spots in Rhode Island. It’s a great place to try fresh local oysters, clams, and lobster.

  • The Contemporary Theater Company: This local theater offers a variety of plays, musicals, and performing arts events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Matunuck: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • East Matunuck State Beach: This beautiful beach is one of the top attractions in Matunuck. With its soft sands and calm waters, it’s ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing.

  • Moonstone Beach: This secluded beach is perfect for nature lovers. Its rocky shore is home to a variety of sea creatures, including crabs, snails, and starfish.

  • Trustom Pond National Wildlife Refuge: This peaceful sanctuary is home to a wide range of bird species and other wildlife. It’s a great place to go hiking, birdwatching, or just enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matunuck

Matunuck is known for its stunning natural landscapes and rugged coastline. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • East Matunuck State Beach Trail: This scenic trail offers stunning ocean views and winds through sandy dunes and coastal vegetation.

  • Narrow River Kayak and Canoe Trail: This water trail is perfect for kayaking or canoeing and offers peaceful views of the surrounding marshes and wildlife.

  • Matunuck Hills Trail: This moderate hiking trail is perfect for nature lovers. It winds through forests, fields, and streams and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Matunuck: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting things to do in Matunuck, from outdoor adventures to cultural excursions. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Go Surfing: Matunuck is known for its great surfing conditions and there are several local surf schools that offer lessons for all skill levels.

  • Take a Boat Tour: There are several charter boat companies that offer tours of the local waters. You can see wildlife, lighthouses, and other scenic sights from aboard a boat.

  • Visit a Farmers’ Market: Rhode Island is known for its fresh produce and Matunuck is no exception. There are several local farmer’s markets where you can sample fresh fruits, vegetables, and other local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matunuck

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Matunuck, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrival and Check-in to Accommodations Day 2: Visit the Old Mountain Field Cemetery and Matunuck Oyster Bar Day 3: Spend the Day at East Matunuck State Beach Day 4: Go Surfing in the Morning, Then Explore Trustom Pond National Wildlife Refuge Day 5: Visit the Contemporary Theater Company in the Evening Day 6: Take a Boat Tour and Visit a Local Farmers’ Market Day 7: Departure and Check-out

Food and Drink Scene of Matunuck

Matunuck is known for its delicious seafood and locally-sourced produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Matunuck Oysters: These fresh, local oysters are a must-try for seafood lovers. They’re served raw or cooked and can be found on many local menus.

  • Clam Chowder: This classic New England dish is a local specialty. It’s a creamy soup made with fresh clams, potatoes, and onions and is perfect for a chilly afternoon.

  • Lobster Roll: This iconic sandwich is made with fresh lobster meat, butter, and a toasted hot dog bun. It’s a must-try for any seafood fan.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer: This local beer is a Rhode Island favorite. It’s a light, crisp lager that pairs well with seafood and other local dishes.

  • Del’s Lemonade: This refreshing drink is a Rhode Island staple. It’s made with fresh lemons and sugar and is perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Matunuck: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matunuck offers a variety of accommodation options, from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some top recommendations:

  • The Break: This beachside hotel offers stunning ocean views and is located just steps from East Matunuck State Beach.

  • The Ocean House: This elegant resort is renowned for its luxurious accommodations and fine dining.

  • The Matunuck Beach House: This cozy guesthouse is located just a few minutes’ walk from the beach and offers affordable accommodations for families and couples alike.

What to Buy in Matunuck: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rhode Island is known for its artisan products and local crafts. Here are some top souvenirs to buy in Matunuck:

  • Ceramic Oyster Shells: These beautiful ceramic oyster shells are handcrafted by local artists and make a unique souvenir or gift.

  • Rhode Island Honey: This locally-sourced honey is a sweet treat that can be used in cooking or enjoyed on its own.

  • Sea Glass Jewelry: This beautiful jewelry is made with sea glass that’s been collected from local beaches. It’s a unique and beautiful souvenir that’s perfect for nature lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Matunuck

Matunuck is a laid-back town with a strong sense of community. Here are some local customs and culture to be aware of:

  • Respect the Environment: Matunuck is a natural wonder, and the locals work together to keep it clean and pristine for generations to come. Visitors should respect the environment and not litter or damage natural areas.

  • Support Local Businesses: Matunuck is home to many small businesses that rely on tourism to thrive. Visitors should make an effort to shop local and support the community.

Best Time to Visit Matunuck

The best time to visit Matunuck is in the summer when the weather is warm and the beaches are bustling. However, spring and fall are also excellent times to visit when the crowds are smaller, and the fall foliage is at its peak.

Getting to Matunuck: Transportation Options and Logistics

Matunuck is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Matunuck

  • T. F. Green Airport: This nearby airport is located in Warwick, RI, and offers domestic flights to many US cities.

  • Logan International Airport: This major airport is located in Boston, MA, and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Matunuck

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: This bus company offers service to several major Rhode Island cities, including Providence and Newport.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Matunuck offers several car rental agencies for visitors who prefer to explore the area on their own.

  • Uber/Lyft: These ride-sharing companies offer convenient transportation to and from Matunuck.

How to Get Around in Matunuck

Matunuck is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors can also use bikes or cars to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Matunuck: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Matunuck is located within driving distance of several other popular Rhode Island destinations, including:

  • Newport: This historic town is known for its colonial architecture, yacht racing, and stunning mansions that line the coast.

  • Block Island: This idyllic island is located off the coast of Rhode Island and is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and beaches.

  • Providence: Rhode Island’s capital city is known for its beautiful architecture, art galleries, and culinary scene.

Conclusion
