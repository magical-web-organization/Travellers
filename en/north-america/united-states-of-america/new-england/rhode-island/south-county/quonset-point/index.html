Introduction to Quonset Point

Nestled in South County, Rhode Island, Quonset Point is a charming village known for its scenic beauty and military base. The point derives its name from the bending shape of the land that faces the sea, forming a large cove with a natural harbor. Quonset Point is home to the Quonset Business Park, which attracts businesses from diverse industries.

With a wide range of attractions and activities, Quonset Point has a lot to offer for visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, Quonset Point has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around Quonset Point.

List of Places to Visit in Quonset Point

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Naval War College Museum

The Naval War College Museum is a fascinating museum that traces the history of naval warfare from ancient times to the modern era. The museum showcases naval artifacts, models of ships, historical documents, and artwork that tell the stories of naval warfare.

2. Casey Farm

Casey Farm is a historic farmstead that dates back to the 1700s. The farmstead comprises of a large white house, a barn, and several outbuildings that serve as a museum and educational facility. The farmstead hosts several events throughout the year, including farmers markets and guided tours.

3. Smith’s Castle

Smith’s Castle is a historic site that was built in the mid-1600s. The castle served as a military stronghold during the colonial period and played an important role during the American Revolution. Today, the castle serves as a museum that showcases the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Quonset Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Quonset Point Bike Path

The Quonset Point Bike Path is a scenic trail that winds through the natural beauty of Quonset Point. The trail is ideal for biking, hiking, walking, and jogging.

2. Allen Harbor Yacht Club

The Allen Harbor Yacht Club is a picturesque harbor that offers boating, sailing, and fishing opportunities. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along the harbor and enjoy the scenic views.

3. Davis Memorial Wildlife Refuge

The Davis Memorial Wildlife Refuge is a nature preserve that offers bird-watching, hiking, and picnicking opportunities. The refuge is home to several bird species, including ospreys, great blue herons, and egrets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quonset Point

Quonset Point offers several trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The most popular trails are:

  1. Quonset Point Bike Path
  2. Rocky Point State Park Trail
  3. Oak Hill Trail

These trails offer scenic views of the bay and provide an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Quonset Point.

If you’re planning to spend a week in Quonset Point, here is a sample itinerary for you:

Best Time to Visit Quonset Point

The best time to visit Quonset Point depends on the season. The peak season is from June to September when the weather is warm and dry. However, if you’re looking for a quieter time to visit, the offseason months of April to May or October to November are ideal.
