Introduction to Block Island

Block Island is a hidden gem off the coast of Rhode Island, with a unique appeal that makes it a favorite destination for travelers looking for a laid-back, relaxing vacation. Blanketed with rolling hills, stunning beaches, and scenic views, this is the perfect place for a getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. With a rich history, diverse landscape, and charming community, there’s plenty to explore and discover on Block Island. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a place to unwind and recharge, this island paradise has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Block Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Block Island Historical Society Museum - Learn about the island’s fascinating history, including the Native American settlements, Victorian architecture, and maritime heritage.
  • Southeast Lighthouse Museum - Explore this historical lighthouse, perched high on the Mohegan Bluffs, and enjoy breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Settler’s Rock - Visit this landmark, which represents the first settlers who arrived on the island in 1661, and learn about the island’s early history.
  • Spring House - Take a tour of this elegant Victorian inn, built in 1852, and enjoy a glimpse into the island’s glamorous past.

Natural Attractions of Block Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mohegan Bluffs - Hike down the steep stairs to the beach below, and admire the stunning views of the clay cliffs and ocean.
  • Rodman’s Hollow - Explore this scenic hiking trail, which winds through woodlands and fields, and enjoy views of the island’s diverse ecosystem.
  • Great Salt Pond - Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore this serene saltwater lagoon, filled with coves and secret beaches.
  • Nature Conservancy - Visit this protected nature reserve, home to diverse habitats such as wetlands, grasslands, and ponds, and view endangered species of birds and plants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Block Island

  • Old Harbor Bike Rentals - Rent a bike and explore the island at your own pace, along trails and scenic routes.
  • Greenway Trails - Take a guided hike through these tranquil trails, and experience the island’s natural beauty up close.
  • Clay Head Trail - Hike this trail and enjoy panoramic views of the island’s shoreline and diverse landscape, including beaches, cliffs, and forests.
  • Coastal Loop Trail - Enjoy a relaxing walk along this loop trail, which leads to secluded beaches and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Block Island: Activities and Tours

  • Block Island Parasail & Watersports - Experience the thrill of parasailing, jet skiing, and fishing, and enjoy the island’s stunning coastline from a new perspective.
  • Block Island Bike Tours - Take a guided bike tour and explore the island’s historic landmarks, natural parks, and scenic views.
  • Block Island Food Tours - Discover the island’s culinary delights and rich history, by sampling its local cuisine, and learning about its cultural heritage.
  • Block Island Yoga Collective - Practice yoga and meditation, surrounded by the island’s serene natural beauty and calming ocean waves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Block Island

Day Activity
Monday Explore Old Harbor, visit the Block Island Historical Society Museum, and relax on Fred Benson Town Beach.
Tuesday Take a bike tour of the island, including Rodman’s Hollow, Southeast Lighthouse, and Mohegan Bluffs.

Food and Drink Scene of Block Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll - Enjoy a classic New England dish, featuring a warm, buttery roll stuffed with fresh lobster meat.
  • Stuffies - Sample this Rhode Island specialty, consisting of baked clams stuffed with savory breadcrumbs and spices.
  • Chowder - Savor a creamy bowl of clam chowder, made with fresh clams, potatoes, and spices.
  • Fish and Chips - Indulge in crispy battered fish, served with french fries and tangy tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Ballard’s Beach Bar - Sip a frozen cocktail, while enjoying stunning ocean views and live music.
  • Block Island Winery - Try a local wine, made with grapes grown on the island’s sunny hillsides.
  • Yellow Kittens Tavern and Grill - Relax with a local beer, and sample their famous buffalo wings or seafood dishes.

Where to Stay in Block Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Block Island Beach House - Enjoy luxurious oceanfront accommodations, and an on-site restaurant, bar, and beach club.
  • The Spring House Hotel - Stay in elegant Victorian-style rooms, with ocean views, landscaped gardens, and a salon and spa.
  • Dewey Cottage - Experience cozy, charming accommodations, with a homey feel and close proximity to the island’s attractions.
  • Surf Hotel - Stay in a budget-friendly hotel, located on the island’s main road, and with easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Block Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewelry - Shop for unique pieces made with local sea glass, shells, and stones, by talented artisans on the island.
  • Block Island T-shirts - Bring home a piece of the island, with catchy slogans and cool designs on high-quality T-shirts, sold in various shops across the island.
  • Sachem Soap - Try this all-natural, handmade soap, made with locally sourced ingredients, and enjoy its soothing benefits.
  • Beach Art - Browse galleries and shops for beautiful paintings, photographs, and sculptures inspired by the island’s stunning scenery.

Local Culture and Customs of Block Island

The year-round community of Block Island, Rhode Island, has a rich cultural heritage, with a focus on preserving the island’s natural resources and promoting local businesses and artisans. Islanders are proud of their unique lifestyle, which combines a love of nature, an appreciation of history, and a strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to connect with islanders, show respect for the environment, and support the local economy by buying from small businesses and artisans.

Best Time to Visit Block Island

The best time to visit Block Island is from May through October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most activities and attractions are open. The peak season is from Memorial Day to Labor Day, when the island becomes more crowded, and prices are higher. However, the shoulder seasons of May/June and September/October offer less crowds, lower prices, and beautiful fall foliage. Winter and early spring may experience lower temperatures and fewer amenities, but can be a great time to visit for a peaceful winter retreat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Block Island

Do:

  • Hike or bike the island’s many trails
  • Explore local shops and eateries
  • Support the island’s artists and artisans
  • Take a sunset cruise or fishing trip
  • Visit natural reserves and wildlife habitats

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the island’s natural environment
  • Drive over the speed limit or park in prohibited areas
  • Ignore posted signs or warnings on beaches and trails
  • Disturb local wildlife, including seals and seabirds
  • Be disrespectful or disruptive to locals or fellow travelers

Safety and Health

Safety in Block Island

Block Island is considered a safe destination, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as locking their doors and windows, staying aware of their surroundings, and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Block Island

The island has a medical clinic and ambulance service, located in the town of New Shoreham. Visitors should bring appropriate medications and first-aid supplies, as well as protect themselves from the sun and mosquitoes, with hats, sunscreen, and insect repellent.

Getting to Block Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Block Island is accessible by ferry, helicopter, and private airplane. The Block Island Ferry offers year-round service, departing from Point Judith, Rhode Island, and Newport, Rhode Island, with seasonal service from Montauk, New York. The ferry ride takes approximately an hour, and offers scenic views of Block Island Sound. The island also has a small airport, with daily flights from Westerly State Airport, and charters available upon request. Visitors can also take a helicopter tour of the island, departing from various locations on the mainland.

Airports Near Block Island

  • Westerly State Airport - Located in Westerly, Rhode Island, approximately 30 minutes from Point Judith Ferry Landing, serving commercial and charter flights.

Bus Stations Near Block Island

  • Providence Bus Terminal - Located in downtown Providence, Rhode Island, approximately one hour from Point Judith Ferry Landing, within walking distance from Amtrak and Greyhound stations.
  • New York Port Authority Bus Terminal - Located in midtown Manhattan, approximately four hours from Point Judith Ferry Landing, with daily service to Providence.

Other Options

  • Uber and Lyft - Available on the mainland, but not on the island itself.
  • Taxi Service - Available on the island The island’s roads are fairly easy to navigate, but rentals of bicycles, scooters, and cars available on the island.

How to Get Around in Block Island

Block Island is a pedestrian-friendly destination, and many attractions are easily accessible by foot or bike. However, visitors may also opt to rent a car, scooter, or golf cart, or take a taxi or shuttle bus. The island’s main road, Corn Neck Road, connects most of the island’s attractions and beaches, and offers stunning views of the ocean and countryside.

Where to Go Next from Block Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newport, Rhode Island - Visit this historic seaside city, with its elegant mansions, trendy restaurants, and scenic coastal views, approximately an hour from the island by ferry or car.
  • Providence, Rhode Island - Explore this vibrant, multicultural city, known for its art scene, culinary delights, and history, approximately an hour and a half from the island by ferry or car.
  • Mystic, Connecticut - Discover this charming coastal town, with its famous seaport museum and aquarium, boutique shops, and seafood restaurants, approximately an hour and a half from the island by ferry or car.

Conclusion

Block Island, Rhode Island, is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a unique, relaxing, and enriching vacation experience. From its stunning beaches and natural reserves to its historic landmarks and charming communities, this island paradise has something for everyone. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply pure relaxation, Block Island is the perfect place to escape and unwind.
