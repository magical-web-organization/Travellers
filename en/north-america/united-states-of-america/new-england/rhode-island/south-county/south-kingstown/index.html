South Kingstown Travel Guide: Explore Rhode Island’s Hidden Gem

Are you looking for a laid-back destination in Rhode Island that offers a mix of cultural and outdoor experiences? Look no further than South Kingstown, a charming town located in South County that’s often overlooked by travelers. With its rich history, scenic beaches, lush parks, and vibrant cultural scene, South Kingstown has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, things to do, and local customs to keep in mind when exploring this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in South Kingstown

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or art, South Kingstown has numerous attractions that will delight you. Here’s our list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. South County Museum: This living-history museum showcases the life and traditions of Rhode Island’s coastal communities through interactive exhibits and demonstrations.
  2. Smith’s Castle: This 17th century plantation house-turned-museum offers a glimpse into the life of Rhode Island’s earliest European settlers.
  3. Old Narragansett Church: This picturesque church was built in 1707 and is the oldest Episcopal church in the state. It’s a must-visit for architecture enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of South Kingstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. East Matunuck State Beach: This pristine, mile-long beach is a favorite among locals and visitors for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
  2. Trustom Pond National Wildlife Refuge: This protected area is a popular spot for birdwatching and hiking. It features sandy beaches, salt marshes, and a freshwater pond.
  3. Featherbed Lane Farm Preserve: This 25-acre preserve offers scenic hiking trails, fishing ponds, and an orchard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Kingstown

For outdoor enthusiasts, South Kingstown offers several scenic hiking and biking routes that showcase the town’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best trails:

  1. The William C. O’Neill Bike Path: This paved bike path stretches for 5 miles through South Kingstown’s rural landscapes. It offers stunning views of fields, farms, and forests.
  2. The Narrow River Kayak Trail: This 4-mile trail takes you along the scenic Narrow River. It’s an ideal route for kayaking, birdwatching, and photography.
  3. The Jean Barach Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is located in the Trustom Pond National Wildlife Refuge and offers beautiful views of the pond and surrounding woodlands.

Best Things to Do in South Kingstown: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and visiting historical sites, South Kingstown offers plenty of other activities to keep you entertained. Here are some of our favorites:

  1. Take a wine tour: South Kingstown is home to several wineries, such as Langworthy Farm Winery and Carolyn’s Sakonnet Vineyard. Join a guided tour and learn about the wine-making process while sampling the local vintages.
  2. Attend a cultural event: South Kingstown is a hub for art and culture. Check out the local theater scene at the Contemporary Theater Company, or catch a concert at the Narrows Center for the Arts.
  3. Go fishing: Whether you prefer freshwater or saltwater fishing, South Kingstown has plenty of options. Head to Green Hill Pond or the Saltwater Anglers Association Fishing Club for a relaxing day by the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Kingstown

Planning a longer stay in South Kingstown? Here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit South County Museum and walk the trails of the Cooperative Wildlife Management Area. Day 2: Head to East Matunuck State Beach for a day of sun, sand, and surf. Day 3: Take a guided tour of one of the local wineries, and sample the region’s best wines. Day 4: Visit Smith’s Castle to learn about Rhode Island’s colonial history, then bike along the William C. O’Neill Bike Path. Day 5: Go kayaking or birdwatching in Trustom Pond National Wildlife Refuge. Day 6: Attend a performance at the Contemporary Theater Company, or catch a concert at the Narrows Center for the Arts. Day 7: Explore the local shopping scene and buy souvenirs at the Matunuck Oyster Bar and other local boutiques.

Food and Drink Scene of South Kingstown

South Kingstown is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of locally produced food and drink to sample. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Rhode Island Clam Chowder: This iconic New England dish can be found in most local restaurants. It’s a creamy soup made with quahog clams, potatoes, and onions.
  2. Matunuck Oysters: These sweet and succulent oysters are harvested locally and can be enjoyed raw or cooked.
  3. Johnnycakes: These cornmeal-based pancakes are a Rhode Island specialty, and are often served with maple syrup or molasses.

Local Beverages

  1. Narragansett Beer: This classic Rhode Island beer has been brewed for over 100 years. It’s a crisp and refreshing lager that pairs well with seafood.
  2. Newport Vineyards Wine: This award-winning winery produces several varieties of wine, including chardonnay and merlot. The vineyard also offers tours and tastings.
  3. Yacht Club Soda: This Rhode Island soda company has been making bubbly drinks since 1915. Try the cream soda or the root beer for a truly local taste.

Where to Stay in South Kingstown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Kingstown offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. The Ocean House: This historic hotel offers luxurious rooms, a private beach, and world-class dining. It’s perfect for a romantic getaway or special occasion.
  2. The Hampton Inn South Kingstown: This budget-friendly hotel is located in the heart of South County and offers comfortable rooms and an indoor pool.
  3. The Andrea Hotel: This charming beachfront guesthouse offers cozy accommodations and stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

What to Buy in South Kingstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to bring back home? Here are some of the top local products to consider:

  1. Handmade pottery: South Kingstown is home to several talented pottery artisans, such as Nancy Carballo Pottery and The Clay Studio.
  2. Gourmet food products: Buy locally made jams, jellies, and sauces at South County Artisan Market or the Farmer’s Daughter.
  3. Beach-inspired jewelry: Shops like Aquamarine Jewelry and Charlestown Gallery carry beautiful, nautical-themed jewelry that make the perfect summer accessory.

Local Culture and Customs of South Kingstown

South Kingstown has a rich cultural heritage that’s worth exploring during your visit. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  1. Respect the beaches: Rhode Island beaches are known for their natural beauty, and locals take great pride in keeping them clean. Make sure to dispose of your trash properly and follow any posted rules or regulations.
  2. Be polite: Rhode Islanders are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. Make sure to say “please” and “thank you” to locals, and don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation.
  3. Embrace the local lingo: Rhode Islanders have a distinct accent and vocabulary that might take some getting used to. But don’t worry, locals won’t mind explaining what a “grinder” or a “cabinet” is.

Best Time to Visit South Kingstown

South Kingstown’s peak tourism season is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are plentiful. However, the town is breathtaking during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the crowds thin out. If you’re looking to avoid the busiest times, consider visiting during the shoulder season (May-June or September-October).

Do’s and Don’ts of South Kingstown

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting South Kingstown:

Do:

  1. Pack appropriate clothing for the season, as the weather can change quickly.
  2. Sample the local seafood, oysters, and wine.
  3. Explore the town’s hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or disturb the natural habitats of local wildlife.
  2. Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.
  3. Ignore posted rules at beaches and parks.

Safety and Health

South Kingstown is generally a safe and healthy destination for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard travel precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding dangerous or unfamiliar areas at night.

Safety in South Kingstown

Rhode Island has a low crime rate compared to the rest of the country. However, it’s always a good idea to keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in South Kingstown

South Kingstown has several medical facilities, including South County Hospital and several urgent care clinics. Make sure to bring any necessary medication or prescriptions with you when traveling.

Getting to South Kingstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Kingstown is easily accessible by car, train, or plane. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near South Kingstown

  1. T.F. Green Airport (PVD): Located in nearby Warwick, this airport is served by several major airlines and offers flights to several domestic and international destinations.
  2. Logan International Airport (BOS): Located in Boston, this airport is a larger hub with many international flights. It’s about an hour and a half drive from South Kingstown.

Bus Stations Near South Kingstown

  1. Peter Pan Bus Lines: This bus service operates routes between major cities in the Northeast, with stops in nearby Providence and Newport.
  2. Greyhound: This bus company also operates routes between major cities, with stops in Providence and New London, CT.

Other Options

  1. Amtrak: This train service operates several routes through Rhode Island, with stops in Providence and Kingston.
  2. Car rental: South Kingstown is easily accessible by car, and rental options are available at nearby airports and bus terminals.

How to Get Around in South Kingstown

While South Kingstown is a fairly small town, having a car is recommended if you want to explore more of Rhode Island. However, there are also several public transportation and taxi services available if you prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from South Kingstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Rhode Island or New England, South Kingstown makes a good base. Here are some nearby destinations worth considering:

  1. Newport: This historic town is known for its Gilded Age mansions, sailing culture, and beautiful beaches. It’s about a 30-minute drive from South Kingstown.
  2. Block Island: This idyllic island is characterized by its stunning beaches and upscale resorts. It’s accessible by ferry from Narragansett and is a popular summer destination.
  3. Providence: Rhode Island’s capital city is a hub for arts, culture, and history. Check out its many museums and restaurants.

Conclusion

South Kingstown may not be as well-known as other destinations in Rhode Island, but it offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural richness. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation, an outdoor adventure, or an inspiring cultural experience, South Kingstown has it all. We hope this travel guide has helped you discover the best this charming town has to offer. Safe travels!
