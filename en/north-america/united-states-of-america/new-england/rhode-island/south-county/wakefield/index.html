Introduction to Wakefield

Welcome to Wakefield, a charming village located in South County, Rhode Island. Wakefield is a popular summer destination for tourists seeking a relaxing vacation experience. Wakefield boasts a mix of natural attractions and cultural landmarks. Visitors can enjoy the pristine beaches, hiking trails, outdoor adventures, and a vibrant food and drink scene. This guide will provide everything you need to know about Wakefield and make your trip memorable.

List of Places to Visit in Wakefield

Here are the top places to visit in Wakefield that should be on your travel itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • South County History Center: This museum has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history of South County, Rhode Island.

  • The Towers: The Towers is a historical landmark located in Narragansett that was built in 1883 as part of a grand hotel.

  • Gilbert Stuart Birthplace and Museum: Learn about the famous portrait artist Gilbert Stuart, who was born in Rhode Island. Visitors can see his childhood home and studio, which is now a museum.

Natural Attractions of Wakefield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Narragansett Beach: One of the most popular beaches in Rhode Island, Narragansett Beach offers crystal-clear water and white sand.

  • Block Island: If you are looking for a day trip, you should head to Block Island. The island has some of the most beautiful beaches in New England and offers breathtaking views.

  • The John H. Chafee Wildlife Refuge: The refuge is an ideal destination for a nature walk or bird watching. Visitors can spot many species of migratory birds and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wakefield

Wakefield has some fantastic hiking and biking trails, and here are the best ones:

  • The North-South Trail: The North-South Trail is a 77-mile paved route that runs from Charlestown to Burrillville. The trail passes through Wakefield and offers scenic views of the surrounding areas.

  • The South County Bike Path: The South County Bike Path is a 7.8-mile paved trail that runs through Wakefield and ends in Narragansett. The trail is perfect for biking, walking, and jogging.

Best Things to Do in Wakefield: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Wakefield:

  • Water Sports: Wakefield is an excellent destination for water sports enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, surfing, paddleboarding, and boating.

  • Golfing: Wakefield has many world-class golf courses, including the Meadow Brook Golf Course and the Point Judith Country Club.

  • Fishing: Wakefield is known for its excellent fishing opportunities. Visitors can try their hand at fishing for striped bass, black sea bass, and fluke.

Food and Drink Scene of Wakefield

Wakefield has a thriving food and drink scene, and here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rhode Island Clam Chowder: Rhode Island is famous for its unique version of clam chowder, which is made with clear broth, fresh clams, and potatoes.

  • Stuffies: Stuffies are stuffed clams that are baked with a mixture of chopped clams, herbs, bread crumbs, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Del’s Frozen Lemonade: Del’s Frozen Lemonade is a Rhode Island specialty that is made from freshly-squeezed lemons, sugar, and ice.

  • Narragansett Beer: Narragansett Beer is a local beer that has been brewed in Rhode Island since 1890.

Where to Stay in Wakefield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wakefield has many accommodations options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Blueberry Cove Inn: The Blueberry Cove Inn is a cozy B&B in a peaceful location that offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

  • Holiday Inn South Kingstown: The Holiday Inn is a budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a gym.

  • The Break Hotel: The Break Hotel is a luxurious beachfront hotel that offers panoramic sea views, a rooftop bar, and spacious suites.

What to Buy in Wakefield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should buy in Wakefield:

  • Seashell Art: Wakefield is known for its seashell art, which is created by local artists. The artwork includes items such as picture frames, jewelry, and sculptures.

  • Rhode Island Coffee: You can bring home a bag of Rhode Island coffee, which is roasted locally and sourced from organic and fair-trade farms.

Local Culture and Customs of Wakefield

Wakefield has a unique culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and the pace of life is slower compared to other tourist destinations. Visitors should respect the wildlife and nature in the area and avoid littering.

Best Time to Visit Wakefield

The best time to visit Wakefield is between May and September when the weather is warm, and the beaches are open. The peak season is July and August when the tourist crowds are high.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wakefield

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors and natural landmarks.
  • Attend local festivals and events.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter on the beaches or parks.
  • Do not overindulge in alcohol in public areas.
  • Do not disturb the wildlife or nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wakefield

Wakefield is a safe destination for tourists. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as securing valuables and locking doors.

Health in Wakefield

Visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccines and take precautions to avoid ticks, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Wakefield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wakefield

  • T.F Green Airport: T.F Green Airport is located in Warwick, Rhode Island, and is a 30-minute drive from Wakefield.
  • Boston Logan International Airport: Boston Logan International Airport is located in Boston, Massachusetts, and is a 1.5-hour drive from Wakefield.

Bus Stations Near Wakefield

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: Peter Pan Bus Lines provide services to Providence, Boston, and other significant cities in the Northeast.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Wakefield.

How to Get Around in Wakefield

The best way to get around Wakefield is by car. Visitors can also rent a bike or walk around the village.

Where to Go Next from Wakefield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit from Wakefield:

  • Newport: Newport is located 30 miles from Wakefield and is famous for its colonial architecture, historic mansions, and scenic views.

  • Boston: Boston is located 80 miles from Wakefield and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the United States.

Conclusion

Wakefield is an ideal destination for tourists seeking an authentic New England experience. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty, rich history, and delicious food and drinks. This guide includes everything you need to know about Wakefield, and we hope you have a memorable trip.
