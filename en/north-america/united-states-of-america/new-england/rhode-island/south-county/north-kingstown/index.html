Introduction to North Kingstown

Located in South County, Rhode Island, North Kingstown is a small town surrounded by stunning natural beauty and boasts a rich history. It has maintained its charm with picturesque villages, historic buildings, and beautiful beaches. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, history, or local cuisine, North Kingstown is the perfect destination for an unforgettable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in North Kingstown

North Kingstown is full of attractions that cater to different tastes, whether you’re looking for culturally important sites, natural attractions, or fun activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning more about the history of North Kingstown and Rhode Island in general, there are several museums and historical places to visit.

  • Smith’s Castle: Built in 1637, this 17th-century estate offers a glimpse into colonial life.
  • Quonset Air Museum: Displays the aviation heritage of Quonset Point Naval Air Station and the history of naval aviation.
  • Casey Farm: A historic farm that dates back to 1740 and showcases traditional farming methods and crafts.

Natural Attractions of North Kingstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Kingstown’s natural beauty is breathtaking, and there are several outdoor activities and scenic spots to explore.

  • Rose Island Lighthouse: A historic lighthouse located on Rose Island that offers tours and overnight stays.
  • Wickford Harbor: A picturesque harbor where you can rent kayaks or paddleboards and explore the coastline.
  • Ryan Park: A beautiful park with hiking trails, a beach, and a pond where you can kayak or fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Kingstown

North Kingstown is home to several scenic hiking and biking routes that take you through stunning landscapes and picturesque towns.

  • North Kingstown Bike Path: This 8.5-mile route takes you through beautiful forests, fields, and wetlands.
  • Calf Pasture Point Nature Preserve: This 190-acre preserve offers a scenic trail along the coast, with stunning views of Narragansett Bay and the Newport Bridge.
  • Wilson Park: A beautiful park with hiking trails, a playground, and a pond where you can rent paddleboats.

Best Things to Do in North Kingstown: Activities and Tours

Besides historical sites and outdoor activities, there are several other fun things to do in North Kingstown, which include:

  • Fastnet Pub: A local Irish pub where you can enjoy live music and delicious food and drinks.
  • KVH Industries: A leading manufacturer of mobile satellite communications systems with factory tours and demonstrations available.
  • Balfour Riverwalk Park: A beautiful park with picnic areas, benches, and trails that lead you to the Pawtuxet River.

Food and Drink Scene of North Kingstown

North Kingstown has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers a mix of local and international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stuffies: A Rhode Island favorite, these large Quahog clams stuffed with herbs, breadcrumbs, and vegetables are a must-try.
  • Lobster Rolls: Fresh local lobster, mayonnaise, and celery served on a hot dog roll make for a delicious and filling meal.
  • Johnny Cakes: A traditional Rhode Island staple, these cornmeal pancakes are perfect as a side dish or for breakfast with maple syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer: A historic Rhode Island beer that has been brewed since 1890.
  • Yacht Club Soda: A local soda brand that has been producing soft drinks since 1915.
  • Dave’s Coffee: A local coffee roaster that offers delicious blends and flavored syrups.

Where to Stay in North Kingstown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Kingstown is home to several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences.

  • The Newport Beach Hotel and Suites: Located in nearby Newport, this beachfront hotel offers excellent views and elegant rooms.
  • TownePlace Suites by Marriott Warwick: A modern and comfortable hotel that is conveniently located near the airport.
  • Wickford Motor Inn: A cozy and affordable option in the heart of Wickford Village.

What to Buy in North Kingstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Kingstown offers several unique souvenirs that are perfect for gifts or to take home as a reminder of your trip.

  • Casey Farm’s Organic Produce: Their fresh and organic produce can be purchased at the farm stand and is a perfect gift for foodies.
  • Sweet Cakes Bakery’s Cinnamon Breads: These artisanal bread are simply delicious and make for a great gift or snack.
  • The Mustard Seed’s Gourmet Mustards: A selection of locally made gourmet mustards that are perfect for sandwiches or charcuterie boards.

Local Culture and Customs of North Kingstown

North Kingstown is a small town that prides itself on its friendly and welcoming people. It’s important to respect the local culture and customs during your visit.

Best Time to Visit North Kingstown

The best time to visit North Kingstown is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and outdoor attractions are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Kingstown

When visiting North Kingstown, keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Do bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Do respect the environment and natural habitats.
  • Do tip generously at restaurants and bars.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind.
  • Don’t smoke in public places.
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing in religious or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

North Kingstown is a safe town with good healthcare facilities. However, it’s always important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health during your visit.

Safety in North Kingstown

  • Keep car doors locked and windows rolled up when driving at night.
  • Don’t leave valuables visible in parked cars.
  • Take precautions against ticks and mosquitoes when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Health in North Kingstown

  • Ensure you have good travel medical insurance before your trip.
  • Drink bottled water if you are not used to Rhode Island tap water.
  • Protect yourself from sunburn and heat stroke during the summer months.

Getting to North Kingstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Kingstown is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near North Kingstown

  • T. F. Green Airport: Located approximately 12 miles away in Warwick, RI.
  • Logan International Airport: Located approximately 77 miles away in Boston, MA.

Bus Stations Near North Kingstown

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines: Stops at Warwick and Providence before arriving in North Kingstown.
  • Greyhound: Stops at Providence before arriving in North Kingstown.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: From Boston or New York City to Kingston Station, followed by a taxi or rideshare service to North Kingstown.
  • Car Rentals: Available at T. F. Green Airport or in Providence.

How to Get Around in North Kingstown

The best way to get around North Kingstown is by car or bike, especially if you plan to explore the scenic routes and outdoor attractions. There is also a local RIPTA bus service that connects North Kingstown to nearby towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from North Kingstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Kingstown is conveniently located to several nearby destinations that are worth exploring.

  • Providence: A historic city that offers culinary diversity, live music events, and must-visit local sites, such as WaterFire, Providence Performing Arts Center, and the RISD Museum.
  • Newport: A nearby coastal town that is home to the famous Newport Mansions, the scenic Cliff Walk, and the Newport Jazz Festival.
  • Block Island: A beautiful island located off the coast of Rhode Island that boasts stunning beaches, hiking trails, lighthouses, and wind turbines.

Conclusion

North Kingstown is a great destination for visitors looking for a relaxing getaway with a mix of natural beauty, history, and delicious food. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or just want to unwind, North Kingstown has something for everyone.
