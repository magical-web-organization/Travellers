Introduction to South County

South County is a charming and scenic region located in Rhode Island, in New England, in the United States of America. This picturesque area boasts beautiful state parks, pristine beaches, and a rich cultural heritage.

Whether you’re planning a family vacation, a romantic getaway, or a solo adventure, South County has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to make the most of your trip to this lovely region.

List of Places to Visit in South County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South County is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich history and cultural heritage. These include:

  • The South County Museum - This museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the region’s agricultural and maritime history.
  • The Gilbert Stuart Birthplace and Museum - This historic site was home to renowned portrait artist Gilbert Stuart and features exhibits on his life and work.
  • The Towers - This historic structure was once a ballroom and social hall, and today serves as a beloved event venue.

Natural Attractions of South County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South County’s natural beauty is a major draw for visitors. From pristine beaches to lush forests, there are plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors. Some of the must-see natural attractions in the region include:

  • Narragansett Town Beach - This popular beach is known for its sparkling waters and soft sand.
  • Ninigret Park - This expansive park features hiking trails, an observation tower, and plenty of opportunities for picnicking and enjoying the great outdoors.
  • Trustom Pond National Wildlife Refuge - This protected refuge offers visitors the chance to see a variety of native bird species and other wildlife in their natural habitats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South County

If you’re looking to explore South County on foot or by bike, there are plenty of trails and routes to choose from. Some of the best options include:

  • The South County Bike Path - This paved path stretches for 7.8 miles and offers beautiful views of the region’s coastline and natural scenery.
  • The Washington Secondary Bike Path - This 19-mile route takes visitors through quaint villages and scenic landscapes.
  • The Narragansett Bay Trail - This 14-mile trail winds through picturesque waterfront communities and offers stunning views of the bay.

Best Things to Do in South County: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural beauty and cultural attractions, South County offers visitors a wide variety of activities and tours. Some of the best options include:

  • Sailing excursions - Take a leisurely sail through Narragansett Bay and enjoy breathtaking views of the coastline.
  • Wine tastings - South County is home to several vineyards and wineries that offer tastings and tours.
  • Fishing charters - The region’s waters are teeming with fish, including striped bass, bluefish, and flounder.

Week-Long Itinerary for South County

If you’re planning a week-long visit to South County, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Narragansett Town Beach and nearby shops and restaurants.
  • Day 2: Visit the South County Museum and take a bike ride along the South County Bike Path.
  • Day 3: Take a sailing excursion or go fishing with a local charter.
  • Day 4: Visit the Gilbert Stuart Birthplace and Museum and enjoy a wine tasting at a nearby vineyard.
  • Day 5: Relax at Trustom Pond National Wildlife Refuge and enjoy a picnic lunch.
  • Day 6: Take a scenic drive along Ocean Road and visit The Towers.
  • Day 7: Spend the day exploring historic Wickford Village and nearby attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of South County

South County is home to a diverse range of restaurants and eateries, offering everything from fresh seafood to international cuisine. When it comes to local dishes and drinks, here are some must-try options:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam chowder - This classic New England dish features tender clams and a creamy broth.
  • Stuffies - These baked clam cakes are a South County favorite.
  • Lobster rolls - Fresh, succulent lobster meat served on a toasted roll is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Del’s Frozen Lemonade - This iconic Rhode Island drink is a refreshing treat on hot summer days.
  • Narragansett Beer - This classic brew has been a Rhode Island favorite since the 1800s.

Where to Stay in South County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South County offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Some of the best places to stay in the region include:

  • The Ocean House - This historic hotel offers luxury accommodations and stunning ocean views.
  • The Tower House Bed and Breakfast - This charming and cozy B&B is located in the heart of Narragansett.
  • The Hampton Inn South Kingstown/Newport Area - This modern and comfortable hotel is located close to many of the region’s top attractions.

What to Buy in South County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to take home from your trip to South County, some of the best options include:

  • Handmade jewelry - South County is home to many talented jewelry makers, who create beautiful pieces using local materials and other unique elements.
  • Local food products - From fresh seafood to artisanal cheeses, there are plenty of delicious treats to bring home from South County.
  • Art and pottery - Many local artists and artisans create beautiful works of art and pottery inspired by the region’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of South County

South County has a unique and rich cultural heritage, which is deeply rooted in its agricultural and maritime history. Visitors to the region can learn about this heritage through visits to museums and historical sites, as well as by experiencing local festivals and events, such as the Washington County Fair and the Newport Folk Festival.

Best Time to Visit South County

The best time to visit South County depends on your preferences and interests. Summer is the most popular time to visit, as the weather is warm and sunny and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events. However, spring and fall can also be great times to explore the region, as the crowds are smaller and the scenery is still beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of South County

Do:

  • Do explore the region’s natural beauty and cultural attractions.
  • Do try local dishes and beverages.
  • Do respect the area’s heritage and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent!

Safety and Health

Safety in South County

South County is generally a safe and welcoming region, but it’s still important to take precautions to stay safe. Some tips include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when out at night.
  • Lock your car and don’t leave valuables visible inside.
  • Be cautious when swimming in the ocean, as the surf can be strong and dangerous in some areas.

Health in South County

South County has several medical facilities, including a hospital and urgent care clinics. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you when traveling and to have travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Getting to South County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South County

The closest airport to South County is T.F. Green Airport, located in Warwick, Rhode Island. This airport is serviced by several major airlines and offers easy access to the region.

Bus Stations Near South County

Greyhound and Peter Pan Bus Lines both offer service to Providence, Rhode Island, which is about 30 minutes from South County.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach South County by car, train, or ferry. Amtrak offers train service to nearby Kingston, Rhode Island, and ferries run from several nearby ports.

How to Get Around in South County

The best way to get around South County is by car. There are several rental car agencies located at T.F. Green Airport and in nearby towns. Biking and walking can also be great options, especially for exploring small villages and downtown areas.

Where to Go Next from South County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South County is located just a short drive from several other popular New England destinations. Some great options for side trips include:

  • Newport, Rhode Island - This famous seaside town is known for its beautiful mansions, historic sites, and lively downtown area.
  • Providence, Rhode Island - The capital city of Rhode Island is home to a variety of cultural attractions, including the Rhode Island School of Design Museum and WaterFire Providence.
  • Mystic, Connecticut - This charming coastal town is home to several historic sites and attractions, including Mystic Seaport and the Mystic Aquarium.

Conclusion

South County is a unique and beautiful region that offers visitors the chance to explore the natural beauty and rich cultural heritage of New England. From pristine beaches to historic sites and museums, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this charming area. Start planning your trip today and experience all that South County has to offer!
