Introduction to Charlestown

Charlestown is a charming small town located in South County, Rhode Island, USA. Known for its pristine beaches, natural beauty, and rich culture, this hidden gem attracts tourists year-round, especially during summers. The town is home to some of the best hiking trails, wildlife reserves, historical monuments, and beachfront activities in the state. If you are looking for a laid-back vacation destination, Charlestown, with its calm ambiance, is the perfect fit for you.

List of Places to Visit in Charlestown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Charlestown boasts of a rich cultural heritage, which is evident in its museums and historical places. Check out the following must-visit places:

  • The Fantastic Umbrella Factory - This historic factory located on Route 1 is known for its unique shopping experience and artisan crafts.

  • South County Museum - If you are keen to explore the history of Rhode Island, this museum is for you. From vintage farming to naval artifacts, you will find an array of exhibits here.

  • Ninigret National Wildlife Refuge - Located on the site of a former Navy airfield, this refuge is home to over 250 bird species, turtles, deer, and other wildlife. You can opt for a guided tour or explore the place on your own.

Natural Attractions of Charlestown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Charlestown is known for its stunning natural beauty, which encompasses picturesque beaches, wildlife reserves, and forests. Here are some outdoor attractions that you can’t afford to miss:

  • East Beach - This natural gem is known for its pristine white-sand beaches and breathtaking views. Take a dip in the Atlantic Ocean or enjoy sunbathing and beachcombing.

  • Charlestown Breachway - This man-made breachway provides opportunities for kayaking, fishing, and swimming. You can also rent a boat or bike and explore the stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Burlingame State Park - This state park offers over 3,100 acres of lush forests, hiking trails, and freshwater swimming. You can also enjoy birdwatching, fishing, and boating in the nearby Watchaug Pond.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charlestown

Charlestown is a paradise for hikers, thanks to its diverse terrain, scenic paths, and breathtaking views. Check out the following hiking/biking routes:

  • Kettle Pond Trail - This moderate hike is a 6.7 mile loop that takes you through lush forests, past Kettle Pond, and over stunning rocky terrain. You should bring bug spray, as mosquitoes are abundant here.

  • Camman’s Pond Trail - This 1.8-mile trail is perfect for beginner hikers. It boasts of scenic views of the pond, the wetlands, and the sand dunes.

  • The Narragansett Indian Trail - This 2.5-mile trail takes you through the heart of the Ninigret National Wildlife Refuge. You can explore the habitats of different bird and animal species while enjoying the lush greenery.

Best Things to Do in Charlestown: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of Charlestown, you can also enjoy a range of exciting activities. Here are some must-do options:

  • Charter a Fishing Boat - Charlestown is known for its fantastic fishing opportunities, so take the opportunity to explore the seas by chartering a boat with local fishermen.

  • Horseback Riding - Gallop along the scenic trails of Watchaug Pond with the help of nearby stables, such as Sunset Stables.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing - Rent a kayak or canoe and paddle down peaceful salt marshes and bays to get closer to nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Charlestown

If you are planning a week-long vacation in Charlestown, make sure you include the following:

Food and Drink Scene of Charlestown

Charlestown boasts of a great food and drink scene that features fresh seafood, local produce, and a wide range of international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll - Charlestown is known for its succulent and flavorful lobster rolls, which are available at popular eateries like The Breachway Grill.

  • Clam Cakes - These fried dough balls with clam bits are a traditional Rhode Island delicacy, and you can try them at eateries like Aunt Carrie’s.

Local Beverages

  • Rhode Island Red Beer - This red lager is produced by the Revival Brewing Company and is a popular choice among the locals.

  • Del’s Lemonade - This iconic frozen lemon slush is a refreshing and delicious summer drink that has been quenching thirsts since 1948.

Where to Stay in Charlestown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Charlestown offers a range of accommodation options, depending on your budget, preferences, and location. Here are some popular options:

  • The Windswept Inn - This motel located near the beach offers spacious rooms, great views, and a peaceful ambiance.

  • The Coachman Motor Inn - This family-owned motel is located on Route 1 and offers cozy accommodations, an outdoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.

  • The Phil & Anns Sunnyside Motel - This quaint and pet-friendly motel is located close to the Ninigret National Wildlife Refuge and boasts of neat rooms, lush surroundings, and attentive service.

What to Buy in Charlestown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can take home a slice of Charlestown by buying some locally produced products and souvenirs. Here are some options:

  • Quahog Shells - These large, hard-shelled clams are found in abundance in and around Charlestown and can be used for crafting jewelry and souvenirs.

  • Artisan Crafts - The Fantastic Umbrella Factory is known for its eclectic collection of artisan crafts, such as pottery, glassware, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Charlestown

Charlestown has a unique culture that revolves around its strong community bonds, fishing traditions, and passion for conservation. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and you can expect to encounter a laid-back and relaxed vibe throughout the town. Be sure to observe local customs, such as respecting the environment and maintaining cleanliness.

Best Time to Visit Charlestown

Charlestown is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is between May and September, when the weather is pleasant and the town is alive with beach activities, festivals, and events. The summers can get crowded and expensive, so be sure to plan your trip and book your accommodations in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charlestown

Do:

  • Respect the environment, flora, and fauna and follow the “Leave No Trace” principles.

  • Bring bug spray and sunscreen, as you will be spending most of your time outdoors.

  • Try the local seafood and clam cakes.

Don’t:

  • Litter, smoke, or damage the beaches and parks.

  • Disturb the wildlife and their habitats by feeding them or getting too close.

  • Go into the water if you are not a strong swimmer or if there are rip currents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charlestown

Charlestown is a safe and friendly destination, but you should take standard precautions while traveling, such as keeping your belongings secure and being aware of your surroundings. Also, be careful while swimming, surfing, or boating, as the tides and currents can be strong.

Health in Charlestown

Charlestown has adequate healthcare facilities, pharmacies, and medical clinics, but it is always advisable to carry a basic first-aid kit and your medications, as available resources may vary in rural areas.

Getting to Charlestown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Charlestown can be easily accessed via various modes of transportation, such as:

Airports Near Charlestown

  • T. F. Green Airport - Located in Warwick, this airport is about 35 minutes from Charlestown.

  • Logan International Airport - Located in Boston, this is a major international airport and is about 1.5 hours from Charlestown.

Bus Stations Near Charlestown

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines - This bus line operates from Providence, RI to New York City and stops at the Kingston Amtrak Station, which is 15 minutes from Charlestown.

  • RIPTA - Rhode Island’s public transportation system operates buses that stop at different points in and around Charlestown.

Other Options

  • Amtrak - The Providence Station is the nearest Amtrak station to Charlestown, and from here, you can take taxis or buses to the town.

  • Car Rentals - You can rent cars from the airport or other local car rental agencies to explore Charlestown and nearby attractions.

How to Get Around in Charlestown

Charlestown is a small town and can be easily navigated by car, bike, or on foot. Walking or biking is the best way to explore the natural beauty of the town. You can also opt for car rentals or charter a boat for exploring the nearby waterways.

Where to Go Next from Charlestown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Charlestown is located in close proximity to many other exciting destinations in Rhode Island and beyond. Here are some options for nearby excursions:

  • Newport - This affluent coastal town is known for its historic mansions, scenic views, and lively nightlife and is about one hour from Charlestown.

  • Block Island - This stunning island is known for its quaint villages, beaches, and outdoor activities and is accessible by ferry from Point Judith, which is about 20 minutes from Charlestown.

  • Mystic, Connecticut - This charming town is known for its maritime heritage, aquarium, and museum and is about one hour from Charlestown.

Conclusion

Charlestown is a hidden gem worth exploring, thanks to its pristine beaches, stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and welcoming locals. We hope our travel guide has provided valuable insights, suggestions, and itinerary ideas to help you plan your perfect getaway. Happy exploring!
