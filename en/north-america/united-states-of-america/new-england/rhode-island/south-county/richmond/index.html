Introduction to Richmond

Located in South County, Rhode Island, Richmond is a charming town nestled between the cities of Providence and New London with a population of just over 7,000 residents. It is known for its natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor recreational activities such as hiking, fishing, and boating. Richmond’s small-town charm, coupled with its rich history and beautiful surroundings, makes it an ideal destination for tourists seeking a peaceful getaway.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Richmond and make the most of your visit. We will cover the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best tours and activities, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Richmond

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Richmond Historic District

The Richmond Historic District is a charming area that encompasses more than 200 buildings with architectural significance. The district has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places since 1983, and it provides a glimpse into the town’s history.

Washington County Fairgrounds

If you’re visiting Richmond in mid-August, be sure to head to the Washington County Fairgrounds, which hosts the Washington County Fair every year. The fair is a fun-filled event that includes musical performances, agricultural exhibits, carnival rides, and much more.

Richmond Grange Hall

The Richmond Grange Hall is another interesting historical attraction that deserves a visit. Built back in 1891, it was once a gathering place for farmers but has now been transformed into a community center. The building hosts various events throughout the year, including fairs, art exhibits, and concerts.

Natural Attractions of Richmond: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Carolina Management Area

The Carolina Management Area is a vast, 3,000-acre swath of land that offers visitors an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. It includes hiking trails, fishing spots, and camping areas for nature enthusiasts.

Beaver River Preserve

Another natural attraction that should be on your list is Beaver River Preserve. It comprises 500 acres of land with abundant wildlife and beautiful scenery. Visitors can enjoy birdwatching, hiking, and picnicking in the park.

Spring Lake Beach

If you’re in the mood for sun, sand, and fun, head to Spring Lake Beach. It is a beautiful and well-maintained beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing in the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Richmond

Richmond offers many opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore its scenic surroundings. Below are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

South County Bike Path

The South County Bike Path is a 7.8-mile trail that runs from Kingston to Narragansett. The path follows an old rail line, and it provides a scenic route through wooded areas and residential neighborhoods.

Tillinghast Pond Management Area

The Tillinghast Pond Management Area is an ideal spot for hiking, with over 1,200 acres of land to explore. With several trails ranging from easy to challenging, hikers can admire the area’s varied landscapes, including meadows, forests, and rocky ridges.

Best Things to Do in Richmond: Activities and Tours

Kayaking on the Beaver River

Kayaking on the Beaver River is an adventurous and fun activity that provides a unique perspective of the area’s natural beauty. Visitors can rent kayaks from local outfitters and embark on a paddling adventure on the serene waters of the river.

Horseback Riding

Get in touch with your inner cowboy or cowgirl by going horseback riding in Richmond. There are several riding stables in the area, and they offer guided tours through the town’s scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Richmond

Day 1: Visit the Richmond Historic District Day 2: Hike in the Tillinghast Pond Management Area Day 3: Spend the day at Spring Lake Beach Day 4: Go horseback riding in the morning and kayaking on the Beaver River in the afternoon. Day 5: Visit the Washington County Fairgrounds Day 6: Take a leisurely bike ride on the South County Bike Path Day 7: Tour the Richmond Grange Hall

Food and Drink Scene of Richmond

Must-Try Dishes

Clam Cakes

Clam cakes are a Rhode Island classic and a must-try when visiting Richmond. Made with clam meat, cornmeal, and spices, these little fritters are a delicious and affordable snack that can be found at many local seafood restaurants.

Stuffies

Another local specialty is stuffies. These are quahog clam shells stuffed with a mixture of chopped clams, breadcrumbs, herbs, and spices, and then baked until golden brown and crispy.

Local Beverages

Coffee Milk

Coffee milk, Rhode Island’s official state drink, is a sweet and creamy beverage that is made by mixing coffee syrup and milk. It is a tasty treat that you can find at many local restaurants and diners.

Del’s Lemonade

Del’s Lemonade is a frozen drink made from lemon juice, sugar, and water. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot summer days and can be found at many roadside stands across Richmond.

Where to Stay in Richmond: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Shelter Harbor Inn

The Shelter Harbor Inn is a historic inn that offers comfortable accommodations and excellent dining options. The inn features beautifully appointed guest rooms and suites, a swimming pool, tennis courts, and stunning gardens.

Charlestown Rathskeller

Charlestown Rathskeller is a cozy and affordable guesthouse that offers a home-away-from-home experience. The guesthouse features comfortable rooms, a shared kitchen, and a wonderful backyard patio.

What to Buy in Richmond: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kenyon’s Grist Mill Products

Kenyon’s Grist Mill has been producing stone-ground grains in Richmond for over 300 years. Visitors can purchase a wide range of products, including cornmeal, pancake mix, and local maple syrup.

Antique Shops

Richmond has several antique shops where visitors can find unique and one-of-a-kind souvenirs. Some of the must-visit shops include Reliable Gold and Silver, The Wooden Midshipman, and Small Corners Antiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Richmond

Richmond has a rich history and a unique culture that visitors can discover by visiting its museums, historic buildings, and natural attractions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions and to be courteous and respectful when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Richmond

The best time to visit Richmond is during the summer months (June to August). The weather is warm, and the area’s outdoor recreational activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the area’s natural beauty during the fall, when the trees turn a vibrant shade of red, orange, and yellow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Richmond

Do:

  • Be courteous to locals and fellow visitors
  • Explore the town’s natural beauty through hiking, boating, and fishing
  • Try the local seafood and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s natural or historic attractions
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Engage in reckless behavior while participating in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Richmond

Richmond is a relatively safe town with low levels of crime. However, visitors should exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities and always follow safety guidelines.

Health in Richmond

Richmond has excellent medical facilities, including South County Hospital and the Westerly Hospital, in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Richmond: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Richmond

The nearest airport to Richmond is Rhode Island T. F. Green Airport, which is located in Warwick and is approximately 30 miles away from Richmond.

Bus Stations Near Richmond

The nearest bus station to Richmond is located in Kingston, approximately 10 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Richmond, with several major highways passing through the area, including Route 95 and Route 138.

How to Get Around in Richmond

The best way to get around Richmond is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent a car or use a ride-sharing service to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Richmond: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Richmond is located in an area with several nearby towns and cities that are worth visiting. Some of the must-visit destinations include:

  • Mystic, Connecticut
  • Providence, Rhode Island
  • Newport, Rhode Island

Conclusion

Richmond, Rhode Island, is a charming and scenic town that has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in outdoor recreational activities, historic landmarks, or delicious local cuisine, Richmond offers a unique and memorable experience. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this beautiful town.
