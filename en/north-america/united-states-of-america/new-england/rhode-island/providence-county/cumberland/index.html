Introduction to Cumberland

Cumberland is a small town located in Providence County, Rhode Island. It is a quintessential New England town, offering visitors beautiful natural landscapes, rich history, and cultural attractions. Cumberland is just a short drive from major cities like Providence and Boston, making it an ideal destination for a day trip or weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Cumberland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town of Cumberland has a rich historical and cultural heritage, and visitors interested in learning more about the area’s past will find many museums and historical sites to explore. The following are some of the most popular:

  • The Museum of Work and Culture
  • The Blackstone River Valley National Historical Park
  • The Hiker’s Haven Trailhead
  • The Captain Wilbur Kelly House Museum

Natural Attractions of Cumberland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cumberland is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, including forests, rivers, and lakes, providing many opportunities for outdoor recreation. Here are some of the must-see natural attractions:

  • Lincoln Woods State Park
  • Diamond Hill State Park
  • Blackstone River Greenway bike trails
  • Cumberland Monastery

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cumberland

Cumberland’s scenic trails offer breathtaking views of the natural beauty of Rhode Island. Hiking and biking enthusiasts will love exploring the following routes:

  • Monastery Trails
  • The Blackstone River Bikeway
  • Diamond Hill State Park Trail

Best Things to Do in Cumberland: Activities and Tours

Cumberland has a range of activities and tours for visitors of all interests. Whether you want to explore the outdoors or learn more about the area’s history and culture, there is something for everyone. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Blackstone Valley Explorer Riverboat Tour
  • Cumberland Town Hall and Memorial Park
  • Phantom Farms
  • Ryan Family Amusements

Local Culture and Customs of Cumberland

Cumberland is a small, close-knit community with a strong sense of local pride. Visitors should respect the town’s culture and customs by being polite and courteous to locals and observing local laws and cultural practices.

Best Time to Visit Cumberland

The best time to visit Cumberland is in the fall, when the leaves are changing and the weather is cool and comfortable. However, the town has plenty to offer all year round, with summer being a popular time for outdoor recreation and winter being ideal for cozy indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cumberland

Do:

  • Take advantage of the town’s beautiful natural parks and trails.
  • Explore the history and culture of Cumberland.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Disrespect local customs or culture.
  • Ignore local laws and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cumberland

Cumberland is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or exploring nature trails.

Health in Cumberland

Cumberland has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage and take appropriate precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Cumberland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cumberland

The nearest airport to Cumberland is T.F. Green Airport in Providence, Rhode Island, which is approximately 28 miles away. Boston Logan International Airport is also an option, which is around 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Cumberland

The nearest bus station to Cumberland is located in Lincoln, Rhode Island, which is about 5 miles away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or Uber to Cumberland.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car from either airport to explore Cumberland and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Cumberland

The best ways to get around Cumberland are by car, bike or foot. The town is relatively small and easy to navigate, with many natural areas accessible by foot or bike. Visitors should be cautious when driving on winding roads and watch for pedestrians and bicyclists.

Where to Go Next from Cumberland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring after a visit to Cumberland. Here are some recommendations:

  • Discover the historic city of Providence, approximately 10 miles south of Cumberland.
  • Visit Boston, which is around 60 miles northeast of Cumberland.
  • Explore Newport, Rhode Island, which is located approximately 40 miles southeast of Cumberland.

Conclusion

Cumberland offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. With its many museums, natural parks, and trails, the town is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Follow this guide to make the most of your stay in Cumberland, Rhode Island.
