Introduction to Scituate

Located in Providence County, in Rhode Island, in New England, in the United States of America, in North America, Scituate is a small town brimming with natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. From stunning hiking trails and scenic views to fascinating museums and historical landmarks, Scituate offers something for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Scituate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Scituate Art Festival: Held annually on Columbus Day weekend, this art festival is the largest smorgasbord of arts and crafts in Rhode Island.
  • Scituate Historical Society Museum: Take a step back in time with a visit to the Scituate Historical Society Museum, which offers a glimpse into the town’s past.
  • The Old Congregational Church: Don’t miss the chance to explore the beautifully preserved Old Congregational Church, a true masterpiece of colonial architecture.
  • Scituate’s War Memorial: Located on the common on the western side of Route 116 are the Scituate War Memorials, paying tribute to the soldiers who served from various wars.

Natural Attractions of Scituate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scituate Reservoir: This picturesque reservoir is the primary source of drinking water for the surrounding areas and offers several hiking trails around its boundary.
  • Barberville Falls: Visit this hidden gem to experience the natural beauty of Scituate, with its winding brooks and cascading waterfalls.
  • Glocester Heritage Trail: This biking and hiking trail winds through picturesque rural towns, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Scituate Town Beach: Relax and soak up the sun at Scituate’s Town Beach, a local favorite.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scituate

  • Tucker’s Trail: This moderate hiking trail offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape, crossing several streams and small waterfalls along the way.
  • The Scituate Loop Trail: A popular route for bikers, this 43-mile loop offers some of the most stunning views of Scituate’s natural beauty.
  • George Washington Management Area: This sprawling forested area offers several hiking trails ranging from easy to difficult, providing opportunities to discover the town’s stunning natural beauty up close.

Best Things to Do in Scituate: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on the Scituate Reservoir: Explore the stunning scenery of the Scituate Reservoir by embarking on a kayaking tour.
  • Golfing at Crystal Lake Golf Club: Take a swing at this challenging, yet beautiful, golf course, considered to be one of the best in Rhode Island.
  • Scituate Farmers Market: Visit the local farmers market to experience the best of Scituate’s fresh, seasonal produce.

Food and Drink Scene of Scituate

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: A classic New England dish, try Clam Chowder at local restaurants such as Belle’s Cafe and Blissful Bites.
  • Rhode Island-style Pizza Strips: A unique local specialty, these rectangular pizzas are topped with thick, tomato sauce and served cold.
  • Stuffies: Sample this delicious local dish, which consists of stuffed clams, at local hotspots such as Stuffed Sandwiches.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer: This beloved local brew is a staple at bars and restaurants across Scituate.
  • Del’s Lemonade: No visit to Scituate is complete without trying Del’s Lemonade, a Rhode Island classic that’s been served since 1948.

Where to Stay in Scituate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Old Scituate Meeting House: This charming guesthouse is housed in a 200-year-old colonial-era building and offers comfortable accommodations at affordable prices.
  • Country Chalet: This cozy bed and breakfast is situated in a beautiful, rural setting and offers a warm and welcoming atmosphere.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Providence/Smithfield: For those seeking modern amenities, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a range of convenient facilities.

What to Buy in Scituate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Print of Scituate: Bring home a unique souvenir of Scituate with a traditional print from local artist, Carolyn Vibbert.
  • Maple Syrup: Savor the sweet taste of Scituate with a bottle of locally produced maple syrup from Highland Farm.
  • Local Produce: Bring home the taste of Scituate’s fresh, seasonal produce with a selection of fruits, vegetables, and other local delicacies.

Local Culture and Customs of Scituate

Scituate is proud of its deep roots, which are reflected in its rich history, vibrant arts scene, and thriving local food culture. Visitors should expect warmth and hospitality from locals, who are happy to share their knowledge and love for the region with travelers.

Best Time to Visit Scituate

Scituate is beautiful year-round, with each season offering a unique charm. The fall, in particular, is a stunning time to visit, with the colorful foliage adding a breathtaking beauty to the already picturesque landscape.

Getting to Scituate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scituate

The nearest airports to Scituate are T.F. Green Airport in Providence, Rhode Island, and Boston Logan International Airport. Both airports offer car rental services and public transportation options.

Bus Stations Near Scituate

Greyhound offers several daily bus routes to and from Providence, Rhode Island, with connections to other destinations in North America.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Scituate by car via Interstate 295. Several car rental companies operate in the area, offering reliable and affordable transportation options.

How to Get Around in Scituate

The best way to get around Scituate is by car. The town offers several car rental services, with options to suit all budgets. Additionally, several companies offer guided tours of the area, providing a convenient way to explore the local sights and attractions.
