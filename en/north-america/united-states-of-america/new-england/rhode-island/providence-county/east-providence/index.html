Introduction to East Providence

East Providence, located in Providence County, Rhode Island, is a bustling city known for its rich history, scenic views, lively cultural scene, and exciting outdoor adventures. This beautiful city, located in the heart of New England, offers a perfect blend of old-world charm and modern amenities, making it a top destination for tourists from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in East Providence

Whether you are an art enthusiast, a history buff, an avid hiker, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, East Providence has something for everyone. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Crescent Park Looff Carousel - one of the oldest carousels in the country, this historic carousel is a must-visit for all travelers.
  • John Hunt House Museum - a beautiful historic house built in 1750, showcasing the history of East Providence and the surrounding areas.
  • Rumford Historic District - a charming historic neighborhood with picturesque homes and beautiful architecture.
  • Pawtucket Arts District - the creative hub of the city, featuring galleries, studios, theaters, and live performances.
  • The Providence Athenaeum - a stunning library filled with rare books, artwork, and beautiful architecture.
  • The East Providence Historical Society Museum - a museum dedicated to preserving the history of East Providence.

Natural Attractions of East Providence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pierce Field - a beautiful park that offers stunning views of the Providence River.
  • Haines Memorial State Park - an idyllic park that is perfect for picnics, hiking, and birdwatching.
  • Ten Mile River Greenway - a scenic hiking trail that winds through East Providence and nearby towns.
  • Crescent Park - a beautiful park overlooking the Providence River, with stunning views and a variety of recreational activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Providence

East Providence offers a plethora of outdoor adventures, perfect for the nature lover looking for a scenic and active vacation. Here are some of the best trails for hiking and biking in the area:

  • The East Bay Bike Path - a 14-mile trail that winds through scenic vistas and beautiful small towns.
  • Blackstone River Bikeway - a 11-mile trail that follows the scenic Blackstone River, from Kelly House in Lincoln to Independence Park in Bristol.
  • Ten Mile River Greenway - a scenic trail that winds through East Providence and nearby towns, perfect for biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in East Providence: Activities and Tours

From museums to parks to restaurants, there is no shortage of things to do in East Providence. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider when visiting the city:

  • WaterFire Providence - a breathtaking spectacle of bonfires and music, set on the three rivers of downtown Providence.
  • Rhode Island Red Food Tours - a food tour that takes visitors to the best local restaurants and eateries in the city.
  • RISD Museum - a world-renowned museum showcasing a range of art, design, and historical artifacts.
  • Farm Fresh Rhode Island Market - a seasonal farmers market featuring fresh, local produce, and artisanal goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Providence

If you have a week to spend in East Providence, here’s a suggested itinerary that will ensure you don’t miss the top attractions and experiences:

Days Activities
Day 1 Arrive in East Providence and settle into your hotel. Take a stroll through the historic Rumford district and visit the John Hunt House Museum.
Day 2 Take a bike ride on the scenic East Bay Bike Path, stopping in the charismatic town of Warren.
Day 3 Visit the RISD Museum and explore the art and design exhibits.
Day 4 Go on a food tour with Rhode Island Red Food Tours, and sample the best of the local cuisine.
Day 5 Take a tour of the local wineries and breweries.
Day 6 Spend the day exploring the parks and trails in East Providence, including Pierce Field and the Ten Mile River Greenway.
Day 7 Relax and recharge at the Crescent Park Looff Carousel, one of the oldest carousels in the country.

Food and Drink Scene of East Providence

East Providence is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, featuring local cuisine, craft beer, and fine wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam cakes and chowder - a classic Rhode Island dish that is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Stuffies - a local delicacy made with quahogs, bread crumbs, and a variety of seasonings.
  • Grinders - a sandwich made with a variety of meats, cheeses, and vegetables, served on fresh bread.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer - a local favorite, this beer has been brewed in Rhode Island since 1890.
  • Yacht Club Soda - a refreshing soda made with natural flavors and pure cane sugar.
  • Newport Vineyards - a winery that produces a range of wines, including red, white, and rose options.

Where to Stay in East Providence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Providence offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options to consider:

  • Hampton Inn by Hilton - a comfortable and affordable hotel located in the heart of East Providence.
  • Providence Marriott Downtown - a luxurious hotel with stunning views of the city skyline.
  • Hilton Providence - a stylish and modern hotel located next to the Rhode Island Convention Center.

What to Buy in East Providence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without souvenirs to bring home. East Providence offers a range of unique and locally made products, including:

  • Lockwood House Antiques - a charming antique shop with a variety of vintage and unique finds.
  • Frog and Toad - a quirky gift shop featuring local artists and makers.
  • Granny Squibb’s Iced Tea - a refreshing and delicious iced tea made with natural ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of East Providence

East Providence is a lively and welcoming city, with a strong sense of community and local pride. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Friendliness - locals in East Providence are known for their warm and welcoming nature, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation.
  • Seafood - seafood is a big part of the local cuisine, so be sure to try some of the fresh catches during your visit.
  • Outdoor Adventures - with so many stunning parks and hiking trails, it’s no surprise that outdoor activities are a big part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit East Providence

The best time to visit East Providence is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events. However, travel during the off-season can also offer smaller crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Providence

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip, it’s important to keep in mind some do’s and don’ts when visiting East Providence.

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and cuisine.
  • Do explore the parks and trails in the area.
  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t be afraid to interact with locals and ask for recommendations.
  • Don’t forget to try some of the local beverages, such as Narragansett Beer or Yacht Club Soda.

Safety and Health

East Providence is generally a safe and healthy destination, but it’s always important to be mindful of your surroundings and take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in East Providence

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and securely stored.
  • Use caution when swimming or boating in bodies of water.

Health in East Providence

  • Bring sunscreen and bug spray for outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.
  • Pack any necessary medication and consult with a doctor before traveling.

Getting to East Providence: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Providence is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near East Providence

  • T.F. Green Airport (PVD) - located in Warwick, RI, about a 20-minute drive from East Providence.
  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS) - located in Boston, MA, about a 1-hour drive from East Providence.

Bus Stations Near East Providence

  • Peter Pan Bus Lines - offers daily service to East Providence from a variety of locations.
  • Greyhound - offers daily service to Providence, located about 10 minutes from East Providence.

Other Options

  • Amtrak - offers daily train service to Providence, located about 10 minutes from East Providence.
  • Rental Cars - a variety of rental car companies are available for those who prefer to drive.

How to Get Around in East Providence

East Providence is easily navigable by car, bike, or foot. Renting a car is the most convenient option for those without their own transportation, and there are a variety of car rental companies located throughout the city. Biking and walking are also popular options, with many trails and bike lanes available.

Where to Go Next from East Providence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East Providence is located in the heart of New England, making it a great starting point for trips to surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Newport, RI - a picturesque seaside town featuring beautiful beaches, mansions, and sailing.
  • Boston, MA - a historic city known for its museums, universities, and sports teams.
  • Cape Cod, MA - a scenic peninsula known for its pristine beaches, lighthouses, and seafood.

Conclusion

East Providence is a charming and vibrant city, with a unique mix of history, culture, and nature. Whether you are a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Plan your next trip to East Providence and experience all the city has to offer!
