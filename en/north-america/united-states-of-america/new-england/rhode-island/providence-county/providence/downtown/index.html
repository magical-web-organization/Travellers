Introduction to Downtown

Downtown Providence, located in Providence County, Rhode Island, is a vibrant city hub that’s home to various cultural and recreational attractions. This bustling district is filled with historic buildings, museums, galleries, and boasts of a lively food and drink scene, all within easy reach.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

With so much to see and do, it can be overwhelming to plan an itinerary for your trip to Downtown Providence. Here are some of the top places to add to your list.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rhode Island State House – This grand building features neoclassical architecture and is the home of the Rhode Island General Assembly. Visitors can take a free tour of the State House on weekdays.
  • Providence Athenaeum – A historic library established in 1836, the Providence Athenaeum is home to over 200,000 books, dozens of artifacts and memorabilia, and rotating art exhibitions.
  • RISD Museum – This esteemed art museum features over 100,000 works from artists across the globe. The museum has a diverse collection that covers various time periods and genres.
  • Benefit Street – A picturesque, cobblestoned street in downtown Providence that is home to numerous historic landmarks, such as Barrington Court and the Providence Preservation Society. Visitors can take a walking tour to discover the full history of this beautiful street.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • WaterFire – A public art event that takes place on certain weekends throughout the year. Visitors can catch a glimpse of over 80 smaller fires lighting up the downtown rivers that run through Providence.
  • Roger Williams Park – A 430-acre park with plenty of hiking trails, gardens, and a lake where visitors can rent paddleboats and kayaks for a fun day out.
  • India Point Park – A scenic green space situated alongside the river. This park is an ideal spot for picnic-ing, sunbathing, or walking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

If you’re an adventure enthusiast, you’ll love exploring Downtown Providence. Here are some routes to check out:

  • East Bay Bike Path – A 14.5-mile paved trail that runs from Providence to Bristol. The trail is suitable for bikers of all skill levels and offers breathtaking views of the Narragansett Bay.
  • Blackstone River Greenway – A 48-mile trail that stretches from Worcester, Massachusetts, to Providence. The trail runs alongside the Blackstone River and is considered one of the most scenic biking routes in the Northeast.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

  • Providence Ghost Tour – A walking tour of Downtown Providence where visitors can hear sinister stories of hauntings and supernatural events that are said to have occurred in the area.
  • Downtown Providence Art and Cocktail Walk – A nighttime tour of Downtown Providence that explores the city’s art scene and includes stops at different bars and eateries.
  • Providence Kayak Company – Visitors can rent kayaks and paddleboards to explore the Providence River and nearby parks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Downtown

Day 1: Visit the Rhode Island State House and Benefit Street Day 2: Explore the RISD Museum and Garden of the Providence Athenaeum Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Blackstone River Greenway Day 4: Discover the Roger Williams Park Botanical Center and Zoo Day 5: Tour Downtown Providence’s culinary scene with a food and drink tour. Day 6: Go kayaking on the WaterFire river or visit India Point Park. Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the local markets and boutiques

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stuffies – Rhode Island’s signature dish is stuffed quahogs, which are usually served as an appetizer.
  • Hot Weiners – A common street food in Rhode Island, hot weiners are flavorful hot dogs that are typically served with meat sauce, onions, and mustard.
  • Clam Chowder – A New England staple, this creamy soup made with clams and potatoes can be found on almost every menu.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee Milk – A Rhode Island drink made by mixing coffee syrup with milk.
  • Del’s Lemonade – A beloved Rhode Island staple, Del’s Lemonade is a sweet and tangy slushie made with fresh lemon juice.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown Providence has many accommodation options that cater to a range of budget types. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hotel Providence – A upscale boutique hotel that features elegant guest rooms and suites.
  • Graduate Providence – A quirky hotel situated in a historic building that features unique design elements and modern amenities.
  • Biltmore Hotel – A luxurious hotel with spacious rooms and suites that offer sweeping views of Providence from their balconies and windows.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silver Jewelry – There are many jewelry retailers in Downtown Providence that specialize in handcrafted silver jewelry.
  • Local Art – From galleries to street art, Downtown Providence is teeming with art pieces that reflect the local culture. There are plenty of art shops that sell various prints and paintings by local artists.
  • Coffee Syrup – You won’t find this sweet syrup anywhere else. Bring home a bottle of coffee syrup to enjoy Rhode Island’s signature drink – coffee milk.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Rhode Island locals are welcoming and engaging and are proud of their state’s rich history and culture. The locals value family and community, and it’s common to see multi-generational families spending time together participating in various community events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown Providence is between May and September when the weather is warm, and there are many outdoor events and festivals happening throughout the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Explore the city on foot or bike.
  • Try Rhode Island’s local dishes.
  • Visit the local art galleries and museums.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Drive aggressively or recklessly.
  • Ignore local cultural traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Providence is a safe city; however, visitors should take general safety precautions while in the city. Avoid walking in poorly lit areas at night and try not to display expensive items such as cameras and jewelry.

Health in Downtown

Visitors to Downtown Providence are encouraged to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen if planning outdoor activities. There are several hospitals located in the city in case of a medical emergency.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

  • T.F. Green Airport (PVD) – 10 miles south of downtown Providence.
  • Logan International Airport (BOS) – 50 miles north of Providence.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

  • Peter Pan Bus Station – A bus station located in the heart of Downtown Providence and is well-connected to various other cities in New England.

Other Options

  • Amtrak – Providence Station is serviced by Amtrak and provides transportation to various parts of the country.
  • Car Rentals – There are several car rental options in Downtown Providence that provide easy access to nearby attractions.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Downtown Providence is a small area that can be easily explored on foot, by bike, or by using public transportation. There are several bus routes that connect Downtown Providence to other parts of the Providence metro area.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newport – This small coastal town is renowned for its historic mansions and beautiful beaches.
  • Cape Cod – A popular summer destination that is famous for its beaches, outdoor activities, and picturesque lighthouses.
  • Boston – The capital of Massachusetts is a mere 45-minute drive from Downtown Providence and is home to many historic sites, museums, and galleries.

Conclusion

Downtown Providence is an excellent destination for history buffs, adventure enthusiasts, and foodies alike. The district is home to a range of cultural and recreational attractions, and visitors are sure to find something that piques their interests. Whether you’re exploring historic landmarks or dining at local eateries, Downtown Providence is a city that’s filled with surprises and is guaranteed to make your vacation memorable.
