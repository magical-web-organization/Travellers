Introduction to North and East

Welcome to North and East, a charming neighborhood located in Providence, Rhode Island, in the heart of New England. This vibrant community is rich in history and culture, with a plethora of exciting attractions and things to do for visitors of all ages and interests. From museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and scenic hikes, there is something for everyone in North and East.

List of Places to Visit in North and East

Here are some of the must-see destinations in North and East:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Natural History and Planetarium: Featuring exhibits on local history, geology, and astronomy, this museum is perfect for those interested in science and nature.
  • RISD Museum: As one of the oldest art institutions in the country and a part of the Rhode Island School of Design, the RISD Museum boasts a vast collection of art pieces from around the world.
  • John Brown House Museum: A National Historic Landmark, the John Brown House Museum is a must-visit for those interested in learning about colonial history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of North and East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Waterplace Park: A beautiful park located in downtown Providence, Waterplace Park is perfect for an afternoon stroll or picnic. During the summer months, the park is home to WaterFire, a popular event that features music, food, and bonfires on the river.
  • Blackstone River Bikeway: This 18-mile trail offers scenic views of the Blackstone River and passes through several charming towns. Whether you’re a seasoned biker or a casual rider, this trail is a great way to experience the beauty of Rhode Island’s natural landscape.
  • India Point Park: Another excellent park in Providence, India Point Park offers stunning views of the Narragansett Bay and is a popular spot for fishing, picnicking, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North and East

In addition to the Blackstone River Bikeway mentioned above, North and East offers several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Ten Mile River Greenway: This 3.6-mile trail runs through several towns and offers beautiful views of the Ten Mile River.
  • Neutaconkanut Hill Park: Located in the heart of Providence, this park offers several hiking trails that lead to stunning views of the city and surrounding areas.
  • Wanskuck Park: Another great park located in Providence, Wanskuck Park features several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for those looking for a more challenging workout.

Best Things to Do in North and East: Activities and Tours

Aside from the attractions and trails mentioned above, North and East offers several other exciting activities and tours:

  • Providence Ghost Tour: Discover the spooky side of Rhode Island by taking a ghost tour of Providence. The tour takes you to several haunted locations around the city and includes chilling stories about their history.
  • Rhode Island Brew Bus: If you’re a beer enthusiast, then a Rhode Island Brew Bus tour is a must-do. The tour takes you to several local breweries, where you can sample some of the best craft beers in the area.
  • Providence Riverboat Company: Take a relaxing boat tour of the Providence River and enjoy stunning views of the city’s skyline.

Food and Drink Scene of North and East

Rhode Island is known for its delicious seafood and unique culinary traditions, and North and East is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam chowder: A New England classic, clam chowder is a creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, onions, and bacon.
  • Stuffies: A local favorite, stuffies are stuffed clams that are seasoned with herbs, breadcrumbs, and spice.
  • Coffee milk: A popular drink in Rhode Island, coffee milk is made by mixing coffee syrup with milk.

Local Beverages

  • Narragansett Beer: A classic Rhode Island beer, Narragansett is a crisp and refreshing lager that pairs well with seafood.
  • Del’s Lemonade: A Rhode Island tradition, Del’s lemonade is a frozen drink made with fresh squeezed lemons and sugar.
  • Autocrat Coffee Syrup: Used to make coffee milk, Autocrat coffee syrup is a sweet and delicious syrup that is a favorite among Rhode Islanders.

Where to Stay in North and East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North and East offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Dean Hotel: Located in downtown Providence, The Dean is a boutique hotel that offers stylish rooms and excellent amenities.
  • HI Providence Hostel: A budget-friendly option, HI Providence Hostel offers clean and comfortable dorm-style rooms and is perfect for solo travelers looking to meet new people.
  • Christopher Dodge House Bed & Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast located in a historic mansion, Christopher Dodge House offers cozy rooms and a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in North and East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some local products and souvenirs from North and East, here are some great options:

  • Rhode Island Red hot sauce: Made with locally sourced ingredients and a blend of spices, Rhode Island Red hot sauce is a delicious and spicy sauce that makes a great gift.
  • Handmade pottery: Many local artisans in North and East create beautiful handmade pottery that is both unique and functional.
  • Gerald’s Farm maple syrup: Located in nearby Exeter, Rhode Island, Gerald’s Farm produces some of the best maple syrup in the area and is a great local product to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of North and East

Visitors to North and East are encouraged to participate in these events and get to know the locals, who are known for their friendliness and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit North and East

The best time to visit North and East is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the parks and outdoor spaces are in full bloom. However, Rhode Island’s fall foliage is also a stunning sight to behold, with vibrant colors and cozy sweaters. Winter in North and East can be cold and snowy, but it’s also a magical time of year, with holiday lights and festivities throughout the city.

Getting to North and East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North and East

  • T.F. Green Airport: Located in Warwick, Rhode Island, T.F. Green Airport is the closest airport to North and East and offers several daily flights to major cities throughout the United States.

Bus Stations Near North and East

  • Providence Station: Located in downtown Providence, Providence Station offers Greyhound and Megabus services to several major cities throughout the East Coast.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The Providence Station also serves as an Amtrak hub, with regular trains running between Providence and Boston, New York, and other major cities.
