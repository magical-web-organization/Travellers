A Comprehensive Travel Guide to North Smithfield, Rhode Island

North Smithfield is a charming town located in Providence County, Rhode Island. The town is known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and rich history. If you are planning a visit to North Smithfield, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your trip.

Introduction to North Smithfield

North Smithfield is a town in the northwestern part of Rhode Island. The town has a total area of 24.7 square miles and a population of approximately 12,000 residents. The town has a rich history dating back to the 17th century, and it was once a thriving mill town.

Today, North Smithfield is a peaceful town with a lot to offer visitors. The town is home to many parks, trails, and outdoor recreational areas. North Smithfield is also known for its museums, historical landmarks, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in North Smithfield

If you are planning a visit to North Smithfield, there are many places to explore in the town and its surrounding areas. Here are some of the top attractions to put on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Slatersville Historic District: The Slatersville Historic District is a historic mill village and is considered the birthplace of America’s Industrial Revolution. Visitors can take a self-guided tour of the village and learn about the history of the industrial revolution.
  • Forestdale Schoolhouse Museum: The Forestdale Schoolhouse Museum is a beautifully restored one-room schoolhouse from the early 20th century. Visitors can step back in time and learn about what school was like in the past.
  • Heritage Hall Museum: The Heritage Hall Museum is an interactive museum featuring exhibits that showcase the history of the town.

Natural Attractions of North Smithfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blackstone River and Canal Heritage State Park: The Blackstone River and Canal Heritage State Park is a 1,000-acre park that features hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the Blackstone River and Canal.
  • Powder Mill Ledges Wildlife Refuge: The Powder Mill Ledges Wildlife Refuge is a 100-acre refuge that features hiking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and some of the most stunning scenery in North Smithfield.
  • Lincoln Woods State Park: The Lincoln Woods State Park is a 627-acre park that features a freshwater beach, hiking and biking trails, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Smithfield

North Smithfield has many trails and paths that are perfect for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Blackstone River Bikeway: The Blackstone River Bikeway is a 48-mile bike trail that runs from Worcester, Massachusetts to Providence, Rhode Island. The section of the trail that runs through North Smithfield is particularly scenic.
  • Pachaug Trail: The Pachaug Trail is a 35-mile trail that runs through Rhode Island and Connecticut. The Rhode Island section of the trail runs through North Smithfield and is a popular hiking spot.

Best Things to Do in North Smithfield: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, North Smithfield has many other activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Farm tours: North Smithfield is home to many farms that offer tours and activities. Visitors can go apple picking, see farm animals, and enjoy local produce.
  • Tour the wineries and breweries: Rhode Island is known for its vineyards and microbreweries, and North Smithfield is no exception. Visitors can take tours and sample local wines and beers.
  • Fishing: North Smithfield is home to many bodies of water that are great for fishing, including the Blackstone River and its tributaries.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Smithfield

If you are planning a week-long visit to North Smithfield, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Slatersville Historic District and take a self-guided tour of the village.
  • Day 2: Explore the Powder Mill Ledges Wildlife Refuge and hike one of its many trails.
  • Day 3: Visit the Forestdale Schoolhouse Museum and learn about the history of education in North Smithfield.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of one of North Smithfield’s many farms and enjoy a day of apple picking and other agricultural activities.
  • Day 5: Explore the Blackstone River and Canal Heritage State Park and enjoy a picnic by the river.
  • Day 6: Take a tour of a local winery or brewery and sample some local wines or beers.
  • Day 7: Spend the day fishing on one of North Smithfield’s many bodies of water.

Food and Drink Scene of North Smithfield

North Smithfield is known for its delicious cuisine and local flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot wieners: Hot wieners are a Rhode Island specialty. They are small hot dogs served in a steamed bun and topped with mustard, onions, and celery salt.
  • Clam cakes: Clam cakes are a type of fritter made with chopped clams and batter. They are a popular snack in Rhode Island.
  • Stuffies: Stuffies are stuffed clams that are baked in the oven. They are a New England classic and a must-try in North Smithfield.

Local Beverages

  • Del’s frozen lemonade: Del’s frozen lemonade is a Rhode Island classic. It is an icy lemon drink that is perfect for hot summer days.
  • Coffee milk: Coffee milk is a sweetened milk flavored with coffee syrup. It is a staple drink in Rhode Island.
  • Newport Storm beer: Newport Storm is a local brewery in Rhode Island that produces a range of beers and ales.

Local Culture and Customs of North Smithfield

North Smithfield is a friendly town with a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s history and customs. Rhode Island is known for its casual attire, so visitors should dress comfortably and casually during their visit.

Best Time to Visit North Smithfield

North Smithfield is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months. The weather is perfect for outdoor activities, and the town comes alive with festivals and events. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, as the foliage in Rhode Island is stunning.

Where to Go Next from North Smithfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion
