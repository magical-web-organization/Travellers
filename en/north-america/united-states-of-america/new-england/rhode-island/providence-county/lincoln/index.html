Introduction to Lincoln

Located in Providence County, Lincoln is a charming town in Rhode Island. It offers a combination of historical sites, natural beauty, and fantastic outdoor activities for adventurers. With friendly locals, this town is a must-visit destination in New England.

List of Places to Visit in Lincoln

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of Work and Culture - This museum provides a glimpse of the lives and struggles of French-Canadian immigrants in Rhode Island. It has interactive exhibits and artifacts that showcase the cultural history of the town.
  2. Eleazer Arnold House - A historic house museum from the 1600s, it reflects the architectural styles of the Colonial era. This National Historic Landmark is worth a visit for history buffs.
  3. Hearthside House Museum - This Victorian-era mansion is a beautiful example of the affluent lifestyle of the 1800s. It offers tours to visitors and hosts special events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Lincoln: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Blackstone River Bikeway - This 18-mile-long path is perfect for cyclists, walkers, and runners. It offers scenic views of the Blackstone River, wildlife, and natural beauty.
  2. Chase Farm Park - A picturesque open space with walking trails, pavilions, and picnic areas, it is an excellent spot for a family outing. The park also hosts events and festivals throughout the year, such as the Lincoln Farmers’ Market in the summer.
  3. Mowry Conservation Area - This 360-acre park offers hiking trails, fishing ponds, and birdwatching spots. It is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lincoln

  1. Lincoln Woods State Park - This 627-acre park is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and fishing. It has numerous trails of varying difficulty levels, including the challenging Ridge Trail.
  2. Blackstone River Bikeway - This path runs through multiple towns and offers scenic views of the river, waterfalls, and old mills. It is an ideal route for cycling.

Best Things to Do in Lincoln: Activities and Tours

  1. Escobar’s Highland Farm - An Alpaca and Llama farm, it is an excellent place for animal lovers and families with children. Visitors can pet the animals, take tours, and buy locally-made products from the gift shop.
  2. Twin River Casino - A popular destination for gambling enthusiasts, this casino has over 4,000 slot machines and 92 table games. It also hosts live music and entertainment events.
  3. New England Air Museum - A fascinating museum that showcases the history of aviation in New England. It has a collection of vintage aircraft, engines, and other artifacts.

Food and Drink Scene of Lincoln

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Rhode Island Clam Chowder - A local specialty soup made with fresh clams, potatoes, onions, and herbs. Served with oyster crackers, it is a delicious comfort food.
  2. Stuffies - A popular appetizer in Rhode Island, it is made with stuffed quahog clams, breadcrumbs, and spices. It is a must-try dish for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  1. Rhode Island Red - A signature cocktail of the state, it is made with vodka, amaretto, and cranberry juice. It is a refreshing drink perfect for a summer evening.
  2. Narragansett Beer - A local beer that has been popular since the 1800s, it is brewed in Rhode Island and has a distinct taste.

Where to Stay in Lincoln: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hampton Inn & Suites Providence/Smithfield - A top-rated hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.
  2. Woonsocket Motor Inn - An affordable hotel that provides basic amenities and is located close to major attractions.
  3. Airbnb Rentals - Various properties in Lincoln and the surrounding areas are available for rent on Airbnb, providing travelers with more affordable and unique accommodations.

What to Buy in Lincoln: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Alpaca Wool Products - You can buy hand-made items such as scarves, hats, and gloves made from alpaca wool at Escobar’s Highland Farm.
  2. Green Olive Soaps - A local company that produces all-natural soaps and fragrances. They use locally-sourced materials and offer unique scents and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln

Rhode Islanders are proud of their heritage and the state’s contributions to American history. They value family and community, and locals are known for their friendliness and welcoming attitudes.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln

The best time to visit Lincoln is during the summer and fall months, from June to October. The weather is pleasant, and many outdoor activities are available. The fall foliage is particularly beautiful in New England.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln

Do:

  1. Talk to locals and ask for recommendations.
  2. Observe pedestrian and traffic rules as they are strictly enforced.
  3. Take care of the natural sites and clean up after yourself.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or engage in any activity that can harm the environment.
  2. Ignore traffic laws or attempt to park in restricted areas.
  3. Disrespect the cultural heritage of the town and state.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lincoln

Lincoln is a safe town overall, and the crime rate is relatively low. However, visitors should take normal precautions such as not leaving valuables in parked cars and avoiding poorly-lit areas at night.

Health in Lincoln

Lincoln has several hospitals and medical clinics that offer medical services to the public. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling to the town.

Getting to Lincoln: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lincoln

  1. T.F. Green Airport - A primary airport in Rhode Island, located about 20 miles south of Lincoln.
  2. Logan International Airport - A larger airport located in Boston, Massachusetts, around 50 miles northeast of Lincoln.

Bus Stations Near Lincoln

  1. Peter Pan Bus Lines - A major bus company that services several locations in Rhode Island, including Lincoln.
  2. Greyhound Bus Lines - Another bus company that travels to many destinations across the United States.

Other Options

  1. Amtrak Train Station - Located in downtown Providence, it offers access to several cities in the Northeast.
  2. Car Rentals - Several car rental companies operate in Lincoln and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Lincoln

It is recommended to have a car to get around in Lincoln as public transportation is limited. Taxis and ride-hailing services such as Uber and Lyft are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Lincoln: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Providence - Rhode Island’s capital city, known for its art, culture, and cuisine. It is located about 20 minutes south of Lincoln.
  2. Newport - A coastal town famous for its mansions, beaches, and maritime history. It is located around 50 miles southeast of Lincoln.
  3. Boston - Known as the “Cradle of Liberty,” Boston is a historic city that played a critical role in the American Revolution. It is located around 50 miles northeast of Lincoln.

Conclusion

Lincoln, Rhode Island, is an excellent destination for visitors who want to experience the state’s natural beauty, rich history, and culture. A combination of outdoor adventures, museums, and delicious food make Lincoln a must-visit destination in New England. Whether you’re a history buff or a nature lover, Lincoln has something to offer to everyone.
