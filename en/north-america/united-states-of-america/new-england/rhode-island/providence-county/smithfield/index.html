Introduction to Smithfield

Smithfield is a charming township located in Providence County, Rhode Island in New England. The town is proud of its remarkable history, with an abundance of historic landmarks, museums, and outdoor adventures. Smithfield is a perfect destination for those who love to explore and immerse themselves in local culture and enjoy nature.

List of Places to Visit in Smithfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Smith-Appleby House Museum: A historical landmark built in 1696, the house now serves as a museum featuring colonial artifacts and exhibits related to the history of Smithfield.
  • Mowry House: This historic Colonial-era house museum is a testament to the rich history of Smithfield.
  • Bryant University: Founded in 1863, the University has evolved into a diverse and globally recognized educational institution.
  • Governor Sprague Mansion: Originally built in 1790, this mansion was home to two Rhode Island governors and now serves as a museum filled with antique furniture and decorative arts.

Natural Attractions of Smithfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Georgiaville Pond: A serene and peaceful freshwater lake perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Esmond-Cassel Preserve: This protected area has trails that pass through wetlands, forests, and streams. The preserve is ideal for a nature walk and birdwatching.
  • Blackstone River Greenway: A scenic and flat multi-use trail perfect for cycling, walking, and jogging.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smithfield

  • Bike Path: Take a scenic bicycle ride on the Blackstone River Bike Path, which stretches almost 11 miles from Woonsocket to Cumberland.
  • Wolf Hill Forest Preserve: This picturesque nature spot is ideal for a peaceful and scenic hike. There are many hiking trails to explore, and the scenic views from the top are breathtaking.
  • Woonasquatucket River Greenway: A beautiful and scenic trail that passes through forests and farmland, alongside the Woonasquatucket River.

Best Things to Do in Smithfield: Activities and Tours

  • Ice Skating: Try ice-skating at the Smithfield Municipal Ice Rink. The facility is clean, and there are various levels of skills for all ages.
  • Driving Range: Practice your swing at the Pinecrest Golf Club. They offer a driving range and golf lessons.
  • Water Sports: Enjoy kayaking, paddleboarding, and canoeing at Georgiaville Pond.
  • Haunted Boat Tours: Experience the eerie beauty of the historic town on a special Haunted Boat Tour the narrates the murky myths and legends of Smithfield.
  • Farmers Market: Visit the weekly farmers’ market at Smithfield Town Hall, which offers fresh produce and farm products.

Food and Drink Scene of Smithfield

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot wieners: This is an iconic Rhode Island staple - grilled hot-dog meat, minced onions, mustard, and celery salt, served on a steamed bun.
  • Famous Clam chowder: Made fresh with local clams, creamy potatoes, and crisp bacon chunks.
  • Doughnuts from Allie’s: A family-owned business famous for unique and delectable doughnut flavors, including grapefruit, blueberry cake, and Boston cream.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer at Whalers Brewing Company: A great place for craft beer enthusiasts, with a range of signature brews and seasonal blends.
  • Maple Syrup at Chepachet Farms: Experience homestead maple syrup at the Chepachet Farms - 2021 State Fair Award Winners.

Where to Stay in Smithfield: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Providence/Smithfield: Situated in the heart of Smithfield, with a fitness center, outdoor pool, and free breakfast.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Smithfield: Offers cozy and spacious rooms, free breakfast, and an indoor pool.
  • Dora’s Dream Airbnb: This cozy and serene cottage offers scenic views of the Woonasquatucket River and is perfect for an intimate weekend escape.

What to Buy in Smithfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artwork by local artists: Visit the local artists’ studios in the town and take home creative and locally crafted artworks.
  • Chepachet Farms products: Take home some of the farm’s infamous maple syrup, honey, or goat’s milk soap.

Local Culture and Customs of Smithfield

The people of Smithfield are warm, welcoming, and proud of their hometown. The locals respect their community’s history and culture and work towards preserving it with immense pride.

Best Time to Visit Smithfield

The best time to visit Smithfield is from May to September, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Smithfield

Do:

  • Respect the town’s historical significance and culture.
  • Take advantage of the town’s parks and nature zones.
  • Try the local seafood cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the town’s wildlife.
  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Smithfield

Smithfield is a safe community, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always take commonsense precautions like locking their cars, staying aware of their surroundings, and following traffic rules.

Health in Smithfield

Visitors should be up-to-date on their vaccinations, keep hand sanitizer and maintain social distancing as needed.

Getting to Smithfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Smithfield

  • T.F. Green Airport: Located 15 miles southeast of Smithfield, the airport offers various car rental services in addition to shuttle and taxi services.

Bus Stations Near Smithfield

  • Peter Pan Bus Station, Providence: A bus station that can connect to other stations in neighboring states like Boston or Hartford.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Trains: The nearest Amtrak station is in Providence.

How to Get Around in Smithfield

  • Bicycle: Biking could be a great option to move around the town since the streets are bike-friendly.
  • Car rental: Rental cars can be rented from Providence and T.F. Green Airport.
  • Uber and Lyft: Uber and Lyft transportation services are available in Smithfield.

Where to Go Next from Smithfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Boston: The capital of Massachusetts is a short hour drive away from Smithfield.
  • Newport: Known for its abundance of historic mansions, waterfront views, and charming oceanside towns, Newport is a 50-minute drive from Smithfield.
  • Providence: The capital of Rhode Island is just a few miles away.

Conclusion

Smithfield is a historical gem, and thanks to its abundance of natural attractions, it offers visitors the unique opportunity to experience the natural beauty and rich history of Rhode Island. Whether you’re there for culture, food, or the great outdoors, you’re sure to find plenty to delight you in Smithfield.
