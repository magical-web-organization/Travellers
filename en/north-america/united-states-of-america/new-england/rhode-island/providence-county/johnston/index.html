Introduction to Johnston

Johnston is a charming town located in Providence County, Rhode Island, in the United States of America. Known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty, Johnston is a popular tourist destination that offers a wide range of activities and attractions for travelers of all ages.

Whether you’re looking to explore its historic landmarks, enjoy outdoor adventures, or simply relax and take in the local culture, Johnston has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Johnston

Located in the heart of New England, Johnston is home to a number of exciting attractions and points of interest. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Johnston Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving Johnston’s rich history and features exhibits on the town’s early settlers, local industry, and more.

  • Clemence Irons House: Built in 1691, this historic house is a great example of Colonial architecture and offers a glimpse into early American life.

  • Governor Sprague Mansion Museum: This stately mansion was built in the 1790s and is now home to an impressive collection of antiques, artwork, and other historic artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Johnston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Snake Den State Park: This picturesque park is a popular spot for hiking, fishing, and picnicking. Its forests also make for some scenic views during autumn.

  • Johnston Memorial Park: Enjoy walking along the trails and wooded areas while also indulging in activities like paddle boating, tennis, and basketball.

  • Waterman Lake: This beautiful lake is an ideal spot for swimming, fishing, or simply relaxing on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Johnston

Johnston offers a range of hiking and biking routes for those who enjoy exploring the outdoors. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Bryant University Loop Trail: This 2.5 mile hiking/biking trail goes through the beautiful wooded areas behind Bryant University.

  • Johnston Memorial Park Trail: A 1.4 mile loop that takes you around the Johnston Memorial Park.

  • Snake Den State Park Trails: 7.2 miles of trails with varying degrees of difficulty and some amazing vistas and views.

Best Things to Do in Johnston: Activities and Tours

From outdoor activities to cultural experiences, Johnston offers a diverse range of things to do. Here are some of the best:

  • Take a food tour: With its rich culinary scene, Johnston offers a variety of food tours that let you sample everything from local seafood to artisanal chocolates.

  • Explore the town’s art scene: Johnston has a vibrant arts community, with galleries and events that showcase the work of local artists.

  • Take in a show: Check out one of Johnston’s theaters to see Broadway-caliber productions, performances by local musicians, or comedy shows.

Week-Long Itinerary for Johnston

Looking for a week-long adventure in Johnston? Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Johnston Historical Society Museum and Governor Sprague Mansion Museum.

Day 2: Take a hike at Snake Den State Park, followed by a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Spend the day exploring the town’s art scene, with visits to galleries and studios.

Day 4: Take a food tour to sample some of Johnston’s best cuisine.

Day 5: Go fishing at Waterman Lake in the morning, then spend the afternoon kayaking.

Day 6: Take in a show at one of Johnston’s theaters.

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at Johnston Memorial Park, enjoying activities like paddle boating and tennis.

Food and Drink Scene of Johnston

Johnston’s culinary scene is famous for its seafood, chocolate and coffee, but the town also has a lot to offer in terms of vegetarian-friendly options, Italian cuisine, and fusion dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stuffies: This classic Rhode Island dish features stuffed quahog clams with a delicious breadcrumb filling.

  • Pizza Strips: A Johnston specialty, these crispy and savory strips of pizza are a local favorite.

  • Coffee Milk: The official state drink of Rhode Island, coffee milk is a sweet and creamy beverage made with coffee syrup and milk.

Local Beverages

  • Harvest Pumpkin Ale: A seasonal ale that is brewed locally, this beer is a delicious way to celebrate the fall season.

  • Narragansett Beer: A Rhode Island staple, Narragansett beer is a refreshing and classic brew.

  • Rhody Coyote Coffee: A local coffee roaster that sources its beans from around the world and serves them up fresh for each cup.

Where to Stay in Johnston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Johnston offers a range of accommodations to suit any budget or preference. Here are some of the best options:

  • TownePlace Suites Providence North Kingstown: A modern hotel offering apartment-style rooms with fully equipped kitchens.

  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites Warwick-Providence (Airport): This hotel offers a range of amenities, including an indoor pool, fitness center, and free breakfast.

  • Old Court Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms decorated in a classic New England style.

What to Buy in Johnston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some great options:

  • Chocolate Delicacy: This gourmet chocolate store features hand-crafted chocolates, truffles, and other sweets.

  • Scialo Bros. Bakery: This bakery has been a Johnston institution for over 100 years and offers a range of classic Italian pastries and baked goods.

  • A Slice of Rhode Island Gift Shop: Filled with locally-made crafts, souvenirs, and other gifts, this charming shop has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Johnston

Johnston’s culture is deeply influenced by its rich history and proud heritage. As in most of Rhode Island, seafood is a staple of the local diet, and arts and music are considered vital components of the community.

Best Time to Visit Johnston

The best time to visit Johnston is during the fall, when the leaves are changing and the weather is mild. However, the town is beautiful and active during every season, and visitors can expect a friendly welcome and a variety of events and activities year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Johnston

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the local arts scene. Johnston has a thriving community of artists and performers, and there are always events and exhibits to check out.

  • Do try the local cuisine. From seafood to pizza strips to coffee milk, Johnston has a variety of delicious foods to try.

  • Do visit some of the town’s historic landmarks. Johnston has a rich history and many important historical sites to visit.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. The town takes great pride in its natural beauty, and visitors should respect that by disposing of trash properly.

  • Don’t be loud and disruptive. Johnston is a peaceful and quiet town, and visitors should be respectful of their neighbors and surroundings.

  • Don’t forget to take a moment to appreciate the natural beauty. Whether you’re hiking through the woods or lounging on the beach, take a moment to enjoy the stunning scenery that surrounds you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Johnston

Johnston is generally a safe and friendly town, but visitors should still take standard precautions to stay safe. As with most travel locations, taking care of your personal belongings, watching where you walk, and following good safety measures would be advisable.

Health in Johnston

Visitors should have no problem finding healthcare facilities in Johnston if needed. There are several hospitals and clinics in the area, and visitors are advised to have travel health insurance in case of any unforeseen medical expenses.

Getting to Johnston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Johnston

T.F. Green International Airport is located approximately 10 miles from Johnston, and offers a variety of domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Johnston

The closest bus station to Johnston is the Rhode Island Public Transit Authority (RIPTA), which offers bus service throughout the state.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive or take a taxi to get to Johnston, as the town is easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Johnston

The best way to get around Johnston is by car or taxi, as many of the top attractions and points of interest are spread out across the town. Alternatively, you could explore the town’s hiking and biking trails on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Johnston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rhode Island has plenty of other exciting destinations to explore. Here are some nearby places to add to your itinerary:

  • Providence: Rhode Island’s capital city is known for its charming streets, historic landmarks, and thriving arts scene.

  • Newport: This picturesque town is famous for its mansions and historic landmarks, as well as its beautiful beaches and scenic coastal views.

  • Mystic, Connecticut: Located just over the border from Rhode Island, Mystic is a charming New England town that’s known for its maritime history and museums.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Johnston is the perfect destination. With a rich history and plenty of natural beauty, this charming town offers a wealth of attractions, activities, and local flavors that will appeal to travelers of all ages and interests. Plan your next trip to Johnston today and discover all that this beautiful town has to offer!
