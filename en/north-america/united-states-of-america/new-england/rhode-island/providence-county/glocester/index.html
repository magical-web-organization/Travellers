Introduction to Glocester

Nestled in the beautiful Providence County of Rhode Island, Glocester is a charming town filled with natural beauty, history, and culture. With its serene countryside landscapes and rich heritage, Glocester offers a unique experience that is not like any other. From outdoor adventures to cultural explorations, this small town has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Glocester

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you love exploring history and culture, Glocester has several attractions that will pique your interest. Visit:

  • Glocester Heritage Society Museum – showcases the town’s history and artifacts from the 18th century to present day.
  • Acote’s Hill Cemetery – an old cemetery with graves dating back to the 1700s and a great spot for a picnic or hike.
  • Harmony Chapel and Cemetery – built-in 1816 and listed on the National Register of Historic Places, the chapel is a stunning example of Greek architecture.
  • Spring Grove Cemetery – this sprawling cemetery boasts several notable graves, including that of the first governor of Rhode Island.

Natural Attractions of Glocester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Glocester is a nature lover’s paradise with splendid landscapes and outdoor activities to explore. Some of the must-visit natural attractions are:

  • George Washington Management Area – spread across 4,000 acres, this lush forest is perfect for hiking, fishing, and hunting.
  • Ponaganset Reservoir – with an area of 354 acres, the reservoir is an ideal spot for kayaking, fishing, hiking, and bird-watching.
  • Steere Hill Farm – pick fresh fruits and vegetables in season, pet animals, or enjoy a picnic at this family-friendly farm.
  • Durfee Hill Management Area – explore the extensive networks of trails, hunt, or simply take in the stunning views from Durfee Hill Tower.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glocester

Glocester has a plethora of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for enthusiasts of all skill levels. Some of the best routes to try are:

  • Old Victory Highway Trail – a 5.4-mile trail that follows an abandoned highway. It is perfect for biking and hiking.
  • Windsor Trail – a 3.2-mile circular trail that leads you through various terrains, including marshes, forests, and open meadows.
  • Tarklin Pond Trail – a 1.5-mile trail that loops around the beautiful Tarklin Pond. It is suitable for walking, running, and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Glocester: Activities and Tours

Glocester offers an array of activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Take a hot-air balloon ride over the beautiful landscape of Glocester.
  • Visit Whispering Pines Conference Center for zip-lining, archery, and team-building exercises.
  • Go on a wine tasting tour at the famous Diamond Hill Vineyards.
  • Participate in a guided night hike at the George Washington Management Area and explore the mysteries of the forest at night.

Week-Long Itinerary for Glocester

If you’re planning to spend a week in Glocester, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Hike the trails at George Washington Management Area in the morning. Visit the Glocester Heritage Society Museum in the afternoon.
2 Explore the Diamond Hill Vineyards and enjoy a wine tasting session.
3 Take a hot-air balloon ride over Glocester and experience the stunning views from above.
4 Visit the Steere Hill Farm and pick fresh produce. In the evening, participate in a guided night hike at the George Washington Management Area.
5 Spend a day at the Ponaganset Reservoir, kayaking, fishing, and hiking.
6 Visit the Harmony Chapel and Cemetery and the Spring Grove Cemetery.
7 Go on a zip-lining and archery adventure at the Whispering Pines Conference Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Glocester

Must-Try Dishes

While in Glocester, do not miss trying these local dishes:

  • Clam cakes – these deep-fried balls of dough mixed with chopped clams are a Rhode Island specialty.
  • Stuffies – stuffed clams made with chopped clams, breadcrumbs, and a spice mix.
  • Coffee milk –a sweetened milk-flavored with coffee syrup, a Rhode Island staple.

Local Beverages

Glocester’s drink scene is unique and diverse. Check out these local favorites:

  • Coffee milk – a Rhode Island specialty that you can try at any local diner or café.
  • Beer – Rhode Island has several award-winning breweries, including the Proclamation Ale Company and Tilted Barn Brewery. Try their craft beers and cider.
  • Wine – try the delicious local wines at Diamond Hill Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Glocester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Glocester has a range of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Noah’s Ark Bed and Breakfast – located in the heart of the town, this cozy guest house offers home-like comfort.
  • The Lodge at Buck Hollow Farm – nestled in a tranquil setting, this authentic log cabin is perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • By The Lake Inn – situated by the Keech Pond, this charming inn is perfect for nature lovers.

What to Buy in Glocester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Glocester back home with you by buying some unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some great options:

  • Rhode Island coffee syrup – bring back the local flavor by taking home a bottle of this sweet syrup.
  • Cranberry bog map – get a beautiful map of the famous cranberry bogs in Glocester.
  • Artisanal cheese – indulge in the delectable local flavors by taking back some artisanal cheese from the Steere Cheese Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Glocester

Glocester is a town steeped in tradition, with its residents deeply connected to their heritage. Respect the local culture by greeting the locals and engaging in small talk. Also, make sure to try the local delicacies and participate in the annual festivals.

Best Time to Visit Glocester

The best time to visit Glocester is between May to October when the weather is pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to indulge in. The fall season, between September to November, is especially beautiful, with the vibrant foliage adding to the town’s charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glocester

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local delicacies, coffee milk, and clam cakes.
  • Participate in the annual festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the natural beauty by littering or damaging the environment.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Glocester

Glocester is a safe town with low crime rates. However, make sure to take precautions while hiking or exploring the outdoors. Wear appropriate clothing, carry plenty of water, and avoid isolated areas during nights.

Health in Glocester

Glocester has excellent healthcare facilities. In case of any health emergencies, visit the Glocester Medical Centre or the Landmark Medical Center in nearby Woonsocket.

Getting to Glocester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Glocester

The nearest airport to Glocester is the T.F. Green Airport in Warwick, Rhode Island, which is approximately 40 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Glocester

The Greyhound Lines bus station is located in Providence, Rhode Island, which is about 30 minutes away from Glocester by car.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best way to explore Glocester and its surrounding areas. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

How to Get Around in Glocester

The best way to get around Glocester is by car. Several car rental options are available in the town itself. Bicycling is also a popular way to explore, with several bike rental shops in the town.

Where to Go Next from Glocester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Providence – about 30 minutes away, Rhode Island’s capital city offers a range of cultural and historic attractions.
  • Boston – located about one and a half hours away, Boston is a beautiful city rich in history and cultures.
  • Narragansett – with its beautiful beaches and ocean views, Narragansett is a must-visit destination just 50 minutes away from Glocester.

Conclusion

Glocester is a hidden gem in Rhode Island that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, history, culture, and adventure. The town is perfect for a weekend vacation or a week-long escape into tranquility. So what are you waiting for? Plan your next trip to Glocester and get ready for an experience of a lifetime!
