Introduction to Burrillville

Burrillville is a charming town located in Providence County, Rhode Island. Known for its scenic beauty, rich culture, and friendly people, Burrillville offers an authentic New England experience. With its vibrant arts scene, historical sites, and natural beauty, Burrillville is a must-visit destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Burrillville

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or adventure, Burrillville has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bridgeton School Museum: Explore the history of local education at this historic schoolhouse-turned-museum.

  2. Jesse M. Smith Memorial Library: This impressive library showcases the town’s commitment to education and culture.

  3. Oakland Mapleville Fire District Museum: For a unique experience, check out the firefighting exhibits and memorabilia at this small museum.

Natural Attractions of Burrillville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Wallum Lake: This beautiful lake is great for fishing, swimming, and boating.

  2. Buck Hill Management Area: A majestic forest with hiking trails, camping, fishing, and hunting.

  3. Spring Lake: This lakeside park offers picnicking, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burrillville

Burrillville is a paradise for hikers and bikers. With its rolling hills, winding trails, and scenic vistas, this town is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes:

  1. George Washington Management Area Hike: This trail winds through dense forests and offers picturesque views.

  2. Clear River Trail: A fairly easy hiking trail that leads to the Clear River Reservoir.

  3. Buck Hill Trail to Buck Hill Management Area: This is a two-part trail that starts at Buck Hill Falls and ends at the beautiful Buck Hill Management Area.

Best Things to Do in Burrillville: Activities and Tours

  1. Adventure Land: This family-friendly park offers go-karts, mini-golf, and arcade games.

  2. Burrillville Farmers Market: A great place to find local produce, crafts, and more.

  3. Haunted Graveyard: Just in time for Halloween! This spooky attraction is a must-visit if you love all things spooky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burrillville

Day 1: Explore the Bridgeton School Museum and Jesse M. Smith Memorial Library.

Day 2: Take a hike in George Washington Management Area and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 3: Check out Adventure Land and go-kart your way to an exciting day out.

Day 4: Spend a relaxing day picnicking or camping at Spring Lake.

Day 5: Explore the Oakland Mapleville Fire District Museum and learn about the local firefighting history.

Day 6: Go for a swim and relax at Wallum Lake.

Day 7: Visit the Haunted Graveyard and enjoy a spooky Halloween adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Burrillville

When it comes to food, Burrillville has some unique and delicious offerings that you won’t want to miss. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stuffies: A classic Rhode Island dish, these stuffed clams are the perfect appetizer.

  2. Fried Clams: Freshly caught and fried to perfection, these clams are a staple in any Rhode Island meal.

  3. Lobster Roll: Savor the taste of Rhode Island’s seafood in a delicious lobster roll.

Local Beverages

  1. Del’s Lemonade: A refreshing drink made of lemon juice and crushed ice.

  2. Beer from Pascoag: Try out the local brews and flavor the local beer.

  3. Newport Vineyards: Experience the taste of Burrillville’s remarkable wine scene.

Where to Stay in Burrillville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Travelers to Burrillville have several options for accommodations. Check out these top-rated options:

  1. Burrillville Motor Lodge: A cozy and budget-friendly motel with all modern amenities.

  2. The Lodge at Buck Hill: Nestled in the Buck Hill Management Area, this rustic lodge offers a secluded retreat for nature lovers.

  3. Hartford Marriott Downtown: A luxurious hotel with excellent amenities that’s only a short distance from Burrillville.

What to Buy in Burrillville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Burrillville with these unique souvenirs:

  1. Burrillville Honey: This local honey is a sweet reminder of your trip to Burrillville.

  2. Burrillville Spices: The local spice scene is worth a try!

  3. Handcrafted Jewelry: Bring home stylish pieces that are unique to the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Burrillville

Burrillville is rich in culture and tradition. It’s a community that values its history and heritage. The town’s unique blend of colonial and modern culture is reflected in its festivals, food, and music.

Best Time to Visit Burrillville

The best time to visit Burrillville is during the fall months when the leaves are changing, and the temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burrillville

Do:

  1. Take in the scenic views at Wallum Lake.

  2. Explore the trails at Buck Hill Management Area.

  3. Check out the local museums.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to bring bug spray and sunscreen during summer months.

  2. Don’t litter in the forest trails.

  3. Don’t forget to try out the local cuisine!

Safety and Health

Safety in Burrillville

Burrillville is a safe place for tourists, with a low crime rate. Be mindful of wild animals when hiking or camping.

Health in Burrillville

Burrillville has a good healthcare system with several medical facilities. If you’re planning to engage in outdoor activities, remember to carry first aid supplies and stay hydrated.

Getting to Burrillville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Traveling to Burrillville is easy, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Burrillville

  1. T.F. Green Airport (PVD) in Warwick, Rhode Island, is only a 40-minute drive from Burrillville.

  2. Logan International Airport (BOS) in Boston, Massachusetts, is a little over an hour’s drive from Burrillville.

Bus Stations Near Burrillville

  1. The Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority (MBTA) and RIPTA bus services connect Burrillville to Boston, Rhode Island, and other nearby towns.

  2. Greyhound and Peter Pan bus services also run routes to Burrillville.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or call for a taxi service to get to Burrillville.

How to Get Around in Burrillville

Getting around in Burrillville is easy with the area’s several transportation options. Car rental services provide visitors with the flexibility to explore on their own, and cabs are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Burrillville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Although Burrillville has plenty to offer, visitors can also explore nearby destinations, including:

  1. Providence: Just a 30-minute drive away, explore the bustling city’s local attractions.

  2. Newport: Only an hour’s drive away, indulge in the picturesque views and seasonal events of the coastal city.

Conclusion

Burrillville offers a unique blend of culture, adventure, and natural beauty, making it a holiday destination worth exploring. This comprehensive guide can help you plan a trip filled with adventure, relaxation, nature, and history. So pack your bags and head out to the charming town of Burrillville!
