Introduction to North Providence

Located in Providence County, Rhode Island, North Providence is a small, charming town that offers plenty of attractions for visitors to enjoy. The town is in close proximity to the state capital of Providence, making it an ideal destination for those looking to explore the region.

List of Places to Visit in North Providence

When it comes to exploring North Providence, there is no shortage of places to visit. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Governor Sprague Mansion: This historic mansion was built in 1790 and served as the home of former Rhode Island Governor William Sprague and his family. Today, it is a museum open to the public, offering a glimpse into life in the early 19th century.
  • Smith-Appleby House Museum: Another historic home turned museum, the Smith-Appleby House was built in the 17th century and offers guided tours and educational programs for visitors.
  • Governor John A. Notte Jr. Park: Named after a former Rhode Island governor, this park features a variety of recreational facilities, including a skate park and a playground.

Natural Attractions of North Providence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wenscott Reservoir: This picturesque body of water is a popular spot for fishing and kayaking.
  • Notte Park: In addition to its recreational areas, Governor John A. Notte Jr. Park also features scenic walking trails and a pond.
  • Blackstone River Greenway: This paved trail travels through several towns in Rhode Island, including North Providence, offering beautiful views of the Blackstone River and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Providence

For those who love to explore the outdoors, North Providence offers some great hiking and biking routes. Here are some top options:

  • Wenscott Reservoir Trail: This 1.7-mile loop trail takes hikers around Wenscott Reservoir, offering beautiful views of the water and the surrounding woodland.
  • Blackstone River Bikeway: This 48-mile bike path travels through several towns in Rhode Island, passing through North Providence and offering scenic river views.

Best Things to Do in North Providence: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, North Providence offers a variety of activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some highlights:

  • Escape Rhode Island: This escape room facility offers a fun and challenging game experience for groups of up to eight people.
  • Rhode Island Sports Center: This indoor sports complex features ice skating rinks and a turf field, offering a variety of recreational activities.
  • Providence Ghost Tour: This guided walking tour takes visitors through the spooky streets of Providence, including North Providence, sharing ghost stories and local legends along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of North Providence

North Providence’s food and drink scene is a reflection of the town’s diverse cultural influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Cakes: A Rhode Island specialty, these fried dough balls are filled with freshly chopped clams and served with clam chowder.
  • Stuffies: Another Rhode Island favorite, stuffies are stuffed quahog clams commonly served with hot sauce.
  • Pizza Strips: A unique take on pizza, these strips feature a thick, doughy crust topped with tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Del’s Lemonade: This famous Rhode Island drink is made with fresh lemon juice, sugar, and ice, creating a refreshing summer treat.
  • Coffee Milk: Rhode Island’s official state drink, coffee milk is made with coffee syrup and milk, creating a sweet and creamy beverage.
  • Autocrat Coffee Syrup: This locally made coffee syrup is a staple ingredient in Rhode Island’s coffee milk and can also be used in cocktails and desserts.

Where to Stay in North Providence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For visitors looking to spend the night in North Providence, there are several options to choose from. Here are some notable accommodations:

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a fitness center and an indoor pool.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Providence/Smithfield: Located just a short drive from North Providence, this hotel offers modern rooms and suites, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Seven Hills Inn Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and a communal kitchen, as well as a garden and a barbecue area.

What to Buy in North Providence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Providence offers several unique shopping opportunities, including local markets and specialty stores. Here are some notable products to bring home:

  • Rhode Island Red Hot Sauce: This spicy sauce is made with locally grown peppers and garlic, creating a bold and flavorful condiment.
  • Rhode Island Coffee Syrup: A sweet and flavorful syrup commonly used to make coffee milk, this locally made product is a great souvenir or gift.
  • Local Artwork: North Providence is home to several talented artists and galleries, making it a great place to find unique artwork to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of North Providence

North Providence’s cultural traditions are a reflection of its diverse population, with influences from Italian, Portuguese, and Irish communities. Visitors can experience these traditions through local festivals, events, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit North Providence

The best time to visit North Providence is during the summer months when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy North Providence’s natural beauty in the autumn months when the foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Providence

When visiting North Providence, there are a few important things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the local history and culture through museums and events.
  • Enjoy the scenic beauty of North Providence’s outdoor attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Enter private property without permission.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Ignore safety guidelines and precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Visitors to North Providence should take the following safety and health precautions:

Safety in North Providence

  • Be aware of traffic and pedestrian crossings.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and in a secure location.

Health in North Providence

  • Stay hydrated during outdoor activities and in hot weather.
  • Protect yourself from ticks and other insects when hiking or exploring wooded areas.
  • Seek medical attention if experiencing any symptoms of illness.

Getting to North Providence: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Providence is easily accessible via various transportation options, including:

Airports Near North Providence

  • T.F. Green Airport: Located in Warwick, Rhode Island, T.F. Green Airport is approximately 12 miles south of North Providence.
  • Logan International Airport: Located in Boston, Massachusetts, Logan International Airport is approximately 50 miles northeast of North Providence.

Bus Stations Near North Providence

  • Providence Bus Terminal: Located in downtown Providence, the bus terminal offers daily service on several major bus routes.
  • Peter Pan Bus Terminal: Located in Providence, the terminal offers daily service to destinations throughout New England.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent a car from several major rental companies at T.F. Green Airport and Logan International Airport.
  • Taxis and Rideshare Services: Taxis and rideshare services are available throughout North Providence and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in North Providence

Visitors can easily get around North Providence by car or bike. Several major roads run through the town, making it easy to navigate. There are also several bike trails and bike lanes throughout North Providence, making it a great place for a scenic bike ride.

Where to Go Next from North Providence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to continue their New England adventure, here are some nearby destinations to consider visiting:

  • Providence, Rhode Island: The state capital of Rhode Island offers plenty of attractions, including historic landmarks, museums, and a vibrant food scene.
  • Newport, Rhode Island: Known for its stunning mansions and scenic coastline, Newport is a popular destination for visitors to Rhode Island.
  • Boston, Massachusetts: Just a short drive or train ride from North Providence, Boston offers a wealth of cultural attractions and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

North Providence, Rhode Island, is a charming town that offers plenty of attractions and activities for visitors. Whether you’re looking to explore the outdoors, learn about local culture and history, or simply enjoy some delicious food and drinks, North Providence is a great destination to add to your New England itinerary.
