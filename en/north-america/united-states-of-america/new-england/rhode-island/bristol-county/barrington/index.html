Introduction to Barrington

Barrington is a picturesque town located in Bristol County, Rhode Island, United States of America. This charming town boasts of scenic views and outdoor activities that are hard to resist. The town revolves around serene neighborhoods, historical landmarks, and cultural centers making it a perfect vacation spot for people of all ages. This guide will take visitors through some of the most exciting tourist sites in Barrington, including cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Barrington

Visitors to Barrington can explore a range of tourist attractions. Here are some of the must-visit locations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Barrington Preservation Society

The society is the pride of Barrington, a local non-profit organization that works towards preserving the town’s history. It showcases local architecture and displays artifacts from the colonial era. The society also hosts an annual Heritage Day where visitors can experience the colonial way of life.

Blithewold Mansion and Gardens

The stunning mansion was built by Augustus and Bessie Van Wickle in the late 1800s. The Mansion and Gardens give an elegant look to the visitors. The gardens around the mansion are well-maintained with flowers from all over the world. Visitors can tour the mansion’s interior to learn more about the family’s history and fascinating stories.

Natural Attractions of Barrington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brickyard Pond

Brickyard Pond is a freshwater pond located in the center of Barrington. Visitors can go fishing, paddleboarding, or kayaking. The place is filled with picnic tables and benches, making it a perfect spot for family outings.

East Bay Bike Path

The path is a 14.5-mile scenic adventure that connects five towns and boasts stunning views of the Narragansett Bay. Rent out a bike and ride through the trails, stop by the parks and beaches to relax and rejuvenate. The bike ride can be a family adventure or a physical challenge, anyone of all ages can enjoy this trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barrington

Barrington has numerous hiking and biking trails that one can explore. Here are some of the popular ones:

Haines Memorial State Park

Haines Park has walking and hiking trails that bring themselves through the park’s woods and to the coastline of Narragansett Bay. Various sites such as picnic tables and benches are available throughout the park.

Burr’s Hill Park

Winner of the Best Park of East Bay of RI, Burr’s Hill Park has everything from picnic tables, baseball fields, basketball courts, playgrounds, and walking and hiking trails that lead you through the woods.

Best Things to Do in Barrington: Activities and Tours

Kayaking in the Narragansett Bay

Barrington has several rental options for kayaking in the Narragansett Bay. Take the family out on a boat or ride solo while enjoying the breathtaking view of the Bay.

Harbor Light Trail

The Harbor Light Trail is one of the most beautiful trails in Barrington. One can experience serene and picturesque views of Barrington Harbor while hiking the trail.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barrington

Day 1: Mansions – Blithewold Mansion and Gardens, Colt State Park, Linden Place

Day 2: Harbor Sailing – Sailing Charters, Narragansett Bay, and Harbor Lights Evening Tour

Day 3: East Bay Bike Path – Ride With Seven Distinctive Towns

Food and Drink Scene of Barrington

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Rolls

Lobster rolls are a must-try dish when visiting Barrington. The dish is a New England staple, and visitors can find the best in town at historical landmarks and local eateries.

Clam Chowder

Barrington, being located on the coast, gets access to fresh seafood. Thus, visitors should not miss trying the delicious clam chowder soup that most of the restaurants offer.

Local Beverages

Del’s Lemonade

Del’s Lemonade is a frozen drink that is refreshing to visitors on hot summer days. This beverage is available most of the local stores around Barrington.

Grey Sail Brewing

Grey Sail Brewing is a local brewery located in the heart of Barrington, offering one of the best craft beers in the state.

Where to Stay in Barrington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barrington has several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that one can stay in. Here are some options:

Barrington Vacation Rentals

Barrington Vacation Rentals provide visitors with the comfort of staying at a home away from home. The rental apartments come with fully furnished facilities, including kitchenettes.

Hampton Inn and Suites Providence

Hampton Inn and Suites Providence is located approximately 8 miles from Barrington. The hotel offers standard rooms and suites with great amenities.

What to Buy in Barrington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Barrington can take a part of the town’s history with them by purchasing souvenirs such as heritage flags, t-shirts, hats, and caps.

Local Culture and Customs of Barrington

Barrington is a welcoming town and displays a charming New England vibe. The locals are polite, and visitors should return the favor by being courteous and respectful.

Best Time to Visit Barrington

The best time to visit Barrington is in the summer months, between June and September, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are abundant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barrington

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ privacy and property.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting historical landmarks and churches.
  • Respect the town’s natural beauty by not littering.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or unruly in public places.
  • Smoke in public parks or beaches.
  • Criticize the town’s history or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barrington

Barrington is a safe and secure town, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking doors and windows at night and not leaving valuable items visible in cars.

Health in Barrington

Barrington has several healthcare facilities that provide immediate and standard medical attention. Visitors should carry health-insurance cards and have their doctors’ contact information on hand.

Getting to Barrington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barrington

Barrington is accessible via the following airports:

  • T.F. Green Airport, Warwick, Rhode Island (19 miles)
  • Logan International Airport, Boston, Massachusetts (60 miles)
  • Bradley International Airport, Windsor Locks, Connecticut (87 miles)

Bus Stations Near Barrington

Visitors can catch a bus from Providence and Warren to Barrington, Rhode Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or take a taxi from any of the airports to Barrington town.

How to Get Around in Barrington

Visitors can rent bicycles, walk, or drive cars to explore the town. The bus system runs every day with good frequency and connectivity.

Conclusion

Barrington, Rhode Island, is a hidden gem with a lot to offer visitors, a town filled with history, culture, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore Barrington’s scenic landscapes, local cuisine, and rich heritage while enjoying the town’s welcoming atmosphere.
