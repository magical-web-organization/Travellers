Introduction to Bristol County

Bristol County is a picturesque county in Rhode Island located in the northeastern part of the United States. Bristol County is steeped in colonial history, and it stands out as a must-see destination for visitors who appreciate the historic character and natural beauty of New England.

List of Places to Visit in Bristol County

Bristol County’s beauty lies in its fascinating blend of museums, historical landmarks, peaceful beaches, and hiking in lush forests. Here are some attractions to visit while exploring Bristol County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bristol Historical & Preservation Society
  • Coggeshall Farm Museum
  • Linden Place Museum
  • Blithewold Mansion, Gardens and Arboretum
  • Mount Hope Farm
  • Herreshoff Marine Museum
  • Bristol Art Museum

Natural Attractions of Bristol County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colt State Park
  • East Bay Bike Path
  • Bristol Town Beach
  • Audubon Society of Rhode Island
  • The Cutler Reserve
  • Mount Hope Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bristol County

Bristol County is an excellent place for cycling and hiking enthusiasts. Traveling through the town of Bristol and along the coast of the East Bay, the East Bay Bike Path offers an outstanding experience that features scenic vistas. Hikers will enjoy the Cutler Reserve, where they can indulge in a fully-wild experience that highlights the area’s nature and history.

Best Things to Do in Bristol County: Activities and Tours

  • Go sailing on a boat tour and enjoy a beautiful sunset on the water.
  • Taste some local seafood and watch the harbor activity at one of the many waterfront restaurants.
  • Kayak through the mangroves and coastal wetlands.
  • Watch a variety of animal species in their natural habitats at one of the local wildlife tours.
  • Visit the Bristol Farmers Market for a taste of the local produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bristol County

Day 1: Explore Bristol Historical & Preservation Society and the Eastern Bay Bike Path Day 2: Visit the Herreshoff Marine Museum and enjoy a boat tour Day 3: Trek through the Cutler Reserve and end the day with a seafood dinner Day 4: Spend a day at Colt State Park Day 5: Walk through the Blithewold Mansion, Gardens, and Arboretum Day 6: Visit the Coggeshall Farm Museum and Linden Place Museum Day 7: Head to the Bristol Art Museum, then hit the Bristol Farmers Market, and end the day with a sunset kayak tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Bristol County

Must-Try Dishes

Bristol County is known for its seafood, especially lobster, which is often featured in juicy rolls or as a main dish served with steamed veggies. Clam chowder is also a popular dish in this area. Don’t forget the Johnnycakes, thin, crispy pancakes made from cornmeal and water.

Local Beverages

The Rhode Island craft beer scene is thriving, with many local breweries making flavorful and diverse brews. Bristol County is also known for its pristine vineyards and signature wineries, such as Westport Rivers Vineyard and Winery and Newport Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Bristol County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Some fantastic places to stay while visiting Bristol County are the Bristol Harbor Inn, Bristol House Bed and Breakfast, and the Point Pleasant Inn & Resort.

What to Buy in Bristol County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Bristol County’s rich history home with you by purchasing unique souvenirs like crafts made by local artisans, antique maps, and nautical artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Bristol County

Bristol County has a rich history and is proud of its colonial ties with England. Visitors should keep an open mind and respect the area’s natural beauty and historic preservation.

Best Time to Visit Bristol County

Summer is the best time to visit Bristol County as the weather is generally sunny and warm, and most of the area’s attractions and outdoor recreation facilities are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bristol County

Do:

  • Take advantage of local cheese, seafood, and wine tastings.
  • Respect the environment and leave no litter.
  • Explore new towns and areas located near Bristol County.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Overwhelm or disrespect the locals.
  • Skip the historical sites and landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bristol County

Bristol County is a safe travel destination, but visitors should remain vigilant and take standard precautions.

Health in Bristol County

Bristol County has many hospitals and clinics that can cater to any health issues that visitors may have.

Getting to Bristol County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bristol County

T.F. Green Airport is the closest airport to Bristol County, Rhode Island. It is located about 14 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Bristol County

Bristol is accessible from Providence, Rhode Island, which has a central bus station that provides both local and nationwide bus services.

Other Options

Car rentals and private vehicles are the best options for those traveling to Bristol County.

How to Get Around in Bristol County

A car is the best way to get around in Bristol County. However, because of the narrow roads and limited parking, traveling by bike or walking can also be enjoyable.

Where to Go Next from Bristol County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby popular destinations from Bristol County are Newport, Providence, and Jamestown.

Conclusion

Bristol County is a place of vast beauty and well-preserved historic landmarks that should be part of any traveler’s bucket list. It is the ideal vacation destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and anyone looking for awesome food, unique shopping, great sights, and a comfortable vacation atmosphere. Don’t hesitate to visit Bristol County and explore its many options.
