Introduction to Orange

Orange, a picturesque town in the beautiful Lakes Region of New Hampshire, offers visitors an array of cultural and natural attractions, making it an ideal vacation spot for the whole family. The town is known for its scenic vistas, outdoor adventures, rich history, and friendly locals. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the top attractions, activities, food and drinks, accommodations, and travel advice for your visit to Orange, New Hampshire.

List of Places to Visit in Orange

Whether you’re interested in history, the arts, the great outdoors, or all three, Orange has something for everyone. Here are the top places to visit in Orange:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Orange Historical Society Museum - Learn about the history of Orange and the surrounding areas through exhibits, artifacts, and photographs showcasing the town’s past.

  • Castle in the Clouds - A historic mansion located atop the Ossipee Mountains, offering stunning views of the lakes and mountains. Take a guided tour of the mansion and explore the beautiful gardens and waterfalls.

  • Wright Museum of WWII - A must-see for history buffs, this museum displays artifacts, vehicles, and personal stories from the Second World War.

Natural Attractions of Orange: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Chocorua - Hike to the summit of this popular mountain for breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks and lakes.

  • Lake Winnipesaukee - The largest lake in the Lakes Region, offering a variety of water sports and beach activities for visitors.

  • White Mountains National Forest - Home to some of the most scenic drives and hiking trails in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orange

Orange and the surrounding areas offer miles of scenic hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Mt. Major Trail - A popular hike in the area, with stunning views of Lake Winnipesaukee from the summit.

  • Cotton Valley Rail Trail - A scenic 12.3-mile trail that follows a former railway route, perfect for biking or walking.

Best Things to Do in Orange: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Orange:

  • Boat Tours on Lake Winnipesaukee - Take a guided tour of the lake on a historic steamboat or modern vessel.

  • Canoeing or Kayaking on the Squam Lakes - Explore the peaceful waters of this scenic lake on your own or with a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Orange

Day 1: Visit the Orange Historical Society Museum and take a walking tour of the town center.

Day 2: Hike to the summit of Mount Chocorua for stunning views of the region.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive through the White Mountains National Forest.

Day 4: Explore Castle in the Clouds and its beautiful gardens and waterfalls.

Day 5: Spend a day on Lake Winnipesaukee, enjoying water sports or taking a boat tour.

Day 6: Visit the Wright Museum of WWII to learn about the town’s history during the war.

Day 7: Relax and unwind in one of the town’s many parks or beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Orange

Orange boasts a variety of dining options, ranging from upscale restaurants to casual diners and cafes. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple-Glazed Pork Chop - A classic New Hampshire dish, often served with local apples and sweet potatoes.

  • Seafood Chowder - Made with fresh local seafood and served in a sourdough bread bowl.

Local Beverages

  • Cider - Orange is known for its delicious locally-made cider, which can be found at various farm stands and markets in town.

  • Smuttynose IPA - A popular local beer, brewed in nearby Portsmouth.

Where to Stay in Orange: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Orange offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are a few top options:

  • The Wolfeboro Inn - A historic inn located in neighboring Wolfeboro, offering beautiful lake views and upscale amenities.

  • Blue Bay Inn - A cozy and affordable option located in the heart of Orange.

What to Buy in Orange: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Products - New Hampshire is known for its delicious maple syrup and other maple-based products, such as maple candy and maple butter.

  • Handmade Crafts - Orange and the surrounding areas are home to many craft fairs and markets featuring handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Orange

The people of Orange are friendly and welcoming to visitors. It’s common to greet people with a smile and strike up a conversation.

Best Time to Visit Orange

The best time to visit Orange is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the lakes and mountains offer plenty of outdoor activities and events. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the stunning foliage and harvest festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orange

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the town’s natural beauty and enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports.

  • Do explore the town’s history and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Don’t be overly loud or disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orange

Overall, Orange is a safe and welcoming town. However, visitors are advised to be aware of their surroundings and exercise caution, especially when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Orange

There are several medical clinics and hospitals located in and around Orange, in case of any emergencies or health issues.

Getting to Orange: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Orange

The nearest major airport to Orange is the Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, located 65 miles from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Orange

There are several bus companies serving the region, including Greyhound and Concord Coach Lines. The closest bus station to Orange is located in nearby Wolfeboro.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors to the area, as it allows for greater freedom and flexibility in exploring the region.

How to Get Around in Orange

The best way to get around Orange is by car, bike, or on foot. There is no public transportation within the town itself, but taxis and buses are available in nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Orange: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby towns and attractions worth visiting in and around Orange, including:

  • Wolfeboro, a charming town with a lovely lakeside downtown area.

  • Conway, a hub for outdoor activities and shopping.

  • Portsmouth, a historic coastal city with great restaurants and shops.

Conclusion

Orange, New Hampshire, offers visitors a wealth of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and friendly locals. Whether you’re looking to hike to a scenic summit, explore local history and culture, or simply relax by the lake, Orange has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has been helpful in planning your visit to this beautiful and welcoming town.
