Introduction to Conway

Conway is a picturesque town located in the northern part of New Hampshire’s Lakes Region, surrounded by the White Mountains. The town offers a perfect blend of nature and culture, with numerous outdoor adventures and historical attractions. Whether you’re looking to escape the city’s hustle and bustle, or experience the true essence of New Hampshire’s wilderness, Conway has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Conway

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Conway is home to some fascinating cultural and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into the state’s past. Some of the popular museums and historical places in Conway are:

  • Conway Scenic Railroad - take a ride on this vintage train and enjoy scenic views of the Mount Washington Valley.

  • Mount Washington Observatory Weather Discovery Center - a great place to learn about the science of weather forecasting and observe real-time weather phenomena.

  • Saco River Covered Bridge - a beautiful and historic landmark situated in the heart of Conway.

  • North Conway Historical Society Museum - a museum that exhibits the town’s history through photographs, artifacts, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Conway: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Conway is a nature lover’s paradise, offering endless opportunities for outdoor recreation and scenic beauty. Some of the popular natural attractions in Conway are:

  • Cathedral Ledge State Park - a breathtaking park with panoramic views of the White Mountains and the Saco River Valley.

  • Echo Lake State Park - a tranquil lakeside park with opportunities for swimming, kayaking, and hiking.

  • Diana’s Baths - a stunning natural waterfall on Lucy Brook, located in the heart of the White Mountains.

  • Kancamagus Highway - a scenic drive through the White Mountains that offers stunning views of the changing foliage in fall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Conway

Conway boasts a plethora of hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Some of the must-visit hiking and biking routes in Conway are:

  • Mt. Chocorua - a challenging trail that offers stunning views of the White Mountains and the Saco River Valley.

  • Black Cap Trail - a moderate hiking trail with panoramic views of the Mount Washington Valley and the Presidential Range.

  • Kancamagus Highway - a scenic route that takes you through the White Mountain National Forest, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and camping.

Best Things to Do in Conway: Activities and Tours

Conway offers an array of activities and tours that cater to all tastes and preferences. Some of the popular things to do in Conway are:

  • Zip lining - fly over the treetops and enjoy the stunning views of the White Mountains.

  • Saco River Tubing - a great way to beat the heat and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

  • Horseback riding - explore the beauty of the White Mountains on horseback.

  • Snowmobiling - hit the snow-covered trails and experience the thrill of snowmobiling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Conway

Day 1: Visit the Conway Scenic Railroad and explore the North Conway Village.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Mt. Chocorua or Black Cap Trail.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive on the Kancamagus Highway and visit the various scenic spots.

Day 4: Go zip lining or horseback riding in the White Mountains.

Day 5: Enjoy water activities such as kayaking or tubing on the Saco River.

Day 6: Visit the Mount Washington Observatory Weather Discovery Center and explore the North Conway Historical Society Museum.

Day 7: Visit Diana’s Baths and explore the scenic beauty of the White Mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Conway

Conway boasts an excellent food and drink scene, with something for every taste and budget. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Conway are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll - a classic New England dish that showcases the delicious local seafood.

  • Maple-glazed donuts - a sweet treat that’s unique to Conway.

  • New England clam chowder - a creamy and hearty soup that’s perfect for a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Maple syrup - a quintessential New Hampshire product that’s used in many dishes and drinks.

  • Frosty Gator IPA - a locally-brewed beer that’s refreshing and hoppy.

  • Conway-made cider - a popular drink that’s made with the state’s famous apples.

Where to Stay in Conway: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Conway has a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Some of the popular places to stay in Conway are:

  • Hampton Inn & Suites - a modern and stylish hotel that offers great amenities and comfort.

  • North Conway Grand Hotel - a luxurious hotel that’s located in the heart of North Conway.

  • Cathedral Ledge Resort - a cozy and comfortable resort that’s perfect for families.

What to Buy in Conway: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Conway offers an array of unique souvenirs and locally-made products that make for great gifts and mementos. Some of the popular souvenirs and local products to buy in Conway are:

  • Maple syrup and maple products - New Hampshire is famous for its maple products, and Conway is no exception.

  • Handcrafted wooden items - the state’s woodworking tradition is alive and well in Conway, with many local artisans showcasing their skills.

  • Handmade jewelry and pottery - beautiful and unique pieces that are perfect as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Conway

Conway is a friendly and welcoming town that’s deeply rooted in New Hampshire’s history and traditions. The state’s love for outdoor recreation and natural beauty is reflected in Conway’s culture and customs, with many locals actively participating in hiking, biking, and skiing.

Best Time to Visit Conway

The best time to visit Conway is during the fall season (late September to early November), when the foliage is at its peak. In winter, the town turns into a winter wonderland, with many opportunities for skiing, snowshoeing, and snowmobiling. In the summer months, Conway offers many water activities such as kayaking and tubing, making it a great destination for families and adventure-seekers alike.

Do’s and Don’ts of Conway

Do:

  • Respect nature and the environment.

  • Try local delicacies and products.

  • Follow the safety guidelines and rules of the park and recreation areas.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm local wildlife.

  • Leave without experiencing the natural beauty of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Conway

Conway is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, it’s always wise to take a few precautions, such as locking your car and keeping valuables out of plain sight.

Health in Conway

Conway has a range of medical facilities and pharmacies, so you can rest assured that your health needs will be taken care of during your visit.

Getting to Conway: Transportation Options and Logistics

Conway is well-connected to the rest of the state and the country, with easy access to major highways and public transportation.

Airports Near Conway

The closest major airport to Conway is Boston Logan International Airport, which is approximately 2.5 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Conway

Conway is serviced by the Concord Coach Lines, which offers daily bus services to major cities in New Hampshire and Maine.

Other Options

If you’re traveling from North Conway, you can take the Conway Scenic Railroad to reach Conway. Additionally, car rentals are available at the Boston Logan International Airport.

How to Get Around in Conway

The best way to explore Conway is by car, as many of the parks and attractions are located outside the town. However, if you’re staying in North Conway, you can use the Conway Municipal Shuttle to get around town and to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Conway: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conway is located in close proximity to many other popular destinations in New Hampshire and Maine. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions to consider are:

  • Mt. Washington - a scenic drive to the top of New England’s highest peak.

  • Portland, Maine - a charming coastal town with great food and scenic views.

  • Franconia Notch State Park - a beautiful park that’s home to the famous Flume Gorge.

Conclusion

Conway is a stunning gem in New Hampshire’s Lakes Region, with endless opportunities for outdoor recreation and cultural exploration. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Conway offers something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready to explore the beauty of this incredible town!
