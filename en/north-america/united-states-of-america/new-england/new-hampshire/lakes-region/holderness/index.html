Introduction to Holderness

In the heart of Lakes Region, New Hampshire lies the charming town of Holderness. Surrounded by pristine lakes, towering mountains, and breathtaking natural beauty, Holderness is the perfect destination for those seeking an adventurous and scenic getaway.

Whether you’re looking to explore the rich culture and history of the region, discover outdoor adventures, indulge in local cuisine, or simply unwind and relax in nature, Holderness has something in store for everyone. Here’s a comprehensive guide on what to see, do, and experience in Holderness.

List of Places to Visit in Holderness

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Squam Lakes Natural Science Center: A perfect place for nature lovers, the Squam Lakes Natural Science Center offers a unique experience to explore native New Hampshire wildlife and learn about their habitats.
  • The Museum of the White Mountains: Experience the rich cultural heritage of New Hampshire at The Museum of the White Mountains displaying fascinating exhibits and collections of art, images, and objects of the region throughout history.
  • The St. Mark’s Episcopal Church: Immerse yourself in the history of Holderness with a visit to The St. Mark’s Episcopal Church, showcasing the beautiful architecture and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Holderness: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Squam Lakes: Known for its crystal-clear waters and magnificent views of the mountains, Squam Lake is a favorite spot for boating, swimming, and fishing.
  • Rattlesnake Mountain: Hike through the trails of the Rattlesnake Mountain to witness panoramic views of the surrounding White Mountains.
  • Mount Prospect: A popular spot for road trippers, Mount Prospect offers unbeatable views of the White Mountains from its summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Holderness

  • Squam Lake Trail: Explore beautiful views of Squam Lake with a scenic hike along the Squam Lake Trail.
  • The Cotton Valley Trail: The perfect trail for bike enthusiasts, The Cotton Valley Trail offers an easy and scenic path for cycling through various terrains.

Best Things to Do in Holderness: Activities and Tours

  • Squam Lakes Cruises: Witness the beauty of Squam Lake aboard a luxury boat with Squam Lakes Cruises, offering various tours and activities tailored to your preferences.
  • Squam Lakes Natural Science Center: Embark on an adventurous tour with Squam Lakes Natural Science Center, offering guided nature walks, wildlife encounters, and interactive exhibits.
  • The Holderness Fishing Derby: Join the annual fishing derby in Holderness to challenge your fishing skills and win exciting prizes.

Food and Drink Scene of Holderness

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup: Savor the heavenly taste of the purest maple syrup.
  • Fish and Chips: Feast on the freshest seafood delicacies, including fish and chips.
  • Apple cider donuts: Indulge in the popular and traditional New Hampshire treat, apple cider donuts.

Local Beverages

  • Kettlehead Brewing Company: Get a taste of locally brewed craft beer at Kettlehead Brewing Company.
  • Squam Brewing: Sip on artisanal beer and spirits at Squam Brewing, located in the heart of Holderness.

Where to Stay in Holderness: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Manor on Golden Pond: A luxury retreat nestled in the beautiful White Mountains, offering elegant accommodations and superb amenities.
  • The Squam Lake Inn: Discover cozy and charming rooms overlooking Squam Lake at The Squam Lake Inn.
  • Holderness Inn: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of the town, offering comfortable rooms and friendly services.

What to Buy in Holderness: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Bring home locally sourced maple syrup and maple candy as a sweet souvenir.
  • Pottery: Discover unique and artisanal handcrafted pottery and other crafts made by local artisans.
  • Antiques: Explore the quaint antique shops and markets throughout Holderness to find vintage treasures and keepsakes.

Local Culture and Customs of Holderness

Holderness is cherished for its traditional New England culture and warm hospitality. Respect for nature, sportsmanship, and community service are the core values, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the spirit of volunteerism and fellowship.

Best Time to Visit Holderness

Holderness is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer and fall months from June to October. The weather is glorious for outdoor activities, and the fall is particularly magnificent, with foliage turning vivid hues of orange, yellow, and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Holderness

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife
  • Embrace the community’s culture and customs
  • Participate in local events and festivals

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Overcrowd or disrespect quiet neighborhoods
  • Ignore local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Holderness

Holderness is a relatively safe destination with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to stay alert while exploring the outdoors and avoid traveling alone in remote areas at night.

Health in Holderness

Holderness has adequate healthcare facilities, including clinics and hospitals, with access to essential medical services. However, it’s advisable to have adequate travel insurance coverage in case of emergencies.

Getting to Holderness: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Holderness

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: Located approximately 80 miles from Holderness, Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is the closest international airport.
  • Boston Logan International Airport: Boston Logan International Airport is a major airport serving the New England region, located approximately 120 miles from Holderness.

Bus Stations Near Holderness

  • Plymouth State University: The Plymouth State University bus station is the nearest bus station connecting Holderness with other major cities in New Hampshire.

Other Options

  • Private cars and rental services are also available to travel to Holderness.

How to Get Around in Holderness

Holderness is a small town and easily navigable by car or bike. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also available for convenient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Holderness: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The White Mountains: Explore the fascinating landscapes and scenic beauty of The White Mountains, located a short drive away from Holderness.
  • Franconia Notch State Park: Discover the natural wonders and outdoor adventures of Franconia Notch State Park, located approximately 30 miles from Holderness.
  • Portsmouth: Visit the historic city of Portsmouth, offering a vibrant food and drink scene and rich cultural heritage, located approximately 85 miles from Holderness.

Conclusion

Holderness is a hidden gem in the heart of New Hampshire, offering a perfect blend of scenic beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re seeking an idyllic getaway, exploring the rich cultural history of New England or looking for an adventure in nature, Holderness has something for everyone.
