Introduction to Belmont

Located in Lakes Region, New Hampshire, Belmont is a charming town with a population of around 7,300. Situated just a couple of hours drive away from Boston, it offers visitors a peaceful retreat with plenty of outdoor activities and local culture to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Belmont

Belmont may be a small town, but there are plenty of places worth visiting. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Canterbury Shaker Village: Get a glimpse into the communal life of Shakers in the 19th century.
  • Belknap Mill: A historical museum that showcases the growth of the town and the industrial revolution in the region.

Natural Attractions of Belmont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Winnisquam: Perfect for swimming, fishing, boating, and picnicking, this lake draws many visitors every year.
  • Winnisquam Scenic Trail: A scenic trail that runs along the shore of the lake. It’s great for hiking, biking, or just a leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belmont

Belmont has plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking, with trails that cater to different levels of experience. Some of the best trails to explore are:

  • Belknap Range Trail - A challenging trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Winnisquam Trail - A scenic trail that runs along the lake and through the woods.

Best Things to Do in Belmont: Activities and Tours

Belmont offers plenty of activities other than hiking and biking. Some of the must-do activities include:

  • Kayaking or Fishing in Lake Winnisquam
  • Golfing at one of the several golf courses in the area

Week-Long Itinerary for Belmont

Here is an ideal itinerary to help you explore Belmont over a week:

Day Activity
1 Visit Canterbury Shaker Village
2 Explore Belknap Mill and learn about the town’s history
3 Go for a hike on Belknap Range Trail
4 Spend a day in Lake Winnisquam swimming, kayaking, or fishing
5 Enjoy a round of golf at one of the nearby golf courses
6 Take a scenic drive around the town and soak in the views
7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Belmont

Belmont has a lot to offer when it comes to food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls from Lobster Connection
  • Pizza from Pat’s Pizza

Local Beverages

  • Sap House Meadery for mead tasting
  • Woodstock Inn Station & Brewery for local craft beer

Where to Stay in Belmont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belmont offers several options for accommodation, from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Some highly recommended options include:

  • The Oliver Lodge: This pet-friendly lodge offers a variety of rooms and suites with excellent views and amenities.
  • Lake House at Ferry Point Inn: This luxurious inn is situated on the shores of Lake Winnisquam and offers beautiful views, cozy rooms, and excellent service.

What to Buy in Belmont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home a piece of Belmont, consider some of these souvenirs and local products:

  • Handcrafted pottery from Canterbury Shaker Village
  • Local honey from Hidden Springs Maple

Local Culture and Customs of Belmont

Belmont is a town with a rich history and cultural diversity. The town has a strong sense of community, and visitors are encouraged to participate in local events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Belmont

The best time to visit Belmont is between June and early October when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The peak season for tourism is from July to August, so expect higher prices and crowded attractions during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belmont

Do:

  • Respect the town’s culture and traditions
  • Be mindful of local wildlife and ecology while exploring outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property
  • Ignore local traffic rules

Safety and Health

Safety in Belmont

Belmont is considered a safe town, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise usual caution and follow basic safety precautions.

Health in Belmont

Belmont has adequate medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics, in case of any emergency.

Getting to Belmont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belmont

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport - 42 miles away
  • Boston Logan International Airport - 86 miles away

Bus Stations Near Belmont

  • Concord Coach Lines - 10 miles away

Other Options

  • Personal car rentals

How to Get Around in Belmont

The best way to get around Belmont is by car, as it offers the most flexibility for exploring the town and its surrounding areas. Bike rentals and public transportation are also available.

Where to Go Next from Belmont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belmont is situated in a stunning location and offers a good base for exploring the region. Some nearby destinations worth exploring include:

  • White Mountains National Forest
  • Portsmouth

Conclusion

Whether for a weekend trip or a weeklong vacation, Belmont offers a fantastic retreat with plenty of outdoor adventures, local culture, and excellent cuisine. Pack your bags and be prepared to be charmed by this delightful New Hampshire town.
