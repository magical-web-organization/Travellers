Introduction to Lakes Region

Lakes Region is a pristine region tucked away in the heart of New Hampshire. Known for its sparkling bodies of water, rolling hills, and verdant forests, Lakes Region is an idyllic destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and history buffs. It is a year-round destination that offers outdoor recreation in the summer months and winter sports in the snowy months.

With its ample opportunities for hiking, biking, skiing, and water activities, Lakes Region has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top attractions and activities in Lakes Region.

List of Places to Visit in Lakes Region

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Wright Museum of WWII: This museum showcases the role of the United States in World War II through exhibits, displays, and artifacts.
  • New Hampshire Boat Museum: The museum has an extensive collection of vintage boats, motors, and postcards. Visitors can take a guided tour of the boathouses and learn about the history of boats in New Hampshire.
  • Castle in the Clouds: This historic mansion is situated on a mountaintop with breathtaking views of the Lakes Region. Visitors can take a tour of the castle and enjoy the nature trails and waterfalls on the estate.
  • Canterbury Shaker Village: This living history museum features restored 19th-century buildings that showcase the Shaker way of life. Visitors can take guided tours and attend workshops on crafts and gardening.

Natural Attractions of Lakes Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Winnipesaukee: This 44,000-acre lake is the heart of Lakes Region. Visitors can go boating, swimming, fishing, or just take in the scenic views.
  • Mount Major: This hiking trail offers panoramic views of Lake Winnipesaukee and the surrounding mountains. The trail is moderate in difficulty and is suitable for families with kids.
  • Squam Lake: This lake is famous for its tranquil beauty and clear waters. Visitors can take a boat tour or rent a kayak to explore the lake.
  • Weirs Beach: This public beach is perfect for a fun day out. Visitors can play volleyball, build sandcastles or go shopping at the countless souvenir shops.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakes Region

Lakes Region is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Cotton Valley Rail Trail: This easy 12-mile trail goes through several small towns and is great for a leisurely bike ride.
  • Red Hill: This moderate 2.6-mile hike offers stunning views of Squam Lake.
  • Mount Monadnock: This strenuous 4-mile hike is the most climbed mountain in the world. The summit offers panoramic views of New Hampshire and Vermont.
  • Mount Washington: This strenuous 7.6-mile hike takes you to the tallest peak in Northeastern United States. The summit offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Lakes Region: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and explore the scenic beauty of the Lakes Region.
  • Fishing: Cast a line and try to catch the large species of fish found in the Lakes Region.
  • Zip Lining: Experience the thrill of zip-lining through the forests of Lakes Region.
  • Moose Watching Tour: Take a guided tour to catch a glimpse of the majestic moose in their natural habitat.
  • Brewery Tour: Get a taste of the local brews on a brewery tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakes Region

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A New England classic, the lobster roll is a must-try when in Lakes Region.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: Made with freshly pressed apple cider, these donuts are a delicious treat.
  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is famous for its maple syrup. Try it on pancakes or waffles for a sweet surprise.
  • Clam Chowder: A hearty soup made with clams, potatoes, and cream. A perfect comfort food to warm you up on a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Local Beer: New Hampshire has a thriving craft beer scene. Try local brews from famous breweries like Smuttynose and Portsmouth Brewery.
  • Fruit Wine: Lakes Region is known for its fruit wines made with local produce like blueberries, raspberries, and strawberries.
  • Apple Cider: Made with freshly pressed apples, the apple cider in Lakes Region is a must-have.

Where to Stay in Lakes Region: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mill Falls at the Lake: A luxury hotel in the heart of Lakes Region with stunning views of Lake Winnipesaukee.
  • The Naswa Resort: A family-friendly resort with private beach access and water activities for guests.
  • Bear Tree Lodge: A cozy bed and breakfast surrounded by nature trails and scenic views.
  • Lake Opechee Inn and Spa: A tranquil retreat overlooking Lake Opechee.

What to Buy in Lakes Region: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: A sweet and iconic souvenir to remember your stay in Lakes Region.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: There are several local artisans in the area who sell beautiful handcrafted jewelry inspired by the natural beauty of Lakes Region.
  • Postcards: There are several markets around the area where you can buy postcards featuring scenic views of Lakes Region.
  • Specialty Foods: Bring home some of the local culinary delights like artisanal cheeses, jams, and chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakes Region

Lakes Region has a rich culture and heritage. The people of Lakes Region are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. The area is steeped in tradition and folklore, and you can witness the customs and traditions during the local festivals and events held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Lakes Region

Lakes Region offers something to do and see year-round. The best time to visit, however, is during the summer months from June to August. The weather is warm and pleasant, and the lakes are perfect for water activities

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakes Region

Do:

  • Do follow safety guidelines while hiking and exploring the outdoors.
  • Do try local brews and cuisine.
  • Do respect the natural beauty of the area and help preserve it for future generations.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces.
  • Don’t feed the wildlife.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakes Region

Lakes Region is generally a safe place to visit. However, it is always a good idea to follow safety guidelines, especially when hiking and exploring the outdoors.

Health in Lakes Region

Lakes Region has several hospitals and medical centers equipped to deal with emergencies. It is always a good idea to have travel insurance before embarking on a trip to Lakes Region.

Getting to Lakes Region: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lakes Region

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: Located 55 miles from Lakes Region.
  • Portsmouth International Airport: Located 70 miles from Lakes Region.
  • Boston Logan International Airport: Located 110 miles from Lakes Region.

Bus Stations Near Lakes Region

  • Concord Coach Lines: Regular bus services to Lakes Region from cities like Boston, Portland, and Concord.
  • Dartmouth Coach: Regular buses from New York City, Boston, and Lebanon, NH.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the area at your own pace.
  • Private Car Services: Several private car services offer transfers to and from Lakes Region.

How to Get Around in Lakes Region

The best way to explore Lakes Region is by car. If you don’t have a car, you can rent one from several rental car agencies in the area. Taxis and ride-hailing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lakes Region: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • White Mountains National Forest: This pristine mountain range is only an hour’s drive from Lakes Region.
  • Portsmouth: A historic seaport town with beautiful architecture and a thriving arts scene.
  • Boston: The bustling city of Boston is only a two-hour drive from Lakes Region.

Conclusion

Lakes Region is a hidden gem in New Hampshire, with its natural beauty, outdoor activities, history, and culture.
