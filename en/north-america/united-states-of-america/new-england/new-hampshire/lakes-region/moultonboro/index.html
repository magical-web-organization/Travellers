Introduction to Moultonboro

Nestled in the heart of New Hampshire’s scenic Lakes Region, Moultonboro is a charming town steeped in history and surrounded by natural beauty. With its crystal-clear lakes, lush forests, and rolling mountains, Moultonboro is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. Whether you’re seeking a peaceful retreat or an action-packed adventure, this New England gem has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Moultonboro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castle in the Clouds: This historic mansion offers stunning views of Lake Winnipesaukee and the surrounding mountains. Built in the early 1900s, Castle in the Clouds is now a museum showcasing the art, architecture, and lifestyle of the era.
  • Old Country Store and Museum: Step back in time at this classic New England emporium, which has been serving locals and visitors alike since 1781. The store offers everything from penny candy to handmade crafts, and the attached museum displays artifacts from Moultonboro’s rich history.

Natural Attractions of Moultonboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Winnipesaukee: New Hampshire’s largest lake is a playground for boaters, swimmers, and anglers. Rent a kayak or a pontoon boat to explore the crystal-clear waters, or simply relax on one of the many sandy beaches.
  • Squam Lakes Natural Science Center: This educational center offers a chance to see native wildlife up close, including black bears, mountain lions, and bald eagles. Take a guided tour, catch a live animal exhibit, or go on a nature walk through one of the many scenic trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moultonboro

  • Red Hill Trail: This challenging 4-mile hike to the summit of Red Hill rewards visitors with breathtaking views of Lake Winnipesaukee and the surrounding mountains.
  • Cotton Valley Trail: This 12-mile rail trail winds through the forests and fields of Moultonboro, offering a peaceful and scenic route for bikers and hikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Moultonboro: Activities and Tours

  • Scenic Boat Tour: Take a leisurely cruise around Lake Winnipesaukee, admiring the stunning scenery and learning about the area’s history and culture.
  • Zip-Line Adventure: Soar through the treetops on a thrilling zip-line tour, experiencing the beauty of the forest from a whole new perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moultonboro

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Castle in the Clouds and take a scenic drive around Lake Winnipesaukee.
Day 2 Go on a nature hike in the White Mountains, followed by a relaxing evening at your hotel or guesthouse.
Day 3 Take a guided boat tour of Lake Winnipesaukee and visit some of the charming lakeside towns.
Day 4 Learn about local wildlife at Squam Lakes Natural Science Center, followed by a picnic lunch in the park.
Day 5 Enjoy a day of water sports at Lake Winnipesaukee, renting a boat or a paddleboard and exploring the crystal-clear waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Moultonboro

Must-Try Dishes

  • New England Clam Chowder: This creamy soup of clams and potatoes is a classic New England comfort food, and can be found on menus throughout the region.
  • Maple-Glazed Ham: Moultonboro is located in an area famous for its maple syrup, and this sweet and savory dish is a delicious example of the local cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: New Hampshire is home to a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering unique and delicious brews.
  • Maple Syrup: No visit to Moultonboro would be complete without sampling the local maple syrup, which is used in everything from cocktails to desserts.

Where to Stay in Moultonboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lodge at Smith Point: This luxurious lakeside resort offers stunning views and upscale amenities, including a spa, a restaurant, and a private beach.
  • Cottage Place on Squam Lake: This cozy and rustic guesthouse offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to the lake and surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Moultonboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: As mentioned above, Moultonboro is known for its delicious maple syrup. Pick up a bottle (or two) to take home and enjoy.
  • Handmade Crafts: The Old Country Store and Museum offers a wide variety of handmade gifts and souvenirs, from handmade soap to woven blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of Moultonboro

Moultonboro is a small town with a strong sense of community, and locals are welcoming and friendly to visitors. The area’s culture is heavily influenced by its natural beauty and outdoor lifestyle, with many events and activities centered around the lake and forests.

Best Time to Visit Moultonboro

The best time to visit Moultonboro depends on your interests. Summer is the peak tourist season, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities. Fall brings stunning foliage and harvest festivals, while winter offers skiing and snowmobiling. Spring is a quieter season, with fewer visitors and the opportunity to experience the natural beauty of the area before the crowds arrive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moultonboro

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings and wildlife.
  • Take advantage of the many opportunities for outdoor recreation.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the maple syrup!

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Drive under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moultonboro

Moultonboro is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should always take normal precautions and exercise good judgment. Be aware of your surroundings and belongings, and follow all posted signs and regulations when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Moultonboro

Healthcare facilities and pharmacies are available in the nearby town of Meredith, about 20 minutes away by car. Visitors should bring any necessary medications and supplies with them.

Getting to Moultonboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Moultonboro

The nearest major airport to Moultonboro is Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, about 70 miles away. Several smaller regional airports are also located within a reasonable drive.

Bus Stations Near Moultonboro

Greyhound and other bus lines offer service to several nearby towns, with connections available to Manchester and Boston.

Other Options

Renting a car is generally the most convenient way to get around Moultonboro and the surrounding area. Amtrak train service is available in nearby towns, providing transportation to larger cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Moultonboro

A car is strongly recommended for getting around Moultonboro, as public transportation is limited and many attractions and activities require a vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Moultonboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • White Mountains: Just a short drive from Moultonboro, the White Mountains offer some of the most stunning natural beauty in the region, with hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic drives.
  • Portsmouth: This charming coastal town offers a wealth of historic and cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Boston: About 2 hours away by car, Boston is a vibrant and historic city with something for everyone, from museums to shopping to nightlife.

Conclusion

Moultonboro is a hidden gem in the Lakes Region of New Hampshire, offering a wealth of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re seeking a peaceful retreat or an action-packed getaway, Moultonboro is the perfect destination for a New England vacation.
