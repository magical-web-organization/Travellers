Introduction to Meredith

Meredith is a town located in New Hampshire’s stunning Lakes Region, known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. It’s an ideal getaway destination for those seeking relaxation and recreation in a charming New England setting.

With plenty of indoor and outdoor attractions, Meredith offers a perfect blend of natural and cultural experiences. It’s a place where you can explore nature, delve into history, enjoy local cuisine, and indulge in adventure activities. Whether you’re traveling with family, friends, or your partner, Meredith has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Meredith

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Meredith Historical Society: This museum showcases the history of Meredith and the Lake Winnipesaukee region through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Castle in the Clouds: A century-old mansion located atop a mountain, Castle in the Clouds offers breathtaking views and historic architecture.
  • The Barn at Pickering House: This 19th-century barn has been transformed into a cultural venue, hosting music concerts, art exhibitions, and other events.

Natural Attractions of Meredith: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Winnipesaukee: The largest lake in New Hampshire, Lake Winnipesaukee offers opportunities for boating, fishing, kayaking, and swimming.
  • Mount Major: A popular hiking destination, Mount Major offers panoramic views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Squam Lakes Natural Science Center: A wildlife sanctuary that features live animal exhibits, hiking trails, and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meredith

Meredith is a hiker’s paradise, with miles of trails and breathtaking vistas. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Moultonborough-Tuftonboro Trail: A 10-mile trail that runs along the shore of Lake Winnipesaukee and offers scenic views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Belknap Range Trail: A 25-mile trail that offers challenging hikes and stunning panoramic views of the region.
  • Winnipesaukee River Trail: A 2.3-mile paved trail that runs through Meredith and offers views of the river and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Meredith: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Lake Winnipesaukee is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, with species including bass, trout, and salmon.
  • Boating Tours: Take a cruise on the lake and discover Meredith’s stunning scenery from the water.
  • Zip-Line Tours: Experience an adrenaline rush with a zip-line tour in the surrounding forests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Meredith

Day 1: Explore the town’s culture and history by visiting the Meredith Historical Society and The Barn at Pickering House.

Day 2: Discover the natural beauty of the region by hiking Mount Major and exploring Lake Winnipesaukee.

Day 3: Take a boating tour of the lake and visit nearby Squam Lake Natural Science Center.

Day 4: Experience local cuisine by visiting the town’s restaurants and trying the must-try dishes.

Day 5: Enjoy outdoor adventure with a zip-line tour or kayaking excursion.

Day 6: Visit the Castle in the Clouds and take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products to bring home and bid farewell to the beautiful town of Meredith.

Food and Drink Scene of Meredith

Meredith is a foodie’s paradise, with a range of eateries offering both classic and contemporary cuisine. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A New England classic, the lobster roll is best enjoyed at Lago in Meredith.
  • Maple BBQ Ribs: The barn at Pickering House serves up delicious ribs marinated in maple syrup.

Local Beverages

  • New Hampshire Beer: The region is home to several breweries, including Lone Wolfe Brewing and Hobbs Tavern & Brewing Company.
  • New Hampshire Wine: Try the local wine at Hermit Woods Winery, which offers a variety of red, white, and fruit-based wines.

Where to Stay in Meredith: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Meredith offers a range of accommodation options, including luxury hotels, cozy guesthouses, and budget-friendly hostels. Some top options include:

  • The Inn at Meredith Bay: A luxury hotel offering stunning lake views and access to private beach and dock.
  • The Nutmeg Inn: A charming guesthouse located in a renovated 1763 farmhouse.
  • The Ballard House Inn: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Meredith.

What to Buy in Meredith: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of Meredith with some locally made products. Some popular souvenirs and local products include:

  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is known for its maple syrup, and you can find it at various shops and farmers’ markets in the town.
  • Handmade Candles: Stop by the Candle Cove & More for locally made candles in various scents.
  • Pottery and Artwork: Visit the Meredith Bay Pottery and Gallery for handmade pottery and artwork from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Meredith

Meredith’s culture is deeply rooted in its natural surroundings, and the locals take pride in preserving the region’s history and heritage. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs, including:

  • Leave No Trace: Respect the natural surroundings and leave no trace behind when hiking or enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Small Town Courtesy: Meredith is a small town, and locals value courtesy and politeness.

Best Time to Visit Meredith

The best time to visit Meredith is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the lake is perfect for swimming and outdoor activities. The fall season, from September to November, also offers stunning views of fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meredith

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings when enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Visit the local museums and historical places to learn about the region’s culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect local customs and culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Meredith

Meredith is a safe town, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as:

  • Locking vehicle doors and securing valuables.
  • Being aware of surroundings when in remote areas or hiking trails.

Health in Meredith

Meredith has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical facilities at:

  • Huggins Hospital: A full-service hospital located in nearby Wolfeboro.
  • Inter-Lakes Health: A healthcare center with doctors and specialists in various fields.

Getting to Meredith: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Meredith through several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Meredith

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: Located approximately 50 miles from Meredith, this airport offers domestic and international flights.
  • Portsmouth International Airport: Located approximately 60 miles from Meredith, this airport offers domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Meredith

  • Concord Coach Lines: Provides regular bus service to Concord, New Hampshire, and Boston, Massachusetts, and stops at Meredith’s Main Street.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also drive to Meredith, with easy access from major highways such as I-93.

How to Get Around in Meredith

Meredith is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the town on foot or by bike. Taxis and ride-share services like Uber and Lyft are also available for visitors who need transportation.

Where to Go Next from Meredith: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Meredith is located in the heart of the Lakes Region, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, including:

  • The White Mountains: Located approximately 50 miles from Meredith, the White Mountains offer stunning scenery and outdoor adventures.
  • The Lakes Region: Explore the many lakes and attractions in the region, including Lake Winnipesaukee, Squam Lake, and Weirs Beach.

Conclusion

Meredith is a hidden gem in New Hampshire, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences. With its outdoor adventures, historical attractions, and lively food and drink scene, it’s an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation. Plan your trip now and discover the many wonders of Meredith.
