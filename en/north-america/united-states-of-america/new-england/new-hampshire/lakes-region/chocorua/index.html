Introduction to Chocorua

Chocorua is a small town in the scenic Lakes Region of New Hampshire, known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, and rich history. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing retreat in nature or an action-packed adventure, Chocorua has something for everyone.

In this guide, we will highlight the top things to do and see in Chocorua, including outdoor activities, cultural attractions, where to eat and drink, where to stay, and more. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Chocorua

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Chocorua:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tamworth Lyceum: This historic building served as the cultural center of Tamworth for over a century and now houses a coffeehouse and art gallery.
  • Remick Country Doctor Museum & Farm: This museum offers a glimpse into the life of a country doctor in the 19th century, along with exhibits on agriculture and rural life.

Natural Attractions of Chocorua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Chocorua: At 3,478 feet, this mountain is a popular hiking destination with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Chocorua Lake: This serene lake offers swimming, fishing, and boating opportunities in the heart of the Lakes Region.
  • White Lake State Park: This park includes a beautiful beach, hiking trails, a campground, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chocorua

Chocorua is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with ample opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Here are a few popular routes to check out:

  • Champney Falls Trail: A moderate 3.2-mile hike through the woods, leading to a magnificent waterfall.
  • Mount Chocorua Loop Trail: A challenging 8.4-mile loop trail with stunning views of the White Mountains and Lake Winnipesaukee.
  • Cotton Valley Rail Trail: A 12.1-mile trail connecting the towns of Wolfeboro and Wakefield, offering a scenic and leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Chocorua: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun and exciting things to do in Chocorua, including:

  • Foliage tours in the fall: Experience New England’s famous fall foliage from the comfort of a scenic drive or hike.
  • Kayaking and canoeing: Navigate the pristine waters of Chocorua Lake or nearby rivers and streams.
  • Horseback riding: Take a guided trail ride through the beautiful New Hampshire countryside.
  • Skiing and snowboarding: Hit the slopes at nearby ski resorts like Bretton Woods and Attitash Mountain Resort.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chocorua

If you’re planning to spend a week in Chocorua, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore downtown Tamworth and visit the Tamworth Lyceum.
2 Hike the Champney Falls Trail and have a picnic at the falls.
3 Kayak or canoe on Chocorua Lake.
4 Take a foliage tour to admire the stunning fall colors.
5 Visit the Remick Country Doctor Museum & Farm.
6 Enjoy a guided horseback ride through the countryside.
7 Ski or snowboard at a nearby ski resort like Bretton Woods or Attitash Mountain Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of Chocorua

When it comes to food and drink in Chocorua, you’ll find plenty of delicious options to choose from. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll: A classic New England dish made with fresh lobster meat and served on a bun.
  • Apple cider donuts: A sweet and decadent treat made with locally sourced apples.
  • Maple syrup: New Hampshire is famous for its high-quality maple syrup, which is perfect for drizzling over pancakes or waffles.

Local Beverages

  • Dunkin’ Donuts coffee: This popular chain started in Massachusetts and serves up some of the best coffee in New England.
  • Local microbrews: New Hampshire has a thriving craft beer scene, with breweries like Moat Mountain Brewing Company and Tuckerman Brewing Company offering a wide selection of beers.

Where to Stay in Chocorua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chocorua offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are a few popular choices:

  • The Darby Field Inn & Restaurant: This charming inn offers elegant rooms, fine dining, and breathtaking views of Mount Chocorua.
  • Tamworth Camping Area: A family-friendly campground with tent and RV sites, as well as on-site amenities like a playground, pool, and store.
  • Sunny Brook Cottages: These cozy and private cottages offer a peaceful retreat in the heart of the Lakes Region.

What to Buy in Chocorua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home from Chocorua? Here are a few ideas:

  • Local maple syrup: Bring home a bottle (or several!) of New Hampshire’s famous maple syrup.
  • Handmade crafts and gifts: Check out the Tamworth Farmer’s Market or local artisan shops for unique and handmade items.
  • Antique and vintage finds: Tamworth is home to several antique stores and flea markets, where you can find one-of-a-kind treasures and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Chocorua

New Hampshire has a rich history and culture that is deeply rooted in the natural environment. Visitors to Chocorua can expect to encounter a laid-back and friendly community that values outdoor recreation, locally sourced food and drink, and preserving the area’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Chocorua

Chocorua is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a quick guide:

  • Summer (June-August): The busiest season, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Fall (September-November): A popular time to visit for the stunning fall foliage and harvest festivals.
  • Winter (December-February): Perfect for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.
  • Spring (March-May): A quieter season with mild temperatures and early-season outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chocorua

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Chocorua:

Do:

  • Try local food and drink, and support small businesses whenever possible.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor recreational opportunities in the area.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and etiquette.
  • Engage in disruptive or disrespectful behavior.

Safety and Health

Chocorua is a safe and welcoming destination, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their health and safety while traveling.

Safety in Chocorua

  • Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against wildlife like bears and snakes.
  • Follow safety guidelines for outdoor activities like hiking and skiing.
  • Use common sense and take necessary precautions to avoid theft or other crimes.

Health in Chocorua

  • Bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you.
  • Stay hydrated and protected from the sun and insects, especially during the summer months.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health issues or concerns during your trip.

Getting to Chocorua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chocorua is easily accessible by car or bus, with several airports located within driving distance. Here are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Chocorua

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT): Located about 80 miles south of Chocorua.
  • Portland International Jetport (PWM): Located about 60 miles east of Chocorua.

Bus Stations Near Chocorua

  • Concord Trailways: Offers bus service from Boston and other New England locations.
  • Greyhound: Offers bus service from New York City and other major cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Chocorua and explore the surrounding area.
  • Private transportation: Companies like Uber and Lyft offer transportation services in the area.

How to Get Around in Chocorua

While having a car is the most convenient way to get around Chocorua and the surrounding area, there are other transportation options to consider:

  • Bike rental: Several shops in the area offer bike rental services.
  • Local taxi and shuttle services: Contact local companies for transportation services.
  • Public transportation: The Conway Scenic Railroad offers scenic train rides, and the Concord Coach provides bus service to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Chocorua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of New Hampshire and the surrounding area, here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Mount Washington: Hike or ride the cog railway to the summit of the highest peak in the Northeast.
  • Lake Winnipesaukee: New Hampshire’s largest lake offers boating, fishing, and swimming opportunities.
  • Portsmouth: A charming coastal city with a rich history and thriving arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Chocorua, New Hampshire, is a beautiful and fascinating destination with something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or simply a relaxing getaway, Chocorua is sure to leave a lasting impression.
