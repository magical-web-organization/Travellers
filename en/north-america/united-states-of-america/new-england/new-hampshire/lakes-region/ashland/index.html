Introduction to Ashland

Ashland is located in the heart of the Lakes Region of New Hampshire in the northeastern part of the United States. It is a small town with a population of just over 2,000 people, but it offers a wealth of attractions that are sure to delight visitors of all ages.

This charming town is best known for its scenic beauty, offering breathtaking views of the White Mountains, Squam Lakes, and the nearby Squam Range. Ashland’s rich history is also a highlight for visitors, featuring a number of museums and historical landmarks that tell the story of the town’s past. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Ashland has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ashland

Here are some of the most popular attractions to visit in Ashland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pauline E. Glidden Toy Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of toys and features a stunning collection of antique and vintage toys from around the world.

  • Whipple House Museum: The Whipple House is a beautifully preserved 19th-century home that provides a fascinating glimpse into life in Ashland during the 1800s.

  • Squam Lakes Natural Science Center: This nature center is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including bears, deer, and mountain lions. It offers a number of educational exhibits and programs that are perfect for families with children.

Natural Attractions of Ashland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Squam Lakes: Ashland is located on the shores of Squam Lakes, which is the second-largest lake in New Hampshire. Visitors can enjoy swimming, boating, fishing, and other water sports on the lake.

  • Squam Mountains: The Squam Range offers some of the most stunning views in the area, with hiking trails that are suitable for all ages and skill levels.

  • Waukewan Golf Club: This 18-hole golf course is located in the heart of Ashland and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It’s the perfect way to spend a day outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashland

Ashland is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, with a number of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the area’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Ashland:

  • Squam Lakes Natural Science Center: The nature center offers a number of hiking trails that range from easy to challenging. Visitors can see a wide variety of wildlife, including bears, deer, and mountain lions.

  • Squam Range: The Squam Range offers a number of hiking trails that are suitable for all ages and skill levels. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the White Mountains and Squam Lakes.

  • Greenway Trail: The Greenway Trail is a popular biking and hiking trail that follows the shore of Squam Lakes. It offers beautiful views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ashland: Activities and Tours

Ashland offers a number of fun activities and tours that are sure to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Ashland:

  • Experience Squam Boat Tours: Experience Squam Boat Tours offers a number of tours of Squam Lakes, including sunset cruises and wildlife tours.

  • Railroad Depot Museum: The Railroad Depot Museum is located in the heart of Ashland and offers a fascinating look at the town’s railroad history.

  • Ashland Farmers Market: The Ashland Farmers Market is held every Saturday in the summer and is a great place to pick up fresh produce, local crafts, and other treats.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashland

Ashland offers a variety of dining options, from cozy cafes to upscale restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Ashland:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple-glazed pork chop: This delicious dish is a New Hampshire specialty, featuring locally sourced pork that is glazed with sweet maple syrup.

  • Lobster roll: Fresh lobster is the star of this classic New England dish, which is served in a buttery roll and topped with celery and other spices.

  • Apple cider donuts: These sweet treats are a must-try in New Hampshire, featuring fresh apple cider and a dusting of cinnamon sugar.

Local Beverages

  • White Mountain Cider Company: This local cidery produces a wide range of hard ciders that are made from locally sourced apples.

  • Squam Brewing: Squam Brewing is a popular local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and ales.

  • LaBelle Winery: LaBelle Winery is a boutique winery that produces a variety of award-winning wines that are made from locally grown grapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashland

Best Time to Visit Ashland

Getting to Ashland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ashland

The nearest airport to Ashland is Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, which is located approximately 80 miles south of the town.

Bus Stations Near Ashland

The nearest bus station to Ashland is located in Plymouth, which is approximately 10 miles east of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Ashland by car or by train via the Amtrak station in nearby Plymouth.

How to Get Around in Ashland

Ashland is a small town that is best explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding area.
