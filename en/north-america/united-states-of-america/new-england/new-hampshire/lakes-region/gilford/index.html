Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gilford boasts several museums and historical places that showcase the town’s rich heritage and culture. The most prominent ones include:

  • Gunstock Mountain Resort: A popular skiing destination in winter, Gunstock Mountain Resort boasts a rich history that dates back to the 1930s. Visitors can take a guided tour of the resort to learn about its past and construction.
  • Weeks State Park: Located on the summit of Mount Prospect, Weeks State Park offers breathtaking views of the Presidential Range and Vermont’s Green Mountains. The park is also home to a historic mansion and museum that showcases the life and work of John Wingate Weeks, former U.S. Secretary of War.
  • Wright Museum of World War II: Located in nearby Wolfeboro, the Wright Museum of World War II offers a glimpse into the personal and cultural experiences of Americans during the Second World War. The museum houses several exhibits and artifacts, including vehicles, weapons, and uniforms used during the war.

Natural Attractions of Gilford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gilford’s natural beauty is best experienced through its outdoor attractions, which include:

  • Mount Major: A prominent peak in the Belknap Range, Mount Major offers panoramic views of Lake Winnipesaukee and the surrounding mountains. Visitors can hike the popular Boulder Loop Trail to the summit and explore the surrounding forest.
  • Ellacoya State Park: Located on the southeastern shore of Lake Winnipesaukee, Ellacoya State Park offers several water activities, such as swimming, boating, and fishing. Visitors can also enjoy picnicking, camping, and hiking at the park.
  • Belknap Mountain State Forest: A popular destination for hiking and biking, Belknap Mountain State Forest offers several trails that lead to scenic overlooks and waterfalls. The forest is also home to several wildlife species, such as foxes, deer, and bears.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gilford

Gilford’s scenic trails and routes offer endless opportunities for outdoor exploration and adventure. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Gilford include:

  • Belknap Range: A prominent mountain range located west of Lake Winnipesaukee, Belknap Range offers several trails that lead to scenic views and waterfalls. Visitors can hike the popular Mount Major or Piper Mountain trails or bike the Upper and Lower Sunrise trails.
  • Winnipesaukee River Trail: A popular biking trail that connects the towns of Franklin and Tilton, the Winnipesaukee River Trail offers scenic views of the river and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can also explore the nearby parks and wildlife sanctuaries.
  • Greenway Trail: A scenic hiking trail that meanders through Gilford and Belmont, the Greenway Trail offers peaceful views of the nearby lakes and ponds. Visitors can explore the nearby forests and wildlife habitats or fish in the nearby streams.

Best Things to Do in Gilford: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural excursions, Gilford offers a plethora of things to do and see. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Zip-lining at Gunstock Mountain Resort: Visitors can experience a thrilling zip-line tour of Gunstock Mountain Resort, which features eight zip-line segments that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Lake Winnipesaukee Scenic Tour: Visitors can take a scenic boat tour of Lake Winnipesaukee, which offers panoramic views of the lake and the surrounding mountains. The tour also includes stops at the nearby islands and towns.
  • Belknap Fall Foliage Tour: Visitors can take a scenic fall foliage tour of the Belknap Range, which offers breathtaking views of the changing leaves and the surrounding mountains. The tour also includes stops at the nearby towns and attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Gilford

Gilford’s food and drink scene offers a mix of traditional and contemporary flavors, influenced by the region’s agricultural heritage and modern trends. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New England Clam Chowder: A classic dish in New Hampshire, New England clam chowder is a creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, onions, and bacon. It’s commonly served in local restaurants and seafood shacks.
  • Maple-Smoked Ribs: A local specialty, maple-smoked ribs are slow-cooked and marinated with maple syrup, soy sauce, and garlic. It’s commonly served in local barbecue restaurants.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: A classic fall dessert, apple cider donuts are freshly made donuts dusted with cinnamon and sugar and flavored with local apple cider.

Local Beverages

  • Apple Cider: A staple beverage in New Hampshire, apple cider is made with locally grown apples and is commonly served hot or cold.
  • New Hampshire Craft Beer: The region is home to several craft breweries that offer a variety of beers, such as the Smuttynose Brewery and the Moat Mountain Brewery.
  • Locally Grown Wine: The region is also known for its wine production, with several wineries offering wine tasting and tours, such as the Hermit Woods Winery and the LaBelle Winery.
