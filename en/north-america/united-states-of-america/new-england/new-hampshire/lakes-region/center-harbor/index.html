Introduction to Center Harbor

Nestled in the heart of the Lakes Region of New Hampshire, Center Harbor is a charming village surrounded by beautiful lakes, rolling hills, and lush forests. The town is known for its stunning natural beauty, historic sites, and outdoor recreation opportunities. Visitors flock to Center Harbor to enjoy hiking, boating, fishing, skiing, and snowboarding, making it a year-round destination.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Center Harbor, we will cover all the things to do, places to visit, and essential information you need to plan your perfect trip. Whether you are looking for a romantic getaway or a family vacation, Center Harbor has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Center Harbor

Here’s a list of some of the best places to visit in Center Harbor:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Hubbard House - A beautifully restored 18th-century home, now a museum showcasing life in early America.
  • Moultonborough Historical Society Museum - Exhibits showcasing the history of Center Harbor and the surrounding area.
  • Klickety-Klack Railroad Museum - A collection of antique trains and railroad memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Center Harbor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Squam Lake - A stunning lake surrounded by miles of hiking trails and home to abundant wildlife.
  • The Sandwich Range - A range of mountains with breathtaking views and hiking trails for all levels of hikers.
  • White Mountains National Forest - Sprawling natural forest lands with trails, camping, and opportunities to spot moose and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Center Harbor

Center Harbor and its surrounding areas offer excellent hiking and biking options for all levels of enthusiasts. Listed below are some popular trails to explore:

  • Steele Hill Resorts Trail Network - Trail network with multiple trailheads, ranging from easy to strenuous hikes with beautiful mountain views.
  • The Red Hill Trail - A moderate hike with a 360-degree view of Lake Winnipesaukee.
  • The Cotton Valley Trail - Relatively easy hike that follows an old railroad bed along the banks of the Salmon Falls River.

Best Things to Do in Center Harbor: Activities and Tours

With its abundance of outdoor recreational opportunities and cultural attractions, Center Harbor has something for everyone. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Boating and Water Sports - Enjoy boating, kayaking, paddleboarding on the lakes of Center Harbor.
  • Snowboarding and Skiing - Hit the slopes at Gunstock Mountain Resort or Ragged Mountain Resort during the winter months.
  • The M/S Mount Washington Cruise - Take a scenic cruise on the M/S Mount Washington, a historic ship on Lake Winnipesaukee.

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Center Harbor:

Food and Drink Scene of Center Harbor

Center Harbor is a foodie’s paradise with a variety of dining options offering everything from local seafood to internationally-inspired cuisine. Here are some of the must-try local dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls - Freshly caught lobster with loaves of bread, light mayo and celery, and pickle combined to make a filling lunch or dinner.
  • Maple Syrup Dishes - New Hampshire’s maple syrup is famous, and you can taste it in many dishes, from pancakes, sprinkled on oatmeal, or added to a pork glaze.
  • Poutine - A classic Canadian dish of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Winnipesaukee Winery - Specializing in fruit wines made from locally sourced fruits.
  • Center Harbor Distillery - A small batch, craft distillery offering tastings of its homemade spirits.
  • Kelsen Brewing Company - A brewery making a variety of beers from classic pilsners to hoppy IPAs.

Where to Stay in Center Harbor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re seeking tranquility and peace or excitement and parties, there are accommodation options for all tastes. Center Harbor offers a range of options to stay, including:

  • The Manor on Golden Pond - A luxurious bed and breakfast that overlooks Squam Lake.
  • Center Harbor Inn - A waterfront hotel with a private beach and stunning views of Lake Winnipesaukee.
  • Birch Knoll Motel - A cozy and friendly motel within walking distance to downtown Center Harbor.

What to Buy in Center Harbor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to take home some souvenirs and local products to remember your trip to Center Harbor! Here are some items to add to your list:

  • Maple Syrup - A classic New Hampshire souvenir to take home.
  • Handcrafted Wood Products - Locally crafted wood products, including furniture, bowls, and cutting boards, can be found in the village shops.
  • Handmade Soap - A variety of fragrant, handmade soaps made with Shea butter, coconut oils, goat’s milk and local herbs.

Local Culture and Customs of Center Harbor

New Hampshire is known for its “Live Free or Die” attitude, and Center Harbor is no exception. People in the town generally have a friendly, relaxed, and welcoming vibe, so be prepared to strike up a conversation with locals.

Best Time to Visit Center Harbor

While Center Harbor is a year-round destination, the best time to visit depends on your interests. Summer and fall are ideal for outdoor activities, while winter is great for skiing and snowboarding aficionados. Spring is the ideal time for those who prefer the quieter side of Center Harbor as it provides mild weather and peaceful surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Center Harbor

Do:

  • Do bring suitable clothing for the season
  • Do appreciate the natural beauty of the area
  • Do check out the local farmers’ market for fresh produce.

Don’t:

  • Don’t speed on Center Harbor’s scenic roads
  • Don’t forget your sunscreen and insect repellent if heading to the outdoors
  • Don’t overlook the chance to try local flavors

Safety and Health

Safety in Center Harbor

Center Harbor is exceptionally safe. The crime rate is minimal, and visitors generally enjoy a safe and peaceful stay. It is still advisable to take standard precautions, such as not leaving valuables in open view inside your car and taking care when hiking.

Health in Center Harbor

It’s always important to take care of your health while traveling, which includes staying hydrated, eating healthy foods, getting enough sleep, and following social distance norms.

Getting to Center Harbor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Center Harbor

  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS) - 2.5-hour drive from Center Harbor
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT) - 1-hour drive from Center Harbor

Bus Stations Near Center Harbor

  • Concord Coach Lines - Operates daily services from Boston to Concord Station, you’ll then need to connect through to Center Harbor via either a rental car or Uber/Lyft.

How to Get Around in Center Harbor

While it’s true that most attractions in Center Harbor are within walking distance, having your own transportation greatly increases your ability to explore the region at your own pace. Car and bike rentals are available. Taxis are also an option, while boat tours are available for a unique and fun experience.

Where to Go Next from Center Harbor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are numerous places to explore near Center Harbor, including:

  • Meredith, a town renowned for its picturesque Main Street, with its shops, restaurants, and beautiful lake views.
  • Squam Lakes Natural Science Center, a wild animal rehabilitation center and nature reserve.
  • Plymouth, a college town steeped in history, with beautiful architecture, museums, and vibrant cultural life.

Conclusion

Center Harbor is an enchanting destination with something for everyone, from thrilling outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, dining options, and colorful local festivals. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your unforgettable trip to this charming town.
