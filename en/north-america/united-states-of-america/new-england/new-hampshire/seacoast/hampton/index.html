Hampton is a charming town located in Seacoast, New Hampshire. Blessed with natural beauty, rich history, and a vibrant cultural scene, Hampton has something for everyone. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, a history buff, or a culture vulture, Hampton is a destination that won’t disappoint.

Introduction to Hampton

Hampton is a small town located in the southeastern corner of New Hampshire, United States. It’s situated on a peninsula between the Atlantic Ocean and the Hampton River. Hampton is known for its pristine beaches, quaint shops, and historic landmarks. It’s a popular summer destination, attracting tourists from all over the country.

List of Places to Visit in Hampton

If you’re planning a trip to Hampton, here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tuck Museum: A museum that showcases Hampton’s rich history through exhibits of artifacts, photographs, art, and more.
  • Hampton Historical Society: A society that collects and preserves documents and other materials related to the town’s history.
  • American Independence Museum: Located in nearby Exeter, the museum features exhibits, events, and educational programs that focus on the history of the American Revolution and the founding of the United States.

Natural Attractions of Hampton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hampton Beach: A wide and sandy beach that stretches for miles. It’s a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
  • Hampton Salt Marsh Conservation Area: A nature reserve that’s home to a diverse range of wildlife, including migratory birds, fish, and crustaceans.
  • Fuller Gardens: A botanical garden that boasts beautiful blooms, paths, and fountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hampton

  • Hampton Beach State Park: A park that features hiking trails and biking paths along the coast.
  • Rail Trail: A 21-mile trail that runs from Hampton to Newfields and is popular among bikers and hikers.

Best Things to Do in Hampton: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Take a tour from Hampton Harbor and witness the majesty of the world’s largest mammals.
  • Tasting Tour: Discover the region’s craft beverages and cuisine on a guided tour of local establishments.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hampton

Day 1 - Visit the Tuck Museum and walk around Hampton’s historical district. Day 2 - Spend the day at Hampton Beach and take in the ocean views. Day 3 - Go on a whale watching tour from Hampton Harbor Day 4 - Visit Fuller Gardens and explore the Hampton Salt Marsh Conservation Area Day 5 - Take a tasting tour of local craft breweries, wineries, and restaurants. Day 6 - Rent a bike and ride along the Rail Trail to see the sights around Hampton Day 7 - Visit the American Independence Museum in Exeter

Food and Drink Scene of Hampton

Hampton has a diverse food and drink scene that is sure to satisfy any cravings.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic New England dish made with fresh lobster meat, butter, and mayo.
  • Seafood Chowder: A creamy soup that’s loaded with clams, shrimp, and other seafood.
  • Fried Clams: Bite-sized pieces of clams that are breaded and fried to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Smuttynose Brewing Company: A local brewery that produces a range of beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Flag Hill Distillery and Winery: A craft distillery and winery that produces award-winning wines, spirits, and hard ciders.

Where to Stay in Hampton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hampton has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference.

  • Ashworth by the Sea: A historic hotel located right on Hampton Beach.
  • Lamie’s Inn and Tavern: A charming inn that features a restaurant and bar.
  • Hampton House Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that’s located just a short walk from the beach.

What to Buy in Hampton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lobster-themed souvenirs: From t-shirts to coffee mugs, you’ll find plenty of lobster-themed merchandise to take home with you.
  • Local honey: Made from bees that gather nectar from local flowers, Hampton’s honey is a delicious souvenir.
  • Handmade crafts: Hampton has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and you’ll find plenty of locally made pottery, jewelry, and other items.

Local Culture and Customs of Hampton

Hampton is known for its laid-back, beachy vibe. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and the town has a rich and fascinating history that’s worth exploring. When visiting Hampton, it’s important to be respectful of the town’s natural beauty and to follow the local etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Hampton

The best time to visit Hampton is during the summer months, from June to September when the weather is warm, and the beaches are open. However, for outdoor enthusiasts, fall is also an excellent time to visit, when the foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hampton

Do:

  • Do visit the local museums and historical landmarks to learn about the town’s rich history.
  • Do take in the beauty of the natural areas and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Do try the local seafood - it’s some of the freshest in the world.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural areas.
  • Don’t be late - punctuality is highly valued in New Hampshire.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask the locals for recommendations - they’re usually happy to help.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hampton

Hampton is generally a safe town, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Be sure to lock your car and keep your valuables out of sight. When swimming at the beach, be aware of the conditions and follow the lifeguards’ instructions.

Health in Hampton

There are several medical facilities in Hampton, including hospitals and clinics. It’s important to have valid medical insurance when traveling, as medical costs can be high.

Getting to Hampton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hampton

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: Located about 40 miles west of Hampton.
  • Boston Logan International Airport: Located about 50 miles south of Hampton.

Bus Stations Near Hampton

  • New Hampshire in Portsmouth: Located about 11 miles from Hampton.
  • Transit Center in Exeter: Located about 5 miles from Hampton.

Other Options

  • Car rental: A good option if you plan to explore the area.
  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft: Available in the area.

How to Get Around in Hampton

Walking and biking are popular ways to get around in Hampton, but having a car is also recommended, especially if you plan to explore other parts of New Hampshire. There are also several taxi and rideshare services available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Hampton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portsmouth: A charming coastal town located about 11 miles north of Hampton.
  • Exeter: A historic town located about 5 miles west of Hampton.
  • White Mountains: A stunning mountain range located about 100 miles north of Hampton.

Conclusion

Hampton, New Hampshire, is a beautiful town that has something for everyone. From its picturesque beaches to its fascinating history and vibrant cultural scene, Hampton is a destination that won’t disappoint. Follow this travel guide to plan your trip and experience the best that Hampton has to offer.
