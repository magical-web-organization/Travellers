Introduction to Plaistow

Located in the Seacoast region of New Hampshire, Plaistow is a quaint town brimming with small-town charm and natural beauty. It is situated in Rockingham County and is home to a population of approximately 8,000 people. Plaistow is a perfect destination for those seeking a getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. It’s the kind of place where visitors can enjoy a peaceful retreat, surrounded by serene natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Plaistow

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a food lover, Plaistow has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Plaistow:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plaistow Historical Society Museum: The museum features artifacts and exhibits that tell the history of Plaistow from the early 18th century. It’s a must-visit place for history enthusiasts.
  • Rockingham Park: Located in nearby Salem, Rockingham Park is a historic horse racing track that has been in operation since 1906.

Natural Attractions of Plaistow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Trails End Ice Cream: Located in the heart of Plaistow, Trails End Ice Cream offers a mouth-watering array of homemade ice cream flavors that visitors can enjoy in a tranquil, relaxed environment.
  • Plaistow Town Forest: The town forest is a popular destination for nature lovers. It features a vast network of hiking trails that wind through forests, fields, and wetlands.
  • Timberlane Regional High School Memorial Forest: The memorial forest is a serene and peaceful place located in the Timberlane Regional High School grounds. It’s a great place for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plaistow

Nestled in the Seacoast region, Plaistow offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in the area. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Plaistow:

  • North Region Water Supply Trail: A tranquil trail that winds through the forests alongside the Merrimack River.
  • Sawyers Crossing Road Trail: A scenic trail that offers magnificent views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Rockingham Recreation Trail: A flat, paved trail that runs through Plaistow, connecting the town to nearby Salem and Manchester.

Best Things to Do in Plaistow: Activities and Tours

Plaistow offers an abundance of activities to keep visitors entertained throughout their stay. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone, including:

  • Plaistow Summer Concert Series: A series of concerts held each summer featuring local and national acts.
  • The Great State of New Hampshire Air Show: A biannual event held at the Portsmouth International Airport that features thrilling aviation performances and displays.
  • Farmer’s Market: An open-air market held each weekend during the summer months featuring the freshest produce, handmade crafts, and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Plaistow

Plaistow has a diverse food and drink scene that will satisfy even the most discerning of palates. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Plaistow:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A New England staple, the lobster roll is a must-try in Plaistow. The local seafood restaurants serve some of the freshest catches of the day.
  • Pizza: Plaistow has a plethora of pizza places that serve mouth-watering pies with creative toppings.
  • Apple Pie: Visit the local orchards and indulge in authentic New England-style apple pie.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Plaistow has a vibrant craft beer scene, with breweries like Smuttynose Brewing and Great Rhythm Brewing Company.
  • Cider: New Hampshire is known for its apple orchards, and Plaistow is no exception. Local cider mills like Appleview Orchard and Sunnycrest Farm offer refreshing drinks that visitors can enjoy all year round.

Getting to Plaistow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Plaistow is located in the Seacoast region of New Hampshire and is easily accessible through several transportation options. Here are the most convenient ways to get to Plaistow:

Airports Near Plaistow

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is the nearest airport to Plaistow and is located approximately 30 miles away.
  • Boston Logan International Airport is located about an hour and a half away from Plaistow.

Bus Stations Near Plaistow

  • Boston Express runs a regular bus service to Plaistow from Boston, Manchester, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Plaistow can be reached by car as I-495 runs through the city.
  • Visitors can also access Plaistow through several Amtrak train stations in the nearby cities.
