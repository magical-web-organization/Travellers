Introduction to Odiorne Point State Park

Odiorne Point State Park is a beautiful and historic park located in Seacoast, New Hampshire on the Atlantic coast. This stunning 330-acre park offers visitors breathtaking views of the ocean, as well as expansive trails for hiking and biking, and plenty of opportunities to get up close with wildlife. As a popular destination for history buffs, the park has several museums and exhibits that showcase the area’s rich cultural history.

List of Places to Visit in Odiorne Point State Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Seacoast Science Center: The Seacoast Science Center is a must-visit museum that provides visitors with hands-on exhibits and educational programs that focus on the ecology, geology, and history of the area.
  • Odiorne Point Military Reservation: This historic site served as a military base during World War II and is now open to the public as a museum. Visitors can explore the bunkers, learn about the military’s role in the area, and enjoy stunning views of the ocean.
  • Wentworth-Gardner House: A restored historic home from the 18th century, showcasing the lives of mariners and merchants who resided there in the past.

Natural Attractions of Odiorne Point State Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaches: The park features a gorgeous rocky beach, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or a long walk along the shoreline.
  • Trails: Explore the park’s diverse landscape through a network of scenic trails, which vary from short and easy to challenging and long. The park has around five miles of trails, including bike trails which are perfect for cycling.
  • Pierce Island: Located at the end of Flint Point Road, this small park offers visitors a peaceful walk along the estuary and observation platforms to view migratory and nesting birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Odiorne Point State Park

Odiorne Point State Park has something for hikers and bikers of all levels. The trails range in length, intensity, and scenery ensuring there is a hike or bike ride designed for your interests. Some notable hiking routes include the Boulder Loop trail, Salt Marsh Trail, and the Seacoast Trail. There are also numerous biking trails to consider, which include the Artesian Wells Trail, Camping Loop Road, and the Ocean loop trail.

Best Things to Do in Odiorne Point State Park: Activities and Tours

The park offers an endless list of activities and tours you can enjoy throughout your stay, whether you are visiting alone or with family. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Whale watching tour: Head out to sea to see inshore species such as humpback or Minke whales. The tours usually get close to the whales, making for a spectacular show.
  • Kayaking: Odiorne Point State Park features a peaceful estuary, which is perfect for a kayaking adventure. Kayak rentals are available at the visitors’ center.
  • Interpretive programs: The park features a range of daily events and interpretive programs that provide insight into the area’s ecology, history or cultural significance. Check the calendar and make time to attend one while staying at the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Odiorne Point State Park

Day 1: Arrive in Seacoast, NH, check in at one of the lodgings and spend the rest of the day exploring the park’s wonders.

Day 2-3: Go hiking on one of the numerous trails or take a kayak through the stunning estuary.

Day 4: Delve into the past and explore the park’s cultural and historic sites.

Day 5: Go on a whale watching tour or take a day trip to the nearby historic port city of Portsmouth.

Day 6-7: Take a bike ride, explore the rocky beach, picnic or just relax in the park before departing on day 7.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A thinly sliced-sweetbread roll packed with lobster meat and seasoned with a splash of lemon.
  • New England Clam Chowder: A hearty soup consisting of clams and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Flag Hill Winery and Vineyard: One of New Hampshire’s best-known wineries, producing a range of wines using locally sourced grapes.

Best Time to Visit Odiorne Point State Park

The best time to visit Odiorne Point State Park is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities. However, if you are looking to avoid the crowds, consider visiting during the late spring or fall when the park is quieter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Odiorne Point State Park

The park is a safe and secure environment. However, it is still advisable to take safety precautions when exploring the park, such as wearing comfortable walking shoes, bringing along water and snacks, and adhering to the trail’s safety guidelines.

Health in Odiorne Point State Park

The park has a limited availability of medical facilities within the park. Travelers are advised to bring necessary medications and receive any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Odiorne Point State Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Odiorne Point State Park

The nearest airport to Odiorne Point State Park is Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, which is located about 45 miles away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Odiorne Point State Park

The Concord Trailways Bus Station is the nearest bus station to the park, located in Portsmouth about 10 minutes away from the park.

Other Options

Taxis, ride-hailing services, and private car rentals are readily available throughout the region, making it easy to reach the park.

How to Get Around in Odiorne Point State Park

The best way to explore Odiorne Point State Park is by foot or by bike. Visitors can rent bicycles at the Seacoast Science Center at the entrance to the park. The trails at the park are well marked, which makes getting around the park easy and enjoyable.

Where to Go Next from Odiorne Point State Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Odiorne Point State Park is located near several notable attractions, making it easy to plan day trips and excursions. Some of our top nearby destinations include:

  • Portsmouth: a historic port city with plenty of good restaurants, quaint shops and scenic paths.
  • White Mountains: a beautiful mountain range located about 150 miles away from the park, home to scenic drives and hikes.
  • Boston: a vibrant city with a rich cultural history, located about 60 miles away from the park.
