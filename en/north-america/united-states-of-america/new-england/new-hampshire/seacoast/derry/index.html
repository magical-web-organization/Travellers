Introduction to Derry

Derry, located in Seacoast, New Hampshire, is a small but vibrant town that is rich in history and natural beauty. It’s known for its charming downtown area with a variety of shops and restaurants, as well as its outdoor recreation opportunities.

Derry is also home to several cultural attractions, including museums and historic sites, that offer a glimpse into the town’s past. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-filled vacation, Derry has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Derry

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Robert Frost Farm: This National Historic Landmark Museum is the former home of poet Robert Frost and is open to visitors for guided tours.
  • Taylor Mill Historic Site: This 1794 gristmill is a fascinating example of early American industry.
  • Derry Historical Society and Museum: This museum is housed in a restored 19th-century schoolhouse and showcases the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Derry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alexander-Carr Park: This park offers a variety of outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking trails, a fishing pond, and a picnic area.
  • Don Ball Park: This park has walking trails, playgrounds, and sports fields.
  • Grinnell Farms: This family-owned farm offers pick-your-own blueberries and other seasonal produce.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Derry

Derry is a great destination for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Rail Trail: This 26-mile trail runs between Derry and Lebanon and offers scenic views of New Hampshire’s countryside.
  • Candia-Derry Rail Trail: This 4-mile trail is perfect for a short hike or bike ride and runs along an old railroad bed.
  • Hood Park Trail: This trail runs through Hood Park and offers a beautiful view of Beaver Lake.

Best Things to Do in Derry: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing at McIntyre Ski Area: This ski area is located just a few miles from downtown Derry and offers skiing and snowboarding lessons for visitors of all ages.
  • Canobie Lake Park: This amusement park has rides and attractions for the whole family, including roller coasters, water rides, and a variety of games.
  • Mosquito Squad of Southern New Hampshire: This unique tour takes visitors through the historic district of Derry with a focus on the town’s many historic homes and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Derry

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boston Chicken and Fish: This restaurant specializes in fried chicken and seafood.
  • Bada Bing: This Italian restaurant is known for its pizza and pasta dishes.
  • The Grind Rail Trail Cafe: This cafe offers coffee, sandwiches, and baked goods for visitors on-the-go.

Local Beverages

  • 603 Brewery: This microbrewery offers a variety of craft beers, including pale ales, lagers, and stouts.
  • Moonlight Meadery: This local meadery produces a variety of honey-based beverages, including meads, ciders, and fruit wines.

Where to Stay in Derry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn & Suites: This hotel offers spacious rooms and suites, as well as a fitness center and indoor pool.
  • Sleep Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers basic amenities and is located near Derry’s downtown area.
  • The Inn at East Hill Farm: This guesthouse offers comfortable accommodations and is located on a working farm.

What to Buy in Derry: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hood Park Trail Map: This map is a great souvenir for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is known for its maple syrup, and several local farms sell their own.
  • Antiques: Derry has a variety of antique shops, making it a great place to find unique treasures.

Local Culture and Customs of Derry

Derry’s rich history is an important part of its culture. Visitors should take time to explore the town’s historic sites and landmarks. Additionally, visitors should be aware that Derry is a small, close-knit community, and should show respect for local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Derry

The best time to visit Derry is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. The fall is also a great time to visit, as the leaves on the trees change colors and the town hosts a variety of fall festivals and events. Winter is a great time to visit for skiing and snowboarding, but visitors should be prepared for cold temperatures and snowstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Derry

Do:

  • Take time to explore Derry’s many historic sites and landmarks.
  • Visit local farms and try seasonal produce.
  • Attend a performance at the Derry Opera House.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the town’s natural areas.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Derry

Derry is generally a safe and welcoming community. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking doors and securing valuables in their vehicles.

Health in Derry

There are several hospitals and medical clinics in the Derry area. Visitors should have health insurance and be prepared to seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Derry: Transportation Options and Logistics

Derry is easily accessible by car and bus. The town is located just off of Interstate 93, making it easily accessible from Boston and other New England cities. There are also several bus stations located nearby, including the Concord Coach Lines bus station located in downtown Derry.

Airports Near Derry

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: This airport is located about 20 miles from Derry and offers flights to several major cities across the country.

Bus Stations Near Derry

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus station is located in downtown Derry and offers daily service to Boston and other cities in New England.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The nearest Amtrak station is located in Boston, about 40 miles from Derry.
  • Rental Cars: Several rental car agencies are located in Derry and the surrounding area, including Hertz and Enterprise.

How to Get Around in Derry

Derry is a small town that is easy to navigate by car or on foot. Biking is also a popular way to get around the town, with several bike trails located nearby.

Where to Go Next from Derry: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manchester: This city is located about 20 miles from Derry and is home to several museums and cultural attractions.
  • Concord: This city is located about 30 miles from Derry and is the state capital of New Hampshire.
  • Seacoast: This coastal region is located about 30 miles from Derry and offers a variety of outdoor recreation opportunities, including beaches, hiking trails, and wildlife refuges.

Conclusion

Derry, New Hampshire, is a small but vibrant town that is rich in history and natural beauty. Visitors will find a variety of cultural attractions and outdoor recreation opportunities, as well as plenty of restaurants and shops to explore. With its location just a short drive from Boston, Derry is a great destination for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation.
