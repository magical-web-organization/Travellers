Introduction to Durham

Durham is a charming town nestled in Seacoast, New Hampshire, widely known for its prestigious educational institution, the University of New Hampshire. The town has a fascinating history dating back to the 1700s, which is evident from the numerous heritage sites dotted around the town. Durham’s beautiful landscapes, countless recreational activities, and rich history make it a perfect destination for history buffs, nature enthusiasts, and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Durham

Durham has no shortage of attractions that cater to a wide variety of interests. From historical sites and museums to natural attractions and outdoor excursions, there is an activity for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • University of New Hampshire Museum of Art: The museum showcases a variety of contemporary art pieces, paintings, and sculptures. Admission is free and open to the public.

  • Durham Historic Association Museum & John Hatch House: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the town’s rich history and its impact on the region’s development. It features exhibitions of the town’s colonial and agricultural heritage, including an impressive collection of photographs. Admission is $5 per person.

  • Emery Farm: A historic farm in the heart of Durham, preserved with its antique equipment for farming and carriage rides, Emery Farm has been operating since 1660 and offers tours and seasonal events that should not be missed.

Natural Attractions of Durham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wagon Hill Farm: A vast tract of farmland and fields that includes rolling hills and tidal marshes. A great spot for hiking, birdwatching, picnicking, and fishing.

  • Oyster River Wildlife Refuge: This 81-acre preserve offers some of the best birdwatching opportunities in New Hampshire. The marshes and tidal creeks are also home to some unique plant and wildlife species, including rare birds.

  • Great Bay National Wildlife Refuge: The Great Bay is a large estuary that covers around 10,000 acres of land and attracts visitors who come to watch the birds and enjoy the breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Durham

Durham is a recreational paradise with its many hiking and biking trails that offer beautiful scenery and miles of adventure. Some of the best hiking and bike trails include:

  • College Woods Trail: A beautiful, serene, and peaceful place for hiking and cycling with over five miles of trails to traverse.

  • Powder Major’s Farm and Forest: Located in the beautiful Oyster River Valley, the farm features a well-preserved barn and many hiking trails in a lush and serene environment.

  • Moharimet Hill Trail: This trail is known for its wildflowers, great views of the seacoast, and is perfect for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Durham: Activities and Tours

  • UNH Observatory: A lovely spot in Durham to view the stars if you’re an astronomy enthusiast.

  • Durham Farmer’s Market: Taking place in the Museum parking lot every Saturday during the summer season, this is one of the best farmer’s markets in the region.

  • Durham Craft Fair: The Durham craft fair is held every year on the weekend before Thanksgiving, and features over a hundred fine craftsmen and artists from around the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Durham

A week is plenty of time to explore Durham fully. Here’s a sample itinerary covering some of the top attractions:

Day Itinerary
1 Visit the University of New Hampshire Museum of Art, explore the campus, and take a guided tour of the university.
2 Visit the Durham Historic Association Museum & John Hatch House and then enjoy a picnic at Wagon Hill Farm.
3 Hike the trails at Moharimet Hill Trail and enjoy the serene and beautiful views of the surrounding area.
4 Marvel at the Great Bay National Wildlife Refuge, go bird watching, and take in the stunning scenery.
5 Stroll around the Durham Farmer’s Market and collect local products and souvenirs.
6 Rent a bike and head out to Powder Major’s Farm and Forest for a picturesque bike ride.
7 Attend a concert or watch a show at the Johnson Theatre on the university campus.

Food and Drink Scene of Durham

Durham offers visitors a dynamic culinary scene with plenty of eateries serving up delicacies that will tantalize your taste buds.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: No visit to New England is complete without trying a classic lobster roll. The UNH Dairy Bar serves up one of the best lobster rolls in town.

  • Seafood Platter: Fresh seafood abounds at Durham’s restaurants and is typically served with French fries and coleslaw, be sure to try this local delicacy.

  • Blueberry Pie: Blueberries are abundant in Durham, and you can try this sweet treat at the UNH Dairy Bar, Betty’s Kitchen, and other local eateries.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Durham has several local breweries, including the Great Rhythm Brewing Company, and many pubs where you can enjoy a cold brew.

  • Apple Cider: Apple cider is a delicious fall beverage that is popular throughout the region, and Durham makes some of the best.

  • Wine: The Durham Vineyard is located in nearby Lee, NH, and has a tasting room where you can sample and purchase their wine varieties.

Where to Stay in Durham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Durham offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors with varying budgets and preferences. Some of the popular stays include:

  • Three Chimneys Inn: A luxurious boutique hotel located in nearby Durham, known for its elegant rooms, fine dining, and personal service.

  • Holiday Inn Express: Located just two miles from the university, the hotel offers comfortable rooms, a free continental breakfast, and an outdoor pool. A great option for those on a budget.

  • Airbnb: Durham has several Airbnb listings that offer a homey feeling and a chance to live like a local.

What to Buy in Durham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Durham has many fine souvenirs and local products to take back home. Some of the best items to pick up include:

  • Maple Syrup: Made from the region’s abundant maple trees, the syrup is a sweet and popular New England staple.

  • Blueberries: A delicious fruit that is abundant in Durham and is often used in local desserts and baked goods.

  • Local Crafts: The Durham craft fair is an excellent place to find some beautiful and unique handmade items.

Local Culture and Customs of Durham

Durham has a rich and fascinating culture influenced by its agricultural heritage and university atmosphere. The locals are friendly, and visitors are always welcome to explore and learn about the town’s history and customs.

Best Time to Visit Durham

The best time to visit Durham is during the fall season from mid-September to mid-November when the trees are ablaze with color, and the weather is perfect for outdoor adventures. Spring is also an excellent time to visit, especially if you plan on hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Durham

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Durham is a beautiful town known for its natural attractions and outdoor excursions.

  • Try Local Food: New England cuisine is unique and offers many delicious treats not found elsewhere in the country.

  • Embrace the Culture: Durham is steeped in history and tradition. Visitors should take the time to absorb all that the town has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment: Durham’s pristine landscapes are a part of its charm. Visitors should treat it with respect by not littering or polluting.

  • Be loud and boisterous: Durham is a peaceful town, and visitors should be respectful of their neighbors and keep the noise down.

Safety and Health

Safety in Durham

Durham is a safe town overall, but visitors should always exercise caution when exploring the outdoors. Be cautious around cliffs or steep ledges and stay on marked trails.

Health in Durham

Durham has several medical facilities and hospitals that visitors can access in the case of an emergency.

Getting to Durham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Durham

  • Manchester - Boston Regional Airport: Located 50 minutes from Durham and served by several major carriers.

Bus Stations Near Durham

  • Durham Transportation Center: Located in the heart of the town, it is a hub for local and inter-city buses.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate at the airport and in town.

How to Get Around in Durham

Durham is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot, bicycle or by car. The town also has local and inter-city buses that visitors can use to get around.

Where to Go Next from Durham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Portsmouth, NH

Portsmouth is located just 12 miles from Durham and is an excellent place to visit for its historical significance. The town is known for having an active port, beautiful beaches, and sea forts.

Old Orchard Beach, ME

Located around an hour from Durham, Old Orchard Beach is one of the finest beaches in Maine. It’s a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water-based sports.

Conclusion

Durham is a hidden gem in the Seacoast region of New Hampshire, with so much to offer visitors. With its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, this lovely town is perfect for a weekend getaway or a longer stay. From hiking trails and historical museums to local cuisine and beautiful accommodations, Durham is an excellent place to explore.
