Introduction to Dover

Dover, a city in the Seacoast region of New Hampshire, is a perfect blend of history and modernity. It offers a variety of attractions, including museums, parks, hiking and biking trails, food and drink, and more. Whether you’re interested in exploring the history and culture of the city, enjoying outdoor adventures, or just relaxing and soaking up the local atmosphere, Dover has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Dover

Here are the top places to visit in Dover:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Woodman Institute Museum: This museum depicts the history of Dover and showcases various artifacts related to different cultures and civilizations.
  • The Children’s Museum of New Hampshire: Perfect for families with young children, this museum offers a variety of exhibits and interactive activities to learn while having fun.
  • The Garrison Hill Tower: Head to the top of the tower for stunning views of the city and surrounding landscape.
  • The Hamilton House: Located just outside Dover, this historic mansion was built in the late 18th century and is worth visiting for its beautiful architecture and lovely gardens.

Natural Attractions of Dover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bellamy River Wildlife Sanctuary: A great spot for birdwatching and hiking, the sanctuary offers several trails of different lengths and difficulty levels.
  • Dover Community Trail: A picturesque biking and walking trail that covers more than two miles along the Cocheco River.
  • Garrison Grounds: A beautiful park with large open spaces, picnic areas, and walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dover

Dover is known for its excellent hiking and biking trails. The following trails are popular with visitors and locals alike:

  • The Bellamy River Wildlife Sanctuary Trail: A 2.6-mile trail that leads through the woods and along the river, offering beautiful views.
  • Wiggly Bridge and Steedman Woods Trail: This 2.5-mile trail winds through the woods and past the Wiggly Bridge, a historic suspension bridge.
  • Cocheco River Walk: This scenic trail winds through downtown Dover along the river and features many historic buildings and monuments.

Best Things to Do in Dover: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities to do in Dover:

  • Take a Walking Tour: Join a guided walking tour and explore the historic buildings and landmarks of Dover.
  • Go on a Food Tour: Discover the best local cuisine and taste some of the city’s most delicious dishes on a food tour.
  • Take a Day Trip: Visit nearby attractions such as Portsmouth, Kittery, or the White Mountains for a fun-filled day trip.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Dover, here’s a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Dover

Dover is known for its foodie culture and boasts many great restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Dover’s proximity to the ocean means that it has a great seafood scene. Try some of the fresh local oysters, lobsters, clams, and fish.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: These donuts are a local favorite, made with fresh apple cider and cinnamon sugar.
  • Cream of Crab Soup: A delicious and creamy soup made with locally sourced crab meat.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Dover has a thriving craft beer scene, with several microbreweries offering unique and flavorful brews. Try some of the local favorites, such as Garrison City Beerworks or 7th Settlement Brewery.
  • Wine: Sample the local wines at Dover Vineyards, a family-owned winery that produces award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Dover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dover offers a variety of accommodations to suit different tastes and budgets. Here are some options:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Portsmouth/Dover: A modern hotel located on the outskirts of Dover, offering comfortable rooms and convenient amenities.
  • The Silver Fountain Inn: A charming Bed and Breakfast with a historic feel, located in downtown Dover.
  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham Dover: An affordable hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Dover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Dover, don’t forget to take some souvenirs and local products home with you. Here are some ideas:

  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is famous for its maple syrup, and Dover is no exception. Pick up a bottle of locally made maple syrup to take home.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Several local artisans make beautiful handmade jewelry using local materials such as sea glass or gemstones.
  • Local Artwork: Dover has a thriving art scene, and you’ll find many galleries and shops selling unique and beautiful pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Dover

Dover is a friendly and welcoming city, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Etiquette: Dover is a relatively conservative city, and visitors should avoid behavior or clothing that is too revealing or offensive.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in restaurants and for other services, and the standard rate is around 15-20%.
  • Outdoor Recreation: Visitors should respect the environment and avoid leaving trash or disturbing wildlife while enjoying the natural attractions in Dover.

Best Time to Visit Dover

Dover is a great destination year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. Summer is the busiest time, with warm weather and many festivals and events. Fall offers beautiful scenery with the changing leaves, and winter is perfect for skiing and other winter sports. Spring is a quieter time, with mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dover

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Dover:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drink: Dover’s foodie scene is one of its highlights, so don’t miss out on the chance to sample some of the local cuisine.
  • Explore the outdoors: Dover is known for its natural beauty, so be sure to take advantage of the hiking and biking trails and parks.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Always dispose of your trash properly and respect the environment.
  • Be rude or disrespectful: Dover is a friendly and welcoming city, so don’t ruin that reputation by being rude or disrespectful to locals or other travelers.
  • Forget to tip: Tipping is customary in Dover, so make sure to leave a gratuity for good service.

Safety and Health

Dover is generally a safe city, and visitors should not encounter any significant safety concerns. However, it is always important to take basic precautions, such as locking your valuables in the hotel safe and being aware of your surroundings. As for health, visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccines and take precautions against ticks if visiting during the summer months.

Getting to Dover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dover is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options:

  • Airports Near Dover: The nearest airport is Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, located about 45 minutes away by car.
  • Bus Stations Near Dover: The closest bus stations are in Portsmouth and Durham, both about 15-20 minutes away by car.
  • Other Options: Dover is also accessible by train via Amtrak’s Downeaster route.

How to Get Around in Dover

Dover is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot, but visitors can also use public transportation or rent a bike or car. The city also has a taxi service, and Uber and Lyft are available as well.

Where to Go Next from Dover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dover is located in a region with many other great destinations, making it a great base for exploring the area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Portsmouth: A historic coastal town with charming shops, restaurants, and museums.
  • Kittery: A cute seaside town across the state border in Maine, known for its outlet shopping and beaches.
  • White Mountains: About 2-3 hours north of Dover, this mountain range offers stunning scenery and outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.

Conclusion

Dover is a hidden gem in New Hampshire, offering a perfect blend of nature, history, and modern amenities. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to plan a perfect trip to Dover, complete with top attractions, activities, food and drink, accommodations, and nearby excursions. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this charming Seacoast city!
