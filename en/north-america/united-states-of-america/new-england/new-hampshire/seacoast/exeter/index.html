Introduction to Exeter

Located in the Seacoast region of New Hampshire, Exeter is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of outdoor activities. Despite being a small town, Exeter has a lot to offer visitors. From cultural attractions to natural beauty, there is something for everyone in Exeter.

List of Places to Visit in Exeter

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, Exeter has plenty of options for you.

American Independence Museum

Located in a historic building in downtown Exeter, the American Independence Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the American Revolution. The museum features exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of the founding of the United States.

Exeter Historical Society

The Exeter Historical Society operates several historic properties in Exeter, including the Ladd-Gilman House and the Folsom Tavern. These properties offer a glimpse into life in colonial-era New Hampshire.

Natural Attractions of Exeter: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Exeter is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures.

Swasey Parkway

Located along the banks of the Exeter River, Swasey Parkway is a beautiful park with walking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the river.

Great Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve

The Great Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve is a protected area of tidal wetlands and forested uplands. Visitors can explore the reserve by kayak or on foot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Exeter

Exeter River Trail

The Exeter River Trail is a 2.4-mile trail that runs along the Exeter River. The trail is popular with walkers, runners, and cyclists.

Kingston State Park

Located just outside Exeter, Kingston State Park offers hiking and biking trails, as well as swimming and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Exeter: Activities and Tours

Phillips Exeter Academy Tour

Phillips Exeter Academy is one of the most prestigious prep schools in the country. Visitors can take a guided tour of the campus to learn more about its history and architecture.

Blue Hawk Aviation

For an adrenaline-packed adventure, check out Blue Hawk Aviation. This aviation company offers helicopter tours and flight lessons.

Food and Drink Scene of Exeter

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Roll

As a coastal town, Exeter is known for its seafood. The lobster roll is a must-try dish, featuring fresh lobster meat served on a buttery roll.

Poutine

While poutine originated in Canada, it has become a popular dish in Exeter. This hearty dish features fries topped with gravy and cheese curds.

Local Beverages

Smuttynose Brewing Company

Located in nearby Hampton, the Smuttynose Brewing Company offers brewery tours and a tasting room. Their beers are available throughout New Hampshire and beyond.

Flag Hill Winery & Distillery

Located in Lee, just a short drive from Exeter, Flag Hill Winery & Distillery offers tours and tastings of their wine and spirits.

Where to Stay in Exeter: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Exeter Inn

The Exeter Inn is a historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Exeter. The inn has been in operation since 1932 and has hosted notable guests such as Eleanor Roosevelt and Daniel Webster.

Hampton Inn & Suites Exeter

Located just outside of downtown Exeter, the Hampton Inn & Suites Exeter offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Exeter: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Applecrest Farm Orchards

Located in nearby Hampton Falls, Applecrest Farm Orchards is a great place to pick up some locally-grown produce and goods.

Water Street Bookstore

For a unique souvenir, check out Water Street Bookstore. This independent bookstore offers a variety of books by local authors, as well as gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Exeter

Exeter is a tight-knit community with a strong sense of civic pride. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs, and take time to appreciate the town’s history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Exeter

The best time to visit Exeter is in the summer, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the foliage in New Hampshire is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Exeter

Do:

  • Try the lobster roll.
  • Take a hike in the Great Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve.
  • Visit the American Independence Museum.

Don’t:

  • Speeding on the local roads.
  • Litter in the town or the Great Bay.

Safety and Health

Safety in Exeter

Exeter is a safe town, but visitors should still take standard precautions like locking their doors and securing their valuables.

Health in Exeter

Visitors to Exeter should be aware that ticks are common in the area. Make sure to wear long pants and use insect repellent if hiking or spending time outside.

Getting to Exeter: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Exeter

The closest airport to Exeter is Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, which is about 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Exeter

Exeter is served by the C&J bus company, which offers daily service to Boston and New York City.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Exeter, I-95 runs through the town. Exeter is about an hour from Boston and 45 minutes from Portsmouth.

How to Get Around in Exeter

The best way to get around Exeter is by walking or biking. Many of the town’s attractions are within walking distance of each other, and there are several bike rental shops in the area.

Where to Go Next from Exeter: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Portsmouth

Located just 15 minutes from Exeter, Portsmouth is a charming coastal city with plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

White Mountains

For a scenic day trip, head to the White Mountains, which are about 2 hours from Exeter. You can take a scenic drive or go hiking in the mountains.

Conclusion

Exeter may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in history, hiking, or just relaxing by the river, there is something for everyone in Exeter.
