Introduction to Pittsburg

Located in the Great North Region of New Hampshire, Pittsburg is a small town near the Canadian border in the heart of the Great North Woods. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Pittsburg offers visitors the chance to explore the great outdoors, from fishing and canoeing to hiking and snowmobiling. With rich cultural history and quaint local charm, Pittsburg is the perfect place to get away from it all.

List of Places to Visit in Pittsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Pittsburg is primarily known for its natural beauty, the town also offers some fascinating cultural attractions. The Pittsburg Historical Society Museum is a must-visit for history buffs, with exhibits on the town’s early settlers, agriculture, and logging industry. Another interesting stop is the Indian Stream Republic Museum, which tells the story of the short-lived independent nation that existed in the area in the early 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Pittsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pittsburg is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, fishing, hunting, and more. Some of the top natural attractions in the area include:

  • Lake Francis State Park: This beautiful park offers swimming, boating, camping, and fishing opportunities, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Magalloway Mountain: This popular hiking spot offers breathtaking views from the summit, with opportunities to spot local wildlife along the way.
  • Connecticut Lakes Natural Area: This pristine wilderness area is home to several rare plant and animal species, and is a great spot for birdwatching and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pittsburg

Pittsburg boasts some of the best hiking and biking routes in all of New Hampshire, with trails for all skill levels. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Terrace Mountain Trail: This challenging trail offers stunning views of the Connecticut River Valley and the White Mountains.
  • Round Pond Trail: This easy trail loops around beautiful Round Pond, with opportunities for fishing and birdwatching.
  • The Canada Falls Lake Trail: This moderate trail takes you through the forest to the scenic Canada Falls Lake, with opportunities for swimming and camping.

Best Things to Do in Pittsburg: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Pittsburg, you won’t be disappointed. From snowmobiling to fishing, there’s something for everyone in this outdoor paradise. Some of the best things to do in Pittsburg include:

  • Snowmobiling: With over 200 miles of groomed snowmobiling trails, Pittsburg is a top destination for snowmobile enthusiasts.
  • Fishing: The area is home to some of the best trout fishing in the region, with plenty of opportunities to catch brook, rainbow, and brown trout.
  • Wildlife Watching: Pittsburg is home to a variety of wildlife, including moose, bear, and bald eagles. Guided wildlife tours are available for those looking to get up close and personal with nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pittsburg

Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to the Pittsburg Historical Society Museum, followed by a scenic drive around the area’s beautiful lakes.

Day 2: Hit the trails with a moderate hike to Canada Falls Lake, followed by a relaxing swim in the lake.

Day 3: Spend the day exploring the Connecticut Lakes Natural Area, with opportunities for birdwatching, fishing, and hiking.

Day 4: Head out on a guided wildlife tour to spot moose, bear, and other local wildlife.

Day 5: Take a break from the outdoors with a visit to the Indian Stream Republic Museum, followed by a delicious meal at a local restaurant.

Day 6: Rent a snowmobile and hit the trails for a thrilling adventure through the snowy landscape.

Day 7: End your trip with a peaceful day at Lake Francis State Park, with opportunities for swimming, boating, and picnicking.

Food and Drink Scene of Pittsburg

Must-Try Dishes

Pittsburg may be a small town, but it still offers some delicious local cuisine. Some must-try dishes in the area include:

  • Trout: With some of the best trout fishing in the region, it’s no surprise that trout is a local favorite. Try it grilled or fried for a delicious meal.
  • Blueberry Pie: Pittsburg is known for its blueberries, and blueberry pie is a must-try dessert.
  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is famous for its maple syrup, and Pittsburg is no exception. Try it on pancakes or waffles for a local treat.

Local Beverages

While Pittsburg doesn’t have any local breweries or wineries, the area does offer some delicious local beverages. Local favorites include:

  • Coffee: The Trading Post Coffee House offers some of the best coffee in the area, with plenty of cozy seating and delicious baked goods to enjoy with your drink.
  • Local Cider: The nearby White Mountain Cider Company offers a variety of delicious ciders made with local apples.
  • Hot Cocoa: There’s nothing better than a hot cocoa on a cold day, and Pittsburg has plenty of places to get it. Try it at the Moose Alley Cones & More for a delicious treat.

Where to Stay in Pittsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite its small size, Pittsburg offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in the area include:

  • Cabins and Cottages: If you’re looking for a cozy, rustic experience, Pittsburg has plenty of cabins and cottages available for rent.
  • Lodges and Resorts: For a more upscale experience, check out one of the area’s lodges or resorts, which offer amenities like hot tubs, fireplaces, and on-site restaurants.
  • Motels and Inns: If you’re looking for a more budget-friendly option, Pittsburg has plenty of motels and inns available for a comfortable and affordable stay.

What to Buy in Pittsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from your trip to Pittsburg, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some of the best things to buy in the area include:

  • Local Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is famous for its maple syrup, and Pittsburg is no exception. Bring home a bottle or two to remind you of your trip.
  • Local Crafts: The Trading Post gift shop offers a variety of local crafts and souvenirs, including pottery, jewelry, and knick-knacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Pittsburg

Pittsburg is a small town with a rich local culture and history. Visitors are expected to be respectful of the town’s customs and traditions, which include:

  • Appreciating the Great Outdoors: Pittsburg is all about the great outdoors, and visitors are expected to show respect for the natural beauty of the area by leaving it clean and undisturbed.
  • Supporting Local Businesses: Pittsburg is home to many small, locally-owned businesses, and visitors are encouraged to shop and dine at these establishments to support the local economy.
  • Participating in Local Events: Pittsburg hosts several local events throughout the year, including fishing tournaments, snowmobile races, and fairs. Visitors are encouraged to participate and enjoy the town’s festive atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Pittsburg

Pittsburg is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique attractions and activities. For the best weather and outdoor activities, the best time to visit Pittsburg is from late spring to early fall. However, the area is also a popular winter destination for snowmobilers and skiers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pittsburg

Pittsburg is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors are still advised to take common sense precautions, such as keeping valuables locked up and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Pittsburg

Pittsburg is a rural area with limited medical facilities. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications with them and to take precautions against tick-borne illnesses, which are common in the area.

Where to Go Next from Pittsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pittsburg is a secluded town with limited nearby destinations. However, visitors can explore nearby natural attractions like the White Mountains or the Great North Woods, or head to nearby cities like Berlin or Lancaster.
