Introduction to Great North Region

The Great North Region of New Hampshire is a perfect blend of nature, adventure, history, and culture. This region is geographically located in New England, the northeastern part of the United States of America, in North America. The Great North Region is a paradise for nature enthusiasts and adventurists, as it is home to high peaks, verdant forests, lakes, waterfalls, and rivers. Besides natural beauty, the region boasts of an array of cultural offerings, from museums, galleries, and historical landmarks to food, drink, and local customs. This tour guide will take you through all the best things to do, places to visit, and must-try dishes, including practical information on how to get around and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Great North Region

The Great North Region is a vast area, with plenty of destinations and attractions worth visiting. Here is a list of the top places to see in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Museum of the White Mountains - Plymouth
  2. The Mount Washington Observatory and Weather Discovery Center - North Conway
  3. New Hampshire Boat Museum - Wolfeboro
  4. Canterbury Shaker Village - Canterbury
  5. The Lake Winnipesaukee Museum - Laconia

  2. The Flume Gorge - Lincoln
  3. Mount Washington - Sargent’s Purchase
  4. Kancamagus Highway Scenic Drive - Conway
  5. The Lakes Region - Laconia

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great North Region

The Great North Region is a heaven for hikers and bikers. Here are the top hiking and biking trails in the area:

  1. Mount Washington - Tuckerman Ravine Trail
  2. Mount Chocorua - Champney Falls Trail
  3. Franconia Notch State Park - Franconia Ridge Trail
  4. Kancamagus Highway - Pemigewasset Wilderness
  5. The Northern Rail Trail - Boscawen to Lebanon

Best Things to Do in Great North Region: Activities and Tours

The Great North Region offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  1. Ride on the Mount Washington Cog Railway
  2. Take a scenic drive along Kancamagus Highway
  3. Visit Castle in the Clouds and take a horseback ride
  4. Experience winter sports at Bretton Woods and Cannon Mountain
  5. Enjoy a sunset cruise on Lake Winnipesaukee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great North Region

The Great North Region requires at least a week to explore its attractions fully. Here is an itinerary for a week-long trip:

  1. Day 1: Arrive in North Conway and visit The Mount Washington Observatory and Weather Discovery Center
  2. Day 2: Hike Mount Chocorua and explore Champney Falls Trail
  3. Day 3: Visit Canterbury Shaker Village in the morning, and enjoy afternoon boating in Wolfeboro
  4. Day 4: Drive along the Kancamagus Highway, stop at Franconia Notch State Park for a picnic lunch, and walk on the Franconia Ridge Trail
  5. Day 5: Drive to Mount Washington and enjoy a ride on the Mount Washington Cog Railway
  6. Day 6: Visit the Museum of the White Mountains in the morning, explore Lake Winnipesaukee Museum in the afternoon, and enjoy sunset cruise
  7. Day 7: Depart from Great North Region

Local Culture and Customs of Great North Region

The people of Great North Region are friendly, warm, and welcoming. They are proud of their history, culture, and traditions. Here are some of the customs and cultures of Great North Region:

  1. Respect for nature and the outdoors
  2. Traditional craft making, especially quilt making and basket weaving
  3. The annual New Hampshire Highland Games and Festival
  4. Maple sugaring season in late winter to early spring
  5. Fall foliage season in September and October.

Best Time to Visit Great North Region

The best time to visit Great North Region is from June to October. During this period, the temperatures are mild, and the outdoor activities and adventures are plenty. Specifically, fall foliage season, which usually runs from mid-September to late October, is a must-see in the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great North Region

Do:

  1. Do respect nature and wildlife.
  2. Do bring warm clothes, waterproof boots, and a raincoat.
  3. Do try the maple syrup and apple cider.
  4. Do embrace the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  2. Don’t feed wildlife.
  3. Don’t underestimate the weather conditions.
  4. Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  5. Don’t forget to carry a map or a GPS device.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great North Region

The Great North Region is a generally safe area, but visitors should take precautions while enjoying outdoor activities, especially hiking and skiing. Visitors should:

  1. Carry enough food, water, and warm clothing.
  2. Hike and ski in groups, if possible.
  3. Stick to designated trails and obey trail rules.
  4. Check the weather before heading out.
  5. Carry a first aid kit and a whistle.

Health in Great North Region

The Great North Region has several medical facilities that offer quality medical care, but visitors should carry their medications and medical insurance. Visitors should:

  1. Carry essential medications and a first aid kit.
  2. Drink plenty of water to prevent dehydration.
  3. Watch out for ticks, especially in the summer seasons.
  4. Take precautions while swimming in the lakes and rivers.
  5. Wear sunscreen and insect repellent.
