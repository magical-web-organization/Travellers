Introduction to Westmoreland

Nestled in the Monadnock Region in New Hampshire, Westmoreland is a small town with a population of just over 1,700. The town boasts scenic views and a rich history that has been preserved over centuries. Visitors who come to Westmoreland can immerse themselves in outdoor adventures with hiking and biking through the beautiful natural landscapes. Westmoreland is a charming destination for all travelers and provides plenty of opportunities for exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Westmoreland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Westmoreland Historical Society Museum

The Westmoreland Historical Society Museum is an excellent place to learn about the history of Westmoreland. The museum displays artifacts and documents that illustrate the town’s evolution over the years. Visitors can also explore the historic Elizabeth Pierce House, which is a 1779 home. The museum is open from June to September on weekends from 1 pm to 4 pm.

2. Madame Sherri’s Castle and Forest

Madame Sherri’s Castle and Forest is a historical site that was built in the 1930s. The castle’s ruins are an architectural wonder and showcase the glory of the past. The castle is located within the Madame Sherri Forest, which offers fantastic hiking and picnic spots. It is open throughout the year from dawn until dusk.

Natural Attractions of Westmoreland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Pisgah State Park

Pisgah State Park is a vast wilderness area that spans over 13,000 acres. It features rugged terrain and several hiking trails that offer sweeping views of the surrounding landscape. The park also offers a few camping sites, mountain biking trails, and horseback riding. Visitors can explore the park all year round from dawn to dusk.

2. Chesterfield Gorge Natural Area

Chesterfield Gorge Natural Area is a scenic spot that offers a breathtaking view of the gorge. Visitors can hike along the trails, relax on the picnic benches, or fish in the river. The natural area is open all year round from dawn to dusk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westmoreland

1. Wantastiquet Mountain Trail

Wantastiquet Mountain Trail is a 3.8-mile hike that winds through the lush forests and leads to the summit of Wantastiquet Mountain. Once at the top, hikers can enjoy magnificent views of the Connecticut River and several surrounding towns.

2. Pisgah State Park Trails

Pisgah State Park offers over 50 miles of hiking and biking trails. The routes range from easy to difficult, making it suitable for hikers of all levels. The trails offer beautiful views of the forest, streams, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Westmoreland: Activities and Tours

1. Canoeing and Kayaking Tours

Westmoreland is surrounded by several rivers and lakes, making it an ideal destination for canoeing and kayaking enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the Connecticut River or the Ashuelot River on guided tours or rent equipment for self-exploration.

2. Fall Foliage Viewing

Westmoreland is famous for its fall foliage, and visitors flock to the town during autumn to witness the stunning colors. The best way to enjoy the foliage is by hiking the trails or taking scenic drives through the town’s countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westmoreland

Day 1: Visit the Westmoreland Historical Society Museum and the Elizabeth Pierce House.

Day 2: Explore Madame Sherri’s Castle and Forest.

Day 3: Hike the Wantastiquet Mountain Trail and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4: Go canoeing or kayaking on the Connecticut or Ashuelot River.

Day 5: Explore the Chesterfield Gorge Natural Area.

Day 6: Visit the Pisgah State Park and hike the trails.

Day 7: Enjoy a scenic drive through the countryside to witness the fall foliage.

Food and Drink Scene of Westmoreland

Must-Try Dishes

1. Maple Syrup

Maple syrup is a local specialty and is found in several dishes in Westmoreland. It is often used to sweeten desserts, pancakes, and waffles.

2. Apple Cider Donuts

Apple cider donuts are another local specialty and a must-try dish when visiting Westmoreland. The donuts are moist, fluffy, and coated in cinnamon and sugar.

Local Beverages

1. Hard Cider

Westmoreland is home to several apple orchards, and visitors can find several varieties of hard cider. The cider is blended with local fruits and spices to create unique flavors.

2. Craft Beer

Westmoreland also has a thriving craft beer scene. Visitors can enjoy local brews at several breweries and pubs in the town.

Where to Stay in Westmoreland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Chesterfield Inn

Chesterfield Inn is a charming bed and breakfast located in the nearby town of Chesterfield. The inn features several comfortable rooms and provides easy access to the natural attractions of Westmoreland.

2. The Putney Inn

The Putney Inn is another nearby accommodation option that offers excellent rooms and amenities. It is located near Putney Mountain, which offers fantastic hiking and scenic views.

What to Buy in Westmoreland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Maple Syrup

Maple syrup is an excellent souvenir to take home from Westmoreland. Visitors can find several varieties of maple syrup and maple products at local shops and markets.

2. Local Crafts

Westmoreland has a thriving community of artists and artisans who create handmade crafts and jewelry. Visitors can find unique pieces to take home at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Westmoreland

Westmoreland has a rich history, and the town’s residents take pride in preserving it. Visitors are expected to respect the cultural heritage and customs of the town and treat the locals with courtesy and kindness.

Best Time to Visit Westmoreland

The best time to visit Westmoreland is during the fall, between September and November. The town’s fall foliage is a sight to behold, and the mild weather makes it easy to explore the outdoors.

Westmoreland is a safe town, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the wilderness. It is essential to wear appropriate clothing and gear when hiking or biking and to follow the park rules and regulations.

Getting to Westmoreland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westmoreland

The nearest airport to Westmoreland is Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, located approximately 90 miles away. Visitors can rent a car from the airport or hire a taxi to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Westmoreland

The nearest bus station to Westmoreland is the Brattleboro Greyhound Bus Station, located approximately 8 miles away. Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car from the station.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Westmoreland on their vehicles or rent a car from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Westmoreland

The best way to get around Westmoreland is by car or on foot. Visitors can rent a car from the nearby towns or explore the natural attractions and scenic views on foot.

Where to Go Next from Westmoreland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Keene is a vibrant town located approximately 8 miles away from Westmoreland. It offers several cultural attractions, restaurants and shops, and outdoor activities.

Brattleboro is a charming town located approximately 15 miles away from Westmoreland. It features several historic sites, art galleries, and museums, and a thriving food and drink scene.

Conclusion

Westmoreland is a hidden gem in the Monadnock Region of New Hampshire that offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in natural beauty and rich history. The town’s charming culture and customs, along with its natural attractions and scenic views, make it a perfect destination for all travelers.
