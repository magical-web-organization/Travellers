Introduction to Peterborough

Peterborough is a charming town located in the Monadnock Region of New Hampshire, in New England. The town is known for its Victorian architecture, cultural attractions, and scenic natural beauty. With a population of just over 6,000, Peterborough has a small-town feel that makes visitors feel right at home. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Peterborough has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Peterborough

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Peterborough is home to several museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage and history. The Peterborough Historical Society Museum is a must-visit attraction for history lovers. The museum features exhibits on the town’s early settlers, its industrial past, and the life and works of famous writer and resident, Willa Cather. The Mariposa Museum is another cultural gem in Peterborough, offering visitors a chance to experience world cultures through its art and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Peterborough: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will be delighted by the natural beauty that surrounds Peterborough. Monadnock State Park is a popular destination for hiking and camping. The park’s centerpiece is Mount Monadnock, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. For a less strenuous outdoor adventure, head to Greenfield State Park, where visitors can swim, boat, or simply relax on the sandy beach. For a scenic drive, take a trip along the Monadnock Region Scenic Byway, which winds its way through the rolling hills and picturesque towns of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peterborough

Peterborough has some of the best hiking and biking routes in New Hampshire. Mount Monadnock is one of the most popular hiking trails in the state, offering a challenging climb with breathtaking views. Another popular hiking destination is Pack Monadnock, which offers a less strenuous climb and stunning panoramic views. Cyclists will enjoy the scenic ride along the Mariposa Trail, a bike path that runs from Peterborough to Dublin, passing through beautiful countryside and quaint New England towns.

Best Things to Do in Peterborough: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities and tours to take in Peterborough. For a unique experience, take a tour of the Cunningham Pond Wildlife Management Area, where visitors can observe a variety of wildlife in their natural habitat. If you’re interested in the arts, catch a show at the Peterborough Players, a renowned theater company that has been entertaining audiences for over 80 years. Foodies will appreciate a culinary tour of Peterborough’s restaurants and bars, where you can sample locally sourced food and craft beverages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peterborough

Day 1: Visit the Peterborough Historical Society Museum and learn about the town’s rich history. Day 2: Hike to the peak of Mount Monadnock for stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Mariposa Trail and explore the quaint towns along the way. Day 4: Visit the Mariposa Museum and explore its exhibitions on world cultures. Day 5: Take a scenic drive along the Monadnock Region Scenic Byway. Day 6: Enjoy a show at the Peterborough Players. Day 7: Relax at Greenfield State Park and soak up the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Peterborough

Must-Try Dishes

Peterborough’s food scene is as diverse as it is delicious. For a taste of New Hampshire’s famous seafood, head to Waterhouse Restaurant, where you can sample fresh lobster and clams. For a hearty meal, visit the Cooper’s Hill Public House, which serves hearty pub fare and a selection of craft beers. Don’t miss out on trying a classic New England dessert, such as apple pie or blueberry cobbler, at Harlow’s Pub.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Peterborough has plenty to offer. Visit Post and Beam Brewing, where you can try a variety of locally brewed craft beers. For a taste of New Hampshire’s famous maple syrup, visit Froling’s Sugarhouse, where you can sample maple syrup and other maple products.

Where to Stay in Peterborough: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peterborough has a variety of accommodations for visitors. For a luxurious stay, book a room at the Woodbound Inn, a historic hotel located on the shores of Lake Contoocook. The Jack Daniels Motor Inn is a budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms. For a home away from home, book a room at Benjamin Prescott Inn, a charming bed and breakfast in the heart of Peterborough.

What to Buy in Peterborough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a souvenir of your trip to Peterborough, head to the Peterborough Basket Company, where you can purchase hand-crafted baskets made from locally sourced materials. For a taste of New Hampshire, visit the Peterborough Oil & Vinegar, where you can find a wide variety of artisanal oils and vinegars.

Local Culture and Customs of Peterborough

Peterborough has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the year. The town hosts several festivals and events, including the Peterborough Folk Music Festival and the Annual Strawberry Festival. The locals in Peterborough are friendly and welcoming, but it is important to respect their privacy and property.

Best Time to Visit Peterborough

The best time to visit Peterborough is during the fall when the trees turn vibrant colors of red, orange, and yellow. The weather is mild, and the town is less crowded than in the summer months. Spring is also a beautiful time to visit, as the flowers bloom and the landscape comes alive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peterborough

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the town’s natural attractions.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the locals or their property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peterborough

Peterborough is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly atmosphere. Visitors are advised to be cautious when hiking in the natural areas and to follow all safety guidelines.

Health in Peterborough

Peterborough has several medical facilities, including Monadnock Community Hospital, which provides emergency and urgent care services.

Getting to Peterborough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peterborough

The closest major airport to Peterborough is the Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, located approximately 40 miles away. Boston Logan International Airport is also within driving distance, approximately 85 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Peterborough

Greyhound Bus Lines and Concord Coach Lines both have bus stations within a short driving distance from Peterborough.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors to Peterborough, as it allows for flexibility in exploring the surrounding area. Taxi and ride-share services are also available.

How to Get Around in Peterborough

Peterborough is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Cycling and driving are also popular options for getting around town.

Where to Go Next from Peterborough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peterborough is located in the heart of the Monadnock Region, which is known for its natural beauty and quaint New England towns. Nearby destinations include Keene, a college town with a vibrant cultural scene, and Dublin, a charming town known for its historic architecture. Outdoor enthusiasts will enjoy a visit to Mount Sunapee State Park, located approximately 60 miles away.

Conclusion

Peterborough is a hidden gem in New Hampshire, offering visitors a chance to experience the best of New England’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or delicious cuisine, Peterborough has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover why Peterborough is one of the most charming towns in New England.
