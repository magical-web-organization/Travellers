Introduction to Claremont

Located in the Monadnock Region of New Hampshire, Claremont is a charming town that offers visitors the opportunity to experience the best of both worlds - modern amenities and breathtaking natural scenery. The town has a long and fascinating history that goes back to the colonial times, and it is now a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, cultural buffs, and history lovers. This travel guide to Claremont, New Hampshire, covers everything you need to know before planning a visit, including the best places to explore, hiking/biking trails, foods and drinks to try, where to stay, local customs, and safety tips.

List of Places to Visit in Claremont

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Claremont has a rich history that is well-preserved in several museums and historical sites, and visitors can explore the intriguing stories of the town’s past. Some of the most iconic cultural attractions in Claremont include:

  • Claremont Historical Society: This museum is a must-visit for history buffs who want to learn about the town’s colonial past and the lives of its residents.

  • Alexander Scammell Bridge: This historic bridge is a well-maintained structure that spans the Connecticut River and connects Claremont to neighboring towns.

  • Sanctuary of Our Lady of La Salette: Visitors can explore the serene beauty of this Catholic shrine set in the picturesque natural landscape of Claremont.

Natural Attractions of Claremont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Claremont is a haven for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts as it offers a wealth of outdoor attractions, including hiking and biking trails, scenic drives, and picturesque views. Some of the essential natural attractions to explore in Claremont are:

  • Mount Ascutney State Park: This park located near the border of Vermont offers hiking trails that lead to the peak of Mount Ascutney, with spectacular views of the surrounding area.

  • Monadnock State Park: One of the most famous parks in New England, Monadnock State Park boasts 80 miles of hiking trails that cater to hikers of all levels.

  • Cornish-Windsor Covered Bridge: This covered bridge spanning the Connecticut River connects Claremont and Cornish and is one of the oldest structures of its kind in the country.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Claremont

Claremont has numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to individuals and families of all skill levels. Here are some of the best trails to explore in Claremont:

  • Sugar River Trail: This scenic trail runs for 9.5 miles through Claremont’s countryside and is ideal for hiking, mountain biking, and snowmobiling.

  • Mount Ascutney Trail: This trail leads to the peak of Mount Ascutney and offers visitors an opportunity to explore the beauty of Monadnock Region.

  • Stevens Rail Trail: This 6-mile-long trail that runs along the Sugar River is perfect for families with children and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Claremont: Activities and Tours

Aside from exploring the natural attractions and museums, Claremont offers numerous fun-filled activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do in Claremont include:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Claremont boasts several rivers suitable for kayaking and canoeing, including the Sugar River, the Connecticut River, and the Ashuelot River.

  • Fishing: Claremont has numerous lakes and rivers that offer excellent fishing opportunities for all skill levels.

  • Autumn Foliage Tours: Claremont is a picturesque town with stunning foliage during the fall months. Visitors can take self-guided or guided tours to explore the beauty of the area during this season.

Food and Drink Scene of Claremont

Claremont has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers local and international cuisine, craft beers, wines, and spirits. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Claremont:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Syrup: Claremont is famous for its locally sourced maple syrup, used as a topping for pancakes, waffles, and French toast.

  • Poutine: This savory dish originated in Quebec but has become increasingly popular throughout New England. Poutine consists of French fries, gravy, and melted cheese curds.

  • Seafood Chowder: Claremont is within easy reach of the east coast, making it an excellent place to enjoy fresh seafood, especially seafood chowder.

Local Beverages

  • Vermont Craft Beers: Claremont is in proximity to Vermont, which is known for its craft beers. Visitors can enjoy a variety of local brews at Claremont’s bars and restaurants.

  • Maple Syrup Seltzer: Made with locally sourced maple syrup, this refreshing beverage is unique to the Monadnock Region and blends the taste of maple with the bubbly sweetness of seltzer.

Where to Stay in Claremont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Claremont has several options for accommodations that cater to every budget, including hotels, motels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Claremont are:

  • Common Man Inn & Restaurant Claremont: Located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, onsite restaurants, and easy access to the town’s attractions.

  • Claremont Motor Lodge: This affordable motel is ideal for budget travelers looking for clean and comfortable rooms close to the city center.

  • Amanda’s House Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B offers a cozy and homely atmosphere with personalized service and amenities.

What to Buy in Claremont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

After exploring the natural attractions, museums, and food and drink scene of Claremont, visitors can take home souvenirs and local products as a remembrance of their trip. Some of the best local products to buy in Claremont are:

  • Maple Syrup: Locally sourced maple syrup remains the most popular and authentic souvenir to bring home from Claremont.

  • Handmade Crafts: Visitors can find unique handcrafted items made by local artisans, such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

  • Wine and Cheese: Claremont is close to the famous cheese-making towns of Vermont, and visitors can sample and purchase local cheeses and wines from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Claremont

Claremont is a small town that takes pride in preserving its cultural traditions and customs. Visitors can expect a friendly and welcoming community that is proud of its heritage. Here are some tips to help visitors get the most out of their trip to Claremont:

  • Respect the Natural Environment: Claremont is known for its scenic beauty, and visitors should follow the Leave No Trace principles and take care of the outdoor spaces they explore.

  • Embrace the Local Culture: Visitors should take time to learn about Claremont’s culture and customs and engage with locals to appreciate the town’s authentic experience.

Best Time to Visit Claremont

The best time to visit Claremont is during the fall season when the foliage is at its peak, and visitors can enjoy scenic drives and tours to explore the colorful landscape. Summer is also a popular time for outdoor activities and events, such as kayaking and hiking trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Claremont

Do:

  • Respect the Natural Environment: Claremont has a delicate ecosystem, and visitors should follow the Leave No Trace principles and avoid littering or disturbing the natural habitat.

  • Embrace the Outdoors: Claremont offers an abundance of outdoor activities, and visitors should take advantage of the opportunities to explore the natural scenery and beauty of the town.

Don’t:

  • Ignore Local Customs: Visitors should respect the local culture and customs of Claremont, such as driving laws, outdoor recreation policies, and health and safety guidelines.

  • Cause Disturbances: Visitors should avoid creating noise or disturbances that may disturb other visitors or locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Claremont

Claremont is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as locking car doors and storing valuables securely. Visitors should also check weather conditions and trail maps before heading outdoors to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

Health in Claremont

Claremont has several medical centers and hospitals that provide comprehensive healthcare services. Visitors are advised to check their health insurance coverage before traveling and obtain any necessary vaccinations or medication for their trip.

Getting to Claremont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Claremont

The nearest airport to Claremont is Lebanon Municipal Airport, which is approximately 23 miles away. Visitors can also fly into Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, which is approximately 75 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Claremont

The closest bus station to Claremont is the Greyhound station in Bellows Falls, Vermont, approximately 10 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Claremont by car or train. Claremont has a train station that connects to nearby towns and cities, and visitors can rent cars from the airport or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Claremont

Claremont is a small town that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent cars or use public transportation to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Claremont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Claremont is ideally located within proximity to several nearby towns and cities, making it an excellent starting point for day trips and excursions. Here are some of the nearby destinations to explore:

  • White River Junction: A charming town in Vermont that offers cultural attractions, shops, and restaurants along the Connecticut River.

  • Keene: A vibrant college town with a range of restaurants, shops, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Claremont is a small town with a rich history, scenic beauty, and an abundance of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.
