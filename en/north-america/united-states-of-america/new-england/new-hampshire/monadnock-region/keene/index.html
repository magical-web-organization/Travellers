Introduction to Keene

Nestled in the picturesque Monadnock Region in New Hampshire, Keene is a charming small town that is steeped in history. Home to Keene State College and the annual Pumpkin Festival, Keene offers visitors a unique blend of culture, nature, and recreation.

List of Places to Visit in Keene

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Historical Society of Cheshire County Museum: Step back in time and discover the rich history of the area through exhibits and collections.
  • Thorne-Sagendorph Art Gallery: Explore contemporary and historic artworks by local and regional artists.
  • Horatio Colony Museum: Tour a Georgian-style mansion and get a glimpse of life in the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Keene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keene Swampbats Baseball: Catch a game and cheer on the local team.
  • Stonewall Farm: Visit a working farm, hike scenic trails, and pet the farm animals.
  • Monadnock State Park: Hike the trails and see stunning views of the Monadnock Region from the summit of Mount Monadnock.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keene

  • Cheshire Rail Trail: Walk or bike on a 42-mile rail trail that passes through Keene and other nearby towns.
  • Gap Mountain: Hike to the top of Gap Mountain for a panoramic view of the surrounding area.
  • Ashuelot River Trail: Walk, bike, or run on this scenic trail that follows the Ashuelot River through downtown Keene.

Best Things to Do in Keene: Activities and Tours

  • Keene Pumpkin Festival: Take part in the annual Pumpkin Festival and see thousands of lit-up pumpkins.
  • Colonial Theatre: Catch a movie or live performance at this historic theater.
  • Monadnock Speedway: Watch cars race around the track at this popular local attraction.

Local Culture and Customs of Keene

  • Keene Pumpkin Festival is a popular annual event in the town. Visitors can expect to see numerous decorated pumpkins lit up in various designs, competitive games, live entertainment, and a festive atmosphere.
  • Make sure to follow local customs such as tipping for services rendered and adhering to traffic rules.
  • Keene is known for its inviting small-town atmosphere, which is worth experiencing for yourself.

Best Time to Visit Keene

The best time to visit Keene is during the fall season when the weather is cool and the foliage changes into a beautiful display of colors. This is when the Keene Pumpkin Festival also takes place, adding an extra fun element to your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keene

Do:

  • Bring warm clothing if visiting in the fall or winter.
  • Visit the farmers’ market to get the best locally produced goods.
  • Explore the historic downtown area for unique shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack comfortable hiking shoes for outdoor activities.
  • Litter or damage natural attractions and historic sites.
  • Drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keene

Keene is generally a safe town to visit, but as with any destination, tourists should exercise common sense while out and about. Keep valuable possessions safe and avoid walking alone at night or in less-well-lit areas.

Health in Keene

For any medical concerns, Keene has several hospitals and medical centers available. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications with them on their trip.

Getting to Keene: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Boston Logan International Airport (100 miles)
  • Hartford Bradley International Airport (100 miles)

Bus Stations Near Keene

Other Options

  • Car rentals are readily available at airports and in surrounding towns.
  • Private shuttles and taxis are also readily available.

How to Get Around in Keene

Keene is a compact town that is easily explored on foot or by bike. Taxis, private shuttles, and car rentals are available for those who wish to explore farther afield.

Where to Go Next from Keene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mount Monadnock: Hike the trails and see stunning views from the summit of Mount Monadnock.
  • Walpole: Visit historic downtown and pick up souvenirs at the artisan shops.
  • Brattleboro: Explore the vibrant arts scene, check out the unique museums, and hike the trails at the Brattleboro Retreat.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a relaxing, culture-filled vacation, Keene is the perfect destination year-round. From scenic outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and delicious food, Keene has something for everyone. Plan your next trip to Keene using this comprehensive travel guide.
