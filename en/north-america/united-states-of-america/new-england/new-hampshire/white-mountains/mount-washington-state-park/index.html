Introduction to Mount Washington State Park

Mount Washington State Park is a 60-acre park located in the heart of the White Mountains. The park is home to the highest peak in the Northeast, Mount Washington, which stands at 6,288 feet. The park is open year-round and offers visitors a wide range of activities, from hiking and camping to skiing and snowboarding in the winter. In addition, the park has a rich history, having played a significant role in the development of weather forecasting in the United States.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Washington State Park

If you’re planning to visit Mount Washington State Park for a week or more, there is plenty to see and do in the area. Depending on your interests, here is a sample itinerary for a week in Mount Washington State Park:

Day 1: Arrive in Mount Washington State Park and explore the Mount Washington Observatory Weather Discovery Center.

Day 2: Hike the Tuckerman’s Ravine Trail and enjoy the view from the summit of Mount Washington.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive on the Mount Washington Auto Road and visit the Mount Washington Auto Road Museum.

Day 4: Discover the region’s history at the Cog Railway Museum and take a ride on the Mount Washington Cog Railway.

Day 5: Go skiing or snowboarding at Mount Washington Alpine Resort or snowshoeing in the Great Glen Trails Outdoor Center.

Day 6: Shop for souvenirs and relax in the local restaurants and pubs.

Day 7: Explore nearby destinations such as Crawford Notch State Park or Franconia Notch State Park.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Washington State Park

Do:

  • Dress in layers, as the weather can change quickly on the mountain
  • Carry a map and compass when hiking in the backcountry
  • Respect wildlife and stay a safe distance from animals
  • Check weather forecasts before heading out on a hike

Don’t:

  • Feed wildlife or approach them too closely
  • Litter or leave trash on the trails
  • Disturb the natural environment by picking flowers or disturbing rocks
  • Ignore warning signs or safety advisories

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Washington State Park

While Mount Washington State Park is a beautiful and exciting destination, it is important to be aware of the potential risks and hazards. Some important safety tips to keep in mind include:

  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions and carry appropriate gear.
  • Stay on marked trails and avoid venturing into the backcountry without proper knowledge and supplies.
  • Be aware of wildlife and carry bear spray.

Health in Mount Washington State Park

Mount Washington State Park is generally a safe and healthy destination. However, it is important to take precautions to protect yourself from tick-borne illnesses and altitude sickness. Some important health tips to keep in mind include:

  • Wear long pants, light-colored clothing, and a hat to protect against ticks.
  • Drink plenty of fluids and avoid alcohol and caffeine at high altitudes.
  • Take it slow and acclimate to the altitude gradually.
