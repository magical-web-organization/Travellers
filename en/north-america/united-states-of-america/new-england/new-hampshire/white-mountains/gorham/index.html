Introduction to Gorham

Gorham is a small town located in the heart of the White Mountains in New Hampshire. Nestled between the Presidential Range and the Great North Woods, the town is a hub for outdoor enthusiasts and adventurers looking to explore the natural beauty of the region. At the same time, Gorham offers a glimpse into New Hampshire’s rich history and vibrant culture with its museums, galleries, and local festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Gorham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gorham Historical Society and Railroad Museum

Step back in time and explore the history and heritage of Gorham. The Gorham Historical Society and Railroad Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts, photos, and documents chronicling Gorham’s past, including its early settlement, industrial boom, and railroad era.

Arctic Museum and Arctic Garden at the White Mountains Community College

Experience the natural beauty of the Arctic at the Arctic Museum and Arctic Garden at the White Mountains Community College. The museum features a variety of exhibits showcasing the flora and fauna of the Arctic, as well as the culture and lifestyle of its indigenous people.

Natural Attractions of Gorham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Washington Auto Road

The ultimate road trip destination in Gorham, Mount Washington Auto Road is a scenic drive that takes visitors to the top of the highest peak in the Northeast. The 7.6-mile road winds its way through the White Mountains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Androscoggin River

Take a break from hiking and biking and head to the Androscoggin River for some kayaking, fishing, or swimming. The river provides stunning scenery and relaxing waters that are perfect for cooling off on a hot summer day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gorham

Presidential Range Trail

A must-do for hiking enthusiasts, the Presidential Range Trail is a 23-mile trek that takes visitors through some of the most beautiful landscapes in New Hampshire. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains, lakes, and forests.

Great Glen Trails Outdoor Center

Bike and hike through the Great North Woods of New Hampshire with the Great Glen Trails Outdoor Center. This 45-acre resort offers a variety of trails, from easy, family-friendly paths to challenging, steep climbs up to the alpine zone.

Best Things to Do in Gorham: Activities and Tours

Raft NH

Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting with Raft NH. Their experienced guides take visitors down the Androscoggin River, providing an exhilarating ride through challenging rapids and stunning scenery.

Omni Mount Washington Resort

Relax and unwind at the Omni Mount Washington Resort, one of the most scenic resorts in New England. The resort offers a variety of activities, including golfing, hiking, and spa treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Gorham

Must-Try Dishes

New England Lobster Roll

No trip to New England is complete without trying a classic New England Lobster Roll. Packed with fresh lobster meat and served on a buttery bun, the lobster roll is a quintessential New Hampshire treat.

Maple Syrup

New Hampshire is famous for its maple syrup, and Gorham is no exception. Try some of the local varieties of maple syrup for a unique culinary experience.

Local Beverages

Moat Mountain Brewery

Sample some of the best craft beer in New Hampshire at Moat Mountain Brewery. The brewery offers a variety of beers, including IPA’s, stouts, and lagers, all made with locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Gorham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gorham Motor Inn

Perfect for budget travelers, the Gorham Motor Inn offers affordable accommodations that are clean, comfortable, and conveniently located near Gorham’s top attractions.

The Notch Hostel

For a unique lodging experience, check out The Notch Hostel. The hostel provides cozy accommodations in a historic building, complete with a communal kitchen and living room.

What to Buy in Gorham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

White Mountain Cider Company

Take home a taste of Gorham by picking up some of the locally-made products from the White Mountain Cider Company. Their selection of jams, jellies, and apple cider vinegar is sure to delight.

Local Culture and Customs of Gorham

Gorham is known for its friendly and welcoming locals who take pride in their town and its history. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local culture, attend events and festivals, and learn more about the traditions of the region.

Best Time to Visit Gorham

The best time to visit Gorham is in the summer when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are abundant. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit when the leaves change color, and the mountains come alive with vibrant hues.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gorham

Do:

  • Explore the local trails and parks
  • Attend a local event or festival
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Gorham

While Gorham is generally a safe place to visit, visitors should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking. Be aware of the risks associated with these activities, and always follow recommended safety guidelines.

Health in Gorham

Gorham has a variety of medical facilities and pharmacies available to visitors. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you and to be aware of any allergies or other health concerns.

Getting to Gorham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gorham

The nearest airports to Gorham are Portland International Jetport, located about 90 miles away, and Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, located 130 miles away. From these airports, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle service to Gorham.

Bus Stations Near Gorham

Concord Coach Lines provides bus service to Gorham from various locations throughout New Hampshire and surrounding states.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Gorham and park their car in one of the town’s many parking lots.

How to Get Around in Gorham

The most convenient way to get around Gorham is by car, although visitors can also rent bicycles or use public transportation to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Gorham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Conway

Located just a short drive from Gorham, North Conway is a picturesque town known for its shopping, dining, and outdoor recreation opportunities. Visitors can explore the many outlet stores and boutiques, hike the nearby trails, or take a scenic train ride through the White Mountains.

Franconia Notch State Park

Located about an hour’s drive from Gorham, Franconia Notch State Park is a beautiful park known for its stunning landscapes, hiking trails, and skiing opportunities. Visitors can explore the park’s natural wonders, including the Flume Gorge and the Cannon Mountain Aerial Tramway.

Conclusion

Gorham offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture that visitors won’t find anywhere else. From its scenic landscapes and outdoor adventures to its museums, festivals, and culinary delights, Gorham has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable vacation in this charming New Hampshire town.
