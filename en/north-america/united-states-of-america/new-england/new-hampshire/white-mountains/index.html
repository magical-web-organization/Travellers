Introduction to White Mountains

Located in the northeastern part of the United States, the White Mountains of New Hampshire offer stunning natural beauty, rich history, and a plethora of outdoor adventures for all types of travelers. Whether you want to hike the Appalachian Trail or explore the cultural attractions, White Mountains should be on your list.

Local Culture and Customs of White Mountains

White Mountain culture is heavily influenced by the natural surroundings. The locals take pride in their outdoor lifestyle, and it’s not uncommon to see towns shut down for hunting season or logging camps. Learn about the local customs and traditions during your stay.

Best Time to Visit White Mountains

Summer and fall are the most popular times to visit White Mountains. The weather is pleasant, and the fall foliage is a must-see. Winter is a great time for skiing and snowmobiling, but it can get very cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of White Mountains

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife.
  • Dress in layers as the weather can change quickly.
  • Carry enough water and snacks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Feed the wildlife.
  • Go off-trail or outside the designated area.

Safety and Health

Safety in White Mountains

The White Mountains can be dangerous to hikers, especially those who are unprepared. Follow these tips to stay safe:

  • Check the weather before setting out.
  • Bring appropriate gear and clothing.
  • Tell someone your hiking plans.
  • Stay on designated trails.

Health in White Mountains

White Mountains have high humidity levels, which can cause dehydration and heat stroke. Stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and take frequent breaks.

How to Get Around in White Mountains

The best way to get around White Mountains is by car. Renting a car is recommended, but you can also take the bus or hire a private car service. Biking and walking are popular options within the towns.
