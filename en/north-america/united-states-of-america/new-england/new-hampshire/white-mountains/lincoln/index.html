Introduction to Lincoln

Lincoln is a small town in central New Hampshire that sits at an elevation of 703 feet above sea level. It is home to some of the most spectacular natural wonders you will ever see. Surrounded by the White Mountains, Lincoln is a gateway to an outdoor paradise that spans wild rivers, majestic peaks, and secluded forests.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln

New Hampshire is famous for its rugged natural beauty, and Lincoln captures much of that spirit. The people here are friendly and welcoming and embrace the area’s stunning scenery and outdoor lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln

The best time to visit Lincoln is during the fall season (September and October) when the leaves turn vibrant shades of orange, red, and yellow. Winters are cold and snowy, and summers are warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln

Here are some simple guidelines to make your visit to Lincoln a pleasant experience.

Do:

  • Respect nature and leave no trace
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and bring sturdy hiking shoes
  • Carry a map and compass and always inform someone where you are going and when you plan to return
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and beverages
  • Be respectful to the locals and follow the town’s rules and regulations

Don’t:

  • Do not litter
  • Do not pick wildflowers or harm wildlife
  • Do not disturb the peace and tranquility of the surrounding areas

Safety and Health

Lincoln is safe to visit, and visitors rarely encounter any problems. However, it’s always wise to be cautious while traveling to unfamiliar destinations.

Safety in Lincoln

  • Stay aware of your surroundings
  • Follow the rules and regulations set out by the town authorities
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place
  • Avoid hiking alone and always inform someone where you are going and when you plan to return

Lincoln is a small town and has limited medical facilities. However, there are some clinics and pharmacies in the area.
