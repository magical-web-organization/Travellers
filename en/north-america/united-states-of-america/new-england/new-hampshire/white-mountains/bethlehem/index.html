Bethlehem is a small town located in the White Mountains of New Hampshire, in New England, USA. This charming town offers a unique mix of culture, history, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for travelers. In this comprehensive guide to Bethlehem, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip, from the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and where to eat.

Introduction to Bethlehem

Nestled in the heart of the White Mountains, Bethlehem is a town full of history and culture. Originally a farming community, Bethlehem became a popular resort town in the late 19th century with the arrival of the railroad. Today, visitors can explore the town’s rich history through its museums and historic sites, and enjoy the natural beauty of the surrounding mountains with outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Bethlehem

With a blend of cultural and natural attractions, there’s no shortage of places to visit in Bethlehem. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Colonial Theatre: This historic theatre has been entertaining audiences since 1915 and hosts a variety of live performances and movies throughout the year.

  • The Rocks Estate: This stunning estate is home to a 1,400-acre conservation area and a historic mansion. Visitors can take a tour of the mansion and stroll through the beautiful gardens.

  • Bethlehem Heritage Society Museum: This small but informative museum showcases the town’s history through exhibits on farming, tourism, and everyday life in Bethlehem.

Natural Attractions of Bethlehem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White Mountains National Forest: With over 800,000 acres of protected land, the White Mountains National Forest is a popular destination for hiking, camping, and scenic drives.

  • Mount Agassiz: One of the most popular hiking trails in Bethlehem, the Mount Agassiz trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Ammonoosuc River: This picturesque river offers opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and swimming. Take a relaxing stroll along the riverbank and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few of our favorite routes:

  • The Presidential Rail Trail: This 18-mile trail follows the old rail line through the White Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and rivers.

  • The Franconia Notch Recreation Path: This 8.7-mile trail passes through Franconia Notch State Park and offers some of the most beautiful views in the region.

  • The Bethlehem Heritage Trail: This self-guided trail takes visitors on a walking tour of the town’s historic sites, including the Bethlehem Public Library and the Maplewood Hotel.

Best Things to Do in Bethlehem: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Bethlehem. Here are a few ideas:

  • Horseback Riding: Saddle up and explore the White Mountains on horseback with one of the many local stables.

  • Zip Line Tours: Soar through the treetops with a thrilling zip line tour of the White Mountains.

  • Snowmobiling: In the winter months, rent a snowmobile and explore the snowy trails of the White Mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bethlehem

If you have a week to spend in Bethlehem, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the town’s historic sites, including the Bethlehem Heritage Society Museum and the Maplewood Hotel.
2 Take a scenic drive through the White Mountains and stop to hike the Mount Agassiz trail.
3 Spend the day horseback riding and exploring the countryside.
4 Take a zip line tour of the White Mountains for a thrilling adventure.
5 Visit the Rocks Estate and take a tour of the historic mansion and gardens.
6 Take a snowmobiling tour of the White Mountains in the winter months.
7 Relax and unwind at one of the town’s many spas and enjoy a massage or other treatment.

Food and Drink Scene of Bethlehem

Bethlehem may be a small town, but it’s big on flavor. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New England Clam Chowder: This creamy soup is a regional classic and can be found on many local menus.

  • Lobster Roll: Fresh lobster meat piled high on a buttered roll is a New England delicacy not to be missed.

  • Apple Cider Donuts: These sweet treats are made with fresh apple cider and are a fall favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is known for its maple syrup, which can be found in many local shops and restaurants.

  • Local Beer: The White Mountains are home to several craft breweries, like the Woodstock Inn Brewery and the Rek-Lis Brewing Company.

  • Blueberry Wine: Made with locally grown blueberries, this unique wine is a local favorite.

Where to Stay in Bethlehem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast or a modern hotel, Bethlehem has plenty of accommodations to choose from. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • The Bethlehem Inn: This charming inn offers cozy rooms and a convenient location in the heart of town.

  • The Arlington Hotel: Built in 1905, this historic hotel has been beautifully restored and offers a luxurious stay.

  • The Franconia Inn: Located just a few miles from Bethlehem, this family-friendly resort offers comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities.

What to Buy in Bethlehem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No visit to Bethlehem is complete without bringing home a few souvenirs. Here are some of the best local products to look for:

  • Maple Syrup: Bring home a bottle of locally made maple syrup to drizzle on your pancakes or waffles.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Several local artisans make beautiful jewelry, including earrings, necklaces, and bracelets.

  • Local Artwork: From paintings to pottery, Bethlehem has a thriving arts community, and you’re sure to find something beautiful to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a welcoming and friendly town, with a rich history and a laidback vibe. Respect for nature and the environment is an important part of local culture, so be sure to follow the “Leave No Trace” principles when hiking and exploring the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a family-friendly destination that can be enjoyed year-round. The fall months offer vibrant foliage and crisp weather, while winter brings snow sports. Spring and summer are perfect for outdoor adventures like hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bethlehem

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Bethlehem:

Do:

  • Explore the town’s historic sites and museums.

  • Respect the environment and follow the “Leave No Trace” principles when hiking and exploring.

  • Sample some of the local cuisine, like clam chowder and lobster rolls.

Don’t:

  • litter or leave trash in the outdoors.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in places of worship or cultural significance.

  • Be loud and disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a safe town to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to keep valuables safe.

Health in Bethlehem

Bethlehem has several medical facilities and pharmacies in the area, so visitors should have no trouble finding medical care if needed. Take precautions against tick bites when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Bethlehem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Located just 2 hours north of Boston, Bethlehem is an easy destination to reach by car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Bethlehem

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: Located around 100 miles south of Bethlehem, this is the closest major airport.

  • Boston Logan International Airport: Located around 150 miles south of Bethlehem, this is a larger airport with more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Bethlehem

  • Concord Coach Lines: Offers daily service to and from Boston and New York City.

  • Dartmouth Coach: Offers service to and from Boston and Hanover, NH.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate out of nearby airports, including Manchester-Boston Regional Airport and Boston Logan International Airport.

How to Get Around in Bethlehem

While Bethlehem is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding areas and attractions. Several car rental companies operate out of nearby airports.

Where to Go Next from Bethlehem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Kancamagus Highway: This scenic highway winds through the heart of the White Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Cannon Mountain: Located just a few miles from Bethlehem, Cannon Mountain is a popular ski resort in the winter months and offers hiking and scenic views in the summer.

  • Mount Washington: The tallest peak in the Northeast, Mount Washington offers challenging hiking trails and spectacular views.

Conclusion

Bethlehem is a hidden gem in the White Mountains, with a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore the outdoors or soak up some local culture, Bethlehem has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip and discover all the best things to see and do in this charming town.
