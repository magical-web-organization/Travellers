Introduction to Bretton Woods

Located in the heart of the White Mountains in New Hampshire, Bretton Woods is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and families. This charming town offers breathtaking scenery, rich history, and an array of activities for all ages. Whether you’re looking to hit the slopes, explore the area’s cultural and natural attractions, or simply relax in the beauty of the mountains, Bretton Woods is the perfect vacation spot.

List of Places to Visit in Bretton Woods

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Mount Washington Observatory Weather Discovery Center

Discover the science of weather and climate at the Mount Washington Observatory Weather Discovery Center. This interactive museum features hands-on exhibits, live video feeds showing weather conditions on Mount Washington, and educational programs for all ages.

The Bretton Woods Ski Area

Originally developed as a ski resort in the 1900s, the Bretton Woods Ski Area is one of the oldest in the United States. Today, it offers a range of activities beyond skiing, including ziplining, snowshoeing, and tubing. You can also explore the resorts’ history through its exhibits and trails.

The Mount Washington Cog Railway

The Mount Washington Cog Railway is the world’s first mountain-climbing railway. Built in 1869, it takes visitors to the summit of Mount Washington on a three-hour round-trip journey. Enjoy panoramic views as you ascend to the highest peak in the Northeast.

Natural Attractions of Bretton Woods: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Presidential Range Trail

Hiking enthusiasts will love the Presidential Range Trail, which offers incredible views of the White Mountains. The trail spans 19 miles and takes hikers over several of the highest peaks in New England, including Mount Washington.

The Rattle River Trail

If you’re looking for a more leisurely hike, the Rattle River Trail is a great option. This 6.6-mile loop takes you through a lush forest, past cascading waterfalls, and along the peaceful Rattle River.

The Mount Washington Auto Road

Take a scenic drive up the Mount Washington Auto Road for breathtaking views of the White Mountains. The road is 7.6 miles long and takes visitors to the summit of Mount Washington, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bretton Woods

Bretton Woods offers a range of hiking and biking routes for all skill levels. From gentle walks to challenging hikes, there’s something for everyone. Some of the most popular routes include the Presidential Range Trail, Rattle River Trail, and Mount Washington Auto Road. For cyclists, the Franconia Notch State Park and White Mountain National Forest offer some of the best biking trails in the region.

Best Things to Do in Bretton Woods: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and skiing, Bretton Woods offers a range of other activities, such as:

  • Ziplining and canopy tours
  • Snowshoeing and snowmobiling
  • Horseback riding
  • Golfing
  • Fishing

For those interested in guided tours, there are several available, including snowcat tours, guided hikes, and cultural tours of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bretton Woods

Day 1: Explore the Mount Washington Observatory Weather Discovery Center and enjoy a scenic drive up the Mount Washington Auto Road.

Day 2: Ski at the Bretton Woods Ski Area and explore its history exhibits and trails.

Day 3: Take a guided hike on the Presidential Range Trail or Rattle River Trail.

Day 4: Try snowshoeing or snowmobiling in the White Mountains.

Day 5: Take a ride on the Mount Washington Cog Railway.

Day 6: Go ziplining or try one of the other adventure activities offered in the area.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery of Bretton Woods, go golfing, or take a cultural tour of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Bretton Woods

Must-Try Dishes

Bretton Woods is known for its hearty, New England cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Lobster roll
  • Clam chowder
  • Apple pie
  • Blueberry pancakes
  • Maple-glazed ham

Local Beverages

Bretton Woods is home to several breweries and distilleries that offer locally produced beer, cider, and spirits. Some of the most popular beverages include:

  • Woodstock Inn Brewery’s Red Rack Ale
  • Moat Mountain Smoke House & Brewing Co.’s Iron Mike Pale Ale
  • Tamworth Distilling’s White Mountain Vodka

Local Culture and Customs of Bretton Woods

Bretton Woods is known for its laid-back, friendly culture. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local area, enjoy outdoor activities, and learn about the history and culture of the region.

Best Time to Visit Bretton Woods

The best time to visit Bretton Woods depends on what activities you’re interested in. Winter is the most popular time for skiing and other winter sports, while fall offers stunning foliage views. Summer is a great time for hiking and other outdoor activities, and spring is ideal for those who want to avoid crowds.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bretton Woods

While Bretton Woods is a safe area overall, visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

  • Extreme weather conditions, including sudden snowstorms and high winds, can be dangerous for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Wildlife encounters can happen at any time, so it’s important to have a basic understanding of wildlife behaviors.

Health in Bretton Woods

Visitors to Bretton Woods should be aware of the following health concerns:

  • Altitude sickness can be a concern if visiting the higher elevations in the region.
  • Lyme disease is prevalent in the area, so visitors should take precautions against tick bites.

Getting to Bretton Woods: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bretton Woods

The closest airports to Bretton Woods are the Manchester-Boston Regional Airport and the Portland International Jetport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle bus to Bretton Woods.

Bus Stations Near Bretton Woods

The closest bus station to Bretton Woods is the Concord Coach Lines station in Littleton, NH. Visitors can take a shuttle service or rent a car to reach Bretton Woods.

Other Options

Bretton Woods is also accessible by train. Amtrak’s Vermonter line stops in nearby White River Junction, VT, where visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle to reach Bretton Woods.

How to Get Around in Bretton Woods

Visitors to Bretton Woods can rent a car to explore the area, or take advantage of the shuttle services provided by local hotels and attractions.

Bretton Woods is a charming destination that offers something for everyone. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and array of activities, it’s the perfect place for a vacation. Whether you’re a skier, hiker, history buff, or simply looking to relax in the beauty of the mountains, Bretton Woods has something to offer.
