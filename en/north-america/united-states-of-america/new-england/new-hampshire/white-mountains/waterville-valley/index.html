Introduction to Waterville Valley

Nestled in the heart of the White Mountains, Waterville Valley is a picturesque resort town in New Hampshire that offers a perfect combination of natural beauty, thrilling adventures, and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by the peaks of the White Mountains National Forest, Waterville Valley is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with endless opportunities for hiking, biking, skiing, and snowboarding. The town also features many cultural and historical attractions, including museums and art galleries, and delightful dining and shopping experiences, making it an ideal destination for couples, families, and solo travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Waterville Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Waterville Valley boasts a rich cultural heritage and is home to many museums and historical places that offer an insight into the town’s rich history and arts. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Waterville Valley Historical Society Museum: Located in the town center, the museum features exhibits, artifacts, and photos that showcase the town’s history and culture.
  • Rey Center: Dedicated to the legacy of famous children’s author and illustrator H.A. Rey, who wrote the Curious George books, the center features exhibits, art workshops, and educational programs for kids and adults.
  • Sunnyside Timber Lodge and Museum: This historical lodge, built in 1905, is home to an extensive collection of vintage skiing and snowboarding gear and offers a glimpse into the region’s skiing history.

Natural Attractions of Waterville Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Waterville Valley is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty that includes majestic mountains, sparkling lakes, winding rivers, and lush forests. The town offers a wide array of outdoor activities, ranging from hiking and biking to skiing and snowboarding. Some of the top-rated natural attractions in Waterville Valley include:

  • Mount Tecumseh: Standing at 4,003 feet, Mount Tecumseh is one of the most popular peaks in the White Mountains and offers spectacular views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Greeley Pond: Nestled in the heart of the National Forest, Greeley Pond is a tranquil oasis that offers great fishing and boating opportunities.
  • Mad River: The scenic Mad River is one of the best places in the region for kayaking, canoeing, and tubing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waterville Valley

Waterville Valley offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in New England, with over 30 miles of trails winding through the town’s scenic landscape. The trails offer a range of difficulty levels, from easy strolls to challenging ascents, and are equally popular among beginner and veteran hikers alike. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Waterville Valley include:

  • The Greeley Ponds Trail: This 4.2-mile trail is a scenic trek through lush forests and beside babbling brooks, ending at two picturesque mountain ponds.
  • The Osceola Trail: With a distance of 7.6 miles round-trip, the trail offers a challenging ascent to the summit of Mount Osceola, which offers dazzling panoramic views of the White Mountains.
  • The Livermore Trail: This moderate 5.6-mile loop offers a spectacular view of Waterville Valley and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Waterville Valley: Activities and Tours

Waterville Valley offers a myriad of activities and tours, ranging from thrilling adventures to relaxing escapes. Some of the top-rated things to do in Waterville Valley include:

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: With over 220 acres of skiable terrain and 11 lifts, Waterville Valley Resort is one of the premier ski destinations in New England.
  • Fishing: Waterville Valley boasts a variety of fishing options, including Greeley Pond, Mad River, and other nearby lakes, streams, and rivers.
  • Guided Nature Tours: Take a guided tour of the beautiful landscapes, including hikes and treks in the National Forest.
  • Mountain Biking: Cruise through mountain forests and stunning valleys on your bike with the many biking trails in town.

Food and Drink Scene of Waterville Valley

Waterville Valley boasts an excellent food and drink scene, offering an eclectic mix of local and international cuisine, craft beers, and fine wines. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Waterville Valley include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Fresh Atlantic lobster stuffed in a buttery roll is a New England classic and a staple in Waterville Valley.
  • Blueberry Pancakes: The town is known for its blueberry pancakes, made with fresh, locally grown blueberries and served with warm syrup.
  • Poutine: Quebec’s national dish of French fries, cheese curds, and brown gravy has become very popular in the region and is a must-try for any visitor.

Local Beverages

  • Local Microbrews: Waterville Valley has a vibrant craft beer scene, and visitors can indulge in locally brewed beers like Olde Waterville Lager and Buggs Island Pale Ale.
  • Wine: The town boasts an array of vineyards and wineries, producing award-winning red and white wines.

Where to Stay in Waterville Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Waterville Valley offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious guesthouses and resorts. Some of the best places to stay in Waterville Valley include:

  • Silver Fox Inn: This charming Inn offers comfortable rooms, hearty breakfasts, and excellent views of the White Mountains.
  • Black Bear Lodge: Nestled in the woods, this cozy Lodge offers serene accommodations, hot tubs, a game room, and a complimentary shuttle to the Waterville Valley Resort.
  • Snowy Owl Inn: The Inn boasts a ski-in/ski-out location, spacious rooms, fireplaces, and a hot tub.

What to Buy in Waterville Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Waterville Valley is home to many specialty shops, boutiques, and souvenir stores, offering an array of unique and locally made products. Some of the best things to buy in Waterville Valley include:

Best Time to Visit Waterville Valley

Waterville Valley is a year-round destination, offering distinct seasonal experiences for visitors. Summer (June-August) is the most popular time to visit, with warm weather, longer days, and an abundance of outdoor activities. Fall (September-November) offers a stunning display of foliage, while winter (December-March) offers excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities. Spring (April-May) is a quieter time to visit, with mild weather and blooming flowers.

Getting to Waterville Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Waterville Valley is conveniently located and easily accessible by various modes of transport. Some of the best ways to get to Waterville Valley include:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, which is approximately 100 miles from Waterville Valley.
  • By Car: Visitors can reach the town by car via the interstate highways or state routes.
  • By Bus: The town is serviced by various bus companies, including Concord Coach Lines and Greyhound, with connections to nearby cities like Boston.

How to Get Around in Waterville Valley

Visitors can explore Waterville Valley by various modes of transportation, including:

  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available at the Manchester-Boston Regional Airport and nearby cities, and visitors can rent a car for the duration of their stay.
  • Shuttle Services: Interconnected in-town shuttle services to famous destinations can be called via phone or checked for schedules online.
  • Bicycle Rentals: Visitors can rent bikes from the many rental shops in town and explore the scenic bike trails at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Waterville Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waterville Valley is located in the heart of the White Mountains, offering easy access to many nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the best places to visit near Waterville Valley include:

  • Cannon Mountain: Located in Franconia Notch, this popular mountain resort offers excellent skiing, snowboarding, and hiking opportunities.
