Introduction to Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley is a picturesque area located in New Hampshire, United States. The area derives its name from the Merrimack River, which flows through the valley, cutting through the beautiful landscape and offering breathtaking views. Merrimack Valley offers ample opportunities for picturesque hikes, biking routes, natural attractions, and cultural activities, making it an appealing destination for travelers interested in exploring New England’s countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley has a lot to offer to visitors and tourists who are looking for an ideal vacation destination. Below are some of the top-rated places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Merrimack Valley is home to a range of museums and historical places that offer a window into the region’s past. Some of the best include:

  • Currier Museum of Art: A museum of European and American art located in Manchester, New Hampshire.
  • Millyard Museum: A museum located in Manchester, New Hampshire, which showcases the history of Manchester’s industrial community.
  • Canterbury Shaker Village: A historic museum located in Canterbury, New Hampshire, featuring 25 original Shaker buildings.
  • America’s Stonehenge: A prehistoric stone formation located in Salem, New Hampshire, that has mystified visitors for centuries.

Natural Attractions of Merrimack Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Merrimack Valley offers a range of natural attractions that offer a unique and exciting experience for visitors. Here are a few of the must-see natural attractions in the region:

  • Franconia Notch State Park: A 6,000-acre park featuring hiking trails, campgrounds, and the iconic Flume Gorge, located in Franconia, New Hampshire.
  • White Mountains National Forest: A vast expanse of wilderness area that offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and fishing.
  • Lake Winnipesaukee: A large freshwater lake located in the Lakes Region of Central New Hampshire, offering boating, swimming, and fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley boasts an excellent network of hiking and biking trails that offer a unique and exciting experience for visitors. Here are a few of the most popular routes:

  • Mount Monadnock: A popular hiking trail that offers stunning views at the summit, located in Jaffrey, New Hampshire.
  • Kancamagus Highway: A scenic drive that winds through the White Mountains of New Hampshire, offering several hiking trails.
  • Northern Rail Trail: A 58-mile bike trail that follows the old Boston & Maine railroad, offering a peaceful ride through the valley.

Best Things to Do in Merrimack Valley: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions mentioned above, Merrimack Valley offers several activities and tour options that visitors can participate in. Some of the best things to do in Merrimack Valley include:

  • Take a scenic drive on the Kancamagus Highway
  • Take a tour of the Currier Museum of Art or Canterbury Shaker Village
  • Go ice climbing or snowshoeing in Franconia Notch State Park
  • Visit a local brewery for a beer tasting or tour

Food and Drink Scene of Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley is home to several restaurants, breweries, and wineries that offer a diverse culinary experience. Here are a few must-tries when visiting the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple cider donuts: a classic New England treat, served fresh at local farm stands.
  • Lobster roll: a seafood delicacy served on a buttered bun.
  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is known for its maple syrup, which is used to sweeten various dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: New Hampshire is home to several world-class breweries, including Smuttynose, Portsmouth, and Moat Mountain Brewing Company.
  • Wine: The region offers some excellent wineries, such as LaBelle Winery, located in Amherst, New Hampshire

Where to Stay in Merrimack Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Merrimack Valley offers several accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Visitors can choose from budget-friendly options or splurge on a luxury stay. Here are some of the top rated accommodations in Merrimack Valley:

  • Omni Mount Washington Resort: A luxurious resort located in Bretton Woods, New Hampshire, offering a range of outdoor activities and amenities.
  • Attitash Mountain Resort: A family-friendly resort located in Bartlett, New Hampshire, offering a range of indoor and outdoor activities.
  • The Highlander Inn: A cozy inn located in Manchester, New Hampshire, offering comfortable lodging and a complimentary breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Merrimack Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Merrimack Valley, there are several souvenirs and local products you can take home as a memory of your trip. Some of the best souvenirs and local products include:

  • Maple Syrup: A sweet and delicious memento from your trip, produced locally in New Hampshire.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans handcraft beautiful jewelry in the region.
  • Pottery: The area has many skilled artisans who create unique pottery pieces that reflect the region’s natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley has a rich history and culture that visitors can explore by immersing themselves in the area’s local customs. Some of the local customs and culture include:

  • Local festivals and events like the New Hampshire Highland Games or the Deerfield Fair.
  • Covered bridges: New Hampshire has more than 50 historic covered bridges that can be found in the valleys and mountains of the region.
  • Agriculture: Agriculture is an essential part of Merrimack Valley culture, and visitors can experience local farming by visiting local farms and farm stands.

Best Time to Visit Merrimack Valley

The best time to visit Merrimack Valley is in fall when the foliage turns into different shades of brown, red, and yellow. This season typically starts in mid-September and lasts through October. The weather is also comfortable during spring and summer, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Merrimack Valley

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the region by hiking and visiting parks responsibly.
  • Bring insect repellent during the summer months.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks and other public areas.
  • Ignore safety signs or leave marked trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley is a generally safe place to visit. However, visitors should take necessary precautions while exploring the natural attractions and hiking trails. Below are some tips to ensure your safety during your trip:

  • Always stay on marked trails while hiking.
  • Bring necessary equipment like sturdy footwear, water, and a map.
  • Secure your valuables while exploring public areas.

Health in Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley is home to several high-quality hospitals and clinics. It’s always advisable to carry travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses you may encounter during your visit.

Getting to Merrimack Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Merrimack Valley

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is the closest airport to Merrimack Valley, located about 10 miles from downtown Manchester.

Bus Stations Near Merrimack Valley

  • Concord Coach Lines offer bus services from Boston to Concord and Manchester in New Hampshire.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car in Boston and drive to Merrimack Valley.

How to Get Around in Merrimack Valley

Merrimack Valley offers several transportation options to make exploring the area more comfortable. Visitors can choose from:

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from Manchester-Boston Regional Airport or other car rental agencies.
  • Public transportation: Manchester Transit Authority operates bus services throughout the area.
  • Bicycle rental: Visitors can rent bicycles from several shops in Manchester, offering guided and self-guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Merrimack Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Merrimack Valley is an excellent starting point to explore other nearby areas in New Hampshire and New England. Here are some of the best destinations for day trips:

  • Portsmouth, New Hampshire: A charming seaport town located on New Hampshire’s coast, features historic downtown, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • Boston, Massachusetts: Boston is located just an hour away from Merrimack Valley, offering world-class museums, restaurants, and historical attractions.
  • Newport, Rhode Island: Located just over two hours away, Newport is a picturesque town on the coast of Rhode Island, famous for its stunning mansions and sailing.

Conclusion

Merrimack Valley is an ideal destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and foodies. With several cultural and natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, and a diverse food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone here.
