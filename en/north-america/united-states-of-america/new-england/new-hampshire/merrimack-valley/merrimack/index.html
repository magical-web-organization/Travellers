Introduction to Merrimack

Nestled in the heart of Merrimack Valley, in New Hampshire, lies the charming town of Merrimack. With a rich history dating back to the 18th century, this quaint town has something to offer to every traveler. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks and bustling city life, Merrimack provides visitors with a unique glimpse into New England’s charm.

List of Places to Visit in Merrimack

Looking for places to visit in Merrimack? We’ve got you covered! Whether you’re a fan of history, culture, or outdoor adventures, Merrimack has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Merrimack Premium Outlets: With more than 100 stores, this outlet center is a shopper’s paradise. Pick up deals from famous brands like Calvin Klein, Nike, and more.
  • Brookstone Park: Perfect for a day trip, this park offers scenic views of the sprawling landscape of Merrimack Valley. Go kayaking on its serene waters, or take a leisurely stroll down its picturesque trails.
  • Anheuser-Busch Brewery: Get a behind-the-scenes look at one of the biggest breweries in the country. Tours include free samples of their world-famous beers, and kids can enjoy a fun playground and game room.
  • Merrimack Historical Society: Discover the fascinating history of Merrimack and its role in the American Revolution. The museum features artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Merrimack: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horse Hill Nature Preserve: Covering nearly 600 acres, this nature preserve is a favorite for hiking enthusiasts. Trails range from beginner-friendly to advanced and offer breathtaking views of the wilderness.
  • Lake Baboosic: Looking for a place to relax and soak up the sun? This freshwater lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, and picnicking.
  • Purgatory Falls: This stunning waterfall is worth the steep hike to get there. Once you arrive, you’ll be rewarded with a peaceful oasis and beautiful views of the falling water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Merrimack

Merrimack is a haven for hiking and biking enthusiasts. With over 50 miles of trails, there’s no shortage of routes to explore. Some of the most popular trails in the area include:

  • Andres Institute of Art Sculpture Park Trail: This 140-acre park features over 60 sculptures and four miles of hiking trails.
  • Wasserman Park Trail: This 150-acre park has several easy trails suitable for families and beginners. It also features a lake, playgrounds, and picnic areas.
  • Horse Hill Nature Preserve Trail: With over 600 acres of land, this park features over 20 miles of trails of varying difficulties. It’s a great destination for both mountain biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Merrimack: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for even more things to do in Merrimack, consider these activities and tours:

  • SkyVenture New Hampshire: Get your adrenaline pumping with indoor skydiving. This high-tech experience simulates the feeling of freefall, without jumping out of a plane.
  • Merrimack Valley Golf Club: This championship course features 18 holes of rolling greens, beautiful water features, and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Majestic Theatre: Catch a show or concert at this historic 900-seat theater. From Broadway shows to local productions, there’s something for everyone.

Week-Long Itinerary for Merrimack

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Merrimack, consider this itinerary to make the most out of your visit:

  • Day 1: Spend the day exploring the historic downtown area of Merrimack. Don’t miss the Merrimack Historical Society and the Anheuser-Busch Brewery tour.
  • Day 2: Visit Lake Baboosic for a day of swimming and picnicking.
  • Day 3: Take a hiking or mountain biking trip to the Horse Hill Nature Preserve and end the day with a sunset at Wasserman Park.
  • Day 4: Shop at the Merrimack Premium Outlets and visit nearby attractions like the Brookstone Park.
  • Day 5: Explore the Andres Institute of Art Sculpture Park Trail and take an indoor skydiving experience at SkyVenture New Hampshire.
  • Day 6: Play a round of golf at the Merrimack Valley Golf Club and catch a show at the Majestic Theatre.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations like Boston or Portland, Maine.

Food and Drink Scene of Merrimack

No trip to Merrimack is complete without indulging in some of its local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New England Clam Chowder: A classic New England dish, this creamy soup is filled with fresh clams, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Lobster Roll: A delicious toasted bun filled with succulent chunks of fresh lobster meat, lightly dressed in mayo and other spices.
  • Apple Cider Donut: Made with fresh apple cider and topped with cinnamon sugar, these sweet treats are perfect for breakfast or snacks.

Local Beverages

  • Anheuser-Busch Beers: Try a variety of beers from Merrimack’s largest brewery, including their famous Budweiser and Bud Light.
  • Local Cider: Made from locally grown apples, these refreshing drinks are perfect for warm summer days.
  • Coffee: Merrimack has several coffee shops where you can enjoy a freshly brewed cup of joe.

Where to Stay in Merrimack: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Looking for a place to stay in Merrimack? Here are some of the best options:

  • Hilton Garden Inn: This upscale hotel is located near several attractions and has luxurious amenities like an indoor pool and a fitness center.
  • Quality Inn: This budget-friendly option offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Residence Inn by Marriott: Ideal for extended stays, this hotel features spacious suites with kitchens and separate living areas.

What to Buy in Merrimack: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs to remember your trip to Merrimack? Consider these local products:

  • Maple Syrup: Made from locally grown maple trees, Merrimack’s maple syrup is a perfect gift to take home.
  • Craft Beer: Grab a six-pack of Anheuser-Busch beers to savor the taste of Merrimack’s best breweries.
  • Handcrafted Items: Local artisans sell handcrafted items like pottery, paintings, and jewelry at various markets and shops throughout the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Merrimack

Merrimack has a rich history and culture that visitors can discover through its landmarks and museums. The town celebrates its heritage with several events and festivals throughout the year, including:

  • Merrimack Fall Festival: This festival celebrates the changing of the seasons with a parade, live music, and lots of food and drink.
  • Strawberry Festival: Enjoy a day of family-friendly fun with games, crafts, and all things strawberry!
  • Fourth of July Celebrations: Merrimack’s Independence Day celebrations include fireworks, parades, and concerts.

Best Time to Visit Merrimack

The best time to visit Merrimack is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm, and outdoor activities like hiking and swimming are in full swing. Autumn is another great time to visit, with picturesque foliage and plenty of fall festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Merrimack

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Merrimack:

Do:

  • Do take a tour of the Anheuser-Busch Brewery and sample some of their famous beers.
  • Do hike to Purgatory Falls and enjoy the stunning views.
  • Do take a day trip to nearby destinations like Boston or Portland, Maine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the parks and trails.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors.
  • Don’t drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are a top priority for visitors to Merrimack. Here are some important tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Merrimack

  • Be aware of wildlife when hiking or camping.
  • Lock your car doors and windows when leaving it unattended.
  • Follow all traffic rules and regulations while driving.

Health in Merrimack

  • Stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Check yourself and your pets for ticks after being outdoors.
  • Seek medical attention if needed at one of the town’s hospitals or clinics.

Getting to Merrimack: Transportation Options and Logistics

Merrimack is easily accessible by several modes of transportation:

Airports Near Merrimack

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: Located just 15 minutes away, this airport serves several domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Merrimack

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus company operates several routes between Merrimack and nearby destinations like Boston, Massachusetts, and Portland, Maine.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Several car rental companies operate in and around Merrimack.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Uber and Lyft operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Merrimack

The best way to get around Merrimack is by car, either by renting one or using a ride-sharing service. Several companies offer bike rentals if you’re interested in exploring the town on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Merrimack: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Merrimack is located near several attractions and destinations that are worth a visit. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Boston, Massachusetts: Boston is just an hour’s drive from Merrimack and is famous for its history, culture, and delicious cuisine.
  • Mount Major State Park: Located just an hour away, this state park features hiking trails and a stunning view of Lake Winnipesaukee.
  • Portland, Maine: Portland is a charming seaside town famous for its fresh seafood, quaint streets, and beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Merrimack is a charming town in New Hampshire that has something for everyone. From natural attractions and outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and bustling city life, Merrimack provides visitors with a unique glimpse into New England’s charm. Plan your trip today to discover all that Merrimack has to offer!
