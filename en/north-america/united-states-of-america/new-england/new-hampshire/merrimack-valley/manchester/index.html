Introduction to Manchester

Manchester is a bustling city located in Merrimack Valley, New Hampshire, in New England. It is a hub of cultural activities, outdoor adventures, and vibrant nightlife. With its blend of urban amenities and natural beauty, Manchester is a popular tourist destination throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Manchester

There are plenty of things to do and see in Manchester. Here are some of the popular attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Manchester’s history is preserved in its museums and historical places. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in the city:

  • Currier Museum of Art: This museum houses an impressive collection of European and American art, including works by Monet, Picasso, and Matisse. The museum’s modern architecture and beautiful gardens make it a must-visit attraction in Manchester.
  • Millyard Museum: This museum showcases the industrial history of Manchester and its neighboring towns. You can discover exhibits and artifacts related to the textile industry, immigration, and manufacturing history of the region.
  • Zimmerman House: Designed by Frank Lloyd Wright, Zimmerman House is a beautiful example of Usonian architecture. You can take a guided tour of the house and explore the architect’s design philosophy.

Natural Attractions of Manchester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Manchester also boasts plenty of natural attractions where you can enjoy outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Livingston Park: Spread across 54 acres, Livingston Park is an ideal spot for picnics, hiking, biking, and fishing. The park has many trails that wind through the woods and offer scenic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Lake Massabesic: This 2,500-acre lake is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and hiking. You can also spot a variety of bird species and other wildlife in the area.
  • Merrimack Premium Outlets: This outlet mall has over 100 stores, including brands like Nike, Under Armour, and Michael Kors. It’s a great place to shop for discounted products and spend a day out with family or friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manchester

Manchester is known for its scenic trails and beautiful parks. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the city:

  • Rock Rimmon Trail: This moderate trail is located in Rock Rimmon Park. It’s a popular destination for hiking, biking, and jogging. The trail offers scenic views of the city and the surrounding hills.
  • Heritage Trail: This paved trail runs for about two miles and is an easy trek. The trail is wheelchair and stroller accessible and is suitable for all ages. You can explore the serene natural beauty of the area while hiking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Manchester: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Manchester has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Segway Tour: Take a Segway tour of the mill yard area and discover the industrial history of the city.
  • Zoo and Gardens: Visit the Stone Zoo and enjoy the beauty of the botanical gardens.
  • SkyVenture New Hampshire: If you’re an adrenaline junkie, then SkyVenture is a must-visit place. Experience indoor skydiving and feel the rush of adrenaline.

Food and Drink Scene of Manchester

Manchester’s food and drink scene is diverse and vibrant. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Manchester is known for its fresh seafood, especially lobster rolls and clam chowder.
  • Poutine: A Canadian classic, poutine is made with French fries, cheese curds, and beef gravy. It’s a favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is known for its maple syrup. Try it with pancakes or as a glaze for meats.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Manchester has a thriving craft beer scene. Visit local breweries such as Litherman’s Limited Brewery and Able Ebenezer Brewing Company to enjoy some of the best beer in the area.
  • Apple Cider: New Hampshire is known for its apple cider. Enjoy it hot or cold, depending on the weather.

Where to Stay in Manchester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manchester has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • The Hilton Garden Inn: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Manchester and is within walking distance to many attractions.
  • Fairfield Inn: This hotel offers comfortable, spacious rooms and is located near the airport and downtown area.
  • Inn at Crystal Lake & Pub: This cozy inn is located in nearby Eaton, New Hampshire, and is a great option for those seeking a more rustic experience.

What to Buy in Manchester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should consider buying while in Manchester:

  • Maple Syrup: As mentioned earlier, New Hampshire is famous for its maple syrup. You can bring some home as a tasty souvenir.
  • Craft Beer: Pack some local craft beer from breweries like Litherman’s Limited Brewery or Able Ebenezer Brewing Company to enjoy later.
  • Handmade Pottery: Manchester has several galleries and shops that sell unique handmade pottery items that can be great for gift-giving.

Local Culture and Customs of Manchester

Manchester takes pride in preserving its rich history and culture. Here are a few customs and traditions you should be aware of:

  • Respect the State Symbols: New Hampshire has several important state symbols, including the state flower (purple lilac) and the state bird (purple finch). These symbols are deeply rooted in the state’s history and must be respected by all.
  • Be Polite: Manchester residents are known for their polite manners. Remember to use “please” and “thank you” when interacting with servers, store clerks, and others in the service industry.

Best Time to Visit Manchester

The best time to visit Manchester is during the fall season, between September to November. The weather is mild, and the fall foliage makes the city look magical. You can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and biking, and also attend the famous New Hampshire Film Festival in October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manchester

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Manchester:

Do:

  • Respect Local Culture: As mentioned before, Manchester residents take pride in their culture and history. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try Local Food and Drink: Manchester has a diverse food and drink scene. Try local dishes such as lobster rolls, poutine, and apple cider.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the city clean by not littering while exploring Manchester.
  • Be Rude: Remember to be polite and courteous to Manchester residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manchester

Manchester is a generally safe city, but it’s always recommended to take precautions while visiting. Here are some safety tips:

  • Stay in Well-Lit Areas: Avoid isolated and poorly lit areas during nighttime.
  • Be Aware of Surroundings: Stay alert and aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Manchester

It’s always important to prioritize your health while traveling. Here are some health tips while in Manchester:

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of fluids while exploring Manchester to avoid dehydration.
  • Carry Medications: If you’re on medication, make sure to carry sufficient supplies with you.

Getting to Manchester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manchester is easily accessible from neighboring areas and other states. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Manchester

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: This airport is located about 10 miles from downtown Manchester.

Bus Stations Near Manchester

  • Boston Express: This bus service provides daily trips between Boston and Manchester.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Manchester has several car rental companies such as Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis.
  • Uber/Lyft: Uber and Lyft services are available in Manchester.

How to Get Around in Manchester

Manchester has several transportation options to get around the city. Here are some of the best ways to get around:

  • Bus: Manchester Transit Authority runs a bus service that covers the entire city.
  • Taxi: Several taxi services are available in Manchester, including Manchester Taxi, Queen City Taxi, and R&G Taxi Services.
  • Rideshare Services: Both Uber and Lyft are available in Manchester.

Where to Go Next from Manchester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, explore some of the nearby destinations from Manchester. Here are a few places to visit:

  • Cape Cod: This popular tourist destination is located about 2 hours from Manchester. You can spend a day exploring the beautiful beaches and quaint towns.
  • Boston: You can easily take a day trip to Boston from Manchester. Enjoy the numerous tourist attractions, shopping destinations, and local cuisine.

Conclusion

Manchester is a beautiful city with something to offer for every kind of traveler. With its rich history, cultural attractions, beautiful parks, and outdoor adventures, Manchester is a must-visit destination in New Hampshire. Whether you’re in search of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, or delicious food, Manchester is sure to have something that will make you fall in love with the city.
