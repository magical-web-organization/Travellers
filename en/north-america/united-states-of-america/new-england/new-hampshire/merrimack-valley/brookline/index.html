Introduction to Brookline

Located in the Merrimack Valley, in New Hampshire, Brookline is a charming town that sits in the heart of the Granite State’s southern region. The town is part of the Greater Boston area and enjoys the best of both worlds - easy access to the urban amenities of the big city and the stunning natural beauty that New Hampshire is famous for.

Brookline is a popular destination for visitors of all ages and interests. The town offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation. From historic landmarks and museums to scenic hiking trails and waterfalls, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Brookline.

List of Places to Visit in Brookline

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Brookline Historical Society & Museum - This museum is housed in an 18th-century farmhouse and offers exhibits on the town’s history and culture.
  2. The Martin Bridge - This historic bridge is one of the oldest in the state and is a popular spot for photos.

Natural Attractions of Brookline: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Potanipo - This picturesque lake is surrounded by wooded hills and offers plenty of recreational activities, including kayaking, fishing, and hiking.
  2. The Andres Institute of Art - This outdoor sculpture park features over 80 works of art scattered over 140-acres of rolling hills and meadows.
  3. Dakin Farm Preserve - This nature preserve spans over 1000 acres and offers visitors plenty of hiking trails, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting opportunities.
  4. Pack Monadnock - A popular hiking spot in the area for its short and easy trails that reward with scenic views of the rolling hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brookline

  1. The Wapack Trail - This 21-mile trail offers hikers and bikers stunning views of the Monadnock and Pack Monadnock Mountains.
  2. Monadnock-Sunapee Greenway Trail - A 48-miles trail that passes through the highlights of Mt Monadnock and Sunapee Mountains.
  3. Brookline Rail Trail - A paved 3.5-mile-long bike trail that offers scenic views along the old railway tracks.

Best Things to Do in Brookline: Activities and Tours

  1. Taste of Brookline - Take a food tour of the town and discover its culinary treasures.
  2. Apple Picking at Brookdale Fruit Farm - Pick your own apples, berries, and pumpkins, and enjoy a wagon ride around the farm at Brookdale Fruit Farm.
  3. Horseback Riding at Maple Hill Stables - Explore the countryside on horseback at Maple Hill Stables.
  4. Gamble Mill Tour - Learn about the history of the Gamble Mill and the village of Brookline through a guided tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Brookline

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Maple Syrup-Baked Beans - A staple dish in New Hampshire, these baked beans are a sweet and hearty treat.
  2. Lobster Rolls - A classic New England dish, a perfect choice during summer months.
  3. Apple Cider Donuts - Made with fresh apples from the local orchards, these sweet treats are a must-try in Brookline.

Local Beverages

  1. Local Craft Beer - with Riverwalk Cafe and Music Bar being a standout favorite featuring in-house brewed beers.
  2. Maple Syrup and Honey Wines - Brookline is known for its maple syrup production, and the local beekeepers extend their honey expertise to a winery experience.

Where to Stay in Brookline: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Captain Woodbury Guest House - A lovely colonial-style home that has been turned into a guesthouse with great reviews.
  2. The Hotel Uma - An upscale boutique hotel that offers luxurious accommodations and outdoor hot tubs to relax in.

What to Buy in Brookline: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Maple Syrup - The town is famous for the production of maple syrup so don’t forget to bring back some while you’re here.
  2. Honey - Brookline also has an abundance of honey farms, and from honey wines to honey sticks, bring back the sweet taste of Brookline in your bag.
  3. Hand-crafted Pottery - Brookline is known for its pottery scene, and the local artisans make some beautiful handcrafted pieces that are well worth the investment.

Local Culture and Customs of Brookline

Brookline is a charming New England town that prides itself on its rich history, local cuisine and outdoor living. Residents tend to be welcoming and friendly, with a strong sense of community that extends to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Brookline

The best time to visit Brookline is during the summer/fall months between June and November, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is a particularly stunning season to visit the region, with its colorful foliage, apple orchards, and fall festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brookline

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities and stunning natural scenery while in Brookline.
  • Do visit the Historical Society & Museum to learn about the town’s fascinating history.
  • Do try the local cuisine, especially maple syrup, honey, and craft beers.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to bring insect repellents and sunscreen.
  • Don’t be shy about asking for recommendations from locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brookline

Brookline is generally a safe town, but visitors should take standard precautions such as locking their vehicles, avoiding walking alone in remote areas, and staying aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Brookline

There are no specific health concerns for travelers to Brookline. Visitors should adhere to standard health recommendations, including staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen, and taking precautions against ticks, especially during the warmer months.

Getting to Brookline: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brookline

Manchester-Boston Regional Airport is the nearest airport, approximately 40 minutes away by car. Boston Logan International Airport is also a viable option, approximately 1 hour away by car, but typically offers more expanded flight schedules and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Brookline

Several bus companies offer service to the area, including Greyhound, Concord Coach Lines, and Dartmouth Coach. C&J Bus Lines also operate services from Boston Logan International Airport to nearby towns.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Brookline and explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Brookline

The town is small enough and most places are within walking or biking distance. Rental cars and taxis are also available for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Brookline: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The region offers a wealth of exciting destinations to visit, including:

  1. Peterborough - A charming New England town located just 10 minutes from Brookline, home to cozy shops, galleries, restaurants and wineries.
  2. Nashua - A larger city located 15 minutes from Brookline, offering big-city amenities, shopping, and dining.
  3. Boston - The cultural and economic center of New England offers world-class museums, dining, and entertainment, just over an hour’s drive away.

Conclusion

Brookline, NH, is an idyllic New Hampshire town that offers visitors a wealth of attractions and activities to suit all interests. Whether visitors prefer to indulge in history, culture, nature or outdoor recreation, there’s something for everyone here. Explore this charming world and enjoy all that Brookline has to offer.
