Introduction to Salem

Located in the Merrimack Valley of New Hampshire, Salem is a charming town known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or simply looking for a peaceful getaway, Salem has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the top attractions and activities in and around Salem, as well as important travel tips to help you plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Salem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salem is home to many cultural landmarks and historic sites that are sure to fascinate visitors of all ages. Here are some must-visit attractions in Salem:

  • The Salem Historical Society: This museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits related to Salem’s fascinating history. Here, you can learn about the town’s prominent figures, cultural heritage, and important events that shaped its unique character.
  • America’s Stonehenge: Located just outside of Salem, this ancient archaeological site features a maze of stone structures and chambers that date back over 4,000 years. The site is shrouded in mystery and offers a fascinating glimpse into the prehistoric past.
  • The Mary Ann List Memorial Museum: This small museum is dedicated to the memory of Mary Ann List, a local woman who became a well-known figure in the abolitionist movement. The museum features exhibits on List’s life and legacy, as well as other notable figures in the anti-slavery movement.

Natural Attractions of Salem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure, Salem won’t disappoint. Here are some top natural attractions to explore in the area:

  • Canobie Lake Park: This amusement park features over 85 rides and attractions, including roller coasters, water rides, and games. Canobie Lake Park is a great destination for families and thrill-seekers alike.
  • Methuen Rail Trail: This scenic trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and jogging. The trail follows the former Boston and Maine Railroad line and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Salem Town Forest: This expansive forest offers miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for fishing, picnicking, and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salem

Salem is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Spicket River Greenway: This 3.5-mile trail follows the Spicket River through Methuen and Lawrence, offering fantastic views of the surrounding landscape. It’s a great option for walkers, hikers, and bikers.
  • The Salem Bike-Ped Corridor: This 5-mile route connects the towns of Salem and Windham. It’s a popular destination for bikers, runners, and walkers and offers scenic views of the New Hampshire countryside.
  • The Harold Parker State Forest: This expansive forest is located just outside of Salem and offers over 35 miles of hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Salem: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, there are plenty of fun activities and tours to enjoy in Salem:

  • The Salem Farmer’s Market: This weekly farmer’s market features fresh produce, artisanal goods, and live music. It’s a great place to sample some of the local flavors and meet other visitors and locals alike.
  • The Rockingham Park: This horse racing track offers exciting live races and plenty of betting opportunities. It’s a must-visit for anyone looking for some exciting nightlife.
  • Ghost Tour of Salem: Explore the spooky history of Salem with a ghost tour. These tours take you through some of the town’s most haunted sites and are a great way to learn about the local legends and myths.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salem

Salem offers plenty of attractions and activities for a week-long vacation. Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay:

Day 1: Visit the Salem Historical Society and learn about the town’s rich history. Day 2: Explore America’s Stonehenge and marvel at the ancient structures and chambers. Day 3: Hike the Salem Town Forest and enjoy a picnic lunch amidst the stunning scenery. Day 4: Visit the Mary Ann List Memorial Museum and learn about her incredible life and legacy. Day 5: Spend the day at Canobie Lake Park, enjoying thrilling rides and attractions. Day 6: Take a cycling tour of the Salem Bike-Ped Corridor and enjoy the beautiful New Hampshire countryside. Day 7: Take a relaxing boat ride on the Spicket River and enjoy the serene views.

Food and Drink Scene of Salem

No trip to Salem is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam chowder: A classic New England dish, Salem’s clam chowder is a must-try. Made with fresh clams, potatoes, cream, and other seasonings, this hearty soup is perfect for chilly days.
  • Lobster roll: Another classic dish, the lobster roll is a staple of New England cuisine. Salem’s version features succulent chunks of fresh lobster meat served in a buttery roll.
  • Apple cider donuts: These sweet treats are a fall favorite in Salem. Made with fresh apple cider and sprinkled with cinnamon sugar, they’re perfect with a cup of coffee or hot cocoa.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Salem is home to several craft breweries that offer a variety of locally brewed beers. Some of the popular breweries in the area include Granite State Growler Tours, Kelsen Brewing Company, and 603 Brewery.
  • Wine: Salem is also known for its wineries, such as Sweet Baby Vineyard and Zorvino Vineyards. They offer wine tastings and tours where you can sample the local flavors.

Where to Stay in Salem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Salem offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some top options:

  • The Red Lion Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Salem and offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites Salem: This affordable hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, as well as a 24-hour fitness center and an indoor pool.
  • The Canobie Lake Hotel: This historic hotel is located near Canobie Lake Park and offers modern amenities and elegant rooms.

What to Buy in Salem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salem is a shopper’s paradise, offering a variety of unique shops and boutiques. Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Salem-themed t-shirts and hats: Show off your love for Salem with a t-shirt or hat featuring the town’s name and iconic landmarks.
  • Handmade crafts and jewelry: Salem is home to several artisanal shops that offer unique handmade crafts and jewelry made by local artists.
  • Candles and other scented products: Salem is known for its candle-making industry, and you can find a variety of scented candles and other products that make great gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Salem

Salem is a vibrant town with a rich cultural heritage. Some important customs and traditions in Salem include:

  • Halloween festivities: Salem is famous for its Halloween celebrations and attracts visitors from around the world each year. The town offers a variety of spooky events and attractions.
  • Appreciation for nature: Salem’s residents have a deep appreciation for the natural beauty of the region and take steps to preserve and protect the environment.
  • Love of history: Salem has a rich history that is celebrated and preserved through its museums, historical sites, and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Salem

The best time to visit Salem depends on your preferences and interests. The fall season is a popular time to visit, as the changing leaves and Halloween celebrations add to the town’s charm. Spring and summer are also great times to visit, as the weather is warm and sunny and outdoor activities abound.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salem

Here are some important things to keep in mind when visiting Salem:

Do:

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions: Salem is a small town with a tight-knit community. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and traditions.
  • Do dress appropriately: Salem’s weather can be unpredictable, so be sure to pack layers and comfortable shoes.
  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks: Salem offers a variety of delicious dishes and beverages that are unique to the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Salem takes pride in its natural beauty, and visitors should be mindful of their impact on the environment.
  • Don’t trespass: Some of Salem’s historical and cultural sites are private property and should not be entered without permission.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather: Salem’s weather can change quickly, and visitors should be prepared for rain and sudden drops in temperature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salem

Salem is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times: Even in a safe town like Salem, visitors should be mindful of their belongings and surroundings.
  • Stay in well-lit and populated areas: Especially at night, visitors should stay in areas that are well-lit and populated.
  • Follow traffic rules: When driving or biking in Salem, visitors should follow traffic rules and be aware of other vehicles and pedestrians.

Health in Salem

Salem offers several medical facilities and pharmacies to meet visitors’ health needs. However, visitors should still take care of their health:

  • Stay hydrated: Salem’s weather can be hot and humid, especially in the summer months. Visitors should drink plenty of water and other fluids.
  • Take precautions against ticks: Salem is located in an area where tick-borne illnesses are common. Visitors should take precautions against tick bites, such as wearing long clothing and using insect repellent.

Getting to Salem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salem

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: This is the closest major airport to Salem, located about 25 miles away.
  • Boston Logan International Airport: This airport is located about 35 miles from Salem and offers more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Salem

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus company offers daily service from Boston to Salem.
  • C&J Bus Lines: This company offers daily service from New York City to Salem.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Salem or take a train to a nearby station in Haverhill or Lawrence, Massachusetts.

How to Get Around in Salem

Salem is a small town that is easy to navigate by car or foot. Visitors can also rent bikes or take public transportation to get around.

Where to Go Next from Salem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Salem is located in the heart of New Hampshire’s Merrimack Valley, making it a great destination for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • The White Mountains: This mountain range is located just a few hours north of Salem and offers stunning scenery and outdoor adventures, such as hiking, skiing, and rock climbing.
  • The Lakes Region: Located to the west of Salem, the Lakes Region is home to several picturesque lakes and quaint towns that are perfect for a day trip.
  • Portsmouth: This coastal town is located about an hour east of Salem and offers a charming downtown area, historic sites, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or simply a place to relax and unwind, Salem has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your perfect trip to this charming New Hampshire town.
