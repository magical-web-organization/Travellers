Introduction to Amherst

Located in Merrimack Valley, New Hampshire, Amherst is a charming town in New England that’s renowned for its natural beauty, culture, and history. With a population of just over 11,000, Amherst is the perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful getaway with plenty of outdoor adventures.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing vacation, Amherst has plenty to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best things to see and do in Amherst.

List of Places to Visit in Amherst

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Amherst Village - This charming village dates back to the 18th-century and offers a glimpse into Amherst’s colonial past.

  • Museum of New Hampshire History - Discover the rich history of New Hampshire through interactive exhibits and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Amherst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Babcock State Park - This 5,700-acre park offers miles of hiking trails, fishing and swimming in the park’s two lakes, and stunning views of the surrounding hills.

  • Peabody Mill Environmental Center - Home to 600 acres of forests, fields, and wetlands, this center offers nature trails, bird-watching, and educational events for all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amherst

  • Joe English Reservation - This 600-acre reservation offers over 25 miles of well-marked trails for hikers, mountain bikers, and trail runners.

  • Souhegan River Trail - This level, scenic trail follows the peaceful Souhegan River through Amherst and nearby towns.

Best Things to Do in Amherst: Activities and Tours

  • LaBelle Winery - This elegant winery offers a picturesque tasting room and tours of the vineyard and winemaking facilities.

  • Amherst Country Club - Golf enthusiasts can enjoy a round at this challenging course surrounded by breathtaking views of New Hampshire’s landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amherst

Day Activity
1 Explore the Historic Amherst Village and Museum of New Hampshire History
2 Hike or bike the trails at Joe English Reservation
3 Take a scenic drive through the Monadnock Region and enjoy a winery tour at LaBelle Winery
4 Visit the nearby town of Milford and see the historic Milford Oval
5 Take a guided tour of the Peabody Mill Environmental Center
6 Play a round of golf at the Amherst Country Club
7 Relax and take in the scenic views at Babcock State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Amherst

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll - This New England delicacy is a must-try when visiting Amherst.

  • Apple Cider Donuts - Made with locally-sourced apples, these sweet and tangy treats are a seasonal favorite in Amherst.

Local Beverages

  • LaBelle Winery - Sample award-winning red, white, and fruit wines made from grapes grown in New Hampshire and around the world.

  • Von Trapp Brewing - This family-owned brewery offers traditional Austrian lagers and ales in a cozy farmhouse setting.

Where to Stay in Amherst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Manchester - This modern hotel is just a short drive away from Amherst and offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

  • Jasmine House Bed & Breakfast - This charming B&B is located in historic Amherst Village and offers cozy rooms and a home-cooked breakfast.

What to Buy in Amherst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup - New Hampshire is known for its high-quality, locally-produced maple syrup.

  • Handcrafted Pottery - Amherst is home to several pottery studios that create beautiful, functional pieces using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Amherst

  • Respect for nature is deeply ingrained in New Hampshire’s culture, and visitors are encouraged to practice responsible tourism by following all park rules and disposing of waste responsibly.

  • Tipping 15-20% is customary in restaurants and bars in Amherst.

Best Time to Visit Amherst

The best time to visit Amherst is during the fall when the leaves change, producing breathtaking views across the town and its surroundings. Spring and summer also offer pleasant weather, making it the ideal time for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amherst

Do:

  • Take in the scenic views of Amherst’s countryside.

  • Practice responsible tourism by following park rules and disposing of waste responsibly.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitats of local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amherst

Amherst is generally a safe destination, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Visitors are encouraged to exercise caution when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities and to be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Amherst

Visitors to Amherst should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you as some smaller towns in New Hampshire may not have easily accessible pharmacies.

Getting to Amherst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amherst

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT) - Located just 20 minutes from Amherst, this airport serves several major airlines.

  • Boston Logan International Airport (BOS) - This airport is located approximately 70 miles south of Amherst and is serviced by several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Amherst

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Nashua, NH, approximately 20 miles south of Amherst.

  • Concord Coach Lines - Located in Manchester, NH, approximately 20 miles east of Amherst.

Other Options

  • Rental Car - Amherst is easily accessible by car, and several rental car options are available at both airports and in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Amherst

The best way to get around Amherst is by car. Rental cars are available at Manchester-Boston Regional Airport and in nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Amherst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mount Monadnock - This iconic New Hampshire mountain offers stunning panoramic views and challenging hiking trails.

  • Canobie Lake Park - This family-friendly amusement park is located just 30 minutes from Amherst and offers rides, games, and live entertainment.

Conclusion

Amherst, New Hampshire offers a unique combination of history, culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. From stunning parks and hiking trails to award-winning wineries and charming B&Bs, Amherst has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect getaway to this hidden gem in New England.
