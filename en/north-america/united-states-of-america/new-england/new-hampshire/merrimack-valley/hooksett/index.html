Introduction to Hooksett

Located in the scenic Merrimack Valley region of New Hampshire, Hooksett is a quaint and charming small town full of natural beauty, historic sites, and outdoor adventures. With a population of just under 15,000 people, Hooksett is the perfect destination for travelers who want to experience the serene beauty of New Hampshire’s countryside while still being close to major cities and attractions.

In this tour guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Hooksett, including culturally important attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, must-try dishes, where to stay, and more.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For travelers interested in the history and culture of Hooksett, there are several museums and historical sites to explore. These include:

  • The Robie’s Country Store, which has been in operation since the 1800s and is the oldest country store in New Hampshire. Visitors can shop for local products, souvenirs, and more.
  • The Currier Museum of Art, located in nearby Manchester, is a world-class art museum featuring works by Monet, Picasso, and other renowned artists.
  • The Millyard Museum in Manchester showcases the history of the Amoskeag Manufacturing Company, once the largest textile mill in the world.
  • The New Hampshire Historical Society Museum, also in Manchester, has exhibits on the state’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Hooksett: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hooksett is also home to many natural wonders and outdoor attractions for visitors to enjoy, including:

  • Head’s Pond, a tranquil reservoir surrounded by lush forests and popular for fishing and kayaking.
  • Bear Brook State Park, a 10,000-acre park with miles of hiking and mountain biking trails, several ponds for swimming and fishing, and winter sports activities such as snowmobiling and cross-country skiing.
  • Merrimack River, one of the longest rivers in New England, offers beautiful views and opportunities for fishing, boating, and other water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hooksett

With its abundant natural beauty, Hooksett is an excellent destination for hikers and bikers. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Hooksett include:

  • The Hooksett Rail Trail, a popular multi-use trail that runs 6.5 miles through Hooksett and connects with other nearby trails.
  • The Bear Brook State Park Trail, a network of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and more.
  • The Auburn Rail Trail, a scenic, 2.3-mile trail that runs through Hooksett’s neighboring town of Auburn.

Best Things to Do in Hooksett: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Hooksett, including:

  • The Southern NH Trailblazers Snowmobile Club offers snowmobile tours through Hooksett’s scenic forests and trails.
  • The New Hampshire Motor Speedway, located just a short drive from Hooksett, hosts NASCAR races and other events.
  • Lake Winnipesaukee, one of the largest lakes in New Hampshire, offers opportunities for swimming, fishing, boating, and sightseeing.
  • Stone Bridge Farm, a working farm and vineyard offering tours, tastings, and farm-to-table dining experiences.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hooksett

To make the most of your trip to Hooksett, consider this itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Currier Museum of Art in Manchester and explore the Millyard Museum.

Day 2: Hike or bike the Hooksett Rail Trail and explore Head’s Pond.

Day 3: Spend the day at Bear Brook State Park, hiking, swimming, and fishing.

Day 4: Do a snowmobile tour with the Southern NH Trailblazers Snowmobile Club and enjoy the winter scenery.

Day 5: Visit the New Hampshire Motor Speedway for a race or other event.

Day 6: Spend the day at Lake Winnipesaukee, swimming, boating, and exploring the area.

Day 7: Tour Stone Bridge Farm and enjoy a farm-to-table dining experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Hooksett

Hooksett is known for its New England charm and hospitality. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions and be prepared for cold, snowy winters and warm, humid summers.

Best Time to Visit Hooksett

The best time to visit Hooksett depends on your interests. For outdoor activities, summer is the most popular season, with warm temperatures and sunny skies. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with colorful foliage and milder weather. Winter is a great time for snow sports and cozy indoor activities, while spring brings beautiful blooms and milder temperatures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hooksett

Hooksett is a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should still take precautions, such as locking cars and securing valuables. Be aware of the weather conditions and potential hazards such as icy roads in the winter.

Health in Hooksett

Health services are available at nearby hospitals and clinics in Manchester and Concord.

Getting to Hooksett: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hooksett

The closest airport to Hooksett is the Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, located just 15 minutes from town. Other nearby airports include Boston Logan International Airport and Portland International Jetport.

Bus Stations Near Hooksett

Concord Coach Lines and Greyhound Lines both serve Manchester’s transportation center, located approximately 10 minutes from Hooksett.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive via train to the Amtrak station in Manchester, or by car using Interstates 93 and 293.

How to Get Around in Hooksett

The best way to get around Hooksett is by car, although there are also taxi services available. Some travelers may prefer walking or biking for short trips, especially in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Hooksett: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Hooksett include:

  • Manchester, New Hampshire: Located just 10 minutes from Hooksett, Manchester is a vibrant city with museums, galleries, and historic sites, as well as shopping, dining, and nightlife options.
  • Concord, New Hampshire: The state capital is just a short drive from Hooksett and offers attractions such as the New Hampshire State House and the McAuliffe-Shepard Discovery Center.
  • The White Mountains: One of the most scenic regions in New England, the White Mountains offer breathtaking views, outdoor activities, and charming small towns.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply soaking up the charm of a small New England town, Hooksett is a wonderful destination. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll have everything you need to plan your perfect trip to Hooksett, New Hampshire.
