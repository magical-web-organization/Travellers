Hudson, New Hampshire, is a charming town located in Merrimack Valley, in New England, United States of America. Featuring a mix of outdoor recreation, cultural landmarks, and historical treasures, Hudson is the perfect getaway for travelers seeking an escape from the bustling city life. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the most popular attractions and hidden gems in Hudson, including the top things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

Introduction to Hudson

Home to nearly 25,000 people, Hudson is a small, yet bustling town located just 45 minutes from Boston. Hudson boasts a rich history dating back to the 17th century when it was once inhabited by Native American tribes. Today, the town is recognized for its vibrant community culture, strong economy, and abundant natural beauty. Hudson is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the country with its wide range of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Hudson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, Hudson has plenty to offer. The town is home to several noteworthy museums and historical landmarks, including:

  • Benson’s Animal Park: Founded in 1925, Benson’s Animal Park was once one of the largest zoo attractions in the Northeast. Although it is no longer functioning, visitors can explore its historic ruins and learn about its rich history.
  • The Safford Monument and Museum: This small museum is dedicated to the history of Hudson and the Safford family, who played a significant role in the town’s development.
  • The Pheasant Lane Mall: Not just a mall, this popular Hudson spot is also famous for its outdoor events. It’s a must-visit during the holiday season.

Natural Attractions of Hudson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Hudson is a paradise. Whether you enjoy hiking, biking, or water activities, you’ll find plenty to satisfy your need for adventure in Hudson. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hudson Dog Park: Take a stroll with your dog at Hudson Dog Park, a popular spot for locals and visitors alike. The park is open year-round and offers ample space for pups to run around and have fun.
  • Benson’s Park: Located behind Benson’s Animal Park, Benson’s Park offers beautiful walking trails, a pond, and plenty of space for picnicking and relaxing with family and friends.
  • Roby Park: A family-friendly park in the heart of Hudson, Roby Park is home to a playground, basketball courts, and open fields for picnicking and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hudson

Hudson is known for its extensive network of trails, making it a prime destination for hikers and bikers. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, you’ll find trails to suit your skill level. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • The Hudson Rail Trail: This popular trail system spans over 6 miles, starting near the intersection of Central Street and Kimball Hill Road, and follows the abandoned railroad bed.
  • Benson Park Trails: Benson Park offers several trails that meander through the park’s beautiful wooded areas and include a nature trail rich in plant and animal life.
  • Roby Park Trails: For a more leisurely hike, Roby Park’s trails offer simple paths perfect for families with small kids or anyone seeking an easy trek.

Best Things to Do in Hudson: Activities and Tours

Hudson, NH, offers plenty of activities and tours to suit any interests. You can explore the town’s rich history or enjoy an afternoon outside. Here are some top picks:

  • Hudson Heritage Tours: Take a guided tour of the town’s historic sites, including the Pheasant Lane Mall, the Pheasant Lane Cemetery, and the Benson Animal Park.
  • The Nashua River Rail Trail: Rent a bike and enjoy a scenic ride along the Nashua River Rail Trail, which meanders through the towns of Ayer, Groton, Pepperell, and Dunstable.
  • Hudson Lanes Bowling Alley: For a fun family activity, visit the Hudson Lanes Bowling Alley. This 20-lane bowling alley offers leagues, after-school programs, and family-friendly hours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hudson

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Hudson, here is our suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the town’s historic landmarks and museums, including the Safford Monument and Museum.

Day 2: Head to Benson Park for a day of outdoor adventure. Spend the afternoon hiking, picnicking, and enjoying the beautiful scenery.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Pheasant Lane Mall and learn about its fascinating history.

Day 4: Visit Roby Park, one of the town’s best-kept secrets. Enjoy the many outdoor activities available, including picnicking and basketball.

Day 5: Take a bike ride along the Nashua River Rail Trail.

Day 6: Relax in one of the town’s many parks or shop at the nearby outlets.

Day 7: Visit Hudson Lanes Bowling Alley and end your trip with a fun family activity.

Food and Drink Scene of Hudson

Hudson’s foodie scene is a mix of traditional New England cuisine with a touch of international flair. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: You can’t visit the New England area without sampling a traditional lobster roll. Head to White Birch Brewing for an unforgettable lobster roll experience.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: Visit Benson’s Animal Park and indulge in a fresh batch of the park’s famous apple cider donuts.
  • Clam Chowder: No trip to New England is complete without a bowl of clam chowder. Try the delicious creamy chowder at the nearby Puritan Backroom.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Enjoy a cold pint of craft beer at White Birch Brewing, a favorite among locals and tourists alike.
  • Wine: Visit MS Walker for a glass of wine and a relaxing afternoon.

Where to Stay in Hudson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hudson offers a variety of options for accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Hudson NH Inn: Located in the heart of Hudson, this inn offers comfortable rooms and convenient access to the town’s top attractions and restaurants.
  • Hampton Inn Nashua: Just a short drive from Hudson, this modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and a wide range of amenities, including a fitness center and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Fireside Inn: This charming hotel is located in nearby Nashua and features cozy rooms, an on-site restaurant, and easy access to shopping and dining.

What to Buy in Hudson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to take home from your trip to Hudson? Here are some ideas:

  • Honey: Visit nearby farm stores and stock up on locally produced honey.
  • Maple Syrup: No trip to New Hampshire is complete without a bottle of pure maple syrup.
  • Craft Beer: Pick up some local craft beers from the White Birch Brewing company.

Local Culture and Customs of Hudson

Hudson has a strong sense of community culture and pride. Locals are known for their friendly and welcoming demeanor towards visitors. When visiting, be sure to respect the town and its traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hudson

The best time to visit Hudson is from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. During the fall season, the area is known for its breathtaking foliage, making it another popular time to visit. Keep in mind that winter can be cold and snowy, but those who don’t mind the cold can enjoy an array of winter sports like skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hudson

Do:

  • Respect the town and its traditions.
  • Tip service staff generously.
  • Explore the town’s rich history and local culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s natural beauty.
  • Ignore the rules and guidelines set by local businesses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hudson

Hudson is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard safety precautions, such as locking your car and valuables at all times.

Health in Hudson

Hudson has several hospitals and medical centers in and around the area, providing visitors with access to quality healthcare services whenever necessary.

Getting to Hudson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hudson is easily accessible by air, car, or bus. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Hudson

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport
  • Boston Logan International Airport

Bus Stations Near Hudson

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Nashua
  • Concord Coach Lines in Nashua

Other Options

  • Car Rental
  • Taxi or Uber Service

How To Get Around in Hudson

Hudson is best explored by car, although it is possible to get around the town on foot or by bike. Most local attractions are within close proximity to each other, making it easy to explore on foot.

Where to Go Next from Hudson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hudson is conveniently located within driving distance of several other popular attractions, including:

  • Nashua: Just a few miles from Hudson, Nashua boasts a lively downtown area, shopping, dining and entertainment.
  • Salem: Located in southern New Hampshire, Salem offers tax-free shopping, excellent dining, and live entertainment.
  • Boston: The bustling city of Boston is just 45 minutes from Hudson, making it the perfect day trip destination.

Conclusion

Hudson, New Hampshire is a charming town located in the heart of Merrimack Valley. With its rich history, outdoor recreation, and cultural landmarks, Hudson offers something for everyone. Use our guide to plan your trip to Hudson and experience the best the town has to offer.
