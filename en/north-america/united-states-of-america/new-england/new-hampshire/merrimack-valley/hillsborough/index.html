Introduction to Hillsborough

Nestled in the serene Merrimack Valley of New Hampshire, Hillsborough serves as a peaceful refuge for both locals and visitors. With its quaint charm and vibrant history, this small town offers a range of natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and cultural hotspots.

Whether you’re seeking a quiet retreat or an exciting getaway, Hillsborough is sure to captivate you. From scenic views to fascinating museums, there’s something for everyone in this picturesque New England town.

List of Places to Visit in Hillsborough

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hillsborough boasts rich cultural heritage and is home to many museums and historical sites. The following are some of the top must-see cultural hotspots in the area:

  • Franklin Pierce Homestead: Explore the life and times of the 14th President of the United States, Franklin Pierce, at his family home. The homestead showcases exhibits and artifacts that offer visitors a glimpse into the history and legacy of Pierce as a politician and an individual.

  • Hillsborough Heritage Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Hillsborough town and its people. With a collection of over 10,000 artifacts, exhibits, photographs, and documents, the museum presents an intriguing insight into the town’s past, including its agricultural, industrial, social, and political heritage.

  • Wilton Heritage Museum: Located in the historic district of Wilton, just a few miles from Hillsborough, this museum offers visitors a chance to discover the town’s heritage. The exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and documents that portray Wilton’s vibrant history as a railroad town and hub of manufacturing.

Natural Attractions of Hillsborough: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its serene countryside and colorful fall foliage, Hillsborough is a nature lover’s paradise. The following are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Fox State Forest: This 1,445-acre forest is a great spot for hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing. The forest has over 20 miles of trails and is home to a variety of birds, deer, and other wildlife.

  • Childs Bog: This nature preserve is a unique and fascinating ecosystem, featuring pitcher plants, bog cranberries, and other rare flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the boardwalk trails and observation decks to get a closer look at the plant and animal life.

  • Miller State Park: With its panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, Miller State Park is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking. The park has several well-marked trails of varying difficulty, leading to scenic overlooks and the summit of Pack Monadnock.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hillsborough

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a casual biker, Hillsborough has plenty of trails to explore. The following are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Hillsborough Hiking Trail Network: This network of trails covers over 90 miles of terrain in and around Hillsborough. With varying degrees of difficulty, the trails offer something for everyone, from leisurely walks to strenuous hikes.

  • Monadnock-Sunapee Greenway: This 48-mile hiking trail traverses the rugged terrain between Mount Monadnock and Mount Sunapee. The trail features stunning vistas, waterfalls, and diverse wildlife habitats.

  • The Antrim Bike Path: This 9-mile bike path winds through the scenic countryside east of Hillsborough. The trail passes through forests, farmlands, and quaint villages, offering visitors a glimpse into the pastoral beauty of New Hampshire.

Best Things to Do in Hillsborough: Activities and Tours

From taking a scenic flight over the town to enjoying a leisurely stroll through the local farmer’s market, Hillsborough offers a wide variety of activities and tours for visitors. The following are some of the top things to do in the area:

  • Flying Monkey Aerial Arts: Experience the thrill of flying on aerial silks, trapeze, and lyra at this innovative and exciting activity center. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced aerialist, you’ll find something to challenge and inspire you.

  • Horseshoe Falls Farmstand: This charming farmstand offers visitors a chance to sample fresh produce, baked goods, and other locally sourced products. With a focus on sustainability and community, the farmstand is a great place to learn more about the local food scene and meet the friendly locals.

  • Woods of Terror: If you’re looking for a spooky and thrilling adventure, look no further than the Woods of Terror. This haunted attraction features a variety of terrifying experiences, including haunted trails, escape rooms, and live-action scares.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hillsborough

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Hillsborough, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the area:

Day 1: Explore the Franklin Pierce Homestead and learn about the life and times of the 14th President of the United States.

Day 2: Hike to the summit of Pack Monadnock at Miller State Park and enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Day 3: Spend a day exploring the charming town of Hillsborough, including the Heritage Museum, the Hillsborough Center Historic District, and the local farmer’s market.

Day 4: Take a scenic flight over Hillsborough and the surrounding area to experience the beauty of the countryside from above.

Day 5: Visit Childs Bog State Natural Area and discover the unique and fascinating ecosystem of the bog.

Day 6: Cycle the Antrim Bike Path and soak up the pastoral beauty of New Hampshire.

Day 7: Experience the thrill of flying on aerial silks, trapeze, and lyra at Flying Monkey Aerial Arts.

Food and Drink Scene of Hillsborough

No trip to Hillsborough is complete without sampling the local food and drink scene. From traditional New England fare to innovative fusion cuisine, Hillsborough offers a tantalizing variety of culinary delights. The following are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Glazed Donuts: Made with locally sourced maple syrup, these sweet and savory treats are a staple of New England cuisine.

  • Lobster Rolls: A signature dish of the New England coast, these chunky lobster sandwiches are a delicious and indulgent treat.

  • Apple Pie: The ultimate classic New England dessert, apple pie is a must-try dish when visiting the region.

Local Beverages

  • Apple Cider: Made from fresh, locally sourced apples, apple cider is a refreshing and tasty beverage that’s perfect for cooler autumn days.

  • Local Wine: Hillsborough is home to several wineries and vineyards, producing some of the finest wine in New England.

  • Freshly Brewed Coffee: Hillsborough boasts several local cafes and coffee shops, serving up delicious and aromatic coffee drinks for caffeine enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Hillsborough: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels, Hillsborough offers a range of lodging options to suit all tastes and budgets. The following are some of the best accommodations in the area:

  • The Greensboro Inn: This charming inn is a great spot for a romantic getaway or a family vacation. With comfortable rooms, beautiful garden views, and a delicious breakfast, it’s the perfect place to relax and recharge.

  • The Hancock Inn: This historic inn boasts elegant decor, luxurious amenities, and a warm and welcoming atmosphere. With its stunning gardens, cozy fireplaces, and delicious fine dining, it’s a memorable and indulgent getaway experience.

  • The Maple Leaf Motel: If you’re looking for a more affordable option, the Maple Leaf Motel offers comfortable and clean rooms at a reasonable rate. With its convenient location and friendly staff, it’s a great choice for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Hillsborough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handcrafted pottery to locally made maple syrup, Hillsborough offers a variety of unique and authentic souvenirs and local products to bring home. The following are some of the top items to buy in the area:

  • Handmade Pottery: Hillsborough is home to several talented ceramic artists, creating unique and beautiful pottery pieces that make ideal souvenirs.

  • Maple Syrup: Made from the maple trees that grow abundantly in the area, locally sourced maple syrup is a delicious and authentic treat to bring home.

  • Wildflower Honey: Sourced from local beekeepers, wildflower honey is a sweet and flavorful product that captures the essence of the New Hampshire countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Hillsborough

Hillsborough prides itself on its rich cultural heritage and strong community spirit, which is evident in its many festivals, fairs, and other community events. Some of the most popular events and customs in the area include:

  • The Hillsborough Balloon Festival: Held every July, this festival features dozens of colorful hot air balloons, live music, food trucks, and other entertainment.

  • The Hillsborough Arts & Crafts Fair: This annual fair takes place in September and showcases the work of over 100 artisans, including pottery, jewelry, painting, and more.

  • The Hillsborough Living Nativity: This beloved Christmas tradition has been entertaining and inspiring visitors for over 20 years, featuring live animals, carolers, and a live retelling of the Christmas story.

Best Time to Visit Hillsborough

Hillsborough’s natural beauty and rich cultural offerings make it a great destination to visit year-round. However, the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s an overview of the different seasons in Hillsborough:

  • Spring: With its fresh blooms and mild weather, spring is a great time to explore the outdoors, visit the local farmers’ market, and attend the Hillsborough Arts & Crafts Fair.

  • Summer: Summer is the peak season in Hillsborough, with warm weather, lush greenery, and a plethora of outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy.

  • Fall: With its colorful foliage, crisp weather, and harvest festivals, fall is the most popular time to visit Hillsborough.

  • Winter: While winter can be icy and cold, it’s also a magical time in Hillsborough, with its quaint town center decorations, skiing and snowboarding opportunities, and cozy fireplace evenings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hillsborough

As with any travel destination, there are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Hillsborough:

Do:

  • Explore the local arts and culture scene, including the Hillsborough Arts & Crafts Fair, the Heritage Museum, and the Franklin Pierce Homestead.

  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages, including lobster rolls, maple syrup, and local wines.

  • Embrace the great outdoors, with hiking, biking, and other nature activities on offer.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of local residents or their property without permission.

  • Litter or pollute the local environment, which is home to a rich array of wildlife and natural beauty.

  • Disrespect cultural or religious customs, such as the Hillsborough Living Nativity or local events that honor veterans.

Safety and Health

Hillsborough is a safe and healthy destination for visitors, with a low crime rate and easy access to medical facilities. However, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and valuables, especially in public parking lots, and being aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Safety in Hillsborough

  • Avoid walking or driving in unfamiliar areas at night.

  • Use caution when crossing the street and always obey traffic rules and signals.

  • Keep emergency contact information and important documents with you at all times.

Health in Hillsborough

  • Bring any necessary medications with you and make sure you have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.

  • Stay hydrated and avoid overexertion when engaging in physical activities.

Getting to Hillsborough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hillsborough is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane, with several transportation options available. The following are some tips and details for getting to the area:

Airports Near Hillsborough

  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: This airport, located in Manchester, New Hampshire, is the closest major airport to Hillsborough, with daily direct flights to major US cities.

  • Boston Logan International Airport: This airport is located in Boston, Massachusetts, about a 90-minute drive from Hillsborough.

Bus Stations Near Hillsborough

  • Concord Coach Lines: This bus company offers daily service from major cities in New Hampshire, Maine, and Massachusetts to the nearby towns of Manchester and Concord, both of which are less than an hour’s drive from Hillsborough.

  • Greyhound: This nationwide bus company offers service to many cities across the US, with several stops in various towns and cities in the New Hampshire area.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in the area, making it easy to access Hillsborough by car.

  • Taxi and Shuttle Services: Several taxi and shuttle services are available in the area, offering transportation to and from local airports, bus stations, and other destinations.

How to Get Around in Hillsborough

Hillsborough is a small town, and many of its attractions and amenities are within walking distance of one another. However, if you prefer to explore the area more extensively, there are several transportation options available:

  • Bike Rental: Several local businesses offer bike rentals, making it easy to explore the local countryside and bike trails.

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in the area, providing easy access to local attractions and the surrounding countryside.

  • Taxi and Shuttle Services: Several local taxi and shuttle services offer transportation to and from local hotels, attractions, and airports.

Where to Go Next from Hillsborough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of New Hampshire and the surrounding area, Hillsborough is a great base to set out from. The following are some of the top nearby destinations and excursions:

  • The White Mountains: Just a few hours’ drive north of Hillsborough, the White Mountains offer some of the most stunning scenery in New England, with hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic drives to explore.

  • Portsmouth: This historic and charming New Hampshire seaport offers visitors a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums, art galleries, and live music venues.

  • Boston: Just a 90-minute drive from Hillsborough, Boston is a world-class city with a rich history, cultural heritage, and culinary scene.

Conclusion

From its charming small-town atmosphere to its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Hillsborough is a destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural enrichment, or simply a quiet retreat, this New England gem is sure to delight and captivate you. With its tasty local cuisine, comfortable lodging options, and easy transportation access, Hillsborough is an ideal getaway for visitors from near and far.
