Introduction to Lebanon

Located in the Dartmouth Lake Sunapee region of New Hampshire in New England, Lebanon is a small city embedded in the heart of nature. With a population of just over 14,000, the city offers an abundance of outdoor recreational activities and an eclectic mix of cultural attractions. Lebanon’s lush surroundings and scenic views make it a perfect haven for nature lovers, while its historical sites and year-round events make it an ideal destination for all travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Lebanon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lebanon Historical Society Museum: This museum offers tours, exhibits, and events which showcase Lebanon’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Aidron Duckworth Art Museum: This museum features contemporary art and numerous exhibitions by local and international artists.
  • Saint-Gaudens National Historical Park: Visitors can enjoy guided tours of the home, studios, and gardens of American sculptor, Augustus Saint-Gaudens.

Natural Attractions of Lebanon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mascoma Lake: A pristine and peaceful lake that offers a range of water activities such as fishing and boating.
  • Lebanon Rail Trail: A hiking path that runs through town, perfect for scenic walks and bike rides.
  • Mink Brook Nature Preserve: An extensive network of trails designed for hiking and bird watching, with plenty of spots to enjoy picnicking in the woods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lebanon

Lebanon is a fantastic hiking and biking destination with well-maintained paths taking you through its natural beauty.

  • The Tuckerman Trail: This trail is just over 5 miles long and is perfect for intermediate-level hikers looking for a challenge.
  • The Blue Ribbon Trail: Another moderately challenging trail, this hike is approximately 6 miles long and provides a scenic view of the river.
  • Boston Lot Lake Loop: A beautiful 4-mile trail that offers a great opportunity to observe the natural beauty of Lebanon.

Best Things to Do in Lebanon: Activities and Tours

  • Lebanon Farmer’s Market: Visit this market featuring local produce and handmade products such as crafts and freshly made bread, which is a must-go.
  • Aidron Duckworth Grass Roots Festival: Festival-goers can enjoy a weekend of art, music, and food, while supporting local artists.
  • Northern Rail Trail: A year-round destination great for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling. The trail is 58 miles long, and it’s one of the longest ones.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lebanon

Day 1: Explore downtown Lebanon, visiting local shops and eateries.

Day 2: Visit the Lebanon Historical Society Museum followed by a nature walk at Mink Brook Nature Preserve.

Day 3: Drive to Saint-Gaudens National Historical Park and enjoy a guided tour of the museum.

Day 4: Try a hike along the Tuckerman Trail followed by dinner at The Carpenter’s Kitchen.

Day 5: Take a swim or rent a boat and enjoy the day at Mascoma Lake.

Day 6: Visit Aidron Duckworth Art Museum and enjoy its current exhibitions.

Day 7: End the trip with a bike ride along the Blue Ribbon Trail and a picnic by the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Lebanon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Basket: Fried clams served with a side of coleslaw and fries. This dish can be found at Salt Hill Pub.
  • The Hungry Trout: An award-winning seafood dish served at Three Tomatoes Trattoria.
  • Lebanon Diner’s Breakfast: Hearty breakfast featuring a variety of delicious options, including waffles, pancakes, and more.

Local Beverages

  • Polyculture Brewing Company: Their beer selection includes local brews such as Aronia Tarty Sour made with locally sourced Aronia berries.
  • Lyme Lymonade: This refreshing lemonade is locally made with natural ingredients.

Where to Stay in Lebanon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Trumbull House Bed and Breakfast: A cozy and charming B&B with beautiful views of the Connecticut River.
  • The Fireside Inn & Suites: Located in the heart of downtown Lebanon, this hotel offers great amenities and complimentary breakfast.
  • Chieftain Inn: A family-friendly place with a heated pool and easy access to outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Lebanon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hanover’s Main Street Shopping: It features a variety of shops and boutiques with unique products such as handmade wares and art.
  • Lebanon Farmer’s Market: Locally made and seasonal products such as handmade crafts, baked goods, and fresh local produce.

Local Culture and Customs of Lebanon

Lebanon has a commitment to sustainable and eco-friendly service, so it’s common for businesses to prioritize local sourcing for their products. Lebanon also is a city with a significant cultural diversity, which can be seen in events and festivals that celebrate traditions from around the world.

Best Time to Visit Lebanon

Lebanon is a year-round destination with its own charm in every season. The fall foliage from October to November is breathtaking, and the winter months from December to January bring the snowscapes to enjoy outdoor winter sports or ice fishing. Spring in Lebanon from March to May brings a rebirth of nature and flowers, and summer from June to August offer the beautiful sunny weather, perfect for swimming, boating, hiking, and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lebanon

Do:

  • Respect nature and local wildlife.
  • Visit local farmer’s markets and buy local.
  • Engage in eco-friendly activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause harm to the environment.
  • Disrespect local culture and customs.
  • Hunt or fish without the necessary permits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lebanon

Lebanon is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, it’s always recommended to take precautions while hiking and biking.

Health in Lebanon

Healthcare facilities in Lebanon are of good quality, and the area’s clean environment guarantees a healthy stay. However, always check with your doctor for any vaccinations you may need before visiting.

Getting to Lebanon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lebanon is easily accessible through different modes of transportation:

Airports Near Lebanon

  • Lebanon Municipal Airport: The closest airport, located just a 6-minute drive from downtown Lebanon.
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport: Located just under 2 hours away from Lebanon, this airport offers bigger flight options than the Lebanon airport.

Bus Stations Near Lebanon

  • Dartmouth Coach: It provides trips to and from Boston and New York City regularly.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding areas.
  • Uber and Lyft operate in Lebanon and the surrounding towns.

How to Get Around in Lebanon

  • Walking and biking are the best ways to explore the city.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lebanon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Quechee Gorge: A beautiful natural attraction with waterfalls and hiking trails located 30 minutes away from Lebanon.
  • White Mountains: A stunning mountain range located about an hour away from Lebanon, offering scenic drives and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Lebanon, New Hampshire, is a hidden gem that provides a unique and unforgettable travel experience. With its beautiful natural surroundings, cultural attractions, and exciting outdoor activities, Lebanon is a must-visit destination for all types of travelers.
