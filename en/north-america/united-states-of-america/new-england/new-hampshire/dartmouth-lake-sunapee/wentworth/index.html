Introduction to Wentworth

Nestled in the heart of Dartmouth Lake Sunapee, Wentworth is a hidden gem of New Hampshire. This picturesque town is known for its abundance of outdoor activities, stunning natural scenery, and rich history. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a history buff, Wentworth offers something for every type of traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything Wentworth has to offer, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Wentworth

Wentworth is home to several culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here’s a list of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wentworth Historical Society and Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s rich history.
  • The Frost Place: Literary fans will appreciate the chance to visit the former home of renowned poet Robert Frost.
  • The Congregational Church: This beautiful, historic church dates back to the early 1800s and is a must-see landmark in Wentworth.
  • The Old Mill Museum: This museum celebrates the town’s industrial heritage, with exhibits on textile mills, farming, and logging.

Natural Attractions of Wentworth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Moosilauke: Hiking enthusiasts will love the challenge of this imposing mountain, which features stunning panoramic views at the summit.
  • Baker Tower: This historic tower is a popular spot for photos and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Rumney Rocks: This natural wonder is a favorite among rock climbers but is also popular for hiking and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wentworth

Wentworth is known for its abundance of hiking trails and biking routes. Here are a few top options:

  • Baker River Trail: This 3.3-mile trail offers scenic views of the river and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or hike.
  • Mount Tecumseh Trail: This 5.1-mile trail is a challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning views at the summit.
  • Asquam Ridge Trail: This 6.6-mile trail is a popular choice for experienced hikers and is known for its stunning views and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Wentworth: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Wentworth offers several other activities and tours to satisfy every type of traveler. Here are some top picks:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take a peaceful paddle along the Baker River or explore the tranquil waters of Stinson Lake.
  • Fishing: With several lakes and rivers in the area, fishing enthusiasts will have plenty of options to choose from.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: During the winter months, head to the nearby Waterville Valley Resort for some of New Hampshire’s best skiing and snowboarding.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wentworth

For those planning an extended stay in Wentworth, here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

Day 1 Explore the town center, including a visit to the Wentworth Historical Society and Museum and the Congregational Church.
Day 2 Hike the Mount Moosilauke Trail for breathtaking views.
Day 3 Go kayaking or canoeing along the Baker River or Stinson Lake.
Day 4 Take a day trip to nearby Franconia Notch State Park for more hiking and outdoor adventures.
Day 5 Visit The Frost Place and take a tour of the former home of Robert Frost.
Day 6 Go rock climbing at Rumney Rocks or take a scenic drive along the Kancamagus Highway.
Day 7 Ski or snowboard at Waterville Valley Resort during the winter months, or take a leisurely stroll along the Baker River Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Wentworth

Despite its small size, Wentworth has a thriving food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Glazed Pork Tenderloin: This savory dish is a popular choice at many local restaurants and features tender, flavorful meat glazed with sweet maple syrup.
  • Wild Blueberry Pancakes: Made with fresh, locally sourced blueberries, these pancakes are a delicious way to start your day.
  • New England Clam Chowder: This classic seafood dish is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • New Hampshire Craft Beer: Wentworth is home to several craft breweries, including Schilling Beer Co. and Reklis Brewing. Be sure to try some of their unique, locally sourced brews.
  • Maple Syrup: New Hampshire is famous for its maple syrup, and there’s no better place to try it than in Wentworth.
  • Apple Cider: During the fall months, be sure to grab a glass of fresh-pressed apple cider from a local farm.

Where to Stay in Wentworth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wentworth offers several lodging options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few top picks:

  • Wentworth Inn: This historic inn dates back to the 1700s and offers charming, antique-filled rooms and suites.
  • Mountain Fare Inn: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in nearby Campton and offers easy access to hiking trails and other outdoor activities.
  • Camping: For a more rustic experience, consider camping at one of the several campgrounds in the area.

What to Buy in Wentworth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Wentworth, be sure to bring home some local souvenirs and products. Here are some top picks:

  • Maple Syrup: Again, you can’t go wrong with New Hampshire’s famous maple syrup.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Several local artisans create beautiful, one-of-a-kind pieces of jewelry using materials like glass, wood, and metal.
  • Local Honey: Many of the area’s farms produce delicious, locally sourced honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Wentworth

Wentworth is known for its friendly, welcoming residents and relaxed way of life. Visitors should embrace the town’s laid-back, rural character and be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Wentworth

The best time to visit Wentworth depends on your personal preferences. For outdoor enthusiasts, the summer months offer warm weather and ample opportunities to hike, bike, and enjoy the natural beauty of the area. Meanwhile, winter months are perfect for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages

Safety and Health

Visitors to Wentworth should take appropriate precautions to ensure their safety and health while in the area.

Safety in Wentworth

  • Be aware of your surroundings and use caution while hiking in remote areas
  • Practice safe driving habits, especially on rural roads
  • Be mindful of wildlife, including bears and moose

Health in Wentworth

  • Stay hydrated, especially during outdoor activities in the summer months
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors
  • Consult with a healthcare professional if you have any concerns about health risks in the area

Getting to Wentworth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wentworth is located about 2.5 hours from Boston and 1.5 hours from Manchester. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Wentworth

  • Lebanon Municipal Airport (LEB)
  • Manchester-Boston Regional Airport (MHT)

Bus Stations Near Wentworth

  • Concord Coach Lines
  • Dartmouth Coach

Other Options

  • Renting a car is recommended for those who want to explore the nearby attractions and scenic routes.
  • Uber and Lyft are available in the area, but availability may be limited.

Where to Go Next from Wentworth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Wentworth may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of activities and attractions for travelers of all ages. From hiking and biking to cultural landmarks and delicious food, there’s something for every type of traveler in this charming New Hampshire gem.
