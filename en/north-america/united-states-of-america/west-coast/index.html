Introduction to West Coast

The West Coast region of the United States is a diverse and breathtaking destination that encompasses California, Oregon, and Washington. From the beautiful coastlines to the majestic mountains, the region is filled with natural wonders, cultural attractions, and exciting adventures. Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, the West Coast offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in West Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Getty Center: Located in Los Angeles, California, this museum is famous for its impressive collection of art, architecture, and gardens.
  • Golden Gate Park: This park in San Francisco, California is home to several museums, including the de Young Museum and the California Academy of Sciences.
  • Museum of Pop Culture: Also known as MoPOP, this museum in Seattle, Washington is dedicated to contemporary popular culture.
  • The Getty Villa: This museum in Malibu, California features ancient art and architecture from the Mediterranean.

Natural Attractions of West Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yosemite National Park: This park in California is famous for its granite cliffs, waterfalls, and beautiful meadows.
  • Crater Lake National Park: Located in Oregon, this park features the deepest lake in the United States and stunning views.
  • Olympic National Park: This park in Washington is home to beautiful rainforests, beaches, and mountains.
  • Big Sur: This coastal drive in California offers some of the most breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Coast

  • Pacific Crest Trail: This 2,650-mile trail runs from Mexico to Canada and offers stunning mountain views.
  • Mount Tamalpais: This peak in Marin County, California, offers several hiking trails with breathtaking views of the San Francisco Bay.
  • McKenzie River Trail: This 26.5-mile trail in Oregon features stunning waterfalls and natural hot springs.
  • Burke-Gilman Trail: This 27-mile trail in Seattle, Washington, takes visitors through neighborhoods, parks, and Lake Washington.

Best Things to Do in West Coast: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Alcatraz Island: This former prison in San Francisco, California, is now a National Park and offers tours.
  • Go Whale Watching: Several locations along the West Coast offer the chance to see whales in their natural habitat.
  • Take a Wine tasting tour: California’s wine country offers several wine tasting tours.
  • Surfing lessons: Beaches up and down the coast offer surfing lessons for all levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Coast

Food and Drink Scene of West Coast

Must-Try Dishes

  • In-N-Out Burger: A California classic, In-N-Out Burger offers delicious burgers and shakes.
  • Oregon Pinot Noir: Oregon’s wine country offers some of the best Pinot Noir in the world.
  • Dungeness Crab: This delicious crab is abundant along the West Coast and can be found in many seafood restaurants.
  • Cioppino: This San Francisco classic is a seafood stew that originated in Italian-American neighborhoods.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The West Coast is famous for its craft beer scene, with breweries in every city.
  • Coffee: Seattle’s coffee scene is famous around the world, with many local roasters to choose from.
  • Californian Wine: California’s wine country offers wine tasting tours and some of the best wines in the world.
  • Apple Cider: The Pacific Northwest is famous for its apple cider, with many orchards offering tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in West Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ace Hotel: Located in downtown Los Angeles, this trendy hotel offers unique rooms and a rooftop bar.
  • Timberline Lodge: This historic hotel in Mount Hood, Oregon offers beautiful views and hiking trails.
  • Space Needle Hotel: Located in Seattle, Washington, this hotel offers stunning views of the iconic Space Needle.
  • The Clift Royal Sonesta Hotel: Located in San Francisco, California, this hotel is famous for its unique decor and luxurious amenities.

What to Buy in West Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Salt Water Taffy: A classic candy found in many coastal towns.
  • Craft Beer: Many breweries along the West Coast offer cans and growlers to take home.
  • Coffee Beans: Bring home some of Seattle’s famous coffee beans to enjoy at home.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Many artists along the coast offer unique handcrafted jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of West Coast

The West Coast is known for its laid-back, inclusive culture. Visitors should be prepared for a more liberal and open-minded approach to lifestyle and politics. Tipping is customary throughout the region, with the standard being around 20%.

Best Time to Visit West Coast

The best time to visit the West Coast is between May and October when the weather is mild, and the days are longer. However, visitors can enjoy beautiful views and outdoor activities year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Coast

Do:

  • Bring a reusable water bottle to reduce waste.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the natural wonders of the region.
  • Try the local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car when parked.
  • Ignore warning signs at the beach or in national parks.
  • Use plastic bags or straws, which are banned in several cities in the region.
  • Assume that all cities have the same vibe or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Coast

The West Coast is generally safe for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions in crowded areas or at night. It’s always a good idea to lock your car doors and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in West Coast

Visitors should drink plenty of water and use sunscreen when out in the sun. The region is also known for its air quality issues, especially during wildfires, so visitors with respiratory problems may want to check air quality reports before planning outdoor activities.

How to Get Around in West Coast

The best way to get around the West Coast is by car, with several rental companies to choose from. Many cities also offer public transportation, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from West Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Las Vegas, Nevada: This famous city is just a short flight or road trip away from California.
  • Vancouver, Canada: Board a ferry or take a train to visit this vibrant Canadian city.
  • Baja California, Mexico: Take a road trip south for beautiful beaches and delicious food.

Conclusion

The West Coast is a must-see destination that offers natural wonders, cultural attractions, and exciting adventures for visitors of all ages. From the coastlines to the mountains, there is always something new to explore and discover. Come and experience the unique culture and beauty of the West Coast for yourself.
