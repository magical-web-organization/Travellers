Food and Drink Scene of Ponte Vedra Beach

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Florida Stone Crab Claws - This dish features tender and succulent crab claws that are served with mustard sauce.

  2. Fried Shrimp - Ponte Vedra Beach is famous for its shrimp, and this dish features freshly caught and lightly fried shrimp.

  3. Gumbo - Gumbo is a Creole stew made with seafood, chicken, and sausage, and is a staple dish in the area.

Local Beverages

  1. Key Lime Pie Martini - This delicious cocktail is made with a mix of vanilla vodka, key lime juice, and whipped cream.

  2. Florida Orange Juice - Florida is known for its fresh oranges, and visitors can sample the state’s renowned orange juice during their visit.

Where to Stay in Ponte Vedra Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Ponte Vedra Inn and Club - This luxurious resort is located on the beach and features several amenities, including a spa, golf course, and several dining options.

  2. The Lodge and Club - This 66-room boutique hotel offers stunning views of the ocean and is known for its fine dining experience.

  3. Four Points by Sheraton Jacksonville Beachfront - Visitors looking for a budget-friendly option can stay at this hotel, which is located on the beach and features several amenities.

What to Buy in Ponte Vedra Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Seashells - Visitors can bring home a piece of Ponte Vedra Beach by collecting seashells on the shore. Uniquely-shaped shells, such as conch shells and sand dollars, make excellent souvenirs.

  2. Local Artwork - Visitors can find several art galleries in and around Ponte Vedra Beach that showcase local artists’ work.

  3. Sweet Treats - Visitors can bring home delicious treats, such as fudge and saltwater taffy, from several sweet shops located in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Ponte Vedra Beach

Ponte Vedra Beach is known for its laid-back lifestyle, and locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Visitors should respect local customs, such as dress codes in certain areas, and follow beach safety rules, such as staying off the dunes.

Best Time to Visit Ponte Vedra Beach

The best time to visit Ponte Vedra Beach is between March and May when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. The months between June and August are busy, and temperatures can be high.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ponte Vedra Beach

Do:

  • Sunbathe on the beaches

  • Explore the Guana River State Park

  • Shop at local markets and stores

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife

  • Litter on the beaches

  • Trespass on private property

Safety and Health

Safety in Ponte Vedra Beach

Ponte Vedra Beach is generally a safe place for travelers, but like any other area, visitors should take precautions, such as locking their car doors, and storing valuables in a safe place.

Health in Ponte Vedra Beach

Ponte Vedra Beach has several medical facilities and hospitals that can provide health care to visitors if needed.

Getting to Ponte Vedra Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ponte Vedra Beach

The closest airport to Ponte Vedra Beach is the Jacksonville International Airport, located 40 minutes away. Visitors can take a taxi, shuttle, or rent a car to travel to Ponte Vedra Beach.

Bus Stations Near Ponte Vedra Beach

Visitors can take a bus to Jacksonville, which is located about 30 minutes away from Ponte Vedra Beach. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach their destination.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Ponte Vedra Beach from nearby cities, such as St. Augustine and Daytona Beach.

How to Get Around in Ponte Vedra Beach

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to explore the area. Biking is also a popular option for visitors who want to explore the area at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Ponte Vedra Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. St. Augustine - The oldest city in the United States is just a few miles south of Ponte Vedra Beach and features several historical landmarks and museums.

  2. Daytona Beach - Located about 1.5 hours away from Ponte Vedra Beach, Daytona Beach is known for its sand dunes and motor racing events, including the Daytona 500.

Conclusion

Ponte Vedra Beach is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a blend of adventure, natural beauty, and culture. With its stunning beaches, unique wildlife, and several outdoor activities, Ponte Vedra Beach offers something for everyone. Visitors can indulge in the local fare, explore the local art scene, or simply soak up the sun on the beach. Whatever your interests may be, Ponte Vedra Beach promises to be an unforgettable experience.
