Introduction to Forgotten Coast

Located in Florida Panhandle, Forgotten Coast is one of the hidden gems of Florida that offers stunning natural scenery, charming small towns, and rich history. With miles of secluded beaches, towering sand dunes, lush pine forests, and crystal-clear springs, the region has everything that nature lovers could ask for. From charming historic sites and fascinating museums to outdoor adventures and delicious seafood, there’s something for everyone in Forgotten Coast.

List of Places to Visit in Forgotten Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Camp Gordon Johnston Museum

Located in Carrabelle, the Camp Gordon Johnston Museum is a must-visit destination for history buffs. The museum celebrates the legacy of the soldiers who trained at Camp Gordon Johnston during World War II and explores the history of the region. Visitors can see authentic military vehicles, weapons, and equipment and learn about the daily life of soldiers during wartime.

John Gorrie Museum State Park

Named after the inventor of modern air conditioning, the John Gorrie Museum State Park is located in Apalachicola and showcases the fascinating history of the region. Visitors can learn about Dr. Gorrie’s inventions and how they impacted the region’s development. The museum is housed in the doctor’s former home, which has been restored to its original 1800s appearance.

Natural Attractions of Forgotten Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St. George Island State Park

St. George Island State Park is one of the most beautiful places in Forgotten Coast, featuring miles of sugar-white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and towering sand dunes. The park offers opportunities for swimming, sunbathing, fishing, kayaking, and wildlife viewing.

Tate’s Hell State Forest

Tate’s Hell State Forest is a vast wilderness area that covers over 200,000 acres of swamps, pine forests, and marshes. The forest offers miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails, as well as excellent fishing and hunting opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forgotten Coast

Apalachicola National Forest

The Apalachicola National Forest is the largest forest in Florida and features some of the most picturesque hiking and biking trails in the state. With over 570,000 acres of public land, the forest offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Visitors can explore scenic trails that wind through dense pine forests, freshwater swamps, and pristine rivers.

Best Things to Do in Forgotten Coast: Activities and Tours

Apalachicola River Cruises

The Apalachicola River is one of the most scenic waterways in Florida and offers visitors a chance to explore the region’s unique ecological landscape. River cruises are a popular way to experience the beauty of the river and learn about the region’s history. Visitors can choose from a variety of tour options, including sunset cruises, eco tours, and historic tours.

Fishing Charters

Forgotten Coast is one of the best fishing destinations in the country, with a rich variety of fish species that can be caught year-round. Fishing charters are available for both saltwater and freshwater fishing, and visitors can choose from guided tours that cater to all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Forgotten Coast

Must-Try Dishes

Florida Keys Pink Shrimp

The Florida Keys Pink Shrimp is a local delicacy that can be found in many of the region’s seafood restaurants. These sweet and succulent shrimp are usually boiled or grilled and served with a variety of dipping sauces.

Local Beverages

Apalachicola Bay Oysters

Apalachicola Bay Oysters are considered some of the best oysters in the world, and they are a staple of the local cuisine. Visitors can taste the oysters at many of the region’s seafood restaurants.

Where to Stay in Forgotten Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Apalachicola Bay Inn

Located in Apalachicola, the Apalachicola Bay Inn offers comfortable and affordable accommodations for visitors. The inn is conveniently located near many of the region’s top attractions, including the John Gorrie Museum State Park and the Apalachicola River.

What to Buy in Forgotten Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Forgotten Coast can bring home a variety of local products as souvenirs, including handmade pottery, locally sourced honey, and sea salt harvested from the Gulf of Mexico.

Local Culture and Customs of Forgotten Coast

Forgotten Coast is known for its friendly and welcoming culture, where locals take pride in their natural resources and local traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the natural environment and follow local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Forgotten Coast

The best time to visit Forgotten Coast is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and crowds are fewer. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities without the heat and humidity of summer and avoid the winter crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forgotten Coast

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Take the time to learn about the region’s history and culture.
  • Sample the local cuisine and seafood.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash behind on the beach or in the wilderness.
  • Disturb or feed wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forgotten Coast

Forgotten Coast is generally a safe destination for visitors, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight. Visitors should also be aware of the dangers of swimming in open waters and take precautions to avoid rip currents.

Health in Forgotten Coast

Visitors to Forgotten Coast should take basic health precautions, such as staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen, and avoiding insect bites. Visitors should also take precautions against waterborne illnesses when swimming in open waters, including freshwater springs and streams.

Getting to Forgotten Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Forgotten Coast

The nearest airports to Forgotten Coast are Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport in Panama City and Tallahassee International Airport in Tallahassee.

Bus Stations Near Forgotten Coast

The nearest bus stations to Forgotten Coast are Greyhound Bus Lines in Tallahassee and Bay Line Railroad in Panama City.

Other Options

Renting a car is one of the easiest and most convenient ways to get around Forgotten Coast, as the region is spread out and many of the top attractions are located outside of town.

How to Get Around in Forgotten Coast

The best way to get around Forgotten Coast is by car. Many of the top attractions are spread out and can be difficult to reach without a vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Forgotten Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Forgotten Coast is located in the heart of Florida Panhandle, making it a great base for exploring the other nearby destinations. Visitors can take day trips to nearby destinations like Tallahassee, Panama City, and Destin.

Conclusion

Forgotten Coast is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique combination of natural beauty, history, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With miles of secluded beaches, charming small towns, and fascinating museums, the region has everything that travelers could ask for.
