Introduction to Port St. Joe

Port St. Joe is a small coastal town located in Gulf County, Florida. Founded in 1835, the town has a rich history and unique culture that sets it apart from other beach destinations in Florida. Port St. Joe is known for its beautiful white sand beaches and blue-green waters, which provide the perfect setting for swimming, kayaking, and fishing.

List of Places to Visit in Port St. Joe

No matter what your interests may be, there’s something for everyone in Port St. Joe. Here are some of the top places to visit in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Constitution Convention Museum State Park: This park commemorates the site of Florida’s first constitutional convention in 1838 and has a museum with exhibits about the town’s history.
  • Port St. Joe Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases artifacts and photographs that tell the story of the town’s past.
  • St. Joseph Peninsula State Park: This park is home to the remains of the town of St. Joseph, which was once one of the busiest ports in Florida before a yellow fever epidemic wiped out the population in 1841.

Natural Attractions of Port St. Joe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • T.H. Stone Memorial St. Joseph Peninsula State Park: This park has miles of white sand beaches, dunes, and pine forests that provide a habitat for a variety of wildlife. Visitors can swim, fish, and kayak in the clear waters of the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Cape San Blas Lighthouse: This lighthouse is located on the eastern end of St. Joseph Peninsula and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Apalachicola National Forest: This national forest has over 500,000 acres of wilderness, including hiking and biking trails, fishing streams, and wildlife viewing areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port St. Joe

Port St. Joe has plenty of hiking and biking opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • St. Joseph Peninsula State Park Trails: This park has several trails that wind through forests and along the coast, including the 10-mile St. Joseph Peninsula Trail and the 3.5-mile Coastline Trail.
  • T.H. Stone Memorial St. Joseph Peninsula State Park Trails: This park has excellent hiking trails, including the 8-mile Cape San Blas Trail and the 5.2-mile Wilderness Preserve Trail.
  • Apalachicola National Forest Trails: This national forest has over 50 miles of hiking and biking trails that lead through pine forests, wetlands, and prairies.

Best Things to Do in Port St. Joe: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Port St. Joe has plenty of activities to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Fishing: Port St. Joe is one of the best fishing destinations in Florida, with lots of options for both freshwater and saltwater fishing. You can fish for redfish, trout, and tarpon in the St. Joe Bay or head out to the Gulf of Mexico for deep-sea fishing.
  • Kayaking: The calm waters of the St. Joe Bay are perfect for kayaking, and there are plenty of rental options in town. You can paddle through seagrass beds and mangrove islands, spotting dolphins and sea turtles along the way.
  • Golfing: Port St. Joe is home to several excellent golf courses, including the St. Joseph Bay Golf Club and the Port St. Joe Country Club.

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for your Port St. Joe vacation:

Food and Drink Scene of Port St. Joe

Port St. Joe has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of options for seafood lovers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters: Port St. Joe is famous for its fresh Apalachicola oysters, which are served up at local seafood restaurants like Indian Pass Raw Bar and Provisions.
  • Grouper Sandwich: Grouper is a local fish that’s often served fried in a sandwich with lettuce, tomato, and tartar sauce.
  • Blue Crab: Blue crabs are caught in the waters around Port St. Joe and are used in a variety of dishes, like crab cakes and crab dip.

Local Beverages

  • Saltwater Cowboy Beer: This beer is brewed by Oyster City Brewing Company in Apalachicola and is named after the local oystermen who work the waters around Port St. Joe.
  • Bloody Marys: Bloody Marys are a popular drink in Port St. Joe, and many bars and restaurants have their own unique recipe.

Where to Stay in Port St. Joe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Port St. Joe has plenty of options for accommodations, from beachfront hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • The Port Inn: This recently renovated hotel is located in the heart of Port St. Joe and offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.
  • Cape San Blas Inn: This bed and breakfast is located on the eastern end of the St. Joseph Peninsula and offers stunning views of the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Pelican’s Nest: This beachfront vacation home rental is perfect for families or groups and has plenty of space for everyone.

What to Buy in Port St. Joe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a souvenir or gift to bring home from your Port St. Joe vacation, here are some local products to consider:

  • Seafood: Fresh seafood is available in Port St. Joe, and you can buy local shrimp, oysters, and blue crab to take home and cook.
  • Artwork: Port St. Joe has a thriving arts community, and there are several galleries in town that sell local art, pottery, and jewelry.
  • Saltwater Taffy: This candy is a classic souvenir from beach towns, and you can find it at many of the local candy shops in Port St. Joe.

Local Culture and Customs of Port St. Joe

Port St. Joe is known for its friendly, laid-back culture, and visitors are often struck by the warmth and hospitality of the locals. As a small town, Port St. Joe has a tight-knit community, and visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Port St. Joe

The best time to visit Port St. Joe is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be chilly, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port St. Joe

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Port St. Joe:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife. Port St. Joe is home to a variety of endangered species, including sea turtles and shorebirds, so be sure to follow any posted guidelines for protecting them.
  • Explore the town’s history and culture. Port St. Joe has a fascinating past, and there are several historical landmarks and museums in the area that provide insight into its history.
  • Sample the local seafood. Port St. Joe is known for its fresh seafood, and it’s a must-try for any visitor.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind. Be sure to take all of your trash with you when you leave the beach or park.
  • Disturb the sea turtle nests. Sea turtles nest on the beaches around Port St. Joe, and it’s important to avoid disturbing their nests or hatchlings.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your Port St. Joe vacation:

Safety in Port St. Joe

  • Be aware of the weather. Thunderstorms can pop up quickly in the summer, so keep an eye on the forecast and take shelter if necessary.
  • Obey beach safety rules. The Gulf of Mexico can be dangerous, especially for inexperienced swimmers, so be sure to follow any posted beach safety guidelines.

Health in Port St. Joe

  • Wear sunscreen. The Florida sun can be intense, so be sure to wear sunscreen and protect your skin.
  • Stay hydrated. It can be easy to get dehydrated in the heat, so be sure to drink plenty of water throughout the day.

Getting to Port St. Joe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Port St. Joe is located approximately 100 miles southeast of Pensacola, Florida, and 75 miles southwest of Tallahassee, Florida. Here are some transportation options for getting to Port St. Joe:

Airports Near Port St. Joe

  • Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport: This airport is located in Panama City Beach, Florida, and is approximately 50 miles from Port St. Joe.
  • Tallahassee International Airport: This airport is located in Tallahassee, Florida, and is approximately 75 miles from Port St. Joe.

Other Options

  • Driving: Port St. Joe is easily accessible by car, and there are several major highways that pass through the area.
  • Bus: Greyhound operates a bus station in Panama City, Florida, which is approximately 40 miles from Port St. Joe.

How to Get Around in Port St. Joe

The best way to get around Port St. Joe is by car. There are several rental car options available at the airports or in town.

Where to Go Next from Port St. Joe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more to do after your Port St. Joe vacation, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:
