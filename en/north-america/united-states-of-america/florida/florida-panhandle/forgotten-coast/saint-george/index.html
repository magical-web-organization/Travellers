Introduction to Saint George

Located in the Forgotten Coast of Florida Panhandle, Saint George Island is a perfect getaway for beach and nature lovers. The island has remained largely untouched by commercial development and offers a tranquil retreat for those who wish to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. The island’s pristine beaches, crystal clear waters, and breathtaking sunsets make it a popular destination for tourists in search of a peaceful vacation. Here’s a comprehensive guide to the best places to visit and things to do in Saint George Island.

List of Places to Visit in Saint George

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. George Island State Park: Spread over 2,023 acres, this park offers a wide range of recreational activities such as swimming, hiking, and biking. It is also a popular spot for wildlife watching.
  • Orman House Museum and Gardens: The Orman House is a beautifully restored 19th-century mansion that offers a glimpse into the lives of the island’s early settlers.
  • Cape St. George Light: Originally built in 1852, this lighthouse was destroyed by a hurricane in 2005 and rebuilt in 2008.

Natural Attractions of Saint George: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dr. Julian G. Bruce St. George Island State Park: This park covers nine miles of sandy white beaches, crystal clear waters, and pristine sand dunes. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities such as fishing, camping, hiking, and swimming.
  • Apalachicola National Estuarine Research Reserve: Covering over 246,000 acres of wetlands, this reserve offers visitors an opportunity to experience the natural beauty of the region up-close. The reserve offers a variety of tours and educational programs.
  • St. George Island Fishing Pier: The pier is a popular spot for fishing and offers stunning views of the Gulf of Mexico.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint George

  • St. George Island State Park Nature Trail: This trail is a three-mile round trip journey through the park’s coastal woods and sand dunes.
  • Apalachicola National Estuarine Research Reserve Nature Trail: This 1.1-mile trail meanders through the reserve’s beautiful wetlands and provides an opportunity to spot a variety of wildlife such as alligators, turtles, and birds.

Best Things to Do in Saint George: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Visitors can explore the island’s beautiful waterways on a guided kayak tour.
  • Charter Fishing: The Gulf of Mexico offers some of the best fishing opportunities in the world, and St. George Island is no exception.
  • Beachcombing: The island’s pristine beaches offer an excellent opportunity for shell collecting.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint George

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Platter: A platter of the freshest catch of the day, including shrimp, oysters, and fish, served with a side of coleslaw and hush puppies.
  • Gumbo: A local specialty, gumbo is a hearty stew made with a variety of seafood, vegetables, and spices.
  • Key Lime Pie: A sweet and tangy pie made with Florida’s famous key limes.

Local Beverages

  • Gulf Coast Brew: A locally brewed beer that can be found at most bars and restaurants on the island.
  • Fresh-squeezed Orange Juice: Florida’s orange industry produces some of the best orange juice in the world.

Where to Stay in Saint George: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • St. George Island State Park campgrounds: The park offers primitive camping options with beautiful views of the Gulf of Mexico.
  • The Buccaneer Inn: This historical hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the island’s beaches and attractions.
  • The Plantation on St. George Island: This luxury resort offers guests a variety of amenities, including an outdoor pool, spa, and restaurant.

What to Buy in Saint George: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Seashells: The island’s pristine beaches offer an excellent opportunity for shell collecting.
  • Locally made souvenirs: Visitors can purchase a variety of locally made souvenirs such as pottery, jewelry, and art.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint George

The people of Saint George take pride in preserving the island’s natural beauty and promoting environmental conservation. Visitors are expected to respect the island’s flora and fauna and refrain from littering or damaging the environment in any way.

Best Time to Visit Saint George

The best time to visit Saint George is during the shoulder season of spring and fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint George

Do:

  • Respect the environment and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Explore the island’s natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Take a guided tour for a more immersive experience.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore the weather warnings

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint George

Saint George Island is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Ensure that vehicles are locked, and valuables are kept out of sight.

Health in Saint George

Saint George offers a variety of healthcare facilities, including clinics and hospitals.

Getting to Saint George: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint George

  • Tallahassee International Airport: Located about 80 miles from St. George.
  • Panama City Beach International Airport: Located about 70 miles from St. George.

Bus Stations Near Saint George

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Tallahassee: Located about 75 miles from St. George.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car or taking a taxi to get to Saint George.

How to Get Around in Saint George

The island is best explored on foot, but visitors can also rent bikes or golf carts to get around. There are also a few taxi services available on the island.

Where to Go Next from Saint George: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Apalachicola: A charming town with a rich history and a thriving arts scene, located just across the bridge from Saint George.
  • Tallahassee: The state capital of Florida, located about 80 miles from Saint George.

Conclusion

Saint George Island is a hidden gem in the Florida Panhandle that offers visitors a perfect blend of nature, adventure, and relaxation. This comprehensive guide will help you plan a memorable trip to this charming destination.
