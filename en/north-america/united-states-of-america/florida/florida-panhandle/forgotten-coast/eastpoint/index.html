Introduction to Eastpoint

Nestled in the heart of Forgotten Coast, in Florida Panhandle, Eastpoint is a small town that offers a peaceful escape from the bustling city life. It is a hidden gem that boasts of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage.

The town of Eastpoint is located between Carrabelle and Apalachicola, two popular tourist destinations in Florida. Eastpoint is the perfect place to explore the beautiful beaches, taste fresh seafood, and indulge in outdoor adventures.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, top-rated activities and tours, best hiking and biking routes, and where to stay and eat in Eastpoint.

List of Places to Visit in Eastpoint

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Apalachicola Maritime Museum: This museum offers an immersive experience into the rich maritime history of Apalachicola Bay.
  • John Gorrie State Museum: Discover the history of modern refrigeration and air conditioning at this science museum.
  • Orman House: This historic house museum dates back to the 1800s and showcases the life and legacy of the prominent Orman family.

Natural Attractions of Eastpoint: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tate’s Hell State Forest: Home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, Tate’s Hell State Forest offers various outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • St. George Island State Park: With its sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and nature trails, St. George Island State Park is a paradise for beach lovers and adventure seekers alike.
  • Apalachicola National Estuarine Research Reserve: This reserve offers the opportunity to explore the ecology of the Apalachicola Bay system with guided tours and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastpoint

  • St. George Island Loop Trail: Explore the beauty of St. George Island State Park by hiking through the 2.5-mile Loop Trail.
  • Tate’s Hell State Forest Trail: This mountain biking trail is perfect for adrenaline junkies who love a good challenge.
  • Bald Point State Park Trail: With its observation tower and scenic views of Ochlockonee Bay, this hiking trail offers a perfect destination for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Eastpoint: Activities and Tours

  • Dolphin and Wildlife Adventure: Take a guided tour on an airboat to witness the beauty of dolphins, sea turtles, and migratory birds in their natural habitat.
  • Fishing Charters: Eastpoint is known for its fresh and locally sourced seafood, and fishing charters offer the opportunity to catch your own delicious seafood.
  • Kayak and Paddleboard Rentals: Paddle through the calm waters of Apalachicola Bay and explore the hidden coves and inlets.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastpoint

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters: Eastpoint is known for its fresh and delicious oysters, served raw, baked, or fried.
  • Shrimp Po’ Boy: This classic Southern sandwich is a must-try, featuring crispy fried shrimp and a light dressing.
  • Gumbo: This hearty stew features shrimp, sausage, and rice and is a staple of Gulf Coast cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A refreshing Southern specialty, sweet tea is a must-try beverage in Eastpoint.
  • Key Lime Margarita: Made with fresh lime juice and local ingredients, this cocktail is a perfect way to cool off on a hot day.
  • Apalachicola Bay Bloody Mary: This spicy and savory cocktail features fresh seafood and is a perfect drink to start your day.

Where to Stay in Eastpoint: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gibson Inn: This historic inn offers Victorian-style rooms and suites and is centrally located in the heart of Apalachicola.
  • Water Street Hotel: This waterfront hotel features luxurious suites and private balconies with stunning views of the Apalachicola River.
  • The Old Carrabelle Hotel: This charming bed and breakfast is situated in a historic building and offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Eastpoint: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oyster Shell Crafts: Handmade oyster shell crafts make for a unique and memorable souvenir.
  • Apalachicola Seafood: Take home the freshest locally sourced seafood such as oysters, shrimp, and scallops, which are readily available in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastpoint

Eastpoint is a small town that takes pride in its deep-rooted cultural and traditional heritage. The people of Eastpoint are known for their hospitality, warmth, and friendliness, so make sure to greet them with a smile and engage in conversations to learn more about their traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Eastpoint

The best time to visit Eastpoint is between September and May when the weather is cool and comfortable with average temperatures ranging from 60-80 degrees Fahrenheit. If you’re visiting Eastpoint during the summer, make sure to pack light clothing and sunscreen, as temperatures can soar up to 90 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastpoint

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural attractions and indulge in outdoor activities.
  • Try the fresh seafood.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches or natural attractions.
  • Offend the locals with rude or disrespectful behavior.
  • Ignore the natural beauty and environment of Eastpoint.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastpoint

Eastpoint is a small town with low crime rates, so travelers do not need to be excessively worried about their safety. However, make sure to take necessary precautions and remain vigilant.

Health in Eastpoint

Make sure to pack mosquito repellent and sunscreen to protect from the sun and insects. If you’re visiting during the hot summer months, make sure to stay hydrated and take necessary precautions to avoid heatstroke.

Getting to Eastpoint: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastpoint

  • Tallahassee International Airport: Located 63 miles from Eastpoint, Tallahassee International Airport is the closest airport and is served by various airlines.
  • Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport: Located 78 miles from Eastpoint, this airport serves various domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Eastpoint

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Tallahassee, 63 miles from Eastpoint.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option to get around Eastpoint comfortably and explore its natural attractions and beautiful surroundings.

How to Get Around in Eastpoint

The best way to get around Eastpoint is by renting a car as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can also rent bikes or scooters to explore the town at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Eastpoint: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Carrabelle: Located just a few miles west of Eastpoint, Carrabelle is a quaint town that offers fishing, boating, and hiking opportunities.
  • Apalachicola: A historic town located just 10 miles from Eastpoint, Apalachicola is known for its seafood and charming Southern architecture.
  • Tallahassee: The capital of Florida, Tallahassee is located 63 miles from Eastpoint and offers various cultural attractions and historical landmarks.
