Introduction to Saint Marks

Located in the Forgotten Coast, in the Florida Panhandle, Saint Marks is a quaint town nestled on the banks of the St. Marks River. Known for its rich history, natural beauty, and outdoor activities, Saint Marks combines culture, adventure, and relaxation, making it the perfect destination for travelers looking to experience the charm of Old Florida.

List of Places to Visit in Saint Marks

Whether you are a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Saint Marks offers a variety of attractions, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge: This 70,000-acre refuge is home to countless species of birds, mammals, and reptiles. Visitors can witness the beauty of nature through hiking, biking, kayaking, and world-class fishing opportunities.
  • St. Marks Lighthouse: Built in 1831, this lighthouse is an iconic symbol of Saint Marks history. Climb to the top for unparalleled views of the surrounding area.
  • San Marcos de Apalache Historic State Park: This park preserves the history of Saint Marks with exhibits and artifacts from the 16th century Spanish settlement, early Native American cultures, the Civil War, and the Civil Rights Movement.

Natural Attractions of Saint Marks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wakulla Springs State Park: This natural wonder features the world’s largest and deepest freshwater spring, surrounded by lush sub-tropical forests. Boat tours offer close encounters with manatees and other wildlife.
  • Saint Marks River Preserve State Park: Home to towering cypress trees and a variety of wildlife, this park offers hiking trails, fishing, and kayaking opportunities.
  • Bald Point State Park: Miles of unspoiled beaches provide a peaceful retreat for relaxation and wildlife observation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint Marks

Saint Marks is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, thanks to a variety of hiking and biking trails, including:

  • The Florida National Scenic Trail: stretches from the Gulf Islands National Seashore near Pensacola, to Big Cypress National Preserve near Miami.
  • Tallahassee-St. Marks Historic Railroad State Trail: This 20.5-mile trail follows the former railbed of the Tallahassee-St. Marks Railroad, and offers opportunities for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Saint Marks: Activities and Tours

For those looking for unique experiences in Saint Marks, here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Explore the Wakulla Springs caves by taking a diving tour of the underwater limestone caverns.
  • Take a sunset or full moon kayak tour of the Saint Marks River and witness the wildlife come alive.
  • Visit the Saint Marks National Wildlife Refuge during the annual monarch butterfly migration, where visitors can witness thousands of butterflies in one place.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint Marks

Saint Marks may be a small town, but it has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some local favorites not to be missed:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gulf Shrimp and Grits: A classic dish that is sure to please seafood lovers.
  • Fried Catfish Po’Boy: A regional delicacy, this sandwich is made with crusty French Bread and a generous helping of fried catfish.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple that can be found in most restaurants and cafes.
  • Apalachicola Bay Oyster Stout: A unique beer that features a blend of local oysters and roasted barley, giving it a distinct flavor.

Where to Stay in Saint Marks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint Marks has a variety of accommodation options, catering to all types of travelers. Here are some suggestions:

  • The Lodge at Wakulla Springs: This historic lodge features beautiful architecture and overlooks the crystal clear Wakulla Springs.
  • Rivers Stone Bed and Breakfast: Located in the heart of Saint Marks, this B&B offers charming rooms and a quaint atmosphere.
  • Shell Island Fish Camp: This rustic fish camp offers cozy cabins and direct access to the Saint Marks River.

What to Buy in Saint Marks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Saint Marks, there are plenty of local products to take home as souvenirs, including:

  • Local Honey: Some of the best honey in Florida can be found in this region.
  • Artisanal Soap: Made with natural ingredients, these soaps are perfect for sensitive skin.
  • Seafood Seasoning: Spices and seasoning are the perfect take-home gifts to bring a taste of Old Florida to your kitchen.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Marks

Saint Marks has a rich history and vibrant community. Here are some tips to help you blend in with the locals:

  • Respect the culture: Saint Marks is known for its laid-back and friendly attitude, so make sure to be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress for the weather: The weather in Saint Marks can be unpredictable, so make sure to pack accordingly.
  • Embrace the outdoors: Saint Marks is a nature lover’s paradise, so make the most of the outdoor attractions in the area.

Best Time to Visit Saint Marks

The best time to visit Saint Marks is from October to April. The weather during this time of year is mild, making it perfect for exploring the natural beauty of the area without the scorching heat that is common during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint Marks

Here are some general guidelines to follow when visiting Saint Marks:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic attractions.
  • Try the local seafood and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitats and trails.
  • Ignore the rules of the National Parks and Wildlife Refuges.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint Marks

Overall, Saint Marks is a safe place for tourists. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Saint Marks

Saint Marks is a small town, so medical services are limited. Visitors should take necessary health precautions and bring any necessary medications and supplies they may need during their stay.

Getting to Saint Marks: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint Marks

  • Tallahassee International Airport: This airport is located 33 miles from Saint Marks, and provides access to domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Saint Marks

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This station is located in Tallahassee, 30 miles from Saint Marks.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: A car rental is the easiest and most convenient way to get around Saint Marks and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Saint Marks

Saint Marks is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, visitors should rent a car if they plan to see the attractions in the surrounding areas, such as Tallahassee.

Where to Go Next from Saint Marks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tallahassee: The capital city of Florida is just a short drive from Saint Marks and offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Apalachicola: This charming coastal town is known for its quaint boutiques, galleries, and restaurants serving fresh seafood.

Conclusion

Saint Marks combines history, culture, and outdoor adventures to create a memorable vacation destination. Whether you’re looking to explore the rich history of Old Florida or simply relax on miles of unspoiled beaches, Saint Marks is sure to exceed your expectations.
