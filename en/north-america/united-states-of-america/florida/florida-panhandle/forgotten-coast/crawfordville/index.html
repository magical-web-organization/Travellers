Introduction to Crawfordville

Crawfordville is a small town located in the heart of the Forgotten Coast, in Florida Panhandle, United States of America. Despite its size, the town has a lot to offer visitors who are seeking a unique and memorable vacation experience. With its beautiful natural attractions, diverse cultural sites, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, Crawfordville is a destination worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Crawfordville

Are you planning a trip to Crawfordville and wondering what to see and do? Here are some of the must-visit sites and attractions in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wakulla Springs State Park: Known as the world’s largest and deepest freshwater spring, Wakulla Springs State Park offers visitors a wide range of activities, including swimming, boating, hiking, and nature trails. The park also hosts historical buildings and sites such as the Wakulla Springs Lodge and the Edward Ball Wakulla Springs State Park.

  • San Marcos de Apalache Historic State Park: This park is home to a Spanish fort that was used to protect the city from invading forces. Visitors can learn about the history of the area and explore the fort and adjacent museum.

  • Panacea Museum: This museum is dedicated to local history and culture, featuring exhibits on the area’s wildlife, fishing industry, and more.

Natural Attractions of Crawfordville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge: This beautiful wildlife refuge boasts over 70,000 acres of protected wetlands and forests. Visitors can hike and bike through the various trails, watch wildlife, or visit the lighthouse.

  • Ochlockonee River State Park: Located on the banks of the Ochlockonee River, this park features scenic picnic areas, kayaking, and fishing opportunities.

  • Apalachee Bay: This beautiful bay is home to several stunning beaches, including Shell Point Beach and Bald Point State Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crawfordville

Crawfordville is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. Here are some of the popular routes in the area:

  • St. Marks Trail: This paved trail is 20 miles long and passes through the beautiful natural surroundings of Crawfordville.

  • Wakulla Springs Trail: Leading up to the Wakulla Springs State Park, the trail is open for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.

  • Ochlockonee River State Park Trail: This 6.5-mile trail passes through the park and offers beautiful views of the river and surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Crawfordville: Activities and Tours

Wondering what activities and tours to add to your itinerary? Here are some suggestions:

  • Wildlife Watching and Eco-Tours: Go on a wildlife watching tour or eco-tour in the St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge to experience the natural beauty of Crawfordville up close and personal.

  • Fishing: Head to the Gulf of Mexico for a thrilling fishing experience, or try freshwater fishing in the nearby rivers.

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Explore the waters around Crawfordville and discover the stunning underwater sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Crawfordville

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to savor during your visit to Crawfordville:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gulf Seafood: Crawfordville is known for its fresh and delicious seafood. Don’t miss the opportunity to savor local favorites such as fried shrimp and crab cakes.

  • Fried Green Tomatoes: A Southern classic, fried green tomatoes are a Crawfordville specialty, often served with a side of remoulade sauce.

  • Hush Puppies: These deep-fried balls of cornmeal and flour originated in the Southern United States and are a staple of Crawfordville cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Tallahassee Brewing Company: This local brewery serves up some of the region’s best craft beer, with flavors ranging from traditional to unconventional.

  • Sweet Tea: No trip to Crawfordville is complete without trying sweet tea, a Southern favorite.

  • Mint Julep: A classic Southern cocktail, the mint julep is a must-try for any adult visitor to Crawfordville.

Where to Stay in Crawfordville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crawfordville has a range of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Best Western Wakulla Inn & Suites: This hotel offers fully-equipped rooms with a free breakfast buffet and outdoor pool.

  • Crawfordville Farmhouse: This historic property has been converted into a cozy guesthouse, offering a unique and comfortable stay in Crawfordville.

  • Camping and RV parks: If you’re looking for an outdoor experience, there are several great camping and RV parks in the area, including Wakulla Springs State Park and St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge.

What to Buy in Crawfordville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some unique souvenirs or local products to bring home with you from your trip to Crawfordville? Here are some suggestions:

  • Seafood and Spices: Local seafood and spices make great gifts or additions to your own pantry.

  • Artwork: Crawfordville is home to many talented artists, so take home a piece of their creativity in the form of paintings or other artwork.

  • Handmade Crafts: From homemade jewelry to knick-knacks, there are plenty of unique handmade crafts to take home from Crawfordville.

Local Culture and Customs of Crawfordville

Crawfordville is a charming and laid-back town, with a welcoming community vibe. Respect the locals, and try to blend in with their way of life by wearing casual clothing and taking things slow. Fishing, camping, and other outdoor activities are popular pastimes.

Best Time to Visit Crawfordville

Crawfordville can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is between March and November when the weather is warmer, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable. The peak tourist season is during the summer months when the beaches are most popular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crawfordville

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Crawfordville:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and conserve natural resources.

  • Enjoy the local seafood and Southern cuisine.

  • Try outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.

  • Take or harm any wildlife, including sea turtles, alligators, dolphins, and more.

Safety and Health

Safety in Crawfordville

Crawfordville is generally a safe place to visit with low crime rates. However, visitors should use common sense and take precautions such as locking their car doors, avoiding walking alone at night, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Crawfordville

Crawfordville has a range of medical facilities and hospitals in case of emergencies. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with them and ensure they have adequate health insurance.

Getting to Crawfordville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Crawfordville is well-connected to nearby cities by a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Crawfordville

  • Tallahassee International Airport (TLH): Located 25 miles away from Crawfordville, Tallahassee International Airport serves several major airlines and offers rental cars and taxis for transportation to the town.

  • Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport (ECP): Located 85 miles away from Crawfordville, this airport serves several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Crawfordville

  • Tallahassee Greyhound Station: Located 25 miles away from Crawfordville, this station is well-connected to Florida’s major cities.

  • Bay Line Railroad Station: Serving several locations around the Panhandle region, this station is a short drive from Crawfordville.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available at airports and surrounding areas.

  • Taxi and Shuttle Services: Several taxi and shuttle services operate in the area for transportation purposes.

How to Get Around in Crawfordville

Crawfordville is small enough to be explored on foot or by bike. However, for visitors who want to venture out, there are several car rental services and taxis available in town.

Where to Go Next from Crawfordville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Crawfordville is surrounded by several neighboring towns, all of which are worth visiting. Here are some suggestions:

  • Tallahassee: The state capital of Florida is only 25 miles away from Crawfordville and has several cultural and historical attractions to explore.

  • Panama City Beach: Head south to this stunning beach town, where you can enjoy water sports, shopping, and nightlife.

  • Apalachicola: A charming historic town famous for its oysters and seafood.

Conclusion

Crawfordville is a hidden gem in the beautiful Forgotten Coast region of Florida. With its natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality, it’s a destination that should be on every traveler’s list.
