Introduction to Apalachicola

Located in the heart of the Forgotten Coast in the Florida Panhandle, Apalachicola is a charming coastal town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and delicious seafood. The town is situated on the banks of the Apalachicola River and is home to a thriving oyster industry. Visitors can enjoy exploring the town’s historic district, shopping for antiques, hiking and biking through nearby state parks, and feasting on locally caught seafood.

List of Places to Visit in Apalachicola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Apalachicola Maritime Museum: Learn about the town’s maritime heritage and take a tour of the fully-restored 58-foot wooden schooner that calls the museum home.
  • John Gorrie Museum State Park: Visit the historic home of Dr. John Gorrie, the inventor of air conditioning, and explore exhibits on his life and work.
  • Orman House Historic State Park: Step back in time at this beautifully restored antebellum home and learn about the families who lived there.

Natural Attractions of Apalachicola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St. George Island State Park: This barrier island is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and opportunities for swimming, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
  • Apalachicola National Forest: Spanning over 630,000 acres, this forest offers endless opportunities for hiking, biking, camping, and bird watching.
  • Tate’s Hell State Forest: This vast wilderness area is home to diverse habitats and wildlife, as well as a network of hiking and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apalachicola

  • High Bluff Coastal Trail: This challenging 4.5-mile trail in Tate’s Hell State Forest offers scenic views of the Gulf of Mexico and opportunities to see wildlife like black bear and white-tailed deer.
  • Chapman Botanical Garden: This beautiful botanical garden features a network of trails through a diverse collection of plants, including many native to the region.
  • Scipio Creek Boardwalk: This half-mile boardwalk offers a leisurely stroll through a tidal marsh ecosystem and is perfect for bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Apalachicola: Activities and Tours

  • Oyster Tours: Take a guided tour of the town’s oyster farms and learn about the history and process of oyster farming from local experts.
  • Riverfront Park: Enjoy a picnic in this beautiful park overlooking the Apalachicola River, or take a leisurely stroll along the riverfront.
  • Apalachicola River Wildlife and Environmental Area: Explore the natural beauty of the Apalachicola River on a guided tour, or rent a kayak or canoe and explore on your own.

Week-Long Itinerary for Apalachicola

Day 1: Visit the John Gorrie Museum State Park and explore the historic district. Day 2: Take a guided oyster tour and sample some of the town’s famous seafood. Day 3: Hike the High Bluff Coastal Trail in Tate’s Hell State Forest. Day 4: Spend the day at St. George Island State Park. Day 5: Explore the Apalachicola National Forest on a guided tour or hike. Day 6: Visit the Orman House Historic State Park and Chapman Botanical Garden. Day 7: Relax in Riverfront Park and enjoy the beautiful views of the Apalachicola River.

Food and Drink Scene of Apalachicola

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters: Apalachicola oysters are renowned for their rich, buttery flavor and are a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Shrimp and Grits: This classic Southern dish is a staple on local menus and is often made with fresh Gulf shrimp.
  • Key Lime Pie: This refreshing dessert is the perfect way to end any meal in Apalachicola.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Oyster City Brewing Company is a local favorite, offering a variety of craft beers made with local ingredients.
  • Wine: The Sweet Grass Dairy Wine Bar and Shoppe features a great selection of wines from around the world, as well as local cheeses and charcuterie.
  • Cocktails: Many of the town’s restaurants and bars feature unique cocktails made with fresh ingredients and local spirits.

Where to Stay in Apalachicola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gibson Inn: This charming historic inn features 30 beautifully appointed rooms and is centrally located in the heart of downtown Apalachicola.
  • Water Street Hotel and Marina: This boutique hotel offers luxurious accommodations overlooking the Apalachicola River and is within walking distance of many top attractions.
  • Coombs Inn and Suites: This elegant bed and breakfast features 23 beautifully decorated rooms and suites, plus a swimming pool and hot tub.

What to Buy in Apalachicola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Apalachicola Sea Salt: This artisanal sea salt is harvested from the Gulf of Mexico and adds a deliciously unique flavor to any dish.
  • Local Art and Crafts: The town is home to many talented artisans and craftspeople, offering everything from handcrafted pottery to handmade jewelry.
  • Fresh Seafood: Take home some of the town’s famous oysters or shrimp to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Apalachicola

  • Dress Code: Apalachicola is a casual town, so there’s no need to dress up unless you’re visiting a fancy restaurant or attending a special event.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in Apalachicola, with standard rates of 15-20% for restaurant servers and 10-15% for other services like taxis and hairdressers.
  • Southern Hospitality: Apalachicola is known for its warm, welcoming locals who are always happy to offer recommendations and chat with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Apalachicola

The best time to visit Apalachicola is in the fall (September-November) when temperatures are cooler and crowds are smaller. Spring (March-May) is also a good time to visit, with mild temperatures and beautiful wildflowers in bloom. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid, but is a great time to enjoy the town’s beaches and water activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apalachicola

Do:

  • Try the seafood: Apalachicola is known for its delicious oysters and fresh Gulf shrimp.
  • Explore the outdoors: With so many parks and natural areas nearby, it’s easy to get out and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Learn about the local history: Apalachicola has a rich history that’s worth exploring, from the antebellum homes to the town’s maritime heritage.

Don’t:

  • Overpack: Apalachicola is a small town with a casual vibe, so leave the fancy clothes at home.
  • Ignore the local customs: Apalachicola is a friendly town with a strong sense of local culture, so be respectful and open-minded.
  • Miss out on the seafood: Seriously, it’s that good.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apalachicola

Apalachicola is a safe town with a low crime rate. As with any tourist destination, it’s always a good idea to take precautions like locking your car and avoiding poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Apalachicola

There are several medical facilities in Apalachicola, including the Weems Memorial Hospital, which offers emergency care and other medical services. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you on your trip and to have travel health insurance in case of any emergencies.

Getting to Apalachicola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apalachicola

The closest major airport to Apalachicola is Tallahassee International Airport (TLH), which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Apalachicola

Greyhound offers daily bus service to nearby Panama City, about an hour’s drive from Apalachicola.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Apalachicola, as it’s a scenic and easy drive from many major cities in the Southeastern US.

How to Get Around in Apalachicola

Apalachicola is a small town that’s easily walkable, with many top attractions within easy walking distance of each other. Rental cars and bicycles are also available for those who want to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Apalachicola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Panama City: This nearby city is a popular tourist destination, offering beaches, shopping, and a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Tallahassee: The state capital is about an hour’s drive away and offers cultural attractions like museums and art galleries, as well as shopping, dining, and nightlife.
  • Wakulla Springs State Park: This beautiful park is home to one of the largest and deepest freshwater springs in the world, as well as diverse wildlife like manatees and alligators.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a seafood lover, or all of the above, Apalachicola has something to offer every type of traveler. With its charming small-town vibes, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage, Apalachicola is the perfect destination for your next getaway.
