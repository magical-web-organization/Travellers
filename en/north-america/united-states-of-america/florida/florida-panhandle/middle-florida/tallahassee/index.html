Introduction to Tallahassee

Tallahassee, the capital of Florida, is located in the Florida Panhandle. Known for its mild winters and hot summers, Tallahassee is a vibrant city that is home to several universities, government agencies, and cultural institutions. Tallahassee’s rich history, natural beauty, and cultural diversity make it a perfect destination for both adventure seekers and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Tallahassee

Whether you’re interested in history, art, or outdoor activities, Tallahassee has something for everyone. Here are some of the best places to visit in Tallahassee:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tallahassee Museum

The Tallahassee Museum is an expansive outdoor preserve located on 52 acres of beautiful forested land. The museum showcases the history of the Tallahassee region with interactive exhibits and live animal encounters. Visitors can explore a historic farmstead, a 19th-century schoolhouse, and an underground cave system.

Florida Historic Capitol Museum

The Florida Historic Capitol Museum is a preserved historic Capitol building that served as the state’s Capitol from 1845 to 1977. The museum provides interactive exhibits that showcase the political and social history of Florida.

Knott House Museum

The Knott House Museum is a historic 19th-century home that was the site of several important events in Florida’s history, including the signing of the Emancipation Proclamation in 1865. Visitors can tour the home and learn about the family that once lived there, as well as the significant events that took place in the house.

Natural Attractions of Tallahassee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alfred B. Maclay Gardens State Park

The Alfred B. Maclay Gardens State Park is a beautiful 1,176-acre park located on the north shore of Lake Hall. The park features hiking and biking trails, scenic views, and a variety of gardens, including a butterfly garden and a reflection pool.

Cascades Park

Cascades Park is a 24-acre park located in the heart of Tallahassee. The park features scenic views, walking paths, and a variety of outdoor activities, including outdoor concerts and events.

St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge

The St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge is a 70,000-acre refuge that provides habitat for a variety of wildlife species, including migratory birds, manatees, black bears, and alligators. Visitors can enjoy hiking, birdwatching, fishing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tallahassee

Tallahassee-St. Marks Historic Railroad State Trail

The Tallahassee-St. Marks Historic Railroad State Trail is an 19-mile trail that extends from Tallahassee to St. Marks. The trail provides scenic views of Florida’s countryside and wildlife, including deer, hawks, and alligators.

Apalachicola National Forest

The Apalachicola National Forest is a 632,890-acre forest that provides hiking and biking trails, scenic views, and camping opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Tallahassee: Activities and Tours

Tallahassee Segway Tours

Tallahassee Segway Tours provides a range of guided tours that offer visitors a unique perspective on Tallahassee’s history and culture. Visitors can tour various historic sites, including the Florida State Capitol and the Governor’s Mansion, on a Segway.

Gulf Specimen Marine Lab

Gulf Specimen Marine Lab is a marine research and education center where visitors can see and interact with a variety of marine animals, including seahorses, sea turtles, and sharks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tallahassee

Plan your ideal week-long itinerary in Tallahassee with the following attractions:

  • Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to the Tallahassee Museum
  • Day 2: Take a walk through the Cascades Park and explore the natural beauty of the area
  • Day 3: Visit the Florida Historic Capitol Museum to learn about the political history of Florida
  • Day 4: Hike the Tallahassee-St. Marks Historic Railroad State Trail
  • Day 5: Visit the Alfred B. Maclay Gardens State Park for a peaceful day of exploring gardens and scenic views
  • Day 6: Enjoy a Segway tour of the Governor’s Mansion and other historic sites
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to the Gulf Specimen Marine Lab for a unique and interactive marine experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Tallahassee

When it comes to local food, Tallahassee offers a variety of dining options. Be sure to try these local dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

Florida Seafood

Tallahassee’s proximity to the Gulf of Mexico ensures that you will find some of the freshest seafood in the region. Be sure to stop by any of the local seafood restaurants for a taste of Florida’s fresh catch of the day.

Grits and Gravy

Grits and Gravy is a popular Southern-style dish consisting of a creamy grits base topped with sausage gravy. Try it at any local breakfast spot.

Local Beverages

Local Breweries

Tallahassee has several local breweries where visitors can sample craft beer. Countless breweries, such as Deep Brewing Company, Ology Brewing Company, and Proof Brewing Company, offer a unique selection of high-quality beer.

Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is the classic southern drink that’s perfect for sipping on a hot Florida afternoon. Enjoy it at any of the local diners or cafes.

Local Culture and Customs of Tallahassee

Tallahassee is a diverse city with several cultures that have significant impacts on its traditions and customs. The city has a rich African American history and is home to several indigenous people.

Best Time to Visit Tallahassee

The best time to visit Tallahassee is in the spring, from March through May, when the weather is mild, and the flowers are in bloom. The fall season is also a good time to visit Tallahassee, from September to November, when the weather is mild and the fall foliage is in full display.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tallahassee

Tallahassee is a very safe city but be sure to take precautions like locking car doors and refrain from leaving valuables in an unattended car.

Health in Tallahassee

Tallahassee is home to several hospitals and healthcare facilities. In case of a medical emergency, dial 911 for immediate assistance.

How to Get Around in Tallahassee

The best way to get around Tallahassee is by car. Downtown Tallahassee is accessible on foot, and there are also several bike rental companies throughout the city. Additionally, buses are available through the StarMetro bus system.
