Introduction to Middle Florida

Located in the Florida Panhandle region, Middle Florida is a charming vacation destination that combines rich history, outdoor adventures, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, there’s plenty to explore and discover in Middle Florida.

List of Places to Visit in Middle Florida

Here are some top-rated attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Florida Historic Capitol Museum - a beautifully restored 1902 government building that now houses exhibits on Florida’s political history.
  • Tallahassee Museum - a 52-acre outdoor museum featuring historic buildings, live animals, and nature trails.
  • Mission San Luis de Apalachee - a reconstructed 17th-century Spanish mission with costumed interpreters and archaeological exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Middle Florida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge - a 70,000-acre refuge with hiking trails, birdwatching, and fishing opportunities.
  • Wakulla Springs State Park - a majestic natural spring that offers swimming, boat tours, and wildlife sightings.
  • Apalachicola National Forest - a sprawling forest with hiking trails, hunting, and camping opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Middle Florida

If you love hiking or cycling, then Middle Florida is the perfect destination for you. Some popular paths to explore include:

  • St. Marks Historic Railroad Trail - a 20-mile trail that follows the route of a historic railway.
  • Red Bug Trail - a 5-mile hike through the Apalachicola National Forest that features lush greenery and a scenic lake.
  • Tallahassee-St. Marks Trail - a 16-mile paved trail that takes you through scenic natural areas and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Middle Florida: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and cycling, there are plenty of fun activities to enjoy in Middle Florida. Here are some suggestions:

  • Kayaking on Wakulla River - explore Wakulla Springs State Park in a kayak or canoe and spot manatees, alligators, and other wildlife.
  • Visit Local Breweries - Tallahassee is home to several top-rated breweries, including Proof Brewing Company, Deep Brewing Company, and Grasslands Brewing Company.
  • Tallahassee Food Tour - sample the best local cuisine with a guided food tour that takes you to top-rated restaurants and markets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Middle Florida

If you have a week to explore Middle Florida, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Florida Historic Capitol Museum and wander through downtown Tallahassee
2 Explore the St. Marks National Wildlife Refuge and take a sunset boat tour
3 Hike the Red Bug Trail in Apalachicola National Forest and visit local breweries in Tallahassee
4 Take a guided kayak tour of Wakulla Springs State Park and have a picnic at Edward Ball Wakulla Springs State Park

6 Visit the Mission San Luis de Apalachee and watch a movie at the Challenger Learning Center

Food and Drink Scene of Middle Florida

Middle Florida has a vibrant food and drink scene with a range of options to suit all tastes. Some must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grits - a southern staple made from ground corn that’s often served with shrimp or sausage.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes - green tomatoes that are breaded and fried, often served with a remoulade sauce.
  • Oysters - Apalachicola Bay is renowned for its oyster beds, so be sure to try them while you’re here.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - a refreshing southern drink made from tea and sugar.
  • Local Beer - Tallahassee has a growing craft beer scene, featuring breweries such as Proof Brewing Company, Deep Brewing Company, and Grasslands Brewing Company.
  • Wine - Florida has several award-winning wineries, including Chateau Elan and San Sebastian Winery.

Where to Stay in Middle Florida: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Middle Florida offers a range of accommodations to fit all budgets and preferences. Some top-rated options include:

  • The Governors Inn - a luxurious downtown hotel with a rooftop terrace and full-service spa.
  • The New Leaf Market Co-op Hostel - a budget-friendly option that features shared kitchens and living areas.
  • Airbnb - Middle Florida has many charming homes and apartments available for rent on Airbnb.

What to Buy in Middle Florida: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home from Middle Florida? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Apalachicola Oysters - bring home a taste of Middle Florida with a bag of fresh oysters.
  • Pecans - Florida produces over half of the US’s pecans, so stock up on these delicious nuts while you’re here.
  • Artisanal Soaps and Skincare Products - Middle Florida is home to several artisanal soap and skincare companies, such as Native Essence and Aromatherapy Bath.

Local Culture and Customs of Middle Florida

Middle Florida is a blend of southern and coastal cultures, with a laid-back vibe and friendly locals. When visiting, it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions. Some cultural tips to keep in mind include:

  • Southern Hospitality - locals are known for their warmth and friendliness, so be sure to return the favor with polite manners and a smile.
  • Casual Dress - Middle Florida has a relaxed dress code, so leave your formal wear at home and opt for comfortable attire.
  • Tipping - tipping is customary in Middle Florida, so be sure to tip your servers and service providers accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Middle Florida

The best time to visit Middle Florida is between March and May or October and November when the weather is mild, and tourism is less crowded. However, if you’re a fan of warm weather and outdoor activities, plan your visit between June and August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middle Florida

To help you make the most of your trip to Middle Florida, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood, especially the oysters.
  • Do take a hike or outdoor adventure in one of the many state parks and nature reserves.
  • Do visit the historic sites and museums to learn more about Middle Florida’s rich culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave garbage in the natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip your server at restaurants and bars.
  • Don’t try to touch or feed the wildlife, including alligators and manatees.

Safety and Health

Safety in Middle Florida

Middle Florida is a relatively safe destination, but as with any tourist destination, it’s important to be vigilant and take precautions to protect your safety and belongings. Here are some safety tips to follow:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Keep your belongings close and watch out for pickpockets.
  • When hiking or camping, be aware of snakes, alligators, and other wildlife.

Health in Middle Florida

Middle Florida has excellent medical facilities and services, but it’s important to take preventive measures to protect your health. Here are some health tips to follow:

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water, especially in the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to prevent sunburn and heatstroke.
  • Avoid swimming in freshwater areas that may contain bacteria and parasites.

Getting to Middle Florida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Middle Florida is easily accessible from major cities in Florida and neighboring states. Here are some options for transportation:

Airports Near Middle Florida

  • Tallahassee International Airport (TLH)
  • Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport (ECP)

Bus Stations Near Middle Florida

  • Greyhound Bus Lines Station in Tallahassee

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a popular option to explore Middle Florida and surrounding areas.
  • Amtrak’s Lake Shore Limited and Sunset Limited lines serve Tallahassee’s Union Station, providing access to major cities across the US.

How to Get Around in Middle Florida

Middle Florida has a public transportation system that includes buses, trolleys, and shuttles. However, renting a car is the easiest way to get around and explore the area’s many attractions.

Where to Go Next from Middle Florida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to extend your trip to Middle Florida? Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Panama City - a coastal city with white sand beaches and a lively nightlife scene.
  • St. Augustine - a historic city on the east coast of Florida with charming shops, restaurants, and landmarks.
  • Cedar Key - an off-the-beaten-path island town with excellent seafood and access to several nature reserves.

Conclusion

Middle Florida is a hidden gem with something for everyone, from natural attractions and outdoor adventures to historic sites and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re planning a week-long trip or a weekend getaway, be sure to explore the best that Middle Florida has to offer.
