Perdido Key is a hidden gem located in the Florida Panhandle and is a perfect destination for those who are interested in outdoor fun, beautiful scenery, and a slower pace of life. This barrier island is nestled between Pensacola, Florida, and Orange Beach, Alabama, providing the perfect spot for those who want to explore the Gulf Coast region. Here is a comprehensive guide to Perdido Key, including top attractions, hiking trails, food and drink scene, accommodation options, and more.

Introduction to Perdido Key

Perdido Key is a beautiful barrier island that is approximately 16 miles long and is located between Pensacola, Florida, and Orange Beach, Alabama. This hidden gem is surrounded by the Gulf of Mexico and the Intracoastal Waterway, which provides plenty of opportunities for water-based activities. Its natural beauty and laid-back vibe make Perdido Key a perfect destination for those who want to unplug and enjoy nature.

List of Places to Visit in Perdido Key

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pensacola Lighthouse and Museum: This historic lighthouse is located on Pensacola Naval Air Station and is open for tours. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse for stunning views of Pensacola Bay.
  • Johnson Beach: This pristine beach is part of the Gulf Islands National Seashore and provides visitors with a glimpse into the area’s military history. Fort McRee, a historic fort from the Civil War, is located nearby and is open for tours.
  • National Naval Aviation Museum: This museum is located on the Pensacola Naval Air Station and is home to over 150 aircraft. Visitors can explore the museum and see historic planes up close.

Natural Attractions of Perdido Key: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Perdido Key State Park: This beautiful state park features white sand beaches, dune trails, and lots of wildlife. Visitors can go hiking, fishing, and picnicking in the park.
  • Big Lagoon State Park: This park is located on the Intracoastal Waterway and provides visitors with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. Visitors can go kayaking, fishing, hiking, or birdwatching in the park.
  • Gulf Islands National Seashore: This beautiful national seashore includes the Perdido Key area and several other locations in the Gulf Coast region. Visitors can enjoy beautiful beaches, historic sites, and plenty of outdoor activities in the national seashore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perdido Key

  • Perdido Key Discovery Trail: This 8.5-mile trail runs the length of Perdido Key and provides visitors with a glimpse into the area’s natural beauty. Visitors can hike or bike the trail and see plenty of wildlife, including birds and dolphins.
  • Big Lagoon State Park Trail: This 2.5-mile hiking trail takes visitors through the heart of Big Lagoon State Park and provides beautiful views of the Intracoastal Waterway.
  • Johnson Beach Trail: This 1.5-mile trail takes visitors through the heart of Johnson Beach and provides stunning views of the Gulf of Mexico.

Best Things to Do in Perdido Key: Activities and Tours

  • Dolphin watching cruises: Visitors can go on dolphin watching cruises and see these beautiful creatures up close.
  • Fishing charters: Perdido Key is a popular destination for fishing, and visitors can go on fishing charters to catch a variety of fish.
  • Kayaking and paddleboarding: Visitors can explore the waters around Perdido Key by renting kayaks or paddleboards.
  • Parasailing: Visitors can enjoy stunning views of Perdido Key and the Gulf of Mexico by going parasailing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perdido Key

Day Activity
1 Spend the day relaxing on the beach at Perdido Key State Park
2 Explore the natural beauty of Big Lagoon State Park by hiking or kayaking
3 Visit the Pensacola Lighthouse and Museum
4 Spend the day at Johnson Beach and visit Fort McRee
5 Go on a dolphin watching cruise

Food and Drink Scene of Perdido Key

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gulf seafood: Visitors can enjoy delicious and fresh seafood, including shrimp, oysters, and crab.
  • Key lime pie: This delicious dessert is a staple of the Gulf Coast region and is a must-try for visitors.
  • Gumbo: This hearty soup is a mixture of seafood, sausage, and vegetables and is a perfect dish for a cool evening.

Local Beverages

  • Bushwacker: This popular drink is a mix of rum, Kahlúa, cream of coconut, and chocolate syrup.
  • Margarita: This classic drink is perfect for sipping on the beach and can be found at many local bars.

Where to Stay in Perdido Key: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Perdido Beach Resort: This beautiful beachfront hotel offers stunning views of Perdido Key and provides visitors with plenty of amenities, including a swimming pool, restaurant, and beach access.
  • Airbnb rentals: There are many affordable Airbnb rentals in Perdido Key, including beachfront condos and apartments.
  • Campgrounds: Visitors can choose from several campgrounds in the area, including the Big Lagoon State Park campground and the Perdido Key KOA.

What to Buy in Perdido Key: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gulf Coast Art: Visitors can bring home beautiful artwork and crafts created by local artisans.
  • Seafood: Visitors can buy fresh seafood to take home and cook.
  • Beach gear: Visitors can buy beach gear, including towels, hats, and sunscreen, to prepare for a day at the beach.

Local Culture and Customs of Perdido Key

Perdido Key is known for its laid-back vibe and friendly locals. Visitors are encouraged to enjoy the natural beauty of the area and participate in outdoor activities. Dress is generally casual, and visitors are encouraged to pack light and bring comfortable clothing for the warm weather.

Best Time to Visit Perdido Key

The best time to visit Perdido Key is in the spring or fall when the weather is cooler and less crowded. However, visitors can enjoy the beach and outdoor activities year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perdido Key

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Perdido Key
  • Participate in outdoor activities
  • Try the local seafood and dishes

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or in the water
  • Ignore beach safety warnings
  • Disturb wildlife in the area

Safety and Health

Safety in Perdido Key

Visitors should be aware of the dangers of the Gulf of Mexico, including strong currents and unpredictable weather conditions. Visitors should also be aware of beach safety warnings and avoid swimming in rough conditions.

Health in Perdido Key

Visitors should stay hydrated and wear sunscreen to avoid sunburn. Visitors should also be aware of the Zika virus and take precautions if needed.

Conclusion

Perdido Key is the perfect destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy the natural beauty of the Gulf Coast region. With its beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, and laid-back vibe, it’s easy to see why Perdido Key is a popular destination for visitors from around the world.
