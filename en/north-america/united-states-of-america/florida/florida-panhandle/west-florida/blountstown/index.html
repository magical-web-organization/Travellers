Introduction to Blountstown

Blountstown is a small town located in West Florida, in the Florida Panhandle region of the United States of America. Known for its beautiful landscapes, rich history, and friendly locals, Blountstown is a popular destination among tourists looking to explore the less-trodden paths of Florida.

From hiking and biking to indulging in local cuisine and immersing in the area’s cultural scene, Blountstown offers a variety of activities and attractions to make your trip memorable. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore everything you need to know before visiting Blountstown.

List of Places to Visit in Blountstown

Culturally Important Attractions - Museums and Historical Places

  • Calhoun County History Museum - Learn about the rich history of Calhoun County and its surrounds. The museum offers a collection of artifacts and exhibits that detail the area’s past.

  • Neal Landing Cemetery - Located near Blountstown, this historical cemetery dates back to the mid-19th century and is the final resting place for many locals, including veterans.

  • Panhandle Pioneer Settlement - Housed in a former schoolhouse, this settlement offers visitors a glimpse of pioneer life in Florida through artifacts, exhibits, and demonstrations.

Natural Attractions of Blountstown - Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Apalachicola National Forest - Known for its rich biodiversity, the Apalachicola National Forest provides visitors with an opportunity to experience Florida’s wilderness. Hiking, camping, and bird watching are popular activities here.

  • Dead Lakes State Recreation Area - This serene park is surrounded by cypress trees and offers a peaceful escape for anglers, boaters, and bird watchers.

  • Torrance Wildlife Management Area - This 4,700-acre area offers hiking and hunting opportunities, as well as a chance to spot rare wildlife like the red-cockaded woodpecker and the Eastern indigo snake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blountstown

Hikers and bikers will find plenty of trails to explore in and around Blountstown. The trails in the area range from short and easy to longer and more challenging.

  • Blountstown Greenway Trail - This trail runs parallel to Blountstown’s Main Street and offers a chance to see the town from a different perspective.

  • Chipola River Paddling Trail - This scenic trail stretches over 50 miles and offers kayakers and canoers a chance to experience Florida’s natural beauty.

  • Torreya Challenge Trail - This challenging 7.5-mile trail features steep climbs, stunning overlooks, and is the perfect hike for experienced hikers looking for a challenge.

Best Things to Do in Blountstown - Activities and Tours

  • Blountstown Farmer’s Market - Open every Saturday, this market features local produce, artisanal crafts, and live music.

  • Blountstown Mule Day - Held annually in March, this festival celebrates the town’s agricultural heritage with a parade, live music, and a mule pulling contest.

  • Panhandle Pioneer Settlement Heritage Festival - Held annually in November, this festival allows visitors to immerse in pioneer life in Florida with live demonstrations and exhibits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blountstown

Day 1: Visit the Calhoun County History Museum and take a stroll around Blountstown Greenway Trail.

Day 2: Explore the Apalachicola National Forest and enjoy a picnic at the peaceful Silver Lake Recreation Area.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Panhandle Pioneer Settlement and learn about pioneer life in Florida.

Day 4: Go kayaking or canoeing on Chipola River Paddling Trail.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Marianna to explore Florida Caverns State Park.

Day 6: Go birdwatching or hiking at Torrance Wildlife Management Area.

Day 7: Attend the Blountstown Mule Day festival and witness the mule pulling contest.

Food and Drink Scene of Blountstown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Catfish - Indulge in some of the freshest catfish in Florida at any one of Blountstown’s local restaurants.

  • Barbecue - Whether it’s traditional barbecue or southern-style, the barbecue in Blountstown is not to be missed.

  • Southern Fried Chicken - Try the classic southern dish at a local restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - The quintessential drink of the South, sweet tea can be found at any local restaurant or cafe.

  • Craft Beer - Check out the local breweries for some artisanal craft beer and a great atmosphere.

Where to Stay in Blountstown - Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly accommodations to luxury hotels, Blountstown has something to offer every traveler.

  • Rivertown Inn - A charming bed and breakfast located on the Chipola River.

  • Quality Inn - A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a great location.

  • Holiday Inn Express - A popular choice for business travelers and families, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a variety of amenities.

What to Buy in Blountstown - Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Honey - Pick up some locally produced honey from any one of the farmers’ markets in the area.

  • Southern-style Crafts - From handmade quilts to pottery, the local arts and crafts scene is thriving in Blountstown.

Local Culture and Customs of Blountstown

The locals in Blountstown are friendly and welcoming. A polite greeting and a smile go a long way in the area.

Best Time to Visit Blountstown

The best time to visit Blountstown is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blountstown

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.

  • Embrace the Southern hospitality and culture.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blountstown

Blountstown is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates. However, it’s always best to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Blountstown

Travelers are advised to protect themselves from mosquito bites, especially during the summer months when mosquito-borne illnesses are prevalent.

Getting to Blountstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blountstown

  • Tallahassee International Airport

  • Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport

Bus Stations Near Blountstown

  • Greyhound Bus Lines (5 miles)

  • Panhandle Transit (Blountstown is a stop on their route.)

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a popular option for travelers who want to explore the area on their own.

How to Get Around in Blountstown

While there is no public transportation in Blountstown, renting a car or a bicycle are popular options for getting around the area.

Where to Go Next from Blountstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Marianna - Home to Florida Caverns State Park, Marianna is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Blountstown is a hidden gem in Florida’s Panhandle, offering visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and rich culture of the area. From hiking and biking to indulging in local cuisine, Blountstown has something to offer every type of traveler.
