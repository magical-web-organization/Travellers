Big Bend Coast: Your Ultimate Travel Guide

Welcome to Big Bend Coast, one of Florida’s best-kept secrets. Home to diverse landscapes, rich history, and exciting outdoor activities, this hidden gem on Florida’s West Coast is a must-visit for any traveler. Read on to discover the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Big Bend Coast

Located in the northern part of Florida’s Gulf Coast, Big Bend Coast covers a large area of six counties, from Pasco County in the south to Wakulla County in the north. This region, surrounded by the Gulf of Mexico and Apalachee Bay, is known for its unspoiled beauty, with mangroves, salt marshes, and white-sand beaches dotting the coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Big Bend Coast

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Big Bend Coast:

Day 1: Arrive in Tallahassee, explore the city’s historic sites, museum, and get your bearings. Day 2: Take a walking tour of Tarpon Springs Sponge Docks. Day 3: Go kayaking in Wakulla Springs State Park, and hike the nearby trails. Day 4: Head to Crystal River, swim with manatees, and explore the Crystal River National Wildlife Refuge. Day 5: Take a dolphin-watching tour in Gulf Islands National Seashore and go fishing. Day 6: Take a guided tour of St. Michaels Cemetery in Pensacola, and explore the heritage sites. Day 7: Relax and unwind on the beaches of Big Bend Coast.

Best Time to Visit Big Bend Coast

The best time to visit Big Bend Coast is between March and May when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. It’s also a great time to enjoy the national parks and outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Big Bend Coast

Big Bend Coast is a safe area for travelers. However, it’s important to take precautions while hiking or participating in water activities.

Health in Big Bend Coast

Mosquitoes and ticks can be present in the region, so it’s important to apply insect repellent and wear protective clothing.
