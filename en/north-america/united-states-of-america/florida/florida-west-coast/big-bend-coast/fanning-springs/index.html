Introduction to Fanning Springs

Located on the Big Bend Coast of Florida’s West Coast, Fanning Springs is a popular tourist destination known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. It is named after the beautiful springs in the town that are fed by the Suwannee River.

The town’s history dates back to the early 1800s when it was a trading post and a port for steamships traveling up and down the Suwannee River. Today, Fanning Springs is a popular destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and outdoor enthusiasts.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best things to do, places to visit, and local culture of Fanning Springs, Florida.

List of Places to Visit in Fanning Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fanning Springs State Park: This state park is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. It has crystal-clear springs, a boardwalk, and picnic areas. Visitors can enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking in the springs.
  • Cedar Key State Museum State Park: This museum showcases the history of Cedar Key, which was an important port for the Gulf of Mexico. Visitors can learn about the early settlers, the history of the railroad and farming, and the town’s impact on the shipping industry.
  • Lower Suwannee National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge spans over 53,000 acres and is home to a variety of Florida wildlife. Visitors can explore the trails, go bird-watching, and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Natural Attractions of Fanning Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Suwannee River: The Suwannee River runs through Fanning Springs and is a popular destination for kayaking and canoeing. Visitors can take a leisurely trip down the river or opt for an exciting white-water ride.
  • Manatee Springs State Park: This state park is a popular destination for seeing manatees and other wildlife. Visitors can swim and snorkel in the springs, go fishing, or explore the hiking trails.
  • Devil’s Den Springs: These prehistoric springs are a unique feature in Fanning Springs. Visitors can swim and snorkel in the springs or explore the underwater caves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fanning Springs

  • Fanning Springs State Park: The park has several nature trails that range from easy to moderate. Visitors can hike through the woods, take in the scenic views, and explore the natural beauty of the park.
  • Lower Suwannee National Wildlife Refuge: The refuge has miles of trails for hiking, biking, and bird-watching. Visitors can explore the different ecosystems and see a variety of Florida wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Fanning Springs: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Visitors can rent kayaks and canoes to explore the Suwannee River and the springs in Fanning Springs. Guided tours are also available.
  • Snorkeling: The clear springs of Fanning Springs are perfect for snorkeling. Visitors can see a variety of fish and underwater wildlife.
  • Fishing: Fanning Springs is a popular destination for fishing. Visitors can fish in the Suwannee River for catfish, bass, and other fish.
  • Cycling: Cycling is a fun way to explore the natural beauty of Fanning Springs. Visitors can rent bikes or bring their own to explore the trails and roads in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fanning Springs

Day 1: Explore Fanning Springs State Park and swim in the crystal-clear springs.

Day 2: Take a guided kayak tour of the Suwannee River.

Day 3: Explore the Lower Suwannee National Wildlife Refuge.

Day 4: Go snorkeling in the Devil’s Den Springs.

Day 5: Visit Cedar Key State Museum State Park and learn about the town’s history.

Day 6: Explore the trails of the Lower Suwannee National Wildlife Refuge on foot or by bike.

Day 7: Take a leisurely trip down the Suwannee River on a rented canoe.

Food and Drink Scene of Fanning Springs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Fanning Springs is a popular destination for fresh seafood. Visitors can try traditional Florida dishes like fried shrimp, oysters, and fish tacos.
  • Barbecue: Barbecue is a staple in Fanning Springs. Visitors can try the local barbecue restaurants for some of the best ribs and pulled pork in Florida.
  • Fried Chicken: Fried chicken is a classic Southern dish that visitors must try while in Fanning Springs.

Local Beverages

  • Florida Craft Beers: Florida has a booming craft beer scene, and Fanning Springs has several breweries that offer a variety of delicious local beers.
  • Sweet Tea: Sweet tea is a staple in the South, and visitors must try it while in Fanning Springs.

Where to Stay in Fanning Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fanning Springs Motel: This motel is located near the state park and offers clean, comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Suwannee Gables Motel and RV Park: This motel and RV park has a pool, picnic area, and is just a short drive from the springs and other attractions.

What to Buy in Fanning Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gator Souvenirs: Gator souvenirs are a popular item to bring back from Florida. Visitors can find all kinds of gator-themed products in Fanning Springs, including t-shirts, hats, and stuffed animals.
  • Handmade Crafts: Fanning Springs has several local artisans that sell handmade crafts like pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Fanning Springs

Fanning Springs is known for its Southern hospitality and warm, welcoming culture. Visitors should be sure to respect the locals and their customs while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Fanning Springs

The best time to visit Fanning Springs is between March and May when the weather is mild, and the parks and springs are less crowded. The fall is also a great time to visit when the weather is cooler, and the leaves are changing colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fanning Springs

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the natural beauty and outdoor adventure opportunities.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Park in areas that are not designated for parking.
  • Ignore safety warnings or signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fanning Springs

Fanning Springs is a safe destination for tourists. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take basic safety precautions like locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Fanning Springs

Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and swimming. They should pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and other essential items to protect themselves from the sun, bugs, and other outdoor hazards.

Getting to Fanning Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fanning Springs

The closest major airport to Fanning Springs is the Gainesville Regional Airport, located about 50 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Fanning Springs

The nearest bus station to Fanning Springs is the Greyhound station in Gainesville, about 50 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get to Fanning Springs.

How to Get Around in Fanning Springs

The best way to get around in Fanning Springs is by car or bike. Visitors can also rent kayaks or canoes to explore the rivers and springs.

Where to Go Next from Fanning Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Crystal River: Crystal River is a popular destination for snorkeling with manatees.
  • Gainesville: Gainesville is a vibrant college town with plenty of museums, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • Cedar Key: Cedar Key is a charming fishing village with plenty of seafood restaurants and scenic views.

Conclusion

Fanning Springs is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the natural beauty of the springs, go kayaking or canoeing, and learn about the town’s rich history. With its warm culture and Southern hospitality, Fanning Springs is a beautiful destination that should be on every traveler’s list.
