Introduction to Spring Hill

Spring Hill is a charming community located in the Big Bend Coast region of Florida’s West Coast. Known for its beautiful natural attractions, lively cultural scene, and friendly locals, Spring Hill is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Florida experience.

List of Places to Visit in Spring Hill

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hernando Historical Museum: This museum features exhibits and artifacts highlighting the rich history of Hernando County, including the civil war, pioneer life, and more.
  • Chinsegut Hill Historic Site: Explore the historical buildings, enjoy the beautiful gardens and nature trails, and learn about the fascinating history of this 114-acre site.
  • Weeki Wachee Springs State Park: Home to the famous Weeki Wachee Mermaids, this state park offers fun performances, water activities, and nature encounters.

Natural Attractions of Spring Hill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Weeki Wachee River: This crystal-clear river is perfect for kayaking, canoeing, and tubing with breathtaking views of natural Florida.
  • Bayport Park: Enjoy a relaxing day fishing, kayaking, or observing wildlife such as dolphins, manatees, and sea turtles.
  • Strong Tower Vineyard and Winery: Visit this charming vineyard and winery and enjoy a glass of locally-made wine with stunning vineyard views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spring Hill

  • Withlacoochee State Trail: This 46-mile trail is perfect for biking and hiking and offers stunning views of wildlife and nature.
  • Chinsegut Conservation Center: Walk the trails and enjoy the beauty of this nature center, which includes pine forests, wetlands, and meadows.

Best Things to Do in Spring Hill: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Weeki Wachee Springs State Park: Enjoy the water activities, mermaid shows, and the nature trail at this beautiful state park.
  • Take a Dolphin Watching Tour: Head out on the Gulf of Mexico for a dolphin watching tour and catch a glimpse of these graceful creatures.
  • Visit Pine Island Beach: Relax on the soft white sand, swim in the clear water, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere of this beautiful beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spring Hill

Day Activity
1 Explore the Hernando Historical Museum and Chinsegut Hill Historic Site
2 Visit Weeki Wachee Springs State Park and enjoy the water activities and mermaid show
3 Check out the beautiful views at Bayport Park and Strong Tower Vineyard and Winery
4 Bike or hike the Withlacoochee State Trail or walk the trails at the Chinsegut Conservation Center
5 Take a dolphin watching tour on the Gulf of Mexico and enjoy the scenery
6 Relax on Pine Island Beach and soak up the sun
7 Visit the Hernando County Farmers Market and grab souvenirs at The Florida Cracker Trading Company for your trip back home

Food and Drink Scene of Spring Hill

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Enjoy fresh seafood dishes like local Gulf shrimp, oysters, and fish.
  • Southern BBQ: Savor the delicious flavors of Southern-style BBQ ribs, pulled pork, and brisket.
  • Cuban Sandwich: Try the delicious combination of ham, roasted pork, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard on Cuban bread.

Local Beverages

  • Margaritas: Enjoy a refreshing margarita made with fresh Florida citrus and local spirits.
  • Local Beer: Try the local craft beers at Marker 48 Brewing or The Florida Cracker Trading Company.

Where to Stay in Spring Hill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Motel 6: Budget-friendly motel with comfortable rooms and a central location
  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites: Mid-range hotel with clean, modern rooms and amenities like a pool and fitness center
  • The Chateau Resort: Elegant and luxurious resort with beautiful rooms, spa, and golf course

What to Buy in Spring Hill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The Florida Cracker Trading Company: Shop for handmade souvenirs such as handcrafted soaps, candles, and jewelry
  • Hernando County Farmers Market: Pick up fresh produce, flowers, and local honey to bring home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Spring Hill

Spring Hill is a friendly and welcoming community where politeness and respect for others are important values. Dress code in public places and restaurants is generally casual, but it’s always a good idea to check ahead.

Best Time to Visit Spring Hill

The best time to visit Spring Hill is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September through November) when temperatures are mild and comfortable. However, winter and summer also have their perks, respectively fewer crowds, and more sunny beach days.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spring Hill

Do:

  • Respect the locals and the unique culture of Florida.
  • Visit the natural attractions and parks.
  • Check out the local seafood and Cuban cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitat and wildlife.
  • Ignore beach safety rules and warnings.
  • Forget your sunscreen and bug spray.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spring Hill

Spring Hill is a safe destination with low crime rates, but taking basic safety precautions like locking up rental cars and securing valuables at the beach is always advised.

Health in Spring Hill

While it’s generally safe to travel to Florida, it’s important to avoid mosquito bites by using bug spray and wearing long sleeves and pants. Also, drink plenty of water to keep yourself hydrated during outdoor activities.

Getting to Spring Hill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spring Hill

  • Tampa International Airport: Located 50 miles south of Spring Hill and served by major airlines from all over the country.
  • St. Pete-Clearwater International Airport: Located 60 miles south of Spring Hill and a hub for low-cost carriers.

Bus Stations Near Spring Hill

  • Greyhound Bus Line Tampa Station: Located 50 miles south of Spring Hill and connected by bus and taxi services.
  • Hernando County Transit: Offers local bus service to and from Spring Hill.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: This is the most popular option to enjoy the best of the coast and explore places around Spring Hill.
  • Taxi Services: Locals recommend locally owned taxi companies for getting around without your own car.

How to Get Around in Spring Hill

The easiest way to get around Spring Hill is by car. The town is easy to navigate, and there are plentiful parking lots and parking areas along the beaches, natural attractions and parks.

Where to Go Next from Spring Hill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Clearwater Beach: Only 1.5-hour drive from Spring Hill, a world-famous beach with crystal clear waters, white sand, water activities, and lively nightlife scene.
  • Tampa: Located 50 miles south of Spring Hill, offering museums, an aquarium, amusement parks, a zoo, nightlife, and more.
  • St. Petersburg: Located 60 miles south of Spring Hill, it has a lively downtown, shopping districts, and offers both museums and beaches.

Conclusion

Spring Hill is a hidden gem of natural beauty giving a laid-back and laid-back Florida atmosphere. Plan your trip with this guide and enjoy your Florida Gulf Coast adventure!
