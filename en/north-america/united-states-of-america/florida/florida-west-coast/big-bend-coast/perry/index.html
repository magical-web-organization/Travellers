Introduction to Perry

Perry is a small city located on the western coast of Florida. Known for its breathtaking natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, this hidden gem is the perfect destination for those who want to explore the outdoors and immerse themselves in local history and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Perry

Whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, admiring stunning views, trying out thrilling outdoor activities, or savoring delicious local cuisine, Perry has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions worth visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Forest Capital Museum State Park: This museum showcases the history and culture of Perry and the Big Bend region. Exhibits include vintage cars, antique tools, and Native American artifacts.
  • Old Perry Post Office: Built-in 1911, this historic building is now a museum that showcases the history of the U.S. Postal Service in Perry and the surrounding areas.
  • Taylor County Historical Society Museum: Located in a former jail, this museum features exhibits on the early history of Perry and Taylor County, including Native American artifacts, early settlers’ implements, and antique firearms.

Natural Attractions of Perry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Econfina River State Park: This park is located along the Econfina River, and visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, swimming, and birdwatching.
  • Keaton Beach: Featuring white-sand beaches and picturesque views, Keaton Beach is the perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Steinhatchee Falls: This 8-foot waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and offers a tranquil escape for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perry

Perry boasts several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer the perfect opportunity to explore the area’s natural beauty. Some of the top routes include:

  • Big Oak Trail: This 0.7-mile trail features a massive, ancient oak tree and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest.
  • Florida National Scenic Trail: This 1,300-mile trail spans the entire length of Florida and passes through Perry, offering hikers and bikers breathtaking views of the state’s diverse ecosystems.
  • Lafayette Blue Springs State Park: This park offers several hiking and biking trails that wind through dense forests, providing ample opportunities to spot wildlife and admire stunning natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Perry: Activities and Tours

  • Gulf Specimen Marine Laboratory: Visitors can take a tour of this marine laboratory and see a variety of local marine species up close, including octopuses, starfish, and snails.
  • Sea Hag Marina: This marina offers deep-sea fishing charters, dolphin-watching tours, and sunset cruises.
  • Taylor County Sports Complex: Sports enthusiasts can enjoy playing baseball, basketball, tennis, and soccer at this state-of-the-art sports complex.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perry

Day 1: Visit the Forest Capital Museum State Park and Old Perry Post Office Museum Day 2: Hike the Big Oak Trail and spend some time at Keaton Beach Day 3: Explore the Gulf Specimen Marine Laboratory and take a sunset cruise from Sea Hag Marina Day 4: Visit the Taylor County Historical Society Museum and enjoy a picnic lunch at Steinhatchee Falls Day 5: Play your favorite sports at Taylor County Sports Complex and grab lunch at a local restaurant Day 6: Go fishing at Econfina River and enjoy a relaxing day on the riverbank Day 7: Take a guided tour of the Florida National Scenic Trail and enjoy the stunning views along the way.

Where to Stay in Perry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perry offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Perry Cottage Bed & Breakfast: This charming guesthouse features cozy rooms and home-style breakfast.
  • Budget Inn: This budget-friendly option offers basic amenities and easy access to local attractions.

  • Artwork and pottery made by local artists
  • Homemade jams and jellies

Local Culture and Customs of Perry

Perry has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply influenced by its Native American and Southern roots. Visitors are encouraged to explore local museums and historical landmarks to learn more about the area’s past and present.

Best Time to Visit Perry

The best time to visit Perry is from March to May when the weather is mild and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perry

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area

  • Go off-trail in protected areas
  • Leave valuables in your car

Safety and Health

Safety in Perry

Perry is generally safe, but visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions to avoid petty theft and other crimes.

Health in Perry

It’s always recommended to check with a healthcare provider before traveling. Visitors to Perry should be aware of potential health risks from extreme heat and humidity and take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Perry: Transportation Options and Logistics

Perry is easily accessible by air or road. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Perry

  • Tallahassee International Airport
  • Gainesville Regional Airport
  • Jacksonville International Airport

Bus Stations Near Perry

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Tallahassee
  • Jefferson Lines Bus Station in Gainesville

Other Options

  • Driving: Perry is located off U.S. Highway 19.
  • Train: The nearest Amtrak station is in Micanopy, about 80 miles from Perry.

How to Get Around in Perry

Visitors can get around Perry by car, bike, or on foot. Several car rental agencies are located in the area, and there are also several bike rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Perry: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perry is located in an area that offers a wealth of nearby excursions and destinations. Here are some options:

  • Tallahassee: The state capital of Florida is just over an hour’s drive from Perry and offers plenty of cultural attractions and historical landmarks.
  • Suwannee River State Park: This park is located about 45 minutes from Perry and offers fishing, kayaking, and hiking opportunities.
  • Crystal River National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge is located about two hours from Perry and offers opportunities to see manatees and dolphins in their natural habitat.

Conclusion

Perry is a hidden gem that offers a blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, exploring local museums and historical landmarks, or simply enjoying the local cuisine, Perry has something for everyone.
