Introduction to Clearwater

Clearwater, located in the Tampa Bay Area of Florida’s West Coast, is a vibrant coastal city known for its stunning beaches, natural attractions, and impressive food and drink scene. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone in Clearwater.

List of Places to Visit in Clearwater

If you’re visiting Clearwater, here’s a list of the top must-visit places in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clearwater Marine Aquarium: A must-visit for animal lovers, Clearwater Marine Aquarium is home to dolphins, otters, and other marine life.
  • Heritage Village: This living history museum gives visitors an insight into Florida’s past and features traditional 19th-century buildings.
  • Dunedin Historical Society and Museum: Located in the nearby town of Dunedin, this museum showcases the history and culture of the area.

Natural Attractions of Clearwater: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clearwater Beach: With miles of sugar-white sand and turquoise blue waters, Clearwater Beach is considered one of the best beaches in the United States.
  • Caladesi Island State Park: Accessible only by boat, this unspoiled barrier island features nature trails, quiet beaches, and excellent birdwatching opportunities.
  • Honeymoon Island State Park: Located near Dunedin, Honeymoon Island offers visitors an opportunity to spot dolphins, sea turtles and other marine life, while hiking, biking, or kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clearwater

Clearwater offers many scenic trails and routes for hiking and biking. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Fred Marquis Pinellas Trail: Covering 38 miles from Tarpon Spring to St. Petersburg, this trail offers stunning views of the Gulf Coast and plenty of opportunities to spot wildlife.
  • Clearwater Beach Walk: This scenic two-mile trail runs along the beachfront, and is a great spot for a leisurely walk or bike ride.
  • Moccasin Lake Nature Park: This park features a nature trail that takes visitors through a variety of ecosystems, including a pine forest, a swamp, and a lake.

Best Things to Do in Clearwater: Activities and Tours

Clearwater is home to a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Clearwater:

  • Dolphin Watching Tour: Take a boat tour to spot bottlenose dolphins as they swim and play in the waters off Clearwater Beach.
  • Parasailing: For a bird’s eye view of the city, try parasailing over Clearwater Beach.
  • Sunset Cruise: Set sail with a sunset cruise, and witness the stunning colors of the sunset as they reflect off the waters of Tampa Bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clearwater

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Clearwater, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1 Arrive and Check-in
Day 2 Morning at Clearwater Marine Aquarium. Afternoon and Evening at Clearwater Beach
Day 3 Day Trip to Caladesi Island State Park
Day 4 Morning at Honeymoon Island, Afternoon at Dunedin Historical Society and Museum. Evening at Downtown Clearwater
Day 5 Hiking/Biking: Fred Marquis Pinellas Trail
Day 6 Take a Dolphin Watching Tour, and spend the evening on a Sunset Cruise
Day 7 Visit Moccasin Lake Nature Park, Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Clearwater

Clearwater has excellent restaurants and bars to cater to different tastes and preferences. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in the city.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grouper Sandwich: One of the signature dishes of the Gulf Coast, this sandwich features a fresh fillet of grouper, seasoned, and served on a bun with lettuce, tomato, and tartar sauce.
  • Cuban Sandwich: A popular sandwich in Florida, the Cuban sandwich features ham, roasted pork, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard on Cuban bread.
  • Key Lime Pie: A Florida classic, Key Lime Pie is a tangy and refreshing dessert made with lime juice, sweetened condensed milk, and a graham cracker crust.

Local Beverages

  • Florida Orange Juice: Known for being the sunshine state, Florida is famous for its fresh and sweet orange juice.
  • Craft Beers: Clearwater has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering delicious and unique beers. Check out the Clearwater Brewing Company and Dunedin Brewery for some excellent brews.

Where to Stay in Clearwater: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clearwater has a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hyatt Regency Clearwater Beach Resort and Spa: Located directly on the beach, this luxury hotel offers excellent amenities and stunning views of the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Pier House 60 Clearwater Beach Marina Hotel: A mid-range hotel situated near the beach that offers excellent value for money.
  • Barefoot Bay Resort and Marina: A budget-friendly option, this hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, with a pool and marina views.

What to Buy in Clearwater: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some of the best places to shop in Clearwater:

  • Sunset at Pier 60: Located on Clearwater Beach, Sunset at Pier 60 is a nightly festival featuring arts and craft vendors, street performers, and live music.
  • Downtown Clearwater Farmers Market: Held on Saturdays, this market features local farmers and artisans, offering fresh produce and handmade crafts.
  • Clearwater Marine Aquarium Gift Shop: The gift shop at Clearwater Marine Aquarium offers a variety of merchandise featuring animals that live in the aquarium, such as dolphins, otters, and sea turtles.

Local Culture and Customs of Clearwater

Clearwater’s cultural identity is shaped by its proximity to the Gulf of Mexico, and its history and natural surroundings. The city has a relaxed vibe, with laid-back locals who are friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Clearwater

The best time to visit Clearwater is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the temperatures are mild, and the crowds are thinner. The summer months (June to August) are the busiest, with high temperatures and frequent thunderstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clearwater

Do:

  • Do follow the beach rules, such as cleaning up after yourself and not walking on the dunes.
  • Do try the local seafood, especially the grouper sandwich, which is a must-try dish when in Clearwater.
  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and water sports.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to use sunscreen and drink plenty of water, as the sun can be intense in Florida.
  • Don’t touch or feed the wildlife, including dolphins and other marine animals.
  • Don’t litter on the beach or in the parks. Clearwater is known for its pristine beaches, so preserving them is essential for the local economy and environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clearwater

Clearwater is a safe city, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise regular safety precautions, such as keeping valuables locked up and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Clearwater

Clearwater has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage for their trip.

Getting to Clearwater: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clearwater is located about 30 minutes from Tampa International Airport and about 90 minutes from Orlando International Airport. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Clearwater

  • Tampa International Airport: Located about 20 miles east of Clearwater, Tampa International Airport is the closest major airport to the city.
  • St. Petersburg-Clearwater International Airport: Located about 10 miles from downtown Clearwater, this airport offers regional flights to major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Clearwater

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Clearwater, this station offers regional and intercity bus services.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental companies such as Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise have outlets in the Tampa Bay Area, including at the airports.

How to Get Around in Clearwater

Clearwater is easy to navigate by car, with plenty of parking options available. Alternatively, visitors can use ride-hail services such as Uber or Lyft, or rent a bike or scooter to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Clearwater: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clearwater is an excellent base for exploring other destinations in the Tampa Bay Area and beyond. Here are some of the top places to visit:

  • St. Petersburg: Located about 30 minutes from Clearwater, St. Petersburg is a vibrant city known for its museums, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Tampa: Located about an hour from Clearwater, Tampa is a bustling city known for its theme parks, museums, and nightlife.
  • Orlando: Located about 90 minutes from Clearwater, Orlando is home to world-famous theme parks, including Walt Disney World Resort and Universal Orlando Resort.

Conclusion

Clearwater is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and excellent food and drink. Whether you’re a family, couple, or solo traveler, there’s plenty to see and do in this vibrant coastal city. Plan your trip today, and experience the best of Clearwater.
