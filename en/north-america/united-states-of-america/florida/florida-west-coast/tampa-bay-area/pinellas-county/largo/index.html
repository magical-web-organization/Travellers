Introduction to Largo

Largo, a city in Pinellas County, is located in the Tampa Bay area of the Florida West Coast. This hidden gem is full of excitement and adventure ideal for both families and solo travelers. Its tranquil sands and pristine waters provide a welcome escape from the hustle and bustle of metropolitan life.

List of Places to Visit in Largo

Largo offers tourists the perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, world-class hotels, and unique experiences. Here are a few must-visit attractions when you visit Largo.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Heritage Village: A quaint step back in time, Heritage Village is a living history museum composed of authentic buildings & artifacts in Largo, FL. Visitors can explore the preserved historic buildings that showcase life during the early 20th century.

  • Florida Holocaust museum: A vital and moving memorial to the six million Jews who perished at the hands of the Nazis, the museum features moving exhibits and educational events for all ages.

### Natural Attractions of Largo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Indian Rocks Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Florida, Indian Rocks Beach features soft sands, turquoise waters, and an array of amenities such as volleyball courts, a children’s playground, and BBQ pits.

  • Largo Central Park: A peaceful and serene oasis, Largo Central Park, is a sprawling park with 70 acres of natural beauty. The park offers walking trails, playgrounds, and open fields for weekend picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Largo

If you are an adventure enthusiast, Largo’s hiking and biking trails offer the perfect terrain to explore the city. Here are the top spots for outdoor exploration.

  • Pinellas Trail: The perfect destination for nature lovers, Pinellas Trail is a 38-mile long trail that extends through Largo and St. Petersburg. The trail offers a blend of scenic beauty, fitness, and recreational opportunities, making it ideal for hiking and biking.

  • Walsingham Park: Covering over 350 acres, Walsingham Park offers an endless network of wetland trails, a lake, two dog parks, and a remarkable bird sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Largo: Activities and Tours

Largo offers many activities and tours for the adventurous and thrill-seekers. Here are some of the best things to do in Largo.

  • Camping at the Pinellas County Remembrance Park: Located between the Gulf of Mexico and Tampa Bay on 16 acres of beautiful preserve land, the Pinellas County Remembrance Park offers an authentic camping experience with fishing, hiking, and paddling options.

Food and Drink Scene of Largo

Largo’s food and drink scene offer an exciting range of locally sourced seafood and traditional dishes from around the US. Here are some of the best dishes to try and the local beverages to sip on in Largo.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: A coastal delicacy, crab cakes in Largo are especially noteworthy due to the abundance of fresh seafood available in the area.

  • Cuban Sandwiches: A giant sandwich with roasted pork, ham, Swiss cheese, and dill pickles served on Cuban bread; foodies swear that its one of the best in the State.

Local Beverages

  • Tampa Bay IPA: The Tampa Bay area is home to a great selection of locally crafted, delicious beers, the Tampa Bay IPA being the most popular among them.

Where to Stay in Largo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Largo offers various accommodation options from popular brand hotels to family-owned guesthouses and cozy vacation rentals. Here are the top accommodation options in Largo.

  • Hampton Inn and Suites: The Hampton Inn, located in the heart of Largo, offers bright and comfy rooms with complimentary breakfast, excellent amenities, and courteous staff.

  • Sheraton Sand Key Resort: The Sheraton Sand Key Resort offers an expansive pool station, private white sand beach, spa, and restaurants. The rooms boast tropical decor and stunning views.

What to Buy in Largo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souvenirs are the best way to remember your trip to Largo. Here is a list of local markets, stores, and products to look out for when exploring Largo.

  • The Art Shoppe at the Quayside Gallery: The Art Shoppe is a non-profit gallery and art center featuring an extensive collection of local artwork available for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Largo

Largo’s culture is a mix of the traditional and modern. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. It’s essential to know a few customs to show respect and enjoy your stay in Largo.

Best Time to Visit Largo

The best time to visit Largo is from November to May when the weather is at its best with sunny skies and low humidity levels.

Do’s and Don’ts of Largo

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities including hiking, biking, and kayaking.

  • Always try the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter. Largo has many natural wonders that need to be preserved.

Safety and Health

Safety in Largo

Largo is a safe and stable destination for tourists. However, it’s advisable to mind your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Largo

Largo has a reliable healthcare system with many excellent hospitals and clinics. It’s advisable to carry your insurance documents when seeking medical assistance.

Getting to Largo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Largo

  • Tampa International Airport: Tampa International Airport, located 18 miles southeast of Largo, is a major regional airport that offers both domestic and international flights from various airlines.

Bus Stations Near Largo

  • Greyhound: Greyhound is a reliable bus service that operates nationally and makes several stops in Largo.

Other Options

  • Uber and Lyft: These ride-sharing apps are easy and affordable options for getting around Largo.

How to Get Around in Largo

Largo is a compact city ideal for exploring on foot or by rental bike or car.

Where to Go Next from Largo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Largo is within easy reach of other tourist destinations in Florida, such as Clearwater, St. Petersburg, and Tampa Bay. All of these nearby locations offer a range of activities and entertainment options for visitors.

Conclusion

Whether you’re visiting Largo for its beautiful beaches, hiking trails, or the historical city center, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your trip. Largo’s charm, casual vibe, and welcoming locals guarantee a truly unforgettable experience.
