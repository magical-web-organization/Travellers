Introduction to Safety Harbor

Safety Harbor, located in Pinellas County, is a charming town on the west coast of Florida. This town is known for its historic resorts, beautiful parks, and natural hot springs that have been attracting tourists for years. Safety Harbor also has a rich history and is home to several historical places and museums. Here’s everything you need to know to plan a perfect trip to Safety Harbor.

List of Places to Visit in Safety Harbor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Safety Harbor Museum and Cultural Center - This museum highlights the history and culture of Safety Harbor, which includes exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • Tocobaga Native American Mound - This is a historical landmark that dates back to the 16th century and is believed to be a burial ground for the Tocobaga tribe.
  • Philippe Park - This historic site was once the home of Count Odet Philippe, who is known as the “Father of Safety Harbor.” The park has several picnic areas, a playground, fishing piers, and trails.

Natural Attractions of Safety Harbor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Safety Harbor Spa Park - This beautiful park is known for its natural hot springs and provides a picturesque view. The park also has a spa that offers various treatments and massages.
  • Old Tampa Bay - This bay provides beautiful views of Tampa Bay and is a great spot for kayaking, sailing, and fishing.
  • John Wilson Park - This park is located in the heart of Safety Harbor and is famous for its majestic trees and the fountain called the “Unity Tree.”

Hiking/Biking Routes of Safety Harbor

  • The Ream Wilson Trail - This trail stretches for 8 miles, starting from Safety Harbor and ending at North Bay Trail. It’s a great spot for biking and hiking.
  • Bayshore Linear Park Trail - This trail is just over a mile long and is a great spot for a scenic stroll or biking.

Best Things to Do in Safety Harbor: Activities and Tours

  • Safety Harbor Spa - The spa has several treatments like massages, facials, and body wraps.
  • Sunset at Philippe Park - The park provides a beautiful view of the sunset over Old Tampa Bay, making it a perfect spot for picnics, walks, or biking.
  • Safety Harbor Farmers Market - This farmer’s market is held every Sunday and has stalls that sell fresh produce, arts and crafts, and handmade items.

Week-Long Itinerary for Safety Harbor

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Safety Harbor Museum and Cultural Center, have lunch at Old Bay Cafe, and spend the evening at Safety Harbor Spa.
Day 2 Go biking/hiking along the Ream Wilson Trail, have lunch at Crooked Thumb Brewery, and enjoy an evening at John Wilson Park.
Day 3 Start the day at the Safety Harbor Farmers Market, go kayaking in Old Tampa Bay, and spend the evening enjoying a sunset at Philippe Park.
Day 4 Visit the Tocobaga Native American Mound and learn about the history of this town, have lunch at Southern Fresh, and spend the evening at Safety Harbor Spa.
Day 5 Have breakfast at Tiffany’s, spend the day at the beach, and enjoy dinner at Whistle Stop Grill and Bar.
Day 6 Visit Safety Harbor Art and Music Center, have lunch at Brady’s Backyard BBQ, and spend the evening at Crooked Thumb Brewery.
Day 7 Go on a dolphin watching tour, have lunch at Parts of Paris, and end the day strolling around Old Main Street.

Food and Drink Scene of Safety Harbor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grouper Sandwich - A classic Florida sandwich, made with fresh grouper fillet, seasoned with lemon and herbs, and served with fries.
  • Guava Cheese Pie - A delicious dessert made with guava and cream cheese filling.
  • Crab Cakes - Delicious cakes made with fresh crab meat, breadcrumbs, and spices, served with a side of salad.

Local Beverages

  • Coppertail Night Swim Porter - A local favorite beer, made with hints of chocolate and coffee.
  • Margarita - A classic cocktail, typically served with fresh lime and tequila, a perfect drink to enjoy during the hot summer months.
  • Key Lime Pie Martini - A delicious Martini that is made with vanilla vodka, lime juice, and simple syrup, garnished with a graham cracker rim.

Where to Stay in Safety Harbor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Safety Harbor Resort and Spa - A historic resort that features a spa, restaurant, and several activities like golf, tennis, and guided tours.
  • Candlewood Suites Clearwater - A budget-friendly hotel that provides several amenities like a pool, fitness center, and laundry facilities.
  • Harbor Palms Nature Resort and Conference Center - A hotel that features several amenities like a pool, hot tub, free Wi-Fi, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Safety Harbor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewelry - Several stores in Safety Harbor sell handmade jewelry that is unique and beautiful.
  • Artwork - Safety Harbor is home to several artists, and their works can be found in local galleries and art stores.
  • Citrus Products - Safety Harbor is known for its citrus fruit, and several local stores sell citrus products like orange marmalade and grapefruit lip balm.

Local Culture and Customs of Safety Harbor

Safety Harbor is a welcoming town that values community and tradition. Residents are friendly and accommodating to visitors, and they are proud of their town’s history and culture. Visitors should respect the town’s culture and customs by being respectful and courteous to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Safety Harbor

The best time to visit Safety Harbor is between November and April, where the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months tend to be hot and humid, making outdoor activities less enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Safety Harbor

Do:

  • Be courteous and respectful to the locals.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities like biking, kayaking, and strolling in the parks.
  • Try the local cuisine and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or vandalize public property.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in public places.
  • Disrespect the town’s history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Safety Harbor

Safety Harbor is a safe town, and visitors can explore the town without worrying about their safety. However, visitors should still take precautions like locking their car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Safety Harbor

Safety Harbor has several medical facilities like hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies that provide medical services to visitors and residents. Visitors should also remember to drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Safety Harbor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Safety Harbor

  • Tampa International Airport - 19 miles from Safety Harbor
  • St. Pete-Clearwater International Airport - 8 miles from Safety Harbor

Bus Stations Near Safety Harbor

  • Greyhound Bus Station - 17 miles from Safety Harbor
  • Pinellas Suncoast Transit Authority - 2 miles from Safety Harbor

Other Options

  • Uber/Lyft - Available in Safety Harbor.
  • Car Rental Companies - Several car rental companies are available at the airports near Safety Harbor.

How to Get Around in Safety Harbor

The best way to get around Safety Harbor is by car, and several car rental companies are available at the airports near Safety Harbor. Visitors can also use ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft. Biking and walking are also good options since the town has several biking and walking trails.

Where to Go Next from Safety Harbor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Clearwater Beach - Just 15 miles away from Safety Harbor is Clearwater Beach, which is known for its white sandy beaches, clear waters, and exciting activities.
  • St. Petersburg - A 30-minute drive from Safety Harbor is St. Petersburg, which is known for its museums, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Tarpon Springs - A 40-minute drive from Safety Harbor is Tarpon Springs, which is known for its Greek heritage, sponge docks, and seafood.

Conclusion

Safety Harbor is a charming town with historical landmarks, natural springs, beautiful parks, and several outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy their stay in the town by exploring its cultural and natural attractions, trying local cuisine, and taking part in outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide contains everything you need to know to plan a perfect trip to Safety Harbor, Florida.
