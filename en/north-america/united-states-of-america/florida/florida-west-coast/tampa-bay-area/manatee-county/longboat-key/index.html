Introduction to Longboat Key

Nestled in Manatee County, Longboat Key is a paradise for those who seek a serene and picturesque getaway. This barrier island, situated in the Tampa Bay Area, is indeed a breathtaking haven that attracts travelers from all over the world. Known for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and tropical climate, Longboat Key offers plenty of fun-filled activities for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just to relax and unwind, Longboat Key has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Longboat Key

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Longboat Key boasts an array of culturally significant museums and historical sites that showcase its rich history and heritage. The Longboat Key Center for the Arts is one of the popular cultural hubs that houses galleries, workshops, and exhibitions showcasing the works of local artists. The Mar Vista Historical Society, an old schoolhouse, has been transformed into a museum showcasing the island’s history and cultural heritage. Additionally, the Mote Marine Laboratory and Aquarium, a world-renowned research center, offers visitors an opportunity to discover the fascinating marine life that thrives in the Gulf of Mexico.

Natural Attractions of Longboat Key: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures and scenic beauty, Longboat Key has plenty to offer. Head to the Bayfront Park and Recreation Center, a 32-acre recreation site with picnic areas, tennis courts, and playgrounds along Sarasota Bay. Take a stroll along the boardwalk at Joan M. Durante Park and catch stunning views of mangroves, tidal creeks, and wildlife. For a more daring adventure, rent a kayak or stand-up paddleboard and explore the calm waters of the Gulf of Mexico.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longboat Key

Longboat Key also boasts various scenic hiking and biking trails that offer awe-inspiring views of the island’s natural beauty. The Islandside Golf course trail is a tranquil route that takes you through the heart of Longboat Key, showcasing its lush greenery and golf courses. The Coquina Beach Trail, on the other hand, meanders through the natural beauty of the Longboat Key Bayfront Park and Recreation Center and is perfect for a leisurely stroll.

## Best Things to Do in Longboat Key: Activities and Tours

In addition to museums, historical sites, and outdoor adventures, Longboat Key offers various activities and tours that give visitors an insight into the island’s culture and beauty. Take a sunset sail or eco-tour with LeBarge Tropical Cruises and witness dolphins frolic in the Gulf of Mexico. For a romantic dining experience, dine aboard Mar Vista Dockside Restaurant and Pub, a 100-year-old building that offers panoramic views of Sarasota Bay. For a truly unique experience, take a guided kayak tour to explore the mangrove tunnels and see exotic wildlife in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Longboat Key

For a week-long itinerary, we recommend starting with a visit to the Longboat Key Cultural Center, followed by a leisurely day at the Coquina Beach. On day three, indulge in some local cuisine at The Dry Dock Waterfront Grill, followed by a kayaking adventure to explore the island’s natural beauty on day 4. Day 5 is perfect for exploring the island’s historical and cultural gems with visits to historic sites such as the Longbeach Village and Mote Marine Laboratory and Aquarium. On day 6, take a sunset sail, and end your trip with a relaxing spa day at The Spa at the Resort at Longboat Key Club.

Food and Drink Scene of Longboat Key

Must-Try Dishes

Longboat Key’s food scene is a delicious blend of fresh seafood, international cuisine, and locally sourced ingredients. For a sumptuous dining experience, try the yellowtail snapper at the Dry Dock Waterfront Grill. The Sandbar restaurant is known for its stuffed flounder and seafood platter, while the Lazy Lobster serves delicious Maine Lobster. The Shore restaurant offers a perfect fusion of Amish-style cuisine and fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

Longboat Key is home to some of the best breweries and wineries in Florida. Head to the Siesta Key Rum Distillery for a tour of their facility and taste a range of award-winning rums. The Island Time Brewing Company offers a range of craft beers perfect for sipping while enjoying the island’s scenic beauty. For a sophisticated wine experience, visit the Park City Wine Cellar, located on Longboat Key.

Where to Stay in Longboat Key: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Longboat Key has a range of options for accommodations, ranging from luxurious resorts to quaint guesthouses. For an unforgettable stay, The Resort at Longboat Key Club offers a prime location, upscale amenities, and exceptional services. The Diplomat Beach Club Resort, Zota Beach Resort, and the Sea Club Resort are other excellent options. For budget-friendly options, try the Gulfside Beach Resort or the Sandpiper Inn.

What to Buy in Longboat Key: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Longboat Key offers an array of unique souvenirs and local products to take home as mementos of your trip. Visit Cannons Marina to purchase maritime memorabilia or the Resort at Longboat Key gift shop for retail therapy. The Longboat Hardware is the perfect place to buy locally sourced hardware, souvenirs, and clothing items. For fresh produce, visit the nearby farmer’s market which operates every Sunday at the Bridge Street Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Longboat Key

Longboat Key observes a laid-back island lifestyle similar to most coastal towns. Sun dresses, shorts, and flip-flops are the norm, and dress codes are relatively casual in most restaurants. The island also invites adventure seekers to explore its natural beauty and wildlife and partake in beachfront activities.

Best Time to Visit Longboat Key

The best time to visit Longboat Key is from February to May when the temperatures are mild, and the crowds are thin. The island’s peak season is from December to January, and if you prefer a quieter vacation, avoid visiting during those months. Summer and fall are monsoon seasons, and while the crowds are thinner, thunderstorms and high humidity are common during those seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longboat Key

Do:

  • Respect the island’s ecotourism and protected wildlife.
  • Bring along sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water when indulging in outdoor activities under the sun.
  • Observe the island’s strict turtle nesting restrictions rules during nesting season from May 1st to October 31st.

Don’t:

  • Swim in private areas.
  • Litter or leave trash on the beach or around public parks.
  • Disrupt the island’s wildlife and natural ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Longboat Key

Longboat Key is a relatively safe island with low crime rates. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions such as locking their vehicles and being aware of their surroundings when out at night.

Health in Longboat Key

Longboat Key is a safe place to visit, and there are no major health concerns. However, if travelers are allergic to pollen or mold, it’s best to visit during dry seasons.

Getting to Longboat Key: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Longboat Key

The closest commercial airports to Longboat Key are Sarasota-Bradenton International Airport and Tampa International Airport, which are about 15 miles and 55 miles away from the island, respectively.

Bus Stations Near Longboat Key

Greyhound and Megabus have bus stations in Sarasota, which is about a 25-minute drive from Longboat Key.

Other Options

Uber and Lyft both operate on Longboat Key, making it easy to get around the island and its surrounding neighborhoods.

How to Get Around in Longboat Key

The most popular way to get around Longboat Key is by biking, walking, or taking the complimentary trolleys that run the length of the island year-round. There are also several rental car companies on the island.

Where to Go Next from Longboat Key: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Longboat Key is a perfect hub for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions. Head to nearby Sarasota for art and cultural experiences, including the Ringling Museum of Art and Sarasota Opera. The nearby Anna Maria Island offers stunning beaches, seafood festivals, and shops to browse through. For those seeking adventure, head to Myakka River State Park, known for its airboat tours and wildlife sightings.

Conclusion

Longboat Key offers visitors an idyllic vacation paradise with stunning scenery, outdoor adventures, cultural institutions, and delicious food. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore the island’s historical sites, or indulge in water sports and adventure, there’s something for everyone. So what are you waiting for? Pack your bags and head to Longboat Key for the ultimate tropical island getaway.
