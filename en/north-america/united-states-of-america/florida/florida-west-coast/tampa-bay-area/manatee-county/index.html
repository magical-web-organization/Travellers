Welcome to Manatee County, the charming region located in the Tampa Bay Area of Florida’s West Coast. With a rich history, diverse culture, and natural beauty, this is the perfect destination for travelers seeking both adventure and relaxation. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around in Manatee County, Florida.

Introduction to Manatee County

Manatee County, named after the peaceful marine mammals found in the region’s waterways, is located in the Tampa Bay Area of Florida’s West Coast. The county seat is in Bradenton, which is also the largest city, and home to many of the area’s attractions. The county has a rich history, from the Native American tribes to the Spanish conquistadors, and is a unique melting pot of cultures, including Hispanic, African American and Native American.

Manatee County’s coastline stretches for more than 28 miles and includes a variety of beaches, marinas, and parks, making it one of the most popular tourist destinations in Florida.

List of Places to Visit in Manatee County

Manatee County offers a variety of attractions for tourists interested in culture, history, and nature-loving adventurers, making it the perfect destination for all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the top attractions you don’t want to miss when you visit Manatee County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Manatee County has a rich history, and there are numerous museums and historical sites that are worth visiting to learn more about the area’s past. Some of the top attractions include:

  • South Florida Museum & Bishop Planetarium: Learn about the area’s natural history, including information about the world’s oldest living manatee, Snooty, who lived at the museum until his passing in 2017.
  • De Soto National Memorial: This park commemorates Hernando De Soto’s landing in Florida in the 16th century, and it includes a historical trail, picnic areas, and a visitors’ center.
  • Manatee Village Historical Park: This living history museum features historic homes, a church, a general store, and a schoolhouse, allowing visitors to experience the daily lives of early settlers in the area.

Natural Attractions of Manatee County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor and nature enthusiasts, Manatee County offers a variety of attractions and activities to explore the local environment. Some of the top options include:

  • Robinson Nature Preserve: Explore wetlands, mangroves, and waterways while walking, kayaking, or biking on one of the many trails in this 487-acre nature preserve.
  • Emerson Point Preserve: This beautiful scenic spot features hiking trails, a fishing pier, and a historic Native American temple mound.
  • Anna Maria Island: This popular barrier island offers stunning white-sand beaches, crystal clear waters for snorkeling and diving, and picturesque sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manatee County

Manatee County is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The region features many natural trails, as well as urban paths that allow visitors to explore the downtown and surrounding areas. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Manatee County include:

  • Manatee Riverwalk: This 1.5-mile path runs along the Manatee River and offers stunning views of the water and the area’s skyline.
  • Robinson Preserve: With over 8 miles of trails, this preserve offers a variety of routes to explore, from leisurely strolls to challenging hikes.
  • Lake Manatee State Park: This state park offers over 5 miles of hiking trails through forests and by the lake.

Best Things to Do in Manatee County: Activities and Tours

Manatee County offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Some of the top options include:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour to explore the local waterways and see dolphins, manatees, and other marine wildlife up close.
  • Golfing: With dozens of golf courses in the area, Manatee County is a golfer’s paradise.
  • Festivals and Events: There are many seasonal festivals and events that take place in Manatee County throughout the year, offering entertainment and insight into the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manatee County

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to Manatee County:

  • Day 1: Visit South Florida Museum & Bishop Planetarium and explore the downtown area restaurants and cafes.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour or go fishing at one of the marinas, and end the day with a beautiful sunset at Anna Maria Island.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Robinson Nature Preserve for a hiking and biking adventure.
  • Day 4: Explore the local culture at Manatee Village Historical Park and the Bradenton Farmer’s Market.
  • Day 5: Visit the De Soto National Memorial and spend some time at Emerson Point Preserve.
  • Day 6: Play golf at one of the area’s top golf courses or attend a seasonal festival or event.
  • Day 7: Relax at one of the beautiful beaches, such as Holmes Beach, and enjoy a local seafood dinner at a waterfront restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Manatee County

Manatee County’s thriving food and drink scene is full of delicious, locally-sourced and cooked dishes, as well as beverages made from the region’s fresh fruits. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stone Crab: This local delicacy is available from October to May and is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Pulled Pork: Slow-cooked to perfection, this BBQ staple is a true southern treat.
  • Gator Bites: This unique dish is a local favorite made with alligator meat.

Local Beverages

  • Oranges: The region’s fertile soil produces some of the juiciest and tastiest oranges in the country.
  • Cocktails: Try a refreshing cocktail made with fresh-squeezed juices and locally-produced spirits from shops like the Sarasota Liquor Locker.

Where to Stay in Manatee County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manatee County offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference, from luxury hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • Waterline Marina Resort & Beach Club: This upscale resort is located in Holmes Beach and offers beautiful waterfront views and an array of amenities.
  • Silver Surf Gulf Beach Resort: This family-friendly resort offers comfortable accommodations and direct access to the beach.
  • Bradenton Hostel: With affordable prices and a central location, this hostel is the perfect choice for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Manatee County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many unique and locally-made products to buy in Manatee County, making it easy to bring a small piece of this beautiful region back home with you. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Pottery by Made in Sarasota: Made in Sarasota is a local shop offering a range of handcrafted local pottery.
  • Cedar Key Sea Salt Company: This local company produces all-natural sea salt using water from the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Florida Orange Juice: A bottle of fresh-squeezed Florida orange juice is the perfect souvenir to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Manatee County

The culture of Manatee County is a unique blend of the area’s rich history, pre-Columbian influence, and modern-day amenities. Visitors to the region will find that locals are friendly and helpful, and the area’s culture emphasizes the importance of conservation and preserving the natural beauty of the local environment.

Best Time to Visit Manatee County

The best time to visit Manatee County is from November to April when the weather is cooler and less humid, making it ideal for outdoor activities. The summer months bring higher temperatures and humidity, making it a great time to enjoy the beaches and water activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manatee County

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and marine animals by keeping a safe distance while observing them.
  • Visit the local farmer’s markets for fresh produce and artisan products.
  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind; help keep the local environment clean and healthy.
  • Ignore warnings and postings for wildlife sightings or unsafe waters.
  • Overpack; Florida’s warm climate means light packing and breathable clothing is a must.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manatee County

Manatee County is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking their cars, hiding valuables, and being aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Manatee County

Visitors to Manatee County should be aware of the heat and humidity during the summer months and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated. Mosquitoes are prevalent throughout the region, so it’s important to use insect repellent and cover up to avoid bites. Visitors should also bring their sunscreen for outdoor activities.

Getting to Manatee County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manatee County is easily accessible, with several transportation options for visitors. Here are some of the best ways to get to Manatee County:

Airports Near Manatee County

  • Sarasota-Bradenton International Airport
  • Tampa International Airport
  • St. Petersburg-Clearwater International Airport

Bus Stations Near Manatee County

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Sarasota
  • Manatee County Area Transit

Other Options

  • Car rentals
  • Taxi service
  • Uber and Lyft

How to Get Around in Manatee County

While the area is quite spread out, there are still many transportation options available for visitors to Manatee County. Getting around by car is the best way to explore the area, but visitors can also use the Manatee County Area Transit buses. Biking and walking are also popular options for getting around, especially in the downtown areas.

Where to Go Next from Manatee County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Manatee County is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions, such as:

  • Sarasota: This vibrant city is just 30 minutes south of Manatee County and is full of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Tampa: Located about an hour north of Manatee County, Tampa is home to world-famous Busch Gardens and many other family-friendly attractions.
  • St. Petersburg: About an hour and a half from Manatee County, this coastal city is known for its vibrant arts scene and famous Salvador Dalí Museum.

Conclusion

Manatee County is Florida’s best-kept secret and an ideal destination for those seeking a unique adventure in a beautiful natural paradise. With a variety of attractions, outdoor activities, delicious cuisine, and diverse cultural experiences, Manatee County is sure to delight travelers of all ages and interests. Plan your trip today and prepare for an unforgettable experience!
