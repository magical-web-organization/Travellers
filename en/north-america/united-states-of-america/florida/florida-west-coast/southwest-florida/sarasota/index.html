Introduction to Sarasota

Sarasota, located in Southwest Florida, is a vibrant city known for its stunning beaches, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. With a rich history and a thriving arts scene, Sarasota is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an exciting vacation experience.

List of Places to Visit in Sarasota

From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures, there are plenty of things to see and do in Sarasota. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Sarasota:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The John and Mable Ringling Museum of Art: One of the city’s top attractions, this museum is home to an impressive collection of art and artifacts, as well as stunning gardens and architecture.

  • Sarasota Jungle Gardens: A fun and family-friendly attraction, the Sarasota Jungle Gardens is home to exotic plants and animals from around the world.

  • Historic Spanish Point: A fascinating look into the history of Sarasota, this site is home to several restored buildings from the early 20th century, as well as lush gardens and scenic views.

Natural Attractions of Sarasota: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Siesta Key Beach: Consistently ranked as one of the best beaches in the world, Siesta Key Beach is known for its soft white sand and turquoise waters.

  • Myakka River State Park: With over 58 square miles of wilderness and wildlife, Myakka River State Park is a fantastic destination for hiking, fishing, and wildlife spotting.

  • Lido Key Beach: Another beautiful beach destination, Lido Key Beach offers stunning views of the Gulf of Mexico and plenty of water activities like kayaking and paddleboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sarasota

Sarasota offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Legacy Trail: This 10-mile trail runs from Sarasota to Venice and is perfect for both cyclists and walkers.

  • Myakka River State Park: With over 40 miles of hiking trails, Myakka River State Park offers plenty of opportunities to explore the Florida wilderness.

  • Celery Fields: A popular spot for birdwatching, the Celery Fields also offers several hiking trails with scenic views of the surrounding wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Sarasota: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, Sarasota has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Sarasota:

  • Take a tour of the John and Mable Ringling Museum of Art.

  • Rent a kayak and explore the mangrove tunnels at Lido Key.

  • Enjoy a sunset cruise on Sarasota Bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sarasota

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Sarasota, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the John and Mable Ringling Museum of Art and explore the scenic gardens. Day 2: Spend the day at Siesta Key Beach. Day 3: Go for a hike in Myakka River State Park and rent a canoe for a scenic paddle. Day 4: Explore the shops and restaurants at St. Armands Circle. Day 5: Visit the Mote Marine Laboratory and Aquarium. Day 6: Take a sunset cruise on Sarasota Bay. Day 7: Relax at Lido Key Beach and watch the sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Sarasota

Sarasota’s food scene is diverse and delicious, with plenty of local specialties and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stone crab: A local delicacy, stone crab is harvested from the Gulf of Mexico and served with mustard sauce.

  • Cuban sandwich: A classic sandwich made with roast pork, ham, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard, the Cuban sandwich is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

  • Grouper sandwich: Another Gulf Coast specialty, grouper is a mild white fish that makes for the perfect sandwich filling.

Local Beverages

  • Siesta Key Rum: Made with sugar cane grown in Florida, Siesta Key Rum is a popular local spirit that’s perfect for sipping on a beach day.

  • Jai Alai IPA: Brewed by Cigar City Brewing in nearby Tampa, Jai Alai IPA is a hoppy and delicious beer that’s become a favorite of craft beer enthusiasts in Sarasota.

Where to Stay in Sarasota: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sarasota offers a range of accommodation options for every budget and preference. Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel on the beach or a cozy guesthouse in the city, there’s something for everyone. Here are some top picks:

  • The Ritz-Carlton Sarasota: A luxurious waterfront hotel with stunning views and a full-service spa.

  • The Sarasota Modern: A trendy boutique hotel with chic decor and a rooftop pool.

  • Sarasota Yacht Club: A charming guesthouse with nautical themed rooms and a cozy outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Sarasota: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or a gift for someone back home, Sarasota offers plenty of local products and artisanal goods. Here are some ideas:

  • Sarasota honey: Made with local honey from Sarasota’s beekeepers, this sweet treat is a great way to bring a taste of Florida home.

  • Handmade jewelry: Sarasota is home to many talented artisans who create beautiful jewelry using unique designs and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Sarasota

Sarasota is a welcoming and diverse city with a strong cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the local customs and traditions, and to embrace the laid-back and friendly atmosphere of the city.

Best Time to Visit Sarasota

The best time to visit Sarasota is between December and April, when the weather is mild and comfortable and the crowds are relatively low. May through November is the rainy season, and temperatures can get hot and humid during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sarasota

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the city’s cultural attractions and natural beauty.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, from stone crab to Siesta Key Rum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in the parks.

  • Don’t ignore the importance of sunscreen and hydration, especially during the hot summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sarasota

Sarasota is generally a safe and friendly city, but visitors should take standard precautions against theft and pickpocketing. It’s also important to be aware of the local wildlife, particularly alligators and snakes in natural areas.

Health in Sarasota

Visitors to Sarasota should drink plenty of water and stay well-hydrated during the hot summer months. Sunscreen and bug spray are also important, as is proper foot protection when hiking or exploring natural areas.

Getting to Sarasota: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sarasota is easily accessible by plane, car, and bus. Here are some transportation options for getting to and around the city:

Airports Near Sarasota

  • Sarasota-Bradenton International Airport.

  • Tampa International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Sarasota

  • Sarasota Greyhound Station.

Other Options

  • Car rentals.

How to Get Around in Sarasota

Sarasota is a walkable city, and many of the top attractions are within easy walking distance of one another. Other options for getting around include:

  • Trolley.

  • Taxi.

  • Bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Sarasota: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sarasota is an excellent base for exploring the Gulf Coast region of Florida. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • St. Petersburg: A vibrant city on the Gulf, known for its museums, galleries, and public art.

  • Anna Maria Island: A charming barrier island with pristine beaches and a laid-back vibe.

Conclusion

Sarasota has something for everyone, from beautiful beaches to cultural attractions to outdoor adventures. Whether you’re visiting for a weekend or a week, this vibrant city is sure to leave you with great memories and a desire to return again and again.
