Introduction to Venice

Welcome to Venice, a hidden gem in Southwest Florida’s Florida West Coast. Known for its beautiful beaches, rich history, and unique culture, Venice is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Sunshine State. This comprehensive travel guide will explore the top places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, and more in Venice.

List of Places to Visit in Venice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Venice Museum and Archives: Learn about the rich history of Venice and its surrounding areas through photographs, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • Venice Area Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the pioneer life of the early settlers in Venice.
  • Venice Performing Arts Center: Catch amazing performances and concerts in this state-of-the-art theater.

Natural Attractions of Venice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Venice Beach: When in Venice, a visit to the beach is a must. Relax on the sandy shores or take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Oscar Scherer State Park: The park offers several trails for hiking, biking, and birdwatching, as well as a lake for fishing and kayaking.
  • Myakka River State Park: Explore the untouched nature of Florida with a swamp tour or airboat ride. Admire the flora and fauna of Myakka River, including alligators, birds, and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Venice

  • Legacy Trail: This 10-mile trail spans from Venice to Sarasota and is surrounded by natural scenery.
  • Venetian Waterway Park: This trail follows the Intracoastal Waterway and offers stunning views of the water and the city.

Best Things to Do in Venice: Activities and Tours

  • Take a dolphin-watching tour: Venice is known for its dolphin sightings. Book a tour to see these playful creatures up close.
  • Shop at the Venice Farmers Market: Held every Saturday, this market offers fresh produce, handmade crafts, and delicious foods.
  • Go on a sunset sail: Watch the sunset over the Gulf of Mexico while enjoying a romantic boat ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Venice

Day Activity
1 Explore Venice Beach and Caspersen Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Venice

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grouper Sandwich: This local favorite features the Gulf’s freshest catch, fried to perfection and served on a bun.
  • Conch Fritters: Made with locally-caught conch, these fritters are a popular appetizer.
  • Key Lime Pie: Made with the state’s famous key limes, this pie is a must-try dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Frozen Cocktails: Try a frozen version of a classic cocktail, such as the Margarita or Piña Colada, to cool off on a hot day.
  • Local Craft Beer: Venice has several breweries offering unique and delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Venice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Venice Beach Villas: This beachfront property offers fully-equipped villas with a private pool.
  • Ramada by Wyndham Venice Hotel Venezia: Located in the heart of Venice, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a pool.
  • The Inn at Venice Beach: This bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms, a garden, and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Venice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shark Teeth: Venice is known as the “shark tooth capital of the world” and is home to various stores selling these unique souvenirs.
  • Local Art: Venice has a thriving arts scene, and many shops sell paintings, sculptures, and other works of art.
  • Olive Oil: Florida produces some of the best olive oil in the world; several shops in Venice sell locally-produced bottles.

Local Culture and Customs of Venice

  • Be Respectful to the Wildlife: Venice is home to many endangered or threatened species. Please be sure to respect the animals and their habitats.
  • Embrace the Beach Culture: When in Venice, do as the locals do and enjoy the laid-back beach lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Venice

The best time to visit Venice is between February and May or between September and November, when the weather is pleasant and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Venice

Do:

  • Do take a stroll on Venice’s beautiful beaches
  • Do try the local seafood and other delicacies
  • Do respect the natural environment and beach culture

Don’t:

  • Don’t wander off alone in areas you’re unfamiliar with
  • Don’t ignore the potential dangers of swimming in the Gulf of Mexico
  • Don’t forget to apply sunscreen and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months

Safety and Health

Safety in Venice

Venice is a relatively safe destination. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions like avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Venice

Be sure to check current illness advisories and make sure you have all vaccines required for travel to the area.

Getting to Venice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Venice

  • Sarasota Bradenton International Airport (SRQ) is just 27 miles away from Venice
  • Tampa International Airport (TPA) is 73 miles north of Venice

Bus Stations Near Venice

  • Venice Train Depot is served by Amtrak and offers service throughout the East Coast and Midwest.
  • Sarasota Bus Station is just 20 miles north of Venice and is served by Greyhound.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great option to explore Venice and the surrounding areas.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Uber and Lyft operate in Venice, offering convenient transportation around town.

How to Get Around in Venice

The best way to explore Venice is on foot or by bike. Several rental companies offer bikes for rent throughout the city, making it easy to explore the beaches, parks, and shops.

Where to Go Next from Venice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sarasota: Just 20 miles north of Venice, Sarasota is home to several cultural attractions and beaches.
  • Tampa: Located 73 miles north of Venice, Tampa is a vibrant city with plenty of entertainment options.
  • Sanibel Island: Just 70 miles south of Venice, Sanibel Island is a beautiful destination for beach and nature lovers.

Conclusion

Venice is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a laid-back beach vacation with plenty of culture and history. From the stunning beaches to the rich history and unique local cuisine, Venice has something for everyone. Plan your perfect trip with this comprehensive travel guide, and get ready to fall in love with Southwest Florida’s hidden gem.
