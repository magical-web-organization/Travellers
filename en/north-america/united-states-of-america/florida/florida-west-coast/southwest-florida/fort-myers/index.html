Introduction to Fort Myers

Located in Southwest Florida, Fort Myers is a popular tourist destination known for its beautiful beaches, natural attractions, and historical sites. Named after Colonel Abraham Myers, Fort Myers offers visitors a rich and diverse experience, with plenty of things to do and see. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Fort Myers.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Myers

Fort Myers is home to a wide range of attractions, from museums and historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of some of the most popular places to visit in Fort Myers:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Edison and Ford Winter Estates: This museum complex is a must-visit for history buffs and science enthusiasts alike. The estates were once the winter homes of inventors Thomas Edison and Henry Ford, and visitors can tour the homes, gardens, and laboratory.

  • Southwest Florida Museum of History: Located in downtown Fort Myers, this museum explores the region’s history, from the Calusa Indians to the present day.

  • Burroughs Home and Gardens: Built in 1901, this beautiful home is now a museum and event venue. Visitors can take a guided tour of the home and gardens, and learn about the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Fort Myers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sanibel Island: Known for its stunning beaches and shells, Sanibel Island is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can bike or walk on the island’s many trails, go birdwatching, or soak up the sun on the beach.

  • J.N. “Ding” Darling National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge is home to a diverse range of animals, including alligators, dolphins, and many species of birds. Visitors can explore the refuge on foot or by bike, or take a guided tour.

  • Manatee Park: Manatee Park is a unique destination where visitors can see manatees up close and learn about their habitat. The park also features a butterfly garden and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Myers

Fort Myers offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking, with trails ranging from easy to challenging. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Fort Myers:

  • Six Mile Cypress Slough Preserve: This nature preserve features a 1.2-mile boardwalk that winds through a wetland ecosystem. Visitors can spot wildlife and birds, and learn about the area’s ecology.

  • Caloosahatchee Regional Park: This park offers over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails through a variety of terrain, including pine flatwoods and wetlands.

  • Lakes Park: This park features a 2.5-mile paved trail that is perfect for biking or walking. It also has a playground, fishing pier, and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Fort Myers: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Fort Myers has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Fort Myers:

  • Boat tours: Fort Myers is home to many boating tours that offer visitors a chance to see dolphins, manatees, and other aquatic animals up close.

  • Fishing tours: From freshwater to saltwater, Fort Myers offers a variety of fishing tours for all skill levels.

  • Art festivals: Fort Myers hosts several art festivals each year, featuring local artists and vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Myers

Fort Myers is home to a lively food and drink scene, with a variety of restaurants and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grouper Sandwich: This sandwich features fresh grouper fillets fried or grilled, served on a bun with lettuce, tomato, and tartar sauce.

  • Cuban Sandwich: A popular Cuban dish, this sandwich features roasted pork, ham, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard on Cuban bread.

  • Stone Crab: A local delicacy, stone crab claws are served chilled and with mustard dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Key Lime Pie Martini: This refreshing cocktail is made with vanilla vodka, lime juice, and a garnish of graham cracker crumbs.

  • Sea Breeze: A classic cocktail, the Sea Breeze features vodka, grapefruit juice, and cranberry juice.

  • Fort Myers Brewing Company Beer: This local brewery offers a wide variety of beers, from IPAs to stouts.

Where to Stay in Fort Myers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort Myers offers plenty of accommodation options for every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Fort Myers:

  • Hyatt Place Fort Myers/at The Forum: This hotel features comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.

  • Hostel Buffalo: A budget-friendly option, this hostel offers dorm-style rooms and a communal kitchen.

  • The Hibiscus House Bed & Breakfast: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic home and features beautifully decorated rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Fort Myers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring back from your trip to Fort Myers? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Sea Shells: Fort Myers is home to many beautiful sea shells, and you can find them for sale at most souvenir shops.

  • Citrus: Florida is known for its citrus fruits, and you can find citrus-flavored treats and products at local farmers’ markets.

  • Artwork: Fort Myers is home to many talented artists, and you can find original artwork for sale at local galleries and art festivals.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Myers

Fort Myers has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Native American tribes, Spanish explorers, and early settlers. Here are some cultural customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Respect for the environment: With its natural beauty and diverse ecosystem, Fort Myers is a place to be respected and protected. Be sure to follow Leave No Trace principles and avoid littering or damaging natural areas.

  • Southern hospitality: Fort Myers locals are known for their warm and friendly nature, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation or offer to help you out.

Best Time to Visit Fort Myers

The best time to visit Fort Myers is during the winter months, when temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner. From January to March, you can enjoy warm weather, clear skies, and plenty of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Myers

To make the most of your trip to Fort Myers, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: From beaches to nature preserves, Fort Myers offers plenty of opportunities to get outside and experience the natural beauty of Southwest Florida.

  • Try the local cuisine: Fort Myers has a rich food scene, with plenty of fresh seafood, Cuban dishes, and citrus-inspired treats.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment: Be sure to follow Leave No Trace principles and respect the natural areas of Fort Myers.

  • Underestimate the heat: Fort Myers can be hot and humid, especially during the summer months. Be sure to stay hydrated and bring sunscreen and a hat.

Safety and Health

Fort Myers is a generally safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always important to take precautions when traveling to a new place. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Fort Myers

  • Avoid walking alone at night: As with any destination, it’s best to avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

  • Lock your car doors: Keep your car doors locked and valuables out of sight to avoid theft.

Health in Fort Myers

  • Stay hydrated: With its warm temperatures and high humidity, it’s easy to get dehydrated in Fort Myers. Be sure to drink plenty of water and take breaks in the shade.

  • Wear mosquito repellent: Especially during the summer months, mosquitoes can be a nuisance in Fort Myers. Be sure to wear insect repellent to avoid bites.

Getting to Fort Myers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fort Myers is easily accessible by car and plane, and there are several transportation options available to get around the city. Here are some tips for getting to and around Fort Myers:

Airports Near Fort Myers

  • Southwest Florida International Airport (RSW): Located in Fort Myers, this is the main airport serving the area.

  • Punta Gorda Airport (PGD): Located about 30 miles north of Fort Myers, this airport serves low-cost carriers.

Bus Stations Near Fort Myers

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This national bus line has a station in downtown Fort Myers.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: There are several rental car companies located at Southwest Florida International Airport.

  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in Fort Myers.

How to Get Around in Fort Myers

Fort Myers is a sprawling city, and having a car is the easiest way to get around. However, there are also several other transportation options available:

  • LeeTran: Fort Myers’ public transportation system includes buses that run throughout the city.

  • Bikes: Several bike rental companies are located in Fort Myers, and there are also many bike-friendly trails and routes.

  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in Fort Myers.

Where to Go Next from Fort Myers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fort Myers is surrounded by several great destinations that are well worth a visit. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Naples: This beautiful coastal town is known for its luxurious resorts, high-end shopping, and pristine beaches.

  • Sarasota: Home to several world-renowned cultural attractions, including the Ringling Museum of Art and the Sarasota Opera, Sarasota is a hub for arts and culture.

  • Everglades National Park: About two hours south of Fort Myers, Everglades National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, with its unique ecosystem and diverse wildlife.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring natural wonders, learning about the region’s history, or simply relaxing on the beach, Fort Myers has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-prepared to plan a memorable trip to this beautiful destination.
