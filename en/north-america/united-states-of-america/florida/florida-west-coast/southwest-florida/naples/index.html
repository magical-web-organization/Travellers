Introduction to Naples

Located in Southwest Florida, Naples is a renowned resort destination offering beautiful beaches, outdoor recreational opportunities, and cultural attractions. It is a paradise for water sports enthusiasts, hikers and bikers, foodies, and art lovers. With abundant sunshine, warm temperatures, and an international airport nearby, it is easy to understand why millions of visitors flock to Naples each year.

List of Places to Visit in Naples

Make the most of your visit to Naples by including these places on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Naples Historical Society: Learn about Naples’ history through exhibits and guided tours at this historical society and museum.
  • The Naples Depot Museum: This museum exhibits Naples’ transportation history and the people who shaped the city.
  • The Holocaust Museum: The museum exhibits the atrocities of the Holocaust, emphasizing the impact and loss of those who survived and those who perished.

Natural Attractions of Naples: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Naples Beach: Relax on this white-sand beach, swim in the warm Gulf of Mexico waters or fish from Naples Pier.
  • Naples Botanical Garden: Stroll through the gardens and immerse yourself in the flora and fauna of Florida.
  • Everglades National Park: Take an airboat ride, go kayaking, or see native wildlife such as alligators and manatees in this 1.5 million-acre national park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naples

Explore the natural beauty of Naples through its hiking and biking trails. Here are some favorites:

  • The Naples Preserve: Meander through this protected mangrove forest with walking trails and educational exhibits.
  • The Greenway: The Collier County Greenway offers 15 miles of scenic trails for hikers, bikers, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Naples: Activities and Tours

  • Sunset Sail: Set sail from Naples Pier or Marco Island and watch the sunset over the Gulf of Mexico as you enjoy cocktails.
  • Golf: Naples is home to over 90 golf courses, many of which host championship tournaments.
  • Food Tours: Take a food tour and savor the flavors of Naples by sampling locally sourced fresh seafood, produce, and other delicacies.

Food and Drink Scene of Naples

The food and drink scene in Naples is world-class. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stone Crabs: A delicacy in Naples, these crabs are a must-try, and best served fresh with mustard sauce.
  • Florida Gator: Gator is a protein-rich, healthy alternative to beef and tastes great grilled or fried.
  • Key Lime Pie: The Florida Keys’ most famous dessert; Naples’ version has a graham cracker crust and a tangy lime filling.

Local Beverages

  • Mojitos: Naples bars and restaurants feature mojitos made with fresh mint, cane sugar, and lime, perfect for a hot day.
  • Truly Spiked & Sparkling: An alcoholic seltzer drink made with lemon and blueberry that is a must-try in Naples.
  • Florida Orange Juice: The state drink of Florida, orange juice, is freshly squeezed, highly nutritious, and available everywhere in Naples.

Where to Stay in Naples: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Naples has a range of options for accommodation, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly options. Here are some of the best places to rest after a day of exploring:

  • The Ritz Carlton: Luxury beachfront resort with world-class amenities and a stunning spa.
  • Lemon Tree Inn: A charming and romantic hotel offering continental breakfast and proximity to downtown Naples.
  • Hostel of Naples: Budget-friendly hostel near the beach and parks with affordable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Naples: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tee-Shirts: Visit a local souvenir shop and take home a unique Naples-themed tee-shirt.
  • Shell Crafts: Souvenir shops in Naples sell shell necklaces, earrings, and other artisanal crafts.
  • Cuban Cigars: Tobacco shops in Naples sell hand-rolled Cuban cigars, a great gift for cigar enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Naples

  • Greetings: In Naples, people greet each other with a firm handshake or hug and a smile.
  • Dress Code: Naples is a relatively conservative town, so dress conservatively if you plan to visit churches or other religious sites.
  • Tipping: In Naples, tipping is customary, and servers and bartenders expect at least 15-20% gratuity.

Best Time to Visit Naples

The best time to visit Naples is from November to April when there is less rain and more comfortable temperatures in the 70s and 80s°F. Summers may be too hot and humid, with temperatures topping 90°F.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naples

Do:

  • Do Enjoy the Beaches: Naples has beautiful beaches, and they are free to the public.
  • Do Get up Early: Naples has many outdoor activities, and the mornings are the coolest and least busy.
  • Do Respect the Wildlife: Naples wildlife is abundant and diverse, act respectfully, and do not harm any animal.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Litter: Naples is a clean city with strict anti-littering laws. Keep your garbage until you find a trash bin.
  • Don’t Feed the Wildlife: Feeding any wildlife, including birds, is illegal in Naples and can alter the natural balance of the ecosystem.
  • Don’t Forget Sunscreen: The Florida sun is harsh, so use sunscreen when outdoors to avoid heat stroke, sunburn, or skin damage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naples

Naples is a relatively safe city and has low crime rates, but as in any city, travelers should be cautious and informed about the surroundings. Avoiding the parks after dark, not leaving valuables visible in the car, and keeping a copy of important documents in a safe place are some of the best practices to ensure your safety.

Health in Naples

Florida has bugs that bite and can transmit different diseases, including the West Nile and Zika viruses. Be proactive and bring insect repellent with DEET, and check for ticks if you hike or bike. Don’t forget to drink plenty of water, avoid overexertion, and stay hydrated in the hot and humid weather.

Getting to Naples: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naples

  • Southwest Florida International Airport: Located in Fort Myers, 35 miles from Naples.
  • Naples Municipal Airport: Offers private charters and connects with Fort Lauderdale, Tampa, and Key West.

Bus Stations Near Naples

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Naples.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Renting a car is the most comfortable and convenient way to get around Naples.
  • Uber: Uber is available in Naples for trips to the airport or other destinations.

How to Get Around in Naples

Naples is a small city, and it’s easy to get around on foot, especially downtown. However, to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car, or booking a shuttle or taxi is essential. Biking is also a popular option in Naples, and many hotels offer bikes for their guests.

Where to Go Next from Naples: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have already explored Naples thoroughly, consider these nearby destinations for a day trip:

  • Sarasota: Approximately 100 miles north of Naples, Sarasota offers a sophisticated cultural scene and stunning beaches.
  • Sanibel Island: Sanibel Island, less than 50 miles from Naples, is a nature lover’s paradise with shelling, birding, and white-sand beaches.
  • Marco Island: Located just south of Naples, Marco Island is a popular vacation spot, features beautiful beaches and water sports.

Conclusion

Naples, Florida, is a vibrant city with something to offer for everyone. Whether it’s exploring its scenic beauty, enjoying its outdoor activities, or savoring its food and drink scene, Naples provides a relaxing and rejuvenating vacation spot. Use this guide to plan your Naples experience, and start packing!
