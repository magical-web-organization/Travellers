Introduction to Key West

Located in the southernmost point of Florida, Key West is an island paradise that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its crystal-clear blue waters, stunning sunsets, and endless activities, Key West is a popular destination for travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Key West

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or natural attractions, Key West has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Key West:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historic Places

  • Ernest Hemingway Home and Museum: The former residence of the famous writer, the Hemingway Home is now open for tours and features original furnishings, manuscripts, and memorabilia.
  • Harry S. Truman Little White House: Built in 1890, this historic house was the winter White House for President Truman and is now a museum that showcases the history of the presidency.
  • Key West Lighthouse and Keeper’s Quarters Museum: This 1848 lighthouse and surrounding museum provide insight into the maritime culture of Key West.

Natural Attractions of Key West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dry Tortugas National Park: Visit this breathtaking national park which is only accessible by boat or seaplane. You can snorkel the crystal-clear waters, explore the historic Fort Jefferson, and witness the stunning sunset.
  • The Southernmost Point: Photograph the iconic buoy which marks the southernmost point of the continental United States.
  • Butterfly and Nature Conservatory: A tropical paradise with over 50 species of butterflies and exotic birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Key West

Key West is the perfect destination for outdoor adventure seekers. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Key West:

  • The Key West Smathers Beach Bike Path: A great route for cycling or jogging, this path runs along the Atlantic Ocean and offers breathtaking views of the water.
  • The Curry Hammock State Park Trail: Perfect for hiking and bird-watching, visitors can explore the local wildlife and enjoy the scenic views.
  • The Florida Keys Overseas Heritage Trail: A 90-mile trail that runs over the Keys, offering beautiful ocean views and the chance to explore the local history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Key West: Activities and Tours

From water sports to sunset cruises, there are countless activities and tours to choose from in Key West. Here are some of the best things to do in Key West:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: The crystal-clear blue waters of Key West are perfect for exploring the colorful marine life.
  • Sunset Sail: Take a romantic sunset sail and enjoy the magical Key West sunset while sipping a cocktail.
  • Ghost Tour: Learn about the haunted history of Key West on a spooky ghost tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Key West

Day 1: Visit the Hemingway home and museum, explore Duval Street, and enjoy a sunset dinner cruise.

Day 2: Take a bike ride through the Historic District, visit the Southernmost Point, and check out Fort Zachary Taylor Beach.

Day 3: Go snorkeling or scuba diving at a nearby reef or wreck, explore the Mel Fisher Maritime Museum, and watch the sunset at Mallory Square.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the Dry Tortugas National Park, snorkel, explore Fort Jefferson, and view a stunning sunset.

Day 5: Kayak through the mangroves, visit the Butterfly and Nature Conservatory, and explore Old Town.

Day 6: Visit the Key West Lighthouse and Keeper’s Quarters Museum, have lunch at a local seafood restaurant, and enjoy a sunset sail.

Day 7: Rent a jet ski or take a parasailing excursion, try some local key lime pie and relax at the beach before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Key West

With its tropical climate and proximity to the sea, Key West boasts some of the most delicious and unique cuisine in the country. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Key West:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Conch Fritters: These crispy fritters are made with local conch and are a staple in Key West cuisine.
  • Key Lime Pie: A tart yet sweet dessert made with local key limes, graham cracker crust, and whipped cream.
  • Seafood: With fresh seafood caught daily, there are countless mouth-watering seafood dishes to try.

Local Beverages

  • Frozen Daiquiri: Cool off with a frozen daiquiri made with locally-sourced fruits.
  • Margarita: Enjoy a classic margarita while watching the sunset over the ocean.
  • Cuba Libre: This cocktail is made with rum, cola, and lime and is a deliciously refreshing Key West classic.

Where to Stay in Key West: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

With a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious resorts to affordable hostels, Key West has something for every budget and taste.

  • The Casa Marina Resort: A luxury resort with a private beach, spa, and pool.
  • The Artist House: A charming guesthouse with a private pool and lush gardens.
  • NYAH Key West: A fun and affordable hostel with communal areas and a pool.

What to Buy in Key West: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From local crafts to delicious treats, there are plenty of souvenirs to bring home from Key West.

  • Key Lime Products: Take home some key lime pie mix, key lime hot sauce, or key lime salt to bring a touch of Key West home.
  • Art and Crafts: Key West is home to many artists and craftsmen, offering unique hand-crafted souvenirs.
  • Tropical Clothing: From Tommy Bahama to Margaritaville, Key West has a wide selection of tropical clothing to bring home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Key West

Known for its laid-back and friendly vibe, Key West locals value a relaxed lifestyle where residents often live by the motto “One Human Family.” The local culture is heavily influenced by maritime history, Cuban heritage, and the island’s unique location as the southernmost point of the United States.

Best Time to Visit Key West

The best time to visit Key West is from December to May, when the temperatures are mild and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities. The summer months can be hot and humid, with occasional storms, but offer incredible deals and fewer crowds for those on a budget.

Do’s and Don’ts of Key West

Do:

  • Explore the local history and culture.
  • Try the delicious seafood and local cuisine.
  • Take a sunset sail or snorkeling trip.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Be disrespectful to the local culture and customs.
  • Drink and drive or operate watercraft intoxicated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Key West

Overall, Key West is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions when driving, cycling, and participating in water activities. It is also recommended to keep valuables out of sight and locked up in a safe place when not in use.

Health in Key West

There are no major health concerns for travelers to Key West, but it is recommended to use sunscreen and insect repellent due to the tropical climate. Visitors should also drink plenty of water, especially when participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Key West: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Key West

  • Key West International Airport: Located just minutes from downtown, this airport offers domestic flights from a variety of airlines.

Bus Stations Near Key West

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The nearest Greyhound station is in Miami, with connecting buses to Key West.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Rent a car from Miami or other nearby cities and enjoy the scenic drive down the Overseas Highway.
  • Ferry: Take the Key West Express ferry from Fort Myers or Marco Island for a unique mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Key West

The best way to get around Key West is on foot, bike, or scooter. Taxis and public transportation are also available for longer distances or transportation to and from the airport.

Where to Go Next from Key West: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Miami: Just a few hours’ drive from Key West, Miami offers endless shopping, nightlife, and entertainment options.
  • Everglades National Park: Located just north of the Keys, this national park is home to unique wildlife and magnificent scenery.
  • Bimini: A short ferry ride from Key West, this Bahamian island is known for its crystal-clear waters and incredible beaches.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Key West is the perfect destination for your next vacation. With its unique location, friendly locals, and stunning scenery, Key West is sure to leave a lasting impression on all who visit.
