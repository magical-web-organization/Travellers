Introduction to Marathon

Located in the heart of the Florida Keys, Marathon is a small island city known for its stunning natural beauty, pristine beaches, and range of exciting outdoor adventures. This charming city attracts thousands of visitors each year who come to enjoy its scenic views, tropical climate, and endless array of recreational activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Marathon has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Marathon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Crane Point Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Marathon and the Florida Keys through exhibits and artifacts that date back to prehistoric times.

  2. The Pigeon Key Foundation: Located on a remote island accessible only by ferry, this historic site features a museum and tours of the once-thriving community that played an important role in the construction of the Florida East Coast Railway.

  3. The Turtle Hospital: A must-visit for animal lovers, this hospital is dedicated to the rehabilitation and release of sea turtles, and offers guided educational tours and interactive experiences.

Natural Attractions of Marathon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sombrero Beach: One of the most popular beaches in the Florida Keys, Sombrero Beach boasts soft, white sand, crystal-clear waters, and several picnic areas and playgrounds.

  2. Dolphin Research Center: A world-renowned marine research center showcasing Atlantic bottlenose dolphins and California sea lions in their natural habitat.

  3. Florida Keys Aquarium Encounters: A fascinating underwater world where visitors can get up close and personal with sharks, stingrays, and other marine creatures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marathon

  1. Curry Hammock State Park: One of the best hiking and biking spots in Marathon, Curry Hammock State Park offers trails through lush mangroves and scenic waterfronts.

  2. The Old Seven Mile Bridge Trail: A popular route for runners and bikers, this iconic bridge was built in 1912 and has stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico.

Best Things to Do in Marathon: Activities and Tours

  1. Charter Fishing: Marathon is often called the “sportfishing capital of the world,” and there are plenty of charter boats that offer offshore and backcountry fishing trips.

  2. Sunset Sails: What better way to take in the stunning beauty of Marathon’s sunsets than on a sailing tour?

  3. Kayak Tours: Discover the beauty of the Florida Keys on a guided kayaking tour through mangroves, canals, and waterways.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marathon

Day 1: Arrive in Marathon and explore the local beaches and parks, such as Sombrero Beach and Curry Hammock State Park.

Day 2: Take an educational tour of The Turtle Hospital and learn about the rehabilitation of sea turtles.

Day 3: Discover the cultural side of Marathon by visiting the Crane Point Museum and walking through the historic Pigeon Key community.

Day 4: Take a dolphin or sea lion encounter at the Dolphin Research Center.

Day 5: Go on a snorkeling or diving tour to explore the vibrant underwater world of the Florida Keys.

Day 6: Take a sunset cruise to relax and enjoy the stunning views of the Florida Keys.

Day 7: Spend the day kayaking through the mangroves or biking the historic Old Seven Mile Bridge Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Marathon

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Conch fritters: A classic Florida Keys dish made from locally sourced conch, these savory bites are a must-try when in Marathon.

  2. Key Lime Pie: This iconic pie, made with tart key lime juice and a sweet graham cracker crust, is a must-have for any dessert lover.

  3. Lobster: As one of the most prominent seafood destinations in the country, Marathon serves fresh and delicious lobster in a range of preparations.

Local Beverages

  1. Key West First Legal Rum Distillery: Take a tour of the distillery and sample locally-made rum.

  2. Florida Keys Brewing Company: A family-owned and operated brewery that offers locally-made craft beer.

Where to Stay in Marathon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Tranquility Bay Beach House Resort: Located on a private beach, this resort features luxurious beachfront villas and a range of amenities.

  2. The Hammocks at Marathon: A secluded and tranquil resort with modern villas and excellent water views.

  3. Islander Resort: A family-friendly resort with a private beach, pool, and easy access to a range of water sports and activities.

What to Buy in Marathon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Key Lime products: Take a taste of the Florida Keys home with you with locally-made key lime products, such as key lime jelly, key lime juice, and key lime tea.

  2. Seafood: For seafood lovers, Marathon offers plenty of options to take home, such as fresh lobster, conch, and stone crabs.

Local Culture and Customs of Marathon

The Florida Keys have a laid-back and relaxed atmosphere that is reflected in the local culture and customs. Visitors are welcome and encouraged to embrace the island lifestyle, which includes taking it easy, enjoying the natural beauty, and embracing the slow pace of life.

Best Time to Visit Marathon

The best time to visit Marathon is during the winter months, from December to April, when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and humid, with more tourists and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marathon

Do:

  1. Bring sunscreen, as the Florida Keys have strong UV rays.

  2. Respect the local wildlife and environment.

  3. Embrace the relaxed pace of life and take it easy.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural environment.

  2. Overdo it with alcohol, as it can lead to accidents and injuries.

  3. Ignore warning signs or safety protocols on outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marathon

Marathon is a safe city, but visitors should be aware of the natural hazards of the island, such as strong tides, dangerous wildlife, and extreme weather conditions. Additionally, practice general safety rules such as locking your doors and windows and keeping an eye on your personal belongings when out and about.

Health in Marathon

Marathon has several medical facilities, including a hospital and urgent care centers. Visitors should be aware of the sun exposure, stay hydrated, and take precautions against insect bites and mosquito-borne viruses.

Getting to Marathon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marathon

  1. Key West International Airport (EYW): Located about 45 miles south of Marathon, this is the closest and most convenient airport.

  2. Miami International Airport (MIA): The second-closest airport to Marathon, located about 120 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Marathon

Greyhound bus lines and the Florida Keys Shuttle are two options for bus transportation to and from Marathon.

Other Options

  1. Renting a car: This is a popular option for visitors to get around the Florida Keys and explore the nearby towns and attractions.

  2. Taxis: Taxis are available within the city limits of Marathon.

How to Get Around in Marathon

The best way to get around Marathon is by car, which can be rented at various rental locations throughout the city. Taxis, bicycles and mopeds are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Marathon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Key West: Just 50 miles away from Marathon, Key West is a vibrant and colorful destination known for its art galleries, historic architecture, and lively nightlife.

  2. Islamorada: Located 20 miles north of Marathon, Islamorada is a small village that is known for its stunning sunsets and excellent fishing.

Conclusion

Marathon is a truly special destination where adventure, relaxation, and cultural experiences converge. With its tropical climate, stunning natural beauty, and range of activities and attractions, this small island city in the heart of the Florida Keys has something for everyone.
