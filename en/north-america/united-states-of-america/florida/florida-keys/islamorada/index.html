Introduction to Islamorada

Islamorada, a village of islands located in the Florida Keys, is world-renowned for its clear blue waters, breathtaking sunsets, and abundance of marine life. The name Islamorada translates to “purple island” in Spanish, and the name is believed to have originated from the beautiful sunsets that turn the horizon shades of purple and pink.

Islamorada is a perfect destination for those who enjoy the outdoors and all things water-related, from fishing and boating to snorkelling and diving. This comprehensive guide will cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Islamorada, including the best places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Islamorada

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The History of Diving Museum: Discover the history of diving through interactive exhibits and displays showcasing antique diving helmets, suits, and weapons.
  • Indian Key State Historic Site: Explore the ruins of a 19th-century settlement and learn about its role in Florida’s history.
  • Lignumvitae Key Botanical State Park: Take a guided tour of the historic Matheson House on Lignumvitae Key, which was once a winter retreat for wealthy families in the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Islamorada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Anne’s Beach: A pristine sandy beach located on Lower Matecumbe Key, ideal for sunbathing, swimming, and fishing.
  • Islamorada Sandbar: A shallow sandbar located in the middle of the ocean; perfect for swimming, snorkelling, and picnicking.
  • Florida Keys Wild Bird Rehabilitation Center: A non-profit organization dedicated to rescuing and rehabilitating injured birds, including pelicans, ospreys, and eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Islamorada

  • The Florida Keys Overseas Heritage Trail: A 76-mile bike trail that spans the length of the Florida Keys, with various trails on Islamorada.
  • Annie’s Trail: A 1.75-mile hike through a hardwood hammock and a salt marsh.

Best Things to Do in Islamorada: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Islamorada is known as the “Sportfishing Capital of the World” with opportunities for backcountry, reef, and offshore fishing.
  • Snorkelling and Diving: Explore the third-largest coral barrier reef system in the world, just a few miles off the coast of Islamorada.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the mangrove trails, shallow flats, and crystal-clear waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Islamorada

Day Activities
1 Enjoy a day at Anne’s Beach followed by dinner at Lazy Days Restaurant.
2 Take a guided tour of the History of Diving Museum followed by a visit to the Florida Keys Brewing Company.
3 Go fishing with a charter boat followed by lunch at The Square Grouper.
4 Explore the natural beauty of Islamorada by kayak or paddleboard followed by dinner at Morada Bay Beach Café.
5 Take a day trip to nearby Bahia Honda State Park for snorkelling and beachcombing.
6 Enjoy the sunset and live music at Lorelei Restaurant & Cabana Bar.
7 Take a leisurely bike ride on the Florida Keys Overseas Heritage Trail followed by drinks at the Green Turtle Inn.

Food and Drink Scene of Islamorada

Must-Try Dishes

  • Key Lime Pie: A classic Florida dessert made with fresh key limes, a graham cracker crust, and whipped cream.
  • Conch Fritters: Fried fritters made with conch meat, onion, and celery; served with a dipping sauce.
  • Stone Crab: A Florida delicacy; seasonally harvested and served with mustard sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Islamorada Beer Company: Local microbrewery with an on-site taproom, serving a variety of craft beers.
  • Rum Runners: A tropical cocktail made with banana liqueur, blackberry brandy, pineapple juice, and rum.
  • Key West Sunset Ale: An amber ale brewed with the zest of key limes; available at many local restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Islamorada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Moorings Village & Spa (luxury): A luxurious resort with oceanfront cottages, spa services, and a private beach.
  • Casa Morada (boutique): A tranquil, adults-only resort with spacious rooms, a private island, and complimentary kayaks.
  • Breezy Palms Resort (mid-range): A family-friendly hotel with a heated pool, BBQ grills, and dock slips.

What to Buy in Islamorada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Key Lime Products: Key lime-scented soaps, candles, and lotions.
  • Conch Shell Jewelry: Handmade jewelry made from locally sourced conch shells.
  • Art and Decor: Shop locally made artwork, pottery, and home decor inspired by the ocean and marine life.

Local Culture and Customs of Islamorada

Islamorada has a laid-back, casual atmosphere, with a strong focus on the outdoors and marine life. Locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, but it’s important to respect the wildlife and natural surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Islamorada

The best time to visit Islamorada is from March to May, when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are smaller. Fall is a great time to visit as well, with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Islamorada

Do:

  • Do respect wildlife and avoid touching or feeding animals.
  • Do wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and heatstroke.
  • Do try the local seafood and craft beers.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the beaches or in the water.
  • Don’t touch or disturb coral reefs; they are delicate and easily damaged.
  • Don’t fish or hunt without proper permits and licenses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Islamorada

Islamorada is a safe destination, but it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid leaving valuables unattended. Always lock your car and hotel room and use caution when swimming in the ocean, as there may be strong currents or rip tides.

Health in Islamorada

Islamorada is located in a subtropical climate, which means it’s important to protect your skin from the sun and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months. It’s also important to be aware of the risks of mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Islamorada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Islamorada

  • Miami International Airport (MIA): Approximately 90 miles from Islamorada.
  • Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL): Approximately 100 miles from Islamorada.

Bus Stations Near Islamorada

  • There are no direct bus routes to Islamorada; the closest Greyhound station is in Key Largo, approximately 20 miles away.

Other Options

  • Rent a car from one of the nearby airports or in Miami and drive down to Islamorada.
  • Take a shuttle or private car service from the airport.

How to Get Around in Islamorada

The best way to get around Islamorada is by car or bike. There are also several shuttle services and taxi companies available.

Where to Go Next from Islamorada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Key Largo: Just a short drive north of Islamorada, Key Largo is home to John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park and the African Queen Canal Cruise.
  • Marathon: Approximately 45 minutes south of Islamorada, Marathon is known for its beautiful beaches, the Dolphin Research Center, and the Turtle Hospital.
  • Key West: Approximately 90 minutes southwest of Islamorada, Key West is a popular destination for its vibrant nightlife, historic landmarks, and laid-back atmosphere.

Conclusion

Islamorada offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventure, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. This guide should help you plan your perfect trip and make the most of your time in this idyllic Florida Keys village. Happy travels!
