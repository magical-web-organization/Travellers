Introduction to Dry Tortugas National Park

Dry Tortugas National Park is a unique, isolated string of seven small islands located 70 miles west of Key West, Florida. The park includes 100 square miles of open water and its coral reefs, making it an unforgettable destination for anyone looking to experience the natural beauty and rich history of Florida Keys. The park is accessible only by seaplane or boat, allowing visitors to truly disconnect and immerse themselves in nature.

List of Places to Visit in Dry Tortugas National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dry Tortugas National Park is steeped in history, and visitors can explore some fascinating historical sites and landmarks, including:

Fort Jefferson

Fort Jefferson is the most famous attraction in Dry Tortugas National Park. It is a massive brick fortress built in the mid-19th century that housed Union troops during the Civil War. Today, visitors can walk around the fort and take guided tours to learn about its history. Don’t miss the chance to climb to the top of the fort for incredible views of the ocean and surrounding islands.

Garden Key Lighthouse

The Garden Key Lighthouse is another must-see attraction in Dry Tortugas National Park. This 19th-century lighthouse is still in use today and provides stunning views of the park and its surrounding waters.

Natural Attractions of Dry Tortugas National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dry Tortugas National Park is a paradise for nature lovers, with miles of pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life to explore. Some of the top outdoor adventures and scenic views in the park include:

Snorkeling and Diving

The coral reefs around Dry Tortugas National Park are some of the best in the world, teeming with colorful fish, sea turtles, and other marine life. Visitors can take guided snorkeling and diving tours or explore on their own to witness the beauty of this underwater world.

Birdwatching

Dry Tortugas National Park is a prime location for birdwatching, with over 300 species of birds passing through or nesting on the islands. The park is home to the largest nesting colony of sooty terns in the world, making it a must-visit destination for bird enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dry Tortugas National Park

While there are no designated hiking or biking trails on the islands, visitors can explore the park on foot or by bike. Bikes are available for rent on Garden Key, and visitors can take leisurely rides around the island to enjoy the scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Dry Tortugas National Park: Activities and Tours

The park offers many activities and tours that allow visitors to experience the beauty and history of Dry Tortugas National Park. Some of the best things to do include:

Seaplane Tours

Seaplane tours provide breathtaking views of the crystal-clear waters and coral reefs around Dry Tortugas National Park. Visitors can choose from a variety of tour options, including sunset tours, lunch tours, and all-day excursions.

Kayaking Tours

Kayaking tours are a great way to explore the park’s many islands and waterways. Visitors can choose from guided or self-guided tours to discover hidden coves and secluded beaches.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Dry Tortugas National Park, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Dry Tortugas National Park

Despite being a small, remote park, Dry Tortugas offers a variety of dining options and beverages to suit all tastes.

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors to Dry Tortugas National Park should be sure to try some of the local seafood, including conch fritters, lobster bisque, and grilled fish.

Local Beverages

Visitors can enjoy a variety of beverages on the islands, including rum-based cocktails and local craft beer.

Where to Stay in Dry Tortugas National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are no hotels or hostels on the islands, but visitors can choose from a few different accommodations options:

Camping

Dry Tortugas National Park offers primitive camping on Garden Key. Visitors must bring all necessary equipment and supplies with them and are responsible for packing out all trash.

Overnight Boating

Visitors can also choose to anchor their boats offshore or stay overnight on a houseboat to fully immerse themselves in the park’s natural beauty.

What to Buy in Dry Tortugas National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home a piece of Dry Tortugas National Park with them by purchasing locally-made souvenirs or products, including seashells, handmade jewelry, and local artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Dry Tortugas National Park

Dry Tortugas National Park is renowned for its natural beauty, unspoiled ecosystems, and rich history. Visitors should respect the park’s flora and fauna and follow Leave No Trace principles during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Dry Tortugas National Park

The best time to visit Dry Tortugas National Park is from April to May and November to December when the weather is mild and dry, with fewer crowds. Summer months can be hot and humid, and hurricanes and tropical storms can occur from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dry Tortugas National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s flora and fauna.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Stay on designated paths and trails.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dry Tortugas National Park

Visitors should take precautions when swimming or snorkeling in the park’s waters, including wearing a life jacket and avoiding swimming in areas with strong currents. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of sunburn and dehydration and take necessary precautions.

Health in Dry Tortugas National Park

Visitors should bring all necessary medications and be prepared for medical emergencies, as there are no medical facilities on the islands.

Getting to Dry Tortugas National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dry Tortugas National Park

The closest airport to Dry Tortugas National Park is Key West International Airport. From there, visitors can take a ferry or seaplane to the park.

Bus Stations Near Dry Tortugas National Park

There are no bus stations near Dry Tortugas National Park.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private boat or charter a boat to Dry Tortugas National Park.

How to Get Around in Dry Tortugas National Park

The best way to get around Dry Tortugas National Park is on foot or by bike, as there are no vehicles allowed on the islands. Visitors can rent bikes on Garden Key at the park’s visitor center.

Where to Go Next from Dry Tortugas National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Dry Tortugas National Park, visitors can explore the other attractions and destinations in Florida Keys, including Key West, Bahia Honda State Park, and the Everglades National Park.

Conclusion

Dry Tortugas National Park is a unique and unforgettable destination for anyone looking to experience the natural beauty and rich history of Florida Keys. With its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life, the park offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventure and exploration.
