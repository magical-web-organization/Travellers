Introduction to Key Largo

Located in the Florida Keys, Key Largo boasts a vibrant atmosphere and gorgeous beaches, making it a popular destination for visitors from around the world. The island is known for its abundance of natural attractions, great food and drink scene, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Key Largo

If you’re planning a trip to Key Largo, here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park
  • History of Diving Museum
  • Key Largo Hammocks State Botanical Site

Natural Attractions of Key Largo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Christ of the Abyss
  • Everglades National Park
  • Florida Keys National Marine Sanctuary

Hiking/Biking Routes of Key Largo

Key Largo offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Key Largo Hammock Botanical State Park Trail
  • Crocodile Lake National Wildlife Refuge Trail
  • Dagny Johnson Key Largo Hammock Botanical State Park

Best Things to Do in Key Largo: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Key Largo offers something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Key Largo:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving
  • Fishing and Boating
  • Sunset Sailing Cruises

Week-Long Itinerary for Key Largo

Day 1 - Snorkeling at John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park Day 2 - Kayaking in the Everglades National Park Day 3 - Fishing and boating activities at Florida Keys National Marine Sanctuary Day 4 - Learning about the history of diving at the History of Diving Museum Day 5 - Visiting local beaches and parks Day 6 - Enjoy sunset sailing cruises Day 7 - Relax and enjoy the local food and drinks

  • Handcrafted jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Key Largo

The local culture of Key Largo is heavily influenced by its natural environment and water-oriented lifestyle. Respect for marine life and nature, as well as hospitality towards visitors, are core values of the community.

Best Time to Visit Key Largo

The best time to visit Key Largo is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Avoid visiting the island during the peak hurricane season, which runs from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Key Largo

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Protect the environment and marine life

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural attractions
  • Touch or disturb marine life
  • Drive under the influence

Safety and Health

Safety in Key Largo

Key Largo is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always best to be aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night and keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded areas.

Health in Key Largo

Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated when spending time outdoors, as the sun and heat can be intense. Be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses and take necessary precautions, such as wearing long-sleeved clothing and using repellent.

Getting to Key Largo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Key Largo is easy, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Key Largo

  • Miami International Airport
  • Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport

Bus Stations Near Key Largo

  • Greyhound Bus Line
  • Key West Transit

Other Options

  • Car rental
  • Private shuttle service

How to Get Around in Key Largo

Bike and car rentals are available for visitors to get around the island. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, although they may be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Key Largo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Key Largo is a perfect balance of relaxation and outdoor adventure, featuring stunning beaches, diverse natural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to the scenic island of Key Largo.
