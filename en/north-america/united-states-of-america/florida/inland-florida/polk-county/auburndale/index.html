Auburndale Travel Guide

Located in Polk County, in Inland Florida of North America, Auburndale is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This quaint city boasts a rich history and culture, along with stunning natural beauty that offers countless outdoor adventures. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide for Auburndale, with all the information you need to plan your trip.

Introduction to Auburndale

Founded in 1880, Auburndale is a small yet charming city in Central Florida. Nestled between Lake Juliana and Lake Ariana, it’s the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. With a warm and welcoming atmosphere, it’s no wonder why visitors are drawn to this “City of Lakes” in Florida.

List of Places to Visit in Auburndale

Auburndale offers a variety of attractions that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the must-see places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Auburndale Historic Depot Museum: This museum is housed in the historic 1914 Atlantic Coast Line Railroad Depot and features exhibits on the history of Auburndale and the railroad industry.
  • Lake Myrtle Sports Park: This sports park features baseball and softball fields, a soccer field, a playground, and a walking/jogging trail.
  • Florida Air Museum: Located at Lakeland Linder International Airport, this museum features exhibits on aviation history and hosts a variety of airshows throughout the year.
  • Fantasy of Flight: This aviation-themed attraction features a collection of rare and vintage aircraft, as well as interactive exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Auburndale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Disney Wilderness Preserve: This nature preserve features hiking trails, birdwatching, and kayaking opportunities in a beautiful natural setting.
  • Lake Myrtle Park: This park features a playground, basketball and tennis courts, a soccer field, and a walking/jogging trail.
  • Lake Juliana: This lake offers boating and fishing opportunities, as well as stunning sunsets.
  • Lake Ariana Park: This park offers a fishing pier, picnic pavilions, and a boat ramp for visitors to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auburndale

Auburndale has several trails that provide beautiful views of the city and its natural surroundings. Here are a few of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Lake Myrtle Loop: This 3-mile loop offers a scenic view of Lake Myrtle and the sports complex.
  • Lake Juliana Loop Trail: This 2.5-mile trail provides beautiful views of the lake and the surrounding wetlands.
  • Van Fleet Trail: This 29-mile trail runs through Auburndale and provides a great opportunity for a long-distance bike ride or hike.

Best Things to Do in Auburndale: Activities and Tours

In addition to the top attractions and hiking/biking routes, there are many fun things to do in Auburndale. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Airboat Tours: Take an airboat tour through the Everglades and see the unique wildlife and vegetation that make this area so special.
  • Classic Car Museum: This museum features a collection of classic and antique cars that are sure to please any automobile enthusiast.
  • Gatorland: This theme park features alligators, crocodiles, snakes, and other reptiles from all over the world.
  • Safari Wilderness Ranch: This ranch offers a safari-style tour of more than 260 acres of Florida wilderness, with exotic animals like zebras, watusi, and camels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auburndale

To help plan your visit to Auburndale, here’s a week-long itinerary to follow:

Day 1: Visit the Auburndale Historic Depot Museum and enjoy a picnic lunch at Lake Ariana Park.

Day 2: Spend the day at Disney Wilderness Preserve, hiking and exploring.

Day 3: Take an airboat tour through the Everglades and visit Gatorland.

Day 4: Visit Lake Myrtle Sports Park and the Classic Car Museum.

Day 5: Go on a safari-style tour of Safari Wilderness Ranch.

Day 6: Spend the day at Lake Juliana, fishing and enjoying the sunset.

Day 7: Relax at your hotel or guesthouse before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Auburndale

No trip is complete without sampling local cuisine. Auburndale has a thriving food and drink scene that you won’t want to miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orange Blossom Honey: Try this local honey that’s made from bees that pollinate the orange groves of Central Florida.
  • Cuban Sandwich: Made with roasted pork, ham, Swiss cheese, mustard, and pickles, the Cuban sandwich is a classic Florida favorite.
  • Gator Bites: For the adventurous eater, try some alligator bites. They taste like a mix between chicken and fish.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is known for its oranges, and Auburndale makes some of the best orange juice around.
  • Craft Beer: Visit any of the local breweries for a taste of some delicious craft beer.

Where to Stay in Auburndale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auburndale has accommodation options that fit every budget and preference, from budget-friendly hostels to fancy hotels. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Hampton Inn Auburndale: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities like an outdoor pool and fitness center.
  • Best Western Auburndale Inn & Suites: This pet-friendly hotel is located near popular attractions and features a complimentary breakfast and outdoor pool.
  • RV Parks and Campgrounds: For those who prefer a more adventurous lodging option, there are several RV parks and campgrounds in the area.

What to Buy in Auburndale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Auburndale with you by purchasing some local products and souvenirs. Here are a few things to consider:

  • Orange Blossom Honey: Bring home a jar of this delicious honey.
  • Polk County Peaches: Florida is known for its citrus fruits, but the peaches grown in Polk County are also delicious.
  • Cypress Wood Products: Cypress wood is a popular local material that’s often used to make home decor and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Auburndale

Auburndale has a rich history and culture that’s worth exploring. Chat with the locals and check out local shops and restaurants to get a feel for the area’s unique vibe.

Best Time to Visit Auburndale

The best time to visit Auburndale is between November and April when the weather is cooler and drier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auburndale

Do:

  • Do visit the museums and historical places to learn more about the city’s history and culture.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do explore the outdoors and take advantage of the hiking/biking trails and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated during outdoor activities.
  • Don’t visit during hurricane season (June-November) or the hottest summer months (July-August).

Safety and Health

Safety in Auburndale

Auburndale is a generally safe city, but use common sense and stay aware of your surroundings. Keep your valuables secure and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Auburndale

It’s important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when participating in outdoor activities. For medical emergencies, call 911 or head to the nearest hospital (Winter Haven Hospital).

Getting to Auburndale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Auburndale is easily accessible by car and lies between two major Florida cities; Orlando and Tampa. Here are some transportation options for getting to the city:

Airports near Auburndale

  • Orlando International Airport: This international airport is the closest major airport to Auburndale, located approximately 50 miles away.
  • Tampa International Airport: This international airport is located approximately 40 miles away from Auburndale.

Bus stations near Auburndale

  • Winter Haven Area Transit: This public transportation system services the city of Winter Haven and stops in Auburndale.
  • Greyhound: The Greyhound bus station in Lakeland is approximately 20 miles away from Auburndale.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient option for getting around Auburndale and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Auburndale

Auburndale is a small city that’s easy to navigate by car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Auburndale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Auburndale is a great base to explore other nearby destinations. Here are some ideas:

  • Orlando: Take a day trip to Orlando to visit theme parks like Walt Disney World and Universal Studios.
  • Tampa: Tampa is only a short drive away and offers attractions like Busch Gardens and the Florida Aquarium.
  • St. Petersburg Beach: Located on the Gulf of Mexico, St. Petersburg Beach is a beautiful and relaxing destination to visit.

Conclusion

Auburndale is a hidden gem in Central Florida that offers something for everyone. With beautiful natural attractions, a rich history, and unique cuisine, it’s sure to be a memorable vacation destination. Use this travel guide to plan your visit and enjoy all that Auburndale has to offer.
