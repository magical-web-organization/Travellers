Introduction to Lakeland

Welcome to Lakeland, Florida - a beautiful city that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious dining experiences. Located in Polk County, in Inland Florida, Lakeland is known for its picturesque lakes, historic landmarks, and natural beauty. The city’s central location makes it an easy day-trip destination from nearby popular cities like Tampa (45 minutes away) and Orlando (1 hour away). Whether you’re looking to explore Lakeland’s rich history, enjoy the great outdoors, or simply relax and unwind, this charming city has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lakeland

Below are some of the must-visit places in Lakeland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Polk Museum of Art

The Polk Museum of Art is a vibrant cultural institution that showcases a variety of contemporary and traditional art. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of exhibitions, educational programs, and events throughout the year.

Florida Southern College Frank Lloyd Wright Visitors Center

The Frank Lloyd Wright Visitors Center is a must-see for architecture and history enthusiasts. The center offers guided tours of the largest collection of Frank Lloyd Wright-designed buildings in the world.

Historic Lakeland Downtown

Lakeland’s Historic Downtown district is a charming area that features beautifully restored buildings, unique boutiques, and a lively nightlife scene.

Natural Attractions of Lakeland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Circle B Bar Reserve

Located on the shores of Lake Hancock, Circle B Bar Reserve is a nature lover’s paradise. This 1,267-acre park offers hiking trails, birdwatching, and a chance to see alligators, bald eagles, and many more species in their natural habitat.

Hollis Garden

Hollis Garden is a beautiful botanical garden that showcases a variety of plants, flowers, and fountains. The garden features over 10,000 flowers and plants, making it a delight for anyone interested in gardening or simply enjoying beautiful landscapes.

Lake Mirror

Lake Mirror is a picturesque lake that is surrounded by scenic walking paths and beautiful, historic homes. The lake is also home to a variety of wildlife, making it a great spot for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakeland

Lakeland offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Some of the best trails include:

  • Fort Fraser Trail
  • Van Fleet Trail
  • Loyce E. Harpe Park Trail

These trails offer scenic views of the city’s lakes, nature preserves, and beautiful landscapes. They are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who want to explore Lakeland’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Lakeland: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with your family, or on a romantic getaway, Lakeland offers plenty of fun activities and tours for all ages. Some of the top things to do in Lakeland include:

  • Take a walking tour of the historic Munn Park
  • Visit the Polk Theatre for a live performance
  • Explore the Lakeland Farmers Market
  • Enjoy a scenic boat tour of the city’s lakes

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakeland

Day 1: Explore the Polk Museum of Art and the Frank Lloyd Wright Visitors Center Day 2: Visit the Winter Haven’s Legoland Florida Resort and relax at Lake Eva Park Day 3: Take a guided boat tour of the city’s lakes Day 4: Hike the Fort Fraser Trail and explore Hollis Garden Day 5: Visit Circle B Bar Reserve for bird watching and wildlife viewing Day 6: Explore the unique shops and restaurants in the historic downtown district Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery at Lake Mirror

Where to Stay in Lakeland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lakeland offers a diverse range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Terrace Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Lakeland
  • Lakehouse at Lake Bonny: A lakeside bed and breakfast that offers stunning views and a quiet environment
  • Hilton Garden Inn Lakeland: A modern and luxurious hotel with top-notch amenities

What to Buy in Lakeland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lakeland offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring back home. Some popular options include:

  • Citrus fruits like oranges, grapefruits, and lemons
  • Antique furniture and collectibles from downtown shops
  • Handmade jewelry from local artisans

Local Culture and Customs of Lakeland

Lakeland is known for its friendly and laid-back culture. Visitors can expect to encounter plenty of Southern hospitality from locals. Here are some customs and traditions that are unique to Lakeland:

  • Saying “hi” and “y’all” instead of “hello” and “you all”
  • Dressing casually for most occasions
  • Offering to help others in need

Best Time to Visit Lakeland

The best time to visit Lakeland, Florida is during the winter months (December to February) when the weather is mild and the skies are clear. This is also the time when most events and festivals take place in the city, making it a popular destination among tourists. The summer months (June to September) can be hot and humid, which may not be ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakeland

Do:

  • Explore the city’s lakes and nature preserves
  • Try local specialties like Cuban sandwiches and Florida Wines
  • Take a guided boat tour of the city’s waterways

Don’t:

  • Swim in the city’s lakes (alligators may be present)
  • Frown upon casual dress code at local restaurants and cafes
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray for outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakeland

Lakeland is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding unfamiliar areas at night and locking their doors and windows when staying in accommodations.

Health in Lakeland

Lakeland has plenty of healthcare facilities and hospitals in case of emergency or illness. Visitors should also be aware of the hot and humid weather and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Getting to Lakeland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lakeland is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Below are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Lakeland

  • Tampa International Airport (45 minutes away)
  • Orlando International Airport (1 hour away)

Bus Stations Near Lakeland

  • Greyhound Bus Station (downtown Lakeland)

Other Options

  • Renting a car or taking a taxi from nearby cities

How to Get Around in Lakeland

Lakeland is best explored by car. Visitors can also take advantage of the city’s ride-sharing options like Uber and Lyft, or the local bus system called Citrus Connection.

Where to Go Next from Lakeland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lakeland’s central location makes it an ideal base for exploring nearby cities and attractions. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Tampa (45 minutes away)
  • St. Petersburg (1 hour away)
  • Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, and other popular theme parks in Orlando (1 hour away)
  • Clearwater Beach (1.5 hours away)
  • Miami (3.5 hours away)

Conclusion

Lakeland, Florida is a hidden gem that offers visitors a perfect mixture of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious dining experiences. Whether you’re traveling solo, with your family, or on a romantic getaway, this charming city has something for everyone. From its scenic lakes and nature preserves to its historic landmarks and vibrant downtown, Lakeland is a destination that should not be missed.
