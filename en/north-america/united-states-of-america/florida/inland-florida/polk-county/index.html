Introduction to Polk County

Polk County, located in Inland Florida, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The county is home to some of the most popular national parks and nature preserves in the state, including the LEGOLAND theme park. If you’re a history buff, you’ll love exploring the county’s museums and historical landmarks showcasing centuries-old Floridian culture and customs. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Polk County offers something for every kind of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Polk County

Polk County boasts plenty of exciting places to visit. Here are some of the top attractions worth exploring:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Polk Museum of Art: This must-visit museum in Lakeland houses a vast collection of artworks, including contemporary, modern, and classical pieces.
  • Bartow Historical Museum: This museum showcases exhibits that tell the story of Bartow’s rich history, specifically focusing on wars, railroads, agriculture, and industry.
  • Frank Lloyd Wright Architecture at Florida Southern College: The campus of Florida Southern College houses 13 Wright-designed structures, making it the largest collection of his buildings in a single location.

Natural Attractions of Polk County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bok Tower Gardens: This national landmark garden in Lake Wales offers a stunning setting with a unique musical bell tower and multiple gardens to explore.
  • Circle B Bar Reserve: If you’re a nature lover, visit this 1,267-acre preserve for hiking and bird watching. You might spot one of the reserve’s resident alligators!
  • Lake Kissimmee State Park: This state park offers an excellent opportunity to explore nature and wildlife. Explore the oak-covered trails, stay in one of the cabins, or paddle along the lake’s winding waterways.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Polk County

Polk County is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular routes worth checking out:

  • Fort Fraser Trail: This 7.75-mile long trail is perfect for biking and hiking as it winds through the northern part of the county and around Lake Hancock.
  • Van Fleet Trail: It is 29 miles long, runs across rural areas from Mabel to Polk City, and offers a perfect opportunity to bike through natural Florida.
  • Lake Alfred Trail: This scenic trail is just under three miles and runs through the city’s downtown, offering lovely lake views.

Best Things to Do in Polk County: Activities and Tours

Polk County is replete with activities for visitors of all ages and interests, like:

  • LEGOLAND Florida Resort: This world-famous theme park features over 50 rides, shows, and attractions.
  • Safari Wilderness Ranch: Want to get up close and personal with some of nature’s wildest creatures, including water buffalo, elands, and zebras? Make a beeline for this unique wildlife attraction.
  • Winter Haven Chain of Lakes: Rent a boat and explore the four interconnecting lakes, take in the views, and appreciate the beauty of nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Polk County

If you’re wondering how to best spend a week in Polk County, this itinerary will help:

Day 1: Visit the Polk Museum of Art and explore downtown Lakeland’s streets.

Day 2: Enjoy a scenic hike at Circle B Bar Reserve, followed by a relaxing spa day at The Spa at Terrace Hotel.

Day 3: Spend a day at LEGOLAND® Florida Resort.

Day 4: Explore the Frank Lloyd Wright Architecture at Florida Southern College.

Day 5: Take a leisurely bike ride on Van Fleet Trail before spending the evening at Lake Mirror Park.

Day 6: Get up close with animals at Safari Wilderness Ranch, followed by a dinner at Harborside Restaurant.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip by exploring the Lake Kissimmee State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Polk County

The food and drink scene in Polk County is where Southern hospitality meets exotic flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Gouda Mac and Cheese at Osteria Italian Restaurant in Lakeland.
  • Catfish Tacos at The Poor Porker in Lakeland.
  • Mert’s Fried Chicken at Mert’s Heart and Soul in Bartow.

Local Beverages

  • Gatorade: Founded in Polk County in 1965 and now a staple drink throughout the United States
  • Grapefruit Beer at Florida Brewery in Auburndale.
  • Craft beer and wine at Grove Roots Brewing Company in Winter Haven.

Where to Stay in Polk County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Polk County offers travelers plenty of affordable accommodations. Here are some popular options:

  • Terrace Hotel: Lakeland’s Terrace Hotel, a historic boutique hotel, offers stunning views of Lake Mirror.
  • Lake Roy Beach Inn: This budget-friendly hotel is located in Winter Haven, just minutes away from LEGOLAND and other attractions.
  • Lake Wales Lodge: Just a stone’s throw from the Bok Tower Gardens, this comfortable motel offers comfortable lodgings at affordable rates.

What to Buy in Polk County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want something memorable to take back home? Here’s what to look for:

  • Citrus: Polk County produces a variety of citrus fruits that make for excellent souvenirs.
  • Arts and Crafts: Check out the Polk Museum of Art Gift Shop and the Historic Downtown Antique District for local handcrafted goods.
  • Fresh Honey: Grab a jar of honey at Merrily Farms in Lakeland or Sleeping Cat Apiary in Frostproof.

Local Culture and Customs of Polk County

Polk County is known for its Southern hospitality and welcoming culture. Some unique cultural elements include:

  • The Skunk Ape: A mythical creature that resembles Bigfoot, and the subject of local lore and urban legend.
  • Cracker Culture: The art of Florida’s rural cowboys and essential for shaping the local economy and landscape.
  • Mosaic Culture: Polk County is home to Mosaic, the world’s leading producer of fertilizers and phosphate-based products.

Best Time to Visit Polk County

The best time to visit Polk County, Florida, is from October to April, when the weather is cooler and dryer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Polk County

Do:

  • Do greet people with a smile and enjoy their company.
  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Do visit the historical landmarks and museums.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural habitats.
  • Don’t ignore the safety regulations and guidelines.
  • Don’t confuse Florida with Miami. Polk County has its unique charm that is worth exploring.

Safety and Health

Safety in Polk County

Polk County is generally safe, but visitors must take some necessary precautions like:

  • Some parks and wildlife reserves, like Circle B Bar Reserve, have alligators and other wildlife, so be mindful of your surroundings.
  • Keep your valuables in a locker at the hotel when you’re walking around downtown or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Polk County

Polk County has plenty of healthcare centers, including hospitals, clinics, and urgent care centers. The healthcare system would suffice in case of any medical emergency. However, it is essential to keep in mind some health-specific guidelines.

Getting to Polk County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Polk County

  • Tampa International Airport (TPA).
  • Orlando International Airport (MCO).

Bus Stations Near Polk County

  • Greyhound Bus Stations in Lakeland and Winter Haven.
  • Lynx Bus Service to and from Orlando.

Other Options

  • Polk County Area Transit (PCAT): Offers public transport services within the county.
  • Car rental: Most major car rental companies service Polk County.

How to Get Around in Polk County

Polk County is vast, so having a car would be the most convenient way to get around. Other options include:

  • The Polk County Area Transit (PCAT) provides public transportation but can be limited.
  • Taxis and ride-hailing services like Uber and Lyft are available.

Where to Go Next from Polk County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Orlando: Just an hour’s drive from Polk County, Orlando is home to world-class theme parks, shopping centers, and much more.
  • Tampa: Tampa is known for its cultural attractions, dining scene, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Polk County is a quintessential Floridian getaway that offers equal parts adventure, culture, and relaxation. With its natural beauty, southern hospitality, and unique attractions, Polk County is a hidden gem worth exploring. So, why wait? Start planning your trip to Polk County, Florida, today!
