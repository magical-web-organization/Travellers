Introduction to Dundee

Tucked away in the heart of Polk County, Florida lies the quaint town of Dundee. Often overlooked by its nearby bustling neighbors, this small-town gem is filled with rich history and natural beauty waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re looking for a leisurely weekend getaway or an outdoor adventure, Dundee has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Dundee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dundee Depot Museum

Located in the heart of Downtown Dundee, the Dundee Depot Museum is a restored railway station that now serves as a museum showcasing the history of the town. Visitors can explore exhibits on the railroad industry and the development of the citrus industry, both of which played a significant role in the area’s growth.

Bok Tower Gardens

A short drive from Dundee, the Bok Tower Gardens is a National Historic Landmark that boasts of a 205-foot bell tower and 250 acres of gardens. The tower houses a carillon, made of 60 bells that ring different melodies throughout the day. The serene gardens make for a perfect picnic spot or a peaceful stroll amongst the flora.

Natural Attractions of Dundee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Marie Park

Lake Marie Park is a beautiful, secluded park situated on the western shore of Lake Marie. The park features plenty of amenities, including picnic tables, a fishing pier, a playground, and a boat ramp, allowing visitors to enjoy the lake’s natural beauty to the fullest.

Ridge Scenic Highway

The Ridge Scenic Highway is a 38-mile long route that runs through the rolling hills of Polk County. The drive offers stunning views of the citrus groves, lakes and hilltops. Be sure to make a stop at the picturesque Lake Wales Overlook for an Instagram-worthy photo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dundee

Lake-to-Lake Trail

The Lake-to-Lake Trail is a three-mile long hiking/biking trail that connects Lake Marie to Lake Rochelle. The trail takes you through pine woods, oak hammocks, and wetlands, offering a glimpse of Florida’s natural flora and fauna.

Chain of Lakes Trail

The Chain of Lakes Trail is an 8.5-mile long trail that runs through the heart of Winter Haven and connects five of the city’s lakes. The trail is a popular spot for locals to cycle, jog, or simply take a leisurely stroll.

Best Things to Do in Dundee: Activities and Tours

Airboat Tours

Embark on a thrilling airboat ride through the swamps and wetlands of Central Florida. These tours offer an up-close view of the alligators, birds, and other wildlife that call this region home.

Fishing

Dundee and its surrounding areas boast plenty of fishing opportunities, with Lake Marie ranking amongst the top spots for bass fishing in the state. Visitors can also explore the numerous other lakes and rivers in the area, with guided fishing tours or by renting their boats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dundee

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Dundee Depot Museum and have lunch at the nearby Blue Bayou Café.
Day 2 Drive to the Bok Tower Gardens and spend the day exploring the gardens and enjoying a picnic.
Day 3 Take a guided fishing tour on Lake Marie and end the day with a sunset airboat ride.
Day 4 Visit the Ridge Scenic Highway lookout point and drive into Winter Haven to explore the Chain of Lakes Trail.
Day 5 Rent a boat and spend the day cruising around the serene Lake Eloise, one of the most picturesque lakes in the area.
Day 6 Take a day trip to nearby Legoland Florida, home to thrilling rides, water parks and thousands of Lego models.
Day 7 Spend the day indulging in local cuisine choices and shop for souvenirs at the Dundee Cafe & Gift Shop.

Food and Drink Scene of Dundee

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Catfish

Catfish is a staple in Southern cooking, and Dundee is no exception. Head to any of the local seafood restaurants to try some of the best fried catfish in the area.

Key Lime Pie

Florida is known for its Key lime pie, and Dundee is no exception. The town is home to several cafes serving up slices of this sweet and tangy dessert.

Local Beverages

Orange Juice

Florida is one of the largest orange producing states in the USA, making the locals proud of their orange juice. Dundee offers some of the freshest and tastiest orange juice you can ever have.

Craft Beer

The brewery scene in Central Florida is booming, and Dundee is no exception. You can find several breweries in the nearby Winter Haven and Lakeland areas, serving up a wide range of local and craft beer.

Where to Stay in Dundee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mary’s Airbnb

This cozy guesthouse is a perfect option for couples or solo travelers looking for a comfortable place to stay. The guesthouse offers a private entrance, patio, queen bed, and all the necessary amenities required for a comfortable stay.

Shergill Grand Hotel

Located in nearby Winter Haven, the Shergill Grand Hotel is a luxurious option for travelers looking for a touch of opulence on their trip. The hotel has a fitness center, swimming pool, and an in-house restaurant.

What to Buy in Dundee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Citrus Fruit

Dundee is known for its citrus fruits, especially its oranges. Bring home some fresh oranges, tangerines, or grapefruits as a tasty souvenir for your loved ones.

Honey

The nearby Haines City is home to several honey farms that produce some of the best raw honey in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Dundee

The people of Dundee are warm, welcoming, and take pride in their small-town charm. Politeness and respect go a long way here, and it is customary to greet others with a smile and a simple “hello.”

Best Time to Visit Dundee

The best time to visit Dundee is during the winter months from December to February when the temperature is milder, and the humidity is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dundee

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Be friendly to the locals.
  • Wear comfortable shoes, especially if you plan on exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural areas or water bodies.
  • Be rude or dismissive of the town or its people.
  • Overlook the simple pleasures of the small-town life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dundee

Dundee, like most small towns in America, is generally safe. However, visitors should practice caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Dundee

Dundee follows strict health and safety protocols in all its public spaces. Visitors are encouraged to carry their medicines and travel insurance.

Getting to Dundee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dundee

The nearest International airport to Dundee is Orlando International Airport, located approximately 50 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Dundee

The nearest bus station to Dundee is the Greyhound Bus station in Winter Haven, located approximately 8 miles away.

Other Options

Dundee is easily accessible via car, and visitors can rent cars from airports or nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Dundee

Dundee is a small town, and the best way to get around is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car or use ride-hailing apps to get around. Biking is also a popular option for those exploring the outdoors.

Where to Go Next from Dundee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Winter Haven

Winter Haven is a vibrant city located a short drive from Dundee, and is home to several attractions such as LEGOLAND Florida, the Chain of Lakes Trail, and the Cypress Gardens Adventure Park.

Lakeland

The nearby city of Lakeland boasts of a thriving arts and culture scene, stunning architecture, and beautiful outdoor spaces, including the Hollis Garden and Lake Mirror.

Conclusion

Dundee offers a unique mix of natural beauty, history, and modern amenities that make it a must-visit destination. Whether you’re a nature lover or a history buff, Dundee has plenty to offer, making it an ideal vacation spot for travelers seeking a laidback and affordable destination. Plan your trip today and experience the charm of this hidden gem in Central Florida.
