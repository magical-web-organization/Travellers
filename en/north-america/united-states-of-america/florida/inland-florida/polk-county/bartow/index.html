Bartow Travel Guide - Your Ultimate Tour Guide to Bartow

Bartow is a charming and historic city located in the heart of Central Florida. The city boasts a rich history, beautiful architecture, and stunning natural beauty that visitors from around the world flock to experience. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Bartow.

List of Places to Visit in Bartow

Bartow has something to offer for everyone. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or just looking to relax and unwind, you will find plenty of things to do and see in Bartow. Here are some of the best places to visit in Bartow.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

Bartow is a city rich in history and culture, and there is no shortage of museums and historical places to visit. Here are some must-visit attractions for history buffs:

  • Polk County History Center
  • Museum of Florida Art and Culture
  • L.B. Brown House Museum

Natural Attractions of Bartow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bartow is surrounded by natural beauty and is home to several parks and outdoor recreational areas. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Bartow:

  • Mary Holland Park
  • Colt Creek State Park
  • Circle B Bar Reserve

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bartow

Exploring Bartow’s natural beauty is best done on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Bartow:

  • Fort Fraser Trail
  • Lake Bonny Park Trail
  • Ridge Ranger Trail

Best Things to Do in Bartow: Activities and Tours

From exploring history to enjoying outdoor activities, there is plenty to do in Bartow. Here are some of the best things to do in Bartow:

  • Attend Bartow’s Annual Bloomin’ Arts Festival
  • Take a guided tour of the Polk Theatre
  • Go fishing at the Peace Creek Wildlife Management Area

Week-Long Itinerary for Bartow

If you are in Bartow for a week, here is a sample itinerary for you to explore the best of what Bartow has to offer:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Polk County History Center and the L.B. Brown House Museum
Day 2 Visit the Museum of Florida Art and Culture and take a guided tour of the Polk Theatre
Day 3 Hike at Colt Creek State Park
Day 4 Enjoy water sports at Lake Bonny Park
Day 5 Go birdwatching at the Circle B Bar Reserve
Day 6 Take a bike ride on the Fort Fraser Trail
Day 7 Enjoy shopping and dining in Downtown Bartow

Food and Drink Scene of Bartow

Bartow offers visitors a variety of culinary experiences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Bartow:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried chicken and biscuits from Carson’s Southern Kitchen
  • Fish tacos from La Fiesta Mexican Grill
  • Braised beef short rib from The Keg Steakhouse + Bar

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea from Rebecca’s Tea Room
  • Craft beer from Munn Park Brewery
  • Fruit wine from Henscratch Farms Vineyard & Winery

Where to Stay in Bartow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you are looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, Bartow has plenty of options for accommodation. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bartow:

  • Hampton Inn Bartow
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Bartow
  • The Stanford Inn

What to Buy in Bartow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking to bring home some souvenirs or local products, here are some items unique to Bartow worth considering:

  • Fresh citrus fruits from local farms
  • Handmade ceramics from Bartow Art Guild
  • Vintage items from Curiosity Market

Local Culture and Customs of Bartow

Bartow is a diverse city with a strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local culture and embrace the many traditions that define the city.

Best Time to Visit Bartow

The best time to visit Bartow is in the cooler months from November to February. During this time, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bartow

Do:

  • Visit the many museums and historical places
  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Bartow by hiking or biking on the trails

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or public areas
  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions
  • Ignore the natural beauty of Bartow by staying indoors all day

Safety and Health

Safety in Bartow

Bartow is a safe city, but it is always important to take precautions when traveling. Visitors are encouraged to follow these safety tips:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings at all times
  • Keep valuables out of sight and secure
  • Use common sense and good judgement

Health in Bartow

Visitors to Bartow should be up to date on routine vaccinations and take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as mosquito-borne illnesses are prevalent in Florida.

Getting to Bartow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bartow

The nearest major airport to Bartow is Tampa International Airport, located about 45 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Bartow

Greyhound Bus Lines serves Bartow with a bus station located in nearby Winter Haven.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for travelers arriving at the airport. Several car rental companies operate at Tampa International Airport.

How to Get Around in Bartow

Bartow is a compact city that is easy to get around on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Bartow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bartow is located in the heart of Central Florida, making it an ideal base for exploring nearby destinations. Here are some places to consider:

  • Legoland Florida Resort in Winter Haven
  • Walt Disney World Resort in Orlando
  • The beaches of the Gulf of Mexico

Conclusion

Bartow is a fascinating city with a rich history and natural beauty that is truly breathtaking. Whether you are visiting for the first time or returning for you ninth, we hope this travel guide has been helpful in planning your visit to Bartow.
