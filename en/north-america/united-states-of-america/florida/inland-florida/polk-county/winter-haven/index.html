Introduction to Winter Haven

Nestled in the heart of Polk County, Winter Haven is a beautiful inland city in Florida, USA. Boasting pleasant weather, friendly locals, and stunning natural beauty, this charming town has become a beloved hub for tourists seeking a warmer alternative to the coastal cities. From world-renowned theme parks to tranquil lakes, Winter Haven offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Winter Haven

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chain of Lakes Park: A former spring training facility for Major League Baseball, Chain of Lakes Park is now a popular venue for concerts, fairs, and other community events. Sports enthusiasts will also appreciate the museum showcasing the long history of Winter Haven sports.
  • Polk County History Center: This building was originally built in 1908 as a court house and jail, but now it’s a museum that tells the story of the county’s past. Exhibits include displays on the Seminole Wars, WWI, and local pioneer life.
  • Bok Tower Gardens: This stunning botanical garden, designated as a National Historic Landmark, features an enormous singing tower and carillon bells. Spend the day wandering through the gardens, birdwatching, and enjoying live music performances.

Natural Attractions of Winter Haven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Eloise: One of the most beautiful lakes in Florida, Lake Eloise is a must-see attraction when visiting Winter Haven. The lake is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and provides plenty of opportunities for fishing, boating, and water sports.
  • Circle B Bar Reserve: This nature preserve offers visitors a chance to experience the real Florida, complete with alligators, birds, and other wildlife. The hiking trails are known for their incredible views of Winter Haven, and the observation deck offers unmatched wildlife viewing.
  • Fantasy of Flight: This aviation-themed attraction is more than just a museum – it is an immersive experience that allows visitors to learn about the history of aviation, and even take a trip on a real aircraft. The site also includes an impressive collection of vintage aircraft and warbirds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Winter Haven

Winter Haven’s beautiful parks, lakes, and nature reserves make it a perfect destination for cyclists and hikers alike. There are several popular trails that locals and visitors can enjoy:

  • The Fort Fraser Trail: This 7.75-mile trail follows the former historic railroad corridor from Bartow to Lakeland. The route includes beautiful natural scenery and is paved and wide enough to accommodate both walkers and cyclists.
  • The Lake-to-Lake Trail: This trail takes you from Lake Silver to Lake Martha through the beautiful downtown Winter Haven. It’s a great way to explore the city while getting some exercise.
  • The Gator Creek Trail: This hiking trail in the upper Green Swamp covers almost 12 miles, passing through swamps, pine flatwoods, and live oak hammocks. The trail is a great place to spot local flora and fauna, including alligators and a variety of bird species.

Best Things to Do in Winter Haven: Activities and Tours

  • LEGOLAND: This world-renowned theme park features over 50 rides, shows, and attractions, all built with over 50 million LEGO bricks! From exciting roller coasters to water parks, LEGOLAND provides endless entertainment for kids and kids at heart.
  • Airboat Tours: Take a guided airboat tour of the beautiful lakes and wildlife refuges in and around Winter Haven. You’ll get up close and personal with alligators, turtles, and other wildlife while enjoying the thrill of airboat speed.
  • Water Ski Show: Winter Haven is home to the world-famous Cypress Gardens Water Ski Team. The team performs every Sunday afternoon on nearby Lake Eloise, offering an entertaining show filled with acrobatic stunts and water ski tricks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Winter Haven

Day 1: Lakes and Boats

  • Start in the morning at Lake Eloise for some fishing and boating.
  • Grab lunch at one of the many lakefront restaurants in town.
  • In the afternoon, explore the Chain of Lakes Park and the Polk County Sports Hall of Fame Museum.
  • End the day with a sunset cruise of the lakes.

Day 2: Theme Park Fun

  • Spend the day at LEGOLAND with the family, exploring dozens of rides, attractions, and shows.
  • Cap off the day with a dinner at the park, or try out one of the many excellent restaurants in town.

Day 3: History and Culture

  • Begin at the Polk County History Center, where you can explore exhibits on local pioneer life, the Civil War, and World War I.
  • For lunch, head to the historic downtown area, where you can sample some delicious local cuisine.
  • Spend the afternoon at the Bok Tower Gardens, enjoying the beautiful flora and tranquil setting.

Day 4: Nature and Wildlife

  • Start your day early with a hike at the Circle B Bar Reserve, where you can explore Florida’s natural habitats.
  • Grab lunch on your way to the Fantasy of Flight, where you can see classic planes and even experience a flight simulator.
  • For dinner, head to a local restaurant for some authentic Southern hospitality.

Day 5: Wildlife and Adventure

  • Start with an airboat ride on the beautiful lakes in the area, observing the rich wildlife of the region.
  • Attend the Cypress Gardens Water Ski Team show, which takes place every Sunday afternoon at Lake Eloise.
  • End the day with a dinner at The Twisted Prop Bar and Grille, one of the best dining spots in the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Winter Haven

Must-Try Dishes

  • Polk Salad: a vibrant salad made from locally grown greens, vegetables, and fruit.
  • Orange-Blossom Honey: a local delicacy that is used as a natural sweetener and can be found in many of the city’s desserts and drinks.
  • Florida Grouper: a fish dish that is caught locally and served fresh with a variety of sides.

Local Beverages

  • Winter Haven Brewing Company: this locally owned and operated brewery is famous for its craft beer selections.
  • Palm Ridge Reserve: a distillery that produces artisanal spirits made from locally grown and harvested ingredients.
  • Bistro 33: a cozy wine bar that features a selection of international wines and local craft beers.

Where to Stay in Winter Haven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Terrace Hotel: a historic hotel located in downtown Winter Haven that offers a charming, elegant atmosphere.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites: a budget-friendly option that offers great amenities and a convenient location.
  • Lake Roy Beach Inn: a lakeside inn that provides a cozy, retro atmosphere for visitors, complete with beach access.

What to Buy in Winter Haven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lego sets: LEGOLAND is a must-visit attraction in Winter Haven, and visitors can take home sets inspired by the park’s iconic attractions.
  • Citrus fruits: Winter Haven is famous for its perfectly ripe and delicious citrus fruits, ideal for baking and juicing.
  • Artisanal soaps and candles: handmade soaps and candles made from natural ingredients make great gifts for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Winter Haven

Winter Haven is known for its family-friendly, relaxed atmosphere. Visitors can expect to be greeted warmly by locals and to be welcomed into the town’s many community events and festivals. In Winter Haven, dressing casually is the norm, with most visitors and locals opting for shorts and sandals year-round. Because of the warm climate, outdoor activities are hugely popular in this lively town.

Best Time to Visit Winter Haven

Winter Haven has a warm, subtropical climate that’s perfect for a visit any time of year. However, it’s worth noting that the summer months can be oppressively hot, with temperatures reaching well over 90 degrees Fahrenheit. The best time to visit is between October and May when the weather is more temperate, and there are also fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Winter Haven

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the many parks and natural reserves in the area.
  • Observe wildlife from a safe distance.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb wildlife.
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and other essentials for staying safe in the sun.
  • Disrespect locals or cultural customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Winter Haven

Winter Haven is a very safe city to visit, with relatively low crime rates. However, visitors should still take standard precautions to remain safe, including locking cars and hotel rooms, securing valuables, and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Winter Haven

When visiting Winter Haven, it’s important to take precautions to protect yourself from the sun, including wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated. Visitors should also be aware of local wildlife, such as alligators, and take caution when exploring natural reserves.

Getting to Winter Haven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Winter Haven is located in Central Florida and is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Winter Haven

  • Orlando International Airport: Located about 55 miles away from Winter Haven, this airport is the closest major airport to the city, with frequent flights to and from major destinations worldwide.
  • Tampa International Airport: This airport is located about 60 miles away from Winter Haven and offers direct flights to many major cities in the US and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Winter Haven

  • Greyhound Station: The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in downtown Winter Haven in close proximity to several hotels, attractions, and restaurants.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: Car rental services are available from both airports.
  • Taxi, Uber and Lyft services: These services are widely available throughout the area.

How to Get Around in Winter Haven

Winter Haven is a flat, bike-friendly town with a great network of bike lanes and trails. Visitors can easily rent bicycles or other forms of transportation from local shops and businesses. Visitors can also use taxi, Uber, or Lyft services, or simply take a leisurely stroll around town.

Where to Go Next from Winter Haven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tampa Bay: Located less than 90 minutes away from Winter Haven, Tampa offers visitors a diverse range of attractions, including theme parks, professional sports teams, and a thriving art scene.
  • Gulf Islands National Seashore: This tranquil nature preserve, located just over 3 hours away, features pristine white-sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and opportunities for outdoor activities like snorkeling and kayaking.
  • St. Augustine: This historic city is located approximately 3 hours from Winter Haven and is known for its charming architecture, quaint streets, and numerous historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Winter Haven, with its charming downtown area, beautiful natural reserves, and world-renowned theme parks, is a must-visit destination for those seeking a relaxing, laid-back getaway in Florida. The city has plenty of entertainment for adults and children alike, and visitors will find ample opportunities to engage with local culture, cuisine, and customs. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your perfect trip, and that your visit to Winter Haven is as unforgettable as it is enjoyable.
