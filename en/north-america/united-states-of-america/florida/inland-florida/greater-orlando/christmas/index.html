Introduction to Christmas

Christmas is a special holiday celebrated worldwide with immense joy and festivity. The holiday season represents love, giving and spending time with family and friends. In Greater Orlando, Christmas is celebrated with a unique flair, with its own set of customs, traditions and events.

List of Places to Visit in Christmas

Whether you’re a local or visitor, there is plenty to see in Christmas, Inland Florida. From museums and historical sites to natural attractions, hiking/biking routes, activities and tours, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those curious about the rich history and culture of Christmas, this town has several museums and historical places worth visiting.

  • Fort Christmas Historical Park: This museum showcases the early history of Florida settlers, featuring reenactments, demonstrations and exhibits about life in the 19th century.
  • Happy Days Emporium: A relic of 1950s nostalgia, this local shop offers old-fashioned candy, nostalgic gifts and vintage memorabilia.
  • Harry P. Leu Gardens: Take a stroll through the 50-acre botanical garden and enjoy the flora and fauna that Florida is known for.

Natural Attractions of Christmas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Christmas is surrounded by lush, natural beauty, making it an ideal spot for outdoor activities.

  • Little Big Econ State Forest: This 10,000 acre forest is perfect for hiking, biking, and fishing. The forest is home to deer, wild turkey, black bears, alligators and over 170 species of birds.
  • Lake Pickett: Go fishing, kayaking or boating in one of the most picturesque lakes in Orlando’s East side.
  • Tosohatchee Wildlife Management Area: This 30,000-acre area is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can hike, fish, hunt or simply take in the natural surrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Christmas

For the more adventurous souls, Christmas has an abundance of hiking and biking routes that showcase the natural beauty of the surrounding area.

  • Orlando Wetlands Park: This park has over 100 miles of trails that pass through marshes and swamps and is home to 250 bird species, making it a prime spot for birdwatching.
  • Little Big Econ State Forest: Explore over 30 miles of trails for hiking and biking that weave through a pine forest and alongside the Little Econ River.

Best Things to Do in Christmas: Activities and Tours

Christmas offers a variety of activities and tours that offer a unique experience for visitors.

  • Santa’s Farm and Christmas Tree Forest: Take a ride on the horse-drawn carriage, participate in the scavenger hunts, take pictures with Santa or explore the Christmas Tree trail with over 40,000 trees.
  • Orlando Balloon Rides: Take a helicopter or hot air balloon ride and view the Christmas scenery from above.
  • Airboat rides on St. John’s: Glide through natural wildlife habitats on an airboat to spot turtles, exotic birds, alligators, and even deer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Christmas

Here’s a suggested itinerary that shows the best of what Christmas has to offer:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Fort Christmas Historical Park and enjoy the exhibits and reenactments.
Day 2 Explore Little Big Econ State Forest on foot or bike.
Day 3 Take an airboat ride and view wild alligators and other wildlife.
Day 4 Discover the botanical beauty of Harry P. Leu Gardens.
Day 5 Visit Santa’s Farm and Christmas Tree Forest.
Day 6 Take a hot air balloon or helicopter ride to see the Christmas lights from above.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy a tranquil visit to Lake Pickett.

Food and Drink Scene of Christmas

Christmas is home to several restaurants and shops that offer a tasty selection of traditional and unique dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: Sonny’s BBQ is a popular restaurant that serves ribs, pulled pork, and barbecue chicken.
  • Bacon and Eggs: The Town House restaurant offers a delicious breakfast of bacon and eggs, served home-style with biscuits or toast.
  • Seafood: Dixie Crossroads is a seafood restaurant famous for its rock shrimp, a sweet and delectable crustacean.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is renowned for its juicy, delicious oranges. Try a glass of freshly squeezed orange juice at the local Dunkin Donuts or Starbucks.
  • Craft Beer: Located nearby in Sanford is the Wop’s Hops Brewing Company, a local brewery producing a range of beers including IPA, Wheat, Stout, and more.

Where to Stay in Christmas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Christmas can choose from several budget, mid-range, and luxury accommodation options.

  • Comfort Inn and Suites near UCF: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, an outdoor pool and is conveniently located near the University of Central Florida.
  • Sundial Motel: A budget-friendly establishment with clean and spacious rooms, easy parking, and friendly staff.
  • Casa Coquina Bed and Breakfast: This magnificent old mansion provides an exquisite experience at a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Christmas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Christmas Ornaments: Christmas Tree Forest offers over 20,000 Christmas ornaments for purchase.
  • Florida Orange Blossom Honey: Bee Friends Farm offer pure, natural, unheated honey made from Florida orange blossoms.
  • Happy Days Emporium: A shop filled with nostalgic gifts and unique souvenirs for all ages.

Local Culture and Customs of Christmas

Inland Florida has its own customs and traditions when it comes to Christmas.

  • Christmas Lights: In the month of December, many neighborhoods in Christmas light up their homes with festive lights, transforming the area into a winter wonderland.
  • Christmas Parades: The Christmas parade is a yearly tradition, with floats from the local schools, police, and fire departments.
  • Holiday Spirit: People in Christmas are friendly, welcoming and full of holiday cheer.

Best Time to Visit Christmas

The best time to visit Christmas is in the month of December when the area comes alive with holiday cheer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Christmas

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Walk alone in dark areas at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Christmas

Christmas is a safe area, but visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid problems.

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Always be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Christmas

Visitors won’t experience any specific health risks in Christmas. However, to avoid any unwanted health issues, be sure to use sunscreen, drink plenty of water, and dress appropriately for the weather.

Getting to Christmas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Christmas

  • Orlando International Airport is the nearest international airport to Christmas, located approximately 32 miles away.
  • Orlando Sanford International Airport is another option, located 17 miles northeast of downtown Orlando.

Bus Stations Near Christmas

  • Orlando Greyhound Bus Station: Offers bus services to and from major cities in Florida and the United States, including New York, Atlanta, and Miami.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: A rental car provides the option for private transportation and greater flexibility when exploring the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Christmas

Public transportation is limited in Christmas, and most visitors prefer to rent a car or use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Christmas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Orlando: Visit Walt Disney World, Universal Orlando, Epcot, and other famous attractions in Orlando.
  • Kennedy Space Center: Visit the space museum, witness rocket launches, and explore the history of space exploration.
  • St. Augustine: Travel to the oldest city in America, known for its historic attractions, museums, galleries, and wineries.

Conclusion

Christmas, Inland Florida, offers a unique experience for visitors with its museums, outdoor activities, natural beauty, holiday customs, and delicious cuisine. Whatever your travel style and preferences, you’re sure to have a memorable time in this charming town.
