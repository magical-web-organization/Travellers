Winter Park Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Winter Park is a picturesque town located in Greater Orlando, in Inland Florida. The town is known for its beautiful lakes, unique history, and cultural attractions. If you are planning a trip to Winter Park, this travel guide will help you discover the town’s best-kept secrets and create unforgettable memories.

Introduction to Winter Park

Winter Park is a small town with a big heart. Founded in the late 19th century, the town is known for its historic buildings, tree-lined streets, and beautiful parks. Winter Park’s natural beauty is showcased by its many lakes, which provide endless opportunities for outdoor recreation and water sports. The town is home to several prestigious museums and art galleries, making it a hub for culture and arts in Central Florida.

List of Places to Visit in Winter Park

Winter Park has plenty of things to offer for its visitors. Here is a list of must-visit places in Winter Park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Morse Museum of American Art: This museum houses the world’s most comprehensive collection of the works of Louis Comfort Tiffany, including his famous stained-glass windows.
  • Albin Polasek Museum & Sculpture Gardens: This museum is named after Czech-born American sculptor Albin Polasek and features his sculptures and gardens.
  • Winter Park Historical Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Winter Park from the earliest Native American inhabitants to modern-day developments.

Natural Attractions of Winter Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kraft Azalea Garden: This park is located on the shore of Lake Maitland and features a beautiful boardwalk, picnic areas, and a stunning view of the lake.
  • Central Park: This park is located in the heart of downtown Winter Park and features beautiful gardens, fountains, and sculptures.
  • Lake Osceola: This lake is one of the seven Winter Park chain of lakes and is ideal for boating, water sports, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Winter Park

Winter Park has several scenic trails for hikers and bikers to explore, including:

  • Cady Way Trail: This trail runs 6.5 miles from Winter Park to Orlando and is perfect for walking, biking, and skating.
  • Lake Baldwin Trail: This trail runs 2.5 miles around Lake Baldwin in Winter Park and is ideal for biking and jogging.
  • Seminole Cross Trail: This trail runs 4 miles through Winter Park and is perfect for walking, running and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Winter Park: Activities and Tours

Experience Winter Park’s local culture, art, and history by taking one of the following tours or participating in one of the following activities:

  • Winter Park Scenic Boat Tour: Board a pontoon boat and enjoy a one-hour tour of Winter Park’s beautiful chain of lakes, where you can witness locals kayaking, paddleboarding, and fishing.
  • Brewery Tour: Satisfy your beer cravings and learn more about the brewing process by signing up for a brewery tour at Purple Sip or Crooked Can Brewing Company.
  • Enzian Theater: Catch a film or take part in their immersive cultural events with live theater performances, poetry readings, and art exhibitions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Winter Park

For the ultimate experience in Winter Park, consider following this suggested week-long itinerary that includes all the best things to see and do:

  • Day 1: Explore Winter Park’s museums and galleries, including the Morse Museum of American Art and the Albin Polasek Museum & Sculpture Gardens.
  • Day 2: Relax and enjoy a scenic boat tour around Winter Park’s chain of lakes.
  • Day 3: Take a walking tour of Winter Park’s historic district and discover the town’s unique architecture and culture.
  • Day 4: Hike or bike the Cady Way Trail and enjoy the Florida outdoors.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Disney World, Universal Studios, or The Kennedy Space Center.
  • Day 6: Sample the local craft beer and take a brewery tour.
  • Day 7: Enjoy the charming ambiance of Winter Park, take a leisurely stroll around Central Park, and shop for souvenirs and local products to bring back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Winter Park

Winter Park’s food and drink scene is thriving, with plenty of choices for foodies and craft beer enthusiasts.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheddar Biscuits & Gravy at Briarpatch Restaurant: This is a delightful breakfast dish that has become a Winter Park favorite.
  • Banh Mi at Swine & Sons Provisions: This sandwich is packed with savory flavors and is a favorite of locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  • Winter Park Honey: This honey is unique to Winter Park and has a distinct taste that comes from bees gathering nectar from local citrus blossoms and tropical flowers.
  • Craft Beer Tasting: Winter Park has several breweries, including the Crooked Can Brewing Company, which offers beer flights to visitors.

Where to Stay in Winter Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Winter Park has plenty of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are the top-rated options:

  • The Alfond Inn: This award-winning boutique hotel is located in the heart of downtown Winter Park and features luxury rooms, a rooftop pool, and an art collection.
  • Park Plaza Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the Winter Park Historic District and offers guests an authentic experience with its antique furnishings and charming ambiance.
  • Thurston House: This bed and breakfast is located in a quiet residential neighborhood and offers cozy rooms with private bathrooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Winter Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Winter Park, be sure to pick up some unique souvenirs that capture the town’s spirit. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Tiffany-style lamp: Purchase a lamp from the artisan glass makers at the Morse Museum of American Art.
  • Winter Park honey: Pick up some delicious honey that is only available in Winter Park.
  • Local art: Browse the galleries and Art festivals during your visit and take home a unique piece of art from local painters and sculptors.

Local Culture and Customs of Winter Park

Winter Park is a friendly, laid-back town that values the arts and supports local businesses. Visitors should respect the town’s culture and customs by being courteous to locals, respecting the natural beauty, and supporting local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Winter Park

Winter Park is a year-round destination. However, the best time to visit is from November to April when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Winter Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Winter Park:

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the town and its lakes
  • Be courteous to locals and fellow tourists
  • Explore the town and discover its unique history and culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property
  • Be disruptive or rude to locals and fellow tourists
  • Ignore the pedestrian signs and warnings

Safety and Health

Safety in Winter Park

Winter Park is a safe and friendly town. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions such as locking their cars, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Winter Park

Visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from the sun, including wearing sunscreen, a hat, and staying hydrated. Visitors should also be aware of the prevalence of mosquitoes and protect themselves from mosquito bites.

Getting to Winter Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Winter Park is accessible by several transportation options. Here are the most convenient ways to get to Winter Park:

Airports Near Winter Park

The closest airports to Winter Park are:

  • Orlando International Airport (MCO): 17 miles away
  • Orlando Sanford International Airport (SFB): 30 miles away

Bus Stations Near Winter Park

The closest bus stations to Winter Park are:

  • Lynx Central Station in Downtown Orlando
  • Amtrak Station in Winter Park

Other Options

Visitors may also take a taxi, Uber or rent a car to get to Winter Park from the airport.

How to Get Around in Winter Park

Winter Park is a walkable town. However, visitors may also use the following transportation options to get around town:

  • Lynx Bus Service: Lynx bus service has several routes that run through Winter Park and the surrounding areas.
  • Bicycle rentals: Several bike rental shops offer rentals for visitors who want to explore Winter Park on a bike.
  • Taxis and Uber: Taxis and Uber are available for visitors who prefer to ride.

Where to Go Next from Winter Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Winter Park is in a prime location for visitors who want to explore other parts of Central Florida. Here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Orlando: Explore the theme parks or take a stroll around Lake Eola.
  • Daytona Beach: Soak up the sun on the beach and enjoy the famous Daytona International Speedway.
  • Kennedy Space Center: Take a tour and learn about NASA’s space programs.

Conclusion

Winter Park is a unique and charming town that offers visitors a blend of natural beauty, cultural history, and vibrant local scene. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, exploring local culture or relaxing, Winter Park has something to offer. With the help of this guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in Winter Park!
