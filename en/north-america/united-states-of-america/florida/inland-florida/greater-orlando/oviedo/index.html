Introduction to Oviedo

Nestled in Greater Orlando, in Inland Florida, Oviedo is a charming city known for its natural beauty, rich history, and cultural attractions. The city is a popular destination among tourists looking for a peaceful and relaxed environment away from the hustle and bustle of modern life.

List of Places to Visit in Oviedo

Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or art lover, Oviedo has something for everyone. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Oviedo.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oviedo Preservation Project Museum: Exhibits artifacts and memorabilia from Oviedo’s history.
  • Lawton House: A historic house with guided tours showcasing the lifestyle of Oviedo’s pioneers.
  • Black Hammock Adventures Airboat Tours: Explore the natural beauty of Lake Jesup on an airboat.

Natural Attractions of Oviedo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Econlockhatchee River: A great spot to kayak, fish, or simply relax by the riverside.
  • Oviedo on the Park: A picturesque park with a lake, splash pad, amphitheater, and playgrounds.
  • Cross Seminole Trail: A 23-mile trail perfect for hiking, biking, or jogging.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oviedo

Oviedo is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in Florida. Here are a few recommendations for adventure-seekers.

  • Little Big Econ State Forest Hiking Trail: A 10-mile trail with scenic and challenging terrains.
  • Seminole Wekiva Trail: A 14-mile trail that winds through natural wetlands, residential neighborhoods and commercial areas.
  • Black Hammock Wilderness Area: A 700-acre natural preserve perfect for hiking and observing Florida’s wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Oviedo: Activities and Tours

From food tours to cultural exhibitions, there’s no shortage of fun things to do in Oviedo. Here are some of the top activities and tours in the city.

  • Taste of Oviedo Food Festival: Oviedo’s biggest annual event with food, drinks, music, and entertainment.
  • Oviedo Farmers Market: Held every weekend, the market showcases local produce, homemade crafts, and live music.
  • Oviedo Historical Society: Offers guided tours and presentations on the history of Oviedo.

Food and Drink Scene of Oviedo

Oviedo has a thriving culinary scene that is sure to please any foodie. Here are some top dishes and beverages to try while in the city.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gator Bites: Deep-fried alligator meat served as an appetizer at Black Hammock Restaurant.
  • Fried Chicken and Waffles: A southern classic served at The Porch.
  • Cuban Sandwich: A traditional sandwich made with ham, roast pork, Swiss cheese, pickles, mustard, and bread at Bagel King Bakery.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is known for its fresh and delicious orange juice.
  • Craft Beer: Oviedo has several craft breweries, including Oviedo Brewing Company and Bowigens Beer Company.

Where to Stay in Oviedo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oviedo has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks for places to stay.

  • The Westin Lake Mary: A luxurious hotel in a great location with top-notch amenities.
  • Budget Inn Sanford: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • The Cuban House Inn: A guesthouse with a cozy and authentic Cuban-inspired design.

What to Buy in Oviedo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and locally-made souvenirs, Oviedo has plenty of options to choose from, such as:

  • Handmade soaps and lotions from The Soap Lady
  • Fresh citrus fruits and jams from Pappy’s Patch
  • Locally-made craft beer from Oviedo Brewing Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Oviedo

Oviedo, like most of Florida, has a laidback vibe and a rich cultural heritage reflected through its architecture, historic landmarks, and outdoor activities. Visitors should respect the local customs and appreciate the area’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Oviedo

The best time to visit Oviedo is during the winter or early spring months (December to April). The weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. However, these months can be more expensive, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oviedo

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts of Oviedo to keep in mind while visiting.

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Oviedo’s rivers and trails.
  • Try the local cuisine, including fresh seafood and Cuban-inspired eats.
  • Respect the city’s cultural landmarks and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural areas.
  • Ignore the local wildlife, such as alligators and snakes.
  • Overlook the charming downtown area of Oviedo.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oviedo

Oviedo is generally considered safe for visitors, but it’s best to take standard safety precautions such as locking doors and windows, being aware of your surroundings, and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Oviedo

Visitors should drink plenty of water and use sun protection when exploring the natural areas of Oviedo. Additionally, be aware of local wildlife, such as alligators, and follow any warning signs or guidelines.

Getting to Oviedo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oviedo

The closest airport to Oviedo is Orlando Sanford International Airport (SFB) that is around 15 miles away, while Orlando International Airport (MCO) is approximately 24 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Oviedo

The nearest Greyhound bus station is in Daytona Beach, approximately 38 miles away. However, there are several local bus services in the region including LYNX bus that serves Central Florida.

Other Options

Taxi, Uber and Lyft services are available in Oviedo city. Visitors can also rent cars from major car rental companies located at the airports or any nearby rental location.

How to Get Around in Oviedo

While having a car is the most convenient way to explore Oviedo, visitors can also use Uber, Lyft, or local bus services to get around. For outdoor activities, renting a bike or kayak is a popular option as well.

Where to Go Next from Oviedo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oviedo is close to a number of other popular destinations worth checking out, such as:

  • Orlando: Home to countless theme parks, shopping, and attractions.
  • Daytona Beach: Known for its white-sand beaches and motorsports.
  • Kennedy Space Center: A fascinating space museum and launch site for NASA missions.

Conclusion

Oviedo may be a small city, but it’s got a lot to offer visitors. From its natural beauty to its rich cultural heritage, Oviedo is a destination worth exploring for any traveler looking for a peaceful and refreshing vacation.
