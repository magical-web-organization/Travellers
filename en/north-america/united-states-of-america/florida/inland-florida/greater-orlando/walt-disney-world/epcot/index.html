Introduction to Epcot

Epcot, located in Walt Disney World in Greater Orlando, is a unique theme park dedicated to showcasing the achievements of human civilization and the beauty of nature. The park is divided into two main sections: Future World and World Showcase. Future World is focused on technological innovations and scientific exploration, while World Showcase is a collection of pavilions representing different countries.

Visitors can expect to learn, explore, taste, and have fun in Epcot. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the top places to visit, hike, bike, stay, eat and drink, and shop in Epcot. You’ll also learn about the local culture and customs, safety and health information, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Epcot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The American Adventure: This attraction features a 35-minute show about the history and culture of the United States, narrated by Audio-Animatronics versions of Benjamin Franklin and Mark Twain.

  2. The Seas with Nemo & Friends: This exhibit is home to over 6,000 sea creatures, including sharks, rays, and sea turtles. Visitors can also ride a clamshell and journey to find Nemo in an immersive attraction.

  3. Mission: SPACE: This simulator ride simulates a space mission to Mars, testing the limits of your body and mind.

  4. Spaceship Earth: This iconic attraction takes visitors on a journey through time, exploring the history of human communication.

  5. Living with the Land: This boat ride explores the innovative ways that humans are growing crops and raising animals in harmony with nature.

Natural Attractions of Epcot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Gardens of Epcot: Epcot is home to some of the most beautiful gardens in the world, featuring exotic plants, flowers, and topiaries.

  2. Soarin’ Around the World: This virtual hang glider takes visitors on a breathtaking tour of the world’s most iconic landmarks and natural wonders.

  3. Test Track: This exhilarating ride lets visitors design their own virtual car and then test it on a high-speed track.

  4. Epcot Forever: This nighttime spectacular features amazing fireworks and music, showcasing the history and achievements of Epcot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Epcot

  1. Epcot to Disney’s Hollywood Studios: This route is about 2.9 miles long and takes visitors through the scenic World Showcase Lagoon, Disney’s Beach Club Resort, and Disney’s Boardwalk.

  2. Epcot Resort Loop: This loop is about 2.5 miles long and takes visitors around Epcot and Disney’s Boardwalk. It’s a great way to see the beautiful architecture and gardens of Epcot.

Best Things to Do in Epcot: Activities and Tours

  1. Behind the Seeds Tour: This walking tour explores the innovative ways that humans are growing crops and raising animals in harmony with nature.

  2. World Showcase Passport: This interactive activity challenges visitors to explore the pavilions of World Showcase and collect stamps in their passport.

  3. Frozen Ever After Dessert Party: This special event includes a dessert reception and reserved seating for the “Frozen Ever After” attraction.

Food and Drink Scene of Epcot

Must-Try Dishes

  1. School Bread (Norway Pavilion): This Norwegian treat is a sweet, cardamom-spiced roll filled with vanilla custard and topped with icing and toasted coconut.

  2. Braised Pork (China Pavilion): This dish is served with steamed rice and seasoned bok choy. The pork is tender and flavored with soy sauce and ginger.

  3. Chips and Guacamole (Mexico Pavilion): This classic Mexican snack is made with fresh, homemade guacamole and crispy, salty tortilla chips.

Local Beverages

  1. Frozen Margarita (Mexico Pavilion): This classic cocktail is made with tequila, lime juice, and orange liqueur. The frozen version is perfect for a hot day.

  2. Grand Marnier Slush (France Pavilion): This refreshing slushie is made with Grand Marnier, orange, rum, and lemon juice.

  3. Sake (Japan Pavilion): This traditional Japanese rice wine is a great way to experience a different culture.

Where to Stay in Epcot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Disney’s Beach Club Resort: This hotel is located within walking distance of Epcot and features a beach-style pool and water slide.

  2. Disney’s Boardwalk Inn: This hotel is themed after an Atlantic City boardwalk and features several restaurants and a lively nightlife scene.

  3. Walt Disney World Swan and Dolphin: This hotel is adjacent to Epcot and features access to several Disney perks, like early park entry.

What to Buy in Epcot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Mickey Mouse Ears: This classic souvenir is a must-have for any Disney fan. Customize your ears with your name or favorite Disney character.

  2. Epcot Food and Wine Festival Merchandise: This annual event showcases dishes and drinks from around the world. Pick up a souvenir mug or wine glass to remember the experience.

  3. Artwork from the pavilions: Each pavilion features unique artwork and crafts representative of the culture. Purchase a piece that speaks to you.

Local Culture and Customs of Epcot

Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting the pavilions of World Showcase. Take the time to learn about each culture and appreciate the differences. Be courteous to other guests and follow the park’s rules and regulations.

Best Time to Visit Epcot

The best time to visit Epcot is during the fall, specifically in September or October. This is when the park hosts the Epcot International Food & Wine Festival, a can’t-miss event for foodies and wine lovers. The weather is also cooler during this time of year, making it more comfortable to enjoy the outdoor attractions and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Epcot

Do:

  1. Plan ahead to make the most of your visit.

  2. Wear comfortable clothing and shoes.

  3. Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Smoke, vape, or consume alcohol outside of designated areas.

  2. Touch or feed the animals at The Seas with Nemo & Friends exhibit.

  3. Bring outside food or beverage into the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Epcot

Epcot has a team of trained security personnel and state-of-the-art surveillance systems to ensure the safety of its guests. Visitors should follow park rules, be aware of their surroundings, and report any suspicious activity to a cast member immediately.

Health in Epcot

Epcot offers several options for those with dietary restrictions or food allergies. Guests can request special menus or speak with a chef at most restaurants. There are also first aid stations located throughout the park for any medical needs.

Getting to Epcot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Epcot

  1. Orlando International Airport: This airport is located about 20 minutes from Epcot and offers several transportation options, such as rental cars, shuttles, and taxis.

Bus Stations Near Epcot

  1. Epcot Bus Station: This station is located within walking distance of Epcot and offers transportation to other parts of Walt Disney World.

Other Options

  1. Monorail: The Walt Disney World Monorail system connects Epcot to Magic Kingdom and several hotels near Epcot.

  2. Water taxis: These boats connect Epcot to other parts of Walt Disney World, such as Disney’s Hollywood Studios and several hotels.

How to Get Around in Epcot

Epcot offers several options for getting around the park, including walking, riding a scooter or wheelchair, or taking a Friendship boat or Disney Skyliner gondola.

Where to Go Next from Epcot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Disney’s Hollywood Studios: This park is located adjacent to Epcot and features classic Hollywood attractions and experiences.

  2. Disney Springs: This shopping and dining district is located about 15 minutes from Epcot and features several upscale restaurants and designer shops.

  3. Kennedy Space Center: This world-renowned facility is about an hour’s drive from Epcot and features exhibits and attractions dedicated to space exploration and technology.

Conclusion

Epcot is a must-visit theme park with a unique blend of culture, technology, and nature. From the adventurous thrill rides to the peaceful gardens, there is something for everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your visit to Epcot and make the most of your experience.
