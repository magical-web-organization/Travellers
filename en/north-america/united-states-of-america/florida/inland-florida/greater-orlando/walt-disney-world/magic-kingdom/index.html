Introduction to Magic Kingdom

Magic Kingdom is one of the four parks in Walt Disney World located in Greater Orlando, Inland Florida. It is the place where dreams come alive, and the world of fairy tales becomes a reality. It has six themed lands, Main Street U.S.A, Tomorrowland, Fantasyland, Liberty Square, Frontierland, and Adventureland. Each land has its unique attractions, shows, parades, and restaurants, making it a must-visit destination for kids and adults alike.

List of Places to Visit in Magic Kingdom

Here are some of the absolute must-visit places in Magic Kingdom:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Hall of Presidents - This attraction features a 25-minute show that presents all the U.S. Presidents through animatronic figures, surrounding visitors by American history.
  • Walt Disney’s Carousel of Progress - Here, visitors can enjoy a show that retells the story of an American family from the 1900s to the present.
  • The Haunted Mansion - This attraction is a mix of technology and spooky effects that make visitors feel like they are a part of the story. The mansion’s décor is worth exploring, too.

Natural Attractions of Magic Kingdom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tom Sawyer Island - A secret island located at the center of Rivers of America, visitors can enjoy doing treasure hunting, exploring the caves, and relaxing in the shade of the stunning oak trees.
  • Swiss Family Treehouse - This attraction allows visitors to climb a treehouse that was inspired by the Robinson family in the movie Swiss Family Robinson.
  • The Liberty Belle Riverboat - This boat transports visitors through rivers of America, providing a stunning view of Tom Sawyer Island, Liberty Square, and Frontierland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Magic Kingdom

  • Tom Sawyer Island - It is one of the best places for a quiet escape from the bustling crowds. The island has caves, nature trails, and an old-fashioned fort that make visitors feel like they are stepping back in time.
  • Frontierland - Here, visitors can take a hiking trail that leads to the back of the park. Along the way, they can explore the unique flora and fauna of Florida.
  • Around the World - Visitors can rent a bike or rollerblades at Disney’s Polynesian Village and ride around the property. The scenic view of the resorts and restaurants is worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Magic Kingdom: Activities and Tours

  • Fantasyland Rides - From Peter Pan’s Flight to Snow White’s Scary Adventures, these rides provide an opportunity to experience fairy-tale fantasies.
  • Main Street Trolley Show and Parades - The Trolley Show is a family-friendly parade with dance and songs down Main Street. The daily parades include characters in costumes, floats, and music that make visitors want to dance along.
  • Disney’s Keys to the Kingdom Tour - This tour provides an unforgettable backstage experience of the Magic Kingdom. Visitors learn about Walt Disney’s vision as they visit secret tunnels and see how the park operates behind the scenes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Magic Kingdom

Day 1 - Main Street U.S.A, Fantasyland, and Tomorrowland Day 2 - Adventureland and Frontierland Day 3 - EPCOT Day 4 - Hollywood Studios Day 5 - Animal Kingdom Day 6 - Resort Day, including Disney’s Polynesian Village and Grand Floridian Day 7 - Second-day Magic Kingdom

Food and Drink Scene of Magic Kingdom

Here are the must-try food and drink options in Magic Kingdom:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dole Whip - This pineapple-flavored soft-serve is an essential treat in Magic Kingdom that can’t be missed.
  • Churros - They are crispy-fried dough sprinkled with cinnamon and sugar, and paired with a chocolate dipping sauce.
  • Corn Dog Nuggets - These fried hot dog bites are a favorite among kids and adults, and they are easy to eat on the go.

Local Beverages

  • LeFou’s Brew - This non-alcoholic drink is inspired by the movie Beauty and the Beast. It is a mix of frozen apple juice, fruity flavors, and topped off with a mango foam.
  • Be Our Guest Restaurant - This restaurant offers a selection of French wines and beers that can be paired with an assortment of entrees.

Where to Stay in Magic Kingdom

Here are some of the best hotel options for visitors to Magic Kingdom:

  • Disney’s Polynesian Village - This resort provides a view of the Magic Kingdom’s castle and has an island theme, making visitors feel as if they’re in the South Pacific.
  • Disney’s Grand Floridian Resort & Spa - This resort has a Victorian-era theme that provides visitors with a luxurious ambiance, and it has easy access to the Magic Kingdom park.
  • Disney’s Wilderness Lodge - This rustic resort is inspired by National Park lodges and provides a unique experience for visitors.

What to Buy in Magic Kingdom

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Magic Kingdom:

  • Mickey Mouse Ears - These iconic ears are available in different styles and colors and can be customized for a more personal touch.
  • Pressed Pennies - Visitors can collect pressed pennies from machines located throughout the park that provide a memory of their trip.
  • Disney’s Kitchenware and Glassware - Visitors can purchase a variety of kitchenware items that have a Disney twist to them, or hand-blown glassware in the shape of Disney characters.

Local Culture and Customs of Magic Kingdom

  • Manners and Etiquette - Visitors are encouraged to use “please” and “thank you,” and respect the Cast Members’ instructions and guidelines.
  • Smiling and Courtesy - Guests and Cast Members alike are known to wear a smile. Disney’s philosophy is to make visitors feel welcome and appreciated.
  • Wear Comfortable Clothing and Footwear - Guests are encouraged to dress for the weather, wear comfortable footwear, and bring out their most festive Disney gear.

Best Time to Visit Magic Kingdom

The best time to visit Magic Kingdom is during the off-season. The months of January, February, September, and October usually have fewer crowds, lower ticket prices, and comfortable weather. However, visitors should note that some attractions might be closed for maintenance during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Magic Kingdom

Do:

  • Plan Ahead - Visitors should research park hours, popular rides, and restaurants before their visit.
  • Use the FastPass+ System - This system allows visitors to schedule rides upfront and avoid long lines.
  • Take breaks - Visitors should take advantage of the many shaded areas in the park and rest frequently.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Visitors are encouraged to use the waste bins located throughout the park to keep it clean.
  • Smoke in Non-Smoking Areas - Disney’s policy is to maintain a non-smoking policy throughout the Magic Kingdom’s property.
  • Running, Jumping, or Screaming in Lines - Visitors should maintain a calm and orderly line and refrain from disturbing other guests around them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Magic Kingdom

  • Lockers are available to visitors - For added security, visitors can utilize lockers to store their valuables and avoid the stress of carrying them around the park.
  • Stay Hydrated - Visitors should bring their water bottles and keep themselves hydrated.
  • Wear Sun Protection - Sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses can help protect visitors from the Florida sun.

Health in Magic Kingdom

  • First Aid Stations - These stations are located throughout the park and provide assistance for minor health issues, including a pharmacy for prescription medication inquiries.
  • Dietary Restrictions - Visitors with dietary restrictions can access a variety of food options, including vegan and gluten-free options, by speaking to a restaurant representative.

Getting to Magic Kingdom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Magic Kingdom

The Orlando International Airport (MCO) is the closest airport to the Magic Kingdom. It is located approximately 30 minutes from the park and is accessible by shuttle bus, taxi service, or car rental.

Bus Stations Near Magic Kingdom

The closest bus stations to the Magic Kingdom are at Downtown Disney, which provides visitors with easy access to the park via the park’s Disney Transportation Services.

Other Options

Visitors can also access the Magic Kingdom by car, which involves parking at the Transportation and Ticket Center (TTC) and then accessing a monorail or ferry to the park.

How to Get Around in Magic Kingdom

Visitors can get around the park using the Disneyland Railroad, which stops at Main Street U.S.A, Frontierland, Fantasyland, and Mickey’s Toontown. Alternatively, visitors can use the park’s complimentary shuttle service to go from one land to another.

Where to Go Next from Magic Kingdom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Magic Kingdom’s close proximity to other Walt Disney World parks and resorts provides visitors with a wide range of nearby destinations to explore, including EPCOT, Hollywood Studios, Animal Kingdom, and other resorts.

Conclusion

Magic Kingdom is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Florida, and for good reason. From its stunning landscapes to its numerous attractions, it offers visitors an opportunity to experience a world of dreams and imagination. With the information provided in this comprehensive guide, visitors will be able to plan their visit, get the most out of their stay, and create unforgettable memories.
