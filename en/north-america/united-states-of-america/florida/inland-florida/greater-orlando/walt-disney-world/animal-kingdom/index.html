Introduction to Animal Kingdom

Animal Kingdom, located in Walt Disney World, is a nature-themed park where guests can experience close encounters with exotic wildlife, immerse in cultures from different continents, and enjoy fun outdoor adventures. This park stretches across 580 acres, making it one of the largest theme parks in the world.

List of Places to Visit in Animal Kingdom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Tree of Life: The tree of life is an iconic landmark at the heart of Animal Kingdom, which stands tall at 145 feet and features hand-carved animal sculptures on its trunk.
  2. Pandora-The World of Avatar: This attraction features rides and shows themed around the blockbuster movie Avatar, including the amazing Avatar Flight of Passage.
  3. Kilimanjaro Safaris: A guided tour through the African savanna where you can spot giraffes, lions, rhinos, and other wildlife.
  4. Maharajah Jungle Trek: A self-guided walking tour along a jungle trail where you can see exotic creatures such as bats, tigers, and birds.
  5. Gorilla Falls Exploration Trail: This winding trail leads you through the lush African forest, where you can see a troop of gorillas.
  6. Festival of the Lion King: A theatrical performance that brings the story of The Lion King to life.
  7. Finding Nemo- The Musical: A live musical version of the popular animated film, Finding Nemo.

Natural Attractions of Animal Kingdom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Discovery Island Trails: This series of nature trails takes you around the island, where you can spot various birds and animal species.
  2. Rafiki’s Planet Watch: Located in the back of Animal Kingdom, this area offers behind-the-scenes tours of the animal health and care facilities, as well as exhibits on conservation.
  3. Wilderness Explorers: An interactive game where guests can earn badges by completing challenges around the park.
  4. The Boneyard: A dig site where children can uncover dinosaur fossils and play on rope bridges and slides.
  5. Expedition Everest: One of the most popular rides in Animal Kingdom, this roller coaster takes you through the Himalayan Mountains and features a thrilling backwards drop.
  6. Kali River Rapids: A raft ride through the rainforests of Southeast Asia, featuring twists and drops.
  7. UP! A Great Bird Adventure: A show featuring birds from around the world, including exotic parrots and trained birds of prey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Animal Kingdom

  1. Gorilla Falls Exploration Trail: This 3/4 mile trail takes you through the lush African forest, where you can see a troop of gorillas.
  2. Maharajah Jungle Trek: This 1/2 mile trail leads you through the jungles of Asia, where you can see tigers, bats, and other exotic creatures.
  3. Discovery Island Trails: These nature trails wind around the island, providing views of gardens, waterfalls, and various bird species.

Best Things to Do in Animal Kingdom: Activities and Tours

  1. Wild Africa Trek: A guided tour through the African savanna, including a safari ride, a trek over a rope bridge, and a meal in a private safari camp.
  2. Savor the Savanna: A guided tour through the African savanna, including a safari ride, a sunset cocktail hour, and a three-course meal.
  3. Disney’s Family Magic Tour: An interactive tour that takes families around the park, completing puzzles and trivia challenges along the way.
  4. VIP Tour Services: Private tours that allow guests to skip lines and customize their itinerary.

Food and Drink Scene of Animal Kingdom

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Braised Pork Shoulder: Slow-roasted pork shoulder served with mashed plantains and chimichurri sauce, found at the Harambe Market.
  2. Shrimp and Tuna Poke Bowl: A fresh seafood salad with avocado, edamame, and Asian sauce, found at Satu’li Canteen in Pandora-The World of Avatar.
  3. Naan Bread and Hummus: A warm, fluffy naan bread, served with spicy hummus, found at the Yak and Yeti Local Food Cafes.
  4. Chicken Fried Rice Bowl: A savory chicken stir-fry with vegetables and jasmine rice, found at the Yak and Yeti Local Food Cafes.

Local Beverages

  1. Pandoran Sunrise: A tropical cocktail with mango and passionfruit juice, rum and boba balls found at Pongu Pongu in Pandora- The World of Avatar.
  2. Dole Whip: A pineapple-flavored soft serve ice cream found at Tamu Tamu Refreshments.
  3. Night Blossom: A tart frozen drink with layers of limeade, apple, and pear, topped with boba balls found at Pongu Pongu in Pandora- The World of Avatar.

Where to Stay in Animal Kingdom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Disney’s Animal Kingdom Lodge: This luxurious resort hotel offers a unique safari-style experience, with views of live animals and African-inspired decor.
  2. Disney’s Coronado Springs Resort: This Spanish-colonial-themed hotel is located on the shores of a 22-acre lake, featuring multiple pools, restaurants, and lounges.
  3. Disney’s All-Star Music Resort: A budget-friendly resort with giant musical-themed decor, including oversized guitars, drums and other musical instruments.

What to Buy in Animal Kingdom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Adorable Baby Animal Plush: You can find a wide range of Disney themed baby animals plush toys at the stores inside and outside the parks.
  2. Pandora Bead Bracelets: These bracelets are sold at Pandora Jewelry shops all over the park, and you can choose packaged sets of different Disney charms.
  3. Wildlife-Themed Clothing: From t-shirts to jackets with animal prints, Animal Kingdom’s shops offer a variety of souvenirs for every type of visitor.

Local Culture and Customs of Animal Kingdom

Animal Kingdom is an attraction that promotes cultural and animal conservation, with most of its attractions and exhibits focused on animal preservation.

Best Time to Visit Animal Kingdom

The best time to visit Animal Kingdom is during the early mornings or later in the afternoon to avoid crowds. The rainy season from June through September is also best avoided, as heavy rains and lightning can impact some of the park’s attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Animal Kingdom

Do:

  1. Respect the animals and their habitats.
  2. Follow the park’s rules and regulations.
  3. Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen.
  4. Plan your itinerary in advance.

Don’t:

  1. Disturb the animals.
  2. Climb on the exhibits or railings.
  3. Bring pets with you.
  4. Litter or feed the animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Animal Kingdom

The park’s staff and security team make sure the park is safe, and there are first aid stations scattered around the park for any medical emergencies.

Health in Animal Kingdom

Animal Kingdom is a subtropical environment, so staying hydrated and using sunscreen is essential. The park does have vegetarian or dietary-restricted meal accommodations as well.

Getting to Animal Kingdom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Animal Kingdom

  1. Orlando International Airport - 20 miles
  2. Orlando Sanford International Airport - 46 miles
  3. Tampa International Airport - 82 miles

Bus Stations Near Animal Kingdom

  1. Walt Disney World Transportation and Ticket Center - 4 miles
  2. SeaWorld Orlando Bus Stop - 8 miles
  3. Universal Studios Orlando Bus Stop - 14 miles

Other Options

You can take a taxi or rideshare services like Uber or Lyft to get to Animal Kingdom too.

How to Get Around in Animal Kingdom

Animal Kingdom offers a variety of transportation options, including buses, gondolas, and shuttles, between the multiple theme parks.

Where to Go Next from Animal Kingdom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Epcot: Another theme park in Walt Disney World.
  2. Disney Springs: A dining and shopping area featuring over 50 stores and restaurants.
  3. Universal Studios Orlando: A movie-themed park located 14 miles away from Animal Kingdom.

Conclusion

Visiting Animal Kingdom is a lifetime experience that can give you an ideal break from your daily life. Follow the guidelines mentioned here to ensure an incredible, safe, and healthy trip to this spectacular theme park.
