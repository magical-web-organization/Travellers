Introduction to Altamonte Springs

Altamonte Springs is a vibrant city located in Greater Orlando, Inland Florida. Known for its stunning natural beauty and year-round sunny weather, Altamonte Springs is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a blend of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and modern amenities. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Altamonte Springs, as well as provide tips on where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Altamonte Springs

Here’s a comprehensive list of must-visit attractions in Altamonte Springs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Altamonte Chapel: This beautiful historic chapel dates back to the 1800s and features stunning stained glass windows and intricate woodwork.

  2. Lake Lotus Nature Park: This beautiful 150-acre park is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, as well as hiking trails, boardwalks, and fishing areas.

  3. Art & History Museums Maitland: This museum features rotating exhibitions, as well as a permanent collection of Florida-themed art and artifacts.

  4. The Waterhouse Residence Museum: This historic home offers a glimpse into Florida’s past, with its 1884 Queen Anne-style architecture and period furnishings.

Natural Attractions of Altamonte Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Wekiva Springs State Park: This iconic Florida park is known for its crystal-clear freshwater springs, which offer visitors the chance to swim, kayak, and snorkel.

  2. Lake Orienta: This scenic lake is ideal for boating and fishing, as well as picnicking and nature watching.

  3. Crane’s Roost Park: This popular park features a beautiful fountain, walking trails, and a variety of wildlife, as well as outdoor concerts and events.

  4. Seminole-Wekiva Trail: This 14-mile paved trail winds through scenic parks and forests, offering hikers and bikers stunning views of Florida’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Altamonte Springs

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a casual biker, Altamonte Springs offers a variety of beautiful trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the best options for outdoor enthusiasts:

  1. Seminole-Wekiva Trail: This 14-mile trail passes through scenic parks and forests and is perfect for both hiking and biking.

  2. Lake Lotus Nature Park: With over 2 miles of hiking trails, this nature park is perfect for those looking to explore Florida’s natural beauty on foot.

  3. Westmonte Park: This park features a 1.5-mile fitness trail that winds past playgrounds, sports fields, and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Altamonte Springs: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities and tours to enjoy in Altamonte Springs. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Boat Tours: Take a relaxing boat tour on Lake Orienta and enjoy stunning views of the city from the water.

  2. Escape Rooms: Test your skills and teamwork as you try to solve puzzles and clues to escape these live-action adventure games.

  3. Kayaking: Experience Florida’s natural beauty up close with a guided kayaking tour of the Wekiva River.

  4. Indoor Rock Climbing: Scale the walls at an indoor rock climbing gym and enjoy a fun and challenging workout.

Week-Long Itinerary for Altamonte Springs

If you’re planning to spend a week in Altamonte Springs, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Arrive and check into hotel, explore local cuisine
Day 2 Visit Art & History Museums Maitland and enjoy a kayaking tour of the Wekiva River
Day 3 Spend a day at Wekiva Springs State Park
Day 4 Visit the Waterhouse Residence Museum and go indoor rock climbing
Day 5 Take a boat tour of Lake Orienta
Day 6 Explore Lake Lotus Nature Park and the Seminole-Wekiva Trail
Day 7 Departure day

Food and Drink Scene of Altamonte Springs

Altamonte Springs offers a diverse selection of cuisine choices, from traditional American fare to international dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Florida Grouper: Fresh from the Atlantic Ocean, grouper is a must-try seafood dish in Altamonte Springs.

  2. Key Lime Pie: This classic Florida dessert is the perfect sweet treat after a day of outdoor adventures.

  3. BBQ Ribs: Florida has its own unique twist on BBQ, and you won’t want to miss the juicy and flavorful ribs.

Local Beverages

  1. Orange Juice: Florida is famous for its oranges, and you won’t find a better glass of orange juice anywhere else.

  2. Rum Runner Cocktail: This fruity cocktail features local rum and is served up at most bars and restaurants in Altamonte Springs.

Where to Stay in Altamonte Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Altamonte Springs offers plenty of accommodation options to fit your budget and travel style. Here are our top picks for where to stay:

  1. Embassy Suites Orlando North: This modern hotel offers spacious rooms, a complimentary breakfast, and easy access to local attractions.

  2. Extended Stay America: This budget-friendly option features comfortable guestrooms with kitchenettes and convenient amenities like laundry facilities.

  3. Residence Inn Orlando Altamonte Springs/Maitland: This all-suite hotel offers modern accommodations, complimentary breakfast, and a 24-hour fitness center.

What to Buy in Altamonte Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a truly unique souvenir, check out some of the local products and goods available in Altamonte Springs. Here are some top options:

  1. Citrus Fruit: Bring home some of Florida’s famous oranges or grapefruits.

  2. Sea Shells: Collect your own shells from the nearby beaches or visit a local gift shop that sells them.

Local Culture and Customs of Altamonte Springs

The local customs in Altamonte Springs are similar to those of other Florida cities. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Tipping: It’s customary to tip 15-20% in restaurants and bars.

  • Beach Etiquette: Be respectful of other beachgoers by keeping noise levels down and cleaning up after yourself.

Best Time to Visit Altamonte Springs

The best time to visit Altamonte Springs is during the fall and winter months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be very hot and humid, but are still enjoyable for those who don’t mind the heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Altamonte Springs

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Altamonte Springs:

Do:

  1. Explore the natural beauty of Altamonte Springs.

  2. Try some of the local cuisine, including fresh seafood and Key Lime Pie.

  3. Take advantage of outdoor activities like kayaking, hiking, and swimming.

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash on the beaches or parks.

  2. Try to feed the wildlife.

  3. Ignore signs warning of alligator or other wildlife danger.

Safety and Health

Safety in Altamonte Springs

Altamonte Springs is generally a safe city, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always lock your car and hotel room doors and be aware of your surroundings.

  • Use common sense when exploring local areas at night.

  • Be cautious of rip currents when swimming in the ocean.

Health in Altamonte Springs

Altamonte Springs has several medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics. Make sure to pack any necessary medications and purchase travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to Altamonte Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Altamonte Springs

The Orlando International Airport (MCO) is the closest major airport to Altamonte Springs, located approximately 25 miles away. Sanford Orlando International airport (SFB) is a secondary option about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Altamonte Springs

The Altamonte Springs SunRail Station offers bus connections on local Lynx bus routes.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring ride-hail services are popular options for getting around and seeing the sights in Altamonte Springs.

How to Get Around in Altamonte Springs

Altamonte Springs offers a variety of transportation options, including buses, rental cars, and ride-hail services. Walking and biking are also great options for those looking to explore the city’s natural beauty on foot or two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Altamonte Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to explore, including:

  • Orlando: The theme parks, nightlife, and dining scene of Orlando make it a popular destination for visitors to Altamonte Springs.

  • Winter Park: This charming historic town offers visitors a glimpse into Florida’s past, with its tree-lined streets, boutique shops, and classically-inspired architecture.

  • Tampa: Located about 90 miles from Altamonte Springs, Tampa offers visitors the opportunity to explore its vibrant arts scene, historic neighborhoods, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Altamonte Springs is a hidden gem in the heart of Florida, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and modern amenities. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a history buff, Altamonte Springs has something for everyone.
