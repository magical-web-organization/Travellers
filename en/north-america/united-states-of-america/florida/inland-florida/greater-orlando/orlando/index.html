Introduction to Orlando

Orlando is a city located in the central region of Florida in the United States of America. It is known to be the theme park capital of the world, with over 12 theme parks in the city and surrounding areas. However, Orlando is not just about theme parks, there is a lot more to explore in this vibrant city. From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, and a bustling food and drink scene, Orlando has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Charles Hosmer Morse Museum of American Art

The Charles Hosmer Morse Museum of American Art is a must-visit museum when in Orlando. It is home to the largest collection of works by Louis Comfort Tiffany, including his famous stained glass lamps and windows. The museum also has a collection of American art pottery, furniture, and paintings.

Orlando Museum of Art

The Orlando Museum of Art features contemporary and historic American art, as well as art from Latin America and the Caribbean. They have a rotating collection of exhibits throughout the year, so there is always something new to see.

Harry P. Leu Gardens

Harry P. Leu Gardens is a 50-acre botanical garden with beautiful tropical and semi-tropical plants. The garden also features a museum in the Leu House, which was built in the 1800s and is an example of Florida living during that time period.

The Wells’ Built Museum of African American History and Culture

The Wells’ Built Museum of African American History and Culture is located in the historic district of Orlando and features exhibits and artifacts that celebrate the contributions of African Americans in the city.

Natural Attractions of Orlando: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wekiva Springs State Park

Wekiva Springs State Park is a nature lover’s paradise with clear springs, hiking trails, and wildlife. Visitors can enjoy canoeing, kayaking, and paddleboarding in the Wekiva River. There are also campsites available for those who want to spend a night in the wilderness.

Lake Baldwin Park

Lake Baldwin Park is a 23-acre park with a beautiful lake where visitors can go paddleboarding, kayaking, and fishing. The park also has a playground, picnic area, and a walking and biking trail.

Gatorland

Gatorland is not just a theme park, it is also a wildlife preserve where visitors can see alligators, crocodiles, and other reptiles up close. The park also has a zip line that takes visitors over the alligator breeding marsh.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orlando

Bill Frederick Park

Bill Frederick Park is a 183-acre park with several trails for hiking and biking. The park also has a lake where visitors can rent a paddleboat and go fishing.

West Orange Trail

The West Orange Trail is a 22-mile trail that goes through Orlando and surrounding neighborhoods. The trail is paved and perfect for biking, walking, and jogging.

Little Econ Greenway

The Little Econ Greenway is a 7.2-mile trail that runs along the Little Econ River. The trail is perfect for biking, hiking, and birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Orlando: Activities and Tours

Kennedy Space Center

The Kennedy Space Center is a must-visit attraction when in Orlando. Visitors can tour the space shuttle Atlantis, see the launch pads, and even meet an astronaut.

Orlando Balloon Rides

Orlando Balloon Rides is a unique way to see the city from above. Visitors can book a hot air balloon ride that takes them over the city and surrounding areas.

Universal Studios

Universal Studios is a popular theme park in Orlando that offers thrill rides, movie-themed attractions, and live shows. The park is a must-visit for movie buffs and adrenaline junkies alike.

Food and Drink Scene of Orlando

Must-Try Dishes

Cuban Sandwich

The Cuban Sandwich is a local favorite in Orlando. It is made with roasted pork, ham, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard on Cuban bread.

Fried Gator Tail

Fried Gator Tail is a popular dish in Orlando, made from alligator meat. It is usually served as an appetizer with a spicy dipping sauce.

Key Lime Pie

Key Lime Pie is a classic dessert in Florida. It is made with fresh key lime juice, sweetened condensed milk, and a graham cracker crust.

Local Beverages

Orange Blossom Pilsner

Orange Blossom Pilsner is a beer that is brewed in Orlando using locally sourced ingredients. It has a light, refreshing taste with a hint of orange.

Swamphead Brewery

Swamphead Brewery is a local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and taste some of their unique brews.

What to Buy in Orlando: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Disney Merchandise

Disney merchandise is available throughout the city, but the best place to buy it is at Disney Springs. Visitors can find everything from Mickey Mouse ears to limited edition merchandise.

Orange Blossom Honey

Orange Blossom Honey is a local product that can be found in many stores throughout the city. It is made from the nectar of orange blossoms and has a unique citrus flavor.

Citrus Fruits

Florida is known for its citrus fruits, and visitors can find fresh oranges, grapefruits, and other citrus fruits at local markets throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Orlando

Orlando is a diverse city with a mix of cultures and customs. Locals are known for being friendly and welcoming to visitors. It is also customary to tip 15-20% at restaurants, and a small tip is appreciated for services like haircuts and taxi rides.

Best Time to Visit Orlando

The best time to visit Orlando is from September to November or from March to May. The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than during the peak summer months and winter holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orlando

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the natural attractions

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray
  • Litter in natural areas
  • Be rude to the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Orlando

Orlando is generally a safe city, but visitors should practice basic safety precautions like locking their car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight. Visitors should also be cautious when walking alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Orlando

Visitors should drink bottled water rather than tap water to avoid any potential health issues. It is also important to protect oneself from mosquitoes, which can carry diseases like Zika and West Nile virus.

How to Get Around in Orlando

Car Rent

Car rental is a popular option for visitors to Orlando, as it allows for easy access to the many attractions and sights throughout the city.

Public Transportation

Orlando has a public transportation system called Lynx that operates buses throughout the city. The Lynx bus system has a wide coverage area and can be a convenient and affordable way to get around.

Taxis

Taxis are readily available throughout the city, and visitors can use services like Uber and Lyft to get around as well.

Where to Go Next from Orlando: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tampa

Tampa is a city located on the western coast of Florida, about a two-hour drive from Orlando. The city is known for its beautiful beaches, and visitors can also explore the historic Ybor City neighborhood, home to many restaurants and boutiques.

St. Augustine

St. Augustine is the oldest city in the United States and is located about two hours north of Orlando. The city has a rich history and offers visitors the opportunity to explore sites like the Castillo de San Marcos and the St. Augustine Lighthouse.

Conclusion

Orlando has a lot to offer, from theme parks to cultural attractions, natural wonders, and a bustling food and drink scene. Whether you’re visiting for a few days or a whole week, there’s plenty to see and do. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip to Orlando with ease and get the most out of your experience in this magical city.
