Ultimate Travel Guide to Clermont, Florida

Located in the heart of Inland Florida, Clermont offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. From hiking and biking trails to museums and historical sites, there is something for everyone in this charming city. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a culture buff, there’s plenty to see and do in Clermont. Here’s our comprehensive guide to the city, including where to stay, what to eat, and the best sights to see.

List of Places to Visit in Clermont

If you’re planning a trip to Clermont, here are some must-see destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clermont Historic Village Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Clermont and its people. You’ll find exhibits showcasing the city’s past, including artifacts and photographs from the early 1900s.

  • Presidents Hall of Fame: This museum contains life-sized wax figures of every U.S. president, along with other historical artifacts. It’s a great place to learn about American history in a unique and interactive way.

  • Florida Citrus Tower: This tower offers stunning views of the surrounding area, including lakes and orange groves. Built in 1956, it is a popular attraction for visitors to Clermont.

Natural Attractions of Clermont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Louisa State Park: This park features natural landscapes including forests, lakes, and wetlands. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and camping.

  • Clay Island Trail: This scenic trail is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding. It offers stunning views of Lake Apopka and the surrounding wildlife.

  • Lakeridge Winery and Vineyards: This winery is surrounded by lush vineyards and offers free tours and tastings. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy a glass of wine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clermont

Clermont is surrounded by hills and vistas, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the city’s top hiking and biking routes:

  • The West Orange Trail: A 22-mile paved trail that runs through Clermont and the surrounding areas. You can walk, run, or bike the trail while enjoying views of forests and wetlands.

  • South Lake Trail: This scenic trail stretches for 13 miles along Lake Minneola and Lake Susan. It’s perfect for walkers, runners, and cyclists of all skill levels.

  • Sugarloaf Mountain Trailhead: This trailhead features a 1.2-mile loop trail that offers stunning views of Lake Apopka and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Clermont: Activities and Tours

Here are some other activities and tours to consider during your stay in Clermont:

  • Hot Air Balloon Flights: Take a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful scenery of Lake County. It’s a great way to see the local landscape from a new perspective.

  • Skydive Florida: For the thrill-seekers, try skydiving over Clermont. It’s an exhilarating experience that will give you an adrenaline rush like no other.

  • Lake County Historical Museum: Located just 20 minutes from Clermont, this museum gives visitors a glimpse into the history and culture of Lake County. It features exhibits on the area’s Native American population, as well as displays of antique cars and farm equipment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clermont

If you’re planning to stay in Clermont for a full week, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Clermont Historic Village Museum, Presidents Hall of Fame, and the Florida Citrus Tower.
Day 2 Hike or bike the West Orange Trail and visit Lake Louisa State Park.
Day 3 Take a hot air balloon ride or try skydiving over Clermont.
Day 4 Visit Lakeridge Winery and Vineyards and enjoy a free tour and tasting.
Day 5 Take a scenic drive to Mount Dora and visit the Mount Dora Historical Society Museum.
Day 6 Explore the South Lake Trail and Clay Island Trail.
Day 7 Relax on the beaches of nearby Lake County, such as Clermont Beach or Palm Island Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Clermont

Clermont offers a diverse range of dining options, from laid-back cafes to upscale restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ pork sandwich from Uncle Kenny’s BBQ: This award-winning restaurant serves up some of the best BBQ in the area, with generous portions and affordable prices.

  • Gator tail from Oakwood Smokehouse and Grill: For a truly unique dining experience, try the gator tail at this local eatery.

  • Fried green tomatoes from Lilly’s on the Lake: This waterfront restaurant offers breathtaking views of the lake and serves up some of the most delicious fried green tomatoes in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Blossom Brewing: This local brewery is famous for its craft beers, including the popular Orange Blossom Pilsner.

  • Lakeridge Winery: Visit the winery and vineyard to taste the locally produced wines, made from Florida-grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Clermont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clermont offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city:

  • Mission Inn Resort & Club: This luxury resort features a spa, golf course, and multiple dining options.

  • Hampton Inn and Suites Clermont: This affordable hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, as well as a free hot breakfast.

  • Florida Bungalow: This charming guesthouse offers a private pool and tropical garden, perfect for relaxing after a long day of exploring.

What to Buy in Clermont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Clermont, check out these local shops:

  • Winter Garden Heritage Foundation: This store offers a range of gifts, books, and souvenirs celebrating the history of Winter Garden and the surrounding area.

  • The Olive Tree: This specialty shop sells a variety of olive oils, vinegars, and spices, as well as other food and cooking-related gifts.

  • Sugarboo & Co: This whimsical boutique has a range of home decor and unique gift items.

Local Culture and Customs of Clermont

Clermont has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should take the time to learn about the local customs and traditions. Here are a few to keep in mind:

  • Clermont is home to a large number of citrus farms, so be sure to try some locally grown oranges and other citrus fruits.

  • The area is also known for its many festivals and events, including the Blueberry Festival and the Pig on the Pond BBQ festival.

Best Time to Visit Clermont

The best time to visit Clermont is during the fall or winter, when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner. Spring and summer can be hot and humid, so be sure to pack accordingly if you plan to visit during these seasons. Additionally, hurricane season runs from June to November, so keep an eye on weather forecasts if you’re planning to travel during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clermont

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Clermont:

Do:

  • Explore the local parks and hiking trails.

  • Try some of the local cuisine, especially the BBQ and citrus-based dishes.

  • Learn about the history and culture of the area by visiting museums and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Remove or damage any natural landscape or historical items.

  • Litter or leave trash behind in public areas.

  • Ignore warning signs or park closures due to hazardous conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clermont

Clermont is generally a safe city to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking doors and windows and not walking alone at night.

Health in Clermont

Clermont has several hospitals and medical facilities, including South Lake Hospital and Orlando Health South Lake Hospital. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with the hot and humid climate, such as dehydration and heat stroke.

Getting to Clermont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clermont

The closest major airport to Clermont is Orlando International Airport, located about 40 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Clermont

There are several Greyhound bus stations located near Clermont, including ones in Orlando and Kissimmee.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Clermont and the surrounding areas. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the city.

How to Get Around in Clermont

The best way to get around Clermont is by car or bike. The city is relatively spread out, and public transportation options are limited.

Where to Go Next from Clermont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clermont is located near a number of other popular destinations in Florida. Here are a few to consider:

  • Mount Dora: This picturesque town is located just 20 minutes from Clermont and is known for its antique shops, art galleries, and charming bed and breakfasts.

  • Orlando: Just a short drive from Clermont, Orlando is home to popular theme parks such as Disney World, Universal Studios, and SeaWorld.

  • Tampa: Located about two hours from Clermont, Tampa is known for its museums, restaurants, and beautiful waterfront views.

Conclusion

Clermont is a hidden gem in central Florida, with plenty to offer visitors. From natural landscapes to historical sites, there is something for everyone in this charming city. With this guide, you’ll be able to make the most out of your stay in Clermont. Happy travels!
