Introduction to Moore Haven

Nestled in the heart of Florida Heartland, Moore Haven is a charming city that offers an abundance of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. This hidden gem of Florida is located in the Inland Florida region, surrounded by vast fields of orange trees, serene canals, and scenic lakes. Whether you’re seeking adventure, history, or just a peaceful getaway, Moore Haven has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Moore Haven

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ortona Locks: The Ortona Locks are an important feature of the Caloosahatchee River, which connects Lake Okeechobee to the Gulf of Mexico. These impressive locks were built in the early 20th century to support the shipping industry, and today, they are an important historical site in Moore Haven.

  • The Seminole Tribe of Florida Museum: The Seminole Tribe of Florida Museum is a fascinating museum that tells the story of the Seminole tribe and their culture. Visitors can learn about traditional crafts, clothing, and life of the Seminole tribe.

Natural Attractions of Moore Haven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Okeechobee: Lake Okeechobee is the largest freshwater lake in Florida and is a popular spot for fishing and boating. Visitors can explore the lake by boat or take a hike on the surrounding trails.

  • Fisheating Creek Wildlife Management Area: The Fisheating Creek Wildlife Management Area is a beautiful natural area that offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and birdwatching. It is home to numerous species of birds, alligators, and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moore Haven

  • Lake Okeechobee Scenic Trail: The Lake Okeechobee Scenic Trail is a 110-mile trail that encircles the lake and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. It is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and jogging.

  • Fisheating Creek Wildlife Management Area Trails: The Fisheating Creek Wildlife Management Area has several trails that offer a peaceful and scenic hike, including the seven-mile Swamp Hammock Trail, which is known for its abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Moore Haven: Activities and Tours

  • Airboat Tours: Visitors can take airboat tours of the nearby lakes and canals to get a close-up view of the wildlife and natural beauty of the area.

  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity in Moore Haven, with Lake Okeechobee being a top spot for bass fishing.

Food and Drink Scene of Moore Haven

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: Catfish is a popular dish in Moore Haven, with many restaurants serving freshly fried catfish.

  • Florida Orange Juice: As Moore Haven is surrounded by fields full of orange trees, freshly squeezed Florida orange juice is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Florida-Crafted Beer: Several breweries in the surrounding towns craft local beer that is worth trying.

Where to Stay in Moore Haven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Best Western of Clewiston: Located just a short drive from Moore Haven, the Best Western of Clewiston offers comfortable rooms and a variety of amenities.

  • The Plaza Travel Inn: Located in the heart of Moore Haven, the Plaza Travel Inn is a cozy and affordable option for those looking to stay in the city.

What to Buy in Moore Haven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Florida Orange Blossom Honey: Orange blossom honey is a sweet and unique souvenir that can only be found in Florida.

  • Seminole Crafts: The Seminole tribe is known for their beautiful and intricate crafts, including beaded jewelry, clothing, and baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Moore Haven

Moore Haven is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are proud of their rich history and heritage. Visitors should respect the local culture and be mindful of the Seminole tribe’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Moore Haven

The best time to visit Moore Haven is between October and May, when the weather is pleasant and the humidity is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moore Haven

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Seminole tribe.

  • Take your time and enjoy the peaceful and serene surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.

  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moore Haven

Moore Haven is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Moore Haven

Visitors should be mindful of the Florida heat and stay hydrated. Mosquitoes are present in the area, so visitors should take appropriate precautions to avoid bites.

Getting to Moore Haven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Moore Haven

  • Southwest Florida International Airport: Located in Fort Myers, this airport is approximately 75 miles from Moore Haven.

  • Palm Beach International Airport: Located in West Palm Beach, this airport is approximately 80 miles from Moore Haven.

Bus Stations Near Moore Haven

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Clewiston, this bus station is approximately 13 miles from Moore Haven.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Moore Haven.

How to Get Around in Moore Haven

The best way to get around Moore Haven is by car. Visitors can rent a car or bring their own.

Where to Go Next from Moore Haven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Clewiston: Clewiston is known as America’s Sweetest Town, thanks to its vast fields of sugarcane.

  • Lake Placid: Lake Placid is known for its unique murals and outdoor sculptures, as well as its beautiful lakefront properties.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals, Moore Haven is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Florida. From the Ortona Locks to Lake Okeechobee, visitors will have no shortage of things to do and see in this charming city.
