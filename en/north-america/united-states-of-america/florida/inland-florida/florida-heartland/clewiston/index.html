When it comes to the Florida Heartland, Clewiston is one of the most interesting places you can visit. Located in Inland Florida, not far from the state’s southern border, Clewiston is a small town with plenty of charm and a rich history that dates back centuries. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or just soaking up the local vibe, Clewiston has something to offer everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do, places to visit, food to try, and more in Clewiston, Florida.

Introduction to Clewiston

Clewiston is a small city located in Hendry County, Florida, United States of America. Founded in the early 20th century as a planned community for sugar, it was named after Alfred C. Clewis, a prominent local businessman. The town has a population of just under 8,000 people and is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor recreation opportunities, and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Clewiston

Whether you’re visiting Clewiston for the first time or revisiting a favorite destination, you’ll find plenty of places to explore in this charming city. Here are just a few of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clewiston Museum: This local museum offers a fascinating look at the history of Clewiston and the surrounding area, from its early days as a sugar plantation to its modern-day economy. Explore the exhibits featuring stories about the people, businesses, and landmarks that have shaped Clewiston over the years.

  • Big Cypress Seminole Indian Reservation: Head out of town to experience the history and culture of the Seminole tribe, who have lived in the region for centuries. Take a guided tour of the reservation, visit the Ah-Tah-Thi-Ki Museum, or try your hand at traditional arts and crafts.

Natural Attractions of Clewiston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Okeechobee: Known as the “Big O,” Lake Okeechobee is a massive freshwater lake that covers over 730 square miles. Fishing, boating, and wildlife watching are popular activities here, and there are several scenic parks and beaches along the shore.

  • Clewiston Sugar Festival: This annual event celebrates the region’s sugarcane heritage and features live music, carnival rides, food vendors, and more. Don’t miss the “Sugar Cane Grinding” demonstrations or the “Sugar Queen” pageant.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clewiston

Clewiston is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with several great hiking and biking trails in the area. Here are a few of the best:

  • Lake Okeechobee Scenic Trail: This 109-mile trail circles the entire lake and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding wetlands. Hikers, cyclists, and roller-bladers are welcome, and there are plenty of access points and amenities along the way.

  • Clewiston Trailhead: Starting at the Clewiston Trailhead, this 5.4-mile paved trail takes you through several parks, past Lake Okeechobee, and through the heart of Clewiston.

Best Things to Do in Clewiston: Activities and Tours

  • Airboat Rides: Take an exhilarating ride through the Florida Everglades and see all sorts of wildlife and amazing scenery. Several local companies offer airboat tours, including “Captain Hendry’s Airboat Rides” and “Alligator Alley Adventures.”

  • Fishing Charters: With Lake Okeechobee nearby, Clewiston is an excellent destination for fishing. Book a fishing charter with a local guide and try your luck at catching largemouth bass, catfish, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clewiston

If you’re planning to spend a week in Clewiston, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Clewiston and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring the local shops and restaurants.

  • Day 2: Head out to Lake Okeechobee and spend the day fishing or relaxing on the beach.

  • Day 3: Take an airboat ride through the Everglades and see all the wildlife.

  • Day 4: Visit the Clewiston Museum and learn about the town’s history.

  • Day 5: Take a day trip to Miami or Fort Lauderdale and explore the big city.

  • Day 6: Try your hand at golfing or disc golfing at the Clewiston Golf Course.

  • Day 7: Enjoy the Clewiston Sugar Festival before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Clewiston

No vacation is complete without trying some of the local food and drink, so here are a few must-try dishes and beverages to look out for in Clewiston:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: A Southern staple, fried catfish is a must-try for any seafood lover. Look for it on the menu at local restaurants like Roland Martin’s Tiki Bar and Clewiston Inn.

  • Gator Tail: For a taste of something a little more exotic, try some gator tail at Everglades Restaurant. It’s breaded and deep-fried, and tastes like chicken!

Local Beverages

  • Sodas from Clewiston Bottling Works: This local bottling company makes its own brand of old-fashioned sodas, including flavors like vanilla cream, cherry limeade, and ginger ale.

  • Sugarcane Juice: Fresh sugarcane juice is a refreshing and healthy beverage that’s popular in Clewiston. Look for it at local juice stands and markets.

Where to Stay in Clewiston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clewiston has a variety of accommodations to choose from, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a more budget-friendly option. Here are a few top picks:

  • Clewiston Inn: This historic hotel dates back to the 1930s and has hosted everyone from dignitaries to movie stars. It has a beautiful pool and courtyard and is located right in the heart of downtown Clewiston.

  • Holiday Inn Express: For a more modern option, the Holiday Inn Express offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Clewiston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

With so many unique shops and markets in Clewiston, you’re sure to find the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home. Here are a few ideas:

  • Homemade Jams and Jellies: Check out the local farmer’s market or fruit stands for homemade jelly and jam made from local produce.

  • Artisanal Soap: At Alligator Skin Soaps, you’ll find handmade soaps made with all-natural ingredients and scented with Florida-inspired fragrances.

Local Culture and Customs of Clewiston

Clewiston has a rich cultural heritage with a strong emphasis on outdoor recreation, Southern cuisine, and sugarcane production. Locals are friendly and welcoming, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation and learn more about the town’s history.

Best Time to Visit Clewiston

The best time to visit Clewiston is during the winter months, from December through February, when temperatures are mild and comfortable. Summer can be very hot and humid, with frequent thunderstorms, but it’s also the busiest season for tourism.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clewiston

When visiting Clewiston, it’s important to be respectful of the local customs and culture. Here are a few tips:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including fresh seafood and sugarcane juice.

  • Spend time exploring the natural beauty of the area, including Lake Okeechobee and the Florida Everglades.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the local wildlife.

  • Ignore safety warnings when participating in outdoor activities, such as boating and hiking.

Safety and Health

Clewiston is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions when exploring new areas. Here are a few tips:

Safety in Clewiston

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

  • Lock your car and hotel room at all times, and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Clewiston

  • Drink plenty of water, especially during the hot summer months.

  • Use sunscreen and bug spray to protect yourself from sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Clewiston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clewiston is located in Hendry County, Florida, and is easily accessible by car or bus.

Airports Near Clewiston

  • Southwest Florida International Airport, located in Fort Myers, is the closest major airport to Clewiston, just over an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Clewiston

  • Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in LaBelle, located about 25 minutes from Clewiston.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is always a good option, especially if you plan to explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Clewiston

The best way to get around Clewiston is by car, as public transportation is limited. Taxis are available but can be expensive, so renting a car is usually the most convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Clewiston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Florida, there are plenty of nearby destinations to consider. Here are a few ideas:

  • Fort Myers: Located about an hour’s drive from Clewiston, Fort Myers is a popular destination for its beaches, shopping, and nightlife.

  • Miami: Just a few hours’ drive from Clewiston, Miami is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city known for its arts, culture, and Latin American cuisine.

Conclusion

Clewiston may be a small town, but it’s full of character and charm. Whether you’re interested in outdoor recreation, cultural attractions, or just soaking up the local vibe, there’s something for everyone in this gem of the Florida Heartland. With this comprehensive guide in hand, you’re sure to have an unforgettable visit.
