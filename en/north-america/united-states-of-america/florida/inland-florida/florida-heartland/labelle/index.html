Introduction to LaBelle

Located in the heart of the Florida Heartland, LaBelle is a charming town that offers a mix of rich history, natural wonders, and modern amenities. With a population of just over 4,500, LaBelle is a perfect destination for those seeking a quiet getaway from the hustle and bustle of big cities.

List of Places to Visit in LaBelle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • LaBelle Heritage Museum: This museum is the perfect place to learn about the history of LaBelle and its surrounding area. It features exhibits on the area’s early settlers, Seminole Indians, and more.
  • Captain Hendry House: Built in 1906, this historical house is a testament to the early settlers of LaBelle. It now serves as a museum and is open for guided tours.
  • Barron Park House Gallery: This gallery is housed in a beautifully restored Victorian home and features the work of local artists.

Natural Attractions of LaBelle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Caloosahatchee River: One of the main attractions in LaBelle is the beautiful Caloosahatchee River. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities on the river, such as fishing, kayaking, and boating.
  • Fisheating Creek: This beautiful creek is located just outside of LaBelle and offers visitors the chance to go hiking, biking, or horseback riding on the trails.
  • LaBelle Nature Park: This park is a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of LaBelle. It features picnic areas, walking trails, and a playground for children.

Hiking/Biking Routes of LaBelle

  • Oak Hammock Park Trails: This hiking and biking trail is located in LaBelle and features beautiful views of the Caloosahatchee River. The trail is approximately 5 miles long.
  • Fisheating Creek Trails: The trails at Fisheating Creek offer visitors the chance to see some of the natural beauty of LaBelle up close. The trails range in length from 1 to 5 miles.

Best Things to Do in LaBelle: Activities and Tours

  • Airboat Tours: Visitors to LaBelle can take an airboat tour of the Caloosahatchee River and experience the thrill of cruising through the water at high speeds.
  • Horseback Riding: LaBelle is home to a number of stables that offer visitors the chance to go horseback riding through the beautiful Florida countryside.
  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity in LaBelle, with a variety of locations available for both freshwater and saltwater fishing.

Food and Drink Scene of LaBelle

Must-Try Dishes

  • Swamp Cabbage: This dish, made from the hearts of the Sabal palm tree, is a staple of the LaBelle area. Try it in a salad or as a side dish.
  • Gator Bites: For those feeling adventurous, gator bites are a must-try in LaBelle. These bite-sized pieces of alligator meat are typically fried and served as an appetizer or snack.
  • Key Lime Pie: No trip to Florida is complete without trying some Key Lime Pie. LaBelle is no exception, with many restaurants offering their own take on this classic dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is known for its produce, and orange juice is no exception. Enjoy a glass of freshly squeezed orange juice with your breakfast in LaBelle.
  • Sweet Tea: A staple of the southern United States, sweet tea is a refreshing drink that pairs perfectly with a hot Florida day.

Where to Stay in LaBelle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Port LaBelle Inn & Conference Center: This hotel features comfortable rooms, a pool, and an on-site restaurant.
  • LaBelle Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean, comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of LaBelle.
  • The House of Stone: This guesthouse is housed in a beautifully restored historic home and offers visitors a unique and luxurious accommodation option.

What to Buy in LaBelle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Swamp Cabbage Jelly: This unique jelly is made from the hearts of the Sabal palm tree and is a great souvenir to take home from LaBelle.
  • Local Honey: Florida is known for its honey, and LaBelle is no exception. Pick up a jar of local honey to take home and enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of LaBelle

LaBelle is a friendly town that values its cultural heritage and natural beauty. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s history and environment.

Best Time to Visit LaBelle

The best time to visit LaBelle is in the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is mild and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of LaBelle

Do:

  • Do respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Do take time to explore the history and culture of the LaBelle area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in LaBelle

LaBelle is a safe town, but visitors should exercise the same caution they would in any unfamiliar place.

Health in LaBelle

Visitors to LaBelle should take care to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and protect themselves from insects.

Getting to LaBelle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near LaBelle

  • Southwest Florida International Airport: Located approximately 40 miles from LaBelle, this airport is a major regional hub.

Bus Stations Near LaBelle

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Fort Myers, approximately 30 miles from LaBelle.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from one of the major car rental companies at the Southwest Florida International Airport.

How to Get Around in LaBelle

Visitors to LaBelle can rent a car, take a taxi, or use a ride-sharing service to get around town.

Where to Go Next from LaBelle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sanibel Island: Located approximately 60 miles from LaBelle, Sanibel Island is a popular destination for its beaches, wildlife, and outdoor activities.
  • Fort Myers: Located approximately 30 miles from LaBelle, Fort Myers is a larger city that offers a variety of cultural attractions and activities.

Conclusion

LaBelle, Florida offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or explore the great outdoors, LaBelle has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the hidden gems of this charming Florida Heartland town.
