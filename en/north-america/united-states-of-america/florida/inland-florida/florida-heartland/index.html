Florida Heartland is often overlooked by tourists visiting Florida’s coastal cities. However, this inland region, located in the central part of the state, features a unique mix of natural and cultural attractions that are worth exploring. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there is something for everyone in Florida Heartland.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the best things to do, places to visit, and local customs in Florida Heartland. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an adventure-packed trip, we’ve got you covered.

Introduction to Florida Heartland

Florida Heartland is a seven-county region located in the central part of Florida. Its location inland makes it a unique destination worth visiting. The region is known for its quaint small towns, citrus groves, cattle ranches, and vast wilderness areas.

Unlike the coastal cities, Florida Heartland offers a quieter and more laid-back vibe. The region is ideal for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and experience the natural beauty of the area.

List of Places to Visit in Florida Heartland

Florida Heartland has a variety of attractions that cater to different interests. Here is a list of the top places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cracker Trail Museum: Learn about Florida’s cowboy culture and history at this museum located in Zolfo Springs.
  • Highlands County Historical Society: Explore the local history of Highlands County at this museum located in Sebring.
  • South Florida State College Museum of Florida Art and Culture: Discover Florida’s multicultural heritage through art and artifacts at this museum located in Avon Park.

Natural Attractions of Florida Heartland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Highlands Hammock State Park: Explore the hiking trails and scenic drives in this beautiful state park located in Sebring.
  • Lake Okeechobee: The largest lake in Florida is a popular spot for fishing and boating.
  • Fisheating Creek: Get a glimpse of Florida’s wild side at this wilderness area located in Palmdale.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Florida Heartland

  • Lake Istokpoga: This 26,000-acre lake features a biking and hiking trail that offers scenic views of the area.
  • Okeechobee Lake Trail: Enjoy a 109-mile bike ride around Lake Okeechobee, featuring campsites and scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Florida Heartland: Activities and Tours

  • Airboat Tours: Take a thrilling airboat ride through the wetlands in Palmdale.
  • Strawberry Picking: Visit a U-pick strawberry farm and taste fresh strawberries in Plant City.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore Florida’s countryside on horseback at the Triple V Ranch in Sebring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Florida Heartland

Day 1-2: Visit the museums and historical sites in Zolfo Springs and Sebring.

Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Highlands Hammock State Park and Lake Istokpoga.

Day 4: Take an airboat tour and discover the wilderness of Fisheating Creek.

Day 5: Go horseback riding at the Triple V Ranch and visit a U-pick strawberry farm in Plant City.

Day 6-7: Enjoy a bike ride around Lake Okeechobee and relax on its shores.

Food and Drink Scene of Florida Heartland

Florida Heartland has a unique blend of Southern and Caribbean cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Green Tomatoes: A Southern classic served with remoulade sauce.
  • Conch Fritters: Caribbean-inspired fritters made with conch meat.
  • Gator Bites: Fried alligator bites served with a spicy dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is famous for its oranges, so make sure to try some fresh orange juice.
  • Swamp Cabbage Beer: A unique beer made from the heart of the state tree, the cabbage palm.

Where to Stay in Florida Heartland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Florida Heartland has a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Inn On The Lakes: Enjoy a luxurious stay with lake views at this hotel in Sebring.
  • Palmdale RV Resort: If you’re looking for a camping experience, consider staying at this RV resort in Palmdale.

What to Buy in Florida Heartland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some ideas:

  • Cattleman’s Award-Winning Beef Jerky: Made from Florida-raised beef, this jerky is a delicious snack.
  • Florida Citrus: Bring home fresh oranges, grapefruits, and tangerines from one of the many citrus farms in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Florida Heartland

Florida Heartland has a unique culture shaped by its history and location. Here are some local customs and traditions you might encounter:

  • Cracker Culture: Florida’s cowboy culture has a strong presence in the Heartland region.
  • Strawberry Festival: Plant City is known for its annual Strawberry Festival, featuring live music, carnival rides, and, of course, fresh strawberries.

Best Time to Visit Florida Heartland

The best time to visit Florida Heartland is during the winter season from November to March. The temperatures are cooler, and the humidity is lower, making it easier to explore the outdoor attractions. It’s also the time when many festivals and events take place, such as the Florida State Fair and Strawberry Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Florida Heartland

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Disrupt wildlife or their habitats.
  • Leave trash or litter behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Florida Heartland

Florida Heartland is a safe region to explore, but it’s always wise to take precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • Be aware of weather conditions and potential hazards such as lightning and alligator encounters.

Health in Florida Heartland

Florida Heartland has no specific health risks. However, it’s always essential to take general health precautions when traveling, such as bringing necessary medications and staying hydrated.

Getting to Florida Heartland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Florida Heartland

  • Sarasota–Bradenton International Airport (SRQ): Located 73 miles from Sebring.
  • Tampa International Airport (TPA): Located 95 miles from Sebring.

Bus Stations Near Florida Heartland

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Avon Park.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental facilities are available at most airports and cities throughout Florida.
  • Uber and Lyft: Ride-sharing services are available in most cities in Florida.

How to Get Around in Florida Heartland

The best way to get around Florida Heartland is by car. The region is spread out, and public transportation is limited. Car rental facilities are available at most airports and cities throughout Florida.

Where to Go Next from Florida Heartland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Florida Heartland is a great starting point for exploring other parts of Florida. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Orlando: Visit theme parks and attractions in the “Theme Park Capital of the World,” located about two hours northeast of Heartland.
  • Gainesville: Explore the natural beauty, historic sites, and cultural attractions in this college town located about three hours north of Heartland.

Conclusion

Florida Heartland is a hidden gem in the Sunshine State, offering a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in museums and historical sites or outdoor adventures and scenic views, there’s something for everyone in Heartland.
