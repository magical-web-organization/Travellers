Introduction to Starke

Located in North Central Florida, Starke is a small yet charming town with a rich history and unique natural beauty. It is the county seat of Bradford County and has a population of around 5,000 residents. The town was named after Madison Starke Perry, the governor of Florida from 1857 to 1861.

Starke is known for its historic landmarks, natural attractions, and vibrant cultural scene. Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or food lover, there is something for everyone in Starke. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Starke, Florida.

List of Places to Visit in Starke

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bradford County Historical Museum: Located in the historic Andrews/Matthews house, the museum showcases the history and cultural heritage of Bradford County. Visitors can explore the exhibits, artifacts, and photographs that provide insight into the lives of early settlers and pioneers.

  • Camp Blanding Museum and Memorial Park: Dedicated to the rich military history of the area, this museum features exhibits on World War II, the Korean War, and the Cold War. Visitors can see a variety of artifacts and memorabilia, along with outdoor exhibits of military vehicles and equipment.

  • Florida Twin Theatre: This historic theatre has been entertaining locals and visitors since 1949, and is one of the few drive-in movie theatres still in operation in Florida.

Natural Attractions of Starke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kingsley Lake: Known for its crystal-clear waters and serene natural beauty, Kingsley Lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating. It is also home to Camp Blanding, a National Guard training center.

  • Gold Head Branch State Park: With over 2,000 acres of pristine wilderness and scenic hiking trails, Gold Head Branch State Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Visitors can see a variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and bald eagles.

  • Santa Fe Swamp Wildlife Management Area: This protected area includes over 21,000 acres of swamp and wetlands, and is home to a variety of animal species, such as alligators, deer, turkeys, and black bears.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Starke

  • Rails-to-Trails: This 29-mile trail runs through Starke and is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding. It runs along the former bed of the Florida Northern Railroad and offers scenic views of natural landscapes and historic landmarks. The trail is also part of the Florida Greenways and Trails System.

Best Things to Do in Starke: Activities and Tours

  • Starke Bikefest: This annual event is a popular attraction for motorcycle enthusiasts, featuring live music, food vendors, and a variety of bike shows and contests.

  • Bradford County Fair: Held every year in March, this family-friendly fair features carnival rides, live entertainment, agricultural exhibits, and food booths.

  • Santa Fe College Teaching Zoo: Located just a short drive from Starke, this zoo is one of the few teaching zoos in the country. Visitors can see a variety of animal species, including primates, big cats, and birds of prey.

Food and Drink Scene of Starke

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southern Fried Chicken: This classic dish is a staple of Southern cuisine, and can be found in many local restaurants and diners.

  • Catfish: Another popular dish in the area, catfish is served in a variety of styles, including fried, blackened, and grilled.

  • Okra and Tomatoes: This simple yet delicious side dish is a perfect accompaniment to any meal, and showcases the fresh flavors of local produce.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern favorite, sweet tea is served with almost every meal in Starke.

  • Orchard Hills Meadery: This local meadery produces a variety of honey-based wines and ciders, and is a must-visit destination for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Starke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Starke: This hotel offers spacious rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a complimentary breakfast buffet. It is located just a few miles from historic downtown Starke.

  • The Magnolia Hotel: This historic boutique hotel features elegant rooms and suites, along with a gourmet restaurant and bar.

  • Peaceful Acres: This cozy bed and breakfast provides a tranquil retreat for visitors, with lush gardens, cozy rooms, and a charming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Starke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bradford County Strawberry Festival Souvenirs: Held every year in April, this festival celebrates the area’s rich strawberry growing tradition, with vendors selling everything from strawberry jams to handmade crafts.

  • Orchard Hills Meadery Products: Bring home a bottle of honey wine or cider from this local meadery, along with other honey-based products like candles and lotions.

Local Culture and Customs of Starke

Starke has a unique cultural heritage that reflects the diverse influences of its early settlers and inhabitants. The area is known for its agricultural traditions, military history, and Southern hospitality. Visitors should also be aware of local customs and etiquette, such as saying “Yes, ma’am” and “No, sir” as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Starke

The best time to visit Starke is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer months can be hot and humid, while winter months can be cooler and rainy. Visitors should also be aware of hurricane season, which runs from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Starke

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of the area by hiking, fishing, or swimming in local lakes and parks.

  • Do visit local museums and historical sites to learn about the history and culture of Starke.

  • Do try local dishes and beverages, such as Southern fried chicken and sweet tea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural habitats, and always follow park rules and regulations.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions, and be mindful of others’ feelings and beliefs.

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug repellent for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Starke

Starke is generally a safe destination for visitors, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for hurricanes during hurricane season, and follow safety protocols issued by local authorities.

Health in Starke

No major health risks or concerns are associated with visiting Starke. Visitors should still take basic hygiene precautions, such as washing their hands regularly and using insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Starke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Starke

The closest airport to Starke is Jacksonville International Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away. Other local airports include Gainesville Regional Airport and Orlando International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Starke

Greyhound bus service is available in nearby Gainesville and Jacksonville.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Starke by car, with access to major highways like I-10 and US-301.

How to Get Around in Starke

Throughout Starke, visitors can get around by car or bicycle. Local bike rentals and tours are also available, and visitors can explore the town on foot to discover its unique shops, restaurants, and other attractions.

Where to Go Next from Starke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gainesville: Located just a short drive from Starke, Gainesville is home to the University of Florida and offers a vibrant arts and culture scene.

  • Jacksonville: The largest city in Florida, Jacksonville is known for its beaches, museums, and historical landmarks.

  • St. Augustine: The oldest city in the United States, St. Augustine boasts a rich history and picturesque architecture, along with a variety of restaurants, shops, and attractions.

Conclusion

With its rich history, unique culture, and natural beauty, Starke is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to North Central Florida. From hiking and fishing to exploring museums and sampling local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this charming town. Plan your trip today and discover all that Starke has to offer.
