Introduction to North Palm Beach

Located in Palm Beach County, North Palm Beach is an ideal location for those who love sandy beaches, rich culture, dining, and shopping destinations. From breathtaking natural views, hiking routes, and outdoor adventures to fantastic museums, historical landmarks, and amusement parks, North Palm Beach has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in North Palm Beach

Here is a comprehensive list of places to visit in North Palm Beach:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Loggerhead Marinelife Center: Learn about marine conservation and protection while visiting sea turtle rehabilitation and release programs.
  • Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse and Museum: Climb the stairs of this beautifully restored lighthouse to admire the panoramic views of Jupiter Island.
  • Henry Morrison Flagler Museum: This meticulously restored estate museum shows the life of the Florida pioneer and railroad developer Henry Flagler.
  • Norton Museum of Art: Experience one of the best art museums in Florida that showcases a vast collection of contemporary and modern art.

Natural Attractions of North Palm Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • John D. MacArthur Beach State Park: Take a walk on the Atlantic coastline, admire the diverse fauna and flora of Florida’s natural environment, and indulge in scenic views of the ocean.
  • Peanut Island Park: This 79-acre park is perfect for snorkeling, fishing, and camping.
  • DuBois Park: The park boasts picnic areas, playgrounds, fishing docks, and mangrove trails.
  • PGA National Resort and Spa: This luxurious resort features a world-renowned golf course, spa, and multiple restaurants for guests to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Palm Beach

North Palm Beach offers many opportunities for people who want to spend time outdoors and explore the natural beauty of the area. Here are some of our favorite hiking and biking routes:

  • Frenchman’s Forest Natural Area: Take a stroll on the nature trail and appreciate the beauty of Florida’s tropical plants and wildlife.
  • Riverbend Park: This park offers 10 miles of biking trails that share the same paths as hikers and runners.
  • Loxahatchee Slough Natural Area: Riders can enjoy an expansive trail network that provides breathtaking views of the preserve.

Best Things to Do in North Palm Beach: Activities and Tours

North Palm Beach offers visitors a wide range of activities, including the following:

  • Palm Beach Zoo: Experience different species of animals in simulated habitats, and learn more about animal conservation while visiting the zoo.
  • Calypso Bay Waterpark: This water park is perfect for families and friends looking for some water-based fun in the sun, with slides, lazy rivers, and wave pools.
  • Jupiter Outdoor Center: Paddleboard, kayak, or surf in the ocean or nearby rivers with the help of a professional guide.
  • Sightseeing/Boat Tours: Take a boat tour through the Intracoastal waterways, and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Palm Beach

North Palm Beach offers plenty to do, so here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the John D. MacArthur Beach State Park
2 Explore the Frenchman’s Forest Natural Area and Riverbend Park
3 Take a boat tour of Palm Beach Island and the Intracoastal
4 Visit the Loggerhead Marinelife Center and Peanut Island Park
5 Take a nature walk at DuBois Park
6 Visit the Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse and Museum
7 Relax and rejuvenate at PGA National Resort and Spa

Food and Drink Scene of North Palm Beach

North Palm Beach offers visitors an extensive range of dining options, so here are our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Key Lime Pie: This delicious dessert is a Florida classic. It’s made from Key lime juice and has a graham cracker crust and whipped cream topping.
  • Conch fritters: Enjoy fried balls made with conch, spices, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is famous for its orange juice, and you can’t leave North Palm Beach without trying it.
  • Local craft beer: Check out local breweries to taste various craft beers in town.

Where to Stay in North Palm Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in North Palm Beach that suit all budgets, preferences, and occasions.

  • Hilton Singer Island Oceanfront Resort: This beachfront hotel offers guests luxury waterfront rooms, with a full-service spa, fine-dining restaurants, and a private beach.
  • Best Western Palm Beach Lakes Inn: It offers comfortable, well-equipped rooms at an affordable rate.
  • The Parc Hotel: There are various luxurious guestrooms and suites, exceptional continental breakfast, and top-notch amenities.

What to Buy in North Palm Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of North Palm Beach by purchasing something from these top souvenir and gift shops:

  • Ormond Arms: For firearm enthusiasts, it’s a one-stop-shop for guns, knives, and related accessories.
  • Flagler Museum Store: There are unique, high-quality gifts, souvenirs, and books inspired by the Flagler Museum and Florida’s history.
  • The Seagate Beach Club Shop: Purchase beach gear and souvenirs, including caps, towels, shirts, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of North Palm Beach

North Palm Beach has a strong culture and community spirit, and visitors are encouraged to respect and appreciate local customs and traditions. Here are some of the most significant aspects of the North Palm Beach community:

  • The Florida oranges are a crucial part of the local cuisine and identity.
  • There’s a strong emphasis on the preservation and conservation of marine life and natural resources.
  • Community members take great pride in their local history, and they celebrate it through art, museums, and landmarks.

Best Time to Visit North Palm Beach

The best time to visit North Palm Beach is from December through April, where temperatures are most comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer is also a popular season, but the heat and humidity levels can be high.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Palm Beach

Here are some essential things to do and avoid when visiting North Palm Beach:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and marine life.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take boat tours and explore the local waterways.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Ignore the weather forecast before going on outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local culture/customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Palm Beach

While North Palm Beach is generally a safe place, tourists should still take necessary precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Make sure to protect your belongings while in public places.
  • Be vigilant while walking alone or at night.
  • Don’t leave valuable items in your car.

Health in North Palm Beach

Florida is prone to mosquitos, and visitors should take extra precautions to avoid mosquito bites. Health concerns and safety guidelines for COVID-19 may vary, so it’s vital to check travel advisories before the visit.

Getting to North Palm Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Palm Beach is easily accessible by various modes of transportation:

Airports Near North Palm Beach

  • Palm Beach International Airport (15 minutes away)
  • Fort Lauderdale Airport (one-hour drive)
  • Miami International Airport (one-hour and fifteen minutes drive)

Bus Stations Near North Palm Beach

  • Greyhound Bus Lines (15 - 20 minutes)
  • Palm Tran (local bus service)

Other Options

If you’re planning to drive, there are several highways connecting North Palm Beach to other cities in Florida, such as I-95 and Florida’s Turnpike.

How to Get Around in North Palm Beach

While renting a car is a good option for those visiting for an extended period, there are other transportation options available:

  • Take a taxi or ride-share service (Uber, Lyft).
  • Rent a bike from various locations throughout the town.
  • Take Palm Tran, the public bus service that serves the area.

Where to Go Next from North Palm Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby towns and cities worth exploring after your visit to North Palm Beach, including:

  • West Palm Beach: Visit the Palm Beach Zoo, Antique Row Art & Design District, and the iconic Clematis Street nightlife.
  • Palm Beach: Explore the high-end shops, restaurants, and boutiques, and enjoy the pristine beaches.
  • Jupiter: Visit the hometown of Florida’s oldest lighthouse or enjoy the great outdoors at Jonathan Dickinson Park.

Conclusion

North Palm Beach is a beautiful destination that offers something for everyone, from natural outdoor beauty to cultural and historical sites, dining, shopping, and entertainment options. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip, knowing where to go, what to eat, how to get around, and what activities to add to the itinerary.
