Introduction to Riviera Beach

Tucked away in Palm Beach County, Greater Miami, the lively city of Riviera Beach boasts pristine beaches, world-class diving sites, and a rich cultural scene. This sunny destination has something for everyone, from thrilling outdoor adventures to relaxing beachfront activities. In this guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Riviera Beach, from the best things to do to where to stay and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Riviera Beach

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palm Beach Maritime Museum: Explore the rich maritime history of Riviera Beach through interactive exhibits, historical artifacts, and a collection of restored vessels.
  • John D. MacArthur Beach State Park: A beautiful natural area featuring a nature center, hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and beach access.
  • Singer Island: A scenic barrier island offering some of the area’s best beaches, shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.

Natural Attractions of Riviera Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peanut Island Park: A small island park that was once the location of America’s Cold War bunker, now transformed into a recreational area featuring a snorkeling lagoon and walking trails.
  • Ocean Reef Park: A pristine stretch of sandy beach with calm waters that is perfect for swimming, lounging, and fishing.
  • Phil Foster Park: A great spot for snorkeling, diving, and fishing located at the eastern end of Blue Heron Boulevard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Riviera Beach

  • Lake Trail: A 5.5-mile walking and biking trail that curves around the eastern shoreline of Palm Beach, offering stunning views of the Intracoastal Waterway.
  • The Grassy Waters Preserve: A 20-mile network of hiking and biking trails that wind through a unique wetland habitat and offer great views of the surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Riviera Beach: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling: The area is known for some of the best diving sites in South Florida, including the USS Clamagore shipwreck and the Blue Heron Bridge.
  • Water sports: From kayaking and paddle-boarding to jet-skiing and windsurfing, there are plenty of ways to enjoy the water in Riviera Beach.
  • Fishing: The area offers some of the best saltwater fishing opportunities in the country, with a variety of species including snapper, grouper, and sailfish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Riviera Beach

Day 1: Spend the day lounging on the beach at Singer Island Day 2: Take a snorkeling tour and explore the underwater world of Peanut Island Park Day 3: Embark on a kayaking adventure at the Grassy Waters Preserve Day 4: Visit Palm Beach Maritime Museum and learn about the area’s rich maritime history Day 5: Go fishing off the coast of Riviera Beach Day 6: Take a bike ride along the Lake Trail and enjoy the scenic views of the Intracoastal Waterway Day 7: Relax at one of the many beachfront restaurants and watch the sunset over the Atlantic Ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Riviera Beach

Must-Try Dishes

  • Key Lime Pie: A must-try Floridian dessert made with fresh key lime juice and crumbly graham cracker crust.
  • Conch Fritters: Delicious Caribbean-style fried balls of dough loaded with minced conch meat.
  • Florida Stone Crab: A local delicacy that is typically served with mustard sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Fresh Coconut Water: A refreshing drink served in a coconut shell that can be found at many beachside vendors.
  • Orange Juice: Florida produces some of the best oranges in the country, and fresh-squeezed orange juice is a staple of the local dining scene.
  • Craft Beer: The area is home to several craft breweries that produce a variety of unique and flavorful beers.

Where to Stay in Riviera Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Singer Island Oceanfront Resort: A luxurious beachfront hotel with spacious rooms, stunning ocean views, and a wealth of amenities.
  • Marriott’s Ocean Pointe: A family-friendly resort that features multiple swimming pools, a fitness center, and a relaxing spa.
  • Palm Beach Shores Resort and Vacation Villas: A top-rated all-inclusive resort that provides guests with access to a private beach, tennis courts, and a range of activities and entertainment options.

What to Buy in Riviera Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Seashells and coral: A popular souvenir from the area’s beautiful beaches.
  • Key Lime Pie Mix: A convenient way to enjoy the taste of this Floridian dessert at home.
  • Local art: The area is home to several talented artists who specialize in creating beautiful pieces inspired by the local scenery and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Riviera Beach

Riviera Beach is a melting pot of cultures, with a diverse population that includes African Americans, Haitians, and Hispanics. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s cultural heritage and open to experiencing local customs and traditions. Some of the most prominent cultural events in the area include the Palm Beach International Boat Show and the SunFest music and art festival.

Best Time to Visit Riviera Beach

The best time to visit Riviera Beach is during the winter months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer months tend to be hot and humid, and the area can get busy with vacationing families. The shoulder seasons of spring and fall can also be a good time to visit, with more moderate temperatures and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Riviera Beach

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.
  • Do wear sunscreen and protective clothing when enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Do try local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in public areas.
  • Don’t feed or disturb wildlife.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for exploring the area’s natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Riviera Beach

Riviera Beach is generally a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to protect their belongings. Avoid walking alone at night and be cautious when using ATMs or carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Riviera Beach

Visitors should take standard health precautions when traveling to Riviera Beach, including staying up-to-date on vaccinations and practicing good hygiene practices. Mosquitoes can be a problem in the area, so it’s a good idea to bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses like Zika and West Nile virus.

Getting to Riviera Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Riviera Beach

  • Palm Beach International Airport: Located about 20 minutes away from Riviera Beach, the Palm Beach International Airport offers direct flights to a variety of domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Riviera Beach

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in West Palm Beach, about a 15-minute drive from Riviera Beach.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Renting a car can be a convenient and flexible transportation option for exploring the Greater Miami area.
  • Taxis and ridesharing services: Taxis and ridesharing services like Uber and Lyft are available in the area.
  • Public transportation: Palm Tran is the local public transportation system and offers bus routes throughout Palm Beach County.

How to Get Around in Riviera Beach

Navigating Riviera Beach is relatively easy, and many of the area’s top attractions are easily accessible by car, bike, or foot. Taxis and ridesharing services are also widely available, and the local public transportation system offers convenient bus routes throughout Palm Beach County.

Where to Go Next from Riviera Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Palm Beach: Just a short drive south from Riviera Beach, Palm Beach is a luxurious destination known for its upscale shopping, dining, and sightseeing opportunities.
  • Miami: One of the world’s most popular travel destinations, Miami is a vibrant city that offers visitors a wealth of cultural and entertainment options.
  • The Florida Everglades: A unique natural wonder, the Florida Everglades are home to a diverse range of wildlife, including alligators, snakes, and rare birds.

Conclusion

Riviera Beach is an exciting and vibrant destination that has something for everyone. From beautiful beaches to outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this Florida gem. With this guide in hand, you’re sure to have a fantastic trip that you’ll remember for years to come.
