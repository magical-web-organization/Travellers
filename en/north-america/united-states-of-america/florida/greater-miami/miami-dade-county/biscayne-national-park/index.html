Introduction to Biscayne National Park

Biscayne National Park is a marine national park situated in Miami-Dade County, Florida, in the United States. Established in 1980, the park covers 172,971 acres, of which 95% consist of water. The park is home to Biscayne Bay, a shallow estuary that encloses several islands, coral reefs, and mangrove forests. The park’s coral reefs are part of the third-largest barrier reef in the world, and its crystal-clear waters offer visitors a unique place to explore marine life.

List of Places to Visit in Biscayne National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Dante Fascell Visitor Center: houses several exhibits regarding the park’s history and natural resources.
  • The Maritime Heritage Trail: a 21-mile trail that narrates South Florida’s maritime history with unique historical sites.
  • Boca Chita Lighthouse: a 65-foot ornate lighthouse with a nearby picnic area.

Natural Attractions of Biscayne National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Biscayne Bay: a shallow, warm-water bay that offers excellent swimming, boating, and fishing opportunities.
  • Elliot Key: the largest island within the park, offering hiking and snorkeling opportunities.
  • Fowey Rocks: located six miles off the coast, Fowey Rocks is a popular spot for diving and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biscayne National Park

  • The Jones Lagoon Trail: an exceptional hiking trail that guides visitors through mangrove forests while highlighting several bird species.
  • The Florida Trail: A 1,300 mile hike that takes visitors through Florida’s rugged terrain.

Best Things to Do in Biscayne National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling: Visitors can experience the coral reefs’ beauty and color with a snorkeling tour.
  • Fishing: Biscayne Bay is home to various fish species, ideal for those planning to catch their lunch.
  • Guided kayak tours: visitors can explore the park’s bays and mangrove forests while gaining insights into the park’s history and ecology.

Food and Drink Scene of Biscayne National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stone Crab Claws: a local delicacy served in various restaurants in the park.
  • Conch Fritters: a popular snack made with a variety of seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Key Lime Margarita: a refreshing cocktail made with lime juice and tequila.
  • Cuban Coffee: a strong, sweet espresso served in small portions.

Where to Stay in Biscayne National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Biscayne National Park Institute: offering camping sites and ranger-led programs.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Miami-South/Homestead: providing visitors with comfortable lodging and easy access to the park.

What to Buy in Biscayne National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coral and shell souvenirs: available in several park gift shops.
  • Mango Chutney, a famous local condiment, significant to the area’s cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Biscayne National Park

  • The park is home to the Seminole Tribe, and visitors are encouraged to respect their culture and customs.
  • Visitors are not allowed to touch or harm marine life within the park.

Best Time to Visit Biscayne National Park

The best times to visit Biscayne National Park are between November to May when temperatures are cooler and the rainfall is low, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biscayne National Park

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the Seminole Tribe.
  • Leave marine life undisturbed in the waters of the park.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind after activities and tours within the park.
  • Attempt to touch or harm marine life within the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biscayne National Park

  • Visitors are advised to wear a life jacket or snorkel vest while swimming in the park’s waters.
  • Be cautious of strong currents that can put swimmers and boaters at risk.

Health in Biscayne National Park

  • Visitors should wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Drinking of park’s water is not permitted. Visitors should carry bottled water.

Getting to Biscayne National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biscayne National Park

  • Miami International Airport: 30 miles from the park.
  • Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport: 49 miles from the park.

Bus Stations Near Biscayne National Park

  • Greyhound: located in Downtown Miami.
  • Miami-Dade Transit: operates several bus routes within Miami.

Other Options

  • Visitors can lease a car or hail a ride-share service to move around Miami.

How to Get Around Biscayne National Park

  • Visitors can take a guided tour or rent a kayak or canoe to explore the park’s waters.
  • Hiking or biking is an ideal way to explore the park’s many trails.

Where to Go Next from Biscayne National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Everglades National Park: home to a diverse collection of wildlife, the park is a designated World Heritage site and biosphere reserve.
  • Key West: famous for its warm oceanic climate, Key West is a popular beach holiday destination.

Conclusion

Biscayne National Park offers visitors a unique experience with warm, crystal-clear waters and beautiful coral reefs. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or a relaxing vacation, the park’s diverse offerings will have you returning for more.
