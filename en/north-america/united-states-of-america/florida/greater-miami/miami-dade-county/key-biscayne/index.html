Introduction to Key Biscayne

Located in Miami-Dade County in Florida, the beautiful island of Key Biscayne is an ideal destination for travelers seeking sun, sand, and relaxation. With its stunning beaches, secluded places, turquoise waters, and lush greenery, Key Biscayne offers a perfect retreat for solo travelers, couples, and families.

List of Places to Visit in Key Biscayne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cape Florida Lighthouse

Built-in 1825, Cape Florida Lighthouse is the oldest standing structure in Miami-Dade County. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse for stunning views of Miami and Key Biscayne.

Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

Named after the editor of The Miami News, this park is one of the most popular attractions in Key Biscayne. Visitors can explore the park’s various trails, picnic areas, beautiful beaches, and learn about the history of the area at the park’s small museum.

Miami Seaquarium

Located on the edge of Key Biscayne Island, the Miami Seaquarium is one of the largest and oldest oceanariums in the United States. The park features several species of marine animals and hosts various animal shows and interactive experiences.

Natural Attractions of Key Biscayne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Crandon Park Beach

Located on the north end of Key Biscayne, Crandon Park Beach is a popular destination for sunbathers, swimmers, and beachcombers. The beach features miles of white sand, clear water, and lush tropical shrubs.

Bear Cut Nature Preserve

Bear Cut Nature Preserve is a 114-acre mangrove forest located on the southern end of Key Biscayne. Visitors can explore the park’s various trails, including the popular Bear Cut Trail, which offers stunning views of the ocean and shoreline.

Biscayne National Park

Located just off the coast of Key Biscayne, Biscayne National Park is a beautiful and unique ecosystem that includes coral reefs, mangrove swamps, and shallow waters. Visitors can go snorkeling, kayaking, or take a boat tour to explore the area’s varied marine life and colorful coral.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Key Biscayne

Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park features several hiking and biking trails that traverse the park’s varied terrain. Some of the popular routes include the Lighthouse Trail, which leads to the top of the Cape Florida Lighthouse, and the Brickell Hammock Trail, which takes visitors through a beautiful mangrove forest.

Bear Cut Nature Preserve

Bear Cut Nature Preserve also offers several hiking and biking trails, including the popular Bear Cut Trail, which offers stunning views of the ocean and shoreline.

Best Things to Do in Key Biscayne: Activities and Tours

Beach Time

With miles of beautiful white sand beaches and crystal clear waters, Key Biscayne is a great place to relax and soak up some sun. Grab a book, a beach towel, and let the sound of the waves wash your worries away.

Watersports

Whether you want to learn to surf, kayak, or paddleboard, Key Biscayne offers visitors an array of watersports activities to try. There are several rental companies located on the island that offer equipment and lessons.

Snorkeling and Diving

With its crystal clear waters and vibrant coral reefs, Key Biscayne is a great place for snorkeling and diving. Visitors can rent snorkeling or diving gear from several local companies and explore the area’s varied marine life up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Key Biscayne

Day Activity
Day 1 Spend the day at Crandon Park Beach and explore the park’s various walking trails.
Day 2 Visit the historic Cape Florida Lighthouse and climb to the top for stunning views of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Key Biscayne

Must-Try Dishes

Key Lime Pie

This tart and refreshing pie is a classic Florida dessert and can be found at many of the island’s restaurants.

Cuban Sandwich

A hearty and filling sandwich made with roasted pork, ham, Swiss cheese, and pickles, the Cuban sandwich is a staple of the area.

Local Beverages

Mojito

This refreshing cocktail made with rum, lime juice, sugar, and mint is a popular drink in Key Biscayne.

Cuban Espresso

Strong and robust, Cuban espresso is perfect for a midday energy boost.

Where to Stay in Key Biscayne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Ritz-Carlton Key Biscayne

This luxurious hotel is located right on the beach and features top-notch amenities such as a spa, several swimming pools, several restaurants, and more.

Silver Sands Beach Resort

A budget-friendly option, Silver Sands Beach Resort offers comfortable accommodations and is located just a short walk from the beach.

What to Buy in Key Biscayne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Key Lime Products

From Key lime-scented candles to Key lime-infused soap, there are a plethora of Key Lime products to choose from at local shops and boutiques.

Handmade Jewelry

Several local artists and artisans offer handmade jewelry made from shells, sea glass, and other beachy materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Key Biscayne

Being part of Miami, Key Biscayne has a vibrant Latin American culture reflected in many cultural events, restaurants, and traditional music. Islanders are very warm and friendly, making visitors feel welcome.

Best Time to Visit Key Biscayne

With its warm temperatures and sunny skies, Key Biscayne is an all-year-round destination. However, the best time to visit is during the winter months, from December to February, when temperatures are cooler and more comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Key Biscayne

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen when visiting the beach or outdoor activities.
  • Be respectful of the delicate marine ecosystems.
  • Be prepared for tropical weather changes.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or trails.
  • Approach wildlife, including turtles, manatees, and alligators.
  • Ignore safety precautions on water adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Key Biscayne

Overall, Key Biscayne is a safe destination to explore. However, visitors should take common-sense safety precautions, especially around water activities.

Health in Key Biscayne

Visitors should drink plenty of water, use sun protection, and wear comfortable clothing and footwear during outdoor activities and tours.

Getting to Key Biscayne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Key Biscayne

The closest airport is Miami International Airport, located 18 miles from Key Biscayne.

Bus Stations Near Key Biscayne

The Key Biscayne bus station is located not far from the Miami River Bridge, offering public transport to other Miami’s locations.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive by boat since Key Biscayne Island is a popular docking place in Miami. Additionally, rental cars and taxis are available at the airport.

How to Get Around in Key Biscayne

Getting around Key Biscayne is relatively easy. Visitors can rent bikes, scooters, or take a cab or a shuttle to navigate the area.

Where to Go Next from Key Biscayne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miami

Visit museums, theaters, art galleries, and experience South Beach nightlife.

Everglades National Park

Explore the unique ecosystem of the Everglades and take a tour of the park’s many trails and nature walks.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a sunny, tropical getaway, Key Biscayne is the perfect destination. With its beautiful beaches, unique cultural attractions, and varied outdoor activities, Key Biscayne offers something for every type of traveler. So pack your bags, sunscreen, and sense of adventure - Key Biscayne awaits!
