Introduction to Little Havana

Nestled in Miami-Dade County, Greater Miami, in Florida, United States of America, North America, Little Havana is a vibrant, colorful, and culturally rich neighborhood. The neighborhood got its name from the Cuban immigrants who created their own community here in the 1960s. Today, Little Havana is a popular tourist destination that showcases the unique blend of Cuban and American cultures and offers visitors a glimpse into the rich history and traditions of the Cuban people. The neighborhood is known for its lively street festivals, authentic Cuban food, and vibrant art scene.

List of Places to Visit in Little Havana

There are several culturally important attractions, natural attractions, hiking/biking routes, and exciting activities to do in Little Havana. Here is a list of some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. CUBA OCHO ART AND RESEARCH CENTER: This art and research center features a gallery of Cuban art, along with a collection of rare books, documents, and photographs related to the history and culture of Cuba.

  2. MIAMI MARLIN BALLPARK: Located in the heart of Little Havana, the Miami Marlin Ballpark is a state-of-the-art sports facility that hosts Miami Marlins home games. With its stunning architecture and modern amenities, it is a must-visit for sports fans.

  3. CALLE OCHO WALK OF FAME: Located on Calle Ocho (Eighth Street), this walk of fame honors famous Cuban and Hispanic personalities, including actors, musicians, and politicians, who have made significant contributions to the community.

  4. TOWER THEATER: Built-in the 1920s, the Tower Theater is a historic landmark that showcases classic and independent films, as well as live performances and cultural events.

  5. BAY OF PIGS MUSEUM AND LIBRARY: This museum and library is dedicated to one of the most significant events in Cuban history - the Bay of Pigs invasion. The exhibits here focus on the history and aftermath of the invasion and its impact on the Cuban-American community.

Natural Attractions of Little Havana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. MAXIMO GOMEZ PARK: Also known as Domino Park, this is a popular gathering spot for locals, where they play dominoes and socialize. It is a great place to take a stroll and observe the everyday life of Little Havana.

  2. LITTLE HAVANA WALKING TOUR: Led by knowledgeable tour guides, this walking tour takes visitors through the streets of Little Havana and offers a glimpse into the local culture and traditions.

  3. LITTLE HAVANA FOOD TOUR: This tour takes visitors on a culinary journey through the neighborhood’s best eateries, where they can taste authentic Cuban dishes and learn about the history and culture behind these foods.

  4. SOUTH BEACH: Located nearby, South Beach is a beautiful, sandy beach that is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Havana

10.​​ LITTLE HAVANA BIKE TOUR: This bike tour takes visitors through the streets of Little Havana and offers a glimpse into the local culture and traditions. With its beautiful architecture and vibrant street art, it is a photographer’s paradise.

Best Things to Do in Little Havana: Activities and Tours

11.​​ CIGAR ROLLING DEMONSTRATION: Cuban cigars are famous all over the world for their unique flavor and quality. At Little Havana Cigar Factory, visitors can watch professional cigar rollers at work and sample some of the finest Cuban cigars.

12.​​ JAZZ NIGHTS: Little Havana is known for its lively music scene, with many bars and clubs featuring live jazz, salsa, and other Latin musical styles. Visitors can head to one of these venues and enjoy a night of dancing and entertainment.

  1. LA FIESTA DEL POLLO: Held every October, La Fiesta del Pollo is a street festival that celebrates the traditional Cuban dish of roasted chicken. Visitors can taste some of the best roasted chicken in the neighborhood and enjoy live music and dancing.

Food and Drink Scene of Little Havana

14.​​ Must-Try Dishes

Little Havana is famous for its authentic Cuban cuisine, and there are several dishes that visitors should try:

  • Ropa Vieja: This is a traditional Cuban dish made with shredded beef, tomatoes, peppers, and onions.

  • Arroz con Pollo: A popular Cuban rice dish that is seasoned with saffron and cooked with chicken and vegetables.

  • Picadillo: A seasoned ground beef dish that is usually served with rice and plantains.

  • Cuban Sandwich: Made with ham, roasted pork, cheese, and pickles, this sandwich is a delicious and filling lunch option.

15.​​ Local Beverages

Little Havana is known for its strong and flavorful coffee, which is usually served with milk and sugar. Visitors should also try traditional Cuban drinks like:

  • Cuba Libre: This cocktail is made with rum, coke, and lime.

  • Mojito: Made with rum, mint, lime, and sugar, this refreshing cocktail is perfect for hot summer days.

  • Batido: A refreshing milkshake made with fresh fruit, milk, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Little Havana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

17.​​ Wyndham Garden: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities, including a pool and fitness center.

18.​​ The Copper Door B&B: This charming bed and breakfast is located in an old Spanish-style mansion and offers a unique and cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Little Havana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

19.​​ CUBAN CIGARS: Little Havana is famous for its authentic Cuban cigars, and visitors should consider bringing home a box or two as a souvenir.

20.​​ ARTWORK: Little Havana is known for its vibrant street art scene, and visitors should check out the local galleries and shops to buy unique and colorful artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Little Havana

21.​​ Little Havana has a rich and vibrant culture that is deeply rooted in Cuban traditions and customs. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

22.​​ Spanish is the primary language spoken in Little Havana, and visitors should learn some basic Spanish phrases to communicate with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Little Havana

23.​​ Little Havana can be visited all year round; however, the best time to visit is from December to April when the weather is cooler and less humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Little Havana

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks and experience the vibrant culture of Little Havana.

  • Visit the local museums and attractions to learn about the history and traditions of the Cuban people.

  • Take a walking tour or bike tour to explore the streets and soak in the local vibe.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Don’t walk alone late at night.

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Little Havana

24.​​ Little Havana is generally safe for tourists; however, visitors should take precautions to avoid petty crime, such as pickpocketing.

25.​​ Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone in dark and isolated areas.

Health in Little Havana

26.​​ Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid tap water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Little Havana: Transportation Options and Logistics

27.​​ Little Havana is easily accessible by several means of transportation, including:

Airports Near Little Havana

  • Miami International Airport: Located approximately five miles from Little Havana, Miami International Airport is the major airport that serves this area.

Bus Stations Near Little Havana

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This station is located in downtown Miami and offers bus services to several destinations in Florida and other states.

Other Options

  • Uber or Lyft: These ride-hailing services are widely available in the Miami area and offer affordable and convenient transportation.

  • Metrorail: Miami’s Metrorail system is a fast and efficient way to get around the city and includes a station in Little Havana.

How to Get Around in Little Havana

28.​​ Walking and biking are the best ways to explore the streets of Little Havana and soak in the local vibe.

Where to Go Next from Little Havana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

29.​​ Little Havana is located near several other popular destinations in Florida. Visitors can consider visiting:

  • South Beach: A beautiful, sandy beach located nearby.

  • Everglades National Park: A unique and diverse ecosystem that is home to several species of wildlife.

  • Key West: A laid-back island that is famous for its beaches and colorful culture.

Conclusion

Little Havana is a vibrant and culturally rich neighborhood in Miami that is a must-visit for anyone interested in Cuban culture and traditions. With its authentic cuisine, lively music, and colorful street art scene, Little Havana offers visitors an unforgettable experience.
