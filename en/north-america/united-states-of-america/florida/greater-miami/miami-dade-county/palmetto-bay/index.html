Introduction to Palmetto Bay

Palmetto Bay is a charming village in Miami-Dade County, located south of downtown Miami. Its prime location on Biscayne Bay means there are endless opportunities for water activities, but there’s much more to Palmetto Bay than just its stunning waterfront. The village boasts a rich history, cultural attractions, and plenty of outdoor activities for visitors of all ages. In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive itinerary of things to see and do in Palmetto Bay, along with tips on where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Palmetto Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Deering Estate

The Deering Estate is a 444-acre environmental, archaeological, and historical preserve that spans across the villages of Palmetto Bay, Cutler Bay, and Pinecrest. Visitors can explore the native ecosystem, including walking trails, butterfly gardens, and bird-watching areas. The estate features historical buildings, including the Deering mansion, where visitors can take guided tours and learn about the history of the estate.

Coral Castle Museum

The Coral Castle Museum, located in nearby Homestead, is a mysterious attraction that is sure to pique your interest. The museum features a bizarre, hand-carved stone structure, including a 9-ton gate that moves with the touch of a finger. Explore the Coral Castle and learn about its history during a guided tour.

Miami Circle

The Miami Circle is a majestic, preserved archeological site located on the bank of the Miami River. The Circle structure was discovered in 1998 and dates back over 2,000 years. Visitors can explore the site and learn about the Tequesta Native American culture, who once inhabited the area.

Natural Attractions of Palmetto Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Biscayne National Park

Biscayne National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, located just a short drive from Palmetto Bay. The park boasts crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, mangrove forests, and abundant marine life. Visitors can take guided snorkeling and scuba diving tours, boat tours, or explore the park on foot along its many trails.

Matheson Hammock Park

Just a short drive from Palmetto Bay is Matheson Hammock Park, a beautiful beachfront park with stunning views of Biscayne Bay. The park features a man-made atoll pool, perfect for swimming and relaxing. Visitors can also go hiking or bird-watching on the park’s many trails.

South Dade Rail Trail

Explore the beauty of Palmetto Bay by bike or foot on the South Dade Rail Trail. This scenic trail runs through natural habitats, including pine rocklands, hardwood hammocks, and wetlands. With beautiful views of Florida’s unique ecosystem, this trail offers an unforgettable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palmetto Bay

No trip to Palmetto Bay is complete without exploring the area’s many scenic trails. Check out these top hiking and biking routes while visiting Palmetto Bay:

Old Cutler Trail

The Old Cutler Trail is a 13-mile trail running along the edge of Biscayne Bay, stretching through Palmetto Bay, Pinecrest, and Coral Gables. The trail features stunning views of the bay and is perfect for hiking or biking.

Biscayne Trail

The Biscayne Trail is another popular trail offering scenic views of the bay. The trail is located on the western side of Biscayne National Park and is perfect for nature walks, bird-watching, and biking.

Best Things to Do in Palmetto Bay: Activities and Tours

Black Point Marina

Black Point Marina is a popular hub for both locals and visitors, offering boat slips, fishing charters, and waterfront dining. Visitors can rent boats, jet skis, and paddleboards for a day on the bay. The marina also hosts a farmers’ market every Sunday, showcasing fresh local produce and artisanal goods.

Escape the Quest

Get your heart pumping and your mind working at Escape the Quest, an escape room located in Cutler Bay. Escape rooms are a great craze worldwide, and Palmetto Bay has its own version with exciting challenges waiting for visitors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Palmetto Bay

To make the most of your time in Palmetto Bay, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Deering Estate and the Miami Circle

Day 2: South Dade Rail Trail

Day 3: Matheson Hammock Park and relaxing beach day

Day 4: Biscayne National Park guided boat tour, snorkeling or scuba diving

Day 5: Old Cutler Trail and biking in Coral Gables

Day 6: Escape the Quest, and Black Point Marina

Day 7: Shopping in Miami’s vibrant neighborhoods or spending more time on Palmetto Bay’s many stunning trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Palmetto Bay

Must-Try Dishes

Palmetto Bay is a food lover’s paradise, and here are some dishes that are not to be missed:

Stone Crab

Stone Crab is a local delicacy served in many restaurants in Palmetto Bay. They’re best enjoyed with a side of mustard sauce.

Key Lime Pie

No visit to Florida is complete without trying Key Lime Pie, a tart, refreshing dessert that originated in the Florida Keys.

Local Beverages

Miami Vice

A classic Miami Vice, a concoction of a pina colada and a strawberry daiquiri, is perfect for hot summer days.

Cuban Coffee

Cuban coffee, known as cafecito, is a strong and sweet espresso served in small portions.

Where to Stay in Palmetto Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palmetto Bay has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from high-end hotels to budget guesthouses.

Palmetto Bay Airbnb

Airbnb is an excellent option for those who want to stay in a homey setting in Palmetto Bay, with several properties to choose from that can fit your needs.

Cutler Bay Hotel

Cutler Bay Hotel is a sleek and modern option with affordable prices, located just a short distance from Palmetto Bay.

What to Buy in Palmetto Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palmetto Bay Artisanal Market

The Artisanal Market is available every second and fourth Sunday of the month. It features a wide array of local homemade items – from souvenirs to soaps, jams, and jellies.

Miam Fruit

Miam Fruit is a local company that specializes in exotic tropical fruits. Visitors can take home unique Florida-grown fruits like dragonfruit, jackfruit, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Palmetto Bay

Palmetto Bay boasts a rich cultural heritage, with many festivals, museums, and art exhibitions throughout the year. The annual Deering Seafood Festival is one of the most popular events, showcasing amazing dishes from some of Miami’s top chefs.

Best Time to Visit Palmetto Bay

The best time to visit Palmetto Bay is during the winter months, from November to February. The weather is cool and dry, allowing for plenty of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palmetto Bay

Do:

  1. Try a Stone Crab, Miami’s unofficial signature seafood.
  2. Visit the Deering Estate to learn about Miami’s history.
  3. Rent a bike and explore the area’s many scenic trails.
  4. Take a day trip to Biscayne National Park for a guided boat tour.
  5. Stay in an Airbnb to get a taste of local living.

Don’t:

  1. Swim in the ocean during Man-O-War season (June to September).
  2. Forget to try Key Lime Pie.
  3. Forget to pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable shoes.
  4. Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palmetto Bay

Palmetto Bay is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions, especially in crowded areas. We recommend using common sense, staying in well-lit areas, and avoiding flashing money or jewelry.

Health in Palmetto Bay

Visitors should also take standard health precautions during their trip to Palmetto Bay. Insects such as mosquitoes can be present throughout the year, so we recommend packing insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants when hiking. Stay hydrated and remember to take breaks if you’re doing outdoor activities.

Getting to Palmetto Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Palmetto Bay

Miami International Airport is the closest airport to Palmetto Bay, located just 19 miles away. You can take a taxi, shuttle or bus service to get to the village.

Bus Stations Near Palmetto Bay

Greyhound Bus Lines operates buses to Miami from several parts of Florida, and the city’s bus terminal is in downtown Miami.

Other Options

For short-distance travel or sightseeing in town, you can rent a bike at one of many possible sites throughout Miami.

How to Get Around in Palmetto Bay

Biking is a favorite way of exploring Palmetto Bay, and visitors can rent a bike at several locations throughout the village. There are also taxi and ride-sharing services available for those who prefer not to bike.

Where to Go Next from Palmetto Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miami Beach

Miami Beach is a famous oceanfront neighborhood known for its beaches, hotels, restaurants, and nightlife.

Everglades National Park

Everglades National Park offers visitors the chance to explore the largest tropical wilderness in the United States, including the Everglades’ unique ecosystem and wildlife.

Conclusion

Palmetto Bay is a unique travel destination with plenty to see and do. From stunning natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and tropical food and drink, a trip to Palmetto Bay is sure to leave an unforgettable impression.
