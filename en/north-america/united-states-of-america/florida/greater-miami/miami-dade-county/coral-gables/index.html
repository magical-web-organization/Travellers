Introduction to Coral Gables

Nestled in the heart of Greater Miami, Coral Gables is a charming city known for its historical landmarks, vibrant culture, beautiful natural scenery, and exquisite cuisine. Founded in the early 1900s, Coral Gables features an eclectic mix of Mediterranean Revival architecture, lush tree-lined streets, and luxurious estates. The city is an idyllic destination for travelers looking for a laid-back, yet sophisticated vacation experience. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, eat, and stay, as well as provide insider tips to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Coral Gables

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Coral Gables Museum

The Coral Gables Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts. Housed in a meticulously restored 1939 building that was once the city’s police and fire station, the museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the city’s past through interactive exhibits, galleries, and programs. One of the highlights of the museum is the outdoor plaza, which features an art installation by renowned artist Michele Oka Doner.

Venetian Pool

The Venetian Pool is a historic landmark that was created in 1924 from a coral rock quarry. The 820,000-gallon pool boasts picturesque waterfalls, hidden grottos, and winding caves. Visitors can take a refreshing dip in the crystal clear waters or lounge on the sandy beach areas.

Biltmore Hotel

The Biltmore Hotel is a legendary icon that has hosted many famous guests throughout its history, including Al Capone, Judy Garland, and Franklin D. Roosevelt. The hotel is a stunning example of Mediterranean Revival architecture and features 275 guest rooms and suites. Be sure to take a tour of the hotel’s historic public spaces and gardens.

Natural Attractions of Coral Gables: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden

The Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden is a 83-acre oasis that features a vast collection of rare tropical plants from around the world. Visitors can stroll through the lush gardens, explore exhibits, and attend events and workshops. The garden also has a butterfly exhibit that is home to over 3,000 butterflies from 45 different species.

Matheson Hammock Park

Matheson Hammock Park is a serene park located on the shores of Biscayne Bay. The park features a man-made atoll pool, a marina, and various hiking and biking trails. Visitors can enjoy picturesque views of the bay, sunbathe on the beach, or rent a kayak or paddleboard to explore the water.

Deering Estate

The Deering Estate is a sprawling nature preserve and historic site that dates back to the 1900s. Visitors can explore the estate’s various ecosystems, including mangrove forests, pine rocklands, and coastal hardwood hammocks. The estate also has various historic buildings, like the Stone House and Richmond Cottage, that offer insight into the estate’s past.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coral Gables

Coral Gables is home to several scenic routes that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. One of the most popular routes is the Old Cutler Trail, which offers picturesque views of Biscayne Bay and lush mangrove forests. Other popular routes include the Commodore Trail, the Granada Golf Course Trail, and the Monty’s Trail.

Best Things to Do in Coral Gables: Activities and Tours

Coral Gables Food Tour

The Coral Gables Food Tour is a delicious way to explore the city’s diverse culinary scene. The tour takes visitors to various restaurants and cafes in the city, where they can sample delicious dishes and learn about the history and culture of the neighborhood.

Art Walk

The Coral Gables Art Walk is a free event that takes place on the first Friday of every month. The event features various art galleries and studios that open their doors to the public. Visitors can explore the vibrant art scene in Coral Gables and meet local artists.

Miracle Mile

Miracle Mile is a bustling shopping and dining district that spans several blocks in downtown Coral Gables. Visitors can browse various boutiques, bookstores, and galleries, or enjoy a meal at one of the many restaurants and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Coral Gables

Must-Try Dishes

Churrasco Steak

Churrasco steak is a popular dish in South America that is enjoyed in many restaurants in Coral Gables. The dish features grilled skirt steak that is seasoned with chimichurri sauce and served with rice and beans.

Stone Crabs

Stone crabs are a seasonal delicacy in Miami that are best enjoyed from October to May. The crabs are steamed and served with mustard sauce and crackers.

Cuban Sandwich

The Cuban sandwich is a staple in Miami’s food scene and can be found in many restaurants and cafes in Coral Gables. The sandwich features roasted pork, ham, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard on Cuban bread.

Local Beverages

Mojito

The mojito is a refreshing cocktail that is made with rum, lime, mint, sugar, and sparkling water. The drink is a favorite in Miami’s cocktail scene and is perfect for sipping on a hot day.

Cafecito

Cafecito is a strong espresso shot that is sweetened with sugar and served in a small cup. The drink is a staple in Miami’s Cuban coffee culture and is perfect for an afternoon pick-me-up.

Where to Stay in Coral Gables: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Biltmore Hotel

The Biltmore Hotel is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Coral Gables. The hotel features 275 guest rooms and suites, a world-class spa, and multiple restaurants and bars. Guests can also enjoy the hotel’s various amenities, including a fitness center, pool, and tennis courts.

Hotel Colonnade Coral Gables

The Hotel Colonnade Coral Gables is a stylish hotel that features 157 guest rooms and suites. The hotel is located in close proximity to Miracle Mile and features various amenities, including a pool, fitness center, and meeting rooms.

Hostelling International Miami

Hostelling International Miami is a budget-friendly option for travelers. The hostel features dormitory-style rooms and private rooms, as well as a common area, kitchen, and outdoor patio.

What to Buy in Coral Gables: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Guayabera Shirt

The guayabera shirt is a traditional Latin American shirt that is perfect for warm weather. The shirt features four pockets and pleats down the front and back.

Key Lime Pie

Key lime pie is a delicious dessert that is made with Key lime juice and a graham cracker crust. The pie is a staple in Miami’s food scene and can be found in many bakeries and restaurants in Coral Gables.

Local Culture and Customs of Coral Gables

Coral Gables is a vibrant city that is deeply rooted in Latin American culture. Residents of Coral Gables are proud of their city’s history and culture and are known for their hospitality and warmth.

Best Time to Visit Coral Gables

The best time to visit Coral Gables is during the winter months, from December to February. During this time, the weather is mild and comfortable, and there are many events and festivals happening in the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coral Gables

Coral Gables is a safe and peaceful city, but as with any urban area, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. It is recommended to avoid walking alone at night and to be aware of your surroundings when walking in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Coral Gables

Visitors to Coral Gables should take precautions to stay healthy during their trip. It is recommended to drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and protect against mosquito bites.

Getting to Coral Gables: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coral Gables

Miami International Airport is the closest airport to Coral Gables. The airport is located approximately 6 miles from the city center and is served by various airlines.

Bus Stations Near Coral Gables

Coral Gables is served by the Miami-Dade Transit bus system. Visitors can take the Transit services to various locations in Miami and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or rideshare service to get to Coral Gables.

How to Get Around in Coral Gables

Coral Gables is a pedestrian-friendly city, and visitors can easily explore the city on foot. Visitors can also take the free trolley service that runs throughout the city or rent a bike to explore the various parks and trails.

Where to Go Next from Coral Gables: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miami Beach

Miami Beach is a world-renowned beach destination that is known for its white sand beaches, clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. The city is located approximately 11 miles from Coral Gables.

Everglades National Park

The Everglades National Park is a natural wonder that features vast wetlands, diverse wildlife, and breathtaking views. The park is located approximately 40 miles from Coral Gables.

Key West

Key West is a charming island destination that is known for its laid-back atmosphere, colorful architecture, and stunning sunsets. The island is located approximately 160 miles from Coral Gables.

Conclusion

Coral Gables is a charming city that offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re exploring the lush gardens and parks, sampling delicious cuisine, or soaking up the city’s laid-back vibes, there’s something for everyone in Coral Gables. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit, and discover the best that this vibrant city has to offer.
