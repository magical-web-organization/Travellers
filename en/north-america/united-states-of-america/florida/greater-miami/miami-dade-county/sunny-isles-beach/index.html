Sunny Isles Beach is a beautiful city situated in Miami-Dade County, Florida. With a plethora of attractions, world-class restaurants, and scenic views, it is a must-visit destination. This travel guide will give you a comprehensive rundown of the best things to do, places to visit, hike and bike trails, food and drink scene, and cultural attractions in Sunny Isles Beach.

Introduction to Sunny Isles Beach

Sunny Isles Beach boasts of over two miles of pristine beaches. The city is a hub of upscale hotels and luxury resorts, making it a perfect spot for relaxation and rejuvenation. The city’s location provides easy access to downtown Miami and Fort Lauderdale, making it a great base for exploring the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Sunny Isles Beach

Sunny Isles Beach is a mix of cultures and customs from around the world. Visitors should be respectful of cultural traditions and customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and respecting the local customs of tipping.

Best Time to Visit Sunny Isles Beach

The best time to visit Sunny Isles Beach is from November to April when the weather is warm and there is little rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sunny Isles Beach

Do:

  • Apply sunblock and wear a hat when outdoors
  • Carry cash for tips and small purchases
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take advantage of hotel amenities such as shuttle services and free breakfasts

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or park
  • Visit unsafe areas or neighbourhoods
  • Assume that everyone speaks English; learn basic Spanish phrases
  • Disrespect local traditions or customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Sunny Isles Beach

Sunny Isles Beach is a safe city for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions, such as locking their belongings and valuables in their hotel safe and avoiding walking alone in dimly lit areas at night.

Health in Sunny Isles Beach

Visitors should be aware of the heat and humidity, which can lead to heat exhaustion and dehydration. Keep hydrated by drinking plenty of water and wearing loose-fitting clothes. Mosquito bites can also be a concern, so apply insect repellent when outdoors.

Getting to Sunny Isles Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sunny Isles Beach

  • Miami International Airport (17 miles away)
  • Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (11 miles away)
  • Palm Beach International Airport (75 miles away)

Bus Stations Near Sunny Isles Beach

  • Greyhound Bus Station (11 miles away)
  • Megabus Miami Bus Station (17 miles away)

Other Options

Visitors can take the Amtrak train to Hollywood, Florida, and take a taxi or rideshare to Sunny Isles Beach. Car rental is also available, and visitors can use the city’s public transportation system.

How to Get Around in Sunny Isles Beach

The preferred mode of transportation in Sunny Isles Beach is walking or biking due to the city’s compact size. Visitors can rent bicycles and use the bike lanes, or use the city’s shuttle service to get around.
