Introduction to Plantation

Plantation, located in Broward County, in Greater Miami, Florida, is a picturesque small city known for its natural beauty, culturally important attractions, and outdoor adventures. It is the perfect destination for solo travelers, couples, and families looking for an escape into Florida’s lush nature, rich history and vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Plantation

There are many amazing places to visit in Plantation that cater to different interests. Here is a list of the most popular attractions and activities in Plantation:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plantation Historical Museum: Discover the rich history and cultural heritage of Plantation, from its Native American roots to the present day, through exhibits, artifacts, and guided tours.
  • Old Davie School Historical Museum: Explore the history of early Florida and the city of Davie through the collections, artifacts, and exhibits of this beautifully restored schoolhouse.
  • Young At Art Museum: A must-visit museum for families, Young At Art Museum features interactive exhibits, workshops, and hands-on activities to engage young minds in art and culture.

Natural Attractions of Plantation: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dorothy O. France Park: A serene park located on the banks of New River, Dorothy O. France Park offers scenic views, playgrounds, and picnic areas for a family-friendly day out.
  • Central Park: A sprawling green oasis in the heart of Plantation, Central Park is a popular destination for jogging, hiking, and outdoor recreation.
  • Sawgrass Recreation Park: A wild and adventurous destination, Sawgrass Recreation Park offers airboat tours, alligator shows, and nature walks in the Everglades’ heart.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plantation

Plantation is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with its miles of scenic trails, parks, and nature reserves. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Plantation are:

  • Loop Trail at Central Park: This scenic 1.5-mile loop trail takes you through lush foliage, beautiful gardens, and lakeside views.
  • Plantation Preserve Linear Trail: A flat and easy trail, the Plantation Preserve Linear Trail is ideal for hikers, bikers, and runners of all levels.
  • Everglades Holiday Park Trail: An exciting adventure trail that takes you through the heart of the Everglades and offers glimpses of Florida’s diverse wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Plantation: Activities and Tours

Plantation has a wide range of activities and tours that cater to every interest. Here are some of the top things to do in Plantation:

  • Airboat tours in the Everglades: Explore the wilds of the Everglades with guided airboat tours, alligator shows, and nature walks.
  • Sightseeing tours: Discover the hidden gems and tourist attractions of Plantation on a guided sightseeing tour.
  • Shopping at Westfield Broward Mall: Shop for souvenirs, local products, and international brands at this upscale mall in Plantation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plantation

If you’re planning to spend a week in Plantation, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day 1 Explore the Plantation Historical Museum and Old Davie School Historical Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Plantation

Plantation offers a wide range of food and drink options, from local and regional flavors to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Plantation:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuban sandwich: A classic Cuban sandwich with roasted pork, ham, Swiss cheese, mustard, and pickles, served on toasted bread.
  • Conch fritters: A Bahamian specialty, conch fritters are deep-fried balls of conch meat, flavored with spices and served with dipping sauce.
  • Key Lime Pie: A Florida classic, Key Lime Pie is a tart and sweet dessert made with Key lime juice and a graham cracker crust.

Local Beverages

  • Café con Leche: A traditional Cuban coffee drink, Café con Leche is a blend of strong espresso and steamed milk, served in a large cup.
  • Gatorade: Invented in Florida, Gatorade is a sports drink that replenishes fluids and electrolytes during exercise.
  • Orange Juice: Florida is famous for its agriculture, and fresh-squeezed orange juice is a staple in Plantation.

  • Renaissance Fort Lauderdale-Plantation Hotel: A luxury hotel with spacious rooms, outdoor pool, and on-site restaurant, located near the city’s tourist attractions and shopping malls.
  • Stay America - Fort Lauderdale - Plantation: A budget-friendly hotel with simple rooms and amenities, suitable for families and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Plantation: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Plantation has many unique and authentic souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the best things to buy in Plantation:

  • Key Lime Pie: Bring home a slice of Florida’s most famous dessert, either frozen or packaged as a dry mix to make at home.
  • Sea Shells: Florida is famous for its beautiful seashells, and you can find a variety of them in Plantation’s beaches and souvenir shops.
  • Local crafts and art: Plantation has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and you can find handmade jewelry, pottery, and paintings in many local stores and boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Plantation

Plantation’s culture and customs reflect Florida’s diverse and cosmopolitan spirit, with a mix of Southern charm, Latin influence, and Caribbean flair. Here are some aspects of Plantation’s culture and customs that you might find interesting:

  • Diversity: Plantation is a melting pot of cultures and ethnicities, with large communities of African-Americans, Hispanics, Haitians, and Caribbean Islanders.
  • Sunny attitude: Floridians are known for their sunny and friendly attitude, and you are likely to receive a warm welcome and smile from the locals in Plantation.
  • Art and music: Plantation has a vibrant art and music scene, with many galleries, museums, and festivals showcasing local and international talent.

Best Time to Visit Plantation

The best time to visit Plantation is from November to April, when the weather is pleasant and dry, and the city is less crowded. However, if you want to enjoy the beaches, water sports, and outdoor activities, summer is the peak season to visit Plantation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plantation

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling to Plantation:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Plantation’s natural beauty and wildlife are some of its best attractions, and you should take advantage of them by hiking, biking, or boating.
  • Try local food and drinks: Florida has a rich cuisine and beverage culture, and you should explore it by dining in local restaurants, cafes, and bars.
  • Get to know the locals: Plantation is a friendly and welcoming city, and you should interact with the locals to learn more about the city’s culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter and pollute: Plantation’s natural beauty is fragile and precious, and you should do your best to preserve it by not littering, polluting, or damaging the environment.
  • Disrespect cultures and traditions: Plantation is a diverse and multicultural city, and you should respect its people, cultures, and traditions, and avoid any offensive behavior or language.
  • Underestimate the weather: Florida’s weather can be unpredictable and extreme, and you should be prepared for hurricanes, thunderstorms, and high humidity levels.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips on safety and health while traveling to Plantation:

Safety in Plantation

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: Plantation is a safe city, but be cautious of your surroundings, especially at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: Florida’s sun can be harsh and damaging, and you should wear protective clothing, hats, and sunscreen to avoid skin damage and heatstroke.
  • Lock your car and valuables: As in any city, thieves and pickpockets can target tourists, and you should lock your car and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Plantation

  • Stay hydrated: Florida’s heat and humidity can cause dehydration, and you should drink plenty of water and fluids to stay hydrated.
  • Keep safe from mosquitoes: Florida’s mosquito season runs from May to October, and you should protect yourself from bites by wearing long sleeves and pants, using repellents, and avoiding outdoor activities during dawn and dusk.
  • Be prepared for emergencies: Florida has a high risk of hurricanes, floods, and other natural disasters, and you should be prepared for emergencies by having a plan, supplies, and emergency contact information ready.

Getting to Plantation: Transportation Options and Logistics

Plantation is a well-connected city with easy access by air, land, and sea. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind when planning your trip to Plantation:

Airports Near Plantation

  • Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport: Located just 10 miles from Plantation, Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport is the major airport for the city and serves international and domestic flights.
  • Miami International Airport: Situated 25 miles from Plantation, Miami International Airport is a large airport serving international and domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Plantation

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Fort Lauderdale, just 10 miles from Plantation, Greyhound Bus Station offers frequent services to major cities and destinations in Florida and beyond.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Plantation is a drivable city, and you can rent a car from many car rental agencies at the airport or downtown locations.
  • Public transportation: The Broward County Transit System offers bus services to Plantation and other parts of the county.

How to Get Around in Plantation

Plantation is a walkable and bike-friendly city, and you can explore many attractions and destinations on foot or by bike. However, if you need to travel longer distances or have mobility issues, here are some transportation options in Plantation:

  • Taxis: Taxis are available in Plantation and can be hailed on the street or pre-booked.
  • Rental cars: Many car rental agencies are available in Plantation, and you can rent a car for the duration of your stay.
  • Ride-sharing services: Uber and Lyft operate in Plantation, and you can use their apps to book a ride easily and affordably.

Where to Go Next from Plantation: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Plantation is located in a prime location, and several nearby destinations and excursions are worth exploring. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Fort Lauderdale: A vibrant and multicultural city, Fort Lauderdale is a popular tourist destination known for its beaches, museums, and nightlife.
  • Miami: A bustling metropolis with a rich Latin culture, Miami is a must-visit city for its colorful art deco architecture, delicious cuisine, and urban vibe.
  • Everglades National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a natural wonder of the world, Everglades National Park is a must-visit destination for its diverse wildlife, stunning views, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Plantation is a hidden gem of Florida, offering a diverse and dynamic mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a lone traveler, a couple, or a family, Plantation offers something unique and exciting for everyone.
