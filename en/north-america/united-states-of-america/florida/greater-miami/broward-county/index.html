Introduction to Broward County

Broward County is located in Greater Miami, Florida, in the United States of America and is a beautiful place to visit. It is known for its beaches, culture, and natural attractions. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Broward County and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Broward County

Broward County has many places to visit and explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in Broward County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Lauderdale Antique Car Museum
  • International Swimming Hall of Fame and Museum
  • Stranahan House Museum
  • Bonnet House Museum & Gardens
  • NSU Art Museum Fort Lauderdale

Natural Attractions of Broward County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hugh Taylor Birch State Park
  • Butterfly World
  • Everglades Holiday Park
  • Flamingo Gardens
  • Anne Kolb Nature Center

Hiking/Biking Routes of Broward County

Broward County has a variety of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Markham Park Mountain Bike Trail
  • Anne Kolb Nature Center Trails
  • Secret Woods Nature Center Trail
  • Pine Island Ridge Natural Area Trail
  • Hugh Taylor Birch State Park Trail

Best Things to Do in Broward County: Activities and Tours

  • Take a water taxi tour of Fort Lauderdale’s canals.
  • Visit the Riverwalk Arts and Entertainment District and catch a show at the Broward Center for the Performing Arts.
  • Try your luck at the Seminole Hard Rock Casino.
  • Take a scenic drive down A1A and stop at the many beaches along the way.
  • Go on a snorkeling or scuba diving trip to explore Broward County’s coral reefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Broward County

Day 1: Explore Fort Lauderdale’s Canals by Water Taxi Day 2: Visit Local Museums and Historical Sites Day 3: Take a day trip to the Everglades and Airboat Tour Day 4: Relax on Pompano Beach Day 5: Go on a Deep Sea Fishing Trip Day 6: Explore Pine Island Ridge Natural Area Day 7: Check out the Local Food Scene and Souvenirs

Local Beverages

Local Culture and Customs of Broward County

  • Respect the beaches and marine life.
  • Tipping is expected in restaurants and bars
  • Casual beach attire is acceptable in most places, but dress up for fancier restaurants and attractions.
  • Don’t forget to thank people with a “thank you” or “please,” it’s considered polite!

Best Time to Visit Broward County

Broward County has a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round. The best time to visit is from November to April, during the winter months. The weather is mild and pleasant, with little rainfall. However, it is also peak tourist season, so expect crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Broward County

Do:

  • Respect the environment and beaches.
  • Enjoy local cuisine and support small businesses.
  • Try new outdoor activities.
  • Make sure to pack sunscreen for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be disrespectful to others.
  • Be disrespectful to marine wildlife.
  • Ignore safety precautions like swimming in dangerous currents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Broward County

Broward County is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should always keep an eye on their belongings and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Broward County

Visitors to Broward County should be aware of the high humidity and heat during the summer months. They should stay hydrated and take frequent breaks from outdoor activities to avoid heat exhaustion or heat stroke.
