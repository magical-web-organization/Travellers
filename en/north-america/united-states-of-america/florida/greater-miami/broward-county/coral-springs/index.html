Introduction to Coral Springs

Located in Broward County, Greater Miami, Florida, Coral Springs is a vibrant city known for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With a population of over 130,000 people, it is the fifth-largest city in Broward County and perfect for a weekend getaway or family vacation.

Coral Springs was named after the natural coral reef formations that lie beneath the city. The city has a rich and diverse history, dating back to the 1800s when it was first settled by pioneers seeking to build a community in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Coral Springs

Coral Springs has plenty of places to visit and explore, from museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Coral Springs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coral Springs Museum of Art: This museum showcases contemporary art and provides art classes for visitors of all ages.

  • Butterfly World: This unique attraction is home to over 20,000 butterflies and offers visitors the chance to walk among them and learn about their life cycle.

  • Coral Springs Center for the Arts: This performing arts center hosts a variety of shows, concerts, and theatrical performances.

Natural Attractions of Coral Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sawgrass Nature Center & Wildlife Hospital: Visitors can meet rescued animals and learn about Florida’s ecosystems.

  • Coral Springs Aquatic Complex: Featuring an Olympic-sized pool, water slides, and a lazy river, this water park is a perfect place to cool off on a hot day.

  • Mullins Park: With a playground, walking trails, and sports fields, this park offers something for everyone.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coral Springs

Coral Springs has many beautiful hiking/biking routes that offer scenic views of nature and wildlife. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Tall Cypress Natural Area: With over 66 acres of natural landscape, visitors can bike or hike the trails and explore the local flora and fauna.

  • Sawgrass Nature Center & Wildlife Hospital Trails: These trails offer visitors the chance to see rescued animals and explore the natural beauty of South Florida.

  • Marjory Stoneman Douglas Trail: Named after the famous environmentalist who fought to save the Everglades, this trail is a tribute to her and her work.

Best Things to Do in Coral Springs: Activities and Tours

Coral Springs offers a plethora of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Coral Springs:

  • Savor the Notes Wine Tasting: This award-winning wine and tapas bar offers an extensive selection of wines from around the world.

  • Goby Pedals: Visitors can explore downtown Coral Springs on a pedal bike and learn about local history and culture from a knowledgeable guide.

  • Kayaking and Paddle Boarding: With multiple waterways and canals, Coral Springs is an ideal place to explore by kayak or paddleboard.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coral Springs

Day 1: Visit the Coral Springs Museum of Art, followed by dinner at Savor the Notes Wine Tasting.

Day 2: Explore nature trails at the Sawgrass Nature Center and Wildlife Hospital, followed by lunch at the Coral Springs Aquatic Complex.

Day 3: Go bicycling with Goby Pedals through downtown Coral Springs, followed by a visit to Butterfly World.

Day 4: Take a kayaking or paddleboarding tour of Coral Springs’ waterways.

Day 5: Visit the Coral Springs Center for the Arts to catch a show or concert, followed by a walking tour of historic neighborhoods.

Day 6: Go hiking in the Tall Cypress Natural Area and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 7: Spend the day at Mullins Park, enjoying the playground and sports fields.

Food and Drink Scene of Coral Springs

Coral Springs has a diverse culinary scene, with restaurants and cafes offering delicious food and unique drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuban Sandwich: This popular sandwich is made with Cuban bread, roasted pork, ham, cheese, pickles, and mustard.

  • Seafood: With its proximity to the ocean, Coral Springs offers fresh and delicious seafood options, including shrimp, lobster, and grouper.

  • Key Lime Pie: This classic dessert is made with key lime juice, condensed milk, and a graham cracker crust.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Coral Springs has several craft breweries that offer unique and delicious beers.

  • Cocktails: Many restaurants and bars offer handcrafted cocktails using local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Coral Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Coral Springs has a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • Marriott Coral Springs Hotel, Golf Club & Convention Center: Located near many of the city’s attractions, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a golf course.

  • Sawgrass Grand Hotel and Suites Sports Complex: This family-friendly hotel features a pool and sports complex, perfect for active travelers.

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Coral Springs South: Located near popular attractions and restaurants, this hotel offers budget-friendly options for travelers.

What to Buy in Coral Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Coral Springs has many unique and handmade products available for purchase. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Coral Springs T-Shirts and Caps: Many gift shops offer T-shirts and caps with the city’s name and logo.

  • Artwork and Crafts: The Coral Springs Museum of Art gift shop offers a selection of locally-made artwork, crafts, and jewelry.

  • Wine and Craft Beer: Visitors can bring home a bottle of locally-made wine or craft beer as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Coral Springs

Coral Springs has a diverse population, with many different cultures and customs. It is important to be respectful of all cultures and customs when visiting the city.

Best Time to Visit Coral Springs

The best time to visit Coral Springs is between December and April when the weather is mild and there is little rain. The summer months can be hot and humid, with frequent afternoon thunderstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coral Springs

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Coral Springs by hiking or biking.

  • Do try the local cuisine, including fresh seafood and craft beer.

  • Do be respectful of all cultures and customs of the community.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.

  • Don’t wear offensive clothing or use offensive language.

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring outdoor areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coral Springs

Coral Springs is a safe city, with a low crime rate. It is recommended to avoid walking alone at night and to be aware of your surroundings when exploring unfamiliar areas.

Health in Coral Springs

Visitors should be aware of the hot and humid weather, especially during the summer months, and stay hydrated. Sunscreen and insect repellent are also recommended when exploring outdoor areas.

Getting to Coral Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Coral Springs is easily accessible by car and public transport. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Coral Springs

  • Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL)
  • Miami International Airport (MIA)

Bus Stations Near Coral Springs

  • Coral Springs – Park and Ride
  • Pompano Beach – Greyhound Bus Station

Other Options

  • Tri-Rail: A commuter train that connects Miami, Fort Lauderdale, and West Palm Beach.

  • Yellow Cab: A local taxi service that can be used to explore the city.

How to Get Around in Coral Springs

Coral Springs can be explored by car, bike, or walking. Many attractions and restaurants are located within walking or biking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Coral Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coral Springs is located near several other exciting destinations, including Miami, Fort Lauderdale, and the Everglades. Visitors can explore these nearby locations before returning home.

Conclusion

Coral Springs offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Its diverse population and friendly community make it the perfect place to visit for a weekend getaway or family vacation. With plenty of things to do and explore, Coral Springs is a must-visit destination in Florida.
