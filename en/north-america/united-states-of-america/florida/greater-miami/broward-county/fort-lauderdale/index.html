Introduction to Fort Lauderdale

Located in Broward County, Greater Miami, Florida, Fort Lauderdale is a vibrant coastal city that offers travelers of all ages and interests an unforgettable experience. Renowned for its miles of beautiful beaches, world-class museums, and rich cultural heritage, Fort Lauderdale attracts millions of visitors each year. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or international cuisine, Fort Lauderdale is an ideal destination for you. In this guide, we’ll show you how to navigate this beautiful city, including where to stay, what to do, and how to stay safe.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale is home to a wide range of attractions, from museums and historical landmarks to natural parks and outdoor activities. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Fort Lauderdale:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bonnet House Museum and Gardens: This historic home was built in the 1920s and now serves as a museum with tropical gardens, art, and artifacts.

  • NSU Art Museum: The museum features both contemporary and modern art, including works by Pablo Picasso, Frank Stella, and others.

  • Historic Stranahan House Museum: This house is one of the oldest buildings in Fort Lauderdale and offers tours and exhibits highlighting the city’s history.

Natural Attractions of Fort Lauderdale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Everglades National Park: The park is best known for its vast marshlands and breathtaking wetlands, where visitors can hike, bike, and take guided tours.

  • Hugh Taylor Birch State Park: A natural oasis in the heart of Fort Lauderdale, this park offers visitors a wide range of outdoor activities, from hiking and kayaking to picnicking and swimming.

  • Fort Lauderdale Beach: With miles of white sand beaches, this is one of the most popular attractions in Fort Lauderdale. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, or taking part in water sports like jet skiing and parasailing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Lauderdale

If you’re an avid hiker or biker, Fort Lauderdale has a variety of scenic trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the best options:

  • Riverwalk Linear Park: This 1.5-mile trail stretches along the New River and offers beautiful views of the city’s skyline.

  • Hugh Taylor Birch State Park: The park features numerous trails that wind through the park’s natural habitats, including mangroves, coastal dunes, and hardwood hammocks.

  • North Fork Trail: This 6-mile trail offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city and takes hikers through a variety of natural settings.

Best Things to Do in Fort Lauderdale: Activities and Tours

Fort Lauderdale is home to countless activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Fort Lauderdale:

  • Water Taxi Tour: This unique tour takes visitors through the city’s famous canals and waterways, offering a unique perspective of Fort Lauderdale’s architecture and culture.

  • Airboat Tour in Everglades National Park: Take a thrilling ride through the park’s wetlands and swamps and observe the wildlife that lives in this extraordinary ecosystem.

  • Shopping on Las Olas Boulevard: This vibrant street offers a wide range of shops and boutiques selling everything from high-end fashion to beachwear and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Lauderdale

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Fort Lauderdale, here’s a sample itinerary that highlights the best attractions and activities:

Day Activity/Attraction
1 Visit Bonnet House Museum and Gardens and take a water taxi tour
2 Explore Everglades National Park and take an airboat tour
3 Spend the day at Fort Lauderdale Beach and try out some water sports
4 Visit the NSU Art Museum and stroll along Las Olas Boulevard for shopping and food
5 Take a hike in Hugh Taylor Birch State Park and enjoy a picnic lunch
6 Explore the Historic Stranahan House Museum and visit nearby attractions like Butterfly World
7 Relax and enjoy the beach or take part in a cooking class or food tour to experience the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale offers a diverse food and drink scene that caters to all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blackened Mahi Mahi: A popular dish in waterfront restaurants, this dish features fresh Mahi Mahi coated in flavorful spices.

  • Key Lime Pie: A sweet and tangy dessert, this classic Floridian dish is made with fresh key limes and a graham cracker crust.

  • Cuban Sandwich: A Cuban sandwich is a staple in Fort Lauderdale, made with Cuban bread, roast pork, ham, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Key West Sunset Ale: A locally brewed beer made with passion fruit and honey, this is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

  • Mojito: Made with lime, sugar, soda water, mint, and rum, this refreshing cocktail is a popular choice for visitors and locals alike.

  • Coca-Cola with Vanilla: This unique twist on a classic soda is a favorite among locals - and you’ll only find it in Fort Lauderdale.

Where to Stay in Fort Lauderdale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort Lauderdale has accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Ritz-Carlton, Fort Lauderdale: A luxurious beachfront hotel with oceanfront suites and amazing spa facilities.

  • Fort Lauderdale Grand Hotel: A moderately priced hotel located close to downtown Fort Lauderdale, with comfortable and clean guest rooms.

  • HI Fort Lauderdale Beach Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers or those traveling on a budget, complete with dorm rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Fort Lauderdale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Fort Lauderdale, here are some local products and souvenirs worth considering:

  • Seashells: Fort Lauderdale’s beaches are home to a large selection of seashells, making them a popular souvenir for visitors.

  • Key Lime Products: From key lime pie to key lime-scented candles, there are many ways to bring home this unique Florida flavor.

  • Local Artwork: Fort Lauderdale is home to many local artists, and you can find their work in galleries and gift shops throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale has a unique culture and customs that are worth learning about before you visit. Here are some key things to keep in mind:

  • Tipping: Tipping is expected in Fort Lauderdale, especially in restaurants and bars. Standard tipping rates range from 15% to 20% of the total bill.

  • Beach Etiquette: It’s considered rude to play loud music or engage in disruptive behavior on the beach. Respect other people’s space and keep the beach clean.

  • Dress Code: Fort Lauderdale has a casual dress code, but some restaurants and bars may require more formal attire.

Best Time to Visit Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale boasts a warm and sunny climate all year round, so the best time to visit depends on your preferences and activities. October to May is considered the peak tourist season when the weather is between 70°F to 80°F and perfect for outdoor activities. June to September is the off-season, with high temperatures in the 80s-90s°F and occasional thunderstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Lauderdale

To ensure a smooth trip, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks, as Fort Lauderdale has a vibrant food and drink scene.

  • Do wear comfortable and breathable clothing as the weather can be hot and humid.

  • Do take advantage of outdoor activities, from hiking to watersports.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches.

  • Don’t bring glass bottles to the beach.

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended on the beach or in your car.

Safety and Health

Fort Lauderdale is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take precautions to stay safe and healthy.

Safety in Fort Lauderdale

  • Use caution when traveling alone at night and be aware of your surroundings.

  • Lock your car and keep valuables hidden from sight.

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items with you.

Health in Fort Lauderdale

  • Apply sunscreen frequently, as the sun can be intense in Fort Lauderdale.

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water, especially if you’re spending time outdoors.

  • Be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne diseases and use insect repellent when necessary.

Getting to Fort Lauderdale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fort Lauderdale is easily accessible by air, sea, or land, and has a multitude of transportation options to choose from.

  • Airports Near Fort Lauderdale: The Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport is located just five miles from downtown Fort Lauderdale, and Miami International Airport is located 30 miles south in Miami.

  • Bus Stations Near Fort Lauderdale: The Greyhound Bus Station and the Megabus stop in downtown Fort Lauderdale, connecting travelers to major cities throughout the US.

  • Other Options: Uber, taxis, and rental cars are also readily available in Fort Lauderdale.

How to Get Around in Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale is a city that is best explored on foot or by bike. Other popular transportation options include:

  • Water Taxi: A unique way to explore the city’s waterways and canals, the Water Taxi provides access to many of Fort Lauderdale’s top attractions.

  • Bus and Streetcar: The city’s public bus and streetcar system are reliable and easy to use.

  • Uber or Taxis: Taxis and Uber are readily available for those who prefer private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Fort Lauderdale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fort Lauderdale is surrounded by other popular destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some of the best options:

  • Key West: Located just a few hours away from Fort Lauderdale, Key West offers a unique and laid-back island vibe.

  • Miami: Only 30 miles away, Miami is the perfect place to visit for those looking for a bustling city and a vibrant nightlife.

  • Palm Beach: Only a short drive away, Palm Beach boasts beautiful gardens, upscale shopping, and luxurious mansions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a relaxing beach vacation, Fort Lauderdale is an ideal destination for you. With its beautiful beaches, world-class museums, and unique attractions, Fort Lauderdale has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your visit and discover all that this vibrant city has to offer.
