Introduction to Central Florida

Central Florida is located in the heart of the sunshine state of Florida, in the United States of America. This region has a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural landmarks, and bustling modern cities. Central Florida is known for its theme parks, breathtaking landscapes, freshwater springs, thrilling adventures, and rejuvenating experiences. It is a destination that appeals to all ages and interests, and no matter why you choose to visit, you are guaranteed to have a good time.

List of Places to Visit in Central Florida

Central Florida is home to many must-see attractions and places of interest. Here are some of the top destinations to add to your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex - It is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in space and technology. You can learn about the history of the space program, see real rockets and spaceships, and watch launches from the center if you’re lucky.
  • The Ringling Museum of Art - The museum houses an impressive art collection, including works by Old Masters, Asian art, and contemporary pieces, plus an impressive collection of circus memorabilia.
  • Stetson Mansion - A breathtaking home built-in 1886, is now a museum that showcases its rich history and stunning architecture.
  • Harry P. Leu Gardens - A historically significant botanical garden home to more than 50 acres of beautiful gardens and forests.

Natural Attractions of Central Florida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wekiwa Springs State Park - It’s an excellent destination for hiking, horseback riding, canoeing, and kayaking. The park also features a natural spring that’s perfect for swimming and relaxing.
  • Lake Eola Park - Located in the heart of downtown Orlando, the park offers incredible views of the city skyline, and you can rent swan boats to paddle around the lake.
  • Ginnie Springs - One of the most beautiful freshwater springs, a perfect spot for swimming, tubing, paddleboarding, and camping.
  • Blue Spring State Park - This destination is a perfect destination for manatee watching, kayaking, snorkeling, and paddleboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Florida

Central Florida is a fantastic destination for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Here are some recommended routes:

  • West Orange Trail - A 22-mile paved trail that meanders through lush forests and charming towns, providing fantastic views of wildlife, art installations, and historic sites.
  • Cross Seminole Trail - This 23-mile trail winds through quiet residential areas, parks, and natural areas, offering great views of nature and a glimpse into the local way of life.
  • Black Bear Wilderness Area - This 7.1-mile trail winds through rugged terrain, dense forests, and scenic views of the St. Johns River, home to bald eagles, otters, and alligators.

Best Things to Do in Central Florida: Activities and Tours

  • Walt Disney World Resort - The world’s most famous theme park, features four amusement parks, two water parks, and many hotels and restaurants. It’s a destination that appeals to all ages, with thrilling rides, shows, and parades.
  • Universal Orlando Resort - A popular destination featuring two amusement parks, a water park, and Universal CityWalk. It’s a perfect destination for families, with exciting rides, shows, and attractions like The Wizarding World of Harry Potter, Jurassic Park, and Transformers.
  • Discovery Cove - One of the best places to swim with dolphins, go snorkeling, and relax on a tropical island.
  • Clear Kayak Tours - An excellent way to discover the natural beauty of Central Florida’s waterways, paddling along with manatees, dolphins, and other wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Florida

Here is a recommended 7-day itinerary for a fun-filled stay in Central Florida:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Orlando and check-in to your hotel. Explore downtown Orlando and visit Lake Eola Park.
2 Spend the day exploring Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex
3 Day trip to St. Augustine, the oldest city in the United States, and visit the Castillo de San Marcos National Monument and other historical sites.
4 Spend the day exploring Walt Disney World Resort
5 Cool off at Typhoon Lagoon or Blizzard Beach water parks at the Walt Disney World Resort.
6 Plan a day trip to Ginnie Springs where you can go tubing, scuba diving, and paddleboarding.
7 Spend your last day at Universal Orlando Resort, or enjoy a relaxing day lounging by the pool at your hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Florida

Central Florida has a fantastic food and drink scene that pays tribute to Southern cuisine and international flavors. Here are some delicious dishes to try and local beverages to enjoy:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gator tail - A traditional Florida dish, usually served fried, tastes similar to chicken, but with its unique flavor.
  • Key Lime Pie - A classic dessert made from fresh Key limes, graham cracker crust, and topped with whipped cream.
  • Cuban Sandwich - A delicious sandwich made with ham, roasted pork, Swiss cheese, mustard, and pickles pressed on Cuban bread.
  • Beignets - A French pastry that is a cross between a doughnut and a fritter.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice - Central Florida is known for its fresh and delicious oranges. Orange juice is a must-try beverage while visiting this region.
  • Florida Craft Beer - Central Florida is home to many microbreweries and brewpubs that offer delicious and fresh craft beer.
  • Cuban Coffee - Known for being strong and sweet, Cuban coffee is the perfect pick-me-up.

Where to Stay in Central Florida: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Central Florida has an abundance of options for accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Grand Bohemian Hotel Orlando - A luxury hotel located in Downtown Orlando that features stylish rooms, a rooftop pool, and an art gallery.
  • Hyatt Regency Orlando - Enjoy a comfortable stay in this elegant hotel situated close to the Walt Disney World Resort and Universal Orlando Resort.
  • Hi Orlando Hostel - A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Orlando near restaurants, bars, and public transportation.

What to Buy in Central Florida: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Central Florida offers a wide range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some to consider:

  • Florida Citrus - Take home bags of fresh oranges or freshly squeezed orange juice.
  • Mickey Mouse Ears - A classic souvenir that you’ll find in most Disney gift shops. You can also get them embroidered with your name.
  • Amish-made furniture - Visit the Amish communities of Sarasota and pick up a traditional hand-made rocking chair or quilt.
  • Florida Wine - Central Florida has many vineyards in its vicinity that offer unique wines, and you can take home bottles for yourself or as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Florida

Central Florida is a melting pot of cultures, and it’s essential to respect them while visiting. Some customs and etiquette to keep in mind include:

  • Tipping - Tipping is customary in the United States, and in Central Florida, a standard tip is usually 15-20% of the total bill.
  • Greetings - Greet people with a handshake, and don’t be surprised if someone greets you with a hug or a kiss on the cheek.
  • Dress Code - Dress code varies from location to location, but in most places, casual wear is acceptable.

Best Time to Visit Central Florida

Central Florida is a year-round destination, with warm temperatures and plenty of sunshine. However, the most popular time to visit is from December to April when the weather is mild, and there’s less chance of rain. The summer months from June to August can be hot and humid, and there’s a higher chance of daily thunderstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Florida

Do:

  • Do practice basic safety measures like keeping an eye on your belongings and locking your car doors.
  • Do have an open mind and respect the local culture.
  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities like swimming, hiking, and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your valuables in plain sight in your car.
  • Don’t litter. It is illegal and harmful to the environment.
  • Don’t underestimate the power of the sun. Use sunscreen and stay hydrated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Florida

  • Keep safety measures in mind while in crowded tourist areas, theme parks, and beaches.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and be careful when traveling alone at night.
  • Watch out for alligators in freshwater areas. Keep your distance, and do not feed them.

Health in Central Florida

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Apply sunscreen all year long to avoid sunburn and reduce skin cancer risk.
  • Get the recommended vaccinations before visiting.

Getting to Central Florida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Central Florida

  • Orlando International Airport
  • Tampa International Airport
  • Orlando Sanford International Airport
  • Daytona Beach International Airport

Bus Stations Near Central Florida

  • Greyhound Orlando Bus Station
  • Greyhound Tampa Bus Station
  • Greyhound Ocala Bus Station

Other Options

  • Amtrak - Train service is available to Orlando, Tampa, and Miami.
  • Driving - Central Florida is easily accessible via the interstate highway system, and there are many car rental options.

How to Get Around in Central Florida

  • Car Rental - The best way to get around Central Florida is by renting a car.
  • Public Transportation - Lynx is the public transportation system that operates in Orlando and surrounding areas, providing affordable and convenient transportation.
  • Ride-Share services - Ride-share services like Uber and Lyft are available throughout Central Florida.

Where to Go Next from Central Florida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sarasota - Visit this charming city located on Florida’s west coast known for its beautiful beaches and lively arts scene.
  • St. Petersburg - Visit this coastal city with a thriving downtown, museums, and bars and restaurants.
  • Cocoa Beach - An excellent destination for surfing, kayaking, and paddleboarding, also home to the famous Ron Jon Surf Shop.

Conclusion

Central Florida is a destination that has something for everyone. Explore amusement parks, unique art galleries and museums, or relax on beautiful beaches, and take in the natural beauty of freshwater springs and wildlife. Enjoy tasty Southern cuisine and local beverages, and take home some unique souvenirs. With so much to see and do in Central Florida, you’ll never be bored, and you’ll leave with wonderful memories that will last a lifetime!
