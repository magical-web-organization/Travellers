Introduction to Treasure Coast

Located in the southeastern part of Florida, Treasure Coast is a region that spans across four counties – Indian River, St. Lucie, Martin, and Palm Beach – and is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, historic sites, and natural landscapes. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply want to relax and unwind, Treasure Coast has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, things to do, and must-try food and drink, and more in Treasure Coast.

List of Places to Visit in Treasure Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Elliott Museum – Located in Stuart in Martin county, The Elliott Museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the history of Treasure Coast, with vast exhibits of classic cars, art, and Americana.
  2. The Sunrise Theatre – This restored Art Deco theatre in Fort Pierce has a rich history, having hosted legendary performers like Louis Armstrong and Jackie Gleason.
  3. Smithsonian Marine Station – Located on the Indian River Lagoon in Fort Pierce, the Smithsonian Marine Station is a research facility that showcases the diverse marine life found in the lagoon, including seahorses and sea turtles.
  4. Gilbert’s Bar House of Refuge – Built-in 1876, the Gilbert’s Bar House of Refuge is one of the last remaining houses of refuge along Florida’s southeast coast. The house is a testament to the brave men who rescued shipwrecked sailors and travelers.

Natural Attractions of Treasure Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. St. Lucie Inlet Preserve State Park – This sprawling state park in Stuart has hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the St. Lucie River.
  2. Blowing Rocks Preserve – You’ll find one of the most beautiful, unspoiled beaches in Florida at the Blowing Rocks Preserve, located just south of Hobe Sound.
  3. Jonathan Dickinson State Park – With over 11,000 acres of land, the Jonathan Dickinson State Park offers a range of outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to kayaking and camping.
  4. Savannas Preserve State Park – This state park in Port St. Lucie is known for its diverse ecosystems, with marshes, wetlands, and lakes all within its borders. It’s an ideal destination for birdwatching and wildlife photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Treasure Coast

  1. St. Lucie River Greenway – This scenic route parallels the St. Lucie River and offers a beautiful view of the river and its mangrove forests.
  2. Hobe Sound National Wildlife Refuge Trail – A 2.5-mile hike that takes you through the mangrove forests of the Hobe Sound National Wildlife Refuge.
  3. Indian River Drive Scenic Byway – Biking the Indian River Drive Scenic Byway is an excellent way to take in the stunning views of the Indian River Lagoon and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Treasure Coast: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a scenic boat tour of the Indian River Lagoon – This tour will give you the chance to see dolphins, manatees, and other marine life up close.
  2. Visit the Navy SEAL Museum – Located in Fort Pierce, the Navy SEAL Museum is a unique destination that recognizes the bravery and sacrifices of the Navy SEALs.
  3. Go on a fishing charter – With its many lakes, rivers, and waterways, Treasure Coast is an excellent destination for fishing enthusiasts.
  4. Explore the Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse and Museum – This iconic lighthouse in Jupiter offers breathtaking views of the coast and is a testament to the region’s maritime history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Treasure Coast

Day 1 – Arrive in Treasure Coast and explore the historic downtown of Stuart Day 2 – Visit the Elliott Museum and the Florida Oceanographic Coastal Center in Stuart Day 3 – Hiking at the Jonathan Dickinson State Park and exploring the Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse Day 4 – Take a boat tour of the Indian River Lagoon and visit the Navy SEAL Museum in Fort Pierce Day 5 – Visit the Blowing Rocks Preserve in Hobe Sound and enjoy the beaches of Treasure Coast Day 6 – Biking at the Indian River Drive Scenic Byway and exploring the Gilbert’s Bar House of Refuge in Jensen Beach Day 7 – Depart from Treasure Coast

Food and Drink Scene of Treasure Coast

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Conch Fritters – A popular dish in the Caribbean, conch fritters are now a staple of Florida’s seafood scene. You can find them in most seafood restaurants in Treasure Coast.
  2. Fried Gator Tail – For something a bit more adventurous, try fried alligator tail. It’s a uniquely Floridian dish that you won’t find anywhere else.
  3. Key Lime Pie – No trip to Florida is complete without trying the state’s official dessert, Key Lime Pie. Treasure Coast has some of the best Key Lime Pie in the state.

Local Beverages

  1. Fresh Orange Juice – Florida is famous for its oranges, and Treasure Coast is the perfect place to sample some of the state’s best fresh-squeezed orange juice.
  2. Craft Beer – With a thriving craft beer scene, Treasure Coast has a range of breweries and taprooms where you can try some amazing local beer.
  3. Rum Cocktails – Rum has a long history in Florida, and Treasure Coast is no exception. Sample some delicious rum cocktails made with local rum at one of the area’s many bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Treasure Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hutchinson Island Marriott Beach Resort & Marina – Located on Hutchinson Island in Stuart, this resort offers private beach access, a marina, and a range of amenities and services.
  2. Jupiter Beach Resort and Spa – This luxury resort in Jupiter overlooks the Atlantic ocean and offers guests access to a private beach, spa, and restaurant.
  3. Caribbean Shores Bed & Breakfast – This adult-only B&B in Fort Pierce offers a unique, tropical-themed stay with private rooms and a pool.

What to Buy in Treasure Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Seafood – Florida’s seafood scene is one of the best in the country, and Treasure Coast has some of the freshest catches. Buy some of the area’s famous seafood to take home and cook.
  2. Citrus Fruits – Florida is known for its citrus fruits, and Treasure Coast has some of the best. Stock up on oranges, grapefruits, and other citrus fruits to take home.
  3. Handmade Crafts – Treasure Coast has a thriving arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans crafting unique and beautiful items like pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Treasure Coast

Treasure Coast is known for its relaxed, laid-back lifestyle, with an emphasis on outdoor activities and marine life. Visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and leave no trace.

Best Time to Visit Treasure Coast

The best time to visit Treasure Coast is from November through May, when the weather is mild and dry, and the crowds are less. However, be aware that winter is popular with snowbirds, so accommodations may be harder to find.

Do’s and Don’ts of Treasure Coast

Do:

  • Respect the marine life and ecosystems of Treasure Coast.
  • Visit the local museums and cultural attractions to learn about the history of the region.
  • Indulge in the area’s delicious seafood and local craft beer.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the natural habitats of the local wildlife or leave trash behind.
  • Ignore the environmental regulations and guidelines for swimming and boating in the local waters.
  • Disrespect the locals and their customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Treasure Coast

Treasure Coast is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking their car doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Treasure Coast

Treasure Coast has excellent healthcare facilities, including several hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have appropriate travel insurance before arriving.

Getting to Treasure Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Palm Beach International Airport (PBI)
  • Orlando International Airport (MCO)
  • Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL)

Bus Stations Near Treasure Coast

  • Greyhound Station in Fort Pierce
  • Greyhound Station in Stuart

Other Options

  • Rental Cars – Several car rental companies have outlets in the area.
  • Private Shuttles – Several shuttles and car services operate between Treasure Coast and nearby airports.

How to Get Around in Treasure Coast

The best way to get around Treasure Coast is by car, as public transport is limited. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use Uber or Lyft to get around.

Where to Go Next from Treasure Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Orlando – A two-hour drive from Treasure Coast, Orlando is known for its numerous theme parks and family attractions.
  2. The Everglades – Located to the south of Treasure Coast, the Everglades offer visitors the chance to hike, bike, kayak, and spot wildlife in this unique, natural environment.
  3. Miami – A two-hour drive from Treasure Coast, Miami offers visitors a thriving arts and culture scene, stunning beaches, and delicious cuisine.

Conclusion

Treasure Coast is a unique destination that offers visitors a diverse range of experiences, from exploring the region’s historic sites to hiking and kayaking through its natural landscapes. With its delicious food and drink scene, excellent accommodation options, and welcoming locals, Treasure Coast is the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life and create unforgettable memories.
