Introduction to Merritt Island

Merritt Island, located on Florida’s Space Coast, is home to some of the most exciting outdoor activities and cultural attractions in the state. From environmental reserves to shopping to beaches and wildlife sanctuaries, Merritt Island has it all. In this travel guide, we’ll highlight the top things to do, places to stay and eat, and local culture to help you plan a trip to remember.

List of Places to Visit in Merritt Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex

Located on the east coast of Florida, the Kennedy Space Center is a must-visit spot while in Merritt Island. Here, visitors can learn about space exploration and NASA’s history through exhibits featuring genuine artifacts. You can also get up close to spacecraft, like the lunar module, and watch a real live rocket launch.

Brevard Zoo

Brevard Zoo, covering 75 acres, is home to more than 900 animals and 200 species. In addition to seeing the animals, visitors can feed, interact, and learn about their habitat. The zoo’s unique feature is its proximity to waterways- you can go kayaking or boat riding around the animal habitats and catch a glimpse of the animals up-close.

Natural Attractions of Merritt Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Canaveral National Seashore

Canaveral National Seashore is a protected area that covers 24 miles of undeveloped shoreline. Visitors can experience a natural beach that stretches as far as the eye can see. Take a swim in the ocean, go hiking, or enjoy a picnic with a view of the beautiful landscape.

Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge

Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge is a haven for endangered animals, and it’s the perfect place to appreciate nature. Here, you can see turtles nesting, manatees swimming, and a large variety of birds from eagles to herons. Visitors can access the site during specific seasons and take guided tours to get the full experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Merritt Island

The Island offers a wide variety of hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Black Point Wildlife Drive: Visitors can see manatees, gators, deer, and birds while driving through this trail from your car.
  • Scrub Ridge Trail: This trail offers a scenic view of the Space Center and the Banana River from atop a hill.
  • Maritime Hammock Sanctuary: Located near the seashore, this trail winds through tunnels of mature oak trees and palm trees.

Best Things to Do in Merritt Island: Activities and Tours

Orlando Balloon Rides

Riding across Merritt Island in a hot-air balloon will provide you with a breathtaking view of the landscape. This tour will give you a chance to see wild animals in their natural habitat and appreciate the stunning scenery.

Indian River Queen Riverboat Cruises

Riverboat cruises provide one of the best ways to get a view of Merritt Island. You can enjoy a leisurely ride along the river, watch the animals, and sample delicious cuisine prepared on-board. It’s a great way to experience the Island’s culture and get to know its people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Merritt Island

This is a sample itinerary for a one-week trip to Merritt Island.

Day 1: Arrive and check into your hotel, relax in the evening. Day 2: Head to the beach for a day of sun, surf, and sand. Day 3: Visit the Kennedy Space Center and learn about NASA’s history. Day 4: Spend a day at the Brevard Zoo and interact with animals. Day 5: Explore Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge and enjoy bird watching. Day 6: Take an Orlando Balloon Ride or Indian River Queen Riverboat Cruise. Day 7: Spend the day at Cocoa Beach or visit the nearby cities of Melbourne or Titusville.

Food and Drink Scene of Merritt Island

Must-Try Dishes

Seafood is a staple of the Island cuisine. Among must-try dishes are Florida lobster, Gator bites, and clam chowder. The place’s dynamic restaurant scene serves a wide range of cuisines from Indian to Italian.

Local Beverages

Famous for its beer craft, Cocoa Beach Brewing Company provides a great experience for beer enthusiasts. They serve a range of beers from light to dark and fruity to malty.

Where to Stay in Merritt Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several places to choose from, based on your preferences and budget, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Some hotel choices are:

  • Cape Crossing Resort and Marina
  • Parrish Grove Inn
  • Econo Lodge Port Canaveral Area

What to Buy in Merritt Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Island’s shopping scene has something for everyone, from souvenir shops to independent boutiques. Here are some popular items:

  • NASA merchandise from the Kennedy Space Center.
  • Surf gear from Ron Jon Surf Shop.
  • Premium T-shirts and other local memorabilia.

Local Culture and Customs of Merritt Island

Merritt Island’s culture is heavily influenced by space exploration, given the Island’s proximity to the Kennedy Space Center. The Island’s residents are friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Merritt Island

The best time to visit is during the late winter or early spring season when the weather is favorable. Temperatures can range from 60 to 70 degrees Fahrenheit; it’s perfect to spend the day outdoors without getting too hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Merritt Island

Do:

  • Wear comfortable clothes and sturdy shoes when exploring the Island’s wildlife reserves.
  • Take a guided tour when visiting the wildlife reserve to discover the most exciting areas.
  • Explore the Island’s cultural heritage during a tourist visit.

Don’t:

  • Do not disturb manatees or other endangered animals in their habitat, as it may harm them.
  • Don’t forget to apply sunscreen and bug spray when going outdoors.
  • Avoid swimming in unknown waters without guidance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Merritt Island

Though generally safe, tourists should exercise caution when exploring the Island’s natural destinations. Don’t get too near to wildlife and be aware of your belongings at all times.

Health in Merritt Island

The Island has an excellent medical facility for travelers. However, it’s best to carry a first-aid kit and prescription medications. Take precautions related to food and water hygiene when eating out.

Getting to Merritt Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Merritt Island

  • Melbourne International Airport: 21 minutes away
  • Orlando International Airport: 45 minutes away

Bus Stations Near Merritt Island

  • Greyhound Bus: Only available in Melbourne

Other Options

Cab services such as Uber and Lyft are also available from major airports. Visitors can rent a car from an airport to drive to Merritt Island.

How to Get Around in Merritt Island

Visitors can rent cars, bikes, or make use of cab services to explore the Island. Biking is a popular option for travelers who want to experience the Island’s beauty up close and have an adventure.

Where to Go Next from Merritt Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations that are worth a visit include:

  • Orlando: An hour’s drive away, Orlando is home to world-famous theme parks and attractions.
  • Daytona Beach: Find this destination an hour and a half away. It’s famous for its beaches and motorsports.

Conclusion

Merritt Island offers a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions. From outdoor activities to museums and historical sites, the Island has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you are well equipped to plan a safe, enjoyable, and unforgettable trip to Merritt Island.
