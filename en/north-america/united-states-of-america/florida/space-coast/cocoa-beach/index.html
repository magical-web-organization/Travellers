Introduction to Cocoa Beach

Cocoa Beach is a beautiful coastal city situated in the Brevard County of Florida, United States. It is a favorite destination for surfers, beach lovers, and space enthusiasts with its ideal climate, long stretches of sandy beaches, and proximity to the Kennedy Space Center. Cocoa Beach is famed for its attractive oceanfront pier, excellent fishing, and surfing opportunities. In this guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, and the culture and customs of Cocoa Beach, so you can experience it to the fullest extent.

List of Places to Visit in Cocoa Beach

Cocoa Beach has plenty of amazing attractions for tourists. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kennedy Space Center: Located only 19 miles from Cocoa Beach, the Kennedy Space Center is a must-visit place for space enthusiasts. There are several exhibits and tours, including viewing a shuttle launch pad and space memorabilia.

  • Cocoa Beach Pier: The Cocoa Beach Pier dates back to 1962 when it was first built, and to this day, remains a cultural icon of the city. Go fishing, grab a drink at the bar, or enjoy a meal at one of many restaurants located on the pier.

  • The Dinosaur Store and Museum: This unique museum features a wide assortment of exhibits, including fossils, prehistoric replicas, and educational materials for young children.

Natural Attractions of Cocoa Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lori Wilson Park: This 32-acre park features a large beachfront, playgrounds, pavilions for picnics, and nature trails.

  • Canaveral National Seashore: This natural gem is located just a 20-minute drive from Cocoa Beach and comprises 24 miles of undeveloped beach, dunes, and marshes. There are plenty of opportunities for wildlife watching, fishing, bird watching, and other outdoor activities.

  • The Thousand Islands: This beautiful cluster of islands can be viewed while kayaking or paddle-boarding through the Banana River. These green islets are home to an abundance of wildlife, including manatees, ospreys, and pelicans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cocoa Beach

Cocoa Beach has an array of great hiking and biking routes. Here are some that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Cocoa Beach Skate Park: This park features over 10,000 square feet of obstacles, including ramps, rails, and half-pipes.

  • Banana River Park Trail: This 9-mile paved trail is perfect for cycling. The trail has several scenic spots to stop, rest and take in the beautiful landscape.

  • Lori Wilson Park Trail: Lori Wilson Park has hiking trails alongside the Banana River and Atlantic Ocean. The trail is ideal for keen walkers and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Cocoa Beach: Activities and Tours

There are numerous activities and tours you can enjoy in Cocoa Beach; here are some of the best:

  • Surfing: Cocoa Beach is famous for its surfing waves. Rent a board from a nearby surf shop or take a lesson from a professional instructor.

  • Kayaking: Explore the Indian River Lagoon or the Thousand Islands by renting a kayak or joining a guided tour.

  • Tandem Skydiving: Embrace your adventurous side with a tandem skydiving experience in Cocoa Beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cocoa Beach

A week-long itinerary can help fully appreciate all the beauty and adventure that Cocoa Beach has to offer. Here’s a sample:

Day Activities
Day 1 Go surfing, visit the Beach Boys Memorial, and have dinner at Coconuts on the Beach
Day 2 Take a guided kayak tour, visit the Dinosaur Store, and grab lunch at Fat Kahuna’s
Day 3 Go on a fishing charter, visit the Space Coast Discovery Center, and have dinner at The Fat Snook
Day 4 Visit Cocoa Beach Pier, take a stroll to Lori Wilson Park, and have lunch at the Pier
Day 5 Go scuba diving or see the local wildlife onboard a Manatee or dolphin-watching tour and have dinner at Grills Riverside
Day 6 Visit the Kennedy Space Center, have lunch at Sunrise Diner, and take a tour of Brevard Zoo
Day 7 Snorkel along Cocoa Beach or visit the iconic Ron Jon Surf Shop and have some local brews at Florida Beer Company

Food and Drink Scene of Cocoa Beach

Cocoa Beach’s food and drink scene features a diverse mix of cuisines, from seafood to international. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Crawfish, mussels, clams, crab, and shrimp are among the abundant seafood options you’ll find in Cocoa Beach. Make sure you try fresh seafood served in restaurants such as Fishlips Waterfront Bar & Grill and Rusty’s Seafood & Oyster Bar.

  • Cuban Sandwiches: Experience a delicious blend of pork, ham, Swiss cheese, mustard, and pickles with this delicious sandwich originally invented in Cuba.

  • Key Lime Pie: This vibrant and tangy pie is made with delicious, locally grown key limes.

Local Beverages

  • Rum Runners Cocktail: A local favorite, this rum-based cocktail is made with pineapple juice, orange juice, cranberry juice, and banana liquor.

  • Florida Beer Company: This craft brewery located in Cape Canaveral produces a variety of beers, including Comet and Evil Twin Brewing.

Where to Stay in Cocoa Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cocoa Beach has several accommodation options to suit every visitor’s needs, preferences, and their budget. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Westgate Cocoa Beach Resort: This contemporary resort is located directly across from Cocoa Beach Pier.

  • International Palms Resort & Conference Center Cocoa Beach: This resort features its beach access, several swimming pools, and a water park.

  • Cocoa Beach Suites: This budget-friendly option offers pleasant accommodation at a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Cocoa Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping, Cocoa Beach has a little bit of everything. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products that you can bring back home:

  • Beachwear: There is plenty of beachwear available in Cocoa Beach, such as swimsuits, flip-flops, and sun hats.

  • Surfboards: There are plenty of surf shops in Cocoa Beach that sell surfboards.

  • Sea Shells and Art: From sea-themed art prints to handmade sea shell ornaments, there are numerous arts and crafts stores that sell all sorts of beach-related items.

Local Culture and Customs of Cocoa Beach

Cocoa Beach is famous for being a laid-back, friendly, and welcoming beach town. The official language spoken is English and the currency is the US dollar. Remember to tip your servers and other service providers as it is customary and expected in the US. It’s important to remember that loud music and parties are not allowed on the beach between 10 pm and 7 am.

Best Time to Visit Cocoa Beach

The best time to visit Cocoa Beach is between the months of February and April. This is when the weather is mild, and prices are cheaper. The peak season is from June to August, which is when the crowds are at their busiest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cocoa Beach

Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow while in Cocoa Beach:

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen, even on cloudy days.
  • Observe caution flags; if the sea is rough, do not swim.
  • Respect the local wildlife, including dolphins and manatees.
  • Try authentic seafood dishes.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or on the streets.
  • Leave your belongings unattended.
  • Swim near piers or jetties.
  • Smoke in public areas.

Safety and Health

Cocoa Beach is a safe and healthy destination in the US, although it is always important to take necessary precautions. Here are some tips:

Safety in Cocoa Beach

  • Lock your car and hotel rooms.
  • Avoid walking alone in desolate areas.
  • Don’t flash expensive possessions and jewelry.
  • Observe the safety flags and warnings on the beach.

Health in Cocoa Beach

  • The intense sun can cause sunburn, always use sunscreen.
  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • The ocean water can have strong currents; follow the safety flags guidelines.

Getting to Cocoa Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cocoa Beach is easily accessible by different modes of transport. Here is a rundown on how to get there:

Airports Near Cocoa Beach

Orlando International Airport (MCO) is located approximately 70 miles away from Cocoa Beach. Other options include Melbourne International Airport (MLB) and Orlando Sanford International Airport (SFB).

Bus Stations Near Cocoa Beach

Greyhound Bus Lines has a bus station located in nearby Melbourne (approx. 20 miles away). From there, visitors can take public transportation to Cocoa Beach.

Other Options

Rental cars are available at all airports, and taxi and private car services such as Uber and Lyft are also an excellent option.

How to Get Around in Cocoa Beach

Cocoa Beach offers several ways to explore the area:

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies are available in the city, including Budget, Enterprise, and Hertz.

  • Public Transportation: Brevard County Public Transportation operates numerous bus routes throughout Cocoa Beach and Brevard County.

  • Bicycle Rentals: Several locations in Cocoa Beach rent bicycles.

Where to Go Next from Cocoa Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of exciting destinations and excursions in the vicinity of Cocoa Beach, including:

  • Melbourne Beach: This beach town offers fabulous art galleries, unique shops, and beautiful seaside views.

  • Orlando: Visit world-class attractions such as Disney World, Universal Studios, and SeaWorld.

  • Canaveral National Seashore: This biodiverse national seashore provides visitors with miles of undeveloped beach and wildlife watching opportunities.

Conclusion

Cocoa Beach is an alluring and attractive coastal city in Florida. With its remarkable beaches, outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and friendly locals, there is no doubt that it is an excellent destination for a relaxing and adventurous vacation. We hope this guide has helped you discover the best of Cocoa Beach and that your visit will be a memorable one.
