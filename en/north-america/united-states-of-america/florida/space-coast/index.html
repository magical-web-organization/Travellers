Introduction to Space Coast

Located in Florida, Space Coast is a destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and man-made marvels. With miles of sparkling coastline, historic landmarks, and world-class theme parks, Space Coast is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Florida.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a virtual tour of Space Coast and give you all the information you need to plan your next trip.

List of Places to Visit in Space Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

History buffs and museum-goers will be delighted by the many culturally important sites in Space Coast. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex: This iconic attraction gives visitors a behind-the-scenes look at NASA’s Space Program. Visitors can tour the launch pads, check out the rockets and shuttles, and even meet an astronaut.
  • Brevard Zoo: Home to over 900 animals from around the world, Brevard Zoo is a must-visit for families with kids of all ages. Highlights include the Treetop Trek, Animal Encounters, and the giraffe feeding station.
  • Historic Cocoa Village: This charming downtown area is home to boutique shops, art galleries, and plenty of eateries. Be sure to visit the Historic Cocoa Village Playhouse, which has been entertaining audiences since 1924.
  • Valiant Air Command Warbird Museum: This museum features an impressive collection of historic military aircraft, including a Russian MIG Fighter and a WWII P-51 Mustang.

Natural Attractions of Space Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Space Coast is nothing short of breathtaking. Here are some of the outdoor attractions that are worth a visit:

  • Canaveral National Seashore: With miles of unspoiled beaches, this is the perfect place to soak up the sun and relax. Visitors can also enjoy fishing, kayaking, and camping.
  • Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge: This 140,000-acre wildlife reserve is home to a stunning variety of birds, sea turtles, and other wildlife. Visitors can hike, bike, or kayak through the refuge.
  • Jetty Park: This popular park offers amazing views of Port Canaveral and the cruise ships that pass through. Visitors can also enjoy fishing, kayaking, and camping.
  • Sebastian Inlet State Park: This park is a surfer’s paradise, with some of the best waves in Florida. Visitors can also fish, swim, and hike along the many trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Space Coast

One of the best ways to explore Space Coast is on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • The Space Coast Trail: This 36-mile trail runs from Titusville to Viera and offers stunning views of the natural beauty of Space Coast.
  • Turkey Creek Sanctuary: This 130-acre nature reserve offers several hiking trails that take visitors through a variety of ecosystems, including hardwood hammocks and mangrove swamps.
  • Wickham Park: This popular park has over 8 miles of trails that are perfect for hiking or mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Space Coast: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of exciting things to do in Space Coast. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Airboat Rides: Experience the thrill of gliding across the water on an airboat tour of the St. John’s River and other marshy areas.
  • Surf Lessons: Learn to surf like a pro on one of Space Coast’s many beaches.
  • Kayak Tours: Take a guided kayak tour of the Indian River Lagoon, home to dolphins, manatees, and a variety of birds.
  • Fishing Charters: Catch your own dinner on a deep-sea fishing charter, or try your hand at inshore fishing in the Indian River Lagoon.

Food and Drink Scene of Space Coast

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Space Coast would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some dishes that you won’t want to miss:

  • Gator Tail: This local delicacy is exactly what it sounds like - deep-fried alligator tail. It tastes like a mixture of chicken and fish, and is often served with a spicy dipping sauce.
  • Fresh Seafood: Space Coast is home to some of the freshest seafood you will ever taste. Be sure to try the grilled fish tacos and the seafood chowder.
  • Key Lime Pie: This tangy dessert is a Florida classic, and Space Coast is no exception. Be sure to try the homemade version at one of the local restaurants.

Local Beverages

In addition to great food, Space Coast also has a thriving craft beer scene. Some local breweries to check out include:

  • Playalinda Brewing Company: This brewery has a taproom in downtown Titusville and another location at the Cape Canaveral Spaceport.
  • Bugnutty Brewing Company: Located in Merritt Island, this brewery has a cozy tasting room that serves up a variety of creative brews.
  • Intracoastal Brewing Company: This brewery in Melbourne has a laid-back vibe and a pet-friendly patio.

Where to Stay in Space Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Space Coast has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hilton Cocoa Beach Oceanfront: This beachfront hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a pool and on-site dining options.
  • Discovery Beach Resort: Located in charming Cocoa Beach, this resort has private balconies with ocean views, as well as a pool and tennis courts.
  • Parrish Grove Inn: This lovely bed and breakfast in downtown Titusville offers beautifully decorated rooms and a scrumptious breakfast.
  • Melbourne All Suites Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, as well as a pool and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Space Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring home from your trip to Space Coast, here are some ideas:

  • Kennedy Space Center Merchandise: The Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex has a wide variety of souvenir options, including t-shirts, hats, and space-themed toys.
  • Surf Shop Gear: Space Coast has some great surf shops that sell everything from surfboards to beach towels to flip-flops.
  • Citrus Products: Florida is known for its citrus, and you can find a variety of fresh oranges, grapefruits, and other citrus products at the local farmer’s markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Space Coast

Space Coast has a laid-back, friendly culture that is reflective of its coastal location. When visiting the area, it’s important to respect the local customs and etiquette. Here are some tips:

  • Dress Appropriately: Florida is known for its warm and humid weather, so be sure to dress comfortably and wear sunscreen.
  • Be Polite: Southern hospitality is alive and well in Space Coast, so be sure to say “please” and “thank you” and be courteous to others.
  • Mind Your Manners: Space Coast is a family-friendly destination, so be mindful of your language and behavior in public places.

Best Time to Visit Space Coast

The best time to visit Space Coast is during the winter months, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. However, this is also the busiest time of year, so be sure to book your accommodations well in advance. If you prefer warmer weather and don’t mind the crowds, the summer months are also a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Space Coast

Do:

  • Visit the Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex
  • Take an airboat tour of the St. John’s River
  • Try the Gator Tail

Don’t:

  • Ignore beach safety warnings
  • Leave valuables in your car
  • Take shells or sand from the beach

Safety and Health

Safety in Space Coast

Space Coast is generally a safe destination. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions to avoid becoming victims of crime. Some tips include:

  • Lock your car doors and don’t leave valuables in plain sight
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night

Health in Space Coast

While there are no major health risks in Space Coast, visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from the sun and insects. It’s also important to stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.

Getting to Space Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Space Coast

The closest major airport to Space Coast is Orlando International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away. The Melbourne International Airport also offers flights to some major US destinations.

Bus Stations Near Space Coast

The Greyhound bus station in Cocoa is the closest bus station to Space Coast.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best way to get around Space Coast and see everything that the area has to offer. Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Space Coast

Getting around Space Coast is relatively easy, especially if you have a car. Some other options include:

  • Public Transportation: Space Coast Area Transit offers bus service throughout the area.
  • Bicycle Rentals: Some hotels and rental shops offer bicycles for rent, which is a great way to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Space Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Florida after visiting Space Coast, here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Orlando: Visit one of the many theme parks, such as Disney World or Universal Studios.
  • St. Augustine: This charming historic town is the oldest city in the US and offers plenty of museums, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Tampa: This vibrant city has a variety of attractions, including the Florida Aquarium, Busch Gardens, and the Tampa Riverwalk.

Conclusion

Space Coast is a destination that truly has something for everyone, from the beautiful beaches and natural attractions to the historic landmarks and world-class theme parks. With this comprehensive travel guide, you should have all the information you need to plan your next trip to Space Coast and make the most of your time in this amazing destination.
