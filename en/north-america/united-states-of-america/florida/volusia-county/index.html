Introduction to Volusia County

Located in Central Florida, Volusia County is a picturesque destination known for its stunning beaches, rich history, and endless outdoor adventures. From Daytona Beach to DeLand, this county offers visitors a wide range of places to visit, activities to enjoy, and cultural experiences to savor. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you plan your visit to Volusia County from start to finish.

List of Places to Visit in Volusia County

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day by the beach or a thrilling adventure in the outdoors, Volusia County has something to offer. Here are our top recommendations for places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Halifax Historical Museum: Learn about the history of Daytona Beach and Volusia County at this museum, which features exhibits on local culture, art, and lifestyle.
  • Ponce de Leon Inlet Lighthouse & Museum: Climb to the top of Florida’s tallest lighthouse and explore the history of the lighthouse and the people who maintained it.

Natural Attractions of Volusia County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canaveral National Seashore: This beautiful park offers opportunities for hiking, biking, swimming, and birdwatching, as well as stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and Indian River Lagoon.
  • Blue Spring State Park: Known for its crystal-clear waters and natural springs, this park offers visitors the chance to kayak, fish, and swim while enjoying the natural beauty of Central Florida.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Volusia County

If you’re an adventure-seeker looking for an outdoor challenge during your visit to Volusia County, hiking and biking are great options. Here are our top picks for trails and routes:

  • Spring to Spring Trail: This 26.2-mile trail takes you from Gemini Springs Park in DeBary to Blue Spring State Park in Orange City, offering incredible views along the way.
  • Daytona Beach Boardwalk Trail: This paved trail stretches for 23 miles along the beach, offering amazing views of the ocean and plenty of opportunities to stop for a quick dip in the water.

Best Things to Do in Volusia County: Activities and Tours

From exploring local wildlife to enjoying family-friendly attractions, there’s no shortage of fun things to do in Volusia County. Here are some of our favorite activities and tours:

  • Daytona International Speedway Tour: Take a behind-the-scenes tour of this famous racetrack and learn about the history of NASCAR and its role in Daytona Beach.
  • Alligator Farm Zoological Park: Home to over 200 alligators and crocodiles, this park also features exotic birds, primates, and other reptiles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Volusia County

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Volusia County, you’ll have plenty of time to explore all the area has to offer. Here’s our recommended itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Ponce de Leon Inlet Lighthouse & Museum and spend the afternoon at the beach. Day 2: Explore Canaveral National Seashore and enjoy a sunset hike. Day 3: Take a tour of Daytona International Speedway and visit the Daytona Beach Boardwalk and Pier. Day 4: Discover local wildlife at the Marine Science Center and the Alligator Farm Zoological Park. Day 5: Cycle or hike the Spring to Spring Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch at Blue Spring State Park. Day 6: Go shopping and enjoy local restaurants in downtown DeLand. Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Volusia County’s beautiful beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Volusia County

Florida’s culinary scene is heavily influenced by its location and diverse cultural heritage. In Volusia County, you’ll find a mix of seafood, southern cuisine, and international flavors. Here are some of our top picks for must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Key Lime Pie: A tangy, sweet dessert made with Florida’s famous key limes.
  • Gumbo: A seafood-based soup with Cajun flavors and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is known for its delicious fresh-squeezed orange juice, which can be found at most restaurants and cafes in the area.
  • Cuban Coffee: A strong, sweet coffee made with espresso and sweetened condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Volusia County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Volusia County offers visitors a wide range of accommodations, from beachfront hotels to quaint bed and breakfasts. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Shores Resort & Spa in Daytona Beach: This luxurious beachfront hotel offers stunning views of the ocean and top-notch amenities.
  • DeLand Artisan Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in downtown DeLand and offers guests comfort and convenience.

What to Buy in Volusia County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Volusia County, here are some suggestions:

  • Local Art: Visit galleries such as Arts on Granada in Ormond Beach or The Hub on Canal in New Smyrna Beach to purchase locally made art.
  • Citrus: Pick up local citrus fruits such as oranges, grapefruits, or tangerines to enjoy a taste of Florida at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Volusia County

Volusia County and Florida have a rich culture and history influenced by its location, climate, and diverse population. Visitors to the area should be aware of some customs and traditions practiced by locals, such as:

  • Respect for local wildlife and natural habitats: Florida is home to many endangered species, and visitors should take care to avoid disturbing their habitats.
  • Politeness and hospitality: Many Floridians take pride in welcoming visitors to their state and are willing to offer assistance and recommendations.

Best Time to Visit Volusia County

Florida’s subtropical climate makes it a popular year-round destination, but visitors should be aware of peak tourist season and plan accordingly. The best time to visit Volusia County is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when temperatures are mild and crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volusia County

To make the most of your visit to Volusia County, here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect local wildlife and natural habitats.
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Take advantage of local hospitality and ask for recommendations if you’re unsure where to go or what to do.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Venture into restricted areas or private property without permission.
  • Engage in dangerous or reckless behavior.

Safety and Health

Ensuring your safety and health during your visit to Volusia County is crucial. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Volusia County

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables on you.
  • Be careful when swimming in the ocean and follow lifeguard instructions.
  • Use caution when driving and observe local traffic laws and speed limits.

Health in Volusia County

  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid prolonged exposure to the sun to prevent heat exhaustion or stroke.
  • Pack any necessary prescription medications or essential medical supplies.

Getting to Volusia County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Volusia County is easily accessible from major cities in Florida and beyond. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

  • Airports Near Volusia County: The closest airport to Volusia County is the Daytona Beach International Airport, but Orlando International Airport is also within driving distance.
  • Bus Stations Near Volusia County: Greyhound Lines operates a bus station in Daytona Beach, with routes connecting to other cities in Florida and beyond.
  • Other Options: Rental cars, taxi services, and ride-sharing apps are also available for transportation within and around Volusia County.

How to Get Around in Volusia County

To explore everything Volusia County has to offer, here are some transportation options:

  • Car Rental: Renting a car gives you the flexibility to explore the county at your own pace and visit destinations off the beaten path.
  • Public Transportation: Votran operates bus and trolley services throughout Volusia County.
  • Biking: Many of the parks and trails in Volusia County offer opportunities for scenic cycling.

Where to Go Next from Volusia County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your vacation beyond Volusia County, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • St. Augustine: Located about one hour north of Volusia County, St. Augustine is known for its historic architecture and charming downtown.
  • Kennedy Space Center: About an hour’s drive south of Volusia County, the Kennedy Space Center offers tours of NASA facilities and exhibits on space exploration.

Conclusion

Volusia County offers something for every kind of traveler, whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a relaxing beach vacation. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan a memorable trip to this beautiful region of Florida.
