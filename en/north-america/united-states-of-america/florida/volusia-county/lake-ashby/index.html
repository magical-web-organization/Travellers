Introduction to Lake Ashby

Looking for a hidden gem to explore in Florida? Look no further than Lake Ashby, a picturesque body of water located in Volusia County. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing vacation, Lake Ashby has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Ashby

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eldridge’s Old Sugar Mill: Explore the history of sugar manufacturing in Florida at this historic site that dates back to the 1800s.
  • Sugar Mill Ruins: Discover the ruins of a 19th-century sugar mill and learn about its important role in Florida’s economy and history.
  • Ponce de Leon Inlet Lighthouse: Climb to the top of this lighthouse for breathtaking views of the surrounding area and learn about its rich history as a navigational beacon.

Natural Attractions of Lake Ashby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Ashby Park: This park provides a perfect spot for picnicking, swimming, and fishing. Enjoy the serene views of Lake Ashby and take advantage of the nearby picnic tables and grills for a fun day out.
  • Paddleboarding/Kayaking: Rent a paddleboard or kayak and explore the tranquil waters of Lake Ashby. It’s a great opportunity to spot some of the local wildlife, including alligators, turtles, and birds.
  • Wetlands Walkway: Nature lovers will enjoy the Wetlands Walkway trail, which takes you through a fascinating ecosystem of cypress trees, tupelo, and oak hammocks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Ashby

  • Lake Ashby Trails: The Lake Ashby Trail System comprises four trails that range from one to three miles in length. They are suitable for both hiking and biking and offer stunning views of the lake and surrounding forest.
  • East Central Regional Rail Trail: This trail stretches for over 30 miles, connecting several towns across Volusia County. It’s an easy ride for beginners but there are also several stops where you can explore local attractions.

Best Things to Do in Lake Ashby: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Boat Tour: Take a guided boat tour of the St. Johns River and spot some of Florida’s famous wildlife, including manatees, ospreys, and alligators.
  • Airboat Tour: For a thrilling adventure, take an airboat tour of Lake Ashby and surrounding wetlands. You’ll get up close and personal with some of the local wildlife and enjoy an adrenaline-filled ride.
  • Golfing: Golf enthusiasts will appreciate Victoria Hills Golf Club, a 18-hole championship course designed by renowned architect Ron Garl.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Ashby

Day 1: Arrive and relax at Lake Ashby Park Day 2: Visit Eldridge’s Old Sugar Mill and Sugar Mill Ruins Day 3: Take a boat tour of the St. Johns River Day 4: Hike or bike along the Lake Ashby Trails Day 5: Explore Ponce de Leon Inlet Lighthouse Day 6: Rent a kayak/paddleboard and explore the lake Day 7: End the week with a thrilling airboat tour

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Ashby

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gator Tail: No visit to Florida is complete without trying this iconic dish. Gator tail is a popular delicacy that tastes similar to chicken and can be found in many local restaurants.
  • Key Lime Pie: Indulge in a slice of this tart yet sweet dessert, made with fresh key limes and a graham cracker crust.
  • Seafood: Florida’s abundance of seafood means there are plenty of delicious options to choose from. Try some fresh shrimp, crab, or fish at any of the local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Florida is known for its citrus, and the orange juice here is some of the best in the country.
  • Beer: Florida has a booming craft beer scene, with local breweries such as Tomoka Brewing Company and Persimmon Hollow Brewing Company offering unique and flavorful options.
  • Cocktails: Try a refreshing mojito made with fresh mint and lime or a spicy bloody mary made with locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Lake Ashby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Debary/Deltona: This hotel is conveniently located near many of Lake Ashby’s attractions, including the St. Johns River and Victoria Hills Golf Club.
  • Artisan Downtown: This charming bed and breakfast is located in nearby DeLand and offers a unique experience full of local flavor and personal touch.
  • Cassadaga Hotel: This historic hotel is located near the Cassadaga Spiritualist Camp and offers an intriguing glimpse into the area’s supernatural side.

What to Buy in Lake Ashby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Orange Blossom Honey: This sweet and fragrant honey is produced from the nectar of orange blossoms and is a delicious reminder of your trip to Lake Ashby.
  • Citrus: Take home some fresh Florida citrus, including oranges, grapefruits, and key limes, and add some sunshine to your diet.
  • Handmade Crafts: Local artisans offer a variety of handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and paintings, that make for unique and memorable souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Ashby

Florida is known for its laid-back, easy-going culture, and Lake Ashby is no exception. Visitors should be courteous and respectful to locals and take time to appreciate the natural surroundings and history of the area.

Best Time to Visit Lake Ashby

The best time to visit Lake Ashby is during the fall and winter months when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. However, Florida’s warm weather means that Lake Ashby is enjoyable year-round, so visitors can choose the time that suits them best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Ashby

Do:

  • Do bring sunscreen, bug spray, and other outdoor essentials.
  • Do respect the local wildlife and refrain from feeding or approaching them.
  • Do take time to learn about the history and culture of the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t pick or disturb any plants or animals in Lake Ashby.
  • Don’t litter or leave any trash behind.
  • Don’t forget to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated in the Florida heat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Ashby

While Lake Ashby is generally safe for visitors, it’s important to take precautions when exploring the outdoors. Visitors should be aware of the local wildlife, including alligators, and know how to react if they encounter them. It’s also important to stay hydrated and wear proper clothing and sun protection to avoid heat-related illnesses.

Health in Lake Ashby

Visitors should also be aware of the potential health risks associated with Florida’s warm climate. Mosquito-borne illnesses, such as West Nile virus and dengue fever, are a concern during peak mosquito season. It’s important to use mosquito repellent and cover exposed skin to minimize the risk of these diseases.

Getting to Lake Ashby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Ashby

  • Daytona Beach International Airport: Located approximately 20 miles east of Lake Ashby, this airport serves several major airlines and offers daily flights to many major cities.
  • Orlando International Airport: Located approximately 50 miles southwest of Lake Ashby, this airport is a major hub for many national and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Lake Ashby

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in DeLand, approximately 6 miles west of Lake Ashby.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Lake Ashby and is recommended for those who want to explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Lake Ashby

The best way to get around Lake Ashby is by car. Some of the attractions and parks are spread out and a car will provide the most flexibility and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Lake Ashby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • DeLand: This charming town is known for its historic architecture, quaint shops, and restaurants.
  • Daytona Beach: Head east to enjoy the sun, surf, and sand of this popular Florida destination.
  • St. Augustine: Located approximately 50 miles north of Lake Ashby, St. Augustine is the oldest continuously occupied European settlement in the United States and home to many historic attractions and museums.

Conclusion

Lake Ashby is a hidden gem in Florida, offering visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty, rich history, and diverse culture of the area. Whether you prefer outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply relaxing by the beach, Lake Ashby has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this special place has to offer!
