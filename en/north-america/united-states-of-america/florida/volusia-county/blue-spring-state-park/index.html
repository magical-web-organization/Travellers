Introduction to Blue Spring State Park

Located in the heart of Central Florida, Blue Spring State Park is a natural wonder that attracts millions of visitors each year. This 2,600-acre state park is situated in Volusia County, about 30 miles north of Orlando, and is home to the largest spring on the St. Johns River.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Spring State Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Blue Spring State Park boasts a rich cultural history that dates back thousands of years. Visitors interested in learning more about the park’s past can explore:

  • Thursby House: This historic house was built in the early 1900s and now serves as the park’s museum. It features exhibits about the history and wildlife of the St. Johns River.
  • Turtle Mound: This prehistoric site is made up of oyster shells and was used by Native Americans for thousands of years.

Natural Attractions of Blue Spring State Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Blue Spring State Park is known for its crystal-clear waters and lush vegetation. Spend a day outdoors exploring:

  • Blue Spring: The park’s main attraction is the “blue hole,” or spring boil, which is located at the headwaters of the St. Johns River. Visitors can swim in the spring, go tubing on the river, canoe/kayak, and scuba dive.
  • Hontoon Island State Park: This park, accessible by boat, offers great hiking trails and picnic areas. You can also visit the historic homes of early settlers or rent a cabin.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Spring State Park

Blue Spring State Park has several trails that offer scenic views and an abundance of wildlife. Some of the most popular include:

  • Pine Island Trail: This 1.5-mile loop takes you through a pine forest and offers great views of the St. Johns River.
  • Wild Persimmon Trail: This 3-mile long trail runs through a hardwood forest and is ideal for hiking and wildlife viewing.
  • Lake Beresford Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is great for hiking and biking and provides access to Lake Beresford, a great spot for fishing.

Best Things to Do in Blue Spring State Park: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day out in nature or an action-packed adventure, Blue Spring State Park has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Swimming: The clear, fresh water of Blue Spring is perfect for swimming. The water is about 72 F year-round and is ideal for a refreshing dip on a hot day.
  • Tubing: For a true Florida experience, you can float down the St. Johns River on a tube. The park offers rentals.
  • Scuba Diving: Experienced divers can explore the underwater cave system at the park.
  • Manatee Tour: Take a guided boat tour to see the gentle giants that make their home in Blue Spring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blue Spring State Park

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Blue Spring State Park, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the historic Thursby House and take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of the spring.
  • Day 2: Explore the park’s hiking trails and picnic areas.
  • Day 3: Rent a canoe or kayak and paddle down the St. Johns River.
  • Day 4: Take a manatee tour and learn about these gentle giants.
  • Day 5: Spend the day at nearby Hontoon Island State Park.
  • Day 6: Rent a pontoon boat and go fishing on Lake Beresford.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the park before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Spring State Park

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Blue Spring State Park is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Gator Tail: A Florida delicacy, gator tail is typically served fried and tastes like a cross between chicken and fish.
  • Fried Catfish: Caught fresh from the St. Johns River, catfish is a popular dish in many local eateries.
  • Key Lime Pie: A traditional Florida dessert, key lime pie is sweet, tangy, and delicious.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Florida is known for its citrus. Some must-try beverages include:

  • Orange Juice: Florida is famous for its oranges, and the juice is a staple at most local restaurants and cafes.
  • Grapefruit Juice: Another popular citrus fruit in Florida, grapefruit is also commonly used to make juice and cocktails.
  • Paloma Cocktail: Made with grapefruit juice, tequila, and lime, the Paloma is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Blue Spring State Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re planning a visit to Blue Spring State Park, you have several options for lodging. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hontoon Landing Resort & Marina: This resort is located on the banks of the St. Johns River and offers cabins, RV sites, and tent camping.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Orange City: This hotel is located just a few miles from the park and is ideal for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area.
  • Blue Spring State Park Campground: If you’re looking for a more rustic experience, you can camp in the park’s campground.

What to Buy in Blue Spring State Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take home from your visit to Blue Spring State Park, here are a few ideas:

  • Manatee-themed gifts: From t-shirts to coffee mugs, you can find a variety of gifts with manatee designs.
  • Florida Citrus: Take home some of the state’s famous citrus fruits, such as oranges or grapefruits.
  • Postcards: Send a postcard to friends and family and share your experience at Blue Spring State Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Spring State Park

Like many parts of Florida, Blue Spring State Park has a unique culture and set of customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Water Safety: Keep in mind that the water in Blue Spring State Park can be dangerous, especially in areas of fast-moving current. Always obey posted signs and lifeguard instructions.
  • Respect Wildlife: The park is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including manatees, alligators, and snakes. Respect their space and do not approach or feed them.
  • Tropical Weather: Florida’s warm, tropical weather can be unpredictable. Make sure to bring sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water to stay hydrated in the heat.

Best Time to Visit Blue Spring State Park

The best time to visit Blue Spring State Park is during the fall and winter months, when the weather is cooler and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Spring State Park

Do:

  • Respect other visitors by keeping noise levels down, especially in campgrounds.
  • Respect park rules and regulations.
  • Use designated hiking trails and stay on designated paths.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute.
  • Approach or feed wildlife.
  • Swim in areas designated as off-limits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blue Spring State Park

Visitors to Blue Spring State Park should be aware of potential safety hazards, which can include:

  • Strong currents in the river or spring.
  • Slippery rocks or uneven ground on hiking trails.
  • Burns from sun exposure or hot sand on sunny days.

Health in Blue Spring State Park

Blue Spring State Park is not currently known to have any significant health hazards. However, it’s always a good idea to bring insect repellent and stay hydrated to avoid heat exhaustion or dehydration.

Getting to Blue Spring State Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blue Spring State Park

  • Orlando International Airport: This airport is about 50 miles from Blue Spring State Park. Rental cars and transportation services are available.

Bus Stations Near Blue Spring State Park

  • DeBary SunRail Station: This station is about 7 miles from Blue Spring State Park and offers local bus service.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Many visitors to Blue Spring State Park choose to rent a car for transportation around the area.

How to Get Around in Blue Spring State Park

The best way to get around Blue Spring State Park is by walking or biking. The park’s hiking trails and pathways are well-marked and provide easy access to the various attractions and amenities.

Where to Go Next from Blue Spring State Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whether you’re looking for more outdoor adventure or cultural experiences, there’s plenty to see and do in the surrounding area. Here are a few nearby destinations worth checking out:

  • Daytona Beach: Home to the famous Daytona International Speedway and miles of pristine beaches, Daytona Beach is less than an hour’s drive from Blue Spring State Park.
  • St. Augustine: Known as the “oldest city in America,” St. Augustine offers a fascinating glimpse into Florida’s colonial past.
  • Kennedy Space Center: Located about an hour south of Blue Spring State Park, the Kennedy Space Center is a must-visit for anyone interested in space exploration.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for excitement or relaxation, Blue Spring State Park has something to offer everyone. From swimming in the crystal-clear waters of Blue Spring to exploring the park’s scenic hiking trails, there’s plenty to see and do. Be sure to respect the park’s rules and regulations, and enjoy your time exploring all that this natural wonder has to offer.
