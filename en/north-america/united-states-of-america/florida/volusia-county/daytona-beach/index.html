Introduction to Daytona Beach

Located in Volusia County, Florida, Daytona Beach is a premier destination that offers a variety of attractions, activities, and events for visitors of all ages. Known for its gorgeous coastline, warm weather, and vibrant nightlife, Daytona Beach has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Daytona Beach

When visiting Daytona Beach, there are plenty of places to add to your itinerary. Here are some of the top culturally important attractions, natural attractions, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, and where to stay and eat in Daytona Beach:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Daytona International Speedway - A must-visit attraction for any car racing enthusiast. Take a tour of the speedway and learn about its rich history as well as its modern-day use.
  • Museum of Arts and Sciences - Explore the museum’s impressive collection of art, science, and history exhibits. Don’t miss the planetarium and the live animal habitat!
  • Mary McLeod Bethune Foundation Home and Gravesite - A historical home that represents the life and achievements of educator and civil rights activist Mary McLeod Bethune.

Natural Attractions of Daytona Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beach at Daytona Beach - Observe the gorgeous sunrise or sunset, walk on the sand and collect shells, or take a dip in the ocean. Don’t forget to bring your sunscreen!
  • Ponce Inlet Lighthouse and Museum - Climb up the 203 steps of the historical lighthouse to get an unforgettable panoramic view of the Halifax River, Atlantic Ocean, and more.
  • Tomoka State Park - Go kayaking on the Tomoka River, hike the scenic trails, or enjoy a picnic and bird-watching in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Daytona Beach

  • Halifax River Trail - Follow the 11-mile scenic trail that runs along the Halifax River, which offers bird-watching, fishing, bicycling.
  • Ormond Scenic Loop & Trail - A 30-mile trail where visitors can bike or drive the path through beautiful beaches, parks, historic sites, and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Daytona Beach: Activities and Tours

  • Jet and Boat Ski Rentals - Choose from a variety of watercrafts including jet skis and boats to explore the waters of Daytona Beach.
  • Deep-Sea Fishing Charters - Take a boat out into the ocean and try to catch some fish, like Mahi-Mahi or Sailfish with a professional guide.
  • Airboat Tours - Go on an adrenaline-pumping airboat tour and observe the natural beauty and wildlife of the nearby marshlands, and see alligators up close!

Week-long Itinerary for Daytona Beach

Day 1-2 - Visit Daytona Beach’s Historical Museums Day 3-4 - Explore Scenic Natural Attractions and Take part in beach activities Day 5-6 - Enjoy thrilling activities such as water sports, tours, and outdoor adventures Day 7 - Relax on the beach, or visit a local festival or event.

Food and Drink Scene of Daytona Beach

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes - Get a taste of the beach with delicious crab cakes offered throughout Daytona Beach.
  • Shrimp and Grits - If you’re a seafood lover, try some fresh shrimp with creamy grits for a mouthwatering southern meal.
  • Key Lime Pie - Daytona Beach’s smooth, tangy pie is the perfect dessert to complete your culinary experience.

Local Beverages

  • Daytona Beach Brewing Company - Enjoy a craft beer using local ingredients in their brewery and taproom.
  • Beachside Brew Pub - A family-friendly atmosphere where you can sip on quality beers and dine on delicious food.

Where to Stay in Daytona Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Shores Resort & Spa - A luxurious oceanfront resort with an on-site spa, upscale dining, and picturesque seaside views.
  • Daytona Beach Regency - A sea-front property offering comfortable suites, suitable for budget-friendly travelers.
  • The Streamline Hotel - A Funky mid-century style hotel that’s perfect for visitors who would love a quirky and retro vibe.

What to Buy in Daytona Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Nautical-Themed Gifts - Beach-themed decor, clothes, and accessories can be found at souvenir shops across Daytona Beach.
  • Saltwater Taffy - Take a taste of the seaside with the classic candy that will melt in your mouth.
  • Local Arts and Crafts - Daytona Beach has an established local art scene offering pieces that range from pottery to watercolors that make a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Daytona Beach

The local people of Daytona Beach are friendly and welcoming, with lots of festivals and cultural activities throughout the year. Locals enjoy celebrating events related to the Daytona 500 car race as well as various carnivals and music festivals that are held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Daytona Beach

The best time to visit Daytona Beach is during the fall, winter, and spring months, between September and May, when the temperatures are comfortable, and the crowds are more manageable. If you’re into motorsports, then February is the prime time to visit so you can experience the famous Daytona 500 race.

Do’s and Don’ts of Daytona Beach

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated when spending time outdoors or on the beach.
  • Take part in the many outdoor activities available, like hiking, boating or fishing on your choice.
  • Attend one of the many festivals and events held throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach or in public areas.
  • Ignore local traffic laws, as there is a significant police presence on the roads in Daytona Beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Daytona Beach

Daytona Beach is generally a safe place to visit, but like any tourist destination, visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings. As long as you take reasonable precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night, Daytona Beach is a safe and enjoyable place to travel.

Health in Daytona Beach

Daytona Beach has plenty of medical facilities and pharmacies catering to visitors. Medical insurance is advised for any unexpected events that may occur.

Getting to Daytona Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Daytona Beach

The Daytona Beach International Airport (DAB) is the closest airport to the city center and is serviced by several major airlines. Visitors can also fly into Orlando International Airport (MCO), which is about an hour’s drive from Daytona Beach.

Bus Stations Near Daytona Beach

Greyhound Lines offers bus service to Daytona Beach from select locations around the country.

Other Options

Several car rental agencies service both the Daytona Beach and the Orlando International Airport.

How to Get Around in Daytona Beach

There are several ways to get around Daytona Beach. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, use Lyft, or Uber. There is also a public bus service called Votran, which connects different areas of Daytona Beach, including the beachside areas and shopping centers.

Where to Go Next from Daytona Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Central Florida Attractions - Daytona Beach is only an hour’s drive away from Orlando, home of the famous Walt Disney World and Universal Studios theme parks.
  • St. Augustine - Just an hour’s ride north of Daytona Beach, this historic town is known as the oldest continuously inhabited European-founded city in the United States.
  • Kennedy Space Center - A popular attraction that showcases the history of America’s space program and offers tours of launch pads and buildings.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures or historical and cultural experiences, Daytona Beach offers a multitude of choices for every kind of traveler. With a vast array of activities and attractions to choose from, there’s something to suit everyone.
