Deltona Travel Guide: Explore the Best of Florida

Welcome to Deltona – a charming city in Volusia County, Florida, known for its natural beauty, great weather, and laid-back atmosphere. With its abundance of parks, lakes, and trails, there’s no shortage of things to see and do here! Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or simply soaking up the local charm, Deltona has something for everyone. So, let’s dive in and explore the best of what this Florida gem has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Deltona

Here’s a quick overview of the best attractions in Deltona:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Deltona Arts and Historical Center: Located at the heart of Deltona, the Deltona Arts and Historical Center is a must-visit for culture lovers. This former elementary school is now home to a museum and art gallery, and also serves as a community center.
  • DeBary Hall Historic Site: Just a short drive from Deltona, the DeBary Hall Historic Site is a beautifully restored mansion built in 1871. Take a guided tour of the property to learn about its rich history and admire the stunning architecture.
  • Orange City Historic District: This charming district in nearby Orange City is home to a number of historic buildings, shops, and restaurants, including the beautifully restored Butterfield Mansion.

Natural Attractions of Deltona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Monroe Park: Located on the shores of beautiful Lake Monroe, this park offers fishing piers, picnic areas, hiking trails, and a playground.
  • Lyonia Preserve: This 360-acre preserve is home to a range of native Florida plant and animal species, including the threatened Florida scrub-jay bird. Take a guided tour or explore the trails on your own.
  • Gemini Springs Park: A popular spot for picnicking, fishing, and nature walks, Gemini Springs Park boasts two large springs and a boardwalk trail that winds through a cypress swamp.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Deltona

Deltona is home to a number of scenic trails, offering a range of options for hikers and bikers:

  • Spring-to-Spring Trail: This 26-mile paved trail connects three state parks – De Leon Springs, Blue Spring, and Gemini Springs – and passes through beautiful natural landscapes along the way.
  • Lake Monroe Park Trail: This easy, paved trail winds through the wooded park and offers beautiful views of Lake Monroe.
  • Green Springs Park Trail: This short, easy trail leads to the historic Green Springs, which has been attracting visitors for over 150 years.

Best Things to Do in Deltona: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or culture, Deltona has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Skydive Deland: If you’re an adrenaline junkie, there’s no better way to experience Deltona than by jumping out of a plane! Skydive Deland offers tandem jumps and training courses for all levels.
  • Blue Spring State Park: Located just a short drive from Deltona, Blue Spring State Park is a popular spot for tubing, kayaking, and swimming in crystal clear waters.
  • Deltona Club Golf Course: Tee off at this beautiful, 18-hole golf course, which features challenging water hazards and scenic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Deltona

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long stay in Deltona:

Food and Drink Scene of Deltona

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Deltona has a range of options to suit all tastes. Here are some local dishes you shouldn’t miss:

  • Gator Bites: Definitely a unique dish that you won’t find everywhere, try some gator bites at the local restaurants and eateries around here!
  • Key Lime Pie: No trip to Florida is complete without trying this classic dessert. Try it at one of the local restaurants or bakeries.
  • Seafood: Being so close to the ocean, Deltona and the surrounding area has several seafood restaurants that offer delicacies made of fresh catch from the nearby waters.

Local Beverages

Florida has a thriving craft beer scene, and Deltona is no exception. Here are some of the best local breweries and bars:

  • Central 28 Beer Company: This family-owned brewery in nearby DeBary offers a range of delicious beers, from crisp lagers to hoppy IPAs.
  • The Abbey: Located in downtown DeLand, The Abbey is a cozy bar that serves craft beer, wine, and cocktails, as well as live music and events.
  • Spirits of Bacchus: This wine and beer bar in nearby Deland offers over 500 different types of wine, craft beer, and specialty cocktails.

Where to Stay in Deltona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Deltona has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses:

  • Hampton Inn Debary/Deltona: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and an outdoor pool.
  • Airbnb: There are plenty of affordable and comfortable options through Airbnb, including entire apartments and private rooms in shared homes.

What to Buy in Deltona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring back home from Deltona? Here are some local products and shops to check out:

  • Live music recordings from the Athens Theatre: Catch a local show at the historic Athens Theatre, and grab a recording of the performance on your way out.
  • Citrus-flavored products: Florida is known for its citrus fruits – bring back some orange juice, marmalade, or even a scented candle.
  • Folk art: Discover some local talent at various art galleries and shops throughout Deltona which sell unique folk art and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Deltona

Deltona has a friendly and laid-back culture, with a focus on outdoor recreation and community events. The locals are welcoming and enjoy sharing their love for the city with visitors. Keep in mind that wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated is necessary when spending time outdoors due to the warm and sunny weather.

Best Time to Visit Deltona

The best time to visit Deltona is between March and May, when the weather is pleasant and there are fewer crowds. Try avoiding the summer months when it gets uncomfortably hot, and winter months when rainfall is more common.

Do’s and Don’ts of Deltona

Do:

  • Do indulge in the many outdoor activities that Deltona has to offer – it’s a nature lover’s paradise!
  • Do try the local seafood and beer scene.
  • Do bring comfortable walking shoes for hiking and walking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to apply sunscreen and stay hydrated.
  • Don’t litter the natural parks and waters – keeping the area clean is a priority.
  • Don’t forget to book ahead for popular attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Deltona

Deltona is a safe and friendly city, with low crime rates. As with any destination, it’s important to take normal precautions and avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Deltona

Deltona and Volusia County have adequate healthcare facilities, ranging from urgent care centers to hospitals. Take necessary precautions to protect against the dengue fever virus – wear mosquito repellant and avoid outdoor activities during early mornings and late evenings.

Getting to Deltona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Deltona

  • Orlando Sanford International Airport (SFB) – 17 miles away
  • Daytona Beach International Airport (DAB) – 24 miles away
  • Orlando International Airport (MCO) – 48 miles away

Bus Stations Near Deltona

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Sanford, 10 miles away
  • Votran Bus Service: Public transportation system serving Volusia County, including Deltona

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental agencies operate in the area, providing easy transportation to nearby attractions and throughout the state.

How to Get Around in Deltona

Deltona is best explored by car, as public transportation can be limited. Taxis, rideshare services, and car rentals are available.

Where to Go Next from Deltona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Daytona Beach: The famous beach is a mere 30-minute drive from Deltona, making it an ideal day trip or weekend excursion.
  • Orlando: Just an hour’s drive from Deltona, Orlando is home to Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, and a range of other theme parks and attractions.
  • St. Augustine: Take a journey back in time and visit the nation’s oldest city, featuring charming streets lined with historic buildings.

Conclusion

Deltona is a hidden gem in the sunshine state with boundless natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and a friendly, laid-back community. It’s the ideal destination for anyone looking for a relaxing, fun-filled break, and there are plenty of activities to suit all interests. Whether you’re planning a long visit or just a day trip, be sure to add Deltona to your list of must-see Florida destinations.
