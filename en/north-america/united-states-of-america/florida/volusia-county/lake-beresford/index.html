Introduction to Lake Beresford

Nestled in the heart of Volusia County, Florida, Lake Beresford is a hidden gem for travelers looking for a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life. It is a 1,100-acre freshwater lake that remains a well-kept secret for those who love outdoor adventures, scenic views, and natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you everything you need to know about exploring Lake Beresford and its surrounding areas.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Beresford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to culturally significant attractions, Lake Beresford has a lot to offer its visitors. Some of the top museums and historical sites to visit in the area include:

  • Stetson Mansion: This Gilded Age mansion is one of the most stunning historical sites in the area. Built in 1886, it is now open to the public for tours and events.
  • DeLand Naval Air Station Museum: Experience the rich history of naval aviation in Florida at this museum that features exhibits, artifacts, and memorabilia from the WWII era.
  • African American Museum of the Arts: This museum showcases the works of African-American artists and features a variety of exhibitions and cultural events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Lake Beresford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, Lake Beresford does not disappoint. Here are the top natural attractions you shouldn’t miss:

  • Lake Beresford Park: Located at the southern end of the lake, this park offers visitors a range of activities like fishing, swimming, boating, and camping.
  • Blue Spring State Park: Only a short drive from Lake Beresford, this state park offers activities like hiking, kayaking, and wildlife viewing. It is also home to the largest concentration of manatees in the United States.
  • Hontoon Island State Park: Accessible only by boat, this off-the-beaten-path island offers visitors a serene escape from the hectic pace of everyday life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Beresford

Exploring Lake Beresford by foot or bike is a great way to experience the region’s natural beauty up-close. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes around Lake Beresford:

  • Lake Beresford Trail: This 2.6-mile trail winds around the eastern shore of Lake Beresford and is ideal for both hiking and biking.
  • Blue Spring State Park Trail: This 4.5-mile trail takes you through the heart of Blue Spring State Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • DeLeon Springs State Park Trail: This 4.2-mile trail takes you through DeLeon Springs State Park and offers plenty of opportunities for wildlife spotting.

Best Things to Do in Lake Beresford: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun and exciting things to do in Lake Beresford. Here are some top activities and tours worth checking out:

  • Airboat Rides: Glide through the waterways of Lake Beresford on an airboat tour and get up close and personal with the local wildlife.
  • Fishing: Cast your line in the crystal-clear waters of Lake Beresford and try your luck at catching a variety of freshwater fish.
  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and explore the many waterways and canals of Lake Beresford at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Beresford

If you’re planning a week-long getaway to Lake Beresford, here’s an itinerary you can follow to make the most of your trip:

Day 1-2: Explore Lake Beresford

Spend your first two days exploring the various parks, trails, and attractions around Lake Beresford. Visit Lake Beresford Park, go kayaking or take an airboat tour, and catch a sunset at Blue Spring State Park.

Day 3: Visit DeLand and Stetson Mansion

Spend the day in the charming town of DeLand, located just a short drive from Lake Beresford. Visit the Stetson Mansion, explore the town’s many art galleries and boutique shops, and sample some of the local cuisine.

Day 4-5: Visit Daytona Beach

Take a short drive to Daytona Beach and explore its many attractions, including the Daytona International Speedway, the Daytona Beach Pier, and the beach itself.

Day 6: Visit DeLeon Springs State Park

Spend the day exploring DeLeon Springs State Park, a beautiful natural area that features hiking trails, a swimming area, and a spring where you can swim, kayak or paddleboard.

Day 7: Relax and Recharge

Spend your last day relaxing and recharging at your hotel, enjoying the beautiful views of Lake Beresford, and reminiscing about all the amazing experiences you’ve had during your week in this beautiful part of Florida.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Beresford

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Lake Beresford is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some dishes you simply must try:

  • Gator Bites: Deep-fried and served with a spicy sauce, these little bites of alligator meat are a Florida specialty.
  • Seafood Platter: Enjoy a platter of fresh seafood, ranging from shrimp and scallops to crab and lobster, at one of the many local restaurants.
  • Key Lime Pie: A classic Florida dessert, key lime pie is a must-try when visiting Lake Beresford.

Local Beverages

Quench your thirst with these local beverages:

  • Orange Juice: Florida is known for its orange groves, so be sure to try a glass of fresh-squeezed orange juice during your stay.
  • Mojo Brew: A local coffee blend made with high-quality beans, often served iced with sweetened condensed milk.
  • Craft Beer: Sample some of the local craft beers brewed in the area.

Where to Stay in Lake Beresford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a cozy guesthouse, Lake Beresford has plenty of options for accommodations. Here are some of the top places to stay in the area:

  • Hontoon Landing Resort and Marina: This resort offers waterfront rooms, a marina, and on-site dining.
  • Artisan Downtown: Enjoy luxury accommodations in the heart of DeLand at this boutique hotel.
  • Alling House Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers guests cozy rooms and a daily gourmet breakfast.

What to Buy in Lake Beresford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider bringing home from your trip to Lake Beresford:

  • Orange Marmalade: Made with locally grown oranges, this tangy marmalade is a delicious souvenir.
  • Florida Honey: Sweeten up your morning toast with locally sourced honey that’s been harvested right from the region.
  • Ceramics and Pottery: Visit one of the local studios to find beautiful, handcrafted ceramics and pottery that make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Beresford

While visiting Lake Beresford, it’s important to be mindful of the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be Respectful of Wildlife: Lake Beresford is home to many species of wildlife, so be sure to observe them from a distance and avoid feeding or touching them.
  • Dress Appropriately: Florida can be hot and humid, so dress comfortably and appropriately for the weather, especially if you plan on engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Be Friendly and Polite: Floridians are known for their friendliness and hospitality, so be sure to return the favor by being polite, respectful, and courteous during your stay.

Best Time to Visit Lake Beresford

The best time to visit Lake Beresford is during the spring and fall months when temperatures are milder and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities. However, if you’re looking to catch a glimpse of the manatees, winter is the best time to visit as they migrate to the warmer waters of the springs.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Beresford

Do:

  • Do Respect the Environment: Keep Lake Beresford clean and respect the natural environment by properly disposing of trash and not damaging any plants or wildlife.
  • Do Bring Sunscreen and Insect Repellent: Protect yourself from the Florida sun and pesky bugs by packing a bottle of sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Do Stay Hydrated: Florida can get hot, so be sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Litter: Avoid littering, and dispose of trash responsibly to keep Lake Beresford clean and beautiful.
  • Don’t Touch Wildlife: While wildlife can be fascinating to observe, it’s important to keep a respectful distance and never touch or feed them.
  • Don’t Forget the Bugs: Mosquitoes and other insects can be a nuisance, so be sure to pack insect repellent and other protective measures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Beresford

Lake Beresford is generally considered safe for visitors, but it’s important to practice common sense safety precautions like locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight, especially in parking lots and public areas.

Health in Lake Beresford

It’s always a good idea to stay healthy while traveling, and Lake Beresford is no exception. Bring any necessary medications, stay hydrated, and avoid exposure to direct sunlight for long periods.

Getting to Lake Beresford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Beresford

  • Orlando International Airport: Approximately 50 miles from Lake Beresford, this airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Lake Beresford

  • DeLand SunRail Station: Located in downtown DeLand, this station connects to SunRail commuter train service.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Many rental car companies have locations at Orlando International Airport, making it easy to rent a car and drive to Lake Beresford.

How to Get Around in Lake Beresford

The best way to get around Lake Beresford is by renting a car or bringing your own vehicle. However, the area is also bike-friendly with several trails suitable for cycling.

Where to Go Next from Lake Beresford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your vacation or explore more of Florida, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Orlando: Known for its theme parks, Orlando is only a short drive from Lake Beresford and offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages.
  • St. Augustine: Located a little over an hour from Lake Beresford, St. Augustine is the oldest city in the United States and offers a rich history, stunning architecture, and beautiful beaches.
  • Cocoa Beach: About an hour from Lake Beresford, Cocoa Beach is a surfer’s paradise with year-round waves and plenty of water sports opportunities.

Conclusion

Lake Beresford is a hidden gem in Florida, offering breathtaking natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or an exciting adventure, Lake Beresford has something for everyone. Plan your visit using this comprehensive travel guide and experience the best that this beautiful region has to offer.
