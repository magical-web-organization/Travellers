Oak Hill is a small, charming town located on the eastern coast of Florida in Volusia County. Set in a picturesque setting, this town is a nature lover’s paradise, offering vast marshes, verdant forests, and stunning wetlands. Whether you’re looking for natural beauty, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, Oak Hill has something for everyone.

Introduction to Oak Hill

Oak Hill is popularly known for its unique blend of history, culture, and nature. This quaint town is sandwiched between the Mosquito Lagoon to the west and the Indian River to the east. It is a perfect base for exploring the local area and experiencing some of the best Florida has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Oak Hill

From culturally important attractions to natural wonders, Oak Hill has a lot to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in Oak Hill.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mary S. Harrell Black Heritage Museum - This fascinating museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that highlights the achievements of African Americans in Oak Hill.
  2. Eldora State House - This historic site is a window into the past, offering a glimpse of Oak Hill’s rich history and architectural heritage.
  3. Eldora Nature Preserve - A nature preserve located across from the Eldora State House, it boasts of over 80 acres of virgin pine forest, marshland, and nature trails.

Natural Attractions of Oak Hill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Canaveral National Seashore - With over 24 miles of pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters, it is a perfect spot for swimming and relaxation.
  2. Indian River Lagoon Scenic Highway - This 218-mile coastal highway offers stunning views, wildlife sightings, and unparalleled opportunities for kayaking and paddle-boarding.
  3. Lighthouse Loop Scenic Drive - This 34-mile loop offers breathtaking views of the Indian River and Ponce inlet, with fascinating landmarks sprinkled along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oak Hill

Oak Hill is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and more. The best trails to explore in Oak Hill include:

  1. Spruce Creek Preserve - Enjoy the picturesque setting while hiking, biking, or horseback riding through this 1,500-acre preserve.
  2. Doris Leeper Spruce Creek Preserve - Along with 5 miles of hiking and biking trails, this preserve also offers spectacular views of Florida’s salt marshes.
  3. Canaveral National Seashore - This park offers over 35 miles of scenic hiking trails that lead through pristine beaches, hammocks, and pine flatwoods.

Best Things to Do in Oak Hill: Activities and Tours

Regardless of your interests, Oak Hill offers a range of activities and tours to suit every taste. Here are the top things to do in Oak Hill.

  1. Oak Hill Flea Market - This market is home to over 1,000 vendors selling everything from fresh produce and handmade crafts to antiques and collectibles.
  2. Mosquito Lagoon Fishing - One of the best places to fish in Florida, this lagoon is famous for its abundant redfish, trout, and tarpon.
  3. Soaring Center - Experience the thrill of glider flights over Florida’s beautiful scenery with experienced pilots.
  4. Kayaking Tours - Explore the Indian River lagoon and look out for bottlenose dolphins, manatees, and other sea creatures.

Food and Drink Scene of Oak Hill

If you’re looking for top-notch dining options in Oak Hill, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some must-try dishes in Oak Hill along with the local beverages to accompany them.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fried gator tail - A popular delicacy in Florida, gator meat is tender and tastes like chicken with a slightly fishy aftertaste.
  2. Blackened fish - Savor some of the fresh catches of the day, blackened and perfectly seasoned.
  3. Conch fritters - Made from a mixture of conch meat, seasoning, and flour, these fritters are crispy and delicious.

Local Beverages

  1. Margaritas - Cool off with a refreshing margarita made with fresh lime juice, triple sec, and premium tequila.
  2. Florida craft beers - Sample some of the unique craft beers made in the state of Florida.

Where to Stay in Oak Hill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oak Hill caters to every budget, with accommodation options ranging from hotels, motels, guesthouses, and campgrounds. Here are some top picks:

  1. River Lily Inn Bed & Breakfast - This charming inn features luxurious suites, scenic views of the river, and a home-cooked breakfast each morning.
  2. Disappearing Island RV and Tiny House Resort - The perfect place to stay if you’re looking for a unique vacation, this resort features pet-friendly RVs and tiny houses equipped with all the necessary amenities.
  3. JB’s Fish Camp & Restaurant - This cypress log cabin-themed hotel offers breathtaking views of the waterfront, cozy rooms, and excellent seafood.

What to Buy in Oak Hill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oak Hill offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts and keepsakes. Here are some things you should consider purchasing:

  1. Seashells - Collect some of the unique and beautiful seashells that wash up on the shore.
  2. Art Deco-inspired home decor - Add some Florida flair to your home with some locally crafted art deco-inspired pieces.
  3. Florida honey - Made from the nectar of wildflowers, citrus trees, and other plants, Florida honey is a popular souvenir item.

Local Culture and Customs of Oak Hill

Oak Hill is a culturally rich community with a relaxed vibe. The town’s proximity to the ocean and Florida’s sprawling nature, combined with its history and heritage, makes Oak Hill a unique destination.

Best Time to Visit Oak Hill

The best time to visit Oak Hill is between December and April when the weather is mild, with less rainfall and humidity. Summertime is hot and humid and prone to thunderstorms and hurricanes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oak Hill

Do:

  1. Respect the environment and wildlife.
  2. Keep the surroundings clean.
  3. Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage natural resources.
  2. Trespass on private property.
  3. Ignore the warnings about alligators and other wildlife creatures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oak Hill

Oak Hill is a safe town to visit and explore. However, it is important to stay aware of the surroundings, particularly when hiking in natural areas or visiting at night.

Health in Oak Hill

Make sure to carry sunscreen, plenty of water, and stay hydrated while out and about in Oak Hill. Bugs can also be prevalent, so consider bringing insect repellant.

Getting to Oak Hill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oak Hill is easily accessible by car, with several major highways leading into the town. Alternatively, the Amtrak train station in Deland is around 22 miles away, and the Daytona Beach International Airport is about 25 miles away.

Airports Near Oak Hill

  1. Orlando International Airport (MCO) - About 66 miles away from Oak Hill.
  2. Daytona Beach International Airport (DBL) - Located around 25 miles from Oak Hill.

Bus Stations Near Oak Hill

The closest bus station to Oak Hill is in New Smyrna Beach, which is about eight miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to rent a car, bike, or scooter at the locations around Oak Hill.

How to Get Around in Oak Hill

Most attractions in Oak Hill are easily accessible by car or bike. Visitors can also choose to explore the town on foot or rent a bike or scooter.

Where to Go Next from Oak Hill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oak Hill’s convenient location makes it the perfect base for exploring Florida’s east coast. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  1. Daytona Beach - Famous for its iconic beach, world-class attractions, and motor racing events.
  2. Orlando - Home to some of the world’s most renowned theme parks, including Disney World, Universal Studios, and SeaWorld.
  3. St. Augustine - The oldest town in America, St. Augustine is famous for its historic landmarks, charming streets, and European-style architecture.

Conclusion

Oak Hill is a hidden gem in Florida, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. From its cultural attractions and hiking trails to its food and drink scene, there’s a lot to explore in Oak Hill. With so many activities and sites to see, visitors are sure to have a memorable experience in this charming town.
