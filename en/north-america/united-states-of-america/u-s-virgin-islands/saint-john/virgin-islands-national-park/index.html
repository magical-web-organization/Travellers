Introduction to Virgin Islands National Park

Virgin Islands National Park features stunningly beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, lush tropical forests, and vibrant coral reefs teaming with marine life. The park is spread over two-thirds of the island of St. John in the United States Virgin Islands and is known for its natural and cultural heritage. This guide will take you on a tour of the Virgin Islands National Park, revealing the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, and how to reach this tropical paradise.

List of Places to Visit in Virgin Islands National Park

Here are the top places to visit in Virgin Islands National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Annaberg Sugar Plantation - Located on the north shore of St. John, this historic site is where enslaved Africans worked in the 18th and 19th centuries to produce sugar and molasses.
  2. Cinnamon Bay Archeology Museum - Learn about the indigenous Taino people who lived on the island for thousands of years.
  3. Cruz Bay Visitor Center - A great place to start your visit and get information about the park’s history, programs, and activities.

Natural Attractions of Virgin Islands National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Trunk Bay - A spectacular white-sand beach with crystal-clear waters, ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.
  2. Reef Bay Trail - A popular hiking trail that descends through a tropical forest to the Reef Bay sugar plantation and petroglyphs.
  3. Honeymoon Beach - A small, secluded beach accessible by hiking or a shuttle from Cruz Bay.
  4. Francis Bay - A serene beach located on the north shore of St. John, with calm waters ideal for swimming and snorkeling.
  5. Hassel Island - A historical and natural treasure adjacent to St. Thomas, with beaches, hiking trails, and ruins of military forts and lighthouses.
  6. Waterlemon Cay - A small, uninhabited island accessible by a short hike and ideal for snorkeling among coral gardens and sea turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Virgin Islands National Park

Virgin Islands National Park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that showcase the park’s natural and cultural features. Here’s a list of some of the most popular trails:

  1. Reef Bay Trail - A 2.3 miles trail that descends through tropical forest to the ruins of a sugar plantation and petroglyphs.
  2. Ram Head Trail - A 2.4 miles trail that leads to a panoramic view of the island and the Caribbean Sea.
  3. Cinnamon Bay Nature Trail - A 0.3 miles trail that passes through forests, mangroves, and beaches, with interpretive signs explaining the ecology and history of the area.
  4. Bordeaux Mountain Trail - A 1.3 miles trail that leads to the highest peak on St. John, with breathtaking views of the island and the British Virgin Islands.
  5. Annaberg Trail - A 0.5 miles trail that explores the ruins of a sugar plantation and offers views of Mary Creek and Waterlemon Cay.

Best Things to Do in Virgin Islands National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling - Virgin Islands National Park has some of the best snorkeling spots in the Caribbean, including Trunk Bay, Waterlemon Cay, and Maho Bay.
  2. Scuba Diving - For more experienced divers, the park offers opportunities to explore coral reefs, walls, and shipwrecks.
  3. Kayaking - Explore the calm waters around St. John on a kayak, either solo or on a guided tour.
  4. Sailing - Charter a boat and sail around the islands, enjoying the scenic beauty and the marine wildlife.
  5. Camping - The park offers campsites on Maho Bay and Cinnamon Bay, allowing visitors to experience the natural beauty of the park up close.
  6. Stargazing - With little light pollution and clear skies, Virgin Islands National Park is a great place to observe the stars and planets.

Food and Drink Scene of Virgin Islands National Park

Virgin Islands National Park offers a range of culinary experiences, from street food to gourmet restaurants, showcasing the local flavors and influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Conch fritters - Fried balls of conch meat, often served with a tangy dipping sauce.
  2. Fish tacos - Soft tortillas filled with grilled or fried fish, cabbage, avocado, and salsa.
  3. Jerk chicken - Spicy grilled chicken, marinated with a blend of Caribbean spices.
  4. Johnny cake - A dense, slightly sweet bread made with flour and cornmeal, often served as a breakfast snack.
  5. Cruzan rum cake - A moist, rich cake made with local rum, often used as a souvenir or gift.

Local Beverages

  1. Rum Punch - A fruity cocktail made with Cruzan rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, and grenadine.
  2. Bushwacker - A creamy frozen cocktail made with Cruzan rum, Kahlua, coconut cream, and chocolate syrup.
  3. Painkiller - A coconut and pineapple-based cocktail made with rum, orange juice, and nutmeg.

Where to Stay in Virgin Islands National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in and around Virgin Islands National Park, ranging from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some suggestions:

  1. Caneel Bay Resort - A luxury resort with seven beaches, five restaurants, and an eco-friendly approach.
  2. The Westin St. John Resort Villas - A villa-style resort with ocean views, swimming pools, and spa services.
  3. Maho Bay Camps - An eco-camp with tent-cottages, solar power, and composting toilets.
  4. Gallows Point Resort - A boutique resort with oceanfront suites, a pool, and a water sports center.
  5. Cruz Bay Boutique Hotel - A small, stylish hotel with a rooftop pool, hot tub, and water views.

What to Buy in Virgin Islands National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Virgin Islands National Park offers a range of souvenirs and local products that reflect the island’s culture and heritage. Here are some suggestions:

  1. Local artwork and jewelry - Local artists create paintings, sculptures, and jewelry inspired by the island’s landscapes and motifs.
  2. Hot sauce and spices - The Virgin Islands are known for their spicy cuisine, and local hot sauces and spices make great gifts.
  3. T-shirts and hats - Merchandise with the park’s logo and slogans is available at gift shops and online.
  4. Cruzan rum - Virgin Islands National Park is located on the island where Cruzan rum is made, and visitors can buy bottles to enjoy or bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Virgin Islands National Park

The Virgin Islands are a diverse and vibrant community, with influences from African, European, and indigenous cultures. Here are some customs and etiquette tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches, museums, and other cultural sites.
  • Remove your hat and sunglasses when entering a building or meeting someone.
  • Don’t touch or disturb historical or archaeological sites.
  • Greet people with a smile and a nod, and say “Good morning/afternoon/evening”.
  • Respect the environment and local wildlife.
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants and bars.

Best Time to Visit Virgin Islands National Park

The best time to visit Virgin Islands National Park is between December and April, when the weather is sunny and dry, with temperatures averaging between 70-80 degrees Fahrenheit. The summer months can be hot and humid, with chances of rain and storms. The hurricane season runs from June to November, with the highest risk in September and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Virgin Islands National Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Virgin Islands National Park:

Do:

  • Respect the natural and cultural heritage of the park.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from sunburn and bug bites.
  • Carry plenty of water and snacks on hikes and outdoor activities.
  • Take your trash with you and dispose of it properly.
  • Be prepared for unmarked trails and rough terrain.
  • Get a map and information from the visitor center before embarking on activities.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb coral reefs, turtles, and other marine life.
  • Feed or approach wildlife, especially iguanas and mongoose.
  • Swim or snorkel alone or in unsafe waters.
  • Litter or leave behind any waste or personal belongings.
  • Ignore signs and warnings about hazardous conditions or areas.
  • Drink tap water, which may contain harmful bacteria.

Safety and Health

Safety in Virgin Islands National Park

Virgin Islands National Park is a relatively safe destination for tourists, but visitors should still take some precautions, such as:

  • Lock your hotel room and car when leaving.
  • Avoid isolated or dark areas at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings in crowded places.
  • Be cautious when swimming or hiking in remote areas.
  • Listen to local news and weather reports for any alerts or warnings.

Health in Virgin Islands National Park

Visitors to Virgin Islands National Park should take some health precautions, such as:

  • Drink bottled or treated water to avoid stomach illnesses.
  • Use insect repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites and the Zika virus.
  • Apply sunscreen regularly to avoid sunburn and skin damage.
  • Consult a doctor or travel clinic for any vaccinations or medications needed before traveling to the Caribbean.

Getting to Virgin Islands National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Virgin Islands National Park is located on the island of St. John, one of the three U.S. Virgin Islands, and is accessible by air, sea, and land.

Airports Near Virgin Islands National Park

  • Cyril E. King Airport (STT) in St. Thomas - The main airport serving the U.S. Virgin Islands, with connections to major U.S. cities and Caribbean destinations.
  • Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport (EIS) in Tortola, British Virgin Islands - A smaller airport with connections to other Caribbean destinations.

Bus Stations Near Virgin Islands National Park

There are no bus stations or public transportation services within Virgin Islands National Park, but taxis and rental cars are available at the airports and ferry terminals.

Other Options

  • Private boats and charters are available for exploring the islands and reaching remote beaches.
  • Ferries run between St. Thomas and St. John, as well as between the U.S. and British Virgin Islands.
  • Hiking and biking are popular ways to explore the park and reach some of the attractions and beaches.

How to Get Around in Virgin Islands National Park

There are several ways to get around Virgin Islands National Park and St. John:

  • Rental cars and jeeps are available on St. John, but be prepared for narrow and steep roads, and drive on the left side.
  • Taxis are a popular and convenient option for getting around, especially for groups or destinations that are hard to reach by car.
  • Biking is a popular and eco-friendly way to explore the island, with bike rentals available in Cruz Bay and Coral Bay.
  • Walking and hiking are also viable options, with several trails and paths connecting the beaches and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Virgin Islands National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The U.S. and British Virgin Islands offer many other attractions and destinations to explore, including:

  • St. Thomas - Known for its duty-free shopping, historic sites, and panoramic views from Mountain Top.
  • Tortola - The largest island in the British Virgin Islands, with hiking, sailing, and cultural attractions.
  • Jost Van Dyke - A tiny island with pristine beaches, beach bars, and a laid-back atmosphere.
  • Norman Island - A popular spot for snorkeling, diving, and exploring sunken ships.
  • Anegada - A flat and sandy island with turquoise waters and a unique ecosystem.

Conclusion

Virgin Islands National Park is a breathtaking and diverse destination, offering a blend of natural and cultural treasures. From the white-sand beaches to the tropical forests and the historic sites, there’s something for everyone to discover and enjoy. Use this guide to plan your trip to Virgin Islands National Park and experience all that this paradise has to offer.
