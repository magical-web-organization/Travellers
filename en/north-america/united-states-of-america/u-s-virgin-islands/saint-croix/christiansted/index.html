Introduction to Christiansted

Christiansted is the largest town in the United States Virgin Islands (USVI), located on the northeast coast of Saint Croix. This vibrant town offers a unique blend of colonial charm, rich local culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or simply relaxing on the beach, Christiansted has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Christiansted

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you want to explore the rich history and cultural heritage of Christiansted, be sure to visit these must-see attractions:

  • Fort Christiansvaern: Built in the 18th century by Danish colonists, this impressive fort is a well-preserved example of European military architecture in the Caribbean.
  • Estate Whim Plantation Museum: A former sugar plantation dating back to the 1700s, now converted into a museum that showcases the island’s history and culture.
  • Scale House: A relic from the whaling industry that once thrived in Christiansted, the Scale House is a tiny building that now serves as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Christiansted: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Christiansted is surrounded by beautiful coastline, breathtaking landscapes, and pristine beaches, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. Check out these natural attractions:

  • Buck Island Reef National Monument: Located just a short boat ride from Christiansted, Buck Island is a National Park that boasts some of the best snorkeling and diving spots in the Caribbean.
  • Sandy Point National Wildlife Refuge: A peaceful haven for sea turtles, birds, and other wildlife, Sandy Point is a picturesque stretch of beach that also has historic significance as the site of the last stand-off between French and British forces in the region.
  • Salt River Bay National Historical Park & Ecological Preserve: A protected area that features vast mangrove forests, archaeological sites, and ancient petroglyphs that tell the story of the island’s first inhabitants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Christiansted

With its rugged terrain, verdant forests, and scenic views, Christiansted is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Here are a few popular routes to check out:

  • Annaly Bay Trail: A challenging hike that takes you through dense forests, rocky terrain, and steep hills, culminating in a breathtaking view of the rugged coastline.
  • East End Loop Trail: A picturesque route that takes you through quiet neighborhoods and along tranquil beaches and coves, perfect for a leisurely bike ride.
  • Mount Eagle Trail: A somewhat difficult hike that offers stunning views of the island and an incredible sense of accomplishment once you reach the top.

Best Things to Do in Christiansted: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some adventure or simply want to explore Christiansted in a unique way, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Some popular options include:

  • Cruzan Rum Distillery Tour: Discover the secrets of the world-famous Cruzan Rum and sample some of the finest spirits produced on the island.
  • Kayak Tour of Salt River Bay: Glide along the serene waters of Salt River Bay as you learn about the region’s history and ecology from your expert guide.
  • Christiansted Food Tour: Savor the flavors of St. Croix by sampling some of the island’s best cuisine and drinks, curated by local experts.

Food and Drink Scene of Christiansted

When it comes to the food and drink scene, Christiansted has something for everyone. From local specialties to international cuisine and trendy bars, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pate: A savory pastry stuffed with meat, fish, or vegetables, pate is a popular snack on the island.
  • Kallaloo: A thick soup made with leafy greens, okra, and meat or seafood, kallaloo is a traditional dish that reflects the island’s African heritage.
  • Johnny Cake: A fluffy and slightly sweet bread that’s a staple of the Caribbean diet.

Local Beverages

  • Cruzan Rum: Widely regarded as some of the best rum in the world, the locally produced Cruzan Rum is a must-try for any visitor to Christiansted.
  • Bush Tea: A refreshing herbal tea made with local herbs and spices that’s said to have many health benefits.
  • Coconut Water: Available fresh from street vendors all over the island, coconut water is a hydrating and delicious way to cool off on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Christiansted: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Christiansted has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a cozy bed and breakfast, here are some top-rated options:

  • The Buccaneer: A family-owned resort with stunning views of the ocean, luxurious amenities, and a private beach.
  • Hotel Caravelle: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Christiansted town, with stylish rooms and easy access to all the attractions.
  • Sand Castle on the Beach: A romantic and intimate resort that features tropical gardens, a private beach, and a spa.

What to Buy in Christiansted: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and authentic souvenirs to bring back home, Christiansted won’t disappoint. Here are a few local products worth considering:

  • Cruzan Rum: As previously mentioned, Cruzan Rum is one of the best products to buy in Christiansted. You can find it at the distillery or at local shops.
  • Hook Bracelets: A popular local accessory that symbolizes love, loyalty, and connection. You can find them in various materials and designs.
  • Local Artwork: From paintings to sculptures, there are many talented local artists whose work reflects the spirit and culture of the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Christiansted

The people of Christiansted are known for their friendly and relaxed demeanor, and their rich cultural heritage. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Dress Code: Although the island is casual, it’s considered inappropriate to wear swimwear or beach clothes in town or at restaurants.
  • Respect for Elders and Community: The island culture places a high value on respect for elders and community. It’s customary to greet people politely and use formal titles such as “Miss” or “Ma’am.”
  • Island Time: Take it easy when it comes to scheduling and punctuality, as the island tends to operate on a more relaxed sense of time.

Best Time to Visit Christiansted

The best time to visit Christiansted is between mid-December and mid-April, when the weather is at its driest and temperatures are comfortable. However, this is also the peak tourist season, meaning it can be more crowded and expensive. Alternatively, you can consider visiting in the shoulder season, from May to November, when there are fewer visitors and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Christiansted

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Christiansted, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Bring comfortable walking shoes and sun protection.
  • Rent a car or book a tour to explore the island.

Don’t:

  • Touch or remove any coral while snorkeling or diving.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Use illegal drugs or substances.

Safety and Health

Safety in Christiansted

Christiansted is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, like any tourist destination, there are some precautions you should take:

  • Avoid walking alone or in unlit areas at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid flashing expensive items.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellant to protect against sunburn and mosquito bites.

Health in Christiansted

As a tropical destination, Christiansted has some health risks that travelers should be aware of:

  • Zika and other mosquito-borne illnesses are a risk in the Caribbean, so use insect repellant and wear long sleeve shirts and pants.
  • The tap water is generally safe to drink in Christiansted, but it’s always a good idea to stick to bottled water.
  • Sunburn and heat exhaustion are common risks, so be sure to use sunscreen and stay hydrated.

Getting to Christiansted: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Christiansted

The closest airport to Christiansted is the Henry E. Rohlsen International Airport, located about 10 miles from the town center. The airport serves direct flights from several cities in the United States and Puerto Rico.

Bus Stations Near Christiansted

Although there’s no bus service within the town of Christiansted, there are public buses that connect the major towns on the island, with fares ranging from $1 to $2.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a taxi is the most convenient and reliable way to get around the island. Many hotels and resorts offer shuttle services to and from the airport, as well as transportation to the major attractions.

How to Get Around in Christiansted

Within the town itself, the best way to get around is on foot, as many of the attractions and amenities are within easy walking distance. For longer distances, renting a car or taking a taxi are your best bets.

Conclusion

With its vast array of attractions, activities, and accommodations, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant town.
