Introduction to Four Corners

Located in the Rocky Mountains of the United States in North America, Four Corners is a unique destination known for its intersection of four states: Utah, Colorado, Arizona, and New Mexico. This destination is beloved by outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike and promises unforgettable experiences for all visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Four Corners

Here are some of the top places to visit during your Four Corners adventure:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Canyon de Chelly National Monument: Walk in the footsteps of the Native American tribes who have lived here for centuries and visit the park’s preserved ruins and historic artifacts.
  • The Anasazi Heritage Center: Learn about the history and culture of the region’s ancient peoples through exhibits and educational programs.
  • Mesa Verde National Park: This park is home to the largest collection of Puebloan cliff dwellings in the world, inviting visitors to explore the homes and lives of these ancient people.

Natural Attractions of Four Corners: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horseshoe Bend: Take a scenic hike to the edge of the canyon and witness the stunning view of the Colorado River carving a horseshoe-shaped meander 1,000 feet below.
  • Monument Valley: This collection of towering sandstone formations is synonymous with the American West and attracts visitors with its stunning natural beauty.
  • The Grand Canyon: One of the world’s most famous natural wonders, the Grand Canyon needs no introduction and is a must-visit when in Four Corners.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Four Corners

With awe-inspiring natural landscapes, Four Corners is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts looking for a hiking or biking adventure. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Maze at Canyonlands National Park: This remote area is home to towering canyons, natural arches, and beautiful rock formations and is perfect for experienced hikers who want to get off the beaten track.
  • Angel’s Landing at Zion National Park: This challenging hike takes you to the top of a towering rock formation with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Colorado Trail: This 486-mile trail showcases the stunning natural beauty of Colorado and challenges hikers and bikers alike with varying levels of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Four Corners: Activities and Tours

Whether you are travelling alone, with friends or with family, Four Corners has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Enjoy the sweeping vistas of Four Corners from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Horseback Riding in Monument Valley: Experience the Wild West with a horseback ride through the stunning rock formations of Monument Valley.
  • Visit a National Park: With a myriad of national parks and monuments nearby, visitors can access unique outdoor opportunities and explore the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Four Corners

A week in Four Corners will be enough time to experience many of the highlights the region has to offer. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Grand Junction, Colorado. Drive to Mesa Verde National Park.
2 Spend the day exploring Canyon de Chelly National Monument.
3 Drive to Zion National Park. Hike Angel’s Landing.
4 Visit Monument Valley. Go on a horseback ride or Jeep tour.
5 Drive to Horseshoe Bend and the Vermilion Cliffs National Monument.
6 Hike the Colorado National Monument.
7 Depart from Grand Junction, Colorado.

Food and Drink Scene of Four Corners

Must-Try Dishes

Four Corners has an array of excellent dining options offering regional flavors such as:

  • Navajo Tacos: a traditional dish consisting of fry bread piled high with beans and other toppings.
  • Green Chili Stew: A hearty dish that perfectly combines beef, beans, and chilies.
  • Chimichangas: A deep-fried burrito with delicious fillings such as chicken or beef and topped with cheese and guacamole.

Local Beverages

  • Hatch Chile Beers: Local breweries take advantage of the region’s Hatch chilies to create refreshing and unique beers that perfectly complement southwestern cuisine.
  • Cortados: This coffee is a typical drink in New Mexico, made with espresso and warm milk, it is the perfect pick-me-up after a day of adventure.

Where to Stay in Four Corners: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you are looking for a luxurious retreat or a budget-friendly option, Four Corners has accommodations to suit every need. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The View Hotel: located on the Navajo Nation in the heart of Monument Valley, this hotel offers breathtaking views of the iconic formations.
  • Kokopelli Hostel: A family-owned hostel located in Moab, Utah, featuring affordable accommodations and a laid-back atmosphere.
  • The Historic Strater Hotel: Located in Durango, Colorado, this hotel has been a staple of the Four Corners area for more than 130 years and offers a luxurious experience.

What to Buy in Four Corners: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Navajo Jewelry: The Navajo are known for their intricate silver jewelry, which combines traditional techniques and designs with contemporary artistry.
  • Hopis Kachina Dolls: These handmade dolls serve both as toys and sacred items that depict spirits and deities.
  • Chili Ristras: This decorative string of dried chili peppers is often seen adorning the exterior of southwestern homes and is a great way to add some spice to your kitchen decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Four Corners

Four Corners is rich in Native American history, with Navajo, Ute, Puebloan, and Apache tribes among those that call the area home. Show respect for local customs and traditions, and learn about the history of their culture by visiting some of the region’s museums and cultural centers.

Best Time to Visit Four Corners

The best time to visit Four Corners, weather-wise, is during the spring and fall, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. However, each season has its own benefits; summer offers opportunities for water-based activities, while winter invites visitors to experience the region’s snowy mountains. Ultimately, the best time to visit is based on your personal interests.

Do’s and Don’ts of Four Corners

Do:

  • Respect Native American customs and traditions.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor adventures.
  • Try regional delicacies such as Navajo Tacos and Green Chili Stew.

Don’t:

  • Climb or deface rock formations.
  • Take items from protected areas.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Four Corners

The area is generally safe, but always exercise caution and take the usual precautions such as locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight. Bring proper gear and equipment for your outdoor activities and prepare for potential emergency situations.

Health in Four Corners

High altitude can cause altitude sickness in those not accustomed to it, so if you are hiking or camping at high elevations, be mindful of the symptoms of altitude sickness and take appropriate precautions. Also, stay hydrated and pack enough sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun’s rays.

Getting to Four Corners: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Four Corners

  • Grand Junction Regional Airport (GJT): Located in Grand Junction, Colorado, this airport is the largest in the area with frequent direct flights from major U.S. cities.
  • Durango-La Plata County Airport (DRO): This airport serves the Four Corners region and is located in Durango, Colorado.
  • Albuquerque International Sunport (ABQ): Located in Albuquerque, New Mexico, this airport offers flights to major U.S. cities and is less than 5 hours’ drive to Four Corners.

Bus Stations Near Four Corners

  • Greyhound: There are several Greyhound stations serving the Four Corners area, including Grand Junction, Durango, and Farmington.

Other Options

  • Scenic Drives: Four Corners is easily accessible by car and features several scenic routes, including the San Juan Skyway, which winds through the San Juan Mountains.

How to Get Around in Four Corners

While renting a car is the best option for independence and flexibility, there are other modes of transport available, including rental bicycles, shuttle buses, and local tour companies that provide transportation services for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Four Corners: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Arches National Park: This iconic national park is located a few hours away from Four Corners and boasts over 2,000 stunning naturally formed arches.
  • Santa Fe, New Mexico: Known for its rich art scene, stunning architecture, and delicious cuisine, Santa Fe is a must-visit destination when near Four Corners.
  • Lake Powell: This stunning reservoir located in Page, Arizona is a popular destination for boating, fishing, hiking, and camping.

Conclusion

Four Corners offers a unique travel experience for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers. Boasting some of the most beautiful landscapes in America, a rich cultural heritage, and plenty of outdoor activities, this destination is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors. Plan your next trip to Four Corners now and explore everything this unique region has to offer!
