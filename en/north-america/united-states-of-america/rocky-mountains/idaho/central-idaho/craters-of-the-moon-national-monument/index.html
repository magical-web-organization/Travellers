The Ultimate Travel Guide to Craters of the Moon National Monument

Introduction to Craters of the Moon National Monument

Craters of the Moon National Monument is a unique place located in Central Idaho’s Rocky Mountains. It is a volcanic landscape that was formed thousands of years ago by a series of eruptions. The area is covered with black, barren lava flows, and a diverse range of flora and fauna that has adapted to the harsh environment. The park offers plenty of opportunities to hike, bike, and explore the rugged terrain.

List of Places to Visit in Craters of the Moon National Monument

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Visitor Center: The visitor center is the starting point for any Craters of the Moon visit. It has exhibits that provide historical, geological, and eco-focused context for the park.

Natural Attractions of Craters of the Moon National Monument: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lava Flow Trail: The Lava Flow Trail is a 1.6-mile hike that gives visitors a view of the park’s primary landscape. The easy-rated hike is perfect for families with small children.

  • Inferno Cone Trail: The Inferno Cone Trail is a short, but strenuous hike up to the top of a volcanic cinder cone. From the top, visitors can enjoy a 360-degree view of the park.

  • Tree Molds Trail: The Tree Molds Trail is a 1.5-mile hike that showcases the park’s unique tree molds. Visitors can see the outline of trees that were once engulfed in lava.

  • Caves Trail: The Caves Trail is a half-mile hike that leads visitors to two lava tubes. Visitors can explore these dark tunnels and experience another side of the park.

  • Big Craters Trail: The Big Craters Trail is a 4-mile loop hike that takes you past multiple craters and provides stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Craters of the Moon National Monument

  • Wilderness Trail: The Wilderness Trail is a 6-mile loop trail, which has stunning views of red cinders, lava fields, and ponderosa pine forests. You can hike it or mountain bike it.

Best Things to Do in Craters of the Moon National Monument: Activities and Tours

  • Junior Ranger Program: The Junior Ranger program is aimed at children aged 5-12 and provides a fun and educational experience to learn more about the park.

  • Lava Tubes Tour: The park offers ranger-led tours of the lava tubes. On this tour, visitors can explore the underground area of the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Craters of the Moon National Monument

Must-Try Dishes

There are limited dining options within the park, but you will find basic snacks, drinks, and groceries at the park’s visitor center.

Local Beverages

Visitors can find a selection of sodas, water, and juice drinks at the park’s visitor center.

What to Buy in Craters of the Moon National Monument: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There is a small gift shop at the park’s visitor center, which sells a range of souvenirs, including t-shirts, hats, and other items that reflect the park’s natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Craters of the Moon National Monument

The park is a sacred place for the local tribes, so visitors are asked to treat the park with respect and be mindful of its importance to the local communities.

Best Time to Visit Craters of the Moon National Monument

The park is open all year, but the best time to visit is from May to September, when the weather is warmer and drier. This period is ideal for hiking and exploring the park’s outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Craters of the Moon National Monument

Do:

  • Visit the visitor center for park orientation and exhibits.
  • Wear sturdy shoes and dress in layers for unpredictable weather.
  • Carry plenty of water, food, sunscreen, insect repellent, and first-aid kits with you.

Don’t:

  • Climb on rocks and mountains; it is dangerous and forbidden.
  • Leave marks or damage the environment.
  • Remove natural items.

Safety and Health

Safety in Craters of the Moon National Monument

The park is safe if visitors follow the park’s rules, stay on the trail, avoid climbing on rocks, and keep the environment clean.

Health in Craters of the Moon National Monument

Visitors to the park should carry plenty of water, food, and other necessities because of limited services. The park’s high altitude may also cause altitude sickness for some visitors.

Getting to Craters of the Moon National Monument: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Craters of the Moon National Monument

The nearest airports are Pocatello Regional Airport (PIH) and the Idaho Falls Regional Airport (IDA).

Bus Stations Near Craters of the Moon National Monument

The nearest bus station is Greyhound Bus Station in Pocatello.

Other Options

Visitors can also use private transportation or rent a car.

How to Get Around in Craters of the Moon National Monument

The park’s shuttle bus provides transportation within the park during peak season, and visitors can rent mountain bikes or bring their own for exploration.

Where to Go Next from Craters of the Moon National Monument: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Idaho Falls: Located only 100 miles east of Craters of the Moon is Idaho Falls, a bustling city with a downtown area full of restaurants, bars, and shops, perfect for a day trip.

  • Sawtooth National Forest: If you are interested in more outdoor adventures after leaving Craters of the Moon, Sawtooth National Forest is just 160 miles to the northwest, offering endless hiking opportunities amid breathtaking mountain views.

Conclusion

Craters of the Moon National Monument is an amazing park full of natural landscapes, beautiful trails, and unique features. From the visitor center to the park’s hikes, there are plenty of things to do and see. Visitors can also explore nearby cities and forests for interesting excursions, making a perfect addition to any holiday itinerary.
