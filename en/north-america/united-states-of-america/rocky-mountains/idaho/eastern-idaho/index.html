Introduction to Eastern Idaho

Eastern Idaho is a region located in the Rocky Mountains of the United States of America. Its charm lies in its natural beauty, fascinating history, and abundance of adventure activities. Home to Yellowstone National Park and the Grand Teton National Park, Eastern Idaho is a must-visit for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Eastern Idaho.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Idaho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eastern Idaho has a rich history that allows visitors to explore the region’s cultural significance. Notable places to visit include:

  • Museum of Idaho in Idaho Falls is a state-of-the-art facility that hosts several world-class exhibitions throughout the year.
  • The Idaho Potato Museum in Blackfoot is dedicated to the history and significance of the Idaho potato, one of the state’s biggest exports.
  • Craters of the Moon National Monument and Preserve in Arco features a vast expanse of volcanic landscape that dates back 15,000 years.
  • Eagle Rock Art Museum in Idaho Falls showcases contemporary art exhibits and creative works by local artists.
  • Bonneville County Historical Museum in Idaho Falls features the region’s fascinating history, including exhibits on Native American culture, early pioneers, and the railroad industry.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Idaho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eastern Idaho boasts a wealth of outdoor adventures and scenic views that visitors can enjoy. Here are some highlights:

  • Yellowstone National Park is a world-renowned national park situated in the northwest corner of Wyoming, but it also extends into Eastern Idaho. Its highlights include the Old Faithful geyser, hot springs, waterfalls, and wildlife.
  • Grand Teton National Park in Wyoming features majestic mountain views, hiking trails, and lakes that showcase its natural beauty.
  • Henry’s Fork Snake River is famous for fly fishing and water sports like kayaking and rafting.
  • Mesa Falls Scenic Byway near Ashton offers stunning views of Upper and Lower Mesa Falls, as well as opportunities for hiking, fishing, and camping.
  • Targhee National Forest in Driggs is a popular destination for camping, hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Idaho

Eastern Idaho is a backpacker’s paradise, with many well-maintained trails and routes to choose from. Here are some hiking and biking trails that are worth exploring:

  • Idaho Centennial Trail is a 900-mile path that traverses the state of Idaho, with a section running through Eastern Idaho.
  • Grand Targhee Resort offers scenic hiking trails and mountain bike trails, including beginner to advanced routes.
  • Teton Pass Trail near Victor is a popular trail for mountain biking, with stunning views of the Tetons and the Snake River Valley.
  • Trails in Island Park offer opportunities for scenic hikes, including Yellowstone National Park.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Idaho: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Eastern Idaho that cater to different interests and tastes. Here are some recommendations:

  • Yellowstone Bear World in Rexburg offers visitors the chance to see live bears and other wildlife up close.
  • Lava Hot Springs in Lava Hot Springs features hot springs, spa treatments, and an Olympic-sized swimming pool.
  • Macks Inn Playhouse in Island Park hosts live theater performances and other shows.
  • Astro Van Shuttle Service in Driggs offers visitors transportation to Yellowstone National Park or Grand Teton National Park without the stress of driving.
  • Grand Targhee Resort in Alta offers activities year-round, including skiing, snowboarding, snowshoeing, mountain biking and hiking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Idaho

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to Eastern Idaho:

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Idaho

Must-Try Dishes

Eastern Idaho has a unique cuisine that reflects its location and history. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Finger Steaks are thin strips of beef that are battered and deep-fried, often served with dipping sauces like cocktail sauce or honey mustard.
  • Huckleberry Jam is made from the wild huckleberry, a common fruit in the region that grows in the mountains and forests. It can be used as a spread or a topping for pancakes, ice cream, or yogurt.
  • Potato Skins are a classic appetizer that originated in Eastern Idaho, made from baked potato halves that are topped with cheese, bacon, and sour cream.
  • Trout is a popular fish in the region, caught fresh from the rivers and lakes.

Local Beverages

Eastern Idaho is known for its craft beer and wine. Some breweries and wineries to check out include:

  • Grand Teton Brewing Company in Victor offers a range of craft beers, including IPAs, lagers, and stouts.
  • Terra Nativa Vineyards in Buhl features local wine made from grapes grown in the Snake River Valley.
  • Kangaroo Wine Company in Idaho Falls produces wines made from locally sourced fruit.

Where to Stay in Eastern Idaho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastern Idaho has many options for accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some options:

  • Snake River Lodge and Spa in Teton Village is a luxury hotel and spa that offers ski-in/ski-out access to the slopes and beautiful mountain views.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham Idaho Falls offers affordable accommodations and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Moose Creek Ranch in Victor features rustic cabins and lodges for a cozy mountain getaway.

What to Buy in Eastern Idaho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eastern Idaho offers visitors many options for souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Potatoes are one of Idaho’s biggest exports, and visitors can buy fresh potatoes at local farmer’s markets.
  • Huckleberry products like jam, syrup, and candies are a popular souvenir and a delicious reminder of Eastern Idaho’s unique cuisine.
  • Handmade crafts and jewelry by local artists are also available at markets and boutique shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Idaho

Eastern Idaho’s culture is closely tied to its natural surroundings and history. Visitors can expect friendly locals, a love of the great outdoors, and appreciation for Native American heritage. Tipping is customary in restaurants and bars, with a minimum of 15% recommended.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Idaho

The best time to visit Eastern Idaho is during the summer months (June - August), when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. However, winter is a great time to visit as well, especially for skiing and snowboarding. Fall (September - November) is a quieter season with stunning foliage displays, and spring (March - May) offers opportunities for wildflower viewing and wildlife spotting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Idaho

Do:

  • Do explore the natural attractions and outdoor activities that Eastern Idaho has to offer.
  • Do respect the local wildlife and refrain from feeding or approaching them.
  • Do visit the museums and cultural attractions to learn about Eastern Idaho’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public areas or on hiking trails.
  • Don’t trespass on private property or natural preserves.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions, especially during winter and mountain hiking trips.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Idaho

Eastern Idaho is a safe region overall, but visitors should exercise caution while exploring the outdoors and taking part in adventure activities. Some safety tips to remember:

  • Always carry a map and plan your routes in advance.
  • Carry plenty of water and snacks on hikes or outdoor expeditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather conditions, and be aware of the changing environment.
  • Watch out for wildlife while hiking or camping.

Health in Eastern Idaho

Eastern Idaho has reliable healthcare facilities, but visitors should be prepared for emergencies while exploring remote areas. It’s recommended to get travel insurance before visiting. Visitors should also be aware of the effects of high altitude and take time to acclimate before engaging in strenuous activities. Be mindful of the risks of sun exposure and carry sunscreen and protective clothing.

Getting to Eastern Idaho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Idaho

The closest commercial airport to Eastern Idaho is Idaho Falls Regional Airport (IDA), which offers flights to several US cities. Other significant airports within driving distance include:

  • Jackson Hole Airport (JAC) in Wyoming (40 miles from the Grand Teton National Park),
  • Salt Lake City International Airport (SLC) in Utah.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Idaho

Bus travel options in Eastern Idaho are limited, but some options include:

  • Salt Lake Express
  • Greyhound

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Eastern Idaho via several highways, including I-15 and US-20. Car rentals are available at the airport and other locations.

How to Get Around in Eastern Idaho

The best way to get around Eastern Idaho is by car. Public transportation options are limited, and many of the natural attractions and outdoor activities require a vehicle. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in other major cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Idaho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eastern Idaho is well-connected to neighboring states like Wyoming, Montana, and Utah, offering many opportunities for nearby excursions.

  • Jackson Hole, Wyoming is about an hour’s drive from Teton Village and features world-class skiing, hiking, and shopping.

  • Bozeman, Montana is a three-hour drive from Island Park, and home to many attractions like the Museum of the Rockies, downtown shops, and parks.

  • Salt Lake City, Utah is a four-hour drive from Idaho Falls and offers many attractions, including Temple Square, hiking trails, and shopping.

Conclusion

Eastern Idaho is a beautiful region that offers something for every traveler. From the stunning national parks and outdoor adventures to the rich history and unique cuisine, there’s plenty to discover. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Eastern Idaho, and explore all the wonders this region has in store for you.
