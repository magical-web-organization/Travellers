Introduction to Rexburg

Nestled in the Rocky Mountains of Eastern Idaho, Rexburg is a small yet vibrant city that boasts of stunning natural landscapes, thriving food and drink scene, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast seeking adventure, a history buff looking to learn more about the local culture, or a foodie in search of the finest gastronomic delights, Rexburg has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Rexburg, we will take you on a virtual tour of the city’s most iconic attractions, hidden gems, and off-the-beaten-path destinations. So, pack your bags, bring your camera, and prepare to discover the best of Rexburg!

List of Places to Visit in Rexburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rexburg is steeped in a rich cultural heritage, and its museums and historical sites are a testament to the city’s past and present. Some must-see culturally significant attractions include:

  • Legacy Flight Museum
  • Teton Flood Museum
  • Hemming Village
  • Idaho’s Teton Valley Heritage Center
  • Brigham Young University-Idaho

Natural Attractions of Rexburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rexburg’s scenic surroundings make it an ideal location for outdoor activities of all kinds, from hiking and biking to fishing and camping. Make sure you visit some of these following natural attractions during your stay:

  • Yellowstone Bear World
  • Mesa Falls Scenic Byway
  • St. Anthony Sand Dunes
  • Cascade Reservoir
  • Heise Hot Springs

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rexburg

Rexburg offers several hiking and biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of the region. These are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes:

  • Henry’s Fork Trail
  • R Mountain Trail
  • Grand Teton National Park
  • Yellowstone National Park
  • Table Rock Trail

Best Things to Do in Rexburg: Activities and Tours

Rexburg offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. From thrilling outdoor adventures to cultural tours and sights, you won’t run out of things to do. Some recommended activities and tours include:

  • Kayaking and canoeing on the Snake River
  • Horseback riding
  • Bison safaris
  • ATV tours
  • Nature hikes and guided walks

Week-Long Itinerary for Rexburg

For a comprehensive week-long itinerary for Rexburg, consider the following activities and places to visit:

Day 1 Legacy Flight Museum, Hemming Village, and lunch at The Hickory

Food and Drink Scene of Rexburg

Rexburg is home to some of the best cafes, restaurants, and bars in Idaho. With a diverse range of culinary options available, you are sure to satisfy your taste buds no matter what you’re craving. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild West Caesar Salad from The Hickory
  • Bison chili from Seven Restaurant
  • Blue Moose pizza from Blue Moose Pizzeria
  • Huckleberry cheesecake from The Royal Wolf
  • Dutch oven pot roast from The Millhollow Restaurant

Local Beverages

  • Milkshakes from Frosty Gator
  • Beer from Snow Eagle Brewing & Grill
  • Huckleberry soda from Brigham Tea

What to Buy in Rexburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to take home, Rexburg offers plenty of options that are sure to please. Some local products to consider include:

  • Huckleberry jam and syrup
  • Idaho-shaped cutting boards
  • Souvenir t-shirts and hoodies
  • Hand-woven baskets and rugs by local artisans

Local Culture and Customs of Rexburg

Rexburg is known for its friendly, hospitable locals, who are always happy to welcome visitors. Some cultural customs and practices that locals observe include:

  • Avoiding alcohol consumption
  • Regular church attendance
  • Emphasis on family values and community involvement

Best Time to Visit Rexburg

The best time to visit Rexburg is during the summer months (June-August) when the temperatures are mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the fall season (September-November) offers beautiful foliage colors, and the winter months (December-February) offer skiing and snow activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rexburg

Do:

  • Respect local customs and practices
  • Be prepared for various weather changes
  • Enjoy the local food and drink scene

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public or on BYU-I campus
  • Disrespect the natural resources and wildlife
  • Leave trash behind in parks and hiking trails

Safety and Health

Safety in Rexburg

Rexburg is a safe city, with little to no crime. However, it’s always best to stay vigilant and be aware of your surroundings when traveling to unfamiliar areas.

Health in Rexburg

Rexburg has several medical facilities and hospitals available for any health emergencies. Consult with your doctor before traveling if you are immunocompromised.

Getting to Rexburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rexburg

The nearest major airports to Rexburg are:

  • Idaho Falls Regional Airport (IDA)
  • Jackson Hole Airport (JAC)
  • Salt Lake City International Airport (SLC)

Bus Stations Near Rexburg

Greyhound Bus Lines services Rexburg. Contact them for specific route information and departure times.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is popular to get around Rexburg
  • Taxis are available in Rexburg

How to Get Around Rexburg

Rexburg is a small city that can easily be navigated on foot or by bike. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Rexburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Rexburg, Idaho, is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. With its exceptional natural attractions, delicious food and drink scene, and rich cultural heritage, it’s the perfect destination for your next trip. Plan accordingly with this comprehensive travel guide to Rexburg and create an unforgettable experience.
