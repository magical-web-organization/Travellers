Introduction to Idaho Falls

Idaho Falls, located in eastern Idaho, is a charming city known for its scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Nestled in the Rocky Mountains, Idaho Falls offers visitors the best of both worlds - from exploring the great outdoors to immersing oneself in the city’s rich history and culture.

With its welcoming locals, historic sites, and magnificent natural scenery, Idaho Falls is a must-visit destination for travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Idaho Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Idaho: This museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It features exhibits on the region’s geology, natural history, and cultural heritage.

  • The Art Museum of Eastern Idaho: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the work of regional artists through various exhibits and art classes.

  • Idaho Falls Greenbelt: This beautiful park is home to a number of historic sites, including the Snake River Greenbelt, the Idaho Falls River Walk, and the Idaho Falls Zoo.

Natural Attractions of Idaho Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yellowstone Bear World: This park offers visitors a chance to get up close and personal with Yellowstone’s wildlife, including bears, elk, and moose.

  • Snake River: Take a stroll along the scenic Snake River Greenbelt or go kayaking on its rapids for a memorable outdoor adventure.

  • Mesa Falls Scenic Byway: Take a drive along this picturesque byway to enjoy stunning views of the Teton Mountains and Mesa Falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Idaho Falls

  • The Kelly Canyon Trail is a scenic hiking and biking trail in the Teton Mountains. It offers stunning views of the surrounding area and is perfect for hikers of all ability levels.

  • The River Walk Trail is a popular hiking and biking trail that runs along the Snake River. It offers breathtaking views of the river and is great for a family-friendly outing.

Best Things to Do in Idaho Falls: Activities and Tours

  • Tandem Paragliding: Experience the thrill of soaring through the skies with a tandem paragliding tour.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Enjoy stunning views of the Rocky Mountains while floating above Idaho Falls in a hot air balloon.

  • Idaho Falls Food Tour: Take a culinary tour of Idaho Falls and taste the city’s famous dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Idaho Falls

Here is an example itinerary for a week-long stay in Idaho Falls:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Museum of Idaho and explore the city’s historic sites.
Day 2 Hike along the River Walk Trail and visit the Idaho Falls Zoo.
Day 3 Take a hot air balloon ride over the Teton Mountains.
Day 4 Explore Yellowstone Bear World and have a picnic at the Snake River.
Day 5 Go kayaking on the Snake River and visit the Art Museum of Eastern Idaho.
Day 6 Join a tandem paragliding tour and watch the sunset from atop the Teton Mountains.
Day 7 Spend the day shopping for souvenirs and tasting the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Idaho Falls

Must-Try Dishes

  • Finger Steaks: This Idaho staple is a must-try for meat lovers. Finger steaks are thin slices of steak that are battered and fried.

  • Potatoes: Idaho is famous for its potatoes, and Idaho Falls is no exception. Be sure to try the local chefs’ potato dishes.

  • Huckleberries: Huckleberries are a regional delicacy and can be found in a variety of dishes, including jams, pies, and syrups.

Local Beverages

  • Idaho Wine: Idaho has been producing award-winning wines for years. Be sure to try some of the local wines during your visit.

  • Craft Beer: Idaho Falls is home to several craft breweries, offering visitors a chance to sample unique local brews.

Where to Stay in Idaho Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Hilton Garden Inn Idaho Falls: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Idaho Falls, and offers guests modern amenities and stunning views of the river.

  • Motel 6 Idaho Falls: This budget-friendly hotel is perfect for travelers looking for a simple, no-frills accommodation option.

  • The Cottage Inn: This guesthouse offers visitors a chance to experience life in Idaho Falls like a local. The rooms are cozy, and the decor is rustic and charming.

What to Buy in Idaho Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Potatoes: Take home some Idaho spuds and cook up your own potato dishes at home.

  • Huckleberry Products: Be sure to pick up some huckleberry jam, syrup, or candy to give as a sweet souvenir to friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Idaho Falls

Idaho Falls is known for its friendly locals and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors should be respectful of the culture and customs of the region, and dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Idaho Falls

Summer is the peak season for tourism in Idaho Falls, due to the comfortable weather and outdoor activities. However, visitors can enjoy the region’s beauty year-round, with winter activities such as skiing and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Idaho Falls

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and natural scenery.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

  • Be respectful of the locals and their culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.

  • Ignore signs warning of dangerous wildlife.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing or attire in cultural or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Idaho Falls

Idaho Falls is a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should exercise basic safety precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Idaho Falls

Visitors to Idaho Falls should be aware of the region’s high elevation and dry climate. Drink plenty of water and bring sunscreen and lip balm to protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Idaho Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Idaho Falls

  • Idaho Falls Regional Airport: Located just 4 miles from downtown, the Idaho Falls Regional Airport is the most convenient option for visitors.

  • Salt Lake City International Airport: Salt Lake City International Airport is approximately 200 miles from Idaho Falls and offers a wider range of flight options.

Bus Stations Near Idaho Falls

  • Salt Lake Express: This bus company offers daily service between Idaho Falls and Salt Lake City.

  • Greyhound: Greyhound also offers daily service to Idaho Falls.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Idaho Falls, take a train, or rent a car.

How to Get Around in Idaho Falls

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Idaho Falls, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also use taxis or ride-sharing apps for convenient transportation around the city.

Where to Go Next from Idaho Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Teton National Park: Located just a short drive from Idaho Falls, Grand Teton National Park is known for its stunning mountain ranges and wildlife.

  • Yellowstone National Park: Yellowstone National Park, located just a few hours’ drive from Idaho Falls, offers visitors a chance to see America’s first national park.

Conclusion

Idaho Falls is a beautiful city with a rich culture and history. From its scenic beauty to its outdoor adventures, visitors are sure to find something to love about this charming destination. Be sure to plan your itinerary ahead of time and make the most of your visit to Idaho Falls.
