Introduction to Shoshone

Located in South Central Idaho nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Shoshone is a small city with an abundance of natural wonders and cultural highlights to discover. Shoshone offers visitors a chance to explore the great outdoors, including winding rivers, majestic mountains and endless forests. In this travel guide, we provide you with a comprehensive list of the best places to visit, must-try local dishes, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Shoshone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Idaho’s Wing of the Air Museum

For aviation enthusiasts, the Idaho’s Wing of the Air Museum is a must-visit. The museum chronicles the history of aviation in Idaho, including WWII fighters, vintage planes, helicopters and many more.

Shoshone Ice Caves

The Shoshone Ice Caves are a unique geological phenomenon made up of underground volcanic chambers filled with ice. Visitors can explore these caves with a guided tour and marvel at the beauty of this natural wonder.

The Mammoth Exhibit

The Mammoth Exhibit is a fascinating insight into the history of these prehistoric creatures and their connection to the area. Visitors can learn about the local history of the area and see real fossils up close.

Natural Attractions of Shoshone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Craters Of The Moon National Monument

Craters Of The Moon National Monument is a unique landscape of volcanic fields with amazing black lava formations. Explore the hiking trails, experience the lava tube, and enjoy the striking views of the surrounding mountains.

The Snake River Canyon

The Snake River Canyon is one of Utah’s most impressive natural wonders, famous for its winding river, majestic vistas, unparalleled geology, and beautiful scenery.

Sawtooth National Forest

Sawtooth National Forest is a paradise for outdoor adventurers who enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and camping. Here visitors can experience the great outdoors amidst the breathtaking views of the majestic mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shoshone

Craters of the Moon Loop Trail

One of the most iconic trails in the city is the Craters of the Moon Loop Trail. This 1.5-mile loop takes hikers through the lava fields and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Perrine Coulee Falls Trail

The Perrine Coulee Falls Trail is a hiking and biking trail that offers spectacular views of the Snake River Canyon and beyond. At 1.75 miles, this moderate hike is perfect for those looking to challenge themselves while soaking in the stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Shoshone: Activities and Tours

Shoshone Falls Park

Shoshone Falls Park is a popular attraction famous for its 212-feet-wide waterfall. Enjoy scenic picnic areas, swimming, kayaking, and fishing while enjoying the breathtaking views.

Guided Horseback Riding Tours

Experience the beauty of the Rocky Mountains on a guided horseback riding tour. Local companies offer tours ranging from a few hours to a full-day that allows visitors to fully immerse themselves in the natural beauty of Shoshone.

Food and Drink Scene of Shoshone

Must-Try Dishes

Huckleberry Pie

A local delicacy, huckleberry pie is a must-try dessert. This sweet and fruity pie is a staple of the region throughout the summer months.

Finger steaks

This unique, deep-fried steak is one of Idaho’s signature dishes and a popular local favorite. The dish has become a staple of most local restaurants and is often served with French fries and coleslaw.

Local Beverages

Idaho Wine

Idaho has a burgeoning wine scene, where visitors can enjoy tastings from local vineyards. Try the crisp and acidic white wines that are grown in Idaho’s high altitude.

Where to Stay in Shoshone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Shoshone

Located in the heart of Shoshone, this historic hotel offers guests a comfortable stay and access to all of the popular attractions. The hotel boasts a range of rooms, free WiFi, and an on-site restaurant.

River Canyon Inn

This family-owned inn boasts elegant decor, free WiFi, and an outdoor hot tub. The River Canyon Inn is the perfect place to relax after a day of exploring Shoshone’s natural beauty.

What to Buy in Shoshone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huckleberry Products

Huckleberries are a staple of the region, and local stores offer a range of products made from these delectable berries. Visitors can buy huckleberry jam, honey, and even huckleberry-infused spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Shoshone

The local culture of Shoshone is heavily influenced by the region’s Native American heritage. Visitors can experience this culture through music, festivals, and art that are held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Shoshone

The best time to visit Shoshone is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is mild and pleasant, making it the perfect time to explore the outdoors without worrying about the harsh winter conditions in the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shoshone

Do:

  • Respect the environment and the wildlife.
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks and outdoor areas.
  • Ignore the safety instructions and regulations of outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shoshone

Even though Shoshone is a relatively safe city, visitors should be cautious of their surroundings and take appropriate safety measures while hiking or camping in the wilderness.

Health in Shoshone

Visitors should also be aware of potential health risks related to outdoor activities, such as sunburns, insect bites, and altitude sickness. Be sure to drink plenty of water and use sunscreen while exploring the great outdoors.

Getting to Shoshone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shoshone

The closest airport to Shoshone is Magic Valley Regional Airport in Twin Falls, just a 25-minute drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Shoshone

There are several bus stations in Twin Falls, which is only a 30-minute drive from Shoshone. Visitors can take bus services from Twin Falls to Shoshone.

Other Options

The best way to get around Shoshone is by car. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or use the local transportation services.

How to Get Around in Shoshone

Visitors can rent cars or bikes to travel around the city, or use the local transportation services such as taxis and buses.

Where to Go Next from Shoshone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sun Valley

Located an hour and a half away from Shoshone, Sun Valley is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts and boasts an extensive range of ski resorts, restaurants, and shops.

Boise

Located two hours away by car, Boise is a beautiful city that visitors can explore. Experience the local arts and culture scene, explore the historic district, or enjoy the bustling nightlife.

Conclusion

Shoshone is a beautiful city located in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, full of natural wonders and cultural highlights. Visitors can experience the great outdoors, explore museums and historical sites, try local cuisine and beverages, and enjoy a variety of activities to make their visit unforgettable. Start planning your trip to Shoshone and discover the charm of this hidden gem.
