Introduction to Twin Falls

Twin Falls is a metropolitan city located in South Central Idaho. It’s a popular tourist destination because of its exceptional natural beauty, as it is nestled in the Snake River Canyon. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, rafting, and other outdoor activities, as well as exploring museums and other cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Twin Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Perrine Bridge
  • Shoshone Falls
  • Herrett Center for Arts and Science
  • Twin Falls County Historical Museum

Natural Attractions of Twin Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Snake River Canyon Rim Trail
  • Centennial Waterfront Park
  • Pillar Falls
  • Auger Falls

Hiking/Biking Routes of Twin Falls

For nature lovers, Twin Falls has plenty of hiking and biking trails to enjoy. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Canyon Rim Trail
  • Auger Falls Trail
  • Centennial Waterfront Park Trail
  • Pillar Falls Trail

Best Things to Do in Twin Falls: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Snake River
  • Kayaking on the Snake River
  • Zip-lining at the Zip Idaho
  • Touring the historic Idaho Base and Jump (IBJ) site

Week-Long Itinerary for Twin Falls

Day 1: Visit Perrine Bridge and Shoshone Falls.

Day 2: Explore Twin Falls County Historical Museum and Herrett Center for Arts and Science.

Day 3: Enjoy a day of hiking on the Canyon Rim Trail and Auger Falls Trail.

Day 4: Take a rafting or kayaking tour on the Snake River and see the Pillar Falls.

Day 5: Zip-line with Zip Idaho Thrill Park.

Day 6: Visit the historic Idaho Base and Jump site.

Day 7: Relax at Centennial Waterfront Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Twin Falls

Western American warmth and hospitality are the hallmarks of Twin Falls culture. Remember to greet your neighbors warmly on the street, at cafes, or other public places. It is advisable always to show respect towards local cultures and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Twin Falls

The best time to visit Twin Falls is in-between June and August when the weather is mild and sunny. This also coincides well with outdoor activities such as hiking and rafting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Twin Falls

Do:

  • Respect for the local culture and customs
  • Try local foods and drinks
  • Follow responsible tourism practices such as not littering and following marked trails while hiking.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the animals or interact with them.
  • Leave your trash in the outdoors or destroy plants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Twin Falls

Twin Falls is generally a safe place to visit, although you should always exercise common sense and take precautions.

Health in Twin Falls

There are good medical facilities available throughout Twin Falls, but it is still advisable to have travel insurance during your trip.

Getting to Twin Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Twin Falls

  • Boise Airport (BOI) - Twin Falls is about two hours away from BOI.
  • Twin Falls Regional Airport (TWF) - Serves the city of Twin Falls.

Bus Stations Near Twin Falls

  • Salt Lake Express
  • Greyhound Bus Lines

Other Options

  • Drive: Twin Falls is about a two-hour drive from Boise, Idaho.
  • Passenger Train: Amtrak

How to Get Around in Twin Falls

Twin Falls has a reliable and affordable public transportation system. However, renting a car or biking is a better option, especially for exploring the best outdoor sites.
