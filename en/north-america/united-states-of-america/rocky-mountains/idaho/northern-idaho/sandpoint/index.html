Introduction to Sandpoint

Sandpoint is a beautiful town in Northern Idaho, situated in the Rocky Mountains of the United States. With stunning natural scenery and a rich cultural heritage, Sandpoint is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking for a peaceful escape from everyday life.

This comprehensive travel guide will take you on a virtual tour of Sandpoint, showing you the top sights and attractions, the best places to eat and drink, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Sandpoint

Sandpoint is home to a range of diverse attractions, from world-class museums and historical landmarks to breathtaking natural scenery and outdoor adventures. Here are just a few of the must-see sights and activities in and around Sandpoint:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sandpoint has a rich cultural heritage, with a number of fascinating museums and historical sites to explore. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Sandpoint:

  • Bonner County History Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and heritage of Bonner County and the surrounding area. Highlights include exhibits on Native American culture, early settlement, mining, and logging.
  • Panida Theater: Built in 1927, the Panida Theater is a historic landmark in Sandpoint and hosts a variety of live performances and events throughout the year.
  • Schweitzer Mountain Resort: This ski resort is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, but it’s also home to a number of cultural attractions, including art galleries and a museum showcasing the area’s mining history.

Natural Attractions of Sandpoint: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sandpoint is nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, surrounded by stunning natural scenery and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in Sandpoint:

  • Lake Pend Oreille: This crystal-clear lake is the largest in Idaho, and it offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including boating, fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Schweitzer Mountain: This mountain offers year-round recreational opportunities, including skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and mountain biking.
  • Farragut State Park: Located just a short drive from Sandpoint, Farragut State Park is home to over 4,000 acres of pristine wilderness, including miles of hiking and biking trails, stunning views of Lake Pend Oreille, and a variety of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandpoint

Sandpoint is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with hundreds of miles of trails and backcountry routes to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Sandpoint:

  • Mickinnick Trail: This scenic trail offers stunning views of Lake Pend Oreille and is a popular destination for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Pine Street Woods: This community forest has over 40 miles of hiking and biking trails, with options for all skill levels.
  • Long Bridge Bike Path: This paved bike path follows the shore of Lake Pend Oreille and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and water.

Best Things to Do in Sandpoint: Activities and Tours

Sandpoint offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in and around Sandpoint:

  • Visit the Cedar Street Bridge Public Market: This unique marketplace features a variety of local vendors, selling everything from artisanal cheeses and fresh produce to handmade crafts and jewelry.
  • Take a scenic flight over the mountains: Sandpoint is home to a number of air tour operators, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape from above.
  • Go fishing on Lake Pend Oreille: With over 111 miles of shoreline and dozens of species of fish, Lake Pend Oreille is a popular destination for anglers.

Food and Drink Scene of Sandpoint

Sandpoint’s food and drink scene is a highlight for many visitors to the area. Here are some of the top dishes and beverages to try in Sandpoint:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry ice cream: This local favorite features the tart flavor of huckleberries, which are native to the Pacific Northwest.
  • Idaho potato dishes: Idaho is known for its delicious potatoes, which can be found in a variety of dishes throughout Sandpoint, from mashed potatoes to potato pancakes.
  • Bison burgers: Bison meat is a common ingredient in Sandpoint’s burgers, offering a unique and tasty twist on a classic favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Pend d’Oreille Winery: This local winery offers a variety of award-winning wines, made with grapes grown in the area.
  • Laughing Dog Brewing: This craft brewery specializes in unique and flavorful beers, including the popular Devil Dog Imperial IPA.
  • MickDuff’s Brewing Company: This brewpub offers a range of delicious beers, made with local ingredients and served in a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.

Where to Stay in Sandpoint: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sandpoint offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top places to stay in Sandpoint:

  • Best Western Plus: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor pool, all within walking distance of Sandpoint’s downtown area.
  • La Quinta Inn: This budget-friendly hotel features clean and comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a pet-friendly policy.
  • Syringa Lodge: This cozy and inviting guesthouse offers stunning views of Lake Pend Oreille, a hot tub, and easy access to outdoor activities and attractions.

What to Buy in Sandpoint: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sandpoint is home to a variety of local products and souvenirs, making it easy to find the perfect gift or memento of your trip. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home from Sandpoint:

  • Huckleberry jam or syrup
  • Local wines or craft beers
  • Handmade crafts and artwork from the Cedar Street Bridge Public Market

Local Culture and Customs of Sandpoint

Sandpoint is known for its friendly and welcoming atmosphere, as well as its rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Sandpoint should be aware of the local customs and traditions, including:

  • Idahoans are known for their love of the outdoors, so dress accordingly and be prepared for outdoor activities.
  • Sandpoint is a laid-back and relaxed town, so take your time and enjoy the slower pace of life.
  • Sandpoint has a thriving arts and culture scene, so be sure to check out the local galleries and theaters for live performances and exhibits.

Best Time to Visit Sandpoint

Sandpoint is a year-round destination, with something to offer visitors in every season. However, the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the best times to visit Sandpoint based on the season:

  • Fall: This is a great time to visit Sandpoint, as the leaves start to change and the weather cools down. It’s also a less crowded time of year, so you can enjoy the area’s natural beauty without the crowds.
  • Winter: Sandpoint is a popular destination for skiing and snowboarding, with Schweitzer Mountain Resort offering world-class slopes and amenities.
  • Spring: This is a great time to visit Sandpoint if you’re interested in outdoor activities like hiking and biking, as the weather is mild and the trails are less crowded.
  • Summer: Sandpoint really comes alive in the summer, with a variety of outdoor concerts and festivals, as well as opportunities for swimming, boating, and other water activities on Lake Pend Oreille.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandpoint

To ensure a memorable and positive experience in Sandpoint, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area, whether by hiking, biking, or taking a scenic drive.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including huckleberry dishes and local wines and craft beers.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, including a love of the outdoors and a laid-back, relaxed pace of life.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities, including proper gear and following trail signs and regulations.
  • Be disruptive or disrespectful to locals or the natural environment.
  • Engage in excessive drinking or other risky behaviors that could have negative consequences for yourself or others.

Safety and Health

Sandpoint is a safe and welcoming town, but it’s still important to take precautions to stay safe and healthy during your stay.

Safety in Sandpoint

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Follow all safety precautions and regulations when hiking, biking, skiing, or participating in other recreational activities.
  • Be aware of any potential wildlife hazards, including bears and other large animals.

Health in Sandpoint

  • Be sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and sunscreen to prevent sunburn and other heat-related illnesses.
  • If you have any pre-existing health conditions, make sure to bring any necessary medications and consult with local healthcare providers if needed.

Getting to Sandpoint: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sandpoint is located in Northern Idaho, about 70 miles from Spokane, Washington. Here are some options for getting to Sandpoint:

Airports Near Sandpoint

  • Spokane International Airport: This airport is located about 90 miles from Sandpoint and offers a range of domestic and international flights.
  • Coeur d’Alene Airport: This smaller airport is located about 35 miles from Sandpoint and offers limited commercial flights.

Bus Stations Near Sandpoint

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This national bus service has a station in Sandpoint, offering connections to other cities throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Driving: Sandpoint is easily accessible via car, with major highways and scenic routes leading to the town.
  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station is located in Sandpoint’s sister city, Ponderay, which is about 3 miles south of Sandpoint.

How to Get Around in Sandpoint

Sandpoint is a small and walkable town, with most attractions and amenities located within a short distance of each other. Visitors can also take advantage of public transportation options, including:

  • Sandpoint Trolley: This charming trolley offers tours of Sandpoint’s historic downtown area, as well as a variety of other destinations.
  • Lake Pend Oreille Cruises: This tour company offers scenic boat cruises on Lake Pend Oreille, with options for dinner and sunset cruises.

Where to Go Next from Sandpoint: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sandpoint is a great home base for exploring the surrounding area, which offers plenty of other sights and attractions to enjoy. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Schweitzer Mountain Resort: This popular ski resort is just a short drive from Sandpoint and offers world-class slopes and amenities.
  • Coeur d’Alene: This charming town is located about 40 miles from Sandpoint and features a variety of outdoor activities, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions.
  • Silverwood Theme Park: Located just a short drive from Sandpoint, this amusement park is a great destination for families and thrill-seekers alike.

Conclusion

Sandpoint, Idaho, is a hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains, offering a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, exploring local history and culture, or simply soaking up the relaxed atmosphere of this charming town, Sandpoint has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your perfect vacation and make memories that will last a lifetime.
