Kellogg, Idaho is a hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains of North America, boasting breathtaking views and endless adventures. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan the ultimate vacation in Kellogg, Idaho.

Introduction to Kellogg

Kellogg is a small city located in Northern Idaho, United States of America. It is nestled in the Coeur d’Alene Mountains, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The city has a population of around 2,000 people and is known for its rich history, stunning scenery, and abundance of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Kellogg

Kellogg may be a small city, but it has plenty to offer visitors. Here are some of the must-visit places in Kellogg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Crystal Gold Mine – take a tour of an authentic gold mine and learn about the mining history of the area.
  • The Oasis Bordello Museum – learn about the infamous history of Kellogg’s red-light district.
  • Silver Mountain Resort – visit this all-season resort for skiing, snowboarding, hiking, biking, and more.

Natural Attractions of Kellogg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lookout Pass Ski and Recreation Area – enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing in the winter, or hiking and mountain biking in the summer.
  • The Trail of the Coeur d’Alenes – a 73-mile-long trail that winds through beautiful scenery and wildlife.
  • Pulaski Tunnel Trail – a hike that takes you through the burnt forest where Ed Pulaski saved the lives of his crew during the Great Fire of 1910.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kellogg

Kellogg is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and offers numerous hiking and biking routes. Here are some popular options:

  • The Pulaski Tunnel Trail – rated as an easy-to-moderate hike, this 4-mile-round-trip trail takes hikers through forests and along creeks.
  • The Trail of the Coeur d’Alenes – as mentioned earlier, this 73-mile-long trail is perfect for biking or hiking.
  • Mineral Ridge Trail – this 3.3-mile-long hike offers stunning views of Lake Coeur d’Alene.

Best Things to Do in Kellogg: Activities and Tours

Kellogg has no shortage of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Kellogg:

  • Take a scenic ride on the Silver Mountain Gondola for stunning views of Kellogg and the surrounding mountains.
  • Book a spot on the Silver Rapids Waterpark, a year-round indoor water park that’s fun for all ages.
  • Spend an afternoon at the Bunker Hill Superfund Site – a massive former lead and zinc mine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kellogg

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kellogg, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Kellogg and take the Silver Mountain Gondola ride
2 Spend the day skiing or snowboarding at Lookout Pass Ski and Recreation Area
3 Explore the Oasis Bordello Museum and learn about Kellogg’s history
4 Hike the Pulaski Tunnel Trail and enjoy the beautiful scenery
5 Take a day trip to Coeur d’Alene and explore the town
6 Book a spot at the Silver Rapids Waterpark
7 Spend the day relaxing at your hotel before departing Kellogg

Food and Drink Scene of Kellogg

Kellogg may be a small city, but it has a thriving food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry French Toast – a local favorite served at Kellogg’s cafes and diners.
  • Bison or Elk Burger – a unique take on the classic hamburger found in local restaurants.
  • Loaded Baked Potato Soup – a heartwarming comfort food available at many Kellogg eateries.

Local Beverages

  • Tope Brothers Coffee – a local coffee roaster that has been around for over a century.
  • Wallace Brewing Company – a local brewery that offers a wide range of craft beers.
  • Huckleberry Shakes – made with locally-sourced huckleberries, this refreshing treat is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Kellogg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kellogg has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Kellogg:

  • Silver Mountain Resort – a four-season resort that offers everything from hotel rooms to condos.
  • Wallace Inn – a charming hotel with modern amenities situated in the heart of downtown Wallace.
  • Stardust Motel – a budget-friendly option that is clean and comfortable.

What to Buy in Kellogg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a souvenir or a unique gift to bring back home, check out these local products:

  • Huckleberry-themed Seattles Best Coffee – a perfect blend of coffee and local huckleberry flavor.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry – local artisans make beautiful jewelry from silver and other precious metals.
  • Silver Mining Relics – Kellogg is known for its mining history, so pick up some relics from mines as a memory of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Kellogg

When in Kellogg, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Kellogg is a small community, so be respectful and courteous to the locals.
  • Most places in Kellogg are cash-only, so be sure to bring enough cash with you.
  • The people of Kellogg are known for their fondness for outdoor activities, so embrace the activities on offer.

Best Time to Visit Kellogg

The best time to visit Kellogg depends on your interests. If you’re interested in skiing and snowboarding, visit in the winter months. If you prefer hiking and outdoor activities, visit in the summer months. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit Kellogg as the leaves on the trees change color.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kellogg

When in Kellogg, here are some things to do and avoid:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities on offer.
  • Explore the city’s mining history.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the locals.
  • Litter or leave a trail behind.
  • Be too loud at night as it disturbs the peace and serenity of Kellogg.

Safety and Health

Kellogg is a relatively safe and healthy community. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Kellogg

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially when outdoors.
  • Follow proper hiking and camping safety protocols, such as staying on designated trails.
  • Be cautious when driving in snowy conditions during the winter months.

Health in Kellogg

  • Be mindful of altitude sickness if engaging in outdoor activities at high elevations.
  • Protect yourself from tick bites when hiking or camping in wooded areas.
  • Bring adequate sunscreen and water for outdoor activities in the summer months.

Getting to Kellogg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kellogg may be a small city, but it’s easily accessible by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Kellogg

  • Spokane International Airport – located approximately 70 miles west of Kellogg.
  • Coeur d’Alene Airport – located approximately 50 miles south of Kellogg.

Bus Stations Near Kellogg

  • Kellogg Greyhound Bus Station – located in downtown Kellogg on McKinley Avenue.

Other Options

  • Renting a car and driving to Kellogg is a convenient option, especially if you plan on exploring the surrounding areas.
  • Hitchhiking is also an option for the adventurous traveler.

How to Get Around in Kellogg

Kellogg is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, renting a car or a bike is also an option if you plan on exploring the surrounding areas. For transportation across town, consider taking a taxi or ride-sharing service.

Where to Go Next from Kellogg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kellogg is located in the northern part of Idaho, making it an ideal starting point for exploring the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Coeur d’Alene – a charming city with an abundance of outdoor activities and scenic beauty.
  • Wallace – a quirky mining town that has been preserved to maintain its historic appearance.
  • Silverwood Theme Park – an amusement park with over 70 rides and attractions.

Conclusion

Kellogg, Idaho is truly a hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains of North America. From its rich history to its stunning scenery and abundance of outdoor activities, Kellogg has something for every traveler. Hopefully, this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable vacation to this beautiful city.
