Introduction to Bonners Ferry

Bonners Ferry is located in Northern Idaho, in the Rocky Mountains, in the United States of America, in North America, and is a small town with a lot to offer. If you’re looking for a peaceful and adventurous getaway, look no further than Bonners Ferry. With a variety of outdoor activities, incredible natural beauty, and a rich cultural history, this town is the perfect destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Bonners Ferry

Bonners Ferry boasts a range of must-see attractions and activities. Here are some of the most popular places to visit while in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boundary County Museum: The museum showcases the history of Bonners Ferry and the surrounding area through displays, exhibits, and artifacts.
  • Kootenai Tribe of Idaho Interpretive Center: This award-winning museum teaches visitors about the Kootenai Tribe’s history, culture, and spirit.
  • Pearl Theater: This historic theater was built in 1921 and still offers live performances to this day.

Natural Attractions of Bonners Ferry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kootenai National Wildlife Refuge: This beautiful refuge is home to a variety of wildlife, including moose, bald eagles, and grizzly bears.
  • Mirror Lake Scenic Area: This stunning lake offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and is a great spot for fishing, hiking, and picnicking.
  • Snow Creek Falls: Located in Bonners Ferry’s Selkirk Mountains, these waterfalls offer a beautiful and peaceful escape into nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bonners Ferry

If you’re looking to get some exercise or simply experience the beauty of the Northern Idaho landscape, Bonners Ferry’s hiking and biking trails are a must-see. Some of the best trails to check out include:

  • Kootenai River Trail: This scenic trail runs along the Kootenai River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and wildlife.
  • Riverside Trail: This trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride, as it follows the Kootenai River and provides access to the town’s many parks and attractions.
  • Myrtle Creek Trail: This trail is a bit more challenging but rewards hikers with stunning views of Myrtle Falls and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Bonners Ferry: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Bonners Ferry has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in town:

  • Fly fishing: The Kootenai River is renowned for its trout fishing, and there are many outfitters in town that can help you enjoy this beloved pastime.
  • Skiing and snowboarding: The nearby Schweitzer Mountain Resort is one of the best ski resorts in the country and offers both day and night skiing.
  • Wildlife watching: Bonners Ferry is surrounded by pristine wilderness, making it the perfect place to spot wildlife such as elk, moose, and bears.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bonners Ferry

Food and Drink Scene of Bonners Ferry

No trip to Bonners Ferry is complete without indulging in the town’s delicious food and drink scene.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry pancakes: Made with local huckleberries, these pancakes are a regional specialty and a must-try.
  • Fry bread: A staple of Native American cuisine, fry bread is a delicious and filling treat.
  • Trout: Freshly caught trout is a popular dish in Bonners Ferry and is often served with local vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry wine: Made with the town’s signature fruit, huckleberry wine is a delicious and unique local beverage.
  • Craft beer: Bonners Ferry is home to several local breweries that offer a range of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Bonners Ferry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bonners Ferry offers a range of accommodation options to fit any budget or preference. Some of the best places to stay in town include:

  • Kootenai River Inn Casino and Spa: This luxurious hotel offers stunning river views and a range of amenities, including a spa, casino, and fine dining.
  • Bonners Ferry Log Inn: This charming log cabin-style inn offers a cozy and comfortable stay, with easy access to the town’s attractions.
  • Campsites: For those looking for a more rustic experience, Bonners Ferry has several campsites and RV parks that allow visitors to connect with nature while still enjoying modern amenities.

What to Buy in Bonners Ferry: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Bonners Ferry

Bonners Ferry is home to a rich history and cultural heritage, with a strong Native American influence. Visitors to town should be respectful of the area’s cultural significance and take the time to learn about the town’s history and traditions.
