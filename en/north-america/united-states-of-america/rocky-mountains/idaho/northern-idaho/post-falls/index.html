Introduction to Post Falls

Located in Northern Idaho, in the Rocky Mountains region, Post Falls is a picturesque town that offers a plethora of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. Named after Frederick Post, the founder of the local lumber industry, the town borders the Spokane River and boasts of stunning waterfalls, lush forests, and remarkable scenic views.

Whether you’re an adventure seeker, nature lover, culture vulture, or just looking for a peaceful getaway, Post Falls is the perfect destination for you. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan a memorable trip to Post Falls.

List of Places to Visit in Post Falls

Make the most of your visit to Post Falls by exploring its many attractions. These are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Post Falls Historical Society Museum - Learn about the town’s rich history and heritage by visiting the Post Falls Historical Society Museum. The museum features exhibits that showcase the town’s early days, the local lumber industry, and more.

  • The Treaty Rock Park - This park is historically significant as it marks the spot where Native American tribes held treaty negotiations with the US government in 1855. The park is a perfect spot for picnics and fishing along the Spokane River.

  • The Jacklin Arts and Cultural Center - The center offers a variety of art exhibits, music events, and cultural performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Post Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Spokane River Centennial Trail - This is a scenic hiking and biking trail along the Spokane River that offers breathtaking views of the river and surrounding areas.

  • Post Falls Dam and Recreation Area - The dam provides a stunning view of the river and offers picnic areas, fishing spots, and water activities like boating and kayaking.

  • Q’emiln Park - This park offers scenic trails for hiking and biking, a playground, and a beach area along the Spokane River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Post Falls

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Post Falls due to the town’s beautiful natural surroundings. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore during your visit:

  • The Post Falls Dam Trail - This easy trail winds along the river and takes you to the dam and recreation area. It is about 2.5 miles long and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding areas.

  • The Riverside State Park Trail - This trail is part of the Centennial Trail and stretches for 37 miles along the Spokane River. It offers breathtaking views of the river and surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Post Falls: Activities and Tours

Post Falls offers a range of activities and tours to suit all interests. Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

  • Go on a Kayaking Adventure - Paddle down the Spokane River and experience the beauty of Post Falls from the water.

  • Go Fishing - Post Falls is a great spot for fishing, with several rivers and lakes nearby.

  • Take a Scenic Drive - Explore the natural beauty of the area with a scenic drive along the nearby highways.

Food and Drink Scene of Post Falls

Post Falls has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers a range of local and international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you should taste during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Pancakes - Made from the local huckleberries, these pancakes are a delicious breakfast item that you don’t want to miss.

  • Famous Northwest Burger - A juicy burger made with locally sourced beef and topped with huckleberry BBQ sauce.

  • Bite Me Pizza - A local pizzeria that serves delicious wood-fired pizzas made with fresh local ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Soda - A sweet and refreshing drink made from the local huckleberries.

  • Idaho Wine - Post Falls is surrounded by excellent wineries that produce premium quality wines that you can taste and buy.

Local Culture and Customs of Post Falls

Post Falls has a rich cultural heritage and a proud history. The local people are friendly and welcoming, and you’ll experience a warm hospitality during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Post Falls

The best time to visit Post Falls is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.
