Introduction to Coeur d’Alene

Located in Northern Idaho within the Rocky Mountains, Coeur d’Alene is a stunning travel destination offering scenic views, outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and more. This guide will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Coeur d’Alene.

List of Places to Visit in Coeur d’Alene

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of North Idaho: This museum offers exhibits showcasing the history of Coeur d’Alene and the surrounding region, including the Coeur d’Alene Tribe, logging, and mining.
  • Old Mission State Park: This state park includes a historic 19th-century Catholic mission and a museum showcasing its history.
  • Coeur d’Alene Public Library: The library hosts events and programs related to the local culture and offers a vast collection of historical documents and books on local history.

Natural Attractions of Coeur d’Alene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coeur d’Alene Lake: With more than 135 miles of shoreline, this crystal clear lake is perfect for swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Tubbs Hill Nature Trails: This nature reserve located in the heart of Coeur d’Alene offers hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the lake and mountains.
  • Silverwood Theme Park: This theme park offers over 70 rides, including roller coasters and an extensive water park.
  • Hiawatha Trail: This 15-mile bike trail offers stunning views of the Bitterroot Mountains with several tunnels and trestles along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coeur d’Alene

  • Centennial Trail: This paved trail stretches 24 miles from Higgens Point to the Washington-Idaho border, following the Spokane River.
  • Mineral Ridge Trail: This trail offers a short and easy hike leading to stunning views of Lake Coeur d’Alene.
  • Canfield Mountain Trail: This moderate trail offers a 5.5-mile hike to the top of the Canfield Mountain, with 360-degree views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Coeur d’Alene: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Rentals: Rent a boat and explore the 100-mile shoreline of Coeur d’Alene Lake.
  • Coaster Pedicab Tours: Explore the city with a guided tour on a pedicab.
  • Safari Aircraft Tour: Fly over the region in a small aircraft and enjoy breathtaking views.
  • Coeur d’Alene Cellars Vineyard and Tasting Room: Visit the vineyard and tasting room for a wine tasting experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coeur d’Alene

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Museum of North Idaho, followed by a relaxing walk around Tubbs Hill Nature Trails.
Day 2 Explore Coeur d’Alene Lake with a rented boat or go fishing.
Day 3 Visit Old Mission State Park and learn about the region’s history.
Day 4 Hike Mineral Ridge Trail for some stunning views of Lake Coeur d’Alene.
Day 5 Visit Silverwood Theme Park and experience the thrilling rides.
Day 6 Take a pedicab tour around the city, followed by a wine tasting at Coeur d’Alene Cellars Vineyard.
Day 7 Fly over the region with Safari Aircraft Tours and enjoy the panoramic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Coeur d’Alene

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Ice Cream: Made from wild huckleberries, this delicious ice cream is a must-try in Coeur d’Alene.
  • Wild Game: Coeur d’Alene offers excellent wild game dishes such as elk, venison, and duck.
  • Steak: The region is known for its high-quality beefsteaks, cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Margarita: This refreshing cocktail is made with local huckleberries.
  • Northern Idaho Huckleberry Wine: This red wine is made from fresh huckleberries grown in the region.
  • Coeur d’Alene Brewing Company: Sample local craft beers at the Coeur d’Alene Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Coeur d’Alene: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Coeur d’Alene Resort: A luxury resort located on the shores of Coeur d’Alene Lake, offering an outdoor pool, a private beach, and a spa.
  • The Roosevelt Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in downtown Coeur d’Alene, offering historic elegance and modern amenities.
  • Adventure Inn: A budget-friendly option offering straightforward rooms, located 2 miles from Coeur d’Alene Lake.

What to Buy in Coeur d’Alene: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Treats: Buy huckleberry jam, jelly, syrup, or candy as a sweet souvenir.
  • Local Artworks: Buy unique artworks made by local artists.
  • Silverwood Theme Park Souvenirs: Buy souvenirs from the Silverwood Theme Park, such as theme park t-shirts, mugs, or hats.

Local Culture and Customs of Coeur d’Alene

  • Respect for Nature: The locals take great pride in the surrounding nature, and they respect it highly, which visitors should do the same.
  • Politeness: The locals are known for being polite and friendly, and visitors should do the same.
  • Wildlife Awareness: The region is home to several wildlife species, including bears, elk, and deer. Visitors should be aware of this and take precautions when necessary.

Best Time to Visit Coeur d’Alene

The best time to visit Coeur d’Alene is between late April to late October when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coeur d’Alene

Do:

  • Respect the Outdoors: Respect the nature and the environment around you while visiting Coeur d’Alene.
  • Try Huckleberry Treats: Taste the huckleberry treats as they are a specialty of the region.
  • Visit the Museums: Visit the museums to learn about the history and culture of Coeur d’Alene.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Littering the natural environment is discouraged.
  • Disrespect the Wildlife: Don’t try to feed the wildlife in the region, as it can be harmful to both the animals and humans.
  • Be Rude: The locals are known for their friendliness, and visitors should be polite and respectful in return.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coeur d’Alene

Although Coeur d’Alene is a safe destination, visitors should take necessary safety precautions when hiking and touring the wilderness trails as there are occasional wildlife sightings.

Health in Coeur d’Alene

The region has excellent medical facilities and hospitals, and visitors are advised to carry appropriate medication and health insurance when traveling.

Getting to Coeur d’Alene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coeur d’Alene

The Spokane International Airport is the closest airport to Coeur d’Alene, located about 30 miles away. Several transportation options are available from the airport, including taxis, rental cars, and shuttle services.

Bus Stations Near Coeur d’Alene

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The Greyhound bus station is located in downtown Coeur d’Alene, providing bus transportation throughout the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Coeur d’Alene, taking Interstate 90 from nearby cities, such as Seattle, Portland, and Salt Lake City.

How to Get Around in Coeur d’Alene

Visitors can rent a car, bike, or take the public transportation available. Local taxis and buses are available throughout the city and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Coeur d’Alene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Spokane: A vibrant city located about 30 miles from Coeur d’Alene, offering cultural and entertainment experiences.
  • Craters of the Moon National Monument: This national monument is located about 180 miles south of Coeur d’Alene, offering stunning volcanic landscapes and hiking trails.
  • Yellowstone National Park: This national park is located about 500 miles east of Coeur d’Alene, offering breathtaking natural wonders, geysers, and wildlife sightings.

Conclusion

Coeur d’Alene is a must-visit travel destination, offering a mix of scenic views, rich history, and outdoor adventure opportunities. This comprehensive guide will help visitors plan a perfect trip to Coeur d’Alene, experiencing the best it has to offer.
