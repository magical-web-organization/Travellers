Introduction to Northern Idaho

Northern Idaho, located in the Rocky Mountains of the United States, offers a blend of natural attractions and cultural experiences to visitors. From outdoor adventures to exploring museums and historical places, there’s something for everyone in this scenic region. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll provide you with a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, the best things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Idaho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, here are some places you should visit:

  1. Museum of North Idaho: Located in Coeur d’Alene, this museum displays exhibits on the history of Kootenai County. You’ll learn about the Native Americans who lived in the region, the lumber industry, and more.
  2. Coeur d’Alene City Park and Independence Point: This park is a must-visit destination with views of Lake Coeur d’Alene. You can also take a stroll around the city’s Independence Point or enjoy a boat ride.
  3. Fort Sherman Museum: Located on the Coeur d’Alene Indian Reservation, the Fort Sherman Museum showcases exhibits and artifacts that highlight the military history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Northern Idaho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Northern Idaho has a lot to offer:

  1. Lake Coeur d’Alene: This lake is one of the most popular attractions in the area, and for a good reason. You can go boating, fishing, or simply enjoy the scenic views.
  2. Silverwood Theme Park: This amusement park features several rides and attractions perfect for families with children.
  3. Spokane River Centennial Trail: This trail stretches over 37 miles and provides a scenic route for biking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Idaho

Northern Idaho offers a range of hiking and biking trails for adventurers of all levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Hiawatha Trail: This 13-mile trail is known for its stunning views of the Bitterroot Mountains. You can rent a bike or bring your own to explore the trail.
  2. Trail of the Coeur d’Alenes: This 73-mile trail offers scenic views of the Silver Valley and provides opportunities for fishing, birdwatching, and wildlife viewing.
  3. English Point Trail System: This trail system offers a variety of routes, ranging from easy to difficult, suitable for hikers and mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in Northern Idaho: Activities and Tours

Northern Idaho offers plenty of activities for visitors, from outdoor adventures to sightseeing tours:

  1. Silverwood Theme Park: This amusement park is a popular destination for families with children and features several rides and attractions.
  2. Lake Coeur d’Alene Cruise: Take a boat tour around Lake Coeur d’Alene and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  3. Wallace Mining Tour: History buffs will enjoy a guided tour through the historic mining town of Wallace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Idaho

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Northern Idaho, here’s an itinerary you can follow:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Museum of North Idaho and take a stroll around Coeur d’Alene City Park.
2 Explore the Trail of the Coeur d’Alenes.
3 Go on the Lake Coeur d’Alene Cruise.
4 Visit Silverwood Theme Park.
5 Hike the Hiawatha Trail.
6 Take a Wallace Mining Tour.
7 Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Northern Idaho.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Idaho

Must-Try Dishes

Northern Idaho offers a range of dishes that you must try:

  1. Huckleberry Pie: This pie is a regional specialty and is made using huckleberries, a type of wild berry that grows in the area.
  2. Wild Salmon: If you’re a seafood lover, you must try the wild salmon that’s caught in the nearby rivers and streams.
  3. Huckleberry Jam: This jam is made using huckleberries and is a perfect souvenir to take back home.

Local Beverages

  1. Huckleberry Margarita: This drink combines huckleberry syrup, tequila, and lime juice and is a popular cocktail in the area.
  2. Local Craft Beers: Northern Idaho is home to several microbreweries that offer a range of craft beers for visitors to enjoy.
  3. Locally Roasted Coffee: Northern Idaho is known for its specialty coffee shops that use locally roasted coffee beans.

Where to Stay in Northern Idaho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Idaho offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some options:

  1. Coeur d’Alene Resort: This popular resort is located on the shores of Lake Coeur d’Alene and offers luxurious amenities to guests.
  2. Wallace Inn: Located in downtown Wallace, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and is a great base for exploring the surrounding area.
  3. Syd’s Motel & Restaurant: This family-owned motel offers affordable rooms and a restaurant that serves homestyle meals.

What to Buy in Northern Idaho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Huckleberry Products: You can find huckleberry jam, syrup, and other huckleberry-themed products at local gift shops.
  2. Locally Made Craft Items: Northern Idaho has several craft shops that sell handmade items, including jewelry, pottery, and textiles.
  3. Outdoor Gear: If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you can find high-quality outdoor gear in the local stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Idaho

Northern Idaho has a unique culture that’s influenced by its history and geography. The people in the area are friendly and welcoming, and you’re likely to strike up a conversation with locals wherever you go.

Best Time to Visit Northern Idaho

The best time to visit Northern Idaho is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is sunny and warm. However, spring and fall are also good times to visit if you want to avoid the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Idaho

Do:

  1. Respect the natural environment and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  2. Take advantage of the outdoor activities that Northern Idaho has to offer, such as hiking, biking, and fishing.
  3. Visit local shops and restaurants to support the local economy.

Don’t:

  1. Ignore safety warnings and guidelines while participating in outdoor activities.
  2. Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  3. Leave trash or litter behind in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Idaho

Northern Idaho is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions while exploring the outdoors. Here are some tips:

  1. Always carry a map and compass with you while hiking or biking.
  2. Be aware of wildlife and carry bear spray if you’re traveling in bear country.
  3. Check weather conditions before heading out and dress appropriately.

Health in Northern Idaho

Northern Idaho has several medical facilities that offer quality healthcare to visitors. However, it’s always a good idea to carry basic first aid supplies and any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Northern Idaho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Idaho

  1. Spokane International Airport (GEG) - located 34 miles from Coeur d’Alene.
  2. Lewiston-Nez Perce County Regional Airport (LWS) - located 83 miles from Moscow.

Bus Stations Near Northern Idaho

  1. Greyhound Station - located in Coeur d’Alene.
  2. Northwestern Trailways - operates bus services to major cities in the region.

Other Options

  1. Rental Cars: You can rent a car from the airport or major cities in the area.
  2. Taxis and Ride-Sharing services: Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in the major cities in Northern Idaho.

How to Get Around in Northern Idaho

The best way to get around Northern Idaho is by car, as public transportation is limited in the region. Rental cars are available at major airports and cities in the area.

Where to Go Next from Northern Idaho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northern Idaho is a great base for exploring other parts of the Pacific Northwest. Here are some nearby destinations that you can visit:

  1. Montana: Montana is located east of Northern Idaho and offers stunning scenery and outdoor activities.
  2. Seattle: Seattle is located west of Northern Idaho and is a bustling city with plenty of attractions.
  3. Yellowstone National Park: Yellowstone National Park is located to the east of Northern Idaho and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Northern Idaho offers a unique blend of natural attractions and cultural experiences to visitors. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in this picturesque region. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to plan a trip you’ll never forget.
