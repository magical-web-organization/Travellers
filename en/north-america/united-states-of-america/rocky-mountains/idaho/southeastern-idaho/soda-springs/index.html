Introduction to Soda Springs

Soda Springs is a small historic town in Southeastern Idaho, in the Rocky Mountains. The town is well known for its natural hot springs, which were discovered in the early 19th century by fur trappers who noticed bubbles rising from the ground. From then on, people began to visit Soda Springs to “take the waters” due to its purported healing properties. Today, Soda Springs is popular with travelers who enjoy outdoor adventures, historical attractions, and the small-town charm.

List of Places to Visit in Soda Springs

Soda Springs has something for everyone. Visitors can indulge in leisurely walks, history tours, cultural experiences and a range of activities, including hiking, biking, and skiing. Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Soda Springs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Soda Springs Geyser Park: The park is home to the world’s only captive geyser, which erupts every hour.
  • Soda Springs Pioneer Museum: A local history museum preserving the city’s culture and heritage for over a century.
  • Hooper Springs Museum: This museum exhibits the Hooper Springs Hotel and the history of the hotel that was built in 1870.
  • Caribou County Historical Society Museum: A museum showcasing the early history of the area with a range of exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Soda Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Soda Springs Hot Pools: Visitors can bathe in mineral-rich water, which is naturally heated to an average temperature of 89°F.
  • Bonneville Salt Flats International Speedway: A world-famous flat salt bed where drivers break land speed records.
  • Pebble Creek Ski Area: A ski resort that offers world-class skiing opportunities.
  • Lava Hot Springs: A nearby attraction famous for its hot mineral springs with a range of outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Soda Springs

Soda Springs has plenty of trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Fish Creek Trail: A 10.5-mile trail for hiking and mountain biking with beautiful views of wildflowers, mountains, and streams.
  • South Fork Trail: A challenging 7.5-mile trail running along the South Fork of the Snake River.
  • Connie’s Trail: A 5.7-mile trail suitable for beginners; it runs through the foothills of the Caribou Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Soda Springs: Activities and Tours

Soda Springs offers plenty of activities for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Soda Springs:

  • Visit the Soda Springs Geyser Park: Explore the only captive geyser in the world with frequent eruptions.
  • Take a Dip in Hot Pools: Relax in mineral-rich hot springs in East Idaho to help you unwind.
  • Go Skiing/Snowboarding: Ski or snowboard on the pristine slopes of Pebble Creek Ski Resort.
  • Drive to Lava Hot Springs: Soak in the hot springs and rejuvenate your mind and body.

Food and Drink Scene of Soda Springs

Soda Springs has a small food and drink scene, offering visitors with a range of options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverage options:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Idaho Style Finger Steaks: Thin slices of breaded beef that are fried and served with horseradish-laced aioli.
  • Huckleberry Ice Cream: A sweet and tart ice cream that uses fresh huckleberries.
  • Dutch Oven Peach Cobbler: A summer dessert made in a Dutch oven with fresh peaches topped with buttery crumbs.

Local Beverages

  • Soda Springs’ Famous Flavors: This is a variety of flavors, including apple caramel, wild cherry, and Italian cream soda served at Junkyard Java.
  • Soda Springs Tea Co.: Tea varieties include Earl Grey, Green Tea, Chamomile, and Chai.

Where to Stay in Soda Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Soda Springs offers accommodations for every budget and traveler preference. Here are some options:

  • Cobblestone Inn & Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Soda Springs Inn: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean rooms and is located minutes away from the city center.
  • Lazy K Ranch Bed & Breakfast: A serene stay in the countryside that offers a unique experience with horse rides and ranch activities.

What to Buy in Soda Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home local products and souvenirs that encapsulate the town’s essence. Here are some options:

  • Huckleberry Candy: Handmade candy that is flavored with huckleberry.
  • Soda Springs T-Shirts and Caps: Branded merchandise that you can purchase from shops.
  • Pottery Made by Local Artists: The city is famous for its pottery that uses geothermal heat from underground pipes.

Local Culture and Customs of Soda Springs

Soda Springs is a town known for its strong community values, manners, and hospitality. Many events are community-oriented, so visitors can participate and learn about local customs and traditions. Soda Springs’s residents also exhibit pride in their historic town, and strong relationships are built through the love of the great outdoor and friendly interaction.

Best Time to Visit Soda Springs

The best time to visit Soda Springs is during the summer months, between June and September when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Winters, between November and April are extremely cold, and some activities, such as hiking and skiing, are only possible with proper gear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soda Springs

Do:

  • Do embrace the local culture and scenery of the area.
  • Do your part in keeping the town clean and green.
  • Do take appropriate precautions when taking part in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your trash or litter around in the hiking trails or waterways.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of the local culture and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to pack your sunscreen, hats, and sunshades while exploring the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Soda Springs

Soda Springs is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors need to be careful while indulging in outdoor activities and avoid hiking alone. Also, it is recommended to keep an eye on weather updates; the region experiences sudden thunderstorms.

Health in Soda Springs

Soda Springs is in a rural area that has a limited medical facility for emergencies. Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance to cover medical emergencies during their stay. It is also suggested to pack medication and other necessities as it may not be available in the town.

Getting to Soda Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Soda Springs is easily accessed by plane and bus services. The region can be beautifully explored by car.

Airports Near Soda Springs

The nearest airport close to Soda Springs is the Pocatello Regional Airport (PIH), Idaho, which is 65 miles away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Soda Springs

Greyhound provides bus services to nearby places, including Idaho Falls and Pocatello, which are 72 and 72 miles away, respectively.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Soda Springs from the east or west along the I-70, or from the north or south via the US Highway 30.

How to Get Around in Soda Springs

The best way to get around in Soda Springs is by car as public transportation is limited to buses and taxis. Visitors can also hike, bike, or walk around the city to explore the scenic beauty of Soda Springs.

Where to Go Next from Soda Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Soda Springs, visitors can head to other attractions nearby, including:

  • Idaho Falls: A nearby city with a picturesque waterfall and cultural attractions such as the Museum of Idaho.
  • Yellowstone National Park: A world-famous national park with the extraordinary geysers and wildlife sightings.
  • Jackson Hole: A famous resort town located in the Grand Tetons Range that offers skiing, hiking, and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Soda Springs offers plenty of experiences for visitors, with natural wonders, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and leisurely pursuits. The town is an ideal location for those seeking refuge in tranquillity, bonding with locals, and immersing themselves in nature. With the comprehensive information provided in this guide, you will be well equipped to have the trip of a lifetime in Soda Springs.
