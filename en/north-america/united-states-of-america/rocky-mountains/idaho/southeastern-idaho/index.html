Introduction to Southeastern Idaho

Southeastern Idaho is a hidden gem located in the Rocky Mountains region of the United States. The area is known for its stunning natural landscapes, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re an avid hiker, history buff, or foodie, Southeastern Idaho has something to offer everyone. In this tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Southeastern Idaho.

List of Places to Visit in Southeastern Idaho

Southeastern Idaho is home to many culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, and exciting outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Southeastern Idaho:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Idaho: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the region’s history, with exhibits ranging from dinosaurs to Lewis and Clark.
  • Idaho Falls River Walk: Enjoy a stroll along the riverfront and learn about the history of Idaho Falls.
  • The Teton Valley Historical Museum: Explore the history of the Teton Valley and the surrounding area, including Native American artifacts, mining history, and more.

Natural Attractions of Southeastern Idaho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yellowstone National Park: Located just a short drive from Southeastern Idaho, Yellowstone is a must-visit destination filled with natural wonders like geysers, hot springs, and wildlife.
  • Grand Teton National Park: This stunning park is home to some of the most picturesque mountain views in the country and offers endless outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and skiing.
  • Craters of the Moon National Monument: This unique formation features a landscape of lava flows and craters that feel like another world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southeastern Idaho

Southeastern Idaho is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of scenic trails for all skill levels. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Teton Crest Trail: This stunning trail is a backpacker’s dream, stretching for nearly 40 miles through the Teton mountain range.
  • Kelly Canyon: This ski resort offers some of the best mountain biking trails in the region, with trails for all levels of experience.
  • City Creek Trail: This easy 4-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride through the scenic City Creek area.

Best Things to Do in Southeastern Idaho: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for even more excitement and adventure, there are plenty of things to do in Southeastern Idaho, including:

  • Kayaking or paddleboarding on the Snake River
  • Skiing or snowboarding at one of the many nearby ski resorts
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the Teton Mountains
  • The Idaho Falls Farmers Market

Week-Long Itinerary for Southeastern Idaho

If you want to make the most of your visit to Southeastern Idaho, here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Museum of Idaho and Idaho Falls River Walk
Day 2 Explore Grand Teton National Park and go on a wildlife safari
Day 3 Discover the history of the Teton Valley at the Teton Valley Historical Museum
Day 4 Hike the Teton Crest Trail or visit Craters of the Moon National Monument
Day 5 Go skiing or snowboarding at Kelly Canyon
Day 6 Take a hot air balloon ride over the Teton Mountains
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs at the Idaho Falls Farmers Market

Food and Drink Scene of Southeastern Idaho

Southeastern Idaho is also home to plenty of delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are a few must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Finger Steaks: These seasoned and battered steak strips are a local favorite and can be found in most restaurants in the area.
  • Huckleberry Ice Cream: Huckleberries are a local fruit that grows in the region and is used in many desserts, including ice cream.
  • Trout: Many local restaurants offer fresh trout from the nearby rivers and lakes.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Lemonade: This refreshing drink is made with local huckleberries and is the perfect way to cool off on a hot day.
  • Snake River Brewing Co.: This local brewery offers a variety of delicious brews, including the popular Zonker Stout.
  • Grand Teton Distillery: Known for its small-batch vodka and gin, the Grand Teton Distillery offers guided tours and tastings of its spirits.

Where to Stay in Southeastern Idaho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southeastern Idaho has plenty of options for accommodations, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a rustic cabin. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Hotel Terra: This modern hotel is located in Teton Village and offers stunning mountain views, a heated pool, and easy access to skiing and hiking.
  • Teton Valley Cabins: These cozy cabins in the Teton Valley offer a peaceful retreat surrounded by beautiful mountains and wildlife.
  • The Blue Heron Inn: This charming bed and breakfast in Rigby offers comfortable rooms and a delicious home-cooked breakfast.

What to Buy in Southeastern Idaho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or locally made products to bring home, Southeastern Idaho has plenty of options. Here are a few ideas:

  • Huckleberry jam: This delicious jam is made with locally grown huckleberries and is a favorite among visitors.
  • Local art: Southeastern Idaho is home to many talented artists and craftsmen, and their work can be found in local galleries and shops.
  • Idaho potato-shaped souvenirs: Idaho is known for its potatoes, and you can find all sorts of potato-themed souvenirs, from postcards to keychains.

Local Culture and Customs of Southeastern Idaho

Southeastern Idaho has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. The region is also home to many Native American tribes, and their history and culture are an important part of the area’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Southeastern Idaho

The best time to visit Southeastern Idaho depends on your interests and the activities you want to do. If you’re interested in hiking and outdoor activities, the summer months (June-August) are the best time to visit. If you’re interested in winter sports, the ski season runs from November to April. Fall and spring are also good times to visit, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southeastern Idaho

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Southeastern Idaho:

Do:

  • Respect the area’s natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be aware of weather conditions and dress appropriately for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or disturb wildlife in natural areas.
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Assume all parts of the region are accessible by car.

Safety and Health

Southeastern Idaho is generally a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should be aware of a few important considerations.

Safety in Southeastern Idaho

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and be prepared for changes in weather conditions.
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife.
  • Carry bear spray when hiking in the backcountry.

Health in Southeastern Idaho

  • Drink plenty of water, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Protect yourself from the sun with sunscreen and proper clothing.
  • Be aware of altitude sickness when traveling to higher elevations.

Getting to Southeastern Idaho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southeastern Idaho can be easily accessed by car or plane. Here are a few transportation options:

Airports Near Southeastern Idaho

  • Idaho Falls Regional Airport: Located in Idaho Falls, this airport offers domestic flights from major airlines like Delta and United.
  • Jackson Hole Airport: Located in Jackson, Wyoming, this airport offers flights from major airlines like American and United.

Bus Stations Near Southeastern Idaho

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus service stops in Idaho Falls, Pocatello, and Jackson.

Other Options

  • Car rental: There are many car rental options available at the Idaho Falls airport or in nearby cities.
  • Taxi or rideshare: Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are available in Idaho Falls and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Southeastern Idaho

The best way to get around Southeastern Idaho is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also explore the area by bike or on foot by taking advantage of the many hiking and biking trails in the region.

Where to Go Next from Southeastern Idaho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring nearby destinations, here are a few suggestions:

  • Salt Lake City, Utah: Located just a few hours south of Southeastern Idaho, Salt Lake City offers stunning mountain views, cultural attractions, and an exciting food and drink scene.
  • Jackson, Wyoming: This charming mountain town is located just over the border from Southeastern Idaho and offers a wide variety of outdoor activities, shopping, and dining.
  • Craters of the Moon National Monument and Preserve: This stunning natural wonder is located just a short drive from Southeastern Idaho and offers a unique landscape of volcanic features.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the history and culture of Southeastern Idaho or immersing yourself in its stunning natural landscapes, this region has something to offer everyone. From the thrill of hiking through the Teton Mountains to the peaceful beauty of the Snake River, Southeastern Idaho is an unforgettable destination for any traveler.
