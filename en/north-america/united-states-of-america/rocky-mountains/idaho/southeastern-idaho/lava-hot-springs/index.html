Introduction to Lava Hot Springs

Lava Hot Springs is a small town located in southeastern Idaho, nestled in the Rocky Mountains of the United States. The town is most famous for its hot springs, which have been enjoyed for their therapeutic properties for thousands of years. In modern times, Lava Hot Springs has become a popular destination for tourists seeking relaxation, outdoor activities, and a taste of local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Lava Hot Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • South Bannock County Historical Center Museum: Learn about the history and culture of the local area.
  • Lava Hot Springs Olympic Swimming Complex: Take a dip in the world-famous swimming complex that was used as a training center for the US Olympic swimming team in 1996.

Natural Attractions of Lava Hot Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lava Hot Springs Hot Pools: Soak in the natural hot springs pools, which are rich in minerals and believed to have healing properties.
  • Portneuf River: Rent a tube and float down the scenic river, surrounded by lush greenery and stunning mountain views.
  • Lions Park: Enjoy a picnic, hike the trails, or go fishing in this beautiful park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lava Hot Springs

  • The Wellness Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that winds through the town and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Pebble Creek Trail: This 4-mile trail takes you through scenic mountain terrain and provides an excellent opportunity for hiking or mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Lava Hot Springs: Activities and Tours

  • Zipline Adventure: Experience the thrill of ziplining through the mountains with a professionally guided tour.
  • Paddleboard and Kayak Rentals: Rent a paddleboard or kayak and explore the scenic Portneuf River at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lava Hot Springs

Day Activities
1 Explore the South Bannock County Historical Center Museum and take a relaxing soak in Lava Hot Springs Hot Pools.
2 Rent tubes and float down the Portneuf River.
3 Hike or mountain bike the Pebble Creek Trail and enjoy the mountain views.
4 Take a zipline adventure through the mountains and go stargazing in the evening.
5 Rent paddleboards or kayaks and explore the Portneuf River.
6 Relax in the Lava Hot Springs Olympic Swimming Complex and picnic in Lions Park.
7 Try out the local cuisine and pick up souvenirs at the Saturday Farmers Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Lava Hot Springs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Ice Cream: A local favorite, made from wild huckleberries picked in the surrounding mountains.
  • Trout: Freshly caught from the Portneuf River and served in many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Lemonade: A refreshing drink made with freshly squeezed lemon juice and local huckleberries.
  • Idaho Wine: The surrounding area is home to several excellent wineries, which produce a wide variety of award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Lava Hot Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Royal Hotel and Pizzeria: Charming historic hotel with a delicious onsite restaurant.
  • Lava Spa Motel and RV Park: Affordable lodging with a variety of options, including motel rooms, suites, and RV and camping sites.
  • Home Hotel Lava Hot Springs: Cozy guestrooms and a popular onsite bar and restaurant.

What to Buy in Lava Hot Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jellies, Jams, and Syrups: Made from locally sourced huckleberries and perfect for bringing home as gifts.
  • Handmade Soaps and Lotions: Locally made with all-natural ingredients and scented with essential oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Lava Hot Springs

  • Respect for Nature: The natural beauty of Lava Hot Springs is highly valued, and visitors are encouraged to practice Leave No Trace principles.
  • Small-Town Hospitality: The people of Lava Hot Springs are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so don’t be shy to strike up a conversation with a local.

Best Time to Visit Lava Hot Springs

The best times to visit Lava Hot Springs are in the summer months, from June to September. During this time, the weather is warm, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to do. In the winter months, the town becomes a winter wonderland, and hot springs visitors are often greeted with beautiful snowy views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lava Hot Springs

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural hot springs and take a relaxing soak
  • Respect the local culture and the natural environment
  • Try the local cuisine and learn about the town’s history

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the natural environment
  • Disrespect the natural hot springs or local customs
  • Engage in any behavior that can harm the environment or wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Lava Hot Springs

Overall, the town is safe and friendly, with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions when traveling, such as locking their doors and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lava Hot Springs

The high mineral content of the hot springs has been known to help with certain medical conditions, such as arthritis and skin conditions. Visitors should always consult their doctor before using the hot springs.

Getting to Lava Hot Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lava Hot Springs

  • Pocatello Regional Airport (IDA): located 32 miles from Lava Hot Springs

Bus Stations Near Lava Hot Springs

  • Greyhound Bus station in Pocatello, ID: located 32 miles from Lava Hot Springs

Other Options

  • Drive: Lava Hot Springs is easily accessible by car, and is located just off Interstate 15.
  • Train: Amtrak stations in Salt Lake City and Ogden are approximately two hours away by car.

How to Get Around in Lava Hot Springs

Lava Hot Springs is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use bicycles or rental cars to get around the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Lava Hot Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Craters of the Moon National Monument: An otherworldly landscape of volcanic rock formations and lava flows, located approximately two hours from Lava Hot Springs.
  • Bear Lake: A beautiful blue lake located on the border between Idaho and Utah, known for its crystal-clear waters and sandy beaches.

Conclusion

Lava Hot Springs is a wonderful destination for travelers seeking a peaceful and relaxing getaway. With its natural hot springs, outdoor activities, and rich local culture, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Plan your trip today and experience this hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains of Idaho!
