Introduction to Shelley

Located in Southeastern Idaho, Shelley is a charming town in the Rocky Mountains region of the United States. With its scenic beauty and rich cultural heritage, Shelley offers a unique experience to visitors seeking adventure and relaxation.

From outdoor activities like hiking, skiing, and fishing to cultural attractions like museums and historical sites, Shelley has something for everyone. This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive list of the top attractions and activities in Shelley, as well as useful tips for planning your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Shelley

When it comes to planning your trip to Shelley, there are several must-visit places that you should add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most unique aspects of Shelley is its rich history and culture. Here are some top museums and historical sites that you should visit:

  • The Shelley Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Shelley and its people. You can learn about the town’s agricultural roots, as well as its role in the development of the railroad in Idaho.
  • The Idaho Falls Zoo: Just a short drive from Shelley, the Idaho Falls Zoo features over 400 animals from around the world. It’s a great place to spend a day exploring and learning about the different animal species.
  • The Museum of Idaho: Located in nearby Idaho Falls, the Museum of Idaho is a world-class museum that features exhibits on history, science, and culture. It’s a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning about the history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Shelley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure or simply want to soak in the beautiful scenery, Shelley has plenty of natural attractions to explore. Here are a few top picks:

  • The Snake River Greenbelt: This scenic trail runs along the Snake River and offers a great place to walk, bike, or run.
  • Firth Lake: A popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming, Firth Lake is a beautiful place to spend a summer day.
  • Kelly Canyon Ski Resort: Located just a short drive from Shelley, Kelly Canyon Ski Resort is a great place to hit the slopes in the winter. With over 50 runs and a variety of ski trails, it’s perfect for skiers and snowboarders of all levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shelley

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Shelley is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. Here are a few of the best hiking and biking routes to check out:

  • Pine Creek Trail: This scenic trail is located just outside of Shelley and features a moderate two-mile hike through pine trees and beautiful scenery.
  • The Greenbelt Trail: As mentioned earlier, this trail runs along the Snake River and is perfect for biking or walking.
  • The Kelly Canyon Trail System: Featuring over 10 miles of trails, the Kelly Canyon Trail System is a great option for hikers and bikers looking for a challenge.

Best Things to Do in Shelley: Activities and Tours

Beyond its natural attractions and museums, Shelley has plenty of other exciting things to see and do. Here are a few top picks:

  • The Shelley Sled Dog Race: Held every February, this annual event attracts sled dog enthusiasts from around the world.
  • The Idaho Potato Museum: Located in nearby Blackfoot, the Idaho Potato Museum is a fun and quirky destination that celebrates Idaho’s love of potatoes.
  • The Teton Scenic Byway: One of the most picturesque drives in Idaho, the Teton Scenic Byway offers stunning views of the Teton Mountains and other natural wonders.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shelley

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Shelley, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Idaho Potato Museum in Blackfoot
2 Explore the Shelley Historical Museum and take a hike on the Pine Creek Trail
3 Spend the day fishing or boating at Firth Lake
4 Take a scenic drive along the Teton Scenic Byway
5 Ski or snowboard at Kelly Canyon Ski Resort
6 Visit the Idaho Falls Zoo or the Museum of Idaho
7 Attend the Shelley Sled Dog Race (if visiting in February) or spend the day exploring the town on foot

Food and Drink Scene of Shelley

Shelley may be a small town, but it has plenty of great options for food and drink. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Idahoan Finger Steaks: Made with breaded steak fingers, these are a popular regional dish that are served with dipping sauce.
  • Huckleberry Pie: A delicious dessert made with local huckleberries, which grow in abundance in the area.
  • Potato Casserole: Another dish that takes advantage of Idaho’s love of potatoes, this casserole is a hearty and delicious side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Snake River Brewing: This local brewery is a great place to stop in for a pint or try a beer flight. They have a variety of beers on tap, including IPAs and stouts.
  • Idaho Wines: Idaho has a small but growing wine industry, and there are several wineries in the area that offer tastings and tours.
  • Idaho Horchata: Made with rice, cinnamon and sugar, this sweet and creamy drink is a refreshing way to cool off during a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Shelley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly option or a luxurious stay, Shelley has several great options for accommodations. Here are a few top picks:

  • Shelley Inn: This budget-friendly option is located in the heart of town and offers comfortable rooms and friendly service.
  • Snake River Lodge & Spa: For a more luxurious stay, check out the Snake River Lodge & Spa, which features a full-service spa, fine dining, and stunning mountain views.
  • Airbnb: Looking for a unique and personalized experience? Consider staying in an Airbnb, where you can rent out a private home or apartment and get a true local experience.

What to Buy in Shelley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to bring home a piece of Shelley with you? Here are a few souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Huckleberry Jam: Made with local huckleberries, this sweet and tangy jam is a delicious souvenir to bring home.
  • Idaho Potatoes: Available at local markets and grocery stores, Idaho potatoes are a delicious treat to bring home and cook up as a side dish or fry.
  • Handmade Crafts: There are several local artisans and craft shops in Shelley, where you can find handmade ceramics, jewelry, and other unique gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Shelley

To get the most out of your visit to Shelley, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Religion: Shelley is a primarily Mormon town, so it’s important to be respectful of religious practices and traditions.
  • Fishing and Hunting: Many locals enjoy fishing and hunting in the area, so it’s important to be aware of any regulations and follow best practices for safety and conservation.
  • Friendly locals: One of the highlights of Shelley is its friendly and welcoming locals. It’s common for strangers to strike up a conversation and offer recommendations for things to do and see in the area.

Best Time to Visit Shelley

The best time to visit Shelley really depends on your interests and preferences. For outdoor activities like hiking and skiing, the best time to visit is during the summer or winter months, respectively. If you’re more interested in cultural attractions, then spring and fall offer comfortable temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shelley

To make the most of your visit to Shelley, here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do explore the local hiking and biking trails to take in the beautiful scenery.
  • Do try the local food and beverages, especially the huckleberry pie and Idaho potatoes.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural resources.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals and ask for recommendations.

Safety and Health

Like any destination, safety and health are important considerations when travelling to Shelley. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Safety in Shelley

  • Be aware of wildlife in the area and follow best practices for staying safe.
  • Be cautious when hiking or biking on unfamiliar trails, especially in remote areas.
  • Use caution when driving on mountain roads, as they can be steep and winding.

Health in Shelley

  • Be aware of the altitude and potential risks associated with high altitudes.
  • Bring appropriate gear and clothing for outdoor activities to stay comfortable and avoid injuries or illnesses.
  • Be mindful of the potential for sunburn or dehydration, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Shelley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shelley is located in Southeastern Idaho and is easily accessible by several transportation options.

Airports Near Shelley

  • Idaho Falls Regional Airport: Located just 20 minutes from Shelley, this airport offers flights from several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Shelley

  • Salt Lake Express Bus: This bus service provides transportation to several destinations in Idaho and the surrounding region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car from several major rental companies at the airport or in nearby towns.
  • Rideshare Services: Uber and Lyft are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Shelley

While Shelley is a small town, it’s still important to have a plan for getting around during your visit. Here are a few options:

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option, especially if you plan to do a lot of driving or exploring in the area.
  • Biking/Walking: Shelley is a great town for biking and walking, especially along the Snake River Greenbelt or through downtown.
  • Public Transportation: While there isn’t a lot of public transportation available in Shelley, the Salt Lake Express Bus provides transportation to nearby towns and destinations.

Where to Go Next from Shelley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area around Shelley, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Yellowstone National Park: Located just a few hours from Shelley, Yellowstone offers stunning natural scenery and a chance to see wildlife like bears, wolves, and bison.
  • Grand Teton National Park: Located just south of Yellowstone, Grand Teton offers breathtaking mountain scenery and a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Jackson Hole: A popular ski and resort town, Jackson Hole is a great place to spend a few days exploring and relaxing.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a culture buff, Shelley has something for everyone. With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, Shelley offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this charming Rocky Mountain town.
