Introduction to Moscow

Moscow is a charming city situated in North Central Idaho, nestled in the Rockies Mountains. It is a vibrant university town, home to the University of Idaho and the New Saint Andrews College, which contribute to the city’s intellectual and cultural scene. Moscow is also known for its natural beauty, with picturesque trails that offer stunning views of the Palouse country. In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive list of things to do, places to visit, and local culture in Moscow, Idaho.

List of Places to Visit in Moscow

Moscow boasts several must-see destinations for tourists, including museums and historical sites, outdoor attractions, and popular activities. Here are our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Appaloosa Museum and Heritage Center: A museum dedicated to the impact Appaloosa horses have had on the American West culture.

  • Hamilton-Lowe Aquatics Center: A popular swimming and diving center with an Olympic-sized pool.

  • Latah County Historical Society: A center that preserves Latah County History.

  • McConnell Mansion Museum: an historic home for the McConnell family that tells the story of early Idaho and the University of Idaho.

  • University of Idaho Arboretum and Botanical Garden: A 63-acre scenic garden providing a place to enjoy nature and experience culture.

Natural Attractions of Moscow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harvest Hills Farm: The farm produces quality, organic produce and maintains beautiful gardens visitors can explore.

  • Idaho Forrest Services: Experience the beauty of Moscow’s natural wonders with various hiking and camping activities.

  • Kamiak Butte County Park: A 300-acre park that offers hiking and panoramic views of the Palouse country.

  • ** Moscow Mountain Trail System:** A three-part trail system covering 77 miles of forested hills, perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  • Riverside State Park: A 1000 acre park with the centerpiece being the 14 miles of Spokane River

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moscow

Moscow boasts many scenic trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, with pleasant outdoor experiences for everyone. Here are some of the famous hiking and biking routes in Moscow:

  • Bill Chipman Palouse Trail: A 7-mile paved trail that runs from Moscow, ID to Pullman, WA. Gorgeous views of the Palouse and several trailheads make it easily accesible.

  • Moscow Mountain Epic Loop: A 30-mile trail system that is a moderately difficult hike with several rewarding scenic views.

  • Paradise Path: A delightful 2-mile walking path that is a great escape and get away location to the park during busy days

  • The Arboretum: For those interested in viewing the area botanical gardens; the university offers guided and self guided tours with a beautiful 63 acre outdoor landscapes.

  • The South Fork Short Loop: A more moderate hike around the South Fork of the Palouse River, with scenic views of the river and surrounding nature.

Best Things to Do in Moscow: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring natural wonders, Moscow offers a range of fascinating activities and tours for travelers. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Moscow:

  • Artwalk: Held on the second Friday of each month from March to December, the Artwalk is a monthly event that showcases the town’s art and artists’

  • Farmers Market: The market is held on Saturdays and provides ample fresh produce and goods.

  • Gyms: The town has several fitness centers and is well-equipped with equipment for exercises, fun activities, and daily workouts.

  • Outdoor Yoga: Practices yoga in parks and other outdoor areas where peace and tranquility surround you.

  • Palouse-Hills Hoof Beats: A beautiful horseback riding tour of the hills of Palouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Moscow

Moscow features an inspired and varied food and drink scene, with local cafés and restaurants serving both traditional and innovative cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Ice Cream: Delicious ice cream combines real Idaho Huckleberries with rich cream.

  • Moscow Mule: crafted using Russian vodka, ginger beer and lime and served over ice

  • Potato Pancakes: Fried morsels of grated potato and other seasonings serve as a classic side dish and a comforting food.

  • Wild salmon: A local catch with a unique and tasty flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Moscow has several popular breweries like Rants and Raves brewery house, which produce the finest craft beers in the Palouse region.

  • Coffee: Visit Coffee Perk, the local coffee joint, for a cup of high-quality coffee

  • Wine: The region is known for its wine-producing and a trip to the Colter’s Creek Winery is the perfect way to explore the wine scene of Moscow.

Where to Stay in Moscow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moscow presents many options when it comes to choosing accommodation, which includes hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Moscow:

  • Best Western: A popular hotel that provides affordable amenities with a friendly staff to ensure that each guest’s stay is comfortable.

  • Red Lion Moscow: A hotel famous for its comfortable rooms and hospitality services, with a 24-hour business center and cutting-edge amenities.

  • The Potlatch: A fun hostel, that provides a serene escape for people seeking with clean rooms and friendly staff aimed at giving the guests a delightful experience.

  • University Inn: An especially convenient location for those visiting and exploring University of Idaho campus.

What to Buy in Moscow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moscow offers a range of souvenirs and local products that tourists can purchase for remembrance and memory. Here are some of the popular souvenirs and local products in Moscow:

  • Artworks: Moscow is a town that prides itself on creativity, and many artists create beautiful works of art that you can take home with you!.

  • Colter’s Creek Wine: Experience the taste of the region with a bottle of Colter’s Creek Winery’s wine.

  • Palouse Country pendants and bracelets: Accessorize with stunning art pieces made with Palouse gems that commemorate your time in Moscow.

Local Culture and Customs of Moscow

The town of Moscow is a blend of cosmopolitanism and historic charm. People living in Moscow are friendly, artistic, and intellectual. They exhibit and appreciate kindness and generosity while embodying the ethos of a college town.

Best Time to Visit Moscow

The best time to visit Moscow is between May to October when the weather is mild and conducive to outdoor activities. The town’s location at a higher elevation is often a great escape from the nearby heat, which can be quite unpleasant during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moscow

Do:

  • Embrace the strong sport and outdoor culture - it is the foundation of the town’s existence.

  • Walk and explore the unique town - it’s an excellent way to experience the town’s unique blend of culture and natural beauty.

  • Show respect to locals and visitors - their vibrant and creative culture is impressive and always on display.

Don’t:

  • Overlook safety precautions while exploring, hiking, or biking - stay on designated paths to ensure a great experience.

  • Do anything to damage the natural environment - remember that preserving the environment is key to experiening the town’s beauty

  • Misappropriate the town’s art culture - always be respectful of artists, makers, and their intellectual property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moscow

Moscow is a safe destination with a low crime rate and friendly people. Be cautious and watchful when exploring the natural beauty of the region, and always stay on designated paths.

Health in Moscow

Moscow has excellent medical facilities and healthcare centers, with several high-quality hospitals and clinics spread across the town.

Getting to Moscow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moscow is located in North Central Idaho and can be reached via several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Moscow

  • Spokane International Airport: About 90 minutes from Moscow and offers a range of domestic and international flights.

  • Lewiston Airport: A small airport about 30 minutes from Moscow that provides flights to major cities in the Pacific Northwest region.

Bus Stations Near Moscow

There are no direct bus routes to Moscow, but Pullman/Moscow Regional Airport is accessible via several bus routes.

Other Options

If you prefer driving from other parts of the US, several car rental companies, Uber, and Lyft services are available.

How to Get Around in Moscow

Moscow is easy to navigate and explore on foot, but renting a car is an excellent option for those who want to take day trips outside the city. Taxis, buses, and bikes are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Moscow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, several famous destinations and excursions are worth exploring:

  • Boise City: The capital of Idaho is a city of outdoor recreation, culture, restaurants, and a lively nightlife.

  • Coeur d’Alene: A charming town that boasts of 55 lakes and 90 parks, with adventurous activities like zip-lining, kayaking, and skiing.

Conclusion

Moscow, Idaho, is an excellent destination for those who love natural beauty, intellectually stimulating activities, and a vibrant culture. It offers something for every traveler, from museums, historical places, and outdoor adventures to culinary experiences, local crafts, and music. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and get the most out of your time in Moscow!
