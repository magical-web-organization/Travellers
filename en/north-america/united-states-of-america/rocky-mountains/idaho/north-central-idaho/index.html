A Comprehensive Guide to North Central Idaho

North Central Idaho is located in the Rocky Mountains, in the United States of America, and is a destination loved by many for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Whether you are traveling solo, with friends, or with family, there is something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at what North Central Idaho has to offer, from must-visit places and hiking trails to food and drink recommendations and local customs and traditions.

Introduction to North Central Idaho

North Central Idaho is a region that includes seven counties in Idaho: Clearwater, Idaho, Latah, Lewis, Nez Perce, and portions of Valley and Adams counties. With vast forests, stunning mountains, and beautiful rivers, it is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. However, the region isn’t just for outdoor enthusiasts; there are plenty of cultural attractions to enjoy as well.

List of Places to Visit in North Central Idaho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North Central Idaho has a rich cultural history, and there are several museums and historical places that you should visit. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Nez Perce National Historical Park: This park tells the story of the Nez Perce people and their struggle to maintain their way of life.

  • Museum of North Idaho: This museum showcases the history of North Idaho, from its geology to its cultural heritage.

  • Lewiston Center for Arts: This center is a hub for the arts and culture in the region, featuring exhibitions, performances, and workshops.

Natural Attractions of North Central Idaho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, North Central Idaho won’t disappoint you. Here are some of the best natural attractions that you should include in your itinerary:

  • Hells Canyon National Recreation Area: This area is home to the deepest river gorge in North America and has stunning views and plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and fishing.

  • Selway-Bitterroot Wilderness Area: This wilderness area is a vast expanse of untamed wilderness with over 1.3 million acres of unspoiled beauty. It is a hiker’s paradise.

  • Dworshak Reservoir: This reservoir is a beautiful place to go boating and fishing, with over 100 miles of shoreline and crystal-clear water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Central Idaho

North Central Idaho is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in the United States. Here are some of the best routes that you should consider:

  • Moscow Mountain Trail: This trail has stunning views of the mountainous terrain and is suitable for hiking and biking.

  • Pine Creek Falls Trail: This moderate hike has beautiful views of the waterfall and is a must-visit for hikers.

  • Ridge to Rivers Trail System: This trail system is an extensive network of trails that provide stunning views of the mountains, valleys, and forests in the area.

Best Things to Do in North Central Idaho: Activities and Tours

There is always something fun to do in North Central Idaho. Whether you are looking for family activities or adventure tours, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Jet Boat Tours: Jet boat tours are a great way to explore the Snake River and Hells Canyon, with breathtaking views and plenty of wildlife sightings.

  • Zip-line Tours: Zip-lining is an adrenaline-pumping adventure that allows you to see the mountains and forests from a unique perspective.

  • Horseback Riding Tours: There are plenty of horseback riding tours available in North Central Idaho, which allow you to explore the mountainous terrain comfortably.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Central Idaho

If you are planning to spend a week in North Central Idaho, here is a sample itinerary that you can follow:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Lewiston, Idaho, and spend the day exploring the city.
  • Day 2: Visit Nez Perce National Historical Park and take a jet boat tour to Hells Canyon.
  • Day 3: Go hiking in the Selway-Bitterroot Wilderness Area.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at Dworshak Reservoir, fishing and boating.
  • Day 5: Visit the Museum of North Idaho in Coeur d’Alene and explore the city.
  • Day 6: Go zip-lining in the mountains.
  • Day 7: Take a horseback riding tour in the Palouse region before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of North Central Idaho

When it comes to food and drink, North Central Idaho has plenty to offer. From local cuisine to craft beer, here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Pie: This regional specialty is a must-try for dessert lovers.
  • Finger Steaks: A unique take on the chicken strip, made with steak instead.
  • Fry Bread: A Native American dish served with various toppings, including honey, jam, or powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: North Central Idaho has several wineries that produce award-winning wines.
  • Beer: With several breweries in the region, you can enjoy some great craft beer.
  • Huckleberry Lemonade: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made with local huckleberries.

Where to Stay in North Central Idaho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in North Central Idaho, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Best Western Plus University Inn: This hotel is located in Moscow, Idaho, and offers a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and a fitness center.

  • River Dance Lodge: This lodge is located in Kooskia, Idaho, and offers luxurious accommodations in a beautiful setting.

  • Hi-Moscow Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the heart of Moscow and offers comfortable dormitory-style accommodations.

What to Buy in North Central Idaho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some of the best options:

  • Huckleberry Products: You can find huckleberry jam, candy, and other products at local shops and markets.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans make beautiful jewelry from natural materials found in the region.
  • Native American Art: The region has several galleries and shops that sell authentic Native American art.

Local Culture and Customs of North Central Idaho

North Central Idaho has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s worth taking the time to learn about the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Idaho is known for its potatoes, and you can find them on almost every menu.
  • The region is home to many Native American tribes, and their traditions are still very much alive.
  • The people in North Central Idaho are known for being friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit North Central Idaho

The best time to visit North Central Idaho depends on what you plan to do. If you’re looking for outdoor activities, the summer months are the perfect time to visit. However, if you’re interested in winter sports or chasing fall colors, consider visiting in the fall or winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Central Idaho

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting North Central Idaho:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Take your time to explore the region, as there is plenty to see and do.
  • Be respectful of the local people and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage behind; always pack out what you pack in.
  • Disturb the wildlife or their habitats.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Central Idaho

North Central Idaho is a relatively safe region, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when exploring the wilderness. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always carry a map and a compass, even if you’re using a GPS device.
  • Bring plenty of water and snacks on hikes.
  • Be aware of wildlife, including bears and mountain lions.

Health in North Central Idaho

North Central Idaho is generally a healthy region, but it’s wise to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips:

  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from the sun and bugs.
  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Know the location of the nearest hospital in case of an emergency.

Getting to North Central Idaho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Central Idaho

The closest airport to North Central Idaho is Lewiston-Nez Perce County Regional Airport, but it has limited flights. The Spokane International Airport in Washington is another option that offers more flights and destinations.

Bus Stations Near North Central Idaho

Greyhound has a bus station in Lewiston, Idaho, which is the main bus terminal for the region.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around North Central Idaho.

How to Get Around in North Central Idaho

The best way to get around North Central Idaho is by car, especially if you plan to explore the outdoors. However, taxi and shuttle services are available in some areas.

Where to Go Next from North Central Idaho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore the surrounding areas, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Coeur d’Alene, Idaho: This town is known for its beautiful lake and outdoor activities.
  • Missoula, Montana: Located just across the border from Idaho, Missoula is a beautiful college town filled with cultural attractions.
  • Pullman, Washington: This college town is known for its beautiful parks and trails.

Conclusion

North Central Idaho is a region that has something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions. With so much to see and do, you won’t be disappointed with a trip to this beautiful region. Be sure to plan your itinerary and take the time to explore all that North Central Idaho has to offer.
