Introduction to Custer Country

Custer Country is a beautiful region located in Montana, in the heart of the Rocky Mountains. It is named after Lieutenant Colonel George Armstrong Custer, who led a famous expedition to explore the area in the late 1800s. Today, Custer Country is known for its unspoiled wilderness, stunning natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors come here to experience the great outdoors, learn about regional history and culture, and explore some of the most beautiful and awe-inspiring sights in the United States.

List of Places to Visit in Custer Country

Here are some of the must-visit sights in Custer Country:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Custer Battlefield Museum: Learn about the famous Battle of Little Bighorn and its impact on American history at this comprehensive museum.
  • Miles City Saddlery: Visit this historic saddlery to learn about the art of crafting fine leather goods.
  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area: Explore the history and culture of the Crow and Shoshone tribes at this beautiful and scenic area.

Natural Attractions of Custer Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yellowstone National Park: Witness some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the world at the world-famous Yellowstone National Park.
  • Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument: Explore the history and natural beauty of the site where Lieutenant Colonel George Armstrong Custer and his troops met their demise.
  • Beartooth Highway: Drive along one of the most scenic routes in America and experience breathtaking vistas, soaring peaks, and crystal-clear lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Custer Country

Custer Country is a hiker’s paradise, with an abundance of trails that lead through some of the most stunning landscapes in the United States. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Granite Pass Trail: This 16-mile hike takes you up to the stunning Granite Pass, with gorgeous views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Beartooth Plateau Trail: This trail winds through the stunning Beartooth Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Glacier Lake Trail: This easy 2-mile hike leads to a crystal-clear mountain lake surrounded by rugged peaks.

Best Things to Do in Custer Country: Activities and Tours

From hiking and biking to fishing and wildlife watching, Custer Country has something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do in the area:

  • Fly Fishing: Cast a fly into some of the cleanest and clearest waters in America and experience some of the best fly fishing in the world.
  • Horseback Riding: Giddy-up and explore the stunning Montana countryside on the back of a horse.
  • White-water Rafting: Experience the thrill of white-water rapids on a rafting trip down the Yellowstone River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Custer Country

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Custer Country, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1

  • Visit the Custer Battlefield Museum and learn about the history and legacy of the Battle of Little Bighorn.

Day 2

  • Hike the Granite Pass Trail for stunning mountain views.
  • Head to Miles City Saddlery and explore the art of leather-crafting.

Day 3

  • Drive the Beartooth Highway and enjoy the stunning scenic vistas.
  • Visit the Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area and learn about the history and culture of the Crow and Shoshone tribes.

Day 4

  • Explore Yellowstone National Park and witness some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the world.

Day 5

  • Go fly fishing on the Yellowstone River and experience some of the best trout fishing in the world.

Day 6

  • Take a relaxing horseback ride through the Montana countryside.
  • Visit the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument and learn about the history and natural beauty of the area.

Day 7

  • Go white-water rafting down the Yellowstone River and experience the thrill of the rapids.
  • Take a sunset hike to Glacier Lake and experience the beauty of a Montana sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Custer Country

Custer Country is known for its hearty, Western cuisine and locally sourced ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Made with locally raised bison meat, this burger is a staple of Western cuisine.
  • Huckleberry Pie: This sweet and tart pie is made with wild huckleberries, a local delicacy.
  • Montana Beef Jerky: Made with 100% Montana-raised beef, this jerky is a great snack for any outdoor adventure.

Local Beverages

  • Montana Microbrews: Try a local IPA or porter from one of the many microbreweries in the area.
  • Horse Thief Red: This award-winning wine is made from grapes grown in Montana’s fertile soils.
  • Bear Creek Distillery Whiskey: Made with locally sourced grains, this whiskey is a true taste of the Montana countryside.

Where to Stay in Custer Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Custer Country has a wide range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs and budget. Here are some of the top options:

  • Sage Lodge: This luxurious lodge offers stunning views, gourmet dining, and luxurious amenities.
  • Yellowstone Valley Lodge: Located just 30 minutes from Yellowstone National Park, this lodge offers comfortable accommodations and stunning mountain views.
  • Red Lodge Mountain Resort: Hit the slopes at this ski-in/ski-out resort and enjoy stunning views of the nearby mountains.

What to Buy in Custer Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products and souvenirs to look for when you’re visiting Custer Country:

  • Bison Jerky: Take some of Montana’s famous bison jerky home with you for a tasty reminder of your trip.
  • Montana Sapphires: These beautiful gemstones are found in the mountains of Montana and make a unique souvenir or gift.
  • Western Clothing and Boots: Shop for authentic Western clothing and boots, made with the highest-quality materials by skilled craftsmen.

Local Culture and Customs of Custer Country

Custer Country is famous for its Western hospitality and friendly locals. Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your visit:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Tip your servers and staff appropriately.

Best Time to Visit Custer Country

The best time to visit Custer Country is from May to September, when the weather is warm and dry and the natural beauty of the area is at its peak. However, the winter months also offer excellent opportunities for skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Custer Country

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when you’re visiting Custer Country:

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural beauty and outdoor activities of the area.
  • Savor the local cuisine and try new foods.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Custer Country is a safe and healthy destination for visitors. However, it’s important to take basic precautions to ensure your safety and health while you’re here.

Safety in Custer Country

  • Be aware of your surroundings and use common sense to avoid dangerous situations.
  • Follow all safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, or rafting.
  • Stay informed about weather conditions and potential hazards.

Health in Custer Country

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and heat stroke.
  • Bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you, and be prepared for minor injuries or illnesses.

Getting to Custer Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Custer Country is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind when planning your trip:

  • Airports Near Custer Country: Billings Logan International Airport is the closest major airport to Custer Country, with direct flights to many major U.S. cities.
  • Bus Stations Near Custer Country: Greyhound buses stop in several towns in the area, including Billings, Bozeman, and Cody.
  • Other Options: Rental cars and private shuttle services are also available.

How to Get Around in Custer Country

The best way to explore Custer Country is by car, as many of the area’s top sights and attractions are spread out over a wide area. However, there are also several tour companies and outfitters that offer guided tours of the area.

Where to Go Next from Custer Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Custer Country is just one of many beautiful and exciting destinations in Montana and the Rocky Mountains. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Glacier National Park: Drive north to explore one of America’s most iconic national parks, known for its stunning glaciers, rugged mountains, and crystal-clear lakes.
  • Yellowstone Country: Head south to explore the wonders of Yellowstone National Park, with its breathtaking landscapes and geothermal features.
  • Bighorn Mountains: Venture west to discover the hidden beauty of the Bighorn Mountains, with its scenic hiking trails, picturesque lakes, and vibrant wildlife.

Conclusion

Custer Country is a unique and special destination, offering visitors the chance to experience some of the most stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage in the world. Whether you’re hiking a mountain trail, fishing a clear mountain stream, or simply enjoying the hospitality of the locals, you’re sure to create unforgettable memories in this unspoiled wilderness.
