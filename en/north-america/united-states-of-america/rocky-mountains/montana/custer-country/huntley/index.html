Introduction to Huntley

Nestled in the eastern plains of Custer Country, Huntley is a small town that offers big adventure. This hidden gem of Montana’s Rocky Mountains is the perfect destination for those who want to explore the road less traveled. Replete with towering mountains, pristine wilderness areas, and fascinating history and culture, Huntley is an ideal place to visit for adventurers of all levels.

List of Places to Visit in Huntley

  • Pompeys Pillar National Monument
  • Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument
  • Pictograph Cave State Park
  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area
  • Montana Cowboy Hall of Fame
  • Yellowstone River

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Huntley’s rich past is showcased in the numerous museums and historical sites throughout the town. Here are a few of the must-see spots:

  • Pompeys Pillar National Monument: This towering sandstone outcrop is a cultural and historical landmark. William Clark carved his name into the rock in 1806, and you can still see it today.

  • Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument: Site of the famous battle between the U.S. Army and the Lakota, Cheyenne, and Arapaho tribes. Visit the museum to learn more about the history and culture of these tribes.

  • Montana Cowboy Hall of Fame: Celebrate the legacy of the cowboy in Montana with exhibits that showcase the history and culture of the American West.

Natural Attractions of Huntley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Huntley’s natural beauty is spectacular, with stunning vistas that can be seen from miles away. Here are a few outdoor adventures to check out:

  • Pictograph Cave State Park: Hike up to the caves to view ancient rock art that dates back more than 2,000 years.

  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area: This stunning canyon is home to an incredible array of natural wonders, including towering cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and a diverse array of wildlife.

  • Yellowstone River: Known for its world-class fly fishing, the Yellowstone River is also a great place to boat or kayak.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntley

The Rocky Mountains are a hiker and biker’s paradise, and Huntley boasts some of the most scenic trails in Montana. Here are a few of the best routes to explore:

  • Crow 405 Trail: Located in Crow Agency, this trail is a moderate-level hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and plains.

  • Bighorn Canyon Trail: This 31-mile trail near Lovell, Wyoming, offers incredible views of the canyon.

  • Yellowstone River Trail: This 20-mile trail runs right along the Yellowstone River and offers easy hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Huntley: Activities and Tours

Huntley offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy during your visit. Here are a few must-do experiences:

  • Yellowstone River Rafting: Experience the thrill of white-water rafting down the legendary Yellowstone River.

  • Horseback Riding: Take a guided horseback ride through the mountains and explore Huntley’s beautiful wilderness areas on the back of a horse.

  • Bison Range Tour: Take a guided tour through the National Bison Range and see these incredible animals up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huntley

Day 1: Visit Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument. Day 2: Explore Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area. Day 3: Hike to Pompeys Pillar National Monument. Day 4: Go horseback riding in the mountains. Day 5: Take a Yellowstone River rafting trip. Day 6: Experience the National Bison Range Tour. Day 7: Enjoy a day of fishing on the Yellowstone River.

Food and Drink Scene of Huntley

Montana is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, and Huntley boasts plenty of delicious local eats. Here are a few must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Made with locally-raised buffalo meat, this burger is a staple of Montana cuisine.

  • Huckleberry Jam: This sweet jam is made from Montana’s favorite wild berry.

  • Dutch Oven Trout: Cooked over an open flame, this dish is a true outdoor eating experience.

Local Beverages

  • Microbrews: Montana is home to some incredible microbreweries, so be sure to try a local ale or lager.

  • Huckleberry Wine: Made from Montana’s famous wild huckleberries, this wine is a true gem of Montana.

Local Culture and Customs of Huntley

Montana is home to a rich and diverse culture, and Huntley is no exception. Here are a few cultural customs to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Respect the Land: Montana is known for its awe-inspiring landscapes, so be sure to tread lightly and take care of the environment.

  • Cowboy Culture: Montana has a deep connection to the cowboy way of life, so take some time to appreciate the history and culture of the American West.

Best Time to Visit Huntley

Huntley is a year-round destination, so the best time to visit depends on what type of activities you’re interested in. Summer is the busiest season, with plenty of outdoor activities and tours. Fall is a great time to visit for the fall foliage and hunting season. Winter is a great time to hit the slopes, with plenty of skiing and snowboarding opportunities in the nearby mountains.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huntley

Montana is a relatively safe destination, but always exercise caution when hiking, biking and participating in outdoor activities. Be sure to check weather conditions and trails before heading out and stay on designated paths.

Health in Huntley

It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications and a first-aid kit, as medical facilities may be limited in the area. Remember to stay hydrated and take breaks if you feel any discomfort while participating in activities.

Where to Go Next from Huntley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, here are a few nearby destinations to add to your itinerary:
