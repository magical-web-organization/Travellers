Introduction to Crow Agency

Nestled in the heart of the Custer Country in Montana’s Rocky Mountains, Crow Agency is a small town with a big history. Home to the Crow Indian Reservation, Crow Agency is steeped in Native American culture and offers visitors a unique glimpse into the tribe’s way of life. From awe-inspiring natural wonders to fascinating museums and historic landmarks, Crow Agency has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Crow Agency

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Battle of Little Bighorn National Monument: This historic site commemorates the Battle of Little Bighorn, where Lieutenant Colonel George Custer and his troops were defeated by the Sioux and Cheyenne tribes. The museum features exhibits and artifacts related to the battle, as well as guided tours of the battlefield.
  • Apsáalooke Nation Museum and Cultural Center: This museum is dedicated to the preservation and celebration of the Crow Indian culture. Visitors can learn about Crow history, traditions, and art through exhibits, interactive displays, and cultural events.
  • Chief Plenty Coups State Park: This historic site was once home to Chief Plenty Coups, a revered Crow leader. The park features his log home, as well as exhibits and artifacts related to his life and legacy.

Natural Attractions of Crow Agency: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pryor Mountain Wild Mustang Center: This center is dedicated to the preservation and protection of the Pryor Mountain Wild Horse Range, which is home to a herd of wild mustangs. Visitors can take guided tours of the range, as well as learn about the history and biology of these magnificent animals.
  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area: This stunning area encompasses over 120,000 acres of pristine wilderness, including the Bighorn Canyon, which is over 1,000 feet deep in some areas. Visitors can hike, fish, boat, and camp in the area, as well as take scenic drives and guided tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crow Agency

  • Pryor Mountain Wild Horse Range Trail: This moderate 8-mile trail passes through scenic grasslands and offers stunning views of the Pryor Mountains. Hikers may encounter wild mustangs along the way.
  • Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument Loop Trail: This easy 1.5-mile trail takes visitors around the battlefield and offers interpretive signage about the battle and its participants.

Best Things to Do in Crow Agency: Activities and Tours

  • Crow Fair Powwow and Rodeo: This annual event, held in August, is the largest Native American gathering in Montana and features traditional dancing, singing, and drumming, as well as rodeo competitions and other activities.
  • Crow Indian Reservation Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the reservation with a knowledgeable guide and learn about the history, culture, and traditions of the Crow people.

Food and Drink Scene of Crow Agency

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Bread: A traditional Native American bread made from flour, water, salt, and sugar, fried until golden brown and fluffy. Often served with honey or jam.
  • Bison Burger: A juicy burger made from locally sourced bison meat, topped with cheese, onions, lettuce, and tomato.
  • Indian Tacos: A popular dish that consists of fry bread topped with ground beef, beans, lettuce, cheese, and salsa.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Lemonade: A refreshing and tangy drink made with huckleberry syrup, fresh lemon juice, and sparkling water.
  • Flathead Cherry Wine: A sweet and fruity wine made from Flathead cherries, a local Montana specialty.
  • Sarsaparilla: A non-alcoholic carbonated drink that originated in the Old West, made from the root of the sarsaparilla plant.

Where to Stay in Crow Agency: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Plains Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Crow Agency, featuring comfortable rooms and on-site dining.
  • Crow Agency Bed and Breakfast: A cozy bed and breakfast located on a working ranch, offering stunning views and full breakfasts.
  • Crow Agency Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to meet new people and stay in the heart of the town.

What to Buy in Crow Agency: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crow Indian beadwork: Beautiful and intricate beadwork is a traditional art form of the Crow people, and visitors can purchase jewelry, clothing, and home decor items featuring this unique craftsmanship.
  • Bison Jerky: A healthy and delicious snack made from locally sourced bison meat, perfect for a long hike or road trip.
  • Huckleberry Jam: A sweet and tangy jam made from huckleberries, a wild berry that grows abundantly in Montana.

Local Culture and Customs of Crow Agency

The Crow people have a rich history and unique culture, and visitors should always be respectful and mindful of their customs and traditions. It is customary to ask permission before taking photographs or participating in any ceremonies or activities, and visitors should dress modestly and avoid wearing any clothing or accessories that may be offensive.

Best Time to Visit Crow Agency

The best time to visit Crow Agency is between June and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The Crow Fair Powwow, held in August, is a particularly popular event and a great opportunity to experience traditional Crow culture and traditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crow Agency

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the Crow people.
  • Take the time to learn about the history and culture of the area.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb any artifacts or sacred places.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Trespass on private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Crow Agency

The crime rate in Crow Agency and the surrounding areas is relatively low, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid leaving valuables in your car, and lock doors and windows when you leave your hotel room or rental car.

Health in Crow Agency

The altitude in Crow Agency is over 3,000 feet, so visitors may experience altitude sickness, especially if they have not acclimated to the altitude. To avoid altitude sickness, drink plenty of water and avoid strenuous activities for the first few days of your trip.

Getting to Crow Agency: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Crow Agency

The nearest airport to Crow Agency is the Billings Logan International Airport, located approximately 60 miles northwest of the town. From the airport, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle or taxi to Crow Agency.

Bus Stations Near Crow Agency

The nearest bus station to Crow Agency is the Greyhound bus station in Billings, approximately 60 miles northwest of the town. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle or taxi to Crow Agency.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Crow Agency, as the town is located just off Interstate 90.

How to Get Around in Crow Agency

Visitors can rent a car or bike to explore the town and surrounding areas. There are also several tour companies that offer guided tours of the area.

Where to Go Next from Crow Agency: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yellowstone National Park: One of the most famous national parks in the world, Yellowstone is located approximately 200 miles southwest of Crow Agency and offers stunning natural beauty, including geysers, hot springs, and wildlife.
  • Bozeman: Located approximately 150 miles northwest of Crow Agency, Bozeman is a bustling college town with a vibrant arts scene, numerous outdoor activities, and historic landmarks.
  • Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument: Located just south of Crow Agency, the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument is a must-visit destination for history buffs and anyone interested in Native American history and culture.

Conclusion

Crow Agency is a unique and fascinating destination that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich history and culture of the Crow people. From natural wonders to historic landmarks to traditional cuisine, Crow Agency has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty and wonder of this special Montana town.
