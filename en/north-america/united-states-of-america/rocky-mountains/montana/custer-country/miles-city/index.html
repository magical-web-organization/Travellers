Introduction to Miles City

Miles City is a city in Custer County, Montana, nestled in the Rocky Mountains of the United States. This charming town is known for its rich history, friendly residents, and stunning natural scenery. With its wide array of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine, Miles City is a fantastic destination for travelers seeking a relaxing and authentic Western experience.

List of Places to Visit in Miles City

Don’t miss out on these top attractions in Miles City:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Miles City is home to several fascinating museums and historical sites that highlight the town’s unique history. Here are a few must-visit destinations:

  • Range Riders Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the American West through exhibits of cowboy and Native American artifacts, art, photographs, and memorabilia.
  • Fort Keogh Memorial Museum: This museum displays the fascinating history of military life at Fort Keogh, a former army post that was active from 1876 to 1900.
  • Sacred Heart Catholic Church: This historic church was built in 1898 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The beautiful stained glass windows and intricate architecture are a sight to behold.

Natural Attractions of Miles City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Miles City offers plenty of opportunities to immerse yourself in the stunning natural beauty of Montana. Some popular natural attractions include:

  • Tongue River State Park: This park features 616 acres of parkland, including hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and picnic areas.
  • Spotted Eagle Recreation Area: Located south of Miles City, this scenic area offers camping, fishing, hiking, and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Pirogue Island State Park: This park is situated on an island in the Yellowstone River and offers camping facilities, hiking trails, and prime fishing locations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miles City

Miles City is surrounded by breathtaking nature and offers a variety of trails to explore on foot or by bike.

  • Yellowstone River Walk: This 2-mile trail follows the banks of the Yellowstone River and offers stunning views of the town’s picturesque surroundings.
  • Spotted Eagle Trail: This 6.5-mile trail offers a scenic hike through rolling hills and prairie grassland.
  • Kinsey Loop Trail: This 3-mile trail winds through the beautiful landscapes surrounding the town of Miles City.

Best Things to Do in Miles City: Activities and Tours

Miles City offers a myriad of exciting activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in town:

  • Bucking Horse Sale: This annual event takes place each May and features a rodeo, parades, live music, and more.
  • Biking and Hiking: Explore the many trails and paths surrounding Miles City on foot or by bike.
  • Fishing: Miles City is a prime location for fly-fishing enthusiasts, with several rivers and streams in the area offering excellent fishing opportunities.
  • Ghost Tours: Take a spooky tour of Miles City’s haunted locations, including the historic Olive Hotel and the Range Riders Museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Miles City

Here’s our recommended itinerary for a weeklong stay in Miles City:

Day 1: Explore the town’s museums and cultural attractions, including the Range Riders Museum, Sacred Heart Catholic Church, and the Fort Keogh Memorial Museum.

Day 2: Hit the trails and enjoy the stunning natural surroundings. Go hiking on the Yellowstone River Walk or the Spotted Eagle Trail, or take a bike ride on the Kinsey Loop Trail.

Day 3: Enjoy a day of fishing on the Tongue River or the Yellowstone River.

Day 4: Take a trip to Pirogue Island State Park for more hiking and fishing, or relax with a picnic in the parkland.

Day 5: Check out the local food and drink scene, with a visit to a brewery or a farmer’s market.

Day 6: Take a ghost tour and discover the spooky side of Miles City.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a scenic drive through the surrounding Rocky Mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Miles City

Miles City’s food and drink scene is a testament to its Western heritage, with plenty of hearty meals and locally brewed beers. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burgers: Try a juicy burger made with locally sourced bison meat.
  • Beef Brisket: Slow-cooked to perfection, this classic dish is a Montana favorite.
  • Huckleberry Pie: This fruit pie is made with the tart and sweet huckleberry, a native Montana berry.

Local Beverages

  • Montana Breweries: Sample locally brewed beers from breweries like the Miles City Brewery and the Carter County Brewery.
  • Cowboy Coffee: This strong, black coffee is perfect for fueling a day of outdoor adventures.

Where to Stay in Miles City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Miles City offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Olive Hotel: This historic hotel was built in 1912 and has been lovingly restored to its former glory. The cozy rooms feature antique furnishings and modern amenities.
  • Miles City Hotel and Suites: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Airbnb: For a more unique accommodation experience, try booking a stay at one of Miles City’s many Airbnb rentals or vacation homes.

What to Buy in Miles City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a special souvenir or gift to bring back home? Here are some ideas:

  • Montana-made Crafts and Artifacts: The Range Riders Museum gift shop sells a variety of locally made crafts and artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, and Native American artwork.
  • Bison Meat: Bring home a taste of Montana with locally sourced bison meat from a farmer’s market or specialty store.
  • Western Apparel: Pick up a classic cowboy hat, boots, or belt buckle to complete your Western wardrobe.

Local Culture and Customs of Miles City

Miles City is proud of its unique Western heritage and traditions. Visitors can experience the local culture through events like the Bucking Horse Sale and by exploring the town’s museums and historical sites. Keep in mind that Montana is a firearm-friendly state, so it’s not uncommon to see people carrying arms in public places.

Best Time to Visit Miles City

The best time to visit Miles City is during the summer months, from June to August, when the temperatures are warm and the trails and outdoor attractions are open. Visitors looking to avoid the crowds may want to consider visiting in the shoulder season of May or September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miles City

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Miles City:

Do:

  • Explore the town’s rich Western heritage and take part in local traditions and events.
  • Take advantage of the town’s stunning natural surroundings and go hiking, biking, or fishing.
  • Sample local cuisine and brews to get a taste of Montana culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be surprised to see people carrying arms in public places. Montana is a firearm-friendly state.
  • Don’t forget to respect the natural environment by following Leave No Trace principles and being mindful of wildlife habitats.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Miles City should take standard travel precautions, like being mindful of their belongings and avoiding dangerous or high-risk activities. Here are some specific safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Miles City

  • Always carry bear spray while hiking in Montana’s national parks.
  • Be aware of the possibility of flash floods and severe weather during the summer months.
  • Use caution while driving on Montana’s rugged back roads and highways.

Health in Miles City

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during outdoor activities.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against sunburn and bug bites.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience symptoms of altitude sickness while hiking in high-elevation areas.

Getting to Miles City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Miles City is located in southeastern Montana, approximately 150 miles east of Billings. Here are some transportation options for getting to Miles City:

Airports Near Miles City

  • Miles City Municipal Airport (MLS) is the closest airport, located just three miles north of Miles City.
  • Billings Logan International Airport (BIL) is located approximately 150 miles to the west of Miles City and offers more extensive flight options.

Bus Stations Near Miles City

  • Greyhound operates a bus station in Miles City, located at 600 Palmer St.

Other Options

  • Many visitors choose to drive to Miles City, as it is conveniently located near several major highways, including I-94 and US 12.

How to Get Around in Miles City

Miles City is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. However, visitors who wish to explore the surrounding areas may want to consider renting a car or using a rideshare service like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Miles City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Miles City is a great basecamp for exploring other nearby destinations and attractions. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Yellowstone National Park: Located approximately 220 miles southwest of Miles City, Yellowstone is an iconic American destination known for its geothermal features, wildlife, and stunning landscapes.
  • Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument: This historic site is located approximately 75 miles south-southeast of Miles City and commemorates the 1876 battle between U.S. Army troops and Native American tribes.
  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area: Located approximately 115 miles northwest of Miles City, this stunning area encompasses 70,000 acres of wilderness, including canyons, cliffs, and clear waters.

Conclusion

Miles City offers an authentic Western experience with its rich history, stunning natural surroundings, and friendly locals. Whether you’re here for the outdoor adventures, the cultural attractions, or the hearty cuisine, this charming town is sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler. Plan your trip to Miles City today and discover the best of Montana’s landscapes, heritage, and culture.
