Introduction to Laurel

Laurel is a city located in Custer Country, in Montana, in the Rocky Mountains, in the United States of America, in North America. Known as the “city with a future,” Laurel is a bustling city with plenty of attractions and activities for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Laurel

Whether you’re interested in learning about Laurel’s history or experiencing the natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Here’s a list of top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yellowstone Art Museum: Founded in 1964, the museum showcases works from over 50 artists and provides educational programs for visitors.
  • Carbon County Historical Society and Museum: A non-profit organization founded in 1978, the museum features objects, artifacts, and photos from the history of Carbon County.
  • Laurel Depot Museum: Built in 1909, the museum is a restored railroad depot and a great place to learn about Laurel’s history.

Natural Attractions of Laurel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Rimrocks: Located just south of Laurel, the Rimrocks are a geological formation that offers outstanding views of the city and surrounding landscape.
  • Yellowstone River: The longest undammed river in the United States, the Yellowstone River flows through Montana and is a popular spot for fishing, floating, and whitewater rafting.
  • Cooney Reservoir: A beautiful lake located just outside of Laurel, Cooney Reservoir offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laurel

Laurel is a great place to hike and bike, with several trails offering excellent views of the surrounding mountains and landscape. Here are some great trails to check out:

  • Laurel Hometown Trail: A paved trail that stretches for nearly two miles along the Yellowstone River, the Laurel Hometown Trail is perfect for biking, walking, and jogging.
  • Zimmerman Park Trail: A scenic trail that offers views of the city, the river, and the surrounding mountains. The trail is approximately two miles in length.
  • Homestead Trail: A moderate hike that takes visitors through the beautiful countryside and offers views of the Yellowstone River.

Best Things to Do in Laurel: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Laurel. Here are some suggestions:

  • Laurel Farmers Market: Held every Thursday from June to September, the market features locally grown produce, crafts, and live entertainment.
  • Laurel Golf Club: A public golf course with 9 holes, the Laurel Golf Club is a great place to tee off and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Laurel Paddle Club: A non-profit organization that offers paddling opportunities on the Yellowstone River and Cooney Reservoir.

Food and Drink Scene of Laurel

No trip to Laurel is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak: Montana is famous for its beef dishes, and Laurel is no exception. Try a juicy steak at the Montana Brewing Company.
  • Swedish meatballs: A nod to the city’s Scandinavian heritage, Swedish meatballs are a popular dish at the Railroad Cafe.
  • Huckleberry pie: Made with local huckleberries, this pie is a must-try dessert at the C-Store Cafe.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Montana is home to a thriving craft beer scene, and Laurel is no exception. Visit Red Lodge Ales Brewing Company for some great local brews.
  • Huckleberry juice: Available at most local restaurants and cafes, this refreshing beverage is made from the local huckleberries.

Where to Stay in Laurel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a budget-friendly option, Laurel has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some top picks:

  • Super 8 Laurel: A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Best Western Yellowstone Crossing: A mid-range hotel with great amenities, including a pool and fitness center.
  • Red Lion Hotel & Conference Center Billings: A luxurious hotel located just 15 minutes from Laurel.

What to Buy in Laurel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some souvenirs or gifts to bring home with you? Here are some great local products to check out:

  • Huckleberry jam: Made from the local huckleberries, this jam is a great souvenir and makes a tasty breakfast spread.
  • Montana-made crafts: Check out the Carbon County Arts Guild & Depot Gallery for some great locally-made crafts and art.
  • Cowboy hats: A nod to Montana’s cowboy culture, a cowboy hat from Maurice’s Hat Shop is a great souvenir to bring back with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Laurel

Montana is famous for its cowboy culture, and Laurel is no exception. The city’s Scandinavian heritage is also celebrated, with several events throughout the year featuring traditional costumes and dances.

Best Time to Visit Laurel

The best time to visit Laurel is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the fall months also offer beautiful scenery as the leaves change colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laurel

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Laurel:

Do:

  • Enjoy the local cuisine, especially the steak and huckleberry dishes.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and paddling.
  • Visit the local museums and learn about the history of the area.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the power of the sun. Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated when spending time outdoors.
  • Disrespect the local wildlife. Practice safe hiking and avoid leaving food out that could attract bears or other animals.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions. Be respectful when attending any local events or celebrations.

Safety and Health

Before traveling to Laurel, it’s important to be aware of any potential safety and health concerns.

Safety in Laurel

Laurel is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions when spending time outdoors. Be aware of the potential for wildlife encounters, and follow any posted safety guidelines when hiking or paddling.

Health in Laurel

As with any travel destination, it’s important to take precautions to protect your health. Be aware of any potential risks of insect-borne illnesses, and make sure to stay hydrated when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Laurel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Laurel is relatively easy, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Laurel

The Billings Logan International Airport is located approximately 20 miles from Laurel, with several major airlines offering daily flights.

Bus Stations Near Laurel

Greyhound offers daily bus service to Billings, which is approximately 15 miles from Laurel.

Other Options

For those driving to Laurel, the city is easily accessible via Interstate 90.

How to Get Around in Laurel

Getting around Laurel is relatively easy, with several transportation options available. Rental cars are available at the airport, and taxis and ridesharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Laurel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Montana, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to check out:

  • Billings: The largest city in Montana, Billings offers a variety of attractions and activities for visitors, including museums, art galleries, and outdoor recreation.
  • Yellowstone National Park: Located just a few hours from Laurel, Yellowstone National Park is home to some of the most stunning natural scenery in the country.
  • Beartooth Highway: This scenic drive takes visitors through the stunning Beartooth Mountains and offers some of the most breathtaking views in Montana.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the local history and culture, enjoying the natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains, or simply relaxing and taking in the scenery, Laurel is a destination that has something for everyone.
