Introduction to Havre

Nestled in Russell Country and the Rocky Mountains of Montana, Havre is a hidden gem that boasts of stunning scenery, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and unique experiences. This charming city is home to friendly locals, fascinating museums, exciting festivals, and unspoiled wilderness.

Whether you’re a history buff, an avid hiker, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Havre has something for you. This guide provides a comprehensive tour of Havre and its hidden gems, so you can make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Havre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • H. Earl Clack Museum: This museum is a treasure trove of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Havre and Montana.
  • Havre Beneath the Streets: Experience Havre’s past firsthand by exploring the underground tunnels and historic buildings of this fascinating attraction.
  • Bear Paw Battlefield: This historic site is a must-visit for history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts alike. It was here that the final battle of the Nez Perce War took place in 1877.

Natural Attractions of Havre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaver Creek Park: This stunning park is home to sprawling meadows, rugged terrain, and abundant wildlife. It offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
  • Bullhook Bottoms: This wetland area is a paradise for birdwatchers and nature lovers. Its diverse ecosystem is home to over 200 bird species, including sandhill cranes, pelicans, and bald eagles.
  • Chinook Point: This scenic overlook boasts stunning views of the Milk River Valley and the surrounding mountains. It’s the perfect spot for a picnic or a photo op.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Havre

  • Rocky Boy’s Trail: This hiking and biking trail winds through the rugged terrain of the Rocky Boy Indian Reservation. It offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and a chance to explore the local culture.
  • Bullhook Bottoms Trail: This easy trail winds through the wetland areas of Bullhook Bottoms and is a great spot for birdwatching and nature observation.

Best Things to Do in Havre: Activities and Tours

  • Havre Festival Days: This annual festival is a celebration of Havre’s heritage and culture. It features live music, arts and crafts, food vendors, and a parade.
  • Havre Farmers Market: This weekly farmers market is the perfect spot to sample local produce, crafts, and baked goods.
  • Havre Historic Trolley Tour: This tour takes visitors on a guided tour of Havre’s historic sites and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Havre

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry pie: This local delicacy is made with Montana’s famous huckleberries and is a must-try for dessert lovers.
  • Bison burger: This flavorful burger is made from locally raised bison and is a great way to sample Montana’s culinary heritage.

Local Beverages

  • Montana microbrews: Montana has a vibrant microbrewery scene, and Havre has several local breweries that serve up craft beers with unique flavors and styles.
  • Huckleberry wine: This unique and delicious wine is made with Montana’s signature huckleberries and is a great way to sample the local flavors.

Where to Stay in Havre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Havre Inn & Suites: This affordable hotel offers clean and comfortable accommodations and is a great option for budget-conscious travelers.
  • Jamestown Hotel: This charming guesthouse is a historic landmark and offers cozy rooms and a unique local experience.

What to Buy in Havre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry products: Huckleberries are a staple of Montana cuisine, and Havre has plenty of local products that feature this delicious fruit, including jams, syrups, and candies.
  • Native American crafts: Havre is home to several Native American tribes, and visitors can find a range of locally crafted pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Havre

Havre is a friendly and welcoming community that values its rich cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including Montana’s cowboy culture and Native American heritage.

Best Time to Visit Havre

The best time to visit Havre is from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the city’s history and culture year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Havre

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Sample local cuisine and products.
  • Explore the city’s history and culture.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local communities and cultures.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore safety guidelines and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Havre

Havre is generally a safe and friendly city, but visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as locking doors and windows, not leaving valuables in plain sight, and staying aware of surroundings.

Health in Havre

Visitors to Havre should be aware of the risks of exposure to extreme temperatures, dehydration, and sunburn. It’s important to drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and take breaks in shaded areas.

Where to Go Next from Havre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Great Falls: This nearby city is home to stunning natural attractions, such as Giant Springs State Park and the Missouri River, as well as cultural attractions like the C.M. Russell Museum and the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center.
  • Glacier National Park: This iconic national park is a must-see for nature lovers, with its stunning mountain landscapes, glaciers, and abundant wildlife.
  • Helena: Montana’s picturesque capital city is a great spot to learn more about the state’s history and culture, with its museums, historic landmarks, and vibrant arts scene.
