Introduction to Great Falls

Great Falls is a charming city located in the heart of Montana’s Russell Country, situated in the Rocky Mountains of the United States of America. Known as the “Electric City,” it is home to many cultural and historical landmarks, natural attractions, and exciting outdoor activities. With its friendly locals, rich culture, and stunning landscapes, Great Falls is an ideal travel destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Great Falls

Whether you’re interested in museums and historical sites, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, Great Falls has something for everyone. Here are some of the places you don’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • C.M. Russell Museum - An art museum dedicated to the works of Charles M. Russell, a renowned Western artist.
  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center - A museum that explores the journey of the famous explorers Lewis and Clark.
  • Giant Springs State Park - A historic park that features one of the largest freshwater springs in the country, as well as a fish hatchery and visitor center.

Natural Attractions of Great Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gates of the Mountains - A scenic boat tour through the canyon on the Missouri River.
  • Giant Springs Heritage State Park - A park that offers great hiking trails, picnic areas, and fishing spots.
  • Ryan Dam Recreation Area - A park that provides camping, swimming, and fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Falls

Great Falls has many scenic hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Here are some of the best trails in and around the city:

  • River’s Edge Trail - A 60-mile trail that follows the Missouri River through Great Falls, featuring many scenic spots and parks along the route.
  • Morony Dam Trail - A 2.5-mile trail that offers great views of the Missouri River and the valley.
  • Sluice Boxes State Park Trail - A scenic and challenging trail that takes you through the narrow canyon of the Belt Creek.

Best Things to Do in Great Falls: Activities and Tours

Great Falls offers a plethora of activities and tours for travelers to experience. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Visit the Sip ‘n Dip Lounge - A famous tiki bar where you can watch mermaids swim in a pool behind the bar.
  • Try Fly Fishing - Great Falls is a premier fly fishing destination, with many great spots and companies offering guided tours.
  • Explore the Underground City - Take a tour of the tunnels below the city that were used for various purposes, including smuggling during the Prohibition era.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great Falls

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Great Falls:

Day Activity
1 Visit Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center
2 Hike the Morony Dam Trail or visit Sluice Boxes State Park
3 Explore C.M. Russell Museum and Giant Springs State Park
4 Take a tour of the underground city
5 Visit Gates of the Mountains and go on a boat tour
6 Try fly fishing with a local guide
7 Relax and enjoy the scenic River’s Edge Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Great Falls

Great Falls has a rich food and drink scene that is worth exploring. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger - A local delicacy that features a juicy and flavorful bison patty on a bun.
  • Huckleberry Jam - A sweet and tangy jam made from local huckleberries, which can be enjoyed on toast or as a topping for ice cream.
  • Montana-style Pizza - A unique pizza that features locally sourced ingredients, such as bison, elk, and venison.

Local Beverages

  • Big Sky Brewing Company - A popular brewery that offers a variety of craft beers, including the famous Moose Drool Brown Ale.
  • Montana Distillery - A distillery that specializes in small-batch spirits, such as vodka, gin, and whiskey.
  • Huckleberry Wine - A sweet and fruity wine made from Montana’s signature huckleberries.

Where to Stay in Great Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Great Falls offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some options to consider:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Great Falls - A modern and comfortable hotel located in the city center.
  • KOA Holiday Campground - A family-friendly campground that offers tent and RV sites, as well as cabins.
  • Great Falls Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that provides basic amenities and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Great Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique and authentic souvenirs to bring back home? Here are some items to consider:

  • Handmade Jewelry - Great Falls is known for its talented artisans, who create beautiful jewelry pieces using local materials, such as turquoise and bear grass.
  • Montana-made Goods - Visit the local farmers’ markets and shops to find a variety of locally made products, such as jams, honey, and woolen products.
  • Native American Crafts - Montana is home to many Native American tribes, and you can find authentic crafts and artworks at local shops and galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Falls

Great Falls has a rich and diverse culture, influenced by its Native American history, Western heritage, and modern lifestyle. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect the Land and Wildlife - Montana is known for its pristine natural landscapes and wildlife, so make sure to follow Leave No Trace principles and be mindful of your impact on the environment.
  • Appreciate Local Traditions - Montana has a strong Western culture with many traditional events, such as rodeos and powwows, which are worth experiencing if you have the chance.
  • Be Friendly and Open-Minded - Montanans are known for their friendliness and hospitality, so make sure to embrace the local culture and connect with the locals during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Great Falls

The best time to visit Great Falls depends on your personal preferences and the activities you plan to do. Here’s a breakdown of each season:

  • Summer (June-August) - The peak season for tourism in Great Falls, with warm weather and many outdoor activities available. However, it can be crowded and expensive during this time.
  • Fall (September-November) - A beautiful time to visit Great Falls, with mild weather, stunning fall foliage, and fewer crowds.
  • Winter (December-February) - Great Falls experiences cold and snowy winters, but it’s also a great time for winter activities, such as skiing and snowshoeing.
  • Spring (March-May) - A quiet and peaceful time to visit, with cool weather and blooming wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Falls

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Great Falls:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, including bison, huckleberries, and craft beers.
  • Do explore the local attractions and natural beauty, such as museums, parks, and hiking trails.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs, and be open-minded and friendly towards the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment, and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Don’t trespass on private property or disturb the wildlife.
  • Don’t stereotype the locals, and avoid making insensitive or offensive comments about their way of life.

Safety and Health

Great Falls is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s always important to be prepared and cautious when traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Great Falls

  • Always lock your car and secure your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist spots.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and avoid wandering alone in unfamiliar areas, especially at night.
  • Keep a first-aid kit and emergency contact information on hand, and be aware of natural hazards, such as wildlife and rough terrain.

Health in Great Falls

  • Consult your doctor before traveling, and make sure to have all necessary vaccinations and medications.
  • Carry bottled water and avoid drinking from natural water sources, which may contain bacteria or other contaminants.
  • Protect yourself from the sun and cold weather, and use insect repellent to avoid tick bites.

Getting to Great Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Great Falls is easily accessible by plane, bus, or car, depending on where you are coming from. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Great Falls

  • Great Falls International Airport - Located just three miles from the city center, this airport serves several major airlines, including Delta, United, and Allegiant.

Bus Stations Near Great Falls

  • Great Falls Transit District - The local public transportation system that serves the city and nearby areas, including buses and shuttles.

Other Options

  • Amtrak - The national passenger train service that operates a station in Shelby, about 90 miles from Great Falls.
  • Car Rental - Great Falls has several car rental companies, including Hertz and Enterprise, which offer a variety of vehicles for rent.

How to Get Around in Great Falls

Great Falls is a compact and walkable city, but it’s also easy to get around by car or public transportation. Here are some options:

  • Walking - Many of the attractions and amenities in Great Falls are within walking distance of each other, making it easy to explore the city on foot.
  • Driving - Great Falls has a well-maintained and navigable road network, and car rentals and taxis are available for those who prefer driving.
  • Public Transportation - The Great Falls Transit District provides affordable and convenient bus and shuttle services throughout the city and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Great Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Great Falls is located in a prime location for exploring other destinations in Montana and beyond. Here are some ideas for nearby excursions and destinations:

  • Glacier National Park - A stunning national park in northern Montana, known for its glaciers, wildlife, and hiking trails.
  • Yellowstone National Park - Located just a few hours’ drive from Great Falls, Yellowstone is one of the most famous national parks in the world, featuring geysers, hot springs, and wildlife.
  • Missoula - A vibrant college town located about two hours west of Great Falls, with a rich arts and cultural scene, as well as beautiful natural surroundings.

Conclusion

Great Falls is a hidden gem of Montana, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you come for the museums and attractions, the outdoor adventures, or the delicious food and drink, you won’t be disappointed with your trip to this charming city. We hope this guide helps you plan your visit and discover all that Great Falls has to offer.
