Introduction to Shelby

Shelby is a city located in Russell Country, in Montana, within the Rocky Mountains of the United States of America, in North America. It’s an idyllic community, surrounded by natural beauty, rich history, and a vibrant culture. Shelby is an adventurer’s paradise, with plenty of outdoor activities, sights, and sounds to enjoy. Whether you’re visiting for the first time or returning for another trip, there’s always something new and exciting to discover in Shelby.

List of Places to Visit in Shelby

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shelby provides a unique window into the Wild West. The city and its surroundings serve as a testament to the rich history of the area. Here are some of Shelby’s best cultural attractions:

  • Marias Museum of History & Art: This museum tells the story of history and art in Montana, and continuous to reflect the artifacts of the variety of native tribes that once roamed the lands around Shelby.
  • Port of Sweet Grass County: The Canadian-American boundary line was an important feature of settlement in this region, and at the Museum of the Sweet Grass County Historical Society you can learn the rich history of the area.
  • The Old Milwaukee Depot: The depot was built in 1907 and is a true gem of Shelby’s architecture. Today, it is the location of the Chamber of Commerce and occasionally hosts events and shows.

Natural Attractions of Shelby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shelby boasts a natural abundance of beauty and wonder. Whether you prefer a leisurely outdoor activity or a challenging adventure, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of Shelby’s best natural attractions:

  • Shelby Riverwalk: This trail along Marias River is famous for its scenic views and offers a peaceful walk through nature.
  • Blackfeet Reservation: Take a day trip to the Blackfeet reservation and experience the spectacular natural beauty of Montana, while learning about the traditions and history of the Blackfeet tribe.
  • Glacier National Park: Just a short drive from Shelby is one of the most beautiful national parks. With its quartzite cliffs, clear mountain streams, and glaciers, it’s a natural paradise for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shelby

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will find an abundance of trails and routes in Shelby. Here are some of the best options near the city:

  • Dry Fork Trail: A 9-mile out-and-back trail, which runs east of the Marias River, with a stunning view of Montana’s open landscapes.
  • Johnson Reservoir Trailhead: A 7-mile, moderately trafficked loop trail, located near Shelby, featuring a beautiful lake.
  • Kevin Nicholson Memorial Trail: A 3-mile, lightly trafficked loop trail, located near Shelby, with a stunning river view.

Best Things to Do in Shelby: Activities and Tours

Shelby has something to offer for everyone. Here are some fun things to do when visiting Shelby:

  • ExplorationWorks!: A great place for the kids to enjoy hands-on science exhibits and classes.
  • Wildlife viewing tours: Explore Montana’s natural beauty with wildlife viewing tours that will take you deep into the wilds where you can see moose, elk, and other animals.
  • Horseback riding: Enjoy an unforgettable experience while you horseback ride to the Rocky Mountains or nearby state parks.

Food and Drink Scene of Shelby

Shelby’s dining scene is varied and rich, offering a blend of international, contemporary, and regional cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Montana Bison Burger: Grilled bison burger topped with a chipotle aioli, served with fries, is one of Shelby’s most popular dishes.
  • Huckleberry Cinnamon Rolls: These local sweet treats will make your mouth water; the tanginess of the huckleberry mixed with the sweetness of the cinnamon makes for the perfect bite.
  • Bison steak: For the ultimate Montana dining experience, you must try the bison steak, it’s juicy and full of flavor, and simply divine.

Local Beverages

  • Wheat Beer: This beer is a popular choice in Shelby, and you can find it in most bars and restaurants.
  • Huckleberry Margarita: This margarita is a delicious twist on the classic, with the added sweetness of huckleberry, Montana’s favorite berry.

Where to Stay in Shelby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shelby has plenty of accommodation options that cater to every preference and budget. Here are some top-rated options:

  • Wingate by Wyndham: An upscale hotel with plenty of amenities in a convenient location.
  • Comfort Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly option that offers comfort and convenience in the heart of Shelby.
  • Hampton Inn by Hilton: A modern and equipped hotel that provides all the amenities needed for a first-class stay.

What to Buy in Shelby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without bringing back a few souvenirs. Here are some of the popular local products and souvenirs to take home from your Shelby trip:

  • Huckleberry Jam: Sweet, delicious, and exclusive to Montana, huckleberry jam is something you must buy and take back home.
  • Handmade pottery: Beautiful handmade pottery by local artists is available in many stores and gift shops all over Shelby.
  • Montana craft beer: Montana’s local beer scene is blooming, and there are many breweries from which to choose.

Local Culture and Customs of Shelby

Montana has a rich history and culture, and its people take pride in preserving and honoring its heritage. Here are some notable attributes of Shelby’s culture and customs:

  • Rodeo: Rodeo is famous in Shelby, with events being held locally throughout the year.
  • Fair and Rodeo Parade: The town celebrates its heritage with an annual parade during the county fair and rodeo in August. It’s a great time to enjoy Shelby’s sense of community.
  • Agriculture: Agriculture is one of the primary industries and a significant cultural part of Montana. Local ranches and farms contribute to the state’s vibrant culture.

Best Time to Visit Shelby

The optimal time to visit Shelby is from June through September when the weather is agreeable, and the roads are accessible for exploring. Avoid visiting in December and January as the winter months can hamper your exploration of Shelby’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shelby

Here are a few things you should and shouldn’t do in Shelby:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and people
  • Enjoy nature’s beauty in a sustainable way
  • Drive carefully on the roads to avoid accidents

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on trails and recreation areas
  • Disturb wildlife by getting too close or feeding them
  • Ignore weather warnings and alerts

Safety and Health

Safety in Shelby

Montana is a safe state, but that doesn’t mean you should let your guard down. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Lock your vehicle while taking a hike or simply visiting a tourist spot
  • Stay aware of your surroundings at all times
  • Stay on marked trails when exploring mountains and parks

Health in Shelby

Shelby has all the necessary health facilities, including clinics and hospitals, in case of medical emergencies. Schuyler Healthcare in Shelby provides emergency, trauma, and rehabilitation services.

Getting to Shelby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shelby

Shelby is serviced by Great Falls International Airport, approximately 81 miles away from the city. Glacier Park International Airport is a little farther, about 150 miles from the city, but it may have better direct flights.

Bus Stations Near Shelby

The Greyhound bus terminal is located in Shelby, offering frequent schedules connecting Shelby to other Montana cities.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best way to get around and explore Shelby. Many car rental companies have rental outlets in and around the city.

How to Get Around in Shelby

Getting around Shelby is easy because the city is well-planned and compact, and you can use a car, bike or walk to explore local attractions.

Where to Go Next from Shelby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore after visiting Shelby, here are some nearby destinations to add to your itinerary:

  • Browning: Located about 70 miles northwest of Shelby and is the headquarters of the Blackfeet Nation.
  • Great Falls: A perfect destination for history buffs with plenty of historical landmarks, approximately 90 miles from Shelby.
  • Helena: The state capital of Montana, located about 160 miles south of Shelby.

Conclusion

Shelby is a perfect destination to get away from the hustle and bustle of modern life and enjoy the natural scenic beauty of Montana. Whether you want to indulge in outdoor activities, learn about the rich history of Montana, or experience the authentic culture of the Wild West, Shelby is an excellent choice. Plan your trip to Shelby today to experience authentic Montana.
