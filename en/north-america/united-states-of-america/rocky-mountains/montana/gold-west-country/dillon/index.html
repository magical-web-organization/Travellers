Introduction to Dillon

Welcome to Dillon - a hidden gem in the heart of Montana’s Gold West Country. This charming town is nestled in the rocky mountains and offers an abundance of natural beauty and outdoor activities. Rich in history and culture, Dillon is the perfect destination for anyone seeking a true Montana experience.

List of Places to Visit in Dillon

Dillon offers a variety of places to visit, ranging from historical landmarks to outdoor activities, such as:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Beaverhead County Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Beaverhead Valley, including Native American culture and early mining settlements.
  2. Dillon Visitor Center: Here, you’ll find information about the local area and can pick up brochures for self-guided tours.
  3. Clark’s Lookout State Park: This historical landmark offers impressive views of the Beaverhead Valley, and was used by Captain William Clark as a lookout during his travels.

Natural Attractions of Dillon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bannack State Park: This former mining town turned state park is a well-preserved ghost town with hiking trails, fishing, and camping.
  2. Red Rocks Lakes National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge is a bird-watcher’s paradise, with over 235 species of birds and scenic hiking trails.
  3. Pioneer Mountain Scenic Byway: This stunning drive offers breathtaking views of the rugged mountain ranges and valleys of Montana.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dillon

Dillon boasts beautiful hiking and biking routes, such as:

  1. Blacktail Trail: This 13.5-mile hiking trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  2. Bannack State Park Trails: This park offers beautiful hiking and biking trails, with varying levels of difficulty and distances.
  3. Grasshopper Trailhead: Located in nearby Big Hole Valley, this trail offers scenic hikes through alpine meadows and views of the surrounding mountain ranges.

Best Things to Do in Dillon: Activities and Tours

Dillon is home to a variety of activities and tours, including:

  1. Beaverhead River Float Trips: Enjoy the scenery while floating down the Beaverhead River, which offers some of the best fishing in Montana.
  2. Ghost Town Tours: Visit nearby ghost towns and learn about the history of Montana’s mining industry.
  3. Mountain Biking Tours: Guided tours of the local mountains and trails for all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Dillon

Dillon offers a variety of local dishes and drinks that you must try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Montana raised beef: Local beef is a must-try in Montana, and Dillon offers several places to sample it.
  2. Huckleberries: These flavorful berries are often used in pies, jams, and other desserts.
  3. Rocky Mountain Oysters: These unique delicacies are deep-fried bull testicles and can be found in local diners and pubs.

Local Beverages

  1. Fish Creek Lager: This local beer is brewed in nearby Hamilton and offers a refreshing taste.
  2. Montana Huckleberry Wine: This sweet and fruity wine is a perfect pairing for local dishes.
  3. Dillon Distillery: Visit this local distillery for a taste of Montana-made spirits.

Where to Stay in Dillon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dillon offers a variety of accommodations, including:

  1. FairBridge Inn & Suites: Comfortable and convenient lodging in the heart of Dillon.
  2. Beaverhead River Lodge: This cozy lodge offers riverside cabins and fishing trips for guests.
  3. Dillon KOA: Tent and RV camping options with amenities such as showers, laundry, and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Dillon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Huckleberry jam and other local jams, jellies, and preserves.
  2. Leather goods, such as belts, wallets, and purses, made from locally sourced leather.
  3. Local artisan crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Dillon

Visitors should be aware of the local culture and customs of Dillon, such as:

  • Dressing appropriately for outdoor activities and weather changes.
  • Respecting local agriculture and wildlife.
  • Greeting locals with a smile and a friendly attitude.

Best Time to Visit Dillon

The best time to visit Dillon is between May and October when the weather is pleasant, and activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dillon

Do:

  1. Respect wildlife and natural resources.
  2. Be prepared for outdoor activities with appropriate gear and clothing.
  3. Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash or litter on public lands or trails.
  2. Approach wildlife or disturb their habitat.
  3. Ignore safety warnings or signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dillon

Dillon is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions when partaking in outdoor activities and follow guidelines for safety and health.

Health in Dillon

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude of Dillon and its potential effects on visitors. Signs of altitude sickness may include fatigue, headaches, and dizziness. Visitors should also be aware of ticks and their potential to carry disease and use proper precautions when hiking and camping.

Getting to Dillon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dillon can be accessed by several transportation options, such as:

Airports Near Dillon

  1. Helena Regional Airport – 86 miles
  2. Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport – 95.8 miles
  3. Butte Bert Mooney Airport – 64.7 miles

Bus Stations Near Dillon

  1. Greyhound Bus Station, Butte – 62 miles
  2. Salt Lake Express, Dillon – In town

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Dillon by rental car or private transportation services.

How to Get Around in Dillon

Dillon is a small town that can be easily navigated on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use rental cars or transportation services for longer travel distances.

Where to Go Next from Dillon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dillon is a great base to explore nearby areas, such as:

  1. Big Hole National Battlefield: A national park that tells the story of the Big Hole Battle of 1877 between the Nez Perce Indians and the U.S. Army.
  2. Lewis and Clark Caverns State Park: This park offers cave tours and hiking trails.
  3. Butte: A historic mining town with numerous attractions, such as the World Museum of Mining and the Berkeley Pit.

Conclusion

Come and discover the beauty of Dillon, a small town with a big heart, that offers an abundance of natural beauty and outdoor activities, along with a rich history and culture. There is something for everyone here in this hidden gem in Montana’s Gold West Country.
