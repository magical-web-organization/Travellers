Introduction to Butte

Butte is a prominent city in Gold West Country, located in the Rocky Mountains of Montana, United States. It is famous for its copper mining history and is known as “The Richest Hill on Earth.” Butte is also home to numerous cultural and outdoor attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers of all interests.

List of Places to Visit in Butte

When you visit Butte, don’t skip these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. World Museum of Mining: Learn about the mining history of Butte at this museum, which features exhibits on mining equipment, historic buildings, and more.

  2. Headframe Spirits: Take a tour of this local distillery to learn about the process of making craft spirits and sample their unique flavors.

  3. Copper King Mansion: Tour this historic mansion, built by William A. Clark, a prominent businessman and politician in the late 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Butte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Thompson Park: This park offers stunning views of Butte and the surrounding mountains, as well as hiking trails and picnic areas.

  2. Berkeley Pit: See the famous abandoned open-pit mine, which was once the largest copper mine in the world but is now a toxic lake.

  3. Our Lady of the Rockies: Visit the iconic statue of the Virgin Mary on the top of the mountain, which offers breathtaking views of Butte and the valley below.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Butte

Butte is a popular destination among outdoor enthusiasts due to its numerous hiking and biking trails. Some of the best routes include:

  1. Mount Helena City Park: This park offers a variety of trails, from easy to difficult, and provides stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.

  2. Maude S Canyon: Enjoy a hike through this beautiful canyon, which offers waterfalls, wildflowers, and wildlife.

  3. Homestake Pass Trails: This network of trails offers numerous options for hiking, snowshoeing, and mountain biking, so visitors can enjoy them year-round.

Best Things to Do in Butte: Activities and Tours

  1. Butte Trolley Tour: Take a historic trolley tour of Butte to learn more about its mining history and architecture.

  2. Mine Underground Tour: Explore the tunnels of a historic mine and learn about the dangerous work it took to extract copper in the early 20th century.

  3. ATV Adventures: Get ready for an adrenaline rush by exploring the rugged terrain of Butte while riding an all-terrain vehicle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Butte

Day 1: Visit the World Museum of Mining and the Copper King Mansion. Day 2: Take a trolley tour of Butte, followed by a visit to the Berkeley Pit. Day 3: Spend the day hiking on Homestake Pass Trails. Day 4: Visit the Headframe Spirits distillery and enjoy their unique craft spirits. Day 5: Explore the historic mine on an underground tour. Day 6: Take an ATV adventure to see the rugged terrain of Butte. Day 7: Relax at Thompson Park and enjoy the stunning views.

Food and Drink Scene of Butte

When it comes to food and drink, Butte has a unique scene that reflects its mining heritage. Must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pasty: A traditional Cornish meat pie that is popular in Butte.

  2. Pork Chop Sandwich: A local favorite made with a pork chop, onions, and cheese on a hoagie roll.

  3. Hot Hamburger: A sandwich topped with mashed potatoes and gravy, a local specialty.

Local Beverages

  1. Miners’ Union Whiskey: A local whiskey that is distilled with a unique blend of grains and aged in charred oak barrels.

  2. High Ore Red Ale: A craft beer brewed by Butte Brewing Company that features a deep red color and a rich malt flavor.

  3. Huckleberry Lemonade: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made with huckleberry syrup and lemonade.

Where to Stay in Butte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Some of the best places to stay in Butte include:

  1. Finlen Hotel: A historic hotel that was once a hub for miners and politicians, located in the heart of downtown Butte.

  2. Toad Hall Manor Bed and Breakfast: A cozy B&B with a beautiful garden, located in a quiet neighborhood close to the city center.

  3. Hostel Butte: A popular hostel that offers affordable accommodation for travelers, located just a few blocks from the bus station.

What to Buy in Butte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Butte, be sure to take home some of these local products:

  1. Montana-made jewelry: Made with local minerals and rocks, this unique jewelry reflects the mining heritage of Butte.

  2. Porters’ Mustard: A spicy mustard that is a staple of Butte cuisine.

  3. Butte T-shirt or hoodie: A souvenir that represents the city’s mining history and rugged culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Butte

Butte is proud of its mining heritage and has preserved many historic buildings and sites to honor that legacy. Visitors should also be aware of the following customs:

  • People in Butte are friendly and welcoming, so expect lots of warm greetings and conversations.

  • Butte is a town that loves celebrations, especially for St. Patrick’s Day, so plan your visit accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Butte

The best time to visit Butte is in the summer, between June and September, when the weather is warm and the hiking and biking trails are open. Visitors can also enjoy outdoor events and festivals during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Butte

Do:

  • Do take an underground mine tour to learn about the mining history of Butte.

  • Do try Butte cuisine, such as pasty, hot hamburger, and pork chop sandwich.

  • Do bring warm clothing when visiting in the winter, as temperatures can drop below freezing.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in parks or natural areas.

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen if you plan to spend time outdoors.

  • Don’t miss out on the local whiskey, Miners’ Union Whiskey.

Safety and Health

Safety in Butte

Butte is a safe city to visit, but visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Butte

Visitors to Butte should be aware of the high altitude and take steps to avoid altitude sickness, such as drinking plenty of water and avoiding alcohol.

Getting to Butte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Butte have several transportation options:

Airports Near Butte

Bert Mooney Airport is located just three miles south of the city center and serves flights from Salt Lake City.

Bus Stations Near Butte

Greyhound Bus Lines serves Butte with a station located in the city center.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Butte or take a scenic train ride on the “Empire Builder” route of Amtrak.

How to Get Around in Butte

Butte is a walkable city, but visitors who want to explore the outer areas should rent a car or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Butte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to continue your adventure in Montana, consider visiting nearby destinations such as Helena, Missoula, or Bozeman to explore more of the state’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Butte is a charming and historic town that offers a unique blend of culture, outdoor activities, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re interested in learning about mining history, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, Butte has something for everyone. Start planning your trip now to experience the best of this Montana gem.
