Introduction to Helena

Nestled in Gold West Country, in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Helena is the capital city of Montana, United States. Its rich history dates back to the gold rush era of the late 19th century, which is still evident in its architecture and charming downtown. Today, Helena is known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreation, and cultural attractions, making it a great destination for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Helena

Helena offers a wide range of things to see and do. Whether you’re interested in art, history, or outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone. Here are some must-visit attractions in Helena:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Montana State Capitol: This magnificent building is the heart of Helena’s political scene and is open for public tours.
  • Montana Historical Society Museum: The museum offers a wide range of exhibits about Montana’s history, culture, and wildlife.
  • Original Governor’s Mansion: A beautiful historic home built in 1888, this mansion is now a museum where you can learn about the lives of the former governors of Montana.
  • Cathedral of St. Helena: This impressive cathedral showcases stunning Gothic Revival architecture, stained-glass windows, and intricate artwork.

Natural Attractions of Helena: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gates of the Mountains: A beautiful canyon that offers breathtaking views, wildlife watching, and boat tours.
  • Mount Helena City Park: Hiking trails leading to the top of Mount Helena provide incredible views of the city and surrounding landscapes.
  • Helena National Forest: A vast wilderness area, offering hiking, biking, fishing, and camping opportunities.
  • Missouri River: One of the major waterways in the United States, the Missouri River offers water-based activities like fishing, rafting, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Helena

For those who love outdoor adventures, Helena is paradise. With several beautiful trails to choose from, hiking and biking in Helena is an experience you can’t miss. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Mount Helena Ridge Trail: An easy trail that offers stunning views of the city, suitable for all skill levels.
  • South Hills Trail System: A network of trails ranging from easy to challenging, offering beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Helena Ridge Trail: A moderate level trail that offers stunning views of the mountains.
  • Centennial Trail: A long and challenging trail, suitable for experienced hikers, offering beautiful views of the mountains, prairies, and rivers.

Best Things to Do in Helena: Activities and Tours

In Helena, you’ll never run out of things to see and do. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a walking tour of the downtown area to see the stunning architecture of historic buildings.
  • Visit the Archie Bray Foundation for the Ceramic Arts, to learn about ceramics and view some beautiful artwork.
  • Explore the Lewis & Clark Trail History Center, where you can learn about Montana’s role in the Lewis and Clark Expedition.
  • Attend the Montana State Fair, held in July/August, for carnival rides, livestock shows, and live entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Helena

If you’re planning to spend a week in Helena, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most out of your trip:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive and relax
Day 2 Visit the Montana Historical Society Museum and Montana State Capitol
Day 3 Take a Gates of the Mountains boat tour and hike the Mount Helena City Park trail
Day 4 Explore the Lewis & Clark Trail History Center and Cathedral of St. Helena
Day 5 Visit the Original Governor’s Mansion and the Archie Bray Foundation for the Ceramic Arts
Day 6 Take a day trip to the Missouri River for fishing or rafting

Food and Drink Scene of Helena

Helena’s food scene offers a wide range of local and international cuisine, suitable for all tastes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: A classic dish that uses locally sourced bison meat, topped with onions, cheese, and mushrooms.
  • Trout: A delicious fish found in the Missouri River, which can be prepared in many ways.
  • Huckleberry Jam: A local berry, used in various dishes, but most commonly in jam, which goes great with bread, scones, or biscuits.

Local Beverages

  • Big Sky Brewing Company: A local brewery that produces various beer varieties such as the “Montana Trout Slayer,” “Moose Drool,” and “Summer Honey”.
  • Ten Mile Creek Brewery: A local brewery that produces a wide range of beer varieties, including IPAs, stouts, and sours.
  • Wine: Montana is home to several vineyards and wineries, producing quality wines that can be paired with the local cuisine.

Where to Stay in Helena: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Helena has several accommodation options to fit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Best Western Premier Helena Great Northern Hotel: A central hotel with a pool, restaurant, and gym.
  • Hampton Inn Helena: A hotel with comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and breakfast included.
  • The Carolina Bed & Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms and delicious breakfast.
  • HI Helena Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers, offering shared and private rooms with a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Helena: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Helena offers many unique and locally-made products, perfect for souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best items to bring home:

  • Huckleberry Jam or Syrup: A local berry that makes a delicious jam or syrup, it’s a great sweet and flavorful gift.
  • Montana Grains: Locally sourced grains like wheat, lentils, and quinoa.
  • Handmade Pottery: Helena is known for its ceramics, and there are several artists and shops selling beautiful handmade pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Helena

Helena has a rich history, and its culture is deeply rooted in its past. Montanans are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Montana is a western U.S. state with a relatively low crime rate and has a significant Indigenous population, and the state has done an excellent job in preserving Indigenous cultures and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Helena

The best time to visit Helena is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and there are several outdoor activities and events taking place. However, if you’re into winter sports, you can visit Helena in the winter months (December-February) when the area is covered in snow, and there are several winter sports activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Helena

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Helena:

Do:

  • Try local specialties like bison meat, trout, and huckleberries.
  • Take care of Montana’s wildlife and natural resources.
  • Be respectful of Indigenous culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Disrespect wildlife.
  • Ignore traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Helena

Montana is a relatively safe state with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Helena

Helena has several hospitals and healthcare centers if you need medical attention while visiting.

Getting to Helena: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Helena

Helena Regional Airport (HLN) located approximately 3 miles northeast of downtown Helena, is the nearest airport. You can also fly into Great Falls International Airport (GTF), which is about 85 miles north of Helena.

Bus Stations Near Helena

Greyhound Lines has a bus station in Helena, located at 220 N. Montana Ave.

Other Options

If you’re within driving distance, you can quickly get to Helena via car or motorcycle. Alternatively, Montana has plenty of scenic train routes, and Amtrak runs through several towns, including Whitefish, East Glacier, and Shelby.

How to Get Around in Helena

Getting around Helena is relatively easy, and there are several options such as:

  • Walking: The downtown area is easily accessible on foot
  • Biking: Bike rentals are available downtown
  • Bus: The Capital Transit Bus System has several routes throughout the city
  • Car rental: Renting a car gives you more flexibility to explore the area

Where to Go Next from Helena: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montana has several nearby destinations and excursions you can explore after visiting Helena, including:

  • Glacier National Park: A beautiful national park known for its glaciers, stunning landscapes, and wildlife.
  • Yellowstone National Park: One of the most popular national parks in the US, known for its geysers, hot springs, and wildlife.
  • Butte, Montana: A historic mining town about an hour’s drive from Helena, known for its stunning architecture and cultural attractions.
  • Flathead Lake: Montana’s largest natural lake with crystal clear waters, perfect for swimming, kayaking, and fishing.

Conclusion

Helena is a charming city filled with history, culture, and natural beauty. By following this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll see the best attractions, sample local cuisine, and experience Montana’s vibrant culture. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a family, or a couple, Helena is an excellent destination to explore, relax, and have fun.
