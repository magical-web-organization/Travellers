Introduction to Townsend

Nestled in Gold West Country, Montana, Townsend is a charming town with a rich history and breathtaking scenery. Surrounded by the Rocky Mountains, Townsend is a hub for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking, biking, and fishing. Along with its outdoor attractions, the town also boasts cultural landmarks, museums, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Townsend offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Townsend

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Broadwater County Museum: Explore the rich history of Broadwater County and learn about the pioneers who settled in Townsend. The museum features exhibits that showcase the town’s architecture, industries, and culture.
  • Old Prison Museum: Step back in time and visit the Old Montana Prison, which served as a functioning prison for 110 years. Visitors can explore the cells, gallows, and yard while learning about the prison’s history.
  • Norris Hot Springs: Enjoy the natural hot springs that the town is known for. Alongside the hot springs, there’s a cafe and live music.

Natural Attractions of Townsend: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canyon Ferry Lake: This man-made lake offers excellent fishing and boating, and visitors can take in the stunning views from the surrounding hills.
  • Missouri River: Take a float trip down the Missouri River to experience beautiful views and abundant wildlife, including bald eagles, elk, and deer.
  • Big Belt Mountains: The Big Belt Mountains offer challenging hikes and stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Hikers can explore the trails and discover hidden waterfalls and lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Townsend

  • Chestnut Mountain Trail: This 2.5-mile trail offers stunning views of Townsend and the surrounding landscape. It’s a great spot for hiking, biking, and picnicking.
  • Black Sandy State Park Trail: This easy, 1.5-mile trail takes visitors through the forest and along the shores of Canyon Ferry Lake.
  • County Line Trail: A 7.5-mile trail that offers breathtaking views of the mountains and is known for its great bird-watching opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Townsend: Activities and Tours

  • Fly Fishing: Townsend is a great spot for fly fishing, with many outfitters and guides available to take visitors on guided tours.
  • Whitewater Rafting: Thrill-seekers can experience the rapids of the Missouri River with guided rafting tours.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a leisurely horseback ride through the mountains or across the plains for a unique way to experience the Montana landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Townsend

Day 1 Explore the Old Prison Museum and learn about the history of the prison.
Day 2 Visit the Broadwater County Museum and explore the town’s cultural heritage.
Day 3 Go fishing on the Missouri River.
Day 4 Hike the Chestnut Mountain Trail and enjoy a picnic with stunning views.
Day 5 Try horseback riding in the Big Belt Mountains.
Day 6 Take a float trip down the Missouri River.
Day 7 Relax in the natural hot springs at Norris Hot Springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Townsend

Must-Try Dishes

  • Montana Beef: Montana is known for its high-quality beef, which can be enjoyed at many of Townsend’s restaurants and cafes.
  • Huckleberry Pie: Huckleberries are a staple of Montana’s cuisine, and visitors shouldn’t leave without trying a slice of huckleberry pie.
  • Bison Burgers: Bison is a leaner alternative to beef and can be found on many menus in Townsend.

Local Beverages

  • Big Sky Brewing Company: Try local brews like Moose Drool or Scape Goat at Big Sky Brewing Company’s taproom.
  • Montana Distillery: Sample craft spirits like Montana moonshine and whiskey at Montana Distillery.

Where to Stay in Townsend: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Broadwater Motel: A charming motel offering clean, comfortable rooms and a convenient location within walking distance of downtown.
  • Canyon Ferry Mansion: Stay in a beautifully restored mansion that dates back to the early 1900s, complete with period furnishings and stunning views of Canyon Ferry Lake.
  • The Lodge at Deadwood Creek: This elegant lodge offers luxurious rooms and suites with breathtaking views of the Big Belt Mountains.

What to Buy in Townsend: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam: Take home a taste of Montana with a jar of huckleberry jam.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Discover unique pieces of jewelry made by local artisans.
  • Western Wear: Pick up a cowboy hat, boots, or a stylish leather jacket to remember your trip to Montana.

Local Culture and Customs of Townsend

Montana is known for its hospitality and friendliness. Visitors are encouraged to slow down and take in the beauty of the state. Montana’s culture values outdoor activities, including hunting, fishing, and hiking.

Best Time to Visit Townsend

The best time to visit Townsend is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and dry. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional thunderstorms. Spring (April to June) and fall (September to November) offer mild temperatures and beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Townsend

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the town’s cultural landmarks and museums.
  • Do hike or go fishing in the beautiful Montana landscapes.
  • Do enjoy a Montana beef steak or huckleberry pie.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or trespass on private property.
  • Don’t approach wildlife, such as bears or bison.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather – be sure to dress in layers and bring waterproof gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Townsend

Montana is generally a safe state, but visitors should take precautions in rural areas. Be aware of wildlife and follow guidelines for bear safety. When hiking, be sure to let others know your route and expected return time.

Health in Townsend

Townsend has a small hospital and limited medical facilities. Visitors with health concerns or pre-existing conditions should consult a healthcare professional before traveling.

Getting to Townsend: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Townsend

  • Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport (70 miles)
  • Helena Regional Airport (26 miles)

Bus Stations Near Townsend

  • Jefferson Lines (45 miles)
  • Greyhound (28 miles)

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available from Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport and Helena Regional Airport.

How to Get Around in Townsend

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Townsend and the surrounding areas. However, visitors can also rent bikes or take advantage of local shuttle services for transportation within town.

Where to Go Next from Townsend: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Helena: Montana’s capital city is a short drive from Townsend and offers cultural attractions like the Montana State Capitol and the Holter Museum of Art.
  • Yellowstone National Park: Visit one of the world’s most famous national parks, which isn’t too far from Townsend.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a lover of outdoor adventure or cultural landmarks, Townsend is a charming Montana town that offers something for everyone. From the Old Prison Museum to the Big Belt Mountains, visitors can explore the town’s rich history and beautiful landscapes. Don’t forget to try some local cuisine and bring home some souvenirs as a reminder of your trip to Montana.
