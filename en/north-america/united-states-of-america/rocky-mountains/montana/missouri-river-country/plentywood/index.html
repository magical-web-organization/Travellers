Introduction to Plentywood

Nestled in the Missouri River Country in Montana, Plentywood is a hidden gem that should be added to every traveler’s bucket list. With its natural landscapes, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, Plentywood offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Plentywood

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, don’t miss out on the culturally significant attractions in Plentywood.

  • Sheridan County Museum: A museum that showcases the history of the surrounding area, including exhibits on the early settlers, Native American artifacts, and local wildlife.
  • Fort Union Trading Post National Historic Site: A former trading post that served as a hub for fur trading between 1828 and 1867.

Natural Attractions of Plentywood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Plentywood is home to some of the most breathtaking natural attractions that offer endless opportunities for outdoor adventurers and nature enthusiasts.

  • Medicine Lake: A stunning lake that boasts crystal-clear waters, excellent fishing, and a picturesque setting for hiking, horseback riding, and mountain biking.
  • Des Lacs National Wildlife Refuge: A wildlife refuge that houses diverse flora and fauna, including wetlands, prairies, and forests. Visitors can go on guided tours, bird watching, and seasonal hunting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plentywood

Plentywood has a multitude of hiking and biking routes that offer indescribable scenic views and opportunities to explore the natural wonders of the region. Some of the best routes are:

  • Medicine Lake Trail: A moderate 4.3-mile trail that winds around Medicine Lake, offering scenic views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • East Fork Des Lacs Trail: A 9.7-mile trail that offers breathtaking views of the Des Lacs National Wildlife Refuge’s diverse landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Plentywood: Activities and Tours

Explore Plentywood’s natural beauty and participate in exciting activities and tours:

  • Guided fishing tours on Medicine Lake
  • Horseback riding and wildlife viewing at Des Lacs National Wildlife Refuge
  • Scenic drives and wildlife tours in the surrounding countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plentywood

Day 1: Visit Sheridan County Museum and learn about the historical significance of the area.

Day 2: Go hiking or biking on the Medicine Lake Trail, and spend the afternoon fishing on Medicine Lake.

Day 3: Join a guided wildlife tour or bird-watching excursion at Des Lacs National Wildlife Refuge.

Day 4: Spend the day exploring Fort Union Trading Post National Historic Site.

Day 5: Enjoy a scenic drive in the countryside, and take in the natural beauty of Plentywood.

Day 6: Participate in horseback riding or wildlife viewing at the Des Lacs National Wildlife Refuge.

Day 7: End the week with a relaxing afternoon by the lake or take a guided fishing tour of Medicine Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Plentywood

Must-Try Dishes

Sampling the local cuisine is an essential part of exploring any destination. Here are some must-try dishes in Plentywood:

  • Huckleberry pancakes: A breakfast delight that features local huckleberries and a stack of hotcakes.
  • Montana beef: A delicious dish that showcases the region’s top-quality beef.

Local Beverages

Quench your thirst with some of the best local beverages in Plentywood:

  • Montana microbrews: Try one of the many locally crafted microbrews available in Plentywood.
  • Huckleberry soda: A sweet and fizzy drink made with the region’s iconic fruit.

Where to Stay in Plentywood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Enjoy a comfortable stay in Plentywood’s best accommodations:

  • Sheridan House Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast that rests on a rolling hill and offers picturesque views of the countryside.
  • Plentywood Inn: A modern and spacious hotel located in the heart of downtown Plentywood.

What to Buy in Plentywood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Plentywood with these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Huckleberry jam and syrup: An iconic local product made with the plump and delicious huckleberries grown in the area.
  • Pottery and handmade crafts: Take home a handcrafted piece of pottery or a handmade souvenir, created by local artisans in Plentywood.

Local Culture and Customs of Plentywood

Plentywood is a close-knit community that values traditions and culture. Some local customs and traditions include:

  • The annual Sheridan County Fair: A week-long event that celebrates the community’s agricultural roots, and features rodeos, live music, and exhibits.
  • The Plentywood Rodeo: A three-day event that highlights the region’s cowboy culture, and showcases world-class rodeo action.

Best Time to Visit Plentywood

The best time to visit Plentywood is between the months of May and October when the weather is pleasantly warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plentywood

Plentywood is a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s essential to take precautions while participating in outdoor activities like hiking and fishing. Be sure to wear proper gear and take necessary safety measures.

Health in Plentywood

Plentywood has a hospital and multiple clinics to cater to basic healthcare needs. However, it’s always advisable to keep a medical kit on hand and have travel insurance before heading out on a trip.
