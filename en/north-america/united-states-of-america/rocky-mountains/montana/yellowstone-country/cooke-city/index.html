Introduction to Cooke City

Nestled in the heart of Yellowstone Country, Cooke City is a charming mountain town that offers visitors a wide range of outdoor activities and breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains. Surrounded by the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness and the Gallatin National Forest, this quaint town is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

With limited cell phone service and no chain stores or fast food restaurants, Cooke City has managed to retain its small-town charm. The locals are friendly, and visitors can expect a warm and welcoming atmosphere. If you’re looking for a peaceful getaway, Cooke City is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Cooke City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chamber of Commerce and Visitors Center: Start your trip with a visit to the Chamber of Commerce and Visitors Center, where you can pick up maps and brochures about the area, as well as information about local events and activities.

  • Cooke City Montana Museum: Learn about the area’s rich history at the Cooke City Montana Museum. The museum features exhibits on local mining, wildlife, and the town’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Cooke City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yellowstone National Park: Cooke City is located just a few miles from the northeast entrance of Yellowstone National Park, one of the most beautiful national parks in the country. Visitors can explore the park’s geysers, hot springs, and wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, and bison.

  • Beartooth Highway: Dubbed “the most beautiful drive in America” by Charles Kuralt, the Beartooth Highway is a scenic drive that has been featured in countless films and television shows. The highway takes visitors through the stunning Beartooth Mountains, with panoramic views of alpine lakes, glaciers, and wildflowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cooke City

Cooke City is a hiker and biker’s paradise. With trails ranging from easy strolls to strenuous treks, there’s something for every level of adventurer. Some of the most popular trails in the area include:

  • Pilot and Index Peaks: This 9-mile hike takes visitors through alpine meadows and past pristine lakes to the summits of two stunning peaks with breathtaking views.

  • Lulu Pass Trail: This 8-mile trail offers spectacular views of the Gallatin National Forest and the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Cooke City: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the rugged terrain of Cooke City on horseback with one of the local outfitters.

  • Floating: Enjoy a leisurely float down the Yellowstone River and take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Snowmobiling: Cooke City is known for its excellent snowmobiling opportunities, with miles of groomed trails and backcountry access.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cooke City

Day 1: Explore Yellowstone National Park

Day 2: Hike to Index Peak

Day 3: Go horseback riding

Day 4: Take a scenic drive on the Beartooth Highway

Day 5: Snowmobile in the Gallatin National Forest

Day 7: Visit the Cooke City Montana Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Cooke City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Made with locally sourced bison meat, this burger is a Cooke City favorite.

  • Huckleberry Ice Cream: Huckleberries are a local delicacy, and the ice cream made from them is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Montana Craft Beer: Cooke City has a lively craft beer scene, with a number of local breweries offering unique and flavorful brews.

  • Huckleberry Margarita: This fruity cocktail is made with huckleberry syrup, tequila, and lime juice.

Where to Stay in Cooke City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cooke City has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the top-rated options include:

  • Cooke City Alpine Motel: This cozy motel offers clean and comfortable rooms, as well as stunning mountain views.

  • Elk Horn Lodge: This rustic lodge is located in the heart of town and features spacious rooms, a fireplace, and a hot tub.

  • The Antler’s Lodge: This charming guesthouse offers cozy rooms, a communal kitchen, and a relaxing patio area.

What to Buy in Cooke City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam: Made from locally harvested huckleberries, this jam is a delicious and unique souvenir.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Cooke City has a number of talented artists who create beautiful and unique pieces of jewelry using local materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Cooke City

The people of Cooke City take pride in their close-knit community and place a high value on the outdoors and nature. Visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and to be mindful of the wildlife in the area.

Best Time to Visit Cooke City

The best time to visit Cooke City is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors who wish to see the area covered in snow may prefer to visit in the winter when the snowmobiling and skiing opportunities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cooke City

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife

  • Be mindful of the local customs and traditions

  • Plan ahead and bring appropriate gear for outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife

  • Litter or leave trash behind

  • Trespass on private property

Safety and Health

Safety in Cooke City

Cooke City is generally a safe destination, but visitors should always take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities. This includes carrying bear spray, staying on marked trails, and being aware of changing weather conditions.

Health in Cooke City

Visitors should be aware of the potential for altitude sickness when visiting the high elevations of the Rocky Mountains. Symptoms include headaches, nausea, and shortness of breath. It is recommended that visitors drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol, and take it easy during the first few days of their trip.

Getting to Cooke City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cooke City

The nearest major airport to Cooke City is Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, which is approximately 90 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Cooke City

There are no major bus stations near Cooke City, but visitors can take a Greyhound bus to Livingston, Montana, and then rent a car or take a taxi for the rest of the journey.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Cooke City, as the town is located on the scenic Beartooth Highway. It is also possible to book a shuttle service from Bozeman or Billings.

How to Get Around in Cooke City

Cooke City is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bike. However, a car is recommended for those who wish to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Cooke City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yellowstone National Park: Just a few miles from Cooke City, Yellowstone National Park offers endless opportunities for exploration and adventure.

  • Livingston, Montana: This picturesque town is located about 80 miles from Cooke City, and is known for its vibrant arts scene and historic downtown area.

Conclusion

Cooke City may be just a small town, but it offers visitors an abundance of natural beauty and outdoor activities. Whether you’re hiking in the mountains, exploring Yellowstone National Park, or simply enjoying a locally brewed beer, Cooke City is the perfect place to disconnect from the rush of everyday life and reconnect with nature. Plan your trip now, and experience the magic of this charming mountain town for yourself.
