Introduction to Livingston

Livingston is a small town in southwest Montana in the United States. It is located in the Yellowstone Country region, near the northern entrance of the world-renowned Yellowstone National Park. Nestled in the heart of Rocky Mountains, Livingston provides visitors with stunning natural views and endless outdoor adventures. The town is also rich in history and culture, making it a perfect destination for those interested in learning about Montana’s past or experiencing the local lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Livingston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Livingston is home to many unique museums and landmarks that offer insight into the town’s rich history and culture. Some of the most popular places to visit include:

  • Livingston Depot Center: This historic train station, built in 1902, now houses exhibitions and events showcasing the town’s past and present.
  • Yellowstone Gateway Museum: This museum features exhibits on the history and culture of Park County, Montana, from Native American heritage to the present-day life of Livingston.
  • Sacajawea Park: This park is dedicated to the Shoshone woman, Sacajawea, who served as a guide for the Lewis and Clark expedition.
  • Dan Bailey’s Fly Shop: This fly-fishing shop has been an icon of Livingston for over 80 years, offering visitors a glimpse into the town’s angling heritage.

Natural Attractions of Livingston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Livingston is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, offering a wide range of outdoor activities. Whether you’re a hiker, a skier, a fisherman, or just someone who enjoys a good view, there’s something for everyone. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • Yellowstone National Park: Just a short drive from Livingston, Yellowstone is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Montana. From geysers to waterfalls to wildlife, Yellowstone offers visitors an unparalleled natural experience.
  • Paradise Valley: This valley, located just south of Livingston, features stunning views of the Absaroka Range and the Yellowstone River. It is a popular spot for hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Bridger Bowl: Just a 45-minute drive from Livingston, Bridger Bowl is a ski resort nestled in the Bridger Range. With over 2,000 acres of ski-able terrain, Bridger Bowl is a winter paradise.
  • Yellowstone River: This river runs through Livingston and is famous for its blue-ribbon trout fishing. Visitors can book a trip with a local guide or simply enjoy a scenic float down the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Livingston

Livingston offers many hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore this natural wonderland. Some of the best hiking/biking routes include:

  • Pine Creek Trail: This 5.3-mile trail takes visitors through a dense forest to a waterfall and a beautiful lake.
  • Sacajawea Peak Trail: This 6.4-mile trail leads to the summit of Sacajawea Peak, offering panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Yellowstone River Trail: This 5.5-mile trail follows the Yellowstone River and offers stunning views of the river and Livingston Peak.
  • Riding Mountain Trail: This 6.1-mile trail takes visitors through a scenic forest and offers views of the Gallatin Valley.

Best Things to Do in Livingston: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Livingston offers many activities and tours to satisfy any visitor’s interests. Some of the best things to do in Livingston include:

  • Fly-fishing: Livingston is considered one of the best spots in the world for fly-fishing. Visitors can book a trip with a local guide or rent equipment and try their luck on their own.
  • Walking tours: Historic Livingston offers many walking tours that showcase the town’s architecture and history.
  • Art galleries: Livingston is home to many talented artists, and visitors can explore their work at local galleries such as the Montana Gallery and the Frame Garden.
  • Park County Fair: Held every summer, the Park County Fair features local food, music, and agricultural exhibits.
  • Yellowstone Wildlife Sanctuary: Just a few miles outside of Livingston, this sanctuary provides a home for many native Montana animals, including bears, wolves, and elk.

Week-Long Itinerary for Livingston

For visitors who want to get the most out of their Livingston experience, here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore downtown Livingston and visit historic landmarks such as the Livingston Depot Center and the Yellowstone Gateway Museum.
Day 2 Take a scenic drive through Paradise Valley and stop to hike or fish along the way.
Day 3 Visit Yellowstone National Park and take in its natural beauty.
Day 4 Book a fly-fishing trip or take a scenic float down the Yellowstone River.
Day 5 Take a walking tour of historic Livingston and explore local art galleries.
Day 6 Ski or snowboard at Bridger Bowl (winter only) or hike one of the nearby trails (summer only).
Day 7 Relax and enjoy local food and drinks before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Livingston

When it comes to food and drink, Livingston offers a wide range of options that showcase the town’s unique flavor. Visitors can enjoy local specialties and craft beer at various restaurants and bars throughout the town.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Elk burgers: Elk is a common game meat in Montana, and many restaurants in Livingston offer delicious elk burgers.
  • Fry bread: This Native American dish is a staple in Montana, and visitors can find it at local restaurants and food trucks.
  • Huckleberry pie: Huckleberries are a Montana staple, and visitors can enjoy them in the form of a sweet, tart pie.

Local Beverages

  • Microbrews: Montana is home to many microbreweries, and Livingston is no exception. Visitors can try some of the local craft beer at the Katabatic Brewing Company or Neptune’s Brewery.
  • Coffee: Livingston is also home to many cozy coffee shops, including the Coffee Crossing and the Stockman Bar.
  • Whiskey: Montana has a rich history of whiskey-making, and visitors can explore this tradition at the Willie’s Distillery and the Bozeman Spirits Distillery.

Where to Stay in Livingston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For visitors looking for a place to stay, Livingston offers many options to fit any budget. From cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious resorts, there’s something for everyone. Some of the best places to stay in Livingston include:

  • Murray Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of downtown Livingston and offers luxurious accommodations.
  • Yellowstone Valley Lodge: This lodge is located on the banks of the Yellowstone River and features rustic cabins and a restaurant.
  • Airbnb: Many locals in Livingston rent out their homes on Airbnb, providing visitors with a unique lodging experience.

What to Buy in Livingston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors who want to bring home a piece of Montana, Livingston offers many unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Huckleberry products: From jam to candy to lotion, visitors can find all things huckleberry in Livingston.
  • Fly-fishing gear: Visitors can shop at Dan Bailey’s Fly Shop or Joe’s Fly Shop for gear and souvenirs.
  • Western wear: Montana is known for its cowboy culture, and visitors can find boots, hats, and other Western wear at local stores such as Whiskey Creek and J.C. Penney.

Local Culture and Customs of Livingston

Montana is known for its friendly and welcoming nature, and Livingston is no exception. Visitors should be prepared to encounter lots of friendly locals who are proud of their town and happy to share it with others.

Best Time to Visit Livingston

The best time to visit Livingston depends on what activities visitors want to experience. Summer and fall offer the best weather for outdoor activities such as hiking and fishing, while winter provides excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities. Spring is a great time to visit for those who want to experience the beauty of Montana’s wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Livingston

Do:

  • Take in the natural beauty of the area.
  • Explore local history and culture.
  • Try local food and craft beer.
  • Be respectful of local wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind in natural areas.
  • Disturb animals in their natural habitats.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Ignore weather warnings and hazards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Livingston

Livingston is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions such as locking their cars and keeping an eye on their belongings. When exploring natural areas, visitors should be aware of wildlife hazards such as bears and snakes.

Health in Livingston

Visitors should be prepared for Montana’s high altitude and dry climate, and should stay hydrated and wear sunscreen. Visitors should also be aware of tick-borne diseases and take precautions to prevent tick bites.

Getting to Livingston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Livingston

The nearest major airport to Livingston is Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport, located about 30 miles away. Visitors can also fly into Billings Logan International Airport, located about 130 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Livingston

Livingston is served by the Livingston Depot Center, which offers local city bus service and serves as a stop for Greyhound Bus Lines.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to rent a car to explore the area, as there are many scenic drives and natural areas to visit.

How to Get Around in Livingston

Livingston is a small town that is easily walkable. Visitors can also rent a bike from local shops such as Chalet Sports or explore the area by car.

Where to Go Next from Livingston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Livingston. Some of the best options include:

  • Yellowstone National Park: Continue exploring this natural wonder and see even more of its wonders.
  • Bozeman: This nearby city offers unique restaurants and shops, as well as the Museum of the Rockies.
  • Chico Hot Springs: This resort offers hot springs pools and spa services.
  • Big Sky Resort: This ski resort offers world-class skiing and snowboarding.

Conclusion

Livingston is a hidden gem in Montana’s Yellowstone Country, offering visitors natural beauty, rich history, and a warm Western welcome. Whether you’re exploring downtown or the great outdoors, there’s something in Livingston for everyone.
