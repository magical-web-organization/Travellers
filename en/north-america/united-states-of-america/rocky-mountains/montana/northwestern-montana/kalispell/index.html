Introduction to Kalispell

Located in the heart of the Flathead Valley, Northwestern Montana, Kalispell is a charming small town that is surrounded by the stunning Rocky Mountains. The town is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture lovers, offering a plethora of activities, attractions, and natural wonders to explore.

With its proximity to Glacier National Park, Kalispell serves as an ideal base camp for visitors who want to explore the park and the surrounding area. Whether you’re looking for stunning landscapes, cultural experiences, exciting outdoor activities, or delicious local cuisine, Kalispell has it all. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and attractions to see in Kalispell.

List of Places to Visit in Kalispell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Conrad Mansion Museum: A beautifully restored 19th-century mansion that offers a glimpse into the life of the wealthy Conrad family and the history of the Flathead Valley.

  • Hockaday Museum of Art: This museum features a diverse collection of contemporary and historic art from Montana and the surrounding region.

  • Museum at Central School: Housed in a historic school building, this museum offers exhibits on the history of the Flathead Valley, the Native American tribes that once lived here, and the early pioneers who settled in the area.

  • Flathead County Historical Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Flathead County, Montana, with exhibits on everything from mining and agriculture to early transportation and communication.

Natural Attractions of Kalispell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glacier National Park: Just a short drive from Kalispell, Glacier National Park is one of the most beautiful and iconic parks in the United States. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, boating, and wildlife watching, as well as stunning views of mountains, glaciers, and alpine lakes.

  • Flathead Lake: One of the largest freshwater lakes in the Western United States, Flathead Lake is a popular destination for swimming, boating, fishing, and water sports.

  • Swan Valley: Located just south of Kalispell, this scenic valley is home to pristine forests, rivers, and lakes, as well as hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

  • Whitefish Mountain Resort: This world-class ski resort offers year-round outdoor activities, including skiing, snowboarding, ziplining, mountain biking, and scenic lift rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kalispell

  • Herron Park: Offering over 11 miles of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding, Herron Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Lone Pine State Park: With more than 7 miles of hiking trails, Lone Pine State Park offers stunning views of the Flathead Valley and the surrounding mountains.

  • Foys to Blacktail Trail: This 15-mile trail connects Foys Lake to Blacktail Mountain, offering a challenging but rewarding hike or mountain bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Kalispell: Activities and Tours

  • Going-to-the-Sun Road Tour: This scenic drive offers breathtaking views of Glacier National Park and is a must-do for visitors to the area.

  • Flathead River Whitewater Rafting: This thrilling adventure takes visitors down the rapids of the Flathead River, with experienced guides to keep you safe.

  • Flathead Lake Boat Tour: Enjoy panoramic views of Flathead Lake and the surrounding mountains on this relaxing boat tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kalispell

Day Activity
1 Explore downtown Kalispell and visit the Conrad Mansion Museum

3 Take a scenic drive on Going-to-the-Sun Road and stop at Logan Pass for some wildlife viewing

7 Take a horseback ride through Herron Park, followed by a beer at one of Kalispell’s local breweries

Food and Drink Scene of Kalispell

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Pancakes: Made with fresh huckleberries that are native to the area, these pancakes are a local favorite.

  • Bison Burger: Made with locally sourced bison meat, this hearty burger is a delicious and sustainable alternative to beef.

  • Flathead Cherry Pie: Made with tart cherries that are grown in the Flathead Valley, this pie is a sweet and tangy dessert that is perfect for summer.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Soda: Made with real huckleberry juice, this sweet and refreshing soda is a popular local drink.

  • Montana Craft Beer: Kalispell has a thriving craft beer scene, with a variety of local breweries that offer a wide range of styles and flavors.

  • Flathead Lake Wineries: The Flathead Valley is also home to several wineries that produce delicious wines made with locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Kalispell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kalispell Grand Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Kalispell that offers luxurious accommodations and modern amenities.

  • The Hostel in Kalispell: A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to stay in a hostel that’s clean, safe, and centrally located.

  • Many Glacier Hotel: Located in Glacier National Park, this historic hotel offers stunning views of the park and easy access to hiking trails and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Kalispell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam: A delicious jam made with fresh huckleberries that is a popular local treat.

  • Local Art: The Hockaday Museum of Art offers a variety of locally made art, including pottery, paintings, and jewelry.

  • Montana-made Products: Montana Marketplace offers a wide range of products made by local artisans, including handmade soap, candles, and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Kalispell

The people of Kalispell are proud of their small town charm and welcoming nature. Visitors are encouraged to take part in the local culture and traditions, such as attending the Kalispell Farmers Market, which takes place every Saturday throughout the summer, or the annual Huckleberry Festival, which celebrates the delicious local berry with food, music, and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Kalispell

The best time to visit Kalispell is from late May to early September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors should be aware that the peak season can be crowded, and accommodations can be expensive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalispell

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and keep a safe distance from bears, moose, and other animals.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and scenic views that Kalispell has to offer.
  • Try the local cuisine, including huckleberries, bison, and Montana craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Leave food or trash in your car, as it may attract wildlife.
  • Trespass on private property or venture off-trail in protected areas.
  • Underestimate the power of the sun at high altitudes. Be sure to wear sunscreen and drink plenty of water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kalispell

Kalispell is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should exercise caution when hiking, camping, or participating in other outdoor activities. Be sure to follow all safety recommendations and guidelines provided by park rangers and other experts.

Health in Kalispell

Visitors should also take precautions to avoid altitude sickness and dehydration when hiking or camping in high-altitude areas. Pack plenty of water and snacks, and take frequent breaks to rest and acclimate to the altitude.

Getting to Kalispell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kalispell

Kalispell is served by Glacier Park International Airport (FCA), which offers direct flights to several major cities, including Seattle, Denver, Salt Lake City, and Minneapolis.

Bus Stations Near Kalispell

Greyhound Bus Lines offers service to Kalispell from several cities in Montana, as well as from Seattle, Spokane, and Missoula.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a scenic train ride on the historic Empire Builder route, which runs between Chicago and Seattle, with a stop in Whitefish, just a short drive from Kalispell.

How to Get Around in Kalispell

Kalispell is a walkable town, with many attractions and restaurants located within walking distance of downtown. Visitors can also take a local bus or taxi, rent a bike, or drive their own vehicle to get around the town and the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Kalispell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Whitefish, Montana: Just a short drive from Kalispell, Whitefish is a charming mountain town with a lively downtown area, world-class skiing, and stunning natural beauty.

  • Flathead National Forest: This vast forest offers a wide range of recreational opportunities, including hiking, camping, fishing, and hunting.

  • Missoula, Montana: Located about 2.5 hours south of Kalispell, Missoula is a vibrant college town with a lively arts and culture scene, excellent restaurants and breweries, and plenty of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and welcoming small-town charm, Kalispell is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Northwestern Montana. Whether you’re looking to explore Glacier National Park, hike through the mountains, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine, Kalispell has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this beautiful part of Montana has to offer!
