Introduction to Cut Bank

Cut Bank is a small town located in Northwestern Montana, nestled in the Rocky Mountains and known for its rugged beauty and outdoor adventure opportunities. The town is named after the Cut Bank Creek, which flows through the area and offers excellent fishing and rafting opportunities. Cut Bank is located just a few miles from the Canadian border, making it the perfect destination for a scenic road trip or a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Cut Bank

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Glacier Park Lodge: This historic lodge was built in 1913 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The lodge features stunning rustic architecture and is located in the heart of Glacier National Park.
  • Cut Bank Depot Museum: This museum is housed in an old train depot and features exhibits on local history and culture. The museum also has a collection of railroad artifacts and memorabilia.
  • Blackfeet Heritage Center and Art Gallery: This center features exhibits on Blackfeet history, culture, and art. Visitors can purchase authentic Blackfeet crafts and artwork in the on-site gift shop.

Natural Attractions of Cut Bank: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cut Bank Creek Trail: This scenic trail follows the Cut Bank Creek and offers excellent hiking and fishing opportunities. The trail is also home to an abundance of wildlife, including grizzly bears and elk.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding at Cut Bank: Cut Bank is home to some of the best skiing and snowboarding in Montana. Visitors can hit the slopes at the Eagle Mountain Ski Area or the nearby Marias Pass.
  • Chief Mountain: This stunning peak is located on the eastern edge of Glacier National Park and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cut Bank

Cut Bank is a hiker and biker’s paradise with trails that cater to all levels of ability. Some of the most popular hiking and biking trails in Cut Bank include:

  • Cut Bank Creek Trail: This scenic trail follows the Cut Bank Creek and offers excellent hiking and fishing opportunities. The trail is also home to an abundance of wildlife, including grizzly bears and elk.
  • South Fork Trail: This trail is located in the heart of the Rocky Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks.
  • Highline Trail: This challenging trail follows the Continental Divide and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Cut Bank: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Cut Bank Creek: This exciting activity is perfect for thrill-seekers and nature lovers alike. Visitors can experience the rush of whitewater rafting while taking in the stunning scenery.
  • Horseback Riding: Cut Bank is home to a number of ranches that offer guided horseback rides through the Rocky Mountains.
  • Glacier National Park Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour of Glacier National Park and explore the park’s stunning lakes, mountains, and wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cut Bank

Day 1: Arrive in Cut Bank and check into your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the town’s museums and historic sites.

Day 2-3: Spend the next two days hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of Cut Bank. Highlights include the Cut Bank Creek Trail, South Fork Trail, and Highline Trail.

Day 4-5: Take a guided tour of Glacier National Park and explore the park’s stunning lakes, mountains, and wildlife.

Day 6-7: Enjoy some of Cut Bank’s other popular activities, including rafting on the Cut Bank Creek and horseback riding through the mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Cut Bank

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Made from local bison, this juicy burger is a Montana classic.
  • Huckleberry Pie: This sweet and tart pie is made with local huckleberries and is a must-try dessert in Cut Bank.
  • Montana Steak: Cut Bank is home to some of the best beef in the country, and the Montana steak is a delicious way to experience it.

Local Beverages

  • Montana Microbrews: Cut Bank is home to a number of microbreweries that craft delicious beers using Montana-grown hops.
  • Huckleberry Margarita: This delicious cocktail is made with fresh huckleberry syrup and tequila, and is the perfect way to cool off after a day of hiking or biking.

Where to Stay in Cut Bank: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cut Bank offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and tastes. Some of the most popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Cut Bank include:

  • Glacier Park Lodge: This historic lodge offers rustic charm, comfortable rooms, and stunning mountain views.
  • Cut Bank Campground: This campground offers affordable camping options and is located close to the town’s top attractions.
  • Homestead House Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers a cozy and comfortable place to stay and is located in the heart of Cut Bank.

What to Buy in Cut Bank: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Authentic Blackfeet Crafts and Artwork: Visitors can purchase authentic Blackfeet crafts and artwork at the Blackfeet Heritage Center and Art Gallery.
  • Montana Huckleberry Jam: This delicious jam is made with local huckleberries and makes a sweet and tasty souvenir.
  • Montana Beef Jerky: Cut Bank is home to some of the best beef in the country, and Montana beef jerky is a tasty and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Cut Bank

Cut Bank is home to a diverse mix of cultures and traditions. The Blackfeet Nation has a strong presence in the area and visitors can learn more about Blackfeet history and culture at the Blackfeet Heritage Center and Art Gallery.

Best Time to Visit Cut Bank

The best time to visit Cut Bank is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in weather and should pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cut Bank

Do:

  • Hike and Explore the Outdoors: Cut Bank offers some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Montana.
  • Learn About Local Culture and History: Take the time to learn about the Blackfeet Nation and its history and culture.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Montana beef, huckleberries, and microbrews are just a few of the delicious local foods and drinks that visitors shouldn’t miss.

Don’t:

  • Leave Your Belongings Unattended: Montana is a safe place, but visitors should still take care not to leave their belongings unattended.
  • Harm Local Wildlife: Visitors should be respectful of the wildlife and should not feed or approach animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cut Bank

Montana is a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Hiking in remote areas and exploring the great outdoors can present certain risks, so visitors should be prepared and should always let someone know where they are going.

Health in Cut Bank

Cut Bank has a few medical facilities, but visitors should still take care of their health and should pack any necessary medications or supplies.

Getting to Cut Bank: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cut Bank

The closest airport to Cut Bank is the Glacier Park International Airport, located in Kalispell, Montana, which is approximately 130 miles away from Cut Bank.

Bus Stations Near Cut Bank

Greyhound buses stop in Cut Bank, and visitors can purchase tickets online or at the station.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cut Bank and explore the surrounding area by car. Cut Bank is located just a few hours from many popular Montana destinations, including Missoula, Butte, and Bozeman.

How to Get Around in Cut Bank

The best way to get around in Cut Bank is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the Glacier Park International Airport or at one of the car rental agencies in town.

Where to Go Next from Cut Bank: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many exciting Montana destinations located just a few hours from Cut Bank. Some of the most popular nearby destinations include:

  • Glacier National Park: Located just a short drive from Cut Bank, Glacier National Park is one of the most popular national parks in the United States.
  • Yellowstone National Park: This iconic park is located just a few hours south of Cut Bank and is home to geysers, hot springs, and an abundance of wildlife.
  • Billings: This vibrant city is located to the east of Cut Bank and features a thriving arts and culture scene, as well as a number of historic sites.

Conclusion

Cut Bank is a hidden gem in Montana, offering visitors a chance to experience the rugged beauty and natural wonder of the Rocky Mountains. Whether you’re hiking, biking, or just exploring the town’s museums and historic sites, Cut Bank has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready for an adventure in one of Montana’s most picturesque towns.
