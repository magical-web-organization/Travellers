Located in the northwestern part of Montana and surrounded by the stunning Rocky Mountains, Missoula is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture seekers alike. This charming city is home to a diverse range of attractions, from world-class museums and historic sites to breathtaking natural wonders and thrilling outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we’ll highlight the top things to do and see in Missoula, as well as provide you with useful tips and recommendations for planning your trip.

Introduction to Missoula

Nestled in the heart of the Bitterroot Valley, Missoula is the second-largest city in Montana, with a population of just over 70,000 people. The city’s location at the confluence of three rivers - the Clark Fork, Bitterroot, and Blackfoot - has earned it the nickname “The Garden City.” Missoula is known for its vibrant cultural scene, outdoor recreation opportunities, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Missoula

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Missoula boasts a rich history, and there is no better place to explore it than at the various museums and historical sites dotted around the city.

  • The Montana Museum of Art & Culture: This museum houses a vast collection of artworks, including contemporary pieces, American Indian art, and European works from the 19th and 20th centuries.
  • The Historical Museum at Fort Missoula: A former U.S. Army fort that served as a military training center during World War II, this museum tells the story of Missoula’s early days and the settling of the West.
  • The Missoula Art Museum: This contemporary art museum showcases the work of artists from Montana and the Pacific Northwest.
  • Garnet Ghost Town: This well-preserved 19th-century mining town is a popular day trip destination from Missoula, offering visitors a glimpse into Montana’s mining past.

Natural Attractions of Missoula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Missoula is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of options for hiking, fishing, skiing, and other outdoor activities.

  • Mount Sentinel: A popular hiking spot, Mount Sentinel offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Rattlesnake National Recreation Area: This beautiful wilderness area encompasses over 60,000 acres of forests, mountains, and rivers, and offers visitors a chance to hike, fish, and camp.
  • Missoula Valley Overlook: This scenic overlook offers panoramic views of the Missoula Valley and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Missoula

Whether you’re looking for a challenging hike or a leisurely bike ride, Missoula has plenty of options for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Mt. Jumbo: A challenging hike up this 4,000-foot peak offers incredible views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Blue Mountain Recreation Area: This popular hiking and biking spot offers miles of trails through beautiful forests and meadows.
  • The Kim Williams Trail: This 5-mile paved trail runs along the Clark Fork River and is ideal for a leisurely bike ride or stroll.

Best Things to Do in Missoula: Activities and Tours

In addition to its museums and natural attractions, Missoula has plenty of unique activities and tours to offer visitors.

  • Visit the Missoula Farmers’ Market: Held every Saturday morning from May to October, this bustling farmers’ market features a wide variety of local produce, crafts, and food.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride: See Missoula from a bird’s-eye view with a spectacular hot air balloon ride.
  • Tour the local breweries: Missoula is home to several award-winning breweries, and a beer tour is a great way to sample some of the best brews in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Missoula

No trip to Missoula would be complete without sampling some of the city’s delicious food and drink offerings. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Pie: Made with locally grown huckleberries, this Montana classic is a must-try.
  • Bison Burger: Buffalo meat is a staple of Montana cuisine, and the bison burgers in Missoula are some of the best.
  • Flathead Cherry Salad: This refreshing salad features fresh flathead cherries, which are only grown in Montana.

Local Beverages

  • Big Sky Brewing Company: This award-winning brewery produces a wide range of beers, including the popular Moose Drool Brown Ale.
  • KettleHouse Brewing Co.: Another local favorite, KettleHouse Brewing Co. is known for its delicious IPAs and stouts.
  • Montgomery Distillery: Stop by this craft distillery to sample their delicious spirits, including gin, whiskey, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Missoula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels, Missoula has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and lifestyle.

  • Gibson Mansion: This charming bed and breakfast is housed in a restored 1903 mansion and offers guests luxury amenities and beautiful gardens.
  • Holiday Inn Missoula Downtown: This centrally located hotel features spacious rooms and a heated indoor pool.
  • City Center Motel: A budget-friendly option, this charming motel is located in the heart of downtown and offers easy access to the city’s attractions.

What to Buy in Missoula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or local product to bring home? Here are some ideas:

  • Huckleberry jam or syrup: Made from locally grown huckleberries, this is a sweet and delicious souvenir to take home.
  • Montana-made jewelry: Local artisans create beautiful jewelry using Montana gemstones and other materials.
  • Handmade quilts or blankets: Many local shops sell beautiful handcrafted blankets and quilts made from wool or other materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Missoula

Missoula is known for its laid-back, friendly, and outdoorsy culture. The locals are proud of their Western heritage and love to share it with visitors. They are also passionate about protecting the environment and preserving Montana’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Missoula

The best time to visit Missoula is between May and September, when the weather is warm and the outdoor recreation opportunities are at their peak. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit when the leaves turn vibrant shades of red and gold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Missoula

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and get outdoors as much as possible.
  • Try some of the local cuisine and craft beer offerings.
  • Learn about Missoula’s history and frontier past at the various museums and historical sites around the city.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the natural beauty of the area in favor of urban attractions.
  • Disrespect the locals or their way of life.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor recreation activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Missoula

Missoula is a generally safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. This includes locking car doors and securing valuable items when parked in public places. Visitors should also be bear aware when hiking in the area and take appropriate measures to prevent encounters with wildlife.

Health in Missoula

Missoula has several hospitals and medical facilities in case of emergencies. Visitors should also take precautions against tick bites and carry bear spray when hiking in the surrounding wilderness areas.

Getting to Missoula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Missoula is well-connected to major cities in the region, making it easy to reach by air or road.

Airports Near Missoula

  • Missoula International Airport (MSO): Located just a few miles from downtown, MSO is served by several major airlines, including Alaska Airlines, Delta, and United.

Bus Stations Near Missoula

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound offers daily bus service to and from Missoula.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Amtrak’s Empire Builder line runs through Missoula, connecting the city to major cities like Seattle and Chicago.

How to Get Around in Missoula

Getting around Missoula is easy, with several options for transportation, including:

  • Buses: Mountain Line is the local bus system and offers affordable and convenient transportation throughout the city.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services: Uber and Lyft are available in Missoula, as are several local taxi companies.
  • Bike rentals: Several local bike rental shops offer options for exploring the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Missoula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Missoula is located in a beautiful part of Montana, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring.

  • Glacier National Park: Just a few hours’ drive from Missoula, Glacier National Park is one of the most iconic natural landmarks in the country, with miles of scenic hiking trails and breathtaking vistas.
  • Flathead Lake: This stunning lake is located just a short drive from Missoula and is a popular destination for swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Yellowstone National Park: While it’s a bit further away, a trip to Yellowstone National Park is well worth the effort, with its famous geysers, hot springs, and abundance of wildlife.

Conclusion

With its rich history, scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and friendly locals, Missoula is an ideal destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Montana. We hope this travel guide has given you a good sense of what to expect from your time in Missoula and has provided you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip.
