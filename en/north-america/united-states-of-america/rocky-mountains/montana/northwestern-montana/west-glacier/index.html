Introduction to West Glacier

West Glacier is a small town located in the northwestern part of Montana, United States, and is situated in the breathtaking Rocky Mountains. Its proximity to Glacier National Park makes it a popular tourist destination for travelers worldwide. The town offers incredible natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, and a laid-back atmosphere where visitors can recharge and connect with the wonders of nature.

In this tour guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to West Glacier, Montana. From the best places to visit to the must-try foods, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in West Glacier

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Apgar Nature Center - Learn about the flora and fauna of Glacier National Park and the Crown of the Continent ecosystem through interactive exhibits, short films, and ranger-led activities.

  • Belton Chalet - This historic hotel and restaurant was built in 1910 and remains a landmark of the town. It is a designated National Historic Landmark and showcases original, elegant architecture.

  • Glacier Park Lodge - Built in the early 20th century, the Glacier Park Lodge is another National Historic Landmark that attracts visitors with its classic design and scenic location.

  • Flathead Indian Reservation - Discover the Native American culture of the region by visiting the Flathead Indian Reservation, where you can learn about the Salish, Kutenai, and Pend d’Oreille tribes.

Natural Attractions of West Glacier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glacier National Park - This UNESCO World Heritage site is the crown jewel of West Glacier, boasting 1 million acres of pristine wilderness, rugged mountains, clear lakes, and abundant wildlife. There are plenty of activities here, including hiking, biking, boating, fishing, camping, and more.

  • Going-to-the-Sun Road – This 50-mile scenic drive is one of the most beautiful roads in the country, taking you through high mountains, alpine lakes, and narrow passes.

  • Lake McDonald - Located in the heart of Glacier National Park, Lake McDonald is a stunning glacial lake surrounded by forests, mountains, and wildlife. Visitors can enjoy fishing, kayaking, swimming, and hiking in the area.

  • Trail of Cedars – This nature walk takes you through the ancient cedars and hemlocks of the Rainforest of the Pacific Northwest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Glacier

West Glacier has some of the best hiking and biking routes in the country. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Highline Trail – This 11-mile trail is one of the most popular hikes in Glacier National Park, featuring breathtaking views of the mountains and the surrounding valleys.

  • Hidden Lake Trail – This 5.5-mile hike offers incredible views of Hidden Lake and the high alpine meadows and peaks surrounding it.

  • Apgar Bike Trail – This 7-mile paved bike trail runs alongside Lake McDonald and offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in West Glacier: Activities and Tours

  • White-water Rafting – Experience the thrill of the rapids in the wild and scenic Flathead River with guided tours.

  • Fishing – West Glacier offers some of the best fishing spots in the country, with lakes and rivers brimming with trout, grayling, and bass.

  • Wildlife Watching – From grizzly bears, mountain goats, and lynx to moose, elk, and bighorn sheep, West Glacier is teeming with wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Glacier

Day 1: Arrive at West Glacier and check into your accommodations.

Day 2: Explore the Apgar Nature Center and nearby Belton Chalet.

Day 3: Drive along the Going-to-the-Sun Road and stop at scenic viewpoints such as Logan Pass.

Day 4: Hike the Highline Trail and enjoy the panoramic views of the mountains and valleys.

Day 5: Take a guided white-water rafting tour on the Flathead River.

Day 6: Go fishing at Lake McDonald or other nearby lakes and rivers.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at your accommodations or take a cultural tour of Native American landmark sites.

Food and Drink Scene of West Glacier

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Jam - A delicious and popular local jam made from the huckleberry fruit, known for its sweet and tart flavor.

  • Smoked Trout - A classic Montana dish made from fresh trout, seasoned with herbs and spices, and smoked using traditional techniques.

  • Bison Burger - A unique and tasty burger made from bison meat, which is leaner and healthier than beef.

Local Beverages

  • Montana Wine – Montana Wine is a new player in the wine market that is quickly gaining popularity. Montana boasts of its wineries that produce unique and tasty wine in a variety of grapes and flavors.

  • Flathead Lake Brewing Company – A popular brewery that offers craft beer and classic drinks, using only the finest ingredients

Where to Stay in West Glacier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Glacier offers a variety of options for accommodations, from luxurious hotels and resorts to cozy guesthouses and camping sites.

Budget Accommodations:

  • Glacier Outdoor Center & Cabin – This riverside campground not only offers camping, but also rents out cabins.

  • Apgar Village Lodge and Cabins – Offers affordable log cabins with stunning views of Lake McDonald.

Mid-Range Accommodations:

  • Glacier Highland Motel - This lodge offers comfortable rooms and private cabins surrounded by tranquil nature.

  • Glacier Haven Inn – Offers charming guest rooms with mountain views as well as cabins with lake views.

Luxury Accommodations:

  • Lake McDonald Lodge - A historic hotel built in 1913, boasts luxurious rooms and suites and offers beautiful views of the lake.

  • Many Glacier Hotel - Located on the east side of Glacier National Park, this luxurious hotel is an iconic landmark of the park.

What to Buy in West Glacier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Products – Huckleberry is a local fruit found in the mountains of Montana, and is used to create a wide range of products, such as jams, pies, syrups, and chocolates.

  • Montana sapphires – Montana is known for its high-quality sapphires that range in color from blue, green, to yellow and orange.

  • Native American Crafts – The Flathead Indian Reservation offers an array of traditional handicrafts, such as beadwork, pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of West Glacier

West Glacier is a small town with a big heart. Its residents pride themselves on being friendly, polite, and respectful of nature. Visitors are expected to follow the Leave No Trace policy and to respect the wildlife and the environment. It is also important to respect the indigenous culture and history of the region.

Best Time to Visit West Glacier

The best time to visit West Glacier is from June to September when the weather is mild, and the national park is fully accessible. Visitors should keep in mind that summers can be crowded and prices can be higher. Winter and spring offer their own unique charm, with fewer crowds and lower prices. However, traveling during these seasons can be challenging due to weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Glacier

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and nature.

  • Follow Leave No Trace policy.

  • Dress in layers.

  • Take plenty of water and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife.

  • Litter or leave trash behind.

  • Approach wildlife or get too close.

  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Glacier

Visitors to West Glacier should be aware of the following safety tips:

  • Carry bear spray and know how to use it.

  • Stay on designated trails and follow park regulations.

  • Be prepared for weather changes.

  • Lock your vehicle and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in West Glacier

Visitors should be aware of the following health tips:

  • Stay hydrated, especially during outdoor activities.

  • Watch out for symptoms of altitude sickness.

  • Bring any necessary medication, such as inhalers or EpiPens.

Getting to West Glacier: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Glacier

  • Glacier Park International Airport (FCA) - Located in Kalispell, Montana, about 25 miles from West Glacier.

  • Missoula International Airport (MSO) - Located in Missoula, Montana, about 145 miles from West Glacier.

Bus Stations Near West Glacier

  • Trans Montana / Glacier National Park Express – Operate various bus services to and from West Glacier.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals – Visitors can rent a car from nearby towns and drive to West Glacier.

How to Get Around in West Glacier

Visitors to West Glacier have the following options for transportation:

  • Car Rentals – Available in nearby towns.

  • Shuttle Services – Provided by Glacier National Park, and connecting major attractions and towns.

  • Bicycle Rentals – Available in West Glacier itself.

Where to Go Next from West Glacier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Whitefish – Located about 25 miles from West Glacier, this charming town offers food, art, and shopping experiences alongside a popular ski resort.

  • Waterton Lakes National Park – Across the border in Alberta, Canada, this stunning park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers a unique blend of Canadian and American cultures.

  • Flathead Lake – The largest natural freshwater lake in the west is located about 40 miles from West Glacier and offers boating, fishing, and recreational activities.

Conclusion

West Glacier Montana offers a unique blend of outdoor adventure, cultural heritage, and a relaxed atmosphere that makes it a perfect destination for tourists worldwide. From hiking in Glacier National Park to exploring local museums and landmarks, there are plenty of things to see and do in West Glacier. The town also has a vibrant food and drink scene and offers varied accommodation options to fit every budget. Whether you’re an avid nature lover or simply looking for a much-needed getaway, West Glacier Montana is the perfect destination to explore.
