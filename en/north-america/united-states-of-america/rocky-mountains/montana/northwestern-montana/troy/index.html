Introduction to Troy

Located in the heart of the Rocky Mountains of Northwestern Montana, Troy is a charming town that offers endless opportunities for adventure, exploration, and relaxation. With its abundance of outdoor activities, stunning mountain views, and rich historical and cultural heritage, Troy is a must-visit destination for every traveler. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Troy, Montana, and discover all the best things to see, do, and experience during your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Troy

Troy is a small town, but it offers a wide range of attractions and activities for every type of traveler. Here’s a list of the must-visit places in Troy:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Troy has a rich history and cultural heritage that is worth exploring. Below are some of the top museums and historical places to visit in Troy.

  • Troy Museum & Visitor Center: Learn about the town’s history and culture at the Troy Museum & Visitor Center, which showcases the area’s Native American roots, logging history, and more.
  • Yaak River Valley Historical Society Museum: located about 30 minutes from Troy, this museum provides an in-depth look into the local history of the area.
  • Kootenai Falls Suspension Bridge: One of the most iconic attractions in the area, the Kootenai Falls Suspension Bridge is a must-visit spot for nature lovers as it provides great views of the Kootenai River gorge.

Natural Attractions of Troy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Troy is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering endless opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains. Here are some of the top natural attractions of Troy:

  • Kootenai River: This river is a popular spot for fishing, tubing, and rafting. You can also enjoy some scenic hikes along the river trail.
  • Ross Creek Cedars: If you’re looking for a peaceful nature retreat, head to the Ross Creek Cedars Scenic Area, where you can enjoy a leisurely hike amidst some of Montana’s oldest and tallest trees.
  • Boulder Creek: For those looking for more challenging adventures, head to Boulder Creek, where you can enjoy hiking, backpacking, and rock climbing, in addition to stunning mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Troy

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will love the trails around Troy. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Troy Trails: The Troy Trails offers over 40 miles of trails for hiking and mountain biking. The trails range from easy to difficult, which means that there’s something for everyone.
  • Ross Creek Cedars: This is a great area for a leisurely hike, and it’s suitable for families with children.
  • Mount Silcox: This is a challenging hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. The trailhead is located about 10 miles north of Troy.

Best Things to Do in Troy: Activities and Tours

Troy offers plenty of activities and tours that will keep you busy during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Troy:

  • Fly Fishing: The Kootenai River is a great spot for fly fishing. There are many guided tours available, which can help you learn the basics of fly fishing.
  • Rafting and Tubing: Rent a tube, or take a guided rafting tour, to experience the adrenaline rush of the Kootenai River.
  • Ghost Town Tour: Explore the town’s history by taking a guided Ghost Town Tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Troy

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Troy, you won’t be short on activities to keep you busy. Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Troy:

Day 1: Arrival Day

  • Check-in to your hotel, take a stroll through downtown Troy and check out the local restaurants.

Day 2: Outdoor Adventures

  • Hike the Boulder Creek Trail in the morning, then spend the afternoon tubing down the Kootenai River.

Day 3: Cultural Attractions

  • Visit the Troy Museum & Visitor Center for an overview of the town’s history.
  • Head to Yaak River Valley Historical Society Museum, which is a 30-minute drive away, to discover more about the area.

Day 4: Scenic Routes

  • Take a scenic drive to the top of Mount Silcox for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Enjoy a leisurely hike through the Ross Creek Cedars Scenic Area.

Day 5: Fly Fishing

  • Take a guided tour to learn the basics of fly fishing on the Kootenai River.

Day 6: Rafting and Tubing Tours

  • Enjoy a day of guided tours tubing/rafting down the Kootenai River, taking in the gorgeous views.

Day 7: Departure Day

  • After breakfast, check out and don’t forget to buy some souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Troy

Troy has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many locally owned restaurants and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burger at Main Street Grille: This local spot has one of the best burgers in town, cooked to perfection.
  • Huckleberry Pie at Fergie’s Café: A Montana favorite is huckleberry pie and you will love it too!

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Margarita at Rockys Roadhouse: Make sure you try the Huckleberry Margarita at this popular local bar.

Where to Stay in Troy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Troy has a range of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to choose from, catering to every budget and preference. Some popular accommodations include:

  • Tree House Riverside Lodge: A charming, rustic-themed lodge with river views.
  • Silver Spur Motel: A budget-friendly accommodation that is conveniently located near the city center.
  • Yaak River Lodge: A premium lodging option, a little farther from downtown, but with luxurious accommodations.

What to Buy in Troy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are various souvenirs and local products to bring back home as a memory of your trip to Troy:

  • Huckleberry Jam: This is a popular locally made fruit jam that makes for a great souvenir.
  • Handmade Pottery: Pottery from local artists is available in many stores and markets throughout Troy.

Local Culture and Customs of Troy

Troy, like most of Montana, has a laid-back and friendly culture. Here are some things to expect during your stay in Troy:

  • Montana is famous for its cowboy culture and love for the outdoors. It’s a good idea to bring comfortable, casual clothes for hiking, sightseeing, and exploring.
  • People in Montana are generally friendly and hospitable, so be prepared for lots of greetings and exchanges of pleasantries.

Best Time to Visit Troy

Troy is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is between May and September when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season is also a great time to visit, especially for those interested in elk hunting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Troy

Here’s a list of do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Troy:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities available in Troy.
  • Greet people you meet on the streets with a friendly smile.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter! Always dispose of trash properly.
  • Don’t disturb the wildlife and always follow park rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Troy

Troy is a safe town, but it’s always a good idea to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Troy

Troy has a medical clinic available, but for more serious medical issues, you may need to drive to a nearby hospital.

Getting to Troy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Troy is accessible by both road and air.

Airports Near Troy

  • Glacier Park International Airport is the nearest airport to Troy.

Bus Stations Near Troy

  • The closest bus station is the Columbia Falls Transportation Center, which is about an hour-long drive from Troy.

Other Options

  • You can also rent a car and drive to Troy.
  • Amtrak trains stop at the town of Libby, which is about 30 minutes from Troy.

How to Get Around in Troy

Troy is a small town and walking is a great way to explore it. Additionally, you can rent a car if you need to travel outside of town or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Troy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have a few extra days, consider visiting these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Glacier National Park: Only a 2-hour drive from Troy, Glacier National Park is home to some of the most beautiful mountain ranges in America. Don’t forget to go the “Going-to-the-Sun Road”.
  • Kootenai National Wildlife Refuge: This peaceful and quiet area is great for birdwatching and hiking.
  • Sandpoint, Idaho: A leisurely drive away, Sandpoint is a small town that offers a laid-back atmosphere and some great local shops.

Conclusion

Troy, Montana, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its magnificent natural landscapes, rich history and culture, and endless outdoor activities, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan a perfect itinerary for your trip to Troy. Safe travels!
