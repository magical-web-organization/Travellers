Introduction to Bigfork

Nestled in Northwestern Montana, Bigfork is a charming town surrounded by breathtaking views of Rocky Mountains, lush forests, and sparkling waters of Flathead Lake. Known for its stunning natural scenery, this picturesque town offers unparalleled outdoor adventures, a thriving arts scene, and a vibrant culinary scene. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or an adventure seeker, Bigfork has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve curated a list of must-visit places, things to do, where to eat and stay, and tips for traveling to Bigfork. Read on to discover the best of Bigfork, Montana!

List of Places to Visit in Bigfork

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bigfork has a rich history that’s celebrated through its cultural attractions. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Bigfork include:

  • Bigfork Art and Cultural Center: A vibrant art center that hosts exhibitions, theatre productions, and cultural events.
  • Bigfork Village Historical Museum: A museum that showcases the history and culture of Bigfork through historical artifacts, photographs, and documents.
  • Swan River Nature Trail: An easy hiking trail that runs along the Swan River and offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Natural Attractions of Bigfork: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bigfork’s natural beauty is what draws visitors from all over the world. Here are some of the best natural attractions you must visit in Bigfork:

  • Flathead Lake: The largest freshwater lake west of the Mississippi River, Flathead Lake offers swimming, boating, kayaking, and fishing opportunities.
  • Glacier National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage site that features 700 miles of hiking trails, stunning glaciers, waterfalls, and wildlife such as grizzly bears and wolves.
  • Swan Mountain Range: Known for its incredible hiking and scenic drives that offer panoramic views of Flathead Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bigfork

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Bigfork is through hiking and biking. Here are some of the most scenic trails in the area:

  • Jewel Basin Hiking Area: A network of trails that offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Swan River Trail: An easy hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Swan River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Foys to Blacktail Trail: A popular mountain biking trail that offers scenic views of Flathead Lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Bigfork: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Bigfork offers an array of activities and tours for everyone. Here are some of the best things you can do in Bigfork:

  • Horseback Riding: Take a guided horseback tour through the picturesque trails of Swan Valley.
  • Fly Fishing: Try your hand at fly-fishing in the Flathead River, known for its healthy population of trout and whitefish.
  • Helicopter Tour: Take a scenic helicopter tour of the Flathead Valley and Glacier National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Bigfork

Bigfork’s culinary scene is thriving, and foodies will be spoilt for choice with the array of delicious dishes and local beverages on offer. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Pancakes: A local Montana specialty, these pancakes are made with fresh huckleberries and served with maple syrup.
  • Bison Burger: Try a bison burger made with locally raised bison meat and served with a side of sweet potato fries.
  • Wild Game Platter: Sample some of Montana’s wild game such as elk, bison, and deer in a delicious platter.

Local Beverages

  • Flathead Cherry Wine: Made with locally grown Flathead cherries, this sweet wine is a must-try for wine lovers.
  • Montana Breweries: Bigfork has a thriving craft beer scene, and you can sample local beers from breweries such as Great Northern Brewing Company and Flathead Lake Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Bigfork: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bigfork offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bigfork:

  • Bigfork Mountain Lake Lodge: A luxurious mountain lodge that offers stunning views of Flathead Lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Bridge Street Cottages: Charming cottages with fully equipped kitchens and private hot tubs, located in downtown Bigfork.
  • Buccaneer Motel: An affordable option with clean rooms and a central location in Bigfork.

What to Buy in Bigfork: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs to bring back home? Here are some of the best local products you can buy in Bigfork:

  • Huckleberry Jam and Syrup: Made with fresh Montana huckleberries, these jams and syrups are a delicious reminder of your trip to Montana.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Bigfork has a thriving arts scene, and you can find one-of-a-kind handmade jewelry from local artists.
  • Montana Artwork: Take home beautiful artwork that celebrates Montana’s natural beauty, wildlife, and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Bigfork

Montana has a rich history and culture that’s celebrated through its arts scene, culinary traditions, and outdoor adventures. Montanans are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect their culture and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Bigfork

The best time to visit Bigfork is from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re planning a trip to ski or snowboard, visit between December to March when the snow is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bigfork

Do:

  • Embrace the outdoor lifestyle and try new adventures.
  • Be respectful of Montana’s rich history and culture.
  • Sample local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the power of nature.
  • Disrespect Montana’s wildlife and natural environment.
  • Ignore local laws and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bigfork

Bigfork is a safe town, but visitors are advised to take precautions when exploring the outdoors. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always carry bear spray when hiking in the mountains.
  • Tell someone where you’re going and when you’ll be back.
  • Check the weather forecast before heading out.

Health in Bigfork

Montana is prone to wildfires during the hot and dry summer months, and visitors are advised to check the air quality index before heading outdoors. Drink plenty of water and take breaks often to avoid altitude sickness when exploring the mountains.

Getting to Bigfork: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Bigfork is easy, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Bigfork

  • Glacier Park International Airport (FCA): 30 miles from Bigfork

Bus Stations Near Bigfork

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Kalispell: 18 miles from Bigfork

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in Kalispell and Missoula.
  • Private Shuttle Services: Several shuttle services operate between the airport and Bigfork.

How to Get Around in Bigfork

The best way to get around Bigfork is by car, but there are also several bike rental and private shuttle services available. Taxis and rideshare apps such as Uber and Lyft are also available in Kalispell, 18 miles away from Bigfork.

Where to Go Next from Bigfork: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bigfork is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty and charming small towns. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can explore:

  • Whitefish: A lively mountain town known for its skiing, shopping, and dining.
  • Kalispell: A vibrant town that offers shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • National Bison Range: A wildlife refuge that is home to bison, elk, and deer.

Conclusion

Bigfork, Montana is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and culinary delights.
