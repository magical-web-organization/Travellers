Introduction to Ravalli

Ravalli is a small town located in Northwestern Montana, nestled in the Rocky Mountains and surrounded by natural beauty. Ravalli is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs. The town is rich in history and culture, and there are several historical landmarks and museums to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Ravalli

Here’s a list of some of Ravalli’s must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Mary’s Mission: One of Montana’s oldest settlements and a national historic landmark.
  • Ravalli County Museum: Displays the history of Ravalli County and the Bitterroot Valley.
  • Daly Mansion: Once the home of copper baron Marcus Daly, the mansion is now a museum that showcases the history of the Daly family and the area’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Ravalli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lee Metcalf National Wildlife Refuge: Provides habitat for a variety of wildlife, including birds, elk, and deer.
  • Lake Como: A serene alpine lake located in the Bitterroot National Forest, perfect for swimming and boating.
  • Blodgett Canyon: A popular hiking spot with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ravalli

Ravalli is a hiker’s paradise with several trails to choose from. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Blodgett Canyon Trail: A scenic hiking trail that winds through the canyon and offers great views of the surrounding peaks.
  • Bear Creek Trail: A popular hiking and mountain biking trail that offers breathtaking views of the Bitterroot Valley.
  • Bella Vista Trail: A challenging mountain biking route that offers great views of the Bitterroot Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ravalli: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy in Ravalli. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Fly Fishing: Ravalli is known for its excellent fly fishing opportunities. Hire a guide and spend a day on the river.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience Montana’s beauty on horseback with one of the many guided horseback tours.
  • ATV Tours: Explore the rugged terrain around Ravalli on an ATV tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ravalli

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ravalli, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Ravalli

Ravalli has a great food and drink scene, featuring locally-sourced ingredients and craft beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Jam: Montana’s state fruit makes for a delicious spread on bread or crackers.
  • Bison Burger: A locally sourced burger made with Montana bison meat.
  • Trout: Freshly caught trout is a local favorite and can be found on many restaurant menus in Ravalli.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Liqueur: Made from Montana’s state fruit, huckleberry liqueur is a staple at Ravalli’s bars.
  • Craft Beer: Montana is known for its craft beer scene, and many local breweries offer tasty options.
  • Montana Wine: Bitterroot Valley is home to several wineries that produce delicious Montana wine.

Where to Stay in Ravalli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ravalli has a variety of options for accommodations, from cozy guesthouses to full-service hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Bitterroot River Bed and Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast located on the banks of the Bitterroot River.
  • Rocky Point Ranch: A guest ranch that offers horseback rides and outdoor activities.
  • Townhouse Inn of Hamilton: A full-service hotel with comfortable rooms and great amenities.

What to Buy in Ravalli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some options:

  • Huckleberry Products: Pick up some huckleberry jam, pie filling, or syrup to take home.
  • Handcrafted Items: Visit one of Ravalli’s many gift shops for locally made jewelry, pottery, and other handcrafted items.
  • Montana-made Products: Look for products made in Montana, such as bison jerky or handmade soaps.

Local Culture and Customs of Ravalli

Montana has a rich ranching heritage, and many of the locals take pride in their cowboy culture. It’s not uncommon to see cowboys and cowgirls wearing hats and boots around town. Additionally, many locals also enjoy outdoor activities and spending time in nature.

Best Time to Visit Ravalli

The best time to visit Ravalli is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and the area is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and camping. Fall is also a great time to visit, with the beautiful foliage and local fall festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ravalli

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Ravalli:

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical landmarks.
  • Explore the great outdoors and go hiking or fishing.
  • Try some local cuisine and craft beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on trails or in public areas.
  • Trespass on private property when hiking or exploring.
  • Approach wild animals and always keep a safe distance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ravalli

Ravalli is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to be cautious when enjoying outdoor activities. Always be prepared for changing weather conditions and make sure to let someone know where you’ll be going. Additionally, bear and other wildlife sightings are common in Montana, so it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions.

Health in Ravalli

Montana has a high altitude, so visitors may experience altitude sickness. It’s important to stay hydrated and take it easy on the first day or two to let your body acclimate to the altitude. It’s also important to wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Ravalli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ravalli

The nearest airports to Ravalli are Missoula International Airport and Helena Regional Airport, both located about 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Ravalli

Greyhound offers bus service to nearby cities such as Missoula and Butte, but there are no bus stations in Ravalli.

Other Options

The Montana Department of Transportation operates a network of highways that connect Ravalli to other destinations in the state and beyond. Additionally, car rental companies are available in nearby cities for visitors who prefer to drive themselves.

How to Get Around in Ravalli

The best way to get around Ravalli is by car. There are also several bike rental companies in town, and cycling is a popular way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Ravalli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Montana, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Glacier National Park: Located about 3 hours north of Ravalli, Glacier National Park is known for its stunning vistas and wildlife.
  • Yellowstone National Park: Located about 4 hours east of Ravalli, Yellowstone is one of the most famous national parks in the world.
  • Bozeman: About 2 hours to the northeast, Bozeman is a charming college town with great dining and shopping.

Conclusion

Ravalli is a hidden gem in Montana, offering natural beauty, rich history, and a unique culture.
