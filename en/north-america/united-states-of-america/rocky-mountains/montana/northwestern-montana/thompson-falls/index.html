Thompson Falls, located in Northwestern Montana, is a hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains. This small town may be easily overlooked, but it actually packs a punch with its breathtaking natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply looking for a peaceful escape, Thompson Falls has something for everyone.

Introduction to Thompson Falls

Thompson Falls is a town in Sanders County, Montana, United States. The town is situated along the Clark Fork River, surrounded by lush forests, rolling hills, and soaring mountains. Thompson Falls is known for its rustic charm, laid-back lifestyle, and outdoor recreation. The town was named after the Thompson River and falls that were once an important site for tribal fishing. Today, Thompson Falls is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Thompson Falls

Thompson Falls has a rich history and culture, as well as astounding natural landscapes. Here are some of the top places to visit in Thompson Falls:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Jail Museum: Explore the history of Sanders County through the Old Jail Museum, which was once a functioning jail. The museum showcases artefacts, photographs, and documents that cover the region’s history, from Native American tribes to the early days of logging and mining.

  • Thompson Falls Heritage Museum: Located in a historic school building, the Thompson Falls Heritage Museum offers a glimpse into the history of the town. Displays include photographs, clothing, and other items that provide insight into life in Thompson Falls during the early 20th century.

  • St. William Church: A beautiful brick church built in the early 1900s, St. William Church is a significant part of Thompson Falls’ history. Visitors can attend mass or simply admire the impressive architecture.

Natural Attractions of Thompson Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thompson Falls State Park: A must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts, Thompson Falls State Park offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, fishing, kayaking, and camping. The park is home to the impressive Thompson Falls, a 120-foot waterfall that cascades into the Clark Fork River.

  • Noxon Rapids Reservoir: The Noxon Rapids Reservoir is a peaceful spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. The area boasts gorgeous views of the surrounding mountains and forests, making it a popular spot for picnicking and camping.

  • Koo-Koo-Sint Bighorn Sheep Viewing Area: Located near the Thompson Falls Fish Hatchery, the Koo-Koo-Sint Bighorn Sheep Viewing Area is home to a herd of bighorn sheep. Visitors can observe these majestic creatures in their natural habitat from a safe distance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thompson Falls

Thompson Falls is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise with plenty of hiking and biking trails in the area. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Cabinet View Trail: This 19-mile trail follows the ridgeline of the Cabinet Mountains Wilderness, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The trail is accessible by foot, bike, or horseback.

  • Cougar Peak Trail: For a challenging hike, check out Cougar Peak Trail, which scales one of the tallest peaks in the area. The summit offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Ponderosa State Park Trail: This easy trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll, hiking, or mountain biking. The trail winds through the forest and offers views of the Clark Fork River.

Best Things to Do in Thompson Falls: Activities and Tours

Thompson Falls offers a range of activities, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to do in Thompson Falls:

  • Fishing and Hunting: Thompson Falls is known for its good fishing, with plenty of opportunities to catch trout and other freshwater fish. Hunting is also popular in the area, with abundant wildlife that includes deer, elk, and bear.

  • River Rafting and Kayaking: The Clark Fork River is a popular spot for river rafting and kayaking, with rapids that range from mild to challenging. There are several outfitters in the area that offer guided trips.

  • Ghost Town Tours: Explore the remnants of Montana’s mining history with a visit to some of the area’s ghost towns, such as Bannack, Granite, and Philipsburg. Guided tours are available that provide insight into the boom and bust times of these towns.

Food and Drink Scene of Thompson Falls

Thompson Falls has a burgeoning food and drink scene, with a range of options to suit every taste and budget. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Jam: A local favourite, huckleberry jam is made from Montana’s state fruit and is delicious spread on toast or biscuits.

  • Bison Burger: Montana is known for its bison, and Thompson Falls has some of the best bison burgers around. Try one at a local restaurant.

  • Pasty: A classic miner’s lunch, pasties are baked pastry filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables. They’re hearty and filling, perfect for a day of hiking or exploring.

Local Beverages

  • Montana Huckleberry Wine: Made from locally grown huckleberries, this sweet wine is a tasty souvenir to bring home.

  • Big Sky Brewing Beer: Big Sky Brewing Company is a local favourite, producing a range of craft beers with fun names like “Moose Drool” and “Scape Goat.”

Where to Stay in Thompson Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thompson Falls has a range of accommodation options, from rustic cabins to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Thompson Falls:

  • Thompson Falls Bed and Breakfast: This cozy guesthouse boasts comfortable rooms, a delicious breakfast, and a tranquil garden.

  • Riverfront Motel: Located along the Clark Fork River, the Riverfront Motel offers clean and comfortable rooms with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Bear Creek Resort: For a more rustic experience, check out Bear Creek Resort. The resort features cabins and yurts, as well as hiking and fishing opportunities.

What to Buy in Thompson Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thompson Falls is filled with unique and locally-made products that make for great souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Thompson Falls:

  • Huckleberry Products: Huckleberries are a staple of Montana cuisine, and you can find everything from jams and jellies to teas and candles that feature this delicious fruit.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans create beautiful jewelry that showcases Montana’s natural beauty, with pieces made from materials like turquoise, silver, and elk antlers.

  • Montana-made Pottery: Browse pottery made by renowned local ceramists that features motifs inspired by Montana’s wildlife, landscapes, and moments.

Local Culture and Customs of Thompson Falls

Montana’s heritage and character are closely associated with its wild landscapes, western values, and cultural identity. In Thompson Falls, you’ll find people who are friendly, hardworking, and proud of their community’s history. When visiting this area, it is essential to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Some of the customs that visitors should keep in mind include tipping when receiving service and being polite and respectful.

Best Time to Visit Thompson Falls

The best times to visit Thompson Falls are in the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild, and precipitation and tourists are at their lowest. The summer season (June-August) is also pleasant, but more crowded, whereas winters are cold and snowy, making outdoor activities limited. Visitors should pack accordingly for the season they plan to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thompson Falls

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and keep a safe distance.
  • try Montana’s specialities like huckleberry jam or a bison burger.
  • Explore the local culture and customs of Thompson Falls.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and river rafting.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions like checking air quality levels, especially during wildfire season.
  • Damage or remove natural resources.
  • Overstep animal boundaries and trespass on private properties.

Safety and Health

Thompson Falls is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, like all outdoor activities, some risks exist. Here are some essential safety tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Thompson Falls

  • Make sure to bring a raincoat for unpredictable weather.
  • Be aware of the location of the nearest hospital and know first aid.
  • Take precautions to avoid wildfire season in late summer and early fall.

Health in Thompson Falls

  • Know Montana’s Covid-19 policies and guidelines for mask-wearing and social distancing.
  • Take the necessary vaccination (such as for tick-borne diseases).
  • Bring insect repellent to avoid bug-borne diseases.

Getting to Thompson Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thompson Falls is easily accessible by car, with major highways, US-93, and MT-200 traversing through. The nearest airports to Thompson Falls are Missoula International Airport and Spokane International Airport, which are 2 to 3 hours away. There are no public transportation options available in the town, and visitors without access to a car may need to arrange a private transfer.

Airports Near Thompson Falls

  • Missoula International Airport(MSO) - Located 90 miles south
  • Spokane International Airport(GEG) -Located 104 miles west

Bus Stations Near Thompson Falls

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Missoula (~90 miles)

Other Options

  • Renting a car or bike from a neighbouring town or city.

How to Get Around in Thompson Falls

Visitors are encouraged to get around by car because there’s only a few public transportations available. Most attractions are within driving distances, including the surrounding forests and mountains’ hiking trails.

Where to Go Next from Thompson Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thompson Falls is surrounded by some of the Northwest’s most famous tourist destinations. Some of the nearby attractions worth visiting include:

  • Glacier National Park: Located about 3 hours north of Thompson Falls, Glacier National Park boasts stunning natural beauty, with glaciers, mountains, and lakes.

  • Yellowstone National Park: A little farther south, Yellowstone National Park is a must-visit attraction, home to geysers, hot springs, and abundant wildlife.

Conclusion

Thompson Falls is a charming town with a lot to offer visitors, from its stunning natural beauty to its rich history and culture. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in Thompson Falls. Take a week to explore this area of Montana and create memories that will last a lifetime.
