Introduction to Superior

Located in Northwestern Montana, nestled in the Rocky Mountains, Superior is a small town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. It’s a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and those who seek tranquility. In this comprehensive tour guide of Superior, we will explore the best things to do and places to visit in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Superior

Superior, Montana, is a destination worth exploring. Here’s our pick of places to visit in Superior:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mineral County Museum: This museum showcases the rich history and cultural heritage of Superior, Montana. It features exhibits on the region’s mining, logging, and ranching history, as well as a collection of local artifacts and antiques.
  • St. Regis Historical Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the St. Regis Valley. It features exhibits on the local Native American tribes, mining, logging, and railroading, and has an extensive collection of artifacts and old photographs.
  • Alberton Railroad Depot Museum: This museum is located in the former Great Northern Depot and showcases the history of railroading in western Montana.

Natural Attractions of Superior: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Flathead National Forest: Located just east of Superior, the Flathead National Forest covers over 2.4 million acres of pristine wilderness, lakes, and rivers. It’s a perfect destination for hiking, camping, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
  • Lolo National Forest: Located just west of Superior, the Lolo National Forest is another popular outdoor destination. It features over 700 miles of hiking trails, crystal-clear streams, and stunning waterfalls.
  • Clark Fork River: The Clark Fork River is one of Montana’s most scenic rivers, and it runs right past Superior. It’s a popular destination for fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding, or simply relaxing by the riverside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Superior

Superior, Montana, is surrounded by some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the state, making it an ideal destination for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Superior:

  • Lookout Mountain Trail: This 3.5-mile trail near Superior offers stunning views of the Clark Fork River and the surrounding mountains.
  • St. Regis Peak Trail: This 4-mile trail offers stunning views of the St. Regis River Valley and the surrounding mountains.
  • Fish Creek Trail: This 8-mile trail follows Fish Creek through the Lolo National Forest and offers beautiful views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Superior: Activities and Tours

Superior, Montana, offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit all tastes, interests, and budgets. Here’s our pick of the best things to do in Superior:

  • River Rafting: The Clark Fork River offers some of the best river rafting in Montana, with rapids ranging from Class I to Class IV.
  • Fishing: Superior and its surrounding areas offer excellent fishing opportunities, with plenty of lakes, rivers, and streams stocked with trout, bass, and other fish.
  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Superior, Montana, with many ranches and stables offering guided trail rides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Superior

If you’re planning on spending a week in Superior, Montana, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Mineral County Museum and explore the historic downtown area. Day 2: Hike the Lookout Mountain Trail and visit Flathead National Forest. Day 3: Take a fishing trip on the Clark Fork River. Day 4: Visit the St. Regis Historical Museum and take a scenic drive through the mountains. Day 5: Spend the day horseback riding in the Lolo National Forest. Day 6: Visit the Alberton Railroad Depot Museum and take a scenic drive on the Pintler Scenic Route. Day 7: Take a rafting trip on the Clark Fork River or spend the day relaxing by the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Superior

Superior, Montana, may be a small town, but it has plenty of options when it comes to food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Made with locally sourced bison meat, these burgers are a must-try for meat lovers.
  • Huckleberry Pie: Huckleberries are a local specialty and are used in everything from pies to jams.
  • Trout: The rivers and lakes around Superior are teeming with trout, making it a must-try dish for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Huckleberry Beer: Brewed locally, huckleberry beer is a popular choice among beer lovers.
  • Montana Wines: Montana’s vineyards produce some excellent wines, with reds, whites, and roses to suit all tastes.

Where to Stay in Superior: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Superior, Montana, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Whispering Pines Motel: This family-owned motel offers affordable rooms with all the necessary amenities.
  • Riverfront Motel: Located on the banks of the Clark Fork River, this motel offers stunning views and comfortable rooms.
  • Diamond S Lodge: This lodge offers luxurious cabins and suites surrounded by nature.

What to Buy in Superior: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Superior, Montana, offers an array of locally made products and souvenirs worth taking home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Superior:

  • Huckleberry Jam: Made from local huckleberries, this jam is a delicious and unique souvenir.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans make stunning jewelry using Montana agate, a semi-precious stone found only in Montana.
  • Locally Made Soap: Made with natural ingredients like lavender, honey, and goat milk, these soaps are a great way to pamper yourself.

Local Culture and Customs of Superior

Superior, Montana, is a small town with a friendly and welcoming community. It’s important to respect the local customs and etiquette. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Montana is a state with a strong hunting culture, so it’s important to respect the hunting seasons and rules.
  • Ranching and farming are important industries in Montana, so respecting local farmers and their land is crucial.
  • Montana has a deep-seated appreciation of wildlife, so it’s essential to respect wildlife and their habitats.

Best Time to Visit Superior

The best time to visit Superior, Montana, is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is mild and comfortable, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Superior

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Superior, Montana:

Do:

  • Respect local wildlife and their habitats.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  • Follow the leave-no-trace policy when visiting nature reserves and parks.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Litter or disturb the natural habitat.
  • Disrespect local cultures and customs.

Safety and Health

Visiting Superior, Montana, is safe and comfortable, but it’s important to keep certain safety and health considerations in mind:

Safety in Superior

  • Be aware of wildlife and their habitats.
  • Follow all traffic rules and regulations.
  • Don’t leave valuables visible in your car.

Health in Superior

  • Wear protective clothing and use bug spray to avoid tick and insect bites.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • If you plan on hiking or spending time outdoors, be prepared for changing weather conditions.

Getting to Superior: Transportation Options and Logistics

Superior, Montana, is served by several airports, bus stations, and highways. Here’s how to get to Superior:

Airports Near Superior

  • Missoula International Airport: Located 64 miles west of Superior, this airport offers daily flights to major cities in the US, including Denver, Seattle, and Salt Lake City.

Bus Stations Near Superior

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Missoula, 64 miles west of Superior, Greyhound offers daily bus services to and from major cities in the US.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore Superior and its surroundings.
  • Train: The closest Amtrak station is located in Whitefish, which is about a 2-hour drive from Superior.

How to Get Around in Superior

The best way to get around Superior, Montana, is by car. However, there are also several bike rental shops in town, making it easy to explore the area on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Superior: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Superior, Montana, is surrounded by several beautiful destinations worth exploring. Here are some nearby excursions:

  • Glacier National Park: Located about a 2-hour drive north of Superior, Glacier National Park is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Missoula: Located about an hour’s drive west of Superior, Missoula is a vibrant college town with plenty of activities and attractions.
  • Helena: Located about a 2-hour drive east of Superior, Helena is a charming historic town with plenty of museums and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Superior, Montana, is a hidden gem in Northwestern Montana, offering a unique combination of rich history, stunning natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or simply relaxing, Superior has something for everyone.
