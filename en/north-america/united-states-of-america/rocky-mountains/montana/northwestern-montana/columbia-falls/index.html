Introduction to Columbia Falls

Columbia Falls is a charming town nestled in the northwestern region of Montana in the United States. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich heritage, and endless outdoor activities. Surrounded by towering mountains and rolling hills, Columbia Falls offers a plethora of adventures for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Columbia Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Museum at Central School

The Museum at Central School is a historic building that served as a school in the early 1900s. Today, it houses a museum that showcases the town’s history through various exhibits, artifacts, and photographs. Visitors can learn about the first settlers, the timber industry, and other important milestones in Columbia Falls’s past.

Meadow Lake Resort

Meadow Lake Resort is a golf resort that features a museum dedicated to native Montana wildlife. The museum showcases various species of animals found in the state’s mountain ranges, from black bears to bison. Visitors can learn about the history and importance of wildlife conservation in Montana.

Natural Attractions of Columbia Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Glacier National Park

Glacier National Park is the crown jewel of Columbia Falls and one of Montana’s top attractions. The park boasts stunning landscapes, pristine lakes, and a network of hiking trails that offer unforgettable experiences. Visitors can hike, bike, camp, or simply take in the natural beauty.

Hungry Horse Reservoir

Hungry Horse Reservoir is a man-made lake that provides a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city. It is surrounded by lush forests and offers scenic views of the Rocky Mountains. Visitors can swim, fish, and boat in the reservoir or take a hike on one of the many trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbia Falls

The Highline Trail

The Highline Trail in Glacier National Park is a 7.5-mile trail that offers stunning views of the park’s mountains and valleys. It is a moderate hike that takes around 4-5 hours to complete and is a must-do for any hiking enthusiast.

The Gateway to Glacier Trail

The Gateway to Glacier Trail is a 1.5-mile paved trail that offers stunning views of the park’s mountains and glaciers. It is suitable for all fitness levels and is wheelchair accessible.

Best Things to Do in Columbia Falls: Activities and Tours

Whitefish Mountain Resort

Whitefish Mountain Resort is a ski resort that offers a range of winter activities, including skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing. During the summer months, visitors can take advantage of the resort’s hiking trails, zip line, and mountain biking.

Blue Moon Nite Club

The Blue Moon Nite Club is a popular spot for live music and dancing in Columbia Falls. The atmosphere is laid back, and the locals are friendly, making it an excellent spot to have a relaxing night out.

Food and Drink Scene of Columbia Falls

Must-Try Dishes

Bison Burger

Bison burgers are a staple of Montana cuisine, and they are available at many restaurants in Columbia Falls. They are made with locally sourced bison meat and served on a bun with all the fixings.

Huckleberry Scones

Huckleberry scones are a beloved Montana pastry, and they are available at many bakeries in Columbia Falls. They are made with huckleberries, a local fruit that is similar to a blueberry but has a tart flavor.

Local Beverages

Montana Whiskey

Montana whiskey is a popular local drink that is made with Montana-grown grains and aged in oak barrels. You can find it at many bars and liquor stores in Columbia Falls.

Huckleberry Wine

Huckleberry wine is a sweet and tangy local wine that is made with huckleberries. It pairs well with Montana cuisine and is available at many local wineries.

Where to Stay in Columbia Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cedar Creek Lodge

Cedar Creek Lodge is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Columbia Falls. It offers spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

Meadow Lake Resort

Meadow Lake Resort is a family-friendly hotel that offers golfing, swimming, and skiing. It is located near Glacier National Park and offers stunning views of the mountains.

What to Buy in Columbia Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huckleberry Jam

Huckleberry jam is a delicious and popular local product that is made with huckleberries. You can find it at many local shops and souvenir stores.

Handmade Pottery

Many local artisans create handcrafted pottery, including mugs, plates, and bowls. You can find unique and beautiful pieces at local galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbia Falls

Columbia Falls has a rich and varied culture that is rooted in its timber and mining heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s history, traditions, and customs.

Best Time to Visit Columbia Falls

The best time to visit Columbia Falls is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you are interested in winter sports, visit from December to March when skiing and snowboarding are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbia Falls

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and their habitats.
  • Dress in layers as the weather can change quickly.
  • Try local cuisines and regional wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources.
  • Touch or feed wildlife, it is dangerous and illegal.
  • Enter private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbia Falls

Columbia Falls is a generally safe town. Crime rates are low, but as with any location, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions. Always be cautious when engaging in outdoor activities and check weather conditions before participating.

Health in Columbia Falls

Columbia Falls has a few medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should bring all necessary medications and supplies with them.

Getting to Columbia Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Columbia Falls

Columbia Falls is served by Glacier Park International Airport, located in Kalispell, MT. The airport is about 10 minutes from downtown Columbia Falls.

Bus Stations Near Columbia Falls

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Kalispell, approximately 10 minutes from Columbia Falls.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Columbia Falls by car. Glacier National Park is within driving distance of many major cities in the western United States.

How to Get Around in Columbia Falls

Visitors can explore Columbia Falls by car or bike. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area. Glacier National Park offers shuttle services during the summer months.

Where to Go Next from Columbia Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whitefish

Whitefish is a charming town located just north of Columbia Falls. It is known for its ski resort, art galleries, and live music scene.

Kalispell

Kalispell is the largest city in the Flathead Valley region and offers a range of shopping, cultural, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Columbia Falls is a hidden gem tucked away in the mountains of Montana. With its stunning scenery, rich heritage, and endless outdoor activities, it is a must-visit destination for travelers of all types. Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, Columbia Falls offers something for everyone.
