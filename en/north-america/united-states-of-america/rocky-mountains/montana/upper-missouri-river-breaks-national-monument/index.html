Introduction to Upper Missouri River Breaks National Monument

Upper Missouri River Breaks National Monument is situated in Montana, spanning 375,000 acres of breathtaking landscapes that encompass sections of the Missouri River, White Cliffs, and Breaks. This unique terrain offers visitors both natural and cultural attractions, attracting outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover the most important aspects of visiting the Upper Missouri River Breaks National Monument, including top places to visit, outdoor activities, where to stay, and local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Upper Missouri River Breaks National Monument

Day 1: Visit Fort Benton National Historic Landmark and take the Missouri River Boat Tour. Day 2: Hike the Cow Creek trail and watch the sunset from the White Cliffs. Day 3: Explore the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center and Museum. Day 4: Take a scenic drive through the region and visit Black Butte. Day 5: Experience river rafting on the Missouri River. Day 6: Visit First People’s Buffalo Jump State Park, followed by bird watching. Day 7: Go hunting and fishing in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper Missouri River Breaks National Monument

Montana is known for its cowboy culture, so it’s not unusual to spot people wearing cowboy boots and hats. Visitors should be aware of local customs, particularly when exploring heritage sites like First People’s Buffalo Jump State Park.

Best Time to Visit Upper Missouri River Breaks National Monument

The best time to visit the Upper Missouri River Breaks National Monument is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, fall is also a pleasant time to visit, as visitors can enjoy the beautiful autumn foliage and hiking without the crowds.
