A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Walsenburg, Colorado

Nestled in the southern part of Colorado and located in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Walsenburg is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. From hiking and biking to museums and historical places, Walsenburg has it all. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover all the must-see attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Walsenburg

Walsenburg is a town in Southern Colorado with a population of approximately 3,000 people. The town was founded in 1870 and has a rich history dating back to the early 1800s. Walsenburg is known for its stunning natural scenery, including the Spanish Peaks, towering above the town’s backdrop. The area provides an outdoor paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts alike.

List of Places to Visit in Walsenburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Walsenburg has a rich cultural and historical heritage. Here are a few culturally important attractions that should make your itinerary:

  • Lathrop State Park: The first state park in Colorado is home to two pristine scenic lakes.
  • Walsenburg Mining Museum: Learn about the town’s mining history in an authentic and engaging museum.
  • Museum of Friends: A contemporary art museum displaying contemporary artwork from established and emerging artists.
  • Walsenburg Wild Waters: This small family-friendly water park offers a refreshing and relaxing day out.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Walsenburg is one of Colorado’s best-kept secrets for outdoor activities and offers something for everyone, from mountain biking to hot springs. Don’t miss out on visiting these natural attractions:

  • Lathrop State Park: If you love hiking and biking, Lathrop State Park offers miles of trails for all skill levels.
  • Spanish Peaks: The iconic Spanish Peaks can be enjoyed from several viewing areas, including Highway 160 and La Veta Pass.
  • Huerfano Butte: This small mountain provides an easy but challenging hike, with a steep ascent to the top. Once at the top, hikers will enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walsenburg

Walsenburg is the ideal destination for hiking and biking with an extensive network of trails for all levels of experience. Here are a few must-visit hiking and biking trails:

  • Apishapa Trail: The Apishapa Trail is a 4.5-mile roundtrip hike that offers endless scenic views along the way.
  • Cucharas Canyon Trail: This trail is known for its spectacular views and unique rock formations found on its 7-mile loop.
  • Huerfano Butte: As mentioned before, the Huerfano Butte provides an easy 3.5-mile roundtrip hike with the ultimate reward: 360-degree views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Walsenburg: Activities and Tours

Here are a few activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Hot Springs: Enjoy a relaxing soak in one of Colorado’s hot springs at Wagon Wheel Gap.
  • Walsenburg Wild Waters: Cool off in this small but fun water park ideal for families.
  • Trinidad Lake State Park: A scenic park with miles of hiking and biking trails. Visitors can also fish or paddleboard in the lake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Walsenburg

Planning a week-long itinerary to Walsenburg is a breeze with plenty of activities to choose from. Here is what we recommend for a week-long stay:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Walsenburg Mining Museum and learn about the town’s mining history
2 Spend a relaxing day at Lathrop State Park
3 Go on a hike in the morning and soak in the Wagon Wheel Gap Hot Springs in the afternoon
4 Visit the Museum of Friends and appreciate the contemporary artwork on display
5 Explore the mountain biking trails at Cucharas Canyon Trail
6 Visit Trinidad Lake State Park for a fun day of lake activities
7 Enjoy a scenic drive up to La Veta Pass and marvel at the Spanish Peaks

Food and Drink Scene of Walsenburg

Hunger pangs and thirst will not be a problem in Walsenburg. Here’s what you need to do to make the most of the food and drink scene:

Must-Try Dishes

Walsenburg has several fantastic dining options. Don’t leave without trying these dishes:

  • Green Chile Cheeseburger: A Colorado staple, made with fresh green chilies and juicy beef patties.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A unique dish made from deep-fried bull’s testicles.
  • Enchiladas: A local favorite, stacked or rolled, stuffed with your choice of fillings, topped with a spicy red or green chile.

Local Beverages

Thirsty from all the hiking or biking? Here are a few local beverages to try:

  • Colorado Craft Beer: Several breweries in the area offer refreshing and delicious craft beers that complement the local food scene.
  • Colorado Wine: Colorado is known for its wineries, and many vineyards can be found near Walsenburg, producing fantastic wines.

Where to Stay in Walsenburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Walsenburg has several options for accommodation that will suit all travelers. Here are a few:

  • La Plaza Inn: Located in the center of town, La Plaza Inn has historical roots dating back to the early 1900s and has been remodeled to its former glory.
  • Spanish Peaks Inn: Offering a homely feel, this cozy and comfortable inn is located just outside of town and surrounded by the stunning natural scenery that Walsenburg is known for.
  • Best Western Rambler: Recently renovated, this hotel offers modern amenities and is conveniently located just off the highway.

What to Buy in Walsenburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Walsenburg offers several locally made products that make for the perfect souvenir. Here are a few to look out for:

  • Pottery: Several local artists produce beautiful and unique pieces of pottery inspired by the surrounding area’s natural beauty.
  • Honey: Local beekeepers offer raw and delicious honey from the abundant wildflowers in the area.
  • Artwork: The town is home to several contemporary art galleries that offer artwork inspired by the surrounding natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Walsenburg

Walsenburg has a rich cultural background dating back to its founding. The town is known for its mining history, vibrant art scene, and unique local cuisine. Visitors will experience a blend of Western hospitality and mountain charm in Walsenburg.

Best Time to Visit Walsenburg

Walsenburg can be visited year-round. Summer months are ideal for outdoor activities, while fall and winter offer stunning views of the area’s foliage and snow-capped peaks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walsenburg

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take time to appreciate the stunning natural scenery.
  • Try the local cuisine and support small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash on hiking trails or outdoor recreation areas.
  • Ignore safety regulations when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Walsenburg

Walsenburg is generally a safe town but, as with any travel destination, visitors should take basic precautions, such as locking vehicles and securing valuables.

Health in Walsenburg

Visitors should be aware that Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever is present in the area and take precautions when hiking. Visitors should also stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun when participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Walsenburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Walsenburg is easy with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Walsenburg

The closest airport to Walsenburg is Pueblo Memorial Airport, located 50 miles away. Denver International Airport is also a feasible option, approximately 3 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Walsenburg

Greyhound offers bus services to Walsenburg, with a stop conveniently located in the center of town.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to drive to Walsenburg, with Highway 160 conveniently running through the town.

How to Get Around in Walsenburg

Walsenburg is a small town, and most attractions and accommodation options are easily accessible on foot. However, visitors can rent a bike or car from one of several rental companies in the area.

Where to Go Next from Walsenburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to extend your trip and explore more of Colorado? Here are some options:

  • Great Sand Dunes National Park: A unique and picturesque national park, with the tallest sand dunes in North America, less than 2 hours away.
  • Pagosa Springs: Explore the town’s hot springs and enjoy the nearby winter activities, like skiing and snowshoeing.
  • Colorado Springs: A quaint city with endless activities to offer, including hiking, biking, and sightseeing.

Conclusion

Walsenburg offers an unforgettable experience for anyone looking for outdoor adventures, unique cultural experiences, and delicious food. Plan your visit today and explore all that Walsenburg has to offer!
