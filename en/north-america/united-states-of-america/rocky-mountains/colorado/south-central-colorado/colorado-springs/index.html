Introduction to Colorado Springs

Nestled in the scenic Rocky Mountains, Colorado Springs is a city that offers natural wonders, cultural attractions, and thrilling outdoor adventures. With its rich history, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant culture, Colorado Springs welcomes visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re traveling solo, with family, or friends, there’s plenty to see and do in Colorado Springs.

List of Places to Visit in Colorado Springs

From museums to natural wonders to outdoor recreational activities, Colorado Springs has something to offer everyone. Here’s a list of places to visit during your stay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • U.S. Olympic & Paralympic Museum: This state-of-the-art museum celebrates the legacy of Olympic and Paralympic athletes and their stories of perseverance and achievement.
  • Garden of the Gods: This park has over 1,300 acres of stunning, natural rock formations, hiking trails, and geological wonders.
  • Colorado Springs Pioneers Museum: Take a walk through Colorado’s history at this fascinating museum, which covers the history of Colorado Springs and the Pikes Peak Region.
  • Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site: This living history museum brings the history and culture of the American West to life with historic homes, barns, and a blacksmith shop.

Natural Attractions of Colorado Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pikes Peak: Standing at over 14,000 feet, Pikes Peak offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Take a scenic drive up the mountain or hike to the summit.
  • Cheyenne Mountain State Park: This 2,701-acre park has over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails and breathtaking views of the southern Rocky Mountains.
  • North Cheyenne Cañon Park: This park offers hiking, rock climbing, and stunning views of the canyon and waterfall.
  • The Broadmoor Seven Falls: With seven cascading waterfalls, this natural wonder is a must-visit during your stay in Colorado Springs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails for all skill levels. Here are a few top-rated hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Manitou Incline: This challenging hike has 2,744 steps and gains over 2,000 feet in elevation.
  • Red Rock Canyon Open Space: With over 1,400 acres of open space and more than 10 miles of hiking and biking trails, this park offers stunning views of the red rock formations.
  • Palmer Park: This 730-acre park has trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as stunning views of the Front Range.
  • Bear Creek Regional Park: This park has over 500 acres of natural beauty, including hiking trails that wind through the forest and along the creek.

Best Things to Do in Colorado Springs: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Colorado Springs. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  • Zip Line Tours: Get a bird’s eye view of the stunning landscape by ziplining through the mountains.
  • Rafting: White-water rafting on the Arkansas River is a thrilling adventure that’s sure to get your adrenaline pumping.
  • Balloon Rides: Take a hot air balloon ride over the Rockies and enjoy the breathtaking scenery from above.
  • Jeep Tours: Explore off-road trails and backcountry terrain in the rugged jeeps.

Food and Drink Scene of Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs has a lively food and drink scene, with a variety of cuisine options to choose from. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colorado Green Chili: This spicy stew is a Colorado staple, made with pork, green chilies, and sometimes beans or potatoes.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A delicacy in Colorado, these are deep-fried bull testicles that are often served with hot sauce or salsa.
  • Bison Burgers: Colorado is known for its bison, and you can find bison burgers on many restaurant menus.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: With over 30 local breweries in the Colorado Springs area, you’re sure to find a beer you love.
  • Local Wine: Colorado is famous for its wine, and there are several wineries in the area that offer tastings and tours.
  • Coffee: Colorado Springs has a thriving coffee scene, with several locally-owned coffee shops serving up delicious brews.

Where to Stay in Colorado Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Colorado Springs has a wide variety of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few top-rated options:

  • The Broadmoor: This luxurious resort has everything you need for a comfortable stay, including multiple restaurants, a spa, and a golf course.
  • Buffalo Lodge Bicycle Resort: This unique guesthouse is perfect for cyclists, with bike rentals, a repair station, and easy access to local trails.
  • Hostel Fish: This hip hostel is located in the heart of downtown Colorado Springs and offers both dorm and private rooms.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Colorado Springs Central: This affordable hotel offers comfortable rooms and convenient amenities, including a free breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Colorado Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home from Colorado Springs, here are a few ideas:

  • Colorado-made Goods: From locally-made pottery to handmade jewelry and crafts, you’ll find plenty of unique items at the Colorado Springs Farmers Market or local boutiques.
  • Outdoor Gear and Apparel: With so many hiking and outdoor recreational activities in the area, it’s no surprise that Colorado Springs has numerous outdoor stores and shops that sell gear and apparel.
  • Colorado Souvenirs: From t-shirts to shot glasses, you’ll find plenty of Colorado-themed souvenirs at gift shops and souvenir stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs is a melting pot of cultures, with a rich history and diverse community. Here are a few things to know about the local culture and customs:

  • Respect for Nature: Coloradans have a deep respect for the natural world and prioritize conservation and outdoor recreation.
  • Friendliness: People in Colorado Springs are generally friendly and welcoming to visitors.
  • Active Lifestyle: With so many outdoor recreational activities and a healthy food scene, residents of Colorado Springs tend to be health-conscious and active.

Best Time to Visit Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the seasons:

  • Spring: This is a great time to visit if you love outdoor activities and want to avoid the crowds. The weather is mild, and the flowers are in bloom.
  • Summer: Summer is the peak season in Colorado Springs, with warm temperatures, sunny skies, and a bustling social scene.
  • Fall: The fall foliage in Colorado Springs is absolutely stunning, and the weather is mild.
  • Winter: The winter season in Colorado Springs brings snow and colder temperatures, but it’s also a great time for winter sports and holiday festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colorado Springs

Do:

  • Do dress in layers: The weather in Colorado Springs can be unpredictable, so it’s best to be prepared for any climate. Dress in layers so you can easily adjust to changing temperatures.
  • Do respect nature: Colorado Springs is home to incredible natural wonders and wildlife, so it’s important to be respectful of the environment.
  • Do tip generously: Tipping is customary in the service industry in Colorado Springs, so be sure to tip generously for good service.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the altitude: Colorado Springs is located at a high altitude, so it’s important to take it easy and stay hydrated to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Don’t litter: As mentioned earlier, Coloradans have a deep respect for the natural world, so littering is not only disrespectful but also illegal.
  • Don’t drive under the influence: Driving while under the influence of drugs or alcohol is not only dangerous but also illegal in Colorado.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s important to take precautions as you would in any city. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Whether you’re hiking or exploring downtown, it’s important to be mindful of your surroundings and stay alert.
  • Lock your car and hotel room: Petty theft can happen anywhere, so be sure to lock your car and hotel room when you’re not there.
  • Stay on designated trails: When hiking or exploring natural areas, be sure to stay on designated trails to avoid getting lost or harming the environment.

Health in Colorado Springs

The altitude in Colorado Springs can be challenging for some visitors, so it’s important to take care of your health during your stay. Here are a few tips:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to avoid altitude sickness and stay hydrated during outdoor activities.
  • Take it easy: Give yourself time to adjust to the altitude and take it easy during the first few days of your stay.
  • Pack necessary medications: If you have any medical conditions, be sure to bring necessary medications with you.

Getting to Colorado Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Colorado Springs is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are a few transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Colorado Springs

  • Colorado Springs Airport: Located just 10 miles from downtown Colorado Springs, this is the most convenient airport for domestic and international travelers.
  • Denver International Airport: Located 90 miles from Colorado Springs, this is a larger airport with more flight options and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Colorado Springs

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Colorado Springs, this is the main bus station in the area.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: If you’re planning to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car is a convenient option.
  • Shuttle Service: There are several shuttle services that offer transportation from the airport and around Colorado Springs.

How to Get Around in Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs is a relatively small city, so getting around is easy. Here are a few transportation options:

  • Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the area and visit nearby attractions.
  • Bike: Colorado Springs has an extensive bike trail system, and there are several bike rental shops in the area.
  • Bus: The Mountain Metropolitan Transit offers bus service throughout Colorado Springs and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Colorado Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Colorado, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Denver: The capital of Colorado is just 70 miles north of Colorado Springs and offers plenty of urban attractions and outdoor activities.
  • Manitou Cliff Dwellings: Located just 6 miles west of Colorado Springs, this historic site allows visitors to explore ancient cliff dwellings and learn about the Anasazi people.
  • Royal Gorge Bridge and Park: Located about an hour southwest of Colorado Springs, this park has the highest suspension bridge in the United States and offers spectacular views of the Arkansas River.
  • Great Sand Dunes National Park: Located about 2 hours south of Colorado Springs, this national park has awe-inspiring sand dunes, alpine lakes, and lush forests.

Conclusion

Colorado Springs has something to offer every traveler, from stunning natural wonders to world-class cultural attractions. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a week-long adventure, our comprehensive guide will help you explore all that this beautiful city has to offer.
