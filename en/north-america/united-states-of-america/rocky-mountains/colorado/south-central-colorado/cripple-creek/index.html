Introduction to Cripple Creek

Nestled in the Rocky Mountains of South Central Colorado, Cripple Creek is a historic mining town that offers a rich cultural experience and incredible natural beauty. Visitors can immerse themselves in the history of the town’s gold-rush past, walk through the charming downtown area, take a scenic drive through the mountains or enjoy outdoor adventures in nearby parks.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the top tourist destinations in Cripple Creek, including must-visit museums and historical sites, scenic hiking trails, top-rated restaurants, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Cripple Creek

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cripple Creek Heritage Center: This is the best place to start your Cripple Creek visit. It showcases the town’s rich history, including its gold-mining past. You’ll find interactive displays, artifacts, and life-sized exhibits that provide a glimpse into the town’s vibrant past.
  • Mollie Kathleen Gold Mine: This mine offers an underground tour of the mine shafts that were in service during the gold rush. This is an excellent way to experience what life was like for miners in Cripple Creek.
  • Victor Lowell Thomas Museum: Learn more about the town’s mining history and the people who worked in mines.

Natural Attractions of Cripple Creek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pikes Peak: The summit of Pikes Peak, which is located near Cripple Creek, offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Take the Pikes Peak Highway to reach the summit.
  • Mueller State Park: This park is situated just outside of Cripple Creek and offers visitors an excellent opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains. You can hike, bike and enjoy the fresh air.
  • Florissant Fossil Beds National Monument: This national monument is located near Cripple Creek, where visitors can visit the unique fossil sites and learn about the geological history of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cripple Creek

One of the best ways to explore Cripple Creek is through hiking and biking. There are various routes for all skill levels, and you can take in the beautiful views of the mountains. Some popular trails include:

  • Mt. Pisgah: A popular hiking trail featuring a 6-mile round trip hike and provides magnificent views of the surrounding areas.
  • Horsethief Park: This 13-mile trail has a moderately challenging hike that takes you through beautiful landscapes. The peak offers a beautiful view of Cripple Creek.
  • The Cripple Creek Heritage Trail: A moderate 1.5-mile walk that takes you through downtown and past many historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Cripple Creek: Activities and Tours

Cripple Creek offers a variety of fun activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Some of the best things to do in Cripple Creek include:

  • Ghost tours: During the ghost tour, you will get to discover the spooky stories and legends that lurk in the historic mining town.
  • Casino tour: You can take a tour of one of the casinos in Cripple Creek, learn the gambling rules and get a chance to win big.
  • Gold Belt Tour Scenic Byway: Take a self-guided tour of the Gold Belt Scenic Byway to discover the surrounding scenic beauty and natural wonders.

Food and Drink Scene of Cripple Creek

Must-Try Dishes

When in Cripple Creek, enjoy some unique dishes that reflect the local flavor. Here we’ve listed a few must-try dishes:

  • Green Chili: It’s a must-try delicacy made with green chilies, roasted tomatillos, pork shoulder, and cumin.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: This dish is made by slicing the testicles of a bull, coating them in batter, and fried until golden brown.
  • Pancake/Waffle Lasagna: It’s an unusual combination of pancakes or waffles layered with eggs, sausage, and cheese.

Local Beverages

When in Cripple Creek, be sure to try some locally brewed beverages that you can’t find anywhere else. Some of the most popular brews include:

  • Phantom Canyon Brewery: This brewery offers a range of craft beers, including some that are only available at the brewery.
  • BierWorks Brewery: This brewery produces traditional German beer, including Pilsner and Dunkel.

Where to Stay in Cripple Creek: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cripple Creek offers several comfortable and affordable accommodations for visitors. Here are some top options:

  • Century Casino Hotel: This casino and hotel provide comfortable rooms and suites, making it the perfect stop for anyone who wants to try their hand at gambling.
  • Dillon Motel: This family-owned motel offers guests spacious rooms and a warm welcome.
  • The Cripple Creek Hospitality House: This guest house provides a truly unique experience, thanks to its blend of old-world charm and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Cripple Creek: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Cripple Creek, you’ll find unique souvenirs and products that you can take home. Some popular items include:

  • Gold: Although gold mining isn’t as vibrant as it used to be, it’s still possible to find gold here.
  • Coffee Beans: Local coffee beans are an excellent souvenir to take as a keepsake.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Cripple Creek is known for authentic, handcrafted jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Cripple Creek

Cripple Creek has a rich history that locals are proud of and celebrate every day. Visitors should respect the local customs and immerse themselves in the culture to have an authentic experience.

Best Time to Visit Cripple Creek

The best time to visit Cripple Creek is during the summer season (June to August) when the weather is dry, and daylight hours are long. Winter months are also beautiful and offer a different kind of experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cripple Creek

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress in layers as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Take a ghost tour to experience the spooky side of Cripple Creek.

Don’t:

  • Litter or deface historical sites.
  • Take gold or artifacts that rightfully belong to the town.
  • Be rude to the locals or other tourists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cripple Creek

Cripple Creek is a safe place for tourists to visit. However, visitors should always take precautions when hiking or exploring the natural surroundings.

Health in Cripple Creek

In terms of healthcare, Cripple Creek has a community hospital, pharmacies, and clinics for minor illnesses and injuries. For more serious health issues, visitors can go to Colorado Springs hospital.

Getting to Cripple Creek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cripple Creek

The nearest airport to Cripple Creek is Colorado Springs Airport, which is about a 45-minute drive away. Other airports near Cripple Creek are Denver International Airport and Pueblo, Colorado airport.

Bus Stations Near Cripple Creek

Greyhound Bus services do not pick up or drop off in Cripple Creek, but there are several bus stations near Cripple Creek in Colorado Springs, Pueblo, and Canon City.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to rent a car and drive to Cripple Creek. This provides a scenic drive through the Rocky Mountains.

How to Get Around in Cripple Creek

Cripple Creek is a small town, and many of the popular attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use the free shuttle service to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Cripple Creek: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other nearby destinations, here are some popular options:

  • Colorado Springs: Visit Garden of the Gods, Pikes Peak, or the United States Air Force Academy.
  • Pueblo: Explore the pueblo nature center or the steelworks museum.
  • Royal Gorge Region: Enjoy the Royal Gorge bridge experience, or ride the Royal Gorge train.

Conclusion

Cripple Creek is a unique and historic town that offers a glimpse into a rich past. The town’s gold-mining history, natural beauty, and unique local culture make it a must-visit destination for tourists. Use this guide to plan your trip to Cripple Creek and explore the town’s highlights and hidden gems.
