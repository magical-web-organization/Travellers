Introduction to Monument

Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Monument is a charming town in South Central Colorado that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor recreation, and rich cultural heritage. With stunning vistas of towering peaks, sprawling valleys, and glistening lakes, Monument is an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Monument

While there are plenty of attractions to explore in Monument, here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Monument Rock: This natural wonder is a must-see for first-time visitors to Monument. Known for its unique red sandstone formations, Monument Rock is located on private property but can be viewed from afar.
  • Western Museum of Mining and Industry: This museum showcases the history of mining and technology in the West and offers guided tours of the exhibits and working machines.
  • Colorado Springs Pioneers Museum: Located in nearby Colorado Springs, this museum features artifacts and exhibits about the local area’s settlement and development.

Natural Attractions of Monument: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Garden of the Gods: This natural landmark features stunning red rock formations and stunning vistas of Pikes Peak. Visitors can enjoy hiking, rock climbing, and picnicking in the park.
  • Pikes Peak: This iconic mountain rises over 14,000 feet tall and is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and scenic drives.
  • Cheyenne Mountain State Park: This park offers over 20 miles of hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Palmer Park: This park features over 25 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as breathtaking views of Monument and surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monument

As an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, Monument offers a wide array of hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few of the most popular routes:

  • Mount Herman Trail: This moderate hike extends over 4 miles round-trip and features stunning views of Pike’s Peak and the surrounding mountains.
  • Limbaugh Canyon Trail: A moderate trail that meanders through stunning alpine meadows with breathtaking views of the area’s mountains and canyons.
  • Santa Fe Trail: A popular mountain biking route that stretches for 8.5 miles, this trail features challenging terrain and is suitable for experienced bikers.
  • Monument Rock Trail: A scenic 2.8-mile hike that features views of Monument Rock and the surrounding hillsides.

Best Things to Do in Monument: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun activities to enjoy in Monument, from family-friendly adventures to thrilling outdoor experiences. Here are a few of the best things to do in Monument:

  • Zip-lining: Experience the thrill of flying through the air on a zip line adventure with Adventures Out West.
  • Horseback riding: Journey through the Rocky Mountains on horseback with one of Monument’s many outfitters.
  • White water rafting: Cool off in the summer heat with white water rafting adventures on the Arkansas River.
  • Hot-air ballooning: Watch the sunrise over the Rocky Mountains on a hot-air balloon ride with Rocky Mountain Balloon Adventures.

Food and Drink Scene of Monument

Experience the unique flavors of Monument with its many local restaurants and bars. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: These deep-fried balls of bull testicles are a unique local specialty that you won’t find anywhere else.
  • Green Chile: A local favorite, this spicy stew is made with green chiles, pork, and beans and is a must-try for visitors.
  • Colorado-style Pizza: Local pizzerias serve up pizzas topped with unique ingredients like buffalo, venison, and elk.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado wine: The state is home to an array of excellent wineries, including several in the nearby Colorado Springs area.
  • Craft beer: Monument boasts several craft breweries, including Pikes Peak Brewing and Red Leg Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Monument: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

No matter your budget or travel style, Monument has the perfect accommodation option for you. Here are a few top choices:

  • Luxury: The Cliff House at Pikes Peak offers stunning views, luxurious amenities, and exceptional service.
  • Mid-range: The Woodland Inn and Suites is a budget-friendly hotel that is located right in the heart of Monument and offers easy access to local attractions.
  • Hostel: The Colorado Mountain Hostel offers affordable, communal-style accommodations and is a popular choice for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Monument: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect memento of your trip to Monument? Here are a few unique local products to bring home:

  • Handcrafted pottery: Monument is known for its local pottery, which can be found at the Willowstone Marketplace.
  • Colorado wine: Bring home a bottle of local wine from one of the nearby wineries.
  • Handmade jewelry: Local artisans create unique jewelry pieces from colorful stones and minerals found in the surrounding area.

Local Culture and Customs of Monument

As a small, tight-knit community, Monument is proud of its local heritage and cultural traditions. Visitors can expect friendly, welcoming locals who are happy to share their hometown with others.

Best Time to Visit Monument

The best time to visit Monument is during the late spring and early fall when temperatures are mild, and the crowds are thinner than during peak summer season. Spring is also a great time to see the wildflowers in bloom, while fall foliage can be enjoyed in late September and early October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monument

Do:

  • Respect private property: Many of the attractions in Monument are located on private property, so be sure to respect signage and stay on designated trails.
  • Pack appropriately: Weather conditions can change rapidly in the Rocky Mountains, so be sure to bring layers, warm clothing, and sun protection.
  • Leave no trace: Help preserve Monument’s natural beauty by packing out your trash and leaving the area as you found it.

Don’t:

  • Feed wildlife: While it may be tempting to feed the wildlife, it is important to refrain from doing so. Feeding wildlife can disrupt their natural feeding patterns and may be harmful to their health.
  • Ignore weather alerts: Rocky Mountain weather can be unpredictable and severe. Always heed weather warnings and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monument

While Monument is a safe town with low crime rates, visitors should still take appropriate safety precautions when exploring the surrounding area. Be sure to hike and bike in pairs, wear appropriate gear, and check weather conditions before heading out.

Health in Monument

Altitude sickness can be a concern for those visiting Monument due to its high elevation. Visitors should give themselves time to acclimate to the altitude and stay hydrated to prevent altitude sickness.

Getting to Monument: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monument

The nearest major airport to Monument is the Colorado Springs Airport, located 35 minutes from the town center. Alternatively, Denver International Airport is located approximately 90 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Monument

Greyhound operates a bus station in Colorado Springs, which is the closest option for those traveling by bus.

Other Options

Those looking for a scenic route can take the Amtrak train to the Colorado Springs station and rent a car to drive the remainder of the way to Monument.

How to Get Around in Monument

While Monument is a relatively small town that is easily walkable, a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding area and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Monument: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore more of Colorado, here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Colorado Springs: Located just 20 minutes from Monument, Colorado Springs is home to an array of attractions, including the Air Force Academy and the Olympic Training Center.
  • Denver: The state capital of Denver is just a 90-minute drive from Monument and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums, parks, and galleries.
  • Boulder: This university town is located just over two hours from Monument and offers excellent hiking, dining, and shopping options.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking stunning natural beauty, outdoor recreation, or rich cultural attractions, Monument offers something for everyone. With its warm community spirit, friendly locals, and array of activities and attractions, this is a destination that should not be missed.
