Introduction to Salida

Located in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Salida is a charming small town that offers visitors a taste of authentic Colorado life. With 300 days of sunshine per year and endless opportunities for outdoor adventure, Salida is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts, outdoor adventurers, and anyone looking for a relaxing escape in the mountains.

This comprehensive travel guide to Salida will help you plan your trip, whether you’re looking to explore the town’s rich history, discover natural wonders, or simply relax and enjoy the stunning scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Salida

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salida Museum

The Salida Museum offers visitors the chance to explore the fascinating history of Salida and the Arkansas Valley. From the town’s early mining days to the present, the museum showcases Salida’s rich culture and heritage.

Hutchinson Homestead & Learning Center

The Hutchinson Homestead & Learning Center is a 140-acre working ranch that offers a glimpse into the life of a Colorado pioneer family. Visitors can tour the original 1873 homestead cabin and explore the many interactive exhibits that showcase life in the Old West.

Natural Attractions of Salida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Arkansas Hills Trail System

The Arkansas Hills Trail System is a network of over 45 miles of trails that meander through stunning natural scenery, including rocky cliffs, lush forests, and sweeping views of the surrounding mountains. Whether you’re hiking, biking, or running, there’s a trail for everyone in this extensive network of trails.

Mount Shavano

Standing at 14,229 feet, Mount Shavano is one of the most popular 14ers in Colorado for hikers and climbers. With several trailheads to choose from, visitors can choose a challenging or scenic route to the summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salida

Monarch Crest Trail

The Monarch Crest Trail is a world-renowned mountain biking trail that takes riders through some of the most spectacular scenery in the Rocky Mountains. Stretching over 30 miles, this trail offers something for every level of rider, from beginner to expert.

Browns Creek Trail

The Browns Creek Trail is a popular hiking and mountain biking trail that leads to a beautiful waterfall. With stunning views of the surrounding mountains and plenty of wildlife to spot, this trail is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Salida: Activities and Tours

Rafting on the Arkansas River

The Arkansas River is a popular destination for whitewater rafting, with Class II-IV rapids that offer an exciting challenge for experienced rafters and a fun adventure for beginners. There are many rafting outfitters in Salida that offer guided trips down the river.

Rocky Mountain Jeep Tours

Rocky Mountain Jeep Tours offer visitors the chance to explore the backcountry of the Rocky Mountains in a guided jeep tour. With stunning vistas, hidden waterfalls, and unique wildlife sightings, this is an experience not to be missed.

Food and Drink Scene of Salida

Must-Try Dishes

Green Chile

Green Chile is a staple of Colorado cuisine, and Salida’s version is not to be missed. This spicy, flavorful dish is perfect for warming up on a cool mountain evening.

Bison Burger

Bison burgers are a local favorite in Salida, offering a lean and flavorful twist on a classic American dish. Many local restaurants offer bison burgers on their menus.

Local Beverages

Colorado Craft Beer

Colorado is known for its thriving craft beer scene, and Salida is no exception. With several local breweries in the area, visitors can enjoy a variety of delicious, locally-brewed beers.

Salida Wine

Salida is also home to several wineries that offer a variety of locally-produced wines. Whether you prefer red, white, or rose, there’s a wine to suit every taste.

Where to Stay in Salida: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amigo Motor Lodge

The Amigo Motor Lodge is a retro-chic motel that has been beautifully restored, offering guests a unique and stylish place to stay in Salida. The rooms are cozy and comfortable, with thoughtful details and modern amenities.

The Palace Hotel

The Palace Hotel is a historic hotel that has been lovingly restored to its former glory. With elegant rooms, fine dining, and a prime downtown location, this hotel offers guests a luxurious and unforgettable stay in Salida.

What to Buy in Salida: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Artwork by Local Artists

Salida is home to a thriving arts scene, and visitors can find a variety of local artwork to bring home as a souvenir. From paintings and sculptures to pottery and jewelry, there’s something for everyone.

Hot Springs Products

Salida is also known for its natural hot springs, and visitors can bring home a variety of hot springs products, including soaps, lotions, and bath salts.

Local Culture and Customs of Salida

Salida is a laid-back, friendly town with a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s natural beauty and take care to leave no trace when hiking or adventuring in the mountains.

Best Time to Visit Salida

The best time to visit Salida is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the mountain scenery is at its most beautiful. Fall is also a great time to visit, with stunning fall foliage and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salida

Do:

  • Do take advantage of Salida’s incredible outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and rafting.
  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the green chile and bison burgers.
  • Do support local businesses and artists.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the trails or in the hot springs.
  • Don’t disregard trail closures or warning signs.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather in the mountains, and be sure to pack appropriate gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salida

Salida is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions to stay safe, particularly when hiking or adventuring in the mountains. Visitors should be aware of wildlife, weather conditions, and other potential hazards.

Health in Salida

Salida is at over 7,000 feet in elevation, and visitors should take precautions to avoid altitude sickness. Visitors should drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol and caffeine, and take it easy for the first few days until they acclimate to the altitude.

Getting to Salida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salida

The closest major airports to Salida are Denver International Airport and Colorado Springs Airport, both located about two hours away.

Bus Stations Near Salida

Greyhound offers bus service to Salida from several nearby towns and cities, including Denver and Colorado Springs.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Salida, or take a shuttle service from the airport or nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Salida

Salida is a small town, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance of downtown. Visitors can also rent a car or take a local taxi or rideshare service to get around Salida.

Where to Go Next from Salida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Great Sand Dunes National Park

Located less than two hours from Salida, Great Sand Dunes National Park is a stunning natural wonder that features the tallest sand dunes in North America. Visitors can hike the dunes, explore the surrounding mountains, and enjoy incredible views of the park’s unique landscape.

Buena Vista

Just a short drive from Salida, Buena Vista is a charming small town that offers visitors stunning mountain views, outdoor adventures, and a thriving arts scene.

Conclusion

Salida is a hidden gem in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, offering visitors an authentic taste of Colorado’s natural beauty and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s rich history and culture, hike through stunning mountain scenery, or simply relax and enjoy the stunning views, Salida is a must-visit destination for any Colorado travel itinerary.
