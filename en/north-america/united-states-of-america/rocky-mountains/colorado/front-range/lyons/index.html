Introduction to Lyons

Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Lyons is a charming town that serves as a gateway to some of Colorado’s most breathtaking natural attractions. The town offers a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary experiences that make it a must-visit destination. In this travel guide, we’ll explore everything Lyons has to offer so you can plan your ultimate trip to this Colorado gem.

List of Places to Visit in Lyons

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lyons has a rich history that is celebrated in its museums and historical attractions. The Lyons Redstone Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum showcases exhibits on the town’s mining and ranching history, as well as its geology and paleontology. Another notable museum is the Planet Bluegrass Ranch, which is home to the annual Telluride Bluegrass Festival. You can also visit Lyons Historic District, a collection of restored buildings that were built in the 1800s and now house boutiques, galleries, and restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Lyons: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lyons is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. The town is surrounded by the Rocky Mountains, which offer plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and rock climbing. One of the most popular spots for outdoor adventures is Button Rock Preserve, which features hiking trails, trout fishing, and stunning views of the Colorado mountains. You can also visit Rabbit Mountain Open Space, a 3,500-acre park with stunning views of the Front Range. If you’re looking for a more relaxing outdoor experience, head to Lyons Sandstone Park, where you can enjoy a picnic or take a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lyons

Lyons has some of the best trails in Colorado, with over 150 miles of hiking and biking paths. Some of the most popular trails include Hall Ranch, which features over 11 miles of trails through scenic canyons, valleys, and meadows. You can also visit the Heil Valley Ranch, a 6,000-acre park with over 15 miles of trails. Another notable spot for hiking and biking is the Picture Rock Trail, which offers stunning views of the Rocky Mountains and the St. Vrain Valley.

Best Things to Do in Lyons: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Lyons. If you’re a fan of craft beer, head to the Oskar Blues Brewery, where you can take a tour and sample some of their famous brews. Another popular attraction is the Lyons Outdoor Games, which take place every June and feature a variety of outdoor activities, including kayaking, mountain biking, and trail running.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lyons

Day 1 - Explore Lyons Historic District and visit the Lyons Redstone Museum Day 2 - Hike the Hall Ranch trails and have lunch at the Lyons Farmette Day 3 - Visit the Oskar Blues Brewery and take a tour of the facility Day 4 - Spend the day at Button Rock Preserve, hiking and fishing Day 5 - Take a scenic drive on Peak to Peak Highway and stop for lunch in Allenspark Day 6 - Attend a concert at Planet Bluegrass Ranch Day 7 - Relax at Lyons Sandstone Park and enjoy a picnic

Food and Drink Scene of Lyons

Must-Try Dishes

Lyons is known for its farm-to-table cuisine and craft beer scene. Some of the must-try dishes include the grilled cheese sandwich from Lyons Dairy Bar, the roasted vegetable bowl from The Stone Cup, and the bacon-wrapped meatloaf from Oskar Blues Grill & Brew.

Local Beverages

Colorado is known for its craft beer scene, and Lyons is no exception. In addition to the Oskar Blues Brewery, you can also visit the St. Vrain Cidery, which offers a variety of hard ciders made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Lyons: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lyons offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Some of the top-rated options include the Stone Mountain Lodge & Cabins, the Aspen Leaf Motel, and the WeeCasa Tiny House Resort.

What to Buy in Lyons: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, head to the Lyons Farmette Market, where you can find fresh produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal food products.

Local Culture and Customs of Lyons

Lyons is a tight-knit community that celebrates its heritage and culture. The town hosts a variety of events and festivals throughout the year, including the Lyons Outdoor Games, Lyons Good Old Days, and the Fourth of July Parade and Fireworks.

Best Time to Visit Lyons

The best time to visit Lyons is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and the town is bustling with outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lyons

Do:

  • Visit the Lyons Redstone Museum to learn about the town’s history
  • Explore the natural beauty of Button Rock Preserve and the Rocky Mountains
  • Taste the local craft beer at the Oskar Blues Brewery
  • Attend one of Lyons’ many festivals and events

Don’t:

  • Leave without taking a scenic drive on Peak to Peak Highway
  • Miss the opportunity to hike the Hall Ranch trails
  • Skip the chance to try a local hard cider from the St. Vrain Cidery
  • Forget to pack comfortable walking shoes and layers for changing weather

Safety and Health

Safety in Lyons

Lyons is a safe and welcoming community. As with any outdoor adventure, be sure to take precautions when hiking and exploring the natural areas around Lyons. Make sure to bring plenty of water and sunscreen, and check weather conditions before embarking on any outdoor activities.

Health in Lyons

Lyons has a clinic and pharmacy for minor medical needs, and larger medical facilities are available in nearby Longmont and Boulder.

Getting to Lyons: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lyons is easily accessible by car, located just 20 minutes north of Boulder. If flying, the closest airports are Denver International Airport (DEN) and Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport (BJC).

Airports Near Lyons

  • Denver International Airport (DEN)
  • Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport (BJC)

Bus Stations Near Lyons

  • Greyhound Station in Boulder

Other Options

  • Rental cars are available at both Denver International Airport and Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport.
  • Uber and Lyft are available in Lyons and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Lyons

The best way to get around Lyons and the surrounding areas is by car. There are also plenty of biking and hiking trails for those who prefer to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Lyons: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lyons serves as a gateway to some of Colorado’s most breathtaking natural attractions, including Rocky Mountain National Park, Estes Park, and Boulder. All of these destinations offer plenty of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary experiences to enjoy.

Conclusion

Lyons is a hidden gem in Colorado, offering a perfect mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary experiences. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to plan your ultimate trip to Lyons and experience all the town has to offer.
