Introduction to Boulder

Nestled in the heart of Front Range, in Colorado, Boulder is a captivating city in the Rocky Mountains, known for its outdoor recreation, vibrant culture, and stunning vistas. This city is a melting pot of nature enthusiasts, foodies, art lovers, and adventure seekers, making it a perfect place for solo travelers, families, couples, and groups. In this comprehensive travel guide to Boulder, we’ll explore the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more. Let’s start!

List of Places to Visit in Boulder

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boulder History Museum: Learn about the city’s fascinating history and culture through interactive exhibits, artefacts, and multimedia.
  • Boulder Museum of Contemporary Art: Explore the contemporary art scene of Boulder through exhibitions, education programs, and events.
  • National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) Mesa Lab: Discover the fascinating world of atmospheric science, climate change, and environmental research through tours and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Boulder: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: Head to the nearby Rocky Mountain National Park for scenic drives, wildlife viewing, hiking, and camping.
  • Chautauqua Park: Take a hike to the iconic Flatirons, enjoy stunning views of the town, and go for a picnic at this historic park.
  • Boulder Creek Path: Walk, jog, or bike along this scenic 5.5-mile path alongside Boulder Creek, passing through parks, bridges, and shopping areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boulder

Boulder is a haven for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with its rugged mountains, lush forests, and serene lakes. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Boulder are:

  • Mount Sanitas Trail: A challenging hike with steep inclines, offering stunning views of Boulder and the surrounding mountains.
  • Marshall Mesa Trail: A 3.3-mile bike or hiking trail, passing through stunning rock formations, open meadows, and wildflowers.
  • Bear Peak Trail: A strenuous hike to the summit of Bear Peak, offering panoramic views of Boulder and the Front Range.

Best Things to Do in Boulder: Activities and Tours

  • Boulder Farmers’ Market: Experience the vibrant local culture, taste fresh produce, and find unique gifts at the Boulder Farmers’ Market.
  • Fiske Planetarium: Watch awe-inspiring shows in a state-of-the-art planetarium, showcasing the wonders of space and science.
  • Celestial Seasonings Tea Factory Tour: Go on a guided tour to learn about the tea-making process, sample a variety of teas, and shop for souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boulder

  • Day 1: Explore Downtown Boulder and its bustling Pearl Street mall, full of restaurants, cafes, shops, and street performers.
  • Day 2: Go on a scenic drive to Rocky Mountain National Park, explore its natural wonders, and go for a hike.
  • Day 3: Visit museums and historical sites in Boulder, such as Boulder History Museum and NCAR Mesa Lab.
  • Day 4: Go on a wine tour and tasting at one of the numerous wineries and vineyards in Boulder County.
  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride for stunning aerial views of Boulder and the surrounding mountains.
  • Day 6: Go on a bike tour to explore the stunning scenery and hidden gems of Boulder.
  • Day 7: Relax at one of the many parks and natural attractions in Boulder, such as Chautauqua Park or Boulder Creek Path.

Food and Drink Scene of Boulder

Boulder offers a diverse range of dining options, from casual cafes to fine-dining restaurants, and local gastronomic delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Boulder.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boulder Burgers: Try the mouth-watering burgers at The Blonding Room or The Sink, two iconic Boulder eateries.
  • Green Chile: Taste the zesty green chilli sauce at Cafe Aion or T/ACO, and experience the unique local flavor.
  • Colorado Craft Beers: Sip on some of the best craft beers in Colorado at Avery Brewing Company or BRU Handbuilt Ales.

Local Beverages

  • Boulder Bloody Mary: A classic cocktail with a twist, made with local vodka, hot sauce, and pickled vegetables, available at numerous bars and restaurants in Boulder.
  • Celestial Seasonings Tea: Savor a cup of sweet and aromatic tea at the Celestial Seasonings Tea Factory or pick up a variety of products for souvenirs.
  • Boulder Wineries: Visit the local wineries, such as Bookcliff Vineyards or Settembre Cellars, for wine tastings and vineyard tours.

Where to Stay in Boulder: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Boulder offers numerous options for accommodation, ranging from cozy guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks for each category.

  • Hotels: St Julien Hotel and Spa, Hotel Boulderado, and the Boulder Marriott offer luxurious amenities, stunning views, and prime locations in Boulder.
  • Hostels: Boulder International Hostel and Hostel Fish provide affordable and comfortable accommodations, ideal for backpackers and solo travelers.
  • Guesthouses: Briar Rose Bed and Breakfast, Alps Boulder Canyon Inn, and Bradley Boulder Inn offer unique, cozy, and welcoming accommodations for a more personalized experience.

What to Buy in Boulder: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Boulder is known for its local products, artisanal crafts, and unique souvenirs. Here are some popular items to take back home.

  • Organic Food: Buy locally grown and organic foods at the Boulder Farmers’ Market or Boulder County Farmers’ Market.
  • Handmade Crafts: Shop for handmade jewelry, pottery, and paintings at the Boulder Arts and Crafts Gallery or OpenArts Studio.
  • Outdoor Gear: Get the best outdoor equipment and apparel at Neptune Mountaineering or Outdoor Divas, two of the best outdoor gear stores in Boulder.

Local Culture and Customs of Boulder

Boulder is a progressive and cosmopolitan city, with a lively culture and customs. Here are some of the unique aspects of Boulder’s culture that you can experience.

  • Environmental Awareness: Boulder is a leader in sustainability and environmental activism, with numerous conservation efforts, eco-friendly laws, and clean energy initiatives.
  • Outdoor Recreation: Boulderites are passionate about outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, skiing, and climbing, and you’ll find plenty of opportunities to join them.
  • Local Arts Scene: Boulder boasts a thriving arts scene, with numerous galleries, theaters, and festivals, celebrating music, dance, theatre, and visual arts.

Best Time to Visit Boulder

Boulder has a temperate climate, with mild summers and chilly winters, and offers year-round outdoor activities. The best time to visit Boulder depends on your preferences and interests.

  • Summer (June-August): The summer months are peak season in Boulder, with warm weather, numerous festivals, and bustling crowds. This is the best time for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and camping.
  • Fall (September-November): The fall season offers beautiful fall foliage, cooler weather, and fewer crowds. This is a great time for scenic drives, wine tours, and cultural events.
  • Winter (December-February): The winter season brings snow and chilly weather, but also offers skiing, snowboarding, and winter sports. This is a great time for winter enthusiasts and festive events.
  • Spring (March-May): The spring season offers blooming wildflowers, milder weather, and spring skiing. This is a great time for outdoor activities and cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boulder

Do:

  • Respect the Environment: Boulder is known for its eco-friendly practices, and it’s important to respect the natural surroundings, avoid littering, and preserve the wilderness.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Boulder offers a diverse range of gastronomic delights, and it’s a great opportunity to try new cuisine and savor local flavors.
  • Explore the Outdoors: Boulder has numerous natural attractions and outdoor activities, and it’s a great chance to discover new adventures and scenic beauty.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or Vape in Public: Boulder has strict smoking laws, and it’s prohibited to smoke or vape in public places, such as parks, sidewalks, or public transportation.
  • Litter or Leave Trash: Boulder is committed to sustainability, and it’s important to dispose of trash properly, avoid littering, and recycle whenever possible.
  • Ignore Trail Regulations: Boulder has designated trail rules, such as staying on the trail, keeping dogs leashed, and respecting wildlife, and it’s important to follow them for safety and preservation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boulder

Boulder is generally a safe city, with low crime rates and well-maintained infrastructure, but it’s important to take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Bear Safety: If you’re hiking in the mountains, be aware of bears, and carry bear spray, make loud noises, and keep a safe distance.
  • Altitude Sickness: Boulder is located at a high altitude, and some visitors may experience altitude sickness, such as headaches, nausea, and fatigue. It’s important to drink plenty of water, rest, and avoid alcohol and caffeine to prevent altitude sickness.

Health in Boulder

Boulder has excellent healthcare facilities, with numerous clinics, doctors, and hospitals. It’s also a leader in health and wellness, with numerous yoga studios, fitness centers, and organic food options.

Getting to Boulder: Transportation Options and Logistics

Boulder is easily accessible via various transportation modes, and here’s how you can get here.

Airports Near Boulder

  • Denver International Airport (DEN) is the nearest major airport, located about 45 miles southeast of Boulder, and offers numerous domestic and international flights.
  • Boulder Municipal Airport (BDU) is a smaller airport, primarily used for private and charter flights, located on the eastern edge of Boulder.

Bus Stations Near Boulder

  • Boulder Transit Center is the central bus station in Boulder, offering local and regional bus services, connecting to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Driving: Boulder is easily accessible via various highways, such as I-25, US-36, and CO-119, and offers scenic drives through mountain passes, canyons, and valleys.
  • Biking: Boulder is a bike-friendly city, with numerous bike lanes, trails, and rental services, and it’s a great way to explore the town.

How to Get Around in Boulder

Boulder is a compact city, and many attractions can be accessed by foot, bike, or public transportation. Here are some transportation options in Boulder.

  • Buses: The public bus system in Boulder is called the Regional Transportation District (RTD), and offers numerous local and regional routes, with frequent services and affordable fares.
  • Bikes: Boulder has a bike-friendly culture, and offers numerous bike lanes, trails, and rental services, such as B-cycle, Zagster, and eGo CarShare, making it easy to rent a bike and explore the town.
  • Cars: Boulder has several car rental services, such as Avis, Enterprise, and Hertz, and offers numerous parking options, such as street parking, lots, and garages.

Where to Go Next from Boulder: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boulder is a great base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions, offering a range of options for day trips and weekend getaways. Here are some popular places to visit near Boulder.

  • Estes Park: A charming mountain town, located about 45 minutes away from Boulder, offering scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and wildlife viewing.
  • Denver: The capital city of Colorado, located about 30 miles southeast of Boulder, offering cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Golden: A historic mining town, located about 20 miles southwest of Boulder, offering numerous tourist attractions, such as Coors Brewery, Clear Creek Canyon, and Lookout Mountain.

Conclusion

Boulder is a unique and captivating city, offering something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts to culture lovers. With its vibrant culture, stunning natural attractions, and diverse food scene, Boulder is a must-visit destination for your bucket list. Be sure to follow our travel guide to make the most of your experience in Boulder, and create unforgettable memories. Happy travels!
