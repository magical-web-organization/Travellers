Introduction to Berthoud

Nestled in the Front Range of the Rocky Mountains, Berthoud, Colorado, is a small town with a rich history and plenty of outdoor adventures to offer. Whether you’re a history buff or an adrenaline junkie, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Berthoud

If you’re planning to visit Berthoud, here are the best places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Little Thompson Valley Pioneer Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Little Thompson Valley, with artifacts and exhibits that date back to the late 1800s.

  2. Berthoud Historic District: Walking around this district, you’ll feel like you’ve been transported back in time. It’s one of the best places to see 19th-century architecture in action.

  3. Peak to Plains Museum: Located in the nearby town of Georgetown, this museum celebrates the engineering marvels of the Georgetown Loop Railroad and the richness of the mining history in the area.

Natural Attractions of Berthoud: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Carter Lake: This large, scenic lake is ideal for boating, fishing, and camping. You can also hike around the lake, enjoy a picnic, or simply soak in the stunning views.

  2. Devil’s Backbone Open Space: This rugged 2,198-acre open space provides excellent opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding, with expansive views of the Rocky Mountains.

  3. Berthoud Pass: Known for its excellent cross-country skiing trails, Berthoud Pass is a must-visit for winter sports enthusiasts. You can also hike, camp, or picnic here in the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berthoud

Berthoud has several great hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  1. Hogback Ridge Trail: This 4.4-mile trail offers stunning views of the Front Range. It’s a moderate hike that’s perfect for families and beginners.

  2. Blue Sky Trail: A 12.5-mile trail that’s great for both hiking and biking, with fantastic views of the foothills and the Front Range.

  3. Devil’s Backbone Trail: This loop trail is 4.2 miles long with some moderate switchbacks and is an excellent place for vista points.

Best Things to Do in Berthoud: Activities and Tours

  1. Ghost tours: With its rich history, Berthoud is home to several ghost stories and paranormal hotspots. Take a ghost tour and discover these eerie tales for yourself!

  2. Brewing tours: Known for its craft beer scene, Berthoud has several breweries that offer tours and tastings.

  3. Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of the area by horseback. Many guided tours are available to take you through stunning landscapes and mountain tops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Berthoud

If you have a week to spare, here is our recommended itinerary for Berthoud:

Day 1: Check out the historic district and try some of the local cuisine.

Day 2: Visit the Pioneer Museum in the morning, then spend the afternoon hiking in the Devil’s Backbone Open Space.

Day 3: Drive up to Berthoud Pass and go cross-country skiing or snowshoeing.

Day 4: Visit Carter Lake and enjoy some boating and fishing.

Day 5: Take a ghost tour of the area at night.

Day 6: Head out for a day trip. You can visit nearby Rocky Mountain National Park or take a drive up to Estes Park.

Day 7: Go horseback riding through the stunning forests and mountains of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Berthoud

Berthoud is well known for its locally sourced food, craft beer, and wine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Rocky Mountain Oysters: This dish made from bull or bison testicles is a local favorite.

  2. Porchetta: This roasted pork dish is a must-try, with its crispy skin and juicy meat.

  3. Cinnamon Rolls: Made fresh every day by Berthoud Bakery, these cinnamon rolls are a sweet treat that you don’t want to miss.

Local Beverages

  1. Berthoud Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers, including seasonal varieties.

  2. Decadent Saint: A unique blend of wines, Decadent Saint is a must-try for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Berthoud: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Berthoud has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few of our top picks:

  1. Berthoud Inn and Events: This boutique hotel is in the heart of the historic district and offers all the modern amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

  2. Mountain Park Suites: These charming suites offer stunning mountain views and easy access to hiking and biking trails.

  3. Bent Fork Lodge & Restaurant: Enjoy a cozy stay and delicious meals at this quaint lodge.

What to Buy in Berthoud: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are a few ideas:

  1. Local honey: Berthoud is known for its honey production, so grab a jar of the local honey to take back with you.

  2. Handmade jewelry: There are several local artisans and craftsmen who create unique jewelry pieces that make great souvenirs.

  3. Yarn from The Loopy Ewe: This yarn store offers a range of high-quality yarns and fibers that make great gifts for craft lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Berthoud

Berthoud has a strong farming and ranching heritage. Farm-to-table dining is a common practice in the city, with local farmers’ markets and breweries emphasizing the farm to table food movement.

Best Time to Visit Berthoud

The best time to visit Berthoud is in the summer, from June to August, as the weather is warm and sunny, and all hiking trails are available for exploration. Winter’s snow-covered landscapes and ski slopes are what bring people to Berthoud Pass during December to the middle of March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Berthoud

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Berthoud:

Do:

  1. Try local cuisine like Porchetta and Rocky Mountain Oysters, which are unique to the area.

  2. Embrace the outdoors and enjoy the beautiful landscapes of the Rocky Mountains.

  3. Be courteous to others. Berthoud is known for its hospitality and friendliness.

Don’t:

  1. Forget your sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.

  2. Leave your trash behind. Berthoud’s nature and beauty must be protected and respected.

  3. Wear flipflops or open-toed shoes when hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Berthoud

Like any small town, Berthoud is relatively safe, with a low crime rate. However, you should still take precautions, such as locking your doors and being aware of your surroundings when walking at night.

Health in Berthoud

Berthoud doesn’t have a significant health concern or outbreaks happening, and the water supply is safe to drink or use for cooking.

Getting to Berthoud: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Berthoud

The nearest airport to Berthoud is Denver International Airport, which is approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Berthoud

There are no bus stations in Berthoud. However, some buses pass through the town as they make stops in other nearby cities.

Other Options:

Berthoud is accessible by car via Interstate 25 and US Highway 287. Both of these highways connect the town to other major cities in Colorado and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Berthoud

Berthoud is a small town and easily walkable. If you prefer to drive, there are several rental car companies available. Cycling is also a good option and there are, bike rentals near the area.

Where to Go Next from Berthoud: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning a longer trip, here are some nearby destinations and excursions you might want to consider:

  1. Rocky Mountain National Park: One of the most popular national parks in the US, Rocky Mountain National Park is just an hour’s drive from Berthoud.

  2. Estes Park: The charming mountain town of Estes Park is just a short drive away and offers plenty of outdoor activities and stunning views.

Conclusion

Berthoud is a charming town that offers a wide range of outdoor adventures, unique cuisine, and rich history and heritage. Whether you’re looking for a scenic getaway or a cultural experience, Berthoud is definitely worth a visit!
