Introduction to Longmont

Located in the Front Range of the Colorado Rocky Mountains, Longmont is a charming city known for its outdoor recreational activities, art scenes, and historical landmarks. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or a thrilling adventure, Longmont has everything to offer.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on an unforgettable journey across the city, from its must-see tourist attractions to its hidden gems. Discover the rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and vibrant food and drink scene of Longmont. Let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Longmont

Here’s an overview of the top tourist attractions in Longmont that you shouldn’t miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Longmont Museum and Cultural Center - Explore the history and culture of Longmont through interactive exhibits, programs, and events.
  • Dougherty Museum - Home to a vast collection of vintage cars, carriages, and other transportation-related artifacts.
  • St. Vrain Historical Society Museum - Located in a historic Victorian home, this museum showcases the legacy and heritage of Longmont.

Natural Attractions of Longmont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boulder Creek Path - A 5.5-mile hiking and biking trail that winds through scenic landscapes, parks, and bridges.
  • Sandstone Ranch Park - A 313-acre park with trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas that offers breathtaking mountain views.
  • Button Rock Preserve - A nature reserve that offers hiking, fishing, and camping opportunities, and stunning views of the Longs Peak and Mount Meeker.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longmont

  • Rabbit Mountain Trailhead - This trailhead offers both easy and challenging hiking trails with stunning views of the Rocky Mountains.
  • Left Hand Trail - A popular biking route that follows the path of Left Hand Creek, offering beautiful vistas and peaceful landscapes.
  • Betasso Preserve Trail - This hiking and biking trail leads through dense forests, rolling hills, and rocky outcroppings, and offers fantastic views of the surrounding wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Longmont: Activities and Tours

  • Brewery Tour - Longmont boasts of many world-class breweries that offer tours and tastings, including Left Hand Brewing Company and Oskar Blues Brewery.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - Get an exciting aerial view of Longmont’s picturesque landscapes and mountain vistas.
  • Art Walks - Longmont has a thriving art scene, with several art galleries offering exhibitions, art walks, and workshops throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Longmont

Day 1: Explore historic landmarks, including Longmont Museum and Cultural Center, Dougherty Museum, and St. Vrain Historical Society Museum.

Day 2: Hike or bike along Boulder Creek Path, followed by a picnic at Sandstone Ranch Park.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over Longmont, followed by a visit to the Boulder County Farmers Market.

Day 4: Go on a brewery tour and tasting, and enjoy a delicious meal at a local restaurant.

Day 5: Discover the natural beauty of Button Rock Preserve, followed by a river rafting adventure.

Day 6: Take an art walk and visit the galleries in downtown Longmont.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local spa or take a yoga class at a nearby studio.

Food and Drink Scene of Longmont

Longmont’s food and drink scene is diverse, with something to suit every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trout - Freshly caught trout cooked to perfection is a specialty of Longmont.
  • Craft Beer-infused Burgers - Many of Longmont’s restaurants offer burgers made with craft beer-infused patties, adding a unique twist to the classic dish.
  • Memphis-style BBQ - Longmont has a vibrant BBQ scene, with many establishments serving up Memphis-style pork ribs and brisket.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado Craft Beer - Longmont is home to many world-class breweries, including Left Hand Brewing Company, Oskar Blues Brewery, and Wibby Brewing, offering a wide selection of Colorado craft beers.
  • Wine - Longmont also has several wineries, including St. Vrain Cidery and Longs Peak Winery, offering a selection of locally sourced wines and ciders.

Where to Stay in Longmont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Longmont offers a wide range of accommodations for every budget and travel style. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Boulderado - A historic hotel that offers stylish rooms and suites, exceptional dining, and luxurious amenities.
  • Courtyard by Marriott Boulder Longmont - A modern and comfortable hotel that features a fitness center, outdoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • MyEconolodge - An affordable and comfortable hotel that offers basic amenities, including free Wi-Fi, breakfast, and parking.

What to Buy in Longmont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Longmont offers an array of unique local products and souvenirs that make perfect gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the best items to buy:

  • Craft Beer - Colorado craft beer is a must-try, and you can bring home a selection of bottles and cans from the breweries in Longmont.
  • Handmade Soaps and Candles - Many local shops offer handmade soaps and candles made with natural ingredients.
  • Local Art - Longmont’s art scene is thriving, and you can take home a piece of locally crafted art as a memento of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Longmont

Longmont is known for its friendly and welcoming culture, and visitors are expected to reciprocate the same respect and courtesy. Here are some of the customs to keep in mind:

  • Tipping - Tipping is a customary practice, and it’s recommended to tip 15-20% at restaurants and cafes.
  • Recycling - Colorado is committed to sustainable living, and visitors are expected to recycle and compost their waste.
  • Outdoor Etiquette - Longmont’s outdoor areas are cherished, and visitors are expected to follow Leave No Trace principles while hiking, biking, or camping.

Best Time to Visit Longmont

The best time to visit Longmont is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the winter months, from November to March, also offer unique experiences, including winter sports and holiday festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longmont

Do:

  • Do try the Colorado craft beer and locally sourced foods.
  • Do respect the natural landscapes and wildlife and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Do explore Longmont’s art scene and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on trails or parks.
  • Don’t approach or disturb wildlife or other animals.
  • Don’t smoke or drink in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Longmont

Longmont is a relatively safe city, but it’s always best to take precautions to ensure your personal safety. Here are a few tips:

  • Stay in well-lit areas and avoid walking alone during late hours.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.
  • Always lock your car and hotel room doors.

Health in Longmont

Longmont has several healthcare facilities, including Longmont United Hospital, that offer emergency services and medical care. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance and carry necessary medications with them.

Getting to Longmont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Longmont is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Longmont

The nearest airport to Longmont is Denver International Airport (DIA), which is approximately a 40-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Longmont

Greyhound operates a bus service to Longmont from several major cities in Colorado.

Other Options

Longmont is also accessible by train or private shuttle services. The city has a reliable local transportation system, including buses and taxis.

How to Get Around in Longmont

Longmont is a walkable city, with many attractions located within walking distance of each other. However, if you need to travel farther, you can use the local bus system, ride-share services, or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Longmont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Longmont is an ideal base for exploring the nearby towns and attractions, including:

  • Boulder - A picturesque college town known for its hiking trails, farmers market, and vibrant cultural scene.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park - One of the most popular national parks in the US, offering stunning views of the Rocky Mountains, wildlife sightings, and outdoor adventures.
  • Estes Park - A charming mountain town known for its scenic drives, outdoor activities, and rustic cabins.

Conclusion

Longmont is a hidden gem that offers endless opportunities for adventure, relaxation, and cultural exploration. From its natural wonders to its thriving art scene and delicious cuisine, Longmont has everything to satisfy any traveler’s taste. Come and experience this unique destination that you’ll never forget!
