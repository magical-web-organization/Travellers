Fort Collins is a charming city located in Front Range, Colorado, in the Rocky Mountains of the United States of America. Known for its laid-back atmosphere, vibrant culture, and unparalleled outdoor recreation opportunities, Fort Collins is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore the American West. In this comprehensive tour guide for Fort Collins, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture in this beautiful city.

Introduction to Fort Collins

Fort Collins is a city that effortlessly blends small-town charm with modern amenities. Home to Colorado State University, Fort Collins boasts a vibrant arts and culture scene, a lively nightlife, and a thriving food and drink scene. But what really sets this city apart is its location at the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, which offers unparalleled opportunities for outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Collins

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Fort Collins has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Fort Collins:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Collins Museum of Discovery: The Fort Collins Museum of Discovery is a must-visit for history lovers. Here, you’ll find interactive exhibits that tell the story of Fort Collins and the surrounding area.
  • Avery House: Built in 1879, the Avery House is a beautifully restored historic home that offers a glimpse into life in Fort Collins during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
  • The Elizabeth Hotel: A modern hotel with a nod to Fort Collins’ past, the Elizabeth Hotel is a cultural hotspot in the heart of the city. It boasts a rooftop lounge with stunning views of the mountains, live music, and art installations.

Natural Attractions of Fort Collins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horsetooth Reservoir: Horsetooth Reservoir is a popular spot for outdoor recreation, including fishing, boating, hiking, and camping.
  • Lory State Park: Lory State Park offers breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains, as well as miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails.
  • Poudre Canyon: Known for its stunning scenery and world-class whitewater rafting, Poudre Canyon is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Collins

Fort Collins is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Fort Collins:

  • Poudre River Trail: A popular biking and hiking route that follows the scenic Poudre River for 21 miles.
  • Horsetooth Mountain Open Space: This expansive open space offers more than 2,700 acres of hiking and biking trails, as well as stunning views of Horsetooth Reservoir.
  • Maxwell Natural Area: With more than 450 acres of natural beauty, Maxwell Natural Area is a must-visit for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Fort Collins: Activities and Tours

From craft brewery tours to hot air balloon rides, there’s no shortage of things to do in Fort Collins. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

  • Fort Collins Foodie Walk: This self-guided culinary tour takes you to some of Fort Collins’ best restaurants, breweries, and cafes.
  • New Belgium Brewery Tour: No visit to Fort Collins is complete without a brewery tour, and New Belgium Brewery is a must-visit. Learn about the process of brewing beer and sample some of their award-winning brews.
  • Rocky Mountain Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take your adventure to new heights with a hot air balloon ride over the stunning Rocky Mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Collins

If you’re lucky enough to have a week in Fort Collins, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Old Town Fort Collins, Fort Collins Museum of Discovery
Day 2 Hike Horsetooth Mountain Open Space, visit horse farms in Northern Colorado
Day 3 Whitewater rafting in Poudre Canyon, visit local breweries
Day 4 Tour Colorado State University campus, visit Avery House
Day 5 Bike the Poudre River Trail, visit Fort Collins Farmer’s Market
Day 6 Hot air balloon ride over Rocky Mountains, visit Lory State Park
Day 7 Explore local galleries and art venues, sunset at Horsetooth Reservoir

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Collins

Fort Collins’ food and drink scene is not to be missed. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colorado Green Chili: A spicy stew made with pork, green chilies, and other ingredients that vary by region.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: These deep-fried bull testicles are a local delicacy in some parts of the American West.
  • Oleson’s Buffalo Burgers: A delicious variation on the classic burger, made with grass-fed buffalo meat.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Fort Collins is known for its craft breweries, including New Belgium Brewery and Odell Brewing Company.
  • Colorado Wine: The surrounding area boasts several wineries, including Blue Mountain Vineyards and Ten Bears Winery.
  • Whiskey from Feisty Spirits: Feisty Spirits is a local distillery that produces hand-crafted whiskey and other spirits.

Where to Stay in Fort Collins: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort Collins offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top places to stay in Fort Collins:

  • The Armstrong Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Old Town Fort Collins, the Armstrong Hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites.
  • Fort Collins Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers, the Fort Collins Hostel offers dormitory-style rooms as well as private rooms.
  • The Remington Flats: These chic and modern guesthouses are perfect for travelers who want to feel at home in Fort Collins.

What to Buy in Fort Collins: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for a unique gift or a souvenir to remember your trip, Fort Collins has plenty of options. Here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Colorado-made craft beer: Pick up a few bottles of local craft beer to savor when you get home.
  • Poudre River Canyon artwork: Local artists have captured the beauty of Poudre River Canyon in paintings, photographs, and other artwork.
  • Fort Collins gear: Show off your love of Fort Collins with a t-shirt, hat, or other piece of swag from a local shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Collins

Fort Collins is a friendly and welcoming city with a strong sense of community. Here are some of the local customs and cultural norms to be aware of:

  • Respect for nature: The people of Fort Collins take the natural beauty of their surroundings seriously, so be sure to follow Leave No Trace principles and be respectful of the environment.
  • Bicycling culture: Fort Collins is renowned for its bicycle-friendly streets and abundant bike paths. If you’re driving, be sure to watch out for bicyclists.
  • Craft beer obsession: Craft beer is a huge part of the culture in Fort Collins, so it’s the perfect place to explore new brews and breweries.

Best Time to Visit Fort Collins

Fort Collins is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re interested in outdoor adventures, the best time to visit is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and the outdoor recreation opportunities are abundant. Fall is a great time to visit for leaf-peeping and to experience the beauty of the changing seasons, while winter is ideal for skiing and snowboarding in the nearby mountains. Spring is a great time to visit for mild weather and blooming flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Collins

Here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Fort Collins:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Fort Collins is a wonderland for outdoor enthusiasts, so be sure to take advantage of the hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities available in the area.
  • Try the craft beer: With so many breweries to choose from, it’s easy to find your new favorite brew in Fort Collins.
  • Get involved in the local community: The people of Fort Collins are friendly and welcoming, so don’t be shy about getting involved in local events and activities.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip: Tipping is customary in the United States, so be sure to leave a tip for your server or bartender.
  • Go off-trail: To protect the environment and stay safe, be sure to stick to designated trails when hiking or biking in the area.
  • Drink and drive: In the interest of safety, it’s important to plan ahead if you plan to drink. Use a designated driver or take a taxi or rideshare service instead.

Safety and Health

Fort Collins is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit to Fort Collins:

Safety in Fort Collins

  • Watch out for wildlife: Fort Collins is home to a variety of wildlife, including bears, mountain lions, and rattlesnakes. Be sure to stay on designated trails and be aware of your surroundings.
  • Lock your car and hotel room: While Fort Collins is generally a safe destination, it’s always a good idea to keep your belongings secure and lock your car and hotel room.

Health in Fort Collins

  • Stay hydrated: The high altitude and dry climate in Fort Collins can cause dehydration, so be sure to drink plenty of water and avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: The sun in Fort Collins can be intense, so be sure to wear sunscreen and protective clothing.

Getting to Fort Collins: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fort Collins is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Fort Collins

  • Denver International Airport: Located approximately 65 miles south of Fort Collins, Denver International Airport is the closest major airport.

Bus Stations Near Fort Collins

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound offers service to Fort Collins from major cities around the country.

Other Options

  • Driving: Fort Collins is easily accessible from Interstate 25, which runs north-south through the city.
  • Rideshare services: Uber and Lyft both operate in Fort Collins.

How to Get Around in Fort Collins

Fort Collins is a very walkable and bike-friendly city, but there are also a number of transportation options available. Here’s how to get around:

  • Biking: Fort Collins boasts more than 280 miles of biking trails, making it easy to explore the city on two wheels.
  • Public transportation: The Transfort bus system offers service throughout the city.
  • Rental cars: Rental cars are available at Denver International Airport.

Where to Go Next from Fort Collins: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fort Collins is conveniently located near a number of other destinations in the American West. Here are some ideas for day trips and excursions:

  • Estes Park: Located just 45 minutes away, Estes Park is a charming mountain town that serves as the gateway to Rocky Mountain National Park.
  • Cheyenne, Wyoming: Just an hour north of Fort Collins, Cheyenne is a historic Western town with a lively frontier spirit.
  • Boulder: A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, Boulder is just over an hour’s drive from Fort Collins.

Conclusion

Fort Collins is a city that truly has it all: stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and unparalleled outdoor recreation opportunities. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, you’re sure to find something to love in this charming American west destination. So what are you waiting for? Start planning your next adventure in Fort Collins today!
