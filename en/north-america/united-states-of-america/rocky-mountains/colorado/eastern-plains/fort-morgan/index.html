Introduction to Fort Morgan

Located in the Eastern Plains of Colorado, Fort Morgan is a quaint town nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains. Known for its rich Western history, breathtaking natural beauty, and welcoming community, Fort Morgan is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Colorado experience.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Morgan

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Fort Morgan. Here are some of the top attractions that you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Morgan Museum: Explore the town’s rich history through exhibits on early pioneer life, the rise of agriculture, and the impact of World War II on the community.
  • The Sherman Street National Historic District: This charming district is home to beautifully preserved examples of early 20th-century architecture, including Victorian, Gothic, and Queen Anne styles.
  • The One Room Schoolhouse: Step back in time to the days of one-room schoolhouses in this lovingly restored historic building.

Natural Attractions of Fort Morgan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pawnee Pioneer Trails Scenic and Historic Byway: Take a leisurely drive along this stunning scenic route, which winds through historic sites, rolling grasslands, and incredible vistas.
  • Jackson Lake State Park: This beautiful park offers fishing, boating, hiking, and more against the backdrop of the stunning Front Range mountains.
  • Quail Dunes Golf Course: Golf enthusiasts won’t want to miss this beautiful 18-hole course set amidst rolling hills and sweeping views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Morgan

For outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the natural beauty of Fort Morgan, there are countless hiking and biking trails to choose from, including:

  • Pawnee Buttes Trail: A challenging, 4.5-mile hike that rewards hikers with incredible views of the dramatic landscape.
  • Barbour Ponds State Park Trail: A leisurely hike or bike ride through wetlands and along the banks of the South Platte River.
  • Jackson Lake State Park Trail System: Over 20 miles of trails wind around Jackson Lake, offering hikers and bikers the chance to explore the park’s stunning landscape.

Best Things to Do in Fort Morgan: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s top attractions, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Fort Morgan. Some of the best include:

  • Ghost Tours: Take a spooky guided tour of some of Fort Morgan’s most haunted sites.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the stunning Rocky Mountains on horseback with a guided trail ride.
  • Farm Tours: Learn about the town’s rich agricultural history with a tour of local farms and ranches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Morgan

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Fort Morgan, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Fort Morgan Museum and the historic Sherman Street National District
2 Take a leisurely drive along the scenic Pawnee Pioneer Trails Byway
3 Enjoy a day of outdoor activities at Jackson Lake State Park
4 Go on a guided farm tour to learn about the town’s agricultural heritage
5 Take a spooky ghost tour of some of the town’s most haunted sites
6 Play a round of golf at Quail Dunes Golf Course
7 Spend the day hiking the challenging Pawnee Buttes Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Morgan

No trip to Fort Morgan is complete without sampling the town’s delicious and unique cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: This iconic dish is made from deep-fried bull testicles and is a must-try for adventurous eaters.
  • Green chili: A spicy, flavorful stew made with locally-grown green chilies.
  • Bison burger: Try a delicious and lean bison burger, a classic Colorado dish.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado craft beer: Savor a locally brewed craft beer at one of Fort Morgan’s many breweries.
  • Palisade Peach Wine: Made with Colorado-grown peaches, this wine is sweet, fruity, and perfect for sipping on a warm summer day.

Where to Stay in Fort Morgan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for cozy and affordable lodging or a luxurious hotel experience, Fort Morgan has options to suit every traveler’s needs. Here are some of the best places to stay in town:

  • Comfort Inn Fort Morgan: This comfortable hotel offers spacious rooms, an indoor pool and hot tub, and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites: The Holiday Inn offers all the amenities you need for a comfortable and relaxing stay, including an indoor pool, fitness center, and business center.
  • Rodeway Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Fort Morgan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a bit of Fort Morgan home with you with these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Jerky: Fort Morgan is famous for its delicious and high-quality beef jerky, a perfect snack for your journey home.
  • Western Wear: Pick up a stylish cowboy hat or pair of boots at one of the town’s many Western wear stores.
  • Colorado-made crafts: Local artisans craft beautiful jewelry, pottery, and other handcrafted items that make the perfect gift for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Morgan

Fort Morgan is a welcoming and friendly town with a rich Western culture that celebrates its agricultural heritage and love of the outdoors. Visitors should be prepared for the town’s laid-back and relaxed atmosphere and take the time to appreciate the town’s unique traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Fort Morgan

The best time to visit Fort Morgan depends on your interests and preferences. Summer is the most popular time of year due to its warm weather and outdoor activities, while fall is a great time to see the stunning foliage and enjoy seasonal events like Oktoberfest. Winter is also a beautiful time to visit Fort Morgan, with snow-covered mountains and cozy holiday festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Morgan

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the town’s many hiking and biking trails.
  • Learn about the town’s rich history and agricultural heritage at the Fort Morgan Museum.
  • Try the unique and delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack warm and comfortable clothes if you’re visiting in the winter months.
  • Miss the chance to explore the nearby Pawnee National Grassland.
  • Leave trash or litter in the town’s gorgeous parks and nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Morgan

Fort Morgan is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking car doors and avoiding isolated or unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Fort Morgan

Visitors should be prepared for Colorado’s high altitude by drinking plenty of water and taking it easy for the first few days of their trip. It’s also a good idea to pack sunscreen and a hat to protect against the sun.

Getting to Fort Morgan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Morgan

The closest major airport to Fort Morgan is Denver International Airport (DEN), which is located approximately 80 miles to the southwest of the town.

Bus Stations Near Fort Morgan

Greyhound operates a bus station in Fort Morgan, making it easy to reach the town via bus from other nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Fort Morgan by car via Interstate 76 and US Route 34.

How to Get Around in Fort Morgan

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Fort Morgan, but visitors can also utilize ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft, or opt for a taxi or public transit.

Where to Go Next from Fort Morgan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to extend your trip beyond Fort Morgan, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, including:

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: A stunning national park located just a few hours’ drive from Fort Morgan.
  • Boulder, Colorado: A lively college town known for its outdoor activities, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty.
  • Estes Park: A charming mountain town known for its quaint shops, restaurants, and breathtaking scenery.

Conclusion

Fort Morgan is a hidden gem in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, offering visitors a chance to experience Colorado’s unique Western culture and natural beauty. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning outdoor landscapes, Fort Morgan is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Colorado experience.
