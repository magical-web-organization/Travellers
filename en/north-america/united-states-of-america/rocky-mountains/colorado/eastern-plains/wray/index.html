Wray, nestled in the Eastern Plains of Colorado, is a small town that offers a big experience. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and charming culture, it is a hidden gem that deserves attention from all types of visitors. If you are planning to explore this town anytime soon, here is a comprehensive tour guide that will help you make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Wray

Wray is a historic town located in the Yuma County of Colorado. Founded in 1874 by Rufus W. Wright, it was named after him in honor of his efforts in the development of the town. The town is well-known for its tranquil environment, impressive Victorian architecture, and a wide range of exciting outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Wray

Regardless of your interests, Wray is home to many attractions that will make your trip worthwhile. Here is a list of some of the must-see places to visit in Wray:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wray Museum: Dive into the history of Wray by exploring one of the oldest buildings in town. The museum has a wide display of the town’s famous structures, antique farm equipment, and an extensive collection of photographs, among others.
  • Carnegie Library: A beautiful historic landmark, the Carnegie Library, was constructed in 1913, and its Beaux-Arts style is something to marvel at. The venue is a hub of community gatherings and events.
  • Yuma County Pony Express: The Yuma County Pony Express is a tribute to the brave riders who delivered mail during the mid-1800s. The site has a replica of the Pony Express station, and you can also get your hands on collectibles.

Natural Attractions of Wray: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wray Fish Hatchery: The Wray Fish Hatchery is a delightful place to stroll and is an excellent location for those who love fishing. Get a taste of Wray’s aquatic population by gathering all the information or watching the fish in their natural habitat.
  • Wray State Fishing Reservoir: A peaceful getaway, the Reservoir offers a range of outdoor activities such as fishing, boating, hiking, and picnicking. If you’re feeling adventurous, fishing licenses can be bought at the entrance for a small fee.
  • North Sterling State Park: Join the locals in the fun at North Sterling State Park. This impressive recreational location is especially great for photographers and hikers alike, with beautiful rocky trails, point-of-view lookout spots, and multiple campgrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wray

If outdoor challenges are your thing, then Wray won’t disappoint. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes of Wray for visitors:

  • Pawnee Pioneer Trail Scenic and Historic Byway: The Pawnee Pioneer Trail Scenic Byway is perfect for those who want to explore the old world charm of Wray. The route is a treat for nature enthusiasts, passing through grassy fields, historic ranches, and panoramic views of the sand dunes.
  • North Sterling State Park: The North Sterling State Park is the place to go for an outdoor challenge. Take on a hike on one of the rocky Mary’s Cove trails, bike ride the South Shore trails and enjoy kayaking on the lake. Peaceful and picturesque.

Best Things to Do in Wray: Activities and Tours

Wray has no shortage of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Wray:

  • Wray Daze: This festival is an absolute winner for those who love good music, good food, and good vibes. Held annually in August, Wray Daze is a family-friendly, fun-filled event with a wide range of outdoor entertainment and live acts.
  • Wagon Wheel Ridge: A local favorite, Wagon Wheel Ridge, offers exciting tours that will show you the ins and outs of farming, ranching, and Western lifestyle. You can learn from guides about cattle, modern farming, and opportunities to buy gifts.
  • Pawnee National Grasslands: For some grand landscape viewing, the Pawnee National Grasslands has miles of grating land that offer the ultimate perspectives. Take your pick and explore all the diverse areas, from sagebrush flats to high buttes.

Food and Drink Scene of Wray

Wray is the place to be when it comes to classic Western dining. It boasts an assortment of culinary treats and local delicacies that will delight even the pickiest of foodies.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Brisket: Juicy and tender, the Smoked Brisket is one to write home about. It’s served with BBQ sauce and is a perfect meal to add to the picnic basket while exploring the scenic land around Wray.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A Wray classic, and suggested to order with a side of courage, Rocky Mountain Oysters are fried bull testicles that are believed to have been harvested in this region since 1880. They are served with cocktail sauce and mustard sauce.
  • Bison Burger with green chili: Want to satisfy your tummy with something delicious and healthy? Try the Bison Burger, served with freshly roasted green chili and American cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Wray Soda Fountain: A classic spot that serves homemade sodas, milkshakes, and signature root beer floats.
  • Colorado Craft Beer: Colorado boasts an impressive craft beer scene and local breweries. Local favorites are Prost Brewing, Great Divide Brewing Company, and Bierstadt Lagerhaus.

Where to Stay in Wray: Hotels, Hostels, and Guest Houses

Wray has a selection of accommodations that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Cobblestone Hotel & Suites: An excellent option for families, couples are those who are after modern amenities and an indoor pool and gym center.
  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham Wray: Spacious, clean rooms coupled with friendly staff, it is an excellent option for those traveling on a budget, especially resting before or after some time at Stanley Reservoir.
  • Wray Guest House and Suite: A modern, newly renovated suite that is perfect for couples or solo travelers who want a comfortable and quiet stay, with a relaxing garden outside.

What to Buy in Wray: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a piece of Wray back home with you, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Western Art: Wray’s rich history and western culture are evident in its art, so be sure to bring home some classic pieces from local artists.
  • Organic Produce: Wray is well-known for its organic produce, particularly the Yuma County Produce made from farm-fresh potato chips, and the bees honey.
  • Local Craft: Souvenir shops sell local crafts such as leather products and intricate carvings made from wood or bones, perfect to bring back as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Wray

Wray is a small town, but the locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. The area is known for its appreciation of Western culture and outdoor sports, and they celebrate a range of festivals such as Wray Daze and the Yuma County Fair. When visiting, it’s best to show respect for the local customs and remember to say, “Hello,” to passerby-even if they’re strangers.

Best Time to Visit Wray

Wray is a year-round destination, with each season offering different attractions to visitors. Summer is ideal for outdoor adventure activities, especially in early August, where the weather is mild, and festivals bloom in the fairground and streets. Meanwhile, in the winter, visitors can enjoy snowmobile trails in North Sterling State Park and winter sports in nearby ski resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wray

Do:

  • Do Respect the western lifestyle and culture.
  • When hiking or exploring trails, please bring necessary safety gear, water, snacks and avoid leaving litter.
  • Do visit local shops, artists, and farm-related businesses.
  • Do ask the locals for advice on things to explore or places to go if you are a first-time visitor.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, especially around the natural attractions of the town.
  • Don’t assume you’re familiar with Western lifestyle stereotypes. Learn how to properly handle farm equipment, animals or anything unfamiliar.
  • Don’t engage in behaviors that are illegal or disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wray

Wray is a peaceful town with low crime rates and welcoming locals. Visitors should take general safety precautions, however, regarding driving safety, staying hydrated and following trail guidelines.

Health in Wray

Wray is home to the Wray Community Hospital for healthcare needs and well-trained EMTs available by calling 911. It is suggested to have medical aid insurance and a first aid kit for any outdoor activities.

Wray is a small town that is easy to navigate by foot or by bike. It is suggested to have a car or rental when visiting out of town. While taxis are available, they are sporadic and expensive compared to neighboring cities.

Where to Go Next from Wray: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those who have the time, here are some excellent options for further exploration:

  • Denver: The capital of Colorado, Denver, is a popular destination that’s home to an excellent food and drink scene, sports arenas, a thriving culture and art scene, and the famous Red Rocks Amphitheatre.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park: For a once in a lifetime national park experience, head out to Rocky Mountain National Park. With mountain views, wildlife and forest hikes, it’s worth the drive.
  • Fort Collins: Fort Collins boasts beautiful Victorian architecture, a vibrant brewery and culture scene, and plenty of indoor and outdoor recreation options.

Conclusion

Wray is a town that has it all, from stunning natural beauty to rich history and welcoming culture. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be able to enjoy the best of what Wray has to offer. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, or simply a getaway from the busy city life, Wray is an excellent choice that offers something for everyone. Start packing your bags and get ready to make unforgettable memories in this charming, rustic part of Colorado!
