Introduction to Strasburg

If you’re looking for a peaceful retreat amidst the Rocky Mountains, Strasburg is your answer. Strasburg is a small countryside town in Eastern Plains, located in the state of Colorado in the United States of America. A historic town, it has plenty of options for tourists to choose from, including natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you plan your perfect Strasburg trip.

List of Places to Visit in Strasburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Comanche Crossing Museum is a must-visit. The museum houses artifacts and exhibits related to the pioneers, settlers, and cowboy culture of the area. The Strasburg History Center is another great attraction, showcasing the town’s history through photos, documents, and other memorabilia. The Strasburg Train Depot is also a popular spot, where visitors can learn about the town’s railroad history and see actual train cars.

Natural Attractions of Strasburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Paint Mines Interpretive Park is a unique attraction near Strasburg. The park is famous for its colorful rock formations, stunning vistas, and unique geological formations. The Comanche Grasslands is another popular attraction, covering nearly 440,000 acres with plenty of opportunities for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching. The Cherry Creek State Park is located nearby and offers opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strasburg

The Comanche Grasslands offer several trails for hiking and mountain biking. Some of the popular trails include the Picket Wire Canyonlands Trail, the Dinosaur Tracks Trail, and the Vogel Canyon Trail. The Paint Mines Interpretive Park also has several moderate hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Strasburg: Activities and Tours

Adventure lovers can go horseback riding, hunting, fishing, or rock climbing in the nearby parks and forests. The Colorado Skydiving Center offers a unique adrenaline packed experience. For a leisurely activity, we recommend taking a hot air balloon ride over the picturesque landscapes of Strasburg. Additionally, the Mile High Farms has a variety of activities, including corn mazes, hayrides, pumpkin patches, and animal petting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strasburg

Day 1: Arrive and explore Downtown Strasburg Day 2: Visit the Comanche Crossing Museum and Strasburg Train Depot Day 3: Take a day trip to Cherry Creek State Park Day 4: Visit the Paint Mines Interpretive Park Day 5: Horseback riding in the Comanche Grasslands Day 6: Hot air balloon ride with Colorado Hot Air Balloon Rides LLC. Day 7: Go shopping and try local cuisine in Downtown Strasburg

Food and Drink Scene of Strasburg

Must-Try Dishes

Strasburg is known for its steakhouses, so a visit to the Black Steer Steakhouse is a must. The restaurant has been serving delicious steaks and burgers since 1966 and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike. Another must-visit place is the The Happy Cooker Cafe, serving hearty home-cooked meals.

Local Beverages

The beers at Comrade Brewing Company are a must-try for beer enthusiasts. The brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are brewed on-site. For wine lovers, the Aurora Cellars Winery has some amazing wines to offer such as Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Chardonnay.

Where to Stay in Strasburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For a comfortable and affordable stay, we recommend staying at the Best Western Plus Deer Park Hotel & Suites or the Super 8, which are both located near the downtown area. For a more luxurious experience, the Cherry Creek Lodge Bed and Breakfast has excellent amenities paired with stunning views.

What to Buy in Strasburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Products made from the region’s agricultural produce, like honey from local farms, are popular souvenirs. Strasburg’s antique shops, like 4 Seasons Antique Mall, have unique treasures to take home as well.

Local Culture and Customs of Strasburg

Strasburg has a rich history, and as such, the people of Strasburg are immensely proud of where they came. Many events throughout the year are about celebrating that history and less about commercialized celebration. It’s essential to respect the small town’s way of life.

Best Time to Visit Strasburg

The best time to visit Strasburg is during late spring, summer, and early fall when the weather is warm and allows for outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strasburg

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Be friendly and polite
  • Rent a car or come prepared to drive

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in scenic areas
  • Leave gates open or enter private property without permission
  • Underestimate the rural weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Strasburg

Strasburg is a safe town, and crime is low. However, it is still essential to be careful while venturing outside after dark and to lock your vehicle at all times.

Health in Strasburg

For immediate medical assistance, head to the Strasburg Family Health Center. Hospitals like the UCHealth Emergency Room are located within a 10-mile radius and are equipped to provide any medical assistance needed.

Getting to Strasburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Strasburg

The Denver International Airport is the most convenient airport to reach Strasburg from, located roughly 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Strasburg

There are no bus stations in the immediate vicinity. The best option is Greyhound bus service, which runs from the Denver bus station.

Other Options

It’s highly recommended to rent a car to get around the area.

How to Get Around in Strasburg

Renting a car is the most feasible and recommended option for getting around and exploring the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Strasburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Denver is the nearest major city, located about 30 miles away from Strasburg. Other nearby destinations include the Garden of Gods Park, Pikes Peak, and the Royal Gorge Bridge.

Conclusion

Strasburg, Colorado, offers a unique small-town experience that is charming and peaceful. With plenty of natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities, you’ll have an unforgettable trip. Be sure to take in the surrounding Rocky Mountains’ picturesque beauty, and enjoy the great company of the town’s friendly residents.
