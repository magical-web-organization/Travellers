Introduction to Eastern Plains

Located in the Rocky Mountains region of Colorado, Eastern Plains is a vast expanse of land that stretches across 60,000 square miles and is home to over 1 million people. Known for its ranches, grasslands, and agricultural production, Eastern Plains offers a unique travel experience that combines outdoor adventure with history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Plains

If you’re planning a trip to Eastern Plains, make sure to add the following places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Fort Morgan Museum: Learn about the history of Morgan County, Colorado, and the role it played in the settlement of the western frontier.
  • Limon Heritage Museum: Explore the rich history of the Limon area through artifacts, exhibits, and photographs.
  • Bent’s Old Fort National Historic Site: Discover what life was like at a 19th-century trading post and military garrison.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Plains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pawnee National Grassland: Hike, bird watch, or picnic in one of the largest prairie grasslands in North America.
  • Comanche National Grassland: Explore the rolling hills, canyons, and rock formations in one of Colorado’s most spectacular landscapes.
  • Sand Creek Massacre National Historic Site: Pay respects to the Native Americans who were tragically killed at this site in 1864.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Plains

Eastern Plains offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that cater to all experience levels. Some of the most popular include:

  • The Bluff Trail: A 4.7-mile looped trail that offers scenic views of the South Platte River.
  • Barr Lake Trail: A 9.2-mile looped trail that takes you through wetlands, wildlife habitats, and fishing areas.
  • Roxborough State Park: A network of trails that take you through scenic red rock formations and stunning vistas.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Plains: Activities and Tours

Looking for some exciting activities and tours to add to your Eastern Plains itinerary? Here are some ideas:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: See Eastern Plains from a bird’s eye view with a hot air balloon ride - an unforgettable experience!
  • Horseback Riding: Get a taste of the cowboy lifestyle by going on a horseback ride through the Colorado plains.
  • Tasting Tours: Sample local beers, wines, and foods with a tasting tour that takes you to some of the best breweries, vineyards, and restaurants in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Plains

Day 1: Visit the Fort Morgan Museum and take a leisurely stroll around Riverside Park. Day 2: Go hiking on the Bluff Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch at Chatfield State Park. Day 3: Explore Limon Heritage Museum and head over to Kiowa Creek Sporting Club for some clay shooting. Day 4: Go on a hot air balloon ride and have a hearty breakfast at Margie’s Java Joint. Day 5: Visit Bent’s Old Fort National Historic Site and take a scenic drive through Comanche National Grassland. Day 6: Do some bird watching at Barr Lake State Park and take a horseback riding tour. Day 7: Go on a tasting tour and sample the best local beers, wines, and foods.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Plains

Eastern Plains is home to some of the best local foods and drinks in Colorado. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: Although not an oyster, this local delicacy is a deep-fried bull calf testicle.
  • Bison Burger: Try a juicy bison burger, a healthier and leaner option than beef.
  • Green Chili: A regional specialty, green chili is a spicy stew made with green chilies, tomatoes, and pork.

Local Beverages

  • Coors Banquet Beer: This beer is brewed at the Coors Brewery in Golden, Colorado, and has been a Colorado favorite since 1873.
  • Colorado Wine: The state has over 100 wineries and vineyards, so be sure to try some local favorites.
  • Whiskey: Eastern Plains also produces some of the best and most unique whiskeys in Colorado.

Where to Stay in Eastern Plains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a cozy bed and breakfast, Eastern Plains has plenty of accommodation options to suit your needs and budget. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • Best Western Plus: A modern hotel with excellent amenities, including free breakfast and an indoor pool.
  • The Ranch House: A charming bed and breakfast that is perfect for couples or families.
  • Comanche Grasslands Guest Ranch: A working cattle ranch that offers guests a chance to experience a real cowboy lifestyle.

What to Buy in Eastern Plains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some unique souvenirs and local products to take back home? Here are some ideas:

  • Bison Jerky: Take home some Colorado bison jerky, a tasty and healthy snack.
  • Local Honey: Eastern Plains is home to some of the best honey in Colorado.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans make beautiful jewelry using natural stones and materials found in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Plains

Eastern Plains has a strong agricultural and ranching culture, which is reflected in its food, music, and festivals. Here are some customs and traditions you may encounter during your visit:

  • Cattle Drives: Watch real cowboys round up and move large herds of cattle from one place to another.
  • Rodeos: Attend one of the many rodeos held throughout the year for a taste of the cowboy lifestyle.
  • Fairs and Festivals: From the Limon Heritage Festival to the Morgan County Fair, Eastern Plains has a variety of festivals that celebrate its culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Plains

The best time to visit Eastern Plains is from May to October when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall months, in particular, offer spectacular foliage and colors, making it an excellent time for photographers and nature lovers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Plains

Do:

  • Take time to appreciate the natural beauty of the area.
  • Embrace the local culture and customs.
  • Try local foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the local ranching and agricultural heritage.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Plains

While Eastern Plains is generally a safe place to visit, it’s always important to take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities. Make sure to bring appropriate gear, such as sturdy shoes and sun protection, and follow all safety guidelines when hiking, biking, or horseback riding. Be aware of wildlife and respect their space.

Health in Eastern Plains

Hikers and outdoor enthusiasts should be aware of altitude sickness, which can occur when traveling to higher elevations. Make sure to stay hydrated and take your time when adjusting to the altitude. It’s also essential to protect yourself from the sun by wearing sunscreen and staying in the shade during the hottest parts of the day.

Getting to Eastern Plains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Plains

  • Denver International Airport: The closest major airport to Eastern Plains, located about 85 miles west of Fort Morgan.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Plains

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Limon, Colorado, and serves several destinations throughout the state.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Eastern Plains. There are also several shuttle services available that can take you to and from Denver International Airport.

How to Get Around in Eastern Plains

The best way to get around Eastern Plains is by car or bicycle. There are several rental car companies in the area, and many of the popular attractions are easily accessible by car. Bicycles are also a great way to explore the area, with plenty of bike-friendly routes and trails available.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Plains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Colorado, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: Located about 160 miles west of Eastern Plains, this national park offers some of the most spectacular scenery in the world.
  • Denver: Colorado’s capital city is only a short drive away and offers a variety of museums, restaurants, and attractions.
  • Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park: Located about 250 miles southwest of Eastern Plains, this national park boasts some of the steepest and most dramatic cliffs in North America.

Conclusion

Eastern Plains is a unique and beautiful destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to explore the region’s rich history, indulge in local foods and drinks, or simply enjoy the great outdoors, Eastern Plains has it all. Plan your trip today and experience the best of what Colorado has to offer.
