Introduction to La Junta

Located in Eastern Plains, in Colorado, in the Rocky Mountains, United States of America, La Junta is a hidden gem that offers adventure, history, and natural beauty. The small town was established in the 19th century as a trading post for Native American tribes and later became a stop along the Santa Fe Trail. Today, La Junta is a popular destination for those seeking to explore the great outdoors, learn about the region’s history, and experience the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in La Junta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Koshare Indian Museum and Kiva: This museum houses one of the largest collections of Native American art and artifacts in the United States. Visitors can also witness performances by the Koshare Indian Dancers, a group of young men who have been performing traditional dances since the 1930s.
  • Picketwire Canyonlands: This area features over 1,000 dinosaur tracks, as well as ruins of ancient civilizations.
  • Otero Museum: Features exhibits on the history of La Junta and the surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of La Junta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Hasty: A popular spot for boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Bent’s Old Fort National Historic Site: An authentic adobe trading post that was significant in the days of the Santa Fe Trail.
  • Comanche National Grassland: A vast expanse of wildflowers, grasses, and prairie dogs that extends far into the horizon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Junta

  • Picketwire Canyonlands: With over 5 miles of trails, visitors can hike or bike through the canyon and see dinosaur tracks and ancient ruins.
  • Comanche National Grassland: With 400,000 acres of hiking and biking trails, visitors can explore the beauty of the Great Plains.

Best Things to Do in La Junta: Activities and Tours

  • Otero Junior College Rattlers Athletics: The local college offers a variety of sports to watch.
  • Tri-State/Denver Buddhist Temple: Offers meditation sessions and guided tours.
  • Historic Arkansas Riverwalk of Pueblo: A nearby destination for those who want to check out what Pueblo, Colorado has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of La Junta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile: A local staple, found in everything from burritos to burgers.
  • Rocky Ford Melons: Known for their sweetness and juiciness, these melons are a must-try during your visit.

Local Beverages

  • La Junta Lager: Brewed locally, this beer is a favorite among visitors and locals alike.
  • Sangria: A refreshing beverage made with fresh fruit and wine.

Where to Stay in La Junta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn La Junta: Conveniently located near local attractions, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a fitness center.
  • Red Lion Hotel La Junta: Offers spacious rooms and an indoor pool.
  • Travel Inn of La Junta: Ideal for budget travelers, this motel offers simple rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in La Junta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Art: Koshare Indian Museum and Kiva Gift Shop offers jewelry, pottery, and other traditional items for purchase.
  • Local Honey: Area beekeepers offer jars of honey made from nearby wildflowers.

Local Culture and Customs of La Junta

  • The history of the Native American tribes that once called La Junta home is an important part of the local culture.
  • The annual Santa Fe Trail Day celebration in June is a popular event in La Junta.

Best Time to Visit La Junta

The best time to visit La Junta is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer months can be hot and dry, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Junta

Do:

  • Respect the local Native American culture and history.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities available in the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or disturb the natural beauty of the area.
  • Approach or disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Junta

La Junta is generally a safe town, but as with any place, visitors should take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

Health in La Junta

La Junta has a hospital and other medical facilities to accommodate any health needs visitors may have.

Getting to La Junta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Junta

  • Pueblo Memorial Airport: 50 miles away from La Junta.
  • Colorado Springs Airport: 100 miles away from La Junta.

Bus Stations Near La Junta

  • Greyhound: Located in La Junta, offers intercity bus service.

Other Options

  • Rental cars are available at both Pueblo Memorial Airport and Colorado Springs Airport.

How to Get Around in La Junta

La Junta is a small town and is easily navigable by car. Hiking or biking is also an option for those who want to explore the outdoors.

Where to Go Next from La Junta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pueblo, Colorado: 50 miles away, known for its riverwalk, breweries, and historic sites.
  • Colorado Springs, Colorado: 100 miles away, home to attractions like the Garden of the Gods and Pikes Peak.
  • Great Sand Dunes National Park: 150 miles away, featuring towering dunes that visitors can climb and explore.

Conclusion

La Junta is a hidden gem with a rich history and beautiful natural surroundings. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical sites, there is something for everyone to enjoy in this small town in the Rocky Mountains. Plan your trip now and experience all that La Junta has to offer.
