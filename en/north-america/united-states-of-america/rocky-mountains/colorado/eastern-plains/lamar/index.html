Introduction to Lamar

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Plains in Colorado, Lamar is a popular destination for outdoor adventurers and history enthusiasts alike. Known for its rich cultural heritage, excellent hiking and biking trails, and scenic beauty, Lamar offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and adventure for all visitors.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit places in Lamar, best things to do, where to eat and stay, and what to buy as souvenirs.

List of Places to Visit in Lamar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lamar is a hub for history lovers and culture enthusiasts. Here are some of the must-visit places that give a glimpse of the city’s rich heritage:

  • Big Timbers Museum: Located in the heart of Lamar, this museum showcases the history of Lamar and its surrounding areas. It boasts a wide range of exhibits, including the Santa Fe Trail exhibit, Kiva exhibit, and more.
  • Lamar Welcome Center: Learn about Lamar’s history and culture through interactive exhibits and information about local attractions at the Lamar Welcome Center.
  • Bent’s Old Fort National Historic Site: Located just a short drive from Lamar, this fort provides an insight into the history of the region during the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Lamar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lamar is a nature lover’s paradise, known for its stunning outdoor landscapes and adventurous activities. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions of Lamar:

  • John Martin Reservoir State Park: Located on the Arkansas River, John Martin Reservoir State Park provides numerous outdoor recreational opportunities, such as fishing, boating, camping, hiking, and much more.
  • Two Buttes Reservoir: A popular destination for fishing, boating, and camping, Two Buttes Reservoir is a great place to unwind and enjoy the serenity of nature.
  • Sand Creek Massacre National Historic Site: An important destination for Native American history, Sand Creek Massacre Site is located just a few miles from Lamar. Walk the peaceful trail, view the exhibits and learn more about the country’s history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lamar

Lamar boasts a plethora of nature trails for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Lamar Loop Trail: A perfect trail for beginners, this loop trail offers a distance of approximately 3.5 miles and encompasses some of the best scenic views of the area.
  • Willow Creek Trail: If you are looking for a more challenging hike, this 5-mile round trip trail presents stunning vistas and diverse wildlife.
  • Sand Creek Greenway Trail: Running along Sand Creek, this trail offers a unique opportunity to witness birdlife and natural habitats.

Best Things to Do in Lamar: Activities and Tours

From outdoor activities to cultural experiences, there are plenty of exciting things to do in Lamar:

  • Take a tour of Bent’s Old Fort National Historic Site
  • Attend the Sand & Sage Fair and Rodeo
  • Enjoy fishing and boating at John Martin Reservoir State Park
  • Visit the Lamar Farmers Market for fresh local produce
  • Catch a movie or a live performance at the Lamar Theater

Local Culture and Customs of Lamar

Lamar is known for its Western cowboy culture, and visitors can experience that in the various festivals and events that take place throughout the year. One of the most anticipated events is the Sand & Sage Fair and Rodeo, which celebrates country lifestyle with live music performances, rodeo competitions, and much more.

Best Time to Visit Lamar

The best time to visit Lamar is from May to October, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are plentiful. July and August are peak months, so expect higher crowds and prices during that time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lamar

Lamar is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should exercise caution when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. It is also advisable to keep valuables secure and out of sight.

Health in Lamar

Visitors should be mindful of the high altitude and the possibility of altitude sickness. Travelers should drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and stay hydrated to prevent any health issues.
