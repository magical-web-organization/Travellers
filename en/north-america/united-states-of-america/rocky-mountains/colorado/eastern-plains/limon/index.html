Introduction to Limon

Limon is a small agricultural town located in Eastern Plains, Colorado. It is situated in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, offering stunning scenic views and a wide range of outdoor activities.

Visitors to Limon can experience a rich cultural heritage that is steeped in the history of the American West. There are plenty of museums and historical sites to visit, as well as natural attractions for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and mountain biking to birdwatching and fishing, there is something for everyone in Limon.

This guide will provide you with a comprehensive list of places to visit and things to do in Limon. From cultural and historical attractions to outdoor adventures and scenic views, this town has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Limon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Limon Heritage Museum: This museum chronicles the history of Limon and its surrounding areas. It features artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the story of the town’s early settlers, its agricultural heritage, and its role in the development of the American West.

  • Tommyknocker Playhouse: This theater company offers live performances throughout the year, including plays, musicals, and variety shows.

  • Limon Railroad Park: This outdoor museum features a collection of historic railroad equipment, including an authentic steam locomotive. Visitors can take a ride on the train and learn about the town’s railroad heritage.

Natural Attractions of Limon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Limon Wetlands Preserve: This wildlife sanctuary is a popular spot for birdwatching and hiking. Visitors can view a variety of bird species, as well as other wildlife such as deer, coyotes, and rabbits.

  • Prairie Ridge Trail System: This extensive network of hiking and mountain biking trails offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and plains. The trails are suitable for all skill levels, and visitors can choose from short, easy walks to strenuous day hikes.

  • Limon Reservoir: This scenic lake is popular for fishing and boating. It is stocked with a variety of fish species, including trout, bass, and catfish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Limon

  • Prairie Ridge Trail System: This network of trails offers a variety of hiking and biking options, ranging from easy walks to challenging mountain climbs. The trails are well-maintained and marked, and visitors can take in stunning views of the surrounding mountains and plains.

  • Limon Heritage Trail: This trail traces the history of Limon and its surrounding areas. It features interpretive signs that describe the town’s agricultural heritage, its role in the development of the American West, and its natural history.

Best Things to Do in Limon: Activities and Tours

  • Limon Heritage Day Festival: This annual festival celebrates the history and culture of Limon. It features live music, food vendors, arts and crafts, and historical reenactments.

  • Limon Railroad Days: This annual event honors the town’s railroad heritage. It features train rides, historical reenactments, and live music.

  • Scenic Drives: The surrounding area of Limon is home to some of the most beautiful scenic drives in Colorado. Visitors can enjoy winding roads, vibrant fall colors, and stunning mountain vistas.

Food and Drink Scene of Limon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile Cheeseburger: This classic Colorado dish features a juicy beef patty topped with melted cheese and spicy green chile sauce.

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A popular local delicacy, Rocky Mountain Oysters are actually bull testicles that are breaded and fried.

  • Colorado Craft Beer: With a growing number of craft breweries in the state, Colorado is quickly becoming a hub for beer lovers. Some of the popular breweries in Limon include The Goose and Wally’s Pub and Grill.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado Wine: The state’s wine industry may be small compared to some others but it produces some outstanding product. There are a number of wineries within a reasonable distance of Limon.

  • Local Brews: Colorado is famous for its craft beer and has the second-largest number of breweries per capita of any state.

What to Buy in Limon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Limon

Limon has a strong agricultural heritage, and visitors can experience the town’s farming culture by attending local events such as the Limon Farmers’ Market.

The town is also home to a significant railway history, and visitors can learn about the town’s role in the development of the American West through its railroads.

Best Time to Visit Limon

The best time to visit Limon is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the surrounding landscape is vibrant with colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Limon

Do:

  • Do dress in layers as the weather can change quickly and dramatically.

  • Do bring sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.

  • Do respect the natural environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or trespass on private property.

  • Don’t approach wildlife, especially bears and mountain lions.

  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions or the potential for sudden changes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Limon

Limon is a small and safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take normal precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Limon

There are no major health concerns in Limon, but visitors should be aware of the high altitude and adjust accordingly to avoid altitude sickness. It is also recommended to bring insect repellent, as there is a risk of tick-borne diseases in the area.

Getting to Limon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Limon

  • Denver International Airport: Located 77 miles from Limon, this is the closest major airport.

  • Colorado Springs Airport: Located 87 miles from Limon, this is another option for visitors flying into the area.

Bus Stations Near Limon

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus line offers services from Limon to Denver and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from Denver International Airport to explore Limon and the surrounding areas.

  • Taxi and Shuttle Services: There are several taxi and shuttle services available in Limon for local transportation.

How to Get Around in Limon

The best way to get around in Limon is by car. However, the town is also bike-friendly, with many bike lanes and trails throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Limon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Denver: The state’s capital city is located 75 miles west of Limon and offers a wide range of cultural and historical attractions, shopping, dining, and nightlife.

  • Colorado Springs: Located 86 miles south of Limon, Colorado Springs is known for its stunning mountain vistas, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.

  • Pueblo: Located 126 miles south of Limon, Pueblo is a historic city with a thriving arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Limon, Colorado, is a hidden gem in the heart of the Rocky Mountains. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and exciting outdoor activities, Limon offers something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this unique and charming town.
