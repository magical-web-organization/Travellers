Introduction to Comanche National Grasslands

Located in the Eastern Plains of Colorado, Comanche National Grasslands spans over 440,000 acres of land. The natural landscape consists of rolling prairies, canyons, and sandstone formations. It is a land rich in Native American history and diverse wildlife, making it an ideal destination for those who love outdoor adventure and cultural exploration.

List of Places to Visit in Comanche National Grasslands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in learning about the rich Native American history of the area, a visit to the Boggsville Historic Site and Museum is a must. This site was once a thriving trading post and was later the location of Kit Carson’s final home. Visitors can take a guided tour of the museum and explore the grounds.

Another important landmark is the Picture Canyon Preserve, which contains thousands of ancient rock paintings created by the Native American tribes that once inhabited the region. These paintings offer a glimpse into the lives of the area’s former residents.

Natural Attractions of Comanche National Grasslands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Comanche National Grasslands is a big draw for visitors. One of the most popular activities is hiking, with trails of varying difficulty levels, including the Picketwire Canyon Trail, which runs for over 11 miles and passes through some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the area.

There are also plenty of opportunities for wildlife watching, with over 300 species of birds, pronghorns, and bison living in the grasslands. The Two Buttes Reservoir is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Comanche National Grasslands

With over 400,000 acres of untouched wilderness, Comanche National Grasslands offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Colorado. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Picketwire Canyon Trail, which runs for over 11 miles and passes along the Purgatoire River and through the canyons with ancient rock art.

  • The Vogel Canyon Trail, which is an easy, 1.5-mile hike through a beautiful canyon with towering walls of sandstone.

  • The Rose Coulee Trail, which is a moderate 8-mile hike that takes you through some of the most stunning and rugged landscapes in the grasslands.

Best Things to Do in Comanche National Grasslands: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Comanche National Grasslands offers plenty of other activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Some of the best things to do in the area include:

  • Taking a guided tour of the Picture Canyon Preserve to learn more about the area’s rich history and culture.

  • Stargazing at the Carpios Ridge Campground, which offers some of the best views of the night sky in Colorado.

  • Attending the High Plains Snow Goose Festival, which celebrates the annual migration of snow geese to the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Comanche National Grasslands

Day 1: Arrive and settle in at the camping ground or hotel. Day 2: Visit Picture Canyon Preserve and learn about the ancient rock art of the Native American tribes. Day 3: Take a guided hiking tour through the Picketwire Canyon trail. Day 4: Go on a wildlife watching expedition, looking out for the pronghorns, bison, and different bird species. Day 5: Take a history tour of the Boggsville Historic Site and Museum. Day 6: Explore the different biking trails at Comanche National Grasslands. Day 7: Relax, take photos, and soak in the beauty of the grasslands before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Comanche National Grasslands

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to traditional cuisine in Comanche National Grasslands, bison is a must-try. Many local restaurants serve bison burgers, roasts, and stews. Another must-try dish is the green chili, which is a popular Colorado specialty.

Local Beverages

Since the area is known for its breweries, a local craft beer is a must-try. Comanche National Grasslands also boasts of wines, most notably the Merlot produced by the Fox Creek Winery on the eastern edge of the grasslands.

Where to Stay in Comanche National Grasslands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For those who love camping, there are plenty of camping grounds in Comanche National Grasslands. One of the best and most popular is the Carpios Ridge Campground, which provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

For those who prefer to sleep indoors, there are plenty of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in nearby towns such as Las Animas and La Junta.

What to Buy in Comanche National Grasslands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Comanche National Grasslands include Native American arts and crafts, bison jerky, and green chili products.

Local Culture and Customs of Comanche National Grasslands

The region around Comanche National Grasslands has a rich Native American history, and visitors are encouraged to respect and appreciate this culture. Visitors should always ask for permission before entering any sacred or culturally significant sites.

Best Time to Visit Comanche National Grasslands

The best time to visit Comanche National Grasslands is during the months of May to October when the weather is warmer and the grasslands are in full bloom. However, visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in weather, especially during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Comanche National Grasslands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for your activities.
  • Bring a camera to capture the stunning views of the grasslands.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property without permission.
  • Disturb or remove any plants or wildlife.
  • Litter or leave any trace behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Comanche National Grasslands

Visitors to Comanche National Grasslands should be aware of occasional hazards, such as rattlesnakes and sudden changes in weather. It is essential to follow safe practices when hiking and exploring the area.

Health in Comanche National Grasslands

While the grasslands are generally safe, visitors should bring plenty of sunscreen, insect repellent, and any necessary medication.

Getting to Comanche National Grasslands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Comanche National Grasslands

The closest airports to Comanche National Grasslands are the Pueblo Memorial Airport and the Denver International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Comanche National Grasslands

Greyhound offers bus services to the nearby towns of La Junta and Trinidad.

Other Options

Those who prefer to drive can easily reach Comanche National Grasslands off the Highway-350, which links with US-50.

How to Get Around in Comanche National Grasslands

The best way to get around Comanche National Grasslands is by car or bike. This allows visitors to explore the vast area at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Comanche National Grasslands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Those looking for additional outdoor adventure can head to the San Isabel National Forest or the Great Sand Dunes National Park and Preserve.

Conclusion

Comanche National Grasslands is a must-visit destination for lovers of outdoor adventure and history. With its stunning landscapes, cultural landmarks, and diverse wildlife, this grassland is sure to provide an unforgettable experience.
