Introduction to Glenwood Springs

Glenwood Springs is a small mountain town located in Northwestern Colorado, situated in the heart of the Rocky Mountains. Known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, Glenwood Springs is a popular year-round destination for visitors seeking to escape their everyday routine and immerse themselves in the beauty of Colorado’s outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in Glenwood Springs

Here are some of the top attractions to see and experience in Glenwood Springs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Glenwood Caverns Adventure Park: Located on top of Iron Mountain, this adventure park offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and features a variety of attractions and activities, including cave tours, thrill rides, and more.
  • Frontier Historical Museum: Explore the history of Glenwood Springs and the surrounding area at this local museum, which features exhibits dedicated to the Native American tribes, early settlers, and mining industry that shaped the town.
  • Doc Holliday’s Grave: Visit the final resting place of infamous gunslinger Doc Holliday at Linwood Cemetery, where visitors can pay their respects and learn about the history of the Wild West.

Natural Attractions of Glenwood Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glenwood Hot Springs: Take a dip in the world’s largest mineral hot springs pool, where the water is heated naturally to a comfortable 90-93 degrees and features 15 minerals that are believed to have healing properties.
  • Hanging Lake: Hike through the picturesque Glenwood Canyon to reach this stunning turquoise lake, which is known for its clear waters and cascading waterfalls.
  • South Canyon Hot Springs: This hidden gem features natural hot springs nestled in the Rocky Mountains, offering a secluded and serene atmosphere to relax and soak up the beauty of Glenwood Springs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glenwood Springs

Glenwood Springs is a hiker’s paradise, with several scenic trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains, forests, and rivers. Here are our top picks for hiking and biking routes:

  • Grizzly Creek Trail: A moderate, 5.8-mile roundtrip hike that takes visitors through a striking canyon and offers stunning views of the Glenwood Canyon.
  • Red Mountain Trail: A challenging hike that rewards visitors with panoramic views of Glenwood Springs and the surrounding area, including the Flat Tops Wilderness and the Elk Mountains.
  • Rio Grande Trail: A scenic biking trail that stretches from Glenwood Springs to Aspen and passes through charming mountain towns and scenic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Glenwood Springs: Activities and Tours

From ziplining to whitewater rafting to wine tasting, there are plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained in Glenwood Springs. Here are our top picks:

  • Whitewater Rafting: Take a thrilling ride down the Colorado River with one of the local outfitters, who offer guided tours for all skill levels.
  • Glenwood Vaudeville Revue: Catch a show at this local dinner theater, which features a variety of acts including comedy, music, and magic.
  • Iron Mountain Hot Springs: Relax in one of the 16 natural hot springs pools or enjoy a massage at this popular spa and outdoor recreation center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Glenwood Springs

Day 1: Explore Downtown Glenwood Springs, visit the Frontier Historical Museum, and enjoy dinner at one of the local restaurants.

Day 2: Take a dip in the Glenwood Hot Springs pool and visit Doc Holliday’s grave.

Day 3: Hike to Hanging Lake and discover the scenic beauty of Glenwood Canyon.

Day 4: Enjoy an outdoor adventure, such as a rafting trip or ziplining tour.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive up to Independence Pass and enjoy the stunning views of the Rocky Mountains.

Day 6: Relax at Iron Mountain Hot Springs and take a guided tour of Glenwood Caverns.

Day 7: Visit South Canyon Hot Springs and enjoy some local wine and food pairings at one of the local vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Glenwood Springs

Glenwood Springs offers visitors a variety of dining options, from casual cafes to upscale restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: These deep-fried bull testicles are a local delicacy and a must-try for adventurous eaters.
  • Trout: Freshly caught from the nearby rivers, this local specialty is often served with a side of veggies or in a fish taco.
  • Bison Burger: A hearty and flavorful alternative to traditional beef burgers, bison is a staple of the Colorado food scene.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Glenwood Springs and the surrounding area is home to several popular craft breweries, such as Glenwood Canyon Brewing Company and 970 West Studio.
  • Hot Tea Cocktails: Local bars and cafes often offer hot drinks with a boozy twist, such as the Spiced Apple Cider with cinnamon schnapps.

Where to Stay in Glenwood Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Glenwood Springs offers a variety of lodging options for visitors, from luxury hotels to rustic cabins. Here are some top picks:

  • Hotel Colorado: A historic hotel that first opened its doors in 1893, the Hotel Colorado boasts a rich history and a prime location in downtown Glenwood Springs.
  • Glenwood Adventure Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers and adventurers, this hostel is located just steps from the Glenwood Hot Springs.
  • The Hideout: A rustic and cozy cabin tucked away in the mountains, the Hideout offers an off-the-grid experience with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Glenwood Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a unique souvenir or gift from Glenwood Springs, be sure to check out these local shops and markets:

  • Rocky Mountain Chocolate Factory: Choose from a variety of delicious chocolate treats, including locally sourced ingredients and unique flavors.
  • Glenwood Springs Downtown Market: Browse handmade crafts, jewelry, and artwork from local artisans at this seasonal market.
  • Iron Mountain Hot Springs Gift Shop: Pick up a souvenir t-shirt or locally made soap at this gift shop located at the popular hot springs attraction.

Local Culture and Customs of Glenwood Springs

Glenwood Springs is a friendly and welcoming community that values the outdoors, the arts, and local history. Visitors should be prepared for mountain weather conditions and should respect the environment by practicing Leave No Trace principles when exploring the trails and natural areas.

Best Time to Visit Glenwood Springs

Glenwood Springs can be visited year-round, with each season offering a unique set of attractions and activities. However, the best time to visit is typically during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and the hiking trails and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glenwood Springs

Do:

  • Do book popular attractions and tours in advance to avoid disappointment.
  • Do pack for the weather, as mountain conditions can be unpredictable.
  • Do respect the natural environment and practice Leave No Trace principles when exploring.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave behind any trash or waste while on a hike or outdoor adventure.
  • Don’t attempt any risky or illegal activities, such as drug use or trespassing.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers and tour guides, as many of them rely on tips for their income.

Safety and Health

Safety in Glenwood Springs

While Glenwood Springs is generally a safe and welcoming community, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety while exploring the town and surrounding areas. Be aware of mountain weather conditions and prepare accordingly, always hike with a buddy or group, and avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas.

Health in Glenwood Springs

Visitors to Glenwood Springs should be aware of the high elevation and take steps to prevent altitude sickness. Drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol and caffeine, and take your time when hiking or engaging in physical activity. Visitors should also follow COVID-19 protocols and regulations during their visit.

Getting to Glenwood Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Glenwood Springs can be easily accessed by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options for visitors:

Airports Near Glenwood Springs

  • Eagle County Regional Airport: Located 30 miles from Glenwood Springs, this regional airport offers flights from several major hubs in the United States.
  • Denver International Airport: Located 170 miles from Glenwood Springs, this major airport serves several major airlines and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Glenwood Springs

  • Glenwood Springs Amtrak Station: Located in downtown Glenwood Springs, this station offers connections to several major cities across the United States.
  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located at 51027 Highway 6 in Glenwood Springs, this station offers bus service to several nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Several major rental car companies operate in Glenwood Springs, making it easy for visitors to explore the area on their own.
  • Shuttle Services: Several shuttle services operate in the area, offering convenient transportation to and from the major airports and nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Glenwood Springs

Glenwood Springs is a walkable town, with many attractions and restaurants located within walking distance. Visitors can also explore the town on bikes or scooters, which are available for rent at several local shops.

Where to Go Next from Glenwood Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Glenwood Springs serves as a gateway to several nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Aspen: A world-renowned ski town known for its luxury resorts and stunning mountain views, Aspen is located just 40 miles from Glenwood Springs.
  • Maroon Bells: One of the most photographed landscapes in Colorado, the Maroon Bells are located just 60 miles from Glenwood Springs and offer breathtaking hikes and views.
  • Steamboat Springs: Known for its natural hot springs and skiing, Steamboat Springs is located 100 miles from Glenwood Springs and is a popular winter destination.

Conclusion

Glenwood Springs is a charming mountain town nestled in the heart of the Rockies, offering visitors a variety of attractions, activities, and natural wonders to explore. Whether you’re seeking an adventure-filled vacation or a relaxing getaway, Glenwood Springs has something to offer for everyone.
