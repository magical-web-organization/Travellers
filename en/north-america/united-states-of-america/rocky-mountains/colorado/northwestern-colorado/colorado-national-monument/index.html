Introduction to Colorado National Monument

Colorado National Monument is a stunning park located in the northwestern part of Colorado, USA. The park covers an area of 32 square miles and was created in 1911 to protect unique rock formations and scenic views in the area. The park has a rich history, including human habitation dating back over 10,000 years, and has many culturally important and natural attractions worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Colorado National Monument

Colorado National Monument has many beautiful and unique places to visit. Here are some of the top attractions in the park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Visitor Center: Located at the entrance of the park, the Visitor Center is a great place to start your visit. Here, you can watch a short film about the park, pick up maps and guides, and speak to park rangers about your visit.
  • Rim Rock Drive: This scenic drive is a must-do activity in the park, offering breathtaking views of the canyon and surrounding landscape.
  • Ute Canyon: This canyon was once inhabited by the Ute Tribe and is a great place to explore the area’s cultural history.
  • Independence Monument: A 450-foot sandstone spire located near the entrance of the park. It is one of the most recognizable rock formations in the park.

Natural Attractions of Colorado National Monument: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coke Ovens Trail: This moderate 1.5-mile-long hike leads to a unique geological formation that resembles a set of “coke ovens.”
  • Devil’s Kitchen Trail: This 1-mile-long hike leads to a cave-like formation and offers breathtaking views.
  • Otto’s Trail: This strenuous 2.2-mile-long hike offers panoramic views of the park and surrounding landscape.
  • No Thoroughfare Canyon Trail: This 6.4-mile-long hike is perfect for those seeking a longer hiking adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colorado National Monument

Colorado National Monument has many great hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Coke Ovens Trail: This 1.5-mile-long trail is a great option for those seeking a moderate hiking experience.
  • No Thoroughfare Canyon Trail: This 6.4-mile-long trail offers a challenging hiking experience with breathtaking views of the park.
  • Cycling Rim Rock Drive: This 23-mile-long drive is very popular among cyclists and offers stunning views of the park’s canyon and surrounding landscape.
  • Cycling the Monument: This 32-mile-long route is perfect for experienced cyclists seeking a challenge.

Best Things to Do in Colorado National Monument: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do at Colorado National Monument:

  • Ranger-led Tours: The park offers a variety of ranger-led tours, including hikes, bike rides, and evening programs.
  • Stargazing: The park has very little light pollution, making it a great place for stargazing.
  • Wildlife Viewing: The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including coyotes, mule deer, and bighorn sheep.
  • Photography: The park’s stunning vistas and unique rock formations offer many great photo opportunities.

Food and Drink Scene of Colorado National Monument

Colorado National Monument does not have many dining options inside the park, but there are some notable options in nearby towns. Here are some of the best places to eat and drink near the park:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A local specialty, made from bull testicles.
  • Green Chili: A spicy dish made of chili peppers and typically served with pork.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Colorado is known for its excellent craft beer scene. Try the local brews at any of the many breweries located in nearby towns.

Where to Stay in Colorado National Monument: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are no accommodations within Colorado National Monument, but there are many great options in nearby towns. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Redlands Mesa Bed and Breakfast: A cozy B&B located in nearby Grand Junction.
  • Grand Vista Hotel: A mid-range hotel located near the park entrance.
  • Sands Motel: A budget-friendly hotel located in Fruita.

What to Buy in Colorado National Monument: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Colorado National Monument is known for its unique rock formations and stunning vistas, and there are many souvenirs and local products you can bring home to remember your trip. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Photos and Artwork: There are many talented photographers and artists who have captured the park’s natural beauty.
  • Geode/Agate: A rock typically found in the park and surrounding areas, known for its unique patterns and colors.
  • Local Produce: Colorado is known for its wide variety of delicious fruits and vegetables, including peaches, cherries, and apples.

Local Culture and Customs of Colorado National Monument

Colorado has a rich history and culture, and there are many traditions and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some important things to know:

  • Respect for Nature: Colorado is known for its outdoor adventures and natural beauty. Visitors should be respectful of the environment and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Tipping: Tipping is common in the service industry in Colorado. Standard tip is around 20%.
  • Dress Code: Colorado has a casual dress code, and visitors should dress appropriately for outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Colorado National Monument

The best time to visit Colorado National Monument is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. The park is open year-round, but during the summer months, the park can be very crowded and hot. Winter brings snow to the high elevations of the park and can make some areas inaccessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colorado National Monument

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Colorado National Monument:

Do:

  • Take Water and Snacks: Always have plenty of water and snacks with you, especially when hiking or biking.
  • Watch Your Step: The park’s trails can be rocky and uneven, so be sure to watch your step.
  • Check Weather Conditions: Colorado weather can be unpredictable, and it is important to check weather forecasts before heading out.

Don’t:

  • Disturb Wildlife: The park’s wildlife should be observed from a safe distance and never approached.
  • Leave No Trace: Visitors should pack out all trash, and avoid damaging or altering the park’s natural features.
  • Ignore Warning Signs: Warning signs should be observed and followed, especially regarding hazardous conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colorado National Monument

Visitors to Colorado National Monument should be aware of potential safety hazards and take precautions to avoid accidents. Here are some important safety tips:

  • Use Sunscreen: The park’s altitude and dry air can cause sunburns, so it is important to wear sunscreen and other protective clothing.
  • Stay on Marked Trails: The park’s trails can be rocky and uneven, so be sure to watch your step and stay on marked trails.
  • Stay Hydrated: It is important to drink plenty of water, especially when hiking or biking in the park.

Health in Colorado National Monument

Visitors to Colorado National Monument should also take precautions to maintain their health. Here are some important health tips:

  • Be Aware of Altitude Sickness: The park’s high elevation can cause altitude sickness. Visitors should take it slow and acclimate to the altitude gradually.
  • Stay Hydrated: Dehydration can cause headaches, fatigue, and other health problems. Visitors should drink plenty of water and electrolyte-replenishing drinks.

Getting to Colorado National Monument: Transportation Options and Logistics

Colorado National Monument is accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Colorado National Monument

  • Grand Junction Regional Airport: The closest airport to the park, located just 7 miles from the park entrance.

Bus Stations Near Colorado National Monument

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Grand Junction, just 7 miles from the park entrance.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Rental cars are available at the Grand Junction Regional Airport and in nearby towns.
  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and Uber are available in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Colorado National Monument

The best way to get around Colorado National Monument is by car or bike. The park’s scenic drives are a must-do activity, and biking is a popular option for outdoor enthusiasts. Hiking is also a great way to see the park up close and explore its unique features.

Where to Go Next from Colorado National Monument: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Colorado National Monument might enjoy:

  • Arches National Park: Located in Moab, Utah, just a few hours’ drive from Colorado National Monument, Arches National Park is known for its unique sandstone arches and stunning landscapes.
  • Glenwood Springs: Located about 90 minutes away from Colorado National Monument, Glenwood Springs is home to a variety of hot springs and outdoor activities.
  • Mesa Verde National Park: Located about 3 hours away from the park, Mesa Verde National Park is known for its well-preserved cliff dwellings and archaeological sites.

Conclusion

Colorado National Monument is a unique and beautiful park that is definitely worth a visit. The park offers a variety of activities and adventures for outdoor enthusiasts, as well as cultural and natural attractions for those interested in history and sightseeing. With the tips and suggestions in this guide, visitors can plan an unforgettable trip to Colorado National Monument.
