Introduction to Arapahoe Basin

Arapahoe Basin, also known as A-Basin, is a ski area located in Summit County, Colorado, about 70 miles from Denver. It is popular for its stunning mountain views, challenging ski runs, and friendly local culture. It is one of the few ski resorts in North America that offers skiers and snowboarders the opportunity to ski through late spring and early summer.

List of Places to Visit in Arapahoe Basin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Summit Historical Society Museum: Located in nearby Frisco, this museum showcases the history of Summit County with exhibits and artifacts from the area’s early settlers and pioneers.
  • Edwin Carter Discovery Center: This museum in Breckenridge is named after naturalist Edwin Carter, who collected specimens from the area in the late 1800s. It is dedicated to his work and the conservation of local wildlife.
  • 10th Mountain Division Hut Association: This organization maintains a network of backcountry huts and trails used by skiers and hikers. Some of the huts are located near Arapahoe Basin and offer stunning mountain views.

Natural Attractions of Arapahoe Basin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loveland Pass: This scenic drive takes you over the Continental Divide and offers breathtaking mountain views. It is popular for hiking, photography, and backcountry skiing and snowboarding.
  • Keystone Bike Park: This downhill mountain biking park is located in nearby Keystone and offers trails for all skill levels.
  • Dillon Reservoir: This large lake is located near the town of Dillon and offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arapahoe Basin

Arapahoe Basin offers several hiking and mountain biking trails in its surrounding area. Some of the popular trails are the Argentine Pass Trail, Lenawee Trail, and Herman Gulch Trail. Visitors can also rent bikes from a local shop and explore the bike trails in the area.

Best Things to Do in Arapahoe Basin: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Arapahoe Basin is known for its challenging runs and long ski season. It offers a variety of terrain for all skill levels.
  • Snowshoeing: Arapahoe Basin and its surrounding areas offer several trails for snowshoeing, including the Colorado Trail and Snake River Trail.
  • Whitewater Rafting: The nearby Blue River offers whitewater rafting opportunities with several local outfitters.
  • Scenic Drives: The area is home to several scenic drives, including the Peak to Peak Scenic Byway, Trail Ridge Road, and Guanella Pass Scenic Byway.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the Rocky Mountains for a unique view of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arapahoe Basin

Day 1: Acclimation day and explore Dillon Reservoir Day 2: Hiking at Argentine Pass Trail Day 3: Skiing and Snowboarding at Arapahoe Basin Day 4: Whitewater Rafting on the Blue River Day 5: Hot Air Balloon ride over the Rockies Day 6: Biking at Keystone Bike Park Day 7: Scenic Drive on Loveland Pass

Food and Drink Scene of Arapahoe Basin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chili: A Colorado staple, green chili is a spicy stew made with pork or beef, green chiles, and potatoes.
  • Buffalo Burger: A local delicacy, buffalo burgers are lean and flavorful.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: This dish is not for the faint of heart. It consists of deep-fried bull testicles and is a delicacy in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado Craft Beer: Colorado is home to several popular craft breweries, including New Belgium and Odell Brewing Company.
  • Colorado Whiskey: The state’s cold winters and hot summers are perfect for aging whiskey, and the area is home to several popular whiskey distilleries.

Where to Stay in Arapahoe Basin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Village at Breckenridge: This ski-in/ski-out hotel offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to Arapahoe Basin.
  • Keystone Lodge and Spa: This luxurious resort features a spa, multiple restaurants, and stunning mountain views.
  • Hostel Tevere: This hostel offers affordable accommodations with a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Arapahoe Basin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey: The area is known for its honey production, and visitors can purchase local honey from several farmer’s markets and specialty stores.
  • Handmade pottery: The mountain town of Breckenridge is home to several pottery shops featuring locally made, one-of-a-kind pieces.
  • Ski gear and apparel: Visitors can purchase ski gear and apparel from several local shops in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Arapahoe Basin

The local culture in Arapahoe Basin is focused on outdoor recreation and appreciating the beauty of the mountains. Visitors should respect the natural environment and leave no trace when participating in outdoor activities. The area has a laid-back and welcoming vibe, and visitors should embrace the local culture by trying new foods and participating in local events.

Best Time to Visit Arapahoe Basin

The best time to visit Arapahoe Basin is during the winter months when the ski resorts are in full operation. Late spring and early summer also offer great opportunities for skiing and snowboarding. The fall months bring beautiful foliage and mild weather, making it a great time to visit for hiking and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arapahoe Basin

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment by leaving no trace when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Try local cuisine and embrace the laid-back local culture.
  • Participate in local events and festivals, such as the Breckenridge International Festival of Arts.

Don’t:

  • Ignore weather warnings or advisories when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Leave valuables unattended in your car or hotel room.
  • Disrespect locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arapahoe Basin

Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities in mountainous terrain, such as altitude sickness, hypothermia, and avalanche danger. It is important to have the necessary equipment and knowledge to participate in these activities safely. Visitors should also be aware of mountain weather conditions and heed warnings from local officials.

Health in Arapahoe Basin

Visitors should stay hydrated and wear sunscreen to protect their skin from the high altitude sun. Altitude sickness is a common concern when visiting high elevation areas, so visitors should acclimate themselves by spending a few days at lower elevations before engaging in strenuous activity.

Getting to Arapahoe Basin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arapahoe Basin

  • Denver International Airport is the closest major airport, located approximately 70 miles east of Arapahoe Basin. Several shuttle services provide transport from Denver International Airport to Summit County.

Bus Stations Near Arapahoe Basin

  • Greyhound offers bus service to Frisco, located about 10 miles from Arapahoe Basin.

Other Options

  • Visitors can drive to Arapahoe Basin and park at one of the several parking lots available.

How to Get Around in Arapahoe Basin

Visitors can rent a car or take the Summit Stage, a free bus service in Summit County that provides access to several ski resorts and other attractions in the area.

Where to Go Next from Arapahoe Basin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Breckenridge: This historic mountain town is located about 10 miles from Arapahoe Basin and offers a variety of dining and shopping options, as well as outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Vail: This famous ski resort town is located about 50 miles from Arapahoe Basin and is known for its luxurious accommodations and world-class skiing.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park: This iconic national park is located about 60 miles from Arapahoe Basin and offers stunning mountain vistas, hiking trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Arapahoe Basin offers a unique blend of outdoor recreation, local culture, and stunning natural beauty. With its long ski season, challenging terrain, and friendly locals, it is a must-visit destination for any avid skier or snowboarder. Its proximity to nearby historic towns and national parks make it a great destination for a longer vacation.
