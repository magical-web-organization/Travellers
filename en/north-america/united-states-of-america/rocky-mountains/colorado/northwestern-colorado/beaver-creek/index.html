Introduction to Beaver Creek

Beaver Creek is a picturesque mountain resort located in northwestern Colorado, at the base of Beaver Creek Mountain. Surrounded by the majestic Rocky Mountains, the resort town is known for its world-famous ski slopes, unparalleled natural beauty, and luxurious accommodations.

Beaver Creek offers a wide range of activities for all seasons, from skiing and snowboarding in the winter to hiking and mountain biking in the summer. With its beautiful vistas, charming villages, and friendly locals, Beaver Creek is the perfect destination for families, couples, and solo travelers.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the top places to visit, best hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, safety and health tips, and more!

List of Places to Visit in Beaver Creek

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beaver Creek Art Gallery: A magnificent collection of fine art and contemporary Western artworks.
  • McCoy Park: A serene nature preserve with beautiful views, woodland trails, and wildlife.
  • Beaver Creek Chapel: A stunning chapel with breathtaking architecture surrounded by the Rocky Mountains.
  • Beaver Creek Stables: A fun activity for visitors of all ages, with horseback riding and horse-drawn carriage rides available.
  • The Ranch at Beaver Creek: A working cattle ranch that offers horseback riding tours, fly-fishing lessons, and other outdoor activities.

Natural Attractions of Beaver Creek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaver Creek Mountain: A fantastic ski resort with plenty of ski runs for all skill levels.
  • Beaver Creek Golf Course: An 18-hole championship golf course with beautiful mountain views.
  • Beaver Creek Nordic Center: Offers cross-country skiing and snowshoeing trails for all skill levels.
  • Beaver Creek Hiking Trails: A vast network of trails that provide access to beautiful mountain vistas, alpine meadows, and pristine waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beaver Creek

Beaver Creek offers a vast network of hiking and biking trails that provide access to some of the best scenery in the Rocky Mountains. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Village to Village Trail: A 2.9-mile trail that connects the towns of Beaver Creek and Bachelor Gulch, with panoramic views of the valley.
  • Berry Creek Trail: A challenging 9.2-mile trail that offers spectacular views of the Gore Range and Beaver Creek.
  • Beaver Creek to Arrowhead: A 3-mile trail that takes you through beautiful aspen groves to Arrowhead Village.
  • Bachelor Gulch Trail: A moderate 6.4-mile trail that takes you through scenic forests and meadows.

Best Things to Do in Beaver Creek: Activities and Tours

  • Beaver Creek Ski Resort: A world-class ski resort with over 1,800 acres of ski runs and 24 lifts.
  • Snowmobiling: Explore the Rocky Mountains on a snowmobile tour, with options for all skill levels.
  • White Water Rafting: Take an exciting rafting trip down the Colorado River or Eagle River.
  • ATV Tours: Experience the beauty of the Rockies on an ATV tour through the mountains.
  • Beaver Creek Rodeo: Watch the excitement of a rodeo in a beautiful mountain setting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beaver Creek

Day 1 - Arrive in Beaver Creek and explore the village. Day 2 - Take a hike in McCoy Park or explore the Beaver Creek Stables. Day 3 - Ski or snowboard at the Beaver Creek Ski Resort. Day 4 - Enjoy a day of snowmobiling or white water rafting. Day 5 - Take an ATV tour through the mountains. Day 6 - Discover the beauty of the Rocky Mountains on a scenic drive. Day 7 - Departure to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Beaver Creek

Must-Try Dishes

  • Elk Medallions: A flavorful dish that features tender elk medallions served with a side of vegetables.
  • Rocky Mountain Trout: Crispy pan-fried trout with a side of seasoned vegetables.
  • Colorado Lamb Chops: A savory lamb dish with a side of rosemary potatoes and seasonal vegetables.
  • Wild Game Sausage: A delicious sausage made with wild game meats like bison, elk, and venison.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado Craft Beers: Enjoy a cold one from local breweries like 7 Hermits Brewing Company and Crazy Mountain Brewing Company.
  • Colorado Wines: Indulge in a local wine from the vineyards of the Rockies at Vines at Vail Winery.
  • Colorado Whiskey: Get warmed up with a local whiskey from Colorado’s first distillery, 10th Mountain Whiskey & Spirit Company.

Where to Stay in Beaver Creek: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beaver Creek offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are a few top places to consider:

  • The Ritz-Carlton, Bachelor Gulch: A five-star luxury resort with its own ski lift.
  • Beaver Creek Lodge: A rustic yet luxurious hotel located at the base of Beaver Creek Mountain.
  • Arrowhead Village: A gated community with ski-in/ski-out condos and a heated outdoor pool.
  • St. James Place: A centrally located condominium complex with a spa, fitness center, and hot tub.

What to Buy in Beaver Creek: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Colorado Craft Beer: Bring home a six-pack of your favorite beer from local breweries.
  • Colorado Wine: Take home a bottle of local wine from the Vines at Vail Winery.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Pick up a unique piece of jewelry made by a local artist.
  • Local Artwork: Add a one-of-a-kind painting or sculpture to your collection.

Local Culture and Customs of Beaver Creek

Beaver Creek is a friendly and welcoming community with a deep appreciation for nature and the outdoors. The locals are proud of their Western heritage and are always happy to share their knowledge of the area with visitors.

When visiting Beaver Creek, it’s important to respect the natural environment and wildlife. Stick to designated trails, pack out your trash, and be mindful of your impact on the local ecosystem.

Best Time to Visit Beaver Creek

The best time to visit Beaver Creek depends on your interests. If you’re a skier or snowboarder, the winter months (December to February) are the ideal time to hit the slopes. For outdoor enthusiasts, the summer months (June to August) offer a wide range of activities, from hiking and mountain biking to white water rafting and horseback riding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beaver Creek

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Stick to designated trails when hiking and biking.
  • Enjoy the local food and drink scene.
  • Book accommodations in advance during peak season.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the trails.
  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beaver Creek

Beaver Creek is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions. Always lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodation, and be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or biking in isolated areas.

Health in Beaver Creek

Beaver Creek has excellent medical facilities, including Vail Valley Medical Center and the Beaver Creek Medical Center. Make sure to pack any necessary medication, and be aware of the effects of high altitude on your body if you’re not used to it.

Getting to Beaver Creek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beaver Creek

The nearest airports to Beaver Creek are Eagle County Regional Airport (EGE) and Denver International Airport (DEN). Both airports have shuttle and car rental services available.

Bus Stations Near Beaver Creek

Greyhound and Amtrak have bus stations in Glenwood Springs, which is about an hour’s drive from Beaver Creek.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, the town is easily accessible by car via Interstate 70.

How to Get Around in Beaver Creek

Beaver Creek provides complimentary shuttle services throughout the village and the surrounding areas. There are also several car rental options available in the village.

Where to Go Next from Beaver Creek: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vail: A short drive from Beaver Creek, Vail is a charming mountain town with excellent shopping, dining, and nightlife.
  • Glenwood Springs: A town known for its hot springs and cavern tours, Glenwood Springs is an ideal destination for a day trip or overnight stay.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park: Located just a few hours’ drive from Beaver Creek, this magnificent national park offers alpine meadows, wildlife, and stunning mountain vistas.

Conclusion

Beaver Creek is a destination that offers something for everyone, from families and couples to outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. With its stunning natural beauty, world-class ski runs, and luxurious accommodations, the town is the perfect place to unwind, explore, and create unforgettable memories.
