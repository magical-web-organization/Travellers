Introduction to Keystone

Keystone is a small mountain town located in the Rocky Mountains, in northwestern Colorado, United States of America. The town is nestled in the White River National Forest and is known for its world-class ski resorts, scenic natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. If you are planning a trip to Keystone, you can expect a unique combination of thrilling outdoor activities, breathtaking scenic views, and a laid-back, cozy atmosphere that will leave you wanting more.

List of Places to Visit in Keystone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Keystone has a lot to offer. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Keystone:

  • Keystone Science School: This outdoor education center is dedicated to inspiring curiosity and a love of learning in children and adults alike. The school offers a variety of programs, including summer camps, wilderness adventures, and environmental education programs.

  • Summit Historical Society: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and heritage of Summit County, which includes Keystone and other nearby towns. The museum features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the region’s mining and outdoor recreation history.

  • Breckenridge Heritage Alliance: This organization offers guided tours, historic hikes, and educational programs that explore the history and culture of Keystone and other towns in Summit County.

Natural Attractions of Keystone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Keystone is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, ranging from snow-capped peaks to serene alpine lakes. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Keystone:

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: This iconic national park is located about a two-hour drive from Keystone and offers some of the most stunning scenery in the United States. The park is known for its towering peaks, pristine lakes, and diverse wildlife, including elk, moose, and bighorn sheep.

  • Dillon Reservoir: This large lake is located just a few miles from Keystone and is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and other water sports. The surrounding scenery is breathtaking, with mountain peaks rising up in every direction.

  • Arapahoe Basin: This ski resort is located just a few miles from Keystone and offers some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the world. The resort is known for its challenging terrain, natural snowfalls, and laid-back atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keystone

Keystone is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails running through the surrounding mountains and forests. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Keystone:

  • Keystone Resort: The resort offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that range from easy to challenging. Some of the most popular routes include the Keystone Aqueduct Trail, the Keystone Outback Trail, and the Keystone Gulch Trail.

  • Sapphire Point: This easy hiking trail offers stunning views of the Dillon Reservoir and the surrounding mountains. The trail is about a mile long and is suitable for all skill levels.

  • Montezuma: This challenging hiking trail is located about 20 miles from Keystone and offers some of the most spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. The trail is about 8 miles long and requires a moderate to high level of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Keystone: Activities and Tours

Aside from outdoor activities, Keystone has plenty of attractions and activities that will appeal to visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Keystone:

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the stunning scenery of Keystone on horseback with a guided tour through the mountains. The tours are suitable for all skill levels and are a great way to experience the beauty of the area.

  • Golfing: Keystone has several world-class golf courses that offer stunning mountain views and challenging holes. The courses are open to the public and offer a unique golfing experience.

  • Sleigh Rides: Take a scenic sleigh ride through the snow-covered valleys and forests of Keystone for a magical winter experience. The tours are suitable for all ages and are a great way to experience the area’s beauty during the winter season.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keystone

If you are planning a week-long trip to Keystone, here is a suggested itinerary that will allow you to experience the best of what the area has to offer:

Day 1: Arrival

Arrive in Keystone and settle into your accommodation. Take a stroll around the town and check out the local shops and restaurants.

Day 2: Outdoor Adventures

Spend the day exploring the natural beauty of Keystone with a hike or bike ride. Visit Sapphire Point for some stunning views of the Dillon Reservoir.

Day 3: Cultural Attractions

Visit the Summit Historical Society and learn about the region’s rich mining history. Take a guided tour of Keystone Science School and learn about the area’s unique ecology.

Day 4: Skiing and Snowboarding

Hit the slopes at Arapahoe Basin for some world-class skiing and snowboarding.

Day 5: Relaxation

Spend the day relaxing at your accommodation or at a nearby spa. Take a dip in the hot tub or indulge in a massage.

Day 6: Food and Drink

Explore the local food and drink scene in Keystone with a visit to some of the town’s best restaurants and bars. Try some of the local specialties, like Rocky Mountain oysters or craft beer.

Day 7: Departure

Depart Keystone and head to your next destination, or take a day trip to nearby attractions like Rocky Mountain National Park or Breckenridge.

Food and Drink Scene of Keystone

Keystone has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects the area’s unique history and culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Keystone:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: Despite their name, these are not oysters but are actually deep-fried bull testicles. They are a popular local delicacy and are surprisingly tasty.

  • Colorado Lamb: Colorado is known for its high-quality lamb, and many of the restaurants in Keystone serve up delicious lamb dishes.

  • Wild Game: Elk, bison, and other wild game are also popular dishes in Keystone and can be found at many of the local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Colorado is home to some of the best craft breweries in the United States, and Keystone is no exception. The town has several breweries that offer a wide variety of locally brewed beers.

  • Spirits: Keystone is home to several distilleries that produce high-quality spirits, including whiskey, gin, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Keystone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Keystone has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top options:

  • Keystone Resort: This world-class resort offers a variety of lodging options, from luxurious hotels to cozy condos and townhouses. The resort is located in the heart of Keystone and offers easy access to the ski slopes and other attractions.

  • Airbnb: There are many Airbnb properties available in Keystone, ranging from small apartments to large cabins and chalets. This is a great option for travelers who want a more private and homey experience.

  • Hostels: There are several hostels in Keystone that offer budget-friendly accommodation options for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Keystone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Keystone, here are some ideas:

  • Handmade Crafts: Keystone has a vibrant arts and crafts community, and you can find a variety of handmade goods, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

  • Local Beer and Spirits: Bring home a bottle of locally brewed beer or spirits as a tasty souvenir of your trip.

  • Outdoor Gear: Keystone is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, and you can find a variety of high-quality outdoor gear, including hiking boots, backpacks, and camping equipment.

Local Culture and Customs of Keystone

Keystone has a laid-back, friendly culture that reflects its history and natural surroundings. Visitors should be respectful of the local environment and wildlife and should follow the Leave No Trace principles when hiking or camping in the area. Additionally, Keystone is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts, so visitors should be prepared for cold weather and snowy conditions.

Best Time to Visit Keystone

The best time to visit Keystone depends on your interests and preferences. If you are interested in skiing or snowboarding, the winter months of December through March are the best time to visit. If you are interested in hiking and outdoor activities, the summer months of June through August are the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keystone

Do:

  • Do respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Do follow the Leave No Trace principles when hiking or camping in the area.
  • Do dress appropriately for the weather and conditions.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the outdoors.
  • Don’t disturb or approach the local wildlife.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather and conditions.
  • Don’t ignore safety warnings and advice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keystone

Keystone is a generally safe destination, but visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or camping in the outdoors. Visitors should also be mindful of the local wildlife and should avoid approaching or disturbing animals. Additionally, visitors should follow all safety guidelines and instructions when skiing or participating in other outdoor activities in the area.

Health in Keystone

Keystone has several medical facilities and clinics in case of emergencies. Visitors should also be prepared for high-altitude conditions, which can cause altitude sickness in some people. Visitors should stay hydrated and avoid strenuous activity during the first few days at high altitude.

Getting to Keystone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Keystone is located about 90 miles west of Denver, Colorado, and is easily accessible by car or shuttle bus. Here are some of the transportation options available to visitors:

Airports Near Keystone

  • Denver International Airport: This is the closest major airport to Keystone and is located about 90 miles east of the town. Several shuttle bus services and rental car options are available from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Keystone

  • Grayline Shuttle: This shuttle service offers transportation between Denver International Airport and Keystone.

  • Summit Express Shuttle: This shuttle service offers transportation between Denver International Airport, Breckenridge, Keystone, and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car from Denver International Airport or from other locations in the region to explore Keystone and the surrounding area.

  • Private Transfer: Private transfer services are also available to visitors who prefer a more personalized transportation option.

How to Get Around in Keystone

Keystone is a small town with many attractions and activities within walking distance. However, visitors may want to rent a car or bicycle to explore the surrounding area and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Keystone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Keystone is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding area and nearby attractions. Some of the top destinations and excursions near Keystone include:

  • Breckenridge: This nearby town is known for its historic charm, outdoor recreation opportunities, and world-class skiing and snowboarding.

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: This iconic national park is located about a two-hour drive from Keystone and offers some of the most stunning scenery in the United States.

  • Vail: This world-renowned ski resort is located about an hour and a half from Keystone and offers some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the world.

Conclusion

Keystone is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and those who appreciate natural beauty and culture. With its stunning mountain scenery, world-class ski resorts, and vibrant food and drink scene, Keystone offers something for everyone.
