Introduction to Redstone

Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Redstone is a small historic town known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreation, local culture, and rich history. Originally named Cleveholm Manor, this magnificent place was the residence of John Cleveland Osgood - a prominent industrialist and philanthropist of the early 20th century. Today, Redstone has become a popular destination for travelers looking for a unique experience in Colorado.

List of Places to Visit in Redstone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Redstone Castle: Step back in time and visit the elegant Redstone Castle. This iconic structure was built in 1902 by John Cleveland Osgood and has been beautifully restored.
  • Redstone Historic District: Take a stroll through the charming Redstone Historic District and discover its rich history. You will find many local shops, galleries, restaurants, and other attractions.
  • Redstone Coke Ovens: Visit the historic Redstone Coke Ovens and learn about the town’s coal mining history.

Natural Attractions of Redstone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crystal Mill: The Crystal Mill is one of the most photographed sites in Colorado. This 19th-century powerhouse once provided electricity to the town of Crystal.
  • Maroon Bells: The Maroon Bells are a pair of majestic peaks located in the Maroon Bells-Snowmass Wilderness Area. The area is also a popular hiking and camping destination.
  • Avalanche Ranch Hot Springs: Relax and rejuvenate your soul in the natural hot springs at Avalanche Ranch. The resort is surrounded by beautiful forests, mountains, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redstone

  • Avalanche Creek Trail: This 9-mile trail is a popular hike that offers spectacular views of Avalanche Creek, waterfalls, and wildflowers.
  • Redstone to McClure Pass: This 23-mile bike ride takes you through some of the most scenic areas of the Rocky Mountains. You will pass by Crystal River, Aspens, and several other breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Redstone: Activities and Tours

  • Jeep Tours: Take a Jeep tour of the surrounding mountains and explore beautiful trails, waterfalls, and scenic views.
  • Fly Fishing: Redstone is a paradise for fly fishing enthusiasts. The Crystal River is home to a variety of trout species, including rainbow, brown, and cutthroat.
  • Horseback Riding: Enjoy a horseback ride through the mountains and discover the beauty of Colorado’s landscapes while having fun.

Food and Drink Scene of Redstone

Must-Try Dishes

  • Elk Tenderloin: Elk is a local specialty that is served at many restaurants in Redstone. The Elk Tenderloin is a must-try dish.
  • Colorado Lamb: Colorado Lamb is also a popular dish in Redstone. It is served in many local restaurants and is known for its delicious flavor.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: Rocky Mountain Oysters are a unique and adventurous dish that can be found in many restaurants in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Redstone is home to a variety of craft breweries that serve a variety of local beer.
  • Wine: Many local wineries offer tastings and tours of the vineyards. These wines are made from the local fruits and berries.

Where to Stay in Redstone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Redstone Inn: This historic inn was built in 1902 and has been beautifully restored. It offers comfortable rooms, great views, and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Avalanche Ranch Cabins & Hot Springs: This resort offers cabins, campsites, and hot springs. It is beautifully located in a serene atmosphere surrounded by forests and mountains, offering a relaxing getaway.
  • Crystal River Resort: This resort is located in the heart of the Crystal River Valley and offers comfortable cabins, fishing, and hiking trails.

What to Buy in Redstone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art and Pottery: Redstone is home to many local artist studios where you can find hand-made pottery, jewelry, and other unique artworks.
  • Handmade candles: The Avalanche Ranch Gift Shop sells hand-poured soy candles made on site that are scented with natural herbs and essential oils.
  • Ice cream from Redstone General Store: The Redstone General Store sells local ice cream flavors made by a nearby artisanal creamery.

Local Culture and Customs of Redstone

Redstone’s local culture is deeply rooted in its mining and ranching history. The historic district and the Redstone Castle are a testament to the town’s rich heritage and offers visitors a glimpse into its past. Locals are friendly and welcoming, and the town has a laid-back vibe that’s perfect for those wanting to escape the stresses of city life. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and should be mindful of the town’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Redstone

The best time to visit Redstone is during the summer months from June to August. The weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are at its peak during this time of the year. Fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit since the fall colors are at their peak. Winters can bring snow and are great for skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redstone

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Pack accordingly for outdoor activities.
  • Shop local and support small businesses.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Redstone.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in the natural areas.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Disrupt wildlife.
  • Ignore safety warnings for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redstone

Redstone is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual safety precautions when traveling in any area. Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Watch for bears and other wildlife, and keep a safe distance from them. Stay within designated trails and follow instructions from guides.

Health in Redstone

Redstone has limited health services available. Visitors should plan accordingly and bring any necessary medications or medical supplies they may need. The nearest hospital is in Glenwood Springs, which is about an hour’s drive from Redstone.

Getting to Redstone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Redstone

  • Eagle County Regional Airport (EGE): This airport is located in Eagle County, approximately 100 miles from Redstone.
  • Denver International Airport (DEN): This airport is located in Denver, approximately 200 miles from Redstone.

Bus Stations Near Redstone

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The nearest Greyhound Bus station is located in Glenwood Springs, which is about an hour’s drive from Redstone.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at many airport locations and in nearby towns.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Glenwood Springs.
  • Private Car Services: Private car services are available from airports and nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Redstone

Walking and hiking are some of the best ways to get around Redstone. For those wanting to venture a bit further, renting a car or using a taxi service is also an option. Biking is also a great way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Redstone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Glenwood Springs: This nearby town is known for its hot springs, cavern tours, and adventure park.
  • Aspen: Aspen is a popular ski resort that is located about an hour’s drive from Redstone. It also offers many summer activities such as hiking and biking.
  • Marble: This small town is located about 30 minutes away from Redstone and is known for its marble quarries and beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Redstone is a gem in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, offering visitors a unique, small-town experience. With its rich history, cultural attractions, and natural beauty, it’s a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or a relaxing getaway, Redstone has something for everyone. So pack your bags and come experience the beauty of Northwestern Colorado.
