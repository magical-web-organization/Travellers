Introduction to Gould

Located in northwestern Colorado, Gould is a small town nestled in the Rocky Mountains of the United States. It is a beautiful place with incredible natural beauty, exciting outdoor adventures, and a rich history that makes it a popular tourist destination.

This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Gould so that you can make the most of your time in this scenic and serene town. From the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and what to eat, we have got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Gould

There are many places to visit in Gould, ranging from culturally significant attractions to natural wonders. Here are some of the top spots to check out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gould Pioneer Museum: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the town’s history and is a must-visit spot for history buffs.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park: Home to a variety of species, including elk, moose, and bighorn sheep, this park offers abundant opportunities for wildlife viewing.
  • Mesa Verde National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage site features some of the best-preserved archaeological sites connected to the ancestral Puebloans, including the famous cliff dwellings.

Natural Attractions of Gould: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hiking and Biking Trails in the Rocky Mountains: Gould is surrounded by stunning mountain scenery, making it an ideal destination for hikers and bikers of all levels.
  • North Platte River: With its crystal clear waters and idyllic scenery, this river is a popular spot for fishing and whitewater rafting.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park: With 300 miles of hiking trails to explore, backcountry camping opportunities, and picturesque waterfalls, Rocky Mountain National Park has something for everyone. Don’t miss spotting its scenic viewpoints including the Trail Ridge Road.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gould

Gould offers a range of great trails for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. Here are some popular routes:

  • Mount Zirkel Loop Trail: This 11.8-mile hike is one of the most popular in the area, offering stunning views of wildflowers and mountain vistas.
  • National Forest Trail: Stretching over 74 miles, this trail offers incredible views of the region’s diverse ecology and wildlife.
  • Buffalo Pass Trail: This rugged trail with high elevation will take you through many beautiful sites and provides amazing scenery.

Best Things to Do in Gould: Activities and Tours

Gould offers a variety of exciting activities and tours that cater to all interests. Some of the best things to do in and around Gould include:

  • Whitewater Rafting: Take a thrilling ride down the North Platte River and admire the stunning Rocky Mountain views.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of the mountains on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Take advantage of the town’s beautiful winter weather with a trip to nearby ski resorts like Steamboat Springs, Winter Park, or Vail.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gould

The following itinerary will allow you to experience the best things that Gould has to offer over the course of a week:

Day Activity
1 Explore Gould Pioneer Museum and the town’s historic buildings

7 Relax in the beautiful scenery of Gould and enjoy local food and drinks

Food and Drink Scene of Gould

Aside from its stunning scenery, Gould is also famous for its food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A unique dish made from calf or bull testicles served in local restaurants in the area. It’s a must-try dish for the adventurous eaters.
  • Green Chili: A popular local favorite, this stew is made with green chilies, pork, and beans and served with tortillas.
  • Bison Steak: Being widespread in the area and known for its remarkable taste, this dish is extremely popular among the locals.

Local Beverages

  • Local Beers: With its many local breweries, Gould is a haven for beer lovers. Must-try local brands include O’Dell Brewing Company and New Belgium Brewing.
  • Colorado Wine: Although the state is better-known for its beer industry, Colorado produces some excellent wines that use grapes grown in high altitude.

Where to Stay in Gould: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gould has a range of accommodations for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  • North Park Lodge: This comfortable hotel offers stunning views of the mountains and is ideally situated for outdoor activities.
  • Wyman Hotel and Inn: For a more luxurious experience, stay at this charming hotel with elegant furnishings that take you back to the past.
  • Old Town Guesthouse B&B: This Victorian-style guesthouse has a cozy atmosphere that makes it perfect for couples and is known for its delicious breakfasts.

What to Buy in Gould: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gould offers a range of unique souvenirs and products to bring home to remember your trip. Some top souvenir ideas include:

  • Handmade ceramics: Buy locally made pottery and pieces of ceramics as it distinctive to the state.
  • Ski and Snowboard Gear: Gorgeous alpine and winter gear are available in the area at friendly prices. Don’t miss the avalanche gear as Colorado is famous for unpredictable weather in winter.
  • Edibles: Colorado law allows adults above the age of 21 to buy edible cannabis products. Therefore, go ahead to pot shops and buy some locally made edible for the memories.

Local Culture and Customs of Gould

Gould has a unique culture that visitors should be aware of. The locals are friendly and welcoming, living a lifestyle influenced by the mountain environment. The town is known for its traditional ranching, rodeo, and logging operations, which have helped shape the history and traditions of the region.

Best Time to Visit Gould

The town of Gould is a year-round destination, offering a variety of activities and experiences for each season; however, the best seasons to visit are summer (June-August) and fall (September-November).

During the summer, the temperature averages from 50-60°F, making it perfect for enjoying outdoor activities. In the fall, the weather is ideal for hiking and fishing as the trees transform into a golden hue, offering breathtaking views. Winters are chilly and snowy, and the town becomes more isolated, while spring might present unexpected weather conditions and snow during the transition.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gould

It’s wise to follow these guidelines when traveling to Gould:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace of your presence.
  • Drive cautiously and stay alert while enjoying mountain roads.
  • Stay in designated campsites and avoid any unmarked areas.
  • Dress accordingly, and keep enough warm clothes when traveling in winter/spring.

Don’t:

  • Never leave your trash or litter behind.
  • Do not try to feed or approach wildlife.
  • Do not rely on GPS overmaps when traveling in remote areas.
  • Never leave an open flame unattended and follow any local fire restrictions.

Safety and Health

Gould is a safe and healthy place to visit as long as you take general safety precautions to avoid the potential risks associated with outdoor activities.

Safety in Gould

  • Be aware and well-informed about the weather conditions before planning your itinerary.
  • Always carry a bear spray while hiking in the wild.
  • Drive cautiously on mountain roads, especially in winter.
  • Follow any restrictions set by the local authorities while camping or enjoying a campfire.

Health in Gould

  • Dehydration, altitude sickness, or hypothermia can be dangerous as Goulds’ subtle atmosphere might take you time to adapt, so stay hydrated and dress accordingly.
  • Avoid water bodies that aren’t clear and transparent, as the river may develop bacteria and parasites that may cause illnesses. So drink bottled water or use a personal filter.
  • Let someone know about your itinerary and try to stay connected throughout your trip for safety reasons.

Getting to Gould: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gould is located about 3 miles off Highway 14, which is accessible via car or bus routes. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Gould

  • Denver International Airport (DEN): Located about 175 miles from Gould, it’s the nearest major airport, offering domestic and international flights.
  • Laramie Regional Airport (LAR): Located about 70 miles from Gould, it’s a smaller airport, offering limited connections to the major cities nearby.

Bus Stations Near Gould

  • Greyhound Bus Line: Provides service to Steamboat, about 30 miles southeast of Gould, and Denver, about 175 miles from Gould.
  • North Park Transportation: Offers daily service from Steamboat Springs and Denver to Gould and nearby areas.

Other Options

  • Renting a car: It’s the best option to enjoy the picturesque drive while you explore the town at your pace.
  • Bikes: Rent a bike to explore the outskirts of the town for a budget-friendly option, or to get closer to nature.

How to Get Around in Gould

The best way to get around Gould and the surrounding areas is by car. Alternatively, visitors can also rent bikes to explore the outskirts of the town.

Where to Go Next from Gould: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gould is surrounded by many charming towns and beautiful natural landscapes, providing plenty of options for further exploration. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Steamboat Springs: Just 30 miles away, this town offers world-class skiing and snowboarding, natural hot springs, and great dining options.
  • Estes Park: Located within Rocky Mountain National Park, this charming town is surrounded by majestic mountains and wildlife and is just a two-hour drive from Gould.
  • Wyoming: The neighboring state is home to many notable attractions, including Yellowstone National Park, Grand Teton National Park, and many other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Gould is a magical place with beautiful mountain scenery, exciting outdoor adventures, rich history, and a unique culture. Hopefully, this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to this charming town. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, Gould promises to leave you with unforgettable memories and experiences. Start planning your trip today!
