Introduction to Idaho Springs

Located in the Northwestern region of Colorado in the United States of America, Idaho Springs is a small town nestled in the Rocky Mountains. It is situated around 30 miles west of Denver and offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure activities year-round. Idaho Springs has a rich history, as it was a gold rush town in the late 1800s. Today, the town boasts a unique combination of rich history and modern amenities, which makes it an ideal destination for both nature enthusiasts and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Idaho Springs

When it comes to places to visit in Idaho Springs, there is no shortage of options. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Argo Gold Mine and Mill

Explore Colorado’s rich mining history at the Argo Gold Mine and Mill. Tour the mill and experience what it was like to be a miner in the late 1800s. Argo Gold Mine and Mill is an excellent place for families, as they offer panning for gold activities.

Heritage Visitor Center

The Heritage Visitor Center is the perfect place to learn about Idaho Springs’ rich history. Here, visitors can take a self-guided tour through a restored miner’s boarding house, which provides information about the town’s mining history.

Natural Attractions of Idaho Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Evans Scenic Byway

Mount Evans Scenic Byway takes visitors on a journey through the Rocky Mountains, offering stunning views at every turn. The road takes visitors to the summit of Mount Evans, which is the highest paved road in North America.

Clear Creek

Enjoy a day of tubing, kayaking, or fly fishing in Clear Creek, which runs through the heart of Idaho Springs. Clear Creek is a popular place for locals and tourists to cool down during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Idaho Springs

Idaho Springs is a hiker’s paradise, with several hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking mountain views. Here are some of the best routes:

Chicago Lakes

Chicago Lakes is a strenuous but rewarding hike. The 9.1-mile route will take you through beautiful alpine meadows, past rushing streams and up to the two crystal-clear lakes.

St. Mary’s Glacier

St. Mary’s Glacier is a beginner-friendly hike that offers breathtaking views of the glacier and surrounding mountains. It’s a 1.5-mile hike that takes about an hour to complete.

Best Things to Do in Idaho Springs: Activities and Tours

Hot Springs

After a long day of hiking and exploring, relax in one of the hot springs in Idaho Springs. There are several hot springs in the area, including Indian Hot Springs, which offers a variety of pools with varying temperatures, making it a great place for families.

Idaho Springs Zip Line Tours

Experience the thrill of flying over trees, rocks, and creeks with Idaho Springs Zip Line Tours. They offer a variety of tours, from 6 to 8 ziplines that will take you through the untouched wilderness of the Rocky Mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Idaho Springs

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Idaho Springs:

Day 1: Explore the town of Idaho Springs, visit the Argo Gold Mine and Mill.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive up to Mount Evans Scenic Byway.

Day 3: Hike St. Mary’s Glacier and visit the Heritage Visitor Center.

Day 4: Rafting or kayaking on Clear Creek and enjoy the natural hot springs.

Day 5: Zip lining tour.

Day 6: Visit the Chicago Lakes or take a scenic drive to Guanella Pass.

Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Idaho Springs

When it comes to food and drink, Idaho Springs has plenty to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Rocky Mountain Oysters

A popular dish in Idaho Springs, Rocky Mountain Oysters are bull testicles that are sliced, breaded, and deep-fried.

Bison Burger

Bison burgers are a healthy and delicious alternative to beef burgers, and many restaurants in Idaho Springs serve this regional specialty.

Local Beverages

Tommyknocker Brewery

Tommyknocker Brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including seasonal and limited-edition brews.

Mountain Moonshine

A true Colorado-style distillery, Mountain Moonshine offers unique spirits using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Idaho Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of lodging options in Idaho Springs. Here are some options for every budget:

Miners Pick Bed and Breakfast

Miners Pick Bed and Breakfast is a top-rated property, located downtown in a historic building that offers comfortable rooms that are decorated to highlight the town’s mining history.

H & H Motor Lodge

H & H Motor Lodge is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms with beautiful mountain views.

What to Buy in Idaho Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many unique souvenirs and local products to buy in Idaho Springs. Some popular items include:

Colorado Honey

Colorado is known for its delicious honey, and there are several local shops where visitors can pick up a jar of their favorite flavor.

Handmade Soap

Several local shops offer handmade soaps made with natural ingredients. These soaps are a great gift for someone looking for a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Idaho Springs

Idaho Springs has a rich history, and the locals take pride in preserving the town’s mining heritage. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s history and show appreciation for its natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Idaho Springs

Idaho Springs is a year-round destination. The best time to visit depends on the activities you plan to do. The summer months are ideal for hiking, rafting, and other outdoor activities, while the winter months are perfect for skiing and snowboarding in the nearby resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Idaho Springs

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and culture.
  • Take advantage of the natural beauty of the town.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources.
  • Ignore safety warnings when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Idaho Springs

Like any mountain town, Idaho Springs has its share of hazards. Visitors should be aware of these risks and take precautions when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking and skiing.

Health in Idaho Springs

Altitude sickness is a common health concern for visitors to Idaho Springs due to its high altitude (7,500 feet). Visitors should take it slow for the first few days and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Getting to Idaho Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Idaho Springs is easily accessible by car or bus.

Airports Near Idaho Springs

Denver International Airport (DEN) is the closest airport to Idaho Springs, located approximately 50 miles east of the town.

Bus Stations Near Idaho Springs

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus station located in nearby Georgetown.

Other Options

Several shuttle services operate between Denver International Airport and Idaho Springs, as well as other mountain towns.

How to Get Around in Idaho Springs

The best way to get around in Idaho Springs is by car or bike. Several local businesses offer bike rentals for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Idaho Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rocky Mountain National Park

Rocky Mountain National Park is located approximately 75 miles to the northwest of Idaho Springs. The park offers scenic drives, hiking trails, and incredible views of Rocky Mountains.

Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs is located approximately 85 miles to the south of Idaho Springs. Visitors can explore Garden of the Gods, Pikes Peak, and other natural wonders.

Conclusion

Idaho Springs is a unique destination that offers a combination of outdoor activities, history, and culture. Whether you’re seeking adventure or a quiet escape, Idaho Springs has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your next adventure to this charming mountain town in the Rockies.
