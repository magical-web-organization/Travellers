Introduction to Frisco

Frisco is a historic town nestled in the heart of Colorado’s Rocky Mountains. This charming little town features stunning landscapes, outdoor recreational activities, cultural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Frisco often served as a transportation hub for miners in the late 1800s, and vestiges of that era—Victorian houses, circa 1880—are still visible.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a tour of the top attractions, a week-long itinerary for Frisco, must-try dishes and local breweries, hiking trails and biking routes, and nearby destinations and excursions, you should not miss on your next trip to Frisco.

List of Places to Visit in Frisco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Frisco boasts several cultural institutions that offer a fascinating glimpse into the town’s rich history and culture.

  1. Frisco Historic Park and Museum: This museum is situated in the heart of Frisco and depicts the rich mining and railroading heritage of the town. The museum features ten authentic buildings showcasing a wide range of stories and exhibits.

  2. Frisco Adventure Park: The park features a tubing hill, a Nordic center, and an outdoor skate park. You can spend hours of fun in the snow during winter and unparalleled hiking and biking trails in summer.

  3. Ten Mile Canyon National Recreation Trail: A 2.8-mile trail that winds through the scenic Ten Mile Canyon, this hike is ideal for those who love to explore the outdoors. The canyon is also famous for rafting, kayaking, and fly-fishing.

Natural Attractions of Frisco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Frisco offers some of the most awe-inspiring vistas to immerse yourself in nature and outdoor adventures.

  1. Dillon Reservoir: With over 3000 acres of unspoiled waters, this reservoir is ideal for boating, fishing, and paddleboarding. You can also take a scenic drive around the shoreline or hike some of the trails surrounding it.

  2. Peak 1: Standing at an elevation of 12,804 feet, Peak One offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding ranges. The trek, though more physically challenging than other trails in Frisco, is a must-do for experienced hikers.

  3. Breckenridge Ski Resort: An icon in the skiing world, Breckenridge offers a wide range of winter sports with 5 peaks, 34 lifts, and 187 ski trails. In summer, hiking, biking, and the renowned Epic Discovery Park await.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frisco

Hiking and riding are among the top-rated summer activities in Frisco, with 100-plus miles of trails and bike lanes connected from Breckenridge to Vail. Here are a few of the must-visit trails:

  1. Mount Royal Trail: A relatively strenuous 5.4-mile hike that rewards with panoramic views of Summit County from its summit of 10,502 feet.

  2. Tenmile Creek Trail: At 3.5 miles long, this easy and flat path is perfect for hikers and bikers of all levels, with tons of spots for picnics.

  3. Miners Creek Trail: This moderate 4.5-mile trail winds through dense woodland and leads you past a pristine lake and cascading waterfall for year-round exploration.

Best Things to Do in Frisco: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Frisco provides plenty of memorable things to do:

  1. Lake Dillon Theatre Company: Located at the Lakeside Bowling Center, the state’s best small-town theater offers various off-Broadway productions throughout the year.

  2. Escape Room Breckenridge: This top attraction is a great way to bond with friends and family in a hidden mysteries-filled environment.

  3. Copper Mountain Adventure Park: This family-friendly park offers an array of activities that include ziplining, mini-golf, and bungee jumping.

Week-Long Itinerary for Frisco

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Frisco that covers all the essential hotspots:

Day 1: Breckenridge Ski Resort

Day 2: Frisco Historic Park and Museum

Day 3: biking the Dillon Reservoir Path and exploring the Summit Marina

Day 4: A hike to the top of Peak One

Day 5: Kayaking or fly-fishing on the Ten Mile Canyon National Recreation Trail

Day 6: Whitewater rafting on Blue River

Day 7: Relishing the adventure-filled Copper Mountain Adventure Park

Food and Drink Scene of Frisco

Must-Try Dishes

Copper Mountain is a culinary hub with a variety of options for food lovers. Here are some of the best dishes:

  1. The Lost Cajun: Seafood platters, gumbo, and po-boys; this Louisiana-style eatery has it all.

  2. Greco’s Pastaria: Greco’s show-stealer is the homemade pasta served with meatballs and basil marinara.

  3. Butterhorn Bakery & Cafe: People rave about the artisanal bread and cinnamon rolls; they’re perfection.

Local Beverages

There are several breweries, bars, and wineries in Frisco. Here are a few of the best local drink makers and shakers that must get on your list:

  1. Highside Brewery: Highside specializes in hop-forward craft beers with a great hangout atmosphere.

  2. Frisco Wine Merchant: This wine boutique has a knowledgeable staff and an exhaustive collection of handpicked wines from top-notch vineyards worldwide.

Where to Stay in Frisco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation options, Frisco hosts a range of choices:

  1. The Lodge at Breckenridge: This cozy boutique hotel is situated on a hill overlooking Breckenridge with a charming outdoor hot tub overlooking the Rocky Mountains.

  2. Frisco Inn on Galena: This romantically designed mountain lodge has an extensive complimentary breakfast and fine dining options.

What to Buy in Frisco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Colorado’s signature gift culture is full of handmade and unique crafts. Here are some of the authentic and creative Colorado products that are famous in Frisco:

  1. Ski Company’s Ullr Shot Ski: A novelty feature known for its seasonal uniqueness, the Ski Company’s Ullr Shot Ski holds the Guinness World Record for the world’s longest shot ski.

  2. Mountain goat sculpture: These crafted wooden mountain-goats are a signature souvenir of Frisco, Colorado, and make for perfect little keepsakes of your trip here.

Local Culture and Customs of Frisco

Colorado’s laidback mountaintop culture is defined by music, art, food, and outdoor adventures. The locals are friendly, and you can always count on heaps of genuine good timin’ in Frisco.

Best Time to Visit Frisco

The best time to visit Frisco is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities. If you are more inclined towards winter sports, the peak ski season of December to March is the ideal season for you.

Do’s and Don’ts of Frisco

Do:

  1. Do try your hand at skiing/boarding or other winter sports on the frigid slopes of the Rockies.

  2. Do indulge yourself in local food and beverage culture, whether it’s beer tasting, apple picking, or culinary events.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to take plenty of water and snacks for your outdoor trip.

  2. Don’t underestimate the amount of sunscreen or hat you need when hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Frisco

Colorado is typically a safe place to travel, and both police and emergency services are easily accessible. While all outdoor activities involve an element of risk, it’s important to exercise caution in open landscapes.

Health in Frisco

Drinking water in Frisco is safe, so don’t worry about getting dehydrated. The high-altitude mountain terrain can make breathing difficult for some; be aware of the symptoms of altitude sickness and, if necessary, make sure you ascend a mountain at a slow and safe pace.

Getting to Frisco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Frisco

  1. Eagle Vail Regional Airport - 54 miles away.

  2. Denver International Airport - 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Frisco

  1. Breckenridge Station - a 20-minute ride by bus.

  2. Vail Transportation Center - a 40-minute ride by bus.

Other Options

A car rental offers the best flexibility and convenience to reach Frisco and explore the area at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Frisco

Walking or biking Frisco’s streets is a great way to experience the sights and sounds of this historic mountain town. If you have heavy gear or wine bottles to tote back, shuttle services are available to your hotel or rental.

Where to Go Next from Frisco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

By the time you’re ready to leave Frisco, you’ll likely be ready for another adventure in the Rockies. Here are a few destinations worth exploring:

  1. Breckenridge: A bustling winter town known for skiing and snowboarding.

  2. Vail: A stunning year-round destination that attracts hikers, skiers, and sightseers.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive guide has given you a glimpse of what Frisco has to offer. Whether you’re seeking adventure sports, cultural and natural attractions, fine dining, arts, and culture, Colorado’s gem of Frisco is a must-visit destination.
