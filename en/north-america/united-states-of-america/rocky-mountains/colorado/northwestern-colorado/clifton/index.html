Introduction to Clifton

Nestled in Northwestern Colorado, Clifton is a small town located in the heart of the Rocky Mountains. It is a charming destination that promises a great mix of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and scenic views. Clifton is a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and get closer to nature. This travel guide provides an in-depth look into what Clifton has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Clifton

Whether you are a history buff, an adventurer, or a nature lover, there is something for everyone in Clifton. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museums of Western Colorado: This museum features exhibits on the region’s natural and cultural history, including artifacts from the Paleolithic era and items from the Native American tribes who called the area home.

  • Dinosaur Journey Museum: A must-visit for any dinosaur enthusiast, this museum boasts an extensive collection of dinosaur skeletons, including a T-rex and a Stegosaurus.

  • Cross Orchards Historic Site: This historic site contains beautifully preserved buildings and artifacts from the early 20th century, showcasing what life was like for early settlers in the area.

Natural Attractions of Clifton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colorado National Monument: This national monument is a breathtaking destination that offers sweeping views of the canyonlands and red rock formations of the Colorado Plateau. There are numerous hiking trails and picnic areas throughout the park.

  • Grand Mesa: The world’s largest flat-topped mountain offers some of the most scenic views in Colorado. There are a variety of outdoor activities to enjoy on Grand Mesa, including hiking, fishing, and skiing.

  • Palisade Peach Festival: If you are visiting Clifton in August, be sure to check out the famous Palisade Peach Festival. This celebration of the area’s famous orchards includes live music, food and drink vendors, and of course, plenty of sweet peaches to sample.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clifton

Clifton is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with many hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Devil’s Canyon Trail: This 3.2-mile out-and-back trail offers stunning views of the canyon and is one of the best places to spot wildlife in the area.

  • Lunch Loop Trail System: This trail system has over 36 miles of trails for hiking and biking. It offers stunning views of the Grand Valley and the Colorado National Monument.

  • Palisade Rim Trail: A popular hiking and biking trail, the Palisade Rim Trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The trail is relatively steep but well worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Clifton: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Clifton. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Wine Tours: Clifton is known for its vineyards and wineries, so be sure to take a wine tour and sample some of the area’s best wines.

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: While not technically in Clifton, the nearby Rocky Mountain National Park is a must-visit destination. With its stunning mountain vistas, snow-capped peaks, and hiking trails, it is a nature lover’s paradise.

  • Whitewater Rafting: For a bit of adventure, try whitewater rafting on the Colorado River. There are several tour companies available in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clifton

If you have a week to explore Clifton and the surrounding areas, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Museums of Western Colorado and the Dinosaur Journey Museum.

  • Day 2: Explore the Colorado National Monument.

  • Day 3: Take a wine tour of the area’s vineyards and wineries.

  • Day 4: Hike the Lunch Loop Trail System.

  • Day 5: Whitewater rafting on the Colorado River.

  • Day 6: Relax in Palisade and sample some of the area’s famous peaches.

  • Day 7: Explore Rocky Mountain National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Clifton

Clifton is known for its fresh produce and delicious wines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colorado Beef: Colorado is known for its high-quality beef, and Clifton is no exception. Be sure to try a steak from one of the local restaurants.

  • Palisade Peaches: No trip to Clifton is complete without sampling the area’s famous peaches.

  • Rocky Ford Melons: This sweet, juicy melon is grown in nearby Rocky Ford and is a must-try for anyone visiting the area.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado Wine: Clifton is home to many vineyards and wineries, so be sure to try some of the local wines.

  • Palisade Cider: Made from locally grown apples, Palisade cider is a refreshing drink that pairs well with food.

Where to Stay in Clifton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clifton has a variety of options for accommodations, ranging from hotels to guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • SpringHill Suites Grand Junction Downtown/Historic Main Street: This hotel is located in nearby Grand Junction and offers spacious rooms and a great location.

  • Wine Country Inn: Located in Palisade, this charming inn offers beautiful views of the surrounding vineyards and orchards.

  • Dreamcatcher Bed and Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast is a great option for those looking for a more intimate and personalized experience.

What to Buy in Clifton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Palisade Peaches: Take home some of the area’s famous peaches to share with friends and family.

  • Local Wine: Bring home a bottle (or several) of the area’s delicious wines.

  • Handmade Crafts: Check out the local shops and markets for unique, locally made crafts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Clifton

Clifton is a small, close-knit community that values its traditions and history. Visitors are welcome to explore the local culture and customs, but should also be respectful of the area’s traditions and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Clifton

The best time to visit Clifton is during the summer months from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the natural attractions in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clifton

Do:

  • Do explore the area’s natural attractions and outdoor activities.

  • Do try some of the local wines and fresh produce.

  • Do respect the area’s local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or traditions.

  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clifton

Clifton is generally a safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Be sure to lock your doors and windows and keep valuables out of view. When exploring the natural areas, be mindful of the wildlife and follow all posted safety guidelines.

Health in Clifton

Clifton has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics in the area. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them and have adequate medical insurance coverage.

Getting to Clifton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clifton is easily accessible by car, with Interstate 70 passing through the area. Visitors can also fly into the Grand Junction Regional Airport, which is located about 10 miles from Clifton. There are several shuttle services available from the airport to Clifton and the surrounding areas.

Airports Near Clifton

  • Grand Junction Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Clifton

  • Greyhound: Grand Junction Bus Station

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Grand Junction Station

How to Get Around in Clifton

The best way to get around Clifton is by car. However, there are also several bike rental companies in the area for those who prefer to explore on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Clifton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Moab, Utah: Just a short drive from Clifton, Moab is a popular destination known for its stunning red rock formations and outdoor activities.

  • Glenwood Springs: Located about an hour’s drive from Clifton, Glenwood Springs is home to many natural hot springs and is a great place to relax and unwind.

Conclusion

Clifton is a charming destination that offers a great mix of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and scenic views. Whether you are looking to explore the natural world or soak up some local culture, Clifton is sure to delight.
