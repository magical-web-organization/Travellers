Introduction to Mancos

Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Mancos is a charming town that offers a unique mix of history and natural beauty. Located in southwestern Colorado, Mancos is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

Founded in the late 1800s, Mancos has a rich history that is still visible in the town’s many historic buildings and landmarks. Visitors can explore museums, art galleries, and historical sites, or simply take a stroll through the town’s quaint streets.

But Mancos is more than just a historic town. The surrounding area is home to some of Colorado’s most stunning natural beauty. From towering mountains to crystal-clear lakes, there is something for everyone here.

In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Mancos.

List of Places to Visit in Mancos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mancos Opera House: Built in 1910, the Mancos Opera House is one of the town’s most important landmarks. Today, it hosts concerts, theater performances, and other cultural events.
  • Mancos Valley Historical Society Museum: This museum offers a fascinating look at the history of Mancos and the surrounding area, with exhibits on agriculture, mining, and Native American culture.
  • Spruce Tree House: Located in nearby Mesa Verde National Park, this well-preserved cliff dwelling is a must-see for anyone interested in Native American history.

Natural Attractions of Mancos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mesa Verde National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to over 5,000 archaeological sites, including some of the best-preserved cliff dwellings in the world.
  • La Plata Mountains: Located just outside of Mancos, these majestic mountains offer some of the best hiking and mountain biking trails in the area.
  • Jackson Gulch Reservoir: This scenic reservoir is perfect for fishing, boating, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mancos

  • Chicken Creek Trail: This 7-mile trail winds through the La Plata Mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Mesa Verde National Park Trails: The park offers a variety of hiking trails, ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes with steep ascents.
  • Kennebec Pass Trail: This beautiful trail takes hikers up to the summit of the La Plata Mountains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Mancos: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take in the stunning scenery of Mancos from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the rugged Colorado wilderness on horseback with one of the area’s many guided tours.
  • Whitewater Rafting: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Dolores River.

Food and Drink Scene of Mancos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chili: A Colorado favorite, this hearty stew features tender pork cooked in a spicy sauce made with green chilies.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: Not for the faint of heart, Rocky Mountain Oysters are actually fried bull testicles – a unique delicacy that is surprisingly tasty.
  • Wild Game: From elk to bison, Mancos is known for its delicious, locally-sourced game meats.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Mancos is home to several acclaimed craft breweries, including Mancos Brewing Company and Fahrenheit 212 Brewing Company.
  • Colorado Wine: Colorado is home to a growing wine industry, and many of the state’s best wineries and vineyards can be found in and around Mancos.

Where to Stay in Mancos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mancos Inn & Hostel: Located in the heart of town, this charming inn offers comfortable rooms and hostel-style accommodations.
  • Echo Basin Ranch: This historic guest ranch offers a range of accommodations, from cozy cabins to spacious lodges.
  • Mesa Verde RV Resort: For those traveling by RV, this resort offers spacious campsites and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Mancos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Crafts: Mancos and the surrounding area are home to several Native American tribes, and visitors can find a variety of authentic crafts and art in the town’s many galleries and shops.
  • Local Honey: Mancos is known for its delicious, locally-sourced honey – a perfect souvenir for foodies.
  • Handmade Soaps: Many of the town’s shops offer artisanal soaps and other bath products, made with natural ingredients and scented with local herbs and flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Mancos

Mancos has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in the town’s events and traditions. From the Mancos Valley Balloon Festival to the Four Corners Folk Festival, there are many ways to experience the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Mancos

The best time to visit Mancos is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in weather, as the region is known for its unpredictable climate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mancos

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the town’s many galleries and shops.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.
  • Do take advantage of the area’s many outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to rafting and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the area’s natural beauty.
  • Don’t underestimate the power of the sun – be sure to wear sunscreen and bring extra water on outdoor excursions.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers and guides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mancos

Mancos is a safe town, with low levels of crime. Visitors should take normal precautions when traveling, such as locking their cars and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Mancos

Mancos is a remote area, and visitors should be prepared for limited medical services. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications and to have comprehensive travel insurance.

Getting to Mancos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mancos

The nearest airports to Mancos are Durango-La Plata County Airport (DRO) and Cortez Municipal Airport (CEZ). Both offer flights from major U.S. cities.

Bus Stations Near Mancos

Greyhound offers bus service to Durango, which is located approximately 25 miles from Mancos.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to rent a car and drive to Mancos, as the area’s roads offer stunning scenery and easy access to nearby attractions.

How to Get Around in Mancos

Mancos is a small town, and most attractions are within easy walking or biking distance. However, visitors who want to explore the surrounding area should consider renting a car.

Where to Go Next from Mancos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Durango: This historic town is located just a short drive from Mancos and offers a range of attractions, from art galleries to outdoor activities.
  • Telluride: This famous ski resort offers world-class skiing and snowboarding in the winter, as well as hiking and mountain biking in the summer.
  • Arches National Park: Located just across the border in Utah, this stunning park is home to over 2,000 natural sandstone arches.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the rich history and culture of Colorado or simply want to experience the beauty of the Rocky Mountains, Mancos is a destination that should not be missed. With its stunning natural scenery, unique cultural attractions, and friendly locals, Mancos offers something for everyone. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready for an unforgettable trip to one of Colorado’s most beautiful towns.
