Introduction to Silverton

Nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, Silverton is a charming small town in southwestern Colorado. Known for its rich mining history, impressive natural beauty, and unique local culture, Silverton is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Colorado experience.

Surrounded by towering peaks and lush forests, Silverton offers visitors a wide array of outdoor activities and adventures to enjoy throughout the year. Whether you’re hiking to scenic vistas, exploring historic mining sites, or simply relaxing and taking in the stunning scenery, there’s never a shortage of things to see and do in Silverton.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Silverton, as well as important travel tips to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Silverton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Juan County Historical Society - Learn about Silverton’s rich mining history and explore a variety of interactive exhibits at this fascinating museum.
  • Mayflower Gold Mill - Take a tour of this historic 1920s gold mill and learn about the process of extracting precious metals from the mountains.
  • Old Hundred Gold Mine Tour - Experience what it was like to work in a 19th-century gold mine with this fascinating underground tour.

Natural Attractions of Silverton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ice Lakes Trail - This stunning hike takes you to the turquoise-colored Ice Lakes, surrounded by towering peaks and alpine wildflowers.
  • Kendall Mountain - A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, Kendall Mountain offers a variety of hiking and biking trails in the summer, and skiing and snowboarding in the winter.
  • Animas River Trail - Take in the natural beauty of the Animas River on this scenic walking and biking path that winds through Silverton’s historic downtown.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silverton

Silverton and its surrounding wilderness offer some of the best hiking and mountain biking trails in Colorado. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Engineer Mountain Trail - A challenging but rewarding hike, this 7.7-mile route takes you to the summit of Engineer Mountain, with stunning views of the surrounding peaks and valleys.
  • Molas Trail to Highland Mary Lakes - This moderate 6.8-mile hike takes you through lush forests and past stunning alpine lakes.
  • The Colorado Trail - This 500-mile trail winds through some of Colorado’s most scenic wilderness areas, including a section that passes near Silverton.

Best Things to Do in Silverton: Activities and Tours

  • Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad - Take a historic train ride through San Juan National Forest and experience the beauty of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Jeep Tours - Explore the rugged wilderness around Silverton with a guided jeep tour, with options for exploring mining sites and ghost towns, as well as taking in stunning natural scenery.
  • Rock Climbing and Mountaineering - With its towering peaks and dramatic landscapes, Silverton is the perfect destination for rock climbing and mountaineering adventures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Silverton

Day 1: Explore historic downtown Silverton, including the San Juan County Historical Society and Mayflower Gold Mill.

Day 2: Take a scenic hike to Ice Lakes and enjoy lunch with a view of the surrounding peaks.

Day 3: Explore the rugged wilderness around Silverton with a guided jeep tour.

Day 4: Take a train ride on the Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad through San Juan National Forest.

Day 5: Spend the day rock climbing or mountaineering in the nearby peaks.

Day 6: Relax and take in the natural beauty of the Animas River on a leisurely walk or bike ride.

Day 7: Take a tour of the Old Hundred Gold Mine and learn about the area’s rich mining history.

Food and Drink Scene of Silverton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger - Made with locally sourced bison, this hearty burger is a staple of Colorado cuisine.
  • Green Chile - A spicy and savory stew made with pork or beef, potatoes, and plenty of green chiles, a signature ingredient of Southwest cuisine.
  • Mountain Trout - Fresh-caught and pan-seared, trout is a delicious and healthy option for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado Craft Beer - With dozens of local breweries, Colorado is famous for its craft beer scene. Check out Avalanche Brewing Company, located in downtown Silverton, for some locally brewed beer.
  • High-Altitude Wines - Though not as well known as Colorado’s craft beer scene, the state’s high-altitude wineries produce some excellent wines. Try a bottle from the Sutcliffe Vineyards, just a short drive from Silverton.

Where to Stay in Silverton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Triangle Motel - A classic roadside motel with clean, comfortable rooms and affordable rates.
  • The Inn of the Rockies at the Historic Alma House - A charming Victorian-era bed and breakfast with affordable rates.

Mid-Range Options

  • Grand Imperial Hotel - A historic hotel in the heart of downtown Silverton, with spacious rooms and a restaurant and bar on site.
  • Wyman Hotel and Inn - A luxury bed and breakfast with elegant rooms and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Luxury Options

  • The Inn at Lost Creek - A luxury hotel in nearby Telluride, with 32 spacious suites and easy access to outdoor activities and dining.
  • Dunton Hot Springs - A luxurious adults-only retreat, with beautifully appointed cabins and access to natural hot springs.

What to Buy in Silverton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewelry and Crafts - Silverton is home to a variety of local artisans specializing in unique jewelry, pottery, and other crafts.
  • Locally Made Hot Sauces and Salsas - Spicy food lovers will enjoy sampling the wide array of locally made hot sauces and salsas available in Silverton.

Local Culture and Customs of Silverton

Silverton has a rich local culture, with a focus on preserving the area’s mining heritage and appreciating its stunning natural beauty. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local environment and support local businesses during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Silverton

Summer (June-August) is the most popular time to visit Silverton, with warm weather and plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. Fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit, with beautiful foliage and fewer crowds. Winter (December-February) is a popular destination for skiing and snowboarding, while spring (March-May) offers milder weather and blooming wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silverton

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs, including preserving historic sites and supporting local businesses.
  • Take advantage of the area’s outdoor activities and stunning natural scenery.
  • Try the local cuisine, including bison burgers, green chile, and fresh-caught trout.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore safety warnings and regulations when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local residents or their property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Silverton

Silverton is a safe destination for visitors, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Silverton

Visitors to Silverton should be prepared for high altitudes and rugged terrain. It’s important to stay hydrated and take necessary precautions to prevent altitude sickness.

Getting to Silverton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Silverton

The nearest airport to Silverton is in Durango, approximately 50 miles south of the town.

Bus Stations Near Silverton

Greyhound buses stop in Durango, with connections to Silverton available via shuttle or rental car.

Other Options

Driving is the most popular way to reach Silverton, with major highways leading to the town from both the north and south.

How to Get Around in Silverton

The best way to get around Silverton is by car or on foot. Parking is plentiful in most areas, and many of the town’s attractions and restaurants are within easy walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Silverton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Durango - A historic town with a variety of outdoor activities and attractions, including the Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad.
  • Mesa Verde National Park - Home to stunning cliff-dwellings and other ancient ruins, Mesa Verde is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Native American history.
  • Telluride - A beautiful mountain town with a wide array of outdoor activities, dining options, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Silverton is a unique and charming destination, with something for everyone to enjoy. From its rich mining history and stunning natural beauty to its unique local culture and mouth-watering cuisine, Silverton is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Colorado. Plan your trip today and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!
