Introduction to Southwestern Colorado

Southwestern Colorado, located in the Rocky Mountains in the United States of America, is a mesmerizing blend of rich culture and stunning natural beauty. This region offers something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts seeking adventure to history buffs looking to explore the cultural heritage of the area. Southwestern Colorado is home to several towns, each with its own distinct character and attractions. In this guide, we will take a closer look at the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in this breathtaking region.

List of Places to Visit in Southwestern Colorado

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southwestern Colorado is home to several museums and historical sites that are worth exploring. Some notable places are:

  • Mesa Verde National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage site is home to some of the best-preserved Native American cliff dwellings in the world.
  • Durango and Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad Museum: This museum celebrates the history of the Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad and is a great place to learn about the development of rail travel in the region.
  • Anasazi Heritage Center: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Ancestral Pueblo people, who inhabited this region for centuries.
  • Ute Indian Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the culture and traditions of the Ute Native Americans who have called this region their home for thousands of years.

Natural Attractions of Southwestern Colorado: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Southwestern Colorado offers a plethora of adventure activities and stunning views. Some of the top natural attractions are:

  • San Juan Mountains: This mountain range offers some of the most spectacular landscapes and hiking trails in the United States.
  • Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park: This stunning canyon features steep cliffs, narrow gorges, and breathtaking vistas.
  • Great Sand Dunes National Park and Preserve: This unique park offers towering sand dunes that are a must-see for any traveler.
  • Telluride: This small mountain town offers year-round outdoor activities, such as skiing, mountain biking, and hiking, and stunning views of the surrounding peaks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southwestern Colorado

Southwestern Colorado is a hidden gem for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking:

  • Bear Creek Trail: Located in Telluride, this 2.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the town and surrounding mountains.
  • Jud Weibe Trail: This 3.5-mile loop trail in Telluride offers stunning views of the San Juan Mountains.
  • Alta Lakes Trail: Located in Alta Lakes, this 3-mile hike offers breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks and alpine lakes.
  • Lizard Head Pass Trail: This 8-mile trail features stunning wildflowers, waterfalls, and majestic peaks.

Best Things to Do in Southwestern Colorado: Activities and Tours

Southwestern Colorado offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some must-do activities:

  • Jeep Tour: Explore the rugged terrain and scenic views of Southwestern Colorado on a Jeep tour.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Get a bird’s eye view of the stunning landscapes of Southwestern Colorado on a hot air balloon ride.
  • Whitewater Rafting: Take an exhilarating whitewater rafting trip down the Animas River, located in Durango.
  • Skiing/Snowboarding: Enjoy world-class skiing and snowboarding in the winter months at Telluride Ski Resort.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southwestern Colorado

To make the most of your trip to Southwestern Colorado, consider following this itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Durango and its historical sites, like Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad Museum.
  • Day 3-4: Explore Mesa Verde National Park and its ancient cliff dwellings.
  • Day 5-6: Visit Telluride and enjoy the scenic views, mountain biking, and hiking.
  • Day 7: Take a hot air balloon ride or a Jeep tour to see the region from a unique perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Southwestern Colorado

Must-Try Dishes

Southwestern Colorado has a unique food scene that combines traditional Native American dishes with Western flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Green Chile Stew: This spicy stew is made with roasted green chilies, pork, and potatoes.
  • Navajo Tacos: These savory tacos are made with fry bread, ground beef, beans, and cheese.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: Also known as prairie oysters or cowboy caviar, this delicacy is made from fried bull testicles.

Local Beverages

Southwestern Colorado is also home to several local breweries and wineries. Here are some of the must-try local beverages:

  • Ska Brewing Company: Located in Durango, this brewery offers a wide range of craft beers.
  • Telluride Brewing Company: This popular brewery in Telluride offers a variety of beers, including IPAs and stouts.
  • Four Leaves Winery: This winery in Durango offers locally-made wines, including reds, whites, and dessert wines.

Where to Stay in Southwestern Colorado: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southwestern Colorado offers a range of accommodations for visitors. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, here are some of the top places to stay in the region:

  • The Strater Hotel: This historic hotel in Durango offers Victorian-era accommodations with modern amenities.
  • Bear Creek Lodge: This lodge in Telluride offers spacious rooms, hot tubs, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Mancos Inn & Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel in Mancos offers shared bunk rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Southwestern Colorado: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southwestern Colorado is known for its local crafts and products. Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs:

  • Navajo Rugs: These handmade rugs are made by the Navajo people and are a beautiful and unique addition to any home.
  • Silver and Turquoise Jewelry: Southwestern Colorado is known for its traditional silver and turquoise jewelry, which is often made by local artisans.
  • Pottery: Native American pottery is another must-buy souvenir. Look for pieces made by local artists in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Southwestern Colorado

Southwestern Colorado has a rich Native American heritage and is home to several tribes, including the Navajo, Ute, and Apache. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. It is also common to see locals wearing traditional cowboy hats and boots, as the region has a long history of ranching and cattle drives.

Best Time to Visit Southwestern Colorado

The best time to visit Southwestern Colorado is in the summer, from June to August, as the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The fall months are also a great time to visit, as the foliage turns to vibrant shades of red and gold. Winter months are ideal for those who love skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southwestern Colorado

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Plan ahead and bring appropriate gear for outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Disrupt wildlife or disturb natural habitats.
  • Ignore safety warnings or advisories.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southwestern Colorado

Southwestern Colorado is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, it is important to take precautions while hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of wildfires during the summer months.

Health in Southwestern Colorado

Southwestern Colorado is located at a high altitude, which can cause altitude sickness in some visitors. It is important to stay hydrated and acclimatize slowly if you are traveling from lower elevations. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of ticks and other pests while hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Southwestern Colorado: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southwestern Colorado

The closest airports to Southwestern Colorado are:

  • Durango La Plata County Airport: Located in Durango, this airport is served by several major airlines.
  • Montrose Regional Airport: Located in Montrose, this airport is a larger hub that offers flights to several major cities.

Bus Stations Near Southwestern Colorado

Greyhound operates a bus route that runs through Southwestern Colorado. The closest stations are in Cortez and Durango.

Other Options

A car is the most convenient way to explore Southwestern Colorado. Visitors can also rent a car or take a guided tour of the region.

How to Get Around in Southwestern Colorado

Several transportation options are available for getting around Southwestern Colorado, including:

  • Renting a Car: Renting a car in Southwestern Colorado is the most convenient way to explore the region, with several major car rental companies offering services in the area.
  • Public Transportation: Several local bus services operate in the region, including the Telluride and Mountain Village Gondola.
  • Taxis and Rideshares: Taxis and rideshare services, like Uber and Lyft, are available in larger towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Southwestern Colorado: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southwestern Colorado is just one part of the larger Rocky Mountains region, which offers a range of nearby destinations and excursions. Some great places to visit after Southwestern Colorado include:

  • Santa Fe, New Mexico: This charming town is known for its vibrant art scene, historic adobe architecture, and delicious cuisine.
  • Denver, Colorado: The capital city of Colorado offers a range of cultural attractions, including museums, theaters, and dining options.
  • Salt Lake City, Utah: This city offers stunning natural beauty, a bustling city center, and a thriving arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Southwestern Colorado is a hidden gem that offers a blend of rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, this region has something to offer. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Southwestern Colorado.
