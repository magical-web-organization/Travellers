Introduction to Pagosa Springs

Pagosa Springs is a picturesque town located in Southwestern Colorado, known for its natural hot springs, stunning scenery, and unparalleled outdoor recreation opportunities. The town is surrounded by the San Juan National Forest and the Weminuche Wilderness, offering some of the best hiking, biking, skiing, and fishing in the state. Whether you prefer outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Pagosa Springs has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Pagosa Springs

Here’s a list of the must-visit places in Pagosa Springs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chimney Rock National Monument: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that features stunning archaeological sites and rock formations.
  • San Juan Historical Museum: An excellent place to learn about the history and culture of Pagosa Springs and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Pagosa Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pagosa Hot Springs: A natural hot springs that offers a relaxing soak after a day of adventure.
  • Wolf Creek Ski Area: An excellent destination for skiing and snowboarding, offering some of the best snow conditions in the state.
  • Treasure Falls: A stunning waterfall that’s easily accessible, making it a great spot for family outings.
  • Pagosa Springs Trail System: A network of hiking and biking trails that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Piedra River Trail: An off-the-beaten-track hiking trail that follows the Piedra River and offers some of the best views of the San Juan Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pagosa Springs

Pagosa Springs is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and its many hiking and biking trails offer plenty of opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the area. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Pagosa Springs:

  • The Continental Divide Trail: A long-distance hiking trail that runs through Pagosa Springs and offers breathtaking views of the San Juan Mountains.
  • Reservoir Hill Trail: A great place for a family hike, offering scenic views of Pagosa Springs and the surrounding mountains.
  • Turkey Springs Trail: A popular mountain biking trail that offers a fun and challenging ride through the forest.

Best Things to Do in Pagosa Springs: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing soak in the hot springs or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, Pagosa Springs has plenty of activities to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Pagosa Springs:

  • Soak in the hot springs: Pagosa Hot Springs is one of the main attractions of Pagosa Springs, offering a relaxing soak in natural hot springs.
  • Horseback riding: Explore the beautiful mountainous scenery on horseback with one of the many horseback riding tours available in the area.
  • Skiing and snowboarding: Wolf Creek Ski Area offers some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the state, with excellent snow conditions and stunning views.
  • Rafting and kayaking: The San Juan River offers some great rapids for rafting and kayaking.
  • Hot air balloon rides: Get a bird’s eye view of Pagosa Springs and the surrounding mountains with a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pagosa Springs

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Pagosa Springs:

Day 1 Visit Chimney Rock National Monument

Food and Drink Scene of Pagosa Springs

Pagosa Springs has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of delicious local cuisine to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green chili: A Colorado staple, green chili is a delicious and spicy dish made with roasted green chilies, pork, and other spices.
  • Rocky Mountain oysters: A unique delicacy made from bull testicles, often breaded and fried.
  • Bison burger: A healthy and flavorful alternative to traditional beef burgers, bison burgers are a popular choice in Pagosa Springs.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Pagosa Springs is home to several excellent craft breweries, such as Pagosa Brewing Company and Riff Raff Brewing Company.
  • Colorado wine: While Colorado isn’t typically known for wine, the state has a rapidly growing wine industry and several wineries located near Pagosa Springs.

Where to Stay in Pagosa Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pagosa Springs has a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Springs Resort & Spa: A luxurious hot springs resort with stunning views of the mountains.
  • The First Inn of Pagosa: A charming bed and breakfast in the heart of Pagosa Springs.
  • Pagosa Lodge: A rustic lodge located just outside of town, offering easy access to outdoor recreation.
  • Aloha Chalet: A cozy and colorful guesthouse located on the San Juan River.

What to Buy in Pagosa Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pagosa Springs is home to several unique boutiques and shops, offering a range of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the best places to shop in Pagosa Springs:

  • Lantern Dancer: A charming boutique that offers unique gifts, jewelry, and clothing inspired by Native American cultures.
  • Made in Colorado: A shop that features artisanal Colorado-made products, including pottery, woodworking, and gourmet food items.
  • R U Flying?: A gift shop that specializes in kites, windsocks, and other toys that are perfect for kids and kids at heart.

Local Culture and Customs of Pagosa Springs

Pagosa Springs is a diverse community with a rich history and culture. The town is home to many Native American tribes, and their customs and traditions are still celebrated today. Visitors should be respectful of these traditions and take the time to learn about the local culture during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Pagosa Springs

The best time to visit Pagosa Springs depends on your preferences. Here’s what you can expect during each season:

  • Spring: Mild temperatures, occasional rain, and beautiful wildflowers.
  • Summer: Warm temperatures, lots of sunshine, and plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Fall: Cool temperatures, stunning fall foliage, and fewer crowds.
  • Winter: Cold temperatures, plenty of snow, and excellent skiing and snowboarding conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pagosa Springs

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Pagosa Springs:

Do:

  • Check the weather before you go hiking or biking.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Take your time to enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on the trails or in the hot springs.
  • Overlook the importance of sunscreen and bug spray.
  • Ignore the warnings of local authorities regarding trail closures or natural hazards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pagosa Springs

Overall, Pagosa Springs is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking their vehicles, keeping an eye on their belongings, and avoiding dark or isolated areas at night.

Health in Pagosa Springs

The high altitude and dry climate of Pagosa Springs can be challenging for some visitors. Visitors should stay hydrated, take frequent breaks, and be aware of the symptoms of altitude sickness.

Getting to Pagosa Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the best ways to get to Pagosa Springs:

Airports Near Pagosa Springs

  • Durango-La Plata County Airport (DRO): Located about 60 miles southwest of Pagosa Springs, DRO is the closest major airport.
  • Albuquerque International Sunport (ABQ): Located about 200 miles south of Pagosa Springs, ABQ is another option for visitors.

Bus Stations Near Pagosa Springs

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Durango: Offers connections to other cities in Colorado, as well as New Mexico and Arizona.

Other Options

  • Driving: Pagosa Springs is located about 5 hours from Denver, and driving is a great way to see the scenic beauty of Colorado.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at any of the nearby airports or in Durango.

How to Get Around in Pagosa Springs

The best way to get around Pagosa Springs is by car. Visitors can rent a car at any of the nearby airports or in Durango. Additionally, some hotels and resorts offer shuttle services or bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Pagosa Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the area around Pagosa Springs, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Mesa Verde National Park: A stunning national park located about 2.5 hours south of Pagosa Springs, with incredible archaeological sites and cliff dwellings.
  • Durango: A charming town located about 60 miles southwest of Pagosa Springs, with great restaurants, shopping, and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Santa Fe: A beautiful city located about 200 miles south of Pagosa Springs, known for its stunning architecture, art galleries, and museums.

Conclusion

Pagosa Springs is a hidden gem in Southwest Colorado, offering something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply a relaxing soak in natural hot springs, Pagosa Springs is the perfect destination. Plan your trip now and experience the natural beauty and rich culture of this beautiful town.
