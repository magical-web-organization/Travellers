Introduction to Delta

Located in the southwestern part of Colorado, Delta is a charming town that offers visitors an array of cultural, historical, and outdoor experiences. It is nestled in the Rocky Mountains, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. Delta is a small town, but it has a lot to offer to its visitors. In this guide, we will explore the various attractions and activities that make Delta a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Delta

Delta has a plethora of attractions that are worth visiting. Here are some of the must-visit places that you should add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Uncompahgre: Fort Uncompahgre is a reconstruction of the original fur trading post built in 1828. It is a living history museum that gives visitors a glimpse into the lives of the trappers and traders who lived and worked in the area.
  • Delta County Historical Society: The Delta County Historical Society has a museum that showcases the local history, including mining artifacts and memorabilia related to the old narrow-gauge railroads.
  • Grand Mesa Scenic and Historic Byway: This scenic drive offers stunning views of the Grand Mesa, the largest flat-topped mountain in the world.

Natural Attractions of Delta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park: Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park is a must-visit for nature lovers. It features breathtaking views of the canyon, hiking trails, and fishing opportunities in the Gunnison River.
  • Grand Mesa National Forest: The Grand Mesa National Forest is home to over 300 lakes and reservoirs, as well as a variety of wildlife. It provides opportunities for hiking, camping, fishing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Escalante Canyon: Escalante Canyon is a hidden gem in Delta that offers stunning views of towering rock formations, hiking trails, and rock climbing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delta

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Delta. There are many trails in the area that provide excellent opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes that you can explore:

  • Crag Crest Trail: This trail takes you on a scenic hike around the Crag Crest Loop, which offers stunning views of the Grand Mesa National Forest.
  • Tabeguache Trail: Tabeguache Trail is a challenging mountain biking trail that runs along the Uncompahgre Plateau.
  • Riggs Hill Trail: Riggs Hill Trail is a short hike that takes you to the top of Riggs Hill, where you can enjoy panoramic views of Delta.

Best Things to Do in Delta: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours that you can enjoy in Delta. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Horseback Riding: Many local ranches offer horseback riding tours that allow you to explore the area’s stunning landscapes.
  • Fishing: The Gunnison River offers plenty of fishing opportunities for anglers of all skill levels.
  • Winery Tours: Delta is home to several wineries that offer tours and wine tastings, allowing you to experience the flavors of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delta

If you are planning a week-long trip to Delta, here is a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the town of Delta and visit local shops and restaurants
Day 2 Visit Fort Uncompahgre and the Delta County Historical Society
Day 3 Hike the Crag Crest Trail
Day 4 Go fishing in the Gunnison River or take a horseback riding tour
Day 5 Drive the Grand Mesa Scenic and Historic Byway
Day 6 Visit Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park
Day 7 Take a winery tour and enjoy local cuisine at a restaurant

Food and Drink Scene of Delta

Delta is known for its local cuisine and wineries. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: While not for everyone, Rocky Mountain Oysters are a local delicacy that you can find at many restaurants in Delta. They are fried bull testicles and are said to taste like chicken.
  • Wild Game: Delta has many restaurants that serve wild game, including elk, deer, and bison, making it a perfect destination for meat lovers.
  • Colorado Peach Pie: Delta is known for its delicious Colorado peaches, and there is no better way to enjoy them than in a pie.

Local Beverages

  • Colorado Wine: Delta is home to several wineries that offer tasting rooms and tours.
  • Colorado Craft Beer: Delta has several breweries that serve locally brewed beer, including the recently opened Delta Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Delta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delta has a variety of accommodations to fit any budget. Here are some of the options:

  • Delta Hotels: Delta hotels include national chains like Comfort Inn and Holiday Inn Express, as well as locally owned motels.
  • Hostels: There are no hostels in Delta.
  • Guesthouses: Delta has several vacation rental options on platforms like Airbnb and VRBO.

What to Buy in Delta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some suggestions:

  • Colorado Peaches: As mentioned earlier, Delta is known for its delicious peaches, making them a perfect gift to bring home.
  • Local Craft Beer and Wine: Many breweries and wineries in the area offer canned or bottled products that you can take home.
  • Art and Crafts: Delta has several art galleries and craft shops that sell locally made art and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Delta

Delta is a small town that prides itself on its western heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they love to share their history and culture with visitors. The town is also known for its annual Delta County Fair, which takes place in August and features rodeos, concerts, and other western-themed events.

Best Time to Visit Delta

Delta is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August). The weather is warm, and many outdoor activities are available. However, the fall months (September-November) are also a great time to visit if you want to experience the beautiful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delta

Here are some do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind when visiting Delta:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be friendly and engage with the locals.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on hiking trails or in the parks.
  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines.
  • Be loud and disruptive in residential areas.

Safety and Health

As with any destination, it’s important to stay safe and healthy when visiting Delta. Here are some tips:

Safety in Delta

  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay on designated hiking trails.
  • Follow the safety guidelines at the parks and attractions.
  • Use caution when driving on mountain roads.

Health in Delta

  • Altitude sickness is a real concern in Delta, especially if you are not used to high elevations. Drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol, and rest frequently.
  • Sunscreen and insect repellent are essential, especially during the summer months when outdoor activities are popular.

Getting to Delta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Delta is located in southwestern Colorado, approximately 30 miles east of Montrose. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Delta

  • Montrose Regional Airport is the nearest airport, located approximately 30 miles away.
  • Grand Junction Regional Airport is another option, located about 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Delta

  • Greyhound does not go to Delta. There are no bus stations in Delta.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is the best option to get around Delta since it is a small town with limited public transportation.

How to Get Around in Delta

Since Delta is a small town, the best way to get around is by car. Renting a car is highly recommended. There are no taxi or ride-sharing services available in Delta.

Where to Go Next from Delta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore:

  • Telluride: Telluride is a historic mining town located a few hours from Delta. It offers skiing, music festivals, and stunning mountain scenery.
  • Glenwood Springs: Glenwood Springs is known for its hot springs, and it offers many outdoor activities, including hiking and skiing.
  • Grand Junction: Grand Junction is a vibrant city that offers wineries, museums, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Delta is a charming destination that offers an array of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, local cuisine, and beautiful scenery. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip with ease and explore all that Delta has to offer. Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation or an adventure-filled getaway, Delta has something for everyone.
