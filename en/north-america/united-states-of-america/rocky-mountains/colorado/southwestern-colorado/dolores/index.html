Introduction to Dolores

Nestled in Southwestern Colorado, Dolores is a quaint town located in the Rocky Mountains of the United States. Known for its scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage, Dolores is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike.

Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s museums and historical sites, hike or bike through its majestic landscapes, or try out its culinary delights, Dolores has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the top attractions, things to do, food and drink scene, local culture, and logistics to plan your visit to Dolores.

List of Places to Visit in Dolores

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Anasazi Heritage Center

One of the top cultural attractions in Dolores is the Anasazi Heritage Center, which provides a glimpse into the ancient civilization of the Puebloan people who once lived in the area. This museum has exhibits and interactive displays that showcase the life and culture of this ancient society.

Canyons of the Ancients National Monument

Canyons of the Ancients National Monument is another great place to visit to learn about the history and culture of the Puebloan people. The monument has thousands of archaeological sites, including cliff dwellings, kivas, and rock art, spread over 176,000 acres.

McPhee Reservoir

The McPhee Reservoir is an excellent place to visit for watersports enthusiasts. The reservoir is surrounded by beautiful scenery and is a great place to go boating, fishing, or swimming.

Natural Attractions of Dolores: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mesa Verde National Park

One of the top attractions in Dolores is the Mesa Verde National Park, which is home to some of the best-preserved cliff dwellings in the world. The park also has numerous hiking trails that take you through stunning landscapes of deep canyons and rugged terrain, including the scenic routes of Spruce Tree House, Mesa Top Loop, and Cliff Palace.

Dolores River Canyon

The Dolores River Canyon is another natural attraction that you should not miss when visiting Dolores. The canyon offers breathtaking views and a chance to go rafting or kayaking on the Dolores River.

Boggy Draw Trail

The Boggy Draw Trail is a beautiful and scenic hiking/biking route that takes you through sweeping meadows, aspen groves, and oak forests. The route is relatively gentle and is suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers/bikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dolores

Dolores offers several hiking and biking trails, depending on your level of experience and interests. Some of the top trails include:

  • The Colorado Trail
  • The Dolores River Trail
  • The Lizard Head Trail

Best Things to Do in Dolores: Activities and Tours

River Rafting

River rafting is a popular activity in Dolores, and you can book various types of guided trips, from calm float trips to more challenging rapids.

Jeep Tours

Jeep tours are also popular in Dolores, and they offer a chance to explore the rugged, off-road terrains and scenery in the area.

Horseback Riding

For a more relaxed adventure, horseback riding is also a great choice. There are several riding stables in the area that offer guided trail rides for both beginners and experienced riders.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dolores

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Dolores, here’s a sample itinerary to maximize your time in the area:

Day 1-2: Explore Mesa Verde National Park, visit Spruce Tree House, Mesa Top Loop, and Cliff Palace.

Day 3: Go river rafting on the Dolores River.

Day 4: Visit the Anasazi Heritage Center and Canyons of the Ancients National Monument.

Day 5: Hike or bike the Lizard Head Trail.

Day 6: Go horseback riding through the scenic landscapes of Dolores.

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at the McPhee Reservoir, swimming, fishing, or just enjoying the views.

Food and Drink Scene of Dolores

Must-Try Dishes

When in Dolores, make sure to try some of the local cuisine, including:

  • Homemade green chili
  • Navajo Tacos
  • Buffalo burgers

Local Beverages

Dolores is home to several small breweries and wineries that offer visitors a chance to sample some fantastic local brews and wines. Make sure to try some of their specialty brews, including the Mesa Verde Amber Ale and the Dolores River Hefeweizen.

Where to Stay in Dolores: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dolores has several options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top-rated places to stay include:

  • The Dolores River Inn
  • The Aneth Lodge Motel
  • The Sophia Retreat and Event Center

What to Buy in Dolores: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Dolores include:

  • Locally made jewelry
  • Southwestern pottery
  • Hand-woven rugs and blankets

Local Culture and Customs of Dolores

The local culture of Dolores is heavily influenced by the Puebloan Native Americans, who have lived in the area for thousands of years. Visitors should be respectful when visiting cultural attractions and follow the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dolores

The best time to visit Dolores is during the summer months from June to August, when the temperatures are mild, and the outdoor activities and adventures are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dolores

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment
  • Follow the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect the local culture and history
  • Neglect your safety during outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Dolores

Dolores is a relatively safe area, but visitors should take precautions during outdoor activities and adventures. Make sure to follow all safety instructions and guidelines provided during activities such as river rafting, horseback riding, and hiking.

Health in Dolores

Dolores has a few medical facilities and clinics, and visitors should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies that may arise during their visit.

Getting to Dolores: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dolores

The closest major airport to Dolores is the Durango-La Plata County Airport, located about 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Dolores

Greyhound Bus Lines and TUFESA Bus Lines are the two main bus services that offer transportation to Dolores.

Other Options

Car rentals are also available for visitors who prefer to drive to Dolores, and there are several car rental services in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Dolores

The best way to get around in Dolores is by car. Renting a car is the most convenient option, as public transportation is limited. However, there are a few shuttle services available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Dolores: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit after exploring Dolores, including:

Conclusion

Dolores offers a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With its rich history and scenic landscapes, Dolores is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the beauty and diversity of Colorado. Plan your trip to Dolores today and discover the wonders of this hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains.
