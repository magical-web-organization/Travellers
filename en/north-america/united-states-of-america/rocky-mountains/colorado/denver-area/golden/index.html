Introduction to Golden

Nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains near Denver, Colorado, the city of Golden offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural allure, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Known as the former gold rush town, Golden is now a vibrant city with rich history and a modern vibe. Its charming downtown, numerous museums, hiking trails, and scenic drives are worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Golden

Here’s a list of some of the best places to visit in Golden.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Buffalo Bill Museum and Grave - Learn about the life and legacy of the famous Wild West icon William F. Cody, aka Buffalo Bill, at this mountain-top museum and gravesite.
  2. Clear Creek History Park - Get a glimpse of life in the 1800s at this recreated pioneer settlement.
  3. Golden History Museum - Discover the history and culture of Golden and the Colorado foothills through exhibits, artifacts, and events.
  4. Rocky Mountain Quilt Museum - Admire the artistry and heritage of quilt-making at this museum that features over 500 quilts and related textile artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Golden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Clear Creek Trail - Walk, run, or bike down this scenic 20-mile trail that follows the picturesque Clear Creek and offers stunning views of the canyon and mountains.
  2. Golden Gate Canyon State Park - Explore over 12,000 acres of pristine wilderness and wildlife habitat in this park that boasts over 35 miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing ponds, and camping sites.
  3. Lookout Mountain Park - Drive or bike up to this mountain-top park that offers breathtaking views of the Denver skyline, the Front Range, and the Continental Divide.
  4. South Table Mountain Park - Hike or bike up this volcanic mesa that offers panoramic views of Golden, Denver, and the foothills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Golden

Golden is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a plethora of trails and routes that cater to all skill levels and preferences. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes of Golden.

  1. Apex Park/Enchanted Forest Trail - A challenging 6.5-mile trail that rewards you with stunning views of Golden and Denver.
  2. North Table Mountain Trail - An easy 7.5-mile trail that offers panoramic views of the Front Range and Golden.
  3. South Boulder Creek Trail - An easy 3-mile trail that follows the tranquil and scenic South Boulder Creek.
  4. White Ranch Park Trail - A moderate 3.7-mile trail that takes you through the scenic foothills and mesa top with stunning views over the city.

Best Things to Do in Golden: Activities and Tours

Golden offers a diverse range of activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Golden.

  1. Coors Brewery Tour - Take a free tour of the iconic Coors Brewery and learn about the brewing process and history of one of the most popular beer brands in the US.
  2. Jefferson Symphony Orchestra Concert - Attend a concert by the Jefferson Symphony Orchestra, a talented group of musicians that performs classical and modern music throughout the year.
  3. Museum Tours - Explore the numerous museums of Golden, including the Buffalo Bill Museum, the Golden History Museum, and the Rocky Mountain Quilt Museum.
  4. Water Activities - Enjoy kayaking, tubing, and fishing in the clear and refreshing waters of Clear Creek.

Week-Long Itinerary for Golden

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring the best of Golden.

Day 1: Visit Buffalo Bill Museum and Grave and take a scenic drive to Lookout Mountain Park. Day 2: Explore Clear Creek History Park and downtown Golden. Day 3-4: Hike or bike the various trails and routes of Golden, including Apex Park, North Table Mountain Trail, and South Boulder Creek Trail. Day 5: Take a tour of the Coors Brewery and enjoy water activities in Clear Creek. Day 6: Visit the Golden History Museum and Rocky Mountain Quilt Museum. Day 7: Drive to Golden Gate Canyon State Park and spend the day hiking, fishing, and camping.

Food and Drink Scene of Golden

Golden offers a variety of culinary choices that showcase the local produce and flavors of the region.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Green Chile - A spicy local favorite that incorporates roasted green chiles, pork, and spices. Try it at Woody’s Wood-Fire Pizza.
  2. Colorado Trout - Freshly caught and grilled to perfection, this dish is a hallmark of Colorado cuisine. Try it at Bridgewater Grill.
  3. Burgers - Golden boasts several burger joints that serve hand-crafted burgers made with local beef and ingredients. Try it at Bob’s Atomic Burgers.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer - Golden is home to several award-winning craft breweries, including Coors Brewery, Golden City Brewery, and Holidaily Brewing Company.
  2. Wine - Sip on local wine varieties at some of Golden’s wineries, including Talon Wine Brands and Silver Vines Winery.

Where to Stay in Golden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Golden offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences.

  1. Table Mountain Inn - A boutique hotel with western-themed rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  2. The Golden Hotel, an ascend hotel collection member - A charming hotel with river-view rooms, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant.
  3. Pansy’s Parlor Bed and Breakfast - A cozy B&B with vintage décor, home-cooked breakfast, and easy access to hiking trails.

What to Buy in Golden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Golden offers unique and locally made products that make for perfect souvenirs and gifts.

  1. Golden Beer Gear - Purchase Coors Brewery merchandise, including T-shirts, hats, glassware, and growlers.
  2. Rocky Mountain Quilts and Textile Art - Buy a handcrafted quilt, textile art, or accessories from the Rocky Mountain Quilt Museum gift shop.
  3. Western Attire and Supplies - Golden has several western wear and supply stores, such as American Cowboy Supply and Golden Saddle Trading Post, that offer authentic clothing, hats, boots, and gear.

Local Culture and Customs of Golden

Golden prides itself on its western heritage, welcoming spirit, and reverence for nature. Residents of Golden are friendly and laid-back, and they appreciate visitors who respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Golden

The best time to visit Golden is during the summer and fall months, from June to October, when the weather is pleasant and festivals, events, and seasonal attractions are plentiful. Spring and winter can be unpredictable and chilly, but they offer fewer crowds and unique activities like skiing and snowshoeing in the mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Golden

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Golden.

Do:

  1. Do explore the various hiking and biking trails of Golden.
  2. Do visit the numerous museums and historical places in Golden.
  3. Do take a tour of the Coors Brewery.
  4. Do try the local cuisine, especially the green chile and craft beer.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural areas and attractions in Golden.
  2. Don’t ignore safety guidelines and instructions when engaging in outdoor activities.
  3. Don’t disrespect the local culture and customs, including western traditions.

Safety and Health

Golden is a relatively safe and healthy city, but visitors should be aware of certain risks and precautions.

Safety in Golden

  1. Be aware of wildlife, including bears, mountain lions, and snakes, if you are hiking or camping in Golden’s parks and wilderness areas.
  2. Avoid leaving valuables in your car, especially when parked in remote or dimly lit areas.
  3. Follow safety guidelines and instructions when engaging in water activities in Clear Creek.

Health in Golden

  1. Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, as the altitude and the sun can be intense.
  2. Allow time for acclimatization if you are coming from lower elevations, as altitude sickness is a potential risk.

Getting to Golden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Golden is easily accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Golden

  1. Denver International Airport - 36 miles east of Golden, the largest airport in the region with multiple domestic and international flights.
  2. Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport - 10 miles south of Golden, a smaller airport with private and chartered flights.

Bus Stations Near Golden

  1. Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Denver, 20 miles east of Golden, serves multiple destinations in North America.
  2. Golden Station - Served by the W and G bus routes of the Regional Transportation District (RTD), which offer connections to downtown Denver and other suburbs.

Other Options

  1. Car rentals - Various car rental agencies operate in Denver and Golden, offering multiple vehicles for rent.
  2. Taxi and ride-sharing services - Uber and Lyft offer ride-sharing and taxi services in Golden and the Denver area.

How to Get Around in Golden

Golden is a walkable and bike-friendly city, with several trails, paths, and sidewalks that connect the major attractions and destinations. For longer distances, visitors can use the RTD bus system, rental cars, or taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Golden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Golden is an ideal gateway to the numerous natural and cultural attractions of the Rocky Mountains and the Front Range region.

  1. Denver - A thriving and cosmopolitan city that offers world-class museums, entertainment venues, restaurants, and shopping opportunities.
  2. Rocky Mountain National Park - A majestic wilderness area that boasts over 350 miles of trails, pristine lakes, soaring peaks, and abundant wildlife.
  3. Peak to Peak Scenic Byway - A stunningly beautiful mountain drive that spans over 55 miles and connects a series of charming mountain towns, historic landmarks, and natural wonders.
  4. Boulder - A vibrant college town that offers a lively arts scene, outdoor recreation opportunities, and a thriving food and drink scene.

Conclusion

Golden, Colorado, offers an unforgettable travel experience full of natural beauty, cultural allure, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Its museums, hiking trails, historic sites, and local cuisine are worth exploring, and its friendly residents and welcoming culture make it a must-visit destination in Colorado.
