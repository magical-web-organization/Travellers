Introduction to Westminster

Nestled in the stunning Rocky Mountains, Westminster is a picturesque city located just minutes from downtown Denver, Colorado. This vibrant community boasts an extensive network of parks and trails, world-class cultural attractions, and a bustling food and drink scene. With close proximity to the mountains and myriad of outdoor adventures, Westminster makes the perfect destination for adventure-seekers and culture enthusiasts alike.

List of Places to Visit in Westminster

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Westminster is steeped in history, with numerous museums and historical sites to explore. One must-visit attraction is the Butterfly Pavilion, home to over 1,600 butterflies and other insects. Learn about the lifecycle of these delicate creatures and get up close and personal with them in the tropical rainforest habitat.

Another cultural attraction in Westminster is the Bowles House Museum, showcasing life in the mid-1800s. This restored Victorian home features original furnishings, photographs, and artifacts from the era, providing a fascinating glimpse into the past.

Natural Attractions of Westminster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will delight in Westminster’s breathtaking natural beauty. A popular destination for hiking and biking is the Big Dry Creek Trail, a 12-mile paved path that winds through open space and parks. Bring your dog along to enjoy the scenic views while exploring this stunning park.

Another must-visit attraction in Westminster is the Standley Lake Regional Park, a 3,000 acre nature reserve that offers camping, fishing, and kayaking on the lake, as well as hiking and biking on the surrounding trails. Don’t forget your camera to capture the stunning Rocky Mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westminster

Westminster is a haven for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and parks to explore. One popular route is the Westminster Hills Off-Leash Dog Park, offering over three miles of hiking trails with stunning views of the Front Range Mountains.

Another popular biking route is the Big Dry Creek Trail, a 12-mile paved path that passes through multiple parks and open spaces, perfect for a leisurely bike ride or intense workout.

Best Things to Do in Westminster: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Westminster will find no shortage of activities and tours to enjoy. For thrill-seekers, the Fly High Trampoline Park provides hours of fun and excitement with their indoor trampoline park, dodgeball courts, and foam pits.

For a more laid-back adventure, book a hot air balloon tour through the mountains with Rocky Mountain Hot Air. Take in the breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains while floating through the clouds.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westminster

Day 1: Explore Butterfly Pavilion and adjacent Westminster Hills Open Space

Day 2: Hike or bike the Big Dry Creek Trail

Day 3: Relax at Standley Lake and enjoy a picnic lunch

Day 4: Take a guided tour through Bowles House Museum

Day 5: Indulge in a brewery tour and tasting at Wits End Brewing Company

Day 6: Go shopping and browsing in the Westminster Promenade

Day 7: End the week with a thrilling hot air balloon tour at Rocky Mountain Hot Air

Food and Drink Scene of Westminster

Must-Try Dishes

Westminster has a diverse food scene, with numerous restaurants boasting unique and delicious cuisine. One must-try dish is the green chile from Santiago’s Mexican Restaurant, a savory and spicy blend of pork, green chili, and spices that is a staple dish of Colorado cuisine.

For a taste of the Mediterranean, visit Ali Baba Grill, known for their hummus, kabobs, and falafels.

Local Beverages

No trip to Westminster is complete without indulging in the local beer scene. Wits End Brewery offers a great selection of craft beers with weekly specials, including their signature green chili beer.

For those who prefer a glass of wine, check out Big Choice Brewing and Winery, offering a unique selection of wine and beer to enjoy.

Where to Stay in Westminster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Westminster has numerous lodging options to fit every budget and preference. For an upscale experience, book a room at the Westin Westminster, featuring stunning views of the mountains and top-notch amenities.

For those on a budget, check out the cozy and comfortable accommodations at the La Quinta Inn by Wyndham Denver Westminster.

What to Buy in Westminster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Colorado by visiting the Westminster Promenade, boasting a variety of local shops and boutiques. Pick up a souvenir t-shirt or hat at the Colorado souvenir shop, or treat yourself to some unique jewelry and ceramics at the Firefly Handmade Gallery.

Local Culture and Customs of Westminster

Westminster is known for its friendly and welcoming culture. The locals pride themselves on their outdoor lifestyle, and a popular pastime is hiking and biking through the beautiful surrounding nature.

Best Time to Visit Westminster

The best time to visit Westminster is in the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny and the outdoor activities are plentiful. However, the fall months (September-November) offer stunning fall foliage and cooler temperatures, making for a beautiful and refreshing vacation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westminster

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the numerous hiking and biking trails in the area
  • Do sample the local craft beer scene
  • Do take in the stunning views of the Rocky Mountains

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat for the sunny Colorado weather
  • Don’t miss out on the local green chili cuisine
  • Don’t forget to bring a camera to capture the breathtaking scenery

Safety and Health

Safety in Westminster

Westminster is generally a safe destination, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take common safety precautions.

Health in Westminster

Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen while enjoying the outdoor activities, as the intense Colorado sun can cause sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Westminster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westminster

The nearest airport to Westminster is Denver International Airport, located just 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Westminster

The Greyhound Bus Station is the closest bus station to Westminster, located approximately 20 minutes away by car.

Other Options

For those driving into the city, there are numerous car rental options available at Denver International Airport.

How to Get Around in Westminster

The best way to get around Westminster is by car, as the city is spread out and not pedestrian-friendly. However, there are numerous bike trails and bike rental options available for visitors who prefer to explore on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Westminster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Westminster is located in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, making it the perfect base for exploring nearby attractions. Consider taking a day trip to nearby Boulder to explore the beautiful Pearl Street Mall and hike the Flatirons.

Another must-visit destination is Rocky Mountain National Park, just a two-hour drive from Westminster. This stunning nature reserve features hiking trails, scenic drives, and breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains.

Conclusion

Westminster, Colorado offers a stunning blend of natural attractions and cultural experiences. From the world-class museums and historical sites to the numerous outdoor activities and vibrant food scene, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Plan your next vacation to Westminster and discover the best of the Rocky Mountains!
