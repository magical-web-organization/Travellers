Introduction to Broomfield

Located in the Denver Area, in the state of Colorado, Broomfield is a charming city known for its breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains and its outdoor recreational activities. It is located about 20 miles north of Denver and serves as a great base for exploring both the city and the mountains. Broomfield offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures that are sure to delight all travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Broomfield

Broomfield is a city full of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s rich history or looking for an outdoor adventure, there is something for everyone in Broomfield.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in learning about the city’s history and culture, you should definitely visit Broomfield’s museums and historical places. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Broomfield Depot Museum: A historic train depot that now serves as a museum, showcasing the city’s railroad history.
  • Broomfield Veterans Memorial Museum: A museum dedicated to honoring the local veterans and their service to the country.
  • Butterfly Pavilion: A unique indoor habitat that allows visitors to interact with live butterflies and other insects.

Natural Attractions of Broomfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Broomfield’s natural beauty is what makes it stand out among other cities in Colorado. It offers many outdoor adventures and scenic views, including:

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: A national park with breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains, and many hiking trails suitable for all levels of hikers.
  • Ralston Creek Trail: A popular trail with beautiful views of the mountains, perfect for hiking, cycling, or jogging.
  • The Broadlands Golf Course: A must-visit for golf enthusiasts, offering spectacular views of the Rocky Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Broomfield

Broomfield is a hiker’s paradise, with many hiking trails suitable for all skill levels. Here are some of the best ones to explore:

  • Chautauqua Trail: A popular trail that goes to the top of the Flatirons, providing stunning views of the surrounding mountains and Boulder County.
  • Gregory Canyon Trail: A moderately difficult trail that offers panoramic views of the Boulder Valley.
  • First Flatiron Trail: A strenuous but rewarding trail that offers breathtaking views of the Flatirons and the Front Range.

Best Things to Do in Broomfield: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Broomfield offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Broomfield:

  • Paddleboarding: Enjoy the beautiful waters of Standley Lake Reservoir by renting a paddleboard and exploring the lake at your own pace.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Enjoy the scenic views of the Colorado Rockies from a hot air balloon ride, a truly unique experience.
  • Segway Tours: Explore Broomfield in style by taking a Segway tour that takes you through the city’s neighborhoods, parks, and trails.

Week-Long Itinerary for Broomfield

For those planning to spend a week in Broomfield, here is an itinerary that will allow you to explore all that the city has to offer:

Day 1: Visit the Butterfly Pavilion, followed by a hike to the top of the Flatirons via Chautauqua Trail.

Day 2: Explore the Broomfield Depot Museum, followed by a round of golf at The Broadlands Golf Course.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning, and follow it up with an afternoon of paddleboarding on Standley Lake Reservoir.

Day 4: Visit the Ralston Creek Trail, and then head to the Broomfield Veterans Memorial Museum in the afternoon.

Day 5: Take a Segway tour of the city, followed by an evening of live music at the 1stBank Center.

Day 6: Visit the Rocky Mountain National Park, and explore its many hiking trails.

Day 7: Wrap up your week in Broomfield with a leisurely day at the Boulder Reservoir, where you can swim, boat, and picnic with family and friends.

Food and Drink Scene of Broomfield

Broomfield has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many options for foodies to explore. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile: A traditional Colorado dish made with pork, green chiles, and spices.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A regional delicacy made from bull testicles.
  • Colorado Craft Beer: Colorado has a vibrant craft beer scene, with many breweries and taprooms to explore.

Local Beverages

  • Stranahan’s Colorado Whiskey: A whiskey distilled in Colorado, offering a unique taste that reflects the region’s ruggedness.
  • New Belgium Brewing Company: A popular brewery that serves a range of craft beers, including the famous Fat Tire Amber Ale.

Where to Stay in Broomfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Broomfield offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to affordable hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best hotels in Broomfield include:

  • The Westin Westminster: A luxury hotel located just a few minutes away from Broomfield, offering breathtaking views of the mountains.
  • Aloft Broomfield Denver: A modern hotel with a great location, offering easy access to all of Broomfield’s attractions.
  • Renaissance Boulder Flatiron Hotel: A chic hotel located just a few miles south of Broomfield, offering easy access to the Flatirons and the Boulder Valley.

What to Buy in Broomfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Broomfield has many locally-made products that make great souvenirs for visitors. Some of the best products to bring home include:

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Many local artists create stunning jewelry pieces that reflect the beauty of Broomfield.
  • Colorado Mountain Honey: A unique honey that is made from the nectar of wildflowers found in the Rocky Mountains.
  • Colorado Potato Chips: A regional twist on an American classic, these chips are made from locally-grown potatoes and handcrafted in small batches.

Local Culture and Customs of Broomfield

Broomfield residents take their outdoor activities, such as hiking and skiing, seriously. Visitors should respect their love for the outdoors and be mindful of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Broomfield

The best time to visit Broomfield is during the summer, from June to August. During this time, the weather is mild, and the city’s outdoor recreation opportunities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Broomfield

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors and visit the many parks and trails in Broomfield.
  • Respect the environment and leave no trace.
  • Try the local food, craft beer, and honey.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the trails.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Disrupt the peace by being loud or causing disturbances.

Safety and Health

Safety in Broomfield

Broomfield is a safe city, with low crime rates and a friendly community. Visitors should still take basic precautions, such as locking their cars and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Broomfield

Broomfield is located at a high altitude, and visitors may experience altitude sickness. Visitors should stay hydrated and avoid strenuous activities for the first few days.

Getting to Broomfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Broomfield is easily accessible by air, ground, and public transportation.

Airports Near Broomfield

  • Denver International Airport (DIA): Located about 30 miles from Broomfield, DIA is the closest major airport to the city.
  • Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport (BJC): Located in Broomfield, this airport serves small private planes and business jets.

Bus Stations Near Broomfield

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Denver, about 20 miles from Broomfield, Greyhound offers daily bus service to and from the city.
  • RTD (Regional Transportation District): Denver’s public transportation system offers bus and light rail services to Broomfield.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from one of the many car rental agencies located at DIA or in Broomfield.

How to Get Around in Broomfield

Broomfield is a small city that can be easily explored on foot, by bike, or by public transportation. RTD offers a bus service that connects the city’s main attractions and neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Broomfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Broomfield is located in the heart of Colorado, and there are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Boulder: A charming university town located just a few miles south of Broomfield, offering many outdoor activities and cultural attractions.
  • Denver: Colorado’s largest city, located about 20 miles south of Broomfield, offering many cultural and entertainment options.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park: A national park located about 60 miles west of Broomfield, offering breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains and many hiking trails.

Conclusion

Broomfield is a city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s rich history or seeking a thrilling outdoor adventure, Broomfield has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip to this charming city today and experience everything it has to offer!
