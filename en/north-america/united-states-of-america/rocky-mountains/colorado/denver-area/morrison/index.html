Introduction to Morrison

Morrison is a small but beautiful town located in the Denver area of Colorado, United States. This picturesque town is nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Morrison is renowned for its world-class music venue, the Red Rocks Amphitheatre, and its rich history related to the Dinosaur Bones found in nearby Morrison Natural History Museum.

List of Places to Visit in Morrison

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Morrison Natural History Museum: Step back in time and learn about the giants that roamed the earth millions of years ago at the Morrison Natural History Museum. The Morrison Museum is home to a vast collection of fossils, dinosaur skeletons, and interactive exhibits.

  • Dinosaur Ridge: Located just a few miles outside of Morrison town, Dinosaur Ridge is a unique geological formation that showcases an awe-inspiring set of fossilized footprints and bones of prehistoric creatures.

  • Red Rocks Amphitheatre: No trip to Morrison is complete without a visit to the world-famous Red Rocks Amphitheatre. This outdoor music venue nestled inside the red-rock formations of the Rocky Mountains is one of the most iconic and picturesque venues in the world.

Natural Attractions of Morrison: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Falcon Park: Located just a few minutes’ drive from Morrison, the Mount Falcon Park offers magnificent panoramic views of the Front Range, Denver skyline, and charming hills of Morrison.

  • Bear Creek Lake Park: Bear Creek Park, located just a few miles from Morrison, offers ample opportunities for hiking, biking, swimming, fishing, and camping, set amidst stunning Rocky Mountains scenery.

  • Colorado Railroad Museum: Located a short drive away from Morrison, the Colorado Railroad Museum tells the story of an integral part of America’s history by showcasing the historic locomotives, railcars, and other artifacts that made rail travel possible.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morrison

Morrison offers some of the most scenic trails for both hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts. The trails in the area range from easy to challenging, catering to all skill levels. The most popular hiking and biking trails in Morrison include:

  • Red Rocks Trail: This moderate trail covers 6.5 miles of beautiful mountain terrain, offering stunning views of the Red Rocks Amphitheatre and downtown Denver.

  • Mount Falcon Trail: This strenuous trail stretches up to 7 miles, snaking through lush meadows, woods, and rust-red rocks that provide a jaw-dropping panorama of the Rockies.

  • Bear Creek Trail: This well-maintained trail is perfect for beginners and traverses along the quiet waters of Bear Creek, providing a relaxed and enjoyable hiking or cycling experience.

Best Things to Do in Morrison: Activities and Tours

  • Red Rocks Sunrise Yoga: Take a sunrise yoga session at the foot of the Red Rocks Amphitheatre and start your day off feeling rejuvenated and energized.

  • Buffalo Bill Cody Museum and Grave: Step into the life and times of legendary Buffalo Bill Cody, an American West Showman, and Frontiersman at the Buffalo Bill Cody Museum located just outside Morrison.

  • Colorado Wine Tour: Take a scenic drive through Colorado wine country and taste-test some of the state’s best wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Morrison

Must-Try Dishes

  • Big Mike’s BBQ: Enjoy some of the best BBQ in Colorado with a visit to Big Mike’s BBQ. Be sure to try their delectable briskets and ribs.

  • The Cow Eatery: Located on the main strip in Morrison, The Cow Eatery serves up delicious burgers, tacos, sandwiches, and salads in a friendly, down-to-earth environment.

  • Tony Rigatoni: Offering a unique fine-dining experience, Tony Rigatoni is a must-visit restaurant in Morrison. Its menu offers traditional Italian and American food with a modern and creative twist.

Local Beverages

  • Morrison Distilling Company: Sample some of the best vodka, rum, and whiskey that Colorado has to offer at the Morrison Distilling Co.

  • Twin Owls Brewery: A visit to Morrison would be incomplete without trying the Twin Owls Brewery’s signature craft beers.

Where to Stay in Morrison: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Cliff House Lodge: Nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, The Cliff House Lodge is an excellent option for a relaxing, romantic getaway.

  • The Golden Hotel: Located a short drive away from Morrison, The Golden Hotel is a full-service hotel in the center of Golden, offering easy access to all of the town’s attractions.

  • Adrien’s Bed and Breakfast: One of the most charming bed and breakfasts in Morrison, Adrien’s offers comfortable and cozy accommodations in a beautifully restored Victorian-style home.

What to Buy in Morrison: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Red Rocks Amphitheatre Merchandise: Take home a piece of history with some exclusive Red Rocks Amphitheatre memorabilia from the visitor center.

  • Morrison Trading Post: The Morrison Trading Post is a vintage store selling some of the most eclectic and unique items. From handmade jewelry, home decor, to vintage clothing, the Morrison Trading Post is a treasure trove of one-of-a-kind finds.

Local Culture and Customs of Morrison

The people of Morrison are proud of their western heritage, and you will find this reflected in the friendliness and hospitality of the locals. Attire is casual, and visitors are encouraged to dress in comfortable, weather-appropriate clothing. While tipping is not mandatory, it’s always appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Morrison

The best time to visit Morrison is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the area enjoys a pleasant breeze. Summers in Morrison can be hot and crowded, and while winters bring a festive cheer, they can be bitter cold with snow on the trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morrison

Do:

  • Do take a guided tour of the Red Rocks Amphitheatre.

  • Do take a tour of the Morrison Natural History Museum to witness millions of years of history.

  • Do bring comfortable, sturdy shoes for hiking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.

  • Don’t hike or bike outside the marked trails.

  • Don’t climb on rocks or sensitive natural features.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morrison

Morrison is a safe destination for visitors. However, when hiking or biking, always practice caution and stay alert to your surroundings. Be aware of local wildlife, including snakes and other potentially hazardous creatures.

Health in Morrison

Morrison has clean air and is a great place to spend time outdoors. Be sure to bring sunscreen and plenty of water when hiking or biking.

Getting to Morrison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morrison

Morrison is located approximately 38 km from Denver International Airport, the closest airport in the area.

Bus Stations Near Morrison

Greyhound operates daily bus service to and from Denver, which is located a short drive from Morrison.

Other Options

Rental cars and taxis are also available at the airport or in Denver for transportation to Morrison.

How to Get Around in Morrison

The best way to get around and sightsee in Morrison is by car. You can rent a car at the airport or in Denver. Bicycle rentals are available in Morrison for biking enthusiasts.

Where to Go Next from Morrison: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Golden: A charming town famous for its historic charm, vibrant downtown area, and picturesque hiking trails.

  • Denver: A bustling city that has something for everyone, including museums, parks, zoos, and art galleries.

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: A must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts, located just a few hours’ drive from Morrison.

Conclusion

Morrison is a vibrant destination that offers some of the most breathtaking views of the Rockies, world-class tourist attractions, and a wide range of outdoor activities. Whether visiting for a weekend getaway or staying for a full week, Morrison offers plenty of opportunities for exploration, relaxation, and fun.
