Introduction to Littleton

Located in the heart of the Denver Area, Littleton is a charming town nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains. With a population of just under 50,000 people, this small town offers a perfect blend of urban amenities and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history, enjoy outdoor adventures, and immerse themselves in the local culture and cuisine. In this travel guide, we’ll help you plan your trip to Littleton by highlighting the best things to do, see, and eat in this hidden gem of Colorado.

List of Places to Visit in Littleton

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or culture, Littleton has plenty of attractions to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in Littleton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Littleton Museum: This museum showcases the history of Littleton and the surrounding area through interactive exhibits, historic buildings, and artifacts.
  • Roxborough State Park: This state park features striking red rock formations and miles of hiking trails that wind through the foothills of the Rocky Mountains.
  • Hudson Gardens: This beautiful botanical garden features a wide variety of flowers, trees, and plants, as well as an elegant event venue.

Natural Attractions of Littleton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chatfield State Park: This park offers boating, fishing, camping, and hiking opportunities, as well as stunning views of the Colorado foothills.
  • South Platte Park: This 880-acre park is a hidden gem for nature lovers, with miles of trails for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.
  • Cherry Creek State Park: This park is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and camping, as well as hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Littleton

Littleton is home to many scenic hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Mary Carter Greenway Trail: This trail is a popular route for biking and running, offering a scenic path alongside the South Platte River.
  • High Line Canal Trail: This trail stretches for 71 miles, following the former irrigation canal of the same name. It offers opportunities for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Waterton Canyon Trail: This trail features stunning views of the foothills and the South Platte River, and is popular among hikers and mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in Littleton: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with your family or looking for a romantic getaway, Littleton offers plenty of activities and tours to suit any taste. Here are some of the best things to do in Littleton:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Experience the beauty of Littleton from above by taking a hot air balloon ride over the Rocky Mountains.
  • Go horseback riding: Explore the scenic foothills of Littleton on horseback, with tours available for riders of all levels.
  • Take a historic walking tour: Learn about the rich history of Littleton by taking a walking tour of the town’s historic districts and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Littleton

Littleton offers a vibrant food and drink scene, with a variety of local and international options available. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Littleton:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colorado lamb: This local specialty is a must-try for meat lovers, with delicious lamb dishes available at many restaurants in Littleton.
  • Green chili: This spicy stew made with pork, chili peppers, and spices is a Colorado classic, and can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Rocky Mountain oysters: This unique delicacy made from bull testicles may not be for everyone, but it’s a must-try for adventurous eaters.

Local Beverages

  • Local craft beer: Littleton is home to several craft breweries, offering a variety of unique and delicious beers.
  • Colorado wine: The vineyards of Colorado produce some of the best wines in the country, with many local wineries offering tastings and tours.

Local Culture and Customs of Littleton

Littleton is a friendly and welcoming town, with a rich history and strong community spirit. Here are some of the local customs and cultural practices to keep in mind when visiting Littleton:

  • Be respectful of the natural environment: Littleton is located in a beautiful natural setting, and residents take pride in preserving its pristine surroundings. Make sure to clean up after yourself and respect wildlife and plant life.
  • Embrace the local lifestyle: Littleton residents are known for their laid-back and friendly attitude. Take the time to relax, savor the local cuisine, and soak in the town’s natural beauty.
  • Support local businesses: Littleton has a thriving small business community, and supporting local stores and restaurants is a great way to show your appreciation for the town’s unique character.

Best Time to Visit Littleton

Littleton is a year-round destination, offering something for visitors in every season. However, the best time to visit Littleton depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the seasons and what they have to offer:

  • Spring: March to May is a great time to visit Littleton for outdoor activities, with mild temperatures and blooming wildflowers.
  • Summer: June to August is the peak tourist season in Littleton, with sunny weather and plenty of outdoor events and festivals.
  • Fall: September to November is a great time to visit Littleton for autumn foliage, with stunning colors and cooler temperatures.
  • Winter: December to February is a great time to visit Littleton if you’re a winter sports enthusiast, with skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities available in nearby resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Littleton

Here are some important do’s and don’ts for visitors to Littleton:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Littleton is known for its natural beauty, so make sure to take advantage of hiking trails, parks, and scenic views.
  • Try the local cuisine: Littleton has a thriving food and drink scene, with many local specialties and international options available.
  • Be respectful of the community: Littleton is a friendly and tight-knit community, so be mindful of local customs and practices.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas: As mentioned before, Littleton residents take pride in preserving the town’s natural beauty, so make sure to clean up after yourself and respect wildlife and plant life.
  • Disrespect wildlife: Littleton is home to many species of wildlife, including bears, mountain lions, and elk. Make sure to keep a safe distance and respect their natural habitat.

Safety and Health

Littleton is a safe and healthy town, with a low crime rate and access to quality medical care. Here are some tips to ensure a safe and healthy trip to Littleton:

Safety in Littleton

  • Use caution when hiking or biking: Littleton’s hiking and biking trails offer stunning views of the mountains, but can also be challenging and rugged. Make sure to bring appropriate gear and plan your route in advance.
  • Be mindful of wildlife: As mentioned earlier, Littleton is home to many species of wildlife. If you encounter a bear, mountain lion, or elk, give them plenty of space and avoid provoking them.

Health in Littleton

  • Stay hydrated: Littleton’s high elevation can cause dehydration and altitude sickness, so make sure to drink plenty of water and take it easy during your first few days in town.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: Littleton receives over 300 days of sunshine per year, so make sure to wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses when spending time outdoors.

Bus Stations Near Littleton
