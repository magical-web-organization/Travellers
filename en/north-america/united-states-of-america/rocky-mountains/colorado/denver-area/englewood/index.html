Englewood, Colorado, is a charming city located in the Denver metropolitan area, one of the most beautiful regions in the United States, nestled in the lap of the Rocky Mountains. Known for its beautiful scenery and a plethora of recreational opportunities, Englewood is a popular destination for travelers. Whether you’re planning a weekend trip or a more extended stay, this comprehensive tour guide will help you discover the best things to do in Englewood, including outdoor activities, cultural attractions, local cuisine, and nearby destinations to visit.

Introduction to Englewood

Englewood is a historic community that dates back to the 1860s, with a rich cultural heritage and a growing urban vibe. The city’s downtown area is home to various restaurants, bars, and boutiques, offering a lively atmosphere for locals and visitors to partake in. Englewood is also renowned for its outdoor activities, including hiking and biking trails, fishing, and other recreational opportunities in the Rocky Mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Englewood

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of Outdoor Arts: A destination for art lovers, MOA is a museum without walls, featuring exhibits that focus on contemporary art, architecture, and design.
  2. Hampden Heights Park: A historic landmark, Hampden Heights Park has a historic barn that houses an art studio and offers classes in painting and other art forms.
  3. Historic Gothic Theatre: This iconic building is home to some of the best indie music concerts and offers a unique experience to visitors with its Gothic styling.

Natural Attractions of Englewood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Belleview Park: A family-friendly park with a miniature train, playgrounds, and picnic shelters.
  2. Chatfield Reservoir: A scenic lake where guests can take part in water-related activities such as boating, fishing, and camping.
  3. Cherry Creek State Park: A popular spot for hiking, biking, and bird-watching, this state park offers a variety of recreational activities in a stunning natural setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Englewood

Englewood has excellent hiking and biking trails, catering to all levels of skill and experience. Popular trails in the area include:

  1. Big Dry Creek Trail: A 12.4-mile trail that runs from Carpenter Park to Standley Lake in Westminster.
  2. High Line Canal: This 71-mile trail runs through Englewood and offers opportunities for running, walking, and biking in a serene natural setting.
  3. Bear Creek Trail: A 15-mile multi-use trail that provides stunning views of the foothills of the Rocky Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Englewood: Activities and Tours

  1. Englewood Farmers Market: Held every Tuesday afternoon from May through October, this is a great place to pick up fresh produce, crafts, and other goodies.
  2. Englewood Block Party: A summer event that takes place in the city streets, featuring live music, food, and drinks from local vendors.
  3. Art Walk: Held every third Friday of the month, the Art Walk allows visitors to view a broad range of art pieces and meet the artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Englewood

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Outdoor Arts, followed by a hike or bike ride on the High Line Canal Trail. End the day with dinner at a local restaurant in downtown Englewood.

Day 2: Spend the day at Chatfield Reservoir, participating in water activities or picnicking in the beautiful setting.

Day 3: Go shopping in the city’s boutique shops and have lunch at one of the local establishments downtown.

Day 4: Head to Belleview Park for family-friendly activities such as a miniature train ride or a picnic.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Denver, visiting attractions such as the Denver Art Museum or the Denver Zoo.

Day 6: Take a morning hike on the Big Dry Creek Trail, followed by an afternoon at the Englewood Swimming Pool.

Day 7: End your trip with a relaxing day at the Cherry Creek State Park, exploring the trails, bird-watching, and having a picnic by the lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Englewood

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Green Chile: A popular local dish made with roasted green chili peppers, pork, and seasonings served with tortillas.
  2. Rocky Mountain Oysters: A delicacy made from deep-fried bull testicles, served with dipping sauces.
  3. Bison Burger: A juicy burger pattie made from locally sourced bison meat, served with various toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. Colorado Margarita: A classic margarita made with blue agave tequila, Colorado honey, and fresh limes.
  2. Colorado Craft Beer: There are numerous craft breweries in the area, offering a wide variety of beers for beer enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Englewood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Inverness Hotel and Resort: A luxury resort with scenic views of the Rocky Mountains, featuring a golf course, spa, and multiple dining options.
  2. The Embassy Suites by Hilton Denver Tech Center North: A high-rise hotel with spacious suites, fitness center, and indoor pool.
  3. Airbnb Guesthouses: Guests can find many affordable and comfortable options such as private rooms or entire flats easily.

What to Buy in Englewood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Colorado Made Products: Numerous stores sell locally made products such as handcrafted souvenirs, artisanal foods, and beverages.
  2. Native Art Artifacts: The pueblo tribes of Southwest to Northern America produce many their own glass art, pottery, and weavings.
  3. Western Clothing: Cowboy hats also remain a sought after souvenirs of the American West and a timeless fashion statement.

Local Culture and Customs of Englewood

Englewood is a melting pot of cultures, and its residents are welcoming and friendly to visitors. The city hosts numerous celebrations throughout the year, honoring traditions, music, and art.

Best Time to Visit Englewood

The best time to visit Englewood is during the summer months when temperatures are mild, and outdoor activities are plentiful. The spring and fall months also offer excellent weather conditions, with lower tourist volume. Winters can be quite cold and snowy but offer a different set of experiences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Englewood

Do:

  1. Respect the natural environment and leave no trash behind while hiking or enjoying nature
  2. Be punctual when meeting locals or attending events.
  3. Be respectful of cultural traditions and customs.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.
  2. Don’t forget to tip for services such as dining or housekeeping.
  3. Don’t be loud and disrespectful in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Englewood

Englewood is a relatively safe city to visit, with a low crime rate. As with any city, it’s advisable to keep a watchful eye on belongings and avoid walking in dark or unlit areas at night.

Health in Englewood

Englewood has excellent medical facilities, and emergency services are readily available. Visitors should take precautions to avoid altitude sickness if hiking at high elevations in the Rocky Mountains.

Getting to Englewood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Englewood

  1. Denver International Airport (DEN): Denver International Airport (DEN) is the closest major airport to Englewood, located approximately 25 miles northeast of Englewood.
  2. Colorado Springs Airport (COS): Colorado Springs Airport (COS) is approximately 70 miles south of Englewood.

Bus Stations Near Englewood

  1. Greyhound Bus Lines: The bus station for Greyhound is located in Denver’s Union Station.

Other Options

  1. Car rental: Car rentals are available at the Denver International Airport, and various other rental facilities are located in Englewood.
  2. Taxi/Uber/Lyft: Taxis, Uber, and Lyft services are available in Englewood.

How to Get Around in Englewood

Englewood is a bike-friendly city, with a growing network of bike lanes. Visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses and trains, or rent a car or bike from a local rental agency.

Where to Go Next from Englewood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Denver: A vibrant city just a short drive away from Englewood, Denver is home to world-class museums, restaurants, and nightlife.
  2. Colorado Springs: A beautiful city located approximately 70 miles away from Englewood, Colorado Springs is the gateway to some of Colorado’s best hiking and outdoor adventures.
  3. Boulder: A scenic city approximately 30 miles northwest of Englewood that offers numerous recreational opportunities, such as rock climbing and hiking through its scenic canyons.

Conclusion

Englewood, Colorado, is a hidden gem in the heart of the Rocky Mountains, offering an abundance of scenic beauty and recreational opportunities for visitors. Whether you’re exploring the city’s cultural heritage or hiking and biking through the trails in the Rocky Mountains, Englewood has something for everyone. With its friendly residents, rich history, and stunning scenery, it’s easy to see why Englewood is such a popular destination for travelers.
