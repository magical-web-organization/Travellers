Introduction to Centennial

Centennial, a city located in the Denver Area of Colorado, is a great destination for those looking for outdoor adventures, scenic views, and rich cultural experiences. This beautiful city, surrounded by Rocky Mountains, has a vibrant food scene, a variety of accommodations, and plenty of activities to keep you entertained.

List of Places to Visit in Centennial

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Centennial has a rich history that comes to life in several museums and historical places located throughout the city. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Centennial Center Park: This 11-acre park is a hub for community events and features a variety of amenities, including an amphitheater, splash park, and a playground.

  • Littleton Museum: This living-history museum is set on 39 acres and features two living history farms, an exhibit gallery, and a research center.

  • Colorado Freedom Memorial: This stunning memorial is located in nearby Aurora and honors the 6,000 Coloradans who lost their lives in military conflicts from 1870 to present day.

Natural Attractions of Centennial: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its location in the Rocky Mountains, Centennial is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, fishing, or wildlife watching, you’ll find plenty of opportunities to explore. Some of the best natural attractions in Centennial include:

  • Cherry Creek State Park: With 4,200 acres of open space, this park offers a wide variety of activities, including boating, fishing, camping, hiking, and biking.

  • South Platte Park and Carson Nature Center: This park and nature center is a wonderful place to explore the beauty of the area. The park boasts miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, while the nature center offers educational exhibits and activities.

  • Rocky Mountain Arsenal National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge is located just a few miles north of Centennial and provides a home for a wide variety of wildlife, including bison, eagles, and coyotes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Centennial

Centennial is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. With miles of trails to explore, you’ll find plenty of opportunities to get outside and explore the natural beauty of the area. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Centennial include:

  • High Line Canal Trail: This 71-mile trail follows an irrigation canal through the Denver metropolitan area and offers beautiful views of the area.

  • DeKoevend Park Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is a popular spot for hiking and biking in Centennial and is known for its beautiful wildflowers and scenic views.

  • South Suburban Loop Trail: This 14-mile trail is a great option for those looking for a longer hike or bike ride and offers stunning views of the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Centennial: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Centennial offers plenty of other activities and tours to keep you entertained. Some of the top must-do activities in Centennial include:

  • Visit Centennial Center Park: This park is a hub for community events and offers a variety of amenities, including an amphitheater, playground, and a splash park.

  • Explore the local breweries: Centennial is home to several local breweries, including Resolute Brewing Company and Dry Dock Brewing Company.

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: For a truly unique experience, consider taking a hot air balloon ride over the stunning landscape of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Centennial

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Centennial, you won’t be short on things to do. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activities
1 Explore Centennial Center Park and nearby hiking trails
2 Visit Littleton Museum and explore the local food scene
3 Take a hot air balloon ride and explore local breweries
4 Visit Colorado Freedom Memorial and Rocky Mountain Arsenal
5 Go fishing or boating at Cherry Creek State Park
6 Visit South Platte Park and Carson Nature Center
7 Take a day trip to nearby Rocky Mountain National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Centennial

Centennial has a vibrant food scene and a variety of local specialties to try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colorado Green Chili: This spicy stew is a Colorado classic and is made with green chilies, pork, and a variety of spices.

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: These unique “oysters” are actually deep-fried bull testicles and are a local delicacy in Colorado.

  • Bison Burger: Bison is a popular meat in Colorado, and a bison burger is a must-try for any meat lover.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Centennial is home to several local breweries, including Resolute Brewing Company and Dry Dock Brewing Company, where you can try a variety of craft beers.

  • Colorado Wine: The warm days and cool nights of Colorado provide excellent conditions for growing grapes, and there are several local wineries to explore.

Where to Stay in Centennial: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Centennial offers a variety of accommodations for every budget. Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, you’ll find plenty of options in Centennial. Some of the best places to stay in Centennial include:

  • Hyatt Regency Denver Tech Center: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding area and is conveniently located near many of Centennial’s top attractions.

  • Home2 Suites by Hilton Denver South/Centennial Airport: This affordable hotel offers spacious rooms and is located close to the airport and several local attractions.

  • Hostel Fish: This budget-friendly hostel is located in nearby Denver and offers dorm-style rooms and private rooms at a great price.

What to Buy in Centennial: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some souvenirs from your trip to Centennial, here are some of the best local products to consider:

  • Colorado beer and wine: Pick up a few bottles of local craft beer or wine to bring home with you.

  • Handmade jewelry and leather goods: There are many local artisans and craftspeople in the area who make beautiful handmade jewelry and leather goods.

  • Local honey and jams: Colorado is home to many small-scale farmers who produce high-quality honey and jams from local ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Centennial

Like many cities in Colorado, Centennial has a laid-back and welcoming culture. Respect for the environment and a love of the outdoors are also important parts of the local culture. While there are no specific customs that visitors need to be aware of, visitors should be respectful of local customs and be mindful of their impact on the environment.

Best Time to Visit Centennial

The best time to visit Centennial is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the fall months are also a great time to visit, as the changing leaves offer stunning views of the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Centennial

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • Do try some of the local specialties, such as Colorado green chili and bison burgers.
  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave any litter or trash on the trails or in the parks.
  • Don’t take any souvenirs from the parks or natural areas.
  • Don’t drive under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

Safety and Health

Safety in Centennial

Centennial is a generally safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight, and be aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking in the area. In case of an emergency, dial 911.

Health in Centennial

Centennial has excellent medical facilities and hospitals. However, visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies that may arise during their trip.

Getting to Centennial: Transportation Options and Logistics

Centennial is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options. Here are some of the ways you can get to Centennial:

Airports Near Centennial

  • Denver International Airport: Located about 26 miles from Centennial, this is the largest airport in the area and serves many domestic and international destinations.

  • Centennial Airport: This airport is located in Centennial and serves private and corporate aircraft.

Bus Stations Near Centennial

  • Denver Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Denver, this station offers bus services to Centennial and other destinations in the area.

  • RTD Light Rail Stations: The RTD light rail system serves many locations in the Denver metropolitan area, including Centennial.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a great option for those who want to explore the area on their own.

  • Taxi or Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Centennial

Centennial is a walkable city, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, for those who don’t want to walk or bike, there are several transportation options available, including rental cars, taxis, and ride-sharing services.

Where to Go Next from Centennial: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Centennial is located in the heart of the Denver metropolitan area, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Some of the top nearby destinations include:

  • Denver: Explore the vibrant city of Denver, with its many museums, art galleries, and cultural attractions.

  • Rocky Mountain National Park: Head west from Centennial to explore the spectacular natural beauty of Rocky Mountain National Park.

  • Garden of the Gods: Located in nearby Colorado Springs, the Garden of the Gods is a stunning natural area featuring towering sandstone formations.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and variety of activities and attractions, Centennial is a destination that has something for everyone. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, you’ll find plenty of reasons to come and explore all that Centennial has to offer.
