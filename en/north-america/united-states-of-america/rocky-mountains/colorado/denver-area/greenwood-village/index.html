Introduction to Greenwood Village

Located in the Denver Area, Greenwood Village is a suburban town in Colorado’s Rocky Mountains. It is well-known for its beautiful scenery, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions, making it a popular destination among travelers. In this guide, you will discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around in Greenwood Village, as well as its unique local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Greenwood Village

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Greenwood Village is rich in history, arts, and culture, and it offers visitors numerous attractions and historic sites to explore. Some of the top ones include:

  • Greenwood Village History Museum: This museum features exhibitions and artifacts that showcase the history and heritage of Greenwood Village and the Denver Area. Visitors can also learn about the Native American cultures, the pioneers, and the early settlers who shaped the region.
  • The Madden Museum of Art: Featuring a collection of contemporary art, this museum showcases the works of local and international artists. It also hosts special exhibitions, lectures, and events throughout the year.
  • Silent Voices: Located at the Curtis Arts and Humanities Center, this exhibit chronicles the lives and history of African-American pioneers in Colorado.
  • Hudson Gardens: This botanical garden is a fantastic place to enjoy the beautiful scenery of Greenwood Village. It offers a wide range of plant species, ponds, streams, and walking paths. Visitors can also enjoy events and concerts in this tranquil setting.
  • Aurora History Museum: Located near Greenwood Village, this museum features exhibitions that highlight the history and cultural heritage of the region. It offers rotating exhibits, special events, and educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Greenwood Village: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Greenwood Village boasts several outdoor attractions that offer visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains. Some of the top ones include:

  • High Line Canal Trail: This 71-mile trail is one of the best places in Greenwood Village to go for a hike, jog, or bike ride. It runs along the historic High Line Canal, offering breathtaking views of the Front Range and the plains.
  • The Bluffs Regional Park: This park offers stunning vistas of the Rocky Mountains and the city skyline. It features trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as picnic areas and playgrounds.
  • Chatfield State Park: This park is a must-visit attraction for outdoor enthusiasts. It offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, fishing, boating, and horseback riding. Visitors can also enjoy a scenic hot air balloon ride.
  • Red Rocks Park and Amphitheater: Located near Denver, this park features towering red rock formations, hiking trails, and an amphitheater that hosts concerts and events.
  • Mount Evans: At 14,265 feet, Mount Evans is one of the highest peaks in Colorado. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can drive to the summit or hike one of the many trails that lead to the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenwood Village

Greenwood Village is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in Colorado. Here are some of the top ones to explore:

  • High Line Canal Trail: This 71-mile trail is ideal for hiking, biking, and horse riding. It winds through the city and offers picturesque views of the Front Range and the plains.
  • Cherry Creek State Park: This park offers a network of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. It features diverse wildlife, scenic vistas, and a reservoir for swimming and boating.
  • South Platte Park Trail: This leisurely trail is perfect for a family bike ride or a relaxing hike. It runs along the South Platte River and offers great views of local wildlife and the Rocky Mountains.
  • Chatfield State Park: This park features over 26 miles of multi-use trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. It offers a range of terrain, including forests, prairies, and wetlands.
  • The Bluffs Regional Park: This park features a network of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding that offer stunning views of the city skyline.

Best Things to Do in Greenwood Village: Activities and Tours

Greenwood Village has plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Fly Fishing Tour: Visitors can enjoy a guided fly fishing tour at one of the many streams, rivers, and lakes in Greenwood Village.
  • Segway Tour: Tourists can explore and discover the town on a Segway, visiting local landmarks, parks, and trails.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: A hot air balloon ride provides spectacular views of the Rocky Mountains and Greenwood Village’s rolling hills.
  • Horseback Riding Tour: Visitors can explore the local trails on a horseback riding tour with a guide.
  • Craft Beer Tour: Visitors can sample locally brewed craft beers and learn about the brewing process by taking a craft beer tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenwood Village

Greenwood Village offers a wide range of dining options, from gourmet restaurants to laid-back cafes and bistros. Some of the must-visit places include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colorado Chili: A spicy stew made with beef, pork, and a variety of chili peppers.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A local delicacy made from deep-fried bull testicles.
  • Smoked Bison Brisket: A dish made with slow-smoked bison, seasoned with a dry rub and topped with BBQ sauce.
  • Green Chili: A dish made from Colorado green chilies, pork, and beans, served with tortillas.

Local Beverages

Colorado is known for its craft beer scene, and Greenwood Village has several breweries and pubs that serve locally brewed beers, some of which are:

  • Comrade Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a range of IPAs, stouts, and lagers in a casual atmosphere.
  • Crazy Mountain Brewery: This brewpub serves a wide variety of beers, including a range of international and local styles. It also offers a delicious menu of pub grub.
  • Dry Dock Brewing Company: This award-winning brewery offers a range of ales, lagers, and seasonal beers that are crafted locally.

What to Buy in Greenwood Village: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Greenwood Village has several shops and stores that offer unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the best places to shop include:

  • The Shop at Hudson Gardens: This shop offers unique floral arrangements, garden gifts, and household decor items inspired by Hudson Gardens.
  • Centennial Fine Wine and Spirits: This store offers a range of locally produced spirits, wines, and beers, as well as gourmet food items.
  • Orchard Equestrian: This shop specializes in equestrian gear, apparel, and accessories, and offers custom-fit services for riders.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenwood Village

Greenwood Village is a town with a rich cultural heritage, arts, and traditions. It is also home to a diverse community that promotes tolerance and inclusivity. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, as well as support the local arts, music, and businesses.

Best Time to Visit Greenwood Village

The best time to visit Greenwood Village is from April to October, when the weather is mostly pleasant, and the outdoor attractions are open. The summer months (June to August) can be crowded, so visitors should book accommodations and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenwood Village

Do:

  • Do explore the outdoors: Greenwood Village is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous parks, trails, and natural attractions to explore.

  • Do try the local cuisine: Greenwood Village offers a range of unique dishes and drinks that visitors should sample.

  • Do contribute to the local economy: Visitors should support the local businesses, arts, and music scene.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Visitors should respect the environment and dispose of waste properly.

  • Don’t engage in disrespectful behavior: Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, as well as the diverse community.

  • Don’t overlook the small businesses: Greenwood Village has several small businesses that offer unique products and services that visitors should discover.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenwood Village

Greenwood Village is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. They should also follow safety rules and regulations when engage in outdoor activities.

Health in Greenwood Village

Greenwood Village has several medical clinics and hospitals that offer emergency and non-emergency health services. Visitors should also bring any necessary medication and follow basic health protocols, such as drinking plenty of water, protecting themselves from the sun, and avoiding strenuous physical activity in high altitudes.

Getting to Greenwood Village: Transportation Options and Logistics

Greenwood Village is easily accessible by plane or car, and it offers several transportation options for visitors.

Airports Near Greenwood Village

The closest airport to Greenwood Village is Denver International Airport, which is approximately 30 minutes’ drive from the town. Visitors can also fly into Colorado Springs Airport, which is about 60 miles from Greenwood Village.

Bus Stations Near Greenwood Village

The town is well-served by several bus stations that offer regional and intercity services. The main ones are the Regional Transportation District (RTD) and Greyhound.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, take a taxi, or use ride-sharing services, such as Uber or Lyft, to reach Greenwood Village from the airport or nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Greenwood Village

Greenwood Village is a pedestrian-friendly town, but visitors can also use public transportation, such as the RTD buses and light rail services, or rent a bike or a car to get around. There are also several private shuttle services that offer door-to-door transportation.

Where to Go Next from Greenwood Village: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Greenwood Village is centrally located in Colorado, which makes it an ideal base to explore nearby attractions and excursions. Some of the best ones include:

  • Denver: The state capital and the largest city in Colorado offer a vibrant downtown area, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities.
  • Golden: This historic town is famous for its beer breweries, museums, and scenic drives along Lariat Loop.
  • Boulder: This college town offers a range of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Rocky Mountain National Park: This national park offers epic mountain vistas, hiking trails, wildlife, and scenic drives.
  • Pikes Peak: One of Colorado’s highest peaks, with an elevation of 14,115 feet, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Conclusion

Greenwood Village is a fantastic destination for travelers who want to experience the natural beauty, arts, culture, and traditions of Colorado. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, local cuisine, and unique shopping, the town offers something for everyone. Visitors should plan their trip in advance, be respectful of the local customs, and enjoy their stay in this charming town.
