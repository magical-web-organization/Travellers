Introduction to Arvada

Arvada is a city located in the Denver Area, in Colorado, within the breathtaking Rocky Mountains. With more than 100,000 residents, this friendly destination offers an abundance of outdoor activities and cultural experiences to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Arvada

Here are our top recommendations for places to visit in Arvada:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arvada Center for the Arts and Humanities: This cultural gem showcases an array of visual and performing arts exhibits to explore.
  2. Cussler Museum: A unique museum featuring rare automobiles and vintage car collections.
  3. McIlvoy House: A local historic site with Victorian architecture and vintage furnishings.

Natural Attractions of Arvada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Standley Lake: A prime fishing and boating destination, this lake offers sublime scenic views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Rocky Flats National Wildlife Refuge: A wildlife haven known for its habitats and hiking trails.
  3. Apex Park and Recreation District: A great park for hiking, biking, and picnicking to enjoy the soothing outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arvada

Arvada boasts an abundance of biking and hiking trails, ranging from moderate to strenuous. The Ralston Creek Trail and Van Bibber Trail are popular with visitors and locals alike, offering scenic views and an opportunity to explore the beautiful natural environment of Arvada.

Best Things to Do in Arvada: Activities and Tours

  1. Olde Town Arvada Historic Walking Tour: A fascinating walking tour that highlights the city’s rich history and architecture.
  2. Arvada Center for the Arts and Humanities Theater Performances: A must-see experience to appreciate a variety of the performing arts.
  3. Arvada Farmers Market: A communal hub where visitors can buy local produce, spices, and much more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arvada

For those planning a week-long trip to Arvada, here is our suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Morning Hike at Apex Park and Recreation District, followed by the Cussler Museum
2 Standley Lake Day Trip
3 Olde Town Arvada Historic Walking Tour, followed by the Arvada Center for the Arts and Humanities for a show
4 Ralston Creek Trail for a morning run, followed by a lunch break on the McIlvoy House Veranda
5 Nature Day Trip to Rocky Flats National Wildlife Refuge
6 Zip Line Adventure at the Outdoor Gravity Park
7 Visit to the Arvada Farmers Market, followed by Dinner with live music at the Arvada Tavern

Food and Drink Scene of Arvada

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Samosa Chaat: This tasty treat is a traditional Indian dish that is popular in many restaurants in Arvada.
  2. Green Chile: A classic Colorado dish that is spicy, savory, and flavorful.
  3. Coloradough Pizza: A must-try for pizza enthusiasts – this pizza boasts local flavors and toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. Colorado Beer: Arvada is home to a multitude of breweries serving up the top local beers in Colorado.
  2. Cinnamon Sugar Cream Liqueur: A mouth-watering cream liqueur that tastes like a cinnamon roll in liquid form.
  3. Plum Creek Wine: A locally-crafted wine that features Colorado-grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Arvada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arvada has a diverse range of accommodation options for all types of travelers. Whether you are seeking a cozy bed and breakfast or a luxurious hotel, you’ll find it all in Arvada. Some of our top recommendations include:

  1. Hilton Garden Inn Denver/Arvada: A contemporary hotel that offers a relaxing, spa-like vacation experience.
  2. The Arvada Dragonfly: A cozy bed and breakfast that provides a comfortable, homely stay.
  3. Arvada Street Car Airbnb: A unique accommodation option that will take you back in time – this renovated streetcar is transformed into a modern and comfortable space.

What to Buy in Arvada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arvada has a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make for an excellent keepsake. Here are our top recommendations:

  1. Handmade Jewelry: From handcrafted rings to intricate necklaces, there are numerous jewelry options in Arvada to choose from.
  2. Colorado Wine: Pick up some locally-brewed wine from Inkwell & Brew – a must-visit wine shop in Arvada.
  3. Colorado Honey: You can’t leave without trying the delicious honey from Bee-Nevolent Honey – a local gem in Arvada.

Local Culture and Customs of Arvada

Arvada embraces a friendly and welcoming culture, with a strong sense of community. The locals take pride in preserving the city’s rich history, cultural activities, and natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Arvada

The best time to visit Arvada is from May to September when the weather is milder and suitable for enjoying outdoor activities. Additionally, you can also experience the local farmers market and enjoy the vibrant cultural events hosted during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arvada

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Be mindful of your surroundings when hiking or biking on the trails.
  3. Take the time to visit and appreciate the locally-run businesses.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural environment of Arvada.
  2. Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  3. Assume the weather will be warm year-round.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arvada

Arvada is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. Nonetheless, visitors should always be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially when venturing out into natural landscapes.

Health in Arvada

Colorado has a high altitude, which may cause altitude sickness for visitors who are not accustomed to high elevations. Take the time to adjust to the altitude and stay hydrated to mitigate any potential health issues.

Getting to Arvada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arvada

The primary airport to consider when traveling to Arvada is the Denver International Airport (DEN), which is approximately 34 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Arvada

There are several bus stations in the vicinity of Arvada, such as the Denver Union Station and the Boulder Transit Center.

Other Options

An Uber or Lyft ride or rental car are also popular options to get to Arvada from nearby cities or airports.

How to Get Around in Arvada

Arvada is a small city that is easily navigable by foot. Biking and walking are popular transportation options, but visitors can also choose to rent a car to access nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Arvada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Boulder: A charming city located about 20 miles from Arvada and home to the famous Pearl Street Mall.
  2. Denver: Colorado’s capital city, known for its thriving art and culture scene, and the iconic Red Rocks Amphitheatre.
  3. Vail: A world-renowned ski destination, Vail is also known for its picturesque mountain landscapes, hiking trails, and vibrant arts scene.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and abundance of outdoor activities, Arvada is an ideal destination for any traveler seeking to explore the best of Colorado’s Rocky Mountains. From hiking and biking to discovering the local food scene, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Arvada.
