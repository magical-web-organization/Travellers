Introduction to Lakewood

Lakewood is a scenic city located in the Denver area of Colorado, in the Rocky Mountains region of the United States of America. With an abundance of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, Lakewood is a great destination for both solo travelers and families.

This guide will provide you with all the essential information you need to know before you visit Lakewood, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Lakewood

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lakewood Heritage Center

The Lakewood Heritage Center is a living history museum, featuring over 30 historic structures and buildings dating back to the 1800s. The museum is located on a 40-acre site and offers visitors a glimpse into the history and culture of Lakewood and the surrounding area.

Colorado Railroad Museum

The Colorado Railroad Museum is a must-visit attraction for train enthusiasts. The museum features over 100 historic locomotives, passenger cars, and cabooses, as well as a model railroad exhibit and interactive exhibits for children.

Natural Attractions of Lakewood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bear Creek Lake Park

Bear Creek Lake Park is a popular outdoor recreation area, offering hiking, biking, fishing, and camping opportunities. The park is home to a large lake, sandy beaches, and scenic mountain views.

Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre

Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre is a world-famous outdoor music venue, but it’s also a beautiful natural area with stunning rock formations and hiking trails. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, or a picnic while taking in the breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakewood

Green Mountain Trail

The Green Mountain Trail is a scenic 7.5-mile hike that offers panoramic views of Denver, the Rocky Mountains, and the surrounding area. It’s a moderate hike that’s suitable for families with children.

Bear Creek Trail

The Bear Creek Trail is a popular biking and hiking route that stretches over 20 miles from Denver to Morrison. The trail passes through charming neighborhoods and offers stunning views of the Rocky Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Lakewood: Activities and Tours

Lakewood Farmers Market

The Lakewood Farmers Market is a popular summer attraction, offering fresh produce, handmade crafts, and live music. Visitors can enjoy shopping for local goods while experiencing the authentic culture of Lakewood.

Lakewood Cultural Center

The Lakewood Cultural Center is a performing arts venue that hosts concerts, theater productions, and art exhibits. It’s a great place to soak up some culture while visiting Lakewood.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakewood

Must-Try Dishes

Colorado-style Green Chili

This spicy stew is a staple in Colorado cuisine, made with pork, green chilies, and a mix of spices. It’s the perfect dish to try if you’re looking for a taste of authentic Colorado cuisine.

Rocky Mountain Oysters

Rocky Mountain Oysters are a famous Colorado dish, made from bull’s testicles that are battered and fried. They’re not for the faint of heart, but they’re a must-try for adventurous eaters.

Local Beverages

Colorado Craft Beer

Colorado is known for its thriving craft beer scene, with over 400 breweries across the state. Some popular breweries in the Lakewood area include Great Frontier Brewing Company, Ironworks Brewery, and WestFax Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Lakewood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hampton Inn Denver West Federal Center

The Hampton Inn Denver West Federal Center is a centrally located hotel with comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a variety of amenities. It’s a great option for budget-conscious travelers.

Hyatt House Denver/Lakewood at Belmar

The Hyatt House Denver/Lakewood at Belmar is a more upscale option, with spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant. It’s perfect for travelers looking for a more luxurious stay in Lakewood.

What to Buy in Lakewood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Colorado-Sourced Honey

Colorado honey is known for its unique flavor and health benefits. You can find a variety of locally sourced honey products, including raw honey, honeycomb, and honey-infused skincare products.

Handmade Crafts

The Lakewood Farmers Market is a great place to find handmade crafts and gifts made by local artisans. Some popular items include pottery, jewelry, and hand-sewn clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakewood

Lakewood is known for its laid-back, outdoorsy culture. Locals are friendly and welcoming, and there’s a strong sense of community in the city. Visitors should respect the natural beauty of the area and follow Leave No Trace principles when hiking or camping in the surrounding parks.

Best Time to Visit Lakewood

The best time to visit Lakewood is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is warm and sunny, and many of the outdoor activities and festivals take place. Fall is also a great time to visit, with cooler temperatures and beautiful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakewood

Do:

  • Enjoy the many outdoor recreation opportunities in the area, from hiking and biking to camping and fishing.
  • Try the local cuisine, including Colorado-style green chili and Rocky Mountain oysters.
  • Respect the natural beauty of the area and follow Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the parks.
  • Overlook the cultural attractions in the city, including museums, theaters, and art galleries.
  • Trespass on private property when hiking or exploring the surrounding area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakewood

Lakewood is a safe city overall, but visitors should still take common-sense precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone after dark.

Health in Lakewood

Lakewood has a good healthcare system and many medical facilities. Visitors should be prepared for the high altitude in the area and drink plenty of water to avoid altitude sickness.

Getting to Lakewood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lakewood

The closest airport to Lakewood is Denver International Airport, which is located about 35 miles from the city.

Bus Stations Near Lakewood

The Greyhound bus station at Denver Union Station is the closest bus station to Lakewood, and can be reached via public transportation or taxi/rideshare services.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great option for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area on their own schedule.

How to Get Around in Lakewood

Public transportation options in Lakewood are limited, so renting a car or using rideshare services like Uber or Lyft is the easiest way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Lakewood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Golden, Colorado

Golden is a charming mountain town located just a few miles from Lakewood. It’s known for its historic downtown area, outdoor recreation opportunities, and craft breweries.

Boulder, Colorado

Boulder is a vibrant city located about 30 miles from Lakewood. It’s known for its college-town atmosphere, beautiful hiking trails, and local food scene.

Conclusion

Lakewood is a hidden gem in the Denver area, with plenty of natural and cultural attractions to explore. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a cultural connoisseur, there’s something for everyone in Lakewood. Use this guide to plan your perfect trip to this beautiful Colorado city.
