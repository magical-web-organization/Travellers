Introduction to Devils Tower National Monument

Devils Tower National Monument is a unique geological wonder located in northeastern Wyoming and is known for its iconic towering rock formation. It stands at 1,267 feet tall and is considered sacred by many Native American tribes.

The monument provides visitors with an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Wyoming’s landscape while learning about the history and culture of the surrounding area.

Local Culture and Customs of Devils Tower National Monument

Devils Tower National Monument is a culturally diverse and significant place, and its importance varies according to different local cultures. Visitors need to understand and respect these cultures while appreciating the site’s beauty.

Best Time to Visit Devils Tower National Monument

The best time to visit Devils Tower National Monument is from May through October, during the warm and dry season. Visitors are encouraged to check the park’s weather conditions during the visit as the weather can be unpredictable at times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Devils Tower National Monument

Do:

  • Plan ahead and book campsites in advance.
  • Stay on designated trails.
  • Take enough water supply.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Attempt to climb the monolith during the month of June in respect to Native American traditions.
  • Bring drones as they are forbidden.
  • Feed the animals.
