Introduction to Sheridan

Sheridan is a city in Northeast Wyoming, in the Rocky Mountains, USA. Known for its rich history, beautiful scenery, and western culture, Sheridan is a must-see destination for travelers.

In this tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, activities and tours, local culture, food and drink scene, where to stay, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Sheridan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Sheridan County Museum - The museum exhibits traces the local history of Sheridan County through artifacts, photographs, and storytelling.
  2. Bradford Brinton Memorial & Museum - Set in a beautiful ranch location, the museum houses Western art, Native American artifacts, and ranching memorabilia.
  3. King’s Saddlery and Museum - The museum displays a vast collection of Western saddles and leatherworking tools, and an art gallery showcasing Western art such as paintings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Sheridan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bighorn National Forest - The forest covers more than 1 million acres of rugged mountain wilderness with hiking, fishing, hunting, and camping opportunities.
  2. Tongue River Canyon Trail - The trail is a popular hiking and mountain biking route that winds through a scenic canyon offering awe-inspiring views.
  3. Shell Falls - This 120-foot waterfall is a beautiful scenic spot for photography, hiking, or a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sheridan

  1. Soldier Ridge Trail - A 6.7-mile loop trail that offers a moderate hiking experience with beautiful views of the peaks and valleys of the Bighorn Mountains.
  2. Red Grade Trail - A scenic 13.1-mile mountain biking route that offers views of the Bighorn Range, wildflowers, and aspen trees.
  3. Cloud Peak Skyway Trail - A picturesque 63-mile paved highway that winds through the beautiful scenery of the Bighorn Mountains with stops for hiking and wildlife viewing.

Best Things to Do in Sheridan: Activities and Tours

  1. Rodeo - Sheridan is considered the “Rodeo Capital of the World” with frequent rodeo shows and events throughout the year.
  2. Fly Fishing - The nearby rivers provide some of the best fly fishing opportunities in the region.
  3. Horseback Riding - Traditional western horseback riding experiences are available in the area.
  4. Gun Range - For those who enjoy shooting, there are many ranges available with shotgun, pistol or rifle shooting options.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sheridan

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Sheridan County Museum and explore the town’s history
Day 2 Hike the Soldier Ridge Trail and have lunch at the Elk View Inn
Day 3 Go fly fishing on the Tongue River
Day 4 Take a scenic drive on the Cloud Peak Skyway and stop for a hike on the way
Day 5 Visit Bradford Brinton Memorial & Museum followed by horseback riding
Day 6 Attend a rodeo show at the Sheridan County Fairgrounds
Day 7 Explore the Bighorn National Forest and camp overnight at West Tensleep Trailhead

Food and Drink Scene of Sheridan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bison Burger - Enjoy a juicy, lean, and protein-packed bison burger while in Sheridan.
  2. Rocky Mountain Oysters - A local delicacy that is not actually oysters, but deep-fried bull testicles.
  3. Fried Chicken - Sheridan has several restaurants that serve the best Southern fried chicken

Local Beverages

  1. Sheridan beer - A craft brewery with a taproom that serves unique beers, such as the Coal Miner Stout made with locally sourced coal.
  2. Wyoming Whiskey - Award-winning whiskey made with Wyoming grains and hand-selected aged barrels.

Where to Stay in Sheridan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sheridan Inn - A historic hotel with onsite dining, in-room amenities, and access to local attractions.
  2. Mill Inn - A friendly and affordable hotel located in downtown Sheridan
  3. YKnot Inn - A cozy and comfortable guesthouse with stunning views of the Bighorn Mountains

What to Buy in Sheridan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Western Decor - Sheridan has many shops that sell traditional Western home decor and furniture
  2. Leather Goods - Visit King’s Saddlery for high-quality leather goods, including saddles, boots, hats, and belts.
  3. Art - Sheridan has many local art galleries with a variety of western art for sale.

Local Culture and Customs of Sheridan

Western culture is deeply rooted in the Town of Sheridan, with a strong history of ranching, rodeo, and mining. In the summer, the town hosts many rodeo events, celebrations, and live music performances.

Best Time to Visit Sheridan

The best time to visit Sheridan is from June to September when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities, the town is bustling with events, and the fall colors are in full display making it a treat for your eyes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sheridan

Do:

  1. Try local cuisine such as bison burgers or Rocky Mountain oysters.
  2. Attend a rodeo event and explore the western culture of the town.
  3. Explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  1. Litter. Keep the area clean and beautiful.
  2. Trespass - Always stay out of private properties in the area.
  3. Ignore wildlife safety rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sheridan

Sheridan is generally a safe town, but it is always good to take some precautions:

  1. Use common sense and be aware of your surroundings in crowded places
  2. Avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas
  3. Be aware of wildlife and use appropriate caution when hiking.

Health in Sheridan

Sheridan has some medical centers and hospitals, but it’s always better to have travel insurance when visiting Sheridan.

Getting to Sheridan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sheridan

  1. Sheridan County Airport - Located just 2.5 miles from downtown Sheridan, it offers flights from Denver, Colorado, via United Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Sheridan

  1. Arrow/Black Hills Stage Lines - Provides bus services from Sheridan to major cities, including Billings, MT, and Denver, CO.

Other Options

  1. Driving - Sheridan is easily accessible by car from Denver, Co, and other Montana and Wyoming cities.
  2. Taxi/Cab Services - Several companies in Sheridan offer taxi and cab services.

How to Get Around in Sheridan

The town is relatively small, making it easily accessible on foot or by bicycle. Taxis, rental cars, and shuttles are readily available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Sheridan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Yellowstone National Park - One of the world’s most visited destinations, located 300 miles west of Sheridan.
  2. Devil’s Tower National Monument - A striking geological formation located just an hour’s drive away from Sheridan.
  3. Black Hills National Forest - The forest provides ample opportunities for hiking and biking and is located three hours from Sheridan.

Conclusion

Sheridan is a beautiful town in northeast Wyoming that offers visitors a blend of natural beauty, western culture, and outdoor adventures. With this tour guide, tourists can explore the best places to visit, local cuisine, where to stay, and how to make the most out of their trip. Come visit Sheridan and experience the rich culture of the American West.
