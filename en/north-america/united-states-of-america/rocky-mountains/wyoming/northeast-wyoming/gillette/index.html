Introduction to Gillette

Nestled in the northeast of Wyoming, Gillette is a hidden gem that offers plenty of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. Known as the “Energy Capital of the Nation,” it is a gateway to the vast prairies and stunning landscapes of the Rocky Mountains. If you are a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, Gillette is the perfect destination to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Gillette

Whether you want to explore the rich cultural heritage or bask in the scenic beauty of Gillette, there is a lot to see and do. Here are some of the must-visit destinations in Gillette:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Campbell County Rockpile Museum - It houses an exceptional collection of exhibits, artifacts, and photographs that illustrate the history of Northeast Wyoming’s coal, cattle, and oil industries.
  • Gillette Railroad Museum - Discover the history of transportation in Gillette with a visit to this museum. Located amidst Union Pacific’s main lines, the museum houses a vast collection of railroad artifacts and restored railway cars.
  • Devils Tower National Monument - One of the most iconic landmarks of the United States, Devils Tower National Monument is just a short drive away from Gillette. It has cultural and religious significance among Native American tribes.

Natural Attractions of Gillette: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keyhole State Park - Located in the Black Hills region, it offers a wide range of recreational activities such as fishing, boating, and hunting. It also has several hiking trails that offer picturesque views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area - This majestic canyon spreads across Wyoming and Montana and offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation, such as hiking, camping, and rock climbing. Explore the canyon by boat, taking in the views of towering cliffs and spectacular rock formations.
  • Thunder Basin National Grassland - This vast natural area is a haven for birdwatchers and wildlife enthusiasts. It is home to several species of animals, including bison, coyotes, and pronghorn.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gillette

Gillette boasts some of the best trails in Wyoming, offering hiking and biking routes for all levels of experience. Here are some of the popular hiking routes in Gillette:

  • Overlook Trail - With panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, this trail is perfect for hikers of all levels of experience.
  • The Belle Fourche Riverwalk Trail - This is a picturesque trail that runs along the Belle Fourche River, offering scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Eagle Creek Trail - This trail is a haven for bikers and hikers, with numerous trails that meander through deep forests and along crystal-clear streams.

Best Things to Do in Gillette: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other activities to do in Gillette. Here are some of the best things to do in Gillette:

  • Wildlife Watching - With vast grasslands and natural reserves, Gillette is a perfect destination for wildlife watching. Head to Thunder Basin National Grassland to spot the bison, pronghorn, and other majestic animals.
  • Fishing - Gillette is famous for its fishing opportunities. Whether you want to catch rainbow trout, walleye, or bass, there are several fishing spots in the area, such as Keyhole State Park or Lake DeSmet.
  • Watersports - If you are an adventure seeker, then you can try your hand at watersports such as kayaking, rafting, and paddleboarding.

Food and Drink Scene of Gillette

Gillette has a variety of dining options that cater to every taste bud, from fast food to fine-dine restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak and Grill - If you are visiting Wyoming, you must try the famous Wyoming beef steak. Head to Gillette’s local restaurants for an unforgettable meat lover’s paradise.
  • Bison Burger - Another famous dish in Wyoming is the bison burger, which is a must-try for burger lovers.
  • Chili - Chili is another iconic dish in Wyoming, and several restaurants in Gillette offer different variations of this comforting dish.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whiskey - Wyoming produces some of the best whiskies in the world. Try out local whiskey, which is distilled in Kirby.
  • Black Tooth Brewing Company - A popular brewery in Gillette, it offers a range of craft beers. It is a perfect place to relax after a day of exploring.

What to Buy in Gillette: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Gillette

Wyoming is known for its cowboy culture, and Gillette offers a glimpse of it. Here are some customs and etiquette tips that will come in handy while visiting Gillette:

  • Ranching Culture - You will see several ranches while traveling in Gillette. It is essential to respect the property and privacy of the ranchers and their land.
  • Cowboy Attire - It is common to see people wearing cowboy hats and boots in Gillette. You can embrace the local culture by taking part and wearing these items yourself.
  • Friendliness - People in Gillette are friendly and hospitable; it is customary to greet people with a smile and a wave.

Best Time to Visit Gillette

The best time to visit Gillette is from June to August when the weather is sunny and warm, making it perfect for outdoor activities. However, if you want to avoid the crowds, then visiting in spring or early fall is ideal.
