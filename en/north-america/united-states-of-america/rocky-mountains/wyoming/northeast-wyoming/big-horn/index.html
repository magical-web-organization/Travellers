If you’re planning a trip to the Rocky Mountains of the United States of America, be sure to make a stop in Big Horn, Wyoming. This charming town is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich history, offering visitors a perfect blend of adventure and culture.

Introduction to Big Horn

Located in Northeast Wyoming, Big Horn is a small unincorporated community nestled in the foothills of the Bighorn Mountains. It’s just a short drive from the city of Sheridan, which serves as the gateway to the area for visitors arriving by air or by bus.

List of Places to Visit in Big Horn

From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Big Horn. Here’s a list of must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Brinton Museum: This museum features a collection of Western and Native American art, as well as artifacts from the life of Bradford Brinton, a local rancher and art collector.

  • Trail End State Historic Site: This beautifully preserved mansion offers a glimpse into the life of the American West’s wealthiest families.

Natural Attractions of Big Horn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bighorn National Forest: Covering over 1.1 million acres, the Bighorn National Forest is a hiker’s paradise with over 1,500 miles of trails.

  • Medicine Wheel National Historic Landmark: This sacred Native American site is over 700 years old and is believed to have been used in religious ceremonies.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Horn

Big Horn is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails offering stunning views of the area’s natural beauty. Here are some popular routes:

  • Tongue River Canyon Trail: This 4-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the canyon and the river below.

  • Red Grade Trail: This 7-mile trail is perfect for experienced hikers and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Big Horn: Activities and Tours

Whether you prefer a scenic drive or a guided tour, there’s no shortage of things to do in Big Horn. Here are some popular options:

  • Scenic Drive on US Route 14: This drive is known as “The Bighorn Scenic Byway” and offers stunning views of the area’s natural beauty.

  • Guided Horseback Riding Tour: Explore the area’s scenic trails on horseback with a knowledgeable guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Big Horn

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Big Horn, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity

Food and Drink Scene in Big Horn

Big Horn may be a small town, but it has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison burger: Made with locally sourced bison meat, this burger is a tasty way to sample the area’s cuisine.

  • Trout: Freshly caught trout from nearby streams is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: The local brewery offers a variety of craft beers made with locally sourced ingredients.

  • Huckleberry-flavored soda: This sweet soda is made with huckleberries grown in the nearby mountains.

Where to Stay in Big Horn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Big Horn has something to suit every taste and budget. Here are a few options:

  • Sheridan Inn: This historic hotel was built in 1893 and has been beautifully restored, offering modern amenities with a touch of Old West charm.

  • Big Horn Mountain Lodge: This cozy lodge offers stunning views of the Bighorn Mountains and is a great choice for outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Big Horn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or a gift for someone special, here are some local products to consider:

  • Handmade pottery: The area is known for its talented potters, who create beautiful pieces in a variety of styles.

  • Western art: Big Horn is home to many talented artists who create beautiful works inspired by the area’s natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Big Horn

The area around Big Horn is steeped in Western culture and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including the importance of ranching and outdoor recreation in the community.

Best Time to Visit Big Horn

The best time to visit Big Horn is during the summer months, between June and August. This is when the weather is the warmest, and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Horn

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Big Horn:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore posted signs or rules regarding outdoor activities.

  • Litter or damage natural areas.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Big Horn should be aware of the following safety and health information:

Safety in Big Horn

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure.

  • Follow instructions posted at outdoor recreation areas.

Health in Big Horn

  • The area around Big Horn is at a high elevation, so visitors should be aware of the risks of altitude sickness.

  • Be sure to drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun’s rays.

Getting to Big Horn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Big Horn is easily accessible by air or bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Big Horn

  • Sheridan County Airport (SHR): This airport is located just 13 miles from Big Horn and offers daily flights to Denver.

  • Billings Logan International Airport (BIL): This airport is located 90 miles from Big Horn and offers daily flights to several major cities.

Bus Stations Near Big Horn

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus line offers service to Sheridan, which is just a short drive from Big Horn.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore the area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Big Horn

The best way to get around Big Horn is by car. The town is small, and many of the area’s scenic drives are only accessible by car.

Where to Go Next from Big Horn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the area, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Yellowstone National Park: This iconic park is just a few hours’ drive from Big Horn and offers breathtaking natural beauty.

  • Mount Rushmore National Memorial: This memorial is located in South Dakota and is just a few hours’ drive from Big Horn.

Conclusion

Big Horn, Wyoming, is a hidden gem in the heart of the Rocky Mountains. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, it offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an adrenaline-packed adventure, Big Horn is the perfect destination.
