Buffalo, nestled in the Rocky Mountains of Northeast Wyoming, is a charming small town that boasts of picturesque views, fascinating history, and wonderful outdoor activities for all ages. Whether you are looking to soak in the natural beauty of the Big Horn Mountains, immerse in the rich local cowboy culture, or simply escape the city hustle, Buffalo has plenty of options that will make your trip unforgettable. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more to help you plan the ultimate trip to Buffalo.

Introduction to Buffalo

Buffalo is a small town with a population of around 4500 people and covers an area of just over 4 square miles. It is named after the buffalo (or bison) that once roamed the area before being hunted to near extinction. The town is the seat of Johnson County and is located along the I-90 corridor, about halfway between Gillette and Sheridan. Buffalo is the gateway to the Big Horn Mountains, which offers numerous outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, fishing, hunting, and camping.

List of Places to Visit in Buffalo

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Buffalo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jim Gatchell Memorial Museum: This museum boasts of a vast collection of authentic frontier artifacts, Native American art, and Western memorabilia, showcasing the history, culture and lifestyle of the area. The museum also hosts various events and workshops throughout the year.

  • Fort Phil Kearny State Historic Site: This historic site is the location of a major clash between the US Army and the Lakota and Cheyenne tribes in 1866. Visitors can learn about the history of the conflict and the lives of the people involved through the exhibits and guided tours.

  • Occidental Hotel: The Occidental Hotel is a historic hotel that has been around since 1880 and is famous for hosting famous guests such as Teddy Roosevelt, Calamity Jane, and Buffalo Bill. The hotel has been restored to its original glory and is now a popular attraction that offers guided tours and fine dining.

Natural Attractions of Buffalo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bighorn National Forest: The Bighorn National Forest is a vast expanse of wilderness that covers 1.1 million acres and offers pristine natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and endless outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, fishing, hunting, and wildlife viewing.

  • Shell Falls Interpretive Site: Located in the Bighorn National Forest, this is a scenic waterfall that drops over 120 feet and offers a fantastic spot for photography. The surrounding area has picnic tables, restrooms, and hiking trails.

  • Crazy Woman Canyon: Crazy Woman Canyon is a beautiful and rugged canyon that offers fantastic views of the Bighorn Mountains. Visitors can explore the canyon on foot, horseback, or ATV.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buffalo

Buffalo is the perfect place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few of the top routes:

  • Soldier Ridge Trail: This is a popular trail that offers stunning views of the Bighorn Mountains and the surrounding wilderness. It is a 10-mile loop that is perfect for experienced hikers and mountain bikers.

  • City of Buffalo Trail Network: This network of trails is located within the city limits and offers various levels of difficulty, making it ideal for beginners and families. The trails offer scenic views of the town and the surrounding landscape.

  • Rock Creek Trail: This trail is located in the Rock Creek Recreation Area and is a 4-mile out-and-back trail that follows the Rock Creek. The trail offers beautiful views of the canyon and is perfect for hiking and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Buffalo: Activities and Tours

Apart from outdoor activities, Buffalo offers many other fun things to do and attractions to explore. Here are a few:

  • Kaycee Cowboy Museum: Kaycee Cowboy Museum is located just a short drive from Buffalo and offers visitors a chance to learn about the cowboy culture of the area with exhibits of working cowboy gear, horse-drawn vehicles, and more.

  • Skiing at Meadowlark Ski Lodge: About 20 miles southwest of Buffalo is the Meadowlarks Ski Lodge which offers skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing opportunities for beginners to advanced level skiers. The lodge also has a restaurant, bar, and ski rental facilities.

  • Rock Climbing in Ten Sleep Canyon: Ten Sleep Canyon is a scenic canyon that is known for its challenging rock-climbing routes. There are several guide services that can take you up the routes safely.

Week-Long Itinerary for Buffalo

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Buffalo:

Day 1: Explore the Jim Gatchell Memorial Museum and the Occidental Hotel Day 2: Hike the Soldier Ridge Trail Day 3: Visit the Bighorn National Forest and enjoy outdoor activities such as camping, fishing, or hunting Day 4: Take a scenic drive to the Crazy Woman Canyon and explore the surrounding area Day 5: Rock climbing or hiking in Ten Sleep Canyon Day 6: Skiing or snowboarding at Meadowlark Ski Lodge Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food scene of Buffalo

Food and Drink Scene of Buffalo

Buffalo offers plenty of options when it comes to food and drink. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo Burger: A classic dish featuring buffalo meat in a traditional burger. The burgers can be found at various local restaurants throughout Buffalo.

  • Bison Prime Rib: This dish is slow-roasted prime rib made from bison meat that is a leaner and healthier option compared to beef.

  • Huckleberry Jam: Huckleberry is the local wild berry of the region and is used to make delicious jams, pies, and other desserts.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whiskey: Wyoming whiskey is a locally produced bourbon whiskey which has won several awards and has put Wyoming on the map for high-quality spirits.

  • Bighorn Mountain Brews: Bighorn Mountain Brews is a craft brewery located in Sheridan. They offer a variety of beers and often have live music and food trucks on-site.

Where to Stay in Buffalo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buffalo offers various accommodations options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are a few top options:

  • The Historic Mansion House Inn: The Historic Mansion House Inn is a charming, historical bed, and breakfast that offers lavish suites, gourmet breakfast, and excellent service.

  • Rodeway Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and easy access to local attractions.

  • Big Horn Motel: The Big Horn Motel is a family-owned motel that offers clean rooms, friendly service, and affordable rates.

What to Buy in Buffalo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Buffalo has a thriving local market scene that offers various options for souvenirs and local products. Here are a few things to consider bringing back home as a souvenir:

  • Handmade Native American Crafts: The area is home to several Native American tribes, and you can find authentic handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

  • Bison Art and Products: Buffalo is home to the Buffalo Bill Center of the West, which has several shops where you can purchase bison-related products such as bison meat, leather goods, and artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Buffalo

Buffalo is home to a vibrant cowboy culture that is deeply rooted in rodeos, horsemanship, and cattle ranching. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the culture by attending local events such as rodeos, attending local markets, and exploring the Wild West history of the town.

Best Time to Visit Buffalo

The best time to visit Buffalo is during the summer months (June - August), when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Winter is also a great time to visit as you can enjoy skiing and snowboarding in the nearby ski resorts; however, the cold and snow can be a deterrent for some visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buffalo

Do:

  • Do attend the local rodeos and cultural events.
  • Do use sunscreen and carry plenty of water while indulging in outdoor activities.
  • Do bring warm clothing when visiting in winter.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions, especially in winter.
  • Don’t litter or harm the natural habitat while exploring the wilderness.
  • Don’t be impatient or rush while driving on the I-90 corridor, which has varying speed limits and can be dangerous.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buffalo

Buffalo is generally a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should stay vigilant and take necessary precautions to avoid petty crime such as theft, especially in crowded areas and during public events.

Health in Buffalo

Buffalo is a small town with limited healthcare facilities. Visitors should carry enough medication and a doctor’s prescription in case of medical emergencies. In case of a medical emergency, visitors can contact the Johnson County Healthcare Center.

Getting to Buffalo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Buffalo is easy, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Buffalo

  • Sheridan County Airport: 45 miles away
  • Gillette-Campbell County Airport: 87 miles away
  • Billings Logan International Airport: 130 miles away

Bus Stations Near Buffalo

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The nearest bus station is located in Sheridan, about 36 miles away.

Other Options

  • Driving: Buffalo is located along the I-90 corridor and is easily accessible through personal vehicles.
  • Renting a Car: Several rental car agencies are available in Sheridan, Gillette, and Billings.

How to Get Around in Buffalo

Buffalo is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by car. Several rental car agencies and taxi services are available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Buffalo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few destinations that you can visit near Buffalo:

  • Yellowstone National Park: Located about 250 miles away, Yellowstone National Park is one of the most iconic national parks in the country, offering breathtaking geysers, waterfalls, and wildlife.
  • Devil’s Tower National Monument: Located about 120 miles away, it’s an impressive marvel of nature that offers hiking, rock climbing, and scenic views.
  • Cody: Located about 160 miles away, Cody is a bustling town that offers several attractions such as the Buffalo Bill Center of the West and the Old Trail Town.

Conclusion

Buffalo, Wyoming, has a rich culture, history, and natural beauty that make it an ideal destination for travelers looking to experience the Wild West. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Buffalo has something to offer for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip, and you are sure to have a memorable time in this charming small town.
