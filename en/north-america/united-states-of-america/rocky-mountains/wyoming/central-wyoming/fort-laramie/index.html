Introduction to Fort Laramie

Located in central Wyoming’s Rocky Mountains, Fort Laramie is a historic site that played an instrumental role in the settlement of the Western United States. The fort was originally built by fur traders in the early 1830s but later became a military post, serving as a stopping point for pioneers heading west along the Oregon, California, and Mormon Trails.

Today, Fort Laramie is a must-visit destination for history buffs and anyone interested in learning more about the Old West. The fort has been beautifully preserved and includes a collection of restored buildings and artifacts. Visitors can take guided tours or explore the site on their own.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Laramie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Laramie National Historic Site Visitor Center: Start your visit at the Visitor Center, where you can view exhibits that provide an overview of Fort Laramie’s history and pick up a map for self-guided tours of the site.

  • Officers’ Quarters: These elegant homes were built for the commanding officers and their families. Today, visitors can walk through the restored rooms and see how the officers lived in the 1800s.

  • Post Hospital: Located near the entrance to the fort, the Post Hospital is a well-preserved example of 19th-century military medicine.

  • Indian Agency & Indian Wars: Fort Laramie played a significant role in the American Indian Wars, and visitors can learn more about this history at the Indian Agency exhibit.

Natural Attractions of Fort Laramie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chugwater Formation: The Chugwater Formation is a geological feature that’s been shaped by millions of years of wind and water erosion, and it provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Trail Ruts: Visitors can see the ruts made by wagon wheels on the historic Oregon, California and Mormon Trails which ran through the fort.

  • Fort Laramie Walking Tours: Take a leisurely stroll through the fort with a knowledgeable guide who can point out interesting features and answer questions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Laramie

  • Oregon Trail: The Oregon Trail passes through Fort Laramie and offers beautiful hiking and biking routes for visitors of all skill levels.

  • Chugwater Creek Trail: This scenic trail winds through the fort and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Fort Laramie: Activities and Tours

  • Ranger-guided Tours: Rangers offer daily tours of the fort from May through September, providing visitors with a wealth of information about Fort Laramie’s history.

  • Living History: On some weekends throughout the year, visitors can experience what life was like at the fort during the 19th century, with costumed interpreters demonstrating daily routines and practices.

  • Junior Ranger Program: Fort Laramie participates in the National Park Service’s Junior Ranger program, allowing children to learn about the fort’s history and culture while earning a special badge.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Laramie

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo Burgers: Enjoy a delicious burger made from locally-sourced buffalo meat.

  • Dutch Oven Biscuits: Biscuits cooked to perfection in a Dutch oven, a tradition that dates back to the fort’s military days.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whiskey: Sample locally-distilled whiskey made with Rocky Mountain water and Wyoming-grown grains.

  • Craft Beer: The breweries in nearby Laramie offer a variety of locally-brewed beers.

Where to Stay in Fort Laramie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fort Laramie Bed and Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms and a hearty breakfast to start each day.

  • Laramie Plains Inn: Located in nearby Laramie, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving locally-sourced cuisine.

What to Buy in Fort Laramie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fort Laramie T-Shirts: Show off your love for Fort Laramie with a souvenir t-shirt.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans offer handcrafted jewelry made with gemstones found in the Rocky Mountains.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Laramie

Fort Laramie is located in a rural area in the Rocky Mountains, and visitors can expect to encounter friendly residents with a deep appreciation for the land and its history. The area has a rich western heritage, and visitors are encouraged to explore this history by visiting local museums and historic sites.

Best Time to Visit Fort Laramie

The best time to visit Fort Laramie is from May through September when the park is open and the weather is mild. Summers in Wyoming can be hot, so visitors should plan accordingly and bring sunscreen and plenty of water.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Laramie

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the site on your own and learn about the fort’s history.

  • Do wear comfortable shoes for walking and hiking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb any natural or historic features.

  • Don’t remove any artifacts or historical items from the site.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Laramie

Fort Laramie is a safe destination, but visitors should take standard safety precautions such as locking cars and avoiding walking alone after dark.

Health in Fort Laramie

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude (Fort Laramie is over 4,000 feet above sea level) and the need to stay hydrated in the dry climate. Visitors with underlying health conditions should consult with a healthcare provider before traveling to the area.

Getting to Fort Laramie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Laramie

The nearest airport to Fort Laramie is in Cheyenne, Wyoming, approximately 100 miles from the fort. Visitors can rent a car at the airport to travel to Fort Laramie.

Bus Stations Near Fort Laramie

Greyhound Buses serve Laramie, Wyoming, which is approximately 60 miles from Fort Laramie.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive or bike to Fort Laramie via the Oregon, California, or Mormon Trails.

How to Get Around in Fort Laramie

Visitors can easily walk or bike around the fort. Guided tours are also available, and visitors can rent bicycles at the park.

Where to Go Next from Fort Laramie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Laramie, Wyoming: This historic town is home to the University of Wyoming and offers a variety of cultural activities and outdoor adventures.

  • Cheyenne, Wyoming: Wyoming’s capital city boasts a rich western history and offers a variety of museums and historic sites.

Conclusion

Fort Laramie is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the history of the American West. From exploring well-preserved historical sites to hiking through stunning natural landscapes, there’s something for everyone in Fort Laramie. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip and make the most of their visit to this extraordinary site.
