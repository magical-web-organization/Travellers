Introduction to Douglas

Douglas, located in the central part of Wyoming, is a small town with a big history. Douglas is the county seat of Converse County and is surrounded by breathtaking views of the Medicine Bow National Forest and the Laramie Range. With a population of just over 6,000, Douglas offers visitors a chance to experience the beauty and charm of the American West.

List of Places to Visit in Douglas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Douglas Railroad Interpretive Center: This museum showcases the history of railroad transportation in the area and educates visitors about the role that railroads played in the development of the United States.
  • Wyoming Pioneer Museum: The Wyoming Pioneer Museum showcases the history of Converse County, with a particular focus on the pioneer and cowboy eras that shaped the region.
  • Fort Fetterman State Historic Site: This historic site features the remains of a 19th-century military post that played a crucial role in the Indian Wars and the development of the American West.

Natural Attractions of Douglas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ayres Natural Bridge: Located just outside of Douglas, the Ayres Natural Bridge is a unique geological formation that spans over 50 feet and is surrounded by picturesque scenery.
  • Glendo State Park: Located about 20 minutes from Douglas, Glendo State Park is home to a large reservoir that offers excellent fishing, boating, and camping opportunities.
  • Douglas Community Fishing Pond: This pond, located in the heart of Douglas, offers visitors a chance to fish for rainbow trout, bluegill, and catfish in a tranquil setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Douglas

  • Douglas Community Trail System: This network of trails winds through the heart of Douglas, offering visitors a chance to explore the town and its surrounding natural beauty on foot or bike.
  • Shawnee Lake Trail: This scenic trail, located just outside of Douglas, takes hikers on a journey through the beautiful Wyoming countryside, with stunning views of Shawnee Reservoir.
  • Laramie Peak Trail: For experienced hikers, the Laramie Peak Trail offers a challenging climb to the top of Laramie Peak, with stunning views of the Medicine Bow National Forest and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Douglas: Activities and Tours

  • Douglas Motorsports Park: If you’re looking for some high-octane fun, head over to the Douglas Motorsports Park, where you can watch drag races and other motorsports events.
  • Douglas Railroad Days: This annual event celebrates the town’s rich railroad history with live music, food vendors, and plenty of family-friendly activities.
  • Douglas Arts and Crafts Fair: This annual event showcases the work of local artists and craftspeople, with plenty of unique souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Douglas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wyoming Chili: This hearty dish is made with ground beef, beans, and a spicy tomato sauce and is a staple of the Wyoming cuisine.
  • Bison Burgers: Made with locally sourced bison meat, these burgers add a unique twist to an American classic.
  • Chicken Fried Steak: A Wyoming favorite, chicken fried steak is a crispy, breaded steak smothered in gravy and served with mashed potatoes and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whiskey: Distilled in nearby Kirby, Wyoming, Wyoming Whiskey is a small-batch bourbon that has won numerous awards for its distinctive flavor and complexity.
  • Snowy Mountain Brewery: Located in nearby Saratoga, Snowy Mountain Brewery is a popular spot for craft beer enthusiasts and features a wide variety of locally brewed beers and ales.
  • Cowboy Coffee: Made with brown sugar, cinnamon, and vanilla, Cowboy Coffee is a sweet and spicy treat that is a favorite of locals and visitors alike.

Local Culture and Customs of Douglas

Wyoming has a rich history of ranching and cowboy culture, and Douglas is no exception. Visitors should be prepared to encounter a friendly, laid-back community that takes pride in its Western heritage. It is also advisable to dress in layers, as the weather in Douglas can be unpredictable, with temperatures ranging from hot and sunny to cold and snowy.

Best Time to Visit Douglas

The best time to visit Douglas is in the summer months, from June through August, when the weather is warm and sunny and outdoor activities are plentiful. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers and thunderstorms. Winter can be very cold in Douglas, with temperatures often dropping below freezing, making outdoor activities less enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Douglas

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the town’s local shops and restaurants and interact with the friendly locals.
  • Do dress appropriately for the weather and bring plenty of water and sunscreen if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Do be respectful of the area’s rich Western heritage and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind when visiting natural areas, such as parks or trails.
  • Don’t trespass on private property or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions, particularly in the winter, and always be prepared for changing conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Douglas

Overall, Douglas is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards when spending time outdoors, including sudden weather changes, uneven terrain, and wildlife encounters.

Health in Douglas

There are several medical facilities in Douglas, including the Memorial Hospital of Converse County, which offers a range of services, including emergency care and primary care.

Getting to Douglas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Douglas

The closest airport to Douglas is the Casper/Natrona County International Airport, located approximately 50 miles away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle or taxi to Douglas.

Bus Stations Near Douglas

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in nearby Casper, where visitors can take a bus to Douglas.

Other Options

Driving is the most practical way to get to Douglas, and visitors can rent a car at the Casper/Natrona County International Airport or in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Douglas

The best way to get around in Douglas is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in nearby towns. Biking and walking are also popular options, particularly in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Douglas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Casper: Located just 50 miles north of Douglas, Casper offers a range of attractions and activities, including museums, art galleries, and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Cheyenne: Wyoming’s capital city is located just over 100 miles southeast of Douglas and offers visitors a chance to explore the state’s rich history and culture.
  • Laramie Range: With its stunning views and challenging hiking trails, the Laramie Range is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Conclusion

Douglas, Wyoming, is a hidden gem of the American West, with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re into outdoor recreation, cultural attractions, or simply taking in the small-town charm, Douglas has something for everyone. With friendly locals, delicious food, and a relaxed, laid-back atmosphere, a trip to Douglas is sure to be a memorable one.
