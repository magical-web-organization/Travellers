Casper is a hidden gem in Central Wyoming, surrounded by the Rocky Mountains and gorgeous scenery. If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path vacation spot that offers outdoor adventure, culture, and history, look no further than Casper. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Casper.

Introduction to Casper

Casper is a vibrant city in Central Wyoming, known for its stunning natural beauty and rich history. The city is located on the North Platte River, nestled between the Laramie and Casper mountain ranges. Casper has a population of around 60,000 people and has been named one of the best small cities to live in the United States. Visitors to Casper can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and skiing, as well as museums and historical sites that showcase the city’s colorful past.

List of Places to Visit in Casper

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casper boasts a rich history, and visitors can learn about the area’s past by visiting the city’s numerous museums and historical sites. Here are some must-see attractions:

  • National Historic Trails Interpretive Center: This museum highlights the history of the Oregon, California, Mormon Pioneer, and Pony Express Trails, which passed through Casper in the 1800s. Visitors can explore interactive exhibits, watch films about the trails, and learn about the pioneers who traversed them.

  • Fort Caspar Museum and Historic Site: This museum showcases the history of the Fort Caspar area, which was a significant trading post and military outpost in the 1800s. Visitors can see the reconstructed fort, view artifacts from the era, and learn about the lifestyle of the early settlers.

  • Nicolaysen Art Museum: This contemporary art museum features a rotating collection of artwork by local, regional, and international artists. The museum is housed in a stunning historic building that was once a power plant.

Natural Attractions of Casper: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Casper is known for its stunning natural beauty, and there are numerous outdoor activities visitors can enjoy during their stay. Here are some popular natural attractions:

  • Casper Mountain: This mountain offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, mountain biking, and skiing. Visitors can take in the stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains while enjoying the fresh mountain air.

  • Alcova Reservoir: This reservoir is located just outside of Casper and is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and water sports. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, picnicking, and wildlife viewing in the surrounding area.

  • Garden Creek Falls: This waterfall is located in the Bridger-Teton National Forest and can be accessed by a short hiking trail. The waterfall is especially scenic during the spring and summer months when wildflowers are in bloom.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Casper

Casper offers a range of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the stunning natural landscape. Here are some popular routes:

  • Bridle Trail: This trail is located on Casper Mountain and is a popular spot for hiking, horseback riding, and mountain biking. The trail offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains.

  • Platte River Parkway: This paved trail runs along the North Platte River and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding mountains. The trail is popular with walkers, runners, and bikers.

  • Rotary Park Trail System: This trail system is located near the heart of Casper and offers a range of hiking and mountain biking trails. The trails are well-maintained and offer stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Casper: Activities and Tours

Casper offers a range of activities and tours that allow visitors to get the most out of their stay. Here are some must-try experiences:

  • Fishing: Casper is known for its world-class fly fishing, and guided fishing trips are available for both beginners and experienced anglers.

  • Wildlife Viewing: Visitors can spot a range of wildlife in and around Casper, including bison, elk, and pronghorn antelope.

  • Ghost Tours: Casper has a colorful history, and visitors can learn about the city’s spooky past by taking a ghost tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Casper

Day 1: Visit the National Historic Trails Interpretive Center and explore the city’s historic downtown.

Day 2: Take a guided fishing trip on the North Platte River, followed by a scenic drive up Casper Mountain.

Day 3: Hike the Bridle Trail on Casper Mountain, followed by a visit to the Fort Caspar Museum and Historic Site.

Day 4: Visit the Nicolaysen Art Museum and take a ghost tour of the city’s historic sites.

Day 5: Enjoy a day on the water at Alcova Reservoir or go wildlife viewing in the surrounding area.

Day 6: Explore the Rotary Park Trail system on Casper Mountain and take in the stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing and enjoying the city’s food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Casper

Casper has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a range of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: This local favorite is made with bison meat and served with a variety of toppings.

  • Trout: Casper is known for its world-class fly fishing, and trout is a popular dish in many local restaurants.

  • Chili: Wyoming is known for its chili, and Casper has numerous restaurants that serve up this hearty dish.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whiskey: This local distillery produces award-winning whiskey that is made from locally-sourced ingredients.

  • Wind River Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers that are made using water from the nearby Wind River Mountains.

Where to Stay in Casper: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Casper offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Casper: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a pool.

  • Parkway Plaza Hotel and Convention Centre: This hotel offers spacious rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a range of amenities.

  • The Gaslight Social: This hostel is located in the heart of downtown Casper and offers affordable accommodations in a lively atmosphere.

What to Buy in Casper: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Casper offers a range of local products that make for unique souvenirs. Here are some products worth bringing home:

  • Handmade Jewelry: Local artisans create beautiful jewelry using natural materials like turquoise and jade.

  • Bison Meat: Bison meat is a staple in Wyoming cuisine, and many local stores sell bison jerky and sausage.

  • Cowboy Hats: Casper is located in cowboy country, and visitors can find a range of cowboy hats at local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Casper

Wyoming residents are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the western culture of the region. Cowboy hats and boots are a common sight, and visitors can experience western hospitality at local events like rodeos and fairs.

Best Time to Visit Casper

The best time to visit Casper is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall and spring are also great times to visit, as the weather is still pleasant and the scenery is stunning.

Safety and Health

Safety in Casper

Casper is a safe city, but visitors should take common-sense precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. It’s important to lock your car and avoid leaving valuables visible. Visitors should also exercise caution when hiking or biking on the trails and follow proper trail etiquette.

Health in Casper

Casper has excellent medical facilities, including the Wyoming Medical Center and several urgent care clinics. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them and make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage for their trip.

Getting to Casper: Transportation Options and Logistics

Casper is located in Central Wyoming, and visitors have several transportation options when getting to the city.

Airports Near Casper

The Casper/Natrona County International Airport is located just outside of the city and offers daily flights to Denver and Salt Lake City.

Bus Stations Near Casper

Several bus companies offer service to Casper, including Greyhound and Jefferson Lines.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Casper using highway I-25, which runs through the city.

How to Get Around in Casper

Casper is a small city, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bike. Taxis and rideshare services are also available, as well as rental cars for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Casper: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Casper is located in an area of Wyoming that offers a range of attractions and destinations. Some nearby locations worth visiting include:

  • Yellowstone National Park: This iconic national park is located just a few hours from Casper and offers stunning wildlife, geysers, and hot springs.

  • Grand Teton National Park: This park is known for its towering peaks and stunning mountain views.

  • Devils Tower National Monument: This striking rock formation is located about two hours from Casper and is a popular spot for hiking and rock climbing.

Conclusion

Casper is a hidden gem in Central Wyoming that offers a range of outdoor adventure, culture, and history. Visitors can enjoy world-class fishing, hiking, and skiing, as well as museums and historical sites that showcase the city’s colorful past. In addition, the city’s vibrant food and drink scene, friendly locals, and stunning natural surroundings make it an ideal destination for anyone looking for an off-the-beaten-path vacation spot.
