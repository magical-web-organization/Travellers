Introduction to Jackson

Located in Northwest Wyoming, Jackson is a historic town nestled in the Rocky Mountains of the United States of America. The town serves as the gateway to the famous Grand Teton National Park and Yellowstone National Park. The scenic beauty of Jackson and its proximity to stunning national parks and amazing outdoor activities make it a popular destination for adventure-seekers and nature lovers.

In this comprehensive tour guide to Jackson, you will discover the top attractions, hiking/biking routes, things to do, safety tips, getting around options, and more. So, get ready to explore the best of Jackson, Wyoming.

Local Culture and Customs of Jackson

The local culture of Jackson is deeply influenced by Native American traditions and cowboy culture. Visitors can experience the cowboy culture and rich heritage of the region at Jackson Hole Rodeo.

Best Time to Visit Jackson

The best time to visit Jackson is from May to September when the weather is mild, and the popular outdoor attractions are open. Summer months are best for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Winter months offer great skiing and snowboarding opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jackson

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Jackson:

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.
  • Do take necessary precautions while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Do try local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the natural parks and public spaces.
  • Don’t feed the wildlife in the region.
  • Don’t try to pet or get too close to the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Jackson should keep in mind some safety and health-related precautions to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Jackson

  • Follow the instructions and safety guidelines while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Keep an eye out for weather alerts as the weather can change quickly in the region.
  • Carry bear spray and mosquito repellent.

Health in Jackson

  • Stay hydrated and avoid dehydration.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear sunglasses and sunhats to avoid sunburn.
  • Be prepared for altitude sickness if you are not used to high altitudes.
