Introduction to Basin

Basin is a picturesque town located in Park County, Northwest Wyoming, United States of America. It is situated in the Rocky Mountains, which offer spectacular views and a range of fun outdoor activities. Basin is known for its unique combination of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and authentic local experiences, making it a popular destination for travelers from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Basin

Here are some of the top places to visit in Basin that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Homesteader Museum: Learn about the history of Basin through its artifacts, photographs, and exhibits.
  • Greybull Museum: Discover the history of the West through exhibits, demonstrations, and collections of local artifacts.
  • Big Horn County Historical Society Museum: Located in nearby Hardin, Montana, this museum features the history and culture of the region, including Native American artifacts and cowboy gear.

Natural Attractions of Basin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yellowstone National Park: One of the most famous natural attractions in the world, Yellowstone National Park is just a short drive from Basin and offers a plethora of outdoor activities and natural wonders.
  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area: This park boasts magnificent views of the Bighorn Mountains and is a haven for water activities like fishing, boating, kayaking, and swimming.
  • Medicine Wheel National Historic Landmark: A circle of stones measuring 80 feet in diameter and believed to be over 700 years old, the Medicine Wheel is an important spiritual site for Native Americans and is one of the most unique natural attractions in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Basin

Basin has a wealth of hiking and biking trails, which offer stunning views of the surrounding mountain range and numerous opportunities for wildlife sightings. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Basin:

  • Porcupine Falls Trail: A moderate 2.6-mile trail that leads to a beautiful waterfall through the lush forested area.

  • Sunlight Creek Trail: A 7-mile moderate trail that provides scenic views of the mountains and is great for hikers of all levels.

  • Basin Creek Lakes Trail: This 9-mile long round trip is a challenging hike that offers spectacular mountain views if you follow it to the two alpine lakes at the end.

Best Things to Do in Basin: Activities and Tours

When you visit Basin, Wyoming, be sure to explore the following activities and tours:

  • Horseback Riding Tours: Enjoy a unique perspective of the surrounding area by joining a guided horseback riding tour.
  • Fishing Tours: Experience some of the best fly fishing in the Western United States by joining a guided fishing tour.
  • Ghost Town Tours: Learn about the history of the region by exploring nearby ghost towns with a local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Basin

Day 1: Visit Homesteader Museum and do some shopping in downtown Basin. Day 2: Explore Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area by taking a guided kayaking or boating tour. Day 3: Go on a horseback riding tour in the forested areas. Day 4: Take a scenic drive to Yellowstone National Park and enjoy the natural wonders. Day 5: Visit the Greybull Museum and enjoy dinner in a local restaurant. Day 6: Hike the Basin Creek Lakes Trail and enjoy the breathtaking views. Day 7: Take a relaxing day visit to the nearby hot springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Basin

Must-Try Dishes

Basin is known for its hearty, Western-style cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Basin:

  • Chicken fried steak
  • Bison burgers
  • Trout
  • Homemade pies

Local Beverages

Wyoming is known for its unique craft beers, and Basin has some great options, including:

  • Gallows Red Ale
  • Haymaker Hefeweizen
  • Big Horn Brown Ale

Where to Stay in Basin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Historic Hotel Greybull: This centrally located hotel has been in operation since 1916 and offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • The Paintbrush Inn: This guesthouse is located in a quiet residential neighborhood and has a beautiful garden and terrace.
  • The Burlington Place: This hotel is located in an authentic Western town and offers spacious rooms and great views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Basin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cowboy hats and boots
  • Authentic Native American jewelry and artwork
  • Wyoming-made products such as honey, jams, and jerky.

Local Culture and Customs of Basin

Basin boasts a rich cultural heritage that includes the Native American tribes, cowboys, and homesteaders. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture and customs by attending community events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Basin

The best time to visit Basin is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild, and most of the outdoor activities are available. However, it is also a great time to visit during the winter months (December-February) for snowshoeing, skiing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Basin

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment by staying on designated trails, camping in designated areas, and packing out your trash.
  • Try the local cuisine and visit local businesses to support the community.
  • Take time to learn about the region’s cultural heritage and history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t engage in any illegal activities such as hunting/fishing without a license or drugs.
  • Don’t forget to tip servers and service workers in the hotels and restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Basin

Basin is generally considered quite safe, but visitors should take caution as they would travel in any destination. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid walking alone at night, and ensure that your valuables are secured.

Health in Basin

There are not many health hazards in Basin, but visitors should be prepared for the high altitude and take the necessary precautions, such as staying hydrated, getting enough rest, and making sure to have medication on hand if any altitude sickness symptoms arise.

Getting to Basin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Basin

  • Yellowstone Regional Airport (COD) is located around 30 miles of Basin.

Bus Stations Near Basin

  • Greyhound offers service halfway between Basin and Cody.

Other Options

  • The city is connected to Cody by public transportation.

How to Get Around in Basin

The most convenient way to get around Basin is by car, as public transportation is limited in the area. However, visitors can also use bikes and walking to get around the downtown district.

Where to Go Next from Basin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Basin is a great base from which to explore some of the other nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Cody, Wyoming: A famous frontier town with the Buffalo Bill Center of the West and the town’s famous nightly rodeo.
  • Thermopolis, Wyoming: Home to some of the world’s largest hot springs, Thermopolis, also known for its world-class dinosaur attractions.

Conclusion

Basin is a hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains of Wyoming, with its unique combination of culture, outdoor activities, food and drink, and breathtaking scenery. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking for an authentic Western experience, Basin has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and prepare to fall in love with this charming town.
