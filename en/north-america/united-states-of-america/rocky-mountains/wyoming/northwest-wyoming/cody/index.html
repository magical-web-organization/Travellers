Introduction to Cody

Cody, Wyoming, is a beautiful town nestled in the Rocky Mountains, known for its rich history, stunning scenery, and outdoor activities. Founded in 1896 by a successful frontiersman and bison hunter, William F. “Buffalo Bill” Cody, the town has developed into a top destination for travelers looking to experience authentic Western culture and adventure.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best sights to see, activities to try, and cultural experiences to have in Cody, from the famous Buffalo Bill Center of the West to the winding trails of the Shoshone National Forest.

List of Places to Visit in Cody

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Buffalo Bill Center of the West

The Buffalo Bill Center of the West is a must-visit destination for anyone coming to Cody. The museum complex includes five museums, including the Buffalo Bill Museum, Whitney Western Art Museum, Plains Indian Museum, Draper Natural History Museum, and Cody Firearms Museum. Visitors can explore the exhibits featuring wild west memorabilia, Native American art, and natural history displays.

Old Trail Town

Old Trail Town is a living museum that replicates an old Western town. It’s a unique way to experience the Old West and learn about the local history. The town features original buildings dating back to the late 1800s, including a saloon, blacksmith shop, and a museum of frontier memorabilia.

Heart Mountain Interpretive Center

The Heart Mountain Interpretive Center is a memorial to Americans of Japanese descent who were imprisoned during World War II. Visitors can learn about the life, struggles, and triumphs of Japanese immigrants and their descendants while exploring the history of the internment camp.

Natural Attractions of Cody: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Buffalo Bill Dam and Reservoir

The Buffalo Bill Dam is a massive engineering marvel that was built in the early 1900s to provide irrigation water for local farmers. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the dam’s inner workings and learn about its fascinating history. The reservoir is a scenic spot to enjoy boating, fishing, and picnicking.

Shoshone National Forest

The Shoshone National Forest is a vast area of untouched wild land, offering breathtaking scenery, and a wide range of outdoor activities. Visitors can explore the hundreds of miles of hiking trails, go horseback riding, fishing, or camping in the pristine wilderness.

Yellowstone National Park

Yellowstone National Park is a must-see destination that’s only an hour’s drive from Cody. Visitors can witness the incredible hot springs, geysers, and wildlife that make Yellowstone famous.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cody

The Buffalo Bill State Park Trail System

The Buffalo Bill State Park Trail System is an extensive network of trails that offer impressive views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the trails by foot, bike, or horse, enjoying the natural splendor that Wyoming has to offer.

The North Fork Trail

The North Fork Trail is a six-mile-long trail that runs along the North Fork of the Shoshone River. The trail offers impressive views of towering cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Cody: Activities and Tours

Cody Nite Rodeo

The Cody Nite Rodeo is a classic Western event that takes place each evening during the summer. Visitors can witness cowboys and cowgirls compete in a variety of events, from bull riding to barrel racing.

Big Horn Basin Geo Science Center

The Big Horn Basin Geo Science Center is a unique educational experience that explores the geological wonders of the area. Visitors can learn about the formation of the Rocky Mountains, the history of the area’s geology, and take home a souvenir of their own rock samples.

Food and Drink Scene of Cody

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors to Cody must try the local cowboy cuisine, such as beef stew, bison burgers, and Rocky Mountain oysters. Don’t miss the town’s famous chili and the classic Western breakfast at the Irma Hotel.

Local Beverages

Wyoming is famous for craft beers, distilled spirits, and locally roasted coffee. Local favorites include beers from the Wind River Brewing Company, whiskey from the Wyoming Whiskey Distillery, and coffee from Cody Coffee Roasters.

Where to Stay in Cody: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation, from upscale hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. The best options for hotels include the Irma Hotel, Hotel Cody, and the Cody Cowboy Village. For those who are looking for budget options, check out the Cody Hostel or the Cody Cowgirl Yurt.

What to Buy in Cody: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home some authentic Western souvenirs such as cowboy hats, boots, and leather goods at many of the shops around town. It’s also possible to purchase authentic Native American handicrafts, such as pottery, baskets, and jewelry at the Plains Indian Museum Gift Shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Cody

Cody is a Western town where cowboy boots and hats are fashionable. It’s also important to be polite and respectful, especially when visiting local ranches or during rodeo events. Tipping is customary, and guests are encouraged to tip hotel staff, restaurant servers, and tour guides.

Best Time to Visit Cody

The best time to visit Cody is in the summer, from June to August, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities available. Visitors can also attend the famous Cody Nite Rodeo and other outdoor events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cody

Do:

  • Try the local cowboy cuisine
  • Visit the Buffalo Bill Center of the West
  • Explore the natural wonders of the Shoshone National Forest

Don’t:

  • Wear heels instead of boots
  • Behave disrespectfully to locals
  • Leave trash while exploring the great outdoors

Safety and Health

Safety in Cody

Cody is a safe town, but visitors should be careful while exploring the wilderness of the Shoshone National Forest. Bear attacks occur rarely, but it’s important to stay aware of the surroundings and carry bear spray while hiking or camping.

Health in Cody

It’s recommended to drink enough water while exploring Wyoming’s dry climate. Visitors should also be prepared with sunscreen and a hat as the altitude causes the sun to feel more intense.

Getting to Cody: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cody

Yellowstone Regional Airport (COD) is the nearest airport, with daily flights from Denver and other regional airports.

Bus Stations Near Cody

Greyhound buses stop in Cody, and visitors can catch a bus from cities like Billings, MT, and Cheyenne, WY.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car from Billings Logan International Airport, which is around two hours’ drive from Cody.

How to Get Around in Cody

Cody is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use public transportation, taxis, or rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Cody: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby towns of Powell and Meeteetse, well-known for their museums, art galleries, and local history. Another must-see destination is the Bighorn National Forest, which is a few hours’ drive from Cody.

Conclusion

Cody, Wyoming, is a unique destination that offers visitors the chance to experience authentic Western culture and explore the stunning scenery of the Rocky Mountains. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Cody with ease, exploring everything this great town has to offer.
