Introduction to Powell

Powell is situated in the northwest part Wyoming, near the eastern edge of Yellowstone National Park. It is a small town that is rich in culture and history, surrounded by the natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Powell has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Powell

Here are some of the top places to visit during your time in Powell:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Homesteader Museum - Dedicated to preserving the history of homesteading in the Powell area, this museum has exhibits showcasing artifacts from early pioneers and settlers.
  • Heart Mountain Interpretive Center - This center provides insight into the history of Japanese internment during World War II, with exhibits and a walking tour of the former internment camp.
  • Powell Visitor Center - This center provides information on Powell’s history and historical sites, as well as the area’s natural attractions.

Natural Attractions of Powell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shoshone National Forest - This forest is filled with stunning hiking trails, scenic drives, and wildlife, making it a great spot for nature-lovers.
  • Yellowstone National Park - Located just an hour’s drive away from Powell, this park offers spectacular views of geysers, hot springs, canyons, and waterfalls.
  • Bighorn Canyon National Recreation Area - This recreation area features a beautiful canyon carved out by the Bighorn River over millions of years, providing ample opportunities for hiking, fishing, boating, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Powell

Powell and its surrounding areas boast several magnificent hiking and biking trails. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Heart Mountain Trail - This trail provides a challenging hike with an elevation gain of over 1,400 feet, but the stunning views of the surrounding area make it worth the effort.
  • Whitetail Loop Trail - This moderate hiking trail is perfect for families or those looking for a more relaxed hike.
  • Greenbelt Trail - This multi-use trail runs through Powell and provides stunning views of the surrounding mountains while also offering an easy walk.

Best Things to Do in Powell: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Powell, including the following:

  • Cowboy Carousel Center - A unique attraction that showcases a restored, hand-carved carousel from around 1904.
  • Powell Golf Club - A well-manicured course with 18 holes, providing a serene and enjoyable way to soak up the natural beauty of the surrounding area.
  • Horseback Riding - There are several outfitters in the area that offer guided horseback riding tours through the mountains or other scenic parts of Wyoming.

Food and Drink Scene of Powell

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison burgers - A staple in Wyoming cuisine, these burgers are made from locally sourced bison meat, providing a lean and flavorful taste.
  • Huckleberry pancakes - A specialty dish in the area, these pancakes are made with wild huckleberries and are delicious for breakfast or brunch.
  • Chile relleno - A regional favorite, this dish features a mild chili pepper filled with cheese and served with sauce and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whiskey - This whiskey is produced in the nearby town of Kirby and has won several awards for its unique taste and quality.
  • Saddle Bronc Brewery - This microbrewery in Cody, Wyoming has several locally sourced beers on tap that are sure to satisfy any beer lover.
  • Wyoming Roasting Company - This coffee roasting company produces a variety of blends and single-origin coffee beans sourced from around the world, ensuring a fresh and delicious cup of coffee.

Where to Stay in Powell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several lodging options for visitors to Powell, including:

  • The Pollard Hotel - Located in Red Lodge, Montana, this hotel offers luxurious accommodations and a historic ambiance.
  • Big Bear Motel - Affordable and conveniently located in Powell, this motel provides a comfortable and welcoming stay.
  • Wyoming High Country Lodge - Offering panoramic mountain views, this lodge is perfect for those looking for a secluded and rustic experience.

What to Buy in Powell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam - Made from wild huckleberries, this jam is a unique and tasty souvenir to bring home.
  • Handmade Jewelry - Powell has several local artists that craft unique and beautiful jewelry, providing a memorable and personalized piece to bring home.
  • Cowboy Boots - A staple in Wyoming style, cowboy boots are handmade and provide a substantial and durable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Powell

Wyoming is known for its “Wyoming cowboy” culture, focusing on agriculture and ranching. Respect for nature, self-reliance, and a strong work ethic are deeply valued in the area. Visitors are encouraged to take part in and appreciate this local culture during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Powell

The best time to visit Powell is during the summer months, from June to August. This provides the best opportunity for hiking, sightseeing, and outdoor activities, with temperatures ranging from the 70s to the 90s. During the winter months, Powell can experience harsh weather conditions, with temperatures dropping below freezing and heavy snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Powell

Do:

  • Respect nature and outdoor recreational areas by leaving no trace and taking care to preserve the environment.
  • Take part in local culture and traditions, including rodeos, fairs, and cowboy poetry events.
  • Be prepared for unexpected weather changes and sudden changes in weather conditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or culture.
  • Litter or destroy natural areas.
  • Underestimate the power and unpredictability of nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Powell

Powell is generally a safe area to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions such as locking vehicles and securing valuables.

Health in Powell

Powell has several healthcare facilities, including Powell Valley Healthcare, offering routine and emergency medical services. Additionally, visitors should take care to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen while enjoying outdoor activities.

Getting to Powell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Powell

The closest airport to Powell is Yellowstone Regional Airport, located in Cody, Wyoming, approximately 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Powell

There are several bus stations within driving distance of Powell, including the Greyhound station in Cody.

Other Options

Renting a car provides the most flexibility and convenience for getting around Powell and its surrounding areas, especially for those interested in exploring Yellowstone National Park or other destinations further away.

How to Get Around in Powell

Driving is the most convenient and common option for getting around in Powell, but visitors can also rent bikes or utilize public transportation within the town.

Where to Go Next from Powell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cody, Wyoming - A charming western town that is famous for its rodeos and museums, offering plenty of activities and attractions to explore.
  • Yellowstone National Park - Just an hour’s drive away from Powell, this park offers spectacular natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Jackson Hole, Wyoming - Located on the opposite side of Yellowstone, this famous mountain town offers skiing, shopping, and sightseeing year-round.

Conclusion

Powell is a small town with a big heart and a lot of opportunities for visitors to explore Wyoming’s rich history and natural beauty. From hiking and biking trails to rodeos and whiskey, Powell has something for everyone, providing a unique and unforgettable experience.
