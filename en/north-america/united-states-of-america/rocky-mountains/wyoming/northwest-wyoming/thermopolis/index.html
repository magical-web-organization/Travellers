Introduction to Thermopolis

Nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, Thermopolis is a charming town in Wyoming that boasts of a rich history and stunning natural beauty. This unique destination has something for everyone, whether you’re an adrenaline junkie seeking outdoor adventures or a history buff interested in the area’s cultural significance.

Thermopolis is famous for its hot springs, which are believed to have medicinal properties. Travellers have been coming here for centuries to soak in these rejuvenating waters, making it a popular wellness destination. But beyond the hot springs, there’s a lot to discover in this small town that’s big on character.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Thermopolis, showcasing its must-visit places, top things to do, local culture and customs, safety tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Thermopolis

Thermopolis boasts of a variety of attractions, both natural and man-made, that are sure to pique the interest of travellers. Here’s a list of the top places to visit when in Thermopolis:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wyoming Dinosaur Center: A world-renowned museum that houses over 30 dinosaur skeletons and an excavation site where visitors can watch paleontologists at work.
  • Hot Springs State Park: Home to the State Bath House, which has been operating since 1896, and the Wyoming State Park Headquarters, which features interactive exhibits on geology, history, and wildlife.
  • The Hole-in-the-Wall: A hideout for famous outlaws like Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid, which now offers guided tours of the area’s history and geology.
  • Wind River Canyon: A scenic drive that takes visitors through breathtaking views of the canyon’s walls and the Wind River.

Natural Attractions of Thermopolis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hot Springs State Park: In addition to the State Bath House and the Wyoming State Park Headquarters, this park offers many outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing and camping. The park also has herds of buffalo which visitors can view in their natural habitat.
  • Thermopolis Wetlands: A natural area that boasts of rare bird species found only in Wyoming and over 300 species of plants.
  • Tepee Fountain: A man-made mineral spring that shoots up to 50 feet in the air, creating a stunning display.
  • Boysen Reservoir: A nearby lake with crystal clear water that offers fishing, swimming, and boating opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thermopolis

Thermopolis has abundance of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best options:

  • Castle Gardens Trail: A 1.5-mile loop trail that takes visitors through ancient rock formations and petroglyphs.
  • Hot Springs State Park Trails: These trails offers a variety of hikes that range from easy to moderate level of difficulty and provide stunning views of the park’s natural wonders.
  • Legend Rock Petroglyph Site: A trail that offers visitors a look at over 300 rock carvings that date back over 1000 years.

Best Things to Do in Thermopolis: Activities and Tours

In addition to the top places to visit and top hiking/biking routes, there are plenty of other activities, tours, and experiences to enjoy in Thermopolis:

  • Soak in the hot springs and experience their healing power at one of the local bathhouses.
  • Take a guided horseback riding or fly fishing tour into the wilds of Wyoming
  • Go on a guided dinosaur excavation at the Wyoming Dinosaur Center.
  • Attend the annual Hot Springs County Fair, a traditional county fair that offers rodeos, live music, and carnival games.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thermopolis

To experience everything that Thermopolis has to offer, a week-long trip is ideal. Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Hot Springs State Park, bathe in the mineral hot springs.
  • Day 2: Visit the Wyoming Dinosaur Center
  • Day 3: Go on a scenic drive through Wind River Canyon
  • Day 4: Horseback riding or fly fishing tour
  • Day 5: Take a guided dinosaur excavation at the Wyoming Dinosaur Center.
  • Day 6: Attend the annual Hot Springs County Fair.
  • Day 7: Relax at local spa or hot spring bath.

Food and Drink Scene of Thermopolis

Thermopolis has a promising food and drink scene that’s worth exploring. Here are some top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burgers: A classic Wyoming dish, these burgers are made with local bison meat.
  • Trout: The local fish are a must-try, cooked fresh with herbs and spices.
  • Cowboy Beans: A staple dish made with beans, pork, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sarsaparilla: A unique carbonated drink that has been popular since the Wild West times
  • Wind River Wine: A local winery that offers handcrafted wines with Wyoming grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Thermopolis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thermopolis offers several lodging options that cater to all budgets. Here’s a list of top accommodations:

  • Best Western Plus Plaza Hotel: Offers comfortable and affordable stay along with amenities like fitness center, indoor pool.
  • Sleep Inn & Suites: Located near Hot Springs State Park, offers free breakfast and hot springs passes to guests.
  • Pioneer Motel: Traditional roadside motel offering affordable rooms
  • Spa at Riverside: Luxury resort with private mineral hot spring pools

What to Buy in Thermopolis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thermopolis offers many unique souvenirs to bring home. Here are some recommendations:

  • Replicas of dinosaur bones from the Wyoming Dinosaur Center.
  • Wyoming Sarsaparilla, a local carbonated drink.
  • Local art and jewelry made by Native American artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Thermopolis

Thermopolis holds strong to its cowboy heritage with rodeos and fairs being a regular feature. The residents are friendly and welcoming and are proud of their history and the natural beauty of their town.

Best Time to Visit Thermopolis

The best time to visit Thermopolis is in the summer when the weather is enjoyable and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall are also good times to visit as the weather is pleasant and the crowds are smaller. Winters can be cold and snowy but the hot springs are a welcome experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thermopolis

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and natural surroundings.
  • Bring your camera - the scenery is absolutely beautiful.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Trespass onto private property.
  • Ignore safety signs or warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thermopolis

Thermopolis is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take basic precautions while hiking or exploring the outdoors, such as carrying a map and plenty of water, staying on trails, and being mindful of wildlife.

Health in Thermopolis

Visitors to Thermopolis should be aware of the high altitude, which can lead to altitude sickness in some individuals. Drinking plenty of water and resting frequently can help alleviate symptoms. Visitors to the hot springs should be aware of the high temperature of the water and avoid soaking for too long to prevent overheating.

Getting to Thermopolis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thermopolis is easily accessible by car, with several roads leading into the town. Here are some other transportation options:

Airports Near Thermopolis

The closest major airport to Thermopolis is Yellowstone Regional Airport (COD) in Cody, Wyoming, about one hour away by car.

Bus Stations Near Thermopolis

There are no bus stations in Thermopolis. The closest Greyhound station is located in Riverton, about one hour away by car.

Other Options

Car rentals are available from the Yellowstone Regional Airport or from several locations in nearby cities like Cody or Buffalo.

How to Get Around in Thermopolis

The best way to get around in Thermopolis is by car. Many attractions are spread out, and public transportation options are limited. Taxis are available but may be costly.

Where to Go Next from Thermopolis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting after exploring Thermopolis:

  • Yellowstone National Park: About two hours away from Thermopolis by car.
  • Hot Springs County Museum: Located in nearby Marquette, about 20 miles from Thermopolis.
  • Paradise Guest Ranch: A working cattle ranch and guest resort nestled in a valley about 30 miles from Thermopolis.

Conclusion

Thermopolis is a hidden gem that’s just waiting to be discovered. With its breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural attractions, and charming small-town atmosphere, it’s a destination that’s sure to leave an impression. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Thermopolis and get the most out of your visit. Safe travels!
