Introduction to Worland

Located in the northwestern part of Wyoming in the United States, Worland is a charming town known for its beautiful natural attractions, historic landmarks, and rich cultural heritage. The town is set against the backdrop of the Big Horn Mountains with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Worland has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Worland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Washakie Museum and Cultural Center - This museum showcases the history and culture of the Big Horn Basin and features exhibits on the region’s Native American heritage, early pioneers, and the area’s geology.
  • Hot Springs County Museum and Cultural Center - This museum offers a glimpse into the history of the area through displays of artifacts, photographs, and documents, including the region’s mining, ranching, and agricultural endeavors.
  • Worland Carnegie Public Library - Located in the heart of Worland, the library offers a wide range of books, audio books, and multimedia resources.

Natural Attractions of Worland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • North Big Horn Mountains - The area is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts with miles of hiking and biking trails, crystal-clear streams for fishing and boating, and stunning views of the Rocky Mountains.
  • Wedding of the Waters State Park - This park is located near Thermopolis, a short drive from Worland. It offers a unique geological attraction where the Bighorn River flows into the Wind River and the name changes to the Yellowstone River.
  • Hot Springs State Park - This park has a variety of hot springs, pools, and pools for swimming, as well as a vast array of other amenities, including picnic areas, trails, and a wildlife preserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Worland

Worland boasts an extensive network of hiking and biking trails, with varying degrees of difficulty and scenic views. Popular routes include:

  • North Bighorn Mountains Trail - The trail begins at Tensleep Campground and winds through the Big Horn Mountains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Bridger Butte Trail - This trail is located near Worland and is ideal for beginner to intermediate hikers. It offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is particularly popular during the fall when the leaves are turning.

Best Things to Do in Worland: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding Adventures - The area around Worland is great for horseback riding, with miles of trails through the scenic mountains and valleys. Several companies offer guided horseback tours in the area.
  • Fishing and Boating - The region boasts some of the best fly fishing ports in the country, with crystal clear waterways brimming with trout. The main rivers in the area include the Big Horn River and the Shoshone River.
  • Hot Springs Soaking - Relax and unwind at the many hot spring pools located throughout the region, where you can soak in the therapeutic waters and enjoy the picturesque views.

Food and Drink Scene of Worland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Western-style BBQ - The area is famous for its delicious BBQ, with many restaurants and food trucks offering succulent ribs, brisket, and pulled pork.
  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine - Many of the restaurants in Worland specialize in farm-to-table cuisine, featuring locally-sourced ingredients and organic produce.
  • Local game - For the adventurous foodie, try the local game such as bison, elk, and deer.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Craft Beers - The state boasts a growing craft beer scene, with several microbreweries producing a range of hand-crafted beers.
  • Wyoming Whiskey - Produced in nearby Kirby, Wyoming whiskey is gaining recognition as a world-class American whiskey.

Where to Stay in Worland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for lodging in Worland, including:

  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites Worland - This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and a host of amenities, including free high-speed internet, an indoor pool, and fitness center.
  • Sundance Motel - This family-owned motel offers clean and cozy rooms and is located close to downtown Worland.
  • Circle C Lodge - Situated in the heart of the Bighorn Mountains, this rustic lodge offers cozy cabins with fully-equipped kitchens, fireplaces, and stunning mountain views.

What to Buy in Worland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crafts and Pottery - The region has a rich Native American heritage, and many local artisans offer a range of handmade crafts and pottery.
  • Wyoming Whiskey - Bring home a bottle of the state’s famous whiskey as a souvenir.
  • Wildlife Art - The area is home to many talented wildlife artists, and you’ll find an array of stunning paintings and sculptures on display in galleries and shops throughout the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Worland

The people of Worland are proud of their western heritage and Native American roots. Visitors should expect to encounter a friendly, welcoming community that is deeply connected to the natural world and the traditions of their ancestors.

Best Time to Visit Worland

The best time to visit Worland is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and dry, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, fall and spring can also be pleasant, with fewer crowds and milder temperatures. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it can also be a great time to enjoy winter sports and hot springs soaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Worland

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities in the area.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Bring firearms into the park or on other public lands.

Safety and Health

Safety in Worland

Worland is a relatively safe town, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions when hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities. Be prepared for sudden changes in weather conditions and avoid hiking alone if possible.

Health in Worland

As with any rural area, visitors should be aware of potential health hazards, such as snake bites, heat stroke, and altitude sickness. Be sure to drink plenty of water and take breaks when hiking, and avoid touching or approaching wildlife.

Getting to Worland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Worland

  • Worland Municipal Airport - Located just minutes from downtown Worland, the airport offers daily flights to and from Denver International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Worland

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Thermopolis, a short drive from Worland, offers daily bus service to and from destinations throughout the region.

Other Options

  • Rental Car - Visitors can rent a car from any major airport or city and drive to Worland.
  • Private Shuttle - Private shuttle companies offer transportation to and from Worland.

How to Get Around in Worland

The most convenient way to explore Worland and the surrounding area is by car. Rental cars are available at the airport and in downtown Worland. For those who prefer to get around on foot, downtown Worland is very walkable, and many attractions and amenities are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Worland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yellowstone National Park - Located just a few hours’ drive north of Worland, Yellowstone is one of the most iconic national parks in the world and offers a wide range of outdoor activities and natural wonders.
  • Cody, Wyoming - This historic town is located just about an hour west of Worland and is known for its cowboy culture and western heritage.
  • Thermopolis, Wyoming - This town is about a 30-minute drive south of Worland and is home to the famous Hot Springs State Park, as well as several other natural attractions.

Conclusion

Worland is a hidden gem in the beautiful state of Wyoming. From its rich cultural heritage and historic landmarks to its stunning natural attractions and outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, learn about the region’s history and culture, or simply relax and soak in the hot springs, Worland is the perfect destination for your next vacation.
