Introduction to Rawlins

Located in Southeast Wyoming, Rawlins is a hidden gem that boasts the perfect mix of rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural wonders. The city attracts adventure lovers, history buffs, and nature enthusiasts who love exploring scenic spots like outdoor adventure opportunities, historical landmarks, and iconic museums.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about Rawlins, from incredible attractions to do’s and don’ts, so you can plan your trip and get the most out of your visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Carbon County Museum

The Carbon County Museum is located in downtown Rawlins. It features exhibits on the history of the area, including industry, mining, and ranching.

The museum has an extensive collection of historical artifacts from the local area, including Native American artifacts, 19th-century clothing, antique firearms, and an old printing press.

Wyoming Frontier Prison Museum

The Wyoming Frontier Prison Museum is the site of Wyoming’s first state penitentiary, which operated from 1901-1981.

Visitors can take guided tours of the prison and its cells, where infamous prisoners like Butch Cassidy were incarcerated. The museum also houses a gift shop, and visitors can buy authentic replicas of guards’ uniforms and other prison memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Rawlins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Seminoe State Park

Seminoe State Park is situated 20 miles north of Rawlins. It has over 20,000-acres of recreational area, which includes a 1800-acre reservoir.

The state park is a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts. It offers a variety of activities, including fishing, camping, hiking, birding, and wildlife watching.

Medicine Bow-Routt National Forest

The Medicine Bow-Routt National Forest offers more than a million acres of outdoor adventures. It has campgrounds, biking trails, hiking trails, fly-fishing, and off-road opportunities.

Rawlins Peak

Rawlins Peak is one of the, if not the, highest peaks in Wyoming. Hiking enthusiasts will love trekking to the peak of Rawlins Peak to experience the panoramic views of the surrounding plains and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rawlins

Aspen Trail

The Aspen Trail is a short hike in Rawlins that follows a stream through some beautiful aspen groves.

Continental Divide Trail

The Continental Divide Trail is the United States’ premier long-distance trail. The Wyoming section of the trail passes through Rawlins and the surrounding area, offering some of the country’s most beautiful hiking opportunities.

Local Culture and Customs of Rawlins

  • Wyomingites are proud to be from the Cowboy State.
  • Rodeo is very important to Wyomingites, so try to catch one in the summer.

Best Time to Visit Rawlins

The best time to visit Rawlins is in the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are abundant. The fall season offers stunning fall foliage. Winter is a great time for skiing enjoyment.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rawlins

Do:

  • Wear layers of clothing since the temperature can change rapidly in Wyoming.
  • Visit a rodeo, get a hat and boots, and experience the true Cowboy State.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather as it can get very cold and snowy in the winter.
  • Don’t forget your camera – Wyoming’s scenery is stunning!

Safety and Health

Safety in Rawlins

Rawlins is overall a safe city for tourists. However, precautions for wildlife and the cold should be made when it comes to visiting the state’s natural areas.

Health in Rawlins

There is a Rawlins hospital, so tourists and locals have access to medical care if needed.

Getting to Rawlins: Transportation Options and Logistics
