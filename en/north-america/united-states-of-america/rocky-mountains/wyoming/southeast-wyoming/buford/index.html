Buford, Wyoming - a small town located in Southeast Wyoming in the Rocky Mountains region of the United States of America. Despite its small size and population of only one, Buford is a charming destination that offers a range of cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or want to indulge in local cuisine and culture, Buford has something to offer everyone! In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at all the must-visit places, best things to do, where to stay, and more to help you plan your visit to Buford.

Local Culture and Customs of Buford

  • Wyoming is known for its cowboy culture, and Buford is no different. Expect to see ranchers in cowboy hats and boots.
  • Hunting and fishing are major pastimes for many locals in and around Buford.
  • Tipping is always appreciated for good service.

Best Time to Visit Buford

The best time to visit Buford is between June and August when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, if you’re interested in skiing and other winter sports, visit between December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buford

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs

  • Try local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Trespass on private property
  • Disrespect wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Buford

Wyoming is known for its wild landscape, so extra caution should be taken while exploring the outdoors and engaging in outdoor activities. Mountain lions, bears, and other wildlife can be dangerous if not treated with respect.

Health in Buford

Make sure you drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen while exploring the outdoors, especially during the summer months to avoid heatstroke.
