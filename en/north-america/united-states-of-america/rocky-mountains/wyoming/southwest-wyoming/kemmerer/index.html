Introduction to Kemmerer

Located in southwestern Wyoming within the Rocky Mountains, Kemmerer is a picturesque small town known for its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and vibrant cultural life. Founded in 1897 as a coal mining town, Kemmerer is now home to several remarkable museums, historical sites, and outdoor recreational activities. Its charming small town atmosphere and friendly locals make it an ideal destination for visitors seeking a quiet retreat in beautiful surroundings.

List of Places to Visit in Kemmerer

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kemmerer is home to several notable museums and historical sites that reflect its extensive history and cultural heritage.

Fossil Butte National Monument

Situated just outside Kemmerer, Fossil Butte National Monument is a must-see attraction for visitors interested in paleontology. The monument features preserved fossils of ancient plant and animal species, including crocodiles, turtles, fish, and insects. Visitors can enjoy guided tours, hikes, and educational programs at the visitor center.

J.C. Penney Historic District

Named after the famous retail entrepreneur who was born and raised in Kemmerer, the J.C. Penney Historic District is a collection of historic buildings and landmarks that have played an important role in the town’s past. Visitors can take a self-guided walking tour of the district and learn about the town’s early settlement days and thriving coal mining industry.

Natural Attractions of Kemmerer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kemmerer offers a wide range of outdoor activities and scenic vistas that are ideal for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

Fossil Lake

Formerly a large body of water that covered the area, Fossil Lake is now an arid expanse that is home to an abundance of fossils and fascinating geological formations. Visitors can wander trails, spot wildlife, and take in breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and desert.

Fontenelle Creek

A popular destination for fly fishing, Fontenelle Creek is nestled in the beautiful Bridger-Teton National Forest and offers an idyllic setting for camping, picnicking, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kemmerer

Kemmerer is famous for its numerous hiking and biking trails that wind through its rugged mountains and scenic valleys.

Hiking Trails

  • Dempsey Ridge Trail: A challenging 7-mile trail that rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Smiths Fork Trailhead: A moderate 5-mile trail that leads hikers through forests and meadows to a picturesque mountain lake.

Biking Routes

  • Kemmerer Rail Trail: A peaceful 6-mile trail that winds through scenic landscapes and offers stunning views of the area’s wildlife and flora.

Best Things to Do in Kemmerer: Activities and Tours

Kemmerer offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests.

Kemmerer Coal Mine Tour

A unique opportunity to learn about the town’s coal mining heritage, the Kemmerer Coal Mine tour takes visitors deep into the underground mines and offers an up-close look at the machinery and equipment used by early miners.

Hunting and Fishing

Kemmerer is a popular destination for hunting and fishing enthusiasts who can enjoy diverse wildlife and an abundance of fish in the area’s pristine waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Kemmerer

Kemmerer offers visitors a delightful culinary experience with its array of local dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: A local specialty, these deep-fried bull testicles are served in many of Kemmerer’s restaurants.
  • Cowboy Chili: Made with locally sourced beef and seasoned with spicy chilies, this hearty soup is a popular dish in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Kemmerer Rodeo Red: A locally brewed beer with a smooth, hoppy flavor.
  • Wyoming Whiskey: A smooth whiskey made locally in Kirby, Wyoming.

Where to Stay in Kemmerer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kemmerer offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences.

  • Best Western Plus Fossil Country Inn & Suites: Conveniently located near Fossil Butte National Monument, this hotel features modern amenities and a free hot breakfast.
  • Kemmerer-Diamondville KOA: A family-friendly campsite with a pool, playground, and mini-golf course.
  • JC Suites: A small, charming guesthouse in the town center that offers comfortable rooms and personalized service.

What to Buy in Kemmerer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Kemmerer can take home a range of unique and authentic souvenirs that reflect the town’s history and culture.

  • Coal figurines and ornaments: Made out of locally-sourced coal, these figurines and ornaments are a popular souvenir item.
  • Western wear: Kemmerer is home to several stores that sell authentic cowboy boots, hats, and other western-style clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Kemmerer

Kemmerer has a friendly and welcoming local community that values its cultural traditions and heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and observe the town’s laws and regulations.

Best Time to Visit Kemmerer

Kemmerer enjoys a mild, dry climate throughout the year, though visitors should be prepared for colder temperatures in the winter months. The best time to visit Kemmerer is during the summer (June to August), when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kemmerer

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s rich history and cultural heritage by visiting its museums and historical sites.
  • Do take advantage of the area’s numerous hiking and biking trails, and enjoy the stunning natural scenery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the area’s natural resources.
  • Don’t trespass on private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Kemmerer is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take normal precautions such as keeping valuables out of sight and securing their accommodation properly.

Safety in Kemmerer

  • Be cautious when hiking or biking in remote areas, and always carry adequate supplies and water.
  • Be aware of the risk of wildfires during the dry summer months.

Health in Kemmerer

  • Ensure you have health insurance coverage before you travel.
  • Check with your doctor prior to traveling and take all necessary precautions.

Getting to Kemmerer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kemmerer is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Kemmerer

  • Rock Springs - Sweetwater County Airport: Approximately 75 miles south of Kemmerer and served by several airlines.
  • Salt Lake City International Airport: Approximately 150 miles southwest of Kemmerer and served by major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Kemmerer

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Rock Springs: Approximately 75 miles south of Kemmerer.

Other Options

  • The nearest Amtrak station is in Green River, approximately 100 miles south of Kemmerer.

How to Get Around in Kemmerer

Kemmerer is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Kemmerer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kemmerer is an ideal base for exploring Wyoming’s many natural wonders and cultural attractions, including:

  • Yellowstone National Park: Approximately 200 miles north of Kemmerer, Yellowstone National Park boasts stunning natural landscapes, geysers, and wildlife.
  • Grand Teton National Park: Approximately 150 miles north of Kemmerer, the park offers a range of outdoor activities and breathtaking views of the mountain range.

Conclusion

Kemmerer is a hidden gem in the heart of Wyoming that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and recreational activities. Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation in peaceful surroundings or an adventurous outdoor experience, Kemmerer has something to offer every traveler.
