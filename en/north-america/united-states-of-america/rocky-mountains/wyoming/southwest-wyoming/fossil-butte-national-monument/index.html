A Comprehensive Tour Guide to Fossil Butte National Monument

Fossil Butte National Monument is a hidden gem located in Southwest Wyoming, United States. Established in 1972, this 8,198-acre park preserves the world’s richest fossil deposits from the Eocene Epoch, which was about 56 million years ago. This incredible site provides a glimpse into the ancient past and is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and fossil lovers. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more!

Introduction to Fossil Butte National Monument

Fossil Butte National Monument is located in the western part of Wyoming, in the Rocky Mountains. The park’s landscape is unique, with wide-open spaces, rolling hills, and breathtaking scenic views. The monument has a lot to offer, from ancient fossils to outdoor adventures. The park is open year-round and offers something for everyone.

  1. Fossil Discovery Trail

Natural Attractions of Fossil Butte National Monument: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fossil Butte National Monument has a lot to offer to adventure seekers and nature lovers. Here are some of the best outdoor activities and scenic views:

  1. Hiking - There are several hiking routes in the park, ranging from easy to difficult. The hiking trail to the top of Fossil Butte offers a panoramic view of the entire park.
  2. Camping - There are three campgrounds within the park, offering primitive to modern camping facilities.
  3. Fishing - Fossil Lake is known for its abundant population of fish species, including trout, bass, and bluegill.
  4. Wildlife Watching - The park is home to several wildlife species, including pronghorns, elk, and coyotes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fossil Butte National Monument

Fossil Butte National Monument has several hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. These trails range from easy to difficult, and some of the most popular ones are:

  1. Fossil Lake Trail - This 3-mile hike takes you around the ancient Fossil Lake and provides an excellent view of the park’s fossils.
  2. Nature Trail - This easy trail is perfect for families with young children. It is 1-mile long and takes you through the park’s flora and fauna.
  3. Blue Rim Trail - This 7-mile trail loop takes you through the park’s topography and offers beautiful scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Fossil Butte National Monument: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and camping, there are several other things to do in the park. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  1. Ranger-led Programs - The park offers several ranger-led programs, including guided hikes, educational programs, and fossil preparation demonstrations.
  2. Stargazing - The park’s remote location offers an excellent opportunity to stargaze and witness the spectacular night sky.
  3. Fossil Dig Programs - The park offers a fossil dig program where visitors can assist the staff in finding and excavating fossils.
