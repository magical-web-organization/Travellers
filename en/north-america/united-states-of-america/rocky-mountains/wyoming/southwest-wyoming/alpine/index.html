Introduction to Alpine

Nestled in the Rocky Mountains of Southwest Wyoming, Alpine is a charming town that attracts visitors with scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. The town is surrounded by national forests and offers easy access to the Snake River and Palisades Reservoir, making it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, while its museums and historical places are perfect for culture lovers. In this comprehensive travel guide to Alpine, we will explore the best of what this town has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Alpine

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Alpine has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated in its museums and historical places. Here are some must-see attractions:

  • Alpine Historical Park - A collection of six buildings, including a log cabin, schoolhouse, and blacksmith shop, that showcase the town’s history.
  • Star Valley Historical Society Museum - A museum that features exhibits on sheep ranching, early pioneers, and Native American artifacts.
  • American Legion Monument - A memorial that honors the men and women who served in the military.

Natural Attractions of Alpine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alpine’s natural beauty is its biggest asset. Here are some outdoor activities and scenic spots to explore:

  • Snake River - A popular destination for fishing, rafting, and kayaking.
  • Palisades Reservoir - A stunning lake surrounded by mountains, perfect for boating and fishing.
  • Grey’s River Road - A scenic drive that takes you through forests, canyons, and streams.
  • Hiking and Biking Trails - There are many trails to explore, including the Blowhard Trail, Sheep Creek Trail, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alpine

Alpine offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Blowhard Trail - A 8.5-mile hike that offers breathtaking views of the Snake River Canyon.
  • Sheep Creek Trail - A 9.2-mile loop that takes you through forests, creeks, and meadows.
  • Alpine Ridge Trail - A 8.5-mile hike that offers panoramic views of the Teton Range.
  • Grey’s River Trail - A 22-mile trail that takes you through forests and canyons.

Best Things to Do in Alpine: Activities and Tours

Alpine offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some must-do experiences:

  • Fishing - Alpine is known for its world-class fishing in the Snake River and Palisades Reservoir.
  • Rafting and Kayaking - The Snake River offers exciting rapids for adventure seekers.
  • Scenic Drives - Grey’s River Road and Snake River Canyon Scenic Drive offer stunning views.
  • Wildlife Watching - The area is home to a variety of wildlife, including elk, moose, and bald eagles.
  • Snowmobiling - The town has groomed trails and access to backcountry terrain for snowmobiling.
  • Horseback Riding - Experience the beauty of Alpine on horseback with guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alpine

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Alpine:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Star Valley Historical Society Museum and take a scenic drive on Grey’s River Road
2 Hike the Blowhard Trail in the morning and go fishing in the Snake River in the afternoon
3 Take a kayaking tour of the Snake River Canyon
4 Spend the day boating on Palisades Reservoir
5 Explore the Alpine Historical Park and visit the American Legion Monument
6 Go horseback riding in the morning and snowmobiling in the afternoon
7 Choose your favorite activity from earlier in the week and do it again or relax and enjoy the scenery

Food and Drink Scene of Alpine

Alpine offers a variety of dining options, from casual cafes to fine dining restaurants. Here are some local dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trout - Freshly caught trout from the Snake River is a local specialty.
  • Sheepherder’s Stew - A hearty stew made with lamb and vegetables.
  • Elk Burger - A healthier alternative to beef burgers.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whisky - Wyoming Whiskey Distillery produces award-winning whiskey.
  • Craft Beer - Melvin Brewing is an award-winning craft brewery located in Alpine.
  • Huckleberry Lemonade - A refreshing drink made with wild huckleberries.

Where to Stay in Alpine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alpine offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Flying Saddle Resort - A full-service resort that offers rooms and cabins.
  • Alpine Inn - A budget-friendly motel with comfortable rooms.
  • Buffalo Valley RV Park - A campground with RV sites, cabins, and tent sites.

What to Buy in Alpine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alpine offers a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home as gifts or to remind you of your trip. Here are some options:

  • Sheepskin Products - Sheep ranching is a local industry, and you can find a variety of sheepskin products, including blankets, rugs, and slippers.
  • Huckleberry Jam - Huckleberries are a local delicacy, and you can buy jam made from wild huckleberries.
  • Local Art - There are several galleries and shops that offer locally made art and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Alpine

Alpine has a friendly and welcoming community that values the outdoors and local traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including:

  • Leave No Trace - Alpine is a popular destination for outdoor activities, and visitors are encouraged to practice Leave No Trace principles to preserve the natural beauty of the area.
  • Respect Wildlife - The area is home to a variety of wildlife, and visitors are asked to respect their habitat and observe them from a safe distance.
  • Be Friendly and Polite - The community is known for its hospitality, and visitors are encouraged to be friendly and polite.

Best Time to Visit Alpine

The best time to visit Alpine depends on your interests. Summer (June to August) is the peak season and offers the best weather for outdoor activities. Fall (September to November) is a great time for hiking and wildlife watching. Winter (December to February) is perfect for snowmobiling and skiing, while Spring (March to May) offers mild temperatures and wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alpine

Do:

  • Do bring sunscreen, bug spray, and plenty of water when exploring the outdoors.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.
  • Do support local businesses and communities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave trash or litter in natural areas.
  • Don’t approach or feed wildlife.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alpine

Alpine is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should always take precautions when exploring the outdoors. Here are some safety tips:

  • Always wear appropriate clothing and footwear when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Check weather conditions before venturing out.
  • Observe wildlife from a safe distance and never approach or feed them.
  • Always tell someone where you are going and when you plan to return.

Health in Alpine

Alpine has a small medical clinic that serves residents and visitors. In case of emergency, visitors can go to Star Valley Health in nearby Afton, Wyoming.

Getting to Alpine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alpine

The closest airport to Alpine is the Jackson Hole Airport (JAC), which is about 48 miles away. Salt Lake City International Airport (SLC) is another option, which is about 220 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Alpine

Greyhound does not have a station in Alpine, but they stop in Afton, which is about 15 miles away.

Other Options

The best way to explore Alpine and the surrounding area is by car.

How to Get Around in Alpine

The town of Alpine is small and easy to navigate on foot, but a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Alpine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alpine is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Grand Teton National Park - A stunning national park with mountains, lakes, and hiking trails.
  • Yellowstone National Park - One of the most famous national parks in the world, and home to geysers, hot springs, and wildlife.
  • Jackson, Wyoming - A charming town known for its art galleries, restaurants, and ski resorts.

Conclusion

Alpine, Wyoming, is a hidden gem in the Rocky Mountains that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local traditions. This comprehensive travel guide is aimed at helping visitors make the most of their trip and experience the best of what Alpine has to offer.
