Comprehensive Travel Guide to Lyman, Wyoming

Nestled in the Rocky Mountains of southwestern Wyoming, Lyman offers a charming gateway to the Wild West. From outdoor adventures and scenic drives to top-notch museums and historical landmarks, this town has it all. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a longer excursion, use our travel guide to Lyman to make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Lyman

Lyman is a small town in southwestern Wyoming with a population of around 2,000. Founded in the late 19th century, it boasts a rich heritage that is steeped in mining, agriculture, and ranching. The town is located close to several national parks and forests, making it a popular base for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Lyman

No matter your interests, there are plenty of things to do and see in Lyman. Here are some of the top attractions to check out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Downtown Lyman: Take a stroll down Main Street to admire the charming storefronts and historic buildings that date back to the town’s original settlement.
  • Union Pacific Depot: This beautifully restored building is now home to a museum that showcases the town’s railroad history.
  • Lyman Museum: Learn about the town’s past and present at this museum, which features exhibits on everything from mining and agriculture to local wildlife and geology.

Natural Attractions of Lyman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fossil Butte National Monument: Just a short drive from Lyman, this park is home to some of the world’s best-preserved fossils, including ancient fish, turtles, and birds.
  • Bear River State Park: This scenic park is known for its hot springs and wildlife viewing opportunities. Visitors can also go fishing, boating, and hiking.
  • Lincoln County Fair: Held every August, this event is a celebration of the town’s agricultural roots, with rodeos, live music, and carnival rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lyman

Exploring Lyman’s stunning natural surroundings is a must-do. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to try:

  • Fossil Butte Loop Trail: This 3.5-mile trail takes you through the heart of Fossil Butte National Monument, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Bear River State Park Trail: This 2-mile trail follows the Bear River through the park, offering opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife spotting.
  • Uinta-Wasatch-Cache National Forest: With over a million acres of wilderness to explore, this forest area offers endless options for hikers, bikers, and outdoor enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Lyman: Activities and Tours

From adrenaline-fueled adventures to family-friendly outings, Lyman has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in town:

  • Bear River Kayak Tours: Embark on a relaxing kayak tour down the Bear River, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and wildlife sightings along the way.
  • Whitewater Rafting on the Green River: For a more exciting adventure, head to nearby Green River to enjoy a thrilling whitewater rafting trip.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience the natural beauty of Lyman atop a horse, with multiple ranches in the area offering guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lyman

Lyman is the perfect base to explore the stunning natural landscapes surrounding the town. Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Lyman and check into your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring historic downtown and visiting the Lyman Museum. Day 2: Head to Fossil Butte National Monument for a day of fossil hunting and hiking. Day 3: Take a guided horseback riding tour in the morning, followed by an afternoon of fishing or kayaking on the Bear River. Day 4: Drive to the nearby town of Kemmerer to visit the historic JC Penney store and tour the nearby coal mines. Day 5: Hit the trails in Uinta-Wasatch-Cache National Forest for a day of hiking and wildlife spotting. Day 6: Go whitewater rafting on the Green River for a thrilling adventure. Day 7: Spend your last day relaxing at Bear River State Park, or shopping for souvenirs in downtown Lyman.

Food and Drink Scene of Lyman

Lyman may be small, but it packs a punch when it comes to food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Sample one of Wyoming’s most famous exports with a juicy bison burger from one of the local restaurants.
  • Cowboy Beans: A staple of Western cuisine, cowboy beans are a hearty and flavorful side dish that pairs perfectly with grilled meats.
  • Huckleberry Ice Cream: Made with fresh huckleberries picked from the surrounding mountains, this ice cream is a uniquely local treat.

Local Beverages

  • Wyoming Whiskey: This locally distilled whiskey is made from a combination of corn, wheat, and barley, and has won several awards for its smooth flavor.
  • Uinta Brewing Company: This Uinta-based brewery offers a range of craft beers, including the popular Hop Nosh IPA.
  • Wyoming Sarsaparilla: A non-alcoholic soda made from the root of the sarsaparilla plant, this drink is a refreshing alternative to traditional sodas.

Where to Stay in Lyman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lyman offers several options for accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in town:

  • Lyman Motel: This affordable motel offers clean and comfortable rooms, with convenient access to downtown Lyman and local attractions.
  • Hitching Post Inn: For a more upscale experience, book a room at this charming guesthouse, which boasts stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Airbnb: Lyman has several Airbnb rentals available, ranging from cozy cabins to spacious family homes.

What to Buy in Lyman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From cowboy gear to local handicrafts, there are plenty of unique souvenirs to bring home from Lyman. Here are some top picks:

  • Leather Cowboy Boots: Wyoming is known for its Western sensibilities, and a pair of cowboy boots is the perfect way to show off your Wild West style.
  • Huckleberry Jam: Made from fresh-picked huckleberries, this jam is a delicious and uniquely local treat.
  • Handmade Pottery: Several local artists sell handmade pottery in downtown Lyman, with unique designs inspired by the surrounding landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Lyman

Wyoming is known for its independent spirit and rugged individualism, and Lyman is no exception. Visitors to the town should be prepared for a warm welcome, but should also respect the locals’ love of personal space and privacy.

Best Time to Visit Lyman

Lyman is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. For outdoor enthusiasts, summer and fall offer the best hiking, biking, and fishing opportunities. Winter sports enthusiasts will want to visit during the ski and snowmobiling season, which typically runs from November through March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lyman

Here are some tips to help you enjoy your trip to Lyman to the fullest:

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and try out some cowboy boots or hats.
  • Visit the natural attractions and national parks in the area.
  • Respect local customs, including a tendency towards independence and privacy.

Don’t:

  • Invade locals’ privacy or space.
  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Show disrespect towards local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Lyman is a safe and welcoming destination, but visitors should take common sense precautions to ensure their health and safety.

Safety in Lyman

  • Be aware of wildlife and follow safety guidelines when hiking, fishing, or camping.
  • Use caution when driving on rural roads, which may be narrow and winding.
  • Be careful when participating in outdoor activities, including kayaking and whitewater rafting.

Health in Lyman

  • Be aware of altitude sickness if you’re not used to higher elevations.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against the sun and mosquitoes.
  • Take necessary precautions when hiking or camping to avoid injury.

Getting to Lyman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lyman is located close to several airports and bus stations, making it easy to get to from major cities like Denver and Salt Lake City.

Airports Near Lyman

  • Salt Lake City International Airport: The closest major airport, located about 150 miles south of Lyman.
  • Jackson Hole Airport: Located about 140 miles north of Lyman.

Bus Stations Near Lyman

  • Greyhound: The closest bus station is located in Rock Springs, about 30 miles from Lyman.

Other Options

  • Driving: Lyman is easily accessible by car, with several highways running through the town.
  • Rental Car: Several rental car agencies operate out of major airports in the area.

How to Get Around in Lyman

Lyman is a small town, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, renting a car or bringing your own vehicle is highly recommended if you plan on exploring the surrounding natural landscapes or taking day trips to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Lyman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lyman is located close to several national parks and forests, as well as charming neighboring towns. Here are some top picks for nearby destinations:

  • Yellowstone National Park: Located about 200 miles north of Lyman, this iconic national park is known for its stunning geysers, hot springs, and wildlife.
  • Grand Teton National Park: Just south of Yellowstone, this park boasts incredible hiking trails, mountain views, and outdoor adventures.
  • Jackson Hole: This charming town is located just outside Grand Teton National Park, with plenty of dining, shopping, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

From natural wonders to cowboy culture, Lyman has a little bit of everything. Use our comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to this charming Wyoming town and experience the best it has to offer.
