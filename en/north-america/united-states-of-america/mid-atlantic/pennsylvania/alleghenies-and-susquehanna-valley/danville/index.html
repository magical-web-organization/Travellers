Introduction to Danville

Nestled in the Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley of Pennsylvania, Danville is a charming town rich in history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With its quaint Victorian architecture, scenic views, and welcoming locals, it’s a perfect destination for a relaxing getaway or an active outdoor adventure.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink recommendations, safety tips, and everything you need to know for planning your trip to Danville.

List of Places to Visit in Danville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Montour County Historical Society

The Montour County Historical Society is a must-visit for history buffs. This small but impressive museum houses artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that provide insight into the rich history of Montour County. Be sure to check out the famous “Danville Railroad Wreck” exhibit that tells the story of a horrific train accident that occurred in 1906.

The Danville Heritage Festival

If you’re visiting Danville in July, don’t miss the Danville Heritage Festival. This annual event celebrates the town’s heritage with live music, food vendors, historical reenactments, and fun activities for the whole family.

Natural Attractions of Danville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shikellamy State Park

Shikellamy State Park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, fish, or paddle, this park has something for everyone. With stunning views of the Susquehanna River and miles of scenic trails, it’s a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

Danville Riverside

Experience the beauty of Danville’s riverfront by taking a stroll along the Danville Riverside. With its serene views of the Susquehanna River and lush greenery, it’s a perfect spot to relax and unwind after a day of sightseeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Danville

Montour Preserve

The Montour Preserve is a hidden gem of Danville that’s perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. With over 16 miles of well-marked trails that cut through forests, fields, and wetlands, it’s a great place to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Susquehanna River Valley

The Susquehanna River Valley offers some of the most scenic biking routes in the region. With its flat roads and breathtaking views of the river, it’s a perfect spot for a leisurely bike ride or a long-distance adventure. Be sure to check out the popular Buffalo Valley Rail Trail, a scenic 9-mile trail that winds through the valley’s picturesque countryside.

Best Things to Do in Danville: Activities and Tours

Danville Walking Tour

Embark on a journey through Danville’s historic past with the Danville Walking Tour. Led by knowledgeable guides, the tour takes you through the town’s various historic sites, including the popular Market Street district and the historic Victorian homes on Bloom Street.

Kayaking on the Susquehanna River

Experience Danville’s natural beauty up close with a kayaking tour on the Susquehanna River. With its calm waters and stunning scenery, it’s a perfect spot for a relaxing afternoon on the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Danville

Day 1 Explore the Montour County Historical Society and take a walking tour of the Market Street district
Day 2 Visit the Montour Preserve for a day of hiking and outdoor adventure
Day 3 Experience the beauty of the Susquehanna River with a kayaking tour
Day 4 Explore the town’s Victorian architecture on Bloom Street
Day 5 Take a bike ride through the scenic Susquehanna River Valley
Day 6 Relax by the Danville Riverside and enjoy the tranquil views of the river
Day 7 Visit the Danville Heritage Festival for live music, food vendors, and historical reenactments

Food and Drink Scene of Danville

Must-Try Dishes

Savage’s Alehouse

Savage’s Alehouse is a local favorite for its hearty pub fare and extensive beer selection. Be sure to try their famous Savage Burger, made with locally-sourced beef and topped with bacon and cheddar cheese.

The Original Italian Pizza & Restaurant

For a taste of authentic Italian cuisine, head over to The Original Italian Pizza & Restaurant. Their menu features a range of delicious pastas, salads, and sandwiches, but the standout dish is their traditional New York-style pizza.

Local Beverages

Old Forge Brewing Company

Located in the heart of Danville, the Old Forge Brewing Company is a popular spot for craft beer enthusiasts. They offer a wide range of unique brews, including their famous T-Rail Pale Ale and Dunkelweizen.

Where to Stay in Danville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re planning a trip to Danville, you’ll find plenty of options for accommodations. Whether you’re looking for a charming bed and breakfast or a budget-friendly hostel, you’re sure to find something that fits your needs.

Pine Barn Inn

For a truly luxurious stay, check out the Pine Barn Inn. This historic hotel offers elegant rooms, fine dining, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Red Roof Inn Danville, PA

If you’re on a budget, the Red Roof Inn Danville, PA, is a great option. With its affordable rates, clean rooms, and convenient location, it’s a perfect choice for travelers looking to save money without sacrificing comfort.

What to Buy in Danville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for a souvenir to remember your trip or a unique gift for a loved one, Danville has plenty of options. Here are some of the best places to shop for local products and souvenirs:

Bason Coffee Roasting

For coffee lovers, Bason Coffee Roasting is a must-visit destination. They roast their own coffee beans in-house and offer a range of unique blends that are sure to satisfy any caffeine craving.

Danville Arts Council

The Danville Arts Council is a great place to shop for unique, handmade gifts created by local artists and artisans. From pottery and jewelry to paintings and sculptures, you’ll find something special to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Danville

Danville is a friendly, welcoming community with a rich history and a strong sense of pride in its heritage. When visiting, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions and to be courteous and respectful to your hosts.

Best Time to Visit Danville

The best time to visit Danville depends on your preferences. Summer is the busiest season, with the Danville Heritage Festival in July attracting crowds of visitors. Fall is a popular time to visit for its colorful foliage and mild weather. Winter can be a bit chilly, but it’s a great time to enjoy outdoor activities like skiing and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Danville

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Explore the town’s historic sites and natural beauty
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural environment
  • Disrespect locals or their culture
  • Ignore safety guidelines and precautions

Safety and Health

Safety in Danville

While Danville is generally a safe place to visit, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Be sure to lock your doors and windows at night, and avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas. Take care when hiking or biking in remote areas and always bring a map and plenty of water.

Health in Danville

Danville has a small hospital and a few medical clinics that provide basic medical care. If you have any serious medical conditions or require specialized treatments, it’s best to seek care in a larger city nearby.

Getting to Danville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Danville

The closest airport to Danville is the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport, which is approximately 60 miles away. Other airports in the region include the Harrisburg International Airport and the Lehigh Valley International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Danville

Greyhound and Trailways both offer bus service to Danville from various locations throughout the region.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Danville is easily accessible from major highways, including Interstates 80 and 180. Amtrak also offers train service to nearby cities, including Harrisburg and Williamsport.

How to Get Around in Danville

Danville is a small town, so it’s easy to get around on foot or by bike. If you’re traveling longer distances, car rentals are available at the nearby airports and bus stations.

Where to Go Next from Danville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Knoebels Amusement Resort

Located just 30 minutes from Danville, Knoebels Amusement Resort is a fun destination for families and thrill-seekers alike. With its whimsical rides, attractions, and carnival games, it’s a perfect spot for a day of fun and adventure.

Ricketts Glen State Park

Just a short drive from Danville, Ricketts Glen State Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. With over 20 waterfalls and miles of hiking trails, it’s a perfect spot for a day trip or a weekend camping adventure.

Conclusion

Danville is a charming, welcoming town that offers a perfect mix of historic sites, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With its friendly locals, delicious cuisine, and stunning views, it’s a destination that’s well worth a visit. Follow this comprehensive guide to plan your ultimate Danville adventure.
