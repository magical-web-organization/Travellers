Introduction to Lewistown

Nestled in the heart of Central Pennsylvania, lies the charming town of Lewistown. It is a beautiful destination full of rich history, picturesque countryside, and hospitable locals. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply seeking a peaceful getaway, Lewistown has something for everyone.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and much more. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Lewistown

  • Mifflin County Historical Society: The Mifflin County Historical Society is a treasure trove of artifacts, archives, and exhibits that showcase the rich history of Lewistown and surrounding areas. It’s the perfect place to start your exploration of the town.

  • Lewistown Station: Lewistown Station is a historical landmark that dates back to the 1880s. It’s a unique building that reflects the town’s rich railroad history and serves as a fitting welcome to the town.

  • Indian Caverns: If you’re into spelunking, Indian Caverns should be on your bucket list. Experience the underground tour that takes you through the scenic caverns filled with stunning geological formations and underground waterfalls.

  • Kishacoquillas Valley: The Kishacoquillas Valley is a scenic place to visit with its rolling hills, serene farmlands, and picture-perfect countryside. It’s also home to many Amish communities, where you can learn about their way of life and purchase handmade goods.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mifflin County Historical Society: As mentioned earlier, the Mifflin County Historical Society should be your first stop to learn about the town’s rich history.

  • Pennsylvania Canal Museum: The Pennsylvania Canal Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It offers an in-depth look at the canal system and its pivotal role in the town’s development.

  • Union National Bank: The Union National Bank is a historical landmark that underwent extensive renovation and restoration. It serves as a living testament to the town’s financial history.

Natural Attractions of Lewistown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Greenwood Furnace State Park: If you’re looking for outdoor activities, Greenwood Furnace State Park offers hiking, fishing, picnicking, and camping opportunities. You can also enjoy stunning views of the mountains and the beautiful lake.

  • Trough Creek State Park: Trough Creek State Park offers plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, rock climbing, and kayaking. It is home to the rustic Trough Creek Lodge, which offers a unique and comfortable stay.

  • Derry Township Community Park: The Derry Township Community Park is a great place to enjoy a picnic, play sports, and relax with your family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewistown

  • Seven Mountains Conservation Corp: The Seven Mountains Conservation Corp offers many hiking trails that allow you to explore the beautiful countryside of Lewistown.

  • Old Crow Traders: Old Crow Traders offer a wide range of bike rentals and trails that take you through the beautiful valleys, streams, and mountains of Mifflin County.

Best Things to Do in Lewistown: Activities and Tours

  • Victorian Christmas: If you’re visiting Lewistown during the holiday season, you must visit Victorian Christmas. It offers live performances, local crafts, food, and drinks, and much more.

  • Chestnut Ridge Golf Resort and Conference Center: The Chestnut Ridge Golf Resort and Conference Center is the perfect place for golf enthusiasts. It offers two championship golf courses, state-of-the-art practice facilities, and much more.

Food and Drink Scene of Lewistown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shoo-fly Pie: Shoo-fly Pie is a traditional dessert loved by locals and tourists alike. Made with molasses and brown sugar, it’s a sweet treat that will leave you craving for more.

  • Scrapple: Scrapple is a classic breakfast dish made with pork, cornmeal, and spices. It might sound unusual, but it’s a favorite of many people in Lewistown.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: Yuengling Beer is the oldest brewery in America and a favorite of many beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Lewistown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quality Inn & Suites: Quality Inn & Suites is conveniently located near downtown Lewistown and offers comfortable stays.

  • Ridge Campground: Ridge Campground is an excellent option for those looking for an outdoor retreat. It offers stunning views and comfortable camping facilities.

What to Buy in Lewistown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amish Handicrafts: Mifflin County is home to many Amish communities, where you can purchase handmade goods such as quilts, baskets, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewistown

  • Amish Culture: While not limited to Lewistown, the Amish community is an essential part of Mifflin County. Visitors should respect their traditions and way of life while visiting the region.

Best Time to Visit Lewistown

The best time to visit Lewistown is between May and October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewistown

Do:

  • Do immerse yourself in the region’s rich history and culture.
  • Do enjoy the beautiful outdoors by hiking, biking, or fishing.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including Shoo-fly Pie and Scrapple.
  • Do respect the Amish communities and their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Don’t make unnecessary noise that may disturb the peace of the area.
  • Don’t visit the Amish communities without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewistown

Lewistown is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take standard precautions like anywhere else. Don’t leave your valuables unattended, be aware of your surroundings and don’t venture into unfamiliar or isolated areas.

Health in Lewistown

Visitors should bring over-the-counter medications and prescription medications as needed. There are several pharmacies in the Lewistown area.

Getting to Lewistown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewistown

The most convenient airport to Lewistown is Harrisburg International Airport, which is approximately 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Lewistown

Greyhound operates a bus station in nearby State College, which offers various routes to and from major cities in the country.

Other Options

Several major highways serve Lewistown, including highways 322, 522, and 22. Visitors can also reach the area via Amtrak, with service to Lewistown Junction, located on the eastern edge of town.

How to Get Around in Lewistown

The best way to get around Lewistown is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car from the local car rental agencies or use a bike rental service.

Where to Go Next from Lewistown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • State College: State College is a vibrant college town located only 30 miles from Lewistown. It offers an array of dining, shopping, and entertainment options like Penn State University and Mount Nittany.

  • Raystown Lake: Raystown Lake is a serene and picturesque destination that offers water sports, boating, fishing, and much more.

  • Gettysburg: Gettysburg is a town that’s famous for its historical sites, such as the Gettysburg National Military Park, Museum, and Visitor Center.

Conclusion

Lewistown is a hidden gem in Pennsylvania, rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip and explore the best that Lewistown has to offer. From exploring natural wonders to indulging in the local cuisine, there’s something in Lewistown for everyone. So, pack your bags and head off to Lewistown for a memorable journey.
