Introduction to Mifflintown

Located in the Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley regions of Pennsylvania, Mifflintown is a small town with a population of just over 1,000 people. Despite its size, Mifflintown has a rich history and is surrounded by beautiful rolling hills and scenic countryside. It’s a great destination for those looking for a quiet and relaxing getaway in nature.

List of Places to Visit in Mifflintown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Juniata County Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Juniata County and features exhibits on local industries, agriculture, and military history.
  • Port Royal Speedway: For racing enthusiasts, Port Royal Speedway is a must-visit location. This dirt track hosts a variety of races throughout the year.
  • Tuscarora State Park: This park offers plenty of outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and boating. Visitors can also explore the park’s historic Covered Bridge.

Natural Attractions of Mifflintown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rothrock State Forest: With over 96,000 acres of land, Rothrock State Forest is perfect for hiking, camping, and hunting. The forest boasts stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains.
  • Jack’s Mountain: This scenic drive offers breathtaking views of the Juniata River and the surrounding Pennsylvania countryside.
  • Juniata River Water Trail: For kayaking and canoeing enthusiasts, the Juniata River Water Trail is a great adventure. The trail is 118 miles long and offers beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mifflintown

  • Tuscarora Trail: This 252-mile trail is a popular destination for hikers and backpackers. The trail takes visitors through many of Pennsylvania’s beautiful forests and ridges.
  • Mid State Trail: This 327-mile trail is perfect for both novice and experienced hikers. The trail offers stunning views and is well-marked.

Best Things to Do in Mifflintown: Activities and Tours

  • Juniata River Adventures: This tour company offers guided kayaking and canoeing tours on the Juniata River. Tours range from half-day to three-day adventures.
  • Mifflin County Youth Park: This recreational park offers activities such as fishing, hiking, and picnicking. The park features a playground and a skate park for children.
  • Amish Country Tours: For visitors interested in learning about the Amish culture and lifestyle, Amish Country Tours offers guided tours of local Amish communities.

Food and Drink Scene of Mifflintown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shoofly Pie: This traditional Pennsylvania Dutch treat is made with molasses and is a sweet and savory dessert.
  • Scrapple: A local breakfast staple, scrapple is a type of pork mush that is fried and served with eggs and toast.

Local Beverages

  • Penns Creek Pilsner: This refreshing pilsner is brewed locally and is made with local hops and barley.
  • Shade Mountain Winery: This local winery produces a variety of wines, including reds, whites, and fruit wines.

Where to Stay in Mifflintown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Econo Lodge: This budget-friendly hotel is located near many of Mifflintown’s attractions and offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • The Juniata River Valley House: This cozy bed and breakfast is perfect for those looking for a more personal and intimate lodging experience.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: This modern hotel features an indoor pool and fitness center and offers easy access to Mifflintown’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Mifflintown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Hex Signs: These colorful hex signs are a traditional form of Pennsylvania Dutch folk art and make great souvenirs.
  • Local Honey: Made from bees in nearby apiaries, local honey is a sweet and delicious souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mifflintown

Mifflintown is a small town with a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors may notice that many locals in the area speak Pennsylvania Dutch, a dialect of German that has its roots in the area’s early German settlers.

Best Time to Visit Mifflintown

The best time to visit Mifflintown is in the fall when the leaves are changing colors, and the weather is mild. The town’s many apple orchards also come into season during this time, offering visitors the chance to pick their apples and enjoy fresh-made cider.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mifflintown

Do:

  • Take the time to appreciate the area’s natural beauty by hiking or taking a scenic drive.
  • Try the local Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or disrespectfully in public places.
  • Trespass on private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mifflintown

Mifflintown is a small and safe town. Visitors should take the usual precautions when exploring the outdoors, such as being aware of wildlife and carrying plenty of water.

Health in Mifflintown

Visitors should be aware of the area’s high tick population and take the necessary precautions, such as wearing long pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Mifflintown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mifflintown

  • Harrisburg International Airport: Located approximately one hour from Mifflintown, this airport offers many national and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Mifflintown

  • Greyhound Bus: The nearest Greyhound Bus station is located in Harrisburg, approximately one hour from Mifflintown.

Other Options

  • Driving: Mifflintown is easily accessible by car and is located near several major highways.

How to Get Around in Mifflintown

The easiest way to get around Mifflintown is by car. Rental cars are available at Harrisburg International Airport, and there are several car rental agencies located in nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Mifflintown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hershey, PA: Approximately one hour from Mifflintown, Hershey is home to Hersheypark and the Hershey Museum.
  • Gettysburg National Military Park: Located approximately one hour and 30 minutes from Mifflintown, Gettysburg is a popular historical destination.

Conclusion

Mifflintown may be a small town, but it has plenty to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in exploring the outdoors, learning about local history and culture, or simply relaxing and enjoying the scenery, Mifflintown is a great place to visit.
