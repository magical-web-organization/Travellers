Introduction to Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

Nestled in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley in Pennsylvania is a must-visit destination for those who love history, nature, and outdoor activities. The region is placed at an intersection of two beautiful rivers - the Allegheny and the Susquehanna, and home to two mountain ranges - the Appalachian Mountains and the Allegheny Mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

There are many places to explore in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley, ranging from cultural institutions to natural wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs, there are numerous museums and historical sites worth visiting. One of the most popular is the Gettysburg National Military Park, which preserves a significant Civil War battlefield. Visitors can take guided tours, explore the museums, and watch theatrical performances. The Pennsylvania Railroad Museum and the Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania are also worth a visit, especially for train enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts have plenty of options, from hiking and biking to fishing and kayaking. The Hawk Mountain Sanctuary is a popular spot for bird watching, where you can see various birds of prey, including eagles, hawks, and falcons. Raystown Lake is great for swimming, boating, and fishing. You can also take a scenic drive through the Susquehanna River Valley and enjoy the stunning views. Don’t forget to stop at the picturesque covered bridges.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

If you are looking for hiking trails or cycling routes, you won’t be disappointed in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley. Some of the popular trails include the Great Allegheny Passage, the Appalachian Trail, and the Pine Creek Rail Trail.

Best Things to Do in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley: Activities and Tours

Apart from outdoor activities, there are other things to enjoy in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley. You can hop on a hot air balloon and take in the aerial views of the area. Or, visit a local winery or brewery and taste the delicious wines and beers. You can also take a ghost tour in Gettysburg and learn about the area’s haunted history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

Planning a week-long trip to Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley? Here’s a sample itinerary for you:

Day 1: Explore Gettysburg National Military Park and the Gettysburg Museum and Visitor Center

Day 2: Spend the day at Raystown Lake, boating, fishing, and swimming

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic Allegheny Mountains followed by a visit to Hawk Mountain Sanctuary for birdwatching

Day 4: Discover the local wineries and breweries in the area

Day 5: Hike the Pine Creek Rail Trail followed by a stop at one of the picturesque covered bridges

Day 6: Visit the Pennsylvania Railroad Museum and the Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania

Day 7: Take a ghost tour in Gettysburg and learn about the area’s haunted history

Food and Drink Scene of Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

Pennsylvania is also known for its delicious food and drink scene, and Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies - A staple dish of Eastern Europe, you won’t find better pierogies than in Pennsylvania

  • Shoofly Pie - This traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dessert is made with molasses and a crumb topping

  • Scrapple - A breakfast dish made with pork scraps and cornmeal

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer - America’s oldest brewery, and a local favourite

  • Pennsylvania Wine - From sweet to dry, there’s a Pennsylvania wine to suit every taste

Where to Stay in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley offers a variety of lodging options to suit every budget and style. You can choose from luxurious hotels, charming bed and breakfasts, rustic cabins, and budget-friendly hostels. Some of the popular options include the Omni Bedford Springs Resort, Best Western Plus University Park Inn & Suites, and the Fairfield Inn and Suites by Marriott.

What to Buy in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home? You can find unique items like handmade quilts, pottery, and woodwork. You can also buy local honey, jams and jellies, and snacks like pretzels and potato chips. Don’t forget to try the fudge and chocolate!

Local Culture and Customs of Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

Pennsylvania has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, influenced by its Amish, Mennonite, and German roots. Visitors can immerse themselves in the unique customs and traditions of the area by visiting local markets, festivals, and museums. The Gettysburg Heritage Center and the Amish Country Homestead and One-Room School are great places to learn about the area’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

The best time to visit Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley is during the fall when the leaves turn into stunning shades of red, orange, and gold. It’s also a great time for hiking and outdoor activities. Spring and summer are also popular times to visit, especially for fishing and boating.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions

  • Take your trash with you and keep the area clean

  • Be friendly and strike up conversations with the locals

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately in Amish areas

  • Ignore the “No Trespassing” signs

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitat

Safety and Health

Safety in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley is a relatively safe area for tourists. However, it’s always a good idea to take normal safety precautions, like locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley

The quality of healthcare in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley is generally good. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and any necessary medication with you while traveling.

Where to Go Next from Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley is a great base for exploring nearby destinations like Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, and Hershey. You can also visit nearby state parks like Ohiopyle State Park or Ricketts Glen State Park for more outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley is a must-visit destination for those who love history, nature, and outdoor activities. With a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty, there’s something for everyone in this region of Pennsylvania.
