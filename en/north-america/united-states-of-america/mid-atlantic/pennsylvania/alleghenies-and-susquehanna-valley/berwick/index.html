Introduction to Berwick

Berwick is a charming borough situated in eastern Pennsylvania. It was founded in 1786 by Evan Owen, making it one of the oldest settlements in the region. Berwick is known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and vibrant community. With a population of around 10,000 residents, Berwick is a small town with a lot to offer visitors of all ages. The borough boasts an array of natural and cultural attractions, including hiking trails, museums, historical sites, and local festivals. Berwick is also home to an array of local restaurants, cafes, and shops, making it the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or longer vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Berwick

Berwick is packed with attractions that offer something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor beauty, historical landmarks, or cultural experiences, Berwick has a lot to offer. Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Berwick:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Berwick Historical Society

    Berwick Historical Society is a nonprofit organization that aims to preserve and promote the history of Berwick. The society operates a museum in downtown Berwick that showcases local artifacts, photographs, and documents. Visitors can learn about the borough’s history and culture through exhibits and special events.

  • Columbia County Historical and Genealogical Society

    The Columbia County Historical and Genealogical Society is dedicated to promoting and preserving the history of Columbia County. The society operates a museum in Bloomsburg, which is located just a short drive from Berwick. Visitors can explore exhibits on local history, genealogy, and culture.

  • The Berwick Theatre and Center for Community Arts

    The Berwick Theatre and Center for Community Arts is a beautifully restored historic theater that hosts live performances, films, concerts, and other events. The theatre is a great place to catch a show and soak up the local arts scene.

Natural Attractions of Berwick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grey Towers National Historic Site

    The Grey Towers National Historic Site is situated on 102 acres of beautiful forestland and includes historic buildings, hiking trails, and scenic views. Visitors can hike the trails, picnic on the grounds, and soak up the natural beauty of the area.

  • Nescopeck State Park

    Nescopeck State Park is located just a short drive from Berwick and offers hiking trails, fishing, boating, picnicking, and camping. Visitors can explore the park’s forests, streams, and wetlands and enjoy the varied plant and animal life.

  • PPL Montour Preserve

    The PPL Montour Preserve is a 965-acre nature preserve that includes hiking trails, wildlife observation areas, and educational exhibits. Visitors can explore the area’s forests, wetlands, and fields and learn about the local ecology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berwick

Berwick is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • River Common Trail

    The River Common Trail is a 2.3-mile paved trail that runs along the Susquehanna River. It offers scenic views of the river and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk.

  • Iroquois Trail

    The Iroquois Trail is a 2.5-mile hiking trail that runs through the woods near Berwick. It offers a scenic route through the forest and is perfect for a day hike.

  • Roaring Creek Trail

    The Roaring Creek Trail is a 10-mile trail that runs through the Roaring Creek Tract of the Weiser State Forest. It offers a scenic route through the forest and is perfect for a long day hike or mountain bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Berwick: Activities and Tours

Berwick offers a host of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained during their stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Berwick:

  • Susquehanna River Adventures

    Susquehanna River Adventures offers kayak and canoe rentals, guided river tours, and other water activities on the Susquehanna River. Visitors can explore the river and soak up the natural beauty of the area.

  • Moon Lake Park

    Moon Lake Park is a beautiful park that offers hiking trails, fishing, and picnic areas. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the area and take advantage of the park’s amenities.

  • Berwick Brewing Company

    The Berwick Brewing Company is a local brewery that offers brewery tours, tastings, and events. Visitors can learn about the beer-making process and sample some of the local brews.
