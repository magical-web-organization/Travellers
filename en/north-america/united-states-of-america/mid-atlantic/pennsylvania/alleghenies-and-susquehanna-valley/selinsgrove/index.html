Introduction to Selinsgrove

Selinsgrove is a charming borough located in the Susquehanna Valley of Pennsylvania, United States of America. Being a part of the Alleghenies region, it provides many exciting opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the area, from lush green forests to stunning mountain views. Selinsgrove’s rich history, engaging culture, and friendly locals make it an excellent destination for tourists looking for a unique experience.

List of Places to Visit in Selinsgrove

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Conrad Weiser Homestead

The Conrad Weiser Homestead is an essential landmark that provides visitors with an insight into the life of Conrad Weiser, a German immigrant who played a crucial role in colonial history. The Homestead has a museum and guided tours to help you discover the Weiser family heritage and their contribution to the settlement of Pennsylvania.

Northumberland County Historical Society

The Northumberland County Historical Society operates the historic Priestley House museum in Northumberland, a township within Selinsgrove. Public tours of the Priestley house are available, which provides insights into the history of Joseph Priestley, an important figure in American history.

Susquehanna River Valley Visitors Center

The Susquehanna River Valley Visitors Center offers a wealth of information about the town of Selinsgrove and other attractions in the Susquehanna Valley. It is the perfect place to start your journey, providing with you maps, pamphlets and advice on what to explore.

Natural Attractions of Selinsgrove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shikellamy State Park

Shikellamy State Park is home to the stunning Susquehanna River, providing excellent opportunities for boating, fishing and kayaking. The park also offers hiking trails and views of the surrounding mountains, which makes it a must-visit destination for nature lovers in Selinsgrove.

Snyder-Middleswarth Natural Area

The Snyder-Middleswarth Natural Area provides visitors with over 800 acres of lush green forest, streams, and meadows to explore. Hiking in this gorgeous landscape is a stunning experience; you can take a dip in the swimming hole or bird watch for hours without getting tired.

Susquehanna Valley Wildlife Preserve

The Susquehanna Valley Wildlife Preserve provides visitors with a unique experience to see native and exotic animals up close. It is a charming spot to enjoy nature and admire rare creatures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Selinsgrove

Isle of Que Trail

The Isle of Que Trail is a three-mile hiking train that runs alongside the Susquehanna River. Along the trail, visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the river and several historic landmarks.

Sunrise Trail

The Sunrise Trail is a 1-mile loop trail that starts from the Shikellamy State Park parking lot, providing visitors with beautiful views of the valley and the surrounding mountain range. The trail is perfect for hiking, jogging, and bike riding.

Buffalo Valley Rail Trail

The Buffalo Valley Rail Trail spans for 9 miles from Lewisburg to Mifflinburg. It takes you through the scenic countryside of the Susquehanna Valley, with plenty of picturesque views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Selinsgrove: Activities and Tours

Selinsgrove Farmer’s Market

The Selinsgrove Farmer’s Market provides visitors with organic and fresh produce grown by local farmers. It is a great place to find fresh ingredients for your home-cooked meals.

Susquehanna Valley Mall

The Susquehanna Valley Mall is the largest shopping center in the Susquehanna Valley. It is the perfect destination to shop for local souvenirs and goods.

Selinsgrove Speedway

Selinsgrove Speedway is a 0.5-mile dirt track that hosts various racing events throughout the year. It is a thrilling activity to watch high-speed cars and racing trucks zoom across the track.

Food and Drink Scene of Selinsgrove

Must-Try Dishes

Berndt- Stief

Berndt- Stief is a traditional German dish that consists of beef, bacon, potatoes, and onions, seasoned with your favorite spices. The dish is served with a side of sauerkraut and is a must-try in Selinsgrove.

Whoopie Pies

Whoopie pies are a famous Pennsylvania Dutch dessert. They are soft, chocolate cakes that are filled with vanilla cream.

Local Beverages

Selinsgrove Brewing Company

The Selinsgrove Brewing Company provides visitors with some of the best craft beers in the Susquehanna Valley region. If you are a beer lover, this is the spot to sample unique flavors and local brews.

Shade Mountain Vineyards

The Shade Mountain Vineyards is a charming winery that offers wine tasting, vineyard tours, and live music. The vineyard is located in nearby Middleburg and is perfect for a romantic or relaxing day out.

Where to Stay in Selinsgrove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Selinsgrove Inn

The Selinsgrove Inn is a cozy bed and breakfast located in downtown Selinsgrove. It is an excellent spot to stay when exploring everything this town has to offer.

Hampton inn & Suites Selinsgrove Uni Area

Hampton Inn & Suites Selinsgrove Uni Area is a great place to stay if you want to be close to the university area and downtown Selinsgrove. It provides comfortable and updated rooms and excellent staff service.

What to Buy in Selinsgrove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Susquehanna University Merchandise

Susquehanna University is located in downtown Selinsgrove, and they sell unique merchandise to represent your visit to the town.

Wolfs Jewelry & Gift Store

The Wolf’s Jewelry & Gift Store is a lovely shop that sells unique jewelry pieces made of diamonds, gold, and other precious stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Selinsgrove

The primary culture of Selinsgrove is deeply rooted in Pennsylvania Dutch and German traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming and take great pride in their heritage and town.

Best Time to Visit Selinsgrove

The fall season is the perfect time to visit Selinsgrove, with the leaves changing colors and the weather cooling down. The winter season is also a beautiful time to visit for those who enjoy cold weather activities such as ice skating.

Do’s and Don’ts of Selinsgrove

Do:

  • Respect locals and their traditions.
  • Leave no trace while hiking or camping in natural areas.
  • Visit local parks and historic landmarks.
  • Try local cuisine and brews.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any natural areas.
  • Disrespect local religious values.
  • Ignore the local dining establishments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Selinsgrove

Selinsgrove is a safe town to visit with very low crime rates. However, tourists should still take precautions such as locking their cars and avoiding any dangerous or sketchy areas.

Health in Selinsgrove

The nearest hospital in Selinsgrove is the Geisinger Medical Center, which provides top-notch medical facilities and patient care.

Getting to Selinsgrove: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Selinsgrove

The Harrisburg International Airport is the closest airport to Selinsgrove and is located about 50 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Selinsgrove

Greyhound lines provide bus service to Selinsgrove at various points throughout the day.

Other Options

If you’re within driving distance of Selinsgrove, you can rent a car or take your vehicle, as there is ample parking available in town.

How to Get Around in Selinsgrove

You can drive around Selinsgrove with ease, and many attractions listed in this guide are a short drive away from downtown, so renting a car is also a great option.

Where to Go Next from Selinsgrove: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hershey

Just an hour and a half drive east from Selinsgrove, Hershey is known for its chocolate factories and amusement parks.

Lancaster

Lancaster is a beautiful town with many historic attractions that offers a look into Pennsylvania’s rich history.

Conclusion

Selinsgrove is one of Pennsylvania’s hidden gems, with a rich and proud heritage that is reflected in its culture and architecture throughout the town. From outdoor adventures to historic landmarks and local cuisine, there is plenty to do in Selinsgrove, making it a perfect destination for travelers seeking a unique and enjoyable experience.
