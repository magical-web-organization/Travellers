Introduction to Johnstown

Located in the Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley area of Pennsylvania, Johnstown is a city known for its vibrant history, stunning natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage. Often defined as the “Flood City,” Johnstown is still recovering from the devastating flood of 1889, a disaster that changed the course of its history forever.

Today, Johnstown is a welcoming and thriving community that offers visitors a range of exciting things to see and do. From exploring its museums and historical sites to enjoying outdoor activities in the surrounding mountains, there is no shortage of attractions in Johnstown.

Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast or just someone looking for a unique travel destination, Johnstown has something for everyone. So, pack your bags, hit the road and get ready to explore this charming city.

List of Places to Visit in Johnstown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Johnstown Flood Museum

The Johnstown Flood Museum is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the city. It offers visitors an immersive and educational experience about the devastating flood of 1889 that destroyed Johnstown. The museum has interactive exhibits, photographs, and artifacts that showcase the events leading up to the destruction and aftermath of the disaster.

2. Heritage Discovery Center

The Heritage Discovery Center is one of the finest museums in Johnstown. It is dedicated to preserving the rich history and diverse cultural heritage of the region. The museum has several exhibits, including the Iron & Steel Gallery, the Ethnic Heritage Museum, and the Johnstown Children’s Museum.

3. Point Stadium

If you are a baseball lover, then Point Stadium is a must-visit. This historic stadium was once the home of the Johnstown Johnnies, a minor league baseball team. Today, the stadium is used for local events and concerts, but visitors can still catch a ballgame during the summer months.

4. Grandview Cemetery

Grandview Cemetery is a peaceful and serene final resting place for more than 45,000 people, some of whom were victims of the famous Johnstown flood. Visitors can stroll the cemetery grounds and marvel at the beautiful monuments, mausoleums, and other historic structures.

Natural Attractions of Johnstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. The Johnstown Inclined Plane

The Johnstown Inclined Plane is a must-visit attraction for anyone visiting Johnstown. This engineering marvel is the world’s steepest vehicular inclined plane and offers breathtaking views of the city and surrounding mountains.

2. The Path of the Flood Trail

The Path of the Flood Trail is a 9-mile trail that follows the path of the 1889 flood that devastated Johnstown. This scenic trail offers walkers, hikers and cyclists a chance to get up close and personal with the natural beauty of the area. The trail includes several historical markers that detail the events of the flood, making it both an outdoor adventure and a history lesson.

3. Laurel Ridge State Park

Located just outside of Johnstown, Laurel Ridge State Park is a beautiful and serene destination for nature lovers. The park boasts scenic vistas, trails for hiking, and several species of flora and fauna, making it an ideal spot for a family-friendly picnic.

4. Yoder’s Guest House

For a taste of the simple life, visit Yoder’s Guest House. This rustic guesthouse is located on a working farm and offers visitors the chance to experience the slower pace of life in rural Pennsylvania. Visitors can enjoy fresh air, tranquil surroundings, and delicious homemade meals prepared by the Yoder family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Johnstown

Johnstown is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city is surrounded by miles of beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer

a chance to explore the stunning natural scenery of the surrounding Allegheny Mountains. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • The Ghost Town Trail
  • The Johnstown Flood Memorial Trail
  • The Laurel Highlands Hiking Trail

These trails provide a range of difficulty levels for hikers and bikers of all skill levels, making it an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation. Be sure to pack some snacks, water, and sunscreen - and don’t forget your camera!

Best Things to Do in Johnstown: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Johnstown will never be bored. The city offers a range of activities and tours that are perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers alike. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  • Johnstown Discovery Network Passport: The Johnstown Discovery Network Passport allows visitors to pick and choose their itinerary, offering access to eight different attractions, including the Johnstown Flood Museum and the Heritage Discovery Center.

  • The Wagner-Ritter House & Garden Tour: This tour is a chance to step back in time and see what life was like for the affluent residents of Johnstown in the early 1900s.

  • Whitewater Rafting: The Stonycreek River in Johnstown is a popular destination for whitewater rafting enthusiasts. Whether you are an experienced rafter or a beginner, there are plenty of options for an exhilarating adventure on the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Johnstown

If you are planning a week-long trip to Johnstown, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Johnstown Flood Museum and the Heritage Discovery Center.

Day 2: Take a hike along the Johnstown Flood Memorial Trail or the Laurel Highlands Hiking Trail.

Day 3: Explore the Path of the Flood Trail and visit the Grandview Cemetery.

Day 4: Visit the Wagner-Ritter House and Garden and take a tour of the surrounding neighborhood.

Day 5: Whitewater rafting on the Stonycreek River.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Altoona and visit the Horseshoe Curve National Historic Site.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at Yoder’s Guest House.

Food and Drink Scene of Johnstown

Must-Try Dishes

Johnstown has a diverse culinary scene that reflects the city’s history and cultural heritage. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Pierogies: Known as a staple of the city’s Eastern European community, pierogies are one of the most popular dishes in Johnstown.

  • Kielbasa: This delicious sausage is a traditional Polish dish that is a must-try when visiting Johnstown.

  • Pepperoni Rolls: This Italian-American dish is a popular snack food in the region.

Local Beverages

Johnstown is also known for its local beverages, including:

  • Iron Horse Brewery: This local brewery offers a variety of beers that reflect the city’s industrial heritage.

  • Pennsylvania Pure Distilleries: This local distillery produces high-quality vodka, whiskey, and other spirits, sourced from local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Johnstown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Johnstown has a range of accommodation options for visitors. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Hampton Inn Johnstown: A modern and comfortable hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • Sleep Inn Johnstown: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

  • Yoder’s Guest House: For a more authentic and unique experience, stay at this rustic guest house located on a working farm.

What to Buy in Johnstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Johnstown, be sure to pick up some souvenirs and local products to take home. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Johnstown Flood Museum merchandise: The museum offers a range of merchandise, including t-shirts, hats, and other memorabilia.

  • Artifacts Gallery: This gallery specializes in handcrafted items made by local artists, including jewelry, pottery, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Johnstown

Johnstown has a proud cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and people. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting religious sites or participating in community events.

Best Time to Visit Johnstown

The best time to visit Johnstown is between April and October when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the city is also a popular destination during the winter months when visitors can enjoy winter sports and activities in the surrounding mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Johnstown

Do:

  • Explore the city’s historic sites and museums.

  • Enjoy outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and whitewater rafting.

  • Try local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions when visiting religious sites or participating in community events.

  • Damage any of the historic buildings or artifacts when exploring the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Johnstown

Johnstown is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should still exercise caution when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, keep valuables out of sight, and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Johnstown

Visitors to Johnstown should take the necessary precautions to protect themselves from the sun, insects, and other outdoor hazards. Be sure to wear sunscreen, hats, and insect repellent when spending time outside.

Getting to Johnstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Johnstown

  • John Murtha Johnstown-Cambria County Airport: Located just outside the city center, this airport offers domestic flights.

  • Pittsburgh International Airport: This is the nearest major airport, located approximately 90 miles from Johnstown.

Bus Stations Near Johnstown

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in the heart of the city, this station offers daily bus service to major destinations throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train: The Amtrak train station is located in nearby Greensburg, approximately 40 miles from Johnstown.

How to Get Around in Johnstown

The easiest and most convenient way to get around Johnstown is by car. However, there are also several taxi companies, rideshare services, and bus routes available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Johnstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Johnstown is located in the heart of the Allegheny Mountains, which offers visitors a range of exciting nearby destinations and excursions. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Blue Knob State Park: Located just 30 minutes from Johnstown, this park offers hiking trails, fishing, camping, and other outdoor activities.

  • Altoona: This nearby city is home to several historic sites, including the Horseshoe Curve National Historic Site and the Railroaders Memorial Museum.

Conclusion

Johnstown is a one-of-a-kind destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its range of attractions, activities, and cultural experiences, it is an ideal destination for anyone looking for an authentic and rewarding travel experience. So, what are you waiting for?
