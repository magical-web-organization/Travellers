Introduction to Shamokin Dam

Shamokin Dam is a scenic borough located in the Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley region of Pennsylvania. Known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and historic landmarks, this charming destination has something to offer for every type of traveler.

If you’re planning a trip to Shamokin Dam, you’re in for a treat. This travel guide is designed to help you explore the best of what this region has to offer. Whether you’re looking for cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or a relaxing getaway, you’ll find everything you need to know in this comprehensive guide.

List of Places to Visit in Shamokin Dam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shamokin Dam is home to several museums and historical landmarks that are worth exploring. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

  • Fort Augusta - a historic military fort that played a vital role in protecting the frontier during the French and Indian War.
  • Shikellamy State Park - offers a great opportunity to learn about Native American culture and history. It’s home to a monument dedicated to Chief Shikellamy, a prominant Susquehanna chief.
  • Joseph Priestley House - the home of Joseph Priestley, a pioneering chemist and inventor, and a National Historic Landmark.

Natural Attractions of Shamokin Dam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shamokin Dam is a nature-lover’s paradise, with plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Susquehanna River - perfect for fishing, boating, and kayaking enthusiasts.
  • Susquehanna Valley Country Club - a scenic golf course that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Little Buffalo State Park - perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring the great outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shamokin Dam

Shamokin Dam has a variety of hiking and biking routes that offer amazing views of the region’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Buffalo Ridge Trail - a 5-mile hike through Little Buffalo State Park that takes you over rolling hills and through tranquil forests.
  • Susquehanna Greenway Trail - a 500-mile trail that follows the Susquehanna River and offers beautiful views of the water and surrounding landscapes.
  • Susquehanna Warrior Trail - a 12-mile route that follows an old railroad bed and includes scenic views of the river and local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Shamokin Dam: Activities and Tours

Shamokin Dam offers a variety of activities and tours that are perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Hershey’s Chocolate World - a short drive from Shamokin Dam, this popular attraction offers a fun chocolate-making experience for all ages.
  • Penn’s Cave and Wildlife Park - a unique adventure that allows you to explore an underground cave and see a variety of wildlife up close.
  • Amish Country - located nearby, this area offers a glimpse into Amish culture and craftsmanship.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shamokin Dam

If you’re planning on spending a week in Shamokin Dam, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore local museums and landmarks, like Fort Augusta and Joseph Priestley House.
  • Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride through Little Buffalo State Park or one of the other trails in the area.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a round of golf at the Susquehanna Valley Country Club.
  • Day 4: Visit Hershey’s Chocolate World or Penn’s Cave and Wildlife Park for a fun day trip.
  • Day 5: Take a tour of Amish Country and learn about the region’s rich history and culture.
  • Day 6-7: Relax and enjoy some of Shamokin Dam’s local restaurants and shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Shamokin Dam

Shamokin Dam has a variety of options when it comes to food and drink. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Pot Pie - a hearty dish made with chicken, vegetables, and handmade noodles.
  • Shoofly Pie - a traditional Amish dessert made with molasses and crumbly streusel topping.
  • Scrapple - a Pennsylvania favorite made with pork scraps and cornmeal, often served with eggs for breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Lager - brewed in nearby Pottsville, this is America’s oldest brewery and a Pennsylvania favorite.
  • Sly Fox Beer - brewed in nearby Phoenixville, this small brewery offers a variety of interesting and unique beers.
  • Pennsylvania Dutch Birch Beer - a non-alcoholic, root beer-like drink that’s a local favorite.

Where to Stay in Shamokin Dam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shamokin Dam offers a variety of options when it comes to accommodations. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Selinsgrove Inn - a charming bed and breakfast with cozy rooms and a great location.
  • Hampton Inn Selinsgrove/Shamokin Dam - a modern hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Staybridge Suites Selinsgrove - great for longer stays and families, this hotel offers spacious suites with fully-equipped kitchens.

What to Buy in Shamokin Dam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or unique local products to bring home, Shamokin Dam has plenty of options. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • Country Cupboard - this large gift shop offers a wide selection of local crafts, food items, and souvenirs.
  • Covered Bridge and Arts Festival - held every October, this festival is the perfect place to find unique handmade crafts, art, and food.
  • Penn’s Creek Pottery - a local pottery shop that offers beautiful, handcrafted ceramics and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Shamokin Dam

Getting to Shamokin Dam: Transportation Options and Logistics

The closest airport to Shamokin Dam is Harrisburg International Airport, located about 45 minutes away by car. Other airports in the area include Philadelphia International Airport and Baltimore/Washington International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Shamokin Dam

There are several bus stations and terminals near Shamokin Dam, including the Susquehanna Trailways bus terminal in nearby Selinsgrove.

Other Options
