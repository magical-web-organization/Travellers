Introduction to Bedford

Located in the heart of Pennsylvania’s Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley regions, Bedford is a charming town that offers the perfect mix of history, culture, and outdoor activities. Known for its beautiful landscapes, historic sites, and warm hospitality, Bedford has become a favorite destination among travelers seeking a peaceful escape from their busy lives.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore some of the best things to do in Bedford, including must-visit attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking trails, local food and drink scene, and much more. So, let’s start planning your next adventure to Bedford!

List of Places to Visit in Bedford

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Bedford has something for everyone. Here’s a list of top places to visit in Bedford:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bedford County Historical Society: Housed in a beautifully restored building, the Bedford County Historical Society offers a glimpse into the town’s past, with exhibits featuring local artifacts and photographs.

  • Fort Bedford Museum: Built in 1758, Fort Bedford served as a military outpost during the French and Indian War. Today, the fort has been restored and turned into a museum, where visitors can learn about the region’s military history.

  • Old Bedford Village: This living history museum showcases life in the 18th and 19th centuries, with over 40 buildings representing a small village of that era.

Natural Attractions of Bedford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shawnee State Park: With over 3,900 acres of lush forests, meadows, and lake, Shawnee State Park is a natural paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can hike, bike, swim, or fish in the park’s serene surroundings.

  • The Espy House: Nestled in the heart of Bedford, The Espy House is a beautiful historic home that offers stunning views of the town’s landscape. It was home to Samuel Espy, a prominent historic figure in the town’s history.

  • Bedford Springs Resort: A historical landmark dating back to 1806, Bedford Springs Resort is a luxurious hotel that offers breathtaking views of the Alleghenies. The resort is surrounded by natural springs, hiking trails, and an award-winning golf course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bedford

For those who love to hike or bike, Bedford is a paradise. Boasting over thirty miles of trails in nearby state parks, state forests, and natural reserves, there is no shortage of outdoor activities in the area. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Bedford include:

  • The Bedford Heritage Trail: This picturesque trail winds through the town’s historic district, passing by some of the town’s most iconic landmarks, including the Bedford Springs Hotel and the Fort Bedford Museum.

  • Jacks Mountain Trail: A scenic hiking trail that takes visitors through the beautiful Jacks Mountain, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Loy’s Loop Trail: A popular mountain biking trail, Loy’s Loop Trail offers a fun and challenging ride through rocky terrain, woods, and streams.

Best Things to Do in Bedford: Activities and Tours

In Bedford, there is an endless array of activities and tours available to visitors. Whether you’re looking to learn about the town’s history, explore the outdoors, or enjoy some local food and drink, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Bedford:

  • Visit the local wineries: Bedford is home to several well-known wineries, including the Briar Valley Vineyard and Winery, which offers wine tastings, tours, and beautiful scenery.

  • Explore the art galleries: Bedford is known for its vibrant arts scene, with several art galleries showcasing the works of local artists, including Bedford Fine Art Gallery and the ArtWorks Gallery.

  • Take a ghost tour: Bedford is rich in history and folklore, offering visitors the opportunity to explore the town’s haunted past with guided ghost tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bedford

If you’re planning on spending a week in Bedford, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the area:

Day 1: Arrive in Bedford and explore the town’s historic district.

Day 2: Visit the Fort Bedford Museum and explore the nearby Shawnee State Park.

Day 3: Take a guided ghost tour of the town’s haunted spots and explore the local art galleries.

Day 4: Go hiking on the Jacks Mountain Trail and enjoy a wine tasting at the local Briar Valley Vineyard and Winery.

Day 5: Spend a day at Bedford Springs Resort, relaxing in natural springs and enjoying scenic views.

Day 6: Go for a bike ride on Loy’s Loop Trail and explore the natural beauty of the area.

Day 7: Visit the Bedford County Historical Society and learn about the town’s rich history before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Bedford

Bedford offers a variety of delicious local food and drink options. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pot Pie: A traditional dish in Pennsylvania, the pot pie is made with slow-cooked shredded chicken and creamy stew, served over homemade noodles or mashed potatoes.

  • Shoofly Pie: A classic Pennsylvania Dutch dessert, Shoofly Pie is a molasses-based cake-like pie. Sweet, sticky, and delicious, it’s the perfect treat to satisfy your sweet tooth.

  • Scrapple: A popular breakfast dish in the area, scrapple is a homestead dish made from pork leftovers, cornmeal, and spices, served fried.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: A local favorite, Yuengling Beer is America’s oldest brewery, dating back to 1829. Made with traditional brewing methods, it’s a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Eggnog: A seasonal beverage, Pennsylvania Dutch Eggnog is made with fresh, locally sourced dairy products and flavored with a blend of spices.

Where to Stay in Bedford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a cozy bed and breakfast, Bedford offers accommodation options to suit every taste and budget. Here are some top picks:

  • Bedford Springs Resort: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Bedford, offering stunning scenic views and natural springs to relax in.

  • Omni Bedford Springs Resort: A historic hotel located next to Bedford Springs Resort, featuring modern amenities and a revitalizing spa.

  • Juniata Hotel and Suites: A charming hotel located downtown, offering comfortable and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Bedford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or a local product to bring home? Bedford has several options. Here are some popular choices:

  • Bedford Cheese Shop: A local shop that offers an array of artisan cheeses, jams, and other delicious products.

  • Bedford Candies: A local chocolatier that creates handmade truffles, fudge, and chocolate-covered pretzels.

  • Bedford Embroidery: A local embroidery shop that creates personalized gifts, including towels, t-shirts, and hats.

Local Culture and Customs of Bedford

Bedford is known for its warm hospitality, love of outdoor activities, and strong sense of community. Locals take pride in their town’s rich history and cultural heritage and are always eager to share it with visitors. While in Bedford, be sure to embrace the local customs, including supporting local businesses and respecting the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Bedford

The best time to visit Bedford is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its best. Visitors can enjoy hiking, exploring the town’s historic district, and sampling delicious food and drink offerings of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bedford

Bedford is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors are encouraged to take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, including being aware of their surroundings, locking their cars, and securing their belongings.

Health in Bedford

There are several medical facilities in Bedford available to visitors in case of emergencies. Visitors are encouraged to bring their medications and contact their healthcare provider before traveling for any recommendations or precautions.

Getting to Bedford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bedford is easily accessible by car, bus, and air.

Airports Near Bedford

The nearest airport to Bedford is the John Murtha Johnstown-Cambria County Airport, located approximately 40 miles away. The Pittsburgh International Airport is also a popular airport choice, located approximately 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Bedford

Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Megabus, operate in the area, making it easy for visitors to travel to and from Bedford.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Bedford, as it is located near major highways, including the Pennsylvania Turnpike and I-99.

How to Get Around in Bedford

Getting around in Bedford is relatively easy, with most attractions located within walking distance of each other. Taxis and rideshare services are also available for visitors who wish to travel further afield.

Where to Go Next from Bedford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bedford is located in an area that offers several nearby destinations and excursions. Some popular options include:

  • Harrisburg: Located approximately two hours away, Harrisburg is the capital city of Pennsylvania and offers several cultural attractions.

  • Cumberland Gap National Historical Park: Located approximately three hours away, the Cumberland Gap National Historical Park offers outdoor adventures and a glimpse into the area’s history.

Conclusion

Bedford, Pennsylvania, is a charming town that offers something for everyone. With its beautiful natural scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture, Bedford has become a must-visit destination for travelers.
