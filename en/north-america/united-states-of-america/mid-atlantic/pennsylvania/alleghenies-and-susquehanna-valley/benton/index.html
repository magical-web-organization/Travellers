Introduction to Benton

Located in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley, Benton is a small borough in Columbia County, Pennsylvania. With its scenic beauty and rich culture, Benton is a hidden gem that attracts tourists who are seeking an authentic and off-the-beaten-path travel experience. From historic museums and natural attractions to hiking/biking routes and local cuisine, Benton has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Benton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Benton Heritage Museum: The Benton Heritage Museum features exhibits and artifacts that reflect the history and heritage of Benton. Visitors can explore various themes, including the industrial revolution, the Civil War, and local agriculture.

  • The Bowman’s Mill Covered Bridge: This historic bridge, built in 1882, is a popular attraction among tourists and locals alike. Visitors can take a short walk on the nearby trail and enjoy the scenic views.

  • The Cooper Cemetery: The Cooper Cemetery is a historic graveyard that dates back to the early 1800s. It is the resting place of many pioneer settlers and veterans of the Revolutionary War and the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Benton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ricketts Glen State Park: Ricketts Glen State Park is known for its stunning waterfalls and scenic trails. Visitors can hike the Falls Trail System, which features 21 waterfalls and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

  • Roaring Creek Tract: Roaring Creek Tract is a natural area that spans over 77,000 acres and is home to various wildlife species, including black bears, elk, and deer. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and camping in this beautiful area.

  • Hartman Jericho Trail: The Hartman Jericho Trail offers scenic views of the Benton area and is a popular destination for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Benton

Benton offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Pennsylvania. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Falls Trail System: This 7.2-mile trail features 21 waterfalls and is located in Ricketts Glen State Park.

  • Hartman Jericho Trail: This 19-mile trail passes through the beautiful natural scenery of the Benton area and is great for hiking and mountain biking.

  • Cherry Run Trail: This 2.5-mile trail is located in the Roaring Creek Tract and is known for its steep climbs and scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Benton: Activities and Tours

  • Visit local farms: Benton is known for its rich farming community, and visitors can explore various local farms and learn about the agricultural practices and products. Some of the popular farms include the Rohrbach’s Farm Market and the Bason’s Creek Farms.

  • Take a brewery tour: Visitors can take a tour of the Berwick Brewing Company and learn about the process of brewing beer.

  • Attend local festivals: Benton hosts various festivals throughout the year, including the Benton Frontier Days, which celebrates the history and culture of Benton.

Food and Drink Scene of Benton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: Pierogies are a popular local dish that consists of a savory filling (such as potato and cheese) wrapped in dough and boiled.

  • Haluski: Haluski is a traditional dish made with egg noodles and cabbage.

  • Shoo-fly pie: Shoo-fly pie is a molasses-based pie that is a favorite among locals.

Local Beverages

  • Berwick Brewing Company: The Berwick Brewing Company is a local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers.

  • Red Shale Ridge Vineyards: Red Shale Ridge Vineyards is a local winery that produces a range of award-winning wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Benton

Benton has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its festivals, museums, and local cuisine. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and interact with the friendly and welcoming locals.

Best Time to Visit Benton

The best time to visit Benton is from June to August when the weather is warm and pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season (September to November) is also a great time to visit Benton when the foliage is at its peak, and the local festivals are in full swing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Benton

Benton is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors are advised to take the necessary precautions to avoid any untoward incidents. Always keep your valuables secure and lock the doors and windows of your accommodation.

Health in Benton

Visitors to Benton should be aware of the potential health risks associated with ticks, which are common in the area. It is advisable to wear long pants and sleeves and use insect repellent when hiking or camping in wooded areas.

Getting to Benton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Benton is easily accessible by various modes of transport.

Airports Near Benton

  • Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport: Located 40 miles from Benton, Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport is a major airport serving the region.

  • Harrisburg International Airport: Located 80 miles from Benton, Harrisburg International Airport offers flights from many major cities.

Bus Stations Near Benton

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The Greyhound Bus Station is located in nearby Bloomsburg.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from the nearby cities of Wilkes-Barre or Harrisburg.

  • Taxi services: Several taxi services operate in the Benton area.

How to Get Around in Benton

Benton is best explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent a bike from the local bike shops and explore the area’s scenic routes and trails.

Where to Go Next from Benton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Knoebels Amusement Park: Located 30 miles from Benton, Knoebels Amusement Park is a family-friendly destination that offers a range of rides and attractions.

  • Lewisburg: Located 25 miles from Benton, Lewisburg is a charming town that offers a range of shopping, dining, and cultural activities.

  • Penn’s Cave and Wildlife Park: Located 50 miles from Benton, Penn’s Cave and Wildlife Park is a unique destination that offers cave tours and wildlife exhibits.

Conclusion

Benton is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, culture, and history. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a foodie, Benton has something to offer you. Plan your trip now and discover the charm and beauty of this beautiful town in Pennsylvania.
