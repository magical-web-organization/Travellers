Introduction to Bloomsburg

Bloomsburg is a charming town located in Alleghenies and Susquehanna Valley, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. This beautifully preserved historic town is home to a welcoming community of friendly locals, picturesque streets, and a thriving arts scene. With its diverse range of attractions, from natural wonders to cultural landmarks, Bloomsburg offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bloomsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Bloomsburg Children’s Museum: Located on the historic west end of Bloomsburg, the museum is a not-for-profit institution that offers interactive exhibits and hands-on learning experiences for children of all ages.

The Bloomsburg Fairgrounds: The fairgrounds are the site of the largest agricultural fair in Pennsylvania, attracting over 500,000 people each year. Visitors can enjoy a variety of events, such as live music, food, and activities.

The Columbia County Historical Society: The society is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Columbia County and hosts exhibits and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Bloomsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Bloomsburg Rail Trail: This 3.5-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and jogging. It offers scenic views of the Susquehanna River and passes through several parks and natural areas.

Ricketts Glen State Park: Located just a short drive from Bloomsburg, this park is known for its stunning waterfalls and hiking trails.

Knoebels Amusement Park and Resort: This famous amusement park is located just 15 minutes from Bloomsburg and features a range of rides and activities for the whole family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bloomsburg

The Bloomsburg Rail Trail: as mentioned before, this trail offers beautiful views of the Susquehanna River and is suitable for beginner and experienced hikers.

The Welch Trail: This trail is ideal for experienced hikers and bikers. It passes through the Montour Preserve and offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Bloomsburg: Activities and Tours

The Bloomsburg Theatre Ensemble: This professional theatre company, founded in 1978, provides a range of performances, including modern and classic plays.

Kayaking on the Susquehanna River: the river provides excellent opportunities for recreational kayaking, fishing, and other water activities.

Annual Celebrations and Festivals: Bloomsburg hosts events, such as the Bloomsburg Fair and the Arts Festival, which showcases the best of local arts and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bloomsburg

Day 1: Visit the Bloomsburg Children’s Museum and explore the downtown shopping district.

Day 2: Discover the Columbia County Historical Society and learn about the region’s fascinating history.

Day 3: Go hiking on the Bloomsburg Rail Trail, stopping off at Rupert Park and the Bloomsburg Town Park.

Day 4: Venture out to Ricketts Glen State Park to hike to some of Pennsylvania’s most beautiful waterfalls.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the town and learn about its diverse history and culture.

Day 6: Enjoy a day at Knoebels Amusement Park and Resort, one of Pennsylvania’s premier amusement parks.

Day 7: Shop at the local farmers’ markets and discover some of Bloomsburg’s locally made crafts and souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Bloomsburg

Must-Try Dishes

Scrapple: a local delicacy consisting of pork scraps and cornmeal, often served as breakfast.

Shoo Fly Pie: A regional specialty, this dessert has a sweet molasses filling and a crumbly crust.

Whoopie Pies: These delicious treats are a cross between a cookie and a cake and are often filled with cream or frosting.

Local Beverages

Yuengling Lager: Known as America’s oldest brewery, Yuengling offers a range of delicious beers.

Pennsylvania Dutch Eggnog: A traditional holiday drink made with eggs, rum, and cream.

Apple Cider: A refreshing drink made with fresh, local apples.

Where to Stay in Bloomsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn at Turkey Hill: A traditional bed and breakfast, offering luxurious rooms and a range of amenities.

Budget Host Patriot Inn: A budget-friendly option, providing comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

Holiday Inn Express Bloomsburg: Offers modern rooms, free breakfast, and a location within minutes of local attractions.

What to Buy in Bloomsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Pottery: There is a range of pottery made by local artists that is both functional and decorative.

Antiques and Vintage Items: There are many stores offering vintage find and antiques, some of them are located on Old Town Road.

Local Crafts and Artwork: You can find paintings, sculptures, and other artwork made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Bloomsburg

Bloomsburg has a rich cultural heritage, dating back to the early days of American history. The town is known for its welcoming community, friendly locals, and diverse range of cultural events and attractions. Visitors can learn about the town’s history and culture, sample local cuisine and drink, and discover unique souvenirs and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Bloomsburg

The best time to visit Bloomsburg is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and most of the outdoor attractions are open. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bloomsburg

Bloomsburg is known for its low crime rate and safe environment. Visitors should exercise general caution, as they would in any other major city.

Health in Bloomsburg

Bloomsburg has a range of medical facilities and hospitals, offering world-class medical care to residents and visitors alike.

Getting to Bloomsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bloomsburg

The closest airport to Bloomsburg is the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport, located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Bloomsburg

Bloomsburg is served by Greyhound buses, which stop at the local bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can reach Bloomsburg by car, train, or rental car, depending on their preferences and budget.

How to Get Around in Bloomsburg

Visitors can explore Bloomsburg by foot, bike or, car. Public transportation options, like taxis, and buses, are also available.

Where to Go Next from Bloomsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Knoebels Amusement Park and Resort

Located just outside of Bloomsburg, Knoebels is one of Pennsylvania’s premier amusement parks, offering thrilling rides and exciting attractions for all ages.

Ricketts Glen State Park

Ricketts Glen State Park is known for its stunning waterfalls and hiking trails, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Harrisburg

Located about an hour and a half’s drive from Bloomsburg, Harrisburg is the capital of Pennsylvania, offering a range of cultural and historical attractions.

Conclusion

Bloomsburg is an idyllic small town located in Pennsylvania’s beautiful countryside, offering a range of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. From the town’s rich history and culture to the stunning natural landscapes and outdoor recreational activities, there is something for everyone to enjoy.
