Introduction to Lebanon

Nestled in the heart of Pennsylvania Dutch Country, Lebanon offers a unique blend of natural beauty and rich heritage. Known for its picturesque farmland, rolling hills, and charming small-town atmosphere, Lebanon is the perfect destination for a relaxing getaway. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there is something for everyone in this charming Pennsylvania town.

List of Places to Visit in Lebanon

If you’re planning a trip to Lebanon, here’s a list of some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lebanon boasts a rich history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore:

  • Cornwall Iron Furnace: This 18th-century iron furnace is a National Historic Landmark and offers a glimpse into Pennsylvania’s industrial past.
  • Historic Schaefferstown: This historic village features several 18th-century buildings, including a tavern, a distillery, and a general store.
  • Lebanon County Historical Society: This museum features exhibits on Lebanon County’s history, including its role in the Civil War and the Underground Railroad.

Natural Attractions of Lebanon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to take in Lebanon’s natural beauty, here are some places to check out:

  • Middle Creek Wildlife Management Area: This wildlife preserve is home to over 200 species of birds and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Mount Gretna Lake & Beach: This lake and beach area offers swimming, kayaking, and other water activities, as well as a hiking trail and picnic area.
  • Swatara State Park: This park features over 3,500 acres of forests, meadows, and wetlands, as well as several miles of hiking trails and a section of the Appalachian Trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lebanon

For outdoor enthusiasts, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails in and around Lebanon:

  • Lebanon Valley Rail Trail: This 14-mile trail follows the former Cornwall-Lebanon Railroad and offers views of the surrounding farmland.
  • Governor Dick Trail System: This network of trails offers over 15 miles of hiking and biking paths through the forests of Mount Gretna.
  • Memorial Lake State Park: This park features several hiking trails, as well as a 3-mile paved loop for biking and rollerblading.

Best Things to Do in Lebanon: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and exploring the outdoors, Lebanon offers a range of other activities and tours:

  • Mount Hope Estate & Winery: This winery offers tours and tastings of its award-winning wines, as well as events like concerts and murder mystery dinners.
  • Hollywood Casino at Penn National Race Course: This casino features over 2,000 slot machines and table games, as well as a horse racing track and several dining options.
  • Stoy Museum: This museum features a collection of over 100 antique automobiles, motorcycles, and other vehicles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lebanon

If you have a week to spend in Lebanon, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore historic downtown Lebanon, including the Lebanon Farmers Market and the Lebanon County Historical Society.
  • Day 2: Visit the Cornwall Iron Furnace and hike part of the Lebanon Valley Rail Trail.
  • Day 3: Take a tour of Mount Hope Estate & Winery and enjoy a picnic lunch at Mount Gretna Lake & Beach.
  • Day 4: Spend the day hiking and exploring the Governor Dick Trail System.
  • Day 5: Visit the Middle Creek Wildlife Management Area and take in the scenic views.
  • Day 6: Try your luck at the Hollywood Casino at Penn National Race Course.
  • Day 7: Discover the antique vehicles at the Stoy Museum and sample some local cuisine at one of Lebanon’s many restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Lebanon

Lebanon offers a range of dining options, from classic Pennsylvania Dutch fare to international cuisine:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lebanon bologna: This local specialty is a type of smoked sweet bologna that is often served with crackers and cheese.
  • Shoofly pie: This traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dessert is made with molasses and a crumbly topping.
  • Chicken pot pie: This classic dish features tender chicken and flavorful broth, topped with a crispy crust.

Local Beverages

  • Lebanon Valley Brewery: This craft brewery offers a range of beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Wertz Candies: This family-owned candy shop is known for its homemade root beer and cream soda.

Where to Stay in Lebanon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From charming bed and breakfasts to modern hotels, Lebanon offers a range of accommodation options:

  • The Inn at Leola Village: This luxury inn features charming rooms and suites, as well as a spa and several dining options.
  • Gretna Hall Hotel: This historic hotel offers cozy rooms and a convenient location in the heart of Mount Gretna.
  • Travel Inn Lebanon Valley: This budget-friendly motel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to area attractions.

What to Buy in Lebanon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lebanon offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Pennsylvania Dutch hex signs: These colorful symbols are a traditional folk art form in Lancaster County.
  • Lebanon bologna: You can bring home this local speciality from many local markets.
  • Wertz Candies: These homemade treats make a great gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Lebanon

Lebanon is a friendly, close-knit community with a strong sense of tradition. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including the Pennsylvania Dutch culture and language.

Best Time to Visit Lebanon

The best time to visit Lebanon depends on your preferences. Spring is a great time to see the area’s blooming flowers and trees, while summer offers plenty of outdoor activities and festivals. Fall is especially beautiful, with the changing leaves and cooler temperatures, and winter can be a great time to see the area’s holiday lights and enjoy cozy indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lebanon

Here are some tips for visitors to Lebanon:

Do:

  • Try local cuisine, including Lebanon bologna and shoofly pie.
  • Take a scenic drive through Pennsylvania Dutch Country.
  • Visit local markets and artisans to find unique souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Speak negatively about local culture or traditions.
  • Ignore safety precautions when hiking or enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Leave valuables unsecured in your car or hotel room.

Safety and Health

Lebanon is a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should take standard precautions when exploring the outdoors or traveling in the area:

Safety in Lebanon

  • Secure your belongings and lock your car and hotel room.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Follow local safety guidelines when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Lebanon

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health concerns during your trip.

Getting to Lebanon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lebanon is located in southeastern Pennsylvania, and is easily accessible by car or bus:

Airports Near Lebanon

  • Harrisburg International Airport (HIA): Located about 30 miles southwest of Lebanon, HIA offers domestic flights and several rental car options.

Bus Stations Near Lebanon

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in nearby Harrisburg, this bus station offers connections to destinations across the country.
  • Lebanon Transit: This local bus system offers several routes throughout Lebanon and surrounding areas.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station is located in Lancaster, about 25 miles east of Lebanon.
  • Rental Cars: Several rental car companies, including Hertz and Enterprise, offer rental cars from Harrisburg International Airport.

How to Get Around in Lebanon

Lebanon is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, visitors may want to rent a car or use a taxi or rideshare service to explore surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Lebanon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Hershey, PA: Known as “The Sweetest Place on Earth,” Hershey offers plenty of chocolate-themed attractions, as well as the popular Hersheypark amusement park.
  • Lancaster, PA: This charming town is known for its rich Pennsylvania Dutch heritage, as well as its Amish culture and delicious cuisine.
  • Gettysburg, PA: History buffs will love this small town, which is home to the historic Gettysburg Battlefield and several museums and historic sites.

Conclusion

Lebanon offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture, making it a must-visit destination in southeastern Pennsylvania. With plenty of outdoor activities, delicious cuisine, and charming accommodations, Lebanon is the perfect destination for a relaxing getaway.
