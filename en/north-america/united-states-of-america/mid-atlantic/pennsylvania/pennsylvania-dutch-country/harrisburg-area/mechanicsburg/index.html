Introduction to Mechanicsburg

Mechanicsburg is a charming town located in the heart of Cumberland County, Pennsylvania. It is a historic town in the Harrisburg area, known for its rich heritage, scenic beauty, and warm hospitality. Mechanicsburg is an ideal destination for people who love history, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. Its small-town feel and friendly community make it an excellent place to relax, recharge, and enjoy quality time with loved ones.

List of Places to Visit in Mechanicsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, Mechanicsburg is a paradise for you. The town boasts a rich heritage, including several museums and historical places that take you back in time.

The Union Church and Burial Ground

The Union Church and Burial Ground is a historic site in the heart of Mechanicsburg dating back to 1817. The church is a beautiful structure featuring a classic Pennsylvania-style design. It is a popular site for weddings and community events. The Burial Ground is the resting place of many of Mechanicsburg’s founding families, Civil War veterans, and other historical figures.

The Williams Grove Historical Steam Engine Association

The Williams Grove Historical Steam Engine Association preserves the unique legacy of steam and gas-powered machinery. Featuring over 10 acres of engines and tractors, the association showcases antique vehicles that have been restored to their original working condition.

The Mechanicsburg Museum Association

The Mechanicsburg Museum Association offers visitors an opportunity to explore the rich heritage of Mechanicsburg by showcasing unique artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that showcase the town’s history from its early days to modern times.

Natural Attractions of Mechanicsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mechanicsburg is renowned for its natural beauty, hiking trails, and outdoor adventure. The town offers several scenic spots for nature lovers, tranquillity seekers, and adventure enthusiasts alike.

The Yellow Breeches Creek

The Yellow Breeches Creek is one of the most popular spots in Mechanicsburg, offering breathtaking views and the opportunity for fishing, kayaking, canoeing, swimming, and tubing. With several access points throughout the town, visitors can enjoy various size sections of the creek.

The Cumberland Valley Rail Trail

The Cumberland Valley Rail Trail is a meandering path alongside the old rail line that offers visitors an enjoyable route for hiking, bicycling, and sightseeing. The 11-mile trail passes through woodlands, farmlands, and over the creek bridges in a pleasant way to enjoy the peaceful scenery.

Kings Gap Environmental Education and Training Center

Kings Gap Environmental Education and Training Center offers visitors access to over 2,600 acres of picturesque vista and an incredible view of Cumberland Valley. The centre provides outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, mountain biking, bird watching, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mechanicsburg

Mechanicsburg has plenty of hiking and biking opportunities for adventurous travelers. Whether you are looking for a relaxing stroll or a challenging trek, Mechanicsburg’s paths offer something for everyone.

The Appalachian Trail

The Appalachian Trail is a famous trail that runs through the east coast of the United States. A section of the trail passes through Cumberland County, offering visitors an opportunity to experience the breathtaking views and exciting trail adventures.

The Cumberland Valley Flooding Plain Trail

The Cumberland Valley Flooding Plain Trail is an easy trail that spans over a 12-mile flat and meandering path. It is ideal for families and casual hikers who want to enjoy the peaceful scenery uninterrupted.

The Wildwood Park Nature Center Trail

The Wildwood Park Nature Center Trail is a well-maintained trail that offers visitors access to over six miles of hiking and walking paths. The trail takes you through scenic forests, wetlands, and meadows, providing plenty of opportunities to view wildlife and plants.

Best Things to Do in Mechanicsburg: Activities and Tours

Mechanicsburg offers plenty of things to see and do, no matter your interests. From shopping and dining to sightseeing and outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in this beautiful town.

Take a Historic Walking Tour

Mechanicsburg is a town rooted in history, and one of the best ways to experience it is by taking a walking tour. Several professionally guided tours offer in-depth insights into Mechanicsburg’s rich heritage, including its fascinating architecture and culture.

Visit Sporting Events

If you’re a sports fan, Mechanicsburg offers several opportunities to catch your favourite teams in action. Head to Giant Centre or Hersheypark Stadium for exciting football, ice hockey, and concerts and musical performances.

Shop and Dine at Local Establishments

Mechanicsburg offers plenty of options for visitors looking to shop or dine out. A range of shopping centres, boutiques, and specialty stores is available, and you can also find locally produced products.

Food and Drink Scene of Mechanicsburg

Mechanicsburg has a diverse culinary scene that caters to people from all walks of life. From cafes to fine restaurants, there’s something to whet everyone’s appetite.

Must-Try Dishes

Utz Potato Chips

Utz Potato Chips is a local snack food company based in Hanover, Pennsylvania, and is known to produce some of the best potato chips in the USA. You can buy their product all over the place, including convenient stores, grocery stores, and online.

Sturgis Pretzels

Sturgis Pretzels is where you can take a factory tour and see how their famous pretzel baked. It is open to the public, and it offers visitors to taste fresh-baked pretzels straight from the oven.

Whoopie Pie

Whoopie Pie is a local favorite dessert traditionally made with two soft chocolate cakes and a cream filling in between. This snack is available all over Mechanicsburg.

Local Beverages

Troegs Brewery

Troegs Brewery is a popular brewery that offers its “Scratch Series” beer, brewed in small batches and not released to be put on a normal tap handle. The tasting room is open to visitors and offers glasses, tours and growler fills.

The Winery at Hunter’s Valley

A trip to the winery at Hunter’s Valley is a must-visit place for lovers of wine. The winery has acres of vineyards and a beautiful tasting room, offering visitors the opportunity to taste their wines.

Where to Stay in Mechanicsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mechanicsburg offers several accommodations for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are three of our top recommendations:

The Radisson Hotel Harrisburg

Located just a mile from Mechanicsburg, the Radisson Hotel is an excellent option for visitors looking for a spacious, comfortable stay. Guests enjoy a fitness centre, pool, business centre, and room service.

The Courtyard by Marriott Harrisburg West/Mechanicsburg

Located in the city centre, the Courtyard by Marriott is within walking distance to many shops, restaurants, and attractions. Facilities include an indoor pool and hot tub, a fully equipped fitness centre, and a business centre.

The Stocks Manor

The Stocks Manor is a charming guesthouse located on a farm offering a unique combination of relaxation and comfort alongside a park-like atmosphere. Each room is elegantly furnished and comes with a full breakfast service.

What to Buy in Mechanicsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mechanicsburg offers plenty of shopping opportunities for visitors looking to bring home a unique gift. Here are a few suggestions:

Hand-Made Crafts

Mechanicsburg is a popular destination for handmade crafts and other rustic items. There are plenty of shops selling hand-made crafts, including folk art, quilts and pottery, and many other crafted goods.

Antique Objects

Antique objects are found in many of Mechanicsburg’s stores. The town is home to several antique shops that feature furniture, lamps, crockery, art and collectables, and so much more.

Local Produce

Mechanicsburg is home to several farms, bakeries, and other producers that offer locally grown or produced goods, including vegetables, cheese, honey, and jams. Checkout Mechanicsburgs Farmers Market located at the Broad and Simpson Street that operates seasonally on Friday late afternoon.

Local Culture and Customs of Mechanicsburg

Mechanicsburg is a town built on traditional values and a strong sense of community. It values its rich heritage, and its customs reflect the warm and friendly nature of its people.

Best Time to Visit Mechanicsburg

The best time to visit Mechanicsburg is during fall from late September to late November for the colorful foliage and pleasant weather, fair dates, including Cumberland Valley School of Music’s annual Apple Dumpling Festival and the Black Walnut Harvest Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mechanicsburg

Do:

  • Do explore the history and nature of Mechanicsburg
  • Do try the famous Utz Potato Chips, Sturgis pretzels, and whoopie pies.
  • Do bring a jacket in fall or winter.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.
  • Don’t ignore seasonal changes in weather. In summers, carry sunglasses and sunscreen, and in winters, wear warm clothing.

Safety and Health

Mechanicsburg is a relatively safe place, with little to no crime, and quality healthcare facilities are readily available in the town and nearby cities.

Safety in Mechanicsburg

Mechanicsburg is a safe town. Personally, taking basic security measures such as traveling in recommended areas, keeping your valuables away in safe storage while in the hotel room, and not traveling alone late at night to avoid any risk are advised.

Health in Mechanicsburg

Mechanicsburg has excellent health centers with qualified personnel, making it a safe and healthy place for visitors.

Getting to Mechanicsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mechanicsburg is located in the Harrisburg area, making it a quick drive from several nearby cities. Here are some ways to get to Mechanicsburg:

Airports Near Mechanicsburg

  • Harrisburg International Airport is just 18 miles away from Mechanicsburg.
  • Philadelphia International Airport is approximately 115 miles away from Mechanicsburg, a two-hour drive.

Bus Stations Near Mechanicsburg

  • Greyhound Station is in Harrisburg, a short distance from Mechanicsburg town.

Other Options

You can also drive or hire a private car to get around and explore Mechanicsburg and its neighboring destinations.

How to Get Around in Mechanicsburg

Mechanicsburg is a small town that is easy to navigate. You can easily explore the town on foot or bike. However, if you prefer, car rental or taxis are available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Mechanicsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mechanicsburg is an excellent base for exploring several nearby attractions, including:

Gettysburg National Military Park

Gettysburg is approximately 40 miles away from Mechanicsburg, and it will take 45 minutes to drive. With over 1,300 monuments and memorials, this military park tells the story of the American Civil War.

Hershey Park

Hershey Park is approximately 19 miles from Mechanicsburg and takes about a 30-minute drive. It is a premier family theme park with water rides, roller coaster rides, and entertainment for everyone of all ages.

Conclusion

Mechanicsburg is a charming town located in the heart of Pennsylvania’s Cumberland County. With its rich heritage, natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and friendly community, it is a wonderful place to visit. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, food, or simply a peaceful retreat, you will find something to love in Mechanicsburg.
