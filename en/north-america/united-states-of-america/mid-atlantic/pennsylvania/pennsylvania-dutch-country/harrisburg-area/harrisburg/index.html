Introduction to Harrisburg

Harrisburg, the capital city of Pennsylvania, is located in the heart of the Susquehanna River Valley. It is a multicultural city known for its historic landmarks, beautiful parks, and rich cultural heritage. There is always something exciting happening in Harrisburg, making it a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.

List of Places to Visit in Harrisburg

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Harrisburg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Civil War Museum: This museum provides a deep understanding of the events leading up to and happening during the Civil War.

  • Pennsylvania State Capitol: This historic building features breathtaking architecture and stunning works of art

  • The State Museum of Pennsylvania: This museum focuses on the natural and cultural history of the state.

Natural Attractions of Harrisburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wildwood Park: This scenic park is perfect for nature lovers with its beautiful walking trials.

  • City Island: This beautiful island is a perfect spot to relax and enjoy with the whole family.

  • Milk & Honey Tours: This tour provides an opportunity to learn about local honey, Chesapeake Bay oysters, and other great foods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harrisburg

Harrisburg offers bikers and hikers from the world the best biking and hiking trails. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Blue Mountain Trail: This trail is perfect for adventure seekers and offers some excellent views of the city.

  • Riverfront Park: Take a stroll along this scenic riverfront trail that provides an excellent view of the city’s skyline.

  • Capital Area Greenbelt: Known as the “emerald necklace,” this scenic trail covers more than 20 miles and is perfect for cyclists and hikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Harrisburg: Activities and Tours

  • Hersheypark: This famous amusement park offers a wide range of attractions and exciting rides for all ages.

  • Pennsylvania Farm Show Complex and Expo Center: This venue hosts a variety of events and performances throughout the year.

  • Whitaker Center for Science and the Arts: This popular destination features 3D cinema, an IMAX theater, and exhibits on science and technology.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harrisburg

Looking to spend a week in Harrisburg? Here’s our recommended itinerary:

Day 1 - State Capitol Building tour & Hersheypark
Day 2 - The State Museum of Pennsylvania & Wildwood Park
Day 3 - National Civil War Museum & City Island
Day 4 - Pennsylvania Farm Show Complex and Expo Center & State Street
Day 5 - Pennsylvania Renaissance Faire & National Civil War Museum
Day 6 - Whitaker Center for Science and the Arts & Capital Area Greenbelt
Day 7 - Hershey Gardens & Indian Echo Caverns

Food and Drink Scene of Harrisburg

Harrisburg has a vibrant food and drink scene that is gaining popularity. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch chicken pot pie
  • scrapple: a breakfast meat made from pork scraps and cornmeal
  • Shoofly pie: a traditional PA Dutch molasses pie

Local Beverages

  • Appalachian Brewing Company: This local brewery offers some great craft beers in a casual, laid-back environment.

  • Crimson Tate Cocktail Bar: This bar is known for its creative cocktails and unique atmosphere.

  • Harrisburg Wine Country: The nearby vineyards offer plenty of wine tastings.

Where to Stay in Harrisburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the top-rated places to stay in Harrisburg:

  • Hilton Harrisburg: A luxury hotel in the heart of the city, offering great views.

  • The Central Hotel & Conference Center: A budget-friendly option with great amenities.

  • Red Roof Inn Harrisburg North: This chic hotel option and boasts of free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Harrisburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the souvenirs and local products that you should consider purchasing:

  • Amish-made furniture: made by local craftsmen and available in a variety of styles and finishes

  • Harrisburg T-shirts: show off your love for the city by purchasing a locally made T-shirt.

  • Local honey and jams: the perfect sweet and delicious souvenir from Harrisburg

Local Culture and Customs of Harrisburg

Harrisburg is an incredibly diverse city, with a vibrant community that welcomes visitors with open arms. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the city’s rich cultural heritage and to engage with the locals and experience their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Harrisburg

The best time to visit Harrisburg is in the spring and fall when the city is at its most beautiful. The summer season can be hot and humid, while winter is cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harrisburg

Do:

  • Take a riverboat ride on the Susquehanna River.

  • Go on a tour of the Hershey Chocolate Factory

  • Visit the Amish country and learn about their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Jaywalk or jay ride

  • Litter or dump trash in public spaces

  • Smoke in designated non-smoking areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Harrisburg

Harrisburg is a safe destination for visitors, though it is still advisable to take necessary precautions. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone after dark. Also, avoid leaving valuable items in plain sight in your car.

Health in Harrisburg

Visitors should take standard health precautions when visiting Harrisburg, including washing hands regularly and avoiding contact with sick individuals.

Getting to Harrisburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Where to Go Next from Harrisburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some great options for day trips and nearby excursions include:

  • Hershey, PA: a nearby town known for its chocolate factory and amusement parks.

  • Gettysburg, PA: site of the famous Civil War battle and national military park.

  • Lancaster, PA: a charming town known for its Amish culture and picturesque countryside.

Conclusion

Harrisburg is a must-visit for anyone interested in history, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. With its rich heritage, vibrant food scene, and excellent cultural attraction, Harrisburg is sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler who takes the time to explore it.
