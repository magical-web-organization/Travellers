Introduction to Harrisburg Area

Located in Pennsylvania Dutch Country, Harrisburg area is a hidden gem in the state of Pennsylvania that offers visitors a range of cultural, outdoor, and gastronomic experiences. As the capital city of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, Harrisburg area is also home to many museums and historical landmarks. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through Harrisburg area’s must-visit attractions, best hiking and biking routes, local food and drink scene, recommended accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Harrisburg Area

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Civil War Museum: This museum showcases artifacts from the American Civil War, and offers exhibits, lectures, and educational events to visitors.
  • Pennsylvania State Capitol Complex: This building complex features one of the most beautiful capitol buildings in the United States, along with a museum and art galleries.
  • State Museum of Pennsylvania: This museum features displays of Pennsylvania’s natural history and cultural heritage, as well as science and technology exhibits.
  • Fort Hunter Mansion and Park: This historic site is a place to explore the history of Harrisburg area and Pennsylvania, with guided tours, educational programs, and special events.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Susquehanna River: This river is the lifeblood of Harrisburg area, and offers visitors a range of water-based activities like boating, kayaking, and fishing, as well as walking and biking paths along the river bank.
  • Wildwood Park: This park features several walking and hiking trails, wildlife viewing opportunities, and environmental education programs.
  • Boyd Big Tree Preserve Conservation Area: Take a hike to see some of the oldest and largest white pine trees in the area, along with other flora and fauna.
  • Hershey Gardens: This botanical garden offers visitors a visually stunning garden area, including roses, herbs, perennials, and trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harrisburg Area

  • Appalachian Trail: This historic trail stretches from Maine to Georgia, and includes a 229-mile section in Pennsylvania.
  • Harrisburg Riverfront Park: This 20-acre park features a mix of paved and unpaved trails, perfect for walking, biking, and jogging.
  • Capital Area Greenbelt: This 20-mile trail is a great way to explore the natural scenery of Harrisburg area.

Best Things to Do in Harrisburg Area: Activities and Tours

  • Harrisburg Brewery Tour: Visit the city’s top craft breweries, taste their unique beers, and learn about their brewing processes.
  • Whitewater Rafting Adventure: Take on the rapids of the Susquehanna River on a guided tour.
  • Hershey’s Chocolate World: Learn about the history of Hershey’s chocolate, take a factory tour, and indulge in some chocolate tasting.
  • Harrisburg Food Tour: Sample the best of Harrisburg area’s local cuisine, including pretzels, Pennsylvania Dutch treats, and Pennsylvania wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Harrisburg Area

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Apple Dumplings: A dessert made with sliced apples, sugar, cinnamon and butter, wrapped in dough, and baked to perfection.
  • Soft Pretzels: A blend of chewy and salty, Pennsylvania-style soft pretzels are a local specialty.
  • Lebanon Bologna: A regional cured meat from the area surrounding the city of Lebanon, Pennsylvania.
  • Shoo-Fly Pie: A molasses-based cake named for its ability to attract flies, the Shoo-Fly Pie is a Pennsylvania Dutch Country tradition.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: America’s oldest brewery, producing traditional lagers and more experimental beers.
  • Troegs Beer: A local brewery specializing in a wide variety of beers, from light to dark, hoppy, and complex flavors.
  • Pennsylvania Wines: Vintages are strong and bold and include wines made from native American grape varieties such as Niagara and Catawba.

Where to Stay in Harrisburg Area: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Hilton Harrisburg: A luxury hotel located in the center of downtown Harrisburg.
  • The Inn at Westwynd Farm: A B&B located in a beautiful farm setting, just a short drive from Harrisburg.
  • Sleep Inn and Suites Harrisburg: A budget-friendly hotel option.

What to Buy in Harrisburg Area: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Country Pretzels and Snacks: Bring home a taste of Pennsylvania Dutch Country.
  • Amish Quilts: Handmade and intricately designed.
  • Chocolates: Take some local Pennsylvania chocolate home as a delicious and memorable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Harrisburg Area

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Country Music and Folkora
  • Pennsylvania Dutch Language: A dialect of German spoken in the region and heard occasionally in casual conversation

Best Time to Visit Harrisburg Area

The best time to visit Harrisburg area is between April and October, when daytime temperatures are mild and the summer events are in full swing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harrisburg Area

Harrisburg area is generally a safe place to visit, but it is always best to take precautions in urban areas like downtown Harrisburg.

Health in Harrisburg Area

The city has excellent medical facilities. Stay hydrated in the warmer months, wear sunscreen, and guard against ticks and Lyme disease in the wooded areas.
