Introduction to Camp Hill

Camp Hill is a charming suburban borough in Pennsylvania, located in the Harrisburg area of Pennsylvania Dutch Country. Its name is derived from its hilly terrain resembling military camps. The borough has a rich history, diverse culture, and endless opportunities for exploration and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Camp Hill

Camp Hill has a variety of attractions catering to all interests and preferences. Here are some must-visit places in Camp Hill:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Shore Farmers Market: A bustling farmer’s market featuring a variety of vendors selling fresh produce, meats, baked goods, and more.
  • Historical Society of Camp Hill: A museum showcasing the history and culture of Camp Hill and its residents.
  • Radnor Green Historic District: A quaint residential neighborhood with tree-lined streets, historic homes, and scenic views.

Natural Attractions of Camp Hill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Negley Park: A beautiful park with walking paths, playgrounds, a pool, and picnic areas.
  • Conodoguinet Creek: A scenic waterway ideal for fishing, kayaking, and boating.
  • Kings Gap Environmental Education Center: A nature preserve with over 2,500 acres of forest, hiking trails, and stunning views of the Cumberland Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Camp Hill

Camp Hill has a variety of hiking and biking trails perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some top routes:

  • Cumberland Valley Rail Trail: A scenic trail stretching 13 miles from Shippensburg to Newville, featuring stunning views of farmlands, forests, and mountains.
  • Capitol Area Greenbelt: A 20-mile trail passing through Camp Hill, offering a mix of urban and natural landscapes.
  • Wildwood Park: A tranquil nature preserve with a 3-mile trail loop for hiking, running, and biking.

Best Things to Do in Camp Hill: Activities and Tours

Camp Hill has a wide range of activities and tours catering to different interests and preferences. Here are some must-do things in Camp Hill:

  • Ghost Tours of Gettysburg: A spooky tour of historic Gettysburg, complete with ghost stories and paranormal investigations.
  • Harrisburg wine country tour: A tour of the local vineyards and wineries of Harrisburg area for wine lovers.
  • Whitewater rafting: An exhilarating water adventure on the Susquehanna River.

Food and Drink Scene of Camp Hill

Camp Hill offers a great food and drink scene with a variety of options to satisfy your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shoofly pie: A traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dessert made with molasses and spices.
  • Lebanon bologna: A cured and smoked sausage made in nearby Lebanon County.
  • Scrapple: A breakfast food made with pork scraps, cornmeal, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Lager: A popular beer brewed in nearby Pottsville, said to be the oldest brewery in America.
  • Apple cider: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made from locally grown apples.
  • Birch beer: A regional soda made with birch bark extract, similar to root beer.

What to Buy in Camp Hill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Camp Hill offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to take home as a memento of your visit. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hex signs: Folk art signs with bright colors and geometric designs, often seen on Pennsylvania Dutch barns and buildings.
  • Amish-made crafts: Handcrafted items such as quilts, pottery, and furniture made by local Amish communities.
  • Harrisburg memorabilia: T-shirts, hats, and other items with Harrisburg-related themes.

Local Culture and Customs of Camp Hill

Camp Hill is a multicultural borough with a rich history and diverse community. Here are some facts about the local culture and customs:

  • Pennsylvania Dutch is a significant part of the local culture and heritage, with many local residents speaking the language and preserving the traditions.
  • The local food culture is heavily influenced by Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine and uses local ingredients.
  • The borough has a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, including the State Street Blues Stroll and the annual Christmas parade.

Best Time to Visit Camp Hill

The best time to visit Camp Hill is during the summer months when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a great time to visit, with the trees changing colors and many local fairs and festivals taking place.

Safety and Health

Here is some information regarding safety and health during your visit to Camp Hill:

Safety in Camp Hill

Camp Hill is a safe borough, and crime rates are relatively low. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and locking doors and windows at night. Visitors should also be mindful of traffic rules and regulations when walking or biking on busy roads.

Health in Camp Hill

Camp Hill has several medical facilities, including Holy Spirit Hospital and Geisinger Holy Spirit Urgent Care. Visitors should take necessary precautions to prevent illness, such as staying hydrated, washing hands frequently, and getting necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Camp Hill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Camp Hill is located near several transportation options, making it easily accessible from anywhere. Here are some ways to get to Camp Hill:

Airports Near Camp Hill

  • Harrisburg International Airport: Located just 15 miles from Camp Hill, Harrisburg International Airport offers direct flights to several major US cities.

Bus Stations Near Camp Hill

  • Greyhound: Located in Harrisburg, just a few miles from Camp Hill, Greyhound offers bus service to several major US cities.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Several rental car companies offer services in the Harrisburg area, making it easy to explore Camp Hill and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Camp Hill

Camp Hill is a small borough and easily accessible by foot or bike. Visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and trains, to get around Camp Hill and the surrounding areas.
