Introduction to Lancaster County

Nestled in Pennsylvania Dutch Country, Lancaster County is a must-visit destination in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Known for its rich history, diverse culture, and natural beauty, Lancaster County offers something for everyone, all year round.

Whether you are looking for family-friendly attractions, outdoor adventures, or a romantic getaway, Lancaster County has it all. From charming Amish farms and quaint small towns to vibrant city life and world-class museums, this county showcases the perfect blend of old-world charm and modern-day attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Lancaster County

From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Lancaster County boasts an impressive list of places to visit. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Amish Farm and House: Experience the lifestyle of the Amish community with authentic demonstrations, tours, and exhibits.
  • The Landis Valley Village and Farm Museum: Explore the rich history and tradition of early Pennsylvania German settlers with over 100 historic buildings, craftspeople, and exhibits.
  • The Lancaster Science Factory: This interactive science museum offers hands-on exhibits and workshops, ideal for families with children.
  • The Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania: Featuring over 100 historic locomotives and railcars, this museum showcases the evolution of American rail transportation.
  • The Lancaster County Historical Society and Museum: Discover the history of Lancaster County through art, artifacts, and architecture exhibits in this historic building.

Natural Attractions of Lancaster County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Susquehannock State Park: This state park offers camping, hiking, and fishing opportunities, situated in the scenic Susquehanna River Valley.
  • The Holtwood Dam: An ideal spot for picnicking, fishing, and birdwatching, the Holtwood Dam also offers scenic views of the Susquehanna River.
  • The Lancaster County Central Park: This park features diverse recreational activities, including hiking trails, bike rentals, and playgrounds.
  • The Wolf Sanctuary of PA: Home to several packs of gray wolves, this sanctuary offers educational tours and hikes.
  • The Turkey Hill Experience: Learn about the history and production of Turkey Hill Ice Cream and participate in interactive exhibits and taste tests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lancaster County

Lancaster County offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for all skill levels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Enola Low Grade Trail: A 29-mile trail that follows the former railroad bed through scenic farmland and woodlands.
  • Conestoga Trail System: Over 63 miles of hiking trails that showcase the diverse natural features of Lancaster County, including rolling hills, streams, and rock formations.
  • Northwest Lancaster County River Trail: A 14-mile paved trail along the Susquehanna River that features scenic views of the river and nearby farmland.
  • Lancaster Junction Trail: A 2-mile paved trail that follows the active railroad line to a scenic overlook of the Conestoga River.

Best Things to Do in Lancaster County: Activities and Tours

Lancaster County offers unique and exciting activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Take a hot-air balloon ride over the scenic countryside
  • Experience a horse-drawn carriage tour of Amish Farms
  • Attend a live theater show at Fulton Theatre or Sight & Sound Theatres
  • Explore the interactive exhibits and hands-on workshops at the North Museum of Natural History and Science
  • Take a historic walking tour of the City of Lancaster

Week-Long Itinerary for Lancaster County

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Lancaster County, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Amish Farm and House and take a horse-drawn carriage tour of the Amish farms.
  • Day 2: Explore the Landis Valley Village and Farm Museum and hike the Conestoga Trail System.
  • Day 3: Take a hot-air balloon ride over the scenic countryside and attend a live theater show at Fulton Theatre or Sight & Sound Theatres.
  • Day 4: Visit the Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania and take a historic walking tour of the City of Lancaster.
  • Day 5: Explore the Lancaster Science Factory or the North Museum of Natural History and Science, followed by a relaxing picnic on the banks of the Susquehanna River.
  • Day 6: Enjoy the natural beauty of Susquehannock State Park and go fishing or kayaking in the river.
  • Day 7: Experience the Turkey Hill Ice Cream factory and take a guided tour of Lancaster Brewing Company.

Food and Drink Scene of Lancaster County

Lancaster County is known for its delicious local cuisine and unique beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Whoopie Pies: A Lancaster County classic, these sweet treats are two soft cookies with a creamy frosting filling.
  • Shoofly Pie: A unique molasses pie with a crumb topping, often served with whipped cream or ice cream.
  • Scrapple: A square meatloaf made from pork scraps and cornmeal, often served at breakfast.
  • Soft Pretzels: A Philadelphia favorite, Lancaster County offers soft pretzels that are fluffy and salty.
  • Pork and Sauerkraut: A hearty, traditional dish that is especially popular on New Year’s Day.

Local Beverages

  • Lancaster County Wine Trail: Take a tour of local wineries and vineyards, offering a variety of red, white, and fruit wines.
  • Intercourse Brewing Company: A craft brewery that offers unique and delicious small-batch beers and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Turkey Hill Brewery: Enjoy a cold and refreshing beer while learning about the history and production of Turkey Hill Ice Cream.

Where to Stay in Lancaster County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lancaster County offers a diverse range of accommodations, catering to different budget and style preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Lancaster Arts Hotel: Located in the heart of Lancaster City, this boutique hotel features unique artwork and spacious rooms.
  • The Bird-in-Hand Village Inn & Suites: A peaceful and cozy inn in the heart of Amish Country, offering elegant rooms and cozy quilts.
  • The Hostel Shoppe: An affordable hostel that offers bike rentals and guided tours of Lancaster County.
  • The Bullock’s Mansion: A historic guesthouse that offers luxury suites and a tranquil garden setting.

What to Buy in Lancaster County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lancaster County offers a variety of unique and locally-made products that are perfect for souvenirs or gifts. Here are some options:

  • Amish Quilts and Crafts: Explore the vast selection of handcrafted goods, including quilts, furniture, and wooden toys.
  • Wilbur Chocolate Factory: Take home some delicious Wilbur chocolate, famous throughout Lancaster County and beyond.
  • Antique Shops: Lancaster County is known for its impressive collection of antique shops and markets, offering a variety of unique and vintage items.
  • Pottery and Ceramics: From salt and pepper shakers to kitchenware and dinner sets, Lancaster County offers a wide variety of locally-made pottery and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Lancaster County

Lancaster County is known for its unique and traditional Amish culture, which is deeply rooted in the county’s history and heritage. Visitors can learn about the Amish way of life through guided tours of farms, markets, and workshops. It’s important to respect the Amish culture and customs and follow some basic guidelines, such as dressing modestly and refraining from taking photographs without permission.

Best Time to Visit Lancaster County

Lancaster County offers something to visitors all year round, but the best time to visit depends on personal preferences. For outdoor activities, summer and fall offer pleasant weather and colorful landscapes. If you’re interested in cultural events and festivals, spring and summer are the best times to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lancaster County

Here are some basic guidelines and recommendations for visitors:

Do:

  • Respect the Amish culture and customs
  • Visit markets and shops to support local businesses and artisans
  • Explore the natural beauty of Lancaster County and participate in outdoor adventures
  • Participate in local events and festivals, such as the Ephrata Fair and Lititz Craft Show

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the Amish community without permission
  • Visit a farm or workshop without a guided tour or invitation
  • Litter or damage any natural or historic sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Lancaster County

Lancaster County is generally a safe and peaceful destination, but visitors should follow basic safety guidelines, such as locking the doors of their accommodation and cars and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lancaster County

Visitors should be aware of tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease, common in wooded areas. It’s recommended to wear long sleeves and pants, use insect repellent, and check for ticks after outdoor activities.

Getting to Lancaster County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lancaster County is easily accessible through various transportation options.

Airports Near Lancaster County

The closest international airport is Philadelphia International Airport, located approximately 100 miles to the east of Lancaster County. The Lancaster Airport offers regional flights and private charters.

Bus Stations Near Lancaster County

The Greyhound Bus Station in Lancaster City offers daily trips to and from major cities, including New York City and Washington D.C.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lancaster County by car, train, or private transportation. It’s easy to rent a car or hire a private driver for transportation around the county.

How to Get Around in Lancaster County

Visitors can explore Lancaster County through various transportation options, including car rentals, bikes, and public transportation. If you’re interested in exploring the Amish farms and countryside, guided tours and horse-drawn carriages are also popular options.

Where to Go Next from Lancaster County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lancaster County is a great destination on its own, but visitors can also explore nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some options:

  • Philadelphia: Known as the birthplace of the United States, Philadelphia is about 90 miles from Lancaster County and offers world-class museums, historical sites, and cultural attractions.
  • Hershey: Home of Hershey’s Chocolate, Hershey is located about 30 miles from Lancaster County and offers fun family-friendly activities, including Hersheypark and Hershey Gardens.
  • Gettysburg: A historic site of the Civil War, Gettysburg is located about 50 miles from Lancaster County and offers a variety of historical and cultural attractions, including the Gettysburg National Military Park.

Conclusion

Lancaster County is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a unique and diverse experience. With a variety of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine, there’s never a dull moment in Lancaster County.
