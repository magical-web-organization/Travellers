Introduction to Elizabethtown

Elizabethtown is a charming town located in Lancaster County, in Pennsylvania Dutch Country, in Pennsylvania, in Mid-Atlantic, in the United States of America, in North America. Founded in the early 18th century, this town offers a rich cultural and natural heritage to its visitors. Surrounded by scenic farmlands and rolling hills, Elizabethtown offers a picturesque getaway to those seeking outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Elizabethtown and explore the best that this town has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Elizabethtown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Elizabethtown Historical Society Museum - Located in the heart of downtown Elizabethtown, this museum showcases the town’s rich history through various exhibits and artifacts.
  2. Amish Farm and House - Take a tour of an Amish farmhouse and learn about the lifestyle and culture of the Amish community.
  3. Landis Valley Village and Farm Museum - This outdoor museum offers a glimpse into the 18th century Pennsylvania German way of life through interactive exhibits and demonstrations.

Natural Attractions of Elizabethtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Conoy Wetlands Nature Preserve - This nature preserve offers hiking trails, wildlife watching, and birding opportunities.
  2. Conewago Recreation Trail - This biking and hiking trail offers scenic views of the Susquehanna River and passes through historical sites.
  3. Gifford Pinchot State Park - This state park offers outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, swimming, boating, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elizabethtown

  1. Conewago Recreation Trail - This 5.3-mile trail runs between Bainbridge and Falmouth, passing through historical sites and scenic views of the Susquehanna River.
  2. Masonic Village Trail - This 2.5-mile trail winds through the Masonic Village campus and offers a serene outdoor experience, passing through woods and meadows.
  3. Chestnut Grove Natural Area Trail - This 2-mile trail offers a peaceful hike through a forested area with a stream and wildflower meadow.

Best Things to Do in Elizabethtown: Activities and Tours

  1. Segway Tour - Take a guided Segway tour of Elizabethtown and its surrounding areas to explore its history and natural beauty.
  2. Canoeing, Kayaking, and Tubing - Rent a canoe, kayak, or tube and float down the Conestoga River or the Susquehanna River for a relaxing and scenic experience.
  3. Golfing - Enjoy a round of golf at one of the many golf courses in and around Elizabethtown.

Food and Drink Scene of Elizabethtown

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shoofly Pie - A traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dessert made with molasses.
  2. Scrapple - A savory breakfast meat made with pork scraps and cornmeal.
  3. Chicken Pot Pie - A hearty entree made with chicken and vegetables in a creamy sauce topped with a flaky pie crust.

Local Beverages

  1. Root Beer - Try a glass of locally made root beer.
  2. Wine - Visit some of Lancaster County’s wineries and try their signature blends.

Where to Stay in Elizabethtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Bear House Bed and Breakfast - A cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of Elizabethtown.
  2. Cameron Estate Inn - A charming inn surrounded by picturesque gardens.
  3. West Ridge Guest House - A peaceful guesthouse located on a farm with scenic views.

What to Buy in Elizabethtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Amish Quilts - Purchase a beautiful handmade quilt from one of the many Amish quilt shops in Lancaster County.
  2. Pottery - Visit the many pottery studios in the area and purchase a handmade piece.
  3. Local Produce - Visit the local farmers’ markets and purchase fresh produce to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Elizabethtown

Elizabethtown is located in Lancaster County, which has a large Amish population. Visitors should respect their culture and customs by dressing modestly, avoiding photography of Amish people, and refraining from smoking, drinking, and loud music in their presence.

Best Time to Visit Elizabethtown

The best time to visit Elizabethtown is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its peak. Summer is also a popular time to visit, but it can be hot and humid, and the tourist crowds are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elizabethtown

Do:

  1. Do explore the town’s rich history and culture through its museums and historical sites.
  2. Do enjoy the outdoors by hiking, biking, or canoeing/kayaking.
  3. Do respect the Amish culture and customs.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t take photographs of the Amish people without their permission.
  2. Don’t play loud music or engage in disruptive behavior around Amish people.
  3. Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elizabethtown

Elizabethtown is a safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables visible in parked cars.

Health in Elizabethtown

There are several hospitals and medical centers in the surrounding area. Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical insurance before traveling.

The best way to get around Elizabethtown is by car. Visitors may also rent a bike or take public transportation.

Conclusion

With its rich history, outdoor adventures, charming downtown, and delicious food, Elizabethtown is a must-visit destination for those seeking an authentic Pennsylvania Dutch Country experience.
