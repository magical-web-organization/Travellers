Introduction to Lancaster

Lancaster, located in Lancaster County in Pennsylvania Dutch Country, is a charming city that offers a rich history and a unique blend of modern and traditional culture. With its rolling hills, picturesque farmland, and quaint towns, Lancaster attracts visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Lancaster

If you are planning to visit Lancaster, here are some of the top attractions that you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lancaster Central Market: Established in 1730, it is the oldest continuously operating farmers’ market in the United States.
  • Lancaster County Historical Society: This museum features artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Lancaster County.
  • Demuth Museum: This museum boasts a collection of works by renowned Lancaster artist Charles Demuth.

Natural Attractions of Lancaster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amish Farm and House: Tourists can experience a glimpse of Amish life with guided tours of a farmhouse, schoolhouse, and blacksmith shop.
  • Lancaster County Park: This park offers hiking and biking trails, fishing, and picnic areas.
  • Strasburg Rail Road: Visitors can take a scenic train ride through the countryside and experience Lancaster’s rich railroad history.
  • Wolf Sanctuary of Pennsylvania: This sanctuary houses more than 40 wolves and offers guided tours for visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lancaster

Lancaster County offers a variety of scenic hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes to explore:

  • Conestoga Trail: This 63-mile trail offers beautiful views of Lancaster County.
  • Northwest Lancaster County River Trail: This 14-mile trail follows the Susquehanna River and is perfect for biking and hiking.
  • Lancaster Junction Trail: This 4-mile trail is a mix of historic railroad lines and scenic farmland.

Best Things to Do in Lancaster: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on the Susquehanna River: This is a great way to experience the magnificent Susquehanna River.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: This is a unique way to get a bird’s eye view of Lancaster County.
  • Lancaster Food Tours: These tours offer a taste of Lancaster’s best local cuisine and beverages.
  • Ghost Tours: These tours take visitors on a spooky journey through Lancaster’s haunted sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lancaster

Day 1: Visit Lancaster Central Market and explore the shops and restaurants in downtown Lancaster.

Day 2: Take a tour of the Amish Farm and House, followed by a hot air balloon ride over Lancaster County.

Day 3: Visit the Lancaster County Park for hiking and biking. Then, take a tour of the Wolf Sanctuary of Pennsylvania.

Day 4: Explore the historical sites of Lancaster, including Lancaster County Historical Society and Demuth Museum.

Day 5: Take a relaxing kayak tour on the Susquehanna River followed by a visit to Strasburg Rail Road.

Day 6: Take a Lancaster Food Tour and sample some of the best local dishes and beverages.

Day 7: Complete your Lancaster adventure with a ghost tour of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Lancaster

Lancaster is home to a thriving food and drink scene that offers visitors a diverse range of experiences. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shoofly Pie: A traditional Lancaster dessert made with molasses.
  • Whoopie Pies: A popular dessert made with two cake-like cookies filled with creamy frosting.
  • Pretzels: Lancaster County is known for its soft pretzels.

Local Beverages

  • Intercourse Beer Works: This brewery offers a wide variety of craft beers made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Thistle Finch Distillery: This distillery offers handcrafted spirits including whiskey, gin, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Lancaster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lancaster County offers a variety of accommodations to suit any budget. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Inn at Leola Village: A luxurious inn featuring gorgeous rooms, a spa, and fine dining.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Lancaster: A highly-rated hotel featuring spacious guest rooms and complimentary breakfast.
  • Red Roof Inn Lancaster: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Lancaster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to take home, consider these Lancaster-made products:

  • Amish Quilts: These handmade quilts are a Lancaster County tradition.
  • Pottery: Lancaster has a thriving pottery scene, with many local artists creating beautiful pieces.
  • Handmade Soap: Many local artisans make soap using natural ingredients and unique scents.

Local Culture and Customs of Lancaster

Lancaster County is home to a large Amish community, which means that visitors should be respectful of their customs. Visitors should avoid taking photos of the Amish without permission and should not intrude on their private property or beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Lancaster

The best time to visit Lancaster is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild, and there are a variety of outdoor activities available. Fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit, as the foliage is beautiful and there are many festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lancaster

Do:

  • Respect the local community and their customs.
  • Explore the outdoors and enjoy the natural scenery.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Intrude on the Amish’s private property or beliefs.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Be insensitive to the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lancaster

Lancaster is a safe city for visitors, but it is always prudent to practice common sense safety precautions such as not leaving valuables in your car and being aware of your surroundings at night.

Health in Lancaster

Lancaster has several medical facilities and hospitals to serve visitors in case of illness or injury.

How to Get Around in Lancaster

Public transportation options in Lancaster are limited, so it is recommended that visitors rent a car for the duration of their trip. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lancaster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hershey, PA: Known as the “sweetest place on earth”, this town is home to the famous Hershey’s chocolate factory.
  • Gettysburg, PA: This historic town is home to the Gettysburg National Military Park.
  • Philadelphia, PA: Pennsylvania’s largest city is home to many historic landmarks and attractions.

Conclusion

Lancaster is a charming city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From the Amish countryside to the bustling city center, Lancaster has something to offer every traveler. Plan your trip today and start exploring all that this amazing city has to offer.
