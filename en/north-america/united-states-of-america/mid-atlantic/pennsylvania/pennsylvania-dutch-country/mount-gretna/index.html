Introduction to Mount Gretna

Nestled in the heart of Pennsylvania Dutch Country lies Mount Gretna, a charming community that offers visitors a wide range of outdoor adventures, scenic views, cultural attractions, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Mount Gretna has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, hike and bike routes to take, activities and tours to experience, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Gretna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Located just a short drive from Mount Gretna, the Cornwall Iron Furnace National Historic Site is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. Built in 1742, this historic blast furnace is one of the oldest in the country and offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of early ironworkers. Another cultural attraction to visit is the Allen Theatre, which hosts live performances and concerts throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Mount Gretna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There’s no shortage of outdoor adventures to be had in Mount Gretna. You can explore the forest on foot or by bike on the Lebanon Valley Rail Trail or hike the scenic trails at Governor Dick Park. For a more leisurely experience, take a stroll through the beautiful gardens at the Mt. Gretna Lake and Beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Gretna

Mount Gretna boasts some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in the area. One popular trail is the Conewago Trail, which follows the course of the Conewago Creek and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Other popular routes include the Lebanon Valley Rail Trail and the Governor Dick Park Trail.

Best Things to Do in Mount Gretna: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Mount Gretna, you won’t be disappointed. Take a guided tour of the Cornwall Iron Furnace National Historic Site, test your skills at the Adventure Zone ropes course, or take a relaxing horse-drawn carriage ride through the scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Gretna

Day 1: Take a guided tour of Cornwall Iron Furnace National Historic Site and learn about the area’s rich ironworking history. Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking the scenic trails at Governor Dick Park. Day 3: Take a leisurely stroll through the beautiful gardens at Mt. Gretna Lake and Beach. Day 4: Test your skills at the Adventure Zone ropes course. Day 5: Take a relaxing horse-drawn carriage ride through the scenic countryside. Day 6: Explore the Lebanon Valley Rail Trail by bike. Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Mount Gretna’s charming cafes or restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Gretna

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food in Mount Gretna, there are plenty of tasty options to choose from. Sample classic Pennsylvania Dutch dishes like shoofly pie or chicken corn soup at The Timbers Restaurant or enjoy a farm-to-table experience at Gretna Timbers.

Local Beverages

No trip to Mount Gretna is complete without a stop at the Mt. Gretna Craft Brewery. Here you can sample a variety of delicious craft beers made with local ingredients, including the popular Hop Asylum IPA.

Where to Stay in Mount Gretna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to spacious vacation rentals, Mount Gretna offers a wide range of accommodations to suit any traveler’s needs. The Gretna Inn and Suites is a popular choice for those looking for comfortable and affordable accommodations, while the Airy View Bed and Breakfast offers a more luxurious experience.

What to Buy in Mount Gretna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Mount Gretna home with you by visiting the Mount Gretna Outpost, where you can find a variety of handmade crafts and gifts made by local artisans. Another great place to shop is the Farmers Market at Gretna Timbers, which offers a wide selection of fresh produce, meats, and cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Gretna

As part of Pennsylvania Dutch Country, Mount Gretna is steeped in tradition and closely tied to its local culture. Visitors can experience this firsthand by attending one of the many fairs and festivals held throughout the year, such as the Mount Gretna Outdoor Art Show or the Gretna Music Festival.

Best Time to Visit Mount Gretna

The best time to visit Mount Gretna is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities, festivals, and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Gretna

Do:

  • Take a hike or bike ride through the scenic trails at Governor Dick Park.
  • Sample the local craft beers at Mt. Gretna Craft Brewery.
  • Attend a fair or festival to experience the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Leave without sampling some of the classic Pennsylvania Dutch dishes.
  • Miss out on a horse-drawn carriage ride through the scenic countryside.
  • Forget to pack your hiking shoes and outdoor gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Gretna

Mount Gretna is a safe and friendly community, but visitors should always take precautions when exploring the great outdoors. Bring plenty of water, wear sturdy shoes, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Mount Gretna

There are no major health concerns for visitors to Mount Gretna, but it’s always a good idea to pack any necessary medications and check with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Mount Gretna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Gretna

The closest airport to Mount Gretna is Harrisburg International Airport, which is about 40 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Mount Gretna

Greyhound offers bus routes to nearby cities like Lancaster and Harrisburg, with a convenient stop in Lebanon, just minutes from Mount Gretna.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best way to get around Mount Gretna and the surrounding area, but visitors can also use rideshare programs like Uber and Lyft.

How to Get Around in Mount Gretna

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Mount Gretna, but visitors can also take advantage of the Lebanon Transit bus system or hire a private car service.

Where to Go Next from Mount Gretna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations that are worth a visit include the charming town of Lititz, which is known for its beautiful architecture and numerous artisan shops. Travelers can also explore the history and culture of Lancaster, which is just a short drive away.

Conclusion

From scenic hiking trails to delicious local cuisine, Mount Gretna has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, this charming community is the perfect destination for your next trip.
