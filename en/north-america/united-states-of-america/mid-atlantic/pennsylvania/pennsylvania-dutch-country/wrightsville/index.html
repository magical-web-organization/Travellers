Introduction to Wrightsville

Nestled in Pennsylvania Dutch country, Wrightsville, Pennsylvania is a charming town that boasts a rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. With its gorgeous views of the Susquehanna River, top-notch museums and historical sites, and outdoor recreational opportunities, Wrightsville is a perfect destination for those looking for a truly unique trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Columbia Crossing River Trails Center

Start your Wrightsville itinerary with a visit to the Columbia Crossing River Trails Center, which provides visitors with a plethora of information about the Susquehanna River. This state-of-the-art museum also has an interactive exhibit that showcases the ecology, heritage, and recreational opportunities of the river.

Heritage Press Museum

Another must-see cultural attraction in Wrightsville is the Heritage Press Museum. This museum is dedicated to preserving the rich history of letterpress printing and typography. Visitors can learn about Pennsylvania Dutch printing traditions and even take home a handmade souvenir, such as a bookmark or greeting card.

Wrightsville Murals

A unique highlight of Wrightsville’s cultural sites are the Wrightsville Murals. These masterpieces depict Wrightsville’s rich history, including Native American settlements, industry, and railroads. The murals are easily accessible by foot and free to see.

Natural Attractions of Wrightsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Burning of the Bridge Monument and Trail

Outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike will enjoy the Burning of the Bridge Monument and Trail, where a pivotal event in the Civil War is commemorated. Visitors can marvel at the iconic Susquehanna River and learn about the historical significance of the area. It’s also a great place to hike and bike, offering picturesque river views and an easy trail for leisurely exploration.

Samuel S. Lewis State Park

Samuel S. Lewis State Park is another highlight of Wrightsville’s natural attractions. This park covers more than 85 acres and features picnic areas, hiking trails, playgrounds, and a pool. Visitors can hike along the park’s trails to enjoy the magnificent view of the Susquehanna River from the cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wrightsville

Wrightsville has a great selection of biking and hiking routes from where visitors can admire scenic views of the countryside. Some of the top options include the Northwest River Trail, the Heritage Rail Trail, and the Susquehanna River Water Trail. Adventurous visitors can rent kayaks or canoes for an unforgettable journey down the Susquehanna River.

Best Things to Do in Wrightsville: Activities and Tours

Long Level Marina

At Long Level Marina, visitors can rent boats, kayaks and paddleboards and enjoy water activities on the Susquehanna River or take a scenic sunset dinner cruise. The marina also features a beach, playgrounds, and picnic areas making it fun for families.

The John Wright Restaurant

Wrightsville is renowned for its delightful culinary scene, and the John Wright Restaurant is a must-visit destination for foodies. This historic restaurant offers a delicious selection of American cuisine and craft beers, paired with an unparalleled view of the Susquehanna River.

Food and Drink Scene of Wrightsville

Must-try Dishes

When in Wrightsville, you must indulge in their local delicacies. These include:

  • Whoopie pies: These pies are scrumptious and a signature dessert of the Amish.
  • Scrapple: A traditional breakfast meat made of pork scraps, and cornmeal.
  • Shoofly Pie: A classic Pennsylvania Dutch dessert.

Local Beverages

Wrightsville’s craft beer scene is remarkable. John Wright Restaurant and Moon Dancer Winery and Cider House offer remarkable craft beer, ciders, and other beverages. A tasting tour is a great way to appreciate and discover the drinks of the area.

Where to Stay in Wrightsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wrightsville offers a vast range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. For a luxurious stay, the ascendant Accomac Inn will leave you pampered and satisfied. The John Wright Store and Restaurant’s bed and breakfast suites offer a cozy and comfortable stay while also offering an amazing view of the Susquehanna River.

What to Buy in Wrightsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Wrightsville home with you by bringing some locally made souvenirs. These include handmade pottery, candles, soaps, quilts, and memorabilia relating to the Susquehanna River.

Local Culture and Customs of Wrightsville

Wrightsville’s culture is deeply rooted in Pennsylvania Dutch traditions. One can easily find these influences in local cuisines, handicrafts and in daily conversations with the locals. Respect and appreciation for local customs and culture are important, including using customary greetings, understanding cultural nuances and tolerating differences.

Best Time to Visit Wrightsville

Wrightsville is a year-round destination. However, if you plan to hike, bike, and explore the outdoors, the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. Spring and fall are ideal for sightseeing trips, while winter is a great time for indoor activities and cozying up in the warm and cozy accommodations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wrightsville

Do:

  • Plan your trip in advance and book accommodation in peak season, especially if you’re visiting during the summer months.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Try the local delicacies.
  • Visit the Susquehanna River to explore outdoor recreational activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any historical sites or memorials.
  • Disrespect the local customs and culture.
  • Make loud noises near residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wrightsville

Wrightsville is relatively safe to travel. However, visitors are advised to be cautious and vigilant while traveling and protect their valuable belongings. Locks are recommended while leaving your bike or vehicle unattended.

Health in Wrightsville

Wrightsville has several pharmacies and healthcare facilities in case of an emergency. The town is surrounded by rural areas, so it’s always recommended to carry basic medication and first aid kits.

Getting to Wrightsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wrightsville is easily accessible by air, train, car, and bus.

Airports Near Wrightsville

The closest airport is Harrisburg International Airport (MDT), which is about a half-hour drive from Wrightsville.

Bus Stations Near Wrightsville

Wrightsville has several intercity bus stations, including Greyhound and Megabus.

Other Options

Amtrak serves Lancaster, which is approximately a 20-minute drive from Wrightsville.

How to Get Around in Wrightsville

Wrightsville is a small town and everything is easily accessible on foot or by bicycle. Free public parking spaces are available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Wrightsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wrightsville’s proximity to other popular destinations makes it a great jumping-off point for further exploration. Lancaster and Hershey offer plenty of attractions, including historic sites, museums, and amusement parks.

Conclusion

Wrightsville is a true gem and has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, this charming town in the heart of Pennsylvania Dutch Country is certainly worth the visit. So, pack your bags, book your vacation and explore one of the most charming towns in Pennsylvania.
