Introduction to York

York, Pennsylvania, is a historic city located in Pennsylvania Dutch Country, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. This charming city reflects its rich past and has a variety of attractions, scenic views, outdoor adventures, and delicious food and drink options, making it a great destination for both solo travelers and families.

List of Places to Visit in York

Get ready to immerse yourself in York’s unique culture and explore some of its must-see attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

York is full of historic landmarks and museums, including:

  • York County History Center: This museum presents an interactive exhibit of York County’s history, with artifacts, documents, and interactive displays, making it a great place to learn about the city’s past.
  • Agricultural and Industrial Museum: This museum showcases the agricultural and industrial heritage of York County, with exhibits on the machinery, tools, and innovative processes that made the county the hub of manufacturing and farming.
  • York Central Market: This central market has been operating since 1888 and is an iconic landmark in York. It still attracts locals and visitors alike with its delicious food, baked goods, and fresh produce.

Natural Attractions of York: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

York is also known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, including:

  • Codorus State Park: This park has a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking trails, swimming, fishing, and boating on the lake.
  • Nixon County Park: This park is a perfect place for bird watching, hiking, and exploring the local flora and fauna.
  • Rocky Ridge County Park: This park is known for its beautiful scenic views, hiking and biking trails, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of York

York County has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Heritage Rail Trail County Park: This trail is a 21-mile rail trail that passes through different towns while providing beautiful scenery and an excellent opportunity for outdoor activities.
  • Rocky Ridge Park Trails: These trails vary in length and difficulty, offering hikers and mountain bikers a challenge and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • John C. Rudy County Park Trails: These trails are perfect for beginner hikers and families and offer a scenic route around the park’s lake and its surrounding woods.

Best Things to Do in York: Activities and Tours

York has plenty of activities to suit everyone’s interests. You can choose from the following:

  • Visit York Revolution: It is a minor league baseball team in the Freedom Division of the Atlantic League of Professional Baseball.
  • Take a walking tour of downtown York to learn about its history and architecture.
  • Explore Bube’s Brewery and take a guided tour of the brewery and its fascinating underground catacombs.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride for a unique perspective of the city and its surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for York

If you’re planning to spend a week in York, here is our suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the York County History Center and the Agricultural and Industrial Museum, and take a stroll through Central Market.
  • Day 2: Explore the hiking and biking trails in Codorus State Park and visit the surrounding towns.
  • Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride and explore the city’s downtown.
  • Day 4: Visit Nixon County Park and take a walking tour of some of the city’s historic neighborhoods.
  • Day 5: Take a road trip to Lancaster County and visit some of its Amish attractions.
  • Day 6: Relax at Rocky Ridge County Park, have a picnic, and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 7: Take a guided tour of Bube’s Brewery and explore some of the city’s nightlife.

Food and Drink Scene of York

York has a rich culinary history, with several local delicacies and a vibrant food and drink scene to explore.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch chicken pot pie: It is a Pennsylvania Dutch version of this classic comfort food, with doughy squares instead of noodles.
  • Shoofly pie: It is a treacle tart dessert that originated from the Pennsylvania Dutch community.
  • New York-style pepperoni rolls: These famous rolls are made with pizza dough, cheese, and pepperoni, creating an explosion of flavors.

Local Beverages

  • York White Rose Ale: It is a locally brewed beer featuring the signature white rose of York.
  • Chardonnay from Naylor Wine Cellars: This local wine is made from locally grown Chardonnay grapes and is perfect to pair with local dishes.

Where to Stay in York: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

York has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference, including:

  • The Yorktown Hotel: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of downtown York and offers a luxurious stay with classic decor and modern amenities.
  • The Warehouse Hotel at The Nook: This unique hotel is located within a sports complex and offers spacious contemporary rooms and easy access to various recreational facilities.
  • Hostel in the Forest: This budget-friendly hostel offers comfortable dorm rooms and private cabins surrounded by nature and is perfect for those wanting to immerse themselves in York’s natural beauty.

What to Buy in York: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and locally made souvenirs to bring back home, look no further:

  • York Pretzels: These pretzels are famous for their unique twist and are sold all over the city.
  • Amish-made quilts and crafts: They are available in various stores throughout the city and make for a great gift.
  • Harley Davidson souvenirs: York is home to one of the oldest Harley Davidson factories, and the city is a great place to buy some souvenirs related to the world-famous motorcycle brand.

Local Culture and Customs of York

York’s culture and customs are heavily influenced by its Pennsylvania Dutch heritage, which is reflected in its cuisine, architecture, and weekly farmers’ markets. Generally, the locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors and take pride in their city’s rich history and culture.

Best Time to Visit York

The best time to visit York is during spring and fall when the weather is pleasant, and the city comes alive with various festivals and events. However, if you’re looking for summer outdoor adventures, June to August is also a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of York

Do:

  • Do take a walking tour of the city’s historic neighborhoods.
  • Do try the local cuisine and shop at the farmers’ markets.
  • Do visit the surrounding counties for more Amish culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.
  • Don’t forget to follow the local traffic laws and regulations.
  • Don’t forget to tip restaurant servers and bartenders appropriately.

Safety and Health

Safety in York

York is generally safe, but it is always advisable to take care of your belongings and avoid exploring less populated areas at odd hours.

Health in York

York has good healthcare facilities, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the city and nearby areas.

Getting to York: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near York

The closest airport to York is Harrisburg International Airport, which is situated around 30 miles away. Other options include Baltimore-Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport and Philadelphia International Airport.

Bus Stations Near York

York has a Greyhound bus station located in the heart of downtown, making it easy to travel to and from the city.

Other Options

York can also be reached by Amtrak, with daily service from major cities like New York, Philadelphia, and Washington, D.C.

How to Get Around in York

York is a walkable city, but if you need to travel around, there are several transportation options available:

  • York County Heritage Rail Trail: This trail passes through various towns and attractions, making it a great option for a scenic bike ride or walk.
  • Bus: Rabbit Transit is the local bus service, and it offers various routes through and around the city.
  • Taxi services: There are several taxi companies available in York, and ride-hailing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from York: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Adjacent to York are a variety of exciting destinations and attractions, including:

  • Hershey: Located about an hour away, Hershey is known for its chocolate-themed amusement park and Hershey’s Chocolate Factory.
  • Gettysburg: Located about 30 minutes away, Gettysburg is a historic city with various museums and landmarks related to the Civil War.
  • Lancaster: Located about 40 minutes away, Lancaster is known for its Amish culture, and visitors can explore Amish farms, markets, and museums.

Conclusion

With its rich history, scenic beauty, diverse culinary scene, and unique attractions, York, Pennsylvania, is an excellent destination for anyone seeking a memorable travel experience. Whether you’re a solo traveler, family, or group of friends, you are sure to find something you love in this charming city.
