Introduction to Adams County

Adams County is a picturesque region in Pennsylvania Dutch Country, located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Known for its scenic countryside, quaint towns, and rich history, Adams County is a charming destination for travelers. The county’s prime location between Gettysburg and Harrisburg makes it an accessible and well-connected destination.

This comprehensive travel guide will give you a list of places to visit, hiking/biking routes, the best things to do, where to stay, where to eat and drink, what to buy, local culture and customs, the best time to visit, do’s and don’ts, safety and health, transportation options and logistics, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Adams County

From museums to historical sites, outdoor adventures to scenic views, Adams County offers something for every traveler. The following are the top attractions in Adams County.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Adams County has a rich history, and its museums and historical sites showcase it beautifully. The following are the top cultural attractions in Adams County:

  • Gettysburg National Military Park - The site of the most famous battle of the Civil War, Gettysburg National Military Park offers an immersive experience of the battlefield, the war, and the aftermath. The park also has a museum and a visitor center.

  • Seminary Ridge Museum - Located on the Seminary Ridge on the Gettysburg Battlefield, the Seminary Ridge Museum tells the story of the battle from the viewpoint of the Lutheran Theological Seminary that served as a field hospital.

  • Eisenhower National Historic Site - The former home of President Dwight D. Eisenhower and his wife Mamie, the Eisenhower National Historic Site offers a glimpse into the life of the former president and his family.

  • Gettysburg Heritage Center - The Gettysburg Heritage Center is a museum that showcases the history of Gettysburg and the Civil War. The museum uses interactive exhibits, artifacts, and multimedia presentations to tell the story.

Natural Attractions of Adams County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Adams County is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The following are the top natural attractions in Adams County:

  • Michaux State Forest - The Michaux State Forest is a vast expanse of forested land with hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and camping sites.

  • Strawberry Hill Nature Preserve - The Strawberry Hill Nature Preserve is a 609-acre natural preserve with hiking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and a nature center.

  • Caledonia State Park - The Caledonia State Park has hiking trails, fishing spots, picnic areas, and a swimming pool, making it an ideal destination for a family outing.

  • Gettysburg National Military Park - Apart from its historical significance, the Gettysburg National Military Park is a beautiful destination for scenic drives and hikes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Adams County

Adams County is a great destination for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. The following are the top hiking/biking trails in Adams County:

  • Gettysburg Battlefield Trail - This 8-mile trail takes you through the historic Gettysburg Battlefield, offering scenic views and a glimpse into the history of the Civil War.

  • Appalachian Trail - The Appalachian Trail is a 2,180-mile trail that passes through 14 states, including Pennsylvania. The section of the trail that passes through Adams County is particularly scenic.

  • South Mountain Trail - The South Mountain Trail is a 14-mile trail that passes through the Michaux State Forest, offering stunning views of the forested landscape.

Best Things to Do in Adams County: Activities and Tours

Apart from its natural beauty and rich history, Adams County offers many fun activities and tours for travelers. The following are the top things to do in Adams County:

  • Adams County Winery - The Adams County Winery offers wine tastings, vineyard tours, and a charming terrace to enjoy a glass of wine.

  • Land of Little Horses - The Land of Little Horses is a miniature horse farm where visitors can watch the horses perform, pet them, and take a carriage ride.

  • Boyer Nurseries and Orchards - The Boyer Nurseries and Orchards offer apple picking, pumpkin patches, and a farm market that sells fresh produce, baked goods, and homemade cider.

  • Gettysburg Ghost Tours - The Gettysburg Ghost Tours offer spooky tours of the haunted sites in Gettysburg.

Food and Drink Scene of Adams County

Adams County is home to many local farms, orchards, and vineyards, making it a great destination for foodies. The following are the must-try dishes and local beverages of Adams County:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Butter - A spread made from simmered apples, apple butter is a delicious topping for toast, bagels, or pancakes.

  • Scrapple - A Pennsylvania Dutch delicacy, Scrapple is a breakfast meat made from pork, cornmeal, and spices.

  • Shoofly Pie - A traditional Amish dessert, Shoofly Pie is a sweet pie made from molasses, crumbly crumbs, and a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Hard Cider - Adams County is the largest producer of hard cider in Pennsylvania, with several local breweries and cideries offering tastings and tours.

  • Chardonnay - The Adams County Winery is a popular winery that produces some of the best Chardonnay in the region.

Where to Stay in Adams County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Adams County offers a range of accommodations, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. The following are the best places to stay in Adams County:

  • Gettysburg Hotel - The historic Gettysburg Hotel is an elegant hotel with modern amenities and a central location.

  • Brickhouse Inn Bed and Breakfast - The Brickhouse Inn offers charming rooms in a historic building, with a hearty breakfast included.

  • Baladerry Inn - The Baladerry Inn is a cozy bed and breakfast with a beautiful garden and a relaxing atmosphere.

  • The Keystone Inn - The Keystone Inn is a budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Adams County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Adams County is a great destination for souvenir shopping, with many local products and handicrafts on offer. The following are the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Adams County:

  • Apple Butter - A jar of apple butter is a tasty and easy-to-pack souvenir that will remind you of the county’s agriculture heritage.

  • Quilts - Quilt-making is a traditional Amish craft, and Adams County is a great place to buy handmade quilts in various styles and sizes.

  • Pottery - Several local potters create beautiful earthenware and stoneware pottery that is both functional and decorative.

  • Hard Cider - Local breweries and cideries offer bottles of hard cider that make great gifts for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Adams County

Adams County has a unique culture and customs, influenced by its Pennsylvania Dutch and Amish heritage. Some local customs to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for the Amish and their way of life, which includes no photographs or direct interaction with the community.

  • Sunday is a day of rest and worship for many residents, so some businesses may be closed.

Best Time to Visit Adams County

Adams County is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own beauty and charm. Autumn is particularly popular for its stunning foliage, while spring is perfect for birdwatching and cherry blossom season. Summer is great for outdoor activities, and winter offers a cozy and quiet respite from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Do’s and Don’ts of Adams County

To make the most of your visit to Adams County, follow these simple Do’s and Don’ts:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or damage the natural environment.

  • Take photos of the Amish community without their permission.

  • Ignore traffic laws or road signs.

Safety and Health

Adams County is a safe destination, but basic precautions should be taken to ensure a hassle-free vacation.

Safety in Adams County

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially if you are traveling alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables in a safe place and only carry what is necessary.

  • Be careful while hiking or exploring the outdoors, and follow the guidelines of the park or trail.

Health in Adams County

  • Adams County has several medical facilities and pharmacies, so medical help is easily accessible if needed.

  • Stay hydrated and protected from the sun while participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Adams County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Adams County is an accessible destination, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Adams County

  • Harrisburg International Airport (MDT) - Located 38 miles from Gettysburg, the Harrisburg International Airport is the closest airport to Adams County.

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI) - Located 66 miles from Gettysburg, the Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport is another option for travelers who want to fly into Adams County.

Bus Stations Near Adams County

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Gettysburg, the Greyhound Bus Station is a convenient option for travelers who prefer bus travel.

Other Options

  • Driving - Adams County is an easy road trip destination, with several highways and interstates connecting it to major cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Adams County

The best way to get around in Adams County is by renting a car or bringing your own vehicle. Public transportation options like buses and taxis are limited, and walking or cycling may not be practical for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Adams County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Adams County is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding region. The following are nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider:

  • Hershey, PA - Known as the “Sweetest Place on Earth,” Hershey is a charming town that is home to the famous Hershey’s Chocolate Factory.

  • Lancaster, PA - Lancaster is a beautiful city that is known for its Amish heritage and countryside, offering several tours and attractions for visitors.

  • Washington, DC - The nation’s capital is only a 90-minute drive from Adams County, making it an accessible destination for a day trip.

Conclusion

Adams County is a charming and accessible destination that offers something for every traveler. From its rich history and cultural attractions to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, Adams County is a must-visit destination for those seeking a break from city life. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Adams County with ease and make the most of your vacation.
