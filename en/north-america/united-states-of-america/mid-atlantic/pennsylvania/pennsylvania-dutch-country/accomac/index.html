Introduction to Accomac

Nestled in the heart of Pennsylvania Dutch Country, Accomac is a charming town that offers a glimpse into the region’s rich history and vibrant culture. The town is known for its beautiful natural scenery, quaint shops, and friendly locals. Whether you’re in search of outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Accomac has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Accomac

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ephrata Cloister

Founded in 1732, the Ephrata Cloister was once a thriving religious community and is now a National Historic Landmark. Visitors can tour the original community buildings, including the Saal and the Sisters’ House, which date back to the 18th century.

Landis Valley Village & Farm Museum

The Landis Valley Village & Farm Museum is a living history museum that offers an interactive experience of Pennsylvania German culture. The museum features over 100 historic buildings, including a working farm and village.

Cornwall Iron Furnace

The Cornwall Iron Furnace is a National Historic Landmark that provides insight into Pennsylvania’s early iron industry. Guided tours of the furnace allow visitors to see how iron was made from the 18th to the 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Accomac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chickies Rock County Park

Chickies Rock County Park is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and birdwatching. It overlooks the Susquehanna River and offers stunning views of the Lancaster County countryside.

Haldeman Island

Accessible only by boat, Haldeman Island is a nature reserve that boasts many hiking trails and a variety of wildlife. Visitors can explore the island’s quiet solitude while experiencing the flora and fauna.

Middle Creek Wildlife Management Area

Middle Creek Wildlife Management Area is a haven for birdwatchers, hikers, and nature lovers. The area spans over 6,000 acres of wetlands and offers ample opportunities to see a variety of birds and wildlife in their natural habitats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Accomac

Lancaster County Central Park Trail

The Lancaster County Central Park Trail is a 4-mile loop that offers scenic views of Lancaster County’s woodlands and meadows. It is suitable for hikers and bikers of all skill levels and is open year-round.

Northwest Lancaster County River Trail

The Northwest Lancaster County River Trail is a 14-mile trail that follows the Susquehanna River. It passes through several scenic towns and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Accomac: Activities and Tours

Amish Country Tours

Visitors to Accomac can take a guided tour of the Amish countryside, where they can see traditional farms and learn about the lifestyle and customs of the Amish community.

Balloon Rides

Balloon rides provide a unique way to experience the beauty of Lancaster County’s countryside. Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the region’s rolling hills and Amish farmland.

Food and Drink Scene of Accomac

Must-Try Dishes

Shoo-fly Pie

Shoo-fly pie is a traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dessert that is made with molasses and a crumbly topping. It’s a staple at many local bakeries and is a must-try for anyone visiting the region.

Scrapple

Scrapple is a Pennsylvania Dutch breakfast meat that is made from pork scraps and cornmeal. It’s often served with eggs and is a hearty breakfast option.

Local Beverages

Locally Made Root Beer

Many local breweries in the area produce their own root beer, which is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for a hot summer day.

Apple Cider

Apple cider is a classic fall drink that’s made from fresh-pressed apples. It’s often served hot and is a must-try during the autumn months.

Where to Stay in Accomac: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn at Leola Village

The Inn at Leola Village is a luxurious hotel that offers top-notch amenities and elegant rooms. It boasts multiple gourmet restaurants and a spa on-site.

Amish View Inn & Suites

The Amish View Inn & Suites is a charming hotel that features picturesque views of the surrounding countryside. It offers spacious suites and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Accomac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Quilts

Quilting is a traditional craft in the Pennsylvania Dutch culture, and visitors to the area can find handmade quilts at many local shops and markets.

Pottery and Ceramics

Pottery and ceramics are also popular crafts in the area, and visitors can find unique pieces at local galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Accomac

The Pennsylvania Dutch culture is known for its unique customs and traditions, including quilting, farming, and horse-drawn transportation. Visitors to the area can learn about these customs by taking guided tours of local Amish farms and villages.

Best Time to Visit Accomac

The best time to visit Accomac is during the fall months when the leaves are changing colors and the weather is mild. The region is also famous for its autumn festivals and harvest celebrations, which take place from September to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Accomac

Do:

  • Respect the customs and beliefs of the Amish community.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the Lancaster countryside.
  • Sample the traditional Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the Amish community without permission.
  • Wear immodest clothing or dress inappropriately when visiting Amish areas.
  • Touch or feed farm animals without permission from the owners.

Safety and Health

Safety in Accomac

Accomac is a generally safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Be sure to lock car doors and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Accomac

Visitors to the area should drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors. They should also be mindful of ticks and take appropriate precautions when hiking or camping in wooded areas.

Getting to Accomac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Accomac

The closest airport to Accomac is the Lancaster Airport, which is approximately 20 minutes away by car. Other nearby airports include the Harrisburg International Airport and the Philadelphia International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Accomac

The closest bus station to Accomac is the Lancaster Bus Station, which is located in downtown Lancaster. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Accomac.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Accomac, as it is conveniently located just off of Route 30. Additionally, they can take a train to Lancaster and then transfer to a bus or rental car to reach Accomac.

How to Get Around in Accomac

The best way to get around in Accomac is by car, as public transportation options can be limited. Visitors can rent a car at one of the nearby airports or in Lancaster.

Where to Go Next from Accomac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gettysburg

Located less than 2 hours from Accomac, the historic town of Gettysburg is a must-visit destination for history buffs. Visitors can explore the battlefield and learn about the Civil War at the Gettysburg National Military Park and Museum.

Hershey

Just over an hour’s drive from Accomac, Hershey is a fun-filled destination for families and chocolate lovers. Visitors can tour the Hershey’s Chocolate World and enjoy theme park rides at Hersheypark.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to dive into Pennsylvania Dutch history and culture or simply enjoy the beautiful natural scenery, Accomac has something to offer every traveler. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
