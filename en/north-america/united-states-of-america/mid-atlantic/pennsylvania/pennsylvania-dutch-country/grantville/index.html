Introduction to Grantville

Grantville is a small unincorporated community located in East Hanover Township, Dauphin County, Pennsylvania.

Nestled in the heart of Pennsylvania Dutch Country, Grantville is a charming destination known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures.

Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s fascinating history, take in breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside, or immerse yourself in local culture, Grantville offers something for everyone.

This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Grantville, detailing the best places to visit, hiking/biking routes, activities and tours, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Grantville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Farmstead Farmers Market: This is the largest indoor farmers market in Central Pennsylvania, featuring local produce, handmade crafts, artisanal cheeses, and more.

  • National Civil War Museum: This museum boasts an impressive collection of artifacts, documents, and photographs that showcase the history of the Civil War. Visitors can take part in guided tours, listen to educational lectures, and watch interactive exhibits.

  • Hershey Gardens: This stunning 23-acre botanical garden houses a wide variety of plants, flowers, and sculptures that captures Hershey’s history and legacy. Visitors can also participate in various workshops, and events, including gardening, beekeeping, and butterfly walks.

  • Indian Echo Caverns: These fascinating natural caves offer a unique glimpse into the geological history of Pennsylvania. Visitors can take a guided tour through the caves and learn about their formation and significance.

  • Hersheypark: Hershey’s world-famous amusement park is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Grantville. With over 70 rides, thrilling attractions, live entertainment, and delectable dining options, Hersheypark offers endless hours of fun and excitement.

Natural Attractions of Grantville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Gretna Lake and Beach: This scenic lake offers a beautiful backdrop for swimming, fishing, boating, hiking, and picnicking. Visitors can also take part in various water-based activities like kayaking, paddle-boarding, and water slides.

  • Swatara State Park: This beautiful park features miles of trails through lush forests, tranquil streams, and rocky terrain. Visitors can participate in hiking, biking, horseback riding, and camping activities.

  • Boyd Big Tree Preserve Conservation Area: This picturesque preserve comprises 943 acres of stunning woodlands, wildflower meadows, streams, and wetlands. Visitors can take a relaxing stroll through the park and marvel at the spectacular views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grantville

  • Swatara State Park Loop Trail: This scenic 12.1-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside, and through dense forests, and across rocky streams and valleys. Visitors can participate in hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding trails.

  • Boyd Big Tree Preserve Conservation Area Trail: This 4.4-mile trail offers a moderate to difficult hiking terrain through thick forests, streams, and meadows.

  • Lebanon Valley Rail Trail: This 15.6-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a walk through scenic farmland, tranquil streams, and beautiful nature.

Best Things to Do in Grantville: Activities and Tours

  • Chocolate World: Indulge your sweet tooth with a visit to Hershey’s chocolate-themed park, offering various live shows, tastings, and tours of the iconic chocolate factory.

  • Hershey’s Chocolate Spa: Pamper yourself with a variety of chocolate-infused spa treatments for a unique and rejuvenating experience.

  • Indian Echo Caverns Gem Mill Junction Mine Tour: Take a guided tour of this historic 1856 mine and witness the mining techniques from that era. Additionally, visitors can purchase various types of precious stones and gems found within the mine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grantville

Day 1: Visit the National Civil War Museum and learn about the history of the Civil War in Pennsylvania.

Day 2: Explore the lush botanical wonders of Hershey Gardens, which showcases Hershey’s history and the family’s legacy.

Day 3: Embark on a breathtaking adventure through the natural limestone caves of Indian Echo Caverns.

Day 4: Head to Swatara State Park for an invigorating hike, bike, or horseback riding experience amidst beautiful nature.

Day 5: Visit Hersheypark and indulge in endless fun, exciting rides, and scrumptious food options.

Day 6: Take a trip to Farmstead Farmers Market and shop for local produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal cheeses.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a quiet day at Mount Gretna Lake and Beach, where you can fish, swim, and take part in various water-based activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Grantville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pretzels and Mustard: Pennsylvania Dutch-style pretzels are a staple in Grantville and are best enjoyed with a side of tasty mustard.

  • Whoopie Pies: This classic dessert is a Pennsylvania Dutch specialty made from two soft cake-like cookies stuffed with a sweet, creamy filling.

  • Scrapple: This savory dish comprises ground pork scraps seasoned with herbs and spices and served with eggs, toast, and coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Grape Jelly Juice: This delicious purple drink brimming with grape flavor is a local favorite in Grantville.

  • Mint Iced Tea: This refreshing tea can be found in most restaurants and is perfect for quenching your thirst on hot days.

What to Buy in Grantville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pennsylvania Dutch-style Quilts: These beautiful, hand-stitched quilts are available in various sizes, patterns, and colors and make for a unique souvenir or gift.

  • Local Cheese and Deli Items: Indulge in delicious cheese, gourmet deli meats, and homemade jams and jellies from the local farmers’ market.

Local Culture and Customs of Grantville

  • Pennsylvania Dutch is a significant cultural heritage in Grantville, with many unique customs and traditions that are still practiced today.

  • The Amish community, known for their simple living and rustic lifestyle, is prevalent in the region and is worth experiencing for an authentic cultural experience.

Best Time to Visit Grantville

The best time to visit Grantville is from May to September when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for outdoor activities and exploring the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grantville

Grantville is a relatively safe destination with low crime rates. However, it’s essential to take general safety precautions to avoid any mishaps.

  • Take note of your surroundings, especially at night.

  • Avoid traveling alone or in unsavory neighborhoods, if possible.

Health in Grantville

It’s essential to take heed of the sun and stay hydrated while partaking in outdoor activities. Ensure you have basic medicines with you on your trip for any emergencies that may arise.

Getting to Grantville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grantville

The nearest airport to Grantville is Harrisburg International Airport (MDT), located approximately 25 miles away from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Grantville

The Greyhound bus line stops at the Harrisburg bus station, located approximately 17 miles from Grantville.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient option to travel within Grantville and the surrounding areas. Additionally, local taxis and Uber services are readily available in Grantville.

How to Get Around in Grantville

The best way to get around Grantville is by renting a car to explore the surrounding areas easily.

Where to Go Next from Grantville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

-Lancaster County: Located about 30 miles east of Grantville, Lancaster County offers an array of attractions and activities, including Amish tours, shopping, dining, and even hot air ballooning.
