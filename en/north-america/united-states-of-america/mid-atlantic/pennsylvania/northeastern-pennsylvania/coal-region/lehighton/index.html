Lehighton is a charming borough located in the heart of the Coal Region of Northeastern Pennsylvania. Visitors to Lehighton are treated to a wide range of attractions and activities, from historic landmarks to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at all that Lehighton has to offer, including the best places to visit, hike and bike routes, must-try dishes and local beverages, accommodations, and more!

Introduction to Lehighton

Founded in 1818, Lehighton is a small but vibrant borough in Carbon County, Pennsylvania, with a population of just under 6,000 people. The town is located on the banks of the Lehigh River and provides a picturesque setting for visitors to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Lehighton

Lehighton is home to a variety of museums, parks, and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into the area’s rich history and culture.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lehigh Canal Park: This park offers visitors the chance to learn about the region’s vital role in the canal era, with exhibits on canal boat building, canal boat life, and more.
  • Carbon County Historical Society: A museum dedicated to preserving and sharing the history of the Carbon County area through artifacts, documents, and exhibits.
  • Harry Packer Mansion: This ornate Victorian mansion is a must-visit for architecture lovers and fans of the novel “The Adventures of Tom Sawyer,” as the house inspired the home of Tom’s antagonist, Injun Joe.

Natural Attractions of Lehighton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glen Onoko Falls: This beautiful trail features several waterfalls that cascade down a rocky gorge, making it a popular destination among hikers and nature lovers.
  • Beltzville State Park: A sprawling state park with over 3,000 acres of land and a 950-acre lake, visitors can fish, boat, hike or sunbathe on the beach.
  • Blue Mountain: A popular destination for skiing and snowboarding in the winter, as well as hiking, mountain biking, and scenic chairlift rides during the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lehighton

Lehighton offers plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors, with a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy.

  • Switchback Trail: A popular hiking trail in Lehighton that is known for its stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • D&L Trail: This trail runs through the Lehigh Valley, much of which is part of the Delaware and Lehigh National Heritage Corridor. This trail is great for biking but, it can accommodate runners and walkers too.
  • Stony Creek Rail Trail: This trail follows a former railroad route, and visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk surrounded by stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Lehighton: Activities and Tours

  • Flagstaff Mountain Park: Great for family-oriented hands-on exploration and guided nature hikes.
  • Lehigh River Adventures: There are different packages ranging from river trips to canoeing, kayaking, and stand-up paddleboarding.
  • Mahoning Valley Speedway: A thrilling experience for adrenaline junkies, visitors can watch stock car racers and even test their driving skills behind the wheel.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lehighton

Day 1: Visit the Lehigh Canal Park Day 2: Switchback Trail Day 3: Carbon County Historical Society Day 4: Glen Onoko Falls Day 5: Mahoning Valley Speedway Day 6: Buy souvenirs from shops and stores around the town Day 7: Explore the Beltzville State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Lehighton

Lehighton offers a wide variety of local favorites that visitors won’t want to miss, including traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dishes and local wine made from grapes grown in the surrounding valleys.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shoofly pie: A molasses and crumb pie.
  • Lebanon Bologna: A type of cured, smoked sausage that is a Pennsylvania Dutch speciality.
  • Pierogies: A pocket of dough that may be filled with several different ingredients. With Central European origins, its popularity is extraordinary in many American cities.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: America’s oldest brewery is situated in Pottsville, which is an hour and a half drive from Lehighton. Revelers make that trip since the brewery’s product is irresistible.
  • Pinnacle Ridge Winery: This winery is located in Kutztown, a short drive from Lehighton. It is notable since its wines are made from grapes grown locally.

Where to Stay in Lehighton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lehighton has several hotels, motels, and guesthouses that cater to various budgets and preferences. Here are a few notable suggestions.

  • Country Inn and Suites by Carlson: A comfortable option for families and groups of friends.
  • Hampton Inn, Lehighton-Jim Thorpe: Great for business travelers.
  • The Mansion House: Offering suites, single rooms and complete facilities, it can accommodate family-oriented visitors.

What to Buy in Lehighton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Hex Signs: The hex signs are colorful, circular designs seen on houses, barns, and other buildings of the Pennsylvanian countryside. They represent a combination of traditions originating from various sections of Pennsylvania’s mixed immigrant heritage.
  • Pierogies: Pierogies could be taken home, heated, and enjoyed with friends and family.
  • Cotton Creative Stationery: A stationery store selling artistic designs and local posters.

Local Culture and Customs of Lehighton

Lehighton has a rich cultural heritage and many traditions celebrated throughout the year. The locals also take pride in their Pennsylvania Dutch roots and their unique cuisine and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Lehighton

The ideal time to visit Lehighton is during the fall, when the leaves on the trees change colors, creating a picturesque scene in the surrounding area. Additionally, winter and summer provide leisure options that appeal to different types of visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lehighton

Do:

  • Explore the local shops and stores in Lehighton and learn more about the area’s unique culture and history.
  • Try out the local cuisine, including Pennsylvania Dutch specialties like shoofly pie and Lebanon bologna.
  • Hike or bike on the many trails around the city.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the natural attractions or landmarks in Lehighton.
  • Engage in any unsafe activities or outdoor adventures without proper equipment and training.
  • Disrespect local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Lehighton is a safe place to visit, and there are no significant health or safety concerns for visitors. However, it’s important to keep in mind the usual safety precautions while traveling.

Safety in Lehighton

  • Always lock cars and hotel rooms.

Getting to Lehighton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lehighton is accessible by several modes of transportation, including plane, bus and car.

Airports Near Lehighton

  • Allentown International Airport

Bus Stations Near Lehighton

How to Get Around in Lehighton

Lehighton is a compact town and best explored on foot or by bike. However, there are car rental options also available in the town. There is good public transport service in Lehighton, but, it’s better to plan accordingly beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Lehighton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jim Thorpe: “The Switzerland of America,” is an irresistible gem, located just a few miles southeast of Lehighton. Jim Thorpe is known for its scenic views, Victorian architecture, and outdoor recreational activities.
  • Kutztown: A beautiful town full of Victorian-era buildings, museums, and art galleries.
  • Bethlehem: This historic town is known for its rich culture and architecture.

Conclusion

Lehighton is full of stunning natural beauty, fascinating history and culture, and exciting experiences for visitors of all ages and interests. By following this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can learn more about what Lehighton has to offer, plan their itinerary, and make the most of their visit to this beautiful part of Pennsylvania.
