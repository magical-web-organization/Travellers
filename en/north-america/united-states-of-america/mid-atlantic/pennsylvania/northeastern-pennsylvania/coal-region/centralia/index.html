Introduction to Centralia

Centralia is a small borough located in the Coal Region of Northeastern Pennsylvania in the United States of America. The town gained notoriety for the underground mine fire that has been burning since 1962, resulting in the displacement of most residents. Despite its tragic history, Centralia continues to attract tourists with its unique attractions and stunning landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Centralia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Centralia Mine Fire Site: The Centralia Mine Fire Site is located in a fenced-off area along Route 61. It contains an active coal seam fire that has been burning since 1962. Visitors can see the smoke and steam rising from the ground and learn about the history and impact of the mine fire.

2. St. Ignatius Cemetery: The St. Ignatius Cemetery is the final resting place of many of Centralia’s former residents. The cemetery has been largely abandoned due to the mine fire, and the surrounding woods have encroached on the headstones. Visitors can see the eerie sight of the smoke rising from the ground in the distance while exploring the cemetery.

Natural Attractions of Centralia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Girard Park: Girard Park is a small park located in the heart of Centralia. It offers a beautiful view of the surrounding hills and a playground for children.

2. Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine and Steam Train: The Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine and Steam Train is located a short drive from Centralia in neighboring Ashland. Visitors can take a tour of a coal mine and ride an authentic steam train through original mine tunnels.

3. Locust Lake State Park: Locust Lake State Park is located about 30 minutes from Centralia and offers hiking trails, swimming, fishing, and camping. It is a popular destination for visitors looking to enjoy the natural beauty of Pennsylvania.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Centralia

Centralia is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some notable trails include:

1. Schuylkill River Trail: The Schuylkill River Trail is a 130-mile trail that passes through many historic towns and landscapes, including Centralia. The trail offers stunning views of the Schuylkill River and surrounding forests and is relatively flat, making it perfect for bikers of all skill levels.

2. Glen Onoko Falls Trail: The Glen Onoko Falls Trail is a challenging, 3-mile hike located about 40 minutes from Centralia. The trail passes through a beautiful gorge and leads to stunning waterfall views.

Best Things to Do in Centralia: Activities and Tours

1. Centralia Ghost Town Tours: Centralia Ghost Town Tours offer a unique opportunity to explore the abandoned streets of Centralia and learn about the town’s history and culture.

2. Coal Region Racing Experience: The Coal Region Racing Experience is a one-of-a-kind experience that allows visitors to ride along in a real race car and experience the thrill of racing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Centralia

Enjoy a week-long stay in Centralia with the following itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Centralia Mine Fire Site and St. Ignatius Cemetery

Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride along the Schuylkill River Trail

Day 3: Visit Girard Park and Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine and Steam Train

Day 4: Take the Coal Region Racing Experience

Day 5: Explore nearby communities and attractions, such as Ashland and the Yuengling Brewery

Day 6: Enjoy a day at Locust Lake State Park

Day 7: Take a Centralia Ghost Town Tour and visit any attractions you missed earlier in the week

Food and Drink Scene of Centralia

Must-Try Dishes

1. Pierogies: Pierogies are a staple of Eastern European cuisine and are a must-try dish in Centralia. These dumplings are typically filled with potatoes and cheese or sauerkraut.

2. Scrapple: Scrapple is a Pennsylvania Dutch specialty made from pork scraps and cornmeal. It is often served for breakfast and is a favorite among locals.

Local Beverages

1. Yuengling Beer: Yuengling Beer is America’s oldest brewery and is located in nearby Pottsville. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and sample some of its award-winning beers.

2. Sarsaparilla: Sarsaparilla is a sweet, carbonated beverage that was invented in Centralia in the early 20th century. It is still a popular drink among locals and can be found in many establishments around the town.

Where to Stay in Centralia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Centralia Motel: The Centralia Motel is a family-owned motel located in the heart of town. It offers clean, comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

2. Carriage House Bed and Breakfast: The Carriage House Bed and Breakfast is located about 20 minutes from Centralia in neighboring Bloomsburg. It offers a charming, historic setting and delightful breakfasts.

What to Buy in Centralia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Centralia Mine Fire Memorabilia: Visitors can purchase t-shirts, mugs, and other merchandise related to the Centralia Mine Fire as a unique souvenir.

2. Coal Region Crafts: Local artisans produce a variety of crafts that reflect the area’s coal mining history, such as coal sculptures and wooden mining tools.

Local Culture and Customs of Centralia

Centralia has a rich history and has been shaped by the coal mining industry. Residents are proud of their heritage and maintain many customs and traditions related to the area’s history.

Best Time to Visit Centralia

The best time to visit Centralia is in the summer months when temperatures are warm, and outdoor activities are plentiful. However, visitors can also enjoy year-round activities and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Centralia

Do:

  • Respect the residents of Centralia and their property
  • Stay on designated trails and areas in the state park
  • Take a Centralia Ghost Town Tour to learn more about the town’s history

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property
  • Start fires or smoke in non-designated areas
  • Ignore safety warnings regarding the mine fire

Safety and Health

Safety in Centralia

Visitors to Centralia should take care to follow all safety guidelines related to the mine fire, including staying away from marked areas and not starting fires in non-designated areas.

Health in Centralia

While the mine fire has not been linked to any serious health issues, visitors should take care to stay away from smoke and fumes when visiting the area.

Getting to Centralia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Centralia

The nearest airports to Centralia are the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport and the Harrisburg International Airport, both located about an hour’s drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Centralia

Greyhound and Martz Trailways provide bus service to nearby towns such as Bloomsburg and Frackville.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Centralia using the nearby highways or rent a car from one of the many rental car agencies in the region.

How to Get Around in Centralia

The best way to get around Centralia and the surrounding areas is by car. Rental cars are available, or visitors can bring their own vehicles.

Where to Go Next from Centralia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby towns such as Bloomsburg and Pottsville, or head to larger cities such as Philadelphia or New York City for more urban experiences.

Conclusion

Centralia is a unique and fascinating town that offers a glimpse into Pennsylvania’s coal mining history. Visitors can explore the town’s abandoned streets, hike through beautiful landscapes, and sample local cuisine and beverages. With plenty of attractions and activities, Centralia is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Northeastern Pennsylvania.
