Introduction to Clarks Summit

Clarks Summit is a charming borough located in the Coal Region of Northeastern Pennsylvania. This small town offers visitors a glimpse of the authentic American experience, with beautiful natural surroundings, rich history, and a vibrant cultural scene. Clarks Summit has something for everyone, from exciting outdoor adventures to delicious local cuisine. Plan your trip to this hidden gem and discover the best of what Clarks Summit has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Clarks Summit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture vultures, Clarks Summit offers a wealth of attractions that showcase the town’s rich past. Begin your journey at the Everhart Museum, a fascinating museum that showcases over 15,000 natural history, science, and art objects. Visit the Lackawanna Historical Society to learn about the history of the region, or explore the Steamtown National Historic Site, which offers guided tours of the steam locomotive shop complex.

Natural Attractions of Clarks Summit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Clarks Summit is surrounded by nature, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. For breathtaking views, hike to the top of the Lackawanna State Park or take a leisurely stroll along the Lackawanna River Heritage Trail. Go fishing at Lake Winola, or kayak along the Susquehanna River. Visit the Montage Mountain Ski Resort for a fun day on the slopes or head to the Elk Mountain Ski Resort for some of the best skiing in the state.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clarks Summit

Clarks Summit offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. The Lackawanna State Park has miles of trails that wind through forests and around lakes. The Trolley Trail is a popular biking route that takes you through downtown Scranton. The Abington Trail is a scenic trail that takes you through wetlands and by historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Clarks Summit: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to choose from in Clarks Summit. Take a ghost tour of the historic buildings in the town, go on a wine-tasting tour at the nearby wineries, or take a hot air balloon ride over the surrounding countryside. Take a cooking class or go on a brewery tour to learn more about the local food and drink scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clarks Summit

If you’re planning to spend a week in Clarks Summit, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the town’s rich history by visiting the Steamtown National Historic Site and the Lackawanna Historical Society.

Day 2: Head to the Lackawanna State Park and spend the day hiking and fishing.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the rolling hills of Northeastern Pennsylvania.

Day 4: Go on a wine-tasting tour of the local wineries and enjoy a delicious lunch at a nearby farm-to-table restaurant.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Everhart Museum and enjoy some of the town’s rich culture and art scene.

Day 6: Spend the day at the Montage Mountain Ski Resort, skiing or snowboarding.

Day 7: Relax and pamper yourself at a local spa or try your luck at the Mount Airy Casino Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of Clarks Summit

Must-Try Dishes

Clarks Summit has a diverse food scene, with plenty of options for everyone. Don’t miss out on the town’s famous pizza, available at several local pizzerias. Try a hearty plate of pierogies, a local staple that can be found at most Polish restaurants. For a unique dining experience, head to the Cooper’s Seafood House, which has been serving up delicious seafood for over 70 years.

Local Beverages

Northeastern Pennsylvania is famous for its microbreweries and wineries. Try the local beer at the Susquehanna Brewing Company, Wallenpaupack Brewing Company, or Breaker Brewing Company. For wine lovers, the nearby Antler Ridge Winery and Nimble Hill Vineyard and Winery are must-visits.

Where to Stay in Clarks Summit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Clarks Summit has plenty of choices. The Hampton Inn Clarks Summit is a popular choice for travelers, with comfortable rooms and a convenient location. The Camelot Restaurant & Inn is a cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of the town, while the Comfort Inn & Suites is a great choice for families. For budget travel, try the nearby Host Inn All Suites Hotel or the Econo Lodge Clarks Summit.

What to Buy in Clarks Summit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

In Clarks Summit, you’ll find plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Check out Everything Natural, a health food store that sells local honey, organic soap, and other natural products. The Abington Artisans Shop is a great place to find locally-made art and crafts, from pottery to jewelry. For a unique gift, head to Adams Outdoor Advertising to make a custom t-shirt or mug.

Local Culture and Customs of Clarks Summit

Clarks Summit is a friendly town that celebrates its heritage and traditions. Residents take pride in the town’s rich cultural history, evidenced by the many museums and art galleries. The town also has a strong culinary scene, with a focus on locally-sourced ingredients and farm-to-table dining. Visitors are always welcome to join in local events and festivals, such as the Harvest Festival and the Scranton Jazz Festival.

Best Time to Visit Clarks Summit

The best time to visit Clarks Summit is in the fall, when the leaves change colors and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. The winter months are great for skiing and snowboarding at nearby resorts, while the summer months offer plenty of outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clarks Summit

Do:

  • Explore the town’s rich history and cultural scene.
  • Try the local pizza, pierogies, and microbrews.
  • Go on a scenic hike or bike ride on one of the town’s many trails.
  • Attend a local festival or event.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the town’s rich cultural heritage or traditions.
  • Leave your car or bike in the middle of the road.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clarks Summit

Clarks Summit is a safe town, with relatively low crime rates. However, it’s always important to practice safety precautions, especially when hiking or biking on trails. Pay attention to your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Clarks Summit

Clarks Summit has several hospitals and medical centers, including the Geisinger-Community Medical Center and the Moses Taylor Hospital. Visitors should come well-prepared with necessary medications and medical supplies.

Getting to Clarks Summit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clarks Summit

The nearest airports to Clarks Summit are the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport and the Lehigh Valley International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Clarks Summit

The Martz Trailways bus service provides connections to several nearby cities and towns from the Scranton Terminal, located just a few miles from Clarks Summit.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Clarks Summit is easily accessible from the nearby cities of Scranton, Wilkes-Barre, and Allentown. Car rentals are available at most major airports and in downtown Scranton.

How to Get Around in Clarks Summit

The best way to get around Clarks Summit is by car, as most attractions are spread out throughout the town and surrounding areas. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available. Biking and hiking are great options for exploring the town’s natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Clarks Summit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, consider taking a day trip to nearby destinations like the Pocono Mountains, the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area, or the city of Philadelphia. The nearby cities of Scranton and Wilkes-Barre also offer plenty of attractions and cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Clarks Summit may be a small town, but it’s full of surprises. From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural surroundings, this hidden gem has something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or food lover, Clarks Summit should be on your travel list. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty and charm of Northeastern Pennsylvania’s Coal Region.
