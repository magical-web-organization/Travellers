Introduction to Shamokin

Located in the heart of Coal Region, Shamokin is a small city in Northeastern Pennsylvania. It’s known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or culture lover, Shamokin has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, where to eat, local culture, and more in Shamokin.

List of Places to Visit in Shamokin

Here are the top places to visit in Shamokin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Anthracite Heritage Museum: The museum provides a glimpse into the Coal Region’s history, heritage, and culture. Exhibits include mining tools, photos of miners and their families, and videos of people sharing their personal stories.
  • Graffiti Highway: Located on Route 61, the Graffiti Highway is a half-mile stretch of abandoned highway covered in colorful graffiti artworks. It’s a popular spot for urban explorers and graffiti enthusiasts.
  • Shamokin Area Historical Society Museum: The museum houses a collection of artifacts, photos, and documents related to the history of Shamokin and its surrounding areas. Exhibits include Native American artifacts, coal mining tools, and vintage clothing.

Natural Attractions of Shamokin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ralpho Township State Park: The park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and a lake for boating and fishing. Wildlife, including bald eagles, can be seen in the park.
  • Knoebels Amusement Resort: Located in nearby Elysburg, Knoebels is a family-owned amusement park and campground known for its classic rides, boardwalk attractions, and delicious food.
  • Centralia: A short drive from Shamokin, Centralia is a ghost town that was abandoned due to a coal mine fire that started in 1962 and continues to burn today. Visitors can see steam rising from the ground and explore the town’s ruins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shamokin

Shamokin has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Shamokin Rail Trail: The trail is a 3.2 miles long rail-trail that starts at the Liberty Street Trailhead and ends at the West Cameron Township. It’s suitable for walking, hiking, and biking.
  • Bear Gap Trail: Located in nearby Ricketts Glen State Park, the trail is a 5.9-mile loop that offers stunning views of waterfalls, streams, and forests.
  • Mt. Carmel Riverfront Trail: The trail is a 1.5-mile-long paved trail that follows the Susquehanna River. It’s perfect for walking, jogging, and biking.

Best Things to Do in Shamokin: Activities and Tours

Shamokin has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained during your visit. Here are some of the best things to do in Shamokin:

  • Take a Coal Mine Tour: The Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine and Steam Train in nearby Ashland offers a fascinating underground tour of a real coal mine. Visitors can ride on an original 1930s narrow-gauge steam train and see a working hoist and mine equipment.
  • Go Fishing: Shamokin is home to several lakes and streams that offer excellent fishing opportunities. Some of the popular fishing spots include Lake Augusta, Lake Chillisquaque, and Shamokin Creek.
  • Attend the Bloomsburg Fair: The Bloomsburg Fair is an annual event held in nearby Bloomsburg that attracts over 400,000 visitors every year. It features agricultural exhibits, concerts, carnival rides, and delicious fair food.

Food and Drink Scene of Shamokin

Shamokin has a mix of locally-owned and chain restaurants and cafes that offer a variety of dishes and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: Pierogies are a traditional Polish dish made with dough filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, and sometimes meat or sauerkraut. They can be found at most local restaurants.
  • Halushki: Halushki is another traditional Polish dish made with egg noodles, cabbage, and sometimes bacon or kielbasa.
  • Pizza: Shamokin is known for its pizza, and there are several local pizza shops to choose from.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: Yuengling Beer is a popular beer brewed in nearby Pottsville, Pennsylvania, and is America’s oldest brewery.
  • Soda from Boylan Bottling Company: Boylan Bottling Company is a family-owned business that produces craft sodas using pure cane sugar and natural ingredients.

Where to Stay in Shamokin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shamokin has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Shamokin:

  • Holiday Inn Express and Suites: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Econo Lodge: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • The Inn at Turkey Hill: A charming bed and breakfast that offers cozy rooms, an on-site restaurant, and beautiful views of the nearby mountains.

What to Buy in Shamokin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring some souvenirs or local products home, here are some of the best things to buy in Shamokin:

  • Coal Mining Memorabilia: Shamokin’s rich coal mining history is reflected in the various coal mining artifacts, such as old mining lamps or coal carvings, sold in local antique shops.
  • Local Craftsmanship: There are several local artisans and craftsmen who sell handmade pottery, jewelry, or wood carvings at street fairs or small boutiques.
  • Regional Food and Beverages: Local delicacies, like pierogies or halushki, as well as Yuengling beer or Boylan sodas, make for great souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Shamokin

Shamokin reflects the history and traditions of the Coal Region, which was shaped by the coal mining industry and the immigrants who came to work in the mines. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Coal Mining Heritage: The town’s history is closely tied to coal mining, and the ancestors of coal miners are often seen as respected pillars of the community.
  • Polish Heritage: Shamokin has a significant Polish population, and Polish traditions, such as pierogi-making and Polish folk music, are celebrated in local festivals and events.
  • Small-Town Charm: Shamokin is a friendly and welcoming community, where people know each other, and visitors can feel at home.

Best Time to Visit Shamokin

The best time to visit Shamokin is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy. However, each season has its charm and presents different opportunities for outdoor activities or cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shamokin

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Shamokin:

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites to learn more about the town’s rich history.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the pierogies and halushki.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as celebrating Polish heritage.

Don’t:

  • Wander into abandoned buildings or mines for safety reasons.
  • Disrespect residents’ property or privacy.
  • Make negative comments about the local community or its traditions.

Safety and Health

Shamokin is a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take some common-sense precautions. Here are some safety and health tips for visiting Shamokin:

Safety in Shamokin

  • Stay on marked trails and paths when hiking or exploring the outdoors.
  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Shamokin

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • Check for ticks after outdoor activities to prevent tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Shamokin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shamokin is located about 70 miles northwest of Harrisburg and 130 miles northwest of Philadelphia. Here are some transportation options to get to Shamokin:

Airports Near Shamokin

  • Harrisburg International Airport: Located 60 miles southeast of Shamokin, the airport has domestic and international flights.
  • Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport: Located 60 miles northeast of Shamokin, the airport has domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Shamokin

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound bus station is in Hazleton, located 30 miles east of Shamokin.

Other Options

  • Train: The closest Amtrak station is in Harrisburg, about 70 miles southeast of Shamokin.
  • Car: Shamokin is located near the intersection of Interstate 80 and Route 61, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Shamokin

Shamokin is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by car. However, if you don’t have your vehicle, there are several taxi and ride-sharing options available.

Where to Go Next from Shamokin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Pennsylvania’s coal country, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Centralia: As mentioned earlier, Centralia is a ghost town that’s worth exploring if you’re interested in urban exploration and abandoned places.
  • Jim Thorpe: Located about 60 miles southeast of Shamokin, Jim Thorpe is a charming Victorian town known for its outdoor activities, historic architecture, and cultural events.
  • Ricketts Glen State Park: Located about 30 miles northeast of Shamokin, Ricketts Glen State Park offers over 26 miles of hiking trails, including the popular Falls Trail, which features 22 waterfalls.

Conclusion

Shamokin is a hidden gem in Pennsylvania’s Coal Region, offering a rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, history, or local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Shamokin. Plan your trip to Shamokin now and explore all that this charming town has to offer.
