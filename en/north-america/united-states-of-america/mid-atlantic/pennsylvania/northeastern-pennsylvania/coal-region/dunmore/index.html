Introduction to Dunmore

Located in the heart of Coal Region, in Northeastern Pennsylvania, Dunmore is a charming town with a rich history, beautiful scenery, and plenty of things to see and do. Home to a variety of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and great restaurants, Dunmore offers something for everyone.

Whether you’re interested in taking a leisurely bike ride through the countryside, learning about local history at a museum, or trying delicious regional cuisine, Dunmore is the perfect destination for your next vacation. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight some of the top places to visit in Dunmore, recommend exciting activities and tours, and provide helpful tips for making the most of your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Dunmore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, Dunmore has no shortage of great places to explore. Here are a few must-see attractions:

  • Anthracite Heritage Museum: Learn about the Coal Region’s industrial history and the hardworking men and women who made it all possible at this informative museum.

  • Eckley Miners’ Village: This authentic 19th-century coal mining town provides an immersive glimpse into the lives of miners and their families.

  • Steamtown National Historic Site: Located in nearby Scranton, this park is a tribute to the age of steam locomotives. It features restored trains, exhibits, and guided tours that explore the history of rail transportation in the United States.

Natural Attractions of Dunmore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a fan of the great outdoors, Dunmore is the right place for you. Here are some top natural attractions to explore:

  • Lackawanna River Heritage Trail: This scenic trail follows the course of the Lackawanna River, winding through forests and fields as it goes.

  • Bear Creek Preserve: This nature preserve boasts over 3,500 acres of pristine wilderness, including streams, wetlands, and forests.

  • Nay Aug Park: In nearby Scranton, you’ll find this sprawling park that features hiking trails, picnic areas, a water park, a zoo, and other recreational activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dunmore

With its lush forests, rolling hills, and scenic landscapes, Dunmore is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few recommended routes:

  • Dunmore Reservoir Trail: This popular 1.2-mile trail circles the Dunmore Reservoir and offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Lackawanna River Heritage Trail: As mentioned earlier, this trail stretches for over 70 miles through the region and provides an immersive outdoor experience.

  • Scranton-Lackawanna Heritage Trail: This 6.5-mile trail runs from Scranton to Taylor and showcases the history and splendor of the Lackawanna River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Dunmore: Activities and Tours

When it comes to things to do in Dunmore, the options are endless. Here are a few fun activities and tours to consider:

  • Kayaking on the Susquehanna River: Rent a kayak and explore the peaceful waters of the Susquehanna River, which runs through the region.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Taste the best local wines while taking in the scenic countryside and meeting local vineyard owners.

  • Shopping at The Marketplace at Steamtown: This recently renovated indoor shopping mall in downtown Scranton boasts over 60 stores and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dunmore

If you’re planning to spend a week in Dunmore, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Nay Aug Park and enjoy the zoo and water park.
2 Visit the Anthracite Heritage Museum and the Eckley Miners’ Town.
3 Take a wine-tasting tour and enjoy scenic countryside views.
4 Hike the Dunmore Reservoir Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch.
5 Go kayaking on the Susquehanna River.
6 Visit the Steamtown National Historic Site.
7 Relax and unwind at a local spa or winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Dunmore

Dunmore is home to some of the best restaurants and bars in the region. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: These potato and cheese-filled dumplings are a local specialty you won’t want to miss.

  • Scranton Style Pizza: This thin-crust pizza with sweet tomato sauce and plenty of cheese is a regional favorite.

  • Burgers: Get a taste of the local meat industry with a juicy burger made from locally-raised beef.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: This popular regional brewery is the oldest in the United States.

  • Coffee: Dunmore has a thriving coffee scene, with several local roasters and specialty cafes.

  • Soda: Try the local soda made with pure cane sugar at the Northeast Pennsylvania soda companies

Where to Stay in Dunmore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dunmore has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Dunmore Inn: This historic inn in the heart of downtown Dunmore offers elegant and comfortable rooms.

  • Radisson Lackawanna Hotel Scranton: This hotel in nearby Scranton offers plenty of amenities, including a pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Dickson City: This hotel offers spacious rooms at an affordable rate with amenities like an indoor pool and complimentary breakfast

What to Buy in Dunmore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home, consider these local products:

  • Pierogies: Bring home frozen pierogies from a local vendor to share with friends and family.

  • Local wine and beer: Pick up a bottle of locally-made wine to enjoy or share as a gift.

  • Coal-related memorabilia: commemorate the region’s history by purchasing souvenirs like posters, playing cards, historical coal tokens

Local Culture and Customs of Dunmore

Dunmore is home to a rich cultural heritage with a small-town charm. Visitors are expected to be welcoming and respectful of the local customs and traditions. The area is friendly and welcoming of outsiders, and visitors are expected to show a similar spirit of hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Dunmore

The best time to visit Dunmore is between late spring and early fall when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its best. The summer months are ideal for outdoor activities, while fall offers colorful foliage views. In winter, many outdoor activities may be challenging with snow generally possible from November through April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dunmore

Do:

  • Do treat the locals with respect, as they are known for their hospitality.

  • Do try to experience the local food and drink scene, which is unique to the region.

  • Do respect the environment by not littering or leaving trails removed.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be reckless with outdoor activities. Please follow posted rules and advice on weather alerts, and bring enough supplies.

  • Don’t dress too casual when visiting more formal sights, such as casinos or upscale restaurants.

  • Don’t be too loud or boisterous and respect the private space

Safety and Health

Safety in Dunmore

Dunmore is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Dunmore

Dunmore’s health care facilities are modern and well equipped. Several hospitals in Scranton offer a range of medical services, and many are associated with local universities.

Getting to Dunmore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dunmore

The nearest major airport is Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle to Dunmore.

Bus Stations Near Dunmore

Greyhound Bus Lines and Martz Trailways are the relatively comfortable and reliable public transportation options to reach Dunmore.

Other Options

Some visitors may choose to travel to Dunmore by car, using the area’s excellent highways and interstates. There are also several rental car agencies in the region, or taxis are available on request.

How to Get Around in Dunmore

Dunmore is a relatively small town, so walking or biking is the best way to explore most of the area. However, if you need to travel farther, renting a car or hiring a taxi is your best option.

Where to Go Next from Dunmore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider extending your trip:

  • Jim Thorpe: This charming town is famous for its Victorian-style architecture and historic sites.

  • Ricketts Glen State Park: This 13,000-acre park features 24 waterfalls, hiking trails, and camping areas.

  • Camelback Mountain: This popular winter sports hot spot is now open year-round, with plenty of outdoor activities for all seasons.

Conclusion

Dunmore, Pennsylvania, is a charming town with a lot to offer visitors of all interests. Whether you’re looking to explore local history and culture, hitting the outdoors for scenic views, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, Dunmore is sure to please. With this comprehensive guide in hand, you’re sure to have a memorable trip to this Coal Region town.
