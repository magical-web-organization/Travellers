Introduction to Ashland

Located in the Coal Region of Northeastern Pennsylvania, Ashland is a small town with a lot to offer. From scenic views and outdoor adventures to museums and historical landmarks, Ashland has something for everyone. This travel guide will help you navigate the town and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ashland

Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Ashland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine & Steam Train: Explore the history of coal mining with an underground tour.
  • Ashland Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine Museum: Learn about the science and history behind coal mining.
  • Anthracite Heritage Museum: Discover the history of the Pennsylvania Anthracite Region.

Natural Attractions of Ashland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Locust Lake State Park: Go swimming, hiking, boating, or camping in this scenic park.
  • Eagle Rock Resort: Ski or snowboard on the slopes, or play a round of golf on the course.
  • Ashland Reservoir: Take a scenic walk around the reservoir and enjoy the wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashland

Ashland has a variety of hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Schuylkill River Trail: A 130 mile trail that passes through Ashland.
  • Old Loggers Path: A 27.1 mile loop trail that offers scenic views of the area.
  • Mid-State Trail: A 520 mile trail that passes through Pennsylvania’s scenic regions.

Best Things to Do in Ashland: Activities and Tours

  • Ashland Community Pool: Cool off in the summer with a dip in the pool.
  • The Ashland Coal Mine Tour: Take a tour of Pioneer Tunnel and learn about the history of coal mining.
  • Ashland Area Farmer’s Market: Stock up on fresh produce and hand-crafted items from local vendors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashland

Here is an example itinerary for a week-long visit to Ashland:

Day Activity
1 Visit Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine and Steam Train
2 Explore the Anthracite Heritage Museum
3 Hike the Schuylkill River Trail
4 Visit Locust Lake State Park
5 Take a day trip to nearby Pottsville for a brewery tour
6 Ski or snowboard at Eagle Rock Resort
7 Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene

Food and Drink Scene of Ashland

Ashland has a variety of food and drink options, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies and Kielbasa: A staple of Polish cuisine.
  • Coal-Region Hot Wings: A unique take on chicken wings, with a spicy twist.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Brewery: The oldest American brewery, located nearby in Pottsville.
  • The Wooden Keg Tavern: A historic tavern with local beers on tap.

Where to Stay in Ashland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Pine Grove: A comfortable hotel just a short drive from Ashland.
  • The Ashland Motel: A budget-friendly option for travelers.
  • The Victorian Lady: A charming guesthouse in the heart of Ashland.

What to Buy in Ashland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coal Mining Memorabilia: Take home a piece of Pennsylvania’s history.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Browse local shops for unique pottery pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashland

Ashland is located in the Coal Region of Pennsylvania, known for its unique cuisine and storied history of coal mining. Be sure to try some pierogies and kielbasa and visit the Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine and Steam Train.

Best Time to Visit Ashland

The best time to visit Ashland is during the summer months when the weather is warm and the outdoor attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashland

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and visit the historical landmarks.
  • Take a scenic drive through the surrounding countryside.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or in abandoned buildings.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashland

Ashland is a generally safe town, but visitors should exercise caution and take normal safety precautions.

Health in Ashland

There are several healthcare providers in Ashland, including the Geisinger St. Luke’s Hospital nearby in Orwigsburg.

Getting to Ashland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ashland

  • Lehigh Valley International Airport (ABE): The closest major airport, about 60 miles away.
  • Harrisburg International Airport (MDT): About 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Ashland

  • Martz Trailways: Service from Pottsville to major cities along the east coast.
  • Greyhound: Service from Pottsville to major cities across the country.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: Several rental car companies serve the area.
  • Private car: Ashland is located near major highways, making driving a convenient option.

How to Get Around Ashland

Ashland is a small town and can be navigated on foot or by car.

Where to Go Next from Ashland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hersheypark: A popular theme park and resort complex located about 70 miles away.
  • Philadelphia: A major city with numerous historical landmarks, about 90 miles away.

Conclusion

Ashland, Pennsylvania may be a small town, but it has plenty to offer visitors. From historical landmarks and natural attractions to delicious local cuisine, Ashland is a wonderful destination for those looking for a unique travel experience.
