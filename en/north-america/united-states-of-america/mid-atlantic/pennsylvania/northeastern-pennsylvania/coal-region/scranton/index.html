Introduction to Scranton

Scranton is a charming town located in Coal Region, Northeastern Pennsylvania, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. Known for its rich history, Scranton offers a unique mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, exciting activities, and a vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Scranton

If you’re planning a trip to Scranton, here is a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lackawanna Coal Mine Tour
  • Steamtown National Historic Site
  • Electric City Trolley Museum
  • The Scranton Cultural Center at the Masonic Temple
  • Everhart Museum

Natural Attractions of Scranton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nay Aug Park
  • Lackawanna River Heritage Trail
  • McDade Park
  • Griffin Pond Animal Shelter
  • Weinberg Memorial Library

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scranton

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure, Scranton has a lot to offer hiking enthusiasts and bikers:

  • Lackawanna River Heritage Trail
  • Scranton Lake Loop Trail
  • McDade Park Trail
  • Laurel Line Trail
  • Roaring Brook Trail

Best Things to Do in Scranton: Activities and Tours

Looking for something fun to do in Scranton? Here are some exciting activities and tours you can try:

  • Escape the Room
  • Montage Mountain Resorts
  • Circle Drive-In Theater
  • F.M. Kirby Center for the Performing Arts
  • Electric City Escape

Week-Long Itinerary for Scranton

If you’re planning to stay in Scranton for a week, here is an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Explore the Lackawanna Coal Mine Tour and get a glimpse of Scranton’s rich mining history

Day 2: Visit the Steamtown National Historic Site to see vintage trains and locomotives

Day 3: Walk or bike along the Lackawanna River Heritage Trail and enjoy the scenic views

Day 4: Head to Nay Aug Park and experience the beauty of the park on the trails or enjoy a picnic

Day 5: Try some exciting activities like Escape the Room, Circle Drive-In Theater, or Electric City Escape

Day 6: Spend the day at Montage Mountain Resorts skiing, snowboarding, or tubing (depending on the season)

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a visit to the Everhart Museum or F.M. Kirby Center for the Performing Arts

Local Culture and Customs of Scranton

Scranton has a vibrant local culture that reflects its unique history and traditions. Here are some customs and cultural practices you should be aware of:

  • People are friendly and hospitable towards visitors
  • Respect the local history and traditions, especially in cultural and historical monuments
  • Proper etiquette and manners are appreciated in social gatherings and events

Best Time to Visit Scranton

The best time to visit Scranton is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities and events are in full swing. Fall (September-November) offers a beautiful foliage display, and winter (December-February) provides opportunities for winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scranton

When visiting Scranton, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Explore the natural beauty of Scranton

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute local parks and attractions
  • Disrespect historic monuments and museums
  • Drive under the influence of drugs or alcohol

Safety and Health

When visiting Scranton, you can be assured of a safe and healthy experience. Here are some tips to ensure your safety and health:

Safety in Scranton

  • Stay in well-lit and crowded areas, especially at night
  • Lock the door and windows of your hotel room properly
  • Avoid leaving valuables in your car or hotel room unattended
  • Follow traffic rules and road signals while driving

Health in Scranton

  • Wear insect repellent while hiking to prevent tick bites
  • Stay hydrated and use sunscreen while outdoors
  • Carry any necessary medications and consult a medical professional for any health concerns

Getting to Scranton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Scranton is easily accessible by air, bus or car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Scranton

There are two airports near Scranton:

  • Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport (AVP)
  • Lehigh Valley International Airport (ABE)

Bus Stations Near Scranton

Greyhound and Martz Trailways operate bus services to Scranton from major cities:

  • New York City, NY
  • Philadelphia, PA
  • Harrisburg, PA
  • Binghamton, NY

Other Options

You can also drive to Scranton, and car rental services are available at both airports and on-site in Scranton.

How to Get Around in Scranton

Getting around Scranton is easy and convenient. You can use:

  • Buses run by County of Lackawanna Transit System (COLTS)
  • Taxis and ride-hailing services like Uber and Lyft
  • Car rentals or Bike rentals

Where to Go Next from Scranton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to explore nearby destinations, here are some places you can consider:

  • Jim Thorpe: A quaint town known for its outdoor activities and scenic beauty
  • Bushkill Falls: A popular tourist attraction with breathtaking waterfalls and hiking trails
  • Pocono Mountains: A stunning natural paradise known for its skiing, hiking, and other outdoor activities

Conclusion

Scranton offers a unique blend of culture, history, natural beauty, and exciting activities.
