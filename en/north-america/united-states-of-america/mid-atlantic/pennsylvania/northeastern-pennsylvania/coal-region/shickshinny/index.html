If you’re looking for a charming small town with a rich history and a stunning natural landscape, look no further than Shickshinny, located in the Coal Region of Northeastern Pennsylvania. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor adventure enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in Shickshinny.

Introduction to Shickshinny

Shickshinny is a borough in the Luzerne County of Northeastern Pennsylvania. It is located 33 miles south of Wilkes-Barre, along the Susquehanna River. The town was founded in the early 1800s, and its name is derived from the Native American word “Schickshinnee,” which means “fine stream.”

List of Places to Visit in Shickshinny

Here are some of the must-visit places in Shickshinny:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shickshinny Historical Society Museum: Learn about the town’s rich history through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Stone Church: This historic church was built in 1844 and is on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Shickshinny Cemetery: Take a stroll through this historic cemetery, which is the final resting place for many of the town’s earliest settlers.

Natural Attractions of Shickshinny: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Susquehanna River: Enjoy kayaking, fishing, and scenic views along the Susquehanna River.
  • Shickshinny Lake: This tranquil lake is the perfect place for fishing, boating, and picnicking.
  • Pinchot Trail: Hike through the stunning Pinchot State Forest and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shickshinny

For those who love hiking and biking, Shickshinny offers some fantastic routes to explore. Some of the best include:

  • Pinchot Trail: This 26-mile trail runs through the Pinchot State Forest and is popular among hikers and backpackers.
  • Susquehanna Warrior Trail: This 12-mile trail runs along the Susquehanna River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Shickshinny: Activities and Tours

  • Shickshinny Farmer’s Market: Visit this local market to sample fresh produce and locally made goods.
  • Susquehanna Brewing Company: Take a tour of this award-winning brewery and sample some of their delicious beer.
  • Kayaking on the Susquehanna River: Rent a kayak and explore the scenic Susquehanna River at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shickshinny

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Shickshinny:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Shickshinny Historical Society Museum, Stone Church, and Shickshinny Cemetery
Day 2 Explore the Susquehanna River by kayak or take a scenic drive along its banks
Day 3 Hike the Pinchot Trail and enjoy the stunning natural scenery
Day 4 Visit the Susquehanna Brewing Company for a tour and tasting
Day 5 Take a day trip to nearby Ricketts Glen State Park for waterfall hikes
Day 6 Relax at Shickshinny Lake and enjoy a picnic lunch
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs at the Shickshinny Farmer’s Market and say goodbye to the town

Food and Drink Scene of Shickshinny

Shickshinny may be a small town, but it has a surprising number of dining options to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: These traditional Polish dumplings are a local favorite and can be found at many restaurants in town.
  • Coal-Region Favorites: Try traditional dishes like haluski (cabbage and noodles) and kielbasa (sausage) at local restaurants.
  • Cheesesteaks: Philadelphia may be known for its cheesesteaks, but Shickshinny has its own delicious versions, often served with locally made cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Susquehanna Brewing Company Beer: Sample some of the award-winning beers brewed by this local brewery.
  • Pennsylvania Wine: Pennsylvania is home to many excellent wineries, and you can sample some of their best wines at local restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Shickshinny: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a few options for accommodations in Shickshinny, including:

  • Pickett Hill Guest House: This charming guest house is located in the heart of downtown Shickshinny and offers comfortable accommodations.
  • Comfort Inn & Suites: This hotel is located just outside of town and offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Russo House: If you’re looking for a unique and offbeat place to stay, check out the Russo House, a renovated historic home with eclectic décor.

What to Buy in Shickshinny: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Shickshinny with these locally made souvenirs:

  • Handmade Crafts: Browse locally made crafts, such as pottery and woodworking, at the Shickshinny Farmer’s Market.
  • Shickshinny T-Shirts: Pick up a souvenir t-shirt at one of the town’s gift shops to show off your love for Shickshinny.

Local Culture and Customs of Shickshinny

Shickshinny has a rich and vibrant local culture, which is best experienced through its many festivals and events. Some of the most popular include:

  • Shickshinny Forward Day: This annual event features a parade, live music, and local food vendors.
  • Kielbasa Festival: This festival celebrates the traditional Polish sausage and features music, food, and craft vendors.

Best Time to Visit Shickshinny

The best time to visit Shickshinny is during the fall months when the leaves are changing and the weather is cool and crisp. However, the town is beautiful year-round, with plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in the summer and winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shickshinny

Here are some helpful tips to make the most of your trip to Shickshinny:

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the Susquehanna River and surrounding forests.
  • Try the local cuisine, including pierogies and coal-region favorites.
  • Attend one of the town’s festivals or events.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the hazards of hiking or kayaking, always be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions.
  • Ignore the rich history of Shickshinny, be sure to visit the town’s museums and historical places.
  • Leave without trying a locally brewed beer or Pennsylvania wine.

Safety and Health

Here’s what you need to know about staying safe and healthy during your trip to Shickshinny:

Safety in Shickshinny

  • As with any outdoor activity, be sure to take necessary precautions when hiking or kayaking to avoid any accidents.
  • Follow any posted signs or warnings when exploring natural areas.
  • Keep your valuables secured, especially when out of your accommodations.

Health in Shickshinny

  • Be aware of ticks while enjoying outdoor activities such as hiking.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring natural areas.
  • Be sure to stay hydrated while enjoying outdoor activities in the sun.

Getting to Shickshinny: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the best ways to get to Shickshinny:

Airports Near Shickshinny

  • Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport (AVP) is the closest airport, located about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Shickshinny

  • Martz Trailways and Greyhound both have bus stations in nearby Wilkes-Barre.

Other Options

  • If driving, take Route 11 or 239 to Shickshinny.

How to Get Around in Shickshinny

The best way to get around in Shickshinny is by car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Shickshinny: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Ricketts Glen State Park: This stunning state park is home to 22 waterfalls and offers excellent hiking opportunities.
  • Jim Thorpe: This historic town is known for its charming downtown and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Bloomsburg: Located 20 miles from Shickshinny, Bloomsburg is home to a popular farmer’s market and annual fair.

Conclusion

Shickshinny may be a small town, but it offers plenty of attractions and activities to keep visitors entertained and engaged. From its rich history and natural beauty, to its local cuisine and vibrant culture, Shickshinny is a must-visit destination in Northeastern Pennsylvania.
