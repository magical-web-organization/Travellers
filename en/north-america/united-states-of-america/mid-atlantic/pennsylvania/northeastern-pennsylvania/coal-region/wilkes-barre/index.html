Introduction to Wilkes-Barre

Wilkes-Barre is a charming city located in the Coal Region of Northeastern Pennsylvania. This city is rich in history and culture and surrounded by beautiful nature. Wilkes-Barre got its name in honor of John Wilkes and Colonel Isaac Barre, two British members of Parliament that defended the colonies during the American Revolution.

List of Places to Visit in Wilkes-Barre

Wilkes-Barre offers a wide range of attractions for every type of traveler.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Luzerne County Historical Society Museum: learn about Wilkes-Barre’s fascinating history at the museum.

  • Mohegan Sun Pocono: if you’re feeling lucky, head to Mohegan Sun Pocono for some gambling and entertainment.

  • F.M. Kirby Center for the Performing Arts: catch a show or concert at this stunning theater.

  • Frederick Stegmaier Mansion: tour this beautifully preserved mansion and learn about the family that owned it.

Natural Attractions of Wilkes-Barre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Susquehanna River: take a scenic stroll or a kayak tour along the Susquehanna River.

  • Seven Tubs Natural Area: explore this unique hiking trail with a series of seven potholes in the stream.

  • Lackawanna State Park: enjoy hiking, fishing, and camping at this nearby state park.

  • Frances Slocum State Park: another great nearby state park with a beautiful lake and plenty of outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilkes-Barre

Wilkes-Barre is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts with several hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Luzerne County Rail Trail: a 12-mile trail that offers beautiful views of the surrounding nature.

  • Susquehanna Warrior Trail: a 12-mile trail along the Susquehanna River that perfect for hiking and biking.

  • Back Mountain Trail: a 7-mile trail that winds through the beautiful Back Mountain region of Wilkes-Barre.

Best Things to Do in Wilkes-Barre: Activities and Tours

  • River Common: a great spot for a picnic, ice-skating in winter, and live music in summer.

  • Anthracite Heritage Museum: learn about the coal mining history of Northeastern Pennsylvania at this interesting museum.

  • Ricketts Glen State Park: a must-visit park featuring 22 stunning waterfalls.

  • Roba Family Farms: a popular farm with a wide range of activities, including hayrides, corn mazes, and apple picking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wilkes-Barre

Day 1: Explore the Luzerne County Historical Society Museum and take a stroll through the River Common.

Day 2: Take a hike at Seven Tubs Natural Area and visit the Anthracite Heritage Museum.

Day 3: Visit Mohegan Sun Pocono and try your luck at the casino.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Ricketts Glen State Park and get lost in the stunning waterfalls.

Day 5: Go apple picking at the Roba Family Farms before returning to Wilkes-Barre to check out a show at the F.M. Kirby Center for the Performing Arts.

Day 6: Relax at Frances Slocum State Park and enjoy some fishing or swimming.

Day 7: Explore the Back Mountain Trail and enjoy the beautiful views of surrounding nature.

Food and Drink Scene of Wilkes-Barre

Wilkes-Barre has a thriving food scene with many delicious local dishes worth trying.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: a local favorite, these delicious dumplings are filled with mashed potatoes and cheese.

  • Scrapple: a breakfast dish made of pork scraps and cornmeal.

  • Haluski: a simple but delicious dish made of sautéed cabbage and noodles.

  • Old Forge Pizza: a unique style of pizza that originated in Wilkes-Barre’s Old Forge region.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling: the oldest brewery in America is located in nearby Pottsville, and Wilkes-Barre inhabitants love to enjoy it at local bars.

Where to Stay in Wilkes-Barre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wilkes-Barre offers various accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • The Woodlands Resort: a luxurious resort offering spa services, golf, and casino access.

  • The Host Inn All Suites: a budget-friendly option that offers free breakfast and spacious suites.

  • The Beaumont Inn: nestled amidst the rolling hills and picturesque countryside, this B&B features beautifully appointed rooms, signature cocktails, and a farm-to-table restaurant.

What to Buy in Wilkes-Barre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Arts and Crafts: find unique and beautiful handmade crafts at the Wilkes-Barre farmers market.

  • Coal Region Favorites: take home some of the area’s famous coal snacks, like Wise Potato Chips.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilkes-Barre

Wilkes-Barre has a rich and vibrant culture, shaped by its coal mining history.

Best Time to Visit Wilkes-Barre

The best time to visit Wilkes-Barre is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilkes-Barre

Do:

  • Take a hike: the gorgeous trails around Wilkes-Barre are not to be missed.

  • Try the local food: the area’s unique culinary delights are a must-try.

  • Visit the museums: Wilkes-Barre has an incredibly rich history.

Don’t:

  • Litter: respect the natural beauty of the area and keep it clean.

  • Underestimate the weather: be prepared for varied weather conditions, especially during the winter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wilkes-Barre

Wilkes-Barre is a relatively safe city, but as with any travel destination, visitors should practice common safety measures, such as being aware of surroundings and locking vehicles.

Health in Wilkes-Barre

Wilkes-Barre is well-equipped with medical facilities and pharmacies in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Wilkes-Barre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wilkes-Barre is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options.

Airports Near Wilkes-Barre

  • Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport: the closest airport to Wilkes-Barre with direct flights to major US cities like New York, Philadelphia, and Chicago.

Bus Stations Near Wilkes-Barre

  • Martz Trailways Bus Terminal: located in downtown Wilkes-Barre, Martz Trailways offers bus services to major cities in the Northeast.

Other Options

  • Car rental: renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Wilkes-Barre

A car is the easiest way to get around Wilkes-Barre and its surrounding areas. However, there is also a public bus system, COLTS, which connects various parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Wilkes-Barre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many great destinations within a short drive from Wilkes-Barre.

  • Philadelphia: a 2.5-hour drive from Wilkes-Barre, Philadelphia is a vibrant city known for its history and culture.

  • New York City: a 2.5-hour drive from Wilkes-Barre, New York City needs no introduction.

  • The Poconos: a short drive from Wilkes-Barre, the Poconos offers beautiful nature and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Wilkes-Barre offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. No matter what type of traveler you are, there is something for you to enjoy in this charming Pennsylvania city. Start exploring Wilkes-Barre today and create unforgettable memories that will last a lifetime.
