Introduction to Coal Region

Located in northeastern Pennsylvania, Coal Region is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of historical landmarks, natural wonders, and rich culture. As the name suggests, this region played a vital role in the coal mining industry during the 19th and 20th centuries. Today, Coal Region invites tourists from around the world to explore its beautiful landscapes, rich heritage, and vibrant communities.

List of Places to Visit in Coal Region

Whether you’re interested in culturally significant destinations, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, Coal Region has something to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in Coal Region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eckley Miners’ Village: A former coal mining town that was abandoned but later restored to its original 19th-century condition. Today, the village offers a glimpse into the hardships and daily life of coal miners and their families.

  • Anthracite Heritage Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Anthracite Coal Region of Pennsylvania, including the mining industry, labor struggles, and cultural contributions of the people who lived and worked there.

  • Yuengling Brewery: The oldest brewery in the US, founded in 1829 and still family-owned, known for its signature beers and historic brewery tours.

Natural Attractions of Coal Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ricketts Glen State Park: A nature lover’s paradise, known for its magnificent waterfalls and scenic hiking trails that span over 13,000 acres.

  • Hawk Mountain Sanctuary: A popular destination for birdwatchers looking to observe raptors in their natural habitat. The sanctuary offers hiking trails, educational programs, and stunning mountain views.

  • Knoebels Amusement Resort: A family-friendly destination that features rides, games, and shows, as well as free admission and parking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coal Region

Coal Region boasts several scenic trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Schuylkill River Trail: A 130-mile multi-use trail that passes through several towns in Pennsylvania’s Coal Region, offering stunning views of the river and its surroundings.

  • Thornhurst Township Trail - Pinchot State Forest: A 21-mile trail that winds through the picturesque Pinchot State Forest, with several opportunities for hiking, fishing, and wildlife spotting.

  • Mine Field Trail at Hickory Run State Park: A 6.7-mile hiking trail that takes visitors through a former coal mining area, featuring old mines and mining infrastructure.

Best Things to Do in Coal Region: Activities and Tours

From brewery tours and live performances to outdoor adventures and unique experiences, Coal Region offers plenty of activities and tours for all ages. Here are some of the top options:

  • Yuengling Brewery Tour: Take a tour of the oldest brewery in the US and learn about its rich history while tasting some of its signature beers.

  • Pioneer Tunnel Coal Mine Tour: Explore a real coal mine and learn about the history and techniques of coal mining.

  • Coal Cracker Cruises: Take a scenic boat ride on the Susquehanna River and enjoy views of the surrounding mountains and landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Coal Region

Coal Region is known for its hearty cuisine and iconic beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: A savory filled dumpling that is a staple in Pennsylvania’s Polish communities.

  • Coal Region Hot Dog: A hot dog served on a roll with chili, onions, and mustard.

  • Potato cakes: A traditional side dish made with grated potatoes and onions, often served with sour cream or applesauce.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: The oldest brewery in the US, Yuengling is a family-owned and operated business that produces a variety of beers, including its signature lager.

  • Soda Pop: Pennsylvania is known for its unique varieties of soda pop that are made with real cane sugar, including birch beer and sarsaparilla.

Local Culture and Customs of Coal Region

Coal Region is home to a vibrant community that celebrates its unique history and traditions. Some of the customs and traditions that are characteristic of the area include:

  • Polish festivals: Pennsylvania’s Coal Region is home to several Polish-American communities that host annual festivals celebrating their culture and traditions.

  • Coal mining heritage: Many of the towns and attractions in Coal Region celebrate the region’s coal mining history, with exhibits, tours, and special events.

  • Yuengling beer culture: Yuengling beer is an iconic beverage that is deeply rooted in Pennsylvania’s culture and history, with a loyal following of fans across the country.

Best Time to Visit Coal Region

The best time to visit Coal Region depends on your preferences and interests. Summers (June - August) are the most popular time to visit, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities. However, fall (September - November) is the best time to see the region’s stunning fall foliage, while winter (December - February) offers the opportunity for winter sports and holiday festivities.
