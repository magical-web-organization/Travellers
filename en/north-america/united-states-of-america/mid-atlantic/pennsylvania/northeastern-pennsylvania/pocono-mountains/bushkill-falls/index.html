Introduction to Bushkill Falls

Located in the Pocono Mountains region of northeastern Pennsylvania, Bushkill Falls is a stunning natural wonder that attracts tourists from all over the world. Often referred to as the “Niagara of Pennsylvania,” it is a series of eight waterfalls cascading over 300 feet into a lush gorge. The 100-foot Main Falls is the most impressive among these eight and can be viewed from a specially designed platform. Beyond its natural beauty, Bushkill Falls offers amazing opportunities for outdoor adventures like hiking, biking, and fishing as well as cultural experiences like visiting museums and historical landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bushkill Falls

Day Activities
1 Visit Main Falls, Adams Creek Trail, and Grey Towers National Historic Site
2 Hike up to Dingmans Falls, explore the Pocono Indian Museum, and go fishing at Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area
3 Take an early morning ride through Bushkill Riding Stable before heading to Hackers Falls Trail and lunch at the Van Gilder’s Jubilee
4 Relax in the day with a guided tour of the Pocono Environmental Education Center
5 Get an adrenaline rush with a zipline experience at Camelback Mountain Adventures
6 Visit the Antoine Dutot Museum and Gallery, then take a guided tour through the Appalachian Trail
7 End the trip by taking in the beautiful views of the Kittatinny Ridge from the Mount Minsi Summit Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Bushkill Falls

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scrapple: Pennsylvania’s take on pork hash with cornmeal and spices
  • Pierogies: Traditional Polish dough dumplings filled with mashed potatoes and cheese
  • Shoofly Pie: Classic Pennsylvania Amish dessert with molasses filling

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: America’s oldest brewery located in Pottsville, PA
  • Kissel Hill Cider: Locally made hard cider using freshly pressed apples

Local Culture and Customs of Bushkill Falls

Visitors to Bushkill Falls can learn about the region’s Native American history and witness the living traditions of the Pennsylvania Dutch community. Pennsylvania is also known for its vibrant fall foliage, making it the perfect time to visit during the autumn season.

Best Time to Visit Bushkill Falls

The best time to visit Bushkill Falls is during the spring and fall seasons when the crowds are smaller, and the weather is pleasant. However, the waterfall is a stunning sight to see during the winter as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bushkill Falls

Do:

  • Wear comfortable shoes suitable for hiking and walking.
  • Bring a raincoat or extra clothes to stay dry in case you get wet from the waterfall mist.
  • Take pictures and enjoy the stunning views Bushkill Falls has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the park or disturb wildlife.
  • Don’t stray from marked trails or barriers. They’re put in place to protect visitors from potential hazards and maintain the natural beauty of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bushkill Falls

  • Check weather conditions before venturing out and avoid any dangerous weather.
  • Don’t hike alone and always inform someone of where you’re going before hiking.
  • Be aware of potential hazards such as rapidly moving water, steep inclines and declines, and slippery rocks.

Health in Bushkill Falls

  • Pack snacks and water to stay hydrated and energized.
  • Dress in layers for changing weather conditions.
  • Apply sunscreen and insect repellent to protect your skin from harmful rays and bugs.
