Introduction to Sterling (Wayne County)

Nestled in the heart of the Pocono Mountains, Sterling is a charming township located in Wayne County, Pennsylvania. Known for its natural beauty and rich history, this destination is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored by travelers.

Sterling is home to diverse attractions that cater to different interests, from museums and historical sites to picturesque hiking and biking trails, and unique food and drink experiences. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway surrounded by nature or a cultural journey filled with discoveries, Sterling has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Sterling (Wayne County)

To make the most of your trip to Sterling, consider visiting the following sites:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wayne County Historical Society: Learn about the rich history of Wayne County and its communities through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Dorflinger Glass Museum: Discover the art of glassmaking and admire the beautiful creations of Christian Dorflinger, one of America’s most renowned glassmakers.
  • Sherman Theater: Catch a live performance or movie screening at this historic theater, which has been entertaining audiences since 1929.

Natural Attractions of Sterling (Wayne County): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Promised Land State Park: Explore over 3,000 acres of forests, lakes, and streams, and enjoy activities such as hiking, fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Lacawac Sanctuary: Located in the heart of the Pocono Mountains, this nature preserve is a paradise for bird watchers and offers scenic trails that offer views of forests, wetlands, and lakes.
  • Shuman Point Natural Area: Take a leisurely stroll and admire stunning views of Lake Wallenpaupack, or challenge yourself with a hike through the rugged terrain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sterling (Wayne County)

Sterling boasts plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities, including hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Sterling Forest Trail: This 3.4-mile trail is perfect for hikers and mountain bikers who want to explore the natural beauty of Sterling Forest.
  • Lackawaxen River Trail: Follow this 7-mile trail along the Lackawaxen River and enjoy stunning views of the water and surrounding forests.
  • Bruce Lake Natural Area Trail: This 5.2-mile trail takes you through the scenic Bruce Lake Natural Area, where you’ll come across beautiful waterfalls and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Sterling (Wayne County): Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more unique experiences, consider the following activities and tours:

  • Wallenpaupack Scenic Boat Tour: Take a relaxing boat ride and admire the breathtaking beauty of Lake Wallenpaupack.
  • Koberlein Environmental Education Center: Learn about the region’s flora and fauna through interactive exhibits and programs.
  • Knockerball Poconos: Have a blast playing bubble soccer, human foosball, and other fun games with your friends and family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sterling (Wayne County)

To make your trip planning easier, consider using this sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Wayne County Historical Society and explore Honesdale, the birthplace of the American Railway.

Day 2: Discover the art of glassmaking at the Dorflinger Glass Museum and hike the Sterling Forest Trail.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive to Promised Land State Park and spend the day exploring its natural wonders.

Day 4: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Delaware River.

Day 5: Enjoy a leisurely boat tour of Lake Wallenpaupack and try your hand at fishing.

Day 6: Visit the Shuman Point Natural Area for a scenic hike and enjoy a relaxing evening at a local brewery or winery.

Day 7: End your trip with a visit to the Lacawac Sanctuary and a picnic lunch surrounded by nature.

Food and Drink Scene of Sterling (Wayne County)

Sterling also has a thriving food and drink scene that reflects its local culture and traditions. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Pot Pie: This hearty stew features tender beef or chicken and vegetables in a thick broth, topped with square-cut noodles.
  • Pierogies: These Polish-style dumplings are filled with potatoes, cheese, onions, or sauerkraut, and are usually served with sour cream or apple sauce.
  • Scrapple: This breakfast meat is made from pork scraps and cornmeal, and is often served fried with eggs and toast.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Lager: This is America’s oldest brewery and makes a variety of beers, including the famous Yuengling Lager, which has been brewed in Pennsylvania since 1829.
  • Maple Syrup: Made from the sap of maple trees, Pennsylvania maple syrup is a sweet and delicious addition to pancakes, waffles, and other dishes.

Where to Stay in Sterling (Wayne County): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sterling has plenty of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Settlers Inn at Bingham Park: This historic inn features beautiful rooms and a farm-to-table restaurant, and is located in Hawley, a charming town near Sterling.
  • Silver Birches Resort: This lakeside resort offers comfortable rooms, a marina, and a tiki bar, and is a popular choice for families and couples.
  • Lake Mocatek Inn: This cozy inn is located in a secluded area of Sterling and offers beautiful views of the lake and surrounding forests.

What to Buy in Sterling (Wayne County): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To remember your trip to Sterling, consider bringing home some of these local products and souvenirs:

  • Hand-blown Glass: Take home a piece of original art from the Dorflinger Glass Museum’s gift shop or one of the many local glassblowers in the area.
  • Maple Products: Sample maple candy, syrup, or cream from one of the many sugar houses in the region.
  • Local Crafts: Browse handmade pottery, jewelry, and other crafts at the local markets and stores in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Sterling (Wayne County)

Pennsylvania is a state with a rich cultural heritage, and Sterling is no exception. Take time to learn about the local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Pennsylvania Dutch: Many residents in the area are descendants of German immigrants who came to Pennsylvania in the 18th and 19th centuries, and they have retained their distinctive language, cuisine, and folk art.
  • Fall Foliage: Every autumn, the forests in and around Sterling burst into colorful hues, attracting visitors from around the world to admire the beauty of nature.
  • Hunting and Fishing: Hunting and fishing are popular recreational activities in the region, and many locals pride themselves on their skills and expertise.

Best Time to Visit Sterling (Wayne County)

The best time to visit Sterling is in the fall, when the leaves are changing colors and the weather is cool and crisp. However, summer is also a great time to enjoy outdoor activities and events, such as fairs and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sterling (Wayne County)

Here are some tips to make your trip to Sterling safe and enjoyable:

Do:

  • Hike in groups or with a trusted guide.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activity.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Ignore warning signs or safety precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sterling (Wayne County)

Sterling is a relatively safe destination for travelers, but it’s still important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Some tips to stay safe include:

  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Stick to well-lit and populated areas at night.
  • Stay alert while hiking and biking, and avoid unfamiliar trails after dark.

Health in Sterling (Wayne County)

The most common health risks in Sterling are related to outdoor activities such as tick and mosquito bites. To protect yourself, make sure to use insect repellent, wear protective clothing, and check yourself for ticks after spending time in wooded areas.

Getting to Sterling (Wayne County): Transportation Options and Logistics

Sterling is located about two hours from major metropolitan areas such as Harrisburg, Scranton, and New York City. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Airports Near Sterling (Wayne County): The closest airport serving Sterling is the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport, located about 50 miles south of the town.
  • Bus Stations Near Sterling (Wayne County): Martz Trailways and Greyhound offer bus services to nearby cities such as Scranton, Wilkes-Barre, and New York City.
  • Other Options: Renting a car or taking a taxi are also viable options, especially for travelers who want to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Sterling (Wayne County)

While there are some public transportation options in the area, such as buses and taxis, having a car is the most convenient way to get around in Sterling. Renting a car is relatively affordable, and it allows you to explore the region at your own pace and on your own terms.

Where to Go Next from Sterling (Wayne County): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spend in the area, consider visiting these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Jim Thorpe: This charming town is known for its Victorian architecture, outdoor activities, and historical attractions, and is located about an hour and a half south of Sterling.
  • Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area: This stunning natural area spans across Pennsylvania and New Jersey, and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, fishing, and boating.
  • Hawley: This picturesque town is located about 30 minutes from Sterling and features charming shops, restaurants, and galleries, making it an ideal day trip destination.

Conclusion

Sterling (Wayne County) is a unique travel destination that offers a combination of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Sterling has something to offer for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip, discover hidden gems, and make unforgettable memories in this beautiful part of Pennsylvania.
