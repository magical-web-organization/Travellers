Introduction to Hawley

Hawley is a charming town located in the Pocono Mountains, Pennsylvania’s northeastern part, known for its serene lake views, outdoor activities, and cozy small-town feel. It’s just a two-hour drive from both Philadelphia and New York City, making it an ideal weekend getaway destination.

Whether you’re a nature lover looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city or a history buff curious about the town’s past, Hawley has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Hawley, covering all the best things to do, places to stay, and where to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Hawley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hawley Silk Mill

Once a thriving silk factory, Hawley Silk Mill is now a cultural center that hosts a plethora of events, from concerts and art exhibitions to farmers markets and film screenings. The mill also has a cafe, bakery, and shops selling locally made goods, making it a perfect spot for a leisurely afternoon.

Dorflinger Glass Museum

If you’re interested in glassmaking and appreciate fine craftsmanship, then the Dorflinger Glass Museum is a must-visit attraction. The museum showcases a vast collection of glassware produced by Christian Dorflinger, one of the most significant glass manufacturers in the 19th century.

Zane Grey Museum

Step back in time and learn about the life of the famous western author, Zane Grey. The museum displays a wide range of artifacts and memorabilia, including Grey’s original manuscripts, photographs, and personal items.

Natural Attractions of Hawley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Wallenpaupack

The most well-known and iconic spot in Hawley is Lake Wallenpaupack, a 5,700-acre lake surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery. You can go fishing, kayaking, boating, or even jet skiing on the lake, or simply take a leisurely stroll on the shore.

Bushkill Falls

About 45 minutes from Hawley, Bushkill Falls is known as the “Niagara of Pennsylvania” thanks to its eight waterfalls that cascade through the lush forest. Hike through the park’s trails and enjoy stunning views of the falls and surrounding landscapes.

Prompton State Park

Located just outside of Hawley, Prompton State Park boasts over 1,500 acres of woodland and a 290-acre lake. Pack a picnic and spend the day hiking, mountain biking, fishing, or simply relaxing in the tranquil natural setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hawley

Lakeside Trail

This easy 2.5-mile trail takes you around Lake Wallenpaupack, offering stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains. It’s perfect for a leisurely walk or a bike ride with the family.

Tumbling Waters Trail

This challenging 3.5-mile trail takes you through the woods and along the Tumbling Waters Creek, with several river crossings and scenic waterfalls along the way. It’s recommended for experienced hikers or those looking for a thrilling adventure.

Wallenpaupack Creek Trail

This 3-mile trail takes you along the tranquil Wallenpaupack Creek, providing peaceful views of the water and surrounding forest. It’s ideal for a relaxing walk, and you can also go fishing in the creek.

Best Things to Do in Hawley: Activities and Tours

Hawley Lantern Tour

Learn about the town’s intriguing history in a unique way by taking the Hawley Lantern Tour. Dressed in period costumes, local guides will take you on a lantern-lit walk through the town, sharing fascinating stories from Hawley’s past.

Wallenpaupack Boat Tour

Experience Lake Wallenpaupack in a different way by taking a boat tour. You’ll get a chance to see some of the lake’s most beautiful spots, including secluded coves and stunning waterfalls.

Hawley Winterfest

If you’re visiting Hawley during the winter months, don’t miss the annual Hawley Winterfest event. The festival features ice sculptures, holiday shopping, live music, and plenty of food and drink.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hawley

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive at your hotel and take a leisurely stroll around town. Grab a bite to eat at The Settlers Inn, a charming restaurant with a cozy fireplace and a farm-to-table menu.
Day 2 Explore the natural wonders of Hawley by visiting Lake Wallenpaupack and hiking the Lakeside Trail. Afterward, head to the Hawley Silk Mill and enjoy coffee and pastries from Cocoon Coffee House.
Day 3 Take a day trip to Bushkill Falls and hike the trails of the “Niagara of Pennsylvania.” Return to town and have dinner at Glass-wine.Beer Bar, which offers a diverse menu of small plates and craft beers.
Day 4 Learn about the town’s history on a Hawley Lantern Tour and enjoy dinner at The Settlers Inn’s on-site restaurant, The Farmhouse at The Settlers Inn.
Day 5 Take a Wallenpaupack Boat Tour to get a different perspective of the lake. In the evening, catch a live performance at Harmony Presents, a performing arts center at the Hawley Silk Mill.
Day 6 Embark on a winter adventure by going skiing or snowboarding at Ski Big Bear or snowshoeing in the picturesque Prompton State Park. Warm up afterward with a cup of hot cocoa at the Cocoon Coffee House.
Day 7 Visit the Zane Grey Museum and learn about the life of the famous western author. End your trip with dinner at the Dock on Wallenpaupack, where you can enjoy a delicious meal and stunning sunset views.

Food and Drink Scene of Hawley

Must-Try Dishes

Scrapple

A Pennsylvania Dutch classic, scrapple is a savory breakfast dish made from pork scraps, cornmeal, and spices. Wilowtree Inn in nearby Stroudsburg serves a delicious version of this local delicacy.

Pierogies

A popular Polish dish, pierogies are filled dumplings typically made with potato and cheese or sauerkraut. Get your fix at Penny Lane Candies and Candles, a local candy shop that also serves homemade pierogies.

Trout

Thanks to the abundant waterways in the area, trout is a popular local catch. Try it at The Settlers Inn, where they serve it with seasonal vegetables and herb butter.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

With a thriving craft beer scene, Hawley is home to several breweries, including Wallenpaupack Brewing Co. and Irving Cliff Brewery. Try their locally brewed beers while enjoying live music and cozy atmosphere.

Wine

For wine lovers, visit the Antler Ridge Winery and sample their diverse selection of handcrafted wines made from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Hawley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Settlers Inn

One of the best places to stay in Hawley is The Settlers Inn, a charming inn with cozy rooms and an on-site farm-to-table restaurant. With its beautiful gardens and picturesque setting, it’s perfect for a romantic getaway or a peaceful retreat.

Ledges Hotel

Nestled in a historic building, Ledges Hotel offers modern, comfortable rooms with stunning views of the nearby waterfall. Equipped with a restaurant serving delicious small plates and handcrafted cocktails, it’s a perfect spot for couples or solo travelers.

Silver Birches Resort

Looking for a lakeside stay? Silver Birches Resort is situated right on Lake Wallenpaupack, with comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a private beach, outdoor pool, and on-site dining.

What to Buy in Hawley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Glassware

For a unique souvenir, head to the Dorflinger Glass Museum gift shop and choose from a selection of handcrafted glassware made in the area, including bowls, vases, and figurines.

Local Honey

Sweeten your trip with a jar of local honey. The Hawley Honey Company sells pure, raw honey made by local bees and offers a range of honey-based products such as candles and lip balm.

Local Culture and Customs of Hawley

Hawley celebrates its heritage through annual events such as the Hawley Winterfest, the Wally Wine Fest, and the Hawley Harvest Hoedown. The town also has a thriving arts scene, with live music, theater performances, and art exhibitions.

Best Time to Visit Hawley

Hawley is an all-year-round destination, with activities and events suitable for every season. The best time to visit depends on your preference - summer is perfect for water-based activities such as fishing and boating, while winter sports enthusiasts will enjoy skiing and snowboarding in the nearby mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hawley

Do:

  • Take a scenic drive through the picturesque countryside.
  • Try the local beer and wine.
  • Attend a live performance at the Hawley Silk Mill.

Don’t:

  • Litter in nature.
  • Ignore the town’s quaint charm and unique heritage.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hawley

Hawley is a small town, but like any other destination, it’s important to take basic precautions such as locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight. The town is generally safe, but it’s still wise to be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Hawley

Hawley has several medical facilities, including the Wayne Memorial Hospital, which is located 20 minutes from town. For emergencies, dial 911.

Getting to Hawley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hawley

The closest airport to Hawley is Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport, which is located about an hour’s drive away. Philadelphia International Airport and Newark Liberty International Airport are both just over two hours away.

Bus Stations Near Hawley

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Scranton, which is about an hour’s drive from Hawley. Martz Trailways also has a station in Scranton, with a daily bus service to New York City.

Other Options

The easiest way to get around Hawley is by car, but if you prefer not to rent a car, you can use taxi services like C&S Taxi or Uber.

How to Get Around in Hawley

Hawley is a small town, and the best way to get around is on foot. Biking is also a great option, with the Lakeside Trail being a popular route.

Where to Go Next from Hawley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Scranton

Just an hour’s drive from Hawley, Scranton is a fascinating city with an industrial past and a vibrant cultural scene. Visit the Steamtown National Historic Site, explore the Lackawanna Coal Mine Tour, or catch a live performance at the Scranton Cultural Center.

Jim Thorpe

Located about an hour and a half away, Jim Thorpe is a charming town nestled in the mountains, with plenty of outdoor activities and a rich history. Take a bike ride on the beautiful Lehigh Gorge Trail, visit the Asa Packer Mansion, or go whitewater rafting on the nearby river.

Conclusion

Hawley is a hidden gem in Pennsylvania, with plenty of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for an active getaway or a peaceful retreat, Hawley has something to offer. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this charming town.
