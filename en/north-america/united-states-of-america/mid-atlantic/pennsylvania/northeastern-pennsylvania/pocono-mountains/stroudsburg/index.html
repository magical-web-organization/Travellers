Introduction to Stroudsburg

Located in the picturesque Pocono Mountains of Northeastern Pennsylvania, Stroudsburg is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of outdoor adventures to offer. Founded in the 1700s, the town has a quaint downtown area filled with artisan shops and a variety of restaurants. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay during your visit to Stroudsburg.

List of Places to Visit in Stroudsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Quiet Valley Living Historical Farm: This living farm museum offers a glimpse into the daily lives of Pennsylvania Germans in the 18th and 19th centuries, with interactive demonstrations of farming, cooking, and crafts.
  • The Sherman Theater: Originally built as a vaudeville house in 1928, the Sherman Theater now hosts a variety of performing arts events, including concerts, comedy shows, and theater productions.
  • Stroud Mansion: This Georgian-style mansion houses the Monroe County Historical Association and offers guided tours of the home and museum exhibits focused on the area’s history.

Natural Attractions of Stroudsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area: A stunning 70,000-acre park that spans across Pennsylvania and New Jersey, with hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic outlooks. Accessible from Stroudsburg, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities like kayaking, fishing, and camping.
  • Appalachian Trail: Hike a section of the world-famous trail, which passes through Pennsylvania and offers stunning vistas of the surrounding countryside.
  • Mount Pocono and Camelback Mountain Resort: A short drive from Stroudsburg, these two popular ski resorts offer year-round outdoor activities like skiing, snowboarding, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stroudsburg

  • Brodhead Creek Heritage Trail: A 20-mile trail along the scenic Brodhead Creek, with access points in Stroudsburg and several other nearby towns. Perfect for biking or hiking, with plenty of spots for fishing and picnicking.
  • Dingmans Falls Trail: A 1-mile boardwalk trail that leads to the stunning Dingmans Falls, with several other waterfall viewpoints along the way. Easy to moderate difficulty.
  • Big Pocono State Park: A 1,300+ acre park that offers hiking and biking trails with panoramic views of the Pocono Mountains. Several trails of varying difficulty, with options for cross-country skiing in the winter.

Best Things to Do in Stroudsburg: Activities and Tours

  • Zip-lining: Take a thrilling ride over the treetops and scenic views of the Pocono Mountains with several local zip-lining companies, including Camelback Mountain Adventures and Pocono Zip Racer.
  • Wine Tasting: Take a guided tour of local wineries, including Barley Creek Brewing Company, Mountain View Vineyard, and Sorrenti Cherry Valley Vineyards. Sample delicious wines and learn about the wine-making process.
  • Ghost Tours: Explore the haunted history of Stroudsburg on a guided ghost tour, including stops at reportedly haunted buildings and cemeteries.

Food and Drink Scene of Stroudsburg

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stromboli: A local specialty, Stromboli is a delicious blend of Italian meats and cheeses baked inside a homemade dough.
  • Scrapple: A Pennsylvania Dutch breakfast meat made from scraps of pork and cornmeal. Often served with eggs and toast.
  • Pierogies: Polish dumplings filled with mashed potatoes and cheese, often topped with caramelized onions or sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: Founded in Pottsville, PA, Yuengling is America’s oldest brewery and a staple of the local beer scene.
  • Weyerbacher Brewing Company: A microbrewery located in Easton, PA, Weyerbacher offers a wide range of craft beers, including their popular Imperial Pumpkin Ale.
  • Pocono Brewery Company: A craft brewery located in Swiftwater, PA, Pocono Brewery Company offers a variety of beers on tap, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

Where to Stay in Stroudsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Penn Stroud Hotel: A historic hotel located in downtown Stroudsburg, with an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Stroudsburg Bartonsville: A modern hotel located just a few miles from downtown, with a fitness center and complimentary breakfast.
  • Airbnb: Several unique and affordable options, including cozy cabins and spacious apartments, are available through Airbnb in and around Stroudsburg.

What to Buy in Stroudsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antiques: Stroudsburg is known for having plenty of antique shops offering a variety of vintage treasures.
  • Handmade Crafts: Browse the artisan shops in downtown Stroudsburg for handmade pottery, jewelry, and other crafts.
  • Maple Syrup: Pennsylvania is known for its delicious maple syrup, and several local farms offer their own varieties for sale.

Local Culture and Customs of Stroudsburg

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Influence: Stroudsburg is located in an area with strong Pennsylvania Dutch roots, which can be seen in the traditional foods and crafts found throughout the town.
  • Outdoor Adventures: With easy access to the Pocono Mountains, outdoor activities like hiking and skiing are popular pastimes in Stroudsburg.
  • Community Events: Stroudsburg hosts several community events throughout the year, including the annual fall festival and farmers market in Courthouse Square.

Best Time to Visit Stroudsburg

The best time to visit Stroudsburg is during the fall or spring. In the fall, the town and surrounding area are vibrant with colorful foliage and festivals celebrating the harvest season. Spring offers mild weather and beautiful blooming flowers. The summer months can be busy with tourists and hot temperatures, while winter offers skiing and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stroudsburg

Do:

  • Do explore the outdoors with hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Do try the local food specialties like Stromboli and scrapple.
  • Do visit the local wineries and craft breweries.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the park or on hiking trails.
  • Don’t underestimate the time it takes to travel between attractions in the Pocono Mountains.
  • Don’t skip visiting the quiet and quaint downtown area of Stroudsburg.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stroudsburg

Stroudsburg is generally a safe town, but like any tourism destination, it’s important to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings. When hiking and engaging in outdoor activities, it’s important to have proper equipment and take precautions.

Health in Stroudsburg

There are several medical facilities in Stroudsburg, including St. Luke’s University Health Network and Lehigh Valley Hospital, Pocono. It’s also important to bring any necessary medications and be aware of any allergies when dining at restaurants.

Getting to Stroudsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stroudsburg

  • Lehigh Valley International Airport: Located in Allentown, PA, approximately 30 miles from Stroudsburg.
  • Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport: Located in Avoca, PA, approximately 40 miles from Stroudsburg.

Bus Stations Near Stroudsburg

  • Martz Trailways: Offers bus service to and from several locations in Pennsylvania and New Jersey, including New York City.
  • Greyhound: Offers bus service to and from several locations in the Northeast, including Philadelphia and New York City.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Several car rental companies have locations in Stroudsburg, including Enterprise and Hertz.
  • Uber/Lyft: Both ride-sharing services are available in Stroudsburg.

How to Get Around in Stroudsburg

Many attractions in Stroudsburg are within walking distance of the downtown area. There are also several taxi services available, including Pocono Taxi and Stroud Cab. Renting a car is recommended if you plan on exploring the surrounding area or visiting nearby attractions in the Pocono Mountains.

Where to Go Next from Stroudsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jim Thorpe: A charming town located about 30 miles south of Stroudsburg, with several historic attractions and outdoor adventures, including the Lehigh Gorge Scenic Railway and hiking trails.
  • Bushkill Falls: About a 20-minute drive from Stroudsburg, Bushkill Falls’ “Niagara of Pennsylvania” is a series of cascading waterfalls with hiking trails and zip-lining tours.
  • Bethlehem: Located about 40 miles southwest of Stroudsburg, Bethlehem is home to historic architecture, unique shops, and the annual Christmas City Village.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, historic attractions, or simply delicious eats, Stroudsburg is an excellent destination. Plan your trip using this comprehensive guide, and get ready to explore all that this charming town has to offer.
