Introduction to East Stroudsburg

East Stroudsburg, located in the beautiful Pocono Mountains of northeastern Pennsylvania, is a charming small town that offers an authentic and immersive travel experience. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine, it is a perfect destination for anyone looking for an off-the-beaten-path travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in East Stroudsburg

Below are some of the top-rated places to visit and things to do in East Stroudsburg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East Stroudsburg is rich in history and culture and has a number of museums and historical sites to explore. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Stroud Mansion: A historic house museum that has been restored to its original appearance. It is home to the Monroe County Historical Association and offers guided tours, exhibits, and educational programs.
  • Quiet Valley Living Historical Farm: Step back in time and discover Pennsylvania German rural life in the 18th and 19th centuries. The farm offers tours, workshops, and special events.
  • Dansbury Depot: A restored 19th-century train station that now serves as the headquarters of the Monroe County Historical Society.

Natural Attractions of East Stroudsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East Stroudsburg is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty and offers a wealth of outdoor activities and adventures. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area: This stunning national recreation area is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with activities such as hiking, biking, fishing, kayaking, and more.
  • Big Pocono State Park: Located on top of Camelback Mountain, this park offers panoramic views of the Pocono Mountains. It is a popular spot for hiking, picnicking, and skiing.
  • Pocono Environmental Education Center: A non-profit organization that offers a wide range of educational programs and activities related to nature and the environment. Visitors can participate in guided nature walks, bird watching, star-gazing, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Stroudsburg

East Stroudsburg has a number of scenic hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking views and a chance to connect with nature. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Appalachian Trail: The famous trail passes through the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area and offers stunning views of the mountains and the river.
  • McDade Trail: This multi-use trail is perfect for biking, running, and hiking along the Delaware River.
  • Levee Loop Trail: A 1.7-mile trail that winds through East Stroudsburg and offers picturesque views of the borough.

Best Things to Do in East Stroudsburg: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are a number of other activities and tours that visitors can indulge in during their stay in East Stroudsburg. Some of the popular ones are:

  • ShawneeCraft Brewery Tour: Take a tour of the local brewery and discover the art and science behind craft beer.
  • Shawnee Mountain Ski Area: Hit the slopes and enjoy some skiing, snowboarding, or snow tubing.
  • Fernwood Resort: A family-friendly resort that offers a wide range of activities including zip-lining, horseback riding, and mini-golf.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Stroudsburg

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to East Stroudsburg:

Day 1: Explore the historic downtown area and visit Stroud Mansion and Dansbury Depot. Day 2: Hike the Appalachian Trail in the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area. Day 3: Discover local flavors with a food tour or visit local farmers markets and vineyards. Day 4: Take a scenic drive through the Pocono Mountains and stop at Big Pocono State Park for a picnic lunch. Day 5: Experience the outdoor adventures and educational programs at Pocono Environmental Education Center. Day 6: Go skiing or snowboarding at Shawnee Mountain Ski Area. Day 7: Relax and unwind at Fernwood Resort with a spa day or family-friendly activities.

Food and Drink Scene of East Stroudsburg

The food and drink scene in East Stroudsburg is a mix of old-school classics and new, exciting flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: A staple of Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine, these delicious filled dumplings can be enjoyed at various local restaurants and cafes.
  • Barbecue: East Stroudsburg has a number of barbecue joints that offer mouth-watering smoked meats and sides.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: A local favorite, these homemade donuts are made with fresh apples and spices.

Local Beverages

  • ShawneeCraft Brewery: Located within the Shawnee Inn, this award-winning brewery offers a variety of craft beers made with local ingredients.
  • Hiawatha Lake: Enjoy locally sourced wines while overlooking the Hiawatha Lake at the Blue Ridge Estate Vineyard and Winery.

Where to Stay in East Stroudsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Stroudsburg has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the popular options include:

  • Shawnee Inn and Golf Resort: A historic resort located on the banks of the Delaware River, offering luxurious accommodations and a wide range of activities and amenities.
  • Staybridge Suites East Stroudsburg - Poconos: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable suites with fully equipped kitchens and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Woodfield Manor Resort: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of the Pocono Mountains, perfect for a romantic getaway or a family vacation.

What to Buy in East Stroudsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East Stroudsburg offers a variety of unique and locally made products that make for great souvenirs or gifts. Some of the popular items are:

  • Pottery: Artisan pottery made by local artists at various pottery studios and galleries.
  • Farm-fresh Produce: Local farmers markets offer fresh fruits, vegetables, and artisanal jellies, jams, and honey.
  • Handmade Soaps: Find various handcrafted soaps, lotions, and oils at local stores and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of East Stroudsburg

East Stroudsburg has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the Pennsylvania Dutch, Native American tribes, and early European settlers. The region is known for its historic architecture, local customs, and festivals, such as the Shawnee Mountain Rodeo and the SteelStacks Oktoberfest.

Best Time to Visit East Stroudsburg

The best time to visit East Stroudsburg is during the fall season, when the foliage turns into vibrant shades of red, yellow, and orange. This is also the time for apple-picking, pumpkin-patches, and harvest festivals. Summer is also a popular time to visit, as the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Stroudsburg

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Tip your servers and tour guides.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety rules and guidelines.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

East Stroudsburg is a relatively safe destination for travelers, with moderate crime rates and a low incidence of contagious diseases. However, visitors are advised to take basic safety precautions and follow health guidelines to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in East Stroudsburg

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings.
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Avoid walking alone at night and in secluded areas.

Health in East Stroudsburg

  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen during the summer months.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Follow health guidelines and procedures during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Getting to East Stroudsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Stroudsburg is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near East Stroudsburg

  • Lehigh Valley International Airport: Located in Allentown, PA, approximately 45 miles from East Stroudsburg.
  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located in Newark, NJ, approximately 75 miles from East Stroudsburg.

Bus Stations Near East Stroudsburg

  • Martz Trailways: Provides bus service from New York City, Philadelphia, and other regional locations to East Stroudsburg.
  • Greyhound: Provides service from multiple locations to East Stroudsburg.

Other Options

  • Driving: East Stroudsburg is easily accessible by car, with several major highways connecting the town to nearby cities and states.
  • Train: The closest train station is located in Stroudsburg, approximately 2 miles from East Stroudsburg.

How to Get Around in East Stroudsburg

East Stroudsburg is a walkable town with most attractions and amenities located within a few miles of each other. Visitors can also hire a car or use various shuttle and taxi services to get around the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from East Stroudsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East Stroudsburg is an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the popular options include:

  • Jim Thorpe: A charming historic town located approximately 35 miles from East Stroudsburg, known for its Victorian architecture and scenic outdoor attractions.
  • Bethlehem: A vibrant city located approximately 30 miles from East Stroudsburg, known for its rich history, arts, and entertainment.
  • Bushkill Falls: A stunning natural attraction located approximately 10 miles from East Stroudsburg, known as the “Niagara of Pennsylvania” and offering beautiful waterfalls and hiking trails.

Conclusion

East Stroudsburg, Pennsylvania, is a hidden gem that offers a unique and authentic travel experience. With its cultural heritage, scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine, it has something to offer for every type of traveler.
