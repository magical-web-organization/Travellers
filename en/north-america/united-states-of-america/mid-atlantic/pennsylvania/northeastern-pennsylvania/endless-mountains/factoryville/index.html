Introduction to Factoryville

Factoryville is a picturesque town located in Endless Mountains, in Northeastern Pennsylvania, in the United States. The town is surrounded by the beautiful natural wonders of Endless Mountains, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Besides its natural beauty, Factoryville boasts a rich history and culture that offers a diverse range of attractions and activities. From museums and historical sites to scenic hiking and biking routes, Factoryville has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Factoryville

Whether you are visiting Factoryville for a weekend trip or an extended stay, there is plenty to see and do in this charming town. Here’s a list of some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Factoryville Historical Society Museum: Explore the town’s history through photographs, artifacts, and exhibits showcasing Factoryville’s unique heritage.
  • Tunkhannock Viaduct: Known as the “world’s largest concrete railroad bridge” and listed on the National Register of Historic Places, this impressive structure spans over the Tunkhannock Creek.
  • Endless Mountains War Memorial Museum: This museum honors the memory of local veterans and showcases military artifacts and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Factoryville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lackawanna State Park: A great place for hiking, camping, boating, and fishing, this stunning state park covers over 1,400 acres of diverse terrain, including forests, wetlands, and open fields.
  • Endless Mountains Nature Center: This family-friendly center is an educational and recreational hub, offering nature trails, birdwatching, and various events and programs year-round.
  • Keystone College Nature Trail: A scenic, 4.5 miles long hiking trail that winds through the beautiful Keystone College campus and surrounding woods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Factoryville

One of the best ways to experience Factoryville’s natural beauty is by exploring its many hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Trolley Trail: This 9-mile trail follows the former Northern Electric trolley line and passes through beautiful forests, fields, and streams.
  • Susquehanna Warrior Trail: This 12-mile trail offers panoramic views of the Susquehanna River and the surrounding landscapes, passing through dense forests and towering rock formations.
  • Lackawanna River Heritage Trail: This 40 miles long trail connects many of the region’s historic sites and attractions, offering scenic views of the Lackawanna River and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Factoryville: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, Factoryville offers many exciting activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests, including:

  • White-water rafting: Join a guided rafting adventure along the Lehigh River, just a short drive from Factoryville.
  • Distillery tours: Visit the nearby Endless Mountains Distillery and sample their award-winning spirits, including whiskey, vodka, and gin.
  • Art galleries and workshops: Discover the local art scene at various galleries and studios around town, offering workshops, exhibitions, and classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Factoryville

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Factoryville, here’s a suggested itinerary that covers some of the town’s best attractions and activities:

Day 1: Visit the Factoryville Historical Society Museum and Tunkhannock Viaduct.

Day 2: Explore Lackawanna State Park and its many hiking trails.

Day 3: Take a scenic bike ride along the Trolley Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 4: Visit the Endless Mountains Nature Center and Keystone College Nature Trail.

Day 5: Join a white-water rafting adventure on the Lehigh River.

Day 6: Learn about the region’s art scene at various galleries and workshops.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local café or brewery, such as Nimble Hill Brewing Company or Chef’s Table Café.

Food and Drink Scene of Factoryville

Factoryville offers a diverse range of dining options, with everything from family-owned diners to upscale cafés and restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: These Polish-style dumplings are a regional specialty and can be found at many local diners and cafes.
  • Scrapple: This traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dish is made of pork scraps and cornmeal, fried to a crispy texture, and served for breakfast or lunch.
  • Chicken pot pie: A hearty comfort food made with chunks of chicken, vegetables, and creamy sauce, all baked under a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Pennsylvania wines: The region boasts many award-winning vineyards and wineries producing everything from dry whites to sweet reds.
  • Craft beers: Nimble Hill Brewing Company is a popular local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers and seasonal brews.

Where to Stay in Factoryville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Factoryville offers a range of accommodation options, from affordable guesthouses and motels to luxurious resorts and hotels. Some of the top-rated properties include:

  • The Inn at Trail’s End: This charming guesthouse offers a cozy atmosphere and comfortable rooms, as well as a delicious breakfast and beautiful garden.
  • Hampton Inn Tunkhannock: Located just a short drive from Factoryville, this modern hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a free breakfast buffet.
  • Stone Bridge Inn & Restaurant: This stunning lodge and restaurant offers picturesque views of the Endless Mountains, as well as cozy rooms, fine dining, and a variety of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Factoryville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or gifts to bring home, Factoryville offers plenty of options, such as:

  • Handmade crafts and jewelry: Various local artisans sell their products at craft fairs and markets throughout the year.
  • Local produce: Visit nearby farmers’ markets and orchards and stock up on seasonal fruits, vegetables, and honey.
  • Quilts and textiles: Many local shops specialize in handmade quilts, blankets, and other textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Factoryville

Factoryville is a friendly and welcoming town, known for its tight-knit community and strong sense of tradition. Some local customs and events to be aware of include:

  • Fall festivals: The region hosts many annual fall festivals, celebrating the bountiful harvest and the changing of the seasons.
  • Hunting and fishing: Hunting and fishing are popular pastimes in the area, and many locals take pride in their hunting skills and game meat.
  • Small town pride: Many locals are proud of their town’s history and culture, and enjoy sharing it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Factoryville

Factoryville is a year-round destination, with each season offering its unique charm and beauty. However, the peak tourist season tends to be in the summer months, from June to August when temperatures are warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also an excellent time to visit, with the stunning fall foliage and various harvest festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Factoryville

When visiting Factoryville, it’s always important to be respectful of the locals and their customs. Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities, depending on the season.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect private property and wildlife when hiking or exploring.
  • Be friendly and engaging with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave a mess, especially in parks and natural areas.
  • Disrespect hunting or fishing laws and traditions.
  • Criticize local customs or values.

Safety and Health

Safety in Factoryville

Overall, Factoryville is a safe and peaceful town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, as with any destination, there are some precautions to keep in mind, such as:

  • Watch out for wildlife, such as bears, when hiking or camping.
  • Avoid swimming in natural bodies of water unless it is marked as safe for swimming.
  • Secure your belongings when in public areas, especially during peak tourist season.

Health in Factoryville

Factoryville has several medical facilities and clinics that can provide emergency care and routine check-ups. However, it’s always a good idea to bring any essential medication and to have travel insurance coverage.

Getting to Factoryville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Factoryville is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here’s a rundown of the transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Factoryville

  • Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport (AVP): Located 30 miles away from Factoryville and offering various domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Factoryville

  • Martz Trailways Bus Station: Located in Scranton, approximately 22 miles away from Factoryville, offering daily bus service to various destinations.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Rent a car from one of the many rental companies located in nearby cities like Scranton or Wilkes-Barre.
  • Rideshare services: Uber and Lyft operate in this area.

How to Get Around in Factoryville

While Factoryville is a relatively small town, having a car or bike can be useful for exploring the surrounding areas and attractions. Taxi services are limited in the area, and public transportation is limited to buses.

Where to Go Next from Factoryville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip or explore nearby destinations, here are some suggestions:

  • Tunkhannock: Just a short drive away from Factoryville, the charming town of Tunkhannock offers a variety of restaurants, shops, and cultural attractions.
  • Pocono Mountains: Located less than an hour’s drive from Factoryville, the Pocono Mountains are a popular destination for skiing, hiking, and various outdoor activities.
  • Jim Thorpe: This charming town is approximately an hour’s drive from Factoryville and offers a rich history, stunning architecture, and many outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Factoryville is a hidden gem in Northeastern Pennsylvania, offering a diverse range of attractions, activities, and natural wonders.
