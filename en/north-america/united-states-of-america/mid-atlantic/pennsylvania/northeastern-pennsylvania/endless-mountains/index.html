Introduction to Endless Mountains

Endless Mountains, located in Northeastern Pennsylvania, is a charming region that offers a unique mix of natural beauty and cultural experiences. The area takes its name from the rolling hills, deep valleys, and breathtaking vistas that seem to stretch on forever. Visitors to Endless Mountains will find a wealth of outdoor adventures and cultural attractions to explore, making it the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Endless Mountains

If you’re planning a trip to Endless Mountains, here are some must-visit attractions you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Endless Mountains Heritage Region: This organization aims to preserve the natural, cultural, and historic resources of the Endless Mountains region. Visitors can explore historic sites, take a scenic drive, or go on a guided tour of the region.

  • Dietrich Theater: Located in Tunkhannock, the Dietrich Theater is a historic movie theater that has been restored and converted into a cultural center. Visitors can enjoy films, live performances, art exhibits, and more.

  • Wyoming County Courthouse: The Wyoming County Courthouse, located in Tunkhannock, is a beautiful historic building that has been in continuous use since 1842. Visitors can take a tour of the building and learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Endless Mountains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ricketts Glen State Park: This stunning park is home to 24 waterfalls and an extensive network of hiking trails that wind through the Endless Mountains. Visitors can also fish, swim, boat, or camp in the park.

  • Worlds End State Park: With its sweeping views, rugged terrain, and diverse habitats, Worlds End State Park is a paradise for hikers, wildlife enthusiasts, and outdoor adventurers of all kinds. Visitors can explore the trails, go fishing or swimming, or enjoy a picnic in the park.

  • Susquehanna River Valley: This beautiful river valley offers a mix of natural beauty and quaint small towns. Visitors can hike the numerous trails, go boating or fishing, or explore the charming shops and restaurants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Endless Mountains

Endless Mountains is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are numerous hiking and biking trails throughout the region. Here are a few of the best:

  • Lackawanna State Park: This park has over 18 miles of hiking trails, as well as a 4.4-mile bike trail. The trails wind through forests, fields, and wetlands, offering stunning views of the Endless Mountains.

  • Endless Mountains Trail: This 28-mile trail runs from Simpson in Lackawanna County through Susquehanna County, and ends in Lanesboro in Susquehanna County. It offers a mix of terrain, from high ridges to deep valleys, as well as numerous scenic overlooks.

  • Rails-to-Trails Conservancy: The Rail-Trail Conservancy has converted a number of old railroad beds into multi-use trails that offer excellent opportunities for hiking and biking. These include the D&H Rail-Trail, the Pine Creek Rail-Trail, and the Lehigh Gorge Rail-Trail.

Best Things to Do in Endless Mountains: Activities and Tours

Here are a few of the top things to do during your visit to Endless Mountains:

  • Fly-fishing: Endless Mountains is home to some of the best fly-fishing streams in the country. Anglers can enjoy fishing for trout in streams like the Lackawanna River and the Tunkhannock Creek.

  • Horseback riding: There are numerous stables and outfitters throughout Endless Mountains that offer trail rides through the stunning countryside.

  • Wine tours: The Endless Mountains region is home to numerous wineries, making it the perfect destination for a wine tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Endless Mountains

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Endless Mountains:

Day 1: Arrive in Tunkhannock, explore the town, see a show at the Dietrich Theater.

Day 2: Hike the trails at Ricketts Glen State Park.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive through the Endless Mountains Heritage Region.

Day 4: Fly-fishing on the Lackawanna River.

Day 5: Visit the wineries along the Susquehanna Wine Trail.

Day 6: Horseback riding through the countryside.

Day 7: Depart Endless Mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Endless Mountains

Endless Mountains is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with a focus on locally-sourced ingredients and traditional recipes. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogi: A staple of Polish cuisine, pierogi are pasta filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, and other ingredients. They’re a popular dish throughout the region.

  • Scrapple: A popular breakfast meat made from pork and cornmeal, scrapple is a local favorite.

  • Shoo-fly Pie: A sweet, molasses-based pie that’s a specialty of the Pennsylvania Dutch.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: The oldest brewery in America, Yuengling Beer is a Pennsylvania institution.

  • Wine: The Endless Mountains region is home to numerous wineries, offering a variety of varietals and styles.

Where to Stay in Endless Mountains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Endless Mountains offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are a few options:

  • Inn at Starlight Lake: This historic inn offers cozy rooms, delicious food, and stunning lake views.

  • Endless Mountains Lodge: A charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable accommodations and friendly service.

  • Camping: Endless Mountains is home to numerous campgrounds, offering everything from primitive tent sites to RV hookups.

What to Buy in Endless Mountains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Endless Mountains is a great place to shop for locally-made products and souvenirs. Here are a few ideas:

  • Pottery: The region is home to numerous artisans who specialize in pottery, from functional pieces to decorative works of art.

  • Antiques: Endless Mountains is packed with antique shops and flea markets, offering a treasure trove of unique finds.

  • Maple Syrup: The region is home to numerous maple syrup producers, and visitors can purchase locally-made syrup to take home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Endless Mountains

Endless Mountains is a friendly, welcoming region that embraces its history and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s natural beauty and cultural heritage, and be willing to immerse themselves in the local way of life.

Best Time to Visit Endless Mountains

Endless Mountains is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charms. However, the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Fall is a popular time to visit for its stunning fall foliage, while summer offers the best opportunities for outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Endless Mountains

Endless Mountains is a safe destination, but visitors should always exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Hiking and other outdoor activities can be dangerous, so it’s important to be prepared with proper equipment and supplies.

Health in Endless Mountains

Endless Mountains has excellent healthcare facilities, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid illness or injury. Be sure to bring any necessary medications and consult with a healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Endless Mountains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Endless Mountains is located in Northeastern Pennsylvania, making it easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Endless Mountains

The closest airports to Endless Mountains are Wilkes-Barre/Scranton International Airport and Binghamton Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near Endless Mountains

Greyhound and Trailways both offer bus service to Tunkhannock and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a popular option, as it allows visitors to explore the region on their own schedule.

How to Get Around in Endless Mountains

A car is the easiest way to get around Endless Mountains, but there are also numerous bike rentals and shuttle services available for visitors who prefer to travel by bike or on foot.

Where to Go Next from Endless Mountains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Endless Mountains is surrounded by numerous other attractions and destinations, making it the perfect starting point for a longer trip. Here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Pocono Mountains: Just a short drive from Endless Mountains, the Pocono Mountains offer a range of outdoor adventures, including skiing, hiking, and water sports.

  • Philadelphia: For a taste of big city life, head to Philadelphia, which is just a few hours drive from Endless Mountains. Explore the museums, historical sites, shops, and restaurants that make this city so unique.

  • Washington D.C.: For a truly grand excursion, head to the nation’s capital, which is just a few hours drive from Endless Mountains. Visit the Smithsonian museums, see the monuments and memorials, and experience the energy of this vibrant city.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure or cultural exploration, Endless Mountains offers something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and friendly welcoming community, this region is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Pennsylvania.
