Introduction to Washington

Washington is a beautiful historical city located in South Pittsburgh region, in Pennsylvania, in the United States of America. Founded in 1781, it was named after the first President of the United States, George Washington. Today, Washington is a bustling city known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful architecture, outdoor adventures, vibrant food scene, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Washington

Whether you’re a history enthusiast, nature lover, foodie, or adventure seeker, Washington has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Washington:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pennsylvania Trolley Museum: Take a ride on vintage trolleys and explore the history of trolley cars in Pennsylvania.
  • The LeMoyne House: A well-preserved 19th-century house that was a stop on the Underground Railroad, which helped escaped slaves reach freedom.
  • The Bradford House: A restored historic house museum that was the home of the Whiskey Rebellion leader, David Bradford.

Natural Attractions of Washington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mingo Creek Park: A beautiful park that offers hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and fishing ponds.
  • Frankfort Mineral Springs: A natural spring that is said to have healing properties and is surrounded by a scenic park.
  • Washington Wild Things Park: A minor league baseball stadium that also hosts concerts and other events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Washington

Washington is a great destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Washington:

  • Panhandle Trail: A 29-mile long rail-trail that passes through Washington and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Montour Trail: A 63-mile long trail that connects Washington County to Allegheny County and offers great biking and hiking opportunities.
  • The Gap Trail: A 150-mile long trail that runs through Pennsylvania’s most scenic landscapes, including the Great Allegheny Passage and the C&O Canal Trail.

Best Things to Do in Washington: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other fun things to do in Washington. From wine-tasting to museum tours, here are some of the top activities and tours to experience in Washington:

  • Washington County Covered Bridge Tour: A self-guided driving tour that takes you through some of the best covered bridges in the area.
  • Washington Winery Tour: A tour of some of the best wineries in the region, including Washington Winery, J&D Cellars, and the Winery at Wilcox.
  • Dancing in the Park: A weekly summer event that offers live music and dance lessons in the beautiful Town Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Washington

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Washington, here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the best things to do in the area:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the historic downtown area and visit the Washington County Courthouse and the LeMoyne House.
Day 2 Go for a hike or bike ride in Mingo Creek Park and have a picnic lunch. In the evening, attend a minor league baseball game at Wild Things Park.
Day 3 Take a wine tour of some of the best wineries in the area, including Washington Winery and J&D Cellars.
Day 4 Visit the Pennsylvania Trolley Museum and take a ride on the vintage trolleys.
Day 5 Explore the local food scene and try some of the must-try dishes, such as pierogies and haluski.
Day 6 Go for a drive on the Washington County Covered Bridge Tour and see some of the best covered bridges in the region.
Day 7 Visit the Frankfort Mineral Springs and take a walk in the scenic surrounding park.

Food and Drink Scene of Washington

Washington has a vibrant food scene, with many local restaurants offering delicious cuisine that reflects the city’s cultural diversity. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Washington:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: A Polish classic made with dough and filled with mashed potatoes, sauerkraut, or cheese.
  • Haluski: A dish made with cabbage and noodles that is popular in Eastern European cuisine.
  • Burgers: Washington has many great burger joints, including The Union Grill and Bubba’s Gourmet Burghers & Beer.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Washington has many local breweries, including Levity Brewing Company and Aurochs Brewing Company, that offer a variety of craft beers.
  • Wine: The region is famous for its wine culture, and there are many wineries that offer wine tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Washington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Washington has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and style. Here are some of the best places to stay in Washington:

  • Hyatt Place Pittsburgh South/Meadows Racetrack & Casino: A modern hotel located close to The Meadows Racetrack & Casino.
  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Pittsburgh - Meadow Lands: A comfortable hotel with a restaurant and bar, located near Tanger Outlet Mall.
  • The George Washington Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Washington.

What to Buy in Washington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Washington, here are some great options:

  • Washington Wild Things Merchandise: Buy a shirt or hat to support the local minor league baseball team.
  • Mineral Water from Frankfort Mineral Springs: Bring home a bottle of mineral water from the natural spring that is said to have healing properties.
  • Local Wine or Beer: Pick up a bottle of local wine or craft beer to enjoy back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Washington

Washington is a friendly city that welcomes visitors from all over the world. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Politeness: Pittsburghers are known for their polite and friendly manners, so be sure to say “please” and “thank you” whenever possible.
  • Tipping: Tipping is expected in restaurants and bars. The usual amount is 15% to 20%.
  • Sports Enthusiasm: Pittsburghers are passionate about their sports teams, so don’t be surprised if you see people wearing Steelers or Penguins gear around town.

Best Time to Visit Washington

The best time to visit Washington is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be cold and snowy. The weather is most comfortable in April, May, September, and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Washington

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Washington:

Do:

  • Do visit the Frankfort Mineral Springs, one of the city’s most scenic natural attractions.
  • Do try some of the delicious local cuisine, such as pierogies and haluski.
  • Do explore the city’s rich history and culture by visiting museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or smoke in public places.
  • Don’t ignore safety tips and guidelines, especially when hiking or biking in the area.
  • Don’t miss out on the beautiful outdoor scenery that Washington has to offer.

Safety and Health

Washington is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions to ensure your safety and wellbeing. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Washington

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep your belongings secure and be wary of pickpockets in crowded areas or tourist attractions.
  • Be cautious when driving on rural roads, especially during the winter months when the roads may be icy.

Health in Washington

  • Be sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as well as any necessary medication.
  • Visit a doctor or hospital immediately if you feel unwell or have any health concerns during your trip.

Getting to Washington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Washington is easily accessible by car, bus, or air. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Airports Near Washington: The closest airport to Washington is Pittsburgh International Airport, located about 35 miles away.
  • Bus Stations Near Washington: The Washington Greyhound Bus Station is located in the city center and offers multiple daily routes to other cities in Pennsylvania and surrounding states.
  • Other Options: If you’re driving, Washington is easily accessible from I-79 or US-19. You can also take Amtrak to nearby cities like Pittsburgh and then rent a car to drive to Washington.

How to Get Around in Washington

The best way to get around Washington is by car, but there are other options available, including taxis, ride-sharing services, and public transportation. The Washington Transit Authority offers bus services throughout the city for a reasonable fare.

Where to Go Next from Washington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Washington is situated in a beautiful area of Pennsylvania with many nearby destinations worth exploring. Here are some of the most popular excursions from Washington:

  • Pittsburgh: A vibrant and cultural city located just 25 miles north of Washington that offers a variety of attractions, including museums, restaurants, and parks.
  • Ohiopyle State Park: A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, Ohiopyle State Park features stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and rock formations.
  • Laurel Highlands: A scenic region that stretches across southwestern Pennsylvania and offers outdoor adventures, historic sites, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Washington, PA, is a beautiful destination that offers a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and a vibrant food scene. From wine-tasting to hiking to cultural tours, there is something for every type of traveler in this friendly city. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to Washington and explore all that this charming city has to offer.
