South Pittsburgh Region Travel Guide

South Pittsburgh Region, located in the Pittsburgh Region of Pennsylvania, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, is a destination filled with incredible natural beauty, rich history, and diverse cultural attractions. From its scenic hiking and biking trails to its renowned museums and historical landmarks, there is something for everyone in South Pittsburgh Region.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a tour of the region’s must-visit destinations, including popular attractions, hidden gems, and local favorites. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in South Pittsburgh Region.

List of Places to Visit in South Pittsburgh Region

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture aficionados, South Pittsburgh Region is a treasure trove of museums and historical landmarks. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • The Frick Art & Historical Center: This iconic landmark is a must-visit destination for art lovers and history buffs. The Frick Art & Historical Center comprises several museums, including the Frick Art Museum, the Car and Carriage Museum, and the Clayton mansion. Visitors can explore the center’s impressive collection of art, artifacts, and historical exhibits.
  • Carnegie Museum of Natural History: The perfect destination for families and nature enthusiasts, the Carnegie Museum of Natural History features a vast collection of minerals, fossils, and exhibits about the natural world. Visitors can also explore the museum’s famous dinosaur exhibit, which includes life-sized dinosaur skeletons and interactive displays.
  • National Aviary: Located in Pittsburgh’s North Side, the National Aviary is the perfect destination for bird lovers. The aviary is home to over 500 birds from around the world, including many endangered species.

Natural Attractions of South Pittsburgh Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South Pittsburgh Region boasts a wealth of outdoor attractions and breathtaking scenic views. Here are some must-visit outdoor destinations:

  • The Great Allegheny Passage: This scenic trail runs for over 150 miles, connecting Pittsburgh with Cumberland, Maryland. Visitors can hike, bike, or even horseback ride along the trail, enjoying stunning views of the region’s natural beauty.
  • Fallingwater: Located in Mill Run, Pennsylvania, Fallingwater is one of the most iconic buildings in the United States. The architectural masterpiece was designed by Frank Lloyd Wright and is built over a waterfall, providing a stunning and unique backdrop for visitors.
  • Point State Park: Located at the confluence of Pittsburgh’s three rivers - the Ohio, the Allegheny, and the Monongahela - Point State Park is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to enjoy the region’s natural beauty. Visitors can take a stroll along the riverfront, enjoy a picnic, or take in the park’s famous fountain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Pittsburgh Region

For outdoor enthusiasts, South Pittsburgh Region offers a wealth of hiking and biking trails, from scenic riverfront paths to rugged mountain trails. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • Three Rivers Heritage Trail: This 24-mile trail runs along Pittsburgh’s three rivers, offering stunning views of the city’s skyline and the region’s natural beauty.
  • Montour Trail: This 46-mile trail runs along an abandoned railway bed, offering scenic views of the region’s wooded areas, rivers, and historic communities.
  • Laurel Highlands Hiking Trail: This 70-mile trail winds through the heart of Pennsylvania’s Laurel Highlands region, offering stunning views of the region’s lush forests, rocky bluffs, and cascading waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in South Pittsburgh Region: Activities and Tours

South Pittsburgh Region has something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural activities and entertainment. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Go on a food tour: South Pittsburgh Region is known for its diverse and eclectic food scene, from classic American dishes to international cuisine. Visitors can take a guided food tour of the region to sample some of its best culinary offerings.
  • Catch a sporting event: Pittsburgh is home to several professional sports teams, including the Steelers (football), the Penguins (hockey), and the Pirates (baseball). Catching a game is a must-do activity for sports fans and visitors alike.
  • Explore the Strip District: Located just east of downtown Pittsburgh, the Strip District is a bustling neighborhood boasting a vibrant mix of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions. Visitors can spend an afternoon exploring the district’s colorful streets and sampling some of its tasty foods and unique wares.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Pittsburgh Region

To make the most of your visit to South Pittsburgh Region, consider planning a week-long itinerary that includes some of the region’s top destinations. Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Frick Art & Historical Center and the National Aviary.
  • Day 2: Hike or bike along the Great Allegheny Passage and visit Fallingwater.
  • Day 3: Explore Point State Park and take a riverboat tour of the three rivers.
  • Day 4: Visit the Carnegie Museum of Natural History and the nearby Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens.
  • Day 5: Take a guided food tour of the Strip District and explore the neighborhood’s shops and attractions.
  • Day 6: Visit the Andy Warhol Museum and the nearby Mattress Factory museum of contemporary art.
  • Day 7: Take a scenic drive through the region’s rolling hills and forests, stopping to enjoy the views and explore the region’s small towns and historic communities.

Food and Drink Scene of South Pittsburgh Region

South Pittsburgh Region is known for its diverse and eclectic food scene, featuring a mix of classic American dishes, international cuisine, and innovative culinary creations. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Primanti Brothers sandwich: A Pittsburgh institution, this hearty sandwich features grilled meat, cheese, coleslaw, and fries served on thick Italian bread.
  • Pierogies: This classic Eastern European dish is a staple of Pittsburgh’s culinary scene, featuring savory dough filled with potatoes or cheese.
  • Chipped ham: A Pittsburgh classic, this sandwich features shaved ham piled high on soft white bread.

Local Beverages

  • Iron City Beer: This iconic Pittsburgh beer has been a favorite of locals for over 150 years.
  • Wigle Whiskey: Pittsburgh’s first distillery since Prohibition, Wigle Whiskey offers a range of locally crafted spirits, including bourbon, rye, and gin.
  • Pittsburgh Punch: This local beverage is a sweet, fruity concoction made with lemonade, cranberry juice, and ginger ale.

Where to Stay in South Pittsburgh Region: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Pittsburgh Region offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  • Omni William Penn Hotel: This historic hotel is a Pittsburgh landmark, featuring elegant rooms, top-notch amenities, and a prime downtown location.
  • Ace Hotel Pittsburgh: This trendy boutique hotel is located in the heart of East Liberty and boasts stylish rooms, a popular restaurant and bar, and a cool, art-filled vibe.
  • Pittsburgh International Hostel: This affordable hostel is located in the heart of Pittsburgh’s vibrant Lawrenceville neighborhood, offering budget-friendly accommodations and a fun, communal atmosphere.

What to Buy in South Pittsburgh Region: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Pittsburgh Region offers a range of unique and distinctive souvenirs and local products for visitors to take home. Here are some top picks:

  • Hand-blown glassware: Pittsburgh is known for its rich glass-making tradition, and visitors can purchase one-of-a-kind hand-blown glassware crafted by local artisans.
  • Pittsburgh Steelers gear: Football fans should definitely stop by one of the many Steelers merchandise shops throughout the city to pick up a jersey or other fan gear.
  • Heinz ketchup: Pittsburgh-based food giant Heinz is famous for its ketchup and other condiments, which can be purchased at local stores and markets throughout the region.

Getting to South Pittsburgh Region: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Pittsburgh Region can be easily reached by plane, train, or car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near South Pittsburgh Region

Bus Stations Near South Pittsburgh Region

  • Pittsburgh Bus Station: Located in downtown Pittsburgh, the Pittsburgh Bus Station offers Greyhound and other bus services to destinations throughout the country.
