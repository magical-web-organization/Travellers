Introduction to Somerset

Nestled in the heart of Laurel Highlands, Somerset in Pennsylvania, USA is a beautiful destination waiting to be explored. The town is a perfect blend of history, natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The backdrop of rolling hills, pristine lakes, and lush forests make Somerset a paradise for hikers, bikers, anglers, and adventure enthusiasts. Let’s explore the best things to do and see in Somerset.

List of Places to Visit in Somerset

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Flight 93 National Memorial: This park commemorates the heroes of United Airlines Flight 93 that tragically crashed on 9/11. The memorial features moving tributes, exhibits, and a chapel.
  • Somerset Historical Center: The Somerset Historical Center showcases the history and culture of Somerset County through various artifacts and exhibits.
  • Quecreek Mine Rescue Site: The Quecreek Mine Rescue Site is a tribute to the nine miners who were rescued from an underground mine in 2002.

Natural Attractions of Somerset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laurel Hill State Park: This park features picturesque scenery, trails for hiking and mountain biking, and a beautiful lake for fishing, kayaking, and boating.
  • Kooser State Park: Kooser State Park is another great park in Somerset County. The park has scenic hiking trails, fishing, and canoeing on the lake.
  • Forbes State Forest: The Forbes State Forest offers miles of hiking trails, camping, bird watching, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somerset

  • Laurel Highlands Hiking Trail: This 70-mile trail is a must-do for hikers as it offers beautiful views and various camping options.
  • Great Allegheny Passage: The Great Allegheny Passage is a great option for bikers and hikers interested in exploring the Allegheny Mountains. The 150-mile trail connects Pittsburgh to Cumberland, MD.

Best Things to Do in Somerset: Activities and Tours

  • Motorcycle Touring: Somerset is a popular destination for motorcycle enthusiasts. The nearby mountains and winding roads offer thrilling rides and beautiful views.
  • Fishing: The area is known for its excellent fishing. You can cast a line in the local rivers and lakes for trout, bass, and other fish.
  • Ghost Hunting: Somerset has a haunted reputation, making it a perfect spot for ghost hunting tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Somerset

Must-Try Dishes

  • PA-style pizza: Sample the local pizza topped with provolone cheese, thick tomato sauce, and a crispy crust.
  • Perogies: These traditional Polish dumplings are a must-try in Somerset.
  • Pot Pie: A casserole with meat, noodles, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Mead: Enjoy locally brewed honey wine.
  • Craft Beer: The area is home to a variety of craft breweries, such as Whitehorse Brewing and Laurel Highlands Meadery.

Where to Stay in Somerset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Omni Bedford Springs Resort: This luxurious resort offers guests a spa, tennis courts, and golf courses, making it an ideal spot to relax.
  • The Georgian Inn of Somerset: This historic inn is located in the town center and features comfortable rooms and onsite dining.
  • Hemlock Lodge: Hemlock Lodge is a cozy cabin located in the woods, featuring a private hot tub and fireplace.

What to Buy in Somerset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Quilts: Somerset is known for its handmade quilts featuring intricate designs.
  • Maple Syrup: Somerset County is the largest producer of maple syrup in Pennsylvania.
  • Pottery: Discover handcrafted pottery made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Somerset

Somerset is a small town with a welcoming community. When visiting, be sure to follow local customs and etiquette, such as respecting the local history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Somerset

The best time to visit Somerset is between May and October when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somerset

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Somerset.
  • Visit the Flight 93 National Memorial and Museum.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and history.
  • Ignore the rules of the parks and forests.
  • Forget to bring comfortable shoes for outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Somerset

Somerset is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions.

Health in Somerset

Somerset has reliable healthcare options available if needed.

Getting to Somerset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Somerset

  • Johnstown-Cambria County Airport: The closest airport to Somerset, located approximately 30 minutes away. Other airports within a 2-3 hour drive include Pittsburgh International Airport and Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport.

Bus Stations Near Somerset

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Johnstown, approximately 30 minutes away from Somerset.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rentals are available from the airports and cities near Somerset.

How to Get Around in Somerset

  • Car: Renting a car or driving your own is the best way to get around Somerset and the surrounding areas.
  • Bike: Biking is an excellent option for exploring the Great Allegheny Passage and the Laurel Highlands.
  • Walking: The town center is easily walkable, making it an ideal spot for strolling.

Where to Go Next from Somerset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pittsburgh: A cultural hub with museums, sports stadiums, and impressive architecture.
  • Ohiopyle State Park: A beautiful park featuring hiking, biking, and waterfalls.
  • Frank Lloyd Wright’s Fallingwater: A stunning architectural masterpiece built over a waterfall.

Conclusion

Somerset is a hidden gem located in the heart of Laurel Highlands. With its rich history, natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, Somerset is a perfect destination for any type of traveler. From hiking and biking to exploring historical sites or just relaxing next to a lake, Somerset has something for everyone.
