Introduction to Uniontown

Nestled in the heart of the Laurel Highlands in the Pittsburgh Region of Pennsylvania, Uniontown is a small city with a rich cultural heritage and fascinating history. Founded in 1776, it played a crucial role in the American Revolution and the Civil War, serving as a strategic transportation hub and industrial center.

Today, Uniontown is a vibrant and diverse community, with numerous attractions for tourists and locals alike. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s historic landmarks, scenic natural beauty, or lively food scene, Uniontown is sure to captivate your heart and mind.

List of Places to Visit in Uniontown

Here are some of the must-see destinations in Uniontown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Uniontown is home to several museums and historical landmarks that offer a unique glimpse into the city’s past and present.

  • Fort Necessity National Battlefield: This historic site commemorates the Battle of Fort Necessity, where George Washington led his troops against the French during the French and Indian War. Visitors can explore the battlefield, hike the trails, and learn about the conflict through interpretive exhibits and guided tours.

  • Fayette County Courthouse: Built in 1850, this iconic courthouse is a masterpiece of Greek Revival architecture. It has hosted several landmark trials, including the Whiskey Rebellion trials in 1794 and the KKK trials in 1923.

  • State Theatre Center for the Arts: A historic theater that has been beautifully restored to its former glory, the State Theatre Center for the Arts hosts a wide range of live performances and events throughout the year.

  • Christian W. Klay Winery: Located in nearby Chalk Hill, this award-winning winery offers tastings and tours of its vineyards and wine-making facilities in a picturesque setting.

Natural Attractions of Uniontown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Uniontown’s location in the Laurel Highlands makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

  • Laurel Caverns Geological Park: This magnificent cave system features stunning rock formations, underground rivers, and crystal-clear pools. Guided tours are available for visitors of all ages and abilities.

  • Ohiopyle State Park: Just a short drive from Uniontown, this 20,500-acre park is a paradise for hikers, bikers, fishermen, and kayakers. It boasts miles of scenic trails, waterfalls, and whitewater rapids.

  • Bear Run Nature Reserve: An ecological gem, this nature reserve is home to an abundance of plant and animal species. Visitors can hike the trails, go bird-watching, or simply admire the beauty of the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uniontown

If you’re looking for some adventure and exercise, Uniontown has plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the best:

  • Sheepskin Trail: This 34-mile trail follows an old railroad bed and offers scenic views of the Youghiogheny River and the surrounding hills.

  • Great Allegheny Passage: A 150-mile rail trail that spans from Pittsburgh to Cumberland, Maryland, the Great Allegheny Passage offers breathtaking views of the Appalachian mountains and numerous historic towns along the way.

  • Laurel Highlands Hiking Trail: This 70-mile trail winds through the heart of the Laurel Highlands, offering challenging terrain and stunning vistas along the way.

Best Things to Do in Uniontown: Activities and Tours

Apart from the major attractions we have already discussed, Uniontown offers numerous other activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best:

  • Explore the Uniontown Mall: This indoor shopping mall offers a wide selection of stores, restaurants, and entertainment options, making it a popular destination for locals and visitors alike.

  • Take a Ghost Tour: Uniontown has a long history of ghostly apparitions and supernatural phenomena, and several local tour companies offer guided ghost tours of the city’s most haunted locations.

  • Attend a Local Festival or Event: From the Fayette County Fair to the Uniontown Italian Heritage Festival, there are plenty of annual events and festivals in Uniontown that showcase the city’s rich cultural heritage and vibrant community spirit.

Food and Drink Scene of Uniontown

Uniontown’s food and drink scene is as diverse and flavorful as the city’s cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: These traditional Polish dumplings are a staple of Uniontown’s cuisine and can be found in many local restaurants and eateries.

  • City Chicken: Despite its name, this classic dish is not made with chicken at all, but rather skewered and breaded cubes of pork or veal.

  • Haluski: Another Polish favorite, this comfort food is made with egg noodles, cabbage, and bacon, and is perfect for a cold winter’s day.

Local Beverages

  • Iron City Beer: A local favorite, Iron City Beer has been brewed in Pittsburgh since 1861, and is known for its crisp flavor and refreshing taste.

  • Wigle Whiskey: Pittsburgh’s first whiskey distillery since Prohibition, Wigle Whiskey offers a wide range of hand-crafted, small-batch spirits, including bourbon, rye, and wheat whiskey.

Where to Stay in Uniontown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Uniontown has something for every traveler. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • The Historic Summit Inn: Built in 1907, this grand hotel offers stunning views of the Laurel Highlands and modern amenities, including an outdoor pool, a fitness center, and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

  • Hotel Mellen: Located in the heart of downtown Uniontown, this boutique hotel features stylish rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a trendy bar serving craft cocktails and small plates.

  • Sunnybank Inn Bed and Breakfast: This charming Victorian-era bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms, homemade breakfasts, and a peaceful garden oasis, making it the perfect retreat for couples and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Uniontown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique and authentic souvenirs to take home from Uniontown, here are some ideas:

  • Pottery and ceramics from local artisans: Uniontown is home to several talented potters and ceramic artists, who create beautiful pieces inspired by the region’s natural beauty and cultural heritage.

  • Local honey and beeswax products: The Laurel Highlands is famous for its honeybees, and several local apiaries sell delicious honey and beeswax candles and skin care products.

Local Culture and Customs of Uniontown

Uniontown is a friendly and welcoming community, with a rich cultural heritage and strong sense of community spirit. Visitors can expect to encounter warm and welcoming locals, who are proud of their city and eager to share its history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Uniontown

The best time to visit Uniontown depends on your interests and preferences, but generally, the spring and fall months offer pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and a vibrant cultural scene. Summer is also a popular time to visit, as it offers numerous outdoor activities and events, but can be quite hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uniontown

Safety and Health

Uniontown is generally a safe and healthy city, but as with any destination, visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their well-being and safety.

Safety in Uniontown

  • Exercise caution when walking or driving at night, particularly in areas that are secluded or poorly lit.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.

Health in Uniontown

  • Make sure to pack any necessary medications or medical supplies, as they may be difficult to find locally.

  • Check with your doctor about any required vaccinations or health precautions before traveling to Uniontown.

Getting to Uniontown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Uniontown is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including planes, trains, and automobiles.

Airports Near Uniontown

  • Pittsburgh International Airport: Located approximately 55 miles from Uniontown, Pittsburgh International Airport is a major hub for domestic and international flights, serving millions of passengers each year.

Bus Stations Near Uniontown

  • Greyhound: Greyhound operates several daily services to and from Uniontown, connecting the city with other major destinations throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent cars at Pittsburgh International Airport or in downtown Uniontown, and explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Uniontown

While Uniontown is a relatively small city, visitors may still need to rely on transportation to get around. Here are some options:

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are widely available in Uniontown, and can be a convenient way to explore the city and the surrounding region.

  • Taxis and Rideshare Services: Taxis and rideshare services, such as Uber and Lyft, are also available in Uniontown, though wait times may vary.

Where to Go Next from Uniontown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time after exploring Uniontown, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Fallingwater: Designed by Frank Lloyd Wright, this iconic house is a masterpiece of modern architecture, and is located just a short drive from Uniontown.

  • Frank Lloyd Wright’s Kentuck Knob: Another stunning example of Wright’s work, Kentuck Knob is a beautiful and serene retreat in the Laurel Highlands.

  • Pittsburgh: Just an hour’s drive from Uniontown, Pittsburgh offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums, art galleries, and sports venues.

Conclusion

Uniontown is a historic and charming city that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures and scenic beauty, to culture, cuisine, and community spirit. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or simply seeking a unique and authentic travel experience, Uniontown is sure to captivate your heart and mind.
