Introduction to Ohiopyle

Located in the Laurel Highlands region of Pennsylvania, Ohiopyle is a quaint yet exciting town that attracts visitors from all over the world. Named after the Ohiopyle State Park, the town is home to an abundance of natural beauty, thrilling outdoor activities, and well-preserved historical sites. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures, cultural exploration, or both, Ohiopyle has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ohiopyle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Frank Lloyd Wright’s Fallingwater - One of the most iconic buildings in American architecture, Fallingwater was designed by Frank Lloyd Wright and is a must-visit for any history or architecture enthusiast.
  • Kentuck Knob - Another Frank Lloyd Wright-designed residence, Kentuck Knob offers tours and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Ohiopyle State Park - Covering over 20,500 acres, Ohiopyle State Park is an excellent place to enjoy hikes, picnics, bike rides, and other outdoor activities. The park also features several historical buildings and landmarks, including the restored Kentuck Campground and the Laurel Highlands Hiking Trail.

Natural Attractions of Ohiopyle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Youghiogheny River Trail - Stretching over 27 miles, this trail offers a scenic view of both the Youghiogheny River and the surrounding countryside. Cyclists, joggers, and walkers all love the trail.
  • Ohiopyle Falls - This stunning waterfall situated in the heart of the town is a must-visit attraction for any visitor. The falls are especially breathtaking during the spring season when the snow begins to melt.
  • Cucumber Falls - One of several stunning waterfalls in the area, Cucumber Falls is a great place for a picnic or a dip in the refreshing water.
  • Great Allegheny Passage - This 150-mile-long trail connects Pittsburgh to Cumberland, Maryland. The trail passes through Ohiopyle, offering scenic vistas of the surrounding mountains and picturesque towns along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Ohiopyle

  • Laurel Highlands Hiking Trail - This famed 70-mile trek winds through some of Pennsylvania’s most beautiful wilderness areas. The trail is a favorite among hikers for its pristine scenery and challenging terrain.
  • Great Allegheny Passage - This 150-mile trail offers a gentler, more accessible option ideal for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Ohiopyle: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting - The area is famous for its excellent white water rafting opportunities on Youghiogheny River, from mild to challenging. Several commercial outfitters are available.
  • Zip-lining - The Laurel Highlands offers fantastic zip line experiences with views of the Ohiopyle State Park.
  • Fly Fishing- Located along the Youghiogheny River, Ohiopyle is a perfect place for those who are looking to fish!
  • Horseback Riding - The rolling hills and spacious ranches of Ohiopyle make it an exceptional destination for equestrian enthusiasts.
  • Walking Tours - Several walking and cycling tours are available that highlight the town’s rich history and architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Ohiopyle

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogi - A Polish delicacy made with a variety of fillings, including cheese and potatoes. Ohiopyle’s pierogis are among the best in the region, thanks to its strong Czech and Slovak heritage.
  • Pulled Pork BBQ - The Western Pennsylvania region is famous for its barbecue and smoked meats. This mouthwatering dish is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.
  • Potato Soup - Ideal for a chilly day, Ohiopyle’s hearty potato soup never disappoints.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer - Ohiopyle Brewing Company produces craft beer with a distinctive local flavor and charm.
  • Distilled Spirits - Ridge Runner Distillery produces premium distilled spirits on a family-owned farm located in the area.

Where to Stay in Ohiopyle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing retreat, a luxurious stay, or a charming bed-and-breakfast, Ohiopyle offers accommodation options for every type of traveler.

  • Camping and RV sites - Ohiopyle State Park offers several camping and RV options for those looking to be close to nature.
  • Lodges and Cabins - Several luxurious lodges, inns, and cabins are available in and around Ohiopyle.

What to Buy in Ohiopyle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery - Several local artisans produce pottery, including pieces inspired by the region’s natural beauty.
  • Outdoor Gear - Gear up for your next adventure with some of the local brands that offer high-quality outdoor apparel and accessories.
  • Artwork and Photographs - Several local artists display and sell their artwork and photography inspired by the nature and landscape of Ohiopyle.

Local Culture and Customs of Ohiopyle

Ohiopyle’s rich cultural heritage is evident in its preserved historical sites, festivals, and community events. Residents are friendly and often eager to share their passion for their town with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Ohiopyle

The warm months between late May until early September are the ideal time to visit Ohiopyle. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and whitewater rafting under pleasant weather conditions

Do’s and Don’ts of Ohiopyle

Do:

  • Take care of the natural surroundings and avoid littering or damaging the environment.
  • Book a guided tour or activity to learn more about the town’s history and natural beauty.
  • Bring your camera to capture the stunning views of the mountains and countryside.

Don’t:

  • Ignore warning signs or advice from park rangers, especially when it comes to safety concerns.
  • Damage or remove historical artifacts or landmarks.
  • Engage in any activity that may be harmful to the environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health in Ohiopyle

Safety in Ohiopyle

  • Always wear a helmet on bike rides and other activities that require protective equipment.
  • Use suitable footwear for outdoor activities, especially on uneven trails.
  • Be cautious and aware of safety issues when engaging in water sports, hiking, and other activities.

Health in Ohiopyle

  • Bring along insect repellents and other outdoor essentials to avoid insect bites and sunburn.
  • If you’re planning on hiking or camping in the area, stay hydrated, and bring enough food to sustain you during your activity.
  • Consult with your healthcare provider before engaging in any significant outdoor activity that might affect your health.

Getting to Ohiopyle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ohiopyle

The nearest airport to Ohiopyle is Pittsburgh International Airport, which is approximately 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Ohiopyle

Several bus lines offer services to the city of Uniontown, which is located 25 minutes away from Ohiopyle.

Other Options

Renting a car in Pittsburgh or Uniontown and driving to Ohiopyle is the most convenient way to travel to this small town. Be sure to bring a reliable map or GPS since some parts of the town do not have a stable internet connection.

How to Get Around in Ohiopyle

The best way to explore Ohiopyle and its surroundings is by car. Consider renting a car or hiring a taxi service. Many of the trails and natural attractions require some degree of hiking, so bring suitable footwear and prepare for a little exercise.

Where to Go Next from Ohiopyle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fort Necessity National Battlefield - Located approximately 35 minutes away by car, the Fort Necessity National Battlefield is an excellent destination for history buffs.
  • Laurel Caverns - A remarkable natural wonder, the Laurel Caverns is located approximately 15 miles away from Ohiopyle.
  • Pittsburgh - A little over an hour’s drive, Pittsburgh has plenty of tourist attractions and is an excellent spot to spend the day before heading back home.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a foodie, the charming town of Ohiopyle has something for everyone. From nature hikes and whitewater rafting to fine dining and luxurious accommodations, this destination is a must-visit for anyone looking to experience the best of Pennsylvania. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your next adventure in this picturesque town.
