Introduction to Greensburg

Located in Laurel Highlands, Pittsburgh Region, Pennsylvania, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Greensburg is a charming city known for its historic architecture, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Founded in 1782, Greensburg has a rich cultural heritage that is visible in its museums and historic sites.

The city also boasts numerous parks, hiking and biking trails, and scenic views, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. So, if you are planning a trip to Greensburg, here is a comprehensive guide to help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Greensburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Westmoreland Museum of American Art

One of the top attractions in Greensburg, the Westmoreland Museum of American Art houses a collection of over 3,400 works of American art, including paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts from the 18th century to the present day.

Historic Hanna’s Town

Step back in time with a visit to Historic Hanna’s Town, a recreated 18th-century village that features log cabins, a courthouse, and a tavern. Here, you can experience life as it was in early America, with costumed interpreters demonstrating various crafts and trades.

Fort Ligonier

Located just 13 miles from Greensburg, Fort Ligonier is a reconstructed British fort that played a significant role in the French and Indian War. Today, you can visit the fort and immerse yourself in a historic era.

Natural Attractions of Greensburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mammoth Park

A popular spot for picnicking, hiking, and fishing, Mammoth Park offers over 200 acres of lush greenery, a lake, and plenty of outdoor recreational activities.

Idlewild & SoakZone

For some family fun, head to Idlewild & SoakZone, an amusement park with over 35 rides and attractions, including roller coasters, water slides, and kiddie rides.

Laurel Ridge State Park

Just a short drive from Greensburg, Laurel Ridge State Park offers some of the best views of the Laurel Highlands, with over 13,000 acres of forested hills, hiking trails, and campsites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greensburg

Five Star Trail

A 5.3-mile trail that runs from Greensburg to Youngwood, the Five Star Trail is perfect for biking, running, or walking.

Westmoreland Heritage Trail

A 21-mile trail that runs from Saltsburg to Trafford, the Westmoreland Heritage Trail offers scenic views of the Allegheny Mountains and crosses rivers, streams, and woodlands.

Twin Lakes Park

Located just a few miles from Greensburg, Twin Lakes Park boasts over 300 acres of wooded hills, walking trails, and two lakes. The park’s paved trails are perfect for biking and walking, offering some of the best views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Greensburg: Activities and Tours

Palace Theatre

Catch a show or a concert at the historic Palace Theatre, a grand 1,400-seat venue that hosts a variety of performing arts events throughout the year.

Greensburg Art Center

Take an art class or browse the galleries at the Greensburg Art Center, a community art center that offers classes and workshops for all ages.

Westmoreland County Courthouse

Tour the majestic Westmoreland County Courthouse, a National Historic Landmark that dates back to 1854 and features a stunning dome, murals, and other architectural details.

Food and Drink Scene of Greensburg

Must-Try Dishes

Pierogies

A traditional Polish dish, pierogies are a staple in Greensburg cuisine. You can find them at many local restaurants, including The Boulevard Restaurant and The Red Brick Restaurant & Lounge.

Shoo-fly Pie

A Pennsylvania Dutch specialty, shoo-fly pie is a molasses-based pie that is sweet and sticky, perfect for dessert. Try it at T-Bones Marketplace.

Haluski

Another Polish favorite, haluski is a dish made with egg noodles, cabbage, and sometimes bacon or onions. You can find it at The Boulevard Restaurant or Theo’s Mediterranean Cuisine.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

Greensburg has a lively craft beer scene, with several breweries and taprooms in the area. Check out All Saints Brewing Company or Yellow Bridge Brewing for some of the best.

Local Wine

Greensburg is also home to a few wineries, including Greendance - The Winery at Sand Hill and Christian W. Klay Winery.

Where to Stay in Greensburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn at Mountainview

A cozy bed and breakfast with scenic views, The Inn at Mountainview offers comfortable rooms, a delicious breakfast, and easy access to local attractions.

Hampton Inn Greensburg

Located in downtown Greensburg, the Hampton Inn Greensburg offers modern amenities, like an indoor pool and fitness center, and easy access to local restaurants and shops.

Greensburg Hostel

For budget travelers, the Greensburg Hostel offers comfortable dorm-style rooms and easy access to public transportation.

What to Buy in Greensburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Greensburg Sauce & Seasoning Company

Stock up on delicious sauces, marinades, and seasonings from Greensburg Sauce & Seasoning Company, a local purveyor of gourmet spice blends.

Fort Ligonier Museum Store

Buy a piece of history at the Fort Ligonier Museum Store, which offers a range of period-inspired gifts and souvenirs, including books, trinkets, and replicas of historic items.

Sand Hill Berries

Pick up some fresh berries, baked goods, and homemade jams and jellies at Sand Hill Berries, a family-owned farm that has been operating for over 50 years.

Local Culture and Customs of Greensburg

Greensburg residents are known for their friendly and welcoming hospitality, so don’t be surprised if people strike up a conversation with you. Make sure to say “hi!” and engage with the locals to get a feel for the community’s culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Greensburg

The best time to visit Greensburg is between May and October, when the weather is mild and the parks and outdoor areas are at their most beautiful. The fall foliage is especially stunning in October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greensburg

Do:

  • Do explore the outdoors: Greensburg is home to many parks and hiking trails, so take advantage of the natural beauty in the area.
  • Do try the local cuisine: From pierogies to shoo-fly pie, Greensburg has some delicious regional specialties that you won’t want to miss.
  • Do engage with the locals: Greensburg residents are known for their friendly attitudes, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation.

Don’t:

  • Don’t neglect the history: Greensburg has a rich history that is visible in its museums and historic sites, so make sure to take some time to learn about the city’s past.
  • Don’t litter: Greensburg residents take pride in their city, so make sure to dispose of trash properly and keep the area clean.
  • Don’t forget to tip: Tipping is customary in the United States, so make sure to leave a tip for your servers and other service providers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greensburg

Greensburg is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, as with any city, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, like locking your car and not leaving valuables visible.

Health in Greensburg

Stay healthy during your visit by drinking plenty of water and using sunscreen when you’re outdoors. If you need medical assistance, there are several hospitals and clinics in the area, including Excela Health Westmoreland Hospital.

Getting to Greensburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greensburg

The closest airport to Greensburg is Pittsburgh International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away. Other airports nearby include Arnold Palmer Regional Airport and John Murtha Johnstown-Cambria County Airport.

Bus Stations Near Greensburg

Greyhound and Megabus both offer service to Greensburg, with stops at the Greensburg Bus Stop downtown.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Greensburg is easily accessible via major highways, including the Pennsylvania Turnpike and Route 30. And for those who want to travel by train, Amtrak offers service to nearby stations in Johnstown and Latrobe.

How to Get Around in Greensburg

If you don’t have access to a car, you can easily get around Greensburg using public transportation. Westmoreland Transit offers bus service throughout the city and the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Greensburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pittsburgh

Just a short drive away, Pittsburgh is a bustling city with plenty to see and do, including museums, theaters, and sports venues.

Frank Lloyd Wright’s Fallingwater

A true architectural masterpiece, Fallingwater is a must-see attraction for architecture buffs.

Laurel Caverns

Explore the underground wonders of Laurel Caverns, a vast natural cave system with over 3 miles of trails to explore.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and welcoming community, Greensburg is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique blend of culture and outdoor activity. Plan your visit using this comprehensive guide and get ready for an unforgettable trip to Greensburg.
