Introduction to Cranberry

Nestled in the heart of Pittsburgh, Cranberry is a picturesque suburb that boasts a perfect blend of urban energy and natural charm. This quaint town offers an array of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and awe-inspiring scenery that avid travellers crave. Whether you’re seeking a peaceful getaway, or a fun-filled adventure, Cranberry has it all. In this comprehensive travel guide to Cranberry, we will explore all the unique and exciting things this destination has to offer.

Local Culture and Customs of Cranberry

Cranberry is known for its friendly, welcoming people and peaceful lifestyle. It is courteous to greet people with a smile and wave when passing them.

Best Time to Visit Cranberry

The best time to visit Cranberry is in the fall when the foliage is in full colour, and the temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cranberry

Do:

  • Do use your turn signals while driving
  • Do visit the Cranberry Township Historical Society & Museum
  • Do try the Cajun Corn Fritters from Nola on the Square

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car in plain view
  • Don’t miss out on wood-fired pizza from Bella Frutteto

Safety and Health

Safety in Cranberry

Crime rates in Cranberry Township are low, making it a relatively safe destination. However, it is still advisable to stay alert and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Cranberry

Cranberry has excellent healthcare facilities, including UPMC Lemieux Sports Complex, a state-of-the-art hospital specialising in sports medicine.

Getting to Cranberry: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cranberry

  • Pittsburgh International Airport

Bus Stations Near Cranberry

  • Port Authority of Allegheny County

Other Options

  • By Car: Cranberry is 20 miles north of Downtown Pittsburgh and is accessible via I-79 and the Pennsylvania Turnpike.

How to Get Around in Cranberry

The best way to get around Cranberry is to drive or rent a car. However, biking and walking are also viable options.
