Introduction to Mars

Mars, Pennsylvania, is a small town located in the North Pittsburgh Region. While its name may evoke images of the red planet, Mars is actually a charming town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly community. Its small size means that Mars is easy to explore on foot or by bike, and it makes for a great weekend getaway or day trip from Pittsburgh.

List of Places to Visit in Mars

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mars Area History and Landmarks Society Museum

The Mars Area History and Landmarks Society Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum is located in a restored train station and features exhibits on the town’s past, including its early settlement, the oil and gas boom, and World War II. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the historic train station and see the original ticket counter and waiting area.

Rachel Carson Homestead

The Rachel Carson Homestead is where the influential environmentalist and writer grew up. The house has been preserved as a museum and features exhibits on Carson’s life and work, as well as the history of the farm and the environmental movement. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the house and see the room where Carson wrote her famous book, “Silent Spring.”

Natural Attractions of Mars: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Park

North Park is a large park located just a short drive from Mars. The park features a lake where visitors can go boating or fishing, as well as picnic areas, hiking trails, and a golf course. In the winter, the park also offers ice skating and sledding.

Moraine State Park

Moraine State Park is a short drive from Mars and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park features a large lake where visitors can go boating, fishing, and swimming. The park also has hiking trails and bike paths that offer stunning views of the lake and surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mars

The Mars-Bakerstown Loop

The Mars-Bakerstown Loop is a popular hiking and biking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is just over 10 miles long and is mostly flat, making it accessible for all skill levels. Along the way, visitors can stop at scenic overlooks and historic sites, such as the Bakerstown Cemetery and the Mars Area History and Landmarks Society Museum.

Best Things to Do in Mars: Activities and Tours

Oglebay Resort

Oglebay Resort is a short drive from Mars and offers a range of activities for visitors. The resort features a golf course, spa, and several restaurants. Visitors can also explore the resort’s gardens and see the seasonal holiday lights display, which includes over a million lights.

Clearview Mall

Clearview Mall is located in nearby Butler and offers a range of shopping and dining options. Visitors can browse through the stores, grab a bite to eat, or catch a movie at the mall’s theater.

Food and Drink Scene of Mars

Must-Try Dishes

Pierogies

Pierogies are a popular dish in this part of Pennsylvania, and a visit to Mars wouldn’t be complete without trying some. These boiled or fried dumplings can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as potato, cheese, or sauerkraut.

Lebanon Bologna

Lebanon bologna is a type of smoked sausage that is popular in this area. It can be eaten on its own or sliced and served in sandwiches.

Local Beverages

Iron City Beer

Iron City Beer is a popular beer brewed in nearby Pittsburgh and is a favorite among locals.

Wigle Whiskey

Wigle Whiskey is a local distillery that produces a range of spirits, including bourbon, rye, and gin. Visitors can take a tour of the distillery and sample some of the products.

Where to Stay in Mars: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Pittsburgh - Cranberry

The DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel is located just a short drive from Mars and offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a fitness center and indoor pool.

Comfort Inn Cranberry Township

The Comfort Inn Cranberry Township is another good option for visitors to Mars. The hotel is located near several restaurants and shops and offers a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Mars: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mars 4th of July Souvenirs

Mars hosts a popular 4th of July celebration, and visitors can purchase souvenirs, such as t-shirts and hats, to commemorate the event.

Knickers Tavern & Grill Merchandise

Knickers Tavern & Grill is a popular restaurant in Mars that serves up delicious food and drinks. Visitors can purchase merchandise, such as t-shirts and pint glasses, to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Mars

Mars is a friendly and tight-knit community that prides itself on its history and natural beauty. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s traditions and customs, and take care to preserve the natural environment during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Mars

The best time to visit Mars is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are moderate and the natural scenery is at its most stunning. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mars

Do:

  • Explore the town’s history and culture.
  • Take care of the natural environment.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially pierogies and Lebanon bologna.
  • Visit nearby parks and outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the town’s traditions and customs.
  • Litter or damage local landmarks.
  • Ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mars

Mars is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect their valuables and personal safety.

Health in Mars

There are several medical facilities located in and around Mars, including hospitals and urgent care centers.

Getting to Mars: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mars

The closest airport to Mars is Pittsburgh International Airport, which is located about 25 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Mars

There are several bus stations located in nearby Pittsburgh, including the Pittsburgh Greyhound Station and the Megabus stop.

Other Options

Visitors to Mars may also choose to drive or take a taxi or ridesharing service from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Mars

Mars is a small town that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors who want to explore nearby attractions may choose to drive or take public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Mars: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pittsburgh

Pittsburgh is a vibrant city with a rich history and culture. Visitors can explore the city’s museums, parks, and restaurants, or catch a game at one of the sports stadiums.

Laurel Highlands

The Laurel Highlands is a beautiful area located east of Mars that is known for its scenic beauty and outdoor activities. Visitors can hike or bike through the area’s forests and hills, or visit the famous Fallingwater house designed by Frank Lloyd Wright.

Conclusion

Mars, Pennsylvania, is a charming town that offers a combination of history, natural beauty, and friendly community. Visitors to Mars can explore the town’s museums and historical sites, hike or bike through the surrounding countryside, and try the local cuisine. With its proximity to Pittsburgh and other nearby destinations, Mars makes for a great weekend getaway or day trip.
