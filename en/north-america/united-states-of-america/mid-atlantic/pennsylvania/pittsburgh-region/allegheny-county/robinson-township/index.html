Introduction to Robinson Township

Located in Allegheny County, in the Pittsburgh region of Pennsylvania, Robinson Township is a charming community filled with friendly locals, beautiful scenic views, and a rich history. Its central location provides access to both urban attractions and outdoor adventures, making it a great destination for all types of travelers. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more in Robinson Township.

List of Places to Visit in Robinson Township

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Settlers Cabin Park: This park and historical village allows visitors to step back in time and see what life was like in the early 1800s for local settlers. The park also offers beautiful hiking trails and fishing opportunities.
  • The Original Fish Market: This famous seafood restaurant has been a local icon for over 30 years. With fresh fish flown in daily, this is the perfect spot for seafood lovers.
  • Pittsburgh Botanic Garden: A beautiful 60-acre garden with walking trails, water features, and a variety of plantlife. Events such as yoga classes and live music can also be enjoyed in this serene setting.

Natural Attractions of Robinson Township: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Robinson Township Community Park: This park offers multiple fields for sports, playgrounds for children, and a dog park for furry friends. The park also hosts community events such as a summer concert series and a fall festival.
  • Montour Trail: A 63-mile long, multi-use trail that stretches through Allegheny and Washington counties for hiking, biking and horse riding.
  • Coraopolis Heights: A scenic viewpoint overlooking the Ohio River that offers panoramic views of the valley below.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Robinson Township

The Montour Trail offers a variety of different routes for hiking, biking, and horse riding. For those seeking more challenging terrain, the Settlers Cabin Park offers a variety of trails that wind through the woods and offer scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Robinson Township: Activities and Tours

  • Pittsburgh Brewery Tour: Take a brewery tour and sample local craft beers from around the Pittsburgh region.
  • Pittsburgh Pirates Game: Catch America’s favorite pastime at PNC Park while indulging in typical ballgame cuisine.
  • Escape Room: Challenge your mind and problem-solving skills in a fun and unique setting at Escape Room PA.

Food and Drink Scene of Robinson Township

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pittsburgh-style sandwich: This classic sandwich features hot roast beef, french fries, coleslaw, and tomato on Italian bread.
  • Perogies: These delicious dumplings are a Pittsburgh staple and can be found on menus throughout the city.
  • Primanti Brothers sandwich: A Pittsburgh icon, this sandwich is loaded with meat, cheese, and french fries, all piled high on Italian bread.

Local Beverages

  • Iron City Beer: A classic Pittsburgh beer that has been brewed for over 150 years.
  • Pittsburgh Whiskey: Wigle Whiskey is Pittsburgh’s first distillery since Prohibition, and offers a variety of unique spirits.

Where to Stay in Robinson Township: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Pittsburgh Airport: A modern hotel with free airport shuttle service and an on-site restaurant.
  • Hampton Inn Pittsburgh-Airport: A convenient location and excellent amenities make this hotel a great choice for travelers.
  • Candlewood Suites Pittsburgh Airport: Suites with kitchenettes and a 24-hour gym make this hotel a favorite among extended stay travelers.

What to Buy in Robinson Township: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Black and Gold Sports Gear: Pittsburgh’s loyal sports fans love to represent their teams with black and gold gear.
  • Steelers, Pirates, and Penguins Memorabilia: Fans of the local sports teams can find unique memorabilia including jerseys, keychains, and other accessories in Robinson Township.
  • Pittsburgh-made Items: Pittsburgh is known for its unique artistic community, and visitors can take home handmade items such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Robinson Township

Robinson Township is known for its friendly and welcoming locals. The community takes great pride in their sports teams, local cuisine, and distinctive art scene.

Best Time to Visit Robinson Township

The best time to visit Robinson Township is from May to October when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are plentiful. The fall season is particularly beautiful as the leaves change color, and fall festivals take place throughout the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Robinson Township

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, particularly Pittsburgh-style sandwiches and perogies.
  • Visit Settlers Cabin Park for a unique look at local history.
  • Take a walk along the Montour Trail for beautiful scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying a Pittsburgh-style sandwich or Primanti Brothers sandwich.
  • Skip the scenic Coraopolis Heights viewpoint.
  • Forget to bring home a souvenir or handmade piece from a local artisan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Robinson Township

Robinson Township is generally a safe community, but common sense and basic safety precautions should still be taken. Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Robinson Township

Robinson Township does have a medical center, but it’s always a good idea to travel with any necessary prescriptions or medical supplies.

Where to Go Next from Robinson Township: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Laurel Highlands: Known for its beautiful hiking trails, skiing opportunities, and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Robinson Township is more than just a suburb of Pittsburgh; it’s a destination filled with history, culture, and outdoor adventures. From scenic views to top-notch cuisine, Robinson Township has something for everyone. So pack your bags and head to this hidden gem on your next vacation.
