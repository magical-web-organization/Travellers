Introduction to Mt. Lebanon

Nestled in the Pittsburgh Region of Pennsylvania, Mt. Lebanon is a scenic suburban community located in Allegheny County. The town’s winding roads, sprawling parks, and historic architecture make it a popular destination among tourists and locals who are looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures, local food, or soaking up the fascinating history and culture of the area, there’s something for everyone in Mt. Lebanon.

List of Places to Visit in Mt. Lebanon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mt. Lebanon Historical Society Museum: This fascinating museum offers a peek into the town’s rich history, from its Native American past to its role in the Civil War and beyond.

  • South Hills Art Gallery: Featuring a wide range of contemporary and traditional art from local and international artists, the South Hills Art Gallery is a must-see for art lovers.

  • St. Bernard Church: This stunning church has been a landmark of the area for over a century, and its breathtaking stained-glass windows and beautiful interior are sure to leave visitors in awe.

Natural Attractions of Mt. Lebanon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bower Hill Park: A beautiful park with a creek, playground, and picnic area, Bower Hill Park is a great place to relax and soak up the natural beauty of the area.

  • Mount Lebanon Nature Conservancy Trails: A network of trails that wind through the forests and hills of Mt. Lebanon, the Mount Lebanon Nature Conservancy Trails are a great place for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

  • Washington Road Business District: With its charming shops, boutiques, and cafes, the Washington Road Business District offers a unique shopping and dining experience that is sure to delight visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mt. Lebanon

  • Bower Hill Loop Trail: A 2.6 mile loop that takes you through Bower Hill Park and offers stunning views of the creek and surrounding forests.

  • Powdermill Trail: A 4.2 mile out-and-back trail that takes you through scenic woodlands and connects to the Montour Trail.

  • Sleepy Hollow Trail: A 2.3 mile trail that winds through Sleepy Hollow Park, offering breathtaking views of the rolling hills and forests of Mt. Lebanon.

Best Things to Do in Mt. Lebanon: Activities and Tours

  • Mt. Lebanon Farmers’ Market: A wonderful place to experience the local culture and taste some of the freshest, most delicious produce available.

  • Attend a performance at the Theatre Historical Society of America: Featuring a wide variety of theatrical performances, concerts, and other cultural events, the Theatre Historical Society of America is a great place to experience the vibrant arts scene of Mt. Lebanon.

  • Mt. Lebanon Artists’ Market: The perfect place to find unique, handmade gifts and souvenirs created by local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mt. Lebanon

Day 1: Explore the charming shops and restaurants of the Washington Road Business District.

Day 2: Hike the Powdermill Trail and take in the breathtaking natural beauty of Mt. Lebanon.

Day 3: Visit the Mt. Lebanon Historical Society Museum and learn about the fascinating history of the area.

Day 4: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Bower Hill Loop Trail.

Day 5: Visit the South Hills Art Gallery and discover the works of local and international artists.

Day 6: Attend a performance at the Theatre Historical Society of America

Day 7: Visit the Mt. Lebanon Farmers’ Market and taste the freshest produce that the area has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Mt. Lebanon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: A Polish specialty, pierogies are little pockets of dough filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, and other savory fillings. They’re a staple of the local cuisine and can be found at many of Mt. Lebanon’s restaurants and cafes.

  • Primanti Bros. Sandwiches: A Pittsburgh classic, these sandwiches contain meat, slaw, and fries wedged between two slices of hearty bread. They’re messy, but oh-so-delicious.

  • Pot Roast: A hearty, comforting dish that’s perfect for a chilly day, pot roast is a signature dish in many of Mt. Lebanon’s restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Iron City Beer: A Pittsburgh institution, Iron City Beer is a must-try for beer lovers.

  • Pennsylvania Wines: With dozens of wineries located throughout the area, Pennsylvania wines are a great way to sample the local flavors.

  • Allegheny Coffee & Tea Exchange: Offering a wide range of locally roasted coffee and tea blends, Allegheny Coffee & Tea Exchange is a must-visit for caffeine aficionados.

Where to Stay in Mt. Lebanon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott Pittsburgh Neville Island: A comfortable and modern hotel located just a short drive from Mt. Lebanon.

  • Crowne Plaza Pittsburgh South: A luxurious hotel with a wide range of amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center.

  • Airbnb: There are many cozy and affordable Airbnb options located throughout Mt. Lebanon, including private apartments and houses.

What to Buy in Mt. Lebanon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artwork: The South Hills Art Gallery and Mt. Lebanon Artists’ Market are great places to find unique and beautiful artwork created by local artists.

  • Handmade Crafts: From handmade soap to crocheted items, there are many local artisans who offer unique and charming crafts in Mt. Lebanon.

  • Local Wine and Beer: Bring home a taste of Mt. Lebanon with some local wine or beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Mt. Lebanon

Mt. Lebanon has a rich cultural history, with a strong focus on the arts and community involvement. Residents take great pride in their town and value the strong sense of community that exists here. Visitors are always welcome, and the locals are friendly and eager to share their love of the area with others.

Best Time to Visit Mt. Lebanon

The best time to visit Mt. Lebanon is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The area can get quite cold and snowy during the winter, and hot and humid during the summer, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mt. Lebanon

Do:

  • Respect the local culture: Mt. Lebanon has a rich cultural history, and residents take great pride in their community and its values. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities: From hiking and biking to exploring the parks, there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in Mt. Lebanon.

Don’t:

  • Be disruptive or disrespectful: Mt. Lebanon is a peaceful community, and visitors should take care to be respectful of others and not disrupt the local peace and tranquility.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mt. Lebanon

Mt. Lebanon is a very safe community, with a low crime rate and friendly, welcoming residents. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take appropriate safety precautions.

Health in Mt. Lebanon

Mt. Lebanon has several excellent healthcare facilities, including St. Clair Hospital and UPMC Mercy. Visitors who require medical attention can rest assured that they will receive high-quality care in this area.

Getting to Mt. Lebanon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mt. Lebanon

  • Pittsburgh International Airport: Located just 17 miles from Mt. Lebanon, Pittsburgh International Airport is the closest major airport to the area.

  • Allegheny County Airport: A smaller airport located closer to Mt. Lebanon, Allegheny County Airport offers a more convenient option for some visitors.

Bus Stations Near Mt. Lebanon

  • Greyhound Bus Station Pittsburgh: Located just a short drive from Mt. Lebanon, the Greyhound Bus Station Pittsburgh offers convenient bus connections to and from the area.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient and flexible way to explore the area on your own schedule.

  • Taxi or rideshare services: Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in the area.

How to Get Around in Mt. Lebanon

The best way to get around in Mt. Lebanon is by car, although walking and biking are also popular options. There is also a local bus system called the Mt. Lebanon Transit, which offers convenient connections to many destinations in the area.

Where to Go Next from Mt. Lebanon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pittsburgh: A vibrant city with a rich cultural history, Pittsburgh is located just a short drive from Mt. Lebanon and offers a wide range of attractions and activities.

  • The Laurel Highlands: A stunningly beautiful area located just east of Mt. Lebanon, the Laurel Highlands offer a wide range of outdoor activities and attractions, including hiking, biking, and skiing.

  • The Allegheny National Forest: Located to the north of Mt. Lebanon, the Allegheny National Forest is a beautiful wilderness area with miles of trails and stunning scenery.

Conclusion

Mt. Lebanon is a hidden gem in the Pittsburgh Region of Pennsylvania, with a rich cultural history, gorgeous natural scenery, and plenty of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re exploring the museums and historical places, hiking the trails, or enjoying the local food and drink scene, you’re sure to have a wonderful time in this charming and welcoming community.
