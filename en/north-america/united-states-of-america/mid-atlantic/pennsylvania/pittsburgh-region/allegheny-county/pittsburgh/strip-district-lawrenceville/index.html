Introduction to Strip District-Lawrenceville

Strip District-Lawrenceville is a vibrant neighborhood located in the heart of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. This historic area offers visitors a diverse mix of attractions, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and delicious cuisine. Strip District-Lawrenceville has something for everyone, making it a must-visit destination for travelers to the Pittsburgh area.

List of Places to Visit in Strip District-Lawrenceville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Senator John Heinz History Center: This museum offers an in-depth look at the history of Western Pennsylvania, including exhibits on sports, immigration, and industry.
  • Andy Warhol Museum: This museum celebrates the life and work of the famous artist, with seven floors of exhibits on Warhol’s art, films, and life.
  • Fort Pitt Museum: This museum focuses on the history of Fort Pitt, a key military site during the French and Indian War and the American Revolution.
  • Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens: This beautiful garden features over 15 acres of gardens, collections of tropical plants, and year-round events.

Natural Attractions of Strip District-Lawrenceville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Allegheny Riverfront Park: This park along the Allegheny River offers walking trails, fishing opportunities, and stunning views of the city skyline.
  • North Shore Trail: This trail runs along the Allegheny River and offers a scenic route for walking, running, or biking.
  • Three Rivers Heritage Trail: This trail runs along the three rivers in Pittsburgh, offering scenic views and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strip District-Lawrenceville

  • Great Allegheny Passage: This trail spans 150 miles from Pittsburgh to Cumberland, Maryland, and offers views of mountains, rivers, and charming small towns along the way.
  • Schenley Park: This park offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking, as well as a beautiful overlook of the city skyline.
  • Frick Park: This park features over 600 acres of natural area, including hiking and biking trails, play areas, and a visitor center.

Best Things to Do in Strip District-Lawrenceville: Activities and Tours

  • Strip District Food Tour: This guided tour takes visitors through the iconic Strip District, stopping at local eateries to sample delicious cuisine.
  • Kayak Pittsburgh: This organization offers kayak rentals and tours of the three rivers in Pittsburgh.
  • Pittsburgh Brew Tour: This tour takes visitors to some of the best breweries in Pittsburgh, offering tastings and behind-the-scenes glimpses of the brewing process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strip District-Lawrenceville

For visitors looking to spend a week in Strip District-Lawrenceville, we recommend the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Senator John Heinz History Center and Fort Pitt Museum
  • Day 2: Phipps Conservatory & Botanical Gardens and Strip District Food Tour
  • Day 3: Andy Warhol Museum and North Shore Trail
  • Day 4: Great Allegheny Passage Trail and Frick Park
  • Day 5: Kayak Pittsburgh and Three Rivers Heritage Trail
  • Day 6: Schenley Park and Pittsburgh Brew Tour
  • Day 7: Allegheny Riverfront Park and free time to explore the Strip District

Food and Drink Scene of Strip District-Lawrenceville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Primanti Bros. sandwich: This Pittsburgh classic features meat, cheese, coleslaw, and French fries all piled high on Italian bread.
  • Pierogies: These Polish dumplings are a popular dish in Pittsburgh, with many restaurants offering their own unique takes on the classic recipe.
  • Fish sandwich: Pittsburgh is famous for its fish sandwiches, which feature a large piece of breaded fish on a bun.

Local Beverages

  • Iron City Beer: This beer has been brewed in Pittsburgh for over 150 years and is a fixture at bars and restaurants throughout the city.
  • Pennsylvania wine: The state of Pennsylvania is home to over 200 wineries, many of which can be found in and around Pittsburgh.
  • Pittsburgh Whiskey: This local distillery produces a range of whiskeys that are made with locally-sourced grains and aged in barrels made from Pennsylvania oak.

Where to Stay in Strip District-Lawrenceville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Distrikt Hotel Pittsburgh: This modern hotel features stylish rooms and is located in the heart of the Strip District.
  • Mansions on Fifth: This historic mansion has been converted into a boutique hotel, featuring luxurious rooms and a restaurant.
  • Pittsburgh Hostel Project: This hostel offers affordable accommodations in the heart of Lawrenceville, with dorm-style and private rooms available.

What to Buy in Strip District-Lawrenceville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Strip District Marketplace: This indoor market features over 30 vendors selling fresh produce, meats, cheeses, and more.
  • Mon Aimee Chocolat: This specialty chocolate shop offers a wide range of artisanal chocolates and confections.
  • Arsenal Cider House: This local cidery produces a range of unique ciders, including a spicy ginger cider and a blueberry cider.

Local Culture and Customs of Strip District-Lawrenceville

Strip District-Lawrenceville is a diverse neighborhood that celebrates a range of cultures and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including traditional dress and religious practices. Pittsburgh is also known for its love of sports, especially football, so visitors may want to catch a game at Heinz Field or indulge in some tailgating before the game.

Best Time to Visit Strip District-Lawrenceville

The best time to visit Strip District-Lawrenceville is between May and September, when the weather is warm and many outdoor events and festivals take place.

Safety and Health

Safety in Strip District-Lawrenceville

Strip District-Lawrenceville is a safe neighborhood, but visitors should still take basic precautions like locking their doors and not walking alone at night. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid any areas that appear unsafe.

Visitors should make sure to stay hydrated during their visit to Strip District-Lawrenceville, especially during the warmer months. Visitors should also wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors, as ticks and mosquitoes can be a problem in the area.
