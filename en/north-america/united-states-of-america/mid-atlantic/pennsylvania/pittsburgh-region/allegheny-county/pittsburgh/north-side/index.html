Introduction to North Side

North Side is a neighborhood located in the Allegheny County of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. It offers a glimpse of the city’s industrial history and serves as a cultural hub to visitors with its outdoor recreational activities, distinct architecture, and museums.

List of Places to Visit in North Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Andy Warhol Museum

The Andy Warhol Museum celebrates the life and art of the late Pittsburgh native. It features the largest collection of Warhol’s artwork globally, and visitors can witness his most iconic works such as the Campbell Soup Cans and Elvis Presley.

The National Aviary

The National Aviary is America’s only independent indoor nonprofit aviary. It champions birds’ conservation and is home to over 500 birds representing 150 species of various continents. Visitors can hand-feed lorikeets, watch a penguin parade, and enjoy other immersive experiences.

The Children’s Museum of Pittsburgh

The Children’s Museum of Pittsburgh is a must-visit for families. It inspires children’s learning and making through hands-on activities, exhibits, and workshops. It also features the Allegheny Waterworks, a wading pool, a rooftop playground, and an art studio.

Natural Attractions of North Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Three Rivers Heritage Trail

The Three Rivers Heritage Trail offers eight miles of a flat, scenic trail for bikers, hikers, joggers, and skaters. It connects to downtown Pittsburgh, allowing visitors to explore the city’s architecture and landmarks, including the Point State Park.

North Shore Riverfront Park

North Shore Riverfront Park serves as a natural oasis in a busy metropolis. It is a perfect spot for picnics, strolls, or simply gazing at the stunning views of the Allegheny River and the Pittsburgh skyline. It’s a great spot to watch a spectacular sunset or to attend various events of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Side

The North Shore Trail

The North Shore Trail provides a 7.3-mile trail on the north bank of the Allegheny River. Visitors can enjoy the picturesque trail while taking in views of the city skyline, along with Three Rivers Stadium, PNC Park, and Heinz Field.

The Rachel Carson Trail

The Rachel Carson Trail features a 35-mile long-distance trail extending from North Park in the North Hills to Harrison Hills County Park in the Allegheny Highlands. Visitors can enjoy a challenging hike along the trail that includes meadows, hills, forests, and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in North Side: Activities and Tours

Pirate City Brew Tours

Pirate City Brew Tours is a fantastic way to explore Pittsburgh’s culture by indulging in local brews. Visitors can try out a selection of Pittsburgh beers, meet talented brewers, and learn about the city’s brewing history.

Ride The Ducks Pittsburgh

Ride The Ducks Pittsburgh takes visitors on a city’s land and water adventure with a unique and fun-filled experience. The tour takes place on amphibious vehicles traveling through Pittsburgh’s streets before making a grand entrance into the water to tour Point State Park and downtown Pittsburgh from the rivers.

Food and Drink Scene of North Side

Must-Try Dishes

Primanti Brothers Sandwich

The Primanti Brothers Sandwich features meat, tomatoes, coleslaw, and french fries between two thick slices of Italian bread. It offers a unique taste that combines several flavors and textures that are Pittsburgh’s traditional side dish.

Pierogies

Pierogies are traditional Slavic dumplings that have become Pittsburgh’s culinary specialty. These stuffed dough pockets contain potatoes, cheese, sauerkraut, and onions and are served pan-fried with butter and onions.

Local Beverages

Iron City Beer

Iron City Beer is a premium lager that originated from Pittsburgh and is an iconic symbol of the city.

Wigle Whiskey

Wigle Whiskey is a craft distillery located in the Strip District and the birthplace of the American whiskey revolution. It offers special blends created from organic and locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in North Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Priory Hotel

The Priory Hotel is a historic boutique hotel that offers personalized service, comfortable rooms, and access to several amenities, including shuttle services to nearby spots.

The Allegheny Inn

The Allegheny Inn is a charming bed and breakfast located near the Mexican War Streets. The inn features beautifully decorated rooms, a private courtyard, and complimentary breakfast with locally sourced ingredients.

What to Buy in North Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pittsburgh Sports Memorabilia

Pittsburgh is a sports hub, with plenty of passionate fans throughout the city. The locals can discover a wide range of merchandise and sports memorabilia in the North Side, including jerseys, caps, and posters.

The North Side Market House

The North Side Market House is a one-stop-shop that features locally made artisanal items, crafts, dairy products, meats, and freshly baked bread.

Local Culture and Customs of North Side

Pittsburgh is a friendly and welcoming place. The locals are known for their hard-working values, and visitors should respect the city’s industrial past and contributions to the country.

Best Time to Visit North Side

The best time to visit the North Side is during the summer, between June and August, when the city enjoys warm weather, festivals, and outdoor events.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Side

Do:

-Pay tribute to Andy Warhol by visiting The Andy Warhol Museum. -Enjoy a stroll along Allegheny Riverfront Park to take in the breath-taking views of downtown Pittsburgh. -Indulge in the city’s famous local dishes, including Primanti Brothers Sandwich and Pierogies.

Don’t:

-Do not litter the city’s streets and parks. -Avoid making any negative comments about Pittsburgh’s sports teams and be respectful towards fans.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Side

Pittsburgh’s North Side is quite safe, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions to prevent mishaps, such as keeping valuable items safe and avoiding unlit areas late at night.

Health in North Side

Visitors to Pittsburgh’s North Side can access quality healthcare facilities, including Allegheny General Hospital and UPMC Mercy Hospital, in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to North Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Side

The closest airport to the North Side is Pittsburgh International Airport, approximately 30-40 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near North Side

Greyhound and Megabus operate buses that provide transport to and from Pittsburgh’s North Side.

Other Options

Several car rental services, taxi, and shuttle service providers offer transport within Pittsburgh and to the North Side.

How to Get Around in North Side

Pittsburgh’s North Side is highly walkable, and visitors can easily explore the city on foot. Alternatively, visitors can use public transportation services, including buses and light rails.

Where to Go Next from North Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mount Washington

Mount Washington offers scenic views overlooking Pittsburgh’s skyline. Visitors can reach the summit by hiking, taking the incline, or by car, and indulge in numerous shopping and dining options.

Schenley Park

Schenley Park is a 456-acre vibrant public park in Pittsburgh. Visitors can enjoy numerous outdoor recreational activities, including a 4.5-mile bike trail, swimming, and ice-skating.

Conclusion

The North Side of Pittsburgh offers endless possibilities for visitors to indulge in its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. With plenty of recreational activities, world-class museums, and delicious local food, North Side Pittsburgh is a must-visit destination that never fails to impress.
