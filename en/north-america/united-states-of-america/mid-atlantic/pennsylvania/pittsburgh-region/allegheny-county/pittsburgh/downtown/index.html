Introduction to Downtown

Located in the heart of Pittsburgh, Downtown is a bustling district where business, entertainment, and culture converge. The area is relatively compact and easy to navigate, with numerous attractions, restaurants, and accommodations within walking distance. Visitors can admire the stunning architecture, immerse in the local arts and culture scene, and enjoy the natural landmarks nearby. Whether you’re visiting for business, pleasure, or both, Downtown has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pittsburgh has a rich history and an impressive collection of museums and historical landmarks you can explore. Visit the Heinz History Center to learn about Western Pennsylvania’s past, or head to the Fort Pitt Museum, where you can experience Pittsburgh’s role in the French and Indian War. The Sen. John Heinz History Center enables you to witness the evolution of the Western Pennsylvania from 1750 to present day. History buffs will enjoy the Museum of Pittsburgh, which celebrates the city’s industrial heritage, while art aficionados won’t want to miss The Andy Warhol Museum, which pays tribute to the life and works of the famous artist.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Downtown may be the business heart of the city, but there is no shortage of outdoor adventures and scenic views. Start by visiting Point State Park - where the three rivers converge - to enjoy a picnic, take a stroll, or admire the fountain which was reopened to the public in 2021. For a more immersive experience, take a boat ride down the Allegheny River or go kayaking in the Ohio. Also, there’s North Shore trail, which will provide you with gorgeous views of the city skyline and PNC Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

Exploring Downtown by foot or bike is highly recommended. The Three Rivers Heritage Trail, which runs for 25 miles alongside the rivers, is perfect for cyclists, joggers, and walkers alike. For those looking for a more challenging terrain, consider taking a hike on the Rachel Carson Trail, a 35-mile stretch through stunning natural scenery that starts in North Park and ends Downtown.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to experience while in Downtown Pittsburgh. For a behind-the-scenes look at the city’s sports legacy, take a tour of the PNC Park or Heinz Field. If you’re looking for a more unique experience, head to Escape Room Pittsburgh, where you can try to solve challenging puzzles and escape before time runs out. If you’re travelling by a river just go ahead for a Just Ducky Tour for the difference.

Week-Long Itinerary for Downtown

For a full week of exploring Downtown, here is an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time.

Day 1: Start your trip in Point State Park and visit the Fort Pitt Museum. Spend the evening exploring the Strip District, which is known for its vast array of markets and food halls.

Day 2: Head to The Andy Warhol Museum in the morning, then take a stroll down the Roberto Clemente Bridge to catch lunch or dinner at Manny’s BBQ, adjacent to the PNC Park. Spend the evening on Mount Washington, where you can enjoy the most beautiful views of the city at night.

Day 3: Take the family to the Carnegie Science Center and explore the Children Museum. Enjoy a sunset dinner at one of the many restaurants along the North Shore Trail.

Day 4: Go hiking at the nearby Frick Park, which features several trails and natural landmarks. After a few hours of hiking, head to Oakland and explore the famous Cathedral of Learning, which is home to one of the largest academic buildings in the world.

Day 5: Explore the city’s bridges, which are a sight to behold. Afterwards, head to Market Square where you can enjoy an afternoon coffee at Nicholas Coffee Co. or visit the City-County Building Observation Deck which offers stunning views of the city.

Day 6: Relax by the river and enjoy some water activities in the morning. Take a boat ride down the Allegheny River and enjoy a sunset dinner at Monterey Bay Fish Grotto, which overlooks the river.

Day 7: End your trip by going to a concert or event at the beautiful Benedum Center for the Arts and Cultural District area which houses multiple theaters and cultural institutions.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Must-Try Dishes

Pittsburgh is known for its delicious cuisine with options for all types of palates. Get a taste of the famous Primanti Brothers sandwiches while at the Strip District or dine at the Capital Grille for upscale steakhouse experience. For an eclectic Asian-inspired cuisine visit the Umami, or for great seafood, try Eddie V’s Prime Seafood.

Local Beverages

Craft breweries and local wineries abound in Pittsburgh. For a unique wine-tasting experience, visit the Engine House 25 Winery, located in a former firehouse in Lawrenceville. Wigle Whiskey is another top spot to try local spirits and craft cocktails.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown Pittsburgh offers plenty of options for accommodation regardless if you’re after luxury or budget stays. The Fairmont Pittsburgh has a prime location and top-notch amenities. The Hampton Inn Pittsburgh is perfect for travellers seeking a mid-range option with downtown accessibility. For those on a budget, try the hostel-style dormitories and private rooms at the HI Pittsburgh Hostel, which started in 2011.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Strip District is known for its specialty stores and markets, selling everything from fresh produce to handmade crafts. Sample local specialties like Pittsburgh-style popcorn or pick up some memorabilia at Yinzers, or head to Heinz History Center for unique keepsakes.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Pittsburgh is a city with a rich mix of cultures and traditions, and visitors will get an opportunity to explore and learn about its unique identity. The city is known for its blue-collar work ethic and attracts visitors with its deep roots in arts, music, and sports. As in most cities, respect to the local customs and traditions is highly appreciated, and it’s important to be culturally sensitive.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

Pittsburgh is a destination that’s worth visiting all year round. Summer offers the most pleasant weather, ideal for outdoor activities, and to partake in cultural festivals. Fall has cool temps and stunning foliage. Winter weather can be chilly but expect festive holiday vibes thanks to the city’s light-up nights. Spring is when the city blossoms, and visitors can watch the cherry blossom trees bloom throughout the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Take your time exploring the Strip District as well as the many restaurants and markets the Pittsburgh has to offer.
  • Walk along the bridge or take a boat cruise and admire the numerous buildings and the impressive skyline of the town.
  • Visit the observation deck at the city-county building for the best views of Pittsburgh.
  • Be environmentally conscious and use public transport whenever possible to reduce congestion and air pollution.
  • Acclimate yourself to Pittsburgh’s particular culture, which includes a healthy serving of hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Wander to areas downtown without enough light at night or without fellow companions.
  • Stay locked indoors on your phone, forgetting to enjoy the sights, sounds, and atmosphere of the city.
  • Considerable littering or failing to recycle will make you stand out in the trash-talking Pittsburgh community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Like any other city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings while in Downtown Pittsburgh. Areas that are well-lit are usually safe, and it’s best to stick to well-populated areas if you’re out late at night. Avoid walking by yourself in alleys at night, especially if you’re carrying valuable items. Car break-ins are prevalent, so you should never leave anything valuable in plain sight within a parked car.

Health in Downtown

For medical attention, the UPMC Mercy and Allegheny General Hospitals are near the downtown area. Additionally, there are pharmacies available in case you require a prescription or any other essentials you might need.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

The Pittsburgh International Airport is about 20 miles west of downtown, and the easiest way to get to your hotel or accommodation is by taking a Yellow Cab or Super Shuttle pick-up service.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

The Greyhound Bus Station is just a few minutes away from downtown Pittsburgh while the Mega bus stop is at a walking distance from the main attractions of downtown.

Other Options

Pittsburgh has an extensive public transit system, including buses and light rail trains, that’s run by the Port Authority of Allegheny County. The city’s bikeshare service, Healthy Ride, also offers an affordable and convenient way to explore the area on two wheels. Lastly, take a stroll via walkable downtown or hire a taxi to get around in comfort.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Walking downtown is the perfect way to experience the area, but should you wish to, check out the rideshare services such as Uber or Lyft, traditional taxis, or buses. Healthy Ride bikes are also available, and there’s even a bike lane running through the heart of the city.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations from Downtown Pittsburgh include:

  • The Andy Warhol Museum
  • Carnegie Science Center
  • North Shore Trail
  • Heinz Field and PNC Park.

More extended out-day trips can include:

  • Fallingwater, situated in the Laurel Highlands, is an hour away and is a testament to Frank Lloyd Wright’s masterpiece designing.
  • Ohiopyle State Park is a gorgeous place to visit, offering hiking trails, fishing, and stunning natural scenery.

Conclusion

Downtown Pittsburgh provides visitors with a blend of cosmopolitan feel and scenic beauty, combining the best of both worlds. With so many options for exploration, culture, and activity, a trip here is well worth your time and energy. Now that you know all the top things to do, eat, and see, head to Downtown Pittsburgh for a trip of a lifetime!
