Introduction to South Side

South Side is a vibrant neighborhood located in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. It is home to a diverse range of shops, restaurants, bars, and cultural attractions, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists. The neighborhood is divided into two parts – the East Carson Street Historic District and the South Side Flats.

List of Places to Visit in South Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pittsburgh Southside Works

Pittsburgh Southside Works is a mixed-use development that features shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues. The development is housed in a steel mill that has been converted into an indoor/outdoor shopping center. The complex is a great place to shop for souvenirs or grab a bite to eat.

The Church Brew Works

The Church Brew Works is a unique brewpub located in a former church. The building still retains many of its original features, including stained glass windows and a pipe organ. The brewery produces a range of beers, including a Scotch ale and a pious monk porter.

John Heinz History Center

The John Heinz History Center is a Smithsonian-affiliated museum that tells the story of Pittsburgh’s past. The museum’s exhibits cover a range of topics, including the Revolutionary War, the Civil War, and Pittsburgh’s industrial heritage. The center also offers a variety of educational programs and events.

Natural Attractions of South Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grandview Park

Grandview Park is located on Mount Washington and offers stunning views of downtown Pittsburgh. The park features a playground, walking trails, and a picnic area. It is a great place to spend an afternoon relaxing in the shade or taking in the panoramic views.

Three Rivers Heritage Trail

The Three Rivers Heritage Trail is a 24-mile trail that follows the banks of the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio rivers. It is an excellent place to hike, bike, or jog and offers scenic views of Pittsburgh’s skyline and riverfront.

South Shore Riverfront Park

South Shore Riverfront Park is located on the south side of the Monongahela River and offers stunning views of downtown Pittsburgh. The park features walking and biking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Side

Three Rivers Heritage Trail

The Three Rivers Heritage Trail is a well-maintained trail that follows the banks of the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio rivers. It is an excellent place to hike, bike, or jog and offers scenic views of Pittsburgh’s skyline and riverfront.

South Side Riverfront Park to the Strip District

The South Side Riverfront Park to the Strip District route is a 4.5-mile trail that passes through the heart of Pittsburgh. The trail offers stunning views of the riverfront and features several parks, including Point State Park and Allegheny Landing Park.

Best Things to Do in South Side: Activities and Tours

Pittsburgh Segway Tours

Pittsburgh Segway Tours offer an exciting way to explore Pittsburgh’s neighborhoods, including South Side. The tours are led by knowledgeable guides and cover many of the city’s top attractions.

Gateway Clipper Fleet Boat Tours

The Gateway Clipper Fleet Boat Tours are an excellent way to see Pittsburgh from a different perspective. The tours offer stunning views of the city’s skyline and riverfront and take guests on a journey down the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio rivers.

Food and Drink Scene of South Side

Must-Try Dishes

Pierogies

Pittsburgh is famous for its pierogies, and the South Side is home to some of the best. Pierogies are a type of Polish dumpling filled with potatoes, cheese, and other ingredients. You can find pierogies at many of the neighborhood’s restaurants, including Carson Street Deli and The Pub Chip Shop.

Primanti Brothers Sandwich

The Primanti Brothers sandwich is a Pittsburgh classic. The sandwich features a combination of meat, cheese, coleslaw, and fries, all piled high on a piece of Italian bread. You can find Primanti Brothers on East Carson Street.

Local Beverages

Iron City Beer

Iron City Beer is a Pittsburgh classic dating back to the 1800s. The beer is known for its smooth, crisp flavor and is a favorite among locals.

Wigle Whiskey

Wigle Whiskey is a small-batch distillery located in the Strip District. The distillery produces a range of whiskey, gin, and rum, all made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in South Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Southside Traveler’s Rest

The Southside Traveler’s Rest is a hostel located in the heart of the South Side. The hostel offers affordable dorm rooms, private rooms, and free breakfast.

Morning Glory Inn

The Morning Glory Inn is a charming bed and breakfast located in the historic South Side Flats. The inn features six guest rooms, each with its own unique decor and jacuzzi tub.

What to Buy in South Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pittsburgh Souvenirs

The South Side is home to many shops selling Pittsburgh souvenirs, including t-shirts, hats, and other memorabilia. You can find these shops along East Carson Street and at the South Side Works.

Local Culture and Customs of South Side

The South Side is home to a diverse range of cultures and customs, with a rich history of immigration. Irish, Italian, and Eastern European immigrants have all left their mark on the neighborhood’s food, architecture, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit South Side

The best time to visit South Side is in the summer, when the weather is warm and the neighborhood is buzzing with activity. The South Side hosts many festivals and events throughout the summer, including the South Side Soup Contest and the Arts & Crafts Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Side

Do:

  • Explore the neighborhood’s many shops and restaurants.
  • Try the local beer and food.
  • Take a boat tour of Pittsburgh’s three rivers.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended in your car.
  • Wander alone in unfamiliar areas at night.
  • Park illegally, as parking enforcement is strict.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Side

South Side is generally a safe neighborhood, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid theft or personal harm. Avoid leaving valuables in your car, especially in plain sight, and be aware of your surroundings when walking at night.

Health in South Side

South Side is home to several hospitals and medical centers, including UPMC Mercy and the Allegheny Health Network. Visitors should have no trouble finding medical care if needed.

Getting to South Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Side

The nearest airport to South Side is Pittsburgh International Airport, which is located approximately 25 minutes away by car. Taxis, ride-sharing services, and public transportation are all available from the airport to South Side.

Bus Stations Near South Side

The Greyhound bus station in Pittsburgh is located in the Strip District, approximately 3 miles from South Side. Public transportation and ride-sharing services are available from the Greyhound station to South Side.

Other Options

Visitors can also take Amtrak or Megabus to Pittsburgh, which both offer service to the city.

How to Get Around in South Side

South Side is easily walkable, with most major attractions located within a few blocks of each other. Biking is also a popular mode of transportation, and several bike rental shops are located within the neighborhood. Public transportation is available via the Pittsburgh bus system.

Where to Go Next from South Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Point State Park

Point State Park is located in downtown Pittsburgh and is home to the iconic fountain that sits at the confluence of the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio rivers. The park also features walking and biking trails, picnic areas, and historic landmarks.

The Strip District

The Strip District is located just across the Allegheny River from South Side and is famous for its food markets, specialty shops, and nightlife. Visitors can explore the neighborhood’s many shops and restaurants or take a food tour of the area.

Conclusion

South Side is a vibrant neighborhood with something for everyone. From its rich history and cultural attractions to its shopping and dining scene, South Side is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Pittsburgh.
