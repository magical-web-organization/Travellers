Introduction to Ross Township

Welcome to Ross Township! Located in Allegheny County, in the Pittsburgh Region of Pennsylvania, Ross Township is a quaint and charming town that offers visitors the perfect blend of history, culture, outdoor adventures, and modern amenities. Whether you’re looking for a romantic weekend getaway, family vacation, or solo adventure, Ross Township has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ross Township

If you’re wondering what to do in Ross Township, here’s a list of top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ross Township Historical Society Museum: Explore the rich history of Ross Township through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Depreciation Lands Museum: Step back in time and experience life in early Pittsburgh at this living history museum.
  • La Roche University Art Gallery: Admire the works of local and international artists at this contemporary art gallery.

Natural Attractions of Ross Township: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • North Park: Spend a day hiking, biking, fishing, or boating at this 3,075-acre park with beautiful trails and lakes.
  • Blueberry Hill Park: Enjoy a picnic, play some sports, or take a walk through the scenic pathways at this popular community park.
  • Montour Trail: Bike or hike along the scenic 63-mile trail that connects Coraopolis to Clairton

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ross Township

Ross Township is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Diamond Run Trail: This easy 3.5-mile paved trail offers breathtaking views of the North Hills and passes through charming neighborhoods and green spaces.
  • Cascade Park Trail: This 2.7-mile trail is perfect for hiking and connects Cascade Park with North Park.
  • Fahnestock Trail: This 1.6-mile trail in Blueberry Hill Park is great for hiking and nature watching.

Best Things to Do in Ross Township: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a fun family activity or a unique experience, Ross Township has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Escape Room Pittsburgh: Solve puzzles and escape a locked room in under an hour at this popular attraction.
  • Wigle Whiskey Distillery Tour: Take a guided tour and taste some of the finest whiskeys in the region at this award-winning distillery.
  • Paint Monkey: Get in touch with your creative side and join a paint and sip class.

If you have a week to spend in Ross Township, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Ross Township

Ross Township has a rich food and drink scene that is not to be missed. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: This Polish dish consists of dough filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, and onions, and is a local favorite.
  • Primanti Brothers’ Sandwich: A Pittsburgh staple, this sandwich is piled high with meat, cheese, coleslaw, and french fries.
  • Klavon’s Ice Cream: Indulge in some of the finest old-fashioned ice cream in the region at this charming ice cream parlor.

Local Beverages

  • Iron City Beer: Brewed in Pittsburgh since 1861, this beer is a local favorite.
  • Wigle Whiskey: Named after a famous Pittsburgh distiller, this whiskey is made from locally sourced ingredients and is known for its smooth taste.
  • Allegheny Coffee & Tea Exchange: Sip on a cup of freshly roasted coffee or tea at this cozy coffeehouse.

What to Buy in Ross Township: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some ideas:

  • Pittsburgh Steelers Merchandise: Show off your love for the Pittsburgh Steelers with a jersey, hat, or souvenir.

  • Pittsburgh Popcorn Company: Take home some gourmet popcorn in unique flavors like caramel bacon and Pittsburgh mix.

Local Culture and Customs of Ross Township

Ross Township has a rich and diverse culture that reflects its history and heritage. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

  • Recognize Steeler Nation: Football is a way of life in Pittsburgh, and visitors should be prepared to see plenty of black and yellow around town.
  • Embrace the Food Scene: Pittsburgh is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, so make sure to try some local specialties.
  • Be Polite: Pittsburgh is known for its friendly and welcoming residents, so be sure to say “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Ross Township

The best time to visit Ross Township is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the colors are beautiful. Summer is also a great time to visit, with plenty of outdoor activities and events, but be prepared for warmer temperatures. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it’s a great time to enjoy winter sports and holiday festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ross Township

If you’re visiting Ross Township, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do visit North Park and Blueberry Hill Park for outdoor activities.
  • Do try some pierogies and Primanti Brothers’ sandwiches.
  • Do visit the Ross Township Historical Society Museum to learn about the town’s history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip your server at restaurants.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to Steeler Nation.
  • Don’t litter or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Ross Township should be aware of the following safety and health concerns:

Safety in Ross Township

Ross Township is a safe place to visit, but visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take typical precautions.

Health in Ross Township

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccines, and it’s recommended to get a flu shot before visiting during the winter months. Ross Township has a good healthcare system and plenty of pharmacies and medical facilities.

How to Get Around in Ross Township

Ross Township is a small town, and most attractions are easily accessible by car or bike. There are also several public transportation options, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Ross Township: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Downtown Pittsburgh: Just a short drive from Ross Township, downtown Pittsburgh offers a wealth of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining.
  • Meadows Racetrack & Casino: Try your luck at this popular casino and horse racing track, located just 30 minutes from Ross Township.
  • Pittsburgh Zoo & PPG Aquarium: Explore the world of wildlife and enjoy hands-on exhibits at this fantastic zoo in Pittsburgh.

Conclusion

Ross Township may be a small town, but it’s packed with plenty of activities, attractions, and cultural heritage. Whether you’re interested in exploring the great outdoors, tasting local cuisine, or learning about the town’s history, Ross Township has something for everyone. Plan your next vacation here and experience the charm and beauty of this hidden gem in Pennsylvania.
