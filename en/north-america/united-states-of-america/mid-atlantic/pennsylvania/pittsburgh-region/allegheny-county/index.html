Introduction to Allegheny County

Allegheny County is a charming region situated in the southwestern part of Pennsylvania, nestled in the beautiful Pittsburgh Region. Known for its diverse attractions, Allegheny County is a popular destination for travelers looking for a mix of historical and natural wonders, outdoor activities, and a thriving cultural scene.

List of Places to Visit in Allegheny County

Here’s a list of must-visit attractions in Allegheny County.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Carnegie Museum of Art: One of the oldest and largest museums in the US, this museum features an extensive collection of contemporary art and natural history exhibits.
  • The Andy Warhol Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of the famous pop artist Andy Warhol.
  • Heinz History Center: This museum showcases the history of the Pittsburgh region, featuring interactive exhibits and engaging displays.

Natural Attractions of Allegheny County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Point State Park: Located at the confluence of the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio Rivers, Point State Park is a lively waterfront park with beautiful views.
  • South Park: A true naturist’s paradise, South Park offers hiking and biking trails, kayaking tours, fishing, and a picturesque golf course.
  • Allegheny County Park: This park features scenic walking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas, making it a perfect spot to relax and unwind with family and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Allegheny County

Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore the scenic wonders of Allegheny County by taking one of these routes:

  • Great Allegheny Passage: This 335-mile trail runs through Allegheny County and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and wildlife.
  • The Three Rivers Heritage Trail: This trail follows Pittsburgh’s three rivers, presenting a mix of industrial and natural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Allegheny County: Activities and Tours

  • Duquesne Incline: Built in 1877, this funicular railway takes visitors on a scenic ride up Mount Washington.
  • Kennywood Amusement Park: A great spot to enjoy thrilling rides, live entertainment, and delicious treats with the whole family.
  • Pittsburgh Zoo & PPG Aquarium: The zoo has over 4000 animals and 475 species, making it a great place for animal lovers to explore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Allegheny County

Here is a suggested itinerary to experience the best of Allegheny County in a week:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Carnegie Museum of Art and Heinz History
2 Explore the outdoors in South Park
3 Take a scenic ride on the Duquesne Incline
4 Enjoy a day full of thrilling rides at Kennywood
5 Visit the Andy Warhol Museum
6 Have a relaxing picnic day at Allegheny County Park
7 Explore the Pittsburgh Zoo & PPG Aquarium

Food and Drink Scene of Allegheny County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: These are a must-try dish in Allegheny County, with a mix of savory and sweet fillings.
  • Primanti Brothers Sandwiches: A true symbol of Pittsburgh, these sandwiches are stuffed with meat, fries, and coleslaw.
  • Tomato pie: A delicious pastry with tomato sauce and cheese, this dish is a popular snack in Pittsburgh.

Local Beverages

  • Pittsburgh-based Wigle Whiskey is a must-try drink for whiskey lovers.
  • Yuengling, the oldest brewery in the US, has its roots in Pennsylvania and is a popular beer in Allegheny County.

Where to Stay in Allegheny County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodation or budget-friendly options, Allegheny County has something for everyone. Here are a few options:

  • The Ace Hotel: Known for its trendy décor and comfortable guest rooms, this hotel is perfect for a modern and upscale stay.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites: A reasonably priced hotel option with comfortable beds and welcoming staff.

What to Buy in Allegheny County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pittsburgh-themed merchandise: You can find all sorts of souvenirs with the Pittsburgh skyline or the Steelers and Penguins logos.
  • Local honey and maple syrup: High-quality honey and maple syrup produced throughout Allegheny County are popular gifts to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Allegheny County

Allegheny County has a rich cultural history, with a unique food, arts, and music scene. Locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Allegheny County

The best time to visit Allegheny County is between March and November when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Allegheny County

Do:

  • Visit the exciting and vibrant cultural attractions of the region.
  • Try out the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Spend time exploring the natural beauty of the county.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave without trying a pierogi or primanti sandwich.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor excursions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Allegheny County

Allegheny County is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, it’s always good to be vigilant, especially in urban areas.

Health in Allegheny County

Travelers should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage, as healthcare costs in the US can be high.

Getting to Allegheny County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Allegheny County

  • Pittsburgh International Airport is located about 20 miles west of Pittsburgh, PA.

Bus Stations Near Allegheny County

  • Greyhound and Megabus both serve Pittsburgh’s bus stations.

Other Options

  • Amtrak train service connects Pittsburgh to major cities across the US.

How to Get Around in Allegheny County

Public transportation options, including buses, trolleys, and light rail, are available throughout Allegheny County. Taxis and rideshare services such as Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Allegheny County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Erie: Located about 2 hours north of Pittsburgh, Erie is known for its beautiful beaches, wineries, and outdoor activities.
  • Cleveland: Just a few hours northwest of Pittsburgh, Cleveland offers a unique blend of history, culture, and fun activities.

Conclusion

Allegheny County is a wonderful destination to experience the rich history, natural beauty and diverse culture of Western Pennsylvania. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, arts, food or just taking in the scenery, Allegheny County has something to offer for travelers of all types.
