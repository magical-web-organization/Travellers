Introduction to Ridgway

Ridgway, ​located in the ​heart of the ​Pennsylvania Wilds, is a small rural town that offers an escape to nature. With lush forests, rolling hills, and meandering streams, Ridgway is an outdoor enthusiast’s playground. Known for its charming downtown and historic attractions, Ridgway is a perfect getaway for families, couples, or solo travelers. The town is rich in culture and history.

List of Places to Visit in Ridgway

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Elk County Courthouse - Visit the impressive Elk County Courthouse, which was built in 1876 and still functions as the county’s courthouse today. It’s listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The building has been restored to its original elegance and features beautiful stained-glass windows.
  • Historic Ridgway Railroad Museum - This museum is dedicated to the history of railroad transportation in Elk County. It has a collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the local railroad industry.
  • Historic Downtown Ridgway - Wander along Main Street and discover the charming shops, galleries, and restaurants. The town’s history comes to life with its well-preserved Victorian-era architecture.

Natural Attractions of Ridgway: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bendigo State Park - Nestled in the foothills of the Allegheny Mountains, this park features hiking trails, fishing, disc golf, and picnic areas. Its name is derived from the Bendigo Furnace, which was an iron-making complex operating in the 19th century.
  • Clarion River - A designated National Wild and Scenic River, the Clarion is a great spot for kayaking and fishing.
  • Elk Country Visitors Center - This is the perfect place to learn about the state’s wild elk herd and observe its magnificent creatures from a safe distance. The center has interactive exhibits, walking trails, and a gift shop.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ridgway

Ridgway offers a wide range of trails suitable for all skill levels. The stunning natural landscapes of the town make Ridgway a destination for hikers, mountain bikers, and nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails that you must explore:

  • Little Drummer Historical Pathway - This trail is a great option for beginners or families with young children. It is a historically rich, educational trail that gives visitors the chance to learn more about the history of the area. The trail is only 1.25 miles long, and it includes interpretive signs that detail local history.
  • Homestead Trail - This is a moderate difficulty level trail that offers beautiful views of Elk County. It is 3.5 miles long and features an old homestead at its midway point.
  • Tannery Dam Trail - This trail is a great option for experienced hikers. It is a 9-mile trail that passes through the Allegheny National Forest and features a beautiful waterfall and scenic wildlife views.

Best Things to Do in Ridgway: Activities and Tours

  • Ridgway Chainsaw Carvers Rendezvous - This is an annual event that takes place in February and is the largest gathering of chainsaw carvers in the world. Visitors can witness artists from around the world transform logs into beautiful works of art.
  • Allegheny Mountain Resort - This resort offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including horseback riding, fishing, and skiing in the winter. The resort is located in a secluded valley and is perfect for those looking for a peaceful retreat.
  • Olde Schoolhouse Village Shoppes - This marketplace features artisans, food, and gift shops in a restored school building that dates back to 1891. The building has been updated with modern amenities and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Food and Drink Scene of Ridgway

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Game - Sampling the local game is a wise choice when in Ridgway. From venison and bison to elk and rabbit, regional cuisine takes center stage in this small town.
  • Pennsylvania Schnitzel - This dish is similar to Southern-fried chicken, but a spiced egg and flour coating on the chicken makes it special.
  • Apple Butter - Apple butter is a Pennsylvania Dutch specialty that is made using a long-simmering process that reduces apples to a smooth, butter-like texture. It’s generally spread on toast or mixed into hot oatmeal.

Local Beverages

  • Straub Brewery - Established in 1872, this brewery is one of the oldest in the United States. Its signature beer is the popular IPA, but its specialty is a seasonal lager.
  • Winery at Wilcox - This family-owned winery in Wilcox produces unique, flavorful wines. Its products include red, white, and fruit wines.

Where to Stay in Ridgway: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ridgway Chain Saw Carver’s Inn - This hotel is close to the downtown area and offers a comfortable and welcoming stay for visitors.
  • Garden Cottage Bed & Breakfast - A charming cottage with thoughtful touches, this property is perfect for a cozy getaway.
  • Ridgway Adventure Basecamp Hostel - This hostel is perfect for those on a budget and brings together like-minded travelers to share experiences.

What to Buy in Ridgway: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Elk-tastic Souvenirs - A plethora of local vendors offer handmade elk merchandise for you to take home.
  • Pennsylvania German Crafts - Traditional crafts, including wooden toys and rag dolls, intricately designed ceramics and pottery, and painted furniture items can be found in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ridgway

Ridgway takes pride in its rural heritage. People here are friendly and welcoming. Visitors must be sure to respect local customs and traditions. Pennsylvania Dutch is often spoken in town, so it’s worth knowing a few basic phrases if you’re not familiar with the language.

Best Time to Visit Ridgway

The best time to visit Ridgway is in the fall, when the leaves change color and the town becomes a beautiful palette of oranges and yellows. The winter months also offer ample opportunity for skiing and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ridgway

Do:

  • Do visit the Elk Country Visitors Center and see the elk herd.
  • Do try the local Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine.
  • Do explore some of the hiking trails in the surrounding area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable hiking shoes and outdoor gear.
  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing if you’re visiting in the winter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ridgway

Ridgway is a small town and is considered safe for visitors. However, it is always wise to use common sense and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Ridgway

There are no major health hazards or concerns in Ridgway. It’s always a good idea to carry a basic first aid kit when hiking or exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Ridgway: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ridgway

  • DuBois Regional Airport - Located approximately 33 miles southeast of Ridgway, this small airport offers daily flights to Pittsburgh.

Bus Stations Near Ridgway

  • Greyhound Bus Station in St Marys - This station is approximately 15 miles southwest of Ridgway and offers daily bus services to destinations across the United States.

Other Options

  • Rental Car - If you’re planning on exploring the area around Ridgway, renting a car is a great option.

How to Get Around in Ridgway

The best way to get around Ridgway is by car. There are several car rental companies in the area, and the town is easy to navigate.

Where to Go Next from Ridgway: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Allegheny National Forest - This National Forest is one of the most beautiful natural settings in the region. It offers endless hiking and biking trails, as well as fishing and hunting opportunities.
  • Pennsylvania Wilds - A patchwork of forests, mountains, and streams, this vast region offers limitless outdoor adventures.
  • Cook Forest State Park - Another great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, this park is home to the Clarion River, a National Wild and Scenic River.

Conclusion

Ridgway is a perfect destination for those seeking a quiet escape into nature. With its historic attractions, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and comfortable accommodations, Ridgway has something to offer every type of traveler. Plan your next getaway to this small town and discover the beauty of the Pennsylvania Wilds.
