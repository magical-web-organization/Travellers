Lock Haven is a charming town located in the heart of Pennsylvania Wilds, offering visitors a wide range of recreational and cultural activities. From hiking and biking to museums and historical attractions, Lock Haven is sure to satisfy every traveler. Here is a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip to Lock Haven.

Introduction to Lock Haven

Lock Haven is a small town in Central Pennsylvania, located in the Clinton County. It is known for its beautiful natural scenery, outdoor recreational activities, and rich history. Visitors can explore various museums and historical sites, as well as enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and kayaking.

List of Places to Visit in Lock Haven

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clinton County Historical Society Museum: This museum explores the history of the town and has collections that include memorabilia from the 1800s, Native American artifacts, and Civil War memorabilia.
  • Piper Aviation Museum: This museum displays the history of the Piper Aviation Corporation and features interactive exhibits on the company’s contributions to aviation history.
  • Heisey House: This historical house museum is a restored Victorian mansion that showcases life in the late 19th century. Visitors can see artifacts such as the original furniture and décor.
  • Castanea Railroad Station Museum: This museum is a restored train station with exhibits on the history of railroading in the region.

Natural Attractions of Lock Haven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bald Eagle State Park: This state park is located on the shores of the Bald Eagle Lake and offers many recreational opportunities, including camping, hiking, fishing, and boating.
  • Pine Creek Gorge: Known as the “Grand Canyon of Pennsylvania,” Pine Creek Gorge is a beautiful scenic area ideal for hiking, biking, and camping.
  • Susquehanna River Water Trail: This network of paddle trails offers over 23 miles of scenic waterways to explore, including Lock Haven’s water trailhead.
  • West Branch Susquehanna River Water Trail: With 228 miles of waterway to explore, visitors can enjoy kayaking, fishing, and bird-watching along the West Branch Susquehanna River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lock Haven

  • Great Island Trail: This 2.5-mile trail is perfect for a stroll or bike ride along the riverbanks of the Susquehanna River and is known for its wildlife.
  • Bald Eagle Valley Trail: This 7-mile trail follows the Bald Eagle Creek through the scenic Bald Eagle Valley.
  • Lock Haven River Walk: The 2.75-mile path follows the scenic Susquehanna River, offering beautiful views and opportunities for fishing and bird-watching.

Best Things to Do in Lock Haven: Activities and Tours

  • Lock Haven University Art Galleries: Art enthusiasts will enjoy the university’s art galleries, which feature rotating exhibits.
  • Clinton County Arts Council Gallery: This gallery showcases local artists and provides art classes for all ages.
  • Millbrook Playhouse: Enjoy a live performance at this professional theater located in Mill Hall.
  • Lock Haven Pedal & Paddle: Rent bicycles or kayaks to explore the town and scenic waterways.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lock Haven

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Lock Haven

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken and Waffles: A classic Southern-style breakfast served at Stella A’s, a local favorite.
  • Burger: The Lock Haven Hotel, a historical hotel and pub, serves delicious burgers made from locally sourced beef.
  • Pizza: Dam Donuts & Deli is famous for their homemade pizza.

Local Beverages

  • Brewery Tour and Tasting: Visit the Rusty Rail Brewing Company, a local microbrewery that offers tours and tastings.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit the Mount Nittany Vineyard and Winery, a nearby winery that offers tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Lock Haven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Lock Haven: A modern hotel close to the downtown area.
  • Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott: A well-priced hotel located near the university.
  • Hemlock Campground & Cottages: A family-run campground and cottage rental with outdoor activities just a short drive from Lock Haven.

What to Buy in Lock Haven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Piper Aviation Corporation Memorabilia: Find unique souvenirs at the Piper Aviation Museum gift shop.
  • Clinton County Historical Society Souvenirs: Shop for unique gifts at the historical society’s gift shop, including books, toys, and clothing.
  • Wineries and Breweries: Take home a bottle of wine or beer from a local vineyard or brewery.

Local Culture and Customs of Lock Haven

Lock Haven is a small town with welcoming locals who take pride in their close-knit community. Visitors are expected to show respect for local customs and traditions, especially when visiting cultural and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Lock Haven

The best time to visit Lock Haven is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. The fall and spring are also excellent times to visit, with mild temperatures and vibrant foliage. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it offers a unique winter wonderland experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lock Haven

Do:

  • Hike and bike in the natural areas surrounding Lock Haven.
  • Explore the town’s museums and historical sites.
  • Sample local food and drink, including craft beer and wine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lock Haven

Lock Haven is a safe town, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the natural areas and hiking trails. Visitors should be aware of potential risks such as wildlife and slippery terrain.

Health in Lock Haven

Lock Haven has healthcare facilities and emergency services available for visitors. Visitors should take precautions against tick-borne illnesses in the summer months, such as using insect repellent and checking for ticks regularly.

Getting to Lock Haven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lock Haven

  • University Park Airport: Located in State College, approximately 35 miles from Lock Haven, this airport offers service from major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Lock Haven

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Williamsport, approximately 26 miles from Lock Haven, this bus station offers connections to major cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the town and surrounding areas.
  • Taxi Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Lock Haven

Lock Haven is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bicycles or kayaks to explore the area or use taxies and ride-sharing services for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Lock Haven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Penn’s Cave and Wildlife Park: Located in Centre Hall, approximately 25 miles from Lock Haven, this tourist attraction features underground caverns and a wildlife park.
  • Raystown Lake: Located in Huntingdon County, approximately 50 miles from Lock Haven, this large water reservoir offers outdoor recreation opportunities including boating and fishing.
  • State College: Located approximately 40 miles from Lock Haven, State College is home to Pennsylvania State University and features many cultural and recreational attractions.

Conclusion

Lock Haven offers visitors a unique blend of outdoor recreational opportunities and cultural attractions. From hikes in state parks to exploring museums and historic sites, visitors are sure to find something to enjoy. With its welcoming locals, charming eateries, and nearby attractions, Lock Haven is an ideal destination for a weekend or week-long trip.
