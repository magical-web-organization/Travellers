Introduction to Mount Jewett

Located in the heart of the Pennsylvania Wilds, Mount Jewett is a charming mountain town that boasts beautiful scenery, adventurous activities, and a rich cultural heritage. Nestled in the midst of the Allegheny National Forest, this small town is a treasure trove of natural wonders, from breathtaking views of mountain ranges to pristine lakes and forests.

Visitors to Mount Jewett will find a wide range of activities to suit all interests, including hiking, biking, skiing, and fishing. The town is also home to a vibrant arts scene and a number of historical and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Jewett

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zippo/Case Museum: Learn about the history of America’s most famous lighter and knife makers, Zippo and Case, at this museum. Visitors can see vintage lighters and knives, tour the factory, and even take part in a hands-on manufacturing demonstration.

  • McKean County Historical Society: Explore the rich history of McKean County at this museum, which houses a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents. Exhibits cover everything from Native American history to the industrial revolution and beyond.

  • Old Smethport Jail Museum: Step back in time and experience life in a 19th-century jail at this museum. Visitors can tour the cells and learn about the history of the jail and the prisoners who were housed there.

Natural Attractions of Mount Jewett: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • State Game Lands No. 44: Located just north of town, this 7,000-acre game lands offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hunting, fishing, hiking, and camping. It is also home to a number of wildlife species, including deer, turkey, and bear.

  • Kinzua Bridge State Park: This park is home to the Kinzua Viaduct, which was once the world’s longest and tallest railroad structure. Despite being partially destroyed by a tornado in 2003, it remains a popular attraction for its breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness.

  • Allegheny Reservoir: This scenic reservoir is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating. Those who prefer quieter activities can take in the stunning views from the many trails that surround the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Jewett

  • Wildcat Hollow Trail: This 18-mile trail offers a challenging but rewarding hike through the heart of the Allegheny National Forest. The trail is marked by interesting rock formations and wildlife, and the views from the high ridges are stunning.

  • Bucktail Trail: This 30-mile trail is a favorite for local mountain bikers, with its challenging terrain and beautiful scenery. The trail passes through old growth forests, along streams and rivers, and even includes a few waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Mount Jewett: Activities and Tours

  • Starlight Wine and Brew Tours: Discover the wineries and breweries of the Pennsylvania Wilds with this guided tour. Guests will be taken to a selection of local producers, where they’ll get to sample their wares and learn about the brewing and winemaking process.

  • Cherry Springs State Park Observatory: This park is one of the best places in the world for stargazing, thanks to its remote location and the strict lighting regulations in the surrounding area. Visitors can take a guided tour of the observatory and attend stargazing events throughout the year.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Jewett

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch pot pie: This hearty stew-style dish features chicken and vegetables in a rich broth, topped with squares of pasta.

  • Baked corn: A savory side dish of corn, onions, and bacon, baked in a casserole dish.

Local Beverages

  • Dogfish Head 90 Minute IPA: This popular IPA from the nearby Dogfish Head brewery is a favorite among local beer lovers.

  • Mead: This honey-based drink has a long history in Pennsylvania, and several local producers create unique meads using locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Mount Jewett: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Holley: This historic hotel in downtown Mount Jewett offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.

  • Blackwolf Run Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms, a home-cooked breakfast, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Mount Jewett: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Zippo lighters and Case knives: These iconic American brands are based right in Mount Jewett, so it’s the perfect place to pick up a souvenir.

  • Local honey: Pennsylvania is known for its high-quality honey, and several local producers offer a range of varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Jewett

Mount Jewett has a strong cultural heritage rooted in the logging and mining industries that once dominated the region. Today, the town is home to a thriving arts community as well as a number of historic sites and museums that celebrate the area’s rich history.

Best Time to Visit Mount Jewett

The best time to visit Mount Jewett is between May and October, when the weather is mild and the natural beauty of the region is on full display. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities during this time, including hiking, camping, swimming, and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Jewett

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities the region has to offer.

  • Do explore the local history and culture by visiting the many museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the rugged nature of the region – be sure to pack appropriate clothing and gear for your outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Jewett

While Mount Jewett is generally a safe place to visit, visitors should take precautions when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities. Be sure to bring appropriate clothing and gear, and always let someone know where you’ll be going before heading out into the wilderness.

Health in Mount Jewett

Visitors to Mount Jewett should be aware of the risks associated with tick-borne illnesses like Lyme disease. Be sure to wear long pants and sleeves and use insect repellent when hiking or spending time in wooded areas.

Getting to Mount Jewett: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Jewett

  • Bradford Regional Airport (BFD): 23 miles away, with flights to Pittsburgh and Philadelphia.

  • Erie International Airport (ERI): 128 miles away, with flights to major U.S. cities and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Mount Jewett

  • Greyhound Bus Station (BFD): 22 miles away, with routes to major U.S. cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in the area, with pickup and drop-off locations at nearby airports and in towns throughout the region.

How to Get Around in Mount Jewett

The best way to get around in Mount Jewett is by car, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from nearby towns or airports.

Where to Go Next from Mount Jewett: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Allegheny National Forest: Just south of Mount Jewett, this forest is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with thousands of acres of pristine wilderness to explore.

  • Ellicottville, NY: Located just across the border in New York, this charming ski town offers fantastic skiing, dining, and shopping.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Mount Jewett has something to offer. With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and wide range of activities, this charming mountain town is a must-see destination for anyone visiting Pennsylvania.
