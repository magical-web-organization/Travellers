Introduction to Clearfield

Clearfield is a small town in the heart of Pennsylvania Wilds, a region known for its vast expanses of preserved woodlands and breathtaking scenic beauty. It is situated in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America, making it an ideal destination for visitors looking for a peaceful and tranquil getaway from the bustling cities.

Clearfield is steeped in a rich history and culture, with several attractions that showcase the town’s heritage. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking, or explore the town’s museums and historical sites. Whether you are traveling solo, with family, or friends, Clearfield has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Clearfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clearfield County Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the rich heritage of Clearfield County, featuring exhibits on local history, genealogy, and culture.
  • Joseph and Elizabeth Shaw Public Library: This historic library was constructed in 1901 and is a cherished landmark in the town of Clearfield.
  • Clearfield County Courthouse: This magnificent courthouse dates back to 1860 and is a must-see for visitors interested in history and architecture.
  • Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania: The Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania is a testament to the state’s rich railroad history. Visitors can learn about the locomotives, rail cars, antique cars, and other exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Clearfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bilger’s Rocks: This iconic natural wonder is a must-see attraction in Clearfield. Visitors can explore the giant rock formations, hike the trails, and enjoy the spectacular views.
  • Curwensville Lake: Curwensville Lake is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming. The lake also features several hiking trails that provide visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Moshannon State Forest: The Moshannon State Forest is a vast expanse of unspoiled wilderness. Visitors can hike the trails, go fishing, or enjoy a picnic amidst the beautiful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clearfield

  • Greater Clearfield Area: Visitors can explore the many hiking and biking trails around Clearfield, including the Elliot State Park, the Black Moshannon State Park, and the Quehanna Wild Area.
  • Clearfield Heritage Trail: This 10-mile trail winds its way through the heart of the town and provides visitors with stunning views of Clearfield County’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Clearfield: Activities and Tours

  • Beaver Tours: Visitors can take a guided tour of the many lakes and waterways around Clearfield to view the local beaver population in their natural habitat.
  • Sportsman Adventure Tours: Sportsman Adventure Tours offers visitors a range of outdoor adventures, including ATV tours, rock climbing, and hiking.
  • Clearfield County Fair: Every August, Clearfield hosts a week-long county fair, featuring rides, food, entertainment, and agricultural exhibits.

Food and Drink Scene of Clearfield

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Pot Pie: This hearty dish consists of square noodles, chicken, and vegetables, all cooked in a broth and baked in a casserole dish.
  • Pierogies: Pierogies are a staple of Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine. These dumplings are filled with potatoes, cheese, and onions and are usually served boiled or fried.
  • Shoofly Pie: Shoofly pie is a sweet molasses-filled pie that is a specialty of the Amish community in Pennsylvania.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: Yuengling is America’s oldest brewery, and its beer is a Pennsylvania tradition.
  • Pennsylvania Dutch Birch Beer: This sweet, non-alcoholic beverage has been a staple of Pennsylvania Dutch and Amish communities for centuries.
  • Whiskey: Clearfield is home to several distilleries that produce top-notch whiskey.

Where to Stay in Clearfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Plus Clearfield: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and an indoor pool.
  • Rodeway Inn Clearfield: This budget-friendly hotel provides guests with clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Harmony Hall Bed and Breakfast: This charming guesthouse is situated in the heart of Clearfield and features cozy rooms and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Clearfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amish Quilts: Amish Quilts are a staple of the Pennsylvania Dutch community and are available for purchase in Clearfield.
  • Dutch Hex Signs: These colorful circular patterns are a symbol of the Pennsylvania Dutch community and are commonly hung on barns and homes in the area.
  • Locally-Made Whiskey: Clearfield is home to several distilleries that produce award-winning whiskey, making it a perfect souvenir for whiskey lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Clearfield

Clearfield is known for its rich Pennsylvania Dutch heritage and its Amish community. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions when visiting the area. The pace of life is slower in Clearfield, and visitors should take it easy and enjoy the simple pleasures of the region.

Best Time to Visit Clearfield

The best time to visit Clearfield is in the fall when the leaves are changing colors, providing a breathtaking backdrop for outdoor activities. However, visitors can enjoy the beauty of Clearfield year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clearfield

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites.
  • Take an outdoor adventure and enjoy the beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Try the local food and drink specialties.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Over pack. Clearfield’s laid-back atmosphere means that casual attire is appropriate for most outings.
  • Miss out on the local whiskey scene.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clearfield

Clearfield is a safe area with low levels of crime. Visitors should exercise common sense and take the usual precautionary measures, such as locking their doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Clearfield

Clearfield has access to several healthcare facilities and hospitals. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage during their stay.

Getting to Clearfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clearfield

The closest airport to Clearfield is the University Park Airport in State College, which is approximately 45 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Clearfield

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in Clearfield and provides visitors with access to regional transportation.

Other Options

Visitors can explore Clearfield and the surrounding area on foot, bicycle, or by car.

How to Get Around in Clearfield

Clearfield is a small town that is best explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Clearfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

State College, Pennsylvania

State College is a vibrant university town known for its charming downtown area and rich cultural offerings. It is located approximately 45 miles away from Clearfield.

Allegheny National Forest

The Allegheny National Forest is a vast expanse of preserved woodlands and is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. It is located approximately 60 miles away from Clearfield.

Conclusion

Clearfield is a haven for nature lovers and history buffs looking for a quiet retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Clearfield has something for everyone. Explore the town’s rich heritage, indulge in the local food and drink specialties, and immerse yourself in the beautiful surroundings of Pennsylvania Wilds.
