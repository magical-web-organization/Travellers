Introduction to Bradford

Nestled in the heart of Pennsylvania Wilds, Bradford is a quaint city filled with natural beauty, rich history and warm people. Its pristine landscapes, endless outdoor activities, and charming downtown make it the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff or just looking for a peaceful getaway, Bradford has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through all the top things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Bradford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zippo/Case Museum: Explore the history of Zippo lighters and Case knives at this museum, showcasing some of the rarest and unique pieces. Take a factory tour and watch the skilled artisans at work.

  • Bradford Railroad Museum: Travel back in time and learn about the role of railroads in Bradford’s early days. The museum has a working HO scale train layout, artifacts, and exhibits showcasing the rich history of the railroad industry.

  • Old Bradford Cemetery: This peaceful cemetery dates back to the 19th century and is the final resting place of many notable figures in Bradford’s history, including pioneers, politicians, and Civil War veterans.

Natural Attractions of Bradford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Allegheny National Forest: Covering over 517,000 acres, the Allegheny National Forest has scenic vistas, diverse wildlife, and endless recreational opportunities. Hiking, boating, fishing, and camping are just a few of the activities offered here.

  • Kinzua Bridge State Park: Once the highest railroad bridge in the world, Kinzua Bridge now stands as a stunning engineering marvel and offers visitors breathtaking views of the Kinzua Gorge.

  • Marilla Reservoir: This 95-acre reservoir is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bradford

  • Rimrock Trail: This 11-mile loop trail is perfect for those seeking a challenging hike with stunning views of the Allegheny Plateau.

  • Marilla Trail: This 4-mile trail encircles the Marilla Reservoir and offers an easy hike that is perfect for families.

  • Tuna Valley Trail: This 9-mile trail is perfect for both hiking and biking and is a great way to explore the beautiful scenery of Bradford.

Best Things to Do in Bradford: Activities and Tours

  • Go on a Zipline Adventure: Experience the thrill of ziplining at the Zoar Valley Adventure Company. They have zip lines ranging from 40 to 850 feet long across the gorge.

  • Take a Kayak Tour: Kinzua Kayak offers guided kayak tours on the Allegheny Reservoir, including sunrise and sunset tours.

  • Visit the Crook Farm Country Market: A weekly market offering fresh produce, local crafts, and homemade baked goods.

Food and Drink Scene of Bradford

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bradford Baked Beans: A local recipe, these beans are slow-cooked with bacon and molasses, giving them a sweet and savory flavor.

  • Smoked Trout: Trout smoked with hickory wood is a local delicacy and can be found on many menus throughout the area.

  • Beef on Weck: This sandwich is a regional specialty, made with slow-cooked roast beef on a Kummelweck roll, topped with horseradish and salt.

Local Beverages

  • Straub Beer: A family-operated brewery that has been crafting beer since 1872. Straub Beer offers a variety of brews like American Lager, Hefeweizen, and Pilsner.

  • Four Mile Brewing: A locally owned and operated craft brewery with a rotating selection of creative and unique beers.

Where to Stay in Bradford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pine Acres Country Club: A family-friendly resort featuring a golf course, pool, and tennis courts.

  • Lantern Motel: A budget-friendly lodging option, the Lantern Motel offers comfortable rooms and a central location.

  • Bradford Inn: This historic hotel features modern amenities and is situated downtown, allowing easy access to all the attractions.

What to Buy in Bradford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Zippo Lighters: A piece of Bradford history, these lighters make for a unique and practical souvenir.

  • Case Knifes: A world-renowned brand originated in Bradford, these knives are known for their quality and durability.

  • Maple Syrup: Pure maple syrup produced locally is a great souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Bradford

Bradford residents are known for their warmth and welcoming nature towards visitors. Common courtesies such as saying “please” and “thank you” go a long way in showing respect. Smoking is prohibited in restaurants, bars and public buildings in Pennsylvania.

Best Time to Visit Bradford

The best time to visit Bradford is during the fall when the leaves change color and the temperatures are comfortable for outdoor activities. Winter is also a beautiful time to visit, with opportunities for skiing and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bradford

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings and the wildlife.

  • Try local cuisine and beer.

  • Take time to explore the small towns surrounding Bradford.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in prohibited areas.

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Leave valuables in the car unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bradford

Bradford is a relatively safe destination, but as with any place, visitors should practice common-sense safety measures such as locking doors, not leaving valuables in cars, avoiding walking alone late at night, and staying in well-lit areas.

Health in Bradford

There are hospitals and medical clinics in the area, but visitors should always have adequate travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Getting to Bradford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bradford

The two closest commercial airports to Bradford are Buffalo Niagara International Airport and Pittsburgh International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Bradford

Greyhound offers daily bus service to Bradford. There are also several regional bus companies that service the area.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies that operate in the area, making it easy for visitors to explore the city and surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Bradford

The best way to get around Bradford is by car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Bradford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Olean, NY

Just over the New York border, Olean offers a charming downtown, historic attractions, and recreational opportunities in the Allegany State Park.

Smethport, PA

A peaceful town with a vibrant arts scene, Smethport is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, including the Kinzua Bridge State Park.

Conclusion

Bradford is the perfect destination for those looking for a peaceful getaway surrounded by natural beauty and rich history. From hiking trails and outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and tasty local cuisine, there is something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the charm of Bradford, Pennsylvania!
