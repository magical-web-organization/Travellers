Introduction to Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania is a state in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. It is known for its rich history, diverse culture, and natural beauty. From bustling cities like Philadelphia and Pittsburgh to scenic countryside and mountains, Pennsylvania offers something for every type of traveler. Plan your Pennsylvania travel with this comprehensive guide that covers everything from top attractions and outdoor adventures to local culture and safety tips.

List of Places to Visit in Pennsylvania

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pennsylvania is home to many significant museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the state’s rich cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Independence National Historical Park
  • Gettysburg National Military Park
  • The Liberty Bell in Philadelphia
  • The Philadelphia Museum of Art
  • The Andy Warhol Museum in Pittsburgh
  • The Hershey Story Museum
  • The National Civil War Museum in Harrisburg

Natural Attractions of Pennsylvania: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pennsylvania’s natural beauty offers a wealth of outdoor adventures and scenic views. From hiking and biking trails to lakes and forests, here are some top attractions:

  • The Pocono Mountains
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park
  • Ricketts Glen State Park
  • Ohiopyle State Park
  • The Great Allegheny Passage
  • The Laurel Highlands
  • Lake Erie

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pennsylvania

With more than 2,000 miles of hiking and biking trails, Pennsylvania is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some noteworthy routes include:

  • The Great Allegheny Passage: a 150-mile rail-trail that connects Pittsburgh to Cumberland, Maryland.
  • The Pine Creek Rail Trail: a 62-mile trail in Pine Creek Gorge, known as the Grand Canyon of Pennsylvania.
  • The Schuylkill River Trail: a 130-mile trail that stretches from Philadelphia to Pottsville.
  • The Appalachian Trail: a 229-mile section of the famed trail runs through Pennsylvania.

Best Things to Do in Pennsylvania: Activities and Tours

Apart from museums and natural attractions, Pennsylvania also offers a range of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some top picks:

  • Visit Amish Country in Lancaster County and learn about the unique lifestyle of the Amish community.
  • Explore Longwood Gardens, one of the premier botanical gardens in the country.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic countryside of the Pocono Mountains.
  • Experience Hershey’s Chocolate World, a themed attraction that offers chocolate tours and tastings.
  • Go on a brewery or winery tour to sample some of Pennsylvania’s finest beverages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pennsylvania

Day 1: Explore Philadelphia’s historic district and visit Independence National Historical Park, including the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall.

Day 2: Drive to Lancaster County and visit Amish Country. Take a buggy tour and enjoy homemade Amish cuisine.

Day 3: Head to Hershey and explore Hershey’s Chocolate World, Hershey Gardens, and Hersheypark.

Day 4: Drive to Gettysburg and explore Gettysburg National Military Park and the town’s historic district.

Day 5: Hike in Ricketts Glen State Park and enjoy breathtaking views of the waterfalls.

Day 6: Go on a hot air balloon ride over the Pocono Mountains and visit the charming town of Jim Thorpe.

Day 7: Explore Pittsburgh and visit the Andy Warhol Museum, the Carnegie Museum of Natural History, and PNC Park for a baseball game.

Food and Drink Scene of Pennsylvania

Must-Try Dishes

Pennsylvania’s cuisine is a blend of traditional Pennsylvania Dutch, Amish, and Appalachian fare with modern twists. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Philly Cheesesteak: A sandwich made with thinly sliced steak, cheese, and onions on a long roll.
  • Scrapple: A breakfast pork dish made with cornmeal and spices.
  • Shoofly Pie: A molasses-based pie that’s a standard of Pennsylvania Dutch cooking.
  • Pretzels: Pennsylvania is famous for its German-style pretzels, available at various bakery and street vendors.
  • Pierogies: A dish of Polish origin consisting of boiled dumplings filled with a variety of fillings, including mashed potatoes, sauerkraut, cheese, or meat.

Local Beverages

Pennsylvania is home to many microbreweries, wineries, and distilleries, known for their craft beers, wines, and spirits. Some noteworthy beverages to try include:

  • Yuengling beer: America’s oldest brewery located in Pottsville, Pennsylvania.
  • Hershey’s Chocolate Wine: A delightful combination of Hershey’s chocolate and wine.
  • Pennsylvania Rye Whiskey: A type of American whiskey produced in Pennsylvania, known for its spicy and fruity flavors.

Where to Stay in Pennsylvania: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pennsylvania has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some top picks:

  • The Rittenhouse Hotel in Philadelphia: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • The Inn at the Amish Farmhouse in Lancaster County: A charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Amish Country.
  • Nemacolin Woodlands Resort in the Laurel Highlands: A luxurious resort that offers golf, spa, and outdoor activities.
  • The Union Hotel in Jim Thorpe: A historic hotel located in the charming town of Jim Thorpe.
  • HI Philadelphia Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of downtown Philadelphia.

What to Buy in Pennsylvania: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pennsylvania has a wide range of locally-made products that make for unique souvenirs. Some top options include:

  • Pennsylvania Dutch hex signs: A colorful symbol of the Pennsylvania Dutch culture and tradition.
  • Hershey’s Chocolate: A classic souvenir from the chocolate capital of the world.
  • Amish crafts: Handmade quilts, furniture, and baskets are typical of Amish culture and make for excellent souvenirs.
  • Yuengling beer memorabilia: Souvenirs from America’s oldest brewery, located in Pottsville, Pennsylvania.

Local Culture and Customs of Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania has a blend of cultures, with influence from the Pennsylvania Dutch, Amish, and other European heritage. Here are some tips for understanding local customs:

  • Pennsylvanians are known for their friendly, hospitable nature. Don’t be surprised if someone strikes up a conversation with you in a store or on the street.
  • The Pennsylvania Dutch have a distinctive culture, including dialect, clothing, and food traditions. Show respect for their traditions, especially in their homes and places of worship.
  • Pennsylvania is known for its world-class sports teams, including the Eagles, Steelers, and Phillies. If you’re visiting during the season, consider attending a game to experience the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Pennsylvania

The best time to visit Pennsylvania depends on your interests and the activities you plan to do. Generally, the best time to visit Pennsylvania is in the spring or fall when the temperatures are mild, and the foliage is beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pennsylvania

Do:

  • Try a Philly Cheesesteak: This quintessential sandwich is a must-try when in Philadelphia.
  • Visit Amish Country: Lancaster County offers a unique insight into the traditional Amish culture.
  • Explore Pennsylvania’s natural beauty: From the Pocono Mountains to the Allegheny National Forest, Pennsylvania offers some of the most stunning natural scenery in the country.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate Pennsylvania’s size: This large state can take a long time to drive around, so plan your itinerary accordingly.
  • Don’t miss Philadelphia’s historical attractions: Independence Hall, the Liberty Bell, and the Benjamin Franklin Museum are all worth a visit.
  • Don’t overlook Pittsburgh: This vibrant city has a lot to offer, including outstanding museums, restaurants, and sports teams.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania is a relatively safe state, with its share of both rural and urban areas. Here are some tips to stay safe:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in large cities like Philadelphia and Pittsburgh.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar neighborhoods.
  • Always lock your car doors and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania has top-quality healthcare facilities, including many world-renowned hospitals and medical centers. It’s important to have adequate travel insurance when visiting Pennsylvania, as medical care can be expensive.

Getting to Pennsylvania: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pennsylvania is easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Pennsylvania

  • Philadelphia International Airport: The largest airport in Pennsylvania, located 20 minutes from downtown Philadelphia.
  • Pittsburgh International Airport: The second-largest airport in Pennsylvania, located 30 minutes from downtown Pittsburgh.

Bus Stations Near Pennsylvania

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The largest bus service provider in the country, serving many Pennsylvania cities.
  • Megabus: A budget-friendly bus service with routes that connect many cities in Pennsylvania.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: A national railway service with direct routes that connect major Pennsylvania cities.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient option for exploring Pennsylvania’s many attractions.

How to Get Around in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania’s size can make getting around a challenge, but there are plenty of options for transportation. Some top options include:

  • Driving: Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Pennsylvania, especially if you plan to visit rural areas.
  • Public transportation: Pennsylvania has a range of public transportation options, including buses, trains, and subways.
  • Biking: Many cities and rural areas in Pennsylvania have excellent biking trails that allow you to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Pennsylvania: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pennsylvania is a great starting point for exploring the Mid-Atlantic region. Some top nearby destinations include:

  • New York City: Just two hours from Philadelphia, the Big Apple is one of the most iconic cities in the world.
  • Washington, D.C.: Just a few hours from Pennsylvania, the nation’s capital is filled with historical landmarks and cultural institutions.
  • Atlantic City: This famous beach town on the New Jersey shore is just an hour from Philadelphia, known for its casinos, boardwalk, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, natural beauty, or outdoor adventures, Pennsylvania is a state that has something for everyone. Plan your Pennsylvania travel using this comprehensive guide and get ready to experience the best of this diverse and exciting state.
