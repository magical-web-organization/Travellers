Introduction to Havertown

Nestled in Haverford Township, Delaware County, in the Philadelphia Region, Havertown is one of the most sought-after locations in Pennsylvania. With its close proximity to Philadelphia, it offers visitors the best of both worlds. It’s a tranquil and peaceful town, but it also has all the conveniences of a big city. With plenty of natural attractions, cultural hotspots, and outdoor adventures, it’s easy to see why Havertown is a popular destination for families, couples, and solo travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Havertown

Here are some of the must-visit places in Havertown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haverford College Arboretum - A 216-acre arboretum and botanical garden featuring more than 4000 types of plants.
  • The Franklin Institute - A science museum with hands-on exhibits and interactive displays that make learning fun.
  • Reading Terminal Market - A bustling indoor market with local produce, artisanal foods, and specialty shops.
  • Independence National Historical Park - A sprawling park that preserves the history of Philadelphia and America’s founding.

Natural Attractions of Havertown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Wissahickon Valley Park - A picturesque park with panoramic views of the Wissahickon Creek.
  • John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge – An urban oasis that offers a serene escape with its rolling hills and many native waterfowl species.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park - A must-visit for history and nature buffs alike, this park offers visitors miles of hiking trails amid the stunning scenery, with plenty of opportunities to explore the Revolutionary War history of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Havertown

Be sure to take in the stunning scenery surrounding Havertown with one of the many hiking and biking trails in the area.

Some of the popular hiking and biking routes are:

  • The Schuylkill River Trail - A 120-mile trail that winds through scenic countryside, providing breathtaking views of the river and surrounding landscape.
  • The Perkiomen Trail - A 19.5-mile trail through Montgomery County that features a variety of scenic landscapes and thrilling wildlife sightings.
  • The Wissahickon Trail - A ten-mile trail through parkland that offers a relaxing escape from the commotion of the city.

Best Things to Do in Havertown: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Havertown:

  • Brandywine River Museum of Art - A stunning art museum with works by N.C. Wyeth, Andrew Wyeth, and Jamie Wyeth.
  • Longwood Gardens - A horticultural haven with more than 1,000 acres of beautifully landscaped gardens.
  • The Kimmel Center for the Performing Arts - A huge performing arts venue located in the heart of downtown Philadelphia.
  • The Philadelphia Zoo - A world-renowned zoo home to more than 1,300 animals.

Food and Drink Scene of Havertown

Here’s what you need to know about the food and drink scene in Havertown:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Soft Pretzels - This iconic snack food is found throughout Philadelphia. Try one in Havertown at one of the many local bakeries.
  • Philly Cheesesteak - A classic foodie favorite, you simply can’t leave Havertown without trying one.
  • Hoagies - The sandwich isn’t just any sandwich, it’s a hoagie in Havertown.

Local Beverages

  • Yards Brewing Company - A local brewery that offers great beer and even better company.
  • La Colombe Coffee - Enjoy a smooth and delicious coffee at this beloved Philly chain.
  • Philly Lemonade - This refreshing drink can be found throughout the city and is a must-try while exploring Havertown.

Local Culture and Customs of Havertown

Havertown has a unique history and contemporary culture. Here are some interesting facts about the area’s customs and traditions:

  • Havertown is known for its love of sports, with many residents supporting the Philadelphia Eagles, Phillies, and 76ers.
  • In summer, many outdoor festivals, including the Havertown Irish Festival, the Haverford Music Festival, and the Havertown Harvest Festival, take place and locals welcome everyone to join in on the fun.
  • While the area has many options for dining out, it’s also common to see people having backyard barbecues and enjoying more casual Western-style food.

Best Time to Visit Havertown

The best time to visit Havertown is in spring or summer when the weather is mild and the flowers are in bloom. Fall is also a wonderful time to visit, as the foliage in the area is stunning and the weather is cooler. Weather-wise, it’s best to avoid the cold winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Havertown

Make the most of your visit to Havertown by being respectful of the area’s customs and traditions. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try local foods like soft pretzels and Philly cheesesteaks.
  • Do explore the outdoors on one of the many biking and hiking trails in the area.
  • Do attend local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave a trail of trash while hiking.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in residential areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip when dining at local restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Havertown

Overall, Havertown is a safe place to visit for both tourists and locals. However, it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Be mindful of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in less-traveled areas.

Health in Havertown

There are many hospitals and medical centers in the area, should you need medical attention. In case of an emergency, dial 911 or head to the closest hospital.

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Havertown:

Conclusion

With its unique blend of history, natural beauty, and cultural hotspots, Havertown is truly a gem in Pennsylvania. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan the perfect trip to Havertown. Remember to take your time and enjoy everything this amazing town has to offer!
