Introduction to Ardmore

Ardmore is a charming, diverse suburb located in Haverford Township, Delaware County, near Philadelphia. The town boasts a rich history, unique culture, and a plethora of outdoor adventures. From hiking and biking trails to historical sites and museums, Ardmore has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ardmore

If you’re planning a trip to Ardmore, don’t miss these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ardrossan Estate - Built in 1911, this magnificent mansion is famous for its rich history and stunning architecture.
  • Ardmore Train Station - A historical landmark and train station serving the community since 1950.
  • Lower Merion Historical Society - Features exhibits and memorabilia celebrating the township’s history and culture.
  • Philadelphia Museum of Art - A world-renowned art museum featuring more than 240,000 works of art from around the globe.

Natural Attractions of Ardmore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Haverford Reserve - A great place to walk or bike along the trails, picnic with family and friends, or hike in the woods.
  • Merion Golf Club - Founded in 1896, this private club is home to some of the best golf courses in the country.
  • Riverwalk Trail - A beautiful walking and hiking trail connecting Ardmore to nearby towns.
  • Wissahickon Valley Park - A 1,800-acre natural refuge that includes over 50 miles of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Ardmore

Ardmore is famous for its hiking and biking trails. If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, don’t miss these routes:

  • Haverford Reserve Trail - A 3.6-mile loop trail that features beautiful wildflowers and is good for all skill levels.
  • Cynwyd Heritage Trail - A 2.9-mile trail suitable for all age groups.
  • Pennsylvania Schuylkill River Trail - Passes through scenic, natural areas with access to picnic areas and other beautiful spots.

Best Things to Do in Ardmore: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something to do while in Ardmore, here are some top activities and tours:

  • Famous 4th Street Cookies - Your visit to Ardmore wouldn’t be complete without a visit to this famous cookie shop.
  • Iron Hill Brewery & Restaurant - A great place to enjoy fine beer and delicious food.
  • King of Prussia Mall - The largest shopping mall on the East Coast of the USA with over 400 stores and restaurants.
  • Philadelphia Zoo - The first zoo in America features more than 1,300 animals and a children’s zoo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ardmore

Day 1 - Explore the cultural attractions and museums in Ardmore, including the Lower Merion Historical Society and the Philadelphia Museum of Art.

Day 2 - Take a leisurely stroll along the Riverwalk Trail and enjoy the scenic views of the Schuylkill River.

Day 3 - Visit the famous Ardrossan Estate and learn about its rich history and stunning architecture.

Day 4 - Hike or bike along the Haverford Reserve Trail, followed by a picnic in the woods.

Day 5 - Take a trip to the King of Prussia Mall and go shopping for souvenirs and local products.

Day 6 - Enjoy a day of adventure at the Philadelphia Zoo and learn about different animal species.

Day 7 - Relax and unwind at the Iron Hill Brewery & Restaurant and enjoy fine beer and delicious food.

Food and Drink Scene of Ardmore

Ardmore offers a wide variety of food and drink options. If you’re planning to eat out while in Ardmore, make sure to try these must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Steak - A classic sandwich made with thinly sliced beef and melted cheese, served on a long hoagie roll.

  • Beef BBQ - Slow-cooked beef served with sweet or spicy barbecue sauce, coleslaw, and white bread.

  • Pretzel Bites - Small, soft pretzel bites served with various sauces, including mustard and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Yards Brewing Company - A Philadelphia-based craft brewery that specializes in handcrafted beer.

  • Chaddsford Winery - A winery located in Pennsylvania that offers a wide selection of wines, including the award-winning merlot.

Local Culture and Customs of Ardmore

Best Time to Visit Ardmore

The best time to visit Ardmore is during the summer months, from May to August, when the weather is pleasant and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Getting to Ardmore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ardmore is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • SEPTA Regional Rail - A train system that connects Ardmore to other towns in the region.

  • Bus Routes - There are several bus routes that run through Ardmore and surrounding towns.

How to Get Around in Ardmore

If you’re planning to get around Ardmore, consider these options:
