Media is a charming borough located in the Philadelphia region of Pennsylvania. Known for its historic downtown, Media offers tourists a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and local flavor. From hiking and biking trails to museums and breweries, there’s something for everyone in this small but vibrant town. Discover the top things to do, where to eat and stay, and how to make the most of your visit with our comprehensive travel guide to Media, Pennsylvania.

Introduction to Media

Media is a borough situated in the Delaware County of Pennsylvania. It has a population of just over 5,000 people. The town is known for its family-friendly vibe, walkable downtown area, and rich history. Media was initially named for its position between two significant creeks, which made it a prime ground for mills and manufacturing.

List of Places to Visit in Media

Media, Pennsylvania, boasts a wide range of attractions that cater to the interests of every traveler. Here are the top-rated places to visit in Media:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Media Theatre: An Art Deco-style movie theater that also showcases plays and musicals. It’s a local favorite and perfect for a night out.

  • Hedgerow Theatre: Founded in 1923, Hedgerow Theatre is the oldest repertory theatre in the United States. It features an intimate, 148-seat theater, and visitors can see classic plays, works by new playwrights, and children’s plays.

  • Pennsylvania Veterans Museum: The Pennsylvania Veterans Museum is dedicated to veterans of all eras. The museum features artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays that educate visitors on the history of those who served in the armed forces.

Natural Attractions of Media: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rose Tree Park: A 120-acre park lined with old trees, rose gardens, and a few walking trails. It’s perfect for a picnic with the family.

  • Tyler Arboretum: The largest public garden in Delaware County, Tyler Arboretum features a 17-acre arboretum filled with historic trees, a meadow maze, and a butterfly house.

  • Rocky Run Trail: An easy 2.3-mile hike that starts at Rose Tree Park and runs along Rocky Run Creek. The trail offers some lovely views of the area, wildlife spotting opportunities, and a chance to escape into nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Media

Media has a series of fantastic hiking and biking routes, perfect for spending a day outdoors. Some of the best options are:

  • Rocky Run Trail: A peaceful trail that starts at Rose Tree Park and offers scenic views of the creek. It’s best for a leisurely bike ride or walk.

  • The Chester Creek Trail: A 2.8-mile segment of the Circuit Trails, which ultimately plans to link more than 500 miles of trails throughout Greater Philadelphia. This trail is flat and wide, making it perfect for a family bike ride.

  • The Ridley Creek Trail: Part of Ridley Creek State Park, this 5.2-mile trailhead has some intermittent hills, adding some challenge to your bike ride or hike.

Best Things to Do in Media: Activities and Tours

Media has a lot of activities to offer visitors, from exploring historic sites to enjoying modern conveniences. Here are some things to add to your must-do list:

  • Tour Media Borough: The Media Historical Society offers an hour-long walking tour with knowledgeable guides that will take you down Broad Street to learn about the history of the area.

  • Microbrew Tour: Visit the town’s very own breweries and taste their unique craft beer.

  • Media Farmers Market: The market is open every Thursday from 3 pm to 7 pm, June through November. A perfect time to indulge in farm-fresh produce and locally made goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Media

If you’re planning to visit Media for a week or more, follow this itinerary for a fun-filled week:

Day 1-2: Exploring The Town

  • Spend the first two days exploring Media’s downtown, starting with a walking tour of Media Historic District. The tour will take you through Victorian architecture, unique shops, and vibrant restaurants.

  • Be sure to take a break at any of the outdoor cafes on State Street.

  • Walk through the gardens at Tyler Arboretum and stop for lunch at The Herb Garden Cafe.

Day 3-4: Enjoying The Parks and Trails

  • Spend day three at Ridley Creek State Park, a 2,600-acre park offering outdoor activities like, kayaking, hiking, mountain biking, and bird watching.

  • Day four can be spent on a leisurely bike ride around Media. Rent a bike and cycle around the Media and Chester Creek Trails.

Day 5: Museums Tour

  • Spend a day exploring local museums. Media has a variety of museums, each showcasing the diverse history of the area. Start with a visit to The Pennsylvania Veterans Museum, then move on to visit Hedgerow Theatre and the Media Theatre.

Day 6-7: Relaxation and Exploration

  • Spend your final two days exploring the town’s brewery scene and unique shops. Visit Sterling Pig Brewery and taste their excellent craft beer.

  • Relax in the early evening with a cocktail at Brick and Brew. It’s a perfect place to unwind and chat with the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Media

Media’s food and drink scene is growing in popularity, and for a good reason. The town has a wide range of eateries, from casual and take-out shops to Michelin-starred restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes while in town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteaks from Diego’s Cantina: The cheesesteaks from Diego’s Cantina are highly regarded by the local population and are an absolute must-try. The tortas and tacos are also a favorite.

  • Scrapple and eggs from Media Diner: The perfect start to your day.

  • Smokehouse Fries from Sterling Pig Brewery: Twice-fried fries seasoned with Sterling Pig’s homemade smoked salt and topped with pulled pork and cheese sauce.

Local Beverages

No trip to Media is complete without tasting some local brews. Some local favorite breweries include:

  • Sterling Pig Brewery: A local favorite that features a rotating selection of microbrews, menu items, and weekend live music events.

  • Iron Hill Brewery: Iron Hill serves its own beers brewed on-site, including a sampler pack that offers customers eight different beers served in their sample-friendly piaster glasses.

  • 320 Market Cafe: In addition to great food, 320 Market Cafe has a great beer selection backed by a passionate group to help you find the perfect beer.

Where to Stay in Media: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Media offers visitors a range of accommodation options, including modern hotels, bed and breakfasts, and charming guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Courtyard by Marriott Philadelphia Springfield: A modern hotel in nearby Springfield, only four miles from Media. The hotel comes with amenities like a fitness center, pool, and on-site restaurant.

  • The Media Inn & Suites: A charming, family-run hotel located right in the heart of Media. The hotel is within walking distance of the downtown area and features clean and comfortable rooms.

  • The Penn’s View Bed & Breakfast: Located in a 200-year-old brick building in Wallingford, only five minutes from Media. The Penn’s View Bed & Breakfast offers five fully renovated guest bedrooms decorated with period antiques and fine art.

What to Buy in Media: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Media has a variety of unique shops and boutiques that offer all kinds of local products and souvenirs to bring back home:

  • Seven Stones Cafe: Shop for unique coffee cups, as well as coffee beans ground on-site.

  • Pennsylvania Veterans Museum Gift Shop: Purchase a copy of the museum’s book, located in a beautiful historical house.

  • Media Farmers Market: Purchase locally grown produce, artisan cheeses, homemade baked goods, and a range of handmade products and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Media

Media is a small town that prides itself on its diversity and community involvement. The town’s culture and customs are unique because they revolve around the people. The community actively seeks out ways to make Media a better place, from tree planting and clean-ups to community-building events like Media Restaurant Weeks and Media Jazz Fest.

Best Time to Visit Media

The best time to visit Media, Pennsylvania, is between May and September when the weather is mild and suitable for outdoor activities. While July and August tend to be the busiest months, May and June are an excellent time to enjoy the town’s flowering parks and gardens.

Do’s and Don’ts of Media

To enjoy a smooth trip to Media, keep in mind these do’s and don’ts of Media:

Do:

  • Explore the walking trails in Ridley Creek State Park.

  • Visit the local breweries and enjoy the local craft beer.

  • Attend the Media Farmers Market and shop for locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Litter. Media is a clean and eco-friendly town.

  • Forget to tip. Tipping is customary in the United States.

  • Wear shoes inside the house. Take off your shoes before entering a residence.

Safety and Health

Safety in Media

Media is a safe town in general. However, it’s always good to be vigilant and keep your belongings safe. Keep an eye on your bags and wallets while in crowded areas.

Health in Media

Media is an eco-conscious town that places an emphasis on a healthy lifestyle. There are many walking and biking trails, a farmers’ market that sells organic food, and fitness classes available all around town.

Getting to Media: Transportation Options and Logistics

The following are different transportation options when traveling to Media:

Airports Near Media

Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) is the closest airport to Media and is only 13 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Media

Most visitors land at the Philadelphia Greyhound Station, which is the most frequented bus station. A taxi ride from Greyhound to Media is roughly a 20-minute drive.

Other Options

Rental cars are available at PHL and several other car rental locations around Philadelphia. A ride-share service might also be an option.

How to Get Around in Media

Walking around Media is the preferred method of transport in town since most key areas are within walking distance, while biking is another popular way to get around Media.

Where to Go Next from Media: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Many fabulous destinations are nearby Media, such as:

  • Philadelphia: Philadelphia is only a 25-minute drive from Media. A city rich in history and culture and some of the nation’s most significant historic landmarks, like The Liberty Bell, Independence Hall, and Fairmount Park.

  • Linvilla Orchards: Just outside of Media, Linvilla Orchards is a fun-filled destination for families and couples. It includes apple and pumpkin picking, farm animals, and a fishing lake.

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: A short drive from Media, the park features a visitor’s center and a seven-mile bike path, and many trails for hiking.

Conclusion

Media, Pennsylvania, is the perfect small town to experience the beauty of the Pennsylvania countryside while still enjoying the comforts of a vibrant and modern city. From its charming streets to a thriving restaurant scene, hiking trails to the cultural experiences, Media is a perfect destination for anyone looking for some relaxation and adventure. Come visit Media and immerse yourself in the local culture, history, and adventure!
