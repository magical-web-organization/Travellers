Introduction to Marple

Welcome to Marple, a beautiful township located in Delaware County, in Philadelphia Region, in Pennsylvania. Marple offers everything from natural attractions to historical places, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and families looking for a fun vacation. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit attractions, the best hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Marple

No tour of Marple would be complete without visiting these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marple Newtown Rail Road Museum: Discover the fascinating history of the Marple Newtown Railroad at this great museum.
  • Pennsylvania Railroad Caboose: Explore this restored caboose and learn about the important role of railroads in Marple’s history.
  • Historic Grange Estate: Soak in the beautiful architecture and history of the Grange Estate.
  • Newlin Grist Mill: Experience the 1704 English water-powered mill and explore 160-acres of wildlands and trails.

Natural Attractions of Marple: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Smedley Park: Enjoy the great outdoors at this expansive park, which offers hiking and biking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.
  • Club front Nature Preserve: Take a relaxing stroll in this natural preserve.
  • MapleZone Sports Institute: Enjoy your favorite sport with a wide range of activities like soccer, basketball and various other sports.
  • Springton Reservoir: Go fishing, boating or just bird-watching in this beautiful reservoir surrounded by nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marple

If you are an outdoor enthusiast, these hiking and biking routes are must-visit attractions:

  • Smedley Park Trail: This trail offers a beautiful hike through a scenic park with rolling hills.
  • Darby Creek Trail: Over six miles of hiking and biking trails along the Darby Creek.
  • Chester Creek Trail: This nine-mile paved trail goes through gorgeous forests, provides views of the river, and offers a great hiking or bike ride experience.

Best Things to Do in Marple: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to participate in Marple. Here are a few:

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take in the beautiful scenery of Marple from a hot air balloon.
  • Bike Rentals: Rent a bike and explore Marple’s scenic routes on your own.
  • Golf Courses: Enjoy golf across several entertaining and challenging courses throughout Marple such as Paxon Hollow Golf Club, Inniscrone Golf Club, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marple

If you plan to spend a week in Marple, check out this itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Marple

The food and drink scene of Marple is something not to be missed. There are plenty of great restaurants and local beverages to enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteaks: No trip to Philly is complete without trying one of their famous cheesesteaks.
  • Hoagies: A local sandwich that is packed full of meats, cheeses, and veggies.
  • Scrapple: A breakfast meat that is a quintessential Philadelphia favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Lager: This beer is the oldest brewery in America and a must-try for any beer lover.
  • Lancaster County Cider: A refreshing local cider made from a blend of ten different apple varieties.

Where to Stay in Marple: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marple offers a variety of options for accommodation:

  • The Inn at Swarthmore: A stunning inn located in a historic building with beautiful décor.
  • Philadelphia Marriott West: A luxurious hotel with great amenities, including an indoor pool.
  • Holiday Inn Express: A budget-friendly option that still offers great amenities and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Marple: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of great souvenirs and local products to bring home from Marple:

  • Philly sports team gear: Whether you’re a fan of the Philadelphia Eagles, 76ers, or Phillies, you can find gear to bring back home.
  • Amish-made products: The Amish are known for their high-quality, handcrafted products like quilts and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Marple

Marple has a rich local culture and customs that visitors should be aware of:

  • Sports Fanatics: Marple is full of passionate sports fans, especially for their hometown heroes like the Philadelphia Eagles and Phillies.
  • Local Cuisine: Marple has a unique local cuisine that visitors will enjoy experiencing.

Best Time to Visit Marple

The best time to visit Marple is in the spring or fall when temperatures are mild and the scenery is beautiful. However, visitors can enjoy Marple all year round thanks to its indoor as well as outdoor attractions.

  • Do try the local cuisine
  • Do immerse yourself in Marple’s local culture

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t be disrespectful towards the local culture

Safety and Health

Whether you’re staying in Marple for a few days or a week, it’s essential to prioritize safety and health during your visit.

Safety in Marple

Marple is a safe place to visit, but visitors should keep the following tips in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Avoid leaving valuables unattended in public places

Health in Marple

Marple is generally a healthy community with no major health risks, but visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Wear sunscreen and bring plenty of water during outdoor activities
  • Use insect repellent to prevent tick bites when hiking or biking

Getting to Marple: Transportation Options and Logistics

Located just 12 miles from downtown Philadelphia, Marple is easily accessible via several transportation options.

Airports Near Marple

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) - located just 12 miles from Marple

Bus Stations Near Marple

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Peter Pan Bus lines

Other Options

  • Amtrak train service from surrounding cities to Philadelphia.

How to Get Around in Marple

The easiest way to get around in Marple is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car or bike from a local rental company like Enterprise.

Where to Go Next from Marple: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of great destinations and excursions near Marple. Here are a few ideas:

  • Philadelphia: Just 12 miles from Marple, the City of Brotherly Love is full of great attractions like the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall.
  • Lancaster: A scenic two-hour drive from Marple, Lancaster is home to Amish communities, historic sites, and beautiful countryside.
  • Valley Forge: Located just 13 miles from Marple, Valley Forge National Historical Park offers visitors a look into early American history.

Conclusion

Marple is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking for a fun vacation. With plenty of must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, great food and drink scene, and nearby destinations, there is something for everyone. When visiting Marple, focus on safety and health, be respectful of local culture, and enjoy your time in this beautiful township.
