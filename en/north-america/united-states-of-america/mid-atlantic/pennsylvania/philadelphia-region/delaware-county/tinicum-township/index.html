Introduction to Tinicum Township

Nestled in Delaware County, in Philadelphia Region, in Pennsylvania, in Mid-Atlantic, in United States of America, in North America, Tinicum Township is a scenic destination that offers visitors an array of experiences to enjoy. From its rich cultural and historical landmarks to its breathtaking natural beauty, this charming township has something to offer everyone. We’ve put together a comprehensive tour guide to help you make the most of your visit to Tinicum Township.

List of Places to Visit in Tinicum Township

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tinicum Township has a rich cultural and historical heritage. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge at Tinicum: The refuge spans over 1,000 acres and is home to a diverse array of wildlife and habitats. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and nature trails.
  • Glenolden Park: A lush green space with plenty of picnic areas, hiking trails, a playground, and a creek, Glenolden Park is a great spot for families to spend a relaxing day.
  • Tinicum Rear Range Lighthouse: Built-in 1904, the Tinicum Rear Range Lighthouse is a stunning, historic landmark. Today, it offers visitors the opportunity to learn about its history and climb to the top for a magnificent view of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Tinicum Township: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts, Tinicum Township offers a wealth of natural attractions to be explored. Some of the top outdoor spots in the township are:

  • Darby Creek: Flowing through Tinicum Township, Darby Creek offers an ideal escape into nature. Visitors can explore its pristine waters by kayaking, canoeing, or fishing.
  • Governor Printz Park: Perfect for picnicking, jogging, or biking, Governor Printz Park is a charming spot that offers beautiful river views and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • Tinicum Marsh: A wetland area that spans over 250 acres, Tinicum Marsh is one of the largest freshwater tidal marshes in Pennsylvania. A haven for bird watchers, the marsh is home to over 300 species of birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tinicum Township

For outdoor enthusiasts, Tinicum Township has a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views and a chance to immerse themselves in nature. Some of the top hiking and biking routes are:

  • Tinicum Trail: The Tinicum Trail is a 1.3-mile loop that offers scenic river views and an opportunity to explore the wildlife refuge.
  • Darby Creek Trail: The Darby Creek Trail runs for six miles and offers spectacular views of the creek’s natural beauty.
  • Heinz Refuge Trail: The Heinz Refuge Trail is a two-mile loop that takes you through wetlands and wooded areas, offering magnificent views of nature along the way.

Best Things to Do in Tinicum Township: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the cultural and natural landmarks of Tinicum Township, there are tons of other activities and tours that visitors can indulge in, making their stay even more enjoyable. Some of the best things to do in Tinicum Township are:

  • Riverboat Tour: Take a riverboat tour on the Delaware River and experience the magnificent views of Philadelphia’s skyline.
  • Tinicum Rear Range Lighthouse Tour: Learn about the history of the Tinicum Rear Range Lighthouse and enjoy a climb to the top to experience spectacular views of the surrounding area.
  • Birdwatching Tour: Join a guided birdwatching tour and explore the John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge, home to over 300 species of birds.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tinicum Township

To help visitors experience the best that Tinicum Township has to offer, here is an outline of a week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge and be enchanted by the marsh and its wildlife.
  • Day 2: Take a riverboat tour of the Delaware River and enjoy its scenic views of Philadelphia’s skyline.
  • Day 3: Explore Tinicum Rear Range Lighthouse and get a bird’s eye view of the surrounding area from the lighthouse’s top.
  • Day 4: Discover Governor Printz Park and its serene creek views and hiking trails.
  • Day 5: Go on a hiking or biking adventure on the Darby Creek Trail, one of Pennsylvania’s most scenic trails.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a birdwatching tour of the John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge and witness some of the region’s rarest bird species.
  • Day 7: Explore Tinicum Marsh, the largest freshwater tidal marsh in Pennsylvania, and interact with nature’s beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Tinicum Township

Tinicum Township’s food scene is a blend of traditional Pennsylvania cuisine and taste-bud tantalizing dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scrapple: A common breakfast dish in Pennsylvania, scrapple is a meatloaf made of pork scraps and cornmeal.
  • Philadelphia Cheesesteak: A mouth-watering sandwich made of thinly sliced beefsteak, cheese, and onions, served on a hoagie roll.
  • Shoo-fly Pie: A traditional Pennsylvania Dutch treat made of molasses and spices, baked in a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Yards Brewing Company: This Philadelphia-based traditional brewing company is known for its craft beer.
  • Reading Draft: Located in Reading, Pennsylvania, this handcrafted line of soda pop is made with natural ingredients and pure cane sugar.

Local Culture and Customs of Tinicum Township

Pennsylvania Dutch and Philadelphia cultures influence Tinicum Township, from its food, architecture, and history. Visitors are expected to be respectful and embrace the locals’ culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Tinicum Township

The best time to visit Tinicum Township is from March to June and September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, offering ideal conditions for outdoor activities and the region’s long list of festivals and events.
