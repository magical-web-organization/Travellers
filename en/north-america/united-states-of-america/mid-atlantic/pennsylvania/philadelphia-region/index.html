Introduction to Philadelphia Region

Welcome to the Philadelphia Region, a diverse and bustling area full of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Situated in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, the Philadelphia Region is a popular destination for tourists from around the world.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the iconic landmarks of Philadelphia, hiking through scenic trails, or indulging in the local food and drink scene, this guide will help you plan your itinerary. Read on for the best places to visit, things to do, and insider tips for your trip to the Philadelphia Region.

List of Places to Visit in Philadelphia Region

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Philadelphia is known for its rich cultural heritage and role as the birthplace of American democracy. Here are some must-visit museums and historical places in the region:

  • Independence National Historical Park: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this park contains the Liberty Bell, Independence Hall and other iconic landmarks significant to early American history.

  • The Museum of the American Revolution: A state-of-the-art museum located in the heart of Philadelphia, dedicated to telling the story of America’s Revolutionary War.

  • The National Constitution Center: A museum dedicated to exploring the history and relevance of the U.S. Constitution and its impact on American government and society.

  • Philadelphia Museum of Art: This world-renowned museum boasts an impressive collection of artwork ranging from ancient to contemporary pieces.

  • The Barnes Foundation: An art museum with an extensive collection of impressionist, post-impressionist, and early modernist paintings.

  • The Franklin Institute: A science museum with interactive exhibits and hands-on activities for visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Philadelphia Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Philadelphia Region is home to a variety of natural attractions, ranging from scenic parks to hiking trails. Here are some of the best outdoor adventures to add to your itinerary:

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: A historical park that features a 3,500-acre park with a rich history.

  • Wissahickon Valley Park: A park with more than 50 miles of hiking trails, creeks, and waterfalls.

  • Schuylkill River Trail: A scenic trail that runs along the Schuylkill River and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge: A wildlife refuge located right in the heart of the city, featuring a variety of native plants and animals.

  • Longwood Gardens: One of the world’s premier horticultural display gardens, featuring more than 11,000 different types of plants and flowers on display.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Philadelphia Region

If you’re looking for a way to explore the natural beauty of the Philadelphia Region, hiking and biking routes are the way to go. Some of the best routes include:

  • Forbidden Drive: A 7-mile trail located in Wissahickon Valley Park, with scenic views of the Wissahickon Creek.

  • Perkiomen Trail: A 19-mile asphalt trail that follows the Perkiomen Creek and offers a scenic view of the surrounding area.

  • Schuylkill River Trail: A 26.5-mile trail that stretches from Philadelphia to Valley Forge National Historical Park.

Best Things to Do in Philadelphia Region: Activities and Tours

From historic landmarks to food and drink tours, there is always something to do in the Philadelphia Region. Here are some top activities and tours to check out:

  • Segway Tour of Philadelphia: tour the city and learn about the city’s history on a Segway.

  • Philadelphia Mural Arts Tour: Philadelphia is Famous for its street art, take a walking tour and discover the city’s murals.

  • Reading Terminal Market Food Tour: a foodie’s heaven with over 80 food vendors serving local and exotic foods.

  • Ghost Tour of Philadelphia: Discover Philadelphia’s spooky past with a ghost tour of some of the city’s most haunted sites.

  • Visit the Philadelphia Zoo: the Philadelphia zoo has over 1,300 animals in over 40 exhibits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Philadelphia Region

If you have a week to spend in the Philadelphia Region, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic landmarks of Independence National Historical Park.
  • Day 2: Take a Segway Tour of Philadelphia in the morning, and visit the Philadelphia Museum of Art in the afternoon.
  • Day 3: Hike through Wissahickon Valley Park and Valley Forge National Historical Park.
  • Day 4: Take a food tour through Reading Terminal Market and sample the local delicacies.
  • Day 5: Visit the Franklin Institute and explore the museum’s interactive exhibits.
  • Day 6: Take a tour of the Philadelphia Zoo and see the exotic animals on display.
  • Day 7: Relax in Longwood Gardens, one of the world’s premier horticultural display gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Philadelphia Region

Philadelphia is famous for its food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteak: a classic Philly sandwich made with thinly sliced steak and melted cheese on a long roll.

  • Roast Pork Sandwich: a popular sandwich with slow-cooked roast pork, broccoli rabe, and provolone cheese.

  • Scrapple: a pork dish made with meat scraps and cornmeal.

  • Soft Pretzels: A salty snack that has been a staple of Philly’s food scene for over two centuries.

  • Hot Buttered and Fluffy Kakes: traditional Pennsylvania Dutch breakfast foods that are similar to doughnuts.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: the oldest operating brewery in the United States.

  • Wine from the Brandywine Valley: this region boasts wineries that produce world-class wines.

  • Soda from Reading: Soda from the Reading Draft Company is nationally known, and the brand is sold in some of the best restaurants in the region.

Where to Stay in Philadelphia Region: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Philadelphia region has a wide variety of accommodations, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some options to explore:

  • The Rittenhouse: A luxury hotel in the heart of Philadelphia.

  • The Warwick Hotel Rittenhouse Square: A historic hotel located in Center City.

  • The Alexander Inn: A boutique hotel in the heart of downtown Philadelphia.

  • The Philadelphia House: A budget hostel popular with young travelers.

What to Buy in Philadelphia Region: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take home, Philadelphia Region offers a variety of local products to choose from. Here are some ideas:

  • Philly Cheesesteak Seasoning: Bring a taste of Philadelphia home with this popular seasoning blend.

  • Amish Quilts: The Amish community in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, produces beautiful handmade quilts.

  • Reading Terminal Market mug: Pick up a souvenir mug from the famous food market in downtown Philadelphia.

Local Culture and Customs of Philadelphia Region

Philadelphia is known for its diverse culture and friendly vibes. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in restaurants and bars in the Philadelphia Region, with a standard tip of 15-20% expected.

  • Philly Pride: The people of Philadelphia take great pride in their city’s sports teams, particularly the Philadelphia Eagles football team.

  • Wawa: Wawa is a chain of convenience stores with a cult following among locals. Don’t leave the area without trying their famous hoagies.

Best Time to Visit Philadelphia Region

The best time to visit the Philadelphia Region is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summer can be hot and humid, but it’s also an excellent time for outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Philadelphia Region

Do:

  • Do Try a Cheesesteak: It’s one of the most famous dishes in Philly, and there’s a reason why.

  • Do Visit the Historic Landmarks: Learn about the birthplace of American democracy and immerse yourself in the rich history of the city.
  • Do Take a Stroll in Reading Terminal Market: This beloved food market is a must-visit for foodies and history buffs alike.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Run Up the Rocky Steps: You’ll see many tourists running up the stairs outside the Philadelphia Museum of Art. It’s probably best not to join in.

  • Don’t Park in Center City: Parking can be difficult in Philadelphia’s bustling downtown area, so it’s best to use public transportation or ride-hailing services instead.

  • Don’t Forget to Tip: Tipping is customary in the Philadelphia region, so be sure to leave a gratuity for good service.

Safety and Health

The Philadelphia Region is a generally safe area, but it’s best to take standard precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy:

Safety in Philadelphia region

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night: If you’re out after dark, stick to well-lit areas and avoid walking alone.

  • Keep your valuables secure: Keep your belongings close to you, especially in crowded areas.

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Stay alert and aware of your surroundings, particularly in tourist areas where pickpocketing can occur.

Health in Philadelphia region

  • Stay hydrated: The Philadelphia Region can get hot and humid in the summer months, so be sure to drink plenty of water.

  • Watch out for ticks: Lymes disease has been reported to be a problem in some areas; be sure to wear long pants and shirts when hiking and use insect repellent.

Getting to Philadelphia Region: Transportation Options and Logistics

The Philadelphia Region is easily accessible by air, train, or car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Philadelphia International Airport: The primary airport serving the Philadelphia Region, it has many flights to destinations all over the country and world.

  • 30th Street Station: Amtrak station with trains that travel to and from cities along the East Coast, including New York, Washington DC, Boston, and more.

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The Philadelphia region is also accessible via low-cost bus services from different bus stations in the city centre.

How to Get Around in Philadelphia Region

Philadelphia city is pedestrian-friendly and visitors can comfortably walk around the city. Alternatively, there are local buses, taxis, and the subway to get around. Visitors can also easily use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Lyft to get around the region.

Where to Go Next from Philadelphia Region: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Mid-Atlantic region, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Washington DC: The United States capital is a 2-3 hour drive from Philadelphia.

  • Baltimore: This historic city is just 90 minutes from Philadelphia by car.

  • Atlantic City: A popular seaside town with casinos and entertainment options.

Conclusion

The Philadelphia Region is a diverse and bustling area with plenty to see and do, from historic landmarks to scenic outdoor adventures and a world-class food and drink scene. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city or hiking through the countryside, this comprehensive guide has everything you need to plan your trip. Come experience the sights, sounds, and flavors of the Philadelphia Region for yourself!
