Introduction to Washington Crossing Historical Park

Located in Bucks County in the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania, Washington Crossing Historical Park is a popular destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and outdoor enthusiasts. The site commemorates the 1776 crossing of the Delaware River by General George Washington and his troops, a pivotal moment in the American Revolution. Today, the park offers a variety of attractions and activities, including museums, hiking trails, scenic vistas, and cultural events. Whether you’re interested in learning about the park’s rich history or exploring its natural beauty, there’s something for everyone at Washington Crossing Historical Park.

List of Places to Visit in Washington Crossing Historical Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Visitor Center: This is where your visit will begin. Get a brief introduction to the park, watch a short film, and see exhibits about the crossing.

  • Washington Crossing Historic Park Museum: Explore the park’s history through a variety of artifacts, including weapons, uniforms, and documents related to the crossing.

  • Johnson Ferry House: This 18th-century farmhouse once served as a ferry crossing over the Delaware River. Today, it houses exhibits on life in the colonial era.

Natural Attractions of Washington Crossing Historical Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bowman’s Hill Tower: Climb 124 feet to the top of the tower for a breathtaking view of the surrounding countryside.

  • Delaware Canal State Park: This park runs 60 miles between Bristol and Easton and offers scenic walking and biking trails, fishing, and boating.

  • Washington Crossing Historic Park and Surrounding Area: The park boasts over 500 acres of scenic trails and open space, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Washington Crossing Historical Park

  • Bowman’s Hill Wildflower Preserve Loop: A 2.7-mile loop trail that winds through a beautiful wildflower preserve.

  • Washington Crossing to Bulls Island Loop: A 32-mile loop that starts and ends at the park. Follow the Delaware Canal towpath and eventually cross the river into New Jersey, where you can explore Bulls Island State Park.

Best Things to Do in Washington Crossing Historical Park: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Tours: Take a guided tour of the park to learn about the crossing and other important events that took place here during the Revolutionary War.

  • Revolutionary War Reenactments: Watch as actors dressed in period attire recreate key moments from the Revolutionary War, including the crossing itself.

  • Special Events: The park hosts a variety of special events throughout the year, including concerts, lectures, and historical demonstrations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Washington Crossing Historical Park

Here’s a sample one-week itinerary for exploring Washington Crossing Historical Park and the surrounding area:

  • Day 1: Arrive and check into your hotel. Visit the park’s Visitor Center and take a guided tour.

  • Day 2: Explore the park’s museums and historical sites, including the Washington Crossing Historic Park Museum and the Johnson Ferry House.

  • Day 3: Enjoy the great outdoors with a hike or bike ride along one of the park’s many scenic trails.

  • Day 4: Take a trip to nearby New Hope, known for its charming downtown area and art galleries.

  • Day 5: Spend the day exploring Philadelphia, just a short drive from the park.

  • Day 6: Take a canal boat ride at Delaware Canal State Park or go kayaking on the Delaware River.

  • Day 7: Depart for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Washington Crossing Historical Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shoofly Pie: A Pennsylvania Dutch specialty, shoofly pie is made with molasses and a crumbly topping.

  • Soft Pretzels: A Philadelphia staple, you can find soft pretzels throughout the region.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: America’s oldest brewery, Yuengling is based in Pennsylvania and produces a variety of beers.

  • Philadelphia-style Lemonade: This thirst-quenching drink is both sweet and tart and can be found in many Philadelphia-area restaurants and cafes.

Where to Stay in Washington Crossing Historical Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Washington Crossing Inn: This historic inn dates back to 1817 and offers luxurious rooms and fine dining.

  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Newtown: This modern hotel offers spacious suites and amenities including a fitness center and outdoor pool.

  • The Ghost Light Inn: This boutique hotel is a cozy and stylish hideaway in nearby New Hope.

What to Buy in Washington Crossing Historical Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: The park’s gift shop offers a variety of locally made pottery, including mugs and plates with colonial-inspired designs.

  • Local Honey: Pennsylvania is known for its honey, and you can find jars of it at many gift shops in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Washington Crossing Historical Park

Pennsylvania, and particularly the Philadelphia region, has a rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors to the area should be respectful of this heritage and try to learn more about the customs and traditions of the local people. Some key cultural practices include:

  • Saying “please” and “thank you”: Pennsylvania is known for its friendly people, and good manners go a long way.

  • Observing historical sites: Visitors to the park should take care not to damage any historical buildings, artifacts, or trails. This includes not littering or leaving anything behind, and not climbing on any historical structures.

Best Time to Visit Washington Crossing Historical Park

The park is open year-round, but visitors should consider the weather and crowds when planning their visit. The best time to visit is between April and October, when the weather is mild and the park is most active. Fall is a particularly beautiful time to visit, as the leaves change color and the weather is crisp and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Washington Crossing Historical Park

Do:

  • Take a guided tour to learn about the park’s rich history.

  • Respect the park’s natural areas by staying on marked trails and not disturbing wildlife.

  • Visit nearby towns and attractions to get a taste of the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave any trash behind in the park.

  • Touch or climb on any historical artifacts or buildings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Washington Crossing Historical Park

Visitors to the park should take common sense precautions to ensure their safety. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Staying on marked trails and paths.

  • Carrying plenty of water and snacks, especially when hiking.

  • Watching out for poison ivy, ticks, and other hazards.

Health in Washington Crossing Historical Park

Visitors should also take care of their health and wellness while visiting the park. Some tips for staying healthy include:

  • Staying hydrated in hot weather.

  • Wearing sunscreen and appropriate clothing to protect against sunburn and insect bites.

  • Bringing any necessary medications or first aid supplies in case of injury or illness.

Getting to Washington Crossing Historical Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Washington Crossing Historical Park

  • Philadelphia International Airport: The closest major airport, about 45 minutes by car.

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: About 1 hour and 15 minutes by car.

  • Trenton-Mercer Airport: A smaller regional airport located just 15 minutes from the park.

Bus Stations Near Washington Crossing Historical Park

  • Philadelphia Greyhound Terminal: About 40 minutes by car.

  • Trenton Transit Center: About 20 minutes by car.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The nearest station is Trenton, NJ, which is accessible from major cities including New York and Philadelphia.

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at any of the nearby airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Washington Crossing Historical Park

Visitors can explore the park on foot, by bike, or by car. The park has plenty of parking available, and bike rentals are available at nearby outfitters. Visitors can also take guided tours or canal boat rides to see more of the park.

Where to Go Next from Washington Crossing Historical Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Hope: A charming town with plenty of restaurants, shops, and art galleries.

  • Philadelphia: One of America’s most historic cities, with world-class museums, dining, and cultural attractions.

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: This park is just a short drive from Washington Crossing and is where the Continental Army made camp during the Revolutionary War.

Conclusion

Washington Crossing Historical Park is a must-see destination for anyone interested in Amerian history, natural beauty, and outdoor recreation. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning vistas, and variety of activities, the park offers something for everyone. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a week-long adventure, be sure to explore all that this park and the surrounding area has to offer.
