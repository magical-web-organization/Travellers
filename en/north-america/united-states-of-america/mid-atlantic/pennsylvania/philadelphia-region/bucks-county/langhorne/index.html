Introduction to Langhorne

Langhorne is a charming borough located in Bucks County, in the Philadelphia region of Pennsylvania, in Mid-Atlantic of the United States. With its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions, Langhorne has a lot to offer to its visitors. The borough is most famous for being home to the popular amusement park, Sesame Place, but there’s much more to explore in and around Langhorne.

In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive tour of Langhorne, featuring the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, where to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Langhorne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Langhorne has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places that you should visit to learn more about the borough’s past. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Langhorne Heritage Farm: This historic farm dates back to the 18th century and is a great spot for families to learn about local farming history.
  • Cairn University Biblical Learning Center: A popular attraction for those interested in religious history.
  • Langhorne Borough Hall: This historic building is home to the Langhorne Borough Council and the mayor’s office.

Natural Attractions of Langhorne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures and scenic views, Langhorne won’t disappoint you. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Core Creek Park: This 1,200-acre park has something for everyone, from playgrounds to hiking trails to fishing spots.
  • Tyler State Park: This stunning park boasts 1,711 acres of rolling hills, woodlands, and scenic vistas, perfect for hiking, biking, and picnicking.
  • Delaware Canal State Park: This state park is located on the banks of the Delaware River and has over 60 miles of trails for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Langhorne

If you’re into hiking or biking, then there are a variety of routes to choose from in and around Langhorne. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Delaware Canal Towpath: This 60-mile-long trail follows the canal from Easton to Bristol, offering stunning views of the Delaware River.
  • Heritage Trail: This 20-mile trail follows the route of the old Reading Railroad, giving visitors the chance to explore the history of the region while enjoying some exercise and fresh air.
  • Neshaminy Creek Greenway Trail: This 22-mile-long trail is perfect for hiking, biking, or even horseback riding and provides stunning views of the Neshaminy Creek and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Langhorne: Activities and Tours

There’s never a dull moment in Langhorne, as there are a variety of activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Sesame Place: This popular amusement park is perfect for families with young children, as it features rides and attractions based on the beloved Sesame Street characters.
  • Escape Room Langhorne: If you’re looking for a unique and challenging activity, then this escape room is the perfect option.
  • Bucks County Antiques Dealers Association: If you love antiques, then you won’t want to miss this tour of the best antique dealers in Bucks County.

Week-Long Itinerary for Langhorne

If you’re planning to spend a week in Langhorne, then here is an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrival in Langhorne, check-in at hotel, and visit Langhorne Heritage Farm Day 2: Visit Sesame Place and have dinner at one of Langhorne’s top-rated restaurants, Mamma Mia Trattoria Day 3: Hike the Delaware Canal Towpath and have lunch at The Picnic Basket Day 4: Visit Tyler State Park and have dinner at Dog and Bull Brew and Music House Day 5: Take the Heritage Trail tour and have lunch at Boccadito deli and Market Day 6: Visit Cairn University Biblical Learning Center and have dinner at La Stalla Day 7: Visit Delaware Canal State Park and depart Langhorne

Food and Drink Scene of Langhorne

Langhorne has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options to suit any taste. Here are some recommendations for must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteak: This iconic Philadelphia dish is a must-try for visitors to the region. Try it at Campbells’ Irish Pub or Carlucci’s Grill.
  • Soft Pretzel: Another Philadelphia classic, you can find these soft and chewy pretzels at Just A Dream.
  • Tomato Pie: This savory treat is a popular local specialty, and you can find it at Tony’s Place or Brother’s Pizza.

Local Beverages

  • Victory Brewing Company: This popular brewery is located just a few miles south of Langhorne and offers tours and tastings.
  • Naked Brewing Company: Another popular brewery located in the area, Naked Brewing offers a variety of unique and delicious beers.
  • Crossing Vineyards and Winery: If you prefer wine, then check out this local winery, which offers tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Langhorne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Langhorne has plenty of options for accommodations, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly guesthouse. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Sheraton Bucks County Hotel: This hotel offers a variety of rooms and suites, as well as a fitness center and indoor pool.
  • Red Roof Inn: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and convenient amenities like free Wi-Fi and free parking.
  • Historic Summerseat Bed and Breakfast: For a unique and cozy experience, stay at this historic bed and breakfast, which dates back to the Revolutionary War.

What to Buy in Langhorne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home from Langhorne. Here are some ideas:

  • Langhorne Coffee House Blend: Pick up a bag of coffee beans from this local coffee shop, which is known for its fresh and delicious blends.

Local Culture and Customs of Langhorne

Langhorne is a friendly and welcoming borough, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs. Here are some tips to help you fit in:

  • Say “hello”: Langhorne residents are generally friendly and welcoming, so don’t hesitate to say hello to strangers you encounter.
  • Respect the environment: Langhorne is known for its natural beauty, so be sure to pick up after yourself and leave no trace when enjoying the outdoors.
  • Embrace the history: Langhorne has a rich history, so take some time to learn about the borough’s past and appreciate the local landmarks and museums.

Best Time to Visit Langhorne

The best time to visit Langhorne is in the fall or spring, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy, so plan your trip accordingly.

Getting to Langhorne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Langhorne

If you’re flying to Langhorne, there are a few airports within driving distance:

  • Philadelphia International Airport: This large airport is located about 33 miles south of Langhorne.
  • Trenton-Mercer Airport: This airport is located about 9 miles northeast of Langhorne and offers regional flights.

Bus Stations Near Langhorne

If you’re traveling by bus, there are a few options in the area:

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The closest Greyhound station is located in Philadelphia, about 30 miles south of Langhorne.
  • Megabus Station: The closest Megabus station is located in New Brunswick, New Jersey, about 44 miles northeast of Langhorne.

Other Options

If you’re traveling by car, Langhorne is conveniently located near several major highways, including I-95, I-276, and US-1. There are also several rental car companies located in the area if you need to rent a car.

How to Get Around in Langhorne

The best way to get around Langhorne is by car, as the borough is somewhat spread out and there isn’t a public transportation system. There are several rental car companies located in the area, and most hotels offer free on-site parking.

Where to Go Next from Langhorne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore beyond Langhorne, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Philadelphia: This vibrant city is located about 30 minutes south of Langhorne and offers a variety of attractions, including Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell.
  • Doylestown: This charming town is located about 20 minutes southwest of Langhorne and is home to several museums and shops.
  • New Hope: This quaint town is located about 20 minutes northeast of Langhorne and is known for its art galleries and scenic river views.

Conclusion

Langhorne is a hidden gem in the Philadelphia region, offering a variety of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re visiting Sesame Place with your family or exploring the region’s history, Langhorne is sure to have something that will make your trip unforgettable.
