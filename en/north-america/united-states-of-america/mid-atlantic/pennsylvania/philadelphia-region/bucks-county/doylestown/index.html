Introduction to Doylestown

Located in Bucks County, in the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania, Doylestown is a picturesque town steeped in history and brimming with cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, art lover, nature enthusiast, or simply looking to relax and unwind, Doylestown has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Doylestown

Here are some of the must-visit attractions and places in and around Doylestown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fonthill Castle: Built by renowned tile-maker Henry Chapman Mercer, Fonthill Castle is a stunning example of medieval-inspired architecture and houses a collection of decorative tiles, prints, and furnishings.
  • Mercer Museum: The Mercer Museum is dedicated to showcasing the tools and crafts of early America, with over 50,000 objects on display.
  • James A. Michener Art Museum: Featuring a diverse range of American art from the 19th century to the present, the James A. Michener Art Museum is a must-visit for art lovers.
  • Moravian Pottery and Tile Works: Founded in 1898, the Moravian Pottery and Tile Works is a living history museum where visitors can watch artisans demonstrate the process of making tile.

Natural Attractions of Doylestown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peace Valley Park: Spread over 1,500 acres, Peace Valley Park offers boating, fishing, hiking, and biking opportunities, as well as scenic views of Lake Galena.
  • Ringing Rocks Park: Known for its unique geological phenomenon, Ringing Rocks Park allows visitors to hear strange ringing sounds by striking the boulder fields with hammers.
  • Nockamixon State Park: Located just a short drive from Doylestown, Nockamixon State Park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including swimming, fishing, hiking, and picnicking.
  • Bowman’s Hill Wildflower Preserve: Home to over 700 native plant species, Bowman’s Hill Wildflower Preserve is a beautiful destination for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Doylestown

Doylestown and its surrounding areas boast plenty of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Some popular trails include the Doylestown Bike and Hike Trails, the Perkiomen Trail, and the Delaware Canal Towpath.

Best Things to Do in Doylestown: Activities and Tours

  • Peddler’s Village: A unique shopping and dining destination, Peddler’s Village features over 70 specialty shops and restaurants in a charming 18th-century village setting.
  • Doylestown Farmers Market: Held every Saturday between April and November, the Doylestown Farmers Market showcases the best local produce, meats, baked goods, flowers, and more.
  • The County Theater: Built in 1938, the County Theater is a classic movie theater that shows a mix of new releases, classic films, and indie flicks.
  • Grapevine Tours: Explore the local wine scene with Grapevine Tours, which offers guided tours of the area’s best wineries and tasting rooms.

Week-Long Itinerary for Doylestown

Day 1: Explore the cultural attractions of Doylestown, including Fonthill Castle, the Mercer Museum, and the James A. Michener Art Museum.

Day 2: Enjoy the great outdoors at Peace Valley Park, Ringing Rocks Park, or Bowman’s Hill Wildflower Preserve.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive to nearby Lambertville, New Jersey, a charming river town known for its antiques shops, art galleries, and restaurants.

Day 4: Go on a wine tour with Grapevine Tours and sample the region’s best vintages.

Day 5: Visit the Moravian Pottery and Tile Works and witness the ancient art of tile-making.

Day 6: Have a picnic at Nockamixon State Park and take a swim in the lake.

Day 7: Shop, dine, and relax at Peddler’s Village.

Food and Drink Scene of Doylestown

Doylestown’s food and drink scene is a delightful mix of traditional American fare and international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Philly Cheesesteak: A classic sandwich made with thinly sliced steak, melted cheese, and onions on a hoagie roll.
  • Shoo-Fly Pie: A Pennsylvania Dutch dessert made of molasses, sugar, and flour.
  • Scrapple: A breakfast meat made with pork scraps and cornmeal.
  • Soft Pretzels: A salty, chewy snack often topped with mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Lager: America’s oldest brewery, Yuengling is a must-try for beer fans.
  • Pennsylvania Dutch Birch Beer: A sweet, non-alcoholic soda with a distinct, herbal flavor.
  • Apple Cider: Made from fresh, local apples, apple cider is a refreshing autumn drink.

Where to Stay in Doylestown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Doylestown offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxury hotels.

Some popular options include:

  • The Doylestown Inn: A historic boutique hotel located in the heart of town.
  • The Hargrave House Bed and Breakfast: A charming 19th-century Victorian mansion with elegant rooms and beautiful gardens.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Doylestown: A modern hotel offering spacious suites with kitchens and free breakfast.
  • The Golden Pheasant Inn: A romantic bed and breakfast set in a historic stone manor house surrounded by beautiful gardens.

What to Buy in Doylestown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Moravian Pottery and Tile Works products: Take home a piece of the museum’s history with a decorative tile or print.
  • Shoo-fly pie: Bring home a taste of Pennsylvania Dutch country with a freshly baked shoo-fly pie.
  • Local honey and maple syrup: A sweet and natural souvenir from the area’s farms and orchards.
  • Antiques and collectibles: Doylestown’s antique shops offer a treasure trove of unique finds, from vintage furniture to rare books.

Local Culture and Customs of Doylestown

Doylestown is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and friendly locals. Visitors should be sure to respect local customs and traditions, such as saying “hello” or “good morning” to strangers on the street.

Best Time to Visit Doylestown

The best time to visit Doylestown is in the fall, when the leaves change color and the weather is cool and crisp. Spring is also a lovely time to visit, with blooming flowers and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Doylestown

Do:

  • Visit the cultural attractions and historical sites in and around Doylestown.
  • Go on a winery tour and sample the local vintages.
  • Take a scenic drive to nearby Lambertville, New Jersey.
  • Shop and dine at Peddler’s Village.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in public places.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Engage in disrespectful or disruptive behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Doylestown

Doylestown is a relatively safe town, but like any destination, visitors should take reasonable precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Be sure to lock your car and hotel room doors, and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Doylestown

Doylestown has plenty of healthcare facilities and pharmacies in case of medical emergencies. Visitors should also be prepared for outdoor activities and dress appropriately for the weather and terrain.

Getting to Doylestown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Doylestown

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): Located about an hour’s drive from Doylestown, PHL is the largest airport in the region and serves many domestic and international destinations.
  • Trenton-Mercer Airport (TTN): Located about 30 minutes from Doylestown, TTN offers direct flights to several domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Doylestown

  • Doylestown Bus Station: Located in town, the Doylestown Bus Station serves several regional and local routes.
  • Philadelphia Greyhound Station: Located about an hour’s drive from Doylestown, the Philadelphia Greyhound Station serves many national and regional destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Doylestown has a train station serviced by SEPTA, which offers regional rail services to Philadelphia and other nearby destinations.
  • Car: Doylestown is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running nearby.

How to Get Around in Doylestown

Doylestown is a walkable town, with many attractions and amenities located within a short distance of each other. For longer distances, visitors can use public transportation (including buses and trains), or rent a car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Doylestown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lambertville, New Jersey: A charming river town with antique shops, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • New Hope, Pennsylvania: Another scenic river town famous for its arts scene and natural beauty.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: A nearby national park that played a pivotal role in the American Revolution.

Conclusion

With its rich history, cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures, Doylestown is a must-visit destination in Pennsylvania. Whether you’re interested in art, history, or just want to relax and unwind, this charming town offers something for everyone.
