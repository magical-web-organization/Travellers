Introduction to Feasterville

Nestled in Bucks County, in the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania, Feasterville is a small but culturally rich town that offers something for everyone. From its quaint cultural attractions and natural wonders to its adventurous hiking trails and impressive food and drink scene, this town has a lot to offer. In this guide, we will take a closer look at all that Feasterville has in store and showcase the must-see destinations, local customs, best places to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Feasterville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Feasterville has a rich history that is reflected in its cultural attractions. These are the must-visit cultural and historical places in Feasterville:

  • The Historical Society of Feasterville: Get a closer look at the town’s rich history and cultural heritage with a visit to the Feasterville Historical Society. It is dedicated to preserving and sharing the town’s unique history with visitors.
  • Neshaminy State Park: Located on the banks of the Neshaminy Creek, Neshaminy State Park is a great place to explore the natural beauty of the area while also enjoying some history. Along the path, there are several sites to see, including ruins of the Davisville Mill, One-Room School House, and the 19th century Stover-Myers Mill.
  • Thunderbird II Alley: This street art alleyway is home to several colorful murals painted by local artists.

Natural Attractions of Feasterville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Feasterville is renowned for its scenic beauty, and several outdoor and natural attractions offer an abundance of opportunities for adventure. Don’t miss out on these top natural attractions in Feasterville:

  • Playwicki Farm Trail: This stunning 2.1-mile trail offers a combination of natural beauty, wildlife spotting opportunities, and history. The trail leads to the picturesque waterfall, which provides a perfect backdrop for a picnic or photo op.
  • Core Creek Park: This delightful park is perfect for a family outing. With boating, fishing, hiking, and biking options, there is sure to be something for everyone here.
  • Tyler State Park: The park offers 1,711 acres of natural beauty that are perfect for hiking, nature walks, and bird watching. The park also features several trails for biking and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Feasterville

Feasterville is a paradise for hikers and bikers alike. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Tyler State Park and Core Creek Park loop is a great option for avid bikers. This 13-mile-loop provides beautiful views of nature while also offering an excellent workout.
  • Garden of Reflection 9/11 Memorial: The Garden of Reflection 9/11 Memorial offers a tranquil setting for a peaceful walk in nature. The paved walking path is approximately one mile long.
  • The Playwicki Farm Trail described earlier is an excellent option for a scenic hike.

Best Things to Do in Feasterville: Activities and Tours

Feasterville is home to many activities and tours that are worth experiencing. Here are some of the best:

  • Guided Horseback Trail Rides: Taking a guided horseback ride through Tyler State Park is an unforgettable experience for both beginner and experienced riders.
  • The Rose Bank Winery Tour: Feasterville is home to several excellent wineries, and the Rose Bank Winery Tour is one that can’t be missed. With a guided tour, wine tasting, and a chance to purchase some great souvenirs, the Rose Bank Winery Tour is a perfect way to spend an afternoon.

Food and Drink Scene of Feasterville

Must-Try Dishes

Feasterville boasts an eclectic food scene with various options for food enthusiasts. Here are some of the must-try dishes in the town:

  • Philly cheesesteak: You can’t visit Philadelphia area without trying a Philly cheesesteak. For an excellent cheesesteak experience, visit The Great American Pub.
  • Schmooey’s Famous Grilled cheese made with Texas toast, gooey American cheese, and crispy bacon is a grilled cheese lover’s dream come true.
  • The German breakfast sandwich, served at Tru Brew Coffee, is made with egg, bacon, and Swiss cheese on a Brezel Twist pretzel roll.

Local Beverages

Feasterville’s craft beer and wine scene is excellent and worth experiencing. Here are some of the best beverages to try:

  • Beers from Straight Bridge Farm, a family-run microbrewery located in nearby Harleysville.
  • Wines from Crossing Vineyards and Winery, which is known for producing some of the best wines in the area.

Where to Stay in Feasterville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Feasterville is an ideal tourist destination, and there are considerable accommodation options available here. Some popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses are:

  • The Hampton Inn and Suites Newtown, which offers remarkable services and is nearby the attractions of the town.
  • The Homewood Suites by Hilton Newtown, located near Oakford and Tyler State Parks, offers a peaceful environment away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

What to Buy in Feasterville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There is an abundance of souvenirs and local products available in Feasterville to bring home as memories. Some of the most popular include:

  • Schmooey’s Award-Winning Tomato Soup
  • Locally roasted coffee from Tru Brew Coffee
  • Beers from Straight Bridge Farm microbrewery, which offers excellent craft beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Feasterville

Feasterville is known for its rich cultural heritage and warm welcome to visitors. Here are some things to keep in mind to respect the local culture and customs:

  • The people of Feasterville are friendly and welcoming, so remember to offer greetings when you meet them.
  • Respect other people’s privacy and do not intrude on someone’s personal space.
  • A lot of businesses and attractions are closed on Sundays due to religious observance, so plan your itinerary accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Feasterville

The best time to visit Feasterville is between March and September. The town offers a plethora of outdoor activities during this time. The annual Briggs Farm Blues Festival held in July is also worth attending.

Do’s and Don’ts of Feasterville

Do:

  • Plan your itinerary and book accommodation in advance of arriving in Feasterville.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the food, drink, and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash behind at the parks or any other natural attractions.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Disrespect the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Feasterville

Feasterville is a safe town to visit. Here are some guidelines to make sure you remain safe:

  • Avoid going to isolated places alone.
  • Lock your hotel room’s door when you leave and secure your valuables in a hotel safe.
  • Get information about potential hazards, such as floods or other storms in advance.

Health in Feasterville

Feasterville has several hospitals and facilities for medical emergencies, including Aria-Jefferson Health, Einstein Medical Center, and Holy Redeemer Hospital. Follow standard precautions to keep yourself healthy.

Getting to Feasterville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Feasterville

Philadelphia International Airport is the closest airport to Feasterville, and it will take about 35 minutes to an hour to get to Feasterville from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Feasterville

Feasterville does not have a bus station of its own, but it is well-connected to several bus depots in nearby cities. The Quakertown Bus Terminal is only a 10-minute drive away.

Other Options

Renting a car is perhaps the easiest way to get to Feasterville as it offers travelers more independence and flexibility once they are in the town.

How to Get Around in Feasterville

Getting around in Feasterville is relatively easy and convenient. You can use a rental car or taxi, or even walk or bike to explore the town independently.

Where to Go Next from Feasterville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Feasterville is close to several nearby destinations that are worth a visit, such as:

  • Philadelphia, a bustling city with top tourist attractions like Independence Hall, the Liberty Bell, and the Philadelphia Museum of Art.
  • New Hope, a charming town on the Delaware River that offers exceptional shopping and dining experiences.

Conclusion

Feasterville is a charming town that offers visitors an excellent combination of natural beauty, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and food and drink experiences. This guide covers all the must-visit places, local culture, recommended activities, best places to eat and stay, and many other useful tips to help make your time in Feasterville unforgettable.
