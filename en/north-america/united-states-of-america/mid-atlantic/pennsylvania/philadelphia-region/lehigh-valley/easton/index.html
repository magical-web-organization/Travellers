Introduction to Easton

Located in Lehigh Valley, in the Philadelphia Region, Easton is a charming city that offers visitors an array of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. It is known for its picturesque downtown area, beautiful parks, and well-curated museums, making it a perfect destination for history buffs, outdoor enthusiasts, and families alike. If you’re planning a trip to Easton, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Easton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Canal Museum: Located at Hugh Moore Historical Park, this museum provides visitors with an insight into the history of the Delaware Canal, which was once a major transportation artery that helped fuel the Industrial Revolution.

  • Sigal Museum: If you’re interested in learning about the history of Northampton County and its surrounding areas, the Sigal Museum is a must-visit. It features exhibits on Native American history, the Moravian Community, the Industrial Revolution, and more.

  • Crayola Experience: Perfect for families, the Crayola Experience is an interactive museum where visitors can explore the world of Crayola and get creative with different art activities.

Natural Attractions of Easton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bushkill Creek: Take a leisurely stroll along the Bushkill Creek Trail and enjoy the stunning views of this picturesque waterway.

  • Lafayette College Arboretum: This serene arboretum is home to over 1400 trees and shrubs and offers visitors a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of the downtown area.

  • Hugh Moore Park: Enjoy a picnic, take a boat ride, or hop on a mule-drawn canal boat and explore the Delaware Canal at this lovely park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Easton

  • Karl Stirner Arts Trail: This beautiful trail runs along the Bushkill Creek and features a variety of art installations by local artists.

  • Lehigh River Water Trail: Take a guided kayak or canoe tour and explore the Lehigh River, which features stunning scenery and abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Easton: Activities and Tours

  • Easton Ghost Tours: Explore the city’s spooky side on a guided ghost tour that takes you through some of the most haunted sites in Easton.

  • Easton Farmers’ Market: Established in 1752, the Easton Farmers’ Market is the oldest continuously operating outdoor market in the United States. It features fresh produce, baked goods, crafts, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Easton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scrapple: A local breakfast staple, scrapple is a traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dish made from pork scraps and cornmeal.

  • Pierogies: Eastern European in origin, pierogies are fluffy, doughy dumplings filled with savory fillings such as potato and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Yergey Brewing: This local brewery features a rotating selection of craft beers and is a favorite among locals.

  • Black River Farms Winery: Sample a variety of local wines at this cozy winery that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Where to Stay in Easton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Eastonian Hotel & Suites: Located in the heart of downtown Easton, this historic hotel offers spacious suites with kitchenettes and easy access to the city’s top attractions.

  • Lafayette Inn: This charming bed and breakfast features elegantly appointed rooms and is located in a quiet residential area of Easton.

What to Buy in Easton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sweet Girlz Bakery: This local bakery offers an array of delicious baked goods, including cake pops, cupcakes, and cookies.

  • Mercantile Home: This curated boutique features a variety of unique home decor, jewelry, and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Easton

Easton is a welcoming and diverse city that values community and celebrates its history and culture. Visitors are encouraged to explore all that the city has to offer and immerse themselves in its rich heritage.

Best Time to Visit Easton

The best time to visit Easton is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the city’s parks and natural attractions are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Easton

Do:

  • Explore the city’s rich history and culture by visiting its museums and historical landmarks.

  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks, especially scrapple and pierogies.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the city’s natural attractions or public spaces.

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions of the community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Easton

Easton is generally a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Easton

Visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves from tick bites when enjoying the city’s natural attractions.
