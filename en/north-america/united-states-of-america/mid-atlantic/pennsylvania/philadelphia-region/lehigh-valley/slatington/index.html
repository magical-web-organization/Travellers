Introduction to Slatington

Slatington is a charming borough located in the Lehigh Valley region of Pennsylvania, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. This picturesque town is nestled at the foothills of the Blue Mountains, surrounded by lush green forests and meandering rivers. With a rich history, natural beauty, and plenty of outdoor recreational opportunities, Slatington is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Slatington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll love the many museums and historical sites in Slatington. Here are a few must-see attractions:

  • Slatington D&L Trailhead: This trailhead marks the starting point of the Delaware & Lehigh National Heritage Corridor, a 165-mile historic pathway that follows the Delaware and Lehigh rivers. The trailhead offers plenty of parking and easy access to the trail.
  • The Slatington Historic Trail Marker: This bronze marker is located near the Main and Franklin Streets intersection, and it highlights the town’s rich history as a slate-producing center.
  • Slatington Railroad Station: This historic train station, built in 1872, is the oldest surviving station in Pennsylvania. Today, it’s used as a community center and hosts various events and exhibits throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Slatington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Slatington is a nature lover’s paradise, offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation and scenic views. Some of the best natural attractions in the area include:

  • Lehigh Gap Nature Center: This 755-acre nature reserve is home to diverse plant and animal life, including rare bird species. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, birdwatching, and guided nature tours.
  • Trexler Nature Preserve: This 1,108-acre nature preserve is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing. It features over 10 miles of trails that wind through forests, meadows, and wetlands.
  • Leaser Lake: This picturesque lake, located just a few miles from Slatington, offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including fishing, boating, and kayaking. Visitors can also enjoy picnicking and hiking on the surrounding trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Slatington

Slatington is a popular destination among hikers and bikers, thanks to its scenic trails and breathtaking vistas. Here are a few of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • D&L Trail: This 165-mile trail follows the Delaware and Lehigh rivers through the heart of Pennsylvania. The Slatington trailhead offers an easy starting point for shorter hikes and bike rides.
  • Lehigh Gap Nature Center Trails: The nature center features over eight miles of trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Lions Club Trail: This 2.5-mile trail winds through woodlands, streams, and marshes, and is an excellent spot for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

Best Things to Do in Slatington: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun things to do in and around Slatington. Here are a few ideas:

  • White-water Rafting: The Lehigh River offers some of the best white-water rafting in the area, with rapids ranging from class I to class III.
  • Horseback Riding: There are several stables in the area that offer guided horseback rides through the scenic countryside.
  • Antiquing: Slatington and the surrounding towns are home to many antique shops and flea markets, where visitors can find unique treasures and vintage items.

Week-Long Itinerary for Slatington

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Slatington, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Slatington, explore town landmarks, such as the Slatington D&L Trailhead and the Slatington Historic Trail Marker.
  • Day 2: Hike along the Lions Club Trail or explore the nature trails at Lehigh Gap Nature Center.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a day of white-water rafting on the Lehigh River.
  • Day 4: Take a guided horseback ride through the scenic countryside or spend the day antiquing.
  • Day 5: Visit Trexler Nature Preserve and explore its hiking and biking trails.
  • Day 6: Spend the day at Leaser Lake, go boating, or fishing.
  • Day 7: Depart Slatington and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Slatington

No trip to Slatington is complete without trying the local cuisine and beverages. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: Pierogies are a Pennsylvania Dutch specialty and can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Shoo-Fly Pie: This molasses-based pie is another Dutch specialty and is a must-try dessert.
  • Cheesesteaks: While not unique to Slatington, the area has its own take on the classic sandwich.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: Pennsylvania’s oldest brewery is located just a few miles from Slatington and produces delicious lagers and ales.
  • Local Wines: The Lehigh Valley region is home to many wineries that produce excellent wines from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Slatington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in and around Slatington, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to upscale hotels. Here are a few options:

  • The Woodstone Country Club and Lodge: This luxurious lodge is located just outside of Slatington and offers upscale accommodations, a championship golf course, and on-site dining.
  • The Comfort Inn: This budget-friendly hotel is located in neighboring Allentown and offers comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and easy access to local attractions.
  • The Hostel Shoppe: This affordable hostel is located in nearby Bethlehem and offers dormitory-style accommodations, a shared kitchen, and a cozy common area.

What to Buy in Slatington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home from Slatington, here are a few ideas:

  • Slate Products: Slatington was once a center for slate production, and many local shops still sell slate products, such as coasters and trivets.
  • Pennsylvania Dutch Crafts: The region is known for its beautiful handcrafted goods, such as pottery and quilts.
  • Local Wines and Beers: The Lehigh Valley region produces some excellent wines and beers that make perfect gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Slatington

Slatington has a strong Pennsylvania Dutch influence, and visitors may notice unique customs and traditions. For example, the area is known for its Hex Signs, which are colorful, geometric designs painted on barns and public buildings.

Best Time to Visit Slatington

Slatington is a year-round destination, offering different attractions and activities depending on the season. The summer months are ideal for outdoor activities, such as hiking and boating, while the fall is a popular time to visit for the colorful foliage. Winter brings opportunities for skiing and snowboarding, and spring is a great time to visit to see the wildflowers in bloom.

Safety and Health

Safety in Slatington

Slatington is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates and welcoming locals. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing valuables.

Health in Slatington

There are several medical facilities located in and around Slatington, including hospitals and clinics. It’s important to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Slatington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Slatington

  • Lehigh Valley International Airport (ABE): This regional airport is located in Allentown, just 20 miles from Slatington.

Bus Stations Near Slatington

  • Trans-Bridge Lines: This bus service offers regular service between Allentown and New York City.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from one of the many car rental companies located at the airport or in Allentown.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Allentown and can be hired for transportation to Slatington.

How to Get Around in Slatington

The best way to get around Slatington is by car. Visitors can rent a car from one of the many car rental companies located in the area, or they can bring their own vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Slatington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding area, here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Allentown: This nearby city is home to many cultural attractions, including art museums and performing arts venues.
  • Bethlehem: This charming town is known for its historic district, shops, and restaurants.
  • Pocono Mountains: Just a short drive from Slatington, the Pocono Mountains offer scenic drives, skiing, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Slatington is a hidden gem in the Lehigh Valley region, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and outdoor recreation. From hiking and biking to exploring local cultural attractions, this charming town has something for everyone.
