Introduction to Whitehall Township

Whitehall Township is a charming community located in Lehigh Valley, Philadelphia Region, in Pennsylvania. This township is famous for its rich history, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Whitehall Township has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Whitehall Township

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Whitehall Township:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lehigh Valley Heritage Museum: This museum has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits related to local history. Highlights include the Liberty Bell Museum, the Black Diamond exhibit, and the Revolutionary War exhibit.

  • Whitehall Historical Preservation Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of Whitehall Township, featuring exhibits dating back to the 18th century.

  • Troxell-Steckel Farm Museum: This museum is a restored Germanic-style farm that dates back to the 18th century. It features antiques and exhibits relating to the daily lives of early settlers in the area.

Natural Attractions of Whitehall Township: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jordan Creek Greenway: This picturesque trail offers miles of hiking and biking opportunities, with stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Coplay Creek Trail: This trail is a 5.5-mile paved path that offers a quiet escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

  • Lehigh River: This river offers kayaking, fishing, and other outdoor recreational activities for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whitehall Township

Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Whitehall Township:

  • Ironton Rail Trail: This trail stretches for 4.5 miles and offers a scenic journey through the lush countryside.

  • Whitehall Parkway Trail: This trail is a 1.5-mile hike that features a beautiful creek and a wildflower meadow.

  • Lehigh Canal Towpath Trail: This trail is a popular river-centric path that follows the route of the historic Lehigh Canal.

Best Things to Do in Whitehall Township: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Whitehall Township:

  • Gettysburg National Military Park: This park offers a fascinating glimpse into American history, featuring guided tours and exhibits that illustrate the events of the Civil War.

  • Allentown Art Museum: This museum features a wide range of exhibits and programs that highlight the art and culture of the Lehigh Valley.

  • Crayola Experience: This family-friendly attraction is a colorful journey through the world of Crayola, featuring interactive exhibits and hands-on activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Whitehall Township

Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample in Whitehall Township:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogi: This Polish dish is a favorite in Whitehall Township, featuring stuffed dumplings with savory fillings.

  • Shoofly Pie: This sweet, molasses-based pie is unique to the Pennsylvania Dutch region and is a must-try for dessert lovers.

  • Scrapple: This popular breakfast meat is made from pork scraps and cornmeal and is a local favorite in Whitehall Township.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: This is the oldest brewery in America and is a staple of local culture in Pennsylvania.

  • Root Beer: This traditional soda can be found in most diners and restaurants in Whitehall Township.

Local Culture and Customs of Whitehall Township

Whitehall Township has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, music, and traditions. Visitors to Whitehall Township can also experience the area’s deep history at the many museums and historic sites scattered throughout the township.
