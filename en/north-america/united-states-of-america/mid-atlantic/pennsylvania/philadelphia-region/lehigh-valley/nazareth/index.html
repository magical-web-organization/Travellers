Introduction to Nazareth

Nazareth is a charming borough situated in the Lehigh Valley region of the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania, United States. It is a small town with a population of just around 6,000, making it an excellent destination for a peaceful and relaxing holiday. Nazareth offers a unique blend of rich history, natural beauty, and cultural landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Nazareth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nazareth was initially founded by Moravian missionaries, and the town has a rich history that visitors can explore through the various museums and historical places. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • Moravian Historical Society: The Moravian Historical Society is the oldest historical society in Pennsylvania and has been collecting, preserving, and sharing the history of the Moravian Church in America for more than 160 years. Explore the museum’s extensive collections and learn about the Moravian Church’s history and legacy.

  • Martin Guitar Factory and Museum: Martin Guitar is one of the world’s most respected guitar makers, and the factory and museum showcase the history and craftsmanship behind these beautiful instruments. Take a guided tour of the factory and see how these guitars are made before exploring the museum’s extensive collection of rare and unique Martin guitars.

Natural Attractions of Nazareth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nazareth is known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor areas that are ideal for hiking, biking, and other outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best natural attractions to check out:

  • Jacobsburg State Park: Jacobsburg State Park offers 1,168 acres of forests, fields, and streams for visitors to explore. The park features a range of activities, including hiking, picnicking, fishing, and hunting. It is also home to the Jacobsburg Environmental Education Center, which offers programs and events for visitors of all ages.

  • Saucon Rail Trail: The Saucon Rail Trail is a 7.5-mile trail that passes through Nazareth and runs alongside the former rail corridor of the Bethlehem Saucon Valley Railroad. The trail is ideal for biking, hiking, and leisurely walks, and it offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nazareth

Nazareth is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • D&L Trail: The D&L Trail is a 165-mile multi-use trail that runs through eastern Pennsylvania, including Nazareth. The trail features a mix of surfaces, including crushed stone, towpath, and single track, and offers stunning views of the Lehigh River, the Delaware Canal, and the surrounding countryside.

  • Nockamixon State Park: Nockamixon State Park is a 5,286-acre park located just a short drive from Nazareth. The park features over 10 miles of hiking trails and 20 miles of mountain biking trails, making it an excellent destination for outdoor adventurers.

Best Things to Do in Nazareth: Activities and Tours

Nazareth offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do while in town:

  • Nazareth Speedway: The Nazareth Speedway is a 1-mile oval race track that has hosted some of the most significant racing events in the country. Visitors can attend races, watch pit crew demonstrations, and even take a ride around the track in a race car.

  • Nazareth Farmers’ Market: The Nazareth Farmers’ Market is held every Saturday from May through October and features local farmers, artisans, and craftspeople. Visitors can shop for fresh produce, artisanal goods, and handmade crafts while enjoying live music and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nazareth

Nazareth offers plenty of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy, making it an excellent destination for a week-long trip. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Moravian Historical Society and learn about the history of the Moravian Church in America.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Martin Guitar Factory and Museum and see how these beautiful instruments are made.

Day 3: Visit Jacobsburg State Park and enjoy some hiking, fishing, or picnicking.

Day 4: Hit the Saucon Rail Trail and enjoy a leisurely bike ride through the countryside.

Day 5: Attend a race at the Nazareth Speedway and witness the thrill of high-speed racing.

Day 6: Shop for fresh produce and handmade crafts at the Nazareth Farmers’ Market.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby attractions like Bethlehem or Allentown.

Food and Drink Scene of Nazareth

Must-Try Dishes

Nazareth is home to some unique and delicious dishes that visitors should try while in town:

  • Stickies: Stickies are a local specialty and are essentially a type of gooey cinnamon bun that is topped with a sweet and sticky icing. These treats are perfect for breakfast or a mid-day snack.

  • Gibblet Gravy: Gibblet Gravy is a traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dish that is made from turkey giblets and served over potatoes or noodles. It’s a hearty and comforting meal that is perfect for a chilly evening.

Local Beverages

Nazareth is also home to a thriving craft beverage scene, with several breweries and wineries in the area. Some must-try local beverages include:

  • Birthright Brewing Company: Birthright Brewing Company is a small-batch craft brewery that specializes in using locally sourced ingredients in their beers. Visitors can enjoy a pint in the taproom or take a growler to go.

  • Vynecrest Winery: Vynecrest Winery is one of the oldest wineries in Pennsylvania and offers an extensive selection of wines made from locally grown grapes. Visitors can enjoy a tasting, take a guided tour of the winery, or attend one of the winery’s many events and festivals throughout the year.

Where to Stay in Nazareth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nazareth offers a range of accommodations for visitors, from chain hotels to charming bed and breakfasts. Some popular options include:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: The Holiday Inn Express & Suites offers modern amenities and spacious rooms at an affordable price.

  • Grand Eastonian Hotel & Suites: The Grand Eastonian Hotel & Suites is a historic hotel that offers luxurious accommodations and stunning views of the Delaware River.

  • The Lafayette Inn: The Lafayette Inn is a charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable rooms and a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Nazareth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Nazareth can find a range of unique and handcrafted items to bring home as souvenirs. Some popular items include:

  • Martin Guitars: Martin Guitars are some of the most respected and sought-after guitars in the world, and visitors can purchase a guitar from the factory or museum gift shop as a souvenir.

  • Local Crafts and Artwork: Many local artisans and craftspeople sell their wares at the Nazareth Farmers’ Market and other local markets, offering a range of unique and handmade souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Nazareth

Nazareth has a rich cultural history that is closely tied to the Moravian Church. Visitors should respect the town’s religious history and customs, including attending church services and being mindful of traditional dress codes when visiting historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Nazareth

The best time to visit Nazareth is in the fall, when the leaves are changing and the weather is cool and crisp. Visitors can enjoy the stunning fall foliage, attend local festivals and events, and take advantage of the excellent hiking and biking trails in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nazareth

Do:

  • Respect the town’s religious history and customs.

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area through hiking and biking trails.

  • Shop for unique souvenirs at the local farmers’ market.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect traditional dress codes when visiting historical sites.

  • Ignore safety precautions when enjoying outdoor activities.

  • Fail to try the local specialties, like stickies and Gibblet Gravy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nazareth

Nazareth is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, like locking car doors and securing personal belongings when out and about.

Health in Nazareth

Nazareth has several hospitals and medical facilities in the area to ensure visitors’ health and wellbeing during their stay.

Getting to Nazareth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nazareth is easily accessible by car and public transportation. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Nazareth

  • Lehigh Valley International Airport: Lehigh Valley International Airport is the closest airport to Nazareth, located just a 20-minute drive away.

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Philadelphia International Airport is located approximately 80 miles south of Nazareth.

Bus Stations Near Nazareth

  • Trans-Bridge Lines: Trans-Bridge Lines offers bus routes from New York City, Philadelphia, and other nearby cities to the Lehigh Valley region.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent a car from a variety of rental agencies located at the local airports or in nearby towns.

  • Taxis and Ride Shares: Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area for visitors who prefer not to rent a car.

How to Get Around in Nazareth

Nazareth is a small town that is easily walkable or bikeable. Visitors can also rent a car or use taxi or ride-sharing services as needed.

Where to Go Next from Nazareth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nazareth is located in the heart of the Lehigh Valley region, which offers plenty of nearby destinations and excursions. Some popular options include:

  • Bethlehem: Bethlehem is a historic city located just a short drive from Nazareth and offers a range of attractions and events throughout the year.

  • Allentown: Allentown is the largest city in the Lehigh Valley and offers plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Nazareth, Pennsylvania, offers a unique and charming destination for visitors looking for a relaxing and enjoyable holiday. From natural attractions to historical sites and local cuisine, Nazareth has something for everyone.
