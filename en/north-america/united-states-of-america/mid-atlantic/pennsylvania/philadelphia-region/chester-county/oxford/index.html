Introduction to Oxford

Oxford is a charming borough located in Chester County, in the heart of the Philadelphia Region, Pennsylvania. The town is named after the prestigious Oxford University in England and has a rich history dating back to the 1700s. Oxford is known for its beautiful architecture, friendly locals, and historic landmarks. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the top things to see and do in Oxford.

List of Places to Visit in Oxford

Oxford has a lot to offer visitors, from cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to food and drink experiences and local shopping. Here are some of the top places to visit in Oxford.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oxford Area Historical Association Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the historic heritage of Oxford and the surrounding area. It features a collection of artifacts, memorabilia, and photographs that tell the story of Oxford’s past.

  • Hopewell Furnace National Historic Site: This national park is located just a short drive from Oxford and is home to a restored 18th-century iron-making community. Visitors can take guided tours of the historic furnace, ironmaster’s mansion, and other buildings, and learn about the importance of iron during the American Revolution.

  • Herr’s Snack Factory Tour: For a unique cultural experience, take a tour of the Herr’s Snack Factory, where you can see how America’s favorite snacks are made. Visitors can watch the production process in action and sample fresh chips and pretzels.

Natural Attractions of Oxford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nottingham County Park: This beautiful park spans over 651 acres and offers stunning natural scenery, hiking trails, fishing and boating opportunities, and picnic areas.

  • Mason-Dixon Trail: This 200-mile hiking trail passes through Oxford and showcases some of the region’s most beautiful landscapes, including the Susquehanna River, Chiques Creek, and Pinnacle Overlook.

  • Wolf’s Hollow County Park: This park features hiking trails and picnic areas, as well as opportunities for birdwatching and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oxford

Oxford is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Nottingham County Park Trail: This 8-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking and offers beautiful views of the park’s diverse natural surroundings.

  • White Clay Creek Preserve Trail: This 7-mile trail offers scenic views of the creek and surrounding woods, as well as opportunities for birdwatching, biking, and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Oxford: Activities and Tours

  • Oxford Arts Alliance: The Oxford Arts Alliance hosts a variety of events, including exhibitions, art classes, and workshops. Visitors can browse the galleries and learn about the local art scene in Oxford.

  • Oxford Mainstreet, Inc.: This non-profit organization hosts a variety of community events, such as the Oxford First Fridays Art Stroll, Summer Restaurant Week, and Harvest Fest.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oxford

If you’re planning to spend a week in Oxford, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Nottingham County Park
  • Day 2: Mason-Dixon Trail
  • Day 3: Oxford Area Historical Association Museum
  • Day 4: Hopewell Furnace National Historic Site
  • Day 5: Herr’s Snack Factory Tour
  • Day 6: Wolf’s Hollow County Park
  • Day 7: Oxford Arts Alliance

Food and Drink Scene of Oxford

Oxford has a diverse food and drink scene that caters to all tastes. Here are some of the top dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese steaks: This classic Philly sandwich is a must-try when visiting Oxford. Local favorites include Grilled Cheese & Co and Jack’s Pizza & Hoagie.

  • Shoofly pie: This traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dessert is a rich and sweet treat made with molasses and crumbly crumbs on top.

Local Beverages

  • Victory Brewing Company: Located just a short drive from Oxford, Victory Brewing Company offers a variety of locally made craft beers.

  • Chaddsford Winery: This winery is located in the Brandywine Valley just outside of Oxford and offers tastings of their award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Oxford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oxford has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay in Oxford:

  • The Inn at Elk Forge: This bed and breakfast is located just outside of Oxford and offers luxurious guest rooms, on-site spa services, and a delicious breakfast every morning.

  • Best Western Plus: This hotel is located in the heart of Oxford and offers a fitness center, outdoor pool, and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Oxford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Arts and crafts: The Oxford Arts Alliance hosts a Gift Shoppe that features works by local artists, including pottery, jewelry, and fine art.

  • Penns Woods Winery: This winery located in Chadds Ford offers a selection of locally produced wines, including Chardonnay and Cabernet Franc.

Local Culture and Customs of Oxford

Oxford has a proud history and unique culture that visitors should be mindful of. Here are some local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Respect for history: Oxford is proud of its heritage, and visitors should be respectful of historical landmarks and cultural traditions.

  • Friendly locals: Oxford residents are known for being welcoming and friendly to visitors, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation.

Best Time to Visit Oxford

The best time to visit Oxford is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. Spring and fall are also great times to visit when the foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oxford

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Oxford:

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical landmarks to learn about Oxford’s rich heritage.

  • Try the local dishes and beverages, including cheese steaks and craft beers.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of historical landmarks and cultural traditions.

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings of Oxford’s parks and outdoor spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oxford

Oxford is a safe destination with low rates of crime. Visitors should take normal precautionary measures, like locking car and hotel doors, and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Oxford

There are no major health concerns in Oxford, but visitors should ensure they are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Oxford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oxford is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Oxford:

Airports Near Oxford

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): Located about an hour’s drive from Oxford.

Bus Stations Near Oxford

  • Oxford Station: Served by the Chester County Transportation Authority.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The nearest Amtrak station is located in Philadelphia, about an hour’s drive from Oxford.

How to Get Around in Oxford

The best way to get around Oxford is by car, but there are also taxi services available. Biking and hiking are great options for exploring the local natural surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Oxford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other destinations in the region, here are some nearby options:

  • Philadelphia: Visit this historic city to see the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall.

  • Lancaster County: Known for its beautiful Amish countryside and charming towns.

Conclusion

Oxford, Pennsylvania, is a hidden gem in the Philadelphia Region with a rich history, natural beauty, and unique cultural experiences.
