Introduction to Kennett Square

Kennett Square is a charming town in Chester County, located in the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania. It’s a small town with a population of around 6,000 people but boasts a rich history, a vibrant culture and is known as the mushroom capital of the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Kennett Square, we’ll take you on a journey to discover all the top things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Kennett Square

Here are some of the top places to visit in Kennett Square:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Kennett Underground Railroad Center - Tells the story of the abolitionists who fought against slavery and their escape from the South.
  • The Kennett Square Historical Society Museum - Exhibits the history of Kennett Square, from Native American tribes to the mushroom industry and beyond.
  • Longwood Gardens - The most popular attraction in the area. An extensive display of gardens, fountains, and conservatories over a thousand acres of land.

Natural Attractions of Kennett Square: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Anson B. Nixon Park - A 106-acre park that has walking trails, picnic areas, playgrounds, and a catch-and-release fishing pond.
  • Red Clay Creek Preserve - A 1,000-acre nature preserve with 10 miles of trails for biking, hiking, and horseback riding.
  • The Mushroom Cap - A mushroom farm that offers tours, sampling, and sales.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kennett Square

Kennett Square has many options for hiking and biking, and here are some of the top ones:

  • The Stroud Preserve - An 800-acre nature reserve with over six miles of trails and an active cattle farm.
  • The White Clay Creek State Park - Over 3,600 acres of forests, meadows, and streams, with beautiful hiking and biking trails.
  • The Kennett Greenway - A proposed trail system that will run through Kennett Square and connect to local parks and preserves.

Best Things to Do in Kennett Square: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Kennett Square:

  • Take a mushroom tour - Learn about the town’s claim to fame and enjoy this fungi-based delicacy.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride - Admire the beautiful scenery from above.
  • Take a Wine Tour - Kennett Square has many vineyards and wineries that offer tours and tastings.

Food and Drink Scene of Kennett Square

Kennett Square has a diverse food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mushroom soup - The town’s iconic dish
  • Crab cakes - Made with fresh crab from the nearby Chesapeake Bay
  • Kennett Square pizza - With mushroom toppings

Local Beverages

  • Victory Brewing Company - A local brewery with a great selection of beers.
  • Penns Woods Winery - A vineyard focused on locally sourced ingredients.
  • Paradocx Vineyard - A vineyard with a farm-to-table approach to wine-making.

What to Buy in Kennett Square: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fresh mushrooms from a local farm
  • Handmade pottery from local artisans
  • Locally sourced wines and craft beers

Local Culture and Customs of Kennett Square

Kennett Square is a close-knit town where people are friendly and welcoming. The town’s culture is founded on a deep appreciation for its history and an unshakable bond with the natural world.

Best Time to Visit Kennett Square

The best time to visit Kennett Square is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the trees and flowers are in full bloom. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold, but visitors will never be bored, no matter when they come.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kennett Square

Do:

  • Explore the vast natural beauty of the town.
  • Try the local foods and drinks found in the restaurants and cafes.
  • Enjoy the town’s history by taking tours of various historical landmarks and museums.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the streets or the parks.
  • Ignore the local customs and people’s ways of life.
  • Fail to respect the natural beauty of the area during your stay.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kennett Square

Kennett Square is a safe and peaceful town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions and avoid unlit and deserted areas at night.

Health in Kennett Square

Kennett Square has a reputation for its excellent healthcare facilities and services. Visitors can rest easy, knowing that they have access to medical care if needed.

Getting to Kennett Square: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the transportation options and logistics for getting to Kennett Square:

Airports Near Kennett Square

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) - Approximately a 45-minute drive
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI) - Approximately a 1 hour 45-minute drive

Bus Stations Near Kennett Square

  • Greyhound - Located in Philadelphia
  • Martz Trailways - Located in Wilmington, Delaware

Other Options

  • AMTRAK - Kennett Square doesn’t have a direct train station, but a transfer from Wilmington, Delaware, is possible, which is 25 minutes from Kennett Square.

How to Get Around in Kennett Square

The best way to get around Kennett Square is by car, bike, or foot. There’s ample parking, and the town’s small size makes it easy to walk or bike to most attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kennett Square: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Kennett Square:

  • Philadelphia - A vibrant city located 25 miles east of Kennett Square, with many historical landmarks, museums, parks, restaurants, and shops.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park - A national landmark located about 20 miles northwest of Kennett Square, famous for its role in the Revolutionary War.
  • Lancaster County - An Amish heavy area with shops, stores, and plenty of beautiful outdoor scenery.

Conclusion

Kennett Square is an idyllic, charming town located in southeastern Pennsylvania. Its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and vibrant culture make it an ideal destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and anyone looking for a peaceful and enjoyable getaway. When you visit Kennett Square, be sure to take your time to appreciate all it has to offer.
