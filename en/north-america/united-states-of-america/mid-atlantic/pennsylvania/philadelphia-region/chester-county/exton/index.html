A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Exton

Located in Chester County, within the Philadelphia Region in Pennsylvania, Exton boasts both natural beauty and historical charm. With a variety of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious eateries, Exton is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique experience.

Introduction to Exton

Exton is a small town located in Chester County, Pennsylvania. It’s known for its scenic beauty and historical significance, making it a great spot for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. With easy access to major cities like Philadelphia and Lancaster, Exton is a convenient location for travelers looking to explore the region.

List of Places to Visit in Exton

Below is a list of must-visit places in Exton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mill at Anselma: This beautifully restored 18th-century grist mill is a National Historic Landmark and a registered Pennsylvania historic site. Visitors can learn about the history of milling and the important role mills played in early American society.

  • The Wharton Esherick Museum: Established in 1972, this museum preserves the work of Wharton Esherick, a prominent sculptor and furniture designer of the 20th century. Visitors can take a guided tour of the museum and see Esherick’s original works on display.

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: Located just a short drive from Exton, this park played a pivotal role in the American Revolution. Visitors can explore the historic landmarks and learn about the brave soldiers who fought for our country’s independence.

Natural Attractions of Exton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Marsh Creek State Park: This state park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and boating. Visitors can rent boats or paddleboards to explore the lake and enjoy the stunning views.

  • Binky Lee Preserve: This nature preserve is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching. Visitors can explore several trails, including the Butterfly Trail which leads to a beautiful meadow filled with wildflowers.

  • French Creek State Park: This park covers over 7,000 acres and offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, swimming, and fishing. Visitors can also explore Hopewell Furnace National Historic Site, which is located within the park boundaries.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Exton

Exton and its surrounding areas offer several excellent hiking and biking routes, including:

  • The Struble Trail: This trail is over 6 miles long and follows the route of the old Pennsylvania Railroad. It’s a popular spot for hiking, biking, and rollerblading.

  • The Chester Valley Trail: This trail is over 14 miles long and connects Exton with Valley Forge National Historical Park and King of Prussia. It’s a great spot for a leisurely bike ride or a long run.

  • The Horseshoe Trail: This trail is over 140 miles long and runs from Valley Forge National Historical Park to the Appalachian Trail. It’s a challenging but rewarding hike for experienced adventurers.

Best Things to Do in Exton: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring cultural attractions, there are plenty of other things to do in Exton, including:

  • Amish Country Tours: Experience the unique Amish culture by taking a guided tour of Lancaster County’s Amish communities. Visitors can learn about the history, customs, and way of life of the Amish people.

  • Glenmoore Eagle Farms: This family-owned farm offers a variety of activities, including hayrides, pumpkin picking, and corn mazes. Visitors can also meet and interact with the farm’s many animals, including horses, chickens, and goats.

  • Exton Mall: Shop ‘til you drop at Exton Mall, which features over 100 stores, including major retailers like Macy’s and Sephora.

Week-Long Itinerary for Exton

Day 1: Visit The Mill at Anselma and explore the nearby Struble Trail.

Day 2: Take a trip to Valley Forge National Historical Park and learn about American history.

Day 3: Spend the day hiking in French Creek State Park and exploring Hopewell Furnace National Historic Site.

Day 4: Enjoy a leisurely bike ride on the Chester Valley Trail and stop for lunch in King of Prussia.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Wharton Esherick Museum and browse the unique gift shop.

Day 6: Go shopping at Exton Mall and try some delicious local food.

Day 7: Take a day trip to Lancaster County and experience Amish culture firsthand.

Food and Drink Scene of Exton

Exton offers a variety of dining options, ranging from casual eateries to fine dining restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteak: A Philadelphia classic that can’t be missed! Try one at Great American Pub for a tasty rendition.

  • Lobster Roll: Get your seafood fix at Brickside Grille, which serves a delicious lobster roll with fries.

  • Barbecue: Enjoy some of the best barbecue in town at Dixie Picnic, with plenty of choices to satisfy your appetite.

Local Beverages

  • Victory Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their famous HopDevil IPA and Golden Monkey Belgian Tripel.

  • Wyebrook Farm: This family-owned farm is also a popular spot for drinking. Customers can enjoy beer, wine, and cider under the farm’s beautiful oak trees.

  • Penns Woods Winery: Located on a scenic property, this winery produces high-quality wines, enjoyed in a rustic and relaxed setting.

Where to Stay in Exton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Exton has plenty of lodging options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • The Desmond Hotel: This upscale hotel is known for its grand architecture and luxurious amenities. It’s located just minutes from major attractions like Valley Forge National Historical Park.

  • Comfort Suites: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and complimentary breakfast. It’s a good option for families.

  • The Waynebrook Inn: This charming inn offers cozy rooms and a homely feel. It’s located in nearby Honey Brook and is a popular destination for visitors looking for a more rustic experience.

What to Buy in Exton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique gift to bring home? Exton offers plenty of options, including:

  • Wharton Esherick Museum Gift Shop: Browse a selection of gifts inspired by Wharton Esherick’s unique designs and aesthetics.

  • Uncommon Goods: This shop offers a variety of handmade products from local artisans, including home decor, jewelry, and accessories.

  • Wyebrook Farm Market: Sample locally made cheeses, butters, canned goods, and sweets from this bustling farm store.

Local Culture and Customs of Exton

Respectful behavior is expected throughout Exton. As the area is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery and is rich in historical significance, visitors should do their utmost to protect the environment and preserve historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Exton

Exton can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and comfortable. Fall, in particular, is a great time to see the beautiful autumn colors that blanket the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Exton

Do:

  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the area.

  • Be respectful of historical sites and cultural attractions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.

  • Leave valuables visible in vehicles or unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Exton

Exton is a relatively safe area, but visitors should exercise caution and take basic safety precautions while exploring,

Health in Exton

There are several medical facilities in the area, including the Chester County Hospital and the Phoenixville Hospital.

Getting to Exton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Exton

Philadelphia International Airport is the closest major airport to Exton and is located about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Exton

Greyhound and Peter Pan bus lines both offer service to the nearby city of Phoenixville.

Other Options

Exton can also be accessed by car via the Pennsylvania Turnpike or Route 30.

How to Get Around in Exton

The best way to get around Exton is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent a car at Philadelphia International Airport or at any major rental car company in the area.

Where to Go Next from Exton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Exton is conveniently located within easy reach of several nearby destinations, including:

  • Philadelphia: Explore the City of Brotherly Love and its many historic landmarks and attractions.

  • Lancaster County: Visit the Amish communities and learn about their unique way of life.

  • Valley Forge: Explore the historic landmarks of this pivotal battlefield in America’s fight for independence.

Conclusion

Exton, PA offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and small-town charm. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff or a foodie, Exton has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip and explore all that the area has to offer.
