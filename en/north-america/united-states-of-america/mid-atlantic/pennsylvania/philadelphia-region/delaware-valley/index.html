Introduction to Delaware Valley

Delaware Valley is a region located in the Philadelphia Metropolitan Area of Pennsylvania in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. It is popular for its rich history, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Delaware Valley.

List of Places to Visit in Delaware Valley

Delaware Valley is home to many culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and historic sites that are must-visit destinations for every traveler.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Independence Hall: This iconic American landmark is the birthplace of the United States of America. It is where the Declaration of Independence was signed in 1776 and where the U.S. Constitution was written.
  • Liberty Bell: Another iconic symbol of American independence, this bell is located in Philadelphia and has been a popular attraction for tourists for decades.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: Visit this historic site to learn about the American Revolution and the story of Washington’s army during the war.

Natural Attractions of Delaware Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area: This park boasts stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and scenic views of the Delaware River.
  • Tyler State Park: For outdoor enthusiasts, Tyler State Park is a must-visit destination. The park boasts hiking trails, fishing, and picnic areas.
  • Nockamixon State Park: Another great outdoor recreation area, Nockamixon State Park has a 1,450-acre lake perfect for swimming, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delaware Valley

Delaware Valley is home to many hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Schuylkill River Trail: This 26.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the Schuylkill River and is perfect for biking, walking, and jogging.
  • Wissahickon Valley Park: This park offers over 50 miles of hiking trails for all skill levels.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: The park’s 19.5 miles of trails offer scenic views of the park’s historic landmarks and beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Delaware Valley: Activities and Tours

  • Philadelphia Segway Tour: Explore Philadelphia on a Segway and see the city’s iconic landmarks and historic sites.
  • Valley Forge Guided Tour: Take a guided tour of Valley Forge National Historical Park and learn about the park’s history and significance during the American Revolution.
  • Bucks County River Country: Enjoy a day on the river with tubing, rafting, and kayaking adventures at Bucks County River Country.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delaware Valley

Food and Drink Scene of Delaware Valley

Delaware Valley is known for its diverse food scene, ranging from new American cuisine to traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteak: A quintessential Philadelphia dish, the cheesesteak is a sandwich filled with thinly sliced steak, cheese, and sometimes onions and peppers.
  • Soft Pretzel: This doughy treat is a Philadelphia staple and is perfect for a quick snack on-the-go.
  • Shoo Fly Pie: A traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dish, shoo fly pie is a molasses-based pie with a crumbly topping.

Local Beverages

  • Yards Brewing Company: A popular brewery in Philadelphia, Yards offers a variety of craft beers and ales.
  • Victory Brewing Company: Another popular brewery in Delaware Valley, Victory offers a variety of craft beers and food at their brewpubs in Downingtown and Kennett Square.

Where to Stay in Delaware Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delaware Valley offers a range of accommodation options for every traveler’s needs and budget.

  • Sofitel Philadelphia: Located in the heart of Philadelphia, this luxury hotel offers elegant rooms and a French restaurant.
  • The Independent Hotel: A boutique hotel in the heart of Philadelphia’s historic district, The Independent Hotel offers rooms with unique decor and modern amenities.
  • Apple Hostels of Philadelphia: A budget-friendly hostel in Philadelphia that offers dorm-style and private rooms.

What to Buy in Delaware Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Country: Take home some traditional Pennsylvania Dutch food items such as jams, jellies, and canned goods.
  • Reading Terminal Market: Located in Philadelphia, Reading Terminal Market offers a variety of local products including freshly baked goods, cheese, and produce.

Local Culture and Customs of Delaware Valley

Delaware Valley has a rich and diverse cultural history, including the Pennsylvania Dutch, Irish, African American, Italian, and more.

Best Time to Visit Delaware Valley

The best time to visit Delaware Valley is during the fall when the foliage is at its peak and the weather is cool and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delaware Valley

Do:

  • Do try a Philadelphia cheesesteak.
  • Do visit the historic landmarks in Philadelphia and Valley Forge National Historical Park.
  • Do explore the outdoor recreation areas such as Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area and Tyler State Park.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and outdoor areas.
  • Don’t miss out on the local food scene.
  • Don’t forget to tip your server at restaurants and cafes.
