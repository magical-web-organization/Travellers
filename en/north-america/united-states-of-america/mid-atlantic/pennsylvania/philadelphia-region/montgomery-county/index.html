Introduction to Montgomery County

Montgomery County is located in the Philadelphia Region in Pennsylvania, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. The county is home to a number of attractions, including historical landmarks, scenic views, outdoor adventures, and a bustling food and drink scene. In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in Montgomery County, as well as where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to stay safe and healthy during your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Montgomery County

Montgomery County is home to a variety of attractions, including culturally important museums and historical sites, natural wonders and outdoor adventures, and a bustling food and drink scene.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Montgomery County boasts a rich history, as evidenced by its numerous museums and historical sites. Here are some must-see attractions:

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: This park was the site of the Revolutionary War’s historic winter encampment of the Continental Army in 1777-78. It offers visitors the opportunity to see historical re-enactments, visit the museum and galleries, and tour the park’s many monuments and markers.
  • Mann Center for the Performing Arts: This open-air venue offers concerts and performances by some of the biggest names in music, theater, and dance each summer season.
  • Peter Wentz Farmstead: This historic farm was built in the mid-1700s and offers visitors a glimpse into the German American farming lifestyle of the colonial era.
  • Elmwood Park Zoo: Home to more than 300 animals from around the world, Elmwood Park Zoo is a great place for families to spend a day.

Natural Attractions of Montgomery County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Montgomery County is home to some of the most stunning natural scenery in the state. Take a look at some of the best outdoor attractions:

  • Schuylkill River Trail: This 26.5-mile trail runs along the Schuylkill River and offers scenic views of the river and surrounding landscape.
  • Perkiomen Trail: This 20-mile trail runs from Green Lane Park to the Schuylkill River Trail, and offers views of the Perkiomen Creek and its surrounding forested areas.
  • Valley Forge Park: In addition to its historical importance, Valley Forge Park offers visitors 3,500 acres of recreational opportunities, including hiking, biking, and picnicking.
  • Evansburg State Park: Located in Skippack, this park features over 1,000 acres of woodlands, fields, and trails, as well as a reconstructed 18th-century Pennsylvania German farmhouse.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montgomery County

Montgomery County is home to some of Pennsylvania’s most scenic hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Perkiomen Trail: This 20-mile trail is a great place to bike or hike and features stunning views of the Perkiomen Creek.
  • Schuylkill River Trail: This 26.5-mile trail runs along the Schuylkill River and offers beautiful views of Philadelphia and the surrounding area.
  • Wissahickon Trail: This 10-mile trail follows the Wissahickon Creek, offering scenic views of the creek and surrounding forested areas.

Best Things to Do in Montgomery County: Activities and Tours

Montgomery County is home to a variety of activities and tours that are sure to keep you entertained during your visit. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Escape Rooms: Montgomery County has a number of escape rooms that offer fun and challenging challenges for groups of all sizes.
  • Food Tours: Montgomery County is known for its food and drink scene. Take a food tour to sample some of the best the area has to offer.
  • Ghost Tours: If you’re interested in the paranormal, Montgomery County has a number of ghost tours that take you to some of the area’s most haunted sites.
  • Hot Air Ballooning: Take a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful countryside of Montgomery County for a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Montgomery County

Montgomery County is home to a vibrant food and drink scene that is not to be missed. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteaks: While the best-known cheesesteaks are found in Philadelphia, Montgomery County has plenty of great spots that serve this regional specialty.
  • Chicken Pot Pie: A classic Pennsylvania Dutch dish, chicken pot pie features shredded chicken, onions, celery, potatoes, and a top or bottom crust.
  • Pierogies: A staple of Eastern European cuisine, pierogies are filled with potato, cheese, or sauerkraut and served with sour cream or applesauce.

Local Beverages

  • Root beer: Montgomery County is home to a variety of small-batch root beer brewers, offering unique flavors that are sure to excite your taste buds.
  • Wine: Montgomery County is home to several wineries that produce a variety of reds, whites, and fruit wines.
  • Beer: Montgomery County has a number of local breweries, offering a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Montgomery County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montgomery County has plenty of accommodation options to suit any budget or travel style. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Courtyard by Marriott Philadelphia Montgomeryville: Located in Montgomeryville, this hotel offers comfort and convenience at an affordable price.
  • Normandy Farm Hotel and Conference Center: This historic hotel is located on a 38-acre estate and offers luxurious amenities and beautiful views.
  • Boyd’s Motor Lodge: This quirky motel in Gwynedd Valley offers travelers affordable accommodation with a vintage vibe.

What to Buy in Montgomery County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Montgomery County, check out these local favorites:

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Country: This is the perfect place to find traditional Amish crafts, handmade quilts, and a variety of other artisanal goods.
  • Farmers’ Markets: Montgomery County is home to a variety of farmers’ markets selling locally grown and produced goods.
  • Chocolate: Montgomery County is home to several chocolatiers, offering handmade chocolates and other confections.

Local Culture and Customs of Montgomery County

Montgomery County has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its food, art, and architecture. Some cultural customs to be aware of include:

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Culture: As home to a large Amish and Mennonite population, Montgomery County is home to traditional Pennsylvania Dutch culture, including homemade crafts, quilts, and food.
  • Revolutionary War History: As a site of several important events during the Revolutionary War, Montgomery County is rich in patriotic history and memorial sites.

Best Time to Visit Montgomery County

The best time to visit Montgomery County is during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the weather is mild. However, the summer months offer plenty of outdoor activities and events, while the winter months offer unique opportunities for ice skating, sledding, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montgomery County

Do:

  • Visit Valley Forge National Historical Park: This site is a must-visit for history buffs and nature lovers alike.
  • Try the local cuisine: Montgomery County is home to a variety of unique and delicious foods.
  • Explore the outdoors: Montgomery County is home to several of Pennsylvania’s best hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Montgomery County takes its environmental stewardship seriously and littering is not tolerated.
  • Speeding: The roads in Montgomery County can be winding and narrow, so be sure to drive cautiously.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montgomery County

Montgomery County is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still exercise caution and common sense. Here are a few tips to ensure a safe trip:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like any other big city, Montgomery County has its share of petty crime. Keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Follow park rules and regulations: If hiking or exploring parks or wilderness areas, be sure to follow all park rules and regulations to avoid danger.

Health in Montgomery County

Montgomery County has a variety of hospitals and healthcare facilities, ensuring visitors have access to quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Montgomery County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Montgomery County is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Montgomery County

  • Philadelphia International Airport: This airport is located approximately 30 miles from Montgomery County and offers domestic and international flights.
  • Northeast Philadelphia Airport: Located in Northeast Philadelphia, this airport offers private plane charters.

Bus Stations Near Montgomery County

  • Greyhound Station: Located in Philadelphia, this station offers bus service to and from Montgomery County

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Montgomery County is located near several major highways, making it easily accessible by car.
  • Taxi/Ride-Share: Taxi and ride-share services are readily available throughout Montgomery County.

How to Get Around in Montgomery County

Getting around Montgomery County is easy and convenient. Here are some of the best options:

  • Public Transportation: Montgomery County is home to several public transportation options, including SEPTA trains and buses.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a cost-effective way to explore Montgomery County at your own pace.
  • Biking: Montgomery County is home to a variety of bike trails that offer scenic views and exercise.

Where to Go Next from Montgomery County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore some nearby destinations and excursions, here are some great options:

  • Philadelphia: Just a short drive from Montgomery County, Philadelphia is a city rich in history, culture, and entertainment.
  • Lancaster County: Located to the west of Montgomery County, Lancaster County is home to the Pennsylvania Dutch and offers a variety of Amish experiences.
  • Hershey: Located to the east of Montgomery County, Hershey is home to Hersheypark and the Hershey’s Chocolate World.

Conclusion

Montgomery County, Pennsylvania, has something to offer visitors of all kinds. From the historical significance of Valley Forge National Historical Park to the stunning natural scenery of the Perkiomen Trail, there is no shortage of things to see and do in Montgomery County. With its vibrant food and drink scene and easy accessibility options, it’s easy to see why Montgomery County is a must-see destination for travelers to Pennsylvania.
