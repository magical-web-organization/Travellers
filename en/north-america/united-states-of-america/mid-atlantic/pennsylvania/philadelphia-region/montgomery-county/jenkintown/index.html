Introduction to Jenkintown

Welcome to Jenkintown, a charming borough in Montgomery County, Philadelphia Region, Pennsylvania, United States of America! Whether you’re a culture lover, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or just looking for a peaceful getaway, Jenkintown has something to offer for every traveler.

Founded in 1874, Jenkintown has a rich history and a strong sense of community, reflected in its well-preserved Victorian architecture, lively cultural scene, and friendly locals. The borough is conveniently located only 10 miles north of Philadelphia, but with its own distinct character and attractions.

In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a complete itinerary of the best things to do in Jenkintown, from cultural landmarks to scenic trails, local cuisine to souvenirs, and much more!

List of Places to Visit in Jenkintown

Let’s start with a comprehensive list of the top attractions and landmarks in Jenkintown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jenkintown-Wyncote Train Station: an iconic landmark of Jenkintown’s Victorian architecture, listed on the National Register of Historic Places;
  • Jenkintown Library: a community hub with an extensive collection of books, events, and resources for all ages;
  • Abington Art Center: a vibrant cultural center with rotating exhibitions, workshops, and performances;

Natural Attractions of Jenkintown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alverthorpe Park: a 22-acre park with walking trails, playgrounds, picnic areas, and an amphitheater;
  • Briar Bush Nature Center: a nature preserve with over 12 acres of woodlands, meadows, and wildlife habitat, perfect for birdwatching and hiking;
  • Fairmount Park: a sprawling parkland with over 9,200 acres of green space, hiking trails, historic sites, and outdoor events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jenkintown

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love exploring the scenic trails of Jenkintown, perfect for hiking, biking, and strolling. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • Tookany Creek Trail: a 1.5-mile trail along the creek, with beautiful views and birdwatching opportunities;
  • Wyncote Trail: a 5-mile trail connecting Alverthorp Park to Cheltenham Township, with varied terrain and stunning wildlife;
  • Pennypack Trail: a 14-mile trail connecting Jenkintown to Northeast Philadelphia, with scenic views of Pennypack Creek and historic landmarks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Jenkintown: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Jenkintown, depending on your interests and preferences.

  • Jenkintown Farmer’s Market: a weekly market showcasing local produce, baked goods, crafts, and live music;
  • Jenkintown Festival of the Arts: an annual arts festival featuring over 100 vendors, food trucks, and live performances;
  • Ghost Hunting Tour: an eerie tour of Jenkintown’s haunted sites, led by local experts and paranormal investigators.

Food and Drink Scene of Jenkintown

Jenkintown’s food and drink scene is diverse, delicious, and full of local flavor. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages to sample during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Philly Cheesesteak: a classic sandwich made with thinly sliced beef, melted cheese, and toppings of your choice, served at West Avenue Grill;
  • Mushroom Risotto: a creamy and savory rice dish with fresh mushrooms, herbs, and Parmesan cheese, served at Cafe On the Corner;
  • Wood-Fired Pizza: a crispy and flavorful pizza with creative toppings and homemade sauce, served at Jules Thin Crust.

Local Beverages

  • Yard’s Brewing Company: a local craft brewery with signature beers like Brawler and Philly Pale Ale, served at Neshaminy Creek Brewing Company and Costello’s Bar & Grill;
  • Chaddsford Winery: a nearby winery with award-winning wines and seasonal events, served at Cafe Lombardi and other restaurants;
  • Yards Brewing Company: a local brewery with signature beers like Brawler and Philly Pale Ale, served at Neshaminy Creek Brewing Company and Costello’s Bar & Grill.

Where to Stay in Jenkintown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jenkintown offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • The Chateau Suites: a charming boutique hotel in the heart of Jenkintown, with spacious rooms, modern amenities, and complimentary breakfast;
  • Hiway Host Motel: a budget-friendly motel with clean and cozy rooms, free Wi-Fi, and 24-hour check-in;
  • Bluebird Inn: a historic guesthouse with stylish decor and a central location, within walking distance of downtown shops, restaurants, and attractions.

What to Buy in Jenkintown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring back home, Jenkintown has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some suggestions:

  • Abington Art Center Gift Shop: a curated selection of local art, ceramics, jewelry, and other handmade crafts;
  • Jenkintown Farmer’s Market: fresh produce, baked goods, and other artisanal products from local vendors;
  • Jenkintown Station Cafe: specialty coffees, teas, candies, and other treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Jenkintown

Jenkintown is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who take pride in their borough’s history, community, and culture. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Using “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals, as a sign of respect and courteousness;
  • Dressing appropriately for the occasion, as some events and venues may have dress codes or expectations;
  • Observing local norms and regulations, such as recycling, traffic rules, and noise ordinances.

Best Time to Visit Jenkintown

Jenkintown is a year-round destination, with activities and events happening throughout the seasons. However, some recommended times to visit include:

  • Spring (March-May): when the weather is mild and sunny, and nature starts to bloom with flowers and trees;
  • Autumn (September-November): when the foliage turns to vibrant colors and the harvest season brings plenty of fresh produce and local fairs;
  • Winter (December-February): when the holiday season brings festive lights, music, and markets, and snow activities like skiing and sledding are available nearby.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jenkintown

Here are some general guidelines to follow when visiting Jenkintown:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs, and interact with locals in a friendly and polite manner;
  • Explore the borough on foot or bike, as many sights, shops, and restaurants are within walking distance;
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and landmarks available, from hiking to biking, from art to history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property, as Jenkintown takes pride in its clean and well-maintained environment;
  • Engage in illegal or dangerous activities, like drug use, excessive drinking, or reckless driving;
  • Disrespect the local flora and fauna, as Jenkintown is home to many protected species and ecosystems.

Safety and Health

Jenkintown is a safe and healthy borough, with low crime rates and quality healthcare services. However, it’s advisable to take some precautions and prepare accordingly.

Safety in Jenkintown

  • Always lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodation, and keep your valuables safe;
  • Avoid walking alone at night in secluded or poorly lit areas, and trust your instincts if you feel uncomfortable or threatened;
  • Use caution when crossing streets or cycling, as traffic can be heavy and fast-paced in some areas.

Health in Jenkintown

  • Check your vaccinations and health insurance before traveling to Jenkintown, and consult a doctor if needed;
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors, as the sun and insects can be strong in warmer months;
  • Carry your medication and first-aid supplies with you, and be aware of local hospitals and pharmacies in case of emergency.

Getting to Jenkintown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jenkintown is conveniently located near major highways and public transportation options, making it easy to reach from nearby cities and airports. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Jenkintown

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): the closest and largest airport, located 34 minutes south of Jenkintown, with multiple airlines and international flights;
  • Trenton-Mercer Airport (TTN): a smaller airport located 38 minutes northeast of Jenkintown, with regional and private flights.

Bus Stations Near Jenkintown

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: a bus station located 15 minutes south of Jenkintown, with national and regional routes to major destinations;
  • Megabus: a bus station located within walking distance of Jenkintown-Wyncote train station, with affordable and frequent trips to New York City, Washington DC, and other cities.

Other Options

  • Car: Jenkintown is easily accessible by car from major highways like I-276, I-476, and I-95, with parking available at many hotels and attractions;
  • Train: The SEPTA Regional Rail offers regular train service to Jenkintown-Wyncote Station, connecting to Philadelphia, Trenton, New York City, and other destinations;
  • Bike: Jenkintown has many bike lanes and trails, and bike rental options are available at some hotels and shops.

How to Get Around in Jenkintown

Jenkintown is a walkable and bike-friendly borough, with many sights, shops, and restaurants within walking distance or a short bike ride away. For longer distances or transportation options, consider:

  • SEPTA Regional Rail: the train system connects Jenkintown-Wyncote Station to Philadelphia, Trenton, New York City, and other destinations, with frequent and reliable service;
  • Uber or Lyft: ride-sharing apps are available in Jenkintown and nearby cities, offering affordable and convenient transportation;
  • Car rental: if you plan to explore other parts of Pennsylvania or the East Coast, car rental options are available at major airports and in downtown Philadelphia.

Where to Go Next from Jenkintown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Pennsylvania and the Mid-Atlantic region, here are some destinations to consider:

  • Philadelphia: the City of Brotherly Love, located only 10 miles south of Jenkintown, with world-famous landmarks like the Liberty Bell, Independence Hall, and the Philadelphia Museum of Art;
  • Valley Forge National Park: a historic landmark and natural preserve, located 20 miles northwest of Jenkintown, with scenic trails, wildlife, and a museum about the Revolutionary War;
  • Gettysburg: a historic town and battlefield, located 120 miles west of Jenkintown, with museums, tours, and events about the Civil War.

Conclusion

We hope this travel guide has inspired you to visit Jenkintown, a charming borough full of culture, nature, and community spirit. Plan your trip carefully, follow local customs and regulations, and take the time to enjoy all the sights, sounds, and flavors that Jenkintown has to offer. Safe travels!
