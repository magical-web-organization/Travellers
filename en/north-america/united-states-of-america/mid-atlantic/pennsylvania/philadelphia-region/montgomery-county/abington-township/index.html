Abington Township is a charming township in Montgomery County, in the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania. This beautiful area is located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America, boasting a rich history and culture, along with picturesque natural landscapes. In this comprehensive guide to Abington Township, we will cover everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip.

Introduction to Abington Township

Located just 12 miles north of Philadelphia, Abington Township is known for its historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and outdoor recreational areas. The area is named after Abington, a suburban town in the north of England, which means “town on a hill”. In this township, visitors can discover rich history, lush parks, delicious cuisine, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Abington Township

Abington Township is home to a wide range of attractions to visit. Here are some of the must-visit places in Abington Township.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Abington Art Center: This contemporary art center features a rotating collection of exhibitions, classes, and events showcasing emerging and established artists.

  • Gratz College: This institution is one of the country’s oldest Jewish colleges

  • Rydal Waters: A historical home that showcases Philadelphia region’s iconic architecture.

  • Abington Friends Meetinghouse: This 18th-century building is still active with a Quaker congregation and is a National Historic Landmark

Natural Attractions of Abington Township: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crestmont Park: This 45-acre park features a playground, picnic areas, basketball court, and a softball field.

  • Baederwood Nature Reserve: A nearly 30-acre nature preserve with a large pond and a walking trail perfect for a peaceful walk.

  • Pennypack Ecological Restoration Trust: 812 acres of natural terrain filled with hiking trails, wildlife and bird watching habitats.

  • Abington Township Bird Sanctuary & Wildlife Garden: A bird sanctuary and garden perfect for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abington Township

Abington Township is famous for its scenic hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the top routes to explore.

  • Pennypack Trail: This trail is situated on a former railroad bed that offers stunning views of Pennypack Creek and the nature that surrounds it. The trail stretches 16.6 miles from Rockledge to Norristown.

  • Crestmont Trail: Located within the Crestmont Park, the trail runs through wooded areas and offers beautiful views of the park’s wildlife.

  • Pennypack Loop Trail: This winding trail is perfect for unfamiliar hikers looking for a scenic route. The 4.3-mile loop trail passes through the most beautiful landscapes in Abington Township.

  • Baederwood Trail: This trail offers scenic views of Baederwood Nature Reserve and its surrounding areas. This 1.2-mile-long trail is perfect for a peaceful walk.

Best Things to Do in Abington Township: Activities and Tours

Abington Township offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Abington Township.

  • Hiking: With a wide range of trails to choose from, hiking is one of the most popular activities to indulge in Abington Township.

  • Visiting the Art Center: Art lovers can explore the Abington Arts Center, which features contemporary art exhibits, classes, and events.

  • Tours: The township offers guided tours to many cultural and historical landmarks guided by local experts and historical societies.

  • Local Food and Drink: The food scene in Abington Township is diverse and delicious. Visitors can taste the best of the area’s cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Abington Township

Abington Township is home to various cuisines from across the globe. Here’s what you must try in the local food and drink scene.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Steak Sandwich: The Township’s take on Philly’s staple is a must-have during a visit.

  • Soft Pretzel: Abington Township has some of the best and softest pretzels you’ll ever taste.

  • Shoo-fly Pie: A pie unique to the Pennsylvania Dutch region, made with molasses and mixed with spices.

Local Beverages

  • Victory Brewing Company: A brewery that channels a blend of German and American brewing methods.

  • Stone & Key Cellars: A charming winery with a farm atmosphere where visitors can enjoy small tasting groups.
