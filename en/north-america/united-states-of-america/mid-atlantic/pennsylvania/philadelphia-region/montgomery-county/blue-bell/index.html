Introduction to Blue Bell

Nestled in Montgomery County, the charming township of Blue Bell is a hidden gem that is certainly worth a visit. Located in the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania, it offers plenty of exciting recreational activities, educational museums, and scenic attractions for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Bell

Visiting Blue Bell and wondering what to see and do? Here’s an overview of the top attractions and must-visit places in Blue Bell:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Peter Wentz Farmstead. Take a step back in time to when the colonists first settled in Pennsylvania. This preserved farmhouse showcases the history of the area and allows visitors to take a glimpse into the past.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park. Experience American history first-hand in the same setting where George Washington camped and trained his troops during the Revolutionary War.
  • The Museum of American History. Explore this museum that showcases the rich history and culture of the United States through interactive exhibits and educational programs.
  • Philadelphia Museum of Art. Just a short drive from Blue Bell, wander through the museum’s vast collections of art and masterpieces, including American, European, and Asian art.

Natural Attractions of Blue Bell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wissahickon Valley Park. Escape the bustle of the city and enjoy nature’s tranquility through miles of hiking and biking trails and picturesque views of the Wissahickon Creek.
  • The Morris Arboretum. Take a relaxing stroll among the Arboretum’s 92 acres of lush gardens, trees, and wildlife. It’s a must-visit for garden lovers.
  • Pennypack Ecological Restoration Trust. Hike the numerous trails that offer magnificent views of the local flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Bell

Blue Bell is known for its stunning natural beauty and offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Wissahickon Trail. The Wissahickon Valley Park offers a multi-use trail system through the forested gorge and alongside the winding creek. With over 50 miles of trails, there’s a route to suit all levels of hikers.
  • Perkiomen Trail. This 20-mile trail follows the peaceful waters of the Perkiomen Creek and is perfect for a relaxing bike ride or leisurely walk.
  • Schuylkill River Trail. This 120-mile trail offers beautiful views of Philadelphia’s skyline, Schuylkill River, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Blue Bell: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding. Take an exhilarating ride through the beautiful countryside with one of the many horseback riding options available.
  • Golfing. Home to several world-renowned golf courses, Blue Bell offers plenty of options for both novice and experienced golf enthusiasts.
  • Amusement Parks. Adventure awaits at the nearby Six Flags Great Adventure theme park, where visitors can explore award-winning roller coasters and thrilling attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blue Bell

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Blue Bell, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Wissahickon Valley Park for a morning hike. In the afternoon, check out the Peter Wentz Farmstead and learn about colonial history.
Day 2 Take a scenic drive to the Morris Arboretum and explore the beautiful gardens. In the afternoon, stop by the Museum of American History.
Day 3 Spend the day at Valley Forge National Historical Park, visit the Washington Memorial Chapel, and hike the park’s numerous trails.
Day 4 Tee off at one of the area’s world-renowned golf courses or go horseback riding.
Day 5 Take a day trip to Philadelphia and visit the Philadelphia Museum of Art.
Day 6 Take a bike ride on the Perkiomen Trail. In the afternoon, visit Pennypack Ecological Restoration Trust for a hike.
Day 7 Spend the day at Six Flags Great Adventure, only a short drive away from Blue Bell.

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Bell

Blue Bell boasts a thriving culinary scene with plenty of options for food and drink enthusiasts. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Philly Cheesesteak. A local favorite, try the famous Philly Cheesesteak at local restaurants.
  • Pretzels. Head to Miller’s Twist Pretzels for some of the best pretzels in town.
  • Hoagies/Grinders. Satisfy your hunger with a flavor-packed hoagie sandwich from Lee’s Hoagie House.

Local Beverages

  • Yards Brewing Company. This craft brewery that offers a wide range of tasty beers.
  • Revivalist Spirits. Try locally crafted gin and whiskey at this distillery that is gaining popularity.
  • Victory Brewing Company. Enjoy award-winning craft beers available at the brewery’s local taproom.

Where to Stay in Blue Bell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are a few recommendations on some of the best places to stay in Blue Bell:

  • Normandy Farm Hotel & Conference Center. A luxurious and historic hotel featuring top-notch amenities and dining options.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Lansdale. Enjoy spacious rooms and great amenities such as an indoor pool and fitness center.
  • Blue Bell Inn. A charming guesthouse that offers a relaxing retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city.

What to Buy in Blue Bell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home, here are a few things that embody Blue Bell’s local culture:

  • Cheese Steaks. Bring home some of the famous Philly cheese steaks from one of the local restaurants.
  • Pretzels. Pack up some locally made Miller’s Twist pretzels to snack on during your trip home.

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Bell

Blue Bell is a friendly, welcoming community with a strong sense of history. The culture here is diverse, and the people are proud of their Pennsylvania heritage. Visitors can take a walk downtown and see locals strolling with their families while grabbing coffee or running errands. The local attitude is cheerful, and you’ll find everyone you meet to be helpful and courteous.

Best Time to Visit Blue Bell

The ideal time to visit Blue Bell is between March and November. In winter, it can get quite cold, and snowstorms can be frequent, making it challenging to enjoy outdoor activities fully.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Bell

Do:

  • Enjoy the local cuisine, including the Philly cheese steak and pretzels.
  • Take advantage of the beautiful surroundings by hiking, biking, or horseback riding.
  • Be respectful of the locals and their culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Be loud and disruptive in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blue Bell

Blue Bell is a safe place, with a low crime rate. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings, just as they would in any unfamiliar area.

Health in Blue Bell

The local healthcare system is excellent, with several highly regarded hospitals and clinics in the area.

Getting to Blue Bell: Transportation Options and Logistics

If you’re arriving in Blue Bell by air, several airports are nearby, including:

Airports Near Blue Bell

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL). This is the closest airport to Blue Bell, located roughly 25 miles from the heart of town.
  • Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR). This airport is approximately 92 miles from Blue Bell, NJ, and offers direct flights to many major international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Blue Bell

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal, Philadelphia. This bus terminal is approximately 25 miles from Blue Bell and offers bus service to many regional and national destinations.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars. Visitors can rent a car from one of the airport rental car companies and drive to Blue Bell.
  • Taxi Services. A taxi offers an alternative to renting a car, and visitors will find plenty of taxi drivers at the airport or the bus station.

How to Get Around in Blue Bell

There are several options for visitors to get around Blue Bell:

  • Renting a car. Renting a car is an option for those who prefer to have their mode of transportation.
  • Uber/Lyft. Both Uber and Lyft offer services in and around Blue Bell.
  • Public Transportation. SEPTA buses are available for visitors who prefer to use public transportation.

Where to Go Next in Blue Bell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have an extra day or two, check out some of these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • New Hope, Pennsylvania. This town is a popular day trip destination, featuring quaint shops, restaurants, and beautiful scenery.
  • Amish Country. Just an hour’s drive away from Blue Bell is the Lancaster County area, where visitors can experience the unique culture of the Amish community.
  • Rehoboth Beach, Delaware. Just two hours south of Blue Bell, visitors can enjoy the beach, boardwalk, and stunning sunsets.

Conclusion

Blue Bell is a wonderful destination that offers a mix of outdoor adventure, local culture, and fascinating history. With so much to see and do, visitors will find themselves immersed in the local culture and enjoying the charm of this great Pennsylvania township.
