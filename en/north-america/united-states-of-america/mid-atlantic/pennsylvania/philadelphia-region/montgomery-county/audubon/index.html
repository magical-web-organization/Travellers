Introduction to Audubon

Nestled in Montgomery County in the Philadelphia Region of Pennsylvania, Audubon is a charming area perfect for those seeking a peaceful time away from the hustle and bustle of the city. This small community is situated in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States and provides plenty of opportunities for visitors to immerse themselves in local history, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. With so much to see and do, this comprehensive travel guide will help you navigate Audubon and plan the perfect visit.

List of Places to Visit in Audubon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • John James Audubon Center - The home of the renowned painter and naturalist, John James Audubon. Visitors can explore the beautiful plantation house, walk the trails, and view original works of art and artifacts.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park - This park was the site of the Continental Army’s winter encampment during the American Revolutionary War. History buffs can tour the reconstructed barracks and explore the grounds.
  • Schuylkill River Trail - An ideal spot for biking, hiking, and walking, the trail offers scenic views of the river and the historic landmarks along the way.
  • Valley Forge Casino Resort - A popular destination for those looking for entertainment, the casino offers table games, slot machines, and live music.

Natural Attractions of Audubon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Evansburg State Park - A tranquil nature reserve with hiking trails, fishing spots, picnicking areas, and sports fields.
  • Betzwood Park - Ideal for families, the park features playgrounds, a ball field, and a historic carousel.
  • Schuylkill River Trail - As mentioned earlier, the trail is perfect for those looking for outdoor activities and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Audubon

  • Perkiomen Trail - A 20-mile trail that passes from Montgomery County into Berks County. The trail is suitable for both hiking and biking and provides scenic views of the countryside.
  • Evansburg State Park Trail System - With over 10 miles of trails, visitors can hike or bike through diverse woodland habitats, mature forests, and open fields.

Best Things to Do in Audubon: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Wine Tour - Visit the charming wineries of Montgomery County with a local guide and taste some of the best wines of the region.
  • Try Your Hand at Fly Fishing - The Perkiomen Creek is a popular destination for fly fishermen. Hire a guide for the day and get in on the action.
  • Take a Segway Tour - Explore the Valley Forge National Historical Park on a Segway for a unique and exhilarating experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Audubon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Philly Cheesesteak - A classic sandwich made of thinly-sliced beef, grilled onions, and melted cheese, served on a hoagie roll.
  • Snapper Soup - A thick and hearty soup made with turtle meat, vegetables, and a dash of sherry.

Local Beverages

  • Pennsylvania Dutch Eggnog - A holiday classic, served with a kick of bourbon or vanilla vodka.
  • Yuengling Beer - America’s oldest brewery, located in nearby Pottsville, offers a variety of beer styles for visitors to sample.

Where to Stay in Audubon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Valley Forge Casino Resort - A full-service hotel with luxurious rooms, a spa, a casino, and several dining options.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Valley Forge/Oaks - A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms, a free hot breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Philadelphia-Valley Forge - A perfect choice for families with spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Audubon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery at Village Arts & Crafts
  • Books on Art and Nature at John James Audubon Center Gift Shop
  • Farm-Fresh Produce at Evansburg Farmer’s Market

Local Culture and Customs of Audubon

Audubon’s culture is deeply rooted in its history, evident in the well-preserved landmarks and traditions. Visitors are urged to respect these customs by treating the local community with kindness and courtesy.

Best Time to Visit Audubon

Audubon experiences distinctly four seasons, with warm summers and cold winters. Spring and fall offer cooler, comfortable temperatures and beautiful foliage. Visitors can plan their trip according to their preferred weather and seasonal activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Audubon

Do:

  • Respect the local community and their customs
  • Practice Leave No Trace principles while exploring the outdoors
  • Partake in local food and drink to experience the culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind in natural areas
  • Disrespect historical landmarks
  • Engage in noisy or disruptive behavior

Safety and Health

Safety in Audubon

Audubon is a safe area to visit, but visitors should take precautions as they would in any destination. It is always recommended to lock valuables away, and to be mindful of your surroundings.

Health in Audubon

Visitors should be cautious of ticks while exploring hiking trails, and wear proper clothing and protection to reduce the possibility of tick bites. Lyme disease is a risk in this area.

Getting to Audubon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Audubon

  • Philadelphia International Airport - The closest major airport, located 30 minutes from Audubon.

Bus Stations Near Audubon

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - located 15 minutes away in Norristown.

Other Options

  • Car rental agencies are available at the Philadelphia International Airport for the duration of your stay.

How to Get Around in Audubon

Navigating Audubon is a breeze with your own vehicle or a rented car. However, visitors can also use public transportation through services like SEPTA or taxis to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Audubon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • King of Prussia - A major shopping mall with over 400 stores and restaurants. Just 15 minutes from Audubon.
  • Philadelphia - Known as the “City of Brotherly Love,” Philadelphia is just 30 minutes away and offers a range of historic landmarks, cultural attractions, and dining and shopping options.
  • Hershey - Located just two hours away, visitors can indulge in all things chocolate at Hershey’s Chocolate World.

Conclusion

Audubon offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation that is sure to captivate visitors from afar. Whether you’re interested in exploring nature, learning about American history or indulging in the local food and drink scene, Audubon has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide as a starting point and design your perfect getaway to Audubon, Pennsylvania.
