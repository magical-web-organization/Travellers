Introduction to Northwest

Northwest Philadelphia is a culturally rich and scenic region in Philadelphia with a vibrant food scene, rich history and heritage, and numerous outdoor recreation opportunities. Nestled in the heart of Philadelphia Region, Northwest is also a perfect base for exploring other nearby attractions. This travel guide will help you plan your stay in Northwest and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest

Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Northwest:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Johnson House Historic Site: A historic house museum and a documented station on the Underground Railroad, the Johnson House is a must-see attraction for history buffs.
  • Cliveden: A National Historic Landmark built in 1767. It was the site of a Revolutionary War battle and also served as a stop on the Underground Railroad.
  • Wyck Historic House, Garden, and Farm: This National Historic Landmark is a slice of American history from colonial to modern era, featuring 300-plus years of continuous patronage by one family.

Natural Attractions of Northwest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wissahickon Valley Park: Known for its hiking trails, Wissahickon Valley Park is a perfect place to explore the beauty of nature in Northwest.
  • Valley Green Inn: This historic restaurant, located in the heart of the park, offers stunning views of the Wissahickon Creek.
  • Fairmount Park: With over 9,200 acres of land, Fairmount Park is one of the largest urban parks in the United States. It offers numerous outdoor recreation options such as hiking, biking, golfing, and much more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest

Northwest is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in the Philadelphia region. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Forbidden Drive: A 7.5-mile trail running along the Wissahickon Creek, Forbidden Drive is a car-free road perfect for hiking, biking, or running.
  • Schuylkill River Trail: Running for over 140 miles, Schuylkill River Trail is a multi-use trail that offers stunning views of the Schuylkill River and connects to other trails in the region.

Best Things to Do in Northwest: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Northwest has a lot of other activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Philly Tour Hub: They offer a range of tours, from history and culture to food and beer, that are perfect for exploring the city and its neighborhoods.
  • Treetop Quest Philly: Go on an aerial adventure at Treetop Quest Philly, an obstacle course in Wissahickon Park that features zip lines, suspended bridges, and more.
  • Ambler Theater: This historic theater in nearby Ambler shows indie and foreign films and is a must-visit attraction for movie lovers.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest

Northwest has a diverse food scene that offers something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteaks: Philly’s favorite sandwich, the cheesesteak is a must-try when in Northwest. Try it at Geno’s or Pat’s.
  • Soft Pretzels: A Philly staple, soft pretzels are sold in many street stands and are perfect for snacking.
  • Hoagies: Often called the Philly sandwich, hoagies are made with cold cuts, cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: The oldest brewery in the United States, Yuengling Beer offers a range of brews that are popular in Philadelphia and beyond.
  • Philadelphian: This unique cocktail is made with green tea vodka and honey from local bees.

Where to Stay in Northwest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northwest offers various accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Chestnut Hill Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Chestnut Hill, which offers luxury rooms and suites.
  • Manayunk Chambers Guest House: A boutique guesthouse that combines modern amenities with old-world charm.
  • Apple Hostels of Philadelphia: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Philadelphia that offers private rooms, dorms, and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Northwest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some things to buy in Northwest as souvenirs or gifts:

  • Mason Jars from Jar Goods: These unique jars are handcrafted in Philadelphia and come in various designs and sizes.
  • Locally Made Cheese from the Cheese Shop of Philadelphia: The Cheese Shop of Philadelphia offers a variety of locally made cheeses that are perfect for snacking or cooking.
  • Souvenirs from the Barnes Foundation: The Barnes Foundation’s gift shop offers a range of souvenirs, from art books to jewelry, that feature the iconic collection.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest

Northwest is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood with a strong sense of community. While visiting, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs, such as participating in community events and supporting local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Northwest

The best time to visit Northwest is from April to June or September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant. July and August can be hot and humid, while December to February can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Northwest:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoor recreation opportunities in the Wissahickon Valley Park and Fairmount Park
  • Visit the historic house museums and learn about the region’s rich history and heritage
  • Try the local food and drink scene, including cheesesteaks, soft pretzels, and Yuengling beer
  • Support local businesses and participate in community events

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Overspend on tourist traps instead of supporting local businesses

Safety and Health

While Northwest is a safe and healthy place to visit, it’s important to take certain precautions to ensure your safety and well-being.

Safety in Northwest

  • Stick to well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings, and keep valuables out of sight
  • Use reliable transportation services and avoid unlicensed cabs
  • Follow traffic rules when crossing the street or biking

Health in Northwest

  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when exploring the outdoors
  • Use insect repellent and check for ticks if hiking or biking in wooded areas
  • Wash your hands regularly and follow COVID-19 guidelines set by local authorities

Getting to Northwest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northwest is easily accessible by various transportation options. Here are some of the ways to get there:

Airports Near Northwest

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located 20 miles from Northwest, Philadelphia International Airport is the closest airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Northwest

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Center City, Greyhound Bus Lines offers services to cities across the United States, including Northwest.
  • Megabus: MegaBus offers affordable bus services between Northwest and several East Coast cities.

Other Options

  • SEPTA Regional Rail: SEPTA Regional Rail provides train services between Center City and different suburbs of Philadelphia, including Northwest.
  • Uber/Lyft: Uber and Lyft are popular ride-hailing services in Philadelphia and offer services to Northwest.

How to Get Around in Northwest

Northwest has an excellent public transportation system, but it’s also easy to explore the region on foot or by bike. Here are some of the ways to get around:

  • SEPTA Regional Rail: SEPTA Regional Rail provides train services between Center City and different suburbs of Philadelphia, including Northwest.
  • SEPTA Bus: SEPTA bus services are available throughout Northwest, making it easy to get around the neighborhood.
  • Biking: With its beautiful parks and trails, biking is a popular way to explore Northwest. Rent bikes from local shops like Cadence Cycling & Multisport Center or Manayunk Cycles.

Where to Go Next from Northwest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northwest is a great base for exploring other nearby attractions. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can take:

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: Located 25 miles northwest of Philadelphia, Valley Forge National Historical Park is a must-visit destination for history buffs.
  • Longwood Gardens: Located 40 miles southwest of Northwest, Longwood Gardens features over 1,000 acres of stunning gardens and landscapes.
  • New Hope: Located in nearby Bucks County, New Hope is a charming town known for its art galleries, boutique shops, and historic sites.

Conclusion

Northwest Philadelphia is a diverse and exciting region that offers something for every traveler. From its vibrant food scene to its rich history and natural beauty, there is no shortage of things to see and do in Northwest. Explore the region’s museums and historical places, hike or bike its scenic trails, and indulge in its delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re visiting for a weekend or a week, Northwest is sure to captivate and charm you with its unique culture and heritage.
