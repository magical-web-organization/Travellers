Introduction to Old City

Old City is a historic neighborhood in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, famous for its cobblestone streets, red-brick rowhouses, and significant role in the United States’ founding history. Located in the heart of Philadelphia’s Center City, Old City is one of the most visited areas in the city, attracting millions of tourists and history enthusiasts every year.

List of Places to Visit in Old City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old City boasts a rich cultural heritage and historical significance that can be seen in the many museums and historical landmarks scattered throughout the neighborhood. Here are some of the must-visit museums and attractions in Old City:

  • Independence National Historical Park: A sprawling complex of historical buildings, including Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell.
  • National Constitution Center: An interactive museum dedicated to the history of the US Constitution.
  • The Museum of the American Revolution: A state-of-the-art museum showcasing the history of the American Revolution and its impact on the country’s founding.
  • Independence Seaport Museum: A maritime museum featuring an array of ship models, interactive exhibits, and boat rides on the Delaware River.

Natural Attractions of Old City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Old City is renowned for its historical significance, it also offers a chance to experience the city’s natural beauty. Here are some of the natural attractions in Old City:

  • Race Street Pier: A scenic spot on the Delaware River to enjoy river views, picnics, and concerts.
  • Penn’s Landing: A waterfront promenade featuring a range of outdoor attractions including a skating rink, a seasonal park, and outdoor events throughout the year.
  • Franklin Square: A historic park in downtown Philadelphia, featuring miniature golf, carousel rides, and a playground for children.
  • Washington Square Park: A serene, tree-lined park that was once a burial ground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old City

Old City boasts several exciting hiking and biking trails that offer an unforgettable experience of the city’s lively atmosphere.

  • Delaware River Trail: A 20-mile trail for pedestrians, cyclists, and runners, that runs along the Delaware River.
  • Schuylkill River Trail: A trail that follows the Schuylkill River and connects to other regional trails, including the Schuylkill River Trail South and Schuylkill River Trail North.
  • Old City Food and History Tour: A culinary adventure on foot that takes visitors through the neighborhood’s best restaurants, bars, and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Old City: Activities and Tours

Old City offers a variety of things to do, including exploring museums, taking a stroll through the park, and visiting historical landmarks. Some of the top activities and tours in Old City include:

  • Old City Ghost Tour: A guided tour of the neighborhood, exploring the spooky history and legends surrounding the area.
  • Old City Art Walk: A self-guided tour highlighting the public art and murals found throughout the neighborhood.
  • The Best of Philly Food Tour: A tour taking visitors through Philadelphia’s food landscape, including stops at popular restaurants and iconic landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Old City

For visitors planning a week-long stay in Old City, here is a suggested itinerary that highlights the best of Old City:

Day Activity
1 Explore Independence National Historical Park, including Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell.
2 Visit the National Constitution Center and take a Segway tour of the city.
3 Take a stroll through Penn’s Landing and Race Street Pier, then head to the Spruce Street Harbor Park for a bite to eat and a drink by the river.
4 Spend a day exploring the Old City art scene, including visits to galleries and public art installations.
5 Visit the Museum of the American Revolution, then take a tour of the city’s top food destinations.
6 Take a hike on the Schuylkill River Trail and visit Washington Square Park.
7 Visit the Independence Seaport Museum, then end the week with a trip to Franklin Square for mini-golf and lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Old City

Old City offers a fantastic culinary experience, reflecting the city’s diverse immigrant heritage and modern restaurant scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Philly Cheesesteak: A classic sandwich made with thinly sliced beefsteak and melted cheese, served on a hoagie roll.
  • Soft Pretzels: A knotted, salted dough shaped like a pretzel, often served warm with mustard or cheese sauce.
  • Tomato Pie: A savory pastry topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: A locally brewed beer, famous for its rich, malty flavor.
  • Root Beer: A non-alcoholic soft drink made from sassafras roots.

Where to Stay in Old City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old City has several accommodation options for visitors looking to stay in the neighborhood. Visitors can choose from historic boutique hotels, modern hotels, and budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Kimpton Hotel Monaco: A well-appointed boutique hotel with an on-site restaurant and fitness center.
  • The Franklin Hotel at Independence Park: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Old City, with a farm-to-table restaurant, fitness center, and spa.
  • Apple Hostels: A budget-friendly hostel offering dorm-style rooms and private rooms with shared bathrooms.

What to Buy in Old City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Old City is a shopper’s paradise, with a range of shops and boutiques offering unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Liberty Bell memorabilia: Items such as keychains, magnets, and t-shirts featuring the iconic Liberty Bell.
  • Handmade Jewelry: A range of handmade jewelry pieces featuring local gemstones, such as garnets and amethysts.
  • Artwork and Prints: A range of local artists’ works, including paintings, photographs, and prints of local landmarks.

Local Culture and Customs of Old City

Old City is a diverse neighborhood that celebrates multiple cultural traditions and customs. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and follow common customs, such as:

  • Greeting locals with a smile and a firm handshake.
  • Dressing conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Tipping between 15% to 20% at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Old City

The best time to visit Old City is from April to June and September to early November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are less intense. July and August are the busiest months, with high temperatures and more extensive crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old City

Do:

  • Explore Independence National Historical Park, including Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell.
  • Sample local snacks and dishes like the Philly Cheesesteak and Soft Pretzels.
  • Visit local stores and boutiques selling unique souvenirs and local products.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places or parks.
  • Ignore local traffic laws while cycling or driving.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old City

Old City is generally safe for visitors, but like any urban neighborhood, it’s advisable to maintain basic safety precautions such as:

  • Avoiding deserted or dark alleyways or areas.
  • Not carrying large sums of cash or valuables when traveling.
  • Using a ride-hailing app or reserving a taxi when traveling at night.

Health in Old City

Visitors should take basic health precautions in Old City, including:

  • Drinking bottled water to avoid tap water contamination.
  • Seeking medical care if symptoms of illness develop.
  • Using insect repellent to prevent insect bites.

Getting to Old City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Old City is easily accessible by several means of transportation, with several airports, train stations, and bus terminals located nearby. Here are some of the top options:

Airports Near Old City

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located approximately 20 minutes from Old City.

Bus Stations Near Old City

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located approximately 5 minutes from Old City.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station: Located approximately 10 minutes from Old City.

How to Get Around in Old City

Old City is a compact neighborhood, and most attractions are located within walking distance of one another. Visitors can also rent bikes or take the public transportation system, SEPTA, to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Old City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Old City is a gateway to other destinations in the Philadelphia region. Some of the nearby locations include:

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: A scenic park and historic site that played a vital role in the American Revolution.
  • Camden Waterfront: A waterfront area with attractions such as the Adventure Aquarium and the Battleship New Jersey.
  • Reading Terminal Market: A bustling indoor food market offering a range of local and international cuisines.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an art lover, Old City, Philadelphia, is a fascinating neighborhood that offers a diverse range of attractions and activities for visitors. So get ready to immerse yourself in the area’s rich cultural heritage, savor its delicious cuisine, and experience the best of Philadelphia in Old City.
