Introduction to South

South, located in the Philadelphia region of Pennsylvania, is a vibrant place with a rich history, scenic beauty, and delicious food. Beyond the tourist attractions, the region is home to world-class museums, outdoor adventures, and cultural events. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, a foodie, or an art lover, there’s something for everyone in South. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit places and things to do in South.

List of Places to Visit in South

Here are some of the top places to visit in South:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Liberty Bell: This iconic symbol of American independence and freedom is located at Independence National Historical Park in Philadelphia.
  • National Constitution Center: Learn about the history behind American democracy and the U.S. Constitution.
  • Philadelphia Museum of Art: One of the largest art museums in the country.
  • The Barnes Foundation: Home to some of the most important art collections in the world.
  • Eastern State Penitentiary: An infamous prison that housed some of the most notorious criminals in the country.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: Explore the history of the American Revolution and enjoy the scenic beauty of the park.
  • John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge: A natural oasis in the bustling city of Philadelphia.
  • Wissahickon Valley Park: A beautiful park with trails for hiking, biking, and picnicking.
  • Fairmount Park: The largest urban park system in the country, it offers endless opportunities for outdoor recreation.
  • Delaware River Waterfront: A beautiful waterfront that offers stunning views of the Delaware River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

South offers an abundance of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best ones to try:

  • Schuylkill River Trail: A 30-mile multi-use trail that runs along the Schuylkill River.
  • Forbidden Drive: A beautiful hiking and biking trail that passes through the Wissahickon Valley Park.
  • John Heinz National Wildlife Refuge Trail: A scenic trail that passes through the marshes of the wildlife refuge.
  • Valley Forge National Historic Park Trail: A 19-mile trail that takes you through historic sites and beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Tour of Philadelphia: Explore the spooky side of Philadelphia on a walking tour with Ghost Tours of Philadelphia.
  • Segway Tour of the City: Buckle up and get ready to discover the city from a different perspective.
  • Mural Arts Philadelphia Tour: Discover the world’s largest outdoor art gallery with a tour of Mural Arts Philadelphia.
  • Visit the Reading Terminal Market: A must-visit for foodies to experience Philly’s local cuisine.
  • Visit the Pennsylvania Amish Country: Take a scenic drive through the picturesque countryside and learn about the Amish lifestyle.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for South:

  • Day 1-3: Explore Philadelphia’s historical sites and museums, including Independence Hall, the Liberty Bell, and the National Constitution Center.
  • Day 4: Visit Valley Forge National Historical Park.
  • Day 5: Take a bike tour of the city or visit Reading Terminal Market.
  • Day 6: Hike in Wissahickon Valley Park or visit the Pennsylvania Amish Country.
  • Day 7: Take a scenic drive along the Delaware River Waterfront.

Food and Drink Scene of South

South, Philadelphia’s vibrant food and drink scene is something that you can’t miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Philly Cheesesteak: This iconic sandwich is made with thinly sliced ribeye steak, sautéed onions, melted cheese, and served on a hoagie roll. Jim’s Steaks and Pat’s King of Steaks are some of the best places to try it.
  • Soft Pretzels: A staple snack in Philly, the soft pretzel is usually salted and served hot with mustard or cheese sauce. Try some at Miller’s Twist or Center City Pretzel Company.
  • Roast Pork Sandwich: Roast pork is slow-cooked to perfection and served with broccoli rabe and sharp provolone on a hoagie roll at DiNic’s.
  • Tastykake: A beloved snack cake made in Philly since 1914. Try flavors like Butterscotch Krimpets and Peanut Butter Kandy Kakes at the Tastykake Factory Outlet.

Local Beverages

  • Yards Brewing Company: A local brewery that offers year-round beers and seasonal brews with a focus on quality and sustainability.
  • La Colombe Coffee: A Philly-born coffee roaster that serves up delicious coffee drinks and pastries.
  • Reading Terminal Market: A great place to try local beverages like root beer from the Franklin Fountain or fresh fruit juice from Lancaster County Dairy.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in South, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.

  • The Logan: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Philadelphia’s Logan Square.
  • Apple Hostels of Philadelphia: A budget-friendly hostel located in the Old City of Philadelphia.
  • The Independent Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the Midtown Village neighborhood of Philadelphia.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Soft Pretzels: Bring home a pack of Philly’s famous soft pretzels as a delicious souvenir.
  • Reading Terminal Market: A great place to buy local products like handmade soap from Okwuma Originals or Pennsylvania Dutch treats from Dutch Eating Place.
  • Amish Country: Buy handmade quilts and baskets from the Amish.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Philadelphia has a rich history and diverse culture. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Tipping: It’s customary to tip at least 15-20% in restaurants and bars.
  • History: Philadelphia played a significant role in the American Revolution, so be sure to visit historical sites like Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell.
  • Sports: Philly fans are known for their die-hard passion for sports, so don’t be surprised to see people sporting Eagles or Phillies gear.
  • Street Art: Philadelphia is known for its vibrant street art scene, with murals and installations that showcase the city’s creativity and culture.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is during spring and fall when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy. The region also hosts numerous festivals and events throughout the year, including the Philadelphia Flower Show in March and the Philadelphia Marathon in November.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Explore the city’s historical sites and museums
  • Try Philly’s famous cheesesteak and soft pretzels
  • Experience the city’s vibrant street art scene
  • Take in the local sports culture
  • Visit the nearby Amish Country

Don’t:

  • Purchase counterfeit goods and knock-off souvenirs
  • Litter or disrespect the natural beauty of the parks
  • Overlook the diversity of the city and its people
  • Be afraid to ask locals for suggestions and recommendations

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South has a relatively low crime rate, but it’s always a good idea to stay vigilant and aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Use common sense, avoid dark alleys and deserted areas, and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in South

Philly has plenty of hospitals and medical centers to ensure quality care in case of any emergency. Be sure to have adequate health insurance coverage and carry important medical information with you, including a list of any allergies or medications you take.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

South is easily accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near South

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): Located about 20 minutes from downtown Philly, PHL offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near South

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Philadelphia, the Greyhound Bus Station offers regional and long-distance trips.

Other Options

  • SEPTA: The Southeastern Pennsylvania Transportation Authority offers public transportation options including buses, trains, and trolleys, making it easy to get around the region.

How to Get Around in South

South is a walkable and bike-friendly city, with plenty of opportunities for biking tours or renting bikes. SEPTA also offers an extensive network of buses, trains, and trolleys, making it easy to get around the region. Uber and Lyft are also widely available.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider after exploring South.

  • Hershey and Lancaster, Pennsylvania: A two-hour drive from Philly will take you to these two popular destinations, famous for chocolate and pretzels, respectively.
  • New York City: A two-hour train ride away from Philly, this bustling metropolis is filled with famous landmarks, restaurants, and museums.
  • Washington D.C.: A two-and-a-half-hour drive from Philly, the capital city has it all, from world-class museums to historical monuments.

Conclusion

Whether you’re planning a quick weekend getaway or an extensive exploration, South, Philadelphia region, has something to offer everyone. From history and culture to food and outdoor adventures, this region has it all. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect itinerary, and get ready for an unforgettable experience in South.
