Introduction to Titusville

Titusville, Pennsylvania is a small city located in the western part of the state, in the Erie Region of the Mid-Atlantic United States. It is known for its oil industry history and is home to many historic landmarks and outdoor activities. With its charming downtown, beautiful parks and trails, and abundant natural beauty, Titusville is a must-see destination for any traveler looking to explore the region.

List of Places to Visit in Titusville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Titusville has many historically significant sites and museums worth visiting.

  • Drake Well Museum and Park
  • Titusville Historical Society and Brown Mansion
  • Oil Creek and Titusville Railroad
  • Venango Museum of Art, Science, and Industry
  • Tarbell House

Natural Attractions of Titusville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Titusville has an abundance of outdoor activities and picturesque scenery.

  • Oil Creek State Park
  • Cook Forest State Park
  • Two Mile Run County Park: Beach, camping, hiking trails, fishing, and boating
  • Goddard State Park: 224-acre lake, boating, fishing, camping, hiking, and swimming
  • Titusville Wetlands Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Titusville

Exploring Titusville’s breathtaking natural beauty is easy with the city’s numerous hiking and biking trails. Here are some excellent routes to explore:

  • Queen City Trail: 4-mile paved rail-trail
  • Oil Creek State Park Trail: 9.7-mile trail for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing
  • Samuel Justus Recreation Trail: 13.5-mile trail for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing
  • Two Mile Run County Park Trails: Multiple trails for hiking, biking, and running

Best Things to Do in Titusville: Activities and Tours

There’s plenty to do and see in Titusville, from exploring the outdoors to learning about the region’s rich history. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a ride on the Oil Creek and Titusville Railroad
  • Rent a boat or canoe and explore the beautiful lakes in the area
  • Visit the Drake Well Museum and Park, the home of the world’s first oil well
  • Take a guided tour of Titusville and learn about its history
  • Go camping, hiking, fishing, and boating at one of the many state parks near Titusville

Week-Long Itinerary for Titusville

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Titusville.

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Drake Well Museum and Park, explore the town, have dinner at the Blue Canoe Brewery
Day 2 Take a ride on the Oil Creek and Titusville Railroad, hike in Oil Creek State Park
Day 3 Spend the day at Two Mile Run County Park, have a picnic dinner
Day 4 Visit the Titusville Wetlands Park, take a nature walk, have lunch at the Nest
Day 5 Go on a guided tour of Titusville and learn about its history, visit the Venango Museum of Art, Science, and Industry
Day 6 Rent a boat or canoe and explore the beautiful lakes in the area, have lunch at Franklin Fine Dining
Day 7 Spend the day at Goddard State Park, have a picnic dinner, and watch the sunset

Food and Drink Scene of Titusville

Must-Try Dishes

Titusville offers a variety of dining options catering to every taste. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Pulled Pork Sandwich at the Blue Canoe Brewery
  • Fried Pickles at the Nest
  • The Red Brick Burger at the Franklin Fine Dining

Local Beverages

The local craft brewery and winery have fantastic offerings. Make sure to try:

  • Blue Canoe Brewery beer
  • Full Circle Winery wine

Where to Stay in Titusville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of lodging options in Titusville, ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious. Here are our top recommendations:

  • The Central Hotel and Conference Center: Historic hotel with modern amenities located in the heart of Titusville
  • Deer Creek Motel: Budget-friendly motel located near Two Mile Run County Park
  • Days Inn: Affordable hotel located near Oil Creek State Park

What to Buy in Titusville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs, Titusville offers a variety of shops and stores. Some of the best places to buy local products include:

  • Titusville Market Square: Local produce, crafts, and gifts
  • Acorn Acres: Wine and local artisanal products
  • O.C. Soaps: Handmade soaps and bath products

Local Culture and Customs of Titusville

Titusville has a rich history and its residents are proud of it. It’s essential to respect the city’s heritage and participate in local activities such as parades, historical tours, and community events.

Best Time to Visit Titusville

The best time to visit Titusville is from May through October, with warm temperatures and many outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Titusville

Do:

  • Take a trip on the Oil Creek and Titusville Railroad
  • Explore the area’s natural beauty
  • Try local food and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter in natural areas
  • Ignore the city’s rich history

Safety and Health

Safety in Titusville

Titusville is a safe place to visit, but it’s essential to take typical safety precautions, like locking your car and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Titusville

There are no specific health concerns when visiting Titusville. However, it is always wise to be up-to-date with your vaccinations and carry any necessary medications.

Getting to Titusville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Titusville is easy with the following transportation options:

Airports Near Titusville

  • Erie International Airport (ERI)
  • Venango Regional Airport (FKL)

Bus Stations Near Titusville

  • Greyhound station in Franklin - 10 miles from Titusville

Other Options

  • Driving: Titusville is easily accessible by car, and major highways run through the area.
  • Amtrak: The closest train station is in Erie, PA.

How to Get Around in Titusville

Titusville is a small town and easy to navigate by car, bike, or foot. Additionally, many trails are available for hiking and biking.

Where to Go Next from Titusville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Titusville, be sure to add these nearby destinations to your itinerary:

  • Erie, Pennsylvania: Visit Presque Isle State Park, the Erie Maritime Museum, and LECOM Park for minor league baseball.
  • Allegheny National Forest: Enjoy bike trails, camping, and fishing in the vast natural beauty of the forest.

Conclusion

Titusville is a charming small town with a rich history and natural beauty that offers many opportunities for exploration and new experiences. Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring historic sites, or just enjoying the town’s many great restaurants, Titusville is well worth a visit.
