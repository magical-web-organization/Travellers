Introduction to Franklin (Venango County)

Franklin, located in Venango County, Pennsylvania, is a picturesque city with a rich culture and history. It is situated in the Erie Region of the state, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and world-class museums, Franklin attracts visitors from near and far. In this guide, we will cover the top attractions, must-try food and drink, where to stay, local culture, safety tips, and transportation options for Franklin.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin (Venango County)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Franklin boasts of a diverse range of museums and historical sites that are home to some of the most intriguing collections. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Venango Museum of Art, Science, and Industry: This world-class museum showcases the rich history and culture of Venango County, and its fascinating technology, art, and science. It is the perfect place to learn about the area’s heritage and significance.
  • DeBence Antique Music World: This unique museum showcases a vast collection of antique music boxes, phonographs, and other musical instruments. It is a must-visit place for music lovers and history buffs.
  • Emlenton Mill: This historic mill dates back to 1839 and is a testament to Pennsylvania’s industrial past and its contributions to the milling industry.

Natural Attractions of Franklin (Venango County): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Franklin is blessed with beautiful natural surroundings, offering visitors breathtaking views and outdoor adventures. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Allegheny River Trail: This scenic trail stretches over 30 miles and offers visitors an opportunity to walk, bike or run through the beautiful countryside of Franklin. The trail connects to other famous trails in the region, making it a favorite among outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Oil Creek State Park: This historical park offers visitors a glimpse into the region’s oil industry and rich history. It also boasts of outdoor adventures such as hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • Rocky Grove Sunken Garden: This beautiful garden is a hidden gem in Franklin and features an intricate design of interlocking gardens and pathways that interconnect.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin (Venango County)

Franklin has plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors an opportunity to soak in the beautiful scenery and explore the natural surroundings. Some of the top trails include:

  • Allegheny River Trail: A 30-mile trail for hiking, biking, and running enthusiasts.
  • Dismal Swamp Trail: A 7-mile trail that takes visitors through the swamp and offers beautiful views of the surrounding nature.
  • Presque Isle State Park: A scenic park with miles of biking and hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in Franklin (Venango County): Activities and Tours

Apart from the outdoor attractions mentioned above, Franklin also has some exciting things to do for visitors. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Franklin Art Scene: Take a tour of the art galleries and studios in Franklin, where visitors can explore the local art scene and purchase unique pieces.
  • Historic Walking Tour: Take a self-guided tour of Franklin’s historic district, where visitors can learn about the city’s rich history and diverse architecture.
  • Nostalgia TV Museum: This museum offers visitors a unique glimpse into television technology’s earliest days and the golden age of TV.

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin (Venango County)

If you’re planning to spend a week in Franklin, here’s an itinerary that covers the top attractions and activities:

  1. Day 1: Visit Venango Museum of Art, Science, and Industry, and Emlenton Mill, followed by lunch at The Franklin.

  2. Day 2: Visit DeBence Antique Music World, followed by a scenic drive on Oil City Memorial Bridge.

  3. Day 3: Take a hike on Allegheny River Trail, visit the beautiful Rocky Grove Sunken Garden, and enjoy dinner at Lago Winery.

  4. Day 4: Visit a nearby site such as Presque Isle State Park or the city of Erie.

  5. Day 5: Explore the Franklin Art Scene, take a tour of the historic district, and enjoy lunch at Trails to Ales Brewery.

  6. Day 6: Visit the Nostalgia TV Museum, take an afternoon stroll in Two Mile Run County Park, and have dinner at Venango General Store and Cafe.

  7. Day 7: Explore more of Franklin’s outdoor attractions, such as Oil Creek State Park, and end your trip with a sunset tour of the area’s wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin (Venango County)

Must-Try Dishes

Franklin has a diverse food scene that features traditional American cuisine along with local delights. Some of the must-try dishes in the area are:

  • Venango Burger: A juicy burger made with locally sourced beef, topped with bacon, cheddar cheese, and honey mustard sauce
  • PA Dutch Chicken Pot Pie: A hearty dish made with chicken, potatoes, and egg noodles in a savory broth.
  • Fried Perch: Freshly caught perch, lightly coated in breadcrumbs, and fried till crispy.

Local Beverages

Franklin is known for its wineries and craft breweries. Visitors can enjoy a variety of local beers and wines, including:

  • Trail to Ales Brewery: A popular brewery that offers a wide selection of beers, including IPAs, ales, and stouts.
  • Lago Winery: A scenic winery that offers visitors a chance to taste some of Pennsylvania’s finest wines while overlooking the beautiful lake.

Where to Stay in Franklin (Venango County): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Franklin offers visitors a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in the area include:

  • Quality Inn and Conference Center: A modern hotel with spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.
  • The Belles Lettres Club: A unique guesthouse located in a historic building that offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful courtyard.
  • The Applebutter Inn Bed & Breakfast: A cozy and charming bed and breakfast with beautifully decorated rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Franklin (Venango County): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Franklin can bring home some unique local products and souvenirs. Some of the best items to buy include:

  • Antiques and Collectibles: Franklin is known for its antique shops that offer a wide selection of vintage items, including furniture, jewelry, and memorabilia.
  • Local Maple Syrup: Venango County is famous for its maple syrup, and visitors can purchase some delicious homemade syrup from the local farms.
  • Handcrafted Items: Franklin is home to a thriving artisanal community, and visitors can purchase handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles from the local craft shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin (Venango County)

Franklin has a diverse culture influenced by its rich heritage and history. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some of the notable customs and festivals of the area include:

  • Oil Festival: This annual festival celebrates the region’s historic oil industry and features parades, live music, and carnival games.
  • Franklin Blues and BBQ Festival: This festival attracts blues enthusiasts from all over the state with its live music, food, and craft vendors.
  • Christmas in Franklin: This festive event is celebrated in December and features a Christmas market, holiday lighting ceremony, and Christmas cookies.

Best Time to Visit Franklin (Venango County)

The best time to visit Franklin is during the summer months, from June through August when the weather is warm and dry. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports. The fall season, from September through November, is also an excellent time to visit, as the region is known for its beautiful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin (Venango County)

Do:

  • Explore the local art scene and check out the art galleries and studios.
  • Try local dishes, including the Venango Burger and PA Dutch Chicken Pot Pie.
  • Go hiking or biking on one of the scenic trails.
  • Purchase unique souvenirs from the local shops.
  • Visit some of the local wineries and breweries for a tasting.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the local customs and culture.
  • Park in areas marked as “No Parking.”
  • Ignore safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin (Venango County)

Franklin is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take standard safety precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid poorly lit areas. Do not leave valuables in plain sight in your car, and be sure to lock your doors when you park. Also, be cautious when engaging in outdoor activities and make sure to follow safety guidelines.

Health in Franklin (Venango County)

There are no specific health concerns in Franklin. Visitors should ensure that they are up to date with their routine vaccinations and carry any medication they may need. If any health-related concerns arise, seek medical attention at the nearest healthcare facility.

Getting to Franklin (Venango County): Transportation Options and Logistics

Franklin is easily accessible from major cities in Pennsylvania, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Franklin (Venango County)

The nearest airport to Franklin is the Venango Regional Airport located within the county.

Bus Stations Near Franklin (Venango County)

Several buses from major cities in Pennsylvania stop in Franklin, including the Greyhound bus service.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Franklin, as it is easily accessible via major highways in Pennsylvania. Car rental services are available at the airports and city centers.

How to Get Around in Franklin (Venango County)

The best way to get around Franklin is by car. Visitors can rent cars at the airport or city centers. Taxis are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Franklin (Venango County): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Franklin is located in the heart of Venango County, making it an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations. Some of the top places to visit in the region include:

  • Erie: A scenic city located on the shores of Lake Erie, famous for its maritime heritage and historic landmarks.
  • Pittsburgh: A vibrant city located about 100 miles from Franklin, known for its rich culture, food, and art scene.
  • Cook Forest State Park: A beautiful park located about 50 miles from Franklin, known for its stunning old-growth forest and scenic hiking trails.
