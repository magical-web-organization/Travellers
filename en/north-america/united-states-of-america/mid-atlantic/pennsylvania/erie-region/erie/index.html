Introduction to Erie

Erie is a city situated in the Erie Region of Pennsylvania, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. It is known for being the fourth most populous city in Pennsylvania, as well as its beautiful and vast lakefront properties. Erie is truly a hidden gem for those who love the outdoors, history, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Erie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Erie Art Museum: This is a must-visit for art lovers as the museum features a vast collection of American ceramics and glass, as well as contemporary art.

  • Presque Isle State Park: This state park features multiple attractions within it, such as the Perry Monument, the Presque Isle Lighthouse, and the Tom Ridge Environmental Center, all worth checking out.

  • Erie County Historical Society Museum: This museum includes the history of Erie County from the arrival of the Native Americans through the modern days of the area.

Natural Attractions of Erie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Presque Isle State Park: The state park is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts as it offers opportunities for a variety of activities like swimming, boating, hiking, and bird-watching. It also has miles of sandy beachfront perfect for sunbathing.

  • Waldameer Park and Water World: Waldameer is an amusement park situated in Presque Isle State Park, it’s easy to get to the park and offers a lot of rides suitable for all ages.

  • Lake Erie Arboretum at Frontier: LEAF is a beautiful park with several walking trails. It features a lot of educational programs and hosts tons of events throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Erie

Erie has a lot of great hiking and biking trails that are well-suited for all levels of experience. Some of the top routes for hikers and bikers include:

  • The trails of Presque Isle State Park: Visitors can explore the park’s scenic beauty while getting a good cardio workout.

  • The Erie to Pittsburgh Trail: This trail is a little more challenging as it spans over 250 miles, but it is worth it. It offers beautiful views of the state’s forests with an excellent opportunity to leave the urban area behind and head to the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Erie: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Erie Maritime Museum: The museum showcases Erie’s maritime history and has an outdoor presentation of the USS Bravo, a submarine launched in 1944.

  • Go on a winery tour through Erie Wine Country: Situated in North East, Pennsylvania, the wineries in the region offer top-rated wines and beautiful vineyards.

  • Visit the Asbury Woods Nature Center: This nature center is a privately funded park located in Millcreek Township. It offers a lot of family-friendly events throughout the year from guided hikes to birding walks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Erie

Day 1: Stroll around the Erie Art Museum, grab lunch, and head to Presque Isle State Park.

Day 2: Hike in the state park, rent canoes, and enjoy the water sports.

Day 3: Visit the historical Erie County Historical Society Museum, and then enjoy a sunset on the beach with a bonfire.

Day 4: Take a winery tour through Erie Wine Country, or head to Asbury Woods Nature Center for a birding walk.

Day 5: Tour the Erie Maritime Museum and get a taste of the submarine life and then head to Waldameer Park and Water World for some amusement rides.

Day 6: Go on a fishing trip to the lake or rent a bike and ride the Erie to Pittsburgh Trail.

Day 7: Spend the day shopping for souvenirs and local products to bring back home and checking out the local bars and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Erie

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smith’s Hot Dogs: This American classic hot dog is a treat for everyone looking for a quick bite to eat.

  • Sponge Candy: Made by the local Pulakos Candy Company, this is a staple Erie candy made from sponge toffee covered in milk chocolate.

  • Fish Fry: The Lake Erie perch and walleye are one of the most sought-after delicacies in Erie and throughout Pennsylvania.

Local Beverages

  • Local Breweries: There are plenty of breweries scattered throughout Erie, making its way for the town to get recognition for it’s brewery boom!

  • Wines: The wineries, again located in North East, Pennsylvania, are home to some of the region’s finest brews.

Where to Stay in Erie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Erie has a host of great accommodation options to suit every budget. Some of the top picks include:

  • Sheraton Erie Bayfront Hotel: A luxurious hotel situated on Presque Isle Bay that boasts beautiful views and amenities.

  • The Avalon Hotel: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of the city and within walking distance to many Erie attractions.

  • Lake Erie Lodge: A modest motel located in the heart of Presque Isle State Park, with easy access to the beach and park activities.

What to Buy in Erie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase a variety of souvenir items or local products while shopping in Erie:

  • Pulakos Sponge Candy: A local candy manufacturer that specializes in this delectable treat.

  • Erie apparel: Visitors can also pick up some Erie-themed apparel or souvenirs from local shops in the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Erie

Erie has an interesting culture that has been shaped by the city’s history and its proximity to Lake Erie. The people of Erie are hospitable, and their culture reflects this. Erie is known for its strong Italian heritage, and visitors can see this reflected in the many churches and local restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Erie

The best time to visit Erie is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny. The fall and winter seasons are still breathtaking, as the city’s landscape is covered in a blanket of snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Erie

Do:

  • Visit Presque Isle State Park and try different outdoor activities on the lake.

  • Visit the Erie Art Museum.

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the fish fry and Smith’s hot dogs

Don’t:

  • Do not over pack expecting the weather will change it can change but it will not go extreme.

  • Try to avoid coming to Erie during winter unless prepared for heavy snow and cold weather

Safety and Health

Safety in Erie

Erie is a relatively safe place to visit. Visitors must take the necessary precautions while traveling, such as keeping their valuables safe, not wandering into strange neighborhoods, and always keeping a form of identification with them.

Health in Erie

Visitors to Erie are urged to use insect repellent when spending time outdoors, especially in the summer months, and to wear a mosquito net if they’re camping overnight. The city has several medical facilities, including UPMC Hamot and St. Vincent Hospital.

Getting to Erie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Erie

Erie International Airport is the closest airport to Erie, PA, only seven miles away. Cleveland Hopkins International Airport and Pittsburgh International Airport are also nearby.

Bus Stations Near Erie

The Greyhound Station is located in the city of Erie, and Megabus offers service to other major cities in the region, such as Cleveland and Pittsburgh.

Other Options

Visitors also have the option to rent a car, take a train, or take a driving tour of the Erie area.

How to Get Around in Erie

The city of Erie offers several transportation options, including driving, riding buses, and using ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Erie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cleveland: Located in Ohio, Cleveland is situated about 100 miles southwest of Erie and is known for its Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

  • Buffalo: Buffalo, NY, is located approximately 100 miles northeast of Erie. Visitors can take a tour of the city’s several historic sites and parks.

Conclusion

Erie is a fantastic city full of rich history, hospitable people, and breathtaking natural scenery. This comprehensive tour guide provides visitors with a detailed look at the city’s hottest attractions, shopping, dining options, and accommodation to help make the most of their Erie experience. Come and experience the Jewel of Lake Erie for yourself!
