Introduction to Hanover

Hanover is a small town located in Central Maryland, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Known for its friendly community, historic landmarks, and beautiful natural scenery, Hanover is a popular destination for both locals and visitors alike. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, and more in Hanover.

List of Places to Visit in Hanover

If you’re planning a trip to Hanover, there are many places worth exploring. Here is a list of our top recommended attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Warehime-Myers Mansion: Built in 1905, this historic mansion is a stunning example of Georgian architecture and is open for tours.
  • Eichelberger Performing Arts Center: This restored 1930s theater hosts a variety of events, including concerts, plays, and movies.
  • The Hanover Area Arts Guild: Featuring the work of local artists, this gallery is a must-visit for art lovers.

Natural Attractions of Hanover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Codorus State Park: A 3,500-acre park with hiking and biking trails, boating, fishing, and camping opportunities.
  • Lake Marburg: A popular spot for fishing, boating, and hiking, Lake Marburg is located within Codorus State Park.
  • The Hanover Trolley Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely walk amidst beautiful natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hanover

For outdoor enthusiasts, Hanover has many great hiking and biking routes with stunning views. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • The Mason-Dixon Trail: A 193-mile trail that runs through six states, including Maryland. The trail offers beautiful views of the countryside and passes through several parks and historic sites.
  • The Gettysburg Battlefield: Just a short drive from Hanover, this historic site offers many hiking and biking trails. Visitors can explore the battlefield and learn about the Civil War through guided tours and exhibits.
  • The Lakes Trail: This 8-mile trail connects Codorus State Park with three other state parks and is perfect for hiking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Hanover: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Hanover, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Ghost Tours of Hanover: This tour takes visitors to some of the town’s most haunted sites, including the Snyder House and Eichelberger Performing Arts Center.
  • Hanover Heritage Walk: A self-guided walking tour that explores the town’s rich history and landmarks.
  • Hanover Farmer’s Market: Open on Saturdays, this market offers a variety of locally grown produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Hanover

Hanover has a diverse food and drink scene with many unique local dishes to try. Here are a few must-try foods and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork BBQ: A regional specialty, Hanover pork BBQ is slow-cooked and seasoned to perfection.
  • Crab Cakes: Made with fresh Maryland blue crab, Hanover’s crab cakes are a culinary delight.
  • Pennsylvania Dutch Pretzels: Made with a unique blend of spices, these pretzels are a delicious snack.

Local Beverages

  • National Bohemian Beer (Natty Boh): A locally brewed beer that’s been around for over 130 years.
  • Susquehanna Rivertowne Brewing: A microbrewery that offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Windy Knoll Farm Market & Creamery: This local farm market offers fresh milk and ice cream made on-site.

Where to Stay in Hanover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Hanover has a variety of accommodations to choose from. Here are some top-rated options:

  • The Hanover Hotel & Banquet Center: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Hanover.
  • The Sheppard Mansion Bed & Breakfast: A beautifully restored mansion offering luxurious rooms and gourmet breakfasts.
  • The Hanover Hostel: A budget-friendly option with shared and private rooms.

What to Buy in Hanover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Hanover has plenty of options. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Hanover Foods: A local food brand offering a variety of canned fruits and vegetables, including their famous “Hanover Green Beans.”
  • Amish Markets: Located throughout the town, these markets offer handmade crafts, baked goods, and local produce.
  • Hanover Clothing: Show your love for Hanover with a t-shirt or hoodie from a local clothing shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Hanover

Hanover is known for its friendly community, rich history, and beautiful natural scenery. The town has a strong Pennsylvania Dutch influence, reflected in its food, language, and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as speaking the Pennsylvania Dutch language when appropriate.

Best Time to Visit Hanover

The best time to visit Hanover is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy. Keep in mind that tourist crowds may be higher during peak travel seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hanover

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Visit the town’s historic landmarks and natural attractions.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • litter or damage natural sites.
  • Disrupt local culture or customs.
  • Ignore safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hanover

Overall, Hanover is a safe town for travelers. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as securing valuables and being aware of surroundings. In case of an emergency, dial 911.

Health in Hanover

Hanover has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and urgent care clinics. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance and contact their provider for any health concerns or emergencies.

Getting to Hanover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hanover

The closest airport to Hanover is Baltimore-Washington International Airport (BWI), which is about 50 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Hanover

The Hanover area is served by Greyhound bus lines.

Other Options

The town also has several car rental and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft.

How to Get Around in Hanover

Hanover is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use car rentals or ride-sharing services for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Hanover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore beyond Hanover, here are a few nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Gettysburg, PA: Known for its Civil War history and battlefield, Gettysburg is just a short drive away from Hanover.
  • Baltimore, MD: About an hour’s drive from Hanover, Baltimore is a vibrant city with plenty of nightlife, shopping and cultural attractions.
  • Washington, DC: A two-hour drive south will take you to the nation’s capital, where you can visit iconic landmarks such as the White House and the Smithsonian museums.

Conclusion

Hanover, Maryland, is a charming town with so much to offer. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventure, or local culture, you’re sure to find something to love. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Hanover.
