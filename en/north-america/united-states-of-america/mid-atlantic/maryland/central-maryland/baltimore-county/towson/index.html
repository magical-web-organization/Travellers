Introduction to Towson

Located in the center of Baltimore County in Central Maryland, Towson is a vibrant suburb that offers a mix of attractions. From natural reserves to cultural landmarks, Towson caters to everyone’s interest. The town is home to Towson University, which adds a youthful vibe to the area.

List of Places to Visit in Towson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hampton National Historic Site: The mansion built in 1790s offers guided tours of the Georgian-style home and expansive grounds.
  2. Towson Courthouse: A popular spot for history buffs, the Towson Courthouse has been the seat of government for more than 150 years.
  3. Fire Museum of Maryland: Experience the history of firefighting with an extensive collection of apparatus, equipment, and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Towson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Loch Raven Reservoir: A serene location for nature walkers, joggers, hikers, and bikers.
  2. Cromwell Valley Park: Walk the trails, picnic on the scenic overlook, and discover hands-on natural history exhibits.
  3. Lake Roland: Experience the beauty of the lake and surrounding woods while hiking, fishing and relaxing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Towson

  1. Torrey C. Brown Rail Trail: This trail stretches 20 miles from Hunt Valley to the Pennsylvania state line, making it a tough but scenic trail to bike or walk.
  2. Loch Raven Reservoir: A diverse range of easy to difficult trails allow hikers and bikers to explore the beauty of Towson with breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Towson: Activities and Tours

  1. Towson Town Center: Shop until you drop at Towson’s premier indoor mall with over 180 shops and eateries.
  2. Maryland State Fairgrounds: This lively spot hosts a variety of events throughout the year, from the Maryland State Fair to holiday markets.
  3. Terrapin Adventures: Have an adrenaline rush with activities such as zip-lining, giant swings, and aerial obstacle courses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Towson

Day Activity
1st Explore the Towson Courthouse and Fire Museum of Maryland

Food and Drink Scene of Towson

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sushi at Minato Sushi Bar
  2. The crab cakes at The Point in Towson
  3. Mussels and craft beer at Charles Village Pub & Patio

Local Beverages

  1. Local microbrews at The Brass Tap, a craft beer pub that ales over 60 kinds of beer on tap
  2. Handcrafted cocktails at The Rec Room
  3. Wine tasting at The Wine Post with over 200 wines from around the world.

Where to Stay in Towson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  2. Towson University Marriott Conference Hotel: A modern hotel that caters to business travelers.
  3. 1840s Carrollton Inn - A boutique hotel located in Baltimore, near Towson with beautifully decorated suites.

Local Culture and Customs of Towson

Towson has a rich local community filled with diverse cultures and traditions. While Maryland is known for its seafood, Towson offers cultural gems, including music, dance, and art venues.

Best Time to Visit Towson

Visit Towson during the spring season(March-May) or fall season(September-November). Temperatures are mild, and the town is bustling with events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Towson

Do:

  1. Explore the outdoors with hiking and biking trails
  2. Visit local museums to learn about Towson’s rich history and culture
  3. Shop and dine at Towson Town Center

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to bring a jacket in case it gets cooler at night.
  2. Don’t litter on scenic trails.
  3. Don’t skip the local seafood and beer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Towson

Towson is a safe suburb, but it’s essential to practice common-sense precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Towson

Towson has several hospitals and healthcare facilities. It’s recommended to take allergy medication during the springtime as allergies can be severe.

Getting to Towson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Towson

  1. Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport
  2. Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport
  3. Dulles International Airport

Bus Stations Near Towson

  1. Greyhound Bus Lines: 7 E Preston St, Baltimore, MD
  2. Megabus: White Marsh Mall, 8200 Perry Hall Blvd, Baltimore, MD

Other Options

  1. Amtrak: Penn Station Baltimore, Baltimore, MD, which is operated nationwide and located about 10 miles south of Towson.
  2. Taxi, Uber or Lyft

How to Get Around in Towson

The easiest way to get around Towson is through biking, walking, or driving a vehicle. The area is served by several local taxi, Uber/Lyft, and shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Towson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. National Aquarium in Baltimore: Located just 20 minutes from Towson in the heart of the Inner Harbor.
  2. Fort McHenry National Monument: Situated on the Patapsco River and 20 minutes drive from Towson
  3. Baltimore Museum of Art: Discover impressive modern and contemporary art pieces from around the world.

Conclusion

Towson is full of attractions for all ages and interests. From natural treasures to historical landmarks, the town’s rich culture, food, and entertainment make it a versatile place to visit.
