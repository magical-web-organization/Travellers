Introduction to Owings Mills

Owings Mills is a suburb in Baltimore County, Central Maryland, in the United States. The city has a rich history dating back to the late 1800s when it was home to farming communities and mills. Today, Owings Mills is a vibrant community that blends modern amenities with historical sites. It is a great place to visit for anyone seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and culinary delights.

List of Places to Visit in Owings Mills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Irvine Nature Center

The Irvine Nature Center is located on a 210-acre property, which includes forests, fields, and wetlands. The center has several indoor and outdoor exhibits, which showcase the local flora and fauna. Visitors can participate in guided hikes, bird-watching, and nature-related workshops.

2. Baltimore and Ohio Railroad Museum

The Baltimore and Ohio Railroad Museum is a national historic landmark located in Owings Mills. The museum tells the story of America’s first railroad, which opened in 1827. Visitors can see antique trains, historic buildings, and railway artifacts.

3. Soldier’s Delight Natural Environmental Area

The Soldier’s Delight Natural Environmental Area is a protected wilderness area that offers scenic hikes and bird-watching opportunities. The area boasts rare species of plants and animals, including the prairie warbler and the Delmarva fox squirrel.

Natural Attractions of Owings Mills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Liberty Reservoir

The Liberty Reservoir is a scenic water body that offers fishing, boating, and hiking opportunities. The reservoir has plenty of picnic spots and is surrounded by walking trails. Visitors can enjoy views of the woodland and waterfowl habitats from the comfort of their boats.

2. Oregon Ridge Park

The Oregon Ridge Park is a 1,000-acre woodland that has hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, playgrounds, and a ski slope in winter. Visitors can explore the park’s natural beauty, including streams and wildlife.

3. Cylburn Arboretum

The Cylburn Arboretum is a 207-acre park that has a historic mansion, gardens, and woodlands. The arboretum has several walking trails, including the popular “Seven Hills Trail,” which winds through the diverse landscapes of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Owings Mills

Owings Mills has several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for exploring the natural beauty of the area. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • The Liberty Reservoir Loop Trail is a 7.4-mile scenic trail that has a moderate level of difficulty. The trail features breathtaking views of the reservoir and the surrounding woodlands.
  • The Soldier’s Delight Trail is a 3.2-mile trail that is perfect for hikers of all skill levels. The trail offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and is known for its wildflowers and wildlife.
  • The Northwest Branch Trail is a 10-mile trail that runs through Owings Mills and connects to other trails in the area. The trail is popular with bikers and offers scenic views of the river and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Owings Mills: Activities and Tours

1. Maryland Wine Tours

Maryland Wine Tours offer guided tours of the state’s best wineries, including those in Owings Mills. Visitors can enjoy the taste of local wines while taking in the picturesque scenery.

2. Escape Room Adventures

Escape Room Adventures is a popular attraction in Owings Mills that offers interactive puzzle-solving challenges. Visitors can choose from a variety of themed rooms, including “CSI,” “Pirates of the Caribbean,” and “The Hangover.”

3. AMC Owings Mills 17

The AMC Owings Mills 17 is a state-of-the-art cinema that offers the latest movie releases. The theater has an IMAX screen and offers luxury seating and a bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Owings Mills

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: A Maryland staple, made with fresh blue crab meat.
  • Bergers Cookies: A local sweet treat made of a German sugar cookie topped with chocolate fudge.
  • Steamed Mussels: A popular seafood dish in Owings Mills, often served with garlic butter or tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Natty Boh: A lager beer that has its origins in Baltimore.
  • Old Bay Bloody Mary: A cocktail made with tomato juice, vodka, and spiced with Old bay seasoning.
  • Black Eyed Susan: A sweet and fruity cocktail made with orange juice, pineapple juice, vodka, and rum.

Local Culture and Customs of Owings Mills

Owings Mills is a diverse community that prides itself on its cultural heritage. Visitors should respect local customs and etiquette, including:

  • Dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Using polite language and tone in interactions with locals
  • Respecting the privacy and property of others.

Best Time to Visit Owings Mills

The best time to visit Owings Mills is from April to June and from September to November, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold with some snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Owings Mills

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette
  • Wear comfortable shoes for exploring the natural attractions
  • Take a Maryland Wine Tour to discover local wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Ignore instructions and guidance from local authorities
  • Purchase souvenirs made from endangered species or illegally harvested materials.

Safety and Health

Safety in Owings Mills

Owings Mills is a safe community for visitors. However, like any urban area, visitors should take precautions in crowded areas and not walk alone at night.

Health in Owings Mills

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in the area, including the Northwest Hospital and the Owings Mills Health Center.
