Introduction to South Baltimore

Located in Baltimore County, Central Maryland, South Baltimore is a diverse and vibrant neighborhood that has something for everyone. Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, this charming area has plenty of attractions to keep you engaged and entertained. South Baltimore is also steeped in history, with a rich cultural heritage that includes museums and monuments.

List of Places to Visit in South Baltimore

If you are planning a trip to South Baltimore, you definitely don’t want to miss these top-rated attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort McHenry National Monument and Historic Shrine: Explore the place where the Star-Spangled Banner was written.
  • Baltimore Museum of Industry: Discover the city’s industrial heritage and traditions.
  • American Visionary Art Museum: Admire the works of visionary artists from around the world.
  • The Maryland Science Center: Enjoy learning about science.

Natural Attractions of South Baltimore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Federal Hill Park: Take a stroll in the park and enjoy panoramic views of the charming city skyline.
  • Middle Branch Park: Enjoy the stunning beauty of nature while kayaking or paddle-boating on the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Baltimore

South Baltimore is home to some of the most scenic biking and hiking trails in the region. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Gwynns Falls Trail: Explore the park’s many trails and enjoy close-up views of local wildlife.
  • Baltimore and Annapolis Trail: Enjoy a scenic ride through the countryside and historic districts of the city.
  • Jones Falls Trail: Take a leisurely walk and discover charming neighborhoods with breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in South Baltimore: Activities and Tours

South Baltimore offers a wide range of fun activities and tours to explore the city’s rich culture and heritage. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Food Tours of South Baltimore: Take a culinary tour of the city and sample the local cuisine.
  • Brewery Tours: Sip and savor local brews on a tour of the city’s breweries.
  • City Sightseeing Tours: Hop on a bus or a boat and explore the city’s highlights.

Food and Drink Scene of South Baltimore

South Baltimore is home to a variety of restaurants, cafes, and bars that offer a diverse range of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Sample the city’s most famous dish made with fresh Maryland crabs.
  • Pit Beef Sandwich: Taste Baltimore’s signature sandwich made with thinly sliced beef, barbecue sauce, and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Natty Boh: The local beer of Baltimore, a light beer with a hint of sweetness.
  • Honest Tea: Refresh with a cold glass of this local brand.

Where to Stay in South Baltimore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Baltimore has a variety of options available for every traveler, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Kimpton Hotel Monaco Baltimore: A luxurious hotel in the heart of the city.
  • Baltimore Inner Harbor Marriott: Located in the city center and offers stunning views of the harbor.
  • HI Baltimore Hostel: A budget-friendly option for solo travelers or groups.

## What to Buy in South Baltimore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Baltimore home with you with these locally made souvenirs:

  • Crab-shaped souvenirs: Cute crab-shaped mugs, plush toys, and keychains.
  • Old Bay Seasoning: A spicy seasoning that’s perfect for seafood and other dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of South Baltimore

Baltimoreans are proud of their city and its heritage, and you’ll see that demonstrated through their commitment to preserving historical sites and traditions. Visitors to Baltimore should be prepared to dive deep into local history and culture by visiting museums, attending cultural events, and learning about the city’s unique past.

Best Time to Visit South Baltimore

The best time to visit South Baltimore is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer can be quite hot and muggy, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Baltimore

Do:

  • Visit historical sites and museums.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially crab cakes.
  • Take a scenic bike ride or hike along one of the trails.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the rules and regulations of local parks and trails.
  • Leave valuables in your parked car.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Baltimore

While South Baltimore is considered to be a relatively safe neighborhood, visitors should still exercise basic safety precautions such as not leaving valuables in their vehicles and being aware of their surroundings while walking or biking.

Health in South Baltimore

Visitors to South Baltimore should be aware of any health risks associated with the area, such as Lyme disease from tick bites during outdoor activities. Check with your doctor before visiting if you have any concerns, and be sure to use insect repellents while outdoors.

Getting to South Baltimore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Baltimore

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: The nearest airport to South Baltimore.
  • Reagan National Airport: Located about an hour away from South Baltimore.

Bus Stations Near South Baltimore

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in the heart of the city.
  • Megabus Station: Located in the city center just a few blocks from the harbor.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station: Located in Baltimore’s Penn Station.

How to Get Around in South Baltimore

Visitors to South Baltimore can use public transportation options such as the Charm City Circulator or the MTA bus system, or rent a bike or car to explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from South Baltimore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time to explore the region, check out these nearby destinations:

  • Annapolis: The state capital of Maryland, known for its beautiful waterfront and historical sites.
  • Washington D.C.: The nation’s capital, only an hour away by car or train.

Conclusion

South Baltimore is a hidden gem in Central Maryland, offering a wealth of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and culinary delights for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re visiting for the first time or returning for another adventure, South Baltimore has something new and exciting to discover around every corner.
