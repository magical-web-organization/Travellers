Introduction to East Baltimore

Located in Baltimore County, in central Maryland, East Baltimore is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood that boasts a rich cultural heritage, a bustling food scene, and a range of exciting outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in exploring the area’s museums and historical attractions, taking in the natural beauty of the region, or sampling some of the local cuisine, East Baltimore has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in East Baltimore

If you’re planning a trip to East Baltimore, you won’t want to miss these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Baltimore Museum of Art is one of the nation’s premier art institutions, and is home to an impressive collection of over 100,000 works spanning a range of mediums and styles.
  • The Reginald F. Lewis Museum of African American History & Culture is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history and contributions of African Americans in Maryland and beyond.
  • Fort McHenry National Monument & Historic Shrine is the spot where Francis Scott Key wrote “The Star-Spangled Banner,” and offers visitors a glimpse into the area’s rich military history.

Natural Attractions of East Baltimore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Patterson Park is a popular spot with locals, and offers a large lake, rolling hills, a swimming pool, tennis courts, and numerous hiking trails.
  • Jones Falls Trail is a scenic route that runs from the Inner Harbor to the north of the city, and offers cyclists and hikers the chance to take in some of the area’s stunning natural beauty.
  • Canton Waterfront Park is a former industrial site that has been transformed into a beautiful park space, complete with a pier and scenic views of the Chesapeake Bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Baltimore

East Baltimore is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several great hiking and biking routes to choose from. Some of the most popular options include:

  • The Gwynns Falls Trail: This 15-mile trail runs through some of Baltimore’s most scenic parks, wilderness areas, and historic sites, making it a great choice for both hikers and cyclists.
  • The Baltimore & Annapolis Trail: This 13.3 mile trail runs from Annapolis to Glen Burnie, and offers stunning views of the area’s rolling countryside.
  • Lake Roland Park Trail: This trail winds through the beautiful Lake Roland Park, and is a great choice for hikers and cyclists of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in East Baltimore: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something to do in East Baltimore, there are plenty of great options to choose from. Here are just a few of our favorites:

  • The Fells Point Ghost Tour: This popular walking tour takes visitors through the streets of East Baltimore, where they’ll learn about the area’s haunted history and hear spine-tingling ghost stories.
  • The Maryland Science Center: This interactive museum is a great choice for families, and offers a range of exhibits and activities designed to spark curiosity and inspire wonder.
  • The Inner Harbor Water Taxi: For a unique way to see the city, hop aboard one of Baltimore’s iconic water taxis and glide through the bustling Inner Harbor.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Baltimore

If you’re planning a week-long trip to East Baltimore, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of East Baltimore

East Baltimore is a foodie’s paradise, with a range of delicious local cuisine on offer. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chesapeake Bay seafood: East Baltimore is famous for its fresh seafood, with crab cakes, steamed blue crabs, and raw oysters all local favorites.
  • Pit Beef: A popular local delicacy, pit beef is a slow-roasted beef that is shaved thin and served on a Kaiser roll with horseradish sauce.
  • Smith Island Cake: A traditional dessert that originated in nearby Smith Island, this rich, multilayered cake is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Natty Boh: Short for National Bohemian, Natty Boh is a crisp, refreshing beer that has been brewed in Baltimore since 1885.
  • Crushes: A favorite summer drink, a crush is made with fresh squeezed orange juice, vodka, and soda water.
  • Old Grand-Dad: Named for legendary whiskey maker Basil Hayden, Old Grand-Dad is a smooth, spicy bourbon that is perfect for sipping on a cold night.

Where to Stay in East Baltimore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, East Baltimore has plenty of great accommodations to choose from. Here are a few highly rated options:

  • The Hotel Revival Baltimore: This trendy, upscale hotel is located in the heart of Mount Vernon, and offers stylish rooms and top-notch amenities.
  • The Ivy Hotel: For a truly luxurious stay, book a room at The Ivy Hotel, a chic, boutique hotel that offers personalized service and elegant accommodations.
  • The Baltimore Hostel: This affordable hostel offers a range of private and dorm-style rooms, and is a great choice for budget travelers.

What to Buy in East Baltimore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave East Baltimore without picking up a few souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Old Bay Seasoning: A classic Maryland seasoning blend, Old Bay is a must-have for anyone interested in cooking local cuisine.
  • Chesapeake Bay Fudge: Made with creamy milk chocolate and bits of real Maryland crab meat, this unique fudge is a delicious and memorable souvenir.
  • Crab Mallets: A practical and fun souvenir, crab mallets are essential for cracking open steamed blue crabs.

Local Culture and Customs of East Baltimore

East Baltimore is a diverse and welcoming community with a rich cultural heritage. As a visitor, it’s important to be respectful of the area’s customs and traditions, and to take the time to learn about the local culture. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Food and Drink: In East Baltimore, food and drink are an important part of the social and cultural fabric, and it’s not uncommon for locals to spend hours lingering over a meal or sharing a drink with friends.
  • Diversity: East Baltimore is home to a diverse range of ethnic and racial communities, and it’s important to be respectful of everyone’s cultural backgrounds and traditions.
  • Hospitality: East Baltimore locals are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming spirit, so be sure to take the time to engage with the people you meet and make new friends along the way.

Best Time to Visit East Baltimore

The best time to visit East Baltimore depends on your interests and priorities. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and festivals, then late spring and early fall are ideal, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of events and activities to enjoy. If you’re more interested in the area’s cultural attractions, then the winter months are a great choice, when crowds are smaller and the museums and galleries are less crowded.

Safety and Health

East Baltimore is generally a safe and welcoming community, but as with any urban area, it’s important to take precautions to stay safe. Here are a few safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in East Baltimore

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: When you’re out exploring East Baltimore, be sure to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Take precautions to avoid petty crime: Leave valuable items in your hotel safe or in a secure location, and don’t leave your bags or belongings unattended in public spaces.
  • Take care when crossing the street: Like many urban areas, East Baltimore can be busy and congested, so always take care when crossing the street and follow traffic signals and signs.

Health in East Baltimore

  • Stay hydrated: During the summer months, temperatures in East Baltimore can soar, so be sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated throughout the day.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: When you’re out exploring, be sure to wear sunscreen and protective clothing to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Seek medical attention if needed: If you’re feeling unwell or have a medical emergency while you’re in East Baltimore, seek medical attention right away.

Getting to East Baltimore: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Baltimore is easily accessible by a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near East Baltimore

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI): Located just 10 miles south of East Baltimore, BWI is a major airport that offers a range of domestic and international flights.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA): Located about 40 miles from East Baltimore, DCA is a convenient option for travelers arriving from other parts of the country.

Bus Stations Near East Baltimore

  • Baltimore Greyhound Bus Terminal: Located in the heart of downtown Baltimore, the Greyhound Bus Terminal is a convenient option for travelers arriving by bus.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: For travelers arriving by train, the Baltimore Penn Station is a major transportation hub that offers a range of Amtrak services.
  • Car Rental: If you prefer to drive, several car rental agencies are available in the area, making it easy to explore East Baltimore on your own.

How to Get Around in East Baltimore

Once you’ve arrived in East Baltimore, there are several great transportation options to help you get around:

  • The Baltimore Water Taxi: A scenic and unique way to see the city, the Baltimore Water Taxi offers a range of routes around the Inner Harbor and other popular destinations.
  • The Charm City Circulator: This free shuttle service runs throughout the city, and is a great choice for getting around East Baltimore quickly and easily.
  • Ride-Sharing Services: Services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in the area and can be a convenient way to get around.

Where to Go Next from East Baltimore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring more of the Mid-Atlantic region, there are plenty of great nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Some popular options include:

  • Washington, D.C.: Just a short drive or train ride away, our nation’s capital is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, or politics.
  • Annapolis, Maryland: Famous for its charming historic district, picturesque harbor, and rich maritime heritage, Annapolis is a wonderful choice for a day trip or weekend getaway.
  • Assateague Island National Seashore: This stunning barrier island is located just a few hours south of East Baltimore, and is home to a range of unique wildlife and natural wonders.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the museums and cultural attractions of East Baltimore, taking in the natural beauty of the region, or sampling some of the area’s delicious local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant and diverse community. With so much to see and do, we hope this guide has helped you plan an unforgettable trip to East Baltimore.
