Introduction to Baltimore

Located in Central Maryland, Baltimore is a bustling port city on the Chesapeake Bay. As the largest city in Maryland, Baltimore is home to a rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural landscapes. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural landmarks, outdoor attractions, or culinary scene, Baltimore has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Baltimore

Baltimore is home to a diverse array of attractions that capture the city’s unique character and charm. From world-renowned museums and historical sites to scenic parks and outdoor adventures, these are the must-visit attractions in Baltimore.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Baltimore Museum of Art: Known for its extensive collection of 19th-century, modern, and contemporary art, the Baltimore Museum of Art is a must-visit attraction for art lovers.

  • The Walters Art Museum: The Walters Art Museum is home to an impressive collection of art from around the world, including ancient Egyptian art, medieval armor, and renowned French Impressionist paintings.

  • Fort McHenry National Monument and Historic Shrine: This National Historic Landmark is the birthplace of the National Anthem and played a significant role in Maryland’s history during the War of 1812.

  • The Maryland Zoo in Baltimore: The Maryland Zoo is a popular attraction for families, with over 1,500 animals from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Baltimore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chesapeake Bay: The Chesapeake Bay is not only one of the largest estuaries in the world, but also a popular spot for boating, fishing, and other water sports.

  • Patterson Park: This 137-acre public park features a variety of hiking trails, sports fields, and beautiful gardens.

  • Cylburn Arboretum: This 207-acre park is home to over 20 unique gardens and collections of rare and exotic plants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baltimore

Baltimore is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the city and its natural surroundings. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Baltimore include:

  • Jones Falls Trail: This 10-mile trail follows the Jones Falls River through downtown Baltimore and offers scenic views of the city skyline.

  • Gwynns Falls Trail: This 15-mile trail winds through Baltimore’s western neighborhoods and features lush forests, waterfalls, and historic landmarks.

  • NCR Trail: The Northern Central Railroad Trail is a popular spot for biking, running, and horseback riding, spanning 20 miles from Ashland to Monkton.

Best Things to Do in Baltimore: Activities and Tours

In addition to its many cultural and natural attractions, Baltimore offers a wide variety of activities and tours that offer unique insights into the city’s history and culture. Some of the best things to do in Baltimore include:

  • Baltimore Inner Harbor: The Inner Harbor is the heart of Baltimore’s tourism district, with a variety of restaurants, shops, and attractions.

  • Baltimore Ghost Tours: Take a spooky tour through the city’s haunted history with Baltimore Ghost Tours.

  • Baltimore Food Tours: Sample the city’s best eats and learn about its culinary traditions on a Baltimore Food Tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baltimore

Day 1: Explore the historic landmarks and museums, such as Fort McHenry National Monument and Historic Shrine and the Walters Art Museum.

Day 2: Take a tour of the city’s famous landmarks, such as the Inner Harbor and the Basilica of the National Shrine of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary.

Day 3: Visit the Maryland Zoo in Baltimore for a day filled with family-friendly fun.

Day 4: Spend the day exploring the beautiful parks and outdoor trails, such as Patterson Park and Jones Falls Trail.

Day 5: Take a food tour of the city, exploring Baltimore’s unique culinary traditions.

Day 6: Visit one of the city’s many cultural attractions, such as the Baltimore Museum of Art or the Reginald F. Lewis Museum of Maryland African American History & Culture.

Day 7: Take a day trip to one of the nearby attractions, such as Annapolis or Washington D.C.

Food and Drink Scene of Baltimore

Baltimore is known for its delicious seafood and unique culinary traditions, making it a must-visit destination for foodies.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: No trip to Baltimore is complete without trying the city’s signature dish, the Maryland crab cake.

  • Old Bay seasoning: This blend of spices is a staple in Baltimore’s cuisine, used to flavor everything from seafood to french fries.

  • Berger Cookies: These iconic Baltimore treats are a chocolate lover’s dream, featuring a rich fudge frosting on a shortbread cookie.

Local Beverages

  • National Bohemian beer: Known locally as “Natty Boh,” this beloved Baltimore beer has been a staple since 1885.

  • Orange Crush: This refreshing cocktail is made with orange juice, vodka, triple sec, and lemon-lime soda, and is a popular drink at many Baltimore bars.

Where to Stay in Baltimore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baltimore offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the top places to stay in Baltimore include:

  • Four Seasons Hotel Baltimore: This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the Inner Harbor and features a spa, fitness center, and rooftop pool.

  • HI Baltimore Hostel: This affordable hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and free breakfast.

  • Hotel Indigo Baltimore Downtown: This boutique hotel is located in the historic Mount Vernon neighborhood and offers stylish accommodations and a rooftop bar.

What to Buy in Baltimore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Baltimore offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home as a reminder of your trip. Some of the top things to buy in Baltimore include:

  • Old Bay seasoning: Bring home a taste of Baltimore with a bottle of Old Bay seasoning, available at many local grocery stores.

  • Berger Cookies: These classic Baltimore treats are available at many local bakeries and grocery stores.

  • Crab-themed souvenirs: From keychains to t-shirts, there are plenty of crab-themed souvenirs available in Baltimore.

Local Culture and Customs of Baltimore

Baltimore has a unique culture and history, with many local customs and traditions that reflect the city’s diverse population. Visitors to Baltimore should be prepared to experience:

  • Hon culture: This kitschy, 1950s-era style is a beloved part of Baltimore’s identity, with many local businesses and events embracing the colorful, retro aesthetic.

  • Crab feasts: Baltimoreans are serious about their crabs, and crab feasts are a popular tradition during the summer months.

Best Time to Visit Baltimore

The best time to visit Baltimore depends on your preferences for weather and events. The spring and fall months offer mild temperatures and a variety of festivals and events, while the summer can be hot and humid but offers a wide range of outdoor activities. Winters in Baltimore can be cold and snowy, but the city still offers plenty of indoor attractions and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baltimore

Do:

  • Do try the seafood: Baltimore is known for its delicious seafood, so be sure to take advantage of the city’s many seafood restaurants and markets.

  • Do explore the city’s history: Baltimore is home to many historic landmarks and museums, so be sure to take some time to learn about the city’s past.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave valuables in your car: Car break-ins can be a problem in some areas of Baltimore, so be sure to keep your valuables out of sight or take them with you.

  • Don’t skip out on safety precautions: Like any city, Baltimore has some areas that are more dangerous than others. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take appropriate safety precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baltimore

While Baltimore has a higher crime rate than some other cities, most visitors to the city have a safe and enjoyable experience. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to avoid crime, such as staying in well-lit areas, locking car doors, and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Baltimore

Baltimore offers a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care centers. Visitors should take steps to stay healthy during their visit, such as washing their hands frequently and staying hydrated.

Getting to Baltimore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baltimore

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: Also known as BWI, this airport is located about 10 miles from downtown Baltimore.

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport: Located in Washington D.C., this airport is about an hour’s drive from Baltimore.

Bus Stations Near Baltimore

  • Baltimore Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Baltimore, this bus station offers service to a variety of destinations across the United States.

  • Megabus Baltimore Stop: This bus stop is located near the Baltimore Visitor Center and offers service to cities in the Northeast and Midwest.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Baltimore’s Penn Station offers service to a variety of destinations across the East Coast.

  • Car rental: Visitors to Baltimore can rent a car at one of the many rental car locations throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Baltimore

Baltimore offers a variety of transportation options for getting around the city, including:

  • Public transportation: The Maryland Transit Administration operates a network of buses, light rail trains, and subway trains throughout the city.

  • Ride-sharing services: Uber and Lyft are both available in Baltimore.

  • Taxis: Taxis are available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Baltimore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Baltimore is an excellent starting point for exploring the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. Some nearby destinations and excursions from Baltimore include:

  • Annapolis: Known for its charming downtown and historic state capitol building, Annapolis is located about an hour’s drive from Baltimore.

  • Washington D.C.: The nation’s capital is located about an hour’s drive from Baltimore and offers a variety of attractions, including museums, monuments, and government buildings.

Conclusion

Baltimore is a vibrant and diverse city with a rich history, beautiful natural landscapes, and a unique culinary scene. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical landmarks, hiking its scenic trails, or trying its famous crab cakes, Baltimore is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States.
