Introduction to North Baltimore

Welcome to North Baltimore, a vibrant and diverse neighborhood located in the heart of Baltimore, Maryland. Known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions, North Baltimore offers a unique experience to visitors from all around the world. From world-class museums and galleries to breathtaking outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in this wonderful neighborhood.

List of Places to Visit in North Baltimore

Discover the top attractions and must-visit destinations in North Baltimore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Baltimore Museum of Art: Explore the stunning collections of modern and contemporary art, including works by Matisse, Picasso, and many more.
  • The Walters Art Museum: Discover the magnificent collections of ancient art, medieval manuscripts, and Renaissance paintings.
  • Baltimore Civil War Museum: Learn about the Civil War history of Baltimore and the role of Maryland soldiers in the conflict.
  • Evergreen Museum & Library: Admire the beautiful Italianate mansion and gardens, and learn about the fascinating history of the Garrett family.

Natural Attractions of North Baltimore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cromwell Valley Park: Go hiking, birdwatching, or fishing in this lovely park, which features miles of scenic trails and a historic farm.
  • Lake Roland Park: Enjoy swimming, kayaking, or fishing in the picturesque lake, or take a stroll through the lovely gardens and nature center.
  • Robert E. Lee Memorial Park: Take in the stunning views of the city skyline and the Chesapeake Bay from the top of this scenic park, which also offers playgrounds, picnic areas, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Baltimore

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a casual cyclist, North Baltimore has plenty of trails and routes to explore. Some of the best options include:

  • The Druid Hill Park Loop: This 3.5-mile loop around Druid Hill Park offers scenic views of the lake and the zoo, as well as a challenging hill climb near the end.
  • The Jones Falls Trail: This 10-mile trail follows the scenic Jones Falls stream from the Inner Harbor to north Baltimore, passing through beautiful parks, historic mills, and charming neighborhoods.
  • The Gwynns Falls Trail: This 15-mile trail winds through the lovely Gwynns Falls Valley, offering scenic views of waterfalls, wildlife, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in North Baltimore: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in North Baltimore, including:

  • Baltimore Food Tours: Sample the delicious local cuisine and learn about the history of Baltimore’s culinary traditions on a guided food tour.
  • Baltimore Water Taxi Tour: Discover the city’s rich maritime history and stunning waterfront views on a relaxing water taxi tour.
  • Urban Axes: Try your hand at axe throwing – a popular new sport – at this unique indoor venue.
  • Sky Zone: Jump your way to fun and fitness at this popular trampoline park.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Baltimore

Day 1: Visit the Baltimore Museum of Art and enjoy lunch at the Woodberry Kitchen. Day 2: Go hiking in Lake Roland Park in the morning and explore the Walters Art Museum in the afternoon. Day 3: Take a food tour of the city’s best eateries and sample local specialties like crab cakes and Berger cookies. Day 4: Visit the Baltimore Civil War Museum and stroll along the Jones Falls Trail. Day 5: Go kayaking on the Patapsco River or take a guided water taxi tour of the harbor. Day 6: Explore the historic Evergreen Museum & Library and enjoy a sunset hike in Robert E. Lee Memorial Park. Day 7: Try your hand at axe throwing at Urban Axes or bounce your way to fun at Sky Zone.

Food and Drink Scene of North Baltimore

Discover the local cuisine and beverage options in North Baltimore:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: A Baltimore classic, these delicious seafood cakes are made with lump crab meat and flavored with Old Bay seasoning.
  • Berger Cookies: These chewy, chocolate-frosted cookies are a beloved local treat.
  • Pit Beef: A popular regional sandwich featuring thinly sliced and grilled beef, usually served with horseradish and onion.

Local Beverages

  • National Bohemian Beer (Natty Boh): A local favorite, this light beer is brewed in Baltimore and features the iconic Mr. Boh mascot on its cans and bottles.
  • Maryland Wine Trail: Discover the best local wineries and vineyards in Maryland, including the Boordy Vineyards in nearby Hydes.

Where to Stay in North Baltimore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, there are plenty of options in North Baltimore:

  • The Ivy Hotel: This five-star boutique hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites, as well as a lush garden and a world-class restaurant.
  • Wilson House Bed & Breakfast: This charming Victorian mansion features cozy rooms and delicious homemade breakfasts.
  • HI Baltimore Hostel: This affordable hostel is located in a historic building and offers shared and private rooms, as well as a kitchen, lounge, and outdoor patio.

What to Buy in North Baltimore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or a delicious local product to bring home? Check out these options:

  • The Book Thing: This free bookstore is a local institution, featuring thousands of donated books and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Jinja Jewelry: This eco-friendly jewelry line is made with natural materials and features unique designs inspired by nature.
  • Zeke’s Coffee: This local coffee roaster offers a range of delicious blends and single-origin coffees, as well as coffee accessories and merchandise.

Local Culture and Customs of North Baltimore

North Baltimore is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood that celebrates its history and culture. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local art and music scenes, as well as the rich culinary traditions of the region. Respect for local customs and manners is appreciated, and visitors should be mindful of the local environment and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit North Baltimore

The best time to visit North Baltimore is in the spring and fall, when the weather is comfortable and the foliage is beautiful. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy. Visitors should check the weather forecast before traveling to ensure that they are prepared for the conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Baltimore

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when visiting North Baltimore:

Do:

  • Explore the local parks and trails for beautiful scenery and outdoor recreation.
  • Visit the Baltimore Museum of Art and other cultural attractions to learn about the city’s history and art scene.
  • Try the local cuisine, including crab cakes, Berger cookies, and pit beef.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and manners, and be mindful of the environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the local wildlife or natural resources.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public areas, particularly at night.
  • Ignore the city’s safety guidelines or laws.

Safety and Health

Visitors to North Baltimore should be aware of the following safety and health tips:

Safety in North Baltimore

  • Use common sense and stay alert when walking or traveling alone at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking in areas that are poorly lit or deserted.
  • Keep valuables out of sight, particularly in busy areas such as the Inner Harbor.
  • Take necessary precautions when swimming or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in North Baltimore

  • Drink plenty of water and protect yourself from the sun and heat during the summer months.
  • Be aware of the symptoms of Lyme disease and other tick-borne illnesses when hiking or spending time outdoors.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness or injury.

Getting to North Baltimore: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Baltimore is easily accessible by a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near North Baltimore

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI): Located approximately 15 miles south of North Baltimore, BWI is the closest airport with major flights from around the US.

Bus Stations Near North Baltimore

  • Baltimore Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Baltimore, this station is approximately 5 miles south of North Baltimore.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station: Located in downtown Baltimore, the station is approximately 6 miles south of North Baltimore.

How to Get Around in North Baltimore

Visitors to North Baltimore can get around using a variety of transportation options, including:

  • Baltimore Water Taxi: This convenient and scenic water taxi system connects many of the city’s top attractions, including the Inner Harbor, Fort McHenry, and Fells Point.
  • Baltimore Bike Share: Visitors can rent a bike for the day or week at one of many stations around the city, and enjoy a leisurely ride along one of North Baltimore’s many bike-friendly routes.
  • Uber/Lyft/Taxis: These popular ride-sharing services are available throughout North Baltimore and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from North Baltimore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Maryland or the surrounding regions, consider these nearby destinations:

  • Annapolis: Located approximately 30 miles southeast of North Baltimore, Annapolis is the charming state capital of Maryland, with plenty of historic landmarks and waterfront attractions.
  • Washington DC: Located approximately 40 miles southwest of North Baltimore, the US capital city offers world-class museums, national landmarks, and political history.
  • Philadelphia: Located approximately 100 miles northeast of North Baltimore, this vibrant city offers a wealth of history, culture, and cuisine.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to explore the rich culture and natural beauty of North Baltimore. Whether you’re looking for museums and galleries, outdoor adventures, or delicious local cuisine, you’re sure to find something to love in this wonderful neighborhood.
