Introduction to Baltimore County

Located in Central Maryland, Baltimore County is a vibrant destination with fascinating historical sites, beautiful natural attractions, and exciting cultural offerings. With easy access to Baltimore City and the region’s major highways, it’s an ideal place to explore for both locals and visitors alike.

In this comprehensive guide, we will provide you with a detailed list of places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to buy in Baltimore County. In addition, we’ll offer insider tips on the local culture and customs, the best times to visit, and how to get around the area.

List of Places to Visit in Baltimore County

Baltimore County offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and history. Here is a list of top attractions to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Hampton National Historic Site- A beautiful, 18th-century mansion and park, where visitors can take guided tours of the house and learn about the history and culture of the area.

  2. The Baltimore Museum of Art- This world-renowned museum features an extensive collection of contemporary and modern art, as well as a beautiful sculpture garden and rotating exhibitions.

  3. The Maryland Historical Society- With over 350,000 artifacts and a world-class library, this museum provides insight into the state’s rich history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Baltimore County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Oregon Ridge Park- A popular outdoor destination, Oregon Ridge offers a range of activities including hiking, fishing, and camping.

  2. Gunpowder Falls State Park- With over 18,000 acres of nature and wildlife, Gunpowder Falls is perfect for exploring waterfalls, hiking trails, and enjoying a picnic by the river.

  3. Loch Raven Reservoir- A scenic spot located just outside of Baltimore City, the Loch Raven Reservoir offers hiking trails, boating activities, and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baltimore County

Baltimore County boasts a range of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of nature and provide a wonderful way to stay active and explore the area’s natural beauty. Here are a few popular routes to try:

  1. NCR Trail- The Northern Central Railroad Trail extends over 20 miles, offering a peaceful escape from the city and a great place to explore on foot or by bike.

  2. Patapsco Valley State Park- This park offers over 200 miles of trails to explore, with options suitable for beginners and experienced hikers.

  3. Cromwell Valley Park- With over 380 acres of preserved land and trails, this park is ideal for hiking, bird-watching, and exploring the area’s abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Baltimore County: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours to suit just about any interest in Baltimore County. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  1. Go on a brewery tour- Baltimore County is home to a range of craft breweries, each producing unique and delicious beers worth trying.

  2. Take a hot air balloon ride- For an unforgettable experience, see Baltimore County from above on a hot air balloon ride.

  3. Witness a horse race- Maryland is known for its horse racing, and Baltimore County is home to one of the country’s oldest racetracks – the Pimlico Racecourse.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baltimore County

For visitors with a week to explore the area, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Baltimore Museum of Art and take a guided tour of the exhibits.

Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Patapsco Valley State Park.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the county and witness the stunning views from above.

Day 4: Visit the Hampton National Historic Site and take a guided tour of the historic mansion and grounds.

Day 5: Go on a brewery tour and discover some of the state’s best craft beers.

Day 6: Take a horse-drawn carriage ride through the beautiful countryside.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a picnic at beautiful Loch Raven Reservoir.

Food and Drink Scene of Baltimore County

Baltimore County boasts a diverse and exciting food and drink scene, with flavors and dishes influenced by the area’s rich history and culture. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Crab Cakes- Baltimore County is famous for its fresh crab, and the crabcake is a dish not to be missed.

  2. Smith Island Cake- A unique dessert unique to Maryland, this cake is filled with layers of rich cream and chocolate frosting.

  3. Pit Beef- A local specialty, Pit Beef is a dish of thinly-sliced beef slow-cooked over charcoal and served on a roll.

Local Beverages

  1. National Bohemian (Natty Boh)- A beloved local brew, Natty Boh is a classic American pale lager.

  2. Maryland Wine- With over 70 wineries across the state, Maryland produces some fantastic wines worth trying.

  3. Zeke’s Coffee- This local coffee roaster produces high-quality beans that are roasted to perfection.

Where to Stay in Baltimore County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baltimore County has a range of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference. Here are a few popular options:

  1. Hotel Indigo Baltimore Downtown- A stylish and modern hotel located in Baltimore City, offering easy access to the area’s top attractions.

  2. The Gramercy Mansion- This luxurious bed and breakfast offers elegant rooms, beautiful grounds, and an ideal location for exploring the area.

  3. HI Baltimore- A welcoming and centrally located hostel that offers affordable rates and a unique and social atmosphere.

What to Buy in Baltimore County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Baltimore County offers a range of unique and locally-made products that make for great souvenirs or gifts. Here are a few options to consider:

  1. Old Bay- A blend of spices from Maryland, Old Bay is used in a host of dishes and is a staple in Maryland pantries.

  2. Maryland Blue Crabs- Freshly caught and available cooked or alive, Maryland blue crab is a local delicacy that makes for an excellent gift.

  3. Rusty Tin Signs- A unique and quirky souvenir, rusty tin signs featuring Baltimore County themes make for a great conversation starter.

Local Culture and Customs of Baltimore County

Baltimore County is a welcoming and friendly place, with a rich history and unique culture. It’s essential to be respectful of these local customs and traditions when visiting, such as:

  • Tipping: in Baltimore County, customary tipping is 15-20% of the total bill for services such as dining, taxi rides, and haircuts.

  • Hon or Hun: A term of endearment used frequently in Baltimore County to show friendliness or kindness.

Best Time to Visit Baltimore County

The best time to visit Baltimore County depends on personal preference and what activities you plan to do. The weather can be mild in the spring and fall, making it perfect for hiking and outdoor activities. Summers can be hot and humid, making it a great time to explore Baltimore City’s indoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baltimore County

Here are a few Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Baltimore County:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore Baltimore’s many museums and historical sites.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious or cultural attractions.
  • Litter in public areas or parks.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Here are a few tips to ensure your safety and health while visiting Baltimore County:

Safety in Baltimore County

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep your valuables and belongings in a safe place when out and about.
  • Keep yourself aware of your surroundings and any potentially dangerous situations.

Health in Baltimore County

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any unusual symptoms or illness.

Getting to Baltimore County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Baltimore County is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are a few options for getting to the area:

Airports Near Baltimore County

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI)
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA)
  • Washington Dulles International Airport (IAD)

Bus Stations Near Baltimore County

  • Greyhound: Baltimore Bus Terminal
  • Megabus: White Marsh Park and Ride

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Baltimore Penn Station
  • Light Rail: Baltimore City and nearby suburbs

How to Get Around in Baltimore County

Here are a few ways to get around Baltimore County:

  • Rental Car: There are many rental car options available in the area for those who prefer to drive.
  • Ride-Share: Uber and Lyft are available in the area and offer a convenient option for those without a rental car.
  • Public Transportation: The Maryland Transit Administration (MTA) offers a range of bus and light rail services in the County.

Where to Go Next from Baltimore County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Baltimore County is conveniently located near many other fantastic destinations in the region. Here are a few recommended excursions:

  • Washington D.C.- Just a short drive or train ride away, the nation’s capital offers a range of historical and cultural attractions to explore.

  • Annapolis- With its charming waterfront area and rich history, Annapolis is a great place to explore for a day trip.

  • Eastern Shore- Take a scenic drive across the Chesapeake Bay Bridge to explore the beautiful Eastern Shore region.

Conclusion

Baltimore County is a fantastic destination for those who love history, nature, and culture. With its many outdoor attractions, museums, and unique food and drink scene, there’s no shortage of things to see and do. Plan your trip with our comprehensive guide, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience.
