Introduction to Glen Burnie

Located in Central Maryland, Glen Burnie is a charming town that offers a unique mix of cultural attractions and natural beauty. With its rich history and diverse recreational opportunities, Glen Burnie has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, this hidden gem is waiting for you to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Glen Burnie

Glen Burnie boasts a range of attractions that cater to various interests and ages. From historical landmarks to outdoor adventures, here’s a list of places you shouldn’t miss during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Banneker-Douglass Museum: A fascinating museum that provides insight into African American history and culture.

  • National Electronics Museum: A must-visit for tech lovers, this museum has a vast collection of vintage electronics from the early 20th century to present day.

  • U.S. Naval Academy: Located just a short drive from Glen Burnie, the Naval Academy is a historic institution that features beautiful architecture and a rich history.

Natural Attractions of Glen Burnie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sawmill Creek Park: A popular spot among bird watchers and nature lovers, this park features a scenic walking trail and a creek that flows into the Chesapeake Bay.

  • Furnace Bay Golf Course: Play a round of golf while enjoying panoramic views of the Bay at this picturesque golf course.

  • Patapsco Valley State Park: Located just a short drive away, this state park offers miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing, camping, and various water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glen Burnie

Glen Burnie has a plethora of hiking and biking trails that cater to various skill levels. Here are some popular routes that you should consider exploring:

  • B&A Trail: This 13.3-mile trail offers a scenic route for hiking and biking and passes through several parks and landmarks.

  • Sawmill Creek Trail: This 1.7-mile trail overlooks the creek and provides stunning views of the Bay.

  • Solley Cove Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely walk and bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Glen Burnie: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re travelling solo or with your family, Glen Burnie has a range of activities and tours that will keep you entertained throughout your trip. Here are our top picks:

  • Go-Kart Racing: For thrill-seekers, head to Batchelor’s Motor Sports and enjoy high-speed go-kart racing.

  • Maryland Sunrise Farm Tour: A unique experience that offers a glimpse into the local farming community and lets you learn about sustainable farming practices.

  • Chesapeake Bay Sailing Tour: Sail along the Bay on a guided tour and admire stunning views of the water and natural surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Glen Burnie

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Glen Burnie, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities/Tours
1 Explore Banneker-Douglass Museum and National Electronics Museum
2 Hike or bike the B&A Trail
3 Visit U.S. Naval Academy and enjoy a round of golf at Furnace Bay Golf Course
4 Take the Maryland Sunrise Farm Tour
5 Go-kart racing at Batchelor’s Motor Sports
6 Cruise along the Chesapeake Bay on a sailing tour
7 Spend a day at Patapsco Valley State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Glen Burnie

Glen Burnie has a thriving food and drink scene that features a mix of cuisines. From local eateries to fine dining restaurants, here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: A Maryland classic, try the local crab cakes for a taste of the Bay.

  • Pit beef: Thinly sliced, smoky and tender, pit beef is a Maryland specialty.

  • Old Bay fries: A delicious side dish that’s seasoned with Old Bay, a local spice blend.

Local Beverages

  • Natty Boh beer: A local beer that’s the pride of Maryland.

  • Margaritas: Pair your meal with a refreshing margarita, a popular drink at many local restaurants.

  • Sweet tea: A southern classic that’s a favorite in Maryland restaurants.

Where to Stay in Glen Burnie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Glen Burnie offers a range of accommodation options that cater to various budgets and needs. From hotels to guesthouses, here are some of the best places to stay in Glen Burnie:

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites: A popular option that offers comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and an indoor pool.

  • Extended Stay America: This hotel is perfect for travelers on a budget who require an extended stay and need amenities like a kitchenette.

  • Cabin Creekwood: For a cozy, intimate experience, this guesthouse offers a peaceful alternative to traditional hotel stays.

What to Buy in Glen Burnie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Glen Burnie offers a range of shopping options that cater to various tastes and budgets. From local souvenirs to handmade crafts, here are some of the best things to buy in Glen Burnie:

  • Crab mallets: A souvenir that’s both practical and unique, crab mallets are a must-buy for seafood lovers.

  • Local art: Glen Burnie is home to several local artists who sell their work at nearby galleries.

  • Old Bay seasoning: Bring home a jar of this iconic spice blend that’s a staple in Maryland kitchens.

Local Culture and Customs of Glen Burnie

Glen Burnie embraces a mix of cultures and customs, with a strong emphasis on the area’s history and traditions. Visitors are expected to be respectful of local customs and practices, and to engage in local traditions and events.

Best Time to Visit Glen Burnie

Glen Burnie has a year-round appeal and offers something unique in each season. From the fall foliage to winter wonderlands, here’s the best time to visit Glen Burnie:

  • Fall: For cooler temperatures and stunning colors, visit Glen Burnie in September and October.

  • Winter: Visit Glen Burnie during winter to enjoy the local festivities and events.

  • Spring: Spring in Glen Burnie offers mild temperatures and stunning flora and fauna.

  • Summer: Enjoy outdoor activities and the Bay’s water activities during the summer months.

Getting to Glen Burnie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Glen Burnie is easily accessible by various means of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Glen Burnie

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport is just a 10-minute drive from Glen Burnie and offers flights to major destinations.

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport and Dulles International Airport are located within a few hours’ drive from Glen Burnie.

Bus Stations Near Glen Burnie

  • Greyhound Bus Lines and Megabus offer regular services to Glen Burnie from destinations across the U.S.

  • Local buses and trains run daily to and from Glen Burnie, making it easy to get around without a car.

Other Options

  • Taxis, ride-hailing apps, and private vehicles are popular methods of transportation within the town and surrounding areas.

  • Bike and car rental services are available for travelers who want to explore the town at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Glen Burnie

Glen Burnie is relatively easy to navigate, with several options available for getting around town. Here are some options to consider:

  • Local buses offer several routes and schedules throughout the town.

  • Ride-hailing apps and taxis are available 24/7 for convenience.

  • Walking and cycling are popular options for exploring the town’s cultural sites and scenic areas.

Where to Go Next from Glen Burnie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you should consider:

Glen Burnie is a hidden gem in Central Maryland that’s waiting to be explored. With its diverse attractions, natural beauty, and rich history, this town has something for everyone.
