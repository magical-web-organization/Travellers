Bel Air, a charming town in Central Maryland, greets guests with its picturesque landscape, vibrant cultural scene and numerous outdoor adventures. The county seat of Harford County, Bel Air is a perfect destination for a memorable vacation experience for families, couples, and solo travelers alike. Here we will provide a comprehensive guide to explore Bel Air.

Introduction to Bel Air

Located in the heart of Maryland, Bel Air’s easiness of access makes it a popular tourist destination. The charming town has numerous great restaurants, shopping, attractions, and outdoor adventures. Bel Air offers everything from the picturesque scenery, popular attractions, to local culture and customs, making it an excellent choice for visitors of all preferences.

List of Places to Visit in Bel Air

Bel Air has much to offer to its visitors, from culturally important attractions to natural wonders. Here are the top places one should not miss in Bel Air:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harford Artists Gallery: The Harford Artists Gallery is an excellent place to appreciate local artists’ amazing work, learn about different forms of art, and buy souvenirs to take home.

  • Hays House Museum: The Hays House Museum is a must-visit place for history buffs. This historical museum is a great way to take a glimpse into the town’s past and learn about the customs and traditions of Bel Air history.

  • Concord Point Lighthouse: Visit the iconic, historic Concord Point Lighthouse, located on the scenic promenade in the town of Havre de Grace.

Natural Attractions of Bel Air: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ma and Pa Trail: Located in the heart of Bel Air, the Ma and Pa Trail is ideal for a family day out or a serene walk. This 3.5-mile walking trail is well-maintained, well-marked, and provides various scenic views of the town.

  • Rocks State Park: Rocks State Park features a series of massive rock formations that were crafted by the Susquehanna River over millions of years. Hiking and rock-climbing enthusiasts can explore the rocks and scenic views.

  • Anita C. Leight Estuary Center: Set on the banks of the Bush River, the Anita C. Leight Estuary Center offers natural trails with scenic views, boat tours, kayaking, birding, fishing, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bel Air

Bel Air is a great place to enjoy outdoor-based activities. Below are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes of Bel Air:

  • Harford County Trail: a 27-mile trail through rural areas, traversing woodlands, parks, and preserved natural spaces of Northern Maryland.

  • Ma and Pa Trail: a popular trail amidst nature & public art installations, a trailhead with ample parking, washroom and cycling support.

  • Belle Isle Loop Trail: a 4.1-mile moderately trafficked loop trail that features a river, wilderness living, and some beautiful foliage.

Best Things to Do in Bel Air: Activities and Tours

Bel Air offers several activities and tours for visitors. Here are a few popular things to do in Bel Air:

  • Visit Bel Air Downtown: Stroll around the downtown area for a great shopping and dining experience. Browse through charming boutiques, explore unique restaurants and cafes that offer delicious cuisine.

  • Watch a movie at the historic theatre : The Bel Air Cinema is a perfect blend mix of classic and modern amenities. It shows the latest movies with maximum comfort.

  • Attend Special Events: Bel Air hosts several special events throughout the year, including the Maryland State BBQ Bash and the Bel Air Farmers’ Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Bel Air

Bel Air has a rich food and drinks scene. Below are must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cake: A staple of Maryland cuisine, the crab cake is a must-try dish when in Bel Air. The Tower Restaurant offers the best crab cakes in town.

  • Old Bay Fries: Old bay fries are classic hand-cut potatoes dusted with a balanced blend of spices. Head to Black Eyed Suzie’s for their delicious version of Old Bay Fries.

  • Goat Cheese Salad: Belle Maison serves fresh goat cheese salad, with a generous serving of green vegetables and balsamic dressing.

Local Beverages

  • Natty Boh: Natty Boh is a light beer that Marylanders have been drinking since 1885. Enjoy a chilled Natty Boh at the Dark Horse Saloon.

  • Boordy Vineyards Wine: Boordy Vineyards Wine is Maryland’s oldest and most prestigious winery. They offer exclusive tastings and tours.

  • Cider from Millstone Cellars: Millstone Cellars produces sublime ciders with fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Bel Air has many unique souvenirs to take back home. Here are a few of the best things to buy in Bel Air:

  • Baked Goods from Coffee Coffee: This coffee shop in Bel Air has several sweet treats like macarons, cupcakes which make an excellent souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Bel Air

Bel Air has a lively and vibrant culture and customs. The city’s rich history, diverse community, and unique traditions contribute to the town’s colorful customs.

Best Time to Visit Bel Air

Bel Air is a pleasant destination throughout the year. However, the best time to visit Bel Air is from March to May and September to November when the weather is perfect for outdoor excursions, events, and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bel Air

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to Bel Air:

Do:

  • Familiarize yourself with the unique customs, traditions, and beliefs of the locals.

  • Respect the natural surroundings, trails, and parks.

  • Visit local shops, boutiques, and markets to support local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Never litter, do not leave trash behind while enjoying outdoor activities.

  • Do not over-plan, allow some flexibility in your itinerary.

  • Do not disrespect the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bel Air

Bel Air is one of the safest places to visit in Maryland. However, like traveling to a new place, some general safety guidelines should be followed:

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings.

  • Avoid walking alone, especially at night.

  • Do not leave valuable items in your car and lock your car doors.

Health in Bel Air

Bel Air is generally safe for visitors from a health perspective. However, visitors should drink bottled water, stay well-hydrated, and carry emergency medication, if needed.

Getting to Bel Air: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bel Air has some great transportation options for local travel.

Airports Near Bel Air

  • Baltimore Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: This is the nearest airport, located only 45 kilometers from Bel Air.

  • Philadelphia International Airport: It is around 150 kilometers from Bel Air, but it’s closeness to the Amtrak train stop makes it a quicker ride than BWI for some travelers rushing towards Bel Air.

Bus Stations Near Bel Air

  • Adams: The Patterson Mill HS: It is a local bus station situated on SCHCR’s site, run by Harford County.

  • Townebrooke Apts: A bus station located east of Bel Air on Route 40 (Baltimore Pike), designed for the Harford County transportation.

Other Options

  • Train Services: The Amtrak train station at Perryville connects Bel Air with other major destinations in Maryland.

How to Get Around in Bel Air

Bel Air is a small town that is best explored on foot or bicycle. There are also car rental services, cab services like Uber, and reliable public transportation.

  • On Foot or Bicycle: Visitors can easily navigate the town on foot or hire bicycles available in the downtown area.

  • Public Transportation: Harford Transit Link provides shuttle and bus services to various destinations in and around Bel Air. You can buy a day tour pass which offers unlimited travel for the day.

Where to Go Next from Bel Air: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bel Air’s proximity to some of Maryland’s biggest cities and natural attractions makes it an ideal starting point for excursions. Here are some of the popular destinations nearby:

  • Baltimore: This vibrant city is just a 30-minute drive from Bel Air, with a diverse range of attractions, including museums, nightlife, great restaurants, and more.

  • Havre de Grace: A waterfront town that is only a 25-minute drive from Bel Air. Enjoy water sports, fishing, and boating.

  • Harriet Tubman Underground Railway State Park: It is located in Church Creek, Maryland and is just a two-hour drive from Bel Air.

Conclusion

Bel Air has much to offer to travelers. It is a town that is equal parts charming and fascinating. From the natural scenery to the many significant cultural attractions, Bel Air has everything one could ask for in a vacation destination. By following our comprehensive guide, you can plan your visit to Bel Air and experience its magic for yourself.
