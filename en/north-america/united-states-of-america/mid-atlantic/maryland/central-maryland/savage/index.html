A Comprehensive Guide to Savage, Maryland

Introduction to Savage

Located in Central Maryland, Savage is a small but charming town that sits between Baltimore and Washington, D.C. This hidden gem is often overlooked by tourists but has a lot to offer. Savage is known for its historic buildings, beautiful parks, and welcoming community. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit and things to do in Savage, showcasing its unique character and hidden treasures.

List of Places to Visit in Savage

If you’re wondering what to do in Savage, look no further. We’ve compiled a list of top attractions to help you explore this charming town’s diverse offerings.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Savage is a town steeped in history and culture, so visiting its museums and historical sites is a must. Here are a few sites you won’t want to miss:

  • Savage Mill: A former cotton mill turned shopping and dining destination with over 175 unique shops.
  • Bollman Truss Bridge: The world’s oldest surviving wrought iron truss bridge is an engineering marvel located in Savage Park.
  • The Carroll Baldwin Memorial Hall: A historic venue that houses a museum of local history, arts, and culture.
  • Annapolis Rock: A scenic overlook with a breathtaking view of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Savage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Savage has several natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are a few worth checking out:

  • Savage Park: A 300-acre park in southern Howard County that offers a wide variety of recreational activities, including hiking, fishing, picnicking, and sports fields.
  • Savage Mill Trail: A 1.3-mile paved multi-use trail that winds through the beautiful Savage Mill complex.
  • Patapsco Valley State Park: Just a short drive away, Patapsco Valley State Park offers over 200 miles of hiking and biking trails in a stunning natural setting.
  • Lake Elkhorn: A serene and picturesque lake with walking paths, playgrounds, and fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savage

Savage’s natural scenery makes it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few of the most popular trails:

  • Patuxent Branch Trail: A 20-mile trail that spans the length of Howard County from Savage to Ellicott City.
  • Columbia Bike Trail: A 14-mile paved trail that circles Columbia and passes by Lake Kittamaqundi.
  • Laurel Lake Trail: A short but scenic trail that winds around the beautiful Laurel Lake.
  • Laurel Highlands Trail: A challenging seven-mile hike that offers stunning views of the Patuxent River.

Best Things to Do in Savage: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, Savage also offers a range of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are a few highlights:

  • Ghost Tours of Historic Savage Mill: A spooky and entertaining tour of the Mill at night during which guests can hear about the history of the town and stories of its colorful past.
  • Forest Park Golf Course: A beautifully maintained and challenging golf course located in nearby Baltimore.
  • Electric Bike Tours: A fun, eco-friendly way to see the sights of Savage and the surrounding area.
  • Saturday Farmers Market: A weekly event showcasing the best of local produce, crafts, and food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Savage

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Savage, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Savage Mill complex, shop in its over 175 unique shops, and enjoy dinner at the Rams Head Tavern.
2 Hike the Patuxent Branch Trail and visit the Bollman Truss Bridge and Savage Park.
3 Take a bike tour of Columbia and visit the famous Lake Kittamaqundi.
4 Visit the Annapolis Rock scenic overlook and picnic at the nearby Patapsco Valley State Park.
5 Enjoy a round of golf at the Forest Park Golf Course.
6 Take a ghost tour of Historic Savage Mill and enjoy dinner at the Great Sage Vegetarian Restaurant.
7 Visit Lake Elkhorn and enjoy the Saturday Farmers Market before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Savage

Savage offers a range of dining options, from chic restaurants to laid-back cafes. Here are a few dishes and beverages that you have to try when in Savage:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: This Maryland favorite is a must-try when visiting Savage. We recommend trying them at Rams Head Tavern or The Turn House.
  • Fried Chicken: Enjoy the best Fried Chicken at Julia’s Restaurant.
  • Pimento Cheeseburger: A scrumptious and unique burger is available at the Great Sage Vegetarian Restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Enjoy craft beer at Manor Hill Brewing.
  • Wine: Visit The Iron Bridge Wine Company and try some of the best wines in the Savage.

Where to Stay in Savage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re spending the night in Savage, you have several options. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • The Savage Inn: This charming guesthouse is an ideal base for exploring Savage and its surroundings.
  • The Columbia Inn at Peralynna: If you’re looking for a luxurious and comfortable place to stay, The Columbia Inn at Peralynna is the place to go in the area.
  • TownePlace Suites by Marriott Baltimore BWI Airport: A stylish and affordable option that’s located just a short drive away from Savage.

What to Buy in Savage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Savage’s shops offer a wide range of souvenirs and local products that are perfect for taking home as a memento of your trip. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Local honey: Purchase Superfoods Market’s honey and swarm your toast with local flavors for breakfast.
  • Handcrafted jewelry: You can purchase some unique pieces at Sweet Elizabeth Jane.
  • Handmade soap: Take home sweet smelling scents of Nature’s Artisan

Local Culture and Customs of Savage

Savage has a rich history and a unique culture worth exploring. The town is home to a tight-knit, welcoming community that values hard work, independence, and tradition. Visitors can experience the community’s warmth and friendly nature by attending local events such as the Savage Fest and the holiday tree lighting ceremony.

Best Time to Visit Savage

Savage is beautiful throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during the spring (April-June) when the weather is mild, and the flowers are in bloom. The autumn months (September-November) can also be an excellent time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savage

Do:

  • Respect the town’s historic buildings and landmarks.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreational opportunities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter in parks and natural areas.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Forget to bring your sense of adventure and curiosity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Savage

Savage is a very safe town, and its residents are friendly and approachable. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings, especially when walking alone at night. If you’re traveling with valuables, it’s best to keep them in a safe place, like your hotel room.

Health in Savage

As with any travel destination, visitors should take precautions to stay healthy while in Savage. Be sure to pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and any medications you may need. If you have any medical concerns, it’s best to consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Savage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Savage is conveniently located between Baltimore and Washington, D.C., and is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are a few options for getting to Savage:

Airports Near Savage

  • Baltimore/Washington International (BWI) Airport is located approximately 20 miles from Savage.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA) is approximately 30 miles from Savage.

Bus Stations Near Savage

  • The MARC Savage Station is located within walking distance of Savage Mill.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate at BWI Airport.
  • Taxi or rideshare: Taxis and rideshares are available from both BWI and DCA airports.

How to Get Around in Savage

Savage is a compact town that’s easy to explore on foot or by bike. If you’d like to venture further afield, a rental car or rideshare service may be your best option.

Where to Go Next from Savage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savage is an excellent base for exploring the Mid-Atlantic region. Here are a few nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Washington, D.C.: Just a short drive away, the nation’s capital is home to some of the country’s most iconic landmarks and museums.
  • Baltimore: Known for its historic neighborhoods, charming harbor, and world-class museums, Baltimore is a must-visit destination.
  • Chesapeake Bay: Head east to explore this stunning natural wonder, famous for its seafood and abundant wildlife.

Conclusion

Savage is a unique and charming town with a rich history and natural beauty that’s worth exploring. Whether you’re interested in culture, outdoor recreation, or delicious food and drink, Savage has something for everyone. We hope that this comprehensive guide has inspired you to visit this hidden gem in central Maryland.
