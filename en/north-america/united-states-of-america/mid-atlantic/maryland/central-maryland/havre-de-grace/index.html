Introduction to Havre de Grace

Havre de Grace is a charming city located in Central Maryland, along the coast of the Chesapeake Bay. Known for its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture, Havre de Grace offers a unique travel experience for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historic sites, hiking through scenic trails, or indulging in local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Havre de Grace. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, activities, and experiences that make Havre de Grace a must-visit destination in Maryland.

List of Places to Visit in Havre de Grace

  • Concord Point Lighthouse: A historic lighthouse built in 1827, which now serves as a museum and provides scenic views of Chesapeake Bay.
  • Havre de Grace Decoy Museum: A museum dedicated to the art of decoy carving, featuring a collection of antique and contemporary decoys.
  • Havre de Grace Maritime Museum: A museum showcasing the local history and culture of the Chesapeake Bay region, including a restored oyster dredging boat and interactive exhibits.
  • Susquehanna Museum at the Lock House: A museum located in a 19th-century lockhouse, offering insight into the lives of canal workers and the history of the Susquehanna and Tidewater Canal.
  • Millard Tydings Memorial Park: A waterfront park with scenic views of the Susquehanna River, nature trails, and picnic areas.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Havre de Grace is rich in history and culture, with many museums and historical attractions to explore. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Havre de Grace Decoy Museum: As mentioned previously, this museum is dedicated to the art of decoy carving, which is a tradition that dates back to the 19th century when hunters used decoys to attract ducks.
  • Concord Point Lighthouse: This lighthouse offers tours and a museum full of historic artifacts and memorabilia that offer insight into early 19th-century living conditions for lighthouse keepers and their families.
  • Havre de Grace Maritime Museum: This museum offers the opportunity to explore the history of the Bay and the significance of marine life in the region. Visitors get to see restored boats, artifacts, and dioramas showing the construction of the Bay Bridge and other milestones in local maritime history.

Natural Attractions of Havre de Grace: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Havre de Grace is home to several natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Susquehanna State Park: This park offers hiking trails, fishing, and camping, as well as opportunities to view local wildlife like deer, bald eagles, and blue herons.
  • Millard Tydings Memorial Park: This park offers picturesque views of the Susquehanna River and the area’s gorgeous sunsets.
  • Havre de Grace Promenade: This is a scenic walkway along the waterfront, with stunning views of Chesapeake Bay and Susquehanna River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Havre de Grace

Havre de Grace has several excellent trails and routes for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Ma and Pa Trail: This trail is located just outside Havre de Grace and is perfect for hiking, biking, and running. The four-mile trail takes you through fields, woods, and small creeks, giving you a glimpse of some of the area’s natural beauty.
  • Susquehanna State Park: This park offers several scenic hiking trails through wooded areas and along the river.

Best Things to Do in Havre de Grace: Activities and Tours

Aside from the top attractions mentioned above, Havre de Grace also offers several activities and tours that are worth checking out. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Havre de Grace Farmer’s Market: The market offers access to fresh, local produce, as well as handmade crafts and baked goods from the area’s farmers and artisans.
  • Swan Harbor Farm: The farm offers tours and events that showcase the area’s agricultural heritage, including hayrides, live demonstrations, and the opportunity to see farm animals up close.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing on the Susquehanna River: There are several local companies that offer kayaking and canoeing tours, allowing visitors to experience the beauty and serenity of the Susquehanna River from the water.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Havre de Grace, here is a sample itinerary that includes some of the area’s top attractions and experiences:

Food and Drink Scene of Havre de Grace

Havre de Grace is home to several restaurants and cafes that serve up delicious local cuisine, as well as craft breweries and wineries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: No trip to Maryland is complete without trying the local specialty, crab cakes. Several restaurants in Havre de Grace offer delicious variations of this classic dish.
  • Old Bay Wings: Old Bay seasoning is a popular spice mix in the region, and several local eateries serve up Old Bay Wings, a unique take on classic chicken wings.
  • Smith Island Cake: This delicious dessert originated from nearby Smith Island, Maryland, and features layers of cake and frosting.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Several local breweries, including Independent Brewing Company and Laurrapin Cask Works, offer a variety of craft beers.
  • Wine: Harford Vineyard offers local wines that can be sampled and purchased on-site.

What to Buy in Havre de Grace: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Havre de Grace is home to several shops that offer unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Susquehanna Lockhouse Museum Gift Shop: The gift shop sells a variety of canal-inspired souvenirs, including t-shirts, hats, and books.

Local Culture and Customs of Havre de Grace

Havre de Grace is a welcoming and hospitable city that values its local culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the community by observing local customs and traditions, including saying please and thank you to shopkeepers and restaurant staff, which is a customary gesture to show respect.

Best Time to Visit Havre de Grace

The best time to visit Havre de Grace is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the area is not too crowded with tourists. The summer months can be quite hot and humid, and winter months can have snow and icy conditions that make outdoor activities less enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Havre de Grace

  • Do take a leisurely stroll along the Havre de Grace Promenade for some scenic views of the Susquehanna River.
