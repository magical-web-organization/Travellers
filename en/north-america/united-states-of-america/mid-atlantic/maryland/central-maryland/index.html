Central Maryland, located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, is a gem waiting to be discovered. From sprawling nature reserves to bustling cities brimming with history and culture, the region is sure to delight any traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, where to eat, and more in Central Maryland.

Introduction to Central Maryland

Central Maryland is home to Baltimore City, the state’s largest city; Annapolis, the state capital; and a variety of suburbs and counties that are rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. With a blend of urban and rural environments, Central Maryland is a great destination for travelers with diverse interests.

List of Places to Visit in Central Maryland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Central Maryland is steeped in history, and you can experience it firsthand by visiting its museums and historical sites. The following are some of the top cultural attractions:

  • Baltimore Museum of Art: home to an impressive collection of European and American artworks.
  • The Walters Art Museum: a diverse collection of art that spans over 7,000 years from around the world.
  • The National Great Blacks In Wax Museum: discover the history, culture, and struggle for freedom and justice of black people.
  • Maryland State House: located in Annapolis and the oldest state capitol in continuous legislative use.
  • Fort McHenry: a national monument and historic shrine that played a pivotal role in the War of 1812 and inspired Francis Scott Key to write “The Star-Spangled Banner.”

Natural Attractions of Central Maryland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are a nature lover, then Central Maryland’s abundance of parks and outdoor spaces are perfect for you. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Assateague Island National Seashore: home to wild horses, pristine beaches, and diverse wildlife.
  • Patapsco Valley State Park: a large park that offers everything from hiking and mountain biking trails to fishing, camping, and canoeing.
  • Deep Creek Lake: the largest inland body of water in Maryland, surrounded by forested mountains and offering opportunities for boating, fishing, skiing, and other water sports.
  • Catoctin Mountain Park: a hiker’s paradise with miles of trails that lead to waterfalls, vistas, and historic sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Maryland

Central Maryland offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. Here are some of the most scenic and popular ones:

  • Gwynns Falls Trail: a paved, 15-mile trail that runs from Baltimore City to the suburb of Owings Mills.
  • North Central Railroad Trail: a 20-mile trail that follows the route of a historic railway line and offers views of the countryside.
  • Billy Goat Trail: a scenic hiking trail in the Chesapeake and Ohio Canal National Historical Park that takes you along the Potomac River.
  • Annapolis Biking and Walking Trail: a 13.5-mile trail that takes you through the historic capital city of Annapolis.

Best Things to Do in Central Maryland: Activities and Tours

In addition to its cultural and natural attractions, Central Maryland also offers a host of activities and tours that are worth checking out. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Boat tours: take a cruise on the Chesapeake Bay or the Potomac River and experience Central Maryland’s coastline from a different perspective.
  • Horseback riding: explore the outdoors on horseback at Patapsco Valley State Park or Prettyboy Reservoir.
  • Brewery tours: Maryland’s craft beer scene is booming, and many breweries offer tours and tastings.
  • Ghost tours: if you are interested in the paranormal, then you will want to check out the ghost tours in Annapolis and Baltimore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Maryland

If you have a week to explore Central Maryland, then consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: start in Baltimore City and visit the Baltimore Museum of Art, the Walters Art Museum, and the National Great Blacks In Wax Museum.
  • Day 2: head to Annapolis and tour the Maryland State House and the U.S. Naval Academy.
  • Day 3-4: explore the natural beauty of Central Maryland by hiking in Patapsco Valley State Park, kayaking on the Chesapeake Bay, and visiting Deep Creek Lake.
  • Day 5-6: take a brewery tour and a ghost tour, and experience the nightlife in Annapolis and Baltimore.
  • Day 7: shop for souvenirs and local products in Central Maryland before catching your flight home.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Maryland

Must-Try Dishes

Central Maryland offers a wide range of culinary delights, from fresh seafood to soul food. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Crab cakes: a Maryland specialty made with fresh crab meat, herbs, and spices.
  • Old Bay seasoning: a blend of herbs and spices that is used to flavor crab, shrimp, and other seafood.
  • Pit beef: thinly sliced beef that is slow-cooked over wood coals and served on a Kaiser roll.
  • Berger cookies: a local delicacy that consists of a shortbread cookie topped with a thick layer of chocolate fudge.

Local Beverages

Maryland is also known for its libations, including the following local beverages:

  • National Bohemian (Natty Boh): a beer that originated in Baltimore in the 1880s and has since become a Maryland icon.
  • Sagamore Spirit Rye Whiskey: a smooth and spicy whiskey that is made in Baltimore.
  • Maryland-style Bloody Mary: a cocktail made with Old Bay seasoning and garnished with crab meat.

Where to Stay in Central Maryland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Central Maryland has a variety of options for accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Four Seasons Hotel Baltimore: a luxury hotel in the heart of Baltimore with stunning views of the harbor.
  • The Westin Annapolis: a modern hotel in the heart of downtown Annapolis.
  • HI Baltimore Hostel: a budget-friendly hostel that is perfect for solo travelers and backpackers.
  • Gramercy Mansion bed and breakfast: a charming guesthouse in Baltimore that offers a homey feel and personalized service.

What to Buy in Central Maryland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, then consider the following options:

  • Crab-themed merchandise: from T-shirts to mugs to magnets, you can find all sorts of souvenirs that celebrate Maryland’s love for crabs.
  • Old Bay seasoning: bring home a bottle or two of this iconic spice blend and use it to flavor your own crab cakes and other seafood dishes.
  • Local art and crafts: from pottery to paintings to handmade jewelry, there are plenty of local artists and artisans in Central Maryland.
  • Sagamore Spirit Rye Whiskey: bring home a bottle of this award-winning Maryland-made whiskey and impress your friends with your new cocktail-making skills.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Maryland

Central Maryland has a unique culture and customs that are worth noting. Residents tend to be friendly and welcoming, and there is a strong emphasis on community. Sports are also a big part of the local culture, with a strong following for the Maryland Terrapins and Baltimore Ravens. Additionally, the area has a rich African-American history and culture that is celebrated through events and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Central Maryland

The best time to visit Central Maryland is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy. However, each season offers its own unique charm and events, so it really depends on your preferences and what you want to experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Maryland

Do:

  • Try the crab cakes: as mentioned earlier, crab cakes are a Maryland staple and a must-try dish.
  • Visit the museums: Central Maryland is home to a rich cultural history that is worth exploring.
  • Take a ghost tour: the region is known for its haunted history, so take advantage of the ghost tours.
  • Explore the outdoors: from hiking to kayaking to fishing, Central Maryland offers a variety of outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Forget your sunscreen: summers can get hot and sunny, so make sure you protect your skin.
  • Be rude: Southern hospitality is alive and well in Central Maryland, so be polite and courteous.
  • Litter: keep the parks and natural areas clean by disposing of trash properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Maryland

Central Maryland is a generally safe region, but like any place, it has its share of crime and safety concerns. Be vigilant in urban areas, carry your belongings close to you, and be aware of your surroundings. Take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities, as weather and terrain can pose safety risks.

Health in Central Maryland

It is always a good idea to check with your doctor before traveling, but typically, there are no major health risks in Central Maryland. Make sure you stay hydrated, protect yourself from the sun, and take any necessary precautions for allergies or chronic health conditions.

Getting to Central Maryland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Central Maryland is accessible by a variety of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Central Maryland

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI): located in Baltimore County and the main airport for the region.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA): located in Arlington, Virginia, and about an hour’s drive from Central Maryland.
  • Washington Dulles International Airport (IAD): located in Dulles, Virginia, and about an hour and a half’s drive from Central Maryland.

Bus Stations Near Central Maryland

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: several bus stations in and around Central Maryland.
  • Megabus: several bus stations in and around Central Maryland.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: several train stations in and around Central Maryland.
  • Car rental: numerous car rental companies are located in the region.

How to Get Around in Central Maryland

Central Maryland is easy to navigate by car, but there are also several other transportation options available:

  • Light rail: a convenient way to get around Baltimore City.
  • MTA buses: an affordable way to get around Baltimore City and the surrounding suburbs.
  • Water taxis: a fun and unique way to get around Baltimore’s Inner Harbor.
  • Bike rentals: several bike rental companies are located throughout the region.

Where to Go Next from Central Maryland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time or want to explore nearby destinations, then consider some of these options:

  • Washington, D.C.: the nation’s capital is only a short drive or train ride away.
  • Gettysburg, Pennsylvania: the historic battlefield and charming downtown are worth a visit.
  • Bethany Beach, Delaware: a popular beach destination that is only a few hours’ drive from Central Maryland.

Conclusion

Central Maryland is a vibrant and dynamic region that offers something for everyone, from history and culture to nature and adventure. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Central Maryland.
