Introduction to Annapolis

Annapolis is a charming city located in Central Maryland, United States of America. This historic town is the capital of Maryland and is known for its rich maritime heritage, historic landmarks, scenic beauty, and vibrant culture. The city is situated on the shores of Chesapeake Bay, which is famous for its seafood, boating, and water sports. In this guide, we’ll take you on a trip to Annapolis, and show you some of the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and local cuisine the city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Annapolis

If you’re planning a trip to Annapolis, here are some of the top attractions you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • United States Naval Academy Museum: This museum features exhibits related to the history of the Naval Academy, the accomplishments of its graduates, the evolution of the U.S. Navy, and more.
  • Maryland State House: Built in 1772, Maryland State House is the oldest state capitol still in continuous legislative use.
  • William Paca House and Garden: This 18th-century mansion is a prime example of Georgian architecture and features a beautiful terraced garden.
  • Hammond-Harwood House: This historic home features an impressive collection of art and furniture from the Federal period.
  • Banneker-Douglass Museum: This museum celebrates the rich cultural legacy of African Americans in Maryland.

Natural Attractions of Annapolis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Quiet Waters Park: This park is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and has a lake for fishing, a dog park, and extensive trails for hiking and biking.
  • Sandy Point State Park: This park is located on the northwestern shore of Chesapeake Bay and offers beautiful sandy beaches, a marina, hiking, and birdwatching.
  • Thomas Point Park Lighthouse: This historic lighthouse is located on a small island at the mouth of the South River, and it’s a perfect spot for a picnic or to watch the sunset.
  • Annapolis Towne Centre: This outdoor shopping center boasts amazing views of the Chesapeake Bay and is perfect for an afternoon stroll while shopping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Annapolis

Annapolis has several scenic hiking and biking trails for nature-lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Here’s a list of some of the most popular routes:

  • Baltimore & Annapolis (B&A) Trail: This 13.3-mile trail stretches from Glen Burnie to Annapolis and passes through several historic sites and parks.
  • Quiet Waters Park Trail: This scenic three-mile trail is perfect for leisurely walks or short hikes and has beautiful views of the South River.
  • Sandy Point State Park Trail: This trail is a 4.7-mile loop that offers stunning views of the Chesapeake Bay and the Bay Bridge.
  • Waterworks Trail: This trail is a 6.2-mile loop that features beautiful woods and water views, and also has access to the South River and Chesapeake Bay.

Best Things to Do in Annapolis: Activities and Tours

Annapolis offers a wide range of fun activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do while you’re in town:

  • Take a boat tour of Chesapeake Bay: This is a great way to explore the bay and see some of the local sights and wildlife.
  • Visit the Annapolis Maritime Museum: This museum offers interactive exhibits related to the maritime history of Annapolis.
  • Enjoy a sunset sail: There are several tour operators in town that offer sunset sails on the Chesapeake Bay.
  • Savor the local cuisine: Annapolis is known for its seafood, so make sure to try some of the local favorites like crab cakes, oysters, and rockfish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Annapolis

If you have a week to spend in Annapolis, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Visit the United States Naval Academy
Day 2 Explore the Maryland State House and take a walking tour of downtown Annapolis
Day 3 Take a boat tour of the Chesapeake Bay
Day 4 Visit the William Paca House and Garden and enjoy a picnic at Quiet Waters Park
Day 5 Take a hike or bike ride along one of Annapolis’s scenic trails
Day 6 Enjoy some water sports or relax on the beach at Sandy Point State Park
Day 7 Savor the local cuisine on a food tour of Annapolis

Food and Drink Scene of Annapolis

Annapolis offers a unique blend of local flavors, fresh seafood, and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Annapolis is known for its delicious crab cakes, which are made with seasoned lump crab meat and served with a side of coleslaw and potatoes.
  • Oysters: Fresh, local oysters are a must-try if you’re a fan of seafood. You can try them raw, fried, steamed, or Rockefeller-style.
  • Rockfish: Also known as striped bass, rockfish is a local favorite and is typically served grilled or broiled with a side of vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Nautical Gin: This locally-made gin is infused with botanicals from the Chesapeake Bay region and has a mild citrus flavor.
  • Sailor’s Mouth: This pale ale is brewed with Maryland-grown hops and has a light, crisp taste.
  • Crushes: This refreshing cocktail is made with fresh-squeezed fruit juice, vodka, and soda water, and is a signature drink in Annapolis.

Where to Stay in Annapolis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Annapolis has several accommodation options that cater to tourists of all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Loews Annapolis Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of downtown Annapolis and features beautifully-appointed rooms, an on-site restaurant, and fitness center.
  • Graduate Annapolis: This boutique hotel is located near the Annapolis waterfront and features nautical-themed rooms, a rooftop bar, and an on-site café.
  • Flag House Inn: This 19th-century guesthouse is a charming bed and breakfast located in the historic district of Annapolis and features period décor, cozy rooms, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Annapolis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Annapolis has several unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or mementos of your trip. Here are some of the best options:

  • Chesapeake Bay-inspired jewelry: Annapolis has several local jewelers that offer unique, nautical-themed jewelry made with seashells, gemstones, and pearls.
  • Maryland Flag Merchandise: The Maryland State Flag is a unique and colorful symbol of Maryland, and you’ll find it on everything from t-shirts and hats to coffee mugs and keychains.
  • Local art and crafts: Annapolis has a vibrant arts community, and you’ll find plenty of local artisans selling handmade items like pottery, paintings, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Annapolis

Annapolis prides itself on its rich history, love of the water, and friendly locals. The city also has a few customs and traditions that are worth noting, such as:

  • Crab-picking etiquette: If you’re invited to a crab feast, it’s important to learn how to properly pick a crab and not waste any meat.
  • Respecting the Naval Academy: The U.S. Naval Academy is an important institution in Annapolis, and it’s important to show respect and honor the customs of the Academy.
  • Water safety: Boating and water sports are popular in Annapolis, and it’s important to take proper safety precautions when enjoying the water.

Best Time to Visit Annapolis

The best time to visit Annapolis is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be quite hot and humid, and it’s peak tourist season, so prices may be higher, and attractions might be more crowded. Winter in Annapolis is also charming, but keep in mind that some attractions may be closed or have limited hours due to the weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Annapolis

Do:

  • Take a boat ride on the Chesapeake Bay
  • Try the local seafood
  • Visit the U.S. Naval Academy
  • Attend a sailboat race or regatta
  • Take a walking tour of downtown Annapolis

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the Chesapeake Bay
  • Park in residential areas without a permit
  • Disturb or damage historic landmarks or sites
  • Feed the seagulls
  • Skip the sunscreen

Safety and Health

Safety in Annapolis

Annapolis is generally a safe city, but it’s recommended to take the usual precautions, such as locking your car and securing valuables. It’s also a good idea to stay in well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Annapolis

Annapolis has several hospitals and medical facilities, including Anne Arundel Medical Center, which is ranked as one of the top hospitals in Maryland. It’s recommended to have health insurance and consult with a doctor or travel clinic before visiting Annapolis.

Getting to Annapolis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Annapolis is located about 30 miles southeast of Baltimore and 25 miles east of Washington, D.C. Here are some transportation options to get to Annapolis:

Airports Near Annapolis

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI): This airport is located about 24 miles from Annapolis and is served by multiple airlines.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA): This airport is located about 38 miles from Annapolis and is served by multiple airlines.

Bus Stations Near Annapolis

  • Annapolis Greyhound Bus Station: This station is located in downtown Annapolis and offers service to multiple destinations in the U.S.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Amtrak trains stop at BWI station, which is located about 22 miles from Annapolis.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are available in Annapolis.

How to Get Around in Annapolis

Annapolis is a pedestrian-friendly city, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance in the historic district. If you need to travel further, you can rent a bike, take a taxi, or use a ride-sharing service. There are also several parking garages and lots throughout the city, but parking can be limited and expensive during peak tourist season.

Where to Go Next from Annapolis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Annapolis is located near several other popular destinations in the region. Here are some nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Baltimore: This bustling city is located about 30 miles from Annapolis and is known for its world-famous Inner Harbor, museums, and delicious food scene.
  • Washington, D.C.: The nation’s capital is located about 40 miles from Annapolis and offers endless opportunities for sightseeing, history, and culture.
  • Eastern Shore: This region is located on the other side of the Chesapeake Bay and offers beautiful beaches, water sports, and historic towns.

Conclusion

Annapolis is a charming city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in exploring historic landmarks, hiking along scenic trails, or savoring the local cuisine, Annapolis has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide will help you plan your visit to this beautiful city and make the most of your trip!
