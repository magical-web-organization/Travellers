Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

La Plata has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places that reflect its past. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in La Plata:

  • Port Tobacco Courthouse: This historic courthouse was once the seat of government for Charles County. Today, it serves as a museum and a testament to the town’s long and storied history. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the landmark cases that were tried here.
  • Thomas Stone National Historic Site: Located just outside La Plata, this site was the home of Thomas Stone, one of Maryland’s signers of the Declaration of Independence. Visitors can tour the home, gardens, and grounds to learn more about the life and legacy of this founding father.
  • College of Southern Maryland: This community college is home to the Tony Hungerford Memorial Art Gallery, which showcases a rotating collection of contemporary art exhibitions throughout the year. It also hosts performances and lectures by local and visiting artists.

Natural Attractions of La Plata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

La Plata offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in La Plata:

  • Gilbert Run Park: This park is a popular destination for hiking, camping, and fishing. The park also has a large man-made lake, perfect for swimming and boating.
  • Friendship Farm Park: This park offers picturesque trails for hiking and biking, along with fields for soccer, baseball, and other outdoor activities.
  • Chapman State Park: Located just outside of La Plata, this park is a great place for a scenic drive, picnic, or hike. Visitors can enjoy the park’s diverse wildlife and stunning views of the Potomac River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Plata

La Plata has several scenic paths and trails for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Indian Head Rail Trail: This 13-mile trail is a popular destination for biking, hiking, and jogging. It is relatively easy, making it perfect for families and casual riders.
  • Cedarville State Forest: This park offers several hiking trails through dense woods, streams, and wildlife habitats. Visitors can also camp, fish, and picnic in the park.
  • Labrot Lane Trail: This 3-mile trail offers stunning views of the Port Tobacco River and is perfect for a casual hike or jog.

Best Things to Do in La Plata: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do in La Plata:

  • Potomac Jazz & Seafood Festival: This annual festival celebrates jazz music and delicious seafood. The festival also features craft vendors, children’s activities, and free boat rides.
  • College of Southern Maryland Theater: The CSM theater is home to several productions throughout the year, including plays, musicals, and one-act plays.
  • Regency Furniture Stadium: This stadium is home to the Southern Maryland Blue Crabs baseball team. Visitors can enjoy a game, along with delicious food and drinks.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Plata

Here is a week-long itinerary for La Plata:

  • Day 1: Visit the Port Tobacco Courthouse and the Port Tobacco Village historical sites.
  • Day 2: Explore the Indian Head Rail Trail for a day of outdoor adventure.
  • Day 3: Take a trip to Thomas Stone National Historic Site and the College of Southern Maryland art gallery.
  • Day 4: Hike the Cedarville State Forest trails and enjoy a picnic in the park.
  • Day 5: Attend the Potomac Jazz & Seafood Festival for a night of music and delicious food.
  • Day 6: Watch a production at the College of Southern Maryland Theater.
  • Day 7: Visit Chapman State Park for scenic views of the Potomac River.

Food and Drink Scene of La Plata

La Plata offers a range of culinary delights, from classic Southern favorites to contemporary global cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southern Fried Chicken: A staple comfort food dish in the South, fried chicken is a must-try in La Plata.
  • Crab Cakes: La Plata is known for its delicious crab cakes, made with fresh and local crab meat.
  • Stuffed Ham: A Maryland specialty, stuffed ham is a unique dish made with ham, kale, and other seasonings stuffed inside a ham skin.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: La Plata is home to several craft breweries, including the Olde Town Pub and Mully’s Brewery.
  • Sweet Tea: Sweet tea is a popular Southern drink made with brewed tea and sweetened with sugar.
  • Local Wine: Charles County, where La Plata is located, has several wineries that offer tasting tours throughout the year.

Local Culture and Customs of La Plata

La Plata is a welcoming and diverse town, with a rich history and storied past. It is important to respect the local culture and customs during your visit. Here are some tips:

  • Dress appropriately: La Plata is a laid-back town, but it’s important to dress appropriately for the occasion. Dress casually for outdoor activities, but bring something more formal for special events or attractions.
  • Be respectful: La Plata is a friendly town, and residents are proud of their heritage. Be respectful of the local customs and traditions, and always ask before taking photos or recording videos.
  • Tip appropriately: Tipping is a common practice in La Plata, especially in restaurants and bars. Tip at least 15% for good service, and adjust upward if the service was exceptional.

Best Time to Visit La Plata

The best time to visit La Plata is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and snowy. Keep in mind that the Potomac River can freeze over in the winter, making outdoor activities more difficult.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Plata

La Plata is a safe town, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, it’s always good to take common-sense safety precautions, especially when exploring the outdoor areas or after dark. Always be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in La Plata

La Plata has several medical centers and hospitals in the area, including the Civista Medical Center and the University of Maryland Charles Regional Medical Center. It’s always important to have proper medical insurance when traveling, and to take necessary medications and vaccines before arriving.
