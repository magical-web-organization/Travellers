Introduction to Leonardtown

Located in Southern Maryland, Leonardtown is a charming town that’s steeped in history and natural beauty. This small town boasts a picturesque downtown, waterfront vistas, and plenty of recreational opportunities.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover the best things to do in Leonardtown, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Leonardtown

Leonardtown has something for everyone, with a diverse range of cultural and natural attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit in Leonardtown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. St. Mary’s County Historical Society Museum is a great place to learn about the history of Leonardtown and St. Mary’s County.
  2. The Old Jail Museum is a historic jail that has been converted into a museum, showcasing the lives of prisoners who were held there.
  3. Tudor Hall is a historic home that was once owned by a prominent family in Leonardtown.

Natural Attractions of Leonardtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Leonardtown Wharf Park is a waterfront park that offers beautiful views of Breton Bay. It has walking trails and picnic areas, making it an ideal spot for a day out.
  2. St. Clement’s Island State Park is a natural island located in the Potomac River that you can access by boat. The park has hiking trails and picnic areas, offering stunning views of the river and the surrounding area.
  3. Newtowne Neck State Park is a natural park located on the banks of the Potomac River. It’s great for hiking, bird watching, kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leonardtown

Leonardtown has some great hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  1. Leonardtown Town Creek Park Trail
  2. Tudor Hall Trail
  3. St. Clement’s Island Trail

Best Things to Do in Leonardtown: Activities and Tours

  1. Sotterley Plantation offers tours of this historic plantation, showcasing the history of the region and the lives of the people who lived there.
  2. Maryland International Raceway is a great spot for car enthusiasts. The raceway hosts drag races and other events throughout the year.
  3. Patuxent Adventure Center offers guided kayak tours of the Patuxent River, showcasing the natural beauty of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Leonardtown

If you’re planning to spend a week in Leonardtown, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Leonardtown

Leonardtown has a great food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stuffed Ham is a traditional Southern Maryland dish that consists of ham stuffed with kale and other vegetables.
  2. Crab Cakes are a local specialty, with fresh crabmeat and a crispy outside and a tender center.
  3. Fried Chicken is a Southern classic, with crispy skin and juicy meat.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Check out the craft beer scene at the many brewpubs and bars in Leonardtown. Patuxent Brewing Company and Leonardtown Brewing Company are two of the most popular breweries in the area.
  2. Wine: Visit Port of Leonardtown Winery, where you’ll find award-winning wines made with grapes grown in Southern Maryland.

Where to Stay in Leonardtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Leonardtown has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly options to luxurious guesthouses.

  1. The Victorian Candle Bed & Breakfast is a charming guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a hearty breakfast.
  2. Comfort Inn and Holiday Inn Express are two of the most popular hotel chains in Leonardtown, offering great amenities at an affordable price.
  3. Airbnb also has a range of properties available, from apartments to cozy cottages.

What to Buy in Leonardtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Leonardtown has a range of unique local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some ideas for what to buy:

  1. St. Mary’s County Oysters are a local delicacy, with a sweet and tender meat.
  2. Jams and Jellies made with local fruits are a great souvenir to bring home, with flavors like strawberry, raspberry, and peach.
  3. Local Artwork: Check out the art galleries in Leonardtown, where you’ll find unique pieces of art made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Leonardtown

Leonardtown prides itself on its rich history and culture. The locals are friendly, and place a great emphasis on community and tradition. Make sure to be respectful and mindful of local customs during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Leonardtown

Leonardtown is a great destination year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the trees are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leonardtown

Do:

  1. Visit St. Mary’s County Historical Society Museum and Old Jail Museum to learn about the history of the area.
  2. Try the local food and drink specialties, like crab cakes and local beer.
  3. Take advantage of the outdoor recreational opportunities, like hiking and kayaking.

Don’t:

  1. Ignore local customs and traditions.
  2. Litter or damage the natural environment.
  3. Be disrespectful to locals or fellow tourists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Leonardtown

Leonardtown is a safe town, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take necessary precautions.

Health in Leonardtown

Leonardtown has several hospitals and medical facilities in the area, making it a great place to visit if you have any health concerns.

Getting to Leonardtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Leonardtown is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

  2. Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI)

Bus Stations Near Leonardtown

Other Options

How to Get Around in Leonardtown

Leonardtown is a walkable town, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Biking is also a popular option, and there are several bike rental shops in the area.

Where to Go Next from Leonardtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Maryland, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Annapolis is a historic town located on the Chesapeake Bay, known for its colonial architecture and naval history.
  2. Assateague Island National Seashore is a barrier island located off the coast of Maryland and Virginia, offering beautiful beaches, surf fishing and wild ponies.
  3. Washington D.C. is the capital of the United States, and home to many iconic landmarks, museums, and monuments.

Conclusion

Leonardtown is a charming town that offers a range of cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor recreation, or local food and drink specialties, Leonardtown has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be able to plan a trip that will help you make the most of your visit to this gem of a town.
