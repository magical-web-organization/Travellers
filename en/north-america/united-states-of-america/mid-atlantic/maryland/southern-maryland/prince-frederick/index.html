Introduction to Prince Frederick

Located in the heart of Southern Maryland, Prince Frederick is a charming town that offers a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. Known for its rich history and stunning natural beauty, Prince Frederick is an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore and experience the best of Maryland and the Mid-Atlantic region. Here’s a comprehensive guide to everything you need to know about visiting Prince Frederick.

List of Places to Visit in Prince Frederick

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking to relax and unwind, Prince Frederick has a diverse range of attractions and activities to offer. Here are some of the top places to visit in Prince Frederick:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Calvert Marine Museum - Discover the maritime history of Southern Maryland through interactive exhibits, displays, and live animals.
  2. Jefferson Patterson Park & Museum - Explore the rich archaeological history of Maryland and learn about the native cultures that once called Southern Maryland home.
  3. Old Village Shop - A charming and historic shop that offers unique gifts, antiques, and collectibles.
  4. Patuxent River Naval Air Museum - Learn about the history of flight in the U.S. Navy and see vintage aircraft up close.

Natural Attractions of Prince Frederick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Battle Creek Cypress Swamp - A tranquil and scenic boardwalk trail through a rare bald cypress forest.
  2. Flag Ponds Nature Park - A beautiful beach and nature preserve that offers swimming, hiking, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  3. Jordan Pond - A serene and picturesque lake surrounded by wooded trails and wildlife viewing areas.
  4. Southern Maryland Blue Crabs Stadium - Catch a baseball game at this charming and family-friendly stadium.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prince Frederick

Prince Frederick offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore its stunning natural beauty. Here are some of the top routes:

  1. Calvert Cliffs State Park Trail - A scenic trail that takes hikers to the beach and offers stunning views of the Chesapeake Bay.
  2. Battle Creek Cypress Swamp Trail - A tranquil boardwalk trail that winds through a rare bald cypress forest.
  3. Solomons Island Loop Trail - A scenic and easy trail that takes hikers through the charming town of Solomons Island and offers stunning views of the Patuxent River.
  4. Flag Ponds Nature Park Loop Trail - A beautiful trail that takes hikers through a diverse range of ecosystems, including beaches, forests, and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Prince Frederick: Activities and Tours

From guided tours to water-based activities, Prince Frederick offers a variety of experiences for all types of travelers. Here are some of the top things to do in Prince Frederick:

  1. Chesapeake Beach Water Park - A family-friendly water park that offers slides, pools, and lazy rivers.
  2. Canoe the Patuxent - A guided canoe tour through the beautiful and scenic Patuxent River.
  3. Herrington Harbour Sailing School - Learn to sail or take a guided tour of the Chesapeake Bay on a sailboat.
  4. Annmarie Sculpture Garden & Arts Center - An outdoor sculpture garden and arts center that offers a variety of exhibits and events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prince Frederick

Day 1 - Explore the historic town of Prince Frederick and visit the Old Village Shop. Day 2 - Take a guided tour of the Calvert Marine Museum. Day 3 - Go hiking at the Calvert Cliffs State Park Trail. Day 4 - Visit the Jefferson Patterson Park & Museum to learn about the indigenous cultures of Southern Maryland. Day 5 - Take a guided canoe tour of the Patuxent River. Day 6 - Relax at the Flag Ponds Nature Park. Day 7 - Take a sailing lesson at the Herrington Harbour Sailing School.

Food and Drink Scene of Prince Frederick

Prince Frederick offers a variety of delicious local cuisine and beverages that are worth trying during your visit. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Crab cakes - A Maryland classic dish made with fresh crab meat.
  2. Fried chicken - A Southern favorite that’s crispy and juicy.
  3. Steamed clams - A local delicacy that’s served with butter and garlic.
  4. Sweet corn - Fresh and sweet corn is a staple of Maryland cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Maryland craft beer - A variety of local breweries offer unique and delicious brews that are worth trying.
  2. Maryland wine - The region offers a variety of vineyards and wineries that produce locally-grown wines.
  3. Old Bay bloody mary - A Maryland twist on a classic cocktail, made with Old Bay seasoning.
  4. Natty Boh - A classic Maryland beer brand that’s been around for over 135 years.

Where to Stay in Prince Frederick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Prince Frederick offers a variety of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top options:

  1. SpringHill Suites by Marriott Prince Frederick - A modern and stylish hotel that offers a variety of amenities, including an indoor pool and complimentary breakfast.
  2. Holiday Inn Express Prince Frederick - A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and complimentary breakfast.
  3. Solomons Victorian Inn - A charming and historic bed and breakfast that offers a unique and personalized experience.

What to Buy in Prince Frederick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or local products to bring home, Prince Frederick offers a variety of options. Here are some of the top places to shop:

  1. Annmarie Gift Shop - A shop that offers a variety of unique and handmade gifts, including pottery and jewelry.
  2. Winegardner’s Wines & Spirits - A locally-owned wine and spirits shop that offers a variety of locally-made products.
  3. Calvert Kettle Corn - A popcorn shop that offers sweet and savory popcorn flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Prince Frederick

Maryland has a unique culture and history, and Prince Frederick is no exception. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit:

  1. Maryland is known for its delicious seafood, so make sure to try some fresh crab cakes or steamed crabs during your visit.
  2. The Chesapeake Bay is a major part of Maryland’s identity and economy, and taking part in water-based activities is a must-do.
  3. Maryland is known for its lacrosse, so if you get a chance, catch a game or learn more about the sport during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Prince Frederick

The best time to visit Prince Frederick is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer is also a popular time to visit, but be prepared for higher temperatures and larger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prince Frederick

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Prince Frederick:

Do:

  • Try the local seafood and craft beer.
  • Explore the outdoors and take part in water-based activities.
  • Visit the local museums and historical sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions, especially when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Leave litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prince Frederick

Prince Frederick is a relatively safe destination, but like any town, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and in a secure location.
  • Stay up to date on local weather and environmental alerts.

Health in Prince Frederick

Visitors to Prince Frederick should take the same health precautions as they would when travelling to any other destination. Here are some tips:

  • Pay attention to any health alerts or warnings issued by local officials.
  • Drink plenty of water and protect your skin from the sun.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health issues.

How to Get Around in Prince Frederick

While having a car is helpful for getting around Prince Frederick, visitors can also take a taxi or use a rideshare service. Biking and walking are also great options for exploring the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Prince Frederick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the area around Prince Frederick, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Annapolis - A historic and charming city that offers a variety of cultural attractions and stunning waterfront views.
  • St. Mary’s City - An authentic and historic colonial village that offers a glimpse into Maryland’s past.
  • Assateague Island National Seashore - A beautiful and remote barrier island that offers stunning beaches and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Prince Frederick, Maryland is a charming and diverse destination that offers a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking to relax and unwind, there’s something for everyone in Prince Frederick. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this beautiful town.
