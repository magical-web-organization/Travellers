Introduction to Lexington Park

Located in Southern Maryland, Lexington Park is a small town with a lot to offer. As the home of the Patuxent River Naval Air Station, the town has a rich naval history that is worth exploring. Visitors can also enjoy natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. In this comprehensive tour guide for Lexington Park, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Lexington Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Patuxent River Naval Air Museum

The Patuxent River Naval Air Museum is a must-visit for aviation enthusiasts. The museum showcases the history of the Patuxent River Naval Air Station, with exhibits on aircraft, weapons, and naval aviation history.

St. Mary’s County Historical Society

The St. Mary’s County Historical Society is a great place to learn about the history of the area. The museum has exhibits on the colonial and maritime history of St. Mary’s County, including artifacts and documents from the 17th and 18th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Lexington Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Point Lookout State Park

Point Lookout State Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, and boating in the Chesapeake Bay. The park also has hiking trails and campsites for those who want to spend the night.

Greenwell State Park

Greenwell State Park is another great outdoor destination in Lexington Park. The park has trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as fishing and boating opportunities. Visitors can also enjoy a picnic in the park’s scenic picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lexington Park

Lexington Park has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the town and its surroundings. Here are two of the most popular ones:

Three Notch Trail

The Three Notch Trail is a 28-mile trail that runs from Charlotte Hall to Hughesville. The trail passes through Lexington Park and offers scenic views of the Patuxent River.

St. Mary’s River State Park

St. Mary’s River State Park has several hiking trails that offer scenic views of the St. Mary’s River. The trails vary in difficulty, and visitors can choose from short walks to longer hikes.

Best Things to Do in Lexington Park: Activities and Tours

Visit a Local Winery

Maryland has a thriving wine industry, and Lexington Park is home to several wineries. Visitors can take a tour of the vineyards and sample local wines at places like Port of Leonardtown Winery and Fridays Creek Winery.

Explore the Patuxent River

The Patuxent River is a central part of Lexington Park’s history and culture. Visitors can take a kayak tour of the river, go fishing, or take a scenic boat ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Lexington Park

Must-Try Dishes

St. Mary’s County Crab Cake

The St. Mary’s County crab cake is a regional specialty that seafood lovers should not miss. The crab cake is made with lump crabmeat and seasoned with Old Bay seasoning.

Southern Maryland Stuffed Ham

Southern Maryland stuffed ham is a unique and delicious dish that is a must-try for foodies. The ham is stuffed with kale, onions, and other seasonings, and then boiled until it is cooked through.

Local Beverages

Maryland Wine

Maryland has a thriving wine industry, and Lexington Park is home to several wineries. Visitors can sample local wines at places like Port of Leonardtown Winery and Fridays Creek Winery.

What to Buy in Lexington Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Mary’s County Flag

The St. Mary’s County flag is a unique and colorful souvenir that is a great way to remember your trip to Lexington Park.

Patuxent River Oysters

Patuxent River oysters are a local specialty that seafood lovers should not miss. Visitors can bring home a bag of fresh oysters to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lexington Park

Lexington Park has a strong naval history and culture. Visitors should be respectful of the naval base and its personnel, and be mindful of any security protocols in place.

Best Time to Visit Lexington Park

The best time to visit Lexington Park is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller.

Getting to Lexington Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The closest airport to Lexington Park is Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport, which is about an hour and a half away by car.

Other Options

Where to Go Next from Lexington Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
