Introduction to Laurel

Located in Prince George’s County, Laurel is a historic city in Maryland that is situated in the Capital Region of the Mid-Atlantic United States. Known for its rich culture, natural beauty, and historical significance, Laurel offers a great experience to tourists of all ages. This comprehensive tour guide will help you explore the top places to visit in Laurel, the best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Laurel

Laurel has a lot to offer in terms of sightseeing and entertainment. Some of the top places to visit in Laurel include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the history of Laurel and the surrounding areas, then you must visit the following museums and historical places:

  • Laurel Museum: Learn about the history of Laurel in this museum that is housed in a restored 19th-century house.
  • Montpelier Mansion: Marvel at the Georgian architecture of this 18th-century plantation house that is now a museum.
  • National Wildlife Visitor Center: Explore the vast natural reserve of the US Fish and Wildlife Service and learn about the local flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Laurel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will be delighted to visit the following natural attractions that Laurel has to offer:

  • Patuxent Research Refuge: A 12,800-acre wildlife refuge that offers hiking and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Supplee Park: A scenic park that offers picnic spots and playgrounds.
  • Muddy Branch Stream Valley Park: A great place for hiking, biking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laurel

Laurel has some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region. Some popular routes include:

  • Laurel Riverfront Trail: A 3.5-mile trail that offers scenic views of the Patuxent River.
  • Patuxent River Trail: A 16-mile trail that passes through the Patuxent Research Refuge and connects to the Laurel Riverfront Trail.
  • The Trolley Trail: A 3-mile trail that follows the historic route of the Laurel Trolley, which operated in the early 20th century.

Best Things to Do in Laurel: Activities and Tours

Laurel offers a variety of fun activities and tours that cater to different interests. Some of the best things to do in Laurel include:

  • Rams Head Live: A popular music venue that hosts local and international artists.
  • Terrapin Adventures: A thrilling adventure park that offers zipline rides, rope courses, and climbing walls.
  • Scuderi’s Gallery: A renowned art gallery that showcases contemporary art and sculpture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Laurel

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for exploring the best of Laurel:

Food and Drink Scene of Laurel

Laurel offers a diverse food and drink scene that caters to all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Maryland is known for its delicious crab cakes, and Laurel is no exception.
  • Old Bay Fries: Made with Old Bay seasoning, these fries are a local favorite.
  • Smith Island Cake: A layered cake made with a thin sponge cake and icing, typically served during special occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Maryland Rye Whiskey: Maryland is known for its rye whiskey, which is produced by several local distilleries.
  • Flying Dog Brewery: A popular local brewery that produces a variety of craft beers.
  • Great Shoals Winery: A local winery that produces fruit and grape wines.

Where to Stay in Laurel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Laurel offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Laurel: A modern hotel located near the Laurel Park racetrack.
  • Sleep Inn and Suites Laurel: A budget-friendly hotel located near the Laurel Museum.
  • Montpelier Bed and Breakfast: A charming guesthouse located near the Montpelier Mansion.

What to Buy in Laurel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or local products to take home, then here are some options to consider:

  • Crab-themed souvenirs: You can find a variety of souvenirs like t-shirts, mugs, and keychains that feature a crab design.
  • Old Bay seasoning: A popular seasoning blend that is used in various dishes, including seafood and fries.
  • Jewelry made with Maryland Blue Crabs: Local artisans make beautiful jewelry with real Maryland blue crabs.

Local Culture and Customs of Laurel

Laurel has a rich cultural heritage that is influenced by African American, Native American, and European traditions. The city hosts various cultural events throughout the year, including the Cherry Blossom Festival and the Riverfest.

Best Time to Visit Laurel

The best time to visit Laurel is during the spring or fall season when the temperature is mild and pleasant. The summer season can be hot and humid, while the winter season can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laurel

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Laurel:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Carry a water bottle and sunscreen when exploring the outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural attractions.
  • Use drugs or engage in other illegal activities.
  • Disrespect the locals or their properties.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to follow when visiting Laurel:

Safety in Laurel

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Avoid walking alone in deserted areas.
  • Secure your valuables and belongings in a safe place.

Health in Laurel

  • Drink filtered or bottled water.
  • Wear sunscreen and mosquito repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • Get medical help if you experience any discomfort or illness.

Getting to Laurel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Laurel is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Some options to consider when planning your trip include:

Airports Near Laurel

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: Located about 25 miles north of Laurel.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport: Located about 26 miles south of Laurel.

Bus Stations Near Laurel

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Washington, DC, about 20 miles south of Laurel.
  • Megabus: Located in Baltimore, about 25 miles north of Laurel.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental agencies are available at the nearby airports and train stations.
  • Uber/Lyft: Ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Laurel

Laurel has a well-connected public transportation system that includes buses and trains. You can also rent a car or bike to explore the city on your own.

Where to Go Next from Laurel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, consider visiting the following nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Washington, DC: The capital city of the US is located about 20 miles south of Laurel and offers countless attractions.
  • Annapolis: A historic city located about 30 miles east of Laurel, known for its colonial architecture and the US Naval Academy.
  • Chesapeake Bay: A large estuary located about 40 miles east of Laurel that offers fishing, boating, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Laurel is a great destination for tourists who want to explore the history, culture, and natural beauty of the region. With its diverse attractions, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, Laurel promises a memorable experience that you will cherish for a lifetime.
