New Carrollton, located in Prince George’s County, is a charming city in the Capital Region of Maryland. With its rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and modern amenities, it has something to offer every traveler. In this comprehensive guide, you will discover the best things to do, places to visit, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and nearby destinations.

Introduction to New Carrollton

Founded in 1952, New Carrollton is a vibrant community that has evolved into a bustling city over the years. With a population of over 12,000 people, it is a diverse city that welcomes visitors with open arms. The city has a rich history that is evident in its many historical and cultural attractions, parks, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in New Carrollton

New Carrollton offers a range of attractions for visitors. Here are some of the places you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Carrollton Historic District: Take a stroll through the historic district of New Carrollton to see some of the city’s finest examples of mid-century Modern architecture. You’ll see residential and commercial buildings designed by some of the most famous architects of the 20th century.
  • Bowie Railroad Museum: This museum celebrates the history of railroading in the region and has a vast collection of vintage trains and equipment.
  • Marietta House Museum: Experience life in the 19th century at this historic mansion, which offers a rare glimpse into the lifestyles of the era’s wealthy and powerful.

Natural Attractions of New Carrollton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Waugh Chapel Park: With its playgrounds, basketball and tennis courts, and walking trails, Waugh Chapel Park offers something for everyone.
  • Patuxent River Park: A popular spot for hiking, fishing, and bird-watching, Patuxent River Park is a must-visit spot for nature lovers.
  • Lake Artemesia: This peaceful lake is an excellent spot for picnicking, jogging, and fishing. You can also rent a boat or kayak to explore the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Carrollton

New Carrollton is surrounded by natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Northwest Branch Trail: This trail stretches over 7 miles and is perfect for both biking and hiking. Along the way, you’ll encounter spectacular views of the natural scenery.
  • Anacostia River Trail: This trail runs for 20 miles and is perfect for those who love long-distance biking journeys. You’ll encounter several landmarks and attractions along the way.
  • Indian Head Rail Trail: This well-preserved trail follows an old railroad bed along the scenic Patuxent River, making it a great spot for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in New Carrollton: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some exciting activities and tours, New Carrollton has plenty to offer. Here are some fun things you can do while you’re in the city:

  • Escape Rooms: Challenge yourself mentally and strategically in one of the city’s many escape rooms that are perfect for groups and families.
  • Bowlero: Take your bowling game to the next level at this fun and modern bowling alley with a bar and a game room as well.
  • Paint and Sip Studios: Unleash your creative side and enjoy some wine and painting at one of these studios with experienced instructors.

Food and Drink Scene of New Carrollton

New Carrollton boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for all tastes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: In Maryland, crab cakes are a must-try dish. Be sure to sample the local version in one of the many seafood restaurants in the city.
  • Old Bay wings: This iconic Maryland seasoning elevates the classic chicken wing to a whole new level.
  • Smith Island cake: This cake, which has over eight layers of sponge cake coated in chocolate ganache, is a Maryland classic.

Local Beverages

  • National Bohemian beer (Natty Boh): This beer, which has been around since 1885, is an iconic Maryland beverage.
  • Blackwater Distilling: Try some locally distilled spirits, such as whiskey, gin, and vodka, at this popular distillery.

What to Buy in New Carrollton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, New Carrollton has a lot to offer. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Crab-themed souvenirs: Whether it’s a keychain or a T-shirt, crab-themed souvenirs are always a hit with visitors.
  • Maryland flag merchandise: With its unique design, the Maryland flag makes for great souvenirs, such as hats, T-shirts, or magnets.
  • Local art and crafts: From pottery to handcrafted jewelry, there’s plenty of local art and crafts to choose from in New Carrollton.

Local Culture and Customs of New Carrollton

New Carrollton is a diverse city that prides itself on its inclusivity. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs, including the emphasis on family values and community involvement.

Best Time to Visit New Carrollton

The best time to visit New Carrollton is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Carrollton

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting New Carrollton:

Safety and Health

Safety in New Carrollton

New Carrollton is a relatively safe city, but visitors are advised to take precautions when traveling.

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Avoid flashing your valuables in public.
  • Take precautions when using public transportation.

Health in New Carrollton

New Carrollton has an excellent healthcare system, with several hospitals and medical clinics throughout the city.
