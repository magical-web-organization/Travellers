Introduction to National Harbor

National Harbor is a charming waterfront community, located in Prince George’s County, Maryland. With a prime location along the Potomac River, it is just minutes away from Washington, D.C. National Harbor offers a vibrant culture, scenic views, delicious food, and a range of shopping and entertainment options. Whether you are visiting for a day or planning a longer stay, National Harbor is the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States.

List of Places to Visit in National Harbor

National Harbor offers a broad range of attractions for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the must-visit places in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of African American History and Culture: This museum explores the rich history and culture of African Americans in the United States.
  • National Air and Space Museum: This museum features the largest collection of historic spacecraft and aircraft in the world.
  • George Washington’s Mount Vernon: Visitors can learn more about the life of the first president of the United States and explore his estate.

Natural Attractions of National Harbor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Capital Wheel: This 180-foot observation wheel offers breathtaking views of the Potomac River and surrounding area from the comfort of an air-conditioned gondola.
  • Cherry Hill Park: This park is situated on a hill overlooking National Harbor and offers stunning views of the cityscape.
  • Potomac River Cruises: Visitors can explore the Potomac River and surrounding areas on a boat tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of National Harbor

National Harbor offers plenty of options for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Mount Vernon Trail: This 18-mile paved trail starts in Arlington, Virginia and passes by Alexandria and Fort Hunt before ending at George Washington’s Mount Vernon estate.
  • Anacostia River Trail: This 20-mile trail runs along the Anacostia River in Washington, D.C. and connects to the Mount Vernon Trail via a bridge.
  • Oxon Hill Trail: This 3-mile loop trail is situated in Oxon Hill Park and offers lovely views of the Potomac River.

Best Things to Do in National Harbor: Activities and Tours

There are a vast array of activities and tours available in National Harbor. Here are a few of the best:

  • MGM National Harbor Casino: This award-winning casino features 3,000 slot machines, poker rooms, and table games.
  • Tanger Outlets: This outdoor shopping mall features over 85 designer and brand name stores.
  • The Awakening Sculpture: This 70-foot sculpture of a giant humanoid is located on the National Harbor beachfront.

Week-Long Itinerary for National Harbor

If you’re planning to spend a week in National Harbor, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of National Harbor

National Harbor has no shortage of delicious dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chesapeake Bay Crab Cakes: These crab cakes are a Maryland specialty and are best enjoyed with Old Bay seasoning.
  • Half-Smoke: This spicy pork and beef sausage is a D.C. delicacy and is served with a variety of toppings.
  • Chicken and Waffles: This soul food classic features crispy fried chicken served with waffles and syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Old Dominion Brewing Company’s Oak Barrel Stout: This rich and creamy stout is brewed in nearby Dover, Delaware.
  • Virginia Viognier: This crisp white wine is grown in nearby vineyards and pairs perfectly with seafood.

Where to Stay in National Harbor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

National Harbor offers a range of accommodation options to fit any budget. Here are a few options:

  • Gaylord National Resort & Convention Center: This luxury resort offers stunning views of the Potomac River and features multiple restaurants, a spa, and an indoor pool.
  • AC Hotel by Marriott National Harbor: This modern hotel is located just steps from Tanger Outlets and offers a rooftop bar with views of the city.
  • Harborside Hotel: This boutique hotel is located in the heart of National Harbor and features comfortable rooms and a fitness center.

What to Buy in National Harbor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, here are some local products to consider:

  • Old Bay Seasoning: This iconic seasoning blend is a staple in Maryland cuisine and can be found at local markets and specialty food stores.
  • Annapolis Wherry Model Boat Kit: These model boat kits are sold at the Annapolis Wherry Boat Museum and make a unique and memorable gift.
  • Chesapeake Bay Candle: These hand-poured candles are made with all-natural ingredients and come in a range of lovely scents.

Local Culture and Customs of National Harbor

National Harbor is a friendly and welcoming community with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Greeting others with a smile and a handshake.
  • Dressing appropriately for the occasion, such as wearing business attire for meetings or formal events.
  • Refraining from using profanity or making derogatory comments about others.

Best Time to Visit National Harbor

National Harbor can be enjoyed year-round, but the best time to visit is between the months of March and November. The weather is mild and pleasant, and many outdoor activities and events are held during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of National Harbor

Do:

  • Plan ahead and make reservations for popular attractions and restaurants.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Pack comfortable shoes for walking and hiking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in public spaces.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or other visitors.
  • Share personal information with strangers.

Safety and Health

National Harbor is generally a safe and healthy destination, but it’s always important to take precautions while traveling.

Safety in National Harbor

  • Keep valuables like wallets, phones, and cameras in a secure location.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay in well-lit areas at night.
  • Use a rideshare service or taxi if you feel unsafe walking alone.

Health in National Harbor

  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen during hot and sunny weather.
  • If you have any medical concerns or emergencies, seek medical attention immediately.

Getting to National Harbor: Transportation Options and Logistics

National Harbor is conveniently located just a few miles south of Washington, D.C. Here are some transportation options to help you get around:

Airports Near National Harbor

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport: This airport is located just 15 minutes away from National Harbor and offers a range of domestic flights.
  • Dulles International Airport: This airport is located 35 miles away from National Harbor and offers international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near National Harbor

  • Union Station: This bus station is located in Washington, D.C. and offers connections to National Harbor via WMATA Metrobus.
  • New Carrollton Amtrak/MARC Station: This train station is located in New Carrollton, Maryland and offers connections to National Harbor via WMATA Metrobus.

Other Options

  • Water Taxi: Visitors can take a water taxi from several locations in Alexandria and Washington, D.C. directly to National Harbor.
  • Car Rental: Multiple car rental agencies are located near National Harbor and are open seven days a week.

How to Get Around in National Harbor

National Harbor is conveniently located within walking distance of many of its attractions. Visitors can also take advantage of the free circulator shuttle, which runs on a loop around the harbor and stops at various points of interest.

Where to Go Next from National Harbor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the surrounding areas, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Washington, D.C.: Just a short drive or train ride away, visitors can explore the nation’s capital and its many attractions.
  • Annapolis, Maryland: This charming historic town is located just 30 miles east of National Harbor and offers a range of colonial-era architecture and history.
  • Assateague Island National Seashore: This protected area in Maryland offers visitors the chance to see wild horses and explore beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

National Harbor is a unique and vibrant destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, getting active outdoors, or indulging in delicious food and drink, National Harbor is the perfect location for a memorable trip. Start planning your itinerary today and discover all that this beautiful location has to offer!
