Introduction to Beltsville

Beltsville is a small town located in Prince George’s County, Maryland. Situated in the Capital Region of the United States of America, Beltsville offers visitors a quaint yet culturally rich experience. Known for its scenic views, outdoor attractions, and historical landmarks, Beltsville is a hidden gem that guarantees an unforgettable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Beltsville

Beltsville has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor activities, this charming town will not disappoint. Here are the top places to visit in Beltsville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beltsville Agricultural Research Center: This is a state-of-the-art research facility located in Beltsville. The center is responsible for a significant amount of research that contributes to the production of food and fiber worldwide. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the research work done in the center.
  • Montpelier Mansion: Montpelier Mansion is a historical landmark located in Beltsville. The mansion was built in 1783 and is now a museum that provides visitors with an opportunity to delve into the lives of early 19th-century Americans.
  • National Agricultural Library: Known as the world’s largest agricultural library, it has a vast collection of books, periodicals, photographs, and other resources that highlight the history of agriculture and agrarian societies in the US.
  • St. John’s Episcopal Church: This historic church was built in 1839 and features beautiful stained glass windows and intricate woodwork details.

Natural Attractions of Beltsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beltsville Lake: Perfect for fishing and boating enthusiasts, Beltsville Lake is a beautiful spot for picnics and relaxation in nature. The paved trails around the lake make for an excellent hike or bike ride.
  • Little Paint Branch Trail: This trail is popular among hikers and bikers and runs through the Paint Branch Stream Valley Park. Experience scenic views of natural wildlife and landscapes. A trail bike can be rented at the Little Paint Branch Visitor’s Center.
  • Greenbelt Park: This is a natural oasis in the heart of the city. An excellent destination for hikers, bird watchers and nature enthusiasts featuring scenic trails, picnic spots, and wildlife sightings.
  • Wheaton Regional Park: The park offers a variety of activities, including hiking and horseback riding. Visitors can relax with a picnic while admiring the natural beauty of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beltsville

Looking for an adventure on foot or two wheels? Beltsville has an abundance of hiking and biking trails, suitable for all levels of fitness, so lace up your boots or rent a bike and hit the trails. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Beltsville Agricultural Research Center Circuit: This trail is perfect for nature lovers as it takes you through the beautiful farmlands of the Beltsville Agricultural Research Center.
  • Little Paint Branch Trail: This trail runs through the Paint Branch Stream Valley Park, and you’ll get to experience scenic views of natural wildlife and landscapes as you bike or hike.
  • Anacostia Tributary Trail System: A network of trails that connect parts of Maryland and Washington D.C., and a perfect route for biking or walking.

Best Things to Do in Beltsville: Activities and Tours

Make the most of your time in Beltsville by experiencing these top activities and tours:

  • A Visit to the Beltsville Agricultural Research Center: If you’re interested in agronomy, botany, entomology, and other agricultural sciences, then this is a must-visit attraction.
  • Horseback Riding at Wheaton Park Stables: Saddle up and go for a scenic horseback ride through the beautiful Wheaton Regional Park.
  • Beltsville Community Pool: Cool off from the Maryland heat by taking a dip in the Beltsville Community Pool.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beltsville

Week-long itinerary suggestions:

Day 1: Visit National Agricultural Research Center and St. John’s Episcopal Church Day 2: Go on the Little Paint Branch Trail Hike or Bike Day 3: Take a Horseback Riding tour at Wheaton Park Stables Day 4: Visit Montpelier Mansion and have a picnic at Beltsville Lake Day 5: Explore Greenbelt Park and have a picnic Day 6: Visit the National Agricultural Library and have lunch at a local restaurant or food truck. Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at the Beltsville Community Pool.

Food and Drink Scene of Beltsville

Beltsville has a diverse food scene that offers something for everyone. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Made from locally sourced crab meat, this Maryland staple is a must-try dish.
  • Smith Island Cake: A classic chocolate cake with multiple layers - a Maryland treasure.
  • Old Bay Seasoned Fries: This dish is the perfect combination of crispy and spicy.

Local Beverages

  • Flying Dog Brewery: This brewery produces excellent craft beer, and they offer guided tours for visitors.
  • Boordy Vineyards: A beautiful vineyard located just a few miles from Beltsville, the winery offers visitors a chance to enjoy the gorgeous scenery while sipping their wine.

Where to Stay in Beltsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beltsville doesn’t have many lodging options. Still, visitors can find comfortable and affordable accommodation with ease. Here are some of the best places to stay in Beltsville:

  • Day’s Inn by Wyndham Laurel: A budget-friendly option located just a few minutes from Beltsville.
  • Hampton Inn Laurel (Fort Meade Area): Perfect for families with kids with suites that boast of a lounge area and a fully-equipped kitchenette.
  • Holiday Inn Washington-College Park: Located in College Park, which is just a few minutes away from Beltsville, the hotel offers both comfort and convenience at affordable rates.

What to Buy in Beltsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to take a piece of Beltsville home with you? These are the perfect souvenirs and local products to bring back:

  • Old Bay Seasoning: The quintessential Maryland seasoning, Old Bay is a must-have in every kitchen.
  • Maryland Flag Accessories: Show off your Maryland pride with a Maryland flag made into a pin, keychain, or a T-shirt.
  • Crab Mallets: To fully enjoy Maryland’s famous crab cakes, you’ll need a crab mallet to crack those shells open.

Local Culture and Customs of Beltsville

Beltsville’s neighborhood is welcoming and friendly, with a rich local culture. A crucial aspect is the town residents’ love for agriculture, particularly farming. Visitors should be respectful of this culture and the local traditions that it entails.

Best Time to Visit Beltsville

The best time to visit Beltsville is during the spring and fall seasons. The weather is pleasant, and the town’s natural beauty is at its peak during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beltsville

  • Do experience the local culture and try out the delicious Maryland cuisine.
  • Do engage in outdoor adventures in the town’s beautiful parks and trails.
  • Do follow the rules and policies put in place to protect the environment and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the parks, waterways, and other natural areas.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Don’t ignore the safety guidelines put in place by attraction sites.

Safety and Health

Visitors are advised to take measures to ensure their safety and health while in Beltsville.

Safety in Beltsville

Beltsville is a generally safe town, but visitors must remain vigilant. Ensure your personal belongings and travel documents are secure and avoid deserted areas at night.

Health in Beltsville

Beltsville is relatively healthy. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions and consult a healthcare professional for any concerns regarding their health.

Getting to Beltsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Beltsville is easily accessible by road and provides multiple travel options for visitors to choose from. Here are the different transportation options available:

Airports Near Beltsville

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: This airport is about 30 miles from Beltsville.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport: This airport is approximately 16 miles from Beltsville.

Bus Stations Near Beltsville

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus station is located in Silver Spring, Maryland, and offers daily services to and from various destinations.

Other Options

  • MARC Train: The MARC train station is located in Laurel, Maryland, and connects Maryland and Washington D.C.
  • Metrobus: Visitors can get to Beltsville via Metrobus buses that have their stops in the town.

How to Get Around in Beltsville

Visitors can get around Beltsville via various modes of transportation, including rental cars, taxis, and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft. Biking and walking are other options to explore the town’s scenic beauty.

Where to Go Next from Beltsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to discover after visiting Beltsville, including:

  • Washington D.C.: Explore the nation’s capital and all of its iconic monuments and museums.
  • Baltimore: Explore the historic city of Baltimore with a rich local history and fantastic cuisine.
  • Annapolis: Known for its rich naval history and beautiful harbor, Annapolis is a great destination for a day trip.

Conclusion

Beltsville offers an authentic cultural and natural experience for every visitor. With everything from scenic beauty to outdoor adventures, delicious local cuisine to rich historical sites, it’s no wonder that this small town in Maryland is quickly becoming a top tourist destination.
