Introduction to Greenbelt

Greenbelt is a city located in Prince George’s County, Maryland, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. The city was built in 1935 as part of President Franklin D. Roosevelt’s New Deal projects. Greenbelt has a unique character as it blends history and nature with modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Greenbelt

Greenbelt is a city that has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Greenbelt:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Greenbelt Museum - This museum showcases the history of Greenbelt and how it came to be. The museum includes exhibits and informative tours.

NASA Goddard Visitor Center - This center offers exhibits on space and the NASA missions. Visitors can also see the James E. Webb Space Telescope, which will be launched in 2021.

Natural Attractions of Greenbelt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Greenbelt Park - This park encompasses over 1,100 acres and provides a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, picnicking, and wildlife viewing.

Indian Creek Trail - This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and bird watching. It is a scenic trail that takes visitors through the forests and streams of Greenbelt.

Lake Artemesia Natural Area - This natural area offers visitors the chance to see various birds, turtles, and fish. Visitors can also enjoy fishing, picnicking, and even kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenbelt

Greenbelt Park offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking. Some of the popular routes include the Perimeter Trail, which is a five-mile loop around the park, and the Azalea Trail, which provides a scenic view of the park’s azalea gardens.

Best Things to Do in Greenbelt: Activities and Tours

The following activities and tours are some of the best things to do in Greenbelt:

Patuxent Research Refuge - This wildlife refuge is the perfect place for birdwatching and wildlife viewing. The refuge also offers educational programs and guided tours.

Learn about Art - Visitors can take art lessons from the Greenbelt Arts Center and the Greenbelt Community Center, which offer various classes for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greenbelt

If you are planning to spend a week in Greenbelt, here is a sample itinerary:

Day 1 - Visit the Greenbelt Museum and take a guided tour.

Day 2 - Spend the day exploring the NASA Goddard Visitor Center.

Day 3 - Go hiking or biking in Greenbelt Park.

Day 4 - Take art classes at the Greenbelt Arts Center or Greenbelt Community Center.

Day 5 - Visit Lake Artemesia Natural Area and enjoy fishing or kayaking.

Day 6 - Explore the Patuxent Research Refuge for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

Day 7 - Visit the Indian Creek Trail for hiking or biking.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenbelt

Greenbelt has a fantastic food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Roppolo’s Pizzeria - This restaurant serves authentic Italian pizza, pasta, and sandwiches.

Beltway Plaza Mall Food Court - The food court offers various food options, including Chinese, Mexican, and American.

Local Beverages

Old Line Spirits - This distillery produces various types of whiskey, gin, and vodka, all made from locally sourced ingredients.

Greenbelt Brewery - This microbrewery offers a wide range of craft beers made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Greenbelt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greenbelt Marriott - This hotel provides an excellent location and access to nearby attractions and amenities.

The Historic Greenbelt Hotel - This hotel offers a unique character, with the added benefit of being close to numerous attractions.

What to Buy in Greenbelt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Greenbelt Co-op - Visitors can purchase locally sourced produce, groceries, and other products at this cooperative grocery store.

Greenbelt Farmers Market - This market is held weekly and offers various food products, arts, and crafts from local vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenbelt

Greenbelt has a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors are encouraged to explore at their own pace while respecting local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Greenbelt

The best time to visit Greenbelt is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is stunning. The summers can be hot and humid, while the winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenbelt

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Greenbelt Park and Indian Creek Trail.
  • Visit Greenbelt Museum to learn about the history of the city.
  • Take art classes at the Greenbelt Arts Center or Greenbelt Community Center.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or outdoor areas.
  • Go off-trail hiking or biking in Greenbelt Park.
  • Disrespect local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenbelt is not a concern for visitors as it is a relatively safe city. Visitors should take normal precautions while travelling, such as keeping valuables secure and not leaving items unattended.

Getting to Greenbelt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Greenbelt is easily accessible through various transportation modes, including:

Airports Near Greenbelt

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA)
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI)

Bus Stations Near Greenbelt

  • Greenbelt Greyhound Station

Other Options

  • Greenbelt Metro Station provides a direct line to Washington, D.C.

How to Get Around in Greenbelt

The best way to get around Greenbelt is by car, bike, or on foot. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Greenbelt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Greenbelt is located near various attractions, including:

  • Washington, D.C. is only a 20-minute drive away and provides numerous historical and cultural attractions.
  • Baltimore, Maryland, is an hour’s drive away and offers various historical and cultural attractions, such as the National Aquarium and Fort McHenry.

Conclusion

Greenbelt is a fantastic city with a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. Visitors can explore various attractions and indulge in fantastic food and drink. Plan your trip to Greenbelt today and immerse yourself in the vibrant culture of Maryland.
