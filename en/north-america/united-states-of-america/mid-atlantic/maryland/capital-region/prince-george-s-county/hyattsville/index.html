Introduction to Hyattsville

Hyattsville, located in Prince George’s County, Maryland, is a hidden gem for travelers looking to explore the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. Known for its history, natural attractions, and cultural landmarks, Hyattsville offers a wealth of opportunities for adventure and relaxation. Whether you’re visiting for a day trip or planning a week-long getaway, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Hyattsville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • American Center for Physics: This small museum showcases the history of physics in the United States, including famous scientists and their contributions to the field.
  • Hyattsville’s Historic District: Take a stroll through Hyattsville’s charming historic district, which features Victorian-era homes, tree-lined streets, and unique shops and restaurants.
  • Mount Rainier Nature and Recreation Center: Learn about the history and ecology of the Chesapeake Bay watershed, take a guided nature hike, and explore the interactive exhibits at this education center.

Natural Attractions of Hyattsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Anacostia Tributary Trail System: This network of trails spans over 60 miles throughout Prince George’s County, offering opportunities for hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing.
  • Magruder Park: This community park features playgrounds, sports fields, and picnic areas for families and individuals to enjoy.
  • Lake Artemesia: This man-made lake and surrounding park offer peaceful hiking trails, fishing spots, and bird-watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hyattsville

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or beginner biker, Hyattsville offers a variety of picturesque routes for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Trolley Trail: This 3.8-mile trail follows the route of the historic streetcar line that once connected Hyattsville to Washington, D.C.
  • Northwest Branch Trail: This 4-mile trail meanders through a serene, forested area and provides views of the Anacostia River.
  • National Mall: For a more urban experience, bike or walk through the National Mall in Washington, D.C., which features iconic landmarks such as the Lincoln Memorial and Washington Monument.

Best Things to Do in Hyattsville: Activities and Tours

  • Pyramid Atlantic Art Center: This nonprofit organization offers workshops and events for artists of all ages, including printmaking, bookbinding, and paper-making classes.
  • Vigilante Coffee: Savor a cup of locally roasted coffee and take a tour of the micro-roastery while learning about the coffee-making process.
  • Live Performances at The Clarice: This performing arts center at the University of Maryland offers a wide range of performances, including theater, music, and dance, throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hyattsville

Day 1: Visit the American Center for Physics and take a stroll through Hyattsville’s historic district Day 2: Hike the Anacostia Tributary Trail System and explore Magruder Park Day 3: Tour the Mount Rainier Nature and Recreation Center and have a picnic at Lake Artemesia Day 4: Bike the Trolley Trail and grab a cup of coffee at Vigilante Coffee Day 5: Visit the Pyramid Atlantic Art Center and enjoy a live performance at The Clarice Day 6: Explore the National Mall in Washington, D.C. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene in Hyattsville.

Food and Drink Scene of Hyattsville

Must-Try Dishes

Hyattsville’s vibrant food scene offers a mix of international cuisines and local flavors.

  • Franklin’s Restaurant, Brewery & General Store: Try their famous brick-oven pizzas, crafted beers, and colonial-style lunches.
  • Busboys and Poets: This popular chain features a healthy and diverse menu, including vegan and gluten-free options, as well as live poetry and music events.
  • Tanglewood Works: Stop by this art gallery and gift shop for ice cream, candy, and other sweet treats.

Local Beverages

  • Streetcar 82 Brewing Co.: This local brewery specializes in small-batch beers and offers a rotating lineup of draft options, including sours, stouts, and IPAs.
  • Spice Crazy Cajun Seafood & Bar: Enjoy a spicy Bloody Mary or margarita at this popular neighborhood bar.

Where to Stay in Hyattsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Plus College Park Hotel: This conveniently located hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • The George Washington University Inn: This affordable accommodation is within walking distance of local attractions and offers a continental breakfast and a cozy lounge area.
  • Hyattsville Hostel: For budget-conscious travelers, this hostel provides shared and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and laundry facilities.

What to Buy in Hyattsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artworks: This gift shop features handmade jewelry, pottery, and other artwork from local artists.
  • Franklins General Store: Browse their selection of locally sourced jams, syrups, and other unique food items.
  • Whole Foods Market: Stock up on healthy snacks and natural beauty products at this popular grocery store chain.

Local Culture and Customs of Hyattsville

In Hyattsville, locals take pride in their community and its history. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s historic district, attend local events and performances, and engage with local business owners and residents.

Best Time to Visit Hyattsville

The best time to visit Hyattsville is during the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hyattsville

Do:

  • Check out the town’s historic district and unique local shops
  • Take advantage of the area’s natural attractions, such as hiking trails and parks
  • Attend a live performance or event at the University of Maryland or other local venues

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s rich history and culture
  • Leave without trying a local craft beer or coffee
  • Forget to try a delicious meal at one of the town’s unique restaurants

Safety and Health

Safety in Hyattsville

Hyattsville is generally a safe town, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking cars and homes, keeping valuables out of sight, and being aware of their surroundings in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Hyattsville

Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them, as well as other essential health items such as sunscreen and insect repellent. Hyattsville has several medical facilities and pharmacies in case of emergency.

Getting to Hyattsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hyattsville

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: 28 miles away
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport: 10 miles away
  • Washington Dulles International Airport: 39 miles away

Bus Stations Near Hyattsville

  • Greyhound Bus Station: 11 miles away
  • Washington DC Union Station: 7 miles away

Other Options

  • Metro: Hyattsville is accessible via the Green and Yellow metro lines, with several stops within the town.
  • Car: Hyattsville is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area.

How to Get Around Hyattsville

Visitors can easily explore Hyattsville on foot or by bike, with several well-maintained trails and sidewalks. Rental cars, taxis, and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Hyattsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Washington, D.C.: The nation’s capital is just a short metro or car ride away, offering a plethora of museums, historic sites, and cultural landmarks.
  • College Park: Home to the University of Maryland, College Park offers opportunities for sports events, concerts, and cultural performances.
  • Baltimore: The largest city in Maryland is just an hour’s drive away and features historic sites, museums, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Hyattsville, Maryland, has something for everyone, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts to foodies. With its charming downtown area, natural attractions, and vibrant community, Hyattsville is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or longer vacation.
