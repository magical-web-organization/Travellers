Fort Washington is a historic town located in Prince George’s County, Maryland, in the Capital Region of the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. It has become a popular travel destination due to its rich history, picturesque landscape and a plethora of things to do. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to Fort Washington by exploring its culturally important attractions, natural wonders, food and drink options, stay options and much more!

Introduction to Fort Washington

Fort Washington boasts a rich history dating back to the early 19th century when it was constructed to protect the nation’s capital. The fort played a significant role in the country’s history during the War of 1812 and the Civil War.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Washington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Washington Park: A historic fort offering stunning views of the Potomac River’s everyday life with military installations.
  • National Colonial Farm: A living history museum that depicts life in the colonial period of Maryland’s history.
  • Fort Foote Park: A historic Civil War fort, with imposing batteries overlooking the Potomac River.

Natural Attractions of Fort Washington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Piscataway National Park: A scenic park that offers walking trails, wildlife viewing, and panoramic views of the Potomac River.
  • Oxon Hill Farm: A picturesque farm offering petting zoo, fishing, and bike trails for visitors of all ages.
  • Tantallon Marine Sanctuary: Perfect for fishing, photo opportunities and enjoying all the beauty the Potomac River has to offer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Washington

  • Fort Washington Park Trail: A five-mile hike starting from the visitor center, boasting stunning views of the Potomac River.
  • Henson Landing Trail: A two-mile trail, offering scenic views of the river, stretching from Piscataway Park to Fort Washington Park.
  • Oxon Cove Park and Farm Trail: A 1.6-mile trail that provides glimpses of the beautiful Oxon Cove and the surrounding farmland.

Best Things to Do in Fort Washington: Activities and Tours

  • Kayak and Bicycle Rental at Piscataway Park: Explore the park by water or land and experience its natural beauty to the fullest.
  • Potomac Riverboat Company Cruises: Take a lovely cruise of the Potomac and enjoy dining, sightseeing, and entertaining narrations.
  • Fort Washington Park Hosted Events: Join various park-hosted events like Full Moon Hikes or Sunset Kayak Tours to experience the park’s magic in a unique way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Washington

Day 1: Explore the rich history and beautiful views of Fort Washington Park and the National Colonial Farm.
Day 2: Take in the beauty of the Potomac River with a relaxing cruise from the Potomac Riverboat Company.
Day 3: Get active with some hiking or biking along the Fort Washington Park Trail or Oxon Cove Park and Farm Trail.
Day 4: Visit Fort Foote Park and learn about its fascinating Civil War history.
Day 5: Indulge in some retail therapy and grab some souvenirs from the Tanger Outlets National Harbor.
Day 6: Visit the serene Piscataway Park and Piscataway National Park for some outdoor fun.
Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a relaxing day at Oxon Hill Farm, enjoying the picturesque scenery and petting zoo.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Washington

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steamed Crabs: An iconic Maryland dish that is a must-try when visiting the area.
  • Smith Island Cake: A delicious dessert that originates from southern Maryland.
  • Maryland Crab Cake: A truly iconic dish that is a must-try when visiting Fort Washington.

Local Beverages

  • Old Bay Bloody Mary: A savory cocktail made with Old Bay seasoning - a true Maryland classic.
  • Heavy Seas Beer: A local brewery offering a range of exotic flavors of beer.
  • Maryland Wine: Vineyards located near Fort Washington are known for producing delicious wines.

What to Buy in Fort Washington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Old Bay Seasoning: A popular spice blend used in Maryland cuisine.
  • Maryland Flag Merchandise: The Maryland flag is iconic in the state and makes for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Washington

The local culture of Fort Washington ties into its rich history, and it is deeply ingrained in the town’s architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Marylanders are typically friendly, hospitable, and enthusiastic about engaging in conversations.

Best Time to Visit Fort Washington

The best time to visit Fort Washington is during the spring and fall, offering perfect weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Washington

Do:

  • Visit the historic forts and parks in the region.
  • Try Maryland’s iconic seafood dishes, especially crabs.
  • Explore the Potomac River and its many water attractions.

Don’t:

  • Consume liquor in public places.
  • Leave valuables in your car while parked outside the hotels.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Washington

Fort Washington is a safe destination; however, visitors should be vigilant of petty theft and exercise general security precautions.

Health in Fort Washington

Visitors should ensure that they receive the necessary immunizations before traveling to the region.

  • Bike and Foot: Many attractions in the town are accessible by bike or foot.
