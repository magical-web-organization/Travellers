Introduction to Suitland

Suitland is a city located in Prince George’s County, Maryland, in the Capital Region of the Mid-Atlantic United States. It is a multicultural city and was named after 19th-century landowner and businessman, Senator Samuel Taylor Suit. It is a perfect tourist destination because of its scenic views, outdoor activities, and historical places. In this travel guide, we have covered everything that you need to know to plan your trip to Suitland.

List of Places to Visit in Suitland

Here are some of the top attractions in Suitland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Museum of the United States Air Force: This museum showcases the history of aviation and the role of the U.S. Air Force in national security.
  • The Smithsonian National Museum of African Art: The museum contains a comprehensive collection of African art, including traditional and contemporary works.
  • The United States Capitol Building: The United States Capitol serves as a symbol of democracy and is a must-visit for those interested in American politics.
  • The Lincoln Memorial: Visit the Lincoln Memorial, a presidential memorial that honors the 16th president of the United States, Abraham Lincoln.

Natural Attractions of Suitland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The National Arboretum: This natural oasis offers visitors a relaxing escape, with over 446 acres of outdoor gardens and plant exhibits.
  • Greenbelt Park: This park has a variety of hiking trails, picnic areas, and campgrounds. Visitors can also enjoy birdwatching or fishing at the park.
  • Kenilworth Aquatic Gardens: This scenic garden contains aquatic plants and flowers and is a perfect location for nature lovers and photographers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suitland

Suitland also has a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors unique outdoor experiences:

  • Southwest Branch Trail: This is a 12-mile trail that explores the natural beauty of Suitland’s parks and waterways and is perfect for hikers and cyclists of all skill levels.
  • Anacostia River Trail: This 20-mile trail winds through the city and is perfect for those who want to explore Suitland’s urban landscape while staying active.

Best Things to Do in Suitland: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Suitland:

  • Capital Wheel: Enjoy the breathtaking views while riding on the Capital Wheel, a large Ferris wheel located on the shores of the Potomac River.
  • Boating on the Potomac River: Explore the Potomac River by renting a boat and enjoy the scenic views of Suitland from the water.
  • Ghost Tour of Suitland: Take a spooky ghost tour of Suitland and learn about the ghosts that haunt the city’s historical buildings and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Suitland

The local food and drink scene in Suitland has something for everyone:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Old Bay Seasoned Seafood: This dish is a local favorite in Suitland and a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Crab Cakes: Crab cakes are an iconic Maryland dish made with fresh crab meat served with a side of fries or salad.
  • Smith Island Cake: This famous dessert from Maryland is a layered cake made with sweet cream and buttercream.

Local Beverages

  • Maryland Craft Beer: Maryland is known for its high-quality craft beer, featuring local breweries such as RAR Brewing and Flying Dog Brewery.
  • Old Line Spirits: This local distillery offers unique spirits made with Maryland ingredients, including whiskey, gin, and vodka.

What to Buy in Suitland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are a few ideas for souvenirs and local products to bring back from Suitland:

  • Chesapeake Bay Region Souvenirs: Take a piece of Maryland home with you by buying souvenirs that feature the Chesapeake Bay region, including t-shirts, mugs, and jewelry.

  • Maryland Craft Beer or Spirits: Bring back a bottle of local Maryland beer or spirit from breweries like RAR or Old Line Spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Suitland

Suitland is a multicultural city, and residents are proud of their heritage. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions and be mindful of their surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Suitland

The best time to visit Suitland is during shoulder season (April-June, September-November). The weather is pleasant, and prices tend to be lower than during peak season (June-August), which coincides with summer vacation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suitland

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and cultural landmarks.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle during outdoor activities.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of individuals without their permission.
  • Disrespect the environment or wildlife.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety in Suitland

Getting to Suitland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some options for getting to Suitland:

Airports Near Suitland

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA): The airport is about 10 miles away from Suitland.

Bus Stations Near Suitland

  • Suitland Metro Station: Located in Suitland offering several bus lines for transport.

Other Options

  • Car rentals and taxi services: Car rentals and taxi services are available at the airport and throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Suitland

The best way to get around in Suitland is by car or taking public transportation. Metrobus and Metrorail are available options.
