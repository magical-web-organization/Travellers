Introduction to College Park

College Park is a city located in Prince George’s County, Maryland, in the Capital Region of the Mid-Atlantic region, United States of America. It is home to the University of Maryland, the flagship campus of the University System of Maryland, and is situated in close proximity to Washington, D.C., which is just a short trip away. College Park offers a perfect blend of cultural attractions, historical landmarks, natural wonders, and recreational activities, making it a must-visit destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in College Park

Here’s a list of some of the best places to visit in College Park to make the most out of your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Language: This unique museum showcases the evolution of language, with a particular focus on the English language.
  • College Park Aviation Museum: This museum showcases the history and evolution of aviation, with a focus on the aviation pioneers of the area.
  • Beltsville Agricultural Research Center: It is one of the largest agricultural research centers in the world and is run by the U.S. Department of Agriculture. Visitors can learn about the history of agricultural research and observe the ongoing work.

Natural Attractions of College Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Greenbelt Park: A beautiful park with a lake, hiking trails, and picnic areas. Visitors can also participate in ranger-led educational programs, camping, and canoe rentals.
  • Lake Artemesia Natural Area: A natural park with a lake, hiking trails, and wildlife observation opportunities.
  • Northwest Branch Trail: This is a scenic trail with hills and running water which is perfect for biking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of College Park

  • Paint Branch Trail: This trail offers a scenic route for hikers and bikers through the natural woodlands of northern Prince George’s County.
  • Indian Creek Trail: This trail is located within the Beltsville Agricultural Research Center which is an agricultural field research facility operated by the United States Department of Agriculture.
  • College Park Trolley Trail: This trail runs through the historic College Park, offering visitors a chance to explore the city while getting some exercise.

Best Things to Do in College Park: Activities and Tours

  • Terrapin Adventures: A thrilling adventure park offering zip-lining, high ropes, and other adventure sports activities.
  • College Park Segway Tours: A perfect way to explore the city with its scenic views of Lake Artemesia, the University of Maryland, and other historic landmarks.
  • Cultural Arts Center: This center offers a variety of artistic and cultural activities, including performances, exhibitions, and classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for College Park

Day 1: Visit the National Museum of Language and explore Greenbelt Park. Day 2: Spend the day in Terrapin Adventures park, enjoying the adventure sports activities. Day 3: Explore Beltsville Agricultural Research Center and paint branch trail. Day 4: Visit the College Park Aviation Museum and enjoy canoe rentals at Lake Artemesia. Day 5: Take a cultural arts class at the Cultural Arts Center and enjoy a Segway tour in the evening Day 6: Explore the Northwest Branch Trail and visit the University of Maryland. Day 7: Spend the day shopping and trying out the famous dishes of College Park.

Food and Drink Scene of College Park

College Park boasts of its diverse food scene with something for everyone - from vegan to Italian to local seafood. Here are some must-try dishes that you should not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Board and Brew: This famous café in College Park offers a wide selection of sandwiches, espresso drinks, and beer.
  • Henley’s Kitchen and Bar: A neighborhood gem offering seasonal and creatively prepared food from sustainable ingredients.
  • Dumpling and Beyond: This restaurant serves delicious Chinese dishes like baozi, dumplings, and handmade noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Old Maryland Farm: This farm offers fresh and locally grown produce, including fruits and vegetables.
  • The Board and Brew: This café also offers a wide selection of beer.

Where to Stay in College Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

College Park offers various options for visitors looking for an accommodation, including:

  • The Hotel at the University of Maryland: This upscale hotel is situated on the University of Maryland campus, just a few miles from Washington, D.C.
  • College Park Marriott Hotel and Conference Center: A full-service hotel that offers complimentary shuttle service to the University of Maryland.
  • Clarion Inn College Park: This affordable yet comfortable hotel is located near many major attractions in College Park.

What to Buy in College Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • A Carved Wooden Terrapin: The Terrapin is the mascot of the University of Maryland, and visitors can buy a carved wooden terrapin as a souvenir.
  • Maryland Blue Crabs: Famous for its seafood, Maryland blue crabs can be bought live or steamed and make a perfect gift to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of College Park

College Park is a diverse city with people from different backgrounds and cultures. Visitors are expected to be respectful of the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit College Park

The best time to visit College Park is from March to May or September through November when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of College Park

Do:

  • Explore the city’s natural attractions, including its parks and hiking trails.
  • Try out the local food scene.
  • Visit the University of Maryland campus.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the scenic trails.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in College Park

College Park is a safe city, but visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety such as:

  • Keep valuables out of sight
  • Stay in well-lit, public areas, and avoid isolated areas at night

Health in College Park

There are numerous medical facilities in College Park, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, that visitors can use if needed.

Getting to College Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near College Park

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: The closest major airport, 23 miles away from College Park.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport: Located 10 miles from College Park.

Bus Stations Near College Park

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Washington, D.C.
  • Boltbus: A popular bus service offering trips from various East Coast cities to Washington, D.C.

Other Options

  • Uber/Lyft/Taxi Cabs are easily available
  • Car Rentals: Multiple car rental agencies are available both at the airports and in College Park itself

How to Get Around in College Park

Visitors can get around College Park by using various transportation options, including:

  • Free Shuttle Service: The University of Maryland offers a free shuttle service around campus and the College Park metro station.
  • Biking/walking: College Park is a bike-friendly city, and visitors can use the city’s bike-share program or their bike to get around.
  • Public transportation: City buses run throughout College Park and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from College Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Washington, D. C.: The nation’s capital is just a short trip away from College Park and offers an abundance of attractions, including the National Mall and famous monuments and museums.
  • Annapolis: The charming capital of Maryland is just an hour’s drive away and offers a unique blend of history and culture.

Conclusion

College Park is a beautiful city with much to offer for visitors of all interests. From its natural wonders to its cultural attractions to its diverse food scene, it is the perfect destination for a short or long stay. With its close proximity to Washington, D.C., visitors can experience the best of both worlds.
