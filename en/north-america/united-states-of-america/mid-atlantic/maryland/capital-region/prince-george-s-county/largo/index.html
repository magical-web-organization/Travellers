Introduction to Largo

Located in Prince George’s County, Largo is a small town in Maryland, USA, that is home to a variety of cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re looking to explore historical sites, go on outdoor adventures, or savor local cuisine, Largo has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, local food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Largo

To fully experience Largo, here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marietta House Museum – This historic house museum showcases Prince George’s County’s cultural and agricultural heritage from 1813 to 1915.
  • Darnall’s Chance House Museum – This late-17th-century plantation-style home features exhibits on the life of a Maryland planter family and its enslaved workers.
  • The Society of the Ark and the Dove – This museum pays homage to the first colony of Maryland, established in 1634 by the Ark and the Dove after a four-month crossing of the Atlantic from England.

Natural Attractions of Largo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Watkins Regional Park – This 795-acre park features several lakes, hiking trails, picnic areas, an antique carousel, and a miniature train ride.
  • Six Flags America – This amusement park features over 40 thrilling rides, shows, and attractions for all ages.
  • Patuxent River Park – This park includes over 7,800 acres of forests, fields, wetlands, and streams, offering opportunities for hiking, kayaking, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Largo

  • Henson Creek Trail – This paved trail runs for 5.5 miles along Henson Creek through meadows, woodlands, and parks.
  • Prince George’s Trail – This 12-mile hiker/biker trail connects Bowie to the Anacostia Tributary Trail System, passing by beautiful scenery, parks, and neighborhoods.
  • Fairland Regional Park Trail – This 8-mile trail travels through scenic forests and streams, with several picnic areas and playgrounds along the way.

Best Things to Do in Largo: Activities and Tours

  • Capital Wheel – Get panoramic views of the Potomac River, National Harbor, and Washington, DC from a 180-feet high Ferris wheel.
  • National Museum of African American History – Explore the history, culture, and contributions of African Americans in the United States, from slavery to the present day.
  • Arena Stage – Watch live theater performances, including Tony Award-winning plays, musicals, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Largo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maryland crab cakes – Freshly made Chesapeake Bay crab cakes cooked to perfection and served with tartar sauce and lemon wedges.
  • Old Bay-seasoned fries – Thick-cut fries sprinkled with Old Bay seasoning, a blend of herbs and spices, popular in the Chesapeake Bay area.
  • Half-smoke sausage – A local sausage made from pork, beef, and spices, grilled or smoked and served with mustard and onions.

Local Beverages

  • National Bohemian beer – A classic lager beer brewed in Baltimore since 1885, also known as Natty Boh.
  • Old Dominion Root Beer – A family-owned brand producing handcrafted, naturally sweetened root beer and other sodas since 1987.
  • Local wines – Maryland has over 100 wineries producing a variety of wines, including Chardonnay, Cabernet Franc, and Viognier.

Where to Stay in Largo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Residence Inn by Marriott Largo Capital Beltway – A newly renovated hotel with spacious suites, a fitness center, an outdoor pool, and free breakfast.
  • Comfort Inn at Joint Base Andrews – A mid-range hotel with comfortable rooms, a fitness center, a business center, and a free hot breakfast.
  • Camping – Watkins Regional Park offers over 100 campgrounds with electric hookup, picnic tables, and restrooms. The park also has cabins, yurts, and group camping options.

What to Buy in Largo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crab-themed souvenirs – T-shirts, magnets, keychains, and other items decorated with Chesapeake Bay crabs and Old Bay seasoning.
  • Maryland flag products – Hats, mugs, socks, and other items featuring the colorful Maryland state flag, with its black, red, and gold design.
  • Local wines and craft beers – Choose from a variety of local wines and beers, including Natty Boh, Old Dominion Root Beer, and Flying Dog IPA.

Local Culture and Customs of Largo

  • Friendliness – Marylanders are known for their friendliness and warm welcomes to visitors. Don’t be surprised if locals strike up a conversation or offer tips.
  • Punctuality – Marylanders value punctuality, so arrive on time for appointments and reservations.
  • Crab feasts – Crab feasts are a popular summer tradition in Maryland, where guests gather to crack open freshly steamed crabs and enjoy local beers and wines.

Best Time to Visit Largo

The best time to visit Largo is from April to June and from September to November when temperatures are mild, and crowds are smaller. July and August are the peak tourist season, with high temperatures and humidity and busy attractions. Winter can be cold but has fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Largo

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine – Maryland is famous for its fresh seafood, Old Bay seasoning, and Natty Boh beer.
  • Take a crabbing tour – Learn to catch your own crabs on a Chesapeake Bay boat tour.
  • Enjoy the outdoors – Take a hike, go kayaking, or have a picnic in one of Largo’s parks.
  • Visit Washington, DC – The US capital is just 11 miles away and offers a wealth of sights and attractions.

Don’t:

  • Forget your sunscreen – The Chesapeake Bay area can get hot and sunny in the summer, so protect your skin.
  • Litter – Keep Largo’s parks and trails clean by taking your trash with you and picking up after your pets.
  • Drive during rush hour – Traffic can be heavy in the DC area during rush hour, so plan accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Largo

Largo is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual safety precautions, such as locking cars and homes, being aware of surroundings, and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Largo

Visitors to Largo should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations, as well as vaccines for hepatitis A and B if they plan to engage in food, water, or close contact with locals.

Getting to Largo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Largo

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA) – 15 miles.
  • Washington Dulles International Airport (IAD) – 45 miles.
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI) – 30 miles.

Bus Stations Near Largo

  • Largo Town Center Metro Station – Serves multiple bus routes, including Metrobus, TheBus, and Fairfax Connector.
  • Bowie State University MARC Station – A commuter rail station with bus connections to Largo.

Other Options

  • Car rental – Largo is easily accessible by car via the Capital Beltway (I-495) and US Route 50.
  • Uber/Lyft/taxi – Ride-sharing and taxis are available in Largo and the DC area.
  • Metro – The Washington, DC Metro Subway has a stop in Largo at the Largo Town Center station, with connections to the DC area.

How to Get Around in Largo

Largo is best explored by car, but visitors can also use public transportation, bike, or walk to get around. The Washington, DC Metro Subway has a stop in Largo at the Largo Town Center station, with connections to the DC area. Several biking and hiking trails are also available in and around Largo.

Where to Go Next from Largo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Washington, DC – The US capital is just 11 miles away and offers numerous museums, monuments, and attractions.
  • Annapolis – The capital of Maryland and a charming historic town on the Chesapeake Bay, famous for its seafood and sailing.
  • Baltimore – Maryland’s largest city and home to the Inner Harbor, National Aquarium, and historic neighborhoods.

Conclusion

Largo is a hidden gem in Maryland, offering a blend of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and a rich food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking to spend a day or a week, Largo has plenty to offer. From historic museums and hiking trails to seafood feasts and crabbing tours, there’s something for every type of traveler. So why not plan your visit to Largo today and discover this charming town for yourself?
