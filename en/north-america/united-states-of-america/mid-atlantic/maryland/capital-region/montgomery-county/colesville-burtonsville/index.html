Introduction to Colesville-Burtonsville

Colesville-Burtonsville is a charming small town located in Montgomery County, Maryland, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. The town is known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and outdoor activities. Colesville-Burtonsville offers visitors a fascinating glimpse of the past and a chance to explore the natural beauty of the region. This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need for a memorable trip to Colesville-Burtonsville.

List of Places to Visit in Colesville-Burtonsville

There are many attractions and landmarks to explore in Colesville-Burtonsville. Here’s a list of some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Savage Mill: This historic mill complex has been transformed into a premier shopping destination, featuring over 175 unique stores, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Sandy Spring Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Sandy Spring and the surrounding areas.
  • Washington D.C. Temple Visitors’ Center: This visitors’ center represents The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and provides guided tours of the temple.

Natural Attractions of Colesville-Burtonsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Northwest Branch Stream Valley Park: This park is a natural oasis in the heart of Colesville-Burtonsville. Activities include hiking, biking, and birdwatching.
  • Rachel Carson Conservation Park: This park offers hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and a nature center.
  • T. Howard Duckett Watershed: This beautiful and serene watershed has miles of hiking trails, wildlife, and water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colesville-Burtonsville

Colesville-Burtonsville is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the beautiful surroundings. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Hawlings River Trail: This 3.4-mile trail offers a scenic route along the Hawlings River and through the rolling hills of Montgomery County.
  • Northwest Branch Trail: This 8.5-mile trail is great for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail runs through scenic woodlands and offers stunning views of the Northwest Branch of the Anacostia River.
  • Cross Trails: Cross Trails offers some of the best mountain biking trails in the area.

Best Things to Do in Colesville-Burtonsville: Activities and Tours
