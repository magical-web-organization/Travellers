Introduction to Kensington

Kensington is a charming town located in Montgomery County, in the Capital Region of Maryland. It is a small suburban community with a population of around 2,500. Although it is a small town, there are plenty of things to see and do in Kensington. In this guide, we will take you on a tour of Kensington and help you plan your trip to this beautiful town.

List of Places to Visit in Kensington

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or culture lover, Kensington has something to offer. Here is a list of places to visit in Kensington:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kensington Historical Society: This historical society museum features exhibits on the local history, from Native American settlements to the present day.
  2. Historic Oakland: This Georgian mansion, built in 1811, has been restored and converted into a museum showcasing the history of the town and its residents.
  3. Glen Echo Park: This National Park features art, culture, and history exhibits, many of which explore the park’s ties to the art deco era.

Natural Attractions of Kensington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Rock Creek Park: This beautiful urban park spans over 1,700 acres and offers hiking trails, biking routes, picnic areas, and scenic views of the Potomac River.
  2. Wheaton Regional Park: This beautiful park features an outdoor ice skating rink, a train museum, picnic areas, hiking trails, and a nature center.
  3. Black Hills Regional Park: This park features an 18-hole disc golf course, a picnic pavilion, fishing, and hiking trails, including the beautiful Lake Bernard Frank trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kensington

For outdoor enthusiasts, there are several hiking and biking trails in Kensington:

  1. Rock Creek Trail: This 14-mile paved trail extends from Washington D.C. to Lake Needwood in Montgomery County, going through Kensington along the way.
  2. Matthew Henson Trail: This 4.5-mile trail winds through Rock Creek Park, connecting Kensington to Aspen Hill.
  3. Strathmore Connector Trail: This 1.6-mile paved trail is great for biking and walking and connects Wheaton Regional Park to Grosvenor-Strathmore Metro station.

Best Things to Do in Kensington: Activities and Tours

Here are a few activities and tours that are must-visit while in Kensington:

  1. Kensington Farmers Market: This market features a variety of local vendors selling fruits, vegetables, artisanal food items, and crafts. Open on Saturday mornings.
  2. Chesapeake and Ohio Canal National Historical Park: This national park has a 184.5-mile hiking and biking trail, as well as beautiful historic sites and attractions.
  3. Private Tours: Several local tour companies offer private tours of the town’s sights and attractions, including private walking tours of historic homes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kensington

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Kensington:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Kensington Farmers Market in the morning and Wheaton Regional Park in the afternoon
Day 2 Visit the Glen Echo Park and Black Hills Regional Park
Day 3 Go on a private walking tour of historic homes in the morning and hike the Matthew Henson Trail
Day 4 Visit the Historic Oakland and Strathmore Connector Trail
Day 5 Hike the Rock Creek Trail and visit the Rock Creek Nature Center
Day 6 Visit the Chesapeake and Ohio Canal National Historical Park and have a picnic at Rock Creek Park
Day 7 Visit the Kensington Historical Society and have a dinner at a local restaurant

Food and Drink Scene of Kensington

Kensington boasts a wide variety of delicious food and drinks. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Crab Cakes: Maryland is known for its delicious crab cakes. Try them at the local restaurants in Kensington.
  2. Chesapeake Bay Oysters: Another local seafood delicacy, Chesapeake Bay oysters are a must-try.
  3. Old Bay Fries: Old Bay seasoning is a staple in Maryland food. Enjoy it on your fries too!

Local Beverages

  1. National Bohemian (Natty Boh): A Maryland-local beer brand that you can find in the local bars of Kensington.
  2. Margaritas: Many local restaurants offer delicious margaritas made with freshly squeezed limes and oranges.
  3. Maryland-style Bloody Mary: Made with Old Bay seasoning and garnished with a crab claw, this Bloody Mary is a unique local drink.

Where to Stay in Kensington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While there are no hotels or hostels in Kensington, visitors can choose from several guesthouses and bed and breakfasts, such as The Inn at Dupont Circle and the Hyatt Regency Bethesda. Alternatively, there are several chain hotels a short drive away in Silver Spring and Rockville.

What to Buy in Kensington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are a few souvenirs and local products you can bring home from Kensington:

  1. Kensington Historical Society Merchandise: Visit the Kensington Historical Society to buy souvenirs like mugs, postcards, and books.
  2. Maryland Blue Crab Artprints: Buy beautiful art prints featuring Maryland’s famous blue crabs
  3. Vintage Cookbooks: Visit the antique stores in Kensington to find unique, out-of-print cookbooks that highlight the region’s culinary traditions.

Local Culture and Customs of Kensington

When visiting Kensington, it’s important to know and respect the local culture and customs. The residents are friendly and welcoming, but it’s important not to disturb the peace and quiet of the town.

Best Time to Visit Kensington

The best time to visit Kensington is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is beautiful during the fall season. Summer months can be hot and humid, and winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kensington

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Take part in the local festivities and events, such as the annual Kensington Labor Day Parade.
  3. Explore the natural parks and hiking trails around Kensington.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or disturb the peace and quiet of the town.
  2. Park in residential areas or block driveways.
  3. Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kensington

Kensington is a safe town, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions when walking or hiking.

Health in Kensington

Kensington has a range of healthcare facilities, including several hospitals and clinics that provide top-quality medical services.

Getting to Kensington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kensington is easily accessible by car and public transport. Here are your options:

Airports Near Kensington

  1. Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA) – about a 25-minute drive from Kensington
  2. Washington Dulles International Airport (IAD) – about a 45-minute drive from Kensington
  3. Baltimore Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI) – about a 50-minute drive from Kensington

Bus Stations Near Kensington

  1. Silver Spring Transit Center
  2. Kensington MARC Train Station

Other Options

  1. Metro (Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority): Kensington is serviced by the Wheaton, Grosvenor-Strathmore, and Forest Glen Metro stations.
  2. Car Rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore Kensington and its surroundings at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Kensington

Kensington is a walkable town, and many of the attractions are easily accessible by foot. Biking is also a great way to explore the town and its surrounding natural areas. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Kensington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kensington is located near several amazing destinations and excursions. Here are a few:

  1. Washington D.C.: The nation’s capital is only a short drive or metro ride from Kensington, making it a great place to visit and explore.
  2. Great Falls Park: This beautiful national park is located just outside of Washington D.C., and east of Kensington. It features stunning views of the Potomac River and beautiful hiking trails.
  3. Potomac River Cruise: Take a boat tour down the Potomac River and explore the beautiful views and wildlife.

Conclusion

Kensington is a charming town with a rich history and beautiful natural surroundings. From hiking trails to local food and drink, this town has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Kensington today and experience all the wonderful things this town has to offer!
