Introduction to Brunswick

Located in Frederick County, in the Capital Region of Maryland, Brunswick is a charming small town with a rich history and natural beauty. Founded in 1890, Brunswick was originally a railroad town and still hosts a historic train station. Visitors can enjoy a variety of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and friendly local events.

List of Places to Visit in Brunswick

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brunswick Railroad Museum: Housed in the historic Baltimore & Ohio Railroad station, this museum offers a fascinating look at the town’s history and railway heritage.
  • Brunswick Heritage Museum: Learn about Brunswick’s early development and see artifacts from local families and businesses.
  • Maryland Civil War Trails: Take a walking tour of Brunswick’s historic sites, including several key Civil War locations.

Natural Attractions of Brunswick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Potomac River: The town sits on the Potomac River, offering stunning views and opportunities for kayaking, fishing, and boating.
  • Brunswick Family Campground: Enjoy a peaceful camping experience surrounded by nature, with access to hiking trails, a playground, and a lake.
  • Brunswick Crossing Trail: This scenic trail runs through the heart of town, offering beautiful views of the Brunswick Gap and Catoctin Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brunswick

  • C&O Canal Towpath: This 184-mile trail runs along the Potomac River and offers a flat, easy walk or bike ride through scenic countryside.
  • Brunswick Ghost Town Trail: Explore Brunswick’s abandoned factory ruins and historic buildings on this unique hiking trail.

Best Things to Do in Brunswick: Activities and Tours

  • The Brunswick Heritage Festival: Held annually in June, this festival is a celebration of Brunswick’s history and culture, with live music, food, and crafts.
  • Potomac Eagle Scenic Railroad: Take a leisurely train ride through the stunning countryside and enjoy the sights of the Potomac River Valley.
  • Brunswick Farmers Market: Shop for fresh produce, baked goods, and crafts at this local market, open on Sundays from May to October.

Food and Drink Scene of Brunswick

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: A Maryland staple, these delicious cakes are made with fresh crab meat and seasoned breadcrumbs.
  • Fried chicken: Brunswick is known for its delicious fried chicken, which can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Pot pie: Another classic comfort food, Brunswick’s pot pie features chicken or beef in a rich gravy with vegetables and a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Flying Dog Brewery: Located in nearby Frederick, Flying Dog offers a range of unique craft beers with bold flavors.
  • Linganore Winecellars: Taste a variety of award-winning wines, made with grapes grown in nearby vineyards.

Where to Stay in Brunswick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Brunswick Inn: This historic hotel offers comfortable rooms and a charming atmosphere in a convenient downtown location.
  • Comfort Inn & Suites: Enjoy modern amenities and a complimentary breakfast at this affordable hotel near the Potomac River.
  • Cozy Cottage: For a more intimate experience, rent a cozy cottage in the heart of town and enjoy the privacy of your own space.

What to Buy in Brunswick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Brunswick Railroad Museum: Browse the museum gift shop for unique train-related souvenirs.
  • Square Corner Gift Shop: Shop for handmade crafts and gifts from local artists and artisans.
  • Brunswick Farmers Market: Take home fresh produce, baked goods, and honey from local farmers and vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Brunswick

  • Respect the town’s historic buildings and landmarks, and help preserve them for future generations.
  • Friendly conversations with locals and a smile can go a long way in establishing connections and learning about the community.
  • Brunswick values hard work, community, and a love for the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Brunswick

The best time to visit Brunswick is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and many of the town’s outdoor activities and events are in full swing. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brunswick

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s historic sites, museums, and local businesses.
  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and boating.
  • Do attend local festivals and events to experience the town’s culture and community.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas or historic landmarks.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals and learn about the town’s history and culture.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate attire for the season and outdoor activities, such as sunscreen, bug spray, and rain gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brunswick

Brunswick is a safe and welcoming town, with low rates of crime. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take standard precautions, such as locking doors and windows and not leaving valuables in plain sight. Outdoor activities can involve some risks, such as slippery trails or water hazards, so visitors should take appropriate safety measures.

Health in Brunswick

Brunswick has several medical facilities, including a hospital and local clinics. Visitors should have their own health insurance and consult a physician before traveling if they have any medical concerns.

Getting to Brunswick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brunswick

The nearest major airport is Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI), which is about 60 miles away. Dulles International Airport (IAD) is also about 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Brunswick

The nearest bus station is in nearby Frederick, which is about 15 minutes away by car. Buses from Frederick connect to many major cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Brunswick, which is located near major highways and interstates. Rental cars are available at major airports and in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Brunswick

Brunswick is a small town, and many of its attractions and amenities are within walking or biking distance. A car or bike rental is recommended for exploring the wider area.

Where to Go Next from Brunswick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Harpers Ferry National Historical Park: Just across the Potomac River in West Virginia, Harpers Ferry offers historic sites, hiking trails, and scenic views.
  • Sugarloaf Mountain: This local landmark offers hiking trails and a winery, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Frederick: Located about 15 minutes from Brunswick, Frederick is a vibrant city with a historic downtown, shops, restaurants, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or just a quiet getaway, Brunswick offers something for everyone. With its friendly locals, scenic views, and charming small-town atmosphere, Brunswick is a must-visit destination in Maryland.
