Introduction to Frederick County

Located in the Capital Region of Maryland, Frederick County boasts a rich history, scenic beauty, and a vibrant cultural scene. From world-famous museums and historic sites to thrilling outdoor activities and charming small towns, Frederick County has something for every kind of traveler.

Whether you’re planning a romantic weekend getaway or a family vacation, our comprehensive travel guide to Frederick County will help you make the most of your trip to this beautiful corner of Maryland.

List of Places to Visit in Frederick County

Frederick County is home to a plethora of fascinating attractions, ranging from historic landmarks to natural wonders. Below are some top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Civil War Medicine: Located in downtown Frederick, the museum showcases the medical practices and advancements of the Civil War era.
  • Monocacy National Battlefield: A significant site of the Civil War, the park offers visitors a chance to explore a historic battlefield and learn about the impact of the war on the nation.
  • National Museum of the Marine Corps: Located in Triangle, Virginia, the museum is a 45-minute drive from Frederick. The museum commemorates the history of the United States Marine Corps.
  • Community Bridge Mural: Located on the Carroll Creek Bridge in downtown Frederick, the mural is a vibrant artistic representation of the community’s diverse population.

Natural Attractions of Frederick County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Catoctin Mountain Park: A part of the Appalachian Mountains, the park offers scenic drives, hiking trails, camping, and fishing opportunities.
  • Gambrill State Park: Located along the ridge of the Catoctin Mountains, the park is known for its breathtaking views, hiking trails, and mountain biking paths.
  • Cunningham Falls State Park: Home to Maryland’s largest cascading waterfall, the park offers swimming, fishing, and hiking trails.
  • Sugarloaf Mountain: The mountain offers scenic views in all seasons and challenging hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frederick County

Frederick County is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of scenic trails for hikers and bikers. Some of the best trails include:

  • Monocacy Battlefield Trail: A 1.5-mile loop trail that takes visitors through the historic Monocacy National Battlefield.
  • Catoctin Trail: A 26-mile trail that meanders through the Catoctin Mountains, offering stunning views and challenging terrain for seasoned hikers.
  • Sugarloaf Mountain Trail: A 5.3-mile loop trail that rewards hikers with magnificent views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Great Allegheny Passage Trail: A 150-mile trail that spans from Pittsburgh to Cumberland, Maryland and goes through Frederick County.

Best Things to Do in Frederick County: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural beauty and cultural attractions, Frederick County offers a variety of activities and tours that appeal to visitors of all ages.

  • Flying Dog Brewery Tour: Located in Frederick, the brewery offers tours of their facility, samples of their award-winning beers, and a souvenir glass.
  • Drive-In Movie Theatre: The Family Drive-In Theatre, located in Stephens City, Virginia, is a 45-minute drive from Frederick and offers classic movies under the stars.
  • Ghost Tours of Historic Frederick: Take a guided walk through the historic streets of Frederick and learn about the chilling tales and ghostly legends of the city.
  • Farmers Markets: Frederick County is home to several farmers markets, showcasing fresh produce, handmade crafts, and local art.

Week-Long Itinerary for Frederick County

To help you plan your trip, here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Frederick County:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Monocacy National Battlefield and National Museum of Civil War Medicine
Day 2 Scenic drive through Catoctin Mountain Park and Cunningham Falls State Park
Day 3 Hike Sugarloaf Mountain and have lunch in downtown Frederick
Day 4 Take a guided tour of the National Museum of the Marine Corps
Day 5 Visit the Community Bridge Mural and explore small towns such as Middletown and Burkittsville
Day 6 Go on a wine tasting tour and visit the Flying Dog Brewery
Day 7 Head to the Great Allegheny Passage Trail for a day hike or bike ride

Food and Drink Scene of Frederick County

Frederick County is a foodie’s paradise, with a diverse range of cuisine that appeals to every palate. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maryland Crab Cakes: Made with fresh Chesapeake Bay crabmeat, these crab cakes are a local delicacy.
  • Pit Beef Sandwiches: Cooked over charcoal, these slow-roasted beef sandwiches are an iconic Baltimore specialty.
  • Old Bay Fries: A Maryland classic, these fries are sprinkled with a secret blend of spices called Old Bay and served with a side of ranch dressing.
  • Smith Island Cake: A layer cake made with multiple thin yellow cakes stacked on top of each other and separated by layers of icing in between.

Local Beverages

  • Heavy Seas Beer: This award-winning brewery offers a range of beers, including the popular Loose Cannon IPA.
  • Linganore Winecellars: Located in Mount Airy, the winery offers a selection of red, white, and dessert wines.
  • Flying Dog Brewery: As mentioned earlier, the brewery is popular amongst beer drinkers.

Where to Stay in Frederick County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Frederick County has a range of options for accommodations, from luxury hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts.

  • The Frederick Hotel: Located in the heart of downtown Frederick, the hotel offers luxurious rooms and modern amenities in a historic building.
  • Hill House Bed and Breakfast: A Victorian-style B&B located in downtown Frederick, offering cozy rooms and home-cooked breakfasts.
  • Main Street Hostel: Located in downtown Frederick, the hostel offers budget-friendly dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Frederick County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring back home, Frederick County has plenty of options.

  • Crab-themed merchandise: Caps, sweatshirts, and other clothing items designed with a crab are popular souvenirs, especially in the seafood restaurants.
  • Antique Furniture: Scour antique shops for unique and valuable furniture items and pieces.
  • Pretzels: A local twist on a beloved snack, these pretzels are baked fresh and come in various flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Frederick County

The people of Frederick County are proud of their diverse heritage and cultural traditions. While there are no hard and fast rules, it’s important to respect the local customs and be mindful of the following:

  • Tipping: Tipping is expected in most restaurants and bars.
  • Religion: The majority of residents of Frederick County are Christian, and religious customs and traditions should be respected.
  • Legal age: The legal drinking age in Maryland is 21.

Best Time to Visit Frederick County

The best time to visit Frederick County is in the fall when the leaves turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. Spring is another popular time as the parks and gardens burst into bloom, although the weather can be unpredictable. Winter boasts holiday festivities and Christmas markets, but the temperatures can be quite cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Frederick County

Do:

  • Visit the numerous parks and trails for some outdoor adventure.
  • Take the Ghost Tours of Historic Frederick as a spooky yet informative tour of the city’s past.
  • Try the local cuisine and attend food and wine tours for a taste of Maryland.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural or historic sites.
  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs.
  • Drive while under the influence.

Safety and Health

Safety in Frederick County

Frederick County is a safe place for travelers, but it’s still important to take precautions like anywhere else. Be aware of your surroundings, don’t leave valuables in plain sight, and lock your car and hotel room doors.

Health in Frederick County

Frederick County has excellent medical facilities, hospitals, and clinics, with highly trained doctors and medical staff. Tap water is safe to drink, and there are no serious health risks.

Getting to Frederick County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Located within easy driving distance of several major cities, Frederick County is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Frederick County

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: Located around an hour drive from Frederick.
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport: Located around an hour and a half drive from Frederick.
  • Washington Dulles International Airport: Located around an hour and 20-minute drive from Frederick.

Bus Stations Near Frederick County

  • Frederick Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Frederick, providing several bus connections with other destinations.
  • DC Union Station: Located around an hour and a half drive from Frederick. Serves as a hub for national and regional bus and train connections.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient and flexible transportation option in Frederick County. If you don’t have a car, Uber and Lyft are available in the area. Biking and walking are great options in downtown Frederick.

How to Get Around in Frederick County

Frederick County has limited public transportation options, and renting a car is the most practical way to get around. Still, many attractions are within walking or biking distance in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Frederick County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located near several major cities and popular destinations, Frederick County serves as a gateway to various exciting excursions. Here are some ideas:

  • Washington, D.C.: The United States capital is only a 45-minute drive from Frederick.
  • Gettysburg, Pennsylvania: A significant historic town in the Civil War and the battle fought there, Gettysburg is a 75-minute drive from Frederick.
  • Annapolis, Maryland: Known for its maritime history, Annapolis is a 90-minute drive from Frederick.
  • Shenandoah National Park: Famous for its skyline drive, Shenandoah National Park is less than a two-hour drive from Frederick.

Conclusion

Frederick County is a hidden gem in Maryland, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, you’re sure to find something to enjoy in Frederick County. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and experience all that Frederick County has to offer.
