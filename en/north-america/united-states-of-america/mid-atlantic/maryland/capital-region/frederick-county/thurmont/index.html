Thurmont, a small town in Frederick County, Maryland, is a hidden gem that boasts the perfect combination of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Thurmont has something to offer everyone.

Introduction to Thurmont

Located in the scenic Capital Region of Maryland, Thurmont is known for its stunning views of the Catoctin Mountains and numerous parks. It is also home to several historical sites and museums that offer a glimpse into the town’s past.

List of Places to Visit in Thurmont

Here is a list of top-rated attractions and places to visit in Thurmont:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • (1) Catoctin Mountain Park: This park offers breathtaking views of the Catoctin Mountains, as well as hiking trails, camping sites, and picnic areas.
  • (2) Camp David: The Presidential retreat, hidden in the Catoctin Mountains, is a fascinating location known for hosting world leaders and acting as a secret meeting place for presidents and their staff.
  • (3) National Shrine of St. Elizabeth Ann Seton: This shrine honors the life and work of Elizabeth Ann Seton, a Catholic saint who founded the Sisters of Charity of St. Joseph.
  • (4) Cunningham Falls State Park: This state park offers swimming, fishing, canoeing, camping, and hiking trails.
  • (5) Pry House Field Hospital Museum: This museum features exhibits on the role of the Pry family, Army medical care during the Civil War, and the life of Clara Barton .

Natural Attractions of Thurmont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • (1) Catoctin Wildlife Preserve and Zoo: This sanctuary provides a unique opportunity to connect with wildlife.
  • (2) Thurmont Vista: Hike the trail from the Visitor Center in Catoctin Mountain Park to the Thurmont Vista, with amazing views across the Maryland countryside.
  • (3) Roddy Road Covered Bridge: This iconic bridge offers visitors a glimpse into the town’s past and the opportunity to capture beautiful photos of the surrounding scenery.
  • (4) The Blue Ridge Summit Overlook: Hiking trails can be found within the Catoctin Mountain Park. The Blue Ridge Summit Overlook one of the best places for bird-watching and taking in views.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Thurmont

Thurmont is a hotspot for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking trails to try out:

  • (1) The Catoctin Trail: This trail takes hikers through the heart of Catoctin Mountain Park and offers spectacular views.
  • (2) Wolf Rock Trail: This trail is ideal for those who want a short, yet rewarding hike. It leads to a granite outcropping overlooking the Piedmont region of Maryland.
  • (3) Gambrill State Park: The park has several trails for hikers of all levels and one gloroius view called the High Knob Scenic Overlook.

Best Things to Do in Thurmont: Activities and Tours

There is an array of activities and tours in Thurmont, here are the best:

  • (1) A walking tour of Downtown Thurmont: Downtown Thurmont brims with local art, markets, delicious foods, and family-owned shops.
  • (2) River and Trail Outfitters: Take a kayak, canoe, or tube down the Monocacy River or take a scenic hike at the Catoctin Mountains with this outfitter.
  • (3) Farm Museum: The Thurmont Historical Society’s Farm Museum immereses visitors in 19th century farm life.

A Week-Long Itinerary for Thurmont

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Thurmont:

Day 1 Explore Catoctin Mountain Park
Day 2 Visit Camp David and Cunningham Falls State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Thurmont

The town’s food and drink scene certainly has something for all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Steamed Blue Crabs: If you’re in Thurmont from May to September, indulge in a Maryland tradition: crab feasts.
  • (2) Fried Chicken: Featured in The Washington Post Magazine, CarterQue’s, a locally-owned smokehouse, is the place to get juicy, crispy fried chicken.
  • (3) Vineyards and Breweries: Thurmont is home to many small-local breweries and vineyards that are famous for their own unique kinds of alcohol.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Catoctin Breeze Vineyard: Savor locally grown, hand-made wines on the Catoctin Mountains.
  • (2) Flying Dog Brewery: Ravens football fans cheer over a brew at the quirky Flying Dog Brewery, where tours and tastings are on offer.

Where to Stay in Thurmont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thurmont has several accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  • (1) The Inn at Sheppard Mansion: This historic property offers guests a unique experience to stay in a 12 room mansion with a stunning garden.
  • (2) Campgrounds in Catoctin Mountain Park: The park offers campgrounds that are perfect for nature enthusiasts.
  • (3) Thurmont East/ West: These motels provide comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Thurmont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir to take home from Thurmont, here are some recommendations:

  • (1) Artisanal crafts from Thurmont Main Street, such as stained-glass vases, pottery, or paintings by locally sourced artists.
  • (2) Flying Dog beer or Distillery-made spirits—the perfect reminder of your trip to Thurmont.

Local Culture and Customs of Thurmont

Thurmont is a quaint and friendly town with a proud history and tradition. The town’s locals are welcoming to visitors and are happy to share their stories and culture.

Best Time to Visit Thurmont

The best time to visit Thurmont is during the summer months, when the town is alive with outdoor festivals, live music, and events. Winter remains cold and covered in snow, resulting in a winter wonderland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thurmont

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when exploring Thurmont:

Do:

  • (1) Respect the natural environment around you and stay on designated trails.
  • (2) Take advantage of local breweries and vineyards
  • (3) Visit Downtown Thurmont to support local businesses.

Don’t:

  • (1) Litter in nature.
  • (2) Make noise at inappropriate hours, as Thurmont quiets down early in the evening.
  • (3) Skip short walks and overlooks, that may have excellent views or interest.

Safety and Health

Thurmont is generally a safe and healthy town for tourists. Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Thurmont

  • (1) Always lock your car doors, and keep valuables out of sight when parked.
  • (2) Hike with at least one other person and take first aid and essentials.
  • (3) Stay on designated trails while hiking to avoid injuries.

Health in Thurmont

  • (1) Stay hydrated and take extra water with you when hiking.
  • (2) Apply bug spray to avoid tick bites.
  • (3) In case of medical emergencies, Thurmont has several medical facilities.

Getting to Thurmont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thurmont is easily accessible by car, bus or plane.

Airports Near Thurmont

  • (1) Washington Dulles International Airport is 60 miles southeast of Thurmont.
  • (2) Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport is 65 miles south-west of Thurmont.

Bus Stations Near Thurmont

  • (1) The Bus Mart in Frederick that provides transport from major cities to Thurmont.

Other Options

Renting a car at either airport is the most convenient and provides the most flexibility for touring around Thurmont.

How to Get Around in Thurmont

The town is small and highly walkable to get around easily. Alternatively, renting a bike or car is another great option to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Thurmont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many great destinations to explore around Thurmont, here are a few:

  • (1) Gettysburg, Pennsylvania: Take a day trip to this historical town to explore its museums and experience the battlefield.
  • (2) Harpers Ferry, West Virginia: Learn about Harpers Ferry’s role in the Civil War by exploring its historic downtown district and visiting some of the area’s museums.
  • (3) Washington, DC: The Capital of the United States of America, is just 70 miles away from Thurmont.

Conclusion

Thurmont is a charming small town that is definitely worth adding to your list of must-visit destinations. Whether you’re a history buff, food lover, or outdoor enthusiast, Thurmont offers a rich and diverse experience. So pack your bags and come ready to explore this hidden gem of Maryland.
