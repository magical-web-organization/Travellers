Introduction to St. Michaels

St. Michaels is a charming town located in Eastern Shore, Maryland, USA. Surrounded by beautiful rivers and bays, St. Michaels is known for its rich history, cultural significance, outdoor activities, and delicious seafood. The town is also known for its peaceful and relaxing atmosphere, making it a perfect destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in St. Michaels

St. Michaels has a lot of exciting places to visit. Here is a list of some of the most popular ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Michaels is rich in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical sites where you can learn more about the town’s past. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum: This museum showcases the maritime history and traditions of the Chesapeake Bay region.
  • St. Michaels Museum: The St. Michaels Museum is located in an old church and offers visitors a glimpse into the town’s heritage.
  • Tilghman Island: Known for its fishing and seafood, Tilghman Island is also home to several historic landmarks.

Natural Attractions of St. Michaels: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Due to its stunning location, St. Michaels provides plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • St. Michaels Nature Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and walking. It offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding waters.
  • Chesapeake Bay Beach: This beach is perfect for a scenic walk, sunbathing, and even swimming during summer.
  • Miles River: This beautiful river offers opportunities for kayaking, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Michaels

St. Michaels is a popular destination among hikers and bikers due to its scenic trails and beautiful views. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in St. Michaels:

  • St. Michaels Nature Trail: As mentioned earlier, this trail offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding waters.
  • Tilghman Island Loop: This route includes a scenic ride across the Chesapeake Bay and is perfect for a relaxing bike ride.
  • Easton to St. Michaels Rail Trail: This trail takes you on a scenic ride through the countryside and is perfect for biking.

Best Things to Do in St. Michaels: Activities and Tours

St. Michaels offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum Boat Tours: These tours offer visitors an opportunity to explore the Chesapeake Bay and learn more about the maritime history of the region.
  • St. Michaels Winery Tasting Tours: Take a tour of the local St. Michaels Winery and sample some of their best wines.
  • Crab Claw Restaurant: A must-visit seafood restaurant that serves the best crab dishes in St. Michaels.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Michaels

Here is a week-long itinerary for St. Michaels that covers all the essential attractions and activities:

  • Day 1: Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum and Nature Trail
  • Day 2: Exploring Tilghman Island and its historic landmarks
  • Day 3: Easton to St. Michaels Rail Trail
  • Day 4: Crab Claw Restaurant and St. Michaels Winery Tasting Tour
  • Day 5: Chesapeake Bay Beach
  • Day 6: Miles River Boating Adventure
  • Day 7: St. Michaels Museum and shopping for local souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of St. Michaels

St. Michaels is known for its delicious seafood and has some of the best restaurants in the region. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maryland Crab Cakes: A local specialty that shouldn’t be missed.
  • Seafood Chowder: A creamy and flavorful soup made with fresh seafood.
  • Oyster Po’ Boys: A classic sandwich made with fried oysters, lettuce, and tomato.

Local Beverages

  • Eastern Shore Brewery: This brewery offers a range of beers made with local ingredients.
  • St. Michaels Winery: As mentioned earlier, this local winery is a must-visit for wine lovers.
  • Flying Dog Brewery: This brewery in nearby Frederick offers a range of award-winning beers to try.

Where to Stay in St. Michaels: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Michaels has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Inn at Perry Cabin: A luxury hotel that overlooks the Chesapeake Bay and offers a range of amenities and activities.
  • The Old Brick Inn: A historic inn that provides a cozy and comfortable stay in the heart of St. Michaels.
  • St. Michaels Harbour Inn: A waterfront hotel that offers stunning views of the harbor and the surrounding town.

What to Buy in St. Michaels: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Michaels has several unique and locally-made products that make for great souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in St. Michaels:

  • Seafood: Fresh seafood is a must-buy when in St. Michaels.
  • Artisanal crafts: St. Michaels is home to several local artisans and craft shops that offer handmade items.
  • Local wine and beer: St. Michaels Winery and Flying Dog Brewery offer a range of unique and delicious wines and beers.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Michaels

St. Michaels has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s essential to respect local customs and etiquettes when visiting. Here are some important things to keep in mind:

  • St. Michaels is a small town, and locals value privacy and quietness. Avoid making loud noises or playing music at high volumes.
  • The town is known for its seafood, and it’s essential to respect local customs and traditions when dining. Avoid wearing casual clothing or flip flops to upscale restaurants or events.
  • The town has several historic landmarks and museums that require respectful behavior. Avoid touching or mishandling artifacts, and always follow museum rules and etiquettes.

Best Time to Visit St. Michaels

The best time to visit St. Michaels is between April to November when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can get crowded, but this is also the time for outdoor activities and festivals. Winters can be cold and quiet, but it’s an excellent time to enjoy indoor activities and relaxation.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Michaels

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting St. Michaels:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Visit local museums and landmarks
  • Try local cuisine and seafood
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and environment

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or natural habitats
  • Litter or damage the local environment
  • Play loud music or make unnecessary noise
  • Ignore safety guidelines and regulations

Safety and Health

St. Michaels is generally a safe and peaceful town. However, it’s essential to keep in mind the following safety and health guidelines:

Safety in St. Michaels

  • Always lock your cars and accommodation
  • Avoid leaving valuable items in plain sight
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night
  • Call 911 in case of emergencies

Health in St. Michaels

  • St. Michaels has several medical facilities and pharmacies in case of medical emergencies.
  • Always carry necessary medication or prescriptions with you.
  • Use appropriate safety gear during outdoor activities, such as hiking or boating.

Getting to St. Michaels: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Michaels is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including:

  • Air: St. Michaels is located about an hour’s drive from the nearest airport, Baltimore Washington International Airport (BWI).
  • Bus: St. Michaels is serviced by several bus companies, including Greyhound and Megabus.
  • Car: The town is easily accessible by car and has several parking lots available for visitors.

How to Get Around in St. Michaels

St. Michaels is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, if you prefer other modes of transportation, such as taxis or rented cars, they are available.

Where to Go Next from St. Michaels:

Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Michaels is located in a stunningly beautiful region with several nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some of the best places to visit nearby:

  • Annapolis: Explore the historic town of Annapolis, known for its beautiful architecture and waterfront promenade.
  • Chesapeake Bay Bridge: Take a drive across Chesapeake Bay Bridge and enjoy stunning views of the bay and the surrounding region.
  • Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife reserve is located about an hour’s drive from St. Michaels and is home to several unique and exciting species.

Conclusion

St. Michaels is a beautiful and culturally-rich town with lots of attractions and activities to explore. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, St. Michaels offers something for everyone. Follow the guidelines and etiquettes mentioned above, and you’ll have a wonderful and memorable trip to this charming town.
