Introduction to Princess Anne

Nestled in the Eastern Shore of Maryland in the United States, Princess Anne offers an ideal combination of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Founded in 1733, Princess Anne is a charming town with small-town hospitality and authentic cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Princess Anne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several culturally important attractions in Princess Anne, including the Teackle Mansion, Princess Anne Historic District, and the Manokin Presbyterian Church.

The Teackle Mansion is a stunning example of Georgian architecture, boasting manicured gardens, exquisite antiques, and intricate woodwork. The Princess Anne Historic District is a walking tour that takes you through the town’s rich history, including Victorian houses, artisan shops, and historical landmarks. The Manokin Presbyterian Church is another must-visit attraction that dates back to the 18th century and offers a glimpse into the town’s religious and cultural roots.

Natural Attractions of Princess Anne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers should head to the Deal Island Wildlife Management Area, located just a short drive from Princess Anne, to explore the natural wonders of the area. The stunning views of the Chesapeake Bay and its tributaries, sandy beaches, and lush marshes make this a popular spot for canoeing, kayaking, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Princess Anne

Hiking and biking enthusiasts can enjoy the scenic trails of the Pocomoke River State Park, which offers over 15 miles of paved trails, perfect for taking in the beauty of the natural landscape.

Best Things to Do in Princess Anne: Activities and Tours

For those who like to get hands-on, the Somerset County Arts Council offers pottery and painting classes, while the Princess Anne Water Tours take visitors on a guided tour of nearby islands and local lighthouses. The University of Maryland Eastern Shore also hosts a variety of cultural and intellectual events throughout the year, including concerts, lectures, and art shows.

Week-Long Itinerary for Princess Anne

Day 1: Take a walking tour of the Princess Anne Historic District and visit the Teackle Mansion. Day 2: Explore the Deal Island Wildlife Management Area and enjoy a picnic by the Chesapeake Bay. Day 3: Take a pottery or painting class at the Somerset County Arts Council. Day 4: Head to the beautiful Pocomoke River State Park for a scenic hike or bike ride. Day 5: Take a guided tour of the nearby islands and lighthouses with Princess Anne Water Tours. Day 6: Attend a concert or art show at the University of Maryland Eastern Shore. Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day exploring the town’s shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Princess Anne

Must-Try Dishes

Experience Southern cuisine at its best by trying some of the town’s signature dishes, including Maryland crab cakes, fried chicken, and sweet potato pie. The Stuart’s Spices Old-Time Deli is known for its delicious sandwiches and soups made with locally-sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

The Eastern Shore is known for its wineries, and Princess Anne is no exception. Try the award-winning wines at the Bordeleau Vineyards and Winery, which also boasts stunning views of the Wicomico River.

Where to Stay in Princess Anne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Princess Anne offers a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. The Washington Inn and Tavern, a historic hotel located in the heart of town, offers elegant rooms and suites with modern amenities. The Somerset House Bed and Breakfast is another great option, with beautifully appointed rooms, delicious breakfast, and easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Princess Anne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Princess Anne home with you by browsing the town’s artisan shops. The Eastern Shore Tea Company offers a variety of delicious blends, while the Main Street Gallery showcases the work of local artists and craftspeople.

Local Culture and Customs of Princess Anne

Princess Anne is known for its small-town hospitality and welcoming community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions and follow the town’s cultural norms.

Best Time to Visit Princess Anne

The best time to visit Princess Anne is during the spring and fall, when the town’s natural beauty is at its peak. Summer is also a popular time to visit, but be prepared for higher prices and larger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Princess Anne

Do:

  • Explore the town’s historic district and architecture
  • Take a guided boat tour to see the lighthouses and islands
  • Try the local cuisine, including Maryland crab cakes and sweet potato pie

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s cultural norms and traditions
  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas
  • Disrupt local wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Princess Anne

Princess Anne is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take standard safety precautions like locking their cars and securing valuables.

Health in Princess Anne

Visitors should be aware of any allergies or medical conditions and plan accordingly for the trip. The town does have medical facilities, but it’s always best to bring any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Princess Anne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Princess Anne

The closest airports to Princess Anne are the Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport and Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport.

Bus Stations Near Princess Anne

Greyhound offers service to Salisbury, which is approximately 30 minutes away from Princess Anne.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Princess Anne or take a taxi from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Princess Anne

Walking and biking are the best ways to explore the town, while renting a car is recommended for those who want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Princess Anne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The charming towns of Berlin and Snow Hill are located just a short drive from Princess Anne and offer a similar small-town atmosphere and welcoming community. Ocean City, known for its boardwalk and beach, is also a popular nearby destination.

Conclusion

Princess Anne is a hidden gem in the Eastern Shore of Maryland, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and authentic culture. With so many attractions, activities, and delicious food options, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.
