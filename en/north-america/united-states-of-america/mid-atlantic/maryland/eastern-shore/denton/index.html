Introduction to Denton

Located in Eastern Shore and overlooking the Choptank River, Denton offers a charming mix of historical sites, natural beauty, and modern entertainment. Founded in 1781, the town is steeped in history, with a rich cultural heritage and an abundance of tourist attractions to match. From outstanding museums to picturesque parks, Denton caters to the interests of all age groups.

List of Places to Visit in Denton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Museum of Rural Life

Visit the Museum of Rural Life to learn about the rural community’s lifestyle and traditions, as well as the agricultural tools and methods that shaped the region. The museum displays interesting artifacts and photographs that give a glimpse into the past of the Denton community.

2. Choptank River Heritage Center

The Choptank River Heritage Center celebrates bountiful events of the town’s past through exhibits, arts, and performances. Visitors have a chance to experience the maritime history of the Chesapeake Bay and learn about the boat-building methods that propelled the town’s prosperity.

Natural Attractions of Denton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Martinak State Park

Martinak State Park is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, offering multiple hiking and biking trails through fairytale-like forests. For those who love water sports and fishing, the park also has a pond with motorboat rentals and fishing opportunities.

2. Tuckahoe State Park

Tuckahoe State Park surrounds the Tuckahoe Creek, an eastern branch of the Choptank River. Visitors can hike the trails, go boating, and indulge in fishing, hunting and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Denton

Whether you prefer hiking, walking, or cycling, you’ll love Denton’s picturesque routes, which offer a blend of history and nature. Start with the Tuckahoe State Park, where you’ll find 20 miles of peaceful, interconnected trails leading to the park’s fabled landmarks. Additionally, the Choptank River Run includes 15.8 miles of rustic trails, featuring coastal forests and stunning vistas of Choptank River.

Best Things to Do in Denton: Activities and Tours

Choose Your Own Adventure Tour

Take an adventure tour in Denton and discover the landscapes and lifestyle of the region. This tour includes kayaking, cycling, and walking options, with the main highlight of the trip being an exhilarating zip line tour through the mountains.

Local Wineries and Breweries Tour

Denton features a thriving winery and brewery scene. From Local Choice Spirits distillery, featuring highly acclaimed wines and spirits, to the Bull & Goat Brewery, with its range of delicious beers, the town offers a slew of excellent wineries and breweries where you can taste the flavors of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Denton

Must-Try Dishes

Denton has a diverse culinary scene that features a fusion of local cuisines and trendy international eats. Don’t miss out on fried oysters, a Maryland staple, or the grilled, seasoned rockfish.

Local Beverages

The Local Choice Spirits distillery offers an incredible selection of unique wines and aged spirits. You can also try Bull & Goat Brewery’s locally brewed beer or delicious cocktails in the many local bars and pubs around the town.

Where to Stay in Denton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For a luxurious stay, Hyatt Regency Chesapeake Bay is an excellent option, offering views of the Chesapeake Bay and a variety of high-end amenities. There are also many charming guesthouses and B&Bs near the waterfront, including the stately Towers Bed & Breakfast, which has been operating since 1785.

What to Buy in Denton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Denton offers a variety of items for visitors looking for mementos of their trip. The town is known for its famous copper tiles, wooden carved ducks, and hand-rolled cigars.

Local Culture and Customs of Denton

In Denton, politeness and respect are highly valued. Greetings are extremely important, and the handshake, followed by the exchange of names, is customary. Locals enjoy dressing appropriately for events and visiting the many cultural attractions around the town.

Best Time to Visit Denton

The ideal time to visit Denton is between April and June or September and November when the weather is mild and the crowds are thin.

Do’s and Don’ts of Denton

Do:

  • Greet people politely and respectfully.
  • Dress formally when attending events.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Denton.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear in public places.
  • Speak in a loud or aggressive voice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Denton

Denton is considered a safe place to visit, but visitors should exercise the same precautions as they would in any small town. Keep valuables safely secured, and be aware of your surroundings when walking in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Denton

Denton has excellent health facilities, including the Shore Health Hospital and many clinics.

Getting to Denton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Denton

The Salisbury-Wicomico Regional Airport is only 35 miles from Denton, and Baltimore-Washington International Airport is 75 miles away. Both airports offer easy access to the town by rental car, bus, or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Denton

Denton is served by the BayRunner Shuttle, which provides daily departures from Baltimore-Washington International Airport.

Other Options

For travelers who prefer to drive, Denton offers easy access to major highways, including US Route 50 and Interstate 95.

How to Get Around in Denton

Walking is a great way to explore Denton’s historical sites, but for more extensive sightseeing, a rental car is essential. Bicycles are also an excellent mode of transportation around the town, with many scenic bike paths.

Where to Go Next from Denton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Denton is in close proximity to several other Eastern Shore towns, including Easton, St. Michaels, and Cambridge. Each of these towns offers its own unique history and attractions, making them great destinations for day trips.

Conclusion

Denton is a town that caters to travelers of all types, providing an incredible mix of natural beauty, historical sites, and modern entertainment options. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, or simply relaxing by the waterfront, Denton is a destination that will leave you with unforgettable memories that you’ll treasure for years to come.
