Introduction to Easton

Easton is a charming town located in the heart of Talbot County on Maryland’s Eastern Shore. Known for its beautiful parks, historic landmarks, bustling downtown area, and vibrant arts scene, Easton is perfect for travelers looking for a mix of outdoor activities and cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Easton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Academy Art Museum: The Academy Art Museum is a cultural hub in Easton that features rotating exhibitions of contemporary art, historic artworks, and cultural artifacts.

  • Historical Society of Talbot County: The Historical Society of Talbot County is home to a wealth of historical archives, including photographs, manuscripts, and artifacts. The Society also offers walking and driving tours of Historical Easton and Talbot County.

Natural Attractions of Easton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pickering Creek Audubon Center: Nature lovers will enjoy exploring the 410-acre Pickering Creek Audubon Center, which features scenic walking trails, bird-watching opportunities, and educational programs for visitors of all ages.

  • Tuckahoe State Park: Tuckahoe State Park is located just 30 minutes from Easton and offers visitors a chance to hike, bike, and enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Easton

  • Talbot County Hikers: Talbot County Hikers is a local hiking club that offers guided hikes throughout the region, including in and around Easton.

  • Talbot County Bike Tours: Talbot County Bike Tours offers guided bike tours of Easton and the surrounding area, including historical landmarks and scenic routes.

Best Things to Do in Easton: Activities and Tours

  • Chesapeake Ghost Tour: The Chesapeake Ghost Tour is a unique way to explore the history and hauntings of Easton.

  • [Third Haven Friends Meeting House](https://www.thirdhaven.org/: The Third Haven Friends Meeting House is a historic Quaker meeting house that dates back to the 17th century. Guided tours are available.

Food and Drink Scene of Easton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Maryland is known for its delicious crab cakes, and Easton is no exception.

  • Rockfish: Rockfish (also known as striped bass) is a popular fish dish in Maryland, and Easton’s waterfront location means you can expect to find some quality options.

Local Beverages

  • Frogtown: Frogtown is a local winery that produces a wide range of wines, including whites, reds, and sparkling wines.

  • Eastern Shore Brewing: Eastern Shore Brewing is a microbrewery in Easton that specializes in small-batch, handcrafted beers.

Where to Stay in Easton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Easton: Hampton Inn Easton is conveniently located in downtown Easton and offers comfortable rooms and amenities, including a fitness center and indoor pool.

  • The Tidewater Inn: The Tidewater Inn is a historic hotel in the heart of Easton and offers guests a luxurious experience, complete with fine dining options and a full-service spa.

What to Buy in Easton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum Store: The Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum Store offers a wide range of gifts and souvenirs, including books, apparel, and locally-made crafts.

  • Talbot County Farmers Market: The Talbot County Farmers Market is a great place to pick up fresh, local produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Easton

Easton has a vibrant arts scene, and visitors can expect to find plenty of galleries, theaters, and live music venues throughout the town. Easton is also known for its history and traditions, including its longstanding tradition of oyster farming and crabbing.

Best Time to Visit Easton

The best time to visit Easton is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are plentiful. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be chilly and rainy, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Easton

Do:

  • Explore the downtown area
  • Sample the local seafood
  • Take a guided tour of the historic landmarks

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural beauty of the area
  • Miss out on the local culture and arts scene
  • Forget to bring comfortable walking shoes

Safety and Health

Safety in Easton

Easton is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take normal precautions and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Easton

Easton has quality healthcare facilities, including the University of Maryland Shore Regional Health, which has several locations throughout the region. Visitors should also familiarize themselves with the local emergency services.

Getting to Easton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Easton

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (90 miles)
  • Philadelphia International Airport (110 miles)

Bus Stations Near Easton

  • Greyhound Bus Station (25 miles)
  • Eastern Shore Transit Authority (0.5 miles)

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Easton or take a train to one of the nearby cities and rent a car for the remainder of their trip.

How to Get Around in Easton

Rental cars are available for visitors who want to explore beyond the town center, while walking or biking is a great way to explore the downtown area and its many attractions.

Where to Go Next from Easton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St. Michaels, Maryland: St. Michaels is a historic town known for its beautiful waterfront location and charming downtown area.

  • Assateague Island National Seashore: Assateague Island is located just a short drive from Easton and is known for its stunning beaches and wild ponies.

Conclusion

Easton is a small town with a big personality and plenty of attractions to keep travelers busy and entertained. With its mix of history, art, and natural beauty, Easton is an excellent destination for visitors looking for a unique and authentic experience.
