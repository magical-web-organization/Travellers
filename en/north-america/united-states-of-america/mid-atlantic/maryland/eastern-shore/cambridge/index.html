Introduction to Cambridge

Cambridge is a charming city located on the Eastern Shore of Maryland in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Situated on the banks of the Choptank River, Cambridge boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Visitors to Cambridge can expect to enjoy the scenic views, explore museums, savor culinary delights, and experience local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Cambridge

Here are the must-visit places in Cambridge:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Harriet Tubman Museum and Educational Center

Founded in honor of Harriet Tubman, a leading figure in the Underground Railroad, the Harriet Tubman Museum and Educational Center is a must-visit museum in Cambridge. Visitors can discover the story of Tubman’s life and learn about the Underground Railroad, a network of people and safe houses that helped slaves escape to freedom in the North.

Richardson Maritime Museum

The Richardson Maritime Museum is an ideal destination for maritime enthusiasts. The museum boasts a collection of antique Chesapeake Bay boats, maritime artifacts, and a library of maritime literature.

The Dorchester County Historical Society Museum

The Dorchester County Historical Society Museum is an excellent source of information on the history of Cambridge and the Eastern Shore. Visitors can examine historical artifacts and learn about the lives of the people who lived here.

Natural Attractions of Cambridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge

The Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge is a great place to take in the beauty of the Chesapeake Bay. The refuge is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including bald eagles, blue herons, and osprey. Hiking, biking, and bird watching are all popular activities here.

Sailwinds Park and Marina

The Sailwinds Park and Marina is a fantastic spot for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can rent a kayak or paddleboard, and paddle through the calm waters of the Choptank River. The park also boasts picnic areas, bike trails, and playgrounds.

Cambridge Creek

Cambridge Creek is a great place to explore on a watercraft. Visitors can rent a boat, take a guided tour, or fish in the creek. The area is home to several fishing tournaments, so if you are interested in fishing, be sure to check the schedule before you go.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cambridge

Cambridge offers several exciting hiking and biking routes that are perfect for those who love the outdoors. The following trails come with picturesque views and diverse wildlife:

  • Blackwater Rails to Trails
  • Great Marsh Trail
  • Iron Club Road Trail

Best Things to Do in Cambridge: Activities and Tours

From guided tours to hands-on experiences, there are plenty of things to do in Cambridge. Here are some of the most popular activities:

  • Take a walking tour of Cambridge
  • Rent kayaks and paddleboards
  • Go fishing
  • Explore the art galleries and antique shops downtown
  • Attend one of the many festivals hosted throughout the year

Week-Long Itinerary for Cambridge

Here is an itinerary for a week-long visit to Cambridge:

Day 1: Explore downtown Cambridge and visit the Harriet Tubman Museum Day 2: Visit the Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge and enjoy an afternoon of bird watching and hiking Day 3: Rent kayaks and paddle around the Choptank River Day 4: Take a guided tour of Cambridge Creek Day 5: Visit the Richardson Maritime Museum and learn about the history of the Chesapeake Bay Day 6: Take a bike ride on the Blackwater Rails to Trails route Day 7: Attend a local festival or explore the local art galleries

Local Culture and Customs of Cambridge

Cambridge’s culture and customs are greatly influenced by the Chesapeake Bay and the Eastern Shore. The cuisine is centered around seafood, and there is a deep appreciation for the area’s maritime history. Visitors should also be aware of the importance of preserving the natural beauty of the area, which is why you will see many environmentally friendly initiatives here.

Best Time to Visit Cambridge

The best time to visit Cambridge is between April to October when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are plentiful. If you are interested in attending the local festivals, be sure to check the schedules since some festivals are seasonal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cambridge

Do:

  • Visit the Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge
  • Try the local seafood
  • Explore the local art galleries and antique shops
  • Attend one of the local festivals

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural areas
  • Overfish or take more than you can eat
  • Overlook the local history and culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Cambridge

Cambridge is a relatively safe destination for tourists. Visitors should take the standard precautions of urban travel, such as being aware of their surroundings and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Cambridge

Cambridge is not associated with any significant health risks. You should be careful to pack the appropriate clothing and gear for any outdoor activities you plan to undertake, particularly during the warmer months.

Getting to Cambridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cambridge is easily accessible from other parts of the Eastern Shore or Washington, D.C. Here are the various transportation options available:

Airports Near Cambridge

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI)
  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA)
  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL)

Bus Stations Near Cambridge

  • Greyhound Bus Lines (nearby in Easton)
  • Shore Transit (local bus service)

Other Options

  • Rental cars are available at airports and in downtown Cambridge.
  • Amtrak train service is available in nearby Salisbury.

How to Get Around in Cambridge

The best way to get around Cambridge is by car or bike. Renting a car can be the most convenient and easiest option, though it isn’t necessary if you plan on staying in the downtown area. There are also several bike rental services that can be a great way to explore the city.

Conclusion

Cambridge is a vibrant city that is home to scenic views, outdoor adventures, historical and cultural attractions, and more. With the help of this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your visit to Cambridge with ease and enjoy a memorable trip that will leave you craving to come back.
