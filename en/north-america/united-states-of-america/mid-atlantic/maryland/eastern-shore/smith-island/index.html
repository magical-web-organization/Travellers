Introduction to Smith Island

Smith Island is a charming small island located in the Chesapeake Bay on the Eastern Shore of Maryland in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. It is a unique location known for its lovely culture, food, and landscapes. It is also the only remaining inhabited island of the Chesapeake Bay’s over 200 inhabitants.

List of Places to Visit in Smith Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Make sure to visit Smith Island Cultural Center and you will get to learn about the life and culture of the local people.

The Bayside History Museum is another place to visit, with exhibits covering the Island’s history since its settlement in the 17th century.

The Ewell School House is another important attraction. It serves as a museum showcasing the history of education in Smith Island.

Natural Attractions of Smith Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Chesapeake Bay is the main attraction in Smith Island. Kayaking, boating, and fishing are popular activities on this blue bay waters.

The wildlife is also a major attraction as visitors get to see a variety of species including ducks, geese, swans, and eagles.

The Sunset Over the Chesapeake Bay is a beautiful way to end the day. Head over to one of the island’s jetties or beaches to watch the sun go down.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smith Island

Smith Island is a small island with no more than 8 miles, but there are a few hiking and biking trials worth exploring.

The Drum Point Lighthouse Trail is a 2.6-mile trail that offers a scenic view of this historic lighthouse.

The Ewell Island Trail is a 1.7-mile trial that takes you through the local village of Ewell, with picturesque views of the Bay along the way.

Best Things to Do in Smith Island: Activities and Tours

The Smith Island Experience Boat Tour is a great way to explore the island. The tour includes a visit to the island’s crabbing industry, a walk around the charming village of Ewell, a visit to Tylerton, the smallest town on the island, and much more.

Fishing in Chesapeake Bay is a great activity. It offers visitors the opportunity to catch striped bass, blue crabs, croaker or spot fish.

The Watermen’s Heritage Tour is a fascinating tour that will teach you all about the history of the Island’s watermen and their catches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Smith Island

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Smith Island, here’s an itinerary to consider:

Day 1: Two-hour Watermen Heritage Tour. Day 2: Rent a bike and explore the various hiking trails on the island, including the Drum Point Lighthouse Trail and Ewell Island Trail. Day 3: Head over to Tylerton, the smallest town on the island, and join a local river trip. Day 4: Take the Smith Island Experience Boat Tour to explore the rest of the island. Day 5: Go fishing in Chesapeake Bay or explore fishing town of Crisfield. Stop at Watermen’s Inn for lunch in Crisfield. Day 6: Learn more about Smith Island’s culture by visiting the Cultural Center and the Bayside History Museum. Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Assateague Island or explore other parts of Maryland’s Eastern Shore.

Food and Drink Scene of Smith Island

Must-Try Dishes

Smith Island is famous for its Smith Island Cake, a multi-layered dessert made with buttercream and cake layers.

Crab Cakes are another must-try dish. They are made from local crabs and are a delicious way to experience the island’s seafood.

The island’s fresh oysters, while not for everyone, are a must-try. They are served raw in shell, or cooked.

Local Beverages

Smith Island is also known for its sweet tea and refreshing lemonade.

Restaurants and Cafes

After your other adventures, be sure to indulge in the island’s culinary delights by visiting the Bayside Inn Restaurant. The place has a great view of the bay and offers a wide variety of seafood, including crab cakes.

Make sure you try some of the local tea and lemonade at the Drum Point Market, while also taking a break from your activities.

Where to Stay in Smith Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Since Smith Island is a small island and retains its unique culture, hotels, hostels, and guesthouses are limited. The Drum Point Market has a few simple apartments and the Bayside Inn Restaurant has a few rooms available.

As an alternative, tourists can stay at the nearby town of Crisfield at the Steamboat Inn, a historic 19th-century Inn which showcases the life and culture of Smith Island.

What to Buy in Smith Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs that represent the local area, make sure to buy a cookbook that features some of the island’s favorite, long-established recipes. It’s a great way to learn about the island while also being able to take home a small piece of the island with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Smith Island

The people of Smith Island describe their culture as being different from other parts of the Eastern Shore, but it is tied to the industries that have supported the island for generations. It includes a focus on preserving the way of watermen and the delicious seafood that supports life on the Chesapeake Bay.

Best Time to Visit Smith Island

The best time to visit Smith Island is between April and October when the weather is ideal. The high-season lasts from June to August, which also offers the most availability of activities and tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Smith Island

Do:

  • Always greet people with respect and say hello.
  • Enjoy the Island’s food, culture, and traditions.
  • Be respectful of the Island’s natural environment.

Don’t:

  • Don’t place anything other than toilet paper in the toilet or drain. The island does not have sewage treatment facilities.
  • Don’t just drive around the Island expecting things to be open when you arrive unexpectedly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Smith Island

Smith Island is a very safe place to visit. However, visitors are advised to take precautions during extreme weather such as hurricanes and tornadoes.

Health in Smith Island

Smith Island has a clinic that provides basic health services. However, if you need additional medical attention, you will need to go to the nearby Crisfield.

Getting to Smith Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Smith Island

The closest airports to Smith Island are Baltimore-Washington International Airport and Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport, both located a few hours away.

Bus Stations Near Smith Island

Greyhound Bus Line goes to Princess Anne, a nearby town, where one can take a local taxi or ferry to Smith Island.

Other Options

A ferry service runs between Smith Island and Crisfield. Additionally, there is limited availability for private charter boats to the island.

How to Get Around in Smith Island

The best way to explore the Island is on foot, bike, or by boat. Visitors can also hire taxis to take them around the island.

Where to Go Next from Smith Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

A visit to Smith Island pairs nicely with a visit to Assateague Island, famous for its wild ponies, island ponies, stocked fishing ponds, bird watching, crabbing, camping, and biking.

Two popular towns on the Maryland Eastern Shore also make great compliments to a visit to Smith Island. In Berlin, visitors can enjoy a walk through the picturesque downtown area of this charming town. In Ocean City, visitors can enjoy the its world-famous boardwalk lined up with amusements, restaurants and phenomenal beachfront. Their ferris wheel is a must-see, especially at night.

Conclusion

Smith Island is a unique destination located in Maryland’s Eastern Shore. It offers everything from cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, delicious cuisine, and access to refreshing drinks. A visit to Smith Island is one of the few remaining opportunities to experience living on a Chesapeake Bay island that has managed to retain its charm and uniqueness for centuries.
