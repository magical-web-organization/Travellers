Introduction to Ocean City

Ocean City is a popular vacation destination located on the Eastern Shore of Maryland. It is a beautiful coastal city that attracts tourists from all over the world. What sets Ocean City apart is its long stretch of pristine beaches, thrilling amusement parks, and exciting nightlife. The city is also home to several culturally significant museums and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Ocean City

If you are planning to visit Ocean City, here are the top attractions and things to do that should not be missed.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ocean City Lifesaving Station Museum: This museum showcases the maritime history of the city. You will find exhibits on shipwrecks, rescue missions, and the development of the city.
  • The Ocean City Life-Saving Station Museum: This museum is located in a restored 1891 Life-Saving Station and has interactive exhibits on the history of Ocean City.
  • Ocean City Life-Saving Station: This is a historic landmark building that was built in 1891 and operated as a life-saving station until 1964.
  • Mission BBQ: This restaurant serves great food and honors first responders and veterans.

Natural Attractions of Ocean City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Beach: The Ocean City Beach is the most popular attraction. The 10-mile-long sandy stretch has lifeguards, restrooms, and showers.
  • Assateague Island National Seashore: This protected island is home to wild ponies and offers excellent opportunities for bird watching, kayaking, and camping.
  • Jolly Roger Amusement Park: This amusement park is perfect for families with kids. It has water parks, roller coasters, and mini-golf courses.
  • Ocean City Boardwalk: This is a 3-mile-long wooden boardwalk that offers plenty of shops, restaurants, and entertainment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocean City

Ocean City is a great destination for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Some of the popular bike routes include Assateague Island National Seashore, Berlin, the Maryland Coast, and the Pocomoke River Trail. On the other hand, if you are looking for some scenic hiking trails, check out the Assateague Island National Seashore, Assawoman Bay State Wildlife Area, and Island Nature Trail.

Best Things to Do in Ocean City: Activities and Tours

Ocean City offers plenty of activities and tours for tourists. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Ocean City Parasail: Take a thrilling parasailing tour and enjoy views of the coast from high up above.
  • Kayaking tours: Explore the waterways and marshes of the eastern shore by kayak.
  • The OC Rocket: This roller coaster offers breathtaking views of the Ocean City Boardwalk and beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Ocean City

Ocean City is known for its seafood, and there are plenty of restaurants that serve delicious crabs, oysters, and other seafood dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: This is a classic Maryland dish made with lump crab meat, spices, and bread crumbs.
  • Steamed Crabs: The seafood feast is incomplete without steamed crabs seasoned with Old Bay seasoning.
  • Oysters: Ocean City’s bayside waters offer an abundance of oysters, which are served raw, grilled, or fried.

Local Beverages

  • SeaQuench Ale: A sour beer by Dogfish Head Brewery, made with sea salt, sea weed, and lime peel.
  • Orange Crush: This is a popular cocktail made with orange juice, vodka, triple sec, and lemon-lime soda.

Where to Stay in Ocean City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ocean City offers plenty of choices for accommodation that caters to all budgets. The beachfront has many luxury hotels, while the midtown area has budget-friendly hotels and guesthouses. Some popular options include Hilton Suites Ocean City Oceanfront, Holiday Inn Oceanfront, and Grand Hotel and Spa.

What to Buy in Ocean City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the popular souvenirs and local products you can bring back from Ocean City.

  • T-shirts and beachwear: You will find plenty of stores that sell beachwear and t-shirts with Ocean City logos and designs.
  • Saltwater Taffy: This sweet and chewy candy is a popular treat.
  • Old Bay Seasoning: This spice blend is a staple in Maryland cuisine and can be found in most stores.
  • Arts and Crafts: Local crafts, such as seashell art, paintings, and jewelry, can also make unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ocean City

Ocean City has a rich maritime culture that is reflected in its culinary traditions, festivals, and events. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and contribute to keeping the city clean and beautiful.

Best Time to Visit Ocean City

The best time to visit Ocean City is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. June and July are the peak tourist months, but May and September offer a comparatively quieter and less crowded experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ocean City

Do:

  • Respect the beach rules and regulations.
  • Explore the city on a bike or by walking.
  • Try the local seafood.
  • Take a boat tour and watch dolphins and whales.

Don’t:

  • Leave your litter on the beach.
  • Bring pets to the beach during peak summer months.
  • Drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ocean City

Ocean City is generally a safe city for tourists. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and other petty crimes that can happen in crowded tourist areas. Follow the standard safety tips such as not leaving valuables unattended and locking your car and hotel room.

Health in Ocean City

Visitors should also take necessary health precautions such as staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen, and avoiding swimming in areas with rip currents. There are many pharmacies and medical clinics available in Ocean City, should you need medical attention.

Getting to Ocean City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocean City

The nearest airport to Ocean City is Salisbury Regional Airport, which is only 30 minutes away. Other airports that are within easy reach are Baltimore Washington International Airport and Reagan National Airport.

Bus Stations Near Ocean City

Greyhound and Peter Pan Bus Lines operate regular services to Ocean City. The bus station is located downtown and is only a few minutes away from the beach.

Other Options

Car rentals are also available at all major airports. Ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft operate within the city, making it easy to get around.

How to Get Around in Ocean City

There are plenty of transportation options in Ocean City, such as buses, taxis, and rental bicycles. The city also provides shuttle services that operate along the boardwalk and downtown areas.

Where to Go Next from Ocean City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are looking to explore the surrounding areas, here are some of the popular destinations that are within driving distance from Ocean City.

  • Berlin, Maryland: This charming historic town is only 15 minutes away.
  • Rehoboth Beach, Delaware: This beach town is only an hour’s drive away and offers excellent shopping and dining options.
  • Chincoteague Island, Virginia: This scenic island is a two-hour drive and is known for its wild ponies and the Chincoteague National Wildlife Refuge.

Conclusion

Ocean City is a perfect getaway destination for those looking to soak up some sun and enjoy exciting outdoor activities. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most out of your Ocean City vacation.
