Introduction to Crisfield

Crisfield, Maryland is a charming seaside town located in the heart of the Eastern Shore, a peninsula in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Known for its rich history and natural beauty, Crisfield is a popular destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in a laid-back coastal lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Crisfield

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking to relax and unwind, Crisfield has plenty of attractions to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in Crisfield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • J. Millard Tawes Museum: Located in the former governor’s mansion, this museum explores the history, culture, and natural resources of the Eastern Shore.
  • Ward Brothers’ Workshop: The Ward Brothers were renowned for their skill in carving and painting decoys, which were used for hunting and are now considered works of art.
  • Somers Cove Marina: Originally built in the early 1900s, the marina has become a hub for boaters and fishermen, and is also home to some of Crisfield’s best seafood restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Crisfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Janes Island State Park: With over 2,900 acres of marshland, beaches, and forests, Janes Island offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, kayaking, and camping.
  • Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel: This engineering marvel spans 23 miles and connects the Eastern Shore to Virginia.
  • Tangier Island: Accessible by ferry, Tangier Island is a charming island town that is home to a unique culture and way of life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crisfield

The Eastern Shore is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and Crisfield is no exception. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Crisfield:

  • Janes Island Loop: This 3.1-mile loop takes you through pine forests, salt marshes, and sandy beaches.
  • Somers Cove Marina to the Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel: This 10-mile round-trip route takes you along the water’s edge, offering stunning views of the Chesapeake Bay.
  • Folly Creek Loop: This 8.8-mile loop takes you through scenic wetlands and forests, with plenty of bird-watching opportunities along the way.

Best Things to Do in Crisfield: Activities and Tours

From kayaking and fishing to historic tours and cultural events, there’s plenty to see and do in Crisfield. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Smith Island Kayak Tour: Explore the tranquil waters and stunning scenery of the Chesapeake Bay on a guided kayak tour of Smith Island.
  • Skipjack Cruise: Take a relaxing sail on a traditional Chesapeake Bay skipjack, offering an up-close look at the area’s rich maritime heritage.
  • Crabbing and Fishing: Join a local guide on a crabbing or fishing trip, and learn firsthand how to catch and prepare the area’s famous seafood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Crisfield

If you’re planning to spend a week in Crisfield, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: J. Millard Tawes Museum, Ward Brothers’ Workshop, and Somers Cove Marina.
  • Day 2: Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel and Tangier Island.
  • Day 3-5: Janes Island State Park, with plenty of time to hike, kayak, and relax on the beach.
  • Day 6: Take a skipjack cruise and explore Crisfield’s maritime heritage.
  • Day 7: Head out on a crabbing or fishing trip and enjoy a seafood feast.

Food and Drink Scene of Crisfield

With its abundant seafood and local produce, Crisfield is a food lover’s paradise. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smith Island Cake: This multi-layered cake is a local specialty, made with yellow cake and chocolate icing.
  • Crab Cakes: Made with fresh Chesapeake Bay crab, Crisfield’s crab cakes are renowned for their flavor and quality.
  • Rockfish: Also known as striped bass, this mild-flavored fish is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • National Bohemian (Natty Boh): This Baltimore-brewed beer is a staple of the Chesapeake Bay region.
  • RAR Brewing: Located in nearby Cambridge, RAR Brewing offers a variety of craft beers and is a favorite among locals.
  • Island Chai: This refreshing tea is infused with local herbs and spices, and is a perfect way to cool off on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Crisfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to waterfront hotels, Crisfield offers a range of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget. Some of the top places to stay in Crisfield include:

  • Somers Cove Marina: With rooms overlooking the marina and a prime location for boating enthusiasts, Somers Cove Marina is a popular choice among visitors.
  • Drum Point Market & Lodge: This quaint bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.
  • Key West Cottages: Located just steps from the beach, these charming cottages offer the perfect coastal getaway.

What to Buy in Crisfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for souvenirs, gifts, or local products to bring home, Crisfield has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to buy in Crisfield:

  • Crab-themed souvenirs: From T-shirts to mugs to keychains, Crisfield has a wide selection of crab-themed souvenirs.
  • Local seafood: Stock up on fresh seafood, including crab, oysters, and fish.
  • Handmade decoys: The Ward Brothers’ Workshop is a great place to pick up a handmade decoy, either as a gift or as a piece of art to display in your own home.

Local Culture and Customs of Crisfield

Crisfield’s unique history and coastal location have helped shape its distinct culture and customs. Some of the things you should know about Crisfield include:

  • Crabbing and fishing are a way of life for many locals, and seafood is an important part of the local diet.
  • The area’s maritime heritage is celebrated with festivals and events throughout the year, including the National Hard Crab Derby and the Skipjack Races.
  • Smith Island Cake, a local specialty, is the official dessert of Maryland.

Best Time to Visit Crisfield

The best time to visit Crisfield is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the water is perfect for swimming and boating. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, you may want to consider visiting in the spring or fall, when the weather is still pleasant but there are fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crisfield

To make the most of your trip to Crisfield, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood, especially the crab cakes and oysters.
  • Do spend time exploring the natural beauty of the area, including Janes Island State Park and the Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel.
  • Do attend one of the area’s festivals or events to experience the local culture and cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and bug spray, as the area can be hot and buggy in the summer.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask the locals for recommendations, as they are often the best source of information about the area.

Safety and Health

Crisfield is generally a safe and healthy destination, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy in Crisfield:

Safety in Crisfield

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Lock your car and hotel room when you’re not there, and keep your valuables out of sight.
  • Observe all safety regulations when boating or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Crisfield

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially in the summer.
  • Wear sunscreen and bug spray to protect against the sun and bugs.
  • Pack a basic first-aid kit, including any prescription medications you may need.

Getting to Crisfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Crisfield is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Crisfield

  • Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport: Located about 45 minutes from Crisfield, this airport offers flights from several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Crisfield

  • Greyhound: The closest Greyhound bus station is in Salisbury, about 45 minutes from Crisfield.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Crisfield and the surrounding areas.
  • Private shuttle: You can also arrange for a private shuttle service to take you to and from Crisfield.

How to Get Around in Crisfield

The best way to get around Crisfield is by car, as public transportation is limited. However, there are several other options to consider:

  • Walking: Many of Crisfield’s attractions and restaurants are within walking distance of each other.
  • Biking: Renting a bike is a great way to explore the area’s scenic bike trails.
  • Boating: If you have access to a boat, you can explore the waterways around Crisfield on your own.

Where to Go Next from Crisfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Crisfield, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Assateague Island National Seashore: Located about an hour from Crisfield, Assateague Island offers miles of pristine beaches and opportunities to see wild horses.
  • St. Michaels: This charming town is located about an hour from Crisfield and is known for its historic homes, boutiques, and restaurants.
  • Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum: Located in nearby St. Michaels, this museum explores the history and culture of the Chesapeake Bay region.

Conclusion

Crisfield, Maryland is a hidden gem on the Eastern Shore, offering a rich history, natural beauty, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, Crisfield has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable coastal getaway in Crisfield.
