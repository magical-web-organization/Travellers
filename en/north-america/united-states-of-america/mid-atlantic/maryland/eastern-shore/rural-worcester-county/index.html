Introduction to Rural Worcester County

Rural Worcester County is a hidden gem of the Eastern Shore in Maryland. Surrounded by lush and green scenic views, Rural Worcester County is a perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and relax in a countryside setting. This travel guide will take you on a journey of Rural Worcester County, highlighting the best places to visit, activities to do, and local cuisine to try.

List of Places to Visit in Rural Worcester County

Rural Worcester County offers a wide variety of places to visit that will cater to any kind of traveler. Here are some of the must-visit spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Julia A Purnell Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the Worcester County region. The exhibits feature artifacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the history of the everyday lives of local people.

  • Furnace Town Living Heritage Museum: This museum depicts the life of the people who worked at the iron furnace in the 19th century. Visitors can learn about the daily lives of the people and watch live demonstrations of traditional crafts.

Natural Attractions of Rural Worcester County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Assateague Island National Seashore: This beachfeatures miles of untouched and pristine sand for visitors to enjoy. It’s also home to over 300 wild ponies, which are a must-see attraction.

  • Pocomoke River State Park: This park is over 15,000 acres and offers various activities like hiking, fishing, and hunting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rural Worcester County

Rural Worcester County has some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Maryland, offering gorgeous views of nature. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • Chincoteague National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge has around 14 kilometers of hiking trails that lead to scenic views of the Assateague Island.

  • Nassawango Creek Preserve: This preserve features two easy-to-navigate hiking trails that allow visitors to see the exquisite flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Rural Worcester County: Activities and Tours

  • Get aboard the Tall Ship El Galeón: This 170-foot long wooden ship is a replica of the galleons used by the Spanish in the 16th century. It offers visitors a glimpse into the past while also providing an unforgettable experience sailing the waters of Rural Worcester County.

  • Atlantic Tails for a sunset sail: Bring your loved ones and make memories as you relax on a catamaran while watching the beautiful sunset.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rural Worcester County

For those who plan to visit for a week, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Julia A Purnell Museum and Furnace Town Living Heritage Museum
  • Day 2: Enjoy the beach and see ponies at Assateague Island National Seashore
  • Day 3: Explore the Pocomoke River State Park
  • Day 4: Take a Tall Ship El Galeón boat tour
  • Day 5: Go hiking at Chincoteague National Wildlife Refuge
  • Day 6: Relax with a sunset sail on Atlantic Tails
  • Day 7: Visit the Nassawango Creek Preserve

Food and Drink Scene of Rural Worcester County

Rural Worcester County boasts of local cuisine that is flavorful and unique. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scrapple: A savory breakfast dish made with pork scraps and cornmeal.

  • Smith Island Cake: A multi-layered cake made with thin and rich buttercream in between each layer.

Local Beverages

  • Assateague Rum: A locally made and distilled rum that’s perfect for cocktails.

  • Evolution Craft Brewing Company: A popular local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Rural Worcester County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rural Worcester County has some great options for accommodation that cater to all types of budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Harrison’s Harbor Watch Inn: A luxurious guesthouse located in Ocean city featuring ocean and bay views.

  • Atlantic Hotel Berlin: A historic hotel located in the charming town of Berlin.

What to Buy in Rural Worcester County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Rural Worcester County, be sure to bring home some local souvenirs and products to commemorate your trip. Here are some popular items to consider:

  • Local art: Many local artists showcase their work in galleries around Worcester County, making for perfect souvenirs or gifts.

  • Blue Crabs: Worcester County is famous for its crabbing industry. Picking up fresh, local crabs from a market is an excellent option for seafood lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Rural Worcester County

Rural Worcester County is proud of its cultural heritage. Some local customs and traditions you can learn about include:

  • Blessing of the Combines: A yearly event that celebrates the life of farmers in Worcester County. It is typically held in August and includes auctions, rides, and musical performances.

  • Delmarva Stargazers - Astronomy: Farmers in Worcester County often gaze up at night, and many individuals enjoy learning about the stars in their free time.

Best Time to Visit Rural Worcester County

The best time to visit Rural Worcester County is during the fall months of September and October when the foliage is at its peak and the weather is moderate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rural Worcester County

Do:

  • Plan ahead and book accommodations in advance.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in scenic areas.
  • Venture into restricted areas in parks and wildlife preserves.
  • Feed the ponies in Assateague Island National Seashore.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rural Worcester County

Rural Worcester County is generally safe, but visitors should still take precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking in unlit areas at night.
  • Hike and bike with caution and stay hydrated.
  • Check the weather before venturing into the outdoors.

Health in Rural Worcester County

Visitors should take steps to ensure their health and wellbeing, such as:

  • Stay hydrated in the summer months.
  • Protect themselves from ticks and bug bites while hiking and biking.
  • Carry any necessary medications and a first-aid kit.

Bus Stations Near Rural Worcester County

Other Options

How to Get Around in Rural Worcester County

  • Renting a car
  • Taking a taxi

Where to Go Next from Rural Worcester County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sinepuxent Bay: A body of water located east of Ocean City with beautiful views from Northside Park.

  • Ocean City Beach: Known for its boardwalk, located about a half-hour away from downtown Berlin.

With breathtaking views, local cuisine, and cultural experiences, it’s a perfect oasis for a relaxing vacation.
