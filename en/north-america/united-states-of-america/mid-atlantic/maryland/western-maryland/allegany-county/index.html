Introduction to Allegany County

Allegany County is a beautiful and historic region located in Western Maryland. Known for its stunning natural scenery, thriving cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, this area has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring American history, hiking through picturesque landscapes, or simply relaxing in a charming small town, Allegany County is the perfect destination for your next vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Allegany County

If you’re planning a trip to Allegany County, there are plenty of must-see destinations to add to your itinerary. Here are some of the top places to visit in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • C&O Canal National Historical Park: This beautiful park spans 184.5 miles from Washington, DC, to Cumberland, MD, and is a popular spot for biking, hiking, and exploring the area’s history.
  • Allegany County Courthouse: Built in 1893, this beautiful building is an architectural masterpiece and a wonderful place to learn about local history.
  • Fort Cumberland: Visit this historic fort and museum to learn about the area’s role in the French and Indian War and the American Revolution.
  • Allegany County Historical Society: Founded in 1936, this non-profit organization is dedicated to preserving and sharing the history of Allegany County and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Allegany County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rocky Gap State Park: This popular park offers a wide variety of activities, including swimming, boating, camping, and hiking. The stunning lake and picturesque surroundings make it a perfect spot for nature lovers.
  • Western Maryland Scenic Railroad: Hop aboard a historic steam locomotive and take a scenic ride through the mountains and valleys of Allegany County. This is a great way to take in the area’s breathtaking natural beauty.
  • Chesapeake and Ohio Canal National Historical Park: This beautiful park spans 184.5 miles from Washington, DC, to Cumberland, MD, and is a popular spot for biking, hiking, and exploring the area’s history.
  • New Germany State Park: Nestled in the heart of the Savage River State Forest, this state park is home to beautiful forests, streams, and trails. It’s also a great spot for camping and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Allegany County

If you’re looking for a great way to experience the natural beauty of Allegany County, consider exploring one of these scenic hiking or biking trails:

  • Great Allegheny Passage: This trail stretches from Pittsburgh, PA, to Cumberland, MD, and offers 150 miles of scenic biking and hiking opportunities.
  • C&O Canal Towpath: Running 184.5 miles from Washington, DC, to Cumberland, MD, this historic trail offers visitors a glimpse into the area’s past, as well as stunning natural scenery.
  • Rocky Gap State Park Trails: Take a hike through the beautiful forests and hills of Rocky Gap State Park and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Green Ridge State Forest Trails: This 47,000-acre forest offers a variety of hiking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. The scenic views and peaceful surroundings make it a perfect spot for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Allegany County: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or family-friendly activities, there’s no shortage of things to do in Allegany County. Here are some of the best activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore the area’s beautiful waterways with a kayaking or canoeing tour. There are plenty of local companies that offer guided trips for all skill levels.
  • Mountain Biking: Allegany County is home to miles of scenic biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. Rent a bike and hit the trails for an exhilarating adventure.
  • Fishing: The area is home to some of the best freshwater fishing in the region. Rent a boat or fish from shore and try your luck at catching trout, bass, and more.
  • Cumberland Historic Walking Tour: Take a guided tour through the charming city of Cumberland and learn about the area’s rich history and architecture.
  • Ghost and Graveyards Tour: Explore the haunted history of the area with a ghost and graveyards tour. Hear spooky tales and discover the area’s dark past.

Food and Drink Scene of Allegany County

If you’re a foodie, you won’t want to miss the chance to try some of Allegany County’s delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maryland Blue Crabs: This local delicacy is a must-try for seafood lovers. Enjoy them steamed, fried, or in a crab cake.
  • Old Bay Fries: This iconic seasoning adds a flavorful kick to classic French fries.
  • Black Walnut Cake: Made with local black walnuts, this sweet treat is a favorite among locals.
  • Western Maryland Scramble: This hearty breakfast dish includes eggs, home fries, onions, and ham or sausage.

Local Beverages

  • National Bohemian (Natty Boh) Beer: This Baltimore-based beer is a regional favorite and pairs perfectly with seafood.
  • Mountain State Brewing Company Beer: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including an award-winning IPA.
  • Knob Hall Winery Wine: Located in nearby Clear Spring, MD, this winery offers a variety of award-winning wines, including a popular Chambourcin.

Where to Stay in Allegany County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel, a budget-friendly hostel, or a charming bed and breakfast, there are plenty of great accommodations to choose from in Allegany County. Here are some of the top options:

  • Rocky Gap Casino Resort: This luxurious resort offers stunning views of the lake and mountains, as well as a spa, casino, and restaurants.
  • The Inn at Vagabond: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Cumberland and offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Quality Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable accommodations and is located near many of the area’s top attractions.
  • Klondike Cabins at Green Ridge State Forest: For a unique and rustic experience, rent one of these cozy cabins located in the beautiful Green Ridge State Forest.

What to Buy in Allegany County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Allegany County without picking up a few souvenirs or local products to bring home. Here are some great options:

  • Appalachian Trail Memorabilia: The famous Appalachian Trail passes through Allegany County, so pick up a t-shirt or water bottle to commemorate your visit.
  • Handmade Goods: Visit one of the area’s many farmers’ markets or craft fairs to find unique handmade goods from local artisans.
  • Honey: Allegany County is home to several local apiaries, so pick up a jar of local honey to take home.
  • Wine: Don’t forget to stock up on some delicious local wine from one of the area’s many wineries.

Local Culture and Customs of Allegany County

To fully appreciate the beauty and charm of Allegany County, it’s important to understand the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Friendliness: Locals in Allegany County are known for their friendliness and warmth. Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a stranger!
  • Appalachian Culture: The area’s culture is heavily influenced by its Appalachian roots. Take the time to learn about the area’s unique history and heritage.
  • Outdoor Living: Allegany County is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Embrace the area’s natural beauty and spend as much time as possible exploring the great outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Allegany County

Allegany County is a beautiful destination year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Here’s a breakdown of the best times to visit for different activities:

  • Outdoor Adventures: Spring and fall are the best times to visit if you’re looking to hike, bike, or explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Cultural Attractions: The area’s museums and historical attractions are open year-round, so any time is a good time to visit if you’re interested in learning about local history.
  • Festivals and Events: Summer is a great time to visit the area if you’re interested in attending one of the many local festivals or events, such as the Allegany County Fair or the Arts and Entertainment District Summer Concert Series.

Do’s and Don’ts of Allegany County

To ensure a positive experience in Allegany County, here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the Outdoors: The natural beauty of Allegany County is a major draw for visitors. Take advantage of the area’s many outdoor activities and explore all that the region has to offer.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Maryland is famous for its seafood and Allegany County is no exception. Be sure to try some of the local specialties, such as Maryland blue crabs or Old Bay fries.
  • Respect the Locals: Allegany County is a close-knit community, so be sure to treat the locals with respect and kindness.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep Allegany County beautiful by disposing of your trash properly and keeping the area clean.
  • Trespass on Private Property: Respect the property of others and avoid trespassing on private land.
  • Waste Water: Water conservation is important in this area, so be mindful of your water usage and avoid wasting water when possible.

Safety and Health

While Allegany County is generally a safe destination, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health during your visit. Here are a few tips:

Safety in Allegany County

  • Be Aware of Wildlife: The area is home to a variety of wildlife, including black bears. Be sure to follow proper safety precautions when in the outdoors.
  • Drive Safely: The mountain roads in the area can be steep and winding. Drive carefully and follow all traffic laws.
  • Take Caution on Waterways: If you plan to kayak or canoe, be sure to wear a life jacket and follow all safety guidelines.

Health in Allegany County

  • Stay Hydrated: The area can get hot and humid during the summer months. Be sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Protect Yourself from Sun Exposure: The high elevation and sunny weather can lead to sunburns. Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from harmful UV rays.
  • Pack a First Aid Kit: If you plan to spend time in the outdoors, it’s always a good idea to pack a first aid kit in case of injuries.

  • Dulles International Airport
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport

Bus Stations Near Allegany County

  • Greyhound Bus Lines in Cumberland, MD
  • Amtrak Station in Cumberland, MD

Other Options

  • Rent a Car: If flying or taking the bus, rent a car to make traveling around Allegany County easier.
  • Take a Train: The Western Maryland Scenic Railroad offers train rides from Cumberland to Frostburg and back for a unique way to travel through the area.

How to Get Around in Allegany County

Renting a car is typically the best way to get around Allegany County, as many of the area’s attractions are located in remote areas. However, there are also several taxi and Uber/Lyft services available in the cities of Cumberland and Frostburg.
