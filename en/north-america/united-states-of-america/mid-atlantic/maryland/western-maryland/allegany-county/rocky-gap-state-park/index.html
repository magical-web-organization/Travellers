Rocky Gap State Park is a hidden gem in Western Maryland that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, scenic views, or cultural attractions, Rocky Gap won’t disappoint. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your perfect getaway to Rocky Gap State Park.

Introduction to Rocky Gap State Park

Rocky Gap State Park is located in Allegany County, Maryland and boasts of 3,000 acres of pristine wilderness, including a 243-acre lake. The park is surrounded by the stunning Appalachian Mountains, which offer some of the most breathtaking views in the region. Rocky Gap State Park is home to 9 miles of hiking trails, beach areas, and several outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, and picnicking during the summer months.

List of Places to Visit in Rocky Gap State Park

Rocky Gap State Park has something for everyone. Here is a list of the top places to visit in the park that you can add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Rocky Gap Lodge & Golf Resort: This is a great place to stay for your Rocky Gap State Park adventure. The lodge is situated on the shore of the lake and features a full-service spa, golf course, and casino.
  • Evitt’s Mountain Cabin: This is one of the few remaining buildings from the 18th century. The cabin is now a museum, and tours are available.
  • City of Cumberland: This historic town has a rich history and boasts of several unique shops, restaurants, and galleries.

Natural Attractions of Rocky Gap State Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Lakeside Loop Trail: This trail is an easy hike with amazing views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Rocky Gap beach: The best place to relax and soak up the sun during the summer months.
  • The Cumberland Trail: This trail offers stunning views of the C&O Canal and Potomac River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rocky Gap State Park

Rocky Gap State Park boasts of over 9 miles of hiking trails, ranging from easy to moderate difficulty. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • Rocky Gap Trail: This is a 5.3-mile trail that offers stunning views of the lake and is perfect for biking and hiking.
  • Lakeside Loop Trail: This trail is a 1.4-mile loop that circles around the lake, providing stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Lakeside Loop Trail: This easy trail is perfect for beginners and offers beautiful views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Rocky Gap State Park: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Rocky Gap State Park:

  • Boating and kayaking: Rent a boat or bring your own to explore the lake’s many coves and inlets.
  • Fishing: Rocky Gap State Park is home to several species of fish, including bass, catfish, and trout.
  • Golfing: Rocky Gap Lodge & Golf Resort boasts of a beautiful 18-hole course nestled in the Appalachian Mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rocky Gap State Park

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you plan your perfect trip to Rocky Gap State Park:

Day 1: Arrive at Rocky Gap Lodge and check-in. Take a tour of the Evitt’s Mountain Cabin museum and explore the beach area.

Day 2: Go fishing or rent a boat and explore the lake. In the evening, try your luck at the casino.

Day 3: Take a hiking trip along the Rocky Gap Trail. In the afternoon, relax at the lodge’s full-service spa.

Day 4: Explore the city of Cumberland and visit unique shops and restaurants.

Day 5: Golf at Rocky Gap Lodge & Golf Resort’s beautiful 18-hole course. Take in breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Day 6: Go kayaking or take a paddleboard lesson. In the evening, enjoy a bonfire at the beach.

Day 7: Take a relaxing day at the lodge’s pool and enjoy a day of leisure.

Food and Drink Scene of Rocky Gap State Park

Rocky Gap State Park is home to several restaurants and food vendors that offer local favorites and scenic views.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maryland Blue Crab
  • Old Bay seasoned fries
  • Lake Trout
  • Fried Chicken

Local Beverages

  • Flying Dog Beer: A local craft beer that has won several awards.
  • Black Ankle Vineyard: Located in the Catoctin Mountains, this vineyard produces some of the best Maryland wines.

Where to Stay in Rocky Gap State Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rocky Gap Lodge & Golf Resort is the most popular place to stay when visiting Rocky Gap State Park. Other options include:

  • Lake View Motel: This budget-friendly accommodation boasts of scenic views of the lake.
  • Cumberland Inn and Spa: This historic hotel is located in the city of Cumberland and offers old-world charm.

What to Buy in Rocky Gap State Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Rocky Gap State Park:

  • Maryland’s famous Old Bay seasoning
  • Hand-crafted wooden decor
  • Local beer and wine

Local Culture and Customs of Rocky Gap State Park

Rocky Gap State Park is known for its friendly locals and rich history. The park prides itself on preserving its natural resources, and visitors are encouraged to do the same.

Best Time to Visit Rocky Gap State Park

The best time to visit Rocky Gap State Park is during the summer months when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. The park does get crowded during this time, so book your accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rocky Gap State Park

Do:

  • Respect the natural habitat and wildlife of the park.
  • Make campfires in designated areas only.
  • Bring your own reusable water bottle to reduce waste.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Disturb wildlife or destroy vegetation.
  • Swim in unauthorized areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rocky Gap State Park

Rocky Gap State Park is generally safe for visitors. However, you should take precautions when exploring the park, such as:

  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities.
  • Always carry plenty of water.
  • Be aware of the potential for inclement weather.

Health in Rocky Gap State Park

Rocky Gap State Park does not have any major health concerns. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid tick bites and be aware of poison ivy.

Getting to Rocky Gap State Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rocky Gap State Park is easily accessible by car and public transportation.

Airports Near Rocky Gap State Park

  • Pittsburgh International Airport
  • Baltimore-Washington International Airport

Bus Stations Near Rocky Gap State Park

  • Cumberland Transit Center
  • Greyhound Bus Station

Other Options

  • Car rental is available at both airports.
  • Amtrak trains stop at the Cumberland Station.

How to Get Around in Rocky Gap State Park

The best way to get around Rocky Gap State Park is by car or bike. However, visitors can also rent a boat or kayak to explore the lake.

Where to Go Next from Rocky Gap State Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
