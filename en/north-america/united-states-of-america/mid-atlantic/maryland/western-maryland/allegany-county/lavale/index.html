Introduction to LaVale

LaVale is a small town situated in Allegany County, in Western Maryland, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. It is a charming town that attracts visitors for its rich history, breathtaking natural beauty, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in LaVale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

LaVale has several museums, historical places, and landmarks that provide a glimpse into the town’s rich history and culture. You can explore the LaVale Toll House, which used to be a toll station for the Cumberland Road, visit the Allegany County Courthouse, an impressive example of neoclassical architecture, or admire the ornate interiors of Emmanuel Episcopal Church, which dates back to the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of LaVale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

LaVale boasts of natural attractions that are very popular among tourists and locals alike. If you are an adventure enthusiast, then the Rocky Gap State Park is a must-visit. It is an excellent place for boating, fishing, camping, hiking, and biking. You can also enjoy the spectacular views of the park’s stunning lake and the surrounding mountains.

You can visit the Dan’s Mountain State Park, where you can hike along the park’s picturesque trails to reach the mountain’s summit for breathtaking views of the town, the Cumberland Narrows, and the surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of LaVale

LaVale has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views. You can explore the trail network at The C&O Canal National Historical Park, a 184.5-mile-long park that features trails used for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

The Great Allegheny Passage is another popular route; it is a 150-mile-long bike trail stretching from Cumberland to Pittsburgh. This trail offers breathtaking views of the LaVale area’s landscapes, as well as the opportunity to learn about the region’s industrial heritage.

Best Things to Do in LaVale: Activities and Tours

LaVale offers several activities and tours that are sure to make your visit unforgettable. You can take a tour of the LaVale Train Station, which is one of the oldest train stations in the country and today serves as a museum.

You can also visit the Town Centre, which is a vibrant shopping, dining, and entertainment complex that features numerous stores, restaurants, and movie theaters.

Week-Long Itinerary for LaVale

Day 1: Visit the C&O Canal National Historical Park Day 2: Explore the Rocky Gap State Park and lake Day 3: Hike through the Dan’s Mountain State Park and climb the mountain summit Day 4: Tour one of the local cultural landmarks, such as the LaVale Toll House or the Allegany County Courthouse Day 5: Shop, dine, and see a movie at the LaVale Town Centre Day 6: Bike along the Great Allegheny Passage Day 7: Take a tour of the LaVale Train Station and learn more about the region’s history.

Food and Drink Scene of LaVale

Must-Try Dishes

LaVale is known for its delicious food, featuring classic American fare. Must-try dishes include crab cakes, fried chicken, and steak.

Local Beverages

When in LaVale, you should also try some of the region’s famous local beverages, including craft beer, cider, and whiskey.

Where to Stay in LaVale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

LaVale has several hotels and guesthouses to choose from. If you are looking for a more affordable option, then Comfort Inn and Country Inn & Suites are great choices. For luxury accommodations, Rocky Gap Casino Resort offers world-class amenities, including a casino, several restaurants, and a full-service spa.

What to Buy in LaVale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

LaVale is known for its regional products, including wine, craft beer, and handmade crafts. You can purchase these products at the local markets or artisan shops. Souvenirs to take home include postcards, magnets, and other trinkets sold at small gift stores.

Local Culture and Customs of LaVale

The local culture of LaVale is heavily influenced by its rich history and the surrounding natural beauty. The town is known for its friendly locals, who are welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit LaVale

The best time to visit LaVale is during the fall season, where you can enjoy the spectacular colors of the autumn leaves. The spring and summer months are also great for outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in LaVale

LaVale is a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to face any safety concerns. However, it is always important to take precautions by not leaving valuables in plain sight, locking doors and windows, and staying alert in crowded areas.

Health in LaVale

LaVale has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer medical care to visitors.

Getting to LaVale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near LaVale

The closest airport to LaVale is the Greater Cumberland Regional Airport, which is just a 10-minute drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near LaVale

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus station in Cumberland, which is just a few miles from LaVale.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to LaVale or take a train to Cumberland, which is just a few miles away.

How to Get Around in LaVale

The best way to get around LaVale is by car or bike. Taxis and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, are also available.

Where to Go Next from LaVale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

LaVale is in a strategic location that makes it easy to explore other attractions in the region. Nearby sites to visit include the town of Cumberland or the Deep Creek Lake, a famous vacation spot that offers water sports, fishing, and skiing.
