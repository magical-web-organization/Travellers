Introduction to Cumberland

Cumberland is a charming city located in the western region of Maryland, United States of America, that attracts visitors with its rich cultural heritage, scenic views, and outdoor recreational activities. Founded in 1787, Cumberland is home to some of the most magnificent architectural landmarks, museums, galleries, and parklands that showcase the city’s cultural and natural diversity.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you discover Cumberland by highlighting its top attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking/biking routes, local culture, where to stay, what to buy, and more. So, let’s dive in and start exploring!

Week-Long Itinerary for Cumberland

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Allegany County Courthouse and Emmanuel Episcopal Church
Day 2 Explore the Allegany Arts Council and George Washington’s Headquarters

Local Culture and Customs of Cumberland

Cumberland has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, festivals, arts, and cuisine. Locals are proud of their heritage and welcoming to visitors, so embrace it with an open mind and ask questions to learn more about their customs.

Best Time to Visit Cumberland

The best time to visit Cumberland is during the spring and fall when the weather is pleasant, and the foliage is breathtaking. However, the summertime also attracts visitors with its outdoor recreational activities, festivals, and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cumberland

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind during your visit to Cumberland:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Ask locals for recommendations
  • Take time to explore the Historic District and Chesapeake & Ohio Canal National Historical Park

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Disrespect religious places and symbols
  • Park illegally or in spaces reserved for the disabled

Safety and Health

Safety in Cumberland

Cumberland is a safe and welcoming city for tourists. However, it would be best if you take precautions like avoiding dark alleys and areas at night, staying alert in crowded places, and keeping your belongings safe by not leaving them unattended.

Health in Cumberland

Cumberland has several hospitals and medical facilities that can cater to your medical needs. It is also recommended that you carry medical insurance that covers your trip.
