Introduction to Hagerstown

Hagerstown is a picturesque city located in Washington County, Western Maryland, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. The city is known for its rich history, beautiful countryside, and vibrant cultural scene. With a population of just over 40,000 people, Hagerstown is a welcoming community that offers visitors a unique perspective into the charm of small-town America.

List of Places to Visit in Hagerstown

Hagerstown has a wealth of tourist attractions to visit. Here are just a few places you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Antietam National Battlefield

Best Things to Do in Hagerstown: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Hagerstown has plenty of fun activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best:

  • Take a ghost tour of downtown Hagerstown
  • Visit the Hagerstown City Park and enjoy miniature train rides, a petting zoo, and paddle boats
  • Go to a Hagerstown Suns minor league baseball game
  • Enjoy live music at the Maryland Theatre
  • Take a cooking class at For the Love of Food

Week-Long Itinerary for Hagerstown

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Hagerstown:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Jonathan Hager House and Museum and explore downtown Hagerstown
2 Take a hike on the C&O Canal Towpath Trail and visit the C&O Canal National Historical Park
3 Spend the day at Greenbrier State Park swimming, boating, and hiking
4 Tour the Antietam National Battlefield and visit the Hagerstown Roundhouse Museum
5 Visit the Washington County Museum of Fine Arts and enjoy a show at The Maryland Theatre
6 Take a scenic drive through the countryside and visit Beaver Creek State Park
7 Explore the city park and enjoy paddle boat rides, a petting zoo, and miniature train rides

Local Culture and Customs of Hagerstown

Hagerstown residents are warm, welcoming, and proud of their city’s history and heritage. Visitors should be respectful and considerate of local customs, such as saying “hello” to passersby and showing appreciation to restaurant staff.

Best Time to Visit Hagerstown

Hagerstown is a year-round destination, but visitors may want to consider the weather when planning their trip. The best time to visit Hagerstown is in the summer, when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities are popular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hagerstown

Do:

  • Take a ghost tour of downtown Hagerstown.
  • Visit the C&O Canal National Historical Park.
  • Try local Maryland cuisine, such as crab cakes and Smithfield ham.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources.
  • Ignore cultural customs and traditions.
  • Fail to tip restaurant staff.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hagerstown

Hagerstown is a safe destination, but visitors should take standard precautions to protect their belongings and personal safety while exploring the city. Be aware of your surroundings at night and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Hagerstown

Visitors to Hagerstown should take standard precautions to protect against tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease. It’s also a good idea to pack sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring natural areas.

Visitors to Hagerstown can get around by car, bike, or on foot. Public transportation options include the Washington County Transit bus system and the Greyhound Bus Lines.
