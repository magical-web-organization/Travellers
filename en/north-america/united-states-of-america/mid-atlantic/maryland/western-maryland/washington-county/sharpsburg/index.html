Introduction to Sharpsburg

Sharpsburg is a small town located in Washington County, Western Maryland. Known for its rich history and stunning natural beauty, Sharpsburg is a perfect destination for both history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts. The town is best known for the Battle of Antietam, the bloodiest one-day battle of the American Civil War, which took place on September 17, 1862. Visitors can explore the Antietam National Battlefield and several other historical sites that commemorate this significant event in American history.

List of Places to Visit in Sharpsburg

If you are planning a trip to Sharpsburg, there are many places you must visit to fully experience the town’s history, culture and natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sharpsburg is home to several cultural attractions that allow visitors to delve deeper into the town’s history and heritage. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Antietam National Battlefield: This historical site, located on the outskirts of Sharpsburg, commemorates the bloodiest one-day battle of the American Civil War.
  • Burnside’s Bridge: Located within the Antietam National Battlefield, this stone-arch bridge played a significant role in the Battle of Antietam.
  • The Pry House Field Hospital Museum: Nestled in the Antietam National Battlefield, the Pry House was transformed into a hospital during the battle.
  • The Sharpsburg Historical Society: This museum displays the town’s historical artifacts and documents.
  • The National Museum of Civil War Medicine: Located in Frederick, MD, this museum showcases the role of medicine during the Civil War and highlights how modern medical practices emerged from the war.
  • John Brown Wax Museum: Located in Harpers Ferry, WV, this museum features life-size wax figures and provides insight into Brown’s life and legacy.

Natural Attractions of Sharpsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sharpsburg is also known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Chesapeake and Ohio Canal National Historical Park: This 184.5-mile park offers opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and boating along the Potomac River.
  • Harpers Ferry National Historical Park: Located on the border of Maryland and West Virginia, this park offers opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s rich history.
  • South Mountain State Park: This park offers stunning views of the Appalachian Mountains and opportunities for hiking, camping, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sharpsburg

Sharpsburg offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular trails include:

  • The C&O Canal Towpath: This 184.5-mile trail runs parallel to the Potomac River and offers scenic views and opportunities for hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • The Appalachian Trail: This 2,190-mile trail runs from Georgia to Maine and passes through Maryland, offering opportunities for hiking and outdoor adventures.
  • The Antietam National Battlefield Hiking Trails: This park offers 16 miles of hiking trails that take visitors through the battlefield and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Sharpsburg: Activities and Tours

  • Antietam Battlefield Guide Service: Visitors can take a guided tour of the battlefield with a knowledgeable guide who provides insight into the battle and the town’s history.
  • Canoeing and kayaking: The Potomac River offers opportunities for canoeing and kayaking adventures.
  • Winery tours: Sharpsburg is home to several wineries that offer tours and tasting experiences.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sharpsburg

Day 1: Visit the Antietam National Battlefield and explore the various historical sites that commemorate the battle. Day 2: Hike the C&O Canal Towpath and enjoy the scenic views along the Potomac River. Day 3: Explore the town’s historical sites, including the Sharpsburg Historical Society and the Pry House Field Hospital Museum. Day 4: Visit Harpers Ferry National Historical Park and enjoy the town’s rich history and scenic views. Day 5: Go on a winery tour and enjoy the local wines. Day 6: Visit South Mountain State Park and enjoy the hiking trails and scenic views of the Appalachian Mountains. Day 7: Take a canoe or kayak trip down the Potomac River.

Food and Drink Scene of Sharpsburg

Sharpsburg offers a great variety of local dishes and drinks that visitors must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried chicken: A popular dish in Sharpsburg, served with biscuits and gravy.
  • Barbeque: The town offers several barbeque restaurants that serve mouth-watering food.
  • Apple pies: Sharpsburg is known for its delicious apple pies, made from locally grown apples.

Local Beverages

  • Local wines: Sharpsburg is home to several wineries that offer locally produced wines.
  • Craft beers: The town offers several breweries that serve a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Sharpsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jacob Rohrbach Inn: This bed and breakfast is a historical landmark and offers a traditional Victorian-style stay.
  • The Inn at Antietam: This bed and breakfast has gorgeous views, farm-to-table breakfasts and offers a real home-away-from-home experience.
  • Bavarian Inn: This hotel is a luxurious option, offering stunning scenes of the Potomac River and the West Virginia countryside.

What to Buy in Sharpsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antiques and art: Sharpsburg has several antique shops and galleries that sell locally made art and crafts.
  • Apple products: Sharpsburg is known for its locally grown apples, so make sure to grab some apple butter or apple sauce to take home.
  • Civil War memorabilia: Several boutiques and shops sell historical artifacts, such as old war guns, clothing, and currency.

Local Culture and Customs of Sharpsburg

Sharpsburg is a small town with a rich American history, which is deeply ingrained in the local culture. The town’s people are friendly and welcoming to visitors and proud of its historical significance.

Best Time to Visit Sharpsburg

Sharpsburg is a great place to visit at any time of the year. However, the best time to visit is from May to October when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities and events like the Sharpsburg Heritage Festival, take place during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sharpsburg

Do:

  • Visit the Antietam National Battlefield and other historical sites to learn about the town’s significance in the American history.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor recreational opportunities, like hiking, biking, and canoeing/kayaking.
  • Try the local dishes and drinks like apple pies and locally produced wine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any historical sites or natural attractions.
  • Ignore the town’s conservation efforts and waste natural resources.
  • Disrespect the local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sharpsburg

Sharpsburg is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should always be cautious and aware of their surroundings while exploring the town.

Health in Sharpsburg

Regionally, water-borne illnesses are a potential problem. All visitors should ensure their immune systems are up to par before including tap or well water in their diets.

Getting to Sharpsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sharpsburg

The nearest major airports are the Washington Dulles International Airport and the Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport, both of which are around 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Sharpsburg

Greyhound bus service is available 20 miles away in Hagerstown.

Other Options

If driving, Sharpsburg is conveniently located off of Interstate 70 as well as Route 65.

How to Get Around in Sharpsburg

Driving, cycling, or walking are the best options to get around in Sharpsburg.

Where to Go Next from Sharpsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Frederick, Maryland: A vibrant city with several historical attractions, restaurants, and boutiques. It’s less than 30 minutes away by car.
  • Shepherdstown, West Virginia: A quaint college town with historic sites, restaurants, and a vibrant arts scene. It’s just a 15-minute drive over the Potomac River.
  • The Gettysburg National Battlefield: Located in Pennsylvania, just over an hour away by car, it is a huge historical battleground for the American Civil War.

Conclusion

Sharpsburg is a must-visit destination for history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts. You can explore the town’s rich history and cultural attractions, taste the local dishes and drinks, hike, and enjoy various outdoor recreational opportunities. With this comprehensive guide, you will have all the information you need to plan your trip to Sharpsburg, ensuring that you make the most of your visit.
