Garrett County is a hidden gem in Western Maryland, known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant local culture. Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, immerse yourself in local history and culture, or simply relax and unwind, Garrett County has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at what Garrett County has to offer and provide some helpful tips for planning your visit.

Introduction to Garrett County

Garrett County is located in Western Maryland, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. It is known for its rugged mountain landscapes and pristine natural beauty, as well as its rich history and vibrant local culture. The county is home to several charming towns and villages, as well as numerous state parks, forests, and wildlife areas, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Garrett County

Garrett County offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Garrett County Historical Society Museum - This museum offers a fascinating look at the history and culture of Garrett County, featuring exhibits on local industries, agriculture, and notable figures from the region’s past.

  • The Spruce Forest Artisan Village - This charming village is home to a variety of local artisans and craftsmen, showcasing traditional Appalachian crafts and handmade goods.

  • The Casselman Bridge - Located in Grantsville, this historic bridge dates back to the 1800s and is a popular spot for photography and sightseeing.

Natural Attractions of Garrett County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Deep Creek Lake - The largest freshwater lake in Maryland, Deep Creek Lake offers a wide range of recreational activities, including boating, fishing, swimming, and water sports.

  • Swallow Falls State Park - This beautiful park features several hiking trails, including the popular Swallow Falls Trail, which takes visitors past several stunning waterfalls.

  • Savage River State Forest - This expansive forest is home to several hiking and biking trails, as well as numerous campgrounds and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Garrett County

Garrett County is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few of the top routes to check out:

  • The Garrett County Rail Trail - This 13-mile trail runs from the town of Frostburg to the Western Maryland Scenic Railroad in the town of Cumberland, offering scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Big Savage Mountain Trail - This challenging hike takes visitors to the top of Big Savage Mountain, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • The Meadow Mountain Trail - This moderate hike features gorgeous views of the surrounding mountains and forests, as well as several historic sites along the way.

Best Things to Do in Garrett County: Activities and Tours

Garrett County offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few of the top things to do in the area:

  • Whitewater Rafting on the Youghiogheny River - This popular activity offers an adrenaline-pumping adventure through the scenic Youghiogheny River Gorge.

  • The Autumn Glory Festival - Held each year in October, this popular festival celebrates the changing seasons with live music, food vendors, and arts and crafts booths.

  • The Western Maryland Scenic Railroad - This historic railroad offers scenic rides through the mountains, showcasing the natural beauty of Garrett County.

Week-Long Itinerary for Garrett County

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Garrett County, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Explore the town of Oakland, including a visit to the Garrett County Historical Society Museum and a hike in nearby Swallow Falls State Park.

Day 2: Spend the day on Deep Creek Lake, renting a boat or paddleboard and soaking up the sun at one of the beach areas.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive through the mountains, stopping at overlooks and hiking trails along the way.

Day 4: Visit local artisans and craftsmen at the Spruce Forest Artisan Village, then hike the Meadow Mountain Trail for stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Day 5: Take a whitewater rafting trip on the Youghiogheny River, then relax with a picnic lunch in one of the nearby state parks.

Day 6: Ride the Western Maryland Scenic Railroad through the mountains, then stop for a meal at a local restaurant.

Day 7: Spend your last day exploring the local shops and markets, picking up some souvenirs and local products to bring home.

Food and Drink Scene of Garrett County

Garrett County is known for its hearty, home-cooked cuisine and traditional local flavors. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maryland blue crab cakes - a staple of the Chesapeake Bay region, these crab cakes are a local favorite.

  • Fried chicken and gravy - a classic Southern dish that can be found on many local menus.

  • Apple pie - with several orchards in the area, Garrett County is known for its delicious apple pies.

Local Beverages

  • Mountain State Brewing Company - This local brewery specializes in handcrafted beers using local ingredients.

  • Deep Creek Cellars - This family-owned winery produces a variety of award-winning wines using grapes grown in the region.

Where to Stay in Garrett County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Garrett County offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are a few of the top places to stay in the area:

  • The Savage River Lodge - This rustic lodge offers cozy cabins and cottages surrounded by pristine forest and mountain scenery.

  • The Inn at Deep Creek - This charming inn offers affordable rooms and suites near the shores of Deep Creek Lake.

  • The Lake Star Lodge - This waterfront lodge offers stunning lake views and easy access to nearby recreational activities.

What to Buy in Garrett County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Garrett County is known for its local crafts and handmade goods, making it a great place to pick up unique souvenirs and gifts. Here are a few things to look out for:

  • Handcrafted pottery - Many local artisans specialize in pottery, with a wide range of styles and designs available.

  • Local honey and jams - Made with locally sourced honey and fruit, these sweet treats make great gifts.

  • Appalachian crafts - Look for handmade quilts, woven baskets, and other traditional Appalachian crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Garrett County

Garrett County is known for its friendly, welcoming locals and strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to embrace local customs and traditions, such as attending the annual Autumn Glory Festival and participating in local outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Garrett County

The best time to visit Garrett County depends on your interests and preferences. For outdoor enthusiasts, the summer months offer the best weather for hiking, biking, and water sports, while the fall is a popular time to visit for the stunning fall foliage. Winter is a great time to hit the slopes or enjoy indoor attractions like museums and shops.

Do’s and Don’ts of Garrett County

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Garrett County:

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and customs, and try traditional local dishes and drinks.

  • Respect the natural beauty of the area by hiking and exploring responsibly, and following Leave No Trace principles.

  • Take advantage of local outdoor activities and attractions, such as water sports and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage local natural and cultural resources.

  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions, such as attending local festivals and events.

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and equipment for outdoor activities, especially during winter months.

Safety and Health

Garrett County is generally a safe and healthy destination, with few major health concerns and low crime rates. Here are a few key safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Garrett County

  • Hiking and outdoor activities can pose some risks, especially during extreme weather conditions. Be sure to check local weather forecasts and trail conditions before heading out.

  • Always practice safe boating and water sports, including wearing a life jacket and following safety guidelines.

  • Be aware of local wildlife, including bears and snakes, and take appropriate precautions when hiking and camping.

Health in Garrett County

  • The most common health concerns in Garrett County are related to seasonal allergies and tick-borne illnesses. Be sure to bring appropriate medication and insect repellent when visiting during warmer months.

  • There are several hospitals and medical centers in the area, as well as local pharmacies and clinics.

Getting to Garrett County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Garrett County is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area. Here are a few transportation options to consider when planning your trip:

Airports Near Garrett County

  • Pittsburgh International Airport - approximately 120 miles from Garrett County.

  • Baltimore/Washington International Airport - approximately 175 miles from Garrett County.

Bus Stations Near Garrett County

  • Greyhound Bus Station - located in nearby Cumberland, MD.

  • MegaBus Station - located in Pittsburgh, PA.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Service - The Western Maryland Scenic Railroad offers scenic train rides through the mountains and valleys of Garrett County.

  • Car rentals - Several major car rental companies operate in the area, offering convenient transportation options for visitors.

How to Get Around in Garrett County

The most convenient way to get around Garrett County is by car, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can also explore the area by bike or on foot, with several bike rental shops and hiking trails available.

Where to Go Next from Garrett County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Garrett County is located in the heart of the Mid-Atlantic region, making it a great base for exploring nearby cities and attractions. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The city of Pittsburgh, PA - approximately 100 miles from Garrett County, Pittsburgh offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and theaters.
