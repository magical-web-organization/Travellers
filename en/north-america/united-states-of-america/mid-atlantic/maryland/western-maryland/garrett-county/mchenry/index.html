Introduction to McHenry

Nestled in the heart of Garrett County, in Western Maryland, McHenry is a small town known for its stunning natural attractions, outdoor adventure, and charming small-town vibe. Whether you’re looking to explore historical sites, sample delicious local flavors, or simply get lost in nature, McHenry has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in McHenry

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you enjoy learning about history and culture, McHenry won’t disappoint. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places:

  • The Garrett County Historical Museum: This museum is home to fascinating exhibits showcasing the area’s rich history, including artifacts from Native American settlements, agricultural tools, and mining equipment that have helped shape the region.
  • The Casselman River Bridge State Park: This park is home to the Casselman River Bridge, a National Historic Landmark that was once the longest single span bridge on the National Road. Visitors can learn about the history of this iconic structure and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Spruce Forest Artisan Village: This charming collection of historic buildings houses local artisans’ studios creating handmade crafts, from weaving to woodcarving, that showcase the area’s rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of McHenry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

McHenry is known for its natural beauty, and there’s plenty to explore outdoors. Here are some of the top natural attractions in McHenry:

  • Deep Creek Lake: This is the largest inland body of water in Maryland and is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming. In winter, try ice fishing, snowmobiling, and skiing.
  • Swallow Falls State Park: Home to breathtaking waterfalls, hiking trails, and natural swimming holes, Swallow Falls State Park is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Herrington Manor State Park: Ideal for fishing, paddling, or simply taking a stroll around the lake, Herrington Manor State Park offers opportunities to unwind amidst nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McHenry

For those looking to get their heart rate up, McHenry offers a variety of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best:

  • The Meadow Mountain Trail: This 6-mile trail takes hikers through a beautiful forest and ends at the summit of Meadow Mountain, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Deep Creek Lake State Park Trail: This 4.6-mile trail takes walkers along the lake, past beaches, and offers a mix of terrain with some great views.
  • The Great Allegheny Passage Trail: This 150-mile trail is perfect for biking enthusiasts who want to explore the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in McHenry: Activities and Tours

McHenry has exciting things to offer visitors. Here are some of the best to experience while in town:

  • The Mountain Coaster: This thrilling ride takes visitors on a scenic journey through the forest and down the mountain, offering adrenaline-pumping fun and breathtaking views.
  • Whitewater Rafting: Visitors can raft down the Youghiogheny river and enjoy an unforgettable adventure, taking in sensational views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • The Firefly Farms Creamery and Market: This local cheese and market shed located in Accident, offers tasting and pairing classes for those who enjoy artisan cheese.

Food and Drink Scene of McHenry

When it comes to food and drink, McHenry has plenty to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Crab Cakes: Maryland-style crab cakes are iconic, and local restaurants in McHenry make some of the best. Try them with hand-cut fries and coleslaw for the full experience.
  • The BBQ at Savage River Lodge: This lodge’s specialty is pit-smoked barbecue, including pulled pork, brisket, and slow-cooked baby back ribs cooked using local hardwood.
  • The Pizza at Brenda’s Pizzeria: This local favorite delivers delicious, hand-tossed pizzas that come in authentic Italian flavors.

Local Beverages

  • The ‘Frostburg Flyers IPA’ at Mountain State Brewing Company: This brewpub offers guests a taste of their grain-to-glass beer from over twenty different craft beer selections.
  • The Moonshine at Deep Creek Lake Distillery: This craft distillery produces small-batch spirits that include premium vodka and white rum.

Where to Stay in McHenry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

McHenry has many places to stay, suitable for all levels of comfort and budget. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • The Lodge at Wisp Resort: A luxurious, ski-in/ski-out resort with modern amenities and spacious rooms, perfect for those who enjoy winter sports.
  • Lake Star Lodge: This lodge offers cozy rooms, suites, and cabins with beautiful views of Deep Creek Lake.
  • The Inn at Deep Creek: An affordable option that offers a comfortable stay close to the attractions and restaurants of McHenry.

Local Culture and Customs of McHenry

Marylanders are famously friendly, and McHenry is no exception. Residents in McHenry take pride in their community, and visitors are often welcomed with warmth and hospitality. Join in the local festivities or take part in the arts and culture scene, rather, than just admiring it.

Best Time to Visit McHenry

McHenry is a year-round destination, with different experiences during each season. Summer is the busiest with its water sports, boating, and swimming, autumn is ideal for leaf peeping, and winter sees the ski slopes come alive with people enjoying the slopes at Wisp Resort. Spring is quieter when the grass and greenery start growing again. All four seasons allow tourists to enjoy McHenry’s natural beauty.

Safety and Health

Safety in McHenry

McHenry is a safe place to visit. However, travelers should take basic precautions, such as locking cars and hotel rooms, watching their belongings, and not wandering on foot in unknown areas in the dark.

Health in McHenry

Health care facilities are available in McHenry, and the water quality is safe for consumption. However, to avoid illness from ticks or mosquitoes in summer, wear protective clothing and use insect repellent.
