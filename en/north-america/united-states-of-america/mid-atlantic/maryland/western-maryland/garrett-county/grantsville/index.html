Introduction to Grantsville

Grantsville is a small town nestled in the heart of Garrett County, Maryland. Located in Western Maryland and part of the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Grantsville is an ideal destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Its rich history, cultural attractions, and natural beauty make it a perfect vacation spot any time of year.

List of Places to Visit in Grantsville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Grantsville boasts a number of museums and historical places that are worth a visit. These include:

  • Casselman River Bridge State Park: One of the state’s oldest bridges and a National Historic Landmark.
  • Spruce Forest Artisan Village: A unique collection of restored buildings featuring the work of local artisans.
  • The Stanton’s Mill: An old mill dating back to the late 1700s that still operates today as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Grantsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grantsville is home to a bounty of natural attractions, including scenic views and outdoor adventures.

  • Savage River State Forest: A natural oasis where visitors can hike, fish, and camp.
  • New Germany State Park: A picturesque park featuring a lake and scenic hiking trails.
  • Big Run State Park: A remote park known for its beautiful vistas and abundant wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grantsville

With miles of trails and beautiful scenery, Grantsville is a great place for hikers and bikers. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Great Allegheny Passage: A 150-mile trail that runs from Pittsburgh to Cumberland, Maryland, passing through Grantsville along the way.
  • Savage River Reservoir Trail: A 7-mile loop that offers stunning views of the reservoir and surrounding forests.
  • Meadow Mountain Trail: A 9-mile section of the Great Allegheny Passage that passes through former mining towns and provides panoramic views of the valley.

Best Things to Do in Grantsville: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Grantsville has something for everyone. Don’t miss these must-do activities and tours:

  • Casselman River Bridge State Park Tours: Learn the history of the bridge and the area on a guided tour.
  • Savage River Lodge Horseback Riding: Go for a scenic ride through the forest with experienced guides.
  • Spruce Forest Artisan Village Workshops: Take a hands-on workshop with local artisans and create your own unique souvenir.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grantsville

Day 1: Visit Casselman River Bridge State Park and take a guided tour of the historic bridge. Head to New Germany State Park for an afternoon hike.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking the Great Allegheny Passage and stop for lunch in one of the small towns along the way.

Day 3: Take a horseback riding tour at the Savage River Lodge and enjoy lunch at their charming restaurant.

Day 4: Visit the Spruce Forest Artisan Village and learn about the work of local artisans. Head to Stanton’s Mill for a guided tour.

Day 5: Explore the Big Run State Park and have a picnic lunch by the lake.

Day 6: Take a self-guided driving tour of the area’s historic sites and quaint country roads.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the peace and quiet of Grantsville before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Grantsville

Must-Try Dishes

Grantsville is known for its hearty, comfort food. Don’t leave without trying these local specialties:

  • Maryland Crab Cakes: Made from fresh local crab meat, these crab cakes are a Maryland staple.
  • Chicken and Dumplings: A classic Southern dish featuring tender chicken and fluffy dumplings in a savory broth.
  • Pot Pie: A rich, savory pie filled with meat and vegetables, often made with a homemade crust.

Local Beverages

Maryland is known for its craft beer, and Grantsville is no exception. Be sure to try some of the local brews:

  • Mountain State Brewing Company: A popular local brewery with a wide selection of beers on tap.
  • 1812 Brewery: A historic brewery known for its unique flavors and charming taproom.

Where to Stay in Grantsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a quaint bed and breakfast or a luxurious hotel, Grantsville has plenty of options for all budgets.

  • The Lodge at Wisp: A lakeside resort featuring luxurious accommodations and access to a wide range of activities.
  • Savage River Lodge: A rustic yet elegant retreat, perfect for couples or those looking for a secluded getaway.
  • The Cornucopia Bed and Breakfast: A charming B&B located in town that serves a delicious homemade breakfast.

What to Buy in Grantsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Grantsville’s artisan community offers plenty of opportunities to pick up unique souvenirs and locally-made products. Some of the best options include:

  • Pottery and ceramics from The Potomac Handcrafted Gallery
  • Handmade soaps and beauty products from Lots to Love
  • Locally-made jams and jellies from The Country Store

Local Culture and Customs of Grantsville

Grantsville has a proud local history and culture that is still alive and well today. Visitors should be mindful of the following customs:

  • Locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should reciprocate this warmth.
  • The pace of life in Grantsville is slower and more relaxed than in larger cities, so be prepared to take your time and enjoy the scenery.
  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace when enjoying the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Grantsville

Grantsville has something to offer year-round, but each season has its own unique charm. For outdoor activities, the best time to visit is during the spring, summer, or fall. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it is also a beautiful time to appreciate the quiet beauty of the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grantsville

Do:

  • Take the time to appreciate the natural beauty of the area.
  • Get to know the locals and learn about the town’s history and culture.
  • Be respectful of the environment and leave no trace when enjoying the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Expect a fast-paced, bustling city atmosphere.
  • Disrespect the natural environment or local customs.
  • Ignore the area’s history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grantsville

Grantsville is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should take common sense precautions when traveling.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and locked away in a safe place.
  • Follow all posted signs and warnings when enjoying the natural attractions.

Health in Grantsville

Grantsville is a small town and does not have a hospital. Visitors should take any necessary medications with them and be prepared for minor medical issues.

Getting to Grantsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grantsville

  • Pittsburgh International Airport: 113 miles away
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: 154 miles away

Bus Stations Near Grantsville

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: 24 miles away in Frostburg, MD
  • MegaBus: 40 miles away in Cumberland, MD

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The closest station is located in Cumberland, MD, 25 miles away.
  • Driving: Grantsville is located just off Interstate 68, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Grantsville

The best way to travel around Grantsville is by car. The town is spread out, and many of the attractions are located outside of town. Taxis and ride-sharing services are not widely available.

Where to Go Next from Grantsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to extend your trip beyond Grantsville, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Deep Creek Lake: The largest freshwater lake in Maryland, where visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Fallingwater: A stunning architectural masterpiece designed by Frank Lloyd Wright, located just over an hour away in neighboring Pennsylvania.
  • Ohiopyle State Park: A scenic park in Pennsylvania featuring stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and white-water rafting opportunities.

Conclusion

Grantsville offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With so much to see and do, it’s no wonder that so many people have fallen in love with this charming town. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure or a peaceful retreat, Grantsville has something for everyone.
