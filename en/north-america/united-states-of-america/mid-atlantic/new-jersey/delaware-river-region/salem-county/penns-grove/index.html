Introduction to Penns Grove

Located in Salem County, Delaware River Region, New Jersey, Penns Grove is a small borough that boasts a rich history and a diverse culture. Known for its beautiful landscapes and fascinating landmarks, Penns Grove is a great destination for those who love the outdoors, history, and culture. In this comprehensive travel guide to Penns Grove, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to stay, and where to eat when exploring this charming borough.

List of Places to Visit in Penns Grove

Penns Grove is home to a variety of attractions that are sure to appeal to travelers of all ages. Here are just a few of the must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Mott State Park: This historic park covers over 100 acres and features several historic buildings, including concrete gun batteries and an officer’s quarters. Take a guided tour and learn about the park’s history.
  • Delaware River & Bay Lighthouse Foundation: Located on the Delaware River, this unique organization works to protect and preserve historic lighthouses. Take a tour of the lighthouse and learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Penns Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Supawna Meadows National Wildlife Refuge: This stunning refuge covers over 3,000 acres and is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including bald eagles, great blue herons, and more. Take a hike or a bike ride on one of the many trail systems.
  • Raccoon Creek State Park: This beautiful park offers a variety of activities, including fishing, hiking, and camping. Take a hike to see the scenic views of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Penns Grove

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure while in Penns Grove, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • The Michael N. Castle C&D Canal Trail: This 16-mile trail runs from Delaware City to Chesapeake City, Maryland and is a popular spot for hikers and bikers. The trail offers stunning views of the Chesapeake and Delaware Canal.
  • The Salem Riverfront Trail: This 2.6-mile trail runs along the beautiful Salem River and is ideal for biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Penns Grove: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling alone, with friends, or with family, Penns Grove offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Penns Grove:

  • Take a scenic boat tour: Explore the Delaware River and enjoy stunning views of the area from the water. There are several tour operators in the area to choose from.
  • Visit the Penns Grove Farmers Market: This popular outdoor market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and more. It’s open on Saturdays from May to October.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Penns Grove, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Penns Grove

When it comes to dining in Penns Grove, you’ll find a variety of options to suit every taste. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: Made with fresh, local crab, this dish can be found in many of the town’s restaurants.
  • Pork roll, egg, and cheese sandwich: A breakfast staple in New Jersey, this delicious sandwich can be found in many of the town’s diners.

Local Beverages

  • Tom Collins: This classic drink is made with gin, lemon juice, sugar, and soda water.
  • Sangria: Made with red wine, brandy, and fresh fruit, this refreshing drink is perfect for warm weather.
