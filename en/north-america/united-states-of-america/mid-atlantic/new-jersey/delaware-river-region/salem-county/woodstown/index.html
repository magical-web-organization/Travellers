Introduction to Woodstown

Located in Salem County, in the Delaware River Region of New Jersey, Woodstown is a charming town surrounded by beautiful farmland, woods, and creeks. The town is known for its rich history, friendly locals, and vibrant community events. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Woodstown has something for everyone!

List of Places to Visit in Woodstown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Woodstown Friends Meetinghouse: Built in 1793, the Woodstown Friends Meetinghouse is one of the oldest Quaker meetinghouses in continuous use in the United States. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the Quaker faith and the history of the meetinghouse.
  • Woodstown Historic District: Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, the Woodstown Historic District features over 150 historic buildings and structures, including the Woodstown-Pilesgrove Library, the Woodstown Borough Hall, and the Olde Towne Tavern.
  • Salem County Historical Society: Located just a few miles from Woodstown, the Salem County Historical Society features exhibits on the county’s rich history, including pre-Colonial artifacts, maritime history, and agricultural exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Woodstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Woodstown Swamp Trail: This 1.5-mile trail takes visitors through a beautiful wetland area filled with a variety of plant and animal species.
  • Alloway Creek Watershed: The Alloway Creek Watershed is a great area for fishing, hiking, and kayaking, with beautiful views of the surrounding farmland.
  • Salem County Fairgrounds: The fairgrounds host a variety of events throughout the year, including the Salem County Fair in August, which features agricultural exhibits, carnival rides, and live entertainment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Woodstown

  • Parvin State Park: Located just a few miles from Woodstown, Parvin State Park offers over 12 miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as lakes for fishing and swimming.
  • Salem County Bikeway: The Salem County Bikeway is a scenic 6-mile trail that runs through Woodstown and connects to other nearby towns.

Best Things to Do in Woodstown: Activities and Tours

  • Woodstown Fall Festival: Held every October, the Woodstown Fall Festival is a community event featuring live music, crafts, food vendors, and a pumpkin carving contest.
  • Benefit of Lights: Held every December, the Benefit of Lights is a drive-through light display that raises money for local charities.
  • 8th Annual Woodstown Music Festival: Held every May, this is a great opportunity to hear some of the best local bands in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Woodstown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jersey Tomatoes: The Garden State is famous for its tomatoes, and Woodstown is no exception. Try them fresh or in local dishes like tomato pie or tomato sauce.
  • Pork Roll: Also known as Taylor Ham, this local favorite can be found on breakfast sandwiches throughout the region.
  • Crumb Cake: A staple of New Jersey bakeries, crumb cake features a deliciously crumbly topping.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Devil Vodka: Made with locally grown corn, Jersey Devil Vodka is a smooth and flavorful spirit made right here in New Jersey.
  • Cape May Brewing Company: Located in nearby Cape May, this award-winning brewery offers a variety of classic and innovative brews.

Where to Stay in Woodstown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at Salem Country Club: This elegant bed and breakfast is located just a few miles from Woodstown and features luxurious rooms, an 18-hole golf course, and a restaurant.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites: Vineland: Located about 20 minutes from Woodstown, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Woodstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Farm-fresh produce: Pick up some locally grown fruits and vegetables from one of the many farmers’ markets or road-side stands in the area.
  • Handmade crafts: Check out the Woodstown Fall Festival or other local craft fairs for unique handmade gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Woodstown

Woodstown is a welcoming and friendly town, known for its strong sense of community and Quaker values. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and norms, and be prepared to experience the genuine warmth and hospitality of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Woodstown

The best time to visit Woodstown is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Summer months tend to be hot and humid, and winter weather can be cold and unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Woodstown

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the area
  • Attend local community events
  • Respect local customs and norms

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Disrupt local wildlife
  • Disrespect local culture or traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Woodstown

Woodstown is a safe community, with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise caution when driving on rural roads, particularly at night.

Health in Woodstown

Visitors should take common-sense precautions to protect themselves from tick bites and exposure to poison ivy when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Woodstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Woodstown

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located about 30 miles from Woodstown, Philadelphia International Airport offers direct flights to many major cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Woodstown

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound bus station is in Philadelphia, about 30 miles from Woodstown.

Other Options

  • Driving: Woodstown is easily accessible by car, located just off Route 40 and Route 45.

How to Get Around in Woodstown

Visitors to Woodstown will need a car to get around, as public transportation options are limited.

Where to Go Next from Woodstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cape May: Located about an hour and a half from Woodstown, Cape May is a historic Victorian seaside town known for its beaches, architecture, and restaurants.
  • Philadelphia: Just a short drive from Woodstown, the City of Brotherly Love offers world-class museums, restaurants, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Woodstown is a hidden gem in New Jersey, offering a unique combination of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s Quaker heritage, hiking the nearby trails, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local cuisine, Woodstown has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the charm and hospitality of this beautiful town!
