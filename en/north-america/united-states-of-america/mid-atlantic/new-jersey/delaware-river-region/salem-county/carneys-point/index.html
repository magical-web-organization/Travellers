Introduction to Carneys Point

Located in Salem County, in the Delaware River Region of New Jersey, Carneys Point is a charming town that offers a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With its rich history, stunning scenery, and friendly locals, Carneys Point is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience.

List of Places to Visit in Carneys Point

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pennsville National Bank: This historic bank building features a museum and a library that showcase the history and cultural heritage of the region.

  • 1812 Memorial Park: Dedicated to the men who fought and died in the War of 1812, this park is home to a monument that honors the gallant service of these brave soldiers.

  • John Fenwick Mansion: Built in 1730, this beautiful Georgian-style mansion is a must-see for architecture and history enthusiasts. Guided tours are available.

Natural Attractions of Carneys Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fort Mott State Park: This beautiful park offers breathtaking views of the Delaware River and features a variety of recreational activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Supawna Meadows National Wildlife Refuge: This 3,000-acre sanctuary is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including migratory birds, waterfowl, and other aquatic animals.

  • Carneys Point Recreation Park: This park features a playground, basketball courts, and a softball field, making it a favorite destination for families and sports enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carneys Point

  • River View Trail: This 1.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the Delaware River and is ideal for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.

  • Fort Mott Trail: This 2.5-mile trail leads through the heart of Fort Mott State Park and offers visitors a chance to explore the park’s historical and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Carneys Point: Activities and Tours

  • Delaware River Cruises: A 90-minute scenic cruise along the Delaware River is a great way to see the town from a different perspective.

  • Wine Tasting at Auburn Road Vineyards: A visit to this charming vineyard is a must-do for wine lovers. Enjoy a tour of the vineyard and a tasting of their award-winning wines.

  • Kayaking on the Delaware River: Explore the scenic beauty of the Delaware River by renting a kayak and paddling along its calm waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Carneys Point

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: This local delicacy made with fresh seafood is a must-try for foodies.

  • Jersey Tomatoes: The delicious and juicy Jersey tomatoes are a treat for vegetable lovers.

  • Pork Roll: A popular breakfast food, the pork roll is a delicious alternative to bacon and sausage.

Local Beverages

  • Cape May Brewing Company: This popular brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are brewed using locally-sourced ingredients.

  • Tomasello Winery: This family-owned winery is known for its award-winning wines that are made using traditional winemaking techniques.

Where to Stay in Carneys Point: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Pennsville: This comfortable and modern hotel is conveniently located close to the main attractions in Carneys Point.

  • Pennsville Motel: This budget-friendly motel is perfect for travelers who are looking for affordable accommodations in the heart of town.

  • The Inn at Salem Country Club: This charming guesthouse is located in a beautifully restored 1835 mansion and offers guests a unique and unforgettable stay.

What to Buy in Carneys Point: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fresh Seafood: Take home a taste of the town’s famous seafood dishes by buying fresh shrimp, lobster, and crab from local seafood markets.

  • Local Honey: The honey produced by Carneys Point’s beekeepers is known for its distinct flavor and quality.

  • Handmade Crafts: Shop for unique handmade crafts and souvenirs made by local artisans at the town’s weekly flea market.

Local Culture and Customs of Carneys Point

Carneys Point is a friendly and welcoming town that is proud of its cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to engage with the locals, try the local cuisine, and participate in community events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Carneys Point

The best time to visit Carneys Point is during the fall season, when the weather is mild, the foliage is at its peak, and the town’s many festivals and events offer visitors a chance to experience its rich cultural heritage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carneys Point

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood dishes and craft beer.

  • Do explore the town’s natural beauty by taking a hike or renting a kayak.

  • Do participate in community events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s natural and cultural resources.

  • Don’t ignore the town’s rich culture and history.

  • Don’t be disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carneys Point

Carneys Point is a safe and welcoming town. However, visitors are advised to take the usual safety precautions, such as keeping their valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Carneys Point

There are no major health concerns in Carneys Point. Visitors are advised to bring any prescription medication they may need, and to stay hydrated during the summer months.

Getting to Carneys Point: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carneys Point

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located 26 miles away from Carneys Point, this airport is the closest major airport to the town.

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located 74 miles away from Carneys Point, this airport is another option for travelers flying in to the region.

Bus Stations Near Carneys Point

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in nearby Wilmington, Delaware, this bus station offers daily connections to major cities in the region.

  • Megabus Stop: Located in nearby Cherry Hill, New Jersey, this bus stop offers daily connections to major cities on the East Coast.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from the nearby airports to Carneys Point.

How to Get Around in Carneys Point

Carneys Point is a small town that is easily walkable. Visitors can also rent a bike or a car to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Carneys Point: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Atlantic City: Located 46 miles away from Carneys Point, Atlantic City is a popular destination for entertainment and gambling, as well as for its famous boardwalk and beach.

Conclusion

Carneys Point is a town that offers a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an active vacation, Carneys Point has something for everyone. With its friendly locals, delicious food, and rich history, this charming town is a must-visit destination for travelers.
