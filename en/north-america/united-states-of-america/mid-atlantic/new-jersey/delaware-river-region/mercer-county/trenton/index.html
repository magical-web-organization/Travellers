Introduction to Trenton

Trenton is a small city located in Mercer County, in Delaware River Region, in New Jersey, in Mid-Atlantic, in the United States of America. Despite its small size, Trenton is packed with cultural and natural attractions that make it a great destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Trenton

Here are some top attractions to visit during your trip to Trenton.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Jersey State Museum: This museum exhibits the cultural and natural history of New Jersey and features engaging displays and interactive exhibits.

  • Old Barracks Museum: A well-preserved historic barracks that dates back to the Revolutionary War. The museum gives an insight into the lives of the soldiers who lived there.

  • William Trent House Museum: This museum is the former home of the founder of Trenton, William Trent. The house dates back to the early 1700s and features period furniture and exhibits about Trenton’s history.

Natural Attractions of Trenton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delaware River: As a city situated on the bank of the Delaware River, Trenton offers many opportunities for riverfront activities. River boat tours, fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding are popular outdoor activities.

  • Mercer County Park: This large park spans over 2,000 acres and contains hiking trails, playgrounds, picnic areas, and sports courts.

  • Grounds for Sculpture: An outdoor sculpture park filled with contemporary art installations amidst beautifully tended gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trenton

Trenton has several beautiful hiking and biking trails, such as:

  • Lawrence Hopewell Trail: A 22-mile trail that loops through the scenic landscapes of Hopewell and Lawrence townships.

  • Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park Trail: A 77-mile trail that runs alongside the historic canal and offers stunning views of the canal, river, and nearby towns.

  • John A. Roebling Park: This park features several miles of hiking trails that take visitors through wetlands and marshes.

Best Things to Do in Trenton: Activities and Tours

  • Get a bird’s eye view of the city on a Hot Air Balloon Ride.

  • Take a walking tour of the city to learn more about its history of the Revolutionary War.

  • Visit Terhune Orchards, where you can pick your own fruits and pumpkins and enjoy homemade treats in their farm’s cafe.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trenton

Here is a recommended week-long itinerary for Trenton:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the New Jersey State Museum and enjoy lunch at Cafe @ the Museum.
Day 2 Explore the city’s historic sites, such as the Trenton Battle Monument, and Old Barracks Museum.
Day 3 Take a hot air balloon ride for a bird’s eye view of the city, or go hiking on the Lawrence Hopewell Trail.
Day 4 Visit the William Trent House Museum and learn more about the history of the city.
Day 5 Spend the day at Grounds for Sculpture and enjoy their garden exhibits and contemporary art installations.
Day 6 Hike the Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park Trail and have a picnic lunch at one of the canal-watching areas.
Day 7 Go apple picking at Terhune Orchards and stop by the Trenton Farmers’ Market before leaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Trenton

The food scene in Trenton reflects the city’s cultural diversity. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Roll Sandwich: A Trenton staple that consists of thinly sliced pork roll served on a roll with egg and cheese.

  • Trenton Tomato Pie: A type of pizza that is cooked in an un-greased pan and with the sauce on the top, produced since 1912 in the city.

  • Salt Water Taffy: Delicious salt-water taffy can be found throughout Trenton.

Local Beverages

  • Trenton Lager: A locally brewed beer that has become a Trenton classic.

  • Tomato Juice: Trenton is widely known for its tomato juice, as the city once was known for its tomato production.

Where to Stay in Trenton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trenton has several options for accommodation to fit any budget.

  • Wyndham Garden Trenton: A central location and comfortable rooms make this a top choice among travelers.

  • SpringHill Suites Ewing Town Center: A modern hotel offering spacious rooms and an indoor pool.

  • Holiday Inn Express Princeton Southeast: A great option for those traveling on a budget.

What to Buy in Trenton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Trenton Pottery: These potteries are known for their intricate designs and make excellent souvenirs.

  • Dried Fruit and Nuts: Stop by local dried fruit and nut orchards for sweet and savory treats.

  • Local Wine: Many wineries can be found near Trenton, and their wines make excellent souvenirs for those who love wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Trenton

Trenton is a city that prides itself on its diversity. It is important to be respectful of this diversity and not to make assumptions about individual customs or language.

Best Time to Visit Trenton

The best time to visit Trenton is during the spring or fall when the city experiences comfortable temperatures. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be quite cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trenton

Do:

  • Try the Trenton Tomato Pie and Pork Roll Sandwich.
  • Visit the numerous historical sites.
  • Bring comfortable shoes for walking or hiking.

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone speaks English.
  • Leave valuables visible in your car.
  • Walk alone at night in unlit areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trenton

Like any city, Trenton has its share of crime. Travelers should take basic safety precautions: avoid walking alone at night, stay in well-lit areas, and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight in their car.

Health in Trenton

Trenton has several hospitals and urgent care facilities. If you need medical attention while visiting the city, do not hesitate to seek out urgent care or emergency services.

Getting to Trenton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Trenton has several transportation options for getting in and around the city.

Airports Near Trenton

  • Trenton-Mercer Airport: Located just outside of town, this airport serves several major cities, including Atlanta and Chicago.

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located approximately 30 miles from Trenton, this major airport has many flight options.

Bus Stations Near Trenton

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located less than a mile from the central business district, this station offers bus service to numerous cities across the U.S.

  • New Jersey Transit: Provides bus service throughout New Jersey and links to New York City and Philadelphia.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: A train can be taken into the city from New York, Newark Airport, and Philadelphia. The Trenton Transit Center is located centrally to Trenton’s downtown and historic sites.

How to Get Around in Trenton

Trenton is fairly easy to navigate using public transportation or rideshare services. However, for those who prefer to walk, much of the city’s top attractions are within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Trenton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Princeton: This nearby college town is home to Princeton University and numerous cultural and historical sites.

  • Atlantic City: Known for its beaches, casinos, and boardwalk, Atlantic City makes for a fun day trip from Trenton.

  • Gettysburg National Military Park: Located less than 100 miles from Trenton, Gettysburg is an important Civil War site that features a museum and a large park.

Conclusion

Whether you are interested in history, outdoor activities, or delicious cuisine, Trenton has something to offer. This guide is just a starting point to help plan your trip to this gem of a city. By following these tips, recommendations, and safety precautions, you can ensure a memorable and enjoyable trip.
