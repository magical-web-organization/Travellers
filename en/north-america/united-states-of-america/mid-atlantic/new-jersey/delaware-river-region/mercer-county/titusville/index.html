Introduction to Titusville

Located in the Delaware River Region of Mercer County, New Jersey, Titusville is a small, charming town that offers a mix of cultural and outdoor attractions. Surrounded by the scenic Delaware River, Titusville attracts visitors looking for a serene, nature-filled getaway or a dose of history and culture.

In this comprehensive guide to Titusville, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Titusville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Washington Crossing State Park: This state park is home to the famous Washington Crossing Historic Park, where George Washington crossed the Delaware River with his troops on December 25, 1776, during the Revolutionary War. Visitors can tour the park and see reenactments of the historic crossing.
  • Howell Living History Farm: This farm museum offers visitors a glimpse of what farm life was like in the early days of the 20th century. Visitors can take a tour of the farm, meet the animals, and learn about traditional farming methods.
  • The Trenton City Museum at Ellarslie Mansion: Housed in a historic mansion, this museum showcases a collection of art and artifacts related to the history of Trenton and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Titusville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delaware River: One of the main attractions in Titusville is the Delaware River, which offers plenty of opportunities for activities like kayaking, canoeing, and fishing. Visitors can also take a stroll along the river and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Baldpate Mountain: This 1,200-acre park in Titusville offers plenty of hiking and nature trails, allowing visitors to explore the area’s lush forests and beautiful wildlife.
  • Howell Farm: Besides being an interesting historical site, this working farm is also a great place to see and learn about farming practices and techniques that date back centuries.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Titusville

  • D&R Canal State Park Trail: This trail stretches for 70 miles from the Delaware River to New Brunswick and follows the historic Delaware and Raritan Canal. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, or horseback riding along the shaded path while taking in the scenic views of the canal.
  • Baldpate Mountain: With its numerous trails of varying difficulty, Baldpate Mountain is a popular destination for hikers, mountain bikers, and nature lovers. The mountain offers stunning views of the surrounding area, including the Delaware River and the New Jersey countryside.
  • Washington Crossing State Park Trails: Visitors can explore the park’s many trails on foot or by bike. The trails take visitors through wooded areas, fields, and along the Delaware River.

Best Things to Do in Titusville: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: The Delaware River is the perfect spot for kayaking and canoeing, with its calm waters and tranquil scenery. Visitors can rent kayaks and canoes, or take a guided tour.
  • Washington Crossing Historic Park Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the park and see where George Washington crossed the Delaware River with his troops on December 25, 1776.
  • Farm Tours: Howell Living History Farm offers farm tours that allow visitors to learn about traditional farming methods and see how they have evolved over the years.

Week-Long Itinerary for Titusville

Day 1: Visit the Howell Living History Farm Day 2: Take a tour of the Washington Crossing Historic Park Day 3: Explore the D&R Canal State Park Trail Day 4: Kayaking on the Delaware River Day 5: Hiking at Baldpate Mountain Day 6: Discover the Trenton City Museum at Ellarslie Mansion Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views along the Delaware River

Food and Drink Scene of Titusville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Roll: A New Jersey staple, this thinly sliced and processed pork product is typically served on a roll with cheese and egg.
  • Tomato Pie: A Trenton classic, tomato pie is similar to pizza, but with the sauce on top of the cheese.
  • Salt Water Taffy: A popular Jersey Shore treat, salt water taffy comes in a variety of flavors and can be found at many local candy stores.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Fresh: This initiative promotes locally grown produce and products, including beer, cider, and wine.
  • Yuengling: America’s oldest brewery, based in nearby Pennsylvania, is a popular beer choice in New Jersey.

Where to Stay in Titusville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at Glencairn: This charming inn offers comfortable rooms and a peaceful location near Howell Farm and Washington Crossing State Park.
  • The Lambertville Station Inn: A historic hotel located in the charming town of Lambertville, just a short drive from Titusville.
  • Loveys Bed and Breakfast: A romantic and cozy choice, located in nearby Pennington.

What to Buy in Titusville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Howell Farm Maple Syrup: This farm-fresh syrup is made on site at Howell Living History Farm.
  • Riverfront Views: Take home a scenic picture from your stroll along the Delaware River that flows through Titusville.

Local Culture and Customs of Titusville

While New Jersey is often associated with its beaches and boardwalks, Titusville offers a different kind of Jersey experience. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly locals, many of whom are passionate about the history and natural beauty of the area.

Best Time to Visit Titusville

The best time to visit Titusville is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Visitors can also enjoy the area’s fall foliage in late October and early November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Titusville

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the natural beauty of the area and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking.
  • Do visit historic sites like the Washington Crossing Historic Park and the Howell Living History Farm.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore the unique food scene in Titusville and throughout New Jersey. Be sure to try local specialties like tomato pie and pork roll.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring the area’s many nature trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Titusville

Titusville is a relatively safe area, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to ensure their safety. Visitors should also be mindful of potential hazards when participating in outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking.

Health in Titusville

There are no major health concerns for visitors to Titusville, but visitors should drink plenty of water and use sunscreen when participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Titusville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Titusville

The nearest airport is Trenton-Mercer Airport (TTN), located about 15 miles from Titusville. Other nearby airports include Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) and Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR).

Bus Stations Near Titusville

Greyhound operates buses to and from Trenton, which is a short drive from Titusville.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Titusville, which is located just off of Highway 29. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Titusville

The best way to get around Titusville is by car, as many of the area’s attractions are spread out and some are located outside the town itself. Visitors can also rent bikes or walk along the Delaware and Raritan Canal towpath.

Where to Go Next from Titusville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Princeton: A charming college town with beautiful architecture and a vibrant cultural scene.
  • New Hope: A quaint town on the other side of the Delaware River, known for its art galleries, restaurants, and historic theater.
  • Philadelphia: One of the most historic and culturally rich cities in the United States, located about an hour’s drive from Titusville.

Conclusion

Titusville may be a small town, but it offers visitors a range of attractions and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Titusville is the perfect destination for a laid-back and relaxing vacation.
