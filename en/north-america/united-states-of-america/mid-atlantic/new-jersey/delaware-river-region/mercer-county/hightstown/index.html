Introduction to Hightstown

Located in the heart of Mercer County, Hightstown is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of attractions to explore. Nestled in the beautiful Delaware River Region, this small town has something to offer everyone, from museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Whether you’re here for a day trip or a week-long vacation, you’ll find plenty of things to keep you entertained.

List of Places to Visit in Hightstown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hightstown is home to a number of culturally important attractions that offer a glimpse into the town’s fascinating history. Some must-visit museums and historical landmarks include:

  • The Peddie School - This prestigious prep school has been educating students since 1864 and is surrounded by beautiful architecture and sprawling grounds.

  • The Hightstown-East Windsor Historical Society Museum - This small museum houses an impressive collection of local artifacts and exhibits that showcase the area’s rich history.

  • The Old Barracks Museum - Located in nearby Trenton, this museum is a fascinating look into colonial New Jersey and features reenactments and interactive exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Hightstown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to get out and explore the great outdoors, Hightstown won’t disappoint. Here are some of the best natural attractions in the area:

  • Mercer County Park - This park is a 2,500-acre oasis with miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as playgrounds, tennis courts, and more.

  • Peddie Golf Club - This championship golf course is set against a backdrop of rolling hills and scenic views, and is open to the public.

  • Lake Assunpink - This beautiful lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking, and also has several hiking trails to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hightstown

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will love the trails in and around Hightstown. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Kingston Branch Loop Trail - This paved trail is a popular spot for bikers and walkers and will take you through some of the area’s most scenic spots.

  • The D&R Canal State Park Trail - This trail follows an old canal and is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching.

  • The Lawrence Hopewell Trail - This trail stretches for several miles and takes bikers and walkers through some of the most beautiful scenery in the area.

Best Things to Do in Hightstown: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Hightstown, including:

  • The Hightstown Summer Concert Series - This annual event brings together some of the best musical acts in the area for a series of free concerts in the park.

  • The Princeton Tour Company - This company offers a variety of tours, including ghost tours, food tours, and historical tours of the area.

  • The Hightstown Farmers Market - This vibrant farmers market is held weekly and is a great place to pick up fresh produce, crafts, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hightstown

If you’re planning a week-long stay in Hightstown, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1 - Explore the Hightstown-East Windsor Historical Society Museum and take a stroll through the quaint downtown area.

Day 2 - Spend the day at Mercer County Park, taking in the natural beauty of the area and maybe enjoying a picnic lunch.

Day 3 - Take a bike ride along the Kingston Branch Loop Trail and explore the nearby towns of Kingston and Princeton.

Day 4 - Visit the Old Barracks Museum in nearby Trenton and learn about colonial New Jersey history.

Day 5 - Take a tour of the area with the Princeton Tour Company and end the day with a delicious dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 6 - Play a round of golf at Peddie Golf Club and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 7 - Relax and soak in the local culture with a visit to the Hightstown Farmers Market and a leisurely stroll through the Peddie School grounds.

Food and Drink Scene of Hightstown

Must-Try Dishes

Foodies will find plenty to love in Hightstown, where the local dining scene is diverse and delicious. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Tomato pie - A regional specialty, tomato pie is a savory pie made with fresh tomatoes, cheese, and herbs.

  • Pork roll and cheese sandwich - This breakfast sandwich features a slice of pork roll, a type of cured meat, with melted cheese and a fluffy English muffin.

  • Blueberry pancakes - The area is known for its sweet, juicy blueberries, and many local restaurants serve up delicious blueberry pancakes.

Local Beverages

Hightstown is home to a number of local breweries and wineries, where you can sample some of the best beverages the area has to offer. Some of the top options include:

  • Triumph Brewing Company - This popular brewery and restaurant serves up a variety of craft beers and delicious pub fare.

  • Working Dog Winery - This beautiful winery is set against the backdrop of rolling hills and offers tours, tastings, and more.

  • Jersey Girl Brewing Company - This award-winning brewery is known for its wide variety of unique and flavorful beers.

Where to Stay in Hightstown

There are a number of great accommodation options in Hightstown, including:

  • The Courtyard by Marriott - This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities like a fitness center and indoor pool.

  • The American Hotel - This historic hotel has been serving guests since 1848 and offers cozy rooms and a charming atmosphere.

  • The Quality Inn East Windsor - This budget-friendly option offers clean and comfortable rooms and is located just a short drive from the heart of Hightstown.

What to Buy in Hightstown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, some great options include:

  • Homemade jams and preserves made with fresh local fruit.
  • Unique crafts and gifts from local artisans and craftsmen.
  • Local honey and maple syrup, both of which are harvested in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Hightstown

Hightstown has a rich history and many longstanding traditions that remain an important part of the local culture. One such tradition is the annual Hightstown-East Windsor Historical Society Craft Fair, where locals and visitors alike come together to celebrate the area’s vibrant arts and crafts community.

Best Time to Visit Hightstown

The best time to visit Hightstown is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the scenery is breathtaking. Summers can be hot and humid, but this is also a popular time for outdoor concerts and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hightstown

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the area’s fascinating history and beautiful natural scenery.
  • Do sample some of the local cuisine, especially the region’s famous tomato pie and blueberries.
  • Do participate in local events and festivals to get a true sense of the area’s culture and community.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring the area’s parks and trails.
  • Don’t miss out on the chance to go wine tasting at one of the area’s many wineries.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals, who are known for their friendliness and hospitality.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hightstown

Hightstown is a safe and welcoming town, but as with any destination, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring parks and trails.
  • Stick to well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone, if possible.

Health in Hightstown

Hightstown has several medical facilities and pharmacies, should you need medical assistance during your stay. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medications with you when traveling and to have travel insurance in case of an emergency.

Getting to Hightstown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hightstown

The closest airports to Hightstown are:

  • Trenton-Mercer Airport (TTN) - This small regional airport is located just a few miles from Hightstown and is served by several major airlines.

  • Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR) - This major airport is located approximately an hour’s drive from Hightstown and offers flights to destinations all over the world.

Bus Stations Near Hightstown

Several bus stations serve the Hightstown area, including:

  • Princeton Junction - This station is located just a few miles from Hightstown and offers service to New York City and other nearby destinations.

  • Trenton Transit Center - This major transportation hub offers connections to bus and train lines throughout the region.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Hightstown is easily accessible from several major highways, including the New Jersey Turnpike and Interstate 295. It’s also a popular stop for Amtrak trains traveling between New York City and Philadelphia.

How to Get Around in Hightstown

The best way to get around Hightstown and the surrounding area is by car or bike. There are several rental car companies in the area, as well as bike rental shops that can provide you with everything you need for a day of exploring.

Where to Go Next from Hightstown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of New Jersey and the surrounding areas, some great options include:

  • Princeton - This charming college town is just a short drive from Hightstown and is full of shops, restaurants, and museums.

  • Six Flags Great Adventure - This popular amusement park is located about half an hour away from Hightstown and is a great spot for thrill-seekers of all ages.

  • Philadelphia - This vibrant city is just an hour’s drive from Hightstown and is full of historical landmarks, delicious food, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Hightstown has plenty to offer. With its charming small-town vibe, beautiful natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage, this New Jersey gem is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States.
