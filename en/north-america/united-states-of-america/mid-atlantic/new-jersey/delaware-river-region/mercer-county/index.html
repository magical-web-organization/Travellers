Introduction to Mercer County

Mercer County is located in the heart of Delaware River Region, in New Jersey, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. It is known for its rich history, charming countryside, and vibrant cultural scene. Mercer County is also home to Princeton University, one of the most prestigious universities in the world.

List of Places to Visit in Mercer County

Mercer County offers a mix of natural and cultural attractions that will leave visitors captivated. Here are some of the must-visit places in Mercer County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Washington Crossing State Park: This historic park is the site of George Washington’s famous crossing of the Delaware River on Christmas night in 1776. Visitors can take a tour of the Visitor Center and Museum to learn more about this pivotal event in American history.
  • Princeton University Art Museum: This museum houses an impressive collection of works from around the world, including ancient and contemporary art. It also has a large collection of art from Asia and Africa.
  • Old Barracks Museum: The Old Barracks Museum is a beautifully restored colonial building that dates back to 1758. It is a great place to learn about the history of New Jersey and its role in the American Revolution.
  • New Jersey State Museum: The New Jersey State Museum is a treasure trove of natural and cultural history. It has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase New Jersey’s rich heritage and diverse natural environment.
  • Trenton Battle Monument: This stunning monument stands as a tribute to the fallen soldiers of the Revolutionary War. Visitors can climb to the top to enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Mercer County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mercer County Park: With over 2,500 acres of parkland, Mercer County Park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. It features a lake, hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and a variety of recreational facilities.
  • Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park: This scenic park is a great place to take a stroll or bike ride. The park has more than 70 miles of trails that run along the Delaware and Raritan Canal and offer stunning views of the countryside.
  • Sourland Mountain Preserve: Located in the heart of Mercer County, the Sourland Mountain Preserve is a nature lover’s paradise. The preserve is home to a variety of wildlife, unique plant species, and scenic hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mercer County

Mercer County has a wealth of scenic hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Lawrence Hopewell Trail: This 22-mile trail offers beautiful views of Mercer County’s countryside. The trail includes paved and unpaved sections, making it perfect for both walking and biking.
  • Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park Trail: This trail offers 77 miles of scenic biking and hiking routes that run along the Delaware and Raritan Canal.
  • Mercer Meadows: With over 1,600 acres of parkland, Mercer Meadows has plenty of trails for hiking and biking. The park is known for its wildflowers, birdwatching opportunities, and stunning views of the Sourland Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Mercer County: Activities and Tours

  • Grounds for Sculpture: This 42-acre sculpture park is a must-visit for art lovers. It features over 270 works of art by renowned artists from around the world.
  • Princeton Tour Company: Take a guided tour of Princeton University and explore the history and architecture of one of the most prestigious universities in the world.
  • Terhune Orchards & Winery: This family-owned orchard and winery is a great place to spend an afternoon. With over 200 acres of crops, visitors can pick their own fruits while enjoying a glass of wine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mercer County

Day 1: Visit Princeton University and the Princeton Art Museum, explore the town of Princeton.

Day 2: Visit Washington Crossing State Park and learn about the Revolutionary War, take a stroll along the Delaware River.

Day 3: Hike the trails at Mercer Meadows and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 4: Explore Grounds for Sculpture, have lunch at Rat’s Restaurant.

Day 5: Take a tour of the Old Barracks Museum, visit the New Jersey State Museum.

Day 6: Go apple picking at Terhune Orchards, enjoy a wine tasting at Terhune Winery.

Day 7: Spend the day at Mercer County Park, rent a paddleboat on the lake, have a picnic lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Mercer County

Mercer County has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases its rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Roll: A New Jersey staple, pork roll is a smoked and cured breakfast meat that is typically served on a roll with egg and cheese.
  • Tomato Pie: A thin-crust pizza topped with tomato sauce and a sprinkle of cheese, tomato pie is a Garden State classic.
  • Seafood: With its proximity to the coast, Mercer County is known for its fresh seafood. Be sure to try the Jersey Shore Clams and Lobster Rolls.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Mercer County is home to a number of craft breweries, including Triumph Brewing Company and River Horse Brewing Company.
  • Wine: Terhune Orchards & Winery is a great place to try the local wines. Mercer County is known for its crisp white wines and full-bodied reds.
  • Apple Cider: Terhune Orchards also makes a mean apple cider, perfect for sipping on a crisp fall day.

Where to Stay in Mercer County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mercer County offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Peacock Inn: A luxurious boutique hotel located in the heart of downtown Princeton.
  • Nassau Inn: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Princeton and offers comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • SpringHill Suites: Located in Ewing Township, this modern hotel offers spacious rooms with all the amenities.

What to Buy in Mercer County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mercer County has a great selection of local products and souvenirs that are perfect for bringing home. Here are some of the best:

  • Princeton University Merchandise: Show off your love for Princeton University with a souvenir t-shirt or sweatshirt.
  • Terhune Orchards Products: Pick up some of their famous apple cider or homemade fruit pies to bring home.
  • Jersey Charm Bracelet: A perfect souvenir to remember your trip to New Jersey, featuring charms of iconic Jersey Shore destinations.

Local Culture and Customs of Mercer County

Mercer County is a melting pot of different cultures and ethnicities. New Jersey is known for its laid-back and friendly culture, with locals always willing to lend a helping hand.

Best Time to Visit Mercer County

The best time to visit Mercer County is in the fall, when the leaves are changing colors and the weather is mild. The spring and summer are also great times to visit, with plenty of outdoor activities and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mercer County

Do:

  • Visit Princeton University and explore the town of Princeton.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Take a hike on the Lawrence Hopewell Trail or at Mercer Meadows.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions while hiking or biking.
  • Disrespect the local customs and culture.
  • Feed the wildlife at Mercer County Park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mercer County

Mercer County is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding poorly lit areas at night. Hikers and bikers should be aware of their surroundings and stay on designated trails.

Health in Mercer County

Visitors to Mercer County should take the necessary health precautions when traveling, such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated. It is also important to carry any necessary medication and to have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Mercer County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mercer County is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some of the main transportation options:

Airports Near Mercer County

  • Trenton-Mercer Airport: Located in Ewing Township, Trenton-Mercer Airport offers regional flights to select cities.

Bus Stations Near Mercer County

  • Trenton Transit Center: Located in downtown Trenton, the Trenton Transit Center offers bus and train services to various destinations.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Amtrak trains stop at the Trenton Transit Center and offer service to destinations throughout the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic regions.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Mercer County, offering convenient transportation options for visitors.

How to Get Around in Mercer County

The best way to get around Mercer County is by car, but there are also public transportation options available. Buses and trains offer service to various destinations in and around Mercer County.

Where to Go Next from Mercer County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Philadelphia: Located just across the Delaware River, Philadelphia offers a wealth of cultural attractions, historic landmarks, and exciting nightlife.
  • New York City: A short train or bus ride from Mercer County, New York City is a must-see destination for any traveler.

Conclusion

Mercer County is a hidden gem in the Mid-Atlantic region, offering a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene. With so much to see and do, visitors will be sure to have an unforgettable trip to Mercer County.
