Introduction to Princeton

Princeton is a picturesque town located in the heart of the Delaware River Region in Mercer County, New Jersey. It has a rich history dating back to the American Revolution and is home to the prestigious Princeton University. With its excellent museums, historical landmarks, outdoor activities, and vibrant food scene, Princeton is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, history, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Princeton

Princeton is home to many culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here are some must-visit places that should be on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Princeton University Art Museum: The museum houses one of the most significant art collections in the country with over 100,000 works of art dating back to ancient times.
  • Morven Museum and Garden: Located in the former Governor’s mansion, the museum showcases New Jersey’s rich cultural heritage, art, and history.
  • Princeton Battlefield: The site of a pivotal American Revolution battle, the park is perfect for history buffs and hikers alike, with over 200 acres of trails that lead to the battlefield, Clarke House museum, and other historical landmarks.
  • Albert Einstein House: The former home of the famous physicist and Nobel Laureate, the house has been preserved as a museum and is open for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Princeton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park: This state park covers over 70 miles of scenic trails and waterways perfect for hiking, biking, canoeing, fishing, and bird-watching.
  • Mountain Lakes Nature Preserve: A 75-acre nature preserve with hiking trails, picnic areas, and beautiful scenic views of the surrounding forests and lakes.
  • Princeton Nurseries: This historic nursery and arboretum boasts over 200 acres of gorgeous gardens, greenhouses, and ponds, and is a popular spot for weddings and events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Princeton

Princeton is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of the region. Some popular routes include:

  • D&R Canal Trail: A 70-mile trail that runs along the Delaware and Raritan Canal and offers stunning views of the surrounding parks, forests, and waterways.
  • Princeton Battlefield State Park Trail: A 2.6-mile loop trail that passes through the historic battlefield and offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Greenway Meadows Park Trail: A 1.4-mile loop trail that winds along the Stony Brook and offers a picturesque view of the town of Princeton.

Best Things to Do in Princeton: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other exciting things to do in Princeton. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Princeton Tour Company: This popular tour company offers a variety of guided tours, including ghost tours, campus tours, art tours, and more.
  • The Escape Room: Perfect for a group outing, this interactive adventure game challenges players to solve puzzles and escape from a locked room within an hour.
  • Mccarter Theatre Center: This world-class theater hosts a variety of shows, concerts, and performances by renowned artists from around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Princeton

If you plan to spend a week in Princeton, here is a suggested itinerary that includes some of the best places to visit, things to do, and local experiences:

Day Activity
Day 1 Take a guided tour of Princeton University including the campus, art museum, and science library

Food and Drink Scene of Princeton

Princeton is a food lover’s paradise, offering a diverse range of cuisine that includes classic American fare, ethnic cuisine, and fine dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Princetonian Sandwich: A regional specialty that includes turkey, stuffing, and cranberry sauce served on a hoagie roll. Try it at the Hoagie Haven or the Princeton’s Main Street Eatery.
  • Blue Point Oysters: A favorite of seafood lovers, these fresh oysters are served at the Yankee Doodle Tap Room.
  • Bent Spoon Ice Cream: A local favorite, the Bent Spoon offers artisanal ice cream made from locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Triumph Brewing Company: Located in the heart of Princeton, the brewery offers a variety of craft beers and bar bites, perfect for a casual night out.
  • Nassau Vineyards: This premier winery produces some of the best wines in the region, including red, white, and dessert varieties.
  • Small World Coffee: A favorite of coffee lovers, this local coffee shop offers a variety of specialty drinks and pastries.

Where to Stay in Princeton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Princeton offers a variety of accommodations that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • The Peacock Inn: A luxury boutique hotel located in downtown Princeton, the Peacock Inn offers elegant rooms, fine dining, and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • The Homewood Suites by Hilton: Perfect for families and business travelers, the Homewood Suites offer comfortable suites, a fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Hostelling International: A popular hostel located in a historic mansion, Hostelling International offers shared and private rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Princeton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Princeton is home to many unique shops and boutiques that offer a variety of souvenirs and local products perfect for bringing back home. Some popular items include:

  • Princeton University Apparel and Accessories: Show your school spirit with a t-shirt, hoodie, or cap from the Princeton University Store.
  • Princeton Pottery Works: Get a unique ceramic piece designed by a local artist at the Princeton Pottery Works.
  • Farmers’ Markets: Visit the local farmers’ markets to pick up some fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Princeton

Princeton is a diverse and vibrant community that values education, culture, and tradition. Some of the local customs and cultural events include:

  • Princeton Reunions: A beloved tradition that brings alumni and students together for a weekend of celebration, reminiscing, and catching up with old friends.
  • Concerts on the Green: A summer concert series that offers free live music and entertainment at the Palmer Square.
  • Princeton Pi Day: Celebrated on March 14th, this annual event features math-themed activities, educational programs, and, of course, plenty of pie!

Best Time to Visit Princeton

Princeton is a year-round destination, with each season offering a unique experience. The best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests:

  • Spring (March - May): Perfect for hiking, outdoor adventures, and enjoying the blooming flowers and trees.
  • Summer (June - August): Ideal for festivals, outdoor concerts, and water activities.
  • Fall (September - November): The best time for leaf-peeping, apple picking, and enjoying the cooler weather.
  • Winter (December - February): Great for indoor activities such as museums, theater, and cozying up by the fireplace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Princeton

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Princeton:

Do:

  • Do visit the local farmers’ markets to sample some of the best food and produce of the region.

  • Do take a guided tour of Princeton University to learn about the fascinating history and culture of the institution.

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions, and interact respectfully with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind when visiting outdoor attractions like parks and trails.

  • Don’t assume that everything will be open on Sundays or other holidays.

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen, bug spray, and comfortable shoes when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Princeton is a safe and healthy town, with excellent medical facilities, emergency services, and public safety programs. Here are some tips to ensure you have a safe and healthy trip:

Safety in Princeton

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

  • Do not park your car in isolated areas or leave valuables visible inside your car.

  • Be mindful of the weather conditions and avoid hiking or biking in extreme temperatures.

Health in Princeton

  • Drinking tap water in Princeton is safe, but if you prefer, you can also buy bottled water.

  • Wear appropriate clothing and apply sunscreen to protect against sunburn and skin damage.

  • In case of a medical emergency, dial 911 or visit the nearest hospital or health clinic.

Getting to Princeton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Princeton is easily accessible by car, train, and bus. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Princeton

  • Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR) is the closest airport to Princeton, located approximately 45 minutes away.

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) is another option, located about an hour and a half away.

Bus Stations Near Princeton

  • The Princeton Junction train station serves as a hub for NJ Transit trains, buses, and Amtrak trains.

  • The Coach USA bus provides daily service to New York City, Philadelphia, and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is an excellent option for those who prefer to have their vehicle or explore the region at their pace.

  • Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are also available in Princeton.

How to Get Around in Princeton

Princeton is a walkable town, and many attractions and landmarks are easily accessible on foot. Biking is also a popular way to get around, with many bike lanes and trails throughout the town. Public transportation options include NJ Transit buses, trains, and local shuttles.

Where to Go Next from Princeton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Princeton is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding towns and attractions in the region. Here are some nearby destinations and day trips worth considering:

  • Lambertville: A charming town known for its antique shops, art galleries, and scenic views of the Delaware River.

  • Trenton: The state capital of New Jersey, home to many historical landmarks, museums, and excellent restaurants.

  • Six Flags Great Adventure: A popular amusement park located about 40 minutes from Princeton, featuring thrilling rides, water parks, and family-friendly entertainment.

Conclusion

Princeton is a hidden gem in the Delaware River Region, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and adventure. With its excellent museums, natural wonders, vibrant food scene, and welcoming locals, this charming town is a must-visit destination for any traveler. Start planning your trip to Princeton today and experience the best of New Jersey!
