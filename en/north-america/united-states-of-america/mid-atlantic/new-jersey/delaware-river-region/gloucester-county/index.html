Introduction to Gloucester County

Gloucester County is a charming destination located in the heart of the Delaware River Region in New Jersey. It’s known for its rich history, picturesque countryside, and a vibrant downtown scene.

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or culinary delights, Gloucester County has something to offer all kinds of travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and how to make the most of your trip to Gloucester County.

List of Places to Visit in Gloucester County

Gloucester County is home to several cultural and natural attractions, each offering a unique experience to its visitors. Here’s our list of must-visit places in the county:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Red Bank Battlefield Park: Step into history and visit this park, which was the site of a Revolutionary War battle.
  • Gloucester County Historical Society and Museum: Discover the county’s rich history through various exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • Heritage Glass Museum: Explore the art and science of glassblowing in this museum, which features several antique glass pieces and tools.

Natural Attractions of Gloucester County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scotland Run Park: A nature lover’s paradise, this park boasts of several hiking trails, fishing and boating spots, and picturesque picnic areas.
  • Wharton State Forest: Hikers, bikers, and campers can explore the largest forest reserve in New Jersey, which houses various plant and animal species.
  • Mantua Creek: Nature enthusiasts will appreciate the beauty of this creek, which offers kayaking and canoeing opportunities, fishing, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gloucester County

Gloucester County has several hiking and biking trails, each offering a different level of challenge and scenic views. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Battleground Trail: This trail passes through a Revolutionary War Battlefield and stretches for 1.5 miles.
  • Red Bank Battlefield Park Trail: Take a 0.6 mile hike and explore the park’s scenic views of the Delaware River and wildlife habitats.
  • Greenwich Trail: Perfect for a leisurely walk, this trail is just 3 miles long and offers a scenic view of a meadow, creek, and a historic mill site.

Best Things to Do in Gloucester County: Activities and Tours

From exploring historical sites to indulging in culinary delights, Gloucester County offers several exciting things to do. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Gloucester County Craftsmen and Home Show: A must-visit for craft enthusiasts, this show features handmade items, artisan crafts, and food vendors.
  • Ghost Tours of Historic Gloucester Town: Take a guided night tour and explore the history and mysteries of the town and its haunted sites.
  • South Jersey Wine Tours: Enjoy a full-day wine tour and sample some excellent local wines while soaking in the beautiful views of the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gloucester County

If you’re planning to spend a week in Gloucester County, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your visit:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Red Bank Battlefield Park and explore its walking trails
Day 2 Discover the county’s history at the Gloucester County Historical Society and Museum
Day 3 Take a full-day wine tour and sample some local wines
Day 4 Experience the natural beauty and wildlife of Scotland Run Park
Day 5 Take a guided ghost tour of the historic Gloucester Town
Day 6 Explore the art of glassblowing at the Heritage Glass Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Gloucester County

The food scene in Gloucester County is a melting pot of several ethnic cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hoagie: A perfect lunch sandwich with mouth-watering cold cuts and veggies.
  • Pork Roll: A breakfast staple, it is a type of spam that is pan-fried and often served with eggs and cheese on a bagel or a roll.
  • Tomato Pie: A savory and tangy pizza-like dish that is topped with a thick layer of tomato sauce and spices.

Local Beverages

  • River Horse IPA: A must-try local IPA made from American and Bavarian hops.
  • Cape May County Brewery: Try some of their specialty beer, crafted from locally grown and sourced hops.

Where to Stay in Gloucester County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gloucester County has several accommodation options to suit all kinds of travelers. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Fairfield Inn Deptford: A budget-friendly hotel that offers basic amenities and easy access to the county’s attractions.
  • The Inn on Holly: A charming guesthouse with comfortable rooms, excellent breakfast, and a prime location in the heart of the town.
  • Courtyard by Marriott: A luxury hotel with spacious rooms, modern amenities, and a convenient location to explore the county’s attractions.

What to Buy in Gloucester County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handcrafted pottery to locally made honey, you can find a wide variety of unique souvenirs in Gloucester County. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Jones mason Jar Co.: Buy a hand-painted mason jar, which is perfect for gifting or as a keepsake.
  • Sweet Life Bakery: Try some of the delicious cupcakes, cookie sandwiches, and locally made honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Gloucester County

Gloucester County has a friendly and close-knit community that takes pride in its history and traditions. Visitors are expected to respect the locals, their customs, and traditions. Here are some of the customs you should be aware of:

  • Respect for the Flag: Americans take their flag very seriously, so it’s essential to treat the flag and its representation with utmost respect.
  • Tipping: Tipping is the norm in the county, and most service workers rely on it to make a living. The standard tipping rate is 15-20% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Gloucester County

The best time to visit Gloucester County is from late spring to early fall when the weather is warm and sunny. This season is also ideal for outdoor activities, sightseeing, and festivals. However, if you’re looking for a quieter and more peaceful experience, you can plan your trip during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gloucester County

Here are some do’s and don’ts that visitors should keep in mind during their trip to Gloucester County:

Do:

  • Explore the Natural Beauty: The county is home to several natural attractions, so make sure you explore them all.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Gloucester County has a diverse food scene; don’t miss out on trying the must-try dishes and local brews.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the parks and trails clean and dispose of your trash responsibly.
  • Speeding: The county’s police are strict about speeding, so avoid driving above the speed limit.

Safety and Health

It’s essential to be aware of the safety and health concerns of the county before planning your trip. Here are some tips to have a safe trip:

Safety in Gloucester County

  • Stay Alert: Don’t leave personal belongings unattended as theft can occur even in this safe community.
  • Lock Your Doors and Windows: Make sure to lock your car and accommodation when you’re not inside.

Health in Gloucester County

  • Stay Hydrated: Keep yourself hydrated, especially during the summer months when the temperatures can soar.
  • Wear Sunscreen: Protect your skin from the sun and apply sunscreen before heading outdoors.

How to Get Around in Gloucester County

Gloucester County has an efficient public transportation system that offers easy access to its attractions. Here are some ways to get around:

  • Train: NJ Transit provides train services to the county.
  • Bus: The county has an extensive bus system that connects its attractions and neighboring towns.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies provide rental services in the county.
  • Bicycle: Renting a bicycle is a cheap and environment-friendly option to explore the county.

Where to Go Next from Gloucester County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gloucester County is a great base to explore other nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some places you can visit:

  • Philadelphia: The city of brotherly love is just a short drive away and offers a wide range of historical sites and museums.
  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: Experience the history and beauty of this park, which was home to the Continental Army during the Revolutionary War.
  • Atlantic City: The iconic beach town is home to several casinos, entertainment venues, and a boardwalk.

Conclusion

Gloucester County is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and culinary delights. Plan your visit well in advance, and you’re sure to have a memorable experience in this charming destination.
