Introduction to Cumberland County

Cumberland County is a hidden gem located in the Delaware River Region of New Jersey. Known for its rich history, beautiful scenery, and welcoming locals, it’s a perfect destination for travelers seeking a peaceful getaway. From charming museums to outdoor adventures, Cumberland County offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cumberland County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. WheatonArts and Cultural Center: This unique attraction showcases an impressive collection of American glass, ceramics, and other decorative arts.
  2. Mauricetown Historical Society Museum: Learn about the rich history of Cumberland County at this museum, which features exhibits on local industries, including glassmaking and shipbuilding.
  3. Bridgeton Hall of Fame All Sports Museum: Sports enthusiasts won’t want to miss this interactive museum, which celebrates the achievements of local athletes.

Natural Attractions of Cumberland County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Parvin State Park: Hike, fish, and picnic in this 1,800-acre park, which boasts beautiful lakes and woodlands.
  2. Cumberland County Birding and Wildlife Trail: Explore the county’s diverse wildlife at this scenic trail, which spans over 30 birding sites.
  3. Fortescue Beach: Enjoy a day at the beach at this hidden gem, which offers stunning views of the Delaware Bay and fantastic fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cumberland County

  1. Cape May Seashore Trail: This 18-mile trail winds through the marshes and woods of Cumberland County, offering beautiful views of the area’s diverse wildlife.
  2. Maurice River Bluffs Preserve Trail: Located on the banks of the Maurice River, this 1.5-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the river and surrounding wetlands.
  3. Union Lake Crossing Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride and offers scenic views of Union Lake.

Best Things to Do in Cumberland County: Activities and Tours

  1. Kayak and Canoe Tours: Explore the county’s waterways on a guided tour with Bayshore Center or Maurice River Kayak.
  2. WheatonArts Workshops: Join a glassblowing or ceramics workshop to learn a new skill and create your own masterpiece.
  3. Grapevine Tours: Visit Cumberland County’s wineries and taste the region’s best wines on a guided tour with Auburn Road Vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Cumberland County

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pork Roll: A New Jersey classic, pork roll is a breakfast meat that’s sliced and fried, often served on a breakfast sandwich.
  2. Clams: Cumberland County is known for its fresh seafood, especially its delicious clams.
  3. Soft-Shelled Crabs: If you’re a seafood lover, don’t miss out on trying soft-shelled crabs at one of the county’s many seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  1. Wine: Cumberland County is home to several wineries, including Auburn Road Vineyards and Bellview Winery, both of which offer delicious local wines.
  2. Beer: Sample local craft beers at Glasstown Brewing Company and Ludlam Island Brewery.

Where to Stay in Cumberland County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Inn on Holly Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in Pitman and offers cozy rooms and a delicious homemade breakfast.
  2. Hampton Inn and Suites Vineland: This modern hotel is located in Vineland and features comfortable rooms and an indoor pool.
  3. Indian Run Bed and Breakfast: This historic bed and breakfast is located in Millville and offers spacious rooms with a country feel.

What to Buy in Cumberland County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Glassware: Pick up a unique piece of glass art at WheatonArts’ Glass Studio or Millville’s Glasstown Arts District.
  2. Wine: Bring home a bottle of local wine from one of the county’s wineries.
  3. Clams: Take home a taste of Cumberland County’s famous seafood by purchasing fresh clams from one of the county’s many seafood markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Cumberland County

Cumberland County is known for its welcoming locals and laid-back atmosphere. Visitors should be respectful of the county’s historic sites and natural areas, and be sure to try a taste of the local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Cumberland County

The best time to visit Cumberland County is in the fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. Spring is also a great time to visit, with colorful flowers and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cumberland County

Do:

  1. Do explore the county’s natural areas, including its many parks and hiking trails.
  2. Do try the local seafood, especially the clams.
  3. Do visit the area’s wineries and craft breweries.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the county’s natural areas.
  2. Don’t ignore the county’s rich history and culture.
  3. Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations and tips.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cumberland County

Cumberland County is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should exercise caution when exploring natural areas and always be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Cumberland County

Travelers should bring any necessary medications and be aware of any allergies, as medical facilities may be limited in the county.

Getting to Cumberland County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cumberland County

  1. Philadelphia International Airport: Located about an hour and a half from Cumberland County, this airport offers domestic and international flights.
  2. Atlantic City International Airport: Located about an hour from Cumberland County, this smaller regional airport offers domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Cumberland County

  1. Greyhound/Cumberland Transit: Located in Bridgeton, this station offers bus service to various destinations.
  2. New Jersey Transit: This regional bus service stops in several towns in Cumberland County.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cumberland County, either by car or rental car.

How to Get Around in Cumberland County

Visitors can easily explore Cumberland County by car or bike. Several transportation services also offer guided tours of the area.

Where to Go Next from Cumberland County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cape May: Visit this seaside town to explore its charming Victorian architecture and beaches.
  2. Atlantic City: Head to this vibrant city to try your luck at the casinos or enjoy the famous boardwalk.
  3. Philadelphia: Only an hour and a half away, this historic city offers museums, nightlife, and great food.

Conclusion

Cumberland County offers visitors a unique combination of natural beauty, rich history, and welcoming locals. From exploring the area’s many parks and hiking trails to savoring its local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem of New Jersey. Plan your trip today and discover all that Cumberland County has to offer.
