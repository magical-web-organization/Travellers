Introduction to Medford

Located in Burlington County, in the Delaware River Region of New Jersey, Medford is a charming town that’s perfect for a weekend getaway or day trip. With a rich history dating back to the 17th century, Medford boasts a variety of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life, Medford has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Medford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kirby’s Mill: Built in 1778, this historic mill is the last remaining one of its kind in Medford. Visitors can take a tour of the mill to learn about its history and see the original machinery in action.

  • Medford Historical Society & Museum: This museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Medford’s history, from its early days as a Quaker settlement to present day.

  • Dr. James Still Historic Office Site and Education Center: Known as the “black doctor,” Dr. James Still was a renowned herbalist and medical practitioner in the mid-19th century. The historic site and education center offers tours and educational programs about Still’s life and work.

Natural Attractions of Medford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Freedom Park: This 200-acre park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking trails, fishing ponds, and a playground. The highlight of the park is its network of trails through serene wooded areas, with plenty of scenic spots to stop and rest along the way.

  • Rancocas State Park: Covering over 1,200 acres of land, this park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and bald eagles. Visitors can explore the park’s many trails on foot, by bike, or on horseback.

  • Strawbridge Lake: This picturesque lake is perfect for a peaceful stroll or a relaxing picnic. The surrounding area is lush with greenery, making it a great spot for bird watching and nature photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medford

  • Freedom Park Trail: This 5-mile trail winds through the woods and along the Rancocas Creek, offering a peaceful nature walk with plenty of scenic views.

  • Hart’s Run Trail: Another scenic trail in Freedom Park, Hart’s Run Trail is great for hiking, biking, or horseback riding. The trail follows the Rancocas Creek for much of its length, offering glimpses of the water and wildlife along the way.

  • Medford Village Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile trail winds through the heart of Medford, offering a glimpse of the town’s rich history with interpretive signs and exhibits.

Best Things to Do in Medford: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing/Kayaking on the Rancocas Creek: Rent a canoe or kayak for an afternoon of exploring the tranquil waters of the Rancocas Creek.

  • Wagonhouse Winery: Take a tour of this local winery and vineyard to learn about the winemaking process and sample their award-winning wines.

  • Medford Arts Center: This community arts center hosts a variety of events and exhibits throughout the year, including art shows, concerts, and theater performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for Medford

Day 1: Arrive in Medford and explore the town’s historic downtown area, including the Medford Historical Society & Museum and the Dr. James Still Historic Office Site and Education Center.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring Freedom Park, taking in the scenic views and hiking or biking along the park’s many trails.

Day 3: Take a canoe or kayak trip down the Rancocas Creek, enjoying the peaceful natural surroundings and abundant wildlife.

Day 4: Go on a tour of the local wineries, including Wagonhouse Winery and other nearby vineyards.

Day 5: Visit Kirby’s Mill and learn about the history of milling in Medford, followed by a relaxing picnic at nearby Strawbridge Lake.

Day 6: Explore Rancocas State Park and take in the stunning views of the park’s many trails and natural wonders.

Day 7: Depart Medford, taking with you memories of a relaxing and rejuvenating week in one of New Jersey’s most charming towns.

Food and Drink Scene of Medford

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taylor Pork Roll: This regional specialty is a type of pork roll that’s native to New Jersey. Try it on a sandwich for a classic taste of the Garden State.

  • Tomato Pie: A local favorite, tomato pie is a type of pizza that’s made with a slightly sweet tomato sauce and a crispy, thin crust.

  • Scrapple: Another regional staple, scrapple is a type of pork sausage made from the scraps of the pig.

Local Beverages

  • Wagonhouse Winery: Sample award-winning wines made from grapes grown right here in New Jersey.

  • Flying Fish Brewery: This popular local brewery offers tours and tastings of their signature beers, including the aptly-named “Exit” series that pays homage to the many highways in the state.

Where to Stay in Medford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Flying W Airport & Resort: This unique resort offers a variety of lodging options, including hotel rooms, RV camping, and tent camping. Located on a private airstrip, this resort is perfect for aviation enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

  • The Sanitas Cottage: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Medford’s historic district, within walking distance of many of the town’s top attractions.

  • The Hotel ML: Located just a short drive from Medford in nearby Mount Laurel, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations and a variety of amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center.

What to Buy in Medford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jersey Devil Hot Sauce: Made with locally-sourced ingredients, this hot sauce is a spicy tribute to New Jersey’s most famous folklore.

  • Medford Wooden Spoons: Handcrafted by a local artisan, these wooden spoons make for a unique and functional souvenir of your trip to Medford.

  • Taylor Pork Roll: Take home a taste of New Jersey with a package of this regional specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Medford

Medford’s culture is strongly influenced by its Quaker heritage, with many historic sites and traditions that reflect this religious community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including a strong emphasis on environmental conservation and sustainable living.

Best Time to Visit Medford

Medford is a great destination year-round, with mild weather in the spring and fall and warmer temperatures in the summer. Visitors looking to escape the crowds should consider visiting in the off-season, when the town is quieter and the natural surroundings are at their most peaceful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medford

Do:

  • Show respect for local customs and traditions, including a strong emphasis on environmental conservation and sustainable living.

  • Take advantage of the town’s many outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and canoeing/kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural habitats, including parks, trails, and waterways.

  • Disrespect historic sites or cultural institutions, including the Medford Historical Society & Museum and the Dr. James Still Historic Office Site and Education Center.

Safety and Health

Safety in Medford

Medford is generally a safe and welcoming destination, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. Visitors should take basic precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe, including locking their car doors and securing valuable items at all times.

Health in Medford

Medford has a variety of health services available, including several hospitals and medical clinics in nearby towns. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, including ticks and other common pests.

Getting to Medford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Medford

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): Located approximately 30 miles from Medford, this airport offers regular flights to destinations throughout the United States and internationally.

Bus Stations Near Medford

  • Greyhound: Provides bus service to nearby towns and cities, including Mount Laurel and Philadelphia.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at Philadelphia International Airport or other nearby rental locations to explore Medford and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Medford

Most attractions in Medford are within walking or biking distance from each other, making it easy to explore the town on foot. Visitors can also use a car or taxi service for longer distances, or take advantage of public transportation options like buses or trains.

Where to Go Next from Medford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Philadelphia, PA: Located just a short drive from Medford, Philadelphia is a vibrant and historic city that offers a variety of cultural attractions, great food, and shopping.

  • Atlantic City, NJ: Known for its famous boardwalk and casinos, Atlantic City is a popular destination for visitors looking for some excitement and entertainment.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Medford is the perfect destination for your next trip. With its rich history, natural attractions, and charming small-town ambiance, Medford is sure to capture your heart and leave you with lasting memories of your time in this beautiful part of New Jersey.
