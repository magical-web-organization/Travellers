Introduction to Burlington

Located in Burlington County, in Delaware River Region, in New Jersey, in Mid-Atlantic, in the United States of America, Burlington is a charming town with rich cultural heritage and scenic beauty. Founded in 1677, Burlington is one of New Jersey’s oldest cities and offers a unique blend of historical sites, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive tour guide for Burlington, we will take a closer look at the must-visit attractions in and around the town, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

Local Culture and Customs of Burlington

Burlington’s local culture and customs are unique, stemming from its colonial history and inclusive community. Some of the salient features of Burlington’s culture and customs include:

  1. Pride in its colonial heritage, with several historical sites and museums dedicated to preserving it.
  2. A thriving agricultural industry that provides fresh produce and a range of farm-to-table dining options.
  3. A love for outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, canoeing, and birdwatching.
  4. A welcoming community that celebrates inclusivity and diversity.

Best Time to Visit Burlington

The best time to visit Burlington is in the fall and spring seasons, between the months of September and November, and April and June. During these months, the weather is mild, and the foliage and flora are especially picturesque. However, Burlington’s attractions are open year-round, and the town holds events and festivals all year.
