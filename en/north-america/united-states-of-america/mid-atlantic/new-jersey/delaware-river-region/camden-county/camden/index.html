Introduction to Camden

Located in the Delaware River Region of New Jersey, Camden is a city rich in history and culture. Founded in 1626, Camden was once a major manufacturing center and transportation hub for the region. Today, the city is home to a thriving arts scene, world-class museums, and beautiful natural attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Camden.

List of Places to Visit in Camden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Battleship New Jersey Museum and Memorial: A true American icon, the Battleship New Jersey is a must-visit attraction for history enthusiasts. Climb aboard the ship and explore the decks and inner workings of this impressive vessel.
  • Camden Waterfront: The Camden Waterfront is home to several cultural attractions, including the Adventure Aquarium, the Camden Children’s Garden, and the Battleship New Jersey. Take a stroll along the waterfront and enjoy stunning views of the Delaware River.
  • Walt Whitman House: Visit the home of America’s great poet, Walt Whitman. The house has been beautifully restored to its 19th-century appearance and is now a museum dedicated to Whitman’s life and work.

Natural Attractions of Camden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cooper River Park: Located just minutes from downtown Camden, Cooper River Park is a beautiful oasis in the heart of the city. The park features miles of hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the Cooper River.
  • Rancocas Nature Center: Get up close and personal with nature at the Rancocas Nature Center. The center features miles of hiking trails, a pond, and a variety of wildlife.
  • Cramer Hill Nature Preserve: Take a break from the city and explore the natural beauty of the Cramer Hill Nature Preserve. The preserve features a network of hiking trails, a pond, and habitats for a variety of plant and animal species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Camden

Camden has a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Camden Greenway: This 3.5-mile trail connects downtown Camden to neighboring communities. The trail is perfect for biking, running, or walking.
  • Camden Waterfront Trail: This 1.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the Delaware River. The trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.
  • Wiggins Park Trail: This scenic trail follows the perimeter of Wiggins Park, offering beautiful views of the Camden Waterfront.

Best Things to Do in Camden: Activities and Tours

  • Adventure Aquarium: Home to over 8,500 aquatic species, the Adventure Aquarium is a must-visit attraction for families. Get up close and personal with sharks, penguins, and other marine life.
  • Treetop Quest: Experience an adrenaline rush at Treetop Quest, an aerial adventure park located in Camden. The park features zip lines, rope bridges, and other exciting obstacles.
  • Camden Night Gardens: This annual event takes place in September and features a stunning display of illuminated art installations. Take a stroll through the gardens and enjoy live music, food vendors, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Camden

Day 1: Explore the Camden Waterfront, and visit the Adventure Aquarium and Battleship New Jersey.

Day 2: Hike or bike the Camden Greenway, and visit Cooper River Park.

Day 3: Explore the city’s artistic side at the Camden Arts Yard and Rutgers-Camden Center for the Arts.

Day 4: Take a tour of the Walt Whitman House and other historic landmarks.

Day 5: Visit the Rancocas Nature Center, and enjoy a picnic lunch in the park.

Day 6: Take a break from the city and visit the Cramer Hill Nature Preserve.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of the city’s local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Camden

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hoagies: A Philly-style sandwich made with Italian meats and cheeses, lettuce, tomato, and onion.
  • Cheesesteak: Thinly sliced steak topped with melted cheese and served on a roll.
  • Water Ice: A sweet, frozen dessert similar to Italian ice.

Local Beverages

  • Yuengling Beer: A local favorite, Yuengling is the oldest operating brewery in America.
  • Lemonade: A popular drink in the summer months, Camden’s lemonade is made with fresh-squeezed lemons and a touch of sugar.

Where to Stay in Camden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Wyndham Philadelphia Historic District: Located just a short drive from Camden, the Wyndham Philadelphia Historic District is a great option for travelers looking for comfort and convenience.
  • The Benjamin Franklin Bridge Toll House: For a unique experience, stay at the Benjamin Franklin Bridge Toll House. The house has been beautifully restored and offers stunning views of the city skyline.

What to Buy in Camden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Philly Cheesesteaks: Bring home a taste of Philly with frozen Philly cheesesteaks that can be cooked at home.
  • Jersey Fresh Produce: Take home some of New Jersey’s freshest produce, including blueberries, tomatoes, and sweet corn.

Local Culture and Customs of Camden

Camden is known for its diverse communities, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Taking the time to learn about the city’s history and culture can help visitors better appreciate their experience in Camden.

Best Time to Visit Camden

The best time to visit Camden is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the city’s outdoor attractions are in full bloom. Summer is a popular time to visit, but temperatures can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Camden

Do:

  • Do explore the city’s cultural attractions, including its museums and historical sites.
  • Do try local cuisine, including the city’s famous hoagies and cheesesteaks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t walk alone at night in unlit or unfamiliar areas.
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car.

Safety and Health

Safety in Camden

Like any urban area, visitors to Camden should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to keep themselves safe. Travel in groups, avoid unlit or unfamiliar areas at night, and keep an eye on your valuables.

Health in Camden

Visitors to Camden should take the same health precautions they would in any other urban area. Drink plenty of water, protect yourself from the sun, and take any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Camden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Camden

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located just across the Delaware River in Philadelphia, the airport is a short drive or train ride from Camden.

Bus Stations Near Camden

  • Camden Bus Terminal: Located in downtown Camden, the terminal is a hub for regional bus services.

Other Options

  • RiverLink Ferry: Visitors can take the RiverLink Ferry across the Delaware River between Camden and Philadelphia.
  • PATCO: The PATCO High-Speed Line provides rail service between Camden and Philadelphia.

How to Get Around in Camden

Most of Camden’s attractions are located in the downtown area and are easily accessible on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take advantage of the city’s public transportation system or rideshare services.

Where to Go Next from Camden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Philadelphia, PA: Located just across the Delaware River, Philadelphia is a cultural hub with world-class museums, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • Atlantic City, NJ: A popular resort destination, Atlantic City offers visitors a variety of attractions, including casinos, beaches, and amusement parks.

Conclusion

With its rich history, thriving arts scene, and beautiful natural attractions, Camden is a must-visit destination for travelers of all ages. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this vibrant New Jersey city. Plan your trip today and experience all that Camden has to offer.
