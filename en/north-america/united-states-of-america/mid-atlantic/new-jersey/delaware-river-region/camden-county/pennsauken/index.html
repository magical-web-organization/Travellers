Introduction to Pennsauken

Pennsauken is a township located in Camden County, in the Delaware River Region of New Jersey. It is a town that has successfully balanced economic development and natural beauty. A visit to Pennsauken is characterized by an array of outdoor activities, stunning views, museums, and historic sites.

List of Places to Visit in Pennsauken

Pennsauken is home to a ton of culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pennsauken Historical Society Museum: The museum is a testament to Pennsauken’s rich history and is full of interactive exhibits. It’s a perfect place for history buffs.

  • Fort Mercer State Park: This is a Revolutionary War battlefield park located in Pennsauken. It features a recreated Revolutionary War-era fort.

  • National Constitution Center: Located in downtown Philadelphia, this center is a short drive from Pennsauken. It is an interactive museum dedicated to the US Constitution and American history.

Natural Attractions of Pennsauken: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pennsauken Creek Trail: A paved trail that offers opportunities for hiking and biking while exploring the picturesque banks of the creek.

  • Cooper River Park: This is a perfect location for those who love greenery and nature, as there are plenty of walking trails, playgrounds, picnic tables, and a lake for fishing.

  • Rancocas State Park: A perfect spot for nature lovers, Rancocas State Park has hiking trails, kayaking, fishing, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pennsauken

Pennsauken is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. There are plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the most popular are:

  • Pennsauken Creek Trail: This is one of the most popular routes for hiking and biking in the town. It is a 4.4-mile paved trail that winds through the town along the banks of the creek, offering stunning views and a chance to spot local wildlife.

  • Cooper River Park: Cooper River Park offers over three miles of paved paths for both biking and walking.

  • Rancocas State Park: Rancocas State Park has over 7 miles of hiking and biking trails and offers scenic views of nature.

Best Things to Do in Pennsauken: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Camden Aquarium: A perfect place for people of all ages, the aquarium has more than 8,500 aquatic animals in different exhibits.

  • Take a Tour of the Battleship New Jersey: The warship was commissioned during World War II and served until the early 90s. Today, it’s a floating museum that offers guided tours.

  • Go on a Food Tour: Known for a diverse food culture, Pennsauken has many options for trying different dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pennsauken

Day 1: Explore Pennsauken Historical Society Museum, followed by a walk along the Pennsauken Creek Trail.

Day 2: Visit Battleship New Jersey and continue exploring waterfront attractions.

Day 3: Experience the natural landscape of the town with a visit to Rancocas State Park.

Day 4: Do a food tour of the town and sample some of the unique local dishes.

Day 5: Visit the National Constitution Center in downtown Philadelphia.

Day 6: Go on a hike or bike ride in Cooper River Park.

Day 7: Spend a day exploring the Camden Aquarium.

Food and Drink Scene of Pennsauken

Pennsauken is home to an eclectic mix of restaurants and bars, offering a wide range of cuisines and styles of dining.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperoni Pizza at Tony’s Baltimore Grill: The classic pizza recipe is a staple at this popular spot.

  • Pulled Pork at the Little Beef’s Cheesesteak Company: This is a must-try dish when visiting Pennsauken.

  • Ice-Cream at Rita’s Italian Ice Cream: The homemade Italian ice and custard are the perfect way to cool off after exploring Pennsauken.

Local Beverages

Pennsauken has a burgeoning craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering a range of styles.

Where to Stay in Pennsauken: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Westin Mount Laurel: A luxurious hotel with spacious rooms and superb amenities.

  • Hampton Inn Pennsauken: A reasonably priced option, the Hampton Inn has well-appointed rooms and friendly staff.

  • Red Roof Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly option, with clean rooms and lots of amenities.

What to Buy in Pennsauken: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The produce market in the town sells fresh, locally-grown produce, which is a perfect souvenir to take home.

  • The town also has many local artists and artisans, and their work can often be found in galleries, boutiques, and shops throughout the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Pennsauken

Pennsauken is known for celebrating its cultural heritage through music and art.

Best Time to Visit Pennsauken

The best time to visit Pennsauken is during the summer, when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed to the fullest. Spring and fall are also good times to visit due to the mild weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pennsauken

Do:

  • Do visit the local museums and historical sites.

  • Do take a bike ride or hike along one of the many trails.

  • Do try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural sites.

  • Don’t engage in any type of anti-social behavior.

  • Don’t visit any restricted areas without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pennsauken

Pennsauken is generally a safe place, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially when exploring certain areas of the town.

Health in Pennsauken

Visitors should be in good health and should consult a doctor before visiting if they have any medical conditions. The town has many pharmacies and clinics for minor medical issues. If there is an emergency, dial 911.

Getting to Pennsauken: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pennsauken is easily accessible by car and public transportation.

Airports Near Pennsauken

  • Philadelphia International Airport: A major airport located around 30 minutes from Pennsauken.

Bus Stations Near Pennsauken

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal in Philadelphia

Other Options

  • NJ Transit Train Station in Pennsauken

  • Uber, Lyft, and taxis are always available.

How to Get Around in Pennsauken

The town is easily navigable on foot, and exploring by bike is a popular option. Rental cars are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Pennsauken: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Philadelphia: A short drive away, it has a plethora of attractions to explore.

  • Atlantic City: A coastal destination that is known for its casinos and nightlife.

  • Cape May: A charming seaside town known for its richness in history and culture.

Conclusion

Pennsauken is a perfect destination for those who love history, nature, and culture. With spectacular museums, historical sites, and natural beauty, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers in the Mid-Atlantic region.
