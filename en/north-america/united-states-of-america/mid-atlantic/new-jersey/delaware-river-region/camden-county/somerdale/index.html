Introduction to Somerdale

Nestled in Camden County, in the Delaware River Region of New Jersey, Somerdale is a serene suburban town that offers a host of outdoor and cultural experiences. From scenic parks and hiking trails to historic landmarks, there’s something to delight every kind of traveler in Somerdale.

List of Places to Visit in Somerdale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gabreil Daveis Tavern Museum: Get a glimpse of the 18th-century tavern life at this museum that features vintage furnishings and exhibits about the region’s early history.
  • Clementon Park and Splash World: A fun-filled amusement park that is a popular destination for families and thrill-seekers alike. Enjoy a range of rides, water attractions, and entertainment options.
  • Paulsdale: A national historic landmark and the birthplace of Alice Paul, a renowned women’s rights activist.

Natural Attractions of Somerdale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Timber Creek Park: A vast nature preserve that boasts scenic hiking trails, fishing ponds, picnic areas, and a multitude of family-friendly activities.
  • Cooper River Park: An idyllic parkland that features waterfront trails, scenic picnic areas, and athletic fields.
  • Kiddlestix Park: The perfect spot for nature enthusiasts, Kiddlestix Park offers hiking, fishing, and bird watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somerdale

For hikers and bikers alike, Somerdale offers some of the region’s best trails. From leisurely paths to challenging treks, there’s a lot to explore. Some of the must-try routes include:

  • Timber Creek Trail: A 6-mile trail that winds through the Timber Creek Park, taking visitors through some of the most scenic spots.
  • Cooper River Trail: This 3.7 mile trail is a great option for a leisurely walk, jog, or bike ride, as it follows the Cooper River for the majority of the route.
  • Heed the Harding Trail: Named after Joseph P. Harding, the first mayor of Somerdale, this 3.5 mile trail cuts through the heart of the town, and offers a great way to explore some of the historic landmarks of the borough.

Best Things to Do in Somerdale: Activities and Tours

  • Battleship New Jersey Museum and Memorial: A historic warship that has been transformed into an interactive museum, showcasing the ship’s wartime history and life at sea. Visitors can explore a range of exhibits related to the ship’s military campaigns, and learn about the experiences of the crew members.
  • Escape Room South Jersey: A thrilling experience that requires visitors to solve puzzles and riddles in order to escape the room within the allocated time. A great option for groups of friends or families looking for a fun challenge.
  • Wildlife Adventures at Kiddlesix Park: This park offers a variety of nature-based activities including hiking, fishing, and birdwatching. Visitors can also attend educational workshops on various environmental topics.

Week-Long Itinerary for Somerdale

Day 1: Start at Gabriel Daveis Tavern Museum and move on to Timber Creek Park for a hike. Day 2: Visit the Battleship New Jersey Museum and Memorial followed by Cooper River Park for a picnic by the waterfront. Day 3: Visit Heed the Harding Trail and stop by Kiddlesix Park for some wildlife adventures. Day 4: Head to Clementon Park and Splash World for a day of fun-filled amusement park rides. Day 5: Take a tour of Paulsdale, followed by Escape Room South Jersey for a thrill. Day 6: Enjoy a leisurely walk or bike ride on the Cooper River Trail and stop by some of the parks scenic areas for a picnic. Day 7: Spend the day exploring the quaint local businesses and boutiques in Somerdale’s downtown area.

Food and Drink Scene of Somerdale

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Roll: A classic breakfast dish that is a New Jersey staple, comprising pan-fried slices of pork roll served with eggs and cheese on a roll.
  • Salt Water Taffy: A popular local candy that comes in a range of flavors including grape, cherry, and raspberry. It’s perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.
  • Taylor Ham Sandwich: Another local delicacy, this sandwich is made with a slice of Taylor Ham, egg, and cheese on a kaiser roll.

Local Beverages

  • Italian Ice: A refreshing summer beverage that comes in a range of flavors, including strawberry, lemon, and watermelon.
  • Craft Beer: The area also boasts a growing craft beer scene with breweries like Zeds offering visitors the chance to sample a range of locally-brewed beers.

Where to Stay in Somerdale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Philadelphia-Cherry Hill: Offering modern and comfortable rooms, this hotel is within walking distance of the attractions on the famous Cherry Hill’s restaurant row.
  • Garden State Inn:This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and spacious rooms that come equipped with a range of amenities including a TV and a microwave.
  • Wingate by Wyndham Voorhees: Located just a few miles away from Somerdale, this hotel is ideal for families or groups, offering a range of guest suites that can accommodate more than four people.

What to Buy in Somerdale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jersey Devil Memorabilia: An eerie and unique gift option, visitors can purchase a range of Jersey Devil merchandise including t-shirts, mugs, and keychains.
  • Local Art: Visitors can find a range of locally made art that showcases the region’s history and scenic landscapes. Choices from greeting cards, paintings, sculptures, etc., will reflect the time and passion these local artists have for their community.

Local Culture and Customs of Somerdale

Like most American towns, Somerdale has its own unique way of life, which is shaped by the various cultural influences of its residents. The town is particularly famous for its Italian-American roots, a fact that is reflected in the area’s cuisine and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Somerdale.

The best time to visit Somerdale is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months can be hot and humid, making outdoor activities less enjoyable, while the winter months can be cold and snowy. Visitors to Somerdale during late September would enjoy the Gloucester County 4-H Fall Festival, a great family event with lots of activities and exhibits.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somerdale

Do:

  • Take a hike.
  • Try the local delicacies like pork roll and saltwater taffy to get the complete Somerdale experience.
  • Attend local events and festivals to get a taste of the town’s cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Littering parks and natural reserves is prohibited. Refrain from throwing trash in public places.
  • Don’t take anything from historical sites or cultural landmarks. Always respect the town’s heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Somerdale

Somerdale, like any other American town, takes its citizen’s safety and well-being very seriously. Visitors can rest assured that the town is safe to explore, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, when exploring the town’s outdoor areas, visitors should ensure that they have appropriate clothing and footwear for different weather conditions and terrain.

Health in Somerdale

Somerdale is a healthy and clean suburban town that offers a range of public healthcare facilities. Visitors who require medical attention can visit local hospitals like Virtua Voorhees in nearby Voorhees.

Getting to Somerdale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Somerdale is located within minutes of several major transportation hubs, making it accessible by car, bus, or train.

Airports Near Somerdale

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located 18 miles from Somerdale, this airport is well-connected to major cities across the US and globally. The airport offers several transportation options for visitors to get to Somerdale.

Bus Stations Near Somerdale

  • NJ Transit Williamstown: This is the nearest bus station to Somerdale and is located 4.4 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Somerdale by car, with Route 30 (White Horse Pike) running directly through the town. Additionally, visitors can take the PATCO Speedline to the Ashland Station, which is just minutes away from Somerdale.

How to Get Around in Somerdale

Visitors can get around Somerdale easily by car, bike, or foot depending on their preferences. Rental cars and bikes are available from several outlets in the town.

Where to Go Next from Somerdale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Philadelphia: A bustling metropolis that offers a range of cultural attractions, historic landmarks, and entertainment options.
  • Atlantic City: Famous for its glitzy casinos, Atlantic City is also home to a range of attractions including the Boardwalk Hall, and Absecon Lighthouse.

Conclusion

Somerdale is a charming suburban town that has something to offer every kind of traveler. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast or a history buff, you’ll find plenty of activities and attractions to keep you entertained in Somerdale. With its friendly locals, variety of quality restaurants and comfortable accommodations, Somerdale is sure to make for a memorable vacation.
