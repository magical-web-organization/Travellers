Introduction to Cherry Hill

Cherry Hill is a charming township located in Camden County, New Jersey. It is situated in the Delaware River Region of the Mid-Atlantic region and labeled as the fastest-growing town in the region. The town is known for its serene neighborhoods, outdoor recreational options, and cultural and historical attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Cherry Hill

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Barclay Farmstead: This 1816 farmhouse has been restored and is open to the public. It is a significant landmark and showcases early American architecture.
  • Garden State Discovery Museum: A museum exclusively designed for children, it features hands-on exhibits that aim at fostering creativity, education, and imagination for young ones.
  • Historic Croft Farm: The Historic Croft farm is a historic site that is ideal for community events, school field trips, and family picnics. Take a stroll around the park, explore farm life, and learn about the history of Cherry Hill here.

Natural Attractions of Cherry Hill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cooper River Park: This Park along the Cooper River offers a tranquil setting for family picnics, dog walking, running and biking trails, scenic nature walks, and more.
  • Cherry Hill Trail System: The Cherry Hill Trail System includes over 10 miles of hiking, biking, and jogging trails through the picturesque terrain of the township.
  • Cropp Family Nature Trail: The Cropp Family Nature Trail is a half-mile natural trail suitable for all ages that lead its way through gardens, meadows, and woods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cherry Hill

Cherry Hill Trail System has an extensive network of over ten miles of scenic trails, including bike trails and walking paths that wind through the lush green woodlands, meadows, and parks in the township.

Best Things to Do in Cherry Hill: Activities and Tours

  • Escape Room Challenge: An escape room challenge is a fun-filled group activity where participants are locked inside a themed puzzle room and must solve clues to escape within the time frame set.
  • Adventure Aquarium: The Adventure Aquarium is one-of-a-kind experience where visitors can see the underwater wildlife, meet the sharks or get an up-close encounter with the penguins.
  • Merchantville Bike and Tours: An exclusive tour operator in the region that provides guided bike tours, segways, and walking tours showcasing nearby towns.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cherry Hill

Food and Drink Scene of Cherry Hill

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheesesteaks: A popular delicacy in the region that features grilled steak, cheese, and veggies, tucked in an Amoroso roll.
  • Soft Pretzels: A twisty soft pretzel, the perfect snack for munching, is sold at almost every store in Cherry Hill.
  • Tomato pie: A pizza variety originating from the neighboring Trenton area, this delicious dish has become a staple for all pizza lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Iced Tea: Freshly brewed iced tea is the top drink of choice in Cherry Hill, a refreshing beverage accompanies almost all meals.
  • Coca-Cola: Cherry Hill also home to the Coca-Cola production factory in the state. Visitors can take a behind-the-scenes tour and learn about the history and production of this iconic brand.

Where to Stay in Cherry Hill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cherry Hill has a wide range of lodging options ranging from high-end hotels to comfortable guesthouses and Airbnb. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Crowne Plaza Philadelphia/Cherry Hill: An upscale hotel with luxurious amenities, located a short distance away from the major attractions.
  • Holiday Inn Philadelphia-Cherry Hill: A budget hotel ideal for families and leisure travelers, situated in a prime location close to many attractions.
  • Residence Inn by Marriott: Perfect for extended-stay travelers, this hotel offers spacious rooms with self-catering facilities, fitness centers, and other amenities.

What to Buy in Cherry Hill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese: Famous for its specialty Cheese, visitors can get a taste of local farmer’s cheese, homemade cheese, and other artisanal varieties.
  • Indoor Plants: With over 25 greenhouses in the vicinity, Cherry Hill is one of the significant players in the indoor plant market. Visitors can find some exotic and unique indoor plants at these greenhouses.
  • Local Honey: Local beekeepers produce some of the finest quality honey in the region, and visitors can purchase this organic honey straight from the source.

Local Culture and Customs of Cherry Hill

Locals in Cherry Hill are known for their warm hospitality and friendly demeanors. They are proud of their history and celebrate their local festivals and events with much gusto. Visiting the farmers’ markets, enjoying the scenic views in the parks, and perusing the local boutiques - the residents of Cherry Hill value these simple pleasures and love sharing them with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Cherry Hill

The ideal time to visit Cherry Hill is from May to October. The weather during these months is pleasant, ideal for exploring the natural trails of the township, and attending the community events. The fall season experiences a rush of tourism as the trees change colors, which is an unforgettable sight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cherry Hill

Do:

  • Explore the natural trails and parks during your trip to the township.
  • Attend the Cherry Hill Restaurant Week and participate in food tours while in the city.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter around the parks and natural trails.
  • Do not smoke in the parks and recreational areas, as it is illegal.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cherry Hill

Cherry Hill is a safe and secure township, with low crime rates and well-maintained public spaces. Visitors are advised to take necessary safety precautions like safeguarding personal belongings and locking their cars and bicycles.

Health in Cherry Hill

Cherry Hill is also home to several quality health and medical facilities, and visitors can access these facilities for any emergency medical help.

Getting to Cherry Hill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cherry Hill:

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): The nearest airport is the Philadelphia International Airport, located less than 30 minutes drive from Cherry Hill.

Bus Stations Near Cherry Hill:

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Greyhound Bus Station is located in Philadelphia, which connects Cherry Hill to several cities and towns across the USA.

Other Options:

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car straight from the Philadelphia International Airport or any other rental service near Cherry Hill.
  • Uber/Lyft: Ride-sharing services are widely available in Cherry Hill, making it easy to navigate around town.

How to Get Around in Cherry Hill

Getting around Cherry Hill is easy and convenient with several options, including:

  • Buses: NJ Transit buses and inter-city bus services are available.

  • Car rental: Renting a car is an excellent option if you prefer to explore places on your own.

  • Taxis: Radio taxis and metered cabs are available in the township.

Where to Go Next from Cherry Hill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Philadelphia: Located just 20 minutes away from Cherry Hill, Philadelphia is a great place to visit for history buffs and sports fans.
  • Atlantic City: A world-famous beach resort only an hour’s drive from Cherry Hill is perfect for a day trip for those who desire the beachside experience.
  • New York City: One of the most enchanting cities globally, New York City is easily accessible by car or bus from Cherry Hill and is only a couple of hours away.

Conclusion

Visitors to Cherry Hill, New Jersey, get to experience many cultural, historical, and natural attractions. The township is known for its serene neighborhoods, outdoor recreational options, and cultural and historical attractions. With so many things to do, including biking and hiking trails, shopping destinations, museum visits, and outdoor recreational activities, Cherry Hill is a great destination for a family vacation. Be sure to visit soon!
