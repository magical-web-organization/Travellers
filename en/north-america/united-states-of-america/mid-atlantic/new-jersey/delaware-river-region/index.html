Introduction to Delaware River Region

The Delaware River Region lies in the heart of New Jersey and offers a wide range of experiences, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures. Not only is the region home to the Delaware River, but it is also dotted with charming towns that are rich in history, making it a popular destination among local and international travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Delaware River Region

If you’re planning a trip to Delaware River Region, here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Delaware River Region is a paradise for history buffs. The region boasts a number of historical sites and museums, including:

  • Battleship New Jersey: This impressive Battleship Museum is a floating museum docked on the Camden waterfront. It’s a great place to visit with the whole family to learn about naval history.

  • Washington Crossing Historic Park: A must-visit for history lovers. The park recreates the famous Delaware River crossing made by General George Washington and his troops during the American Revolution.

  • Old Barracks Museum: Take a journey back in time and explore the life of 18th-century soldiers. The barracks are a visual representation of the Revolutionary War and what life was like at that time.

  • Trenton War Memorial: A historic venue in Trenton that has played host to various musical and theatrical performances since its opening in 1933. Be sure to check the schedule to see if anything is happening during your visit.

Natural Attractions of Delaware River Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Delaware River Region is an oasis of natural beauty, with plenty of activities to keep you busy. Here are a few natural attractions and outdoor activities to consider:

  • Valley Forge National Historical Park: Located in King of Prussia, this park is an excellent spot for a picnic or a scenic hike.

  • Wharton State Forest: This is the largest state forest in New Jersey, offering over 125,000 acres of unspoiled wilderness to explore. It is known for its natural beauty, wooded trails, and diverse wildlife.

  • The Pine Barrens: Located in southern New Jersey, this area is a UNESCO World Heritage site, home to some of the most beautiful forests, rivers, bogs, and wildlife.

  • Adventure Aquarium: One of the best places to explore Delaware River’s marine life. The aquarium is home to different species of fish, sharks, and rays. You can also enjoy interactive experiences and special events for children and families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delaware River Region

Delaware River Region is an incredible destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking trails and bike routes to keep you engaged. Some of the popular hiking and biking trails in the region include:

  • Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park Trail: This 68.5-mile trail is a popular destination for both hikers and bikers. You will pass through charming towns while enjoying scenic views of the Delaware River and beyond.

  • Washington Crossing State Park Nature Trail: This five-mile loop takes you through scenic woodlands to the historic Johnson’s Ferry House and back. It’s a perfect spot for a picnic or to soak in the peaceful surroundings.

  • Mercer County Park Trail System: This trail system features over 20 miles of pathways, perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. It meanders through the State Park, with scenic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Delaware River Region: Activities and Tours

Delaware River Region is a hub of activities and tours that are sure to keep visitors engaged and entertained. Below are some of the best things to do during your trip to Delaware River Region:

  • Take a boat ride on the Delaware River: Nothing beats being on the water and enjoying the beauty of the Delaware River. You can take a guided tour or rent your boat.

  • Visit Grounds for Sculpture: This 42-acre sculpture garden is home to over 100 contemporary sculptures from renowned artists. You can also enjoy live music, art exhibitions, and workshops throughout the year.

  • Go wine-tasting at Old York Cellars: Take a break from the outdoor activities and indulge in a wine-tasting experience at Old York Cellars. You can taste some of the most exceptional wines from the region along with scenic views of the vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Delaware River Region

Food, drink, and travel go hand in hand, and the Delaware River Region does not disappoint food enthusiasts. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try during your trip:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hoagies or Subs: A classic East Coast sandwich that is famous in the region. Packed with fillings, these stuffed sandwiches are a perfect grab-and-go meal.

  • Taylor Ham or Pork Roll: New Jersey’s iconic breakfast meat. It’s a must-try for anyone who visits the region.

  • Tomato Pie: A unique pizza variation that is popular in the region. It features a crispy crust topped with fresh mozzarella and plum tomato sauce, creating an irresistible taste.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The craft beer scene in Delaware River Region is booming, with plenty of breweries worth mentioning, such as River Horse Brewing Company, Flying Fish Brewing Company, and Triumph Brewing Company.

  • Apple Cider: A seasonal beverage that is available in abundance during the fall months. You can also find different varieties of apple ciders and local apple orchards.

Where to Stay in Delaware River Region: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delaware River Region offers an array of accommodation options for different budgets and preferences. You can choose between the traditional Bed & Breakfast options or modern hotels. Below are some recommended places to stay during your visit to Delaware River Region:

  • Lambertville House: A charming boutique hotel located in the heart of Lambertville. It features elegant rooms and suites with a cozy ambiance.

  • Hyatt Place Mt. Laurel: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor swimming pool.

  • The Woolverton Inn: A historic bed & breakfast with beautiful gardens and spacious rooms, perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Delaware River Region: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Delaware River Region

Delaware River Region is a melting pot of cultures, and the locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Some customs to note when visiting the region include:

  • Tipping: Tipping in the United States is standard and expected, with 15-20% being the usual practice.

  • Small talk: The locals love small talk, so be prepared to engage in conversations with waiters, bartenders, and even strangers.

  • Casual dress code: Delaware River Region has a relaxed dress code, so you can leave your business suits at home.

Best Time to Visit Delaware River Region

Delaware River Region is perfect for visiting all year round, with something to enjoy in every season. However, the best time to visit is from May through September, where you can enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and boating, and festivals, such as the New Jersey Renaissance Faire and Princeton Reunions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delaware River Region

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Delaware River Region offers plenty of opportunities to explore nature and the great outdoors.

  • Try the local cuisine: The food scene in Delaware River Region is a must-try, and you’ll find some unique items that you can’t find anywhere else.

  • Check out the festivals: The region has a variety of festivals throughout the year, such as the New Jersey State Fair and The Mercer County Italian American Festival.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Delaware River Region is full of natural beauty, and it’s essential to keep it clean and preserved for others to enjoy.

  • Over-Tip: A tip of 15-20% is customary in the United States, and it shouldn’t be exceeded unless exceptional service is provided.

  • Leave valuables in your car: To avoid any potential theft, don’t leave anything valuable in your car and always lock your vehicle.

Safety and Health

Safety in Delaware River Region

Delaware River Region is generally safe for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling to new places. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay alert at night: As with any major city, there is a higher chance of crime at night. Stay in well-lit areas and avoid isolated places.

  • Watch out for traffic: Delaware River Region’s streets can be busy, so always pay attention when crossing the road.

  • Secure your belongings: Always keep your valuables close to you and never leave them unattended.

Health in Delaware River Region

Delaware River Region has a good healthcare system, and medical services are easily accessible. It’s always important to have travel health insurance in case of any emergencies. Bring any necessary medications with you and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Delaware River Region: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options that can take you to Delaware River Region, including:

Airports Near Delaware River Region

  • Philadelphia International Airport: The largest airport serving the Delaware River Region, located 20 minutes from Camden.

  • Trenton-Mercer Airport: Camden is just a 25-minute drive from Trenton-Mercer Airport, which serves some domestic routes.

Bus Stations Near Delaware River Region

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal, Philadelphia: The Greyhound Terminal in Philadelphia is the main transit hub in the area, with services to other major cities in the U.S.

  • NJ Transit Bus Service: NJ Transit offers bus services from New York City, Trenton, and other major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Services: Train services from New York City, Philadelphia, Trenton, and other major cities are available throughout the day.

  • Car Rentals: Car rental services are available at various locations in the region, providing an alternative way to explore the Delaware River Region.

How to Get Around in Delaware River Region

While driving is the most convenient way to explore Delaware River Region, there are several transportation options to get around:

  • New Jersey Transit System: Provides bus and rail services throughout the state.

  • Taxi Services: Taxis are readily available in the region and are an excellent option for those who want to travel from point to point quickly.

  • Bike Rentals: Bike rentals are a popular way to explore the region and are available at various locations.

Where to Go Next from Delaware River Region: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
