Welcome to Montague, a beautiful town located in Sussex County, Skylands, New Jersey, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Montague offers breathtaking natural scenery, rich history, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the cultural and natural attractions of Montague, the best hiking and biking routes, the must-try dishes, the best places to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Montague

Montague is a peaceful and charming town surrounded by rolling hills, forests, and serene lakes. It is known for its historic landmarks and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure seekers alike. The town is home to several parks, hiking and biking trails, and breathtaking waterfalls. Additionally, Montague offers a glimpse into the rich history of the region through its well-preserved landmarks and museums.

List of Places to Visit in Montague

There are many places to explore in Montague, from cultural attractions to natural wonders. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Foster-Armstrong House: A historic home built in the 18th century and currently listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Montague Museums: The town is home to various museums such as The Montague Association for Restoration of Community History (M.A.R.C.H.), The Montague Grange, and the Montague Glass Museum, which showcases a collection of glassware from the 19th and 20th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Montague: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • High Point State Park: Home to the highest point in New Jersey and offering beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area: A scenic park that offers opportunities for hiking, camping, and water activities.
  • Buttermilk Falls: A stunning waterfall located in the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area and offers a picturesque picnic area to relax and unwind.
  • Stokes State Forest: A wilderness park with miles of hiking trails, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montague

Montague is a heaven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails that vary in difficulty. Here are some of the best routes to discover:

  • Appalachian Trail: This iconic trail that runs through the town of Montague traverses various terrains and offers stunning views of the region.
  • Lakeside Trail: A pleasant walk that circles Lake Marcia and provides spectacular views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Pine Grove Trail: A 1.5-mile trail located in Stokes State Forest that takes you through a beautiful forest and past a serene pond.

Best Things to Do in Montague: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities to do in Montague that cater to all interests and age groups. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Go Fishing: The town has abundant fishing spots where you can cast a line to catch trout, bass, and other fish species.
  • Take a Boat Tour: Discover the beauty of the area’s lakes and rivers through boat tours offered by local companies, such as Kittatinny Canoes and Delaware River Trips.
  • Go Camping: Experience the great outdoors of Montague by pitching a tent at one of the many campsites in the area, such as High Point State Park and Stokes State Forest.
  • Attend Festivals: Experience the vibrant culture of Montague by attending one of the many festivals, such as the Annual Harvest Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montague

If you have a week to spend in Montague, here is a sample itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Montague

Montague offers an array of dining options that cater to various tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to savor:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Roll, Egg, and Cheese Sandwich: A New Jersey favorite that features pork roll, eggs, and cheese, served on a Kaiser roll.
  • Blueberry Pancakes: A pancake that features the state’s famous blueberries.
  • Tomato Pie: A pizza-like dish that features tomato sauce and cheese on top of a crust.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Cream: A rich, creamy milkshake made with vanilla ice cream and raspberry syrup.
  • Apple Cider: A refreshing beverage made with fresh apples from local orchards.

  • Hotel Fauchere: A luxurious hotel located in nearby Milford that offers elegant rooms, superb dining, and spa services.
  • Camping: Pitch a tent at one of the many campsites in the area, such as High Point State Park and Stokes State Forest.

What to Buy in Montague: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montague is home to several unique stores and boutiques that offer various locally made products and souvenirs. Here are some must-buy items:

Local Culture and Customs of Montague

Montague has a rich history and unique culture that reflects the customs and traditions of the region. The town is known for its love for nature, and locals take pride in preserving the natural beauty of the area. Additionally, Montague celebrates its history and culture through various festivals and events held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Montague

Montague is a year-round destination with different attractions to offer in each season. The best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Spring and fall are ideal for hiking and road trips when the temperatures are pleasant, and the foliage is at its peak. Summer is perfect for outdoor activities, including water sports and camping. Winter offers snow sports and cozy indoor activities like wine tasting.

Getting to Montague: Transportation Options and Logistics

Montague is easily accessible via various modes of transportation. Here’s how to get to Montague:

Bus Stations Near Montague

  • Trailways Bus Station: Milford, PA (8 miles from Montague)
  • Greyhound Bus Station: Stroudsburg, PA (23 miles from Montague)

Other Options

How to Get Around in Montague
