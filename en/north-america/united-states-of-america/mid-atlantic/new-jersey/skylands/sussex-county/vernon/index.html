Introduction to Vernon

Vernon, located in Skylands, Sussex County, New Jersey, is a charming town blessed with natural beauty and rich culture. Known for its awe-inspiring vistas, along with unique shopping and dining experiences, this small town is an ideal escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. This travel guide to Vernon aims to provide you with all the information you need to make the most of your stay in this picturesque town.

List of Places to Visit in Vernon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Theosophical Society in America, an international organization that promotes theosophy, has its headquarters in Vernon. The society’s facilities include a library, bookstore, Theosophical museum, and archives.

The Historical Society of Vernon showcases the town’s rich history and culture. The society has an extensive collection of artifacts and documents that reflect the lifestyle of residents in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Vernon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vernon is a popular destination among outdoor enthusiasts, boasting several hiking and biking trails and hidden delights such as lakes and waterfalls. Wawayanda State Park is one of the most visited parks in the region, located in nearby Sussex. The park is a prime bird-watching site, and there are also opportunities for swimming, fishing, and kayaking.

When planning your trip to Vernon, don’t miss the breathtaking views from the peak of the Hamburg Mountain Natural Area. Also, be sure to check out the Glenwood Mountain Trail for an easy and scenic hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vernon

The Appalachian Trail is one of the most popular hiking trails in Vernon. The trail spans over 72 miles and takes hikers through the beautiful Kittatinny Mountains. The Stairway to Heaven trail is another popular hiking route that takes you through the lush forests of Vernon.

Biking enthusiasts will love exploring the trails of the Wallkill River National Wildlife Refuge, which offer picturesque views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Vernon: Activities and Tours

A scenic horseback ride through the rolling meadows of Kittatinny Valley State Park is a must-do for visitors to Vernon. If you’re looking for adventure, you can try your hand at ziplining, enjoy high-speed tubing on the slopes of Mountain Creek Waterpark, or experience a vertical drop at Action Park, one of the world’s highest and steepest water slides.

Food and Drink Scene of Vernon

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to dining out in Vernon, the town doesn’t disappoint. Try the smoked brisket at Smokey’s Brick Oven Tavern or indulge in some classic Italian food at Milano’s Pizzeria.

For breakfast, be sure to try Vernon’s famous pancakes at the historic Vernon Diner, or grab a quick bite at the Stokes Grill & Bar. For dessert, head over to Heaven Hill Farm for a wide selection of pies, cider donuts, and ice cream.

Local Beverages

Craft beer enthusiasts will love the offerings at High Point Brewing Company, located in Butler. This brewery is known for its handcrafted, small-batch beers, made with locally sourced ingredients.

For wine lovers, Ventimiglia Vineyard is the perfect destination. Situated on a hilltop, the vineyard offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

Where to Stay in Vernon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crystal Springs Resort is a luxury resort in Vernon that offers a range of amenities, including a spa, golf course, and several dining options. If you’re on a budget, the Appalachian Hotel is a great choice. This hotel offers comfortable accommodations at an affordable price. Another option is the Minerals Resort & Spa, which features multiple outdoor and indoor pools along with a spa.

What to Buy in Vernon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home some of Vernon’s rich history by stopping at the Vernon Historical Society Gift Shop. Here, you’ll find everything from books on the town’s history to t-shirts and mugs. Head over to Heaven Hill Farm for local honey, homemade jams, and seasonal produce.

Local Culture and Customs of Vernon

Vernon locals take pride in their town’s rich history and natural beauty. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Vernon

The best time to visit Vernon is during the fall season, from September to November. This is when the town’s vibrant foliage is at its peak, and the weather is mild. Summer is also an excellent time to visit, with several outdoor festivals and events taking place throughout the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vernon

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and culture
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic vistas
  • Visit the local museums and historical attractions

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions
  • Disrespect the town’s values and customs
  • Ignore safety guidelines while exploring outdoor routes

Safety and Health

Safety in Vernon

Like any small town, Vernon has its fair share of safety measures. When exploring the outdoors, be aware of local wildlife and take the necessary precautions. Visitors should also carry a map or GPS while hiking or biking.

Health in Vernon

Visitors exploring the outdoors or participating in adventure sports should wear proper equipment to prevent injury. Health services are available in Vernon and the surrounding areas.

Getting to Vernon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Vernon by air, train, or bus. The nearest airports are Newark Liberty International, John F. Kennedy, and LaGuardia. From here, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Vernon. Vernon is also accessible by train and bus services, with NJ Transit providing routine services from several nearby stations.

How to Get Around in Vernon

The most convenient way to get around Vernon is by car or bicycle. Visitors can also take advantage of the local taxi service or hike to explore the town’s natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Vernon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vernon is conveniently located near several exciting destinations, including New York City, which is just an hour and a half’s drive from Vernon. Visitors can also explore the scenic countryside and wineries of neighboring towns like Warwick and Goshen.

Conclusion

Vernon is a hidden gem of Sussex County, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and rich culture. With so much to see and do, this small town is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation.
