Introduction to Newton

Nestled in the heart of Sussex County, in Skylands, New Jersey, Newton is a charming town with a rich history. Founded in 1751, the town encompasses a blend of modern and historical sites that attract visitors from across the country. From exhilarating outdoor adventures to world-class museums and historic landmarks, there are endless opportunities to explore and discover in Newton.

List of Places to Visit in Newton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Newton Theatre: Housed in a restored Art Deco building, the Newton Theatre hosts a variety of concerts, plays, movies, and other performing arts events.
  • The Newton Fire Museum: Located in the oldest firehouse in the town, this museum displays a stunning collection of firefighting equipment and memorabilia, including an antique hand-drawn fire engine.
  • Sussex County Historical Society Museum: The museum showcases an impressive collection of artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits that highlight the rich cultural heritage of Sussex County.

Natural Attractions of Newton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Swartswood State Park: The park offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts, including hiking trails, fishing spots, picnic areas, and a sandy beach along the Swartswood Lake.
  • Kittatinny Valley State Park: This park is ideal for scenic drives, hiking, fishing, and birdwatching. The Plains Trail is a must-do, offering a picturesque view of the Kittatinny Mountains.
  • The Appalachian Trail: Hikers can tackle a portion of one of the longest hiking trails in the world while taking in stunning views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newton

  • The Sussex Branch Trail: A scenic 20-mile trail that follows an old rail line with excellent hiking and biking opportunities.
  • The Paulinskill Valley Trail: This 27-mile trail includes three different sections that provide great views of the Paulinskill River and Valley.
  • The Sterling Forest Trails: Located a short drive from Newton, these trails offer a range of difficulties for hikers and bikers with stunning views of the surrounding forest.

Best Things to Do in Newton: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Delaware River: Head to the Delaware River for a day of thrilling rafting on the water.
  • Skylands Stadium: Catch a minor league baseball game or concert at this stunning 4,200-seat venue.
  • Ghost Tours: Join a spooky nighttime tour and hear the tales of haunted buildings and ghost sightings in the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newton

Day Activity
Day 1 Take a stroll through the town’s downtown and visit The Newton Theatre for a show or movie.
Day 2 Explore the Sussex County Historical Society Museum and learn about the town’s rich history.
Day 3 Head to Swartswood State Park for a day of swimming, fishing, and hiking.
Day 4 Go on a ghost tour and hear the spooky tales of the town’s haunted buildings and ghost sightings.
Day 5 Take a scenic drive through Kittatinny Valley State Park and enjoy the views of the Kittatinny Mountains.
Day 6 Go on a rafting adventure on the Delaware River.
Day 7 Hike a portion of the Appalachian Trail and take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Newton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Newton Bakery: Famous for their pepperoni bread, this bakery serves up tasty treats like pastries, donuts, and cakes.
  • Hayek’s Market: A long-standing market known for its delicious sandwiches and homemade soups and salads.
  • The Barrel House: This gastropub offers a rotating list of craft beers and serves up delicious comfort food with a modern twist.

Local Beverages

  • Angry Erik Brewing: This microbrewery serves up an array of delicious craft beers with unique flavor profiles.
  • Czig Meister Brewing: A local brewery that offers a wide range of beer styles with seasonal flavors ranging from light to dark and hoppy to malty.

Where to Stay in Newton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Wooden Duck Bed & Breakfast: An elegant B&B set on a 10-acre woodland property with beautifully appointed rooms and an excellent breakfast.
  • Quality Inn: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and continental breakfast. Within a short drive of local attractions.
  • The Carriage House: This charming guesthouse features tastefully furnished rooms with classic decor and a lovely outdoor area.

What to Buy in Newton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Peters Valley School of Craft: Purchase beautiful handmade crafts created by skilled artisans from the country.
  • Sussex County Sunflower Maze: Take home beautiful sunflowers and other locally grown products from this family-fun farm.
  • Farm Markets and Craft Stands: Explore various roadside farm markets and craft stands found along the town’s scenic drives.

Local Culture and Customs of Newton

Newton prides itself on its rich community spirit and small-town charm. While here, explore the town’s history, attend a community event, or support local farmers and small businesses.

Best Time to Visit Newton

The best time to visit Newton is in the fall when the foliage is at its peak. During this season, temperatures are cool, and the town is alive with various fall festivals and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newton

Do:

  • Do attend a show at the Newton Theatre.
  • Do hike a portion of the Appalachian Trail.
  • Do attend one of the town’s cultural events or festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the town’s beautiful natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip your tour guide or service staff appropriately.
  • Don’t miss out on any of the town’s unique local eateries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newton

Newton is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as securing valuables and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Newton

The town has adequate medical facilities and pharmacies, so it’s easy to access healthcare in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Newton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Newton

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located roughly an hour’s drive away, the airport provides direct flights to various destinations worldwide.
  • Stewart International Airport: Located about 45 minutes’ drive away, the airport provides direct connections to several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Newton

  • Newton Bus Station: NJ Transit provides bus services from the station to other local and regional destinations.
  • Sussex County Shuttle: Provides scheduled shuttle services to various parts of the town and county.

Other Options

  • Driving: The town is easily accessed via major roads, including the I-80 and the US-206.
  • Train: The closest train station is in Hackettstown, about 15 miles away from Newton.

How to Get Around in Newton

Visitors can get around Newton via rental cars, taxis, bikes, and Uber. Still, it’s essential to note that the town’s downtown area is easily walkable, making it a perfect fit for exploring on foot.

Where to Go Next from Newton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Morristown: About a 40-minute drive away, the town is known for its historic architecture and museums.
  • Lafayette: This historic village is located a short drive north of Newton and is home to several vintage shops, wineries, and art galleries.
  • Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area: Located about an hour’s drive away, the area offers endless outdoor adventures, including hiking, boating, and swimming.

Conclusion

With its unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures, Newton offers visitors endless opportunities to explore and discover its beauty and charm. Whether you’re interested in hiking scenic trails or exploring historic landmarks, make sure to add this vibrant town to your must-visit destination list.
