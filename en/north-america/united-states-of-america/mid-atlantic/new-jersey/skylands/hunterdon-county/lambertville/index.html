Introduction to Lambertville

Nestled in the heart of Hunterdon County, Lambertville is a quaint, charming town steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known for its picturesque architecture, vibrant arts scene, and scenic river views, Lambertville is the perfect destination for visitors looking to experience small-town living at its best. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or art lover, Lambertville has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lambertville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lambertville Historical Society Museum - Located in the heart of Lambertville, this museum is dedicated to preserving the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • The New Hope and Ivyland Railroad - Take a ride on the historic New Hope and Ivyland Railroad, which offers scenic tours of the surrounding countryside and historic towns.
  • The James A. Michener Art Museum - Just a short drive from Lambertville, this museum features a comprehensive collection of American art, including works by renowned artists such as Charles Sheeler and Walter Baum.

Natural Attractions of Lambertville - Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delaware & Raritan Canal State Park - This sprawling park is a popular destination for hikers, cyclists, and nature enthusiasts. Explore the miles of scenic trails that pass through lush forests, rolling hills, and sparkling waterways.
  • Bowman’s Hill Wildflower Preserve - This 100-acre nature reserve is home to over 700 species of plants and animals, making it a must-visit for nature lovers and hikers.
  • Washington Crossing State Park - Located just a short drive from Lambertville, this historic park offers visitors the chance to explore the site where George Washington famously crossed the Delaware River during the American Revolution.

Best Things to Do in Lambertville - Activities and Tours

  • Antique Hunting - Lambertville is known for its bustling antique shops, which offer a treasure trove of vintage furniture, artwork, and collectibles.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking - The Delaware River is an ideal spot for water sports enthusiasts, with numerous rental companies offering canoe and kayak rentals.
  • Walking Tours - Take a leisurely stroll through the town’s historic districts, which are home to numerous elegant Victorian homes and charming shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Lambertville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Thermidor - A signature dish at Lambertville Station, this classic French dish features succulent lobster in a rich, creamy sauce served over rice.
  • Seasonal Oysters - A delicacy at The Boat House Restaurant, these fresh, locally sourced oysters are served with a variety of dipping sauces.
  • Pork Roll Sandwich - A New Jersey classic, this savory sandwich features crispy pork roll, eggs, and cheese served on a Kaiser roll.

Local Beverages

  • River Horse Brewery - This award-winning brewery offers a variety of beers, including the popular River Horse IPA and Triple Horse Belgian Style Ale.
  • Unionville Vineyards - One of the region’s premier wineries, Unionville produces a variety of fine wines using grapes grown in their own vineyards.

Where to Stay in Lambertville - Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lambertville House - This historic boutique hotel is located in the heart of town and features elegant rooms, a full-service restaurant, and an outdoor terrace.
  • The Ghost Light Inn - This charming bed and breakfast offers guests cozy accommodations, a gourmet breakfast, and access to a lush garden.
  • Chimney Hill Estate Inn - Set on a 10-acre estate surrounded by rolling hills and lush gardens, this inn features spacious rooms and a scenic outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Lambertville - Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antiques - Lambertville’s numerous antique shops offer a treasure trove of unique and vintage items, including furniture, artwork, and collectibles.
  • Farm-to-Table Products - Lambertville is home to numerous farms and markets that offer fresh organic produce, handcrafted cheeses, and other artisanal foods.
  • Pottery and Ceramics - Many of the region’s top potters and ceramicists showcase their wares at the town’s various art galleries and studios.

Local Culture and Customs of Lambertville

Lambertville is a welcoming and friendly town that values community, history, and culture. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s rich artistic heritage, sample the local cuisine, and immerse themselves in the beauty of the surrounding landscape.

Best Time to Visit Lambertville

The best time to visit Lambertville is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most vibrant. Visitors can enjoy scenic hikes and bike rides, explore the town’s charming shops and art galleries, and savor the flavors of the local cuisine.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lambertville

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s historic districts and elegant Victorian homes.
  • Do visit the Lambertville Historical Society Museum to learn about the town’s rich history and culture.
  • Do sample the local cuisine, including fresh seafood, farm-to-table produce, and artisanal cheeses.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave town without taking a scenic stroll along the banks of the Delaware River.
  • Don’t miss the chance to explore the natural beauty of nearby parks and nature preserves.
  • Don’t forget to bring your camera - Lambertville is a photographer’s dream destination!

Safety and Health

Safety in Lambertville

Lambertville is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. Visitors are advised to use common sense precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially when exploring the town’s historic districts.

Health in Lambertville

Lambertville has several medical clinics and pharmacies, as well as several hospitals located within a short drive from town. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication with them and to consult with their doctor or healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Lambertville - Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lambertville

  • Philadelphia International Airport - Located about an hour’s drive from Lambertville, this major airport is served by numerous domestic and international carriers.
  • Newark Liberty International Airport - Located about 1.5 hours’ drive from Lambertville, this busy airport offers flights to destinations around the country and the world.

Bus Stations Near Lambertville

  • Trans-Bridge Lines - This bus company offers daily service from New York City to Lambertville, with multiple stops along the way.
  • Greyhound Bus - Greyhound operates bus service to nearby cities such as Trenton, Philadelphia, and New York.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals - Several major car rental companies offer services in the area, including Enterprise, Hertz, and Budget.
  • Private Transportation - Several private transportation companies offer taxi and shuttle service to and from the airport and other destinations.

How to Get Around in Lambertville

The best way to get around Lambertville is on foot or by bike. The town is small and easily navigable, with numerous scenic trails and paths for hikers and cyclists. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Lambertville - Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Hope, PA - Just across the river from Lambertville, this charming town is home to numerous art galleries, shops, and restaurants, as well as beautiful parks and scenic river views.
  • Princeton, NJ - Located about 30 minutes’ drive from Lambertville, this historic university town is home to numerous cultural attractions, shopping destinations, and beautiful parks and gardens.
  • Doylestown, PA - About a 45-minute drive from Lambertville, this quaint town is known for its vibrant arts scene, historic landmarks, and award-winning restaurants.

Conclusion

Lambertville is a charming and vibrant town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From scenic hikes and bike rides to world-class museums and galleries, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this charming corner of New Jersey. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or inspiration, Lambertville is the perfect destination for your next trip.
