Introduction to Stockton

Located in Hunterdon County, within the Skylands region of New Jersey, Stockton is a small town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its charming streets and stunning architecture, Stockton offers a unique experience for visitors seeking to explore the area’s rich heritage and enjoy an array of outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Stockton

  • Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park
  • Prallsville Mills
  • Stockton Market
  • Stockton Borough Park
  • Centre Bridge-Stockton Bridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stockton is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the town’s fascinating past, including:

  • Holcombe-Jimison Farmstead Museum: Visitors can tour this beautifully restored 18th-century farmstead and witness the daily life of early farmers in the region.
  • Delaware River Mill Society: This historical site is a must-visit for history enthusiasts and showcases the industrial and agricultural significance of the milling industry from the 18th century onwards.
  • Prallsville Mills: The Prallsville Mills is a complex of historic industrial buildings which served the local community as a grist mill, feed mill, and more.

Natural Attractions of Stockton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers and adventure seekers can enjoy a range of activities in Stockton, including:

  • Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park: This 70-mile-long historic park is a haven for nature enthusiasts, offering hiking, biking, bird watching, and fishing opportunities.
  • Stockton Borough Park: This park is perfect for a relaxed day out with family and friends. It’s also a great spot for a picnic with its open fields and playgrounds.
  • Centre Bridge-Stockton Bridge: This scenic bridge is a perfect spot for taking stunning photos of the town and the Delaware River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stockton

Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park has several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views while incorporating the town’s rich heritage. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • The Delaware Canal Towpath: This 60-mile-long trail is ideal for hiking and biking, leading visitors along the beautiful Delaware Canal Towpath through the heart of the town, offering scenic views of the river and nearby villages.
  • Lock 12 Loop Trail: This scenic 2.5-mile-long trail is great for a hike, taking visitors to a historic lock and a picturesque pond.
  • Bull’s Island Recreation Area: This park features several trails to explore, including the popular River Trail, which affords visitors a chance to catch a glimpse of the river’s wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Stockton: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Stockton:

  • Stockton Market: This indoor market showcases the region’s finest produce and food products, including handmade chocolates, fresh vegetables, artisanal cheeses, and much more.
  • Delaware River Tubing: Beat the summer heat by tubing down the scenic Delaware River, taking in stunning views of the natural surroundings along the way.
  • River Country Cycle: Rent a bike and take a leisurely ride through the picturesque countryside surrounding Stockton and the Delaware River.

Stockton boasts a thriving food and drink scene, showcasing local culinary talent and the finest regional ingredients. Here are some highlights:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Glazed Pork Chops at the Stockton Inn

Local Beverages

Where to Stay in Stockton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in the vicinity of Stockton to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Woolverton Inn: This charming bed and breakfast offers luxurious guest rooms, gourmet breakfast, and stunning countryside views.
  • Lambertville Station Inn: Located in Lambertville, just a few miles from Stockton, this historic inn offers comfortable rooms and fine dining.
  • Sand Castle Bed & Breakfast: This unique guesthouse in nearby Barnegat Light is perfect for those wanting a beach vacation in conjunction with their visit to Stockton.

Local Culture and Customs of Stockton

Stockton is a small town with an inviting community spirit. Its culture is heavily influenced by the Town’s rich industrial heritage and its close association with the Delaware River, which facilitates trade and commerce.

Best Time to Visit Stockton

The best time to visit Stockton is in the summer months from June to September when the weather is warm and pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also an excellent time to visit if you want to take in the stunning colors of the foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stockton

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the community
  • Ask for permission before taking pictures of people
  • Take care not to litter and help keep Stockton clean and beautiful

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines when enjoying adventure activities, such as tubing and biking
  • Trespass on private property

Safety and Health

Safety in Stockton

Stockton is generally a safe place for visitors, but it’s always important to remain vigilant, especially during outdoor adventures. It’s advisable to avoid poorly lit areas at night, avoid carrying large sums of money, and lock your car when parking.

Health in Stockton

Stockton and the surrounding area are not known to have any unusual health risks. However, as with any travel experience, it’s essential to stay aware and adhere to standard health and safety procedures. If you have any specific concerns, consult with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Stockton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stockton

The nearest airports to Stockton are Trenton-Mercer Airport in Ewing, NJ, and Lehigh Valley International Airport in Allentown, PA, both of which are within a 45-minute drive.

Bus Stations Near Stockton

The Trans-Bridge Lines provide direct bus service from New York City, Allentown, and Doylestown, with connections from other locations across the region.

Other Options

Stockton can also be reached by car or bike, with several major highways including I-295, I-95, Route 202 and Route 32 running nearby.

How to Get Around in Stockton

Visitors can get around Stockton and its surroundings via car, bike, or on foot, with most attractions within easy driving or biking distance. Taxi and rideshare options are also available.

Where to Go Next from Stockton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stockton is situated in an area of stunning natural beauty, with several nearby highlights to explore, including:

  • Historic New Hope, PA
  • The nearby city of Trenton, NJ
  • The scenic Hunterdon County countryside

Conclusion

Stockton is a hidden gem, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to learn about the region’s rich heritage, enjoy outdoor adventures, or simply relax and soak up the atmosphere, Stockton has something for everyone.
