Introduction to Clinton

Welcome to Clinton, a small town located in Hunterdon County, New Jersey. This picturesque town offers a serene and peaceful retreat for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Clinton is known for its charming historic district, beautiful parks, and scenic views of the Raritan River. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Clinton has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Clinton

Clinton boasts a rich history and a variety of attractions that cater to all interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in Clinton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Red Mill Museum Village – This historic museum showcases the history of agriculture and industry in Hunterdon County. The site features a working mill, a blacksmith shop, a wagon collection, and more.
  • Hunterdon Art Museum – This museum celebrates contemporary art and craft in all forms. The museum hosts temporary exhibits, classes, workshops, and other events throughout the year.
  • The Clinton Historical Museum – This museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Clinton’s past. The museum is located in a historic home that was once owned by a prominent Clinton family.

Natural Attractions of Clinton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spruce Run Recreation Area – This expansive park offers a variety of activities, including swimming, hiking, fishing, and boating. The park is also home to a scenic reservoir that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Red Mill Pond – Located in the heart of Clinton, this tranquil pond offers fishing and boating opportunities. Visitors can also enjoy a peaceful stroll along its banks.
  • The Columbia Trail – This former railroad bed has been converted into a multi-use trail that offers hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail runs through lush forests, past fields, and over streams, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clinton

Clinton is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking opportunities in and around the town. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Columbia Trail – As mentioned above, this scenic trail is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding. The trail is relatively flat and easy, making it suitable for all ages and skill levels.
  • The Highlands Trail – This trail traverses through the Highlands Region of New Jersey, offering hikers and bikers a chance to explore rugged terrain and stunning vistas. A portion of the trail runs through Clinton, making it easily accessible for visitors.
  • The Musconetcong River Greenway – This trail follows the Musconetcong River, offering hikers and bikers stunning views of the river and its surrounding landscape. The trail is relatively flat and easy, making it suitable for all levels of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Clinton: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing weekend away or an action-packed adventure, Clinton has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Clinton:

  • Take a Canal Boat Ride – Explore Clinton’s history and scenic beauty on a leisurely boat ride down the Raritan Canal. The ride offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is suitable for visitors of all ages.
  • Attend an Event at the Hunterdon Art Museum – The museum hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including art exhibits, concerts, and workshops. Check the museum’s website for upcoming events.
  • Visit the Red Mill Museum Village – This historic site offers a glimpse into Clinton’s past, showcasing the area’s rich agricultural and industrial history. Visitors can explore the mill, forge, blacksmith shop, and other historic buildings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clinton

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Clinton, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Clinton Historical Museum and learn about the town’s past.
2 Go for a hike on the Columbia Trail and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
3 Take a canal boat ride and explore the historic Raritan Canal.

5 Attend an event or workshop at the Hunterdon Art Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Clinton

Clinton has a vibrant food and drink scene, offering visitors a chance to taste local specialties and enjoy drinks at cozy bars. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies – These Polish dumplings are a local favorite in Clinton. Try them at the Clinton House Restaurant, a historic establishment that dates back to the 1800s.
  • Jersey Sourland Pie – This savory pie is made with Jersey sweet potatoes, mushrooms, and ham. Try it at the Clean Plate Kitchen, which serves farm-to-table cuisine.
  • Tomato Pie – This classic New Jersey dish is made with fresh tomatoes, cheese, and herbs. Try it at the Garden State Salad Company, a local favorite for healthy eats.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer – Clinton is home to several craft breweries, including Muller’s Tavern, which has been serving beer since 1898, and Conclave Brewing, which offers a range of unique brews.
  • Wine – Clinton is part of the Hunterdon County Wine Trail, which includes several wineries that offer tastings and tours. Try the award-winning wines at Beneduce Vineyards or Old York Cellars.

Where to Stay in Clinton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clinton has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • The Riverside Victorian Bed and Breakfast – This historic B&B offers charming accommodations and is located near the heart of Clinton.
  • Courtyard by Marriott Lebanon – This modern hotel is located just a few miles from downtown Clinton and offers comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • Camping – For those who prefer the great outdoors, Spruce Run Recreation Area offers campsites and RV sites.

What to Buy in Clinton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home, Clinton has plenty of options. Here are some of the best products to buy in Clinton:

  • Handcrafted Gifts – The Hunterdon Art Museum’s gift shop offers a selection of handcrafted gifts made by local artisans.
  • Antiques – Clinton’s historic district is home to several antique shops that offer a range of vintage and antique items.
  • Local Produce – Clinton is surrounded by lush farmland, and visitors can buy fresh produce and other local goods at the Clinton Farmers’ Market, open from May to October.

Local Culture and Customs of Clinton

Clinton is a small town with a unique culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the Town’s Historic Heritage – Clinton is proud of its rich history, and visitors should be respectful of the town’s historic buildings and sites.
  • Be Mindful of Noise Levels – Clinton is a quiet town, and visitors should avoid excessive noise, especially after dark.
  • Say Hello to Locals – Clinton residents are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should take the time to greet locals and strike up conversations.

Best Time to Visit Clinton

The best time to visit Clinton is during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the weather is cool and crisp. The town is also popular during the summer months, when visitors can enjoy swimming, boating, and other outdoor activities at nearby parks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clinton

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Clinton:

Do:

  • Explore the Historic District – Clinton’s historic district offers a wealth of architectural and cultural treasures, including historic homes, museums, and antique shops.
  • Enjoy the Outdoors – Clinton is surrounded by beautiful parks and scenic trails, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Try Local Foods and Beverages – From pierogies to craft beer, Clinton offers a variety of local specialties that visitors should try.

Don’t:

  • Be Disrespectful of Historic Sites – Clinton takes pride in its history, and visitors should treat historic sites and landmarks with respect.
  • Litter or Leave Trash Behind – Clinton’s natural beauty should be preserved, and visitors should dispose of trash properly.
  • Make Excessive Noise After Dark – Clinton is a quiet town, and visitors should be considerate of their neighbors and avoid making excessive noise at night.

Safety and Health

Clinton is a safe and healthy town, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in Clinton

  • Be Aware of Traffic – Clinton is a small town that can be easily navigated on foot, but visitors should still be aware of vehicle traffic.
  • Stay Hydrated – During the summer months, visitors should drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Health in Clinton

  • Watch for Ticks – Like most wooded areas, Clinton is home to ticks that can transmit Lyme disease. Visitors should wear protective clothing and use insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Clinton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clinton is easily accessible by car, plane, or public transportation.

Airports Near Clinton

  • Newark Liberty International Airport – Located 46 miles east of Clinton, this airport offers domestic and international flights.
  • Lehigh Valley International Airport – Located 28 miles west of Clinton, this airport offers domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Clinton

  • Greyhound Bus Lines – This bus line stops in nearby Flemington, approximately 7 miles from Clinton.

Other Options

  • Car Rental – Clinton is easily accessible by car, and several car rental companies operate in nearby towns.
  • Train – The nearest train station is in High Bridge, approximately 8 miles from Clinton.

How to Get Around in Clinton

Clinton is a small town that is easily navigated on foot or by bike. Visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car from one of the nearby rental companies.

Where to Go Next from Clinton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clinton is located in the heart of the Skylands region of New Jersey, and visitors can easily explore nearby towns and attractions.

  • Lambertville – This charming town is located just a few miles from Clinton and offers a variety of shops, restaurants, and galleries.
  • Delaware River Valley – This scenic region offers stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside, as well as opportunities for hiking, biking, and water sports.
  • Princeton – This historic town is home to Princeton University, as well as a variety of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Clinton, New Jersey, offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and small-town charm. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s rich history, enjoy the great outdoors, or indulge in local cuisine, Clinton has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this delightful town has to offer.
