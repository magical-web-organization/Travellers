Introduction to Dover

Welcome to Dover, a charming small town located in Morris County in Skylands, New Jersey. Known for its rich historical heritage, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions, Dover is a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer stay. Here you will find a mix of pre-Revolutionary War history, an artistic community, and modern-day fun. In this guide, we’ve put together a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, where to eat, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Dover

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Dover Historical Society: An organization dedicated to preserving the history of Dover. It has a museum and three historic sites to visit: the Allamuchy Freight House, the Box Car Exhibit, and the Morris Canal Lock 2 East.
  • The Revolutionary War Powder Mill: A historical site featuring the remains of the Revolutionary War Powder Mill and a nearby observation tower that offers a panoramic view of the area.
  • Ford Mansion and Museum: The home of Jacob Ford Jr., a prominent figure in the Revolutionary War, as well as a museum featuring exhibits on the history of Morris County and the Revolutionary War.

Natural Attractions of Dover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alstede Farms: A family-owned and operated farm that offers seasonal fruit-picking activities, hayrides, and a petting zoo for kids.
  • Hacklebarney State Park: A nature preserve with over 1,000 acres of forests, fields, and streams, perfect for hiking, fishing, and bird watching.
  • Lake Hopatcong: New Jersey’s largest freshwater lake, surrounded by parks and small towns offering a variety of recreational activities such as fishing, boating, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dover

  • The Sussex Branch Trail: A three-mile trail that follows the old Bedminster Branch Railroad and offers beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Tourne County Park: A popular hiking destination in the area with eight miles of trails, a picnic area, and a viewpoint of the New York skyline.
  • The Paulinskill Valley Trail: A 27-mile trail running through Sussex and Warren Counties that passes through forests, wetlands, and picturesque farmlands.

Best Things to Do in Dover: Activities and Tours

  • Delaware River Tubing: A great way to explore the Delaware River with a four-hour tubing experience including a free BBQ meal at “The Famous River Hot Dog Man.”
  • Skylands Stadium: A family-friendly destination with a variety of activities, including baseball games, car shows, and the annual Christmas Light Show.
  • Scavenger Hunts: Explore downtown Dover with engaging and interactive scavenger hunts that teach history and fun facts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dover

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Ford Mansion and Museum in the morning and enjoy lunch at the Dover Town Diner. In the afternoon, explore the Allamuchy Freight House and end the day with dinner at Blackwell’s Bar & Grill.
Day 2 Take a hike on the Sussex Branch Trail in the morning, have a picnic at the Tourne County Park, and explore the Morris Canal Lock 2 East in the afternoon. End the day with dinner at La Strada Ristorante.
Day 3 Go Fruit Picking at Alstede Farms, have lunch at The Corner Bistro and Bake Shop and, in the afternoon, visit the Revolutionary War Powder Mill. In the evening have dinner at Dover Grill.
Day 4 Get off the beaten path with a scavenger hunt in Dover, then enjoy lunch at Mr. Chili, after in the afternoon enjoy a scenic drive through the verdant hills to Lake Hopatcong. At the lake end the day with dinner at Alice’s Restaurant.
Day 5 Explore Hacklebarney State Park, have a picnic lunch at the park and after in the afternoon visit the Box Car Exhibit. End the day with dinner at J&K Steakhouse.
Day 6 Visit Skylands Stadium for a morning baseball game and lunch at the stadium. After, in the afternoon, enjoy Delaware River Tubing, and for dinner, visit Portofino’s Ristorante.
Day 7 Visit the Dover Historical Society for a morning tour, have lunch at The Quiet Man Pub and Restaurant, & in the afternoon visit the Paulinskill Valley Trail. End the day with dinner at Nikko Japanese Restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Dover

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cream of Crab Soup: A hearty and flavorful soup made with fresh crab meat and cream.
  • Jersey Tomato Salad: A light and refreshing salad made with fresh Jersey tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, and balsamic vinaigrette.
  • Pork Roll, Egg & Cheese Sandwich: A classic New Jersey breakfast sandwich with a fried egg, pork roll, and cheese, often served on a hard roll.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Wine Trail: A collection of wineries and vineyards throughout New Jersey, with many in the Skylands region, offering a variety of locally sourced wine.
  • Craft Beers: Several local breweries in the area offer excellent craft beers, including Czig Meister Brewing Company, Angry Erik Brewing, and Manskirt Brewing.

Where to Stay in Dover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Whistling Swan Inn: A beautifully restored Victorian inn offering luxurious rooms, a full breakfast, and afternoon tea.
  • Holiday Inn Express: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a continental breakfast.
  • The Mount Tabor Country House: A charming guest house with cozy rooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Dover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dover Honey: Honey from the local beehives is available in several stores in the area.
  • Sussex County Maple Syrup: Perfect gift of locally produced maple syrup
  • Paintings, photographs, and crafts: Many local artists offer their artwork, ranging from watercolor paintings, photographs, to jewelry, and pottery, at The Artists’ Co-op.

Local Culture and Customs of Dover

Dover is a friendly and welcoming town, known for its rich history, outdoor activities, and artistic community. The locals are proud of their town and are happy to share its history and culture with visitors. Respect for the environment and the community is a prevalent cultural custom in Dover.

Best Time to Visit Dover

The best time to visit Dover is in the fall when the leaves are changing color, and the weather is pleasant. Also, during the spring, when the wildflowers are in bloom and outside activities thrive. Summer is busy because of the holiday season and the many festivals and events happening in the area. Winter is cold and less active, except for the annual Christmas Light Show held at Skylands Stadium.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dover

Do:

  • Respect the environment and the community.
  • Try the local delicacies and visit the artisanal shops.
  • Explore the historical sites and natural attractions.
  • Participate in the many festivals and events that Dover and the surrounding areas have to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter and pollute the environment
  • Disrespect the town’s history or culture
  • Ignore traffic regulations and pedestrian safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dover

Dover is considered a safe town, but visitors should take precautions such as locking cars and using common sense when walking in secluded areas at night.

Health in Dover

Visitors should take normal health precautions, such as keeping a bottle of water with them, applying sunscreen, and being aware of ticks in open areas.

Getting to Dover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dover

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: 33 miles away
  • Allentown-Bethlehem-Easton Airport: 61 miles away
  • LaGuardia Airport: 68 miles away

Bus Stations Near Dover

  • Rockaway Townsquare Mall: 3 miles away
  • Denville Station: 4 miles away
  • Mount Arlington Station: 6 miles away

Other Options

  • Drive: Dover is easily accessible by car, with major highways such as I-80 and US Route 46 running through it.
  • Train: The nearest train stations to Dover are the Dover Station and the Mount Tabor Station, offering services to major cities in the area.

How to Get Around in Dover

The best way to get around Dover is by car, but biking and walking are also an option. Several car rental companies operate in the area, and organized tours are also available.

Where to Go Next from Dover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Morristown: A historical town just fifteen minutes away from Dover, full of museums and historical sites, including the Ford Mansion and Museum.
  • Lake Hopatcong: The largest freshwater lake in New Jersey, only a thirty-minute drive away from Dover.
  • The Delaware Water Gap: A picturesque area just over an hour’s drive away, offering outdoor activities such as hiking, kayaking, and fishing.

Conclusion

Dover is a charming small town in Skylands, New Jersey, with a lot to offer visitors. From its rich history to its natural beauty, and its culinary delights, Dover is the perfect destination for a weekend escape or a longer vacation. With this guide, we hope you have all the information you need to plan your trip to Dover and make the most of it.
