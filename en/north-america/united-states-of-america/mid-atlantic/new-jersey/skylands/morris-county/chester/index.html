Introduction to Chester

Chester is a quaint and charming township located in Morris County, New Jersey, in the heart of the Skylands region. Known for its picturesque landscapes, vibrant farmland, and wide array of cultural attractions, the township is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the best of what New Jersey has to offer. Whether you’re looking to explore historical landmarks, meander through hiking trails, or simply relax and unwind amidst stunning natural vistas, Chester is sure to impress.

List of Places to Visit in Chester

Chester is home to numerous attractions, each offering a unique glimpse into the township’s rich history and natural beauty. Here are some must-see destinations to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cooper Gristmill: A restored 1826 water-powered mill that offers hands-on exhibits, grinding demonstrations, and various seasonal events.

  • The Black River Railroad Historic Trust: A railway museum that features vintage trains, a restored 1901 station, and fun train rides to nearby destinations.

  • The Historic Chester Courthouse: A stately colonial structure that dates back to 1791 and features rich historical exhibits about the township’s past.

Natural Attractions of Chester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hacklebarney State Park: A sprawling 978-acre park that boasts multiple hiking trails, fishing ponds, and stunning scenery.

  • The Willowwood Arboretum: A 130-acre expanse of gardens, woodlands, and meadows featuring beautiful sculptures, a sensory garden, and educational programming.

  • Schooley’s Mountain County Park: A nature preserve spanning 577-acres, offering hiking, fishing, ice-skating, cross-country skiing, and panoramic views of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chester

Chester is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking/biking trails winding through its charming countryside. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Patriots Path: A scenic 35-mile trail that passes through Chester’s historic downtown area and features stunning views of the Black River.

  • The Hacklebarney Loop Trail: A 3.8-mile loop that takes you through the heart of Hacklebarney State Park, offering breathtaking scenery and a challenge for experienced hikers.

  • The Morris Canal Greenway: A 7.8-mile trail that follows the historic Morris Canal and offers stunning views of Chester’s quaint farmlands and rural architecture.

Best Things to Do in Chester: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking trails, there are numerous other activities and tours to enjoy in Chester, such as:

  • Wine tastings at local wineries: Chester is home to several vineyards that offer tastings, tours, and private events.

  • Farmer’s market: The Chester Sunday Farmers Market offers a wide variety of locally sourced produce, meats, and baked goods.

  • Shopping: Visit some of the unique boutiques and antique shops that line Chester’s downtown area for a one-of-a-kind shopping experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chester

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Chester, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1: Explore Hacklebarney State Park and the Cooper Gristmill.
  • Day 2: Visit historic downtown Chester and tour the local boutiques and eateries.
  • Day 3: Take a scenic drive to Willowwood Arboretum and experience the beautiful gardens.
  • Day 4: Enjoy a wine tasting tour at one of Chester’s wineries.
  • Day 5: Hike along the Patriots Path or Morris Canal Greenway.
  • Day 6: Attend the Chester Sunday Farmers Market and indulge in locally grown produce and homemade food.
  • Day 7: Board the Black River Railroad Historic Trust and take a train ride through the countryside, enjoying its historic charm.

Food and Drink Scene of Chester

Chester offers a diverse range of culinary options, from charming cafes to upscale restaurants, and everything in-between.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jersey Fresh produce: Enjoy freshly grown fruits and veggies, ranging from blueberries to sweet corn and everything in-between.

  • Pork Roll: A Jersey staple, crispy and salty slices of pork on a warm and toasty bun.

  • Lobster Rolls: Dig into these scrumptious rolls bursting with fresh lobster meat and accompanied with a side of fries.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey wine: Savor some of the best varieties of wine produced by local wineries.

  • Local Beer: Quench your thirst with locally-brewed beer, which includes options ranging from lagers to IPAs.

Where to Stay in Chester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chester offers various accommodations to suit any travel budget, from luxurious hotels to affordable hostels and cosy guesthouses.

  • The Main Street Manor Bed and Breakfast: a cozy, romantic B&B situated in the heart of downtown.

  • The Chester Hotel: a luxurious and comfortable hotel with elegant décor and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Chester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Chester back home with you with the following souvenir items:

  • Jersey cow collectibles: Unique and quirky gifts for your loved ones.

  • Local artwork: Beautify your home with artwork created by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Chester

Jerseyans are known for their hospitable nature, and Chester’s residents are no exception. They welcome visitors with warmth and kindness and are proud to showcase their town’s culture and history.

Best Time to Visit Chester

The best time to visit Chester is during fall, when the trees’ foliage gets vibrant, or during spring, when blossoms cover the town. The winter would be less desirable due to cold weather and less varied activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chester

Do:

  • Visit local wineries or farmer’s markets to experience the local culture and cuisine.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and shoes to enjoy hiking trails or outdoor activities fully.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be unprepared in the winter if you plan to explore outdoor activities, bring proper gear that can keep you warm and dry.
  • Don’t be afraid to interact with the locals, they’re always happy to answer your questions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chester

Chester is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always better to take precautions to avoid incidents like theft or pickpocketing.

Health in Chester

Chester has good medical facilities available for treatment.

Getting to Chester: Transportation Options and Logistics

You can reach Chester through various modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Chester

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: located about 35 miles away from Chester

  • Lehigh Valley International Airport: located about 50 miles away from Chester.

Bus Stations Near Chester

  • Interstate Bus Terminal in New York City: located approximately 50 miles away from Chester.

  • Lakeland Bus Line: located in Dover, New Jersey, approximately 12 miles from Chester.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: You can rent cars from nearby automobile rental companies like Enterprise Rent-A-Car or Hertz Rental Car service.

  • Taxis/Ubers: Taxis or Ubers are readily available in the area.

How to Get Around in Chester

It’s best to use your car or rental car to get around town, but biking or even walking can be excellent alternatives for travelers who enjoy outdoor activities. Taxis or Ubers are available in case you don’t have a vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Chester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore beyond Chester, here are some great destinations in the surrounding region:

  • Princeton: renowned for its history, Ivy League university, and beautiful architecture.

  • Morristown: a destination for history buffs, featuring numerous museums and landmarks such as George Washington’s Headquarters.

Conclusion

Chester is a charming and beautiful destination that is sure to fulfill all travel cravings. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or a relaxing weekend getaway, Chester is a fantastic choice. This travel guide includes all the necessary information needed to plan the perfect escape to this picturesque New Jersey township.
