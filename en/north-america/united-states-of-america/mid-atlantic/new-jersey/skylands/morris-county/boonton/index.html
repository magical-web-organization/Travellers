If you’re looking for a peaceful and picturesque destination, Boonton, New Jersey, is the perfect place for you. Located in Morris County, and part of the Skylands region, Boonton boasts idyllic sceneries, cultural landmarks, and exciting outdoor activities. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover all the best things to do, where to stay, and what to expect during your visit to Boonton, New Jersey.

Introduction to Boonton

Boonton is a charming small town located about 23 miles west of New York City. The town has a population of 8,347 and covers 2.4 square miles. Boonton’s history dates back to the 18th century when it was a thriving ironworking community. Today, Boonton retains its historical charm, with antique stores, art galleries, and well-preserved streetscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Boonton

Boonton is a laid-back and friendly town that values its history and natural beauty. The residents are welcoming and embrace visitors who respect the town’s customs and cultural heritage. Remember to be courteous and mindful of the town’s guidelines, such as littering and noise pollution.

Best Time to Visit Boonton

The best time to visit Boonton is during the autumn and spring seasons when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. The summers can be hot and humid, and the winters come with snow and freezing temperatures.

Safety and Health

Boonton is a safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution when exploring its parks and trails. The following are some safety tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Boonton

  • When hiking or biking, stay on designated paths and pay attention to trail markers.
  • When in parks or public spaces, keep an eye on your belongings and avoid leaving them unattended.
  • Avoid walking or traveling alone at night and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Boonton

  • Boonton has many health facilities, including urgent care centers and hospitals. Make sure to have travel insurance in case of any health emergencies.
  • Wear sunscreen, insect repellent, and appropriate clothing when hiking or spending time outdoors to protect yourself from ticks and other insects.

Getting to Boonton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Boonton is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some key transportation options:

Airports Near Boonton

  • Newark International Airport is the closest airport, located about 27 miles away from Boonton.

Bus Stations Near Boonton

  • The NJ Transit bus system serves Boonton, with several routes that stop throughout the town.
  • Greyhound buses also stop in nearby cities, such as Newark and Parsippany.

Other Options

  • If you’re looking for a scenic route, consider taking the Morristown and Erie Railway, which offers a scenic train ride to Boonton.

How to Get Around in Boonton

Boonton is a walkable town, but you’ll need transportation if you’re exploring the outskirts or surrounding areas. Taxis, ride-sharing apps, and car rentals are all available in Boonton.
