Introduction to Parsippany

Parsippany is a vibrant town in Morris County in Skylands, New Jersey. Its strategic location, between the two major cities of New York and Philadelphia, makes it easily accessible for visitors by air, train, or road. The town has retained its old-world charm, and the natural beauty of its surroundings is truly stunning. Parsippany is a great place to explore museums and historical sites, outdoor adventures, and local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Parsippany

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Parsippany has a rich history that can be explored at its museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Parsippany are:

  • The Stickley Museum at Craftsman Farms: This museum is a must-visit for lovers of Arts and Crafts movement. The museum exhibits the works of Gustav Stickley, a prominent furniture designer of the early 20th century.
  • The Knoll East Country Club: This historical site was once known as the Rockaway Hunting Club and was a popular destination for the wealthy in the early 20th century. The site is now a public golf course that has retained its old-world charm.
  • The Parsippany Historical and Preservation Society: This society documents the history of the town and arranges tours of the historic sites in the town. The society also hosts lectures and events that celebrate the town’s heritage.

Natural Attractions of Parsippany: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parsippany is home to several natural attractions that can be enjoyed by outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Parsippany are:

  • The Troy Meadows: This area is home to several trails that are great for hiking and birdwatching. The marshy land is home to several unique species of flora and fauna.
  • The Tourne County Park: This county park offers stunning views of the New York City skyline and is great for hiking, biking, and picnics.
  • The Great Swamp National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge offers visitors the chance to experience an untouched natural environment. The refuge is home to several species of birds, mammals, and reptiles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parsippany

Parsippany has several hiking and biking routes that are great for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes in Parsippany are:

  • The Troy Meadows Trail: This trail is great for hiking and birdwatching. The trail is 3.7 miles long and takes visitors through the marshy land of Troy Meadows.
  • The Tourne Trail: This trail is great for hiking and biking and offers stunning views of the New York City skyline. The trail is 1.7 miles long and is great for beginners.
  • The Mount Tabor Trail: This trail is great for hiking and offers visitors stunning views of the surrounding hills. The trail is 1.6 miles long and has varying levels of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Parsippany: Activities and Tours

Parsippany has several activities and tours that are great for visitors. Some of the must-try activities in Parsippany are:

  • The Wineries and Breweries Tour: This tour takes visitors to several wineries and breweries in the surrounding area. Visitors can taste local wines and beers and learn about the history of winemaking in the area.
