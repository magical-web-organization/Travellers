Introduction to Madison

Located in Morris County, in the Skylands region of New Jersey, Madison is a charming borough in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. Settled by European colonizers in the 1700s, Madison is steeped in history and natural beauty. Visitors to Madison can enjoy a blend of cultural landmarks, outdoor recreation, and a thriving food and drink scene.

Best Things to Do in Madison: Activities and Tours

Madison offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit every taste. Some of the must-try things to do in Madison include:

  • Take a guided tour of the Museum of Early Trades and Crafts
  • Visit the Drew University Museum
  • Enjoy a theatrical performance at the Shakespeare Theatre of New Jersey
  • Attend the Bottle Hill Day festival in September
  • Rent a bike and explore the Patriots Path

Week-Long Itinerary for Madison

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Early Trades and Crafts, explore the Madison Historic District, and enjoy dinner at Nautilus Diner.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of Drew University Museum, enjoy lunch at 54 Main Bar & Grille, and explore Loantaka Brook Reservation.

Day 3: Explore the wildlife at Great Swamp National Wildlife Refuge and take the African Queen Trail.

Day 4: Visit the Shakespeare Theatre of New Jersey and enjoy lunch at Takuma Japanese Restaurant.

Day 5: Rent bikes and explore the Patriots Path, have a picnic at Madison’s Community Garden, and watch a movie at The Rose Theater.

Day 6: Have a fun day at Morris County Park Commission and explore the natural beauty of the Pyramid State Park.

Day 7: Visit Tourne County Park and have dinner at The Belmonte.

Local Culture and Customs of Madison

Madison locals are known for their hospitality and respect towards visitors. The town’s culture is a blend of modernity and history. Dress code in the borough is casual, with the dressier options being acceptable only at some of the classier restaurants or events.

Best Time to Visit Madison

The best time to visit Madison is from May to October when the weather is warm and favorable for outdoor activities. During the summer season, Madison offers numerous cultural festivals, concerts, and events. Winter is chilly, but provides a great opportunity to take part in winter sports activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Madison

Madison is a safe borough, but taking general precautions such as being aware of surroundings and avoiding walking alone at night is still advised.

Health in Madison

Madison has several medical facilities in its vicinity. In case of an emergency, one can head to Morristown Medical Center, The Madison Health Department for health concerns and rehabilitation.

Getting to Madison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Madison

The nearest airport to Madison is Newark Liberty International Airport(18 miles) .

Bus Stations Near Madison

Madison is accessible via the NJ Transit bus routes at Coach USA Suburban Transit and Lakeland Bus.

Other Options

The train is also a great option to get to Madison. NJ Transit’s Morristown Line trains stop at Madison Station, located on the Westbound end of Kings Road, in the heart of Madison’s downtown business district.
