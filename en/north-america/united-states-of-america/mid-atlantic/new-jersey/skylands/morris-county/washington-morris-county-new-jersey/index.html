Introduction to Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

Nestled in the picturesque Skylands region of Morris County, Washington is a charming town filled with natural beauty, fascinating history, and a thriving arts scene. The town boasts a small, walkable downtown with a variety of shops, galleries, restaurants, and bars that make it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer retreat.

List of Places to Visit in Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Washington has a rich history that can be explored through various museums and historical sites. The Morris County Historical Society at Acorn Hall gives an insight into the county’s storied past. You can also visit the Washington Historical Society Museum, which contains a variety of exhibits and artifacts about the town’s local history.

Natural Attractions of Washington (Morris County, New Jersey): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Washington is a nature lover’s paradise with plenty of outdoor activities and scenic views to explore. One of the most popular destinations in the town is the Long Valley Greenway, a 17-mile hiking and biking trail that passes through several picturesque landscapes. For more outdoor adventure, head to Schooley’s Mountain Park, a scenic recreational area with hiking trails, fishing spots, and picnic facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

The Long Valley Greenway is a 17-mile long trail that winds its way through some of Washington’s most scenic vistas. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The Patriots’ Path system is another popular trail in the area, which runs from East Hanover to Washington and offers a variety of terrains for hikers and bikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Washington (Morris County, New Jersey): Activities and Tours

The town has a range of activities and tours to enjoy, from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. Visit the Washington Township Art Walk, a self-guided tour that showcases local artists’ works. You can also take a guided tour of the Four Sisters Winery, which offers a unique wine tasting experience in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

Day 1: Explore the Morris County Historical Society at Acorn Hall, followed by a stroll through the town center.

Day 2: Visit the Washington Township Art Walk and explore the latest works of local artists.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of Four Sisters Winery and enjoy a unique wine tasting experience.

Day 4: Explore Schooley’s Mountain Park, an outdoor recreational area with hiking trails, fishing spots, and picnic facilities.

Day 5: Go on a hike or bike ride on the Long Valley Greenway.

Day 6: Visit the Washington Historical Society Museum, containing a variety of exhibits and artifacts about the town’s local history.

Day 7: Try out a local restaurant and enjoy a nice meal while relaxing in the town center.

Food and Drink Scene of Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

Must-Try Dishes

Washington has a thriving food scene, and there are some dishes you should try during your stay in town. The Black Forest Inn serves authentic German cuisine while Mattar’s Bistro offers a range of seafood options. You can also try a slice of pizza at Maria’s Pizzeria or grab a bite to eat at one of the many local taverns with classic pub food.

Local Beverages

The town has a range of local beverages to enjoy, from beer to wine. The Four Sisters Winery is a must-visit if you are a wine enthusiast, and Canal Winds Brewery is a favorite among beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Washington (Morris County, New Jersey): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Washington, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The National Hotel is a historic spot in the town’s center, offering a traditional bed and breakfast experience. Another option is The Whistling Swan Inn, a luxurious bed and breakfast with impressive gardens. The Inn at Millrace Pond is a rustic option with charming guest rooms in a beautiful natural setting.

What to Buy in Washington (Morris County, New Jersey): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a piece of Washington home with you by picking up some local souvenirs and products. The Long Valley Green Market is a local farmers market selling fresh produce and handmade crafts from the area. The Vintner’s Circle also offers a selection of wines for purchase and provides a custom wine-making experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

The town has a rich history and hosts several cultural events throughout the year, such as the Annual Spring Festival and Fall Festival. Art plays a significant role in the community, and you can see various exhibitions of local artists’ works at the Washington Township Art Walk.

Best Time to Visit Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

The best time to visit Washington is from early summer through the fall, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its most picturesque. The town is also a popular destination during the holiday season, with festive decorations and several events in the city center.

Do’s and Don’ts of Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

Do:

  • Explore the town center on foot, as it’s entirely walkable and full of charm.
  • Try some of the local cuisine at one of the many delicious restaurants.
  • Visit the Morris County Historical Society at Acorn Hall to learn about the town’s history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas while exploring the outdoors.
  • Ignore the small-town charm - be sure to take advantage of the town’s historic center and cultural offerings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

Washington is a safe town, but it is always wise to take precautions when traveling. Keep your valuables safe and secure, be aware of your surroundings at all times, and avoid deserted areas at night.

Health in Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

Washington has several medical facilities serving the area, including the St. Clare’s Hospital. The town also has a range of fitness centers and yoga studios for those looking to stay healthy and active.

Getting to Washington (Morris County, New Jersey): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

The closest major airport is Newark Liberty International Airport, which is approximately 40 miles away from Washington. Philadelphia International Airport and LaGuardia Airport are also options, but are further away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

The Lakeland Bus Lines and NJ Transit both service the Washington area with several stops in town.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies in the area, making it easy to explore the region on four wheels.

How to Get Around in Washington (Morris County, New Jersey)

The town is small and walkable, but having a car can make it easier to explore the surrounding areas and hiking trails.

Where to Go Next from Washington (Morris County, New Jersey): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hackettstown

Just a few miles away from Washington is the town of Hackettstown, a charming area with a rich history and a vibrant cultural scene. There are several historic sites, museums, and galleries to explore in this small town.

Morristown

About a 35-minute drive from Washington is Morristown, which played an important role in the American Revolutionary War. The town boasts several historic sites and museums, including George Washington’s headquarters.

Conclusion

Washington (Morris County, New Jersey) is a charming town with plenty of attractions to explore for both culture and outdoor enthusiasts alike. From its scenic vistas to its rich history and local cuisine, it’s a destination worth visiting.
