Introduction to Somerset

Located in Somerset County, in Skylands, in New Jersey, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Somerset is a city with a rich history and a plethora of unique attractions. Whether you’re interested in learning about the city’s cultural heritage, exploring the outdoors, indulging in local cuisine, or unwinding at a cozy bed and breakfast, Somerset has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the must-visit places, best things to do and eat, and provide tips to make your visit to Somerset an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Somerset

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Franklin Inn

The Franklin Inn, located in Somerset, NJ, is a historic tavern that was established in the 1700s and is famous for serving as a meeting place for George Washington and his military aides during the Revolutionary War. The old-world charm of the place still remains intact, with restored fireplaces, wood paneling, and beautiful antiques adorning the walls.

Abraham Staats House

If you’re a history buff, be sure to visit the Abraham Staats House, which is a 1740s Dutch Colonial homestead that is on the National Register of Historic Places. The house played an important role in the Revolutionary War when it served as a meeting place for military leaders.

Natural Attractions of Somerset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Colonial Park

Stretching across 685 acres, Colonial Park is an excellent destination for nature lovers. The park boasts an arboretum, sports fields, a rose garden, a fish-stocked pond, and an Environmental Education Center, which offers nature-related activities and classes.

Duke Farms

Duke Farms is one of the largest nature preserves in the country, covering an area of 2,700 acres. It is an ideal destination for bird watchers and nature enthusiasts and boasts a greenhouse, orchid range, and cycling trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somerset

D&R Canal State Park Trail

The D&R Canal State Park Trail is a 70-mile-long trail that runs from Trenton to New Brunswick. It’s an ideal route for biking or walking and offers stunning views of the canal and surrounding landscape.

Washington Valley Park

Washington Valley Park features more than 10 miles of trails that meander through lush forests and rolling hills, showcasing the stunning natural beauty of Somerset.

Best Things to Do in Somerset: Activities and Tours

Guided Tours of Rutgers University

Located in New Brunswick, just minutes from Somerset, Rutgers University is a historic institution that offers tours. Explore the beautiful campus and its notable features, including the Old Queens Chapel, the Voorhees Mall, and the Voorhees Hall.

Somerset Patriots Baseball Game

If you’re a sports fan, visit the TD Bank Ballpark and catch a Somerset Patriots baseball game. The team, which is part of the Atlantic League, has won six championship titles since its founding in 1998.

Food and Drink Scene of Somerset

Must-Try Dishes

Pork Roll

Pork roll is a staple of New Jersey cuisine and can be found at many local diners. It’s typically served as a sandwich and is a must-try for those visiting Somerset.

Salt Water Taffy

While not a food item, Salt Water Taffy is a sweet treat that is famous in New Jersey. It’s available in many local candy shops and can be bought in many flavors.

Local Beverages

Jersey Tomato Juice

         The Jersey Tomato Juice is a refreshing drink that is made from locally sourced tomatoes. It's a must-try for those looking for a unique beverage experience.

Where to Stay in Somerset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some top recommendations for places to stay in Somerset:

The Heldrich Hotel and Conference Center

The Heldrich Hotel and Conference Center is a luxury hotel that features comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and many other amenities.

Hyatt House Branchburg

Located in nearby Branchburg, NJ, the Hyatt House Branchburg offers spacious rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Somerset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jersey Devil

The Jersey Devil is a local legend that has become an iconic symbol of New Jersey. It’s available in many souvenir shops and is a great way to bring home a little bit of Somerset.

Salt Water Taffy

As mentioned earlier, Salt Water Taffy is a must-try treat in New Jersey and can be bought in many flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Somerset

Somerset is known for its rich cultural heritage and the locals are proud of it. There is a strong sense of community in Somerset, and visitors are warmly welcomed.

Best Time to Visit Somerset

The best time to visit Somerset is during the fall season (September to November) when the foliage is at its peak, and the weather is pleasant. However, if you’re looking to beat the crowds, visit during the shoulder seasons (March to May or November to December), when the weather is still good, and attractions are less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somerset

Do:

  • Be friendly and open to the locals
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Visit the local museums and historical places

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Park in unauthorized areas
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Somerset

Somerset is a generally safe place to visit. However, travelers should stay vigilant and take the usual precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and not walking in poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Somerset

Visitors should get a doctor’s opinion on vaccinations required before a trip to Somerset. Local hospitals are generally well-equipped and staffed with trained medical professionals.

Getting to Somerset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Somerset

Newark Liberty International Airport

Located about an hour’s drive from Somerset, Newark Liberty International Airport is the closest major airport to Somerset.

John F. Kennedy International Airport

Located in New York City, John F. Kennedy International Airport is about 80 miles from Somerset.

Bus Stations Near Somerset

New Brunswick Station

The New Brunswick Station is located just 15 minutes from Somerset and offers bus services to various destinations.

Greyhound Bus Station

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in Trenton, about a 45-minute drive from Somerset.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, are also available.

How to Get Around in Somerset

The best way to get around Somerset is by car. There are several car rental services available in the city and surrounding areas. Additionally, taxi and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, are available.

Where to Go Next from Somerset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Princeton

Located just 20 minutes away from Somerset, Princeton is an attractive destination with its picturesque campus, world-renowned museums, and upscale shopping.

New York City

With its world-famous attractions, such as the Statue of Liberty and Times Square, and lively entertainment scene, New York City is a must-visit destination. It’s about an hour and a half from Somerset by car.

Conclusion

Somerset is a charming city with a lot to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, or just want to relax and unwind, there’s something for everyone in Somerset. This comprehensive travel guide should help make your visit to Somerset an unforgettable experience.
