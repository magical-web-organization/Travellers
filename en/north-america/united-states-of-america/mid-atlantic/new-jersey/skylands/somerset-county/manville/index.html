Introduction to Manville

Manville, a borough in Somerset County at the heart of Skylands region in New Jersey, is a charming town with a rich history and scenic natural beauty. Once a part of Hillsborough Township, Manville is known for its industrial past, vibrant arts and culture, and numerous outdoor recreational opportunities. Whether you’re looking for a family-friendly vacation, a romantic getaway, or an adventure-filled trip, Manville has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Manville

  • The John Basilone Memorial is a must-visit attraction located on Somerset Street. The memorial honors the brave American hero who was awarded the Medal of Honor for his service during World War II.
  • The Manville-Hillsborough Elks Lodge is a historic building that dates back to the early 1900s. It was historically used as a clubhouse by the Hillsborough Elks, a fraternal organization. Today, the lodge hosts various events, parties, and meetings.
  • The Duke Farms is a stunning 2,700-acre estate that is open to the public to explore the natural beauty of the landscape. The estate features numerous walking trails, a café, a gift shop, and a farmer’s market.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Andrew Toth Blacksmith Shop is a historic building that dates back to the 19th century and is home to a fascinating blacksmith museum. Visitors can immerse themselves in the history and the craft of blacksmithing.
  • The Old Dutch Parsonage Museum is a historic house museum that was former home to the Dutch Reformed Church’s ministers. It now houses a collection of art, furniture, and other historical artifacts.
  • The Wallace House & Old Dutch Parsonage Museum Complex features two historic sites - the Wallace House and the Old Dutch Parsonage - that chronicle the American Revolution.

Natural Attractions of Manville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Duke Farms is a serene oasis that offers visitors the opportunity to walk on extensive trails, explore the botanical gardens or observe wildlife in its natural habitat.
  • The Manville Wildlife Observation Area offers a beautiful place to observe a range of migratory birds. The observation area is easily accessible via walking paths and is a great spot for a picnic lunch.
  • The Sourland Mountain Preserve covers more than 4,000 acres of scenic beauty and boasts an extensive network of well-maintained trails that will take visitors through thick forests and rocky outcroppings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manville

Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Manville include:

  • The Sourland Mountain Preserve offers a variety of trails that range from easy to challenging. For a moderate challenge, try the Roaring Rocks Trail, which offers spectacular views of the Sourland Mountain Range.
  • The Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park spans 77 miles and is a great place for biking and hiking. The canal path is relatively flat, making it perfect for a leisurely bike ride.
  • The South Branch Reservation features numerous trails that offer breathtaking views, including the Indian Trail, which takes hikers through a beautiful hardwood forest.

Best Things to Do in Manville: Activities and Tours

  • River and Trail Outfitters offers guided kayaking tours on the Raritan River, as well as stand-up paddleboarding and tubing options.
  • The Pine Island Cranberry Company is a working cranberry farm that offers visitors a chance to learn about the harvesting process, history, and culture of cranberries.
  • The Flemington Speedway Historical Society Museum is a treasure trove of Flemington Speedway memorabilia, including race cars, photos, and artifacts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manville

Day 1: Visit The Duke Farms and explore the extensive trails. Day 2: Take a tour of the John Basilone Memorial and learn about his heroic legacy in World War II. Day 3: Go on a guided kayaking tour with River and Trail Outfitters. Day 4: Visit the Wallace House and Old Dutch Parsonage Museum Complex to learn about American Revolution history. Day 5: Take a hike through Sourland Mountain Preserve. Day 6: Explore the Pine Island Cranberry Company. Day 7: Bike through the Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Manville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Porchetta Sandwich is an Italian-style pork roast sandwich with an herb crust, served with provolone cheese, roasted pepper, and aioli.
  • Sliders and More is a local gem for sliders with a variety of delicious fillings.
  • Recovery Sports Grill offers classic pub fare with a great selection of beers on tap.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Devil Beer Co. is a craft brewery serving up cold and delicious beers inspired by New Jersey folklore.
  • Old York Cellars is a winery located on an extensive vineyard and offers a wide variety of locally grown wines that you can taste and buy.

Where to Stay in Manville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Bridgewater Marriott offers luxurious rooms and suites in upscale surroundings with a host of amenities such as a pool, gym, and an in-house restaurant.
  • The Howard Johnson by Wyndham Bridgewater offers budget-friendly accommodation with clean and comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.
  • The Hampton Inn & Suites Bridgewater is always a crowd-pleaser, offering modern amenities rooms, an indoor pool, and a hot breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Manville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Manville Market is an excellent choice to pick up some locally grown fruits and vegetables.
  • The Belford Jewelry Store sells unique, locally made jewelry as well as international pieces.
  • The Garden State Farm Market is another excellent choice for locally grown produce, including fresh fruits and veggies, jams, and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Manville

Manville is a multicultural community that celebrates art and culture through events and festivals held throughout the year. The town also boasts a strong sense of community and local pride, as evidenced by its many community activities and initiatives.

Best Time to Visit Manville

The best time to visit Manville is from May to September when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are in full swing. If you’re a history buff, make sure to visit in July for the annual John Basilone Parade, which honors the local hero.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manville

Do:

  • Do visit local restaurants for authentic cuisine.
  • Do indulge in outdoor activities such as hiking, kayaking, and biking.
  • Do participate in the many community events and festivals held throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t miss out on local brews and wines from Manville breweries and wineries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manville

Manville is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always wise to take general precautions such as not leaving valuables in plain sight in parked cars. Stay informed of local weather alerts in case of any severe weather.

Health in Manville

No specific health risks are associated with visiting Manville. However, always maintain healthy hygiene habits, such as washing your hands frequently, and wear sunscreen.

Getting to Manville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manville

  • Newark Liberty International Airport (36 miles)
  • Trenton-Mercer Airport (23 miles)

Bus Stations Near Manville

  • Somerset (NJ Transit)
  • Raritan Valley Community College (Lakeland Bus Line)

Other Options

  • You can take an AmTrak train to Newark, N.J. or Trenton, N.J., and then take a bus or cab to Manville.

How to Get Around in Manville

Getting around Manville is easy and convenient via bus or taxi, provided by NJ Transit buses and other services such as Uber. Alternatively, renting a car is also a feasible option.

Where to Go Next from Manville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Summit is an upscale town located 30 miles north of Manville, offering parks, shops, and restaurants.
  • New Brunswick is a bustling city that lies 10 miles east of Manville and is home to Rutgers University.
  • Princeton is a picturesque town and home to Princeton University, museums, and historic wooden mansions.

Conclusion

Manville is a hidden gem in New Jersey that has plenty to offer visitors. The town’s combination of natural beauty, rich history, and community spirit makes it an ideal destination for a cultural and outdoor experience that’s worth exploring. So pack your bags and explore this charming borough and enjoy the various attractions and experiences it offers!
