Introduction to Bernardsville

Bernardsville is a charming borough located in the heart of Somerset County, New Jersey. It’s part of the Skylands region, which is known for its rolling hills, sprawling forests, and picturesque landscapes. With a population of just over 7,500 people, Bernardsville is a small, tight-knit community that prides itself on its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie looking for your next culinary adventure, Bernardsville has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bernardsville

If you’re looking for things to do in Bernardsville, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the top places to visit in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bernardsville Train Station Museum - Housed in a beautifully restored train station from 1872, this museum gives visitors a glimpse of what life was like in Bernardsville during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

  • Ross Farms - This historic working farm offers tours and educational programs that showcase the agricultural heritage of Somerset County.

  • Somerset Hills Historic Village - Take a walking tour of this charming historic village to see a variety of buildings dating back to the 18th and 19th centuries, including a one-room schoolhouse, a general store, a blacksmith shop, and more.

Natural Attractions of Bernardsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scherman-Hoffman Wildlife Sanctuary - Covering over 276 acres, this nature reserve is a haven for birders, hikers, and nature lovers. With over 200 species of birds and a variety of wildlife, you’re sure to see something amazing.

  • Washington Valley Park - This park offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and picnicking. It’s also home to the picturesque Chimney Rock and scenic overlooks that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Natirar Park - Originally the estate of a wealthy industrialist, this park now offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and fishing in a serene natural setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bernardsville

Bernardsville is a hiker’s paradise, with trails that wind through picturesque countryside, charming historic villages, and expansive nature reserves. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Patriot’s Path - This multi-use trail covers over 35 miles and runs through several counties, including Somerset County. It’s perfect for hikers, bikers, and equestrians and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Sourland Mountain Preserve - With over 3,000 acres of forests, streams, and rocky outcroppings, this preserve is a hiker’s paradise. The highlight of the preserve is the Sourland Mountain Ridge, which offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Columbia Trail - This 15-mile trail runs through Hunterdon County and passes through quaint towns, historic landmarks, and beautiful countryside. It’s perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a long hike.

Best Things to Do in Bernardsville: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there are plenty of things to do in Bernardsville. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Take a horse-drawn carriage ride through downtown Bernardsville and its surrounding countryside.

  • Visit Greenberries, an artisanal bakery that specializes in organic, locally sourced pastries, bread, and other baked goods.

  • Go on a vineyard tour of Old York Cellars, a local winery that produces award-winning wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bernardsville

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Bernardsville, here’s an itinerary that will allow you to make the most of your time in the area:

Day Activities

4 Tour the Natirar Park and enjoy a wine tasting at Old York Cellars.

Food and Drink Scene of Bernardsville

Bernardsville has a thriving food and drink scene, with a variety of restaurants, cafes, and bars to choose from. Here are some of the must-try dishes, as well as the local beverages you can enjoy in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bernards Inn Lobster Bisque - This classic dish from the Bernards Inn is rich, creamy, and filled with chunks of fresh lobster.

  • Grilled Filet Mignon from The Station - This restaurant is located in a restored train station and serves up some of the best steak in town.

  • Duck Carnitas Tacos from Cafe Azzurro - This upscale Mexican restaurant offers a unique twist on traditional Mexican fare.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Blueberry Wine from Old York Cellars - Made with locally sourced blueberries, this wine is a refreshing and delicious treat.

  • River Horse Brewing Company Beer - This New Jersey-based brewery offers a variety of unique and flavorful beers, including the popular River Horse Tripel Horse.

Where to Stay in Bernardsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxurious accommodations or budget-friendly options, Bernardsville has plenty of places to stay. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area:

  • Bernards Inn - This historic inn dates back to 1907 and offers luxurious accommodations and exceptional dining.

  • Skytop Lodge - This elegant resort is located on a sprawling 5,500-acre estate and offers a variety of activities, including golf, swimming, hiking, and more.

  • The Peacock Inn - Located in nearby Princeton, this elegant boutique hotel offers luxurious accommodations and exceptional dining in a beautiful setting.

What to Buy in Bernardsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, check out some of these unique shops and boutiques in Bernardsville:

  • The Good Earth - This store offers a range of organic and handmade skincare products, as well as wellness products and unique gifts.

  • Bernardsville Farmer’s Market - Held on Saturdays from May through November, this market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other local products.

Local Culture and Customs of Bernardsville

Bernardsville has a rich cultural heritage, with a variety of customs and traditions that have been passed down through the generations. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting the area:

  • Bernardsville is a tight-knit community, and locals tend to be friendly and welcoming to visitors.

  • The area has a rich history, and many of the local attractions focus on preserving this heritage.

  • There are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in Bernardsville, and it’s not uncommon to see locals hiking, biking, or fishing in their spare time.

Best Time to Visit Bernardsville

The best time to visit Bernardsville is during the fall, when the changing leaves and crisp weather make for a picturesque setting. Spring and summer are also great times to visit, as the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bernardsville

Bernardsville is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s still important to take standard safety precautions, such as staying aware of your surroundings and locking your car doors.

Health in Bernardsville

Bernardsville has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. It’s important to take precautions to prevent tick bites when enjoying the outdoors, as Lyme disease is prevalent in the area.

Getting to Bernardsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bernardsville

The nearest airport to Bernardsville is Newark Liberty International Airport, which is about 33 miles away. The airport is served by several major airlines and offers a variety of transportation options to the area.

Bus Stations Near Bernardsville

The nearest bus station to Bernardsville is in nearby Somerville, which is about 8 miles away. The station is served by several bus companies, including NJ Transit and Greyhound.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Bernardsville is easily accessible from several major highways, including Route 78 and Route 287. It’s also a short drive from several major cities, including New York City and Philadelphia.

How to Get Around in Bernardsville

The best way to get around Bernardsville is by car, although the area is also fairly bike-friendly. There are also several taxi and ride-sharing services available in the area.
