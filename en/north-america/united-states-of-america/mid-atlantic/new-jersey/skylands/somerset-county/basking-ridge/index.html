Introduction to Basking Ridge

Nestled in the heart of Somerset County, in the Skylands region of New Jersey, Basking Ridge is a picturesque town known for its historic charm, natural beauty, and friendly community. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, Basking Ridge has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Basking Ridge

From museums and historical sites to hiking trails and scenic views, Basking Ridge offers a wealth of attractions that cater to all interests. Here are the must-visit places in Basking Ridge:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brick Academy: This historic building was built in 1809 and served as a schoolhouse. Today, it houses the Historical Society of the Somerset Hills and a museum that showcases the town’s rich history.
  • Lord Stirling Park: The former estate of William Alexander, a hero of the American Revolution, this park offers tours of the estate, as well as hiking trails and fishing ponds.

Natural Attractions of Basking Ridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Swamp National Wildlife Refuge: This 7,600-acre wildlife refuge boasts several hiking and biking trails, as well as great opportunities for bird watching and wildlife spotting.
  • Sourland Mountain Preserve: This scenic park covers over 6,000 acres of land and offers a variety of hiking and mountain biking trails ranging from easy to difficult. From the top of the mountain, you can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Basking Ridge

For hikers and bikers, Basking Ridge and the surrounding area offer a plethora of scenic trails and routes. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Basking Ridge:

  • Lord Stirling Park: This park has several hiking trails that take you through fields, forests, and along streams. The Ecosphere Trail is a popular hiking trail that provides a glimpse into the unique ecosystem of the Great Swamp.
  • Columbia Trail: This 15-mile rail trail takes you through several small towns, offering scenic views of rolling farmland and woodlands.

Best Things to Do in Basking Ridge: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Basking Ridge also offers a variety of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Basking Ridge:

  • Somerset Hills Country Club: This exclusive golf and social club features an 18-hole golf course, a tennis court, and a swimming pool.
  • Farmstead Arts: This nonprofit organization hosts a wide range of art classes, exhibits, and workshops throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Basking Ridge

If you have a week to spare in Basking Ridge, here is an itinerary to make the most of your visit:

Food and Drink Scene of Basking Ridge

Basking Ridge has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a range of local and international options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza at Cafe Rustica: This family-owned pizzeria offers delicious thin-crust pizza made with fresh, local ingredients.
  • Gnocchi at Pheasants Landing Restaurant: This cozy restaurant serves classic Italian dishes made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Wine at Old York Cellars: This award-winning winery offers a variety of red, white, and fruit wines made with locally grown grapes.
  • Craft Beer at Lone Eagle Brewing: This local microbrewery features a rotating selection of craft beers made with local hops and grains.

Where to Stay in Basking Ridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Basking Ridge and the surrounding area offer a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Basking Ridge:

  • The Bernards Inn: This luxury hotel and restaurant offers impeccable service and elegant accommodations.
  • The Peacock Inn: This historic inn features stylish rooms and serves contemporary American cuisine.

What to Buy in Basking Ridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Basking Ridge, here are some souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Honey from E.B. Hathaway and Sons: This local beekeeping company produces high-quality honey and beeswax products.
  • Pottery from Fired Up!: This local ceramics studio offers a range of handcrafted plates, bowls, and mugs.

Local Culture and Customs of Basking Ridge

Basking Ridge is known for its welcoming and friendly community. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as holding doors open for others and using polite language.

Best Time to Visit Basking Ridge

The best time to visit Basking Ridge is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its most beautiful. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Basking Ridge

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Plan ahead and make reservations for popular restaurants and attractions.
  • Tip your servers and service staff.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural surroundings.
  • Use offensive language or behavior.
  • Walk in the street when there is a sidewalk available.

Safety and Health

Safety in Basking Ridge

Basking Ridge is a safe and welcoming town. However, visitors are encouraged to take common-sense precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Basking Ridge

Basking Ridge has several hospitals and medical facilities, including Morristown Medical Center and Overlook Medical Center, both of which are equipped to handle emergency medical situations.

Getting to Basking Ridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Basking Ridge is located approximately 30 miles west of Newark Liberty International Airport, and is accessible via several major highways, including the Garden State Parkway and Interstate 78.

Airports Near Basking Ridge

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Approximately 30 miles east of Basking Ridge.

Bus Stations Near Basking Ridge

-NJ Transit offers bus service to and from Basking Ridge from nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Basking Ridge is easily accessible from major highways, and car rental options are available at Newark Liberty International Airport.

How to Get Around in Basking Ridge

Basking Ridge is a relatively small town, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. However, a car or bike rental may be useful depending on your itinerary.

Where to Go Next from Basking Ridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Morristown: A historic town known for its Revolutionary War history and vibrant downtown.
  • Lambertville: A charming town known for its antique shops, art galleries, and scenic views of the Delaware River.

Conclusion

Basking Ridge offers a unique blend of history, nature, and culture, making it the perfect destination for travelers looking to experience the best of New Jersey. With this comprehensive guide, you can explore the town’s hidden gems and experience everything Basking Ridge has to offer.
