Introduction to Montgomery Township

Montgomery Township is a charming township located in Somerset County, in Skylands, New Jersey, in Mid-Atlantic, the United States of America. It is a small township with a population of around 23,000 people and is known for its scenic beauty and cultural attractions. The township offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, historic attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through some of the exciting things to do, places to visit, and where to eat and stay while in Montgomery Township.

List of Places to Visit in Montgomery Township

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Montgomery Township has a rich history that is reflected in the many museums and historical places spread across the township. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Rockingham Historic Site: This historic site was once home to a notable patriot and signer of the Declaration of Independence, John Berrien. The Rockingham Historic Site is a restored farmhouse and offers a glimpse into 18th century life.
  • Princeton Battlefield State Park: This historic park marks the spot of the Battle of Princeton, a significant battle during the American Revolution.
  • Van Harlingen Historical Society Museum: The Van Harlingen Historical Society Museum is located in a 19th-century church and showcases Montgomery Township’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Drake House Museum: The Drake House Museum is a historic house that tells the story of the American Revolution and the role Montgomery Township played in it.

Natural Attractions of Montgomery Township: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Montgomery Township is also home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in New Jersey. The township is surrounded by lush green forests, rolling hills, and rivers. Here are some of the outdoor activities and scenic views to explore:

  • D&R Canal State Park: The D&R Canal State Park is a beautiful park that runs along the Delaware and Raritan Canal. It offers a peaceful setting for a morning walk or a bike ride.
  • Six Mile Run Reservoir Site: The Six Mile Run Reservoir Site is a natural reserve that covers over 3000 acres of land. It is home to diverse flora and fauna and is perfect for nature lovers.
  • Zion Crossing Trail: The Zion Crossing Trail is a peaceful hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of Montgomery Township and the surrounding areas.
  • The Sourland Mountain Preserve: The Sourland Mountain Preserve is known for its beautiful hiking trails, scenic views, and abundant wildlife. It is a perfect day trip destination for hikers and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montgomery Township

Montgomery Township has some of the best hiking and biking trails in New Jersey. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Belle Mead/Montgomery Trail: This 7-mile trail runs along Kingsbridge Road and is perfect for an afternoon bike ride or a morning hike.
  • D&R Canal State Park Trail: This 77-mile trail runs along the Delaware and Raritan Canal and is perfect for a weekend biking trip.
  • Rock Brook Trail: This 6-mile trail is perfect for hikers looking for a moderate hike that’s not too strenuous.

Best Things to Do in Montgomery Township: Activities and Tours

Montgomery Township has a range of fun activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of the best:

  • Go apple picking at Terhune Orchards: Terhune Orchards is a local farm that offers delicious fruits and vegetables. It’s the perfect destination for families with children.
  • Go wine tasting at Unionville Vineyards: Unionville Vineyards is a winery that offers wine tasting, tours, and events throughout the year.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride with Balloons Aloft: Balloons Aloft is a great way to experience Montgomery Township’s natural beauty from above.
  • Explore the township’s history with a tour from Montgomery Friends of Open Space.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montgomery Township

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Montgomery Township:

  • Day 1: Visit the Rockingham Historic Site and take a hike in the D&R Canal State Park.
  • Day 2: Take a tour of the Van Harlingen Historical Society Museum and go wine tasting at Unionville Vineyards.
  • Day 3: Explore the Princeton Battlefield State Park and take a hot air balloon ride with Balloons Aloft.
  • Day 4: Go apple picking at Terhune Orchards and visit the Sourland Mountain Preserve.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of Montgomery Township’s historical sites with Montgomery Friends of Open Space.
  • Day 6: Visit the Drake House Museum and go for a hike on the Rock Brook Trail.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy a day at your leisure.

Food and Drink Scene of Montgomery Township

Must-Try Dishes

Montgomery Township has a diverse food scene that reflects the township’s cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Lobster mac and cheese at The Peasant Grill: This dish combines luxury with comfort food and is a must-try for foodies.
  • Pizza at Mamma Rosa’s: Mamma Rosa’s is a family-owned Italian restaurant that is known for its delicious pizza.
  • Grilled chicken souvlaki at Gyros To Go: Gyros To Go is a casual eatery that serves the best and most affordable souvlaki in town.

Local Beverages

Montgomery Township is home to some of the best wineries and breweries in New Jersey. Here are some of the must-try local beverages:

  • Wine from Hopewell Valley Vineyards: Hopewell Valley Vineyards is a family-owned winery that produces award-winning wines.
  • Beer from Czig Meister Brewing Company: Czig Meister is a brewery that offers an extensive selection of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Montgomery Township: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montgomery Township offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the popular choices:

  • Courtyard by Marriott Princeton: This hotel offers modern amenities and is located close to Princeton University.
  • Red Roof Inn Princeton - Ewing: This budget hotel offers affordable and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Montgomery Township: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montgomery Township is well known for its artisanal food and craft products. Here are some of the souvenirs and products to bring home:

  • Handcrafted pottery from the Princeton Arts Council: The Princeton Arts Council offers beautiful pottery crafted by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Montgomery Township

Montgomery Township is home to a diverse community, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. The people of Montgomery Township value education, and visitors should seek opportunities to learn about the township’s history and cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Montgomery Township

The best time to visit Montgomery Township is during the fall when the trees start to change colors. The weather is cool and pleasant, making it perfect for outdoor activities and hiking. Spring is also a good time to visit when the flowers are in full bloom.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montgomery Township

Montgomery Township is a safe place to visit, and visitors are advised to take reasonable precautions to keep themselves safe. Visitors are encouraged to be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or enjoying outdoor activities.

Health in Montgomery Township

Montgomery Township has several hospitals and medical facilities, including Somerset Medical Center and Capital Health. Visitors with medical concerns are advised to seek medical assistance immediately.

Getting to Montgomery Township: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Montgomery Township

The nearest airport to Montgomery Township is Newark Liberty International Airport, which is approximately 45 miles away. Other airports in the vicinity include:

  • LaGuardia Airport (63 miles)
  • Philadelphia International Airport (73 miles)

Bus Stations Near Montgomery Township

NJ Transit operates several bus routes that serve Montgomery Township. The nearest bus stations include:

  • Princeton Junction Train Station
  • Somerville Train Station

Other Options

Montgomery Township is accessible by car, and visitors are encouraged to rent a car if traveling from out of town. Amtrak trains also run through New Jersey, making it a convenient option for visitors coming from Boston, New York, or Philadelphia.

How to Get Around in Montgomery Township

The best way to get around Montgomery Township is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in-town car rental offices. Biking is also a popular option, and visitors can rent a bike from local bike rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Montgomery Township: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montgomery Township is located close to several exciting destinations, making it perfect for day trips. Here are some of the nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Princeton: The charming college town of Princeton is just a few miles away from Montgomery Township. It is known for its beautiful architecture, museums, and galleries.
  • New Hope: New Hope is a charming town located in Pennsylvania, just across the bridge from New Jersey. It offers a range of cultural and recreational activities, including galleries, theaters, and parks.
  • Lambertville: Lambertville is a quaint town located on the Delaware River that offers a mix of art galleries, antique shops, and restaurants.

Montgomery Township is a charming township known for its beautiful natural scenery, cultural attractions, and delicious food.
