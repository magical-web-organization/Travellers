Far Hills Travel Guide: Explore the Best of Somerset County, New Jersey

Introduction to Far Hills

Nestled in Somerset County, New Jersey, Far Hills is a quaint, rural town that’s known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage and warm hospitality. The region is home to rolling hills, verdant pastures, charming farms, and historic landmarks, making it a popular destination for nature lovers, history buffs and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Far Hills

From historical landmarks to natural wonders, Far Hills boasts a range of attractions that appeal to travelers of all interests. Here are some of the top things to see and do in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Far Hills Race Meeting
  • The United States Golf Association Museum
  • Jacobus Vanderveer House and Museum
  • Leonard J. Buck Garden
  • Natirar Park
  • The Old Dutch Parsonage Museum
  • The Wallace House

Natural Attractions of Far Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Great Swamp National Wildlife Refuge
  • Hacklebarney State Park
  • Far Hills Wildlife Management Area
  • The Raptor Trust
  • The Environmental Education Center at Lord Stirling Park
  • Black River County Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far Hills

Far Hills has a variety of biking and hiking trails that offer stunning views of the area’s natural scenery. Some of the notable routes include:

  • The Columbia Trail
  • The Traction Line Recreation Trail
  • Cooper Mill Loop Trail in Hacklebarney State Park
  • Black River Trail in Black River County Park

Best Things to Do in Far Hills: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, here are other exciting activities and tours visitors can indulge in:

  • Private hot air balloon rides
  • Horseback riding and carriage tours
  • Wine tasting tours
  • Farmers markets and farm tours
  • Fly-fishing in the streams and rivers

Week-Long Itinerary for Far Hills

Day 1: Visit the Leonard J. Buck Garden and the Jacobus Vanderveer House and Museum. Day 2: Hike the Columbia Trail and then visit The United States Golf Association Museum. Day 3: Go on a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the beautiful views of Far Hills from the sky. Day 4: Go on a horseback riding and carriage tour. Day 5: Go wine tasting at one of the nearby wineries. Day 6: Visit Far Hills Wildlife Management Area for bird-watching and nature walks. Day 7: Explore the town’s food scene and local markets before departing.

Local Culture and Customs of Far Hills

Far Hills has its unique local culture, and travelers should familiarize themselves with these customs:

  • People in Far Hills are warm and friendly. It’s customary to greet people on the street with a smile and a nod.
  • Tipping is a standard practice, and restaurant servers expect at least 15 to 20 percent gratuity.
  • When visiting historical landmarks and museums, be respectful of the exhibits and artifacts.
  • The Far Hills Race Meeting, a steeplechase horse race, is a beloved annual event held every fall.

Best Time to Visit Far Hills

Far Hills experiences all four seasons, with warm summers and cold winters. The best time to visit the town is in the fall, from late September to early November, when the autumn foliage is at its peak, and the Far Hills Race Meeting is held.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far Hills

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and culture.
  • Take advantage of the natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities in the area.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Attend the Far Hills Race Meeting.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect cultural and historical landmarks.
  • Be rude or impolite to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Far Hills

Far Hills is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, travelers should still exercise caution and take basic safety measures such as locking their doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Far Hills

The town has several good medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, and travelers should have no trouble finding quality healthcare.

Bus Stations Near Far Hills

Other Options

Driving is the best way to get around Far Hills, given the rural nature of the area. Renting a car is a good option for travelers who don’t have their vehicles.

How to Get Around in Far Hills

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Far Hills and explore the region’s attractions. If you’re not driving, you can use alternatives such as:

  • Ride-hailing apps like Uber or Lyft
  • Taxis
  • Bicycle rentals

Conclusion
