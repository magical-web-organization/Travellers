Introduction to Hillsborough

Hillsborough is a picturesque township in Somerset County, New Jersey. With its charming small-town feel and rich history, it’s no wonder that Hillsborough has become a popular destination for tourists. The township is situated in the Skylands region of New Jersey, an area known for its beautiful state parks and rolling hills. Hillsborough is located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, making it easily accessible for travelers from all parts of the country.

List of Places to Visit in Hillsborough

Whether you’re interested in exploring the local history, taking in the natural beauty of the region, or indulging in some delicious eats, Hillsborough has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hillsborough has a rich history that is preserved and celebrated through its many museums and historical sites. Visit the Dr. John Vermeule House, a historic property that dates back to the 18th century, or the Old Dutch Parsonage, where General George Washington once stayed during the Revolutionary War. The Duke Farms, once owned by heiress Doris Duke and now open to the public, is another popular attraction featuring a range of walking trails and a cafe.

Natural Attractions of Hillsborough: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those looking to experience the natural beauty of Hillsborough, there is no shortage of options. From hiking the trails at Sourland Mountain Preserve to fishing or kayaking along the Millstone River, the area offers endless opportunities to enjoy the outdoors. For a more leisurely outing, take a stroll through the Duke Farms’ Arboretum and Butterfly House or visit the Gardens on Spring Creek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hillsborough

There are many great hiking and biking trails to choose from in Hillsborough. The Sourland Mountain Preserve offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside and a glimpse into the local wildlife. The area’s Colonial Park is another popular outdoor destination that includes hiking trails, fishing ponds, and a golf course. Cyclists will enjoy the Bedminster-Hillsborough Loop, a scenic route that winds through the rolling hills of the region.

Best Things to Do in Hillsborough: Activities and Tours

In addition to its many natural and historical attractions, Hillsborough also offers plenty of fun activities and tours for visitors. Take a hot air balloon ride with nearby Balloons Aloft, visit the Somerset County Farmers Market on Saturdays during the summer months, or visit the Hillsborough Cultural Arts Commission’s annual art show. You can also tour the local vineyards and taste regional wines with the Central New Jersey Wine Tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Hillsborough

Hillsborough has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local spots to enjoy. From classic diners to upscale eateries and trendy cafes, there is something for every taste and budget.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular dishes in Hillsborough include the house-made pierogi at Polish Kitchen, the juicy burgers from 22 West Tap and Grill, and the inventive sushi rolls from Sushi Palace. Be sure to also try the wood-fired pizzas from Amici’s Brick Oven Pizza or the homemade ice cream from Hillsborough’s own Rita’s Italian Ice.

Local Beverages

There are also plenty of great options for drinks in Hillsborough. Grab a beer or cocktail at one of the many local bars like The Landing or 69 Main Lounge, or sample regional wines at the Old York Cellars tasting room in nearby Ringoes.

Where to Stay in Hillsborough: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a cozy bed and breakfast, Hillsborough has plenty of options for places to stay. The Fieldstone Farm Bed and Breakfast is a popular choice among visitors, offering charming rooms and easy access to nearby attractions. For a more budget-friendly option, the Days Inn by Wyndham Hillsborough is a great choice, offering comfortable rooms and easy access to the local highways.

What to Buy in Hillsborough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Hillsborough home with you by picking up some locally made souvenirs. Visit Something’s Amiss pottery studio and gallery for unique handcrafted pottery and gifts, or stop by the Hillsborough Antique and Artisan Center for one-of-a-kind vintage finds.

Local Culture and Customs of Hillsborough

Locals in Hillsborough value their rich history and the natural beauty of the area. Many residents are active and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and biking. The township is also home to a number of community events and festivals throughout the year, including the popular Hillsborough Rotary Fair in August.

Best Time to Visit Hillsborough

Hillsborough is a great place to visit year-round, but the fall season is especially beautiful when the leaves change and the weather cools down. Spring is also a great time to visit, with the local plants and flowers coming back to life and the warmer temperatures making it a great time to enjoy the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hillsborough

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many hiking trails and outdoor activities.
  • Visit the local wineries and try the regional wines.
  • Check out the many community events and fairs throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying the local pierogis or pizza.
  • Neglect to pay a visit to the area’s many historical sites and museums.
  • Forget to bring a camera to capture the region’s natural beauty.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hillsborough

Hillsborough is a relatively safe area with a low crime rate. Visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings when exploring the area, especially at night.

Health in Hillsborough

There are several hospitals and medical centers in the nearby area, including the nearby Robert Wood Johnson University Hospital and Somerset Medical Center.

Getting to Hillsborough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hillsborough

The nearest airport to Hillsborough is Newark Liberty International Airport, located about 32 miles away. Philadelphia International Airport is another popular option for travelers.

Bus Stations Near Hillsborough

The NJ Transit Bus Line 114/117 connects Hillsborough to New York City and other nearby areas.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Hillsborough by car via Interstate 287 and Interstate 78 or by train via nearby NJ Transit stations.

How to Get Around in Hillsborough

While Hillsborough is generally a drivable region, it’s also possible to get around by public transportation or ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft. Many of the outdoor attractions, like the hiking trails and parks, are best accessed by car, however.

Where to Go Next from Hillsborough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hillsborough is located near many other popular destinations in New Jersey, including the historic towns of Princeton and Flemington. Visitors can also take a day trip to nearby Philadelphia, PA or New York City.

Conclusion

Hillsborough, New Jersey is a charming and picturesque destination that offers something for everyone. From its rich history to its natural beauty and vibrant food and drink scene, there’s no shortage of things to see and do. Plan your visit today and discover all that this hidden gem of a township has to offer.
