Introduction to Raritan

Raritan, located in Somerset County in New Jersey, is a charming Mid-Atlantic town known for its rich history and scenic beauty. The town is situated in the Skylands region, renowned for its rolling hills, stunning foliage, and outdoor recreational opportunities. Visitors to Raritan will discover a town filled with cultural attractions, natural wonders, and plenty of opportunities for fun, adventure, and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Raritan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Raritan’s rich history is reflected in the town’s numerous cultural attractions, including museums and historical landmarks. These attractions not only provide insight into the town’s past but also serve as a testament to Raritan’s enduring cultural significance.

  • Raritan Public Library: Located in the heart of downtown Raritan, the Raritan Public Library is a community hub and one of the town’s most beloved landmarks. The library features a wide variety of literary works, cultural programs, and events.

  • The Wallace House: The Wallace House is a beautifully preserved 18th-century Georgian-style mansion that once served as a temporary residence for General George Washington during the Revolutionary War. Visitors can explore this historic landmark and learn about the history of the American Revolution.

Natural Attractions of Raritan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Raritan’s stunning natural beauty is another draw for visitors. From lush forests to scenic rivers and rolling hills, the town is a nature lover’s paradise. Some of the top natural attractions in Raritan include:

  • Duke Farms: This 2,740-acre estate features beautifully landscaped gardens, forests, waterfalls, and nature preserves. Visitors can explore the property’s extensive trails, including a stunning Orchid Range and the Arboretum.

  • The Raritan River: Running through the heart of Raritan, the Raritan River is a scenic waterway that offers endless opportunities for boating, fishing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Raritan

Raritan offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure, including a wide range of hikes and bike rides. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • The Sourland Mountain Preserve: This 4,000-acre preserve is home to more than 20 miles of hiking trails and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • The Delaware and Raritan Canal State Park: This park features 77 miles of scenic towpath and offers a variety of hiking and biking opportunities. Visitors can explore historic landmarks, enjoy the scenic views, and take part in guided tours and events.

Best Things to Do in Raritan: Activities and Tours

In addition to these outdoor activities, there are plenty of other things to do in Raritan. From arts and culture to entertainment and recreation, there’s something for everyone in this charming Mid-Atlantic town. Some of the top things to do in Raritan include:

  • The Somerset County Library: This state-of-the-art library features an extensive collection of books, movies, and music. Visitors can attend cultural events, workshops, and classes, and enjoy the library’s many amenities.

  • The Raritan Valley Farmers Market: This local farmers market is open on Sundays and offers a wide variety of fresh produce, meats, baked goods, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Raritan

To help you plan your visit to Raritan, here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore downtown Raritan, visit the Raritan Public Library, and enjoy a sunset stroll along the Raritan River.

  • Day 2: Discover the history of the American Revolution at the Wallace House, then head to Duke Farms to explore the beautiful gardens and nature preserves.

  • Day 3: Take a scenic bike ride along the Delaware and Raritan Canal, or enjoy a hike at the Sourland Mountain Preserve.

  • Day 4: Discover local art and culture at the Somerset County Cultural and Heritage Center, then head to the Raritan Valley Farmers Market for the freshest local produce.

  • Day 5: Explore the outdoors with a kayaking or fishing trip on the Raritan River, or take a guided nature hike through the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Day 6: Indulge in some retail therapy at the nearby Bridgewater Commons Mall, then head to the local winery for a tasting and tour.

  • Day 7: Relax and unwind with a picnic at the picturesque Duke Island Park or take in a movie at the nearby AMC cinema.

Food and Drink Scene of Raritan

No trip to Raritan would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. From classic American fare to international delights, there’s something for everyone to enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Raritan River Burger: This classic American burger is made with grass-fed beef, local cheddar cheese, and topped with crispy shoestring onions.

  • The Wallace House Salad: This fresh and delicious salad features locally grown baby greens, heirloom tomatoes, and a homemade red-wine vinaigrette.

Local Beverages

  • Raritan Valley Winery: This local winery offers a wide variety of artisanal wines, including Cabernet Sauvignon, Chardonnay, and Merlot.

  • The Raritan Brewery Company: This local craft brewery offers a range of delicious brews, including their signature IPA and seasonal offerings.

Where to Stay in Raritan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for budget-friendly accommodations or luxurious digs, Raritan has a wide range of options to suit every taste and budget. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Raritan include:

Getting to Raritan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Raritan is conveniently located near several major transportation hubs, including airports, bus stations, and highways.

Airports Near Raritan

The closest airport to Raritan is Newark Liberty International Airport, which is approximately 30 miles away. Other nearby airports include Philadelphia International Airport and Trenton-Mercer Airport.

Bus Stations Near Raritan

Raritan is served by several local and regional bus lines, including NJ Transit and the Greyhound Bus Company. The closest bus station to Raritan is the Bridgewater Commons Park & Ride.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Raritan by car, bike, or train. The town is located near several major highways, including I-78 and I-287, and is served by several Amtrak train stations.

How to Get Around in Raritan

Raritan is a small town, so getting around is easy and convenient. Visitors can explore the area on foot, by bike, or by car. There are several car rental companies available in the town, as well as local bike rental options.
