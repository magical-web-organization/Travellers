Introduction to Bridgewater

Bridgewater is a cozy township in Somerset County, New Jersey. It is part of the Skylands region and situated in Mid-Atlantic in the United States of America. The township has a rich history, breathtaking natural landscapes, and an array of cultural and leisure attractions. Whether you want to spend your days hiking scenic trails, enjoying outdoor activities, exploring cultural sites, or indulging in some local food and drinks, Bridgewater has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bridgewater

Here are some must-visit places when you’re in Bridgewater:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Meadows Foundation: This landmark is an 18th-century farmhouse that was transformed into a museum, showcasing the early days of Bridgewater and the surrounding area.
  • Walt Whitman House: This historical location is the birthplace of legendary American poet Walt Whitman. It is now a state historic site, which features his life and works through permanent and rotating exhibits.
  • Somerset County Courthouse: It is the oldest courthouse in continuous use in the country and was built in 1750. It is an excellent example of Georgian architecture and retains many of its original features.

Natural Attractions of Bridgewater: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Duke Farms: This large estate is accompanied by extensive walking and hiking trails, an orchid greenhouse, bird watching, and a learning center.
  • Washington Valley Park: It spans across 719 acres of rolling hills, meadows, and forests. The park is a great spot for biking, hiking, and fishing.
  • Bridgewater Commons Trail: This paved trail is suitable for biking, rollerblading, and strolling. The trail winds by patches of forest, fields, and shopping centers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridgewater

Here are some biking and hiking routes to explore in Bridgewater:

Route Name Distance Description
Duke Island Park Trail 1.4 miles A paved perimeter trail that provides a scenic view along the Raritan River and close to the park’s inner ponds.
Patriots’ Path 14.1 miles A hiking and biking trail that spans across Morris, Sussex, and Warren counties. The trail features stunning views of the Great Swamp National Wildlife Refuge, Black River County Park, and Wildcat Ridge.
Chimney Rock Park Trail 3.2 miles A wooded trail that features a steep incline to the overlook over the meadows of Martinsville.

Best Things to Do in Bridgewater: Activities and Tours

  • Bridgewater Sports Arena: It features skating, laser tag, arcade games, and more indoor activities for families and friends.
  • Northlandz: This non-profit miniature view attraction features a 16-acre indoor model railroad, doll museum, and art gallery.
  • New Jersey equestrian Center: Offers horseback riding lessons for all ages and skills.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bridgewater

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Bridgewater:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Meadows Foundation, Washington Valley Park, and Duke Farms.
2 Explore the Bridgewater Commons Trail, have lunch at Maggiano’s Little Italy, and go shopping at the mall.
3 Visit the Walt Whitman House, and Chimney Rock Park Trail. Grab a bite to eat at the local favorite Raritan bakery.

5 Enjoy horseback riding at the New Jersey equestrian Center. Later, grab dinner at Green Knoll Grill.

Local Culture and Customs of Bridgewater

Residents of Bridgewater are friendly and welcoming. You should avoid littering as Bridgewater is environmentally conscious and has strict laws against littering. While shopping, remember that sales tax rates are higher than in most other states.

Best Time to Visit Bridgewater

The best time to visit Bridgewater is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm, and there are many outdoor events and activities to partake in. The fall is also a great time to visit when the leaves change color and produce scenic views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bridgewater

Do:

  • Respect the local laws, including the strict littering rules.
  • Try local cuisine.
  • Visit the local historic sites.
  • Enjoy the outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t smoke in non-smoking areas.
  • Avoid illegal activities, vandalism, and damaging local sites.
  • Don’t litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bridgewater

Bridgewater is a safe township among the other New Jersey townships. The crime rates are relatively low. However, as with any city, it is best to keep your belongings safely to avoid theft.

Health in Bridgewater

There are several medical centers and pharmacies in the township. There are several clinics and government-based health facilities, including Somerset County Public Health Department and Robert Wood Johnson University Hospital.

Getting to Bridgewater: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some travel options to get to Bridgewater:

Airports Near Bridgewater

  • Newark-Liberty International Airport is approximately 35 miles away from Bridgewater.

Bus Stations Near Bridgewater

  • Coach USA provides bus services from major New Jersey and New York cities directly into the Bridgewater Commons Transit Center.

Other Options

  • By car, Bridgewater is easily accessible from several major highways and Interstates, including I-287 and I-78.

How to Get Around in Bridgewater

There is a local taxi service and Bridgewater Connector bus system for those without their transportation. There is private transportation available via Uber or Lyft.

Bridgewater is a town with a rich history, beautiful natural landscapes, and numerous tourist sites. It’s an excellent destination for those who love outdoor activities and relaxed holidaying.
