Introduction to Southern Ocean County

Southern Ocean County, located in the Jersey Shore region of New Jersey, is a popular destination for tourists looking for sun, sand, and water activities. The area is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions, making it an ideal vacation spot for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Ocean County

Here are some of the top attractions to consider adding to your Southern Ocean County itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tuckerton Seaport Museum: This living history museum showcases the rich maritime history and traditions of Southern New Jersey.
  • Barnegat Lighthouse State Park: Climb to the top of Barnegat Lighthouse for stunning views of the surrounding coastline, and learn about its history at the on-site museum.
  • Ocean County Historical Society Museum: Exhibits and displays showcase the history of Ocean County from prehistoric times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Southern Ocean County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Island Beach State Park: A undeveloped barrier island with miles of pristine beach, wildlife, and hiking trails.
  • Edwin B. Forsythe National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge is home to hundreds of species of birds and other wildlife, and features hiking trails, fishing, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Long Beach Island: Stretching for 18 miles, Long Beach Island boasts some of the best beaches, fishing, and water sports in New Jersey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Ocean County

Southern Ocean County has plenty of opportunities for biking and hiking:

  • Barnegat Branch Trail: This 15-mile trail runs from Barnegat to Tuckerton and offers a scenic route along the coast.
  • Bass River State Forest: This state forest has over 23 miles of hiking and biking trails, through pine forests and cedar swamps.
  • Long Beach Island Bike Path: This 22-mile paved path is great for leisurely biking along the beach.

Best Things to Do in Southern Ocean County: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Charters: Take a guided fishing charter and try your hand at catching striped bass, bluefish, and other local fish.
  • Tuckerton Seaport Eco-Cruises: Learn about the local ecology and wildlife on a guided eco-cruise through the bays and wetlands of Southern New Jersey.
  • Surf Lessons: Take surf lessons in Long Beach Island and catch some waves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Ocean County

Here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your week in Southern Ocean County:

Day Activities
1 Visit Barnegat Lighthouse and Tuckerton Seaport Museum
2 Explore Island Beach State Park and go fishing
3 Take an eco-cruise through the wetlands and bays
4 Visit Long Beach Island and take a surfing lesson
5 Hike or bike in Bass River State Forest
6 Check out the Ocean County Historical Society Museum
7 Relax on the beach and enjoy the local food and drink

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Ocean County

Southern Ocean County has a variety of food options, ranging from fresh seafood to traditional American fare.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Southern Ocean County is known for its fresh crab, and crab cakes are a popular local dish.
  • Saltwater Taffy: A classic Jersey Shore sweet treat, saltwater taffy comes in all sorts of flavors and can be found in many local candy shops.
  • Asparagus: Asparagus is a local specialty in the area and can be found at farmers’ markets and restaurants throughout Southern Ocean County.

Local Beverages

  • Beach Plum Gin: This local gin is infused with beach plums, a small fruit found on the coast of New Jersey. It’s a popular ingredient in many local cocktails.
  • Local Beer: Several microbreweries in the area offer craft beer that pairs well with local seafood.

Where to Stay in Southern Ocean County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Ocean County has a variety of accommodations to choose from, depending on your budget and preferences:

  • Hotels: From budget to luxury, there are plenty of hotel options in the area. Some popular choices include the Sea Spray Motel, Daddy O Hotel, and Gables Historic Inn.
  • Vacation Rentals: Renting a beach house or condo is a popular option for families and larger groups.
  • Campgrounds: A number of state parks in the area offer camping options, including Island Beach State Park and Bass River State Forest.

What to Buy in Southern Ocean County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beachwear: With so many beaches in the area, there are plenty of shops selling beachwear and souvenirs.
  • Local Produce: Visit one of the many farmers’ markets in the area and bring home some fresh, locally grown produce.
  • Local Crafts: Support local artisans and pick up some handmade crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, or paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Ocean County

The local culture of Southern Ocean County is heavily influenced by its coastal location. The area is known for its maritime history, fresh seafood, and love of the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Southern Ocean County

The best time to visit Southern Ocean County is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the beaches are open. However, spring and fall can also offer mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Ocean County

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the natural beauty and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking or beach time.
  • Do try local seafood and other local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beach or in nature areas.
  • Don’t underestimate the strength of rip currents when swimming in the ocean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Ocean County

Southern Ocean County is a relatively safe area, but visitors should still take precautions to keep themselves safe:

  • Be aware of the risks of swimming in the ocean, including rip currents and jellyfish.
  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight to prevent theft.

Health in Southern Ocean County

Southern Ocean County has several hospitals and medical facilities, so visitors shouldn’t have any issues with medical care. However, it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medications with you.

Getting to Southern Ocean County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Ocean County

  • Atlantic City International Airport (ACY): about 30 miles from Southern Ocean County.
  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): about 80 miles from Southern Ocean County.
  • Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR): about 90 miles from Southern Ocean County.

Bus Stations Near Southern Ocean County

  • New Jersey Transit Buses serve the area.
  • Greyhound Bus Lines serve the area.

Other Options

  • Travelers may want to consider renting a car to get around Southern Ocean County or take a taxi.

How to Get Around in Southern Ocean County

Driving is the easiest way to get around Southern Ocean County, but there are also several public transit options available.

  • NJ Transit Trains or Buses: NJ Transit has train and bus service connecting Southern Ocean County to other destinations in New Jersey and New York City.
  • Taxis/Cabs: Taxis or cabs are available within the area.

Where to Go Next from Southern Ocean County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Atlantic City: This popular casino town is just a short drive from Southern Ocean County.
  • Cape May: This charming seaside town is located to the south of Southern Ocean County.
  • Philadelphia: The birthplace of America is only a short drive away, making it a great option for a day trip from Southern Ocean County.

Conclusion

Southern Ocean County, New Jersey, offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or explore the area’s rich history, Southern Ocean County has something for everyone.
